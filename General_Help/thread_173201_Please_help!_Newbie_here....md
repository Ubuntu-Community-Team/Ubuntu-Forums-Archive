### Post by Herman on 2006-05-10
I used to have dial-up too, not so very long ago, and I never had to install anything in Ubuntu to get my ISP recognised. I used to make sure my dial-up connection was plugged into my computer while I'm installing Ubuntu and it always detected the network automatically and would go right on the internet without any effort on my part at all. At least not that I can remember.
When I used to install 'Warty', it used to install updates from the internet during installation, and with a 56k dial-up connection it used to take all night, I remember that!
I always perform my installs with broadband plugged in, now that I have that. 
When you run Ubuntu or Knoppix from a Live CD they go right on the internet too, without needing anything installed. 

Try enabling the connection by going 'System', ' Administration', 'Networking' and see if there's anything you can do in there that might help. Click 'Modem Connection', and click 'properties', maybe, and see if the checkbox for 'enable this connection' is ticked. 
There are some fields there for you to fill out too, with account details (phone number). If they don't get filled out automatically, you'll need to find out those from old notes, from looking up your old Windows settings, (in control panel somewhere), or by asking your ISP.
You don't need to install anything though, either it works or it doesn't. There's no 'smoke and mirrors' and putting in silly installation CDs in Ubuntu.
If that doesn't work you might need to figure out to help Ubuntu to recognise your modem, you might have some special stuff to do for that, but it's rather rare to have to go to that much work. I hope what I said above will fix it for you. :D Good luck, from Herman
