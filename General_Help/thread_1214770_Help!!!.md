### Post by drs305 on 2009-07-16
Yes, you should post your questions on the forums. Try to pick the correct forum (Absolute Beginners, General, or one tailored to more specific problems such as Multimedia). You can see the different forums by clicking on the "Ubuntu Forums" link in the upper left of this page.

Posting questions on the forums allows other users to find solutions to their problems by reviewing previous posts. Which leads into a request: search the forums first - your problem has probably been addressed in the past. If not, we will be glad to help.

In answer to your second post, the Quick Reply icon is at the lower right of a post, near the "Quote" link. It is the icon that looks like a return arrow.

### Post by t0p on 2009-07-16
I don't know where you got the idea of emailing tech support questions to [email]site@ubuntuforums.org[/email]. Where did you get that address from anyway?

You need to bear in mind that these forums are run by volunteers. Everyone who offers help around here is a volunteer. No one gets paid for helping other users. There is no free support service to answer your emails. If you *expect* someone to answer your emails and help you with your problems, you should think about paying for support from Canonical. If you want free help from this site, post your questions in these forums. And be nice! Remember, no one here has to help you. If we do, it's because we are a friendly, helpful community. Not because we owe you anything.

While I'm giving you advice, here is some more. When you post a question in these forums, give your post a *descriptive* title. Using the word "Help" as a title is not going to attract anyone's attention. Think about it: 95% of the ports here are pleas for help. Do yourself a favour, use a thread title that explains what you want. Then you will be more likely to get the help you need. 

I hope you don't take all this the wrong way. Fact is, the ubuntu community is very friendly and helpful. I love helping out if I can. But you need to learn how to help us help you.

Oh yeah, Welcome to ubuntu!
