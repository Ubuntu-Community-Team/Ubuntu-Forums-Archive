### Post by jonobr on 2012-03-23
Try opening a terminal and typing the command top
This will show you the process running on your machine and and gives them in order of usage.
Heres a quick video on it [Here]("http://www.linuxjournal.com/video/commandline-101-using-top")

Have that in one window and then skip to another and do your normal stuff,
when it looks  like its repeating, see if you can identify if a program or application is taking all the mem or cycles.

I would also try to SSH in and see if that is subject to the same  effect....
