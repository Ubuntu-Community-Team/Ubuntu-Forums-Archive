### Post by mdpalow on 2007-12-26
came back to check on you....

They work pretty simple in that you install them (either VMware or Virtualbox) and once done, you basically run it and when asked to stick in your WinXP disk, you put it in and install WinXP (or whatever). Then you install your Photoshop stuff or whatever windows s/w you have. Then when you need to do some PS work, just bring up your Virtual system and run WinXP. When done, shut XP down and you're in Ubuntu. That is not to suggest you can't use Ubuntu at the same time; you can. Just imagine running Ubuntu and bringing up a window that has WinXP in it.

Virtualbox you can download from Synaptic, but a friend of mine tried it and it seemed more difficult to me then VMware.

VMware you download from the site, get a free serial from them by registering (everything is free) and then install.

Here's a good link for installing vmware.

[HERE]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183209")

Don't be intimidated... I only follow the first few instructions and I'm done.

Good luck..
