### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-10-13
Copies that come preinstalled on PCs are known as OEM copies and, those are licensed to the original machine -- NOT to the person who purchases the machine. So, technically, YOU do not own the license; the machine vendor owns the license.

Without a CD, you're NOT going to be able to install MS Office again. MS will NOT send you a CD because, being a pre-installed copy, it is an OEM version and comes with the machine.  The machine vendor MIGHT send you a CD, but you would have to ask them, not MS.

Also, if this is a different machine, trying to move or copy an OEM-licensed copy violates the licensing agreement and is considered illegal.

And ... assisting folks with performing illegal activities is a violation of the Code of Conduct for these Forums.
