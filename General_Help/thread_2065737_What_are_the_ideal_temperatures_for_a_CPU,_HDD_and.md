### Post by lukeiamyourfather on 2012-10-02
There are no ideal temperatures. As long as they are less than the maximum threshold for the component there's nothing to worry about. For processors that's typically 80 degrees Celsius (same for the chipset). Hard disk drives typically have a maximum operating temperature of 60 degrees Celsius.

### Post by JKyleOKC on 2012-10-02
It will vary from one device to another, but the general rule is "the cooler, the better."

AMD processors tend to run a bit hotter than the Intel variety; my machines use AMD while my wife's system runs an Intel. One of mine holds steady at 40 C for the CPU and 43-44 for the HDD; the other varies wildly for the CPU but seldom gets above 60C and stays around 50 most of the time while the HDD there can vary from 32 to 45. On my wife's machine we only monitor the CPU and it runs in the low 40s but tends to freeze up if it gets above 46 C.

The most important thing is to set a maximum temperature in the BIOS, if you can, so that the machine will shut down automatically before it gets hot enough to damage the CPU. You can find the maximum for your CPU in the manufacturer's specs for the chip. I set mine around 10 degrees below what the chip maker recommends, though.

It's also important to be sure there's adequate ventilation, and the vents on the case aren't clogged or blocked.
