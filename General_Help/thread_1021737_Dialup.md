### Post by Bartender on 2008-12-26
First things first.  Even if you get dial-up to work, it's a pain in Ubuntu.  Ubuntu updates are often 10, 20, 40+ MB.  I remember a 90+MB update.  That's why I bought a laptop for Ubuntu - wi-fi available in town!  
Dial-up gets no love from the Ubuntu developers.  We've had to make do with ancient dialers.  Dial-up in the Networking section was broken back in Ubuntu 7 - has it even been fixed?  I don't know.   
Here's an old guide/diary I worked on a while back
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717)
As far as I know the basic info is still valid.

Only one thing in dial-up hell has improved that I know of - USR's nifty little USB modem.
[http://www.usr.com/support/product-template.asp?prod=5637](http://www.usr.com/support/product-template.asp?prod=5637)

You didn't record your dial-up password??  Better get out a pen and notebook cause you'll probly need it.

Some ISP's work just fine with Linux.  I don't know where you are in the world, but here in the US frys.com & copper.net are two that I know work fine.  There are many others.  ISP's which require proprietary software (I'm lookin at you Juno, AOL, and PeoplePC) are difficult to impossible and should be dropped if you are using them.
