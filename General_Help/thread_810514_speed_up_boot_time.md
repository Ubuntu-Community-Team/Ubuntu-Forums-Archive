### Post by PmDematagoda on 2008-05-28
Get rid of unwanted services(meaning disable them), this will mean some good speed increases but be sure that whatever service you disable isn't required, otherwise it will cause problems. Another way of speeding things up is comipling a kernel that matches your specific system. There are more, but those two are just a few examples of how you can speed things up.

On a more difficult note, you could also tweak the init scripts, I for one know that one particular person managed to reduce his boot time to just 15 seconds, but of course, all that necessitates good experience, skill and time, so don't do it unless you can:).
