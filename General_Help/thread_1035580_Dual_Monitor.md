### Post by kenm_uk on 2009-01-09
Hi.  I recently bought a second monitor for my Fedora machine.  I had problems getting dual monitors to work so decided to give Ubuntu a try.  Ubuntu installed flawlessly and I love it.

But I am still having problems getting the two monitors to work as I would like.  When I boot up and get to the login screen, the **two screens act as one big display**.  I can move the mouse seamlessly from one screen to the other (exactly as I would like).

However, once I log the two screens are clones of each other and I can not figure out how to get back to the big desktop behaviour I had at the login screen.

Hardware: I am running a Gigabyte GA motherboard GA-MA78G-DS3H 780G.  This has a built in ATI video card with a VGA, HDMI, and DVI output.  My VGA monitor is 1280x1024 and my HDMI monitor is 1680x1050.

I have tried the "Screen Resolution" app and the "ATI Catalyst Control Center" but neither fix this issue.

Does anyone have any suggestions?

Thanks so much!

Ken

### Post by kenm_uk on 2009-01-10
One quick comment in case others have the same issue:
ATI Catalyst Control Center allowed me to set up the big desktop but when I rebooted the settings were not saved.  I found in a recent post that you have to go to System > Preferences > Screen Resolution in order for the settings to be saved for the next login.
