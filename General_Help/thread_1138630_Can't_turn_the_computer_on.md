### Post by fooman on 2009-04-26
well first of all,  do not use a vacuum to clean the pc.  you should be using a can of compressed air to blow the dust and crap from the insides.  blow out all the fans,  including case fans, cpu fan and heatsink, video card fans, power supply and any other fans or places where there is dust.  be sure to hold the blades of each fan as you clean them,  it is not good to let them spin widly as you use the can of air.

to try and fix your problem,  i would first shut down and unplug the computer.  then open the side panel and touch some part of the metal frame in the case to dissapate any static electricity from yourself.

then try reseating the hardware on your motherboard,  in particular the ram modules and video card.  carefully remove them and reseat them again firmly, but carefully.

close it up,  plug it in and see if that helps.

### Post by ljerabek on 2009-04-26
Those long beeps usually mean there is a hardware issue. If you know the system type you can lookup the beep pattern to know what the issue is. For example 2 long beeps may beeps could be that the ram is not properly seated. I would start with the re-seating the ram. Also when you power it up can you see if the fan on the CPU is spinning? If it's not spinning then you may have had a faulty fan which fried your CPU.

1. Re-seat the ram (powered off and unpluged) then plug back in and try to power on again. 
2. Check the CPU fan ( If not running could be trouble )
3. Worst case buy a new CPU \ RAM for a few hundread bucks and call it an upgrade! :)
4. In most cases poorly seated cards cause a system just power off or not do anything even beep.


If you have another computer which takes the same ram you could try to test the ram in it. You can always take the ram to get tested at a computer store will cost you 5 bucks. They could also test the power supply to ensure it's working properly.

Good Luck
