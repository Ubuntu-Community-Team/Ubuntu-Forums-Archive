### Post by P4man on 2009-09-02
leave it as it is. Not worth a reinstall. Should you ever do another install, then consider using the 64 bit kernel, but there isn't much to be gained (or lost). Its not likely you'll ever need more than half the address space and RAM the 32 bit kernel gives you.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-09-02
You're probably missing out on 700 megabytes of RAM. If you actually manage to fill up your RAM with cache, then usually the extra 700 megabytes of addressable space would get filled up too; because usually you can only fill your RAM when copying files or ripping DVDs.

Just keep using your system as normal. If you really screw things up you should install 64-bit, but until you actually need to reinstall Ubuntu, just stick with your 32-bit system. The investment in time to reinstall doesn't pay off unless you needed to reinstall anyway. And as Ubuntu can be dist-upgraded from release to release, there's even less excuse to reinstall :-)
