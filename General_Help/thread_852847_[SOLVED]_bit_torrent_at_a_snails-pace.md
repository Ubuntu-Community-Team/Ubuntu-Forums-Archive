### Post by cyclobs on 2008-07-08
thats sort of right,

bit torrent usally has someone seeding (someone with the full file).
when another person loads that same torrent they connect to a tracker that has a list of all the people connected to that same torrent and gives you seeders and etc.

once the other person connects to the seeder the seeder uploads the contents of the torrent to the leacher (known as downloader).

once the leacher has 100% of the torrent file the leacher then becomes a seeder and shares his/her bandwidth with the torrent so when the next person comes to download the torrent it will be faster.

usally the more seeder you are connected to the faster the torrent goes, but note there are also many factors determining the speed of the download, i'll list some for you.

1. Your isp might be restricting your bit torrent traffic to a certain speed.
2. the seeders are not sharing much upload speed to their torrents
3. the seeders might be using all their bandwidth on other torrents
4. the seeder might be on dial thus making all suffer dearly 
5. there might be a lot of leachers on the torrent, this would make it slower because there isn't enough bandwidth to go around to everyone on the torrent.

hope that helps :)
