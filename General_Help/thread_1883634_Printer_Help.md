### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-11-19
I don't use Brother printers, preferring HP as they tend to be very compatible with Linux. However, I did some homework and found that Brother offers a MFC-240C cups print driver for Linux [here]("http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html"). It also offers an information page regarding said driver.

Not owning that type of printer (or needing drivers for it) I can't say that the driver will be totally functional in Ubuntu. Just thought I'd offer a potential solution. If I were you, I'd read through the info and then try the driver. Nothing ventured, nothing gained. ;)

Hopefully, other members who may use Brother printers will respond with even more information.

Good Luck & hope this helps.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2011-11-19
I have a couple Brother printers.  Where did you get the drivers for your MFC-240C? Tamlynmac gives you good advice. The drivers from Brother will likely present the best chance of success although I've had good luck with the Foomatic Database for my supported printer. It's also worth reading the 'before you install' portion of the Brother Linux web pages although most items are not applicable with modern Ubuntu distros. It might also be worth looking at any FAQs for your machine.  Support from Brother is via email so it can take a couple days to get an answer from there.  If you don't know about them, here is the driver & instruction page:   [http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/index.html)    Some FAQs:  [http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/faq_prn.html](http://welcome.solutions.brother.com/bsc/public_s/id/linux/en/faq_prn.html)    I apologize if I'm telling you what you already know. I'm just relating hard-learned lessons.
