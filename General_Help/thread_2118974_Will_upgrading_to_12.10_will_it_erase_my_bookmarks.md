### Post by oldfred on 2013-02-22
Upgrading should not erase anything. Be sure to remove any PPAs for proprietary software as those may not upgrade and stop upgrade in the middle.

But some systems do not upgrade as easily as other, so always best to have good backups before upgrading in case you have to come back and do a new install.

Do you have a separate /home. All the user settings including hidden files & folders for programs are in /home. And a list of installed applications makes it easier to reinstall if need be. 

       Oldfred's list of stuff to backup May 2011:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1748541](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1748541)
    
If you have 10 to 25GB I might do a new install in that partition just as a test so you see that new version works well with your system. And you then have another system to experiment with.
