### Post by stream303 on 2008-06-17
One thing that is very important is to approach it with the right attitude:

Don't try to memorize all the commands and switches in a day. It is like a language, the more you use it, the more you remember - at first you may seem to stumble, but with practice you will be amazed at what comes flying off your fingers without having to look everything up, much like touch-typing. :)

My #1 command:  clear

What's the date:  date
How about a calendar: cal

Can we put them together?
clear ; cal ; date

Obviously there is a lot more than this. :)  I see more new users getting totally frustrated at their inability to remember 300 commands and their switches at their first couple of attempts.  You have to actually USE it, and despite your protests, you'll find it getting increasingly easier.

One big advantage is that what you learn now will most likely be applicable in 20 years, so it isn't a waste of effort.  As nice as gui desktops are, you may not remember them even 10 years from now. :)

### Post by VMC on 2008-06-17
Oddly enough, I just replied to someone who was or is bored. His system is running perfect.

What I suggested to him and now to you would be to just look at the troubles posted on this forum. Read what problems they have. When someone suggests to output a file, then you output that file and compare their results to yours. You'll learn the "list" command. and see how to combine different options to commands.

Grub is a big stumbling block. Follow along and do what is suggested to the poster and in no time you will become and expert in grub issues. Don't worry about the fact their setup is different than yours. Just read and use the commands given. That's just for starters. Just follow each topic and use the Terminal whenever it comes up. And it comes up a lot.

Before you know it, you will be able to suggest and help someone else. The power of that is, not only are you helping someone fix their system, but your helping yourself in the process. It will give you the feeling to pride. Someone who was frustrated you were able to help. Maybe not now but in shorter time than you think.

Learning by route is not good learning. Follow along with real time issues. You will be amazed at how quickly you will master the Unix command set.

I can't stress this enough. Want to learn Unix, and Linux specifically. Read and follow the forums troubles. See where it leads you. You will be going to directories yet unknow to you. You will be entering commands you had no clue existed. In a word you are going to learn the file structure!
