### Post by Rallg on 2009-03-01
The original question is perfectly reasonable. For all we know, a website could be programmed so that it rejects (or looks different) with certain operating systems or browsers.

Keep in mind that when most Linux systems are first installed, they do not have Flash or Java. Many web sites rely on these technologies. Some other technologies, such as Windows Media or QuickTime, may be difficult to implement in Linux, or may play differently. All of this is especially true if a 64-bit version is in use.

Another question would be, is browsing with Linux safe, in terms of the possibility of viruses or trojans? That, I would not know. The fact that most of these are directed towards Windows does not mean that they would not eventually be targeted to Linux. Also, "phishing" scams don't much care what OS you use.
