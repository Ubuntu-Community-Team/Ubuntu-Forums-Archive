### Post by pak33m on 2007-07-16
bsawler,

You should easily be able to right-click on either drive and select Unmount.  It may take a few seconds and you may be prompted about this if you have been working off the drive(s).

Otherwise you can go to the Terminal and type umount <path to drive> to manually unmount the drive(s).

Like any operating system you will be reminded to not just remove the drive without ejecting or unmounting because it could be damaging to your data.
