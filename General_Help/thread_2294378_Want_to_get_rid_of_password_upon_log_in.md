### Post by Topsiho on 2015-09-12
I hope this time my answer will be correct:

Vivid Vervet is Ubuntu 15.04 (I just checked this), which is not an LTS, but an intermediate version.
Intermediate versions are only supported for 9 months after release, 15.04 is released in April 2015, and I let yóu compute when support ends... to not "fall again on my beak", as we Dutch say.

LTS versions (12.04LTS, 14.04 LTS, 16.04 LTS and so on) are supported during 5 years, so 12.04 LTS is supported until April 2017 (as howefield stated), 14.04 LTS until April 2019, and 16.04 LTS will be until April 2021, and so on.

The LTS versions are meant to be very stable, and are somewhat conservative, while the intermediate versions are more state of the art, and meant to be experimental.

So the choice is up to you: are you adventurous, and do you want to help try out some new techniques, or do you need a stable version, which you don't want to reinstall ever 9 months or so.

Topsiho
