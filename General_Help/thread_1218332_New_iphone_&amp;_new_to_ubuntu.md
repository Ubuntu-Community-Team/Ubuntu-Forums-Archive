### Post by grayn0de on 2009-07-20
You could get a Windows person to activate it for you, but you will have to make sure they clean the info out of iTunes afterward, as the iPhone is heavily tied to your iTunes account. If you converted from Windows and have a non-OEM Windows install disk (one straight from Microsoft w/ the shiny bronzish graphics), then I would say checkout VirtualBox. Install windows in a VM to handle any of your iPhone stuff.

I wish I could be of more help on the iPhone/iTunes stuff, but I don't have an iPhone (Android, baby! ;) ). On the VirtualBox/Windows stuff, though, I'll help you out if possible.

You could always contact AT&T or go into an Apple store and see if one of the 'Geniuses' can activate it for you. Just tell them that you do not have a computer that runs iTunes. They might be able to do it for you and are bound by contract (and necessity) not to keep any of your data. It's worth a shot.

Personally, I would check with the closest Apple store, first.

Also, you could try installing iTunes (Windows) under Wine. Perhaps that will get you through the activation process, at least. I wouldn't use it for media playback, though (I recommend Songbird).

See here: [http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=1347](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=1347)
and here: [http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=16848](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=16848)

It shows a silver rating for Ubuntu 9.04 (Jaunty).
