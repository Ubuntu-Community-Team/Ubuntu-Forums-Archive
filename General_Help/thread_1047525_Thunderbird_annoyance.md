### Post by HDave on 2009-01-22
Does anyone know how to get Thunderbird to never prompt for a password if it can't reach a server?

Or at least automatically have the "save password" checkbox checked on the password prompt by default?

I love Thunderbird, but whenever it is unable to connect to the email server, it pops-up a dialog box for me to re-enter the account password.  However, 99.999% of the time its a network problem -- I know this because I rarely change email passwords.  This is annoying.

But more important is this....if you just hit "enter" on the pop-up dialog, and it cannot connect again (because of a persistent network problem) your stored password will go away....causing you to retype it in.  If you have long passwords or multiple accounts this is enough to make you crazy.  The fix is to always check "save password" before hitting enter, but when I am in the middle of editing a document and the stupid pop-up steal focus (don't even get me start on that issue...) I too often accidentally hit enter quickly....arrgggghhh.](*,)
