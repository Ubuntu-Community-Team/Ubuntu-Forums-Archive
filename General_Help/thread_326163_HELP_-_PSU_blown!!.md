### Post by _simon_ on 2006-12-27
Very easy to remove and fit.

Removal:
Simply unplug the power connector(s) taking note of where they go to save you time when you put the new one in. Then just unscrew the 4 screws at the back holding it in.

Fitting:
Put the PSU in place (normally sits on runners, if not hold it) and screw it in with the 4 screws. Then attach the power cables. They should only fit on way.

As for your USB, do you have a manual for your motherboard? If not you'll probably find it online.

There should be a description usually with a picture as to how to wire up your USB. It helps a lot if the wires are already labled. 
You must refer to the manual, don't guess where the USB connectors go, if you get it wrong you may blow your USB ports. As the connector order may vary, double check that the manual / instructions are for your specific board.
The pins on the board are probably labelled but they can be very hard to read. You're looking for a bank or upright pins, probably about 6 or 8 together, it should be tabled as "USB".

The actual "connecting" is simple but fiddly - they just push onto the pins.

### Post by _simon_ on 2006-12-29
Yes that PSU is fine.

Power cord means the kettle cord that you plug in the wall, just use your existing one. 
I've never seen a PSU that did not have a power connector for a floppy but if it doesn't you might be able to get an adapter for it.

Your IDE HDD's will use the molex connectors as will any CD or DVD drives you have.

Best air flow 
- 1 or 2 fans at the front of the case sucking air in. 
- 1 or 2 fans at the rear of the case blowing air out.
- 1 or 2 side fans sucking air in.
- 1 or 2 fans blowing air out of the top.

You don't need all of the above but you do really need the front and back ones.

Make sure any other holes in the case are covered up.

It also helps if you have filters behind the fans that are sucking air into the case else it can get dusty.

It's not that important during winter, it's summer when things can get too hot.
