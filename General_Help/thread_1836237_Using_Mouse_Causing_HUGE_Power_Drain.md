### Post by steevven1 on 2011-08-30
I am running Xubuntu 11.04 with Linux kernel 3.0.

By using PowerTOP, I am seeing that using my mouse is a huge source of power consumption (and thus battery drain) on my laptop.

If I simply move my mouse around and around in circles, my estimated battery life drops to nearly half of what it is with the same programs open, not touching my mouse.

In fact, browsing the web, etc with keyboard shortcuts instead of using the mouse significantly increases my battery life.

This occurs with both my TrackPoint and my USB optical mouse.

Any explanations for this or ideas on how to solve it?

---

### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-08-31
I would suspect that the power reading are bogus.  One way to check would be to use a wireless mouse.  These are battery powered -- and thus, can NOT be a drain on the laptop battery.  If you still see a power drain, you then know that the readings are worthless.
