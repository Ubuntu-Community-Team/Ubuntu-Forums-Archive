### Post by t0p on 2009-06-22
If I was you, I wouldn't bother with the eeepc.  It's a nice machine and all - I love my little 701 - but it isn't any more suited to "beginners" than any other machine.  The eeepc got its "beginners" reputation because of the super-simple kindergarten version of Xandros it came with.  But if you're going to install Ubuntu, it'll be no less advanced than a regular laptop running ubuntu - but it'll have smaller screen, smaller keyboard, possibly smaller solid state disk... go with a full-size laptop.

As for NetBook Remix... Nah!  Full-size laptop, full-size Ubuntu.  That's what I recommend.

But then we have to take into account the importance you place on iPhone synching.  I really don't think there's a simple way to achieve this in Ubuntu.  So maybe Ubuntu isn't the right OS after all...
