### Post by drewsus on 2011-02-13
Okay, okay, now I am on board. Some of your wording confused me, but I think I got it. 
So, on your bottom panel, right click on some empty space and select "Add to panel...".
Find and highlight "Window List" and click the "Add" button. Now you will see the list of open windows down there!
However, its probably not where you want it. So, right click JUST to the left of the left-most tab and select "Move." Move it to where you like, then do the same right click thingy and select "Lock" to keep it there.

I am more than happy to help!
You might want to try an alternative that I use called dockbarx. I looks a bit like the new windows 7 window list. 

```
sudo add-apt-repository ppa:dockbar-main/ppa
sudo apt-get update
sudo apt-get install dockbarx dockbarx-themes-extra dockmanager dockmanager-daemon libdesktop-agnostic-cfg-gconf libdesktop-agnostic-vfs-gio
```

And add it the same way that I just told you to, but replace "Window List" with "Dockbarx" and see what you think. It can be customized via dockbarx preferences
