### Post by oldfred on 2010-05-17
My story bottom line was a new video card.

One night I was using computer and heard a loud pop, but was not sure where it was from (even a bird into the outside wall?). Computer kept working so I did not think I had a problem. Till next morning my wife says computers not working. Mine had not beeps and nothing came on at all.

So I did think power supply was a little light and wanted something large so I installed a new one. Nadda. Then since it seemed like motherboard was not working I installed a new motherboard. Nadda. Finally tried now video card and voila, success. On closer inspection the the nvidia 7600gs had capacitors with the tops blown open - like 6 of the 10. It turns out the motherboard would not even start without a good video card. And all my data in the then 2 drives was still there.

### Post by jerome1232 on 2010-05-17
If it beeps twice that usually means a post error. ie your motherboard, ram or cpu is dead in the water. Only ways to really figure it out is to swap out parts or use a post card that will give you codes and tell you exactly why post is failing.
