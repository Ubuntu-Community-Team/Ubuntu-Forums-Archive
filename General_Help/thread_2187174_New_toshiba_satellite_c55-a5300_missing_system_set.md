### Post by spiritualphilosoph on 2013-11-11
Nevermind about the missing settings (Blushes)... They're in the setting manager just under mail reader. Some apps that were included in 12.04 were not with 13.10 I guess. I just downloaded everything from the ubuntu software center. If I forgot to mention, I am running in UEFI mode with this ubuntu install. All I had to do is turn off secure boot and manually tell the so to speak bios to start from the DVD drive.

I just did an update on the language packs from the settings manager, gmusic player still show weird character, but I am uninsatlling it anyways as I use VLC media player.

Everything thus far appears to work, even the wifi. The only thing that doesn't is the volume icon in the top menu bar, but the hotkeys do work. Just remember, on a toshiba, press "FN" then what ever hot key you wish to use.

Other than that, this thing is FAST! 

I'll mark this as solved. Also I came back to clarify incase anyone comes across intalling ubuntu on their newer toshiba.
