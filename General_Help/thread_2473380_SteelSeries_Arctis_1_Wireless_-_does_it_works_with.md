### Post by grahammechanical on 2022-04-02
When a manufacturer says it does not support Linux it means two things. It does not provide device drivers for its product and/or it has not trained its support staff to trouble shoot and fix problems in the Linux OS. It does not necessarily mean that the device will not work in Linux. The Linux developers work very hard providing device drivers for all kinds of hardware. At present the Linux firmware package (drivers) is more than 226 MB in size. All these drivers are automatically available in Ubuntu.

The Lenovo T570 comes with a Bluetooth adapter built in. If you go to System Settings>Bluetooth you will see if Ubuntu has a driver for that Lenovo Bluetooth adapter. I suspect that Ubuntu has a driver and you can activate Bluetooth.

As for the headphones please consider this. Bluetooth wireless technology is an established protocol that has become a standard which product have to meet in order to be described as Bluetooth devices.

> Bluetooth is managed by the [Bluetooth Special Interest Group]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth_Special_Interest_Group")  (SIG), which has more than 35,000 member companies in the areas of  telecommunication, computing, networking, and consumer electronics. The [IEEE]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Electrical_and_Electronics_Engineers") standardized Bluetooth as **IEEE 802.15.1**,  but no longer maintains the standard. The Bluetooth SIG oversees  development of the specification, manages the qualification program, and  protects the trademarks.[SUP][[4]]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth#cite_note-4")[/SUP] A manufacturer must meet [Bluetooth SIG standards]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth_Special_Interest_Group#Qualification") to market it as a Bluetooth device.[SUP][[5]]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth#cite_note-5")[/SUP] A network of [patents]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patent") apply to the technology, which are licensed to individual qualifying devices.

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth)

As for the headphones that you are interested in I notice that they are not described as Bluetooth but as wireless, I see this reply to the question, Does it use Bluetooth?

> No, the Arctis 1 Wireless uses a gaming-grade 2.4GHz wireless platform,  the same technology used in the Arctis 7 and Arctis Pro Wireless  headsets. This gives you significantly better performance than  Bluetooth. The audio is lossless instead of compressed for far better  quality. It also has ultra-low latency so that you hear every sound as  it happens on screen, as opposed to Bluetooth, which can have  significant delays.

So, I have been barking up the wrong tree! You need to find out the wireless standard those headphones comply with. Is it a WiFi standard?  From the pictures on the web site it seems to come with a dongle. It is any body's guess if the dongle is recognised by Linux. What wireless standard do these headphones use?

Regards
