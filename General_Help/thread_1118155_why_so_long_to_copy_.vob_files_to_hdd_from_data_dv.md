### Post by mc4man on 2009-04-06
Are you 'copying' the .vobs (file operation) or 'ripping' the dvdr? (video_ts folder

Your 'copy' (file operation) speed shouldn't be more than a hair less than the speed you originally ripped at.
Figure a dvdr with 4 .99Gb .vobs should copy back in about 4.5 - 5 min. The speed would increase as data was copied, a decent start speed would be 4-4.5 Mb/sec, should end up at 13 -15 Mb/sec min.

If you burnt the dvdr as a dvd video disk (video_ts) then re 'ripping' should as fast if not faster than the original rip, again in the 4 min or so range for dvdr (single layer

Was your original rip speed slow?


edit: those times would be for a desktop dvd drive, laptop drives are typically 50% or so slower (even more if they're 'riplock' enabled drives
