### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-11-09
Unsure of your version of Firefox, if you are talking about doing this in Firefox, but Edit> Preferences> Privacy. Under 'History' in the right panel you will see a link to 'delete individual cookies'. Click that and you'll see the cookies.

Always best to do a little research prior to posting, though, as this info is readily available [online]("https://duckduckgo.com/?q=firefox+delete+cookies+ubuntu"). :)

### Post by ajgreeny on 2015-11-09
There are lots of cookie managers in FF add-ons.

I have been using [cookie-culler]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/cookieculler/?src=ss") for years with no problems.  It has not been updated for a very long time but still works perfectly.

It allows you to protect cookies from certain sites which you want to allow, and you can delete everything else with a single click, or manually delete the cookies you want, one by one.  You can stop the sites from which you've just deleted cookies from setting any more on your system.
