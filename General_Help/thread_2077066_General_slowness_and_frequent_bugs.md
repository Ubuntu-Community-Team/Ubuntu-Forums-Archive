### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-10-27
Firstly, I'll take a punt you are using 12.10. It has been released for two weeks. Try 12.04 LTS (long-term support) as stabler and supported until April 2017.

Secondly, bump once every twenty four hours in future please and, while it can be frustrating, please remember everyone is here voluntarily and is under no obligation to help you or anyone else. People have looked at your thread; obviously no-one has a fix or they would have posted. 

Thirdly, profanity will not be tolerated, real or implied. I have edited your post accordingly.

Fourthly, good luck, but as I say, try 12.04 if you're not running it already, and take a deep breath; you are new to this so there will be a learning curve. If you are on 12.10, 12.04 would be a much better place to find your feet. Once you know a stable system you can progress to testing things on the 'bleeding edge'. (Raring Ringtail, 13.04, is testing at the moment with daily builds available; see the Ubuntu +1 sub-forum). ;)

PS: The obvious question is have you done an update? If not, do that and reboot. As 12.10 is so fresh, presuming that is what you're using, updating daily would be the go at the moment. Generally after install there are a few bugs and tweaks to work through after a fresh install to get things' just so', but once done it is pretty much smooth sailing, which is one of the reasons I love Linux. I need my main machines to be solid, reliable workhorses. 

Side note: I personally can't stand Unity but no issue because I have used Xubuntu for ages. Rips along on a machine with 4Gb of RAM and I don't need the fluff and bloat of Ubuntu. I have a minimal install with Xfce4 desktop environment from Xubuntu running on the old 32bit box with 1Gb of RAM. There is only what's required on that box installed and it is as fast as the 4Gb RAM laptop!
