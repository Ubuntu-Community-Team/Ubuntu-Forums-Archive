### Post by jimv on 2008-07-01

### Post by zarqoon on 2008-07-01
Since your laptop did not even start reading the cd since you did not yet change the boot order if i understand correctly, it could not have been ubuntu.

Try removing your battery and power supply wait about 30 seconds then reconnect them.
If it still does not work try again and reconnect just the battery or the supply.
If neither of these work most likely some internal component of the power system is fried. 
If you know what you are doing you could try to open the case and look for something that looks burned and try to replace it. This is not easy, may not work and may do further damage if you are not careful.

If your laptop is still under warranty use it before opening anything.
