### Post by jflaker on 2010-03-01
remove the power source...

Disassemble the keyboard and rinse with water......

dab dry the innards, and shake the water out....

If you got water into the electronics, pour a little bit of alcohol on the circuit boards and again shake excess from parts.

Speed up drying with a hairdryer...if the innards a just damp, it should not effect the operation....

Re-assemble and continue to use.

I have done this to 2 of my computer's keyboards at least 3 times each and I am still using them.

(22 years in IT....I saved more keyboards with unconventional cleaning in this way than I can count....This method is also effective in dislodging the most stubborn dirt under the keys....
