### Post by gerowen on 2010-12-14
So recently I noticed that the Windows/MAC versions of Yahoo are looking very spiffy.  There used to be a Linux version, but it was nowhere near as good as its counterparts.  So I decided to engage Yahoo on this issue, and they've confirmed that they don't even offer it any more.  Here's the transcript from my conversation:
[quote=Yahoo Support]One moment as we route your chat to a Yahoo! Agent. The average wait time is 0 minutes and 1 seconds.
Welcome to Yahoo! Messenger. My name is Trixie.
Your Issue ID for this chat is LTK42601966613X
Trixie: Hi! Welcome to our Yahoo! Messenger Live Chat service. I'm glad you've joined us.
Trixie: Thank you for providing us the details of your issue.
Marcus: Hello
Trixie: I understand that you would like to know if Yahoo! Messenger is available for Linux. Am I correct?
Marcus: Yes, I remember a couple of years ago there was a Linux version, but it was very ancient looking and didn't
support all of the features as the Windows version, but now I don't even see any links for a Linux version. Have you guys
dropped support for it altogether? It'd be kind of nice to be able to use all of the features in a native Linux application.
(Yahoo Call/Voice, etc.)
Trixie: Thank you for that information.
Trixie: Please keep in mind 
[/quote]
