### Post by Cheesemold on 2007-01-01
> **thomasa93 said:**
> Just a question: what are your issues with Gaim?:confused:

Mmmm.. It's one of those things where if you're asked to give examples, not too many come to mind.

For one, it screws with my contact list -- If I delete a contact in the regular MSN client, GAIM re-adds them without my permission.  

File transfers are a bit iffy at times-- though that could be because of my wireless network, instead of GAIM.

I have some issues with how it displays information, and I wish I had some way of moving buttons and data around ( Maybe if I knew anything about programming, this would be a non-issue).

Also GAIM is pretty dry on the visual side, but I'm not sure what it is that should be improved aesthetically.

Other than that, I think GAIM is a pretty good application.  I only have MSN, so I don't require any of it's extra, multiple-different-IM-network features, but I like it's simple little features that MS overlooks.  Stuff like dating every messege that goes in or out, having the GUI-notifications tell me stuff not only like when someone logs in, but also what messege they send me when the window is not in focus ( that lets me not worry about switching back and forth between windows when someone gives a simple response to something that doesn't require my full attention, it's dang convinient).

A lot of wanting a different client is just the desire to have a consistent experience between the current official client and the linux one.
