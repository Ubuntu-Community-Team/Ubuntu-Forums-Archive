### Post by colorlessprism on 2010-03-20
[http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html](http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html), is a handy little book, link should also have been included in your "welcome to the forum" message from ubuntu-geek. i worked through two books one titled "LINUX" and another titled "Running Linux". both were ok, but honestly google is much more help. ive learned much more in my "quests" than in any other book. with that in mind you are bound to tank your system at least as much as i have, so remember to backup frequently i use a combination on "Remastersys" and tar. once you take the plunge to linux especially ubuntu you will not go back...(psst...i haven' t dual booted for a very long time)

more info about remastersys (the BEST backup program i have ever used...)
[http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/remastersystool.html](http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/remastersystool.html)
[http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/ubuntu.html](http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/ubuntu.html)

more info about tar backups/restores (i do this for my "home" folders)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem/TAR](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BackupYourSystem/TAR)
