### Post by LFMZZ on 2010-02-15
> **lovinglinux said:**
> See [Firefox optimization and troubleshooting thread](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1193567).

Thanks so much for your suggestion. I am posting what I did in hopes that it might help other noobs like me.

- I followed the instructions on the Firefox optimization and troubleshooting. Particularly useful was the part about removing conflicting addons, in my noobness I had installed both free and non-free flash players.

-I checked I had the latest version of flash player on adobe.

-I installed Google Chrome. I still need Firefox as I use Zotero bibliography manager (not available on Chrome), but I am thinking of sticking to Chrome for everything else.

-I tried a youtube video on Chrome and it was horribly choppy (like in Firefox).

-Discovered I had no video drivers (noob extraordinaire!). I am using an IBM T42, so I downloaded the ATI Binary X.Org driver.

-Restarted.

-WOOT! Fast browsing, no crashes so far. Using Chrome though.

-Minus side: No full screen videos, it makes the flash plugin crash (but luckily not the browser or the computer!)

I feel that being an Ubuntu/Linux noob is more dangerous than a loose monkey with a scalpel. I hope I survive.
