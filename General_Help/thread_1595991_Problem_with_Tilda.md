### Post by Ocxic on 2010-10-13
The Command "tilda -C" doesn't open the Preferences, it actually launches tilda with the preferences windows open.
so every time you type the command your launching a new tilda session and getting a new tilda window. 

you open tilda
you type "tilda -C" => you open tilda window #2 and get a preferences dialog"
you set preferences, and close the window bringing you to tilda #2's window
you type "tilda -C" again to make another change => you get tilda window 3 and it's preferences window, and all you options and setting have dissapeared.
then your confused (lol)

Just right-click on your tilda window and select preferences.
