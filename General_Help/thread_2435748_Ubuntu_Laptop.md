I'm in desperate need of a laptop to run Ubuntu. I was using Ubuntu in two laptops from two brands (Asus and HP) for years and they both had these issues:

- The battery got killed after about 2 months. I can't use them unless I plug in the charger. Both have Windows installed previously longer than 2 months (the Asus one about 2 years) and never had this issue.

### Post by CatKiller on 2020-01-25
> **souvenirque said:**

My XPS 13 came with Ubuntu pre-installed. I've had it for about five years. It's been flawless. The battery was getting a bit tired so I recently replaced it, which was pretty easy. I think it came with 14.04, and I upgraded it to 16.04 and then 18.04. I used to use Cinnamon on it, but I recently switched it to KDE. 

If I needed a new laptop I wouldn't hesitate to get one of those, particularly since the new models come with a 16:10 screen.

They've got other models that come with Ubuntu pre-installed now. Those are marketed as Developer Edition, or referred to internally as Project Sputnik.

### Post by Autodave on 2020-01-25
Dell and an HP and a Sony Vaio.  All were thrown away.  One had a bad HD in it, one had something spilled on the keyboard (a soak of the keyboard in warm water fixed that), and one was deemed too old to run new software. 2 of the 3 had bad batteries when I got / found them.
