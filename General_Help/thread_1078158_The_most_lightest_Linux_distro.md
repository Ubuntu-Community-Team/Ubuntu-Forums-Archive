### Post by joey-elijah on 2009-02-23
Your problem is more to do with power management than an heavy OS. E.G. running XP wouldn't eat your battery up like this and it's not a light-weight OS.

Firstly are you sure that your battery sin't just starting to get worn out? Battery's capacity diminish with time. 

Secondly, have you tried installing the CPU-Frequency scaling applet (assuming you use Gnome) ? This will let you select from various power saving 'modes' for when your computer is on such as 'power save' or 'on demand'. This will help battery time.

Secondly, what kind of tasks do you run? Any CPU intensive tasks will drain the battery quicker - E.G watching full screen video, video editing etc.

Power Management is something that should be better in the next release of Ubuntu.

### Post by stalkingwolf on 2009-02-23
It sounds like you have a defective battery or that you have developed 
"battery memory".  This happens with cell phones as well as laptops and other "rechargeables".

Most manufacturers recommend that you let the battery run almost all the way down if not all the way, before charging.  If you constantly recharge at say half way eventually the battery will develope a "memory" and that is
all it will charge.
