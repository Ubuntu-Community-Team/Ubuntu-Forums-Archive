### Post by rdh61 on 2010-01-19
This could be a bug in ACPI.[U]

WORK AROUND:[/U]  Gleaned from other threads and various sources.

Edit file /etc/modules: add line "apm" (no quotation marks)
Edit file /etc/default/grub: at GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT add "apm=power-off" in quotation marks and directly after any other commands without leaving a space between them (if I left a space it didn't work!)
Then in terminal type: sudo update-grub

After rebooting shutdown works normally.
