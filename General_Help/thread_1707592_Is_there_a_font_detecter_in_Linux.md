### Post by rubylaser on 2011-03-15
This is the one I always use. It's web based, so it works on any platform.  You just answer some questions, and it will suggest a bunch of fonts that match your criteria.
[http://www.identifont.com/]("http://www.identifont.com/")

The whatthefont one is web based and should work fine on Ubuntu as well.
[http://new.myfonts.com/WhatTheFont/]("http://new.myfonts.com/WhatTheFont/")

One other way that I just found now

It's the simplest and I don't know how it never came to my mind!

- Copy/paste the text to OpenOffice.org Word Processor
- Then just click anywhere on the text (in between one of the words)

On the "font name" tab (that nrmally shows "Times New Roman") you should see it change into whatever type of text you want to copy

Done!

In my case I found that I had "DejaVu Sans Mono" :)
