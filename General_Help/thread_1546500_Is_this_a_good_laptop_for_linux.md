### Post by Rubi1200 on 2010-08-05
Quick answer: the specifications seem to be good
 
Better answer: download the ISO and burn to CD then try Ubuntu as a LiveCD to see if everything works nicely.
 
You should do this anyway before installing to see if you like the look and feel of the system as well as to get an idea of what is installed and how it works etc.
 
Just remember that a LiveCD works slower than a hard-drive install; don't be surprised if things seem to lag.
 
I also suggest you read this:
 
[http://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/](http://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/)

### Post by JBAlaska on 2010-08-05
The Asus U30JC-A1 will work with 10.04 with a integrated Intel graphics.

But since it also has nVidia switchable graphics with Nvidia Optimus technology, you will not be able to use that discrete chip at this time, from what I understand it would take major Xorg recoding to work in Optimus mode.

Personally I've had very good compatibility with my older Acer Aspire 6920G (except for the biometric scanner), and have installed Ubuntu on a couple of Acer netbooks with good results.

Recently my Acer 24" LCD monitor gave out, just for fun I checked if it was still under warranty..To my surprise it had a 3 year warranty and was covered and returned to me in 2 weeks FREE, so I'm kinda partial to Acer ATM.

If you want to go with Asus, try this [Wiki](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport/Machines/Netbooks) for Ubuntu hardware support, it has several Asus laptops listed.
