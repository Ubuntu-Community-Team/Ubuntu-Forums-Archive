### Post by TBABill on 2011-09-07
It's difficult to provide any advice on something you did that's still considered experimental at best. Putting Gnome 3 on a system built around Gnome 2 is itself a recipe for problems, but it can be done. I have seen posts where users have successfully upgraded, but I never tried because of the difficulties I have seen others post about it. To answer your KDE/Gnome question, yes you can install both and choose which to use at login. However, having Gnome 2, Gnome 3 and KDE installed on a borked system is bound to further complicate the problems.
 
If it were my system I think I'd chock it up to a loss and reinstall after saving my /home docs. There's no direct support for your problem so few users may have encountered the problem in the manner you did, making it difficult to assist.
