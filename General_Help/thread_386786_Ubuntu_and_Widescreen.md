### Post by fragos on 2007-03-17
What's the resolution of your widescreen?  I have a Viewsonic VA1912wb and its native resolution is 1440x900.  I'd also like to see your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file.  It has the X configuration in it.  Also what video card do you have.  Video card determine video driver which in turn uses only resolutions that it feels are appropriate.  "vesa" driver for example won't do more than 1024x768.  In a terminal window run "lspci |grep VGA" and provide the output.

### Post by fragos on 2007-03-17
you only have the open source diver "nv", it's only 2D.  Use Synaptic to install package nvidia-glx.  "nv" will be changed to "nvidia" in xorg.conf.  You will then get 3D acceleration and your resolution may then work.  Also I've never seen your particular resolution before.  Why don't you add 1440x900 as an option in xorg.conf.  I know its supported by the "nvidia" driver.  I'm not sure what the driver does when the only configuration option it is isn't supported, it has nothing to fall back to.  You need not worry about frequency.  The default 60 works well for all LCD monitors and flicker isn't a problem as with a CRT.
