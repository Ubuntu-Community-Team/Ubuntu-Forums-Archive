### Post by efflandt on 2017-05-22
I don't think there is anything in Ubuntu that limits how much a laptop battery charges. That is likely either controlled by circuitry in the battery or laptop (to limit over charging a li-ion battery) or maybe the circuitry accurately reflects how much of a charge the old battery can currently hold at max voltage for a li-ion battery.

I have an old Dell laptop with shot battery, its BIOS told me the battery needs to be replaced, the charging LED on top of the laptop constantly flashes between green and amber. And while it appears to take a 100% charge in Linux, it does not last very long on battery power.
