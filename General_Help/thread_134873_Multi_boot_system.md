### Post by steveneddy on 2006-02-22
I have a six year old PIII system with two 40 Gig HD's, one of which has Win2K and the other Ubuntu. You need to understand how Linux labels the HD's. There won't be a C: or D: drive, it will be hda and hdb. 

I have 512mb RAM and the machine runs like a top. I must add that I am a Nazi about keeping everything updated and running properly. My case is vacuumed on a regular basis on the inside. I still keep Windows updated, even though it is never booted. I followed the instructions I found on this forum.

Install Ubuntu, install Automatix, then update the system. You will have lots of fun. Play with the system. Break it, fix it and re-install if you have to. That how I learned and you can do the same. 

Linux is on the second HD. If you are new to this much computer stuff, get an A+ cert book or read about hardware installation. Make sure the first HD is the master (jumpers) and the second drive is the slave. Ubuntu will do the rest.

It's easy. Have fun.
