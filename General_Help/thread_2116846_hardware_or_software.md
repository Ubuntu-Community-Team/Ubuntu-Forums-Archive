### Post by MrsUser on 2013-02-17
Indeed, sounds like a RAM problem. I head something similar once on my old computer. It also started with bluescreens and video problems. Luckily the RAM was not damaged, it was just not properly plugged to the mainboard. Sometimes it helps to remove the RAM, clean the contacts a bit and plug it back in. This is what I would do before running a RAM check software on it.
