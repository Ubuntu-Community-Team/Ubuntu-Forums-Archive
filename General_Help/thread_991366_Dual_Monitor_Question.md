### Post by jimmy the saint on 2008-11-23
Your best bet is to either get another monitor of the same vertical size or switch to Xubuntu.  I spent 18 months toying with different solutions in gnome.  None work well for mismatched monitors.  The biggest problem is that gnome does not recognize the difference and you lose icons.  Real pain.  Xubuntu on the other hand recognizes the mismatch and works beautifully.  It is really a nautilus issue, as that is what controls your desktop.

I finally came accross a used monitor that was just what I needed.  In the meantime, if you get your smaller monitor and put it to the right and aligned with the top of your right, it is the best of the worst solutions.  Also, the smaller one better not be vga with the bigger being DVI or you HAVE to have the smaller one on the left bevause VGA is always disignated primary.

FUN!  At least dual monitors is simple with Ubuntu!!
