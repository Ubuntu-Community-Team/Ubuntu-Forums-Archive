### Post by mozetti on 2006-05-03
Yes and Yes.

I have a Logitech Cordless Desktop (keyboard & optical mouse) and it works fine. Ubuntu recognized it right away, but I had to change my BIOS to use the "Legacy USB Mouse and Keyboard". Without that, I wasn't able to use the keyboard to get into the BIOS setup, or to choose the Kernel/OS to boot from GRUB.

I also use a Linksys WRT54G v1.1 router with both a Linksys WMP54G PCI card (ralink chip) for my desktop and a SMC 2632W WiFi-B (atmel chip) on my laptop. Breezy recognized them both natively.

### Post by Jason_25 on 2006-05-03
Unless it's bluetooth wireless, wireless keyboard and mice are not dependent on software running on the computer.  They are treated like any other usb keyboard.
