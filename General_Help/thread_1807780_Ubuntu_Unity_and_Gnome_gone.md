### Post by Joris Donders on 2011-07-19
Try to press Ctrl+Alt+ Backspace this brings you back to the login-screen. 

Choose there at the right side bottom for starting up in Gnome Classic. 

Now you can proceed with accessing the terminal (Ctrl +Alt+t) and purge what you have installed. 

Again go back to the login screen and start up in Unity. I think it is advisable to open again the terminal and type ```
unity --reset
``` and press enter. 
This action resets your Unity to default values so you can begin again. 

The bottom line is getting back to your login screen and choose the Gnome enviroment, above all ! 

Succes !
