### Post by scouser73 on 2009-08-26
I like to cut down on some of the start up applications like the Bluetooth, login sound and so on.  I do have four screenlets that start up and they are; Clear Calendar, Uptime, Clear Weather & Perfect Clock.

I use Bootchart to show me a graphical image of the system as it's loaded and this can be very useful in determining bottlenecks.

### Post by compiledkernel on 2009-08-26
A few things really, or at least what I do. 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=89491](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=89491) -- Using sysv-rc-conf. The thread is old for sure, but many things in it are still pretty valid.

What scouser73 also said, cut back on your start-up processes which you don't use. This reduces start-up time, but also leaves free more memory since the processes are not loaded.
Yes, there are lightweight alternatives for programs to increase CPU performance, but, as you also said, you don't want to loose functionality. It's said that Abiword is an alternative for OpenOffice.org Writer. Maybe it is, but can it do the same? I don't know.
Of course you can search a long time to really squeeze the programs you will use, but how much time do you loose by that?
I don't know your computer, what CPU you have, how much memory you have, what kind of programs you need to use, but maybe (if necessary) a hardware update will bring you more. That way you can use the larger programs with all of their functionality and still have a fast system.
