### Post by CatKiller on 2013-04-18
9.10 is pretty old. You might consider a more recent version since you're just starting out.

Installing random stuff from the Internet is not a good plan for a new user. Stick to the packages in the repositories. There should be a program listed as Additional Drivers or similar that will find and install the appropriate closed-source drivers, or you can do the same thing through Synaptic.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-04-18
Try visiting your local library or contact your local Linux User Group for a copy of Ubuntu 12.04.

Ubuntu 9.10 is ancient - like Windows 98 in Linux terms. It's repositories are long gone as that version of the distro is out of support.

With Ubuntu 12.04, you can plug in your phone and turn on tethering on the phone. Ubuntu will automatically start using your phone as the internet connection, and you can install the Nvidia driver the regular way in Additional Drivers.

But not with 9.10.
