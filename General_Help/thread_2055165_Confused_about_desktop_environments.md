### Post by sgage on 2012-09-08
> **jacatone said:**
> What's the difference between Gnome and Unity? Are they separate desktop environments or is Unity part of Gnome? I guess Linux Mint is still using Gnome or is it using a different DE? Thanks.

Unity and Gnome Shell, and Gnome Classic, are all user interfaces on top of the Gnome 3 "plumbing". Gnome Shell is the Gnome Project's official UI. Unity is Ubuntu's take on a UI. But they both rely on the underlying Gnome 3 infrastructure.

Linux Mint has all sorts of options. "Cinnamon" is basically a collection of extensions for Gnome Shell, and thus sits atop Gnome 3. Their "Mate" thingy is a fork of Gnome 2, and is in my opinion a dead end and a waste of time and effort. They really should have poured their effort into Gnome Classic, if that's the kind of thing they wanted. Oh well.

If you keep in mind the different layers, it is a bit easier to comprehend.

### Post by thnewguy on 2012-09-08
The easiest way to think about it might be that Unity and Gnome Shell are two different environments and they are developed by different people. However, both Gnome Shell and Unity use some of the same building blocks. They share some technology, but they are separate desktop environments.
