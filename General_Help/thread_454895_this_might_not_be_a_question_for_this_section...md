### Post by aicrono on 2007-05-26
It does sounds like an overheating problem to me. Is your laptop made by Dell? I recall that they have placed their exhaust fans on the bottom of the laptop, and if you have your laptop bottom completely covered up, the hot air might not have any where to go and just keep on heating up your system. I would suggest propping your laptop up on something on the corners for a little while during gaming just to see if it will keep it slighting less hot.

### Post by m.musashi on 2007-05-26
It's amazing how hot a laptop can run and not have problems. I've been running folding at home on a laptop for about a month straight. It runs the cpu at 100% and I've not had any problems. However, if it isn't getting good airflow then it will get too hot and I think most will shut down to avoid permanent damage. Check your fans and make sure they aren't blocked and are working. If it keeps dying then it could be a hardware issue. Check with the maker.
