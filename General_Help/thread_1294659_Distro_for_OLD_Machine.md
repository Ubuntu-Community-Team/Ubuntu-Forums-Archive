### Post by 2blue on 2009-10-21
> **vinutux said:**
> I am alredy tried ubuntu main version....but it is :confused:very slow


Any processor above 1GHz and RAM 512 should work OK with Ubuntu, not too slow, but not at all fast compared to smaller distros. I doubt Xubuntu will be noticeably faster. I have played around with small minimal laptops and linux-distros, and it looks like the Xfce desktop environment alone doesn't make a big difference. I don't know Dreamlinux, but it looks comparable to Puppy from their website. 

Is 512MB RAM maximum for your computer? If not it might run more easily with double that, but it's a "try and see" kind of situation. If you are a bit lucky you can find two new memory cards each 512MB on ebay for about 20 &#8364;, depending on what you computer requires. How large is the processor?

You might find the Puppy linux derivatives interesting (called puplets), they are worth checking out if you don't already know them. The are oftena bit larger than the official Puppy and when they are up and going, Puppy really are very fast, flexible and I doubt there is much to miss compared to larger distros like Ubuntu, Mandriva or Freespire. Pyppy has separate update and package managers not unlike Ubuntu. At first the Puppy-desktop might seem a bit cluttered, but it easily adjust to most needs and requirements both visually and practically.    

I find it hard to rate many of the small linux distros much better than the rest, it very much depends on hardware and how easily drivers and software adjust to it.

Good luck with your research :)
