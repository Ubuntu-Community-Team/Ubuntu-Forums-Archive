### Post by StuartN on 2010-06-09
> **nonhocapito said:**
> What is it, painful to just come out and say "no, sorry, ubuntu doesn't have this feature"?

_It hurts like hell_, but no, Ubuntu does not have this feature. There is a Recent Documents under the Places menu.

If you really like this feature, then go to [http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/) and add a description where other people will be able to support and comment on it. Some developer may take up the challenge.

I have NoteCase installed and I add my long-term and current projects as links, e.g. **file:///home/stuart/file.html** opens in the default application, **file://gedit /home/stuart/file.html** forces it to open in an alternative application and even **file://gnome-terminal -e "/home/stuart/script.sh /home/stuart/file.html"** runs a script on the file. It is a collapsible notepad, so I can switch between pages or hide a project or hierarchy of projects until I work on them again. It is an alternative to modifying the menu.
