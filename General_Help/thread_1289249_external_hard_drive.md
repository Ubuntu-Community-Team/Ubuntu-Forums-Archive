### Post by mechro on 2009-10-12
> **abhilashm86 said:**
> 

many of my friends have windows installed, when i data transfer with them, will my files in external hard disk gets corrupted? How to overcome it?



Most file formats will easily transfer between operating systems without corrupting. E.g. all your photo jpg's will still be photo jpg's wherever you put them. 

Issues usually arise when you try to read or edit certain formats that are either proprietary or you don't have the app to manipulate it, but there's usually no "currupting", you just won't be able to "read" it.

I don't think you can protect your files from being infected by viruses on someone else's computer just by using one manufacturer's disk drive rather than another's. You protect your files by not exposing them to a virus in the first place. The way to achieve this is by getting your friends to use anti-virus software.

On the subject of backups: you should backup all of your data, whether that is 1 MB, 1 GB or 1 TB. Failing to backup your files because there is "too many of them" is a good way to lose the lot - especially if you are connecting your disk drive to unprotected Windows computers.
