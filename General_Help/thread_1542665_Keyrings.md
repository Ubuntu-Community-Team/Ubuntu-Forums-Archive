### Post by donato roque on 2010-08-02
Hi
Your login password (the one you give when you login) is different from the keyring password stored in Password and Encryption (Seahorse).  Gnome applications like Evolution, Empathy and Gwibber store your account information in a folder protected by your keyring password.  Very much like a master password.  

This is very useful if you have to let friends use your computer but don't want them opening your email or private messages.  

Otherwise you might find it more convenient to just have one password open both login and keyring.  You may modify the keyring by typing alt+f2, enter seahorse.  right-click on the folder and change your password.
