### Post by Dennis N on 2014-09-02
1) Grab the corners (easier than the edge) and drag to adjust the size.

2) Use the Window Menu and click the resize option. This will auto-grab the corner and let you resize without holding a mouse button. It is cancelled if you click a mouse button to release it.

### Post by amanchesterman on 2014-09-17
Did you try Dennis N's suggestion (2)? That's what I do, it's so simple and quick. Open your Terminal window (Xubuntu 14.04), left-click on the small downwards pointing arrow at the top left of the window (this is using the default Greybird theme), left-click on 'Resize' and move your cursor to where you want the new bottom right corner of the window to be: then left-click again to finish. Three clicks and it's done, takes about a second to do it -- no 'hunting and pecking' for pixels, no need for shortcuts. It works for me!

### Post by mcduck on 2014-09-17
Many window managers also have fairly simple shortcuts for moving & resizing windows, try *Alt+Right-click* anywhere inside the window to resize it...
