### Post by DuckHook on 2016-10-04
Welcome to the forums, harleybronco.

Though few things can be stated with absolute certainty when dealing with things as ornery as computers, it is almost certain that the problem is an HW one. I want to cover off just one thing: what virus did your sister catch? If a BIOS type (which is very rare), then it is remotely possible that it would affect multiple OSes. Otherwise, given the fact that three very different OSes across two different media produce the same effects, then the high probability is HW failure.

Another possibility is a wonky BIOS setting, but this usually won't give you intermittent issues. With BIOS, it's usually all one or the other. However, it is worthwhile to check the BIOS settings.

If laptop is still under warranty, she should get Toshiba to fix the touchpad. If past warranty, then perhaps an eternal USB or bluetooth trackpad?

### Post by DuckHook on 2016-10-04
Before you waste further time communicating long-distance:

It is possible that your sister had no virus at all. Instead, what she may have been experiencing was failing hardware. A bad MOBO or PSU might look like a virus to a non-technical general user. Did she actually find malware from a virus scan? It is possible (even probable) that she just jumped to an unjustified conclusion. I find it far too coincidental that the suspicion of malware popped up at the same time as subsequent identical problems on three different OSes, one of which was run from a USB stick and so, bypassed the HDD. Further, if the problem has expanded into external input devices and not just the trackpad, then it is almost certainly HW.

You can try repairing the thing, but if the problem is the MOBO, then it just isn't worth putting any time or money into it. Googling shows that the system is probably well past warranty&#8213;unless she bought some form of after-market extended warranty, in which case, she can try going that route.

Sorry for the bad news, but I don't think any further fiddling with any OS is going to fix this. It may simply be time for a new laptop.
