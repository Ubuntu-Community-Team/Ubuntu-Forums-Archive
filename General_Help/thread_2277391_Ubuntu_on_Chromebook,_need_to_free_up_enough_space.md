### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-05-08
Every Linux distribution comes with default applications. They all take up disk space. Ubuntu usually needs about 6 - 7 GB of space. So, start deleting/removing applications that you do not use and never will use.

If you do decide to remove Unity do not do it until after you have installed an alternative desktop otherwise you will not have a user interface. And be careful about removing components of Unity. You may find that ubuntu-desktop gets removed at the same time and if that happens you will not have a user interface.
