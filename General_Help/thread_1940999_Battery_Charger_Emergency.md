### Post by JKyleOKC on 2012-03-14
You did understand correctly. The 3.5 amp rating is the maximum that the device can provide; it actually will only supply as much as the laptop is willing to take.

Since the original unit was rated at only 2.5 amp, the laptop probably will not take any more than that much, and the result will be that the replacement power unit will run a bit cooler than the original!

The critical items here are the voltage, and whether the device provides AC or DC output. Some laptops take low-voltage AC from the power pack and convert it to DC inside the computer, and others require that the power pack do the conversion and give them DC. If the new one doesn't match the old one in this respect, it COULD cause damage to the computer itself, not just to the battery. If they do match, however, then no problem.

The half-volt difference in output voltage should not be at all significant. Computers themselves work at lower voltages internally, and the internal power circuits will maintain the correct voltage levels so long as the input voltage is high enough to let them do so.
