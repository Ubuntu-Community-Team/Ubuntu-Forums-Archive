### Post by Dr Small on 2008-10-21
To answer your first question:
The differences between GNOME and KDE is that they both use different code, libraries, engines and look different. KDE looks more like a Windows setup, whereas GNOME is elegant and simple, yet bloated.

And your second question:
Ubuntu is the main distro. The rest are derivatives. For example, Kubuntu use KDE, Xubuntu uses XFCE, Edubuntu was made for schools and classrooms, etc. There are a load of *buntu distros. Not all are officially supported by Canonical, but people create them to fit their specific needs out of the box.

Third question:
Not many Windows users ever venture beyond 'the desktop' of Windows. So they never hear of Linux, and if they do, it was from some ex-Linux user who didn't know what he was doing, couldn't get it to work, and then leaves negative comments about Linux. So naturally, Windows users think "oh, Linux won't work with my computer then" or "it won't run my software".
