### Post by flipper9 on 2009-10-29
> **kemorc said:**
> I've been trying to watch videos, best example are all here: [http://www.nascar.com/video/](http://www.nascar.com/video/)

Try to watch one, adjust the volume, go to full screen or whatever.  For some reason, I'm not able to.  I happened to pick Adobe flash, appears to have been a BIG mistake.

You installed the 32-bit version of Flash, but are using a 64-bit version of the operating system.  There is a known bug with running the 32-bit version of flash on 64-bit Ubuntu, with all of the hacks that are done behind the scenes to make it work.  The alternative is to install the alpha-version of the Adobe Flash plugin that is natively 64-bit.

See: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1259102](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1259102)

There is also a work-around with the 32-bit flash plugin...just right-click to bring up the flash menu, then click off the menu ONCE with the left-click, then you can click something in the flash just once.  Repeat to click again.  Sucks, but that's the way it is right now until Adobe releases the official 64-bit release instead of an alpha.
