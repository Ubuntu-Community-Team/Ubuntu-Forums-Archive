### Post by kpfuser on 2017-10-05
Although it is rather unusual for one to answer his own questions, I am going to do it here for the sake of completeness since in the time following my previous post I was able to discover the answer to my own question i.e., how to type characters appearing at the right (upper or lower right) of any key in a given keyboard layout. To begin with, one must have a picture of the keyboard layout corresponding to a language one has added to the default language of the original installation. This picture can be obtained by clicking on the default language icon at the top of the right-hand-side of the screen (En in my case). This will produce a drop-down menu listing among others additional languages added (Gr in my case). Click on the additional language to switch to it. Then click the last entry of the drop-down menu i.e., Text Entry Settings (see screenshot attachment in earlier post by ajgreeny). In the Text Entry window that opens select  the language that was added (Gr for me) and then click on the keyboard icon that is located at the bottom of the field marked as "input sources to use." This opens a picture of the keyboard layout for the respective added language. As mentioned earlier, up to four characters can be typed using any keyboard key and the question becomes what keystrokes must be employed to type a given character. For characters appearing on the left-hand side of a given key, the answer is the familiar one. For the character at the lower-left corner of a given key, just strike that key. For the character at the upper-left corner of the key use Shift+Strike Key. For characters appearing at the right-hand side of a key the required action is a bit lengthier. More specifically, for characters at the lower-right corner of the key use Right+Alt+Strike Key. For characters at the upper right corner of a key the proper sequence is Right+Alt+Shift+Strike Key necessitating either an additional finger or two in one's hands or at least longer fingers if not an additional hand. In the latter two cases, "Right" means striking (and holding) the key marked with "-->" which is located at the bottom centrer-right area of the standard US English keyboard.
