### Post by hanzj on 2010-02-13
Hello,

I turn on my computer most times with the intention of doing work, but somehow get carried away with  time-wasting activities.  What I would like to do is keep track of how my time is spent on the computer and what I do on the computer. 

**What do I want to have recorded?**
I hope to have a record which would answer these questions:

Which application has the focus? Firefox? VLC?
 If Firefox, which domain/website/URL am I on (or which domain/website/url does the current tab in the browser on)? 
How long was I there?  If I was on timewaster.com/abc.html for 5 minutes and timewaster.com/xyz.html for 10 minutes, then I don't mind having a daily report generated that adds up the total time spent on a domain (e.g., timewaster.com = 15 minutes on Feb 3, 2010).
What time was I on timewaster.com? E.g., 9:30 am - 9:45 am.


Also, because I have a dual monitor setup, it is possible that I have a browser window open on either monitor. One browser window wouldn't have focus, but I still would like to somehow have recorded that when I have 2 split/open browser-windows, both windows (or the front/open tabs of both browser windows) get noted/recorded. 

Also, maybe a screenshot every 30 minutes just to get a "quick glance" at how I'm making use of my day.


**Why do I want to have this record?**
What I hope to accomplish is that by reviewing the daily report daily, or the weekly report  or the monthly report, I would see HOW MUCH time I've wasted. I think I'm more likely to waste time if my mind thinks "Oh, it's just a 5 minute "break". But if I see how that little 5-minute break here and that 10 minute break there all add up, I'll be more likely to firm up in my resolution to be productive.


**How would I like my report to look like?**
As to how I'd like my report, I think a simple spreadsheet would be fine. But if I could have a spreadsheet plus maybe some pies/graphs that put the data into pictures, that would be good, too.

As for the screenshots/snapshots of my monitors, I don't mind them just going to a folder as is (jpg). 

Thank you so much!


**Some responses to questions/comments that may be posed to me.**

[LIST]
[*] We are already using some content filtering (opendns; /etc/hosts).
[*] Leechblock (firefox plugin) is nice, but though I use firefox usually, I may be tempted to use another browser not just to bypass leechblock, but because chrome/chromium loads up faster than firefox.
[/LIST]
