### Post by kaldor on 2008-11-10
I always had a stable, perfect Ubuntu installation. Never crashed. I am still on Hardy, I did not upgrade to Intrepid.

Around the beginning of October, I upgraded the kernel in the update manager. Ever since then, Ubuntu has been the most unstable and untrustworthy system I have ever used.

1) Apps randomly freeze: Sometimes I could be on firefox, and then suddenly out of nowhere, it closes. I cannot open firefox again without logging back in.

2) Random crashes: Sometimes the entire system freezes up. I could be just browsing my home folder and suddenly I would need to do a hard reboot because everything would freeze. Not even ctrl alt f1 to bring up the CLI will work. (What happened to UNIX/Linux's amazing uptimes? I have not been able to keep Ubuntu going for more than 2 hours without needing to reboot or relogin..)

3) Words jumble up: Sometimes the text/words will jumble up. If I scroll through my home folder/through a web page the words will stay in place and pile up into a huge mess.

4) Sound: If I go to a site that uses Java, I cannot hear sound until I reboot.

5) Random clock changes: Rare, but sometimes the clock is completely wrong and I need to change the time.



I have been using Ubuntu since 7.10, and have no plans to go back to Windows. But right now, Ubuntu is looking rather bleak for me. I have waited since October to see if it would sort out. This gets very annoying, especially when talking to someone or using OpenOffice.

 If I cannot get these problems fixed, could someone recommend a more stable distro of Linux?

### Post by shane2peru on 2008-11-10
I just went through something similar, and I've got you beat, I have used Ubuntu since Breezy days. :)  I too got very frustrated, and started "shopping around"  After all is said and done, a fresh install of Ubuntu solved all my problems.  I don't know what to tell you, other than take the time to do a clean fresh new install.  I know this is frustrating, but it sure beats the viruses of Windows.  I keep all my data on a separate partition called /home and upgrading, or re-installing is a snap, and takes very little time.  Also when I reinstall, my desktop doesn't change, because I use the same home account etc. (This too can have disadvantages)  If you really like Hardy, re-install.  If you are just using Hardy because you don't want to upgrade, then install Intrepid.  In all reality it takes about an hour, and then get all everything selected to install and let her run through the night downloading and installing all the apps that you use and work with.  Just my 2 cents worth.  I just came from checking out the other distros, the reason I always come back to Ubuntu, is it has all the apps I need, and while it isn't perfect (neither is anything else) it is still the best thing out there for me.  The grass isn't any greener on the other side of the fence.

Shane
