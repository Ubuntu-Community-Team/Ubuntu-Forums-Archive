Perhaps I'm being daft, but I can't figure out the significance of the default colour scheme in the terminal.  What does the blue, magenta, green colouring of the text indicate?

---

### Post by po0f on 2007-01-09
firebadger,

Blue indicates a directory.  Red indicates an archive file (tarball, zip file).  Magenta usually indicates a picture file or a 'socket' file.  Green indicates an executable.  Cyan for symlinks, yellow for pipes.  Red background with white text either indicates a broken symlink or a setuid program, I don't rightly recall.  There are others, I could only remember these off the top of my head.
