### Post by softtower on 2008-11-14
After installing Ubuntu 8.10 from scratch, Gnome no longer controls the volume: when I press "volume up" or "volume down" keys (they're customizable via "System/Preferences/Keyboard") I see Gnome's big sliding bar on screen, going left/right, but the volume does not cahnge.

But when I adjust it via volume applet on the top bar - it works. 
Any help would be appreciated.

### Post by hazysonic on 2009-05-07
had the same problem but the fix turned out to be easy. System>Control Center>Sound... On the "devices" tab, change "default mixer tracks" to your sound card's master output. Mine defaulted to control the input for some reason.
