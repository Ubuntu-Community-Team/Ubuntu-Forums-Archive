### Post by The Thunder Chimp on 2010-08-21
This article I wrote back in April should give you a few explanations on why it's basically not possible anymore (unless you haven't updated your PS3 since March 2010). Check it out [here](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1447304).

I've installed Ubuntu on PS3 once (on a Standard Definition TV), and it was both slow, and it was impossible to click on the "OK" button in messages because they were out of range (weren't on the screen). On my HD TV, I've had problems with some flickering and with the screen being black on boot.

What you used to do was to enable a feature from the settings menu on your PS3, install a boot loader (usually kBoot or Petit Boot), slip in a [PSUbuntu](http://psubuntu.com/) Disc (which is actually a PowerPC architecture (PowerPCs include some Mac computers and PS3s) instead of 32-bit or 64-bit), launch the Bootloader from the PS3 menu (again in Settings), select boot from disc from the Petit Boot (from the command line in kBoot), and finally follow Ubiquity, Ubuntu's installer.

The loss of Linux on the PS3 sucks, but it isn't such a big loss for normal users like you and me (as compared to the US army who used hundreds of clustered PS3s running Linux), because it wasn't really great in the first place.

Once again that is only by using PSUbuntu. I have heard about (and unsuccesfully tried) [Yellow Dog Linux](http://www.yellowdoglinux.com/). I couldn't even get the visual part of the software to work (commands such as "poweroff" still turned off the PS3).

You might want to take a quick look at the [wikipedia article](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OtherOS) that explains this issue in greater detail.

Hope this helps!
