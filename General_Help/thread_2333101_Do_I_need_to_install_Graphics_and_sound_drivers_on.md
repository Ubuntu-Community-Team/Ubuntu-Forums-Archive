### Post by deadflowr on 2016-08-07
Answer is maybe.
Depends on hardware.
More likely a probable for graphics than for sound.
It's very rare you would need sound drivers beyond those already installed.

But if the system works out of the box then you probably don'y need to do anything about drivers.
Linux has builtin drivers that support a wide variety of hardware.

But if you feel performance lacks then you might look into Ubuntu driver section in the program **Software and Updates >> Additional Drivers**.
If there are any known drivers that are not part of the default install available for your current hardware using your current system.
They will be listed and installable from here.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-08-07
Welcome. As above. Is anything not working? If so, you'll may a driver. If not, you probably don't. If you do, don't go looking in the normal Windows places.

Ubuntu comes with most of the drivers you'll need, but third-party drivers, if required, can be found in Additional Drivers, as mentioned, the official Ubuntu repositories, a community maintained PPA, or your problem might be fixed with a bit of tweaking help from the community.

Are your sound and video working as expected? If so, I would advise 'enjoy!'. :)
