### Post by KingGrant on 2008-02-13
I am writing a long document in Open Office. I use two different computers, one with Windows XP and one with Ubuntu. The document is swapped from one computer to the other, depending on where it's convinient for me to write. 

When I printed my first draft, I realized that there are two different styles of quotation marks and apostrophes throughout the document. It's kind of distracting.

My XP computer displays the straight marks you would see from an old typewriter, [SIZE="5"]"Like This"[/SIZE]. This is what I prefer.

But my Ubuntu computer's marks are curved. [SIZE="5"]“Like this.”[/SIZE]

The two styles are mixed up within the same document. I don't like it. 

Does this have anything to do with the operating system? 

I tried to Find & Replace all the quotation marks (with quotation marks) on one computer, hoping it would make them uniform. It didn't work. If anyone has a simple fix I could apply after I'm done writing the document to make all the marks uniform, I can live with the differing marks for now.

### Post by trsdndkc on 2008-02-13
When you did find and rep**l**ace, d**i**d you u**s**e &#8220; and &#8221; for **t**he s**e**arch, but " for the replaceme**n**t? I only ask because these characters all have different ascii codes.

EDIT: I tried this in OpenOffice and it worked just fine. The trick is that when you do search and replace, make sure to copy and paste the different quotation mark into the find field.

### Post by p_quarles on 2008-02-13
You can also turn the autocorrect feature for quote marks off. Under the "Tools" menu in Writer, you'll find the Autocorrect option. Open that up, switch to the Custom Quotes tab, and set it up how you like.
