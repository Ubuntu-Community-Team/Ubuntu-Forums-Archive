### Post by t0p on 2009-07-11
Which email service do you want to use through evolution?  Hotmail?  Your ISP?  Someone else?

Let's assume you want to use evolution to fetch your mail from hotmail and to use your hotmail account to send emails.  You need to find out what settings hotmail uses for POP/POP3 (receiving email) and SMTP (sending email).  If you go to the hotmail webpage and look up Settings, you will find the info you will need to get evolution working for you.

I don't know what hotmail's like (never used them) but when I started to use evolution with my gmail addresses, I had to go into Settings (on the gmail webpage) and click some boxes to activate the POP and SMTP services.  I guess you might need to do the same with hotmail...

If you want to use your ISP's email service through evolution, you'll need to call their customer services and ask for the POP and SMTP settings for your email client.  I'm sure they'll be happy to oblige.
