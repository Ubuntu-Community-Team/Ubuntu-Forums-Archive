### Post by cbecker78 on 2010-04-30
Just a warning: Toshiba uses insyde H20 and Phoenix to supply bios now... neither of these seem to cooperate with ubuntu/linix apci control and a lot of us were having problems with these BIOS and fan control.  It was a big pain to get the fan to work, and some folks were never able to resolve the issue for them.  Also, most of Toshibas Fn hot-keys do not yet work with these new BIOS models.  The fix for this works only on the older toshiba-made bios computers. 

So basically, although the Toshiba is a far superior computer imho, it may not be the best bet to run linux.  I personally wish I had not purchased a new toshiba with the sole intent of running linux on them.  However, dell may have some issues too.  A good idea might be to bring a live CD to best buy or wherever you are going to buy them, and try to boot up and see how they work. (Store staff might freak out if they see what you are doing though...)

Note that Dell has a site somewhere with a lot of prebuilt drivers and resuources for getting their machine to work with ubuntu/linux.  they also offer linux tech support to some degree.  On the other hand, Toshiba will tell you to go bugger off if they get even the hint that you have installed an OS other than the one that came with the PC.
