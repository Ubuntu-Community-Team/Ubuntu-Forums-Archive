### Post by Mark Phelps on 2013-05-21
There historically have been two "installers" for Ubuntu.  The one most folks see and use has a graphical interface, and in some cases, that uses so much resources that there are problems (like in yours).  The "alternate" installer is a text-mode installer that does not have such a fancy interface, and as a result, uses less resources.  You get it from the Ubuntu website.

However ... if your laptop is running so hot that you can't use the regular installer, then it's most likely going to overheat when you try to use Ubuntu later.
