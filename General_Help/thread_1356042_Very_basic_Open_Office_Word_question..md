### Post by coffeecat on 2009-12-15
I don't know about excluding footnotes, but just a FYI. You're going to get different word counts in MS Word and Open Office whatever you do. MS Word counts hyphenated words separately. OOo counts hyphenated combinations such as 'pedal-cycle', up-to-date' and 'devil-may-care' as one each.

I found this out doing a word count in a 200,000+ document in both word-processors and wondered why I got such discrepant counts. Then I tried with Abiword and got yet another count.

**Disclaimer**: if the behaviour in Word is different now, I wouldn't know about it. This was with Word 95. I've not bought a copy of MS Office since the mid nineties, **and neither do I intend to**. :wink:
