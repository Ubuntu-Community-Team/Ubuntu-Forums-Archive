### Post by kebes on 2007-01-08
Best thing to do is check the compatibility list:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters)

Also good thing to do is check the vendor's webpage and see if they provide Linux drivers. I was quite amazed to find that a Xerox Phaser (at work) had fully-functional Linux drivers. A snap to setup.

I've also had good results with HP laserjets and I have an Epson at home that works, too. A Canon i850 that I used for awhile was only partially functional.

You should check out this previous similar thread where people offered opinions on printers that worked for them in the past:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=236907](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=236907)
