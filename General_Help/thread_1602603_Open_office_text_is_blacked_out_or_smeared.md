### Post by efflandt on 2010-10-21
Check your font sizes in System > Preferences > Appearance > Fonts.  Previously the default size for all fonts was 10, but default for the new "Ubuntu" fonts is 11 (others are still 10).  Maybe with an upgrade sizes did not change (or did you manually set a different size?).

Someone else had artifacts with different letters at different times when Ubuntu fonts were set to 10.  Not sure if it is related to certain video cards or drivers.

### Post by grashdur on 2012-02-15
I had this exact same problem just today, using 10.10 Meerkat. (It happened right after I removed the clock battery from my motherboard in order to go get a replacement at a store--but that may be pure coincidence.) 

I solved the problem by going to System > Preferences > Appearance > Visual Effects and then switching from "Normal" to "None." It must be some kind of a video card driver issue.
