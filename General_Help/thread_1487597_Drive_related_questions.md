### Post by exodus_ on 2010-05-19
Hey there!

Ubuntu does not use drive letters like windows does!  It might take some getting used to for you, but everything in Ubuntu is found under /

So for example, instead of D: for your cd-rom it might be /media/cdrom or even /media/name_of_disc depending on your settings.  I was confused at first too, when I switched to Slackware from windows 5 years ago.  Once I got used to it, I liked it a lot better than what I consider to be silly drive letters.

Look under the Places menu, and you will find a "my computer" alternate.  Your Home folder is virtually identical to "My Documents" so thats where all your "stuff" will go.

[Here is some handy info]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwitchingToUbuntu/FromWindows") for switching from Windows.  Give that a read and I hope it can help you!

Welcome to Ubuntu! I hope you love it as much as I do! :D
