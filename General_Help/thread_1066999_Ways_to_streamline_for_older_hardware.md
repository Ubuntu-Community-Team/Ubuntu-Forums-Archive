### Post by snowpine on 2009-02-11
Hi Panicked Thumb, I just googled your laptop specs... I have a Dell Latitude Cpx, which makes your D410 look like a racehorse. :)

I have tried many Ubuntu derivatives on this machine. By far the fastest were Fluxbuntu (fluxbox) and Crunchbang (openbox). Based on this experiment, I definitely think a switch to one of the *boxes would be beneficial for you. An even bigger factor is which applications you choose. If you can live without Firefox, Nautilus, etc. you will find there are lighter alternatives. Fluxbuntu, for example, uses Kazehakase browser, Rox file manager, Leafpad editor, Xmms music player, etc.

I will not suggest any non-Ubuntu alternatives since you specifically said you'd like to stick with Ubuntu. I personally have moved on with this particular Dell laptop, but that is off-topic... :)

### Post by snowpine on 2009-02-11
Well since you asked :)
It is a dual boot (on a 6gb hard drive!): 
1. Sidux LXDE with autologin enabled. I haven't done a dist-upgrade, so it's stuck at 2.6.27, which is stable on this hardware. This computer sits in the living room by the TV; my girlfriend and I use it for casual websurfing (kazehakase), music listening (rhapsody.com), uploading photos from the camera (f-spot), etc. Performance is impressive for a Debian-based distro. LXDE is a very nice transition to the world of lightweight windows managers (think of it as "openbox with training wheels"). My g.f. is not a linux expert; she has no idea she's using "Debian unstable with the lightweight x desktop environment;" she thinks it's just another flavor of Ubuntu! Link: [http://www.cap.gediam.de/index-en.htm](http://www.cap.gediam.de/index-en.htm) (if you want an "official" flavor of sidux, try the Xfce version, also very nice: [http://sidux.com/](http://sidux.com/))
2. SliTaz, for me when I'm feeling geeky. Hands-down the fastest distro I've tried on this hardware. Not Debian-based, very different from Ubuntu. Also uses LXDE.
