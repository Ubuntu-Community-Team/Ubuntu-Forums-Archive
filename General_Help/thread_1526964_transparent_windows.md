### Post by WorMzy on 2010-07-08
You can't. At least not in the GNOME environment (standard for Ubuntu). I'm not sure about KDE, or the other Desktop Environments. The best you can do (as far as I know) in GNOME is make the whole thing transparent with the Compiz settings you mentioned.

Edit: Some apps have such a feature built in, like Gnome-Terminal, and gnome-panel (although Gnome-panel only supports "fake" transparency, where it just copies the background image onto itself)
