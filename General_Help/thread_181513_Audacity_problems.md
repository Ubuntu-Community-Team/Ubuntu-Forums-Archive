### Post by Decade on 2006-11-15
Audacity 1.3 also makes that bouncing bottle effect, even at its minimum noise removal setting. In addition, it lets you put the slider between the minimum and 0, in which case it silently doesn't remove any noise.

Basically, you select a section that's supposed to be silent, get noise profile, select the entire record, and remove noise. I've decided to standardize on minimum noise removal, as that dramatically reduces the noise, and is the setting with the least of the bottle effect. The trick is to find a truly silent patch, and not have breathing or paper shuffling or such in the noise profile.

I think the noise removal is most effective with electronic noise, as in the colored noises or hums, and least effective with clangy noises, like the air conditioner in the roof above the very quiet talkers that I have to record.
