Get a piece of copper wire, strip back the insulation about 20cm, wrap it round your wrist.
connect the other end to a piece of copper pipe, central heating, radiator, or such like, that is the cheapest way.
otherwise buy an antistatic matt and wristband from an electronic suppliers. (maplin, RS, etc.etc)

### Post by mbailey on 2008-12-27
Leave the power plugged in, switch the power supply off, touch the power supply. Keep touching metal parts of the case as you work. Don't work wearing synthetic shoes, don't stand on synthetic carpet.

---

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2008-12-27
As in the previous post, make sure you make solid contact with the case before diving in. Just grab onto it for a few seconds. Try to avoid touching exposed electronics (ram etc) and gentle and steady as you go. You are normally making some contact with the case as you work so make sure you do and should be fine. I have never used any thing else and I've built and been elbow deep in them plenty. :)
