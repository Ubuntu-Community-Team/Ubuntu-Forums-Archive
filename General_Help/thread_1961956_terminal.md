### Post by Lars Noodén on 2012-04-21
It will also help if you don't think of them as commands, rather that what you are doing is shell script programming.  The scripts you write will be mostly one line ;)  at least at the beginning.  

Think also of the programs as functions to be strung together rather than as "commands"  That way you'll approach it from a just-in-time perspective rather than trying to "learn all the commands".  Learning to use the [manual pages](http://manpages.ubuntu.com/) via [man](http://manpages.ubuntu.com/manpages/oneiric/en/man1/man.1.html) is the best step you can take in that direction.  That way you'll be better able to look up how to do what you need to do.
