### Post by Tony_Schountz on 2022-04-13
I just installed 20.04 on a newly assembled computer with an Intel i9-12600k that has UHD 770 graphics only. However, the only available resolution is 1024x768. I am relatively new to Ubuntu so I am not sure if I need to install a driver for the UHD 770, and if so what the precise process is for doing so. 

I am also unable to get to my BIOS screen to check the video settings. I suspect the Gigabyte board came with Fast Boot enabled and I did not check it before installing Ubuntu. I have the battery out and will leave it out and unplugged overnight to see if I can get to the BIOS in the morning.

TIA.

---

### Post by Tony_Schountz on 2022-04-14
So, it turns out that I had to update to kernel 5.17.3, which has support for the UHD 770 (Alder Lake). My issues are now resolved.
