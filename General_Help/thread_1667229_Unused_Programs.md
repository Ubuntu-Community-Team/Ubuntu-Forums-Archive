### Post by Rayston on 2011-01-14
Is there any way to have Ubuntu list all my unused or rarely used programs in a way that would make it easy for me to uninstall them? I have a bad habit of downloading stuff that looks neat or temporarily useful and after awhile I have filled my hard drive with rarely or never used programs.

---

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-01-14
One easy way is to open your Synaptic Package Manager. After it opens on the left column "left click" on the "status button". Then from the list right above the buttons, "left click" one time on "installed". In the right side top window a listing of installed apps will appear.

When you find an app on that list you wish to uninstall, "right click" on it and select "Mark for Complete Removal". Then continue going down the list holding the control key down and right clicking (selecting Mark for complete removal) on each app you wish to uninstall. When done - from the toolbar select "Apply".

This will uninstall all the apps you selected for complete removal.

Good Luck & Hope This Helps.
