### Post by cazort on 2008-06-09
Unfortunately, Kexi and OO-database are the best ones out there.  You may want to try OpenOffice-2.4, if you have used previous ones and been unsatisfied.  It's not quite to where Access is, but it's much better than previous versions.

Also, OpenOffice is already FAR superior to Access when it comes to integrating natively with other database back-ends (MySQL, etc.).  Access can only handle them through ODBC, which presents all sorts of problems (truncations and modifications of various types, problems working over a secure connection, all sorts of other limitations).

Both OpenOffice and Kexi have seen a huge amount of development recently so keep checking back...if they don't work for you yet, you may find they work for you soon.  :-)
