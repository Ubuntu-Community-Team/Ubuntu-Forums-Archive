### Post by Zoot7 on 2009-11-06
Sorry to hear the upgrade has botched everything for you. I found out the Hard way myself, Hardy to Intrepid was a disaster for me - it broke everything.

But as been said time and time again, a clean install is always the better option. 
Stick with what works for you. Running off upgrading an OS a few days after it's release is asking for trouble; look at the Windows 7 problems now, reboots galore, or the Snow Leopard bugs. It's true of every OS, you're far better off you stick away from it until it's had time to mature.
Besides there's no reason to upgrade Jaunty at the moment if it's working for you; it's maintained with updates for another year or so is it not?

I've Hardy on my home built desktop for that reason exactly, I'm not home all that often, and when I am home it's not for long really. I just want my PC to work when I turn it on; don't have time for breakage on that.
Stick with LTS's if you want rock solid stability.
