### Post by guiverc on 2022-12-31
You are aware Lubuntu 18.04 LTS is ***end of life***.

Refer [https://lubuntu.me/bionic-eol/](https://lubuntu.me/bionic-eol/)

It was also the *end of the road* with regards *release-upgrade* options too, as it was the last release that used the LXDE desktop.  ie. this [warning]("https://lubuntu.me/focal-2-released/") followed it

> ***Note***, due to the extensive changes required  for the shift in desktop environments, the Lubuntu team does not support  upgrading from 18.04 or below to any greater release. Doing so will  result in a broken system. If you are on 18.04 or below and would like  to upgrade, please do a fresh install.

If your machine is *i386* (or x86 32-bit), that release was definitely the *end of the road* with Ubuntu, as whilst later releases (18.10 & 19.04) provided *i386* support/upgrade for the life of those releases, they were not LTS releases thus support ended a long time ago.

[Ubuntu 18.04 LTS]("https://fridge.ubuntu.com/2021/09/17/ubuntu-18-04-6-lts-released/") still receives *security fixes & patches*, as five years applies to Ubuntu Desktop, Ubuntu Server, Ubuntu Cloud, and Ubuntu Base (*the three years for flavors ended in April 2021*).

FYI:  I can't read the non-english, but it looks like network issues to me.

### Post by Impavidus on 2023-01-01
The title of your post says ru.archive.ubuntu.com, which is the Russian mirror, but the body of your post says ua.archive.ubuntu.com, which is the Ukrainian mirror. Which one do you want to use? I wouldn't be surprised, given current circumstances, if either of them is blocked from the other country. Try finding a different mirror or switch to the main server.

Lubuntu 18.04 is end-of-life, so no more updates. Ubuntu 18.04 is still supported, so the repositories are still available and the parts Lubuntu has in common with Ubuntu are still supported and receive regular updates.
