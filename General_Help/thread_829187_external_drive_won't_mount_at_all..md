### Post by cybrsaylr on 2008-06-14
I used to have the same problem on my dual boot system with XP & Ubuntu with my ext HDD, that has a Seagate drive.
It would not mount in linux after running XP.
When in XP or Vista you have to click on the 'safely remove hardware' button next to the clock when exiting Windows. Otherwise that ext HDD won't mount when you boot up in linux. On Windows this doesn't matter but on linux it's a problem if you don't shut it down right in Windows before going into linux.
