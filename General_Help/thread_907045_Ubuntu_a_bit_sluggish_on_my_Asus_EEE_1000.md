### Post by jeremysan on 2008-09-01
Hi all.  I recently bought an Asus EEE 1000 laptop, the one with a 40 GB solid state disk (SSD).
Because I was bored and didn't like the operating system that it came with, I installed Ubuntu on one of the partitions of the SSD.  I've been running Ubuntu now for the last few days but I've noticed it has been running rather slowly, just kinda laggy and bogged down.  
An example of what I mean:  After starting the computer and letting everything load, I will launch Firefox and have to wait nearly 10 seconds before I can start web browsing.  Even navigating through different web pages yields poor load times and performance.  In almost any program in Ubuntu, any drop down menu (such as File, Edit, View, etc)  it takes maybe two or three seconds sometimes just to show the menu!  I mean a quick and snappy operating system should make everything go bang bang, quickly, but everything loads and performs so poorly!!!  This reminds me of the days when I was on my Pentium III laptop (which was quite weak).
I specifically bought the EEE which had the SSD, as opposed to the hard disk because I thought that the SSD would be quicker / snappier.  I am not, however, finding this to be the case.  

Perhaps I am just way too picky?  I'm simply finding the performance not up to my wants.  Is there something I am missing?  Are SSDs no good?  Is there some other hardware problem with this computer?  Is it a driver problem?  Any advice would be great!!!
