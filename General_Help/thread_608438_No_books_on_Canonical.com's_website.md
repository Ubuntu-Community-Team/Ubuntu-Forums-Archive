### Post by ofb on 2007-11-10
At a guess, probably because it's more trouble than it's worth.

The physical merchandise listed is advertising swag - it doesn't have regular distribution channels like books. If Canonical didn't make it available directly, it wouldn't be available.

Books have regular channels. It's much more practical for Canonical to use those, and put their limited effort & office space towards what they /must/ do.

No doubt they had a discussion about this at some point and floated a number of reasons, but I expect that will be the key one.

What /is/ interesting is the book doesn't seem to have an official website, or even a proper page at the publisher. There may be more subtle reasons for that, and if you do a search for 'ubuntu book' it doesn't seem to have hurt at all.
