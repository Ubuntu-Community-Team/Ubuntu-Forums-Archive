### Post by Topsiho on 2013-08-29
Be sure the cpu of your laptop supports pae, otherwise you'll not be able to put the newer Ubuntu's on it.

I have an (older, 2005) Toshiba laptop which has a non-pae processor, and I had to quit using Ubuntu on it (use Xubuntu now).
If you choose a laptop which is several years old, please be aware of this issue :)

pae is physical address extension, with which **32-bits** processors can support more than 3 or 4 GB of RAM, which they can not without this pae option.
