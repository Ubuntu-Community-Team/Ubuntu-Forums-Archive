### Post by PhilGil on 2010-08-01
The obvious choice would be Debian.  I found the initial setup a bit more challenging than Ubuntu, but you can leverage a lot of the knowledge you already have.  If your machine isn't brand new, Debian Stable would be very low-maintenance, but you'd have to put up with older versions of software (or try to find up to date versions in backports).  Debian Testing is reasonably stable (some would say more stable than a typical Ubuntu release) and has newer packages, but you won't be completely immune from breakage.  System-borking breakage is very rare, though.

Another option might be to try a newer kernel.  Due to regressions, 2.6.32 didn't play nice with any of my 3 computers.  An upgrade to 2.6.34 on my home box and my netbook has worked wonders (finally got 2.6.32 working on the 3rd box).
