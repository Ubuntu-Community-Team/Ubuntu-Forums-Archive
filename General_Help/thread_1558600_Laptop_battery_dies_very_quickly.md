### Post by dougalkerr on 2010-08-22
Depending upon the age of your battery may mean it is time to get a new one.
Have you tried letting it die altogether and then recharging it?
Batteries used to suffer from a memory effect that meant that when it came time for the recharge they would only charge to a percentage of their full capacity and this got less with every charge.
The trick was to drain the battery completely and then charge it. The battery would then have a full charge because it was completely drained.

Newer batteries don't have this problem though so, depending on the age, it is either old, duff or you have a charger problem. The only other possibility I could see is if there is a problem in the computer which is draining your battery quickly.
This would normally show itself as something getting hot compared to not having been hot in the past or something will blow as it is draining too much power for some reason.

If the computer runs fine on mains power alone (battery removed) then I would suspect the battery.

Have a good look around before you buy one as they can be expensive. Try the likes of CPC in the UK as they often have compatible batteries for several popular makes and models.
