### Post by hardyn on 2008-07-28
energy savings and hardware saving are two different things.

from what i understand is things hard disks have a estimated number of spin-ups / spin downs... and while powering them down will save energy you are actually contributing to the ultimate failure of the device.  that keeping the disks spinning, while will wear the bearings is actually easier on the device than an aggressive power saving policy.

personally on AC, i have the disks keep spinning. and power them down after 5min while on battery, to save battery.  however you have to careful when picking the spin down number, too aggressive will case excessive power cycling, while too long with will do the same.  things like physical ram will also contribute significantly to the amount the machine will have access the drive.

as far as performance... all on on the time.  otherwise you have to deal with the spin-up spin-down latency.

for things that don't have moving parts keeping them within operating temperature and not overclocking etc, will maximize longevity.  however practically you will replace the computer before things really have a chance to degrade.  if the component makes it past the warranty period, it will probably last for a long time.
