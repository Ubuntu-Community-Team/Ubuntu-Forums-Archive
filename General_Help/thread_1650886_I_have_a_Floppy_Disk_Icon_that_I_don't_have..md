### Post by timgood on 2010-12-22
Most computers have a 'legacy' floppy disk drive enabled in the BIOS. Removing this from your BIOS will remove the drive. BIOS settings vary according to manufacturer, but normally setting the FDD to 'not installed' will solve this. 

You can enter your BIOS screen when your computer boots up by pressing a key combination (this also varies from maker to maker) but most desktops use the delete key. Google is your friend.

You can leave it as it is if it doesn't bother you, and it won't affect your computer.

Hope this helps.
