### Post by JohnPhys on 2007-05-05
If I'm correct, the reason is that they just don't.  They like to have a feature/package freeze when developing, to make sure that the distro is as stable as possible when they release.  That way they don't have to support many different versions of the same packages at the same time.

I'm pretty sure there's nothing preventing you from grabbing the .tar and compiling your own pidgin.  Someone may even put it in the backports repository, but it would think it will certainly show up in the gutsy gibbon, due out in October.

I should mention that I have no official affiliation with Ubuntu or Canonical, this is just from what I remember when reading around the forums.
