### Post by grahammechanical on 2013-07-05
> [COLOR=#000000]Why isn't ubuntu tablet available for download somewhere for me to at least try and install it on the thing.[/COLOR]

It is still under development. October is the target date to have Ubuntu Touch devices on the market. You should not think that Ubuntu touch will be distributed in the same way that Ubuntu is distributed today. Do not think that there will be an ISO image that will work on every device on the market. That will be next to impossible to achieve.

[http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/06/01/ubuntu-phone-dogfooding-update/](http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/06/01/ubuntu-phone-dogfooding-update/)

[http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/07/05/dogfoodable-core-apps/](http://www.jonobacon.org/2013/07/05/dogfoodable-core-apps/)

But if you want to mess around and possible break your device, then look here

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Install](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Install)

[http://iso.qa.ubuntu.com/qatracker/milestones/270/builds](http://iso.qa.ubuntu.com/qatracker/milestones/270/builds)

Scroll down until you see Product (Ubuntu Touch) but notice those images are for selected Nexus devices.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Touch/Devices)

In the iso image list you will see something like Ubuntu Desktop armhf+omap4. They are images for ARM devices but certain specific ARM devices. So, be careful.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ARM/DeviceSupport](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ARM/DeviceSupport)

Regards.
