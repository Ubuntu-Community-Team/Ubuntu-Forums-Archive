Prtty much, and not just Limewire but other tracking services too.

By using a "John Doe" discovery motion they get a list if customers/IP's who've visited the site from a service provider and then file individule law suits assuming anyone using the tracker did so to "steal" copywritten material.

The suit is normally filed in a different state then the target and @ the last minute so there's very little time before the first court date. Then they offer to settle the suit for "only" $2000 or $3000  knowing few can afford a court fight.

Pretty much legal blackmail, they don't know what you may have downloaded or illegally redistributed. Unlike a crminal charge with "innocent till proven guilty" in a civil case _you_ have to convince the court that the accuser is wrong. RIAA lawyers probably have a higher income then 95% of the artists.

The American legal system allows for this and many other abuses in our civil courts that would have many cops and D.A.s' in jail for using the same methods in criminal prosection.

All the legal/moral issues of piracy vs. fair use and fair compensation for the artist are another issue, well disscused in many places and yet to be resolved.
