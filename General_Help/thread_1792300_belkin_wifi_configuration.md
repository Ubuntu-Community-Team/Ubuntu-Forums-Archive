### Post by amanchesterman on 2011-06-28
Wolfen69's advice is good: try resetting the router first.

If that doesn't work, the reason your browser can't access the configuration page may be because it can't access the network. In that case you need to check
a) can your computer detect the wireless network? (i.e. is the router transmitting a wireless signal and can your computer detect that)
b) is the router assigning a network address to your computer? (i.e. is the router allowing your computer to connect to the network)
c) if your network is protected with a password -- which it should be -- are you signing on with the correct password?

You should be able to check (b) by connecting your computer directly to the router with an ethernet cable. At least that way you can check that the router is running the network correctly (and you can access the router configuration page that way too). If that works OK, then you know that the problem lies with the wireless, not with the networking function.
