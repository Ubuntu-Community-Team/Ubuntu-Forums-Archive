### Post by jocko on 2011-04-03
I think the main reason you don't get any help is because your title is utter nonsense. If people don't understand the title, they generally don't think they can help.

What's a "front dispositive" and what's it got to do on a cpu?


A cpu (central processing unit, or processor) is the piece of hardware  in your computer that carries out all the instructions and calculations  from the software. It is composed of a little chip mounted on your  motherboard with a big heatsink and fan on top.
My guess is that you really mean to say "case", "chassis", "box",  "tower", "enclosure", "housing" or "cabinet" (= the box where your  motherboard with all it's attached hardware, hard drives, optical drives  and the power supply is mounted).

My guess from reading through the thread is that by "front dispositive" you mean you've got a front panel with USB ports and audio output/input connections on you computer case?


To solve your problem you need to supply more information. What motherboard do you have? "Black series" doesn't tell me anything. A manufacturer and model number would do.

If your computer shuts down when you connect something to the audio output something must be seriously wrong with how the audio output is connected to the motherboard. Get the motherboard manual and make sure every cable from the front panel is connected to the correct pin on the motherboard.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-04-03
You are correct. Output is sound coming out of the computer into headphones or speakers. Input is sound going into the computer from microphone or some other audio device.

Are you sure that these inputs and outputs are not muted (switched off)? Can you see a sound icon? Like a loudspeaker with sound waves coming out of it. Left click and select Sound Preferences. Look through the tabs and make sure the boxes labeled mute are not ticked and that the slider for output volume is moved off of the zero percent position.
