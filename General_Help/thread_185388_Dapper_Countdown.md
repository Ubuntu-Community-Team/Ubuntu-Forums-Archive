In practise, they are the same.
In theory, they're different.  GMT is a timezone like CET, it is location bound.  UTC is the international reference time to which all other zones are offset.

so, GMT is actually UTC+-00:00.

### Post by nocturn on 2006-06-01
To be real geeky:

"Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) is a high-precision atomic time standard which approximately tracks Universal Time (UT). It is the basis for legal civil time all over the Earth: time zones around the world are expressed as positive and negative offsets from UTC. In this role it is also referred to as Zulu time (Z), or using the historical term "Greenwich Mean Time" (GMT)."
