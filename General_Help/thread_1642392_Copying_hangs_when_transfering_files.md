### Post by kulverstukas on 2010-12-10
Hello. This is a problem that I am having and trying to solve for a while now. I searched everywhere and asked everyone - didn't get a solution, so I am asking here for help.
This problem is serious and really annoying - when I try to transfer files between flash drives, it starts to copy normally and then after few seconds it starts to lag. Transfer rate drops with each second by 0.2 MB down until 5 MB/s where it completely hangs. This happens to every single flash drive I put in and this is annoying because I have external HardDrive connected over USB port and I keep all my data in there, so when I try to transfer the files from the hard drive to the flash drive - it lags and hangs. Same thing if I want to copy from internal harddrive to flash drive. Although everything is fine when I copy from the flash drive to the internal harddrive and everything is fine when flash drive or the external harddrive is alone (no other flash drives or something is connected).

I need a solution to this. I am also using Ubuntu 10.04. I have tried transfering files on my netbook running ubuntu, because I thought that it might be a USB port issue but the same thing happened on the netbook too...

### Post by kulverstukas on 2010-12-13
Today I found a solution. It seems that I was using almost dead flash drive without me realizing it. And also I was using a USB port that seems to be faulty. Now when I put another 16 GB flash drive at the back everything was great - I was able to copy from and to the hard drive without a problem.
I understood that my flash is somewhat dieing by plugging it into a good USB port, that 16 GB flash drive was functioning and it diddn't work very well...
So anyone who has this problem - try other USB ports at the back of the box. If problem won't go away then maybe the flash drive is faulty.
