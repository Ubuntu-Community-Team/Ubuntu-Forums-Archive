### Post by earthpigg on 2010-08-09
Hi,

Apple's iPhone updates often intentionally break Ubuntu support.

Your best bet is probably to stop updating your iPhone, wait a few months and accept all Ubuntu updates, and hope for the best.

It's a cat-and-mouse game:

-Apple only wants the iPhone to work with iTunes.
-Open Source hackers want the iPhone to work with Open Source Software.

So, if you stop accepting Apple updates and continue to accept Ubuntu updates... you may get lucky.

Ideally, you would not have been accepting malicious Apple updates for the last 6 months, to give the hackers time to circumvent Apple's intentions. Had you not been accepting updates for the last several months, your iPhone would have worked perfectly with Ubuntu 10.04 out of the box. [Source 1]("http://www.webupd8.org/2010/02/confirmed-ubuntu-1004-supports-iphone.html"). [Source 2.]("http://www.geek.com/articles/news/ubuntu-10-04-sports-plug-and-play-support-for-the-iphone-and-ipod-touch-2010032/")

Maybe you could revert the iPhone to factory defaults and accept no updates from there?


And, to play devil's advocate: the box on your iPhone clearly states that Apple only wants the device to be used in conjunction with Steve Jobs-approved software. iTunes is on that list, and nothing else is. The God of Apple, Steve Jobs, does not approve of what you are trying to do.

have you looked into jailbreaking?

or Android phones? Google's Android works perfectly with Ubuntu, and their updates (unlike Apple's) aren't malicious and do ***not*** eradicate your personal Freedom to do what you please with a device that you purchased and (theoretically) own.
