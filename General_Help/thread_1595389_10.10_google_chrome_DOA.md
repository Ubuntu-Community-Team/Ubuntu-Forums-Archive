### Post by gradinaruvasile on 2010-10-13
Open a terminal and type:

ls -al /opt/google/chrome/google-chrome

Maybe a permission problem.

Should be something like:

-rwxr-xr-x 1 root root 1335 Oct 12 13:54 /opt/google/chrome/google-chrome

If it does not have the x-es, type:

sudo chmod +x /opt/google/chrome/google-chrome
sudo chmod a+x /opt/google/chrome/google-chrome
