### Post by QIII on 2018-06-27
I'm going to make a suggestion, even though I really don't like to tell people how to spend their money:  remove the old disk and replace it with a new one to install Ubuntu on.  That is the only sure-fire way to be sure you don't accidentally overwrite your important data if you make a mistake while installing Ubuntu -- particularly given the fact that you are new to Linux.  After you have Ubuntu installed on the new disk, a cheap USB3 disk enclosure will give you access to your Windows disk as you work on recovering your data.

You'll have to weigh for yourself the cost of a new disk against the risk of loss and the value of your data.
