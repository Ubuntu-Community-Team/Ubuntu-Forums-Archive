### Post by MorayJ on 2009-03-10
I second the respect for the withdrawal of the particular criticism - well done for fronting up.

The real problem for Linux not getting mainstream usage is simply because it hasn't got mainstream (desktop) usage.

I've used Linux exclusively for a few years and every time I have doubts about it due to getting problems (and I do get problems which are OS problems - I've passed the stage of thinking Linux is a miracle), little thoughts enter my mind - "I bet Windows is perfect now and there are no more problems; it's been a few years and it's probably faultless and here's me persisting in causing myself problems!"

Then a friend who is keen on online gaming persuaded me to buy a new PC and I thought I would future proof on the Windows side whilst I was there by buying a machine that had Vista on it :) The good news is, that put my mind at rest!

Anyway, Linux has created a choice - even to the point of forcing Microsoft to keep XP alive with its threat on netbooks. So, XPers who enjoyed continued support can thank Linux for choice.

I think that the mental state with Windows being familiar is that the public at large feel that if there is a problem someone can fix it, and that if there is a Linux problem it is an insurmountable and fatal flaw.
