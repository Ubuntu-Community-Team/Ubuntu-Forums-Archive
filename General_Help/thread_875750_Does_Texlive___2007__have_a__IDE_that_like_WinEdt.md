### Post by darthmob on 2008-07-31
there are a some plugins for gedit to interact with latex, but I'm using kile. it's quite powerful yet simple including things like word completion, syntax highlighting and a nice list of formulas and special characters (greek, mathematical, relations, operators etc.).

+1 for Kile, it is a great environment, although I personally prefer Emacs and AucTeX.

Aside: There seems to be quite a lot of confusion going around using KDE programs in Gnome and vice versa. As a general rule: Gnome programs work in KDE and the other way around. Why the confusion? Gnome and KDE programs can look little out of place in the "foreign" environment. There are ways to try to fix it and you can either ignore this, spend five minutes on this (e.g. install a Human theme for kde) or you can spend endless amounts of time on it to get it perfect. I am of the middle category.
