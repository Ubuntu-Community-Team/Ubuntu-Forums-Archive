### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2012-03-11
OP, it isn't clear to me whether you are talking about tabs and bookmarks in Firefox as the above posters seem to assume, or Nautilus or some other file browser.  Right now I'm in Oneiric with Mate, 32 bit, Firefox 10.0.2. To get another tab on Firefox I can either press a little + icon the the right of the tab or tabs I have open or press "Bookmarks" to get a dropdown of bookmarks and right click one of them and click "open in a new tab". There are some firefox preferences settings that affect exactly how this works. Most of them you find by clicking "Edit", "preferences". Some you change in the about:config page. To get to that type "about:config" in the address field and click the arrow or press enter, etc., etc.  Deleting a firefox bookmark couldn't be simpler - just right click on it and click "delete".  Now if you mean tabs and bookmarks in Nautilus, you have to set two things in the View, Sidebar menu - Places, rather than tree, and "show side bar". Then your bookmarks show up in the side bar. And deleting one of those bookmarks is just a matter of right clicking on it and clicking "remove" unless it is a folder Nautilus considers too special to be removed so easily and then it gets a wee bit more complicated and depends on the circumstances.
