### Post by krishna84 on 2010-11-03
A [program]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/P/program.html") that performs a very specific task, usually related to managing [system]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/S/system.html") [resources]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/R/resource.html"). [Operating systems]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/O/operating_system.html") contain a number of utilities for managing [disk drives]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/D/disk_drive.html"), [printers]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/P/printer.html"), and other [devices]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/D/device.html"). 
 Utilities differ from [applications]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/A/application.html") mostly in terms of size, complexity and function. For example, [word processors]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/W/word_processor.html"), [spreadsheet programs]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/S/spreadsheet.html"), and [database applications]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/D/database_management_system_DBMS.html")  are considered applications because they are large programs that  perform a variety of functions not directly related to managing [computer]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/C/computer.html") resources. 
 Utilities are sometimes installed as [*memory-resident*]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/M/memory_resident.html") programs. On [DOS]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/D/DOS.html") systems, such utilities are called [*TSRs*]("http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/T/TSR.html"). 
  example:dstat
***Dstat*** is an all-in-one utility which lets you handle almost all aspects of system monitoring in a single command.
