### Post by Nerevar144 on 2010-08-02
I've been using Ubuntu since 7.10, and I must say it's come a LONG way.  10.04 is the most compatible release out of the box yet for my almost ancient Dell Inspiron 8600.  All hardware is automagically sensed and installed as soon as I installed.  And best of all this is the first release that I don't have to use ANY restricted drivers.  I used to have to use the ATI drivers for the video card, which to put it frankly SUCK.  MESA finally got support for the RV350 chipset (Radeon 9600 Pro).  

My only qualm is that although MUCH prettier then Windows XP, the performance in several key areas still isn't there.  For example in XP I can play full screen YouTube videos at 360p and below, but it's not possible on Ubuntu -- the frame rate goes town to 2 or 3 FPS and the screen tearing is terrible.  If the video runs in the browser window, it plays back flawlessly.  I can't blame MESA for it, I'm just happy they finally support the RV350 for 3D acceleration.  Besides, the proprietary ATI fglrx drivers are basically identical performance wise to MESA; they same issue with fullscreen videos.

Another issue is the latency of the sound card.  The SigmaTel card in this notebook has a latency correction of 175ms in Audacity under Ubuntu, while the same version Audacity in XP has only 135ms latency correction.  Nothing huge and its still 100% correctable, but still its a performance difference.

My question is why?  Everything else about Ubuntu is faster.  It's faster sensing USB devices (and I usually don't even need drivers!).  It's faster launching and closing programs.  It's MUCH faster starting up and shutting down.  But these key areas are very bothersome for me.  Why is Window's video playback so much better/faster?  I know the hardware can handle it simply because it does in XP.  What are the hurdles for accomplishing this in Ubuntu?  If I had a newer/faster notebook I probably wouldn't notice any difference, but being this hardware is around 5 years old, it's very noticeable.  

Why the inefficiencies?  Just posing the question to those more knowledgeable than myself.

Other than that Ubuntu ROCKS!
