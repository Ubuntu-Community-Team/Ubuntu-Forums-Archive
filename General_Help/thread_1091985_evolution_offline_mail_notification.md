### Post by pytheas22 on 2009-03-09
I use Evolution because I think it looks nicer than Thunderbird, but I think the only places where it "integrates" in any special way into Gnome are that it can link scheduled appointments/tasks into the Gnome calendar, and it flashes a notification when you get new mail (while Evolution is running).

If I understand your question correctly, you want a way to be notified of new mail even when Evolution is not running.  In that case, you might be interested in [this program]("http://www.nongnu.org/mailnotify/"), which I've never used personally but which is supposed to watch any POP/IMAP mailboxes that you specify and flash a pop up when new mail arrives.

Of course, you could just configure Evolution to start when you log into the desktop (System>Preferences>Sessions>Startup), and move it to another desktop if it gets in your way.  This ensures that you'll always receive a notification upon the arrival of mail.  That's my approach.

I don't think Thunderbird has any way of notifying you of new mail unless it's opened either, but I haven't used Thunderbird since Ubuntu 7.04 so I could be wrong.
