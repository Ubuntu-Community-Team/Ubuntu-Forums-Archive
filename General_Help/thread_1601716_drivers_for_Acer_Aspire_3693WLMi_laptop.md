### Post by ki4jgt on 2010-10-20
I know Ubuntu has Russian localization b/c I've tried it. I don't know how accurate it is (I'm American and speak English) but I know they have it and assumes it's of high quality.

As for the Drivers, have you tried the liveCD yet? If not, then try it with you laptop to see if you need any drivers or not. After you've tried the LiveCD, If it doesn't work, then look for drivers.

Also, if you don't know how to use the LiveCD, Stick it in your computer before bootup and let your computer boot into the CD without booting from your hard drive. Select the option to try Ubuntu without making changes to your computer. It will let you try it out, before you install it. You can also see if your localization is to your liking.


**EDIT: to do this, simply press ESC while your on the purple screen with the two icons at the bottom of the screen. You will be brought to a menu and shown localization options. After you choose one, you will be able to select try Ubuntu without making changes to your computer
