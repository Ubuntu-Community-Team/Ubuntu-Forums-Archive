### Post by snova on 2008-11-19
Short: No. It's not possible.

Long answer: Kicker was the KDE 3 panel, and could have *probably* be used standalone with Gnome, but no longer with KDE 4.

The Kicker/KDesktop combination was replaced with Plasma, and takes over the entire screen. No dice.

If you want to try KDE without the rest of the apps, install the kde-core package, which I *think* will suffice to get a KDE environment without the apps.

### Post by snova on 2008-11-19
Right click on the desktop, Unlock Widgets.

Click the little "cashew" on the right end of the panel.

Just click and drag the line on top downwards. (It doesn't actually resize until you let go of it.)

When you're satisfied with the changes you make to the desktop, Lock Widgets again.
