### Post by ad_267 on 2008-10-25
Compiz fusion is the normal and extra desktop effects. It's installed already. Normal and extra just refers to which plugins are used by compiz fusion.

With the extra effects I think you get things like your windows wobble when you move them. Also try pressing Super-E for the expose plugin.

You can install compizconfig-settings-manager for complete control over what plugins are used.

### Post by ad_267 on 2008-10-25
Well you don't have to install anything extra. You can just install compizconfig-settings-manager and then enable the desktop cube and rotate cube plugins. I think that's all explained in the sticky in this forum.

I don't personally use the cube, but one effect I do really like is the expose plugin (Windows key + E), which lets you organise all your workspaces.

I haven't found it takes up too much resources, but I have had issues with 3D applications like Google Earth and games not working well with compiz, so I've disabled the desktop effects.
