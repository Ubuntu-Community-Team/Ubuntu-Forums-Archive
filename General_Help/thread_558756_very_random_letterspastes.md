### Post by lavajumper on 2007-09-25
I HATE that kind of stuff!

9 out of 10 times when I get quirky typing information its the fault of the keyboard. I don't think I've ever had a problem like yours and found it to be bad software, bad motherboard, or other horrible things I've imagined. Here's a couple of thoughts that might put you in the right direction:

1. My laptop (Dell1505) has a touchpad. In ubuntu its very sensitive. I mean that my wrist will "hover" over the touchpad when I'm typing and it will pick up a 'tap', which makes for some very quirky behavior.

2. My kid's keyboard was typing 3 letter for 1 press. After almost throwing away the whole computer I found that some yogurt spilled on it 3 months ago had goo-d up and would cause the shift and ctrl keys to activate. Tapping 'esc' three times and beating the crap out of the shift and ctrl keys would clear it up for a while. A new keyboard solved the problem.

Try plugging up a good external keyboard and see if it clears up. It works on my laptop because it keeps my hands far away from the touch-pad.
