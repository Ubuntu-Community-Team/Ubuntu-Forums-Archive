### Post by sargeras on 2007-04-20
As far as I know, when you unmount the hard drive, it just stops spinning.  This extends your hard drive life, but is only really useful if you don't frequently need anything that is on it.  For example, if you have a drive that contains backup archives of your system, you can unmount it by default and only have it spinning (mounted) when you are backing up your system.
