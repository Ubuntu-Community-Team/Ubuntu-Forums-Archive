### Post by timswait on 2009-02-23
I have several hard drives that I also use with Windows. Some of the folders on those drives (including very annoyingly the "My Documents" folder) have little padlocks on the icons for them. I then can't write to them unless I right click and do "Open as adminstrator". Then I can read and write to them. When I right click them and go to properties>permissions I see that they have the owner as root, and the group as route. When I try to change these to my username it won't let me, it says "Sorry, could not change the owner of "My Documents": Error setting owner: Operation not permitted". Why is this, and how can I force it to give me acces to my files without having to open them as Administrator? I have also tried the "chown" command in terminal and get the same "operation not permitted" message.

### Post by spiderbatdad on 2009-02-23
I believe you are talking about mounted file systems on your Ubuntu desktop.
Have a look at /etc/fstab. Depending on the file systems of your windows hdd's you can set options in /etc/fstab like: uid, gid, umask, fmask, dmask.

man mount gives a lengthy discussion, and bodhi.zazen has written a good guide to understanding fstab:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=283131](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=283131)

if you are not the owner, you can change the permissions. mounting by uuid and setting permissions at mount time can take care of that.  Maybe if you post the contents of /etc/fstab someone can help you.

### Post by spiderbatdad on 2009-02-23
if I understand fmask and dmask correctly, I believe this will help:
```
/dev/sda4   /media/documents  vfat users,uid=1000,gid=100,utf8,dmask=027,fmask=137 0 0
```Feel free to experiment, obviously, if you want to combine what you have with what is here. Unfortunately, I don't have a windows file system to experiment with :( otherwise I would try this myself.
