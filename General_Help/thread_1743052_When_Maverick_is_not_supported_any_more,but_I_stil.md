### Post by Lateralis on 2011-04-29
Nothing will happen.  You will still be able to login and use your system, but the repositories will not be updated (if they actually still exist - they may be annihilated) so you're system will remain in the same state indefinitely unless you manually update packages.  But in principle you can continue to use a system.

### Post by Gibbs on 2011-04-29
It will still work fine. When it becomes unsupported you will stop receiving updates (including security) through the official Ubuntu repositories. If you want longer support you need to use an LTS version such as 10.04.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-04-29
The repositories will no longer be accessible from their normal location. You will have to change your sources.list to point to the "archives". You'll no longer receive security updates.

By the time a version of Ubuntu reaches EOL, it's really getting old and crusty, and it's time to update. If Ubuntu 11.04 is not to your liking, you could update to 11.10 when it comes out; but remember it's a very bad idea to stay on an EOL distro.
