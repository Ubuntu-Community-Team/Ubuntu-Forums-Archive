### Post by webgrunt on 2009-05-16
I have Ubuntu 9.04 and a Soundblaster Live! 24-bit.  I just installed Ubuntu and was able to add the volume control to the taskbar.  I have the volume set as low as it can go, but it soulds like I have it turned all the way up.  If I increase the volume level, nothing happens.  It's like the volume control is stuck on maximum and the slider doesn't change that.

I tried changing my sound playback from Autodetect to ALSA, but that didn't change anything.  Then I tried setting it to "Live! 7.1 24-bit [SBO410] CA106 (ALSA)" but then there's no sound at all, no matter where I put the volume.  Next, I tried "Live! 7.1 24-bit [SBO410] CA106 (OSS)" but it's back to speaker-shreeding volume.  I tried every setting on there.

Finally, I right-clicked the volume control, selected preferences, and selected "Playback" for the selection there.  Suddenly, my volume slider was at max--and when I moved it down, it adjusted the volume!  :D
