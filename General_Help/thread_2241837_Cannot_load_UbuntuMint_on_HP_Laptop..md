### Post by sturdy on 2014-08-28
I am having an odd problem with a newly purchased HP Envy laptop. I used the computer for a couple of weeks with Mint 17 installed on a new SSD from a bootable thumb drive and all was well except for a very, very slow wifi upload speed (.1MBps). I then installed the HD with win8.1 that came with the laptop and now I can no longer boot from the thumb drive or the SSD. I talked to HP tech spt who eventually said that when Win 8.1 is installed, the laptop will no longer boot from the thumb drive unless it is made on that computer. Neither can I now boot from the SSD that worked previously. I also noticed a BIOS update from HP as Win 8.1 was installed.

My question: Does anyone know a way around this requirement. This HP Envy laptop is about as useful as a boat anchor (I don't have a boat). This makes no sense but obviously is a M$/HP collusion to lock users into M$.

### Post by sturdy on 2014-08-28
Well, as usual, I found the answer just after I posted the question. The BIOS has a settinjg for Legacy Support which is disabled by default. Change to Disabled and Secure Boot auto changes to disabled which allows booting from a USB device. It's a shame somebody didn't tell HP Support.:mad:
