### Post by TommyMann on 2007-09-10
Here's what you do, mate.

First some preparation.

Right click on the panel and if it's not unlocked unlock it. Then go to panel settings and move it to the top or the side. You probably want the top.

Now the real stuff. It's not actually called a menu bar, it's called a dock.

There's three main docks for KDE:

kxdocker
super powerful and has some cool features, but it's a total pain in the butt.

Enlightenment Dock
Can be really pretty, but also mildly a pain in the butt

Or as I'm going to help you with:
Kooldock

Open up adept
Type Kooldock in the search bar
Install

Once that's done, either look for it in the menu or

Open terminal (Konsole)

type kooldock

then right click on it and select configure

To add programs go to the program tab and drag the program icons into it

Good luck

-Tommy

### Post by shirilover on 2007-09-10
If you are referring to the finder bar commonly seen in Mac screenshots, it's easy.

First, right-click on the desktop and select Configure Desktop.
Choose Behavior from the list at the left.
In the General tab, under the area Menu bar at top of screen, select Current application's manu bar (Mac OS-style).
