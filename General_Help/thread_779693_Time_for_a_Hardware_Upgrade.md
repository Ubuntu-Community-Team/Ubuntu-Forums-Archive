### Post by elmer_42 on 2008-05-02
As tamoneya said, if you feel fine using it just use it. I just got a new system (see sig) about 3 months ago after using a laptop slightly worse than that. I never ran Ubuntu on the laptop, but Ubuntu runs blazingly fast on my current rig. But look at us still talking when there's science to do.

If you just want a fresh start, a brand new, format everything install would do well. Just make sure you back up your music and stuff. While a hardware upgrade might make your computer run faster, my PC only uses about 400MiB with Firefox, Totem, Deluge, and Pidgin open.

RAM is usually a reccomended upgrade, but check how much RAM you are using to see if you actually need to use it. A DVD-RW drive is extremely helpful to me because me and my friends often share mods for games that take up huge amounts of space. And a bigger hard drive would be great. I don't see how you can live with that little amount of space.
