### Post by cpetercarter on 2008-08-03
Also, right click on the file which is saved to your desktop, and then click on "permissions". This should tell you who the "owner" of the file is.

If you are the sole user of your computer, you already are an administrator. However, Ubuntu, unlike Windows, asks the administrator for a password before doing things like changing configurations or installing new software. This makes it a lot more dificult for you to do something catastrophic to your system, and it also makes it virtually impossible for unwanted programmes (viruses) to install themselves without your knowledge. Bear with it - you'll soon find that the Ubuntu way comes naturally.
