### Post by HermanAB on 2007-11-08
Those things are typically fine for your own use, but you are not allowed to distribute it - examples include things like MPEG and MP3 codecs.  So in order to distribute it, people put it on a server outside the US and then users have to download it themselves.

BTW, if you have to distribute MPEG codecs yourself, then you can get a license for about $4 per, so it is not excessive.

It's not the use of the codecs on Linux that makes it illegal, it is a licensing issue.  For example, when you buy software to play DVDs on a Windows machine, part of the price includes the licensing fee to legally use code to read the encrypted DVD.  The very nature of open-source projects is to eliminate proprietary software and licensing, so an OS such as Ubuntu does not pay licensing fees to these companies, the consequence being that Ubuntu does not natively support DVD or MPEG playback.  By downloading packages that 'hack' the DVD encryption so you can watch DVDs on Linux without having to pay the licensing fee, you agree to be held legally responsible for what is essentially copyright infringement in case someone decided to pursue legal action against you for not paying the license fee.
