Parameters missing in your post:
(a) your own personal grasp of modern technology trends such as AI

(b) the volume and types of historical documents to be scanned

(c) is there a local university (professor in Maths department) you can approach to seek the help of a tech graduate who might consider this task to supplement research work (perhaps in reading old cursive writing).

(e) do you have any concerns about privacy of handwriting images if they are pushed to a cloud service? If yes then the task becomes more difficult but achievable on a Ubuntu desktop.
Such images can be presented to hand writing engine for initially training the engine to learn the nuances of the writers' handwriting styles (which change over the years) to then apply this &#8220;training&#8221; to batches of handwritten documents.

Understand that in such analysis there will be a probabilty of failure in transcribing and the final handwriting to text will equire human intervention to validate (tweak) the output.  But like any training process, performance will improve over time.

### Post by TheFu on 2024-02-25
Performing OCR on handwriting is very difficult, since it isn't consistent in the same way that OCR on computer/typewriter text is.  Basically, the exact same glyphs are used with the computer for each font, so there are only 200 or so different glyphs that need to be trained for the computer OCR to work for each font.

With handwritten words, the characters nearby change how we write making the problem infinitely harder.

Don't forget that voice-to-text has gotten fairly good now, so you might just need to read aloud for the text to be created.
