### Post by undecim on 2009-09-28
If you want everything to work right out-of-the-box, avoid ATI graphics cards and Broadcom wireless cards. Though I've never really had any major issues that I couldn't fix myself (although I consider myself to be a power user... not quite an expert yet).

One thing I've noticed about compatability: Intel products play the nicest of all hardwares (though don't look at the CPU for that... Intel CPU doesn't imply any other Intel hardware).

I've actually found Acer laptops to be quite nice (only thing I've ever had to do with those is install the restricted wireless driver, which requires a temporary ethernet connection).

The best thing to do is to research (read "Google") the specific laptop model you are considering and see if other Linux users are having issues. A lot of times you will find posts on forums where people have lists of things that are working and things that aren't.

### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-09-29
I use a Gateway FX1173.

Dual core, 4G DDR3, 7200RPM 200G disk, nvidia 9800M GPU w/ 512MB, 17" 1920x1200 screen, DVD writer, eSATA, 1Gbps LAN, full-size keyboard (!!!), etc.

Got it for $1349 and swapped the CPU to P9500 (2.8Ghz, 6MB L2 cache), added a second hard drive, replaced the newer intel wifi card with the pricier i4925.

I sold the pulled parts. Final cost: $1310

It dual boots Vista Ultimate or 9.04.

Runs Counter Strike at full res, 64FPS. Compiz just works. No sound problems.

I use WiCD for wifi (Network Manager is sub-optimal).

Looks good, but it's BIG.
