### Post by ArthurWoodgate on 2012-07-29
I have a KVM switch connecting 4 Windows PCs, and on one of them I installed Ubuntu as a secondary OS. The hardware on all PCs cannot detect the video monitor through the KVM, but software that came with the video cards lets me specify the resolution I want -- and keep it even I reboot. Ubuntu accepts the monitor as a standard 4x3 ratio video device only and that is a problem, because mine is a 1080p 16x9 monitor with  a maximum resolution of 1920x1080. 1680x1050 will work well also. 

It would help us KVM fans to allow for the default standard graphics display to be offered to the user as a choice of standard monitors described as either 4x3 or 16x9 ratio. If that were the case, I could pick the right ratio by hand and, within that range, the correct resolution -- that is known to me.

To prevent a disaster when the monitor is changed, on start-up, instead of defaulting to the 4x3 standard monitor device outright, Ubuntu might allow the user to stay with the known setting or switch to a standard 4x3 ratio monitor, at the user's discretion.

Thanks.
