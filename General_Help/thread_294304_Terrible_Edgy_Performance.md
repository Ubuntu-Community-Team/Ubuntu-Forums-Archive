### Post by doobit on 2006-11-07
> **Avian00 said:**
> Thanks!

No apparent conflicts are shown.

Are you sure an IRQ conflict could cause performance problems?  Wouldn't stuff just not work?

Not necessarily. I've seen seriously degraded performance, but the computer still functioning, with a mouse conflicting with a sound card IRQ. A major problem can also be a firewire card IRQ sharing with a sound card. That normally happens when you have an onboard sound card. Soyo boards were famous for that. The result is very slow, jerky video, even though doesn't seem that the video card should have anything to do with it.

A DMA conflict will stop your system.

X controls your mouse and keyboard too, by the way.

### Post by flameproof on 2006-11-21
That little trick improved my video performance A LOT:

In terminal type:

```
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf.bak
```
(that backsup xorg.conf , to be safe)

Then type:

```
gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

xorg.conf has all sort of configeration things, just like system.ini in Windows. Look for:

```
"Device"
	Identifier	"Generic Video Card"
	Driver		"vesa"
	BusID		"PCI:0:5:0"
EndSection

```

And change "vesa" to "nvidia"

```
"Device"
	Identifier	"Generic Video Card"
	Driver		"nvidia"			# "vesa"
	BusID		"PCI:0:5:0"
EndSection

```

at least pages now scroll very fast.

Any more hints I can do from here?
