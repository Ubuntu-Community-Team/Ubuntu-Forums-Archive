### Post by efflandt on 2010-10-21
It depends how much you want to spend.  You will still need some ISP to connect you to the internet and provide you with routable IP addresses.  But you can get a block of static IP's from them, do your own forward and reverse DNS if you want to, e-mail, etc. for your own (registered) domain.

Even our factory with a DS3 connection is connected to the internet through an ISP (Internap).

Some people confuse the term ISP with other additional services, but it really just means **I**nternet **S**ervice **P**rovider (a connection to the internet) whether other services are provided or not.

### Post by srs5694 on 2010-10-21
You *can't* be independent of ISPs -- at least, not if you want Internet access. What you *can* do is change ISPs. Since you seem to be on cable now, you'll need to switch to DSL or some other telephone-line access, or perhaps satellite or another exotic service type, depending on what's available in your area.

Call your telephone company and/or check [DSL Reports.](http://www.dslreports.com/) There's a "find service" link, but I haven't thoroughly investigated it.
