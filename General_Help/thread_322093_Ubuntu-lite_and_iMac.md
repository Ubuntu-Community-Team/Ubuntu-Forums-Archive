### Post by samden on 2006-12-20
Quite a few people are running Xubuntu on similar iMacs - check the PPC section of the forum for more info on these computers.

With the Mac classics, I have no idea what Linux version would run on them. But I would point out that they are real antiques now. They are probably running Mac OS 7, which in my opinion is the most stable Mac OS that exists - they will never crash, and I mean never. I love those old machines as they are. They will go on the net etc if you want with old Mac software, although they'll struggle with many websites. They are fine for typing - I did almost all my undergraduate assignments on similar machines I had picked up for free or cheap, up to about 2 years ago when I splashed out and got myself an iBook. I'd leave them as Macs. They are useful, stable, and nice old machines. 

If they have At Ease installed on them, they would be perfect for children. This is a GUI that runs on top of OS 7 and makes it really simple for kids to use. If they don't you could find copies of At Ease and set them up with some older games and educational programmes, and basic word processing, and give them to someone to use for their kids. The great thing about children is that they are naive and won't know their very own flash computer is actually 15 years old, and won't care until they reach a certain age! The amount of pointless educational "laptop" toys you can buy for kids now is laughable, they cost far more than an old Mac computer and are nowhere near as functional, expandable or educational as an old computer is. Save someone spending hundreds this Christmas on a junky plastic toy and give them something much better for free!

Or you could keep them for 10 years in good storage and hock them off to collectors for a nice profit as working antiques in 2016...
