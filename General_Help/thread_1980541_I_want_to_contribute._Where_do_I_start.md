### Post by QIII on 2012-05-15
A new user, never having used any OS before, might generally take to Linux just as another person first introduced to Windows, except that some things, like drivers, do not exist in a PnP driverbase like Windows has.  Linux can, these days, be used almost entirely without using the command line, but there are some things that are best to do there.

Why would people still use Windows?  Because that is what they learned and that is what they are used to.  People's time is valuable and learning something new takes time.

Some colleagues and I were just talking about this this morning.

Take the analogy of a car.

Most people simply need to go to the Ford dealer and drive away in a new car.  They do not want to have Ford send them a truck load of crates full of parts so they can assemble a Ford exactly to their own liking.

Although you can get the Ford in Linux, some of the things that make it a great Ford still require turning wrenches, even if that doesn't always mean the command line.
