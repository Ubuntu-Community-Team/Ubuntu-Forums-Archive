### Post by halok on 2009-03-08
Generally Nvidia has good (almost) out of the box support. Also Intel graphics are well supported in Linux. Difference between the two is that nvidia can also come as a built in chip to the motherboard (and use proprietary drivers) and Intel are onboard (dont think i've heard of a Intel graphics card) and are open source. I use both in my various computers and both work well.

### Post by Chemical Imbalance on 2009-03-08
There are some pretty well-supported cards out there from ATI, but if you want to buy a new one, I suggest Nvidia.  Intel is only integrated on the motherboard as far as I know.

ATI seems to be opening up to the opensource community more readily as of late, but right now Nvidia seems to be the way to go.

Here is the hardware wiki to check your prospective card for compatibility:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCards](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCards)

Good luck with your purchase
