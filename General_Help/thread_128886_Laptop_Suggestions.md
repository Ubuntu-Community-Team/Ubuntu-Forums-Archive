### Post by hw-tph on 2006-02-12
If you want that kind of power for that price you're almost certainly will have to pay the price - weight. If you can find them, the Compaq R3000z series (or HP z5000v series or the HP Compaq nx9105 - they're all the same, just differently branded) provide excellent value for the money and are certainly very powerful. With these, not a whole lot of configuration is needed.

You might want to install the nvidia drivers (hey, it's voluntary) and perhaps use ndiswrapper for the wireless until Dapper comes out with the free [bcm43xx driver](http://bcm43xx.berlios.de/) which makes ndiswrapper redundant.

We have a Wiki about Linux on the R3000z series, but it is becoming increasingly redundant since these laptops has been around for some time now and most things are picked up and configured correctly out of the box. Ubuntu really shines here, but I still wrote a page about [Ubuntu on these beasts](http://prinsig.se/weekee/index.php/Ubuntu).


Håkan

### Post by nanotube on 2006-02-12
if you want to check which laptops are good with ubuntu, check out this link:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam)
this lists a whole bunch of laptops, as well as all the stuff that works/does not work on them. you can browse around and find a few that work "perfectly".
you could also use the "laptop testing data scraper" (linked to at the top of that page) to get all the data and play with it. 
personally, i have a dell inspiron 5150, and it works flawlessly.
