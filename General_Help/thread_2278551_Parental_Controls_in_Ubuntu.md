### Post by amanchesterman on 2015-05-17
I don't know about parental controls in Ubuntu so I'll leave that part for someone else to answer.

However as regards the internet, bear in mind that most big Internet Service Providers these days offer a 'parental control' service that restricts what sites you can access via broadband. I'm in the UK and our ISP is Virgin Media and this is what they offer:
[http://store.virginmedia.com/discover/broadband/your-broadband/protect-family/parental-controls.html](http://store.virginmedia.com/discover/broadband/your-broadband/protect-family/parental-controls.html)
That's just an example of course, I'm sure that the other big companies will offer something similar.

The point is, that doesn't require any particular settings on the **computer**: so long as the machine accesses the internet via that broadband connection, the filters and controls will apply.

Of course, if the lad's parents want unrestricted access, while limiting the sites that he can get to, that's a different question. But then they will need to deprive him of internet access via his phone too ...
