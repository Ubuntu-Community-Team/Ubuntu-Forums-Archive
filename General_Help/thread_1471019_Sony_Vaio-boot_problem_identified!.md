### Post by germanix on 2010-05-03
I have a Sony Vaio VGN-FS315E. I installed Ubuntu 10.04 and it runs beautifully. Today when I tried to boot the machine the boot sequence stopped half way through and would not finish. At first I thought that something had gone wrong with the hardware/software but I then noticed that the wireless switch was switched off. The Sony has a manual switch for the Wlan/Wireless and when I then switched this on the boot process continued and all was well. This was not the case with any of the previous releases. It seems that 10.04 will not boot on this Sony when the wireless switch is turned off. I thought I should report this in case any other users of this machine have a similar problem and do not know how to fix it.
This is a bit of a hassle for me. I often have this switch off as I use this machine to teach and when I am in the classroom doing presentations I do not need the internet and to save power and time by not having to connect to the internet and providing a password etc. I just leave the wireless switch off. This is what I did today and  the machine would not boot.
