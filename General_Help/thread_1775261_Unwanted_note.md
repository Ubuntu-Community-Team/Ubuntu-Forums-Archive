### Post by Zorael on 2011-06-04
Could you take a screenshot?

To force close a window without knowing its name, you can run '**xkill**' from a terminal or a run box (Alt+F2), and then just click on the window when the cursor changes.

### Post by Mariane on 2011-06-05
> **Zorael said:**
> 
Could you take a screenshot?


Thanks for your suggestion, it solved the problem. I'm not joking, I promise! 

To take a clean screenshot I right-clicked on the desktop and in the menu which appeared I chose "Desktop Activity Setting", then "Activity" and finally for "type", "Desktop view". I usually have lots of icons on my desktop, this hides them (or displays them when you choose "Folder view"). 

This also changed the look of the yellow note. It got bigger and a 'T' appeared on the bottom left. When I clicked on the note the little menu popped up on the side of it, and this time it contained an 'X' at the bottom. Clicking on the 'X' got rid of the thing. 

I still did the screenshots, if you're curious, the first is what it looked like before I changed the desktop activity (recreated) and the second is what it looked like after the change. 

Changing the activity back to "Folder view" left the yellow note in full view, with the 'T' and the 'X' reachable. I think the problem I had with this note was that it was half out of the screen (some of my program windows overlap the edges when they first open) but as this one could not be dragged (or I didn't find out how to drag it) I was stuck with it. 

Mariane
