Hello, Tipped OuT. :-)

I'm glad that you're feeling more and more comfortable with Linux, and considering switching over as a full-time Ubuntu user. 

This is a decision that you have to make, however.

I don't know much about Flash alternatives, but for me, flash works fine.  Maybe you have an outdated version?

Also, as far as Windows Live Messenger, someone suggested Pidgin (the GNOME messenger client.)  While I prefer GNOME by far, KDE has one-upped us GNOME users with their messenger client, in my opinion.  Kopete has a wider functionality, including voice chat and video chat in Windows Live Messenger, a functionality that Pidgin hasn't yet implemented.  Again, though, it's your choice.  Why not try both of them? :-)

Also, as far as the speed goes, this is something that really varies from user to user.  Since Jaunty is relatively new, though, it's bound to be a little clunkier, as it's just learning to walk.  As it grows over time, though, it will learn to run pretty quickly, I'm wagering. :-)  

Anyway, I agree wholeheartedly about taking the time to back up your other OS first, and all of that "just-in-case" business.

Whatever you decide to do, best to you, and good luck. :-)

Keep us posted on what you decide to do.
