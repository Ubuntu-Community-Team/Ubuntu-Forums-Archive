### Post by Iowan on 2010-07-06
Have you checked the MD5 checksum to verify the download? It's usually best to burn the ISO at the slowest speed possible... and CDRW's can cause problems on some machines - CDR's generally work.

### Post by Leppie on 2010-07-06
download the md5sums file: [http://releases.ubuntu.com/lucid/MD5SUMS](http://releases.ubuntu.com/lucid/MD5SUMS)
then download winmd5 free: [http://www.winmd5.com/](http://www.winmd5.com/)
and check the md5sums for the images you downloaded.
you should also burn at low speeds, preferably 8x or less.

there could be some funny coincidence that both writers are defective/need maintenance...
