### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-03-19
In my experience, certification is a bit overrated.  Some jobs (particularly some government positions) require certification, but most employers are satisfied if you can demonstrate your knowledge in the area.  Some like their employees to be certified (because it looks good), and are willing to pay for employees who were hired based on knowledge and experience to get their certification.

That's not to say that you shouldn't get certified.  The certification itself is just a piece of paper, but studying for certification can help you identify your strengths and weaknesses.  No matter how much you know, there's always room for improvement.  And the paper is a nice bit of proof of the time and effort you've put into refining your trade.  Just don't think that certification is a magic ticket to a great job.

People knowledgeable of administrating Linux machines and networks are increasing in demand as more governments and companies move to open-source software.  If you know your way around a shell prompt, I don't think you should have much problem finding a job.  You'll probably have to work your way up to network administrator, though, since that's not exactly an entry-level position.
