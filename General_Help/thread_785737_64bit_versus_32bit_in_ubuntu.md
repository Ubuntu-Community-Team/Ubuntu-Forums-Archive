### Post by cotcot on 2008-05-07
Depends on your apps. If you have heavy apps (video editing for example) than 64 bit is a friend. If you have a specific driver for your hardware (for instance for a printer working only in 32 bit) then you might have an argument to stick to 32 bit.

My suggestion is to install 64 bit in dual boot to 32 bit. Run your apps in parallel on both install and switch to 64 bit when you feel confortable.

Some say that the packages for 64 bit are less stable, give more problems or are less available. I saw this also in the past and stepped back from 64 bit to 32. But Hardy improved the 64 bit situation a lot so that I switched back to 64 bit. All my apps are running fine.

### Post by ASULutzy on 2008-05-07
I would say for the majority of the people 64 bit is the way to go if your system is capable of running it.

The limitations on usable RAM in 32-bit just makes it not future-ready whatsoever.

If you're using mostly very popular hardware, and not stuff you picked up at a PC show made by some guy in his basement, the driver thing really isn't a big issue.

If you ever want to use >=3.2 GB of ram or so, get 64 bit.
