### Post by Roasted on 2014-04-10
Some chips (mostly just stubborn Broadcom chips) require closed source drivers which cannot be packaged to work out of the box. For situations like this, the resolution is typically the same.

1) Plug in via ethernet.
2) Open your driver menu (often called Additional Drivers. I don't use Lubuntu, but it would either be a tab under Software Sources or somewhere in the applications menu, or maybe even under a System Settings dialog)
3) Download and install the driver.
4) Restart.

Once done you can disconnect from the ethernet. The idea is that you are downloading the closed source driver from Canonical. From there, it should work.
