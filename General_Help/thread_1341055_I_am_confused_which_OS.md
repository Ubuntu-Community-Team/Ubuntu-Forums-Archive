### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-11-29
Ubuntu works great for most standard stuff: web surfing, watching movies, listening to music.

What is does NOT do well is native MS Windows stuff.  Don't believe the rabid fans about Wine -- it is NOT a miracle cure for running MS Windows stuff in Ubuntu.  It runs some stuff very well, other stuff OK, and most stuff -- not at all.

So, how well your online banking and games perform in Ubuntu will depend critically on how much they depend on MS Windows specific stuff (infrastructure like .Net, apps like IE7/8 or Silverlight, accelerated 3D video drivers, etc).  The more they depend on this stuff, the less likely they'll work well in Ubuntu -- or any other flavor of Linux.

### Post by GepettoBR on 2009-11-29
> **pandurang_m said:**
> How is your experience with Ubuntu? 
Great. It's more of a headache to set up initially than Windows, but after you've configured everything it requires no maintenance apart from installing the regular updates. It barely ever crashes and is very responsive.

> **pandurang_m said:**
> Is bank transactions are safe? 
That depends less on your OS and more on your browser. An online bank transaction done from Firefox in an uninfected Windows computer or a Linux computer would present exactly the same risks. But since in Ubuntu you aren't in any risk of being infected by a worm capable of stealing your information, you could say it's safer.

> **pandurang_m said:**
> How about Anti Virus for Ubuntu? 
You don't really need one. Wndows viruses don't affect Linux and there are no Linux viruses in the wild. However, there is an antivirus, ClamAV, if you want to make sure your files are virus-free before transferring them to a Windows machine.

> **pandurang_m said:**
> How about applications which I use? 
Most Open-Source applications have a Linux version. Most common use Windows applications have an equivalent in Linux, that may or may not do everything you want it to do and may or may not have features that the Windows app doesn't have. 

There is also the Wine Project for running Windows applications in Linux. In most cases they work flawlessly, but sometimes they'll be buggy or not even work at all.

You should research what applications you'll be using. [www.osalt.org](www.osalt.org) is a great site for this. The app you use to update your phone might pose a problem.

> **pandurang_m said:**
> How about games? 
Wine and its many children (Cedega, PlayOnLinux) can do a great job with games. You can check if your games work with Wine on [appdb.winehq.org](appdb.winehq.org). You can also check if your other Windows apps work well with Wine in this same location.

> **pandurang_m said:**
> After couple of years will there be viruses for Ubuntu like Windows now?
Short answer: No. Long answer: Since anyone can see the source code for Open-Source software, it is much easier for any and all vulnerabilities to be spotted by the large community of active programmers. "With enough eyes, all bugs are shallow". So, even when a flaw exists that a virus might be able to exploit, it will be fixed much more quickly than a virus could be coded to take advantage of it. Therefore, Linux viruses are a virtually nonexistent risk. Just be sure to keep up with the software updates.
