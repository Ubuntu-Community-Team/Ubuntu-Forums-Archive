Well it is not a case of whether your netbook supports them but the other way around.

It depends on your hardware specifically.

Ubuntu is pretty good with most hardware these days, and seems to run well on notebooks as far as i know but like i said depends on the specific drivers required.

As for Backtrack, though it is based on Ubuntu 10.04 LTS it is a specialised kernel built around alot of specific wireless drivers for example, however that doesnt prevent you compiling your own in the event something doesnt work.

If you cant do that then it is unlikely you should be using BT in the first place as it requires a certain level of knowledge to use efficiently for any Security Audit based task.

### Post by gennatolls on 2011-12-11
A netbook will always be somewhat slow since they are a very low power machines. Your netbook should be fine with them both installed, it all depends on you HDD space and the how much you intend on using. You may consider setting up backtrack as a persistant usb install. Here the backtrack wiki: [http://www.backtrack-linux.org/tutorials/](http://www.backtrack-linux.org/tutorials/)
and heres for a persistant usb install : [http://www.infosecramblings.com/backtrack/backtrack-4-usbpersistent-changesnessus/](http://www.infosecramblings.com/backtrack/backtrack-4-usbpersistent-changesnessus/)

I have a dual boot laptop with ubuntu and backtrack and I don't find myself using backtrack for anything other than what its meant for, I always reboot back to Ubuntu its a much smoother,pleasant experience. If you have never used either OS's, I bet you will be relieved from windoze. There is a learning curve, but once past that you will never look back. Good luck.
