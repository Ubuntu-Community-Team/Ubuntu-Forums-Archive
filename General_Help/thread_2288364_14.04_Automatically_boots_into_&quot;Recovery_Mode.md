### Post by Vladlenin5000 on 2015-07-26
ANY new monitor with that graphics is an aberration. 

Here's what you have to understand:
There are NO good drivers for SiS in Ubuntu currently and never were.
One version sort of worked until 12.04. 
Then happened a change in a basic component to any graphical environment in Linux, let's called it X, and the old driver was suddenly incompatible.
Then someone updated the driver and that become the default.
It's still the same old driver but even worse and it cannot possible detect new monitors and many new monitors and especially TVs don't even provide a standard identification because, yeah, who cares about VGA nowadays?!?...

What I know about it is from users' reports here and elsewhere:
First there are tweaks that slightly improve the graphics performance and/or reliability; different SiS chips may require different approaches.
Then the resolution problem. Some users had success with the xrandr workaround; others used the VESA 'hack'. However, the generic one is very limited.

In a nutshell: You're in a catch 22.
