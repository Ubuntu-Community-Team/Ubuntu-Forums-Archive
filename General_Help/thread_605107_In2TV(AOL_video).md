### Post by orwellus on 2007-12-07
Just an in general reply. I got AOL Videos to play on both Firefox and Epiphany Web Browser. Here is what I did. I selected a video, I waited unti the commercial at the begining was over, and when the episode started to play, I selected the full screen option on the AOL Video player (Until the commercial finishes the AOL player won't let you access any of the controls on the AOL Video player).. In Epiphany a window popped right up and loaded the video (both picture and sound). In Firefox I did the same thing (selected the fullscreen option), but got a message saying that Firefox was blockng pop-ups. So I selected the preference tab that popped up, and selected "Allow Pop Up for AOL Video". Then I selected the full screen option again, a window popped up and played the video. BAM! The only problem now is since it is full screen the quality of the video isn't the greatest. You can also go into the setting option and choose a lower bandwith, (I selected low). When I did that,  I got the video to play straight through the entire episode. In general, I found the video seemed to work better in Epiphany rather than FIrefox. So, to the brave among you, give it a try.
