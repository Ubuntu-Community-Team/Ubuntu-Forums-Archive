### Post by wilee-nilee on 2009-11-17
I am not familiar with Ubuntu studio, but generally you want to either pop the live CD in and let the install adjust the partitioning in the GUI that is the partition allocation portion of the install.

Or you can use gparted in the live CD to shrink the XP leaving a open space and when you get to the partitioning GUI in the install choose use open space. If none of this makes sense wait for another post to get help or if you have any friends that know about dual booting. 

You don't want to do anything without having your XP backed up and the ability to reinstall it with knowing the verification keys, in case something goes wrong.
