### Post by jonobr on 2009-06-06
I would recommend you look at and include the history of unix and linux systems and show how they evolved into so many distributions and how they became so popular.

I would throw in topics into that about people like Richard stallman (some consider him a fruit cake and some, a genius)
and GNU project.

I would investigate a bit about Linus Torvalds,
the so called  father of the linux kernel.
As a start to the the discussion (and to have a bit of humour), you could mention the often used airline analogy of operating systems. [HERE]("http://www.zyra.org.uk/os-air.htm")

Lastly, if your doing the presentation on a projector,
you should hopefully be using the ubuntu/linux distro on an openoffice presentation using openoffice handouts, and then explain that only the hardware cost you money.

I would sit in  a presentation like that for hours!!!!:-)
