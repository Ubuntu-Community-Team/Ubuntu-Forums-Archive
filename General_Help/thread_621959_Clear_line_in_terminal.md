### Post by mbailey on 2007-11-24
How do you clear the command line? let's say I have typed in a command and changed my mind, or used up arrow to scroll through history and didn't find what I was looking for. The only way I know to get rid of it is to backspace through it all - is there a key sequence that will delete it all?

### Post by gepatino on 2007-11-24
ctrl+u does exactly that... cleaning to the begining of the line.

it's also usefull when you realize that you're typing the wrong password ;)

---

### Post by tubunu on 2007-11-24
```
clear
```
clears the whole terminal screen, though CTRL+u might be what you asked for
