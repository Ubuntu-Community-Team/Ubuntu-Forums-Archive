### Post by synapsys on 2009-07-08
Assuming you have already 'made space' for this new distro and you are not overwriting your current installation. 

You must have at least one partition mount / (root filesystem) or you will not have a linux installation. The reason there are other options is that some people like to use more than one partition for their installation.... such as.....

one for root        /
one for home      /home

So... if you are only installing to one single partition then make the mount point root.
