OK, here's what I know about video card drivers. For Linux there are two types of drivers: open-source, and proprietary. Open-source drivers are good because they are completely free to use, as is open-source software. Proprietary drivers are my drivers of choice, mainly because they make better use of the graphics card.

You may use either, depending on whether you want your Ubuntu to remain truly open-source. I don't think that you can get 3D support with the open-source drivers though. Try setting them to where they both use the **fglrx** driver. One other thing; I don't know why you have what looks like two graphics cards. If you can give me more information it would help.

Are you sure this is the only thing you changed?
I'm having the same problem here... also with an ATI Mobility Radeon 9700

The weird thing: it used to work before with [this guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide#Method_2:_Generating.2FInstalling_Ubuntu_packages_for_the_8.24.8_drivers_in_Ubuntu_Dapper_Manually"). Just with the latest upgrade to 2.6.15-23, it stopped working.

edit:
It seems this is not needed anymore (copied from the guide mentioned above):

>  Blacklist old fglrx module from linux-restricted-modules

sudo gedit /etc/default/linux-restricted-modules-common

Edit DISABLED_MODULES to include fglrx
File: /etc/default/linux-restricted-modules-common

DISABLED_MODULES="fglrx"

I removed fglrx, and now it works again :)
