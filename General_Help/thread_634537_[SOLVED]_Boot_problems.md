### Post by Herman on 2007-12-08
:) Hello lmlypfan,
Try working through the checks, tests and execises in this link,  [P.O.S.T. Troubleshooting Steps]("http://www.computerhope.com/issues/ch000607.htm") from Computer Hope.com,
I highly recommend that link, it has helped me in the past and I think it is the best I have seen on this subject.
It has been improved since last time I used it too.   :)

Also, take a look in your BIOS in [    System Health Check]("http://www.users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p1.htm#System_Health_Check"). to see if your voltages are steady or if they are fluctuating. It's true that the power supply is the component of PC hardware most commonly found faulty. This is probably due to the fact that most of us want to purchase our PCs for the lowest price and not very many people ask the salesmen questions about the quality of the power supply before deciding which PC they will buy. Actually, the power supply unit is very important because it can protect or cause damage to all the other parts.

However, using the first link I gave you, I have traced booting problems to RAM modules being loose or not in good contact in their sockets and even the CPU somehow not being in good contact with it's socket, (don't ask me how that's possible, it doesn't seem likely, but that's the truth). In those instances, the PC was able to be fixed just by cleaning the whole area, removing the parts, cleaning the contact areas and re-inserting the same parts.
[COLOR=Red][B]Be sure to read, understand and obey all safety rules both for you and your computer before touching any hardware.
[/B][/COLOR]
