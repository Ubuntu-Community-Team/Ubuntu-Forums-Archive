No.

Your hardware is probably not compatible, or not working well with ubuntu.  Or it does not have stable drivers because the manufacturers are not cooperating with the linux community.

For *any* of those reasons, ubuntu is not for you.

Majority of people want stability and got it from ubuntu.  
Take this as an unlucky experience that you belong to the minority who cannot get ubuntu to work,  and move on.
