### Post by iofthemourning on 2008-07-22
For starters, I'm running 8.04. I've been playing around with compiz recently and I like a lot of the features. Sadly, when I enable normal/extra effects, the desktop boxes in the workplace switcher shrink to about half their normal size.

The boxes return to their normal size when I change settings to no visual effects, but then I can't use compiz. Attached is a picture of the small workplace switcher.

I searched the forums and it seems like people on older versions of ubuntu had the same problem with beryl, but I cannot find any option in the advanced desktop effects settings menu to fix my problem.

---

### Post by iofthemourning on 2008-07-22
well. I figured it out.

I had 4 desktops set up in 2 rows while I wasn't using any extra effects.

All I had to do was:
1. switch back to no extra effects.
2. right click on the workplace switcher and click preferences.
3. Switch rows to 1.

It does not shrink anymore when I enable normal/extra effects.

I first experienced this issue when switching effects on and off to use Blender, and I agree that this is a bug. However, there is a solution to your complaint:

To have 2 rows AND the effects, turn effects off and right click on the switcher to get to the preferences. Set the number of rows and workspaces to 1. Yes, this will jam all of your open windows into one workspace, but it will only be for a minute.

Now turn effects back on, keep the settings, and then go back into the workspace switcher preferences and set it up as you like. The switcher windows should be back to their normal size.
