### Post by fmartinez on 2008-05-02
You can't really "uninstall" ubuntu because it's an Operating system. It's just like trying to uninstall Windows XP. Your best bet is to try and recover the files from you need from you device. There are a couple of options: 
- Opt 1: When you to what you think is the log in screen. Hit this key combination "alt+f2" this will end the xsession and put to a command line interface where you can log in and access your files. If you can get to that point where you can search through your directories... you can put in a USB device and copy the finals you need to that device to access to your files.
-  Opt 2: You can post your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file. This is the file that manages your Xsession.... video drivers, display settings... then we help you edit it and try to fix that display. REMEMBER THIS OPTION WILL ONLY WORK IF OPTION 1 WORKS.
- Opt 3: You boot to a live cd (or from a USB with Linux) and try  access the device with your files for recovery. 

GOOD LUCK HOPE THIS HELPS! GOOD LUCK ON YOUR FINAL....!
