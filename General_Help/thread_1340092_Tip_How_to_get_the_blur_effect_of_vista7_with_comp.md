### Post by xrecar on 2009-11-28
Hello all. We all know that we have the OS with the best graphic effects out there, however, many of you wonder how to get the Vista blur effect on your transparent themes.I'll try to help with this.

[COLOR="DarkRed"][SIZE="1"]Thanks to [BobCFC]("http://www.gnome-look.org/usermanager/search.php?username=BobCFC&PHPSESSID=3ab233ab5a0cf970a91513879d157670") at [GNOME-Look.org]("http://gnome-look.org/") for posting the original instructions [here]("http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/True+Aero+Glass?content=93696").[/SIZE][/COLOR]

Ubuntu already comes with **CompizConfig** Settings Manager. Get to it by **System** > **Preferences** > **CompizConfig Settings Manager.**
If you do not have it, install it via **Ubuntu Software Center** or **Add/Remove software**.

Once in the settings manager (CompizConfig Settings Manager), click on **"Effects"**.

**Now, the fun part:**

After you click on **effects**, click on the **check-box** next to **"Blur Windows"** to activate it, then click on **"Blur Windows"** to customize it.

Make sure that **"Alpha Blur"** is **Checked**.
Then, on **"Blur Filter"**, change the option to **"Gaussian Blur"**.

Once that is done, set the **"Gaussian Radius"** to **11** (you can play with ths later to achieve a custom effect).

Afterwards, make sure that the Gaussian Strength is set to 1.0000.

Finally, enjoy your new Blur effect.
