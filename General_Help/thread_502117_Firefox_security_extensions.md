### Post by scooper on 2007-07-16
In this case there's literally no difference between Linux and Windows.  These extensions protect the browser more than the operating system.  Whatever your concerns are in Windows regarding safe browsing don't change when switching to Linux.  What you use depends on your tolerance of risk vs. your tolerance of complexity (via extensions).

I periodically copy my entire Firefox profile between Linux and Windows.  So they are generally using similar extensions, cookies, saved passwords, bookmarks, etc.  It works fine, except for extensions that save file paths and for paths held by Firefox, e.g. the download path.  The paths are just a one time tweak after the copy.

EDIT:
I just noticed you mentioned some McAfee thingy.  Not sure what that is, so don't take my advice above about that tool.
