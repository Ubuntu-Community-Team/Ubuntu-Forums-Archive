May I assume a few things here first?

English isn't your first language, right?
If it is, please get your grammar in order. If not, no worries.

1. The theme can be changed and is usually the first thing a user changes on any new Ubuntu install. 
Themes can be found at [gnome-look.org]("http://www.gnome-look.org")  or [here]("http://www.bisigi-project.org/?option=com_content&view=frontpage&Itemid=24&lang=en") for a cleaner look. On the second link just install the ppa.

2. Please post a thread in the [Hardware and Laptops forum]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=332") to get your keyboard and trackpad issues addressed properly. This forum really isn't a help section but I am sorry that you are having issues.

3. Remember that Ubuntu version 9.10 is not an LTS version nor is it considered stable. Koala is going to have issues throughout it's entire cycle. 8.04 is the LTS with three years of support. 8.10 is still stable but only offers 18 months of support. After that the versions are considered cutting or bleeding edge releases. These are just ways for Ubuntu to be tested out in the wild for the next LTS, 10.4, coming in April of 2010.

Please be patient with Ubuntu, and if you are having issues that are difficult or time consuming to resolve then please try an earlier release. I would personally recommend 8.10 unless you need a super stable machine in a total production environment. In that case use 8.04.

9.10 is bleeding edge. Expect some issues. If you post a thread in the appropriate forum, someone else may benefit from your issue also. 

Good luck.
