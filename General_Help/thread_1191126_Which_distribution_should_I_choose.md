### Post by t4thfavor on 2009-06-18
How much did you pay? Mine was stolen out of my car Last Saturday :( I used to use Jaunty on it, and it ran fine. However I did notice a moment or so pause every once in a while that I attributed to disk IO on the slow Flash chip. If you have a 32GB upgrade I would assume its slightly quicker than the preinstalled one.

I just made a USB boot disk from the live CD (System -> Administration -> USB startup disk) and the install ran fine. Everything worked "out of the box" Just remember that you will not have Hibernate unless you have a swap partition as big as your RAM.



EDIT:

I found the NBR to be too tooney, as the access to things I use all the time was non existent unless I wanted to drop to a command line. Go with the straight Ubuntu, and I believe you can install the NBR gui pretty easily.


Your definitely going to like it over lugging around a full size laptop, and I am definately replacing mine as soon as I get more cash on hand.
