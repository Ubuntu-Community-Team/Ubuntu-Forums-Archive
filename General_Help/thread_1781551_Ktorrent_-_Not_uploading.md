### Post by ankspo71 on 2011-06-14
Hi,
It could be that those leechers are too far away from you causing them to have a very poor connection to your computer, or they are on a very slow connection and are unable to connect. It's also possible that they don't need the parts of the file you have already downloaded, but this wouldn't apply if you have already finished the download.

Have you tried seeing if another torrent client (such as deluge or transmission) has this problem, or have you tried downloading something in Ktorrent that has alot of leechers (such as a ubuntu ISO file which should have 500 to 1000 or more leechers)?

Another problem is your ISP might be throttling your torrent traffic. [http://wiki.vuze.com/w/Bad_ISPs](http://wiki.vuze.com/w/Bad_ISPs) If your ISP is listed in there, you might have some luck changing the encryption options in Ktorrent's settings.

Hope this helps.
