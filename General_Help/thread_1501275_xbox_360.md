### Post by Jeroensum on 2010-06-04
If understand you correctly you want to use your laptop as a bridge for your xbox?
Like so:
xbox---cable--->laptop---WIFI--->AP/router-->INTERNET



1. Make sure you have **different** subnets for the two nics on the laptop

wireless 192.168.0.x
wired 192.168.1.x

2. Turn on ipforwarding.

Open /etc/sysctl.conf and you should see this line (or add it). It should look just like this.

# Uncomment the next line to enable packet forwarding for IPv4
net/ipv4/ip_forward=1

3. on the xbox make sure the gateway points to the ip address of the wired nic on the laptop (you can set these manually). And you set the ipaddress and subnet to correspond with the ip of the laptop nic
Also check your /etc/resolv.conf on your laptop for the DNS server(s) and add these to the xbox setup, else it won't find it's way on the interwebs.

That should be it. :)
