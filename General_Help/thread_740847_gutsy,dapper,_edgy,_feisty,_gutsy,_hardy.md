### Post by aeiah on 2008-03-31
7.10 is gutsy. google will help you find things like that :p

basically they're all updates. ubuntu releases a new version every 6 months. 7.10 was released in october 2007, hence 7.10. the next one will be in april, and will be version 8.04. there's not a desperate need to upgrade each time there's a new one, although certain ones are LTS (long term release), and they're supported for longer and are supposed to be more stable. the next one in april will be an LTS and is thus probably worth upgrading to, but there's no rush. i usually upgrade about a month after they've been released, so certain creases have been ironed out.

but its usually best to go by the rule of 'if it aint broke, dont fix it'. if everything works fine for you in xubuntu 7.10, there's not much reason for you to upgrade unless there's a feature that you'd like. xubuntu will probably be released later than ubuntu 8.04 anyway. probably may at a guess.
