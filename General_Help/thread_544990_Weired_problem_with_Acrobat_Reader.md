### Post by mocoloco on 2007-09-07
If it happens under your user and not others there must be something running that's conflicting somehow.  If you're running desktop effects (ie compiz or beryl) disable them, they're a big culprit for stuff like this.  
Or there might be something else starting up in your session that's causing the problem.  First try logging in with "Failsafe Gnome", you choose that in the little menu on the login screen, you'll find it.
If that doesn't work you could go to System -> Preferences -> Session and disable things, logout, login, until you find out which app is the culprit.
