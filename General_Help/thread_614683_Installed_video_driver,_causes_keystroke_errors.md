### Post by ldauvin on 2007-11-16
Hello everyone,

I am new to Ubuntu, and have recently installed 7.10 to dual boot with Win XP on my desktop machine. That went quite well, and thanks to Steven Harper and his wonderful USB modem manager, I got connected to the internet, and could then enable the repositories and use the Restricted Driver Manager to load up the Nvidia video driver.

 That seemed to go well, but on start up, I found that at the login screen, the keyboard input can alternate between "nothing", "late", and "multi-repetitive". This makes login a hit and miss affair, and can take up to 15 minutes to get right. After getting into the Desktop at last, I removed the video driver, and re-started. Login via keyboard was then normal. I found that keyboard behaviour was OK once into the Desktop ie at the administrator password pop-up, with either the video driver installed, or not.

Thinking that maybe the Video driver wasn't right ( it's a Nvida Geforce2 MX400, so apparently quite old ), I went down Alberto Milone's ENVY route to re-install the driver. The first re-start was OK, but subsequent  startups have shown up the keyboard input errors again. 

Anybody got any ideas, please? Such a shame that this spoils the experience, as I find Ubuntu to be very good so far. 

Update 19th Nov 2007:  By using ENVY in manual mode, and subsequent configuring of the X console, I now have a working set-up. Still do not know what was the root cause of the problem , but it was definitely video driver related, so that's good enough for me!

Thanks.
