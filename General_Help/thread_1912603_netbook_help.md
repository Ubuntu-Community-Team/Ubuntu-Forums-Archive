### Post by t0p on 2012-01-21
I run Lubuntu on my Asus EeePC 701.  The only real probem I encounter is one of storage - it's got a 4GB SSD (a solid-state "HDD") and a 8GB SD card that I have in the card reader and which has /home mountedon it.  I've also upped the RAM to 2GB.

Even with all this faffing about, it's not a good all-round PC.  But it's fine at what I use i for - basic browsing (Youtube ain't so cool), creating text files with a text editor and even some light word processing with Abiword.  And Backgammon - damn GBG is a gooood player.

Netbooks nowadays tend to have HDDs rather than SSDs.  This would be an issue for me, as I drop stuff all the time, and the netbook walks away from accidents that would have killed a HDD.

As for the screen size: I find 7" to be fine for most things.  If you have to do jobs that require a larger screen, use a proper computer.  That's what I do.  Oh yeah, and Lubuntu was *made* for low-spec machines.
