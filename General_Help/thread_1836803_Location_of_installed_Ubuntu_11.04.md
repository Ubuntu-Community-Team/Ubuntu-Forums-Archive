### Post by krytorii on 2011-08-31
It depends on whether your computer supports booting from a usb. Even if it does, it'll probably be slower as IIRC USB transfers data slower than SATA.

What you can do is make a tiny partition in your internal HDD for ubuntu, then put all your files (downloads/music/documents) onto the external HDD. Or you could (probably) make a home partition on the external HDD, but install ubuntu on the internal. That would save a lot of space on the main HDD.
