### Post by zami on 2007-02-25
Can anyone recommend a simple photo editor?

I've tried digiKam, but it seems more like album management.

GIMP does what I need, but every change is a multi-part process, and I always mangle the ratio when I crop photos with GIMP.

What I need is a very simple program to
- crop photos while keeping a proper landscape or portrait ratio
- shrink photos (If I could set a default resolution instead of hand entering my values every flipping time like I do with GIMP.... oooooh that would be fantastic.)
- does NOT organize my pictures for me, auto rename them, make multiple copies on my hard drive, etc!  I don't want management, just simple editing.

A program that could also edit contrast, brightness, gamma, etc would also be great, but that sort of stuff I don't mind doing in GIMP.  It's really just the resizing/cropping that I want to speed up (and mangle less).

Free is preferable, but (when I can) I'll gladly pay for any dynamite photo software, so suggestions for both/either are welcome.

Linux native is preferable too, of course!  But I'll gladly try a Windows program under Wine.

Thanks for any suggestions.
