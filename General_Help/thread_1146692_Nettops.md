### Post by pkkid on 2009-05-02
OK Kiddos,

Since no one seems to know anything in this area.. I thought it might be good to update this thread with what I found out so far.  I didn't realize recent (good) products in this area are as new as they are.

It's not considered a nettop, and its not new.  But for small form PC's the AOpen seems to be the best fit, and will pack all the punch with what I need to do an more.  It's basically a full PC in mini form.  Only problem.. It's damn expencive.
[http://system76.com/product_info.php?cPath=27&products_id=83](http://system76.com/product_info.php?cPath=27&products_id=83)

Acer's Revo looks almost like exactly what someone wanting a cheap PC next to the TV to have.  It seems this can be bought on May 5th.  It's running NVidia's new Ion graphics card and can even run Spore and some other high end games.  I don't care much about this, but to know it can do it makes me a little less nervous about normal computing.  The only problem is that it's running a single core processor, and the reviewers blame bad flash performance on this (and of course Adobe).  Because Hulu will be such a big part of the PC, this is a problem.
[http://www.engadget.com/2009/04/28/acer-aspirerevo-review/](http://www.engadget.com/2009/04/28/acer-aspirerevo-review/)

There are two more PCs that look very promising and are basically the same machine as the Revo mentioned above, but can't seem to find much more information on it except the very recent announcement (April 29) that they were unveiled in Taipai.  The big difference in these machines is they will be running a dual core processor and could fix the slow Flash playback issue.
[http://www.slashgear.com/asrock-ion-330-and-pegatron-cape-7-nvidia-ion-nettops-2942244/](http://www.slashgear.com/asrock-ion-330-and-pegatron-cape-7-nvidia-ion-nettops-2942244/)

So I guess I am looking about a month or two too soon for what I want. ;)
