### Post by mark2741 on 2010-12-15
I'm in the same boat as you, but I have tried all of the above in the past except for Skrooge.

There are 2 requirements for me for a program:

1. Supports Online Banking
2. Has Bill tracking capability (and preferrably online bill pay via Direct Connect with Online Banking)

Mint.com, to me, is completely useless as it doesn't even have a bill management/scheduled transactions capability. Do all of the web 2.0 people not have bills? : ) Quicken Online used to have it and of course when they discontinued that everyone assumed Mint would get it but alas, no...

KmyMoney2 and MoneyDance are both the best I've found for linux. KmyMoney2 is really quite slick. MoneyDance is nice but not free. Neither of them, unfortunately, interfaces 100% properly with my bank via Direct Connect, but they are 95% as good as Quicken 2010, imo.

Gnucash is cool if you're an accountant or understand (or want to understand) accounting terms and double-entry in practice. Most of us don't. I tried SQLLedger years ago and it was a bit bulky for what I needed, and like Skrooge, doesn't support online banking. 

All that said...the state of personal finance apps on linux is light years ahead of what it was just a few years ago, when none other than MoneyDance supported online banking (gnucash allegedly did but it was a hack job to get it to work).
