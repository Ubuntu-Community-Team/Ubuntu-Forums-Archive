### Post by aysiu on 2010-01-31
I don't like the idea of saying that some sites are safe and others are not safe. There are varying degrees of safety. For example, for years, gnome-look.org was a "safe" place to find themes. Then someone uploaded a malicious .deb masquerading as a theme, and people realized "Hey, anyone can upload a 'theme' to gnome-look.org."

I believe GetDeb is "safe" in the sense that the people who created it and who maintain it are not malicious individuals, and they do not **intend** to host malware. At the same time, I don't know what kind of vetting process uploaded packages get on GetDeb. How vigorously is the code audited? Who has access to uploading .deb files? How secure is the server the .debs are hosted on?

It's even possible (though not likely) that an official Ubuntu repository could get compromised somehow.

Just keep in mind that software is developed, maintained, and distributed by human beings using tools (machines) created by other human beings, and humans are fallible. Nothing can ever be 100% trustworthy. You just play the odds (using common sense) and hope for the best (always make backups).
