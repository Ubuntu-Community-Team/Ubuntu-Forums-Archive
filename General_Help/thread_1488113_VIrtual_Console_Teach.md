### Post by angry_johnnie on 2010-05-19
if you mean the virtual terminals that are accessed by pressing ctrl+alt+fx, then [read]("http://www.tuxfiles.org/linuxhelp/shell.html").

for the most part, a virtual terminal is useful when the gui or a part of the gui starts acting up, when your guy freezes or becomes unresponsive.

one can do pretty much everything on a virtual terminal, from playing music, browsing the web, downloading torrent files, using an instant messaging program, or editing image or text files.

vlc can even play video on a virtual terminal (it looks rather ugly, but it's a lot of fun :p).

you have six virtual terminals to play around with, and you won't know what they're for unless you use them.  so, just try. :)
