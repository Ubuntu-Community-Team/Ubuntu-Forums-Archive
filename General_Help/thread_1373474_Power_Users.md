### Post by cariboo on 2010-01-05
There a lot of people that have used Linux for a long time, that are happy with the way the distro they use works, and they see Ubuntu as dumbing Linux down. As with any thing, when someone discovers something new they feel everyone should do the same as them, this leads to what is called the fanboi mentality, where a new user tries to convert everyone to their way of thinking. The general love-fest around Ubuntu can get quite annoying at times, especially with some of the outrageous claims that have been made.

All Linux distributions are the same under the gui interfaces. The main difference is the package management RedHat, OpenSUSE and others use rpm, while all the Debian based distros use apt.

You can do anything with Ubuntu, that you can do with any other distributions.

My personal feeling is that you should use what works for you no matter where the OS comes from.
