### Post by ahallubuntu on 2013-01-01
To rule out software, reboot the computer and enter BIOS Setup (not sure the make and model - on a Dell you hit F2, on HP hit Esc, on many other computers you hit the "Del" key or something else).  Does the screen have the same problem there?  (start using the arrow keys to move around the screen without changing anything).  If so, it's a hardware problem for sure - but it sounds like it anyway if an external monitor works fine.

If it's an older laptop (3+ years old or so), it's probably an LCD screen with a CCFL backlight inside.  In that case, two things possibly wrong: the inverter that powers the backlight has failed or the backlight inside the screen has failed (or is failing).  The inverter may be pretty easy and inexpensive to fix.  You might find a replacement for about $10 USD on eBay.  If it's the backlight itself, you're probably going to want to get a replacement screen - more like $80 USD on eBay or so.  Still doable to do yourself, probably.  Depends on make/model of laptop.  Look on YouTube for tutorials to replace a laptop screen - lots of examples there.

If it's a newer laptop that's an LED screen, there is no inverter or backlight, so you can disregard the paragraph above.
