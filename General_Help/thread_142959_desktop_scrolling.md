### Post by magnusbb on 2006-03-11
Yes, it is possible. We discussed this in another thread in this forum yesterday.

With a little assistance from a nice program called... Brightside, this is possible in GNOME. Yes, just install it through Synaptic and find its configuration in System -> Preferences -> Screen Actions.

Now you can drag windows from a workspace to another, and also get the "magic edges" (actions when you move your cursor to the edges of the screen), known from OS X.

Here's a link to more information:
[http://catmur.co.uk/~ed/main/brightside/](http://catmur.co.uk/~ed/main/brightside/)

In KDE there is a good feature where you can use the scroll wheel on your mouse between the different desktops - that's a good feature, unfortunately impossible in GNOME (Metacity).

But Brightside should be your best bet.
