### Post by taurus on 2006-03-08
All you need is a nice config file to include whatever you want in it.  Look at its website and see which one you like and save it to your home directory as ~/.conkyrc.  Here is the site [http://conky.sourceforge.net/](http://conky.sourceforge.net/).

### Post by towsonu2003 on 2006-03-08
conky works with variables put in ~/.conkyrc (/home/yourusername/.conkyrc, it's a text file)
There is a forum post around here that have a couple of screen shots and sample .conkyrc files that you can study, mine included (search for "conky" in forum thread titles). ```
man conky
``` will be very helpful to you during that study, as it explains the meaning of the variables that you will encounter. expect to invest some effort to come up with your own nice .conkyrc file. you can launch conky by typing alt+f2 than conky, that's easier to do, in my setup at least.
