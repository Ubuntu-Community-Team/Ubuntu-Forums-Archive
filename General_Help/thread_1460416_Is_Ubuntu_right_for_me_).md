### Post by envalin on 2010-04-22
What I would recommend is actually three partitions, in your case. I'm just starting into an engineering field, and (k)ubuntu, for me, is great for all the programming I do (I find Kubuntu to be a slightly nicer interface :) ).

That said, why three? One for Windows 7, one for Kubuntu, and one as a documents dump. You can just read the Windows partition from Ubuntu, but Windows cannot read EXT4 yet. So save all your documents on either Window's side or put the Home folder on a separate partition (as NTFS) in the middle and you're all set to go. This has worked out great for me in a few ways: putting documents and programs on separate partitions has some speed benefits, and if you ever need to wipe out/upgrade an OS, you will never lose your documents because the OS and documents are separated. If you start playing around with Ubuntu a little too much, you'll see what I mean ;-)

But yes, use Ubuntu/Kubuntu. Experiment now, there's no better time to do it.

### Post by envalin on 2010-04-22
You can, but like I said, slight speed differences in separating programs from documents on different partitions.
