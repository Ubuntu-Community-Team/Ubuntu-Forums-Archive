So what you're trying to do here is impress upon your students that Linux, and especially Ubuntu, is so wonderful that you're wanting to give them an actual branded version in the Ubuntu case. Even though you appear to have no problem burning the other CDRs, you don't want to leave such a cheap impression in their minds about Linux. Moreover, you sort of sound like, as a professor, you don't have the time to do this, but yet you would wait so long for them to arrive, regardless.

If it were me in your shoes, with money being no option, I would upload an Ubuntu image to one of these websites that sends you ready-to-go, silkscreened CDRs with your logo on them. I would not make it look version-specific but just say Ubuntu on them. These can arrive in perhaps a week turnaround time. When they arrive, I would spend the time to burn the CDRs. Then, place them in clear jewel boxes. When the young students receive these, they won't know whether they were sent from Canonical or not unless they're fairly experienced with this, already.

Yeah, it's kind of an odd pickle. It's not like you can actually pay Canonical to ship you more CDRs, or run down to the local Wal*Mart or BestBuy product store to get these, or get them online with the Canonical branding on them. You have to deal with this your own way or try to apply to "ShipIt" on the Ubuntu site and wait this thing out.

Oh, and I would not recommend sending Canonical a complaint letter. I wouldn't break the spell. The company appears to me to be working under some kind of magical trance, like elves or something, giving out free CDs for the good of humanity. Good God, man -- I wouldn't want to tear the very fabric of the space-time continuum. :shock:
