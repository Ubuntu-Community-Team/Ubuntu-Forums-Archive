In any case here's the difference:
Gnome: Pressing, but not holding, super alone initiates the resizing mode. It ends if I click the mouse. The side being resized depends on which direction I move the mouse after pressing super.

Xfce: Pressing (and holding) super + left click initiates resizing mode. It ends when I release either one of the two. I usually resize by dragging mouse button to the direction I wish. This way is much more intuitive than the one in gnome.
