### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-09-10
Just to clarify, you see the name of your network/SSID? Or the name of the wireless card?

Usually if there's a wireless card listed and greyed out, it means that you need firmware for it. If it's a Broadcom wireless card, you can just plug in an Ethernet cable to your router (to get a connection) and go to System > Administration > Additional Drivers*. You'll be offered a download.

Also, just wondering why you're using Ubuntu 10.04 as a new user? It's getting quite old now; Ubuntu 11.04 is the current version, and 11.10 will be out soon. There are two Ubuntu releases each year.

* I'm not entirely sure if it was called Additional Drivers in Ubuntu 10.04; it might have been called Restricted Drivers or something similar. You should be able to find it.
