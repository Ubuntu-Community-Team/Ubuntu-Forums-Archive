### Post by halibaitor on 2011-08-14
The best sharpening tool in Gimp or PhotoShop is called _Unsharp Mask_.  Play with it for a bit, and you'll figure out how to achieve maximum sharpness without introducing artifacts. (hint: it's different for each photo you work on)

If your problem is actually caused by a fingerprint on the lens, then your biggest problem is probably a lack of contrast due to "lens flare".  You should probably fix the lens flare issues prior to working on the sharpening.  Under _Colors_, click on _Levels_ and adjust the black point and white point using the end sliders.   Adjusting the middle slider makes the photo look darker or lighter without losing detail in the light or dark parts of the photo.  Play with it and you'll see how it works.  Yes, I know, there is a tool to adjust contrast, but using that one will cost you the detail in the finished work.

Gimp is free, and will do almost anything you want or can think of.  It just takes a while to learn how to use it.  :)

A word of caution.  NEVER work on your original image file.  If you mess it up by mistake, you can't go back again.
