### Post by Tiddens on 2009-08-24
I purchased three Bluetooth headsets to work with Ubuntu 9.04 with a PC with an Atom processor and an internal Billington Bluetooth module GUBTCR42M, with the following results:
JABRA BT4010 - pairs and works fine with Skype.
Motorola HS850 - pairs but won't work with Skype ("Problem with audio playback").
Plantronics Explorer 220 - pairs but won't work with Skype ("Problem with audio playback").
 
I also have been trying to get an HP All-In-One C5550 printer that came with a Bluetooth USB stick to work.  It will pair with the Billington Bluetooth module, and with the TRENDnet TRW-105UB noted below, but it won't communicate and print anything...
 
I also removed the Billington Bluetooth module and tried the following three Bluetooth USB sticks, with the following results:
 
TRENDnet TBW-106UB - Bluetooth icon doesn't show up on PC
Cirago BTA-6130 - Icon shows up, but won't pair
TRENDnet TBW-105UB - Paired with HP printer and JABRA headset
 
Seems like there should be a central repository for Ubuntu/Linux/Bluez Bluetooth device compatibility reports (although, even better, would be if all the devices would work, or at least more than 1 out of 3 - and we expect the average user to use Linux and Ubuntu?)!
