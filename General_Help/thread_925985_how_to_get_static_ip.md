### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-21
You need to either pay your ISP to assign you a static IP, or use one of the free services that redirects a static IP to your dynamic IP.

Tough decision, right? :)

I use [no-ip]("http://www.no-ip.com/").  It does the job, and the biggest downside is that they send you an email every 30 days or so to confirm that you're still using your account.
