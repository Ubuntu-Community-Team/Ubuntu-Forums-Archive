### Post by GabFromDenver on 2009-12-30
Hi all!

I'm posting a tip that I searched for weeks. My Ubuntu 9.04 has been "getting stuck" randomly (from within a few minutes to several hours) for a while, and I pulled my hair trying to understand what was going on... Basically, the computer would suddenly become unresponsive (keyboard, mouse, sound, ...), completely frozen. The only option was to reboot. It happened with pretty much all users, at any time, for no apparent reason. Again, I googled for "Ubuntu getting stuck" and all, but couldn't find anything. I tried a whole bunch of stuffs to identify whether the problem was hardware or software, and what component(s) was screwing up... Nothing until the day I finally figured it out: It seems that it is because the user's partition (/home) is too full (99%). BTW, the file system was ext3. 
I moved a bunch of data to another partition, and since then, no frozen computer!! :)
I apologize for all the pieces of software I suspected, and all the swearing I did! :p 
I hope this'll help others.

Cheers

Gabriel
