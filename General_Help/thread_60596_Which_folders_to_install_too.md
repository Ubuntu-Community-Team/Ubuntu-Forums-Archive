For starters I would recommend that you just use your /home to download and so on. You can make another folder within it if you like. That isn't the traditional place that programs installed by the package manager (aptitude, synaptic, apt-get) go, but that is usually taken care of automatically when things are installed so you shouldn't need to worry about it, especially while you are just getting your feet wet. In fact, to make your life easier I recommend making a new folder in your /home for each program you download/install--give it a name related to the program itself.

For the long answer and the full story about the Linux filesystem take a look here: [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Linux-Filesystem-Hierarchy/html/](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Linux-Filesystem-Hierarchy/html/)
At a minimum I suggest you read about the /home, /usr and / directories.
