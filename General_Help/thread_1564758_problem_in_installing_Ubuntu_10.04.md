### Post by -kg- on 2010-09-04
I have an apology to make...for not thinking.

I was reviewing this thread and realized that the available distro was version 10.04[COLOR="Red"]**_.1_**[/COLOR], which is an updated version of Lucid.  You likely downloaded the 10.04 version (without the .1).

I downloaded the same version back a few weeks after it was released (it was a good download and is what I installed this with), so I pulled my .iso up and checked the md5 sum against what you posted.  It matched.

What this means is that it should have worked for you, since the checksum matched what you downloaded.  All the ".1" release is is Lucid with the updates up to the date of that release, and I'm already at that level, since I've applied all updates as they came out.

Varunendra is likely right that there may be hardware issues with your computer.  Further troubleshooting may be necessary to find out why you can boot no further than the boot splash screen.

The problem could be anything from a bad burn on the CD (A good checksum doesn't mean the CD will burn right.  Did you burn it at the slowest speed possible?  Did the burn confirm? It is also possible that you just had a bad CD and the next one you burn will be good.) to a misconfiguration for your system (for instance, some computers require "noacpi" to boot correctly) to (Heaven forfend) hardware problems.

Why is it that you "hate Windows"?  Was that a general statement, or has it been misbehaving badly lately?  If it has been misbehaving, it might be due to hardware that has malfunctioned.

If your download still doesn't correct the problem and you return here, we will need to know your hardware configuration so we know what you're dealing with.  If you are successful, congratulations!  We will always be here for further support with any issues you may have.
