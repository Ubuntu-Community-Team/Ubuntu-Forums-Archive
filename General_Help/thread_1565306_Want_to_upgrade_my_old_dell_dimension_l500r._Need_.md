### Post by RedRat on 2010-08-31
Hate to say this but that computer is 10 years old. Installing more RAM and graphics cards are the least of your problems. You ought to consider a new motherboard and all that goes with it, e.g., a new power supply. Frankly, I would advise against putting any money into that computer, it will be money down a rat hole at this point. 

You might be able to pick up an inexpensive motherboard and cpu combo, but you may also need a new power supply since the newer boards use a 24 pin power plug on the board, those older computers used a single 2 pin type. The reason I am pushing the motherboard is that most graphic cards today are PCI and I doubt that your board has one. Also, a new board gives you more RAM than that board.

Do you have any used computer shops near by, perhaps a shop that repairs computers. I would suggest getting a machine that is only a few years old and upgrading that. I think that would be cheaper and you get a reliable machine. Many businesses swap out computers after about 3-5 years, especially large corporations. Any in your vicinity. 

If it is game playing you are interested in, then you might be better off sticking with Windows.

Well the first thing to aim for is a salvage MB, but again you might find that it might not fit the Dell, I had an old Dell but I think it took the ATX MBs--been some time ago. Try to salvage a case too, then maybe a power supply. Back then 10 years ago, 250 to 350 watts was considered big, today, you need at least 550 watts to power the numerous USBs and high end graphics cards. 

Keep in mind that probably the first thing to crap out in a computer is the graphics card. I have gone through 3 of them. Sometimes you get a warning and sometimes not. The next thing to look into is the on board little CMOS battery (in the older machines the battery could be larger about the size of a current 9 volt, but some more recent ones are the small coin sized types. 

Definitely look for a MB as first priority (hopefully with a cpu on board). An older MB without a cpu may pose a problem since finding a current "old fashioned" cpu may be difficult. Make sure the Power Supply is adequate, 250 to 350 watts. Also make sure the MB has PCI slots for the graphics cards you make want to get. Shouldn't be a big problem with boards that are 5-6 years old, gets a bit touchy for those pushing 10 years. 

It's good experience to get in root around the innards of a computer, you will find that they are not that complicated. 

Well good luck on your project, sounds like it will be fun.
