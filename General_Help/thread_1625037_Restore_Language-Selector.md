### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2010-11-19
OK, I thought about this. Language-selector is connected to the User's Desktop file. So, I added a user, discovered I had Language-selector back up. I deleted the bad User account and put them back in with their old Log-in Account name after carefully preserving the documents they had on their Desktop.

DON'T change the text-format option In the multi-tab Language-selector panel before you change System-wide language in the first tab for the GUI change is what I have learned.

To change the GUI language System-wide, I lose the Shut-down panel in top menu. There is a fix for that using Terminal, but it is tricky when you have a foreign-language keyboard. It is better to find a step-by-step on restoring Top Panel from a web page and copy and paste the commands into Terminal.
