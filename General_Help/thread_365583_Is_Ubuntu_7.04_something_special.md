### Post by hikaricore on 2007-02-20
Kubuntu and Ubuntu are essentially the same, as in the same basic operating system.  The difference is Kubuntu uses KDE as it's window manager, and Ubuntu uses Gnome.  The differences between the two are too many to name, I suggest looking at screenshots and searching the forums/google for info on KDE.  Or better yet, download a Kubuntu edgy live cd, burn it and play around on it see if you like it.

You'll find that just about every program that works in gnome (very few exceptions and for sometimes avoidable reasons) will run in KDE albeit sometimes a hair slower, and vise versa.  Kubuntu also ships with a different set of default applications that in my expericene run more smoothly than Gnomes.  Anyway like I said I won't be able to name the number of differences and similarities in my post just search around and try it out, besides you don't have to install kubuntu to test the live cd content.


I use Ubuntu at home and Kubuntu at my office personally.

Here's my work desktop, as you can see the colors can be changed to your liking as well as nearly every other aspect of your desktop, more easily than in gnome in most cases.
