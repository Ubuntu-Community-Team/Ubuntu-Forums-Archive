### Post by srs5694 on 2010-09-20
> **samb0057 said:**
> Your only chance would be to bring it to a company that does data recovery, but that will cost you. It's not something you can do yourself.

IMHO, this is overly pessimistic; however, it really does depend on exactly what function numbness05 was using. If it was a simple repartitioning, [TestDisk](http://www.cgsecurity.org/wiki/TestDisk) will probably recover the disk. If it was creating a new filesystem, [PhotoRec](http://www.cgsecurity.org/wiki/PhotoRec) stands a good chance of recovering many, and perhaps most, of the original files, but probably not their filenames or the original directory structure. If it was a secure erase function or secure filesystem creation that writes 0s to the drive, then it'll be nearly impossible to recover the data unless you've got *very* sophisticated tools.
