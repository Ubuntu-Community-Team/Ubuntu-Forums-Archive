### Post by taylonr on 2007-02-09
I have a little bit of Raid experience, and Ubuntu is on my 2nd HDD.  So I have just enough knowledge to be dangerous.

I would be willing to bet that you didn't wipe your first HDD, but might have destroyed the Master Boot Record (MBR).

If it is a windows 1st disk, put in the install CD and go until you can get a recovery console.  Once you get there I think the command you want is fixmbr and it should fix the mbr and you could be up and running.
