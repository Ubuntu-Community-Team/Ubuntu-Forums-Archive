### Post by viper250 on 2010-08-07
if you live in even a slightly dusty environment the cpu fan picks up a lot of dust that gets trapped in the cooling fins these need blown out with canned air I recommend doing this about once a month to keep them clear.
also there are cooling pads you can get for your laptop these have cooling fans that are powered by the usb port, and there are others that raise the laptop up a bit so it can get better air flow as well.

toshibas and acer laptops tend to overheat easily so the above tip is of importance 
the shut down is the computers bios shutting the system down before heat damage can occur.

oh one other tip! inspect your battery once a week if they leak any fluid you have a short time to back up your data because the fluid and gasses that leak from the battery are very caustic. (Ive been a repair tech for many years and I've seen a lot of parts scrapped due to battery damage)

### Post by elricscripts on 2010-08-10
This used to happen in my HP laptops.  The reason why it is more apparent in Ubuntu is because Windows V/7 has powersafe limiting processor power.  You probably noticed your laptop is running allot faster in Ubuntu.

You can do this in Ubuntu as well, just download the temperature monitor and cpu limiter.  You can add them to your panel to see the heat and change limit.
