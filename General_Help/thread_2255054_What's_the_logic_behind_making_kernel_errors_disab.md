### Post by ronniepinsky on 2014-12-01
If someone has dropped to the console then they generally have a failure somewhere that they are desperately trying to fix.  Kernel errors interrupt the console and prevent autocomplete from working.  Furthermore, the errors prevent even reading the output from ls to manually type a file name.  The only option is to have a photographic memory or take a picture of the screen.  No other Unix-like systems behave in this way.  Otherwise they would be impossible to fix when things really go wrong.  Why is this an added punishment - I mean feature?  Who's idea was this?
