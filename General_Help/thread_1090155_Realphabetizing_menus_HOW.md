### Post by T.J. on 2009-03-08
Sorry to say that GNOME menus are a pain! I know of no way to do what you ask, short of editing the XML menu files by hand, and that is beyond the knowledge of the average user.

Sorry if this sounds like flamebait, but that had been a sore point with GNOME users for years, one (among many) that the GNOME Project has chosen to consistently ignore.  The excuse that is commonly used by the GNOME dev team is that you don't need to, or that having the ability to make changes to GNOME's internal settings would confuse the average person and is counterproductive.

Alacarte, in my opinion, is nothing more than an inexcusable dirty patch for the fact that GNOME Project has not provided a decent menu editor since the introduction of GNOME 2.
