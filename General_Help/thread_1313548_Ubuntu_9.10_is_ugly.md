### Post by peakpc on 2009-11-03
I'm not a big fan of brown, I changed the xsplash and GDM backgrounds by replacing the scaled .jpgs in /usr/share/images/xsplash folder with a set of my own star fields (sized with gimp) it goes well with the cosmos timed backgrounds in karmic and gives a more seemless boot IMHO, with no brown. I use a customised "glossy P" theme for a nice aqua look, and a custom (gimp) panel background to finish the blue look.  None of this stuff is hard, and you get to change just about anything (unlike MS) so experiment and find what is distinctly you!

### Post by ranch hand on 2009-11-03
Take a look in you /usr/share/backgrounds file for some images that you can use for wallpaper or scale for xsplash and menu images.  Some of them are pretty nice.

And what peakpc was saying about the xsplash is a good thing to do.  I use the same image for my menu, xsplash, GDM and wallpaper.  The transition, if you get the resolution and size right, from login in to wallpaper is seamless.

With wallpaper and the startup fixed you can pretty much do the rest in the properties for the panels and by clicking "customize" in Appearance>themes.
