### Post by BobJam on 2009-09-10
I have the Dell Inspiron 15n and am satisfied with it.  Got it about a month ago.

There are two things you should realize about it though.

1.  It** DOES NOT** have a caps lock LED.

2.  It **DOES NOT** have an HDD activity LED.

However, there are two pretty slick Ubuntu software packages that remedy this shortcoming. (And I can give you the info on them if you want it . . . just am too lazy to look it up right now).

So, other than these two, what I would consider minor flaws, it's a pretty slick machine.

A coupla' things to keep in mind when making a computer purchase.

If you're pretty confident of your own troubleshooting skills, then I wouldn't bother too much considering support ranking (and, yes, Dell has declined in that arena).  However, if you're not very confident and likely will be depending on support, then the support ranking/reputation is important to consider.  The more you're confident of your skills, the cheaper the machine you can get (up to a point).

Dell is pretty good on reliability out of the box, but of course they're pretty shoddy on service.

(Oh . . . while I'm thinking of it . . . I had expected the machine to be laden with a lot of Dell crapware . . . the usual Dell marketing stuff that you spend the first few hours getting off your machine.  But with the Ubuntu distro preinstalled, the only Dell crapware I had was the Dell recovery partition . . . which is kind of useless considering you can do exactly the same thing with a Live CD.  So I was pleasantly surprised that the thing wasn't infused with a bunch of Dell crapware.  I guess that only happens on Windows preinstalls).

The next consideration is drawing conclusions from opinions on manufacturer forums.  Careful there.  People don't visit forums just to say "Atta boy".  They go to forums because they have a problem.  So forums are going to be skewed.  You might get a good idea about the problems that frequently occur with that model, but beyond that I wouldn't draw any conclusion.  A lot of people rant because they got a lemon.  You can get a lemon with any brand, even the best (whatever you consider that to be).

With Dell, your risk of the service being "lemonny" is high, but your risk of poor machine reliability is lower (and I'm sure some would argue that point, so you'll have to decide for yourself).

Good luck with your purchase, whatever you decide.
