### Post by XProflmfao on 2009-07-06
Hey everyone, I'm having problems with my Ubuntu sound. I just downloaded Ubuntu yesterday, and everything was going well. I was watching a video online, when I realized that the laptop speakers were making sound as well as my headphones! It was a really weird experience (for me) since in Windows you would just plug in a headphone and the laptop speakers would go out. Again, I'm a newbie, so I haven't really gotten around the Ubuntu desktop thing much. I tried to go to system>preferences>sound, but there were no options to make only the headphone create sound. I clicked on the sound button in the upper panel, and I did find an option that stopped the microphone from creating sound, but there were no options to stop the laptop speakers from creating sound. Can someone help me, any help would be great, thanks in advance!

Update: Actually I found a solution already! By muting the volume panel that says "Front", I was able to hear from only my headphone, then for some reason after muting Front, I had to control the volume using Front for my headphone. Weird.... However, it seems that I can't control the microphone sound by just clicking on the sound button and then controlling it from there. I have to go to Front and control it there, even though the front has been muted....

### Post by dark.generic on 2009-07-06
Hehe i also found that a bit irritating

But luckely found a easy and simple solution.

Just click on the speaker icon located top right. then click on "Volume Control" then on the tab labeled "Switches" and tick the box next to "Headphone Jack Sense."

hope this helps
