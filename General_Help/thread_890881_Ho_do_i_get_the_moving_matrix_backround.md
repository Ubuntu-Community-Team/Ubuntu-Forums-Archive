### Post by Mgiacchetti on 2008-08-16
Well, lets go through the steps for making the screensaver become a "background"

1 disable (or otherwise kill) Nautilus's ability to draw the desktop (and all the icons along with it)
2 run whatever program noted in your situation (xwinwrap for example) to get the screensaver as a "background"

ok

as you can see, the first step is to get rid of your desktop and icons, right click windows, basically everything desktop related (even  screenlets get hidden)

this would solve why part of your problem exists

the second step requires a lot of processor usage (i have a 2.2 ghz, 512 ram and it eats up 85-90% of it) so that would solve the other part of the problem.
