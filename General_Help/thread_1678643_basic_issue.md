My computer randomly shuts down at irregular intervals.  Starts back up fine. never shows any error message.

### Post by restorator on 2011-01-30
Try cleaning it out, literally. It is very possibly an overheating problem. If its a desktop, turn of the machine and open the cover and check for dust and buildup inside the case. Get a can of compressed air and blow it all out. If its a laptop, you probably don't need to open the case, but turn off the computer and blow the compressed air through the vents to clean them.

### Post by Jummel on 2011-01-30
I agree with restorator. Also go into the BIOS and turn on all the warnings/ alarms. If you have a proper air compressor, they are are more effective at cleaning out your machine than just a can. Just never ever run any type of compressed air through a fan, you could over rev the fan.

Other things that can cause your computer to shutdown are power issues. Not just bad AC, but also a bad or poorly speced power supply. If you have a small power supply and a few drives, then when a drive or a fan spins up, you can exceed your power supply. The surge power required by a component is generally greater than it's rated draw, and some power supplies take a long time to increase their output, leaving a large gap between supply and demand.
