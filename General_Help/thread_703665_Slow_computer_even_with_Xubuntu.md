### Post by hilltop_yodeler on 2008-02-22
CrunchBang Linux is something that you should really look at.  I am running it right now on two different old laptops (a PII and a PIII), both have very limited resources.  CrunchBang runs great on both machines and is based on Ubuntu 7.10, so you get all of the other wonderful attributes of having the newer OS, such as auto-mounting of external media, wifi, etc.  It uses the OpenBox window manager, and it is highly configurable.  It runs a little bit slow on my PII, but so does Damn Small Linux and Puppy.

[http://crunchbang.org/projects/linux/](http://crunchbang.org/projects/linux/)
