### Post by danwood76 on 2008-07-23
You can buy dust gaurds which have removable filters you stick on the fronts of all the fans going in.
They can be a bit funny to fir to a pre built case though.

I bought my case for the purpose of keeping dust to a minimum and it has an open front of which all my fans are covered by dust gaurds.
I tend to hoover them once a month and it keeps my case quite clean though you will never stop all the dust.

One good thing to do is keep your house very clean and dont smoke around your PC as that increases the dust aswell.
If theres less dust in your house there will be less in your PC.

### Post by tamoneya on 2008-07-23
dust guards help but you have to consider that any guard or barrier you put in the way will decrease airflow and it just means that the dust accumulates on the guard instead of the CPU.  This makes it easier to clean but as it accumulates you get even less airflow.  Personally I just clean my computers out every couple of months.

Keeping your computer area as dust free as possible is also a good idea but not always possible.

### Post by danwood76 on 2008-07-24
With regards to static I think you are being a bit OTT.
If you're touching the side of your case (leaning on it or with your hand) and your hand is touching the nozzle the static is discharged through you and is no risk to your PC.

I have been cleaning and repairing PCs for years and the hoover is one of my most useful tools.
I have never had anything break due to static, if your extra nervous about static get an earth wrist strap but to be honest just touching the case is enough.
