### Post by booe on 2007-03-02
Most graphics cards nowadays have 2 video output ports.

What you're looking for is any card that is advertised with "2x DVI ports" or "1x DSUB **and** 1x DVI".

Getting 2x DVI is **HIGHLY RECOMMENDED** over any DSUB ports. The reason is that DVI = digital and DSUB = analog.

This all of course depends on what monitors you have... but a lot of 2x DVI graphics cards also come with a pair of DVI -> DSUB connectors :)

You can use newegg.com to find any cards with a certain number/type of outputs. Just select the "Home >  Video Cards & Video Devices > Video Cards" area and then click "Advanced Search" on the top left hand side. You will now see a whole bunch of dropdown boxes where you can specify what sort of card you're looking for. If you want dual DVI, just select "2" under the "DVI" dropdown box.
