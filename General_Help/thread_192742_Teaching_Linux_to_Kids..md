### Post by tribaal on 2006-06-09
No one should ever be punished for using the command line! Of course, no one should have enough system rights to screw anything up, especially a kid :)

My take on the situation would be to create separate accounts for the family members (guess you did that already), and make sure the system rights a null for kids and parents.

Then just go tell her parents that no matter what she does, she cannot break anything up (yes ma'am, that includes playing with the command line). 

Try to put you best "IT expert face"  on, kids are good with computers, but parents usually... are better with humans :)

### Post by steve196 on 2006-06-09
I agree, that handing out punishments for doing something on a computer (except for willfully destroying something) will prevent kids from experimenting  and therefore from learning. Additionally the command prompt is THE most important tool in linux and therefore learning linux has to include it.
By default, the kids have write permission only to their home folder, and they don´t have sudo unless their account is the first one you created. So whatever desaster they may cause is limited in scope, unless you grant them more rights.
I personally would ditch edubuntu in favor of one of the mainstream ubuntus. I just don´t see its advantages, even for kids.

### Post by rplantz on 2006-06-09
I would try to provide her with her own computer. Perhaps an older used one if money is an issue. Then let her hack away to her heart's content.

As for books, it's hard to tell. I was a CS professor for 21 years, and I learned that we all have different learning styles. I would make a variety available for her. You might check out a few from the library and let her look through them. If she takes to some of them, then you know what to buy.

I don't think it's a good idea to try to find things "appropriate for her age." Another thing I learned from my CS teaching experience is that we don't know very much about teaching CS yet. It doesn't sound like she will loose interest because things are too advanced. She will just set the advanced thing aside and find something else about the computer that interests her.
