### Post by Parkotron on 2006-04-25
Personally, I prefer abcde. It's a command line ripper, so it's not for those who want pretty progress bars, but it's remarkably flexible. It's ideal for those who want a lot of control over the ripping and tagging process, but it does require mucking around in a config file. 

abcde also has three features that I haven't seen in any other ripper:
[LIST]
[*]It allows you to rip to multiple formats at once.
[*]You can set the starting count for numbering tracks. (I like to number multidisc albums as 101, 102, 103, ... 201, 202, 203, ...)
[*]It doesn't store genre information (after a little bit of hacking on my part. I hate genres!)
[/LIST]

I'm not even one of those "konsole über alles" types, either. I just haven't found anything else as powerful.
