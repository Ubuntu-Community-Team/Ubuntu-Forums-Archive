### Post by kpkeerthi on 2008-03-28
Ubuntu has 6 months release cycle. So you are stuck with a software for six months. Only security patches and critical fixes get added to the repo. 

If you need latest and greatest, you need to compile from source or find an external repo or wait for someone to backport to the ubuntu backports repo.

### Post by handaxe on 2008-03-28
The next version of Ubuntu - Hardy Heron - is less than a month away from release.  That surely will help you.

But for reasons of testing and stability, Hardy will not have the VERY latest software, as the devs freeze the version some time before release. For good reason - go to [https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/) and look at with what they have their hands full in any case.

Simply put, if you want the latest and greatest, then compile your own. It really is not that hard (tho' you might beg to differ at times :)) and you learn a lot.

The following site can also help [http://www.getdeb.net/](http://www.getdeb.net/) (tho' it is down as of writing..)

Some software sites also offer debs, or links to such. Greater risk there of course, as who knows what the software is. Generally most offers are honest stuff.
