### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-06-18
I have been buying most of my books as electronic copies for about 10 years now (I started using an eBook reader when they were much bulkier then my little kindle I use today).

As long as your original source is just text, you can convert that to whatever format you my need as time goes on.  If you have images as part of the book, that means at least an HTML style of storage.

My electronic library is about 600 books.  Because they are mostly text, the files are really quite small. So far I have not needed to use anything larger than a CD to back them up.  Whenever I have purchased additional books, I burn the **whole** library to a CD.  Someday I will need to use a DVD, and it doubt I will live long enough to ever need a bluray to backup the library (my music files are another story, that takes 2 blurays to back then up).

Lastly - if you have not already found it, Project Gutenberg offers a large number of classic books that are in the public domain. These are free and include many of the classic I didn't read in high school because I was reading 2 SciFi novels a week instead.
