### Post by P4man on 2009-09-22
It almost certainly needs proprietary software if its a USB connected VoIP device, and that software is equally certainly not gonna be available for linux. i got a Skype USB dongle and cordless phone (normal POTS phone and skype phone), there is no way to get it working with linux (besides using windows in virtual box). Its recognized, its even seen as USB audio device, but without software to control it, initiate call, pick up, hang up, its useless.

If you want something like that to work with linux, it needs to be some standard device; a bluetooth headset uses some well defined protocols (like HSP,  A2DP) so it can be made to work with linux (requires a bluetooth stick on your PC), although even that, like I said, is far from easy to achieve

You can always contact the manufacturer and ask, but I'll bet $50 it won't work with linux and $5 it won't even work with Mac.

### Post by P4man on 2009-09-22
> **PsychedelicWonders said:**
> I plan on starting to use Skype, but it needs a special dongle & phone?

Typically, skype runs on a computer, and will use whatever you have on your computer (wired headset, mic+speakers, whatever). That is no problem, not with linux either.

If you want a cordless headset solution, you could consider a bluetooth headset and bluetooth dongle for your computer. That probably works with skype on linux, but I promise you you'll have some issues getting it to work.

What I have is something else still, its a normal cordless (dect) phone, that also supports skype, but needs to connect to the computer for that, using an USB dongle. Works fine in windows, does not work in linux.

Then you have  "computer-less" solutions, there are skype phones available that work independent of your pc. They connect to your wireless network( btw, Ive tried one from belkin and it sucked sound quality wise). Some of those need a basestation, others dont, but any of those would "work" regardless of OS, as they share nothing with your computer.

I thought initially the model you where looking at was one of those, but using a base station and a bluetooth connection to the headset. In that case you could probably also pair that same headset to a bluetooth dongle on your pc. But if it needs to connect to a PC with USB, then Im pretty sure you won't be able to use it as skype phone on linux.
