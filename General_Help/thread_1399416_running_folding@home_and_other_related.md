### Post by audiomick on 2010-02-05
If it will be on a local network, you could look into getting into it for administration purposes with SSH. Although I don't know the details of how to do it, I know that this is not difficult, and that the Linux system as such is designed to allow that sort of thing.

Effectively, you can log into the machine from another, and then you can control it as if you were using a keyboard, mouse and monitor attached to the computer itself.

This would, I imagine, also be practical for retrieving the results of the program and sending them of, or do they do that themselves these days? My seti@home experience is a few years back now.
