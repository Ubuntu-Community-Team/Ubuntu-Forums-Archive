### Post by Giblet5 on 2009-11-18
Linux doesn't care about the device as much as it cares about the processor.

What CPU does your GPS use? StrongARM? There's a kernel for that.

You folks want to buy a book: "Building Embedded Linux Systems" by O'Reilly and associates. It discusses how to build these things from the ground up, or how to put your solution on existing devices.

I'd kind of agree on the "Why on a GPS?" thing: the input capabilities are extremely limited meaning you'd have to power the USB interface to use external bling like a keyboard. That'll kill your battery time...
