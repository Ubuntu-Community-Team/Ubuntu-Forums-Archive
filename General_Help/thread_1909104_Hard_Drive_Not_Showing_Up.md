### Post by spacecheck on 2012-01-14
There are a number of things you could check. Since it is a laptop, perhaps recently mobile, you may wish to ensure the hard drive (hdd) is seated properly in its socket (while the device is turned off).

There is also a possibility, that the hdd has been damaged or has died. This can happen occasionally, whether by overheating, high g-force (dropping, jarring, etc.) age, or manufacturing defect. If the latter, hopefully it happens during the guarantee period.

Sometimes the BIOS can be changed, perhaps during online BIOS upgrade,  which may change the controller settings so the drive is not initialized.

Just a few things that come to mind off hand.

Good luck!

### Post by spacecheck on 2012-01-14
Not so fast. Remove all power (battery out) remove hdd, dvd, possibly keyboard and memory and let the device air dry slowly, perhaps with a small fan blowing at it, until you can be sure all of the components are completely dried out. Can take several days, depending upon how much water was involved and how far it seeped into the device's components.

If you are lucky, it may eventually start up again, if did not get shorted out while wet.

Good luck!
