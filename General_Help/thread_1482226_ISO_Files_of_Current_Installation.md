### Post by spydeyrch on 2010-05-13
> **Karmic Kiwi said:**
> Is it possible to create an ISO file of the current installation that I could burn to a CD/USB? I want to make a bootable disk that already has certain packages, drivers, etc. already on it.

[Here is a website]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/backup") that will show you how to create a personalized .iso of your current installation. You can then even make that .iso a live CD/usb and boot off of it and still have your apps.

The only thing I am not certain about is if it also keeps your files (pics, vids, docs,e tc). I would assume that no, it doesn't. Also, there is AptCD to make a list/cd of just your apps. Also, I seem to remember that there was something like Ubuntu Ubiquity, or something like that.

I haven't use any of them as of yet but looked into the same thing that you are.

-Spydey :guitar:

EDIT: After looking over the info a little more in depth, it does allow you to keep your personal files if you want.

> 
*Remastersys* makes a custom live/installer CD image out of your  existing installation. You have the option to do so just for your system  files or for also your personal preferences... or your entire  installation (of course, if you have a lot of personal files, the CD or  DVD image might be too big to be useful. Instructions [here]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/remastersys") on how to  use it.
