### Post by coypu76 on 2011-01-13
I'm not connected with OO or Libre Office, just an ordinary end user, however there is some good reason for the switch to Libre Office.

Oracle Corporation acquired Sun Microsystems, which owned Open Office.org

Oracle's management decided to try to move Open Office in different directions than the developers wanted to go, so most of the developers left the company and formed the Open Document Foundation as the parent organization of Libre Office with the aim of keeping an Open Source Office application Open.

Canonical wants to keep Ubuntu as open as is reasonably possible and moved to Libre Office.  There are some understandable logistical bumps in the road, some of which you have hit.  I know it must have been really inconvenient for you, and I really empathize with the trouble - but I hope it helps to know there's not some dark conspiracy behind the inconvenience.  It doesn't end the inconvenience but maybe it helps to explain it a bit.

Kind regards,
Coypu
Once again, I am just another end user like you - these are just opinions, not anything authoritative.

### Post by uRock on 2011-01-13
From your other thread, so others who may run into this problem can find the easy fix.
> **cariboo907 said:**
> Having the ppa enabled is why LibreOffice  overwrote OOo. LibreOffice is supposed to be in the Natty repositories  during alpha 2.

Anytime you have ppas enabled the version in the ppa will overwrite the  current version you have. The easiest way to remove a program installed  from a ppa is to use ppa-purge, the command would look like this for  LibreOffice:

```
sudo ppa-purge ppa:libreoffice/ppa
```the above command will also remove the ppa from your sources list.
