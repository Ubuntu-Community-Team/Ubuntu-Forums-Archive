Not. Helpful. At. All. 

If you have a widescreen laptop (1650x1080), you may find it useful to apply a vertical tile to two windows. This is what the wide XGA standard was intended to do -- have two correctly proportioned pages open side by side and fully visible on the screen. 

I am beta testing Windows 7 right now and am actually quite pleased with some of the window management features. Drag a window to the left side of the screen and it attaches there taking up exactly half the screen--same goes for the right side. Even better, there is a keyboard shortcut (Win+left or Win+right) that accomplishes this. I didn't realize how useful it is until I started using it. Unfortunately, I still can't figure out how to drag or resize windows without using the titlebar (GNOME has Alt-click + drag anywhere in the window).

As far as I know, there is no way to easily tile two windows vertically in GNOME without installing some kind of "helper". As mentioned, Compiz can probably do it, but do you really want those bugs?
