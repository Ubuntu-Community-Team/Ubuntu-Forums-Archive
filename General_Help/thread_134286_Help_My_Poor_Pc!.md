### Post by reynoldlariza on 2006-02-21
if both the red and green LEDs are ON, make sure that you didn't put in the IDE cables in reverse.
just remove everything, hdd,memory,vga,sndcard,moden,etc except for a motherboard and powersupply. then turn it ON. start from there..

generally , if you hear any beep, that means your motherboard is OK, otherwise it is NOT.

I have encountered this kind of problem many times in our company. And here's how I fixed it.

no need a monitor to find out.

IF YOU CAN TRANSFORM youself INTO AN ELETRONIC PC-TECHNICIAN then you can do the ff. otherwise replace the motherboard no matter how.

--
look at the capacitors on your motherboard. specifically those near the power-socket. (it is a group of colored wires, connected from power supply to motherboard). 

if you see any of em like a tank of water almost ready to explode due to pressure. then you'll really need to replace it.

for more info you may look at [http://www.badcaps.net/](http://www.badcaps.net/)
i found this site very useful for the job.
