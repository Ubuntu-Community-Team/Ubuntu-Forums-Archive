### Post by amohanty on 2008-04-29
Unless you are planning on running a high throughput database server, quite frankly this is way overkill. If you are you might want to look into buying a decent hardware RAID card and three other drives (preferably SCSI). For normal desktop use you are probably not going to get a big boost out of quad-code cpus. Also I think 1200 might be a bit ambitious for this beast :)

I would recommend - any intel or amd dual core with a decent L1/L2 cache combo.
And SLI based mobo - to ramp up your gaming experience with two mid-range cards instead of one monstrously priced card.
Two 160G SATA instead of 1 (can increase speed in some cases and also provides a [small] measure of safety) OR a single 10-15k scsi (for either the OS or data files - if you do IO intensive work like video editing) and a huge SATA OR a 160G SATA (that's a lot :) and a .5TB RAID 1 external solution (~450 USD).
One or two mid-range ATI/NVIDIA cards. Bleeding edge hardware usually has some compatibility issues in my experience.
24" monitor from Costco (best price I have been able to find - ever)

Of course the usual knick-knacks like 
- CD/DVD-RW
- any 5/7 channel creative sound card (on board usually sucks)
- CPU fan (zalmans are good)
- passive north-south bridge coolers (again zalman are good - for quiet PC)
- case with at least a 550 w PSU and two 120mm fan slots (antecs are pretty damn good)
- 120 mm fans (nexus are quite good and whisper quiet)
- compatible RAM (buying 4G at once will be steep, I would probably recommend buying two sticks of 1GB usually cheaper)

among other things :)
