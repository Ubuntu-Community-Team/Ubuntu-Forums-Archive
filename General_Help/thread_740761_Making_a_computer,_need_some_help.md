### Post by chirpilittle on 2008-03-31
Well if you need a fast computer regardless of operating system, I would suggest an Nvidia GeForce 8600GT (or better, depending on how much you're willing to spend)  as a graphics card. And make sure you have _at least_ 2gigabytes of RAM. As for a processor, if it's for a desktop computer, I suggest one of these ([http://compreviews.about.com/od/cpus/tp/DesktopCPUs.htm](http://compreviews.about.com/od/cpus/tp/DesktopCPUs.htm)) but if it's for a laptop, an Intel core 2 duo processor (2.0 GHz) should be more than enough. 
As for hard disks, I would not buy anything with less than 140 gigabytes of memory.

If you want a brilliant motherboard, an ASUS P5B should be more than enough. (if you can't afford such a powerful motherboard... go for something less intense, but ASUS is always good)


Those are minimal requirements... you can always get something even better than that... but I kept it to a minimum to be reasonable in price. With that kind of hardware, your games will be very smooth and should work fine.

### Post by stefangr1 on 2008-03-31
What you should buy depends entirely on what you are going to do with it...

In general, for good prestations @ low energy consumtion I would suggest an Intel C2D (not a quad core, unless specific tasks that you have in mind require one) of the new E8#00 series.

As a motherboard you are best off with an A-brand (Asus, MSI, Intel), with Intel chipset. Make sure the memory you buy is compatible with it (and buy at least 2GiB).

As a video card, I would suggest an NVIDIA but not the 8800 since it's not very well supported yet.

Then one last suggestion: if you are in the shop, specifically explain that you are building a Linux system, that you have carefully selected all parts so that they are linux compatible, and ask if you can return any part that might - by small chance - not be linux compatible, even if the box is already opened.

I just built mine about a month ago and am loving it.  I might have gone a little over the edge for Linux, but oh well...lol.  I went with an XFX 680i SLI LT since it pretty much had everything I was looking for and was not badly priced either.
