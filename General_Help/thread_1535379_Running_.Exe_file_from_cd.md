### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-07-20
You really need to check hardware compatibility with Ubuntu BEFORE buying a new piece of hardware. A quick Google search would have told you that the best webcams to buy for Linux are the ones that are UVC-compatible; they work without any extra drivers on any UVC-compatible operating system, including Linux.

Still, you've got this piece of hardware now, so let's hope it's workable. Have you tried viewing the output from the webcam? Cheese will let you do this, of course assuming that Linux already has the correct driver.

Don't follow any instructions about your device until you've checked that it works, and don't follow any instructions unless they are about your specific version of Ubuntu (as in, 10.04).

### Post by themusicalduck on 2010-07-20
So is both the webcam and the microphone not working?

Presumably you have normal sound output already. It might be worth looking in Sound Preferences (which you can click to from the volume icon menu or under System > Preferences > Sound)

Go to the Input and Output tabs and check there is something listed, that the volumes are turned up and that the Input level on the Input tab is moving when the mic is plugged in and you talk into it.
