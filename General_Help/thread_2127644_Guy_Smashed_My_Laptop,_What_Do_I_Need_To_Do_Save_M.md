### Post by QIII on 2013-03-20
*Moved to **General Help.***


Hello!

If it's a SATA drive, the connections on the back will be exactly the same as the SATA power and data connections on any other drive.  I just recovered data from my son's laptop (it died a natural death) so we could get it on his new one.  All I did was pop it in my case, which has mounts for those drives.  That would save having to buy anything new.

The unknown factor may be the amount of acceleration the disk was subject to in the smashing process.  Drives will only withstand a certain amount of shock without being completely wrecked.  Even if not destroyed, the heads can bounce off of and scratch the platters.

If your data is on an NTFS partition, any computer will do.  If on an ext partition, use a Linux machine.

Best wishes!

### Post by coldraven on 2013-03-20
You can get an enclosure on ebay for $5, search for "2.5 sata enclosure"
I bought one, it is powered by USB (two plugs) and works fine.
Always plug the cable into the USB sockets before plugging the drive in on the other end of the cable.
This way you will not exceed the power limits from the USB ports.
