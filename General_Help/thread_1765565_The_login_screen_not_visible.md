### Post by aphatak on 2011-05-23
Are you working on a laptop?  If you are, I can see two possibilities -
[LIST=1]
[*]You probably have "ALS' - Ambient Light Sensor.  If this thinks there is not much light on your laptop, it can dim the screen down.  If you were working in very dim light, this will save power - and your eyes, too!  If you accidentally cover the ALS, it is fooled into thinking it's dark, and will dim the screen down.
[*]Is the laptop connected to the power source?  If it is not, the power manager application will dim your screen down to save power.
[/LIST]
