### Post by monsterstack on 2009-06-06
Linux isn't all about techowizardry and geekdom. Sometimes people use the cold hard logic of computers to do something beautiful for someone they care about.

I used [metapixel]("http://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/schani/metapixel/") [tuwien.ac.at] to create an enormous photo-mosaic for me and my girlfriend's anniversary. I had it printed out on to canvass. This is the sort of thing I'm talking about (click for the full-size version):
[URL="http://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/schani/metapixel/tinischani_mosaic_subpixel_global.jpg"]
[img]http://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/schani/metapixel/tinischani_mosaic_subpixel_global.small.jpg[/img][/URL]
I made the individual tiles to be pictures of us, obviously. Turned out a treat. 

Another thing I did was when I had to work abroad for six months. Not seeing your lover for so long is hard. So before I left I made a simple Bash script that strung a few words together to make a random complimentary/romantic sentence every time she ran the script. Geeky? Yes. Cheesy? Very yes. Still the thought was there.

What has your computer allowed you to do for your loved ones? [SIZE="1"](I need ideas for our next anniversary dammit!)[/SIZE]
