Spot on according to this:

> There has been controversy over the meaning of the name, but in early  versions of the UNIX Implementation Document from Bell labs, the section  for /etc is clearly commented as *[etcetra directory]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Et_cetera")*[[9]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki//etc#cite_note-8"),  as this directory historically held everything that did not belong  elsewhere (though in FHS it is clearly restricted to static  configuration files and may not contain binaries)[[10]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki//etc#cite_note-9").  Alternative meanings that have been suggested, such as "Editable Text  Configuration" or "Extended Tool Chest" are almost certainly [backronyms]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backronym") that were introduced much later on[[11]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki//etc#cite_note-10").
