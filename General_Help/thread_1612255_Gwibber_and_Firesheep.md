### Post by thewooleymammoth on 2010-11-03
the only way to protect against this is for the site to get an ssl certificate (which you then use to encrypt the information exchanged between yourself and the website). Facebook is vulnerable but i dont know if gwibber uses one, if they do not have one than an attacker can steal your cookie and login as you.

To check if the site has an ssl certificate (in firefox) click on the icon next to the url in your browser. This should bring up the security information on that website.

Also, i think that firesheep is an excellent thing. It will force websites into properly securing their pages by opening up this attack to everyone (instead of only allowing people with a lot of computer knowledge and bad intentions to continue vicious attacks on people who use these sites)
