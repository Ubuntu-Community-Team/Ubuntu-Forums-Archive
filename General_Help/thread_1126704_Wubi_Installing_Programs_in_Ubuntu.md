### Post by Xero Xenith on 2009-04-15
There's two 'forms' of uninstalling in Linux. One is removing, which gets rid of all the files for the program but keeps everyone's personal settings, while purging gets rid of all files for program and all personal settings.

There's no registry to get clogged up or anything like that, an application is just files that can be run.
