### Post by TBABill on 2010-11-08
> **ki4jgt said:**
> Hey guys. I was wondering if there was a wireless frequency which offered more range? Like in the HF segment of the bands. I want to blanket my city with a computer network. Or hook up one of those 30 mile walkie talkies from walmart. Is there a way to get wifi like that?
 
You must have some incredible funding to be able to go city-wide. Many cities have done so and a quick Google search will show all those cities. But the problem lies in the various types of access points and the frequencies available to do so. HF is obviously good for long range communications, but it is HIGHLY affected by atmospheric conditions (besides being well outside the approved frequency range). And to just go city-wide would be overkill with HF since it can travel half way around the world. You have to use a device that can communicate in the same range as the end user's equipment, unless you have a plan to also invest in a home receiver/transmitter that can communicate on the same frequency, but communicate within the home on 802.11b/g/n. At that point you would probably be hitting cost prohibitive range anyway.
 
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wi-Fi](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wi-Fi)
 
FCC regulations prohibit using frequencies outside those allocated for the type of service provided so you'd probably be forced to install many access points to reach an entire city (literally hundreds or thousands of them).
