### Post by jeremyjjbrown on 2009-04-20
I would ask why you are using Ubuntu?

Do you just want to have a stable free operating system? Or, do you like spending your time troubleshooting and testing software? Both are legitimate reasons to use Ubuntu.

If you want a stable system stick with the LTS versions. The LTS versions are _way way way_ more stable. 8.10 still has multiple regressions that have not been fixed, and probably never will. The video and sound problems alone are enough for me. 8.04 LTS just works, with minimal fuss. I keep it on three of my machines, because I need them to work properly all the time. The other machine, I play around with the newest version in my free time because it is fun for me and I can contribute to the Bug reports.

Bottom line is if you just want your stuff to work stick with 8.04, and upgrade to 10.04 when the 10.04.2 version comes out.
