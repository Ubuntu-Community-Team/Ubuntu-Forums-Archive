### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-04-30
Except perhaps for 1 and 2 in your list, these are not problems in 12.04.

I have tested the upgrade path from 10.04 and 11.10 to 12.04 several times before 12.04 release date. I did not have any problems.

Mind you, I was upgrading from a default installation. Many of us have been concerned for months that those with a heavily modified OS will have issues when upgrading and not fresh installing. It seems to be a standard problem. It was there4 with 11.10 and it will be there with 12.10.

I have also tested fresh installs of all the Ubuntu flavours many times before release date.

I do not agree that Item 1 in your list is actually a problem with 12.04.

Some of the things that you complain about have been absent from Ubuntu for a year. Yes, that is how long there has not been a bottom task bar/panel in Ubuntu.

The same is true regarding adding/removing applets from the top panel but we can install application indicators. I have an app indicator for playing radio over the internet, monitoring motherboard temperatures, and CPU/memory/internet activity. There are many more of these kind of applets called app indicators. There are provided by the Ubuntu community and need to work with Unity.

Why should the time app indicator include a weather function? There are app indicators available to do that.

Ubuntu is leaving room for the community to develop applications for Ubuntu. Do not expect Canonical to do it all. That is not the Ubuntu way.

I have icons on my desktop that launch programs. Generally, speaking it is not Ubuntu policy to put icons on the desktop any more but it can still be done. If you know how.

The side menu is called the launcher. Its purpose is to launch applications. The Dash is the place to open folders and not just folders but files, as well as programs.

What you call problems in 12.04 is only a failure to meet your personal preferences for an OS. That is all.

As we do not purchase Ubuntu we loose nothing if we chose not to use it. It is your choice.
