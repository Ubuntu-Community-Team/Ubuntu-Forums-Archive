1 -- Many hosting providers will also help you register a domain name for a nominal fee , google hosting providers , many ads will come up. Make sure you familiarize yourself with the difference between dedicated hosting , shared hosting and VPS hosting.

2 -- That is an opinion it seems many people's experiences differ greatly I would suggest doing your own research. You may not necessarily have the ability to be on a Ubuntu host, they may run Fedora or CentOS. If this is an issue you may have to buy dedicated hosting (can get expensive)

3 -- Depending on what type of hosting solution you purchase, they may install it for you, or you may install it yourself remotely, just depends on what the host offers.

Once you find information about a hosting provider you are interested they will be able to answer all of those questions for you.

There are other methods too, including running your own server from your home. This takes more factors into account though. Including a lot of details about your connection quality that your provider may not easily give up. As well as taking into consideration static vs dynamic ip's , what services your ISP allows, bandwidth, overall network connectivity.

### Post by iMisspell on 2011-07-10
Hello RSASKA...
Just alittle food for thought...

vBulletin can get alittle costly. If i rember correctly, there is one fee to buy it then yearly fee's after that to continue to get upgrades (its been a number of yours since ive owned it, so things could have changed). Being your just starting off, maybe a free forum will better suit you and later down the line get vBulletin and 'import' your current free forum to vBulletin (vBulletin has utilities which you run from your web browser).
_[phpBB]("http://www.phpbb.com/")_ and _[Simple Machines]("http://www.simplemachines.org/")_ are free opensoure forums which have been around for a while and are solid. (Note: i used vBulletin for acouple years (and _[IPS]("http://community.invisionpower.com/")_ when it was free) then moved to something different which fit my needs better. If i was to run only a forum, i would look back into both, but i would also really consider phpBB or SM - ive played with both in the past and can not knock eather of them)

As for in installing vBulletin, as said above, its possiable that a host will do it for you or you have to do it yourself, which is not hard at all. Most software *you* "install" on a web server consists of (one way of doing it), downloading the software (from the makers site) to your home computer, uncompressing it, uploading (some people call this "FTPing") to your host, then following the direction which come with the software. Normal there will be a "install.php" file that you head to in your web browser, then just follow the web pages after that - normal its pretty easy.

Most web hosts have something called a 'cPannel'. In short, its a web page they have on there server, you are giving a UserName and Password, you log in and thats how you control your account (like a "major" user control pannel; same concept as your UserCP here, but has much more options). You can install a forum, wiki, mailing list, commerce software and other stuff with the click of a button, also you can create email address for your domain, if you have 'subdomians' (somethingOne.youraddress.com or somthingTwo.youraddress.com) you can control (and make) that amount of bandwidth they can use from your main account. 

As for as host...
I would do your own research, see what you want (bandwidth, number of email accounds, number of MySQL databases (a forum will use a single Database, a Wiki will use a single Database, a Mailing-List will use a single Database (atleast that's the best way to install multipule software)) you can have, harddrive space, etc.) Then ask around if the choices you are looking at are good. As you will find out, theres tuns out there. Ive used *[https://dathorn.com/](https://dathorn.com/)* since 2003 and have only good things to say.

Since you are new to this and if you have not already, install Apache (web hosting software), PHP (scripting engine) and MySQL (Database engine) to your home computer and get the feel for things. Its all simple to do, and its all free. Then you can download and test out different software (forums, mailing-lists, wiki's, etc.) and you will find out what you will like or need.

You can also do the above and _[host your own]("http://www.google.com/search?q=hosting+web+page+from+home+computer")_ web site from your home computer so friends can help your out with ideas.

Anyway, hope it helps.
Good luck.
