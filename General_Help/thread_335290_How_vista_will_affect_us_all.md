> so if someone downloads an avi file from say bittorrent, would they still be able to watch that on vista?

I think so. Mostly what that analysis is saying is that Vista is basically going to put restrictions on what you can and cannot do when it comes to DRM media.

I think its best summed up by the fact that if you have DRM music, and a sound card that doesn't support a specific specification, Vista will *reduce the quality of playback* so that you can't pirate it. Which basically means, if you want to play DRM music, you have to upgrade your sound card to a new one.

If you want to play that new game thats only avaliable on Vista, you'll have to buy a new (Not even out yet) DirectX 10 graphics card. But from what I understand, games makers will support DX9 for a while, so that argument is a bit weak.
