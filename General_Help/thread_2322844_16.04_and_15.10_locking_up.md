I am having an issue with my computer completely locking up. The only way to power down afterwards is to hold the power button down.  This was not happening on 15.04. When I upgraded to 15.10 the issue started. I reverted back to 15.04 and never had a chance to look into the issue. The issue has continued to occur with 16.04. I did a hard drive test for corrupted sectors but no errors came back.

I'm currently running Ubuntu Gnome 16.04 64-bit on a Toshiba Satellite Click 2 L35W-B3204
Intel® Pentium(R) CPU N3530 @ 2.16GHz × 4
Intel® Bay Trail Graphics 

Please let me know what other information I can provide to help.

### Post by mörgæs on 2016-05-01
This is a classic problem for Bay Trail processors. Until a fix is out the boot option ```
intel_idle.max_cstate=1
``` solves the problem.

### Post by rgrwtrs on 2016-05-04

### Post by mörgæs on 2016-05-05
Good, please mark the thread solved.
It disables one of the power saving steps.
