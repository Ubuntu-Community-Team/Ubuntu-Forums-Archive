### Post by beninburgh on 2006-05-22
Ok, so I've finally got my touchpad to work with tapping and dragging, and scrollbars, but the speeds are all way to fast. I know I could go and edit xorg.conf, but then I need to reboot to test each trail and error step. Is there a way to reload xorg.conf without rebooting the machine?

---

### Post by nanotube on 2006-05-22
if you are using gnome, you can just go to System>Preferences>Mouse, and edit the speed of the cursor.

but to answer your question directly- you dont have to reboot, you can simply restart X+Gnome, without rebooting the machine. to do this, just close your apps, "log out" (just to be on the safe side, you dont really have to log out), then hit Ctrl-Alt-Backspace. that will restart your windowing system without a reboot.
