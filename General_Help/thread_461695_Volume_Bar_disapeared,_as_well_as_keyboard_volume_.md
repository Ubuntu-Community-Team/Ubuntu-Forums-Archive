### Post by Ramaddan on 2007-10-31
I'm very sorry, but I don't remember what I did.

I changed many Ubuntu's since then, so I don't remember whether I found the problem, or just re-installed everything.

I wish I could remember what I did exactly, but I do remember it was an issue with the current user I was using, but your problem could be different.

So you can try making a new user, and if it works with no problem, then just pass your specific files, such as files you downloaded, and other stuff like that to the new user, just to save your self the time and agony it takes to figure out which file is the problem.

If that works out for you, then just delete the old user from the "Users & Groups" in administration.

This might still leave behind the physical folder containing your old user files in the "/home" folder, which you can also delete if you want (after taking what you need).

Hope this helps in any way.
