### Post by tripolitan on 2005-01-08
I've only tried running it on RedHat/Fedora2 but the hardware requirements are way too much, it ran ok on my dual-processor Athlon machine with 512 ram (which happens to meet the minimum requirements for running the software) but when it came to rendering, it almost always crashed. They recommend a cluster to do any rendering.

Cinelerra is not a consumer video editor, I'm still using the "other" platform for doing any video manipulation as there is nothing, that I know of, for Linux. (in short, if you don't have a cluster for rendering, I think you may be wasting your time with Cinelerra)
