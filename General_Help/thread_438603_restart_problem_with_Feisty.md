### Post by trak87 on 2007-05-10
If you have only one hard disk installed on your system, you may not need a jumper to set it as master, in which case it can be jumperless. But this isn't the case with all drives in my experience. Look at the top of the drive and there will be a guide describing how to set the jumper. However, I don't think this jumper has anything to do with your computer turning on when you plug it in. You might try going into the BIOS (generally press the DEL key during bootup) and select the option to restore the default settings. Maybe that will fix it.
