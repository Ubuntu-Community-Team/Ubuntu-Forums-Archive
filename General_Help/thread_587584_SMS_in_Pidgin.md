### Post by JohnnyXmas on 2008-02-11
Here's my experience, if it helps:

I used AIM and added a buddy to it with my phone number ( +19999999999). It shows up as online in my contact list.

My cellular provider is AT&T (Cingular). 

I "IM" myself a message, and my phone INSTANTLY tells me I have an SMS  message from an AIM user, that I can reply to it through SMS, and then what the message is. 

When I "text" myself a reply from my phone, it instantly pops up in the IM convo window. if I sign off completely from pidgin, and then log on from another computer, the "SMS ID number" it assigns to my screen name remains the same. this means that people can initiate an IM\SMS convo with me from their phones once they know my SMS ID#.

I can't help you guys troubleshoot why it doesn't work for you specifically, just that I know my setup works. Also note that AIM is not the only protocol that supports SMS (ICQ is another I know off the top of my head, though it still uses AIM's servers), so maybe try that?

Also, SMS messages are only charged to the sender when sent from a cellular device. AIM SMS is like sending a message form the carriers website, which you all know does not ask you to pay a fee to do so. In those instances, only the recipient is charged the fee. The only exception there is if you are using AIM to send an SMS to somebody else's AIM SMS ID, in which case its free for everybody, and you have the benefit of it being incredibly silly to boot.
