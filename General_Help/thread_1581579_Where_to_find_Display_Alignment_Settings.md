### Post by robsoles on 2010-09-26
Welcome to UF.

There is a chance that a control on the monitor itself can correct this  but it probably is a bad refresh rate setting or some such, the video  output configuration editor in use on your system could be under  'System'->'Preferences'->'Monitors' or it could be under a  manufacturer specific entry in 'System'->'Administration'->????  due to proprietary drivers and other details. 

Please try to determine the manufacturer and model of your monitor and tell us.

Please try to determine what video card your PC uses and tell us that too.

Do you know if it is using VGA, DVI or HDMI connection between PC and monitor?
