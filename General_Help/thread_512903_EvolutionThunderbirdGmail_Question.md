### Post by p_quarles on 2007-07-29
As far as I know, no. Gmail uses POP for non-web e-mail access, which means that once you download a message to one machine, it won't automatically go to any other machine. I just checked, and Gmail specifically says it doesn't offer IMAP access.

One possible workaround (I haven't tried it) is to use an experimental plugin for Thunderbird that gives it direct access to webmail. I understand it's buggy, but works fairly well with Gmail. Unfortunately, I can't remember what it's called, and it's not available on the main Mozilla extensions page. Worth researching, though.
