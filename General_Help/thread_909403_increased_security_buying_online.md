### Post by RequinB4 on 2008-09-03
ALWAYS use ssl encryption for transfers of sensitive information.  You can tell if a site is using this if it has https:// instead of http:// in the URL.  Even  then, trust only trusted SSL certificates - A "self-signed" certifciate gives you no security.  Depending on your broswer settings, it may give you warnings when you enter/leave an encrypted page.

It may also be helpful to enlist the services of a trusted "money proxy" such as PayPal.  Ideally, this would allow you to purchase from any vendor that allows that service while only giving your personal information to the trusted service.

Besides that, the general rules about internet security apply.
