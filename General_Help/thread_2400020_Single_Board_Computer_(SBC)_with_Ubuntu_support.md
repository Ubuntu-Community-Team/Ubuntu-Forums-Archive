### Post by webmiester on 2018-09-02
Ive been working on Ubuntu Mate 16.04 for RPI 3.  I think its not an official release and it isnt supported well by the RPI community (they support Raspberian)

There is a korean board which is called "Odroid" it packs more power than the RPI and it supports Ubuntu Mate too.  You may wnat to take a look at that one.  I think it doesnt have as much information in the forums on it though, but its an alternative to the RPI.  I think the ubuntu flavor that works for it is also Ubuntu Mate, but Im not so sure.

I think the Thinkerboard from Asus has Lubuntu on it and Xenial available.  Ive never tried them, but their forums make mention of them.

In case you prefer working with x86 instead of ARM, there is UDOO.
