### Post by Rex Bouwense on 2014-11-30
There is a "How to" located here,
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Lubuntu/Windows#How_do_I_assign_certain_program_to_open_to_a_desktop_.232](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Lubuntu/Windows#How_do_I_assign_certain_program_to_open_to_a_desktop_.232)
In the example the browser Chromium is used.  You just have to substitute your program for Chromium.

After I posted I realized that I only answered half of your question.  To get a program to auto-start go to Main menu->Preferences->Default applications for LXSession->Auto-start.  Enable your program for autostart or do it manually.
