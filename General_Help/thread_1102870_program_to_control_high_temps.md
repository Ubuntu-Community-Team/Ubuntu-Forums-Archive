### Post by oldsoundguy on 2009-03-22
most of the current crop of boards have built in fan thermostats that control the processor fan speed .. some have a second thermostat to contol and additional case fan and most quality power supplies also have their own built in sensors.
So, with most, no program required.
I found it is very advantageous to keep any cables that may block air flow AWAY from the processor.  But, with some processors, the wimpy HS/fan assembly just HAS to be replaced.  (I am partial to Cooler Master units, but there others that are just as good) .. the one I have on a hyperthreaded 3.06 dropped the temperature by 25 C over the stock HS/fan, and that is a LOT! Now runs at 40-45 C and the processor is always at 100% as I do stuff for BOINC .. ten bucks on eBay!
