### Post by donkyhotay on 2009-06-30
> **Mikethebook said:**
> I'm so new to Ubuntu. How do I use the terminal? I started to write sudo or should in begin "sudo and then could go no further. Don't understand.

Do not type in 'sudo' before any command unless you are confident in what you are doing or confident that the person telling you to do so knows what they are doing. Sudo gives root access to the system which (temporarily) removes one of the strongest security measures put in place on your computer. If you tell your computer the equivalent of 'shoot yourself in the head' with sudo it will cheerfully tell you the equivalent 'sure thing! you're the boss!' right before it keels over into a very ugly mess you may or may not be able to salvage. I'm not saying never use it, there are many very good uses of sudo, but use it like you would a knife or a gun, only when you have to and very *carefully*.
