### Post by oldfred on 2011-11-27
My computer class from 40 years ago (mainframe based) was to choose the software that best fits what you do, then choose the operating system. Often software only runs on one system. 

Your Adobe, Autodesk & Microsoft will not work in Linux. If you do not like & use alternatives like LibreOffice that are acceptable then you will have to dual boot or have two computers.

Years ago I converted from Outlook to Thunderbird, from IE to Firefox & Office to OpenOffice. But then my wife started using Picasa and liked it. The Linux version was not quite good enough, but it turns out Google worked with Wine to improve Wine & Picasa together. I can install the Windows version. I doubt Adobe, Autodesk nor Microsoft will work with Wine or develop native Linux versions.

### Post by linuxrev on 2011-11-28
My partial, but unpartial answer:

1. Unless you are a really heavy Excel or PowerPoint user, LibreOffice should serve you well if not better: can work with Office 2007 files, converts more formats than MS Office, can make PDFs on the fly, etc.

2. Photo editing should be very well possible with GIMP, I think. If you really want more, you should probably starts thinking about buying a Mac...

3. As far as I know Autodesk software will not run on Linux. However, you could consider virtualization, since you obviously already have licensed Windows ans Autodesk versions. I have used VirtualBox, but recently swapped to VMware, which I find to work better. In your case Wine does not seem an option to me.

4. 'Ubuntu or Kubuntu' means 'GNOME or KDE'. I still prefer KDE for its configurability, but I guess it's just a matter of personal preference.

Hope this helps you make your own choice!
