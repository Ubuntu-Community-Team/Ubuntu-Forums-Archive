### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-05
While I'm not on the Gnome or Ubuntu teams, I would guess it's because Ubuntu is designed to be accessible.  Brasero's interface is more straightforward and is more friendly to less experienced users.  It's not as full-featured as k3b or gnomebaker, but it's sufficient for most users (and also works for most users).

For more experienced users, other applications are available.  Personally, I prefer k3b.  But it's a KDE application, and Ubuntu obviously would prefer to use Gnome apps in Ubuntu and KDE apps in Kubuntu.  But if my mom wanted to burn a disc, I'd probably point her to Brasero, since it practically walks users through the process.
