### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-02-20
> **freefixkorma said:**
> hi sorry my ignorance does any expernal hard drive supported the one i want to buy is 2.0 usb thanks for all the support i love you guys

External hard drives come most commonly in two types: PATA interface amd SATA interface.  If you're purchasing one already contained in a case, the type doesn't matter.

As to connecting it to your PC, there are three general kinds of connections: Firewire, USB, E-SATA.  USB also comes today in three flavors: USB1.0, USB1.1, USB2.0.  Each one is a lot faster than the previous.

Newer hard drive cases tend to come with USB connectors, most commonly, USB 2.0.

What speed you get is limited by the connector and speed on your PC.  If it's only USB 1.1, which is common amongst older PCs and laptops, you'll only get that speed connection -- regardless of what it says on the box of the hard drive you buy.
