### Post by -kg- on 2008-12-13
Though it's a bit dated, I've been studying [[COLOR="Blue"]_this Linux tutorial._[/COLOR]]("http://www.linux-tutorial.info/index.php")

It's real good and fairly comprehensive though, like I said, it is a bit dated.  Most of it was written years back.  Ubuntu is not mentioned, nor is Fedora, though Red Hat and Debian are mentioned, as are a few others.  It is fairly deep and comprehensive and gives you a fairly detailed "look under the hood" of Linux.

That will give you the basics and details of Linux...then you can check out other tutorials for more up-to-date information.  You can also find your own.  Just Google "linux tutorial" (with or without the quotes) and you'll find a wealth of tutorials on the subject.

Good reading and learning! 8-)

Oh, BTW:  Yes, if you have plenty of hard drive space, a secondary Linux installation would be a fine thing to experiment with.  Knoppix might be a choice, but from what I've read you might want to make a secondary installation of [[COLOR="RoyalBlue"]_Slackware._[/COLOR]]("http://www.slackware.com/")  I've heard that it is highly stable and very configurable, being a basic Linux installation that you add what you want to.  Worth checking out.
