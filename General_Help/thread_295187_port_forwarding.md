### Post by CatKiller on 2006-11-08
To configure your router, you need to access it with a web browser. So open Firefox and type in the IP address of your router. This may be 192.168.1.1, although it could be something else if you've changed it. Your router manual should say what the default address is. It will ask you to log in: a password box will pop up. Your router manual should say what the default username and password is.

The actual location of the port forwarding setup page varies by router make. For example, mine is on a page titled "Application & Gaming". There **will** be a page, or screen, or similar for your router though - port forwarding is one of the two things that a router does. There you will say which port number you wish to expose, and which IP address you would like that port forwarded to. For example, for SSH you might be forwarding port 22. For access to a local web server it might be port 80.

Take it a step at a time, and try not to get too frustrated. [This portforward page]("http://www.portforward.com/english/routers/port_forwarding/Dlink/DI-704UP/DI-704UPindex.htm") has instructions for the DI-704UP. Despite your assertion that you need to buy their service to get any use out of the site, it has very detailed general instruction of the concepts involved, and very specific instructions - with pictures, no less - for how to configure a wide range of routers for many popular services.
