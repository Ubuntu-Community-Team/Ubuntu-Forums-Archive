### Post by NT4usB on 2010-05-05
You'll get nowhere with nvidia because you're running ATi graphics.

iirc, ATi (fglrx? radeon?) has a config tool similar to nvidia-settings... no idea what it is since I use nvidia.

You'll want to end up with two xservers running with some sort of 'ServerLayout' in your xorg.conf.
...at least, that's how I've done it in the old days of Jaunty, etc.

Anyway, here's an example I ran on Dapper. I've used the same xorg on Edgy, Feisty, all the way to Jaunty. Just changed the monitor specific stuff...
None of the settings would be the same for you, the configuration is what you're after. 
I removed the config specific stuff so it wouldn't clutter up the layout. 

Basically you're telling x you have two monitors connected, where they are, what driver to use ((Radeon? in your case?) and specifics for the monitors.
 
Most of this *should be* autodetected and might just work with the 'ServerLayout', 'Device', and 'Screen' sections...

I'm shooting in the dark here since I haven't tried 10.4 yet and it may be totally different.

Configuring displays has _always_ been a battle for me. Lots of tinkering in xorg.conf to get it working properly.

fwiw, xorg.conf are gold to me. Once I get a good one, I save it and use it over and over again whenever I do a fresh install or upgrade Ubuntu.

Video has never worked out of the box for me.

```
Section	"ServerLayout"
    Identifier     "Simple Layout"
    Screen      0  "Screen[0]" 0 0
    Screen      1  "Screen[1]" RightOf "Screen[0]"
  EndSection

Section "Monitor"
 #LCD
    Identifier     "Monitor[0]"
    HorizSync       30.0 - 62.0
    VertRefresh     50.0 - 75.0
    Option         "DPMS"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
 #TV
    Identifier     "Monitor[1]"
    HorizSync       30.0 - 50.0
    VertRefresh     60.0 - 60.0
EndSection

Section "Device"
    Identifier     "Device[0]"
    Driver         "nvidia"
    BusID          "PCI:1:0:0"
    Screen          0
EndSection

Section "Device"
     Identifier     "Device[1]"
    Driver         "nvidia"
    BusID          "PCI:1:0:0"
    Screen          1
EndSection

Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Screen[0]"
    Device         "Device[0]"
    Monitor        "Monitor[0]"
    ...bla...
    EndSubSection
EndSection

Section "Screen"
    Identifier     "Screen[1]"
    Device         "Device[1]"
    Monitor        "Monitor[1]"
...bla...
    EndSubSection
EndSection

```

### Post by subdermal on 2010-05-07
Okay, here's the issue: My motherboard has an embedded nVidia GPU with a single output and no option to disable the controller in the BIOS. Under 9.10, I added the ATI card in hopes of having three displays (one on nVidia, two on ATI). However, no video would come out of the nVidia controller. I assumed that the BIOS detected a new video card and auto-disabled the nVidia card. Two out of three ain't bad, I thought, and filed it under "problems to worry about later". Dual displays worked perfectly on the first login, and I went about using 9.10 no problem. 

However, it doesn't actually disable the nVidia card, it just stops using it. In the upgrade, 10.4 detected the nVidia hardware and installed the drivers for it, and I'm guessing they conflicted with the ATI drivers in such a way that prevented the ATI's DVI port from working properly. If there was a monitor connected to the VGA port, the one on the DVI port was a clone of it regardless of proper configuration to the contrary. If there was no monitor plugged into the VGA, the one on the DVI port would work fine as either the left or the right half of my screen, depending on how I set it. Possible point of interest: I don't have a DVI cable; I have a DVI->VGA adapter and then a VGA cable going to the monitor. 

So after at least 3 days of futzing around with it, I got it to "work". I paved the box, and reinstalled 10.4 from scratch. No go. All the same problems. Paved again, and installed 9.10 - and it worked on first boot.
