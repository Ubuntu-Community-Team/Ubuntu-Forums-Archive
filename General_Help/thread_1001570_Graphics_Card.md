### Post by ajcham on 2008-12-04
Sorry for not being clearer - the 7600 is an nVidia chipset, and various manufacturers make graphics cards based on it, such as Asus, PNY and of course, nVidia themselves.  As I said, AGP cards are increasingly scarce so you might have to look around a bit. When I google '7600 AGP' many of the sellers appear to be out of stock.

The nVidia 6200, is a little easier to find.  I have a PCI-e version of this, although there are plenty of AGP options.  The card is, however, completely unspectacular - it is merely adequate for my purposes, but it is running fine. If all else fails this would be a good backup option for you.

Good luck!

That seems to be the general consensus, and it did influence my choice of card. Certainly, in recent years it seems nVidia were the only real choice if you wanted decent Linux drivers. ### Post by ajcham on 2008-12-04
> **Valok said:**
> And gaming in Linux isn't as hard as some people may have you think, once you figure WINE out (which isn't too hard) you can run pretty much any game!

That's a little optimistic - Wine has, without doubt, come on in leaps and bounds over the last few years (I remember, not so long ago, you were lucky if you could get Minesweeper running).  I wouldn't go as far to say that you can run almost any game though. There is perhaps a 'sweet-spot' where you get a better ratio of success to failures - maybe games circa 2001-2006 (admittedly, I pulled those number from a place where solar activity is less abundant) - where older games have been forgotten about and newer ones haven't had time for the issues to be sorted out by the Wine maintainers.

Ultimately, however, I must say that there are a sufficiently large number of Windows games that run well in Wine for me to be perfectly content running a Linux-only desktop.
