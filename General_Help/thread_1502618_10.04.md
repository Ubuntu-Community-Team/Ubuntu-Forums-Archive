### Post by yetiman64 on 2010-06-05
> **mikeellis22 said:**
> Sorry, I'm not that familiar with terminal commands. What is supposed to go in front of that command?

 Nothing goes in front.

With an open terminal beside your browser window. Left click and drag your cursor over the command (highlight it). Then right click on the highlighted command and select copy. Anywhere in the terminal Right click and select paste. Press enter. 

All the command cat does is look in the file /etc/mtab, the | (pipe symbol) outputs a copy of the content to grep (global regular expression print) which in turm looks for any entry regarding sr0 (/dev/sr0). /dev/sr0 is the block device for optical drives.

Copy and paste the ouput from the terminal back in the Reply to thread editor, so we can see if the optical drive is being picked up by the system.
