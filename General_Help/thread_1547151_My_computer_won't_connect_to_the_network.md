### Post by samg34 on 2010-08-06
I agree. The problem is most likely in the hardware not the software and thus must be resolved by means of changing (fixing) the hardware. If you have triple checked the hardware, maybe Ubuntu and Microsoft do not recognize the networking hardware and/or do not know the driver. That would be quite unprobable. Furthermore, it would be much more likely that you should need to connect the Ethernet port to the (correct) port on the motherboard.

### Post by samg34 on 2010-08-06
Upon review, I discovered that if your computer at least senses that there are not problems with the connection from the motherboard to the Ethernet port. Kindly disregard the reference to that being an issue in the previous comment. Now the lists are lowered and the possible problems must be either in the modem or that the computer cannot find the driver software for a networking hardware. The first would be much more likely than the latter.
