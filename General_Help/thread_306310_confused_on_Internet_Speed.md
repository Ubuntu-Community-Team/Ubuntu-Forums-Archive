### Post by elettronicha on 2006-11-25
> **DougieFresh4U said:**
> Download Speed: 431 kbps (53.9 KB/sec transfer rate)
Upload Speed: 192 kbps (24 KB/sec transfer rate)  could some explain to me difference in speed and transfer rate.
The speed you say (it is called bit-rate) is measured in bit per seconds (kbps = 1000 bit per seconds => 431000 bps), the transfer rate is measured in bytes per seconds. One byte is 8 bits, so 431000/8 = 53900 bytes = 53.9 kB/s
;)

### Post by elettronicha on 2006-11-25
> **DougieFresh4U said:**
> But it varies.
It varies because speed tests don't depend only on your connection speed but also on several factors, such as the speed (bandwidth) of the servers which you're doing the test on, the traffic on the net (if you go downtown with your car at rush hour you will go more slowly than by night), the speed of the several routers between your pc and the server, the wear and tear of copper cables, etc.
These considerations are also valid in general with all the servers you connect to for all the types of services you request.
I think the only trusted speed test you can do is to choose a fast server and launch a download of one or two big files. You can measure the time (in seconds!) the download get finished and divide the megabytes of the file by the seconds it took, so you'll get the transfer rate.
