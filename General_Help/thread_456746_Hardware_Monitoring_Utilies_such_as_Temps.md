### Post by janascii on 2007-05-28
First off... (I'm assuming you are running gnome) there is an applet that should be installed by default that can put your temps, voltages, and fan speeds on your panel.  Just right-click on which ever panel you want and "add to panel" and select "hardware sensors monitor"  This should get you started pretty well, depending on your system there may be a few sensors already working and you might have to do some digging around the forums to find out how to get more... but its possible.

### Post by The Noble on 2007-05-28
Linux has plenty of sensor utilities, but very few benchmarking suites. You can dig and find a few, but good luck comparing them to anyone else, as very few people use them. If you want a simple cpu benchmark, search for the pi speed test in the forums.
