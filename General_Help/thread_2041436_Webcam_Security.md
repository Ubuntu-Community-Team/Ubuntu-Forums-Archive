### Post by Paddy Landau on 2012-08-19
I suspect that you have authorised Flash to allow webcam access.

Go to any Flash site ([here's one example]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sOVLhnOCLMM")). Right-click the Flash item; select *Settings* and check what you have set.

### Post by Paddy Landau on 2012-08-20
> **ki4jgt said:**
> My settings won't allow me to click on the webcam tab (or allow/deny it when I visit video sites).
Hmm. I'm getting the same thing! I think there is a bug. Do your current settings allow or deny access (they are on a site-by-site basis, so you need to check the site where you have problems)?

If you right-click > Global Settings, it leads to a page that tells you to go to Adobe Flash Player in the system settings for Linux. Problem is, that doesn't seem to exist!

Bear in mind that Adobe no longer supports Flash on Linux, and will phase it out completely for all platforms soon. It is being replaced by HTML5.

The only thing that I can suggest is to reset your Adobe settings completely. To do this, close anything that may be using Adobe products, including Firefox; delete the folder [FONT=Lucida Console].adobe[/FONT] in your home folder (or, if you want to be cautious, rename it instead); then log out and in again (or reboot).

Try your problematic sites again and let me know what happens.
