### Post by dmizer on 2006-05-19
what kind of setup do you have anyway?  you should just be able to plug the screen in and go.  not sure why you would need a "clone" in windows or otherwise.

often there are function keys on the keyboard.  for my laptop, i can switch between the laptop crt to external monitor by hitting fn+f3.

test it by loading a live distro.  there's one for ubuntu, or you can try knoppix too.  that way you can be sure your external monitor works and you can get a good idea of how difficult it will be to make your hardware function, and it won't hurt anything if you have to do a hard shutdown.
