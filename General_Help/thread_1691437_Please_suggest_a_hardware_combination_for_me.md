### Post by PaulReaver on 2011-02-19
basically the phenom II 550 dual core is a phenom II 955 quad core with two of the cores disabled....

some 955 cpu's had faulty cores and weren't up to standard so amd disabled the faulty cores and sold them as triple or dual core cpu's... 

other cpu's had cores disabled to make up the batch numbers...

luckily for me my 550 BE was the latter :) so with a bios option I can use the disabled cores.... I paid for a dual and I got a quad :)

motherboard manufacturers put an unlocking option in some motherboards of a certain chipset... namely boards with an amd 785G chipset


If you're goin to buy a phenom 550 don't buy it because it may possibly unlock to a quad you more than likely will be disappointed.. it is a good cpu anyway..... it might not unlock, might run hot as a quad or it might take too high a voltage unlocked to be safe for your board.

Although if it does unlock then of course it is a nice wee bonus :)
