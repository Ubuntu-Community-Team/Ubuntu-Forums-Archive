### Post by WorMzy on 2010-11-21
Running Windows games is possible with Wine, but the performance and stability is nowhere near as good as that which you get on Windows. There are some Linux-compatible games about, like World of Goo, Dwarf Fortress, Minecraft, PokerTH, et al. As well as Linux-only games, such as Tuxkart, Stone-soup, gtkballs, et al.

There is very little difference between distros in terms of ability to run games.

### Post by WorMzy on 2010-11-22
Like I said earlier, there's not much difference between Linux distros when it comes to their ability to play games. You can use Debian, Ubuntu, Arch, Fedora, or anything you want. Arguably, the most important factor when running Windows applications of any kind is the version of wine you have installed. Check the [Wine AppDB]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&sTitle=Browse%20Applications&sOrderBy=appName&bAscending=true") for your applications, and see which versions of wine have been reported to work with the application in question. Also check the bottom of the application's page, as there is often a HowTo which shows you how other people have got it to work.
