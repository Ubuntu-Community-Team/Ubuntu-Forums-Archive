### Post by snowpine on 2011-07-26
CrunchBang is what I personally use on my older (and some not-so-old) hardware. It is based on Debian Stable so it is a good choice if you're looking for very reliable, slightly older software and a slower development cycle (roughly 2 years between releases instead of 6 months like Ubuntu). 

A word of advice: Software cannot fix hardware problems! If your computer is failing then switching to a different Linux distro won't magically repair it. A good first step you can take is to open up your computer case and use a can of compressed air to clean out your fans, heat sinks, etc. It is amazing how dirty old computers can get inside, which can cause all kinds of overheating problems! :)
