### Post by aysiu on 2007-03-05
I partially agree with ArieT. I'll phrase it this way, though:
There's no point in "learning" (i.e., memorizing) commands.

The best way to learn how to do anything (command or otherwise) in Ubuntu is by *doing*, not *memorizing*. Pick a task and try to do it. When you need help, ask for it. Eventually, you'll put the pieces together in your mind.

For example, you could say, "I think I'll try to install a theme." And then work with us on installing it. Then you'll have learned something. Scanning tutorials won't make the knowledge stick. Experience will.
