### Post by Mark Phelps on 2010-04-18
> **ivh90 said:**
> hey all ...
as a part of the open source community at my college , we are trying to convince the students to use open source software ...

Like it or not, Open Source is not a free version of MS Windows; it's a free ALTERNATIVE to MS Windows -- meaning, SOME of the look and feel will be different. Period. Open Office provides many of the features of MS Office -- but it doesn't look like MS Office and it doesn't work like MS Office.  The same is true of Evolution, GIMP, and a host of other Linux "alternative" applications.  The goal in producing them was functionality, not to clone the MS applications' look and feel.

As your folks get more into using Open Source stuff, they're going to find more and more differences from what they're used to in XP.  So, if you can't even get them to login unless it looks and works like XP, then what are you going to do when they catch site of Open Office, or Evolution, or GIMP? What happens when they don't find the same menu items, or the items aren't in the same places? Come back here and ask us how we fake those into looking like their MS counterparts?

You can make some things look like MS Windows, and you can make some things work like MS Windows, but I can tell you from experience, you can't make the Linux experience look and work exactly like MS Windows. If cloning their "XP world" is what it's going to take to "convince" them to move to Open Source, then you've chosen a path doomed to failure.
