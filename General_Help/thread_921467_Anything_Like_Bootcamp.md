Yes, there are several Virtual Machine managers, my favourite is the PUEL version of Virtualbox. However if the main purpose is to play your game in a virtual machine, you will most likely be out of luck.

Your best bet might be to dual boot with ubuntu and windows and boot as required, or as you are not keen on that, maybe installing Ubuntu via Wubi would give you the best of both worlds relatively easily.

Worth mentioning that there are a ton of games that play natively on linux, but as a non gamer I can't testify as to how good they are.
