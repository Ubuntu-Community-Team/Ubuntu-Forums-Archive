### Post by varunus on 2005-12-21
Pretty much, you should get an NVIDIA card.  Their Linux support is excellent, and they have several great features like compositing already.  (Check out poofyhairguy's Composite Manager howto in the tips and tricks section to see what it is; it lets you get tons and tons of eye candy.)

ATI Cards also work, as ATI has drivers for linux too, but its harder to get them to work, usually.  Also, they don't have some of the cooler features like compositing yet.

So I'd go with an NVIDIA; any card will do.

To install them, you'll have to do two things; you'll have to edit your xorg.conf file, and you'll have to install the proprietary ATI or NVIDIA driver from the company.

Once you decide on a card, post back here (different procedure for each company); it shouldn't be too bad.
