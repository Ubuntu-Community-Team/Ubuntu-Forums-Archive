It's hard to say without checking every component, but it seems to me the laptop is more than capable of running Karmic 9.10
What you could/should do is download the iso file, burn it on a cd, boot from that cd and choose the live version. That way nothing gets changed in your computer and you can experiment as much as you want. It is much slower than after installing it on your harddisk, but it's a nice way of playing with Linux without having to re-install the other OS when you don't like Ubuntu or when things don't work.
It'll only cost you 1 empty cd, which you probably will use again to install the OS.

### Post by JC Cheloven on 2009-10-29
Hello, and welcome :-)

Ubuntu is awesome, in my experience. And I succesfully installed it in every HP laptop I tried (perhaps half a dozen, not sure).

But: I wouldn't install 9.10 for now, specially if you are new to linux. The practice tells us that releases usually need some weeks to get bugs and inconveniences ironed out. Now, the previous version, 9.04 is well polished, and has been the better for me so far. I'd go for that, or wait a month before installing 9.10. 

Just my opinion though.

### Post by P4man on 2009-10-29
A quick google reveals you might have problems with the soundcard, at least there where issues with ubuntu 9.04. I also found a [solution]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=7299632&postcount=60") that seems to indicate the problem would be solved with a newer alsa (=linux sound system and drivers) which should mean in ubuntu 9.10, the fresh release of today, the problem probably wont occur.

But why guess, just make a livecd or usb stick, boot from it and find out. 

As for drivers, you most likely wont need any drivers, other than the ones built-in the kernel. Most likely just about everything will work out of the box, whatever doesnt work, report back and we'll figure it out.
