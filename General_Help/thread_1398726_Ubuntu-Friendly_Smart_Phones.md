### Post by junapp on 2010-02-19
I have a palm pre as well - like you I have had palms for awhile. I would not buy again or recommend the pre. It does mount automatically to Karmic, and can be used as a external drive. Hold one in your hand and open and close it 20 times to see how it starts to feel of poor build quality. I'm also in Canada so I get only one choice (Bell) which has terrible support when things go wrong (in my experience). Although I really wanted to like the pre and was quite excited about it, the more I use it, the more it feels sluggish, it takes about 4-5 minutes to power cycle (I'm having some issues with mine where it powers off at about 40% battery and takes a power cycle to operate correctly afterward). I'm locked into a 3 year deal, but will likely have to replace the phone as finding it off in your pocket and having to wait 4-5 minutes for it to start up is getting frustrating. They can fix some problems with software updates, but it seems it is not quite ready. Other little things are bothering me as well, for instance, the date and time is not shown on text messages, just things like 'last week', or 'yesterday'. Maybe there is a setting - I haven't found it.

I'm not trying to convince you of anything else to buy, just that having the pre available as a mounted device doesn't seem to be enough of a reason to recommend the device. Other people love their's so perhaps I have a dud. Best of luck. The gmail syncing of contacts is quite a nice feature though.

### Post by the_eternal_dark on 2010-02-21
I have a Nexus One and previously the N900 (sold on craigslist when Nexus One released), and there is no comparison, the Nexus has superior hardware. The Nexus One feels solid and has an amazing screen, in terms of color reproduction and sensitivity. Capacitive screen is a must for finger friendly web browsing, on screen keyboard, multitouch in Maps, etc. N900 felt a little flimsy with the keyboard out and the screen had too much give for my liking (not as bad as N97 though). N900 does have a good hardware keyboard.

From an OS standpoint, Android feels more polished and finished as compared to Maemo, but Maemo did allow you to run apps built for ARM without too much modification. However, I like having more apps readily available without having to track down crap on the net, +1 to Android Market, the number of Android apps is pretty good, creeping up on iPhone slowly but steadily.

I love it.
