### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-05-29
Boot up with the liveCD that you know works in the laptop and try burning the ISO image from there.

It may also have to do with insufficient RAM. That model of desktop came standard with 256 MB of RAM, so you may have to use the alternative install CD or a "smaller" distro, like Puppy. (Puppy4 just came out not too long ago and it is really nice, I use it on a hard driveless system.)

A couple of other 'Light" ideas would be Damn Small Linux with FVWM-Crystal (I really don't like the standard WM DSL uses) or one of the light Distros based on Ubuntu, like Ubuntulite or Fluxbuntu. 

Have fun!
