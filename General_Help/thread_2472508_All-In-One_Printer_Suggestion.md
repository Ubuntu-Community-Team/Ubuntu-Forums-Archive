### Post by Impavidus on 2022-03-01
Have you checked every retailer in your area? Some may still have some.

It's not that other printers don't work with Linux, but you may need proprietary drivers to get them working. If you find a model you may like, check beforehand that it works with Linux out of the box or has drivers available. But there's still a bit of a risk, as drivers may not work with future releases of Ubuntu.

### Post by kurt18947 on 2022-03-01
I recently purchased a Brother MFC-J4535DW. I unpacked it, installed the ink cartridges (yes Inkjet with oversized cartridges) connected it to the ethernet network, went through the setup just to be sure everything looked right. It was. Initially the scanner's document feeder didn't work but I think that was user error. I've tried everything but faxing on an Ubuntu 20.04 install and no issues. Brother does offer drivers including an installer but the 'driverless' installation seems to work. Brother seems quite the viable alternative to HP. I'm not sure about HP lasers but HP has played some games with their inkjet machines 'expiring' the ink after so many months no matter how much ink remains. Brother doesn't and I think Brother ink might be less $$. We also have a Brother HL3170 color laser. We use 3rd party toners in it and no issues.
