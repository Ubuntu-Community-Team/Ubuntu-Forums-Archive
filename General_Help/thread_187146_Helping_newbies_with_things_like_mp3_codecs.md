His music is in MP3 format, and although I completely understand and agree with not distributing mp3 codecs in the OS, it does look poor when a new user tries to play one and gets an error from totem. What I think would be a good solution is to have mp3 files associated with some other program or script, so when the new uer tries to play them, he is presented with some information explaining why he can't.  He would then be directed to a simple step by step guide to install support.  The same kind of thing could be done with other formats...

### Post by musther on 2006-06-03
I'm fairly sure it is not ilegal to direct a user to a piece of software which may or may not be legal (depending on where in the world they are) so long as the usual 'This software may not be legal in your country, you should.....' is included. Besides, the mp3 codec was simply an example, it can easily be avoided by saying something like 'For reasons of licensing (see _[COLOR="Blue"]here[/COLOR]_ for details) Ubuntu cannot be distributed with the ability to play all multimedia formats, please see [COLOR="Blue"]_this wiki page_[/COLOR] for information on playing these formats.'

Please don't get me wrong, I'm not bashing Ubuntu in any way (or the documentation come to that), I love it.  My point is simply that we are all trying to improve Ubuntu (in, oddley enough, the spirit of ubuntu) and that one way to do that is to make it as useable as possible by new users.  

New users MUST be introduced to the forums, they may even need a little guide on how to use them, after that they'll be ok, they'll have somewhere to go.  On first login (of any new user account), how about a window popping up with an 'introduction to ubuntu - and where to get assistance'.  Something similar to the tour which used to pop up in Windows 98, don't wait for the user to go and get help, throw it at them as soon as they log in, show them where to get it back if they close it.
