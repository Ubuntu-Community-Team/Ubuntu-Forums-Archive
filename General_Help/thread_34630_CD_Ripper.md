### Post by Brian McConnell on 2005-06-05
I like goobox quite a bit more than Sound Juicer. It's basically the same interface, only you can easily adjust your encoding settings and surf for artwork with goobox, neither can you do with Sound Juicer. My only problem with goobox is that it's wonky. I can't get it to encode anything on the PPC and it won't do flac on my x86 box. I'd take a working goobox over Sound Juicer, which also won't encode to mp3 on my PPC. I have a love/hate for them both. Perhaps Grip might be considered the "best"?
