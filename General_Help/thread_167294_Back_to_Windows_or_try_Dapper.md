Hello 1002285,

On my Ubuntu, I didn't set the keyboard or layout to Portuguese during the installation, since in the majority of time, I need to use English. So, my keyboard is an American one, and it is setted up to work with the English language.  Ok. All you need to do to use English with dead keys is go to:

System --> Preferences --> Keyboard

A window pop up will open (Keyboard Preferences). On that window, click on 'Layouts' tab. Click on 'Add' botton, and add 'US. English International (with dead keys)'. After that, switch to 'US. English International (with dead keys)'.
See the link below for a guide to use dead keys on your Language (note: on that link, click on the 'BY LANGUAGE' tab, and look for your language there, etc):

[http://tlt.psu.edu/suggestions/international/index.html](http://tlt.psu.edu/suggestions/international/index.html)

For an explanation about dead key, see: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_key](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_key)
