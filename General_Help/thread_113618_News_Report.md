### Post by johna11en on 2006-01-06
The article bundled all the Linux/Unix operating systems as one when producing those figures.  They didnt go into detail as in how many were remote vulnerabilties or the number that were related to the Linux kernel itself.  They included third party software and services running on Linux.  That would be why it out ranked Microsoft Windows in security flaws.
