### Post by QIII on 2012-03-09
1.  No, but the urban myth persists.

If you want good hardware acceleration out of the box, NVIDIA may be your choice.  It takes a few steps for AMD/ATI. 

ATI was bought by AMD in about 2007.  They've come a long way since the ATI days.

AMD is a member of the Linux Foundation.  NVIDIA not so much.

AMD has some work left to do to root out the filth left in the corners by ATI, but for general use AMD/ATI cards are no more troublesome than NVIDIA.

Truth be known, my ATI cards are a damn sight faster for the number crunching I do than my NVIDIA cards.  NVIDIA got a jump on ATI in market share with CUDA.  But with OpenCL compliance ATI cards are what I go with.  NVIDIA can only count on their early start and ubiquity for so long.
