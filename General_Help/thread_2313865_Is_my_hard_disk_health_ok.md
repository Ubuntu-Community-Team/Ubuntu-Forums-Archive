### Post by mcgess on 2016-02-18
Hi

The values shown in the Normalized & Worst column values look okay to me. High values in these columns indicate good health. When these values are around the same or lower than the threshold that indicates a likelihood of problems around the corner. The Assessment column shows OK which is what you want to see.

[URL="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S.M.A.R.T."]https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S.M.A.R.T.

[/URL]> **ATA S.M.A.R.T. attributes**

 Each drive manufacturer defines a set of attributes,[SUP][[14]]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S.M.A.R.T.#cite_note-FOOTNOTEStephens2006207-14")[/SUP][SUP][[15]]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S.M.A.R.T.#cite_note-15")[/SUP] and sets threshold values beyond which attributes should not pass under normal operation. Each attribute has a *raw value*,  whose meaning is entirely up to the drive manufacturer (but often  corresponds to counts or a physical unit, such as degrees Celsius or  seconds), a *normalized value*, which ranges from 1 to 253 (with 1 representing the worst case and 253 representing the best) and a *worst value*,  which represents the lowest recorded normalized value. Depending on the  manufacturer, a value of 100 or 200 will often be chosen as the initial  normalized value.[URL="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S.M.A.R.T."]
