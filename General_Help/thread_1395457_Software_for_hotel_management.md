### Post by kalaharix on 2010-02-01
Hi,

In a previous life I was once involved with Hotel front-office software and all I can say is that the permutations and combinations make for very complicated logic. I have looked at guesthotel and, much as I love Gambas, I suggest you proceed with caution.

It is open-source: the developer offers no commitment. Who would support it when you have a problem? Gambas is unusual enough that you would find it very difficult to get support. The front desk is the sharp end of the Hotel. Get it wrong and the Hotel loses goodwill very quickly. Perhaps it would be different if the developer could demonstrate a number of sites using the software other than his own (the classic chicken and egg).

rgds

### Post by egalvan on 2010-03-13
> **kalaharix said:**
> 
 I have looked at guesthotel and, much as I love Gambas, I suggest you proceed with caution.

This is true of any software product that your business may depend on.

> It is open-source: the developer offers no commitment. Who would support it when you have a problem?

Have you ever **read** the license that comes with almost all software sold by "ClosedSource Proprietary MegaBux" companies???
Most of them only commit to refunding your money if the software does not work...
and most of them will refuse to refund your money if the seals are broken, and the seals need to be broken to be able to install the software... a catch-22... :)

At least with Open Source software, you can SEE the software, and TRY the software.
And I'll bet the author will  be glad to refund every penny paid for his software if you don't like it :)


>  **Gambas is unusual enough that you would find it very difficult to get support**. The front desk is the sharp end of the Hotel. Get it wrong and the Hotel loses goodwill very quickly. Perhaps it would be different if the developer could demonstrate a number of sites using the software other than his own (the classic chicken and egg).

And the hotel package has been updated in the last month, so it's not exactly abandoned.

And in the end, you have the source code, so it's at least **possible** to have the code worked on.

Try *that* with any closed source stuff.
