### Post by amanchesterman on 2014-11-30
We'll need some more information if we are to help you.

First to clarify what you have already: You have a LibreOffice Calc spreadsheet, and in each cell there is one verse of the New Testament? Are the verses arranged in a single column, i.e. 7956 cells from top to bottom down the page? If not, how are they arranged?
And is there any numbering attached to them? i.e., does the chapter and verse number appear in each cell along with the text, or is there only the text of each verse in each cell?

Second, you ask for guidance on 'where the chapters are organised'. I don't understand what you mean. The online table you have hyperlinked in your post shows how the books and chapters of the New Testament are organised. If the book, chapter and verse numbers are not included in your spreadsheet, but if the verses in your spreadsheet are in the correct sequential order, then simply follow the table: the first 25 verses are Matthew chapter 1 vv. 1-25, the next 23 are Matthew chapter 2 vv. 1-23 and so on. You just have to add those data to your spreadsheet.

If the verses in your spreadsheet are **not** in sequential order -- i.e. if they are in a random sequence -- then give up now!

Third, you ask how to sort out '365 verses of the day'. Again you need to tell us more about what you are trying to do. Which Bible reading scheme do you want to follow? Do you mean the church Lectionary? If so, which church? Most of the major denominations have their own Lectionaries, which aren't always the same and don't follow a simple order: for example, there may be special readings for feast days. Or do you mean a proprietary 'read the Bible day by day' kind of scheme? Again, there are many of those, and most of them (so far as I am aware) are copyright, so you may have to get permission to use them.
