### Post by mcduck on 2011-02-09
Couple of problems there... :D

First, don't extract the .iso file.

Second, don't make a boot CD out of the extracted data. Doing this would usually just put a bootable DOS-based system on the disk, together with whatever other files you add there. it's not what you want.

(also don't burn the .iso image to the disk as a file, that won't work either)

The .iso file is already a disk image of a bootable CD. You need to *burn it as image* to get a working CD.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto")
