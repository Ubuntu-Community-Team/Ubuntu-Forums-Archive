### Post by jfed on 2010-10-04
I have beyond experience with this. The help I will offer in steps may seem a bit insulting at first, but honestly, this has happened to me before.

Make sure your volume is actually on.

Make sure your speakers are set up correctly.

Go to System> Preferences >Sound

^Once hear go to the 'Output' tab. 

Mess around with this, it has the devices for sound available, the integrated speakers (if on laptop) and usually the options for a sound system if you have it available.

Make sure it is set on the correct device.

On the bottom in the 'connector' widget usually has two options, output and headphones, this makes a huge difference. 

Make sure this is set the the correct connector you with to use.

Lastly on the 'Hardware' tab it shows your sound card options.

Off, input, output, and duplex. I usually have it on duplex for outputting sound, as well as recording sound. 
If this is giving you a problem however, try setting it to only output.

If all else fails just mess around with things inside of the sound preferences until something works. 

Hope I helped.
