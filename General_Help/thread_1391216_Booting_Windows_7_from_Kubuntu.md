### Post by buntunub on 2010-01-26
Yes. Kind of. What your looking at there is a MAC, and the link is most likely to Boot Camp, which allows basically dual booting into Windows. You can easily do this with Linux. You can also virtualize a Windows install in Virtualbox, and boot that up on top of a running Linux environment. There currently is no way to have both Windows and Linux up and running as seperate entities and at the same time in any System that I am aware of in existence to date. There are other things you can do with a KVM switch and 2 seperate computers (one running Windows, the other Linux) and just switch back and forth.
