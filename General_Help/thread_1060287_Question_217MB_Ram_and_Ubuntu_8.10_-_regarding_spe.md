### Post by snowpine on 2009-02-05
I've tested a bunch of different distros on my old pentium 3 laptop with 256mb of ram. Ubuntu was the slowest; Xubuntu was about 10-20% faster. The only Ubuntu derivitives that gave close to acceptable speed were Fluxbuntu and Crunchbang. I've since moved away from anything Ubuntu-based on this particular computer, and I couldn't be happier. I'm currently dual-booting Sidux LXDE, which is okay, and SliTaz, which is the fastest I've tried. Puppy Linux was also very nice on this older computer. My point is that you can't beat a distro specifically designed for older hardware if you have older hardware.
