I have a PC with a removable hard drive tray that I use to swap between Ubuntu & XP, so they remain separated rather than share a single drive, and I'm having trouble keeping the times sync'ed between them.  When I correct the time in one OS the other OS's clock is thrown wildly off.  Is there any way to fix this?

### Post by RudolfMDLT on 2006-06-12
I'm going to try to tell you this from memory because I've broken my system and can't get back into ubuntu to give you the exact descriptions.

In ubuntu, rightclick on the system clock, click prefrences. In the box that appears, uncheck UCT(If it doesn't say UCT, uncheck any other other 3 letter acronym). This will keep your clock from being adjusted +2,+3 or whatever your time zone is from what you set it in XP.
