### Post by Steve1961 on 2007-08-24
> **OzzyFrank said:**
> Got a query from someone who uses Linux but hasn't been able to access the web since switching the connection from broadband to wireless. She's wondering if Ubuntu will do any better. Any takers?

At the present time wireless support is still in its infancy.  That said, with Ubuntu many cards will work out of the box, but others require a little more work.  Most cards can be configured to work using a utility called ndiswrapper and windows drivers, and the procedure is fairly straightforward (lots of how tos on this forum).  However, your friend may have to do a little reading first.  Why not try the live cd to see if her wireless card is supported?
