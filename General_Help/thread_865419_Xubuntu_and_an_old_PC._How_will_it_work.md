### Post by CatKiller on 2008-07-20
My mum's got a pretty ancient laptop - about half the speed of that computer IIRC, with 160MiB of RAM. It runs Xubuntu OK. Not really fast, but not really really slow. It's not quite as user-friendly to set up as a Gnome deskop, but if they know where the applications they want to use are then it's fine.

I was running straight Ubuntu on a 1.2 GHz Athlon (320 MiB of RAM) and that was perfectly adequate, so I think you'll be OK with Xubuntu on that one. There are other options, like setting up Enlightenment or Fluxbox, that might be faster or friendlier, but it depends on how much setting up you want to do in advance, really.

More RAM is always nice, of course.

### Post by ahmedh on 2008-07-20
Yes, I can corroborate CatKiller's story. My mom got a free laptop from her place of work, but even four years ago it was terribly underpowered: 660MHz, 112Mb RAM, with a 20Gb hard drive. My grandparents' even older computer died, and my mom's laptop was sitting idle. Windows XP was running so unbearably slowly, and I really wanted to try Linux (this was my first ever experience with it!), so I installed the alternate version of Xubuntu (which I believe removes the 192Mb RAM requirement check from the install. The alt version also does not have the live environment capablilty). Xubuntu is by no means fast, but it is much, MUCH quicker than XP, and MUCH more stable. As for usability, after you install it, you might want to give your grandparents a quick tutorial about the interface...how to use Abiword instead of Word, Gnumeric instead of Excel etc...but I think it is a good idea. Go for it!
