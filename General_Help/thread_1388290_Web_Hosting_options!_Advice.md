### Post by bpalone on 2010-01-23
Another item to keep in mind is that your upload speed will probably be your bottle neck if host at home.  If you are not hosting a graphics intensive sight and you are not expecting much traffic, then at home is viable option.

I host my own (web presence) site at home.  It is a bit of a hassle, making sure that everything is working.  But, I enjoy messing with all this computer stuff.  So, if you are using a lot of graphics or will be getting a lot of traffic, I would stay with commercial hosting.

Good luck.
