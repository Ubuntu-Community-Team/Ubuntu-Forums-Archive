### Post by Earthwormzim on 2007-11-27
I have an HP laptop, and just above the touch pad, there is a button that you can push to disable the touch pad.  This is a very handy feature, as it is very sensitive, and it can become quite annoying if you are typing, and your palm or thumb accidentally, ever-so-lightly, grazes its surface, and then all of the sudden you are typing in another part of the page.  

The only problem is, when I push the button to turn the touch pad back on (say, when I need to move the cursor), the "Ubuntu Help Center" thing pops up...every time!!!! Needless to say, this is annoying!  Is there a way to disable the "Ubuntu Help Center"?

### Post by Earthwormzim on 2007-11-29
Eureka!  I found out how to disable it!  For those that are interested:

Start Menu -> System -> Preferences -> Keyboard Shortcuts.  Under the "Desktop" section, locate the "Launch help browser" item, and click the Shortcut to the right of it.  Press the backspace key to disable it.  Click "Close".  Done!
