### Post by Splat_NJ on 2011-01-20
Right after your Bios posts press and hold the <shift> key. Keep holding the <shift> key until until you get to the GNU Grub menu that lists the kernels. The first/top-most  one should already be selected; press the <e> key to temporarily edit that  entry. Using the arrow keys go to the end of the line that loads the Linux kernel.... it ends with "quiet splash". Add a space  and the word "text" without the quotes to that line. Then press  <Ctrl>-<x> keys to boot. After bootup you should get the  login shell. You then can try "aticonfig --initial" and then reboot. If you get your desktop back then you should be OK. If not,  then you pro'lly need to reinstall your video drivers. See my post [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1646367") to read how I fixed my problem similar to yours.
