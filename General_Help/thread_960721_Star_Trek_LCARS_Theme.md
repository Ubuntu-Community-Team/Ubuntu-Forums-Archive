### Post by ve4cib on 2008-10-27
Hello, everyone.  I know I'm not the first person to do this, but I've created a new GTK theme inspired by the Star Trek TNG/DS9/Voyager era computer screens.

It's based on another theme from a few years ago.  I've updated it a bit, modified parts of it, and gone to town integrating the window decorations with the backgrounds, panel graphics, and even gone so far as to integrate Conky status monitors right into the wallpaper.

A couple of quick screenshots:
[img]http://www.gnome-look.org/CONTENT/content-pre1/91988-1.png[/img]

[IMG]http://img.photobucket.com/albums/v215/laucian_meliamne/LCARS-theme-3.png[/IMG]


You can download the GTK theme here: [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/LCARS-Desktop?content=91988](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/LCARS-Desktop?content=91988)

And you can download a bunch of 1280x800 wallpapers to go with it here: [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/LCARS-Desktop+Wallpapers?content=91989](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/LCARS-Desktop+Wallpapers?content=91989)


To install & configure the theme, simply download it and install it.  I'm still working out a few bugs with the controls themes (this is my first time trying that, so there are a few glitches here and there).

In the download there is a sample conkyrc file that, if used, will make Conky fit into the background just like in the screenshots.  I suggest making a copy and saving it as ~/.conkyrc.

There's also a (very) simple Python script you can add to your startup scripts to automagically change your desktop background at regular intervals.  Depending where you install the theme to you may need to edit that script (there's a variable near the top to specify what folder your backgrounds are stored in).

As I said, there are still a few bits of minor tweaking to do, but it's generally-usable in its current state.  Hope you enjoy it!

Engage.
