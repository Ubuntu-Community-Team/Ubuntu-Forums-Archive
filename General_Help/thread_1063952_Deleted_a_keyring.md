```
rm ~/.gnome2/keyrings/default.keyring

```

And when I installed TweetDeck, it asked me for a new default keyring. I did that, and no problems so far.

---

### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-08
Keyrings are only for keeping track of passwords and keys used for applications, such as the password for a secure wireless network.  It has nothing to do with your login password.
