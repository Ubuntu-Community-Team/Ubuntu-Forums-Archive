### Post by Raylax on 2009-08-13
I've recently installed the latest Ubuntu (9.something) using the Windows installer (I believe it was called wubi), and have come across a strange problem with my mouse. For some reason, after about 5 minutes of being logged in (although sometimes it's less time than that, sometimes it's immediately on log-in) the mouse completely stops responding. Buttons don't do anything, the cursor doesn't move. The keyboard still responds normally, and there's no indication from the computer that it's busy with something. I've tried removing and reattaching the mouse, and using other mice, but that doesn't fix it. Also strangely, after the bug has stopped my mouse working, I'm unable to shut Ubuntu down properly - after pressing the power button and choosing the Shut Down option, the machine makes a short series of error bleeps (overlapping, the sort of buzz noise you get when a computer has been hanging for a while and suddenly begins to respond to your frantic random keyboard hammering) and stops responding altogether. Pressing the power button again just results in another error beep until I hold it in to force the power off.

I've had this bug before on Ubuntu 8-point-something running from a CD, so I presume it's a strange problem with my system rather than the version of Ubuntu itself. The mouse operates perfectly fine until I log in, and it works fine when I'm using Windows XP Home.
