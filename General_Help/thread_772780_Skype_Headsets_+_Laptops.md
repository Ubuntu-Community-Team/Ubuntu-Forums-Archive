### Post by grashdur on 2008-05-14
Hi, if you want to use a VOIP service like Skype, you need to leave your headset plugged in. Once a call comes in, it's generally too late to plug in a USB headset and switch over to it -- in Windows the sound just won't switch over until you restart the program, and in Ubuntu you might spend too long fumbling around with plugging the device in and changing all the sound settings -- by then you've probably missed the call. 

On the other hand, if you use a more old-fashioned audio headset, you may be able to switch the sound over immediately just by plugging it in -- although in this case you'll be plugging in two audio plugs, not one quick USB plug, and those audio jacks will wear out after a while. 

Ultimately, I think your best bet is to just leave the headset plugged in and the right sound options in place whenever you want to be able to receive calls. For example, on my desktop computer I leave the headset plugged in at all times. 

btw I had absolutely no success installing Skype on Hardy Heron -- this and so many other things didn't work that I reverted to Gutsy Gibbon. But still, sound settings for Skype in Ubuntu are difficult. (For example, sometimes for me there is no ring, and I have no means of controlling the volume on my Skype-certified, Ubuntu-supported headset.)

Hope this helps.
