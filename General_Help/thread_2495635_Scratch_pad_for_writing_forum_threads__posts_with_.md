### Post by dragonfly41 on 2024-02-25
I have myself hacked away in  replying to forum threads. But I have come to realise that such inputs are transient and might be lost in the depths of different forums. Look at the number of repeats on themes such as backup, dual boot, you name it.
My view is that it is more productive to write ideas in separate nuggets which can be referred to by links in different discussion - perhaps wrapped in just a few words of context..  So I'm gearing up to leverage Hugo (markdown) to generate nuggets and place them in a datavault such as tresorit.com. Then simply post a link in discussion threads.
To create the markdown required by Hugo the Zettlr markdown editor can be used. But again there are many choices. In summary apply a toolchain.  This way you keep a vault of your contributions for future reference. I can remember contributing to Atom forum until it was "sunset" by GitHub in favour of VS Code. Lots of ideas lost.

If you prefer a simpler solution keep your notes in CherryTree editor.

### Post by him610 on 2024-02-27
If my entry is more than two lines long, I write it using *mousepad* editor that comes with XFCE4. That way, I can check the phrasing, grammar, and spelling then copy and paste into the reply box. After pasting it into the reply box, there is still another opportunity to review it again by clicking the **Go Advanced** widget before actually submitting it.
By the way, when I saw my reply was going to be two lines plus, I switched to using *mousepad*.
