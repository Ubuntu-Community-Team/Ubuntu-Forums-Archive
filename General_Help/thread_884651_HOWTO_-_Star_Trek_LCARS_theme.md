### Post by moocher on 2008-08-09
If you use KDE you're in luck, just google GLCARS and you get a very nice and complete LCARS package. But if you, like me, have gnome and don't want to install another environment just to get a theme you like running, there's nothing much. So you can do what I did and set it up yourself:
Here's what the finished product should look like: [IMG]http://i99.photobucket.com/albums/l312/speciall_ed/Screenshot.png[/IMG]
(actually, I'm still trying to get that terminal to work right. See this thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=884413](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=884413))
So, here's how you set that up:
1. [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=42306](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=42306) Download this theme. It will give you those blue window borders. He links to another howto on these forums, but it also involves downloading a lot of additional programs that you may not really need.
2. [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Polycarbonate-DarkOne?content=39187](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Polycarbonate-DarkOne?content=39187) Here's the other theme you'll need. This gives you the general window look we're going for. 
3. Now just go through System-Preferences-Appearance and select the polycarbonate, then Customize. Use the LCARS window borders, and the Crux icons. Looks more Trek already, doesn't it?
4. You'll want a good background now, here's the one I used: [http://jonathanhoellinger.de/startrek1.jpg](http://jonathanhoellinger.de/startrek1.jpg) Don't stretch it, just use a black BG color. Naturally, you can use any image you want here, it's the Trek feel and black background that are important.
5. I know, you now want the appropriate login screen for your new look. Well here you go: [http://freshmeat.net/projects/gdm-lcars/](http://freshmeat.net/projects/gdm-lcars/)
6. But your desktop still doesn't look right, eh? That would be because of the fonts. This will get you some nice Trek fonts: [http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Dreamworld/2460/fonts.htm](http://www.geocities.com/Area51/Dreamworld/2460/fonts.htm) I used Federation for window headings, LCARS for desktop icons, and Trek Generation for basically everything else. You can change fonts through System-Preferences-Appearance-Fonts
7. Let's get your panels right now. I had to download and shop images, you can just use mine: left on the left hand panel, top on the top panel. Tricky, no? You will want to alter them yourself for your own screen size.
8. Having your panel backgrounds, now set them up. Right click your panel Properties-Background and change the background image to the appropriate one. Now you move the icons around to get the right look. By default they're locked, you have to unlock them first. It's just a matter of right clicking on them Unlock, and then Move and drag to the appropriate place. Once it looks right to you move on.
9. Now for sounds: [http://synthesize.us/Maemo_sound_effects](http://synthesize.us/Maemo_sound_effects) Just go through System-Preferences-Sounds and get your computer to give you the right noise when warning you that something's gone catastrophically wrong.
10. Last step, now we get to that annoying terminal. I haven't actually got this working right on startup, but if you can get a terminal as  background working, just set the font to Trek Generation and font color to orange with the background color black and/or transparent window.
Also, if you can get that working, help me to do the same.

Any step along the way you think could be improved, just let me know.
