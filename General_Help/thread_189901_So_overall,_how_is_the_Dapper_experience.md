But if it had happened after I set everything else up, I would have been furious.

The speed boost of gnome 2.14 is *enormous!*  Even little things like opening gnome-terminal are blazing fast now.  Highlight fifty text files and "open with" gedit.  You'll notice.

It might be my imagination, but I swear I'm getting better sound.  My upper range used to get scratchy, now I can't hear it *at all*.  I damn near wept with joy when I queued up a song in gmusicbrowser for the first time after reinstall.

Gdebi!  W00t w00t!!!

The consistency of interface from Breezy.  Not only is everything you're expecting still there, but it's mostly right where you left it.  Very little re-learning required, which is as it should be (are you listening, Bill?  Of course you're not.)

I've heard this Deskbar thing is supposed to be great, but can anyone actually get it working?  And can they then help me?
---
Overall five days in impression: I have had lots of little issues over a very short time span, but they seem to be specific *bugs,* not overall *flaws,* and I have no doubt they will be fixed, and if the installation/upgrade forum traffic is any indication, lots of them will have to be fixed fairly soon.  But beneath that, there seem to be several solid, non-trivial improvements, most of which I'm sure I have not yet seen (I work for a living).  I'm gonna stick with it and see what the next month or so brings in terms of updates.
