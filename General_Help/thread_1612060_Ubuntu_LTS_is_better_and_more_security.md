### Post by sanderd17 on 2010-11-02
10.10 and 10.04.1 have the same security patches. So that's no difference. The only difference is the versions of applications you get. With 10.04.1, you stick with the same (older) apps. That means they are tested to the bone but they don't have bleeding edge new features. When you go for 10.10, you'll have newer apps but they are less tested.

It's your choice, do you want new apps? Or stable apps? I always want to try something new, so I'm always with the latest release.

It's true that every upgrade brings problems with it, but upgrading over 6 versions (from LTS to 3 years later) gives more problems than upgrading from version to version. When you use LTS, the best method would be a fresh installation after 3 years, while upgrading one version should go (but gives problems).

### Post by ezsit on 2010-11-02
The better version is the one that works best with your hardware. 10.04 and 10.10 are very similar in appearance. However, 10.10 has a much newer kernel and updated drivers, so if your hardware works better with 10.10 go with that, if your hardware works better with 10.04 go with that. You do not "need" to upgrade unless you want to do so. 10.10 will be supported until 12.04 comes out, 12.04 being the next LTS release, so it really is a wash which version you install if you are worried about EOL (End Of Life) support.
