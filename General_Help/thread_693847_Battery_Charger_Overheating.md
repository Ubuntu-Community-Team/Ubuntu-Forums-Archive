### Post by isparkes on 2008-02-17
When you say "overheating", do you really mean that it is overheating (i.e. thermal shutdown) or does it just get very hot?

If the unit shuts down unexpectedly, this might be a hardware problem that Acer does not admit there is - after a suspend or hibernation, the power management does not work. It only works after a clean boot. Repeat: this only happens when you wake up from a suspended or hibernated state.

### Post by isparkes on 2008-02-17
Having said that - I'm not sure that the power management is up to much - the battery life seems quite short. However not ever having run the system under Windoze I have nothing to compare with.

BTW, I got it that the the charger is overheating, not the actual laptop itself, but I have not experienced any problem with the charger, just the strange shutdown of the laptop after re-animation from hibernated state.
