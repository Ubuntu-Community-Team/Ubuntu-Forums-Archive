### Post by tropdoug on 2008-05-02
Can someone tell me what the word 'bump' is used for in posts, I have seen a few of these, and I am wondering if it's a way to get a post that hasn't been answered to show up and get an answer. (if that makes sense)

thanks

---

### Post by lunnjd on 2008-05-02
Yes. It is essentially a way to get a post to show up as "new" without reposting.

---

### Post by munkyeetr on 2008-05-02
Also, as a general rule, give about 24 hours after posting a thread to **bump** it, if it hasn't gotten a response. Usually they are answered quite quickly, but sometimes they take time. By leaving your post at 0 replies, you stand a better chance of getting helped by someone who is searching for 0 reply posts. Quite a few people look for these specifically.

You'll find some rude posters who bump and post replies under their own thread every five minutes, impatiently trying to solicit a response. Don't do that. The reason no one has answered is because the people who have seen your post don't know the answer and won't bother responding. Someone who knows the answer should come along (but not always). Spend the time waiting doing more internet searches looking for the answer on your own!
