### Post by Autodave on 2021-05-31
Restarting, to me, usually means that a RAM chip is going bad.  Is the machine overheating?  Have you checked fans for proper operation?  Cleaned the dirt off of them?  Start there.  Next, do you have more than one stick of RAM?  If so, try removing all of them except the one in slot #1.  See what happens.  If if keeps running, swap the chip in #1 slot with another.  Be sure to put it in slot #1.  Keep doing this until you encounter the problem: that will be the bad chip.
