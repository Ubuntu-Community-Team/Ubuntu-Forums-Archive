### Post by foldingstock on 2010-04-19
Building a custom PC can be cheaper, but the price difference is only noticeable on higher-end computers. If you are trying to build incredibly cheap machines, you will have a hard time building them for less than a big manufacturer like DELL could, since they can get bulk discounts. Building it yourself, though, you can use higher-quality parts that will last longer. 

At the end of the day, if you want decent computing for low price, netbooks/nettops are quite nice and almost unbeatable at their price/spec level.

### Post by RedRat on 2010-04-19
At the end of the day it all depends on what your needs are in a computer. If you are just going to surf the web, email, and listen to music, you can probably build your own, but keep in mind that your time is worth something. If you value your own work at $0/hr, and this includes all the running around to get the parts, price checking on the web, etc. You can build one under $600, probably closer to the $300 mentioned above. 

The Big Boys (Dell, HP, Gateway, etc) have the distinct advantage of having the machines built in Asia at dirt labor prices. They also get enormous savings on the component parts because they order in volume. You are only ordering for 3 computers, they order on lots of 10,000 give or take. Who is going to get the deal here?

By building it yourself, you get to choose the quality of the components and this may be important to you. Again, it depends on what you want to do with the machine. However, since you mentioned the $600 mark, I would go with one of the Big Boys on this one, take your pick of Dell, HP, Gateway,etc.
