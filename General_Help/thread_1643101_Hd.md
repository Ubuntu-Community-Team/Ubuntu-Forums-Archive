### Post by CharlesA on 2010-12-12
You don't need HDMI for "HD" resolutions. DVI or VGA work fine, but DVI (HDMI is just DVI with audio) will usually have a better picture quality.

If you monitor can support 1920x1080, that's (full) HD. 1280x720 is HD as well. If the monitor doesn't support either of those, you aren't getting "HD" resolution.

IMO: It doesn't matter much if it's a computer monitor, there are monitors that can do 1920x1200, which gives you a higher resolution (and more screen real estate) then 1080p.
