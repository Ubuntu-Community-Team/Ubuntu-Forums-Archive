### Post by dpaint4 on 2006-03-16
I've never had any luck getting USB WiFi to work with any distro. I'm sure there must be whole sites devoted to it, but when I looked everything I found was way over my head.

Your best shot, I think, is to plug the ethernet cable directly into your laptop and use the package manager to look for updates that address your particular hardware.

You won't find 'downloadable drivers' on websites. Wish it worked like that, but no Linux ever has in my experience. If you download it you'll most likely have to compile it and hunt down the dependancies on your own, and if you use the Pacakge manager you're limited to whatever's in the repositories that you've got active at the time.

Best shot is getting it online any way at all to update the thing, and then find other people with your exact setup to compare notes with.

Good luck!
