That doesn't mean you have to search for a company who would sign that agreement but rather ask the company you work for. So that they won't try to say that the software you wrote is theirs.

### Post by geirha on 2009-03-18
Firstly, you are meant to print that document on paper (two copies) and have the company sign them and then both parties keep one copy each, like any other contract.

And you only need to use such a contract if you developed the program while working for companyXY. If you developed it while working at companyXY, they may claim ownership of your work, but if they sign that contract, they are saying its your work and not theirs.

### Post by heindsight on 2009-03-18
You don't need to some company or school etc. to sign a declaration that you created the software.

The idea behind the copyright disclaimer thing mentioned in the GPL is basically the following:

If you worked for company FooBar Inc or were a student at BazQuux university at the time you created the software, then it is a good idea to get a signed statement from them to say that they will not claim copyright on the software.
