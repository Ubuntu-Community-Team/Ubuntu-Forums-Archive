### Post by Steven_S on 2010-04-28
Once you move files over the Internet, the fact that it is in different countries, or within one country, shouldn't matter. 

I use dropbox to share workfiles with my colleagues. For free up to 2GB (maximum 8GB if you can agree with some people on invites); paying afterwards. The advantage of that is that it is easy to use for anybody, that you are actually syncing between computers, and that you can share different folders with different people while keeping it all in one interface. And it is pretty fast.

I have also used my web host for file transfer. I thought they would mind (the contract states that I can't use it for back-up), but support told me it was ok - they look at traffic and cpu use more than at storage space. 

Another option can be Skype, through which you can send files also. And then there is a file transfer functionality in Teamviewer (which I haven't tried out yet). 

I couldn't tell you how to set up FTP from your system to another though - but I'm sure someone will tell us in here ;-).
