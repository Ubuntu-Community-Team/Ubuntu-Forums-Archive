### Post by poptones on 2005-10-21
Ubuntu-desktop is nothing much to worry about, but removing evolution won't serve much purpose. The evolution app itself is only a few megabytes but its core - evolution data server - is tightly entangled within gnome. You just about can't run gnome without evolution unless you compile your own custom (and pretty much broken) version of it.

Think of it like removing internet explorer from windows...

### Post by celdar on 2005-10-22
I removed Evolution and a bunch of the language packages included in ubuntu-desktop when I was on Hoary. I encountered no problems with Gnome or any other apps after I cleaned those packages off.

When I did the dist upgrade to Breezy-development over the summer, I did not have ubuntu-desktop in place, and did not seem to miss it.

When I did the final dist-upgrade the week of Breezy's final release, I DID re-install the ubuntu-desktop packages, so as to be sure to have "everything" (and because the upgrade guide recommended it to avoid problems). I've still not decided if I will just leave it alone, or go through the effort again of removing all those packages. (Why get rid of them? Well, for one thing, the numerous language packages include fonts that I will never use, and that just clutter up the OOo font selection dialog. A small thing, but still...)

Anyway, FWIW, I do not expect you will encounter too much problem with removing Evolution if you wanted to... just read the onscreen text carefully, to make sure you are not automatically losing something else that you DO want to keep...
