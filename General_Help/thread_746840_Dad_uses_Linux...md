### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-04-08
There is a school of thought which says "Don't make someone's Linux look like Windows, because then they're more likely to try and do things the Windows way, and get frustrated when it doesn't work the same way".

You should be thinking more about "Is he a KDE kind of guy (tweaker), or a Gnome kind of guy? (easily confused by options)."

I'm putting Ubuntu onto the computer of someone who originally wanted me to put Windows on. I'm using a fairly stock Ubuntu with Gnome because they're not very computer-literate and would probably be confused if they went to change the theme and saw a massive heap of options. KDE's theming controls confuse me, much more her :-)
