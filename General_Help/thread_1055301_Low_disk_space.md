### Post by cclofton on 2009-01-30
OK, I had no idea which group to post it in, because I couldn't find a group for "angry diatribes", but there should be. To put it simply, I'm f**king livid. I've loved ubuntu dearly since I began using it nearly a year ago, but one little thing has always annoyed me - no warning given when I run out of disk space. Seriously, everything else in ubuntu has been spot on, just that one little problem. A number of times it has caused my system to do funny things through lack of memory, but they all pale into comparison compared to what's just happened. I've had transmission running for nearly three months downloading stuff, most of which had completed and was being seeded, but as yet unwatched/read. Then boom, last night it filled up the hard drive and crashed. This morning, I restarted transmission and walked away while it was verifying data, thinking it would happily get back to where it was since I'd now cleaned a load of space. 

Then just now, I went to have a look at it, and guess what. All files had been restarted. To nothing. 0. I had the World at War downloading (I'm British and I pay the License fee, so don't tell me it's illegal) - 18gb - and it had taken nearly 3 months to get up to 90%. Now, it's got 56megs.

Sorry, I'm rambling - I just need to vent a bit else I'll start breaking things. Please - ffs can we have a warning for low disk space. It doesn't need to be intrusive. It can have an option to be turned off. It should just pop up quietly once you go below a certain threshold, and never bother you again. I've been using computers for twenty years - I'm not an idiot - but equally I don't sit there and obsessively check my disk space every day like ubuntu assumes we should. 

Of course, if anyone knows a fix in transmission to get it to magically restore all my data, I'd love to hear it, but I've searched and can't find one.

Seriously, I'm unbelievably pissed off about this, and I can't be the only one it's happened to. Ubuntu people, if you want me to continue spreading your software (4 converts so far, all of whom I provide free support for) add this feature.
