### Post by srs5694 on 2010-09-28
The LPIC-1 certification is for entry-level system administrators. There's nothing in there about kernel programming, so if you want a certification to prove that you know what you're doing as a kernel programmer, LPIC-1 will be useless to you. The higher-level LPIC-2 and LPIC-3 are also focused on system administration, not programming. In fact, I don't know of any certification that will do for the purpose of verifying Linux kernel programming skills specifically, although I haven't researched the issue, so I may simply be unaware of something that does exist. If there is nothing, though, I'd suggest that if you've got code that's been included in the kernel, pointing out your name and contact information in the CREDITS and/or MAINTAINERS file should do the job, perhaps in conjunction with copies of whatever files or patches you've contributed.

I believe there are more general-purpose programming certifications, but I don't know much about them. There's also the option of taking college courses on programming, or earning a BA or BS degree in computer science, although of course this last will be a big investment in time and money.
