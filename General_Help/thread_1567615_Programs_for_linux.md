### Post by inameiname on 2010-09-04
You can upload and share all sorts of things at _[http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/)_[]("http://www.gnome-look.org"). It's great for sharing scripts, wallpapers, icon sets, themes, screensavers, desklets, screenlets, fonts, cliparts, systemsounds, etc. There is an option to add other stuff as well, including apps. 

Also, depending on what your applications are, check out [http://www.webupd8.org/](http://www.webupd8.org/) and [http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/) too. 

And yes, launchpad is a great source to upload debs, especially those you can't find in the official repos. Often, those sites I mentioned above advertise apps that they find interesting and help you to share your creation with others. So if it's debs, go Launchpad. If it's scripts and whatnot, gnome-look is a good option. And if it's really, good, often the Webupd8 or OMUbuntu folks will put up a posting for it as I know they always check it out.

### Post by JedMeister on 2010-09-04
I think inameiname's advice is good. Although I think LaunchPad is definitely the go, especially if you'd like to see your apps make it into the standard Ubuntu repos. If you have a dig around you'll find that there are specific steps to take to get your app included in the repos (sorry I haven't got any links handy). Depending on the application of your scripts, they too may be able to be packaged as .debs and uploaded to LaunchPad.

Another thought is that it may also be worth looking at applying to get your apps into Debian repos. From what I know (which admittedly is limited) getting Debian apps (ones included in Debian repos)  added to Ubuntu repos is somewhat straightforward (and may take less effort than getting it straight into Ubuntu?)
