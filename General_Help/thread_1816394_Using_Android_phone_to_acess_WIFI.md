### Post by benmoran on 2011-08-01
It's possible to do. 
There is a program called "Easytether" that lets you connect to your phone via usb cable, and use your phone's internet access. It uses a small command line app that you run on your Ubuntu machine to connect with the phone. No root required. It's intended to use your phones 3G connection for internet. However, if you connect your Android to your dorm's WIFI, it will use that instead of 3G.

[PC] <---usb cable---> [Android+3G]
or:
[PC] <---usb cable---> [Android+WIFI]

Only issue is that the Free version of Easytether doesn't support https, but it should get you going until your wifi card comes in.
