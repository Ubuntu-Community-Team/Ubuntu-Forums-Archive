### Post by samborambo on 2009-01-28
Do some googling for "linux digital picture frame".

Loads of people do exactly what you've done - repurposing a laptop for digital photos, etc.

Install 'feh', an image viewer/slideshow that can be controlled via shell commands. The simplest way to control feh would be with a home brew infrared receiver, an old TV/VCR remote and lirc. Once you configure lirc to output commands, you'll be able to load a lightweight web browser in full screen with a custom page, etc.

I'm using a Compaq Evo T20 thin client and a normal 15" LCD monitor instead of a laptop. Two reasons - one, the thin client has no fans and uses less power than a laptop and two, laptop screens don't normally have a wide viewing angle which is really annoying.

My T20 arrived today so I'm off to do some tinkering after work. Good luck with your project :)

Sam.
