### Post by Grolsche on 2008-12-05
Let me just try and get what your asking.

Are you wanting your usb hard drive to boot at start up? or is the usb drive just being used for storage of files etc?

BOOTING FROM USB HARD DRIVES
-----------------------------

If you are wanting to use a USB hard drive to boot, then first you would need a motherboard that has a bios that can recognise USB during boot. Newer computer models should be bale to do this. If your computer is fairly old then forget it.

Also, not all USB external Hard drives allow you to boot from it. I have one and it can't be used like an internal one.

### Post by mybunche on 2008-12-05
Yes, you can, but it depends on what you want. I have a 3 year Dell and I was running Ubuntu 6.10 for 20 months off a permanently attached USB harddrive with not one problem. I didn't change anything in the bios at all and it is was same as installing it on a internal hard drive. Mine needed the USB hard drive plugged in to boot the PC, but if I booted into Windows I could of removed the USB hard drive (no need for me though). That's not a good solution for you as you have a laptop and you want to be able to boot into Windows without first attaching the USB hard drive. You probably need to do bios changes. But it's possible. Hope someone posts a solution for you.
