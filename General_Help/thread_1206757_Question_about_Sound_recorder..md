### Post by cptrohn on 2009-07-07
I bought a very nice KISS boxed set of CD's at a garage sale, I went to rip them into flac and there is some type of DRM on them that is very annoying (it will cause the CD's to skip and mess up some of the song electronically if played on the computer it doesn't do this with a regular CD player).... my question is, if I used sound recorder to put them into flac would I be able to tag the tracks just like if I had ripped them from a CD?

### Post by ajgreeny on 2009-07-07
Simple answer, yes.

The thing about ripping from a CD is that the tracks are all exactly the right lengths etc etc for applications like rhythmbox to detect the CD, show the album cover, the title and everything automatically.  You will have to do it manually, probably.

Can you just play them as an audio CD on the computer as opposed to trying to rip them?  If so you can use sound recorder to record, or even better, audacity to record and then split them into separate tracks.

I have done it a few times with old 12" LPs, which I still have a deck to play, (and they still sound great!)
