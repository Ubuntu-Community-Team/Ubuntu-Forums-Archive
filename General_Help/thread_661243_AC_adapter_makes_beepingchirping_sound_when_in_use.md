### Post by Tally Solleni on 2008-01-07
I've had my Compaq Presario for around four years now, so I recently replaced its power cord, which was barely even working anymore.  Now, the new cord makes a bizarre, high-pitched  chirping sound whenever it is in use.  I cannot describe this noise very well, but it is a repetitive "beep beep beep beep", with each note exactly the same length and with a predictable rhythm.  It has a somewhat squeaky quality as well.  I have verified that the sound is coming from the adapter, and NOT from the speakers or any other component.  The sound persists even when the laptop is on stand-by mode, and when the cord is removed from the laptop or the wall, the sound immediately stops.
My theories so far are:
- The new AC adaptor is not the same kind as the previous one and can handle higher wattage (90w).  This might make it function slightly differently, creating the sound.
- The temperature of the laptop may be at fault (since it is quite old, and the first power cord was discarded because it seemed to have been warped by extreme heat), but I have yet to find a trustworthy program to inform me of such things as temperature and fan speed.  Also, since it is a laptop, replacing internal components is nigh impossible.
- I noticed when studying the metal connector (the end that goes into the computer; forgive me, but  I am unaware of its technical name) that it is tipped with black plastic.  This plastic seems a bit uneven, and [there is an irregular raised section on one side of it]("http://img147.imageshack.us/img147/5761/diagramofadaptorua3.png") (diagram drawn by me in The GIMP, for want of a photo of the item in question).  The sound might be produced by this "bump" as the electrical current runs through it, or by it simply rubbing against the interior chamber.  It could also mean that the new cord is being warped by the heat (see above) and that the two factors are working in tandem to produce the sound.

I am mostly wondering if anyone else has experienced something similar.  Of course I understand that my problem is a rather unusual one, but I thought it would be worth to ask, regardless.  Please regard me with kindness.
