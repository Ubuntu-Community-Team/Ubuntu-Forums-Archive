### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-01-27
Ubuntu is used in some businesses, and I would guess they are almost all running an LTS release. Outside of that, you are looking at enterprise-class Linux systems like RHEL from Red Hat or SUSE from Novell. These systems come with commercial support, which is obviously paid for. CentOS is a community build of RHEL from the source code released by Red Hat, and is possibly also used, though the support is community-based instead of commercial.

If you have no idea what you're doing you have two choices.
Ubuntu OR Fedora (Redhat).

Ubuntu has a few versions and spin-offs. Just stick with Ubuntu you'll get help here easier.
