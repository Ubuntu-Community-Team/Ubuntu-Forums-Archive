### Post by gh0st on 2007-01-04
This sounds like a Flash Player version problem to me. It sounds as though you have version 7 which is a bit out of date but is still the official version for Linux. Windows and Mac use version 8 or 9 and for some reason flash videos done in version 8 or 9 of Flash don't work properly in Flash Player 7. The videos on YouTube are done with all different versions of Flash but only the ones done with Flash 7 will work for you at the moment. Which should explain your intermittent problem and some pages asking you to upgrade to a newer flash player.

Don't worry there is a simple solution, you need the beta version of Flash 9 for Linux installed and you'll be sorted. It came out about 3 months back. It is a beta but it works great for me.

Here is a link to a tutorial on Lunapark on installing Flash 9 beta on Ubuntu - [http://lunapark6.com/?p=2412](http://lunapark6.com/?p=2412)

This should sort your problem out. I think Adobe changed the audio encoding in version 8 onwards of Flash and so version 7 can't read it, you get no sound or worse a page saying "please update to latest Flash player"

I hope that helps, let me know if you need anything else. Sorry about my long winded explanation :)
