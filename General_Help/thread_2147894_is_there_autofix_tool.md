### Post by zombifier25 on 2013-05-23
You mean TuneUp-like optimization tools? There is none in Ubuntu. Even in Windows I don't trust those tools since they tend to
1. Emphasize the problems your computer has, usually ones that does not affect the system visibly
2. Fix only really minor errors, ones you can easily do yourself, and is incapable of fixing really major problems, and
3. Cost a ****ton of money.

The closest program that came close is BleachBit, which cleans out unnecessary junk files that clog up your hard drive (think CCleaner), but otherwise does not provide performance boost or anything. Ubuntu is pretty optimized by itself.

If you have any specific problems with Ubuntu, do tell.
