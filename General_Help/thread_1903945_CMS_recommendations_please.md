### Post by linfidel on 2012-01-03
It's not totally clear to me exactly what you have and desire.  Are you now paying 9.99/month for the site, and you don't even have support for databases and php?  I have sites with all that for $10/year for low volume stuff.  What hosting are you using?

I personally like Wordpress for a mix of features, ease of use, customization abilities, etc.  It works well for static sites, and can do most anything you need.  Once you learn how it works, it's very simple to install and setup, and I move Wordpress sites from server to server all the time, including to my little home testing server.  It requires a database and PHP, in addition to a web server.  It helps to be able to at least understand simple programming in PHP to do much, though.

I used Drupal for a site once to learn more about it, and 6 months later, when I needed to make changes, I could barely remember how.  I decided to forget about Drupal the rest of the way, and use Wordpress whenever I could.

I've done a couple of fairly big non-blog Wordpress sites in the past few months, learning as I went.  But I was a programmer for many years before doing this.
