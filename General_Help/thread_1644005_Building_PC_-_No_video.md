### Post by 3Miro on 2010-12-13
The motherboard and the RAM are compatible (you have done your homework to ensure you get proper parts). This leaves only a few options:

1. PSU is not connected properly, which is highly unlikely considering there is only one cable that fits only one way. 
2. The PSU is not adequate (again unlikely for an Atom CPU) or the PSU is defective.
3. There is a setting that is very specific to the motherboard to make sure the video works. Your best bet is to ask on hardware forums about the specific model (I did not see any jumpers, but I could have missed them).
4. There may be an issue with the monitor. The video is hardly powerful and if you are using very large monitor there may be an issue with the resolution supported.
5. It is possible that the RAM or Motherboard is DOA.

I cannot think of anything else, although that doesn't mean anything.
