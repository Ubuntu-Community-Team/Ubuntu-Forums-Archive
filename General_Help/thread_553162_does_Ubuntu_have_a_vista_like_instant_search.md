### Post by Les_Caesars on 2007-10-08
From my humble amount of experience, I've found that the closest you can get to Vista's instant search is installing Kickoff. With Kickoff, you can literally launch applications with only one click. Just hover over the menu, type the application you want, watch your application pop up, click, and you're ready to go.

Here's a screenshot: [http://en.opensuse.org/Image:103-Kickoff-Search.jpg]("http://en.opensuse.org/Image:103-Kickoff-Search.jpg")

And you don't even need to click on the search bar!

However... you'll need to use KDE instead of the default Gnome if you want to use Kickoff. But for that matter, But KDE may be a better fit for you than Gnome anyway, because search bars seem to be everywhere in KDE. Konqueror, Kconfig, Amarok, Kopete, and even the default KDE menu all have search bars. If you drink tea from the same cup that I do, then you'll be much happier with a KDE+Kickoff setup than vanilla-flavored Gnome.

Get KDE by going into synaptic and installing kubuntu_desktop

Get kickoff here: [http://linux.softpedia.com/get/Desktop-Environment/Tools/Suse-Kickoff-for-Kubuntu-20919.shtml]("http://linux.softpedia.com/get/Desktop-Environment/Tools/Suse-Kickoff-for-Kubuntu-20919.shtml")

### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2007-10-08
if you use gnome there is the deskbar applet, works pretty good if you ask me.
KDE really doesnt have such a thing, but its search tool is relatively easy.
For gnome just go to your top bar and right click and select "Add to panel"
another window will pop up
scroll down until you see the deskbar applet icon
just drag and drop it onto the panel like you do in windows (assuming you know how to drag and drop) and there you go, then press ok.
now initally it will be a little icon that looks like a magnifying glass, just right click on "properties" to customize it.
you can make it look like a search bar like you see in mozilla firefox.
mostly gnome kind of works like firefox in some ways, it uses drag and drop when customizing the panels, the "add to panel" is very much like the customize toolbar function in firefox.
