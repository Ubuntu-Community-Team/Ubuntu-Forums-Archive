### Post by fbhosted on 2010-10-31
vBulletin 4 has CMS built in along with forums, so its a good choice for a CMS and forum platform. I believe individual users can create their own blog within the forum. It allows for longer amount of time of users on the site. Implement a blend of CPM (cost per thousand views) and CPA (cost per action, for instance, when a user signs up for a service or product) advertising and your forum is now profitable to cover your hosting costs and vBulletin license.

Me personally, I like Wordpress. Easy to use, easy to update and so many free themes out there. Premium themes which cost between $5-20, all the way up to $100 or so are out there and look really good. I've administered vBulletin before. It's painless like Wordpress but can be overwhelming for the novice moderator.
