### Post by roystreet on 2009-01-14
I hate to throw a wrench in here, but I think that office 2007 is the best build of MS office ever.  I love the GUI better than any previous versions.  3 things stop me from using OpenOffice: 

Mainly that it is not 100% compatible with word files.  There may be many things it is compatible with, but I have found formatting an issue over & over again.  I can create a doc in word & will not look the same in OO.  Or I can create it in OO & it will not look the same in word.  If I have to work with files from my employer that uses word, I easily see at least formatting issues. This has been true with OO concerning previous versions of word & OO - It is not limited to 2007.  

Next, I do some programming using the VB portion of word to automate some tasks.  I know OO has it's own basic programming ability, but it is different & it will be an investment and I can't use the things I previously made in VB.  

Third I find the GUI much easier to use & less boring in Office 2007.  Previous to 2007, the MS office suites were looking pretty much the same after a while.  Mostly textual type of commands.  They would just add thousands of new commands, etc to each version, maybe a little more eye candy.  I think they did a very good job with 2007, although I'm not excited about their xml format that is proprietary -No surprise coming from them.

I use to use Access a lot & did a lot of programming with VB for that app & OO has nothing that can touch that app.  I use it much less now.  If OO would update the GUI further & work on the compatibility more, I may be more inclined to go that way.  Excel still far exceeds what OO offers in its vast abilities. 

I am trying to convert over to Ubuntu. I have used crossover office to install 2007 & so far (It's only been 1 day) it's working good.
