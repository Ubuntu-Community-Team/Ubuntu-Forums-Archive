### Post by hakermania on 2011-05-01
I respectfully disagree with the decision of Ubuntu to disappear the tray! [-X
With what kind of seriousness does somebody release a new version of an OS in which the half of the applications working on the previous release, don't work in the current?

As you've maybe realized, minimizing/closing to tray, simply doesn't exist. When an application minimizes/closes to tray it disappears from the panel and runs in the background, with any actual access to the application.

As you can see [SIZE=4][HERE]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/752371")[/SIZE], the actual reaction to this was that applications themselves have to fix their code in order to work with the Unity environment!
I'm posting here because, as i saw, too few people are complaining about this so useful feature of an OS(only 4 people have reported that are affected by this bug)...
**VLC**, **Clementine**, **Wallch** (my app :() and so many others are ALL now missing their feature of the tray and they are unusable if they are entirely minimized to tray. Some apps run through the tray exclusively as well (like **Desktop Drapes**) and now don't work at all.
For example, consider how buggy TrueCrypt has become now, that minimizes to tray when you push 'Exit' and there is a volume already mounted, and you cannot unmount the volume by hand (only truecrypt itself can). Think of it, how many problems have been created by disabling the tray (without any obvious reason, because there can still be indicators but not tray icons)

So, what does Ubuntu do to support the Open Source projects? It disables tray to turn them buggy?:-s

So, what I am asking is your help in order to complain against this change.
What you can do is to report [HERE]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/752371") that the bug does affect you too, and you can leave a comment as well!

*This action has the purpose to make Canonical reconsider the importance of tray and restore it!*:D
