### Post by davidoloan on 2012-05-28
On all 11.10 and now 12.04 installs on various different hardware, from time to time I have experienced a slight graying of the screen and hesitation when you do something like open a folder. After a few seconds the graying stops and the system responds properly again.

System Monitor always shows that the processors are not working hard and only a small amount of RAM is being used.

In the last 10 days I have noticed that if I run handbrake or watch streaming video that after a while the graying and hesitation is much more evident to the extent that the system is annoying to use.

System monitor shows no more than 15% of 8GB RAM being used, or 20% of 4GB on another.
It also shows that after using Handbrake the Processors run at almost 100% for at least 10 minutes after handbrake is finished and closed. 

If I try to use the system while video encoding or streaming video the systems becomes much worse very quickly and eventually becomes unusable.

If I don't use the system while video encoding but attempt to use it afterwards it works at first but becomes unresponsive and after a short while unusable.

System Monitor says that everything is fine at all times.

A reboot makes the system useable again.

This is on 4 different systems with all updates carried out all the time. The only new software is Dropbox and that is only on 2.
