### Post by stream303 on 2008-05-13
It is probably apparent that using an animated screensaver doesn't actually save much power.  While it is entertaining for passers-by, how much are we wasting energy with them?

I did a quick check using a wattmeter (from P3 International "Kill A Watt(tm)") and noticed the following:

When my system is idle, (not sleeping, no hibernation, no screensaver, and the cpu frequency scaler active) the system consumes about **88 watts.**  (had to use cpudyn vs powernowd)

With nothing but a blank screensaver active, power drops a little, or stays about the same.

But with any animated screensaver, power ramps up to about **121 watts** or so.  Pretty significant over the long run and just a waste if you aren't zoning out staring at it all the time :)
