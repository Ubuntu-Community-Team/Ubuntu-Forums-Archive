### Post by ragtag on 2011-02-04
It's possible, but I don't know if it's advisable. See [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=769883") for how it can be done using VirtualBox. I guess you can do the reverse from Windows.

There are some things that may simply conflict. Most virtual machines don't behave exactly like the hardware of the physical machine. Hardware accelerated graphics are limited, and mentioned in the post above, installing Guest Additions on the physical partition, may break the OS. Not to mention, I've no idea what would happen with Windows activation.

Do you really need to have all your OS's available from within each other, or would it be enough to just be able to access the files within them?
