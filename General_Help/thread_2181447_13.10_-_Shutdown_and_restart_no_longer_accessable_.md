### Post by Wheel on 2013-10-21
You can write you own shutdown shortcut in 13.10. 
Open System Settings-Keyboard-Shortcuts-Custom.
Hit the + sign to open a small entry field.
There, enter your name of choice.  
Um...Shutdown?
For the command type in  : gnome-session-quit --power-off --force
Hit Apply.
Your shutdown shortcut appears on the right.
Simply click on "Disabled" to assign any keystroke combo you like.

This brings up the 4 option dialogue : shutdown/restart/suspend/lock
In a perfect world, logout would also be included as a 5th option.
Either that or include these options in the Cntrl-Alt-Del shortcut which brings up the Logout Dialogue.

it took me 2 days to find this.
Delight in the small things life has to offer!
