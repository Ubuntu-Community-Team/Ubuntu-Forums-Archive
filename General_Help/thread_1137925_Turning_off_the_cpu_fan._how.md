### Post by tkelito on 2009-04-26
Even if you were to turn off the CPU fan the temperature would spike very quickly causing components as stated to burn out.  

My suggestion would be if it is a desktop you can purchase a fan controller where you can just turn the speed down (not off).

Also you could consider replacing the CPU Fan with a quieter version.  I am guessing it is a stock Intel or AMD which are louder than say an aftermarket CPU Fan (or HSF possibly).
