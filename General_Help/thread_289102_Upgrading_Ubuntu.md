### Post by meng on 2006-10-30
Traditionally, upgrades must be between consecutive releases. I don't know if this will change because Dapper is an LTS release and Edgy is not. However, regardless of this I would advise you to back up your personal/work data AND create a separate /home partition (if you don't already have one).
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome)

Then when you wish to change versions, do a fresh install to overwrite the root partition, and leave your /home partition intact. You will preserve most of your settings, all your data, and it should be relatively painless to reinstall whichever special applications and multimedia codecs you have grown accustomed to.
