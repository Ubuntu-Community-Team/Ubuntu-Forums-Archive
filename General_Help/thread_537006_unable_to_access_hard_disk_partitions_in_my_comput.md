### Post by notwen on 2007-08-28
To unmount simply try 

> sudo umount /media/hda2

hda2 would be an example of the drive(s) you may or may not have under /media =]

[Here]("http://www.minix3.org/manpages/man1/umount.1.html") a page containing the man info for the 'umount' command.

As far as not showing up in your computer, I am not exactly sure of what you're looking at and depending on whether it's Nautilus, Konqueror or something different all together there would be different methods to get them showing up.

### Post by notwen on 2007-08-28
> **cevil203 said:**
> hwo would i go about do something like this for an external usb hdd?

If you're using gnome and your drive is detected automatically it should pop up a Nautilus window w/ the contents of said drive right in front of you.  You should also see your USB drive in the left frame of Nautilus.  If you're more comfortable w/ using the GUI you can simply right click on your USB drive here in the left frame and select unmount.  Otherwise simply type this into a terminal

> sudo umount "insert address for your USB Drive"

It should also be located under /media along w/ any other drives.
