### Post by az on 2006-09-08
> **Hairy_Palms said:**
> wow i didnt know they ran out that soon, does that mean mp3 becomes freeware or public domain or what?

There is already free-libre open source software to encode and decode MP3.  There always has been.  Lame is the archetypal free-libre implementation of the mp3 codec and it is GPLed.  Always was.

The problem is what your initial statement pointed out in the first place:  The *patent* expires in 2010.

So, on a software level, it is all free-libre.  No one owns the code, it belongs to everybody, in the way that the GPL enforces software freedom.

However, many countries enforce patent rights on software, which makes the implementation of an MP3 encoder or decoder subject to ownership and royalties.

Most reasonable people do not think that software patents make sense.  Coding software is not the same thing as building material goods.  Most of the algorythms that make up software would have been written by someone else - there is no big discovery in writing software, just a logical progression of ideas.

A patent on software is much like putting a patent on a book about a man and a woman - it's that unfair.  No one would think that just because one person wrote a book about "a man and a woman" that they can claim the patent rights on all future books on the topic.  The same goes for software.  But that is not obvious to some people.

So the result is that you get a proprietary format with free-libre software implementations.  The legal problems with MP3 do not have anything to do with the writing, modifying or redistributing the code.  The problem is just using the MP3 format.
