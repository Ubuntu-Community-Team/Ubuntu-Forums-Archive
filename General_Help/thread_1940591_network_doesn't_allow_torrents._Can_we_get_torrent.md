### Post by miegiel on 2012-03-15
It depends on how torrent is being blocked. If it's just the default torrent ports that are blocked, you could use different ports (see your clients settings). Http uses port 80 ;)

If your traffic is being analyzed, turning on encryption in your torrent client might do the trick (not all torrent clients support it, but the good ones do). Traffic analysis is spying and in a democratic country it is illegal for people to spy on other people, which includes businesses and employers spying on their customers and employees. So if you know your traffic is being analyzed, throwing a law book at your network administrator might also be an option.

If I'd need to use "proxy for torrents", I'd just go fullthrottle and start using [tor]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tor_%28anonymity_network%29")

However _keep in mind_ torrent might be blocked for a valid reason, even though the method is crude. Think of network(over)load, employers who want to avoid morally sensitive stuff (might cost them customers) and "the law".
