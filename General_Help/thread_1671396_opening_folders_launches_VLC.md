### Post by yetiman64 on 2011-01-20
On any folder in your /home partition, right click and choose "Open with other application".

From the list choose "Fie Browser" and ensure the tickbox for "Remember this application for "folder" files" is TICKED. 

Note you have most likely done the same for a folder with VLC in the past with this tick engaged (annoyingly it is ticked on by default) which causes the problem initially.

Edit: as per Rasa111's post #4. "Open folder" (I just noted the option here) or "File browser" will work though I suspect the correct one to use is "Open folder" as noted below. Both open a nautilus window.

### Post by Rasa1111 on 2011-01-20
edit: oops, too slow. lol

It is actually called **"Open File"**, not "file browser". 

SO,

 Go to your **"Places"** menu..

Open up **"Computer"**.

Once in the **"Computer"** folder..
 Open the "**File system".**

 Once in **"FIle System"**

FInd your **"Home"** folder.. 

Right click on your **"home"** folder.. 

and choose **"open with other application".**

When the list of programs/apps comes up..

Scroll down to find **"Open File". **

Select "Open File" , **and make sure the checkbox for  > "Remember this application for "folder" files" at the bottom is ticked. **
As yetiman said. 

Then just click the **"Open"** button, at the bottom right. 

 Now you should be able to double click any folder and have it open properly.
