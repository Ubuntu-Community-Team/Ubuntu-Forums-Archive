### Post by Mirge on 2009-05-04
Is there any "easy" way to port over my existing Ubuntu 9.04 setup onto another computer? Basically, this is my work PC, and when I have to travel I take my laptop with me, and essentially I'd like the exact same setup on my laptop as I have on my desktop. Hardware is different, so I wouldn't want to re-use video settings, etc... but things like software I have installed, configuration of my taskbar, etc.

Any thoughts? Thanks!

---

### Post by Woody1987 on 2009-05-04
What i would do in this situation is simply install a fresh copy of ubuntu on your laptop. Copy over your entire Home directory including hidden files from you work to your laptop. Then add the relevant programs. The reason for including all the hidden files is that they will have in them the settings for all your programs and configurations.

### Post by cariboo on 2009-05-04
You could use something like [Remastersys]("http://www.geekconnection.org/remastersys/remastersystool.html") to create an image of your installation and transfer it to your laptop.
