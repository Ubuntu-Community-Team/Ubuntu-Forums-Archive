### Post by jjex22 on 2011-11-28
Hi there, just to warn you now, this is an almost impossible question to answer - there are just so many laptops out there, you'd first have to narrow down by what kind of laptop you want - price, region you're in etc, then any suggestions you get aren't even guaranteed because over the life of a laptop model almost any of the components in it -trackpad, motherboard, graphics card - you name it, can be changed by the manufacturer without any change to the name or the model of the laptop, or it's description to most vendors. Then different manufacturers may go be different names in different regions, or the specs of the same model may be different from one region to the next.

By example my netbook's model number on the case is different to the model number a crucial scan comes up with - because it's a UK model, not a US one. Then there's the fact that any vendor will simply list it as a NC10, despite there being 4 different versions in the UK, with at least two different motherboards, let alone wireless cards, etc.

Generally the advice is buy known brands - I have best luck with Samsung and Dell, Don't buy cutting edge - the newer the hardware, the more issues,  basically try and get the most common hardware possible - the more users, the more fixers! I learnt this with the NC10 - it was one of the most popular netbooks of the day, so there was a guide to everything online! Ofcourse the vast majority of hardware can be made to run Ubuntu these days, you just have to spend more time playing about with drivers.

Another good way I find, if rather dark, is scroll though the beginners and laptop & hardware forums - people often list the model numbers of devices they are having REAL trouble with ;)
