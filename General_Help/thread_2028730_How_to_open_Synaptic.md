### Post by JC Cheloven on 2012-07-18
... or press the super (windows) key, click on the apps icon, click on the filter at the top-right of the window, click on the system tools category, an synaptic should appear over there. If not, click on the "show more" button that appears on the "installed" category.

I know that's terrible, but I tell you because it's the more similar thing to a classic menu that we have in Unity. You might want to know for emergency cases (when you don't remember the name of the app you want).

EDIT: 
... or open a terminal (Ctrl-Alt-T), and type ```
gksudo synaptic
``` The program will run, and its icon will appear on the launcher. You can take the opportunity to make it permanent in the launcher.

Best
JC
