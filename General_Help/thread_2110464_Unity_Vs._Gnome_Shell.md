### Post by timgood on 2013-01-30
There's absolutely nothing stopping you from having both and trying them out to see which you like. I find that desktop integration is better in Unity (though I dislike the interface) and the minimal desktop of Gnome Shell is very usable. I found some things like notifications worked better in Unity, though a dedicated Gnome 3 distro like Fedora seems to have sorted these. Give them both a whirl - install Ubuntu and then 'sudo apt-get install gnome-shell' and try it out. You can always uninstall it if you don't like it.

### Post by jim_deadlock on 2013-01-30
I like both but I use Gnome Shell because it just seems easier to get around. For example, to display the launcher you whizz your mouse up to the corner and it appears, along with a choice of open windows. Unity does the same thing but involves more clicking and keyboard shortcuts to achieve the same result. Also, I think Gnome handles desktop switching better, it automatically creates as many as you need and again you can drag stuff between desktops without having to click anything (or brutally force the window off the side of your screen). I also prefer the way notifications pop up at the bottom and stay there until you move your mouse, so you don't miss any if you go away for a while. I also happen to think Gnome looks more elegant, the razor-straight Unity launcher looks clunky to me.

The one thing I really miss about Unity is the global menus. Compiz is nice too but the novelty of the cube and pretty effects soon wears off.

Edit: another plus for Gnome is the web-based extensions manager, that's really cool.
