### Post by jespdj on 2008-01-09
> **LauraSakura said:**
> Its only on one monitor, as I use Ubuntu on my laptop with no issue. I'm beginning to think it had something to do with my video card or drivers, as it doesnt matter if I'm reading much or playing games. Also, does it make sense for my monitor to say it is running as analog instead of digital?
Is it a CRT or an LCD monitor?

Using an analog instead of a digital connection can certainly make display quality worse, making text more blurry, so you'll have to strain your eyes more (even when you don't consciously notice it all the time). If your computer and monitor have a digital connection (DVI, HDMI, DisplayPort), you should always use that instead of the analog connection (D-Sub / VGA) - it will make the quality better and text will be sharper and easier to read.
