### Post by argedion on 2010-12-13
There are programs like Gparted that can help however you really need to know what your doing before you mess with partitions and their tools. Before you attempt anything you should do a full backup of your files over to an external drive. Also you need to know how much more space you need before you start (may have to do some math) If you are passing the experimental stage you may want to just consider doing a dual boot. Many in here have a dual boot setup including myself. I find that this is a much better setup than doing anything in Windows. You could use Disk Utility in your Ubuntu (System>Administration>Disk Utility) Again DO A FULL BACK UP BEFORE MESSING WITH PARTITIONS.
