### Post by cscj01 on 2010-05-21
I just upgraded to Lucid from Karmic.  Since I do not like the way Ubuntu categorizes applications in some cases, I customize my main menu.  For example, I do a lot of photographic work.  Therefore I prefer to create a folder called Photography under Applications and place all my photography applications under Photography.  However, Ubuntu likes to put most of my photography applications under Graphics (i.e., Gimp, UFRaw, Hugin, etc.).  To date, the only way I know to do this is to create a new item for each of the application entries I want in the Photography folder I have created.  Essentially, I add an item under Photography and copy the information from the entry under Graphics to the new one under Photography.  I then uncheck the entry under Graphics so I do not have the application available in two different folders.  And I do this for other applications such as financial, security, and personal applications.  This is tedious and time consuming.

Is there another way to do this such as drag and drop?  I have had to do this manual customization for every upgrade since changing to Ubuntu with Hardy.
