### Post by aysiu on 2006-07-09
I'll expand a bit further on Buffalo Soldier's take:

There are some things you can do easily in both desktop environments. Some things are easier to do in Gnome.

There are some things you can do in both DEs, but KDE has a point-and-click "obvious" way to do it, and Gnome requires you to go to *gconf-editor* or to manually edit a configuration file.

Then, there are some things Gnome just plain can't do--these are few and far between, but they do exist.

Nevertheless, some prefer Gnome because of its simplicity and say that KDE offers to many options to the point of being "confusing."
