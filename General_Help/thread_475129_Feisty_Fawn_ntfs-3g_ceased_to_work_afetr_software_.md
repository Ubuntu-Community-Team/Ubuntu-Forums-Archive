### Post by szaka on 2007-06-15
Move the ntfs-3g lines in the fstab to the end of the file. Ntfs-config inserted them into the wrong place. This bug is fixed in the latest ntfs-config release.

Please also submit an Ubuntu bug report that the automounting of NTFS partitions depends on the order of the fstab entries. Ubuntu developers should install the the ntfs-3g and fuse binaries onto the root file system to ensure successful mounts during boot.

### Post by szaka on 2007-06-17
> **merlinus said:**
> The former is correct -- no separate /usr partition.  I do have two partitions that are listed after the ntfs, though.

Mounting NTFS doesn't depend on the two parititons after its line in your case. This is why I wrote in the first place that successful mount depends on the order of the fstab entries. All entries on which NTFS mounting depends on must be before its entry in fstab. Puting the NTFS entries at the end of fstab ensures this. It guarantes that its mount dependencies are satisified but this not always necessary.

When Ubuntu developers will install both NTFS-3G and FUSE with --exec-prefix=/ then the ntfs-3g fstab entries can be put anywhere.
