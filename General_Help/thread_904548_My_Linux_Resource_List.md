### Post by NewDisciple on 2008-08-29
I have decided to post a list of my linux resources that I use for reference.  This list is not all inclusive as many are threads from this forum.  Additionally I have made word documents out of hundreds, maybe even thousands of linux how-tos and other info.  I currently have 20 large notebooks and three file cabinets filled with said documents.
Why?  I like learning about linux and I like to experiment.  When you bork your system as a result of experimenting which I have done so many times that I have lost count it sure is nice to have a hard copy of a tutorial which pertains to your problem.
I also save linux information even if its unrelated to what I am doing at the time just because you never know when you might need it.  I also do all of this because I am definitely not a command line warrior or computer guru.  In fact I discovered the computer rather late in life, having started four years ago and with linux three years ago.  At 57 I need all the help I can get.
This list does not contain links so you will need to copy & paste the urls.  I suggest copying the entire list to a word document as that will allow you to peruse it at your own convenience.  After going through this list you should also gain a greater understanding of how to look for information.  Which, is the secret to any success I might enjoy.  Some of these resources are not intended for newbies as these were for the most part copied from my bookmarks, but they will show areas that you may eventually arrive at as you expand your knowledge base.
Please feel free to add to this list.
