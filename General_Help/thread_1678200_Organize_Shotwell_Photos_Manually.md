### Post by Dragonbite on 2011-02-07
If you are looking for the ability to place images in certain folders for events then you might want to look at DigiKam. While it is a KDE-orientated application it does work in Gnome.  

With DigiKam, when you choose to import pictures you choose which Album to place them in, or create a new one.  This Album is the folder where the pictures will be placed and it can be any number of levels as you want.

I've been moving to Shotwell lately because our Nikon camera will not be recognized unless F-Spot first recognizes it, and trying to make the most of it.  Plus it took almost 3 minutes per picture to import, compared to the 45 seconds it does in F-Spot and Shotwell.  I think it is either a configuration thingy, or missing library that it has to time-out before continuing.

The other feature I like about DigiKam is the ability to right-click on a button and copy it.  This makes it very easy to place the picture in an email or document.  F-Spot and Shotwell don't provide for this, you have to go to the folder where it resides and find it.

The only other thing I can think of to look for is Picasa for Linux.  It runs in its own Wine session but the last time I looked at it, it worked pretty well.
