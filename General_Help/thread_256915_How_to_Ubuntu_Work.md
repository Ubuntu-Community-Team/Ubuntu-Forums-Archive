### Post by peabody on 2006-09-13
> **cobbweb said:**
> but I have to know that what I am contributing towards isn't just being sold and going to some business guys pocket making a roalty.  


This is a large point of the GPL software license.  While individuals are allowed to charge for the software, they have to give you source code, and they have to allow you to distribute it freely.  They also cannot lock up the software in their own proprietary product that you are forced to compete with.  In other words, if they distribute binaries, they have to distribute their modifications to the source code as well, and they have to allow free distribution of those modifications, once they've distributed it.

The BSD and MIT licenses on the other hand, don't require that you freely distribute modifications, so those are ones to watch out for.  They're not bad per se, but anyone can essentially take BSD or MIT licensed code and create their own proprietary version of it.

Understanding software licensing will go a long way if you're concerned that someone is going to steal your work.
