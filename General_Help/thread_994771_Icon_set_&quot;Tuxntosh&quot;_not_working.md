### Post by Ouguiya on 2008-11-27
Update: Ok, I think I figured this one out:

The situation is as follows:

Some guy created all those icons, since he is a real linux AND mac fan. Since he used MAC, but didn't want to miss his linux, he created MAC-Linux icons.

He later simply transformed them to png, allowed people to download it, and voila.

Soon after, someone took those icons, and made a KDE and a Gnome theme from them.

This seems kind of nice and all, with just one problem: All this is already 5 years old.
The last update to all those icons was made in 2003, and, as sad as this may seem, no one has made a diffrent set of Tux n Tosh icons yet, or took up the idea. That's the reason why you still often see those Tux-Apples around, since no one took up the idea since then.

So, for a reintroduction of said tux code, things look grim. An author which hasn't sent a word since 5 years cannot be expected to simply show up again in "*bling* here I am!"-Style.

Since I still want to penguinize my Ubuntu, I shall take the hard approach: Taking all the icons, resizing them, writing an own index.theme, and make a new, gnome 2.x upward compatible theme.

Any interested in this version (as soon as I have it done, since I get the feeling this will take me some time, as my experience in creating item themes is nil), please write in here, or PM me, then I will also take the time to upload it to some popular sites, so it's downloadable once again.

Thanks also to eternalnewbee, who helped me confirm that this was indeed an icon theme error, and not a messed up configuration on my part.

Yours,

Ouguiya
