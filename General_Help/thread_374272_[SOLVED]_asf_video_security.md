### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-03-03
You know worst scenario, you may screw up your home directory. If there was a malicious code (a virus, trojan, etc.) in there, without root access, it is contained to your home directory. You then would just need to setup a new user. There may be some data loss, for whatever is in the home directory, but the core of the system will remain intact. That is why you never put in your root password, especially if it randomly comes up. You wouldn't never a complete stranger your car keys if they asked?

Keep in mind this warning you got means nothing though, the content just does not match the extension. There is no threat at all.
