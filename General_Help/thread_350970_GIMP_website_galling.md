### Post by Paerez on 2007-02-01
Unlike the world of paid software, open source software does not focus on the marketing and sale of its products. Those at Gimp are more concerned with creating the coolest and most useful piece of software they can.

I actually enjoy this style of web site since there is very little handwaving and annoying click-twelve-times-to-get-where-you-want. It is very up front, clean, and clear.

Just to reiterate: Open Source web sites are not designed to sell to users. They are designed to help work on the program and download the program.

Also, Important Links is actually very relevant to the Gimp. They concern the license for distribution, a way of reporting bugs to help the program improve, etc.

EDIT: I forgot to mention that swears are only useful to convey anger, which is not a productive emotion, thus they are frowned upon on a web site designed to help others.

I never quite saw where you listed what you were looking for? Along with the docs page you showed, [http://www.gimp.org/tutorials/](http://www.gimp.org/tutorials/) might be of help. The Gimp User Group might also be useful: [http://gug.sunsite.dk/](http://gug.sunsite.dk/)

IMHO, that was an excellent decision. Flaming the GIMP Team, one of the larger of the free software projects, on their own bugzilla hardly seems like a productive way to spend time. If you'd like to pitch in and help, [http://www.gimp.org/develop/](http://www.gimp.org/develop/) will lead you in the correct direction.

A quote from that last link, that pretty much restates Paerez' earlier comment:
> In the free software world, there is generally no distinction between users and developers. As in a friendly neighbourhood, everybody pitches in to help their neighbors. Please consider the time you give in assistance to others as payment.
