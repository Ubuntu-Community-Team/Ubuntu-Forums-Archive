### Post by mrsticks on 2015-03-07
It all started so innocently enough.  Upgrade Dad's dying platter drive with an SSD.  At the same time go from Xubuntu 12.04 x86 to Xubuntu 14.04 x64.  The only changed hardware in play here is a new Sandisk SSD.  All parts are about 2.5 years old.  Dad plays cards, browses google news and reads email, not doing any heavy lifting on his desktop.

So hardware:

mobo - Gigabyte M68MT-S2, 8GB RAM
SSD - SanDisk SDSSDA120G
onboard video - VGA compatible controller: NVIDIA Corporation C61 [GeForce 7025 / nForce 630a] (rev a2)

I did a fresh install of 14.04 x64 on this SSD, and saw major LCD freezing issues from the start.  Just opening thunderbird and firefox would cause the mouse to start slow down horribly, then eventually the entire system would freeze.  I suspected the video driver.  I installed the xorg-edgers ppa and installed nvidia-current and rebooted.  This did not help the cause.

So I purged the ppa and those drivers and went the GUI route.  I am currently using the "NVIDIA legacy binary driver - version 304.125 from nvidia-304 (proprietary, tested)".  This has been the best performing driver of the day.  But on boot I am now seeing a screen, mostly black (but backlit), with dashes of color in the top third of the screen.  The cursor is visible and right clicking will produce the context menu.  I can switch to a text login (CTRL + ALT + F1) and than back to (CTRL+ALT+F7) and the screen corrects itself.  It's here I can see the context menu.

I found this page, [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Troubleshooting/BlankScreen](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Troubleshooting/BlankScreen), and in an attempt to collect more data, I updated /etc/default/grub to include some extra kernel args:

GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT="vesafb.invalid=1 drm.debug=0xe vesafb.nonsense=1 plymouth:debug"

I've made a video of the boot process, with me going back to F1 and F7 again at the end.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SsYklfQBHyE](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SsYklfQBHyE)

I've attached all relevant files I can think of.  The xorg.conf was generated by the NVIDIA X Server Settings app.

Any help or hints you can provide would be greatly appreciated, thanks!
