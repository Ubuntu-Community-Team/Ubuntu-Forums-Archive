### Post by timothylegg on 2017-11-30
I have a lot of electrical components that I've acquired over the years, as well as gears, linkages, motors, special tools and some not-so-special tools.  Presently, I spend upwards of 50% of my time searching through boxes to find parts that I remember having, but also may have given to maker friends.  My storage consists of shelves, cabinets, maybe 20 plastic totes, 20 tackle boxes, and 2000+ storage trays arranged in several 10x25 racks hung along my wall (yes, I'm also into ham radio, experimental aircraft, bicycles and homemade gadgets).

It would seem that there should be a web-based front-end to an SQL database where container types can be defined, maybe even with attributes such as physical dimensions and location, and then become created so that the records may be filled with the description of their contents.

One approach I had is to use a wiki.  For example, give one of these storage racks a name and have that name exist as a sign above the rack.  And then populate the wiki page for this rack with 250 entries, one for each tray in the rack (that they themselves may be a page of their own or also be vacant).  So if I want to build something with a 7-segment display, I search for "7[FONT=sans-serif] segment" and can find where my [/FONT][FONT=sans-serif]74LS47 chips are as well as my LED display modules that are, for example, stored in "Rack 7, Column E, Row 18" or find a prototyping box in "Storage Tote K".
[/FONT]
What tool can help me organize my maker-hoarder lifestyle?  By the way, if anybody needs some 4017 decade counters for their homemade clock project, I have about 100 of them from Hamvention 2002, but only if you help me find them.  I last saw them 12 years ago, in one of these boxes...
