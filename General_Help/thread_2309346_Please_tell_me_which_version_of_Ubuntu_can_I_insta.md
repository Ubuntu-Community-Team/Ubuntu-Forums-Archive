### Post by DuckHook on 2016-01-09
Hi robi4 and welcome to Ubuntu and the forums.

There is little that you can do to improve on the OS front. Lubuntu is already one of the lightest weight flavours going. Another flavour is not going to make an appreciable difference to your experience.

The bigger issue for you is that you are probably surfing with Lubuntu's default browser. Firefox is very resource intensive, as are all the main browsers. They have to be, to support all of the modern Audio and Video bells and whistles that people have come to expect.

More physical RAM would help. It always does.

But the biggest help will probably come from installing a light (and feature poor) alternate browser like Midori or Links2. Now, it is very important to note that these browsers will not even come close to Firefox or Chrome in looks, functionality, or versatility. They only allow you to look at static content. If you want flash sites, 3-D sites, streaming video or audio, etc, Midori is problematic, and Links2 just won't work at all on them.

There is no magic browser that will let you get all of the eye- and ear-candy and is yet light and nimble. You must choose one or the other, but not both. If you want the spectacle the explosions and the flashing lights, then it's time for another computer.

My computer - with 747 MB of RAM, ran Xubuntu 12.04 great!  Xubuntu 14.04 seems sluggish on it, so I tried Lubuntu and had the same issue with hanging.  I installed Xfce4 (*not* xubuntu-desktop, just Xfce) and it runs quick and nimble, hang-free.  I find Seamonkey (a Mozilla project that does what Firefox and Thunderbird do - integrated like Opera, only faster and simpler) to use much less of my computer's RAM and CPU than Firefox and Thunderbird do.  Check out Ubuntuzilla on these Forums for how to get Seamonkey!
