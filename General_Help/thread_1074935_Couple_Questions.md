### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-19
As to the first, it depends on what you mean by "music manager".  As for music *players*, you can't get much lighter than mplayer, since you can run it from the command line.  The GUI version has some limited playlist functionality, but nothing like what you'd find in something like Rhythmbox or Amarok.  

As to the second, bittorrent has pretty much eclipsed every other P2P protocol.  For music stores, I'd go with Wal-mart, Amazon, or any other site that sells mp3s.  Mp3 isn't as good as aac (usually found in mp4 or m4a files) in terms of sound quality, but you don't have to worry about DRM'ed files.
