### Post by Hector23 on 2011-03-02
I installed 10.04 on an SSD drive and everything was very straightforward. In Ooo, thought, I had problems writing " with a Canadian multilingual keyboard. When I pressed the key, I had, alternatively, « and ». Those 2 characters should be and are indeed on ALT CAR, Z and X, respectively. So there's no need to repeat them on the " key, which is still useful in French for feet -- as in 15" from the ground -- or for quotes inserted within quotes.

Both Kunbuntu and, if I remember well, LinuxMint, have got this right. I wonder how come there is a problem with Ubuntu. The fix, though, is easy. In Ooo -- the only place where this problem appears --  you go to Tools, Correction options, Custom quotes, Double quotes, and change both Start and End quotes by clicking the quote and chosing the second character at the top of the list in Basic Latin. Which is ".

That this kind of problem would be solved in Correction options wasn't evident at all. This problem should be fixed.

Then, there was the dictionaries problem. While I installed I grabbed aspell and hunspell and my spell and pretty much everything I thought could be useful. Then, while writing in Evolution the word "instinct" caused no error but, if I wrote "l'instinct de survie" -- the survival instinct -- an error was indicated. If I wrote only "instinct de survie", there was no problem. I thought the problem was with the apostrophy not being the right character. I searched and found nothing.

So I installed the whole paraphernalia of dictionaries, which took about 15 minutes. The space occupied on my 6 GB SSD / partition went from 51 to 57%. (I installed on the SSD as on a standard drive.) Everything si now OK. but this is downright ridiculous. Though there are a few differences between US and British English, French spelling is the same all over the world, be it in France, Switzerland, Belgium or Canada. There might be colloquial expressions or words proper to every country, but they could be included in the same dictionary. And there's not a different French for Ooo, The Gimp or Thunderbird.

This should be fixed. One click should install the one and only French dictionary for all applications. Different clicks should install US and British dictionary. I wouldn't believe that the Australian and Zairian dictionaries should be that different from the British one. If they have to be included, a 10 k additional file should do the trick. So what the hell are all those MB of dictionaries for? Can hyphenation account for those extra megabytes? I doubt it.

Another problem arose but, this time, with LinuxMint, which I first tried to install. While updating after installation, I received a message asking if I wanted the last empty line in configuration files to be erased. I knew this wasn't very standard procedure but the message said that developers providing the software asked for this priviledge and, since those developers had been audited by Ubuntu and LinuxMint, I thought maybe the standard formating of configuration files had changed. So, despite the message saying that the default answer was "No", I gave my OK. After the installation, I went on the net to see what I could find about this message and there was nothing. I thought maybe servers providing LinuxMint weren't very well protected and I installed Ubuntu.

Does anybody have any idea of what happened?
