About freedom, freenet.

As part of the resurge i am doing on anonymity in various distributions for Linux. As a extend i write howto's installing these applications in disro's.
A couple of days ago i tried to install freenet 0.5 on a Ubuntu 6.06 system. It failed. I had used freenet before but then on a windoze system and i had good experience at it. As good costume i searched the internet for howto's to get me going. I found none. This is weird becouse v0.5 is developed for at lease 15 years. It didn't take me long to discover that all the info on 0.5 is manually deleted or removed. A google search gives little or non howto's, there were 0.5 is mentioned, it is about upgrading to 0.7.
Anyway, to get things done i joined #freenet-chat, it resulted in “0.5 is not supported any more”. Yet there was questioning on WHY i wanted 0.5 and not 0.7, i didn't feel like answering because it is my freedom to use open source any way i want. Same as it is your freedom not to help any one. I accept that. But to actively censor a open source project (even if it is your own) is evil. I will have no part in this. On the opposite. The last days i spent reading in the 0.5-0.7 myths. 

Come to a point.
In order to present and implement freenet to Linux distros we need a stable version. Therefore 0.5 is our best guess. It would benefit your project if you assist us. We discussed 0.7 and came to the conclusion that the concept of exchanging node-reference-key could be a security hazard.  Also 0.7 is not stable. How long did it take to make a stable version of 0.5? How long will it take to make one for 0.7? To spread and implement 0.5 in Linux would speed up the process of development on both version. In the end the best will win. This is done not by boycotting but by freedom and support of both versions.

If you have backups from old forums, chat-logs, bbs, howto's, change-logs, builds, bug-reports ect please make them public again or contact us at [email]harpitap@yahoo.com[/email]

As for now i will stop my research on freenet for obvious reason.
