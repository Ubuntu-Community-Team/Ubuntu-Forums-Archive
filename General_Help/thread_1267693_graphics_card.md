### Post by P4man on 2009-09-16
Any ATI or nVidia card you can buy today should work if that is your only requirement. If you're looking to pick up something cheap off ebay, I suggest you go for nVidia Geforce 7 or later, to be assured of up to date driver support or an ATI Radeon 8500 or newer.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-09-16
If you're running 8.04, then look for an 8000-series card. I had an Nvidia 8600 for a while, worked fine on Hardy. They are old now, but so is Ubuntu 8.04.

If you're going to update to a more recent Ubuntu, or you don't mind manually installing the driver, then you could go for a 9000-series or a GTX-something.

Note that cards aren't actually branded ATI or Nvidia, they are made by other manufacturers using ATI or Nvidia chipsets. So, for instance, a Galaxy 9600 is an Nvidia-based card. A Sapphire 4850 is an ATI card. The logo of the chipset manufacturer is always on the box.
