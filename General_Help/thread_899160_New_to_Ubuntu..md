### Post by ad_267 on 2008-08-24
Welcome to Ubuntu.

I suggest you try out the native Linux applications first before resorting to Wine unless they have features you have to use and stick with a dual boot until you feel comfortable.

The wine appdb site has information on installing most applications and an indication of how well they work in wine. [http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/)

For ventrillo you could use Ekiga or Skype (not really sure though as I haven't used ventrillo) and Rhythmbox, Amarok, Exaile or Banshee could be a replacement for Itunes. Most people seem to be able to get WoW running well in Wine but theres also some good native Linux games like Savage, Nexuiz, Alien Arena, Enemy Territory Quake Wars and Tremulous. Have a look at [http://gaming.gwos.org/doku.php](http://gaming.gwos.org/doku.php).

This is always good to read if you're new to Ubuntu as installing applications is one of the major differences: [http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/](http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/)
Edit: That link above doesn't seem to be working right now but check it out later.
