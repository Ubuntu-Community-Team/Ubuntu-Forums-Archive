Hi,

This is a little off beat, but I recently preordered a
[URL="http://ardrone.parrot.com/parrot-ar-drone/usa/"]remote control
wifi helicopter[/URL] that is controlled by an iphone.

I'm thinking to myself, "the only thing that is cooler than a
helicopter controlled over wifi with a phone is doing it long distance
using a directional antenna."

I currently have a netbook, some usb 802.11 adapters, and a
[cantenna]("http://www.cantenna.com/").

Now, the toy is only controllable by an iphone or a ipod touch.  I
would like to have Ubuntu act as a wireless repeater, like below.

iphone --> ~802.11~ --> wlan0 onboard --> Ubuntu forwards packets --> wlan1 usb connected to Cantenna --> ~802.11~ --> helicopter.

that sounds really cool. I just want to caution you that helicopters require pretty quick reaction time from the controller....I'm not great at it, and I know some who can't do it at all...that being said, that is a person.

If the wireless or repeaters slow down the inputs even slightly you'll find it very difficult to do anything but crash. You'll end up chasing your tail all the time and never really being in control.

Good luck though. Keep us posted as to the progress.
