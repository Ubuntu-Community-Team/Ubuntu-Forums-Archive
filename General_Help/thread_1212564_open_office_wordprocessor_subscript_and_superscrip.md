### Post by newagelink on 2009-07-13
If you intend to prepare scientific documents, I highly recommend the LyX Processor, available from the Ubuntu repository: Applications -> Add/Remove... -> Show: "All Open Source applications" and Search: "lyx". It will make your life immensely easier: you supply the content, it supplies the formatting. (I began learning it by reading through its Help files, which are very helpful.)

As for OpenOffice's Word Processor, highlight character -> Format -> Character -> Position -> click Superscript or Subscript radio buttons, etc. If you were using LyX, it would be a very simple typing of ^ for an exponent, and _ for an underscore (using Math mode).

### Post by newagelink on 2009-07-22
Well, look at the menu bar, I see letters that are underlined corresponding to the Alt key. So ... for
[LIST]
[*]superscript: Highlight character, hold down Alt key and press "o,h,s,o" (in that order).
[*]subscript: Highlight character, hold down Alt key and press "o,h,b,o" (in that order).
[/LIST]
To resume typing normally, hold down Alt key and press "o,h,n,o".

Significantly faster than clicking through the menus, this navigates through them via keyboard. Maybe not what you'd like, but it *is* a "keyboard shortcut".
