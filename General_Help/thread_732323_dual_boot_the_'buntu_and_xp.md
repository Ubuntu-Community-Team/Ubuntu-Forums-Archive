### Post by Nathan_M on 2008-03-22
Yeah, it's pretty easy. Did it myself when I was just trying Ubuntu, then made the switch permanent. It's just a case of deleting the xp partition in gparted and increasing the size of the ubuntu partition. If you have a shared fat32 partition, you could just leave it as it is, or you may want to copy all the files over to the ubuntu partion, delete the fat partition, and stretch the ubuntu one some more. When you come to do it, if you need help just ask back here, and someone will help.
What can XP do that ubuntu can't. Have you had a look at whether the particular program(s) you need run on Wine?
