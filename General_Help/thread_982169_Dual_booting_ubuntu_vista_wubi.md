### Post by wahoobob on 2008-11-14
You probably want to use WUBI or dual boot, _not both_.  I would try WUBI first and make sure you like it on your new machine for a couple of weeks, then note your settings, either by writing them down as you do them or by not making many, then delete WUBI and reinstall as a dual boot system.  WUBI doesn't leave the grub traces, etc so you won't screw up your boot the way you might to try the Ubuntu in its own partition (dual boot).  If you need any help, the new user forum is very helpful.
