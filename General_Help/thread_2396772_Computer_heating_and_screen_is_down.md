### Post by Impavidus on 2018-07-20
With such a long list of problems it's hard to troubleshoot. Also consider that Kubuntu 14.04 is quite old and getting close to end of life (nine months left). Kubuntu 16.04 is equally close to end of life (support for Kubuntu LTS releases went from 5 to 3 years), so you could consider jumping 2 versions ahead and try a fresh install of 18.04. But first I suggest running a live disk (Kubuntu 18.04). If that works fine, it's probably not a hardware issue.

### Post by sp40140 on 2018-07-20
You should provide details of your hardware, such as the make and model, dedicated video card or intel onboard?
Household environment matters a lot as well, if you have pets and / or smoking home, you should be cleaning the exausts quite often. Also, try cooling pad, use it on hard surfaces where the airflow isn't blocked, like on pillow or even lap.
These issues are operating system agnostic, they are much more dependent on the type of laptop and the environment it's being used in.
If you get consumer grade (cheap) laptop, it will start giving you trouble after three four years even if you have high end components in it.
On the other hand if you get enterprise grade laptop, even with middle of the pack components, you will get many more years of service out of them.
I would be really surprised if upgrading or even changing the os to windows improves this situation.
