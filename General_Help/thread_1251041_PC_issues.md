### Post by fela on 2009-08-27
It could be pretty much any kind of hardware failure. But I think it's the harddrive.

Do you hear a dreadful 'clicking' sound - or a sound like a fan whirring against something in the blades? If so, it's probably a harddrive physical failure. It could still be a HDD failure even if you don't hear a sound - the HDD circuit board could be blown (have you recently had a power surge or power supply failure?).

It could also be the motherboard. Does the system get past the POST (BIOS screen)? On OEM computers they normally have a Dell, HP, etc logo on the BIOS screen, on self builds it usually has a logo of the manufacturer of the motherboard (Gigabyte, Asus, Foxconn, etc.). Sometimes the BIOS screen is instead text, with something like 'award bios', or 'phoenix bios' or something in the corner with text flashing past quickly.

If it gets past the POST, the motherboard isn't malfunctioning. Is the CPU overclocked? It could be overheating. What do you mean by 'hang'? At what stage does the hang occur? Is it at the POST/BIOS screen? If so, the motherboard could well be malfunctioning.

It could also be dud RAM, although if so the hang/crash normally occurs much later in the game (often when you've already installed the system, but you might have a very bad stick of RAM indeed). Have you tried running memtest86+ off a CD or floppy? Try swapping different sticks of RAM in and out, see if it makes a difference.

And of course run the old, take out all non-essential hardware, see if it works. If it does work bring back your other hardware piece by piece, until you find the evil hardware.

Good luck! You'll need it! :P
