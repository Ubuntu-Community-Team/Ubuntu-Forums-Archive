### Post by Bartender on 2007-08-05
Dex -
It's a weird paradox - so many things are so easy now in Linux.  Dial-up can still be a real pain.

Hurdle #1 - Your modem.  What kind is it?  If it's the typical cheap "winmodem" that comes inside 99% of the PC's made in this century, you may be able to get it working but it's not easy for the average newcomer to Linux.
Hurdle #2 - Linux dialer software.  There's wvdial, pppconfig, then GUI's like KPPP, gnome-ppp, the Modem Monitor in Dapper - the software works OK for the most part but unfamiliarity will cause trouble.
Hurdle #3 - Your ISP.  Some just don't play nice with Linux.  Any one of them that requires you to install their software is either difficult or impossible.  Juno, PeoplePC, MSN, etc.

So take some time to analyze your situation, and try to post back with answers to hurdles #1 and #3.  I don't remember the command, but if you look at some of the other "dial-up" threads there's a command that should tell you what your modem is.

Oh, yeah, please take a look at this.  It's my  [attempt]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717") to help out on dial-up issues.
