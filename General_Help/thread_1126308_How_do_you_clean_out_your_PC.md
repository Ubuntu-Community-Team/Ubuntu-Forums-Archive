### Post by canoemoose on 2009-04-15
I have a needle adaptor used for pumping up footballs attached to a bicycle pump.  I use it to blow all the dust out as it even gets it out of heatsinks.  I'm sure I've used a hoover before with no ill effects.  At work experience we used a compressed air line.
The best solution, of course, is to put a filter over the air inlets to catch the dust before it goes inside the case.

### Post by 3Miro on 2009-04-15
I unplug everything (and stop of course) and then I use wet wipes. Just make sure they are wet and not soaking/dripping.

The moisture gathers the dust without spreading it out (if you use a vacuum to suck, there are many palaces that can be missed, and for blow you need to do it outside). Then I make sure it is try ( wet-wipes have some alcohol in them and that evaporates almost instantly ). Then turn it back on.

The problem is if there is really a lot of dust, but I try to do this regularly enough so I never get there.
