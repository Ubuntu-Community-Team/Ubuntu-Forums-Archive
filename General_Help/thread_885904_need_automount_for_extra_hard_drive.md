### Post by hal8000 on 2008-08-10
Mount points for an an internal hard drive can be fixed and mounted automauically, the option "defaults" in your /etc/fstab can take care of this.

Post the outputs of:
fdisk -l

and also:
cat /etc/fstab

USB drives are not the same. Because they are unplugged and replugged then the mount point can change  as the HAL daemon and USB subsystem takes care of mounting the device.

You need to be clear what you want to do. If your data is on an internal hard drive, IDE or SATA then this can be controlled, a USB drive is different but there is a way round this also.

Post the outputs from the above commands first.
