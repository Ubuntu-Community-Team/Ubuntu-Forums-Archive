That makes sense, 1 GB of RAM is realistic. Computer sold now come with 1 GB usually, 512 at the least. My computer has 2.5 GB of RAM. (512 MB == .5 GB)

Installing Ubuntu is easy, the guide shows you how. It is all GUI :-)

Compiz is the effects you see. GNOME is the desktop envirnoment and Compiz is the Window manager. With Compiz, you will want to install the settings manager, and the plug ins. To do that, you go to System->Synaptic Package Manager and search for "compiz". You get a list of things, just find the settings manager and the plugins and check them off and apply the change.

Most users aren't programmers. In Linux, all settings are stored in text files. (Windows uses a registry) Sometimes, you may see advice to ask you to post the contents of files, or to edit them. Normally, you don't need to.

### Post by fela on 2008-03-23
if it's an old computer, especially a laptop, it probably won't run compiz. Even new laptops sometimes have built in graphics that struggles with those desktop effects. And on a laptop, your more likely to notice lag with compiz (as built in graphics shares the main CPU and doesn't have it's own GPU)
