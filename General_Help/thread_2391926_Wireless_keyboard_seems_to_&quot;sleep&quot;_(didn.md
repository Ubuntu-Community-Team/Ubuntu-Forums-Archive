### Post by qantens on 2018-05-14
Hi, Folks, much appreciation if you can help.

I have a Logitech K260 keyboard/mouse combo wireless USB. It worked fine under Ubuntu 17.10.

Now the mouse continues to work fine. The keyboard works fine except if I haven't used it for a few minutes. Then it seems to have gone to "sleep". I touch lots of keys on it and maybe ten seconds later it comes back to life and works.

Is it possible something is putting it to sleep? It's rather annoying. I tried fresh batteries (it wasn't the problem). I tried switching the USB port the fob was plugged into (no joy). I'm so frustrated, I'm considering getting a wired keyboard...

Please help me if you can. :)

UPDATE: I think I solved it. It seems the USB wifi fob was too close to other USB connections (drives in my case), and this was causing interference. I moved the fob to the front of my tower, away from the other USB connected items, and it seems to work now. I did this after having visited [http://support.logitech.com/en_us/product/wireless-combo-mk260/faq#link_38032](http://support.logitech.com/en_us/product/wireless-combo-mk260/faq#link_38032) 

It's not sustainable having the wifi USB fob in the front of the tower for me, as I might knock it off with my leg, given how my system is set up, so I've ordered a USB extender cable and will sort it out for a more permanent solution that way.

Thanks to anyone out there who puzzled over this problem! :)

PS If you're wondering why I said it didn't do that with Ubuntu 17.10, well, I think it was because that, at the same time I switched to 18.04, I also hooked up three external USB hard drives to the machine. So here I was thinking it was the change of Ubuntu version that caused the problem when it was something else entirely that just happened to occur at the same time (ie, my setting up my system with three external USB hard drives). :eek:;):D
