### Post by daniel of sarnia on 2007-05-04
Do you have your computer set up to something other than US or UK English. I always have this problem. after I install ubuntu and put toronto canada for my time zone. The spell check gets set up for Canadian English. But there is only US and UK dictionary's. 

So what you have to do is open up open office click tools then click options, then the options window will pop up. From there click on Language settings on the left side, then under language settings and above writing aids you will see Languages. Click on that and set the "Default languages for documents" to US or UK English. 

That should do it, if not I don't know what the problem is.

As a side note, although "Canadian English" is listed, their is no real Canadian English. That's what my grade 12 English teacher told me. She said that was are supposed to use UK or US English but not mix the two in any one document. I just wish ubuntu would put it on one or the other by default when set up in Canada so that I don't have to go back and change it every time I install or have to tell my friends, family, and other people over and over because it dose not do it by default.
