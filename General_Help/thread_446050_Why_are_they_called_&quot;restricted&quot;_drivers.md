### Post by stchman on 2007-05-16
I have never understood that term for the nvidia and ATI proprietary drivers.  Also the non-free terms as well.  It give a very negative connotation to them.  I have never known a company that charges you for a driver if you purchase the hardware so in essence they are FREE.  The term proprietary or closed source should be use instead of restricted or non-free.

Just because a vendor does not give you the source code does not make them bad.  I am of the mindset that if I buy a piece of hardware I am entitled to the driver from the manufacturer.  I want my hardware to work I personally don't care that much if the vendor does not supply the source code.

### Post by iAlta on 2007-05-16
In the world of opensourse, there are two types of free. Free as in 'free beer' and free as in 'free speech'. 

When you hear someone talking about 'free' it is mostly the latter, unless noted otherwise.
