### Post by Ramses de Norre on 2007-03-22
You only get that cube with a special keystroke and you can't work on your desktop while you're moving the cube. It's just for show when switching workspaces, when you're doing anything normal on your pc you've just got one side of the cube so it looks like a regular desktop (besides the other effects).

The stuff with menu bars and such is configurable, and you can install as much window managers and desktop environments as your hard disk can handle (and there are MUCH of them out there ;) ).
They are just packages in the repos like all the other programs and you can choose which one to use when you log in.

About the 3D flipping: I haven't used vista yet but I can tell you that the beryl/compiz 3D effects are amazing and seem more advanced to me than the vista ones.
