### Post by outofnicks on 2007-03-31
cynniesue,
You can create a Desktop icon in Ubuntu with the following steps:

1.) Copy the link from the bar into the clipboard, by highlighting it completely with the mouse cursor, then press the Ctrl key, hold it down, and press "C" to load the URL into the clipboard.   Highlight, then Ctl-C .

2.)  Right-click on the Desktop, and choosing "Create Launcher...".

3.) A window pops up, looking like screenshot below if you are using a fresh install of Ubuntu. First, down where it says "Type", change "Application" to "Link" in the popup menu. Just click on it, you'll see.  Just above that, the word "Command" changes to "URL".

4.) In that new "URL" box, insert your mouse cursor by clicking in that box. 

5.) Paste your link from the Clipboard to that box just by using the key combo of Ctrl-V.

6.) Go up and add the Name to that link, it will appear under the icon. 
That's it. Click "OK". You'll see an icon appear on your Desktop, looking like the one above the browser window in the screenshot.

You can customize your link icons by right-clicking on one and choosing "Properties" in the popup menu.  Try out different Emblems, or even make your own icons and associate them with individual links.
