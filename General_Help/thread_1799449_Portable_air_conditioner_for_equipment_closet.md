### Post by decoherence on 2011-07-07
I've never seen anyone set this up in their home but I've certainly seen it in network equipment closets. If you've got a drain and a place to exhaust the hot air, you're set! 

I would guess that these two requirements are the main reason you don't see this being done more often in the home. Also, the additional noise and power draw are probably issues for some people. Electricity is extremely expensive where I live but we rarely need or want air conditioning ;)

### Post by decoherence on 2011-07-08
That's a good point. Taking the water out of the air can cause static electricity discharges. Ideally you want humidity somewhere in the range of 40 - 50 % but that might be hard to do in a desert.
