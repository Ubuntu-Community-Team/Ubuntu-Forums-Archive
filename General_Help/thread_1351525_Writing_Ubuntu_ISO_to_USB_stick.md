### Post by spydeyrch on 2009-12-10
Unetbootin is still a great way to do it. You can also use the usb startup installer built into ubuntu. It is located in the system menu -> admin. -> usb starter....... (down towards the bottom of the menu)
 
If you do the second one, you can install it on your netbook (actually both ways will do it for you), AND also keep it on your usb stick as an alternative pocket os. the cool thing about the one built into the ubuntu os, is that it creates a persistent file, so even though the usb is only a live version, you can store all your docuemtns, photos, and if there is enough space, your videos on it. then every time you boot up of of the usb, even though it will be just a live version, you will still have access to your personal files on that usb drive. You can make changes to them and save them and everything,. Then you shutdown the system, unplug the usb and off you go, ready to use it in another system if needs be. :D You cant make any permenant changes to the live system on the usb but to your personal files you can.
 
the down side is that it is only available on the linux side of things. So no version for windows, as far as I am aware. but unetbootin does have a windows variant and it works very well.
 
enjoy! :popcorn:
