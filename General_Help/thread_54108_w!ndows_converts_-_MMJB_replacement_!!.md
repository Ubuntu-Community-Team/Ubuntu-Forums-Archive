### Post by cobelloy on 2005-08-03
Hi there all, 

the thing I have missed the most about windows is playing music with Musicmatch Jukebox, it was a music library, music player and CD burner in one package, and there is nothing in Linux that can compare - because I was going about things the wrong way...

For the benefit of anyone else who misses their all-in-one music package I have just discovered the solution.

Download "**madman**" with either apt-get or synaptic, it is a music catalogue that scans for music and organises it by its ID3 tags (which you can also edit) and you can create playlists of your favorite songs. But, the best part is that it will also send your playlist, or individual songs to XMMS to play, or to K3B to burn !! (of course you also need to have XMMS and K3B aswell)

One of the big differences between software available for Windows vs Linux (apart from the cost difference!!) is that windows packages are often whole suites of stuff that work together, while linux packages just tend to do one thing only, but as it turns out there are also a lot of linux packages that can link together several things into a cohesive unit so that you end up with a similar end product.

I think I am finally getting it, I hope this little thread can help someone else out there too.
