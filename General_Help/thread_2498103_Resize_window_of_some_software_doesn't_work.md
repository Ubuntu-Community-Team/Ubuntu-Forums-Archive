### Post by Holger_Gehrke on 2024-05-30
Many programs have a minimum size for their window; some have a minimum size which depends on the contents of their window. These sizes are usually chosen to fit on a normal screen, but with display scaling this often leads to unexpected results. The easiest way to access off-screen parts of such a window is to move the window. In most desktop environments there is a way to do this even if the title bar is out of reach or needs to be moved above the screen. Usually it is some combination of dragging the window while holding some key on the keyboard, e.g. in XFCE/Xubuntu it's holding 'Alt' on the keyboard while pressing the left mouse button anywhere in the window and moving the mouse. According to [https://help.gnome.org/users/gnome-help/stable/shell-windows-states.html.en](https://help.gnome.org/users/gnome-help/stable/shell-windows-states.html.en) in Gnome this can be done in the same way but using the 'Super'-key (should be the one marked with a Windows-Logo on a lot of keyboards).
