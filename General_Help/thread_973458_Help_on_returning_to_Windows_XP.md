### Post by ragtag on 2008-11-06
Hi,

Before you start, realize that the following will wipe all the data, files, pictures etc. you have on your machine, so make a backup of what you want to keep first.

I'm assuming you want to remove Ubuntu completely and just have WindowsXP on your entire machine. This is relatively simple if you have all the cd-s and stuff that came with your machine. All you need to do is to insert the WindowsXP and boot from that. Then just follow the instructions on the screen. If you have a diskette with SATA drivers (newer machines might have this), choose load additional drivers early in the install process, and insert that diskette. Else the installer won't find your harddisk.

Take a look [here]("http://support.microsoft.com/kb/316941") for basic instructions.

Installing WindowsXP on top of Ubuntu, is no different than installing XP on an empty machine.
