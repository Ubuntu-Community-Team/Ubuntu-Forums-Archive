### Post by HappyLinux on 2011-02-09
Ok folks, I know that this query isn't directly relevant to computing, although it does have an adverse effect on computers. I also can't find anywhere else to post this query.

Surge protection is basically a must to in electronic appliance protection, whether it be your computer and its peripherals or your TV, but what about dirty electricity?

In my home, and my parents and quite possibly other homes in the area, we have a problem with dirty electricity or whatever it's called. I call it the electric version of limescale.

Surge protectors certainly protect against surges in electricity, but they don't act like filters or whatever. In our area we're prone to the odd low level power surge, hence why we use a lot of protectors. One incident years ago welded a modem card to the motherboard.

Dirty electricity seems to do to appliances like limescale to your washing machine. It takes it toll.

From what I've been told, it's basically irregular frequencies in the electricity. Although appliances are rated to handle certain frequencies, they are not rated to handle constant changes in the frequencies.

I've been told of several pieces of equipment that would filter this problem out. An Uninteruptable Power Supply (UPS), Monster PowerCentre surge protectors and Graham-Stetzer Filters.

Having to replace stuff that fry due to dirty electricity is quite expensive. Does anyone have any suggestions.
