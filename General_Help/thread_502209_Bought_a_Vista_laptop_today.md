### Post by ago on 2007-07-16
My laptop gave up on me yesterday and I needed a quick replacement, all notebooks in the shop(s) had vista, and there is no linux-dell/system76 here + I needed something quick. Went for a Samasung Q45 laptop, it comes with vista premium. I need some form of windows for work anyway + wubi testing of course... 

...but Vista... where is the "wow" exactly?

1. The system was already preinstalled by the oem and even so it required 3-4 reboots and almost an hour before being usable!!!

2. It feels slaggish even with a T7100 and 2GB memory. 

3. Nice window borders, but people that claim that vista is on par with compiz/beryl/fusion must be on drugs. I felt aero was in my way more than it was helping out, and it was taking way too many resources, I quickly settled for the basic interface (and I am seriously considering to go back to the classic one). 

4. Annoying popups every 5 seconds. I know I can turn them off, but they managed to make me feel as a little baby. 

5. Then I checked the free HD space: a bare installation of a mere 17GB with nothing in!!! The truth be told, after running cleanup (cleanup after first boot???) it went down to 12, but still... 

6. To me it smells A LOT like XP, which is not necessarily too bad, but it's like XP with all things slightly out of place + the popups. I'll have to relearn all their GUI again (long live the CLI!!!).

The best part so far was that at boot I was asked how much space to allocate to Vista, not sure if it is a Samsung-only feature but I liked that. I liked less that 10GB were automatically allocated for restoring vista without me having any say in the matter... 

Anyway I am going to install feisty and gutsy now. Looking forward to try KVM, hope it works well. If it does better than VirtualBox I'd be pleased.

### Post by ago on 2007-07-17
I must say that I am also disappointed by the laptop compatibility under Ubuntu, I bought it since it was all intel and I thought that it was well supported. But the linux support for the graphic card (X3000) is below par (the card does not work at all out of the box, and even with the intel driver, 3D is completely unreliable, not that use it much, but...) and for some reason even if the VMX extension is there, and I can modprobe kvm-intel, I still can't use kvm (exception 12). I upgraded the bios to no avail. This really upsets me, since kvm support was probably the most important feature for me. Last but not least, the wifi card requires a restricted driver and acpi works only with latest kernel. On the plus side the webcam works.

In short if you are thinking about buying a Samsung Q45 to work with Ubuntu/Linux, think again. If you are shopping for a Laptop here in Europe, you'd probably better off waiting for Dell to start shipping Linux. Not that Dell is perfect... Their Linux compatibility is generally very good, but their power connector is absolute junk, it desoldered in 3 out of 3 laptops... True, I do not treat my laptops with the due respect, but still if they had invested a few cents more in a decent connector the useful life of my laptops would have doubled...

Ok, it was my time to complain, now I feel better ;P
