### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-11-21
Xubuntu is simple and pretty and fast, even on my ancient old hand-me-down hardware. But Xubuntu is more than just "Ubuntu with Xfce added on," it's the default settings, themes, artwork, and default applications (Abiword and Gnumeric in place of LibreOffice, for example, and other lightweight defaults).  But with those specs I think you'll have no problems running Unity or any other desktop environment.

But consult the [Ubuntu Certified Hardware]("http://www.ubuntu.com/certification/") page to search for your hardware.

### Post by evilsoup on 2012-11-21
Your hardware is definitely up for using Unity, but I'm going to say +1 to ssam's suggestion: why not try out both? [Here](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/pureubuntu[/url) are instructions for reverting to standard Ubuntu, and [here](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purexubuntu) are instructions for getting to pure Xubuntu, for when you decide which one to keep.

I would note that you should definitely give Unity a try. It's different from the normal way of doing things, but I for one have found it an improvement. Others disagree, of course. Try using it for at least a week or so before you make up your mind.
