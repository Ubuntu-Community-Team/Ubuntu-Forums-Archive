### Post by ank2 on 2018-04-29
I doubt you need any firewall rules, unless your existing firewall rules prevent a connection.

I recommend to go offline from the internet while still being online in your local network and flush all firewall rules to see if it then works.

By the way, we have Linux here. :-) If you don't start any services on your computer you don't need a firewall at all. Even if you use some services for local purpose they can often be bound to localhost and won't listen to the outside world.
