### Post by ispmarin on 2006-05-23
I think that the easiest way is to do a fresh install, install all the programs that you use in the laptop, and then copy your /home/<user> to there... This will copy your user configurations (like window decorations, keyboard accelerators, etc).
What configurations you want to keep?
(I think that in this case dd is not an option (hardware configuration too different)).

### Post by aysiu on 2006-05-23
Is the other computer the same exact hardware?

If so, you can just use a live CD and [PartImage](http://www.partimage.org) to copy your partition as is and then restore the partition as is on the other computer.

If they're different hardware, it may be trickier...
