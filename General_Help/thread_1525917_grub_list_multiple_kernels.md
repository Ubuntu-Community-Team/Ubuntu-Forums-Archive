### Post by dino99 on 2010-07-07
its a good idea to have 2 kernels: if 1 break you still can use the other. To remove a kernel (or any package) goto synaptic (system admin synaptic), search your package by scrolling down, right click on it to completly remove it.

for each kernel installed, you have at least 3 packages: header(s) and image to remove
