### Post by TheFu on 2015-01-30
Start learning here: [http://www.voip-info.org/](http://www.voip-info.org/)
There are probably a few beginning VoIP videos on youtube.

SIP is the protocol. If 2 internet connected devices both speak SIP, then they can communicate. It isn't usually program specific - meaning different SIP programs are inter-operable. However, SIP doesn't like NAT, which makes it less useful for home-to-home connections.  Most people who use VoIP pay a service provider to connect into the POTS network (regular telephones). After all, grandma isn't going to use a computer as a phone. 

The best providers to use are different around the world and the answer is different if you run a PBX or just run a SIP soft-phone/ATA device.

The more hands-on you are, the less you can pay for VoIP service.  I pay $5/month for unlimited inbound calls and $0.01/min outbound calling.  $65/yr covers our use.  I have my own ATA equipment ($35) to connect a normal cordless phone system with 6 handsets into the ATA. Configuring the ATA settings requires some VoIP-specific knowledge.  Where I live, most full service VoIP solutions provide an ATA (preconfigured) for their network and cost $20/month.  Clearly, if you can learn to setup your own ATA, there are huge savings (relatively) to be had. For a working family, these costs are still half what AT&T charges for local phone service. 

I've tried a few different VoIP providers over the years, but switched to voip.ms about 7 yrs ago. It is excellent service and the cost is fair. Around here, callcentric is another reputable provider with good quality service.  I've been burned by terrible voice quality or outages by 4 other providers - including my ISP.

If you have a smart phone and live where google voice is available, that is probably the easiest way to get into VoIP.
