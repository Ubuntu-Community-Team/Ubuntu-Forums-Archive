### Post by Vaedrah on 2009-04-16
I am not dismissing the concept of returning to unanswered questions but I wonder if long standing questions might have lost their relevance by now?

Some questions, if time invariant and generic will either receive a plethora of replies, some useful, or and absence of satisfactory answers may just indicate that no realistic solutions are possible. Not all problems have solutions (despite Dr Who's Daleks that quoted "every problem has a solution! - Godel's incompleteness theorem may suggest otherwise.

Other problems may relate to older hardware and as we know, computers soon become outdated. Even if some allow upgrades, the rubber band only stretched so far. For example, RAM memory can be expanded to a limit; however fast RAM may not be a substitute for older, slow RAM. 

Equally problems with old distributions that are no longer supported possible need little attention.

Example; I have another notebook that was OK 4~5 years ago but now it is a dinosaur. Its graphics work fine with U8.04 but not with U8.10 and U9.04. I did ask about a solution but none came; however the solution is to leave it with U8.04!

In future, if the past predicts the future, the 64-bit threads will become much more relevant. Current 32 bit machines are soon ready to be put to pasture - equally older 64 bit problems will be addressed in new 64 bit machines.

My guess is that the technology has a 5 year approx window of application. The first wave of technology will have teething problems, older wash-out technology will become increasingly incompatible with new OS updates. 

Therefore, I ask in all due respect, if pursuing solutions to old problems is actually a misguided cause?

Perhaps we would be better off considering how to make improvements in future hardware and OS development. Certainly RAM cost increasing falls, and RAM speed continually improves. Also multiple cores are now in vogue. How long will it be before the idea of a "hard drive" is an obsolete an idea as vinyl record players and audio cassette taps? :popcorn:
