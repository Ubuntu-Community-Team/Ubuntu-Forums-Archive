### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-11-27
I have had problems with BBC iPlayer on 64bit 11.04 and 11.10. The Adobe Flash Plugin version 10 for Linux is still a beta version (I think). From the Ubuntu Software Centre I installed Adobe Flash Plugin (installer for the Adobe Flash plug-in for Mozilla) instead of Adobe Flash Plugin 10.

This worked for me. If BBC iPlayer works on Windows then it cannot be a problem with being in Korea. This is what the UK TV licensing web site says

> As long as the address where you live is licensed, you&#8217;re also covered to watch TV outside your home using any device powered solely by its own internal batteries. This includes your mobile phone, laptop and tablet

**Exception:** If you only watch catch-up services online, then you don&#8217;t need a licence. For example, you don&#8217;t need one to use BBC iPlayer, or ITV player, to catch up on programmes after they have been shown on TV.

So, there will only be a block on live broadcasts to Internet Users outside the UK and there should not be a block on the Catch up side of iPlayer.

Regards.

P.S. I use Chromium as well as Firefox and this is what is said under plugins: 

> Adobe Flash is directly integrated with Google Chrome and enabled by default. Any updates available for Adobe Flash are automatically included in Google Chrome system updates.
