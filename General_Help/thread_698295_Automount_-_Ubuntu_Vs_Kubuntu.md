### Post by Shippou on 2008-02-16
In my experience, automount does not work with Kubuntu.

To mount the device, go to system menu (located in panel) --> Storage Media.

There it will show you all the detected devices connected to your PC.

Just click on the icon and it will get automatically mounted.

Automount does work sometimes in Kubuntu, but then I'm looking for ways to disable it.

But then look at the bright side: It is good that automount does not work in Kubuntu, especially also autoplay: it is the best way (one of the best ways, I mean) for getting malware.

Windows implements autoplay by default, that's why malware always get them without the user's knowledge. :)
