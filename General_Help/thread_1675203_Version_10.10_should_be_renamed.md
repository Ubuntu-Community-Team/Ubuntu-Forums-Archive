### Post by Grenage on 2011-01-25
> **rich_b said:**
> Am I the only one with all these issues?

Possibly, but I'd imagine there are a few others.  *Most* people don't have a problem with 10.10, but *most* people don't have a problem with 10.04.  No OS is ever a perfect fit for all setup, with the exception of Mac OSX, which runs on static hardware.

If all of your machines are broken in the same way, and all of your machines were upgraded - consider a fresh install on one machine, as a test.

Regarding old versions losing support after a set period of time, that is unfortunately the way it must be.  The overhead required for maintaining every version for many many years would be far too high.  If the release cycle was longer (say, every 2 years) and a version didn't work that well for you, you'd have to use a release that was 2 years out of date; that's not ideal.  The release cycle also helps prevent stagnation, and renews interest.

There has yet to be a pair of shoes that fit all feet.
