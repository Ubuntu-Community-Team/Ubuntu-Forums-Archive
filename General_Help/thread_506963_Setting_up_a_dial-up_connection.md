### Post by Bartender on 2007-07-23
Hmmm - OK, I'm lookin' at the link you sent.  Here's my [link]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717") for you to look at.

Whether you use duncan's directions for ppp-config or mine for wvdial/gnome-ppp (gnome-ppp is, as far as I know, just a graphical front-end for wvdial) it looks like this should work.

I'm assuming you already know about the necessity of having a Linux-compatible modem.  

There's a free dial-up number that works all across the country?  With no long-distance charges?  That's pretty neat...

Anyway, I'd sure practice a few times at home with this even though you won't be able to actually dial.  Maneuvering in ppp-config is all done via the keyboard, no mousing.  I made the mistake of using the mouse the first several times.  In ppp-config, the only change to duncan's directions is to use the manual DNS settings option and type in those DNS addresses.
The username/password looks to be very simple. 
I could easily be missing something but it appears pretty straightforward.  Enjoy your trip!
