### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-03-18
I sort of agree with DeMus here.  When I first installed Ubuntu a few years ago, I loved playing with the desktop effects and showing off the desktop cube and wobbly windows to friends.  But after a while the novelty wore off.  

Now my 2GHz Intel Core 2 Duo with 2GB of memory and an nVidia GeForce 8400M GS, which runs Compiz under Gnome just fine, is using Openbox with PyPanel and feh (for the wallpaper) as the default session.  It doesn't have anywhere near the number of bells and whistles as my Gnome session (which I still use on occasion), but it's reasonably attractive, very responsive, and does what I need it to do without consuming unnecessary resources.  Even on a powerful system, there's not much sense in wasting resources.  Every MB of memory you don't use for your desktop is a MB you could be using for an application.  And video will be much smoother on a mid-level video card if it's not having to deal with those fancy desktop effects as well.

If appearance is a priority for you, then that's perfectly fine.  Go with Compiz and all the fancy desktop effects that people (including myself) show off in YouTube vids.  Otherwise, a fancy window manager is a waste of resources that could be used elsewhere.  Of course, the best part is that in Linux you don't have to choose.  You can have a minimalist session for work, and a fancy session for showing off to your Windows-using friends. ;)
