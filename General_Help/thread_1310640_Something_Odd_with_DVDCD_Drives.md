### Post by oldefoxx on 2009-11-02
I work with DVD drives, making copies, storing ISO files on hard drive for client access, modifying images, and venturing into creating new images, some bootable.  Brasero is usually my application of choice, but sometimes when something odd happens, I try an alternative application to see if the same problem surfaces there and what else I can learn about it.

That oddity is this:  I can burn some images that verify just fine, but when I try to mount and use the CD later, Brasero can't seem to see anything on the disk, or what it sees must be flawed because it will sit there and do nothing that I can see.  Or it may come back and tell me that no media is mounted in the drive, when it really is there.

This happens with Ubuntu as the host.  On a Windows client, a similar problem if the CD is enabled in VirtualBox settings.  Either way, I cannot mount, unmount, or eject the inserted disk because there is no media.  I can try to manually eject the media and it seems stuck, as the tray will not open.  Now why can't I get a DVD/CD drive tray to open with either the eject command or pushing the front button whether there is a disk in there or not?  After all, you turn on the PC and use the eject putton to open the tray to insert a bootable CD sometimes, don't you?  Or push the button to remove a bootable CD when you want it to boot from the hard drive instead?

This problem is not just with Brasero, but seems to be fundamental to the way that Ubuntu is recognizing and dealing with the DVD/CD drive, and the problems with Brasero and the client are just extensions of this.  Fact is, in the Windows' client, I get a USB warning that the VBOX CD-ROM drive was apparently powered off by the user, when actually the DVD/CD drive being referenced and used is not connected by USB.  Just very odd, and caused an hour project to create two sets of backup CDs to take me all evening instead.  I just felt it was time to say something, but I don't know what package to report a problem against.  Besides, if I post it here, others that have had similar experiences might add their own comments and observations as well.

Brasero itself has worked very well for me, except when I encounter this problem under Ubunto 9.04.  I bet the problem is still there under 9.10, and earlier releases as well.  It's not the sort of thing you would jump on unless you were really involved with burning or copying DVDs or CDs.  Even then, you might just think it was bad media or a problem with the drive itself.  But this problem involves two recently purchased PCs, different drives, and media that normally works quite weii elsewhere, such as TDK.
