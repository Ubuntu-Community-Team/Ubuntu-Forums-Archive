### Post by igfud on 2008-05-13
Welcome to the forums and to Ubuntu!

The overlapping icons are a known bug in Ubuntu (see [https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/nautilus/+bug/40872](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/nautilus/+bug/40872)).  Some have recommended right clicking on the desktop and ensuring "Keep Aligned" is checked, however this didn't seem to solve the issue for me.  If you don't might regrouping the icons, you can right click on the desktop and choose "Clean up by name."

The only real solution I've found is leaving the left area of my desktop clear for external media icons to automatically show up so that there are no other icons to cover up.  That, or you can just drag the icons away from each other (a bit of a nuisance, but a small price to pay for ditching Windows :) ).

I assume the moving cursor is palm touch issue?  Most touchpad programs/drivers in Windows are programmed to ignore accidental palm pressure, so you probably didn't have this issue in Vista?  This is probably because you have touchpad tapping enabled to work as equivalently clicking.  What brand of touch pad do you own?  You may want to take a look at this HOWTO to see how you can decrease the sensitivity and effectively ignore palm taps: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=351058](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=351058)

igfud
