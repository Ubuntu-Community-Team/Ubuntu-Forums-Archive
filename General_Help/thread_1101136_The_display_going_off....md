This problem (most likely) has nothing to do with Ubuntu.... but **what else** do have plugged into the USB ports of that particular PC?

I recently had two work PCs with USB keyboards on a kiosk, and these keyboards had ports with a USB mouse plugged in and all worked ok for months. We then plugged in USB Barcode scanners into the remaining USB port on the keyboard and the machines seemed ok, until you used the Barcode scanners - then the PCs rebooted all by themselves!

Solution: Remove the power hungry USB Barcode scanners and move to a different USB bus as they were drawing too much current and the reaction from the PC was just to crap out and reboot!

Nothing to do with whatever OS you run on the hardware, just the USB devices affecting the hardware.
