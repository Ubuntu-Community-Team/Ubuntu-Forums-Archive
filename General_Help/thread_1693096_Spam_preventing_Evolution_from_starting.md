### Post by Riverglen on 2011-02-22
I have version 10.10 running on two different machines (with all available updates applied), with no problems at all, until now.  Yesterday for reasons unknown, when I try to open Evolution to read e-mail, it comes up, apparently downloads the latest messages from the server, and then goes off the rails.  On one machine, I get a message box overlaying the Evolution window (which is dimmed), informing me that the application crashed. On the other machine, I don't get the message.  The Evolution window opens as expected, but before I get a chance to do anything with it, it vanishes (a couple of seconds, max).

The reason I went to Evolution in the first place was that I wanted a safe way to take a look at a spam e-mail that I have been receiving multiple copies of in my Windows installation.  It is clearly some sort of scam, and I refuse to open it in Windows but figured it wasn't likely to contain anything that would cause problems in a Linux environment.

So, does it make any sense that a "scam spam" e-mail could be bringing down Evolution before I even try to open it?  Anybody have an alternative explanation?

---

### Post by Riverglen on 2011-02-22
Evidently my conjecture was right.  I went to the ISP web mail site and deleted the offendig e-mail from the server.  (I normally access e-mail with either Thunderbird or Evolution.)  Now, Evolution opens just fine.  Interestingly, there was no evidence that the spam that was causing the problem ever got downloaded.  Didn't show up in the inbox, junk folder, or anywhere else.
