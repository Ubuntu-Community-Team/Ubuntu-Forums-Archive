### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-01-17
> **slooksterpsv said:**
> Mint Linux uses .deb and loads apps through Apt-get, it also already has flash, gstreamer, dvd playback, etc. preloaded.

I have a few questions though:
1. Has anyone used Mint and found if it has better performance than Ubuntu?

Linux Mint is basically Ubuntu underneath, so no it doesn't have better performance.

> 2. A few people I know get lots of viruses and spyware, they just use their computer for email (hotmail), pictures, and internet - would Mint Linux be a better OS for them?

Better than Windows, not necessarily better than Ubuntu as the two are really very similar.

> 3. What do you like with Mint vs Ubuntu (any variants)?

Mint has some more preloaded programs and a differently configured Gnome desktop that some people might prefer. It also has proprietary/restricted codecs and format support preinstalled. Mint also contains original programs. On the downside, there's less of a community around Linux Mint, and dist-upgrading is MUCH less reliable than regular Ubuntu. Also there's no ARM port, no PowerPC port, and to my knowledge there's no 64-bit edition of Mint.

If you know how to install the "ubuntu-restricted-extras" package from the main Ubuntu repositories, and you know how to enable the Medibuntu repository and install the "non-free-codecs" and "adobereader" packages from there, then you don't really need Linux Mint.
