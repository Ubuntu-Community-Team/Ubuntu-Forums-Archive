### Post by tubezninja on 2008-10-23
> **Spaceman9 said:**
> So, what OS does the iphone have?

[iPhone OS]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPhone_OS"), which is a derivative of [OS X]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac_OS_X").  If you go down the lineage, it's a mish-mash of [NEXSTEP]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NEXTSTEP"), the Mach Kernel and FreeBSD.

As for Android, it's hard to say.  Right now, the early adopters are people who probably already have some investment in Linux and are buying it *because* of Android's linux foundations.  So won't get many converts their because they're already exposed to Linux and are using it.

The remainder will by and large not know about Linux or care.

Why, you ask?

Keep in mind who ultimately is behind Android.  Google isn't interested in promoting Linux with this phone.  They're interested in promoting *Google*.  Linux just happens to be a convenient and inexpensive means to that end, and if Linux enthusiasts are going to stand by the phone and actively promote it, then it's all the more convenient for Google.

You don't sync with a linux desktop (or Windows or Mac for that matter).  Rather, you sync with *Google*. The back of the phone doesn't say anything about linux, but it has that Google logo in prominence.  And *that's* ultimately what they want people to associate Android with.

I like my GMail, but I'm not willing to drink the full glass of Google Kool-Aid at this time.  For this reason, I'm not pining much for an Android phone.
