> why is it that something as simple as video card drivers work out of the box in windows or mac but in linux its such a problem that i had to leave and go back to windows just to get video drivers to work.

I suppose it can be frustrating, but the devs of Linux distributions like Ubuntu are supporting a wider range of hardware blind, with no (or little) help from the vendors of your computer or the graphics card manufacturers. 

MacOS supports a much more limited amount of hardware, produced by the same company. Windows will be helped by it's dominant position as graphics card vendors most likely supply the drivers, I guess the hardware may even be designed to work with Windows. 

Bearing this in mind, the devs do an excellent job.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-03-23
Actually most cards do not work out of the box in windows or mac; you almost always need a CD with the drivers to install in order to get newer video cards to work properly.

However, to your problem!

Have you looked for a driver on the AMD/ATI website, where newer drivers than are available then those in the additional drivers are often made available by the manufacturers, before ubuntu gets them in its repos.  I can certainly not guarantee anything but see [http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/linux/Pages/radeon_linux.aspx?type=2.4.1&product=2.4.1.3.42](http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/linux/Pages/radeon_linux.aspx?type=2.4.1&product=2.4.1.3.42) if you have not already looked.  It says it supports xorg 7.5 (Oneiric) so should be OK for you.
