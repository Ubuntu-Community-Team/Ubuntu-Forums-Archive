### Post by upchucky on 2010-06-22
After much beard growth, and no help from rogers tech support, I figured out how to get evolution to sendmail when I am on a rogers isp.

First, a bit of history, I move around a lot, and every time I connected to rogers.com I could only receive mail but not send mail. To solve part of the problem, I had to open a rogers account and get a rogers email address. I do not want to use rogers email cause it uses Yahoo and is full of junk.

However to be able to use evolution as my mail client with my preferred email address through rogers, I had to verify that my preferred email account is a valid account. this is a security feature of rogers.

I suspect that any other isp that will not allow sendmail with email clients other than their own may also have this fixable problem.

I have verified that the below link does make evolution work as the email client through rogers, and I suspect that it will also allow Thunderbird and others to work.

the below link has clunky instructions, (the directions to actually get to your account management page are wrong, but with a bit of poking around you will find it.) In your rogers account management you can complete the instruction listed there and it will work.



[http://www.rogershelp.com/yahoo/article.php?id=10H-F](http://www.rogershelp.com/yahoo/article.php?id=10H-F)
