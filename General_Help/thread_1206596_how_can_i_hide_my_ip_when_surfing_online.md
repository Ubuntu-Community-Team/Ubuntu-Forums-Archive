### Post by Paddy Landau on 2009-07-07
You can use a secure paid-for VPN from a commercial company (you have to trust the company not to reveal your IP address, though). This hides your IP address from the target, and all your activity from everyone (even your ISP) but the company itself and of course the target (but the target won't know your IP or who you are).

Two examples that I found were
[XeroBank]("https://xerobank.com/")
[ShadowVPN]("http://shadowvpn.com/")

I caution you to use it for legal purposes, though (e.g. not for downloading pirated stuff), because the law can force the company to reveal you and your activities.

### Post by DGortze380 on 2009-07-07
You can't.

Ultimately, you need a valid address for traffic to be routed to. 

Because this address exists, and is being used, there is always a way to figure it out.

You can obscure your address with the methods in previous posts, and a few others; but it shouldn't be necessary under ordinary circumstances.
