### Post by MrObvious on 2008-07-21
Alright here is what I basically did:

1) Enable GMail's IMAP service.
2) Configure Thunderbird and my phone to download e-mail according to GMail's instructions. (If you have 6.1, don't accept the default settings. To bypass you want to enter your email address as something invalid like a@a. After that continue to follow directions.)
3) If you have a ton of e-mails like me, use the filters to archive e-mails and set it up so that it doesn't show in the Inbox.
4) On the phone, go to your Inbox, click Menu->Tools->Manage Folders and you can check which folders you want to synchronize. Just don't do the folders you have archived older e-mails. 
5) Enjoy!
