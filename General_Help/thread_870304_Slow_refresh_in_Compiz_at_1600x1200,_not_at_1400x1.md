### Post by pcjason on 2008-07-25
I'm trying to setup an older Dell laptop for my brother with Hardy on it.  The system is a Dell Inspiron 8200 with 512Mb RAM, 1.6Ghz P4, and a 32Mb Gerforce2 Go video card.  It is running the 96.43.05 version of the nVidia binary drivers.  I have enabled compiz and it works perfectly for any resolution below 1600x1200, but at 1600x1200 (the native resolution) the system is, by my standards, unusable.  By this I mean that scrolling in anything (even gedit) is so choppy that it is impossible to stop in your intended spot.  Also, the scrolling problem is very noticable in Firefox.  The problem is not present if compiz is not enabled, but what is the fun in that? :)  So, I'm wondering if I have a configuration problem, or if this old graphics card just is not up to the challenge of pushing that many pixels.  It seems strange that it is perfectly fine at a resolution of 1400x1050 (1.47M pixels) and not at 1600x1200 (1.92M pixels).  I'm all out of ideas, so if you have any let me know!  Thanks!
