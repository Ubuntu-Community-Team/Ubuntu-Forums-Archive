### Post by nekr0z on 2009-05-05
Jaunty made typing in Esperanto really easy: just go to "System > Preferences > Keyboard > Layouts > Layout options", choose what is comfortable for you under "Adding Esperanto circumflexes" and make sure you know where your 3rd-level-chooser is (AltGr by default). Then you can have "&#285;" by simply pressing "AltGr+g", for exampe.

But there's a trouble about all this: if you are using "extra typographic characters" (under "Various compatibility options" in the same window), those "extra" characters override your Esperanto letters (for example, "&#293;" is still "&#293;", but "&#265;" becomes "©" and "&#349;" becomes "§"). "Extra typographic characters" are really useful when typing in Russian, since Russian typography makes heavy use of "«»" and "—", so switching those off is a bad idea either.

I just wonder if someone knows a way to make Esperanto letters override "extra characters", or switch some selected "extra characters" off...
