### Post by cortman on 2012-02-22
Hi, and welcome to Ubuntu!

11.10 came with Rhythmbox as the default music player. It's very good (better than Banshee, IMHO), and should work fine for you. You can get to it by clicking the dash icon in the upper left, and browsing your apps or searching for it in the search box.
I personally just use the Movie player and a file browser, primarily, but that's more how I listen to my music.

If your sound card is functioning, there's no need for extra drivers.

Good luck!

### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-02-22
First of all, the drivers for your sound card are for Windows are they not? No good for Linux.

Second, on the top panel at the right is a sound icon. Click on that and you will see both Rhythmbox and Sound settings.

When you open Rhythmbox if you move the mouse pointer over the left side of the top panel you will see Rhythmbox menu names - Music, Edit, View. Control, Help. They all give you options.
