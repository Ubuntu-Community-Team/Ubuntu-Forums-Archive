### Post by mcarro on 2009-06-30
Hi.  I am a very heavy Emacs user (I'd say 90% of what I do is done with Emacs) and a big fan of View Mail (VM), which I have been using for many years.

However, over the last months / year VM has had a couple of glitches related to message coding (it may be that I am receiving ill-formed messages) which may be or not be very difficult to solve, but which effectively prevent VM from being used by me.  I  patched VM not to break, but I know I did not solve the cause of the symptons.

Unfortunately VM does not seem to be under heavy development (for which I cannot blame the maintainer, because he has surely done way more than what I could possibly do), or maybe the maintainer is making big changes and he prefers to release all of them in a bundle.  

In any case, do you know of any suitable replacement for View Mail?  I switched to VM from RMAIL time ago because of the treatment attachments; maybe RMAIL has improved on that, but I do not know up to which point it currently compares with VM.  I tried to use MEW some years ago, but I was unable to understand wholly how it worked before testing it, and I did not dare to make the switch.  

Is there any other candidate people can recommend me?

Thanks!

### Post by picopir8 on 2009-06-30
Well there is always gedit, nedit, and the like. However if you want a powerful the emacs and vim variants are the only way to go.

For a list see:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vi#Derivatives_and_clones](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vi#Derivatives_and_clones)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emacs#Other_modern_forks_and_clones](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emacs#Other_modern_forks_and_clones)

As an emacs person, vim probably appears a bit convoluted, however if you start off using gvim the transition is not so bad.  It took me less than a month to become as proficient in vim as I had been in emacs.
