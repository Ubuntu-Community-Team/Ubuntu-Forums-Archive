### Post by varunus on 2005-12-19
gThumb, which I think comes with Ubuntu (or is installable from synaptic) lets you do slideshows.  It lets you pan around too, like you wanted; I'm not sure if its the same way as iPhoto, but you can pan and zoom.

It doesn't play music unfortunately, but you can use rhythmbox/mpg123/xmms.

Just curious, why don't you use rhythmbox for mp3s?  It won't play them out of the box, btw, you need to follow the "install codecs" howto in the ubuntu help.  Click the help icon on your panel (if you haven't deleted it), click "ubuntu 5.10 starter guide", select "applications" and then "music and movies."  Follow the install codecs section, and rhythmbox will be able to play mp3s.

You can use google's picasa through wine as well, or use f-spot (which will be the new photo manager in a while, but is currently still not finished).
