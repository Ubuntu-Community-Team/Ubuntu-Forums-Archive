### Post by mcduck on 2011-10-20
The suitable amount pretty much depends on how powerful the graphics card is. A low-end card will not benefit at all from having a large amount of graphics memory, it simply doesn't have the power to process that much data anyway. On the other hand a high-end card will be able to do process data much quicker, and therefore needs more memory to keep all the data in.

The high amounts of RAM you might see on graphics cards are usually just a marketing trick, they provide a nice large number that's easier to sell to the people than the technical details of the graphics card and the model numbers, amounts of streaming processors, memory types and so on.

If you see a graphics card with, say, 2GB of memory, it's most likely a low-end card and rather bad buy, even the high-end professional workstation cards rarely have that much memory, usually ranging around 512MB-1GB until you get to the extreme high-end cards with prices around (and over) 1000&#8364;.

Memory is cheap, especially if the manufacturer uses normal DDR instead of much faster GDDR. Fast GPUs on the other hand are expensive, and like I said, harder to actually sell to the generic mass. ;)

Anyway, 512MB is definitely enough, especially for a mobile GPU.
