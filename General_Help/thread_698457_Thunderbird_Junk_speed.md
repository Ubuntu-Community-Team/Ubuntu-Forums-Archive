### Post by raydar on 2008-02-16
Anyone else notice Thunderbird's "junking" process occuring at two different speeds--slow and fast--for no apparent reason?

Usually, whether Thunderbird's the only thing running or all kinds of other apps are running, hitting the "Junk" button causes a selected message to be junked over a time span of about a second or a second and a half.  The time taken for "junking" a single message is virtually the same as the time it takes to "junk" a whole slew of selected messages at once, so to avoid the frustration of click Junk, wait, click Junk, wait, click Junk, wait (or the same using the J key), I go out of my way to block-select multiple messages instead of junk them individually, even when there are only 2 or 3 to be junked.

However, sometimes the "junking" time is just a fraction of a second, such that I can click the "Junk" button repeatedly without having to wait a moment before I click it again for the next message.  (This happens to have occurred for me only when lots of other apps are running, but I can't imagine that has anything to do with speeding it up, directly anyway.)  This speedy operation happens for me about 1 in 10 times I use Thunderbird, always unexpectedly and never right after I start Thunderbird; I'll be doing something else, switch over to Thunderbird, and be pleasantly surprised to find the junking operating as fast as I'd like it to.

I'd love it if someone could tell me how to make the speedy-ness remain, permanently . . . .
