### Post by mcduck on 2010-08-02
It depends on the video and audio format used in the file.

Just like .avi, .mov is a container file, not a video format,  and can contain audio and video encoded in many different formats. The live-CD (and default installed setup) is able to read the .mov container but can't handle many of the commonly used video and audio formats so you still might not be able to view the file.

(the short answer is no, you probably won't be able to play the .mov file without installing the required codecs)
