### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-18
You don't need to tell people "go to a terminal and install gnome-fallback-session".

Unity is easy enough to use that people will figure out how to use it by themselves. You're used to classic Gnome, but someone coming new to Ubuntu is more likely to just accept Unity as "Ubuntu's interface" rather than "they took away MY interface".

Unity is really not difficult to learn. Here's a hint: It's Gnome underneath. The top-right area of the top panel is basically the same as it was before. You can right-click on the icons on the left to get lists of things to do with them. Click on the Ubuntu button to bring up the dash, and you can start typing to search. You can click on the icons at the bottom of the dash to browse or search according to what you want to find.

You can also drag and drop files onto Unity icons to open the file in that program. Oh, and clicking twice on a Unity icon will show you all the running windows of that program, if there is more than one.

Congratulations, you've just learnt Unity. Now go out there and continue spreading the word!
