Your question cannot be answered without knowing more about your network setup... 

Many people have a wireless router/DSL-modem through which they connect to the internet, and their computers have wireless interfaces that connect to the router. So, one way is to connect your printer to the same router -- read the router manual on how to do that. The previous answer already said that many printers expect to have a static network IP, the manual can help there as well. This way the printer is accessible to all computers on the same network.

Another way is to connect the printer directly to your computer, you need a second interface for that (a second wireless card), and you can set it up as a direct pointopoint connection. 

So, you see, all depends on your network setup...
