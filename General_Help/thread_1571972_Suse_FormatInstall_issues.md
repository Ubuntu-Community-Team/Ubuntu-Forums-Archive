### Post by coffeecat on 2010-09-10
> **mamioop said:**
> I guess it's the same, coz, all are Linux OSes!

Not at all. Suse and Ubuntu share the Linux kernel, but there are differences in the way they are compiled for each distro. Ubuntu and Suse both use the gnome and KDE (for Kubuntu) desktops, but again there are significant differences between the two distros. And, most importantly, Suse has very different system configuration tools to Ubuntu. Yast is peculiar to Suse.

And then you are using Enterprise Suse which is somewhat different from Opensuse. Lastly, your Suse recovery partition seems to be an important consideration and I doubt whether many (any?) members of this forum have experience of that.

Unless you want to install Ubuntu, you'd be better off on a Suse forum. I mean this kindly.
