### Post by oiper on 2008-05-01
This may be startling news to some this morning, but you need to be informed. The ghost of Windows 98 has found it's way from the depths of Hell to plague mankind once more. It has possessed Firefox and gains full control over this host when the FlashPlayer is run.

My good friend John discovered this last night. Sadly, John killed himself. His last words, as found near his body, were the following:

"My Lord, the horror. I wouldn't have believed it if I didn't see it for myself. At first I thought, it's only one crash, no big deal. Then they continued. Five, Ten, thirteen, eighteen crashes! I was in the middle of working when everything started to crash around me. My productivity dropped at a quadratically exponential rate. Halfway into emails, ten tabs open, deep into a thought process while doing some complex web development, it was shattered repeatedly. It wasn't until I had eight crashes within two minutes that I knew; Windows 98 had returned.
I've grown so fond of Ubuntu and the world it offers me. To have it shattered to bits and torn from me! ... I can't go on living like this. If I can't work, I can't get paid. For me, this is the end. I'm sorry. May God have mercy on us all."

John was unable to email that letter. Perhaps if he was, I could have reached him in time.

Today we are faced once again with a serious enemy and this time, there is no one to blame. No one that can stop it. An exhaustive search on the web (using Firefox from inside a VirtualBox Windows XP install) reveals that no one is to blame for this possession. Some suggest that Adobe is the cause, others Firefox, still others blame soundcards and possibly PulseAudio. A few stragglers even blame X.Org. The simple fact is, all of these people swear the problem is not with their product, and now, with John's letter, we know they are right. It's not Adobe, the loving corporation that works so hard on bug fixes for it's software or Mozilla Firefox that is such a good browser that we don't even question it anymore. It's the freaking ghost of Windows 98. 

As you sit down to kill yourself tonight, just remember one thing. The massive instability and crashing of Firefox/FlashPlayer **is not anyone's fault**, if it was, somebody would fix it. It's the ghost of Windows past.
