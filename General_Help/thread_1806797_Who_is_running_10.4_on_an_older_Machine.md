### Post by claracc on 2011-07-18
I am running xubuntu 10.04.2 in an old laptop ( I think year 2002 or 2000 I don't remember) pentium III 1 GHz CPU, 512 MB ram from which 64 MB go for the integrated sis 630/730 video card, 20 GB HD.

It takes 1'5 minutes booting the system and the os takes 3 GB in HD.

I have mplayer for music and other videos than flash and the computer play them properly.

Also I have installed k3b to burn CDs and it works very well.

### Post by Topsiho on 2011-07-18
I run 10.04 on a Toshiba laptop (A50) which is at least 6 years old now, without any trouble. It's important that you have more than 256 mB of memory, the laptop has 512 mB's, which some consider as a minimum.

And of course the harddisk should be large enough, the laptop is 40 GB.
I guess that 20 GB is minimum for any serious use, the sysyem takes 4 GB or more, you'll have swap (twice the memory), and the rest is for your personal files, best is in /home in a separate partition.

I am running 10.04 also on a netbook, with an 8 GB SSD (solid state hard disk, lovely silent), and 512 mB memory, which runs just fine, with a rather minimal install. The SSD is a bad one (old Samsung) and probably the bottleneck, as the screen gets grey often, and the system halts for a while (nowadays (some?) SSD's are far better than this).

Topsiho
