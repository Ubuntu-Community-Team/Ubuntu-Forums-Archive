### Post by Anlace on 2008-10-05
Greetings,

An interesting little dilemma here.  We had a prolonged brown-out today during which time my computer shut itself down.  Everything seems to be fine now that power is back up except for a single "stuck" email in Kmail.

The email in question was readable before the computer shut down, now I can see the entry line in the list of emails but the email itself is now blank - no header, no message body, nothing. Kmail says that it's unread and nothing changes that. I can't delete it - dragging to the trash does nothing, delete key and "X" button does nothing except put a line through the email subject in the list.  Rebooting didn't help either (I know, I know, old Winblows habit there but I did try everything else I could think of).

I figured it's corrupted and would love to get rid of it before it causes any problems.  Any suggestions as to how to do that would be very appreciated.

Peace,
Gail

---

### Post by Anlace on 2008-10-05
I opened my email folder in /home/gail/.kde/shared/apps/kmail, searched for, found and deleted the email message from there.

Problem solved.

In the unlikely event that any of the Kmail development people see this post - Thank you for making it so easy to fix email issues!  This "problem" of a corrupt email message would have crippled Mail in OS X and would have made Outlook or Outlook Express crash endlessly.

Thanks for making such quality programs available to us!
