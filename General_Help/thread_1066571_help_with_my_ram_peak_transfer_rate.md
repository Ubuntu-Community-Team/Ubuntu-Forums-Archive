DDR (1) technology peaks out at DDR400, so it is unlikely that the DDR533 is DDR (1), it is most likely DDR2.

Your board will take only DDR1 ram (sometimes just called "DDR") not DDR2 RAM.

The two are incompatible, and though they have the same number of pins, the "key" notch is out of alignment, and will not allow a DDR2 clip to be put into a DDR1 slot, and vice versa. Aside from physical differences, DDR2 operates at a much lower voltage than DDR1, and thus should not be forcibly slotted in a DDR1 slot.

Also, DDR1 is now treated as obsolete technology, so the RAM clips for DDR1 are more expensive than DDR2.

I'd suggest confirming if the DDR533 RAM is DDR 1 or DDR 2. If it is DDR1, there is no reason why it should not work with your board.
