### Post by editrix on 2008-06-27
You can map any character that your font has with your compose key. Enable xkb options, choose a Compose key (usually the right Windows key) and play around.

Also, if you're in Gnome, there's an ability to generate characters with (I think) Ctrl-Shift and a number the way you can with Windows Alt-and a number.

Sorry I can't be more specific but I'm in KDE where you can't do that, but I think if you look at the character palette and click around you eventually get all the characters in that set, with a number.
