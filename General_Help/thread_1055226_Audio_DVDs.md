### Post by Yellow Pasque on 2009-01-30
I'm not sure if this is the same thing, but I know there is such a thing as DVD-Audio, which is a super high quality audio format.
Burning one wouldn't make much sense unless you had a high-quality recorded source (e.g. 24-bit 192kHz as opposed to the CD standard 16-bit, 44KHz), or you had already ripped a DVD-Audio in a lossless format.

Also, I believe your soundcard needs special hardware and/or drivers for  playback of this format. I tried to play (a Neil Young) one under Audacious using OSS4 with an M-Audio Revo 5.1 and I was unsuccessful.
