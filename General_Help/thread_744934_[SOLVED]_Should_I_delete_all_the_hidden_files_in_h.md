### Post by kpkeerthi on 2008-04-04
You may safely delete all the dot folders and dot files. But remember that you will loose your customizations like firefox bookmarks, music player's playlist/database, themes etc (which may or may not be important depending on your need). In other words, you will be in a state you were when you installed ubuntu for the first time :)

### Post by fsmithred on 2008-04-04
If you really want to compare the default installs of ubuntu and kubuntu, you should probably have separate /home directories. When I test a new distro, I don't like to let it mess with my /home. It's easy enough to mount it so I can access my files.

 If you just want to be able to switch betweek kde and gnome, it probably makes more sense to have them both on the same installation. Then you only need to log out and in again to change from one to the other.

If your computer is getting noisy, it's likely a problem with moving parts, like fan or hard drive. Figure out which. It's better to replace it before it fails.
