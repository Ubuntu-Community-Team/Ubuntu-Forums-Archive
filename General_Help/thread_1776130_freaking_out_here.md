### Post by TBABill on 2011-06-09
I had an Acer laptop that performed similarly. It was the strangest thing and it seemed almost like someone had an extra mouse attached and clicked randomly just to mess with me. What I found my problem to be, which is probably not  your problem but throwing it out just in case, was that there was pressure put on the touchpad from the case of the laptop, whether from picking it up, setting it down or moving it around. The movement itself did not cause the problem, but rather it squeezed the case at different points depending on how it was moved, which in turn left pressures against the touchpad (sides, bottom, unknown). So I would be using it and all of a sudden the mouse would start to move or buttons would click. 
 
I only figured it out by grabbing opposite corners of the laptop (not the screen) and slightly bending it (very slightly!!). I could get it to duplicate erratic behavior after doing it and sometimes doing it would clear the problem for a period of time. I almost hated to move the thing, but being a LAPtop I actually used it in my lap so I had to life and move it. It was so frustrating I ended up getting a new laptop instead of trying to have it repaired. Short of throwing it through the window I just couldn't tolerate the behavior after I knew what caused it. Why that happend I will never know since no other laptop has done so.

### Post by strycat on 2011-06-09
Are you using Gnome or KDE?

If KDE (and I assume there is something similar for Gnome), logout.  Switch to a terminal (Ctrl-Alt-Fx) and log in.  Then do rm -rf .kde

That should make KDE restore all of the default settings and return your panels.  You'll of course have to customize them again but at least you'll have them back.
