### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-09-14
Yes, several times over.

The minimum requirements for Ubuntu are a 300Mhz (700MHz recommended) processor, 64MB (384MB recommended) of memory, and 4GB (8GB recommended) of drive space, so you're definitely fine as far as those are concerned.

The usual problem areas, especially with laptops, are the video card and the wireless card.  Can you provide specs on those?

### Post by fragos on 2008-09-15
Your video will be supported with the "vesa" driver and I believe your video is Intel which has drivers that will be selected for you during installation. The Wifi of many lower cost laptops is Broadcom which can be made to with with ndiswrapper. From a horsepower perspective you should be able to run Ubuntu. I've seen less capable machines do so nicely. At any rate the Live CD test costs you nothing.
