### Post by lechien73 on 2012-09-28
Have you tried looking at the microphone levels in Sound Settings?

On some machines (particularly Acer Aspire) I've found it beneficial to install PulseAudio Volume Control from the Software Centre. Sometimes PulseAudio treats everything as stereo - even if the microphone is mono. The two inputs cancel each other out.

After installing PulseAudio Volume Control, I click on the little padlock icon to unlink the microphone channels, and drag one channel down to 10% while leaving the other one at 80%. This often fixes it.

I blogged about the problem a couple of years ago here: [http://blog.mattrudge.net/2010/03/08/microphone-problems-in-skype-on-ubuntu/](http://blog.mattrudge.net/2010/03/08/microphone-problems-in-skype-on-ubuntu/)

Hope this helps
