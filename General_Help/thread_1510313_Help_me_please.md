Hello jspivey, and welcome!

I'm afraid I can't help with wireless, but try your wireless question in the Networking & Wireless forum. Be sure to include information about your problem, like which which version of Ubuntu you are using, and what hardware your computer has...is it a laptop? I'm sure they'll help you, but they need you to be specific about your problem.

As for learning how to use Ubuntu (or whatever Linux distro you have chosen), I've found the best way is to do searches using Google.  For instance, try searching "ubuntu tutorials" and see what comes up.  Sometimes I learned about things I didn't even know I wanted to learn :)

Or you can be more specific and add "wireless" or "compiz" (or whatever you want) to the search. 

Also, you might start with this "[Ubuntu Pocket Guide and Reference]("http://www.ubuntupocketguide.com/index_main.html")" - and there are a lot of links under the your System "Help and support" menu. But the real joy in learning to use Ubuntu is to just try things out. I also read a lot of "Sticky, How-To" threads on the forums. 

The truth is there are soooo many resources you can easily feel overwhelmed...so the best thing to have when you start out is patience, both with yourself and with folks on the forums.  The people here are great.  This is the best help forum around, I think. But you have to give as much information as you can when you have a problem.  Try to search for answers first, and let people know what solutions you have already tried.  

Also, keep in mind that you aren't really alone with learning how to use your new system.  There's LOTS of us out here that are still learning, too!  We're glad to see you here. :)

[Edit] ha ha, I am such a slow poke! When I started to answer jspivey's post there were no other comments...which proves my last statement, we really aren't alone here - that's the joy of Ubuntu forums :)
