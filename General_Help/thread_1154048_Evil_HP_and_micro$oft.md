### Post by Alterax on 2009-05-09
Don't let them bully you.  When you first turn on your computer, you will be presented with the Windows End User License Agreement, which is, like it or not, a legally binding contract.

You do not have to agree to this contract, and in it, it is very cleanly stated that you may contact the vendor and request a refund.  Which is in your case clearly HP.  Tell them that in accordance with the terms of the licensing agreement, you want your money back for the operating system that you cannot use because you do not agree to the further terms of the EULA--and have no desire to run the product anyhow.

They tell you that you have no choice, remind them that you did have a choice when you chose HP, and request the person's name, ID number, and a transfer to his/her supervisor.  By all means be polite, but be firm and resolute.

Ultimately, they cannot sell you an unusable product, nor can they force you into a legally binding contract without your consent.  If they could, you would never be presented with the terms of the EULA or the option of not agreeing to it.  You probably won't get the retail cost of a full install (299.00 US), but you may end up with about $40.00 US--roundabout what an OEM gets as a deal per license.
