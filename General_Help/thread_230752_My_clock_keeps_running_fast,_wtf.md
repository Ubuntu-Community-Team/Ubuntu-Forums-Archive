### Post by mcduck on 2006-08-07
If your CMOS battery (on your motherboard, typically somewhere in bottom right corner of the board) is running low on power it causes computer's clock to run faster. You could try replacing it with a new one..

(syncing to time servers keeps your clock in time, but doesn't fix the original problem. And when the battery finally runs out of power you need to configure your BIOS every time you boot the machine, as without the CMOS battery your settings are lost every time you cut the power..)
