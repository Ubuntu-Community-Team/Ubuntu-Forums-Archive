### Post by Firstpenguin on 2010-01-18
Hi,

I've been playing with Ubuntu for a while now.  I'm not an expert but I'm getting around just fine so far with online guides and it works great for 90% of my personal needs.  One of my older relatives needs a system that is simple and requires little to no end-user maintenance.  I've configured a Dell Zino HD with Ubuntu 9.10 and I'm really happy with the performance I'm getting I almost don't want to ship it off.

My problem is I live in the northeast and my grandmother is in the midwest.  I need a way to configure a router and the computer so that I can ship them configured to my Grandmother and have one of my other relatives plug everything in so that when it's plugged into the modem I can gain remote access with out any expertise on there end.  I know this usually not the case but I'm open to ideas.  I'd like to be able to do this from any internet connection on my laptop since I'll be the only one in my family capable of troubleshooting any problems with ubuntu or make any desired changes.

On another note does anyone know of any related enrichment classes on linux or related networking
in the NYC area.


Thanks in advance.

### Post by little_penguin on 2010-04-25
This post probably comes too late for you, but for the benefit of others in a similar boat - you can try Teamviewer, which is now available for linux as a deb, rpm or tarball. Install this on both your grandmother's and your PC. No port forwarding necessary. All your grandmother needs to do is open Teamviewer and tell you her Teamviewer session ID and password.
