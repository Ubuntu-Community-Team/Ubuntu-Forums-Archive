### Post by MoebusNet on 2018-09-13
I'm one of those who holds on to their PC's perhaps longer than they should. In my experience, the areas of computer incompatibility with Linux in general tend to be the bits that depend on proprietary code such as video cards, wireless cards, track pads and to a lesser extent oddball CPU's. Examples include Nvidia dropping support for FX5200 and older video cards, some non-Synaptic track pads losing support and dropping support for Pentium M mobile CPU's that don't advertise PAE capability. In addition, the current trend is to support only 64-bit-capable CPU's. The best way I've found to guarantee compatibility is to utilize an Ubuntu-Live DVD or thumb drive and to boot up the system. Other than that, there are computers sold with Linux preinstalled; System76 comes to mind. Picking up a PC that came new with Linux preinstalled shouldn't cost much more than a Windows machine, plus you know it should be compatible.
