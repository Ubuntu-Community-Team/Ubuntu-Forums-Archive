### Post by Telengard C64 on 2011-12-01
> **oldos2er said:**
> It's usually not a good idea to mix repositories from different distros unless you're absolutely sure of what you're doing.

^this
And there's no reason to try since anything available to one GNU/Linux distro is most likely available to all others.

A lot of the things people think of as Bash commands are actually separate programs external to Bash. Many of them are from the GNU Core Utilities suite and common to all GNU/Linux distros.

Instead of trying to use packages from another distro you can always compile from source. Before doing that though, you should try really hard to find a precompiled binary specific to your distro and architecture.
