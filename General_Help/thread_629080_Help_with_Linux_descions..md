### Post by NightCrawler03X on 2007-12-02
I would suggest Xubuntu first. Play around with your system, learn new things. Learn the command line, how to edit system files and configs with a text editor, etc. You don't "need" to do this usually in Xubuntu, but doing so will help you get a bit of experience. 

When you're confident and you know your way round, I suggest [Arch Linux](http://archlinux.org).

@ everyone else
By the way, this thing about "if you're computer is powerful enough, run ubuntu, otherwise run xubuntu, is a bit flawed".
you're right in your own opinion, but in practical terms, what you run on your computer (even if it's a super computer) should be as lightweight as possible.
You don't need desktop effects (at least in my opinion, they are annoying).

Saying that is like saying "hey, let's use more resources just because we actually *have* more".
You can do everything you do in gnome in xfce aswell, but at less expense of resources.

Of course, it's more a matter of preference.
I prefer simplicity, that is, only have what I need, which is why Arch Linux rocks because it literally doesn't automatically do anything for you; you have to do everything yourself, so you get exactly what you want, no more no less.
