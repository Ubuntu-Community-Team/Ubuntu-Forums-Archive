Ok, here is reality:

[list]
[*] Most viruses are made for Windows, because Windows is the easiest to infect and many people use it. Windows also has severe security/permissions issues and a fragile registry. 
[*] If I a virus is going to be made for Linux, it will have a lot to take into account. It would have to target a specific distro and setup. In Windows, IE, Outlook, etc are very commonly used and exploited. Linux has no such standard applications.
[*] If you just went to a site, you aren't affected. In theory, everything is possible, but there are limits in reality
[*] If you did get a virus or some sort of malicious code, it would only affect your home directory. Is everything still there?
[/list]

If you went to a site, and you heard your harddrive doing things, it was likely just a script/flash object or whatever trying to crash your browser or computer, possibly just swap. It didn't work apparently.

(Even if you have wine, that doesn't mean much. Web attacks are based on scripts and Wine wouldn't do that.)
