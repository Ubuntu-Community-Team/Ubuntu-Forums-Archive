### Post by scottro on 2008-03-11
Well, there are many of them.  Sometimes independent consultants or independent companies.   Even with MS, I don't think most of the support is from MS itself, it's usually from a VAR (Value Added Reseller.)

I assume there's already a company giving you outside support.  It's quite possible that they also offer OSS products as well, since the demand seems to be increasing. 

On the other hand, if your current IT staff is only familiar with Windows, they probably, with good reason, don't want to change.  They are the ones who will be getting the calls for problems.

### Post by cmnorton on 2008-03-11
Canonical supports Ubuntu; server support is $750.00 / year for Ubuntu server. Canonical's support is quite good. In addition to Canonical, there are lots of professional services companies. You have to interview them and get the best feel for who is going to do the best job for you.

We run two of our most important applications on Linux. One of them used to be on SCO Unix. That is on RH EL 4 now. The other is migrating from RH 9 to RH EL 4 this summer. In the four years I have maintained these systems, we have had to have a consultant for purely OS issues fewer than six times. Most of the time it was for SCO, not Linux.  

You just have to think about what you want supported and the cost.
