### Post by sudodus on 2014-02-06
Welcome to the Ubuntu Forums :-)

You have two months of possible dual booting before the end of life of XP. You can use that interval to evaluate different desktop environments (flavours of Ubuntu or other distros), and also learn the various tasks, and migrate bookmarks and address books. But if you know what you want, you can just backup your personal files and overwrite Windows. That is by far the easiest and fastest option.

One way to backup is to clone XP to an external disk. You cannot run it from there, but you can mount it and access the files from linux.

Before you do anything else, I suggest that you*** post the specs of your computer*** to make it possible to give relevant advice, what desktop environments (flavours of Ubuntu or other distros) to try. ***Try running them live*** if possible, before you start installing anything to the internal drive.

- Brand name and model of the computer or motherboard
- CPU
- RAM (size)
- graphics chip/card
- wifi chip/card
