### Post by Thee on 2013-01-21
Well the best thing to do is to scan it at maximum DPI possible (600 DPI or more if your scanner allows it) and if you have option to save it in TIFF or PNG format and not JPG while scanning, do so, because that will also have impact on the quality.

After you do that, then you can start playing with GIMP's brightness, contrast and levels options. You can also use Sharpen (but not too much) to get the text to be more clear.
