### Post by Mark Phelps on 2009-07-05
> **XProflmfao said:**
> Hi everyone! My life is ruined....! When I downloaded Ubuntu, I accidentally downloaded a clean install without knowing that it actually deletes everything from your old OS, 
The Ubuntu install provides several different options -- all clearly indicated during the installation process. IF you were careless and didn't read closely, you wiped out your Vista installation yourself.  This is not something that Ubuntu did to you.
>  mine was windows and it had extremely important files such as pictures, documents, etc. 
 So important that you didn't bother to check in the forum for advice when you clearly didn't know what the different installation options would do?
> Is there any way to get back everything? 
Everything -- no.  Your files -- maybe.  If you search the forum for uses of "testdisk", you'll find examples where folks were able to recover SOME of their erased files.
> also, is there a way to reinstall windows vista without a disk? when I bought my PC, it didn't include a disk. That's why I can't go back to vista.
Your machine came preinstalled with Vista, and part of the licensing agreement with machine vendors is that they provide a way to restore the OS, not that they provide a disk for doing that.  You're machine most probably came with a boot option for restoring the OS from a hidden file or partition -- which you may have wiped out when you chose to install Ubuntu using the entire disk.  If you really want Vista back, check with the provider of your machine, They may be willing to SELL you a restore disk for a very low price --- which will restore Vista, but not your data files.
>  No offense, but I've had it with Ubuntu, and I only started using it a few hours ago...
Which, if you'd bothered to read the forums, you would have learned that there is something called the LiveCD mode which would have enabled you to boot into Ubuntu and try it out without installing it.

So, you need to figure out what your priorities are as soon as possible, because the more you mess with your machine, the greater the possibility that you will not be able to get ANYTHING back.

If your priority is to recover data files, you will need an external disk or USB stick to use to recover files from your existing drive, and either search for advice on using "testdisk", or go out and purchase GetDataBack, or RecoveryMyFiles, or some other such MS Windows utility, boot from that, and use the utility to try to recover as much of your data files as possible.  And then, contact your machine vendor to see about getting a Vista recovery CD from them to restore your machine.
