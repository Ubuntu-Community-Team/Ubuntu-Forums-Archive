Why do you think it's your CMOS?  

If the CMOS memory is dammaged, you would have to physically change the chip.  But that's not your problem.  If you want to flash your motherboard with the latest BIOS, you would have to look up what motherboard make, model and version it is and find the latest one from the manufacturer.  They usually provide a tool for flashing your motherboard.

If your only symptom is that your computer reboots and has ram problems, it may be a few things.  Is it overheating (fans wear out over time)?  Is the power supply overtaxed (added new hardware)?  Is there debris in your case that is causing a short circuit?  Etc...
