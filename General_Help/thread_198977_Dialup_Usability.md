### Post by Bartender on 2006-06-18
Mau -
Dial-up can be a royal pain since most modems won't work without some tweaking, which can be a challenge for newbs.

There's something else to consider even if you get dial-up working. 
 
I'm stuck with dial-up at home, but with a Windows PC I've gotten around this roadblock by taking advantage of the broadband connection at my workplace and a USB stick.  OpenOffice and other big d/l's woulda been impossible otherwise.
For most things, Linux doesn't work the same way.  You can't just grab the package you want with a USB stick and bring it home.  The PC that you're tweaking has to be the one connected to the net.  This has to do with dependencies.
What that means is you'll always feel like you're dragging an anchor.  Automatix sounds interesting?  Not gonna happen.  You want to upgrade to Dapper online?  I don't think so.  Ubuntu with a broadband connection would be exciting.  On dial-up it'd be one frustration after another.  
Someone will probly say I'm full of it.  Whatever.  Just my 2 cents worth.

### Post by Malac on 2006-06-26
Just my two-penneth but this thread seems a little negative so on the positive side.
I have used Windows for years since 3.0 and recently changed my XP/98 dual-boot into a triple-boot Ubuntu/XP/98 machine (in that order) and would say I'm a newbie to Linux myself.
A lot of modems are now covered under Ubuntu/Linux by galant souls struggling away to write drivers without breaking copyright and so on.

I have a PCI PCTel HSP 56k MicroModem (winmodem)
I found setting this up was really easy it was setup in 10 mins in Ubuntu.
True I had to go into Windows to download the Linux driver files but once they were extracted it was just a case of running the ./setup command and voila all was working.
The dialout settings only required a bit of typing in of DNS servers and username/password (no worse than windows). I now have a shortcut on my desktop to dialout and one to hangup.

I admit dialup is slow/frustrating/expensive if you only have 56k and have to download huge files/updates but then that is equally true of Windows.


Anyhoo, have fun Ubuntu-ing. :D
