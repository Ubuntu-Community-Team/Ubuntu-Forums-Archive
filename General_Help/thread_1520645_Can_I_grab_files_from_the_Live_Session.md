### Post by efflandt on 2010-06-29
The problem is that you would need enough RAM to hold the files, because the drive (and any swap) cannot be mounted while repartitioning and formatting your internal drive.  So it would best to copy or back up any files you want to save to something external, whether USB flash or hard drive, or CD/DVD, or network shared space on another computer.

USB flash or hard drive can come in handy for other things.  For example I did not want to tamper with my work laptop,  So I am posting this from a motel running 64-but 10.04 LTS booted from a USB WD Passport using the motel wifi.
