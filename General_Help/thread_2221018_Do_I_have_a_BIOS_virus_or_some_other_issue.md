### Post by Darryl_Bordeleau on 2014-04-30
Hi all!

A few months ago I made the switch to Ubuntu from Windows 8. My goal was to get rid of a virus and have an all-around more secure OS. The virus that I had on Windows interfered with internet browsing by redirecting me to other sites (presumably to get me to buy travel packages to various exotic locals!) 

Anyway, I switched to Ubuntu 12.04 and for a month everything was good. Then the virus came back (the exact same virus affected my Ubuntu internet browsers in the exact same way funneling me to the exact same sites). I suspected that somehow this was one amazing virus that managed to find a way through my partition from windows. So I deleted the windows partition and gave that space back to Ubuntu using dparted.

Everything was okay again for about a week. The virus came back so I reinstalled a clean copy of 12.04. The virus (same virus same effects mind you) came back right away. This time it came back before I even copied my backed up files from an external hard drive, so I don't think the virus is in there.

I installed 14.04, doing another full erase of the old OS, not a upgrade from 12.04, again the same deleterious effects on internet browsing returned.

I read up on BIOS viruses and it seems pretty close to what I have.

There is one other possibility, my Android phone seems to be plagued by the same problems and they too survived restoring factory defaults (the Android issue isn't really a problem since I rarely browse with my phone, still I didn't want a virus on my phone). Obviously, because of this, when I updated to 14.04 I kept the phone off until the problems made their inevitable return just to make sure a virus wasn't reinfecting me via wireless internet.

So from what you have read, my question is in the topic: Does this sound like a BIOS virus or something else?

### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-04-30
Does this browser hijacking happen on all sites that you visit or only on certain sites? All browsers have a cache, a folder, where they store images and scripts to speed up the loading of sites. Unless that cache is cleared it could be that this redirection is happening not because of a virus but because of some script (cookie) in the browser's cache.
