### Post by shel-hall on 2009-05-03
> **randyklein99 said:**
> It got me thinking that you could probably use a netbook to do the same thing, control a NAS, and probably alot more.  Would this just be overkill though, or is it a viable option?
I've been thinking along those lines, and I don't see why it wouldn't work Just Fine.

Of course, it depends on what kind of server you're talking about, for how many users, etc., etc., so forth, and so on.

For most uses, the real speed limit is going to be the network, either the LAN or the connection to the internet.  You don't have to have a whole lot of CPU power to keep a 100baseT network busy, much less a DSL connection to the 'net.

For a fileserver, a lot depends on hw you connect the disks to the netbook.  If you do them via USB, file access might be a little slow, but doing them the over the LAN (in a little NAS box, say) adds Another Dam' Box to Break.  In either case, unless you have a real Need for Speed, it ought be good enough.

In my particular case, I want the machine to serve DNS (so I can blackhole ad servers, malware purveyors, and other bad actors), NTP, and hold file backups for later transfer over the internet.  I'm thinking that a netbook with a small SSD drive (just for the OS), a bunch of RAM (so stuff gets cached), and a big external hard disk (for real data) would do fine.  This would have an extra advantage for me and my current peripatetic lifestyle in that it would be very portable.

So, to quote my mother, "to make a long story short, if I haven't missed my chance," I'm thinking seriously along the netbook-as-server lines.  Now we just need to hear from someone with real experience.

-Shel
