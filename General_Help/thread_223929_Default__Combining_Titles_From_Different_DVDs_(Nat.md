### Post by grimmson on 2006-07-27
No problem. You can still use dvd shrink in linux / wine. Google install instructions. If you don't mind re-encodeing your movies to mpeg-4 acidrip can do it also. I had to play with acidrip for a while to really get the picture. Use wine / dvd decripter to get the vob's to your harddrive (seems like that step works well in shrink to combine movies.) Open acidrip>load dir of movies and select clips from window. If you have a good understanding of a dvd structure it'll be a piece of cake.

Although I haven't done it myself, I do think Avidemux is what you want. You can reencode the ripped data into DVD format at any size you want (so, each episode would be at a size so they could all fit on one DVD).

Avidemux is located in the multiverse repository.

You should also be able to create simple menus to access the 7 episodes should you fancy it. I think [ManDVD](http://www.kde-apps.org/content/show.php?content=38347) would be a good app to put it all together. Not sure of a GNOME equivalent though.
