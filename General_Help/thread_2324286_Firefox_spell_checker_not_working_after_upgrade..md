### Post by irvine_himself2 on 2016-05-15
It's basically a play on gender that doesn't easily translate: 


[LIST=1]
[*]"ma pièce" is street slang for small change and is feminine. So a beggar asks for "une petite pièce" 
[*]"le sous" is an archaic coin similar to a farthing and is male. With the introduction of the Napoleonic code, under French law it is illegal to be broke. A beggar must have enough money to buy a coffee and a baguette. Hence, they used to cry "malheureusement je pas de sous". With inflation it became "malheureusement je pas dix franc", and nowadays "malheureusement je pas deux euro" 
[/LIST]
 
Anyway, putting it all together we have a trans-gendered sentence where the subject, (ie your loose change,) starts off as female and becomes male.

Personally, I think it is quite topical
