### Post by Raptor45 on 2007-05-03
6.06.1 > 7.04 > 6.10 > Older Releases

Thats they way stability "technically" should go, though in certain situations it might be different.

6.06 is an LTS, and has been around for quite some time. As an LTS its specifically made to be extra stable, and since its "well aged" by now, its got plenty of testing to make it so.

7.04 is the next newest release, so you've got all the latest software and such, with, theoretically, the most bug-fixed software. In general, people seem to praise it as a pretty stable release too.

In my experience, stability just drops off beyond that, as you get older.

### Post by jerrylamos on 2007-05-03
Depends.  For me, Dapper 6.06.1 is the most stable.  I have run Edgy 6.10, wasn't as stable, and Feisty 7.04 quite unstable HOWEVER one system which invariably crashed booting 6.06 runs just fine with 7.04.  Now this post is from a -very- early level of 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon, new packages, new kernel,  which seems to have all 7.04's troubles with a couple more.  However as you can see it is running, and to be fair some of Feisty 7.04's problems with new function/packages are technically complex to fix.

You pays your money and takes your choice.  Just because there's a new release doesn't mean it's better for you and doesn't mean you need to "upgrade", i.e., change to it.  That is, unless you're looking for trouble like me.
