### Post by _AltF4_ on 2011-11-13
I am trying to figure out a way to completely disable my touchpad. In previous versions this was not difficult, I am not sure how to do it anymore. gconf /desktop/gnome/peripherals/touchpad/touchpad_enabled no longer seems to have any relevance on touchpad operation.

I had been using the following script to enable/disable my touchpad.
```

#!/bin/sh
gconftool --toggle /desktop/gnome/peripherals/touchpad/touchpad_enabled
gconftool --toggle /desktop/gnome/peripherals/touchpad/disable_while_typing
exit 0

#if anyone is wondering I disabled disable_while_typing because the touchpad
#would enable its self anytime I typed anything when it was enabled.

```I like to keep my touchpad sensitivity as high as possible but this can be problematic when trying to type. It is very annoying to accidentally click in the middle of typing. I have never found the disable_while_typing setting to be sufficient when writing longer documents.

### Post by jboyette on 2011-11-13
I too have not figured out a way to completely disable my touchpad, but what I do to turn it off after booting is to type the following in a terminal window:

sudo rmmod psmouse

It allows a usb mouse, wireless or otherwise, but shuts down the touchpad.
