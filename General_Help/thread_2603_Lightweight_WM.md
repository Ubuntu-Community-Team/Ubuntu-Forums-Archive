### Post by Joeb on 2004-10-29
Someone in a previous post complained about how sluggish the Live CD was on the box he was running (something like a P3 600Mhz 256MB).  A comment was made that running gnome from the compressed CD was most likely the root of the problem (although it runs fine on my AMD XP1900+ with 512MB!).

Anyway, that got me to thinking, since a lot of people use a live cd to try things out and many of them might have older hardware, why not use a lightweight windowmanager for the live CD instead of gnome?

Specifically, I was thinking of something like a nicely configured XFCE.  Since it uses many of the gnome libs and gtk2, it wouldn't be a big departure and yet would save tremendously on limited machine resources.

Any thoughts?

### Post by misGnomer on 2004-10-29
Jspired is right in saying that it wouldn't be Ubuntu if it used another WM, but such a distro would be quite [doable](http://www.morphix.org/modules/sections/index.php?op=listarticles&secid=2) and  [maintainable](http://www.morphix.org/modules/xoopsfaq/) using Alextreme's modular Morphix as a starting point.

I know that Alextreme is participating in Ubuntu-live's development and Ubuntu is indeed listed on Morphix pages as being a derivative.

Since there is already also an XFCE4 module, one should be able to rebuild a complete updated ISO with his/her fave Ubuntu apps and charasteristics. IIRC the current XFCE4-based ISO is just over 200MB so people with 512MB or even 256MB USB sticks might like such a very-slim version. It might also be an interesting proposal in developing countries where hardware is often a couple of generations old.

I wonder if the XFCE developers would be interested in creating and maintaining their own "XFCE reference distro" in parallel with Ubuntu...
