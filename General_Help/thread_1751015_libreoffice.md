### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-05-06
There were two types of person developing OpenOffice, those that worked for the company that owned OpenOffice and those that did not. Some developers felt that their contributions were being ignored in favor of the contributions by those employed by the company.

The company also wanted to claim all the code in OpenOffice as belonging to the company even if it had not been produced by one of their employees.

So, many developers left the project and set up another project and LibreOffice is the result. The people at Canonical have accepted LibreOffice as a replacement for OpenOffice because the LibreOffice developers are closer to the ideals of open source development than the company that has ownership of OpenOffice.

The long answer as I see it and the reason why I am now using LibreOffice.
