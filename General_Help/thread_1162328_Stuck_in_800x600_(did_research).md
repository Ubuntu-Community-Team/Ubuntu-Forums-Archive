### Post by JKyleOKC on 2009-05-17
Try adding the line "Defaultdepth 16" to the Screen section of your xorg.conf file. I have had to do that on several systems here, where the new xorg routines refuse to recognize the full capabilities of the hardware (it wasn't necessary under 7.04 but when I upgraded to Hardy, everything went to low resolution).

The depth is the number of bits in each pixel; at 24 or 32 the drivers are going to want huge amounts of video RAM to get any resolution higher than 800x600. At 16, only a quarter as much RAM is necessary and you still have some 64K possible colors. Since most of us can't tell the difference between that and the 16 million colors possible with 32-bit pixels, it's a trade-off worth making...

I suspect it's a bug in xorg that's preventing the system from grabbing enough video RAM, since all these boxes happily go to even higher resolution than 1024x768 when running Windows or older Linux versions (I'm running this one at 1440x900)!
