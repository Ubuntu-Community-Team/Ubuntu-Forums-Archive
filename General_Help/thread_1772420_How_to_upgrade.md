### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-05-31
Update Manager should have given you an option to upgrade to 9.10. Did it not show a button to click to do this upgrade? You go from 9.04 to 9.10 on to 10.04 and then to 10.10 and finally to 11.04. Or you can stop at 10.10 if you want. Or 10.04. Each time Update manager will give you an option to upgrade to the next version.

This worked for me about a year ago.

+1 for this suggestion. When you do a clean release, if you have not already, I suggest making a separate /home partition when setting up your partition table. This way if you want to re-install at some point in the future you can simply reinstall / and mount your existing /home partition which contains all your personal settings, files, etc.

The backup software I recommend for ease-of-use and its ability to clone your entire hard drive is [Clonezilla]("http://www.clonezilla.org")
