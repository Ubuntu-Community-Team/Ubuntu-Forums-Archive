### Post by cariboo on 2010-07-05
1080p or 720p has nothing to do with web pages, depending on how your computer is connected to the TV is what determines the resolution. Most TV's are limited to 1920X1080 maximum resolution if you use an HDMI connection, using a VGA connection usually means a lower resolution.

### Post by efflandt on 2010-07-05
Personally when I use my 1280x800 laptop or when I tried an older 27" 1280x720 (true 720p) HDTV Ready LCD, I found myself constantly having to scroll, compared with my previous 19" 1280x1024 monitor.  So I got a 32" 1080p HDTV to use as a monitor and love it.  Note that a 1080p HDTV works just as well with VGA from my laptop as with DVI to HDMI on my desktop.  However, a digital connection usually automatically uses the native resolution, but I have to use xrandr script to set 1080p for the VGA laptop.

Since my older computer is not that quick I have an external player for BluRay and streaming Netflix HD. 1080p works well for that and lots of data as a computer monitor, but I can switch it to 720p to play Hulu.com video at an acceptable speed on the old PC.

Most 720p displays call themselves 1366x768 although WinXP used 1360x764 for my 40" and optimum for Linux might be 1360x765 or 1360x768.  But even that is about 30% less lines than 1080p.

Especially for a larger screen like that, I would look for a deal on 1080p.
