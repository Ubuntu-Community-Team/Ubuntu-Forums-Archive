### Post by vivakh on 2011-08-23
I think you should be ok switching over if you use it lightly like that. As it is still in beta, however, don't rely on it too much. If you're going to store a lot of files onto the file system etc, there's always the chance of the beta getting corrupt due to some faulty update and will render your data inaccessible. If your media/files and other important information are on a separate hard disk/external drive, then you can go ahead and use it, it shouldn't be much of a problem, and i guess the foreground of 11.10 is solid, so light users may benefit ok from it.

Thats all assumption though, ok? You can take the risk if you don't plan on using it heavily and you are not going to rely on it heavily for getting work done, etc. Otherwise, stick to natty and wait it out.

### Post by oldfred on 2011-08-23
Do you have a spare 20 or 25GB? If so just install 11.10 into another partition. Then if for any reason it does not work, you can still boot your current install.

I have almost all my Ubuntu's still bootable. I have a couple of 100GB partitions for data (no movies) and many installs. At 25GB each my 650GB drive will hold a lot of Ubuntus ( and a few others).
