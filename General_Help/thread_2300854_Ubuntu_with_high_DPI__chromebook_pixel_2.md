### Post by TheFu on 2015-10-29
All new computers come with HW that isn't always supported for 6-12 months by non-commercial Linux installs. For about 18 months, I had to manually compile touchpad drivers for my chromebook, for example. Of course, at 19 months, the cheap keyboard started acting up. Cleaned it and most of the keys started working well enough, but for the*last 3 weeks the space-bar hasn't worked correctly.  Disassembled it today and now  it works about 40%  of the time.   

A cheap  keyboard can really ruin an otherwise fine machine.

For  that  price, think I'd get  the  Dell XPS 13 with Ubuntu pre-installed. I've played with it  and for $1300, it is a beautiful machine with huge resolution (3200x1800), normal keyboard and long battery. Just sayin'.
