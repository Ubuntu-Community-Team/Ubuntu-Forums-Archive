### Post by TheFu on 2023-09-04
First, read this: [https://www.wired.com/story/logitech-harmony-smart-remote-lost-its-way/](https://www.wired.com/story/logitech-harmony-smart-remote-lost-its-way/)
> Once, we dreamed of one controller to rule them all. But when _Logitech killed off the Harmony line_, it ended an era.

I looked into getting a Harmony RC years ago, to replace a manually programmed HTM high-end remote that only had MS-Windows software and a 2.5mm serial jack connector.  HTM was bought and the replacements appear to be the same, just with a black case, not the gray case. HTM remote used batteries like they were free.

I saw that Harmony didn't work with Linux, so I skipped it completely. Life is too short for me to deal with companies that don't properly support the OS I choose to use. We all have different hassle levels we are willing to allow. This is my "forgetaboutit" level these days.

Another alternative ... for your consideration    ....    Had a cheap MCE-RC6 remove for about $17 to use with my Kodi system since around 2011.  That worked for about a decade until a few buttons wore out in late 2022.  

Last fall, I bought a 4-in-1 Remote that has thousands of codes for different devices.  $20, I think.  It is labeled as an MCE-RC6 remote on amz, which I think means it was designed for MS-Windows Media Center. **Inteset** is the brand. It doesn't feel cheap. It is substantial, much nicer than the one before.  I have no idea if it works with optical devices (DVD/Bluray), but it does work with TVs, streaming boxes/sticks, Rokus, and my Onkyo receiver.  There are codes for all the popular receiver brands.  [https://www.amazon.com/Inteset-Universal-Backlit-Learning-Streamers/dp/B00M4I1BAY](https://www.amazon.com/Inteset-Universal-Backlit-Learning-Streamers/dp/B00M4I1BAY) <--- not affiliate link.  A USB-to-IR receiver is necessary if you plan to connect it to a computer.

However, it doesn't support our Epson projector with a code, but since it is a learning remote, manual setup can be performed to replace any IR remote.  For the projector, I really only need the power button 99.999999% of the time anyway.  For anything more complex, pull the Epson remote from a plastic bag (dust protections) put batteries into it to do all the fancy things.

A number of reviewers for the Inteset remote seem to be old Harmony users and rated it, if that helps.
