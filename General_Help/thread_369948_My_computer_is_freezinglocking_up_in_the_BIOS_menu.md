### Post by chewearn on 2007-02-25
If you are thinking about a BIOS re-flashing, maybe it will be better to try setting it back to default first. Usually, there is a jumper on the motherboard to clear the CMOS settings (refer to your motherboard manual).

Another thing is, some motherboard have alternate BIOS flashing mode (my Asus motherboard used to have that).  If your can do so, you should be check to see if you can enter the flashing program; then download the flash bin before resetting the CMOS.  Then if resetting don't work, you can straight away try the reflashing from BIOS without needing to boot to the OS.
