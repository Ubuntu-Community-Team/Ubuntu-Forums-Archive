### Post by Roger Mudd on 2006-10-25
Have you enabled 3d acceleration on your video card? If not, try getting that set up before running complex screensavers. Or, just choose the blank screensaver.
Screensavers are nice, but some can be taxing on video hardware. Personally, I think they're nice to look at, but I'm not sure they're worth it in the long run. Screensavers were designed to protect your monitor from burn-in (which may or may not be an issue with newer monitors). Yet the more complicated screensavers seem to stress systems more than they should    at idle. Kind of ironic.
