### Post by grahammechanical on 2014-10-11
Lubuntu has a software centre just as Ubuntu and its other flavours do. Hydrogen and LMMS are in there for sure. And Audacity as well if that is what you are looking for instead of Audasity. The software centre will take care of dependencies. Which are libraries that one application depends on to function correctly. But wait a minute.

What are the specifications of these machines? The CPU? The amount of RAM? The graphic adapter and the amount of memory in the adapter? I have nothing against Lubuntu but you might want to look at Ubuntu Studio. It is an official re-spin of Ubuntu with default applications specifically for audio, visual and other specialist areas. We get a choice of installing all the applications or just those for our specialist area.

Ubuntu Studio also uses a low-latency kernel or a real time kernel. I do not know what that means but it think that it is a thing to have for people doing audio work.

[https://ubuntustudio.org/tour/](https://ubuntustudio.org/tour/)

[https://ubuntustudio.org/tour/audio/](https://ubuntustudio.org/tour/audio/)

What is more, there is a section of the forum for Ubuntu Studio related matters.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=335](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=335)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio/SupportedHardware](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio/SupportedHardware)
