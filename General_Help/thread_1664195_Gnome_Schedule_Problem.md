### Post by anon_user on 2011-01-10
Hello Ubuntu.

A very brief introduction - I am an experienced user.

I have recently installed gnome schedule in order to shut down at a certain time each day with the help of [http://sachinssharma.blogspot.com/2009/02/scheduling-shutdown-in-ubuntu.html](http://sachinssharma.blogspot.com/2009/02/scheduling-shutdown-in-ubuntu.html). That part of it works. However, when I want it to run a simple command to open a wav file, i.e. gnome-open ~/Documents/example.wav or do the xdg-open equivalent, it will only do it when I press the 'Run Selected Task' button, but when it reaches the pre-configured time it disappears from the tray not having executed as I want it to. Any suggestions? Thank you.

### Post by anon_user on 2011-01-10
Solved - it was a case of making it an "x application" and running it under my login, and not as 'root'.
