### Post by WorMzy on 2010-05-16
You can't remove Plymouth. Annoyingly, removing it also removes all the base Ubuntu files. Unless that's been changed in a recent update you'll have to live with Plymouth installed.

However, it may be possible to have usplash show instead. I have no idea how you'd go about setting that up, and it'd probably have a detrimental effect on your boot-up time, if you still want to do it, I suggest searching on google (or equivalent) for instructions on how to set usplash up as the default splash screen.
