### Post by manimal347 on 2008-02-04
Adobe has always had a weak commitment to the Linux platform. For example, Adobe skipped version 8 flat out, and jumped us from version seven to a late arrival of version 9. While the Linux version has brutal system requirements for what it does, any system new enough to have onboard 6150 graphics has a powerful enough CPU to at least play Youtube videos at "full" framerate. As mediocre as the program might be, no this actually isn't normal, and you may have found something wrong. What, I really don't know.

I do assume you have a CPU that's clocked at at least 2GHZ or equiv (EX: Sempron 2800,) otherwise that would be your problem. It sure is a slideshow on my Athlon 850; perhaps 3-4FPS under Linux, despite the fact that Mplayer can decode those same .flv files I download from youtube in realtime.

In fact here's a crude workaround. Use a site that grabs Youtube videos, and watch them in Mplayer with OSS sound. On the last bit, Mplayer is slightly faster working on OSS, so say lots of people.

Here's a site that grabs the movies from the Youtube server and downloads them in .flv format to your hard disk.
[http://www.techcrunch.com/get-youtube-movie/](http://www.techcrunch.com/get-youtube-movie/)
