### Post by Zyell002 on 2015-07-25
Hey robn30,

I have been running Ubuntu on my yoga 2 pro for almost a year now.  I started with Ubuntu 14.04, but just recently upgraded to 15.04.  I would definitely recommend 15.04, as it works much better on the system, for example wifi had to have a kernel fix applied in 14.04 but has no issues on 15.04. 

Honestly, I find that the laptop functions better on Ubuntu and even gets better battery life than windows.  I would recommend the following:

[LIST]
[*]Install TLP for battery savings, I get about 1.5 hours more of battery life with it.
[*]Use this code to enable autorotation of the screen [https://github.com/pfps/yoga-laptop](https://github.com/pfps/yoga-laptop)
[*]I also wrote a script to fill in what I think is a missing essential touchscreen gesture, the right click, that you can find here [https://github.com/Zyell/Python-Touchscreen-RightClick](https://github.com/Zyell/Python-Touchscreen-RightClick) 
[/LIST]

There are also some great posts in other forums, though I have found that running 15.04 rather than 14.04 doesn't require most of the workarounds recommended in some of the posts.

Anyway, good luck with your linux installation if you haven't already done it.  I hope you enjoy it as much as I have!
