### Post by AlphaLexman on 2011-01-28
That is really not a ubuntu or even a microsoft issue, but a BIOS situation. Usually there is a 'hot-key' during the boot process that will let you edit the settings. 

Most common 'hot-keys' are: [del, F1, F2]. Some manufacturers have a splash screen which hides the instructions to edit the BIOS, so you may have to guess, if that is the case.
