### Post by ubunterooster on 2010-07-19

Edit: If you go to the menu, and click and hold an item, you can bring that to the desktop as an icon

Here is a link that confirms that it was the enlightenment e17 desktop shown in the link posted above.
[http://www.desktoplinux.com/news/NS7114714037.html](http://www.desktoplinux.com/news/NS7114714037.html)
That was the Gos Linux desktop from a few years back (now they are using Gnome + Google Gadgets [http://www.thinkgos.com/gos/screenshots.html](http://www.thinkgos.com/gos/screenshots.html)).

Here's a pretty good video of Enlightenment e17 in action:
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=10kg2aSB8q4](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=10kg2aSB8q4)
And another:
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nCJ8QDfA95U](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nCJ8QDfA95U)

If you would like to try out the E17 desktop, it can be found by searching for e17 in synaptic package manager. 

I can't say that the Enlightment e17 desktop is the best way to get a modern and bubbly looking desktop, but I can say that it is not the only way to get a certain type of look. Enlightenment is truly unique in it's own rights though. However, There are many nice docks and widgets and multitudes of themes that can transform the Gnome desktop (and even the KDE desktop) into something special to look at too.

To spice up your current desktop:
For widgets check out adesklets, gdesklets, screenlets, and google gadgets. For docks, check out docky, avant-window-navigator (awn), cairo-dock, and more. A nice stand alone clock applet is cairo-clock. Many people are into using conky, a light-weight system monitor (and much more) that displays on the desktop.

Themes can be found at
[http://e17-stuff.org/](http://e17-stuff.org/) (enlightenment themes)
[http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/) (for gnome desktops, Ubuntu)
More themes sites like these can be found by going to [http://opendesktop.org/](http://opendesktop.org/) and browsing the menu at very top of the page.

Hope this helps. ;)
