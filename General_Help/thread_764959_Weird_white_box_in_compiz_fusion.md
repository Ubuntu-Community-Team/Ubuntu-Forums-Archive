### Post by reasoner on 2008-10-05
I had a similar problem with a white box but it didn't involve passwords. When I left my computer unattended for a long time it would try to suspend and a notification would pop up near the upper right corner of the screen saying that my computer failed to suspend. Actually I didn't know it was a notification because it was always just a pure white box with nothing to read. Only after I went to System/Preferences/Appearance/VisualEffects and switched from Normal desktop effects to None, was I then able to read the message box. The white box would cover everything else and make some applications unusable. The only way I was able to get rid of it previously was to restart gnome with Ctrl-Alt-Backspace and log in again. I'm running Ubuntu 8.04 64bit on an NVIDIA GeForce Go 7150M graphics card with the NVIDIA binary driver.
