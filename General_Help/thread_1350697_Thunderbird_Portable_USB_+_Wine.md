### Post by Yeti can't ski on 2009-12-09
My hint will probably be too obvious for most people, but I hope to be of assistance to other newbies like myself. 

If you have to work with different computers, some of them running Window$ and others (fortunately) running Ubuntu, and you would like to have access to your e-mail client, on and offline, in all of them the following might be helpful. 

I installed Thunderbird portable for Windows on a 8GB USB pendrive:

[http://portableapps.com/apps/internet/thunderbird_portable]("http://portableapps.com/apps/internet/thunderbird_portable")

After that I imported e-mails and addresses from an Outlook account (it may take a very long while) in the computer running Window$. 

Then, and this is the best part, I tried running it with Wine with Ubuntu 9.10 and (so far) it works seamlessly.

By setting up all necessary SMTP servers it is possible to have all data you need in your pocket and send/receive e-mails from almost any computer.
