### Post by Lars Noodén on 2010-01-15
@ metalf8801  : The protocol more than the tool is what to pay attention to.  Skype spreads the idea that there is a 1:1 correlation between unique protocols and unique applications.  

For voip, look for SIP and H.323.  Any SIP phone can call any other SIP phone regardless of individual package or operating system.  Same for H.323.  I can't answer which application will be best for you or which criteria you use to decide best, but here are some options to start with which support SIP:

[list]
[*][Ekiga](http://ekiga.org/)
[*][Qutecom](http://www.qutecom.org/)
[*][Kphone](http://sourceforge.net/projects/kphone/)
[*][Twinkle](http://www.xs4all.nl/~mfnboer/twinkle/)
[*][pjsua](http://www.pjsip.org/)
[*][Tapioca](http://sourceforge.net/projects/tapioca-voip/)
[*][Empathy](http://live.gnome.org/Empathy)
[/list]

Those are the ones that are on my list to look into myself, not an endorsement or recommendation.

Again, any SIP or H.323 phone can call any other SIP or H.323 phone regardless of individual package or operating system.  Think about real phones, it does not matter (outside the US) which brand of phone you are using to call and which brand the callee is using.
