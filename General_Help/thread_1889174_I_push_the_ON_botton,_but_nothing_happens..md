### Post by buntudawg on 2011-12-01
Damn!

Firstly, > Should the fan in the power supply come on
Not generally when you turn the switch on the back of the PSU on, but yes when you turn the computer on.

Is the PSU at least the same specs as the old one? That is, does it provide sufficient power output?

Make sure you check all the connections (to the drives etc but especially to the motherboard and the CPU fan) to ensure they are correctly seated. The main connection to the motherboard can be hard to get on sometimes.

> Have you try to double check the power cable if it is working?
It is possible the power switch itself is faulty but the initial failures you talked about don't sound like that.

Apart from that it is hard to tell and may need to be taken to a repair person. If it is getting old it could be things such as leaky capacitors, a heat transfer issue or any number of other things, but to me it still really sounds like a PSU issue.

All the best with this and let us know how you get on.
