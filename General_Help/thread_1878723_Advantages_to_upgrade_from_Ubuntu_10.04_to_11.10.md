### Post by Topsiho on 2011-11-10
Depends on what you want.

10.04 is an LTS-version, which means it is intended to be more stable than the intermediate and experimental non-LTS versions. So that is one question answered.

10.04(.3) still uses the Gnome2 desktop, which to my personal and humble opinion (IMHO and also: YMMV) is far superior to the new and still under construction Unity interface. If you like Unity, you may go for Unity. I may too some day, when Unity gets as good as Gnome2. So that's your second question answered.

My 2p :)

Topsiho

### Post by carranty on 2011-11-10
There is certainly no advantage stability wise, 10.04 is a long term release (LTS) that has had 18+ months to work out the kinks, its as stable as you're going to get.  

Whether it is advantageous GUI wise is a matter of opinion. If you like Unity, then yes, if you don't then no.  If you do decide to give it a go, I'd recommend trying it in virtualbox first to see if you like it.  

Personally, I'm waiting for 12.04, the next LTS (I too currently use 10.04).  I'll upgrade to that a month or two after its release, once any major bugs have been ironed out.
