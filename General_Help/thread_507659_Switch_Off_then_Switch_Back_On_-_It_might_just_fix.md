### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2007-07-23
Don't need help, just offering some advice based on last nights experience.

Have been reconfiguring layouts and drives on my server (4 x hard drives / LTSP / NFS / Apache / Gnump3d)

Changed all the names and mounts for my 4 NFS directories being served up to the LAN, and the music directory for GNUmp3d.

Soft reboot.....

GNUmp3d refuses to work and when it did, no search facility, and odd named volumes, and no icons on desktop of server. Grrr.

Spent a while with "e2label" changing volume names, checking nfs settings etc, soft rebooting for changes to take effect. Eventually gave up and shut down the server and switched it off, went for coffee, etc, and then came back, switched on, and hey presto all volumes labelled as they should be and GNUmp3d serving up nicely.

So sometimes it is worth switching the darn thing off, as a soft reboot may not be man enough to do the job :)
