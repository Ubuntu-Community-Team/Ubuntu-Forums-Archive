### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-12-24
Avoid 12.** and 15.** and download 16.04 LTS. Don't bother with installing one to upgrade to another. Can be a problematic waste of time (and installing an unsupported release is a waste of time anyhow). ;)

12.10 is ancient, hasn't been supported for years and you won't be able to upgrade to much from it. 15.** also EOL and unsupported.

If you want a supported release, do a clean install of 14.04 LTS (supported until 2019) or 16.04 LTS (2021). LTS = long-term support and five years support. Everything else is interim with nine months support.
