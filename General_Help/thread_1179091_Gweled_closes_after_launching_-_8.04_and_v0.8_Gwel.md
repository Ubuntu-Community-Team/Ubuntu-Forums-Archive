### Post by grungedoobie on 2009-06-19
Gweled is giving me the same problem.  I have yet to discover where the error is.

As for the audio, have you tried this:

1.  Right click on volume icon.

2.  Select preferences.

3.  Change audio device to "ALSA" or to the specific type of audio chip your computer uses.

That is the easy way to fix audio hick-ups.

If that doesn't fix it, then try going into system settings and matching the audio interface there.  System settings are more technical and more powerful, so you may want to "Google" your hardware to see what is working for other people.
