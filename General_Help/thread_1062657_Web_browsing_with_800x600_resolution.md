### Post by mb_webguy on 2009-02-07
You're using an 800x600 resolution monitor?  Wow.

Anyway, if you're using Firefox, you can zoom out by going to the View menu, or by pressing Ctrl+- (that's Ctrl and "-" together).  I don't know how well it will look on an 800x600 screen, but it will shrink the page down to size.  You can also zoom the text separately, so you can shrink the page but keep the text a readable size.  The NoSquint plugin maintains your zoom settings from page to page and session to session.  It was designed for users with very high-resolution monitors, but would work just as well for low resolution monitors.

### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2009-02-11
websites can be built in a way that allows them to automatically adjust to any screen width.

however, this becomes increasingly difficult the more complex a page is designed and structured. therefore, many websites are fixed in width at something around 950 pixels so they always look the same regardless of resolution. unfortunately, this is causing problems for people that use resolutions below 1024x768.

you can't really do much about this on your end short of switching to a larger resolution and update your hardware if it doesn't allow this.
