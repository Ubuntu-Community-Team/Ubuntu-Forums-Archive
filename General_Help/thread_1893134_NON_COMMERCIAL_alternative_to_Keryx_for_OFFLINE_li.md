### Post by Elludium_Q-36 on 2011-12-09
[Keryx]("http://keryxproject.org/") was a great, free project that allowed to add and update packages on an offline machine via a USB drive. :)

However, it seems that it has now gone fully commercial but the site's blog and download links are currently broken...  Jumping through the redirect hoops, to get to a download, you read: "Try us out free for 30 days." and get a 404 error. :(

It was great while it lasted and had a good run.  While I MAY have a 1.0 .deb but what's next? :confused:

Someone suggested trying Muon but Muon was not released 'til 10.10 Maverick. I still have 8.04 at home and am trying 10.04 from a USB boot on a public computer. They also suggested using Synaptic but in my installation of 8.04, I could never get Synaptic Package Manager to install manually downloaded packages.  One reason for sticking by the Long Term Support releases is that I am OFFLINE at home. ](*,)  

I plan to go to 12.04 around SEP 2012, so that any major bugs have been worked out... :-k

Keryx did work nicely in that from a USB drive, it downloaded any fixes/updates, new package lists, install choices, etc. for the "project" machine and installed them just like a native package manager, automagically.  No going through each package with gDebi, etc. and finding out dependencies were not satisfied. :(

I'm sure in this economy and in less fortunate areas of the world, there are many who have offline machines.  I hope that another solution can be developed that lives in the repositories, from a NON COMMERCIAL developer... :icon_frown:
