### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-07-06
The basis is the continuing development and release of kernels by the Linux developers. And kernel development is based on the fact that manufacturers are all the time bringing out new hardware.

[https://www.kernel.org/](https://www.kernel.org/)

Ubuntu has a team specifically tasked with keeping Ubuntu up to date with the latest improvements to the Linux kernel.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/KernelTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/KernelTeam)

It is by having new releases of Ubuntu every six months that Ubuntu keeps up to date with new hardware. Long Term Support (LTS) releases get 4 or 5 upgrades during the first 2 years. They are called "point" releases. The next 14.04 point release will be at the beginning of August when 14.04.3 will be released. It will contain the Linux kernels that are already in Ubuntu 15.04.
