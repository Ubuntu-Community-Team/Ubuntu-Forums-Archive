### Post by Herman on 2008-05-09
If they have physical access to your computer they can find out if they really want to.

Even if they don't have physical access to your machine, (if you keep your room locked), someone who has the right software and knows how to use it can see what packets are moving around in their LAN.

Internet packets contain a lot of information about where they came from and where they are trying to go to, something like the name and address on an envelope or on a real package which is sent in the mail.

Probably most people wouldn't have the time, or know how, or the curiosity to bother finding out where someone else has been on the internet.

As long as you are not doing anything that's against the laws in whatever country you live in, probably you have nothing to worry about.

If a person was doing something bad though, it is possible for a government to get information from an ISP about where you have been on the internet. 
People who live in an undemocratic country might need to be very careful about what they do on the internet if it's something that can get them in trouble with the government of their country.

Your ethernet card has an ID number in it like a license plate on a car and that is stamped on every internet packet your computer sends out, and so is the mac number of the router and ADSL modem. 
You can be tracked down by a government agency if you attract their attention. 

Try clicking on some of these links and see if they would lead to your front doorstep,
[What Is My IP Address? - Dedicated to IP address discussion]("http://www.whatismyipaddress.com/")

[What is my IP Address? Show my IP Address and IP Address tracer]("http://www.ip-adress.com/")

[IP Chicken - What is my IP? Find Your IP Address!]("http://www.ipchicken.com/")

[My IP Information]("http://ip-address.domaintools.com/")

[What can people tell from my IP address? - Ask Leo!]("http://ask-leo.com/what_can_people_tell_from_my_ip_address.html")

Then if those do show where you live, the mac address of your equipment will be able to prove which computer the signals are coming and going from.

Even if those links don't pinpoint your house on a map, your ISP would still most likely be able to bring that information up for a gov't deptartment if it was required. That depends on the privacy laws in your country, (whether they are forced to give out that information or not).
Probably most good law abiding citizens in free and democratic countries would never have anything to worry about.

Hey, there's a really neat free video you can download, it's only about a twelve minute film, about the internet and internet packets. It's quite entertaining and educational at the same time. Most people would enjoy it I think, [Warriors of the Net]("http://www.warriorsofthe.net/").

Also, don''t forget Hardy Heron comes with 'seahorse' passwords and encryption key manager as standard now. If you value your privacy that's the program for you. 'Applications'-->'Accessories'-->'Passwords and Encryption Keys'.
You can get Seahorse to generate PGP keys for you and RSA keys too. 
With PGP keys you can send and receive encrypted email, and from the right-click menu on your mouse you can encrypt/decrypt any or all files in your computer that you want to keep confidential. 
Your RSA keys are for helping to boost the security of the already encrypted and secure [SSH Networking]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p11.htm") which you can set up in Ubuntu. You can use SSH networking within your LAN or over the internet.
It's easy to use seahorse, but just to make sure I made an illustratd how-to for it here: [http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p5.htm#Install_Seahorse](http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p5.htm#Install_Seahorse)

I put a lot of time into searching and selecting the best links to include in that how-to, so be sure to read the links too, I think most people will really enjoy them.
Anyone interested in protecting/maintianing their privacy will find that Ubuntu Hardy Heron is the best operating system for that.
