### Post by TwilightDawn on 2008-01-11
You could look into an automatic defragmenter, which runs in the background and defrags as required during system idle time  (even when you are using the PC). Under XP and Vista, the presence of the defragger is barely noticeable- it gives priority to all other programs so not interference with normal computing. IMO, it's more efficient and convenient than scheduling defrags for every night or running it manually yourself. If the drive is fragmented, whenever the system has free idle resources,  the defragger will do the job for you without any intervention on your part.
