### Post by Newfoundlander on 2010-04-01
I would suggest installing the same version on her laptop that you have on your computer.  This way if she phones with a question, you can follow along on your computer and guide her.

If she only uses web, text editing, and mail then she can just ignore the other stuff.  I find that the Ubuntu desktop is quite intuitive and easy to learn.  Don't worry about trying to make it looks similar to Windows, that may just cause more confusion by trying to do things she did before that won't work in Linux.  When people switch to Mac, they don't try to make it look like Windows, they just accept that it is different.

My dad used Microsoft Windows for years and got fed up with the crashing & viruses and switched to Ubuntu a year ago.  After a short introduction and tutorial, he has been able to use it to do whatever he wants.

On the rare occasion that something went wrong or he didn't know how to do something, he would call.  But he had 8.04 installed and I had 9.04 and sometimes I would tell him to try a menu option that didn't exist in his version so following along was tricky.  The remote desktop feature helped a few times so I could fix things.
