### Post by jimmy the saint on 2008-08-02
You can use a thumb drive as swap, but it is not recommended.  What Vista doesn't tell you is that using your thumb drive to "boost" performance is a pretty bad idea. Flash drives can only be written to so many times before they are destroyed.  You would be better off just buying more ram.  Heck, now days you can buy 1Gig sticks for 10 bucks at newegg.  If you really want to do it though, here are a few resources that discuss it.

[http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-hardware-18/using-a-usb-thumb-drive-or-flash-drive-as-a-swap-partition.-453152/](http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-hardware-18/using-a-usb-thumb-drive-or-flash-drive-as-a-swap-partition.-453152/)
