It's not hard to install. There's a version in the repositories.

I find it very functional, but it's quite a *different* experience if you're used to GNOME or KDE. However, you will be able to use your familiar GNOME/KDE apps in Fluxbox if you want to.

No, it's very easy to install.  If you have never run it before, read the documentation carefully.  First time I ran it, I had no menu, no keys, nothing.  I had to CTRL-ALT-F1, and log in through the console.  After that, I could edit the keys file, and the menu file.  

It's a very nice desktop.  I like the speed and flexibility it gives me on old hardware.
