### Post by KCPokes on 2008-01-09
While Xubuntu is faster then the full-blown Ubuntu, it is not a night-and-day difference, IMO.  Your machine is severely lacking if you expect to see vast improvements.  I say that because I've been there.  I have the same specs, on an old Toshiba Tecra, and have been through the spectrum of different distros looking for one that would give me those noticeable improvements in speed.  I was expecting a lot, going from Ubuntu to Xubuntu, and while it was better it was still just too slow.  

My recommendations is to look at a more lightweight distro then Xubuntu.  I prefer Arch linux, which I'm running on my Tecra now, but you can also check out Puppy linux, Damn Small Linux, Mepis, Zenwalk, etc...  I've tried them all, which is part of the fun.  Some worked better then others; some just worked, while others didnt.  

Of course if you are set on staying with Xubuntu, the first thing is to ensure that you cut down on the "large" applications.  Find lightweight replacements for those full blown apps.  Going Xubuntu, on a slow machine, and then running Amarok is not going to gain you any improvements in speed.  

Good luck.
