### Post by DirtDawg on 2006-11-15
In my experience, Xubuntu is more difficult in that it doesn't always provide an obvious GUI for certain tasks. For example, the menu editor in Xubuntu is very limited so you sometimes have to [edit the desktop files]("http://xubuntu.wordpress.com/2006/07/12/manually-edit-the-xfce-menu/") in /usr/share/applications.

Another example is the lack of a 'search' option when browsing folders. To get a search option, you need to do some [prep work]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=214059&highlight=xubuntu+search").

Neither of these things are difficult, but they require some time to hammer out. I would definitely recommend Xubuntu for your older machines. It's not as snazzy as Ubuntu, but it's fast and you'll feel right at home being an Ubuntu regular.
