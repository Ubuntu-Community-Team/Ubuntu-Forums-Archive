### Post by unavenged on 2010-01-05
I just finished making my own USB Wii Sensor Bar and tested it on our Wii and on Ubuntu and it works

All you need is: 
4 IR LED's (scavenged mine from 4 old tv remotes)
An old USB cable
Some wire
Something to put it all in (I used a 1.5cm trunking offcut)

I cut my trunking about the same size as the Wii sensor bar that came with the console which is about 24cm, then i measured about 3cm in from both ends and marked the spot for the inside LED's then i went 1.5cm towards the edge and marked the spot for the outside LED's, I then made holes in it using a screwdriver (PS. I made the holes while playing Borderlands coop with friends while waiting for their PC's to load and i only had a screwdriver)

After that i striped my USB cable and cut off the green and white wires, the red(+) and black(-) wires are for power (+-5V)

The cathode(-) is on the side of the LED with a flat edge (on the little neck around its base) and the anode(+) on the round side

*The image i linked to doesn't work you can just follow the link at the bottom

Then i covered all the points that i soldered with insulation tape and put it all together

To check if all the LED's are working, just plug it into a open USB port hold infront of a USB camera or use your cellphone's camera
