### Post by coffeecat on 2008-08-22
> **Mb0742 said:**
> What exactly is it?

If you're talking about the 'Music' folder in your home folder, it's exactly that - a folder called Music. If you haven't put anything in it, it will be empty. It's just one of a number of empty folders that you find as a default in an Ubuntu installation, 'Documents' and 'Public' being two others. They're there for convenience. You can do what you like with them: delete, rename them, put anything you like in them.

> **Mb0742 said:**
> All my music is stored in a folder on my desktop what will happen if I delete it

Delete what? The empty Music folder in home or the music on your desktop?

> **Mb0742 said:**
> I would love back my extra 6.7gb.

If you delete an empty folder, you certainly won't get an extra 6.7GB. If your music files take up 6.7GB and you delete them, you'll get back the 6.7GB, but then you wouldn't have your music files. :wink: Perhaps you need to clarify what you mean.
