### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-10-04
> Hector23
I just found out that if you save a message with an attachment and you  then modify the attached file, the file gets sent as it was first saved.

I doubt many users would want an original e-mail modified. Should one  think about it, if an original was accidentally (or even deliberately)  altered, auto saving any part of the the modified e-mail could result in  a loss of info in the original e-mail. Potentially, rendering the  entire e-mail useless.

From a security standpoint editing/altering an original e-mail could  have significant impact. Consider it from a contractual or business  standpoint. Even personal data (medical records, etc.) that is sent via  e-mail, could loose all validity. Questions arising from why the e-mail  and/or it's attachment(s) were modified could impact the value of said  e-mail.

No changes should be permitted to an original e-mail that's been  received by a user. Unless, it's been saved as a different file. This  helps assure the validity of received data.
