### Post by maxim219 on 2010-06-04
The solution for this problem is not very elegant, but Google  implemented it: you'll need to send the messages using the email  provider's SMTP servers. 

The problem is, you should know the server url and if it's smtp capable, witch is by the way don't apply for universities emails systems, in my case, and last but not least, you also should know the proper port number and cross fingers till you find it included in the available list. :confused:

The other solution I found by coincidence, and that may be possible for people who, let's say,  send less than 10 emails a day, is to set every time the email adress you're planing to use as a default email in the gmail settings. I know... but I can't find an easier way better than this to keep my master gmail account private.

Is there someone out there who knows better ? please share your ideas so we can all benefit from them !

### Post by maxim219 on 2010-06-04
hi kerry_s,

Thank you for your response. you're right, Gmail has a feature that lets you send messeges from other addresses. "Gmail's custom 'From:' feature lets  you use Gmail to send messages with another of your email addresses  listed as the sender in place of your Gmail address. This way, it's  easier to manage multiple accounts from the Gmail interface."

 The inconvenience is that Google needs to include your primary email  address in the headers and some mail clients inform users that the  message is sent "on behalf of" a different address. Outlook is the  biggest offender, especially for business messages sent from a Gmail  address "on behalf of" a custom domain.
