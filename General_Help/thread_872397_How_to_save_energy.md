### Post by covingtonenergy on 2008-09-25
Easy. Get your power factor checked out. An appropriately sized bank of capacitors either at the panel box or closer to your computers and heat pump will cut your electricity demand through the meter, which is what you pay for (assuming your trying to reduce costs). It also means that the utility will be generating less electricity (if you're trying to be green).

How much money are you looking to spend? This has an impact on your options.

Firstly, do the servers contain any power hungry kit that they don't need? If they are servers I doubt they'll need the latest graphics card for example. Perhaps start by considering if the kit is built for the job, or if it is using loads of unneccessary power (are all the hard disks necessary, are the CPUs running at full whack and do they need to be?).

Secondly, what about power saving settings? What software settings are available to you in terms of power saving? Is it practical to stop the disk drives after a period of inactivity (unless you have huge RAM caches, I doubt that would be practical).

Finally, as others suggested, is there a way you can arrange the servers and the air circulation so that the heat is not wasted, but instead conveyed into working areas, thus reducing heating requirements in those areas while still cooling the server area.
