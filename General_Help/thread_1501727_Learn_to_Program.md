### Post by Neezer on 2010-06-04
Well, c++ is where I started...I am by no means an expert in any way shape, or form. It is pretty simple to set up the g++ compiler in ubuntu so that is good.

There are "Beginner Programming Challenges" here on the forums going all the time. They might be a bit above the absolute beginner level, but if you have resources with you it would be a great opportunity to try things out and get good feedback on your code. You can also compare to what other people are doing. If you do in fact try that, I'd make sure to not read other people's code until you have something working. Then compare to what other people have done and see if you can improve your own.

It can be a lot of fun and sometimes you want to pull your hair out! stick with it and you can become a decent programmer.

There is a certain amount of math involved, but you probably don't need to know vector calculus to do most of the things a programmer does....
