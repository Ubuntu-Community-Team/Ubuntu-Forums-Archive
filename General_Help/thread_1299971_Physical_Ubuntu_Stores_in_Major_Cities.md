### Post by mark.c.fernando on 2009-10-24
Hi am looking to purchase a new computer, and wanted to by one with Ubuntu pre-installed. Unfortunately, all of the shops provided by Linux are online, some seem shady and the shipping costs really don't help to make this option very attractive. Dell only has Ubuntu on select models.

System76 seemed to be the only reputable vendor but the shipping costs were $120 to Canada, making the unit much more expensive than a similar unit with Windows pre-installed. I work with many Linus users, and they all purchase windows machines then load Ubuntu due to the costs. I think Ubuntu has the potential to benefit here.

I think Ubuntu can today easily compete with Windows and Apple for desktop market share. I would think that there is enough demand in major cities to support one Ubuntu store to start. Can't Ubuntu pair with a laptop manufacturer and open up a shop to sell Ubuntu machines pre-installed? I would think that such a move may be profitable if stores are located in large population centers.

For example, the Toronto area has 6 million people, lets assume 80% use computers.  Then that 2% use Linux and 30% of that number use Ubuntu. That leaves us 31,680 users. If users replace their machines every 5 years that leaves 6,336 machines per year. This does not include  business use. Not sure if this would be profitable, but I think a pilot may be used to prove profitability. Even if it breaks even the initiative would serve to advance Ubuntu as a desktop OS.

I think having a store would have a number of  benefits:

[LIST]
[*]Marketing, many don't know what Ubuntu is and it would provide a place for users to check out the OS. When I mention Ubuntu to many and they hear Linux they automatically think terminal.
[*]Help to advance the OS against other Linux competitors, Windows and OS X.
[*]Improve brand recognition.
[*]Help to capture consumers who would not purchase a computer online.
[/LIST]

I don't want to give Microsoft more money but unfortunately now buying a Windows machine then loading Ubuntu seems to be the most cost effective option by a long shot.

What do you guys think?
