### Post by Linux BASHer on 2011-08-19
I'm planning on setting up a new Linux box expressly for distributed computing (BOINC, SETI@home, etc.). All things being equal, what's better- More clock cycles or more cores?

---

### Post by elgordodude on 2011-08-19
Both, in theory a 2.0 ghz hexcore is going to run close to the pace of a 3.0 ghz quad core. Depending of course on how you use your computer, if you overclock, etc. But the real answer if you're running SETI is a gpu, that's what really crunches.

---

### Post by Linux BASHer on 2011-08-20
So GPU over CPU power for SETI? NVidia only, or an AMD/ATI GPU work as well?

---

### Post by elgordodude on 2011-08-20
Definitely, gpus crush cpus in terms of results per hour and kilowatt. Nvidia and ATI both work, just has to be at least cuda capable.

Have a look at seti's crunchers board, you'll probably find most of your answers there.

[http://setiathome.berkeley.edu/forum_forum.php?id=10](http://setiathome.berkeley.edu/forum_forum.php?id=10)
