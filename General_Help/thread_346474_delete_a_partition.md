### Post by agustin.g on 2007-01-26
if i understood your post, you wish to delete a partition containing ubuntu so you can use the free space on your windows installation... am i correct? 

In that case, you can boot from the LiveCD, start GParted (the GNOME Partition Editor) and delete and reformat (in FAT32 or NTFS) the partition on which ubuntu was installed. GParted tells me you can "grow" both FAT32  and NTFS filesystems, so you can do that afterwards if you wish to have it all in one partition.

hope it helps
