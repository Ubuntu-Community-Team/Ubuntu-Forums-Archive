### Post by az on 2005-07-08
If you can avoid printing photos, please do.  It is a pain.  It is expensive.  It is slow.  Just when you have send twenty prints to the buffer, you get an inkjet clog up and you wasted twenty sheets plus ink.  The quality is never perfect.

Nuts!

I have been thrilled by the low-cost and easy service at the local pharmacy, or electronics store (or even walmart) to do photo finishing of digital prints.  I either upload them or copy them using a cd or memory stick.  It usually takes an hour (or two days online by mail) and costs a lot less.  The quality is top notch.

The web interface for Future Shop photo (in canada) runs on debian stable!

With all the money I have saved, I think I will go buy a cheap laser printer for my "regular" printing needs.
