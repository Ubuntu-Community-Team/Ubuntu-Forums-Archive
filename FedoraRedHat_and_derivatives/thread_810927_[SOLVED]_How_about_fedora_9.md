### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-05-28
> **bhadotia said:**
> So , is it ok to do so ? And which OS should be preferred (I also have a live cd of PC linux OS)?

Here's a tip. Wipe out your hard-drive and setup partitions kinda like this:
~7GB partition OS1
~7GB partition OS2
~1GB parttiion swap
~Extended partition for data

This way, you have all your data on a separate partition and you can just make links from it to your home folder. Then, you have one OS that you know you like and you know it works. Then you've got this second space where you can try out different ones. You can try F9, or Xubuntu, or SUSE, or Windows 7, or whatever. And if you don't like it you just boot back into your old one. If you do, then you start using it and the old system becomes the space where you start trying out new OSs. 

Those numbers aren't firm, but you get the idea. A sane partitioning scheme now will save you loads of time down the line.
