### Post by igknighted on 2007-08-16
Sidux, Gentoo and Arch are probably the three most up-to-date distros there are.  Sidux is sort of new-user friendly, but the others are not by any stretch.  Any of these distros are so bleeding edge that you need to be aware that with any update you risk breakage.

After that tier, your next best bets would be Fedora or Sabayon.  Fedora is only free software by default and will require you to install third party drivers and codecs, while Sabayon is based on Gentoo (and hence you must learn portage to keep up to date...).

Beyond this, PCLinuxOS and Mandriva and Ubuntu and Mint are all easy distros to use, and have fairly regular updates (every 6 months), but if a new app or kernel is released mid-cycle it probably wont end up in these distros.
