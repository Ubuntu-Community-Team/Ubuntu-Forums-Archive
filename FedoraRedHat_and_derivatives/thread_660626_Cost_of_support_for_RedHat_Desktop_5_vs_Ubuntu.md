### Post by colo on 2008-01-07
"If something happens" and it's the hardware that is letting you down, your shiny commercial support won't be able to do anything about it.

I'd just get a second, identical machine to the one you're developing on (you won't need much more than a 600US$ rig for web development anyway, unless you'd be using WIndows Vista, maybe ;)) and set it up the very same way as your primary machine, and verify both of them are in the same working condition. All your user data gets a daily backup to some external hard drive. If something shoots your first box in the foot, you just virtuallly seemlessly fall back to the second box, whilst the first is getting fixed.

Does that sound reasonable to you?

### Post by WinLinSolCoder on 2008-02-14
Commercial Support is worth it.

80$ is cheap, 200+ is very much expensive for a "Desktop" Linux Distro.

I had Red Hat Enterprise Linux subscriptions through version 3 and 4 and their support rocked even for someone with just a development machine.  Never felt hesitant to call them or send them an email.

They did make licensing more tedious by splitting everything up into components and add-ons for version 5, so I've been lookign around and I may get SuSE's distro soon (within next couple weeks).  It's cheap at 50$ a year and has everything included.

239$ for desktop support is too high, and especially from a company that seems to not really give much back to the community.  Will they be able to provide me with a fix when I find a bug in the compiler?  Or will they just tell me it wasn't in the base package and I'm on my own?
