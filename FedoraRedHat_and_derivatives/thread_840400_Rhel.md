### Post by mthei on 2008-06-25
Fedora is sponsered by Red Hat, but is not the same as RHEL, as what appears in one version of Fedora will be in future releases of RHEL. CentOS is a complete redesign of RHEL, but comepletely free.
So it's a tough call, as one is run by Red Hat and used as a "testing bed", while the other is based on the current version of RHEL.
If I were in your position, I'd go with CentOS, as it would be far more similar to the recent version of RHEL.
If you compare the packages on their respective DistroWatch pages, you'll find that [RHEL](http://distrowatch.com/table.php?distribution=redhat) has packages with are closer to [CentOS](http://distrowatch.com/table.php?distribution=centos) than they are to [Fedora](http://distrowatch.com/table.php?distribution=fedora).
