### Post by BrokeBody on 2007-08-06
Red Hat confirmed on Aug. 3 that it would be delaying the release of the newest member of its desktop Linux family, Red Hat Global Desktop, because the company is seeking to provide certain multimedia codecs. Sources close to Red Hat said obtaining some of these codecs was dependent on Red Hat coming to an agreement with Microsoft.

More about it [here](http://desktoplinux.com/news/NS4294334547.html).

If the computer industry really wants change it will not come from open source or from proprietary camps.  It will come from an outcry of users who have had enough and who refuse the spoon fed control of media content they own.

People need to stop being lazy and be responsible for what they do with their computers and companies need to need cease making it so freaking hard for them to do this.

Just for your info:  I have admin'd 8 different operating system families for over two decades.  I am not at all a  nooblet nor someone spewing my opinion.  These issues I deal with on a daily basis.

Bottom line people want their computers to work the way they think they should, even if they are totally wrong, they still think they should work that way.
