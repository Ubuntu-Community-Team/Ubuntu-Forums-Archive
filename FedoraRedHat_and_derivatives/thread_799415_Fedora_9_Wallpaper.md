### Post by msrinath80 on 2008-05-19
You can say that I am a convert from Ubuntu. That would be an fairly accurate description. I prefer Fedora over Ubuntu primarily for two reasons:

1. Out of the box hardware support is much much better than what Gutsy or Hardy have to offer.

2. Fonts look lovely on Fedora as it uses autohinting. 

People like to spread the FUD that Fedora is too bleeding edge and therefore not a stable distribution. While I agree with the bleeding edge part, I don't always agree with the stability argument. I have been using F9 since it was released and I find it very stable and polished. My suggestion to anyone who wants to know more about Fedora is to install and use it (NOT on a virtual machine!).

### Post by wulfhound on 2008-07-16
I know this is kind of an old thread, but I used to use Ubuntu a lot. Now I use Fedora.

I changed because I had a lot of issues while compiling programs. While Ubuntu has lots of patches to get lots of things to work, if no one else is interested in patching to get such and such a program to work, then you are out of luck if you don't know how to get it to work.

The flip side, many things are already patched and ready to work. Since Ubuntu is popular with new users, almost half of your issues are readily solved by searching in a forum.

When you take the leap into Fedora, you won't have that. Yes, there are good forums such as linuxquestions and fedora forums, but sometimes you have to dig deeper.

Then again, I said about "half" will be easily solved on Ubuntu. I see many unanswered threads - but that could also be the sheer number of people asking questions, and not enough people answering. Often they are questions about compiling this and that.

I think Ubuntu is more stable when you just stick with what you use and don't bother compiling a lot of things. This is just MY experience. When I switched to Fedora, I had no issues compiling many things. In fact, I've never had an issue. 

I agree with the poster above. If you are happy with Ubuntu - by all means, stick with it. Being happy with a distro is a hard place to come by for some people. :D
