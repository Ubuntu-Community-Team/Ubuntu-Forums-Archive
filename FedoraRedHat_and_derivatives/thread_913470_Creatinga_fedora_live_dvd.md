### Post by Therion on 2008-09-07
You need to burn the CD as an "image" (an .iso) not a data CD.  Use Brasero and click on the "Burn Image" button from the main menu.  I suggest burning it very, very slowly.  Say, 8X or even 4X.

### Post by whitefang5412 on 2008-09-10
The quickest way to go about this:
 
Download the ISO image, and it appears you already have. (Make sure to save it to your desktop.)
 
After it has downloaded, and the ISO image is on your desktop, it will have a picture of a CD image, or something along those lines. Take your blank DVD-R and put it in your computer.
 
After a disk has popped up on the desktop, and your computer has recognized the disk, right click on the fedora ISO image and click write to disk. Write it as SLOW as the burner will possibly go.
