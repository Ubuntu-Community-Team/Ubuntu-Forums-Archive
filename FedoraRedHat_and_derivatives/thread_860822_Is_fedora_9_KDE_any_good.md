### Post by ryaxnb on 2008-07-17
The biggest issue is that Fedora 9 uses KDE 4 by default. You'd have to manual compile KDE 3.5.9 (ouch!) or just suffer with  Fedora 9's fairly bad KDE 4.0 implementation. So basically your choices are:
* Use GNOME
* Stay with another distro, such as Ubuntu
* Use Fedora 9 and live with KDE 4.0 and the bad implementation that comes with Fedora 9.
* Use Fedora 8 until Fedora 10 comes out with KDE 4.1/4.2.
