### Post by ErkDemon on 2011-08-24
Ubuntu has some very nice tablet components, but a few of them could do with a little final tweaking. 

**Xournal/Jarnal.** These apps do most of the same main things as **MS Journal**, but MSJ is slicker, partly because it does handwriting recognition (difficult to emulate), but also because its designers added a simple dropdown box with recent files on its toolbar. It's a trivial thing, but it it lets you flip between notes much faster than going to the file menu, selecting "recent files", and then selecting something from a cascading list. It makes it much easier to navigate "ink" notes quickly.

Similarly, with **CellWriter**. It's an unreasonably good program, until you come to try to load and save your settings, and find thast there's no obvious way to do it. If you have Ubuntu on your laptop, desktop, netbook and tablet, you presumably have to train the program four times, and if you've trained it for, say, handwriting recognition, you can't obviously experiment with a Palm-graffiti method without losing your earlier settings. People can't exchange "trainer" files. Again, it's a trivial gripe compared to all the clever work that's obviously gone into building the rest of the program, but it's still a stumbling-block. 

The **onscreen keyboard** is okay. But to make it really impressive on a tablet device that's competing with the iPad, it'd be cool to have the option of alternative bitmaps for the key backgrounds. 

And here's a cool one: a tablet screen can be used in four orientations, right? And Ubuntu has four switchable desktop screens … so why not include the option of connecting them together? Put the tablet on its dock in landscape mode it switches to a calendar-appointments-clock-new-emails display. Pick it up and hold it in portrait mode and it transforms into a clipboard. And then you still have another two orientations to play with. 
Gimmicky, yes. Would a lot of people hate the feature and want to turn it off?  … yes. But it'd be something that people could show their friends, wich the iPad can't do. 

There's probably other simple things that I haven't thought of, so does anybody else want to chip in with ideas, or suggestions, or reactions?

Eric
