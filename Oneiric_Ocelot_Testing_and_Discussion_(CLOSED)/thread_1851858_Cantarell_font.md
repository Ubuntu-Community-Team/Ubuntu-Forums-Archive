### Post by jbicha on 2011-09-29
The GNOME 3 default theme, Cantarell, has just been pushed into the Ubuntu archives (it may take a few hours for it to show up in your software manager).

A bit surprising is that upon installation, GNOME Shell immediately uses Cantarell as the UI font. (This won't happen automatically in Unity though.) Personally, I prefer the Ubuntu font, especially for the Applications section of the Activities overview which is difficult to read with Cantarell, but more free fonts is good news.

[https://launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/fonts-cantarell](https://launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/fonts-cantarell)
