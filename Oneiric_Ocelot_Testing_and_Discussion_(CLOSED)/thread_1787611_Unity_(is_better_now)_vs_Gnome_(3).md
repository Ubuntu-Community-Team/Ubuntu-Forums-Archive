sartic, Ubuntu is also concerned about users with slow Internet connections. While downloading multiple gigabytes is not a big deal for some users; for others it's a very frustrating experience.

700 MB is enough to include a good selection of apps by default. There is obviously not room for more than 1 app in any particular category (Thunderbird & Evolution cannot both be on the CD). Even if space were not an issue, picking a single app per category to be the default recommended app is good design.

As Ubuntu is used by millions all over the world, I think they do a pretty good job picking what to showcase on the CD.
