### Post by seeker5528 on 2011-05-02
> **macroshaft said:**
> I quite like the idea in theory but looking at the scrollbar that pops up it still has and up and down arrow on it. Can't those be made to function as they did on the old style bar? Best of both worlds.

I think having the thumb split into four to allow for both page and scroll would make the hot zones too small and paging is more useful than scrolling, to me. I could see having a key combination that would allow switching the function between paging and scrolling.

For the sight impaired I would probably suggest flashing the thumb if the mouse cursor crosses over the edge of the window pane where the scrollbar resides but outside of the area the would cause the thumb to pop up long enough to click or drag it.

If length/width can be used to determine the size of the indicator area within the scroll area, it seems like it should be able to be used to determine if a small drag should result in scrolling at a sane/manageable pace within a large document while still allowing a longer drag to move to a relative position, or always move to a relative position for shorter documents.
