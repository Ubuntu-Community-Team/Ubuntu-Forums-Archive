### Post by effenberg0x0 on 2011-10-12
Despite of current design / usability specs and decisions (and wars), we know some aspects of Unity interface will eventually have to change and mature to fit relevant user demands.

One of the things users mention frequently is the lack of "drawers" in the launcher, for an example. 

Another is the possibility to customize the dash main shortcuts and create customized sections in it (not only "Most Frequently Used", "All installed Apps", but "My Develop Tools", "Work Stuff", etc.).

Real world situation / user demand for change: I am seeing problems on making the case of Oneiric with Unity in the business / government environment. These clients don't want "Listen to Music", "View Photos", etc in the Dash shortcuts. They want stuff like "Launch ERP", "Search Invoices", etc. 

Do you see Unity going this way, becoming more customizable, considering PP or future releases? 
Or is Unity not developed to be used in Biz/Gov PCs at all?
