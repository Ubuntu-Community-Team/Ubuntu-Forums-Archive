### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2011-09-26
> **cybergalvez said:**
> The control center in 11.10 is not as useful as in 11.04. In 11.04 almost anything could be done via the control center, in the new one, there is very little functionality

Gnome 3 is moving in a direction where something like your default theme and font is a little more strict; it's something that people who like tweaking things can change (happily), but that isn't something they want to push. This allows app developers a little more room to make things look right with the defaults, because they have something of a guarantee that the defaults are the &#8220;proper&#8221; appearance. It isn't that apps will start being badly made and ignore good practice for choosing colours and fonts, but it does make things a little easier to support.

In this way, some of the additional settings are living in [GNOME Tweak Tool]("http://live.gnome.org/GnomeTweakTool"), which is developed as a part of the GNOME project. You can change your theme, font size, etc. in there.

With regards to options, I kind of disagree with you. Some panels have been reduced, and we obviously don't have the theme / font selection we had before, but there is lots of new stuff as well. A lot of this is possible *because* the goals have been adjusted, and some designers have gone through and thought about what these panels should achieve in a very unified way.

For example, the Network panel has a clearly labelled button to use a particular Wifi card as a wireless hotspot. There's the (granted, quite young) Online Accounts thing, Screen Preferences lets you set the lock timeout vs. when the screen turns off, there's a brand new panel for Wacom tablets, there's a brand new panel for colour management, and the Removable Media panel isn't hidden in Nautilus. I think this thing has a lot of potential :)
