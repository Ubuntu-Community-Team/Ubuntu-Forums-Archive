### Post by ErkDemon on 2011-08-27
It seems to me to be a little bit wierd that Ubuntu 11.04 has switched to a global menu, apparently because "if its good enough for Apple, it must be okay" ... when actually, (1) the GM was the old default WIMP beahaviour before Windows and co moved away to program-integrated menus because it was felt that there was more visual integrity to a program if all its controls were in the same window rectangle, and (2) the global menu is arguably one of the most problematic parts of the OSX interface, and I wouldn't be too surprised if they came up with a way of making it optional in the next release or two, if they could find a way to do it without losing face (like the way they eventually implemented an effectively multi-button mouse without making it /look/ like a multibutton mouse.

OSX has a particular problem with the global menu because a disproportionately high number of its users have huge monitors and multi-monitor setups, so the menu bar can be quite a way away from a program pane.

Anyhow, I was pondering about how Apple could get themselves out of this, and I thought: why not just move the menu text one or two character-widths to the right, and add a little tiny unobtrusive handle at the left-side of the menu bar, and if you click and hold the handle, it turns into a rectangle outline that can be dragged to the top of the screen to activate global menu mode, and if you click on the global menu's handle and drag it to a menu-enabled program's window, it jumps back out of "global menu" mode and back to "programs have their own menus" Everybody happy, no loss of face, everybody gets what they prefer, and the desktop get a new "feature" in the shape of the little handle, which makes it look like sophisticated feature rather than an ugly switch needing to be retrofitted to a settings menu somewhere.

And then I thought ... Why didn't Ubuntu 11.04 do this? Think about how much betatester time and energy got wasted arguing about a dratted global menu that Apple themselves would probably secretly prefer not to have. And now we're wasting even more time trying to think up workarounds for users of large monitors, who didn't have a problem with the previous system. 


Just let people drag the menu to wherever they want it. Heck, if they have a multi-monitor system, let them choose with monitor they want to drag it to, too. That way, there's two user-interface problems solved in one go.
