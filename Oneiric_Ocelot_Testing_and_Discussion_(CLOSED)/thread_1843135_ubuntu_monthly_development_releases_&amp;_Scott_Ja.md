### Post by julianb on 2011-09-12
[http://netsplit.com/2011/09/08/new-ubuntu-release-process/]("http://netsplit.com/2011/09/08/new-ubuntu-release-process/")

Scott James Remnant wants us to have monthly releases, rather than a six month development cycle. 

Part of what appeals to me about that is I have been using Ubuntu like it's a "rolling release" type operating system for about a year. As soon as my Ubuntu development release became a stable release, I found myself wanting to switch to the next development release. For my purposes, this gave me an acceptable level of stability -- the alphas and pre-alphas had bugs but nothing I couldn't put up with! 

It strikes me that for enterprise-desktop administrators (or for OEMs like Dell) the nonLTS releases are far from ideal. These kinds of organizations could be really important to Ubuntu's future, and to meet their wants we might want to focus more energy on our LTS releases (?). For people who don't want Long Term Support releases' predictability and lack of new features, a monthly release might be just the thing. A release that acknowledges that Ubuntu, beginner-friendly though it is, also appeals to people like me who want new features and don't mind reinstalling software that breaks on occasion. If you're using Oneiric or even Natty-with-Unity, you are probably someone who likes being on the cutting edge. Someone who is interested in helping imperfect software succeed because it offers the promise of software freedom and great features in the future.

Ubuntu is starting to look like a "more polished version of debian and a LESS polished version of Mint". Do we want the developers to shake things up and go into this uncharted territory?
