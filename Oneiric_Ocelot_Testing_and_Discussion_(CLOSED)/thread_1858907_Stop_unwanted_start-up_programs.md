### Post by zeroseven0183 on 2011-10-13
You can deselect some applications that you don't want loaded during startup in Startup Applications. To access this, press the Super key (Windows key, that is) and type Startup Applications.

Make sure you uncheck those that you really don't need.

What is the hardware specs of your computer if you don't mind sharing?

### Post by jerrylamos on 2011-10-13
Biggest thing to remove is "Compiz" that does a whole bunch of "window dressing" without affecting function at all.

You remove "Compiz" by choosing "Unity-2D" at logon.

I preferentially use "Unity-2D" even on the pc's that will run "Unity-3D" because I don't like fuzzy see thru images and way out of focus windows.

Compiz even chews up pc cycles and memory running in the background when I've got full screen applications up, and anything Compiz is doing is useless and not even visible.
