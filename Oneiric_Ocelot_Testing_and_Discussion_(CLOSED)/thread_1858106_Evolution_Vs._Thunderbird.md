### Post by Bluesan on 2011-10-11
You see, there's a big box of Lego bricks, and the developers of any distribution will pick out the bricks they want to use to build their version of Linux. And, they have to use different bricks, or all of the distributions would be the same.

So, if you don't happen to like the brick they used (Thunderbird), replace it with a brick you like (Evolution). It's in the repositories. Install it, and move on, it's as simple as that. It's certainly nothing to lose sleep over.

Personally, I like Thunderbird, and have installed it in every version of Ubuntu since Dapper. If Evolution had still been the default, I would have installed Thunderbird again. And, I don't use Lightening. I prefer Google Calendar. (You see, there's a big box of Lego bricks...)

Edit:

Oh, and for those very few that don't know, Firefox and Thunderbird are modular by design. There's not a lot of anything "built in". They rely on extensions to provide customization, and to add additional features. I certainly would expect that long time computer users would have known this mucho moons ago. Mozilla's philosophy is nothing new.
