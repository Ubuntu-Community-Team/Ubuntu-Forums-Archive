### Post by seeker5528 on 2011-07-14
> **jbicha said:**
> and it's rare for software to be removed from the repos unless it no longer works or the bugs are too severe and aren't being fixed. Nearly all of that software is maintained by Debian developers.

The number of packages that get removed at any given time is small, but it's not that rare, it's just that usually the ones that get removed were not used much to begin with, not used because people have been transitioned to some other replacement, not used because people willingly or because of conflicts chose to go to something else.

Sometimes the maintainer of a package will ask for it to be removed, sometimes a package will have been orphaned for some time and only getting minimal amounts of QA work by the time it's removed.

The ones you hear about are rare because more often than not if there are that many people using something somebody is willing to maintain it in the archive until it gets so outdated and buggy that it becomes more work than it is worth to try to keep it working with the newer stuff.
