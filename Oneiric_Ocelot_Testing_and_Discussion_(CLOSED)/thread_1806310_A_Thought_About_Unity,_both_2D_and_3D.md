### Post by DeadSuperHero on 2011-07-17
So, I decided to bite the bullet and update to Oneiric the other day, to see how the performance improvements were on my machine. After waiting through a long updating process (ah, memories!), I finally booted in to a wonderfully fast, visually slick environment. I could tell some bits were rough around the edges, but everything was mindblowingly fast, and the Unity dock has a visual polish to it that I hadn't seen before.

Then I realized I was running Unity2D, much to my shock. After enabling my Radeon driver and entering into the default Unity3D environment, I was brought back into regular old Unity. After adjusting the icon size on the bar (from 50px to 48px), the performance picked up to be roughly about that of Unity2D as far as speed is concerned, but I really want to bring up the point of all this:

Unity2D is wonderfully polished. The dock has a few visual features (specifically, the animations it uses, and the graceful speed it uses when sliding up or down, or how everything hides itself under the trash can icon rather than folding up like a Rolodex) that I would really love to see in Unity3D.

I realize that Unity3D probably requires a little bit more processing power, but wouldn't it be great to at least converge the two projects from a visual and functional standpoint? Even the Unity2D Dash is a bit more functional for me.

I guess I'm just curious as to what the plans are for this. I'd love to see the performance and visual enhancements carried over from Unity2D to the default Unity environment, because I honestly think it's a huge improvement.

Edit: Maybe I should upload a few screenshots to demonstrate specifically what I'm talking about.

First, the Unity2D dock has a level of elegance that I feel surpasses the actual Unity dock itself. The drop-shadow/hidden-compartment-that-icons-slide-out-of is visually stunning, and actually pretty functional.
