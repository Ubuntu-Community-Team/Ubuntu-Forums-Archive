### Post by gunksta on 2011-09-21
My beef with Unity was/is its incredibly poor support for multi-monitor setups. I connect my laptop to an external monitor when I am at work and when I am at home and use it as a two screen setup.

Switching applications in Unity using Alt + Tab works as expected. Using the mouse does not. For example (this is but one example of how this drives me crazy) visualize two monitors, each running a maximized application. Monitor 1 is running Firefox and Monitor 2 is running Thunderbird. I am using Firefox and all is well. I then decide I want to access a menu option in Thunderbird. But, in order to access the menu, I must first shift focus to Thunderbird and that can only be doe by clicking, needlessly, somewhere on the Thunderbird chrome. Clicking on the title bar above Thunderbird, where the menu is hiding does not change the focus. True, it is only one click but I bounce back and forth between my two screens all day and I want consistent behavior.

I put up with it during 11.04 and tried filing another bug in 11.10 but they both got ignored. I also tried the infamous Gnome Shell. It is pretty, perhaps prettier than Unity, but it is even less useful. On a 12.5" laptop, it is important for the UI to get out of the way. Unity does an excellent job of this. Gnome Shell + Adwaita take up way too much space. I also thought the difference between the shell menus and the application menus was weird. But those things can be dealt with and improved upon fairly easily. What really drove me away is the way it handles multi-monitor setups. The primary monitor changes desktops but the  secondary does not and by default, the laptop screen remains the  primary. Thus, I would plug in my 21" monitor to the laptop and have to use it as a secondary monitor rather than the primary which is obviously what I want to do. 

A little over a week ago I moved my system to Kubuntu. I was already using several KDE applications. I looked at the collection of apps that I use and realized that none of my favorite apps depends on Gnome. I still use Firefox, FileZilla, Vim and several other world-class GTK based apps, but I just don't actually need Gnome.
