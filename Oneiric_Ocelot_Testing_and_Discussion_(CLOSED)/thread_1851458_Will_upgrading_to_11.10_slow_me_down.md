### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-09-28
> **cariboo907 said:**
> Lubuntu is now an official member of the Ubuntu family, so you have access to everything available in the Ubuntu repositories.
Even though Lubuntu 11.04 was not an official ubuntu family member it still has full access to all the repositories for ubuntu, including all the available ppa repositories.  I use it on an old laptop, where it is much faster and better than Ubuntu, which even with 512MB ram did crawl a bit.

I can therefore vouch, for the 11.04 version being excellent, but haven't looked at 11.10 yet, so even though you will need to learn how to configure a few things manually with edits of text configuration files, I would definitely recommend Lubuntu on that machine.  There is also a lot of development at the moment of the LXDE desktop, and the change between the 10.04 and 11.04 Lubuntu versions was pretty large, and certainly worthwhile.  11.10 will probably be another big step forward.

I can not help with a comparison of speeds of Lubuntu vs Xubuntu , but I know some users swear that Xubuntu is just as fast as Lubuntu, so you may need to try both.
