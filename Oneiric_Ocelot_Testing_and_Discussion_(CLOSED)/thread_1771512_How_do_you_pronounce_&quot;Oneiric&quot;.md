### Post by Squonk07 on 2011-05-30
MacUntu has it right. English orthography may be enigmatic, but IPA never lies (unless it's transcribed improperly). In rough phonetic translation, it comes out to oh-NIGH-rick (or oh-NY-ric, as ratcheer has it above). Some IPA transcriptions seem to have it /&#333;-&#712;n&#299;-rik/ (the "I" sounds as in "bid"), though, which only adds to the confusion. This version is pretty close to what Mark Shuttleworth says.

I'm sticking with the former, in any case.
