### Post by VinDSL on 2011-09-24
Okay, I see what's happening now...  edge resistance is janky.

Normally, I run unmaximized windows, e.g. not in full-screen mode.

When I move an entire unmaximized window, it meets edge resistance, like normal. But...

If I resize an unmaximized window by dragging the top or bottom frames individually, it doesn't meet proper edge resistance.

Even though everything looks okay, upon closer inspection, I am actually dragging the top and bottom frame into another desktop - thus, causing a white line on the top left of the display.

If I make the top and bottom frames slightly smaller, then they don't extend into adjacent desktops, and the white line goes away.

Does that make any sense?!?!?  :D

Anyway, it's totally obvious, when you look at these windows in the Workplace Switcher. You can see white lines on both the bottom and top of the various desktops.

The workaround (until they patch it) is to make the top and bottom frames slightly shorter.  ;)
