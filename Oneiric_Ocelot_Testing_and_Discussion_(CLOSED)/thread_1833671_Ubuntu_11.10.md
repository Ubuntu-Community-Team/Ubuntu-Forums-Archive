### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-08-26
Have a read through this link and notice the direction that Ubuntu is going in.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Unity/Lenses/Ideas]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Unity/Lenses/Ideas")

See these comments:

> The lenses appear to be logically moving away from the launcher (dock) and into the Dash in the 11.10 cycle. A few considerations for this integration follow below.

> This is particularly important in any scenario where the Dash becomes full of enough features to eliminate the dock / springboard / launchers launcher (a logical outcome), especially for as long as the Big Funny Button is not restorable to the top Unity panel.

It seems that the launcher will not last very long or be less obvious.

You can contribute to 11.10 and onwards by downloading and installing it on another partition and then test and put forward ideas for improvement. But, 11.10 is now at feature freeze. So, may I suggest that you think 12.04 and 12.10 and also think Lenses and what they can be used for. Learn to code Lenses and what are called Scopes.

You will not be able to turn Ubuntu away from the course that has been set for it.

Check out this blog from Mark Shuttleworth. We are seeing in 11.04 ideas that have been worked on for a while. And will continue to be developed up to and beyond 12.04

[http://www.markshuttleworth.com/archives/date/2011/08]("http://www.markshuttleworth.com/archives/date/2011/08")

Regards.

P.S. in 11.10 the Big Funny Button is now at the top of the Launcher. When you maximize an application the Max, Min and close buttons of the application go to the very left of the panel. This was the reason these buttons were moved to the left. That shows just how long these ideas have been in development.
