### Post by cank1090 on 2007-10-27
> Microsoft has a majority market share in the new desktop PC marketplace. This is a bug, which Ubuntu is designed to fix.
*[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/1](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/1)*

I totally agree with Marc Shuttleworth at this point. But: How is Ubuntu going to achieve that? To find an answer for this question, one has think like a non-Linux user (which many of us were before we changed our OS). Why should someone change from Windows Vista or Mac OS X to Ubuntu? What does it make attractive?
I'm sure many of you know what. It's free, it's open-source, it has some nice applications, it's stable, it's secure, it's easy. But one thing never appears in this list. It doesn't have a nice "Look and Feel". I know that beauty is a thing of individual flavor and that there are a lot of people who like Ubuntu's Human theme as it now is with its brown color scheme. But did you ever hear someone saying "Wow, Ubuntu's look is so nice/great/amazing/beautiful/stunning!"? I didn't. I heard a lot of people saying this about Vista's and OS X's look (especially in the media!), but if someone says something positive about Ubuntu's look at all, it's mostly a consolatory comment like "Hmm well, that's quite nice...". Just a few really get attracted by Ubuntu's look.

Now, why is this point so important at all? I mean of course, one could just check gnome-look.org, get himself a new theme and install it. That's not even hard. But I think, in the sense of competition, Ubuntu has to come with a default theme comparable and able to compete with the ones of the other two major Desktop OS. The "Look and Feel" is one major component of a Desktop OS. I don't want Ubuntu (Linux) to copy any design from any OS, that's not why I'm asking for a design improvement. Ubuntu (Linux) has to define itself, like it ever did. But what do you think would be the result of a public survey like "Here you have screenshots of Vista, OS X and Ubuntu. Choose the one you like most"? I think Ubuntu would lose this survey.

That's why I think: Improve the Human theme! Make it more attractive for new users. Ubuntu must not abandon it's brown color scheme, but the icon theme for example looks like a comic theme. The metacity theme could be from 1998. Actually, Ubuntu's unmodern design doesn't fit to the modern desktop effects by Compiz Fusion. That's almost like a contradiction.

I know that regarding design there are countless opinions and ideas. And I don't want to state concrete ideas for an improved design here (particulary because I'm not a designer). That's the job of the many great designers in the open source world. But please, do something at all! Improve Ubuntu's design, make it look more modern and elegant, more attractive, more glassy/shiny/milky/glossy or whatsoever.

### Post by 23meg on 2007-10-28
People who are able to change their clothes also judge others, or at least get a certain impression of them, by their "default looks", which is to say, the kind of clothing they typically choose to wear to work, school, etc.

Same goes for OS looks: people who do know how to change their wallpaper do get a certain impression of an OS from the default theme that it **chooses** to ship with. Looks don't necessarily reflect anything about the underlying technicalities of an OS, but the kind of look **that has been chosen** as the default look of an OS says something, no matter how abstract and subtle, about the background that OS is coming from, just like the packaging design of a tangible product says something about the kind of world view, principles, economy etc. that produced it, and the kind of market it's intended for.

Looks do talk. Whether they can be changed or not, and whether their seers are aware of the fact that they can change them or not, does not change the fact that they convey information.
