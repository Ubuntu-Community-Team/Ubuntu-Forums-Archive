### Post by gnomeuser on 2008-05-16
> **Toadinator said:**
> basically they're saying that the developers are getting weird ethnically and only put features in that they want to put in, not users.

Though it is rare that the developer doesn't knows best, most often what the user asks for is not what he wants. A request to make something an option indicates that said function does not work. The correct solution is said case would be to fix the functionality not enabling people to turn it on or off.

How does listening to your users literally make your program worse?

1. More code to maintain
2. Less time to fix bugs 

The latter naturally leads to more option being requested, thus enstating a vicious cycle that can only lead to a buggier program, burned out developers and angry users. You also have code complexity which increases thus making it harder for new people to enter the project and it makes it an order of magnitude harder to debug. 

Having a strong development team with a vision for the project often helps to direct the focus on solving problems and bring about clever functionality by innovation. In the end leading to a better project.

At any rate calling it selfish for them to decide what to use their time and skills on rather that take orders from someone else is a somewhat shocking reversal of logic.

An example of such developer wisdom:
[http://mystilleef.blogspot.com/2005/12/just-add-option-to-turn-it-off-or-on.html](http://mystilleef.blogspot.com/2005/12/just-add-option-to-turn-it-off-or-on.html)
