### Post by Timon&amp;Pumba on 2008-07-09
Since now fingerprint support is added, I like to use it as my main device for authentication. It works great, both in terminal and to login to gnome.

Although it works to login to gnome, the user does not get any visual feedback. It is like this:
You enter your username, and you would expect the password prompt to show up.
Instead the username entry is disabled, and you have to provide a fingerprint scan. If it succeeds, you are logged in, else you have to provide your password.

It works, but you have to know when you have to provide your scan. It would however be nice if gdmgreeter could detect other forms of authentication other than a password, and would adapt its interface
