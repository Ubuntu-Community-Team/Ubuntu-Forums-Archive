### Post by ygolohcysp on 2008-03-18
Well, since you want wild ideas, I was thinking about what I'd like out of my computer with regards to technology that's available, or almost available now.  For one, there seems to be this uprising of cases with so many fans that they are incredibly loud.  Because of this, I've been thinking about building my computer into a wooden box (foil lined on the inside so grounds on motherboards and such still do the job) that I could mount to the bottom side of my coffee table.  Throw in a few of the larger low speed 120mm fans and create a nice quiet wind tunnel.  Aside from that, I wanna have an internal sata DVD or Blue Ray for data transfer rates when I really need it, but aside from that, I want everything to be wireless.  An external DVD drive, monitor.  I already have wireless keyboard and mouse.  I'm not sure bluetooth is fast enough for that yet, but there are some formats that are.  I'm not just talking about the data cables for wireless though.  AC or DC power as well.  New tech is becoming available to allow for wireless charging or powering of cell phones and laptops.  Why not have a large flat panel with a built in transformer on my coffee table with absolutely no wires, along with the DVD drive, with the wireless "charging" station mounted underneath?  Hell, if I owned my home, I'd consider just using a plug on the bottom of one leg going into a socket flush mounted in the floor.  Absolutely no wires anywhere then.

That, at least, is what I think my living room computer should be.  If only I had more time and money to put into making it possible.  And an electrical engineering degree. ;-)

If Linux could offer hardware like this first in "prebuilt and bundled packages", along with compiz, Vista would just be a bad memory in no time.
