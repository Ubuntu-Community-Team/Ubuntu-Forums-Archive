### Post by ankursethi on 2008-01-04
Read :
[http://www.whytheluckystiff.net/articles/theLittleCodersPredicament.html](http://www.whytheluckystiff.net/articles/theLittleCodersPredicament.html)
[http://www.salon.com/tech/feature/2006/09/14/basic/index.html](http://www.salon.com/tech/feature/2006/09/14/basic/index.html)

These two are about the problems kids are facing. I'm 17, and had a tough time doing anything with my computer until I found Python. In fact, back in 2004 I had installed Ubuntu and was just browsing through the documentation and found something called "Dive Into Python". Since I had done some Visual Basic (don't say VB is horrible, to a 13 year old kid it was paradise) and JavaScript (which was and still is horrible) I had little trouble understanding the first few chapters. Then I found out Guido's tutorial at Python.org and got hooked.

Anyway, we have here Ubuntu that :
1. Ships with Python, Ruby and Perl.
2. Ships with "Dive Into Python".
3. Is the most damn popular distribution around.

That means Ubuntu is very much capable of solving the problems outlined in the above two articles.

Why not a "Welcome to Ubuntu" screen which, among other things, has a link to "Dive Into Python"? Of, if DIP is too complex, we can throw in irb and [this]("http://hacketyhack.net") thing for kids and Why's Poignant Guide to Ruby for the rest of us. We have a lot of crap on the Ubuntu CD like the GNOME sound recorder that has never worked and Ekiga Softphone that nobody I know of uses. We can probably remove some stuff and make room for something that can actually be used.

Now, I'm a Python guy but really, there is nobody in the Python community who can beat Why the Lucky Stiff. Hackety Hack and the Poignant Guide make learning Ruby a snap for everybody. Guido's tutorial is nice, but it's pretty dry. Also, Why's been working on something called Shoes which, as far as I know, is a dead easy way for kids to make simple GUI's.

Another language that has caught my attention is REBOL. It's proprietary, but nice.

(By the way, NO home user will EVER touch Edubuntu.)

Is there any representative of the Ubuntu reading this?

### Post by ankursethi on 2008-01-06
> I agree with not having it, If someone wants to learn to program but can't be bothered to google "learning to program on ubuntu" then they're not going to be a very good programmer.
Isn't this the sort of mentality we're supposed to get rid of? If an 5 years old kid wants to learn to read, let him look for a school/library himself, right? The point of the exercise is not helping those people who know about programming and want to learn about it, the point is getting people *interested* in programming in the first place, telling them something they don't know about.

> Also note that Dive Into Python is meant for experienced rogrammers, not people just getting into programming.
DIP was just a suggestion. "Why's Poignant Guide ..." is a nice starting point for many people, and Hackety Hacky might suit children better. The language doesn't matter, Python, Ruby, Perl, even BASIC will do.

openSuSe shows a welcome screen on first boot that is completely useless. Why can't Ubuntu show a welcome screen on first boot that actually has useful resources? Ubuntu has a not very helpful default homepage in Firefox. Why can't a link about programming be added to that?

IMHO people using Ubuntu should know that they can actually do whatever the hell they want with their system. Tell them they can write tiny apps to suit their needs, or small applets, or plugins for Pidgin, AmaroK etc.

Does it hurt to spread information?
