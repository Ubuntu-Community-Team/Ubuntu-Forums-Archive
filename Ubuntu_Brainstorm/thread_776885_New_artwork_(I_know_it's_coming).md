### Post by irrdev on 2008-05-13
I give Ubuntu the "Worst Theme Award" when compared to either Fedora or Opesnsuse. The Orange/Brown Human theme is starting to look really dated, and it even reminds of the Windows "Classic" theme. For example, when I first showed off my Ubuntu Laptop to my family, my mother's instant reaction was "That's ugly. Give me back my blue XP theme anyday." If Ubuntu is truly trying to market itself for the new Linux user, looks are especially important. Most new Linux users don't know how to initially change a theme, or even know where to download one on the internet. Companies, too, prefer to use the desktop AS-IS, and the Human theme hardly portrays professionalism.

I myself find the Human theme too light, even though I generally don't appreciate eye-candy. I ended up installing Fedora's theme, using the instructions found [here]("http://www.ubuntu-unleashed.com/2007/09/howto-make-ubuntu-look-like-fedora-with.html"). Opensuse 11 will probably mark the best standard Linux theme yet, and I am amazed that even KDE4 has been fully themed to perfection. Ubuntu & Kubuntu both have a lot of catch-up to do on the Artwork side, and I hope that the Ubuntu Team prioritizes this in the upcoming release(s).
