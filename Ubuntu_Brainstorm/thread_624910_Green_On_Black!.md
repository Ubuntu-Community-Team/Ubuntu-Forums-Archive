### Post by francesco44 on 2007-11-27
Dear all
One of the most serious question for any computer user is the question of readability of the screen he uses. As a person with a deficient view I have searched for a longtime some solutions. Ubuntu is clearly much more readable than any other OS and especially Windows or OS X. From that point of view DO NOT modifie too much the actual parameters of display and your very convenient fonts.

But you can improve the thing for peaople with poor eyes. One great thing is to display the characters IN GREEN ON A BLACK BACKWARD

THE GREEN is the coulour where the sensitivity of vision is max so it is much less tiring for the eyes than black characters on white back.

One of the more awsome innovation of apple was to generalise the white on black like any newspaper. Unfortunately this is a bad solution although everyone stick to it....including Ubuntu.

My ADVICE: MAKE a SPECIAL DISPLAY SETTING for People with a bad sight......USE GREEN CHARACTERS ON BLACK....Although this might seem some sort of nostalgia of of computer of the 6O'...it is not.....Physics and physiology of sight justifies it completely...

Best things to all
