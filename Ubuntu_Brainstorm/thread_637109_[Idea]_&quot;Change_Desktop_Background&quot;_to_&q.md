### Post by Fireblend on 2007-12-10
I have to say, one of my favorite changes and inclusions on Gutsy was the fact that themes, wallpapers, fonts, interface and effects administration applications were unified in the appearance application.

I gotta admit I love making my computer look as good and aesthetically pleasant as possible, so being able to change all that stuff in a single application made this task way easier. Plus, the administration of these components is definitely more intuitive with only one manager, and that's not mentioning the infinitely more intuitive wallpaper administration, displaying my collection in a grid arrangement. I hated having to go one by one to browse through my collection.

Also I'm sure most people like customizing their desktops as well, the freedom this OS gives one to do so is just another of the countless great things about it.

Anyway, my idea is just a little string correction. Right-clicking on your desktop brings up that options menu, in which there's the "Change Desktop Background" option. However now that all those options mentioned above have been united in the same application that pops up once you select such option, wouldn't it be more correct if it said "Change Desktop Appearance"? 

I feel that's what it really does, so why just say background when you can manage most of the desktop's look and feel within it? It might sound like a little, pointless request, but this way people realize they can manage all this with just a right click on the desktop, while leaving it as it is now may not create that kind of association.

I like doing tasks as fast as possible, so I feel this is essential to improve the user's experience. For example, it took me a while to discover that instead of going to System->Preferences->Edit Menus to arrange the main menu, I could just simply right click on the Ubuntu symbol and select "Edit Menus". The same thing happens with this appearance option, since it can be tedious to go through the menus when you could just right click on the desktop.

Anyway, just a little idea. Keep up the brilliant work.

### Post by 23meg on 2007-12-10
Items in context menus shouldn't be global shortcuts; as the name suggests, context menus should act in their own context, and show options that are applicable to the selected area. 

If you're right clicking the desktop area to evoke the menu, it should give you the available options about that area, such as creating a folder there, creating a document there, creating a launcher there, or changing its background image. 

"Change Desktop Background" does the last one, and is thus correctly titled. The fact that it shows the rest of the capplet as well is secondary to the fact that it focuses the "Background" tab on startup, which you can consider as the equivalent of the old wallpaper capplet. 

This is somewhat similar to Evolution having two entries in the "Applications" menu, one in "Internet", and one in "Office". The one in "Internet" is titled "Evolution Mail", and launches the mail component, but the one in "Office" is titled simply "Evolution", and it launches whatever component you were last working with.

If you want shortcuts to certain frequently used capplets, it's easy to create them on your desktop or panel.
