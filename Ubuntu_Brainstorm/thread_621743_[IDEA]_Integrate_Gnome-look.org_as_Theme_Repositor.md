### Post by SoftVision on 2007-11-24
I ran Emerald successfully for the first time yesterday on Arch (former Ubuntu user). I noticed there were integrated theme repositories. Now today I was just reading a thread in Community Cafe of cool things you can do in linux that you can't do in Windows. "Full customizability without expensive sofware like WindowBlinds" was one of the points I read. It then hit me that maybe themes need to make use of a little integrated "repo". 

So with great sites like gnome-look.org out there for adding themes, wallpapers and screensavers, maybe the devs should consider making it even more easy to import this stuff. I also noticed something similar in Amarok where I could download plugins through a small window that previewed and described the plugins before downloading and installing them. 

This integration could be something along the lines of Emerald and import the latest, highest rated and most downloaded wallpapers, themes, icons and screensavers. There is a possible glitch where it may require people to test the themes (wallpapers seem the easiest to implement) or something to prevent crashes and faulty installs. Later maybe an inbuilt search could be added to search for these goodies. Also something like a "thumbs up/down" to say a theme works might make it clear what is stable and what is not

Also, this implementation might make the GNOME Desktop more "online", if this idea is not already a feature.
