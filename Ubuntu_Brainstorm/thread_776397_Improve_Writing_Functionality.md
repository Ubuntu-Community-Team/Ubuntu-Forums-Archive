### Post by megamanXplosion on 2008-04-30
Writing in Ubuntu (or Linux in general) can be daunting and frustrating.

My first frustration was the missing Alt-Code functionality. In Linux, typing an em dash (&#8212;), plus or minus (±), the logical not symbol (¬), or some other character not found on a keyboard is fairly difficult. Each character requires pressing a multitude of keys and there's a million things to remember. The alt-codes in Windows were easier to remember, easier to use, and easier to explain to others how to use.

Afterward, I figured I would find the characters I need in the Character Map application. After looking around for five minutes I realized the textual characters I wanted weren't listed.

After that, I turned to the Ubuntu forums for help. I was kindly provided instructions and helpful resources and I thanked those members. But that's when I learned that to use the various textual characters I was accustomed to using in Windows, I would have to sift through that large and convoluted Unicode listing. Grrr!

This situation should be remedied.
