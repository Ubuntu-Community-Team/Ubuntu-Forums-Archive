### Post by 23meg on 2007-11-03
What does iTunes have over various FOSS that lets you transfer stuff to your iPod?
[list]
[*] The store, which Apple chooses to keep to iTunes only
[*] Good connectivity without problems or compromises, or at least a mostly reliable assumption of it: since it's Apple that produces both the software and the hardware, you can expect trouble-free operation with all features even on the newest devices, which isn't always the case with FOSS, especially with new devices
[*] Market saturation
[/list]
Which of the above does writing software from the ground up that mimics iTunes, without having the proprietary advantages that Apple has, solve? None. Attacking the symptoms of the problem doesn't make the problem go away.
