### Post by davbren on 2008-10-01
Hey, You can shoot me down if you like but a few things came to mind...

I think that there should be better help for the average user on how to customise. Theres so much thats possible but some of it just isn't obvious. Lets face it, the Gnome default isn't exactly the most beautiful sight in the world. To most Linux users this is an obvious thing to change. It isn't easy however, for the average user.

When the user first installs or switches on his/her PC for the first time, there should be a smal tutorial on the basic stuff. Like choosing a wallpaper (I know its trivial but its important). I'm talking just little tooltip type things on where to get started making this Linux PC your own. 

Personalisation is important. I went onto my vista partition and I got really frustrated as I couldn't change anything. It just didn't feel like *my* pc. 

Programs like screenlets or at the very least gDesklets should be installed by default or made obvious on where to get them from. These little eyecandy things are valued by people. Come on, it sucked in at least 10 people into buying Vista (tongue firmly in cheek ;-) ) 

In terms of user friendliness, there has to be some kind of difference between Linux and the competition. At the moment, the competition is winning... An explanation of the menu system and the perhaps even the file system. 

I've found that people just aren't willing to learn a new way of thinking without someone telling them *exactly* how it is. Present company excepted of course, we're all in this together :D. 

Maybe some kind of "survey". I think new users would like this, A choice of what they would like in their system. They can choose what eye-candy they want and maybe a chance to remove stuff they don't. 

This could make them aware of just how vast the community is and educate them to the possiblities. This would perhaps be mostly appreciated among students. I found that the Project management software was most useful as was KMPlot. Its also reassuring to know that their are programs out there that are familiar. As long as these are emphasised then I think we're on to a winner...

Let me know what you think.

Cheers,
Dave.

### Post by Idefix82 on 2008-10-03
Well, I think that a tutorial at start up is a good idea, although what it should contain is a totally different question. There are lots of things a new user has to learn to properly use Linux.
However to have screenlets pre-installed is a very bad idea. It may seem to you that they are important, other people consider other things important. Since there is very limited space on a Live CD, I don't think one should clutter it up with screenlets. Some toys are fine, but in my opinion it's much better to teach the user to use Synaptic and to google for other goodies Ubuntu has to offer rather than having a Live CD full of toys.
