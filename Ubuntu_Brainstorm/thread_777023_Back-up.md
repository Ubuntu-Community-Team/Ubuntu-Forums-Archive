### Post by HandyAndy on 2008-05-01
Another great new release - the same old glaring omission.

A back-up utility is such a basic requirement for the safe and responsible use of any system that I find it incredible that there still isn't one in Ubuntu. Even Windows has one, for goodness sake!

I know there are ways of backing-up, using scripts or programs in the repos. I use one myself - Simple Back-up (sbackup). And I know that no one solution is going to meet everyone's needs and people with more 'techy' requirements are capable of looking after themselves. That's not my point. No user, first time or otherwise, should have to go beyond the menus to find a user-friendly, reliable back-up tool designed to 'just work' with Ubuntu.

I'm aware that any request for something new means something else has to go, but there are a lot of things less essential than back-up and sbackup is only a 67.5 kB download.

I'm not necessarily suggesting sbackup; I'm not technically qualified to know how good it is and it needs work to make it more intuitive to use. But it's a start. I think I read that development of it has stopped. If that's right, perhaps someone could pick it up with a view to getting it up to scratch for inclusion in a future Ubuntu release? I'm not aware that there's anything else in the pipeline.
