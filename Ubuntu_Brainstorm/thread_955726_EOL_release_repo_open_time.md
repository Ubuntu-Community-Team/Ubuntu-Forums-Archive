### Post by panda726 on 2008-10-24
I simply was thinking of the possibility of a security update coming out right at the end of the life of a release.  Sometimes people may not update quite that frequently, and this would help make sure that something like that can be downloaded by those planning to run the old release until the upcoming release more safely.

Of course they will shortly have to change versions altogether anyways, and maybe the time is short enough that even a security update isn't all that important anyway.

If the repos were open until after the new version came out this would be a moot point, as the version that many of those folks might be upgrading to would already be available.

### Post by gnomeuser on 2008-10-25
I quite like what Fedora does, every release has a lifetime of 2 cycles + 1 month, that gives people time to perform the upgrade after the new release is out, work out the few problems that might occure and such, or determine if the cutting edge or a release that has proven itself for 6 month is the way to go. It's only a small cost in maintance but users seem to appriciate it.
