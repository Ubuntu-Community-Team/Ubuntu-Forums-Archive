### Post by KIAaze on 2008-04-28
Panel applets are actually GTK applications from what I understood.
This means that it is already possible to "animate" them.
The volume applet and wifi applet already change their image depending on the volume level or signal strength.

So it is possible to animate the applets. Maybe it's not optimal on system resources the way it's done now, but it's possible.

Some tutorials about applet development:
[http://www.gnome.org/projects/ORBit2/appletstutorial.html](http://www.gnome.org/projects/ORBit2/appletstutorial.html)
[http://developer.gnome.org/doc/tutorials/applet/index.html](http://developer.gnome.org/doc/tutorials/applet/index.html)
[http://www.pygtk.org/articles/applets_arturogf/](http://www.pygtk.org/articles/applets_arturogf/)

I made one myself recently:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4751333&postcount=9](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4751333&postcount=9)
It's far from perfect, but it works. :)
