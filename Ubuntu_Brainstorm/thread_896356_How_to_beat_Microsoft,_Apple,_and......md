I have an idea how Canonical can beat:

Microsoft, Apple, Adobe, Sun, Nvidia, AMD/ATI, Intel, Dell, HP, Lenovo, IBM, AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, Sprint, Comcast, Charter, Cox, Ford, Toyota, Honda, GM, BMW, Mercedes, Volkswagon, Hyundai, Cessna, SDG&E, Exxon, Chevron, Mobil, and pretty much every company in every industry except for groceries, foods, and services, such as teaching and repairs.

Here is a copy paste of my idea I submitted to brainstorm.  Obviously some of this is "blue sky", but please consider it realistically and let me know which parts of this plan are not technically feasible.  I believe all of them could be feasible, though a few may be prohibitively expensive, or subject to government regulation and controls in certain countries.  Feel free to point those issues out as well.  Please be aware that some of this technology may be drastically improving or coming down in price, and could come down in price much further with economies of scale.

Meet Phonebuntu, the Zeppelin Autopilot:
I would like to see an Ubuntu machine with the following characteristics:

iPhone form factor.

USB 3.0 port(s) both for charging, and for docking with an honest to goodness keyboard, monitor, mouse, speakers, and gamepad.

A powerful Field-programmable gate array that can emulate any modern multi-core CPU/GPU/chipset/soundcard combination popular up to say 3 years ago, capable of running any OS currently on the market, including a new custom Ubuntu*.

unencumbered wifi, an integrated webcam (on the same side as the screen!), and speex and h.264 hardware acceleration built in, and a pidgin plugin to connect p2p automagically over any network already available (MySpace, MSN, AIM, Yahoo, Gmail, ICQ, IRC, etc.) for VOIP, video chat, high speed file xfr, VNC, and automatic speech to text to babelfish to text to speech capabilities.

Tom Tom like GPS capabilities.

A microSD slot.

a mode of ad-hoc wifi on by default that can leap frog phone to phone until it finds an internet connection from any location near a likewise enabled phone, thereby extending wifi service, VOIP, VidChat, VNC, and file xfr to any phone in range of a phone in range of a phone in range of a phone in range of a wifi router.

*The New Custom Ubuntu

Will act exactly like an iphone (but with better GPS, ie TOM TOM level usage) when in phone mode (but with no DRM, all based on FOSS), but also have real time VNC capabilities to and from any other machine connected to the internet, having the touchscreen act as the mouse cursor in that mode.

Resemble Windows identically in layout ("Start" menu, "QuickLaunch", "Window List", "Systray", Clock), but with compiz in "Computer" mode, when hooked up to the docking station with mouse, speakers, keyboard, mouse, gamepad (and charging).

Include in the "Quicklaunch":
Firefox, Pidgin, Rhythmbox, VLC, Remote Desktop Viewer, Open Office Writer, Zsnes, Mupen64plus, Urban Terror, and Virtualbox.

Include in Virtualbox:
Latest ReactOS from the repos, Include most popular FOSS apps for Windows, mostly resembling the selection at portableapps.com.

Also include option to install Windows XP into a VDI, and copy license/compatible programs over the network from existing computer.

Include Mdrop plugin for Rhythmbox, working out of the box perfectly, including effects transitions, and coverflow option vis a vis iTunes, or iPhones. Include Time Machine style app, and ability to back up entire phone as a VDI for Virtualbox.

Install this phone in a docking station on a large cherry wood desk in a giant luxury RV solar powered zeppelin, near the jacuzzi.

I'm looking for venture capitalists.

Oh, and one more thing:

[http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/12326/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/12326/)

I want my Zeppelin either green or purple, whatever the solar panel skin color ends up being.

I also intend the Zeppelin to guide itself using GPS, lidar, radar, infrared, sonar, and wifi communication.  For safety every zeppelin should have two internal gyroscopic parabolic dishes for wifi communication, and emergency communications.

The Zeppelin should be designed in a modular fashion, out of light weight carbon-fiber materials and including a large bed, small kitchen, bathroom, shower, hot-tub, LCD movie theater (with Couch, and Wall to Wall video chat, in other words a small webcam in the middle of one wall relays the entire room onto the LCD wall of someone else's LCD projector, and vice versa.  Virtual presence technology, including OpenAL surround sound).

The Zeppelin should be constructed out of the cheapest, most reliable off-the-shelf parts.  The solar array should be an inexpensive CIGS type panel completely covering the exterior.  Alternately (or in conjunction), the zeppelin could be "parked", or tethered, and the propulsion fans put into neutral in order to provide additional electricity much like windmills.

Floatation should be provided by large carbon fiber spherical cells filled with hydrogen.  Additional lift for altitude adjustment should be provided with small helium filled intertubes and a small compressor.

The Solar Array (and propellers while tethered) should charge a large bank of lithium ion rechargeable batteries.  These batteries should hold enough charge for an overnight flight in absolute darkness, and still be trickle charging the batteries while in flight during the day, provided there is no head wind, or particularly in cases where a tail wind is present.
