### Post by mikasjoman on 2008-01-19
Hi. This is a proposition for me to solve the following:

- Ease of connecting/manage to your home computer/s from any internet connected computer
- Online automatic backup of written files
- Get more money to Canonical + new develloper that don´t know how to get going.
- Web hosting included (LAMP) + possibility to add your own domain
- Get a lot of cash to Canonical
- Get new web developers/programmers to help out that today are closed out by their newbie level of programing.
- open a Ubuntu Function Market

THE IDEA: Is to make a (dot)Mac for the Ubuntu desktop sold on the front page of ubuntu.com. Today Apple makes GREAT money from selling .Mac accounts, but for me the service has never been appealing. I want something better and since there are way too few people programming Ubuntu a focus on getting cash from the community to Canonical should be focused on.

PROPOSAL: A DOT Mac for Ubuntu, but open-source and way more useful.

The problems:

PROBLEM: 
I just cant connect to my office server from outside. I can, but it´s difficult to set up with dynDNS and requires the user to know how to set up their routers to send the user from outside to the right computer.

SOLUTION:
When I buy the 12 month subscription from Cannonical (try 60 days for free), I can connect to my computer by just entering: [www.myubuntu.com/myUserName/AnyOfMyRegisterdComputerNames](www.myubuntu.com/myUserName/AnyOfMyRegisterdComputerNames)
So, in effect, I can connect to any registered (online) computers and manage them with a web interface.
To add more computers, I just log in to myubuntu.com and affirm my new ubuntuinstallation both on the computer and online. In my list online I see all online computers.
This way an administrator could close a whole school when being home (saves enviroment and money). 
If I want to use VNC, just click and it opens up.

PROBLEM:
I always want my files with me, but I don´t like to care about USB sticks or online backups.
SOLUTION: When you log into any Ubuntu installation, ad your myubuntuusername(email?) + password + Click the (MyUbuntu checkbox) and your online disk will show up at login.
But, nobody want´s to care about online disks, so instead of saving files to your web disk by user interaction, every "save" will also be saved on your online disk but by a background process. This way the files are always there, but without any user interaction. 

PROBLEM: dot Mac sucks, it was cool when introduced but today it´s just not really amazing.  I don´t have LAMP and I don´t have the ease of adding an domain to it.
SOLUTION: Well that does not need to be explained, just do it.

To make it waaaaay more cool than dot Mac, I propose to open the system up for everybody this way:
- Have an online market for new functions on myubuntu.com. 

This way, if somebody really needs an non existing function they can put a bounty up on the homepage for any developer to get their hands on. This then becomes part of GPL, and the whole community thrives from the new functions as pluggins anyone can add.

This would get new developers in to contribute to the Ubuntu development with ease. Sure, they are not developing the system, but functions with PHP that so many today know how to program. They will feel that they are in the group. This will hopefully give cash to both Canonical and new developers, and an incentive to deep even deeper on how to help out with future of Ubuntu.

At last, I would also like to have this Ubuntu Market opened for the system. If anyone want´s a function, publish it, add a bounty (through paypal), let other people who wish that function add some more money to it so the bounty grows bigger the more people want the function. 
1000 USD will be allot for a chinese graduate developer, a hundred bucks too. Who said you cant get developers to an free open source project? ;)

Sincerely yours

### Post by Mr Greencastle on 2008-01-19
Your 'bounty' development model intrigues me. 

How would one regulate who gets the bounty when the function is complete? Does this model encourage collaboration between several developers? Who is to say that I, the developer, gives poorly written code? Do I still get the bounty? If I were to work with some other developers, who gets the bounty? Is it split? We would need a way to track who does what. Should it be split evenly? Or lean toward the developer who does more work? Or better work? Who judges that?
Sounds very proprietary to me...

I don't think this really feeds the open source development model. I think you've dreamed up a new development model entirely. Or have you?

I think that there too many questions that need be answered and figured out before something like this could really take off. How does this   development model prevent stealing? Doesn't it really encourage it? But this is open-source, which theoretically eliminates stealing from the equation anyway.

Perhaps I misunderstood.

### Post by onlineapps on 2008-07-22
I like the idea, but dotubuntu isn't the greatest name. How about Netbuntu or something?

Also, I agree with probono about the lock-in thing. Let's make this cross platform. And make it open source, so you don't HAVE to use Canonical servers.

The following can be used:

* Some open source Apache/PHP hosting, hosted on Canonical servers. MySQL is probably overkill.
* File hosting, hosted on Canonical servers
* Email (POP and IMAP), hosted on Canonical servers
* Calendar (ical and CalDav), hosted on Canonical servers
* Chat (Jabber), hosted on Canonical servers
* VNC (easy to use, rather than the current thing where you have to open up software and hardware firewall ports. Something like Crossloop)
* File access (SSH), easier to use than current options where you have to mess with software and hardware firewalls.

Obviously, you'd integrate the email and calendaring with Evolution and file hosting into Nautilus. The VNC could be an extension of the current screen sharing app.

I also prefer the idea of it being non-profit, only paying what it costs.
