So I got a Dellbuntu (a dell preinstalled with Ubuntu), and realized that the reason I have a lot fewer problems than most folks is cause (other than the graphics card) everything on this machine was picked to go with Ubuntu.
But why should I have an advantage over other folks?  I know there are a few places that build Ubuntu machines to order, but by now we have a good list of which hardware items have good support and which don't (I think, noob here).
So why not make a web site or utility or something that will allow a user to input a computer configuration and tell them which items are supported (Yes, limited, or no) and offer supported alternatives.  If web based it could be on the Canonical site, and have a link from the Ubuntu forums site.  That would help users who are buying new computers to get a computer that works well with Ubuntu "out of the box," and reduce help needs and folks who leave our community.
