### Post by bwhite82 on 2008-10-12
I recently installed a demo of RHEL5 and was impressed by its "newb-friendliness" (even though I am not a newb) and its ease of use. A big reason it was so was Redhat's RHN (Redhat Network) which is a webmin of sorts for your machine/s on your network.

You can check out a demo video of it here:

[https://www.redhat.com/f/rhn20050117.swf](https://www.redhat.com/f/rhn20050117.swf)

Screenshots:

[https://www.redhat.com/rhn/rhntour/index.html#update](https://www.redhat.com/rhn/rhntour/index.html#update)

Consider this an informal proposal for something like this being created for Ubuntu as well as encouraging discussion of the merits/benefits of such a solution.

Edit: If you like this idea, vote for it at Ubuntu's Brainstorm:
[http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/14334/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/14334/)
