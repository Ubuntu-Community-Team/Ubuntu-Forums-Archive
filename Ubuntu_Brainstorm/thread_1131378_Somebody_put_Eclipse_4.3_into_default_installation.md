### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-04-21
You want a integrated development environment in the default install? Why would 99% of all users need this? 

If you want a package upgrade for Eclipse (I assume you mean 3.4 as 4.3 doesn't exist) then there will be a session at UDS to debate such an upgrade. You can also volunteer your skills to make it happen making it far more likely that this target will be met. Volunteers are important, they are why our Mono stack e.g. is so well kept so you can make a huge different. It is far better to have software in the hands of people who care about it.

There are additional reason why Eclipse will never get into the default install, aside it being only useful to a small procentage of users, it is huge. Putting it on the cd would require pulling large amounts of software that is actually useful.
