### Post by xlinuks on 2007-10-28
OpenOffice doesn't seem to effectively address the startup time and speed as it progresses. Disabling Java and increasing the default memory doesn't actually improve its startup time which IMHO is about 300-500% more slow then the MS Office2003. OpenOffice has proven that this isn't going to change drastically any time soon. We can't wait for like 10 years..
IMHO now is the high time for Canonical (to team up with RedHat and Google) to create a fast and memory-efficient Office Suit.
The developers are much in agreement that OpenOffice is hard to develop for not being modular and thus virtually having to compile the whole office suit before testing a new feature. Besides there are serious issues of Sun controlling what goes into the OpenOffice and what doesn't. Mark AFAIK has confirmed this.
I think this would be a smart move. I tried migrating some people to OpenOffice (from MS Office2003) and their first impressions and words were "It's way too slow, I can't work with it".
