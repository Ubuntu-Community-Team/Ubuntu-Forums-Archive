### Post by Anthem on 2008-04-21
Why does a base install of Ubuntu give two versions of Solitaire?  There's AisleRiot solitaire, which contains a billion and one versions including Klondike, FreeCell, and Spider, and then there's also a separate FreeCell Solitaire.  Why include both?  The mechanics are identical, and it's actually somewhat confusing to the new user, who isn't expecting the many different versions of solitaire in AisleRiot.

Wouldn't it make sense to remove FreeCell, and add a splash screen to the first use of AisleRiot stating that it contains a bunch of different solitaire games?

The average user thinks that "solitaire" = klondike.

### Post by fromgi on 2008-04-30
With a real deck of cards you can't do the same thing (at least honestly).  With a real deck, cards are randomly displayed, but using AisleRiot Solitaire, you let the computer decide the next card to display. It's like holding onto the cards, and based on the cards on the table, you choose the next card from the deck your holding.  Ever notice if you choose between two cards of the same value, say you have a choice of two tens to put over a jack, you will always choose the wrong 10?  If you do an undo, you'll notice the better choice is the other 10.  Many other issues.  Check to source code.  You'll appreciate other solitaire games versus this one.
