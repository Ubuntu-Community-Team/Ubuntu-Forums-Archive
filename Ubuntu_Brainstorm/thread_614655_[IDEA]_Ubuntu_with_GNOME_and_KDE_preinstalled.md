### Post by runemaste644 on 2007-11-16
I would like a flavor of Ubuntu to have Gnome and KDE (GTKDE?) installed by default. I did that to my system and am very satisfied. (though it is more like *boxamixfcegtkde, all of the WMs combined ;) )
Both GTK and KDE are very impressive with cool features like Konqueror's thing where you can listen to music while browsing the web and GTK's FireFox. The problem with integrating them is that KDE apps do weird stuff in Gnome (especially if you have the Mac-style menu bar enabled) and in KDE, GTK apps sometimes look... well... hideous. That might be fixable by making both WMs start and making them connect via seamless RDP.

Basically what i am saying is that we should fuse Gnome and KDE somehow and maybe a few other WMs.

### Post by dhobbs on 2007-11-16
I like the separation between KDE and Gnome.  I'm not saying that you shouldn't do it, but I like how simple Gnome is when compared to KDE.  KDE feels bulky to me.  Therefore, my opinion, and probably the opinion of a few other people, is to keep them separate, which is essentially what has happened since both of the projects were started.

But you are more than welcome to start fusing them together, or making a distribution that installs them all by default :).  I love F/OSS.
