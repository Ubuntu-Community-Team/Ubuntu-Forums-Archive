### Post by Tundro Walker on 2008-01-03
I think this post would be better off in the tech-support forums, so folks there could help you out.  There are some wireless cards that just plain don't work in Linux.  More drivers come out all the time, but as they do, newer wireless cards show up that don't work, or a company changes the chipset on a card that used to work, and now it doesn't work again.

Your point about the wireless being easier to manipulate / get going isn't falling on deaf ears, though.  There's been a lot of grumbling about that from the Linux community in general.  Ubuntu's provided several methods, but because there's not just one sure-proof method, it still gets the majority of criticism.

Even when wireless does work, there's issues with having it bump offline, or it not auto-connecting on startup.  Folks have tried ndiswrapper, wicd, and various driver sub-sets for cards (mad-wifi, etc).  

This might be one of those issues where something fundamental with Linux has to get worked out, but folks take it out on Ubuntu since it's the "messenger" telling them it doesn't work at the time.  All the Ubuntu folks can do is pass the message along and hope to hear about something getting worked out sooner or later.  I'm not sure.
