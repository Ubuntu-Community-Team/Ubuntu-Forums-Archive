Medibuntu contains codecs which Canonical CAN'T redistribute, because it would more than alienate people in the U.S., or may even be illegal in U.K. as well.

The reason that there are some people who post about files is because they use some strange format (ie, my video camera uses a .tov movie format which is only supported by the manufacturer's drivers). Also, this is a forum, so it is logical that people would ask here.

Most AVIs that can't be played have DRM encryption. Same with DVDs. It is illegal in many places to work around it, and that is why only Medibuntu can distribute the required libdvdcss codec for DVDs (I don't know about AVIs with DRM).
