### Post by gnomeuser on 2008-11-14
I believe this happens because the consistency of use is different. You think of the applet as being nautilus (where you are by default expected to double click). However all applets require a mere single click. 

The trash applet is really both. We can pick either consistency with nautilus or with the applet experience.

There is a lesson to be learned in this with regards to user expectation, the inconsistency is not with the trash applet requiring a single click but nautilus' decision to default to double clicking being the operation mode. 

What else on the desktop requires double clicks. Surely not the menu, the window list, the clock or any menu items. Clearly the inconsistent item here is nautilus.

(So I voted you were wrong even if you pointed out a real problem, since your solution appears wrong)

### Post by steeleyuk on 2008-11-14
[QUOTE=gnomeusers' blog]This made me think, where outside of nautilus do we in GNOME default to requiring double clicks.[/QUOTE]

There are a few places that require double clicks, usually they involve a selection then action, even when they arguably don't need to.

Examples:

Totem's playlist sidepane
Dictionary's similar words sidepane
GNOME file selection dialogs (even when you enable single click in Nautilus - its been reported as a bug somewhere I think)
Selecting a character for copying from the character map

Its a little bit inconsistent in some areas. The file selection dialog is a big one that is instantly noticeable when you enable single click.
