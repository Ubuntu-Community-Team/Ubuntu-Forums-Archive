PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE create a way for users to easily enable playback for commercial and encrypted DVDs. We really need to get away from things like Automatix (which doesn't even do the job in Gutsy) and endless tutorials to accomplish these things for us. ### Post by x64Jimbo on 2007-11-14
Because the other non-free things are just like you said: legally murky. When you get into the area of DVD decryption and mp3 support, you're treading on what the US patent and legal system has deemed to be black and white territory. Were Ubuntu to make these "circumvention" technologies readily available, Canonical would be in a lot of trouble from the US gov't. Because the long arm of the law is often under the control of whoever is giving the most money, DVD and mp3 support out of the box will be the lowest priority on Ubuntu's to-do list. Anyone who is bold enough to try Ubuntu must also be bold enough to search Google once or twice. A quick search for "Ubuntu DVD playback" will get you more than enough tutorials that spoon-feed you all the way to DVD watching bliss.
