### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-01-05
I vote keep the branding we have, we can't accomplice anything major in the remaining Jaunty timeframe and we still lack a lot of underlying technology before we can do a real overhaul.

We can naturally continue to offer alternative themes in the community package but I think throwing away the branding we have now for minor experimentation would be a mistake. A move like that needs to be planned and it needs a roadmap with milestone goals we can hit with each release to get to a point where we want to be.

I believe a new look should go beyond a new theme, it should encompass everything, and it should be done as much as possible upstream or the process with drown us in work. Now is a good time to start the underlying work, look at what we need to go into the GNOME3 stack, what kernel and driver work we need. 

Realize it will be a multiyear project to get right, one that will require major investments but also one that will bring Linux as a whole to a much nicer place. It is not about how Linux looks, it's about how Linux feels and works.
