### Post by saphil on 2008-05-04
Getting kids used to Ubuntu, and focusing on making the multimedia work right out of the box, will have a huge impact on acceptance, I believe.  Show benefits that match the usage pattern of specific age-groups.  Zero cost is not enough.  MS has a program to put zero-cost software into schools.  Students learn to use that OS and voila! They start to buy the marketing concept that it is "user-friendly."  There is no such spec as user-friendly.  It is just marketing hype, but if you say it often enough, people will start parroting it back.  
They start thinking, "If it is user-friendly, but I am having difficulties, it is my fault for being so dim."  
User-friendly is an over-full category, but without it, the old, elitist attitude prevails.  We should embrace all the newbies, and be friendly to them, just as we are to each other.  The Ubuntu-forum is the friendliest on earth, but people have to get here, and find out.  If they think we are all a bunch of egg-heads who cannot say a comprehensible thing to help them with their computer issues, they just get sad and discouraged and run home to Windows or MacOSX, that they feel more hopeful about, because they are told it is user-friendly.
