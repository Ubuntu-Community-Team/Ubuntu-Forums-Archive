Photoshop:  Unless you are a graphic designer by profession, the GIMP is a perfectly reasonable substitute, and so far I've been able to do everything I did in Photoshop in the GIMP.

Windows Media Player:  After getting all the codecs and nonfree stuff that doesn't come out of the box with Ubuntu (playing DVDs, proprietary codecs, etc), VLC is an amazing media player that can play just about anything you throw at it.  You have to configure it well though, but to this day is all I use and my only complaint is that I can't pause wmv net streams.

Maya: I much prefer working with Blender.  Why?  Maya is an incredible resource hog which practically renders (no pun intender) your system unusable. I hope I clarified the reasons for why I today also think its a bad idea to incorporate wine into Ubuntu, because I think that for home, small business users, and hobbyists, *Ubuntu Linux can be a great alternative completely free of Windows software*.
