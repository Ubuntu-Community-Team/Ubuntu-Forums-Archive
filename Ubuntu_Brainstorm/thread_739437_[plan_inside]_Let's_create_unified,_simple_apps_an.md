### Post by Redrazor39 on 2008-03-29
I see lots of posts every day saying "Ubuntu needs more unification" or "The apps are too separated" or "There's no really good place to start with apps". Well, what I think we should do is take existing FOSS apps and work with them to create a unified set of applications that the user can start off with, kind of like on Mac OS X but with even more, and allow full customization and keep the freedom to get rid of and replace apps.

With this good starting base, casual users can get straight to work (or play) and powerusers can explore deeper things within the apps and even replace and get rid of ones they don't like.

Attached is a Document of my thoughts of how to start (not the final plan by any means, but what I think should happen).



I would do this myself, but I don't know any code. If some developers who knew how to tinker with apps and change the behavior (add buttons, etc.) could work with me somehow and we would all find something to do to help, that would be great.


Please read what I have attached. It includes the plan I have thought of and worked hard on compiling from notes scattered across my partitioned hard drive. If anyone knows how to do this or knows anyone who can help, please let me know.

I can even upload this as a wiki if people agree to help edit it.
