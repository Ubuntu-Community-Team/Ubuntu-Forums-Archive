### Post by Turgon on 2007-10-22
I just had a look at the new video of the new OSX ( [http://www.apple.com/macosx/guidedtour/](http://www.apple.com/macosx/guidedtour/) ) and there was one little feature I thought both could be easy to have in Ubuntu and that could really help new users  and that is predefined e-mail settings for the most common web mails (like gmail, yahoo) so that you only need to add your name, email address and service.

By simplefying this more users will be able to use and discover other cool features with apps like evolution and thunderbird. 

Its too bad that many users doesn't use these programs and rather opens their browsers and goes through the hazzle of going in to the site (may not even be bookmarked) and typing in both their email address and password when they can save much time by using one of the good open source email-clients ubuntu can offer.

What do you think?
