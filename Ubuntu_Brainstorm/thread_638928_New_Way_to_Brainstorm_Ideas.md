### Post by pmn03 on 2007-12-12
I have been thinking that a forum isn't the best way to brainstorm new ideas, it needs some web2.0-ification. (Yes, I hate that term too).

In the past few months I put together a brainstorming website. I think it adds a little more to brainstorming than forums like this, where the ideas at the top of the list are the most recent, or the most discussed. Which isn't necessarily the best ideas. Great ideas might get lost.

I added a couple of simple improvements over a traditional forum: voting and wikis. Now if you like the idea you vote it up, so the cream rises to the top. And each idea gets a wiki page, to organize the idea as it comes together. What I really want to do is create a community that helps projects mature from the point of inception to writing the first lines of code.

There is a general purpose site for any and all ideas, mostly open source projects: 
[mindgrapes.org]("http://mindgrapes.org")

And a site specifically for ubuntu ideas:
[ubuntu.mindgrapes.org]("http://ubuntu.mindgrapes.org")

Anyway, please check it out and post your ideas. Feedback is greatly appreciated.
