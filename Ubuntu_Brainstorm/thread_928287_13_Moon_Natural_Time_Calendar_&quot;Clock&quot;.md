### Post by PirateChef on 2009-01-22
There's the French Revolutionary Calendar, which was introduced in the French Revolution along with the metric system. It has 12 months, with 3 10-day weeks. 12x30=360 + a 5-day celebratory period at the end of the year.

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_revolutionary_calendar](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_revolutionary_calendar)

Metric is definitely the way to go!

Unfortunately, the reason why there isn't more support for different calendars in Linux, or any other OS, is there's just no demand for it. Most people don't care. Sure, it's a little awkward having to remember which months have 30 or 31 days, but it's a minor annoyance that people are happy to live with. 

Basically, it looks like you have two options:
1) Learn to program or,
2) Hire a programmer

I think #2 would be much easier. And faster.
