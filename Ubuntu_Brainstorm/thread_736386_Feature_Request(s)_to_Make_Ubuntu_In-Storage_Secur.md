### Post by Bllz on 2008-03-26
Hi guys,

I do apologize if these features have been discussed before, but I think i have a few (hopefully not-too-compex) ideas for making ubuntu a better experience.

I'm writing from the perspective of a guy who knows a little bit about the workings of computers, has primarily a background in windows OS, but has finally made the shift to linux.  In other words, I'm not a professional or an expert, but I do feel very comfortable around computers.

Here goes...

I like the full disk encryption offered via LVM, but there are a few problems.  The automatic setup makes it easy, but doesn't allow for special configuration without a decent amount of technical knowledge.  I, for example, like to have my /user separate from /, so my first suggestion is to be able to encrypt the entire system on-the-fly from within the OS.  Such a procedure could be handled much like in the windows version of TrueCrypt, or PGP.  That way, I can set up my system the way I like it, and THEN encrytp it -- it guarantees simplicity and good results.  On a side note, it would be nice to select which cipher one wishes to uses -- twofish runs faster than AES on my system.

Authentication is also a concern of mine.  It would be really nice if Ubuntu supported keyfiles hosted on external media to authenticate at boot time.  Such keyfiles could be hosted on SD or CF flash cards, USB flash devices, CDROMs, etc...

An extra step would be to host the boot loader on such an external device, thereby achieving 100% system encryption, but I do understand the inherent risks of doing so.

Lastly,  I was really hoping that an application similar in scope and depth to Eraser 5.8 (windows software) could be written for Linux.  i am not very happy with Wipe, and have yet to see a similar product that can both securely erase files from magnetic media, and wipe unused space for added security.  A GUI would be a huge plus too.

Anyway, thanks for taking the time to consider my requests, and please understand that I submit this with the utmost humility and respect -- You guys know way more than I do about this stuff.

Bllz
