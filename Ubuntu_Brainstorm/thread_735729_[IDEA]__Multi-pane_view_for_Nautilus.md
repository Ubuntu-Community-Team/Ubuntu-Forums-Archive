### Post by dustigroove on 2008-03-25
I would love to see the ability to [open multiple panes in a Nautilus window]("http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/5854/") in a side-by-side fashion.

Think along the lines of GNOME Commander or KCommander, but without the limitation of just two panes and within the design aesthetics of Nautilus. A button at the top of each pane could act as the method of showing the current location for that pane as well as allowing for a drop-down cookie-trail selection when clicking on it. It would show that pane's current directory + or - a number of directories above or below that location when the drop-down is open. The drop-down would also have an entry at the bottom to browse for a different location if it is deeper in the directory structure. 

Anyway, I would love to hear any feedback from folks reading this, embellishments, or constructive criticism on this idea. I do realize mentally picturing someone else's vision can lead to totally different views so I'll try to post a mock-up of how I see it when time allows and would love to see anyone else's vision of it if they feel so inclined.

Cheers
