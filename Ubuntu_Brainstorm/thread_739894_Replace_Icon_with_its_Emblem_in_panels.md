### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-04-13
I think this would be problematic, unfortunately, as emblems are built to be generic; anything could have that emblem. The icon, on the other hand, is meant to say exactly what that thing is. Think of it as nouns and adjectives. An icon is the noun, the emblem is the adjective. "This is a picture folder".

Theoretically works in this case, but I can think of many where it causes regressions and usability issues. For example, a mounted drive becomes a big USB emblem alongside many other big USB emblems. The icon for that drive, on the other hand, in the ideal situation (still waiting...) says precisely what kind of drive it is.
