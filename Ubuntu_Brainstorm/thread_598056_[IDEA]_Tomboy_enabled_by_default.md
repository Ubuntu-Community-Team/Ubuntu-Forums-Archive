### Post by phrizek on 2007-10-31
I'm using the latest 0.9 release of Tomboy and have come to the conclusion that this nifty little program is truly one of the most useful tools on the GNOME desktop. I used it religiously before, but with the new addition of to-do lists, it has really become one of my favorite productivity tools.

Unfortunately, while being included on the disc and installed by default, I feel that most people simply won't even notice it and get to experience this really cool program. So I propose that it should be enabled by default and placed somewhere on the top panel (it's very lonely up there). That way, people will see the eye-catching yellow memo pad and be inlined to click and find out just what it is.

If anybody is worried that adding it to the panel will just clutter things up, I'd like to chime in and point out that the icon is small, pleasing to look at, and very functional. In addition, the program is one of the simplest around, and won't really confuse newcomers. For these reasons, I feel that the added functionality outweighs any downsides.

Your thoughts?
