### Post by jfrorie on 2008-03-31
I wanted to solicit input in something I've been thinking about.  There seems to be a lot of interest in updating the graphical theme under Ubuntu.  I think there has been some movement to to procure the talents of DeviantArt to create some new stuff.

I was thinking a of a similar approach to update the sounds within the desktop as well and also using the Open Source community.  I have been in contact with a few individuals in the sound/music arena and there has been a good bit of interest.  Of particular note, Raf at Kompoz.com is very interested in this and is looking to pitch it to his community. (If you haven't been to kompoz.com, check it out.  It's quite cool.)

Yes, some people find sounds annoying. But they can always be turned off.  I want to to see Ubuntu provide options for the others.  My list of pros:

1) It provides a more polished experience to new users
2) Sounds aid the visually challenged
3) Sound provide subtle cues for events without the need for annoying pop ups and other distractions.  (New mail, etc)
4) Sounds provide notification for people that are running headless machines, such as in a KVM environment.

There are a few brainstorms on this that are languishing:

[http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/5842/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/5842/)
[http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/5413/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/5413/)

My thinking is that since the idea is to use the open source community, this is win-win situation for Ubuntu. We need to have the packagers add the final product, but beyond this, it's all external. It brings new participants into the fold that may not have found a way to contribute before.  It raises Linux awareness among the sound community, a largely non-technie group.  

My angle is Linux advocacy.  I want to see us expand beyond the current base and spread toward more new arenas.  I think this has that potential.

Comments?

Jim

### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-03-31
Sound themes are definitely a good idea, and something we need. Working on a fun little patch for the GNOME volume control that plays a test sound as the user adjusts it, but it seems horrible to need a directly exposed option where the user chooses that sound. (Setting a bad precedent; soon, everything under the sun will be asking "what sound should I play?", where the choice should at least be centralized).
Thus, my fiddling is at a standstill until we have sound themes. Could be as simple as moving the current sound selection for GNOME sound preferences to an Advanced section, with the basic section being selecting profiles (the latter options). Ideally, the Advanced options would be a list where new sounds can be added openly instead of being a fixed, arbitrary set... or themes could provide tons of sounds with that choice outside of direct control, such as Icon themes. (In that case, it is up to the Ubuntu art team to be tasteful, with maybe a few options provided for common sounds like the current "error message" sound option).

Desktop sounds *are* working better in Hardy, by the way; for some reason, sound effects with dialog boxes did not happen with ALSA, but they work well with PulseAudio.
