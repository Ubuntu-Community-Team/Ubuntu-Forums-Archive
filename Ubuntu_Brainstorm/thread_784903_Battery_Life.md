### Post by spacefreak86 on 2008-05-06
For the future editions of Ubuntu, I would like to see increased battery life and quicker response time on loading programs. I noticed that on Kubuntu Hardy, my battery usually lasts about 2 hours, maybe 2 hours 15 minutes, while Windows XP would report 2 hours 45 minutes. 

Both measurements taken with screen brightness set at lowest setting and desktop sitting on idle (ie, no programs running, cannot guarantee background programs). Is it possible to squeeze out an extra 15 minutes of battery life on Ubuntu? At the least, I'd allow someone to watch a movie on their laptop during a flight or bus ride without having to keep the screen brightness so dark they can barely see the darker scenes.
