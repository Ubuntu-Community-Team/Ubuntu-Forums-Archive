### Post by mikeyfbi on 2008-07-10
When I think of why I LOVE Ubuntu...a major reason that always comes to mind is the plethora of keyboard shortcuts I use for everything I do.

I use so many, in fact, it's hard to keep track of them.  What I mean is they seem to become second nature.

This wouldn't seem like a problem, but it becomes one when I want to add MORE shortcuts for various things!  Then I'm sometimes at a loss of what I can bind it too.

Then I thought, what if there was just a picture of a keyboard, showing you all the keys that were currently being used, and what they were used for.

You could possibly sort by: Shift, Alt, Ctrl, Shift+Alt, etc

So lets say you view the "Alt" keys.  It would then just change the colors of the keys that are currently binded to "Alt+KEY"
Then possibly you could just mouse over a key, and it would tell you what that key did.
ie - with "alt" selected, mouse over F2, it would show you "Run Application"

I have a limited programming knowledge but this would seem to be a fairly 'easy' job to apply to the shortcuts in Preferences -> Keyboard Shortcuts...but if you wanted to get more advanced...possibly include all program shortcuts ;)

Anyway, let me know what you think.
