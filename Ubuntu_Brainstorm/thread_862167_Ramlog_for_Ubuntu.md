### Post by Ub1476 on 2008-07-17
Hi, for those who doesn't know, Ramlog is project that keeps your system logs in RAM, and writes them to disk when your computer shutdown. This can improve batterly life, less writes to disk (good for ssd), and less RAM usage (I think). The side effect is that it wont be that easily to troubleshoot if a deamon starts rapidly to send error messages, spinning up your HDD etc. 

Well, the whole story is on [Linux.com]("http://www.linux.com/feature/141231")

I think this would be nice to have as an option in the power managment settings. Opinions?
