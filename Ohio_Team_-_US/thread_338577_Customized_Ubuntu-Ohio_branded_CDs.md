Lightscribe is monochromatic, but I believe you meant the *media* is expensive. It doesn't use any ink as it etches the image directly onto the special surface of Lightscribe media. I just looked on [Newegg]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.asp?Category=10&N=2010100071+1087408093&Submit=ENE&SubCategory=71&Description=lightscribe&Ntk=all") and they have Verbatim 30 packs for $15 - not too terrible, about 50 cents each, but not as cheap as plain old CD-Rs. 

When all is said and done though, it'd be a lot easier and cheaper than using labeling software, buying the labels, printing labels (unless you have a color laser, ink is expensive!), putting them on the CDs, etc. - it'd be a lot more than 50 cents / CD. I don't have a LightScribe burner or I'd offer to make the disks if that's the route we decide on, but I'd pitch in to buy some media.
