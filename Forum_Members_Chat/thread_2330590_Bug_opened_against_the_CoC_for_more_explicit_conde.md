### Post by Bucky Ball on 2016-09-13
I like this

> The Mozilla Project welcomes and encourages participation by everyone. It doesn&#8217;t matter how you identify yourself or how others perceive you: we welcome you. We welcome contributions from everyone as long as they interact constructively with our community, including, but not limited to people of varied age, culture, ethnicity, gender, gender-identity, language, race, sexual orientation, geographical location and religious views.

From [here]("https://www.mozilla.org/en-US/about/governance/policies/participation/"). IMHO, this is about modernising the guidelines for participation in the community, though, not 'condemning harassment' as far as I can see. Broader than that. Discrimination is not harassment, though they can play together. ;)

Most users, at least here, do not identify as anything except male or female. That's it. "I cross-dress" or "I'm building a bomb for religio-political reasons" doesn't generally come in to it when a cross-dresser or religious loon is trying to get their wireless happening, so a lot of the time, gender is the main issue or straight up disagreement between two or three users.

I think I have made my feelings about the gender thing at least clear in the distant past, but to reiterate: we should aspire to be a unisex space, all participants equal, and gender/religion/sexual orientation or anything else should be irrelevant in the support forums. Merge my thinking with excerpts of the quote above. Therefore, any form of discrimination/harassment of any user, regardless of gender or anything else and according to the guidelines, would be infringe-able, if that's a word ...

I'm happy to see this is being revisited after a long time and thanks for posting. All are equal in virtual space, at least if it's going to be productive and constructive.
