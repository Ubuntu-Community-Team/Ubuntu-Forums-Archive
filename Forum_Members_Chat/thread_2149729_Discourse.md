### Post by coffeecat on 2013-05-30
I'm struggling to work out what the point of it is. I'm trying to keep an open mind and intend to explore it more fully over the next few days when I have time - and am also trying to put aside my irritation at the way it was publicly announced. But from what I've seen so far, much of the discussion there seems to circle around trying to reproduce the functionality that we already have here with a VBulletin forum. As already mentioned - re-inventions and wheels. If there are *significant* features that it is impossible to provide on traditional forum software, then that would certainly be valuable. Are there? Perhaps someone could enlighten me.

As far as replacing vbulletin because it is proprietary, why? Cost? - I suspect the annual licence fee, a couple of hundred dollars, is a flea-bite compared with the cost of running and maintaining the servers. If because of some sort of open source evangelism, then I personally am not interested. If because of concerns about the future of vbulletin, then yes I am interested. From what I can make out, the company that bought up vbulletin a couple of years ago appears to have a poor customer reputation, which if true would be concerning. And the way they are taking vbulletin in VB5 looks like the Microsoft Office story all over again. You have a feature full piece of software and to keep your revenue stream growing, you have to add obscure bells and whistles. Which may mean that to get the security enhancements of newer versions we may need to commit to the hard work of upgrading to feature sets that we don't actually need. The VB3 -> VB4 upgrade earlier this year was hard work and stressful. So for those last two reasons alone, I'm prepared to have a look at open source alternatives, but with a healthy dose of scepticism.
