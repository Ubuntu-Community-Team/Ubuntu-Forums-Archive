### Post by Irihapeti on 2013-06-19
I started with 7.04, around the 3 month mark when most of the "worst release ever" threads have quietened down. Since then, I've seen the "worst release ever" threads about every single release. Funny thing is that 6 months later, you'll hear that [release -1] is rock solid and the ideal.

I took a brief foray into Fedora and Debian a couple of years back. I was having major "can't get the system to boot" problems with the Lucid development version on my main PC, so needed to look at alternatives in case it never got fixed. Once that got solved, I realised that I was setting up the alternatives - especially Debian - to look/behave as much like Ubuntu as possible. To me, I couldn't really see the point of that.

Around that time, I realised that I could do all sorts of interesting things by starting with a command-line install (or even debootstrap) and building up from there. To me, it makes more sense to start with a base system whose behaviour I kinda sorta know, instead of branching out into the wild unknown and having to ask questions on forums which are a lot less friendly than this one.

Should also mention that I was quite involved with Puppy at one stage. I quit that when the consequences of their moderation policy (or lack of it) started to look scary and as though they might affect me personally. For that reason, forum policy and behaviour play a big part in what I'll get involved in.

Currently, running 12.04 as main OS on PC and netbook, with a couple of 13.10 installs floating around to see what's happening.

I'm probably going to end up using Xubuntu in the near future, as my machines get too old for the latest. One has a weird graphics chip that won't do 3D, which I have to take into account. And, no, I simply don't have the money to upgrade at the moment.
