### Post by MAFoElffen on 2022-10-21
It's a "go". See attached screenshot of my Ubuntu Pro Dashboard.

The quote from the answer from Canonical
> 
"Everyone can register for the free personal subscription for up to 5 machines. If you register (for Ubuntu Pro) with an email (address) associated with the Official Ubuntu Community list, then this limit goes up to 50 (machines). Unfortunately, they will not see the increased limit in the UI, but they can continue attaching machines, and this can go up to 50 (instead of 5)."

As you can see from the attachment, under Machines,it says "5". "Active Machines" it says "7"...

To address Paddy's first question, "Active Machines" shows how many different/unique machines with an Active Ubuntu Pro subscription were actively polling within the last 24 hours. So you added a machine, then detached it. It shows up for in the dashboard for 24 hours.

On the second question, that will be a mess. I know with helping people get Ubuntu Pro issues resolved, you detach, generate a new 'machine_id" then attach with that new generated machine_id... Logically, someone would have to audit the machine_id's of their current / active machines for Ubuntu Pro to resolve what should not be there, taking up a subscription.
