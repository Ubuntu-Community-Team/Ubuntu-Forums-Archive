### Post by jsgotangco on 2009-05-31
If the intruder has your credentials (or the lack of credential check in your machine), then encryption won't be any use for you. 

If you have multiple credentials in your machine, then make sure the superuser access is limited to your use, and have home directories encrypted from each other. All modern operating systems have support of this either inherent in the OS or via a third party.

Unfortunately, computing is still an arcane art because of the complexity of certain concepts which most computer users won't even bother to learn/use.
