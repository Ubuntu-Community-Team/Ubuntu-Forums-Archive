### Post by Script Warlock on 2009-04-30
talking ics sa 9.04 is really simple, just edit the eth1 at punta ka sa ipv4 settings tapos method at piliin ang shared to other computers. at kung di pa rin makakonek ang mga workstations now try using iptables by hand..

go to terminal and be a root:

#ifconfig eth1 192.168.0.1
#iptables -t nat -A POSTROUTING -o eth0 -j MASQUERADE
#echo 1 >/proc/sys/net/ipv4/ip_forward
#apt-get install dnsmasq
#apt-get install ipmasq
#/etc/init.d/dnsmasq restart
#dpkg-reconfigure ipmasq (start after the networking has been started)
#ifconfig eth1 192.168.0.1
#iptables -t nat -A POSTROUTING -o eth0 -j MASQUERADE
#echo 1 >/proc/sys/net/ipv4/ip_forward
#gedit /etc/sysctl.conf ---- (hanapin mo line na to # Uncomment the next line to enable packet forwarding for IPv4
#net.ipv4.ip_forward=1 <------ delete the "#")

mamya na reboot after this:

network applet---- autoeth1-- edit ---- ipv4 setings ---- choose manual
at add then type 192.168.0.1 --- 255.255.255.0 ---- 192.168.0.1 ---ok

its time to reboot....after reboot syempre ang mga autoeth nagkany-kanya na set..at be sure to left clik the network icon tapos see if autoeth0 ang naging host ng autoeth1 kc pag hindi cgurado wala pa rin internet connection mga workstations mo. just choose autoeth0 to host..

Ano ang specs ng gagamitin mong server?
Ilan ang clients mo?

As I've said, sa LTSP, the more clients you have, the more powerful your server should be. LSTP is a good setup if you have low-end clients and a high end server since all application will run on the server and not on the client (meaning, all keyboard and mouse inputs are sent to the server to be processed and after processing, the server will send data to the client's display).

With DRBL

> DRBL uses PXE or etherboot, which is similar to Linux Terminal Server Project (LTSP), to boot the client machine. While LTSP is a centralized server, all the client machine users' access the LTSP server to run their applications on it. The client's keyboard and mouse are used to input whereas the client's monitor is used to display the results. This is great when useing a thin client. The server requirements must be increased when more than 20 or 30 clients are being used. On the other hand, DRBL uses NFS and NIS to provide boot services to the client machines. In essence, the DRBL server is just a NFS and NIS server. All users from all client machines just access the DRBL server to request files or authentication. Packages are loaded to the client machines and they use their own CPU and RAM for processing. A regular PC can be used as the DRBL server since it is only serving files and authenticating. The client machines, however, should be powerful enough to run the applications they need.
[http://drbl.sourceforge.net/faq/fine-print.php?path=./1_Common/01_DRBL_LTSP.faq#01_DRBL_LTSP.faq](http://drbl.sourceforge.net/faq/fine-print.php?path=./1_Common/01_DRBL_LTSP.faq#01_DRBL_LTSP.faq)

DRBL, you don't need a powerful server since the client will run the applications. But you have to have a standard client with DRBL (not low-end systems), but no need for a high end server.

It is completely up to you kung alin gagamitin, you have to assess first kung anong applications ang gagamitin mo, how many clients you have, what are the specs, and what is the spec of your server..

*Here's a little unsolicited advice. Though I believe in the principle MYOB, I hope I could impart to you something that came from my experience. For what it's worth: 

Business is a culmination of technical as well as financial aptitude. Each business is like a person. It has its own personality and it has its own way of doing things. However, one thing that applies generally is that business is about making a profit. I know it sounds a little bit cold and harsh - pero etymologically, if you remove "profit making" from a business - it ceases to be one. It becomes a foundation, a religion (depending on who you ask) it becomes a charitable organization or whatever. Without profit it is not a business.

In my own experience, business sometimes can be an extension of who we are. Henceforth most small entrepreneurs extend themselves through their business. They extend their personality, they extend their way of doing things, they extend themselves so that the business is "them". 

You are your business. It is your hobby, it is your passion, it is your direction - it is you.

Sometimes, take note, "sometimes", this however, could deflect us from our goal of running a sustainable business. Prime case is Gerry Yang of Yahoo! Corp. Yahoo! is Mr. Yang's baby. The whole dynamic has however been affected by the fact that Yahoo! has other shareholders who do not necessarily share Yang's passion, enthusiasm, sentiments... - to them Yahoo! is not "them". That is why, when they thought that Yang's business move in rejecting Microsoft is not "profitable" is not "feasible" and is not "sustainable" they took it upon themselves that they install a CEO who shares the same perspective: that Yahoo! is a business and that therefore they should care about making it profit the most in terms of efficiency, in terms of process, in terms of the bottom line...

Now, going back to your concern. It is a financial concern brought about by a technical concern. The technical concern being an equipment is not functioning because it's parts are defective, not functioning, not doing what it is supposed to do. The financial concern is - replacing all those defective components is going to put a hole in your pocket.

Simply put, **sometimes the best solution is the simplest solution**. It is your call. I believe that your medium of exchange is primarily time. For you to earn your customers should have "time" to lease your equipment to do whatever it is that they need to do. The more "time" that is wasted the more it becomes unprofitable.
