### Post by gbutalid on 2008-05-27
It would be better if you teach them something about FOSS that is related to your/ their jobs (programming/ development).  I had Math and Com Sci friends in college who were primarily Windows users but tend to shift to Ubuntu or Fedora when they need to program something.  

If they're not up for Linux then show them that you can run Windows in a virtual machine in Ubuntu. Or how to have a dual boot laptop, how to partition your drive for this, etc.

Well, this will boil down to how you are actually using Ubuntu in your work. You could show how your overall workflow is improved by using FOSS apps in Ubuntu.

### Post by dominiquec on 2008-07-02
The usual questions people ask when it comes to Linux (or any new OS):

1) Games: will it play my games?
2) Multimedia: will it play my MP3s and movies?
3) Hardware: drivers for my printer, scanner, etc.
4) Viruses and Spyware: anong klaseng antivirus ang gagamitin ko?
5) Additional software: where do I get my additional software? how do I install?
6) Office productivity: paano yung Microsoft Word files ko?

And I think from there you have a good launching point.

Are these trainees going into software dev?  Might be helpful to introduce them to the tools early on? (Eclipse, Komodo, etc.)
