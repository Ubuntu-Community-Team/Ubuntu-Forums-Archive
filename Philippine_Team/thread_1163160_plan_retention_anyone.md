### Post by GeorgeVita on 2009-05-20
Hi  **tagabukid** and **jsgotangco**,

although I am not in Philippine, I would like to share with you my experience: The last 14 months I am using +mobile -broadband (it is more mobile than broadband) as my ONLY internet connection for business and personal use.

I live in Athens Greece, with a population of 3-4 million. We all have at least one GSM phone, 3 major 3G/HSPA providers and a very good network (up to 7.2Mbits/s 4-5 bars signal).

The actual speed differs within the day from 50 to 1500 KBits/s. An average "good condition" gives you a download rate of 30-50KBytes/s (any site) reaching the 200-250KBytes/s downloading only from the provider's mirror. Cost is around 30EURO (35USD) per month for a 5GB data traffic. To be within 5GB I do not download Video, Mp3 (neither using streaming Radio, etc).

The above status is today! One year before the rate was compared with PSTN or at better with an ISDN line! Networks are always "upgrated". Do not forget to *call technical support of your provider* and polite state your problem! Especially when you are paying extra money for the data service. Possibly they could raise thee data to voice ratio, or check the signal to your area. Note also that most times (at home 100%) I am using an USB extension cable to move the 3G modem closer (or out) of my window! Better signal, less radiation.
