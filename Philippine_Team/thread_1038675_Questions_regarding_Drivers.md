### Post by jsgotangco on 2009-01-13
That's a fallacy. The Linux kernel does not have ALL the drivers of hardware out there. If it did, its the only system that we'll use.

Drivers are part of the kernel and the major hardware has some form of support, either official or reverse engineered. Not all hardware drivers are official drivers. A lot of it are really reverse engineered for lack of documentation and stuff. But in a way, its true that the Linux kernel has more driver support than a stock Windows installation (or even OS X).
