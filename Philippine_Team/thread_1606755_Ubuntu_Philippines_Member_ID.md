### Post by angheloko on 2010-10-28
I'm 30/70 (leaning towards the negative side of things).

The idea of having these ID's and the *possible *stuff that comes with it (privileges, recognition, etc) and the feeling of being recognized as a member of a group or family is OK and all, especially if the group is as awesome as this.

But, somehow, it does feel out-of-place in a world where openness is king and where anonymousity is common.

To add in, the image of Ubuntu (and Linux, and to an extent - the OSS world) may be reflected (directly or not) by whoever is using the ID. There's no one stopping anyone from using the IDs, so yeah, it can and will be abused, whether by members, by posers, or by anybody who just finds the ID appealing.

Also, in a way, it might feel restricting for people outside the community who are looking for other OS alternatives, i.e. Windows community doesn't have these ID's, how come your community does.

So yeah, I'm just bringing my concerns forward. Overall, the idea sounds good. Better get those guidelines as suggested previously, if this is going to push forward.

The Philippine Flag, as per R.A.1696

The flag should not be lowered to salute or accolade any person or object.
The flag should not be used as a household linen, example table cloth, drapery, covering for various objects and other interior embellishments.
The flag should not be worn as a **uniform or costume.**
The Philippine flag should not be displayed under ** any picture or painting**, below any platform facing up horizontally, or in cockpits, night clubs, pubs, discotheques, casinos, or any other places of vice or places where frivolity prevails&#8221;
There should not be any sought of untidiness in the form of drawing, design, imprint or any such marks on the flag.
The flag should not be printed on any articles of merchandise such as napkins, handkerchiefs, cushions.
The flag should not be displayed as a part of any advertisement.
The flag should not be displayed in front of offices or buildings that are occupied by non Filipino citizens&#8221;.

ISA PA 
Section 10 of RA 8491 states that when the flag is displayed on a wall during peacetime, the blue field is to the **observers' left**
