### Post by Samhain13 on 2009-07-24
I was introduced to Open Source when I (self-) studied web design some time around 2000. I encountered some discussions then that revolved around copyright, when searching for scripts and HTML code to learn from. I didn't take much interest in Open Source at the time, thinking that it was only for programmers (I was just a graphic designer then and knew nothing outside of drawing, painting and using graphics applications).

I was reintroduced to Open Source when I got employed by a small company in 2004. My direct superior was a FOSS freak! That was the first time I used Firefox; it was also the first time I saw what The GIMP was and what a Linux desktop looked like (my boss had a Red Hat box). Although at the time, I worked primarily in Windows XP and using apps like Adobe Photoshop, Flash and Dreamweaver.

I didn't stay long in that company but that boss and I stayed in touch. And when Ubuntu 6.10 came out, he was the one who encouraged me to try it. The rest is history.
