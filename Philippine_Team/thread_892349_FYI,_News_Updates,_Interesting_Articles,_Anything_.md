> **Debian Turns 15 Years Old**
Submitted by srlinuxx on Sat, 08/16/2008 - 06:21.

The most popular and stable GNU/Linux operating system Debain has turned 15 today. In these last 15 years Debian has established itself as the most stable operating system. Most of the popular GNU/Linux distributions, like Ubuntu and Knoppix, are based on Debian.

All the versions of Debian are named after characters from the film Toy Story. There are always three versions one is stable, other is testing and third one being experimental. Since experimental version is the most bleeding edge and a bit unstable so it has been named after Sid, the emotionally unstable next-door neighbour boy who regularly destroyed toys.

By Miguel Helft

Google applied for incorporation as a business 10 years ago Thursday, according to a timeline supplied by the company. The application was accepted on Sept. 7, which is Sunday.

In that decade, the search engine company has quickly emerged as the most successful business on the Web, and many expect it to dominate the next era of computing as thoroughly as Microsoft dominated the era of personal computers.

Microsoft&#8217;s age: 33
