### Post by arscariosus on 2011-07-23
I do not have my own printer so I've made it a practice, ever since OpenOffice was the default productivity suite for Ubuntu, to export the document to PDF before going to the computer shop to have it printed. I'm doing this to preserve the formatting of the document.

Until yesterday, while I was in the midst of midterms week cramming, I used LibreOffice for the first time then exported my paper to PDF. Much to my dismay, the Adobe Reader on the computer shop at Morayta rendered the document with the correct formatting sans the letters: they were replaced by asterisks. LOL.

It was a good thing that I have my laptop with me so I just gave the .doc copy from LibreOffice, as expected the layout was screwed, but I have to submit the paper immediately so I had no choice.

Any idea what had happened? I don't remember experiencing it with OpenOffice before.

### Post by Hagar Delest on 2011-07-25
You can run OOo and LibO in parallel if you use the vanilla versions, it may help if one can do the job that the other one fails to do. See [[Ubuntu] Installing OOo on Debian and Co.](http://user.services.openoffice.org/en/forum/viewtopic.php?f=16&t=68)

Else, it may be that one: [[Issue] Writer replaces quotes with weird characters?](http://user.services.openoffice.org/en/forum/viewtopic.php?f=7&t=33582) But if you use to work in .doc, better avoid that and use native ODF (.odt) and export in .doc only when needed.
