Conversation format: Sayo ba to?
Proper written format: Sa iyo ba ito?

Sa'yo ba ito? / Sa'yo ba 'to?
Sa 'yo ba ito? / Sa 'yo ba 'to?

The problem is, the development of the Filipino language happens too fast.  During my time, "Sa iyo ba ito?" is the proper / professional written format.  "Sa'yo ba ito?" is a format for poetry.  Today it's different.  But still, conversation Filipino should be written correctly if one wants to be professional.

So instead of "Sayo ba to?" it should be "Sa'yo ba 'to?"  A simple change that most Filipinos doesn't care about when writing / typing in Filipino ;)

Umm… "Tagalog" is _not_ a dialect, it is a language.  ;)

The Filipino (national) language is mainly composed of Tagalog language, there are very few words that were added from the other Philippine languages like Cebuano and Bikolano (to name a few) and even less when it comes to dialects, and far less from "foreign languages".

I'll take this moment to clarify the difference between a language and a dialect, it seems there is confusion between the two.

Filipino, Tagalog, Cebuano, Bikolano, Waray, Tausug, Hiligaynon, Iloko, Pangasinense, Kapampangan, etc.  these are all (Philippine) languages.

Caviteño, Batangueño, Manileño - these are dialects of the Tagalog language.
Central Bikolano, Buhi-Daraga, Iriga Bikolano, Pandan - these are dialects of the Bikolano language.

And so on.  One will notice also that dialects were mostly named after its people as much as regional languages are.  So in the Philippines there are 3 levels - National Language > Regional Language > Provincial Dialect.

For the difference between Filipino and Tagalog, well, long story short:
1) Filipino - A B C D E F G H I J K L M N Ñ N&#771;g O P Q R S T U V W X Y Z; whereas Tagalog - A Ba Ka Da E Ga Ha I La Ma Na Nga O Pa Ra Sa Ta U Wa Ya
2) Filipino: Titser; Tagalog: Guro
Filipino: lenggwahe; Tagalog: wika
Filipino: klasrum; Tagalog: silid-aralan
and so on…
