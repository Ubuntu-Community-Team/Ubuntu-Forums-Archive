This is a classic argument/opinion against localization. But remember, this is something that is meant to improve the language. Why is Google in the Philippines in Filipino? Why can't an operating system? You have a good point on the spell check/grammar check, but you have to start with a proper translation of the system to glue together the components. Why do you think are we posting in this forum mostly in English? Because we do not have a defined translation of a lot of things, especially technical.

Ironically, a lot of people who are in favor of localization are not based locally, and feel that it is a way of giving back, or who were born outside the country and looking for a way to learn the language.

As with all open source projects and efforts, it is voluntary and therefore not being forced to everyone. For some, a stab at localization allows learning of system internals because a lot of message strings and components are intertwined with each other, along with learning how software development and documentation works.
