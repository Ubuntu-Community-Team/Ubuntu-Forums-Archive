### Post by _duncan_ on 2008-08-31
It may be prudent to take a 2nd look at the P600K/month figure mentioned. For all we know, they may have just migrated all their computers to Vista and MS-Office 2007, kaya mataas ang software costs for that year.

If that's the case, then migration to FOSS / Linux need not be immediate. It can be timed and phased to coincide with the next major software upgrade, which is probably 2-3 years from now. I would suggest introducing OpenOffice as an alternative to MS-Office first, coz my hunch is this is where the bulk of the costs are coming from. My info are a bit outdated, but last I checked, MS-Office costs a lot more than Windows itself.

Since office staff are also part of the migration, it might also be prudent to take stock of what applications they are using. Most schools have custom-built payroll, grading, enrollment, accounting systems, etc. that are Microsoft-centric. These specialized software will cause migration problems down the line since it may be difficult to find FOSS equivalents.

### Post by eilu on 2008-09-01
I think the bigger problem would be the teachers or other staff. Most kids are computer savvy and can quickly adapt, particularly if the GUI is familiar or similar enough (e.g., OOo/MS Office), and they won't be scared to poke around and try a few things, experiment a little.

The teachers and staff, in general, may be more wary and less computer savvy. Takot na may masira or madaling ma-overwhelm, particularly for the older ones who did not necessarily grow up with computers.

What you can do to ease the transition is to skin things so they look familiar- maybe XP-ish ang look ng Ubuntu, with the 'start button' (ibaba ang menu bar), and have OOo save to .doc and other MS formats by default (so they can easily open files at home, assuming MS office/Windows sila sa bahay) then as they become more familiar, you can modify it again.
