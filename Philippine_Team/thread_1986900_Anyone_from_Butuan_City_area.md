I live in Cabadbaran about 45 minutes south of Butuan, I am an american, and I live very close to the beaches one of the above members mentioned. 

If you want to fish bring your own pole and tackle, because the idea of poles in Mindanao where we are are a bamboo stick with a rope and hook LOL most people here use electroshock in the rivers or the nets in the big ships. I am sure you can get a pole here and others surely have them but so far in my past year and a half living here bamboo is what i see.

Housing, Never NEVER NEVER purchase land thru a broker, NEVER. Always talk directly to the owner and get their true price. Especially if the broker is friends of your wifes family, close or acquiescence, and not even if the broker is the cousin of your wife, if they are a broker, tell them you want to talk directly to the property owner. Always get a clean and clear title, under no circumstances allow a seller to sell you property without a CLEAN tax declaration and a clean title, If they dont pay their taxes the past 20 years and you buy the property, you will then be responsible for the taxes they did not pay. If the title says the land is an inheritance and their are like 5 brothers and sisters on the title to be given the property, walk away, getting a clean title on that property could take up to a year.  

Computer parts and everything is kinda easy to get, if you go to Cagayan or Davao you could probably find what you need if butuan does not have it, Davao will absolutely have everything we can get in America. Its a hefty trip, so maybe like 5 or 6 hours from you, But you can find things. Butuan does have plenty of computer parts, My wife and I are into arcade cafes and we are making a trip to Butuan this weekend and visiting maybe 8 computer shops in butuan so getting your computer parts there are OK. 

If you are retired, I am not, Not working is super ok, besides that getting hired as an american here is almost impossible unless your in the technology and can do the work behind the scenes. The Filipinos automatically see foreign and assume we are all super rich and have money, and they will take advantage of you every chance you give them. 

When shopping always ask for a discount. ALWAYS :) because if its a wet market or a bungalo shop you can bet when they see you they have added 100 pesos to whatever it is you are about to purchase. I solved that problem the first month i was here, I only shop where the prices are clearly marked on the package. And be sure to see the price, because I and my friends watched as a employee of a shop tried to remove the price off the package and add 100 pesos to the price for me. I laughed and picked the one up next to the one she had in her hand and said Ill buy this one, and pointed to the price tag LOL Not everyone is like that, I now have been here long enough and have business relationships with a few places, my bike shop, my building supply store, and other places, and I will only shop with those businesses, because I know bthe and they know me, and we have a great business relationship together. 

This next section absolutely does not apply to all Filipino people. Just like america you have some good and some bad, but I especially wanted to share this experience with you. 

Yes yes yes learn the language, most Filipinos do not know I understand tagalog and bisayan, and they will directly insult you to your face and smile like they are saying something nice, They will make fun of you and talk about how they are gonna scam you directly in front of you. Its nice to know the language but not let them know you know it. I have a pretty good amount of patience, so i dont blow up when they say rude things to my face, shake my hand and smile like they are giving me a compliment. I personally think it is funny and only belittles them and lets me know who is good people and who is not. I also do not let them know I understand the language, once I do that my cover is blown. LOL By doing what I do you quickly find out who are people you can trust and who is not, because they will just insult you with no problems if they think you do not understand the language. 

Most Filipino people want to be hospitable and they actually are, they will give you food a place to rest, take very good care of you, and then you have the ones always looking for a quick buck. Most Filipinos do not understand 100 pesos today is not better than 100,000 over a years time. What they have now is most important to them. And then ther eis always some very good, wonderful people, you unfortunate job is sifting thru the people, finding the good apples and throwing out the bad ones, after a year and a half, I have finally accomplished that. 

But to be honest this is not much different than america, we too have good people and very bad people, I personally like life in the Philippines, regardless of its negative things, I think the positive outweigh the negative, I think a lot of people here only need a small push in the right direction, and I personally would love to be able to give my community where I live something more than they could accomplish on their own. As Americans we have the ability to change a whole town, a whole community, with very little effort. We can bring happiness to so many Filipinos and it will barely cost us anything. I just wash away the bad stuff and keep pushing forward, i will not allow a few bad people to keep me from making so many people happy.
