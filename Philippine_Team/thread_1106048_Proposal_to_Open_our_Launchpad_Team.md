### Post by daxumaming on 2009-07-05
> **badrra said:**
> 1. Ang intindi ko sa launchpad is  that software applications are developed, debugged, improved, etc. thru collaboration of launchpad members all over the world. Is that correct?

Correct

> **badrra said:**
> 2. So it follows na merong series of debugging and testing until you arrive at a stable software.

Correct. But please understand that this is not an Ubuntu nor a Canonical initiative. Most projects hosted at Launchpad are owned by individuals and groups remotely associated with Ubuntu, Canonical, and Launchpad. **So the debugging and testing would depend on the individual or group.**


> **badrra said:**
> 3. Finally if the software is stable and it becomes a final release, does it form part of Ubuntu? Thru Synaptic

Again, this depends on the project authors - if they wish it uploaded to the Universe repository, then it'll be available on your Synaptic. If not, they can simply create a PPA which you can add to your apt config file - which makes it available on your Synaptic. But sometimes, they skip it altogether and just make the source code available.


> **badrra said:**
> Am I correct in assuming that launchpad is purely for programmers and developers?

Not only... it is also used by translators and Local Community (LoCo) groups. Groups have mailing list too that so **members can collaborate on anything**. And if Launchpad developers add an option to upload pictures and update status, hey, it can even be Facebook for Developers.

> **badrra said:**
> Am not too sure about the concept but it sounds good. Too bad I am no longer practicing the art of programming so if this is so then I guess this isn't for me.

The one thing you need to figure out for yourself is **your needs**. I've seen writers _(non-developers)_ use Launchpad to host and revision their articles. I've seen at least two use Blueprint for planning and Answers to collaborate on it. So although Launchpad's designed for software developers, if it helps you out - then why not use it?
