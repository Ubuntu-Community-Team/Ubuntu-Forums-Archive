### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2011-09-02
I did something similar to my netbook.  I run OSX on my ASUS EEEPC 1015PE so I went with the apple inlay.  As soon as I finished doing it to my netbook, I wanted to make an Ubuntu inlay for my other laptop.
[IMG]http://img847.imageshack.us/img847/412/dscf5442.jpg[/IMG]

Not to bag on anyone, but I think the way I did it was a little better than putting a vinyl sticker directly onto the screen.  I bought a screen lid from an old iBook on ebay for like $10 and then cannibalized the clear plastic apple inlay from it.  the cool part was that the screen lid was aluminum so I was able to use it as a pretty good routing template.  After I knocked out the biggest holes I did the rest of the "fine tuning" with some small mill files and sand paper.  The end result wasn't exactly perfect, so I painted the bottom lip of the apple inlay with black acrylic paint and filled the gaps between the inlay and the case with super glue.  After I let it dry, I took a DA sander to the lid and made everything flush.  It's a work in progress at the moment, but I'm going to be wet sanding and polishing the lid in a few days.  I'll probably start with 800 grit, then 1000, 1500 and 2000 grit wet-or-dry followed by some polishing compound and my electric buffer.  I don't plan on spraying clear coat on it because spray paint takes months to fully harden.  Plastic should buff out pretty easily.

### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2011-09-04
Yes, but... where there is a dremel, there is a way!  The leaf part on the apple was pretty hard, I won't lie.  It came out pretty good in the end.  I definitely learned some good lessons doing this project and if I decide to do another computer (either an apple logo or an ubuntu logo) I will definitely take a lot more time on it.  Sure it came out looking good but I really only spent about 2 hours total doing the mod.  Had I taken a bit more time and done things a little bit more carefully, I would have had a lot less touch up to do in the end.  I dremeled out pretty much to the shape of the apple and then cleaned everthing up with mill files and sand paper and I ended up with small gaps in some places.  I should have cut the rough shape out leaving a bit of room to work with and then fine tuned the shape with the files.  I was being impatient though.

The big draw back of doing this with an Ubuntu logo is that you have to fabricate one yourself (probably out of plexiglass).  The apple logo was premanufactured.  :D
