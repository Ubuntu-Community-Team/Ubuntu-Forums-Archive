Computer Gaming Apps are proprietary or commercial products, but most of online gaming nowadays, which introduced by koreans and other asian game developers are free install and use with paid service/per service like Ran Online, Rangarok, etc...

Ubuntu and linux on the other hand... is an open source product... note that there's a very big difference between a freeware and an open source.

Kahit na free to install and use yung mga current online game ay hindi pwedeng gawing open souce ang mga ito dahil sa threat ng abuse on client and even on its server part.

GNU-GPL both version 2 and 3 ay bagsak na agad yung mga games na yun... pero may possibility na ma-fork sa linux / ubuntu ang mga games ... pero most kasi ng library na ginagamit nila currently are proprietary at ready made na sa MS... Kung hindi naman GNU-GPL ang license ng games pwede rin... treat as a freeware / closed-source product with paid service. Pero may problem pa!!!

Kasi sa drivers pa lang may problem na... Xgl / openGL  are still in experimental status and buggy , AIGLX .. dumb pa rin, ... maybe support novell's open drivers development or fedora's open audio para mag-step forward ang gaming sa linux / ubuntu. So makikita nyong hindi lang developer ng game ang ayaw gumawa ng game sa linux...kasi pati mismo mga hardware providers... hindi gaanong supported ang linux or not giving a damn.

Well currently ubuntu and other linux distro are working well on the other side of computing...in business aspect... pero for gaming masyado pang maaga...
