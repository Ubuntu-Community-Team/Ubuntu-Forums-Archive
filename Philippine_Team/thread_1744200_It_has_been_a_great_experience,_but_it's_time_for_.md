### Post by franchoy on 2011-04-30
This will be a sad day for me, as I decided to leave Ubuntu and migrate to Linux Mint. It is sad because I had loved Ubuntu for a long time. It was a love at first site experience when I tried Ubuntu 8.04, it was the first Linux distro that I thought was very user friendly. 

But a sad fall for Ubuntu and Canonical, as they decided to introduce Unity. The idea was good, and so was its vision, but the product failed. Maybe Unity would work if Ubuntu doesn't limit its users to tweak their interface, I couldn't add items on my panel anymore. Linux is supposed to be customizable, and Ubuntu/Unity removed that from the user. 

So now, after searching for a better predecessor for my laptop, I decided to shift to Linux Mint, and base on the articles that I have read, Linux Mint does not have the plan to use any "shell" on their interface and they got me for that. 

Maybe in the future releases of Ubuntu, I will try to look at their developments but for now it is goodbye.

P.S. "This is maybe my last note, using Ubuntu; and yes this is with Unity (it really sucks)."

### Post by _duncan_ on 2011-05-01
> **Samhain13 said:**
> Laicoms, still wondering whether it's possible to uninstall Unity and replace it with Gnome (or KDE for that matter)... come to think of it, I might as well try it myself. Hehehe!

My understanding of Natty is that classic mode is based on gnome 2, with Unity built on top of it. If you are OK with the older gnome 2, you don't have to replace anything. Just chose Ubuntu Classic on the log-in screen and you'll be greeted with the familiar desktop environment of previous releases.

Gnome 3, however is a totally different beast. I did not try it myself, but talks during the development phase of Natty has it that putting in Gnome 3 in Natty, while possible, constitutes a point of no return. There is no way to bring back Unity after installing gnome 3 without reinstalling.

Another option if your graphics card gives you problems with the default Unity is to install the 2D version of Unity, available as ppa. Didn't have to take this route myself since I was able to get 3D Unity working with all my test computers.

But, until that time, I believe that the present intended changes will see a sizeable proportion of the formerly Ubuntu faithful, myself amongst them, seeking pastures new.

Like the cloud computing that is supposed to sound the death knell of the HDD, I think rumours of the demise of the desktop are being vastly exaggerated.  I have no doubt that both netbooks/tablet and cloud storage options will have a future, but do you think I'd trust a corporation with my data?  Not likely!

I shall. like many others, cling to my desktop, and for that we shall require a decent and intuitive desktop environment, not a Mac wannabe.  Though I'm referring to Unity here, much of the above applies to Gnome 3 too.

I have tired 11.04 and Unity, and found it a depressing experience.
