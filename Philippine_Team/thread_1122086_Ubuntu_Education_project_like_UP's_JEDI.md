### Post by daxumaming on 2009-04-17
I've been holding off in replying to this thread but there is a need to set things straight.

Jerome is in the better position to explain what our LoCo is for, but I'll give it a shot.

The Ubuntu-PH LoCo team is **just** a group of people with the same interest - the Ubuntu Operating System. It also acts as a support group for new users of Ubuntu and from time to time, an advocacy group.

Now let's discuss what this team is not:
1) It is not a religion nor a religious cult
2) It is not an officially sanctioned organization by SEC
3) It is not an established institution

And of course, we need to list down our resources:
1) individual talents
2) individual efforts
3) individual spare time

Then, what we don't have
1) official recognition outside Ubuntu
2) financial support
3) office

And lastly, what we've accomplished
1) Various seminars for schools and private institutions both here and abroad
2) Parties and Meetups
3) Trainings in the usage of Ubuntu

If we take a good look at what we've accomplished so far, you'll notice that every single one of them **is an individual effort with very minimal resources**. Nhatz' conversion of ACLC into Ubuntu, my Trainings at Daet, and Jerome's Seminar at Korea. There's no Philippine contingent who did it - but individuals who donated their time for Ubuntu.

Our geo-location's one factor that prevents us from truly organizing - and yet it's the same reason why Ubuntu in the Philippines has spread - take a good look at Yolynne Medina's accomplishments in Mindanao, an amazing feat that no one in this forum can even think of surpassing. And she's doing it with the spirit of Open Source - FOR FREE.

I'm currently in touch with an upstream dev (Debian) regarding the abolition of Tagalog translation and retention of Filipino to prevent redundancy of work. I am yet to hear from the owner of Filipino translation group(pinoy101) though but will ask the Translators group to assign Filipino translation to our group if pinoy101 fails to respond after a few days.
