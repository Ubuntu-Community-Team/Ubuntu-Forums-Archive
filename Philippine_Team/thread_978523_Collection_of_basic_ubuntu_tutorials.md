### Post by ragadanga63 on 2008-11-15
> **superpink99 said:**
> ok i just installed ubuntu on my laptop, and the graphical interface is really great but i have problems with installing softwares and the like, i realize its not like windows where you just click and follow steps then its ok.

so anyone there who could lend me there tutorial notes or maybe someone there can direct me to a good site for a tutorial?


Ubuntu or Linux is not Windows.  You may have to do a little reading.  Try [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/)

For software installation, you may use Synaptic Package Manager (Click System>Administration>Synaptic Package Manager or you may use the Add/Remove Program feature.  Unlike Windows where one has to download the installer file, and then run it, Ubuntu uses the above features where available softwares are listed and will do the downloading and installation for you (easier than Windows really.).

Some audio and video files may not play in Ubuntu because  in keeping with the spirit of Free and Open Source Software, the proprietary media codecs for those files are not installed in Ubuntu by default.  However, to make things easier for you, everytime you open such media, Ubuntu will give you a prompt to ask you if you want the codecs installed.  For this, you need an internet connection and everything will be done automatically when you give it a go.

Don't be discouraged by those little things. Many of us here passed the same route.  A little reading and gung-ho attitude will go a long way in making your Ubuntu experience enjoyable.
