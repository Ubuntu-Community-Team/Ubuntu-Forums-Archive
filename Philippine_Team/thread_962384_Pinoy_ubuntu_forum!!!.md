### Post by Jucato on 2008-10-30
Una sa lahat, maraming salamat sa inyong aktibong pagsuporta sa Ubuntu-PH, lalo na dito sa forums na ito. Kung maaari lamang ay pahintulutan nyo akong gumamit ng wikang Ingles sa kadahilanang mahirap ipahayag ang ilang bagay ukol sa paksang ito sa wikang Filipino.

[waits for objections...]

Ok, wala, sige. Go! :popcorn:


A little history...

We just very recently moved to ubuntuforums.org (henceforth, UF) this year. Before that we had our own forum in our own website. The forum was a Drupal module (which was, IMHO, quite weird as a forum) as part of the whole Ubuntu-PH website, which was in Drupal. However, after quite a long time, we ran into some problems. Eventually, after some discussion, we finally decided to do away with the separate forum and use the facilities provided by UF instead. Let me share some of the factors that we considered:

1. Maintainability: This is one of, if not the most important factor. The amount of spam and spambots that we got in Drupal simply overwhelmed the amount of **real** traffic/conversation that we had in the forum. Having our own forum meant that we will have to do a lot of the heavy work ourselves. While this is certainly not a technical or theoretical impossibility, experience has shown us that it wasn't effective nor efficient, because of reason #2.

2. Resources: Manpower most specially. Guys, let's face the fact: Ubuntu-PH is not really a solid organization. It's more like a peer group. "Barkada", kung baga. We simply do not have the people who are going to be there to **always** maintain the forum. Sure, some people might come up once in a while to volunteer. That's more than welcome. But we cannot be 100% sure that they will be always there. At least in the case of UF, we're sure that it will exist far longer than any separate forum. Of course, volunteers are still welcome to work on our website (when it goes back up again).

3. Knowledgebase: Keeping the Ubuntu-PH forum as a subforum of UF means that users will have access to more information faster and more conveniently. They don't have to have a separate account to post, nor to search two different forum to search for answers. 

4. Official LoCo Guidelines: Ubuntu LoCo's (Local Communities) have some guidelines. One of which is to use Ubuntu-provided resources as much as possible instead of going off on your own (unless you already have one and want to keep that). The reason behind this is so that the LoCo can focus more on doing more substantial things and less on discussing about infrastructure, websites  and whatnot. The time we spent having to discuss this topic is proof enough of that.

If and when Ubuntu-PH becomes big and solid enough to really push for our own forum, then why not. But please consider the four points I mentioned above and weigh them against your reasons for wanting a separate forum. I haven't encountered yet any case that makes it necessary to have a "Filipino-run" forum. Of course, we're open to suggestions and we might have overlooked some important cases, so we'd love to hear from you. But at this point and in the very near future, we're sticking to UF.
