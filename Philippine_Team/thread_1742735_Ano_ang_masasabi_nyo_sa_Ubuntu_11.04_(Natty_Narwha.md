### Post by _duncan_ on 2011-04-30
Tested Natty on 3 different machines with varying levels of success. Most of the issues had to do with graphics cards.

I was pleasantly surprised though with tests on an ancient Sempron 2400+ computer with 1 GB RAM and an equally ancient nVidia 5500 graphics card. As expected, first boot defaulted to classic mode (w/o effects). I was presented 2 choices for hardware driver: the closed-source 173 driver from nVidia and the experimental open-source driver (Nouveau?).

The 173 driver allowed me to activate compiz effects using the classic mode (with effects), but failed miserably under Unity.

The experimental 3D driver, however, not only run compiz well under classic mode, but was somewhat successful running Unity. The 1 glitch is that the icons on the left panel doesn't show up, but the text descriptions appear when the pointer is hovering over an icon. The button is fully functional however once I click on the invisible icon.

Resource usage is not much different from past ubuntu versions. System monitor shows 230 MB RAM usage and approx. 10-15% CPU usage immediately after boot, running Unity with some Compiz effects enabled after a vanilla install.
