### Post by Samhain13 on 2010-07-08
Here's something you can try:

1. Install VLC if you haven't installed it yet.
2. Go to Media > Convert/Save; a new dialogue window will pop-up.
3. Choose the file you want to open (File tab, Add...).
4. Click the Convert/Save button at the bottom; new dialogue window will pop-up.
5. Click the "Browse" button beside the "Destination file" field and make sure you're in your home directory. For now, use the name, "myvideo.avi"; click "Save" in the browser window.
7. Check "Display the output".
8. In the Settings part, click the new profile button; it's levelled with "Profile" and it's the right-most button, beside the **X**; new dialogue window.
9. Put a profile name (for now, use "Video - zilu54").
10. Per tab:

10.1. Encapsulation, choose AVI.
10.2. Video Codec, check "Video" and "Keep original video track". Then choose "MPEG-1" in Codec and "0.5" in Scale.
10.3. Audio Codec, check "Audio" and "Keep original audio track". Then choose "44100" in Sample Rate.
10.4. Subtitles, do nothing.

Click save (will take you back to the "Convert" window).

11. Click "Start" at the bottom.
12. Watch the video in full and stop it when it's done. Close VLC immediately (to prevent disasters) and look for your .avi file in your home directory.
