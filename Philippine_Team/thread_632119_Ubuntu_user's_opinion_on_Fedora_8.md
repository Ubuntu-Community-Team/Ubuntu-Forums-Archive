### Post by daxumaming on 2007-12-07
Ok, back to topic.. can Fedora compete with Ubuntu? Here's my 2 opinions:

1) Package Management
The last Fedora I've tried is FC6. Back then, Yum is a little bit behind Apt. If I remember correctly, I think Yum broke some packages before which caused Eric Raymond to migrate to Ubuntu. I may be wrong on this - too lazy to Google - but it's that or someone uploaded a package that broke something. Now I'm not sure how good Yum is on FC8, I do hope it's better though.

2) DVD Install ISO vs. Installable Live CD and Shipit
Now this would depend on how fast your connection is. Some people would prefer to have all their applications on DVD, this way they don't have to bother downloading packages from the net. And then there are those who love the Installable Live CDs. I find it ironic though that those who have slower connection prefer the DVD ISO over the CD ISO. I'm thinking that they'd rather sacrifice a couple of days downloading the ISO and not worry about installing apps over the internet once installed. Why? 'coz all the apps they need is on the DVD. I can't say much for Ubuntu's CD ISO though, but I think Shipit CDs makes Ubuntu better than Fedora. Why? Because Ubuntu has managed to reach users without internet connection (or those still on dial-up) at home.
