[SIZE="4"]System Administrator[/SIZE]

- Technology Degree or equivalent with a year or 2 experience in implementing and maintaining open source software running on Linux. You should have the basic Linux system administration skills on the remote server without the use of graphical user interface tools
- Knowledge in Database implementation and backup, preferably with PostgreSQL.
- Experience with Apache httpd administration, plus points if you are familiar with reverse proxy and caching to load balance a cluster of web servers; if you have done NGINX before, that would be killer.
- Experience with proactive security and monitoring with Monit, Nagios, Systemtap, etc.
- If you have done work with AWS EC2 and S3, along with SQS, you definitely have an advantage.
- The position is based in Cebu, or the applicant is willing to be relocated. Expect some travel back and forth to Manila and Cebu in a regular basis, and probably in Hong Kong by Q2 2008.
- Certifications are a plus, but not required.
- Position is full-time

[SIZE="4"]Front Line Platform & Infrastructure Support[/SIZE]

- Technology Degree or equivalent with a year of experience in implementing and maintaining open source software running on Linux. You should have the basic Linux system administration skills on the remote server without the use of graphical user interface tools.
- new grads will be considered, depending on level of knowledge and job capability
- You will be responsible to provide immediate support to Morph Exchange customers via email or IM. Therefore, you should have good written communication skills as well as technical.
- OS X experience a plus as you are expected to give support to internal personnel with issues in their OS X environments.
- Position is full-time


[SIZE="4"]Inside Morph Labs[/SIZE]

- The office is located at the Ground Floor of the i1 Building at the Asiatown IT Park in Apas, Cebu City. If you are in Cebu, you know the place is also known for its coffee shops and hang outs.
- Work with a team also known for their contributions to open source. Employees are known contributors to upstream projects like Drupal, Ruby, Ruby on Rails, as well as distribution projects like Debian, Ubuntu and Fedora.
- MacBooks are standard issue - Yes its not Linux, but you can always use Linux with the servers and even virtualize your MacBook, so you are not missing anything.
- Remote work possibilities - if you are given the opportunity to work remotely, 3G or HSDPA equipment is standard issue.
