### Post by wersdaluv on 2008-07-17
Marami nang usapan tungkol dito sa ibang mga parte ng forum pero maganda siguro rin na pagusapan nating mga pinoy 'to.

Sa katunayan, KDE ang preferred ko dati kaso mas polished ang Ubuntu kaysa Kubuntu. Hindi ko naman gusto gumamit ng ibang distro. Nagustuhan ko na rin ang GNOME dahil ito ang mas matagal ko nang gamit. 

Mas gusto ko ang look 'n' feel ng KDE kaysa GNOME. Mas modern siya, mas maraming effects, at mas customizable. Malaki ring improvement ang Oxygen theme. Napaka-gandang icons, qt theme, kwin theme, at sounds.

Kung apps ang paguusapan, masaya ako dati sa KDE apps. Ayos naman ang Kopete. May webcam support, customizable smiley themes, customizable interface, at may group message support. Kung sa media players, maaring lamang ang Amarok at Kaffeine sa Rhythmbox at Totem. Well, kanya-kanyang preference yan. Kung gusto ninyong maraming ginagawa at maraming options, Amarok at Kaffeine. Rhythmbox at Totem naman kung gusto ninyong light lang at simple interface. Sa IRC clients, medyo patas naman para sakin. Kung sa web browsers, ayos ang Konqueror at Qt pa ang Opera pero syempre, no match pa rin sila sa Firefox 3. May sari-sariling pros and cons eh. Sa mga apps na di natin gaano madalas siguro gamitin, lamang ang KDE dahil sa K3b, Kooka (based on XSane pero ang pangit ng interface ng XSane), at Gwenview sa opinyon ko. Lamang naman ang GNOME sa gtk apps gaya ng GIMP at Gnome-do. Medyo patas naman kung apps ang usapan at pwede namang mag-run ng KDE o GNOME app sa kahit anong DE. Mas maganda lang na local app para sa interface consistency at konting libs lang ang paandarin.

Sa laban ng KDE at GNOME, ang masasabi ko lang ay kanya-kanyang preference lang yan eh. Mas preferred ko noon ang KDE pero mas sanay na ako ngayon sa GNOME. Isa pa, mas polished ang, at mas marami kang makukuhang support sa Ubuntu kaysa Kubuntu. 'Yon ang talagang mahalaga. Maganda man o hindi, Linux is reduced to Ubuntu lately eh.

### Post by Tuxoid on 2008-09-10
I am a Gnome user. I feel more productive in Gnome compared to KDE.
I also very much like it's default menu setup. Instead of chunking everything into a single menu (like most desktop UIs), they separate the menu into three categorical menus.

However, I will usually turn to KDE if I have an issue with my system. I've had more luck fixing issues in KDE than in Gnome.

As for Xfce..., I find Xfce difficult compared to Gnome and KDE. Chunking every utility and application under a menu labeled 'Applications', is correct in a technical sense, as these things are Applications realistically. However, due to the way in which some utilities are used, they seem less and less like applications, because they help the user perform tasks that are less elaborate than say a graphics editor application, or audio editing software.

I have never used CDE, but one interesting little fact about CDE, is that source model is Proprietary Software, but the name of the company/entity that made CDE is The Open Group.
