### Post by jsgotangco on 2008-09-15
The problem that comes out here is caused by the fact that Canonical ships the CDs which have a corresponding value despite given free of charge. So even though you got them for free, the local customs office can levy tax on it based on the value of the item being shipped. In Quezon City, if the value does not go beyond $50 USD, this is only charged for the flat customs rate.

I think the reason why they (QCPO) wants the recipient to claim for the item rather than being delivered is they want to centralize the  collection of the customs fee.

I decided not to get ShipIT CDs anymore as I do not want this inconvenience. Also, since I can download the ISOs I can burn as many copies as I want anyway.

### Post by Laibcoms on 2008-09-19
Philippine Post Office satellite offices...

1) Depends on the CUSTOMS personnel on-duty in your respective Post Office
2) Depends on their knowledge (especially English and the Law)
3) Depends on your surname (let's face it - palakasan system)
4) House delivery depends on the satellite offices - Makati Post Office doesn't deliver packages; while Mandaluyong does.

For ShipIt CDs, according to what I can remember from their package note, all recipients are ONLY to pay for the post office cost, no more and no less.

For Metro Manila, standard post office cost is PhP35.00 already (two years ago it was PhP30.00).  I am not sure how much it is outside of MM.  If you paid more than that, time to ask why.

In one of the replies, he has to pay for the package 'locker' fee, however, do ask why you have to, because you never asked for that 'locker' in the first place.

Finally, no importation tax and software tax, not a single centavo.

For #1 above, the Makati Post Office and Customs people are very much familiar with Ubuntu so no problems with me, I just paid PhP35 and I'm gone.
