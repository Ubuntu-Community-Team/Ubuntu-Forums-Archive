### Post by daxumaming on 2007-12-01
I've tried bayanihan, pero I'm not really impressed. Tama kayo, dami pa ring bugs. Pero I'm still hopeful especially now na nag-Debian sila.. but something's really preventing me from contributing to Bayanihan e.... ung focus nila wala sa distro and community.

Distro
- antagal bago maayos ung mga Bayanihan-specific bugs
- pag me updates sa Debian, tska lang magkaka-updates ang Bayanihan
- interface needs a lot more polishing
- older apps doesn't mean that it's stable for Bayanihan

Community
- composed of only a handful of people
- replies sa forum posts usually takes days
- no CoC... some won't hesitate to shout RTFM
- no activity on their IRC channel and mailing list

As for the Bayanihan team themselves, only a few really interacts with the community - like Rage. Most are too engrossed with their own projects.

What will make me leave Ubuntu for Bayanihan?
- adopt a Social Contract or Code of Conduct
- base their distro on Ubuntu
- more upstream activity
- more activity on IRC, Mailing List, and Forums
- and promote transparency on the development of their distro (so far, walang updates and they don't usually allow an outsider any say on what goes in and what gets rejected on their distro)

### Post by Jucato on 2007-12-05
Sinubukan ko na minsan ang Bayanihan Linux 4. Nagustuhan ko ang ilang bahagi nito, pero medyo hindi naging maganda ang kabuuan.

Simple lang ang installation. Gumagamit sila ng simplified Anaconda installer (medyo weird kasi Anaconda is Red Hat-based, samantalang Bayanihan 4 is Debian-based. Siguro leftover nung RH-based pa sila).

I like their artwork best of all. :D

And it's KDE, so it's certainly a plus for me.

Pero hanggang dun lang nagustuhan ko. Medyo feeling ko parang tagpi-tagpi ang BL4. Parang mga pinagsama-sama lang pira-pirasong software para makabuo ng distro. Isa pa, very dated ang packages nila. It's based on Debian Sarge pa nga ata eh. And since wala pa akong masyadong karanasan noon sa pure Debian, parang nakaka-intimidate mag update to a more recent Debian repository. Ang pinakanakakatawa sigurong experience ko sa BL4 ay yung NVIDIA driver. Pag sinunod ko yung instructions to install the nvidia-glx driver, it will install the driver, but will uninstall X server... so may driver ka nga, wala ka naman X. :D

Anyway, I might try Bayanihan again in a few weeks, after I make a run through of other distros on my laptop. Sana may updates na. Unfortunately medyo naguguluhan ako as to what their goal really is for Bayanihan, whether just for stable use in government or for wide-acceptance among Filipinos. I'm also not sure on how much they are community-oriented/aware. I mean, do they just do the development amongst themselves, while the rest of the community just stand by? Without a doubt, they have the developers, but we (Ubuntu-PH people) have the numbers when it comes to users.

It would be interesting to see what role a local or localized distro will have in our country in the (near) future. :)
