### Post by rg4w on 2011-11-17
What do I expect from it?

1. Ability to choose which side the Launcher is on.

2. No background color for icons in Launcher (unless perhaps limited to indicating open apps; as it is the color washes out the icon shape, making it hard to distinguish; most telling is the Ubuntu Software Center icon, which renders like a shapeless pile of orange mud in the current version, its outline completely lost in the visual noise).

3. An icon labeled "Advanced Options" in the System Settings panel so long-time users who know what they're doing can configure things as fully and easily as was possible in earlier versions, and labeled as such they won't be bothered with by noobs.

4. Menus visible by default, but configurable for those who prefer them hidden (why conceal an app's features from new users who need them most?)

The good news is that's pretty much all I have to complain about with Ubuntu's new Unity direction.  It's so close to being truly great, it would be a shame to miss these small refinements which would win over a majority of current naysayers and let Unity live up to its name by unifying the Ubuntu community.
