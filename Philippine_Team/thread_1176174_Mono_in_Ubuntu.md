### Post by creek23 on 2010-07-15
> **jsgotangco said:**
> I don't have any philosophical issues with Mono. I'm more interested in the technical benefits that it can offer. It is probably because I tend to focus more on the pragmatic side of software engineering.

Besides, the guy who started Mono is the same one who started Gnome. He is perhaps looking into the pragmatic side of the technology as well.

Miguel de Icaza said in his Foreword for the book "The Official GNOME 2 Developers Guide",
> "The Gnome project began in 1997 as a way to bring free software to the computer desktop. At the time, the scene looked a bit like this: Microsoft had released Windows 95, which was a very big improvement over their previous OS, and they were fiercely competing with Netscape for the browser space. Linux and BSD
Unix were increasingly being used to run servers and were the source of most of the server&#8722;based innovation at the time. But at the time, the free software story of desktop software was looking pretty bad. Oh, there were a few proprietary desktop environments, and the KDE effort, but even KDE was, sadly, built on top of a proprietary platform."

Seems like back then when Miguel initiated GNOME, he wasn't the pragmatic type of guy.
