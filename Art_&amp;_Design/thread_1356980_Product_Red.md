### Post by AdotB on 2009-12-19
I am all for the idea of raising awareness of and participation in charitable funds like the Global Fund and AIDS research, however I don't quite grasp the purpose of an Ubuntu Red initiative.

[LIST=1]
[*]Product Red is a brand of which a percentage of revenue from products that are sold with its branding are donated to the Global Fund.
[INDENT]Ubuntu isn't sold, so there is no revenue to be gained directly from an Ubuntu Red. Revenue would probably have to come from systems sold with Ubuntu preinstalled, which means the company selling the systems would join Product Red, do the branding, and do the donations. [/INDENT]

[*]I've seen some criticism of Product Red (and the companies selling branded products) as well, Such as the percentage of of each product purchased is going to the Global Fund.
[INDENT]It also seems a little like branding a product with Product Red is used more as a way to appear charitable and have people think highly of them with minimal effort or cost. especially when we don't always know just how much of the money we spend on a product is actually going to the cause.[/INDENT]
[/LIST]

Maybe there is another way to contribute to, help out, or raise awareness of a cause like this.

If I am incorrect on anything, mistaken, or if you have any thoughts, opinions, or ideas on any of this please let me know.
