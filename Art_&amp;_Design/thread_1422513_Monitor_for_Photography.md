### Post by Gerhardus on 2010-03-24
Bob,

Monitors are a rather difficult balance when it comes to photography. Most (and by most I probably mean all) screens in the price range you're looking at are made using the twisted nematic display type. The issue with this is that they only have a 6-bit color range. They use different processes to make up for it, and human eyes are terrible with color so for most applications this doesn't matter. In photography you'd ideally want to get a IPS (or in-plane switching) display, which are the panels used in Apple's cinema displays and Dell's ultra sharp (they actually use the same panels for their screens.) They allow a full range of 8-bit color. Though those still aren't in the range of large color gamut screens (notably made by NEC and Eizo) which in many cases almost or do cover the full AdobeRGB color space. I do photography myself, and have spent many hours researching too many things, so I know how frustrating it can be. CRTs do definitely cover a wider gamut and range of contrast but are harder to find and aren't even recommended by professional sources anymore. I'm on a budget just like you and recently picked up a 23" samsung to replace a 22" viewsonic that blew some pixels. I was happy with the viewsonic I had but find the new samsung much better. IMHO if you can afford it go for the better equipment, but getting a reasonably priced (I wouldn't go too low, like those budge $150-25" screens) and making sure you color balance and you'll be alright.
