### Post by lyceum on 2007-02-24
> **DizzyTech said:**
> I'd be happy to follow a tutorial, I don't need to be spoon-fed.

I am working on putting one together with pictures, but here is a quick one, let me know if you need more details. (it is taking more time than i thought)

GIMP:

take a blank back drop File/New), make it bigger than you need.

On the GIMP, click on the top loft square, then double click on the black color square and make the color you want.

Now, use your mouse to make a box on your new picture. click on the pail dumping stuff and when you click on the box, it changes color.

Do this again, but change the color, make the box the size you want over the first box, leaving a bit of the top and the right side (per your picture)

Do this a third time.

Now, this is your back ground. For the for ground, here is an easy way to do it. Go to the website for all the programs you want to make an icon for and steal their logos :)

to give an example, if you have a red icon with a white back ground.
open your newly acquired logo in GIMP. Click on the magic want in the top column. Now, click on the white back ground. Everything but the log is now selected.

Now, go to "select" on your logo page in GIMP and chose "invert". Now just your logo is selected. copy it with ctrl+c and paste it onto your icon with ctrl+v. (be sure to save an original of the icon, so they all look the same first). 

once everything is lined up the way you want go to "Layer" and select 'merge down"

now for the cool effects. go to "Tool" "Selection Tools" and chose "Ellipse" use your mouse to make and ellipse that is too big. Now invert your selection, as you did above. Now "Tools" "Color tTools" "Hue-Saturation" and turn up the brightness a bit.

Here is one I did:

[IMG]http://freud.franklin.edu/ferrim01/ULoCo1.jpg[/IMG]

Once you are done, you can have Xara trace them to vector graphics.

This may be the long way around, but I find it quick and easy.
