### Post by Bluejacket on 2008-02-12
I'm an amateur photographer. I've submitted several photos to gnome-look.org as wallpapers and gotten decent votes & downloads. I've used GPL license so far. Recently I've started to wonder what would be the best license to choose. I'm happy when people download my photos to use as wallpaper. I don't mind them sharing them or modifying them.

However I don't want someone using my photos on their book, a newspaper using the photo on their article, someone using my photo on their advertisement or commercial website.

GPL license is made for software, so I'm not sure how it would work for use of a photo in advertisement etc. Is artistic 2.0 or CC license better for me? The license should allow the use of my photo on a linux distribution cd, and selling the CD for nominal fee.
