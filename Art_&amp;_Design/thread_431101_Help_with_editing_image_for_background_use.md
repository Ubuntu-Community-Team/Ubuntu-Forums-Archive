### Post by rsambuca on 2007-05-06
A few tings I would definitely consider...

1.  Clean up the specks first.

2.  Up the contrast using the Channel Mixer.  After fixing the specks, duplicate the layer and then open the channel mixer in monochrome mode.  You can play around with the channel levels, but you'll find for a picture like this a lot of good contrast is hidden in the blue channel.  Try setting the green and blue levels to around 70, and pull the red back to -20 or so and see what that looks like.  Then set the monochrome layer to "overlay", and it will really bring out the highlights.  You may have to blur the background after that, as such high levels of blue will definitely pull in a lot of noise in that dark background.

3.  After making these types of adjustments, you will probably want to pull the red levels down a bit in the Colors -> Levels dialogue box as the red looks a bit blown out to me.

Great Photo, by the way!
