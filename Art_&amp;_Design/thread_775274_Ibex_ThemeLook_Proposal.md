### Post by BradwJensen on 2008-05-01
Hey everyone!

***I am the one*** who mocked these two &#8220;themes&#8221; up (quite a while back might i add)..  I based it off the theme from [www.miro.com]("http://www.miro.com/"), i think.. (but the colors to me didn't really matter at the time, I just needed a layout to show my points.)

**Explanation:**
 My main point with these mock ups were the space Gnome takes up right now for tasks that can be less space consuming and easier to understand..
 
If Gnome would work to make things take less space and be more defined and integrated with each other in Nautilus or anything else they make - the OS would be a lot more efficient and easier for getting things done.  It would also greatly take strain off your eyes by having less sitting around when you don't need it.  Less thinking would be needed to get a simple task done.


 ***-- Starting a cleanup --***
 
**Cleaning up the Main Menu:**
I have done a lot more thinking about the menu at the top of Nautilus..  I think it would be nice to have simple checkbox choice on wether it is in the "titlebar" (like iTunes) "gnome-panel" (like Mac OS), or where it is now in Nautilus (below the titlebar.)

-- *Note:*
When using the menu in the titlebar,  If the window is not wide enough for the title and menu items to be shown, the menu could either collaps to just a drop down button on the top left of the titlebar, or the menu could drop down one line and be like it is now in Nautilus.
 

 

 **Cleaning up the toolbar buttons:**

*-- The slider*
 Notice the slider bar at the bottom of Nautilus in this window.  This take away the TWO space consuming buttons at the top toolbar plus the drop down for choosing "icon" or "list" view.  You can set that view as default in the preferences of nautilus.  The slider would work like it does in Microsoft Office with word document files (showing a small % sign maybe.)  As you make it larger, more info is shown, just like Nautilus works now.
 

 -- *Navigation buttons:*
This is also another interesting part of Nautilus.  Mac OS does seem to have a cleaner file manager by connecting the related (Forward/Backward/Up) buttons.  This to me makes sense because these three buttons relate well (all are used to navigate through a tree-like structure somewhat the same way.)

I personally can't think of a better way to take less space and connect related buttons than this. (Anyone with any better possible ideas, i would love to hear them :))
 

 *-- Cleaning Up 'Refresh' and 'Stop' Buttons:*
These are something that have always bothered me in Linux.  These two buttons are related.  They can and should be easily connected as ONE button on the toolbar.  Just like in Firefox.  When a page is loading, you only need the stop button.  When the page is loaded you don't need the stop button, but you might want the refresh button.  Simple as that, those two are related and should be one button that changes when needed, just like in Firefox.
 

 *-- Cleaning up the last 3 toolbar buttons ('Home', 'Computer' and 'Search'):*
 Having these be the last 3 buttons left, I think we can place 'Home' and 'Computer' on the same line as the forward, backward, up, and refresh button.  The refresh button might possibly be best placed next to the right where it is somewhat related to the Foot icon on the right that shows the page is loading (by moving its toes)..  Maybe we don't even need that foot icon if we have a button that shows either Stop or Refresh based on the state we are in at the time..
 

 *-- Adding things to the cleaned space:*
 Apple and Windows both have this search feature in the upper right (where we now have free space! :))
 Also, Firefox has one here too!  This must be a sign of some significance. Noobs would be familiar this being placed here and anyone could easily search for what they want with it.  This would also eliminate the extra search button that we don't need anymore.  Having the search be separate from the address bar lets us easily know where we are and what we want to search for at the same exact time..  Right now if we click search is clears the address bar.  Some people might be confused about where they were why they're searching in a address bar.  Also, having a separate search we could add a drop down to it like Firefox' has.  In this drop down people could choose to search the current directory, whole computer, or whatever else we want to add as a filter to it.
 

 

***A******ll this is just the tip of Nautilus and how it needs to change.***
 

 [I][B]I have many more ideas, and would like to hear others ideas for fixing these (possibly simple) problems in Gnome.

[/B][/I]Honestly, &#65279;I want to get in contact with the Gnome desktop people and get them to work with "my ideas" (more like our ideas) to make Linux a better computer OS for people :P
