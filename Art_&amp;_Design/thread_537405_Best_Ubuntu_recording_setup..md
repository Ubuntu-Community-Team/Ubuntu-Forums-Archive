### Post by Dragonbite on 2007-08-29
I have 2 Pentium IIIs at home and only 1 is able to handle 512 Ram (a Dell Dimension 4100 running @ 1 GHz). The other (Sony Vaio running at 500MHz)  is maxed out at 256 Ram.

The weaker one is running with Gnome in it and while it isn't a speed demon it does work.  I am NOT trying anything like Beryl .. that would just be insane.

Chances are you can probably work with either Gnome or KDE and you just have to understand that it isn't going to be a speed demon or as fast and responsive as your main computer (which is probably newer and more powerful).  The less glam and glitz you have running, the better but you shouldn't be stuck with bland-and-white.

The times the slower system rears it's ugly head is when it has to run anything intensive.

On my 500MHz system I took off Open Office because it's too much of a hog. Ironically Blender does seem to work (so far) but I haven't really tried it yet or tried rendering anything.
