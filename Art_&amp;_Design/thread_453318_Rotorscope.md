I don't know of any good free compositing software, but basically what you'll need is something to separate your movie into frames. Open those up in Gimp and trace the action you want, then put the frames back together again. Rotoscoping is very labor intensive.

If you shoot your footage on a blue or green screen, you might be able to use Gimp to automate some of the tracing. You can do  it in Photoshop with actions, but I haven't used Gimp enough to know if you could do it. It will almost certainly look better to do it by hand, though. It will go about ten times faster if you use a tablet.

### Post by Aphorism on 2007-05-24
[LIST=1]
[*]The defacto compositor in Free Software is Blender currently.
[*]Rotoscoping is generally referred to when something cannot be keyed.  If you require keying, GIMP GAP has a plugin, as does Blender.
[*]If you require hand done rotoscoping, get out your tablet and use GIMP GAP.
[/LIST]
