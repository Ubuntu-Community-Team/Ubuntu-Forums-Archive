### Post by paullinux on 2009-06-08
In a shop I saw a EEE-pc with at touchscreen with (sadly) WindowsXP on it.  It worked surprisingly well.  But the thing is : it is not designed to work with fingers.

So I'm wondering why no one has yet come up with  a theme especially designed to have buttons large enough so it can be easily used with  a finger.  As GTK-theming is very flexible, would it be possible?

requirements?

 I think buttons should have a 40 x 40 pixel size.
 Menu's and panel should also have large enough buttons.

The problem is:

screen-resolution:  should be large enough to accommodate a workable desktop.  Large buttons means large panes, dialogs, etc... so I believe some applications would require a really large screen. 

So my question is:  Who can come up with a trial-version of such a GTK-theme?  Would it be a workable theme?  And finally :  Is there a market for it?
