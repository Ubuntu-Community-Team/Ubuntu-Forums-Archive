### Post by Aelfric5578 on 2006-09-25
The simplest way I know of would be to first find a picture of Tux with a transparent background.  For example, this one ( [http://www.binaryshift.us/images/penguin-sit3.gif](http://www.binaryshift.us/images/penguin-sit3.gif) )could work, but it's a bit grainy.  Then open it in GIMP.  Once it's open, increase the canvas size to the size of your screen (Image -> Canvas Size) and in the same dialog, drag Tux to the bottom right corner.  Save it, and set that image as your background.  The transpareny will be respected and you'll just set Tux in the corner with the rest of the screen being whatever background color you choose.  Alternatively, you could do the same as above and add a solid color background in place of the transparency.
