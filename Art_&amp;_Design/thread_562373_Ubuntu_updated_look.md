### Post by Enjoimurr on 2007-09-28
Ok, by no means am I a designer. But I do have the perspective of a newcomer. As such, I have some suggestions for an update in the default theme of the next Ubuntu. Right now (7.04) the default start/task bars in Ubuntu sort of look like older windows start/task bars. Also, the default start bars look too much like other linux distros. In no way am I criticizing ubuntu, because I have recently switched from windows and am loving it. But it seems that it still might be possible to create a more intuitive (and pretty) user interface. I am aware that this stuff has been suggested before. But i think it is VERY critical that Ubuntu make its most critical reach for users right now, considering all the weaknesses windows vista has (neg. publicity) and the positive publicity Ubuntu has been getting. To me Ubuntu seems like the linux that has a great chance of succeeding amongst people (I have switched my whole FAMILY over to Ubuntu, and they all like it despite having no experience). I am also aware that looks are functionally less important, but they make a very strong impression on people. Therefore I suggest updating the default theme in the next ubuntu distro. I have created a prototype of what I had in mind:

[IMG]http://i20.tinypic.com/oadbv4.jpg[/IMG]

Note that this is a very early idea, and I cannot create themes, but hopefully this mockup could guide someone who has more technical experience.

I think that as Ubuntu moves to the next stages of development, acceptance and usability it is important that it differentiate itself from other operating systems. It is time that we considered Ubuntu among the rank of the corporate giants (Mac, Windows) and started moving it towards mass acceptance in the public. Being on a college campus, I have great opportunities to distribute Ubuntu and, in my opinion, if the looks could be polished we would be making a great step towards acceptance.

Anyway, my speech is over, but if someone could give it consideration, that would be great. (and hopefully, sometime there will be a "ubuntu fair" on campus.

If you have any questions, PM me.

Not an argument, but, the first thing most users (novice that is) judge is the looks of the system. I can guarantee you that people would be pissed if vista looked like 9x. It is sort of shallow, but it is true. Power users on the other hand, focus more on the function on the operating system. I am new to linux (about 1 year) but, the reason I changed from windows is because of a youtube video showing custom themes. Also, Ubuntu had gotten a lot of publicity recently, so I checked out wikipedia, and this changed my decision from Mandriva to Ubuntu. So getting people to switch is the first part, making their first experience an enjoyable one is the second part. Ubuntu is great with wireless and drivers, it all just works, but I think that more people would switch if the first image they saw was one which stunned them (as beryl did to me).
