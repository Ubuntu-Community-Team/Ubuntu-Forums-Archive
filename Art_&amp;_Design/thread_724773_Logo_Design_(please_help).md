### Post by st0n3cutt3r on 2008-04-02
I do all of my image creation in pixel editors instead of vector programs, because I learned on pixel software, and it's easier for me. (I think in pixels, not vectors)

I've done some colorations, not at all difficult to reproduce as vectors when the time comes, but just experimenting, and trying to work black in.

here's what I've got.

For the most part, unless the black is worked in somehow as seen in the second image, I still don't think it can be done.  This is primarily due to the fact that the white spots on the planets is supposed to look like a highlight on a glossy object.  A marble in space, if you will. By making the highlight black, it becomes a sharply defined shadow.... not something that you see on round objects very often, and it doesn't really make sense in the context of the picture.

my roommate suggested that I try swapping the black and the blue in the second image, and I may try it out, or try adding some thin streaks that follow the swirl of black to give it some contrast, but for the most part, I can't see this existing on a black background. I apologize for the inconvenience. (take my word for it, it looks MUCH worse if I have a black background, and fill in the highlight on the planets white or anything but the black)

food for thought, here's a different view of the same image with a few slight, but interesting modifications....  [http://i17.photobucket.com/albums/b98/st0n3cutt3r/portal3.jpg?t=1207116036](http://i17.photobucket.com/albums/b98/st0n3cutt3r/portal3.jpg?t=1207116036)
