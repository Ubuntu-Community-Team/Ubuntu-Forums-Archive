### Post by jem7v on 2007-01-16
Opaque foreground windows and transparent in the background?  Yes, Compiz can do that.  I used it for a while and it was a breeze to get running with Ubuntu Edgy and an old Geforce2 MX and it never once crashed on me, but it didn't feel very polished and it was sometimes a bit buggy in its implementation of window transparencies.  I don't use it now because it can't do the specific things I wanted it to, yet has plenty of silly effects that are utterly useless for me.  I really don't need my windows to wobble or my menus to disintegrate in a burst of flames.  It just slows things down.

At this stage, practically all the Linux composite desktops are still in alpha-beta stages, so if you want guaranteed stability and polish it would probably be safest to leave them alone for a while - i.e., until they get closer to releasing Version 1.0 instead of 0.3.6 or 0.1.2.  (It might be a bit of a wait, though.)
