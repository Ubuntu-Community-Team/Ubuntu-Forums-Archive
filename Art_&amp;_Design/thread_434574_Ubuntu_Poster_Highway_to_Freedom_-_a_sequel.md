### Post by Jenda on 2007-05-06
Hello forum artwork folks. I have been printing Ubuntu posters (see my sig for links) of HanZo's Highway to Freedom poster.
The design really rocks, IMO, and people out there agree: they have bought over 650 prints to date, worth a total of $1340 (with a net profit of $142).
When they were made... Ubuntu was a startup. It was new, exciting and... newly popular. However, today Ubuntu has a neat chance of becoming mainstream. I was thinking it would be awesome to have a sequel to the poster, which would reflect that fact by transforming the barren desert environment into something more... bloomed.
(My ideas so far: there could be some life, or at least some traffic on the road, or the picture could move on from the _beginning_ of the Ubuntu Highway further on, to show how the road continued)

It could also offer a Kubuntu variant... or incorporate Kubuntu in the main picture... Have a look at this inspiring variant: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=2601261&postcount=47](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=2601261&postcount=47)

Another thing I've considered was that because all HanZo got for his marvelous design was 10 free posters, I could perhaps try selling these with a real profit, and reward the author of the sequel a little better.

I would like to know you thoughts, or, even better, a designer :)

(I will reserve the final word on which designs I put money into and which I won't, however, for those that I do, I will consult the use of the profits with the designer and agree on an adequate reward with them)

(Oh, yes - did I say this was my dear mum's idea? &#12484;)

### Post by HanZo on 2007-06-01
btw... sice I never know where the "source" for the poster can be downloaded here, people sometimes ask me about it and I definitely like the idea of people working with it, I put it up on my site with a Creative Commons noCommercial share alike licence. The first release of the sources was GPL but that really isn't a good type of license for a poster, Not that it is not good per se (because it is a great license) but it was concieved for software, so applying it to a poster does not work very well... and besides, CCPL licenses have been concieved just for that: images, music and video. I'm not really sure about the noCommercial thing... it does not mean you cannot sell the posters you make, just that you should not do it for profit, but still I could leave that free too, just keep the share alike option, so people must share the result with the same license.

If anybody need it, the package can be downloaded here:
[http://www.gasteropodica.net/hannes/2007/05/28/ubuntu-poster-sources/](http://www.gasteropodica.net/hannes/2007/05/28/ubuntu-poster-sources/)

soon you will see a sketch for the new poster...
