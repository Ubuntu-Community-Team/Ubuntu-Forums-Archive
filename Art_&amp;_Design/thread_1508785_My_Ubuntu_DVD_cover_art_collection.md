As far as making the designs, here's all you need to know.

1. It's for the actual disk, so we're talking about the same dimensions as a stick-on CD label.

2. It needs to be in some sort of "standard" image format.  Generally PNG.

3. Lightscribe burners only produce labels in gray-scale, so you'll want to make sure the labels look right in gray scale.  In fact, you may as well just make them that way to begin with.

4. You loose a bit of contrast when burning a label onto a light scribe disc, so make sure the images you produce have extra high contrast.

### Post by VeeDubb on 2010-07-03
> **kiddykoff said:**
> I've heard that if you reburn the image multiple times, the image darkens. I have a lightscribe, but i never installed the drivers.

I've tried it.  It sucks.  

About 1/4 of the time, the images don't line up, so you're just left with a funky rotated double exposure.

When they do line up, you're left with really ugly banding.  For some images, it's 'ok' but rarely if ever worth the trouble.

There's a command line option you can issue that sets your drive to a higher contrast mode, but it's only a marginal improvement.

The best solution I have found after a great deal of trying different things, is to make sure my images are black and white* before hand, and very high in contrast.  





*The software will work just find with a color image, even though drive only burn in gray scale.  The reason for making them black and white before burning, is to make it easier to see what the final product will look like.
