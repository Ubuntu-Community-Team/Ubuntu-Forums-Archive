### Post by Paul820 on 2007-07-05
> **trak87 said:**
> How did you do the 3d text?

The 3D text is easy, just do a normal text layer with your lettering. Give it a gradient from light to dark from top to bottom. Then duplicate it four or five times, depending on how thick you want it. Start from the bottom layer, move it 1px to the left and 1px up. The next layer up, move it 2px to the left and 2px up, and so on. The when you have done that, go back to the bottom layer, alpha to selection, give that a gradient with the same colours, but in the opposite direction, bottom to top. That's it.

### Post by WinterWeaver on 2007-07-16
For your Blender Lighting, might I suggest looking up tutorials on 3 point lighting.

Lighting is very tricky to get right, and what most newcomers dont realize, is that light in your room for example, does not come from only one source (ie, the ceiling light), light is also comming from other angles as they are bounced about the room.

3 point lighting is the easiest way to simulate this, and it's the way I light most of my scenes.

You need a main lamp, that will cast your shadows.
then you need a second, "fill Light" to fill in the "unnatural black shadows.
lastly you use a backlight to fill in the rest.

your last two lights can have a lower intensity than the main light, but remember to turn of specular highlights for them, otherwise you will end up with additional bright spots on your models. Also remember to turn off the shadows for these them.

Hope it helps... keep up the good work :)
