### Post by QIII on 2014-11-04
Hello!

My opinion?

If your son uses the app for school, it would be wise to continue to use it.  It could be that work submitted using another tool will not be accepted for credit.  College is what it is and course requirements are what they are.  

This is an oft-asked question that sometimes generates emotionally charged discussions.  The fact is that although there may be Linux alternatives, there are no perfect replacements.

GIMP will surely be offered as an alternative.  However, the objective truth is that it is not a drop in replacement that will always suit the bill.

One should use the tool that works best for the task at hand.  Dedication to a particular platform and forsaking all others is not pragmatic.

### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-11-04
Another thing to consider is what your son will be doing with Photoshop after this course.  If he is looking to work in a commercial art or design house, he needs to know the Adobe tools because that's what they will be using.  GIMP is great for amateurs like me who just want to create graphics to put on a web page, but for applications with commercial printing that use things like PANTONE® colors, GIMP isn't an option.
