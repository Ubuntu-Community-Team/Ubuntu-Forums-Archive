### Post by gustavomazza on 2010-12-21
My first photos completely edited in Linux, some of the programs tested are GNU while others commercial but with 64 bit linux versions.

 6 images processed with UFRaw. This is a stack of 6 photos with Canon MP-E 65mm macro @ 2x and f/7.1. The images were stacked in the 64 bit Linux edition of Zerene Stacker which worked nicely for the stack and retouch. Final adjusts of levels in GIMP 2.6

[[IMG]http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5123/5258990841_e45b07851c_z.jpg[/IMG]]("http://www.flickr.com/photos/gmazza/5258990841/")

This is a manual align in GIMP, the raw was developed with UFRaw, and after the images were processed in zerene stacker, 11 images, used wavelet deoise plug-in in the final adjusts

[[IMG]http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5082/5261730737_5f916fa6ca_z.jpg[/IMG]]("http://www.flickr.com/photos/gmazza/5261730737/")

Stack of two photos taken with  natural sunlight and iso 400 (opposed to flash photography and low isos  of the previous photos), there is the sky and trees reflected in the  wasp abdomen and I think for these reflective surfaces the natural light  is the way to go.Developed the  raw with krita (a linux image editor, slow but allow 16 bit processing  and liked the color result). Final adjusts in GIMP were used wavelet  denoise plug in and unsharp mask.

[[IMG]http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5008/5280035492_dcd5bb9237_z.jpg[/IMG]]("http://www.flickr.com/photos/gmazza/5280035492/")
