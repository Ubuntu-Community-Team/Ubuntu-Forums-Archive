### Post by Nonno Bassotto on 2007-11-09
I don't understand why, but I can give a reason not to change now. Both gThumb and F-Spot are photo management apps, meaning that you can tag your photos and organize them based on these metadata.

Sadly the two systems are not compatible: presently there is no way to translate gThumb categories to F-Spot tags or viceversa. So a user is tied to whatever application he chose to use first (in my case gThumb). Tagging a second time thousands of photos seems not reasonable.

So choosing one over the other is not only a matter of taste: some people HAS to use one or the other.
