### Post by gunashekar on 2008-07-14
> **Merk42 said:**
> What exactly does a CMS do?  How is it different from using something like Dreamweaver/Amaya/KompoZer (or the for the elitist, a text editor)?
Dreamweaver/Amaya or a text editor are used to create web pages and web sites. If you want to add content to your website, or move things around you may have to redo the site or some pages.
A Content Management System (CMS) makes it easier if you have to often update the content online, allow different users to post content on the site, add and maintain different components to the website such as a picture gallery, a discussion forum, allow people not familiar with creating web pages to add content, and many other uses.

### Post by pelle.k on 2009-04-18
Over the last couple of months i've been trying out many, many cms systems. Most of them do the same thing, with varying results.
I feel there's a need for more complex flat-file based cms's, since most low end web space accounts lack mysql. I think skybluecanvas is interesting, but it's in development ATM.
As for mysql based cms's (i only do PHP), these are my favourites, because they provide something like drupals "CCK" and "Views", and if you don't know what that is, you're missing out on the best (and to me, essential) part of a "real cms".
[LIST]
[*]modx - "template variables" and templates. snippets and stuff in PHP. Nice. This is for the PHP developer.
[*]phpwcms - uses "content fields" instead. more point and click, less do-it-yourself. Very competent. Too bad it's a "one man show", and in constant development. It's got many core plugins/modules out-of-the-box.
[*]typolight - the "big brother" of phpwcms, even though they're not even related. Uses something pretty much like phpwcms's "content fields". Seems more grown up than phpwcms. Requires a bit more work from you as well.
[/LIST]
None of these three are particularly easy to "set up", but that goes for Drupal and Joomla too. In the case of Drupal, i could never find any modules that did what i wanted them to do. Or, they are not released for 6.x yet. Joomla is too much (like most other CMS's really) "square peg in round hole". I laughed the first time i saw that expression, about Joomla, because it's so true :)

Maybe i should have written my own CMS? It'd take pretty much the same amount of time it took to evaluate all of them. But, in my case, i wanted some experience with the most popular ones, in case i have to work with one in the future, and in case i build my own, i want to take the best bits and ideas from the most popular cms systems.
