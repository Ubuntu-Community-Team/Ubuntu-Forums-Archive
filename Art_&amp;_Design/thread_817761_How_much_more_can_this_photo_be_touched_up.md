### Post by HappyHenry on 2008-07-02
Really nice job! hmmmm, let me see, you want me to find something wrong....lol.
The wall really looks fine to me. Sorry cant see a need there. The monitor shows a little of the original screen on the edges of your paste job. You may get a better result by first putting a black border on the screen shot then pasting it on the monitor. Black on black no telling where the paste job is. The tree, well, I would just use the clone tool to replace the leaves with wall then free paint the leaves with a custom brush, on a new layer,that has a custom stager to it.The reason for painting on a new layer is you can add noise to it so it looks more like a photo. My favorite trick in Photoshop and not sure if it will work in Gimp, Im still a noob with Linux, is to change the focus on the edges of the image. 
In your image the table with the monitor seems to be the focus point so just leave that clear on your adjustment layer and use a graident fill around the outside edge of the image, then blur the layer. The result is a soft gradual blur towards outer edge. This is helpful to hide the little imperfections like the hand painted tree not exactly matching with the image. You should end up with a soft out of focus effect on just the edges of your image. It seems to help pull together my images also, make them look like everything was there for the photo shot. Im no tech writer so if you need more info ill try to , lol, clear it up. Really though looking at your job, you did great! I hope i can get my mind around Gimp enough to do this well!
