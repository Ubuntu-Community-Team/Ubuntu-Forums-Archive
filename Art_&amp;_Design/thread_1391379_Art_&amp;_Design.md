### Post by mcduck on 2010-01-27
art is free, and can exist just because it's nice.

Design has a purpose, and is based on intelligent decissions to serve that purpose.

(For example an artist can select colors because they look nice, while a designer has to consider the meaning of those colors and how well they fit the purpose the work is intended for)

Note that art and design are not always separate from each other. Design can be art, even though it doesn't have to be.

### Post by RabbitWho on 2010-01-28
Art is more expensive but design makes more money... 

Design tries to be functional and practical while art tries not to be. 

Art draws attention to the artist or their personal agenda, design draws attention either to the product (advertising for google chrome, coca cola) or away from the product (google chrome, a chair ) 

Art attempts to be obscure vague and subtle. Design attempts to be clear and easily understood.

Art wants to be singular, design wants to be ubiquitous.
