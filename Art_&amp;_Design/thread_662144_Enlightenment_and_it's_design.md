### Post by Nevon on 2008-01-08
I want to start off by saying that of course people have different taste, and that should be respected. However, there are certain things that are considered to be more universally accepted as beautiful than others. Take for example OSX 10  vs. Windows 95. While I'm not particularly fond of either one, OSX is generally accepted as the most elegant of the two. 

This brings me to what the topic is really about. The desktop environment "Enlightenment" is being marketed as a breeze of elegance sweeping through the damp cavern that is Linux - or in other words, a better looking desktop environment.

So now I wonder: am I losing my sight?

I am a bit of an amateur graphical designer. My main area of interest has always been web design, but all kinds of interface design has always interested me. As a matter of fact, I am thinking about pursuing a career in interface design. With this being said, I do consider myself to have good taste. Although it may be subjective, I did establish that there are some things that are considered universally beautiful, in the first paragraph. Another indication would be that if you were to go to a site such as [www.customize.org](www.customize.org) (not [www.gnome-look.org](www.gnome-look.org)) and look at the top submissions, they are the kind of interfaces that me, and many of my contacts in the design world regard as aesthetically pleasing. 

But then we come back to Enlightenment. A DE that is being marketed as the best looking DE out there. Yet I think it looks absolutely horrible! I'm sure that with the proper configuration it could look great. It has similarities with Fluxbox, and although fluxbox's default themes look horrible, it can be made to be the cleanest, most modern-looking DE of all (openbox, blackbox, and IceWM could probably be made to look great too). 
But from what I can tell there aren't very many themes out there except for the default ones. So when they're saying that Enlightenment is a beautiful DE, they must be referring to the default themes. 

An example of a distribution that uses Enlightenment is Geubuntu, these are some screenshots showing the default look of it:
[http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2144/2094705957_3eab43055d_o.jpg](http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2144/2094705957_3eab43055d_o.jpg)
[http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2111/2094705885_36b3f9b4ef_o.jpg](http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2111/2094705885_36b3f9b4ef_o.jpg)

And this is OZOS:
[http://www.cafelinux.org/OptickleArt/albums/userpics/OzOs_Rusted_Emerald_Theme.png](http://www.cafelinux.org/OptickleArt/albums/userpics/OzOs_Rusted_Emerald_Theme.png)

For a DE that is marketing itself as the best looking DE out there, those themes look okay, at best. In my opinion, a default theme of a distribution should be truly visually stunning. 

This is my standpoint. I am in no way bashing Enlightenment, Geubuntu, OZOS or any of its users. I am simply saying what I think about "the best looking DE out there", along with a question to you:

[SIZE="5"]What is it that I am not seeing? What is it that makes Enlightenment "magnificent and beautiful"?[/SIZE]

### Post by Nevon on 2008-01-08
I suppose you could say my taste has a touch of minimalism in it. However, the themes I showed you do not only reflect my personal preference, but also the general opinion of the design community. If a desktop environment aims to have a profile of high design quality, and include a lot of eye-candy, should it really be cluttered to death? 
Eye-candy, in my opinion, is anything that is aesthetically pleasing. Not widgets, dodgy shadows and outer glows. Nor should it include penguins that walk down the side of your window. 

I'm not questioning the individual usage of Enlightenment. I am simply questioning how it can be called "the best looking desktop environment", and asking you what you think is appealing about it.

I agree with you 100%

I believe enlightenment has very much potential, but here is where it runs into issues: 'Bling' does not necessarily make an 'eyecandy' desktop, or even a very elegant or attractive desktop. From my experience with enlightenment things have seemed like they are so focused on delivering the most bling that they lose sight of what the real purpose is, and end up with just a cluttered desktop that does not look neat.

Before you judge, I am currently typing this message using gOS which runs enlightenment as its default. I have not changed the default settings one bit (partially because i find it a bit tricky and I'm lazy, but thats not the point). I think that the default setting for enlightenment on gOS is very, very attractive and think with just a few minor adjustments it could be a more effective dock than even what Mac has to offer right now. Unfortunately, there are a few minor annoyances that make it difficult to use but I wont get in to those right now.

Let me just finish by saying I LOVE eyecandy. To me, eyecandy means a feature-rich desktop that is attractive and still functional. I have spent literally days on my Ubuntu desktop trying out GDM themes, installing icon sets, customizing icon sets, trying out emerald themes, installing and configuring screenlets, AWN, just about everything really. And to me, my desktop is something I find beautiful and I am terribly proud of it.

I believe that enlightenment has a lot of potential, but that potential is mostly wasted on useless bling that does not add to the overall attractiveness of the desktop or even functionality.
