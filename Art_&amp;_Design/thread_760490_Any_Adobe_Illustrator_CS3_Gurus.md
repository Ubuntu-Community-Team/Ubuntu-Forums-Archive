### Post by lswest on 2008-04-20
yeah, if you choose to do a "LiveTrace" in illustrator, it will vector it for the main part (play with threshold and such until it is to your liking) then you can just touch it up with the path tool.  To find live trace:

1.select the image

2.the lowest level of the top task bar should have a button called "liveTrace" and it might take a minute, but give it a shot.

### Post by geoken on 2008-04-22
I've always found it beneficial to make a rough outline first, then go in and fine tune (ie. adding nodes where needed, and refining the curves). I just find it easier to perfectly match the curve when both nodes are made rather than trying to get a perfect curve as the line is being drawn.

### Post by aethralis on 2008-04-26
> **PartisanEntity said:**
> Thanks very much, I actually do have a question, is it possible to join paths?

When dealing with an open path, select two end points with direct selection tool (a) and Ctrl+j to join them. To join 2 objects, select both with selection tool (v) and add to shape area and then expand (see the attachment).

### Post by aethralis on 2008-04-26
> **PartisanEntity said:**
> Thanks very much, I have another question, I saved my image as PDF and noticed weird lines around the paths, why are they appearing?
Probably just PDF rendering glitch. Happens sometimes on screen but not when printed. When two objects are side by side the renderer draws them so, that thin lines appear. Try zooming in and they will usually go away.
