### Post by bambootree on 2008-11-22
or you can:
1. Make a selection.
2. Select > Float (or Shift+Ctrl+L).
3. Select the Scale tool and drag the floating selection to resize it (or type numbers in the Scale dialogue box).
4. When you're happy with the result, just click the *Anchor the floating layer* button in the Layers Dialog.

### Post by Orographic on 2008-12-03
Okay, found a great way to do this that is as good or better than what I was doing in PS7:

Choose a select tool then roughly select the area that you want to shrink, then go to:

Filters -> Distorts -> Iwarp.

Select "shrink"

and then you can shrink it or grow it etc by clicking your mouse over the preview picture within Iwarp.
