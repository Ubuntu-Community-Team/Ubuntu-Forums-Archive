### Post by squaregoldfish on 2008-11-24
Someone may have thought of this already, but the idea popped into my head randomly, so I thought I'd share it for those who like eye candy and mad animations on their desktops.

Pretty much every composite window manager has drop shadows for the windows, and (I assume) it's possible to specify which way the shadows fall (bottom right in most cases). Would it be groovy to have the mouse pointer act as the 'light source' for these shadows, so that each window's shadow is darkest on the side furthest from the pointer? The window that the mouse is over would end up with a shadow all the way around, which may also provide a kind of natural highlighting as the window casts its shadow on everything else.

This is just a random thought - I have no intention of doing anything about it. Feel free to experiment with the idea as you see fit.

Steve.
