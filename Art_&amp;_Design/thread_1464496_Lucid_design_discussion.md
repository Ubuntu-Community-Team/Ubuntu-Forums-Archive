### Post by alex.rayu on 2010-04-28
About being original, I think there are a few things about it. Whoever invents the the first bicycle dooms others to not being original. The companies who first developed the window-button-desktop paradigm in a way, doomed others to not being original. Say, Apple has first come up with great design guidelines - clean, unobtrusive, driving attention to what's important, etc. Now, if you want to be clean, unobtrusive, intuitive, you are doomed to copy them at certain points. In fact, if you try to reinvent the bike differently, you will most likely end up spending all your money to come up with something useless. If you have a new bright idea then you can go for it, but if you have already a desktop (Gnome), which already looks to some extent like Apple (and KDE like MS) - then its kinda predictable when they can do about it - namely, polish it up to where its more esthetical and intuitive. And this time they did an especially good job.
