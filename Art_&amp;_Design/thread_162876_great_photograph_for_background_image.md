### Post by kar-tar on 2006-04-20
I don't know... I wouldn't say that it's more distracting than the default backgrounds of Microsoft, Apple, or most other Linux Distros.  Many backgrounds feature bright landscapes, giant text, a full prism of colors, and very bright or high contrast design.  This photo of a softly glowing bulb is positively demure by contrast.  I'm of the opinion that the current default desktop image is excessively bland, and has room to be a little bit "louder" before it becomes in any way distracting.

Most Ubuntu users (so it seems to me) are somewhere in the "power user" category - not newbs (to computers in general at least) but not big-time unix/linux geeks either.  I think most everyone here customizes their desktop to some extent, and I doubt any default background image will remain on very many desktops for a long time.  Still as it's pretty much everyone's first impression of a fresh install (*especially* since Ubuntu - wisely - goes with a blank canvas approach to default desktop configuration) it would be nice to have something more grabbing than an abstract brown swirl.

Also, having worked with the image as my background for a day now, it's much less imposing when it's a full screen, the shrinking of the flickr image on the small preview makes the image look sharper than it is.  You pretty much forget that it is there until you're shutting down the system and all the windows close.

Man I really like that photo.
