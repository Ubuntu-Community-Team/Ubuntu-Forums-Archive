### Post by st0n3cutt3r on 2008-06-19
The point wasn't that you can't use your Windows software on an Apple computer, but that you can't use it natively on the primary OS. If you've never tried switching between OSs on one computer so that you can use certain pieces of software one OS has, and then have to reboot to use other software, then you have no idea how truly disruptive to a work flow and annoying it is. 

"Great, got this piece done. Now I just have to quit everything I'm doing, wait 4 minutes for my computer to restart, remember to hit the right keys on boot to get it to allow me to boot into the other OS, wait for that *other* software I need to load, copy my files over to a place where there usable on this OS -- ****, forgot to do that before I restarted, gotta restart back into the previous OS, copy my files over, and then restart again in this one, wait for my software to load again, and I can continue my work."

Even if every time isn't like that, waiting for an OS to unload, and then a different one to load is one of the most boring things ever. At least when "watching paint dry" you can let your mind wander, as you won't suffer any ill effect by not paying attention and waiting for the moment to hit the right key to get it to do what you want as you do with switching OSs on an Intel-based Apple computer.

People who are buying Apple computers aren't using Windows on them nearly as often or in as large of numbers as they are using the Mac side. They would have bought a cheaper piece of hardware if all they wanted was Windows. -- It's still a big switch.
