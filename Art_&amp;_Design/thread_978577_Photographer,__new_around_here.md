I don't know much about the technology of setting up an RSS feed, but the benefit of it would be that instead of relying on people coming to the web on their own, they could receive a message "Hey, I just added a picture to my gallery. It is a shot of ... <blah><blah><blah>" and a link to the gallery page.

They see that and there is a good chance they'll click on the link, even if just to see what this picture is you are talking about (curiosity is great, isn't it?:lolflag:)

Most of the time, you think of RSS feeds being associated with people's blogs and when they post a blog entry you get the message.  Most news sites, though, are providing feeds.

People could use any number of readers out there. I use Google Reader and I subscribe to feeds in my MS Outlook ('07) at work.  I don't have to keep going to all these sites to keep up with what's new (blog posts, galleries, etc.).  There are stand-alone readers and many email programs can handle RSS feeds these days (like Thunderbird).

If you use Firefox or IE 7+ you should notice an orange square in the URL address box.  Clicking it either add the site's RSS feed to your chosen reader or open the actual RSS file that you can copy & paste the address to your reader.

Heck, it was my RSS feed of this forum that brought up this thread.. I read it, clicked it and well the rest is history!
