### Post by samden on 2009-05-26
The old Mac educational software KidWorks had an interface that was a picture of a room - you'd click on the toys to play, the paintset to paint etc. Not an office, and not 3D, but a similar concept. Although it looked cool initially it was really a nuisance to use as you could never remember where anything was (there were a lot of things you could do hidden in the picture). I expect a virtual office would be just as annoying.

It would however be a great way of showing off the capabilities of the operating system and attracting new people to it. Who would mostly switch back to Gnome or even Fluxbox after a few weeks of frustration and slow speeds.

It might be ok for children in some circumstances - but it wouldn't be an office then. It might also be fun for someone who couldn't afford an office and liked to pretend... You'd just end up with another "fun toy" like FSN.

However if you wanted to simulate 3D space and use it practically, forget making it look like an office, and have a look at Apple's latest ideas:
[http://www.appleinsider.com/articles/08/12/11/apple_working_on_3d_mac_os_x_user_interface_images.html](http://www.appleinsider.com/articles/08/12/11/apple_working_on_3d_mac_os_x_user_interface_images.html)

I'd still say it was impractical, but someone might find something like that useful. You'd have to be careful not to break Apple's patents though.
