### Post by scojo on 2006-12-23
> **viciouslime said:**
> Hi, what is an X-face exactly?

An x-face is a tiny graphic that travels encoded in the header of an email or newsgroup posting. Many news and email readers are able to display them.   I use Gnus as my news/mail client, which is an application that comes bundled in Emacs.  

If you view all the headers of an email that contains an x-face you'll see a mix of alphanumeric characters.  This header is translated into the graphic by the email client (which may rely on some other app - I'm not sure of how this actually works).

Anyway, there are apps that will take a graphic and convert it into the "code" that is in the x-face header that travels in the email.  I was hoping that someone else had already converted the graphic into an x-face header that I could use.
