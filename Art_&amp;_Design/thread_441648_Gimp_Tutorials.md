To "Grow" a selection:  Once you have a portion of your image selected (you see the dotted lines around your selected part), on your image title bar go to Select -> Grow. ### Post by rsambuca on 2007-05-13
It looks like you somehow closed your "tools options" from your main window.  If you want to get it back (I certainly find it very handy to have automatically open), double click on any tool eg. ellipse select.  (see attached pics).  By double clicking on the tool, it will open a new window with the tool options for the ellipse select tool.   To move the options menu onto the main gimp window, move your mouse over the tool options where it says "Ellipse Select" and hold down the left mouse button.  You can then drag it over to the main Window and place it on the thin line under the foreground <-> background color selector.  Then whenever you select a tool (now with a single click), you get the tool options at the bottom of that window.

### Post by rsambuca on 2007-05-14
Dude!  Quit pressing those "x's" in the corners of the gimp windows!!!;) 

Go to File -> Dialogues -> Create New Dock -> Layers, Channels, Paths

After that is open, you may want to add the Brushes dialogue box to the bottom of it.  Press the left pointing arrow button to the right of the work "Layers".  Click "Add Tab" -> Brushes.  It will then be at the top.  You can drag it to the bottom like you did with the Tools Options.

Then, when closing gimp, ONLY CLOSE THE MAIN GIMP WINDOW so the others will reappear next time!!!
