### Post by xl_cheese on 2010-05-26
Start out by taking a theme you like an altering it.  That'll help you get the feel for what the widgets use.

Consider which theme "engine" you want to use.  

Clearlooks,
murrine,
etc...

Then choose a theme that works with that engine to customize.

I took it a step further and altered the clearlooks engine to get what I wanted.  A link to my customized engine is in my sig.

### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-03-24
Most of it is defined by which desktop environment is being used. Fedora on Gnome could use Ubuntu Gnome themes and Kubuntu could use OpenSUSE KDE themes, but Ubuntu can't use Kubuntu themes.

Some small things are specific to distros or versions, but the DE is the main thing.
