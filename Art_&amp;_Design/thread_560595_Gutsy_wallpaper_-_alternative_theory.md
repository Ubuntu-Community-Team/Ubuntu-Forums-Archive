### Post by Gadren on 2007-09-26
I can understand what you're getting at -- but should Ubuntu really be in the business of creating controversy in order to get attention?

And I don't think it would work that way anyway -- there's already a certain stigma about Ubuntu's brown color scheme that would only be made stronger if the brown wasn't done right.  I doubt that potential new Ubuntu users would even pick up Ubuntu if the reviews were mixed like that.  I know that it was only because the reviews were very positive at the time that I picked up Ubuntu myself.  

Working off of controversy only works when the act of checking out the controversy is easy.  For example, when there's some controversial video, people give it a lot of attention when there's a YouTube clip of it, because it's very easy to check the video out.  But installing an OS requires more time and thought than just checking out a video, and so if there's controversy, I don't see potential users going to all the trouble of installing Ubuntu just to check it out.
