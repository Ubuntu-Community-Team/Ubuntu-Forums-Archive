Go FOSS! :P

I think you have a misunderstanding of how FOSS works. Every part of Ubuntu is copyrighted. It's copyright that makes the GPL work. The GPL basically says, "We have this copyrighted code here, but you can distribute it and use it as long as you abide by the terms of this license. And if you don't want to agree to the license, then regular (more restrictive) copyright restrictions apply." If it weren't copyrighted, then anybody could do whatever they wanted with it, including closing the source and not making changes available to the community and not attributing works to their authors.
