### Post by neoflight on 2006-06-29
i think inkscape is a good one...its vector graphics and nice rendering...Gimp is another option...the key is to have the background transparent so that you dont get white or black around your icons... 

i havent made one but i tried seriously...but i know it works...just look for icon samples in 
/usr/share/pixmaps and see the sizes of files and resolution.... saving as .png file helps...other extensions are also possible...

i guess its difficult to write a how to since everyone's talent/skill set differs...

if you install inkscape (its in the repository) and if you look at the tutorials then you will get a better idea...the tutorial is unique as in the way they demonstrate the samples. tool by tool listing.. i was excited by the opportunities inkscape offers...

i visualize gimp as an editing program rather than a creating one...

hope this helps..
