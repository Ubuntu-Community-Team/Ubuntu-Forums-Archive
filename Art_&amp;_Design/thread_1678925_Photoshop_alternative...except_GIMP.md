Inkscape & Karbon14 are both vector drawing programs (more like Adobe's Illustrator program), and not good for image editing or painting. 

Blender, while excellent, is aimed more at creating 3D art and animation (like Big Buck Bunny), not really useful for image-editing. 

CinePaint is a beautiful program, but is rather challenging to install, is very resource intensive and basically overkill for someone that just needs photo-editing and design capabilities for print or web work.

Pinta is just like Paint.net, not really something I'd think to consider when someone asks for a Photoshop replacement. 

Xara is nice enough, but, like Gimp, it will still take some time to learn if someone's been a long-time Photoshop user. Also Xara doesn't seem to grow and improve in the same way as Gimp, and definitely doesn't have the same supportive community.

There are a lot of tools available for Artists and photographers, and it isn't that hard to find tools for simple tasks.  But anyone who has to create work for clients, without using Adobe apps, Gimp is the closest.  

I rarely recommend Photoshop in Wine, due to my own experiences. It's fine for simple things, but attempting large and complex artwork was every bit as frustrating as trying to use Gimp.  So I just dug in and learned Gimp. It didn't really take that long.

I know I'm probably sounding argumentative (if so, I apologise), but my intention is more to address the idea that Gimp can be so easily excluded from the Photoshop discussion. Gimp may not be the only choice, but it is definitely at the top of the class.
