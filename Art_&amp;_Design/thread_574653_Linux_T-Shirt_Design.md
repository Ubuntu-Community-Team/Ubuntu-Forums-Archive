I think you may have more feedback if you also offer a small cut of the profits too - unless the t-shirts will be sold at-cost.

That being said, I'm sure many people **would** be willing to help out for free - but since graphic design for things like this are generally 'one-person jobs', there's a fairly high chance they'll be taking time out of something else to do this. If you're willing to offer some incentive, I reckon you'd get more offers. I myself have done work requested for people who offered something in return - I usually turn down the money or whatever after I've finished, but it's nice to know people respect the time you put in. At the end of the day, your name on a website doesn't pay the bills! I think people just like to know they're not approached just because the 'client' (or whatever you call the person asking you to do something) won't pay for a professional designer.
