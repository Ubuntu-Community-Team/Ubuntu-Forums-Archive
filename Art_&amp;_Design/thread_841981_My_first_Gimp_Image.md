### Post by lyceum on 2008-06-27
It is kind of hard to tell you how to improve it when we really don't know what you are trying to do with it. I think it looks good, like a abstract energy alien. If you are going for realism, you have a long way to go ;)

If you are learning the GIMP I would say to start with either a picture and play with it, making as many different things from it as you can, or Google some how-to's. 

[http://www.gimp.org/tutorials/](http://www.gimp.org/tutorials/)

[http://www.thecredence.com/beginners-tutorial-with-gimp-how-to-manipulate-photos/](http://www.thecredence.com/beginners-tutorial-with-gimp-how-to-manipulate-photos/)

You can also buy a book:

[http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-GIMP-Novice-Professional-Second/dp/1430210702/ref=pd_bbs_sr_4?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1214576704&sr=8-4](http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-GIMP-Novice-Professional-Second/dp/1430210702/ref=pd_bbs_sr_4?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1214576704&sr=8-4)
