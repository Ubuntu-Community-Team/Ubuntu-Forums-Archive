### Post by alex.rayu on 2008-11-18
It looks professional. But a few things which I think of when I look at this design.

1. It's quite simple. Simple does not mean bad, don't get me wrong. But. What if you were ordered also a left panel with top ten of "10 Top Pieces" and "Testimonials", and a right panel with "Related videos" block, "Upcoming events" block, and a login box. And what if you were asked to have a number of areas on the front page, like "Welcome", "Our latest piece", "The next great event", etc.?

That would really stretch you, since GIMP works badly with layers and does not have layer folders and layer comps. So you are **forced** into a simple design. 

2. And the worst, is what if after showing your mockup to the client, he tells you to change quite a few things? This is where it will get a nightmare for you. Text and shapes get rasterized in GIMP and can not be edited. They have to be redone!

So, making a complex design in GIMP is a huge waste of time and resource. Good for you if the client does not request changes to be made, does not request more complex elements added, or does not request a PSD file (which is a given standard in Web Design, because it's so editable). If one or more of these things happen, this is when you run out of luck.
