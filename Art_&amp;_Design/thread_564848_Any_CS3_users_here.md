### Post by Dogsisgood on 2007-10-08
***Legality*** should not bet a problem with a copy of CS3 that you legally own.  You can do with it whatever you want.  You can install it on up to two machines, if I read my licensing agreement correctly.

I doubt that there is any legal mumbo-jumbo about installing it on a machine that is running Windows, and ONLY Windows.  Even if there was, but you were complying with all of the other terms of the license agreement, I doubt that Adobe would do anything about, even if they found out.  Adobe is only out to protect itself from being ripped off.  In my opinion they go way too far, but they really won't bother you if you are experimenting around with your time and effort, and doing it legally.  Besides, you might wind up helping them by showing a whole new market that CS3 can work on their (Linux/Ubuntu) computers.

***Support*** is another matter, though.  Adobe is not going to support you running CS3 under virtualization on a Linux box.  Period.  You will be on your own if it does not work properly.

The only problem that I can see is that if you need to deactivate your Ubuntu/virtualization install of CS3, and deactivation does not work properly, you are going to be doing a lot of explaining to Adobe Activation Support about why you need an extra activation number when you go to reinstall it on a plain Windows machine.  There is an outside chance that they might even deny it to you.  They are usually pretty decent, but rarely, one hears about them being mean.

BTW, when I do it, I plan on doing the activation by telephone, NOT letting CS3 do it automatically over the internet.  It just gives me a little more control, but not much.  I also have the activation request code, and the activation key in my possession that way if problems do occur later on.
