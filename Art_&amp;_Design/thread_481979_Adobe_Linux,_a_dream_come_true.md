### Post by rhardie on 2007-06-24
I use Adobe Lightroom for my digital photography and I posted on their user forum for Lightroom about porting over to Linux.  There were some good comments pro and con.

The biggest two or three issues are:

No color management for Linux.  There needs to be some user friendly way to calibrate a monitor or flat panel
Driver Support - not enough drivers are in place at this time to support the graphics cards
Market Share vs Cost to Develop - Linux has historically not held more than 2.5% market share and the development costs can not be recovered.

I made recommendations, as did one or two other rational minds, that Adobe keep an eye on Linux and the open source software that is being developed.

My first recommendation would be that someone develop a color management system for Linux and then get Pantone, maker of Huey display calibration tool or the Spyder calibration tool to develop something to run with Linux's new color management system.

Rocky
Avid photographer
Austin, Texas
