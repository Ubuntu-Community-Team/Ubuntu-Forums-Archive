### Post by tommywright on 2011-06-16
I'm sorry but the answer is no.  You would need an image with multiple exposures (the definition of an HDRI) in order to have the information you need to tonemap (the process of using that data to create a more dynamic image).  You can't just take a regular image and tonemap it... it doesn't have enough visual information.  It's really not complicated... it's just hard to explain.  :P
