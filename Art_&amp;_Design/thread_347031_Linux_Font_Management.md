### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2007-01-27
I, too, wanted to keep extra fonts out of the system files. I found fonty python -- the limitation is truetype and coming soon, openType. Type1 fonts, with their two files to make a font, can be a problem especially coming from the PC versions. ie you have filenames like helv___bk.ps and helv_rm.tm --> just getting the proper pieces recognized by the system can be a problem. I know, I coverted files from customers at a print shop for a long, long time. TrueType makes a better font. The OpenType standard will be cool because we won't be stuck with "locale" so much anymore. Use FontForge to convert. If you have Mac fonts, search the forums about a HowTo grab the data side of a Mac forked-file.
