### Post by MattDTownsend on 2007-06-27
Of course -- you can use whatever colours you wish.  However, you need to edit the list of colours to do so.  This is done for printing purposes, eg, if you wanted to do a two colour printing, or somesuch, it's better to have a list of colours than arbitrarily chosen wheel values.  Even CMYK has certain limitations.

In Scrubus 1.3.3.9, I go to the Edit menu, and then Colors.  From there, I can add whatever I like.  The "Remove Unused" button is quite useful, as well, since it will remove all of those lovely Scribus presets (which are included, at the expense of disk space, in every SLA document you create.  Again, printing purposes).  This way, you won't have to weed through all of theirs, and any of them that you are using won't be removed from the list.

Avoid very bright magentas and chartreuses, as most CMYK printers will not be able to accurately reproduce them.
