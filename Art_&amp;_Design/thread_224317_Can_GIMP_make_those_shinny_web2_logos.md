### Post by commodore on 2006-07-28
In inkscape you can make vector graphics and usually that is what's used for printing. GIMP allows you to make only raster graphics. If you want to make the logo for a website only you can make it in gimp too. I can't write a tutorial how to make a specific logo because they are all made differently but I can tell you how to make a reflection:

1. Create you're logo on one layer with a transparent background
2. Duplicate the layer (right click on the layer in the layers box)
3. Choose Layer>Transform>Flip Vertically
4. Move the new layer in place
5. Right click on the layer in the layers box and choose Add Layer Mask.
6. Now choose the Gradient tool (press L), make sure you have black and white as colors, choose "FG to BG (RGB)" as "gradient:" and "linear" as "shape:".
7. Drag from down to up on the reflection layer.

### Post by commodore on 2006-07-29
That reflection is also easy to do.

1. Create you're logo
2. Create a new layer
3. Right click on the logo layer and choose Alpha to Selection
4. Choose Edit>Fill with BG color
5. Pick the path tool and create a selection of what you want to cut off the white
6. Click on "Create selection from path" in the toolbox
7. Choose Edit>Clear
8. In the layers box choose overlay as mode
9. Modify the opacity of the layer to your likeness
