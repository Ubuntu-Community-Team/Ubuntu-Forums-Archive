### Post by Next.Step on 2007-03-29
I don't think that would be a good step. Brown is Ubuntu, Ubuntu is brown. Its color palette is one of the most recognizable trademarks of Ubuntu.
Also it wouldn't be consistent enough, and a completely new palette wouldn't make Ubuntu recognizable nor for intermediates, nor for newbies. And the feeling of "continuousness" would also dissapear. By that I mean that when I install a new version of Ubuntu, I would realize that it is something new, but I still realize that it's still Ubuntu. When talking about default stuff, these details like consistency and recognizable paletteb are important, because they appear on reviews and the default look en-/discourages new users to get the distro. It is also important because there are so many GNOME distros around, and they mainly differ in artwork, panels layout and support. So these three should be convincing enough to get that distro.
It is not a good idea to implement this by default,(it would create confusion) however, it would be nice to develop this idea as a side project which could be downloaded separately as a "color pack".
my 0.02$

### Post by daynah on 2007-03-29
It's also a fact that many others love and adore the brown. Brown is the new black. :) Also, doesn't debian change colors every upgrade? We'd be one less step different and Ubuntu! and one more step, just like what we used to be.

I think a fun idea for a family release theme would be to embrace the brown. Have (not as the default wall paper, but as other included wallpapers) the chosen animal... colored brown. Or have learning wallpapers of things in the world that are brown like a cross-section of dirt with all the worms and such. There are definately things we can do to have fun wallpapers for kids (the streaks of colors aren't very interesting for an active kids mind, but they're there because everyone wanys a clean look).

Sometimes we become our most creative when we are restricted. Pick up a camera and go into the world and restrict yourself to things only brown. Pick up a paint brush and only paint in brown. It forces you to look at things differently, with a different, wider eye. It makes your brush work in ways you never thought to use it... normally you would have just changed the color.
