### Post by gschoppe on 2008-04-28
@Mr.Wellington:  I'm not sure how well my techniques carry over to the gimp, because I still use windows and mac for my design work.

I did these in Corel (formerly Jasc) Paintshop Pro...

essentially 4 steps:

[LIST]
[*]duplicate background layer
[*]use "colorize" or "hue/saturation" control on the background layer
[*]play with blending modes and opacity of the duplicate layer
[*]use the curves tool to bring out the details
[/LIST]

the first three steps are the big ones, using the colorize or hue/saturation tools tend to leave either a very drab image, or create odd color pairings... correctly blending the original with the colorized version tends to bring back a bit of the original "feel" that a piece starts out with.

I use this same sort of technique with tone-mapped/hdr photos and really any image where I've played with the color/brightness/curves too freely... I purposely overshoot the mark, then tone it back with the original... it minimizes blown out colors and over-saturation, plus you can vary the strength of your effects with selective erasing and multiple layers.
