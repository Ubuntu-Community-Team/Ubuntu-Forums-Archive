I got the idea to make a 3D apartment based on the place my Fanfiction Character would be staying. The layout is similar to that of Rei Ayanami's apartment from NGE, however, this is...cleaner (to be polite.)

Here's the top-down sketch:
[http://imagebin.ca/view/GxCnk9.html](http://imagebin.ca/view/GxCnk9.html)

The next day I fired up 3DS Max 2009 and laid out the walls, doors, and window. After that I began furnishing the place. Unfortunately, I lack the time needed to model the furniture myself so I hit the net and downloaded some free models Like the kitchen featured here:
[http://imagebin.ca/view/0xkNa0Fu.html](http://imagebin.ca/view/0xkNa0Fu.html)

Once all was in order, bathroom, living area, and kitchenette; I began adding appropriate materials, adjusting where necessary. To allow me to get realistic looking textures and materials; I switched from Max's "Default Scanline Renderer" to the "Mental Ray Shader."

This of course opened up 75% more of the materials I had at my disposal, and allowed me to further expand on ideas, and setups. Most of the objects, like this door below will be a "Multi/Sub-object Material" using the "ProMaterials" Libraries within Max. Further alterations - demonstrated below - show what one can do with glass, wall paints, and a freshly laid wooden floor:
[http://imagebin.ca/view/p_zWmnVM.html](http://imagebin.ca/view/p_zWmnVM.html)

Ok so the apartment from [this thread]("http://zunited.net/showthread.php?23237-Apartment-Construction") ready for lighting.

I'm splitting the project into two files. One for day (which I think is done.) And one for Night.

Here's a test of the day project.

I added the Tokyo skyline to fit the [fan fiction]("http://blackwood550.110mb.com/fanfiction.html") setting. 

[http://imagebin.ca/view/w_wNXLR.html](http://imagebin.ca/view/w_wNXLR.html)

The first part of my fan fiction takes place in 2015 Tokyo III, during the Neon Genesis Evangelion story timeline. The first few chapters. But I just couldn't get my ideas on the apartment out of my head. The apartment layout is based on the layout Rei Ayanami had during her "tenure" with NERV. This apartment is...not her's. (to be polite.) I'm not modeling that either...

The Tokyo shot was just a jpg taken from the internet. I made a plane in 3DS MAX, and applied material. Inside the material is a defuse map of tokyo, giving it the "illusion" of an actual skyline. I had to crank the "Self-Illumination" up to give the illusion of daylight.

However, the image does NOT project the light. I added a mental ray Area Spot light and placed it infront of the my skyline. I set the target towards the middle of the apartment's living room. 

By default's it set to 1 (multiplier) it left the room a bit dark. Shown in the above shot. I've come to rest at a value of 5, since it's pretty close.
