### Post by Ioky on 2010-03-25
Have been watching GNOME SHELL video for long time, but I haven't actually try it myself. So today I finally did it.

Very First Impression: Annoying.....

I know complaining doesn't produce anything, but I just have to point out this two problems. (to me, they are seriously problem. And they are the facts, what determine I will use GNOME or NOT)

Problem #1:

Activity button: I don't know if that I am the only one, but I find that Activity button super annoying. Every time you pass it, the entire desktop shift. Not just that, everything within all these desktop shift. I think it is totally cool when I first see it, but hated it sense the second time. Imagine when you are watching TV. Every time you pass a button in your remote, the screen zoom out and show you all this thing you don't really need. It just keep zooming in and out and in and out and in and out..... I know animation is &#8220;Impressive!&#8221; and people have been wishing it for years. But really, are those zooming and shifting solve any problem, and make anything better? OK, now you want to open an App Zoom and Shift, open a file Zoom and Shift, doing whatever, Zoom and Shift. If you don't know what I am talking about, just take a look at any video about GNOME Shell. That is pretty much all you see is Zooming and Shift. 

I mean they can totally just add a button/shortcut call &#8220;Show all Desktop&#8221; which allows you to add and delete desktop and review what is on that Desktop. And OK now you can add up to 1000s of Desktops or even 10s, So what? What can you possibly do with it?  You will only dealing at max 4 App at a time, you can open as much as you please and your box can take, but there is no way you can physically or mentally dealing with some many. So why make this Add/Delete feature much big deal? It could be useful, but I think it is more like &#8220;a nice thing to have&#8221; than &#8220;a thing leads to next generation of Desktop&#8221; 

Not to mention that menu on the left is taking ¼ of the screen space, showing things you aren't looking for. How about just add a &#8220;search&#8221; element that you can add or delete as an option on the tradition menu, with will show you what you are searching for? I think that will make the already wonderful traditional much better. 

Problem #2:

Just what's up with that Clock being on Dead Center in the Panel? What? You turn on your computer just to look at that tiny clock see what time is it or something? I mean this can be change easily, but why is it the default? And what's up with those space to the right of that dead center clock. Are GNOME try to tell their User and the rest of the World they need to hire more professional Graphic Designer or something? 

Few Words:

In many way, I think This Next Generation of Graphic User Interface have a lot of Graphical problem. Set the &#8220;look&#8221; aside, and look at it closely, I think is is like a DO a lot but Done little kind of thing. Personally I am not afraid to change, or learn something news. When I first switch to Linux, I do it without even tried it. And switch to 100% Linux from windows in one day. But when I switch, things actually makes sense, and even have stuff that I wanted long time from windows but never get. Like Multi-Desktop, and system monitor applet on panel. It is like heavenly GUI. 

Changing doesn't mean, you take every good out, and put something just different and call it &#8220;Next Generation&#8221; and better. This is just Abusing the word Creativity. Keep trying to be &#8220;Creative&#8221; by doing something differently, without even understand the main problem, yet learn nothing from the past. Things doesn't have be different. Just like now days with have multi-touch technology doesn't mean it is the best solution for a problem. And it clearly doesn't mean the next generation keyboard would be a touch screen keyboard, which is totally stupid, you can't feel the key, and you have to look at it to type. And someone would say OH now you can switch to any keyboard layout as you wish, and it can turn into tablet, and use it as mouse-pad. Right~ is like you know 27 difference keyboard layouts, most people aren't even good with one. Yeah, as cool as new as it might sound like. But all I want to do is just type fast, which is the only thing I cannot do with this things. 

One of the biggest problem with the Activity thing is it is trying to be the &#8220;Master/all in one&#8221; button. It forced the user to address things that they don't actually need. I just want to open an App, why you need to  show me all the Desktops I have on my session? Not to mention 90% of them doesn't have anything on it, and keep annoy me with those &#8220;cool&#8221; graphic. Like Compiz can turn your desktop into a cube, it is cool and I love to have it around, but would people keep doing that to impress themselves? Maybe once for a long while, when they try to impress their friends or they are totally bored and got nothing better to do. It Clearly is great sale point to most people who have never see such thing before, but once you use it, it doesn't do much. It is perfectly fine to have it because Compiz function pretty well, and it do enhance user's experiences, and best part is it is optional. 

I am a GNOME fan for years because it did a great job since I have used it. And I would love to see a better GNOME, so I can still use it. And I do thanks all the Developer's hard work. But just because I love it. Doesn't mean I think everything it does is current. And I think when you see problem. You should speak up. (Maybe not the best place here, but I would like to share the though.)
