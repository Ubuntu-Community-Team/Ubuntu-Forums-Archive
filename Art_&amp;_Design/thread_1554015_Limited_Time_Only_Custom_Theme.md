Greetings all,

**Blue Elegance Theme** features soft, subtle, and neutral earth-tones, which are easy on the eyes, combined with vivid color buttons for ease of use. The overall effect is an understated elegance punctuated by pleasantly powerful eye candy. 

The GTK theme is a modified version of Dust Sand.
The Metacity theme is a modified version of Ambiance.
The icon set is a compilation of Bluestep Humanity, Hi Color, & Gnome

Get the Blue Elegance GTK & Metacity Theme here:

Get the Blue Elegance Icon Set here:

P.S. 
The color scheme is geared for an LCD screen and is so very subtle that it may not view as well on a CRT monitor. Additionally, the screen shots appear bland and do not do it justice. If in doubt, check out the Dust Sand Theme which is, most likely, already installed on your system.

> **BlazeFire247 said:**
> Your theme has a few flaws:

It's true there are some flaws that I'm still working on. Many of which, you wouldn't notice until using alternate sizes of certain icons. Additionally, I respect your views. From a strictly aesthetic point of view, you are correct. From a professional design standpoint, I'm breaking the rules. But then... I am a Maverick. 

However, this theme was designed specifically for my own personal use. It wasn't actually intended for general audiences. It's really only posted here as creative inspiration for others and to illustrate the nearly endless possibilities for custom design. 

It simply amazes me how easy it is to change anything & everything. I'm a noob to Ubuntu & Linux. M$ Windows is extremely  restrictive by comparison.

BTW - originally, I tried 3 dimensional buttons that weren't so bright. Personally, I preferred these. Too many themes are completely soft, faded, and dull. I like dark themes, but I need a few visually distracting bright spots. 

As for the blue Ubuntu Logo, that is not my work... That is from the Bluestep Humanity icon set. But you are correct, it could use a makeover. The folders are also from that set and again I would prefer them a bit darker. Concerning the blue and pink Min & Max buttons, they fall under the 'strictly for my personal gratification' items. They are probably too distracting and a poor color combination for most people.

As for the black text on the top panel, I agree... It is not a good choice. White would be better. I simply haven't hacked around enough to figure out how to change it without effecting the text color of menu & tool bars. Regardless, the black text works on my LCD screen because of the backlight and the viewing angle.

Anyway, thanks for the feedback! It's interesting to read people's responses.
