### Post by AJB2K3 on 2009-09-12
[CENTER]**Creating HDRI images from a jpg image in QTPFSGUI.**[/CENTER]


QTPFSGUI is a graphical front end on windows and linux for the HDRI toolset.

Creating your first HDRI.

First up load up QTPFSGUI,
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_qtpfsgui001.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]QTPFSGUI 1.9.3 on M.S Windows Vista[/SIZE]

Once finished loading click on “New Hdr...”
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/new_hdr.jpg[/IMG]
And the HDR creation wizard will appear.
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_hdrwiz002.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]HDR Creation Wizard step 1 of 2[/SIZE]
Once loaded Click on “Load Images...” and the file selector will appear, Select a file and click OK/Load (depending on OS) and you will be returned to the HDR Creation Wizard step 1 of 2
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_hdrwiz001.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]Hdr Creation Wizard with images and EV values.[/SIZE]

Hopefully you will find the EV meta data will have been found and set, if not you will need to manually set the EV value but putting in a number or by adjusting the slider.
One done Leaving Alignment alone and click on Next >
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_hdrwiz020.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]HDR Creation wizard step 2 of 2[/SIZE]
At this time leave all the setting as shown (Default) and click on Finished.
You will be taken back to the main interface and the image will look like its of low contrast.
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_qtpfsgui002.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]QTPFSGUI with preview image.[/SIZE]
Before moving on click on “Save Hdr as...”
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/saveas.jpg[/IMG]
Give the file a name (don't forget to put .hdr on the end) and click save.
Once saved Click on “Tonemap the Hdr...”
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/tonemaphdr.jpg[/IMG]

### Post by AJB2K3 on 2009-09-12
And the Tonemap wizard will appear.
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_tmwMantiuk.jpg[/IMG]
[SIZE="1"]Tonemap wizard[/SIZE]

Click on ether the drop down box or the tab (depending on version and os) and select “Mantiuk”,
Leave all settings at default except for size and pick on “1024X768” and click on “Apply”.
[IMG]http://i703.photobucket.com/albums/ww40/Adam_Bryant/Tutorial/web_tmwMantiukrender.jpg[/IMG]
After a wait (length depends on pc specs) the tone map image will be generated using the “Mantiuk” routine.
Click on “ Save as ...”, name the file (don't forget to put the .jpg on the end).
Then click on “Exit” to close the Tonemap Wizard then click on “Exit” to close QTPFSGUI.

Sit back and give yourself a pat on the back for creating your first HDR image from a jpg.
