### Post by BslBryan on 2009-05-06
Hello, Marvin666. :-)

Well, if you use the smart scissors to select the outside portion of an item, you are using the wrong tool.  Use the fuzzy select tool, instead.  :-)

That being said, to answer your question, it may be that you are clicking one side of a portion of an image and then the other side, when what you should be doing is creating a path.  

For example, if there is a picture of a smiley face, like this one: :-) , you would click multiple times around it, moving a small amount at a time around the perimeter of the image until it is selected in its entirety.  

Since the only way to cut images in two, though, with the smart scissors, is to click the opposite side of the image, I would definitely try the fuzzy select tool, as I think this is the function you potentially have confused with smart scissors.

I hope I've helped, and best to you. :-)

### Post by BslBryan on 2009-05-07
That's when the smart scissors *can* be pretty handy.  :-)

All you have to do is click around the perimeter of the multicoloured object you'd like to select, and make a path around it.  Then go to Select --> From Path, and you're all set. :-)
