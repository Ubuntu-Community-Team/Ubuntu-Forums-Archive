### Post by IYY on 2006-10-27
I like Ubuntu's brown and orange and I like Gnome's icons. So, I decided to combine the two, creating a tango-ish theme that's almost 100% Gnome in shape, but with a bright new colour scheme. The new colours are a specific type of pale orange and tango-like blue.

I believe that a mistake the Ubuntu artists made (have been making since the very beginning, actually) is to stick to only one colour. Very few professional designs use only a single colour. Most have at least two. So, I think this new look will be nice and uniform, and at the same time bright and not monotonous.

Here's a screenshot of the look:

[[IMG]http://img157.imageshack.us/img157/5811/screenshotilyafilebrowswe2.th.png[/IMG]](http://img157.imageshack.us/my.php?image=screenshotilyafilebrowswe2.png)

The nice thing about this theme is that it's very easy to make. It mostly just involves changing the colours of existing Gnome icons, and sometimes borrowing icons from themes like Tangerine (also Tango based and with similar colours, so they look good together).

If you like this look and want to see a completed theme, consider helping me with some conversions. Very minimal inkscape/gimp(for the smaller icons) skill is needed.
