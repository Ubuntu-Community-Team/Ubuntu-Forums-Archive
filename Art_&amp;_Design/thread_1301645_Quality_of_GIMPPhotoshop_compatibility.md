### Post by kayosiii on 2009-10-26
Photoshop is fairly limited in the file formats it can work with.
XCF is not one of the formats it deals with. 

Photoshop files up to about version 6 have documentation that is available to 3rd party softwares. More recent versions not so. You can expect gimp to be able to read most things that were available in version six but not newer stuff.

Going the other way should be a lot less bothersome. The only thing I am not completely sure about is how the text will translate.

[edit] The description of the job you are doing sounds like something I personally would be much more likely to do in inkscape than in the gimp. In which case you would have quite a few more interesting options at your fingertips, and much better text tools :p[/edit]
