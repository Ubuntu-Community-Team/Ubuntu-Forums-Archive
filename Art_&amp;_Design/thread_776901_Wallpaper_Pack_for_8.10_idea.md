### Post by Mr.Wellington on 2008-05-01
In another thread recently ([here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4847799#post4847799")), it was brought up that most distros have the same default swirly wallpaper pack for every new release.  While not a new idea, it triggered something in my head that has been brewing for a bit. 

The same swirly default wallpaper for every distro is pretty much what can be made in The GIMP in 2 minutes tops. 
I hate to say we need a windows-like background pack, but [The Hamad Darwish Microsoft Vista Wallpaper Pack]("http://www.flickr.com/photos/darwishh/sets/72157594510047657/") is amazing work for just wallpapers to put with your operating system.  Microsoft may be onto something finally. 

While the slightly less well received brown, red, and yellow Heron was a step in the right direction, I believe that the right way is in photography and capturing something truly breathtaking to stare at when your not working. 

Is there a way to get photographs under the same license as the artwork for Ubuntu? Maybe we can attract some people using the rumored 8.10 graphical...uh..."enhancement".  I know some people that would LOVE to not see OMGBROWN when they load up default Ubuntu. 

Is this considerable?
