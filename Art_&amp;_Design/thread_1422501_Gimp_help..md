### Post by ajy0852 on 2010-03-05
There are a number of plugins in the package "gimp-plugin-registry":
```
sudo apt-get install gimp-plugin-registry
```
or search for that package in Synaptic - it has a description that says which plugins are included.

I think most of them are targeted more toward photography though, I don't know what sorts of plugins you would need. It may be best to use GIMP for a while, identify a few shortcomings, and search for particular scripts or plugins to address those.

### Post by quixote on 2010-03-09
If you work with raw format photos at all, then ufraw and the ufraw plugin for gimp are both necessary.  (Both can be installed from synaptic.)  One huge difference between PS and gimp is that gimp handles 16-bit? or even only 8-bit? color depth, tops.  PS will do the full 32-bit? or was it 24-bit?  Anyway, a bunch more.  For professional photographers, that's critical.  For logo type work like sigs, that probably wouldn't matter.  And anything you can do to make sigs easy on the eyes is much appreciated! :mrgreen:

### Post by rmccutchan on 2010-03-10
Glad to see another person switch to Gimp.  I was a CS user until August 2009, and now I only use Gimp for weddings, seniors, but mostly fine art black and white.

One filter I use frequently is the "High Pass" filter for sharpening.  It is not a flexible as using the unsharp mask, but for down and dirty quick sharpening on a lot of pictures, it does fantastic.  I just duplicate a layer, apply the High Pass filter with the default settings, then change the blending mode of the layer to "overlay", and it does a decent job of sharpening.  It is in the Gimp plugin registry:  [http://registry.gimp.org/node/7385](http://registry.gimp.org/node/7385)  Also, the "Sharpen (Smart Redux) filter is another good sharpening filter, but takes longer to apply than High Pass.

There are even filters to convert color photographs into your favorite black and white films:).

Gimp has come a long way in the recent past, and I believe it is about to make a few more big strides this year.  In my opinion, it is just about everything Photoshop 7 was.
