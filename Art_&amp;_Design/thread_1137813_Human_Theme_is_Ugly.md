### Post by jowilkin on 2009-04-25
Does anyone else find the default Human theme really offputting?  I immediately change it whenever I install Ubuntu.

I'm glad to see that Dust is included by default, it is a big step up from Human in my opinion.  I also prefer Clearlooks, New Wave, and Dark Room to Human.  As for themes that are not installed by default, I like Shiki colors a lot, it is very polished and complete.

I think when many people give Ubuntu a test drive and are deciding whether or not to install it instead of, or in addition to, Windows, they most likely base much of their judgement on the look of the OS (i.e. the theme).  Thus an ugly default theme would likely hurt adoption.

So am I alone in finding Human to be an ugly theme or do others agree?

### Post by Sand &amp; Mercury on 2009-04-26

Anyway, my first impression upon seeing the theme back in 7.10 was not at all bad. In comparison to other distros it suffers somewhat, but it is at least unique. Most other distros use Gilouche as their metacity theme and while that's a very nice theme, they start to look the same after a while.

They need to take a leaf out of the OS X school of design; not aping it, but definitely following through on a few principles. Making it more compact and integrated-looking would be a step in the right direction.

I'm talking purely about the window borders here, I find the murrine GTK theme to be very good, as well as the wallpapers.
