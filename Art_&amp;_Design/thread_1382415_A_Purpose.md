### Post by OS Jupiter on 2010-01-15
I hate to say it but so far as an enjoyable OS, Ubuntu doesn't make the cut. I'll admit I'm biased towards Windows XP slightly since I've used it a lot. Vista is at first glance, well, gaudy, though it appears to be a fair OS from what I've seen. Mac OS 10.5-6 is just amazing to me. Compared to Ubuntu it just feels better. It's not the eye-candy at all, it's a feeling that I'm using what a computer should be.
    We should rethink the reason were making Ubuntu. Are we doing it so we can say "Ha! We made an OS!" or are we motivated by something else? In the early days of computing you wrote software to have fun and to "change the world". I'm devoted to Ubuntu now not because I feel it's a great OS but because there's a guilt trip that it's my fault Ubuntu is not truly great. Look at what Steve Jobs did to the original 1984 Macintosh; he wouldn't give up till it was perfect, and I believe that because of that, he made something great. The idea of an open source world is false. The greatest open source achievements are often company-funded. Projects like Firefox succeed because there is a physical team in the middle that can physically communicate. In Google teams are limited to five people to encourage friends and work relationships.  In the "open source world" we must cling to some belief so we accept things like "Windows sucks" or "payware sucks" or "proprietary software sucks" as a guiding philosophy when it should be to make something (as  Steve Jobs would put it) insanely great.
