### Post by Grimhound on 2010-06-09
With the bar system, I mean. They've been using GNOME, and honestly the deviations I've seen whether they be GNOME Shell or Unity seem to be ineffective as compared to the basic GNOME 2 that has come with everything to 10.04. I have GNOME configured in a similar fashion to how Windows' toolbar operates, with one solid bar at the top or bottom of my screen with pretty much everything I need. It's smart, it fits into a 15 pixel width, and most importantly it's clean. What I've seen of GNOME Shell or Unity seems to be an emphasis on some artistic imperative rather than the utility and operational worth of the amount of screen space taken up. GNOME is like a swiss army knife tooled to meet most challenges you need. I'd prefer to see that versatility improved upon rather than for the experimental attempts to cost Ubuntu valuable development time. The wheel has been invented, and I feel they should just roll with it.

Clarification: The title is based on one of my biggest desires being an icon-only auto-grouping bar for GNOME. I do not agree with the fat style that was adopted with the default Windows 7, however I like that they were attempting to address issues of space and convenience rather than trying something entirely new.

Icons are meant to identify the program, and so they are a small representation of that program. Windows 7 does the multiple instances in two ways. With Aero active, you get text and a box containing a living image of the program. Without Aero, you get a classic dropdown of all the different instances running as full text.
