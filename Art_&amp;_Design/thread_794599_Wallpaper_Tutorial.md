### Post by Tux.Ice on 2008-05-14
Tutorial for -gabe-noob-:popcorn:

1.Create a new image in the gimp (1600x1200).

2.Delete the 'Backround' layer and create two new transparent layers. 

3.Set your foreground to #FF6309 and backround to #FFB515

4.Select the gradient tool (press 'L') and do the gradient from the bottom right corner to the top left. 

5.Now set your foreground to #C90016 and select your second top transparent layer. 

6.Now re-select the gradient tool and make sure its on radial. now drag from the middle bottom to middle top. MAKE SURE IT IS STRAIGHT!!!!!

7.Finally set the transparency to 40.7%. :)
[B]
SO NOW THE BACKGROUND IS DONE[/B].

*now for the text:*

1.select your font and use the text tool to type in whatever text you   want. Dont forget to move it to where you want it to finnaly be.

2.now right click on your text layer and select 'alpha to selection'

3.click new layer, select white.

4.Now click 'Ctrl-I' and then 'Del'.

5.Now for the gaussian blur. select Filters>Blur>Gaussian Blur.
  *For the values put the X & Y as 10 to 20(make sure both X and Y are the same, i like to use 15 as a value).*

6.Now position your new layer under your text layer.
[B][COLOR="Red"][SIZE="5"]
And thats how you do it![/SIZE][/COLOR][/B]
