### Post by MenZa on 2007-10-25
A few years ago, there was a discussion about whether someone should design a Certificate of Authenticity for Ubuntu---a kind of mock of Windows 95/98/ME/2000/XP/Vista/<proprietary software here>'s.

I've done so (thanks to MasterCheese for the idea), and I bring you the certificate of authenticity:

[[img]http://i24.tinypic.com/rsbt7c.png[/img]](http://menza.org/upload/ubuntucert.svg)

(Click the image for a vector version).

The barcode matches the value of the key, which is the HEX value of "GNU/LINUX", but I had to add '01' to the end of the key for the number of figures to balance out. 

It's released under the [GNU GPL](http://gnu.org/licenses/gpl.html), so feel free to copy/edit/redistribute it under the terms listed in the license, and don't forget to leave comments!

Feel free to print it and stick it to your laptop, desktop, server, toaster, cat (etc).---bonus points if you include pictures.

**Edit:** Tiny edit, fixed some spacing issues between barcode and small Ubuntu logo.
