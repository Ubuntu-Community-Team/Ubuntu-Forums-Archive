### Post by Hobo Joe on 2008-04-06
At the beginning, it's very difficult, it's really hard to get over the hump where it goes from being 'I don't know how to do anything so all I can do is follow tutorials step-by-step', to where you have finally accumulated enough knowledge to make something of your own.

But I assume that'd be pretty much the same with any app. The biggest detractor for new users is the Blender GUI, which, at first glance, looks like a nightmare. But later you find it's one of the best desigend, most intuitive GUI's you've ever used.


So my words to new users would be to hang in there!

### Post by vboblinux on 2008-05-18
When I first used blender I couldn't do anything on it. Not even a viewport changing or extrude a face or anything. :P
It was so strange and different as an application. Many times I got frustrated in the first 5 minutes...!!! :P
Then I readed the manuals at internet and find the shortcuts and the tools.
It was a matter of a week to learn most of the basics of polygon modeling in blender and the "commonly used tools". 
I had some kind of experience and knowledge of 3d modeling in general, before I use blender for first time though.

I believe that most users at first look get frustrated and have a tend to quit learning blender, but...once they realize it's intelligent interface and power...they will love it...!!! Blender is one of the most amazing applications ever. It's Interface is Amazing. My opinion is that maybe it is the first program on earth that the developers took it very seriously in the "user friendly" and "speed" matters (Selection tools, Knife tools, Face Rotations, etc, Rotations, Scaling, etc). It is a Very Powerful application that "Don't Waste Your Time" when creating something with it (once you learn it). As it gets more and more powerful, Professional users will love it (wasted time = wasted money for them). :-)
It is like learning to drive a car..!!! Once you learn it and feel comfort...you rock...:-)

3D Modeling is not easy to learn, but I believe that blender is an "easy to learn" program, but you must be ready to unlock yourself from your "old/obsolete habits" and.... "think different"...!!! :-) :popcorn:
