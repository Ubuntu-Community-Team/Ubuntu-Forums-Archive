### Post by hxcobd on 2010-05-16
A few of these are kind of moot:

1.) The shot of synaptic that says "window shadow needed" generally DOES have a shadow, if you're using any kind of compositing at all. All windows have shadows on my system, using Metacity compositing.

2.) The maximized shot of KDE's Dolphin is kind of subjective. I thought them putting CLI comments in the bottom center was pretty clever, actually.

3.) I don't understand the shot with a huge vertical arrow saying, "large mouse movement length." Basically every OS is laid out this way (OSX dock on bottom of screen while program controls are at top; Windows has panel at bottom while program controls are at top...)

4.) A lot of your critiques about color choices/gradients/contrast changes are completely dependent upon the user's themes and color scheme. All of those things are totally customizable in linux; it's essentially impossible to regulate something like that with open theming.

I basically agree with all of your KDE criticisms, though. KDE is an absolutely horrendously laid-out environment. It's pretty clear they were striving for uniqueness, which is cool to look at for like 10 minutes, but it's an absolute chore to actually use... It needs a total overhaul, IMO.
