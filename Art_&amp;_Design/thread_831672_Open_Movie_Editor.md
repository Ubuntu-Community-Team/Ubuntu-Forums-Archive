Blender offers four types of video effects:
[LIST]
[*]Built-in effects (see [here](http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Manual/VSE_Built-in_Effects))
[*]External plug-in effects (see [here](http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Manual/Sequence_Plugins))
[*]Compositor effects (see [here](http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Manual/Compositing_Nodes))
[*]Renderer effects (e.g. video textures, see [here](http://wiki.blender.org/index.php/Manual/Animated_Image_Textures))
[/LIST]

Sound in Blender uses ALSA by default. In earlier versions you had to explicitly select ALSA by setting an environment variable (export SDL_AUDIODRIVER=alsa). You can still use this variable to select another sound system (e.g. ESD). I don't know how you got the idea that sound requires compiling Blender from source. It's not true. All you have to do is click the speaker button.

### Post by eye208 on 2008-06-18
> **DAC1138 said:**
> These are all great points. My problem with blender being developed for a NLE application is that it's become more and more resource intensive. I used to be able to edit hour-long documentaries on a 2.2 Ghz system with a gigabyte of RAM. With blender, it's having trouble on that same system with a 6 minute video clip. Memory management of the Blender VSE was redone for version 2.42. There is a cache limiter now. The VSE can easily handle hours of video in a single timeline.

If scrubbing is slow, it's because you are editing material that has already been compressed for distribution. For example, if you load a MPEG-4 clip with a GOP length of 250 into the VSE and scrub back and forth, the VSE may have to decompress up to 249 P- and B-frames on the fly to display the frame you are pointing at. This will happen with _every_ video editor. Sorry, you can't defeat the laws of physics here. The only workaround is proxy editing. Professional video editors support proxy editing for exactly that reason.

If you want to avoid proxy editing, keep your video in DV format, MJPEG, or MPEG Intra (i.e. with a GOP length of 1) until you are done with editing. As long as the whole stream consists of I-frames only there's no need for a proxy.
