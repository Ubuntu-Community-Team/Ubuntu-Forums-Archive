### Post by lode on 2007-07-27
It looks really slick, but I'm not sure this is the kind of advertising Ubuntu would profit from.

But that mainly has to do with the fact that I've never found the Ubuntu slogan 'Linux for human beings' that suitable, either. I personally think Ubuntu should present itself more as an alternative (to the proprietary options), free, complete and usable operating system rather than a usable Linux variant (which might leave many people to wonder whatever *that* may be). The 'perfect advertisement' (an oxymoron), I imagine, would breathe a sense of easiness and 'fun working experience' (another oxymoron, in most cases :P), people at first wouldn't associate with Linux (if they even knew what it was).

The kind of advertisement I suggest here doesn't even have to be directly advertising Ubuntu, but rather the FOSS movement. Albeit by immediately proposing Ubuntu as *the* most usable solution (rather than, let's say, Fedora), the would-be Ubuntu user doesn't have to be inaugurated into the world of FOSS to begin using a free operating system (Ubuntu, in this case).

Your ad doesn't really tell anything. It's only two CD's. 'So, this new music CD from this group called Ubuntu, 'Linux for human beings', is it any good?'

Of course all of these thoughts are linked to a certain audience (the general public, rather than some 'nerds' for whom the tagphrase 'Linux for human beings' is perfectly usable).

On another note: does Ubuntu even still ship with two CD's (a yellow and a red one) nowadays?
