I don't know if this is the right place to put it. But I've read somewhere that there is no forum for photographers on UF.

> Bibble 5 is an ambitious project to revolutionize Digital Photographic Workflow. Expanding on Bibble 4's speed and power, Bibble 5 is designed to unite unparalleled photographic editing capability and blazing speed in a sleek, modern interface.

It is competing against Adobe Lightroom, and the result is really fantastic. It is proprietry and isn't free. But I do support proprietry software that makes photographers life easier on linux.

The best part is: ATM it is free. The release has postponed a couple of times, and they decided to release some preview versions before the final (also to get feedback etc...). You can request a free license here:
[http://www.bibblelabs.com/products/bibble5/preview/](http://www.bibblelabs.com/products/bibble5/preview/)

This is the first preview release that is quite stable for me. Bare in mind that some features are still not included and some cameras have yet to be supported:
[http://www.bibblelabs.com/products/bibble5/specs.html](http://www.bibblelabs.com/products/bibble5/specs.html)

The actual release date is still a well-guarded secret.

Thought I'd share this with you guys.

Cheers

### Post by xv1700 on 2009-09-09
I spent my money on Bibble back in 2006. It allowed me to transition to Linux whilst still being able to do everything I need to with photography. It costs money (but not a lot) but when you look at the features they have brought together on the Linux platform it is fantastic. It is the only realistic alternative I have found to Adobe software and comes at a fraction of the cost.

They are obviously struggling as a company at the moment as you can see by their continued delays in the release of version 5. Saying that, version 4 is great anyway; just a shame they shot themselves in the foot by offering the version 5 carrot far too early.

I agree that trialware can be very annoying but GIMP is not photo editing software. If you want to do proper photo editing and management Bibble is great. The 30 day trial is plenty of time to realise that it does most of the things you can do with Adobe software and there are a number of features that are significantly better. Best bits? Try out the 'Perfectly Clear Technology' and the 'Andy Black and White' plugin.

I think Bibble may benefit from producing an Open Source 'not for commerical use' style delivery but they would have to cut out a large number of features to keep their revenue stream going.
