### Post by Minipalmer on 2010-03-08
Hey guys, I'm running Ubuntu 9.10 and loving it.

I installed KDE on a flash drive just to play with it when I saw they have revamped. It looks so amazing. Their designers did fantastic work. It has its own very unique identity, and everything is very sharp and glassy. Super appealing to the eye.

I didn't change to KDE (despite loving eye candy) because I am used to Ubuntu's synaptic and such. Just didn't want to make the change. But now I am thinking about how we can bring Ubuntu up to par to compete with how nice KDE looks. Here are some of my ideas:

**New Panel: **Many people are beginning to use Docky or AWN as their default panels now. The standard Gnome panel looks like it belongs in the 90s. We need something slick, simple, and fresh. I've even heard AWN proposed as the new panel for Gnome, but I don't see that ever happening.

**New Icon Theme: **I was actually very impressed with the Humanity theme when I first saw it. It was consistent and clean. Still a little low-definition though. It would be smart for Ubuntu to adopt a theme like Hydroxygen or Eikon that is very complete and hi-definition. Scalable icons are becoming more and more popular with docks.

**New Theme: **This is fairly obvious, and one of the more heated topics of 10.04. I'm actually a big fan of human themes if they are made properly. The tan themes are very unique from any other OS and gives Ubuntu an identity. I can see why Ubuntu is looking to change. Many people suggest blue, but that is what nearly every OS uses. I am disappointed in Ubuntu trying to look like Mac though.

**New task manager: **Mac uses the dock, Win7 has the new taskbar, and Ubuntu needs to catch up. Many people are installing DockBar(X) just for a new fresh way manage windows. I feel like Ubuntu will need something new like this right out of the box. Probably not a dock though, as these are easy to install and many people do not like them.

**New Menu: **The "Applications/Places/Systems" menu is getting fairly outdated and most people replace it with just one icon or GnoMenu or something. Linux Mint and KDE have both developed pretty solid menus away from this that work well. Ubuntu should too.

I realize that I'm not the most Ubuntu-educated guy around, and I'm not really up to date with Ubuntu ditching compiz and picking up Gnome3 or whatever it is they are doing. But this is just my $.02.

**[SIZE="5"]What do you guys think?[/SIZE]**
