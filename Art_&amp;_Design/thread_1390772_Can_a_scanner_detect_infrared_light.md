### Post by jocko on 2010-01-26
> **sofasurfer said:**
> Computers use millions of colors. Are any of those colors infrared?
Scanners scan collors. Are any of those colors infrared?
I wounder if a scanner can be used to detect faded pencil writing on an old photo just as infrared film is used for that purpose?
Computers use millions of colors if they are set to use 24 bit color. Only light that is detected by the human eye can be of any color, so since the IR spectrum is outside of the visible spectrum it is *not* a color. It would be a waste of memory and resources to encode a range of "colors" that can't be seen anyway, so the IR spectrum is *not* part of the "millions of colors" that makes up the 24 bit/pixel color space.
So I really doubt that a scanner that is designed to scan only visible pictures would have both an IR radiation source and an IR detector.
