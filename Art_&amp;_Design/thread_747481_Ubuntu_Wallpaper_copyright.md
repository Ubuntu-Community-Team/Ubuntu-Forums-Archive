### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2008-04-07
> If I can release them exclusively for ubuntu Linux, I'd be more than happy to do so.


I'm not sure that is possible without developing your own licence.  You can certainly protect against unauthorized commercial use under a GPL but not, as far as I'm aware, against a particular OS.  Pity.


On image sites generally,

Another alternative space is deviantart.com - a lot of linux users post images there too.  I do find DeviantArt a bit too large and you have upload your work into a one category only (eg. landscape photography OR wallpaper).  But a lot of professionals and semi-pros seem to use it.

You are more than welcome to use [OptickleArt]("http://cafelinux.org/OptickleArt/") to post your images.  OptickleArt was started by some Ubuntu forum members (including me) to have a Linux-centric gallery space.  It is mainly for private/amateur use (you can create your own gallery space and upload images there, then link to them from elsewhere) but is open to all legitimate users.

The galleries on the Ubuntu Forums would also be appropriate but they have a file size limit that would prevent high res images.

Good luck.
