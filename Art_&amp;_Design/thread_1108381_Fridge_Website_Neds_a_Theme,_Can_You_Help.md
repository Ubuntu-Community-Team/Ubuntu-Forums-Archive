### Post by MattNuzum on 2009-03-27
Hello community members,

[The Fridge]("http://fridge.ubuntu.com") has been neglected for a while and is coming nowhere close to achieving it's full potential. Tons of people visit this site regularly to get Ubuntu related news. It needs a theme, can you help?

Three steps:

**Step 1: Mock Up - DUE Thursday April 2nd**

The Ubuntu News team will select a mockup and provide feedback needed for final polish. You do NOT need to create a full-blown design here, just enough to sell your idea.

This is purely a *graphic design* job, submit your mockups and post them as attachments in the Phase 1 section of [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme)

Following phases will create HTML/CSS and a Drupal theme. I realize that some people who are great at making designs may not know how to make drupal themes and those who can do drupal stuff may not be great at design. Lets make this a team effort (because I know we've got talent in all the necessary areas).

*** The goal is to GO LIVE in time for Jaunty release. ***

**Here are the requirements:**

== Design ==

[LIST]
[*] A new visual appearance, harmonious with the ubuntu website but not **just another ubuntu knock off**

[*] My suggestions would be to stick with the colours and fonts of the ubuntu site but not feel constrained to using the rounded borders with the top right drop down nav

[*] Attractive, people will feel the site is credible

[*] A layout that looks like a news website

[*] Possibly include a special layout for Ubuntu Weekly News, since it is a regular item and has a different format than the other news stories
[/LIST]

== Content Types ==

This is the types of content people will view on the site:

[LIST]
[*]News stories
[*]UWN
[*]Events
[/LIST]

== Blocks ==

These side-bar items will be shown on the site, this may change over time

[LIST]
[*]Popular content
[*]Syndication/rss
[*]Events
[*]Search
[*]Video podcast
[/LIST]

Discussion will take place on the [Web Presence Team mailing list]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo/ubuntu-website") but I'll subscribe to this thread so you can ask questions here and I'll reply.

### Post by MattNuzum on 2009-03-28
Regarding your job, alex, there is going to be about three tasks:

 * graphic design
 * html/css
 * drupal theme

If you are a drupaler the last step may be a very good way to contribute. Different people can do each step (or one person can do all steps if appropriate).

If you have a visual design in mind then post a mockup to the wiki. If you'd rather help with the drupal or html part then hold tight and let the news team pick a design.

To post something to the wiki, go to this page:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme) and log in (uses launchpad login)
Then click the attachments button and attach your file. After completion you'll see your attachment in the list and at the right side of the line showing your attachment will be something that looks like this:

  [[attachment:your-file-name.png]]

Copy that (including the square brackets). Then edit the page, scroll down to the phase 1 section and add a paragraph with your name, any notes you feel are useful and then paste in that attachment bit of code. Then hit save.

Glad to see you on the team, I appreciate your willingness to help. If you don't feel like making a graphic design but do want to do the drupal stuff then just hold tight, in a little over a week we'll need that skillset.

### Post by MattNuzum on 2009-04-03
Hello team, there is still three more days to submit themes. In order to aid the process I've requested that key members of the news team check out what we have and give me some feedback. The fridge team is very thankful for the work done so far and is getting excited to see this project come to fruition. Here's some direction:

Everyone liked the colours and vibrancy of "Sand and Mercury" [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme?action=AttachFile&do=view&target=mockup3.png](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Website/FridgeTheme?action=AttachFile&do=view&target=mockup3.png) but felt it relied too much on articles having graphics, which does not happen often enough to count on.

The team preferred that the new theme have the rounded borders over a coloured background.

Instead of featuring full-stories on the homepage one paragraph teasers should be shown, sometimes (but not always) with a picture to go with them.

The "Work in Progress" theme was probably liked the most so far because it looks like a news site and does not rely on articles to have accompanied graphics.

Overall the feeling of the theme should be simple and clean, the colours should not be grey, preferably not pastels, and there should be a clear distinction between the page's content area and the background. Regarding the page background, they'd prefer something other the current brown on ubuntu.com but not eye-bleedingly bright. Texture, gradient or a subdued background image would be OK. For example, [www.marcus-fischer.com](www.marcus-fischer.com)  twitter.com and [http://betalabs.nokia.com](http://betalabs.nokia.com) all were recognized, especially the first of these.

A note from me is that this site needs to honour the Ubuntu logo branding guidelines and be full-colour, un-modified and have the "Ubuntu" be either white or black. Also, I'd encourage you to not use the Ubuntu title font and to avoid over-styling the text.

So has a design been selected yet? NO, there's three more days. You can still help. Can you incorporate the above feedback into a layout and design and submit it before the end of the day Monday?

Great work so far, thanks to everyone who has helped!
