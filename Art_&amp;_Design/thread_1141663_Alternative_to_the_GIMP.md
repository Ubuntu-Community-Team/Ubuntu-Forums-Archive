### Post by mpt on 2009-05-03
> **Saint Angeles said:**
> i think the term "photoshop" is offensive. i mean... photo? shop? eeewww. photos could contain nudity and shops could sell bad stuff.

The reason your joke isn’t funny is that it’s not even close to being an accurate analogy. Most photos and shops do not, in fact, have the qualities you describe. And the rest of the names that you claim sound like something rude do not, in fact, sound anything like it. But most gimps *do* have the qualities dennis123123 was complaining about. Therefore, Gimp is a bad name for an application.

Abiword, CinePaint, GarageBand, iChat, Inkscape, Internet Explorer, iPhoto, Linux, OneNote, OpenOffice, PulseAudio, Rhythmbox, Songbird, Windows Vista, and Word are examples of cool software names.

Baobab, Brasero, Cheese, Excel, Firefox, Mac OS X, Nautilus, Opera, PowerPoint, Thunderbird, Tomboy, and Ubuntu are examples of weird software names.

Aisleriot, Banshee, Gimp, Gnome, GNU, PolypAudio, and Yelp are examples of names that are just plain bad.

It’s possible for software to have a cool name but get a bad reputation (as with Windows Vista). And it’s possible for software to have a weird name but to overcome this with marketing (as with Excel, Firefox, Mac OS X, Opera, PowerPoint, Thunderbird, and Ubuntu). But if your name is just plain *bad*, it will be difficult for anyone to take you seriously until you change it. This is why, for example, PolypAudio was renamed to PulseAudio.

Thinking that a program’s name isn’t important is like thinking that its icon isn’t important, or that its stability isn’t important, or that its performance isn’t important. You might not like that it matters, but it does.
