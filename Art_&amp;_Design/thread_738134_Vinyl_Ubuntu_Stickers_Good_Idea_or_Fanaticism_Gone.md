### Post by VSack on 2008-03-30
> **asmiller-ke6seh said:**
> Umm, pardon me for saying this - and while I love the idea of the Ubuntu logo on my auto - but part of the spirit of Ubuntu is the multi-ethnic/multi-racial, pan-global concept that is evoked by the use of multiple colors. That is lost in the all-white logo. Apple gets away with it because we see the apple, and think red ... or green ... or whatever, but it the apple is one color and works without color because we all see apples in the color that we want. The ubuntu logo needs the contrast to really work.

Again, pardon me for my humble opinion, but that is my opnion.

I can appreciate your concerns, but firstly you must assume that anyone who doesn't "get" the logo is going to have it explained to the person who is clarifying it.  Its also assumptive that the person doing the explaining is even actually aware of the intricacies of the logo, considering much of the "purpose" behind the logo is lost on the public at large whenever it leaves the confines of those who take it to heart.

Another factor to consider is the cost/benefit analysis of production.  Vinyl cuts in and of themselves are inherently costlier to produce, and generally are monochromatic.  For every additional layer of color you wish to produce, you are doubling your cost of materials and exponentially increasing the time of production.

All in all, most people are going to ask "What is Ubuntu?" versus "Can you explain the logo further, or why it is designed as such?"  There is a much more enriching story behind the open source movement that represents Ubuntu and Linux than there is behind the ideology of the logo color choices themselves.  Furthermore, explaining what Ubuntu means (insofar as why it was chosen as a name when asked "Why Ubuntu") is going to be far more gratifying for the evangelist as well as the questioner in the long run.

Sorry for the long winded answer, but I had actually considered this before producing my first one :)
