I don't know if this is possible or not, but I'm looking to create a macro (something I have no knowledge in whatsoever) that can serve a very basic, but very useful function in simple art programs such as KolourPaint.

I saw this done when I was in highschool to MS Paint on Windows, apparently a student made it. Here's what and why it does:


In .jpg images there are hundreds of shades of every color that can potentially be used. many of these, unfortunately, look exactly the same to use. I'm sure anyone who has used an art program like paint has encountered this: there is a block of color (lets say white, for an easy example) that to us looks like one distinct color, the flood-fill (bucket) tool is selected and used in that field of color, and a tiny patch of that white field, changes color, while the rest of it remains white because it's a very, very, slightly different shade of white.

So, what this macro did was, when used, it would select one shade of white, use the flood fill, select another shade of white, use the flood fill again, and agian, and again, until every shade of white had been selected and filled. at the end of the macro filled the field of color with a bright orange so the user could tell when the cycle was complete. This all took about a second and a half. Each time the shade of white changed and was filled, it would envelope the shade of white that matched the current selection, expanding the 'patch' of color. If the macro was used three of four times in the same spot in the image, it usually cycled through enough times to make the entire field of white the same shade, which could then be filled to the desired color.


any way, I'm looking for a similar macro for the .png format so I could make the field of color transparent. This would make isolating specific objects within an image very easy (to see an example, first look at this image: [http://www.artgame.com/eagle16.jpg](http://www.artgame.com/eagle16.jpg), and then look at the portrait image at [http://www.myspace.com/kevinbays](http://www.myspace.com/kevinbays) - its just the head! but to get just the head and not the body or background of the image I had to go around the border of the head choose, one pixel at a time, which to keep as part of the head and which to omit as the background or body. Then I deleted the background and body and replaced them with the transparent "color" ...this takes a very long time)
