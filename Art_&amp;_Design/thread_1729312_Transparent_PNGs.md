### Post by Dragonbite on 2011-04-15
If it works in IE 7+ then don't worry about it. IE was the only browser to not support transparency in PNGs until recently. I don't know about 7 but 8+ are pretty good.

That being said, if you NEED to support users coming from IE 6 then the only other recourse that comes to mind is to use GIFs with a transparent background (which is what they used in the "good ol' days" ;) ).

Depending on the design, you could also take the PNG and replace the transparent layer with a layer the exact color of what is going to be behind it but this isn't ideal.

Good luck!
