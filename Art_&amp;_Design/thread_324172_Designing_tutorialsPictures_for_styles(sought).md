### Post by Albi on 2006-12-23
I usually just copy something and edit it.  For that one, I just copied the buttons from one of the beryl themes, played around with the darkness/hue/saturation until i got the right look, manipulated the sizes, and voila.

If you have a really original idea for a theme, the best way to start if you don't have many image editing skills is to just look for other themes that match an aspect of what you have in mind (eg button styles, panel texture) and work from there.  Because the pixmaps are so small, they're very easy to edit, just go down to 800-1600% zoom, and you can do it pixel by pixel even.

Hope that helped.

### Post by Albi on 2006-12-30
Well for the vista one it would be very easy.  You basically make a gradient (experiment until you position right).  Then to get that "glowing" effect, you go to filters, supernova, position it below the image, set spokes to 1,  set colour to the colour you want the button to appear, and voila you have a button that looks like the one in that theme.  Just keep in mind that they're very small images, it's much easier to make the cool effects you want.
