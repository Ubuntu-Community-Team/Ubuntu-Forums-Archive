### Post by NeuralEskimo on 2007-09-05
The screen saver in KDE works pretty well for my families picture-frame-check-the-weather-before-you-go-out-file-server-etc computer, but I suspect you have gnome. There is a way to create a desktop file for the gnome screen saver. Don't remember how to do it, but googling for "desktop file", "gnome", and "screen saver" might get you going in the right direction. As for other programs, like gwenview, I found the screen saver to be the most simple approach.
