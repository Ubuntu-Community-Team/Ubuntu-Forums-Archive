### Post by Paul820 on 2007-08-30
You need an image of some text, you could make it yourself or open a file with an image file with a hex editor and grab a screenshot of the text in the hex editor. This will be your random text, load it into gimp and use the magic wand to get rid of the white background. Also get rid of some of the text by using the selection tool and delete on your keyboard as you won't need that much text. Then go to colours->colourise and change the text colour to green. Make a black background under the text. Then you need to do a filters->blur->motion blur and set it to zoom, this is on the text layer, play around with it a bit.

The second one is very easy. There is a filter in gimp called flame. Just make a new layer with whatever colour you want for your background. Make a new layer over that and then go to filters->render->nature->flame, play with the different settings and especially the camera settings. Each one you do put it on a new layer, you can also do the colourise on these aswell to get more vibrant colours.
