### Post by slegrand on 2008-07-02
Vue 5 will install on wine. However i havn't checked how stable it is and if all the features worked it ran however. Vue 6 installer seems to have issues after it tries to install the C++ redistributable package. I havn't looked into fixing this yet but I will soon.
I heard Terragen ran on wine too. It's definitely not to Vue standard but is quiet cool nonetheless.
Otherwise you can use maya with paint effects and... skills. :) Granted it'll take more work but you can get similar results, although the spectral lighting is freaking wicked in vue. Mental ray final gather can give good results or just full blown GI. Volumetric clouds can be achieved with maya fluids to and the results look better than vue (imo) and render a lot faster.
I would love to see an opensource or a linux alternative to vue. Lots of major VFX studios would benefit from being able to have such a tool on their linux render farm.
