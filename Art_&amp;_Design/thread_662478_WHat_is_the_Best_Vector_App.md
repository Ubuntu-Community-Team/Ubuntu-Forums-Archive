### Post by Carlitus on 2008-01-12
All sistems around, the best vector app is Freehand. But it's discontinued, and never apeeared under Linux.

Why is better than Illustrator?

It's fast. It's intelligent. It's intuitive. It can do the same things as Illustrator, with less steps than Illustrator (for example, creating a new colour and using it).

Under Linux (today):

- Xara. It's the best. UIntuitive, without many palettes. You can create things very fast! Their problem: export options, under Linux.

- Inkscape. Can do many things. But miss many professional tools: styles, find/replace. Some palettes are very very ineffective and improductive. BTW, the best option to create SVG files. Slow, but a must have!

I NEVER recommend:

- Corel Drraw. Innacurate, exports very very badly, improductive (not fast and intuitive as Freehand). Many times, in professional pre-press, the app who creates most problems.

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-01-17
Click on the bottom of the app, in the left corner, where is says Fill: (color) Stroke: (color) (Alternatively, you can right click on what you want to change, and select Fill and Stroke from the menu that comes up), then select the Stroke Style tab. Change the stroke stuff there.

Also, if you made ie a rectange or circle, you can go to Path and select Object to Path, and then you would be able to edit it with the path tool (right under the button with a black cursor).
