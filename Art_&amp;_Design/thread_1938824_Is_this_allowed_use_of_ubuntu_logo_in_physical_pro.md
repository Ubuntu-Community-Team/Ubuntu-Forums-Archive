### Post by rg4w on 2012-03-10
I'm not attorney, and I'm certainly not in a position to speak for Canonical, but FWIW I had a similar question about the use of their logo on some of my web sites and they wrote back quickly with a definitive and helpful answer.

They key with trademark usage is that it can help a brand or detract from a brand, depending on how it's used.  Using the Ubuntu logo to promote distasteful things would obviously be bad for the brand and I'm guessing they'd prefer it not be used for those.  But in their reply to me they welcomed use of the logo in ways that promotes the work they're doing in a positive light.

How this applies to something entirely unrelated to software like earrings is anyone's guess, but I would encourage anyone uncertain about use to contact the Canonical legal team and if your experience is like mine you'll find them helpful and prompt.
