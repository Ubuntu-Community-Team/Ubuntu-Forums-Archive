### Post by Xfcn on 2008-06-23
GIMP isn't really hard or counter-intuitive. It's just not Photoshop, which is one of the biggest (and least relevant) complaints I hear about the software.

By cut the photo and blur the boundary, I'm going to assume you mean something along the lines of cutting out a section of photo and blurring the edges of what's left.

You can do that by Feathering the Selection. Which is located under Selection -> Feather. Give it a shot, and play around with it a little. There's no point in having either GIMP or Photoshop if you're not willing to experiment. That's how you find out about these nifty features. Trust me: Adobe does NOT document these things. Nor does the GIMP crew.
