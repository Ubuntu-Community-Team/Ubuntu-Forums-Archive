### Post by oedipuss on 2007-12-31
If you are interested in non-native commercial applications, you could try Artrage2. It is not expensive, has a free version, and it provides natural media simulation that is quite good. Apart from a few minor glitches it runs flawlesly in wine. (your mileage may vary of course)
 I imagine you could tweak a drawing with its pencil tool and it wouldn't loose its natural feeling.

If you need to vectorize your drawings, then Inkscape would be the tool for that. 
I'm afraid none of these tools provide a seamless way to import your drawings directly. I think gimp has an inbuilt scanning tool, but I've never actually tried it.
