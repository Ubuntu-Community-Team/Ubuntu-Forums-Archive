### Post by lyceum on 2008-03-21
I am working on a website to help new FOSS users find the software they need. My plan is to have a page for each product with something about the program, what OS's it will run on, what other programs it is comparable to, ratings and reviews from users and links to how-to's. I have the first one pretty much done, GnomeBaker. I still need the poll w/ reviews and the like programs. There were no how-to's for GnomeBaker, so I made my own and linked them. 

So why am I posting to the art section? I would like for this site to make a good first impression for new users. I was wondering if I could get some opinions about the look and feel of the site, not the whole site, just the info about the program and the how to's, as I plan to make how to's for any programs without one available. I am using the logo I made for the site, but the theme I down loaded. I plan to create my own theme, after I get some feed back, so feel free to be honest and don't be afraid to hurt my feelings. :)

Hello!

- Things that I think could use work are:

For the links under "Choose the options below to learn more about using GnomeBaker"Have them have bullets and make the links a little bit smaller. for style reasons.. the big links make me feel uncomfortable and i personally don't like being uncomfortable, so i wouldn't read the site on a daily basis for information on new and applications. The original info/text about the application should be bigger than the text to stand out more.  Use a certain color for the links to have their own stand outness. lol

also,

For the "works with" part -- maybe you should have a tux, windows, and mac icon when it works with that OS rather than typing out Linux: yes Windows:no Mac: yes..  So if it works with windows, just have a windows icon.. that eliminates having to have "windows: yes".  *The icons would not only save space, but more importantly look attractive to give a very quick and eye popping sight at what the application works on.*  (Less time reading more time learning)..
Umm..

I think it would be smart to have one page for each kind of application..  Like have a "music player" page -- on that page have all the music players that people could use, and separate them each with a horizontal ruler below each application and then some space, and then the title of the next application in bold colorful text.

tip: Colors are your friend and can make things pop out more.  Important things, or i should say, navigation things should stand out more.. like titles (titles are somewhat of a navigation to eyes, they tell eyes where the next post is at).

Also, black and while kind of makes people bored so they will not want to come to your site as much for information, at least no where near as much as a site with simple elegant colors that make them feel more warm and comfy..  Certain parts of the site should be in specific colors..  Keep the  colors for titles the same as each other, but use different colors for different parts of each post.[I].

[/I]I know iam leabing out a lot, but tat's a start.
