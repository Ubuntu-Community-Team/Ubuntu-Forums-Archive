### Post by Exodist on 2009-12-27
Ever tried to give your artwork in GIMP that metallic chip look just like your favorite sports cars paint. This really simple tutorial will show you in 5 simple steps how to do this.



1 - Create you artwork, here I am just using a very simple circle with a random blue/teal color.

[IMG]http://lh4.ggpht.com/_04pMYWw39X4/SzbpHjh_I9I/AAAAAAAAA48/kH7YTH79mLQ/s720/Glitter-HowTo-1.jpg[/IMG]



2 - Now create a new layer. Next use the Fuzzy Selection Tool to select the circles shape and then select the new layer. Fill the circle selection area in black as shown.

[IMG]http://lh6.ggpht.com/_04pMYWw39X4/SzbpHpq1GfI/AAAAAAAAA5A/6LZtkNdoSRM/s720/Glitter-HowTo-2.jpg[/IMG]



3 - Now that you have done that go to Filters --> Noise --> RGB Noise as shown here. 0.2 or 0.3 like I have chosen should work fine.

[IMG]http://lh6.ggpht.com/_04pMYWw39X4/SzbpH9QUTmI/AAAAAAAAA5E/chsuqnrsxe8/Glitter-HowTo-3.jpg[/IMG]



4 - Now go to Colors --> Desaturate and choose Luminosity as I have. This will make a flat grayish pallet for us to universally work with any color.

[IMG]http://lh3.ggpht.com/_04pMYWw39X4/SzbpH87lI8I/AAAAAAAAA5I/Gkwl7UJ4K14/Glitter-HowTo-4.jpg[/IMG]



5 - Final step is to your Layers, Channels, Paths and Undo Tool Bar. Under MODE, change the option from Normal to Addition. This should give you the final appearance as shown here.

[IMG]http://lh3.ggpht.com/_04pMYWw39X4/SzbpH4d5kpI/AAAAAAAAA5M/EP5nO5kjYws/s720/Glitter-HowTo-5.jpg[/IMG] 



Hope this helps,

Exo
