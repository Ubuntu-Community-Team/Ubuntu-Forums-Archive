### Post by graphius on 2007-11-25
I have been making the migration from Adobe's suite of design programs, specifically Illustrator and InDesign. I have found that Scribus covers about 60% of what I need to do, and Inkscape covers a different 60%. Scribus is very good with text, but it is very difficult to import this designed text to Inkscape.
Has anyone figured out a decent workflow? Is there a way to transfer files between the two programs? 
Scribus will save as svg, but strokes are not preserved. Saving as a raster format defeats the whole purpose. Inkscape will not import pdf. aaargh!
One of the very nice things about Adobe Suite is that all the programs are designed to work together. In the Linux world it seems that each program exists in a vacuum.
I really wish some of these great developers would communicate with some of the other related developers.... /end of rant
