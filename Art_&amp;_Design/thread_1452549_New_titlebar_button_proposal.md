### Post by TechWizrd on 2010-04-12
There has a been a lot of discussion over the titlebar buttons (maximize, minimize, close) and their position on the titlebar, as well as new ideas for utilizing the titlebar. This is mine.

Ubuntu's sound system, PulseAudio, gives users the ability to change the volume of specific applications, as well as the overall system volume. However, to change it, you have to right-click the Sound Applet, open Sound Preferences, click the Applications tab, and then change the volume using the right slider. My idea is to add a volume icon to the titlebar so users can control the application volume much more easily (as well as introduce man users to the feature). I use this feature on a regular basis, so this would be really useful.

Look at the top-left corner of the titlebar for the proposed volume button. The button's size and position are subject to change (eg. putting it on the right to fit in with the window button change in Lucid)
