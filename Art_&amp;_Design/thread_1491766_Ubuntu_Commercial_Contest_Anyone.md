### Post by gbowles on 2010-05-28
I think an effective commercial line could mean a great success for Ubuntu. Let's analyse the Mac vs. PC series, actually put out by the same firm as the Geico Caveman ads and the creepy Burger King costume. 
Apple's marketing is successful because it doesn't burden the customer with specs. Just look at say an HP commercial. "Hey this laptop has this and that and this other thing." People don't care except for techies, and do techies use commercials to evaluate gear? No, we jump online.
Apple sells the widget hammer not with pushing the hammer but by showcasing the deck you've just built with it. A treehouse with  laughing kids waving, while Dad stands there arms folded in accomplishment, etc... People want lifestyle products.
So how does this fit in with Ubuntu? That question my friends is the concept I would go for when producing a commercial for Ubuntu. We don't use our computers for different reasons than anyone else, so what lifestyle can we attach to Ubuntu? I'm not being negative, I'm trying to define this. Doing so is the visual brand.
I think this is a great time to push this kind of effort. People have a growing distrust for large cash-centered corporations, government, environmental security and the economy. We are concerned with being more eco-friendly. Ubuntu (Linux in general) is by the people and for the people. We recycle / re-purpose our machines. This can be a huge selling point. Link Ubuntu to responsibility. That's an avenue.
