### Post by banago on 2010-02-07
I'm a web designer and developer and I work only with open source and it rocks.

I buy my domains at **1and1.com**. Fair price and good domain management tool. 

I design with **Inkscape** - **GIMP** is a help on my work too.

I develop with **Bluefish**, but there are other good options out there too.

For FTP you have **FileZilla** and/or the default FTP support of **Nautilus**, files explorer of Ubuntu (gnome).

Good luck!

### Post by harmck on 2010-02-10
I'm a professional web developer, I freelance as well and I use GIMP over Photoshop. I have Photoshop CS2 installed with Wine, and it works fine, but if you know what you're doing with GIMP you can do almost anything within reason. 

There are far more resources available for Photoshop, such as textures, brushes and the like. But there are quite a few sites dedicated to offering GIMP Tutorials, and free brushes, available in GIMP and PS format. 

[http://www.blendfu.com/](http://www.blendfu.com/) - this is a fine example. 

Good luck with your site, if you ever need any advice let me know. I find Wordpress is great for getting a blog style site up and running quickly. There is minimal setup required, leaving you to focus most of your time on creating a class design.
