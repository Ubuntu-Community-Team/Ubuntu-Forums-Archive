### Post by kiddo on 2007-07-22
buy wacom, that's your best bet. I picked one up at a local retail store (100 canadian dollars, that's about 40-50 pounds?). Other third party tablets are usually not worth the (slightly) lower price tag, because you will waste HOURS, DAYS, YEARS (more than two years in my case) until you finally convince yourself that you should have bought a wacom and go buy one.

Besides, wacom has a very nice technology (it is patented, so I assume other tablets don't have that :| ) that allows the pen to need no batteries. Same goes for the wireless mouse that is included. It's actually the tablet that provides energy to those, it is impressive. My previous acecad tablet (never worked, never will it seems, even though I exchanged 9 emails with the Xorg driver's author) used a AAAA (yes, 4 A) battery which was expensive and subject to leaks. Now I know that my tablet will be able to last forever and I will never be worried about preserving battery power again, so I leave it plugged on at all times (there is a bug in ubuntu if you unplug it too, I think)

Works out of the box. Look ma! I just drew this in 35 seconds while on a gutsy liveCD!
