### Post by scrapmetal on 2010-02-11
What you are asking here is **[SIZE=3]huge[/SIZE]**, so lets break it down to see if I understand what you want. :p
As a commercial graphic artist, I don't know if you are asking about programs to use, or What is "good graphics". :confused:
Programs are the easy one so we will leave that until we know what you want to produce.** ("ART?")**

[CENTER]Good graphics are how each individual perceives them and as varied as there are people with opinions.:rolleyes:
Some like clean, crisp, precise delivery of an idea (simple to navigate website, no distractions).  ):P
While others are the complete opposite, and of course there is every combination of both opposing structures. ](*,)
It's what you like that probably counts the most. ;)
[/CENTER]

**1.** You will have to be more precise on what you mean by "art skills". Do you mean digital art?
**2**. What software are you creating the website with? Do you know to space lettering for headline art? (old trade name "headlining" as in newspapers etc)
**3.** Concepts of drawing?, pencil, pastel, charcoal, ink, etc. I have been learning ordinary pencil drawing all my life and am still learning.
Some indication of your skill level is necessary. I have less natural ability in cartooning as I am naturally a classical artist.
**4**. Find sites you like and analyse why you like them, layout, font selection, color selection, tricks and gimmicks (flashing attention grabbers,3D look)
**5.** As for good graphics rather than web design, good web design (how the complete page presents itself to you) is good graphical skills in the 'real world'.
GIMP (the program) would probably be your pick for button design, **but others will know better than I.**

You may have to learn the basics of balance, flow (where the observers eye travels), negative space (as in headlining), typography (type selection, and an art form in itself), and many other things.

PROGRAMS to start you. GIMP (think photoshop), Inkscape (vector)
PROGRAMS for special work. Scribus (graphic page layout - publication) Blender (modeling, UV unwrapping, texturing, rigging, water simulations, skinning, animating, rendering, particle and other simulations, non-linear editing, compositing, and creating interactive 3D applications, including games.)

These programs have tutorials and interest groups on line as well as books (& Ebooks) and downloadable PDF files. Google away.
Hope this helps, if not, ask more precise questions please. :oops:
