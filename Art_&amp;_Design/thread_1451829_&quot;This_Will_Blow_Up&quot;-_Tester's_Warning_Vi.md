### Post by Ellipsis on 2010-04-11
We have an issue with many first time users, non-testers and "production users" using pre-release versions of Ubuntu. This is a problem with human psychology: we want the best right now and we sometimes don't think of the consequences. 

[A thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1449139") has suggested limiting distribution of the pre-releases in some form would help... but everyone agrees this is not the most "open source" way to go about it. My own idea is that we make a video explaining the risks, cautioning first time users to "TURN BACK NOW" and how to describing/encouraging proper testing.

Notes on the Video:
-Make it fun/flashy/funny (so people want to watch it)
-Include only the basic stuff 
-Make it short (around 3-5 mins)
-Make sure people are aware of the risks of pre-releases.
-Make it not specific to a version of Ubuntu. (ie. reusable)  


Information that needs to be included:
-"Not for new users" Warning
-"This will blow up" Warning  
-Explain where to find help specifically 
-Explain how to report a bug (link/tell them to Google the bug manual) 
-(optional) Include a brief description of what an Alpha, Beta and RC are and who/what they are meant for.

If you are willing to produce it I might be willing to give out a small cash bounty/reward for it. (Small meaning a few beers small: not a new iPod small).

Interested? Post here or email me at ellipsis18 AT zoho DOT com with ideas/questions about what to include and the format. Don't go making the video just yet. 

I do not want to make this into a "contest" and rather people cooperate to build the best one possible.
