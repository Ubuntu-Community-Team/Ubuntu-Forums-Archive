When you minimize a program it goes down onto the bottom panel (which I'm calling the 'shelf', since it fits in with the idea that it's part of a 'desk').

I thought about this. Personally I hate having bordered windows, they're just so 90s and unnecessary imo. Holding ALT and dragging the middle mouse button makes it quicker and easier. If you must be able to resize a window without holding a modifier, you can always just do it at the title border, which is always there. 

In any case though, it's kind of a moot point; if this ever did get developed, I guess there'd be nothing stopping a developer from adding support for bordered and borderless windows just like Metacity does along with just about every other window manager on the planet.

> I have just found a "tabbed windows manager" for Windows: [http://windowtabs.com/](http://windowtabs.com/)
That's awesome and pretty much exactly the kind of functionality I had in mind when I thought of this. 

I wouldn't plan on this being a full-fledged DE like GNOME or KDE; that'd be too big a task and too much duplicated effort. I'd favour something more along the lines of OpenBox where it's just a window manager and a program or two that you can run either standalone or on top of an existing DE.
