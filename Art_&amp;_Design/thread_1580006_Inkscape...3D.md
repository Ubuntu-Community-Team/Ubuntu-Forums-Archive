### Post by Paul820 on 2010-09-23
Here is a video for inkscape: [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gL-VV-u4TSM]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gL-VV-u4TSM")

Blender: [http://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=3d+text+blender&aq=f]("http://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=3d+text+blender&aq=f")

That was just a quick search, there will be many more out there that you could use. You can also take some ideas from 3D text effects that others have used in Gimp and Photoshop and make them in Inkscape. Just a little imagination is all that is needed :)

The reason i said i would use blender is that you can do some fantastic effects really easily by making the text look like anything you want and then set the camera to any position you want. You can then save the image as a PNG with a transparent  background ready to paste into Gimp or Ghotoshop for modification.
