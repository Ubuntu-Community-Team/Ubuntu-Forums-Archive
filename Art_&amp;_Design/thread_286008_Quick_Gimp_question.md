### Post by dpaint4 on 2006-10-27
To round the corners on just a certain corner of a rectangle you should probably make the same rectangle on two layers, and use the rounded corners feature on one of the layers with the rectangle, but not the other. Then delete the corner of the unrounded rectangle and substitute it with the rounded corner you made on the other layer.

As for your glossy logo and script-fu. My best advice is to reconsider your automated approach.

But if you just want an answer, you'll need to make your own background layer and remove the layer that script-fu has made. There's nothing stopping you from putting whatever you want behind the image you've created.

I think you're limiting yourself too much by the basic settings and functions of the program instead of allowing yourself to produce work more organically.

Also, if you're making a 'logo' in Gimp, you might be barking up the wrong tree altogether. Most clients prefer logos in vector format, such as Inkscape can provide.

Hope this helps.
