niko

As for the tutorial, I can't find it, but it is SUPER simple.

**I do *_NOT_* take credit for this tutorial, someone else created it, I just can't fine it at this time**

Writing this as I do: Eagle.jpg (see attached ~ if I remember to add them :lolflag:

1. Open an image.

2. >Filters>Distorts>Polar Coordinates.
 2a: Circle Depth in Percent: 100
 2b: Offset Angle: 0.00
 2c: Map Backwards: Unchecked
 2d: Map From Top: Checked
 2e: To Polar: **UNCHECKED** <~~ very important
 2f: OK

3. >Image>Transform>Flip Vertically

4. >Filters>Distorts>Polar Coordinates.
 4a: Circle Depth in Percent: 100
 4b: Offset Angle: 0.00
 4c: Map Backwards: Unchecked
 4d: Map From Top: Checked
 4e: To Polar: **[COLOR="Red"]CHECKED[/COLOR]** <~~ very important
 4f: OK

5. Select the "Continuous Regions Tool"
 5a: Select the "white" (or whatever colour is outside the circle) area.
 5b: >Select>Invert
 5c: >Edit>Copy

6. Edit>Paste as new.

You now have two images.
1.  with a solid background and,
2.  with a transparent background.

I've been having fun with it.  :)
