### Post by st0n3cutt3r on 2008-04-11
it's obvious that you've drew the outline (black) first.

You could have very easily drawn that on a transparent layer, and then on a layer underneath that done all of your coloring.  the colors can go outside of the lines as much as the please so long as the don't actually cross a line to the other side of a line, and when it comes time to export the image, nobody will be the wiser.  Further, in more complex coloring such as painting highlights, shadows, and shading to scales on a dragon, fish, snake (I'm sure you can figure out other options) coloring like this under an outline can lead to some incredibly interesting images.  Toss the outline (didn't you learn that in high school art?) and sell it.
