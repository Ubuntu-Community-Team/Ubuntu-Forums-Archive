### Post by Aquila99 on 2011-01-09
Try avidemux  It has some powerful video filters.

Just select the video encoder yu want (other than the default "copy"
There are lots of filters that you can apply ("Hue" and "Satuation" for colour balance and "median" to de-noise are I think what you want but try others).  Go to menu->Video->Filters
I suggest you look at the input and output side-by-side to see what each filter does.
You can clean up the audio why you are at it.
Of course you will never get your VHS to look like  hi definition, but you should get some improvement.
Have Fun!
