### Post by oedipuss on 2008-01-15
Good ideas, but I'm a bit hesitant about the compiz 3d videos. Most of them are showcases which means that their creator tried to display as many effects as possible. Often a regular desktop will only use a subset of those. Responses like 'too bloated' or 'just for show, and not useful' are understandable, from users who are not familiar with the way compiz can be customized. 
Then there's also the whole beta-quality thing. Compiz will not work in every video card, even if it's relatively recent, some plugins will require shaders or cause glitches with older cards, and CCSM, the only current configuration tool, is too confusing for a first time linux user, and not installed by default. Not to mention that it needs to be shut down for 3d apps and games, and there's no switcher installed by default (such as fusion-icon). 
I guess my point is someone that sees a usual compiz video in ubuntu.org and then has severe problems with it as many will , would link that experience with ubuntu in general..
