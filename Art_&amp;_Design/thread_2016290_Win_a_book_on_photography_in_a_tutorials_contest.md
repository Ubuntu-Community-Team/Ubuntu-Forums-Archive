### Post by prokoudine on 2012-07-04
Folks,

We at Libre Graphics World are running our first contest ever, and for that one we decided to appeal to a wider audience. Hence the theme: photography tricks.

If you learned and improved some great photography technique you can share (night sky shooting, studio lighting setups etc.), write a tutorial on that, submit, and you can win one of two awesome books on photography:

[LIST]
[*]Sketching Light: An Illustrated Tour of the Possibilities of Flash, by Joe McNally
[*]Photographically Speaking: A Deeper Look at Creating Stronger Images, by David duChemin
[/LIST]
The last day for submissions is July 22.

The contest's jury are:

[LIST]
[*]**Mark Probst**, avid street and studio photographer and creator of the [GIMP Mathmap]("http://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/schani/mathmap/") plugin
[*][**María 'tatica' Leandro**]("http://tatica.org/"), freelance graphic designer and photographer, working with Fedora team.
[*]Someone called Alexandre Prokoudine :)
[/LIST]
The full announcement with a short list of rules is [here]("http://libregraphicsworld.org/blog/entry/photography-tutorials-contest-announced").

We shall publish winning entries under CC BY SA 3.0 Unported.

If you don't do photography, but have friends who are crazy about taking pictures, please share the news with them.
