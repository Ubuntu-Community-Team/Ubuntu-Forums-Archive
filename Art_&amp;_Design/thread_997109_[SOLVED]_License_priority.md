I am the creator of [ClearLUX]("http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/ClearLUX?content=94004"). This theme is based on SzerencseFia's LUX 0.9x and Human-Clearlooks 0.28.6 by Kenneth Wimar & Conn O'Griofa. ### Post by Grant A. on 2008-11-29
The GPL is a viral license. You have to license all derivatives under the same license. Not sure about the CC one.

---

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-11-29
Well, actually, the CC licenses are incompatible with GPL ones. That means that, technically, you can't create that theme, and have to take it down. :(

Reference: [http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/license-list.html](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/license-list.html)

### Post by dabugas on 2008-11-30
Thank you for your replies.

I resolved my problem by contacting the author of theme with the more restrictive license and getting his permission to release it under the other license.

This is an interesting problem in general, however, and I can only begin to imagine how debilitating license issues would be in the case of large projects, as was the case with Debian and Firefox, etc.
