### Post by airtonix on 2008-09-02
Having been a strong user of Macromedia Fireworks, inkscape instantly presented a familiar enviroment to me...indeed it's shaping up to be a opensource version of fireworks. 

Granted there are a few differences, mainly because fireworks (when i was using it back in 2000-2004) main purpose was for creating vector based graphics that would end up in 8bit bitmap form destined for a website.

As i mentioned earlier in this thread my avatar was made with inkscape, as you can see it's quite simple made up of around 12 shapes. 

My only gripe with inkscape compared to fireworks is that when you start to get a large amount of shapes that contain lots of vertices, editing & navigating around the canvas becomes sluggish, even on my coreduo with an nvidia8800gt.

> I also wish it had an eraser. If it does, I have no clue how to use it. It's a vector based editor, an eraser is for bitmap editors like gimp. if you want to peform erasing type actions on a shape: 
1 - draw your desired 'eraser' shape and raise it above your target shape to modify.
2 - select both your target shape and your eraser
3 - choose from the Menus : Path -> Difference.

Your eraser will be destroyed in the act of punching through the target shape. Think of it as a custom shaped bullet.

The brush thing that **Dannyramsnes **maybe talking about is the modify path which behaves similar to a bitmap/photo editors 'smudge/smear' tool.

Recently decided to get back into graphics and website design, so I expect to churn out some more artwork/designs

### Post by smartboyathome on 2008-10-16
> **Thricemin said:**
> i like these alot they are cool, but what is the difference between inkscape and gimp?

Gimp is a raster image editor (ie, you use colored pixels to create stuff). Inkscape is a vector graphics editor (ie, it generates equations and such which are used to generate shapes and such to make a picture). The former is limited to the size it was created, as if you try to make a raster image bigger, it gets pixelly, whereas the latter can be resized to any size without this loss of quality. The former also has more effects available to it, whereas the latter is more limited to what you can do with it.
