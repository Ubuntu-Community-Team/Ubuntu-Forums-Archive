### Post by youbuntu on 2010-10-27
Hi guys. I just wanted to provide some feedback here. I don't understand *who* is responsible for allowing design decisions to be made @ Canonical, but I feel they really should revise things a little, or hire someone who knows what to do.

I am talking about the current trend in Ubuntu of using **ghastly** bright orange circles all over the place. See this [Ubuntu ad]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vYTJPaM82nQ")

This SCREAMS everything that is bad about graphic design, marketing and musical ability. Why this whole obsession with cramming in as many rounded corners & orange circles as possible? Ugh! I want to scream "nooooooo! don't do it!".

It is a well known fact that software engineers should *never* be allowed to design interfaces or anything graphical or artistic; I don't know if this IS the case, but it appears to be. Ubuntu is wonderful, *software* wise, but it seems the graphical & interface team need a good hard think. I am not saying Apple rule the world - I LOVE GNU/Linux, but they don't often go far wrong with creative and beautiful, simple aesthetics. The whole balance just seems "wrong". You know how, when you look at something, your subconscious *instantly* reacts, one way or the other. Calm, simple and subtle is the way to do it, and this ain't that way.

This is a mess - any comments?
