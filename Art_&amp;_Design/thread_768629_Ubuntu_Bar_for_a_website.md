### Post by st0n3cutt3r on 2008-04-26
I think there is too much shadow at the bottom of the image.

Improvements I recommend include blending the middle colors behind the ubuntu logo so that the horizon line only stretches about half-way across the bar from the right, and vertically centering the ubuntu logo/name.  You might also consider centering the tree after you've centered the logo, but I would do the logo first and see how it looks.  

I understand/expect that you probably divided the image horizontally with the gradient first to make a shiny-looking button, and added the other elements later, but by adding the tree you've created a ground plane, and then placed the logo on the plane. The result is an unbalanced image with a large, and detail-free bottom half, and a heavy top.

There are a few other things you could do to change it, including using the heron image instead of the tree, but if you're sick of the official heron art, you could always do other things, or change nothing at all.  something that moved away from the horizon in both directions would be nice though -- if not entirely necessary.

Sorry it probably seems like I'm really ripping it apart. I'm not trying to; I actually like it quite a bit. I wouldn't have bothered to comment and critique if I didn't.
