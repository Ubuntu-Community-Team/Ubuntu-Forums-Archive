Yes, but those 
1) are commercially made
2) cannot be altered to say "tested with" or "designed for XP, but runs better with Ubuntu"
3) says Linux instead of GNU/Linux
The last point is important, as the whole name of Ubuntu is - Ubuntu, not Ubuntu Linux, and if you want to add that it's a Linux (makes sense for the "Tested With" and others) to make it more broad, the proper and more descriptive way is GNU/Linux.
