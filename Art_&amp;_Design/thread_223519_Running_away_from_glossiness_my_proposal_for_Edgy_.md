### Post by Aphorism on 2006-07-27
While I appreciate the enthusiasm, I feel that I must address some of the issues in this thread.  I apologize for not being able to read the forums more often, but this is purely a byproduct of my career.

First, if you look to focus group studies, you will see that people *are* attracted to shiny things.  While this is not a huge concern to some, it *does* imply that some thought went into the glossy / sheeny approach.  Also, please consider the default folder icon and the *rest* of the larger "picture".  They too are glossy and we have *zero* current control over their production as they are **funded** for development by Mr. Shuttleworth.  I am certain that there is potential for change in this, assuming the newly revised art team can prove itself.  Yes, one could argue that the other two commercial OSes apply gloss / translucency.  Perhaps they do it for a reason however?  And regarding the complaints of professionalism, I would encourage all to research exactly the funding spent on Ubuntu artwork.  You might be surprised to learn that indeed much of the artwork was designed by paid professionals unassociated with the Ubuntu community.

Second, while submissions are good, this is unfortunately a poor forum.  The members of the art team have taken great strides to centralize the information structure for Ubuntu art.  The fundamental trio of information channels is listed at [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork)

Third, again regarding submissions, please note that we have formal specifications.  [http://www.launchpad.net/people/ubuntu-art/+specs](http://www.launchpad.net/people/ubuntu-art/+specs) for a full listing.  Read the related wiki pages and you will also see why 'gloss' is a necessary element.  Also note that we must attempt to fit within the current design scope of Ubuntu for default artwork.  In this light, the submissions, while valid on their own, fail to embrace the other elements of the default look and feel.  I would encourage *everyone* to get involved with the art team now however, as Edgy+1 is shaping up to be a wonderful opportunity for real progressive adjustments.  Also remember that it is always easier to tear apart than build upon.

Fourth, all of the art talk is wonderful to see -- *finally*.  People are interested and energetic.  This is terrific!  We have, however, yet to prove ourselves to Canonical.  Edgy is a 'test flight' for the ubuntu-art team.  While some people would like to see more accomplished, the level of contribution simply isn't sufficient to get major changes accomplished.  To this end, we targeted a very clear and relatively attainable number of targets for Edgy -- especially considering the extremely limited time-line.  [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Specs/EdgyArtworkPlan](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Specs/EdgyArtworkPlan) if you would like to see the exact development time-line.  This is directly related to the Edgy cycle decided at Paris by the core developer team.  It is my personal opinion that it would be more productive to achieve a reasonable level of professionalism across a more limited spectrum of targets than a hodge-podge of unprofessional and disconnected plethora.  Please consider this approximately two months in towards true growth and development.  Seen in this light, the team has thus far shown significant potential.

Fifth, and finally, regarding the ongoing discourse on palette issues...  Brown is distinct as far as operating systems go.  Current implementations haven't been quite as successful as they possibly could have, however.  That said, there have been elements of interesting potential (for example, consider the mystical colour spread on the default Dapper wallpaper).  In my opinion, keeping a distinct colour palette is to our advantage.  Blue has been done, and well overdone.  In fact, one could argue, that both Apple and Microsoft are guilty of over blue (more-so the latter) when one considers their default appearances and marketing.  This is irrelevant however, as Ubuntu was founded on brownish / earth tones, and it will probably remain that way.  As stated before by a number of people -- Ubuntu simply isn't a democracy.  It may also be worth noting that perhaps this facet has contributed to it becoming a tidal wave of success.

As for the future, there are many wonderful ideas.  Please get involved if you have the time.  While I can not pretend to speak for all of the participants, I hope this post helps clear up a few things and perhaps sheds a little light on the current goals of the artwork team.

Sincerely,
Aphorism (Troy of ubuntu-art)

PS:  Feel free to contact me directly if you have any questions, comments, etc.  I try my best to keep up on the email, but I will admit that of my mail program has been seeing more CPU cycles being sucked up by incoming letters.
