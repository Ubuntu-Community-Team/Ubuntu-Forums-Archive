### Post by Daniel G. Taylor on 2004-12-03
The features of Xorg you are talking about probably involve the XDamage and XComposite extensions, and actually have nothing to do with theming as we are thinking of it. What you are probably talking about is creating a compositing manager to create special effects as seen in OSX or Sun's Looking Glass. The compositing manager basically gets drawn windows and composites them into the full image you see on your desktop (and since you get each window you can add shadows, distort windows on minimize, etc).

We are thinking of GNOME icon themes, GTK themes, and window manager themes, and you did mention Nuvola, which confuses me a bit. Please explain...

P.S. You might want to take a look at [gdesklets](http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org/) if you want some nice graphical things to show off.
