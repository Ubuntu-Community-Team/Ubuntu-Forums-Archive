### Post by Six Ways on 2007-11-18
I personally think Blender is functionally better, actually. Maya has a lot of things where you go to do something obvious and then spend a day working out that no, you actually can't do it. 

Besides which it's definitely worth learning Blender because it will always be free - if some major new technique or something comes along, you've got to pay to get the latest version of Maya but you just download the next version of Blender.

Blender IS harder to learn, yes, but not nearly by as much as a lot of people would have you believe and it's worth the effort. In many ways I would say that it's more powerful than Maya. It's certainly more flexible. 

In regards to your signature, Merk, selection with right click is one of the smartest basic features of Blender - in Maya, if you've got something selected and the thing you want to select is behind the transform widget, you have to deselect and select again. In Blender, right click has no interaction with the widget so you just select the other thing. It keeps a lot of things separate which in Maya are sort of fudged together. Ctrl-W for saving isn't really defensible though!

Oh, and just say no to 3ds Max (IMHO), I hate it :p

### Post by BIGtrouble77 on 2007-11-20
I'll post a few of my thoughts... I used to work in the video games industry and my brother currently works for a major studio doing television graphics.

The others here that mentioned Blender won't get you anywhere in the industry are correct to a point... It depends on what industry you want to get into.  If it's games you might be able to get away with using blender, but most of the SDK's are designed for 3dsmax and maya.  If you are going to write your own 3d engine then blender is perfect.  

If you plan on getting into just doing 3d shorts then Blender may actually be better.  You won't get caught up in using tons of plugins and cool effects you snag from BT.  It'll focus you on developing your core skills.  

Most studios will only look at your portfolio and/or past experience.  They really don't care what software you use, but they will expect you to learn the software they are using VERY quickly.  It's good to have a working knowledge of Maya, Max and Cinema 4d (if possible).  

I find the workflow in Maya to be much better than Max.  Maya encourages you to be precise and organized with your project while Max encourages a very messy workflow.  I haven't used max since V7, but my brother tells me it's not much better in that respect.  I also despise the Max material editor.  Maya and Blender's are much better.  What max is great for is getting work done quickly.  

I worked with max for many years (back when it was just 3d studio) so I have seen it mature.  It's very featureful now, but I still prefer Maya.  I plan on using Blender from now on (for leisure) mainly because it's opensource and multiplatform, so it's convenient.
