### Post by Iandefor on 2006-06-04
Screenshot #2 looks like it's running Engage. It's a bitch to set up, but if you want to look into it, [here's]("http://www1.get-e.org/EFL_User_Guide/English/_pages/3.4.html") a starting point. You can also get [kxdocker]("http://www.xiaprojects.com/www/prodotti/kxdocker/main.php"),[ ksmoothdock]("http://ksmoothdock.sourceforge.net/"), or [gdesklets]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/gdesklets"). I recommend just using a stock gnome-panel set to transparent, however. It's less resource-intensive than any of the above solutions, and integrates into GNOME better.

### Post by Nonno Bassotto on 2006-06-04
If you want to have a centered panel, then you can use a dirty trick. Just choose as a image background for the panel one which is transparent at the sides and shows a fake panel in the center. Not a very elegant solution, but it works, see here (fifth and seventh posts)
[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183577](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183577)
This won't allow you to have icons that become bigger when the mouse is over them. To achieve this you'd better remove the panel, install gdesklets and choose the desklet named "Starterbar"
[http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org/categories.php?func=gd_show_app&gd_app_id=210](http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org/categories.php?func=gd_show_app&gd_app_id=210)
