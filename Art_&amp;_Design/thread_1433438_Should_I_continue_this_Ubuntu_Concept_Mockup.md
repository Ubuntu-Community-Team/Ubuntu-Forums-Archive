### Post by mojo2012 on 2010-03-26
I also have my own ideas how the perfect ubuntu desktop should look like.

The main goal is to minimize visual clutter by reducing the amount of used colors (except for icons). So, eg. in nautilus all the various borders etc are set to 1px. Furthermore all widgets are tremendously slimmed down.

As you can see from the attached screenshot, the menubar is at the top and there is not "taskbar". Instead the most important apps are "pinned" to the bottom panel as it is done in Mac OS X or Win7. Note: this are not just launchers, but is a complete replacement for the taskbar. If an app has serveral windows, a click on the app icon invokes compiz scale on the application.

The most used folders are on the right bottom side. Currently, these are just launchers/links to the folders. To have some kind of stacks-like features, as the Mac OS X Dock provides, would be better though (menu with contents ofthe folder, drag and drop support, ...).

Btw, this is not a mockup! It's real ;-) ([http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Simple+eGTK?content=119812](http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Simple+eGTK?content=119812))
