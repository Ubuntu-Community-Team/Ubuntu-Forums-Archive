### Post by off1 on 2009-05-17
I love AcdSee 9, some of the features I like about it (and I use them a lot!) are:
[list]
[*]Double click for real full screen (black background, no tool bars).
[*]Advanced rename, and sort options.
[*]Fast browsing of folders.
[*]Advanced resize, rotate and convert features.
[*]Advanced and automatic color, contrast, etc correction features.
[*]Advanced printing options.
[*]Supports customizing actions, like renaming, when copying files to a folder with duplicate names (I love this one because my camera always starts naming pictures from IMG0001.jpg).
[*]Duplicate image finder.
[*]Simple (yet very useful) image editing, without having to open Photoshop or Gimp.
[*]Very customizable.
[/list]
In a few words, AcDsee has a lot of very advanced and useful features, yet they managed to make it easy to use. Everything in the same program.

All the software packages I've tried in Ubuntu are just too basic (gThumb, fSpot, even infraviw through wine). They may be enough for mommies who want to sort a few pictures of the last vacation, but they are definitely not suited for power users.

So far the best software I've used in ubuntu is digiKam, but it's still years behind AcDsee. It lacks a lot of the features and ease of use I'm looking for. Still I would be willing to use it if it wasn't for:
[list]
[*]Its inability to browse folders (that's so dumb!). I'm not going to move 10 Gigs of well organized photo folders just because digiKam wants to create its own albums.
[*]I cannot stand the big blue icons of the KDE theme everywhere. KDE is like a Fisher Price or Gymboree-like environment (sorry for those KDE users, it's just my opinion).
[/list]

If any one has had any success in finding (or developing) a real contender for AcdSee (v9, v10, or Pro) please let me know!!!

OFF1
