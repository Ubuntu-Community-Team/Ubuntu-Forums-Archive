### Post by Paul820 on 2007-07-16
You can go to filters->animation->playback to preview it. To save it, save it as gif, don't flatten the image. When the popup box comes up with the title 'export file', click on 'save as inimation'. It can be viewed in a web-browser or gThumb image viewer can play them. I don't know how to convert it to something else to view on a cd. I'll have a look around.
