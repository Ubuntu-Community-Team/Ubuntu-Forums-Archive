### Post by BslBryan on 2009-04-30
> **Neon Lights said:**
> Sexy!

:lolflag: Well, I do declare, I'm gettin' the vapors! 

Anyway, thanks, guys.  

I had a few senior pictures taken earlier this year, considering I was a senior in high school.  Today was my last day of high school, so I decided to celebrate. :) 

Anyway, it was pretty simple.  I took the photo I had and made the background transparent, so all that was left was me.  The next thing I did was open up a new project and load a random picture of a building I happened to have.  I desaturated the colour, and filtered it so it would look like it was a sketched photo, and flipped it about the y-axis.  Then I opened up a new layer and loaded up my photo.  I scaled it to the correct scale size and placed it where I wanted.  Then I applied the same filter to me that I did to the background photo.  

After some motion blur was added to me, I rendered some orange fog, and burned the layers, making it half sepia, half black and white, and much darker than the original.  It also added a very urban effect to the photo, I think.    

Saved it, and loaded it here. :)
