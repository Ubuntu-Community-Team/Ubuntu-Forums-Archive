### Post by 4KvRMU7Nnv on 2007-06-04
Ok, first of all, I love the default theme.  I spent a really long time trying to find a theme that I liked when I realized that the default orangey-brown theme was perfect once I got used to it.  SO, keeping that in mind, I looked around for things that would make it even better.  First, since I didn't like the pixely rounded corners on the windows, I got the "Human-Squared" metacity theme from Gnome-look.  Once I had a squared window border, I realized that I wanted the insides to be squared as well.  To accomplish this, I downloaded the "Murrina-Human-Classic" Gtk2 theme from Gnome-look as well as the "New murrine configurator."  After installing, I made everything about the theme square with the configurator and removed those weird vertical bars in the menus and Ta-daaa! I have an improved, sleek, very useable ubuntu theme!  check the attachment for a screenshot.
