### Post by Roger Mudd on 2008-07-01
Keep in mind that form should meet function. In other words, what do the U.S. civil rights movement and "Web 2.0" have in common. I'd argue nothing and push you towards a more conservative design. Perhaps something along the lines of the [New York Times]("http://www.nytimes.com"). Your graphics look good, I'm just not sure they fit this purpose.
As far as legibility goes, I like the narrow, fixed body column. Studies show narrow columns are more legible than wide columns. I do believe your link tabs under the header need to be larger. Also, try mixing fonts. Perhaps a larger, bold sans serif (Arial, Helvetica, etc.) for headers and a smaller plain serif (Times, Georgia, etc.) for body copy.
