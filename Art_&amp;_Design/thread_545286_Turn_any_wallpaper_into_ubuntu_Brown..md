### Post by xl_cheese on 2007-09-07
With the recent gusty wallpaper submissions I got the itch to create some of my own.
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Incoming/GutsyIdeas](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Artwork/Incoming/GutsyIdeas)

At any rate I figured I could post a few gimp steps to turn any wallpaper into the brownish colors being presented.

I started with this:
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Glass+Threads?content=64408](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Glass+Threads?content=64408)

And converted it to this:
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/brownGlass?content=65822](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/brownGlass?content=65822)
and this:
[http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/rootbeer+?content=65460](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/rootbeer+?content=65460)

There were basically two tools I used to make them brown.

1.  Filter -> Colors -> Colorify  
  -This one is pretty simple.  Choose custom color and pick a brown color.  

2.  Tools -> Color Tools -> Hue Saturation
  -Pretty easy here too.  Adjust Saturation all the way up.  Then move the Hue around until you get the color you want.
