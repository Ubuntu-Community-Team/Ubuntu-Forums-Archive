### Post by AutoCAD_Guru on 2010-12-19
I use AutoCAD's online DWG viewer and printer [https://www.autocadws.com/](https://www.autocadws.com/) to view drawings in Ubuntu.
It does not matter if I am in Unubtu or windows or what browser I am using such as Mozilla or Internet expolrer, I can upload drawings to it and do a number of handy things such as measure, get areas, layer control, print, insert blocks, redline mark up drawings and even share the drawings with other users.

[www.autocadws.com](www.autocadws.com) is a simple solution to viewing AutoCAD drawings on Ubuntu or any other operating system.

Hope that helps.

### Post by AutoCAD_Guru on 2010-12-21
> **Ceiber Boy said:**
> this looks like a neat solution.

Glad to let you know about it. It is a great free solution that was only released this year and not publicized much at all. 
I like the cloud computing side of it and that I can access my AutoCAD drawings from anywhere. They even have an iphone app available too.

Like many others, I have tried numerous CAD packages in Ubuntu and found the best AutoCAD drafting equivalent is BricsCAD available from [https://www.bricsys.com/en_INTL/bricscad/index.jsp](https://www.bricsys.com/en_INTL/bricscad/index.jsp) 

Unlike much of the software for Ubuntu, it is not free, although I consider it extremely good value compared to AutoCAD for those that need to do a lot of drafting and do not want to be tied to a Windows operating system. A free 30 day trial of it can be downloaded. It can even run AutoLISP routines (macros) although I did not test this aspect of it. If I had not already bought AutoCAD I would definately buy BricsCAD.
