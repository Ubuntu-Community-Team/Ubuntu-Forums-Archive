### Post by SpiderGorilla on 2008-02-20
[http://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2008/02/adobe-pushes-drm-flash](http://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2008/02/adobe-pushes-drm-flash)

According to the above article, Adobe is planning to add DRM to their Flash suite of products, which will effectively destroy Gnash and will force ridiculous licensing agreements that will effectively either kill Flash as a format (making way for Microsoft's Silverlight), or cause a problem with independent production means. Mostly they mean this to be used on their direct-video usage, but the DRM will still exist for regular vector Flash software as well.

So my question is this: what can the Open Source community do to meet this head-on? Should we attempt to continue trying to work with Flash and their standards, or should we push for a whole new standard altogether? I know that Mozilla is doing an SVG animation suite... I think maybe it's time to step it up, which would handle SOME of the animation issues. But we're still looking at supporting sound, programming, video inclusion.

I feel we need a new alternative, and that it's high time we went ahead and went forward with an open standard alternative to Flash. Any other thoughts or opinions?

### Post by eye208 on 2008-02-21
> **SpiderGorilla said:**
> Adobe is planning to add DRM to their Flash suite of products, which will effectively destroy Gnash
This will not affect Gnash at all.

Commercial video/audio on demand requires DRM. The media corporations are asking for it. In the past, Flash was never used in that area because it lacked DRM capability. Adobe wants to position Flash as a competitor to iTunes/Quicktime and Windows Media Services, i.e. things that worked on Windows and Mac only, where you have to purchase a licence key to play back the content. These things never worked with Linux in the first place, so nothing will change for us, and Gnash will not be affected.

YouTube and similar sites will never ask you to install a licence key for viewing their content, so these things will continue to work with Gnash just as they did before.
