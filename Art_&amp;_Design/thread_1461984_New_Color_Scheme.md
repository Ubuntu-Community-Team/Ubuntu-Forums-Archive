### Post by Alan James on 2010-04-25
I think the forum is community driven but overseen and organized by Canonical, which in my mind is great. I always imagined it was controlled by them because the point of open source software is to involve the community while also giving back.  
 

 A company that creates a distro with no forum for the community to converse in is not likely to get very far or become very popular. Creating a single forum also creates an centralized place for all communication to be held. If multiple Ubuntu forums were on the internet the communication would be fragmented and the community wouldn't be as strong.
 

 Its all part of a brand image that Ubuntu is easy to use and has a great community behind it if you ever have a problem. This helps sell the idea that Ubuntu is the distro you want to use for your server thus causing you to buy support from Canonical. If both Ubuntu.com and UbuntuForums.org had matching colors to the current LTS version it would show great uniformity, organization and commitment to a centralized idea.
 

 After all, look at how successful Apple is just by being committed to a unified design.
