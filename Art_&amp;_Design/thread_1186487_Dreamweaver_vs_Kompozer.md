### Post by quixote on 2009-06-14
Well, Dreamweaver can take a lot more money off you ... :p

It's been a while since I've used it, but I think the main difference is ease of use.  It's got little buttons to include Flash, or graphical ways of making maps of a web page.  For me, it didn't do that stuff the way I wanted it to, and I'd wind up editing a text file after all.  

If you're at all comfortable editing straight html, I'd say Kompozer is better.  Cheaper, less computer overhead, and it doesn't get in your way as much.  If bells and whistles are important, though, Dreamweaver is probably the winner.
