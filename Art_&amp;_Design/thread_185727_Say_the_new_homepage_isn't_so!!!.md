### Post by dolphinholmer on 2006-06-02
I'm glad to see change among the four pictures that originally prompted my post. (I suspect that 23meg didn't see the worst of it, linking as she does to the two pictures of the original four that remain on the homepage.) My main objection was that I saw in the new homepage yet another example of the appearance of what George Ritzer described as the 'globalisation of nothing' and it rankled with me so because this is exactly what Ubuntu ISN'T about.

If you're interested listen to him on BBC radio:
[http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/factual/thinkingallowed_20030416.shtml]("http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/factual/thinkingallowed_20030416.shtml")

Or take your pick from here:
[http://www.google.co.uk/search?hl=en&safe=off&rls=GGGL,GGGL:2005-09,GGGL:en&sa=X&oi=spell&resnum=0&ct=result&cd=1&q=the+globalization+of+nothing&spell=1]("http://www.google.co.uk/search?hl=en&safe=off&rls=GGGL,GGGL:2005-09,GGGL:en&sa=X&oi=spell&resnum=0&ct=result&cd=1&q=the+globalization+of+nothing&spell=1")

While I appreciate that many may simply shrug and think 'what's the big deal?' I think that the way we communicate in words, symbols and images reflects collectively who we are and what we are driving towards.
The picture of the 'young affluents' to represent the ubuntu community is quite literally unreal. Perhaps they have been caught off-guard while they discuss how to iron out some bug they have found? But we know they haven't, right? This is then quite literally a lie. The only reason we are generally not bothered by such things is that we are surrounded by such lies constantly and have become numb and unconcerned by the inauthentic. We become deadened to life and unaware that we have become so.
