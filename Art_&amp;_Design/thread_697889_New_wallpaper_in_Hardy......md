### Post by alvevind on 2008-02-18
What **is** on the minds of the Ubuntu art team?!

The previous wallpaper depicted the dry skin from an elephant. Some liked it and some found it repulsive. It was also criticised for being a bit too dark.

Then comes the new default wallpaper. The darkness is gone, being replaced by midtones.  So far so good. But then on to the image itself:

It clearly shows a bird (heron) on the right hand side. No problem there. But what's happened to it's neck?! Red splotches. A heron does not have red neck feathers. This is blood. It looks like the poor bird was attacked by some predator and its throat has been badly damaged, and it is bleeding severely. The chaotic feathers enhance the image of a bird that has been fiercely attacked. But the heron still seems to radiate a proud steadfastness, perhaps having sacrificed it's life for a higher purpose, defending it's bethren or something. Having interpreted the foreground (and having that in the back of our mind), we move on to the background, upper left. Is that a human figure? Striding towards the corner holding something aloft? Something red. From the bird image we interpret that the red is blood. A bloody shroud? The bloody overcoat of a fallen war comrade? How does the person relate to the wounded bird? Did they fight in the same war? Does it symbolize something like Pablo Picasso's Guernica?
</emo>

Art should invoke strong emotions. 
Art should be ambiguous. 
I find this wallpaper very good as **art**. Very good.

But should the **default** wallpaper in Ubuntu invoke emotions?
Is that is's purpose?
**Strong** emotions?

I find this wallpaper **bad** as a default wallpaper. **Very** bad.

First impressions should always be "it's ok" or better. It should not be such that half of people say "yeah that rocks dude!" and the other half says "that's gross!". The latter one being the reaction of several **girls** experiencing Ubuntu for the first time with the elephant skin wallpaper. 

I want the very best for Ubuntu, so I am playing a "Devils advocate" here, exaggerating, to hint to the fact that there are multiple perspectives. You may find this a very "girly" perspective, and dislike that. "Phah! If you can't stand watching a cow being slaughtered you don't deserve to be using a computer!" You may be happy leaving Linux in the "boys domain". I am not. I want everyone to use Ubuntu, especially those that do not use Linux already. I want the family shopping for a new school computer for their daughter in 2009 and go looking at Windows, Mac and Ubuntu screens side-by-side, to not overlook Ubuntu because of the "gross" wallpaper! I do **not** care if a 20 year old geek does not like the default wallpaper. Not a bit! He knows how to change it and he would change it no matter what the default is. 

Please give us a "newbie safe and family friendly" wallpaper! Look at Windows, look at Mac, these people are not fools. They spend millions on design and testing. These people want users and money and it is not a coincidence they are not showing animal carcases on the desktop.

I really wish that the Ubuntu art team would take a good look at the [wallpapers at vladstudio.com]("http://www.vladstudio.com/wallpapers/?start=0&show=1000&order=viewed&orderdir=desc").
Kubuntu and KDE has recently selected their default wallpapers from this artist, and I think there are several good wallpapers there that with some modification would be very good Ubuntu defaults.
