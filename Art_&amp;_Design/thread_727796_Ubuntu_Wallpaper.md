### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2008-03-20
> Thanks for the feedback renzokuken, I tried to clean the edges up and couldn't find a tool to do the job, but after a play around went with the cloth filter. I'm using gimp by the way!!

One solution to the pixelated edges is to use a vector program to smooth out the silhouette.

I've attached a version of your people that I cleaned up in Inkscape by:

1. Opening your original image in GIMP

2. Selecting the black (color selection tool)

3. Inverting the selection and deleting everything except the black

4. Saving the image as a .png and then opening it in Inkscape.

5. Select the imported image and then do Path>Trace Bitmap and selecting Brightness Cutoff>OK

6. Discard the original imported image

6.1 You can optionally play around with the vector nodes to remove any unwanted bumps (eg. I fixed a bumpy bit on the trouser hem of the second figure from the right)

7. Export the new image as a .png

You could then combine it back into the original image - especially easy if you used layers for the different image components.

Sound complicated at first but this honestly took me two minutes.

Nice idea, by the way!
