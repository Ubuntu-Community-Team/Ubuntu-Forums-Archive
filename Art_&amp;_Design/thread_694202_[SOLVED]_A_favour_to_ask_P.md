### Post by SpiderGorilla on 2008-02-11
I don't know how he did it, but I would've gone into the image's properties and cranked the brightness to wash out the background while also cranking the contrast to up the content of the black.

Could've also done a color-grab on the black, with a decent thresh-hold to compensate for the gray in the image and then done a flood fill on a separate layer.

*EDIT* Incidentally, with GIMP I found I could achieve similar results by cranking the contrast to about 33, then making a new layer above it, flood-filling it with black and setting its mode to "overlay". Seems to pretty handily replicate the output from Remmy.
