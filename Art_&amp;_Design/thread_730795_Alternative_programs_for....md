### Post by badmedic on 2008-03-22
I agree that Bluefish is great. I also like Kompozer, and it might be an easier transition if you worked in Dreamweaver before.

The combination of Gimp and Inkscape is more than enough to do professional 2D work. Just don't make the mistake of expecting them to be just like Photoshop and Illustrator. A lot of people do and seem to end up disappointed.

I think if you keep an open mind and approach these applications as something new and different they aren't that hard to learn and become comfortable with. For me they have some slight limitations, but are both still perfectly usable.

I haven't done any 3D in years and have yet to try Blender so I can't really comment on that area.
