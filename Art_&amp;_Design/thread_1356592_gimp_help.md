### Post by Exodist on 2009-12-17
> **lukjad007 said:**
> For actually creating art, GIMP is not the best option, even though it can be used for this. Try learning about Inkscape or some other art program. GIMP is meant to be used as a manipulator of images, not a creator.


As far as any raster graphics. Gimp is very good. You can easily creat nice looking web graphics and other nice graphics. Like my avatar for example was easily created in GIMP. 

As far as desktop wallpapers and such, many folks jump back and forth between Inkscape and GIMP.

In my honest opinion, Learn GIMP first. The OP stated he hasnt used any drawing program before. I just hope for the OP sake he is artistically gifted or has been taught art before. Else none of the programs are going to help.

My suggested progression would be. *After* you have mastered basic drawing fundamentals on paper.

Get very familiar with GIMP and its tools.
Then take on Inkscape and learn it.
Third learn to use Inkscape and GIMP together.
Lastly, then its time to learn Blender.

Blender is the god of opensource drawing programs. Its still way above my head.

I can't believe you said there are no tutorial out there!! Google is your best friend.

I'm not that great a gimp user  but i do know a few tricks or 2. So my advice:

Start by creating things you like.


A good start would be to make shiny orbs simply by using circle and gradient tool.

### Post by hyperdude111 on 2009-12-21
Gimp is really easy to learn, i have made loads of wallpapers with it and it only takes about 40 mins to make one.

To first learn the basics I would recommend a basic tutorial to get started and build on this. I started with this [Mac like wallpapers tut]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4569363")

Although at first everything you make will look awful just give it time and after a few days tinkering and playing you WILL create something to be proud of. My first released work went no.1 on gnome look for a short time period (not anymore). Then once you are used to the controls and small quirks of gimp you are ready to leave the tutorials behind and just freestyle your own ideas onto the page.    

If you want to try an app, gimp, in my opinion is second to none. The only serious irritation I have with it is the three pane setup but you get used to that with time.

Good Luck.
