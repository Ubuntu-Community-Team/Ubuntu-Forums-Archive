### Post by cubeist on 2010-04-18
The problem I see is the image doesn't really have any bokeh, thus your eye is fighting different focus regions in the image.  The only thing that should be in focus is the lynx in the foreground (and the logo overlay of course).  Everything else should be blurry, this will draw your eye to the lynx.

I would open the image in gimp, select the lynx and part of the log, then invert the selection, feather, and blur the background.  Then select a wide ellipse, invert, feather, and blur the heck out of all four corners.
