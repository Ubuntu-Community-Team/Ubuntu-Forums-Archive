### Post by Newfoundlander on 2010-05-18
OpenPrinting.org is a good site to check...

[http://www.openprinting.org/printers/manufacturer/Epson](http://www.openprinting.org/printers/manufacturer/Epson)
[http://www.openprinting.org/printers/manufacturer/HP](http://www.openprinting.org/printers/manufacturer/HP)
[http://www.openprinting.org/printer/Canon/](http://www.openprinting.org/printer/Canon/)

The [HP OfficeJet Pro K8600]("http://h10010.www1.hp.com/wwpc/ca/en/sm/WF06a/18972-18972-236251-3328075-3328075-3434445.html") seems like a good match for your needs.

I did silk screening so I never got into dye sublimation.  My experience with inkjets were that Epsons had very good print quality but were noisy, slow, and dispensed a lot more ink than necessary (double-sided printing wasn't an option because the paper got soaked through).  The results of a simple search on the Epson site yielded this result: Your search for "linux" did not return any results. :(

I've been using HP printers for 15 years with happy results.  Nearly every printer they offer has Linux drivers for download from their site.  In general, I am very satisfied with HP quality and their commitment to Linux so I will continue to use and recommend their products.

If I were you, I would call HP, Epson, and maybe even Canon and ask them to recommend a wide-format sublimation printer within your price range that works perfectly with Linux.

Cheers,

Stephen
