### Post by SteveNorman on 2008-04-10
First figure out if its an original or a print. Charcoal will fall of and settle to the bottom of the frame. Any art store/student/frame shop employee can tell you if its a real drawing. If its an original drawing the price will be more. If its a print it needs to be from a limited edition run to be of any real value. That will require a bit of homework to figure out. Find a reputible art dealer and ask them. Ask more than one.Then find an auction of horse racing memorabilia and enter it in.

oh yeah..even if its not valuable as an art piece it can still have value as a nostalgic piece for the right buyer. And the more you know about the history of the piece the more valuable it becomes (provenance), who owned it first, who owned the wardrobe etc..

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provenance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provenance)

### Post by valleyP on 2008-05-07
Dr Chris Jennings is an English painter with a very distinguished career, although he is little known to the public and his work does not fetch high prices. 
Chris is also a musician and at art school, or shortly after, in the early 1960's he was one of the original members of the Bonzo Dog band, who became very well known in Britain. To earn money in the 1960s he also painted and drew race horses, but his main professional work is very different. 
A recent exhibition is reviewed at [http://www.commentart.com/exhibition/Attachments_and_Separations](http://www.commentart.com/exhibition/Attachments_and_Separations)
