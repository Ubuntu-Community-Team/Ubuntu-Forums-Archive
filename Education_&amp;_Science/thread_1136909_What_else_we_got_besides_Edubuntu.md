### Post by hubie on 2009-04-27
I'll caveat this by saying that I have not tried the following two suggestions.  I have two children and they too love GCompris and TuxPaint.  My eldest particularly loves making animation movies in TuxPaint.

You could try [Linux Letters and Numbers]("http://lln.sourceforge.net/").  After finding that, I think I will have to give it a try as well.

The other, which is one that I have heard of but have never really looked into, is the [Sugar project]("http://wiki.sugarlabs.org/go/Main_Page").

Forgive me if those are bundled with Edubuntu, but I did a quick scan at the [applications page]("http://www.edubuntu.org/applications/8.04") and didn't see either one.

My kids also love playing with [Google Earth]("http://earth.google.com/").  Since you can do all the navigating with the mouse, they love making the Earth spin, zooming in and out, etc.  

In a similar vein, my eldest also loved playing with [Celestia]("http://www.shatters.net/celestia/"), basically for the same reason (flying around, etc.).  That one is probably beyond your kids at this point.

Outside of linux, if you have a Wii, I was shocked how quickly my 3 yr old picked up using the controllers.  He loves the Lego games (Star Wars, Indiana Jones) and he is remarkably good at them.
