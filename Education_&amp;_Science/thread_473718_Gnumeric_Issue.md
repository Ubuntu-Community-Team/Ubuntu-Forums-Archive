### Post by tweedledee on 2007-06-15
Not solutions, but possible alternatives:
> **in_flu_ence said:**
> 1) How can I insert a typeset formula to display in a gnumeric spreadsheet? In OpenOffice, I can key in a formula to display using openformula but I don't seem to see that in gnumeric.
Insert the formula as an image from another program?  Gnumeric doesn't seem to have any formatting capabilities like that (frankly text formatting is one of its weakest areas), unless there's a plugin I'm unaware of.  Openoffice is much superior here.

> **in_flu_ence said:**
> 2) Anyone having trouble with their graphs when they save their gnumeric spreadsheet in .xls format? I seem to lose the title/axis labels and having additional lines drawn on x-y plots.
I haven't had a reason to export to .xls.  However, when I just tried it, and read it into OpenOffice (no Excel around at the moment), it worked about as well as most .xls files do (graphs looked okay, but labels and some formatting off).  Possibly this would make it worse, but could you try exporting to .ods, then from Calc to .xls?  Calc isn't perfect either, but perhaps it will avoid the issue for the file(s) you are working with.

Also, given that Gnumeric can do a few things that Excel can't with respect to graphs, you may simply be asking for something the .xls format isn't capable of.
