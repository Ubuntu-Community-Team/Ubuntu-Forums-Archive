### Post by Steve R. on 2009-06-12
Students may be an underutilized resource in Open Source projects.  Over at TechDirt, I ran across this post: "*[Student Wins Against Professor's Threats Over Posting Code Online]("http://www.techdirt.com/articles/20090611/1027095200.shtml")*".

This article reminded me of a long term concern that I have had with how computer science is taught at universities.  I have the impression that students simply go through a process of re-inventing the wheel with their projects. Well instead of reinventing the wheel, why not integrate the computer science curriculum with ongoing projects such as UBUNTU?

Students, as part of their learning experience, would be a wonderful source of help in resolving issues, writing drivers, etc. This could be accomplished either as individual student projects and/or class projects.

Has the LINUX/UBUNTU community approached any universities as a means of integrating LINUX development with a college's curriculum?  Seems like a good idea.
