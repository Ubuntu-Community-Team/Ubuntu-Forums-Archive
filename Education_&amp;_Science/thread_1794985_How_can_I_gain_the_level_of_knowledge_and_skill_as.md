### Post by haqking on 2011-07-01
Learn everything about everything ;-)

A true hacker (hacking) covers so many aspects, there are thousands of books to read and lots to do.

Learn all the different OS (in Linux learn to read the man pages)
Learn to code (in different languages)
Learn about hardware (building,installing,upgrading,repairing,modifying...legacy and upto date (dont just learn how to stick a processor in, learn what a ALU is etc
Keep learning about everything, everytime you can.
Learn to be as comforatable in Denary, Binary, ternary, Octal, Hex as you are in everyday speak, learn ASCII so you know it off by heart

Your best bet at the beginning is to learn to program...choice of first language is debatable...for modern day OOP is recommended, i would say learn C then add onto it later with OOP concepts and port to whatever you choose.
C is close to hardware and so will teach you alot, once you know about hardware and software and how it is built) that is the foundation for everything else.

To be a true hacker is to hack......keep hacking away at it ;-)...there is no book to teach you everything about all you need to know, unless you count GOOGLE ;-)

the list of how to become a hacker is as follows:

1. Learn everything ad nauseum ad infinitum
2. first learn rule number 1 ;-)

EDIT : A better more...Complete answer to your question.

If you want books , there are tons but you don't really have to spend money on them, as much of the knowledge is freely available.

As far as networking and protocols go - read RFC's and lot's of them as many as you can get your hands on.

Coding is very important as well, as haqking mentioned. I would suggest definitely learning C, I would also suggest learning  ASM,C++ , Perl, Python, Java, PHP, Ruby and pretty much anything else you can wrap your brain around. IMO hacking is about the quest for knowledge, there is never enough, and you're never good enough at it.

Tinker with anything and everything you can hardware wise, doesn't matter if it's a Blade Server, or a toaster, figure out how it works, analyze it learn something from it. Learn to solder, trust me this will be very useful, learn to make different types of cables , cat 5, coax, fiber optic (this is both expensive and painful for beginners wear gloves) , and everything in between. 

Make something do something it shouldn't (legally) Think of the scene in back to the future, where Doc Brown has his crazy little lab set up with the thing that automatically feeds his dog when the alarm clock goes off. That dude was a hacker.  Strive to make square pegs fit round holes. 

Start writing apps to do things , even if there is already one to do it for you.

Look at Open Source projects, try to tackle something on their Todo list. 

As you can see the list could go on for days, basically just jump into it and give it all you got. There is no end to the journey you wish to embark on only a beginning.
