### Post by PC_load_letter on 2010-12-28
could you please be more precise in what kind of software you're asking about? I don't know (and I don't think there is) any open source educational software that will teach you Math or science. Take a look at Khan Academy for that: [http://www.khanacademy.org/](http://www.khanacademy.org/) 

Instead, if you're willing to grab a textbook to brush up on your Math skills, there are a few tools that will help you relearn the concepts faster and give you deeper insight while working out examples or using 2d and 3d plots. In this category, I find SAGE to be one if not the best tool to use along a standard Calculus textbook, you can get it from: [www.sagemath.org](www.sagemath.org)

From the repos, you may try: Kig, KAlgebra (albeit has a few bugs, but easy for plots), and Qalculate.
