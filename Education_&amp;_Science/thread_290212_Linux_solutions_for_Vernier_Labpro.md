### Post by reide on 2008-10-18
I have successfully installed the free Logger Lite download to my xo-OLPC netbook (one laptop per child project).  Vernier's new Fall 2008 newsletter claims to support Ubuntu 8.04 with a free Logger Lite program also, but the linux page of vernier.com says "coming soon," so hang on -- that should be a good solution.  The Lite program doesn't have all the advanced features of Pro, but the main features, graphing and analysis and ability to accept as many sensors as you have usb ports for, are there. The OLPC version became available May 1.  

You have to buy a $40 adapter for each sensor (or the $90 GoMotion if you want a motion detector), but don't need another interface such as LabPro or ULI.

Feel free to call or email Dave Vernier or his staff with questions -- I've found them very responsive.

### Post by adamlamee on 2009-01-28
I have used LabPros for many years, but have just recently been using them with Ubuntu 7.04 (some of my machines have Intel 815e graphics cards that aren't supported in newer releases, though this method should work with all Ubuntu versions).

I installed Wine and Vernier's LoggerPro.  There's no usb support so I connect the LabPros via serial cable (included with purchase).  The real-time data acquisition doesn't work, so I use TI-83s and LabPros to collect the data using Datamate (installable from the LabPro to the TI).  When finished, store the data to the LabPro, disconnect TI cable, and attach serial cable.  LoggerPro will be able to import the data set.

It's a less elegant solution than either PC-LabPro directly or TI/LabPro and usb connection via LoggerPro or Graphical Analysis, but it works every time.  Feel free to email if you'd like more specifics: [email]lameea@mail.lincoln.leon.k12.fl.us[/email]

Cheers,
Adam
