### Post by aburgess on 2011-03-04
I just started learning python a few months ago. There are lots of good tutorials and free books online. I'm working through this one right now:

[http://openbookproject.net/thinkCSpy/index.html](http://openbookproject.net/thinkCSpy/index.html)

It's a complete book, so it's got lots of detail. My BEST find however, is this series of videos from google, with files you can download to do the exercises yourself. This one got me writing real, useful scripts faster than anything. I HIGHLY recommend it, especially the babynames exercise.

[http://code.google.com/edu/languages/google-python-class/introduction.html](http://code.google.com/edu/languages/google-python-class/introduction.html)

The teacher moves quickly but explains well, definitely an efficient introduction.

### Post by tordeu on 2011-03-22
You can also try to find Python Tutorials or Intro on Youtube or using Miro. While this definitely not cover everything, I generally like video tutorials when it comes to new things I want to learn. It somehow does feel effortless.

Other than that, I also recommend the python docs, but watch under "tutorials" and not under "language reference", because that might be overwhelming or too specific at first.

And there really are a lot of tutorials on pretty much everything online. So, just google for a tutorial and if you don't like it, go to the next one. It shouldn't take too long to find a tutorial you like.

And the most important thing: Don't just read a book/tutorial or watch a video. Start trying out everything right from the beginning and play around with every new thing you learn. This gives a lot better results than just reading about something.
