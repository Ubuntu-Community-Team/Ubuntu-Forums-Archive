### Post by cdnaerogeek on 2009-08-20
Very interesting. 

I haven't taught any computer classes, but I used to go to 1-week computer camps when I was in middle school. These camps tended to be centered around a general theme like graphic design, programming, animation, etc. If your kids are younger, they may be more interested in applications rather than how the computer actually works. I would agree with PGScooter, though, that you should include more advanced projects if there are keeners in the crowd.

Ubuntu, and Linux more generally, is definitely the way to go for teaching how the computer actually works, since you need to jump through hoops in Windows to get to the lower-level stuff. However, Windows and Mac tend to support more advanced applications (in my experience), so keep that in mind when designing the curriculum.
