### Post by languageZ on 2009-03-16
I recommend you use a good audio-based German language course to master the basics. 

**[Rocket German]("http://www.learnalanguage.me/rocket-languages/rocket-german-premium.html")** consist out of audio lessons and software games. They also have their own members forum where you can post all your learning questions not covered by the complete course. They sell their complete course for 100% less than the other big names out there. I must admit I didn't like their site design but heir product is top notch.

You can also have a look at the audio-based **[Pimsleur German course]("http://www.top-audiobook-downloads.com/title.aspx?titleId=6014")**. It's more expensive but also a very good program to learn German with.

Also read the article "**[How to Learn German Fast]("http://www.submityourarticle.com/articles/Gerrit-Du%20Plessis-5352/how-to-learn-German-fast-52778.php")**" for some more ideas.

Good luck!;)

### Post by Sentinel83 on 2009-09-21
Hi LongTom,

I'm a little late to this thread, but I have also been learning German for the last 3 years.  Here are a couple of good resources that I use:

[http://www.livemocha.com]("http://www.livemocha.com") (social site for language learning, free account, registration needed)

[http://dict.leo.org]("http://dict.leo.org") LEO Dictionary - best I've found for translations and phrases.  Based out of Chemnitz Uni I believe.

The LiveMocha site is very similar to the software you get from Rosetta Stone (did that too) but it is free and available on the web.  It tries to use the same phrase/immersion principles as Rosetta Stone.  Not sure if that would be useful for young kids, but who knows.
