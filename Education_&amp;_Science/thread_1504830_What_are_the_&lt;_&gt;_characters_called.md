### Post by BoneKracker on 2010-06-09
ISO8859-1 refers to them as the "less than" and "greater than" characters.

The Unicode specification refers to them as the "less than sign" and "greater than sign".

Also, as far as I know:

(these are parentheses)
[these are brackets] a.k.a. "square brackets"
{these are braces} a.k.a. "curly brackets"
<these are angle brackets> (because they are bing used as a pair, for grouping)
`these are back-ticks` (because they are bing used as a pair, for grouping; normally "accent grave")
^ that was caret (some say "power of"; normally "circumflex")
| that was a pipe (in code), normally "vertical line"
~ that was tilde (no, it's not called a "squiggly")
