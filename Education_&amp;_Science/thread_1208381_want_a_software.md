### Post by samden on 2009-07-09
Basic: OpenOffice. Will do it all simply and nastily, just like Microsoft Office.

If you are prepared for a steeper learning curve, I'd suggest LaTeX too. It does high-quality scientific typesetting and presentations. Won't draw 3D diagrams however, for that you'd want something else.

You won't find one program that will do everything you list, but you will easily find a few that will work well together and do it all.

### Post by wanichan on 2009-07-19
> You won't find one program that will do everything you list, but you will easily find a few that will work well together and do it all.

This. For presentations I would use open office impress, it does the job. You can also make tables in open office writer. They won't be as fancy as latex but you'll save a ton of time.

As far as 3d figures go, use R. There's a bit of a learning curve but once you get the hang of it it isn't that bad and I find it much easier than gnuplot. The functions persp and scatterplot3d do what most people need. You can also draw contour plots pretty easily.
