### Post by vanadium on 2009-02-27
I am using Bibus for this. It is a bibliographic database that integrates well with OOo writer, allowing cite-while-you-write. If you fill out the path where your PDF is stored (local or on a server), you can immediately open the PDF by righ-clicking on the reference.

You could also use Zotero for this. Zotero is bibliographic management software that actually is a firefox plugin. The strength of Zotero is that it can import record data very easily from several web resources. You can attach as many PDF's or other documents to a record that then can also be opened from within the application.

Zotero also supports cite while youwrite via a writer plugin. You may find that this is all you need, and more. For me, I found that Zotero becomes somewhat slower with my large collection (3000+ records), while Bibus still handles this in a snapp way. That is why I decided to continue with Bibus. I use Zotero for importing references from websites, which can then be conveniently exported as ris or bibtex for import in Bibus.
