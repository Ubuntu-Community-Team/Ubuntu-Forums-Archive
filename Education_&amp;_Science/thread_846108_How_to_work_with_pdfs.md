### Post by jraab22 on 2008-07-08
I've always found taking notes directly on a paper to be useless, I can never find that paper again and the note is buried somewhere.  The system I've settled on has been to use a reference manager (I use zotero, although I'd imagine this would work with any of them) and linking plain txt notes to them. I just open up a txt file and take notes, link it to the pdf, or citation through zotero, and now its easily found again.  Not quite what you do, but it is an alternative.

### Post by sierrashirley78 on 2011-09-28
With the full version of [Adobe Acrobat]("http://adobe.com/")  you can select a table of data and then choose to copy or save it as a  table, or import it into a spreadsheet. You can also save PDFs in text,  HTML, XML, Word or other formats. Those formats may make it easier to  extract the information you need.  
There are also other paid alternatives that will extract text from a PDF. Some are cheaper than Acrobat:
