### Post by eumetaxas on 2010-05-25
Hi all!

In the department where i work as a resident, we are planning to release a medical e-book with some pictures from procedures taken exclusively from our department. The book will be made using entirely free formats and software (pdf, GIMP, latex, texmaker, gentium fonts, Ubuntu etc). We will distribute it for free and we are not planning to make any money from it. We will upload it to our website ([www.pneumevah.gr](www.pneumevah.gr)) and maybe to gigapedia etc. 

I am trying to choose **a license under which the book will be released**. The features that i would like the licence to have are:

1: Able to protect our work (intellectual property)
2: **Allow the book to be freely distributed** in a way that anyone can use (copy, translate, quote etc) the included material (document and  pictures) as long they **put a reference to us** and also distribute it for free as we are
3: **Not to be able for someone to sell it** (either electronically or printed) without our permission

I know that GNU allows someone to sell your material without your permission and this is something that my director will reject. I also found creative commons licence but i got lost in the details.

Any suggestions?
Thank you
