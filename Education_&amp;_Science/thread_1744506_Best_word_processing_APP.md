### Post by irrdev on 2011-04-30
LibreOffice is a recent fork of OO by the open source community. Last year, Oracle bought Sun, the company that has supported OO for years, and Oracle's direction for OO was seen as very detrimental to the project. That assessment proved very correct, as two weeks ago Oracle announced that they are altogether dropping future development support for OO. LibreOffice is definitely the way to go, as it includes all the features of openoffice plus some much-needed additions that Sun, and now Oracle, didn't see fit to include in their business model.

### Post by Lateralis on 2011-04-30
I'm writing my physics PhD thesis and I find there is *only* one program to use for anything approaching high level writing where figures, tables, references and cross-referencing are required: LaTeX.  It takes a bit of time to get used to, but when you do there's no going back!  

To make use of Latex you will need to install the texlive package and a Tex editor.  I personally use TeX Maker but there are dozens available.  If you need help with latex there are thousands of pages on the internet that provide assistance, and you can always drop me a PM with specific questions.
