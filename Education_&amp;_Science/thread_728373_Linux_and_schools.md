### Post by TJCIB on 2008-03-27
I am in a small, private, American school in northern Brazil.  We cannot get American software since its technically illegal to bring software across the border in bulk.  You can do it for private use...

Being small, our budget is close to nothing for software and upgrades.

I have researched for about 4 months.  This week I switched our 10-computer lab to Ubuntu.  I did not use Edubuntu or a terminal server setup because we don't currently have a machine with enough gusto to be the server.

I am using NIS and NFS for centralized login and storing home partitions on one large machine.

The kids love it so far.  It meets all of our needs.  It is much safer/secure.  And I find it way easier to administer (coming from a teacher who does this voluntarily with no formal training).

If I ever move back to America to teach again, I would lobby for my district to move to Linux.

*edit*
We still use windows in the office because of Quickbooks.  Which I know has alternatives, but are not feasible for us, yet.  My thinking is running Windows in a virtual setting with Quickbooks in the end.
