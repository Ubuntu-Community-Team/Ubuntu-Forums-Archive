### Post by Bruce H on 2009-12-22
Please keep in mind that Linux is a good ten years younger than either Windows or Mac. Furthermore, it did not receive financial backing for the first few years of its development. So, the phenomenal rates of growth on the desktop in the last couple years combined with very heavy usage on the server for the past ten years might tend to argue against your thesis.

You might consider re-thinking your starting point by finding the answer to the question, "is Linux really seeing slower rates of adoption than either Windows or Mac?".

### Post by Bruce H on 2009-12-22
If I might speculate... if Linux really is seeing slower rates of adoption, it might be because when Windows (and to a lesser degree, Mac) came out, there was practically nothing available that could do the things that people needed to do and was affordable. So they stormed the market.

Linux does not have that advantage. Linux has to grow by being better in enough ways to win converts.
