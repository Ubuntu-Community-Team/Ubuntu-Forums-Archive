### Post by vincentertainment on 2010-09-13
I've been looking for a program that can not only diagram a decision tree, but also prompt users with available options at every step and integrate with a calendar, to-do list, address book, and email.
 
For example, if I need to make a sales call to John Doe, I'd start with a task to contact John. If I got his voicemail, I'd want a prompt a task to follow-up with again in a few days if I hadn't heard back from him. But if I do reach him, I'd like to be promted with options like scheduling an appointment, writing a work order or opening point-of-sale software, sending more information, adding a task such as finding the answer to a question, archiving the contact as a dead lead, adding a note, or following up again at another time. Each of these options may lead to different options and many may share the same option. 
 
The ability to sync with handheld devices would be great too.
Support for multiple users and the ability to delegate steps to other users or transfer a process to another user.
Such a program designed as a Thunderbird/Lightning extension could also make everything cross-platform.
 
Some CRM programs have 'Process builders' that can be customized with any decision tree and saved to a process. I've used Franklin Covey's [www.planplusonline]("http://www.planplusonline") before to follow customized processes. (Only the Sales and Business plans have the Process feature)
The limitation is that its browser-based and requires a monthly fee.
I'd prefer a client app that runs on my computer, especially if its open-source and cross-platorm.
