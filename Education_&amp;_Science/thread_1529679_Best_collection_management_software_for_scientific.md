You don't have to use Mendeley's file synching and you can have it just "watch" your Dropbox folder for new files and turn off the file organization so you can do it all yourself.

Personally I think I tried Referencer and other options, kept coming back to Mendeley.

### Post by libssd on 2010-07-13
I'm not quite sure what you mean by "collection management software," but Zotero, which is a FireFox plugin, is pretty much the gold standard for managing scholarly references and integrating them into papers.

**Get Zotero here**: [http://www.zotero.org/](http://www.zotero.org/)
**Review**: [http://www.linux.com/archive/articles/57841](http://www.linux.com/archive/articles/57841)
**Ubuntu Community Documentation**: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Zotero](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Zotero)
