### Post by BLTicklemonster on 2007-03-31
Look in the repositories, there's tons of programs for ham radio.


BUT, you don't have to take CW any more to get a license. You used to have to take a novice test, technician test, then general, extra, and more, PLUS pass 5 wpm, up to technician to get voice in HF, then 13 wpm for general and above, but they dropped CW (morse code), so now there's three classes. Technician, General, and Extra.

[http://www.google.com/search?num=30&hl=en&safe=off&client=firefox-a&rls=com.ubuntu%3Aen-US%3Aofficial&hs=1yw&q=ham+radio+exam+question+pool&btnG=Search](http://www.google.com/search?num=30&hl=en&safe=off&client=firefox-a&rls=com.ubuntu%3Aen-US%3Aofficial&hs=1yw&q=ham+radio+exam+question+pool&btnG=Search)

I studied and took the novice, tech and general class tests all the same day, and nutted up on 13 wpm, (though I found that after the initial psyche out, I should have stuck with it, I'd have passed it) but passed the 5 wpm. I was stuck with a tech plus license and never got around to taking the 13 wpm to get my general class, darn it. But it's okay now, because now all I have to do is pass the exam for general again, then the one for extra.


GOOD LUCK!

Oh, and the questions change for some of these classes after June 31, 2007, so pay attention.

### Post by n3gbz on 2007-05-29
I just downloaded my favorite morse code tutor from [http://c2.com/morse/](http://c2.com/morse/) which is based on the May 1977 QSL article A Fully Automatic Morse Code Teaching Machine by Ward Cunningham.  

To make it work I had to 
1. change owner to root
2. change group to plugdev
3. chmod to 550 

(These were the settings for the version on my Windows partition.  I just made my Ubuntu file match.) 

This tutor sends code and allow entering the characters from the keyboard.

### Post by xe1ufo on 2007-12-28
Though Morse Code (also known as CW) is not required in U.S. and a few other countries, it CERTAINLY IS required in many others, such as Mexico. But even if it is not required, Morse is a fascinating means of communication, allowing a person to build simple transceivers small enough to fit in an Altoids mint tin, and take them backpacking.  

And Morse code signals are far more efficient than voice. One watt of Morse will do what 8 watts of voice will do. (Low power operation -- under five watts of CW or digital modes, or 10 watts of voice-- on the ham bands is known as QRP.) And CW is a WHOLE lot of fun! 

Check out these links for more information:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QRP_operation]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QRP_operation")
[http://www.qrparci.org/]("http://www.qrparci.org/")
[http://hfpack.com/]("http://hfpack.com/")

Dr. Steve, XE1UFO (in Mexico) and KA5SUT (in U.S.)
