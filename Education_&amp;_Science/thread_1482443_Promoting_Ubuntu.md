### Post by radioboy on 2010-05-16
I know that the effort should be put in promoting Ubuntu, Linux and open source in general, instead of wasting time in telling the bad stuff about Windows, but surely you will need some preparation for some possible tough questions.

All these news are related to Argentina:
[http://techrights.org/2010/03/22/microsoft-trial-in-argentina/](http://techrights.org/2010/03/22/microsoft-trial-in-argentina/)
[http://techrights.org/2009/09/02/microsoft-vs-public-fs-speech/](http://techrights.org/2009/09/02/microsoft-vs-public-fs-speech/)

Another effort should be put in how old machines currently rendered "obsolete" -in terms of the current versions of Windows-, can be brought back to productive and good life with Ubuntu or light-weight distributions.

It's also important that you show that there are resources in Spanish related to Ubuntu:
[http://www.ubuntu-es.org/](http://www.ubuntu-es.org/)
[http://ubuntu-ar.org/](http://ubuntu-ar.org/)
In the last of these, there is a note warning that "ubuntu.com.ar" is NOT an official Ubuntu site, so take note of that.

There is also a nice column on upgrading to Lucid in an Argentinian newspaper here (in Spanish):
[http://www.lanacion.com.ar/nota.asp?nota_id=1264390](http://www.lanacion.com.ar/nota.asp?nota_id=1264390)
