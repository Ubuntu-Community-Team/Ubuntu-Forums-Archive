### Post by JadenRosen on 2011-07-30
Hello, this is a message mainly directed at Canonical, but also at technology teams in School districts everywhere.

Dear Canonical,

I am a technology administrator for the school district here where I live and just yesterday, after over a year of persuasion, our school board finally accepted my proposal to switch our school district 100% to Ubuntu. But I have a few suggestions for you.

First, I have created my own version of Edubuntu essentially and I think if you implicated a better version of my edits, the American educators would really like the ease of set-up. The main suggestion is a slight tweak to Wubi, that would be (only when installing Edubuntu of course) asking the installer if the computer's main account was going to be a Student (with restricted abilities) or a Teacher (which would install a grade-book and such).

A simple check and uncheck menu would suffice to define the abilities of the student's account to give schools flexibility. Other than that, to go one step farther I would request an option (again in Edubuntu) that would allow the installer to choose the grade level or school (elementary, middle, high) and install the programs accordingly.

Second, I would recommend to Canonical that you stop focusing the software on Corporations. Why? Answer this please. Is it easier to learn English first and speak it forever? Or learn Russian first and then take English courses?

I would say English first, wouldn't you? So instead of having Corporations switch to Ubuntu, get schools to switch to Ubuntu so those students take it with them. These businesses now can only adapt so much, but the businesses these students are going to create in the future, will have Ubuntu at their heart if they grow up with it.

That is a firm belief of mine, I really think you guys in Canonical's marketing team should also think about contacting the United States Military. Their training schools all together have millions of computers. If they didn't have to pay $300,000,000 for the Windows software, I am sure a lot of soldiers would be very thankful to you. If the American government needs to make cut-backs why not capitalize on it?

Finally, the Unity desktop is a great addition to the Ubuntu Desktop. I haven't got to experience it yet on the school machines because I have decided to only use LTS builds on the network. But it looks great and I thought I would commend you on finally beating the ugly Windows start bar.

A fan for life and a missionary to the masses brainwashed by Microsoft,

Jaden.
