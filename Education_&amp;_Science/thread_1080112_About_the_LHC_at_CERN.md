### Post by hubie on 2009-02-27
I don't think it is media spin; this is a real race if the Higgs is detectable within the range of the Tevatron.  I think the fact that the LHC deciding to run all winter shows that.  Also, one of the justifications for building the LHC was to detect the Higgs.

The Higgs has been such a big deal for so long, that I am sure a Nobel will be awarded if it is ever detected.  The tangible benefits from the race, apart from the two or three people who would be awarded the Nobel, are not much more than bragging rights for the thousands of people involved.  But as a physicist, it is highly motivating to be able to be part of such an important experiment.  It is one thing to take a lot of data and build up models that describe what you see, but here there is the Standard Model, which has shown itself to be such an excellent model, and this model says that this particle exists and it has such-and-such energy.  To be able to go out and verify a model prediction is very exciting. 

This is very similar to Einstein's general theory where it was an odd and hard to understand theory, but it was able to describe some unusual things like the precession of Mercury's orbit.  But it also said predicted that grazing-incidence starlight to the Sun should be bent by so-and-so arcseconds, and Eddington and others went out and found that to be true.
