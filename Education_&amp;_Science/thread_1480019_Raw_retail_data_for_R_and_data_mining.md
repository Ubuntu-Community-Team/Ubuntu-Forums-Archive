### Post by gunksta on 2010-05-11
US Census data is always a treasure trove and numerous other federal agencies make data available for research and policy analysis.

I don't know of anyone selling POS data on the cheap, which is IMHO, a good thing. It annoys me to pieces that it is for sale at all. It would really annoy me if it were available to someone who just wanted to "play" with it.

Besides, POS data would only really be useful from a data mining point of view if you had some data to connect to it. For example - it might be interesting to know that certain people, with certain profiles shopped at Circuit City AND your store too. What does that mean? What assumptions can you make about those individuals and how can you better target them. If all you want to do is dig around and learn a little about data mining - you don't really need something costly like POS data. I would think you can do equally amusing things with census data or other sources of publicly available data.
