### Post by HydrologyGuy on 2009-11-28
Hi,

I'm afraid that the OOo conversion to PPT is truly horrible.
I have had presentations completely mangled. If you're presenting at a conference, you may be able to get way with giving them a PDF file rather than a PPT. That's what I usually do.

If you *have* to convert a presentation to a PPT file, my advice would be to use bitmaps in your slides, rather than vector graphics, as they seem to convert better. Also use simple backgrounds as the converter can screw these up too. 

Make sure you always preview your document in PowerPoint or the PowerPoint viewer pptview.

Good luck!

### Post by samden on 2009-11-29
Depending on your needs, you could just install OpenOffice on the PCs you use, or carry around OpenOffice portable on a memory stick, and ditch Microsoft formats completely.

PDF is definately the best format for final versions of a presentation, but is obviously no use if you are trying to do part of the editing in Word and part in OpenOffice. You're best to do all the editing in one application if possible, ie OpenOffice on all computers, producing a .pdf or .ppt version as the last step.

If you're up for something different there are also alternative ways of making presentations, such as using LaTeX beamer...
