### Post by samden on 2009-08-18
I think the * section actually gives you more flexibility - as it means you can buy a computer without it already running an appropriate OS. Without that you would have to buy a netbook running a Greek OS, and if you couldn't find that in Linux you'd be stuck with XP. But with the * you can buy a netbook running an English OS with your coupon, and install your own Greek localised OS on it.

I agree however that the scheme seems a bit silly. In a time of recession, the last thing the government needs to be doing is taking more money from taxpayers to fund yet another experimental scheme. The biggest thing that will affect whether a child learns or not is whether they value education or not, and whether they themselves want to succeed. The more you give them for free, the more they will take for granted, and the less they will value education. So you end up achieving the opposite of what you intended.

It is in Africa where kids have nothing that they are willing to walk barefoot for hours to get to school. Yet in the West kids are given everything, and dragged to school on a bus, only to muck around in class and sneak off downtown to have a smoke if they can possibly get away with it. 

The scheme is doomed to fail to improve educational achievement, but you may be able to use it to encourage a few students to use FOSS. Good luck!
