### Post by machoo02 on 2007-10-01
ok...I just looked at the endnote website, and they have a [BibTeX export style]("http://www.endnote.com/support/enstyledetail.asp?SORT=2&PAGE=1&METH=0&DISC=none&JOUR=export&BSRT=none&FF1=none&FF2=none&FF3=none&CITE=none&DKEY=714200664457JBA") available for download (if it's not already part of the installed style list).  Choose this as your output style, then export all of your references into a .txt file.  You should then be able to import this file into pybibliographer.
