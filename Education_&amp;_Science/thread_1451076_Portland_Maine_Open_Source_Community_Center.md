### Post by Jeff Anthony on 2010-04-10
I'm a member of a local neighborhood organisation who recently won a stimulus grant which must put a portion to economic development. Last week I was successful in bringing the idea for an open source driven community center near the top of their list of preferred options. The goal is to have a free place where members could bring their laptops and work with each other learning and sharing whatever their heart desires as long as it had something to do with open source. Ubuntu would be the primary OS and the ubuntu philosophy adopted. 

I have a few really great ideas for first projects as well (which brings me to why I posted in this section). I do have a patent pending but it is open source, if anyone wants to help set up my drupal website we can let everyone else get to it. It's essentially an Ubuntu VM completely integrated with Google Services using Chrome with a hacked ChromeOS interface for the browser. It's fast, stable and I've been selling them but have had even more compliments than sales.

Long story shorter, I want to give one of one of these devices for free to every student  living in the area covered by the grant plus two adjacent areas, locally referred to as the Portland, Maine peninsula. The Portland High School serving this area caters to over 70 languages. I'd love some input on how we could easily give each student a version in their own language.

The end objective of the community center is we can hopefully reduce the cost of computer service, training, and upkeep while increasing the computer usability, skills and understanding for the entire community. The projects, being community based, are apt to help everyone in the area (population ~2500) at one point or another and in turn develop a deeper understanding of ubuntu.
