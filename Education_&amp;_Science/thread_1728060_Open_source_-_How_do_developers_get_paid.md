### Post by rg4w on 2011-04-13
Many contribute out of passion, but I've read that on the larger projects as many as 75% of the programmers working on FOSS projects are indeed salaried.

Ironically, much of the funding for FOSS projects comes from contributions from companies that make the money from proprietary systems.

Ostensibly, the long-term ideal pursued by many FOSS projects is to make revenue from services and support in the ecosystem surrounding the software rather than the software itself.

This is a difficult thing to do, however, since it disincentivises quality and completeness of the software: the more it "just works" the less one needs supplemental services.

That said, some orgs have been doing very well with this model.  Red Hat, for example, is on track to hit more than US$1 billion in sales this year.  Not bad for free sofware. :)  Let's hope Canonical is able to do the same in due time.
