### Post by rewyllys on 2014-01-02
The University of South Carolina has programs up through the Ph.D. in both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering.  You could attend it at the in-state tuition rates.

If you want to get a little farther away from home, North Carolina State at Raleigh has long been a noted engineering, science, and technology institution.  A bit farther north is Virginia Polytechnic University, also a fine EST institution.

As the #1 student in your class, it would be worth your time to try for scholarships to MIT and CalTech.
