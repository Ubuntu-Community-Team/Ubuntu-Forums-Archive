### Post by akniss on 2008-03-27
If you're in the US, try this:
[http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/oa/climate/stationlocator.html](http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/oa/climate/stationlocator.html)

It takes a little while to get used to, but you can get historical data form many weather stations around the country.  I use it all the time to get temperature and precipitation data for agricultural research.

Search for a station by zipcode, state, etc.  Once you click on a weather station, there should be a link to "Data Inventories" on the left. There should be an option to look at daily summaries.  I usually choose the "DAT" option for Daily Surface Data,  Some cooperators require payment, but most Airport and University cooperators provide data for free.
