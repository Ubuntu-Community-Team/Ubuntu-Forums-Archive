### Post by kvk on 2008-12-29
I have never found any training software that wasn't more than a glorified spreadsheet, and they are usually not very good. I would create a series of general spreadsheet templates that he can modify to each client, with entries for daily training along with weekly/monthly/etc. assessment measurements (BF %, max VO2, limit strength, sprint speeds, caloric intake, whatever is appropriate). Then from the spreadsheets he can set up graphs that chart the client's progress in a given area. It takes a little while to do the initial set-up, but once it's done it's nice to see all the blanks fill in with data that can be used to fine-tune the training schema.
