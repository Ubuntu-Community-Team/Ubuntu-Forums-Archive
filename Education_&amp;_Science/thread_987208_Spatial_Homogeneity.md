### Post by WW on 2008-11-19
What do you mean by "present"?

If c(x,y,z) is the concentration at position (x,y,z), then you have c(x,y,z) = F(z) for some function F.  In other words, the concentration depends only on the height (or depth, depending on your orientation of z).

### Post by Rumo on 2008-11-20
There are two possible ways I can think of. In mathematics there is the term "discrepancy", which tends to zero for large N and equidistributed sequences:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrepancy_of_a_sequence#Discrepancy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrepancy_of_a_sequence#Discrepancy)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-discrepancy_sequence](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low-discrepancy_sequence)

A physicists way to do this would be to think of your molecules as charges on a surface. You can then use Coulomb's law to calculate the total potential energy of your charged particles which should be minimal for the equidistributed case (U_12 =~ 1/r_12)
