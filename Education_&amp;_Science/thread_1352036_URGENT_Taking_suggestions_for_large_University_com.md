### Post by PandaGoat on 2009-12-11
I would definitely consider installing Aquamacs or some other build of emacs for OSX.  As an argument, emacs is an all-around text editor even without customization; it provides syntax highlighting, indentation, and much much more for nearly all programming language and general text editing.  Furthermore, your lab can provide lisp procedures to cover specific needs.  Finally, there is no foreseeable reason (by me anyway)why not to have emacs installed; also, installing it will provide a better choice of text-editors.

I do not know how much mathematical studying will go on in the lab, but I would certainly install [Sage Mathematics]("http://www.sagemath.org/").  Actually this isn't necessary to install on all computers since sage can run as a server to which many people can connect through a browser without needing sage installed locally.  Some free websites provide this like sagenb.com, but it would not hurt to have sage installed locally, would it?

I can't think of anything else although I'm certain there are other essential programs.
