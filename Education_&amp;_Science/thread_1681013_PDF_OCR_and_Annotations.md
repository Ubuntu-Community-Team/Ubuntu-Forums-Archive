### Post by nathan28 on 2011-02-03
So I discovered Okular and have been very happy with it, overlooking the bloated aspects of its KDE interface with ease. The beveled, rounded edges even made it feel kind of special, like a cat that you know should lose weight but is too damn adorable.

But then, I got my first scanned PDF today that the office assistant didn't bother to OCR in quAcrobat. Now, the highlighter tool is... USELESS!!!

There's a simple solution for this, I thought. I'll use the rectangle tool, that's what I did with Acrobat when this happened. But... there is no rectangle tool in Okular. There is, but apparently it can't be made the default annotation. Instead I have this useless ellipse tool that obscures entire lines of text, and an impractical polygon tool--to get to the rectangle I have to edit the annotation each time. There's the freehand line, but I don't like carpal tunnel that much and my hands shake.

Is there any way to make the rectangle, with a thin (rather than 5-pt) line, default? Why doesn't Okular support persistent modifications to the default annotation settings? For a program with such forward design and interface, this makes me feel like I'm using AutoCAD to review documents.

Beyond that, what are the better OCR tools for linux? CLI is fine for something like that.
