### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-12-19
> **rebeltaz said:**
> I want to display temperature, humidity, forecasts, precipitation chances/amounts, windspeed, alerts, etc... in a nice full screen graphical environment.

Weather data availability, cost, and format varies from country to country.

In the United States, weather.gov has a vast amount of advertising-free weather information available, in both graphical and non-graphical formats.

The simplest way is to use the web pages generated by forecast.weather.gov.
Example for Shelby, AL: [http://forecast.weather.gov/MapClick.php?lat=33.0818&lon=-86.5388](http://forecast.weather.gov/MapClick.php?lat=33.0818&lon=-86.5388)
Note that the *exact* Lat/Lon does make a difference in the data returned!

In the USA, you can pull highly specific data (conditions, forecasts, probabilities, alerts, radar images, etc) from NOAA servers, all for free, and display it any way you wish. It's not difficult, though the process of integration can be a bit tedious if you are not interested in the different API formats.

See [http://cheesehead-techblog.blogspot.com/2012/09/us-national-weather-service-info-feeds.html](http://cheesehead-techblog.blogspot.com/2012/09/us-national-weather-service-info-feeds.html) for perhaps more detail about US weather data than you really wanted to know.

### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-12-19
If you're in the U.S.,  using your Zipcode with [www.weather.gov](www.weather.gov) will get you to the NOAA weather page for your location.  E.g., [www.weather.gov/12345](www.weather.gov/12345).

And here are NOAA's RSS feeds: [http://www.nws.noaa.gov/rss/](http://www.nws.noaa.gov/rss/)  Those can be parsed and displayed.

Pretty sure the UK's MET office and other comparable national services offer similar services.

You might look at the source for some of the weather apps and applets that are available. As far as I know, most pull data, like an RSS feed, from one or more national services.
