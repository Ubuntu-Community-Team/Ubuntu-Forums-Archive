So as a dedicated Ubuntu convert I thought I would take a Linux exam to assess my overall skills and have some extra motivation to study,

The LPI seems to be just another multiple guess exam so I set sights on the RHCT. It is a performance based lab exam.

I took it today and am somewhat optimistic about the results. I passed the troubleshooting part of the exam, which is compulsory. Meaning if you do not get past fixing your system you are out of luck.

I think it was a fair exam and very passable. I will update when I get my results. If I did not pass I think I was close.

You can book it through VUE or Prometric (I used Prometric). You first need to pass the LPI Level 1 exam. I passed the UCP, but I thought it was too desktop oriented due to the presence of many questions about gnome, gconf and other gnome specific stuff. If you use any other version of *buntu, then you would probably find that part difficult. Other than that the questions were good quality and there were a few that were not multiple choice (you had to type in the answer). I am going for the Novell certs and then later on in the year I may go for RHCE.
