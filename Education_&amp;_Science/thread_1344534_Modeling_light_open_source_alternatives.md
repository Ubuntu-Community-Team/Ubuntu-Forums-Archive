### Post by hubie on 2009-12-03
I think it really depends on what your goals and needs are.  If you have a very specific interest, such as these parabolic or spherical reflectors for this one particular project, then (as I'm sure you have found out) dishing out the money for a professional optical design software can be very expensive.  In this case, you might be better served (perhaps, as I have never looked into the costs) in contracting a specific study from an optical design person.  I'm sure the [ORA]("http://www.opticalres.com/engr/illumination.html") or [ZEMAX]("http://www.zemax.com/index.php?option=com_frontpage&Itemid=1") folks can do that for you, or you can contract with someone who is an experienced user of that kind of software.

If your needs are very simple, such as very simple geometries, then I think you could get away with either rolling your own, which actually is not too hard to do, or you could use an established engine like Blender.  There is, of course, a learning curve to this.

The most expensive option is purchasing optical design code and doing it yourself.  The cost is in the license as well as the learning curve.  I am a ZEMAX user, so I am quite familiar with their code and pricing (I run mine on a Win2k machine that I have virtualized on my Ubuntu system).  Besides them and the CodeV guys, there is also the [ASAP]("http://www.breault.com/software/asap-projectapps.php") people.

If you want to be a bit more descriptive in what you are doing, I might have a better suggestion (or for what it is worth, I would tell you what I would do).
