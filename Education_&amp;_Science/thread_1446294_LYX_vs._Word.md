### Post by anoop999 on 2010-05-08
LyX is different from a word processor. It is a graphical interface to LaTeX, and allows you to describe the structure and meaning of the information in your document. It is more difficult to specify the exact layout than with a typewriter or word processor.

Highlights of LyX:
Formula editor - type a formula in LaTeX or build it using the formula editor and it is displayed instantly and beautifully
Branches - allows you to easily show or hide parts of your document (e.g. if you are writing an exam paper with questions and answers, you can show just the questions, or just the answers, or both)
Bibliography support (BibTeX); integrates with JabRef bibliography manager

Disadvantages: you cannot easily import a word processor document into LyX or LaTeX.

You can convert your LyX document via LaTeX to various outputs:
HTML, with cleaner and smaller file sizes than word processors
PDF, for printing
OpenDocument (ODF), for sharing with word processor users
These conversions are very straightforward and just require choosing from a menu.

LyX documents are stored small plain text files, and are roughly 20% the size of a Word document or 50% the size of an ODF document. You can see all the formatting, and documents cannot become 'corrupted'. You can extend the features of LyX by including any LaTeX code, but you can use most of the features of LyX without knowing LaTeX.

### Post by euler_fan on 2010-05-08
LyX is great for many, many things as a step from Word/OO.org towards LaTeX.

Given you're doing chemistry, the big thing is to what extent do you want to just import the graphics you want into the file as .jpg (or whatever) versus creating them within LyX using one of the Chemistry-related LaTeX packages.

Formulas are definitely easier, styles are easier, but at some point I recommend just breaking down and going for LaTeX. [Math Into LaTeX]("http://www.ctan.org/tex-archive/info/mil/mil.pdf") is a great introduction to the system with an emphasis on mathematics. I have the hardcopy version of same and love it.
