### Post by nateperson on 2009-07-30
Don't know about getting one donated to you, but if you're willing to purchase cheep serviceable laptop or computer I recommend goodwill.  Don't know where exactly you are in the US but there a a lot of computer specific goodwills scattered about.  The best is probably in Austin: [http://www.austincomputerworks.org/]("http://www.austincomputerworks.org/")
I've done a bit of shopping there, and they even have a return policy. 

Another option is to look at charities that help the disabled, one of the things they often do is refurbish and sell donated laptops and computers, many often get certified in their repair.

### Post by urukrama on 2009-08-02
Go to a charity shop (like Goodwill in the US). They often have very cheap computers. Or try and find a company that is renewing their equipment and getting rid of their old computers; you'll be amazed how many good computers end up in the bin.

When you find an old computer, just install a light weight linux distro on it (Debian, Slitaz, DSL, etc.) and you'll have a fully functional system for very little money.
