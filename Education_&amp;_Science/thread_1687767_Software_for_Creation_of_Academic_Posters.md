### Post by akniss on 2011-02-15
I've used Scribus for a couple posters. I really liked the way they turned out. I've had ok luck with OOo Impress as well. Scribus takes a little bit longer to learn (not because its difficult, but because its just different from powerpoint) but I think the look of the poster ends up standing out a little since its not the typical powerpoint look. OOo impress is very similar to powerpoint so there's not much learning curve, but the look of the poster will be similar. Both have good export to PDF for printing. I think either one should suit your needs.

### Post by earlycj5 on 2011-02-17
To create a poster, I would've preferred to use Inkscape in most instances.

It's the best suited tool for the job, IMO.  Powerpoint/Impress are for computer presentations, not for creating posters and print material.

However, I was locked into using Impress because the printing in my old department would only accept Powerpoint files to print files.

For LaTeX equations what about using KLatexFormula and generating transparent .png files?

[http://kde-apps.org/content/show.php/KLatexFormula?content=40430](http://kde-apps.org/content/show.php/KLatexFormula?content=40430)

Generate .pngs of the formulas, import into Inkscape.

YMMV.
