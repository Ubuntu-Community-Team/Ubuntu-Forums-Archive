### Post by samden on 2009-01-26
Install GnomeSword (it is in the repositories, sudo apt-get install gnomesword). Gnomesword is bible study software that will let you download and read the Bible on your computer, you can read different translations and languages too. It allows you to search the Bible, which is great when you are interested in particular topics. 

As well as the Bible there are many different commentaries and other texts available - it uses modules from the Sword project. There is a lot of good historic Christian literature available in this project.
[http://gnomesword.sourceforge.net/]("http://gnomesword.sourceforge.net/")
[http://www.crosswire.org/sword/index.jsp]("http://www.crosswire.org/sword/index.jsp")

If you want a good introduction to Christianity by a former atheist it is hard to go past Mere Christianity by CS Lewis. It should explain what Christianity is actually about and show you where spirituality fits in. I would recommend you buy a hard copy, but there is an ebook available from here (may be pirated):
[http://usminc.org/FLASHGIFS/MereChristianitybyCSLewis.pdf](http://usminc.org/FLASHGIFS/MereChristianitybyCSLewis.pdf)

There is a lot of rubbish on the net about spirituality, and all sorts of crazy beliefs. I recommend you check anything you read back to what the Bible actually says (which is why I recommend installing GnomeSword first). I'd also recommend you stick with some widely accepted authors - C.S. Lewis' books are great and easily read, C.H. Spurgeon's writings are very reliable - search and get some info on the author before believing everything they say (well, never believe everything - run it by the Bible first), remember the internet gives you access to great stuff and tonnes of rubbish.
