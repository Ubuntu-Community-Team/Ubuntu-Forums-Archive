Perhaps you are unaware that gnome-translate uses google's translation services as well as those of other companies?  The point of using gnome-translate is that it that integrates to the Ubuntu operating system user interface.  The stated objectives of the canonical business plan is to move toward an operating system that acts more as a coherent interface to the internet as a whole (kind of like a super "skin") rather than as a fully independent rig with a line to the net.  Fixing and improving gnome-translate helps move toward that goal.  Using a variety of different web services from unconnected sites moves away from it.

Besides, different services offer different translations.  Some services are better than others at certain tasks.  I would rather be able to access all of them through a consistent structure than to have to go to each website independently.  I would also rather not be beholden to Google, beneficent though they may be... for now.
