### Post by zmr on 2007-04-07
Maybe this does not relate to your situation at all, but presently Gnome Office's Abiword does not have great support for complex text layout (CTL; this is relevant to Indian languages and languages in other places in south and central Asia). Further, I have found Abiword to perform slow on large files (300 pgs).  Having said that, OO is not much faster, but does have good support for complex text. Note that the Abiword website says they are working on both issues (large files and CTL).

### Post by edsesq on 2007-04-07
I've used OOo for a while, and recently discovered Gnumeric, which I love!  It's graphing and data analysis capabilities are much more flexible for my purposes than in OO.  I'd go so far to say that it's not just an "Excel replacement" but a better program in an absolute sense.

  I suppose that an advantage to OpenOffice is the integration between the programs, as mentioned above.  While it's difficult to judge, I'd be inclined to say that OOo Calc is geared more toward the business world (a la MS Office/Excel) whereas Gnumeric offers more for scientists.

I do all my writing in LaTeX, so OOo Writer isn't much use to me except when other people send Word documents to me.  I have not yet tried Abiword, but am inclined to do so, given the sentiments expressed here.  How do the ".doc" importing capabilities of Abiword compare to those of OOo?  I've had the occasional problem with OOo where the formatting of a Word doc is nowhere near what it was intended to be (though this may be as much a word problem as anything).
