### Post by ctdahle on 2010-02-25
I would second the choice of Elluminate for anything requiring you to share dynamic content. I am in the middle of a graduate level analytical geometry class that is being presented in the Elluminate environment.

You can use a web cam to transmit pencil and paper drawings to other members of the class, you can also share documents from other programs like Geometer's Sketchpad, GeoGebra (opensource, Java based), or kiG (Linux based clone of Geometer's sketch pad).

You can deliver sets of prepared problems to your students, and the students can annotate or comment on each other's work. 

Elluminate has extensive chat capabilities as well as the ability to group students together who are working in different locations.

The class I am taking is lead by professors in Denver. The students are located in 5 or 6 different rural locations. I can choose to participate at work, a local cafe with a wireless hotspot, or at home where I have carrier pigeon based (i.e. less than lightning speed) dsl.

However, I think Moodle may do many, or even all of the same things
