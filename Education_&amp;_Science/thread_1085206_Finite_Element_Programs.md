### Post by Xnst on 2009-03-05
Hi,

I have tried some open packages and easily got them to run in ubuntu. CalculiX and Salome.

On a daily basis I use ABAQUS on ubuntu and it works as it was intended to. CalculiX have the great advantage to use the familiar abaqus input-file format. However, the pre and post processor needs some "getting used to" time. 

Salome-MECA with code-aster is promising. I have not really tried it, but it is up and running on my home computer. The pre and post precessor contain much more than cgx. If that is good, I do not know. I guess it is a matter of taste.

You can get Salome MECA at the download section on [http://caelinux.com]("http://caelinux.com") if you like to try it out.

Hope you got the answer you were looking for
