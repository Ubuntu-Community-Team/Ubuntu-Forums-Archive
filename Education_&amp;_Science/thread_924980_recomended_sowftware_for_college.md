### Post by Xnst on 2008-09-23
In mathematics classes you probably end up using matlab. Where I come from it is basically the standard software for that purpose. 
**Octave** is the easy way to matlab programming in linux. The syntax is basically the same and I do not know if the matlab student version exist for linux (It's a windose world, you know). Combine that with emacs and a bunch of compilers and latex you have basically all you need.

But in the end, the professors at your college will tell you what formats they expect the reports and assignments delivered in. Even though pdf:s are considered "good taste" some might want the reports in .doc format. They might want to run the code you just wrote which means text format and the right compiler. And so on. 

The good thing is: probably every thing you need is in the repositories, it is just to wait and see what you need :)

Have fun at collage!
