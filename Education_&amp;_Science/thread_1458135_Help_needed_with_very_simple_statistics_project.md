### Post by gunksta on 2010-04-19
Well, at least you've got some spunk.

There are three parts to this assignment.

1 - I am assuming that range and cross-range are provided by the teacher. In your spreadsheet, these are the colored columns. Don't over-think this. I think it is as simple as it sounds. I'm not sure what you are trying to accomplish in your mean and standard deviation columns, but I will tell you this. Your answers should be composed of two means and two standard deviations. You have way more than that. You seem to know how to use the tool to calculate the mean and standard deviation, but your slicing of the input columns doesn't make any sense to me.


2 - I don't think your graphs are what the prof wants. You forgot to include the PDF, which presumably tells you the location of the catapult in 2-D space. The catapult's location is really important, unless you were told to assume it is at 0,0. 

I think the prof wants you to plot the impact points using the range and cross-range data as x,y coordinates. (Note - I don't know which is supposed to be x and which is supposed to by y, since I've never seen the PDF). Don't even worry about #3 until you have this done.


3 - This is the best part. Reminds me of a particle dynamics teacher I had in college, although he would have used rockets and kittens, but I digress. What are the odds of hitting the square? Hint hint - odds

Now, if you get this knocked out and you want to blow your teacher's mind - throw some of this at 'em.

[http://www.cerebralmastication.com/2010/04/simulating-dart-throws-in-r-part-1-of-many/](http://www.cerebralmastication.com/2010/04/simulating-dart-throws-in-r-part-1-of-many/)
