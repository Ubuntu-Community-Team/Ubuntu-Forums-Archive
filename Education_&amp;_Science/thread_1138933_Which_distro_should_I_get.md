### Post by Greslore on 2009-04-26
I am not so sure it should be the distro you should focus on, but the amount of software available in the respective repositories.  For me, I like Ubuntu in this regard.  Most apps I need or want are just an apt-get install away as opposed to compiling them.

That being said, if you are going into computer science or are just curious to see what else is out there, by all means explore other distros as it can be a fun learning process.  After seeing what a bunch have to offer, you'll appreciate the more "robust" distros. I used to really like slackware, mandrake (mandriva now?), and suse.  It wouldn't be bad getting used to redhat (fedora or centos nowadays) either.

Take a look at: [http://distrowatch.com](http://distrowatch.com) ... They keep a close eye on all the latest developments with linux distros, and rate the popularity of them.
