### Post by Tony Flury on 2011-04-06
If you know the distance from B to C you can calculate the ratio of that distance as part of A to B, and apply the same ratio to the distances between the x,y and z co-ords of the end points.

Essentially if on a straight line you are a given fraction along that line - you are also the same fraction on your various axes to get the co-ordinates.
