### Post by mamid on 2009-09-24
I approached the principal earlier this month because the provincial government in their wisdom decided to cut school budgets and told him about Ubuntu and how my 2yo (she turns 3 in a couple of weeks) has been using GCompris and Child's Play all summer.  More importantly, that my 5yo and 7yo at his school were also using them and that the games scaled to age appropriateness.  I didn't show him Tuxmath at the time when I brought in my dual booter and did a demo.  My 7yo demoed GCompris right there in his office.

He's convinced.

The problem?  The IT department.  It "can't be on the network" if he sets up a box for the school and of course all the licensing issues.  But he wants it for a developmentally delayed child who he thinks it would help.  I'm tempted to loan him one of our desktops if it wasn't that hubby was too keen on getting them up and running with Ubuntu that he forgot to make sure flash and other stuff was installed (like a parental admin and making the kids' accounts desktop users only).

So I'm looking for links and information to make a presentation for the principal as to why free and open source software would be the better way to go.  There was a pallet of used iMacs and desktops that were, from what I understood, just going to be replaced.  I know if we were to put Ubuntu on them, they could be used for years longer.
