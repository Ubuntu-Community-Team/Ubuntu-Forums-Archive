### Post by hsweet on 2011-02-08
Hi.  I switched my high school lab ~5 years ago to Ubuntu/LTSP, still keeping working windows as needed.  It never got bigger than the one lab. I'll just answer your ques briefly.  

1. What made you consider switching formats in the first place? License fees, costly upgrades, etc.

Lots of reasons, the biggest was that the old, slow win machines worked much better as thin clients.  
#2 software costs and upgrade headaches disappeared.  
#3 much more resistant to kids buggering up the system.

Or faster, cheaper, more secure. 

2. Who introduced the idea of switching over? Was it a computer lab tech or a teacher or a concerned parent? Was there a presentation about pros and cons or just discussions?

It started as an individual project of the 2 tech teachers.  We presented it and had just enough support to continue, but not enough to expand past our lab.

3. What distros did you look at? Suse, Ubuntu, Uberstudent, etc. How well equipped was your school to make the switch? Do you use moodle, does each student have an email, etc.

Mostly Ubuntu.  Looked at Suse.  2 complex.  I set up a moodle, (amoodle.org) but after the first few years of LTSP.  


4. When you settled on a distro, did you customize it or just use it straight out of the box? Did you choose KDE or Gnome? What spurred these choices?

Some customizations, (custom firewall whitelist) but mostly Gnome Ubuntu out of the box.  Gnome/Ubuntu was nice and simple, kids learned their way around very quickly, good performance on old boxes.  the kids like to customize their desktops. 

5. What problems did you run into with the switch? Did staff and student productivity suffer initially and then level out or are people still struggling?

Switch was smooth.  Students pick up very quickly, and the only staff using it was us, and we are pretty knowledgeable.

6. Do you pay for technical support? Did you have to re train your entire staff?

No pay.  We solve our problems ourselves, sometimes with help from the forums.

7. Are you happy with the decision to switch? Would you recommend the switch to other schools?

Absolutely happy.  Yes

8. Has switching really lowered you costs? Initially and in the long term.

We are still using now 10 year old computers in the lab rather than replacing them as would have happened.

9.Having gone through the switch, is there anything you would change to make the process smoother?

Make sure you have good backups.  We screwed things up experimenting several times.  It would be nice to have a test lab to learn on.

10. Anything you would like to add
