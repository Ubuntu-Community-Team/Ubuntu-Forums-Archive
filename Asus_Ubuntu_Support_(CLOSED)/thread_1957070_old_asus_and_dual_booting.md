### Post by arubislander on 2012-04-12
Hi Pyridine,

Welcome to the forums.

As for your questions. 

1. The age of the laptop should have no effect on its ability to dual boot succesfully. In dual booting only one OS runs at a time, so once running they will not be aware of each other or use up extra CPU, memory or what not. 
What is important though is the amount of space you have on your hard drive. (More is better!) Ubuntu will be happy with as little as 10 GB, but XP might want some more..

2. You did not mention which version of the various programs you have (and I will assume you have the appropriate licenses for each.) I have Office 2007 running fine on my Acer netbook under Wine (Wine is a compatibility layer for Windows programs on Linux). But I have no experience with Photoshop or Fruity Loops. You might want to spend some time on [http://appdb.winehq.org/]("http://appdb.winehq.org/"). Enter the program you are looking to run in the search box and you will find information on how well it runs under Wine.
