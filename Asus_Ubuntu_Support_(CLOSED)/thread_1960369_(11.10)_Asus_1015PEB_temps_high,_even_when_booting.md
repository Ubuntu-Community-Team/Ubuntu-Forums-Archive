### Post by hetmes on 2012-04-17
Same old story, wondering if there's been news on this subject. Hadn't found a thread telling my story, so here goes.

My situation. I've had a **Asus 1015PEB** running Win 7 for well over a year now. No problems whatsoever, and I can say I know the netbook fairly well in terms of how it behaves. Tried **Ubuntu 11.10** through wubi.exe last weekend. After installation, noticed fan was blowing louder, even when doing nothing. Everything else, except the touch pad, which was a bit flaky, worked fine out of the box.

The kicker. I had set it up as dual boot, Win 7 and Ubuntu side by side. And even when booting into Win 7, the fan was now blowing louder than before! I uninstalled Ubuntu, and lo and behold, fan speed returned back to normal!! To clarify, I'm not looking for fan speed control. Ubuntu runs hot on my netbook, negating two major selling points of the netbook: quietness and, presumably, battery life.

The questions. Any settings I'm missing? Are there other distros that do run quietly on my netbook? Any chance this problem will be addressed for my netbook in the future?
