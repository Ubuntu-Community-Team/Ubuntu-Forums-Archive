### Post by Juliatan on 2012-05-11
While googling the same issue with my Asus 1201N I came acros this topic and after long searching  also the solution for my Eee-PC concerning getting Jupiter correctly installed with settings-Icon in Ubuntu 12.04 Precise. Installing via terminal didnt work (because of missing or not compatible ppa if I understood it well).
 

 At the next link you can download debian-packages of Jupiter and the EEE-extenion for Precise: [https://launchpad.net/~webupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+packages]("https://launchpad.net/%7Ewebupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+packages"). Ater download doubleclick the .deb-package and Ububtu Software opens the packages for installation. After installation reboot.
 

 Direct downloadlink Jupiter .deb : [https://launchpad.net/~webupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+files/jupiter_0.1.2-1%7Ewebupd8-1_all.deb](https://launchpad.net/~webupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+files/jupiter_0.1.2-1%7Ewebupd8-1_all.deb)
 Direct downloadlink for Eee-extension: [https://launchpad.net/~webupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+files/jupiter-support-eee_0.0.13-1%7Ewebupd8%7Eprecise1_all.deb](https://launchpad.net/~webupd8team/+archive/jupiter/+files/jupiter-support-eee_0.0.13-1%7Ewebupd8%7Eprecise1_all.deb)
 

 Jupiter works fine here now with systray-icon and also the Asus Super Hybride options. I run Ubuntu 12.04 64 bit. A little more battery-life and less hotter CPU.  
 

 Untill this moment however still not as good as Windows 7 HP 64 bit (without set in power saving mode in Asus Super Hybride Engine and with aero) on the same machine. Windows 7 is also faster in booting and working. Which I wouldn have expected from Ubuntu after a few years of not using Ubuntu.
 

 I still wonder if their is an updated version of Eee-control from Asus itself compatible with Precise which seems to have more options.   
 

 Good luck!

### Post by Linuxisfast on 2012-10-24
The first thing I did was to dump Win7 that came with my ASUS 1015-B netbook, even though I was afraid of warranty issues. For four days I tried my level best to get used to the slow boot, slower shutdown, general lazy slow response of the OS for application launch and performance. I tuned Win 7 to the best of its abilities by defragging with Perfect Disk, using a lighter anti virus like Avira etc but to no avail. Finally was left with a choice, return the netbook within its seven day return period and exchange for a Android tablet like ASUS Transformer or as a last try, install Ubuntu, I chose the later and the netbook is still with me. Jupiter and its eeepc engine made life better for it and now I get battery life close to Win 7, I used 12.04 and now have 12.10 which boot blazingly fast and even with Unity enabled and AMD Catalyst, HDMI movies run smooth.
