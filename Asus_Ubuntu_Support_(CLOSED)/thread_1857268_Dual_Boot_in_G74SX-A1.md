Someone may have come up with a way to load Ubuntu on NTFS formatted drives, but I certainly wouldn't do it that way, even assuming someone has (which I don't think they have, because I don't think it would work at all).

Instead, what I would do - and what I plan to do when I get my IRS refund next March - is dedicate one drive to Windows, and one drive to Ubuntu. The Ubuntu drive will be reformatted to Ubuntu's native format - which will mean Windows won't be able to access the drive. Not a problem, as I'll be spending most of my time in Ubuntu, which can easily see and mount the Windows drive. If I want to do something - say tweak a photo in Photoshop - in Windows, I just have to copy the file over to my Windows drive, then reboot into Windows.
