### Post by stuartb on 2006-09-04
Hello,

I have recently installed Ubuntu and have been very impressed thus far...

Especially with the Wireless support... You guys have done a grand job.

Now I have been playing around with a dual hard disk boot from Fedora5 which I use for work purposes.  That uses ethernet ICS to a windows pc... So that means my Ubuntu server had both Ethernet installed & Wireless.

Today I noticed that I was getting terrible download speeds, ifconfig reported many errors, and had dropped thousands of packets... strange.  I read on another post that a guy disabled his ethernet and the problem was solved.

I can confirm that when I disabled my onboard LAN adapter the problem with wireless has vanished.  I am downloading at full 250kb/sec, rather than 2.5!  Also many problems with dropping off,etc, sorted.

Note: it is not enough just to disable the ethernet in Gnome... you must physically remove/disable it, in my experience.

Hope this helps some frustrated people.
