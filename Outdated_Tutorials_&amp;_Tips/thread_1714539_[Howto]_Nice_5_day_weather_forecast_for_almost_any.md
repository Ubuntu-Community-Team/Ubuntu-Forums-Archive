### Post by apochry on 2011-03-25
I've always  wanted do have a weather forecast that's only one click away and was unhappy that the Weather Report application in Gnome's panel doesn't provide any for my city. Even if it was provided, I don't think I would use it, because of the way it's presented... plain text.

After several hours wandering in the forums I have found a way to this I was pretty satisfied with the result, so I decided to share it with whoever would like to do the same.

This is the result, if you like it may proceed reading this thread:

 [IMG]http://img405.imageshack.us/img405/4504/weatherforecast.png[/IMG]

Basically there are tree things you need to do to get this forecast in your Weather Report:

**1. ****Add Weather Report** to your panel by right-clicking the panel --> **Add to panel** --> **Weather Report** --> **Add**. [IMG]http://img849.imageshack.us/img849/1931/wrinpanel.png[/IMG]

**2. ****Go to **[_**foreca.com**_]("http://www.foreca.com/"), search and find your city/town/village, locate the big **'Detailed 5 day forecast'**-graph in the middle of the page and **copy its URL**.

**3. **Right-click the **Weather Report** in the panel --> **Preferences** --> Check the **'Enable radar map'**-box --> Check the **'Use custom address for radar map'**-box --> **Paste** the copied URL in the field below --> **Close**


That's it. Now click the Weather Report, hit 'Update' and you should see the forecast in the 'Radar Map'-tab.
