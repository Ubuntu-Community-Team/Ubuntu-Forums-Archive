### Post by Periswell on 2008-04-24
dear ubuntu users

ever looked at website like gnome-look.org at the GDM login themes and there is nothing there for you? if so (like me) you might like to make your own but how? well there is a easy way wit no scripting just simple configuring. so if interested read on. lets begin....

its always easy to start of with a already made theme it saves a lot of hassle. so go to [http://www.gnome-look.org/](http://www.gnome-look.org/) , then to GDM themes and just download the first on in sight.

now what you will get is a .zip or a .tar.gz. extract the whole of that folder to somewhere (i dont care just choice one like desktop or documents).(screenshot 1).

Next you locate yourself into the extracted folder and you will find an average of 16 files. first what we are going to change is the background.

get a picture of your choice (dont copyright infringe). make sure that it is of good size. not small as when it is expanded to your monitor size it will look rubbish. Put the picture in the extracted folder and open it (i like gthumb as you can change the size if needed). goto file>save as and then click on the original background image of your downloaded GDM theme. (screenshot 2) click yes to over-wright and youve done the background.

Now would be a good time to do the screenshot. so before you close the program save as again and this time over-wright the picture screenshot.

After this it is time to change some properties. Right click on the .desktop file and go to properties. next go to the permissions tab and click make excutible. (screenshot 3).

Click on the icon that now is a blue diamond with gears in then click display when ask to. change the name to what you want to call it. change the artwork to your name and author to your name. then save and close.

no to make it an archive. right click on the folder you've just been modifying and click make archive. save it as you want and your done.

Goto system> administration> login window and click on the tab local. drag your newly made archive into the box with all the themes and allow it to be installed. after that test it and if alls well you are done. if you have any problems about the appearence then go into you folder and go onto the .xml file. just modify the bits that you want and you are done.

hope you enjoyed this tutorial.

-josh
