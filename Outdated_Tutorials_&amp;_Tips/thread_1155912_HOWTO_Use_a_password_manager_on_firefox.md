### Post by Washer on 2009-05-11
**Intro:**
Too many people use the same or similar passwords on multiple sites. This tutorial gets you using the firefox extension [Passwordmaker]("http://passwordmaker.org/") for unique & strong passwords. It's a tricky & complex app so suggestions/improvements are quite welcome.

Will protect you from
Hacked sites revealing all your passwords
Keyloggers, sort of. They'll get your master password but may not know how to use it.

Won't protect you from
password reset attacks that use your city of birth etc



**Steps:**
Install [passwordmaker]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/469").

After restarting firefox, you notice the ring at the bottom right. Click it & play with that window a bit to get used to it. Choose a master password & see how the generated password changes when you use a different domain. Look around the advanced options. Under global settings, I recommend turning off "confirm twice" and switching coolkey to "populate empty fields"

Right-click customize your main firefox toolbar to add the golden key "PwrdMkr CoolKey" icon. The big ring isn't necessary, but it looks pretty.

To get used to this app, I recommend that you change one of your passwords. For this howto, we'll be changing the [gmail password]("http://mail.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?hl=en&answer=6567"). 

Login & run through those steps to change it. When it comes to setting the new password, go ahead generate it & use the "Copy generated password to clipboard." Make sure it is correct.

Logout. To log back in, type your username, select the password field (gmail is a bit anal about that), click the golden key, enter your master password & done.

Go back over the minor settings to see that everything works for you.


[B]
Notes:[/B]
Autofill user name is there & it *mostly* works. Go to advanced options, accounts, default, right-click settings, advanced auto populate. Click the field on the webpage & give it a value. This is fine for most purposes.

Should you save the master password? Your choice, I suppose. It's [trading security for convenience]("http://passwordmaker.org/Firefox/Mozilla/SeaMonkey/Flock/Netscape/Tips&Tricks#Super_Security_Tip").

Some sites don't allow weird characters? I think you can change the allowed chars somewhere in the default account settings.

This app ties you to using firefox and passwordmaker. I recommend using portable firefox when moving around but I hear you can also use their webpage.
