### Post by slux on 2005-05-15
Don't se a thread about this here so I'll list the ones I know:

[Linuxgames](http://www.linuxgames.com/)
The longest running Linux gaming news website I know of, part of the TeleFragged network. A great place to keep up on gaming news.

[Linux-gamers.net](http://www.linux-gamers.net/)
A relatively recent addition that has survived unlike the ill-fated Linuxgaming.net. They offer forums, Linux clans and news. If you're in Germany you might be interested in the Linux gamer LAN's they organize as well.

[The Linux Game Tome](http://www.happypenguin.org/)
A catalogue of GNU/Linux games, both proprietary and free complete with ratings. This one's been running even longer than Linuxgames, I think. They have relatively active forums as well.

[Icculus](http://www.icculus.org/)
The home of several open source gaming projects and .plans of some significant people such as the man responsible for many GNU/Linux ports of major commercial titles, Ryan "Icculus" Gordon.

[Holarse](http://www.holarse.de/)
For those fortunate enough to speak german, this is yet another good gaming news site complete with HOWTOs, reviews, etc.

[Linux Installers For Linux Games](http://liflg.org/)
They offer installers for native, Wine-run, free and proprietary games and mods.

[Linux Game Publishing](http://www.linuxgamepublishing.com/)
The major porting company currently porting exclusively for Linux. Tries to fill the shoes of the former Loki games but with a viable plan for survival. They port mostly good quality non-AAA titles.

[TransGaming](http://www.transgaming.com/)
The developers of the well-known WineX software based on WINE and designed to run Windows games on Linux.

And one not strictly gaming related but of interest to Linux gamers:
|URL=http://www.linuxhardware.org/]LinuxHardware[/URL] hardware news and benchmark website focusing on Linux. The place to get information on processor and graphics card performance. ([Anandtech](http://www.anandtech.com/) is competing with their recently started Linux section though).

Feel free to add to the list if I missed anything important. These are the sites I've followed for years now to stay up on current Linux gaming news. :)
