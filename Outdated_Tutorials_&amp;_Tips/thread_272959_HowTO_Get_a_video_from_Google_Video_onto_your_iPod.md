### Post by Old Pink on 2006-10-07
Hello,

How many times have you watched something on Google Video, and wished you could show your workmates the next day, or wished you could watch the rest elsewhere? 

Google video is a great place for not only short, hilarious clips, but older movies and cartoons past their copyright expiration date. 

For this example, I'll use a short (7 minute) cartoon I uploaded. The copyrights have expired, as the cartoon is very old, and it's 100% free for public distribution, whilst still being very funny.

Used in this example: [http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-6672877891396611672](http://video.google.com/videoplay?docid=-6672877891396611672)

_**Steps for downloading**_[LIST=1]
[*]Head over to Google Video, and find the video you want to download. Or, use the example URL above.
[*]Check for a download option on the left. If available, change to iPod format and click Download. If not available, the uploader has disabled it, and you can't download it, sorry.[/LIST][U][B]Steps for getting it on your iPod
[/B][/U][LIST=1]
[*]Open gtkpod (don't have it? put **sudo apt-get install** **gtkpod **in a terminal)
[*]Plug in your iPod, and click "Read" - your files will be read off the iPod.
[*]Click "+ files" and select your video
[*]Scroll around in the list below, and find where your video has been added. Edit the information to the title etc. you desire.
[*]Click "Sync"
[*]Wait for your iPod to recieve the new file. On USB 2.0 it takes around 10 seconds, I have no experience with 1.1/1.0.
[*]Eject your iPod, watch the movie![/LIST]Hope that all works out OK! Remember, not all videos are available, as they're not technically supposed to be available anyway, but the vast majority are.
