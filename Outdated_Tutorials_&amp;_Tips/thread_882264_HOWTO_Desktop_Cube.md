### Post by PCessna on 2008-08-06
Hey all, out of all the little troubles I've gone through to get a little cube to work, I'd thought I'd right a tutorial, not just to enable, but to get the full entire effect out of most things possible with the Ubuntu 8.04 desktop cube.

This tutorial may either be the same, or similar in older versions of Ubuntu, so keep that in mind.
[B]
Getting started.[/B]

This will obviously require that you have desktop effects on the highest settings, which requires your NVIDIA, etc., video drivers are in perfect working condition with Ubuntu, so if you can't get past this step yet, look around the ubuntu forums. I recommend Envy which I use.
[B]
Step 1: Getting it all up and running[/B]

Before we can mess with cube, will need to make some minor configuration changes.

1.) Right click workspace switcher on top menu, if you dont have it, add it to the top or bottom menu, atleast for now, right click it, if you still had 2 like me, where gonna need to change it, 1 you can do anything, 2 you get a flat 3D object, 3 workspaces and triangle, and 4 workspaces, our goal, the cube. Now if you want more workspaces, from 4 and on, you'll get all the effects of a cub, but the space will keep changing to fit all the spaces, and it doesnt give you the cool cube shape, my suggestion is that if you want more then 4, not to go past 8.

2.) Go to System, Preferences, Advanced Desktop Effects Settings 
Now if you don't have this little manager app yet, heres how to get it.
Applications --> Add/ Remove Programs --> Search for 'Compiz' and select Advanced Desktop Effects Settings (ccsm)
2b.) Check the following
_DESKTOP_
Desktop Cube (Required)
Rotate cube (optional)
_EFFECTS (ALL OPTIONAL)_
3D windows (optional)
Cube Reflection (optional)
Cube Gears (optional)
_Utility_
Cube Caps [Recommenced]

**Step 2 The first simple commands:**
Now that we have it all up and running, here's a couple commands and such to get you going.
CTRL + ALT, and then hold these town with one hand, and with the other, drag your mouse out, in, and around, having done the proper steps above, you should see a YouTube hit, your dream, and my pleasure. A CUBE :D
CTRL + ALT + Left or Right switch workspaces.

Hope those quick starters helped!

**Step 3: Customizing**
Here comes the cool part where I show you the gist of making the Ubuntu Desktop Cube your own Cube :D :3

Now we'll start in Desktop, Desktop Cube, click this from the advanced Desktop settings manager and now here's the start of personalization! 

Now select the color of the cube, I don't see this really barley does ANYTHING at all, so don't get excited, likely only if you didn't enable cube caps like I did, and of course the image for the caps, or top and bottom, which if you enable cup caps, you can simply go in and choose your own images for both the top AND the bottom.

Under behavior choose some little performance tweaks for your cube, click the key-hole looking like bottom all at the right to reset if you made a mistake you don't like D:

Under transparent, choose how transparent you wish your cube to be, when not being moved, and when be moved in shortest words or terms.

Under rotate cube, some small tweaks that you can make, very small part of the cube experience. 

To effects, 3D window which, you really have to experience it yourself, makes the Windows 3Dish, since it's hard to explain, give it a try, and tweak it around, theres always a reset button for each setting.

Cube reflection is a small little feature compared to the whole cube experience, speaks for itself, another tweaker to be tweaked and reset if needed. 

Cube gears is only visible when transparency is high or at least not 'disabled', nothing special, You may or may not want this, with high transparency it may annoy you, mostly when on the desktop, your choice. 

Cube caps as stated above lets you choose your own images for the top and bottom.
Under appearance, find where to select your own images on your system, there are two by default of compiz. 

Feel free to mess around yourself, I'll try my best to add on more and more as I discover more

Hope this helped
