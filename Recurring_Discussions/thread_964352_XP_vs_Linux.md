### Post by POWMS on 2008-10-30
When I got home last night I needed to burn the Red Bull 07 movie for Will, my son to take to school today.
I thought a 5 to 10 minute operation would be adequate.
I started the comp, got distracted by the kids and the dual boot defaulted to Windows, so that started before I could intervene.
Oh well, that’s O.K. I’m just burning a cd.
As this is a fresh install of XP (after a major crash) I decided to load the Logitech mouse driver as the default Windows one is total crap.
Easy job. Insert the disc, load the mouse driver. Install hangs at 99%.
I think, Oh crap, what’s going on now?
I go away and make a cuppa.
Come back, still at 99%.
I can’t close the dialogue box, Windows has now stalled and is frozen.
I’ll just use Task Manager to stop the install.
This doesn’t work. Windows is truly frozen.
A few attempts at Start, Shut down fails, I hit the OFF button.
This works.
Its 15 minutes into the cd burn project now.
I restart Windows, go to Control Panel to uninstall the Logitech mouse driver.
Uninstall fails as the folder is corrupt, says Windows.
I try a manual delete. Some files remain as ‘they are in use by another application’
I restart Windows.
I try another delete, but that fails. I think I will retry to reinstall the mouse driver as this may solve the problem.
I start the install, and at 99% the Installation Wizard has a problem and stops. Do I want to tell MS?
No, as I think they have enuff problems to fix.
I restart Windows.
I delete as many Logitech files as it will allow. I check for large temp files left after an app failure and delete them.
Now 30 mins into the cd burn project.
Oh well, I’ll just use the crappy default mouse as it is.
Hmmmm, I can’t find a Windows default CD/DVD burning program. Where is my Nero disc?
I don’t think I will install this as I don’t want to spend another ½ hr trying to fix Install Wizard.
I give up and re boot onto Linux.
I open the pre-installed CD/DVD burning app. (better than Nero)
Load a blank CD, pick the file to burn.
Burn and verify the file, about 30 secs.
Shut down the comp.
Hmmmmmmm I wonder why there is a mass exodus away from MS?????????
Just my rantings for today..........
