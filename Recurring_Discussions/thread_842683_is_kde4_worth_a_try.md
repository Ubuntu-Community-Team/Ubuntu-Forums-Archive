### Post by Rick Myers on 2008-06-27
On my systems, yes it's slower. It's not that much different than KDE3.5 in terms of configuration. I must agree with damis648 in that it's not very intuitive considering moving from 3.5 to 4 makes you search for settings.

Very slick though. Try it for a glimpse into the future. DO NOT use the KDE4 KDM or you'll find some bugs. Go into Synaptic and search for kubuntu. Find the kubuntu-kde4-desktop version and run the install. Do not install any other goodies until you've determined you want to keep KDE4. Leave GDM as your default display manager. Once installed login to a KDE4 session and play. If you don't like, uninstall it.

### Post by benerivo on 2008-06-27
KDE4 is a slowly, but definitely ever improving desktop. The beta of 4.1 is worth a try if you can afford to mess around with your current setup. In terms of performance there's nothing special about it. There seems to be a 'bigger idea' behind it which i take to be how it deals with the graphical disaply and interaction of locations and files - to be more specific, how your monitor is used as a display of folder locations + optional widgets (the plasma technology) and how your files are managed (the metadata of the [nepomuk technology]("http://nepomuk.kde.org/")). This may not be very useful to many people right now, who enjoy the unambigousness of the gnome desktop. Personally i admire the braveness of the kde people who have pushed their technology to try to extend what we do with our computers, but i think they may have gone off at a tangent for the moment.
