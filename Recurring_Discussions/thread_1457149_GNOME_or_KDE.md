### Post by cespinal on 2010-04-20
Okay here I go. 

I have been Gnome user for the past 18 months, and then switched to KDE for the last 6. 
At first glance, I believe gnome can be less intimidating, since its interfaces look simpler and everything looks quite easy to use. A lot of recurring discussions between ''simplicity and usability'' have taken place around gnome a kde, when in reality, you could make both environments as simple or feature rich as you want. And I really want to stress this last idea, because its really up to you. 

However, I have seen HUGE improvements in KDE in terms of usability and customization that, to my extent, finally made it break away from any comparisons that could have made in the recent past. Nowadays, I keep saying that gnome is simple and easy to use, but KDE is now quite a bunch of miles ahead in terms of flexibility. 

First, lets have a look at KDE-only features that REALLY improve usability over gnome. 
1)Automatic window rezising: drag a window to a lateral desktop edge and the window rezises to occupy one half of the screen. Do it with the upper edge and the window maximixes. Simple but super intutive and effective for reviewing documents. 
2)Desktop grid: similar to compiz's expo, but in this case, once its activated, all the windows within each desktop becomes rezised to there are not any overlapped windows hiding from you. Just think about a marriage between expo and gnome shell. Once you get used to this feature you will no longer need task managers, docks or window lists to manage your windows. 
3) Window tabbing. The same way you tab in chrome, you can do it in KDE with any application. Its super efficient when you can just gather 6 or 8 PDFs in only one window while keeping a cleaner desktop. 

Now, the cons. 

KDE, although its pretty stable, continues to have some bugs, which will remind you at least once every other day, that gnome is still a more solid environment. Plasma, Krunner, Kopete and Kmail are usual players in this, hanging when you close them or when you force the system a little too much.

As an extra plus. KDE will give you a blast if you are up to customizing your desktop.

In my own experience: I have made my KDE into a simpleton that looks more like openbox than any other thing. Which makes it even simpler than gnome. Fun thing is that KDE itself has the GUIS and menus to let me achieve this. 

Hope this helps.
