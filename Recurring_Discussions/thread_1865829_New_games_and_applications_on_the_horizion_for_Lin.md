### Post by KoopaTroopa on 2011-10-20
Okay.

So there's been lots to get excited about of late to do with Linux. Valve the company that makes the source engine and steam have confirmed that Linux support is on the agenda (although no actual release date has been set). So it's safe to assume all of their own games such has team fortress, half life and counter strike will be rolling out when Linux support does. Unity another popular game engine has also confirmed their going to support compiling games made with their unity game SDK for Linux and finally Google's developing their native client (NaCl) that intends to run high end software in the browser that will also support Linux (no doubt to aid sales for their new Chrome OS).

Speaking of the Chrome OS why all of a sudden has everyone started taking a shine to Linux? Lord knows everyone's ignored the penguin for quite some time. Could it be that everyone thinks whatever Google touches turns to gold and wants a piece of the market? Whatever the reason the future seems promising.
