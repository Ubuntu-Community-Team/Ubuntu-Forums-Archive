### Post by CherylMPaine on 2009-11-02
First off - no offense to anyone.  I love my Ubuntu - works for me.  But I also like my Windows, my Mac, and my Atari 800XL.  :D

Ubuntu may mean "Humanity to others" and be the "Linux for Human Beings" ... but it has one major issue that just won't allow it to break the Micro$oft/Macinto$h barrier and will almost certainly relegate it to Second Class status for a long time.

Hardware support.

Look - we've been fussing with the stupid Lucent or Agere 56K integrated laptop WinModems for longer than I care to remember to get dialup and faxing.  Linmodem, Martian, yada yada yada - all bandaids and not 100% solutions.  (If I have to go to terminal and SUDO my modem device to fire it up for use, not have it there at boot like it should be - that ain't a full solution).  No one has gotten IBM ThinkPad On Screen Displays to work right (or at least load at boot).  And WebCam video and WebCam integrated microphones seem to be more of a mystery to us than it is to a Windows coder. There are some solutions, but most have no instructions how to make them work and the instructions people post on forums are usually incomplete, convoluted, take too much into account about the Linux experience a user may (or may not) have, or are just outright wrong.

To give Micro$oft and Macinto$h credit - they truly are operating systems for Human Beings.  You buy a webcam, load its install disk, and voila - you can talk to Grandma on Skype in under 3 minutes.  But you pay for all this immediate functional convenience.  And you pay a lot.

In Ubuntu - after two hours ... you realize your QuickCam's integrated microphone won't work with Ubunto (so back to plugging in a headset microphone), you have to downgrade video libraries to get video to work - but at a crappy resolution, the Ubuntu Skype version is not for your build of Ubuntu and the Deb package is way too old.  And your Grandma on Windows XP got tired of waiting around for you to call - so she went out to play Bingo and see the Chippendales.

Ubuntu community can't just keep acknowledging the existence of hardware, but claiming that the hardware won't work.  Like the Agere WinModem.  Just saying "it won't do anything with Ubuntu, go buy a USB US ROBOTICS or ROSEWILL 56K REAL modem" isn't good.  People use Ubuntu because they can't afford Micro$oft or Macinto$h anymore - what makes you think they have $56 in spare pennies to go out and buy a new modem just for Ubuntu when the existing Agere Winmodem worked years without issue in Windows.

The bottom line is - Ubuntu as an OS may be free.  But since it's hardware support is fairly specific (due to hardware mfgs writing specifically for Micro$oft and Macinto$h - not making code open source) - it's not all that "free" when you have to buy things like modems, webcams, WiFi/NIC cards all over again because the ones you had don't play with Ubuntu.

This (I know) has turned a lot of people away from Ubuntu - because in many cases it doesn't "just work" ... my Granny isn't a techie.  My parents could care less about open source and the entire "Ubuntu World Community."   My little sister just wants to be happy and play her Barbie computer games.  None of them want or will fiddle with a temperamental Ubuntu that won't come out of hibernation state on a laptop.  If you have to do that all the time ... in their eyes ... Ubuntu is a half-baked potato and waste of their time.  They'll willing fork cash over for Micro$oft and Macinto$h which actually do "just work" with stuff they can buy off Walmart's shelves.  Saying "will be addressed in the next Ubuntu 11.4 Ludacris Lemur" just won't cut it with anyone.

Hey - it's like the old Betamax and VHS days.  I liked BetaMax - better quality.  But it could never topple VHS.  BetaMax had too many limitations and not enough hardware support.  Same here with Ubuntu.

Ubuntu is fun to play with, but never consider it FREE.  It's not free.  You spend a lot of your time researching issues and buying compatible hardware for it.  So if you want to play with Ubuntu, you better be prepared to "pay to play" as well.  

Just my opinion.  Feel free to screech, hollar, yell, and beat your chest in rage.  I'm using Ubuntu 9.10 now and have come to live without an internal Agere Modem (got a US ROBOTICS 56K for faxing) or fully functional webcam.  I realize this is all volunteer, pro bono development too. I know why a lot of device support doesn't exist currently.  Does everything else I need.  If I need a webcam, I use my Windows XP laptop.  But I like to play and often clear off my hard drives and load fresh OS every few months.  Most people, don't.  I'd sleep with Ubuntu if I could!  Well - I kinda do now - I fall asleep with my laptop on the couch.

:p
