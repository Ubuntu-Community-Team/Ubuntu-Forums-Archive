### Post by CarpKing on 2011-03-14
One thing that both shells seem to miss is the importance of backwards compatibility in interfaces.  Even if you rewrite your project from scratch with a completely new interface model in mind, the changes can be much less disruptive if the old way of working is still available seamlessly with the new.  For instance, maybe you'd like a task list on your panel.  Can you run gnome-panel at the same time as gnome-shell?  Even if you can, you still can't combine it with that bar at the top of the screen.

No matter how technologically disruptive a change is, with good planning a project can keep the benefits of gradual change by adding new ways to work while keeping old ones relatively intact.
