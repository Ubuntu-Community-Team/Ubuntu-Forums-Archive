Other than the kde vs gnome aspect there are a few applications that are specific to KDE such as Amarok and K3b. But you can install kde apps on a gnome desktop and vice versa. Use the DE that you like best.

Some people will argue that pure KDE or pure Gnome is faster or less bloated. While this is true you can safely run a "mixed" desktop unless you are extremely short on disk space. In fact most people I know run a mixture. Thats the beauty of Linux, choice :)
