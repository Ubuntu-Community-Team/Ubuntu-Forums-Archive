### Post by rg4w on 2012-01-11
Advertising is most effective for mass audiences, but mass audiences don't care about OSes, they only care about computers, and when they buy a computer it already comes with an OS.

The very notion that you can replace your computer's OS with another one is completely alien to the average person.

So more than adverstising, Ubuntu needs to be bundled on more computers.

Fortunately, if you read some of the Ubuntu blogs you'll find that Canonical has successfully gained several new OEM bundling agreements in 2011, with plans for more in 2012.

It's a slow process to bring OEMs on board, but one model at a time Ubuntu will eventually take over the world.  Or at least 10% of it. :)

The people who will pull in revenue for Canonical, through service contracts or other such deals are the very people who know what Linux is (and have known for many years), and for whom any sort of advertisement would be a complete waste of time and money

 - Corporate IT depts.
 - Government IT projects

Agreed.

To clarify my point above, it's not that advertising to the masses is pointless, merely that advertising an alternative to the OS they already have on their computer is pointless.

Far more effective to sell the whole solution:  advertise computers that ship with Ubuntu.

And that seems to be where Canonical is focusing a lot of its effort, toward evangelizing the OS as suitable for preinstallation, where consumers can get a whole solution, Ubuntu+computer.

Outside the US we're seeing more vendors like Asus doing this, and with any luck we'll see this in the States some day too.

Props to System76 and ZaReason, but they don't have the kind of marketing budget the big OEMs have.

As more and more OEMs become further disenchanted with doing business with Microsoft and thus begin looking in earnest for alternatives, and as they at long last consider the possibility of differentiating themselves from other Windows-based PC vendors, I suspect we'll see ever more of them putting Ubuntu on their boxes.

These sorts of relationships take time.  Patience, Grasshopper.  The revolution is coming, quietly....
