All Debian-based distros use the same .deb package managment (dpkg, apt-get, aptitude, etc).

Other distros use different package management; for example Fedora uses .rpm (yum).

All of this information is readily available at [sites like distrowatch.com]("http://distrowatch.com/dwres.php?resource=major"). :)

### Post by handy on 2011-02-27
I expect that Xfce must be continually gaining more users, users who want a lighter desktop than the other two & who may not like the way that the other two have & are changing.

The most recent release of Xfce has made menu handling much easier now & has simplified other aspects of configuration. It is still fast, modular & configurable.

The modularity is great, I use Openbox with the xfce4-panel & about 4 plugins.

Anyway, I've just thrown the Xfce bit in for the OP to think about. A distro that uses Xfce as the desktop might suit you. I used Mint Xfce some time back & it still felt fairly light even though Ubuntu was underneath.

Prior to running Mint Xfce I gave CrunchBang (uses Openbox) a shot, it had problems at the time so I didn't stay with it for long. That was well over a year ago now, so I'm sure CrunchBang has come a long way since then.
