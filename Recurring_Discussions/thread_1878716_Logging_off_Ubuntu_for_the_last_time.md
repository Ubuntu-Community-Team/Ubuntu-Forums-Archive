### Post by TBABill on 2011-11-10
I took the route of, "Ok, Gnome 3 is here and it's the future for Gnome, like it or not." So I installed Ubuntu, Lubuntu Desktop, Xubuntu desktop and Kubuntu desktop. I use each for a period, then log out and log into something else when the boredom or frustration mounts. I've found I really do like all of the DE's for different reasons so I'll continue to switch between until my efficiency and happiness point me to the most logical choice for my own needs. 
 
Right now that path is unclear and I find likes and dislikes in each DE. Maybe that's the distro hopper urge in me rather than something logical. But now I just distro hop within the Buntus and switch at will. I think LXDE or Xfce will win out in the end, but I have not committed to anything yet.
 
Summary: I feel your pain along with everyone else. The problem is Gnome 3 is all we'll have to pick from eventually on the Gnome side from any distro and the players on the dev side are measuring and reacting to risks by choosing the paths for their distros during this transition period. Seems like a big deal now, but not too far from now it'll be similar to the KDE 3 to 4 evolution that most people no longer care about.
