### Post by beew on 2011-08-02
I think mpayer2 is way better than VLC in terms of performance if you have a Nvidia card or a multicore machine . Problems with VLC: uses only one core and doesn't make full use of hardware acceleration (vdpau, vaapi) so unless you have a powerful single core cpu high definition videos are often unwatchable in VLC but mplayer2 or mplayer handles them just fine. With mplayer2 and mplayer you can use different GUI frontends, smplayer, gnome-mplayer, umplayer and kmplayer (for KDE), to name a few. Also mplayer's firefox plugin (gecko-mediaplayer) is amazing whereas VLC's plugin rarely even works.

But VLC does have some cool options like supporting dvd menus! I am not sure why this seems to be such a huge problem for Linux though. Also its build-in codecs are quite amazing. I have encountered some odd videos that I can't play in any Linux player (or Windows player for that matter) but VLC.
