### Post by fuduntu on 2011-01-11
> **Ovocean said:**
> What you people call a "politic" attached to GNU is only about defending your own freedom. One can disagree with the radical position of Stallman but you can't really be against defending your freedom unless you're a masochist.

The problem with your statement is that it is not your business to define (or in the case of GNU, redefine) software freedom.

The advantages of free software, by RMS
[http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/practical.html](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/practical.html)

[quote=RMS]Nonfree software is bad because it denies your freedom. Thus, asking about the practical advantages of free software is like asking about the practical advantages of not being handcuffed. Indeed, it has advantages:
[/quote]

Some non free software restricts some end user freedoms, but the polar opposite is to restrict the freedom of the entity that creates software, thus creating a paradox.

[quote=RMS]
You can wear an ordinary shirt.
You can pitch a baseball.
You can carry a backpack.
[/quote]

What nonsense.  To put it simply, RMS wants to take the make believe handcuffs and move them from the user (who wasn't wearing them, because they **choose** to purchase software), and place them on software developers forcing them into his vision of software freedom.

[quote=RMS]
We could find more, but do you need these advantages to convince you to reject handcuffs? Probably not, because you understand that your freedom is what's at stake.
[/quote]

RMS tells a nice story of doom and gloom, just accept god^H^H^HRMS into your heart, and all is forgiven^H^H^H^H^H^H^H^Hgain software freedom.

[quote=RMS]
