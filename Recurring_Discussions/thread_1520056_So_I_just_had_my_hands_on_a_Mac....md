Customizable Icon Docks were in CAD systems since before Mac or Windows.  

Some folk think that mouse driven GUI's started with operating systems first.  Nope.  They got their clue from applications.  Many apps were GUI running under text-based O/S's like Unix, CP/M, or DOS.  

At first they put the "dock" on the digitizer face.  You'd make an overlay and customize it through the software.  Then they put the icons on the screen themselves, as monitors got enough resolution to make that work.  You could pick and choose what Icons you wanted in any case.

20+ years ago:

Icons display themselves and order themselves based on use.  Stuff you don't use disappears.  You use it again, it reappears. 
Multiple Icon Bars for different tasks.  Click to switch trays.  
Open a file, and the icons are targeted for that file.

Now, even today, keyboard instructions are FASTER to use than all the hundreds of different Icon Trays that have been used for CAD over the decades.

Most software innovation doesn't occur in a divine vision.  Most is a natural progression of existing  apps.
