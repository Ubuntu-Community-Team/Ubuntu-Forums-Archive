### Post by rg4w on 2012-10-29
> **vibaviattigala said:**
> ...i think the reason because Microsoft windows has more market share they have hired engineers designers etc  .thats why we can see some nice interface compared to our linux but i know someday we can achieve that level with our contributors etc so long live linux :)
Personally, I think Linux us there now, at least with Ubuntu.

Win7's Aero UI is, IMNSHO, noisy.  The translucence helps show off what the engineers could do but provides no usability benefit, reducing the visual distinction between the foreground window and everything behind it.  Then consider the dizzying array of controls all over the screen, and the whole gestalt of the UI is just a mess.

Compare to the simplicity of Unity.   While far from perfect, Unity is IMO a far more engaging UI, more accessible, clearer, and cleaner.

One thing the Ubuntu community could benefit from is the same level of attention given to UI details in apps as there is in the desktop itself.  With all due respect to third-party devs, many free projects were made to scratch an itch, and tend to reflect that in the fit-and-finish, or lack thereof, in the UI.

Canonical recognizes that ultimately any OS is just an app launcher; the true scope of meaningful work one does with any OS is defined by the apps available for the platform.

Accordingly, with the Q cycle and forward Canonical has put significant effort into providing resources to improve both the number and quality of apps for Ubuntu.

The new developer site at [http://developer.ubuntu.com](http://developer.ubuntu.com) is just the beginning.  They've also hired a new UI person to draft a set of Human Interface Guidelines for the platform, so devs can provide a more fluidly consistent experience for users that better integrates with the OS. 

Canonical also realizes that to attract ports of quality apps to Ubuntu will require that the number of users increases to provide a positive ROI for developers, and have secured agreements with vendors around the world such that in 2013 some 5% of all PCs sold will have Ubuntu preinstalled:
[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=MTA5ODM](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=news_item&px=MTA5ODM)

Collectively, these efforts suggest an increasingly great experience for Ubuntu users, with more apps of ever higher quality available for the platform.

And in the meantime, even as it is today I feel that Ubuntu is a much more attractive and usable OS than the best Microsoft has come up with.
