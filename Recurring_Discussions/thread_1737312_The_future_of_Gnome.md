### Post by RoflHaxBbq on 2011-04-23
Personally, I'm a little worried about the future of Gnome, and Linux in general.

It seems like it's following the popular trend of appealing to the noobs.

I like using Gnome 2. I dislike KDE, because personally I find it hard to use, and obscure for the sake of being obscure. But that's the good thing about Linux. You get to choose.

Gnome 2 is nice and easy to use. It also has enough visual effects and trivial stuff like that to make it fun and nice looking, but not so much to bog it down.

With the release of Gnome 3, it is obvious that it is leaning more towards looking cool and being easy and fun for the noobs.

And that's all well and good, but how about the future of Gnome for people like me?

Continue to use the good old Gnome that we like, but is no longer supported?
Move to the bogged down Gnome 3 that we don't like?
Move over to KDE which we don't like?
Or change completely to a minimalist DE, which for some of us just isn't our cup of tea.


The bottom line is: Gnome 3 is all well and good, but whatever you do, don't abandon Gnome 2.
