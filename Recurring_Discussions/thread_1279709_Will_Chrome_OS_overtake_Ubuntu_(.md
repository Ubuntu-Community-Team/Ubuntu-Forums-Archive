### Post by Dragonbite on 2009-10-01
In the paid-for consumer market, pulling people largely from Windows and some from Linux; possibly.

In the Hobbyist, Enthusiast, home market; possibly not.

In the Server market; not with this iteration of ChromeOS.

Chrome has the advantage of a large company to market and push but is still new (dare say "beta"?) and while people like and use Google they are starting to get that mystique of being the next Microsoft and from people's experience with Microsoft fresh in their minds they are keeping an eye on what else is out there.

Ubuntu has the advantage of already coming out installed on systems, a healthy development and support structure (is there or will there be a ChromeOS forum?), already working with Moblin and UNR and is more "community owned" Linux.

Ubuntu has been proven, let's see how ChromeOS goes when it gets here.  If Google can't generate enough buzz over it and/or get enough systems makers to include it, it could be a bust.  

Although Google can survive a bust a little bit better than Microsoft can a Windows bust because it will not negatively impact their other services (Gmail, Search, Advertisement, Android and Chrome browser) except by siphoning off some of their R&D budget.
