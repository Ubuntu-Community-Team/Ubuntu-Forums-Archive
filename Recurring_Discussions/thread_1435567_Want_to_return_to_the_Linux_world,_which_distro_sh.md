This is probably the wrong place to ask, because people here are generally biased toward Ubuntu. Anyway, I suggest you look at Gentoo Linux:

[http://www.gentoo.org/](http://www.gentoo.org/)

It is a source-based rolling distribution that lets you decide what direction your installation takes. You can specify what software and which versions of that software you want installed on your system, how it is compiled and what sub-features of that software is compiled into it. If you do not want to upgrade something to a new version, you can tell its package manager that you do not want newer versions and it will honor your request when doing upgrades. It has a bit of a learning curve, but anything new does and it is completely worth it in my opinion.

My laptop has it installed and I have quick boot-times (14 seconds from BIOS to KDM), fast application start times (applications never take more than 2 seconds to start) and quick shut-down times (which I never timed, but I would put them at about 5 seconds). It also can be configured to make very efficient use of memory by stripping out stuff from KDE that you do not need (e.g. nepomuk, akonadi); after logging into KDE and opening konsole, my laptop only uses 148MB of RAM.
