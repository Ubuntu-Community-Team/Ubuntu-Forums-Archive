### Post by NMFTM on 2010-07-08
Why I use Ubuntu:
 
**1.** Things are constantly changing in the world of Linux. If you don't know how to do something using some obscure distro you'd have to Google "how to do X on Linux" and hope that the HOWTO wasn't written so long ago that it doesn't apply. Or that the way to do something on distroY that the guide was written for isn't slightly different than distroX that your using. But since Ubuntu is the #1 used distro, you can just Google "how to do X in Ubuntu" or sometimes even "how to do X on Ubuntu 10.04", to be sure that your reading the most up to date version. Or something that's very close to being up to date.
 
**2.** Ubuntu has a better update schedule than Debian. I wasn't satisfied with the update schedules of either Debian Stable (every few years), Debian Unstable (daily), or Debian Testing (every 9 days). But default Ubuntu (major release) updates every 6 months, which I think is just right.
 
**3.** With the exception of Debian. Ubuntu has the largest official repos of any distro. Although, Arch might have bigger if you enable certain ones. But, unlike distros like Fedora or OpenSUSE you don't have to install a bunch of unofficial repos. You can stick with the official ones and never have to worry about whether you added the correct ones or about dependency hell issues sometime in the future. Unless your installing packages manually, but that's true of any distro.
