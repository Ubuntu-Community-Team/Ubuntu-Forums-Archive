On Windows, Flash is hardware-accelerated.

On OS X and Linux, it isn't - these are both rather shoddy ports of the original software that really do cause performance issues - and, in many cases, can kill the entire browser if it isn't sandboxed.

(A similar example: take a look at iTunes and QuickTime on Mac OS X and iTunes and QT on Windows.)

With this in mind, it's also worth noting that the HTML5 technologies (CSS3, canvas, the <video> tag) *are* open - even if the *de facto* video format isn't. (Also worth noting: the *de facto* audio format, MP3, is also proprietary. This isn't necessarily a *good* thing, but it's not a particularly major issue.)

Flash, on the other hand, is wholly proprietary. It's slow and unstable on every platform except Windows, and there's no alternative except perhaps Gnash (although that's still incomplete and even worse in most respects, lagging several versions behind in compatibility.)
