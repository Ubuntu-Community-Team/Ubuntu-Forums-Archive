I'm a graduated economist and I'll give you some insight on what I really think is important to understand such imperfect market. Price is not everything that matters. Product differentiation is much more important, and here is not just brand loyalty. It is more a matter of complementarity with hardware and software. What use is an operational system if it can't run on your hardware or the software you need? The incentives for independent software vendors (ISVs) to write apps for a platform are the average profit per unit and the market size. Windows market size is huge and Mac average profit is very big, so almost everybody writes software for Windows, and those that don't have a minimum efficient scale write for Macs. Linux on the other side have a tiny market, but have much uncertainty about the average profit. The end result is what they call "vendor lock-in". Linux don't have many users because there are not enough software, and there are not enough software because there are few users.

That said, you better read Industrial Organization literature than standard microeconomics handbooks.
