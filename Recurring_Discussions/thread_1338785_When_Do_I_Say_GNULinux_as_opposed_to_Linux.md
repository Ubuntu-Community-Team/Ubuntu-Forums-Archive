### Post by t0p on 2009-11-27
Whether you refer to the operating system as "Linux" or "GNU/Linux" depends on a couple of things:

1.  What do *you* believe?  Are you, like Stallman, convinced that GNU really needs to be credited in the OS's name?  Do you think that the correct name is "Linux" because that's what it's known as by most people nowadays?  Do you have no opinion either way?

2.  What does your college teacher believe?  Is he a Free Software enthusiast/activist who calls it "GNU/Linux"?  If so, it might be a good idea to call it "GNU/Linux" yourself - it might make no difference; or it might get you extra credits. Plus if he's a real stickler for what he believes is right, using the "incorrect" term "Linux" might lose you points.

Of course, all the above is applicable only in reference to the name of the whole operating system.  If you're just discussing the kernel, the correct name is "Linux".
