### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-05-08
The truth is that we need a good enough competitor to Adobe to diversify the design market and keep people from their addiction to single sets of software. It's unhealthy for users, unhealthy for the market, and silly. GIMP has a lot of built-in support for PSDs, except for the features GIMP doesn't feature itself (the layer effects).

Synfig/Inkscape/GIMP/Kompozer/Openshot is our most viable alternative to Photoshop CS 5. And they're not all that integrated.

Of course, over time, these projects will probably recognize their usefulness when integrated with each other, and it seems Adobe's running out of ideas for new features that their users will actually be willing to pay for. Good thing most of them don't, or GIMP would rule the hobbyist editing market. Tell me all the kids on deviantART would buy Photoshop if they were forced to, and tell me that they use any of the other programs included, on average.

I'm pretty sure that if we included the layer effects and perfected PSD support, then someone made a spin-off of the project with Single-Window-Mode by default called 'Photoshop', we'd have a crap ton more users.

Trust me, people are that obsessed. I agree, though, we should get Adobe's products on here in the meantime. The more people we can get to use open source software, the more people will realize there are alternatives to all their crap, and the more often they will question the ubiquity of some products in the market. I mean, really, GIMP supports PSD and .xcf. Isn't it about time Photoshop did the same?
