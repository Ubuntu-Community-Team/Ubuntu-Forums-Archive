### Post by cardinals_fan on 2008-08-10
1. Openbox - lightweight yet full-featured
2. Xfce (slimmed-down) - modular and powerful
3. dwm - the tiling causes me some problems with image editing, but this little WM is great
4. xmonad - another very solid tiling WM
5. Pekwm - nice and light, but a touch buggy
6. Fluxbox - a very good WM; I just never really liked it
7. Evilwm - VERY minimal but still usable.
8. Awesome - a rather odd but still decent tiling WM
9. wmii - I never liked this tiling WM.  It just felt... wrong.
10. KDE 3.5 - a decent DE, both configurable and light
11. GNOME - slow and hard to configure.  I'll pass.
12. KDE 4 - bugs + slow + fewer features (so far) = FAIL.
13. Ion - unmentionably bad.

EDIT: I forgot E17.  Unremarkable and buggy in my opinion.
