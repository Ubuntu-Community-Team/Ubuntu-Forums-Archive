### Post by asuastrophysics on 2009-11-11
Hey everyone,

I abandoned Windows Vista back in January, because my laptop crashed due to the "registry being corrupted". I already had Ubuntu installed on my desktop computer as an experimental OS on a separate partition, but hadn't really bothered to use it. I decided to make a cold-turkey complete switch to Ubuntu. (This led to a *very* steep learning curve, BTW, as no Windows to fall back on) 

This learning curve was hard at first, as I felt like I wasn't really in control of what the OS was doing (I still have a long way to go, but I'm past the roughest part). But if you're persistent enough and don't give up on it, you'll find you're past the curve and you can really marvel about how great the Linux Operating System really is. 

_My #1 reason for liking Ubuntu:_
**Absolutely no maintenance is required at all on the part of the user** :p
- Seriously, I don't have to do a thing. This means no scanning for viruses, no spybot sd, no malware, no trojans, no defragmenting, no "disk cleanup", no rogue programs that install crap all over the hard disk, nothing. When I remove a program, it's totally gone and I don't have to worry about little pieces of it remaining all over the file system.

Bottom line: It's as fast today as it was a year ago, and that's what I love about it. 0% bloat. UNIX-like systems are very efficient. 
Anyway, that's why I'm never going to use Windows as my primary OS again.

Why do you use Ubuntu? What are your reasons for picking Ubuntu and not some other distro? Why Linux and not Windows or OSX?

### Post by QIII on 2009-11-11
We used to say in the Army:  Rank among Lieutenants is like virtue among whores.

Discussing the relative merits of Windows/Linux/Solaris/Unix is somewhat unimportant.

They are different things, do different things well (and poorly) and their use and adoption by users is primarily based on a set of aesthetics that is different from user to user.

My aesthetic preference is for Linux or Solaris as a result of my long familiarity with Unix and my technological bent.

I can't say, by any valid scientific measurement, that either Windows or OS X is better or worse than Linux.  There are things I do not like about each.

There are things I do not like about Linux.

However, my most pressing concern is not the OSs themselves at all.

My decision to use Linux and Solaris is based on the fact that I simply detest Microsoft's business practices, monopoly, hegemony and the use of its weight in the market to put a strangle hold on users, hardware OEMs and software companies.

"Microsoft supports ..." is an incorrect notion.  Microsoft supports nothing but that which Microsoft produces.

Hardware OEMs are forced by market conditions to build drivers that work with Windows.  Microsoft is under no obligation to make sure Windows works with the OEM's drivers.  Microsoft does not support hardware.  Hardware OEMs either create drivers that work with Windows or they go out of business.

Software Developers are forced by market conditions to create software that works with Windows.  Microsoft is under no obligation to make sure Windows works with the software.  Microsoft does not support non-Microsoft software.  Software developers either produce software that works with Windows or they go out of business.

Henry Ford once said:  "Any customer can have a car painted any color that he wants so long as it is black."

In Microsoft's case, that's blue.

I want to color my "user experience" any color I darn well please.

### Post by chrinabuntu on 2011-03-17
The main thing that brought me to at least check it out was to have an alternative to MicroSoft. I had heard of Linux, but didn't even realize it was a GUI system...just thought it was an old terminal-only type system that hard-core geeks were still using...like underground or something. LOL! I have a friend who is blind and is a huge Linux guy, and he was always talking about it (if I'd gotten back in touch with him earlier in life, I would probably have been a Linux user at that point...but he was an old classmate who I found online). He was constantly telling me I should use it and how much ease of access they have for blind folks (as well as folks with other special needs).

Anyway, I wasn't really interested in all this geeky Linux talk for a while...then one day it caught my attention for whatever reason. He explained how it is not what I was thinking...told me how to try it. He suggested Ubuntu (tho he was always pronouncing it wrong because his screenreader pronounced it wrong!)...so I looked it up and when I saw it was a regular looking OS...with a regular GUI, internet, email and everything Windows had, but was free and read all about the philosophy, etc...I was in love before I even tried it! I began with what was the new thing at that time...Feisty. Been all up in it ever since!  ;O)

I LOVE having an alternative to MicroSoft. All those years of them monopolizing the computer world...so many people still don't even know this is even a choice. Many still have never heard of Ubuntu. I'm glad I'm a convert!
