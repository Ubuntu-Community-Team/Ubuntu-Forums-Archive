### Post by TBABill on 2010-07-22
Hmmmm....I have 8 computers and all but 2 have Linux on them, either as the sole system or as a separate partition. I have ATi cards in 2, nVidia in 1 and Intel in 3. They all work flawlessly for video, but maybe I got lucky with my video cards that came onboard. Sound works in all but one and that's a single line edit in a modprobe file (definitely not a noob fix). Wireless works on all but 2 and they have Broadcom cards that have to have drivers installed after installing the OS...forces a plug-in to the router for a 1-time download/install.
 
For my HP printer I have to plug into USB for a 1-time setup, then I can use it wirelessly. Not necessary in Windows...just pop the CD in and go or scan the network and download the driver online.
 
Other things some people don't want to deal with is the change in feel about the desktop. My wife hates any Linux distro I install "because it's a pain in the a** when all I want to do is what I know how to do." She doesn't mind surfing the web with it, but using OpenOffice or another "non Windows" app is just frustrating since she has no desire, or need, to learn. 
 
Lots of people just don't like change, even if "normal" sucks for them like the constant frustrations with Windows.
