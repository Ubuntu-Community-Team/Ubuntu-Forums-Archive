### Post by saulgoode on 2010-04-23
> **bash said:**
> As I said it is not true **anymore**. More details here:

[http://tirania.org/blog/archive/2009/Dec-17.html](http://tirania.org/blog/archive/2009/Dec-17.html)

Or to summarize it using Miguel's words:
> The new patent covenant ensures that other third party distributions can distribute Moonlight without their users fearing of getting sued over patent infringement by Microsoft.

While Miguel de Icaza's statement is accurate, it evades the issue that third party *distributors* are excluded from the covenant (only end users are covered). 

Not only does this mean that distros such as Red Hat, Debian, and Ubuntu are not covered by Microsoft's covenant, but as soon as you (as an individual) decide to give a copy of Moonlight to a friend, you are no longer considered a "downstream recipient" and become an "intermediate recipient", forfeiting your patent indemnity. As far as any Microsoft patents are concerned, this disqualifies Moonlight from being considered Free Software (and not even as "Free" as closed-source shareware).
