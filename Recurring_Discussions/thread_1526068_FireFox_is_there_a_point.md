### Post by aysiu on 2010-07-07
There's only a point if Firefox does things for you other browsers don't. Now you may not care about these things, but below are the reasons I prefer Firefox to other browsers: [list][*]Even though this isn't in by default, a simple about:config changed flag gives me *exactly* the tab opening and closing behavior that I want. There is an extension in Chrome that comes close but isn't the same. And there is an Opera setting that comes close but isn't the same. Basically, I want the tabs to go in order. If I open a new tab, I always want it on the far right. If I close a tab from the middle, I always want to focus on the tab to the right of it. If I unclose a recently closed tab, I want it open to exactly where it was when I closed it. No other web browser does all of these things. Believe me--I've tried.[*]Even though Firefox is generally a slower browser, it's fast enough for me. At a certain point, speed of page loading doesn't matter any more (when you have to measure it in milliseconds). But what Firefox does do is consistently load pages at the same speed. In Chrome and Opera, I usually get a fast page load (almost instantaneous). Occasionally, though, the page will freeze up mid-load. This is *not* a Ubuntu-specific problem. I've experienced it on Windows and Mac as well.[*]The Firefox smartbar is actually smart. If I type any part of any URL or page name I visited recently or bookmarked, it'll immediately bump up to the top the site I either most recently visited or most often visit that fits the criteria. Opera and Chrome do not do this. I often have to wait several seconds for the result I want to appear in the drop-down... or it just doesn't appear at all.[/list] Chances are if you start a thread called *Firefox is there a point?* then those things won't matter to you. They matter to me, though, and I can genuinely say after extensive use of Opera and Chrome that Opera and Chrome do **not** do the things I just listed.
