### Post by dpny on 2011-10-23
You can't answer this question without asking, "better for what?" and "better for whom?"

Do you like to tinker and want to have a lot of control over how your OS works? Do you want the option to, within reason, pick and choose hardware? Then Ubuntu wins.

Do you want to run commercial programs, like InDesign and Photoshop? Do you want all of the devices you buy to work together well? Do you want tech support and warranties? Then OS X wins.

I comes down to what you want to do. For all of the day-to-day things we do--web, email, music, video, etc.--Linux, OS X or Windows will do just fine. After than it really becomes about personal preference.
