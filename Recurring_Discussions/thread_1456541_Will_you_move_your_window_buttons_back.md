### Post by Odd-rationale on 2010-04-17
I was first rather shocked that the designed team decided to change the locations of the buttons. But after giving it some consideration myself, I think it may not be so bad of an idea.

I wonder if the decision was somewhat related to what Matt Asay (new Canonical COO) wrote in a comment in his blog, "The debate has shifted. The team to beat isn't Microsoft. It’s Apple." [1]

According to Matt, Canonical's new target is Apple, not Microsoft. I believe this is a smarter choice. Even Shuttleworth hopes that Ubuntu will surpass Mac OSX in the near future [2].

Since Ubuntu is pitting itself against Mac OSX instead of Windows, it makes sense that the UI should mimic that of OSX. But I don't think they changed the buttons just to be make it easier for Mac users. Apple products have been known for their beautiful design. I think design is the message Ubuntu wants to convey.

But what Windows users who make up the majority of the market share? I remember the first time I used a Mac (coming from Windows), one of the things that stuck out to me the most was the button placement. I didn't know much about computers at the time, but I knew it was different.

This is a good thing. For new users coming from Windows, they will be continually reminded that Ubuntu is not Windows. It is different.

And for us experienced Linux users, we know that there is always choice. Those of us who dislike it will move the buttons to where ever they like.

So personally, I don't think either button placement has an objective advantage over the other. I think it may be part of Canonical's plan to re-brand Ubuntu against Mac OSX and to compete in terms of design and user experience.

[1] [http://voices.canonical.com/matt.asay/2010/03/29/lucid-made-me-a-believer/](http://voices.canonical.com/matt.asay/2010/03/29/lucid-made-me-a-believer/)

[2] [http://itmanagement.earthweb.com/osrc/article.php/12068_3814021_1/Ubuntus-Shuttleworth-Planning-to-Overtake-Apple.htm](http://itmanagement.earthweb.com/osrc/article.php/12068_3814021_1/Ubuntus-Shuttleworth-Planning-to-Overtake-Apple.htm)
