I don't have a problem with people who prefer one OS over another, but I do have a problem when they try to say that one piece of software is "evil" or that I'm wrong because I like Windows or that I'm unethical because I use proprietary software or that someone is unethical for writing proprietary software.

On another note, the FLOSS community advocates freedom. Freedom of the user, etc. What about the developer? He put his energy and time into creating the program. Why is he "wrong" for choosing how to release it? I prefer open source, whether that's a BSD license, a GNU license or just licensing the code so that institutions can tweak a program to their needs, but I believe that the developer gets the first freedom and I don't think it's right to say he's wrong for his decision.
