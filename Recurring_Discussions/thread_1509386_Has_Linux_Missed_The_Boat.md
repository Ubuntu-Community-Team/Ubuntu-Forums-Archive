### Post by samalex on 2010-06-14
> **Johnsie said:**
> Apple are huge now... They took advantage of Windows weaknesses in a way that the Linux community could not. Has the Linux community missed the boat when it comes to competing against Apple and Microsoft?

The biggest catch with Linux and most open source projects is marketing.  The software is written by devoted teams of coders, but unfortunately little to no effort is put into marketing the products to the general public unless it's done by one of the bigger vendors like [IBM's adds about 8-10 years]("http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KwEWxpOWOok") ago.  Those however were geared more to businesses than individuals.

Apple, Microsoft, and similar companies have the cash flow and man power to put their stuff in magazines, on TV, billboards, etc so the public sees it, but Linux and other FOSS projects haven't been in a position to do that.  I think if Canonical or one of the larger corporate companies like Red Hat were to start advertising Linux for the individual so people saw Linux as something more than a toy or geek OS we would see more adoption outside the IT crowd.

As for Apple overtaking Microsoft, I think Apple makes good computers, but they've grown to what they are today thanks to their non-computer devices like the iPod, iPhone, iTunes music store, and now the iPad.  Heck they're saying now that [the iPad may overtake the Mac]("http://www.theregister.co.uk/2010/05/20/ipads_outselling_macs_two_to_one/") here pretty soon, which is crazy!  So to say Apple has beat Microsoft in the same game Linux plays isn't too accurate.

Sam
