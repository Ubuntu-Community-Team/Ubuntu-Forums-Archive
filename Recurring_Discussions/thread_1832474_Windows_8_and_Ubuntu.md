### Post by ErkDemon on 2011-08-27
Oh, touchscreens do have their uses (for casual browsing, or checking your calendar, or reading emails). You don't need the higher resolution of a stylus or mouse to scroll webpages or flip the pages of an ebook. 

They do suck for anything that requires precise cursor positioning though, hence the big square finger-friendly blocks in Unity. 

The big problem with Unity's user-interface that icked people off was that it was optimised for hardware that most users didn't have. Unity's interface is optimised for small landscape-format wide-screen touchscreen netbooks.


Which sounds great, except that ... unless Nokia still do their old "communicator" range ... I'm not sure that anyone actually /*makes*/ a small landscape-format widescreen touchscreen netbook. So Canonical took a popular desktop/laptop operating system and seemed to be compromising it for the benefit of a section of its userbase that ... as far as I can work out ... doesn't actually exist. It's not surprising that a lot of people wondered what the heck the company thought it was doing. Making a big deal about repurposing an OS release for a hardware plaform that doesn't actually exist seems a little ... strategically questionable.

### Post by ninjaaron on 2011-08-28
As someone who uses 11.04 on a tablet an has spent many, many hours scripting it
to the proper behaviour, I can tell everyone here that Unity is most certainly not
optimised for tablets. It can be made to work well with tablets, but I'm unsure that it
is an improvement over gnome 2 in terms of touch accessibility, and it definitely needs TONS
of work before it could be called "touch optimised."  I cannot imagine how nightmarish
it would be to try either as the primary OS for a smartphone.  It does work ok on my
tablet after extensive scripting

I've actually found the greatest advances in Unity to be for Keyboard access, which
is why I love it for all of my computers with keyboards.

The next iteration of KDE is supposed to include a "real" mobile OS.  We'll see how that goes.

ps- The menu isn't a problem with a touch-screen. You just touch the area where
it should be, and it appears.  Sometimes this causes a menu that you don't need to appear,
but this is never a problem.
