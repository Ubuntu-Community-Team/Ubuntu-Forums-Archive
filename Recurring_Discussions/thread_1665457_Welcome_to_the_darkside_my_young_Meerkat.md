### Post by JC Cheloven on 2011-01-13
Cregory: I'm also worried about the free software principles. It's what makes possible what we have today. In fact, I'm sad when some people over here thinks in Ubuntu (or any other distro for that matter) only in a practical manner. Coming from long-term linux users, statements like "it's only an OS", "simply use that works", scares me: the fact that it respects your freedom, and the fact that there is inside the effort of thousands of volunteers working for a better world, doesn't count?

In fact, if you care to read, recent things like [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1630801&page=6") (#59) or [this]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=10350416") (#7), makes me dubious about whether the success of Ubuntu in terms of user base is a real success or not. I mean, a zillion people using Ubuntu only because it works great, but having no worries about DRM, observation of user freedom, or other ideals, will really help to make real the Ubuntu philosohy? 
That kind of things also tempts me to switch back to debian. But I want to think that these are only personal opinions, and that there is another side, for example shown in bug#1 at launchpad, which has more weight.

That said, I think a bit of flexibility is also in order. This is a long way. When you plant tomatoes, you don't expect to have the fruit right the next day. And when the tiny plant sprouts, you care for it, you don't kick it saying "bah, this is not a tomatoe".
Now, the free software community faces the big problem of proprietary formats being *de facto* widely used. This can't be changed in a single day, and will probably need the gradual help of political willingness. BTW: proprietary is closed, to figure out how that works is very difficult, not that proprietary is better. 

By the time being, and this is an opinion, it's worth to spread the world with the awareness of free software (at the cost of some proprietary elements being used). The other way around, keeping the system "pure" but not usable for the average people, would lead nowhere IMO.

Cheers
