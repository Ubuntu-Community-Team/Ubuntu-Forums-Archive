### Post by mickie.kext on 2010-07-26
You can't get a virus on Linux in a way you can in Windows. 

Just by surfing Internet and activating ActiveX content on malicious site is enough to get pwned in Windows. Heck, sometimes is enough just not to install anti-virus, firewall and anti-spyware and then let computer sit idle while it's connected to network cable. 

In Linux, that can't happen. You have to actually download infected program from untrusted source, and install it by yourself. And even when you do that, virus will not spread to other computers. You have to install it on each. Not to mention that Linux viruses are very rare and usually can't make same kind of damage that Windows ones can, because of more security-aware system architecture. 

So it is safe to say that you are lot more secure with Linux. But don't try and install everything you see on Internet. Stick with repositories and you'll be fine.
