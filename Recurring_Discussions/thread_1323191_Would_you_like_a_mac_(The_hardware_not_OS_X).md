### Post by handy on 2009-11-12
I came from the Amiga world.

Everything went down hill after that. As the AmigaOS was so simple.  Though I do understand that part of the reason for that was the ROM chips that were inaccessible & therefore invisible to the user.

I used to (I certainly don't anymore) know what every single Amiga system file did.  There were only about 200 of them.

I migrated to windows 3.11 in 1996, & then to win95.  The first thing that shocked me in 3.11 & then most especially in win95, was the number of system files & their dreadful 8.3 names.

The Amiga didn't use file extensions.  A file was interrogated by the system to identify its type & use.

Anyway I learned windows & made a living out of it for 10 years, until I couldn't stand it anymore & retired.

Back to Apple.

I owned a Mac Plus, in 1990 for a while.  Still being an Amiga user, I naturally found the Mac Plus system & the hardware to be incredibly boring & limited.  So I sold it.

I bought a 15" Powerbook in 2000/2001. It had Panther on it. It was interesting for a couple of weeks, & then it also became quite quickly boring.  

So I gave it to my wife, who still uses it in her private music teacher business, as it runs Sibelius & some other critical software that she needs for her work.

A couple of years ago, we bought 2 x 24" aluminium iMacs.  My wife loves the size of the screen & the quality of the display.  She of course can run all of the software that she needs for her business.

I love the saving of space, & have been using Linux, (short term Ubuntu, long term Arch) on the machine as my primary system.

I have no complaints about the iMac.  It is what I knew it to be, & I'm interested in it for the hardware.

I recently upgraded the (cloned with Clonezilla) 320GB internal HDD to a 1.5TB drive.  So it now has the storage space that I require.

Really, the only complaints that I have about the iMac, is that the hardware is harder to upgrade than your average notebook. 

(A suction cup to remove the plasti-glass screen, & tiny torx drivers - T6 & T9, plus a couple of phillips heads (23 screws in all, not counting the 4 on the HDD) are needed to get at the HDD to replace it.)

& like all but the very rare notebooks, you can't upgrade the GPU, or (less important) the CPU.

I would only recommend the iMac to people that are short on desktop space, &/or, need OS X, to be able to do what they must do to make a living.

The iMac has served me & my wife well, I have no regrets, though my next machine will not be made by Apple. Who, by the way, are just another, tiny by comparison to the other monstrous computer/electronic giants out there, corporation.

Don't let their advertising &/or Steve Jobs put you off? Choose an Apple product only if it satisfies your requirements, the hardware can be just what some of us need, the system is what others need, most Apple owners want the pair.
