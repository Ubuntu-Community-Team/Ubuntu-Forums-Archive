That's because the two are not compatible. A rolling release distribution is the antithesis of user friendliness. Eventually, it will break. And when it does, this "user friendly" distro is going to demand some technical skills to get it going again.

Not to mention the fact that nothing ever works 100% of the time. You get an update that fixes an old bug, and another update that introduces a new one.

### Post by forrestcupp on 2011-04-24
> **linuxyogi said:**

Why would it not feel right for Ubuntu to be a rolling release? Well, looking at your starting date, I guess it wouldn't make a difference to you because you haven't had time for the Ubuntu way to become a part of your life. For those of us who have been around for a long time, anticipating regular releases and the ability to scale back to a certain release that works perfect for you is just a big part of what Ubuntu is. There is an excitement in knowing that at certain times every year, there will be a new release, and maybe this release will be the one that is so awesome and changing that it will blow our minds.

Plus, if they never had any more regular releases, we wouldn't be able to make fun of all of the crazy names they come up with every 6 months. :)
