### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-11-20
I hate this question.  The better question is, "Which is better *for me*."  And the answer depends on what hardware the user has, what she wants to use her computer for (there's stuff one does better than the other).  Xubuntu is better *for me* because I'm not a major gamer and I don't use applications that only work in Windows.  I can do all my school work (so far, at least) in Abiword and Gnumeric.  And even if not, I bet I could do it in Googledocs or some other cloud-based application that can be accessed from any web browser. All the other stuff I use my computer for - music and pictures, slideshows, social networking, whatever - I can do easily and quickly in Xubuntu.  And faster than I did them in Windows before. And for free.  I have old, hand-me-down hardware, which ran Windows very slow, but which runs Xubu 12.04 very fast and very pretty.

For heavy gamers, and for people who do CAD and for those whose jobs or schools or other uses *require* OS-specific applications, then obviously Windows is best *for them*.
