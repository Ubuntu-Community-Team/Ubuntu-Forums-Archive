### Post by saulgoode on 2009-12-24
It is rather a misnomer to call the inadequacies of the Microsoft patent covenant "holes" -- they are not hidden subtleties or unforeseen ramifications. 

Microsoft's "Covenant to End Users of Moonlight 3 and 4" is quite clear in stating that you are only covered if you are an "end user" and you obtain Moonlight directly from Novell. Other distros such as Ubuntu, Debian, Fedora, etc are not covered.

Other conditions of the covenant are also problematic, but they are likewise not "legal holes"; they are explicit terms which must be met to be covered by the "covenant". It should be eminently clear that Novell intends Moonlight to be exclusively *their* product and that they are quite amenable to exercising Microsoft's patent threats against other Free Software distributions.
