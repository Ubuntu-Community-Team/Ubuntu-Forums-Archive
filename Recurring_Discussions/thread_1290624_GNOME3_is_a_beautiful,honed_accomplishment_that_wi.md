### Post by Warpnow on 2009-10-13
LXDE is an amazing Desktop Environment, and *so is XFCE*. I think XFCE and LXDE are amongst the best DEs available, but for very different reasons.

LXDE is immensely small while still being very easy to use.

XFCE on the other hand is very intricate. Amazing settings manager, very easy to manage themes, immense amount of options, and a built in compositor that is actually very nice. While maintaining similar feature lists, it is much lighter than gnome or KDE. 

LXDE is obviously much lighter than XFCE, but its not fair to compare the two. They aren't the same at all. Openbox is amazing as a window manager, but it isn't as polished as xfwm. Look at how GTK apps like firefox look in LXDE, for instance. No application will look that bad in XFCE. Also, run a compositor with LXDE. xcompmgr is good, but its buggy, and can cause problems in basic tasks like scrolling. XFCE's compositor is likely one of its best features.

That said...I really don't like Xubuntu, so, meh.
