It's about utility, not slickness. And beauty is in the eye of the beholder. You may have other aesthetic differences of opinion as well. If you like abstract art and he likes realism, so what?

My retort would be this:

First of all it's a matter of choice, and the freedom that Linux provides. Aside from a lot of games, many of the things you do on Windows (don't ask, but did he purchase Windows 7 or pirate it, and if purchased, how much did he pay? do you pay your own expenses, and does someone subsidize his?), I can do very well with open source software. When it comes to security while using the internet, very few scams, trojans or phishing schemes target Linux users. I am not immune to malware, but my resistance to it is pretty good before I even need to engage counter-measures. And there is anti-malware software for Linux available, free and tested for compatibility and stability just as everything else that makes it into the Ubuntu repository is. Finally, by saving money on software, being able to use older hardware or hardware I build myself, I can buy more cooking and kitchen gadgets (or whatever else) than I otherwise would be able to.

Don't think of it as a battle. How does his using Windows threaten you, or you using Ubuntu threaten him? There's no need to go negative about Windows--he's entitled to his opinions and choices just as you are. Accentuate the positive aspects of the trade-offs, and you will keep a friend.

Above all, be nice. Remember what [Ubuntu's underlying philosophy ]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/philosophy")is.
