### Post by bryanl on 2011-04-16
change is tough.

Gnome is changing as well and the new Gnome interface is very similar to Unity. If I understand right, you might still be able to select the old Gnome2 to get the classic desktop during install.

The reasons for the change include the implementation of developments and ideas in user interfaces in recent years, the idea that screens are changing from 4:3 to 16:9 aspect ratios, making better use of graphics cards capabilities as it has developed in recent years, and utilization of improvements in the graphics software interface standards. 

Figure that a 30% of the desktop market is still an 11 year old desktop - Windows XP. People get used to one way of doing things and have a bit of a problem getting comfortable with something new and different no matter how much better it is. That is what happened when Windows Vista came out.

It appears to me that Canonical is leading in regards to a vision for Linux. As they say, it's the leaders that often get the arrows. 

But, from what I can tell, there are good solid reasons for the Unity choice and many of them are forward looking towards integrating into a whole lot of technical and design visions anticipated in the near future. The road may be a bit bumpy, but at least we're traveling towards the future rather than towards the past.

### Post by Rifester on 2011-04-16
Scott-Ian,
I switched completely to Gnome Shell after using and testing Unity.   I personally like Gnome Shell better.   I think the move to Unity was brave and innovative but could cause some extra work down the road for Ubuntu developers (compatibility between GNOME 3, Gnome Shell, and Unity).  I know Canonical was not happy that some of their work was not going to be implemented in Gnome 3, but I am not sure an entire new shell was or is the answer.   I am one of the users that have found Gnome 3 with Gnome Shell is what I want to use and help become better.  I don't wish the Unity project anything but success, but it does not appeal to me.  The next few releases will be interesting.   I hope Ubuntu gives some consideration to the fact that some users want Gnome Shell.  If not many users choosing Gnome shell may look toward other distros that are developing more then a vanilla Gnome shell install.   Either way, the future will be interesting.
