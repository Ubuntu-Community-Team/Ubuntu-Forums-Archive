### Post by PeterJS on 2008-03-01
There is no such thing as better, only personal taste. KDE focuses on being more windows like and having everything as configurable as possible. Gnome focuses on being intutive, easy to use, and looking good/consistent.

Gnome support around here is much better simply by virtue of that being what the vast majorty of people use. I used KDE for a number of years, it's powerful once you get it the way you want. Ultimately I succumb to vanity, gnome is easier to theme in some respects, gnome can be made to behave like KDE it just takes a bit of effort.
