### Post by arsenic23 on 2010-07-08
As a business with only so much money and manpower you don't want to take on every project that will make you money.  Instead you want to take on the projects that will make you money in the most efficient manner.  Why spend money developing for linux when you can develop something else with a better yield?

That is assuming the primary goal of your company is to maximize profits, and if it's a public company, you can bet that's your primary goal.  That's not saying a company couldn't make money producing linux games.  It's just more efficient, right now, to do other things.  Or that's the idea anyway.

### Post by theraje on 2010-07-08
If you sell your game for $50 and make $250,000,000 in sales, no way on Earth would it equal $250,000,000 in net profit. Not to mention that only very tiny number of games have sold 5 million units. Games take a lot of money to produce (unless you're a "garage" game developer, like me :P ), and big game companies will always target the lowest common denominator to sell more copies.

Just look at the open-source gaming devices like the Gamepark Holdings GP2x Wiz. It runs Linux and is free to develop for, but the vast majority of people have probably never even heard of the thing. Companies make more money with proprietary technology, because then they can charge for their SDKs, development tools, etc.

I personally plan to support Linux with my endeavors, but I don't expect to bring the world to its knees and get filthy rich. I don't think developers shun Linux at all... it's simply that the profit margins aren't as appealing.
