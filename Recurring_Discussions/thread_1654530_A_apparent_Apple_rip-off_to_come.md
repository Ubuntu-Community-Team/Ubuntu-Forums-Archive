### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2010-12-28
Frankly, their app store work is considerably better than our current model when it comes to managing thousands of third party apps. Our current system would fall to pieces if we tried to do what Apple is doing with what we have today (and in the near future).

For one thing, every Ubuntu machine would have this index of 300,000 applications stored locally (with an icon for each one). In addition, there would be name collisions all over the place if people even managed to fit them all into the repositories. The current naming guidelines give us package names like &#8220;dict,&#8221; and even the most completely GUI-centric software will happily throw its binary in /usr/bin because it's in your $PATH. Now, consider that a couple thousand of those 300,000 apps are repackaged demo apps and you will probably see the problem.

I'm not saying the repositories are a bad design (they do a lot of things really well!), but Apple is ahead of us as far as app stores are concerned and we shouldn't pretend they aren't. Thankfully, Software Centre and the App Store are not doing the same thing (yet) and a lot of the problems &#8212; like icons being stored locally &#8212; are being fixed, so my above whinging is moot for the time being.
