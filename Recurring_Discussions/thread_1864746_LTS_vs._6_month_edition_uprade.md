### Post by mdavis1231 on 2011-10-19
Right now I'm running Ubuntu 10.04.3 LTS.  I used to upgrade every 6 months to the new Ubuntu edition, but I thought I'd give a try to only upgrading when it's a LTS edition.  Does anybody have any thoughts as to the pros & cons of only upgrading if it's an LTS edition?  Thanks.

---

### Post by 11jmb on 2011-10-19
Obviously its a personal choice. I personally upgrade to the LTS releases after it has been out for a few months. I think the major tradeoff is between stability and flashy new features. I know that if I stick with a LTS release, i will have a more reliable desktop that I only do major upgrades on every 2 years.

---

### Post by 11jmb on 2011-10-19
I should also note that I have used a small dual boot partition or VM for every release since I started with 7.10 just to check out new features. That way if I really like the new release and it is appreciably different from the LTS I can switch over (which I did for 9.10)
