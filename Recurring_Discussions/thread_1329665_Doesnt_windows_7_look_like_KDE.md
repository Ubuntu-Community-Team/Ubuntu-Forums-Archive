### Post by Gwasanaethau on 2009-11-19
Getting this slightly back on track: I haven't used (or even seen) either Windows 7 or KDE 4.3, so I couldn't comment about those specific releases. However, when I first switched over to GNU/Linux from Windows XP back in 2006, the first distributions I tested were Ark and Kubuntu Breezy, and I hated both of them! The main reason was because I was fed up with Windows XP at the time, and I wanted something completely different, but the KDE interface in both distributions I tried was just too Windows-ish for my liking (at least that's what I saw from my perspective at the time). That's one of the big reasons why I've stayed with GNOME ever since. KDE and Windows certainly have a very similar appearance to those who haven't seen anything else (they're certainly more alike than, say, GNOME and Windows or any of the Mac Operating Systems and Windows). Underneath, however, they are vastly different, and just a few minutes of using each of them will reveal it (and no, I don't mean that Windows is rubbish or anything like that; I simply mean it has a different feel to it, no matter how similar they look).
