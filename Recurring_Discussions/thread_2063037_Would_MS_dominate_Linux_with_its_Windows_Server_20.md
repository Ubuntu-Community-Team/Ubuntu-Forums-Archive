### Post by doktorOblivion on 2012-09-26
IMHO Microsoft is going through similar issues of how to become relevant in an Apple/Google world, much like IBM had to in the mid-90's when Microsoft was the 900lb. gorilla on the block.  The problem they have is cost, their server OSes don't come cheap.  Moreover, much of the leaning of their server OS is due in part to the force placed upon it by Linux server (e.g. headless, small OS footprint, fast,...)  In the face of it, they are playing catch-up, following, much like on the tablet.  Their last big success was Xbox-360.  Also keep in mind they have a large legacy of s/w they have to tow along with them, that cost money too.

No. I don't think MS Server 2012 will replace many, if any, Linux Server installs.  Greenfields, they possibly have a good story to tell, for those that are MS shops and willing to pay the big $$$.  But just as a cloud story, I think not, they came late to the game...and it wasn't being played on Xbox.
