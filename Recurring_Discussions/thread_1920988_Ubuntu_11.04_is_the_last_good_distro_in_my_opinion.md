### Post by grahammechanical on 2012-02-05
You do not think that 11.04 was experimental?

It had

1) a user interface intended to run on Gnome 3 but stuck on top of Gnome 2. 

2) A classic mode that was going to be ditched in six months time.

3) Compiz special/enhanced effects that broke the desktop.

4) Lots of people hated 11.04 and said that they were going over to some other distribution.

The new features that you speak of are experimental in the sense that they are still being developed. They are not experimental in the sense that they are being tried/tested with the possibility that they will be discarded if they do not work out.

There is not, and I think never was, any intention of discarding the new features that came in with 11.04. Yes, 11.04 was the start of the changes. Gnome 3 became the desktop environment in 11.10 and Unity is up to version 5.2 in 12.04 development branch.

The experiment continues but there will be no going back, in my opinion. Everything about Linux is an experiment or under development, if you like.

Regards.
