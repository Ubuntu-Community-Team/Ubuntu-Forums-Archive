### Post by Cyclone42 on 2011-12-12
Linux users and advocates have long made fun of Windows Vista with all of its problems.  As of Ubuntu 11.04, such advocates have lost the moral right to do so.  Ubuntu 11.04 has far more problems than Vista could ever hope to have, and 11.10 just continues most of the problems.  The scary part is, a "Long Term Support" release is coming up, which will discontinue support for what has become the "Windows XP" of Ubuntu releases, 10.04.  I have tried, over and over, to give 11.04 and 11.10 a real shot, only to go back to 10.04 every time.

Just a few problems off the top of my head: 

That overlay-scroll-bar thing, makes scrolling more difficult, in the name of prettiness.  An "improvement" should NOT make things more difficult for the user. 

In "Gnome Classic", most icon gadgets have disappeared from the Gnome Main Toolbar in file manager windows, especially the icons for reducing and increasing the view size.  Gnome Classic needs look AND perform EXACTLY like Gnome 2 in EVERY way.

In Gnome Classic, when I add an app to a Gnome panel, and then try to move the icon, the icon stays in place.  If I reboot, the icon appears where I moved it upon the next boot.

You should NOT have to hold the 'alt' key to add stuff to the Gnome panel.

Basically, in almost every way, Ubuntu 11.04 and 11.10 are big steps backward in terms of user-friendliness and ease-of-use.  IMO, the 12.04 release needs to concentrate TOTALLY on removing all the problems, and should have few (if any) "improvements".  Also, support for 10.04 needs to be continued until all of these problems are eliminated, as a lot of people such as myself will continue to use it.

We are more conservative in our package merge with Debian, auto-synching with Debian testing, instead of Debian unstable.
