### Post by admiralspark on 2010-08-12
Haha, nice! I have an old Texas Instruments 486DX, and the only linux I could find to work on it was BasicLinux (also known as BasicLin on google). It has basic email and internet capabilities, and only needs 3mb ram if launched from DOS (and you must have DOS on there). Unfortunately, I don't think you can run a complete X server on it because of the low amount of ram, but I also seem to remember a co-worker of mine mentioning that when she got her computing degrees back with DOS, they also used a now very-old version of Red Hat on lab computers. 

Good luck! And if none of that works, buy a microboard and hide it inside the old case, and run full-blown linux on it!
