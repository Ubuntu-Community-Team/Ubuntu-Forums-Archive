### Post by cloyd on 2010-04-21
Geos was indeed awesome. Yes, it took me twenty minutes to get set up, loading all the programs, fonts, etc. that I needed on one disk before I could really get started. But I loved it. It made the Commodore computer act like something much more sophisticated. Part of me wishes I still could use it. But then, I know that newer OS's are so much better. It is just that the programmers at  Berkley Softworks got a lot of performance out of a pretty limited machine.  There are things I could do with GEOS that I haven't yet figured out how to do with any other operating system.

It had to be good programming to get so much out of limited hardware. If Windows was programmed with half as much skill, it might be worth using. I think this aspect is what has attracted me about Ubuntu and Linux. I am using a netbook with Ubuntu that now performs better than my $800 Windows laptop with a 64 bit version of Vista and 4 gig of RAM. Better programming counts  more than money spent on hardware. I _don't_ program. I've learned a bit of code back in the days of MSDOS, just enough to know that I wrote a lot of "spaghetti" code. 

I did enjoy Geos at the time. I'd probably be bored with it today, but at the time, it was _good_.
Long live Ubuntu!
