### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-08-09
It's simple. Google makes Chrome and gives the source away under the Chromium project. That way they can also receive patches for community users. But because Google does use some proprietary things in Chrome (like a pdf viewer), those things are filtered out before the code is made open-source. Maybe some other things (like call-home features) are also filtered out, who knows?

So if you only trust open source, than you should use Chromium. If you don't care about open or closed source, than Chrome has just a bit more features.
