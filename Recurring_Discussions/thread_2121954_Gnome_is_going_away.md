### Post by ajgreeny on 2013-03-03
Gnome 2,which is what I suspect you mean by gnome, is going, I'm afraid, to be replaced in ubuntu either by unity, gnome-shell, or gnome-classic (see [12.04 LTS Gnome Classic]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1966370")), or of course you can use another desktop entirely such as xfce (Xubuntu) KDE (Kubuntu) or LXDE (Lubuntu).  They all have the same ubuntu base, and you can still install and use any package from the ubuntu repos whatever DE you have.

There are also some newer DEs available, which I have not used yet such as cinnamon and MATE, and as MATE is a fork of gnome 2, you may find that is the one for you.
