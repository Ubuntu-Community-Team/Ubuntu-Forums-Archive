### Post by yeats on 2011-12-10
> Mint VS. Ubuntu?

> I remember I've read somewhere that mint is the linux that has most users which has overtaken ubuntu.

1) It's not a competition.  

2) There is not a way to measure actual distro usage, so I wouldn't take what you "remember [you've] read somewhere" too seriously.

3) If there are more new Mint users, it's probably in reaction to the other major (GNOME-based) distros switching to GNOME3 and (in Ubuntu's case) Unity.  Mint's GNOME3 is tailored to resemble "classic" GNOME and Mint includes [MATE]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MATE_(desktop_environment)"), a GNOME2 fork.

I use Ubuntu on one workstation, Mint 12 on my netbook and Fedora 16 on two other computers.  You can (and should, IMO) use what you like without having to pick sides.

> I just wanna know what the main difference is between ubuntu and mint...

This is well documented on [Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_Mint#Comparison_with_Ubuntu") and elsewhere.

> ...and why ubuntu can be overtaken while it has such a strong community.

You'll find that the Ubuntu community has many Mint and other distro users involved with it too.  Again, there's no need to take sides.

### Post by TBABill on 2011-12-10
To answer the original question, Mint IS Ubuntu. The base system is Ubuntu and for the most part the underlying system of Mint really is Ubuntu with Mint's tools and tweaks added, along with some codecs. In comparison of 11.10 to Mint 12, the desktop environment really is the difference. And Mint gives you all the codecs during install, along with Sun Java. Besides that, most differences are cosmetic and up to personal taste.

I use both. Right now I like Ubuntu better between 11.10 and Mint 12, but previously Mint had the classier desktop in my personal taste when comparing them as they are right after install without additional software or tweaks post-install. But with personal preference being in my entire statement, you may feel the opposite. I'd just try both and pick the one you like.
