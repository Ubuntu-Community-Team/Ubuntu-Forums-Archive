Come on, it's not nearly that bad :D Horses for courses anyway.

Gnome has always mixed together loads of different technologies & runtimes like this, it's one of the things that separates it from KDE, it's more like desktop "glue" than a complete monolithic desktop suite.

Many have criticised that approach in the past (including Linus I seem to recall) but I think more recently we have seen the advantages. Gnome has certainly been more "agile" than KDE I think. We've seen a more steady, consistent progression on all fronts, unlike the major architectural overhauls (and their breakage) with the KDE method.

Each way has it's pros and cons. In any case, using Mono in Gnome - even a lot of it - is not some fundamental architectural issue that could spell doom for the project, because Gnome just isn't built like that.
