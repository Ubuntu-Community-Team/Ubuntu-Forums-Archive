### Post by glaesemann on 2009-03-31
Hello All,

I'm a Linux/Ubuntu beginner with 25 years in PC and Mac IT. I've been messing around with Ubuntu and it dawned on me that this may be a good solution to a nagging problem.

Family and friends turn to me for computer help. When I can, I move the older ones to Mac to avoid the constant Windows updates, Zone Alarm access questions and security issues that scare the hell out of the elderly. Once they get accustomed to OS X they love the ease of use and fast boot-up. For some though the cost is too much to justify the switch.

Most all of the elderly I have assisted with computers do about the same thing. They write a few letters, check their bank accounts on-line, visit a few websites, use web based email and a few use Skype to stay in touch with family far away. Not a heavy load, but frustrating when half of their time is spent dealing with Windows questions, updates, etc.

After using Ubuntu my question for the forum is this:

Is Ubuntu a wise choice for replacing Windows for an elderly person?

For almost all of them I trouble shoot via VNC or other remote method.

All replies are greatly appreciated!

Tim

### Post by perpetualcacophany on 2009-03-31
As long as you set up the computer correctly it should be fine. I got my grandparents to use Ubuntu instead of XP about a week ago. I installed, troubleshot the small problems that arose, and then set up the desktop for ease of use. For all of the programs that they use, I simply put large icons on the desktop for them. That may be a little extreme for some, but it really helped them out.

As long as you are willing to put the initial time into it, and as long as the elderly person you are helping isn't too attached to the way things work in Windows and its programs, you shouldn't have any problem at all getting them to love Ubuntu.
