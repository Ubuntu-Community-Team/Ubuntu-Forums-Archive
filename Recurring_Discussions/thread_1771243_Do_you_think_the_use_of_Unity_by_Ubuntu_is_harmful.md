### Post by flick on 2011-05-30
No, I don't think it harms the Linux community at large. 

Canonical/Ubuntu are striving to make a mainstream accessible desktop, they disagree with some of the decisions made for Gnome Shell, so they made their own graphical shell for their desktop. Last I checked, Unity is free software ( GNU GPL v3, GNU LGPL v3 ) so the greater Linux community is free to study it, modify it, etc. 

Canonical does not have the same level of interest in the kernel and other such "guts of the system" pieces as, say, Red Hat. Unity is one way for Canonical to share and give back to the community with something they are good at/interested in : user experience/GUI design.

I'm no rabid Unity fanboy, but it took me less than a week to get comfortable with it. If I absolutely hated it, I'd probably have used Xubuntu, or maybe Kubuntu, or maybe just installed Fluxbox or Openbox and used that.

I had read a lot of good things about Gnome Shell, but when I tried to install it in Ubuntu 11.04, everything went even further down hill.

I love Ubuntu, and I hope to come back to it down the road, but until some things change I am staying away.

I am just saddened because when I began using Ubuntu over four years ago, I chose ti because of the philosophy behind it. I am sad to say that the distro has somewhat drifted away from the original philosophy, and seems to have more concern for other thigns than the users fo the system.

I appreciate the great deal of help I have received from this community, and I hope that one day I can once again be a part of it.

My best wishes to everyone.
