### Post by linux411 on 2009-12-21
IMHO, running Linux since '93, there have been a lot of discussions about which desktop is best. The only way to find out is to try 'em. After all, Linux is a free system as are the desktops and window managers. That said, you can try different DM's and WM's until your heart is content. That is the beauty of Linux. You don't have to take what they (MS) give you. You can change and alter and adjust until your heart is content. Personal thought : GNOME is simplistic and geared toward those familiar with Windows XP / 2000. KDE 3.5 is intermediate for those familiar with Linux. KDE 4.X has come into it's own and is more simplistic than it's predecessor and has a familiarity to Windows Vista. Or does Windows Vista have a familiarity to KDE 4.X? :lolflag:

In regards to specific distros? Canonical has brought Debian into the forefront of Linux. I'm sure Ian Murdock is happy about the attention! The mention of Slackware, however, compels me to say, it is advanced, but WELL worth the learning curve. Before using Ubuntu this trip, I had Slackware versions on my system for about 4 years. I just love tarballs! :lolflag:

Linux is choice. Choice is freedom. You have the ability to drive before you "buy".
