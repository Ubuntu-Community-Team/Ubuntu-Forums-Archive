So, the other day I had an interview over skype. 

I just decided that I would rather use XP than take a chance with ubuntu.

If you see my link [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1317346&highlight=skype"), you can see I have had a lot of problems with skype. Nevertheless I have pretty much managed to resolve them and for the most part I am able to have conversations on skype with various people (even with video chat).

I am a very "loyal" ubuntu user and I NEVER use windows except in emergencies (e.g. an app doesn't work in ubuntu or wine). However, in this case as I had an interview I felt I couldn't "trust" the ubuntu version of skype. The following things have failed when using the ubuntu version of skype (in Karmic):

1. CPU usage goes up to a 100% and the computer slows down.
2. Numerous sound problems, such as the microphone (which is built-in to my webcam) becomes disabled.
3. The video becomes unclear or "choppy"
4. The controls from the skype window disappears.
5. Skype crashes

When I am chatting with friends and such problems occur I don't mind taking a minute to fix them. In this case I had an important interview and I felt that there was no way I could risk something failing. Therefore, I ended up using Windows.

For the most part I find Windows buggy and not very user-friendly. I am sure Windows 7 has improved a lot (after all XP is about eight years old) but I am happy using Ubuntu. But, until developers are able to polish their apps to the extent that they do in Windows it becomes difficult to use Ubuntu for serious computing.

I know that the problems with Skype aren't really the fault of the Ubuntu distribution or it's creators, but, at the end of the day my choice is Windows or Ubuntu and for work that cannot afford any "hiccups" I will never use Ubuntu.

Anyway just my 2 cents
