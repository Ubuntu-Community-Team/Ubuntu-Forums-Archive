[IMG]http://img.techpowerup.org/071229/Screenshot.png[/IMG]


Here are my observations in reference to the top 4 big guns, Ubuntu, Fedora, OpenSuse and PCLinuxOS.

Over the past 12 months, Ubuntu has been the only distro to lose popularity over all categories of 12, 6, 3 and 1 month comparisons.

Fedora has had a good year across the 12, 6 and 3 month categories. But for some reason dropped slightly in the last month. I suspect that will change as Fedora 8 seeps into the community.

OpenSuse has not really performed as good as I would have expected this year. As both Gnome and KDE releases of 10.3 were solid releases and well-worthy of an install.

PCLinuxOS has had a ripper of a year and has edged out Ubuntu from the #1 spot in 3 of the 4 categories. Overall after 12 months, Ubuntu remains #1. But PCLinux not being far behind Ubuntu at 2495 and 2534, respectively.

My preditions for 2008:
Ubuntu will still be popular as it has been for several years now. But I don't think it'll remain #1. I'm not saying it won't get #1 again, but I don't think it will be in 2008.

Fedora will have a good year and seems to be getting more popular all the time. And as each release gets more polished and more stable, it's got to be doing something right.

OpenSuse. Well, I was wrong about them for 2007, so I don't think I can tell what's going to happen with the in 2008. I suspect it'll be much the same as this year. Stable. Still, OpenSuse remains one of my favs on my personal list of tux distros.

PCLinuxOS will be a strong contender in 2008. I think it'll be a good tough battle between Fedora nd PCLinuxOS in 2008.
