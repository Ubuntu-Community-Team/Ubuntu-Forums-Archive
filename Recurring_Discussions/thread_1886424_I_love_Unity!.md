### Post by Ubun2to on 2012-07-01
I think Unity is the BEST! I have tried all the DEs I have come across. Here are my thoughts on the ones I liked the most, besides Unity:
Gnome 3: Easy access to applications, but it also lists every program/executable on the hard drive, some of which have low res icons, so it ends up looking awful. No such thing as multitasking. No minimize/maximize buttons. No real superbar integration.
Gnome 2/Gnome Classic: Boring. Easy to access apps, and not every single executable item on the hard drive is listed-just the real applications. Application menu bar only displays on 1 monitor (and it doesn't let me choose which one I want; but this is good when I use Seamless mode in VirtualBox). No superbar integration. Has minimize/maximize buttons. You don't have access to all 4 workspaces when you have multiple monitors.
KDE Plasma: Cool widgets, beautiful interface. Lots of lag and long load time-a serious drag on productivity. Application list makes for easy access. Applications take much longer to load here than on any other DE. Too similar to Windows for me to want to use it.
XFCE: Basic, quick, but buggy for me (I probably got a bad download file or something like that). I can see it being useful for low power computers, but it doesn't utilize modern graphics cards to their full potentials.
Cinnamon: Seems like they have tried to copy Lozedoze. Slow for me. Good design elements.
Now, here is what I like about Unity:
Easy to use. Beautiful interface. 12.04 stopped the default action from being Hide-I like that. Unity Quicklists are GREAT! They make it so easy to access items (like I have my VMs on the quicklist for VirtualBox). Loads quickly. No application list. I like the superbar integration. The ALT key brings up a command entry point-super useful for clearing browsing data in Chrome if I feel too lazy to click on the menus. Also make it easier to find specific functions rather than digging around in menus (super helpful in apps like LibreOffice, where there are hundreds of options). Unity launcher can be on both monitors-and it's easily changable via the Displays tool. Workspace switcher gives me 4 workspaces even though I have 2 monitors. I can easily change things via MyUnity.
