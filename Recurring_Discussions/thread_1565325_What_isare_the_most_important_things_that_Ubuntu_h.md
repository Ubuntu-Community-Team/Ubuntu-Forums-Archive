> **NightwishFan said:**

Not Gnome, nor any other part of the upstream development of Linux is given much at all by Canonical.

At this stage, contributing to the Linux community is not what Canonical can afford to do. The first priority is the development/establishment of the Ubuntu brand distro. 

What they do primarily, is repackage, represent & market Linux via the Ubuntu label, in an ever increasingly more palatable way. Canonical is at least superior to the average bunch of marketroids, in that they do actually contribute & make genuine changes to their product. 

A product which has as one of its primary focuses that of being particularly attractive for new Linux users. Which Ubuntu undeniably succeeds in doing, & this is certainly no mean feat. 

This aforementioned feat is in my opinion all but completely reliant on the Ubuntu forums, due to its content, the speed of reply due to the number of users & of course due to the way it is moderated, which basically gets rid of the trolls which of course contain the element of arrogant elitists, immature idiots, drunks & the other poor unfortunate closed minded people.

What Ubuntu essentially is, imho, is other peoples work with a few additions here & there that enhance the goal of presenting a repackaged Linux in a new format that most effectively hits the target audience(s) as there are currently three targets on my radar. (I expect that there are targets that are still beyond my radars range at this point.)

Canonical have demonstrated that they are not truly interested in supporting the other *buntu versions extant. Whether that changes as they inevitably become more wealthy remains to be seen? I expect that they will, hopefully for the communities sake I'm correct on that one.

Marketing Ubuntu to corporate entities & offering paid for support is certainly a Canonical priority. As obviously the desire is for Ubuntu to not only pay for its upkeep but for it to become as profitable as possible. As that is unfortunately in my opinion what businesses do (this is a very complex topic which we are really not allowed to talk about here anymore.)

As Ubuntu becomes more profitable I expect that Canonical will give more back to the Linux community, as the founders know that Ubuntu is completely reliant on & owes its existence all but completely to the work of others outside of the Canonical corporation. [**_As my previous post in this thread demonstrated._**]("http://www.geek.com/articles/news/the-linux-foundation-details-who-contributes-to-linux-development-20090821/")  For those that say that is an old set of statistics, if you have a search you will find that the more current ones are even less favourable to Canonical.

Thus far Canonical has done nothing more than packaging, presentation & marketing of code that has been created by many other people outside of the Canonical enterprise, with very few exceptions. (Don't get me wrong, they have done a ridiculous amount of work, & great work at that.)

It really is false to think that Canonical/Ubuntu have done more for Linux than to add to its popularity due to its revamped presentation of Linux via multi-levelled marketing & by supplying the huge support base that exists in the Ubuntu forums that is freely available to all & sundry.

At this point in time, Canonical/Ubuntu have made a huge contribution to the Linux community by making Linux more well known & increasing the user base more than any other distro has been able to do. As previously stated, the presentation of Ubuntu & the multi-level marketing are responsible for this & an enormous amount of credit goes to this forum, as without the support that comes out of the UF, Ubuntu would just be another great distro that sits a few notches from the number one position on the DistroWatch top 100.
