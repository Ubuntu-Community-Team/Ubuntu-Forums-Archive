viruses needs to be written to target a given platform, so windows viruses cannot directly attack or effect Linux/Ubuntu.

There have been viruses written to target Linux but there are none in the wild currently, and if there was there is no AV solution which would combat them anyways.

So for the time being you are "safe" as far as virus threats are concerned, however viruses are a very small part of overall security in general, infact their payloads are often nuisance based and not a overall security concern anyways in terms of a system compromise

Read here to get a good grounding [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BasicSecurity](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BasicSecurity)

As for faster, better looking, stable that depends on your hardware, your skillset and your own perception.

Try a live CD to test it out first to see if you like it.
