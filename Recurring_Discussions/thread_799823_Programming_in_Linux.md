### Post by graham-cracker on 2008-05-19
First off, what do you want to do? I find it's always helpful to have a goal when trying to learn some sort of programming language or library (that way you know where to go, and when to stop).

That being said, I agree with Joeb454 in that python is a great place to start [http://docs.python.org/tut/](http://docs.python.org/tut/)

I starting learning it less than a year ago (as a way to play around with the one-laptop-per-child Sugar UI) and have started using it for everything from robotics to data analysis to gui (gtk-based) applications. I started off with the tutorial and found that it was a great help.

As far as the programs I use to program, I just use gedit and a terminal.

### Post by rajan on 2008-05-19
this forum is a really good place to find really helpful people, but you absolutely have to have a specific question. this means that you're going to have to struggle through the basics and any vague questions you may have. then when you get to a problem that you can't figure out even though you've put a lot of effort into it, you can take the easy road and ask the community. if it's obvious that you've put a lot of work into it, people are much more likely to help you out. i've personally been floored at how helpful the ubuntuforums are in my programming needs. they're much better than any of the other places i've found for my intermediate-level problems. 

in answer to your other questions, i think that html is a good place to start. you can really pick it up easily and it will get you used to the linux platform. after that i would say shell scripting and then some python would also be really nice tools for you to have. good luck.
