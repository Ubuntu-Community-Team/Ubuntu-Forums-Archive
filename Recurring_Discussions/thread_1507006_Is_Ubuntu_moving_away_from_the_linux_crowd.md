### Post by Dragonbite on 2010-06-11

Yes, Ubuntu may be moving this way and there is absolutely nothing with it.  This is what it needs to do to move towards its goals and for its target users.

That is the beauty of open source and Linux; it doesn't have to be "everything for everybody".  One would not ask Android to be the best high-end-supercomputer-calculator, nor is Solaris the ideal choice for a smartphone.

While just about all distributions can be made into anything (including the Solaris smartphone and Android super-computer) with enough customizing, the better method is to decide what the system will be used for and determine which distribution is focused the same, or "drops you off" closer to your goal so you have less to do to make it into the system you want it to be.

I applaud Mark Shuttleworth and Ubuntu for making the tough decisions (style, integrating social networking, moving buttons, UbuntuOne & Music Store, easy proprietary drivers, etc.) and focusing on the "user experience".  For their target/goal that is what they need to do and Ubuntu is a wonderful distribution towards that end.

I also applaud Fedora's focus and passion for open source, and the work they do trickles down into just about every Linux distribution (network manager, etc.).  While I may admire their FOSS work I also know that in some cases I am not able to follow their philosophy completely but that makes me admire it no less.
