I have lost a lot of respect for the Gnome team. (Not over this).

### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-04-13
[QUOTE=Linuxratty]I actually saw on OMG Ubuntu someone advocating tabs must die..Really? Don't like tabs,dude,don't use um...But don't tell me I can't use tabs.[/QUOTE]
Yeah, that's pretty absurd. Tabs in web and file browsers and other MIDs were first implemented on systems that used conventional taskbars. It's fairly obvious from that that they serve different purposes than the taskbar, even if the two resemble one another. I guess the alternative is either an iOS-style Exposé-within-the-browser deal or separate windows for everything. 

I'm indifferent to tabs in most applications - say, Nautilus - but they're necessary in web browsers, and anything document-related really *ought* to have them, or would be better for it. I wouldn't at all mind seeing them become *more* ubiquitous. I like sorting related things in stacks.
