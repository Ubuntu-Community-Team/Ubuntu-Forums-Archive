### Post by haqking on 2011-11-08
There is no such thing as a MUST HAVE app.

There is a WHAT DO YOU NEED TO DO app though ;-)

Dont blindly install anything because people say you need it, you may end up with unwanted services and such installed and running on your machine.

Linux assumes you know what you are doing and what you want, be careful what you ask it for.

Find out what you need to do and achieve, then ask about the apps for those purposes.

Always beware of the possible effects it may have on your machine, such as placing a listening service on a port for example.

### Post by dniMretsaM on 2011-11-09
> **JoshuaMiller0 said:**
> I do not quite understand what FFmpeg does... please help :)

It's a converter for audio, video, and images. It can go from audio to audio, video to video, video to audio, images to video, video to images, etc. etc. It can resize, change the aspect ratio, and change frame rates on videos. It also does screen casting (desktop recording), stream media, and play media. It pretty much does everything. See [http://ffmpeg.org/](http://ffmpeg.org/).__
