### Post by cwsnyder on 2012-10-08

Epiphany is still the best browser if you need to communicate with a Microsoft Windows server based site.

### Post by 1clue on 2012-10-08
I've tried most of the Linux-based browsers out there, I keep coming back to firefox.  I have not tried Chrome on any platform.

I don't trust Google with my information.  They're in the information harvesting business and there's no telling what they get from Chrome.  Frankly it doesn't have to be what's in the browser as much as what they ship off to a Google server at the first opportunity.  Google publicly acknowledges that they have never deleted a search submitted to their engines, and that they sell that information.  What they don't seem to mention is that law enforcement occasionally gets a search warrant in order to get enough information to figure out who made the search.  It needs to be combined with information from your ISP of course, but if they have one warrant they can get the other at the same time.

I'm using an Android phone, which is also certainly shipping info off to both Google and to my carrier.  No escaping that no matter what you do, even iPhone does all its speech recognition on an Apple server somewhere, and shipping off a bunch of other things too.  I can't tell you how many people I've seen talking to Siri in socially unacceptable ways, not realizing that all that data is being collected somewhere.
