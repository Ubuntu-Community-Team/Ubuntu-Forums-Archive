### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-10
Above all, this Forum is a support forum. So, consider this:

You were as a technical support guy for Widget Inc. and your job is to answer the phones and help people with their problems. You will notice that, by the end of the day, almost every single call is negative. 

Should your response to this be: "Most people who use this product are frustrated with it" or "Most people that call tech support are frustrated with their product"?

There is a concept known as the "silent majority" that was popularized by Richard Nixon. It refers to the fact that the majority of people remain silent about issues, in reference to political issues, it is referring to those who doesn't hold protests or strikes or do other things that grab the media's attention. Those that do are referred to as the "vocal minority" because they are a small portion of society that are over-represented in the media.

The same principle applies here. 

Those that do post are likely either going to be very positive or very negative. They are going to have strong opinions and be more than willing to share them. So you aren't getting the whole picture. A large majority of Ubuntu users don't post to these forums about how great or terrible Ubuntu is, they just use it. 

In the same way, most Windows users don't post about Windows on the Internet all the time. Those that do are very vocal, but are very much in the minority.
