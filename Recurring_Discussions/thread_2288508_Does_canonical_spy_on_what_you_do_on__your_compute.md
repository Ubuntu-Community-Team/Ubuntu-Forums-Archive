### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-07-28
Seriously, though, your question seems unrelated to OCD.

It seems unlikely that thousands of volunteers and Open Source enthusiasts would support a system that actively spies upon it's users...including upon their own activities.
Occam's razor would suggest that spying assumption is, therefore, invalid.

In the single case from 2012 where Canonical did indeed sell dash search query data (not all keypresses nor all data) to Amazon, user information was not included. You could search for, say, 'condoms', and Amazon would return appropriate results (via Canonical) to you, but Amazon would not know who or where you were unless you selected an offered product and then logged into Amazon. This shopping service, by the way, is still included with Ubuntu, turned off by default in response to user feedback.
