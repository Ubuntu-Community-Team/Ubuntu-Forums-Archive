### Post by Bartender on 2009-05-14
It's funny that so many people decide they don't like the brown look and ditch Ubuntu.  You can change the desktop to anything you like by adding your own pictures to "Wallpaper". I often keep a small folder in /home>Pictures with just wallpaper pictures in the folder so that the OS and I know where to find them.
Or download something cool.  I've foraged thru the choices at gnomelook and picked a few.
Then go into System>Preferences.  Click on "Appearance".  It'll open to the "Themes" tab.  The default Theme is "Human", I believe.  Click on "Customize".  Click on "Colors" tab.  Click on the colored box under "Background" column, to the right of "Selected items:".  Spin that color wheel to whatever color you like.  Click "OK".
Now back out and see what you think.
With just a couple of easy steps you can make most of the brown go away.  Play with fonts, try some of the other themes in Appearance.  Or you can really dig into this and download different themes, icons, etc.
