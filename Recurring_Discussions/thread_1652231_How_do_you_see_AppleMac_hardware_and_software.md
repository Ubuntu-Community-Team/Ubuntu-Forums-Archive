### Post by Spr0k3t on 2010-12-24
I've been baited.

Hardware:
Since I handle repairs for apple products for when the local "geniuses" can't figure out how to work on them, I see a lot of the really severe problems.  From that, most of what I've seen the craftsmanship of the macs are horrid.  They use low quality caps, glued down cabling, poor air-flow design, one-way "fixed position" screens, and extremely high dollar replacement parts for lower than average items (DeskStar hard drives, crappy scroll cage fans, and very low grade capacitors you would expect on products like audiovox).

Software:
The OS feels like a toy, not much more.  I can't change some of the aspects of the operating system to the way I would want to use them.  With the laptops, the touch-pad drives me insane that I have to plug in an external mouse to use it for what I want.  Applications always hide unless specifically told to quit (the memory managment for that is fantastic though).  There are some innovations in the OS I do think need to be praised... but outside of serious desktop-video/publishing or rigid cut audio work, everything else can be done on any other system.

Company:
Two-faced to their customers offering "the greatest thing since sliced bread" when it's nothing more than just sliced bread cut thinner or thicker.  The company in my opinion is no better or worse than Microsoft, or big oil for that matter.  Fat cats getting fatter off the lower and middle wage classes.  There's a problem when a company has a multi-billion dollar fine and can still report record profits.

Will I ever own an apple product?  Not a chance.  I would sooner throw money on the street to some unknown begger who does nothing but purchase drugs to get their fix.

Hardware is nice looking and seems to work well, generally speaking, though my three-and-a-half-year-old macbook just stopped turning on one day. That was a really bad day. A year later, it started to work again. The whole thing was weird and bad. The optical drive was always a pile and behaved strangely, though it usually works. and it shipped new with a dead pixel in the display. It has always run very hot, and the fan is extremely loud. I get dirty looks in class when it turns on. I don't care though. I just laugh. For the price, however, these things should not be. When I installed Ubuntu on it, the hardware caused all kinds of weird problems.

Software seems to be top-of-the-line, if not always as customizable as I would like. I think Apple is probably the best software company out there today. Unfortunately, it costs money, and that's something I don't have a lot of. I put Ubuntu on my macbook because they came out with a new version of OSX, and some of my most important software (not from mac) stopped getting updates for the version I had. I couldn't afford the update, so I was stuck.

Bottom line: When my Mac broke, I bought a $400 Compaq, cheapest computer in the store. I installed Ubuntu, and it worked perfectly from the beginning (ok, I had to add on line to a config file to get the headphones to work, but no biggy). Windows 7 Home Premium was actually very nice and functional as well, but it didn't come with an office suit, and the bloat eventually drove me insane, so I removed it altogether.

This computer does everything my mac ever did, and it does it better (though it is several years newer, and running the same software, so that's a given). The hardware seems a lot cheaper, and certainly isn't as sexy, but I haven't had a problem with anything at all in the first year, and I got a two year warranty from the dealer. It runs much cooler, and the fan is quiet. All of the pixels work. I've upgraded my OS twice since then at no cost.

It's not to say that apple is bad, and I think the software is actually very good, but I personally achieve better results at less than half the price with Ubuntu and a cheap system.

As a point of reference, I mainly use my computer to playback media, surf the Internet, write papers for grad school, use some Bible study software (that's my field of study). I've also done a tiny bit of video editing on it, but I'm not seriously into that. Ubuntu is more secure and nearly as stable as OSX for all of these things.

If I ever have extra money, wouldn't rule-out getting another mac, but I have trouble thinking of a reason to do so. By contrast, I'm sure I will never be without a Linux system.
