### Post by The Llama on 2011-08-31
> **SavageWolf said:**
> Also, Chrome has good security features...

It doesn't have a startup password option. If you're using a shared computer, your administrator can easily go into your Google-chrome hidden folder (In App-data on windows) copy and paste the folder to their account and use your version of Chrome, with your cookies, your history, your bookmarks ext. Firefox however does not allow this, it must encrypt the data. You may say that it's not the browser's job to provide security features but I'm not going to make a new account for each of my friends if they come over and want to use the computer... There is no option to not remember history, there is no option to Discard cookies after a set period of time, which you can easily edit in Firefox's About:Config

Basically, Chrome is good if you work only on your own computer, don't allow anybody else to use it and aren't too fussed about having cookies from every site you visit remain on your computer for as long as they like (I know you can block cookies but there should be time option too).
