### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-07-13
Hey ... Good Luck in your "fight" -- you'll need it.

You knew the PC came with Windows 7 BEFORE you bought it, right?  Did you see if the PC supplier had any option available for you to buy the bare-bones PC (e.i., without any OS installed)?

If they did have such an option and you didn't explore it -- you're simply out of luck. In such a case, they are under NO obligation to refund you anything.

As to the concept of "voting with your wallet", while in an open marketplace, that is actually a very sound idea, in one dominated almost entirely by one supplier (sometimes called a "virtual monopoly", it doesn't accomplish anything.

Case in point -- saw something the other day claiming that MS has sold on the order of 400 MILLION licenses of Windows 7.  So, even if everyone in the Linux community REFUSED to by a Windows PC, that would still not even make a noticeable dent in that 400 million.
