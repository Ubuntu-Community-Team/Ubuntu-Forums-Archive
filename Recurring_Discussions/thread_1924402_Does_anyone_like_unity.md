### Post by BigSilly on 2012-02-13
I love Unity personally. I've had to eat my hat recently, after decrying the loss of the dodge windows function in 12.04. I really liked that functionality, and thought it was just insane to remove it. I imagined it would just be a deal breaker for me. However, I decided to try a daily build the other day and see how it's looking, see what the Ubuntu team have in mind for the new Unity, and see what I think. And I have to admit Mark S is absolutely right to insist that the Unity bar is always visible. They've added in the option to shrink the icons a bit too, so that makes it even better.

Before in 11.10 I would trigger the bar appearing whenever I accidentally missed the back button on the browser. This becomes a bit of a pain, and many have remarked on this problem too. Having the bar consistent means this type of thing never happens, and there's always the Unity tool handy when you need it. One of the things I've found a wee bit tiresome over time with Gnome Shell is the constant use of the overview function. With Unity, there's none of this. It's all there, clear and easy to use.

So yes, I like it. :)
