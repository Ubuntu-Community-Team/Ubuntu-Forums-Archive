> **mrgnash said:**

Well, that's a personal choice of course, but it seems a little extreme to me -- and a position that is vulnerable to hypocrisy, considering that most (all?) alternative providers for similar services also have their own objectives. Canonical is certainly for promoting open source, but [Launchpad is not GPL'ed yet](https://answers.launchpad.net/launchpad/+faq/195), and the forum software used by these forums is proprietary (with source available, if not able to be freely distributed).

We don't live in a black-and-white world, despite what some people would like to think. It's not so easy to just say "This company is all bad"' and "This company is all good." We live in a world of interdependence. Many companies that do "evil" in the world contribute to charities and non-profits that do a lot of "good" in the world. As a matter of fact, I can't think of many charities and non-profits that do not, in some way, rely on contributions from philanthropists who may have earned their money in industries that have also been questionable.

From what I've seen, Google, more than any other major search engine, can be trusted with our privacy. Now, privacy can be trusted only so much (obviously, if you're that paranoid, just don't use the internet at all--seriously), but I'm saying that when you compare Google to its peers, it is the company that is going to at least [try to resist or actually resist a US government subpoena for your searches and information. Yahoo!, MSN Search, and AOL will hand over your information right away without a fight at all.](http://www.nytimes.com/2006/01/26/technology/26privacy.html)

Now, as for lock-in, well, that's up to you. If you use GMail, there may be a certain degree of lock-in, as all your email messages will be in your GMail account, and that's the email address all your friends and family will know to reach you at. I don't use GMail. I use my own personal email account not tied to a commercial entity. But using Google's search engine or news aggregator won't lock you in. At any point, you can switch to another search engine or news aggregator. It's not like switching operating systems or even switching web browsers. You just visit a different webpage.
