### Post by frup on 2008-07-04
Since Lindows was begun, re-branded as Linspire and until they sold Linux has been ever improving. Desktop Linux is not futile.

- In that time we have seen KDE and Gnome improve A LOT.
- Wine, something Lindows initially cared a lot about has improved a lot (and has a long way to go, but the future looks good for now).
- Virtualisation has gained the potential to be main stream even on the Desktop. (It kind of is already main stream with Linux users, especially distro-hoppers and testers)
- Hardware support has become amazing in juxtaposition to where it use to be.
- Asus eeePC, Everex, Dell. etc.
- Apricot, Big Buck Bunny & Elephants Dream... :)

What is futile is trying to compete with Microsoft pretending the half-baked version of Linux you a shipping is windows with the only feature being a flawed, subscription based software installation system where your more direct competitors (e.g Ubuntu) offer the same thing for free (In terms of end result)

I hope the acquisition of Linspire results in a Xandros/Linspire Hybrid that is an improvement. There is potential there to really compete with Ubuntu, for those that don't mind patent covenants, proprietary technology by default etc.
