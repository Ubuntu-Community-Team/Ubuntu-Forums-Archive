### Post by mdsmedia on 2008-12-22
There's no accounting for taste. The beauty is that you can change it in any way you like.

Find your favorite picture and upload it as your background, play with the transparency so that your picture is always behind your panels and windows. Then, if you don't like the colors you only have yourself to blame.

Personally, I prefer the color scheme to that of Kubuntu. I find the color scheme comforting after spending any time in Windows.

It's all about taste, and as for Gnome-Look, while there are lots of nice themes there, you don't HAVE TO have a ready made theme. Change the theme to what you like.

### Post by meho_r on 2008-12-22
A theme suggestion: search at gnomelook for "shiki" theme. It's fantastic theme with four color schemes: orange, red, green and blue, a combination of light and dark themes. You may try also Slickness if you like dark themes, or Dust which is very nice too.

Beside that, you may add a picture (or a small sample, transparent, semi-transparent or opaque for additional effects) as background of your panels too (right click on panel > Properties > Background). The possibilities are limitless. But changing distro because of theme...hmmm...
