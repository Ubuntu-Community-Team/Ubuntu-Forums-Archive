### Post by chessnerd on 2009-08-09
I've had mixed experiences with Linux users and Linux fans and I've come to conclude that, like all groups, there is no cookie-cutter for its members. Each person on this forum is different and has different opinions about Linux, Windows, and Mac and each person has a right to express those opinions openly.

That being said, I do thing that Windows bashing is counter productive and happens too often. (The number of times I've seen "Windoze" appear on this forum is far to frequent for my taste.) So I now present, for your edification and enjoyment, a widget analogy about product bashing:

Let's say you buy widgets from the massive Widgets-R-Us corporation. You enjoy your widgets and while you don't think of them as the best widgets in the world, you like using them in spite of having a few problems. One day you meet a person who buys their widgets from the Widget Hut, a much smaller company that you've never even heard of. You begin talking about your widgets and, upon revealing that your widgets come from Widgets-R-Us the Widget Hut fan goes on a long rant about how Widgets-R-Us widgets are poorly made, more expensive, and constantly have problems and that Widget Hut widgets are better in every single way.

Two things could happen: 
1. You are offended because, while you have had a few problems with your widgets they don't constantly fail on you and you feel like this man is insulting you personally because he hates your widgets. 
2. You think about his point and buy some Widget Hut widgets. They are much cheaper, but that makes you suspicious that they might also be lower quality. Soon, you realize that these widgets, instead of coming fully made as a one-size-fits-all widget are some-assembly-required and, once you get them built, you don't see them as measurably better. They do almost everything that your other widgets do, but do so in a weird way that you aren't used to. Also, they have a few problems but, unlike your Widgets-R-Us widgets, you don't know how to fix them without going to others for help. Each problem makes you more and more angry until you finally decide that the Widget Hut fan was just an overzealous Widgets-R-Us hater. You throw out your Widget Hut widgets and go back to Widgets-R-Us to get a fresh supply.

Either way, the bashing of widgets doesn't help anything.

In the same way, bashing Windows doesn't make people want to switch. What you need to do is explain the benefits of Linux without saying that Windows is bad. Don't say that Windows is unsafe, say that Linux is secure. Don't say that Windows is expensive, say that Linux is free. And, most importantly, don't downplay any problems that Linux has. 

If someone asks whether or not Linux will work with their computer, say honestly that Linux works with most computers, but it might have problems with newer hardware. If you lie and say that Linux works with everything Windows does and some of their computer hardware is too new for Hardy they'll become angry at Linux and feel like it's crappy software. 

Finally, a really good way to make a person's transition to Linux better is to offer to help people who you convince to switch over. Don't just give them an install CD, point them to ubuntuforums.org, and say "Have fun." Help them install, configure, and work out all the major kinks in Linux so they won't start out feeling overwhelmed and frustrated. Then, offer them some tech support either via e-mail, phone, or in person so they won't jump ship at the first sign of trouble.
