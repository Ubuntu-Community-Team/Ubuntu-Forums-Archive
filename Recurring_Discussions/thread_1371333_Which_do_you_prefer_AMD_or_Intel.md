### Post by slooksterpsv on 2010-01-04
AMD - One of my reasons is this little story:

My brother and I bought a computer that was an Intel Celeron 2.5GHz from a store - cleaned it up, installed more RAM (had 128MB initially, we upgraded it to 640MB DDR 266 (128+512)) - it ran a bit better - we ran scans on it, removed bloatware, installed new HDD, added video card, etc.

I had a computer that was an AMD Duron 1.1GHz with 384MB RAM PC133 40GB HDD, etc. etc. The AMD ran laps over the Intel (granted both are low-end low class processors). I could run games at higher FPS, same video card, same OS, etc. The AMD just ran so smooth, it was the best little computer.

My brother and I ran UT 2003 on both computers, one level made the celeron computer lag so much its FPS was about 5-6, my AMD it's FPS were about 17-18.

Anyways, other than that, the price, the value, the speed, the everything. AMD had the first x64 64-bit processor for Desktops; it's able to keep up with Intel even though it has lower cache, slower GHz, relative FSB, HyperTransport Technology is just awesome too.

Intel, price is what turns me away from them, also they've had a lot of law suits against them for paying PC makers to use their CPUs, and just lots of bad karma.
