### Post by trinitydan on 2011-01-02
[SIZE=2]I fear this might be doomed to the recurring discussions section,  but it seems that it needs pointing out.  Lately I have seen posts where people are saying things like Linux is floundering, or that it won't gain popularity for some reason or another.  I thought it was time for a reality check.  Linux is the fastest growing computer OS.[/SIZE]

> The trend is very slightly downward for Windows.  It went from 93.66%  in January, 2009 to 92.21% – a drop of 1.45%.  At that rate it would  take Microsoft 30 years to drop below 50% market share.  But that rate  won’t continue.
  The trend is upwards for OS/X, 4.71% in January to 5.11% – an  increase of 0.4%.  And its upwards for Linux, 0.90% in January to 1.02% –  an increase of 0.12%.
  Another way to look at this:
  
[LIST]
[*]Microsoft lost 1.6% of their market share
[*]Apple gained 8.5%
[*]Linux gained 13%             [See entire article]("http://tedwise.com/2010/01/21/its-market-share-time-again-whos-winningwindows-of-course/")
[/LIST]
[FONT=Arial,Helvetica][SIZE=3][SIZE=1]
[/SIZE][/SIZE][/FONT]>  The [W3S stats]("http://www.w3schools.com/browsers/browsers_os.asp") for last month (June) show the **Linux market share at 4.8%**.  Sure, it's not 100% accurate but it's nevertheless impressive. The  stats only include internet-connected computers (desktops only - servers  are not included in these stats) but since there is a fairly big number  of computer using Linux which are not connected to the internet, the  number may actually be pretty close to the actual Linux market share.

This is a 0.3% increase since  last month which even though it might not seem like much, comparing it  with the total Linux market share is actually a lot!
           [See entire article]("http://www.webupd8.org/2010/07/linux-market-share-at-record-high.html")[FONT=Arial,Helvetica][SIZE=3][SIZE=1]
[SIZE=2]
Sure we are still a small minority, but that will not last.  The market share reports aren't even accurate.  They do not take into account the many computers purchased with Windows that get Linux installed on them.  I personally installed Ubuntu on five computers in 2010, but not all of them will be counted and each of them came with a licensed copy of Windows.  For reasons like this I believe the figures are skewed.  In spite of this Linux continues to dominate as the fastest growing computer OS.[/SIZE]
[/SIZE][/SIZE][/FONT]
