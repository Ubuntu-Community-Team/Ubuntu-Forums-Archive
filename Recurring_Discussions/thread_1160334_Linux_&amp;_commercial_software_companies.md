### Post by aysiu on 2009-05-15
It really is just a simple matter of supply/demand and potential profit.

Usually it's a combination of commercial software companies thinking that there are virtually no Linux users (or that Linux users only pirate commercial software) and some vocal Linux users proclaiming that do not want their Free systems contaminated with proprietary "crap."

I don't really think WMP and WinAmp are beautiful, but the GUI interface should have nothing to do with porting applications. Opera and Skype are both closed source and have both ported their applications to Linux.

The wide variety of distros has nothing to do with it. If a commercial company were too lazy to compile multiple binaries, they could just release a Ubuntu .deb and a Fedora .rpm and get very few complaints.
