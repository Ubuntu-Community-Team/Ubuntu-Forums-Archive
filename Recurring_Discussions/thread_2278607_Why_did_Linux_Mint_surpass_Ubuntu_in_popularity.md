### Post by ian-weisser on 2015-05-18
> **eric-dunbar said:**
> I'm trying to figure out why that  is (lots of positive press for Linux Mint). There was a time where Ubuntu was nearly the only Linux distro mentioned in the popular press

Human psychology. Humans tend to view new, small organizations more positively.
New projects seems cooler, more interesting, scrappier. It's easier to read our dreams into them, to pre-judge them positively.
There's a common phrase in English for it: Everyone loves the underdog. It's true. Journalists too.

Six years ago, Ubuntu was new and scrappy and an underdog and trying to make a popular Linux desktop. And it got a lot of great press for it. But 'new' only lasts so long. Ubuntu aged and grew out of 'new-and-scrappy'.

Today, Mint is new and scrappy and an underdog and trying to make a popular Linux desktop. And it gets great press for it. And Mint will age and grow out in a few years, too.

Not trying to compare Mint to Ubuntu, nor to imply the good press for either is undeserved. It's *good* to have great choices available.

Gnome is inescapably prettier, but that's really and truly all it has going for it in contrast to Unity.
