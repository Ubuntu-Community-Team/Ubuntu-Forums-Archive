### Post by craig10x on 2015-03-23
They never did spy at all...the amazon results you see in the dash was just a little means of generating some extra money for canonical to support it's development (not for making a profit) but they have always provided an easy means to shut it off in system settings...you just go to "security and privacy" and under the "search" tab you hit the off switch and no more online searches (takes about 30 seconds to do)...that's it!
All that did was send (anonymously) the information when you do a dash search, to amazon so it could make some product suggestions...works much like when you get those "targeted ads" on yahoo home page for example...

Canonical (nor Amazon) was/is not looking at your files, data, personal information on your computer...and they never did or do...If you love ubuntu otherwise, seems like a rather silly reason not to use it...
Ubuntu may be free to you but it's isn't free from them to develop...I only turn mine off because i don't care for the "clutter" it creates in the dash search, so instead i send an actual donation to Canonical periodically...
