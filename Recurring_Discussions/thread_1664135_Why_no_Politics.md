### Post by MisterGaribaldi on 2011-01-10
Here's my question, and really it's up for grabs to both regular UF members (such as myself) and UF mods and admins alike:

Official forum rules and policies notwithstanding, it is undeniable that straight-up government politics and the technological interests of many communities such as this one have become inextricably intertwined to the point that it is often difficult or even impossible to discuss one without at least strongly implying the other.

That being the case, where is it we're supposed to draw the line any more?

Whether it's UK MPs or U.S. congresspeople and senators, or members of anyone else's government, it is fundamentally *their* own attitudes and agendas (and those of associated lobbyists and other parties) which directly have and continue to impact us as citizen users of technology, continue to impact things such as the free and open-source software movement/ecosystem/etc., and really the tech industry as a whole.

Now, I'm not advocating anything like having a "Politics" area on UF any more than I would advocate having a "Religion" or other social-type issues area since such things are at best disruptive and at worst destructively detrimental to the membership and its morale, but again so much of technology shapes or becomes public policy. Someone much wiser than me once said that we create our own reality. Well, we the tech enthusiasts and developers and businesses and avant-garde movers-and-shakers are through action or our mere presence creating this reality.

Should discussion of *that* or discussion along *those* sorts of lines be either actively encouraged or actively discouraged here?

Tell ya what: I wouldn't want to have to shoulder the burden that I *know* the mods and admins here have to shoulder each and every day, and so if nothing else, my hat's off to you guys and gals.
