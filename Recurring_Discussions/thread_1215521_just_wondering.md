### Post by Ms_Angel_D on 2009-07-17
Talk to them about what they use their computers for, then explain to or show them how the same things work on ubuntu. If they use their computers for mainly chat, email, and such then show them how it work on ubuntu. Most people learn better by example, not by being told about it. So I suggest taking the day to show them things on your Ubuntu, that you feel they would enjoy. 

If they don't know much about computers, touting the Virus, Ram bit won't mean much to them. But letting them know they can spend less time worrying about their computers being broken and more time actually using them might mean something.

The main thing is that if they are interested in Using Ubuntu once you show it to them, that you be there to help them through the transition, as most people will get frustrated and return to what they know during that frustration. 

You can also try a dual-boot Most users who are new like the "safety-net" of being able to go back to what they know just in case.

Good Luck :D
