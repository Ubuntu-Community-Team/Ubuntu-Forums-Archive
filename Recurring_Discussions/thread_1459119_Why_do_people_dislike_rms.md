### Post by 1clue on 2010-04-21
Addressing the original topic rather than disgusting hygiene habits:

To give him credit where credit is due, Richard Stallman has done a lot for Open Source.  Some of the GNU projects are pretty much indispensable to the OS community.

The problem comes with his extremism.  OS is wonderful.  I've used exclusively OS operating systems (mostly Linux but also FreeBSD) for over 10 years.  That recently stopped, because I bought a laptop and it had Windows 7 on it -- My first Microsoft operating system ever.  I don't use it, but I own it.

The problem comes with his insistence that all software must be free, and free by his rules.  The GPL is toxic, meaning that if you're not careful, including a GPL component in your commercial software causes your commercial software to be GPL.  GNU ran into so much opposition from that that they came up with the LGPL for libraries.  For years, enterprise business refused to allow Open Source in their doors.  In my opinion, that stance was ENTIRELY because of Stallman and GNU.  Other popular licenses (FreeBSD and Apache, for example) offer a lot more freedom and they have extremely healthy communities.

While it's IMO true that gcc and its associated tools probably enabled Open Source to thrive, had the license not been loosened up a bit OS would not have taken off at all.

Open Source is wonderful.  I use it every day, and I have made small contributions to several projects.  I BELIEVE in it.  But I don't believe in extremism in any form.  Insisting that everyone use ONLY open source is extremism and entirely unreasonable.  It's every bit as unreasonable as a commercial software vendor insisting that NOBODY use Open Source.  Insisting that a Linux distribution make it difficult for people to use commercial software is completely out of line.
