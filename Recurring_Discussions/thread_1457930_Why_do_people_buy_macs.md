Why did you spend so much on Windows?

Different people use their computers in different ways, and for different tasks, and therefore might like different operating systems.

### Post by aysiu on 2010-04-19
I can't agree with KiwiNZ on the interior hardware, but the exterior hardware is extremely well designed and engineered.

Macs offer people a balance between the lack of hassles they had with Windows and the lack of third-party support they would have in Linux. For example, whereas Windows defaults you to administrator with UAC, which (if they are to be of any use to you security-wise) have to be annoying, Mac OS X implements *sudo* in a way similar to Ubuntu, so that you are operating as a limited user almost all the time and have to password-authenticate for only a few things. There is no activation key for the Mac you bought, because you can just stick the install disk in. No drivers to install, because Apple has only a few hardware configurations it supports.

At the same time, wireless resumes immediately after sleep. You get Netflix streaming and native, full, and perpetual support for popular items like iPhones and iPod Touches. Moreover, you can try out a Mac OS X-preinstalled computer at any Apple store. I haven't found any stores that let me try out Linux-preinstalled computers.

So are Macs for everybody? By no means. I've used Windows, Mac, and Linux all extensively, and they all have their pros and cons. I wouldn't recommend Windows to everyone. I wouldn't recommend Mac to everyone. And I wouldn't recommend Linux to everyone. It all comes down to what you prefer, what your friends and family can support, what software you require for fun and/or productivity, and how much money you have to spend. And, don't forget--it doesn't have to be an either/or choice. You actually can use Windows, Mac, and Linux.
