### Post by aysiu on 2008-04-09
If you use any OS long enough, you eventually develop workarounds and find solutions for problems. I've been fortunate enough to have had significant exposure to Windows XP, Mac OS X, and Ubuntu; and I've overcome most obstacles and annoyances.

For the problems Seti mentioned, there are a couple of approaches you can take.

For launching applications, you can expand the limit number of how many recent applications are displayed in the Apple menu (so your top 50 apps will always be available there and not crowding your Dock with icons). You can also use Spotlight (Cmd+Spacebar) and start typing the name of the application you want to launch. If you don't like Spotlight, you can try QuickSilver, which accomplishes more or less the same thing and is much like Alt-F2 in Linux.

The window resizing thing is definitely a problem, which is why my wife has to put her Dock on the side instead of at the bottom of the screen. Again, a workaround, and not a solution, but better than nothing.

The universal toolbar is annoying, but it can be dealt with. It doesn't become *extremely* annoying unless you are using an Apple Cinema Display as an extended desktop... haven't found a workaround for that yet...
