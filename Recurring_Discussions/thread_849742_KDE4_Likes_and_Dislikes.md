### Post by apswartz on 2008-07-04
I keep going back and forth between KDE3 and KDE4. 4 is improving but still isn't quite there.

Things I like about KDE 4
1. Okular - wow! this is great. I use it in KDE 3 as well.
2. Sound - much better than KDE 3. When I plan to listen to music I log into KDE 4.

Things that are (still) lacking in KDE 4
1. I miss my Alt-F2 run dialog.
2. My laptop sound controls on my Dell Inspiron 1420 don't work
3. Where is suspend?
4. I like the older KTorrent better
5. I miss being able to configure KDE the way I could in earlier versions.
6. I miss my apps on the toolbar ready to launch!

What's not to like?

### Post by samwyse on 2008-07-06
My current issues:

1. QT4(?) is slow and glitchy.
2. Useless and distracting animations; color scheme sliding effect in Konsole, titles fading effect in settings, but I suppose they can be turned off somewhere.
3. Gwenview is too simple, no thumbnail preview yet in Konqueror.
4. The bread crumb view is too crowded. Avoiding the "extra" buttons like the folder list button makes it slow to use. It would be much more useful if the crumbs were normal buttons like in Nautilus. Luckily the normal location bar can still be used.
5. Panel items aren't movable (until 4.1).

The rest is fine or I haven't tested it enough due to problem #1. There's supposed to be a problem with NVidia cards, but I'm not sure in what cases it applies.
