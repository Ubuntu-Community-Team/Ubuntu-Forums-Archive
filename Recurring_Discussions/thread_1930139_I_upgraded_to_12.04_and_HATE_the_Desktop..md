### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-02-24
> **click4851 said:**
> curious as to how much time people are willing to commit to relearn how to use their DE, just to do what they used to do.

It took me about a week to be comfortable with the switch. Then many more months to find my way around.

When I say "switch", I mean from Mac OS 9 to Ubuntu 5.10 with Gnome 2. Mac OS 9 was an evolution from a desktop environment released in 1984 which I had used for 13 years. And I switched to an entirely different operating system, whose DE was incomparably modern (Ubuntu).

Why did I switch? Well, the old desktop was no longer maintained. I would have to switch to something anyway, and it seemed like Ubuntu was the way forward.

As for Unity, it only took a week to get comfortable. I switched because I would have to switch to something anyway (Gnome 2 isn't maintained) and Unity seemed to be the way forward. It is incomparably more modern than Gnome 2 and has a lot of lovely features.

It's not a case of "doing what we used to do". It's a case of having the tools to do it faster or more pleasantly.

If I can switch from hopelessly outdated Mac OS 9 to Ubuntu, I'm sure you can love Unity within a few weeks.
