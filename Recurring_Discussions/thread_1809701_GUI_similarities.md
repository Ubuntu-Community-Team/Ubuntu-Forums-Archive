### Post by Orbatos on 2011-07-22
Primarily for the sake of looking at the pros and cons of UI design in the workplace, I got access to a laptop running the latest Apple operating system. So, has anyone else taken note that many of Apple's new UI changes for OSX Lion are similar to long published work on Unity and Gnome 3? This isn't a nitpick on specific similarities, but a note that the focus of UI changes made by Apple are to the same purpose (borrowed or not) as the design concepts that Linux developers are working on.

Like it or not, what apple does in this space impacts desktop linux in general, and Ubuntu directly, as Canonical has made great pains to build a reputation of cutting edge ease of use. Also note that Apple has historically attempted to "own" design concepts regardless of their origin, making this a problem for proponents of desktop Linux.

This includes everything from the purely cosmetic (animations while handling workspaces, transparent panels), to the practical (workspace management, launching applications).

For example:
Launching applications, Apple now has "Launchpad" which looks similar to both the Unity and Gnome 3 lauchers, with no searching or categories.
Application vs. Window based window management (Unity and Gnome 3)
An almost modal approach to full screen applications (Unity)
Dynamic workspace creation (Gnome 3)

### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-07-23
Yeah, aside from Apple's tendency to attack other UIs that verge too close to their presentation, it's nothing surprising and not at all unwelcome. The new Mission Control is Shell's Activities overview turned 90°, and Launchpad is quite similar to Gnome Shell's dash, but to be fair, they're variations on things that started as Exposé and iOS's home screen, and Apple has always used application-based rather than window-based task management.
