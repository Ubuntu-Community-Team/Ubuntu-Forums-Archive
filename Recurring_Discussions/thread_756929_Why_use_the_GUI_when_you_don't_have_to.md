### Post by treehouse on 2008-04-16
Because Ubuntu's not every other Linux distro. One of the main points of it is to make Linux accessible to people who aren't power users, or haven't used Linux before and only really know how to use a GUI. If they can get a lot of GUI functionality that already works, and only have to use the CLI for a few small issues at first, then work their way onto more in-depth CLI stuff, they're more likely to stick it out and learn it, rather than switching back to Legacy or Apple OS's.

### Post by joninkrakow on 2008-07-02
Boy! I didn't get past page 2, and I'm commenting! sigh...

> **qazwsx said:**
> I think even normal human beings should start learn to use computer via command line at first and then later learn to use GUI.
CLI makes things clear. For example using mkdir, rm, mv, cp  etc commands are so much easier to comprehend than same operation in gui based filemanager. GUIs are overrated. I blame Windows. 



I am not sure about blaming Windows! I remember when the Mac first came out, all the arguments that larosa made for the CLI were used to defend DOS against the single-mouse Mac gui. Of course, BG saw that, and demanded his people copy it, but Microsoft at that time was the leading CLI interface, and people were fiercly loyal to it. Myself, I was a DOS command-line master, using "copy con"... to create batch files, and could use edlin with the best--but I also saw the beauty of the Mac, and later saw the lameness of Windows, and left the CLI, I thought, forever. ;-) 


> 
Sometimes GUIs are even pointless at least for me. I can rip DVD with  one simple  command. It takes 'countless' of clicks in GUI.

Any gui that requires countless clicks to perform simple functions is broken, IMO. A good graphical interface is just as good at staying out of the way, as it is in helping you. 

For instance, I don't like E17 because of its nested menus (nor KDEs nested menus) that keep moving on you--horrible design feature. First of all, flatter menus are always better, but to have your nested menus _move_ on you??? blech. 

Good design is good design, and should minimize labor, not add to it. (oh, and I don't like modal dialogs either--especially ones that ask you to verify what you just chose to do!)
