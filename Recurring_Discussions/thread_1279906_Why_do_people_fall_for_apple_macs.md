### Post by fela on 2009-10-01
I've seen lots of posts on the net by people who are constantly saying how marvellous Apple and all its products are, but never with any proof or reason, just "that sucks, get a mac".

I have used Macs and I can tell you one thing: the hardware sucks. With all macs except the mac pro, you can't customize the hardware AT ALL except maybe a RAM upgrade. You can't overclock it. If you have anything but a mac pro, and would like to upgrade the video card, you have to get a whole new computer. What a waste. Hard drive? No can do, better get yourself a new Mac if you want some more space! RAID? Umm...isn't that some kind of enterprise server thingy? Sorry macs don't do that (sure mac pros do but not everyone has five and a half grand to waste).

Sure none of this really counts with mac pros, but most mac users aren't mac pro users. Still, even with a mac pro, you can't upgrade the CPU (if you want nehalem and have a mac pro with an older xeon chip, you have to get a whole new computer); you can't fit more than four HDDs, two DVD drives, etc etc etc.

So what compels so many people to suck up to Apple's overpriced (in the mainstream) computers? Don't tell me, is it the operating system?

I can tell you one thing: if I were to buy a PC, the fact that it came preinstalled with OS Y and I thought that OS Y was alot better than OS Z which came installed on most computers, that would play absolutely no part in my choosing of what PC to buy. I go by the hardware, because that's where it gets messy. You can install what operating system you like (as long as its supported), but with bad hardware you'll have a bad experience all round.

So do mac users really not care at all about the hardware they're running on?

PS. just for the record, this is my 666th bean...
