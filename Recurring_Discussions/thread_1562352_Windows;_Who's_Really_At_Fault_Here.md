### Post by Captain Smiley Pants on 2010-08-27
Now, I know I'm making this post on a bit of, to be frank, biased forum. But you guys seem alright. I'm going to note that I can distinguish posts that read between the lines of rabid fan of a product and someone who can really open their mind and be a key part of a discussion with criticism and knowledge.

Another note: I've had an on-off relationship with Linux throughout the years, and was a happy camper with Ubuntu releases like 5.10 and all the old school stuff. I am also working on Microsoft certifications, and may end up with Linux certs someday. I acknowledge both operating system's strengths and weaknesses, and hopefully others will as well.

I don't have too much experience with Apple products, so I won't try and generalize or talk about them too much.

With that said, here I go:

**Windows; Who's Really At Fault Here?**

I hear the complaints a lot around here.

 - Windows is insecure
 - Windows takes too long to patch
 - I have a virus, screw Windows

etc etc etc

Which, to an extent, can be valid. In a particular users case, this and that could happen. Viruses do happen, so does malware, spyware, trojans, and a myriad of other problems.

Windows has had a bumpy road, for the most part, until 7. But I'm not here to praise or lament 7, I'm here ask; just what in the world are users doing?

I've had my experiences. When I was younger, our first family computer was something like a Pentium II with 98 Plus!, it isn't as oldschool as some of you on here may have had to use, but here's what I'm getting at: I basically owe my father a computer, because I wrecked havoc on that poor thing.

As an irresponsible child, it wasn't anything too common with what can be done through todays high speed internet, it was more along the lines of thinking critical operating system files needed to be moved to a different location, just... because. That was a nice surprise the next boot.

With what Internet we did have, though, I was basically pro-click on ANYTHING I saw. Which, I'm sure, didn't help out the computer at the time.

But I've grown. I've own my own hardware, and thinking about children someday, perhaps I can pass my knowledge to them (of course I wouldn't force it). I have my own file structure within the structure so I know where *everything* is. I only run basic free antivirus software, like AVG, and make sure it's updated. I don't go to great lengths to purchase software like that because I *know* whats going on, and if I don't, a good ten minutes later with a search engine I'll be fine.

Windows, in my experience, has been alright. During highschool, it seemed like every few months I'd back up my data, install Linux, then after a few more months I'd back up Linux, and install Windows. I don't have the patience or time to do this anymore, and even with using Windows XP of a long period of time, I hadn't noticed any huge slowdown without the monthly visit from the Defrag fairy and cleaning out any temporary files I didn't need.

Now, I'm using Windows 7. Yes, I have tried 10.04. I really did appreciate the new work that's gone into it, especially the Facebook and chat implements worked into the taskbar, but after a while of not being able to get certain work done (Again, getting MS certs, so I kinda need it), I decided not to use the ancient powers of XP and upgrade, and haven't really regretted it since. The whole bubbly taskbar has taken some time to get used to, but now I really appreciate it and when I'm on a XP machine it just... doesn't *feel* right.

What I'm asking is: Do people just not want to take responsibility for their actions and blame the Operating System? I always check the status bar before clicking. If I think a website is phishy, I'll Google it up and take 10 seconds from my life to know if it's OK or not. All of my files are structured so I don't need to have services like Indexing on, I can follow the folder directory.

Am I a super user? No, I don't think so. Do people have a legitimate case on preferring Linux to Windows? Of course they do. But will they ever take responsibility for their actions? Maybe, someday.
