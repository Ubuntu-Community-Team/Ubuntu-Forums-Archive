### Post by standingwave on 2010-07-03
> **Zimmer said:**
> XP gets trashed by Trojans and cruft.... user tries system restore  and Windows reboots..but is unstable due to unfinished Windows Updates.. then Windows fails to boot . Yeah, this happened to my nephew. I had set him up with some anti-spyware/malware programs but I don't think he wasn't very diligent. I booted Ubuntu from a CD and brought up Nautilus ->  Computer and we could see all his drives and partitions. We quickly  navigated to his windows user folder and found the folders  he wanted  recovered. We plugged in his brand new external drive and it immediately showed up  in Nautilus. We started the file transfer and then I asked him if he  would like to check his email while the files were transferring. He had been off the internet for about a week.

He was like, "We're on the Internet right now?" I said sure, and brought  up Firefox. By the time he was done handling his email and the  files  were done transferring, he was sold. "I want this," he said. So we went  ahead and did the full install. Then I set him up with 'restricted  areas' (how easy has that become in the last couple of years?!) and made  sure everything (Flash, Java, codecs, etc.) was working and then we  copied everything back from the external drive. While that file transfer was  going, I gave him a short GNOME tutorial.

This is going to be my SOP for working on borked windows systems in the  future. I have a Linux Rescue CD and could have done it all via the  terminal or maybe Midnight Commander but why not give the person a  little taste of the modern Linux Desktop?

### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-03
I will give you two stories:

1. My mother has a laptop that is really slow. I've done my best to boost its performance under Windows, but I just can't seem to get it to work. I told her about Ubuntu and showed her a LiveCD running on her system. She was fine with it and thought that the mahjong game was fun. However, she soon became frustrated because she couldn't find things. She began asking me where her desktop shortcuts were and how to get to her files. She didn't really understand the idea of this being an entirely different OS. In the end, she told me not to install it.

2. A friend of mine had his computer fail on him. Windows was attacked by a piece of malware that crippled his system. Another friend and I worked to restore his system, but could only get back his files. I suggested to him that he might try Linux, more specifically, Ubuntu. The more I explained it to him, the more he liked the idea. I told him that, if he didn't like it, he could simply reinstall Windows. I began to tell its flaws (no MS Office, no gaming) and he began to brush them off as unimportant. In the end, I gave him a LiveCD of 9.10 and my Linux for Dummies book. To my knowledge his computer has Ubuntu on it right now.

The difference in "turning someone" is not how you do it. The best Linux converter in the world couldn't convert some people. It is all about the attitude of the other person. If they are willing to try something new then they will be "converted" quite easily. If they are unwilling then they won't be.
