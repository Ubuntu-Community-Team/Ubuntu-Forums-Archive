### Post by doas777 on 2009-03-28
though i am somewhat loathe to wade into this discussion, I have only encountered a handful of abusive or mean users in all my time at the forums. mostly they are folks posting broiler-plates about how the op needs to read the sticky, or needs to use more descriptive titles, or to more completely describe the problem.

that last group is the toughest, because when a new user is frustrated and panicking, and they lack basic troubleshooting skills, they have a lot of trouble describing what is wrong. theres really no good way to ask some of those posters for details without making them feel bad about it, and start to get angry. asking for information via terminal commands makes this frustration even worse. unfourtunatly, we ask for terminal commands for good reason, they work the same for everyone.


a final thought, the vast majority of the times I see the word 'Noob', it's being used by a poster to describe their technical level. this is important to know when trying to assist someone, and I find it very helpful when the poster admits their noobitude from the start. as you said, we were all there once.

If we all try to be nice to each other, and assume that the ambiguity of emotion behind posts is benign, then we may get some problems solved. if we get angry, we're not going to accomplish anything.
