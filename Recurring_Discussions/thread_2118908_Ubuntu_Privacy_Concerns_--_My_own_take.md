### Post by NightCrawler03X on 2013-02-22
I didn't know where to post this, so I chose general. Please move it if you think it is appropriate.

--

Having tried both Ubuntu 12.04 and Ubuntu 12.10 I must say -- it's nice. Unity is a really simply desktop environment, it's easy to use. I say this as a long-time Linux user.

A lot of people really hated Unity when it came out, and we too, but I sort of gave it a change, it's really quite nice.

That's not what I want to talk about.

-- 

One concern that people have is privacy, about the way user search strings are stored by Cannonical. Some complain about privacy intrusions and potential sharing of sensitive data.

Some say that it should be an opt-in (and disabled by default) with regards to online search and shopping lens. 

Some say  -- just disable it.

I have a perfect solution that is so simple. It involves keeping it enabled by default (opt-out, not opt-in) and with users encouraged more and more to leave it switched on. You could even disable the opt-out, and make it harder to uninstall.

At the same time you would satisfy all those people who are flaming Ubuntu for its perceived invasion of privacy.

The problem is not the feature itself, that's not what these people complain about.

Here's what they complain about: the fact that Cannonical keeps logs. Even if it doesnt share that data (with amazon, facebook, bbc and the like), it's irrelevant -- Cannonical shouldn't keep that data.

So here is my ever so simple and perfect solution:
don't keep logs!

The online intergration is really useful! I don't use it myself (I'm not even running Ubuntu right now, I only tried it for a few days in VirtualBox).

Just don't keep logs!
That way users can use the shopping lens, dash search and online features all they want. Of course their search strings will still go to Cannonical, but as long as they are not stored (and most certainly not monitored) by Cannonical. 

That way, there is no problem!

The way I see it, it already is opt-in to a certain degree. You *choose* whether or not to tell Unity your facebook/google/etc logins to intergrate your online files etc.

Just don't keep records!

And update the privacy policy to reflect this change.

I would like all opinions from everyone. Not just normal users, but maybe core devs too.

I only want to help, so this advice could be helpful. 2 camps, both at odds -- both camps will become happy. Simple solutions are the best solutions.
