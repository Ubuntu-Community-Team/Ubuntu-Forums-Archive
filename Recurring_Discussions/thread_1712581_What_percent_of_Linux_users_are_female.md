### Post by DZ* on 2011-03-25
> **duanedesign said:**
> The problem with stereotypes and empirical generalizations is that they are not always true for all individual cases.

No, it is not a problem, because stereotypes and empirical generalizations are about tendencies rather than about individual cases. "Men tend to be physically stronger than women" is a fact. Rephrase it in terms of percentages of how many men vs women can lift a certain weight and you have something close to the title of this thread. But this fact is not about any individual case, it is about the tendency: a particular woman can well be stronger than a particular man. These types of facts are not useless either. In fact, stereotyping is very much related to abstraction in the way humans think about problems.
