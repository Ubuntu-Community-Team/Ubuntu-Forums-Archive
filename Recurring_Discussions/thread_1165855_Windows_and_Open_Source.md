### Post by monsterstack on 2009-05-21
> **mthalis said:**
> I want to know about why most of the people go to open source and give up windows ? also what happen windows in the future and what kind of changes happen to Open Source?

Thank You.

The future has few problems for any of the major open source projects as far as I can see. They have been going from strength to strength in recent years. The biggest problem I think they will face is increased attacks from proprietary vendors such as Microsoft. I think the fates of Windows and open-source will be unhappily entwined for a while, yet.

As the price of hardware continues to plummet, Microsoft will eventually have to consider new business methods to stay relevant. Ten years ago, when a decent spec desktop computer cost a grand or more, a $100 Windows licence wasn't that big a deal. Now we have the prospect of sub-$100 netbooks and the like. It's very much a big deal. So far Microsoft have had to give away copies of Windows XP for much less than what it would have hoped. Microsoft would prefer not to have to use XP to compete at all on this platfrom, hence development of Windows 7. Remember, XP was supposed to be dead long ago. Microsoft have enough reserve cash to distribute operating systems at little to no cost for a while yet, but sooner or later they're going to have to find some new revenue streams.

For Microsoft, maintaining their marketshare profitably will become a huge problem. A direct consequence of this will be increased attacks on open source. Patent attacks, shady *Novell*esque licensing "agreements", more efforts to enforce vendor lock-in. It's the same as always, except this time it will be worse as Microsoft will be more desperate in the years to come. They will be more vicious.
