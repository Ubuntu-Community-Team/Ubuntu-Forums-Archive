### Post by kahlil88 on 2009-02-24
The only downsides to running a GNU/Linux are all politics oriented. People complain that GNU/Linux systems don't support MP3, AAC, DivX, etc. out of the box, but they don't realize that these formats are patented and that the decoders can't legally be distributed. Then there's the games issue. Windows runs the most and best commercial games because it's the most popular platform. Then there's hardware support. Many vendors decide not to open their hardware specifications, which leaves the community to write the drivers themselves in their precious free time.

### Post by Paqman on 2013-02-28
> **i.mehrzad said:**
> 
Any tips on how to win such a conversation.


What do you get if you "win"?

Changing another person's opinion is difficult, you can't really do it just through confrontation. People just dig their heels in. All you can do is show them things that are cool about Linux. If they're impressed they'll want to know more, if they aren't just drop it. Don't try to push your opinion on others, especially since they're actually right about some stuff. If you're a gamer Windows actually is the best platform to use. Judging an OS on the quality of its software library is an entirely sensible and defensible position to take IMO.

Just enjoy using Linux. Maybe others will notice your enjoyment of it and give it a go. If you try to evangelise you'll probably just annoy your friends. And in the long run friends are waaaaaaaaaaay more important than operating systems.

Besides, there's no reason someone can't use both Linux and Windows, and appreciate their different virtues. It's not a religion, we don't tie people to stakes and burn them for using Windows.
