### Post by neurostu on 2008-04-13
> If I'm a CFO, why should I choose Canonical over Redhat or Novell when my IT manager comes up with 3 possibilities and can't really tell me which is best?

Three words: ** SUPPORT STABILITY SUPPORT **
All the distributions (more or less) are quite similar,  but they differ widely when it comes down to support and stability. 

I use both RHEL and Ubuntu. Each distribution has its strengths. For the most part Ubuntu stays pretty up to date with the latest software releases and new packages. It has a lot of eye candy and I would describe it as "Very Fun". Ubuntu is targted at end users who want to play with linux and replace windows.

RHEL on the other hand is targeted at Enterprises and setup for mission critical tasks.  My RHEL box is ROCK SOLID it has never crashed and I don't expect it to. On the other hand there isn't as much software available for it. I have the latest version of RHEL and it is still running Firefox 1.5.  Everything RedHat does they do with stability in mind. They don't care about whistles or bells, they don't care about eye candy. They care about creating ROCK SOLID servers.

REDHAT is known to be one of the best support centers in the world. Sure you pay through the nose for it, but they can and will fix any problem as fast as is humanly possible.

Ubuntu support (I've never used the paid support) is great but not stellar. These forums are some of the best I've seen online but they don't come anywhere near the level and quality of support you get from RHEL.

If you are in fact a CFO or IT manager I would 100% of the time chose RHEL.  You don't need a fancy GUI, you don't need cutting edge software, you need STABILITY and SUPPORT.  You need your business to be running at 100%, 100% of the time

I personally don't mind developers reinventing the wheel because sometimes they bring in new ideas that change Linux.
