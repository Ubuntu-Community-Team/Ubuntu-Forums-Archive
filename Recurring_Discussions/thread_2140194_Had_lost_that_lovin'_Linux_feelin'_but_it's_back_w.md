### Post by screaminj3sus on 2013-05-09
I am loving XFCE as well. Its rock solid, very stable, and has a classic user-friendly (and customizable UI). It has all the features of a full DE while still being pretty light and modular too (moreso than gnome 2 was). XFCE isn't the prettiest or the fastest, or the most feature rich, but I've found it to be the best combination of being sane, quick, stable, having all the features I need and decently good looking. XFCE's apps all have very sensible UI's and just enough features, and no bloat. I love the latest version of thunar, its much better than the latest nautilus. (nautilus 3.6 has all sorts of annoying issues for me, such as my bookmarked network shares randomly disappearing from the sidebar, and crashing when mounting/unmounting shares).

My only problems with vanilla XFCE are two things:

No pulseaudio support. XFCE-volumed and xfce4-mixer are very broken if you use pulseaudio, its 2013 and most linux desktops use pulseaudio, its long overdue for xfce to support this properly. I'm very thankful for the excellent xubuntu distro, because it is the only XFCE distro I've seen that addresses this (they use ubuntu's sound-indicator + pavucontrol instead of xfce4-mixer and re-wrote xfce4-volumed to support pulseaudio).

The other issue I have is lack of opengl compositing. I really don't care about fancy effects or any of that crap, but I absolutely *loathe* video-tearing. I have intel graphics, and the only way for me to watch videos without tearing reliably is using an opengl compositor (I tried using opengl output in vlc and mplayer, but it didn't help at all in vlc, and with mplayer it only removes tearing on certain videos for some reason. and if I have xfwm's xrender compositor on both mplayer and vlc have tearing for all videos even with opengl output). Compiz is really buggy in XFCE in 13.04 (unclickable window decorations), and I was close to ditching XFCE because of this issue, which made me said because I really love using XFCE. Luckily I recently discovered compton, which solved all my issues: [https://github.com/chjj/compton](https://github.com/chjj/compton). Very stable and lightweight compositor that has a good optional opengl backend that can totally remove tearing, and it doesn't need to replace xfwm like compiz does!.

xubuntu 13.04 and XFCE 4.10 is the happiest I've been with any distro/desktop in a long time. It just works, and I no longer have to deal with unity and gnome's annoying bugs and lack of quality control (and yes I've tried unity in 13.04. better, yes, but still way too buggy). I was getting sick of always feeling like I was using beta software when using "stable" releases of unity and gnome. XFCE has rescued me. Maybe unity or gnome will shape up and win me back over some day, but at least I have a desktop that actually works in the meantime.
