### Post by samjh on 2008-05-31
> **OzzyFrank said:**
> Just wanted to see what people thought of KDE4, whether people think it is stable for use by the general public yetIn my opinion, no.

KDE4 at the moment, is aimed more at developers than the general public.  There are still a lot of missing features, customisation, and some applications have not been fully ported to KDE4.

> how they feel it compares to the last of the 3x series. I've personally only looked at Kubuntu 8.04 KDE4 on Live CD, and then installed it into Virualbox (where the screen went black after enabling the built-in desktop effects). Do you find it stable, or barely useable? Prefer all the glitter and gadgets over the previous incarnation of KDE, or find them comfusing or an unwelcome distraction? (And do some find it resembles Mac OS X too much for their liking, hehe?)I very much prefer to new look and feel.  It is smoother, easier on the eyes, and distinctive compared to other mainstream desktop environments (eg. MacOS, Windows XP and Vista).

The widgets I've seen are quite good, but not much better or worse than their KDE3 cousins.

As for Virtualbox screen going blank when turning on desktop effects, keep in mind that Virtualbox does not support hardware 3D acceleration (I don't think any virtualisation software does), so that isn't the fault of KDE, but a limitation of Virtualbox.
