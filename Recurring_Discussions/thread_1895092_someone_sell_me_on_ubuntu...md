### Post by Mark Phelps on 2011-12-14
Your lists are both ridiculous!  Every single claim is easily refuted by experiences to the contrary.

**Pros**
never need to bother with drivers -- not so, visit the hardware section some time and tell that to the folks who can't find drivers for their hardware.
can read all file formats -- many perhaps, but not all.
no viruses? (who gets these anyway lol) -- true, but if you run decent AV SW on other OSs, you don't get viruses, either.
connects to a new wifi the first time, without the need to reset computer 2-3 times -- tell that to the folks in the Networking forum who can't get their Wifi to work.
picture gallery has built in .GIF support -- so what, most graphics are JPGs these days anyway
loads way faster -- not on my PC -- Win7 loads way faster than Ubuntu 11.10


**Cons**
no where near as much support -- disagree. This forums provides LOTS of support, easily better than MS TechNet
hard to find stuff for -- disagree.  The Software Center, with the added repos, provides access to thousands of apps, and in many cases, 2-3 apps for the same function.
if you have a problem, nobody irl even knows what it is -- we're not minder readers here.  Posting "PC won't boot" and leaving it at that is pretty much useless.  Folks here have an excellent record at solving problems.
all of your favorite programs dont exist for it -- if "all" your favorite programs are in Windows, then you have no business even being here, as you're in the wrong place.  Linux is about acquiring new "favorite" programs to replace your old ones, not about figuring out how to remain dependent on Windows apps.
youve used windows your whole life, and can pretty much fix everything yourself -- As we have to say more often than I like "Linux is not Windows", a lot of the excitement of using Linux is the opportunity to learn new things.  Once again, if you're stuck in the "windows rutt", then you have no business being here.

If you need to be "sold" on Ubuntu ... then you need to develop a list that is more realistic of the true pros and cons.

### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2011-12-14
It is free as in freedom as well as no cost.
There are things I can't do in Windows that I can do in Linux,and it is usually because Windows licenses stop you as much as the architecture preventing it.
To be honest, that is more about open source than about Windows v Linux.
I resent being told what I can't do by someone who rents me software.

One thing I really like is that I can build things my way.
For example, I set up a fax server with the help of CUPS, Hylafax and a couple of perl scripts, which takes a postscript print job consisting of pages with phone numbers in, splits it into individual pages and faxes them to their individual destinations.
I could have done it in Windows, but it would have cost me close to a thousand pounds in license fees.
Or MythTV, which allows me to use multiple tuners and record multiple channels from each, along with advert skipping and the ability to stream live TV across my home network.

Oh, and if Win7 boots faster than Ubuntu 11.10 (10-12 seconds on my netbook), either you haven't installed any software in 7, or your Ubuntu install is hopelessly screwed.....
