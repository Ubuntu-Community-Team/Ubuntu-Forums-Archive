### Post by Meep3D on 2010-03-25
After using a Mac for a couple of weeks now after being a lifetime Windows user, I am slightly disturbed by Ubuntu's drift towards trying to look like OSX.

For example the top panel has slowly got more and more OSX like in terms of fast user switching and general layouts, then the notification area was added (which is very OSX like), now the window decorations have been moved to mimic* OSX and the whole theme and the usage of purple even looks like OSX's wallpaper.  At least with brown there was a sense of identity.  In fact the indent that the decorations sit in looks out of place and was only added as without it they would be pretty much a clone of the 'glass orb' effect in OSX.

It's pretty disappointing that Ubuntu seems to be losing its identity (and soul) in an attempt to try to imitate a commercial OS.  I really doubt all the UI ideas are used up - and the similarities between where Ubuntu is going and OSX are too many to simply be an accident.

** There is no good reason whatsoever for this change except 'OSX does it this way', and plenty of downsides.*
