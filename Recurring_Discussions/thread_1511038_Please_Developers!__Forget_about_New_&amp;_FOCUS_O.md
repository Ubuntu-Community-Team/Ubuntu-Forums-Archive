### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-06-17
It isn't really fair to say that "*Linux* is unstable" when it's really just the latest *Ubuntu* that isn't quite stable until a few weeks or months into its life.

If you are looking for absolute rock-stability, try Debian Lenny (or a distro built on Debian Lenny, like Mepis). Ubuntu and it's siblings and offspring are more "up-to-date" and thus a little less stable.

I usually tell Ubuntu users who want more up-to-date stuff without too many stability issues to just stay one release behind the current one, or use one that works for you until it's end-of-life. Or stick with the LTS versions (and wait until Hardy's EOL before switching to Lucid).

But rock-solid, super ultra mega stable? Debian Stable. It just doesn't get any more stable than that!

My own experience with Ubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu has been quite trouble-free, and the li'l issues I've had have all been easily fixed with a few minutes of Googling. Your mileage may vary, of course. If your experience is so frustrating, have a look at Mepis (alot of people find it much easier than Debian) which is built on Debian Stable as Ubuntu is built on Debian Testing (LTS version) and Debian Unstable (non-LTS versions).

Cheerfully,
Robin
