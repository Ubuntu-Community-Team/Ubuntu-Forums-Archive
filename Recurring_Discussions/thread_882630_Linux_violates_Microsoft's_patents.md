### Post by bonzodog on 2008-08-07
First Off, will people *please* remember that Software Patents **only** apply inside the continental US.

Canonical happen to be a UK company, which has firmly rejected the foolish notion of Software Patents.

The greater majority of the Linux community deliberately avoids the US because of its laughable idea of patent law and copyright law. Also you cannot prosecute something that you cannot see. The very idea of Open Source evades the notion that it is produced by one person in one country at a time. It isn't -- its produced by many people all over the world and is then uploaded to the internet including mirrors in countries who would happily reject some other countries claim of IP violation.

Secondly, the MS claim on IP violations was put out as a warning, Likewise, Novell could reasonably kill off windows development overnight, as MS have actually violated *A LOT* of much older Unix patents. It's a cold war situation, as an actual case would assure the mutual destruction of both sides. Microsoft did it initially to highlight the problem with Software Patents, in that they have done nothing but cause havoc to US software innovation. Bill Gates made it very clear that he hates them, and would love to see them outlawed.
