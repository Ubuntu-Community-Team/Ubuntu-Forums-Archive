### Post by mb_webguy on 2010-05-17
So you can get the newest versions of various apps?

While I can't see your attachment -- the forum says it's invalid for some reason -- nothing you mention in your post seems like it would be affected by upgrading to 10.04 as long as you don't do a clean installation.  The software you've installed will be upgraded, but none of the settings and preferences will be changed.  Likewise, any customizations you've made to your desktop will remain as they are.  

So it seems to me that the better question is why you *shouldn't* upgrade to Lucid.  The only problems I have with Lucid are the indicator applet (which I think is pretty pointless and a bad idea poorly executed), and that I'm having problems connecting to secured wireless networks (which is a known problem with my wireless card, and a step up from it not working at all in previous versions of Ubuntu).  I probably wouldn't be having the second problem if I hadn't done a clean install, since I'd made some hacks just to get the wireless card to work in previous versions of Ubuntu, and the first problem was fairly easily fixed by removing the indicator applet and adding the volume control applet back to the notification area.  And then there's the issue of window controls being moved to the left of the titlebar, but that's easily fixed by using a different theme, which it sounds like you're doing already.

Overall, I think it's a fairly small price for getting updated versions of any software released in the past six months.  And it's not like anyone is forcing you to upgrade.  You can go right ahead and continue using 9.10 until you're old and gray.  The only downsides would be that in another few months you'll cease getting updates and for your system, and you'll still be using outdated versions of software unless you use PPAs to get them.
