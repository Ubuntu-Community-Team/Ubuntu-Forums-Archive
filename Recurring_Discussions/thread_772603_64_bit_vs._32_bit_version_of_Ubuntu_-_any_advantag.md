### Post by timzak on 2008-04-28
I have to disagree with MasterSander.  I never noticed any speed difference (I'm sure it was there, just not enough to be noticeable).  For Hardy, I switched back to 32-bit.  For the most part, you can do everything in 64-bit that you can in 32-bit, but you may be forced into workarounds or different software applications.  Spend some time in the 64-bit subforum here and see for yourself.  My understanding is that the speed improvements are most noticeable in cpu-intensive number-crunching tasks. 

What broke the back for me was having to resort to Swiftdove (from Thunderbird) because I needed the latest version of Mozilla's calendar extension (Lightning) and it wasn't available in 64-bit.  Luckily, the developers of SwiftWeasel/Swiftdove were on the ball with porting the latest version of Lightning to 64-bit.

If you don't mind some workarounds to get apps working or installed, it might be worth it to you.  Just depends on how much "tinkering time" you have available.  

Good luck.
