### Post by murderslastcrow on 2011-05-25
Gnome 3's really good. The disagreements are usually on either design or working with fglrx. Aside from that people seem to appreciate the quality of it, even if they don't plan on using it.

Honestly, I saw more negative attention over Ubuntu switching window buttons than I am over Gnome 3. A lot of it is because the majority of Gnome 2 users were probably Ubuntu users, so they have mixed feelings about what's going on. Usually they're smart enough to either move on or stick with Xfce4, rather than put up a fight.

Qt-lovers didn't really have anywhere to go when KDE 4 came out, aside from window managers- I remember e17 being discussed quite a bit, although back then it wasn't nearly as stable as it is now.

I'd say less, definitely. But I don't really hang around immature people, so I'm not sure what they're saying about it. Even the people who had problems had a lot of good to say about it, from what I've read.

I think it all comes down to the fact that, aside from Fedora, when Gnome 3 was released there wasn't a very easy way to try the stable versions in a supported distribution (Arch was basically the only reasonable choice, aside from LiveCDs and Betas). So Gnome 3 just doesn't have as many people using it on launch, and Unity's release is picking up all the attention from Ubuntu users (the majority of Gnome 2 users, who are likely still using a classic option if they don't like Unity).

The attention is so dispersed, and no one's really forced to use Gnome 3 to keep their software and preferences, so I don't think it'd be easy to get more hatred out of the situation.
