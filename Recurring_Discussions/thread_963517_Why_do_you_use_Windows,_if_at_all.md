### Post by putonghua73 on 2008-11-02
For many, many years it [Windows] was the path of *least* resistance. Almost written with a completely straight face, since I was a Windows 98 (ab)user, and I recall with considerable bitterness my discussions with one of my friends concerning which of our Windows boxes went down harder: mine or his (with Win ME). 

Despite what amounts to spousal abuse, I stuck with Win 98, trying my damnedest to figure out the constant crashes - because it was the path of least resistance re: the devil you know and all that guff. 

Fast forward many years later to Win XP (after SP1), and I felt a whole new lease of life! My Win box worked! I could no longer make disparaging remarks concerning "plug and pray" because the whole installation process took approx 40 minutes and all hardware was detected and worked out of the box first time. It was a joyous occasion after years of misery with that pile of faeces of an OS [Win 98]. 

That's not to say that I had to switch to a more secure browser (FF), use a firewall, virus checker, malware / spyware checker, turn off a number of services open in the default configuration, manually disabled TCP/IP over netbios or whatever the hell it was, close a few ports, use a third party application to physically stop applications and processes starting up at boot, yada yada yada.

Win XP *delivered*.

So why have I installed a persistent version of Ubuntu 8.10 on a USB flash memory stick, and find myself posting on this forum? 3 principal reasons:

1. SP3 - crashed my hard-drive upon installation and to this day, I still cannot recover all that JAV, er I mean my data. Ahem.

2. Vista - I absolutely loathe the development path that MS has taken. Win XP delivered, it worked, so why provide 'features' that were not asked for, include 'features' that would seriously annoy the majority of the user-base and threaten to stop supporting Win XP - not only annoying home users but IT departments who support Windows ERP deployment.

Oh, 'Windows Vista Ready!'. My girl-friend's laptop came with one of those stickers and 512MB Ram - I kid you not. Of course, she didn't know any better. Performance? I weep every time I'm forced to use her laptop.

3. A linux distro (Ubuntu) that the average home-user can use, and opens up a window in the world of linux distros (my memory of linux was tainted by being forced to use vi(le) in the mid-90s. I hate that text-editor).

To answer the question? Like a few of the other posters, I use Skype due to a LDR (long-distance relationship), and my cordless VOIP handset is Win XP / Vista only. Thus, I cannot remove Windows until I (a) find an application that allows me to use my handset with linux, or (b) find a cordless VOIP handset that works with linux.

My 8GB nano iPod. Since I no longer buy music via iTunes, I have no real reason - so strike one reason.

The final reason is checking to see whether my Casio Exilim digital camera will work under linux.

In the next week or so, I'll buy a new hard-drive because working off a USB flash memory stick has it's limitations, and my current HD is an embarrassing 10Gig (thanks SP3). Then dual-boot by keeping Win XP on my 10Gig HD and partitioning my new one for Unbuntu (/home in separate partition) and maybe one or two others (Arch springs to mind).

Windows days are numbered on my machine.

Right. That's it! I've finished. Really.

Close as many ports as possible (only ports to allow Skype), turn off as many services as possible, close various security holes, etc, a Ubuntu partition, and two separate linux partitions (Arch / Slackware ??), and one large partition for applications and data.
