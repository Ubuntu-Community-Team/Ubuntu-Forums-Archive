### Post by hoppipolla on 2009-09-23
An interesting thread!

Ok, I think Linux has good apps and they are plentiful, but yes after being on a Mac you might find they are not quite as good on the whole. However, they ARE free and very easy to download, and are also flexible and open source :)

Hardware incompatibility... sometimes. But then of course Linux is designed to run on as much as possible, and yet doesn't have much support. OSX is designed for specific hardware, and Windows gets much more support from hardware manufacturers.

And I love the Linux crowd hehe :)  These forums are good fun with a nice variety of people and personalities!

Ok erm... problems... sometimes when things break they can be hard to fix, and sometimes you will have to drop down to the terminal. Sometimes installing software can be a little tricky, usually it's very easy :)

Oh, be prepared for software to not always be perfect, as there are a lot of "in development" projects and also a lot of unfinished projects but they are usually pushed aside.

What else... oh, sometimes the package manager goes a bit squiffy and you have to drop down to Synaptic, very occasionally prompt commands.

Other than that it's pretty smooth... I have also tried Suse (found package handling to be poor and it was kinda buggy, but in some ways a good professional distro), Fedora (found bugs and just generally didn't seem as accessible and usable as Ubuntu) and Slackware.

I hope you enjoy your time on Ubuntu! At the moment I don't feel like it's the perfect OS for everyone, but I love it to bits ^_^

Hoppi!
