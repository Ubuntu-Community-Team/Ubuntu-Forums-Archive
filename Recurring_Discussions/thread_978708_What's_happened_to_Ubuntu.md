### Post by DrMega on 2008-11-11
Ok, this is not a rant, just a series of observations I've made over the years. I was just wondering what other people thought.

When I first "discovered" Ubuntu (after nearly giving up on Linux), I was really impressed with the whole package. Ubuntu Dapper (6.06) was rock solid, light weight, fast, and very professional looking. The forum was great too, and it didn't take me very long to find my feet in Linux, thanks to the speedy responses to most of my questions. When I just wanted to chill, and not discuss technical stuff, the forum had plenty of places for light hearted discussion with like minded people.

There is no wonder that Ubuntu became the distro of choice for so many, not just for the great OS, but the whole package including OS, Forum and user community.

By the time Gutsy came along, Ubuntu was showing real promise. It required little or no skill to configure and use, and looked very professional. It was tantalisingly close to be ready for mainstream users to come flocking along. The OS was great, the forum was great, Ubuntu was a winner.

But then Hardy, and LTS version, was released with a buggy web browser (FF3 beta), and many users (myself included) found it completely unusable due to random freeze-ups caused by an unresolved kernel issue. An issue which, to my knowledge, has yet to be resolved.

The quality of support at the forum changed also. Anyone who reported that the system was randomly freezing would just be told that it is probably their hardware, or their configuration, ie the report would be dismissed out of hand despite the easily found launchpad entries describing the known issue. Also, despite this being a known, and logged, issue, I have even seen warnings from Ubuntu staff on this forum warning Gutsy users that it would be a good idea to use the LTS instead. There seems to be a general concensus that because Hardy works for many, it most be great and anyone who experiences any of the known and logged issues just don't matter.

Then the hype is now all about Intrepid, but the kernel freeze issue that existed in Hardy remains unresolved and apparently exists in Intrepid as well.

Of course, if anyone chooses to raise questions about issues with Ubuntu using the forum, depending on the time of day they choose to do so, they may have to endure repeated page time-outs and painfully slow navigation, only to be told to run memtest, upgrade to a newer version, or to be silent and not dare to criticise the almighty Ubuntu.

Is it just me, or are we going down hill? What's happening here?
