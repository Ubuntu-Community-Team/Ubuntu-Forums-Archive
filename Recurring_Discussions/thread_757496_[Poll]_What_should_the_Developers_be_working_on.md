### Post by ofb on 2008-04-17
Can we add suggestions that aren't on this list?

I'd like Linux Networking to be finished. 

NFS is not trivial to set up for people new to Linux, and even then it's flawed for home use because your file managers lock up when the sharing computer is shut off. A project to make a full NFS GUI wrapper for setup and operation would bring us out of the 'slackware era' with networking.

SMB has made it to that stage, which is very nice. It would be good to have NFS match it. 

Or just make it easy to mount SMB shares NFS style. Anything that would make home LAN use simple, seamless, and secure would do.

### Post by ofb on 2008-04-18
> **p_quarles said:**
> You might take a look at sshfs.

Or use sshfs - the underlying technology doesn't matter. My point is only that we don't have good networking for Ubuntu's target new user.

SMB has gotten pretty good. It's pre-installed and everything is handled by GUI now. These need to be refined a bit so new users aren't tripped by permissions, but mainly a SMB share doesn't work like a mounted share, so not all our applications can use them.

By that I mean an app like XMMS can play music in an NFS share, but it can't with a SMB share.

Hence we get a recurring new user enquiry on the forum - they've just installed Ubuntu, they've got a rounter and at least one other machine at home, and they'd like to move files around and play music like they did in Win/Mac. It's nontrivial, and they get their first taste of the 'Linux learning curve' that Ubuntu was supposed to avoid.

That's what I mean by suggesting networking should be finished. It's commonly used and hasn't been brought up to Ubuntu's standard yet, so it's a good project candidate.
