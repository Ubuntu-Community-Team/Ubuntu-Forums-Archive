### Post by rg4w on 2011-05-05
> **Peter09 said:**
> Very true - I'm just pointing out that the criticisms that Unity is too confusing for beginners does not really stand up to examination.
Respectfully, the same could be said of claims to the contrary.  The fact is there's simply no way to know.  

As far as I can tell there have only been two user testing sessions done for Unity, one in November and another in April.  For such a bold departure from long-established conventions (hiding menus by default, for example), those two tests of relatively limited scope won't be able to provide adequate information to validate the design decisions.  

In fact, some of the most valuable info from those was where they exposed weaknesses in the design.  While the April test was done too late in the game to affect release, we can expect the lessons learned from it to have a role in refining the UI for 11.10.

Personally, I think Unity got most of it right.  But there are some details which need refinement, and these occur in areas pervasive enough in usage patterns that they may legitimize perceptions that there's more work to do.

In short, neither random Unity-bashing nor random Unity-bashing-bashing is especially productive.  What would be more helpful would be to participate in the bug database, and in Ayatana, and encouraging adoption of distributed user testing.
