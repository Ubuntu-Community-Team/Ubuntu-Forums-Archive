### Post by thatguruguy on 2012-07-28
> **Nixarter said:**
> 
Any chance of Ubuntu coming out with a more direct descendent to gnome 2 (like MATE), and splitting it from the new UNITY ubuntu releases?

Who is this "Ubuntu" you are referring to?

Ubuntu is a Linux-based operating system, derived from Debian. Alternatively, Ubuntu is the community of people who use and/or contribute to that operating system.

If you are referring to the operating system, it doesn't "come out" with anything. Just like Windows doesn't "come out" with anything; Windows is developed by Microsoft. Microsoft "comes out" with new versions of Windows. (Likewise, Apple "comes out" with new versions of OSX).

If you are referring to the community of users and contributors, you are part of that community. You can use pretty much any desktop environment you want. If you look [here]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DerivativeTeam/Derivatives"), you'll see that there are community-supported derivatives of Ubuntu that use LXDE, KDE, and XFCE (among others) as the DE of choice.

As it happens, there is a privately-owned company, [Canonical Ltd.]("http://www.canonical.com/"), which pays the bills for Ubuntu. The chance of ***Canonical*** "coming out with a more direct descendent to gnome 2 (like MATE), and splitting it from the new UNITY ubuntu releases" is somewhere between zero and whatever number is less than zero.
