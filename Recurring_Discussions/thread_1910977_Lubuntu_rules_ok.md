### Post by kalaharix on 2012-01-18
I just want to share my relief at having found Lubuntu. It is plain, simple and just allows me to get on with my work. It is a bit rough at the edges but I am sure that will change. For example, putting launchers on the desktop is not as intuitive as in Ubuntu but it is not something I do very often anyway. It benefits from the excellent package management of Ubuntu and its parent Debian.

I just cannot understand the constant need to 'develop' desktops: they are just a conduit to the applications. Why don't they stay that way. Having said that, I respect Canonical's Unity. It seems that Linux has lost the desktop battle and Canonical is right to look forward.

For the while, I'll use Lubuntu!
