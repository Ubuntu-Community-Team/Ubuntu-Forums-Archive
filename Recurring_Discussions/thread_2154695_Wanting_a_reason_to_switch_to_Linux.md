### Post by Copper Bezel on 2013-06-16
I personally think that Linux tends to have a better workflow than Windows desktops - not just the big, flashy UI stuff, but the little details, like being able to scroll a document with the mouse without selecting that window or being able to copy and paste without using the keyboard, stuff that applies to any Linux UI. But that's little stuff. If you're in a discipline where you'll need Windows programs - and that's most disciplines - it's hard to commit to Linux full-time, and dual-booting and switching between installs is going to be more of a burden to your workflow than will be offset by anything that Linux gives you. That said, it's fun to play with, and tinkering with the bits inside is a good puzzle for an engineer, I think. = )

### Post by TNFrank on 2013-06-16
Because it's Free, there's a million different versions(ok, maybe not a million but more then I can count) of Linux some of which are awesome for Penetration Testing systems like Kali or Back Track and you get a lot of Free Open Source programs you can use with it like LibreOffice, VLC video viewer, Clementine music program(kind of like iTunes) and Gimp(think Photo Shop here only Free) and tons of other stuff. Plus it's more stable and less buggy IMHO then Windows, it has less problems with viruses, malware, spyware and trojans and did I mention it's FREE? 
 Throw some kind or another version of Linux on a USB using UNetbootin(also a free program) and give it a test drive, if you don't like it then shut down, remove the USB stick and reboot and you're back to your old op system, easy as that. 
 I was a die hard OS X Mac Fanboy until my Mac died and I saw what it'd cost to get a replacement Mac. With Linux I have all the benefits of Mac OS but I don't have to buy the Apple hardware to run my Op System on, any ol' PC will generally work with one version or another of Linux.  Oh, and did I mention that it's FREE?:D
