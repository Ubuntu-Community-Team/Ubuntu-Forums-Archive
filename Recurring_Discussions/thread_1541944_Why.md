### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2010-07-30
This is why I use Ubuntu:

1. It stays updated just enough. I don't have to wait for Windows Service packs to come out, yet it doesn't get updated too often to the point where it would get annoying.
2. Ubuntu provides you freedom, it will let you install what you want, remove what you want, customize the way you want, and many other freedoms.
3. It is free, this is a price that can't be beat.
4. It is one of the most secure OSs out there. Any viruses that come out are fixed in the kernel within days, not weeks.
5. Ubuntu has a great community. Ubuntu has a community that is growing and helps you no matter what your question. They are also what you would call "mature" in comparison to some of the PC users out there (at least that I have seen).
6. It is a very fast operating system. The Linux kernel in Ubuntu manages the CPU and other resources very well. On Windows my laptop would lock up if I even attempted to play 720p video, now I can play it flawlessly (no hardware upgrades).
7. Your HDD will not need to be defragmented. Fragmentation is something that plagues Windows users and causes their computers to slow down over time. In Ubuntu you never have to defragment.

Need I say more?

I noticed you said that the last time you used it was with Ubuntu 7.04. Has it changed much? It sure has! I got my first taste of Ubuntu in 8.04 and I fully converted over in 9.04. Ever since I have started using it I have noticed that Ubuntu has become faster, and more user friendly. I have no doubt that from here on out it will only get better.
