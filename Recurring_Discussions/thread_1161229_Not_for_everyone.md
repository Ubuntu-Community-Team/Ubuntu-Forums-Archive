### Post by Giovannino on 2009-05-16
Ubuntu is not for everyone. The wonderful thing is, Ubuntu, other Linux distributions, Solaris, Windows and Mac all give us a choice. We must decide what works for each of us, by either trying new systems or simply settling for what came installed on our computers when bought. While I am happy with Ubuntu, it does take some effort and has some hardware incompatabilities. I strongly recommend using the live version before installing Ubuntu, or even before upgrading from a previous version. Try it out before you do a permanent install, check that your webcam, printer and floppy disc all work, and back up all of your data. 
If you try Ubuntu and don't like it, you are free to remove it and install another operating system, be it another Linux distribution or Windows. That is a part of the freedom of Ubuntu!

### Post by freebeer on 2009-05-16
> **growled said:**
> Choice is a wonderful thing to have, isn't it?

Yes and no.  While *some* choice is a good thing, too much choice can be detrimental.  The fear of trying something new, or going through all the work of trying to find the *optimal* option can lead to disatisfaction even when the right choice is made.  It's a human-nature thing, I guess.  I also think it's one of the reasons why Linux has a relatively slow adoption rate.  There's a good video found [here]("http://www.ted.com/index.php/talks/barry_schwartz_on_the_paradox_of_choice.html") that does a good job of covering the topic.
