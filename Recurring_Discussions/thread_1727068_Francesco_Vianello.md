### Post by coljohnhannibalsmith on 2011-04-12
Hi,

Has anyone here heard of Francesco Vianello aka "Fravia?"

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fravia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fravia)

Shortly after I got my first computer, back in the '90s, I quickly realized how expensive software could be; and that without it, my computer would be pretty useless.  Back then, I was so poor I had to buy a 'bootlegged' copy of Windows '95, to upgrade from 'Windows 3.1 for Workgroups.'  This actually made me feel kind of clever and adventurous. It was at this time that I started to learn about 'Cracked' software, 'Keygens,' and 'Warez.'  I also learned that there were organized 'hacking' groups, such as "Phrozen Crew," and "United Cracking Force," that made Warez, such as PC-Oscar available.  I even came to regard these people as counter-culture heroes, who democratized the information age, and addtionally took a few dollars out of the pockets of the 'fat-cat' industrailists.  I can't tell you how many porn-sites I had to visit to get my bootlegs, before I discovered these guys.  However, the person that I admired the most was Fravia, aka +ORC, Francesco Vianello.  I didn't know his real name at the time though, nobody did.

He was the purest of them all. He not only 'cracked' his own software.  He taught others how to do it as well, through his reverse engineering site.  The archives from this site can be found here:

[http://71.6.196.237/fravia/what_new.htm](http://71.6.196.237/fravia/what_new.htm)

Fravia had many aliases.  He even invented the myth of 'Fjlar Ravia.'  See below:

[http://www.searchlores.org/io13.htm](http://www.searchlores.org/io13.htm)

[http://www.woodmann.com/crackz/Orc.htm](http://www.woodmann.com/crackz/Orc.htm)

In real life I believe he was a professor of history and philosophy.  I don't know how he became interested in computers.

At some point after he met Richard Stallman, he became very interested in the free-software movement, and became an outspoken user and advocate of 'Ubuntu.'  It was at this point that he gave-up his work in reverse engineering to concentrate on what he called "Searching."  This is what 'we' would call datamining; but his approach was philosophical.  It involved not only finding information, but learning to be discriminating about that information, and the 'reality' it represented.  He even coined the term 'reality-cracking.'  His 'Searchlores' website is still being hosted.  I believe it's being maintained by his sister, and it can be found here:

[http://www.searchlores.org/](http://www.searchlores.org/)

Tragically, Sr. Vianello is no longer with us.  He passed away on May 3rd 2009, having succumbed to cancer.  Even though I never met the man, I felt like I knew him.  For me, he represented the honorable and fearless side of humanity.  A side that we so seldom get to see in ourselves and others. At the end of his life, he instructed those that were maintaining mirrors of his reverse engineering site, to take them down, calling them a 'danger' to the 'Community.'  Frankly, I don't know what caused this change-of-heart in him.  While what he was doing was certainly anti-capitalist, and ultimately contributed to what ***some*** may consider intellectual property theft, I consider his reverse engineering work to be a reclaiming of power, not only by the inividual, but ultimately by society itself.

He will be missed...
