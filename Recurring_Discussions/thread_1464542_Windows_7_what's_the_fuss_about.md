### Post by kavon89 on 2010-04-28
It's what Vista should have been. Everything "just works" in a leaner and cleaner way.

For example, Windows Update finds all of the drivers from the manufacturers for you correctly these days. If you're having wireless troubles the Troubleshooter is actually smart and can solve problems for you (like telling me my wireless adapter needs a reset and doing it, which solves the problem). Once you get use to the new taskbar you'll wonder why you didn't have it earlier (but with modification, I like the window labels on). The start menu is well organized and has more useful and quick info in there, like Devices and Printers.

Press the Windows key and start typing the name of a program, file, even a setting you want to change in your computer but don't know the formal name for, and it appears as one of the first choices in the very quick search feature. It's a MUCH better version of Gnome-Do.
