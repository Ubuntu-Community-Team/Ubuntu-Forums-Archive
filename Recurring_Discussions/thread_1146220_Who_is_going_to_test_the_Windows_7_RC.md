### Post by SteveHillier on 2009-05-29
Being a supporter of Windows!!!!.................
by which I mean I have to support Windows on clients machines I have done the necessary and installed Win7.  I placed it on an entirely new platform.
I do have a small whinge about the install process in that while it is formatting the disk there is no progress bar.  Unwary and inexperienced installers might just think this is broken after a few minutes and reboot thus getting into exactly the same position over and over again.
As with other contributers, I have not broken it yet.
I have it set up sharing keyboard, mouse and monitor via a KVM with my Vista machine.  Juxtaposing them like this does highlight some of the inherent slowness of Vista.
Any one can see the family resemblance to Vista which is disappointing - it might give people a resistance to accepting it.
I have installed Open Office, Firefox, Thunderbird, Opera, Safari, GIMP, FileZilla - they all seem to work OK.  I have not installed Office 2007.
Where there was a lack of drivers I used the Vista drivers from the motherboard disk and they seemed to work OK
I installed AVG Antivirus which seems to be OK.
I am trying to use it as if it were a production machine but hasten to add I am not trusting it with any data - that is all stored on our server.
Win7 does seem to have the edge over it's predecessor but I am still reserving judgement.
