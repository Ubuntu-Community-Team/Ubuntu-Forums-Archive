### Post by Telengard C64 on 2011-05-20
I honestly believe that Linux will *never* go mainstream the way some people want it to, and I'm okay with that. As long as Windows exists, most people will use it instead of an alternative OS. Windows is preinstalled and that's good enough for 90% of desktop users (whom I refer to as users).

Anyone who knows enough to look into alternave operating systems probably has their own reason to want something more than the default, preinstalled stuff. Some will want OS X, some will want Linux, some will want FreeBSD (which does share a surprising number crossover users with Linux). A very tiny minority will want experimental operating systems such as AROS, but why worry about .0001% (a very, very tiny number I just picked at random) of the total desktop market?

IMHO the more people coming to Linux expecting it to be a Windows clone, the worse off we all are. People who want Windows should just use Windows. Those people aren't our enemies, nor are they misguided really, they just don't want the same things a Linux user wants.
