### Post by DuckHook on 2013-01-03
> **Yezinki said:**
> What would be your expert recommendations to install Ubuntu on dell XPS 1710, as a single OS replacing W7 Pro

I'd imagine that an "expert" recommendation is not particularly valuable nor even applicable in the Linux world given that its guiding principle is the freedom to choose. Your needs are different from mine which are different from some guru's. Example: I run a different flavour of *buntu on each of my machines, including a number of minimal CLI-only installs. Your computer is powerful enough to run Ubuntu proper, but many users dislike Unity, so it isn't a question of horsepower for them, but of what they want their desktop to look like. What do you like? Kubuntu? Xubuntu? Lubuntu? Or maybe you want a different distro altogether like Fedora or SUSE? If you are a heavy gamer, the best option might even be to stick with Win7. The promotion of any one flavour would be more a function of the poster's quirks more than an objective recommendation. I happen to like Ubuntu. But then, I'm quite happy with Unity. Some people hate it, so they go with Xubuntu.

Since it's so easy to burn a cd/dvd of all the various flavours, why don't you run a bunch of LiveCD sessions to feel them all out and decide which ones suits you best?

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2013-01-03
@ Duckhook: All good points, and I would add to them that if you don't have a list of what you require your machine to do, i.e. what do you do with your computer? ... because of the myriad configurations possible, you would be severely limited in choosing a suitable OS and setting it up the way you want/need. How stable do you need it? Is it to learn about Linux? Is it a production machine?

@ Yezinki: As we are saying; the possibilities are endless. You really need to be clear about what you want your machine to do to make the right choice. Start with something simple - Xubuntu or Lubuntu using the stable LTS release 12.04 LTS - and work from there. You can add the Unity desktop, all the apps from another flavour, desktop environments, upgrade to 12.10, turn it into Ubuntu more or less ... whatever. Once you've decided then reinstall and set it up the way you want it. 

Or try them all out from the LiveCD/USB and see which suits (and which runs best on your machine). Most of all, do some research on it and make a plan.
