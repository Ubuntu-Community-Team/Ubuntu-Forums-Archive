### Post by AnotherDave on 2009-10-19
In Ubuntu, the answer is Yes, says PulseAudio creator Lennart Poettering.

Just as I was thwarted by another impossible Ubuntu sound install, I came across this [Slashdot article]("http://linux.slashdot.org/story/09/10/19/0155235/PulseAudio-Creator-Responds-To-Critics") claiming that PulseAudio is 'overly complex' and not a 'good idea.' Pottering began by defending his software, then subsequently finds that Ubuntu is actually making it "perform worse" in the about-to-be released new Karmic distro!

Pottering is so pissed off that he wrote a special [rant]("http://0pointer.de/blog/projects/pa-in-ubuntu.html") on the subject.

I have been waiting the last six months for Ubuntu to produce a reasonably usable sound architecture, one that can be set up and understood without resorting to some special one-hundred and fifty-two page Forum article THAT STILL DOESN'T FIX THE PROBLEM. It looks like the new 9.10 Karmic won't be any better.

As far as I'm concerned, if Ubuntu doesn't have the sound straightened out in the new release, there's no reason to release at all. I'll be choosing a distro that can handle the basics first.
