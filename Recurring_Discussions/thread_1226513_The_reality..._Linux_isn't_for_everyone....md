### Post by seteshf on 2009-07-29
Two of the biggest reasons I keep Vista around despite all the problems:

1. Wine is getting developed, but copy protection in Windows programs can stop a lot of games from even installing. If I have to copy 5 gigabytes of game folder over to Linux after installing it in Windows, why don't I just play it in Windows?

2. Linux doesn't tend to play well with a lot of de facto industry standards that have been around for years or decades. Media Transfer Protocol is excruciatingly hit or miss and Gstreamer's LAME encoder settings make my brain explode. (Really, what was wrong with LAME's default settings?)

If desktop Linux is going to succeed, I think it has to move past the juvenile anti-Microsoft hate circus. 

It would be easier to win over users by having some transitional support for these formats and protocols, than to just break _everything_ and send the user screaming back to Windows.

1. There's a few ways to fix Windows depending on how bad it's broken and what version you have.

There's automated recovery tools like chkdsk and "Last Known Good Configuration" if the system won't start up. A lot of that has been replaced by self-healing NTFS and per-user registry settings.
