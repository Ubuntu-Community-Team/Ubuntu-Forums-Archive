### Post by ninjaaron on 2011-07-17
I just use ubuntu cause it's what I started using when I switched to Linux and I know it the best.  I've messed with other distros, but they basically seem to come out to the same thing except the organization is different, which can make things frustrating.  I know Ubuntu's quirks, and that's enough for me.  I'm also hopelessly addicted to the on-board cloud services, and I like the software center.

I also like getting the shiny new goodies quickly, which Ubuntu does a decent job with.  I usually have the working dev release on a partition on one of my computers, just so I can try everything as it comes out, and I usually have it as my primary OS on most of my machines before the development cycle is totally finished (usually around the time of beta2 or RC1).

I like the Windows 7 interface, but the ad-ware drove me batty (could never seem to zap all of it), I don't know enoug about it's guts to do all of the tweaks I want, and you actually have to open a browser and use google if you want to find a program (and it might turn out to be malware!).  You might even have to pay for it.

I like OSX a LOT, but I don't have the money for that kind of thing.  With Linux, I can buy the cheapest computer in the store and get reasonable performance. Still, if I had the money, I would have at least one OSX machine alongside my Ubuntu machines.
