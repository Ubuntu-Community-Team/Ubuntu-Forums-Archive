### Post by cardinals_fan on 2009-04-05
I can't give you any reasons because I don't know you.  Everyone has their own individual needs, and Linux may not be right for you.  So, all I can do is tell you why **I** use Linux and BSD:

- Choice: The incredible variety of distributions and window managers is amazing.  You really can have your cake and eat it too - many users here have spinning cubes and flaming windows on their desktop, while I personally prefer a very minimal tiling window manager with no panels.  Many use graphical wizards to get set up, while I prefer to do everything with the command line.  The great thing about Linux distros is that there is one for everybody.

- Security: I don't want you to misunderstand this point.  I have never had a security issue with Windows, and changing your OS for security reasons is a terrible idea.  Only you, the user, can make sure that you stay safe.  I like Linux/BSD because it is easier for me to perform the necessary tasks to secure the system.  These jobs can be done on Windows too; they just take more work and more of my time.  I also feel much safer with open source software, because all vulnerabilities are out in the open where I can know about them.

- Control: I'll admit it; I'm a control freak.  Everything on my system must be under my control for me to be happy.  This is much easier on a transparent Linux or BSD system where I can get down and dirty with the command line and edit simple configuration files.  I love being able to decide everything that happens on my computer.

These are my reasons.  They may or may not apply to you, but I hope that they will be helpful.

By the way, don't judge yourself based on your age.  Us teenagers (I'm 15) may be immature idiots, but we can let the "responsible adults" handle the judgments :P

### Post by ryaxnb on 2009-04-07
I switched to ubuntu because i like the concept of a Free Software OS.
By free software, i mean, free to pass on, free to use, free to inspect, free to modify, free for someone else to do any of those things. These are freedoms you just can't get with Windows or Mac OS X. With Ubuntu you get a truly Free Software OS. (mostly anyway)

I also love the customization. 
Ubuntu is extensively customizable  in every way, Ubuntu is truly what you make with it.
Ubuntu also brings several other features to the party:
* LiveCD/DVD support
* Boot from flash
* Runs well on Intel Atom, Pentium III and other lowpower processors
* Runs well on any amount of RAM from 256MB up (note: full GNOME not usable on 256)
* Compiz gives great flash and fun effects, much better than Aero 
* The GNOME HIG provides for a consistent, cohesive expeirence
* Supports other architectures then x86, and better x64 support than Windows.
* Command line provides power and flexibility lacking in windows, although not in OS X
* Great community.
