### Post by Roasted on 2010-01-04
I recently compared Kubuntu and Mandriva KDE. There's no doubt Ubuntu is pretty solid with Gnome, but I'm not really into Gnome that much anymore. I keep flirting with KDE. Comparing Kubuntu and Mandriva, I think it's pretty evident Mandriva focuses more on the KDE side. It's very sad to see Kubuntu getting the left-overs, because Fedora, SuSE and Mandriva all do a much better job of making KDE feel at home - whereas on Kubuntu it just doesn't feel... right. However, I run Kubuntu as we speak, and it does the job for me and I still enjoy it.

I will say, however, it was VERY nice having Mandriva already have my broadcom sta driver integrated and supported out of box. Sure, Kubuntu notified me about it to download it, no big deal, but Mandriva supported it out of box.

And the hands down winner in terms of network managers between Mandriva and Kubuntu, no doubt, goes to Mandriva. The default Network Manager in Kubuntu is so horrible it's unreal. The network manager in Mandriva was extremely slick to use and felt very snappy and did the job well. I use WICD in Kubuntu, which I like a lot, and Mandriva's network manager felt more WICD-ish - which caught my attention right away.

I plan to install either Mandriva or openSuSE KDE when I get home on my spare computer. I'll always love *buntu, but being a growing KDE fan, I just want to use KDE to the max - something Kubuntu is (currently) somewhat lacking.

Just my 2 cents. Your mileage may vary.
