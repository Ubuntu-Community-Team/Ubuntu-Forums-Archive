I know that Mint fixes some, perhaps many of Ubuntu's bugs, but from what I have read, it inherits most of them and simply passes them along. Mint arrives a few months after an Ubuntu release (after Ubuntu has updated a few times, fixing a few bugs that got past Alpha and Beta) and includes Ubuntu's updates. So it always has the advantage of *appearing* to be "less buggy than Ubuntu." In fact Mint *is* Ubuntu, and if new releases of Mint corresponded to the corresponding Ubuntu release, they would both have identical bugs in equal amounts.

Yet there are some things that Mint simply does better. The current Xfce edition, for example, even though based on Xubuntu Jaunty, runs circles around it!
