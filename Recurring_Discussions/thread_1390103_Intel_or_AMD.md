### Post by ECPCLINUX on 2010-01-25
Hi, I have the AMD Phenom II x3 720, Intel Core2Duo e6850 & e8400. I find the AMD is best for gaming but the Core2Duos are best when I'm transcribing DVDs. My sound shudders a bit when transcribing in my AMD. Now that could easily be attributed to the on board sound nevertheless, they all use on board sound. If you go the AMD route, for new CPU value, make sure you use a motherboard which is compatible with Linux. My AMD uses a MSI K9A2 CF v2 motherboard and it will not work with Linux. It even gives me some messg. to contact vendor. I cannot remember what the messg. was but, it was due to no support for linux. My chipset is the AMD 790x  back when I tried to installed Ubuntu and Mint Linux it was not supported. I love my AMD and I use it with Win 7 (which I got for free, the house party thing) for gaming mostly, the e6850 is my wife's and the e8400 games fine but I use it mostly for transcribing movies (rarely), browsing, Skype, etc. My server is my wife's old mobo, memory, case, etc. using a Celeron 3200 dual core, 2GB stick of memory and works great with Ubuntu. All other computers have the same amount of memory. Now as for overall value, the Intel C2D are old now, so you can get them used at eBay for a decent price, is how I got mine. Otherwise, AMD seems like a better value overall when buying it new. Nevertheless, My AMD cost me $123 out the door new but now Newegg, where I purchased it, has it for 165 dollars before tax, big jump.  The e8400 vs the AMD X3 720, in my opinion, are very close in performance. I feel AMD x3 720 edges out a bit on games and the e8400 in transcribing movies. My recommendation, Just checkout prices compare and buy based on what you like do most, price on CPU, motherboards, memory, comparability with your favorite OS. Finally, if you don't mind old tech checkout prices on old used C2D like the e8400 or above and if you don't game newer games, transcribe movies or other resource hogs activities, just get a cheap dual core CPU AMD or Intel and Ubuntu will do great. My server, which is very basic using Ubuntu Desktop, work very nice and fast I notice no lag or issues for basic stuff.
