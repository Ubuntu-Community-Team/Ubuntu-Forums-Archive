### Post by Grimhound on 2009-10-16
Adobe Flash, once Macromedia Flash, has become an essential part of the internet experience. It is integrated into most websites to some degree, with many being entirely reliant on it to function properly. Yet here-in lies the greatest issue with Linux (for me). Flash support for Linux is atrocious to a degree where I cannot access much of the internet due to the simple inability of Flash to correctly render and play things. For instance:

[[IMG]http://imgur.com/FppPkl.png[/IMG]](http://imgur.com/FppPk.png)
Here is the site for Borderlands, an upcoming game for the PC and modern consoles. Now, where could the issue possibly be in this? Well, the issue is that the boxes that should be appearing to enable the entrance of your age to enter the site simply do not appear. They aren't there. They don't show up. This is one of the big issues with Flash on Linux.

[[IMG]http://imgur.com/LKAO2l.png[/IMG]](http://imgur.com/LKAO2.png)
The second issue. This is what occurs most often for me. When I attempt to start a video, a million clicks can be had, yet it never properly begins to load the actual video.

[[IMG]http://imgur.com/FcxJAl.png[/IMG]](http://imgur.com/FcxJA.png)
The third issue. After many refreshes on several sites, I finally get the video to get past the intro screen. Usually by forcing it via the Play command. Yet now I'm stuck on an infinite unconnected load screen because Flash failed to pick up the link to the actual content.


Note: This is not an issue with me or my hardware. This has been a constant struggle that I have tested. All of the content appears fine for me when accessed via a PC running Windows 7 through any of the 4 browsers I have installed. It loads correctly and without delay. On Ubuntu, the only thing I have installed is the Ubuntu Restricted Extras package to enable multimedia support.

### Post by Exodist on 2009-10-20
I big thing about jerky Flash Video on Linux is the CPU scaling dropping back down. Simple way to test this is to move your browser around the desktop for a few seconds. If the video plays good for a second or two after you stop moving the browser it the issue is CPU scaling. Just go into your BIOS and turn it off or for Gnome Users add the CPU throttling applet to your task bar and turn it up to max CPU usage when playing flash videos.
