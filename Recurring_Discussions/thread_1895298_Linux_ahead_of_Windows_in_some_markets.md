However, in the poorer countries in Eastern Europe, there are whole cracking communities that operate in a way very similar to the FOSS communities. People over there don't use Linux, because there is no real benefit. They practically get a GPL version of Windows 7 Ultimate and all the software that they need (again practically distributed under GPL). 

This is working to MS's advantage in a big way. They are not really losing money since all those users and crackers wouldn't be able to afford the license fee anyway. On the other hand, if all the efforts of those communities were turned to the benefit of Linux, then that would help Linux incredibly. Just think about the impact of increasing the number of Linux users from 1% to over 10%-20% (if everybody in Eastern Europe switches over).

### Post by sunfromhere on 2011-12-16
Ah, Eastern Europe, my home :D A couple of opinions...

The overuse of pirated software here is such an interesting topic, and I believe that finances aren't the nr 1 reason anymore, "prestige" is. If you can afford 500-1000 euros for a computer/laptop, why don't you include an OS price in your calculations (usually consumer electronics are bought in 12-24 monthly payments)? One "must" have the newest cell phone, branded clothes and a computer running the most expensive software.

Example 1. Recently I had to "fix" a Windows 7 machine for a friend, there's three of them using that computer, all under age of 20. I asked them what they did on the computer; Facebook, Youtube, general surfing, music was the answer. 
What I found on that computer: Windows 7 Ultimate Edition, MS Office 2010, Adobe CS 5, Nero, couple of Alcohol software and TWO! paid (cracked) antivirus programs, both of them never updated and never run. All of these programs were pirated. They had no need for any of those programs; but they just -HAD- to have them. Why? Well, because "everyone" has them, if you don't have Windows 7 Ultimate (!!) you are "poor". Nevermind that 90% of those who have them installed have no clue on how to use them. 

Example 2. On our local forum I follow I found a post that said this: I just bought a new laptop, specs are ..., do you think it's a good one, I will use it for surfing and Facebook; it came with Linux but I will remove it because it's no good and install Windows 7 Ultimate.

Example 3. In a small family company I worked at this summer they had 3 computers. As inspections for business software are harsh, all of the software there is licensed. Interestingly, they are using OpenOffice. When the person in charge heard about Linux (ok, all she heard was "free of charge") she immediately asked me what it is and how she can get it (their computers are quite old, running XP still, and will probably be replaced next year).

Aside companies, I personally do not know a single person who owns a paid Windows (and Office) product (don't get me started on the games...). Even when a Home Edition comes preinstalled, they remove it for Ultimate Editions. Somewhere I read that Microsoft themselves said that they will not fight piracy in home computers, because it's much more important to have people USING Windows, even pirated (as opposed to choosing some other OS). 

So, until the mentality here changes, the nr1 OS will be **pirated** Windows. I wonder what is the percentage of licensed Windows compared to Linux for home users. My guess? The same.
