### Post by bunburya on 2010-02-25
There's evil and there's irresponsibility. Evil is a very difficult concept to define, and as I don't really believe in such a thing as absolute evil (because it carries divine connotations), defining it in any useful way is subjective by nature.

Nobody is suggesting that making a profit is evil *per se* (except maybe communists), but it is not difficult to see how some corporations behave irresponsibly. Examples are violating competition law, disingenuous marketing campaigns, violating employee protection legislation, reneging on promises to customers/employees/trade unions/etc, allowing conflicts of interest to occur and continue without disclosing them, making secret profits, abusing confidential information, etc etc etc. There are many more examples.

It is entirely correct to say that corporations are not evil just because they make money. But some posts here some close to rejecting the concept of corporate social responsibility and advocating an "anything goes" approach to the market which would be a recipe for disaster. If "evil" is defined as being similar or identical to irresponsible practices then it is very possible for a corporation to come within the definition of "evil". Microsoft would be one such entity. Google may be though I have seen no evidence that would lead me to believe that they are. Many firms nowadays have a corporate sociual responsibility section on their website.
