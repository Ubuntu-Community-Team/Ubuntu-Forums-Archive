### Post by mudfly9 on 2011-11-06
I've finally un-installed Ubuntu from my computer and am back to the excellent Windows 7. Why? Simple:

1. Accidentally opening the Unity sidebar every other time I click the 'back' button on my browser. Extremely annoying.

2. Triple clicking on the address bar in Chrome and the unbelievably arrogant way the whole issue is dealt with. 1001 extensions but not a mouse clicking option?

3. Screen/power saver still active when watching streaming content causing blackouts. Which leads on to...

4. I have one of those graphics chipsets (admittedly made by that niche company, Intel) that Linux has just deemed "wrong" so I get a black screen on startup. That's right, the first ever operating system to upgrade and decide your screen should be black. I've managed a workaround for startup but once it's gone into power saving, there's no going back. Quality stuff.

I've been a passionate advocate for over a year now, but a mixture of arrogance and bumbling errors now make Linux less useful for me than Vista. I hope to come back one day and find an operating system that has truly caught up because there's some amazing work going on with it. It's just not quite ready, for me.

Exactly. Ubuntu != Linux. There are so many other distros to choose from that there's no reason not to give one or two others a shot. Test driving new distros is fun!
