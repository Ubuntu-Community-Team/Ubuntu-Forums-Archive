### Post by Smallwheels on 2011-08-19
The HP Touchpad was discontinued August 17, 2011. Best Buy and others  took in 500,000 of them and have sold only a few thousand. HP just paid  Best Buy off for their purchase due to them being really angry that they  didn't sell. 

Now Best Buy and other outlets will be dumping the Touchpads. Originally  the small memory model sold for $499. Just last week the price was  lowered $100 to $399. Who knows what the price will be by next week. It  is possible they will just dump them for $99 or less very soon. 

If there is any version of Linux that has a chance of working on one of  these I'd like to scoop one up. I'm a beginner at Linux and don't know  much about using the terminal, but people here have helped me get 10.04  working on my HP computer. 

I really would love to have a functioning tablet that could use the web  via WiFi and play movies or music that I transfer from my desktop  computer. I'd also like to show video and other OpenOffice.org  presentations to potential clients using a very portable tablet  computer. 

Is there any group of people currently hacking or coding for tablets  like the HP Touchpad? If 500,000 of them get dumped on the market it  would seem like a great opportunity for the Linux community to get some  new toys. 

Let me know what's up.

Thank you.
