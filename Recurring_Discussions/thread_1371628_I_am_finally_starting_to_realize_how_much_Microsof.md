### Post by Cavsfan on 2010-01-03
I had never jumped into an operating system that was really new. I bought a new fairly decent PC
and I got Vista Home Premium attached to it. Well, I had been used to XP Pro before this and Vista 
was a huge disappointment as I soon learned. It had been around a while and one would have thought MS 
would have worked the bugs out of it by the time I got it. Not so... Pure crap!

My son likes games and plays a few. He found we could install windows 7 RC and dual boot both systems. 
About this time I wanted to try Ubuntu, so I made a tri-boot system. Windows 7 was clearly a lot better than Vista!
I really liked Ubuntu though with it's small "profile" and powerful capabilities.

I ended up getting a big discount on a Windows 7 Home Premium edition, which at first I thought was very good.
Then I run into a problem backing up my files and data using the built-in program.

I submit the error in their forum. It appeared I was the first to get this problem with 7, although Vista had the 
same problem, it was not identical nor did it fix my problem. Their solution was to use a third party free backup program. 
But, none of the programs they offered did differential, incremental backups like I want.

So, then all of a sudden their solution turns to doing a clean install. I had just installed it about 2 months ago and had 
no other problems. Why the crap should I go to all of that trouble to backup everything AGAIN and do a clean install? 
I have only one problem and that was it. I found that to be very disappointing that that "was the solution"!

I should have either used Vista until they worked the bugs out of 7, which will probably take until they are almost 
ready to release their next OS, or I could go with Ubuntu full time.
I think I will go with Ubuntu! My son will still use windows for his games, but the hell with Microsoft and their bloated crap!

It took me a while, but I think I am finally seeing the light! :guitar:
