### Post by barthel on 2009-10-02
GeForce Go 7900 GS, voted "yes"

Personally, I'd prefer to use 100% open source. I don't want my hardware or my data held hostage. I don't want to be forced to buy an upgrade I don't need (and perhaps cannot afford) just because a company's bottom line is at stake.

However, nVidia is currently making (IMNSHO) the best cards at a decent price **and** they are providing decent drivers for Linux et al. So when I bought this laptop, one of the requirements was the nVidia card.

The next best thing would be for nVidia to set up a source code escrow. If for any reason nVidia should go under, the source would be released to the community to provide legacy support. (Again, I don't know whether that's possible, depending upon any cross-licensing they've done.)

BTW--as far as the "everything being done on the software side" goes, it just so happens that is a direct result of a Microsoft tactic started in the 90's to lock peripherals into the MS-Windows platform. The first peripherals to become casualties of this change were modems and printers. If you ran anything other than MS, you had to pay a premium for "un-lobotomized" hardware...
