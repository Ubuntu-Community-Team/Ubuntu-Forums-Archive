### Post by stwschool on 2009-05-05
I notice some definite religious themes in both the way people talk about linux, and how they react to criticism, and a good deal of it really is very similar to my experiences dealing with American evangelical Christians (I ran into a bunch of them in LA, a truly scary experience).

I see a thread talking about 'converting' people, and people looking down at Windows users as though they are inferior, and it's exactly the same as the evangelical religious types, talking of the need to save people, and looking down with pity at the non-believers.

I see no point in trying to convert people for it's own sake. Linux numbers don't really matter, after all, if we like it and it works for us, that's great right? So I don't convert people.

I have pushed Linux at work (I'm a teacher) to replace the machines used in the staff room, not for the 'religious' reason, but to reduce my workload. I had enough of dealing with malware installed by assorted teachers. Had I just done it for ideological reasons, it wouldn't have worked. Instead, I looked at what they needed, looked at the problem, and came to linux as the solution. Had I gone the religious route, I'd have started with linux as solution, and looked for a problem for it to fix.

Similarly, my boss. I was so tempted to evangelise, then stopped myself as I realised what I was doing. A few months later he came to me asking for ubuntu on his laptop as he was really not enjoying his viruses (teachers kept sending him virus-ridden files). I installed, but only after a good long conversation about his needs so I could custom-build his experience and make sure that it was appropriate for him. He's now enjoying the linux experience, and I have a feeling that this will spread.

So the point is, we don't need to get religious about it, instead, we need to be sensible, think about people's needs, and implement the right solutions for them, be they Windows (for gamers), dual-boot (for gamers who want their serious apps on a proper OS), linux (for people who can get all the apps they need on linux and don't want malware) or linux + xp virtualbox (for those who need photoshop and have a decent computer) or something else.

</war and peace>

Personally, I am completely deaf when people come up to me and tell me that they (and only they) know who god is, what he wants and how he wants me to spend my life. But I have an open ear if somebody comes up to me and tells me that I am acting in irresponsible or morally wrong ways.
