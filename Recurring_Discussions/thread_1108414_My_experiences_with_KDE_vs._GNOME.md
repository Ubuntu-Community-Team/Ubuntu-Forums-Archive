### Post by jbysmith on 2009-03-27
Plenty of "KDE vs Gnome" threads all over the place, hoping this doesn't turn into one of those ;D

That said, they're both good. Gnome was designed for simplicity, KDE can be anything but if you let it.. some people find it a little too complicated for their needs. KDE4 out of the box is actually rather slick. As far as the gimmicks go, KDE is absurdly customizable... if the bouncy cursors bug you, turn 'em off.

In the end, it doesn't really matter.  You can run KDE apps on Gnome, and vice versa.  Pick the one you feel more comfortable with.  There's other choices to pick from too of course, Gnome getting the most exposure here as it's Ubuntu's default.  A fairly complete list is on [Wikipedia.](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X_window_manager)

### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2009-03-28
I hate to say it, because every interaction I've ever had with Kubuntu developers has been very positive; but Kubuntu is probably one of the worst KDE distros around.  Add to that the fact that 4.x has been rough going for the last couple of versions, and it's not surprising you'd come away with a negative opinion.

I started out with KDE years ago on SimplyMEPIS, which was and still is a great KDE distro (still with v3, though, which is part of the reason I switched).  I find Kubuntu usable because I've been with KDE long enough to know how to make it into what I need it to be.  If you're comparing the two as Ubuntu vs. vanilla, out-of-the-box Kubuntu, I'm not at all surprised GNOME wins (especially if you're already familiar with it).

The thing about the gimmicks, it's kind of like Compiz: everyone looks at it and immediately notices the gimmicks and the eye candy, and says "it's all about gimmicks and eye candy".  But all the good, solid usability features that are there with the gimmicks get passed over, because in general they are too boring to mention.  I mean, nobody makes youtube videos about the Samba configuration tool or using kio-slaves in konqueror.
