### Post by NightwishFan on 2010-09-27
Sane, secure defaults. That is why I like Ubuntu's Gnome Desktop. It works the way you want it without having to muck around in a system control panel.

Speed. Ubuntu (using Linux) has a top notch cpu and io scheduler that works invisibly under the hood to keep tasks responsive whither they are running all the time, or idle for months. Just the other day I was converting a video, downloading files, listening to music, and playing a complex 3d game on my low end laptop. Not a hint of a stutter.

Security. Ubuntu is a strict multiuser system, which means that files and permissions are handled from the ground up, making things a lot more secure. All networking ports are closed by default, meaning you can access services, however others can not access yours.

Ease Of Use. The interface is clean, friendly, and well defined. Consistency, correctness, multilingual support are all high priority.

Administration. Ubuntu has free updates for as long as the current version is supported, with Ubuntu 10.04 I believe this is around 3 years. Upgrades to new versions are always free.
