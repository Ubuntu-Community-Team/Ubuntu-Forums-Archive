### Post by conundrumx on 2011-08-18
There's a couple things to note about this.  First off, this is not how Apple wants people to upgrade their OS.  It's in their best interest for people to use and be aware of the Mac App Store, since they not only get a nice chunk of each sale (30%), but it also makes the already simple process of installing applications on OS X even easier; which makes happier customers.  They want you to get everything you can from their app store.

Additionally, this is not your only option for a recovery disk.  Apple released a free utility that will turn any flash drive into an OS X recovery disk, in addition to the partition 10.7 creates by default.  And finally, this is not the only way to make a bootable install disk for 10.7  There's a DMG (Apple's home baked disk image format) inside the installer which has been widely circulated online since 10.7 went GM that can be burned to DVD or copied to a thumb drive.

This product exists for IT departments and people who live out in the boonies.
