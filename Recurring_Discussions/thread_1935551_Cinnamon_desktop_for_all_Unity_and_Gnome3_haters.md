### Post by Bao2 on 2012-03-04
As [Linus Torvalds says]("http://www.theregister.co.uk/2011/08/05/linus_slams_gnome_three/"), Gnome3 (and Unity) are bad bad desktops for people using Linux to work, not just surf the web.

The main problems in my view are:
- By default they don't want you to create launchers in the desktop easily (you can create them using some "guru" but not rightclicking and choosing that option in a pop up menu)
- Dash launcher bar is tremendously annoying compared to having the launchers in the upper panel 
- Knowing the programs that are opened is much more difficult that just looking to the taskbar in old gnome
- Everybody was using some great little apps running in the panels. No more.
- Apps go in maximize mode when opened
- I usually have many programs installed and searching for them is a pain compared to the List of apps in old gnome.
- Launching an app in old gnome was always two clicks away even if you didn't knew the name of the app. It goes to around five clicks in new desktops Gnome3 and Unity
- There were not need using the hand in old gnome. Now you must use the hand on the keyboard many times. For example to Alt+W and see the programs running in actual desktop.

But great news: Cinnamon. Is becoming absolutely great and restoring in each version more and more of the desktop we all want (all that use Linux for work, not for surf the web I mean). 
Just install ubuntu and first thing you do after is install Cinnamon. Log out and Log in and your nightmare disappears and Joy returns to use Linux again.

Is incredible Linus Torvalds and other important developpers have no influence in the wrong direction of Gnome. Who wants their desktop 1920x1200 screen working as an iphone??? Is just stupid as Linus says.

Well: Cinnamon seems the answer to all our desperation lately in ubuntu.:p

### Post by Copper Bezel on 2012-03-06
[QUOTE=Bao2]The iphone look is only liked by teenagers that spend their time just customizing linux but never using it for some real work where they find that scrolls bars suck completely and hotkeys that use Alt don't work at least the app is maximized.[/QUOTE]
Look, could people please stop saying this? These rant threads are common, and so are threads on Cinnamon, and you can prefer these things and talk about these things and have whatever preferences you like, but don't make claims about how something that you don't use works. There are plenty of people using Unity and Gnome Shell for real work, every day, and who find them to be more productive than the old desktop model. You don't have to like, use, or get to know these desktops in depth; use what you like. But don't make the assumption that everyone else's preferences and workflow are the same as your own. 

If you don't understand it, fine - say so. I don't get Unity. It feels like a cluttered mess to me. But there are people who find it more productive for their purposes, and not just to open a web browser and forget about it. I happen to think that Gnome Shell is the most intuitive and productive desktop I've ever used. That doesn't mean that I expect Unity users, or Cinnamon users, or Windows and Mac users, to feel the same way. There's a learning curve to every desktop, and there are habits and use cases unique to ever user. You *cannot* say "objectively" that one or another of these is more productive. 

I mean, you mention scrolling. I work with a lot of documents and do a lot of scrolling. I also have a multitouch trackpad, and I find scrollbars painfully slow and inefficient to use. I do not want to work with something like that; it would be a serious step back. You could create a list of things you don't like about an interface, and I could explain point by point how each of the features you miss is addressed by something else, and ultimately, you *wouldn't*, in fact, be convinced, any more than I would be if we reversed the situation.

But - that's what we expect. You're not *supposed* to be convinced. You have your preferences. But don't confuse your preferences for some kind of objective fact.
