### Post by oarion7 on 2010-12-20
Well, my first experience with Linux was through Red Hat Linux 9 (ca. 2002 or 2003). *Not* the best distro to start with, most certainly, but at the time I had too slow an Internet connection to even consider downloading an OS, and it was the only Linux distro for sale near me. It was because of the difficulty level of Red Hat, and the frustration of trying to get my modem to work under Linux with no command line experience, that the experience did not last long and I only ever barely dabbled in Linux until I tried it again in 2007 or even early 2008.

The beautiful thing about Ubuntu is that it slowly adjusted me into working in a Linux environment; slowly, but *surely*, and inevitably. One certainly cannot get the **most** out of Ubuntu without a great deal of command line work, but it is **usable** with none at all. Inevitably, after some time and interest, you become a user who could comfortably sit behind any Linux operating system and work, possibly after learning a couple of new, distro-specific commands and file trees.

After having used it for only a few years, Ubuntu has made me a competent user of all major Linux and Unix-like distros after negligible prior experience. That is what makes Ubuntu a great distro in my book, and a **solid candidate** for the best. I cannot state that it *is* in any way the best distro for certain, however: A better judge is one who has used a great deal more than have I.
