### Post by Untitled_No4 on 2010-04-08
Kubuntu started as community project before it became an official Ubuntu release and by what I can tell the Kubuntu developers have slightly different goals for it than those of Ubuntu, and there make different decisions than those which are made for Ubuntu.

As for the lack of Kubuntu-specific themes, it's actually a decision taken by the developers as they aim to make the distribution as the KDE developers intended.

Personally I don't feel it's neglected at all, I think the Kubuntu developers do a great job and I appreciate the freedom they are given to spin the distribution as they see fit. Take the drama about the control buttons position in Ubuntu, it's due to this freedom that Kubuntu users could completely ignore the issue (or be amused by it, take your pick). 

As for Ubuntu One, my understanding is that Ubuntu developers are expecting the Kubuntu community to make a KDE client for it, but it looks like Kubuntu developers have other things they want to achieve first and since I'm happy with the way things are going, I have no problem with that. After all, if I really wanted to use Ubuntu One I could have used the Gnome client and theme it to look like Kubuntu. 

As for the name, It's Ubuntu + K as it features KDE. It also has a meaning:
""Kubuntu", pronounced /ku&#720;&#712;bu&#720;ntu&#720;/ koo-BOON-too, means "towards humanity" in Bemba, and is derived from ubuntu ("humanity"). The K at the beginning represents the KDE desktop/applications which Kubuntu runs. By coincidence, Kubuntu also means "free" in Kirundi."
([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubuntu](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubuntu))
I wouldn't call my first born Kubuntu, but it really is a non-issue for me.
