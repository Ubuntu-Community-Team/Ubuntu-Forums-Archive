### Post by forrestcupp on 2012-11-01
GNU Hurd began in 1990 as a FOSS replacement for Unix. Linus came out with the Linux kernel after they had already started Hurd. When Linux came out and Linus decided to use the GPL, they realized it was a waste of time to keep developing a kernel, when there was a good option already usable. After that, there was no real motivation to continue with the Hurd. The only reason it's still in development is because there are a few people who have hobbyist interest to keep it going.
