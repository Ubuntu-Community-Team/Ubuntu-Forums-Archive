### Post by jrothwell97 on 2010-06-07
> **kaldor said:**
> NeXT and *BSD. It's been changed to the point where you won't see much in terms of BSD anymore though. At least on the average user's view.

Not really: there's still a terminal, a root file system, sudo, file and folder permissions, etc.

The only difference is the way it's presented: the FHS folders are hidden by default on OS X, / is labelled in the Finder as being the boot hard drive (Macintosh HD by default) and everything else is mounted under /Volumes. (This is also hidden by default in the Finder, since everything already appears in the sidebar and in Computer.)

So, it's still BSD (really, NeXTSTEP 5) underneath. The difference is primarily in the tightness of the API - practically everything on the surface is written in the built-in APIs, Carbon (transitional) and Cocoa (managed, not dissimilar to .NET).

Returning back to the OP's point: fanboyism is as foolish as any other sort of fanaticism.
