### Post by freebird54 on 2007-07-15
It is a lot longer than 10 years!

There is actually good sense behind the original adoption of the 'blue' themes in Windows (and elsewhere).  There was a very expensive research project that determined not only that blue was optimum for a greater number of people (in terms of clarity without eyestrain), but that a certain SHADE of blue was best.  In the early days of colour, even such programs as WP (yes, on DOS) were quick to default to blue (w/white text) - and Amiga came with a blue theme originally as well - based on the same research.

However - that doesn't mean that it is the ONLY theme that works well - especially as people age, the colour values that are optimum can change as well.  You may nave noticed how common black themes are amoung the younger users of computer systems - to the point that they are nearly unusable for us old farts!

Long live the 'brown' (or the gold, or the orange) as a reasonable alternative!!

Another very classy looking theme set would include a wine colour, along with some silver and perhaps some tan and/or deep blue.  Experiment people - that is the point!
