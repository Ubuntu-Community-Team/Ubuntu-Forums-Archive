### Post by WinRiddance on 2012-02-08
They've actually been promising Linux compatibility since 2008. We do use MagicJack. We actually have two of them now. Initially we kept an old Windoze laptop running just for the MJ. But ever since the MJ PLUS was released we bought two of them so we wouldn't have to use Windoze at all anymore. Just create a small Windoze partition which you'll need in order to register the new MJ PLUS devices. Select the option for ... using without a computer ... during the registration process.

Once the registration is completed, you can unplug the MJ PLUS from your computer and plug them directly into a wall outlet and your Internet router instead. That's what we did. Granted, you don't have the on-screen dialpad that way. We purchased a cordless phone with 3 handsets. The phones have internal phone books for instant one button dialing and since only the main handset needs to be plugged into the router, you can place the others anywhere that you like. MJ PLUS works great for us and nobody can top $30 per year for local and national long distance combined.
