### Post by issih on 2009-05-06
The linux community has always been a self selecting group of slightly tech oriented individuals. Generally we have a basic grasp of what a computer can and can't do, and have an understanding of the cli (even if its just that program name then enter makes that program run).

For these people the linux community works perfectly, the level of help needed and supplied match, and we all get on happily.

The problems arise when people who do not have that background try to join the party, and we do need to find ways to solve this.

As I've commented before, the use of cli solutions happens for well understood reasons (brevity, specificity and ease of communicability in a text based forum) but and its a big one, for those users that really haven't a clue, we need to find a way to help them learn what is going on.

For a true newbie an arcane cli command is nothing more than magic, and they glean no insight from running it. We would do well to explain what these commands do when we supply them to new users.

In the past I  have advocated banning cli solutions from the absolute beginners forum, and I still feel that something like that, or a requirement to explain the commands being given in detail would help educate those users to the point where the community works for them too. Sadly no solution to implement such a policy in a workable manner exists.

Nonetheless, imo, all of us should endeavour to help those who are really lost more carefully. Ubuntu's stated aim is to be useable by "human beings" we should remember that..
