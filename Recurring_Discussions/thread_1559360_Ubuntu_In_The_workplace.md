### Post by Grenage on 2010-08-23
This is really more of a Community Cafe discussion.

The problem isn't people's ability to use a new OS; the problem is that most businesses are built around MS products.  It's so easy to be locked in.

I'm a network administrator, and I use Ubuntu on my main PC.  It does most of the things *I* need, but it wouldn't be very useful for most of the users.  When you've got proprietary databases that tie in with Office, you are pretty screwed.

That aside, I've yet to see anything that could replace the Microsoft Active directory system.  It's damn good for centrally managing things.

### Post by Johnsie on 2010-08-24
It's a shame this topic got buried. This is an important issue for me as I work as a programmer at a company of around 30 people. We use Ubuntu on one of our servers and one desktop but I am hesitant to force people to migrate to Ubuntu.

The main reason for this has already been mentioned in a previous post. Outlook, exchange and Microsoft Active directory system are extremely useful in business. This technology is at the core of most businesses. Ubuntu doesn't have a high quality solution to replace this combination. Evolution is really ugly and looks unprofessional. The Active Directory features in Evolution are absolutely horrible. Communication is the most important thing in business and Ubuntu just doesn't cut it yet.

There are other issues that involve getting specialised software and hardware to work, but getting communication to work in a professional way is probably the most important thing.

I do a wubi install on every machine in work, but most people choose Windows because it's what they are familiar with.
