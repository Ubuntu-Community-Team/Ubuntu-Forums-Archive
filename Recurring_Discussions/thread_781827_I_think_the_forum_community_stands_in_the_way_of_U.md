What happened to Linux for Humans? it seems to me the forums are moving to "Linux for Linux users."

 It seems to me that the people that come here regularly don't have the opinion that Ubuntu needs to progress. I keep on hearing cries for stability, when Ubuntu is 1000x more stable than Windows or Mac. People seem to want things, and the devs give them to them, that only a small percentage of Ubuntu users even know about. most Ubuntu users dont come to these forums, especially if the huge 10-20 million numbers are anywhere near correct. The only opinions expressed here are of computer tech/geek type people who dont care about looks, intuitiveness, or organization. They mod it out how they want it to, and dont care anymore. They then voice their opinion, an we get nowhere.

I make this prediction. Intrepid Ibex will not have any huge advancements we have been waiting for ( we as in the general Ubuntu/Computer user populace). We might get better internet support, but I doubt we will get the step needed to put Ubuntu into the stratosphere.

Here is an experience I will share with you illustrating my point.

I was with a friend of mine the other day, and she is a HUGE Mac fan. She was showing me photobooth, and all of the cool effects it can do. I said " Hey, my computer has the same thing," so I showed her Cheese. She immediately said " That is not as good though." I showed her all of the coll things it could do, and it could do just as much if not more. I asked her why she thought I wasn't as good, and know what she said? 

"It looks like a toy."

That is just it. we need to progress, but this goes beyond my recent crusade for a better look. 

The community is more than just these forums. Isn't our aim to create a Linux for Humans? Yes the current interface is fine, come one now- I use it. Of course I like it. We need to push for an interface that makes it look as good as it is- we all know that

Ubuntu is stable
Ubuntu is professional
Ubuntu is easy

But it doesn't look that way. We innovate right? How about for once we not only make a better interface, but do more to go beyond the big wigs at Redmond and Apple.

Edit: I know that there are "plans," but there were "plans" for Hardy. None of the themes though all people can agree on, and by default I have a feeling that we will be stuck with something that still is bland, unorganized, and not the best ( we are striving for the best right?) by the default.

Edit: I know stability is important, but how about we focus on the look for this release, and maintain stability... its something like 70% of people want to focus on stability here, and only 10% think we should focus on the new look.
