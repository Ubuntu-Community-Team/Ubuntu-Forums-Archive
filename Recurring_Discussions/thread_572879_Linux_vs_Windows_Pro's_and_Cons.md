### Post by Ceemee on 2007-10-11
As a side note to the gaming/Linux thing, if your computer BIOS can boot off USB, to prevent messing with partitioning at all you can install to an external USB hard drive.

This can be done without messing with your boot sector at all really easy.

1. Open your computer case, disconnect all your hard drives. (to prevent your MBR from being modified at all.)

2. Boot your ubuntu CD, with your USB hard drive connected, install as normal, selecting your only option, the USB drive.

3. shut down your computer after installation, and re-connect your internal hard-drive(s).

4. re-order your boot priority in your BIOS to boot
       1. USB
       2. CD
       3. Hard drive

5. Enjoy windows booting with NO difference at all (no LILO or GRUB), and booting straight into (X/K/Ed)buntu whenever your USB drive is connected at boot-up.

will also help people who want to try Ubuntu with the drivers and different things installed that require a re-boot (Nvidia driver) that a Live-CD cannot do.

NOTE:  This install will be for your hardware configuration only, you will get boot errors if you connect this to a different computer that is not identical in hardware configuration.  This is not a LIVE install.

NOTE #2: This install will only work with Linux on the USB drive, windows is too gay, and feels they need to re-initialize the USB driver during boot-up, and will not work.  You can get through the CD installation portion fine, but booting up anytime after that will just give you the BSOD.

I personally use this type of install myself, that way I can use two OS's, without the pain in the *** of dual booting ( I always HATED missing the prompt at boot-up to choose which OS I wanted, and haveing to re-boot)  This way, I can plug in my USB drive, (or un-plug for windows) and press the power button, and go get a drink or chips or whatever without worrying about missing any prompts.
