Linux has a problem. (Well, it has lots, but the few I have are mostly my fault :) ). The problem is: Nobody likes it. It makes up %1~ percent of the desktop market, and only %75~ of the server market. I think we need to have a nice sit down talk (wat) about Linux's image, which is really it's main problem.


-Point 1: It costs... too little?
I actually think that linux not costing money is one of it's drawbacks. I think people belive that if it dosent cost money then it must be bad, as it was made by a couple enthusiasts in their closets. Instead of getting a corporate capitolist-competitive product (which is apparently better), you get some "communist free-for-all" going on.

Macs cost money, and they are apparently "linux". Technically no (as they do not use the linux kernel) but i know quite a few mac users who love to claim that their macs are oh so secure because their "linux". They aren't linux. Their UNIX-based, and that is a big difference, one few recognize nor give credit for.

People think "oh microsoft/apple must be so good cause it cost's $500 and it's shiny and i can email some guy in india for the first 180 days if i have a problem". But you say you use linux and people go "oh, you must be one of those eco-nuts who has a car running on vegetable oil". Funny thing, because they brag that their computers are "linux-based" (circular logic, anyone? O.o)


-Point 2: Coolness
You see the mac ads with the shiny mac and the jazzy indie music while the guy explains how it's the most eco-friendly laptop available, or the windows ads of the 8-year-old making a movie, and how easy that is to do. Linux has no ads, no banners, nothing cool to describe it; No offense to Tux, but a penguine isn't the best mascot.

The fact that linux is also widely used on servers is also a problem. People think server, they think giant data center with cables running everywhere, and either their computer can't support that or they woulden't know how to use it. True, linux can be hard, but on of the best tools of linux is that the fix is most times always easy.

Of course, if you get a nice shiny new mac, or brand-new pc, you like all the snazzy features that they include right. Hm... 4 desktops.... oh, window scroller. Most people don't even realize that linux can provide all these things, mostly because the word hasn't gotten out yet.


-Point 3: Secuirty
Sure, linux may be moar secure, but people think that because they are not paying over 9000$ for Synamtec to hog their net connection and resources overnight AND have the nerve to demand money as they do that that somthing must be wrong. It must be hard secure linux, it must prevent some connections, some programs must not work, etc.

Of course, people are also over-confident in the abilities of linux. I know i'll get some posts ITT that say "oh linux is unhackable". Truth be told, it isn't. I know, and i learned it the hard way: Somebody hacked my computer, then deleted /etc/passwd. That was a pain to fix, considering i coulden't login.

-Point 6: The Admins
Honestly, who of you remembers the original hackers (back in the day). Long, greasy hair, tye-dye, etc. Hell, the hacker ethic is a great example. For instance, [Richard Stallman]("http://www.sorbaioli.org/img/h/rms.jpg"). No offence man, but cut the hair.

Linux is also highly associated with the hacker underground - it's their ultimate brain child. But hackers are interpreted by a good portion of people to be evil (although they usually arent).

-Point 5: Creativity
Honestly, how creative are you on linux. The only good media-related program availble is GIMP. There is no video editing, very few image display programs can display animated GIF's, the Office Suite sucks (no offence openoffice, but i can't use the presentation software for love or money), you have to install multimedia codecs, etc.

When i took my PC out of the box, it had all the drivers factory installed, the bloatware ran, the mouse worked. I slave away at my graphics card to get it running and my mouse randomly cuts out every 6 hours. Networking will occasionally drop out and only come back on on a reboot. CIFS cant mount my boot-time-mounted network shares. On an easy-to-setup scale, linux is about a 5. 

However, we've gotten better. Moved from the days of parted and TASKREL to a nice graphical liveCD. And i think we can continue to get better.
