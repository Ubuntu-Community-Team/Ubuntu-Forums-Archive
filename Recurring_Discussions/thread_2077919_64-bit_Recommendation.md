### Post by Jakin on 2012-10-30
I think RAM allocation takes second place, the main thing is the speed- 32x2=64, twice the amount of operations percycle, so if you have an OS that can take advantage of 64bit, you have the potential of being twice as "fast" as the 32bit OS.



As for RAM, todays OS (no matter which) should have atleast 2GB, regardless 32bit or64bit OS. Of course though, if you want more than 4GB of RAM, 64bit is your best option.
