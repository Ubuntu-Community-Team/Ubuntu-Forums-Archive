### Post by Ecclesia on 2008-03-19
I think that people get confused between what Microsoft has done for hardware/software on the windows platform and what other companies have done for this.  Yes, more hardware works on XP than on Linux platforms, but this is facilitated by the companies making the hardware, not by Microsoft.

If companies started making their devices with Linux in mind and stopped producing for the Microsoft platforms suddenly everyone would criticize the poor hardware support for Windows.  None of this has anything to do with Microsoft as a company or their level of innovation.  It does have something to do with their marketing and business strategy - which, IMHO is shameful.

Meanwhile the hardware support that you see in Linux is hugely dependent on the Linux community and their willingness to provide free support and drivers.  Looking at this from a sociological perspective this is quite amazing and it shows how great the core of Linux users are.

The problem is that open source projects currently lack the business muscle to force many businesses to come on board.  Perhaps if we all refuse to buy closed source products (or at least products that don't place nicely with open source) we'd see things change.

Also the benefits of Linux are only really pronounced if it's running on the right hardware.  Yes, stability is beautiful on some machines... on others Linux is not stable, but these are related to hardware issues and problems that the Linux community has of always playing catch up to the closed source business machine.  If you buy your hardware carefully Linux can't be beat.  But there are lot of people who buy a brand new laptop without doing any research decide that Linux is junk when they can't get their wireless and webcam working.
