### Post by codeseer on 2009-04-22
There are some keyloggers, rootkits and viruses for Linux; although, there are very few. The design of Ubuntu basically makes it where you have to purposely want such a thing on your system in order for it work. From my readings and personal knowledge, no viruses have ever been found 'in the wild' on an Ubuntu machine, only in lab settings where the infection was on purpose.

If you stick mainly to the official repositories and don't install anything as sudo/root that you can't trust, then you have little to worry about. As always, back up your data, not only for the rare event that something of this nature could damage your data under your /home directory, but to have backups in case of other potential data loss.

In summary, you are seeking to write viruses for Linux, then you're wasting your time and it would be in vain. Also, there are no backdoors in Linux, because it is open source and reviewed by many.

The general meaning of hacking in unauthorized access to a computer system. A hacker is someone who gains unauthorized access to a computer system.

On the Internet there are a few other slang meanings though. A hacker can also be a good programmer (Linus Torvalds is a good example). 

A hack, in the context of games, means that someone has added unfair modifications to the game.

A hack, in programming, sometimes refers to the use of something in a non-standard way to achieve a goal.

Malware is a general term meaning malicious software. The term malware refers to any software that is malicious. Most non-computing use the word virus for this, malware is actually the correct word.

A virus is a specific type of malware. It is malicious software that replicates and then piggy backs on files you move between computers to spread.

A worm is a type of malware that replicates and spreads by itself.

A trojan is a type of non-replicating malware that is installed when you install another program.

Telnet is a defunct (still used though) Internet protocol for communication. It has been replaced by more secure protocols.

Ping is the milliseconds latency with the server. If you a ping reading of 100 it means that it takes 1/10th of a second for the server to get your data and then reply to you.
