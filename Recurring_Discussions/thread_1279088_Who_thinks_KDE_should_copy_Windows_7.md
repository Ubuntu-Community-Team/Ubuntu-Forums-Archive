The thing is, it's not always the same people responsible for both.  I've written two plasmoids now, but I'm in no position to address the stability of KDE, my c++ is pathetic.  The idea that it's a choice of either features or stability is a false dichotomy.

Once A is implemented in KDE, then other devs write new features around this new capability.

That's just an example which I've observed, it's not always the case.  But the point is you can't just say "stability not features" as if the two are not intertwined in any way.
