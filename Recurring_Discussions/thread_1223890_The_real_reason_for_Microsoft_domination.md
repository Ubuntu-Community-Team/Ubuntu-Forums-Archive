### Post by tsali on 2009-07-29
> **Dullstar said:**
> Then why don't they just allow him to use it, but refuse to give him technical support?

Most employees computers are owned by the company. It is their machine and their network. Allowing individual users to install non-standard software without evaluation jeopardizes the efficiency and integrity of an enterprise system.

While I am quite capable of installing all kinds of software on my corp laptop, I don't do it. They have provided me with the software they expect me to use...so I do.

We had an infamous case of an individual who installed a music library and streaming server on one of our network file servers. Didn't take long before legitimate business was being slowed by music traffic.

Unauthorized, uncontrolled software is bad for enterprise. Your company should have a process to "authorize" non-standard apps if you make a case for them.
