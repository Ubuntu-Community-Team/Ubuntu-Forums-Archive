. Simple/Elegant/Sexy UI (User Interface).

Why?

. Small fonts, small tabs, small everything. I don't have to full screen it. 

. Nickname + User message + Avatar. (Yours and your contacts) Nothing more. Nothing less. 

. No ugly search bars, menus, buttons, icons, text boxes/labels etc. taking up screen space and making the application look "cluttered". 

. The chat window has tabs, keeps track of your last conversation and differentiates between your messages and your friend's messages. 

. No emoticons, no avatars, no custom fonts. Just simple, small text and some nice curved borders with pleasant colors. 

That said,

I **love** Adium and it's not because of the code that's behind it ([libpurple]("http://developer.pidgin.im/wiki/WhatIsLibpurple")). It's because they actually put in quite an effort into making (what I believe to be) a SIMPLE, ELEGANT and SEXY User Interface. 

I don't really care about the screen space I save, but I enjoy the UI because its refreshing and relaxing to my eyes. I've used numerous IM clients, such as: Pidgin, Kopete, Empathy, Emesene, aMSN, MSN Messenger, Windows Live Messenger and Miranda IM. So far I've found none of them really "aesthetically pleasing", they look cluttered with thousands of menus, big ugly icons, huge avatars, bad/ugly fonts and a lot of themes look like experiments made by 13 year-olds playing with CSS for their *awesome* myspace profile. 

**Now, is it too much to ask for an IM Client for Linux that has a simple/elegant/sexy UI?**

I realize many might be tempted to reply "Well, *aesthetically pleasing* is subjective term." I would agree, but since this is my thread... I think I should be able to define the term and receive suggestions based on my definition.

Some might believe I'm insulting Linux Distributions or people that create software for Linux Distributions. I'm **not**. I love Linux! My distro of choice is Arch Linux and I find it simple, fast and elegant. I love OpenBox WM, KDE and even Gnome to some extent. I enjoy using bash and I'm learning how to use emacs. I don't mind editing text files to configure my apps or startup daemons. I always update my system with 'sudo pacman -Syu', elegant command vs ugly GUI alternatives. Furthermore, I think AUR and yaourt are brilliant.

I should mention... I'm not interesting in applying a a cocoa/OSX theme. I think GTK/Qt/whatever apps should be able to look sexy without copying from another OS. I also (blindly) believe some Linux developers must care about UI design as much as what the code does "behind the scenes". Isn't a sexy + smart girl/boy better than a *meh* + smart girl/boy?

I'm sitting here with my Hackintosh, wanting to **install Arch** once more. I need a goal, a little push to send me over the edge. **I want to replicate some of the features I fell in love with in OSX.**

. **Spotlight**, I find it easy and useful to tap Ctrl+Space and have a text field popup I can use to: launch apps, find documents/folders, email messages, web pages from my browser's history, do some quick calculations and lookup words in the dictionary. I wish I could: Index SAMBA network drives. By the way, yes, if I need to do some "hardcore" searching I use *find* in the terminal. 

. **Apps in OSX are hidden** when you click Ctrl+H, they are not closed unless you specifically kill/quit them. This is great, better that prefetching/caching, they open up instantly! When I want to take a break from programing (for example) I can just hide eclipse, the pdf viewer and the browser I'm using and use the desktop normally, as in, not cluttered with tons of open apps when I want to navigate opened windows with 'exposé' (exposé in KDE > that OSX's). When I want to get back to what I was doing I just hide the current windows and open the old ones. Relatively modern computers seem to have no trouble with this approach, I never max out my ram usage and I just max out my cpu usage when I'm doing something really intensive (rendering a video file for example).  

**Please recommend/share a WM/DM, Apps, scripts, themes or whatever you think I'll like.**

**Share your opinions too**, even if you don't recommend anything or just think I should stay with OSX.

Sorry for the long post. ^^U

### Post by matthewbpt on 2010-01-30
For Spotlight I'd recommend Gnome Do (I you use gnome of course, tho I think it may work on other DE's too I just haven't tried) Gnome Do does exactly what you want Spotlight to do, and is invoked with Ctrl+Space or however else you may want to invoke it. You can use it to open apps, install new ones, search your computer, choose a new song for your music player to play, add an appointment to your calendar, etc etc.

When I want to take a break from a programme or some work etc, I put it in the last workspace of my Desktop, I have four but I never need to use all. So I move the program to that workspace so it doesn't clutter the desktop of the rest, and then bring it back when I need it again, pretty simple, and I can do it in a second without even touching the mouse. This system works pretty well for me you should try it.

-Matt
