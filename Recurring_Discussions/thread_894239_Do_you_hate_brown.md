### Post by SeanHodges on 2008-09-02
crhylove: Have you considered rolling your own Ubuntu CD with artwork that you think everyone would like, and making that available on a website?

Theres a lot involved to designing artwork that works for everyone. I love the current one, and actually have had no problem with it when introducing non-technical people to Ubuntu. Quite often they actually remark that they like the colours and prefer the clean uncluttered style of the desktop.

When designing artwork for the base Ubuntu distribution, you have to consider not only what "looks nice", but also what will STILL look nice on a low-res, low colour depth CRT monitor, on a Pentium 2 laptop with 256MB RAM. Far too often I see people suggest delicate colours and themes that look great on their 24" TFT monitors running Compiz and Gdesklets, who seem to be oblivious to the fact that this simply will not work on low-spec machines.

So when you pick the colour scheme for "Crhylove's Blue Ubuntu", make sure that you consider 256 colour monitors when working on things like contrast and hue.
