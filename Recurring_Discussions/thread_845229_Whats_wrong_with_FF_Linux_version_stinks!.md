### Post by ayenack on 2008-06-30
Some users have been experiencing problems with Fire Fox 3 if you're not happy with it un-install it and try Fire Fox 2. Make a backup of your bookmarks like this.

**cp ~/.mozilla/firefox/profile/bookmarks.html ~/Desktop** (This will copy the file to your desktop. cp stands for copy.)

Then remove FF3 like this.

**sudo apt-get remove --purge firefox** (This will remove FF3 and purge the config files.)

Then install FF2 like this.

**sudo apt-get install firefox-2** (This will install FF2.)

Then copy your bookmarks over to the new FF2 install.

**cp ~/Desktop/bookmarks.html ~/.mozilla/firefox/profile/** (This will copy you bookmarks into FF2.)

And you should be happy with the results.

### Post by Greyed on 2008-06-30

This is different from any other piece of software in Ubuntu's repositories how, exactly?

In case you're wondering why this is the case and not just ranting to get attention the reason is simple.  Ubuntu (and Debian, it's base, and Red Hat and all its derivatives, and so on) manage installed software with their package managers.  MozDevs, as far as I know, do not package Firefox for Ubuntu (etc, etc).  Hitting update inside Firefox would not be useful since where it would be placed (if anywhere) might not be where the distribution you've chosen to run has placed it.  So you'd update, restart Firefox and, presto changeo, are still on the old version because your links point to where your distro installed it not where MozDevs put it.

There's nothing preventing you from downloading and installing manually.  However given that the reaction time of Ubuntu's packagers to critical updates is often on the matter of hours chances are by the time you're even aware of such a "critical" update it's already available.  That is, of course, unless you confine yourself exclusively to browsing Mozilla's site in which case you should have nothing to fear as they would not actually act upon those exploits to take over your computer.
