I get the feeling you too might be under the impression that LTS means something more than security updates for many years. That's all it means. **L**ong **T**erm **S**upport releases are not less buggy or better tested than any other Ubuntu releases. They are just points of safety for those who'd prefer not to upgrade every six months. This is what stability means in LTS. It means keeping the base free of changes for a long period of time.

Ubuntu's goal of keeping fresh with the latest and greatest unfortunately means they will never run out of bugs to fix. They can't sit still and fix all the bugs because then they'd essentially be Debian.
