### Post by NightwishFan on 2010-12-22
My opinions are...

ReactOS: Very interesting, and a good idea. It means to make a completely open source system compatible with NT. though I am not sure when it will be as usable as say Gnome/KDE.

Haiku: Awesome. Feels great, looks great, runs great. What to do on it? Not much that I can think of other than play music. I plan on keeping my eye on this, and if it takes off I might main this on all of my portables.

Debian GNU/KfreeBSD: Only BSD I remember trying. It uses the Debian and GNU userspace on a FreeBSD kernel. It is very light and has a lot ported to it from the Debian repositories. Might be great if you have a heavy Linux background.

The Haiku file-system is brilliant; it has the built in capacity of being a very fast database, which is highly suitable for people with large music & video collections (be they residing on or off of their HDD).
