### Post by stwschool on 2009-08-01
Might I suggest that if your current wi-fi isn't working you might want to try Linux Mint? It's Ubuntu-based but comes with a whole bunch of stuff to make your wireless work better.

Regarding Ubuntu breaking your XP network connection, that's unlikely. For that to be the case Ubuntu would have had to modify files on your XP partition relating to network configuration. This is unlikely. That said, you've used NDIS which means you were using XP drivers, so maybe in retrieving drivers from your XP partition you inadvertantly broke XP?

Dual-Monitors: I've not run a 2-monitor setup myself so can't speak from experience but I reckon your most sensible route is to begin with a simple set-up (1 monitor) on the nicer GFX card and see what happens. What graphics cards are you running anyway? If ATI or NVIDIA and reasonably new you should be able to use the proprietary drivers which will come with a control centre to address matters like dual-monitors.
