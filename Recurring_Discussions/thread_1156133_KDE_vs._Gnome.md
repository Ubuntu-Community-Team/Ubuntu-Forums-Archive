### Post by gjoellee on 2009-05-11
KDE vs. GNOME.... it is all about what YOU think is best. Do software or OS is best. Something might be best for YOU, but maybe not for others...

My opinion to this is that KDE wins...

**Performance:**
If you have a modern computer you wont really feel any difference between GNOME and KDE in performance. It is just that KDE has more eye candy and requires a slightly modern graphics card to run without any graphic lags. KDE runs in about 260MB of RAM.

GNOME is simple and clean and because of that it is faster on slightly older computer hardware, but on modern harware there is not really any difference between GNOME and KDE. GNOME runs in about 230MB RAM.

GNOME wins because of a slightly lower RAM usage.

**Eye Candy:**
When it comes to eye candy, nothing can beat KDE. Because dekstop effects and such is included in KWin (KDE's Window Manager), you don't need Compiz to get any effects. This lets you keep your high frame rates while playing games, and there are less bugs. KDE's design is also delightful, although it might remind you a little of Vista.

GNOME does not really have any eye candy included, and you have to get eye candy by using a compositor like Compiz. Using Compiz may decrease stability and frame rates while playing games.

KDE wins.

**Usabilty:**
The first versions of KDE 4 are bad, but after KDE 4.2 it is finally usable. If you decide to use 5 minutes changing some preferences in different applications you will pretty much have exactly what you want. KDE does however have a lot of options, but thay are not so visible so thay are not confusing. KDE does also have a control panel which is veyr good to have.

GNOME is a very clean and simple look which makes it easy to use. GNOME does have a Control Panel inclused but it is not good, and when you click on an application you want to configure from the Control Panel you will just end up with a window of the current program. If you right click or check in your menu bar you will see a lot of different options, so many options that they are quite confusing. Hopefully this will get cleaned up with GNOME 3!

KDE wins because it is cleaner, and has moved most options to a options window which is clean and eays to use. KDE also has a very good Control Panel.[B]

Result:[/B]
GNOME: 1 points
KDE: 2 points
