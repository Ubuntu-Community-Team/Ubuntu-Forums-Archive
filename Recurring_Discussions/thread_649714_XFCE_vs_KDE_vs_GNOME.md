### Post by maniacmusician on 2008-01-03
I use both Gnome and KDE; KDE on my desktop, Gnome on my laptop.

The main differences between KDE and Gnome are the toolkits they use to render the desktop elements (Gnome uses GTK, KDE uses Qt), and the basic user experience. The toolkits, of course, affect how your desktop environment will look...both GTK and Qt are quite customizable, but some people prefer the look of one to the other. 

As a rule of thumb (with some exceptions), KDE is more easily customizable than Gnome is. It's for people that like to tweak the look and behavior of their system down to the last detail. That's pretty much why I prefer it; I have a lot of fun tailoring my system to my own needs, and I find that a lot of Qt based apps are usually more versatile for me as well. 

XFCE is really lightweight; this makes it better for older systems (though not too old), although a lot of people prefer to use it on modern systems as well, because of its speed.

It's just a matter of personal choice. You don't **have** to change your environment; you can stick to just one, or use 2 or all three of them. I use Gnome on my laptop because the Ubuntu team does a lot of work making life easier for laptop users, and a lot of the time, those improvements don't carry over to Kubuntu. I use KDE on my desktop because I find it to be way more configurable and fun to use, and I know how to use it. It also offers me usability perks that are important to me, like setting keyboard shortcuts to launch apps. I never figured out how to do that in Gnome, but it allows me to work a little faster and have more tools immediately at my fingertips. 

I would give each one a try, and then end up using whatever you want. I started out with XFCE, but tired of it. I moved on to Gnome, then KDE, which is my preferred system now. I'm particularly excited about KDE4. I think it will be pretty good once 4.1 and 4.2 are released. But to each his own. Use what makes you happy.

### Post by julian67 on 2008-03-11
yes, no problem. Install the package xubuntu-desktop; this is a meta-package which means it describes all the packages necessary for Xubuntu default desktop. You just select xubuntu-desktop and everything is installed and set up for you. You can then choose your Gnome/Xfce session at log-in. Your Gnome desktop will be unaffected. Underlying system performance won't be much different  but your Xfce desktop apps (like the file manager Thunar) will be fast to open compared to the gnome apps.  I think doing it this way might leave metacity as the default window manager though I'm not sure, you'll need to check for yourself or maybe someone else knows for sure and can post here. If so then change your Xfce session so it uses xfwm4 as window manager, then you will have a snappier experience.
