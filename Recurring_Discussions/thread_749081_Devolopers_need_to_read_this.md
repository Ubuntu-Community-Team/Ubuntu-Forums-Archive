### Post by roderick on 2008-04-08
Of course you know most of the drivers are developed by the general Linux community and not Ubuntu by itself...

The community is comprised, not only of the various distro providers (Red Hat, SuSE, Gentoo, etc) but by a large community of unpaid developers...

This is not a Ubuntu specific problem you are asking to solve. 

Typically, the distro providers work on customizing what they package, to make it their own, and usually that means eye-candy, usability, etc. 

Sometimes they have the ability to influence what gets worked on first, if it fits into their needs and user wishlists (i.e. post a request in Launchpad and get people to vote/support it). It's a simple matter of demand. If only one person has card X, and no one sees a need for it, it won't get any attention, except by someone who has a genuine interest in making it work.

For example, I had a X-Box 360 dance mat which wasn't supported in the xpad.c driver. I posted up and no one else had it working. I took it upon myself to write a patch. That's what it takes.

So, if you want something worked on, be specific, and open up bug reports on what specifically isn't working or supported. Someone will pick it up if there's enough interest to warrant it.

### Post by p_quarles on 2008-04-08
@buried: To reiterate, no one in this thread has flamed or insulted you in the least. What is evident is that you do not have a complete grasp on the development process. Open source development is simply not a game of "either we fix this, *or* we fix that." In fact, one of the most frequent complaints against GNU/Linux development is that it has too many developers contributing to too many redundant projects.

The vast majority of hardware problems that one is likely to run into at this point have nothing to do with a lack of interest on the part of developers. These problems have everything to do with the fact that the obstacles in the way of better hardware compatibility are ones which require a tremendous amount of specialized expertise to even begin addressing. The chances that a Compiz developer could switch tracks and figure out how to make another brand of laptop suspend properly is next to nil.
