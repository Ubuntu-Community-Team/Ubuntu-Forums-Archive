### Post by Bill Tetzeli on 2013-05-29
Okay, first - before anyone accuses me of being a troll or trying to start a flame war, they should know I'm posting under my real name.  Not the kind of thing a troll will do.  I'm just posting this because lately the Redmond demon has been whispering in my ear, "Come back, Bill, come back..."  But one thing has intrigued me of late.  Defenders of Linux, Ubuntu in particular (myself included) often say the following:

"Windows 8 sucks!  Metro is an abomination, a huge step back in usability AND you need third-party applications such as Start8 or Classic Shell just to bring back the Start Menu.  What junk!"

"Ubuntu's excellent! Sure, the Unity interface is offputting to a lot of people BUT you can install third-party desktop environments like xubuntu or lxde that give you a more traditional look.  What freedom!"


So yeah, I did a double-take at that.  And now I'm faced with an internal battle.  The honeyed M$ argument, like an abusive spouse turning on the charm, goes like this:

"See, you can get your start menu and desktop back, just a few clicks.  You can start using Samplitude, Ozone and all that other Windows software you've missed.  Sure, some of them may not install properly after you 'upgrade' to Win 8, but most of that will work eventually, or you can shell out some bucks for new versions.  I've got them all in my pocket, they'll have to come to me eventually.  And no command line!  You want multimedia?  Hardware support?  Remote desktop?  All the best programs?  Point, click, point, click, point, click.  And no glitches - there never have been glitches in Windows, never will be, your memories of them are false.  Forget, forget - point, click, point, click..."


Meanwhile, the Ubuntu angel on my right shoulder says:

**"Are you crazy?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?!?**

"Windows and various programs tripping over each other with their updates, breaking each other, and it's not like you didn't have a program or piece of hardware working one day and buggered the next by something Windows did.  You've torn out more hair than Einstein had over the years trying to fix problems in Windows.  You've tossed computers into *dumpsters* because of Windows.  And while Ubuntu has made huge strides in making Linux user-friendly, M$ is making huge leaps away from it.  Their motives for ripping the Start menu out are nakedly mercenary (app store), proving your interests mean nothing to them.  You are their food, period.  Win 8 is their Bank of America $5 ATM surcharge - proof that you can't trust them, ever, with anything.

"Ubuntu is not Windows, and never will be.  First, Microsoft is a corporation, and while Canonical is one too, Linux is a *community*.  If you have a problem, you don't have to wait on the good offices of Microsoft to fix it.  There are tons of people willing and able to help.  If Canonical ever does a Win 8 on us, there are plenty of Ubuntu derivatives to keep a GUI guy like yourself happy.  But they won't, because Canonical needs their free OS to be popular for their commercial services and products to make money.  They can't afford MS's 'Take it or leave it' attitude.

"Speaking of which, in the next few years Ubuntu's likely to be on enough computers to force the commercial developers to accommodate it.  Then you'll get your gee-whiz killer apps that you miss on Windoze.  In the meantime, did you ever do anything in Samplitude that you can't with Audacity?  Do you even know what most of those knobs and levers were for?  What's Ozone got over sox?

"You've got everything you need right here and now.  You're learning Ubuntu much faster than you learned Windows.  Have p a t i e n c e and you'll learn the rest as you go, as you need it.  The fact is, with so much choice and ease of movement between distros in Linux, the odds of your digital life being upended the way MS did it to you are next to nil.  Have a cuppa and chill. :-)"


My gut's telling me the second voice is the one to listen to.  I'm just too sleep-deprived right now to figure out the intellectual argument to back the feeling.


Sorry for the diary entry.  Don't feel you have to respond unless you want to.  Thanks for letting me vent. :-)

Thanks, but I'm not going anywhere. :-)  To be honest, if I thought Win 8 was just another Vista or Millennium (brrrrr) wave I could ride out until MS righted itself, I'd hold onto my Windows 7 partition and cross my fingers that I would land somewhere safe by the time Win 7 Home support runs out in 2015.  But M$ is so recalcitrant, so unrepentant and hell-bent on continuing their mistakes, desperately manipulating sales figures to make Win 8-Track look more popular than it is, that I just plain don't trust them any more.  It's one New Coke too many.  And while Mac definitely has its appeal, you have to pay at least $1200 for anything with an optical drive.  I don't like a hardware monopoly any more than an OS one.  Plus, I'd endure endless heckling from both my brothers if I caved and bought a Mac. ;-)  Also, now that Jobs is dead it wouldn't surprise me if those now in charge start changing up Apple in less than positive ways.

Which leaves Linux.  It really does do everything I need, if not always what I want.  I think it's got the best future of any OS out there because it's the most adaptable.  I admire Shuttleworth for his efforts not just to make Linux accessible to everday users like me, but also to prove that there is real $$$$ to be made from desktop Linux.  What stresses me out the most - connecting from one computer to another, professional-level audio editing software - isn't going to matter to most people.  They'll just see an OS that you can install and surf the net with in less than 20 minutes, secured with the latest updates and antivirus in less than an hour.  They'll see everything working out of the box, mouse, webcam, touchpad, printer, networking - except they won't see it, because it will all be taken care of quietly without them even knowing it, which is what they expect.  As long as Shuttleworth stays on his current trajectory, I DO see a day when it will become profitable, even necessary, to advertise your software as "available for Windows, Mac and Ubuntu".

I guess I'm just a bit of a worrywart because Windows 8 has left me with OS paranoia.  I feel safest with Linux because there's always another distro to go to, and always a way to cure what ails your box.  I deliberately took months between installing Ubuntu Studio and making a permanent switch last year, and only this spring felt confident enough to dump Windows entirely.  I should adopt the same patience now with the very minor things I need to figure out now.  I didn't rush then, I won't now.

So yeah, I'm definitely sticking around. :D
