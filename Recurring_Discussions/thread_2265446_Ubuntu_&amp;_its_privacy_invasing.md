### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-02-19
Frankly, the entire notion of "privacy" when you use a machine that's on the internet is more than a bit naive. It's a network and it's public. 

We users need to assume more responsbility for managing and protecting our own "privacy", however each of us might define it. Taking the initiative to acquire at least minimal knowledge of how the net works can prepare you to make better decisions about the personal data you choose to expose or not expose to the net. This includes taking action to adjust default settings and to consciously avoid some activities. 

Currently, though, most people seem to lack this minimal understanding, and simply choose to put their uninformed trust in the people who make their browser, their distribution or their other software. That kind of blind trust is often regretted.
