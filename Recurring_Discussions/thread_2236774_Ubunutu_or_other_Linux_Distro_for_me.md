### Post by kira-yamato-2008 on 2014-07-28
Even with top of the line hardware you're gonna notice issues with the most windows only games such as Skyrim and Fallout 1-New Vegas in Wine even with Play On Linux. Especially in Ubuntu with it's fancy resource hungry Unity Desktop Environment. I suggest keeping Windows (7 ideally) for gaming, and either installing Lubuntu 14.04 LTS, or chucking Unity and replacing it with the LXDE. You could use Wine with, or without Play On Linux, for windows games so long as you make sure to check the [WineHQ AppDB]("http://appdb.winehq.org/"). If a game is listed as anything below gold it won't be worth your time to try and play it in Wine, and even if it's gold or platinum you'll still see much lower performance than you would in windows. That's not just with Ubuntu and it's flavors either, it's pretty universal to all Linux distros.

Hope that helps.
