No matter what you do, the more invested you get in a certain OS/network, the more painful and difficult it will be to move away from it. It's not much to do with any inadequacies with Linux.

The main issue I see is that we have more complainers than coders these days (not to say you're complaining, it sounds more like you're just mentioning why you can't MOVE).

Thing is, even though you can use coLinux on Windows or Fink in OS X, if you're getting ultra involved in any computer software, you should think of getting multiple OSes runnable so you don't lose any benefits of any of them. So really, use what works for you. You don't have to MOVE to anything. You can keep Ubuntu on the side, it's the default install option. Of course, if all you ever do is chess with these niche programs when you're on a computer, I guess it doesn't matter so much.
