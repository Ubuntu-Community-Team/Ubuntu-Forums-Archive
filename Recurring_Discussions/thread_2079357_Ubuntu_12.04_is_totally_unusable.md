### Post by RedRat on 2012-11-02
Much depends on exactly how you use your computer and your past experiences with those machines and their various GUIs. Some are more technically inclined and like to fiddle with the GUI and some do not. I think those coming from the Mac experience find the Unity/Dash approach comfortable. Those of us who have come from Ubuntu and Windows find it less so. 

Personally, I like to fiddle with my desktop experience. I don't care for most of the pre-packaged themes, most do not work for me. That is why I liked the older Gnome experience where I could change icon fonts and their colors. It is pretty clear from Unity and even the Gnome approaches, that most like dark backgrounds (it is very hard to figure out how to change the color of the icon fonts from white to black--impossible maybe?). I prefer light backgrounds--that's me. I am clearly in the minority. 

As to Unity, my opinion is that it is OK at best and "Meh?" at worst. I prefer the Gnome tool bars and putting icons on my desktop--that's they way I work. I really don't like developers making these decisions for me, how I should use the desktop--that is up to me to decide. My ability to decide is empowering and that is what I want my OS to do.
