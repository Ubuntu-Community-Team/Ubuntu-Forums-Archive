### Post by daengbo on 2008-04-23
Evolution is the default mail client in Ubuntu because it's the default in Gnome. It is a Gnome application which continues to be more tightly integrated into the Gnome desktop.

For example, the clock on your desktop shows your Evolution tasks and appointments for the day. Nautilus uses Evolution for "send to ..." as well.

[quote=Eisenwinter]Why do we even need a mail client? best option (imo) is to have none installed by default.[/quote]

Best option ***_for you_***. Perhaps not for somebody else.

I use Thunderbird with the Lightning extension to keep e-mails, addresses (including snail mail), phone numbers, appointments, Web mail can't necessarily do that, and if some idiot down the street messes up the cable TV (which is where I get my broadband), then I lose access to that information. From a privacy point of view, it's also better to keep your e-mail and address book data on your computer rather than on somebody else's server.

Don't like Evolution. Doesn't look right, doesn't feel right. The address book interface especially needs work. Thunderbird's address book (indeed, the whole application) looks more usable and polished.

Unfortunately, Synaptic has a cow every time I try to remove Evolution. :mad:
