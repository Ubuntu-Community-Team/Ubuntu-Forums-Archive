### Post by makitso on 2010-01-01
Greetings, 

I have been using Ubuntu for two years -- and I really like it.  However, there are some things that just burn my socks and its time I unload :-)  I know that everyone has their grips and Canonical has the 100 paper cuts.  But there are some problems are so basic that I just can't understand why they have not been addressed by now.

1.  GNONE: 
A.) THE CLIPBOARD!!  I am so sick and tired of having the cut/paste fail.  I finally installed Parcellite but why should I need to do this?  New users to Ubuntu expect this to work, just like a phone.  Lets get into the 20th century.
B.) So you want to change an icon on the desktop.  When you right click on the desktop icon and then click again on the icon to change it, your put into your home directory!  Why not put you in the directory where the original graphic exists?
C.) Apps you install that don't appear in the Applications list

2.  Nautilus:  
A.) Why can't you create a folder in List View?  Pain to have to switch Icon view to do this.  
B.) Why can't I cut and paste a file while in List view?  One needs to go up a level and then you can paste into the folder.  

I could go on an on.  But these are my hot ones.

Now I feel better! :P
