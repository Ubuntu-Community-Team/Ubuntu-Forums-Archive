I found this post on another forum (original post [here]("http://www.thezeitgeistmovement.com/joomla/index.php?option=com_kunena&Itemid=99999&func=view&catid=5&id=247243")), thought I would share it here since I basically agree with everything being said :)

> Apple prides itself on its perceived innovative ingenuity, but its  recent iPad product has called Apple's motives into  question once more...

Just to be absolutely clear: The iPad presents **no new  technology** and it is an insult to refer to it as  innovative/creative/revolutionary... all it really comes down to is a  bunch of very well-thought-out marketing schemes/ploys. Apple has once  again convinced the consumer population they they all NEED this new  piece of garbage!

I've always despised Apple for I feel they are a prime example of what  the Monetary System nurtures -- not innovation, but a self-serving  profit-driven manipulative marketing scheme that only succeeds through  planned obsolescence and morally questionable advertising techniques.

I have long accepted the depressing fact that Apple will continue to  perpetuate their fake ideology of *innovation* through releasing  products that are, in the eyes of consumers, new and technically  superior to competitors' current products and Apple's very own  "obsolete" products... Apple could offer any piece of rubbish off the  street, and their brain-washed fanboys would be queueing outside the  Apple stores within minutes.

This isn't just an issue with the iPad... Apple screws the consumers on  every front! The fact is, if I buy an "Apple MacBook" today, it will  eventually become obsolete in my eyes, through no technical failing of  the technology at hand but simply because Apple's marketing techniques  imply, using the same techniques that first drew me in, that the new  product will enhance my productivity and therefore my life, more so than  the originally sufficient "MacBook". This is the same deal with all of  their products, and pretty much all products in society today! And this  SUCKS!

The idea of technology is to make our lives easier, not to make us  constantly feel incomplete. Apple doesn't perpetuate or affect  innovation to the extent that they claim. They only release their  "innovative" products when it best suits their profit scheme (and their  shareholders' pockets), not for the cause of innovation nor for the  benefit of humanity!

I know -- it's silly of me to specifically target Apple with this little  rant, but they truly are a prime example of what I HATE about modern  consumerism. They do it better than Microsoft, HP, Dell, Sony ... Apple  are so good at marketing that they'll screw you, again and again and  again, and you'll still walk away with a smile on your face, thinking  that you got a good deal...

### Post by Macchi on 2010-04-14
Does any of the people writing their critics around here have any idea on how hard it is to develop and manufacture computers, software, and embedded systems in industrial scale? 

Let's speak seriously: I agree with some of the criticism because NEN technology, I mean really new, is probably only seen by a few eyes in the research labs and applied research enterprises. Eventually this technology will be improved and cost-optimized for many many years before it can be used on consumer products.  
 
Nevertheless, we now that marketing departments (and even software developers) love the adjective "new". And since we are all in the technology business it becomes simply "new technology"!

Actually what the masses are buying is technologically speaking not new. It is STATE OF THE ART, that is, the best product that the market can offer at a reasonably low price tag. (You know, this is not a charity business. Prices are as high as possible to make the product profitable but as low as possible to compete and win in the market!).   

But please think twice. The technology seen in the latest consumer products IS ACTUALLY NEW FOR THE MASSES, given the capabilities of the product and the price tag! Thus in a way, new technology in the shop. (not in the lab!)

Whether If you like it or not, developing, manufacturing, selling, (and delivering!) a sleek and nicely integrated surf computer with color touchscreens weighting only 1.5 pounds at 500 dollars is as new technology as it gets today. From the point of view of consumer masses!

But of course, many people may still have ideological, emotional, or practical reasons for disagreeing with Apple's success story.
I thing that we have to observe with care their marketing success and try to apply the best of it for Linux and Ubuntu!
