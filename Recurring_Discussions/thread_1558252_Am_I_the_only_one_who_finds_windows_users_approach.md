### Post by jroa on 2010-08-22
I guess that antivirus is sort of like health insurance.  You hope that you will never need to use it, but when something does happen, it is nice to know it is there.

While I do think that a lot of people are clueless about how malware gets on the computer, I would not say that everyone is just "click happy".  Yes, a lot of people get bad stuff from going to untrustworthy sites or downloading anything that sounds good at the time, but the stuff can get on your computer from common every day tasks as well.  DNS hijacking can lead to malware and so can the corruption of data from a hacker on a valid server.  You can get a virus from just opening an email attachment from one of your friends or letting someone plug a thumb drive into your computer.  So, no matter how careful you are, you can get attacked.

Of course, your chances of being infected are much lower with Linux OSs than they are with Windows, but we are not completely immune.

Now, on the other hand, when I am asked to fix a computer that has no antivirus software and the person has been downloading everything they could think of from a P2P service, then I do not feel bad for them when I tell them that I will have to wipe out their computer.  But, when the person has been doing their part to take precautions and something still manages to slip through, I will do my best to save their data.
