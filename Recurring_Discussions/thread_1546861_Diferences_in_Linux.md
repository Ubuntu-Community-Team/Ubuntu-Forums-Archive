### Post by YeOK on 2010-08-06
Ubuntu is targeted at Windows users and beginners to Linux. Fedora is the Red Hat supported community distro targeted at developers and tends to be used by more experienced users.

Fedora won't ship patented codecs / drivers, Ubuntu will offer to enable them. Fedora tends to have more bleeding edge tools, though its server offerings are pretty solid. Ubuntu tends to be a little behind on the tools and some other things, like xorg, but its not vastly different.
