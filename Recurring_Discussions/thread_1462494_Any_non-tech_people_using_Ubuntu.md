### Post by cloyd on 2010-04-25
I'm in a non-tech profession. I'm United Methodist clergy.  I got tired of a super-slow netbook. My step-son (who is an engineer) suggested I try Linux. I had to search the websites, read about the different distros, and make my choice. Tried Moolux first. It worked, but didn't work right out of the box with my wireless. Found Ubuntu, and it sounded like there was lots of support. Tried a live flashdrive. My wireless worked right out of the box. I worked with it on the flashdirve for a few weeks, and then partitioned the hard drive for a dual boot. 

It is dependable, and gives me something new to learn, puzzle over, and play with. Love it.

It did help that I had been using open office for about 4 months (again, at the suggestion of my step-son). I already had a little confidence in the idea of "open source" and free software. It wasn't something shady used by computer criminals, and it was dependable and safe.
