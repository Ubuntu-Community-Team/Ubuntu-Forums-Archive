### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-02-24
I have tried Ubuntu on test boxes over the years, and always liked where it was going.

The weird thing is that while I have Windows at work and am on the PC all day long - I'm not really using Windows much at all.

I spend the day using Telent to Linux boxes and X-term to Solaris boxes.  Windows just holds the executables and runs the corporate required OUTLOOK.

I spend 90% of my time at home in Ubuntu.  Surfing the web and listening to music is the same.  I love Compiz effects.

It's not because I am cheap - I built a killer box for Win7, but only really run it in Vbox now.  I own 5 Win7 licenses, 3 XP licenses and 2 MS Office licenses. And yet, I keep finding myself not using them.

I think that the real measure of it is this:  I am not running Ubuntu, I am playing with the computer and it is playing back.  It's no fun play catch alone, you know?
