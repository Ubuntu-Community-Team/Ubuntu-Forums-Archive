### Post by aysiu on 2009-08-13
Getting a Windows refund is not for the faint of heart.

Basically, there are four ways you can go if you don't want Windows on a non-Mac laptop: [list][*]Buy a laptop with Linux preinstalled or with no OS on it. This will severely limit your options, and may not even be an option, depending on what country you live in.[*]Buy a laptop with Windows and go through customer support, outsider consumer protection agencies, and even the civil courts to get a refund for the unused Windows license.[*]Buy a laptop with Windows and install Linux over it. Microsoft will count this as a Windows sale even if you never use the Windows license.[*]Build your own laptop. Again, not for the faint-hearted.[/list] So there you have it.

Limit your options. Get in a big legal battle. Or get in a big hardware battle. Or just tell Microsoft you're using Windows, even if you aren't.

Personally, I choose to just buy Linux preinstalled. It does limit what hardware I can buy, but I don't have to go through endless documenting and fighting back and forth with a company to get US$30 back, and I don't vote for Windows with my wallet.
