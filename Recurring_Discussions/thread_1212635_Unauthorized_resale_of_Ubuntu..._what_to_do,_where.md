### Post by aysiu on 2009-07-15
Is it legal to sell GPL'ed software?

Yes. Theoretically, you can sell for it as much as anyone's willing to pay for it.

Is it moral?

If you give someone the impression that Ubuntu can be had for only your price of $5000, then you're immoral for *lying* to that person (Ubuntu can be had for free).

But if people are aware Ubuntu can be had for free but choose to pay you for it anyway, then the market will force you to sell Ubuntu only at a reasonable price (to cover the cost of CD production, shipping, or offered support to the customer).

Not very many people would pay more than US$15 for Ubuntu, and I doubt anyone who knows Ubuntu can be had for free would pay more than US$50, unless some kind of corporate support contract is involved.
