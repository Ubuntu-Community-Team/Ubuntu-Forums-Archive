Alright, I'll bite...

If a film comes out that I am interested in, and I *know* people who see it and recommend it - I will and do go to the cinema to watch it, sometimes spending a vast amount of money. I see the Cinema as the movie equivalent of a Gig for a band, and likewise if a band I like comes to town, and their ticket prices are sane, then I'll go see them and support them.

If I see a DVD reasonably priced (e.g. < £5) of a film I want to see I will buy it. What I won't do is pay £12.99 for a DVD that I will watch once, might not like, and will make me sit through 10 minutes of adverts telling me that piracy is equivalent to stealing a car. 

I'm quite happy to part with money for a reasonable price. Hell, if new DVD releases were a fiver each and had no unskippable piracy ads, I'd buy so many DVDs I'd have nowhere to store them.

As it is, even at the bargain-bin "out for a year" prices, I'm still being screwed. The disc is still encrypted and it still has those unskippable ads.

The big thing about movies, books and music is they form our culture. We watch them, discuss them, share moments from them, but not everyone can afford them - should they be deprived of culture because they are deprived? If there is a solution sat right there, that affects no-one adversely, should those people that can't afford to pay £10 to go to the cinema be culturally excluded?

I guess I'm not being straight to the point, I'm just saying there is much more to it than "stealing", it's not black and white. 

I download from torrents only when I wouldn't have paid for it in the first place, and unfortunately the normal reasons for that are outrageous pricing and restrictions. If I could buy an MP3 album that costs almost *NOTHING* to reproduce for a sensible price like £2.50 I would never pirate music again. If I could pay £3-4 online for a movie that costs the seller nothing but bandwidth, in an open format, that I could OWN forever, then I'd never pirate movies again. If I was treated like a customer instead of a criminal - I'd start to respect the media industries again.
