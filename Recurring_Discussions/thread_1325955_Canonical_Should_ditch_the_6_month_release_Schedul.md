### Post by lykwydchykyn on 2009-11-13
For the last two months, we've had a forum filled with complaints that people had too much trouble installing Firefox 3.5, wanting to know why it wasn't immediately available as a simple upgrade, complaining that it wasn't called "firefox", etc. etc.

A few months before that, the forum was full of complaints that OpenOffice.org 3.1 wasn't in the Jaunty repositories, that it was too much trouble to install from Sun, wanting to know why they had to wait until Karmic to get it, etc.

Now Karmic's out, and some people have problems, and the new bandwagon is that six months is too short a time for a new release. 

Yet in the coming months there will be a new OpenOffice.org release, a new KDE release, and probably a new firefox release (among many others). And with each release, people will come here wanting to know how they can install it on Karmic, and there will be a whole lot of pontificating about how hard it is to keep up-to-date on Ubuntu, and how a few more months is just too long to wait for updated applications.

There are distros with 12 month or longer release cycles.  Have you used one of them?
