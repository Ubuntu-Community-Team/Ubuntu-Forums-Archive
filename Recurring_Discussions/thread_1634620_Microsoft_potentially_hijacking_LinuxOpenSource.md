### Post by czr114 on 2010-11-30
They can't patent that which is prior art.

They can't close up what is protected by the GPL.

Microsoft, and other giants like it, operate on [embrace, extend, extinguish]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embrace,_extend_and_extinguish"). The threat is from proprietary extensions or formats snuck into open frameworks and protocols, and other situations where encumbered IP or restricted technology becomes a de facto standard, such as what Facebook is trying to do to email, or what Adobe partially did to the browser and tried to do to PDF.
