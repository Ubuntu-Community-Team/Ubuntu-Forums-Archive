### Post by HappinessNow on 2009-07-29
> [IMG]http://ubuntuforums.org/attachment.php?attachmentid=122879&d=1248901866[/IMG]While employed on the campus of [Swansea University]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swansea_University"), he installed a very early version of [Linux]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux") on one of the machines belonging to the university computer society. This was one of the first Linux installations on a busy [network]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_network"), and revealed many bugs in the networking code. Cox fixed many of these bugs, and went on to rewrite much of the networking subsystem. He then became one of the main developers and maintainers of the whole kernel.
 He maintained the 2.2 branch, and his own versions of the 2.4 branch (signified by an "ac" in the version, for example 2.4.13-ac1). This branch was very stable and contained bugfixes that went directly into the vendor kernels. He was once commonly regarded as being the "second in command" after [Linus Torvalds]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linus_Torvalds") himself, before reducing his involvement with Linux to study for an [MBA]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration").[[1]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox#cite_note-0") His informative and friendly comments have guided many programmers on the [Linux kernel mailing list]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel_mailing_list"). However, on July 29th 2009, Cox walked away from the TTY layer, which he still maintained, after receiving harsh criticism from Linus Torvalds.[[2]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox#cite_note-1")[[3]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox#cite_note-2")
 Alan was employed by Linux distributor [Red Hat]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Hat") for ten years leaving in January 2009. He is now employed by [Intel]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel").[[4]]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox#cite_note-3")
 He has also been involved in the [GNOME]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME") and [X.Org]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X.Org_Server") projects, and was the main developer of [AberMUD]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AberMUD"), which he wrote whilst a student at the [University of Wales, Aberystwyth]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Wales,_Aberystwyth").
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Cox)

Interesting man this Mister Cox, Honestly, I think a Coxnus Kernel would be most interesting.
