### Post by Arand on 2010-03-24
@philinux
This is in my opinion a discussion, not a bug report.

@bth73
This is the development model ubuntu has chosen, and as far as they/we can tell the combination of standard and LTS releases satisfies a lot of people, and since it's popular, it seems to be a good compromise.

Also, M. Shuttleworth has stated that this is how he intends the release schedule to be, and hence it may be rather unlikely to change.

I myself agree that a the final releases often have unnecessary bugs still lingering, which, with a longer development cycle might be taken care of.

But then again, the quick releases is what keeps the steam up for ubuntu, it brings hype, media, developers, testers and users. And if that would decrease with a slower release cycle, I think the unfixed bugs might be an acceptable trade-off.

There are several other distributions out there which aim for stability rather than new features. And if it's a big concern, ubuntu might not be for you.

- Arand
