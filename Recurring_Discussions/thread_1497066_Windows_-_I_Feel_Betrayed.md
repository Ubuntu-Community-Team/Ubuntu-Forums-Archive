### Post by gerowen on 2010-05-30
I have a story to tell, a warning to any of you who have ever considered leaving Ubuntu and returning to the dark side.  If you're not ready to read my story, just take my word for it, DON'T!

So I decided to give Windows 7 a try.  I was not particularly unhappy with Ubuntu, but there were many games that I wanted to play that I didn't feel like messing around with to get running in Wine.  I'd heard that Windows 7 was a good operating system, and so I figured I would pick up a copy at my local Best Buy.  I should have done my homework.  First of all, unless you specifically purchase an OEM copy, you can only register each copy once online.  No more of the "For use on up to 3 personal computers" like in the days of XP Pro, just once and you're done.  If your computer crashes before the 6 month server purge, then you're out of luck trying to re-activate your Windows until that license key shows up as not being used for 6 months.

Now onto the stability.  While the user experience was fairly nice, I didn't get to spend much time there.  Most of my time was spent trying to figure out which Windows Update broke my computer, or watching the "Startup repair", or booting into Safe Mode and doing a System Restore because it randomly decided to continually BSOD on startup.  Not to mention that it didn't support my AHCI controller.  My first clue should have been a clear sign to put it back in the box before activating it, and thus probably sinking my chances of getting a $350 refund tomorrow.  When I first started the installation disc I got a BSOD, and had to go into the BIOS and set my hard drive to "Compatability" mode in order to actually install it.  This introduced a noticable performance hit, including a couple of "Windows has stopped responding" messages while I waited for the hard drive to catch up with the rest of the system.  My wife had installed the 32 bit version (I was using 64) from the same disc, un-activated due to the one time limit, and her computer, a completely different system, had similar issues.

I'm here to tell you all that if you're comfortable and doing everything you need to do in Ubuntu, stay here, the grass really is greener.  Don't let the alure of a couple of proprietary games pull you over like it did me.  I just wasted $350 and made my computer completely unusable.  I'm restoring my documents to an external hard drive and re-installing Ubuntu 10.04 64bit as I write this.  Windows 7 is the most unstable piece of crap I've ever installed on my machine.

### Post by SnickerSnack on 2010-05-30
After reading the first sentence of your story, I have my own to tell (I'll read yours in a bit).

I was trying to get my laptop to connect to a mac over the wireless network so I could backup some files (mac needed some work and I couldn't find my ide/sata to usb adapter [moved recently, so it's still boxed up]), but I was having trouble getting things to work.  I have very little experience with samba.

After about 30 minutes of fumbling, I remembered that I have windows vista installed also, so I booted in to that.  I thought it might be easier to network the two computers in windows.  I never got that far.

First reboot: Windows starts and is a bit sluggish for the first minute or so, but it chugs along okay after that.  I'm reminded that I haven't update virus definitions in X days, where X > 300.  I also swat away a few more popups.  I go to start > network.  The network is empty, so I click on the "network and sharing center", ooh, a center!  This program hangs while loading.  So a wait for a few minutes, then I try ctrl-alt-del to get the process manager, but nothing happens.  So I do a hard reboot.

Second reboot: Windows starts up a bit sluggish for the first minute or so, and it continues to be sluggish.  I'm again reminded about the state of the antivirus software and logitech asks for permission to check for updates.  I close those things and click start.  It .... o-p-e-n-s.  I move my pointer to the "Network" button with lightning speed.  Sleep 5.  The button highlights.  Out of curiosity, I move my pointer down to the next button.  Sleep 3.  The next button highlights.  Hmmmm.  Not good.  So, I click in the middle of the desktop to close the menu and wait a few seconds to give windows some time to get its stuff together.  I try the start menu again, and it's not any better, so I click on the "Network" button and am presented with a spinning blue ring to let me know that windows is working hard on ..... something.  And I hard-reboot after waiting for about half a minute.

Third reboot: Back to ubuntu and contemplating formating the partition that windows is currently violating.
