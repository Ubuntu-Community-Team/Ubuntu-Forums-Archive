### Post by insane_alien on 2011-12-18
there are some under the hood differences as well.

but really, the distros are just cosmetic wrappers and package managers for linux.

All the upstream apps are worked on by their own developers. Most distros will just make those apps easily accessible through a repository and perhaps apply a few custom patches that haven't made it to the upstream sources yet.

where custom patches are applied by a distro, the development work usually finds its way upstream. 

The apps are developed separately, think of distros as the bag you put all those apps into.
