The trick is, viruses are typically files, and they can still reside on Linux systems.

A more accurate term would be: "Linux can get viruses, but they are designed for Windows, and thus would have no effect on a Linux machine."

I often use my Linux system to scan my Windows partition. On top of that, I use an anti virus on my Linux system to scan my network drive, which is where windows users back their data up daily. It's good to do that because then that can notify me on whether a system on my network may be infected, all while having zero effect on the "server" itself.
