### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2008-07-05
You can speed-up regular Ubuntu by just turning-off Compiz.

Navigate to System>Preferences>Appearance
When the window opens, click on the Visual Effects tab.
Select none.

Also, when using Ubuntu, if you have not loaded the correct video driver for your PC, it can also slow things down quite a bit, as you would have no hardware rendering available, so your CPU has lots of extra work to do.

Unless you have a good graphics card, with lots of dedicated RAM compositing can slow down your PC significantly.

As far as X-Ubuntu is concerned, give it a go. If you want a lightweight desktop and speed, it may be exactly what you are looking for. XFCE is a really great lightweight desktop.

### Post by jimmy the saint on 2008-07-05
I have been playing with Xfce for a while now and I will tell you that the reason I am about to switch is dual monitor support.  Gnome sucks with TwinView (in my opinion) because there seems to be no way (that I have found) to prevent icons from being placed in the dead zones.  XFCE prevents this easily and allows for different backgrounds on each screen as well!!  I have also found that firefox acts like it is on steroids under xfce compared to gnome.  I am not talking about starting up, I mean that for some reason the pages display faster.  I have no idea why that is, but it is.  Xfce is definitely more minimalistic, and some things (like support for my volume keys) require a bit of tweaking, but all-in-all two days with xfce convinced me to make the switch.  In fact, a lot of the xubuntu specific tweaks are going to be removed because I found the XFCE defaults to be more convinient (such as the right click menu)

If you are just starting out with ubuntu and linux in general, I would stick with Gnome for a bit and ease into it a little.  If you have been using it for a bit, or like to jump in head first and just get it over with, XFCE is great (in my opinion)

### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2008-07-05
xubuntu is lighter because the default applications are lighter :)

Once you feel proficient with Ubuntu you can try a minimal install :

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/MinimalCD)

    [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems)

You can lighten gnome up a fair amount :

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=298818](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=298818)

+1 on XFCE being better with multiple monitors. Gnome lags behind both KDE and XFCE in this regard, not just in icons, but also in setting background images.

One thing I like with XFCE, the menu.

Go to XFCE Settings Manager -> Desktop. In the "Behavior" Tab select (check off) "Show desktop menu on right click". Now you do not need to move the mouse to the panel, just right click anywhere on the desktop.

Also if you like effects go to "Window manager tweaks" -> Compositor tab -> "Enable Display composting".

Other options are to run a light weight distro such as puppy linux and light weight applications : [http://users.skynet.be/six/gpure/tech/linux/apps.html](http://users.skynet.be/six/gpure/tech/linux/apps.html)

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=575456)
