### Post by Whiffle on 2008-03-25
> **sunexplodes said:**

Ditto that.  Vanilla KDE on ubuntu is better than Kubuntu IMO.  Kubuntu over-simplifies some stuff and breaks other things.  KDEMod on Arch is flat out great.  It runs very very nicely, not to mention having a default theme that I havn't had the desire to change yet.

But as for gnome vs kde.  The biggest draw for me is KDE integration.  I can use the kio_slaves in any KDE file menu, just type in the address of the file and you're off.  

The configurability is very nice as well, I've made easy to use keyboard shortcuts for all my common apps, all you have to do is right click the app in the kmenu to get to the editor.  Its also got a handy Universal Sidebar that can control Amarok, open RSS feeds, bookmarks, files, etc.

In addition, Konqueror is way ahead of nautilus in the file management department.  Tabs, split windows, integrated terminal, web page archiving, rss reader, ssh/sftp/ftp/fish, etc, etc.  Some people don't like that Konq kind of goes against the "one job, one tool" philosophy of linux, but I rather like having such a swiss army knife at my disposal, it comes in very handy.

I keep venturing into other WM's, but its always KDE that I end up coming back to, once you change the theme and get over the fact that absolutely everything has a k in the name, its quite excellent.
