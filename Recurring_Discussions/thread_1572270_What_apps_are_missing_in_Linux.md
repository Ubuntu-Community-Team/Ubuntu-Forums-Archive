Just like with a Mac, if you can get people excited about trying something new with Linux, and make it clear that it's new and different, they won't mind as much giving up an application for the experience. We need to stop stressing ourselves out about catering to every Windows user out there and focus on making the best applications around.

I think it's far more important to help people on other platforms become familiar with good open source applications and get as much open source as they can, rather than trying to bring tons of applications from Windows over to Linux. Due to their unique nature, Adobe CS and Games are the only things I think are really justifiable to bring over, at least at this point in time. Other than that, we should focus on improving our own applications.

And maybe some day in the next couple decades, our creativity applications will become as good compared to the competition as our kernels and DEs.
