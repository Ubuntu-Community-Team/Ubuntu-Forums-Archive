### Post by QIII on 2009-07-04
Just a couple of cents here, folks.

There are some myths about Linux that we should take care not to believe, because we may keep our snooty noses in the air long enough to get our behinds whipped.

Myth 1:  Linux is impervious to viruses.  

The Linux community has enjoyed some obscurity in the past and so has not captured the full attention of virus authors.  We have not been heavily targeted YET.

Myth 2:  There are no Linux viruses in the wild.

In fact, there are something on the order of 800 of them in the wild.  At the moment they have been primarily targeted at servers.  Your PC has not been heavily targeted YET.

If we continue to be convinced of our invulnerability and keep our guard down, all it will take is for one PFY in Des Moines to be fiddling around with his Linux distro to go "Hey!  Way cool!" before he brings us all to our knees.
