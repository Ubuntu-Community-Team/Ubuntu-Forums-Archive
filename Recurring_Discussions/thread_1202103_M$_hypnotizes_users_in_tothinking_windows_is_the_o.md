### Post by monsterstack on 2009-07-01
It isn't some fancy Microsoft mind-meld. Linux has no presence in the market. Or at least it has a very small one. Walk into a computer shop and you will see computers running Windows. Until that changes, vast swathes of the population won't even know Linux exists. Forget that it runs half the internet, or runs on almost all of the world's supercomputers, or in hand-held devices all over the globe, or in cash machines, hospital equipment, routers, and such. For most people, all that stuff is irrelevant because they don't think about it. How Microsoft managed to get PC manufacturers to install Windows and only Windows on new desktop PCs way back when wasn't hypnotism either: it was bribery and bullying that achieved that feat.
