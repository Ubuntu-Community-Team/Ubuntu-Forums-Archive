### Post by Copper Bezel on 2011-11-06
The UI is nothing to do with it. That's part of Torvalds' point, and part of the reason that Canonical is getting a lot of flak regarding Unity in the first place for thinking that a pretty UI can sell an OS. (Gnome's in a different position.) 

We had that thread on Miguel de Icaza's point of view on why Linux doesn't take over on the desktop, too, and he pointed to a poor base of apps instead of hardware support, but it's a similar sentiment. He made the same point, too, on the desktop gradually becoming irrelevant anyway.

In any case, these arguments are really, seriously nothing new. If your top three list of reasons Linux isn't common on the desktop doesn't consist of Windows' incumbency, a limited driver base, and poor support for commercial apps, you're wrong. Sorry. The only debate is on which of those things is a bigger problem, and ultimately whether or not it matters.
