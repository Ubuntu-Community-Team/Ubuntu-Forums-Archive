### Post by snowpine on 2011-06-07
Long Term Support (LTS) means that if you customize your Ubuntu 10.04 install to your liking in April 2010, it will work *exactly* the same way in April 2013 when 10.04 goes "end of life." New features or major software upgrades are not considered "LTS" because they risk introducing new bugs or changes to configuration. In other words it is not possible for LTS to "go downhill!" because you know exactly what you are getting from day 1.

Are you aware of the "backports" repository? It exists for precisely the purpose you mention: to give users the option to install newer applications on top of a generally stable "base" installation.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuBackports](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuBackports)
