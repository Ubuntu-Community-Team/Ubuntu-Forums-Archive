### Post by lovinglinux on 2010-08-03
Get [Google Alarm]("http://fffff.at/google-alarm/"), an extension that triggers audible & visual alerts when you are sending data to Google servers.

It is quite funny :)

[Install in Firefox]("http://jamiedubs.com/googlealarm/google-alarm.xpi") [[no sound version]("http://jamiedubs.com/googlealarm/google-alarm-nosound.xpi")]

[Install in Chrome]("http://jamiedubs.com/googlealarm/google-alarm.user.js") [[no sound version]("http://jamiedubs.com/googlealarm/google-alarm-nosound.user.js")]

[COLOR="red"]**Warning:**[/COLOR]

The Firefox version is self-hosted, which means it hasn't been reviewed or approved by Mozilla. Google doesn't review any extension published on their site and although Chrome is known to be secure, a malicious extension could easily bypass the security measures (privileges).

So, use it at your own risk. If you want to test it, I would advise to do it on a VM or at least on a clean Firefox profile.

That being said, I have tested and it works as advertised, but I haven't inspected the code yet. BTW, I'm not the developer. The developer profile can be found at [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/user/1164904/](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/user/1164904/)
