### Post by Brunellus on 2010-09-10
I object to the use of "evil."

Neither corporation has any obligation, legally or otherwise, to anyone but their own share-holders.  They can and will do everything they can (within the law) to maximize profits.  Is that "evil"?  I don't think so.  

Corporate profit seeking, without more, isn't "evil."  The overuse of the word blinds us to **real** evil in the world--and there's plenty of that to worry about without confusing the issue with geeky worries about tech companies representing some formless nerd "evil."

### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-09-18
Apple is the better company, but Microsoft is the lesser evil. Microsoft isn't evil, they just make crappy software. The last time they did anything really bad was with OOXML, and before that it was probably something back in the pre-open-source-acknowledgement days.

Since Apple plays nice with open source, it's all the more obvious where they've made decisions that explicitly screw users.

Of course, they're both out to make money, and the customer experience will inevitably suffer this way. But now that computers are important for work and personal use, not just gaming and playing around with cool technology, it's seriously affecting people when each company tries to push its own closed standard above the other.

The users will always be in control, though. Whether the code is closed or not, they find a way.

But seriously, Microsoft isn't evil, they just don't do a very good job of making software, compared to their competition (they still do an alright job of it). Apple are the people filing lawsuits left and right, forcing the people who use their products to use them in a certain way, and talking about every good thing they do as if it's a first, a revolution.

And the sad thing is that people buy into it so hard. It's like a religion. Software is like anything else in life- why should you be forced to eat an apple over an orange or a strawberry, or vice versa? You should be able to eat whatever you want. The cult of the Apple, stealing people's fruit and replacing it, claiming the Apple is the only fruit worth eating, claiming the Apple is the first fruit to be sweet, red, or juicy... it's just uncalled for.

Why can't Apple just say, "we did this cool thing!" rather than, "we did this cool thing FIRST, and we're INNOVATING EVERYTHING!"

I'll take innovation into my own hands, thanks.
