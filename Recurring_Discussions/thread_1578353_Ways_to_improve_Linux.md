### Post by HolidayQueen on 2010-09-20
Im not just talking Ubuntu, but linux at large. I have a few comments (and possibly complaints) on the subject.

**Community Support** I find this is a joke for most distributions. The only thing most of the forums are good for, is to see if someone else has already had, and solved, your problem. Otherwise you're on your own. You could spend weeks tinkering with the wording of your google searches before anyone comes along to ask you for the output of dmesg. And if you're lucky enough to get a response, most of the time your told there's nothing wrong with the system, just the user.

**Variety of Distributions** We have hundreds of distributions which for the most part, all want to be the same thing, ie, *your distribution of choice for everything*. What we end up with is a bunch of distributions, with only slight variances on the original it's based on, that are all bloated. Most people have varying needs for diferent computers, and most geeks have more than one computer.

Personally, i have 3 computers.  One for chatting and surfing the web (Ubuntu), one for storage and playing movies, tv, music (Crunchbang XFCE), and one for downloading and seeding (Crunchbang Openbox).

It would be nice to have more distributions that cater to specific needs, instead of having a bunch of distributions that offer their spin on what programs you should be using for everything.

**Cross-platform Packaging** It would be nice to see a new unified format for packages, instead of having debs, RPMs, PUPs, etc.

**Fixing real problems** Do i really give a flying monkey that i have a new wallpaper and theme pack, when the items on my gnome-panel still shift positions by themselves unless i lock them all down? This bug has been present since i first switched in 2006, and shows no signs of being resolved any time soon.


Feel free to argue, comment, or add to this list :-)
