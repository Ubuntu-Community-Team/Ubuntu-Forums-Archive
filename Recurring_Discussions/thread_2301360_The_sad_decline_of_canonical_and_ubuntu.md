### Post by ThoseTimesAreGone on 2015-11-01
The reason why i started using linux a long time ago was because of canonical and ubuntu. i was a windows noob back then and i was glad to see that there was one company that was trying to make an easy to use linux OS. It got visibly better with every single release.

Somewhere along those lines canonical changed their focus. Possibly because the desktop ubuntu wasnt making the company profitable. Development shifted to servers, and then came ideas to do ubuntu for TV, mobile devices, and tablets. I can understand why canonical was looking for a different revenue stream. But i think that they overextended themselves to the point where nothing they are making is any good anymore. Ubuntu for TVs never materialized. Still cant buy any ubuntu tablet. Ubuntu phone released in Europe, thats the only sign of progress. Still no USA version, after working on it for years. And in order to achieve that they close to entirely abandoned the desktop variant. 

Yes i know they are working on unity 8 and mir and all of that fine stuff. But the fact remains that the desktop OS has barely changed for several years now. I dont blame them, but the point is if the team is so small they shouldnt be splitting it up among so many different tasks. That and the insistence to create everything from scratch on their own (mir) etc. How do they plan to do all of these things without the man power? And all of the stupid scandals and controversies (Kubuntu, amazon). What the heck happened to Canonical?

So we have the current result; ubuntu is #3 on distrowatch, and still looks and feels like a pig (where the hell is some transparency or anything that resembles a modern UI?). While some guy named Clem makes an OS that is miles ahead of anything ubuntu can offer right now. 

Sad to see because When they originally started out i always hoped that Canonical would become the apple of linux world (at least the good parts). 

Have to say I have been using linux mint for almost a year now, and ended up buying an android based phone yesterday after waiting for ubuntu phone for a few years. At this rate maybe by the time Im retired (should be a short 40+ years) they can release something thats worth using.

Sorry if the post is overly pessimistic but honestly I cant see these guys making any serious impact in the near future (minus the server side).

They should have either stayed focused on only a few projects, or used the work of the community more and not try to reinvent the wheel every chance they get.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-11-01
Is it the time of year or the phase of the moon? It must be something. This is the third troll in as many days.

Just a small but significant point.

Linux Mint would not exist without Canonical. The original Ubuntu developers set up their own Ubuntu archives of Debian packaged binary files. Without Canonical maintaining those archives and syncing them with the Debian archives every development cycle to keep them up to date with Debian, then the Linux Mint developers would have to build their own Mint archives from the Debian archives and regularly rebuild the archives. Just like Canonical does for Ubuntu. And also for the benefit all those derivatives, official or otherwise that make use of the Ubuntu archives.

### Post by Sweet_Baby_Jamie on 2015-11-02
I spent a little while reading after Googling the history of Linux.

Ubuntu wouldn't exist without Debian. But Ubuntu makes Debian easier to install, configure, and use.  And it works on a wider range of hardware than Debian.  Because it's so much simpler, alot more people choose Ubuntu over Debian, but they're still using Debian in a sense.  Lots of people have made Debian easier, but no one has done better at simplifying and improving Debian than Canonical!  They made "Linux for Human Beings" and have done more to bring desktop Linux to us ordinary people than anyone.  

It's the same thing with "some guy named Clem" and his friends, isn't it?  One brilliant developer does a bunch of stuff to Ubuntu, because it's licensed that way, and makes it "better" (a matter of opinion of course).  But Mint wouldn't exist without Ubuntu and Debian.  He is doing the same thing with Ubuntu that Ubuntu did with Debian, but just like Canonical, Clem is building on the work of others.  Others have worked to improve Debian too (Corel, Linspire, Mepis, Kanotix, others) but without the fantastic success of Ubuntu.  The most successful distros that seek to simplify and improve upon Debian borrow alot from Ubuntu or build from Ubuntu instead of from Debian directly (including Mint).

The most new innovations are coming from Canonical, and they are aiming for an interface that can be the same from desktop to tablet to tablet to smart phone.  So I don't get what you mean by nothing has changed on the desktop for years.  And with the laws being different from Europe to the US, and competition being what it is, I think the progress has been amazing!

**It's hard to understand how people can be angry or disappointed with a company that makes an awesome OS and gives it away for free!**  If you don't think you're getting your money's worth, consider how much you spent on Ubuntu.  I don't think you'll find a better price anywhere else.
