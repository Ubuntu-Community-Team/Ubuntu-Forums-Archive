### Post by NMFTM on 2010-08-31
> **beew said:**
> I don't know why gaming is so important. Mac doesn't do much gaming either. If gaming is so important for you get a console. I don't really think of gaming as a main function of computers. But then I am not a gamer
so I am probably biased.
A lot of people prefer the home computer as a gaming platform because it's the easiest system to play pirated games on. Especially for people (e.g., college students) who are probably on a tight budget and might not want to spend several hundred dollars on a gaming system and than spend $20-60 on each subsequent game for said system. In this day in age, almost everyone in a first world country has a computer. So, most people probably figure that rather than spending money on a computer and then shelling out at least $300 more on a PS3 or Xbox360. They could just take the $300 they would have spent on the gaming system and use it to upgrade to a more powerful computer. Since they'd need to buy one anyway. Figuring that it'd also be a more long term solution since even if newer games a year or two down the road couldn't be played at at high video settings. They could just turn down the settings and play like that for awhile longer before needing to upgrade their hardware again.

Especially when it comes to even the smallest of LAN parties. Talking even a small handful of people into buying X game so it can be LAN'd is challenging, especially if everyone has different tastes in games. But with piracy everyone can be given a copy of the game without a ton of arguing over what games should be played at the party. Since they don't actually have to go out and buy it. Based on my experiences and talking to friends who have been to many more LAN parties than I have. I'd venture to guess that upwards of 75% of LAN parties that aren't centered around one particular game (e.g. Halo or WOW parties) are also mostly centered around piracy as the primary means of software distribution. And that's a conservative estimate.

I'm not saying it's fair to the game developers or publishers. Or that it's not also illegal. I'm just saying that it goes on extensively among certain groups of people and to pretend that it's probably not one of the bigger reasons why a lot of people prefer a computer to a console would be naive.
