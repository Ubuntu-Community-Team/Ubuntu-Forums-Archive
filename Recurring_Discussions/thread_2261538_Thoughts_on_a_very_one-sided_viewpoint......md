### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-01-19
I've been checking in on that Narod piece for a few years. If you pay close attention to the details of his remarks -- releases, version numbers, sources and references sited -- he does a good job of pointing to problems and deficiencies in Linux. He also revises his remarks when circumstances warrant.  If you read it thoroughly, you'll see that, if anything, he's more negative about Windows.

### Post by grahammechanical on 2015-01-19
I have not read that blog post, yet. I may not do so. I only have 7 years experience with Linux and this is with Ubuntu but I would say the Linux community has been tearing itself apart from almost the beginning. It is in the DNA of FOSS to fork the code.

Richard Stallman disagreed with the way software was written and distributed but having built the utilities to write the code and having formalised the freedoms he wanted for himself and other programmers he could hardly demand that everyone work on his ideal OS. People took their freedom and his utilities, said thank you very much and worked on Linux or Gnu/Linux.

The founding developers of Ubuntu saw something good in Debian and Gnome but thought that they could add value and so we use Ubuntu. Other developers took the code and replaced Gnome with some other desktop environment. And so we have a Ubuntu family.

We hear talk of "community" but it is really just the usual assortment of people that are found in every field of endeavour. Call it tearing apart or fragmentation or vibrant and alive, it is really all the same thing. It is human activity.

Regards.
