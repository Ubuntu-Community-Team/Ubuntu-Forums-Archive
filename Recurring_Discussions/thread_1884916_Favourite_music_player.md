### Post by Erik1984 on 2011-11-23
Rhythmbox is my favorite player overall. So if I had to pick one player and ditch all others my choice is Rhythmbox. However for specific purposes I like other players as well:

- Audacious for just playing some files in case you don't need a library and .cue file support.
- Music Play Daemon and a terminal client to control it. Great when you work in a terminal environment like Terminator or Tmux to reserve one panel for an MPD client.

### Post by Thad E Ginathom on 2011-11-24
**Aqualung** because ...

--- it does truly, really, **gapless** playing (that is, you get gaps when the tracks are separate, and *none at all *when they are not) which is essential for things like classical or rock concerts.

--- output to jack is an option and it works well (necessary for us Firewire-audio people) 

--- its interface is simple and plain. It plays music; it does not pretend to be a shop or a browser.

--- it plays the music straight, but if you want to mess with it, you have access to the huge array of LADSPA effetcs.

--- no problem with playing CDs.

--- tabbed playlists is neat (and it remembers them between sessions): it's like being able to keep your currently favourite records on the top of the pile.

It was my hunt for gapless play that brought Aqualung and I together. I think it is going to be a long-term relationship!
