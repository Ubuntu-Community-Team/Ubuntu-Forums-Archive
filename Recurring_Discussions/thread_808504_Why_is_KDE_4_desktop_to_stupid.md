Err, not really.

4.1 will be released this summer, and while it will have enough features to be usable, it won't reach feature parity with 3.5 until 4.2 is released, which is planned for January of next year. 

If you want to test 4.1 before it's out, KDE4Daily is a great way to do it; [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=807766](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=807766) or [http://dot.kde.org/1211789022/](http://dot.kde.org/1211789022/) . 

Also, KDE4 is re-thinking the concept of the old "Desktop" where it was simply a representation of the "Desktop" folder. If you want folders on your desktop, it can be achieved through the "Folder View" widget that's being shipped with 4.1; it's already in SVN trunk, and it's included in the latest KDE4Daily as well. 

The folder view wiget makes it so you can have representations of as many or as few folders as you want, and lets you neatly organize the concept of having folders on the desktop. I think it's a huge improvement over the old system, where things from the ~/Desktop folder were simply sprawled across your screen messily.
