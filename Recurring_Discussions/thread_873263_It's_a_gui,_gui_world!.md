### Post by kansasnoob on 2008-07-28
I'm just curious why so many folks still seem to consider the gui almost unworthy when it comes to modifying Ubuntu?

I mean cli will always have it's place, but many people devote many hours to developing gui's for nearly everything ................ yet, when we have total nOOb's ask for help we often steer them into cli land, even though a sweet gui is available in synaptic.

Now, this is not meant as a complaint. I simply think most of us would like to see Ubuntu really thrive, but I really think if we stick to gui solutions for nOObs (like me) wherever possible it will eleviate most of the stress migrating from Windows to Ubuntu.

To put it simply: We want this to be so easy anyone can just DO IT! And it's getting there!

I'd just suggest at least including gui alternatives with cli instuctions from now on. And, yes, I do know that amounts to more work! But creating the gui's is a ton of work too.

IMHO, that's a large part of what Ubuntu is all about .............. making great Linux available to everyone!

### Post by t0p on 2008-07-28
I don't think anyone dismisses the GUI in Linux - my installation of Ubuntu sports  TWO GUIS (Gnome and KDE).  But it is a fact that the command line very often provides a more powerful, and more adaptable way of solving problems and inputting commands.

Sure, GUI solutions may often *seem* easier to the new user fresh from the Windoze environment.  But a carefully-crafted Terminal command can save you many hundreds of point-and-click mouse and wrist movements.  Who wants repetitive strain injury? Use the command line!! ;)
