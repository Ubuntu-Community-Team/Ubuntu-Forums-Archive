### Post by tillie on 2011-12-05
Hey people,

This probably has been suggested many times, but why won't all the makers of distros for an organised group with the goal of making the best distro ever, that can compete with mac and windows? 

In that way you tackle the following problems:

- drivers: people want the stuff to work, so you need drivers. In a combined force you can get the drivers from the manufacturers easier, or you can buy them. Than people can check on the internet by simple filling in an easy form to check if their computer is supported and than download the OS.

- technical perfection: all the little bugs and errors and small things can be worked on, since everyone is working on 1 thing

- user friendly: you can make a version that is for advanced users and make a sort of layer on top of that and call that the noob version. That layer pre-installs everything needed for a 70 year old to not be able to make any mistakes. 

- better image: people want a perfect system and they don't want to be reminded that is made by people (who can make mistakes), or that the system is made up by components like KDE or Audiopulse. It should be one flawless system that at least looks like it was designed as a whole with one goal and purpose. 

And there are probably some other benefits.

### Post by tehcavil on 2011-12-05
There will never be a one-distro development model because that's not how open-source/free software works. People have the right to fork projects, and they do. Although there is some collaboration on certain things (like the kernel).

Secondly, all the different distros are made by different companies (Novell, Red Hat, Canonical) and some aren't even made by companies but by individuals and groups that aren't corporate.

People like having choice. Choice = freedom and Linux is all about freedom. If there was only one distro, if you didn't like it, you would have no choice but to stick with it (no choice). Unless you wanted to make your own version in which case you have forked it.

drivers: all the drivers that Linux has are in the kernel, which is what all distros have in common, so, actually, there is already collaboration on this.

technical perfection: Not sure how "one distro" would solve this, windows and mac only make one version at a time, and they still have bugs. Every system will have some sort of bugs, that's just the reality of programs/operating systems.

user-friendly: we already have a user friendly distro, it's called Ubuntu. With the advent of unity, its even more user friendly with nice big colorful buttons to push.

Basically what your suggesting goes against the idea of Linux because Linux is supposed to be something flexible which you can modify and have choice/freedom.

If you wanted a shiny perfect box, you should have bought a mac.
The closest thing we have to that is Ubuntu, which has the vast majority of the desktop Linux market already.
