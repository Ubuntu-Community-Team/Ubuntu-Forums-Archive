### Post by Vaphell on 2009-10-07
still linux suffers from a chicken-and-egg problem. People expect things just work - gfx cards, sound, cameras - you name it, they want plug their ipod in and use itunes to copy music, they want their skype, etc, etc. 

Linux supports an awful lot of hardware out of the box, more than windows ever will but windows users at least get their driver cds. If there is no driver for linux and manufacturer has no intention to provide support, you are pretty much SOL. There may be reverse-engineered open source driver but i wouldn't expect it to work reliably.
Clueless users also are afraid of doing arcane stuff in command line - we know this is in fact superior way of fixing things than describing how to find your way through the gui)

People are trained to use windows for photoshop, plethora of games and such. They are not willing to break their habits, learn free alternatives. Learning is unpleasant - you are a noob again.
I agree that restrictive law enforcement would do a lot of good to linux marketshare but microsoft is not that stupid and knows well how to train future generations of users - not punishing private use piracy, giving away software to schools, universities, etc almost free... when people have their habits in place when they enter the world of commerce, this is the right moment to mercilessly milk them dry.

linux will grow slowly but steadily because:
- it's free, economy sucks and for majority of non-specialized tasks on desktop it is good enough so companies switch at least partially
- it's flexible, so it can be adjusted to run on a range of hardware. Netbooks were the big chance but MS managed to take that niche with resurrected XP. But there are netbooks2.0 coming soon (based on ARM chips) and MS doesn't really have anything to show here.
- powerful mobile phones running linux and other embedded devices

when linux community reaches critical mass it will go much faster - more drivers, more supported software, games.

### Post by aysiu on 2009-10-07
There are actually many reasons Linux isn't the most popular OS.

1. Most people haven't heard of it

2. Out of the few who have heard of it, many think it is as only a server or embedded OS and not as a desktop/laptop OS

3. Out of the fewer still who have tried it as a desktop/laptop OS, most are dependent on Windows-only programs or Windows-only hardware peripherals, so they give up and go back to Windows

What can you do to change Linux's popularity?

1. Stop shoving it down people's throats as a panacea for all Windows problems.

2. Stop pretending Linux doesn't have any problems.

3. Be honest about what Linux can and can't do.

4. File bug reports, donate to open source projects, write accessible documentation, and contribute code... or just do one of the aforementioned things.

5. Buy Linux preinstalled instead of buying Windows preinstalled.
