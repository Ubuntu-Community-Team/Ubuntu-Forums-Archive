### Post by raronson on 2009-08-08
I'm operating under the opinion that antivirus software (like ClamAV) can be safely ignored for my laptop and home server at the moment.  If I were administering Linux though, it'd be a different story.

Everyone that uses a computer *should* be concerned about viruses, but when you know how they work, make regular backups, and don't have 100 machines to worry about, the fear of viruses is minimized.  It's probably dumb that a lot of us don't use antivirus, especially when it's easy to install and readily available, but FOSS operating systems still enjoy the low-profile status.

I can't remember who or where, but someone on the forums once said Windows is around to keep flies off of the rest of us.  I got a chuckle out of that, but I foresee a day when antivirus will be needed for anyone connecting to the Internet.  You could argue that it's that way right now, especially with the latest Flash vulnerability coming to light, but I'm not so worried.  If worse to came to worse, I'd only have to do a couple of reinstalls and apply backups.  The enterprise would be a much different scenario.
