### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-03-28
If you're worried about your search queries being sent to Amazon with your knowledge, but without your IP, and you call that "spyware"; well, you probably haven't thought too much about the internet.

Every web page you visit gets your IP address, your screen resolution, your operating system and browser, the version of Flash and Java you are running and the web page you previously visited. If you got to the page using a search engine, they also get your search terms. With your IP address, a web server can find out what your ISP is. A web page can also use a number of tricks to store little bits of information on your computer ("cookies") that other sites can read back. Your browsing and online shopping history becomes common knowledge!

Your local search terms being sent to Amazon really should be the least of your privacy concerns. Heck, if you use the Videos lens your query gets sent behind the Great Firewall of China; but nobody's interested in that because it's not a successful and much-maligned American company. Also, because they can't pronounce the name of the Chinese site.

Got a supermarket loyalty card? It's used to build up a profile of what you buy. Okay, so you tear it up - what about your credit card? Your bank card? These can be used in the same way. Public transport smartcard? The government knows where you travel and when. Got a phone? Your mobile phone carrier knows where you are to the nearest kilometer, every hour of every day. If it's an Android phone, then Google probably knows too. Been out in public today? You've been watched by CCTV cameras.

That's all a lot worse than "Amazon knows an Ubuntu user searched for Brasero and a photo of my friend Sonia, or maybe it was two different people looking those things up".
