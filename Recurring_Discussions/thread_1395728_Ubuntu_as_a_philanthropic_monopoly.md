### Post by agger on 2010-02-01
Have anyone seen [this posting]("http://www.nettime.org/Lists-Archives/nettime-l-1001/msg00074.html")?

It exposes a criticism of the Ubuntu project which I think higlights 
some things which should concern us (is "free" used as meaning "gratis, 
of no cost" in the Ubuntu Software Center? Why does Canonical's 
contributor copyright assignment not promise to always release assigned 
code as free software, as the FSF's does?).

Is Ubuntu's commitment to software freedom being watered down because of 
   the installation by default of an integrated client to a proprietary 
network service (UbuntuOne)?

I do not quite agree with jaromil's assessment that Ubuntu is harmful, I 
think it has made a huge contribution to free software and think it will 
continue to do so, however the concerns as have also been voiced on the 
devel list, certainly seem valid to me.

Any thoughts on this?

### Post by agger on 2010-02-01
> **mkvnmtr said:**
> You know, I really do not care about any of these high sounding arguments. I just want a distro to use. Today I am using Ubuntu. Who knows what I will use next year. I do not mind anyone making money off of software and will not support those that critize those that do.

Nobody criticizes people making money from software. I think everyone agrees that is a perfectly legitimate thing to do. 

Muddying the waters by setting "free software" against "commercial software" instead of "proprietary software" as may be the problem with the Ubuntu Software Center (at least in the Italian translation) or hinting that proprietary software or solutions are "the way to go" may be problematic, on the other hand. At least from the point of view of software freedom, which freedom movement is the main reason that Ubuntu and other Linux-based systems exist in the first place.

Like I said, I don't agree with jaromil's assertion that Ubuntu is harmful, quite the contrary, but I do believe there's some valid points there that should be addressed by Ubuntu as a community. 

Of course, to some extent they are being addressed and discussed, cf.  the thread in question on the ubuntu-devel list, which is well worth reading:

[https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel/2010-January/029976.html](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel/2010-January/029976.html)
