### Post by foresthill on 2011-07-04
My thoughts on the subject are as follows.

Once upon a time, I downloaded and installed the Netbook Version of Ubuntu 9.10. I tried it, and said to myself, "Wow, that's an interesting concept, but not very practical. I don't like it very much." So I went back to the "regular version" and was happy as a clam.

I suspect other people felt the same way, since very few support requests seemed to be from people using the netbook version. What were the download figures for the two versions, I wonder?

Fast forward to April 2011. Some faceless apparatchik has suddenly decided that the netbook version is so bloody good, everyone is going to use this bizarre dumbed-down GUI whether they like it or not. 

Sorry, but I don't think so. 

KDE 4 has already ruined KDE and made it unusable for me, and now this bs is determined to ruin Gnome too. 

I will go back to Windows if I have to, but I am not using Unity, it is a steaming pile, IMO. And I am not alone in this belief. From the grumblings I read daily on these forums, I strongly suspect I am in the majority. 

Fortunately, I am a "low beaner" and have not yet been whipped into submission and conformity by the forces of group peer pressure, I can am still able to think for myself on the subject. :p

But I might as well speak my mind. From the looks of things, I won't be around much longer anyway.
