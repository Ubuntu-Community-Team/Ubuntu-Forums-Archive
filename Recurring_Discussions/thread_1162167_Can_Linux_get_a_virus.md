### Post by aysiu on 2009-05-17
> **bgs100 said:**
> [http://www.geekzone.co.nz/foobar/6229](http://www.geekzone.co.nz/foobar/6229)
That's not a virus.

Yes, you can trick a user into running a malicious file in any OS (Macs have trojans, Windows have trojans, and Linux could easily have trojans, too).

If people are willing to double-click any .deb file from an untrusted source, their entire installation is compromised, but that is not a virus.

Maybe you should read up on social engineering:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_%28security%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_engineering_%28security%29)

This question of whether Linux can get a virus or not is not practically interesting. It's like debating whether there is life similar to human life in another galaxy or debating what our world would look like if all weapons were destroyed and we had world peace. I mean, I'm in my apartment on a sunny day. Is it possible that an earthquake could tumble everything around me, and then all of a sudden I get struck by lightning? Sure. It's possible. Am I losing any sleep over that possibility? No. Intriguing theoretical discussions really have no practical bearing on day-to-day living.

Questions more relevant to day-to-day living with Linux are:

Q: Do I need to worry about malware automatically infecting my Linux OS?
A: No.

Q: Will running antivirus do anything to protect me?
A: No.

Q: Is there anything I can do to secure Linux?
A: Yes. Don't enable extra network services unless you know what you're doing. Use strong passwords. Understand how to avoid being the victim of social engineering. If you're really paranoid, run Firefox with NoScript and configure AppArmor profiles.

Q: If I don't do those things is it likely my Linux OS will be infected with malware?
A: No. It's still highly unlikely.
