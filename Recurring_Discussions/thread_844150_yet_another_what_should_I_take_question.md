### Post by nvteighen on 2008-06-29
Dear OP,
in these forums you'll be recommended to take Python, mainly because it's meant to be a language for beginners but also quite powerful to continue using it all your life. Also, because Ubuntu uses it and these are the Ubuntu Forums...

But, programming languages are tools. And, as in life, for each task you need the best tool. There's not "A language to rule them all", aka "the Holy Grail of programming".

First, get familiar with the names of the most popular programming languages and research a bit on them (Wikipedia should be enough), on what each language is meant to do, which drawbacks it has and which advantages, etc. After having seen the different possibilities, choose one and stick to it persistently until you have got some real level on it: **don't jump from language to language**.

And most important: keep asking questions! :)
