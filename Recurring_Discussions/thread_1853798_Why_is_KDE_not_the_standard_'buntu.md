### Post by mp035 on 2011-10-03
First of all, this is partly a little bit of a light hearted troll, so please don't get all gnome-zealous on me :p

My first 'buntu was kubuntu, but I switched to gnome some years ago mainly because kubuntu always felt like the poor cousin of the group.  I LOVE xubuntu, and use it often, but I like the bells and whistles (like gvfs for iphone) that ubuntu has.

While I think that unity is good, it is not really ready for mainstream use yet so I decided to return to kubuntu for another look.

Well I have to say WOW!!!!!!!!!!! :o

It is a really sweet setup.  Seeing as ubuntu has dumped gnome in favour of unity, and they even use Qt (the kde toolkit) for unity 2D, why don't the ubuntu team adopt kde as the standard setup.

Some things I have noticed:
1) Qt is an excellent toolkit, and works well (which is why they picked it for Unity 2D).  It is cross platform, and currently maintained by Nokia.  It is available under GPL, LGPL and commercial options.  Why not make more use of it?  Better toolkits mean more time developing apps and less time squashing bugs and making workarounds.
2) Kpackagekit is almost a clone of ubuntu-software-center
3) KDE Has WAY sweeter apps (compare for example, k3b to brasero )

I welcome opinions (and rants if you so desire ;) )
