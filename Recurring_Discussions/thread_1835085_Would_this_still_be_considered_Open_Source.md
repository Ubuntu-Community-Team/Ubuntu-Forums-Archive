### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-08-30
> **FlameReaper said:**

Or would I just rather make my code proprietary if I really want to focus on "lets make people buy what I made?"

You *can* charge people to download/buy your program (even the source code), but then whoever bought your program or code is free to redistribute their copy to other people. Unethical, yes, but still legal under every FOSS license.

The best solution I've come up with is making all the *artwork* in your program proprietary (say, CC-BY-NC-ND), but keep the code under a FOSS license. (This works especially well for video games, BTW.) That way, people are free to use the code in your product, but you maintain complete control over all the artwork. RMS himself recommends this approach - see [http://www.libervis.com/article/rms_on_the_ethics_of_non_free_art](http://www.libervis.com/article/rms_on_the_ethics_of_non_free_art).
