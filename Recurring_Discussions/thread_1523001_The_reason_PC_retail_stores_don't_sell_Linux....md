### Post by chessnerd on 2010-07-03
Earthpigg,

I can agree with you on some points, and I think you have a fair understanding of human nature, but you must consider this:

A well maintained machine will always do better than a poorly maintained one in the long run.

The most used computer in my house is an 8 year old Gateway desktop. Windows has only been reinstalled once. It has been upgraded from 256 MB RAM to 768 MB RAM. It has had a Wi-Fi card installed. Those are the only major modifications that have been made to it from its original configuration. It runs great. My family occasionally compliments me on my achievement of turning it from a pathetic, sluggish machine to a solid workhorse. Windows on that machine can have an uptime of two weeks or more. It doesn't get malware. It does what it needs to.

The Macs at my high school, however? Even though they were newer, they were slow, buggy, and crashed regularly. They always ran old versions of programs. Everyone at my school hated them. My sister says that she will never own a Mac because of her experiences in high school. The issue was that the school didn't maintain them.

The root of the problem is not that there is some conspiracy going on. It is that people choose not to maintain their computers. They choose not to learn what is going on behind the screen, in fact, some think that the monitor is the computer, not the tower. I would like to see 100 average users use Linux for a few months as part of an experiment. I would bet that, by the end of it, they would suffer the same performance issues.

As for everyone overcharging, I'm thinking about starting a small business at my college next fall that would be made up of CS and IS students. We would charge reasonable prices for PC maintenance. Best Buy's Geek Squad starts at $50 for anything, including simple tune-ups. I would think that we could do that for only $15, maybe less. It's just running updates, defragging, and maybe CCleaner. We could offer file recovery via LiveCD for only $20 or so. I was even thinking of offering free consultations, which would be almost unheard of in the PC maintenance world. There is no need to gouge people, and those who do it need to get some competition.
