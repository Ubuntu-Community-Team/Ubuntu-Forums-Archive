### Post by yc2 on 2012-10-27
Yes, Ubuntu is a great distro and I will continue using it. But I think introducing ads is bad. It will give an advantage to financially strong companies. The real merit of things will have to stand back to financial strength. For example, I think this is why Windows has a much larger market share on PC than has Linux. Microsoft makes lousier programs but they have more marketing resources.

What I also find bad in Windows is how difficult it is to find good programs. The information given about programs is twisted due to marketing reasons. A program that is said to be fully functional freeware can often lack important parts that have to be bought later. Many Windows programs make changes in the computer (system registry etc) that are difficult to survey. This is, I think, often due to commercial reasons. Just simple standalone applications are often simpler to use and are more commonly found in Linux.

If you look for information about Linux programs it is more accurate and it takes much less time to find a good program.

I think many of the advantages we find in Linux are due to its less developed commercialization. 

I don't know either how Debian developers see this. If users take the programs produced and use in their companies, maybe for accounting, that is OK. open source programmers are happy so see their programs come to use. But if you produce a distro and another company takes it, modifies it and makes money from it, I am not sure it feels OK?

Introducing ads breaks with long time practice in GNU/Linux. I think Canonical is playing with fire here.
