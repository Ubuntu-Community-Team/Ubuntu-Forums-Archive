### Post by blade4 on 2012-10-14
The basic difference between 32 bit and 64 bit systems is that [s]32[/s] 64 bit systems can process larger chunks of data at any time . What this translates into ( and I hope should answer your question ) is that 32 bit systems can only support upto 4 gigs of ram while 64 bit systems support larger ram quantities - e.g , the 8 gig laptops today are all shipped with 64 bit oses . In the end , your choice of system should be based on your ram requirement . And yes , the future is 64 bit .... and beyond
