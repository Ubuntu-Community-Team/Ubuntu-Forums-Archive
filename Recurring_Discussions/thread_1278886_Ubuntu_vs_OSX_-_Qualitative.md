### Post by qamelian on 2009-09-30
> **earthpigg said:**
>  I will willingly concede that on hardware specifically designed to run OS X, OS X may run better for certain tasks.
You've got it backward. The hardware is _not_ specifically designed to run OS X. Rather, Apple chooses a limited hardware footprint to support in the OS to minimize potential compatibility problems. The hardware in a Mac (at least, the bits that do the actual job of being a computer) are no more tailored to the job that in any machine that ships with Windows or Linux. The OS is where all the magic takes place. 

So, Apple may actually optimize the OS to take best advantage of the limited hardware scope they select, whereas Ubuntu, or any other typical Linux distro, remains more generic. In theory, you could optimize the heck out of Ubuntu by recompiling key bits like the kernel, various libraries, and even specific applications, etc, but at the end of the process what you have is not necessarily Ubuntu anymore!
