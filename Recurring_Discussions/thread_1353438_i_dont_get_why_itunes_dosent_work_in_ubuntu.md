### Post by Tibuda on 2009-12-12
Short answer: Apple does not want to.

Long answer:
1. Mac kernel is based on FreeBSD, not on Linux. The binary format is different. Even if the binary format were similar, Mac uses its own toolkit not available on Linux.
2. Source code has nothing to do with this. Skype, Nero, VMWare are closed-source, but they provide a Linux version.
3. Windows is a very very big market to ignore.
