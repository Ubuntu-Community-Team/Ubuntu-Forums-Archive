### Post by markbl on 2011-11-02
I find Unity to be a "confused" design. Both gnome-shell and unity basically drop the taskbar in favour of expo like views but gnome-shell does this better because the single super key overloads the launcher, search, expo view, and workspace view, in the one single key press. Unity keeps these separate (launcher, super, super-w, super-s). The unity dash search looks good but is otherwise a waste of screen space, I prefer to see the expo view as gnome-shell does, until I start typing. And who wants that silly unity launcher popping in and out all the time? Gnome-shell is simple, you are either alt-tabbing between apps/windows or you press the meta key (or move mouse to corner hot-spot) to do all app/window/workspace management.

Only after trying out gnome-shell did I "get" the paradigm both are trying to achieve. Gnome-shell made me realise where unity has gone wrong. All is not perfect with gnome-shell though, the notifications/tray system needs work.
