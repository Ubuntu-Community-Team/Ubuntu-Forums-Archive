### Post by HTML-insane on 2010-04-07
> **keljaden said:**
> What Widget(s) are so amazing it is worth using?  I rarely program anymore(thinking about getting into C++ again though). It's less just *what* widgets, but how I use them, that makes KDE so nice for me.

The ability to have multiple folders displayed on the desktop, each in its own little container, is very nice. The ability to have literally as many icons on the Desktop folder and still restrain them in a tiny area is also nice: it makes my desktop feel cleaner. I also have a news widget that displays the latest KDE and Kubuntu news without opening a web-browser. I added another news widget especially for Engadget, but I wanted access to it all the time, so I dragged it from the desktop to the panel very simply.

If I'm working on a college project, I'll do the same with folder-views, dragging folders from Dolphin straight to the panel and creating folder-view widgets that pop-up when you click on them. It means I can have documents in full-screen and have quick access to my other documents and folders without minimizing/closing the window I'm focussed on, and without opening a new Dolphin window and obscuring what I'm currently doing.

And of course, I use twitter, so the desktop widget is nice to have ;)

Most of this stuff is really useful, really simple and yet completely impossible with Gnome and other DEs. That's the kind of workflow I'm used to now, and when using another DE or OS I often feel really crippled.

Then, of course, there's the contrast between one System Settings window with search and the System --> Preferences menu, which consists of opening and closing windows until I *finally* find what it was I was looking for.
