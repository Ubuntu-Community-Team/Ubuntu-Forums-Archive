### Post by echobox on 2011-11-05
I hear a lot of negative things about Unity. I actually quite like it.

I have been with Ubuntu since Feisty Fawn. Unity was a bit of a change, but I got to like it very fast.

There are still some issues there, but lots of good things too.

I'm happy to be using it and anxious to see how it develops. Here are a few thoughts, which probably all have been aired before:

1) The 'dash' bar should go all the way up to the top of the screen. It looks weird to have a maximised program have its buttons over more to the left than the program itself.

2) Clicking on an open window's dash icon should minimise it, like the old days.

3) Theme-wise, why not have the colour of the top panel and the dash panel be controllable via a colour wheel?

4) Maybe make something like ClassicMenu Indicator standard, for those who want to delve into menus and can't always remember the name of a particular program.

5) In the Systems Settings, there should be a button for Advanced users, to tweak things the ordinary users might not want to bother with.

Anyway, I have to admit I love Unity. :)
