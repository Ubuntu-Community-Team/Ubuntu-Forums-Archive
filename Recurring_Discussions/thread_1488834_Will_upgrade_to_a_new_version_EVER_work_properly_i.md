### Post by Roasted on 2010-05-22
> **ssri said:**
> Clean installing... Sounds too much like the windows-way of doing things.

Not really. Clean installs are the logical thing to do, no matter what OS platform you're on.

Partition root and home separately. When you do your clean install, select root, format, select home, mount point = /home, but don't format. When you're done installing, all your personal documents are left there (as long as you didn't check format). Meanwhile, you just have to reinstall the applications you had previously installed. No worries, all the personal settings you had tweaked are saved since you kept your home directory intact.

For the record, I installed Ubuntu 10.04 on my dual core Dell laptop and timed it. 10 minutes and 35 seconds. 

So... no. It's not the Windows way of doing it. In fact, it's quite painless once you get the partitioning structure down initially. Which I encourage EVERYBODY to split root and home up. It makes things much easier...
