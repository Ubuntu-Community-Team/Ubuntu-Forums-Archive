### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-26
> **RogerD said:**
> 
Sorry, but I just can't see that 11.10 is an improvement. In particular, I really miss the old Places menu, which, via bookmarks, gave me a quick way of getting to working documents.

Unity is a very different way of working. The Places menu is quick at getting you to a location, whereas Unity is quick at getting you to a particular file through its search function. Most of the time you want the latter, I'm guessing; and when you want quick access to a location, just click the Home Folder button and get to your bookmarks in the sidebar.

> Also, the appearances preferences options is much more restricted.

Not Ubuntu's fault - it's the latest version of Gnome. In fact, Ubuntu patched the "Wallpaper" program in Gnome so it also has provision for changing the theme. In stock Gnome you can only change the wallpaper - anything more requires installing gnome-tweak-tool (which you can do if you want some extra options, FYI).

> Finally, there's something terrible wrong with the way windows are moving round the screen - very slowly. Under 10.04 Appearances Preferences>Visual Effects, I was able to select 'Normal' which, with my rather under-powered graphics card, worked fine.

Not sure what the problem is, but you could try Unity 2D.

> Why is a distribution that gives you significantly less seen as a step forward?

Ubuntu 11.10 is definitely a step forward, but it involves losing some of your old habits from Gnome 2 and learning to make best use of the new Unity features.
