### Post by Xianath on 2010-07-30
Because, believe it or not, there are people who want to work *with* their computers and not *on* them?

If you get a Mac, you basically take it out of the box and you can get to work. With Windows, even if you have to install it yourself, once you're past the initial nuisance (drivers, updates, A/V), you have a working box -- and even those hurdles are not invasive, you can do something else in the meanwhile. 

With Linux you may very well have to spend a lot of time getting your computer to work, and sometimes you just can't. Then it's the matter of getting your software to work, whether it's one you're used to, or some new one that has to blend in your environment. All this is time better spent elsewhere. That's *especially* true for people who use computers at work. Kids, co-eds and other folks whose time does not directly translate to money (theirs, and someone else's), may -- and probably should -- play with Linux as much as they want to for the benefit of learning. I know I did.
