### Post by sunexplodes on 2009-04-03
Okay, so why is it that KDE4 is still such a pain to use? I'm going to outline a few things that are driving me absolutely crazy, let me know if I'm just plain wrong about them, because I WANT to use KDE, I just can't stand the way it's working right now.

A couple years back, I was a huge fan of KDE 3.5. Endlessly customizable, quick, and could be pretty slick to look at if you used the right engines. 

Now, it's this shaky mess. I'm not sure if Kubuntu is a sub-par implementation or not (I suppose I'll find out soon when I go back to my beloved Arch and try out the new KDEmod), but it's SLOW. Konqueror, which used to be my favorite web browser, takes ages to render pages that Firefox takes care of in a snap (not to mention that it won't save window dimensions and is always small when I open it). 

The plasma desktop is glitchy and sluggish, and the customization options are organized pretty strangely.

Like, why isn't the Plasma appearance settings dialog in the System Settings application? When I first started using KDE4, I was pretty confused to find Style, colour, window decorations, icons, everything else in there, but I've gotta right-click on the desktop for the Plasma settings. Come on. Also, how the hell can I change transparency settings for plasmoids/panels? 

And Amarok. God, what happened to Amarok. Let's take what is essentially the best music library application out there for ANY platform, completely turn the interface upside down (with no option to move it back the way it was), add some hideous, overlapping and oversized buttons, and remove ipod support!

Of course, it's not limited to major stuff. Some minor conveniences have been removed as well. There's no way I can replace a desktop context-menu with an applications menu like I used to. 

I guess I'm pretty out of touch with what modern users like to use, but it's a real shame that the DE that's always been the most customizable has decided to remove nearly all tweakability.



And this is just off the top of my head.
