### Post by GeneralZod on 2008-08-08
Ok, instructions are for KDE 4.1, but I'm fairly sure this feature was also in the 4.0 series.

First, open the Kickoff menu, and navigate to where your desired app is.  Then drag the app near to the desired location on the panel.  You'll notice (unless you got lucky) that the "forbidden" icon appears by the cursor, so it looks like this isn't possible.  However, it actually is: it's just that it's all rather finicky (a known issue, and one that will hopefully be addressed in due course).  If you slowly move close to the edge of an existing widget - the kickoff launcher icon is a good one - the "forbidden" icon should disappear and you'll be able to drop your application launcher onto the panel where you want it.

Alternatively, you can just right-click on the item in Kickoff and choose "Add to Panel", and then move the newly added icon to the desired place (this last stage is only possible with 4.1) :

[http://etotheipiplusone.com/plasma-kickoff-add-icon.png](http://etotheipiplusone.com/plasma-kickoff-add-icon.png)

Here's a screenshot where Gwenview, Okular, KPat and Kopete have all been dragged onto the panel:

[http://etotheipiplusone.com/plasma-panel-icons.png](http://etotheipiplusone.com/plasma-panel-icons.png)

Hope this helps! If you're missing anything else, post here: I follow KDE development very closely (and have in fact recently started hacking on it myself) so I can probably let you know where they are or when they're coming :)
