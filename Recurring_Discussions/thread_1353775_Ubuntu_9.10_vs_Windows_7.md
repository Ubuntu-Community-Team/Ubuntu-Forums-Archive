### Post by Schtoo on 2010-01-20
I am late to this, but anyway...

My limited experiences with both.

Karmic 9.10 and Win 7 Professional (no media centre) both 64 bit.

Install on a clean system. Ubuntu takes Win 7 outside and kicks it's teeth in. Looks better, simpler and much, much faster. I had trouble believing how amateurish Windows looked compared to Ubuntu.

However, since I needed to install restricted extras and a few other doodads like Flash 64 bit by hand, I'll call the time a draw. Still doesn't change the fact that Ubuntu was prettier and nicer to install though.

Boot time. Karmic, 36 seconds from button push to Google homepage. Win 7, 66 seconds from button push to MSN homepage. On an AMD 64 3000+ plugged into an ECS MoBo with 2 Gb whatever ram it takes and a current model 500Gb Seagate HD. Nothing else in the box.

Using the two, no comment. That's personal preference, and I don't hate or outright love either flavour.   

Issues? 

Win 7 has no sound driver at all as yet, and the video driver is the default VGA driver, so I can't get a decent screen resolution. Effectively a deal breaker so I haven't put any more time into than enough to discover that it's not going to work as of right now. An aftermarket video card and sound card would cure this. Other than that, it was all working out of the box as well as it could, and working fast enough that I wasn't complaining. You could see it rummaging around for things though, so after time the poor thing is going to end up a bit of a slug. Windows still can't make the keyboard work properly, nor accept that I want all messages in English, even though I live in Japan. I just accepted it, until I discovered Ubuntu has no such trouble.

Ubuntu has a video driver issue. I got the correct resolution for the onboard video and monitor capability, but there are static lines down either side. The video is SiS based, and I have spent too many hours trying to install the correct driver that I have given up. It's not too bad, and only slightly less annoying than the Win 7 lack of resolution. It's also telling me that the HD has bad sectors and I should replace the HD soon. Problem is Seatools tells me all is fine, and I tend to agree with that. According to Ubuntu, the HD is losing a sector a day. Very freaking annoying.

I guess a video card would solve both OS video issues. 

Ubuntu cannot make our Canon iP2500 printer work on a 64 bit OS and I am not savvy enough to make the 32 bit driver work. Windows shouldn't have the same issue, but who knows?

Joys? 

Win 7 is not that bad. I am not a Win hater, I save that emotion for Macs (fully justified, yes I have been inflicted with one for long enough to know what they really are...). If I can stuff a little more HP in the box (MoBo/CPU/RAM/Video) then Win 7 might be pretty handy. I can certainly see why folks were saying it is much lighter than Vista, because on our marginal machine, it does run slick. 

Ubuntu makes the slickness of Win 7 look like glob of slime. Everything happens instantly, it has not ever gotten bogged down no matter what I have done to it. After fooling around making it work how I need it to work, it's the default OS now and for the foreseeable future.


A few caveats there though. I was given a copy of Win 7 64bit to try out. A real, registrable one, so money is not the issue (but if I had paid for it, heads would be rolling by now). I also have a copy of Win 7 32 bit to see if that will cure the video/sound issue. Should also free up some RAM, at the sacrifice of outright speed. 

The box itself is new case, new DVD multi-thingy (LG) and new HDD. NOS MoBo, CPU and RAM, and pre-assembled. 

I consider myself very fortunate that I have both operating systems to use as I need to, and because of that I can exploit the strengths of both (when they are both on the top of their game) and avoid any weaknesses. 

If Ubuntu was a little less geeky (command line?) and had adequate driver support from manufacturers and everything worked out of the box (for a fee even) then it might be a different story. Until something like a paid for Linux with lotsa support arrives, I can't see it being a Windows beater. 

Even though Karmic does beat Win7 in my minimal experience by being a smaller collection of gripes and grumbles...

FWIW, I also run Ubuntu on this here laptop, and am spending the next few nights taking it to 9.10 for 8.04. Just got sick of 8.04 rummaging around in it's guts trying to work out what to do. 

(Oh yeah, something telling me that the double click was acknowledged would be nice rather than leaving me hanging for a few seconds. Windows still wins that one by a mile!).

HTH, HAND. ;)
