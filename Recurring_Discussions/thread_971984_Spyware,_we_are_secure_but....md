Cookies are managed by the browser. Whether they are spyware or not is up to debate and also dependent on how the website that places the cookies uses them. But the major difference between browser cookies and traditional spyware is this: cookies can be easily cleared through the browser options. Spyware is usually quite resistant to easy removal.

Is it possible for spyware to work in Linux? Sure. But how would it get installed? By tricking the user? If the user is susceptible to social engineering, then, of course. That we cannot help from a software standpoint.
