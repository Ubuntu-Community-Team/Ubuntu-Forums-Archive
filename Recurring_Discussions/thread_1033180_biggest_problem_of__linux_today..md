### Post by adamlau on 2009-01-07
I always recommend Xfce over KDE, or GNOME. Unless you plan on running a somewhat minimal install, or distro, anyone running a properly [nLite](http://www.nliteos.com/)d 2K/XP/Vista box will feel weighed down with either GNOME, or KDE. That was the biggest problem I faced when I recommend Linux to prospective converts, that the GNOM/KDE UI felt (to them) less responsive than their Windows builds. This issue is particularly acute when facing a user coming over from NT in that it is terribly difficult to recommend a simple, full featured distro that feels as fast as NT. Also, one of the great (and oft overlooked) benefits of moving to Xfce is the discovery (or rediscovery) of lightweight, GTK+ only apps of which in many cases, best their GNOME/KDE lib-dependant counterparts. LXDE? It is coming along and rather responsive, but has locked up on me across multiple distros ten too many times for me to recommend to anyone but devs and testers right now...
