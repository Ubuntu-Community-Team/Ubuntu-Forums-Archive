### Post by samjh on 2010-06-21
> **linuxyogi said:**
> Hi, 

Wine Compromises Privacy  ........... Why ?

1) A general nature of most of windows programs is that they will try to access the internet as soon as they sense a network connection. Now, mind you this is without the knowledge of the user. But if you study Linux apps, they hardly access the internet without the user's knowledge unless configured to do so.Wrong.  Most Windows programs will not try to "access the internet as soon as they sense a network connection".  Some programs are configured to check for updates from the software developer's server, but you can usually turn off the feature, if you wish.  It is no more a compromise of privacy than your Linux distro doing an automatic check for updated packages from the repository.

> **linuxyogi said:**
> 2)Viruses and Spywares : I love to play games (I don't have windows installed) but fear of viruses & spywares is stopping me from installing wine. This is not an issue in Wine unless you cause the problem by running virus-infected software via Wine.  In fact, even on Windows, you should never be infected with viruses or spyware unless you do something that compromises security, such as running software created or distributed by untrustworthy entities.  Most security issues on Windows are caused by user negligence or ignorance, just as it is with Unix or Linux systems.

### Post by Paqman on 2010-06-21
> **linuxyogi said:**
> 
1) A general nature of most of windows programs is that they will try to access the internet as soon as they sense a network connection.


Eh? Apps which are supposed to have a connection might, but that's exactly the same on Linux.

> 
2)Viruses and Spywares : I love to play games (I don't have windows installed) but fear of viruses & spywares is stopping me from installing wine. 


You won't get viruses in Wine, it's not an operating system, and has been designed to minimise the risk. People have posted technical demonstrations of deliberately running malware in Wine on here before, at worst you'll mess up Wine's virtual C drive in your home folder. The virus won't be able to do anything really nasty to your Linux system unless it's designed to exploit a flaw in Linux as well, which is pretty unlikely IMO.
