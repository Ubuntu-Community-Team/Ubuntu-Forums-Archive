### Post by Dragonbite on 2010-08-23
[LIST]
[*]Easier to setup, maintain and use than Windows.  Almost too easy.  I get disappointed when I do an update and nothing is there! ;)
[*]Works on older hardware much better.  My P4 runs Ubuntu beautifully, while Windows 7 is a draaaaag.  Plus things like Spider Solitaire works reliably with Ubuntu, but on Windows 7 it "requires" Aero, which my graphics card doesn't handle so I'm SOL!
[*]OS and Application are (usually) free ($).  That has also allowed me to provide things like Stopmotion animation to my son, or mapping the stars with Stellarium, or try LTSP and see if that would work for the family.
[*]Unencumbered by licenses and restrictions.  I was able to try LTSP for a while and not have to worry about paying for each seat license, or try to test it out in 120 day trial period. Plus being able to install-reinstall over and over and never need an activation code is spoiling!
[*]Community.):P
[*]Flexibility/customizability.  My laptop is smaller and Gnome-shell works on the smaller screens better while I can take advantage of the larger screen of my desktop.
[*]Great for learning. Learning about config files, how things work, codecs and licensing at the least.
[/LIST]

When I started, I liked the idea of going from Windows to Mac but there was no way I could afford a new machine, let alone the software to bring it close to "on par" with what I had in my Windows system (mind you, this was early 2000's).
