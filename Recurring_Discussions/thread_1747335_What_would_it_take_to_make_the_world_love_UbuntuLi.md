### Post by aysiu on 2011-05-03
It's not simply a matter of advertising. Can you imagine Google advertising Android without any Android-optimized and preloaded phones for people to buy? No, don't actually buy an Android phone... just buy some other phone and install Android on it yourself and troubleshoot hardware compatibility issues yourself. Wouldn't have happened. No one except Linux geeks would have had Android phones.

You can't just advertise an operating system. People have to have easy ways to buy products with that OS preinstalled and preconfigured from a vendor they trust.

When Microsoft advertises Windows 7, they know people can just go to the nearest Best Buy and buy just about any computer with Windows 7 preloaded on it. If you advertise Ubuntu, where are people going to go to get it?

In terms of partial upgrades not working... well, if you've ever done tech support for Windows, you know things aren't pretty there... or Mac support. Things can always go wrong and not be easy to fix, and that's independent of operating system. Here's what I've generally found in terms of things going wrong and people's reactions to it:

**Something goes wrong in Windows**: "I hate computers. This is so frustrating. How can I get this fixed? Argh!!!!"

**Something goes wrong with Mac**: "How annoying. Oh well. I still love my Mac!"

**Something goes wrong with Linux**: "See? This is why Linux will never succeed. It's only for geeks. I'm going back to Windows. At least that works!"
