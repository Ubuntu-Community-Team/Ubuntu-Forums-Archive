### Post by digger95 on 2008-04-12
For what it's worth, the acpi problems I experienced myself were in the Linux kernel and not any particular distro.  Many acpi issues were fixed in kernel 2.6.23.1 and later.   Once I upgraded my kernel all acpi functions have worked perfectly in both Ubuntu and Slackware.  I used to have to turn acpi=off just to get Ubuntu to even install.  Now I can use acpi and all functions work fine (suspend, hibernate, system fans, etc).  Not saying everyone should run out and upgrade, that's just what worked for me.  I wouldn't necessarily blame Ubuntu for these issues though.
