### Post by vexorian on 2012-08-10
When you start using unity seriously. I am starting to notice something that can get annoying accumulatively. Specially when using firefox as I need to take the mouse to the top to change tabs.

The global menu bar also works as a global title bar. Which means that when the mouse stays over it, it changes to menu bar mode. This serves well in making the interface use less space. But I am starting to think that the menu bar "blinking" instantly whenever the mouse cursor is over it can become literally a head ache. It is becoming a point of stress to me. I have to be constantly careful not to make the menu bar trigger...

What if the menu bar had delays?
- 0.X seconds delay before menu bar starts appearing. This way if the mouse just got there accidentally as part of a movement to another area, nothing happens.
- 1 second delay before hiding the menu bar again. Maybe the mouse cursor is moving erratically, or maybe I exited accidentally and temporarily.

I think I read in some game design site that there needs to be a delay between control and reaction because that is the way the human brain expects things to work. Completely instant reaction to input is counter-intuitive.
