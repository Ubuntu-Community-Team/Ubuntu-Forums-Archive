### Post by GothPanda on 2008-04-02
I, personally, think it's going to be a while before linux gains that much of the market share.  Is Microsoft going to die?  Certainly.  Unfortunately, I think Mac may over take them for a while...

The thing is, Mac is popularized as something easy, virus-free and stable.  And that's what people want.  Something that's easy to use, and stinking works.

Which leads me to believe that it'll be Macintosh that overtakes the Home market, while Linux is still used by faithful, unwilling to use anything else (and for good reason).

The problem with Linux in general is that it's too difficult for the average consumer to use.  Compile from source?  That just sounds scary.  Even though at best it's only about two or three commands.

Face it, if you have to open a terminal to get hardware working, it's too scary for the average consumer.

Business, Linux may lead the way, and that will work to our advantage, but we're going to need some time to perfect all of the crap currently present in the Open Source Universe first.

We need some set standards for package distribution and installation.  And, we need to figure out a simple way to handle dependencies.  APT-GET is an amazing system, but it's just a step in the right direction.
