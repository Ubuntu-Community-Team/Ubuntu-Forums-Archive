My teacher said this today. We were talking about monopolies. A few people in the class started bashing Apple and calling Mac so hard to use. I look at this in an optimistic point of view.

If the people that want to use Windows use it, let them. If you know how to handle Windows and want to support a major company, do it.

Yes, there are slight bugs in Ubuntu, but seriously, Ubuntu gets so much better every update, as does Apple. What does Microsoft do? Change a theme, make it slower, and ship it out!

In my opinion, we are not the problem, they are. They are the people who never change. They are the people who care more about today than tomorrow. They are the people who would vote for the same president every election if they had the chance. They are the same people who don't explore possibilities.

Don't get me wrong. I know tons of people who love Windows, but to say that it is the best because everyone uses it is just wrong. Especially when nobody else in the room has even heard of Ubuntu or even Linux.

### Post by tdrusk on 2008-05-06
I fixed the virus part.

> In the English language, the standard plural of virus is viruses. This is the most frequently occurring form of the plural, and refers to both a biological virus and a computer virus.

The less frequent variations viri and virii are virtually unknown in edited prose, and no major dictionary recognizes them as alternative forms. Their occurrence can be variously attributed to hypercorrection formed by analogy to Latin plurals such as alumni or false analogy to Latin plurals such as radii; idiosyncratic use as jargon among a group, such as computer hackers; and deliberate word play, such as on BBSs (see, e.g.: leet).

To complicate matters further, viri is already used in Latin as the plural of vir, meaning "man" (thus making viri mean "men").[1]
