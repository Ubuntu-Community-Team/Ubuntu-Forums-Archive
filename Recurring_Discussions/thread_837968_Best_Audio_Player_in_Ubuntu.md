### Post by suprfish on 2008-06-23
Rhythm Box is overlooked a lot, I guess because it doesn't have all as many features as other players. But, it does do all the essential stuff and has some neat plugins as well, all with a really small footprint.

One of the things I like about amaroK is the ability to have keyboard commands to skip/play/pause your music. Rhythm Box does not have this ability in the application itself but if you have those [_common macro buttons_]("http://pro.corbis.com/images/42-15727461.jpg?size=572&uid={C738A18B-9715-4433-BF9B-DD2DC5C82C82}") on your keyboard (such as internet, mail, ...) GNOME will have probably configured the music-related ones to work with Rhythm Box. You can check by going to System > Prefs > Keyboard Shortcuts and System > Prefs > Pref Apps.

With this feature, I like Rhythm Box more than any other music player. :guitar:

### Post by imwithid on 2009-05-30
I'm a big fan of Winamp. I categorize my own music and so don't need iTunes clones. There are times when I have a folder that's in queue for sorting with Winamp, I can go through 300 songs in a matter of minutes finding those songs I don't want from the ones I wish to keep as I can sample them using a keybaord (next song) mouse mix while adjusting volume with the scroll and scanning.

So far, I'm not a fan of Amarok, Banshee and Rythmbox (too busy - iTunes in their feel). So for my least favourite is XMMS2. It's small but too simple. The default Totem is tolerable but only because it's already installed so it's more of a convenience feature.

So far Audacious is the winner for me. It's almost identical to Winamp and has all the features and shortcuts of Winamp (the response time relative to Winamp is a bit slow but I can't complain that much as it's measureable in ~+7ms).

I've tried others media players (VLC and MPlayer) and can see that they're not exactly meant for audio only playback.

I haven't tried Songbird or the other one or two mentioned. Audacious +1
