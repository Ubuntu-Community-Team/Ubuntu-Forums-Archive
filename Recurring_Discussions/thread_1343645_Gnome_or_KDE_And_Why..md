### Post by ZankerH on 2009-12-02
GNOME, because KDE isn't properly integrated into ubuntu.

KDE, because it is properly integrated on Debian and Mandriva.

KDEmod, because it's the only properly developed and easily configurable full DE on Arch.

LXDE and xfce, because I need a lightweight gui on my netbook.

Awesome WM, because I need a minimal x environment on my server that I can ssh into over a 512kbps connection.

Why do these kind of threads always assume everyone uses a single DE/WM that suits all of their needs?

Voted gnome because I spent 48% of my up time in it during the last month according to my statistics. KDE, KDEmod, LXDE, xfce and awesome saw 3%, 25% 17%, 2% and 6% each, respectively.
