### Post by Nixarter on 2012-09-01
Honestly it is all BS.  Courts have thrown out the lawsuit already, around the world.  Even Japan did it... JAPAN.  If Japan says Samsung, a KOREAN company didn't infringe, then you can rest assured that they surely did NOT infringe.  The moronic US judge that presided over the case threw out evidence showing that Samsung clearly did not infringe... so of course they rules that the did, given what evidence was available to them.

This is one point of law that is really messed up in the US system (and the reason why I decided not to pursue Law as a career, once I found out).  When this country was founded, our constitution said how the country should be set up... but didn't set rights.  Some said we would slip back into the old UK style of ridiculous laws, so they amended the Constitution with the Bill of Rights, so that they shall not be infringed.  Save for some supreme court justices adding in stuff that simply isn't there, it works OK.

Unfortunately, there was no such idea with the federal rules of evidence.  No evidence that does not comply with the rules of evidence can be used in court.  Seems fair, right?  In fact, it is.  The problem is that they never thought to add one critically important bit... judges are not forced to admit all evidence that can exonerate the defendant.  Judges are free to exclude whatever they want.  This is why there are so many people in prison who everyone knows is innocent... but since judges are often lazy and arrogant, biased, or what-have-you, they just play with the system to suit their needs.  Every time they try to amend the rules to add such a clause, they get beaten down by power-hungry judges.

There are MANY, MANY cases where they take out exceptions to laws ("this law only applies except in this situation..."), literally deleting them before giving the laws to the jurors as if the text was the entire law.  Without knowledge that the accused was not guilty because of an explicit exception to the law, they have no choice but to rule guilty.  Unfortunately to Samsung, the same thing happened here.

If our rights are not protected  from the very beginning, they have a good chance to last for a while.  If they are not explicitly mentioned (like our right to bear arms, for example) those rights are likely to get taken away quickly... and it is practically impossible to get them added in after the fact.

That is the only reason why samsung lost.  It is a BS ruling, and it will cost a legitimate company over BILLION dollars.
