### Post by Macfunky on 2011-05-30
This is not a bashing thread of any sort. I see what every environment has to offer and I love the freedom of choice. That is, in fact, why I am posting this thread. At the moment I see a huge surge in the Linux world. A lot of software projects are making leaps and bounds, the same can be said for desktop environments. I love Unity and I think Gnome 3 is great too. Upon seeing a video of elementary OS earlier on today, it got me thinking, what's going to be the next big Linux os, i.e. the one that people tell novices to try out as the easiest Linux distro? Unity is looking great and Gnome 3 has come along nicely. Both of these will, as KDE had to, sort out a few problems, but they are really very good environments if you allow yourself to give them the chance. I hate this bashing of everything new. This is an open source environment we use and we ALL have the choice to use what we want. When I saw the video of Elementary OS doing it's own thing, it got me thinking. It really is in its very early stages but I see where it's going and I think eventually it could become something very interesting for newcomers. Prejudices aside, in 2 years to come, which distro do you see yourself recommending to newcomers if thrends continue the way they are going?

Edit: I understand the Linux kernel is being used in all sorts of places and on all sorts of hardware at the moment. I see it becoming more popular in places other than the desktop, if that has not already happened, but for the purposes of my question I was referring to Linux on the desktop :)
