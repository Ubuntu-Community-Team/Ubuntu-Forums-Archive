### Post by tehcavil on 2011-10-26
I think my dad just might be the ultimate windows zealot.

A few months ago I started experimenting with Linux as I was building my own computer and didn't want to spend the money on windows (especially after I had a terrible experience with Vista) So I opted for Linux. After reading a few forums I saw the name Ubuntu come up the most so I tried it. I've tinkered with programming in the past, (I made a version of Tetris with LOGO when I was 9 at computer camp - yes, the one baby programming language with the turtles), it was always on windows. Plus until recently I was a hardcore gamer and needed windows to play all the games.

Liking it thus far, I accidentally broke my computer when upgrading from 11.04 to 11.10, but I fixed it by using a mint live CD gparted to wipe my computer and install Mint 11 (which is basically ubuntu + gnome 2 anyway, right?).

Anyway, I installed mint to dual boot with vista on my brother's computer too (it was mine but I gave it to him after I went to college). My dad uses that computer sometimes too as his broke several years ago and he uses it to browse the web or check email. This is when the complaints started... He said, "hey why is that screen [grub] coming up? you installed Linux and now its the 'dominant system' over windows. I don't care what you say, that's not a windows screen!". So I switched grub so that windows is the default.

But he still didn't like it. I bought an Asus laptop to use and installed mint on that too (dual boot with win7 this time) and he said "why are you using this crappy bulls**t Linux? Why use it when you already have windows? it's not even standardized! Down with Linux!". Well, that is the first time I have actually heard anyone say the phrase 'down with Linux' since Linux isn't a government or corporation it's hard to fathom how that would be possible. Down with the community? :(

Anyway I've had several more interesting conversations with my good ol dad in which he describes Linux users as "pirates". He also says free software is "Communist".

I'm not sure how I can change his mind about these sorts of things.

### Post by matt_symes on 2011-10-26
> Anyway, I installed mint to dual boot with vista on my brother's computer too (it was mine but I gave it to him after I went to college).

What does your brother think of Linux ? If he does not like it then just remove it. No point in forcing it down their throats.

If he does like it, as it's predominantly his computer, then set the grub menu timeout to zero and update grub. Ensure it boots into Windows first.

Show your brother how to display the grub menu by pressing the shift key at PC boot time. This will enable him to boot into Linux when he wants to.

This way you will keep both your dad and brother happy.
