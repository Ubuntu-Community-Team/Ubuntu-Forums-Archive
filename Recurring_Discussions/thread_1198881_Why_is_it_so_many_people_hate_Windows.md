### Post by Kazade on 2009-06-28
I don't *hate* Windows, I think that's a little strong. This is my big cause of anger towards it and Microsoft...

Go into any high street PC store, find a laptop or desktop not running Windows, you won't (even finding a non-Windows netbook is hard now). Ask any sales assistant if something will run on Linux, or even if they know what Linux is, chances are they won't know. Ask any friend/relative who hasn't been formally introduced to Linux if they know of any alternative to Windows excluding Mac. They won't know. And here's the bit that makes me angry, NOBODY seems to realize how wrong this is. Nobody looks around at all the labels next to the PCs that say "Operating System: Windows" and think, "hang on, why is there only one?" If this situation occurred in ANY OTHER industry, there would be uproar, governments would get involved... it just wouldn't happen.

Then you need to ask yourself the question, in fact, ask a friend or relative. How do you think that happened? Why is there only Windows? I've done this, and there are 2 common replies:

 * Windows is the only operating system for PCs.
 * It's the best one, that's why it's so common.

Both arguments are easily destroyed when you ask them what other operating systems there are, and they don't know, and you explain there are others. This is the point I usually explain that Microsoft is a convicted monopolist, have been endlessly caught for underhand business tactics, make it damn near impossible for any vendor to switch away if they want their profits to keep coming in. Then it sinks in, then they start asking questions, until they walk out the door and remember, it doesn't matter as long as they can play WoW and surf the net.

I don't hate Windows, it's an OK OS, I think Windows 7 should be pretty good. What I hate is the sheer unfairness of it all. I hate the fact that governments haven't stepped in like they would if there was a single oil company, or a single car manufacturer. This monopoly is good for no-one except Microsoft.

Then of course on the more trivial level, there is the DRM (which treats me like a criminal), Genuine Advantage (which treats me like a criminal), lax security in the name of backwards compatibility, the inability to remove Windows Mail/IE/etc. (whose PC is it?), the endless bugs with ridiculously obscure error messages and numbers (you'll know what I mean if you've worked with SQL Server or AD), the [vendor lock-in]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vendor_lock-in#Microsoft"), the lack of customization options, the appalling command prompt (I know that powershell fixes this somewhat), the decision to not provide DX10+ on XP (force upgrading). I could go on but you get the point.

To paraphrase Star Wars, Ubuntu is our last, best hope - 20% market share would make me happy, that's all it will take to bring balance to the desktop.

P.S. I personally think Ubuntu 10.10 will be the game changer- KMS, new look, Pulseaudio should finally be solid, Nouveau, Gallium3D, ATI drivers will have made real progress, Wine's test suite (hopefully) should be passing for everyone, meaning massive progress without regressions. XInput2/multi-pointer X will have been out for ages. DeviceKit, PackageKit, Gnome 3, client-side GTK windows. Then there are 18 months of improvements to the kernel. Ubuntu Netbook Remix should be making inroads on netbooks. 

All this stuff on the horizon, can't wait :D
