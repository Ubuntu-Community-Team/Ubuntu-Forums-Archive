### Post by LaRoza on 2008-05-09
> **hotchkikr said:**
> Just wondering how OSX and Ubuntu compare side by side, I want an apple computer and just would like to know

If you don't need any specific applications (like those video/music apps that are popular on Macs) you will most likely be disappointed.

OS X is pretty nice, but it is a Linux user's nightmare. You either take it or leave it, there is very little to make your own.

On the other hand, it is very GUI oriented.

You can check out some screenshots of OS X and look at DreamLinux or other OS X like Linux distros to get a feel for certain aspects.

The hardware will be expensive, so wiping OS X is normally something that I wouldn't suggest. The hardware is nothing special. It is only there to run OS X, otherwise it is just an overpriced Intel based PC. Perhaps you can find a store or someone you know where you can try one out?

### Post by CSMatt on 2008-05-09
From what I understand, Mac OS X is more like BSD than GNU+Linux.  Mac OS X is based on Darwin, which in turn is based on NeXTSTEP and FreeBSD.  That being said, it seems to have more in common with Windows than with most UNIX-like systems (at least the ones that I know of anyway).

Since the Intel transition, there doesn't seem to be much of a point to owning an Apple machine from a hardware standpoint.  The only Apple-specific hardware in Apple machines is the signed TPM.  Otherwise, Apple is now nothing more than a PC OEM that makes their own software.
