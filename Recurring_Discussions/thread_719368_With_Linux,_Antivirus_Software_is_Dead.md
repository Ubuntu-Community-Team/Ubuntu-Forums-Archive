### Post by aysiu on 2008-03-09
I disagree.

It makes about as much sense to say Ubuntu is the best anti-virus as to say living in the rich suburbs of America is the best armor for a US soldier in Iraq to wear. You are no longer a US soldier in Iraq if you're in the rich suburbs of America.

Likewise, you are not using anti-virus if you're using Ubuntu--you're using a completely different operating system. This marketing ploy is just a slightly subtler form of "Hey, Windows users--Ubuntu isn't a completely different operating system; it's just Windows-without-problems," which will just lead to more "Ubuntu sucks. I'm going back to Windows!" threads in the Ubuntu Testimonials and Experiences subforum here.

No thanks.

The best anti-virus for Windows users is running as a limited (instead of administrative) user.
