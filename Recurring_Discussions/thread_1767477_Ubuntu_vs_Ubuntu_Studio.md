### Post by theorganloft on 2011-06-06
I just installed US 11.04 X86. The next new machine will get the 64bit version. Although my machine may support it, I want to use a new platform for that. 

I am impressed.  It has come a very long way since I have started using it 4 years ago. My older US machine runs 24\7 with Jack, Ardour, Hydrogen, ZynAddSubFX, and my Midi controllers on. Excellent response and no recorded latency. I push it hard while playing live.  I split the midi keyboards, add effects, and switch instruments.  

I only had one problem with the new 11.04 US.  The machine would hang during boot at the splash screen.  I use a dual port NVidia Card and for some reason the splash screen would hang after I installed the special drivers. I did not bother to troubleshoot. 
Disabling the splash screen fixed the problem. I don't need it anyway.

I will only use this new machine for audio and audio recording so I did not installed any video editing software. 

Applications for audio have vastly improved.  Two of my favorites are MIXXX (a DJ mixer application) and Audacious (a media player. 

I added oother applications that I like to use for editing.  I added Audacity (excellent audio editing program) and EasyTAG (for fixing tags on Mp3 and others)

MiXXX has the new AutoDJ feature and I think it is great. The library and playlist are also vastly improved. 

I use Audacious as a compliment to MIXXX (repeat play and shuffle) and as an audio tool for EasyTag.  

I have two US systems where I use one to drive Jack and the DAW's. My Midi controllers are connected to System one. The other is for audio recording and music play. 

I use a mixing board, mics, and other sound equipment between them.  :guitar:
