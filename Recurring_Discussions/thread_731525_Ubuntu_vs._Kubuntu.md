### Post by perspectoff on 2008-10-22
Converts from M$ Windows will more readily adapt to Kubuntu.

Kubuntu is more customizable. The KDE applications included with Kubuntu are generally more powerful than the applications included with the Gnome-based distribution.

Gnome applications are simpler, less customizable. Gnome seems oriented towards old Apple Macintosh users.

LinuxMCE (media Center Edition) uses Kubuntu as its base OS because there are more powerful apps integrated into KDE.

I have used Debian, Xubuntu, Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Mythbuntu, LinuxMCE for the past 3 years.

Now I only use Kubuntu (and LinuxMCE).

I have converted 11 computers and die-hard Windows users to Kubuntu, 5 in a corporate environment.

They would not accept the Gnome Ubuntu desktop, but can barely tell the difference with Kubuntu (with Open Office and Firefox installed).
