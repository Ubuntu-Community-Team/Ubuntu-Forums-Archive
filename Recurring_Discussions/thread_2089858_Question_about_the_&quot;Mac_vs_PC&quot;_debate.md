The debate has always been about the "Mac experience" vs the Windows OS. that is why the argument has never really made much sense . People have often compared apples and oranges. The Mac is a closed system, one company that controlled everything from hardware to OS. The old arguments that Macs "just work" have never made sense to me, of course they just work, if you control the hardware and the OS things had better just work, no real mystery. Windows have always had issues becuase they do not control the entire process computer vendors do, so obviously there will be times when things do not behave as expected. *nix is no different, if you use the "right hardware" things just work, use not well supported hardware and you are going to have issues. 

If you want to debate the quality of apple vs <pick your favorite hardware> go for it, that makes snese. if you want to debate the usability of OSX vs WindowsXP/7/8 vs <pick your favorite flavor of *nix> go for it. if you want to debate the Mac experience and how easy it is compared to some piece of crap no-name computer preloaded with useless software it makes no sense at all.
