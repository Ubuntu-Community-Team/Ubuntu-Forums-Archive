### Post by aysiu on 2008-07-21
I think you'll be amazed to find out a lot of Linux users have full-time jobs, committed relationships, non-computer-related hobbies, and *still* manage to print without writing their own drivers (I've never written a driver in my life and wouldn't have the foggiest idea where to begin in that process). It's called buying a Linux-compatible printer. Pure and simple. That's it. Mac OS X suffers from a very similar problem, since it also uses CUPS for printing (it was one of the reasons my wife and I couldn't successfully migrate her parents to Mac from Windows - they had a Canon printer that wasn't Mac-compatible).

In your dream world, you're a busy person with a life, and Linux users have the exact same printer as you and are unable to print until they learn how to reverse engineer a closed driver.

In real life, you have a Linux-unfriendly printer and people who are able to print in Linux have Linux-friendly printers.

You can also create your own Launcher shortcut.

```
sudo gedit /usr/share/applications/dmenu.desktop
```

And then copy paste the code below in to that shortcut

```

[Desktop Entry]
Encoding=UTF-8
Name=Dmenu
Comment=DMenu
Exec=dmenu
Icon=/full/path/to/icon.name
StartupNotify=true
Terminal=false
Type=Application
Categories=Applications;Network
```

Then save that gedit
There will now be a shortcut in your Applications>Internet Menu
If you are hunting for Icons to get creative - they are normally found in the
/usr/share/icons/ area.
