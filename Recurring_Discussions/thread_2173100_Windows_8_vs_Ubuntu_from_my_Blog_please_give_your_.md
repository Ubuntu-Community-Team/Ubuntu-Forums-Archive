### Post by buzzingrobot on 2013-09-08
I did not know Ubuntu was working on Windows 8. :D

I don't see my choice of software as indicating "support".  It's a product.  I use what I like.

Touching, swiping, etc., makes sense on small devices with small displays that host tools with limited functionality.  Menus, for example, aren't needed if the tool only offers a few functions.

Desktop software that delivers the capabilities people buy 24-, 27-, and 30-inch monitors to exploit, often offers hundreds of inter-layered functionalities.  Touch/swipe is not capable of responding to that demand. (Plus, who's going to keep their arm stretched out all day poking at Photoshop, for example?)

MS should have kept the tablet interface on small devices, and the desktop interface on things that, well, need to sit on a desk.

They did not.  That's the reason for the rejection of Win8, not some fuddy duddy resistance to change.
