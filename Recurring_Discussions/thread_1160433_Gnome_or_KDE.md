### Post by starcannon on 2009-05-16
I used KDE for quite a long time before I tried Ubuntu; when I downloaded Ubuntu I went with the "flagship" cd, and it had/has Gnome as its default desktop manager. To be honest I didn't think I'd like or keep Gnome, I actually figured I'd install KDE, but I gave it an honest month, and just seemed to flow with the Gnome way of doing things better (once I had my feet wet), and so, I never bothered installing KDE. I've grown so accustomed to Gnome now, that when I finally downloaded Kubuntu, I was a bit lost in the menu's. I just got outta touch with KDE.

Ultimately, I think both desktop managers are excellent, and each has its strong suites and its weak ones. Go with the one that makes YOU the most efficient, thats what I did.
