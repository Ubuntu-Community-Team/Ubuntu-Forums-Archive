### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-09-18
It's called Ultimate Edition. This is why we have distros and offshoots- just find the one that's right for you, or use Reconstructor or Remastersys to make your own remix DVD and share it. But really, there are projects that already do this, and it's a bad idea to confuse users with so many editions of the same OS. Kubuntu/Xubuntu/Ubuntu is enough, methinks. People can install their favorite software easily, and with 10.10 the codecs and drivers and updates can be added by a simple checkbox during installation.

So yeah, I don't think it's at all necessary, and it screws with optimal visibility of products on the site.

Also, it's just one more thing you're asking Canonical to pay for bandwidth on their server for. Just not a good idea, while I understand your desire for it.

The simple fact is, anyone who would want that probably knows how to remix it themselves or can easily find a distro like that.
