### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-12-11
Two things drove my from Ubuntu to Xubuntu:

First was the bitter ending of my brief love affair with LXDE, which is all kindsa buggy on Ubuntu (and on every Ubuntu derivative I tried). But just as much:

I never upgrade right away. I always wait a few weeks and read the forums in advance of upgrading. With Karmic it seemed that there were more than the usual number of issues, many relating to PulseAudio.

I was going to look at Xubuntu anyway after the disappointment of LXDE, but then I read that **[COLOR="Blue"]Xubuntu[/COLOR]** doesn't ship with PulseAudio (nor Mono, for those to whom that might matter), and that was enough to convince me to try it.

**[COLOR="Red"]It rawks![/COLOR]** I have had *none* of the issues of Karmic Ubuntu users I read about in UF in the first weeks after Karmic was released. And it's faster on an old Dell with a scant 512 megs of RAM than even Crunchbang is on a newer and more powerful laptop!

LXDE works great on Debian Lenny (awesome) by the way, so I'm assuming (noobishly, perhaps) that my issues with LXDE are 'buntu-specific.

-Robin
