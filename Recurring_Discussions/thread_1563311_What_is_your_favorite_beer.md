### Post by handy on 2010-08-31
Green Man, a brewery situated in Dunedin, on the south east coast of the South Island of New Zealand, brews by far the best beers I have ever tasted.

Their beers are all brewed from organic ingredients. The subtle blend of flavours in their beers is beyond any other (& I have tried a few over the decades).

My favourite was called "Strong" it was a dark beer & at over 7% alcohol it was amazing that it could taste so good. 

Every other beer I have ever tasted that gets up to 7% & beyond suffers in the flavour department. This one didn't at all, far from it.

I've never seen any of the Green Man beers in Oz, but I'm sure there are specialty shops that would stock it in Sydney somewhere.

My usual favourite is Coopers Sparkling Ale. It is brewed in the bottle, so it misses out on all the chemicals that the other types of beers have incorporated into them. It is cloudy, has sediment in the bottom of the bottle like home brew, which is basically what it is. It is 5.8% alcohol, which means you don't have to drink as many of them which helps as they are more expensive than our run of the mill Tooheys & VB beers.
