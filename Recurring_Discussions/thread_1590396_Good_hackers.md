### Post by Mike'sHardLinux on 2010-10-07
Originally, the word hacker was not a bad thing. A hacker is someone who builds, modifies, or takes things apart to learn about them. A programmer is a hacker. Someone who works on car engines could be considered a hacker. The media has skewed the meaning of this word, and now, everybody thinks a hacker is someone who tries to destroy or steal.

### Post by Ahava591 on 2010-10-10
Learn to write, (and read,) whatever language is your native language. If English is your native language, then work on your grammar and orthography. 
-Stop writing, "becuz," and "interesting also... lolz, uhhh... since the first two of my questions are answered, can someone answer the third one?..." 

ADD or not, there's no reason for this. It's annoying and boring; if you can't communicate clearly in writing then learn how to do so. 

You're going to be reading through a lot of code and comments. You're going to be writing a lot of code and comments to that code; if not, especially if you don't comment your code well, nobody is going to bother maintaining or improving it.

Be physically active. I don't recommend an organized sport like basketball or football, there's no point to them; but get out and around. Don't forget your other interests. Read books and get into hobbies. It doesn't matter if you're a car mechanic or a teacher or a carpet cleaner; if you're able to learn, if you're able to take suggestions, to take jokes, to ask clear, concise questions then it's not going to matter what your "real world," job is.

It doesn't matter how old you are or what ethnic group you're from. It doesn't matter to me whether or not you are a native English speaker; what matters to me, what I really hate, is a person who is not respectful enough to do whatever they do to the best of their ability. If you don't speak a language fluently, don't worry about it; we're going to try to work with you no matter where you are from, be patient; but do not half-jerk anything.

The biggest thing you need to do is to learn. You need to want to learn. You don't need to be good with math, with science. If you aren't willing to learn, to be argued with, to be shut down when you're wrong you are not going to make it.

Good luck and I hope to see you around.
