### Post by TBABill on 2010-02-03
> **TheNessus said:**
> Mint is a useless politically-driven distro.
>  Mint is a useless politically-driven distro.

 
That's a bold statement for something open source that is available and encouraged to be used freely. My belief is Mint devs decided to take Ubuntu, which is pretty easy in its own right, and make it something that the average Windows user can migrate to Linux with more easily. People who use the forums, read, interact, ask questions and are aggressive enough to find answers have no problem switching to the Linux distro of their choice. However, people who are just users and do not troubleshoot their own machines when they have problems in Windows (my kids!) have more difficulty understanding how to navigate, update, get proprietary drivers working, setup printers, etc. in Ubuntu. So Mint comes along and takes a great distro and just adds some flashy looks, plugins, apps and menu changes to smooth that transition for users. 
 
Sure, other users also love Mint for other reasons, but I think it was primarily aimed at that new Linux user to get acquainted, learn the ropes (more slowly than an Ubuntu user), figure out how things work and just start using it without having to fight drivers to get it going. It's not always that easy, but the intent is there.
 
I don't see it as useless, but I was bored of it within a day. I like the challenge of setting up my system and fixing it or configuring it. But that's not how the average Windows user is (think of the people who can't add a printer to Windows, do not know the difference between save and save as, who click anything and hope not to get a virus, etc). Those are prime Mint candidates and I would hope they would be as welcome to the Linux community as the expert computer user running Gentoo.
