### Post by Zill on 2013-04-29
> **Wior said:**
> And what about Debian, there is also need to remove the trademarks?
See [http://www.debian.org/trademark](http://www.debian.org/trademark)

It is really not clear what you are trying to do.

If you are trying to produce a really ***new*** distro then the issue of Ubuntu and/or Debian trademarks becomes irrelevant.  Just write your own code from scratch and away you go.

However, if you just wish to reuse someone else's code then it is necessary to give credit to the people and/or organisations who have originally written and developed the code.

Please be aware of the relevant licensing for *all* the code you reuse.  A good example is given in the [GNU GPL]("http://www.gnu.org/licenses/licenses.html")

One of the fundamental requirements of the GPL is that when you distribute object code to users, you must also provide them with a way to get the source.

If you advise here exactly what you wish to do then you may receive better advice on how to licence it and what trademarks are relevant.
