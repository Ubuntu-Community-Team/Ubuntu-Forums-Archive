### Post by ve4cib on 2010-02-21
I tend to use Firefox for most things.  A combination of having an extensive bookmark collection, lots of plugins I've come to rely on, and a general sense of comfort with it after having used it almost-exclusively for... seven? years does that though.

I've used Chrome a few times, and wasn't all that thrilled with it.  I could probably get used to it, but whatever.

I used Opera for a little while (just for a change from Firefox).  I liked it more than Chrome.  The UI is similar enough to Firefox that I was comfortable with it right away.  I did miss the plugins though.  And Dragonfly is just no replacement for Firebug.

Finally, my standard fallback browser for when everything (including X) goes to hell is w3m.  It saved my butt a few times when updating something killed my Nvidia driver a few years back.  And since I'm paranoid about similar things happening again I always install it and leave it available in case I ever need it again.
