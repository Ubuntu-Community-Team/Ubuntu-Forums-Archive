### Post by Colo2 on 2010-03-28
[CENTER]**For all interested**[/CENTER]

I would like to start by having everyone rest assured. I hate Windows. It's more than just a hatred, its a passion. 
I now use Ubuntu on my laptop and I love it. So glad that Windows has finally gone.

But there is one thing about Linux, which I have learned by experience that is stopping me from attempting to convert all of my friends. 

It seems that, because Linux is so advanced, it's still not totally noob-friendly in the way that one little cock-up, can ruin your entire system. One mistake on your behalf, can screw everything up. I'm not denying that these things can be fixed, but fixing them is normally extremely difficult. My friends - most of them being thick when it comes to computers - would not know what they had done, what had gone wrong or where to go to get it fixed. 

Windows seems to be a bit more bullet proof when it comes to people's silly mistakes.

All that said, It hasn't stopped me using Ubuntu, and I will continue to love it as it evolves.

In my honest opinion, it's good to screw things up once in a while, you have to then fix it hence raising your knowledge in the selected field. I just don't think my friends feel the same way

Any thoughts? :KS
