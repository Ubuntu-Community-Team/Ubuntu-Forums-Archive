### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-01-29
Yeah, about 1/6 of the population owns a computer, last time I'd heard (two weeks ago). But a lot more people visit libraries to use computers, anyway. In some places, personal computers are mainly used as tools for professional work, and it's considered financially irresponsible to give your children a computer.

This is why operating systems like Linux are very important. They are modern, free, and run on less than explosively expensive computers.

There are many things in which Windows has glaring problems, if you compare it to Linux. But just as we want people to take Linux on its merits, not if it's Windows-like enough, we should all look at Windows for what it is, and not pretend it's something better or worse.

In comparison to Vista, Windows 7 is underwhelming, although it brings small improvements. Just like OS X Snow Leopard, Windows 7 is just a periodic polish of the OS, nothing too special or mind-blowing.

On this note, please don't install Windows 7 on a PC with 1 GB of RAM, since you will experience noticeably decreased performance. To experience the OS to the fullest, it is suggested to use a computer with 2 GB of RAM and a good video card.

If you're not willing to spare this expense, you may have to stick with XP. Of course, if you can use Linux with Wine to satisfy all of your computing needs, this would prove the best decision until you can get a newer computer.

Believe me, I have close experience with these operating systems. Please don't be sensationalist about it, for your own sake.
