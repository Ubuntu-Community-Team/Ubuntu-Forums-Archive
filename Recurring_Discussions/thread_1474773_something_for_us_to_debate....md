### Post by Calash on 2010-05-06
I disagree with your comparison of the every 6 month release as a new OS.  I see them as Service pack releases, with the LTS versions being much more closer to a new OS.

A 6 month cycle is quite aggressive when you consider that there are variants of Linux that can go decades without the need for updates.  However I feel it is a good thing as it allows the developers to push boundaries that a longer release cycle would not.  You can take a few more risks knowing that you can back out in 6 months should it not work as you intended.

Keynote for LTS does sound good to me as well.  They tend to be major changes and deserve more focus.
