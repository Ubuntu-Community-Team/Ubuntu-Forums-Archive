### Post by scratman on 2009-01-08
Ubuntu is not failing.  Full stop.  It is merely not being as successful as Microsoft Windows currently is, and there are myriad reasons for this.  We have to remember that Ubuntu, apart from a small number of Dell machines, is not the default Operating System.

As has been mentioned in the past, your average Joe Bloggs who has a computer because he wants to check his email, browse ebay, and listen to Music will be delighted by Vista, Outlook, IE and Windows Media Player, and why wouldn't he.  It does exactly what he wants, and if it breaks, he asks a more knowledgable mate to fix it, or goes back to MS for help.  It's like buying a second hand car with the option to take it back to the vendor to for repairs, or having a buddy who'll do it for free.  Continuing the car analogy, how many people do you know that have ever modified their car in any way?  Seriously, apart from seat covers and neccessary repairs, almost nobody alters their car from the standard spec at purchase.  The same applies to computers.

I would concede, however, that a huge number of people want a computer that does what they want, when they want, and reliably, and that Ubuntu does not necessarily tick all of those boxes.  The vast majority of these people have very little understanding of the workings of their PC, or how to customise it to do what they want.  Again, I can draw an analogy with cars, and quote a neighbour of mine, "I can drive, but I'm buggered if I can explain how an (internal combustion) engine works, I just know when it doesn't."

Ubuntu is succeeding, but only in terms of a relative underdog.  It is not, and will never be a universal operating system.  The best that can realistically be achieved is that it will achieve a market share roughly half that of Macintosh.  With the exceptions of certain things like Firefox, whereby there has been a remarkable level of market penetration, most customers do not know, understand or care about Open Source Software, they just want a computer that works, and Ubuntu, in line with the rest of the Linux community is still chasing Microsoft for that title.
