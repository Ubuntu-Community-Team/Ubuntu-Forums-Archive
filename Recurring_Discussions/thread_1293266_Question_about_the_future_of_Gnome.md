KDE adopts a different approach to Gnome. With KDE 4 for example the desktop started not as solid as Gnome 2.x, but had an ambitious and powerful codebase and went from there, becoming more fleshed out and stable with every release.

Gnome possibly can still be used to build a slightly more consistent desktop (due to it's 2.x branch being more mature than KDE's 4.x branch) but I think to say it simply "looks better" would be considered incorrect by a large number of people.

As for Gnome Shell... I will be watching it closely as well. I hope they do rethink it a bit and I'm sure they will... it's nice to see the Gnome project doing something a bit new (I have been using it on and off for about 3 years and it has changed relatively little in that time to my eyes) but I hope they manage to integrate whatever changes they make into the desktop well and they don't drive away their old userbase! :)
