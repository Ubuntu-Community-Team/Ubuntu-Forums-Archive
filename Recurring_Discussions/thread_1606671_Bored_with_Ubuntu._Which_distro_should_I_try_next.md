### Post by bonixavier on 2010-10-26
> **pcdude2143 said:**
> It would be nice if people would elaborate a little more on their answers.
I suggested Arch because it gives you the choice of fiddling with the packages or not, while it doesn't try to conceal anything from you. From what I could make of the OP, you're interested in something like that.
It's not as hard on the sysadmin as Slackware (when it comes to installing new things - I believe that people shouldn't go ./configure && make && make install on their systems, but take the time to prepare some nice scripts to tell the program where to put everything so you'll be able to track it and easily remove it if it's necessary. Or choose a system with nice packages), but it's definitely faster to set-up than Gentoo. Its install, although not as simple as Ubuntu's, is well documented and hard to miss. You get to download binaries for pretty much anything you'll ever need and it's up to you how you'll set your system. Not everything gets configured for you so you'll still need to tweak it.

I chose to make a lightining fast Arch with LXDE here on my PC and it's faster than my Gentoo. I could have as easily installed Gnome, KDE, or have gone to tiling wms, whatever. It's all there, neatly packaged and documented for you.

I think that, if you're bored with Ubuntu, a Debian-based distro would not be the best call.

Sorry for the wall of text.
