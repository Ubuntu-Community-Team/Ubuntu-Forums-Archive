### Post by MP002 on 2010-01-30
Still have one Windows (7) machine at home and it just completely crashed due to viruses.
I took the opportunity to test ClamAV. Since the toppic asks for a _good_ antivirus program and Wikipedia (and various other sources) states ClamAV must be one of the worst antivirus out there, I've decided to put it to the test.
I've tested the infected hard drive along with the zip file with viruses from my other computer that went down roughly a year ago. (And back then it was the only reason I went the Linux way.)

I'm happy to report ClamAV did not fail to live up to it's fame. 
It found absolutely nothing... zero, nada, niente.
AVG on the other hand found plethora of them. In thousands of files.

I fail to understand why ClamAV is even mentioned so many times when it fails to do its job so miserably. The way I see it, it must be better to have no antivirus whatsoever and tell friends to be extremely cautious when dealing with files I give them, then live in a false belief that antivirus software has taken care of it.

So please, stop suggesting ClamAV, because it's more like a perforated condom. It just may not work.
