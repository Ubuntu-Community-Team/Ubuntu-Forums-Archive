### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-13
To the OP:

sit down and write on a piece of paper what you want form a distro/DE. Consider the pluses and minuses. Then go over distro by distro and check which one gives you all that you want. If you cannot figure out the best distro for you, nobody can.

Also, note that you don't have to stick to one distro. You can switch back and forth as much as you want. Variety can be considered a distro, also you can expand beyond the *buntu family (which as people noted will make you appreciate it even more). At one point, on my laptop, I had a different distro every week. At the end, I found what I was looking for.

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-04-14
If you think Xfce "looks like crap," you can fix it! Xfce is super easy to customize. In fact any of the desktops can easily and quickly be changed to look pretty much any way you want. Colors, fonts, icons, panels, docks - all can be just about anything you want, no matter what desktop environment you're using.

It sounds to me like Xfce is what you want. Lightweight but full-featured (especially with Compiz on for wicked cool looks and effects), simple, and won't slow your 'puter down.

But if you're unwilling to change whatever desktop settings you don't like, no desktop known to mankind will satisfy you.
