### Post by Frak on 2007-10-18
Ubuntu uses the Gnome 2.20 interface, and is more heavily developed compared to its counterpart Kubuntu. Ubuntu uses GTK+, XUL, and Zenity for 99% of its applications.

Kubuntu uses the KDE 3.5.7 (or KDE4 if you want the bleeding edge, polished desktop), and uses QT, QT+, K, and X for 99% of its applications. Most of its applications start with K, such as Kopete, Kedit, Kate, Konversation, Ksudoku, Ktorrent, etc.

Ubuntu is developed more than Kubuntu, but some people (such as myself) rather use Kubuntu over Ubuntu.

Note: Kubuntu does need a little more tinkering to work as well as its Gnome counterpart.
