### Post by Copper Bezel on 2013-01-11
The Launcher isn't even supposed to look like the windows and panel. It's coordinated to the desktop wallpaper and the Dash search screen, among other things (like the pop-up notifications from NotifyOSD.) Mint is based on Gnome Shell, which has the panel themed to match the search screen and notifications instead of the windows. They both use two sets of colors, just in different places. The Mint taskbar doesn't match the windows for the same reason that the Ubuntu Launcher doesn't.

And the preset colors are *all shades of gray anyway*. = ) Though I do think that Ambiance is a little claustrophobic, while Radiance is quite attractive and warm, although I wish that the background color for GTK2 apps matched the sort of champagne-white used for the titlebars and the GTK3 theme.

[QUOTE=deadflower]I don't have a gray panel.[/QUOTE]
And, amusingly, the Mint screenshot *did*. I have to admit that my panel is gray, but it's silver-gray, since I'm using an Apple-style theme.
