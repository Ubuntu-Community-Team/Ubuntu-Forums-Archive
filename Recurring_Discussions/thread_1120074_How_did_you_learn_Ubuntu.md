### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-04-09
[KnottyMars]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=693826")

As many have already explained, the best way to learn Ubuntu is to spend time getting to know the system and becoming comfortable in the environment.

Unlike some, once I've setup the system to meet all our requirements - I leave it alone. Adding applications cautiously and only when required.

This has led to a very stable and user friendly platform for daily activites. On two of our systems, many of the options available in the Preference & Administration sections of the menu have been removed from the menu and any panels are locked to minimize mistakes by guest. Almost anyone can use these two systems with a bare minimum of instructions. When people visit they inevitability wish to try Ubuntu and I've found simplicity to be in everyone's best interest. Especially, mine.

When I first installed Ubuntu (FF), like others I immediately started playing with the system and spent time investigating all the apps, etc. Now we seek stability/functionality and have learned to be patient. With Ubuntu (like many things in life), patience is often rewarded.

Enjoy and have fun.
