### Post by FoamRocket on 2008-04-30
This review of Ubuntu has been printed in the [Gothenburg Post](www.gp.se) and published on their website [here](http://www.gp.se/gp/jsp/Crosslink.jsp?d=400&a=417132). 

Do you consider this review to be fair? 



> _Sorry Linux, I don't want to_

If Mac is a cool dude, and Windows is a office guy, then what is Linux? A bright girl from the alternative movement? Unfortunately, no. Behind a exciting and beautiful exterior there's a crazy nerd. 


This Thursday a new version of Ubuntu, the flavor of Linux that many consider to be the best, was released. I had recently tried Linux in Asus small surf computer and was hoping for something better looking for a bigger computer. And the first impression was good, very good. 

The desktop is beautiful. A foreign bird in orange, brown and red. In the menus there's programs and settings. All that I need, nothing more. 

But even though Ubuntu is an African word for humanity to others, this is a system that seems to lack compassionate ability. It starts right away after that I've begun to use it, as I need to input a password. It's not possible to leave the field empty. 

Then I'm going to set the time. To change it I need to input name and password. Hello! I don't want to erase the hard drive, just set the time correctly!

After that I want to use the Internet. It doesn't work to write the adress because the commas turns into little hooks. I manage to figure out how to change into Swedish keyboard layout. Hello, again! I've downloaded a Swedish version. How hard can it be to understand that I'm probably using a Swedish keyboard? 

I manage to start the web browser but it can't connect to the Internet even though I'm in a open wlan. I open the network settings. Ubuntu demands name and password everytime something changes. 

I download Google earth. I need to open a terminal window and write code to get it to install. I have to visit a blog to get help. I never manage to figure out how to make the program icon to appear in the programmenu. 

Ubuntu, you are beautiful, but I don't want to live with you. You want everything done in your way, the way you've decided. You don't even try to understand how I think, you never oblige me. You don't want me to have any programs of my own. You control everything I do and you don't trust me. You always ask me for my name all the time, even though we've been together for two days now. You want me to learn everything about you, but honestly, I don't feel like hanging around on your nerdy forums and get plucky answers about how to handle you. 

But your bird is nice. I'll save it as a memory.
