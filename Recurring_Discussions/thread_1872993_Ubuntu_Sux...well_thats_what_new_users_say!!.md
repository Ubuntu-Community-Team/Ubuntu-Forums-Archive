Hi everyone!  This is exactly what I did for a buddy.  His computer was no longer functional due to Windows crashing, likely from a virus.  He remembered that I use Linux, and asked if that would fix it.  I told him it would, but Linux is a little different than Windows, and he'd have to be prepared for a few differences.  Buddy said he didn't care, as long as it worked, and he could e-mail and use the internet.

So I put on 10.04 LTS because I figured it would be the smoothest transition.  About a week later he called an said his webcam wouldn't install.  Sure enough, he was trying to install from a windows disc!! I said it should "just work" and showed him that it was working in Cheese.  He was impressed that he didn't need to "install" the webcam.  Another week or so later he called and thanked me for fixing his computer, a new Linux user was thrilled!

Also, I should note that after using Unity for about 6 months, the old 10.04 LTS looked a little dated to me.  For me, I really prefer Unity.

But I understand entirely how a Windows person would find 11.10 a little more confusing than say 10.04.
