### Post by MaxIBoy on 2008-10-28
To the original poster:

The interface you see is entirely seperate from the operating system itself. These interfaces are put together by different organizations. Canonical (the company that sponsors Ubuntu) does not *own* GNOME, or KDE, or Xfce, or BASH, or OpenBox, or FluxBox, or LXDE, or ZSH, or ASH, or SH, or BlackBox, or Enlightenment, or Linux. 

Pure Linux with absolutely nothing else would be completely unusable, because you wouldn't see anything and you couldn't give it any input. It would just sit there on your desk, harmlessly babbling to itself. It needs a desktop environment or window manager to make it usable. 

Different DEs are good for different tasks. Each one is maintained by its own developers. Ubuntu is just Linux, plus some software, plus GNOME. Xubuntu is the same thing with Xfce instead. And since the developers of these DEs package them, it's really easy to install one. (Try it! install Xfce from the repos, and you can switch between Xubuntu and standard Ubuntu with a simple ctrl-alt-backspace!
