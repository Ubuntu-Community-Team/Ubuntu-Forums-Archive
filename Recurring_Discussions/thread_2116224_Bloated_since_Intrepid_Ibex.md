### Post by snowpine on 2013-02-15
"Bloated" is such an ugly word. It makes me think of a dead cow, its belly distended with decomposition gasses. Ubuntu is not "bloated!" It fits on a single DVD and only uses a few GB's of hard drive space. Any new computer on the market in 2013 (and quite a few pre-2013 systems) can easily run Ubuntu (or one of its variants) with good performance. 

2008 may not seem that long ago, but according to [Moore's Law]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moore%27s_law"), hardware power has increased 4 to 8 fold during that interval. Ubuntu is merely keeping pace (in fact significantly behind the pace--minimum requirements have "only" doubled) of hardware development.
