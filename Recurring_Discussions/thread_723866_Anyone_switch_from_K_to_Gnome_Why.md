I switched back and became a huge KDE fan, GNOME 2 wasn't looking to ever be released and when it was the initial 2.0 version was underwhelming when I tried it. KDE 3 was extremely powerful but suffered a lot of problems in terms of complexity, I started to feel like I was doing a whole lot of work just using my machine instead of the environment being designed to solve my problems. It started to feel like I spend more time configuring and learning applications instead of them doing the obviously right thing.

This however is when the GNOME people started to impress me, they went from a 2.0 release to the 2.2 release in 6 months and solved all the problems I had, they also added a bunch of useful polish and generally I found myself agreeing with the design ideas (usability for the greatest common benefactor). I switched back to GNOME and stuck with it to this day. The application that really did it for me though was acme, the application that enabled multimedia keys on modern keyboards - it just worked so wonderfully and it was easy to use, at the same time I was using a horribly complex KDE alternative that involved snooping on x keyboard events then designing your own .ini style configuration file listing all your keys and their actions. At that moment GNOME just seemed a whole lot more right in it's approach.

I have tried KDE 4 on a separate machine but I somehow feel like KDE is still stuck in the 90's with their design choices and to me it feels like a Windows 98 with more gloss. The plasmoid stuff is all cool but once you scratch underneath that glitter it is grey on grey, the same e.g. file browser type you'd see in the 90's and no plan to progress to a truly better computing experience. 

I also tried the gnome-shell and so far I am not happy with it one bit, though looking back I was never happy with the first time GNOME did anything major in terms of a redesign. I am though happy with pretty much all the other decisions for the GNOME 3 platform and I believe that stable of technology has promise - perhaps one day the gnome-shell will not suck or will be replaced.
