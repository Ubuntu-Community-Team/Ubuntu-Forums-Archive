### Post by murderslastcrow on 2011-04-28
Xfce4 doesn't lack anything obvious from Gnome 2, and has more configuration options than Gnome 3 or Unity, at the moment. Or rather, easier configuration options. I assume the next Xfce, or some release in Xfce4, will actually adopt GTK 3, as well. So I've been telling people who hate Unity and Gnome 3 to just try KDE 4 for once, or use Xfce 4 for a good, safe, familiar, fast environment.

I, for one, will probably use Gnome 3. But there's no reason you can't use Compiz as a window manager, docky/AWN as panels, etc. etc. You can still use all sorts of software in any configuration you like if you choose the WM route.

So, if everything falls to pieces and I hate it all, at least I have me some compiz and AWN to stay alive. And that's the worst-case scenario, the one that will never happen.

I think it'll be interesting to see just how much the community changes as we accept these new environments. I think the Gnome-centric purist attitude is finally fading a bit, and we can expect a bit more Linux-love in this community than simply Gnome or KDE love. It's a shame to cut yourself off from an application just because it uses a different toolkit you associate with a different DE you don't like. Developers work hard to make everything interoperable and visually coherent between their applications and environments, so it would be a shame to ignore them on the basis of some deranged toolkitism.
