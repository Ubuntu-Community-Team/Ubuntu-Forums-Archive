### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-09-27
> **silvergoat said:**
> I don't know how to do it, but I'd rather switch it back to the previous interface.

It's really easy.

Log out. Click on your username at the login screen. At the bottom of the screen a popup menu will appear where you can choose Ubuntu Classic. Choose this and type your password and continue logging in as usual. You'll be at the ye olde Gnome 2 desktop, and this setting will be remembered for future logins.

Unfortunately, Ubuntu 11.10 will no longer be compatible with Gnome 2. However, all is not lost. The gnome-fallback-session package gives you a desktop that's fairly similar to Gnome 2. Once you've installed gnome-fallback-session you can log into it as I've told you.

I personally quite like Unity; it's very efficient on space, and it looks nice, and I love being able to drag and drop files onto the launcher icons to open the file in a specific program. I also like how the Compiz window management features are integrated, and certain programs show quicklists, status emblems and progress bars in their launcher icons.

But if you've given Unity an honest change and it's still not for you, I'm glad you have the ability to switch to something more your style.

------------
EDIT: Not sure about your wifi problem, but Ubuntu 11.10 will be out next month. It's in beta at the moment and is quite stable; maybe your wifi issue is sorted in 11.10.
