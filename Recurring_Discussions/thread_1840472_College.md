### Post by meh_phistopheles on 2011-09-07
look up the rankings online. they are important, regardless of your personal belief on the subject, because of the fact that others hold them as being important. it's a necessary evil, as they say.

then, depending on how you did in school, apply for a couple you would consider high in the rankings for you, do a couple in the middle, and have a couple fallbacks. don't worry about tuition for the high ranking schools, because if you get in, they're pretty judicial about giving funding (a TAship). regardless, you should apply for a TAship wherever you go. they cover full tuition, living expenses, and usually are guaranteed for 4 or 5 years. so, they're basically like getting a graduate degree for free.
