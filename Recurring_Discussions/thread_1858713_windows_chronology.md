### Post by decoherence on 2011-10-13
> **vehemoth said:**
> I have to wonder why if macs were so much better that windows is the most widely used desktop os today, maybe you can clue me up on that one.

High end Macs were at least twice the price of 'equivalent' Windows machines. Of course, the reason Macs sold at all was because there was no such thing as an equivalent Windows machine before NT4. Your only other option for what we now call 'content creation' was SGI and they made Macs look cheap.

The cheap Mac was the Performa. It was still more expensive than a PC, had crippled hardware and were notoriously bad sellers. At that time Apple couldn't market themselves out of a paper bag. It was people who were willing to spend $15,000 on a computer to use professionally that were Apple's bread and butter, once upon a time.

Windows and DOS were cheap in other ways, too. Anyone could ship a DOS box -- that was the first nail in Apple's coffin, and the 90's sealed that company's fate, with people speculating that Sun or SGI might buy them (honestly!) But while Apple was lingering in a zombie-like state, a parasite called NeXT infiltrated its brain and started controlling its body and that's the Apple we have today. More or less.

### Post by jwbrase on 2011-10-13
> **vehemoth said:**
> The op made it sound like macs were better during the MS DOS era however I thought that that was the time that miscrosoft passed apple in size.

Macs had a better UI, a lot of the same "under the hood" problems, and had higher prices.

Meanwhile, MS DOS was running on hardware that (initially) was made by IBM, and the business world knew and trusted IBM, and wanted to get as much work done as possible for as low a price as it could, and thus saw no reason to pay a premium on Macs just to get a better interface.

So Macs had an interface that was more targeted at consumers, but pricing that was out of reach for a lot of people, while PCs were more targeted towards businesses, but were also more reasonably priced than Macs were for consumers. So Macs got the high-end consumer market, while PC's got the low-end consumer market and the business market.

So MS DOS got its market share by being the incumbent OS on a hardware platform that catered to a broader market (as well as some rather sleazy business maneuvers on MS's part to make sure that it remained the dominant OS on that hardware).
