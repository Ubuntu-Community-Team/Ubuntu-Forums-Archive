### Post by snowpine on 2010-03-14
Answers to Question 1

1. DSL is a dead project
2. There are a few things I don't like about Puppy (package manangement, auto-login as root) but I have tested it briefly on a fast computer and it is, well, fast. :)
3. I love SliTaz, it has more of a "serious distro" feel than Puppy and is just as fast (if not faster).
4. Tiny Core is popular too but I haven't tried it.

Answers to Question 2
1. Personally, if my hardware meets the minimum requirements, I always choose one of the major distros (Ubuntu/Debian/Fedora/Arch/CentOS/etc.) due to their support, stability, repository size, etc. The smaller community projects are too buggy and unprofessional for my personal tastes.
2. You would be wasting one core of your dual core processor (and any ram over 2gb) using a distro designed for old 32 bit computers. I would choose Arch+JWM (or openbox) over Puppy for a fast, lightweight, 64-bit install, in a heartbeat.
3. By all means put Puppy on a USB thumb drive and use it for traveling, system rescue, etc. I do the same with SliTaz. :)
