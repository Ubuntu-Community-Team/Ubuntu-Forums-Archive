### Post by SomeGuyDude on 2009-01-12
When people say Linux, especially something like Ubuntu, is "hard", all they mean is "it's not Windows". People who think that if it's different than how they're used to it, it's hard.

Funny thing is, I adjusted to Ubuntu FAR faster than trying to go the other way. Since I haven't had Windows on my computer in over a year, nowadays when I try and figure out how stuff is done on it I'm just baffled. Seeing system files just thrown in the same folder with user files blows my mind. The labyrinthine Control Panel isn't exactly useful half the time, and why is "system" a different menu than the CP stuff?

Once again, take the old test of snagging two people of the same age and background who've never used a computer. Give one a laptop with Ubuntu, give one a laptop with Windows. Seem who's more productive after a week.

Point is, if it wasn't for the fact that Windows has been the "standard" for the past ~20 years it wouldn't be considered easy. Considering the layout of Windows hasn't changed since Window 95, obviously people are going to think it's easy. There's no way something brand new won't seem scary and foreign to someone whose OS hasn't significantly altered how it functions in more than a decade.
