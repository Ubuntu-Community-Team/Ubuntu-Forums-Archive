I actually don't mind Unity too much in that I can ALMOST get it back to how I want my linux desktop compared to Gnome-Shell.

Like [almost 74% of users pre 11.xx]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1441794"), I liked to customise the gnome2.

I had the following:
1) Specific Compiz settings to enable a more efficient desktop feel.  (I can still make the same changes in Unity, but no in Gnome-Shell).

2) Emerald to change the window decorations to my liking beyond the gnome theme options (can't do that in either Unity or Gnome-Shell).

3) Single Panel at the top and cairo-dock on the bottom (OK on both Unity and Gnome-Shell).

4) Useful stuff on the top panel (eg server config/connect icon; custom launchers/icons/scripts; a proper weather app - the one included with Unity is ridiculously inaccurate for my area; etc) (Very regressed in both but Unity is a little better than Gnome-Shell)


Point 1 is solved in Unity, I can live without Point 2; Point 3 is not an issue; but Point 4 is really bad.  Before the side-panels started to appear in the netbook editions, I was already playing around with having a side panel in addition to my top panel and my cairo-dock.  I must say that I'm finding it quite useful for certain programs.  I do keep it hidden until needed though.

However, I know that 11.10 and 12.04 are not about extending the features in a huge way, but rather about stability.  So I'm keenly waiting for 12.10 to come around to fix the regressions (albeit minor) that I am experiencing.

Customisation aside, I had to boot Windows 7 a little while ago to check compatibility of a triaging program I wrote on Linux for Windows machines at work.  My gripes about 11.10 are cosmetic.  However, I cannot imaging going back to a world of Windows.  I personally find the OS intrusive and awkwardly organised.  I haven't run Windows as my main distro on any computer since 2009 and I must say that I don't miss it at all.  My perspective is that my laptop is a Mac and Ubuntu is still way more customisable than that.

Well you can start any app with one click if you put in on the Unity launcher, without scrolling. :)

I am not sure what you mean by "the panel", I use the classic menu in gnome2 to start apps, and I have the Cairo dock for commonly use ones. Maybe I am not a heavy panel user, I am not aware of any other thing that the panel does (I switch desktop and rotate the cube with the keyboard and the workplace switcher on the Cairo dock instead of the panel)

yes, you can put tray icons on gnome2 panel  but you can do that in Unity as well, just install dconf-tools, type alt+f2 and in the run box type "dconf-editor" (without quotations) When the dconf-editor appears, choose desktop > Unity > panel and then change the systray icon setting to ['all'] (with the square brackets and quotations) this will whitelist all applications and when they are running the icons will appear on the panel.

Er... did you miss the big bar down the left side of the screen that holds a bunch of icons?

Drag a program to that big bar and it will appear as a one-click-launches icon within that bar. Alternatively (or if you are using Unity 2D) open the program in the usual way, then right-click its icon in the Launcher and choose "Keep In Launcher".
