### Post by MaxIBoy on 2009-12-25
Smells like recurring discussions jailbait.

I use Debian Sid on my laptop (with experimental repos enabled,) because that combination is the only commonly-used bleeding-edge Debian-based rolling-release distro I know of. 

A lot of people prefer Fedora because it's a bleeding-edge *non*-rolling-release distro and the security is a bit tighter by default.

And I know there are quite a few people here with good reasons for using Arch, and I intend to try out Arch myself sometime.


Basically, different distros are good for different tasks and preferences. Infinite choice is the only thing that keeps us from arguing all the time instead of just *some* of the time.
