### Post by Miguel on 2010-01-02
I had an awful experience quite a few years ago with an RPG, Lands of Lore 2.

Nobody at home (this was our first computer) wanted anything to do with Win95, so we chose DOS as the OS. I bought LOL2 because the box stated compatibility with both Win95 and DOS, and had already played LOL1. However, it was impossible to play that game in DOS. No matter what, mingw always failed and couldn't get LOL2 to run.

After two or three different copies, I managed to convice the salesman that the issue actually lied with false advertising on the box and got my money back. In any case, it was a horrible experience.

If you want an easy route to pain and suffering, you may be interested in buying Nikon's CaptureNX 2. Beautiful colour rendition and a great demosaicing algorithms being wasted with an awful UI, the most abominable resource management ever and horrible workflow. It wouldn't be so painful if you didn't become addicted to the end results. At least they offer a 60 day free trial (the longest I know in the world of photography).

My final story also goes about another game: the Falcon 4.0 flight simulator. The spanish retail box came with the 1.06 patch, and that could be updated to 1.07. English speaking countries got the "much less awful" 1.08i2... and then Hasbro dismantled Microprose (the company behind Falcon 4.0). At that point we had a great flight sim with an unbelievable potential... with horrible instabilities and very bad internal parametres. The community work did wonders to that game, especially with the release of RP5.

A correct patched installation was a nightmare for newbies, and was correctly named "the falcon dance". However, this left you with what's probably the best fighter jet sim ever made (and it was released 10 years ago!). The only real alternative is... Falcon 4.0 Allied Force, which is basically a fork of Falcon 4.0. Lock-on has its strong points, but it doesn't have the complex avionics nor the dynamic campaing that made F4 so brilliant.
