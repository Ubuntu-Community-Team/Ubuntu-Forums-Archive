### Post by thatguruguy on 2011-11-26
Canonical also pursues various streams of income in order to help fund Ubuntu (other than right out of Mar Shuttleworth's pockets):

[LIST=1]
[*]They make a percentage from any music bought through the Ubuntu One Music Store or through the Amazon Store in Banshee (when run on Ubuntu);
[*]They make a percentage on any for-purchase software sold through the Ubuntu Software Center;
[*]They offer paid support of Ubuntu (mainly to corporate clients, but anyone can buy support);
[*]They make some money when people do searches through the Ubuntu homepage on Firefox (and maybe Chromium; I don't know).
[/LIST]
There is some chance they make money when OEMs install Ubuntu as the OS on production machines, but I'm obviously not privy to how they handle that.

### Post by Penguinnerd on 2011-11-26
I'll compare Ubuntu's model to Debian's to highlight the differences:

Debian prides itself on not being for-profit or in any way commercial. The coding is done by generous people who are good at programming, and others do things like paying for the servers, hosting, etc with donations of money.

Ubuntu, on the other hand, has Canonical backing it to make sure it's nicely polished. They employ a some people to make this happen, but a very significant part of the work is still done by generous volunteers.

Canonical earns money by ways described in an earlier post.

Not really.
