### Post by lovinglinux on 2009-05-15
There is a distinction between [Obsessive–compulsive disorder (OCD)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsessive-compulsive_disorder) and [Obsessive–Compulsive Personality Disorder (OCPD)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsessive%E2%80%93compulsive_personality_disorder). While both can share some symptoms, the [perfectionism](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perfectionism_(psychology)) is usually associated with OCPD. See quote below:

> In the book Too Perfect, (Mallinger & DeWyze 1992) the authors describe perfectionists as having obsessive personality types. The obsessive personality type is distinct from obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD); OCD is a clinical disorder that may be associated with specific ritualized behavior. According to Mallinger and DeWyze, perfectionists are obsessives who need to feel in control at all times to protect themselves and ensure their own safety. By being constantly vigilant and trying extremely hard, they can ensure that they not only fail to disappoint or are beyond reproach but that they can protect against unforeseen issues (such as economic downturn). Vigilance may include constant monitoring of the news, weather, and financial markets.

Basically a person with OCD would install each new Ubuntu version 3 times before actually using it, fearing that something inexplicably bad would happen if he didn't do this way. Additionally, this person would install new versions of Ubuntu ending with .10 but would not install versions ending with .04, for the same reasons. He might be aware that this procedure is completely nonsense, but he can't avoid it. 

On the other hand, the person with OCPD would install every new Ubuntu release only once before using it, but would re-install when he feels something is not working like the day before, even if he is not sure about the difference. Additionally, he would tweak and customize the OS almost endlessly, because he would never reach the desired perfection. He would also have a hard time accepting criticism about the OS and would demand nothing more than perfection from others around him. He would have a hard time trying to accept that other people don't like Ubuntu and why they still use Windows.

I almost certainly have OCPD. In my opinion, the question should not be "Are Linux Users OCD" but "Do OCPD users tend to like Linux more than other OS?".

Theres a tweaker hidden in every human being, and certain things seem to trigger it. You can minimize damage to your Operating System by giving your inner tweak his or her very own account. Be sure not to let the little deviant have any admin priv's; you don't want it getting carried away and hosing up a perfectly good install.
