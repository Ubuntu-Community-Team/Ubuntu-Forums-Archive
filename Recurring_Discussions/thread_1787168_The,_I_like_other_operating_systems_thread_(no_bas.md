### Post by handy on 2011-06-20
I'm grateful to Ubuntu & the Ubuntu forum community. It made my crossover from the Windows world, (in which I had been very much entrenched in my repair business) so much quicker & easier in so many ways.

It helped me to learn about other distros & to some extent BSD, & got me on my feet so I could distro hop until I found Arch, after which I gradually slowed down on the hopping to the point that it is now extremely rare that I look at another distro/system.

Though I'm about to burn the Haiku R1 Alpha 3, image & check it out with a great deal of anticipation. ;)

On my main machine I dual boot OSX. I don't like OSX, but it does some things for me that I still can't do, or can't easily do in Linux i.e. Simple & easy web page creation via the software called Sandvox & easy reliable maintenance (mainly just the occasional backup these days) of my iPod Touch.

Windows I'm not interested in, I did my time for others & have been well over 5 years retired & away from it. That said, I will probably have to install XP on an old PIII that I have, so that I can run slot car race management software. That will be that machines only job, it won't connect to the web or the LAN, so it has a chance of being reliable & not wasting my time. 

Time will tell.
