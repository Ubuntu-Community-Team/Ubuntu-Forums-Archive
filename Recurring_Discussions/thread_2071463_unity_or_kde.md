### Post by PcMojo on 2012-10-15
When checking out different Desktop Environments (DE's), keep in mind that they are highly customizable. The same DE can change considerably between distros. I was looking at Enlightenment a while back and liked what I saw in one distro. I ran a live cd of another distro with Enlightenment and hated it. Although I really like the huge Ubuntu and Mint software repository, I've seen many distros that had really nice DE implementations out of the box. (PcLinuxOs Kde and Enlightenment comes to mind) Another consideration is the speed of your hardware. If you have an older processor or limited RAM, you may want to opt for some of the more lightweight environments; Xfce, Enlightenment, Lxde, etc. A newer DE that is quickly gaining popularity, especially on Mint – an Ubuntu derivative, is Cinnamon. It is quicker than KDE or Gnome on my laptop and much more intuitive. I was never a fan of Xfce (or Lxde) until the latest Ubuntu Studio came out using Xfce. It boots quicker than anything I've ever used and taxes my Ram less when multitasking. If you are into creating or editing music, video and graphics - check out Ubuntu Studio! You would think with all those heavy duty programs it would be slow, but it isn't. Mint / Cinnamon prides itself with working right out of the box and is a great choice also. My advice to anyone wanting to compare DE's is instead of using Live Cd's, to learn how to use virtual box or read up on putting live CD iso's on a flash drive with persistence (so you can do a little customization that will be available when you reboot). When I first moved over to Linux, I was overwhelmed at the sheer number of distros and Desktop Environments, but it sure is nice to have a choice! Good luck and happy desktop hunting!

### Post by Mikeb85 on 2012-10-15
I personally prefer Unity.  I find the default behaviour to be the most acceptable and most productive of all the DEs, I also like the look of it.  The Dash is great for quickly launching programs, while there are a few programs for other DEs which do the same, I find the dash to be the most useful (and it's even customizable with lenses).  I also haven't found any equivalents to the HUD for other DEs, though I haven't looked into it extensively.  

The Unity haters are loud and make their opinions known, but IMO they are simply resistant to change.  

Unity really is the best DE.  

The easiest way to try new DEs if you're still inclined, is to install them from Synaptic.  Then you can simply log into different sessions from the login screen.  This way you can test each one on bare metal, see how each performs...
