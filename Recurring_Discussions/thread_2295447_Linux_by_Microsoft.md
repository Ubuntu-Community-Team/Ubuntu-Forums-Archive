### Post by buzzingrobot on 2015-09-20
This is a manifestation of Nadella's dismissal of Ballmer's "Windows Everywhere" approach. Ballmer might have insisted that Azure be 100% Windows, even if Windows wasn't the best fit for a particular role.

Azure, though, is a service, not something you can buy in a box or download. Microsoft won't gain any additional revenue by avoiding non-Windows software on Azure.

Microsoft had been talking for months about "Windows As a Service" and declaring that Windows 10 is the "last" version of Windows.  That could mean many things.  But, I suspect it does mean Nadella has decided, like Google and Apple, that the days of betting the corporation solely on selling an operating system and a few applications are over.
