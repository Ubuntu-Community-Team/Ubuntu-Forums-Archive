### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-04-28
What's really happening is that the mobile phone platforms are relatively new. As they are new, they have modern, attractive graphics and visual effects. They also have modern features such as pervasive search.

Gradually the desktop PC operating systems are catching up in terms of improved appearance, and in incorporating the similar kinds of features that mobile OSes have. This requires somewhat of a redesign of the desktop.

Other than the visual style and having touch-friendly icons instead of textual menus, there's really not much that's the same between Unity and a phone OS such as Android or iOS. For instance, Unity does windowing - no mobile phone platform can display more than one program onscreen at the same time. That's going to be the defining difference between "mobile" and "desktop" OS.

Windows 8 is a different story, but when Microsoft realises what a massive mistake they've made putting Windows For Tablets onto the desktop, they'll promote the traditional desktop to a first-class citizen.
