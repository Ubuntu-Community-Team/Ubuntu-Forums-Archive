### Post by Mikeb85 on 2011-10-24
Today I was in the mood for some games, so I decided I was going to reinstall Windows Vista.  Installation went fine and dandy, then I boot into Windows, and no internet connection.  Apparently Vista couldn't detect the cable plugged into the motherboard (among other things, it also didn't detect the video card, monitor, webcam, printer, etc... Ubuntu has spoiled me).  So I find all my driver CDs, put the first one into the DVD drive and, nothing.  I forgot about the issue Vista had with DVD drives.  

When I first got my computer it was a self-build, so I know how to fix all these things, but then it came back to me how much of a pain it is, which quickly got rid of my mood for games (probably for the better, my girlfriend wouldn't appreciate me wasting too much time), so I took the opportunity to install Ubuntu 11.10 fresh.  I suppose I could just go out and pay 200 dollars for the Vista patch (aka Windows 7), or install XP (which is also a pain to get drivers and stuff working), but Ubuntu is just easier and works better.  

Sometimes I think if the average computer user was forced to install their own Windows, MS market share would be about zero.  Linux and especially Ubuntu has me spoiled, recognizing all my stuff out of the box and all...
