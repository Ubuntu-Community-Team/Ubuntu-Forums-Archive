Personally? Stable and on my desktop doing the business.

I suspect that Windows 8 / 9 will be a GNU/Linux OS with an X frontend which looks like Seattle wants it and a subscription Windows Application Store and Online Configuration and Update Service. Like Ubuntu + regular payments from the consumer. Much of the drive in consumer / domestic computing will be towards subscription Cloud Computing Resources and special dedicated appliances. Microsoft will continue to make a lot of money out of computing, of course, but they will save megabucks by not having to pay for OS development.

I am guessing that the world of consumer / business computing will be migrating fast to fully supported GNU/Linux networks and desktops. Many of the big corporate systems rolled out in the mid-nineties to mid-noughties were Windows by design; many of them will be up for refreshment in the time frame 2015 - 2020. Follow-on systems are, now, being specified on a purely functional basis independently of platform. Cost of deployment and lifetime support are the critical determinants. Business, big and small, has not bought into the hardware and software replacement every 3 - 5 years business model that MS has offered to date. Big bucks to be made by the system integration / re-integration and legacy data conversion sectors.

Just guessin'
