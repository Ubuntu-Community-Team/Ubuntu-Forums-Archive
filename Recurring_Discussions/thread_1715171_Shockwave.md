### Post by Dark_Stang on 2011-03-26
> **ecthileon said:**
> Why hasn't a third party made a shockwave player for linux?  I believe there is one version, but it costs money.

I can't help but believe that someone in the linux community has/is able to make a linux compatible version.
Several reasons.
1. Not enough motivation. Nobody is being payed to do create a player for Linux and there isn't a large enough community need for it. I can't remember the last time I've seen ShockWave being used anywhere for anything.
2. Even if a developer or small team was commissioned to do this, they would need to support and modify this if ShockWave continues to live.
3. Adobe is extremely proprietary. Adobe probably wouldn't release any useful documentation or spec to any outside organizations if there is any chance they'd make money on it.
