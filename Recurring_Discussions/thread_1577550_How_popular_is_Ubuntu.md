### Post by sikander3786 on 2010-09-19
Ubuntu is the most popular Linux Distro, no doubt.

Why is it so popular? Well it depends. Basically on the user experience. One who has had a good experience recommends it to others and the marketing goes on as I am trying to do with my friends, colleagues etc. :-)

A secondary reason might be the Canonical/Shuttleworth factor.

And another positive factor is one of the most friendliest community on the globe.

You also cannot forget the free CDs including free shipping. Awesome.

Many other factors include release cycle, pre-compiled deb packages when comparing to Fedora etc (more than 23,000 officially stated), Ubuntu One, LTS plans etc etc. You can't get more than this on any other Linux Distro.

Still the most important factor in my opinion is user experience. And that is what the Ubuntu Developers have achieved quite remarkably.

### Post by kaldor on 2010-09-19
Depends on the area.

-SuSE and RHEL are most common in business (local government offices use them here, along with XP, Win7 and OS X)

-Ubuntu is most common amongst home users (hence why everyone recommends it)

-CentOS and Debian are most common on servers (from my experience, at least).

-Schools vary; I usually see either Ubuntu or an Ubuntu respin being used.

-Hobbyists... varies.
