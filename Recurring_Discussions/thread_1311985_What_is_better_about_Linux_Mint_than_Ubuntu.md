### Post by unamanic on 2009-11-02
What's "better" about mint:
-the menu - typing the name of an application you don't have installed will give you the option of installing it.
-Blatant disregard for software patents means you have all of the codecs you want out of the gate

What's not:
-While Mint is based off of Ubuntu (uses alot of the same repos) its releases are not completely synced you'll have to wait a wile for the 9.0 based version
-Some don't like the politics
-Blatant disregard of software patents mean that in the US are committing a crime (well, not really a crime, copyright violations are a civil matter) by both downloading (possessing) and seeding (distributing) the iso.
