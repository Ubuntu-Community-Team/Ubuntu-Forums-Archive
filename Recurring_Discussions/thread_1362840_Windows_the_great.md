### Post by onyxwolf on 2009-12-23
Ok I originally posted this with a negative start (a reaction as Anjali Mehta was looking for), but thats not how we like to handle ourselves. Let me help enlighten you as to why Linux is making it onto PC. Let's start with peace of mind-- since most people who are not technical don't care to try anything other than what is the main go to (i.e. Windows, or Apple) its going to be a long time before mean people develop truly effective viruses against it. You'll find that most Linux users don't even use Anti-Virus, (not to mention that its even harder to get around Linux's permissions architecture). How about system performance. Let's start my Dell D830 up with my Ubuntu and someone using the same exact hardware with even Windows 7 on it and I'm willing to bet everyone who uses Ubuntu Desktop will put their money on my machine being ready to use first. How about updates-- Windows users have to trust that the crappy updates they are getting from MS are not going to fry their OS or cause major performance issues; whereas, Ubuntu users get updates from developers all over the globe filtered down and approved before it ever makes it to our update manager. How about support, sure you can pay Canonical Ltd. for reliable quick support (which is probably cheaper, quicker, and more reliable than MS, but I don't know I've never had to do that), but the vast number of Linux communities are eager to help. I want you to compare the incredible number of posts on this on site to any, and I mean any, MS or Windows forums sites and see if they even compare! I have never ran into an intentionally misleading post here. (Although I'm certain there are a few jokers who want you to wipe your system with powerful commands, those guys are VERY few and VERY far between).
