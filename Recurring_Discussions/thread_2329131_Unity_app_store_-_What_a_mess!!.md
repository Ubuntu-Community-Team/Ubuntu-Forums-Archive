### Post by grahammechanical on 2016-06-28
You mean Gnome Software. Do you not?

The developers decided that something called AppStream would be nice in the Ubuntu Software Centre. But to put AppStream in the Ubuntu Software Centre required a major re-write. So, the developers decided to replace the Software Centre with Gnome Software which already handled AppStream and then rename it Ubuntu Software.

But for AppStream to be of any use the maintainers of applications have to provide the metadata for their apps. And many have not done that. Also, I do believe that Gnome Software is still not a finished project and the Gnome developers have shown in the past that they have their own ideas about how software show look and work and they do not shift in their opinions.

I tested Gnome Software in 16.04 months before 16.04 was released. Believe me, when I say that if you think things are bad now, then you should have seen it when Gnome Software was first made available for testing. It was a store without any products.

Ubuntu developers have worked hard and are working hard. People care or not as the case may be. Commercial organisations, in fact any kind of organisation, cannot care because they are not living beings with emotions.

Do you care enough to test Linux and its applications and then report bugs. Or do you only care about finding something to complain about?
