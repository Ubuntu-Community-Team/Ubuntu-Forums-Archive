It is a matter of personal preference. I use GNOME, having tried KDE. There are people who swear by KDE, others who prefer XFCE, and others, like me, who prefer GNOME. If you work your way through many responses in the various forums, you will find:

1. Many flamewars - my GUI is better than yours type of discussions.
2. Many people who say that Linux is about choice. Choose what works for you.

Enjoy the freedom to choose!

### Post by Chilli Bob on 2009-04-14
As always on this topic, it is all opinion, but here you go......

Gnome:  Simple, elegant, easy to use, easy to customize, attractive GTK default apps. Looks very professional.

KDE:  Ugly, looks like designed for children's use, Ugly QT default apps, difficult to use and customise.  Horrible Konquorer (though I believe many use Dolphin now for file manager).

XFCE:  Simple and attractive, looks very professional.  Some issues with use, eg difficult menu editor, and sometimes task bars disappear for no apparent reason (rare).  Default apps are lightweight, but functional.  Can be lighter on resources than others.

LXDE: Attractive and Lightweight, but still some issues when installed on a system with another DE.  Works fine when installed by itself.

ROX:  Can make a nice desktop environment, but I hate Rox-filer.

### Post by ufugu on 2009-05-14
I'm a distro hopper and a DE shopper, I try everything I can get my hands on, and for no good reason...

What I'm starting to realize is that what really matters to me is not the desktop environment but the applications I use every day.  Any any of those applications will run on most any DE these days, so all I want from the DE is speed, good looks, and to stay out of my way. 

For example, the new KDE is so cool and looks great, but it doesn't help me run GIMP or Firefox any better. It's not like we sit at our computers and stare at the desktop!

I currently favor Xfce because it's so zippy and it has my favorite collection of panel applets.  The weather, mail checker, the dual row launcher, all the clocks, iconbox, system monitors for the panel all look and work better for me, plus it can run Gnome panel applets too.  I haven't found any features missing that I actually need or want.

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-05-21
Always looking for the lightweight stuff for low resources hardware (or just to take it easy on new hardware), I experimented with [LXDE]("http://lxde.org"), by far the most lightweight desktop environment yet. It's super simple (and it plays nice with Ubuntu if you let PCMan - the built-in file manager - manage the desktop). It looks like WinXP.

Applications are found in */usr/share/applications* and appear as icons! Just drag and drop the ones you want to your desktop.

Wallpapers can be added to */usr/share/lxde/wallpapers* and chosen from the Edit>Preferences>Desktop menu in PCMan.

Very lightweight, speedy, and "Windows-newbie" friendly.
