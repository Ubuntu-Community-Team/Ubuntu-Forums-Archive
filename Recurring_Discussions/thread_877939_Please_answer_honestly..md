### Post by NightCrawler03X on 2008-09-06
My favourite distro is actually ArchLinux --> [http://archlinux.org](http://archlinux.org)

It's extremely lightweight, and extremely non-user-friendly. It installs only the bare bones; kernel, some libraries, a package manager, shell, etc, etc, etc. A clean install of ArchLinux could easily run on older machines... with plenty of resources to spare. After installing, you are free to install *only* what you want, and nothing else. I could take hours to explain how I've set up my system, but basically Linux+Xorg+OSS4+Fluxbox. My system uses about 20mb of ram when not running X, and about 50mb of ram when running X. I have everything I need; GNU compilers, web browsers, text editor, media player, office suite, etc..

From my description, it may sound as if Arch isn't as good as other distros; it's actually better. In general, the software packages you get through it's package manager (pacman) are fully up-to-date, the drivers you get through it, are also up to date... the system is generally very secure, fast, and stable. I wouldn't even called Arch a "distro". The default install is the distro, but the system is so extremely customizable that after a day or two of tweaking, your system has absolute no resemblance to other people's archlinux installations. Well, to understand more, go to their website, and also, read [http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Arch_Linux](http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Arch_Linux) and [http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/The_Arch_Way](http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/The_Arch_Way), and then, download and install it --> [http://www.archlinux.org/download/](http://www.archlinux.org/download/)

You ubuntu users may be used to your system doing everything automatically, and when user-input is required, you may be used to ubuntu giving you a nice graphic interface. ArchLinux doesn't do anything for you automatically, and assumes that you are able to manually edit the associated text configurations. This may sound harder, and in fact it is at first, but when you get used to it, you find that you have a much more reliable system, because you have complete and total control.
