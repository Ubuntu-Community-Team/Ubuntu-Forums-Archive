So any way. Likes:
[LIST]
[*]No malware of any kind.
[*]Package management.
[*]No fragmentation issues.
[*]No registry bloat or slowdown due to having too many applications installed, or limited disk space.
[*]Better performance on older hardware.
[*]Updating updates *everything*.
[*]Endless customization options, if you want them.
[*]Freedom!
[*]Almost everything is free-beer.
[*]Lack of reboots.
[*]Has tonnes of stuff installed out of the box.
[*]Multiple workspaces.
[*]Support is easy to find if you know where to look.
[/LIST]

And the things I don't like.
[LIST]
[*]Not all modern hardware works with it.
[*]hardware manufacturers are loathe to open their specs so the community can build drivers.
[*]I have to think about things before I buy peripherals.
[*]I don't play many games. But it would be nice if there were more of them available.
[/LIST]

### Post by chessnerd on 2009-07-02
Pros: OS customization (both visually and under the hood), virtually no viruses or malware, good community-based help (thanks for a lot of help Ubuntu Forums :)), a distro for every need, Live CDs to test distros without installing, progresses more quickly than Windows or OS X, and most importantly, it's free to use

Cons: doesn't work natively with most games and professional applications, can't find any good system cleanup applications (such as CCleaner), problems (both major and minor) come up a lot more than in Windows, hardware compatibility problems when new hardware is added, and the biggest disadvantage is that there is no System Restore/Time Capsule. Because of the afformentioned problems I need to mess around under the hood in Linux much more than in Windows so System Restore would be an important feature for me
