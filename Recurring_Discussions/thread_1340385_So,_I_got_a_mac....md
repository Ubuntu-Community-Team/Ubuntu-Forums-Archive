### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-11-28
> **bedhead75 said:**
> Just curious as I've never owned a Mac, but why does something like this need to be done on a Mac OS but not in Ubuntu?  Like...what could possibly "corrupt" the permissions when running a Mac?

Apple's engineers use hacks to get around the Unix-like security system, rather than working within its framework. This causes the underlying permissions to get messed up, and that's why you need to repair them all the time.

Programmers on Linux always work with the system, rather than against it, which is why no "repair permissions" needs to be done on Linux.

### Post by handy on 2009-11-28
I've had a 2007 24" iMac for 2 years. During which I had the optical drive replaced under warranty, as it used to vibrate noisily from time to time. The other problem is the headphone jack stopped working, whether it was my fault or not I don't know.  I haven't bothered to look at it internally to see if I can fix it.

I fairly recently pulled the plasti-glass screen off, removed the 23 screws, (all but one are quite small T-6 & T-9 torx) to get at the HDD, removed it, cloned it onto a 1.5TB WD Green drive using Clonezilla, & reinstalled it & everything worked like a dream.

I should mention I rarely ever use OS X, I personally prefer to use Arch on the iMac, as it gives me control over everything.  There are quite a few things that I can't change, or change to my satisfaction in OS X; most especially the size of all of the fonts.  Some fonts are limited to a max' size of 16 (from memory), others can't be changed at all.  I find this a real pain, when running a 1900x1200 24" screen.

Anyway, as far as the hardware goes, I'm not complaining about the noisy optical, it happens, or the headphone, as was just as likely me being clumsy.  I do wish that they had of built the thing so we could upgrade the GPU, like some notebooks will allow.

My machine has been on & working for most of its 2 years of life & I'm really quite happy with it.

That said, I doubt I'll buy another Apple in the future, as I do like to build them & upgrade them further than any Apple I could afford will allow me.

The iMac really is a great use of space in my cramped office though. :)

Oh! I just remembered two things that I REALLY don't like, that came with the iMac was the horrible keyboard that offers no touch/feel, & the miserable mighty mouse. Worst mouse I have ever used bar none.  The mouse lasted about two hours before I threw it into a drawer, replacing it with my old faithful Intellimouse Explorer.  The keyboard stays plugged in (out of the way) for the very rare & strange sets of circumstances when I need to get optical media out with its button. I've used a few standard type USB keyboards that I've had laying around here; keyboards that let me touch type easily, allowing me to feel where I am on the keyboard.  Just a personal quirk I'm sure. :)

### Post by bashveank on 2009-11-29
My main OS went from Windows, to Debian, to Ubuntu, to OS X, and I have had an unequivocally better experience on my MacBook Pro than any other machine.

As far as general tips go, you can install QuickTime 7 on Snow Leopard by either using the Additional Software package from the install disk, or by trying to open an unsupported audio/video file in QuickTime X (.qtz files work well, just search for them, you have plenty in your Library folder)

Also, you can find some crazy deals on software, even free software on [MacHeist]("http://www.macheist.com/"). These deals are literally unbelievable. Last MacHeist, they sold $900 worth of legitimate, high profile, very well done Mac software, for $40. This is not a store website though, to get the best deals, you have to be involved, as the deals are usually pretty short-lived, and sometimes you have to solve puzzles to get them. A few weeks ago they gave away $150 worth of software for free, but only for 5 days. A week before the giveaway went live, they gave away another $20 app, but only if you could decipher coordinates, based on seemingly innocuous beeps coming from the home page.


Finally, some of my favorite Mac software include:
> 
[**1Password**]("http://agilewebsolutions.com/products/1Password"): Keeps an encrypted database with all your passwords and anything else you choose to store in it. Also, it hooks into your browser so that you can log into all your websites with your master password. $40, but for the amount of polish that went into this thing, it's well worth it.

[**Aperture**]("http://www.apple.com/aperture/"): As a very avid photographer, iPhoto doesn't cut it for me. Non-destructive edits on RAWs, very straightforward interface, great plugin ecosystem. $200, competes with [**Adobe Lightroom**]("http://www.adobe.com/products/photoshoplightroom/") for $300

[**AppFresh**]("http://metaquark.de/appfresh/"): Automatically updates all of your third party software
[**Audio Hijack**]("http://www.rogueamoeba.com/audiohijack/"): Pull the audio from any application and record it into a high quality file.

[**Awaken**]("http://www.embraceware.com/awaken/"): Very polished alarm clock app. $15, but worth it. [**Free knock-offs**]("http://www.google.com/search?client=safari&rls=en&q=free+mac+alarm+clock&ie=UTF-8&oe=UTF-8") abound.

[**Burn**]("http://burn-osx.sourceforge.net/Pages/English/home.html"): Very well done disc burning application.

[**Coda**]("http://www.panic.com/coda/"): All in one HTML editor, CSS editor with live previews, and automatic FTP uploader. $100, but as a designer, there's nothing like it.

[**DaisyDisk**]("http://www.daisydiskapp.com/"): Very nice visualization of your used disk space. $20, but unlimited-use trial.

[**Dropbox**]("https://www.dropbox.com/"): Free 2GB of space to sync files automatically between OS X, Windows, Linux, and supported mobile devices.

[Evernote]("http://www.evernote.com/"): Sync your notes between the web, your desktop clients, and any supported mobile devices.

[**FotoMagico**]("http://www.boinx.com/fotomagico/overview/"): Very easy to use photo-presentation creator. I use this every single time I have to create an advertisement reel of my photography. $30, easily justifiable if you use it as much as I do.

[**HandBrake**]("http://handbrake.fr/"): Rip DVDs very easily.

[**Illustrator**]("http://www.adobe.com/products/illustrator/"): Like I said, I'm a designer. If you're not, and you don't want to spend $600, [**Inkscape**]("http://www.inkscape.org/") will suffice.

[**iWork**]("http://www.apple.com/iwork/"): The best office suite I have ever used, ever. Very streamlined interface. Works seamlessly with MSOffice. $80 and well worth it. Although, you can use [**MSOffice**]("http://www.microsoft.com/mac/products/Office2008/default.mspx") if you must, for some God forsaken reason. If you really don't want to spend any money on it, [**OpenOffice**]("http://www.openoffice.org/") and [**NeoOffice**]("http://www.neooffice.org/neojava/en/index.php") work, I guess.

[**PathFinder**]("http://cocoatech.com/"): Same functionality as the built-in Finder, but with a ton of additional features and a bit of polish. $40, if those features are worth it, to you.

[**Perian**]("http://www.perian.org/"): Adds native support to pretty much every video/audio format to Quicktime 7.

[**Photoshop**]("http://www.adobe.com/products/photoshop/photoshop/"): Again, designer. If you're not, and you don't want to spend the $700, [**Pixelmator**]("http://www.pixelmator.com/") is really, really cool, and competes with Photoshop for only $60. [**Gimp**]("http://www.gimp.org/") sorta works if you really don't want to pay anything.

[**NetNewsWire**]("http://www.newsgator.com/INDIVIDUALS/NETNEWSWIRE/"): Very well done RSS reader. Syncs with Google Reader.

[**Smultron**]("http://smultron.sourceforge.net/"): My favorite text editor. Though, I'm told by coders that [**TextMate**]("http://macromates.com/") is better if you can spend some money on a text editor, and [**BBEdit**]("http://www.barebones.com/products/bbedit/") is pure awesome if you can spend even more.

[**The Unarchiver**]("http://wakaba.c3.cx/s/apps/unarchiver.html"): Adds native support for pretty much every archive format to OS X.

[**Things**]("http://culturedcode.com/things/"): The best GTD app I have ever used. Period. $50, but very well worth it for how polished and streamlined it is.

[**Transmission**]("http://www.transmissionbt.com/"): Very straight forward torrent downloader.

[**Transmit**]("http://www.panic.com/transmit/"): Pretty decent FTP client. Somewhat cheap at $30, but I think [Cyberduck]("http://cyberduck.ch/") gets the job done just as well for free.

[**VisualHub/AudialHub**]("http://www.techspansion.com/"): Very, very well done ffmpeg front end. Has been discontinued, but open sourced and picked up by multiple teams.
