### Post by k@e on 2008-08-21
I quit KDE in favor of GNOME mostly because Kicker crashed frequently and sometimes applications would not open at all although they appeared in the task list for a short time.
Kate/KWrite would not remember the window layout and I only could sort KMenu entries manually.

However, I do like KDE for the multitude of configuration possibilities and Konqueror as a far superior file manager than Nautilus. Oh, and Amarok would not immediately play an audio file when clicked it but it would be queued. A feature that I still heavily miss on GNOME.

Anyway, judging KDE by just having used Kubuntu is not a good idea since Ubuntu and most of the apps from its repos are focused towards GNOME.
