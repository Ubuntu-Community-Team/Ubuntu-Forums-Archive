### Post by mdsmedia on 2009-04-03
+1 to BDNiner.

I'd add that it's not only free, but also Free as in freedom.

You can copy it as many times as you like, put it on as many machines as you like.

You can change any hardware in your machine and not have to phone home to be authorised to use your OS with your machine.

I started using Ubuntu because I got sick of XP slowing my machine to a crawl when it was 6 months old. That may not have been XP's fault, as such, but I've used Ubuntu now for over 3 1/2 years and it actually gave my machine a new lease on life.

In Windows, if you want a program for a certain task, you have to google it and wade through the masses of freeware, shareware, and other paid for programs, and hope that the one you download, if you can find it, isn't laced with malware. In Ubuntu, you search Synaptic for a specific tool, install it, try it, uninstall it if you don't like it, and be very confident it won't include mal/spyware.

Every 6 months, in Ubuntu's case, there is a new version, with updated interface, new programs, tools, etc....all available for free. In Windows, lets say you're using XP, if you want a new version, you pay again, or are stuck with your current version. Yes, you can upgrade a lot of the programs as they become available, but programs such as Office will only be upgraded if you buy a new version.

For me, it's mainly a matter of freedom. Also the fact I can find tools and programs in the repositories that I couldn't find an alternative for on Windows. The world is your oyster in Ubuntu/Linux. In Windows, I felt stuck.

### Post by LowSky on 2009-04-03
Im have used Windows, Mac, and many flavors of Linux. For me Ubuntu was the best choice.
