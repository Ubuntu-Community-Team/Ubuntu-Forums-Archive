### Post by tjwoosta on 2009-07-17
Debian Stable, or Lenny, is focused on ultimate stability, they use the older most thoroughly tested stable versions of everything to ensure you have a stable server.  Debian testing, or Squeeze, is what many people would consider stable software. Debian unstable, or Sid, I believe is what Ubuntu is based off. It has all the more up to date software that still requires some testing before debians ready to send it down the line.

### Post by decoherence on 2009-07-17
Another big difference is that Ubuntu only officially supports x86 and x64, with ARM waiting in the wings and PowerPC dropped (officially) a couple of years ago.

Debian is released for 11 different processors, which is one reason the releases take as long as they do, as they try to achieve a certain level of parity between them.

From a practical standpoint, this means you can run an official Debian release on a non-Intel Mac (PowerPC or even 68k,) your SGI, DEC Alpha, Sun SPARC and other cool and froody architectures like that.
