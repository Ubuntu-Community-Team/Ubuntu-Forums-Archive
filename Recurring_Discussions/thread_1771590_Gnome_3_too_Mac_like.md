### Post by Anuovis on 2011-06-04
The real problem with Unity is that it is not based on you-see-you-click interface. A lot of usability comes from shortcuts which are easy to learn and use but hidden. 

In general, it lacks the good introductory tutorial to familiarize users with the new philosophy of doing things. This is the real flaw of Unity, you have to figure out a different environment all on your own.

Aside from what akandi said already, if you hold down the super key for a few moments without releasing, the panel will slide out with numbers/letters on each shortcut. This means you can bring it to the sreen any time. Also, if you hit super key+that symbol, it will automatically "click" that button for you. Super+2 will launch the second application from the top, super+t will open trash and so on. 

Another very nice thing is that it directs you to the workspace where the app is running. If you have Firefox on the 2nd workspace and LibreOffice writer on the 1st (where you are working now) and you click the Firefox shortcut on the panel, it will move you to the 2nd workspace and show the browser.

If you need to launch another instance of the same app, click the shortcut with a middle button, in that way you can get another Firefox window on the workspace you are at.

It's awesome.
