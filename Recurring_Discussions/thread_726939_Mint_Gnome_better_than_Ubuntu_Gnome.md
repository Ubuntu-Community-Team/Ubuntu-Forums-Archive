### Post by Limbic on 2008-03-17
I actually used Mint for a while and it's definitely nice.  I ended up switching back to Ubuntu, though, mostly because they decided to push a silly "Linux Minut" Google search screen on you; seemed just a bit counter to the idea of open source.  Other than that, though, it really is just a re-skinned Ubuntu that comes with most of the same codecs that are in the Ubuntu-restricted-drivers package in the Repositories.

On the skinning end of things, though, I'm actually extremely happy with the layout of my Desktop now.  If you want to try a new setup very easily, there's a nifty package called "Art Manager" in the Repositories that goes right into the Gnome Look servers and pulls out the different packages.  Even easier to use than the Gnome Look website (although not as complete) because you don't have to flip around through the pages.

I'm using the Glossy-P package and think it's great.  Sort of OS-Xish looking with silver windows and the little yellow/green/red buttons for minimizing and closing windows, but in the right hand corner.

### Post by fredbird67 on 2008-03-21
I beg to differ with the person who started this thread.  I tried Linux Mint here while back, and only 2 or 3 days after installing it, I began having problems with the "Quit" entry in the Gnome menu in that clicking on it did NOTHING -- I kid you not!  In order for me to be able to log off, shut down, what have you, I had to press Alt-F1 in order to get a popup menu on the desktop that had a functioning "Quit" entry, and I grew real tired of that real fast.  I therefore switched to Mint's Xfce edition, and I must say that I like how Mint's Xfce edition is laid out in a way that's familiar to Windows refugees.

However, another problem I had with Mint, both in the Gnome and Xfce editions, was that when typing a comment on a YouTube video, I couldn't type more than one letter a second without it interrupting the video.  And this was even on the Xfce edition, and even with me having 512 MB of RAM!

I later found a magazine article at work (I work as a reference and systems librarian, BTW) on how to instantly add all the multimedia codecs to Ubuntu in one shot, so when I found that, I went back to Ubuntu, armed with new knowledge that I didn't have before, and now, everything in Ubuntu, codecs included, works like a charm! :)

I've also learned how to fix that problem I had in Mint's Gnome edition, too, when I was playing around with the desktop for a couple of other accounts I have here on my Ubuntu box.  On the accounts for my wife and for guests, I've set those up to where the desktop resembles Windows XP, although I've kept the default Ubuntu layout for myself.

That said, I will agree that Mint does have a better, more inviting default look than Ubuntu.  I can live with the default Ubuntu GTK theme, but the default wallpaper in Gutsy is just plain butt-ugly, IMHO.  I think Ubuntu would do well to ditch the brown and go with a theme and wallpaper that features the yellow, orange, and red in the Ubuntu logo.
