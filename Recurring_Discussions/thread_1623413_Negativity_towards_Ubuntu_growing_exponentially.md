Other distros & systems that are both older & younger than Ubuntu also have great communities/forums.

What Ubuntu has is a very *large* community/forum.

The size is why so many of us that are a part of it (often we got our first true start with the Linux kernel based distros in this forum) are here. We keep coming back even though we may not have used Ubuntu for many years.  

The number of members of this forum, means that the posts are more varied than if the numbers were smaller. This in combination with the excellent moderation & near perfect CoC, keep this community, interesting for the many of us that no longer use Ubuntu. There exists a snowball effect in this regard.

Ubuntu *is* still just another Linux kernel based distro, built on the same foundations as all of the other distros. 

Personally I greatly preferred the community on these forums some years ago when it was much smaller & more intimate. There were still enough users to support diversity, the Other OS Talk sub-forum & the Backyard existed & hadn't yet become troublesome to maintain.

Enjoy the good times whilst ever they last, as communities are in a constant state of change.
