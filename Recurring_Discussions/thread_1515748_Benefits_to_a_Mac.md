### Post by kaldor on 2010-06-23
I love my MacBook. It runs Linux very nicely too.

If you just want a "get up and go" computer, get a Mac. No configuration. The default web browser (Safari 5) is amazing, openoffice is free (I don't like iWork), iLife is wonderful and I simply love the UI. 

It's also very Linux-like in that it is a UNIX-based OS. I wish people would stop bashing Macs, they're not crappy freedom stealers like the Linux/Windows fanboys want you to believe. My 1200 dollar MacBook is loads better than my HP notebook that's only slightly less powerful and bought for about 1000. You get a very good quality machine and a stable and solid OS. 

If you like messing around, don't get a Mac. If you want to get work done and not think about your computer, use a Mac.

I suggest either...

1) MacBook; entry level only 999 USD. Very portable - light, thin and has long battery life.
2) Mac Mini; no keyboard, mouse, monitor etc. Only about 700-800. Plug in your own hardware.

Run Ubuntu in a dualboot and you're good to go.
