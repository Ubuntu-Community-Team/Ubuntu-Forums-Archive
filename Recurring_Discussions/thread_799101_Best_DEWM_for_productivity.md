### Post by xer0kill on 2008-05-19
Thanks for the input, guys! Here's my personal observations of some of the most popular DE/WM's:

**Gnome:**
+ I'm used to it, so it's quicker to find what I need
+ Nice clean interface
+ Evolution Email rocks
:confused: Gnome loads pretty slow
:confused: No options for tiling/organizing windows (I know compiz-fuzion supports this somewhat, but it also slows down my 3d applications, and I hate having to switch WMs back and forth)
:confused: Scroll wheel does not switch between virtual desktops

**KDE:**
+ Loads faster than Gnome for me
+ More/better included Productivity Apps (KOrganizer, KAlarm with email notifications, etc)
+ Scroll wheel switches desktops!
+ Unclutter desktop (not tiling, but better than nothing)
+ Ability to store settings for individual windows
:confused: Don't like the way applications stack on top of each other in the taskbar. Makes it harder for me to find what I need. (I guess this can probably be adjusted.)

**Xfce:**
+ Fast! Lightweight, yet still very nice to look at.
+ Scroll to switch desktops
+ Store settings for individual windows (I think... can't remember for sure)
+ File manager (Thunar) is much simpler and nicer looking, IMO
:confused: Not as many productivity Apps included

**Wmii**
+ Makes efficient use of my 28" monitor
+ Incredibly fast
:confused: Feels like there's somewhat of a lack of freedom
:confused: Some things really are just easier/quicker to do with the mouse. Studies have indicated that keyboard-related tasks may "feel" faster because they are a higher level mental process than mousing, when really they are less efficient. Not only can they be slower, but they use brain power that could be dedicated to something else.
:confused: Can I even run the Nvidia driver in this? 

**My Conclusion:**
For now I've been sticking mostly with Gnome because that's what I'm used to. I was excited to hear that Gnome 3.0 will have tiling support! Who knows how long until 3.0 comes out, though? Either way, I'll probably still be using KOrganizer and KAlarm, as those apps simply kick ***.

If any of you guys have any suggestions for some good productivity apps/scripts/tweaks/etc, I'm all ears!
