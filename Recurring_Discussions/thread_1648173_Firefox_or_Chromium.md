### Post by Kalimol on 2010-12-19
So is Chromium, though.

I use the branded Chrome, with quite a few extensions installed, more than I ever used for Firefox - the bookmark tool mentioned above is a necessity, or in my case the Google Bookmarks extension, and the new "apps" category especially has some nice items, like an annotating screencap extension. Using the default skin, it's a minimalist interface, really making the best use of screen space, and of course it launches and navigates faster. I don't generally have more than six or seven tabs going at a time, but I encounter few problems on 1.6Ghz + 2G ram, a basic netbook.

Of course I have Chromium, Firefox 3, Firefox 4, and Epiphany installed, just to have a fallback for things. I almost exclusively use Epiphany for video, because it tends to be smoother and because Epiphany doesn't seem to get confused by nonstandard Flash items as readily as Chrome does.
