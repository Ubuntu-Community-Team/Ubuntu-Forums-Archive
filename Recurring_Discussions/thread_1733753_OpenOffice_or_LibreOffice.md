### Post by fuduntu on 2011-04-20
> **ajgreeny said:**
> But as I understood things, there are doubts about whether Oracle would keep OpenOffice free.  Apologies if I have got that wrong.

Certainly from my use of both suites, the main differences I have seen are simply the splash screen at startup of any of the apps, and the name in the title bar of the windows.  Everything else seems to be the same, even extensions for OO work in LO without problem.

It was a "the sky is falling" scenario, based on the same sort of fear uncertainty and doubt that is often used to herd Linux users that don't know better into doing the dirty work of other Linux users that should know better.

It was formed around how Oracle handled the MySQL transition, many of them (starting with the go-oo team) decided that they wanted to fork OpenOffice, and then demand Oracle's newly acquired trademarks.  Oracle told them to take a hike.

You see, forking the code is explicitly allowed under the license, but Oracle had no reason to give up their trademarks to a bunch of angry translators (and a half a dozen developers if memory serves).

Oracle did absolutely nothing wrong (in the case of OOo), and they aren't the bad guys, the LibreOffice team in this one particular scenario are.

I'm not saying Oracle didn't do anything wrong in other scenarios, just not in the case of OOo.  The whole fork was based on nothing more than Fear Uncertainty and Doubt.

/opinion
