### Post by webdesigncompanyla on 2012-10-18
I have been using Ubuntu since about Ubuntu 9 and it's worked great except for not being able to get my Apple products, scanner, and web cam to work.

I would love it if my iPhone and my iPod would work with Rythmbox and/or I could load iTunes.

Wine also needs to be improved.

But with the new switch to Unity it's making me switch back to Windows 7 or MacOSX so I can have a fully functioning computer again.

Buttttt, I tried out Gnome 3 (shell) and with some simple extensions I have to say that this works really great.

Think of the "Activities" more as a "Global Search" or "Global Menu"  and you can understand the concept better, because by default there are not any activities going on when you first boot up your computer so the name "Activities" is a bad choice.  Once you have that concept understanding it is easier.

I need a computer where  I can click on documents that are frequently used in one click.  This is where MAC OSX is superior because they designed OSX in a document centric concept.  Typing in the application only works for people who are left brained/analytical which is only about 10-25% of the population, most people want to point and click.  Also how are you going to find an application that you've never used and you don't know exists in a type based global search?  This was a huge mistake by Unity to make this the central part of their OS, huge mistake.  Don't try to re-invent the wheel.  Linux does not have the experts nor the resources in GUI development that Apple and Windows have.  Go with what people like which are task bars and docks and status bars.  People want to see what they want immediately and be able to click on it and get the information they want or make the update they want in one click or less.  People want a virtual desktop to place things on.  People do not want a tablet for their desktop.  Desktops and tablets are not same use devices.  Tablets are just fancy output devices(books), while desktops are computers for input/output.  They should not have the same GUI interface.  No exceptions.

Problems with Unity:  

1)  switching between open application is a nightmare, serious desktop users usually only have less than 5-10 windows open so there is no need to treat this like window overload and change what worked in Gnome 2.

2) switching between desktops is horrible, most people use different desktops for different activites, some which are private, there is no way to switch between a picture of naked lady, or a confidential image/document to the desktop that has your work, in essence desktops are like BOSS BUTTON features, Unity doesn't allow you to switch between desktops without showing the entire room your porn on the other desktop

3) there is no easy way to directly access a single document, power users want to click once and get to a text file or another document that is used 10 times each day, this is ABSOLUTELY NECESSARY TO BE ABLE TO PIN DOCUMENTS TO THE TASK BAR/DOCK

4) why have a DOCK with a bunch of empty space and a status bar that is 99% empty space that you can't put things on?  just remove the top status bar and put the time/date/on/off into the unity DOCK

5) put the important stuff first, make the important stuff easy to get to, in the Unity global search screen it starts with a blank page, it took me months to figure out that by clicking on the pinky size icons of crayons i could see the entirety of the applications  installed on my computer

6) make the DOCK on the bottom (only 1% of users want a dock on the side or top of the screen), or just make use of DOCKY, the Unity dock really sucks, some concepts, when i press on the icon for an application, i want it to open in the current window, not take me to another window where it was last used, for example, i am in desktop 1 looking at porn, and now i move to desktop 6 and i want to conduct some business research so i go to desktop 6 and i click on the icon for firefox in the Unity dock, what do I get?  I get immediately taken back to desktop 1 where my porn is open, WRONGGGG, it needs to just open the freaking application in one click, now I have to right click and select open new window to get a new window to open in desktop 6

7) fix the ALT-TAB between open windows, it totally sucks, i can't tell which icon is selected and then it's hard to cycle between open applications, Gnome 3 shell works fine in this regard

8) people have been using menus for thousands of years, we use them when we go out to eat, when we order anything we use a menu, when we read a book we go to the table of contents which is a menu, when we buy something we look at the sunday ads to get a sale price which is a menu, don't try to change human nature, stick with menus and only use the global search concept as a secondary feature

When in doubt just copy Windows and OSX concepts, they have years and years and millions and millions of dollars of A B testing and development behind them.  They are made that way for a reason, because they work best.

I'm using Gnome 3 shell for my desktop environment with the DASH on the left removed (dash is useless) and docky installed in 3d on the bottom of the screen with intellihide, I aslo installed the weather extension and the extension to put an icon in the top right for ON/OFF.  With Docky I can get to my frequently used files in one click and if I use the "Activities" feature by moving my mouse to the top left corner as a way to see which windows are open and to switch desktops (I can also switch desktops from Docky) but I can see what's on each desktop by click on "Activities" or swiping the mouse to the upper left corner.

I think in years to come once the iPad phase trend has passed Linux will come back to a more sensible idea on what  GUI should be.
