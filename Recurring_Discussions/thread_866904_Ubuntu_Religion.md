I always find it interesting when these discussions come up that people point out the Christian edition of things as a target.  No one seems to mention the fact that there is a muslim edition too.  No one seems to question it.  I supposes that fanatical, head lopping, suicidal jihadists are expected to have their own operating system, but more sensible Christians are not supposed to.  I wonder some times if islam were governed by the moderate faction that controlled its radicals if they would have the same criticism thrown at them.  Would ubuntu Jewish edition come under criticism?  What about Zoroastrian ubuntu or ubuntu Shinto edition?

The point is that I think it does no good to ask why there is a Christian ubuntu, as this thread seems to really be asking (though it uses the pretence of asking if **religious** ubuntu is necessary).

I personally do not understand why there is Suse, Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, Edubuntu, Gobuntu, OpenGEU, Redhat, Fedora, PCLinuxOS (my personal favourite by the way), Puppy, Slackware, Goblin X, Damn Small, Sabayon, Mint, Debian, Freespire, nUbuntu, Mandrake (Mandriva), OpenSuse, Arch, Simply Mepis, Gentoo......  Maybe you get the point now.

Why do we need so many linux distributions?  Never mind religious ones?

The answer is personal taste.  Moderate muslims deserve their own edition if that makes them happy.  Christians deserve an edition if it makes them happy.  For that matter Jew, Shinto, Taoist, Zoroastrianism....all deserve their own editions if they want them.  Why?  For the same reason someone deserves Edubuntu or Slackware or Knoppix.  To question why a Christian or other religious edition without questioning why someone deserves a Kubuntu while someone else deserved to have a PCLinuxOS or a Suse, or a Mint or a Debian, is a sign of prejudicial thinking.  An anti religious bias that irritates the mind and prevents someone from answering or even asking the larger question or seeing the context wherein their own question is contained.

Religion is not the issue.  The question is not why is there a Christian or other religious ubuntu.  That question is just a part of the larger question "why are there so many linux distributions?"  The answer is no distribution is to the taste of everyone.  In a world of so much choice and opportunity to create variety, with not everyone liking the same thing, the logical conclusion is the creation of variety.

To see a larger example ask why is there variety in any thing?  George Carlin (God rest his soul) once said that "any activity engaged in by more than 4 people has a magazine devoted to it."  That may be true.  The same pattern has happened in magazines and it will happen any time there is the opportunity to create choice.
