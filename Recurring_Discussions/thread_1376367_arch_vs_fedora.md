### Post by Simian Man on 2010-01-14
I like both.  They both try to stick with unmolested upstream sources - unlike Debian.  I like this because, if I have a bug, I can report it to the original project rather than the sometimes inept packagers.

Also they both push updates when the developers release them rather than when they are deemed "stable".  I think the whole "only old stuff is stable" mentality is a falsehood perpetuated by Debian packagers that introduce bugs with their modifications.

On the whole I usually prefer Fedora because I like a nice out of the box experience.
