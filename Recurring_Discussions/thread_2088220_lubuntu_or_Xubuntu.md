### Post by Artemis3 on 2012-11-26
With 512MiB of ram or more, you can safely use Xubuntu.

Tip for beautifying XFCE (and other DE/WM): Some GTK themes (eg. in xfce-look.org) can include gtk2 and gtk3, but most only include gtk2.

It is not easy to tell, you have to download the theme and inspect its contents; sometimes the description says so.

If your theme is gtk2 only, some apps will look real ugly (file-roller, nautilus, audacious, etc).

Xubuntu uses greybird, a grayish boring looking theme, but this theme has both gtk2 and gtk3 specs. Another example would be Plasma Shock or oxybird. Note: these might need additional gtk2 and gtk3 engines...

If your video driver is ok, you can turn on compositing for shadows and transparency.
