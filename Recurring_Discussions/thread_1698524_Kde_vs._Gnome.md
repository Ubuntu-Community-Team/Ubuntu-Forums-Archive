### Post by StephanG on 2011-03-08
Well, let me start by saying that my laptop is running Ubuntu, and my Desktop PC, is running Kubuntu.

Personally, I find that Kubuntu takes a hell of a lot less time to setup than Ubuntu.  But I guess that's just because KDE works like I expect it to, while I have to change a lot of settings with Ubuntu.

For example:  I like Yakuake.  It provides a terminal that's out of the way, until I press F12, and then it drops down.  When I restart my PC, its already running!  With Ubuntu, I discovered that there's a similar app called Guake.  But with Guake, I first have to go into the settings menu, and tell Ubuntu that it should start at boot.  (And setting Guake's width, is not even an option.)

Another example:  Ktorrent.  I click on a torrent and it begins the download.  I only use torrents for large files, and as a result, they take long to download.  So, I click on the 'X' at the top of the window.  The window dissappears and happily continues downloading from the System tray, completely out of mind until the torrent is finished.
          With Ubuntu, I click on a torrent, and it opens Transmission.  But as soon as I close the window it stops downloading!  So I either no progress is being made, or there is a tab in my taskbar that I have no intention of using.

I guess in the end it just feels as if Kubuntu knows what I want.  Whereas Ubuntu is seems to be guessing.  If I open something in Kubuntu, it stays open until I close it.  If I close something, it stays closed until I open it.  Whereas I always have to tell Ubuntu what I want to have started on start-up, and what not to start.

And, of course a big factor in my preferences is "The Folder View Widget".  Some love it, some hate it.  Personally, I fall into the former category.  Let me explain:    

The beauty of Folderview, is that it can point to ANY folder.  So, what I've done, is create two.  One that points to my Desktop, and one that points to my Home directory.  Its just seems far more functional and organized to me.  Now I don't have to open a file browser just to open a single file. (I've attached a screenshot to make my point.)

And then, the settings.

I have been using Ubuntu for a while now, but everytime I want to do something new, I first have to scan through all the menus and often still open a browser to google my problem.  With Kubuntu, all the settings are in one place.  And if that's not enough, there's a search bar that will show you where a certain option is.  If I can't find it there, KRunner will open the relevant window for me.  Just press Alt + F2, and type 'Samba' or 'Display' or 'Desktop Theme' and it opens the relevant window.

And in Ubuntu, I have to install themes the 'Windows' way.  Which is, open a browser, download the file, install, etc.  With Kubuntu, if I want a new Application Style, I go to relevant settings, click on "Get New Themes", and it will open a small window displaying available themes.  And with one click it will install it for me.

And the last major factor in my decision to choose KDE, is that there is always a choice.  I could find transparent styles for KDE (see second screenshot), but with Gnome I simply found a lot of webpages telling me how to make the panel transparent.

P.S.  I agree with Bigtime_Scrub on the default menu thing.  That's why I use Lancelot.  (Did I mention KDE has more choice?)  Its miles ahead of Kickoff, and light-years ahead of the Gnome menu.

P.P.S.  Sorry about the incredibly long post.  I guess I got a little carried away.
