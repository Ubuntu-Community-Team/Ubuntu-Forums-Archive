### Post by snowpine on 2009-11-06
To the OP, Arch is *exactly* what you're looking for (in my opinion)... definitely start there!

> **samjh said:**
> Sidux has no Gnome support?  That's interesting, considering how Debian was once very prejudiced toward Gnome and against KDE.

sidux is not Debian; sidux is an independent project, free to decide their own goals and priorities.

Last time it came up on the sidux forums, the answer was basically "if two committed developers step forward to develop and support it, sidux will gladly offer a Gnome edition." None of their current developers are Gnome fans, so they're not going to waste their time on something in which they have no personal interest.

Check out Debian Testing, too. It is also rolling release, not as "bleeding edge" as Sid/Unstable (roughly comparable to the current Ubuntu release) and Gnome is fine.

Arch or Debian Testing, those are my recommendations based on your criteria.
