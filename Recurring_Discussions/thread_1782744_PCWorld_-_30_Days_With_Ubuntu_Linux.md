### Post by earthpigg on 2011-06-17
Comment I made regarding the docx and odt file sizes on day 16:

> So, I opened an old resume in OO.o 3.2, saved a copy in docx and a copy in odt and tested scaling. selected all, and copied and pasted it 10 times, hit save.

docx went from 7kb to 9.3kb, a 32% increase in file size.
odt went from 39kb to 43kb, a 10% increase in file size.

I did it again, meaning this time each document would be 100 times larger if i printed.

docx went from 9.3kb to 28kb, a 300% increase in size.
odt went from 43kb to 51kb, an 18% increase in size.

Here is the tentative conclusion I am coming to, and I'm going to whip out my Econ 101 skillz to do it:

The "flat cost" of saving a document in .docx is much smaller, but "variable costs" of odt shine with larger and larger files.

Since OO.o can save and open both its own .docx and its own .odt files perfectly, the ideal solution would be to save small documents as .docx and large ones as .odt in order to leverage the comparative advantages of both.

A novel publishing company, for example, storing hundreds or thousands of novels, should use odt.

Who wants to try saving a copy of the Bible in both formats?

edit: one could probably come up with a linear function to determine when odt should be used, and when docx should be used, and then integrate that into LibreOffice... hrm.
