### Post by LunaticHiatus on 2009-12-01
The different browsers have different mentalities. As far as security goes, pretty much every browser with the exception of Internet explorer is good.

Firefox = a basic browser with the intention of expanding it yourself with add ons and extentions. Open Source. utilizes Gecko

Opera = a full featured browser, with the intention of you removing what you don't want. Closed Source. Utilizes Presto

Chrome = A very small browser. Open Source. Utilizes Webkit.

There are some others to try as well though. Such as Kasahakaze, Midori, Epiphany, and Konquoror.

Konqoror is lightweight, open source and KHTML based. Webkit is the result of people from the KDE team working with Apple to make the Safari Browser.

Midori is a light webkit based open source web browser that uses GTK. GTK is what Gnome is made with.

and Epiphany is a browser originally based on Gecko but later based on Webkit. They decided to drop Gecko since Minimalism was the overall idea. It is open source and developed by the Gnome Project.

kazehakase is an Open Source Gecko based web browser.
There are even more, but I figure thats enough and those are the most usable and well known.
