### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-02-17
Though my join date says I've been here since November of 2008, I've only been an active member for about a year.  Over that time I've contributed a fair number of postings, many of which contain detailed explanations of solutions in response to requests for help.  Often I'm rewarded by hearing that I've helped someone solve his or her problem, and I often expand my knowledge of Linux in the process.  However I'm writing today to voice my concern that more and more of my postings never get any response from the very people asking for help.

Every day I review my past few days' worth of postings to see if there are any responses I need to attend to.  Over the past few months I've seen a disturbing rise in the number of my postings in help threads which never get a reply.  In some threads I am the only respondent to a request for help, and the OP never appears in the thread again.  

I find this behavior rude and discourteous; it makes me less and less willing to invest the effort required to compose an accurate response to a technical question.  I can easily spend up to half an hour on a single posting making sure it is technically correct and written in comprehensible and grammatical English.  Many questions are sufficiently complex that they demand such efforts. Perhaps those asking the questions think they all have simple answers that we can jot down in a moment or two, but experienced people know that is hardly the case.  

I don't know what to do about this matter other than raise it as a concern and open it as a topic for discussion.  The frequency of dropped help requests seems to have increased over the past few months.  If that trend continues, I will probably find it much less rewarding to frequent the Ubuntu Forums.

### Post by Khayyam on 2012-03-08
Seiji, et al ...

I hadn't paid any attention to the Community Discussion forums until yesterday, I generally only read/post in General Help and Programming Talk, so I was pleased to have done so, as this is an issue I've had some reason to ponder over myself, and though there is no obvious solution, I think its worth hashing out.

Two points to begin with:

1). In my experience this phenomenon is particularly bad on Ubuntu Forums. I have had a long history on various support forums, mailing lists, and the like, and in my short (and therefore limited) time here, have been far more aware of it than I had previously. This may be entirely subjective, the forums may be no better or worse than others in this regard, so this can be taken as a subjective observation on my part.

2). As has been pointed out, this is not about "pats on the back" or "recogniton". A community (in any meaningful sense of the term) requires there be exchange in both directions, it requires there be "interaction". The latin '*communitas*' is derived from two words "together" and "gift", meaning, quite literally, *a coming together in exchange*. Of course, this can't be **made** to happen, otherwise no "gift" is involved, but its the very thing that consititutes a "community". So, it not a question of being rewarded, but of what is required to constitute and sustain a community.

When people contribute, even if if that contribution is no more than an acknowlegement that "it worked", then the engine that drives the whole machine is provided with a certain momentum, when that contribution is lacking then the process is short-circuted and momentum stalls. If the ratio of input/output falls to a level where there is more taken out than put in you have entropy, and entropic systems either require more inputs (fresh blood) or collapse.

Anyone who contributes here has a vested interest in the thermodynamics of the system, its not a question of balancing checkbooks, or having ones contribution recognised, its simply a necessary component of the community they contribute (gift) to.

In my case I noticed that rarely would there be a response to a post, **unless** there was an additional question, most threads are simply left hanging. On one occasion I aksed a poster (a regular poster who's posted upwards of 800 posts) why they left threads hanging as they did, and on another occasion asked the same poster why I should bother answering given that once they have their solution they move on to the next question, they have yet to provided an answer and so I nolonnger read or post in any of their threads. Its not that I feel dis'ed but I refuse to reward behavior that is detrimental to "community". If they want to suck up the gift of community without contributing in even the most minimal of ways then they can suck it 'till its bone. The difference between me and them is that wherever I go community follows, where they go community retreats.

Anyhow, thats about me for this post .. +1 for rasing this.
