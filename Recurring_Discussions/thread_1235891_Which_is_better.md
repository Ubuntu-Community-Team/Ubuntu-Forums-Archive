### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-08-09
The answer is "Whichever is best for *you* is best."

It just depends on what you want to use your 'puter for and what you want it to do and to look like.

Y'want lots and lots of pretty eye candy and versatile, time-tested applications? KDE.

A little less fancy eye candy but more functionality overall? Gnome.

Blazing, mind-bending speed with ultimate simplicity? LXDE.

Moderate-to-fast speed with simplicity? Xfce.

Minimalist with trans-warp velocity speed? OpenBox (no desktop environment at all, just the best window manager around).

Lightweight eye candy? E17.

All-out, rock-the-house, fancy schmancy special effects and show-off-it's -good-looks trophy desktop (and you have the RAM for it)? KDE with Compiz and Conky and all the trimmings.

It just depends on what you prefer. Ubuntu can be any one or all of those things. That's one of the reasons it's so awesome!

-Robin
