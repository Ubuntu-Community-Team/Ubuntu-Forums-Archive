### Post by Paddy Landau on 2011-03-07
Apple has modified its license to prevent developers from using automated tools to convert flash programs into iPod, iPad and iPhone compatible apps. Therefore, flash developers have to write their app twice, once for flash and once for the iPxxx products.

The BBC article, *[Apple boss Steve Jobs explains ban on Flash]("http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/10092298")*, says Steve Jobs "described Flash as a closed system and said that it was bad for the  smartphone era because it did not support multi-touch systems and helped  drain battery power unnecessarily."

Hmm. The phrase "pot calling the kettle black" comes to mind. My wife has an iPad, and it is one of the most closed systems I've ever come across.
