### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-07-11
Chromium's open source. People look at it, not just Google. They're giving people as much power as they can to reassure them that they mean no harm, other than keeping their non-intrusive ads around to stay afloat.

And if that's not good enough for you, there are plenty of other webkit-based browsers out there to choose from. Seriously, the paranoia in this community is killing me. It is a valid question worth consideration. However, I find no evidence to assure anyone that Google wants to steal your spleen, your browser, your identity, etc. It may find some information you enter into Google useful to make their services more relatable to you, but that's entirely up to you to decide. I haven't found an incidence in which something Google intentionally did personally hurt an individual.

We fear big, money-filled, powerful things and question their authority automatically, partly because the vastness of their reign is so unfathomable. But Google's just a company that people have found reliable and innovative. I think Google's a good thing- I'd hate to imagine where we'd be if someone else were the popular one, like Yahoo, Bing, or AskJeeves.
