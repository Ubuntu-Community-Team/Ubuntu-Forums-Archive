### Post by Dragonbite on 2011-04-12
Yes I prefer Ubuntu or Fedora. I just won't tell you which one ;)

Actually, Ubuntu LTS is running on my home (family) desktop because [LIST]
[*]It is supported for 3 years so I don't have to worry about updating it anytime soon so I can stay "current"
[*]The PPAs ensure I can get updated applications even though I'm using a 2010 release.
[*]More 3rd party applications that are not in a PPA is packaged for Ubuntu than Fedora (in my experiences)
[*]I haven't HAD to use the command line, and neither does my family.
[*]Is easier to set-up, maintain and configure.
[/LIST]

At the same time, I have Fedora on my laptop because it is the only distro that handles my Intel video (giving me desktop effects, video and handles external monitors) as well as detecting my Broadcom wireless card out-of-the-box!  An added bonus was when the KDE on my Fedora 13 was upgraded to KDE 4.5 without having to upgrade to Fedora 14!
