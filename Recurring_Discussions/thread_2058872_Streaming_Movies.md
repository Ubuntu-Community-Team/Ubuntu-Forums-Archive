### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-09-17
Amazon Instant Video uses Flash and will work with Linux, though for 12.04 and later you need to install the deprecated hal and libhal1.  Also Amazon does not support HD playback on computers; the best you'll get is 480p.

Hulu works since it uses Flash but drives me crazy with the amount of advertising required.  Hulu Plus is a subscription service, but it only buys you access on other devices like smartphones.  Subscribers still have to watch ads.  This makes no sense to me.  I can't imagine that the average Hulu viewer generates more than $10/month in advertising revenues given how tiny web advertising returns are.  Why not just let us subscribe and have ad-free service?  

(Well, to be honest, it might make sense because the studios and networks that own Hulu do not want to annoy their cable television partners like Comcast.  If people can subscribe to Hulu for $10/month and get ad-free content, the incentive to buy expensive cable TV packages will decline.)

I watch anime on [Crunchyroll]("http://www.crunchyroll.com/").  For $7/month I get no ads and resolutions up to 1080p, though the source material usually tops out at 720p.

We've been hearing how HTML5 media streaming is just around the corner for at least two years now.  As far as I know only YouTube really supports HTML5; otherwise it's just lip-service.
