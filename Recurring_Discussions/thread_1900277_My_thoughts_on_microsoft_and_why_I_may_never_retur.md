### Post by guyver_dio on 2011-12-25
This is not another 'which OS is better' debate, we know all OS's have strengths and weaknesses but I think anyone could agree with a development team as large as microsoft has working on windows it should be much more tidy, fast and efficient that it has been and I want to explore the reason for that which is my main reason that I may never return to windows.

It's quite simple, microsoft make it abundantly clear that they're a business first and OS developer second. This I feel is the sole reason why windows will never be as good as it can be and can never be trusted (they need to revisit their priorities). The quality of their products will always take a back seat when it comes to maintaining market share and thus keeping the benjamins rolling in. I think vista was a good example of this. 

When the release candidates came out there was alot of positive feedback, they were good for release candidates but microsoft jumped the gun on development and released the final version which was really a release candidate at best (in fact I remember thinking the release candidates I tried were better than the final product), but then microsoft kept everyone onboard by promising a service pack to be released very soon. This I feel was a total business based decision. Vista needed to be released at the right time and the cost to the quality of the product didn't seem to phase them, it was strictly about getting people onboard first then keeping them there with the promise to fix the mistakes. 

Windows 7 was much more stable but I felt it wasn't that much of an improvement. They cared so much about eye candy they let other things slip. Vista and Win7 were both bloated, slow and bad at handling resources. It's just like when I create web applications, I'm the programmer and someone else is the visual designer. As the programmer I will almost never get any compliments, all the glory goes to the visual designer because unless the program is totally unusable the UI impacts general users the most. So all average pc users would think is that, oh it's a more modern OS so needing higher specs to run it makes sense. Windows could get away with that.

Now comes windows 8 which I think finally microsoft is putting the focus and effort where it should be which is probably not on purpose, it's probably due to the fact they decided to go with a single OS to suit both tablets and desktops so they needed it to run smoothly on a wide range of hardware (a lucky side effect which I think will redeem windows a little bit). The biggest complaints and aggression towards win8 is the UI, people are seeing that it looks like win7 with a crappy metro start menu. I wouldn't like that start menu on my desktop either but luckily you can switch it back to classic start menu very easily. Seeing at it looks like win7, people automatically think nothing really has changed. It's really all under the hood for win8 which is really what win7 needed. Boot times are a hell of a lot faster, much better resource management, can run on lower specs than win7. But I'd say don't buy into it just yet, remember that microsoft still see's themselves as a business first, the promising improvements that have been seen in the development previews may possibly take an ugly turn just as vista did.

My main concerns are the priorities of the company behind it and is the reason I don't want to support it or put my trust in it, even if win8 final is very stable and efficient. I probably still won't buy it for that reason.
