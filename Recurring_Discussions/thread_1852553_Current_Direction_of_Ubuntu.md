### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-09-30
LTS releases, beginning with 10.04, are built from Debian *Testing* rather than Debian *Unstable* (see [here]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LTS")) like the regular releases are. Thus the LTS releases are "arguably" a little more stable than the regular ones.

If KDE puts you off, there are other Ubuntu-based choices from Xubuntu and Lubuntu to a minimal Ubuntu install with any desktop environment you like - or none at all. Openbox (not a true desktop environment) is plenty awesome on a minimal install.

I couldn't agree more with this statement! It's right on target.

Without benchmarks and systems designed specifically for the OS's (under test), debating comparisons IMHO is a waste of time. Thus, one should limit themselves to comparing "versions" on their system(s). Even then, one should keep in mind that upgrades may include so-called improvements which might be considered beneficial by some, but not others.
