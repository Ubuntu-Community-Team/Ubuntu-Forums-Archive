### Post by dpny on 2011-09-29
> **whatthefunk said:**

In fact as far as Im concerned a tablet is 100% useless. In the long run--the next decade or so--the desktop as we know it will be dead. Which is to say, that for the needs of your aver average computer user (web, mail, music, video, games, porn) a tablet is all they will need. For many users, this is already the case. Give the hardware one or two more generations and it will be even more true. There will always be people, like me, whose job requires them to have lots of processing power, memory and storage, but I think the desktop market will contract until that segment shrinks to a more realistic size.

Another way of looking at this is that we've reached a point where hardware keeps getting faster, but the majority of people's computing needs has reached equilibrium. If you have tablet hardware which adds something like Office--Word, Excel, Powerpoint--you've just satisfied a bug chunk of corporate use as well.

This isn't a new process. It happened to the original, floor-size computers when minicomputers came along. It happened to minicomputers when powerful workstations came along. It happened to workstations when commodity hardware become powerful enough. And it will happen to commodity desktop hardware when tablets mature.
