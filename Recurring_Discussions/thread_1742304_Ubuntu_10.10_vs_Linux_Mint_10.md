### Post by Zero Prime on 2011-04-28
I like Mint better.  Ubuntu is good, don't get me wrong, but a lot of work goes into de-bugging before the final Mint release.  As for configuring, you get all the same options as in Ubuntu.  Your not locked into anything.  The biggest difference is the update manager.  Potentially harmful updates are not installed by default until they are thoroughly tested.  I've had plenty of crashes with a standard Ubuntu update, never one with a Mint update.

### Post by false truths on 2011-04-29
Linux Mint is basically, at the core, Ubuntu. What makes it unique in the interface perspective is simply the Mint Menu. What really makes it nice is the pre-installation of commonly used restricted packages such as codecs, flash, and java. Basically, it's a compromise between the existing awesomeness of Ubuntu and the usability of a system with proprietary tools installed by default.
