### Post by balaknair on 2010-10-07
> **3rdalbum said:**
> Microsoft Security Essentials.

Seconded.
Pros:
It's low on system resources even though it has real time protection turned on, doesn't prolong system start up noticeably, pretty un-intrusive, easy to use and update. It also has a malware detection rate at par with the best of the paid-for AV suites(with low false negatives, false positives).
It also works well without trashing other apps(including security software) and doesn't trash your filesystem, unlike the Norton suite.
 
Cons:
Not very customizable/tweakable(it keeps the UI very simple though)
No firewall component
Scanning can be a little slow on some systems. 

All in all I'd say it's the AV app on Windows for home use(better even than the paid ones). The only 'drawback' is that you have to be using a legit copy of Windows to get MSE.


Personal recommendation:
MSE + Online Armor firewall(free).
Throw in MalwareBytes Free version for speedy on-demand scanning and to get rid of the rare rogue app that gets past MSE and Online Armor. And Secunia PSI to keep your software updated.
