### Post by BertN45 on 2011-04-13
The market share of the Linux distribution stays around 1% and there is no significant improvement. Making Ubuntu better will not really help. These type of fights are not decided on technical quality only. I have seen many good technical companies disappear, because of poor marketing or the lack political power. It is not about technical quality anymore, Ubuntu/Linux have surpassed Windows in that respect, both in internal quality and user experience.  
 

 The stagnation is caused by the lack of marketing and political power. First let us look at the market for desktops/notebooks/laptops to [SIZE=1]keep[/SIZE] it simple. The natural segments for Ubuntu/Linux are:
 
[LIST]
[*][SIZE=1]The group of people that 	understand technical quality and make the choice based on that.[/SIZE]
[*][SIZE=1] 	The people that do not like to pay for a Windows License.[/SIZE]
[*][SIZE=2][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Liberation Serif, Times New Roman, serif]The 	people that [/FONT][/COLOR][/SIZE][SIZE=1][SIZE=1]are not able to pay for a 	Windows License[/SIZE].[/SIZE]
[/LIST]
  The **first group** is basically the current set of Linux users and there is not much growth possible. There are only a limited number of people, who understand these issues and many of them are involved in Windows in some way and they will stay with Windows for that reasons.
 

 The **second group** exist, but is currently still relatively small, because most users do not even realize they have to pay for Windows, when buying a new PC. The costs are hidden at the moment. This is a political problem, it is simply unfair competition. The PC hardware and PC software must be unbundled and Windows should be priced separately.   
 

 The **third group** is huge, maybe not in the Western World, but surely in the developing world (90%). Here _Ubuntu/Linux is competing with another operating system that is available for free, the hacked versions of Windows._ The windows hackers hamper the deployment of Ubuntu/Linux. More and more the type of persons producing the hacks, move from the Robin Hood type  to the Al Capone type and probably foreign James Bonds.
 The one who should stop these hacks is Microsoft, but it is not really in their interest. Most of those users could not afford a Window license anyhow. More important for Microsoft is, that they stop the spread of  Linux. _Sometimes stopping potential competition early, is more important then selling your own product_, especially if you are making enough profit already.  Tolerating these hacks is a passive form of dumping, dumping is an illegal business practice. It is also bad, because the unintended side effects are criminal activities (identity theft, Internet banking scams, etc.) and probably espionage.
 

 The stagnation of market share of Ubuntu/Linux is caused by unfair competition, so it is a political problem. What do you want for Linux:
 
[LIST]
[*]A system of the nerds by the nerds 	for the nerds or
[*]A system used by a larger 	community. In that case the open source movement, so everybody (you, 	me and the Linux companies) have to put pressure on the politicians 	for maybe a decade to stop this unfair competition.  	
[/LIST]
  Stop flogging yourself, you have a great system, good quality and a beautiful palette of choices for the  desktop (More is better :) ).  Push for a level playing field.
