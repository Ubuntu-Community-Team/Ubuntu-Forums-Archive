### Post by benfindlay on 2008-07-16
Hey, theres lots of different views on which flavour is best for what, but here's the basics (people may well have other veiws on this):

xubuntu is lightweight, so is ideal for older machines

ubuntu and kubuntu require more powerful computers due to more powerful desktop environments. I personally use ubuntu, purely because it's canonical's flagship version. Some people like kde, others like gnome, so choosing between ubuntu and kubuntu is very much an aesthetics issue (in my opinion anyway, there may well be functional differences that I am not aware of)

edubuntu is specifically aimed at schools and education

medibuntu is aimed at media, entertainment etc

ubuntustudio is aimed at people wanting to do digital music and multimedia work

gobuntu is ubuntu with purely free software (and thats free as in the FSF's view of what free means)

Your choice should be based purely on your needs. Also don't forget that you can run these different flavours together. For example by installing ubuntu, then installing the kubuntu-desktop packages will effectively give you ubuntu AND kubuntu without taking up the hard drive space of installing them separately (you choose which desktop to use when loggin in).

Hope this helps!
