I'm aware of the naming controversy, and I agree that the GNU project has made significant contributions to this OS. Typing out GNU/Linux is OK, but using it in casual conversation is just annoying, especially if you consider the fact that the slash character simply doesn't carry the same  meaning in my (and many other) languages as it does in English. Calling it GNU/Linux just confuses people ("You mean Linux?", "Oh, GNU is a distribution?"). Generally, if I'm talking to an audience familiar with the subject or trying to educate someone, I'll use the full form and explain the naming convention if needed. If I'm just casually answering the occasional "ooh, wobbly windows, what OS is that?", I'll just mutter something that sounds like "Linux" and move on.
