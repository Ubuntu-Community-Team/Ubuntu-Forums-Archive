I've only ever had to hold packages back once (due to Catalyst incompatibility with xorg-server versions beyond 1.6) in over 2 years of using Arch. This was incredibly easy to do by adding the following line to pacman.conf:

*IgnorePkg    = nerolinux kernel26 catalyst xf86-video-vesa xorg-server xf86-input-evdev xf86-input-keyboard xf86-input-mouse*

Nerolinux is also being ignored because I don't want to upgrade to the next version as 3.5 is fine by me.

My experience says that Arch isn't unstable & that when problems do occur they are incredibly easy to handle (once you know how), due to the inherent simplicity of the Arch way.
