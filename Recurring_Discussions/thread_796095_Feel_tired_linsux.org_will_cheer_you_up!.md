It may be satire, but the satire is not very well done. I think satire needs a couple things in order to be effective.

- Whether the satire is exaggeration or sarcasm, it needs a point. "Linux sucks" is a very broad and almost childish point.

- Satire needs a certain amount of self-awareness, and most importantly a hint that shows readers that things aren't to be taken literally. If that's not there, as is the case in most of linsux, then it could be interpreted as ignorance or bullheadedness as easily as it could be interpreted as satire.

- Satire is most effective when it's witty. It needs some element of humor to draw people in and make its point. This usually follows from the above point, where the "hint" is often some form of self-deprecation in sarcastic satire, and often something blatantly ridiculous, to the point of being unambiguously absurd, in exaggerated satire.

The other thing is that satire is most often used as a rhetorical weapon against those in power. When it's used by those in power (or by those who support those in power) against the powerless, it quickly becomes bullying. It's ostensibly saying "down with the people who are...already down." The Windows proponents on linsux are blasting an OS with 2% desktop market share, it reeks of insecurity and cowardice.
