Of course it doesn't. Firefox 3 is probably in feature freeze by now for the (much delayed, it was originally targeted for November **2007**) final release. Changes on the scale needed to pass Acid3 will be in Mozilla 2.0 (the foundations for Firefox 4) and won't be made until later on. The Firefox developers are currently focusing all of their efforts on getting Firefox 3 stable and out of the door. Acid3 can wait.

It has been pointed out that Acid3, like Acid2, is just a way of getting browser manufacturers to support some useful features that most are lacking. It's not a comprehensive test suite of anything, and the results of gaining Acid3 compliance are mostly bragging rights. The actual user experience isn't enhanced that much.
