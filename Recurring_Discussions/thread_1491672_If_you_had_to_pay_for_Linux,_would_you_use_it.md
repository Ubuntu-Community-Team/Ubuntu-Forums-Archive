Getting back on topic, I also have no idea what this has to do with the Linux kernel. Linux Torvalds himself said that using the GPL was the best thing he ever did with Linux:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel#Licensing_terms](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel#Licensing_terms)

Without it, people would not have been contributed to the Linux kernel as much as they had. This is evident when you consider the rate at which lines of code were contributed to the kernel before it was licensed under the GPL and the rate which lines of code were contributed to the kernel after it was licensed under the GPL. The fact that companies must make their improvements to the Linux kernel available to others had had a stacking effect, where the improvements one company made to the kernel made the kernel something attractive to another company, which then in turn made its own improvements.
