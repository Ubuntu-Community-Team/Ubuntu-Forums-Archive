### Post by silverglade00 on 2010-08-23
Probably the best place for you to look is [Distrowatch]("http://www.distrowatch.com").

Basically it comes down to different default programs to use, different desktop environments, different ways of maintaining your system, and different target uses.

For example, regular Ubuntu uses the GNOME desktop, uses .deb and apt for package management, and is meant for general desktop usage. Red Hat uses GNOME desktop and rpm packages with yum for management, and is meant more for server installs. Of course, you can use either one for the other purpose, but some things might be harder to configure or find support for.

### Post by TBABill on 2010-08-23
Distro hopping (moving from one distro to another) is fun in the beginning (and even now for some of us!) and you learn the quirks, capabilities, weaknesses, etc. of all of them. And you learn what you like in each to be able to choose whatever is best for 1) your equipment and 2) your tastes. They are NOT all the same, nor do they all use the same kernel (core). So where you may have great success in one distro, another may be a total failure for you.
 
Give some a shot and see what you like, what level of manual work you don't mind putting into it, see which work the fastest, smoothest and best with your machine(s). You may be surprised how vastly different a lot of the distros are to each other.
