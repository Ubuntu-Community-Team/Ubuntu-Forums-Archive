### Post by snowpine on 2010-11-22
Yes, Ubuntu absolutely is better than Debian *to the people who are Ubuntu's target market*.

Debian releases come about every 2 years. The creators of Ubuntu said "wouldn't it be neat if there was an operating system just like Debian, except it comes out every 6 months, so its users will always have the latest applications?"

So if you prefer Ubuntu's release cycle, then it is the right choice *for you*.

### Post by libssd on 2010-11-23
Linux Mint Debian Edition was released recently (currently, only 32-bit). [http://www.linuxmint.com/download_lmde.php](http://www.linuxmint.com/download_lmde.php)

LMDE combines the speed of Debian with a friendlier installation and package management UI, plus the Mint menu (of which I am not a fan). However, the updating process, is S L O W -- probably takes 3-5 times as long to install updates than Ubuntu or Mint 9.

LMDE is worth looking into if you want to experiment but don't have lots of experience or time to invest in learning Debian's quirks. I still prefer Ubuntu, but it's nice to have choices.
