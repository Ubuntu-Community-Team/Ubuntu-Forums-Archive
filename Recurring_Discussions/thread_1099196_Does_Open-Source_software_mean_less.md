### Post by Therion on 2009-03-18
> **Dekkon said:**
> When someone rants about a legitimate problem, people reply with rude comments such as "Why don't you ask for a refund?", or "you know the saying, You get what you pay for". 
That's a huge and unfair generalization.

A "rant", to use your word is, by definition, bombastic and emotional.  So it's to be expected that a certain percentage of the population are going to respond on the same emotional level; but let's be honest.  Those are the minority.  The vast, vast minority, so to speak.  Those responses stand out so starkly because the ARE such a poor reflection on the community as a whole.  Polite, helpful replies don't really stick in your mind the way a sharply worded, sarcastic "slap" does.  Find me 1 sarcastic response on the forum, like you refer to, and I'll find you 100 helpful, well-intentioned ones to match it every time.  Ubuntu users are people and sometimes people are irrational and contradictory.  It's part of the human condition.  

Further, I don't know where this idea came from that paying for something grants me a right or privilege to complain while using something that is offered for free not only does NOT grant the same right or priviledge of complaint but, further, *snuffs it out* entirely!  I'm sorry, but my right to voice my opinion on something IS mutually exclusive of what I paid for that particular something.

In the Open Source community it is, in my opinion, one the few tools we have improve what's available.  Rational feedback, even if negative, is essential for growth.  In the private sector you have profits and losses to motivate you to offer a better mouse trap.
