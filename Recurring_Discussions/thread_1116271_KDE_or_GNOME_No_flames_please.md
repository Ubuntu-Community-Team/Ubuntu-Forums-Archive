### Post by rarsa on 2010-09-02
I reached this thread by chance (searching for something else) but I have a comment here:

Most people seem to focus on the visual appeal of each of the Desktop Environments: Which one looks better.

Being this a matter of opinion, the argument will never be clearly settled (What to use, when).

The desktop environments provide more services than just the visual experience. Too bad that these services aren't always clear to the end user.

Having said that, here is my take:

Gnome seems better suited for getting out of the way and letting use your computer forgetting about the DE. [The Gnome libraries]("http://library.gnome.org/devel/references") are more geared for a single application development and are not too cohesive.

KDE seems to be geared to tinkerers, the QT and [other APIs]("http://api.kde.org/") focus more on application integration even [across the network]("http://nepomuk.semanticdesktop.org/xwiki/bin/view/Main1/"). There are more possibilities for great applications which are user/system/location aware.

So if you are using the computer for single user tasks then Gnome.

If you are part of a network of users that need interaction consider KDE 4.x. You may end up creating some network aware plasmoids that will make your life easier.

The initial looks are "irrelevant" as you can make both look however you want.
