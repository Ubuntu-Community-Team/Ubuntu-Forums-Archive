### Post by ankspo71 on 2010-02-03
Here' my top 10 list.
1 There are currently no viruses that effect Linux (dialers too I think).
2 The only real spyware threat is cookies, but firefox and it's addons can eliminate that.
3 Linux is invisible on the internet by default, without having to mess around with a firewall.
4 No need for an antivirus program, unless you share files with windows users or run a server.
5 By default, linux desktop has no inbound(?) traffic (which is safer) (see #3)
6 All Administrator (root) privileges are locked, making it harder for malware and hackers to do damage or break in.
7 we are not bound to a restricted license agreement.
8 it's free to use, modify, and distribute.
9 90% of the software is also free in the same ways
10. No more slow downs because of excess fragmentation.

Of course there are advantages to windows as well. Such as a larger number of game titles available for windows, and there are some sites that don't support Linux yet, such as netflix. I liked the graphics drivers and sound card drivers better in windows too, but it's not too big of a difference. Other than that, I'm quite happy with Linux.
Hope this helps.
