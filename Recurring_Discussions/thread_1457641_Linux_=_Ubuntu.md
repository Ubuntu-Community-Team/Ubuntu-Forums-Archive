I guess things have changed, though.

Because it seems to me that one simple web search on the word "linux" would be enough for a person to realize that Ubuntu is only one of many Linux distros.  Are there lots of people who are being mislead?  Do any of you think this is a problem that Linux people should be concerned about?  Very interested to see any comments coming from these forums about this.

### Post by malspa on 2010-04-19
One of the things recently that had me thinking about this was Caitlyn Martin's article, "Ubuntu Is A Poor Standard Bearer For Linux," where she said, among other things:

"To whatever part of the general non-geek public is even aware of Linux the names "Linux" and "Ubuntu" are all but interchangeable. Over the past few years I've come to the conclusion that this state of affairs is, at best, unfortunate." -- [http://broadcast.oreilly.com/2010/04/ubuntu-is-a-poor-standard-bear.html](http://broadcast.oreilly.com/2010/04/ubuntu-is-a-poor-standard-bear.html)

And in the follow-up article to that, she says:

"I really believe that the reason many people think the names Ubuntu and Linux are interchangeable is almost solely due to marketing." She goes on, "Until the day comes when we can point to Ubuntu as a model Linux distribution I still believe it is incumbent on the wider Linux community to decouple the name Linux from Ubuntu." -- [http://broadcast.oreilly.com/2010/04/how-canonical-can-do-ubuntu-ri.html](http://broadcast.oreilly.com/2010/04/how-canonical-can-do-ubuntu-ri.html)

 
Or, something from a few years ago that I recently ran across:

"Recently I got to read more and more stories about Linux in general (at least that what it was insinuated by the name of the article) where term "Linux" is quite replaced by Ubuntu." -- [http://www.thetechandcents.com/2007/12/theres-more-to-linux-than-ubuntu.html](http://www.thetechandcents.com/2007/12/theres-more-to-linux-than-ubuntu.html)

Or, this one from 2008:

"Has anyone else noticed an increasing number of Linux newbies who seem to think that Ubuntu is Linux and Linux is Ubuntu?" -- [http://blogs.zdnet.com/hardware/?p=1641](http://blogs.zdnet.com/hardware/?p=1641)

Or, this blog:  [http://jonreagan.wordpress.com/2008/07/07/the-ubuntu-equals-linux-paradox/](http://jonreagan.wordpress.com/2008/07/07/the-ubuntu-equals-linux-paradox/)

Lots of other examples out there, other people discussing this, from different angles.  A few days ago, someone at another distro's forums mentioned being "bothered" by the idea that the two terms are synonymous.  That got me to thinking about how many times I've noticed other people talking about it, and I wondered what kind of reaction I might get if I brought it up here...

### Post by Bodsda on 2010-04-19
I think that this all boils down to peoples willing to learn. I have a compulsion to learn about the things I use, Computers, Motorbikes, Mobile phones etc. to the point where I am comfortable to indulge in a technical discussion about them.
 
When someone picks something off a shelf, presses a few buttons and starts using 'The Internet' then they don't learn anything. This is one of the reasons that I encourage the use of the command line, be it on Microsoft systems or on Linux machines. When using the command line, you learn what the graphical user interface is actually doing, and you soon will learn that not all shells will work the same.
 
I think that many of the old school Linux users may dislike Ubuntu due to its overly user friendly interfaces. Linux was a hobby for technical users, it soon grew to be a useful tool for the slightly competent user, but now with distro's like Ubuntu (Not slagging it off or anything) it has become a usefull tool for the unaware user.
 
I personally do not think that this is a good thing in general. I agree that everyone should be able to use it if they wish, but I also agree more with the philosophy of the more 'hardcore' distros.
 
Saying that Linux = Ubuntu is as false as NTOSKRNL.EXE = XP
 
I am not annoyed by new users making this mistake, but when technical authors or computer bloggers do it, it kind of grates on me. 
 
Bodsda
