### Post by KingTermite on 2008-07-23
I saw a post over on OS Talk about a new distribution coming out and it got me thinking about this topic.

What are some things that "take away" from Linux in your opinion? I don't mean this to be about bugs or new ideas or things like that. Things more in the general sense of how things are.


This is not meant to be a Linux bashing thread in any way. In fact, more the opposite. Some ideas that detract from Linux that we, as a community, could change to make it (GNU/Linux) better.

Some examples, here are some things that "take away" from my confidence or the popularity of Linux.



**- Too many distributions**

It seems to be utter chaos. I would much rather see one, two, maybe three max distributions with work going in to those. It seems good work is being taken away by groups who want to do simple changes and make a whole new distribution out of it. Having fewer distributions with those people working on enhancing that one distribution would be much more effective, I think.



**- Chaos of community**

This is kind of a different side, but possibly the same root cause. Because of all the distributions, it seems that you have communities that rally around distributions more than Linux itself.



**- Standards (or lack thereof)**

From package format to directory structure, things vary from distro to distro. It would really make things much more stable and easy to do "real work" (rather than fighting simple nuance differences) if more things were standardized. It's quite difficult to fight installing something that is in the wrong package format and not converting well, or that won't build because of a difference in environment variables or location of libraries.



Those were a few basic ideas. I know many won't agree with me, and there's nothing wrong with that. This is a two-way discussion, not me telling you what's wrong.

What do you think? Agree? Disagree? Have other issues worth mentioning?
