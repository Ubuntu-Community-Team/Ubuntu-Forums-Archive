### Post by aysiu on 2010-11-29
I've worked a lot with Macs, and my main computer is now a Macbook Pro.

I can tell you that the truth is a balance between the two sides that have been presented so far (1: Mac hardware is the same as non-Mac PC hardware; 2: Mac hardware is superior to non-Mac PC hardware).

The interior major components are *exactly the same* as other PC components. The hard drive isn't a superior hard drive. The RAM isn't superior RAM. The Intel processor is the same as other Intel processors. You're just as likely (if not likelier) to get a failed hard drive or failed optical drive on a Mac as you are on a non-Mac PC.

That doesn't mean the hardware is exactly the same, though, or that there aren't other ways in which Macs have a superior build.

For one thing, the MagSafe power cord is pure genius. I'm a total klutz and have tripped over and ruined many a power cord (including my netbook cord, which I had to buy a replacement for). If you trip on a Macbook or Macbook Pro cord, it just pops right out with no damage. I haven't seen any non-Mac laptops that have such a cord (maybe they exist, but I have not seen them ever).

Macbooks now (not the one I have, but mine is kind of old) have extremely long battery life (7-8 hours). Most non-Mac laptops have 2-3 hours of battery life **or** a huge bulge coming out of the bottom.

Macbook Pros have a very nice unibody casing that feels really solid and well-constructed. Not as many nooks and crannies for crumbs or cat hair to fall into.

The sleep light when Macbooks are in suspend-to-RAM has a gentle fading in and out instead of a hard blink on and off.

That said, some of the Mac niceties are missing if you don't use Mac OS X. I know this because I'm dual-booting my Macbook Pro right now. For example, if I'm using Mac OS X and lift the lid to wake my MBP from sleep, it wakes up *instantaneously*. If I'm using Ubuntu and lift the lid, it takes about five seconds to wake up. If I'm using Mac OS X, the wireless connects immediately upon resume from sleep. If I'm using Ubuntu, it takes anywhere between 10 and 25 seconds to reconnect.

So if you are planning to get a Mac only to install Linux on it, be aware you're getting it only for how nice the Mac looks. Not even all the hardware functions will function the same way if you're using Linux on a Mac (instead of using Mac OS X).
