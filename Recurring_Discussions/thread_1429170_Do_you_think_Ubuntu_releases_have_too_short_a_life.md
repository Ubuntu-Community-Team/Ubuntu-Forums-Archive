### Post by falconindy on 2010-03-13
The problem of "repos, repos, everywhere" (yes, I consider it a problem) would only be compounded by a longer release schedule. Understand that the only updates passed out during the life cycle of any given release are bug and/or security related. A 2 year release cycle means that mainstay applications such as Gnome, Firefox, OpenOffice, etc would all be up to 2 years behind the most recent major release. Worst of all would be your kernel lagging behind 2 years. This would result in more choice backporting by Canonical devs, resulting is the possibility of an even less stable system.

LTS releases aren't more stable. It's been stated on these forums before that the only difference is the length of time they are supported, hence the name. Don't expect the release of Lucid to be any more "stable" than the release of Karmic.
