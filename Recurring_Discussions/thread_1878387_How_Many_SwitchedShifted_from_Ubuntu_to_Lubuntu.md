### Post by jabema on 2011-11-09
I just switched to Lubuntu from Kubuntu.  I tried the xfce and lxde desktops on Kubuntu before I made the switch.  

My goal was to keep system resource usage to a minimum.  My linux machine is an old Dell Dimension 2400, 2.4Ghz p4, with 620mb of ram.  

The Kubuntu system with lxde running, used around 200mb of ram at idle and was running reasonable well.  After installing Lubuntu, the system feels even more svelte and idle ram usage is right around 100mb.  Absolutely mind blowing that a 10yr old computer with zero upgrades can still be a usable machine for web surfing and moderate usage.

After doing quite a bit of research I narrowed my chioces down to 3 distros.  Lubuntu, #!crunchbag lxde, and Linux Mint lxde.  Linux mint felt a little heavy for a lxde distro and #! was just a little too light.  Lubuntu fits a very nice middle ground for me, being light enough while still offering enough of the bigger desktop features.  

My hats off to the devs for putting out a nice product.  I see this distro getting installed on my laptop in the very near future as well.
