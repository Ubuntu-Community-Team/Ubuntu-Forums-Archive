### Post by tommcd on 2009-04-10
Well, you obviously had a reason to take the time to download Ubuntu and spend several days working with it. Nobody is forcing you to switch though. Use what you want.
Linux is free (as in freedom and mostly as in beer also) software. I have found linux to run faster and use fewer resources than Windows. Linux is also more secure. No need to worry about malware or DRM crap. You also have more control over how you configure your computer with linux than with Windows. These things are important to me, so I use linux.
There is a learning curve to using linux though; no question about it. I was totally cluless when I first started with Ubuntu. Rest assured, it is possible to learn this stuff. You will have to do some reading to get the most out of linux. Here is a good place to start:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/)
You can very likely solve your wireless problem, and any other problems you may be having, with some research.

And welcome to the Ubuntu forums!
