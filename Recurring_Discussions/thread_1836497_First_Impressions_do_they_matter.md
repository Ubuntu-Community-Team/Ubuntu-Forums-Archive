### Post by Off Shore on 2011-08-31
Do first impressions count?
I think they may be far more important than Canonical seem to believe.
Im talking about the mess Ubuntu users have to endure know as Plymouth. 
Sometimes it works. Sometimes it doesnt. The internet has numerous tutorials and fixes relating to Plymouth problems. Sometimes they are useful and sometimes they arent.
The problem has been with us since version 10.04 and Canonical has steadfastly refused to either fix it or remove it. Obviously its a very low priority.
However, non Ubuntu users will frequently witness the on screen mess when booting and either laugh or exclaim what the **** is that supposed to be!!
This is very disheartening if, like me, one is trying to demonstrate the benefits of Linux per se to youthful candidates !
I have personally spent hours on my installs trying to get a reaonable boot experience.
Sometimes I have sort of been successful other times I havent.
Even worst, upgrades to the kernel will frequently wipe out any progress made in taming Plymouth.
Its disheartening, it puts off new recruits and its there to remind you, every time you boot your computer, that maybe Ubuntu isnt quite the finished article.

So yes, I think first impressions do count and unfortunately Plymouth gives a negative first impression of Ubuntu.
Can anything be done to ensure everyone gets a reasonable boot experience ?
 ..after all USplash seemed to work well enough
