### Post by Nick_Jinn on 2010-08-28
A lot of governments and larger corporations help develop linux, so its not just a weekend hobby distro. Google and other search engines provide funding for Linux, so you ARE making money off of us. Cononical and the others also sell tech support, online services, and the user base is a testing ground for what goes into the servers. The popularity we create contributes to Linux profits in the server markets. Name recognition among distros has some degree of cross over into server popularity, though its not always proportional.....despite not being proportional, you cant say that name recognition doesnt help advertise one server over another.

People are making money off of us using Linux. Open source developers are usually paid more than than proprietary developers, surprisingly.

How do you get to be an admin without believing in Ubuntus functionality and potential? Many people already prefer it to Mac/Windows and the user base is growing, not shrinking. Its making huge inroads with smartphones and now tablets. 


That said, I dont object to there being a store for commercial software, but I dont think the distro should be based around it....I do NOT want a Lindows type experience where the whole distro is a walking advertisement for paid software....it should be in the backround, easy to ignore, primarily based on free software but you can OPTIONALLY support a few indie developers and pay a small bonus that goes back into the community.


I think that the ideal model would be linux commercial versions of linux....Like Elive costs money to download, but somebody else could still copy your work and make a free version....but some things can be owned.

That way we wont have an important piece of Linux owned by some developer, where we can always make a free version of the commercial alternatives, but it still gives the developer a chance to make some money by 'selling' their apps rather than waiting for donations. If somebody really needs a free version they can rebrand it and make a new GUI for it. 

I think a lot of people would choose to purchase from the original developers. They might not make as much as they would if it was proprietary, but it would still put money in their pockets as an incentive to develop.


It should be something that is OFF-SITE though, maybe you can download a tool for browsing and downloading/installing apps. It should be something that is out there, but the distro should not be built around commercial apps.
