### Post by ian-weisser on 2016-11-10
Two different data sets; not comparable.
Insufficient information to draw statistically-valid conclusions.

One fully expects the number of searches for "Ubuntu 12.04" to be greater than for "Ubuntu 16.04" for a number of reasons.

Here's my data: In 2012 I took a straw poll of my local Linux User Group - about 40% used Ubuntu daily. In 2016, I repeated the poll - about 80% used Ubuntu daily.

### Post by ian-weisser on 2016-11-10
"Is Ubuntu on the correct path?" is certainly a worthwhile question to ask...and is already regularly discussed in this and other forums.

Your insight is welcome, and your data has been discussed before.

People have been claiming that some other distro will supplant Ubuntu for a decade. Maybe one will.

Remember that Ubuntu's goal is NOT to be the world's most popular Linux distro.
Ubuntu's goal is to be a (1) multiplatform OS that is (2) free in every sense, (3) sustainable, and (4) comparable in quality to paid-for systems.
You are, of course, welcome to help us pursue that goal. We need the most help in (3) right now.
