### Post by rg4w on 2010-05-12
I find netbooks a bit cramped for developing, and depending on the IDE you're using it may or may not work well with UNR's method of maximizing windows by default to put the title bar in the top panel.  But for small tasks working within the WSVGA limits has been acceptable for me, and I much prefer the good use of space in UNR over the desktop version.

### Post by cariboo on 2010-05-14
If you are actually going to be compiling programs and creating packages, I would suggest something with a better cpu, as the atom is a bit weak in the knees for heavy lifting. :)

As an example, I was transcoding some video files, I did one DVD on an atom powered system and another on my regular system, the atom powered system took 5.5 hours, while the desktop system took just over an hour for the same dvd.
