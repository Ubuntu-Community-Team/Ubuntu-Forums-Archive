### Post by mfox on 2009-11-26
I have heard good things about the Dell mini 9, and one member of my local Linux group has it and is very happy with it.  The wireless range is very good; much better than that of my MSI Wind.  I'm less familiar with the Dell mini 10, but I would think it is good as well.

Regarding version of Linux to put on it, I think that UNR 9.10 is an excellent choice.  I have it, along with another Ubuntu derivative (#!CrunchBang 9.04) on my netbook and I really like both.  CrunchBang is more lightweight as it uses openBox in place of Gnome.  I find it to be fast and very attractive, but a new user might prefer the bells and whistles that come with Gnome.

### Post by ricegf on 2009-12-02
I've also heard good things on the Dells, though they tend to come with older versions of Ubuntu. I'm quite happy with my System76 Starling - see my extensive review at [http://tr.im/wYOg](http://tr.im/wYOg) - which includes a 160 GB disk but still has excellent battery life. You might also look at products from ZaReason, another Ubuntu specialty company with a good reputation.

Unless you're a developer who wants to target Moblin, I'd personally stay with NBR. It's very stable and production quality. I'm very pleased with it.

Unless you're ONLY interested in web browsing and web apps, you'll be disappointed by Chrome. It is very much a thin client, appliance OS. You can't install local apps, store local files, or otherwise do "traditional" computer operations. If you live entirely in the cloud, though - e.g., Gmail, Google Docs, Facebook, Twitter, etc. - and don't want to spend any time managing your local computer, it's perfect.

Hope this helps.
