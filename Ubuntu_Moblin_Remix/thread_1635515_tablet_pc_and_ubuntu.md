### Post by akand074 on 2010-12-01
I have the HP Touchsmart tx2, its the series they made right before the tm2. HP's touchsmarts are about the best/cheapest consumer tablet PCs. Right now in Ubuntu I have single touch and stylus working right out of the box. There is no multi-touch (or else there is no software written to support multi-touch), and screen rotation does not work. Though those can be manually set up, there are threads in this forum about it. Touch is still quite a bit better in Windows, I also have 4 touch points with multi-touch manipulation (not just gestures). Windows itself is designed well for touch, as in I can use my finger to scroll through folders/browsers and everything and it will know when I'm trying to scroll or trying to click, Ubuntu does not have that yet. 

In conclusion, if all your going to be using touch for is taking notes, well then its just fine. It's just a matter of the novelty of it that will be diminished quite a bit, though you get over it after a while.
