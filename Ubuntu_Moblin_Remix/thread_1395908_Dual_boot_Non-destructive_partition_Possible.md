### Post by oldfred on 2010-02-01
Most recovery disks are just to reformat your entire hard drive to the state just as purchased. It will ease everything including all your data. There may also be another choice to do a backup. If you have a choice to make a CD or DVD recovery disk you should just in case you ever need it. It may not even be a full install but just a disk for repairs.

While different versions the process to shrink Win7 and install are the same. Many instructions do not say but you should boot into windows several times after shrinking the windows partition to make sure it boots. It often wants to run check disk or other repairs to verify the new size. It is better to use the windows tools  to resize the partition.
