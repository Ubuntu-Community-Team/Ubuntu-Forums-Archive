### Post by Aped on 2011-02-21
It isn't as simple as moving an existing Netbook subdistro onto the hardware. There are a number of obstacles to overcome, most notably 1. ARM architecture and 2. potential bootloader issues. 

[Someone on an Adam forum mentioned the same thing]("http://www.notionaddicts.com/forums/showthread.php/2328-Running-a-gentoo-Linux-dualboot-on-the-adam"), linking to some gentoo devs who have tried [getting gentoo onto tegra-based platforms]("http://dev.gentoo.org/~armin76/arm/tegra2/install.xml#doc_chap3"). 

It looks like a good place to start, but without some actual Ubuntu developer/hacker types, I don't think that us casuals, no matter how enthusiastic we are, will get too far.


EDIT: Looks like [another forum]("http://forum.xda-developers.com/showthread.php?t=931214") is working on this already, woohoo.
