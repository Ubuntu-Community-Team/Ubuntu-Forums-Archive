 I agree.  I've been playing around a bit with the Windows 8 preview release. 

If you can adjust your screen's sharpness level, see what that does.

Evaluating font rendering is very subjective, apparently.  I see Windows users complaining that Mac fonts are fuzzy and indistinct, while I think they are sharp, solid, and distinct. 

Meanwhile, the fonts I see in Windows have edges that appear pixellated and uneven. Large black headline characters are positively ragged.

I want onscreen fonts to look as much like fonts on paper as possible.  Apple and Ubuntu get a lot closer to that than Windows.
