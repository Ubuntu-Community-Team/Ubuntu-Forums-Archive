### Post by jessejazza on 2013-02-02
> **OpenSourceRules said:**
> Ok, we have a third choice between RPM and Debian!

There has always been the third choice - but Slack was there before rpm and debian. When it comes to choice there isn't such great choice. Ubuntu (xubuntu in my case) has got worse and worse this last couple of years. They put abiword 2.9.2 (development version) in 12.04 which was utterly stupid... virtually unusable... along with the.goutputstream**** bug. I spent a month looking at alternatives. Folk love to talk about rpm dependency hell - there isn't any now with yum (used to be with rpm package management). Several rpm distros like Centos may be ok for servers but you'll find to use rpmforge as a repo for desktop apps is dodgy. Debian is fine but any app e.g. Emacs 24 will take about a year to get in the repos due to debian's long testing procedure.

I've just tried Salix. Slackware (and derivatives) is awesome as it is somehow much faster than the debian and rpm distros. The package management is much superior if anything. You install the main package which looks for the dependencies held in a separate repo. It's interesting that PV of Slack decided to give up with gnome a couple of years ago... good reason given all the trouble with gnome 3 and unity.

The advantage of Salix and Vector is that there is just one CD. Slackware is on four...(or one DVD) which is a lot to download. Have look i think you'll be amazed. As the saying goes "Go Slack... you'll never come back".
