### Post by SeijiSensei on 2013-02-02
I'm more concerned about the degree of access the app requires.  At least the app is supposed to inform you about which OS functions it requires.  I don't know how well Google polices the developers, though.

As an example, I installed "[Hacker's Keyboard]("https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.pocketworkstation.pckeyboard&hl=en")" because I wanted the full array of keystrokes like Ctrl characters since I sometimes connect to a server with the phone.  A developer with evil intentions could have included a keylogger in this app.  I installed it anyway, but the whole process does require a level of trust in the developer, and Google, that may not always be warranted.

> **robbie 348 said:**
> I'm looking to buy my gf a tablet with Android  4.0 ice cream sandwich but  after searching I can't find a definite yes or no answer to needing  an AV.

Usually AV is for Windows, if you are sending info to a friend who is using Windows, it is nice to make sure the message is clean of virus's. Though most methods of messaging already have filters in place.

Malware might be of concern, but again, safe secure practices with any machine will help prevent this. For Android, using trusted repos - their app store, is good.

Personally, I do not see the need of third party AV's, as most applications are in secure - filtered repos, whether Mac, Windows or Linux. And most messaging is filtered. The pathway for virus and malware tends to be the user - and careless untrusted installs.
