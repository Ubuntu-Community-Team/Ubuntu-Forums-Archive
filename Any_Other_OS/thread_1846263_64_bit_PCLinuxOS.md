### Post by exploder on 2011-09-18
I have been running PCLinuxOS KDE for a good 6 or so months and have been totally impressed with it. Today I installed the 64 bit test 3 iso after reading so much positive feedback about it. I have to say I am very impressed! I have tried 64 bit systems in the past and did not think they were all that great but this test 3 iso is fantastic!

All my hardware worked perfectly, the proprietary ATI drivers were installed by default and everything works smooth and fast. Flash playback works great and I am very happy with the memory use for this being a 64 bit KDE system. Texstar is still building packages for the 64 bit release but everything I needed was in the repos except for a couple of codecs and I don't mind waiting. :D

I think that everyone that has been waiting for the 64 bit version is going to be very pleasantly surprised when this goes final. Very impressive work!

### Post by PendragonUK on 2011-09-19
I have not used PCLinuxOS for about a year, but love the Distro. More for it's communality and the whole feel. I think I learnt more in the couple of years running PCLinuxOS than I have in all the years of running Ubuntu and other Distro's. I have spend some time flitting between Ubuntu and PCLinuxOS running on different boxes. 

I heard talk of Tex working on a 64bit version but it was at PCLinuxOS I learnt the benefits of running 32bit with the PAE Kernel. I still do that in Ubuntu, run 32 bit with PAE rather than the full 64bit version. 

I have been out of touch and had not realised that he was that close to release. I might have to download the ISO and give it a go... 

For the those that don't know PCLinuxOS it's probably the best implementation of kde there is. It is also just about as far away from Ubuntu as you can get but still be Linux! Smaller homely community, no corporates sponsor, A very different experience. I recommend every one should run PCLinuxOS and visit the forum for sometime to get to know a different way.
