### Post by firefish5000 on 2012-05-06
Standards can change, but the fact is backtrack is firmly planted right now as THE standard. I'm not sure what it will be in 10 Years, but even if they don't change their distribution at all, I wouldn't be surprised if it was still the standard.

If you don't like the way they are responding to your questions then try rewording them, Googling them, And make sure to ask yourself if what you are asking and/or trying to do legal, does it sound legal? If the community thinks what you are trying to do is immoral or breaking a law they probably wont answer you. (Morals because hacking into your neighbors wifi to steal his credit card information just seems wrong whether your in a country, on a boat, or a island you own).

As previously stated, BackTrack is not made to be used as a main OS for daily activities, nor for your average user. Most people should find no need nor reason to download backtrack other than because they can.

If you really do need help and the people on their forums are unkind then try asking somewhere else such as their IRC, other popular Linux IRCs, other Linux forums. You could try asking here as well though it is not recommended to ask for tech support here.
