### Post by Chdslv on 2012-11-03
Yesterday, I got a email, which linked me to the Peppermint web site. I always liked Peppermint for its beauty, simplicity and snappiness.

The last Peppermint was 3 based on 12.04. The developer was asking people to join the team to make Peppermint 4 based on 13.04, which would be released in June 2013, after the 13.04 release. Of course, it'd have some new cloud and other features. But, its a long time to wait.:)

So, I decided to go for it my own way. I had done this with all 12.10s except Edubuntu and some 12.04s. 

I simply upgraded Peppermint 3 to Raring and then installed the kernel 3.7 rc2
I didn't change the wallpaper with Peppermint Three on it yet. The lsb-release states that it is Ubuntu, as I didn't change the ID.

It is very fast.
Here is the result. :)

### Post by Chdslv on 2012-11-03
I like Gnome-shell, but the 3.6.1 and 3.7.1 releases don't have much extensions, especially the autohide top panel one. This is with Gnome 3.4.1. I wanted to have Gnome-shell with any Ubuntu or Ubuntu based distro. Peppermint is so quick, the Gnome-shell would work quickly too.

I installed Gnome-shell 3.4.1 in Peppermint 3 first. (I forgot to tell about it in the other post.) I got Gnome 3.2.1 and Gnome-shell 3.4.1.  

Then, went back to Peppermint 3 and changed the repos to Raring, updated and upgraded it. It took in Raring, but without changing the Gnome-shell, which was nice. Then, I installed kernel 3.7 rc2 and Peppermint is flying.

Peppermint doesn't have any repos or any Peppermint ppas, so it was just Ubuntu.:)
