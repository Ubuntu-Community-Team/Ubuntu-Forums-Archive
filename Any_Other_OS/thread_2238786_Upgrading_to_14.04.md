### Post by Rob Sayer on 2014-08-10
OK, I've never heard of Cylon before so I just looked at their site.

Their 'support' consists of a link to askubuntu and an unimpressive looking forum.

You're dual booting with Mint 17, which is based on ubuntu 14.04 but doesn't actually use as new a version of the kernel.  And their support site doesn't hold a candle to ubuntu support.

I've had Mint installed a couple of times on my netbook.  It's had hardware support issues with older kernel versions so I'd distro/DE hopped with it.  I think Mint's fine for really experienced linux geeks but I actually had to look up things on ubuntu sites because there didn't seem to be anyone there who could answer the question.

Since ubuntu is the only distro I know of that I'd recommend to less experienced users who need support, and you're dual booting already with 2 distros both based on ubuntu that I'd really consider inferior, why not just reinstall with a single boot ubuntu 14.04 setup?

If you haven't used all the ubuntu DE flavors try downloading all of them and booting the live CD, preferably from a USB stick, and play around with them.

I'd also highly recommend installing with a separate partition for / and your /home folder.  It's actually not much harder at all and it means you can reinstall your OS without losing your data or user program settings (back it up though).  The performance is better too.  I think that partition scheme is the default in Debian and after trying it I can see why.
