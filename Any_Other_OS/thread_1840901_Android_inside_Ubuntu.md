### Post by rg4w on 2011-09-08
Last night I was switching between my Asus Transformer and my Ubuntu system when I found myself wanting to have both OSes running on one machine.  I have Ubuntu apps and Android apps that I love and use daily, but many of them are only available on one OS and not the other.

So I began to wonder:  How cool would it be if we could have Android Honeycomb running in a window within Ubuntu?

Or perhaps even better, multiple windows, so those apps could take on a role similar to Widgets in OS X or Gadgets in Windows, but have everything in the Android Market available.

This would not only be useful, allowing you to enjoy all of your Linux apps from one OS, but the marketing benefits for Canonical might be quite strong as well.

"Everything you love about Android, and a whole lot more."

I would imagine many OEM discussions would be quite easily greased with such a feature.

Any reasons why this would be unworkable?
