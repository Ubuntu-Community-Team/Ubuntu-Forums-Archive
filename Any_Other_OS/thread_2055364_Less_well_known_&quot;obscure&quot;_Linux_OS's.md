### Post by LiamOS on 2012-09-09
Linux from Scratch is a fantastic project to do. You learn a lot about the core system of Linux.

Somebody also suggested Gentoo, Arch and Slack. Out of these, I'd recommend Gentoo the most, since it's extremely configurable, very up to date, and had a plethora of documentation, as well as one of the friendliest forums going. I'm also absolutely in love with the package manager and init system.


As far as even more obscure distros go, you could try Funtoo(easier to install than Gentoo), Sabayon(Gentoo-based, works out-of-the-box), Bodhi Linux(Ubuntu based, E17 desktop), or perhaps even try something like FreeBSD.

### Post by ratcheer on 2012-09-11
I love **Siduction Linux**. It is a fork of Aptosid which is a fork of Debian sid.

Siduction somehow manages to have everything at the leading edge, yet be stable at the same time. It also has a lightweight memory footprint.

Being so close to Debian, it may not be different enough for what you want. But is it certainly obscure.
