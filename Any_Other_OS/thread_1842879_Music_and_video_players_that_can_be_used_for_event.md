Hi. In an upcoming big event / convention, I was tasked and assigned as the lead technical staff who is responsible for the stage music and video display (at the video wall) of the stage. There are parts in the program wherein I will be needing to display some videos or play background music.
 
Anyway, with my past experience in another event / convention, which is my first time to be the lead technical staff, there was the problem of determining if a video or music is going to end.
 
My laptop, with Linux Mint 10 Julia based on Ubuntu 10.10 Maverick Meerkat distro, I used dual screen during that past event. The 2nd screen will just display the one that was shown at the video wall of the stage - meaning that everything on the 2nd screen of my dual monitor will be seen by everyone watching the stage of the event.
 
I used the video player which I always use whenever I play personal videos, SMPlayer. Problem with SMPlayer is that I have no idea how long will the video end or can't display the timestamp once it is is being displayed on the video wall (as I can't just let the audience also know the timestamp that will be displayed at the bottom of the player. I wanted that only the 1st monitor, located at my laptop, will display those properties and also the controls). There's also that automatic behaviour of SMPlayer that once the video ended, the player goes to the "restored size" also displaying my desktop (or wallpaper with other opened applications) along with the player in "restored size" - which is not really that good to be displayed where everyone at the event can see in the video wall :P
 
I also try to avoid using VLC player, as in my experience, there are some crazy pixelations or morphing or graining at random times whenever I use that player to display videos ^^;;
 
As for the music player I used, I only used Audacious but is there a way where that the time left (instead of time passed) will be displayed? I think I used the GTK theme. Or are there other players you recommend than Audacious?
 
It is best that I use dual screen. The first screen (which only me and other technical staff will only see) will have the controls of the video or audio and where the 2nd screen will be located at the video wall of the stage. It is also best that there is a playlist window so that I will have a queue on what video or music will be played next. Also the controls of the player and also timestamp displaying the timeleft of the videos and music to be played.
 
I've researched the crossplatform DJ software Mixxx but I still have to fully test and familiarize if it's alright for events. I've also researched about the distro, PureDyne, which I'm about to download at the moment to also familiarize and test if that's also okay (or just get the softwares that are installed in there). Though I might use PureDyne in another computer just in case if there is a backup laptop I can use that has an OS that lacks softwares I need for event (emergency purposes). And also to take account that I might not have internet access when I'm at the event.
 
My question is that what are good softwares you can recommend that has these properties or capabilities that can be used in events / conventions with video wall at the stage:
 
Video Player
> Will have the video properties, playlist, timestamp (displaying timeleft), video & audio controls, etc, on a separate window and the video being displayed at another window (for dual screen purposes).
> Once video being played is done, it will stay fullscreen and have something like a wallpaper of the video at the player when it's not playing anything (I think Media Player Classic has that ability as I remember using it way back when I'm using Windows XP).
> Easy control of the video and especially the audio controls (as I don't think always using the mouse to control the audio lever is good)
 
Music Player
> Have playlist, audio control, timestamp (displaying timeleft).
> Easy control of the audio controls (as I don't think always using the mouse to control the audio lever is good)
