### Post by trent.josephsen on 2013-04-20
Well, in some ways it's a tribute to open source that making an *entirely new distro* because you don't like *one part* of the default install of your main is even thinkable. (Just imagine creating a new distribution of Windows in which everything works the same but the Start menu is by default located along the right side of the screen.) I'd encourage OP to go for it; after all, "[e]very good work of software starts by scratching a developer's personal itch." But inherent in that model is the realization that my personal itches aren't your personal itches, and so on and so forth for the rest of the world. What's more, most people probably have other settings in which they like to deviate from the default (as an Archer, I take this to what some might call unnecessary extremes), and so it's probably not a big deal for most people to change that one setting when they're installing for the first time.

I wouldn't consider using such a distro; I don't use Ubuntu to begin with (in part because of exactly this kind of thing, although there are other reasons). Maybe OP is the only one who would. But that doesn't make it a bad idea. Good ideas don't always have to make tons of money and take the world by storm.

(But I'd also agree with MG&TL that remastering a vanilla CD is probably a good way to start.)
