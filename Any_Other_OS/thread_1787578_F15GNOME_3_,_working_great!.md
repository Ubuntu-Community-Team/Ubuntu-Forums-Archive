### Post by Roasted on 2011-06-21
I have been testing out Gnome Shell for many, many months. I was not that big of a fan of the earlier versions of it, but I liked the overall idea. I kept hearing users talk about Gnome Shell on Gnome 3 recently so I got a little curious... then I fired it up on Ubuntu 11.04 via Gnome 3's PPA.

I absolutely love G3. It's fast, very very resource light, and still offers an intuitive way to manage your running applications. At first I knew it would be a transition, but it took a matter of minutes for me to adapt. For those of you barking that it interrupts your productivity, just stop. It's not that hard to adapt to it, though it does take a little bit of time since it is a massive change.

While I like and respect Unity, Gnome 3 is really where it's at, in my opinion. After running into some issues with Gnome 3, I decided to try out Mint 11 with the same PPA, just to see if I got different results. My reasoning behind this was due to the fact that Ubuntu is in bed with Unity while Mint is not. I figured I might have slightly different results, but I ended up having some similar issues. I can only attribute this to the fact Mint 11 and Ubuntu 11.04 are both Gnome 2.X based still. PPA'ing them up to Gnome 3 and then dumping Gnome Shell on top of it certainly doesn't sound like a non-risky task...

I installed Fedora 15 on my laptop, and so far I have not had any issues, but granted it hasn't been under weeks of use either. The only issue I had with Fedora 15 was I tried to install RPMFusion and it was hanging. I ended up rebooting in the middle of the process and I was presented with a kernel panic upon each boot. Mind you, I'm running a CR-48 Google laptop, which required quite a few steps of BIOS hacking to even get a regular OS on this darn thing. Being it was a vanilla install, I just nuked it and redid the F15 install quick.

I have to keep in the back of my head that my early Gnome 3 issues may be related to hardware, since I WAS previously running Ubuntu w/ XFCE and had some weirdness as well. So I don't want to chop the knees of the Gnome 3 PPA on Ubuntu/Mint, because I'm not 100% convinced it was an issue relating to that. However, if I had to bet a dollar, I'd lean that way. Not to mention, I've heard of other users hearing similar feedback, so that kind of makes me think it's just due to it being early yet and it'll solidify itself in a few short weeks.

Going back, so far Fedora 15 is working great. Being a Debian guy and long-time Ubuntu user, I of course miss a few things, but Fedora is a very, very nice distro that deserves every ounce of credit that's thrown its way.

I can only hope that 11.10 being based on Gnome 3 and including the option to install Gnome Shell right there within the repos will result in better success than the current Gnome 3 PPA I was using on 11.04. **Take note, Ubuntu Developers.** We respect Unity's existence, but for the substantial amount of users who want to use Gnome Shell, don't cut us short, or the inevitable Ubuntu/Gnome Shell fork will come about.

Until then, I'll continue to use Fedora 15 on my laptop here to see how things go.
