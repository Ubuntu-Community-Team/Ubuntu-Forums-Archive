### Post by NormanFLinux on 2012-09-25
> **Max Blyss said:**
> Jupiter?  Seemed to me like it was just Ubuntu 10.10 with Docky preinstalled, some alt. software items, and, like, a repo or two...

I actually distro - hopped away from it due to it's lack of perceived difference from Ubuntu at the time, having expected more of a departure.

If you're referring to an older build of Luna, I haven't tried any of them yet.

The current version is based on Ubuntu "Precise Pangolin" 12.04 LTS and uses the same Ubuntu repositories - however the second iteration is radically different. What makes it different from the last Jupiter build is that Luna now has its own unique desktop environment named Pantheon. Its lighter and faster than Unity or GNOME 3. The switchboard menu in the upper left corner is similar to the Unity Dash and works the same way. The Plank dock is customizable. However you cannot use the desktop for work since it does not support right click. You have to do work in folders which you can still right click and save your work there. Ditto for deleting stuff. It keeps the OS simple and tidy at the expense of what casual users do in a desktop environment. Boot up is incredibly fast and this will work for all but very old computers.

By all means, do grab the "unstable" build from Sourceforge. I haven't had a single serious crash or freeze on elementaryos. Its what an OS should be  - and I expect the final version to be well-received.
