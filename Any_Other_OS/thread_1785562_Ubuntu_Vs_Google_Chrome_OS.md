### Post by bupahs on 2011-06-19
After using both Ubuntu (for years) and ChromeOS (since the beta test period began) I have to say both have their advantages. ChromeOS is what a netbook should be, connected to the internet and nearly everything stored or done from there. There is offline storage for your documents, pictures etc either on the drive on on removable storage. Its fast, secure and simple to use. I had serious doubts about it when I was first invited to participate, the CR48 devices we were given were very limited (obviously beta) but as releases and fixes were put out the OS became quite interesting. I use it (ChromeOS) on a daily basis now. I will never give up a full OS, but you're not expected to, ChromeOS is designed for use on the go not as a principle device, it serves a purpose and I think the people it is geared toward will adopt it wholeheartedly since those people will already use Google's services or work for companies that do. As Google pushes it cloud services and Apps accounts more and more people will get on board.. Personally I see businesses handing out Chromebooks to their employees for work use (especially those with Apps Accounts), simply from a cost, maintenance and ease of use standpoint it makes perfect sense.

I usually tell people don't knock it unless you've tried it. :)
