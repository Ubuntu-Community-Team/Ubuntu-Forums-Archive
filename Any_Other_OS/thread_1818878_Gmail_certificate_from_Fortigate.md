### Post by Frubli on 2011-08-06
Fortigate is a brand of network firewalls, and that makes me think you are talking about a work/office/university/libary computer network, right?


> I have not been able to find out if any malicious software/certificate actually has been installed on my PC.

No, it's happening on the network not on your computer.

> My quistion is: Am I being spied on now? and how can I find out?

Someone is intercepting your secure network traffic, and unsecuring it so they can see inside. Whether it's actually 'spying' or innocent antivirus scanning, you'd have to ask them to find out, but it could be.

> Is there any risks with regards to other use of my PC (Banking - access the gmail accounts via www etc.)?

Yes, if you get the same sort of certificate warnings. If it is a trustworthy well run network, the risks are probably quite low, but still.

This thread has more discussion on this issue: [http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-1460407.html](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-1460407.html)
