### Post by xenopeek on 2013-01-28
If your problem is that GRUB gives some error, it may not have been installed correctly. You can reinstall GRUB from your Linux Mint installation DVD / USB stick, see the steps here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Boot-Repair#A2nd_option_:_install_Boot-Repair_in_Ubuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Boot-Repair#A2nd_option_:_install_Boot-Repair_in_Ubuntu)

If your problem is a black screen after boot, or garbled graphics, and you have a AMD or Nvidia graphics card, this post may help: [http://forums.linuxmint.com/viewtopic.php?f=46&t=122257](http://forums.linuxmint.com/viewtopic.php?f=46&t=122257)

Please also note that Linux Mint 14 has been obsoleted and reached end of life as of October 2012. I'd recommend you install Linux Mint 13 or 14. 13 is the current long-term support release, with support till April 20127. 14 is the most recent release with the newest software, with support till April 2014. You can download either from here: [http://www.linuxmint.com/oldreleases.php](http://www.linuxmint.com/oldreleases.php)

If you are currently using the main edition of Linux Mint 11 (GNOME 2), you would probably enjoy either the Mate or Cinnamon edition of Linux MInt 13 or 14.
