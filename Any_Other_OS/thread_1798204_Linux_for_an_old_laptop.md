### Post by wolfen69 on 2011-07-08
There's just *so many* good lightweight distros out there, that your best bet is to get a stack of blank cd's or flash drives and try a bunch of stuff. There is no *one size fits all* when it comes OS's. Each will have it's own nuances, compatibility issues (or not), memory/gpu requirements. The best way to learn is to try out as many distros as possible. Plus it's *a lot* of fun. :)

Some of my favorite lightweight distros are:

Crunchbang

Antix Mepis

Peppermint OS

Puppy

Madbox

Debian/Ubuntu net installs

Lubuntu

MacPup

Slitaz

INX 1.1 (no longer active though, but a great command line live cd but easy to use and learn from)

I know there are other good ones, but I havn't tried some of them yet. I'm gonna go down the list at distrowatch and go nuts in vmware.
