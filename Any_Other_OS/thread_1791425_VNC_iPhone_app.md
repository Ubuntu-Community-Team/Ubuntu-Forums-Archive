### Post by DanBender on 2011-06-26
I have a Mythbuntu system as an extra PVR/tuner and shared files server for my house. I normally do configuration work on it by VNCing to it from my main computer so that either my wife can watch TV (on the cable box PVR) without me taking up the TV screen doing something uninteresting, or I can be accessible to my toddler while he's napping (TV computer is in the other corner of the house, my computer is just one room over).

Now, my wife and I just got new iPhone 4's, and I was thinking maybe I could use that to VNC over for some things. I've downloaded Mocha VNC Lite v2.9, but it's not working great for me -- at least not the free "lite" version. It only has left-click mouse support (no drag, no RMB, etc.) and doesn't have full keyboard emulation. The limitations are causing some small problems like being unable to open xfce's application submenus (only the main menu will open, and all the actual programs are located in submenus), and some terminal features you take for granted (like select, paste, and previous/next command). The pay version of the app has more features, but it's not clear to me whether that will fix the menu problem, and I'm loath to plink down the cash just to find out it still doesn't work.

Has anyone had success VNCing to your Linux boxes using an iPhone or Android app? If so, which app (there are several available), and how well does it work?

Thanks,
Dan
