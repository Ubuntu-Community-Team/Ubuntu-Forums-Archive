### Post by kazuya on 2011-07-18
I installed Mint for a colleague of mine last week. She loves it now and hardly goes to the windows 7 option of her laptop. Mint in my opinion, is one of the easiest of first choice recommendation to install for beginning users.
 
This ofcourse depends on the PC or laptop specs.
 
1 Gig of RAm and below, I would install (1) crunchbang (2) archbang (3) macpup/mepis antix. (4) Vectorlinux/Zenwalk
 
2 Gig of RAM and above (1) Mint / Ubuntu / Mepis (2) crunchbang (3) Archbang w/gnome
 
Preferred DE In order of Liking and usage.
 
(1) XFCE4 - faster and customizable replacement for gnome 3. Can be made to look as nice as gnome 2.0 but much lighter on resources.
 
(2) Gnome 3 - I can tweak it now and run cairo-dock on it so no problem with UI. Prefer this by far to Unity and dare I say gnome 2.0. Looks gorgeous and less bloated than unity.
 
(3) Openbox - Archbang and Crunchbang has the best example of these that I have ever seen.
 
(4) kde 4.6 - nice, but the network manager is always a pain to deal with sometimes.
 
(5) E17 - Fun sometimes, but buggy sometimes DE crashes when selecting wrong themes. Not acceptable for me. I then have to go to another DE and delete .e config to reset.
 
(6) Gnome 2 - barely supported. Much customizable, but feels dated now compared to gnome 3.0. 
 
(7) Unity - The dock on the left is extremely irritating to see. too huge and unappealing to me. I admire their effort with it though. It is also more resource hungry. This is my least favorite DE. But it is very functional.
 
These are merely my opinions. Folks have to understand that Ubuntu 11.04 at least gives option to use classic gnome 2 so users are not forced to use Unity. Mint defaults to classic gnome. Ubuntu comes installed with classic gnome as option but defaults to unity.
