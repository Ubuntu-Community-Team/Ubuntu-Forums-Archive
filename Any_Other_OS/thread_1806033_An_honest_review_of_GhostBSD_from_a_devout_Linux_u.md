### Post by Dlambert on 2011-07-17
I had long been interested in BSD ever since watching _Revolution OS_and doing some research into the history of Gnu/Linux. So on the BSD's Wiki i saw the link to FreeBSD website, but no GUI installer deterred me at the time. And very recently I heard about [GhostBSD]("http://ghostbsd.org/") from [ThisWeekinLinux]("http://www.thisweekinlinux.com/") (Great guy and show). GhostBSD is basically FreeBSD with the GNOME environment and a GUI installer. The project is maintained by a small group of developers. Since you normally see KDE on Clone BSD distros, i decided to give this one a try. I downloaded the iso (2.0), and after some bumps with UNetBootin, finally burned it to a dvd. I also downloaded PCBSD for a method of comparison (since this is my first try at BSD)I booted up from the DVD, just to find myself waiting for an incredibly long time, a normal Linux live DVD will be up and running in 5 minutes tops, but GhostBSD took 10-15 Minutes (So does PCBSD). So i did some research and found out that this was normal for BSD, and i must say that is really a con for me being used to how fast Linux boots. Then again, this is new to me. So once the OS finally booted, i was greeted by the GNOME Loading watch for another few minutes before i was finally introduced to a standard GNOME 2.3x Desktop. Being on a laptop the first thing i wanted to do was hook up to WiFi, but no joy, driver wasn't pre-installed, which is fine because its a proprietary Broad-com WL driver (neither did PCBSD), so nothing against the Distro. (I don't even know if the card works with BSD). The list of applications seemed a little out dated to me, for one the use of Firefox 3, i know 4 may/may not have been ported yet but still, a big change from Linux. The design is really nice, reminds me of OpenSuSE GNOME, so the GUI was nothing new to me. The package manager, although new was still understandable. So, after my experiences of GhostBSD coming from a devout Linux user trying something completely new i give it a 5.5/10 in terms of switch-ability from Linux to BSD.

PROs:
Beautiful Gnome (even if it's not 3.0)
Resolution/Display worked out of the box
Sound Worked out of the box

Cons:
SLOW boot, to me, again I'm a Linux user, not BSD.
Outdated in my opinion
Slow even for a LiveDVD, compared to my Linux experiences 
Seems almost bloated, like something is holding the OS back.

Again, i will repeat: This review is from a Linux user interested in a new operating system. I know BSD and Linux are not the same thing, and in no way should be compared to each other. I am simply testing out how easy it would be for a Linux user to switch over to BSD.

It is possible I guess I mean no technology/platform is forever but unlikely. What is unfortunate in the FOSS landscape is a more and more applications and projects are geared to Linux more and more as opposed to be portable to other unix-like systems.  I don't see that trend changing anytime soon.
