### Post by exploder on 2012-12-24
I tried a lot of different DE's looking for something that had all of the features I really wanted. I settled on Mint 14 KDE mainly because it was so convenient having all of the codecs already installed. 

I have not used KDE for some time but it really did not take long to get used to it and I really like how compositing works, it is just so handy once you get used to it. In all fairness though, KDE 4 has had a long time to mature where some of the other DE's have not been around as long.

KDE 4.9.4 seems rock solid, it looks good and it's so customizable. I think that whatever KDE distribution you choose as long as your hardware is supported, you are going to be pretty happy with KDE.

Certainly there are a lot of great KDE distributions to choose from and they all have their good qualities. I went for the most convenient route just to save myself some time but I have seen people having very good comments about, Sabyon, OpenSuse, Kubuntu, etc. 

KDE was the last DE I looked at but having all of the right click options in Dolphin and the way KDE had desktop effects settings out of the box really sold me on it. It was worth the small learning curve to me.
