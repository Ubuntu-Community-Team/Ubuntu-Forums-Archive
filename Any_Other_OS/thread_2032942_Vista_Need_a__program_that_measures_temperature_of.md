### Post by Cavsfan on 2012-07-25
Yes, I quad boot and on windows 7, I have SpeedFan and CoreTemp

[[COLOR=blue]_http://www.alcpu.com/CoreTemp/_[/COLOR]]("http://www.alcpu.com/CoreTemp/")

I had them both on Vista before I upgraded. You can set them both to startup at boot and display the temps of all your cores and your GPU temp as well.
SpeedFan displays the GPU temp and CoreTemp displays all of your core temps. Which makes sense. :)
I set my GPU temp as yellow and changed the 4 core temps to different colors and they are always there If I want to glance at the bottom right corner of my screen.
I even have them displaying Fahrenheit or you can leave them to display Celsius.
