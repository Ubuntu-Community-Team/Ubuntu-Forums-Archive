### Post by d-cosner on 2014-06-27
[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande]A couple of days ago I installed Antergos with Gnome on my desktop. I had been trying Sabayon but updates destroyed it! Antergos is pretty much Arch made easy and has a nice graphical installer much like Ubuntu's. I installed the new system with no problems at all. [/FONT][/COLOR][IMG]http://forums.linuxmint.com/images/smilies/icon_biggrin.gif[/IMG][COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande] The installer allowed me to install various things for multimedia playback, Libre Office and enable AUR, etc.[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande]I did do a little package swapping out to make things the way I like them, the end result was very nice to say the least. I did search the forum to learn how to install pepper flash in Chromium but the information was easily available. I asked a couple of questions in the form too because I am not familiar with Arch. My questions were answered pretty quick![/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande]The developers participate on the forum and are very nice and helpful, this really impressed me! [/FONT][/COLOR][IMG]http://forums.linuxmint.com/images/smilies/icon_biggrin.gif[/IMG][COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande] I have always read good things about Arch and the Antergos team sure makes getting started very easy. My main goal is to have the current stable version of Gnome Shell and so far Antergos has made this easy to accomplish.[/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande]Shuld you find yourself wanting to try Arch but are having a hard time installing it, this is an easy way to get the job done. There are other desktop environments available from the Antergos Live CD and of course you can look it over before you do the actual install. Arch seems a lot less prone to breakage than Sabayon was in my opinion and I have really been enjoying it![/FONT][/COLOR]

[COLOR=#333333][FONT=Lucida Grande]There is a graphical package manager included by default and it does take a little bit of getting used to. A little bit of reading in the wiki and the forum goes a long way! I have always wanted to try Arch and so far I see why so many people like it.
