### Post by craig10x on 2012-05-26
I have been a long time user of mint who returned to ubuntu 11.10 and then to ubuntu 12.04 with unity...i got to like it a lot but recently i tried Zorin 6 Release Candidate (the iso has a few bugs which seem to go away when installed) and i must say i am really impressed! The RC is only available in 32 bits but the final release will of course have both 32 and 64 bit...I have used Zorin in the past and always liked it but never installed it before...for me, Zorin 6 will be a "keeper"...

It's based on Ubuntu 12.04 of course, and is very fast, smooth and snappy and just has a great "feel" to the whole thing....To adopt for Gnome 3 they very cleverly worked out the use of the AWN (Avant Window Manager) dock and it's used to create the lower panel it has instead (just like mint...1 panel on bottom)...and for the menu it is the good old gnomenu which is preset to a "Windows 7" look style slab menu to help windows converts feel more comfortable (though there is about a dozen other slab menu styles one can use if they prefer...i kind of like the windows 7 default one though...it is cute...lol)...

Otherwise, it is like a highly customized ubuntu with cute compiz effects by default, nice fonts in the windows (ubuntu style) colors, theming, etc...and i think they do other little things to ubuntu to make it run snappier too...

Here is a newly posted video review of it:
[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P5K6nYhXwdY](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P5K6nYhXwdY)

Only weak spot is the Zorin community is a bit light on activity...my favorite forums are ubuntu and mint...but of course it is ubuntu underneath, so plenty of places to get help...though they do try over there...   

But when the 64 bit version is released, i am installing for sure...using it made me realize that though i like Unity, the old style way of working a desktop is a bit easier to work with for me...   

I am fond of Cinnamon also but i have to give the "edge" to Zorin 6....really love it  :D

### Post by craig10x on 2012-05-26
They've been around for about 3 years now and within the past year i understand that they have had about 2 million user downloads of their isos which isn't bad for a small operation like theirs...

There had been a long delay between Zorin 5 (a year ago and based on 11.04) and Zorin 6 (based on our current 12.04 ubuntu) because they were trying to figure out how to deal with the gnome 3 change over....looks like they solved it in fairly simple way that works well...

Actually, other then the default slab menu appearance (which is changeable though i left it because i like it) and the styling of the lower panel (which can be reduced in height in the settings and which i did)...it is really pure ubuntu underneath with some nice compiz tricks and nicely style windows, fonts and colors that (like ubuntu) give it a very professional and really nice look...

I think they also do some other tweaks to ubuntu to make it run a bit faster and extra smooth...they put a LOT of attention into details i noticed...

It also has all the codecs installed already (like mint) and even itunes (quicktime) movie previews play flawlessly...it even uses the ubuntu software center and has access to ubuntu one as well...

It's a nice alternative if one loves ubuntu (as i do) but wants to use a more "conventional style" desktop instead of unity...so not just for "windows convert newbies" (although it is presented that way to help make them comfortable....

If you want to check it out, access it from the blog about it on the Zorin website...otherwise you will be downloading the old Zorin 5 (which was gnome 2 based)...
