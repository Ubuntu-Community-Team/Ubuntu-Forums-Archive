### Post by FormatSeize on 2011-08-03
No.
Red Hat Enterprises and Suse are two other Linux based system that is based on the Linux kernel that have proprietary products. Kernel is the keyword there. Linux is just the name of the Kernel, and nothing else in the system. But that's not common knowledge, and people from GNU have gone on record saying that it should be called GNU/Linux, while 90% of people are still sticking with calling the entire system "Linux" not "system with a Linux kernel".
