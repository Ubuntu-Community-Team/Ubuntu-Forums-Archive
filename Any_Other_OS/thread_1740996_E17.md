### Post by boydrice on 2011-04-27
ou can install E17 on just about any distro so you don't really need a distro that does it by default.  Most tend to be hobbyist distros that offer E17 or just respins of larger distros.  Personally while I am curious about E17 I find it to be the least intuitive DE/WM I have ever used and that includes GNOME 3 and Unity.  

I have toyed with the PClInuxOS E17 version and it was fine.  Elive was interesting and used to be the defacto E17 distro but when folks found out they wanted you to pay mid-install for it the buzz around it fell off.  The dev for Bodhi seems to post everywhere and comment on any blog or review that mentions his distro so that guy is well passionate.  From what I gather though it is a barebones E17 on a Ubuntu base.  Good luck.
