### Post by MadmanRB on 2012-11-01
No seriously,. I have just decided to give openSUSE 12.2 a chance on my machine after having countless issues with my last distro.
I have tried openSUSE off and on with mixed results, sometimes it was lousy with dependency hell all over the place, to semi decent, to pretty good all things considered.
The major drawback for opoenSUSE is its lack of packages, at least in the past but now its soaring high in packages due to contributors and new steam from within.
Honestly if people want a good nice alternative to Ubuntu 12.10 right now this may be the one, as for me here are some of the things I like about it:

My graphics card was detected right away, even my HDMI audio worked out of the box on it which is shocking considering my ati HD 4200 never quite worked that well under Ubuntu 12.10 and I needed to install the driver in the older versions of it to make it work.

YAST is much faster then it used to be

Its very flexible when using KDE and or Gnome 3, its also very stable

Boot times are very quick now, on par with the current Ubuntu

There is actually pretty much nothing bad I can say about it, I am very impressed with it and it will probably be my new default linux for some time.

### Post by karthikn_jay on 2012-12-16

What i didnt liked:

3. Mounting of other drives requires password everytime after login. And mounting is happening only thru Dolphin. 

I had some difficulties trying with openSUSE, so i switched to Kubuntu 12.10 now. openSUSE 12.2 surely can be tried. Its a good distro, may be i would try in next release again...
