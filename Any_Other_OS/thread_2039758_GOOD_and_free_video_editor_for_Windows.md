### Post by qyot27 on 2012-08-09
On Windows, there's really no compelling reason to even use Avidemux since VirtualDub is available.

Generally, prosumer (or higher) quality free and/or open source video editing software is scant to begin with, with most of the energy to making one comparable to Premiere or Vegas or Final Cut or Avid being directed toward Linux-only solutions like Cinelerra/Lumiera (which aren't very comfortable fits either).

VLMC is probably still in an extremely alpha state, and if you can get it to work correctly, then all the power to you.  But I wouldn't hold my breath.

Blender's Video Sequence Editor might be of interest, although I have no experience using it.

Beyond that, it is possible to use [AviSynth](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AviSynth) as an NLE, but that takes a lot of patience.  If you decide to go that route, then VirtualDub can be used to interface with it.  Also, even though 2.6 is listed as a 'preview' or 'alpha', it's generally recommended over 2.5.8 simply because of all the bugfixes, extra colorspaces, and being otherwise more up-to-date.
