### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-08-19
Unfortunately the classic gnome2 desktop is going, even from Mint soon, as gnome2 will be no more and mint will probably be forced to use gnome3.  Eventually mint may not even be based on ubuntu, but on debian, as are several mint versions already.

There will be a fallback desktop for gnome3, similar to gnome2 with panels, but at the moment the only way to see it is to try Fedora 15, and try the fallback desktop.  At the moment that does not give many configuration options, and very little can be changed, nor applets added to the panels, so at present it is almost useless to me.

Have a look, by all means at Mint, but don't run away with the idea that you will be able to retain its current DE for very long, as that is most unlikely unless mint takes over gnome2 and continues support and development for it;  most unlikely, I think.
