### Post by manzdagratiano on 2011-04-30
I did not like Fedora when it first came out - mostly because of what Gnome looked like with it. In fact, because of that, I did not give Gnome a shot until Debian Lenny.
After eight years, I saw some fancy-looking screenshots, and decided to try it again. So I installed Laughlin beta in QEMU. Installation was as fast as Ubuntu - 5 mins. And as soon as I rebooted, I discovered the all-important fact:

I STILL don't like it. Because of the time I shall have to spend to UNDO what comes with the default configuration. The cluttered startup, SELinux policies, 'yum', no easy way to upgrade one release to another, etc etc etc.

Reminds me of my days with Windows, when after a first install, I would have to spend a good few hours DEmanufacturing (yes I love Fear Factory) what it had enabled by default - Windows messenger, disk space policies, remote registry, etc etc etc.

So no, I shall not install Fedora anytime in the near future.
