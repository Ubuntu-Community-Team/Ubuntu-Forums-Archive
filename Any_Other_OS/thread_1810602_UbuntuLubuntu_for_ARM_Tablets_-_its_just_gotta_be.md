I know Canonical has their work going on for ARM support.

I'd really like to hear from anyone that may know of progress on substituting Ubuntu or Lubuntu (because its so much lighter in rqmts) as a complete replacement for Android on Tablets.

I've got the really great Barnes & Noble Color Nook ($250) tablet.

Rooted with CyanogenMod to get a full android OS on it and the full marketplace.

I then created & installed a "chroot" Ubuntu.. later changed to Lubuntu on the SDCARD.

It all works REALLY great... I can touch a single button to switch back/forth btwn Android & Lubuntu ... synaptic & software center work etc.

I think Ubuntu with Unity is destined for Tablets sooner or later and the way its designed with the toolbar etc would just be great on a Tablet.

But to be honest I'd just like to use Ubuntu/Lubuntu and replace Android altogether and not waste extra memory/storage resources for the 2nd OS (Android).

Anyone know where Canonical's ARM work is heading since many tablets are either ARM.   Intel ATOM based tablets will probably be an easier issue to resolve.

But ARM is getting so good and uses so little power (tablet use time).
