### Post by IWantFroyo on 2011-12-26
I choose what distro I use based on several factors.

1) Popularity. There are plenty of good distros that aren't as popular, but one general thing to remember is that the poorly-coded ones never stay near the top.
Mainly this is so that you don't end up trying some broken spin-off of a spin-off of a spin-off that was last updated 5 years ago.

2) Updates. Check out the distro's philosophy, and see if it matches yours. Do you prefer a system that has guaranteed stability and won't change much, do you want the newest version of apps ASAP, or do you take the middle road?

3) Lightness. How old/slow is your hardware? Would you allow for some lag, or are you going for a lightning fast system at all costs? Are you running it on a computer that stays near an outlet, or do you need to conserve the battery?

4) GTK 2, GTK 3 or Qt? What type of apps do you use? The importance of this depends on how integrated you want your desktop to be.

5) Appearance. This isn't a big one, considering Linux is generally easy to modify. Just don't pick something that's really radically different from your tastes, unless you already know how to fix it (a Mac lover using Jolicloud isn't always a good idea).
