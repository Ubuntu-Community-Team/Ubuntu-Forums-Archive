### Post by exploder on 2012-11-22
I always seem to have something with problematic hardware in Linux... My desktop has NVidea graphics and works just fine with Mint 14 Cinnamon but my laptop has ATI graphics and has problems with recent Ubuntu variants. 

I tried Ubuntu 12.04, 12.10, Mint 14, PCLinuxOS, etc but either the graphics drivers were poor or the sound would not work.

Last night I gave Fedora 17 a try on the laptop. I installed the ATI drivers, codecs and some additional apps that are usually a pain to get working. I used the scripts provided in the Fedora forum and everything worked out fine. Plymouth displays perfectly, no screen flicker or any issues with the ATI proprietary drivers, everything just works the way it should!

I went with the Gnome version of Fedora because I wanted something that looked half way elegant, Fedora is not quite as elegant as Mint with the latest Cinnamon desktop but it does not look bad. The larger icons are kind of handy considering the laptop only has a 15 inch wide-screen display and the launcher is pretty handy. 

The Gnome accessibility features are great, I used them before when I had eye surgery and they were a great help to me. I have read many comments about the Ubuntu Gnome remix and that got me interested in Gnome Shell too. The Fedora developers have done an amazing job with fixing bugs and providing updates. VLC does not get scratchy sound, plymouth works perfectly and I really can't find anything wrong anywhere. Glad I finally got my laptop useful again!
