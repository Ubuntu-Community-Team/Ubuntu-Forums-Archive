### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2011-07-12
I find Xubuntu to be kid-friendly and parent-friendly. [Qimo4Kids]("http://qimo4kids.com") is based on it as well because of the simplicity (as well as the versatility and beauty) of the Xfce desktop. Have you tried the Xfce edition of Mint? The LTS version (Isadora) is awesome for technophobes, kids (even mildly autistic ones like me), and grandparents who "don't like all this newfangled stuff." I would avoid the new Debian-based Xfce Mint, though, unless it works well on your hardware. Xubuntu and Xubuntu-based stuff always work flawlessly on my old hand-me-down Dell, and Xubuntu is tastefully and simply put together.
