### Post by BrokenKingpin on 2011-06-24
> **fremantle said:**
> im going to try lxde once again. is it as customizable as xfce4? i mean like compisiting, trnasparency, cool applets and whatnot...
For compositing in LXDE you need to install Xcompmgr. LXDE can be very configurable as the design of the DE is very modular. That being said I feel XFCE has more applets and whatnot out of the box, and has a built in compositing.

So XFCE seems like a more complete DE out of the box, but LXDE makes it easier to swap out different components (such as the window manager, panels, etc.).
