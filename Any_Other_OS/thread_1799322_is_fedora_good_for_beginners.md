### Post by kazuya on 2011-07-07
I am starting to like gnome 3 much better than unity especially after gnome-tweak-tools is included. It lets you customise the desktop more. Unity is nice also, but gnome3 looks much nicer. I am now so much more comfortable with gnome 3 that I prefer it by far over gnome 2.
 
I have one thing is a requirement for me liking gnome 3. I have cairo-dock with its plugins also installed at lower dock.

I use an arch-based distro though, but I imagine it works similar for all other distros.

### Post by wolfen69 on 2011-07-07
> **shibu_sawyer said:**
> Are you saying, there is no good community support for fedora..:(

The forums are not so good for useless banter like over here, but the help sections are just fine. My questions were answered within minutes.

As far as being good for beginners, it depends. I think it's not quite as beginner friendly as ubuntu, but certainly not something you won't be able to overcome with a little patience. Fedora does not hold your hand and guide you to codecs or drivers. Although there are setup scripts (can't remember the names) that will install most everything you'll need.

If you have good, linux compatible hardware, Fedora is a good choice. And fedora is ahead of the game compared to other distros as far as Gnome 3 goes, since it's been the default DE for a couple months now.

Try it and find out for yourself.
