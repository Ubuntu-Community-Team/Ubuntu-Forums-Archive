### Post by MBybee on 2011-06-02
> **C!oud said:**
> 0

Precisely. 

Why would you want it to be GPL? That's like asking when the GNU utilities will be released under a commercial license.

The BSD license is incredibly short (2 paragraphs), simple, and says you can do anything you like with any of it.

The GPL has several versions, is complex, viral, and has all sorts of limits and requirements.

This is why BSD powers tools like SSH, many commercial firewalls, lots of TCP/IP stacks, and served as the basis for most of the transition from a pre-TCP/IP world (Trumpet WinSock, IPX/SPX, SNA) to a TCP/IP world.
