### Post by Guilden_NL on 2012-02-19
In my role as an Enterprise Architect, I end up using Red Hat, SUSE and Ubuntu.  All are good; they tend to have the same level of support for older HW, but there's always one oddball combination where one distro may support better at any given time.

SUSE has good support from HP; but other than that, I don't see it supported much outside of Western Europe, especially Deutschland.

Red Hat has a tremendous amount of market share in servers, but not in desktops.

Ubuntu has a tremendous amount of global community support.

I can only say great things about all three.  But it appears that you're looking for the best distro for an individual user, and you didn't mention your HW.  I would say Ubuntu is a good choice for most people.

For what it is worth, I run Lubuntu 11.10 on nine systems at home; from a 8 yr old Toshiba laptop, to a 24 core/96GB RAM dekstop :P , I like the LXDE conventional desktop and it screams on even old, light HW.
