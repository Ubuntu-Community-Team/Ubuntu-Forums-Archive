Mint is faster:
- Faster boot (tried both, natty and mint 11). on same PC I7 Quad Core, 27 seconds (Mint) vs. 35 (Natty). 8 Seconds faster, 22.8% faster.
- Faster switch between applications. Instant app switcher, vs. about 1 second with unity.
- Faster to find an applications. Oh my! the "Search" for application in unity is so much slower than Gnome Do.
- Faster to access a menu for an open application. Try running in a two monitor an application on your lower right corner, you have to travel for over 2000 pixels to get to the menu for "File/Open"!

Ramon Ramon and its crew, and expert in open source migrations made a research, and found out that people are not afraid to new things, they are afraid to loose what they took for granted, things that made their every day work fast and easy.

- Task bar
- System tray
- Gnome panels
- Gnome applets
- Hierarchical menus
- Keyboard shortcuts
- Middle button and anyplace click to "go to" in scroll bars.
- Nice and stable dual monitor desktop (Unity flicker and miss draw every workspace switch).

And not so long ago: 
- Stable multi protocol instant messenger (Empathy is just not mature enough)
- Right window minimize/maximize/close buttons

I'm a Mint user, since Ubuntu 11.04 made its appearance. 
I'm trying right now latest beta for Ubuntu 11.10, and not much has changed (the only thing that went away, was the flicker).
Yet another Ubuntu distro that I will not use, the second one, after 12 straight releases that I've happily installed.
