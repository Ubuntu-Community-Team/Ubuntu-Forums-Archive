### Post by jrothwell97 on 2011-07-20
> **mips said:**
> Not everyone has has high speed internet contrary to popular belief and then people also have data caps to make it even worse.
True. Note that Apple are intending to sell a USB stick installer for Lion, though, and that they have said that users with a slow net connection are welcome to take their machine to an Apple store and have the Lion upgrade performed there.

> **mips said:**
> When you need to reinstall you first have to install Snow Leopard & then upgrade to Lion
No you don't. The installer creates a [recovery partition](http://support.apple.com/kb/HT4718). The newly announced Macintoshes have this feature baked into the firmware, and it's clever enough to detect if the recovery image is corrupted or not present (e.g. in the event of the internal disk being replaced), and, if so, to fetch a fresh install image over the 'net from Apple's servers.

> **mips said:**
> Sorry, I see this practice as idiotic and to add there is a whaling & gnashing of teeth where I live about this idiocy.
I see it as progress.
