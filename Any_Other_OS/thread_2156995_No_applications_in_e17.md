It does *look* great.  But it doesn't *work* great on my hardware.  Very persistent bugs and quirks more than made up for the eye candy.  But if it works on your machine, it's certainly a wonderful way to have an ultralight system with eye candy that rivals KDE!  Some very imaginative themes and artwork and stunning effects for an absolutely awesome *looking* desktop.

Enlightenment isn't new.  I fact it's older than most of the major desktop environments!  I guess one of the things that kinda dissapointed me with Enlightenment is the mere fact that it's older than most of the others, yet in all that time remains buggy and irksome for so many casual users like me.  But Bodhi has done a great job making E17 work on a lot of hardware (must be the Ubuntu LTS foundation that Bodhi is built on) and keeping it stable.  I will surely try Bodhi again some day and hope for a better experience next time.
