### Post by threedaysmore on 2012-07-26
I agree with drawkcab.  If you feel as though you can do this without damaging the computer, try to open it up enough to get to the fan.  If you don't know how to do this, try googling "how to clean the fan in a (enter your computer brand and model here)".  

I've done this now to several computers and seen it be all kinds of things.  Sometimes it's just dust and that means it's just a simple fact of cleaning the fan with some compressed air you can buy at most general or any tech stores.  Other times something is lodged within the fan that keeps it from spinning.  

Provided the fan is not dead though, it's usually a pretty simple fix.
