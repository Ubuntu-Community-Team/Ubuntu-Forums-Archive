### Post by jjex22 on 2011-11-28
To be honest you've had all good suggestions - the thing to remember I guess is that KDE is such an inclusive set of programs and extensions that it's often much the same - especially on distro's designed for gnome - ubuntu, fedora, mint etc - you just get the default kde sat on top of the underlying system - little in the way of tweaking. 

The three best tweaked distro's in my experience are OpenSUSE, PC linuxOS and Mandriva - all rpm based, but PClinuxOS does use apt-rpm, so it's the easiest to switch. As for gnome distros with KDE flavours, there's little to choose between them - if you like the underlying distro, you'll probably like the kde varient, but as I say, they are very rarely tweaked - for example there is little difference between kubuntu and adding kde to ubuntu, yes you wont have all the gnome packages, and you get a different software centre, but for the end user that's about it.

As you may have guessed I just add kde to my Ubuntu/mint. whichever I'm using at the moment - I don't add another distro to the hard drive just for a different DE, but that's personal choice - my advice, if you're running an Ubuntu/mint, and you want to add another distro, why not add something with a very different philosophy/ approach? variety and such!

Edit: Forgot the recommendations! - I'd go OpenSUSE 12.1 -well, I did; it's the only other one installed to the metal! If you are the kind of person who finds themselves at home on unity - with the launcher, check out Mandriva 2011 - KDE with a Unity style launcher - not my cup of tea, but still a rock solid distro with some very good features.
