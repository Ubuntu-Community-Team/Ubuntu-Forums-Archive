### Post by cwklinuxguy on 2011-12-10
Hello there, Ubuntu users!!

I have long desired, and heard of the desire, for an Ubuntu remix with Gnome 2.

I launched that very project on November 2, which I call Paradise OS, and just a few days ago I released the iso.zip file for Alpha 1.

Please note that I am not a programmer or developer (yet), but merely an enthusiast that knows how to read wikis and use Remastersys.

Please also note that the primary/default Desktop Environment is currently MATE (the Gnome 2 fork) *not *the original Gnome 2 project.

Unity is included in the Alpha 1 release, but I do not currently have the intention leaving that in as a long-term thing. However, part of my goal is to listen to my community of users and do my best to build this distribution based on their own needs, preferences, and desires, so should the community wish for Unity to be left in on a long-term basis, I will see to it. 

The Paradise OS main site is [here]("http://paradiseos.wordpress.com"). The release notes and download link are available at [URL="http://paradiseos.wordpress.com/2011/12/05/a1-release/"]this link.
[/URL]
If you're hankering for up-to-date Ubuntu with Gnome 2, please do try out my remix (which will eventually have its own branding) and let me know what you think.

### Post by cwklinuxguy on 2011-12-12
At the present moment, this offers a product not a whole lot different than Mint, but I hope to be able to impliment some sort of system for a rolling release without the need of a repository (some script that can update components that have been changed in the new version). This is also about community--this project is NOT a meritocracy, but rather a democracy--I will take the side of the community at large whenever possible when it comes to decisions.

Also, why does Gnome 2 HAVE to die? This is the open source community, and as long as people want a project to exist and are willing to put some effort into it, nothing neither *has to* nor *can* die. Gnome 2 is the first DE that I ever used--I find it to be the most comfortable to work with, and the one that best suits my needs. Gnome 3 is targeted at a different audience, and is not going to be everyone's thing. I think for that reason it is important to keep the PERFECTLY GOOD, PERFECTLY USABLE technology alive for people to enjoy.
