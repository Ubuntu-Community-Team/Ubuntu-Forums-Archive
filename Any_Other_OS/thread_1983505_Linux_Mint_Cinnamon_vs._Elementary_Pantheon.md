### Post by Purplerob on 2012-05-20
Many people are very excited about Linux Mint's Cinnamon. Personally I find it a step back in almost every single way. It looks cluttered, has less customization than gnome2 (or xfce, or kde4, or any other popular non-gnome) and doesn't really have anything special. Unity, whether you like it or not (I do) is definitely different than the standard Linux desktop, Gnome Shell is a similar story. Many people are complaining that these new gnome3 shells, interfaces and forks are plainly bad. So, they think cinnamon will be better with its more classic looks. But I wish people would look at the Elementary Team's Pantheon environment. It is lean, looks very functional, has a nice and good-looking (even if you think it is mac-ish) design and is **completely modular!** And that is why I am so excited about it. By default it has function and form but you can change and swap out *anything* you want.

### Post by Chdslv on 2012-11-24
>  Cinnamon vs PantheonGood question. I asked myself the same, and you can see the results in my other posts.

The question is not about Cinnamon or Pantheon, but the window mangers under them--Muffin or Gala.

If you install Cinnamon or Pantheon, they pull in a lot of stuff, not giving us the freedom we talk so much about.:)

In Cinnamon 2 things are good, the Wm and the panel. The Cinnamon panel is a beauty as it can be configured. All other applications could be added or subtracted. The panel autohides too. 

In Pantheon, the most needed ones are the WM - Gala and the launcher - Slingshot, the rest is not really necessary. The Wingpanel is a headache, looks like Gnome-shell panel and absolutely unconfigurable. The same goes with Plank, an under-developed child of Docky.

You may notice, if you've got rid of the Wingpanel, that Slingshot opens at the top left corner, wherever you put the panel or not. Slingshot opens always at that point, so if the Wingpanel is auto-hidden, it'd appear there. So, there won't be any modifications to it, and eOS Luna might not see a final release.:( 

The panel and its high configurability is the best achievement in Cinnamon, even without all other Mint-related applications. All the other stuff it pulls, when installed, I find a way to get rid of, just for the case of freedom in Linux.

The best part of eOs Luna is its WM, Gala and Slingshot. Actually, I prefer the older version of Slingshot, so I freeze the Elementary OS Team's ppas, after the installation. I don't want their "bugs" they talk so much about.:)

Now, the combination of Cinnamon panel, Gala and Slingshot would be ideal. I am going to try to get that working...:)

If you look at the quality of both WMs, they are quite equal, and they are really snappy, much snappier than Gnome-shell and Unity!:)
The other one that's really fast is Lxpanel.

Have a good day!

Ch
