### Post by Glencore on 2011-06-08
What is the issue? 

You are frustrated with Linux and/or Ubuntu and wish to remove it, without messing up your dual boot system and therefore stopping you booting into windows? 

You have a couple of options if that is the case. I would recommend using the Windows Installation CD to repair your MBR/Fixboot and then reformat the harddrive/partitions that Linux is installed on from the Computer Management tool with in Windows and reformat it as ntfs or FAT. (right click on 'my computer' and select 'manage' from windows).

Hope that Helps.
