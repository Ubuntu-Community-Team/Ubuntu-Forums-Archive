### Post by AlexandreP on 2013-05-11
Hi!

If you maxed out your Windows license, you will either need to free one of your license or buy a new one. You can consider this:

Freeing a license does not apply to an OEM license. An OEM license is tied to to machine or the peripheral along which it was provided. (When you buy a new computer with a Windows OEM license, you get it at a discounted price; the deal is you cannot transfer or reuse the license on another computer.) So freeing a license can only be done with a retail version of Windows. If you only have OEM license(s), then you know you cannot go that way: you will have to buy new a retail license.

To free a retail license, you only need to reformat a Windows installation from one of the computer. (You might have to call Microsoft and explain what happened to complete the activation.) Activating it on the second computer will automatically deactivate the license for the first computer. So going that way means you will have to remove Windows from one of your other computer. If you need to keep Windows in all your other computers, then you will need to buy a new license.

Windows 7 can still be found in some stores, both online stores and your local computer shop. Microsoft itself doesn't seem to sell Windows 7 anymore on its online store.
