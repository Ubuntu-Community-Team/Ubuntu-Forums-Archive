What you need to do is choose a version of Linux that is for beginners. UBUNTU12,4  or Fedora17 or Mint13 are three that come to mind.
Download the liveCD version, do some research to make a bootable USB Flash key,  and try each of them out.  Because they run from memory of from Flash, response time will be slow.

After you install a version that appeals to you, join the forums for that distribution.  Walk, don't run, and spend time getting familiar with your choice distribution.  After you are familiar with your choice (give it a few days), then look for applications that are directly related to your personal interests. These softwares can be from finance, games, software development, etc. etc.
