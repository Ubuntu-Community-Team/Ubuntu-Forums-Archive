### Post by fremantle on 2011-04-27
This is a remaster of elementary os from the elementary project. The default had several things missing so I decided to create a full featured version of it.

New things that are included:

[LIST]
[*]Pinta for image editing / Painting.
[*][Ease]("http://www.ease-project.org/") for presentations.
[*]Firefox 4 for internet surfing. I removed midori because many people think its not as full featured and stable as firefox.
[*][Exaile]("http://www.exaile.org/") for playing music.
[*][Openshot]("http://www.openshotvideo.com/") for video editing.
[*][BleachBit]("http://bleachbit.sourceforge.net/") for cleaning system.
[*]Unetbootin.
[*]All audio, video codecs.
[*]Java, Flash installed.
[/LIST]
Few important changes:

[LIST]
[*]All packages updated
[*]Desktop enabled
[*]Dexter, Shotwell, Transmission, Empathy upgraded to latest version
[*]Many 3rd party software ppas are added so that you can always have the latest software.
[/LIST]

### Post by blaede22 on 2011-04-29
> **Warpnow said:**

Going with Midori for Elementary never made sense to me. Midori was a good project. Still is...but at the moment..its behind. Chrome beats its speed with more stability and features. 

I went onto the IRC and asked why they picked Midori...the answer I got was because the Elementary developer and the Midori developer are friends...

Warpnow, that's not really accurate. They're not just "friends," they have a good designer/developer relationship. Daniel Fore, the leader of the elementary project, has worked extensively with Christian Dywan, the lead Midori developer, to vastly improve Midori's interface. The advantages of including Midori are that the interface is very snappy since it's 100% native GTK, it follows the elementary app guidelines so its interface is consistent with the other apps, and elementary has a good relationship with the developers so it's easier to suggest feature and interface improvements.

Now, the version of Midori that's currently in Jupiter is admittedly a bit lacking; compiling Midori and GTK's Webkit from git should give you a glimpse of the improvements that are being made. ;)
