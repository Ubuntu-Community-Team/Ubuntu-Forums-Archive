### Post by slixz85 on 2012-08-22
for easy using i suggest ubuntu or debian all the way. ubuntu is usually the best setup out of the box for wifi and graphics and such or you have a graphical interface to whatever you want to do over other distros that you have to compile things yourself which i am not a fan of even though whatever you compile works well. i like using minimalist desktops to know that my machine is pulling the best out of it all. i use bodhi which is at [www.bodhilinux.com](www.bodhilinux.com) it is very small 525mb download compared to 1gb on average or 2gb sometimes. once downloaded it basically gives only the bare necessities to get started. to get more stuff all you do is open synaptic package manager and go on the look.
if you like to not worry about downloading stuff maybe check out zorin. it is very light and fast also but gives you a few applications to get started. then there is even bigger os's. i suggest you just search through [www.distrowatch.com](www.distrowatch.com) if you somehow haven't already. it is the main stop for downloading distro's just click on the distro name and make sure it says ubuntu or debian at least although debian sometimes doesn't have most all work out of the box. the zorin i mentioned though is very nice distro.

also since ubuntu is easily used from windows converts it is quickly catching on, therefore i have noticed more applications available in its synaptic repositories to download without a problem. if u get another os you dont know much about you get get stuck not knowing how to do anything.
