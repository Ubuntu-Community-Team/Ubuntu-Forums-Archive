### Post by Alejandro Nova on 2011-12-26
No user of The Chakra Project has stepped in... and I find that almost strange, considering that I am one of them now, and I've been using Chakra several months, without major issues (other than those caused by me).

Let's watch the landscape first.

1. Nepomuk and Akonadi are two troublesome points, but those are the points where KDE's true power is. Nepomuk can index all of your files, tag them, and help you to find them. Akonadi can gather information from your mail accounts, your Exchange profiles, your Facebook account and your Google services, and you won't need even a mail client to do that.

2. As you all know, Akonadi and Nepomuk history has been troublesome. No KDE release has been trouble free in the Akonadi + Nepomuk department, and a lot of people, simply, says "disable them". The real game changer here is KDE 4.8 RC 1; it's the very first release where Nepomuk and Akonadi work without eating system resources for lunch. And it's awesome.

3. The only options if you want an updated KDE are:
- Kubuntu 11.10 + Kubuntu Backports.
- OpenSuSE 12.1 + special repos.
- Arch Linux.
- Chakra Project.
- Fedora + kde-redhat repo.

4. You need to enable unstable repositories in those four distros to get KDE 4.8 RC 1. However, if everything goes well, results will be, surprisingly, more stable than KDE 4.7.x.

My personal winner has been Chakra, because a) the installation process was painless, unlike Arch, b) it doesn't crash with the NVIDIA drivers like OpenSuSE, c) there's a lot of KDE exotic software in the repos, unlike Fedora or Kubuntu, and d) I want a distro who updates its KDE immediately after announcement, or even before.

Kubuntu is also a great choice, but you need to tame it, and the Ubuntu internals are a lot flakier than Chakra's. Also, you'll need to compile more software, and there is a division between proprietary and free software, unlike Chakra (Chakra is exactly how our systems are: if a FOSS alternative exists, we use it, but if non-free software is essential for us, let's pack it). Remember that Chakra isn't bitten by US patent laws; there is a strong Iranian Chakra community, and US software can't even reach Iran.
