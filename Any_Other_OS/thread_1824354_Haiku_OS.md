### Post by handy on 2011-08-14
The Haiku community is supported by some corporations. They raise funds from contributors & pay to get serious roadblocks removed by employing coders.

The improvements between alpha 2, & alpha 3, have been quite substantial.

I think that Haiku will become a popular media centre system. Its media player can handle just about everything already!

The filesystem is absolutely brilliant too. Have a read of this if you are interested:

### Post by natgab on 2011-08-20
Just wanted to chime in about Haiku.

It really is very nice little OS.  I have it installed on a partition to play around and see how it is coming along. I have a soft spot for it since it is based on the old BeOS made by ex-Apple people.

Plays HD videos fine, wireless works on WEP, no WPA2 yet.  Flash sorta works for some people, personally I couldn't get the port of GNASH to work. I would say its about Puppy Linux quick on my IBM X40 1.2GHz Thinkpad. I picked it because it was Haiku/Linux friendly :D

Can't complain about the 90s Desktop Environment, since so many Linux users love to use things like Fluxbox or LXDE. 

Give it a try on a USB stick, works really well that way.
