### Post by bcschmerker on 2011-07-20
The July 2011 issue of [LinUX Journal™](http://www.linuxjournal.com/) includes a cursory preview on p. 35 of a [Zorin PC™](http://www.zorinpc.com/), which comes with an apparently Debian-based LinUX distro built in; the software briefing at ZorinPC.com/Product.html#Three%20editions%2E%20Endless%20possibilities, mentions, among other items common to Debian-based distros, Ubuntu® One™ Music Store™ (a Canonical® service) in specific.  What do we know of Zorin OS' relation to other distros?  Is it an unofficial relative of Ubuntu® starting with 11.04 Natty Narwhal™, as I suspect the case to be?  (Good OS LLC's distro, gOS®, is known to have been derived from Ubuntu® 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon™ and 8.04-LTS Hardy Heron™ in all released variants in the firm's short lifetime.)

### Post by bcschmerker on 2011-07-21
Thanks for the confirmation on origin.  According to a listing at LinUX.com, [Zorin® OS](http://www.zorin-os.com/) dates back to September 2008, and therefore either 8.04-LTS Hardy or 8.09b2-pre Intrepid in development; and 9.04 Jaunty, if Zorin® OS 1.0 was in fact released May 2009.  Zorin OS 5.0, the current release as of July 2011, may very well be derived from 11.04 Natty.  Apparently engineered to replicate the GUI of [Microsoft® Windows® XP™](http://windows.microsoft.com/) as closely as practicable for a LinUX environment, Zorin® OS has GNOME and LXDE window managers available.
