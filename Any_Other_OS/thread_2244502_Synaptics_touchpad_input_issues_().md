### Post by dave131 on 2014-09-17
+1.  I would also watch where and on what you are placing it since you lost the feet off the bottom and the fan is on the bottom.  Chances are that bottom "fan" is actually a cool air intake to draw up around the inside of the laptop and cool it.  The fan is probably blowing out the back or a side.  Anything that interferes with that air access is going to cause your laptop to over heat, which can cause you all kinds of problems.  A build up of heat like that, continued over many times of using your PC, can GREATLY reduce the life expectancy of your laptop.  I would:

(1) be sure there is at least 1/4" (I don't know what that is in other units of measure) free space under your laptop at all times, and keep the sides and back clear.  

(2) consider 1 of the laptop "rests" that has a built-in USB powered fan.  This usually helps reduce the heat as well, and can keep things cooler on your leg or lap if you use your laptop while sitting down as well

Yes, on a lot of older laptops the heat does build up.  But you also need to be sure the fan is actually working correctly in your laptop while in Ubuntu.  There have been sporadic posts in the forum about fans not working on ubuntu on some laptops, but there seems to always be a resolutions as well.
