### Post by handy on 2011-06-21
I installed it on a 2GB drive over the top of my previous install, so it kept my configuration & any files I had.

It seems to be more polished (as you'd expect), the web browser from my brief usage is working better. 

I like how when I installed a couple of things they were automatically added to the menu & in the correct location. That's polish. :)

I'm too tired to relearn all that I've forgotten since my last serious look at Haiku, hopefully before the weeks out I'll get enthusiastic to do a pile of reading & fiddling about. 

My favourite love of Haiku is the file system. It is so clever you can use it to create a relational database:

[http://www.haiku-os.org/docs/userguide/en/workshop-filetypes+attributes.html](http://www.haiku-os.org/docs/userguide/en/workshop-filetypes+attributes.html)

I believe they put a bounty on the getting wireless working, & they will certainly be working to get the WPA encryption working as soon as they can. I'm sure that at the worst it will be organised by R1.

The video drivers are a bigger problem technically I think. At least they can benefit from AMD's assistance with the open-source driver stack, in combination with all of the work that the other people who have been working on that stuff have done.

It is hard to predict the future, but it certainly looks like it could take another two or three years before we reach the R1 milestone. If Haiku is lucky they will pick up some more sponsors so they can pay for more coding to be done.
