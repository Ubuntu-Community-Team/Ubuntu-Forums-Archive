If your primary concern is stability and reliability, then any of the popular consumer-oriented distributions, including Ubuntu, is much more likely to make you happy than a deliberately cutting edge distribution like Fedora. Fedora is a testbed for Red Hat. It's a great distribution, but Fedora is not aiming at the typical consumer.

I've used and enjoyed OpenSUSE.  It's extremely well supported and the documentation is about the best I've seen.

[Edit:  Debian Stable is just that:  very stable.  The tradeoff is that it's Gnome 2 and the software is 1-2 years old.  CentOS, as well as Scientific, recompile the source code of Red Hat Enterprise Linux, which means they can take advantage of Red Hat's patches and fixes.  They're also of the Gnome 2 era, but have reputations as solid systems.]

How about figuring out what the problem is with Ubuntu, and trying to solve the issue? That's what forums are helpful for, after all. Linux is (or at least used to be) about rolling up your sleeves and figuring things out.
