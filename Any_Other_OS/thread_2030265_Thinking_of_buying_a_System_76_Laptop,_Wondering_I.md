### Post by Version Dependency on 2012-07-21
> **Coriander Redgoat said:**
> It seems that Mint is much more customizable than Ubuntu with Unity, so that makes it preferable, but I'm new to Linux so I really don't know.

Well, you don't have to use the default Unity desktop that will come with your system76 machine.  You can install any desktop environment/window manager you want in any distro...including installing Cinnamon (the default desktop in Mint) in Ubuntu.  All it takes is about three lines copied and pasted into the terminal, and a couple of minutes of downloading.  Then your done.  Alot quicker and easier than formatting the drive and installing a new distro...just to try out a new desktop environment.

But if you are mostly a tinkerer who wants to play with extreme customization, then you probably want to go ahead and move to Kubuntu with the KDE desktop.  Gnome 3 and it's various shells (Gnome Shell, Unity, Cinnamon) aren't really that customizable yet.
