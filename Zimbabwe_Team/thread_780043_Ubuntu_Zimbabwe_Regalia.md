### Post by NIT006.5 on 2008-05-03
Hi Ronald,

As stated on the mailing list, I think this is a great idea! 

For use of the Ubuntu logo we will need approval from Canonical however. I have e-mailed their trademarks department but it could be a few weeks before we receive a response. 

I have also asked for approval of our derivative Ubuntu Zimbabwe logo.

I don't know of any clothing manufacturers off-hand who could do this, but I'll see what I can find out next week when I'm back at work.

Since we're approaching winter here, I think jackets should be the priority!

I think we will need to decide whether or not we want to use the official Ubuntu logo, the Ubuntu Zimbabwe logo, or both. I think simple designs work best, but perhaps that's just my taste. For example simple black jackets, shirts and caps with the colourful logo, works fine for me. I don't think we should over-complicate it. Another idea might be a black and/or white button-up shirt with a logo on the pocket? Could be nice for more "formal" occassions.

Raymond Swart was the man who came up with our Ubuntu Zim logo - perhaps we can seek his assistance on this too.

Of course this all depends on the response we receive from Canonical regarding the use of the logo, so perhaps we shouldn't get ahead of ourselves just yet.
