### Post by yakker.yak on 2008-10-28
Just came across this and looks pretty promising, especially if it's coming from Crucial. The suggestion is that these will be available by the end of the year. No word whether MLC or SLC based (or both).

Based on the article, it's not really clear to me whether the author is trying to say that right now the max size that we have for pci-e mini is 32 GB and for regular pci-e it's 64GB or whether Crucial will eventually release a 64GB pci-e mini. Note that the Dell Mini 9 requires an SSD for the pci-e mini interface.

If you're thinking of getting a Mini 9 with Ubuntu and need more storage than that provided from the 16GB maximum from Dell, may be worthwhile to get a 4GB or 8GB Mini 9 in the short term, save some money, and then upgrade to 32GB (or more?) in a couple of months.
