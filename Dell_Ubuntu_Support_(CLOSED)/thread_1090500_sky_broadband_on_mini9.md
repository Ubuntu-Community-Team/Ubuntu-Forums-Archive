### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-03-08
Sky broadband in the UK uses a standard wifi router (802.11b/g) to connect you to the internet.

There will be no problem with this whichever OS you choose.  Both will give you point, click, enter password, connected ease.

You should not need to change anything on the router, unless you use MAC-filtering for security (in which case, the solution would be the same, irrespective of OS).

The only (potential) problems will be getting mobile broadband connectors to behave with Ubuntu (e.g. Vodafone, Three etc), although the Three dongles are apparently well supported (with a few minor tweaks).
