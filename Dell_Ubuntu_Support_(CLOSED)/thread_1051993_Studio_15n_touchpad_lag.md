### Post by NTolerance on 2009-01-27
Just got a Dellbuntu Studio 15n last week.  I'm having a touchpad issue [described here](http://forum.notebookreview.com/showthread.php?t=309852) in which the cursor intermittently drops out every 5 seconds or so for a split second.  This gets really annoying as you never know when your cursor is going to jump far beyond your intended target due to the lag/catch up cycle.  

On initial analysis this appears to be a hardware problem unique to a subset of these laptops.  I booted into 8.10 installed on my HDD, 8.04 live CD, and a Windows XP live CD environment and reproduced the problem consistently.  I then noticed the forum thread I linked to previously has a few people mentioning a new Alps touchpad driver potentially fixing this, and also that some people had their touchpads replaced by Dell to no effect.  

Furthermore, I went to a local store yesterday and found a Studio 15 on display running Vista.  At the Vista login screen the touchpad behaved with the exact same laggyness.  This makes me begin to think that this is an inherent problem with all of these Alps touchpads, fixable only by running Windows Vista with a driver that somehow works around this defect.  

I'm going to go to a different store today and try to further prove my theory.  Dell has offered to replace my touchpad but if it may be pointless.  I normally use an external mouse, but it bothers me that I paid hundreds for this thing and it's not perfect.  

FYI I have Gsynaptics installed/configured which makes no difference in regards to this problem.
