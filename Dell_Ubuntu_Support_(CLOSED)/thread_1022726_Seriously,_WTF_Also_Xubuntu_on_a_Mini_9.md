### Post by james_xxx on 2008-12-27
Jrusso2, I would concur with you that Xubuntu, at least, is not as dramatically lighter than Ubuntu as many have apparently been lead to believe. I think, however, that has more to do with the apps, etc., that XFCE has been packaged with in Xubuntu than anything else. Xubuntu pulls in quite a lot from Gnome. A person can configure a fairly light system, for example, by doing a minimal install of Ubuntu, then adding XFCE along with lighter-weight apps. (This is probably all information you already know.) I still think, though, that Xubuntu is at least marginally lighter than Ubuntu.

Sirebral, I apologize if I somehow tempted you to flaming. I did not just 'zip right past' the intent of your original post, but I guess I didn't clarify myself very well, either. First off, I don't think that Dell chose the GUI it did for the Mini 9 due to Gnome/Netbook-Remix supposedly having a smaller footprint. It chose that GUI due to its having been optimized for smaller screens, while at the same time still providing the same basic applications familiar to Ubuntu's substantial fan base. Secondly, it could be argued that XFCE lacks some of the out-of-the-box user friendliness possessed by the Gnome desktop environment. (Think of things like the process with which icons are added to the panel, among a number of other examples.) The tendency is, the lighter the GUI, the steeper the learning curve -- even though, to many of us, that steeper learning curve is worth it. 

Also, LPIA has nothing to do with the desktop environment. LPIA packages are, as I understand it, supposed to be packages that have been compiled in a way that maximizes performance on low-power CPUs, like the Intel Atom. I'm really not too sure how much difference compiling these packages in this way really makes. Maybe someone more knowledgeable can fill in more details on this. The Mini 9s do ship with the full Gnome environment. There is an option in the menu that allows a person to switch back and forth between the Netbook-Remix GUI, and the Gnome GUI one normally sees in Ubuntu. Additionally, any other desktop environment/window manager one wants, including XFCE, can be added (or removed) at will, as they should all be in the repositories. 

I also prefer XFCE by far to Gnome, but would I pick XFCE to be the default desktop environment for mass-marketed netbooks, that will very often wind up in the hands of folks completely new to Linux? No, but that is just my opinion, of course.

BTW, I only posted the response that I did earlier to let you know that installing Xubuntu on the Mini 9 could be done quite easily, in the off chance you did not already realize this (which you probably did). I really wasn't intending to belittle your ideas in any way.

Anjilslaire, thanks for the info on network-manager, I will have to check that out :-) .

At any rate, good luck with all of your Mini 9s! Peace, Sirebral!
