### Post by stopie on 2009-01-18
I'd recommend making a liveUSB, as its how I installed Ubuntu on my mini 9, and found it to be very painless, and I was able to do it in a very short period of time. All on top of that, I was also able to do it with very basic knowledge of linux, as it was my leap of faith into the big world of linux after using windows for my 20 years of existence. 

If you have a working machine thats running Ubuntu now, System>Administration>Create USB Startup Disk. Make sure your USBdrive is inserted and follow the prompts.

If you have another OS, [_UNetbootin_]("http://unetbootin.sourceforge.net/") will work just fine. Download [_Ubuntu_]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/WhatIsUbuntu/desktopedition") if you dont already have the .iso needed. Then just follow UNetbootin prompts.

This is the _[site]("http://www.ubuntumini.com/2008/10/installing-ubuntu-on-dell-inspiron-mini.html")_ that I used as my walkthrough to make the switch.

Hope it helps!
