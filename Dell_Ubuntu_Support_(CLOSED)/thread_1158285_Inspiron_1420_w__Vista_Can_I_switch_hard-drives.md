### Post by coffeeaddict22 on 2009-05-13
Couple of suggestions.
If you've got the space, why not add it instead of removing the old one?  The problem you'll run into is the hardware is going to be _just_ different enough to make you tear your hair out.  If you add the drive in, you'll have less to fix when you have to give the PC back.

Otherwise, may as well give it a shot.  The most likely thing to cause grief will be graphics settings; if they're set too high on your Ubuntu disc you'll fry your display if you're really unlucky.  Might be worth going into a recovery console to start and choosing "Attempt to fix my Video settings" or whatever the option is; it runs xfix, and should prevent that.
