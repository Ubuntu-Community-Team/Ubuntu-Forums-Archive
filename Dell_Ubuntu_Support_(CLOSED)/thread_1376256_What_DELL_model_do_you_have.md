### Post by Kir_B on 2010-06-14
Dell inspiron 531s, which came with an Amd 64 Athlon X2 (4000+)  processor, 1 gigabyte of ram, a Dell 18.5 inch lcd monitor and Windoze  Vista home premium (32bit).
 
 I've made a few alterations to this box. I had several problems with the  installed operating system that lead to me, on three separate  occasions, having to use the option "restore to factory condition"  (restores the operating system back to day one). After the first time  using the system restore, causing the loss of my contents and  configurations (Microsoft installing the "[.net framework]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mozilla_Firefox#.Net_Framework_3.5_Service_Pack_1")" crap into my O.S.  &  Firefox was a major motivating factor also), I began the quest  for a _different_ operating system, which ended up being Ubuntu  9.04 Jaunty Jackelope (I've since upgraded to 9.10 Karmic Koala, and  now, 10.04 Lucid Lynx). One of the problems I had in choosing a  Distribution, was that the onboard graphics (nvidia 6150se, if I  remember correctly) had severe issues with many Linux distributions.  Sometimes they wouldn't boot at all, getting stuck in busybox, init or initramfs. On the more successful ones (Ubuntu's), I would get a _quarter  screen_ containing the _lower right_ hand quarter of the desktop, located  in the _upper left_ hand quarter of my monitor. This wasn't a useful working  environment and researching the issue showed **_no_** results to remedy the  situation. I stumbled upon Ubuntu & Xubuntu with the wubi and I was  hooked, but I still had the quarter screen problem (outside of the wubi  installation) that seemingly, only I was having with the live cd's.  After booting the Ubuntu and Xubuntu 9.04 live cd's twenty or thirty  times into quarter screen desktop, I noticed that there was the option  to boot the cd in "safe graphics mode". This option gave me my first  full screen look at Linux without the use of wubi. Once I found "safe  graphics" mode, my fear of trying a full installation went away and I've  never looked back. 
 
 After installing Ubuntu beside the vista partition, I of course went about installing various  applications, one of which was the compiz, for all the extra  desktop effects. The installation of the proprietary drivers went without incident, but the onboard graphics were inadequate  at best and couldn't quite handle some of the features that compiz  offered (let alone some light gaming). After a tonne of research into  the hardware upgrade options for this box, I finally settled on needing a  video card, as this was one of the cheaper upgrade options. I ended up  purchasing a [geforce 9600 gt]("http://www.newegg.ca/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16814162030"), which once installed, remedied the  situation quite nicely, leaving me wondering, what kind of improvement a  gigabyte if ram would be. (Note: I also upgraded the fans for the case  and cpu)
 
 Now, to finally get to the point that has made this post as lengthy as  it is. The new graphics card not only remedied my systems shortcomings  graphically, making it a bit quicker and nicer to use, it also had the  bonus of allowing me to boot into any Ubuntu variation's live cd  environment, without having to use the "safe graphics" mode. Another  discovery, is that the new video card gave this box the ability to boot  all (if not, most) of the other Linux live cd's, that up until now, I'd  only been able to check out on other peoples computers.
 
 In closing, the graphics chipset was the only compatibility issue that  I've encountered and the few other issues I've had in the past nine  months were brought on by myself (rookie mistakes), and my lack of  education for Linux _and_ grub2.
 
 Sorry for such a large entry, but I thought that my experience of  incompatibility regarding the 6150 chipset issue was worth noting.
 
 Peace to you all, Kirby :lol:

Side note: It only boots the Ubuntu now, as the upgrade to Lucid left the Dell utility, recovery and Vista partitions un-bootable. :confused: The vista will be easy to recover, but the other two are another story, as there is little to no information available to assist me!
