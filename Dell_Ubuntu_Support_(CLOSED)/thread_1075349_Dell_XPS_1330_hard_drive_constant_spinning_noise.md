### Post by TobyReynolds on 2009-02-20
If you Google "xps hard drive noise" there are many, many discussions of this topic, but they almost all seem to concern scratchy hard drive noises being amplified through the laptop case which Dell now seem to have fixed.

My problem is different, in that I can constantly hear the hard drive (7200 rpm free-fall sensor) spinning at what I presume to be maximum rpm. It's not excessively noisy -- when the fan kicks in this is noticeably louder -- but I am in an absolutely silent room and it is even just about audible from the far side of the room, sounding something like a second fan inside the laptop. I applied the power-saving scripts from [this HOWTO thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=847773") and when on battery power the hard drive spins down after the alloted hdparm -S time, and it's like aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaahhhhhh, silence! My girlfriend's laptop on the other hand (older Inspiron 6400), is almost completely silent when the fan isn't running, apart from a very slight electronic buzz when I put my ear right against the case.

So I am interested in the experience of other XPS laptop owners: is this noise normal, or should I be calling Dell to report it?

### Post by remistp on 2010-04-04
Hi!

I've just bought a new studio xps 1640 yesterday, I haven't notice that spinning noise but this morning, with everyone sleeping and no tv playing.  I find it a little bit annoying; I was thinking at first that it was the fan trying to cool the laptop but I monitored the core temperature and it was always around 35-40 degree.

I opened the laptop to see where the hard drive was and it's just on the left side of the touchpad where my left palm rest.  I can now confirm the sound is coming from the hard drive which is constantly spinning , I can even feel the spinning and a little bit of heat with my hand on the side of the touchpad.

The hard drive could have been in another place at least to don't feel the heat!

I don't know if this issue come from a faster HD (7200 rpm) or if the 5400 rpm do the same.  The solution would be to go for a Solid-state drive, which is a little bit expensive....

Let me know if you have something new on this topic!
