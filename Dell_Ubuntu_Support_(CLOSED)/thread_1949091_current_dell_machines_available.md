You've got two entirely different systems, most likely with different generations of processor, different chipsets, even different standards of RAM.  We can only assume that your newer computer has superior graphics and processing power, not to mention faster DDR3 RAM.

 


Of course, a more serious issue here is your advocacy of 6GB of mismatched RAM.  Unless you have a system with 3 or 4 slots, you're running two sticks of mismatched RAM.  I'd assume it's one 4GB stick and one 2GB stick.  You can get away with it but it's not a great idea.  Most Windows and Linux PCs do just fine with mismatched RAM, but Macs tend to be rather picky about RAM.  And yes, a lot of mainstream OEMs ship computers with mismatched sticks of RAM.  Why sell a PC with 6GB of RAM?  It leaves the possibility of upgrading the user to 8GB - more profit.
