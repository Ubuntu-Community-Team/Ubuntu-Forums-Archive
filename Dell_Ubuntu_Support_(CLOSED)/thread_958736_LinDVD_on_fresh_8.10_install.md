### Post by gbrainin on 2008-10-25
The "fresh OS install" (as opposed to upgrading using the upgrade manager) probably killed the copy of LinDVD that came with your computer.  AFAIK, no available version of Ubuntu, from Dell or otherwise, includes LinDVD.

You could, of course, restore 7.10 from your restore partition (assuming you haven't gotten rid of it), but other than that I am not aware of any way to restore the (legal) DVD playback capability.

To my knowledge, Dell has never given a straight answer to this question, nor have they followed through with their promise to those of us who bought computers with 7.04 that LinDVD would be available for purchase as an upgrade.

Actually, Dell Ubuntu phone support is not Canonical.  Dell provides hardware-only support for computers sold with Linux; Canonical (I believe, not having purchased or used their support) provides software-only support.

I wouldn't think that Canonical would provide support for third-party commercial (non-free) software (LinDVD) installed by a hardware vendor (Dell), but I could be wrong.
