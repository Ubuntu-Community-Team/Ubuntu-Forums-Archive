### Post by mikewhatever on 2009-09-03
There shouldn't be any speed issues for Open Office work, since 1GB is quite adequate for text editing. That said, you might find the screen size too small. If you need to do some serious office work and don't enjoy tiny fonts, I wouldn't recommend any netbook, other then the now retired Dell mini 12.

PS: If you think you need more then 1GB of RAM, take a look at HP mini 1000. You can customize it with 2GB at least in the US.

### Post by Greyed on 2009-09-04
You didn't mention which OO application you were interested.  Attached are screenshots of the two most common, Writer and Calc, taken from my Mini 10v.  This should give you an idea of how small the screen is.  Note that I have done some modification of GNome that came with Dell's stock 8.04.  I can't stand the top-and-bottom bars, especially on such a tiny screen, so the top bar is removed.  This gives 24 pixels back to the application.  These are stock startups of both applications, nothing really loaded.

If I were to use them on a regular basis I would probably do something about the toolbars at the top.  They take up a lot of vertical space, which is at a premium on this machine, for very little gains most of the time.  I'd either remove them or see about shifting them to the sides where they would take up less of the available screen space, percentage wise.
