### Post by andreasis on 2008-12-08
I've had too many experiences with Dell and their carelessness/ignorance.

The way to approach this situation is the following:

The two things that I would recommend doing: restore your notebook to its factory software settings (reload Dellubuntu on it).  Call tech support and tell them that you want a replacement, and not any more support.  They will replace it for you, shipping paid by them.

Then, after you get the replacement, call them and say that you want to return the laptop.  They will then offer you an incentive to keep it, usually an amount of money.  If for any rare reason they do not, just say that you changed your mind, and you're still left with your (hopefully working) replacement laptop.

This will work.

### Post by gbrainin on 2008-12-08
> **yakker.yak said:**
> If it's an issue with your video card then it's a hardware issue and there should be no need for Ubuntu support (or that's my understanding).

Dell's Linux support is (or was) hardware-only--software support is an optional add-on, and is provided directly by Canonical.

The upshot of which is, talking to the non-Linux support drones about a hardware issue on a machine that was purchased with Linux on it is about as useful as trying to teach a pig to sing.  I had a bum floppy drive on the computer I bought, which could be (and was) diagnosed by using the Dell hardware testing partition.  I mistakenly called the general support number and tried to explain that I booted into the hardware test partition and it told me the hardware is bad, here's the error code.  I could not get them to stop telling me to click on the "Start" button and sorry, we do not support "lin-icks."

Once I called the Linux support line, I got a very helpful person who quickly realized that I knew what I was talking about and arranged to send me a replacement part.  Unfortunately, it seems that the Linux support line may have gone the way of the dodo.

One can hope that this is a temporary problem, but if it isn't, then it's a very big deal.  Without the Linux support line, we in effect have no hardware support for Dell Linux purchases.
