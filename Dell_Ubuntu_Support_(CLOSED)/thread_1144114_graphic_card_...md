### Post by coffeeaddict22 on 2009-04-30
Hi,
Welcome to Linux!
Your graphics card is working (That's how you've got a picture to look at :-) ). 3D graphics on Linux with some cards is only available if you download the driver.  The driver is what's called a "binary blob"; the manufacturer makes it up, but doesn't let anyone see how it works.  Because this is not the way the Free and Open Sourced Software world works, it's reluctantly included in Ubuntu.  In this case, that means you've got to click on the button that says "Activate".  If you closed that window, its in System/Administration/Hardware Drivers.  Have a look at [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-1084341.html") for a bit more information.

Like the window says, it's for 3D acceleration (and 2D in newer cards).  If you get all heated up about proprietary drivers, you don't NEED it.
