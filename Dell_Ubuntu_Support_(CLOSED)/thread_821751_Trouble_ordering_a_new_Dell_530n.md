### Post by gbrainin on 2008-06-08
> **fromgi said:**
> I would certainly prefer getting it right the first time and lesson my chance for incompatibility errors upgrading to 8.10 on my own.  Thanks!

I just wanted to address this.  Dell has not yet come out with a customized version of 8.04, which is probably why they are still selling units with 7.10 installed.  However, as a general rule, newer versions of Ubuntu are more compatible than older ones, given the same hardware.  So I would not worry too much about compatibility with upgrades.

Further, numerous people (myself included) have tried 8.04 on an Inspiron 530, generally with success.  The only problem _introduced_ in 8.04 is the "IDE" vs. "RAID" mode hard drive issue, which is a known problem with an easy workaround.  (There are other new issues, but they only seem to affect laptops.)

All that having been said, you have to expect some learning curve and setup challenges with Linux, especially with a version that has a new release every 6 months.  On the plus side, this board provides better and cheaper tech support than Microsoft, so you're already ahead of the game.

Whatever you decide to order, good luck!
