### Post by misGnomer on 2009-08-11
Well the Vostro 1014 now [shows prices in Dell's Singaporean site]("http://www1.ap.dell.com/sg/en/business/vostronb/laptop-vostro-1014/pd.aspx?refid=laptop-vostro-1014&s=bsd&cs=sgbsd1") and the same base model goes for SG$799, or US$553. And that is with MS vista; there's no Ubuntu preloaded version at the moment.

Maybe they will also use coupon tricks in Singapore to sell MS-ware like in the US, but at least the list price for the vista-encumbered ("home basic") model is about US$155 more in Singapore than the Ubuntu version in Hong Kong. There are no prices yet for other markets.

[Dell India now has listing for it too, although there's no pricing yet.]("http://www1.ap.dell.com/in/en/business/notebooks/laptop-vostro-1014/pd.aspx?refid=laptop-vostro-1014&s=bsd&cs=inbsd1")

If the ~US$400 pricing (with some tax and import duty variation) for the base model applies across markets, these 14-inchers might be quite an attractive option as preloaded and fully supported Linux / Ubuntu laptops.

One question though: Since they are sold under the Vostro label and listed as offerings for the small/medium businesses and corporations, can individual customers still order them?
