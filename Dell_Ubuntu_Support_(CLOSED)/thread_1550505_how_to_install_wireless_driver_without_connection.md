### Post by mörgæs on 2010-08-11
By far the best is to have a wired internet connection while installing. Wired net cards are more or less standard, but the wirefree cards can cause some trouble. 

In general, hardware and particularly graphic cards are the main problem for Linux. Not all vendors support Linux well - sometimes because the market is to small, sometimes because the company behind a major, closed-source operative system has convinced them to do so. Best is to boot with a live CD before installing. 

Feel free to ask again, if more questions arise.
