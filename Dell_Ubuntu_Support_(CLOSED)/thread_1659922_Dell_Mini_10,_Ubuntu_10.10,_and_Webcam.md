### Post by fjgaude on 2011-01-05
I'm not sure I can help but I've gone through much upgading of my mini 10n, the one with the 1366 x 768 high-res screen. It worked perfectly coming from Dell with Ubuntu 8.04.

I decided to upgrade to 10.10. The screen was stuck at 1024 x 768, not good. The video camera worked. I installed the PSB driver from:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCardsPoulsbo/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsVideoCardsPoulsbo/)

From there I got educated about the Intel GMA 500 chipset (Poulsbo).

There is a 10.04 netbook upgrade that Dell offers which with all the little patches added by me it works perfectly, suspend, etc. I also have 10.10 using the Poulsbo PSB and it is fast, the fastest, but the camera cannot be made to work.

Read over, study the site above and see what your options are.

Let us know how you are doing.
