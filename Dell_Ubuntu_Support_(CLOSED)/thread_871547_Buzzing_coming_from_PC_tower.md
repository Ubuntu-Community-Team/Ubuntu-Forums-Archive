### Post by arvevans on 2008-08-06
It is too late for you now that you have taken it in to the PC hospital, but for future reference:

[INDENT]Most all PC fans have very cheap bearings (even those with expensive ball bearing fans), and these fans work in a hostile environment (running long hours sucking air and dirt through them).

If you are careful you can repair almost all of these fans yourself.  With the computer turned off and power plug removed from the wall outlet, carefully unplug the fan leads from the motherboard and unbolt the fan from it's mounting.  There should be a sticky backed foil cover over the center bearing of the fan.  Peel this back until you can see the fan shaft and bearing.  Add one drop of light oil (just one drop) and spin the fan by hand for several revolutions.  Wipe off any excess oil and re-stick the foil bearing cover in it's original position.  Mount the fan back in it's original mounting, making sure that the blades rotate freely when you are done.  Plug the fan power cable back into it's original position.  Now you can restart your computer and the fan should be quiet for another year or so before you have to do this again.[/INDENT]

It would be interesting to know how many service shops actually replace noisy fans versus how many just lubricate them and charge you for a new fan.
