### Post by tgalati4 on 2011-01-27
Sometimes older CD drives need cleaning.  Try a gentle Q-Tip to the lens.  This requires removal and disassembly which is not fun.  Try reburning DSL on another CD-R using a more expensive, brand name, and burn it at the slowest speed.  Older CD drives have trouble with CD-R disks because CD-R's were developed later and the old drives aren't sensitive enough to read them.

Your option is to swap the drive with a newer drive or one from another machine.  You can also remove the hard drive and put it in another machine then install DSL to it.  Swap drives and boot into DSL.
