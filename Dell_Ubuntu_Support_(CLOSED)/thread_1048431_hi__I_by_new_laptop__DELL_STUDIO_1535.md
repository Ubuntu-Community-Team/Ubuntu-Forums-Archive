### Post by ridgeland on 2009-01-24
Hi 3obad,
If you partition your PC for it you can have multiple Linux installations on your PC.  My PC (a Dell desktop) has two hard drives each with 15 partitions.  I don't dual-boot, rather multi-boot. Grub offers me about a dozen options of what to boot. I keep the latest Ubuntu, Mandriva, Fedora, OpenSuSE on my PC and multiple olders versions of the same.  Even a couple of Linux partitions for reckless testing.  I give each OS partition 10 GB for "/" then have a common /Data partition for all the things like mp3, jpg, odt, bookmarks etc.
Have fun distro-hopping.
