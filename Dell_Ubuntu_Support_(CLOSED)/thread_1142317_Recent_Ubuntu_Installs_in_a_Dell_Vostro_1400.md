### Post by ullaspa on 2009-04-29
Hi,
I have had a terrible experience with Jaunty on my Vostro 1510. Intrepid worked like a charm. I did a fresh install of Jaunty over my Intrepid and I should say that this has been my worst Ubuntu experience till date. None of the other releases have given me the kind of problems that I have encountered with Jaunty on my Vostro 1510. Apparently, these problems are also common to all Vostro editions.

First, the keyboard and the touchpad do not work when cold booting. One needs to press the capslock or some random key just when the boot-up screen starts for both of them to work. An external USB mouse works fine, but is of no use without the keyboard. I did modifications to the menu.lst as suggested on other threads, but no luck.

Second, there are random system freezes. I did go in and switch off the desktop effects and thought that this was solved, till I encountered it today morning.

One common problem in both Intrepid and Jaunty is the shifting of the caret when typing. This leads to text getting mixed-up even while typing simple emails. But this is ignorable and corrected easily by looking at the screen when typing text. But, it is a major irritation though.

I'd stick with Intrepid till there are patches for the keyboard and touchpad problems for Jaunty.:(

Regards,
Ulhas
