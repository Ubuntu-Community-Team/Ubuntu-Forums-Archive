### Post by uniquename999 on 2010-04-29
Hey Iceland! How's the volcano doing? But I digress ...

In most web browsers you can override the
background image (and text foreground and background).

For example, in the Seamonkey web browser (that I use
because of the great, heavy-duty Bookmark Management
that it has inherited through its previous incarnations
as Netscape and as Mozilla),
go to Edit > Preferences > Appearance > Colors.

At the bottom of the 'Colors' panel,
there are a couple of radio buttons labeled:

1. Always use the colors and background specified
   by the web page

2. Use my chosen colors, ignoring the colors and
   background image specified.

Radiobutton 1 is the default. Simply click on
radiobutton 2. 

You will see the result immediately, on whatever
web page you are currently viewing.

(I suspect that you may be seeing a darker background
than I see on my monitor at 1024x768 resolution because
you are using a very high resolution monitor --- or
because your brightness setting is rather low.

I have a friend with a Mac high-res monitor who has
found some of my screenbackgrounds much darker,
reportedly, than I found them to be on my old 800x600
monitor.)
