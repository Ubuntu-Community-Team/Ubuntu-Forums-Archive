### Post by MarzEz on 2010-07-21

While I was running 9.04, I would often have trouble resuming from hibernation, and some things on my screen would appear as completely corrupted, while most things appeared fine.
I recently upgraded to 10.04, and Ubuntu crashes whenever I close the lid, resume from hibernation, or wake up from sleep mode.
I use my laptop from school work, and the constant instability is becoming a major problem.
Has anyone got any suggestions for how to improve my stability?

____

Machine Specs:
1.0 GHz Pentium III M - steps down to 733MHz (Not overclocked to 1.00GHz)
No CD/Floppy drive, 2 batteries.
256mb ram
12gb HDD
kernel 2.6.32-23 (generic)

### Post by theozzlives on 2010-07-21
If your specs are current, and you want to do some real computing, I'd recommend more RAM. I had a hard time over 2 years ago with 256 MB. While I can't guarantee more stability, it would make your experience more enjoyable. 

From your specs, I can't figure out if you're overclocked or not. Overclocking can make your system unstable and shorten the life of your processor. The processor is designed to run at a certain speed.
