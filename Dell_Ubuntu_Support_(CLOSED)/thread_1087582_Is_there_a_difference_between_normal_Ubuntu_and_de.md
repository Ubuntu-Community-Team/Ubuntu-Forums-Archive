### Post by sirebral on 2009-03-05
This is what I like about DELL's 8.04.  **Stability**.  Here is why.  I am a recent Linux user, only starting right at the end of 7.10, and one of the main problems I have faced is little bugs that just cause headaches.  With DELL's Mini 9 I never face a compatibility issue that causes a head ache.  I really love that!

This is what I dislike about DELL's 8.04. **Featureless**.  I know they are minor but it is kinda painful.  Part of the reason is because they have used the LPIA version (Low Power on Intel Architecture).  We can thank Intel for that Architecture.  Because of this LPIA there is less support for the cool stuff (Update Manager does not display the updates, CPU Scaling displays but you can not Scale the CPU, Metacity is the Windows Manager and you get *ONE* Setting, Windows need to be resized a lot, some others.)

My Desktop uses 8.10 and I really like 8.10 a lot more.  I'm with armandh that 8.10 is the better OS.  Again though I have faced some stability issues.  YouTube can kill the sound, Pidgin and Skype break sound, Hibernate doesn't work, and these are getting less.

I've read a lot of people are happy with 8.10 on their Mini 9 and have no stability issues.  I didn't want to risk it after I tried the DELL version.  The only thing that prevented my from wiping the LPIA OS from the Mini 9 was the difficulty I had (No USB Flash Drive big enough) or I would be running Xubuntu.  Now that I like it I want to keep it and show support for DELL's decision.  I still want Xubuntu 8.10 on it though.

### Post by snowpine on 2009-03-05
I just got a Dell Mini 9 this week. Within 15 minutes, I replaced the Dell Ubuntu with Crunchbang 8.10. The problem is that the Dell Ubuntu uses the lpia kernel and its own special repositories. This means the software selection is limited and a little slower to upgrade. The only advantage of the lpia kernel is perhaps 10 extra minutes of battery life.

On a positive note, I thought the Dell Ubuntu was nice looking and easy to use. I would recommend it to a Linux newbie with limited needs (web surfing and the like). Just not to my tastes.
