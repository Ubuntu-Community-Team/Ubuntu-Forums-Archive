### Post by Paulplex on 2010-04-30
> **jdkchem said:**
> I was mistakenly under the impression that with a laptop I could go from one wireless access point to another without having to reboot.  Not so if you are unfortunate to be stuck with a crappy broadcom based wireless card.  Broadcom is quite possibly the worst hardware vendor in the computer business and the exact reason why my next laptop will come from someone other than Dell.  What makes this more pathetic is that I bought this laptop with Ubuntu pre-installed.I agree about the Broadcom part: however, when you buy a Dell, it's all customisable; if ever you buy a new one in the future, do so from the Dell website and ensure you buy it with an Intel wireless card.

Still, it's not all doom and gloom if you have a Broadcom wireless card: I had a D510 and inherited a second hand D610 (with the dreaded Broadcom wireless card) and you can easily swap the wireless cards around - if my brain is in gear, the wireless card is installed under a hatch on the underside of the laptop (it might be under the keyboard on a D610 - it's certainly on the underside on the D510).

Take a look at the Dell service manual for the D610 ([here]("http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/latd610/en/sm_en/index.htm")) and if you don't have another Dell you can rip an Intel card from, see if you can find one on eBay - a quick search today showed me three Ebay users who were selling what you want for less than £20, including delivery ([here]("http://shop.ebay.co.uk/?_from=R40&_trksid=p3907.m570.l1313&_nkw=dell+d610+intel+wireless&_sacat=See-All-Categories")).

If you don't have the bluetooth module for your D610, that's again very easy to install (it's connected under the battery compartment) - again, a quick eBay search shows me sellers knocking these out at around £20 also ([here]("http://shop.ebay.co.uk/i.html?_nkw=dell+d610+bluetooth&_sacat=0&_trksid=p3286.m270.l1313&_dmpt=UK_Computing_LaptopAccess_RL&_odkw=dell+d610+intel+wireless&_osacat=0&bkBtn=")).
