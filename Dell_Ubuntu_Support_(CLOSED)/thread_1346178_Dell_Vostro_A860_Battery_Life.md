### Post by teward on 2009-12-04
First off, Windows and Ubuntu are **different.  this will NEVER EVER IN A QUINTRILLION YEARS change.**

Second, I can get a good 5 hours out of my Dell Latitude E6500's battery.  I installed a program called kpowersave, and it allows you to have different powersave options.  Useful especially when I need to conserve my CPU usage (i restrict it to minimum which is 800MHz on a 2.66GHz processor).  It can set various options, as well.  It even has predefined power settings, including Powersave (which I auto-boot to).

And if you get it, stick it in your startup sequence too.

### Post by yossell on 2009-12-04
Allow me also to recommend that you give powertop a go.

I have a Dell M1330. When I first worked with Ubuntu, I too found that battery life was much worse compared to Windows. That changed when I installed Powertop: 

[http://www.lesswatts.org/projects/powertop/](http://www.lesswatts.org/projects/powertop/)

To give it a go: Install it, run powertop as root, and follow its suggestions - and see how it works out for you.
