### Post by benmoran on 2009-08-28
They are pretty much all 64-bit these days. From a quick look at the specs of the 1525, it looks to come with a 64-bit processor in most configurations. Microsoft has been behind in adopting 64-bit, so it's not rare to see 32-bit OS on a 64-bit machine. 

If it was me i'd burn a 64-bit disc. However if he just wants a quick look and you don't want to waste a disc, just burn the 32-bit version. Go 64-bit for the final install though, if he decides to make the switch.
