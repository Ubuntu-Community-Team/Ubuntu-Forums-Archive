### Post by blur xc on 2010-08-20
I don't know about any of those releases directly from Dell.  I've only run Ubuntu nbr downloaded from the main ubuntu website.

Try Jolicloud from what I hear it's the smoother running linux for that computer.  I was going to give it a try, but I'm not keen on the whole "cloud" business.  10.04 runs like crap on the mini 1010.  Get 9.10, and hit up the mega gma500 thread.  The broadcom chip, and the gma500 will be almost guaranteed to make you work hard at getting it to a usable state.  Your life will be made a lot easier with a temporary wired Internet connection while installing everything.

Good luck.

I won't lie, your hardware has very poor support under most flavors of Linux. I have heard that Jolicloud is the only one that will work "out of the box". (note: I have not personally used Jolicloud and cannot vouch for its quality. It is a very new project.) I can't say for sure as my Mini 9 has slightly different hardware. Anyway, there are a lot of existing threads on this topic here on Ubuntuforums... use the Search feature and look for Broadcom and GMA500 and you'll see what I mean. :(
