### Post by drs305 on 2010-09-09
SallyVolved, welcome to the Ubuntu Forums.  :-)

You should be able to interrupt Grub booting by holding down the SHIFT key. 

Prior to that, entering the BIOS setup is usually accomplished by pressing the ESC, DEL or one of the function (F*) keys. I don't have a DELL but you can probably find out the key by doing a search. Some Dells use F2 but if that doesn't work check for your particular model.

### Post by SallyVolved on 2010-09-11
Problem solved.  I am more of a desktop gal and new to the netbook/notebook things.  When BIOS starts and says hit F12 (or whatever it is on your computer) that does not mean hit Fn/F12 like you would do with an OS loaded.  It means hit F12.
