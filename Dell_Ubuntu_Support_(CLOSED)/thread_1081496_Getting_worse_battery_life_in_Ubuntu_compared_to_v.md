### Post by sdennie on 2009-02-27
No, the built in battery program is just fine.  The two programs are reporting battery life in different ways.  The first one is trying to calibrate to your battery life based on your actual usage of your machine.  After a while of using your battery (especially if you drain it to very low), it will become more accurate in telling you how much battery life you will actually get.  The second program is probably telling you your battery life based on intermittent polls of battery drain.

The first method will eventually give you more accurate information for "average usage" while the second will usually give you more accurate information for "if you keep doing what you are doing right now until the battery dies".  Both could be considered correct so, it's a matter of preference.  If you use your machine on battery a lot, you'll know how long it lasts based on what you are doing so, you probably won't use either and instead just look at the percent battery left and compute that into your own estimate based on what you are doing.
