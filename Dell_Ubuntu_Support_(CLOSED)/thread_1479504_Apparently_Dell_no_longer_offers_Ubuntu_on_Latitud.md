Easy to do. The only "warning" is that make sure the power plug to the motherboard and from your power supply match up. On the newer boards, they use a power plug with 24 taps, some more and some less. Take a close look at the motherboard you are replacing and ensure that the new one can be powered. Might want to look into a used computer or refurbished. Many businesses on the enterprise scale, unload machines that are only a couple or several years old, and some local computer shops pick them up and resell them. That is the great advantage of Linux in that it does not require super hardware like Windows.
