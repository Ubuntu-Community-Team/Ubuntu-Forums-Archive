### Post by snowpine on 2009-11-04
Mixed feelings. I love my Dell Mini 9--it is a great little computer (except for the non-Linux friendly Broadcom card) at a great price--and I got a smug little sense of superiority by ordering it with Ubuntu pre-installed instead of Windows.

BUT I deleted the bizarre "Dellbuntu" 8.04 within 15 minutes (tested many distros; currently running Arch) and have never once used Dell for software support. Dell is a great hardware manufacturer, but the thought of turning to them for OS support is kind of ridiculous. 

The problem with offering Ubuntu preinstalled is that a new version comes out every 6 months. If you're a hardware manufacturer, figure it takes at least 6 months to develop the product, manufacture it, market it, and get it into the consumer's hands. Dell tried to get around this problem by offering 8.04 (the long term support release) but a lot of people ripped them for doing so. It is a lose-lose situation for the manufacturer in my opinion, trying to offer a Linux distro that is a constant moving target.

In my ideal fantasy world, hardware would be hardware, and the end user would choose & install the operating system of choice.
