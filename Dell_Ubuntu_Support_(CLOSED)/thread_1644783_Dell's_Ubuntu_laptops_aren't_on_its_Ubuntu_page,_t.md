### Post by linuxforartists on 2010-12-13
Not sure why Dell did this, but its Ubuntu laptops are no longer on its [Ubuntu page]("http://www.dell.com/content/topics/segtopic.aspx/linux_3x?c=us&cs=19&l=en&s=dhs").

They seem to have moved Ubuntu onto some of its entry-level small business laptops.  These are the two I've been able to find:

[Vostro V130]("http://www.dell.com/us/business/p/vostro-v130/pd?%7Eck=anav") - Ubuntu 10.04 US$429

[Latitude 13]("http://www.dell.com/us/business/p/latitude-13/pd") - Ubuntu 9.04  US$459

Thought this would be helpful in case anyone else was looking for a cheap, Linux-pre-installed laptop.  

If anyone randomly stumbles across Ubuntu on other Dell laptops, I'd like to know. It'd be great to see machines with dedicated graphics cards and other higher specs.

### Post by Jay Car on 2010-12-13
This is interesting.  

Windows® . Life without WallsTM . Dell recommends Windows 7. is plastered along the top of every page.

In the descriptions below each laptop picture, various operating systems are shown. Five of them are Windows. Ubuntu is down at the bottom just above FreeDOSTM. 

Genuine Windows®  7 Home Premium 32-Bit
Genuine Windows®  7 Professional 32-Bit
Genuine Windows®  7 Ultimate 32-Bit
Genuine Windows Vista®  Business 32-Bit
Genuine Windows Vista®  Home Basic 32-Bit
Ubuntu®  Linux® 
FreeDOSTM  operating systems 

It's amusing that all the Windows versions are defined as "Genuine". I know, it's just part of the brand name, but still. Ubuntu isn't exactly un-genuine, is it?

Also, the Windows versions are only the two latest ones, but the only version number of Ubuntu I could find on that page was an old 8.10 version. I have no idea why.

My daughter-in-law needs a new laptop, and I've been keeping my eye out for deals. She's very careless with her laptops so she goes through a lot of them, so it's best not to spend more than necessary on something that will likely just get left on the roof of her car one morning as she heads down the freeway to work. (sigh)

Anyway, the price seemed reasonable on the Dells, so I figured I'd sheck it out.  

The further I went through the process of "customizing" the laptop I chose (Inspiron 14 (1440) Laptop) the less Ubuntu was mentioned and the more "Dell recommends Windows 7" and "Dell recommends Internet Explorer 8" was shown.

I never was able to get to a place where I could actually chose anything but Windows. I considered it to be rather insulting.  

Needless to say, I didn't buy from Dell. 

I went with this little netbook instead, pre-installed with Ubuntu 10.10.: [http://zareason.com/shop/Terra-HD.html](http://zareason.com/shop/Terra-HD.html)
