### Post by TobyReynolds on 2009-03-23
Hi there.

I think you would get 3 hours from the 6-cell battery, especially if you tweak the powersaving features, e.g. [this HOWTO]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=847773"). I don't use all the tweaks in this thread but I still get well over 5 hours with the 9-cell, probably nearer to 6 hours. Assuming the 6-cell gives you 2/3 of this, you should get at least 3 1/3 hours and possibly up to 4, so long as you are not running computer-intensive applications. But obviously this is just speculation; hopefully somebody with a 6-cell battery will give you a more reliable answer...

### Post by mihai.ile on 2009-03-26
Well I also ordered with the 9 cell battery:
nvidia card
led screen
T8300 (2.40 GHz) CPU
500GB hdd

Ok so with this configuration, with wireless ON all time I could say that I get a power consumption of about 13-16Wh.
So my 9cell battery has 85Wh, that would give me almost 6h.
Your 6cell battery has 56Wh and would give you almost 4h.
Even at an average consumption of 18Wh you could still get 3h.

Now to pass the 3h it would only depend if you use the CPU and graphics a lot during this time or not, because the 6cell is well capable of passing the 3h.

### Post by talent03 on 2009-03-29
I have had mine for over a year with the 6 cell. Currently the battery says that it is at 70% capacity. My warning is that it heats up alot if you forget and cover the fan area. I would do it often before and I figured it was no big deal, but if you let it heat up a lot then the battery capacity will drop much faster. Saying that, right now I am using rythmbox and have 15 tabs open on firefox with torrent running. I am using wireless and a bluetooth mouse. So I am definitely working it. It is running at 17.0W with 2.7 hrs left. When I first got the computer, with care I would get almost 4 hours with the 6 cell.
