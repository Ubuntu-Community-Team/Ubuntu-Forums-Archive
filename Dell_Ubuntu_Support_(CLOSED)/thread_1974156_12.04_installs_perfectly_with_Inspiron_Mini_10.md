### Post by gfe on 2012-05-05
I have within the past few days done a fresh install and configured the latest Ubuntu on my Inspiron Mini 10 and am here to report a problem-free installation. Unlike some earlier versions, the current installation includes the driver for the wireless adapter, and I've come across no problems whatsoever in getting the software to handle the hardware.

I will say, though, that the standard Unity interface is too much of a resource hog for this netbook, although the 2D version works OK. I've ended up using the Gnome Fallback desktop -- it looks nice and appears to be easy on the resources. Since I do 95 percent of my work on the netbook using just a browser and text editor, a simple panel with a couple of launch icons makes more sense than the more versatile Unity.

My thanks to all those who made Ubuntu 12.04 a reality. By the time the support period ends, I'll be ready for a new netbook!
