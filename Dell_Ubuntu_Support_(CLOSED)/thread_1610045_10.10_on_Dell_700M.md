### Post by shuttleworthwannabe on 2010-12-25
I have the same machine dell 700m (resurrected from the basement had XP it for the last 6 years), the video is choppy and flash in full screen mode is very jittery. It has to do with the kernel not working well with the Intel 855GM video chipset.

I searched around and probably this will help: [https://bugs.launchpad.net/nubuntu/+source/netbook-launcher/+bug/368394/comments/57](https://bugs.launchpad.net/nubuntu/+source/netbook-launcher/+bug/368394/comments/57)

Update:

That link above did not allow me to get into my desktop--only a silent black screen--only booting in safe mode gets you to the GUI login. sorry for this suggestion--i read elsewhere that adding i915.modeset=1 to kernel parameter reactivates the GUI--but I have no idea how to do this.

I then came across this brilliant wiki post officially from Ubuntu--it explains the problems and offers a solution: read more here: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Bugs/Mavericki8xxStatus](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/X/Bugs/Mavericki8xxStatus)

In short, > To enable the Intel driver you need to create a file called /etc/X11/xorg.conf containing the following:

Section "Device"
        Identifier      "Configured Video Device"
        Driver          "intel"
EndSection

Section "Monitor"
        Identifier      "Configured Monitor"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
        Identifier      "Default Screen"
        Monitor         "Configured Monitor"
        Device          "Configured Video Device"
EndSection

Do not forget to logout and back login--all the 3D and smooth video will work right out of the box now. I am happy now!
According to this wiki, stability may be an issue--so watch out for this. I am sure if you want to get back to the previous settings, just delete the xorg.conf and reboot??? please correct me here I have not tested this.
