### Post by Kixtosh on 2010-03-04
In my opinion:
 
**If you already know Ubuntu well, **can use terminal commands, and don't mind using advanced features to fix any issues (such as sound not working, or video problems): you could certainly use Karmic Koala 9.10. If you intend to switch versions when 10.04 is released, then you should probably keep your files on a separate partition from the O.S. (I think it's the /Home partition, but you'll have to check), so that you can easily upgrade the O.S. from scratch without wiping out your document and media files etc.
 
**If you are relatively new to Ubuntu** (like me), and don't expect to change to a newer version every six months: then I would wait for Lucid Lynx 10.04, since it's a Long Term Support release. It should be more stable, possibly with fewer issues (if any at all), and you won't have to update (unless you decide you want to) for two years.
 
From what I have read, there's no real advantage to using the Dell distro at present, and I don't think there will be any further Dell versions in the near future in any case.
