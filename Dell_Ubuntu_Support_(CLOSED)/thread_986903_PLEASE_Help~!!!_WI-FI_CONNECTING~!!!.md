### Post by ex.pat on 2009-01-24
I am not sure what you are attempying to do.

Do you have a broadband connection and wireless router? If so you shouldn't need a usb card unless your mini 9 was shipped without a WLAN card inside (and I thought all mini 9's had WLAN). If you do have a WLAN card inside the mini 9 you will need to check whether your wireless router is set to hide the SSID and either uncheck the hide part on the router config page or manually set the network up with the mini 9's network manager. If you are still having problems more info might be needed.

If, on the other hand, you are trying to connect with a mobile broadband usb dongle (3G, GSM, HSPDA, what ever acronym you want to call it), it is a little trickier. You generally wont get it to recognise without installing more software on the mini 9. So you will either need a wireless connection to a router somewhere or a LAN connection via an ethernet lead. I have just done mine and I needed to upgrade the network manager from ver .6 to .7. I am running Hardy Heron on mine 8.04 with a Huawei dongle. But not all dongles will be recognised so I would suggest you google search the dongle model with ubuntu and version which will almost certainly give you more than enough answers.

I took a wee while to get used to the operating system but now I am sold with the speed over the steam driven windows. Netbook steams ahead faster than my quad core desktop now.
