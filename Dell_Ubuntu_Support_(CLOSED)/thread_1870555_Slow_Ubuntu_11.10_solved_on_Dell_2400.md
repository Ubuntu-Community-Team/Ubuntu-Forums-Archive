### Post by fritzk on 2011-10-27
I'd been running Ubuntu on my Dell Dimension 2400 since Ubuntu 9.x and was very satisfied with it. When I installed Ubuntu 11.10, performance slowed to a crawl.  System monitor showed CPU utilization averaged more than 75 percent and frequently went to 100 percent for minutes at a time. It might take a minute or more to bring up an application after I clicked on it. ](*,)

Searching other comments on Ubuntu forums indicated the CPU was bogged down with updating graphics.  I put the installation CD into a Dell Studio XPS 8100 with an Intel i5 CPU and saw CPU utilization averaged less than 5 percent on any core.

So, I purchased an EVGA brand GeForce 6200 PCI VGA card and installed it in my Dell 2400 with the recommended Nvidia drivers. I'm pleased to say that CPU utilization now averages around 25 percent. :D It doesn't respond as quickly to starting applications as fast as Ubuntu 11.04, but at least now it's usable. I may drop Unity and return to Gnome 2 to increase the speed.

Even though my Dell 2400 is old, there's still enough life left in it to experiment with various Linux tweaks that I'd prefer not doing on my newer computer. Note: If you try my solution by adding a new video card, don't buy a PCI-E (Enhanced) card, :redface: because the Dell 2400 slots only support PCI cards.
