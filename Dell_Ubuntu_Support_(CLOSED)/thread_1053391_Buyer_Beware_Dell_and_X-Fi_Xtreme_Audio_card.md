### Post by Xica_da_Silva on 2009-01-28
I don't think Dell intentionally did this, but I do feel compelled to warn other people...

  If you're like me, and you bought a pc pre-loaded Studio with Ubuntu like I did, and used the 'customization' options for the pc like I did, then you need to be careful with the sound upgrade suggestion that Dell gives for this pc. The suggested upgrade is 'ExpressCard Sound Blaster X-Fi Xtreme Audio Sound Card(Notebook)'. But as it turns out, although there are indeed several soundcards made by the same mfr(Creative Labs)for which you can download drivers to make them compatible with Linux, this particular one isn't one of them. There may be some work-around for the pros here but if you're a beginner like me I'm trying to save you a little trouble. Fortunately, Dell was more than happy to refund my money. I suggested that they fix this problem on the website but just in case it takes a while for them to follow up on this...
