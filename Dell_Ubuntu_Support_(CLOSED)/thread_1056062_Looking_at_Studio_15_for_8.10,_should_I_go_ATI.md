### Post by MatthewMetzger on 2009-01-31
I've heard and experienced that Ubuntu has great support for Intel graphics, but also that the ATI drivers for linux are very good.

I have had a few problems with NVIDIA cards. When the 3D driver is enabled, weird screen effects occur, like the menus not showing up in OpenOffice.

### Post by theApokalypsis on 2009-01-31
I have found Intel graphics to have great out of box support. I have a Asipre One, Intel and composite effects that always worked fine and dandy.

ATI's drivers are getting better everyday as well. Not to mention the open source drivers are on the rise.

if you want minimal eyecandy though either would work. Just the more eyecandy you use or want to use with integrated graphics (intel in this case i believe) is going to use your RAM as opposed to the GPU having its own memory.
