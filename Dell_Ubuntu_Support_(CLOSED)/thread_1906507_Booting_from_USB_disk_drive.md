### Post by Scott Baker on 2012-01-12
*QUOTE "Yes the computer had a virus ridden windows xp installed originally & USB is how I managed to get Ubuntu installed"*

Your previous post states that you have Ubuntu *installed*. Did you have Ubuntu up and running from a "live distro", or is it already on your hard drive? Also, if it is on your hard drive, how did you configure it? Was it a complete replacement for Windows, or set up to dual boot (Windows and Ubuntu) to both OSs.

Next, I believe that you state that you are trying to boot from a USB DVD drive. I don't know about others, but I have personally found that some of these drives can be hit or miss, with regards to being a bootable drive. If you need to reload your OS, go with a flash/thumbdrive. Besides, I've found these drives much faster when it comes to booting to Ubuntu.

Lastly, take a moment to check the cable on your hard drive. It's not real difficult. Unplug your machine. Open the case per your user manual. Once inside, you should find a gray, flat cable That's the cable for your hard drive and your DVD drive. *CAREFULLY* push the cable back onto the drives, and if you can reach it, the system board (if you can't reach it, don't worry about it). Close the case back up, plug it back in, and let us know your results.  8-[
