### Post by jbelmonte on 2008-10-17
This may be a noob questions, but...

I just received my Ubuntu pre-loaded Mini-9 (1GB RAM, 16 GB SSD, bluetooth, 1.3 MP webcam).

I tried to use the card reader without success. I inserted a Transcend 16 GB SDHC Class 6 card in the reader (yes, I put it in fully and correctly), but nothing happened at all.

The SDHC card came with a USB reader, so I tried to access the SDHC card that way. As soon as I inserted it into the USB port, the system recognized it, mounted it, and began browsing it. 

Is there something I need to do to activate the card reader? My removable storage preferences are set to mount the drives when hot plugged, so it should mount if it is seen and recognized, just like what happened when I used the USB reader. I thought that the media card reader was capable of reading SDHC cards. Any help would be greatly appreciated.

-----------------
edit: Changing to the standard Gnome interface and then rebooting seemed to solved the problem. The card reader just began recognizing the SDHC card without my doing anything else.
