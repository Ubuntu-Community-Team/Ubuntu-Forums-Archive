### Post by MrDelish on 2009-02-27
Well, got it to work somehow. I had tried reinstalling Intrepid the first time around, but still had the same problem. This time around I wiped the drive, put back in the original 2 GB of RAM, installed Intrepid and selected the log in automatically option when installing. When it was done, keyboard and mouse worked fine. Not sure what fixed it exactly though.

Also on a fantastic note, the wireless connection worked right off the bat... When I installed Hardy on my two laptops, I had to tweak it to get the wireless to work on both. It's nice to have it work out of the box. :)
