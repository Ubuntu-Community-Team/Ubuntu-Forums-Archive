### Post by Mark_in_Hollywood on 2008-10-17
You should be able to "have your cake and eat it too". Ubuntu doesn't run slow itself. Nor are applications running slowly, either. Having said that, how much RAM do you have? Are you loading all the applications onto the ribbon bars? What is your CPU speed? 

I hate to answer a question with a question, but the problem of slowness isn't likely to be the software itself.

### Post by snowpine on 2008-10-17
Applications do not slow your computer down unless you use them. Uninstalling applications will free up some space on  your hard drive, but won't make your computer run faster.

I'd recommend starting by going to System->Preferences->Sessions and unchecking any services you don't think you need. I find that Tracker in particular kind of slows things down.

Good luck!
