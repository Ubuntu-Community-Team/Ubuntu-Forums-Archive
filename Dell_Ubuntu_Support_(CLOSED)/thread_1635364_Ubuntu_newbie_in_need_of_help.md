### Post by mciverza on 2010-12-03
Hi   We'd need to know the hardware specs (I'll get to that in a mo) if the wireless isn't being picked up.  By default the network tool is at the top right of your screen, near the clock and part of the notificatinon area. 

Left-clicking on that will display all the wireless networks your computer has detected.  In my image attached, I have taken a screenshot of the network applet after it has been "clicked".

If your system is not detecting any wireless links, then please would you check whether additional drivers are required by going via your menu to: System --> Administration -- Additional Drivers.

Some wireless chipsets are not supported out of the box, but most of them can be gotten working with very little effort.
