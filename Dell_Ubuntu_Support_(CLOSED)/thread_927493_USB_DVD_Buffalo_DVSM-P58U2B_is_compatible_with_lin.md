### Post by gregconquest on 2008-09-23
UPDATE: It works fine. The very first boot it missed and went to the hard drive. Thereafter, hit "0" during boot and select CD/DVD/CD-RW (even though it is on USB), and it will boot from the optical drive. The Buffalo drive is working even without using the power cable.

Note: I did have to use the most recent version of CloneZilla, v1.2.1-17 (2008/11/13). The prior version, v.1.2.0-25, gave me an error during boot.

---

I wanted an external USB CD/DVD for my mini 9, and the one offered by DELL Japan said it was compatible only with Windows. So, I got the Buffalo DVSM-P58U2/B here in Japan on 2008/09/21. It cost me 9,000 yen (about $90 US). I attached it to my DIY Linux desktop (running ubuntu). I could read CD's and data DVD's with no problem. I even burnt 4GB of videos onto a DVDR. There were no problems. I did not have to manually install any drivers.

I'm suspicious the DVD-RAM won't work without drivers, but I don't know. I don't need that functionality.

I haven't received my mini 9 yet, but I'm reasonably certain that I'll be able to boot from the Buffalo DVSM-P58U2/B drive and to read and write CDR's and DVDR's.
