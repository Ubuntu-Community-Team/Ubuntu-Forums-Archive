### Post by Brandon Williams on 2009-10-15
I've heard mixed impressions from people running i386 Ubuntu on the Dell mini. Some say flash video is perfectly watchable and some say that it's a major problem. My experience with i386 Ubuntu on the mini (both the 9n and the 10v) is that the graphics do not perform well in general, and that flash video is unwatchable.

On the other hand, flash video has been perfectly fine for me on Ubuntu for lpia (both Dell's custom Hardy and lpia Jaunty). Many people have suggested that there is no benefit to running lpia Jaunty on the mini instead of i386 Jaunty, but my experience suggests that, if you are going to install Jaunty, it's well worth the trouble of installing the lpia version. 

FWIW, I heard from at least one mini user who said that he got better performance with an install off of the UNR image than he did with the i386 image, even though the UNR image is essentially a customized i386 image (not lpia).

### Post by ahood on 2009-10-17
I have a Dell Mini 9 with the default lpia Ubuntu OS installed.

I find Hulu video (via firefox browser) in full screen on this Dell Mini 9 to be watchable. There is an occasional flicker but it isn't distracting at all and audio is in sync. This is on a wireless connection (802.11b/g) with 4 bars (54 mb/s). 

For the modest power in this machine, it is quite remarkable.

Hope this helps.
