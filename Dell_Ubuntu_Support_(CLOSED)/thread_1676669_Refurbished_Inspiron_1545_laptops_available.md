### Post by themrfreeze on 2011-01-27
Hey all.

I didn't see any mention of this recently, but NewEgg is selling factory refurbished Inspiron 1545 (ie: 15N) laptops starting at $369.  These laptops are certified by Canonical to run Ubuntu.

I bought a new 1545/15N about 18 months ago, and it's been a good laptop for my wife, so I went ahead and bought one of these refurbs for myself.  The thing looks like it's absolutely brand spanking new, and has been working great so far.  Only thing that doesn't work after installing Ubuntu is wifi, and all you have to do is enable the proprietary Broadcom driver for it to work.  Easy peasy.

It's a good basic laptop, and at this price, it's pretty hard to beat.

### Post by bobcollard on 2011-02-03
When (not if) your wifi goes dead buy a USB dongle and pull the built in card.  Mine did not even last a year and others have had the same problem with Broadcom 4312 cards.  I can recommend one, Netgear WG111v3 is about $30.00 US, Much cheaper than a Broadcom replacement and faster.
