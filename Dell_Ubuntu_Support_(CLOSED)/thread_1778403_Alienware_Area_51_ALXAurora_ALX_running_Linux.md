### Post by GWBouge on 2011-06-09
A friend of mine has an Alienware Aurora (one of [these]("http://www.dell.com/us/p/alienware-aurora-alx/pd?cs=19") I think), although it's all liquid cooled, and I didn't notice the lights changing colors, but that doesn't mean it didn't happen, lol.

I threw 11.04 on there just to test it out, and everything seemed to work fine, except for it came with dual ATI video cards (either 6750 or 6770) which didn't work very well with the drivers from 'Additional Drivers' and I didn't feel like messing with that much to see if I could get better performance out of it.

As for the machine itself, I was actually somewhat let down by it.  It took a while to boot up, and didn't perform nearly as well as I expected for common tasks (web browsing, e-mail, etc etc).  Now, I loaded Blender on it, and the render times were great, but as far as normal operation my old XPS420 feels much snappier.  It could be the difference between the dual ATI's and my single GTX460, but it felt sluggish both in Ubuntu and Windows.

Oh, and the water pump on the cooling system ... is LOUD.
