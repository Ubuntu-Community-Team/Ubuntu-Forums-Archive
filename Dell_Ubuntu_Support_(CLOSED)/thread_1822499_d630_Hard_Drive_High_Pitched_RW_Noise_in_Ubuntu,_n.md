### Post by Guin on 2011-08-10
Hi, I recently installed Ubuntu 11.04 dualbooting with Windows XP on my Dell d630 laptop.

Both OSes are on the same physical drive, the laptop's internal drive.

After installing ubuntu, I noticed that (what I am assuming is) the read/write noise for the hard drive has become very high pitched and not good sounding. It sounds perfectly normal in xp, and also when I boot ubuntu from a live USB. I did a bit of searching in the forums, and it was suggested that when such noises occur, the drive is getting ready to die. There was also some talk about setting power management in the bios helping reduce drive noise.

I set the drive performance settings to economical in the bios, and the noise has become less frequent, but is still present. I'm hoping the drive isn't getting ready to die, but if it is, at least I have advance warning.
(I ran ubuntu's disk check utility, and it claims the drive is healthy.)

I am curious as to why Ubuntu would cause this noise, and not windows. Is it something to do with how Ubuntu handles reading/writing to hard drives? If so, is there something I can do to fix it? It's not something I'm really prepared to put up with.
