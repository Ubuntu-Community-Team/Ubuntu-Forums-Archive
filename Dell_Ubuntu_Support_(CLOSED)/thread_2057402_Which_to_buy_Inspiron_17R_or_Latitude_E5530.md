### Post by resander on 2012-09-23
I agree with Gordonintoronto. Don't buy a laptop for program development. A desktop with separate monitor and keyboard is much better because you can:

- choose exactly the monitor you want, upgrade to bigger screen etc
- add memory, add/change graphics card, add ssd etc
- get a better system for less money, for example a Vostro 470 with an i7 processor, 8gb ram, 1TB hard disk
- position screen and keyboard (ideally on a computer table) for comfort
I once had to use a laptop for programming work 8 hours a day for three weeks and ended up with red eyes, stiff neck and severe backache.

The Inspiron 17r received some bad customer reviews (3 out 5 stars) and the answers to the questions section also contains useful information. Could not find any review or answer section about the Latitude.

I am thinking about buying a Dell Vostro 470 with an i7 processor. I need Ubuntu for my work, so I have been asking if i-series processors will work with Ubuntu in the Beginners forum [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2058207](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2058207) and the answers say they will.

However, JKyleOKC in this forum thread [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2061096](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2061096) is flagging up an annoying problem when dual booting. The preinstalled parts of Windows 7 cannot be moved freely to make space for Ubuntu resulting in 200GB of a 500GB disk being wasted for Windows, even when not needed. I think there must be a way around this possibly by explicitly defining the partitions and not rely on Ubuntu to do install by 'install alongside existing operating system', but I am really guessing don't know yet...

### Post by masgeeks on 2012-10-07
Hi just some thoughts here about the e5530 since I just bought one.  Put precise 64 bit on it, usually have good luck with dell stuff and ubuntu, but this laptop is too new, I guess - pretty funky stuff that I have had to chase around to fix.  standard wireless card is bcm4313 and is slow as molasses with n and wpa2 personal (doesn't bother me, I am using ethernet anyway, but still) and with sta driver not much better speed, still tops at 65 mbps.  bluetooth has been very fidgety with microsoft mobile keyboard 6000 (yeah, well - i know, m$ keyboard, but its a nice little keyboard) and the dell bluetooth module.  half the function keys don't work, most annoying for that is the brightness fn keys, found a grub fix for that, but caused other transient hardware weirdness so had to cease and desist with that fix.  it has the sandy bridge mobile graphics.  I got so tired trying to make everything play nice that I blew it out and reloaded precise and just did bare minimum everything.  I use it for writing books sitting on the couch, it sits on a coffee table and I use a wireless keyboard... have ethernet running over there so like I said don't care that the wireless is slow, i turned off wireless, and bluetooth as well since the keyboard kept disconnecting (i am now using wireless keyboard with usb receiver).  was kind of bummed, since i have always had very good luck with dell stuff, but gonna get a lemon every once in a while, lol.  I have a nice DELL Latitude L13 laptop I bought a few years ago that just rocks - I love it to death its awesome...!!!  such a work horse, but its a little thing, small screen,and its not really good for writing books on it.  sorry so long, just wanted to give my experience with the e5530.  BTW - it is THE ugliest laptop I've EVER seen, but - got it cheap so didn't care, dell had a blowout sale and so... lol...
