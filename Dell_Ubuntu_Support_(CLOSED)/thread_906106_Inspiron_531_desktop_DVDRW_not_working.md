### Post by gbrainin on 2008-08-31
First, did you buy your computer from Dell at a time that they included DVD playback?  For legal reasons, you need to pay a license fee to (legally) play DVDs.  The more recent Dell Ubuntu computers include software which has a license (I think it's called Lindvd), but if you didn't buy it, then you cannot play DVDs out-of-the-box.  It's not something broken with your computer.

Assuming you have the proper software, can you read other types of discs in the drive?  It's certainly possible you have bad hardware; if you do, I strongly suggest that you call Dell's LINUX support line, not the general one, to diagnose the problem.  The number is 1-866-622-1947.  If it turns out to be bad hardware, they should swap it out no problem, once they determine that's the problem.
