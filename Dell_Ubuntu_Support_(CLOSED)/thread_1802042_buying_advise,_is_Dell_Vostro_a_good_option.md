### Post by November Snow on 2011-07-13
Hi!
I'm more into 13" laptops, but allow me nonetheless to give you my impressions on the Vostro V130 I bought last winter.
I agree with you: pricewise, there's nothing to complain about. I got it for $400. CAN!
It  is sleek, very light (3.5 pounds). It has an antiglare screen. And it  delivers the goods, at least regarding my needs (internet, music, word  processing...).
There's one snag, though... the battery. If you're  lucky you'll squeeze 2:45hr out of it, 3 maybe, with a bit of luck... As  I understand it (I also have a 15" Inspiron laptop), poor battery life  is common at Dell's... That's probably why they can roll back their  prices...
In comparison, the 13" Acer Timeline I got a couple of years ago yields a good 6 hours.
So if battery life is important for you, you might want to think twice before opting for a Vostro.
Best regards!

Or get a stronger battery. Doesn't dell let you pick what you want? Or is that only in the US? Either way, I don't know that model off the top of my head, but there is high performance/low battery laptops, and there's low performance/high battery laptops, and there's mid performance/mid battery.. though the last two kind of merge. Get the model that's right for you. Often the thin, small, portable laptops are designed for use on the go more often. While larger, big screen, heavier models are designed to not be moved a lot, and hence not the best battery life. Though, times are changing and battery life is getting exceedingly better. 

Just a note. 2.5-3h battery life is pretty standard. Before the recent couple years you couldn't really find better than that.

### Post by aeronutt on 2011-07-14
I have a Vostro 3550. Battery life (with discrete graphics card disabled) is not an issue for me, ~4 hours is pretty common doing normal stuff.

There are a lot of other issues with Ubuntu 11.04 though. I've been keeping track of them, here's a summary. You can decide if these are an issue for you or if you'd be better off with another laptop. I have Intel i3 sandy bridge processor, Radeon discrete graphics, 3GB RAM, 250GB HD, .... etc.


- Trackpad doesn't show up in GUI; unable to adjust and change settings, eg ignore while typing is not enabled.
- Trackpad scroll area is offset from edge.
- Sometimes trackpad goes into a mode where it is extremely sensitive, not requiring actual touch to move the mouse. Must reboot.
- Unable to use Radeon graphics chip.
- Sometimes screen dims for no apparent reason.
- Boot hangs at login screen randomly (infrequent).
- 'problem occured' screen on login, have to relogin at gdm
- Unity slow in search (0.5-1 second between key strokes)
- Gnome3 logout pulldown slow (delaying ~2sec sometimes)
- Gnome3 sometimes slow/laggy
- Unable to stream music to xbox (randomly stops playing)
- Chrome and chromium somehow cause web sites to become unavailable.
- unable to use integrated cam with chat
- cam frame rate much lower than with windows (using cheese)
- fingerprint scanner unoperable (no driver available, no dev occuring.)
- When using USB cable with camera, copying multiple pictures locks up.
