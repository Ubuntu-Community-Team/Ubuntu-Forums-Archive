I think that other systems may say Intel X3100, but not necessarily have the same chipset.

This same person went on to say(referring to the Studio Desktop) "The system's Intel graphics chipset[ed: Eagle Lake] isn't supported in 8.04, but I would expect 8.10 to work very well."

### Post by onpaws on 2009-01-28
**My personal experience in installing Ubuntu 8.10 fresh onto a Dell Studio Hybrid **

Studio hybrid here. the one with wireless N, from Best Buy. $399 etc etc


Here's the status of my config:
Wireless? Check. Out of the box, the wl driver was used. WPA2 worked ok.
X3100 graphics acceleration, non-vesa? Check.
smooth acceleration with compiz, awn, the cart game? Check
1680x1050 resolution available on 20" monitor? Check

Today I spent most of my day pulling hair out of my head for two of the following reasons:

**_VIDEO_**
On bootup, (after dark ubuntu loading screen, and before x display), the screen would go blank. I suspected that the video was getting redirected to VGA (or rather the analog-portion of the DVI-I connector). This was actually the case. I did some hacking of xorg.conf such that both VGA and TV outputs are now set to "Ignore" "True". 

This fix helped alot. Otherwise I would have had to cycle through ctrl+alt+f1 (to display some text), then ctrl+alt+f7 to get back to "normal" state.

**_AUDIO_**
