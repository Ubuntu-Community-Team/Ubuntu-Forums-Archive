### Post by 1clue on 2011-01-18
Welcome!

File hierarchy standard is here:
[http://www.pathname.com/fhs/](http://www.pathname.com/fhs/)

Windows NT and on are mostly POSIX compliant.  There are a lot of shell commands that are the same as you would get in Linux, probably with different options.  Things like "exit" are pretty universal.  Netstat exists on both, although most of the options are different.  netstat -an behaves the same way on both Windows and Linux.

Windows doesn't offer you nearly as much of a choice as Linux, which is both a blessing and a curse from either perspective depending on how you look at it.

I think the normal Ubuntu crowd is probably a different breed of cat than you are.  I'm 45, and I'm just saying that you strike me as more of an old-school hands-on guy.  I think that once you get your training wheels off and get the hang of things, you might want to take a look at Gentoo.  It's as nuts and bolts as it can possibly get.  Takes a lot of knowledge and time but teaches you an amazing amount about operating systems and applications in general.

You can learn "what" easily enough on Ubuntu, but the Gentoo forums can teach you "why."

I know a lot of guys who use both Gentoo and Ubuntu, including me.  Ubuntu tends to be about as easy as it gets to set up and keep running, and the community is more user-oriented and friendly.  Gentoo is the opposite end of the scale, except that it's still a very friendly community.  It is all about maximum choice on the part of the system administrator.
