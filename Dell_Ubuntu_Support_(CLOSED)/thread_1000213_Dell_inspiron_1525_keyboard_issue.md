### Post by BunnyGirlDoom on 2008-12-03
My Dell Inspiron has always done this. Whether in Ubuntu or WinXP. I used to think maybe it was the keyboard but I replaced that and still the same problem.

After seraching for info, there may be a few causes. Some have said driver issues, but since it tends to happen in both OS's (at least for me) I am hesitant to assume it's that. The most common suggestion was that laziness has caused us to inadvertently hit our palms on the touch pad when typing. Another option based on this idea is to disable the touchpad and use a USB mouse. I already use a USB mouse on occasion so this idea, if it works, wouldn't be a big issue, though impractical for those who don't want the extra peripherals attached. 

Yet another suggestion was to disable the cursor when typing in the mouse configuration.

Hope this info helps.
