### Post by alejaaandro on 2009-03-09
i got my mini a couple of days ago and i still haven't decided what to install on it.. i ve tried
-ubuntu 8.10 with which i am familiar because that's what i have on my desktop
-linuxmint which i kinda liked (nice looking, familiar because it's ubuntu based, nice custom apps like the start menu)
-elive which was nice too but since i got the version that's under development it wasn't very stable
-opengeu which is a good candidate cause i realy liked e17
-dells' ubuntu 8.04.1 which is great having an os configures exactly for your hardware..


i m really between the last two..
>>dell's customized ubuntu -pros:REALLY FAST (bootchart gives 12 sec, stopwatch timed to desktop about 30 sec.. i think this is not because of the lpia architecture but because the kernel was striped down), everything obviously just works out of the box (including all fn buttons)
  -cons: you're stuck with that since lpia architecture makes it almost impossible to install new stuff (i found a script to convert i386 deb files to lpia and show installed packages in synaptic, but it doesn't handle dependencies) and you can't upgrade to 8.10 ( i don't know if later dell will release an upgrade)

>>open geu -pros: better looking, e17 is faster than gnome, e17 is already installed and configured with a bunch of extra modules ( i m not a noob, neither an expert, and enlightenment isnt the easiest thing to get going- i installed e17 on dells' ubuntu and a lot of stuff wouldn't work and i m not interested in spending a lot of time configuring a system i ll use now and then)
   -cons: dells' ubuntu boots REALLY FAST... i was amazed.. and, isn't that one of the clue points for buying a netbook?

i m sure your replies (if any) will be that it depends on my needs etc, but i just wanted to share my impressions so far, and see what you guys have installed and how it's working...

### Post by alejaaandro on 2009-04-21
hmmm, after trying a bunch of distros i think i 've found a really neat one for "instant-on" computing..

check out xpud.. it's less than 50 MB and has a really nice layout.. it's still under heavy  development.. there is an extra package with a variety of modules to fit your hardware (though you have to load them manualy.. but there is a tool to build your customized image with all necessary modules loaded)..wifi for mini 9 is not supported yet but i saw somebody uploaded the module to google codes, i ll try it out later...

i really like it cause, (even though not fully operational yet) it provides a handful of applications for day2day work and boots up in less than 10 sec..pretty cool, ain't it?
