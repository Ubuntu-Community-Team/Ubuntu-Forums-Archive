### Post by bobcollard on 2009-11-21
The Dell repositories only offer Ubuntu in i386, I wanted to get a 64 bit Distro.  The main problem was what everyone else has lately and that is the lack of drivers for wifi.  Not that they cannot be found, just that this system does not come with it.  It took awhile reading all kinds of forums and using other distros until the real solution came up.  "sudo apt-get install b43-fwcutter"  Dell has Broadcom cards in many of their laptops.  Anyway, I added Kubuntu-Desktop and upgraded to KDE 4.3.3  Why didn't I just go with Kubuntu?  KDE sometimes has problems after an upgrade, then I would have Gnome in the same partition to fall back on to fix it.  As for backup and restore, I use sbackup and a USB external HD.  Backup daily, it is not a question of "If your HD will fail"  It is "When will it fail?"  They all fail, usually when you need them the most, or you just did a restart or an upgrade.  Then you have to wonder; is it the software or the hardware?  Have a nice day!
