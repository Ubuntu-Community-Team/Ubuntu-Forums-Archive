I have a Dell GX270, the model just before yours, and I also tried out Ubuntu 10.04 LTS (Lucid, for short, k?). I did my tryout from a USB drive though 'cause my machine didn't come w/ a burner. Anyway, it does take FOREVER to load the trial but it DOES load. I just start it and either watch t.v. or go make myself a snack.

My specs were/are:
Proc: Pent 4, 2.4Ghz
Drive: Was: 40G, 7200 RPM; Now: 80G, ditto
RAM: 2G (self-installed)
OS: Was: Win XP Pro; Now: Lucid on entire 80G hd

At first I installed it side-by-side w/ my Win OS but they weren't playing well together due to lack of space (not surprising w/ a 40G HD, right?). However, the short time I was able to use Lucid made me a believer. I already had an ASUS PCEee 901 w/ 2 ssd's w/ a total of 20G between the 2 and 2G of RAM, preloaded, on which I'd had Karmic but had probs w/ a USB Modem, so I updated to Lucid and problem solved. Which is more or less why I was even considering using Lucid on this desktop.

I'm SO happy I did the conversion. I did, however, installed the 80G hd after compressing and storing all my data files from my Win hd on USB memory sticks and which I'm planning to restore to this hd.

My only beef is that although the trial w/ Lucid made me believe I'd get my sound back (it had disappeared from my Win OS after a bunch of driver updates done some months back), no joy: no sound. I did do some manual updating and installation of some files but they were tweaks (for Firefox and some Facebook games that needed Flash player files installed).

I think your only problem will be two-fold: sizing your partitions (I **[COLOR=Purple]STRONGLY[/COLOR]** suggest you read the tutorial for installing Ubuntu 10.04, even if you don't understand some of the terms.), and figuring out if your BIOS supports booting from a USB stick. Mine didn't until I visited the Dell support site for my model and found out I hadn't noticed an OLD BIOS update I'd missed. THEN it worked.

I don't know if it makes a difference but try an installation w/ a USB stick. It might go faster ...

For your convenience: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStickQuick](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStickQuick)

I hope this helps. And, btw, I only began educating myself on Ubuntu about 2 mos. ago but I believe in reading, even if it's AFTER I've gotten into trouble (which is why I give the above suggestion so strongly).

I hope this helps. :)
