### Post by Xavier_To on 2009-07-16
The vostros 1520 is not a bad computer as long as you get a decent graphic card such as the option geforce that you can get with it, unless you enjoy lower end resolution (1280x800). A monitor can be used too, but as I found out this morning, it doesnt play well with the default graphic card that comes with the vostro 1520 (intel integrated crap). The wifi works fine too. I'm just dissapointed of the low quality of the intel card....

### Post by Xavier_To on 2009-07-16
Disregard the last post, i found a solution to the bad resolution : The xorg.conf generated at the installation was not properly generated. I loaded a livecd and used the xorg.conf from there by transfering it to a usb stick. The other issue i've found was the sound. I have yet to figure out why the headphones do not mute the speakers when i plug them in... but that's a minor annoyance
