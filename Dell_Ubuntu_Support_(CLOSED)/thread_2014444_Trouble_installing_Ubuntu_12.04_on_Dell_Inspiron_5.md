### Post by weirdAbacist on 2012-07-01
After several years of unreliable internet connections, my parents finally purchased a new router, and while my father and I are getting good use out of it my mother cannot access the internet at all. My mother's computer is a Dell Inspiron 5150, and the problem seems to be that Windows XP only has WEP encryption and our new router uses WPA. Since I blame the OS and not the hardware itself, and I've been happily running Ubuntu for just over a year now, I thought I would try installing Ubuntu on her laptop.

So far I've followed the same process I used when installing Ubuntu on my computers, as outlined on [this webpage]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/FromUSBStickQuick/"). To be more explicit, I downloaded Ubuntu Desktop 12.04 LTS (32 bit), which was an ISO, and then installed a Universal USB Installer, which made the ISO bootable from USB. I then proceed to install Ubuntu on the Inspiron from bootup.

Everything seems to be going fine except that at intermittent points during the installation process the computer suddenly shuts down. This seems to occur right after the internal laptop fan revs up, so I believe the shutdown is related to overheating. I tried using an external fan to aid the cooling, but to no avail. After 4-5 attempts I decided to give up for the night.

Is there a way I can reduce the heating to prevent this shutdown? And if not, should I look into using an earlier version of Ubuntu instead?

---

### Post by mikewhatever on 2012-07-02
Overheating is usually caused by dust-clogged heat sinks or by broken cooling fans. If the fan only spins up after you begin installing, that's definitely wrong.
