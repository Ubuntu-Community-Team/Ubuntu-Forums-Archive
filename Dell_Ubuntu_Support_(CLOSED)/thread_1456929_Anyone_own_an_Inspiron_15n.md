I use one for school. If you have one with a dedicated graphics card, you'll appreciate this, though the Linux support for the intel gma graphic card plays videos better than Linux support for my ATI Radeon card on my dekstop. So, I'm not complaining about the lack of the dedicated...

### Post by irv on 2010-05-30
I bought a Dell Inspiron 1521 almost three years ago. Never had one thing go wrong with it. I paid over $800 but saw them on sale at Walmart for under $500 about 6 months ago. I added 2 gig of memory last year so now I have 4 gig total. I saw a speed increase after doing this.
I am running Win7 and Ubuntu 10.04 dual boot. Both Win7 and Ubuntu boots in about 20 seconds. I use Ubuntu for my everyday work and play, and use Win7 for Netflix, Quicken, and one puzzle game I like. 
My wireless card needs to use a third party driver, but This was simple, with the laptop plugged into the internet via the wire:

Go to "SYSTEM" select "ADMINISTRATION" then "HARDWARE DRIVERS" 
I use the Broadcom STA driver.
At first I did like the laptop because older versions of Ubuntu was harder to setup the wireless card, but now it's a snap.
