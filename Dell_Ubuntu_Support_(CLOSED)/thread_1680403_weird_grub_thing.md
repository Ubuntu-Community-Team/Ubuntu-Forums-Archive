### Post by sikander3786 on 2011-02-02
One option is for the latest kernel and one for its recovery mode.

One option is for the older kernel and one for its recovery mode.

It is recommende that you keep at least one older kernel so if the new one breaks or causes problems, you can boot the older one and diagnose/solve the problem.

For removing older kernels, see here.

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1587462](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1587462)
