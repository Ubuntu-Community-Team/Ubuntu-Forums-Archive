### Post by Cappy on 2007-09-06
They are about the same, 8600 is just a little better than the 7900GS. I know because I bought the 7900GS just a week before the 8600 came out. I thought about returning it but the speed difference wasn't enough to justify the cost of returning it.

8600GS definitely. The only bad thing is you have to run the Envy program (which is a piece of cake) instead of using the built-in ubuntu video drivers (nvidia-glx/nvidia-glx-new). The only actual downside is that you have to rerun envy whenever you upgrade your kernel.

8600 supports DX10 and that will effect what kind of effects can occur in Linux too.

I'm kind of hoping the next generation of Nvidia cards will come out sometime before the end of the year. I'm sure not going to plop down $500 on a year old video card 8800GTX.
