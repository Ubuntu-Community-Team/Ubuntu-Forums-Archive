### Post by a-guitarist on 2008-06-10
Free to play, free to download, depending on what client you use to connect.  [Forsaken Lands]("http://www.forsakenlands.org").  Yes, it is text based, but a little imagination never hurt anyone. ;)  Based off of the Rom2.4 code base, I believe, but it has been massively rewritten over the last 8 or so years that it is now it's own type of code base ([FLCodeBase 3.1]("http://www.flcodebase.com").

Great game, very balanced.  PK, RP, Quests, Clans, Cabals, Quest Races, Quest Classes, Newbie Class (safe from pk, can't hold rare equipment or go past the 30th level.  Has many skills/spells to help a new comer learn the game), Newbie Friendly.

If you do head there, check the forums out, specifically the essays section.  They have a lot of great information, including a first character guide.  I'd also suggest reading "help tour" (when in game), some of the information is a little dated, but it is still a good source.

And I'm all for the open source/free software/free service ideas.  But at the same time, the costs for running a game online can be beastly.  A MUD?  They're cheap compared to a graphical MMO.  Sure, WoW might make 15 dollars for each player online every month, plus software sales, but at the same time... how much does it cost to buy servers, keep them running (mostly) 24/7, and have the bandwidth to take all those connections from around the globe?  That can't be cheap... so I when I did play I had absolutely no qualms with paying a monthly fee.  I liked to play there, I wanted to continue to play there, so paying to play in order to keep the server up was question action for me.

a-g
