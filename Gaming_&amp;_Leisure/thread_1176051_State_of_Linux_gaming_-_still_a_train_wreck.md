### Post by efikkan on 2009-06-04
I don't agree that Linux gaming is a train wreck.

First of all, the experience of running Windows games in GNU/Linux is depending on several factors; hardware, drivers, Wine/Crossover/Cedega and more. Even with the same configurations this can vary from one computer to another. (this is also a problem in Windows)

Customer complaints are ignored especially if you're trying to run an older title.  The attitude is: "give up and buy out new game!".

A good example of such behaviour is SEGA.  Medieval 2 Total War was a buggy mess on release and fans had to petition and lobby the forums in order to get the game patched.  In the end it was patched no less than three times - each patch weighing in at around 700MB!  And still many of the issues were not fixed.  Next they released an expansion pack call "Kingdoms".  Kingdoms was still full of the same bugs that plagued the main game.  The CA announced that there would be no more patches.  The result was an uproar with widespread condemnation and in the end another patch was released.  Still the game is not bug free.

The latest release from this company "Empire" is also an unfinished product.  This time around they have distributed it using Steam and patches seem more regular - yet still the problems that are reported are not being fixed.

The graphics hardware manufacturers then update their drivers and break compatibility with older games.  They ignore complaints and instead blame the game developer who in turn blames the graphics hardware developer, etc.

The problem is this culture and the dominance of MS and it's DirectX API.  But it was the PC gaming industry that allowed all of this to come about in it's bottomless greed - and nothing has changed.

The bottom line is: Don't blame GNU/Linux, blame games developers/publishers.
