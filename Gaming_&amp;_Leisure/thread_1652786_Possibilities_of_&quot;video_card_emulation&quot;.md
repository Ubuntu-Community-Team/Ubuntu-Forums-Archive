### Post by mips on 2010-12-27
> **thenumbereight said:**
> I have a 5-year-old Dell Latitude laptop that only has the standard, integrated graphics hardware.  I'm inquiring because I'm trying to find out if there's any way to emulate the n64, ps1, and Dreamcast consoles on my machine without having to update any hardware...

NO.

When you try and emulate something your current hardware must be more powerful than what you are trying to emulate.

Have a look at the hardware requirements for the relevant emulators as some of them really don't need that much, Project64 etc for example will work well with a old TNT card. The later integrated cards are not that weak you know.

Your best bet is to simply try it and see what works and what doesn't. n64 & PS1 emulation might work while DC will not. You on't know until you try.

You have no upgrade path for the majority of laptop GPUs. There are very few laptops where you can upgrade the GPU and those that can already use nvidia or ati. Your only option is to buy a new laptop or PC with a nVidia card if you are using Linux.
