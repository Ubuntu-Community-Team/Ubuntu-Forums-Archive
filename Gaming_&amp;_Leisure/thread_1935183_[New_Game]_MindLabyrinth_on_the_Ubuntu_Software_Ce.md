### Post by lbedubourg on 2012-03-03
Hello,

I am one of the two indie developers who created **Mind Labyrinth - The ultimate journey of Pachamama's eternal hero**.

This is an old school puzzle game where you have to safely lead the hero to the exit. You cannot control the hero who's going straight, only level elements which change his direction and his overall chances to survive :)

This game was first created to have fun with Neurosky's MindWave headset (a brain/computer interface [http://neurosky.net](http://neurosky.net)). If you own a MindWave you have to concentrate to activate level items and often meditate to energize the eternal hero (ie: regain lives).

We also created a faster and more intense GamePlay so the game can be played and fully enjoyed without the MindWave controller.

The game is on the Ubuntu Software Center since this morning and contains both modes for $3.99 (launch prize, 1D6 - 0.01cts).
[https://apps.ubuntu.com/cat/applications/oneiric/mindlabyrinth/](https://apps.ubuntu.com/cat/applications/oneiric/mindlabyrinth/)

Mind Labyrinth has been developed with the haxe programming language ([http://haxe.org](http://haxe.org)) and NME toolset ([http://www.haxenme.org](http://www.haxenme.org)).

The game is a friendly co-production of Thinkslow ([http://thinkslow.net](http://thinkslow.net) - François) and labe.me ([http://labe.me](http://labe.me) - Laurent, hi that's me).

You can download a free demo with exclusive levels on our websites and I also made a "promotion" video (no hypnotoad, I swear) there [http://labe.me/en/mindlabyrinth.html](http://labe.me/en/mindlabyrinth.html)

The game is humble but created with passion and we hope players will enjoy it!

If you have any question just ask, I will be glad to share our first experience after a good night of sleep.

Best regards

Laurent
