### Post by eqisow on 2006-06-15
Well, for those without internet connection there is a disk iso with some extra resources on it [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=183933"). However, I don't think it includes games. So, as far as that goes, I don't really have enough bandwidth to go posting a few hundred MB ISO. Your best bet, imo, would be to simply download the .debs from [http://packages.ubuntu.com]("http://packages.ubuntu.com") and stick them on a thumb drive or burn a CD. The only caveat is that you will need to make sure that you have the required dependencies for everything. 

Depending on how high end your computer is, there are also some more graphically advanced games for Ubuntu available elsewhere on the net. Enemy Territory, TC: Elite, Nexuiz, Sauerbraten, and Tremulous are all fairly cool FPS games, if that's your thing. There are also a few commercial games available including UT 2004, Doom 3, and Quake 3. (pretty much everything from iD software is available in one form or another)
