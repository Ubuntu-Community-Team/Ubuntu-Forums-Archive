### Post by matthewcraig on 2008-02-17
Sweden and movies on the Internet is very curious to me.

There is lots of bandwidth and loose copyright laws.

Seems to me a perfect place to set up your own movie distribution services.  Not with American films, but with Swedish films.  Seems to me this could be a place where everyone could have their own YouTube site and where the film schools could make all their class projects available online.

You have the infrastructure to do for yourself what everyone else is restricted to do.  Why not take advantage of it?  Sweden could end up being the kings of digital content in the long term, when the rest of the world catches up with the infrastructure already available there.  (And when I say infrastructure, I mean both 100 Mbit/s connections and legislation.)

The alternative is continuing to embrace the Hollywood content, which acts as a deadweight anchor for your culture and for your progress for digital distribution.
