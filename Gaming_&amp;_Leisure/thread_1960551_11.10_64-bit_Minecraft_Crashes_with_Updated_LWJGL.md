### Post by regor210 on 2012-04-18
update. My bad. Optimus hybrid is Nvidia only so Bumblebee will not help with an ATI dual-GPU ati/intel hybrid video adapter.
---------------------------------------------------
It looks like your laptop has an Optimus hybrid.

In other words your laptops video adapter is both a ATI and an Intel adapter.

The ATI uses more power but is good for playing games.

The Intel side is for eveyday use and uses less power.

In GUN/Linux Ubuntu you have 3 choices that I know of.

1. Go into your BIOS and disable your Intel side of your video adapter. Your 3D video will work grate but your batter run time will suffer.

2. Go into your BIOS and disable your ATI side of your video adapter. Your battery run time on a charge will increase but gaming will suffer.

3. Theres an open source video driver called  Bumblebee.

Bumblebee Stable PPA
[https://launchpad.net/~hybrid-graphics-linux](https://launchpad.net/~hybrid-graphics-linux)
[https://launchpad.net/~bumblebee/+archive/stable](https://launchpad.net/~bumblebee/+archive/stable)

Using this driver the ATI side is only used  when you use the optirun switch like this

$ optirun java -jar .minecraft/minecraft.jar

Its a bit of a pain to run all of your games from the Command line $

I dont have the hardware , Optimus hybrid but I helped someone to get a nvidia Optimus hybrid up and going here.
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1907341&page=4](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1907341&page=4)
