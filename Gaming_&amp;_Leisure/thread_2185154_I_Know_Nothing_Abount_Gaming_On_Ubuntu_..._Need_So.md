### Post by Kirboosy on 2013-11-01
Hi, I researched your model and it looks like you have a **GeForce GTS240** or **GTX260** graphics card. Gallium 0.4 on NV92 isn't the actual Graphics card.[ Wikipedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gallium3D") has more information on Gallium3D if you are interested.

However, Your card isn't the best but it isn't the worst. I have a GTX250 in my desktop from a few years ago and I have no problems running most games. Some games you won't be able to run maxed out, turned all the way up. Most games will run really well. You might just have to tweak the settings of the game until you find that sweet spot. If you decide that your computer isn't good enough for gaming you can easily pop in another graphics card and take your gaming to the next level.

I would highly recommend checking out [Steam]("http://store.steampowered.com/") for Linux. They have a good Library of games and its constantly expanding.

If you get a bluetooth adapter I believe you can hookup a PS3 controller to the computer or you can plug it in through USB. Also for the Xbox 360 style controllers if you buy a USB corded version that would work with your computer. I personally have a Razer Sabertooth controller for my Xbox360 that I can use on my system. It works really well and I didn't have to configure much with Steam.

Hope that helps!
