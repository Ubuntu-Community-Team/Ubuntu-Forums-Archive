### Post by DarkAmbient on 2013-08-21
The specs your aiming at is similar to my lappie:

CPU: Intel(R) Core(TM) i7-4700MQ CPU @ 2.40GHz (4 Cores, hyperthread meaning there is 2 threads a core. It's supposed to have some turbomode where speed inc. to 3.2GHz, havn't noticed.)
RAM slot 1: 4GiG SODIMM DDR3 Synkront 1600 MHz
RAM slot 2: 4GiG SODIMM DDR3 Synkront 1600 MHz
Internal GPU: Haswell Integrated Graphics Controller
Dedicated GPU: GeForce GT 745M (2GiG DDR3 or GDDR5, unsure)
HDD: 750GiG SATA 5200RPM.

The bottleneck of my laptop is the harddrive, I meanth to switch for an SSD when I bougth it. But there is no way to replace the HDD without opening the chassie, meaning I'd void the guarantee doing that.
So if you'r able, I'd recommend getting an SSD with your laptop.

Games I've tried so far:

Killing Floor runs totally smooth with everything max. (Using dedicated, almost max using internal)
Burnout Paradise (using Wine) also runs smooth, all effects max except anti-alias at only x2, games won't start otherwise, could be Wine?.
Dota2 I've been unable to launch using the dedicated GPU, but it runs well on the internal one, using almost full effects.  

All games with 1920x1080 resolution.


According to benchmark-sites you'd be able to play modern games on low to medium graphics with a decent FPS, with a Intel HD 4000. To me it seems the GPU will be the least powerfull part in your laptop to be.
Allthough you will have no issues playing Quake3 with high graphic and res.
