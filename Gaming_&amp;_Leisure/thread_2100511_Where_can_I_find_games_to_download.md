### Post by LillyDragon on 2013-01-02
Not referring to warez, are we? =P To answer your question, it's difficult to find one site that covers all the games you can enjoy on Linux. Don't be afraid to search around on Google in a genre you enjoy the most. The Software Center has quite a few of them in the Games section, but definitely not all of them; its copy of OpenArena even is a few version numbers behind. Just digging around older threads in this Games forum should have links to plenty of other games you may interested in as well.

Although Desura a is good place to start : [http://www.desura.com/](http://www.desura.com/)

If you're handy enough with WINE, and love shopping for incredibly cheap digital titles that are DRM-free, there are a lot of older Windows PC games you can enjoy from the comforts of your Linux desktop available on GoG as well : [http://www.gog.com/](http://www.gog.com/)

### Post by LillyDragon on 2013-01-02
Well, if you're not picky about certain genres, you can always try the Aleph One engine.

[http://source.bungie.org/](http://source.bungie.org/)

Bunjie released the entire Marathon Trilogy's data files and engine source code some years ago for free, (Still copyrighted, of course, but free to use.) and the community has done a lot to improve it since then. Now it's possible to play all three shooter games on Windows, Mac, and Linux, with an improved HUD, OpenGL rendering, and HD texture packs.

And if that's not enough, there are several or more finished large scale mods with stories of their own too. You're looking at hundreds of hours of game play right there. The difficulty curve is even greater than DOOM, IMO, but the content is there if you have the skill. If you want "big", this is it. =P I can't think of anything else with more content to choose from.
