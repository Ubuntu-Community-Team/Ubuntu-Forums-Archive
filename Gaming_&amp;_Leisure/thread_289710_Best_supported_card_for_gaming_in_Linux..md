[http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.asp?DEPA=0&type=&Description=7600gt&Submit=ENE&N=0&Ntk=all&Go.x=0&Go.y=0](http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.asp?DEPA=0&type=&Description=7600gt&Submit=ENE&N=0&Ntk=all&Go.x=0&Go.y=0)
The difference between card manufacturers is usually very minimal. Sometimes manufacturers overclock or underclock their products, but this generally equates to small differences. 

My general rule is to get the cheapest one, or if you are uncomfortable with small manufacturers, get the cheapest one from a big name manufacturer, such as Asus, Biostar, Diamond, EVGA, Foxconn, Gigabyte, MSI, Sapphire, or XFX to name some.

And just as an added note, for the price, its a tough call between the 7600GS and GT. -- The GS will play any game you can think of at very high settings, but the GT will max the settings on most games but for about $40 more.
