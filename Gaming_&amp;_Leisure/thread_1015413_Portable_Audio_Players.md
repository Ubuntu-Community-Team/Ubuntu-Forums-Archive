### Post by Naegling23 on 2009-01-15
[http://www.rockbox.org/](http://www.rockbox.org/)

Its an open source firmware for mp3 players.  It has photo, video, ogg support, games (doom, game boy emulator!) and possibly more depending on what your hardware can handle (for example, I also have FM tuning, but ipods dont have the hardware to support it).  Just browse the site, and buy any mp3 player listed.  The best part is that the support for a lot of older players is pretty good, so you can get a great deal on an older, or used model, but with rockbox you get all of the features of the modern players too.

I've got a 5+ year old iriver H10 running rockbox, and every once in a while I think of trading it in, but anything else would be a downgrade from what I already have.  (alright, the zune, would be an upgrade, but it doesnt have linux support so I cant use it).  

Some of the downsides are that you are running older hardware, so the screens usually are not as great, and since its 3rd party firmware, the buttons might change, or other minor annoyances, and possibly a feature or two will not be supported.  For the most part, the interface looks great, and is customizable with a bunch of skins available if you dont like the default theme.  Still, some of the "artwork" is a little rough, and some of the organization is questionable (there is a music database, but not photo, or video, so you have to browse for them yourself).  Still, I suggest it for anyone looking for an mp3 player.
