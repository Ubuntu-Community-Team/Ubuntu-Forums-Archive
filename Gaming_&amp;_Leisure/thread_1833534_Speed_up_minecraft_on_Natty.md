### Post by Happeh on 2011-08-26
Hello, New to the forum and would like to help people with the same problems iv'e had with minecraft on natty.
Make sure your using java 7, Iv'e had a few audio/lag issues with the openjdk one. [http://brunoreis.com/tech/intalling-java-7-ubuntu-natty/]("http://brunoreis.com/tech/intalling-java-7-ubuntu-natty/") 

A second thing  is to disable compiz & use Metacity (Install Compiz Fusion Icon) from synaptic or ubuntu software center.

The last thing is to use OptiFine a minecraft mod, 

[http://www.minecraftforum.net/topic/249637-173-optifine-hd-g-fps-boost/]("http://www.minecraftforum.net/topic/249637-173-optifine-hd-g-fps-boost/")

Right click the minecraft.jar file in ~ .minecraft/bin, and click Open with archive manager, right click the META-INF and click delete.

If you have two or more cpu cores, download [http://hulkshare.com/x4lbkj0xmqww]("http://hulkshare.com/x4lbkj0xmqww") 

If you have one cpu core, download [http://hulkshare.com/s04f9sssgdri]("http://hulkshare.com/s04f9sssgdri")

Drag and drop the files from the archive you just downloaded to the minecraft.jar open in archive manager already.

Once done, run minecraft join a server or play in singleplayer and hit esc, options, video settings, You can mess around with them but iv'e found mine to give me the smoothest gameplay :>
[http://i.imgur.com/fmiYT.png]("http://i.imgur.com/fmiYT.png")
Click other, and turn Smooth FPS & Smooth input on.

You should be getting a few more fps and less laggy.

Enjoy! :p

:edit: forgot to mention, if you have ATI, make sure to play around with the settings and change most of them to speed instead of quality, and vsync disabled, no idea with nvidia/intel
