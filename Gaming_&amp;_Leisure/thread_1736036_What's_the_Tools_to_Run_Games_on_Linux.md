### Post by handy on 2011-04-22
Codeweavers Crossover Games is good for installing & playing many Windows games on both Linux & Mac:

[http://www.codeweavers.com/](http://www.codeweavers.com/) 

Codeweavers are the prime Wine supporters, making Crossover is how they make money to live. They are honest, friendly & helpful people. The dev's watch the forum pretty closely & often take part in the discussions & work toward solving problems.

Another project with a completely different aspect is DJL:

[http://en.djl-linux.org/](http://en.djl-linux.org/)

I've used it in the past, it has some valuable benefits when it comes to organising your Linux games on your machine, & being able to download all of the required libraries & whatever else that you need to be able to run the games.

From memory you could download all of these files & store them in one directory that a variety of games could share.

It has other tricks up its sleeve also & is definitely worth having a look at.
