### Post by donkyhotay on 2009-06-11
Actually the graphics are very good for a hex map turn based strategy game. Most of them (even the proprietary windows only ones) are really cheesy. Take a look at heroes of might and magic series for comparison. The graphics there are all 2d sprites (except for the the most recent which I have never played) and look really lame compared to the average RTS or FPS. Honestly though graphics (while nice) have no bearing on whether a game is any fun or not. One of my favorite games even before migrating to linux is bzflag which is *horrible* compared to almost any other FPS still being played these days. It also has great gameplay to it with having semi-realistic physics while in the air (momentum and no turning) and no strafing (you're a tank). If you go even further and look at nethack, angband, dwarf fortress, etc. which have no 'real' graphics but have some of the most in-depth gameplay around and (especially in regards to nethack) have been around for years. Taking it another step some of the games I have the most fond memories of are the old infocom games (like zork) which are all text-based. Now I'll admit I appreciate good graphics in a game but I'd rather have a really fun game that looks ugly then a pretty game that is completely boring and pointless.

Wesnoth has mastered this type of gameplay, and that is exactly why I find myself playing it again, and again, and again.
