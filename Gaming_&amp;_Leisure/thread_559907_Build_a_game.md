### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2007-09-25
> Building a game like Halo 3 would be a massive undertaking for only a few people. Of course you couldn't build something like Halo 3, because it is impossible to build a sequel to a nonexistent project :P

Good luck, though. With a bit of effort, a good story-oriented game in Linux that is *not* an "interactive fiction" text-based game (fantastic though they are) is definitely possible -- and would be quite good.
My random recommendation is to try a hybrid between 2D and 3D; 2D graphics let you cram a lot of cool detail into the game world that is just too heavy in 3D. (Whereas characters and the like tend to be much more interesting in three dimensions).

Also, whatever you do, try something open-ended. Do not hard-code unless absolutely necessary, because a large-scale game developed over time by a large community of random individuals is a powerful thing indeed. People quite often think of little details to add - even just characters that say certain things - and it would be cool if that sort of addition could be quickly swallowed into the project's main version.
