### Post by acoustibop on 2007-12-15
Certainly.  It's not so much to do with the operating system; it's to do with having a working capture (i.e. one you can input video to) card in your machine.

For instance, I have a Hauppauge Impact VCR card in this machine, and when I run my Playstation, I usually run a composite signal from that to the card and the audio to my sound card, and I can view the output in TVTime.  Just as good as Windows.

Unfortunately, not all the capture cards that work in Windows will work in Linux because the manufactures don't release drivers that support the capture functions themselves and won't release documentation on this to people who might develop them themselves.

ATI cards (AIW and VIVO cards) that include capture functions fall into this category.  Nvidia I'm not sure about, but Hauppauge cards, for instance, generally work well in Linux.

What capture card were you thinking of using?
