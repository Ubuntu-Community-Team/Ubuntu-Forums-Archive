### Post by oldsoundguy on 2008-11-05
what you are dealing with here is the normal lifespan of software.  Yesterday's hot new item becomes this month's "just OK" and then gets surpassed by the next hot new item.  (think about your games that way, too!)
As the games advance, the software has to play catchup.  If there is nothing in it for them or the budget is too low to keep developing, it will, eventually, die by the wayside!

People, especially in the field of FOSS or Open source, are reluctant to put out cash to "try" a system.  What Crossover did was kick their sales in the tush by running that one day FREE promo .. simply because, now there are actual USERS of their program that will "tell their friends".  Since the program is no longer free, and the free versions we got are only good for a year, it WILL boost their sales. As they DO have, what appears to be, a quality product that IS worth the bucks to many users.

Their next step is to add support to more programs .. both in the gaming area and in the business area (such a full Adobe CS3 suite as it only supports CS2 Photoshop in full right now) to be able to really make inroads and be able to SELL their adaptive program.

Either that, or someone else will come down the road with a better mousetrap!
