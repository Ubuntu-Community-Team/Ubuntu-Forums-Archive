### Post by byuu on 2009-06-01
> Well I just tested it out and it crashed on a quad core with 4gb of ram about 2 minutes into Star Ocean. (which ran like crap btw)Sorry, I have to be very conservative with the default drivers because many Linux drivers don't have hardware acceleration (nVidia OSS driver lacks OpenGL, older ATI OSS driver lacks X-Video). It starts with SDL which is absolutely painfully slow at scaling video. If you have an accelerated video driver, you can set the video output to X-Video or OpenGL from Settings->Configuration->Advanced. That would almost double the speed.

Since [this]("http://blogs.adobe.com/penguin.swf/linuxaudio.png") is the state of Linux audio, you'll probably need to try a few of the five drivers we've made thus far. One of them should work well, so long as you haven't made the mistake of installing PulseAudio (eg LatencyAudio.)

I don't know why Star Ocean crashed for you, it doesn't on any of my machines. A gdb backtrace would be very helpful if you wanted me to fix it.

At any rate, I apologize that you had a bad experience with it. I have a hard time programming for Linux APIs.

> in addition, this 'accurate emulation' is to a limited number of games that do not contain any special, additional chips. It's too early for BSNES to be preached upon everyone anyway.Which special chip does ZSNES or Snes9X support that bsnes does not? Given you'd need a WIP for SuperFX, but that should be out shortly.

> Why would some one want a emulator that is "more accurate" to the snes hardware at higher hardware cost and efficiency when zsnes works fine on low end hardware with speed hacks and so on? To me as long as it works than why improve upon it, especially since its an emulator meaning it will never be 100% accurate anyway.A couple of reasons. Maybe they want to play one of the ~100 or so games that don't work right in ZSNES (Super Mario RPG, Actraiser 2, Battle Blaze, F1 Grand Prix, ...) or Snes9X (Mega Man X3, Sink or Swim, Cu-On-Pa, ...), or either (Der Langrisser, Zoop, Pebble Beach New Tournament, Masters New, SD Gundam G-NEXT, ...) Or maybe they wanted to hear the vocals in Earthworm Jim 2, Breath of Fire 2 (G), or N-warp Daisakusen. Or didn't want Star Ocean / Star Fox running almost twice as fast as the real system. And some people are just purists and want it to be as perfect as we can get. Sure it's not 100% perfect, but it's the difference between 90% and 99.8%.

Some people just like the GUI. It's the only official one that uses a standard, native GUI toolkit on Linux. Though BearOso's GTK+ port of Snes9X is very nice as well.

The downsides are obvious and severe: horrendously bad speed and no savestates.

But by all means, please use ZSNES or Snes9X if they work better for you. They have a lot more features and are aimed at end users playing them, especially on older hardware. Mine is not, it is for research. The reason I make bsnes is to reverse engineer more of the hardware to help improve those and other emulators. You'll see I've submitted dozens of patches to both to help them improve their emulators without hindering speed. They've helped me out with mine, too.

We really don't mind which emulator you use, as it's not a competition. They all aim for different goals (ZSNES = speed, Snes9X = portability, bsnes = research), and are all worked on by all of us together.

> If I want accurate I will go and play it on the console.That works for now, but SNES games and hardware will start to fail mechanically in two dozen or so years most likely. We're doing the research now while people still care so that it can be preserved long into the future as much as possible.
