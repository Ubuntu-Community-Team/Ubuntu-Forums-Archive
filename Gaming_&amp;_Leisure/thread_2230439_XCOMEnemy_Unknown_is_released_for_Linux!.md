### Post by oldrocker99 on 2014-06-19
Oh yes, there are lots of Windows games which don't work on Linux (or, rather, wine) and are unlikely to be ported soon (I'm looking at **you**, Elder Scrolls). However, I am very, very grateful for the games we **do** have, which are (in some cases), games I never thought I'd be able to play without Windows. Keep in mind that Paradox is releasing their new games cross-platform on the same day, and it's likely that Firaxis will now follow suit. And it's more than possible that other studios will join them in the near future.

For all the whinging and bitching about Steam, even RMS admits that Steam is a Good Thing for GNU/Linux users. I'm personally fine with it; I have no problem paying for a closed-source program that runs on my open-source OS. I've been buying closed-source games since the first Humble Bundle.
