Halfway through the year, Linux gaming is looking better than ever. Still to come, in 2014, are native Kickstarted Linux versions of Wasteland 2 and Infinity Quest. Already, Portal and Half-Life 1+2 are out of beta, Europa Universalis IV will come out for Linux the day it first is released. Legend of Grimrock on Steam has allowed easy installation of mods from the Steam Workshop. Of course, I want to see more native Linux versions of major games I want to play; for a long time, for me, the only such port was Neverwinter Nights, which I still play after 11 years. Crusader Kings 2 has lately held my interest. It will be possible (it says here) to, if one can establish a long-lasting dynasty in Crusader Kings 2, that Europa Universalis IV will import the save files, so you can continue playing that nation. The Paradox "grand strategy" games are certainly not for everyone, but I like 'em.
