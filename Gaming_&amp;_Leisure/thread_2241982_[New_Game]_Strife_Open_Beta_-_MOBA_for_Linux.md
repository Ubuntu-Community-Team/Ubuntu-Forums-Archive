### Post by Doc_Pixelway on 2014-08-29
Hey fellow Linux gamers! I just started playing Strife not too long ago, as my search continued to play a MOBA similar to LoL on Linux (even tried to get WINE to work with LoL with no success :( ). I then stumbled upon this game while it was in closed beta and managed to get a key and enjoy it immensely. The characters are quirky, fun, and much of the graphics are amazing, especially for a Linux game. When I found out that this game supported Linux (S2 did this for HoN, no surprise here :D) I quickly signed up for a beta key and waited patiently.

However! You don't have to wait because [Strife is now open beta]("https://strife.com/create?raf=2FNDGJ9C")!

Please come join me and enjoy this game with me as a fellow MOBA Linux player. Feel free to add me in-game, whether you need some tips or simply want to hang out (I am one of the few regulars in the Linux channel). Although, if you're already familiar with MOBAs, you'll find the game pretty easy and fun to get into. Even if you're not familiar with the MOBA genre, I HIGHLY recommend this one due to its more enjoyable casual experience. One aspect I really like in this game is the gold sharing it provides, making support roles more interesting and not starved for gold income. 

They also added a fun and interesting story mode, Prophecy of Darkstone, for single players (while still giving 1st play completion rewards). They also plan on adding more single player chapters/stories if you're into backstories and such. I seriously hope they continue this trend because it felt really fun to watch/play my favorite Hero's introduction mid play-through.

So far I have heard it works on many Linux distros, but specifically Ubuntu 12.04 and 14.04 I have tried and played on. I have noticed no difference from the Windows counterpart (no negatives to really report here). The game comes pre-packaged as an executable for Linux, no installer is needed. Just a few clicks and begin patching the latest version. :)

Remember, it's beta, so expect a few things you don't like about it. S2 listens, and the closed beta community has shaped the game to what it is now, please be apart of that and share your bug reports, suggestions, ideas, etc with all of us to make this game better.

- Doc (IGN: Keizgon, Nick: "Doc")
