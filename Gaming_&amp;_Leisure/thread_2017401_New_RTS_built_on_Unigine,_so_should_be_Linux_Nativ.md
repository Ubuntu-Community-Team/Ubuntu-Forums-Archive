The RTS TRYST. 
> 
Tryst is an RTS set in the year 2381. Tryst achieves its quick,  action based gameplay through a clever system of upgrades, unique to our  two distinct races. Here are some of the features we bring to the table  &#8211;
 **1. Unique Character Abilities and Upgrades** &#8211; Almost all our  battle units come with special abilities of their own. Its upto the  player to choose what they want and how they want to match the powers of  one with that of another.
    
    **2. Multiplayer &#8211; **Up to 8 player free for all maps that always promise to keep people reinventing new strategies to outdo their opponents.
    
    **3. Environmental Hazards &#8211;** Maps are filled with dangers that can both hinder a player or help him set up the perfect ambush against an enemy.
    
    **4. Keep on Going &#8211; **You don&#8217;t have to worry about players who go  offline or have to leave in the middle of a game. They&#8217;ll be replaced  with competent AI immediately.
 5. **Unigine Graphics Engine &#8211;** State-of-the-art visuals powered by UNIGINE engine and race specific UI, etc...
> **Welcome to Ishtonia IV**

 
    Ishtonia IV lies at the very edges of the Kibeld System. Discovered  wholly by accident, this was a system whose only inhabitable planet  became its sole claim to fame and the salvation of man&#8230; or so that was  what the conglomerates and governments told its people to believe.
 This lonely planet contained an abundance of a compound unfathomable  to the human mind &#8211; Lohum, a substance that could, via a deceptively  simple process, be altered to form any carbon compound conceivable; and  in this it was indeed the salvation of &#8216;humanity&#8217;. This momentous  discovery was succeeded by the colonization of the distant planet.  Multiple mining, processing and shipping facilities became the logical  next step.
 It stood to reason and social order that the &#8216;employees&#8217; of said  mining facilities would be of an incarcerated nature. In abundance and  unwilling to protest their posting, these inmates became the major  population of the planet while a smaller number of mercenary overseers,  scientists and administrators managed them.
 This set up worked well for a while but as newer generations were  born into the planet, so was the want for change - The kind of which  could jeopardize the efficiency of the various mining operations on  Ishtonia.
 Civil war would have fragmented the planet had not a new threat  arrived, raining down from the fiery Ishtonian sky. The change that had  been fought over for the duration of the war was briefly forgotten&#8230; 
 A race of Sentient Ferrous-based life forms calling themselves the  Zali had found this planet for much the same reason that the Humans had.  Lohum became a common need, a unifying factor.
 First contact was made amid much fanfare, awe and repressed fear. On  Ishta IV, civil war was forgotten in the light of a combined  Human-Machine effort to mine and process Ishta. A process that by no  means eased the bruises the Civil war was being fought over, but did  serve as a brief distraction. Most Rebel factions were mollified, others  simply chose to go underground, recuperating and waiting. The Civil  movement continued under Aeryn Ozarr, but its gusto replaced with an  uncertainty and inborn cynicism.
 Time wore on and as efforts to understand and appropriate alien  technology were hindered, fissures grew ever wider between the working  and ruling classes of Ishtonia. At the same time, the Command&#8217;s relation  with the Zali began to rupture into animosity. Frustrations and hidden  motives boiled until the cauldron of war tipped over. The Zali launched a  global assault on all Major Human settlements. Given that both sides  were operating on limited supplies, the human remnant managed to hold  onto their major colonies and production facilities.
 Bruised, bloodied but refusing to back down, it is now up to a new  generation of heroes to direct the mechanisms set in motion by their  forbearers.
  
 [B]Aeryn's Telling
     [/B]

 "I&#8217;ve only heard stories of how Ishtonia was colonized. Each  retelling is different from the last but after you&#8217;ve heard them all,  spat out like gadflies eager to annoy, there&#8217;s a simple version to this  glorious prison&#8217;s history &#8211; &#8216;If you ain&#8217;t rich, you ain&#8217;t calling the  shots.&#8217;
 That&#8217;s how all this began. This little structured society of hardened  criminals booted off of their home planet. Sure, some of them had it  coming and some didn&#8217;t really have the humanity to care &#8211; but the rest  of them&#8230; they were victims of stereotype. What would a man in a suit  know about survival? Real survival, that is. Not those little games they  play in their modern day towers and halls but real life down in the  streets and gutters&#8230; in the world that exists in the shadow of theirs.  The world they turn to when they need some real work done. From what I  hear, that world back on Earth looked a lot like the one here on  Ishtonia IV.
 All those conniving little leeches saw was cheap labor and a chance  to grab some primo real estate in the process&#8230; bet they made themselves  look all noble in the process as well, telling the masses on earth that  they were moving all the &#8216;bad people&#8217; away where we couldn&#8217;t hurt them  anymore. Somehow they forgot to mention that some of those &#8216;bad people&#8217;  were only small timers&#8230; desperate folk, kids who didn&#8217;t know better,  guys who just couldn&#8217;t catch a break.
 So, here we are. The 5th generation of those poor saps, fighting like our mothers and fathers before us.
    If you ain&#8217;t rich, you ain&#8217;t calling the shots.
 Yeah, we&#8217;re trying to change that. We want our lives back. That&#8217;s all.
 The name&#8217;s Aeryn, by the way and I&#8217;ve been at it ever since my Mother  died. She was one of the founders of our movement. Damn crazy woman,  she was&#8230; Taught me all I know. It&#8217;s a pity she died so young. She had a  lot of fight in her and plenty to spare. I bet the High Command  celebrated that day.
 My dad? Couldn&#8217;t care less. All I&#8217;ve got to go on is my mom calling  him the stupidest man she could have fallen for. She loved him, though.  For all I know, he was a bastard that left us. Couldn&#8217;t handle the heat  and did what his type are best at.
 It&#8217;s because of scum like him that our efforts are held back. The  Command keeps shipping in new scum by the year, too. Keeps us divided.  Who&#8217;s to decide if you&#8217;re fighting for freedom because you just want it  or because you&#8217;ve been denied it since birth&#8230; and then there&#8217;s the  psycho&#8217;s that just love the carnage.
 And here&#8217;s the fun part &#8211; For all their little boardroom scheming  they didn&#8217;t see the Zali turning up from a mile away. Who&#8217;d have  factored in Alien sentience into a business plan anyways? Madness&#8230; a few  years ago, at least. But that isn&#8217;t turning out to be a rosy picture  either. The Command&#8217;s good at screwing things up and whatever  arrangement they had with the Zali, they botched it up royally because  those mechanical nutters are beginning a war march that&#8217;s going to barge  down the gates of any human colony they find. What do they care. Humans  all look the same, to them&#8230; Just like &#8216;bad guys&#8217; look the same to a  suit.
 Funny, isn&#8217;t it?"
