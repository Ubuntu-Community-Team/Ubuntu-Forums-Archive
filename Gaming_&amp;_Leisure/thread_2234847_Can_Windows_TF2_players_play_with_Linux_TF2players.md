If you have an AMD/ATI video card, your Linux performance is likely to be sub-par. That's not because of Linux, but the notoriously lousy closed source drivers for those cards. If you can swap your card out with any nVidia card, your performance will improve, as nVidia's closed source Linux drivers are pretty good.

If you look at the Steam forums for Painkiller;Hell and Damnation, you'll see many posts from ATI owners frustrated with their inability to play the game until ATI upgraded their drivers, which, with my GeForce 650ti, ran the first day.
