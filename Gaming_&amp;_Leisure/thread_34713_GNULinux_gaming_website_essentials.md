### Post by slux on 2005-05-16
Here's a fairly complete list of essential GNU/Linux gaming-related websites along with short descriptions of what they've got to offer. Feel free to add to the list if I missed any. :)

[LinuxGames](http://www.linuxgames.com)

*The* Linux gaming news website. It's one of the oldest gaming sites for Linux and is the place to go for up to date news. If you read only one of these, I recommend this one. Part of the TeleFragged network.

[Linux-gamers.net](http://www.linux-gamers.net/) 

A more recent news site complete with their own clans, HOWTOs, forums etc. If you're lucky enough to live in Germany you could also attend one of their LAN parties.

[The Linux Game Tome](http://www.happypenguin.org/)

A catalogue of all games for GNU/Linux with ratings and a central place of the Linux gamer community. I believe they're the longest-running website on the subject.

[Holarse](http://www.holarse.de/) 

Another news and resources site for the german-speaking people seems really nice and I've used some of their HOWTOs even if I don't know the language very well. They've been around for few years.

[Linux Game Publishing](http://www.linuxgamepublishing.com/) 

The only game publisher currently focusing only on GNU/Linux. They try to fill the void Loki left while maintaining a viable business plan for the future. This means you don't see many AAA games from them, but what they publish usually seem to be good-quality underdogs from a few years back.

[Icculus](http://www.icculus.org/) 

The namesake website of Ryan "Icculus" Gordon. Hosts a number of free software projects such as the GNU/Linux ports of the Build engine games (Duke Nukem 3D, etc.) and Freespace 2.. They have .plans of many prominent developers such as the already mentioned porting wizard who's responsible for many FPSs currently available  for GNU/Linux.

[LinuxHardware](http://www.linuxhardware.org/)

Strictly speaking not a gaming website but of interest to many gamers this is a hardware site focusing on Linux. Go here for the latest video card and processor benchmarks on your favourite OS. [Anandtech](http://www.anandtech.com/)  recently launched a Linux section that's certainly worth a look too.
 
[Linux Installers For Linux Games](http://liflg.org) 

Installers for both native and Wine-ran games and their mods. The easy way to get many Linux games lacking installers running.


[Slashdot Games](http://games.slashdot.org/)

The games section of the (in)famous Slashdot news & discussion site. More than just Linux but more likely to include Linux than other general games sites.

[Transgaming](http://www.transgaming.com/)

Known for their WINE-based Cedega/WineX product that enables gamers to play Windows games on GNU/Linux they create mixed feelings but are still a valuable resource if the only thing you're concerned about is getting that one killer-game running on your Linux system.
