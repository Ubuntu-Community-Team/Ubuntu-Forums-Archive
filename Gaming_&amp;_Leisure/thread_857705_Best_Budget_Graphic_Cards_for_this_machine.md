### Post by RIchard James13 on 2008-07-12
Here is what I would do. (Note I would choose Nvidia because of drivers)

First I would write down two figures, how much I would expect to pay and then a higher figure for what I possibly could afford to pay.

Then I would look at my machine, particularly the power supply. Can my power supply run the card I am going to buy? What can it run? Also look at the motherboard, I do have a PCI Express slot? 

Then I start looking through a few magazines and notice that Nvidia is rolling out it's 9 series cards. And the magazines are saying that the high end of the 8 series are looking better budget wise than mid range 9 series cards.

Then I would go to a few hardware sites that have graphics card roundups or reviews and read them, looking especially at the differences between OpenGL games and DirectX games. Because UT3 and ETQW are OpenGL games. I would also be conscious that the OpenGL driver in Windows may not be the same as the OpenGL driver in Linux.

After having picked a number like 9600 or 8800. I would then choose a fairly good brand but be careful not to pay for a brand name. I would definitely shun any cheaper brands as generally the quality is bad. I would be going for 75% quality brands.

So I might end up looking for say a XFX9600. Then I would go shopping around to find the best deal on that particular card.

Of course the process is entirely iterative since you need to see what people are selling the cards for before you decide what brand/model you want. 

The more research you put into your purchase the more likely you will find the best piece of hardware for your money. Research takes time and sometimes money.
