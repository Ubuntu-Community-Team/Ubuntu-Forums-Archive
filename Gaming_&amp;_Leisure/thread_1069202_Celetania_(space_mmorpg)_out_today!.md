### Post by curvedinfinity on 2009-02-16
> **Bios Element said:**
> I'll download it once I've heard some reviews. From the screenshots (Forgive me) It looks like a cartoon network style game with crayon drawn ships and menu's covering a game better suited for a browser based game...

I know it's probably not that but I don't really see much else. Anyone have some videos or something of it?

I've played it for the past two days. Basically, its Eve, except not as pretty and you can own multiple ships.

Yes, it has the same obnoxious queue-up and turn off the game "system." Yes, all you do in the game is research, fight, and mine.

Is it fun? I like it better than Eve, and I bet the high level strategy in this game is pretty intense. However, it literally takes two hours to get your first space ships up and going, and you even start will all of the components necessary to create a couple ships. It will literally take days to have the technology to make your first "colonization module", which is the thing that lets you grab new worlds. The offensive race doesn't start with the capability to transport resources, so it will take days for them to setup new colonies (if you don't know that you should go directly to the "resource silo" in the tech tree).

Yes... REAL LIFE DAYS.

The game play is ... really slow. You literally can only "play" 10 minutes or so before you run out of stuff to queue up, and then you have to wait oh, in my current case, a good 16 hours before I can do my next 10 minute stint of "playing". I think it would be better suited for a browser ... or at the very least, if it didn't hog so much memory and require pretty hefty graphics, I could run it in a window while I did other stuff.

Oh, and the battle system isn't great. To move a unit, you have to press a button, and then click what position on the XZ plane you want, and then click again for Y offset. Its ... obnoxious that the basic combat command is *three* player commands. As far as I can tell, there is no area attack command, so you have to tell your ships to destroy each enemy at a time (with pinpoint accuracy on the player's part).

Anyway, despite my gripes, give it a shot. Its filling a slot in the Linux game universe that is currently empty. And I liked it better than Eve, so that's worth something, right? :)
