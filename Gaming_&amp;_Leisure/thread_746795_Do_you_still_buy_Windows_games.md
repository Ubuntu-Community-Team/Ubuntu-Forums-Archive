### Post by ZeromusMog on 2008-04-24
Sorry but I can't buy into the "there are thousands of free Linux games!!" hype, because it's dumb. If you're OK with soulless replications of games released over a decade ago, go play gTetris or whatever and knock yourself out. However if you have friends who enjoy video games and want a quality experience, recent commercially released games are the way to go.

The conflict is deep here; besides the normal issues of video cards, performance, and fine-tuned platform-specific code that pushes out the most polygons, while productivity software thrives in an open free-sharing environment, games don't do quite so well. It takes a lot of effort to make a polished, quality game--the kind of effort that requires cash to get it done. As such I have no problem paying for a good video game.

Mostly I play Wii and DS games anyway, since even if I was using Windows all the time, spending half my play time messing around with video settings and dealing with crashes isn't fun at all. However much like Synaptic has endeared me to Ubuntu in ways that I likely don't give it enough credit, and that I've spent more on the Wii Virtual Console than on full Wii titles, Steam has gotten me to purchase more PC games than I ever have before. I don't like hunting down cardboard boxes with bits in them when I pay good money every month for a perfectly viable and much more impulse-fulfilling content delivery system to be connected straight to my house.

As such, I really hope that Steam really is coming to Linux soon, even if the selection is initially limited. Virtualization is getting closer every day to making the user's choice of OS irrelevant as far as what software they can run, and I am chomping at the bit for this brave new world. :) I hope Wine 1.0 is as amazing as it sounds, and that some day very soon I can load up Steam and Team Fortress 2 on Ubuntu and not even think about it twice!
