### Post by Glic2000 on 2013-09-10
I'm not a Linux user, but I discovered this project because it was linked from the official Sims forums.  I was thrilled to hear about someone other than EA making a game like The Sims, but I see quite a few problems with your Kickstarter.   
 
For starters, you claim that the narrator is Morgan Freeman, but he clearly isn't.  That's a lawsuit waiting to happen.  If I was Morgan Freeman, I'd be ready to sue for unauthorized use of my likeness.  This really needs to be changed.   
 
 The project doesn't seem to have a clear vision, just a lot of vague promises with no indication of the talent behind it.  The graphics showcased are very ugly.  If the finished game is going to look better than this, there should be some concept art showing how good the finished game could potentially look.   
 
 Why would I want to play this game instead of The Sims?  What is it going to do differently &#8211; and hopefully better than &#8211; The Sims?  The details of the gameplay are so vague.  The pitch says, "The choice is limited only by your imagination!"  which just sounds like a crazy promise.  I'm quite sure the game will have limitations besides my imagination.   
 
It also says you can "be a nun!"  What does this really mean?  Does that mean my character can dress up like a nun, or does the game include an entire system of religions, churchs, beliefs, etc?  Will I be able to run my own abbey?  The video only shows a single house, so it's not clear if the game has an entire simulated town.   Is it an open world game, like The Sims 3, or am I stuck with one house at a time, like The Sims 2?  Similarly, the text implies you can control your "dude" (singular).  Does that mean I can't play more than one character at a time, like I could in The Sims?   
 
 What sort of interesting things could I actually do in this game?  Can my characters get married, fall in love, have children?  Can they make enemies with each other?  What might the consequences of that be?    There's a lot of telling, but not much showing.  All we really get to see is a rather crude-looking 3D engine.  That's fine for a start, but to get people excited enough to invest, we really need some clear indication of the potential here, not a lot of vague statements like, "In this game you can do anything!"   
 
Finally, the pitch text has a lot of grammatical errors in it which make it look unprofessional.   
 
 Good luck with the project!   I would love to watch how it develops, but I think at the moment, your Kickstarter campaign is a bit premature.
