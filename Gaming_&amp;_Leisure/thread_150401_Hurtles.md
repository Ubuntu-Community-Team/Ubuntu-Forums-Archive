### Post by gerbman on 2006-03-26
[QUOTE=masooga]I know the options so far, Winex, Cedega and having a partition for gaming on Windows, but how many hurtles are in the way of adequte gaming support on Linux?[/QUOTE]
I guess it depends on what you want to play. [Here's]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=120615&highlight=world+warcraft") a tutorial for getting WoW working with Wine. [Wolfenstein - Enemy Territory]("http://www.splashdamage.com/") is a superb game that is free and runs on Ubuntu with no hacking required. If you're looking for a workout, [Stepmania]("http://www.stepmania.com/") is the way to go (pydance is in the repositories, but it's not as good IMO). Stepmania takes a little to make work, but it's not a big deal. I'm sure there are plenty of other games that run under Linux, but those are the ones I've played.
