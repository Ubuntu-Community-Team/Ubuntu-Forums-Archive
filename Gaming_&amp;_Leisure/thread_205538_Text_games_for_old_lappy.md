### Post by DirtDawg on 2006-06-28
[Super Star Trek]("http://almy.us/sst.html") is tons o' fun!

I also enjoy [Empire]("http://www.classicempire.com/") (available in synaptic), though I highly recommend ye olde "man empire" command.

There's tons of Nethack variants (Angband, slash 'em, etc), many available in synaptic.

Also, if you like text-adventure games, the Zork games are now free plus tons of TADS and Infocom games written by hobby programmers. The [I.F. Archive]("http://www.ifarchive.org/") is a good place to check all this stuff out. Interpreters are available through synaptic.

If you can dig up those old Zork games and an interpeter, that's fun. Oh, go check out the [Interactive Fiction archive]("http://www.ifarchive.org/"), too.

Not sure how well DosBox will work, but nab that and try to get ZZT going on it. (Epic Megagames says ZZT and all the registered adventures are public domain now) There's a ton of really good ZZT games. MUDs are fun, if you have an internet connection. Telnet will work for most of them.

Thinking about it, most good text based games are really dos based. If all you want are games, putting a copy of MS-DOS or FreeDOS on that thing is really the way to go, unless you want to connect to networks and stuff.

### Post by B0rsuk on 2006-07-02
Linley's Dungeon Crawl
[http://dungeoncrawl.org/](http://dungeoncrawl.org/)
( 'crawl' in repositories )

For me it's THE best roguelike, ever. I played ADOM for years and it's just boring compared to crawl. Angbands want you to die of boredom. Nethack didn't catch my attention.

Crawl is great for conneseurs. There's hardly any way for scumming. Environments are very varied, unlike, say, ADOM. You get a lot of variety in levels - water, rivers, lava pools, idols, temples, and other vaults; monsters are much more unique than in other roguelike games, and they don't feel randomly thrown together. Enemy spellcasters, group creatures, ranged, melee, skirmish (giant bats use hit&run) etc. *working* skill-based system. Races that actually matter. A lot of fun, varied spells. Varied maze-generating algorithms, each dungeon branch has its own. Gods who are much more complex than a healing potion, and reward or punish you for many different things, not just kiling creatures of opposite alignment.
Crawl is difficult, but many things make it feel just. For example, monster respawn rate is very low, meaning you can usually retreat to a safe area if you meet something mean, and most probably it will still be a _safe_ area. As an added bonus, it makes scumming much harder.
You can eat chunks cut from corpses, so hunger is rarely a serious problem, but sickness can be. So save normal food rations for later.
At first, you like ADOM, then you get fed up with story and bottlenecks. You always have to play ADOM in the same way, same order of levels. Crawl lets you choose, and there's great variety in randomly generated levels. There are many approaches to beating the game, and each has different pros and cons. You can use offensive magic, and no two magic schools are the same. You can just slash monsters up close. You can rely on armor or dodging. You can grab a powerful item and play like talismancer. You can summon or charm allies, backstab etc.


If you're serious about playing crawl. compile it from source with Darshan's Travel Patch, which makes a lot of very nice interface additions, and fixers some bugs. But you can still install via apt-get for quick introduction.
