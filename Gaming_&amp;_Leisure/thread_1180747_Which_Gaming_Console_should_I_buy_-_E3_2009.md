alright, I havn't owned a console since the sega genesis, and do not own any of the current generation, so your not getting a fanboy opinion by me (thats not to say any of the above posts have been biased, they seem quite fair, I just wanted to throw my 2 cents into the ring).  I have however, spent time with all three systems. 

Wii
-Its inexpensive, has innovative gameplay, and makes a great party game system/casual gamer/game with friends.  The games are typically easy to pick up and learn.  The drawback is that the motion sensor so far is...well....gimmicky, in that it is fun for a party game, but not yet well suited to hardcore gameplay, games are also less in debth, and are last generation graphics. Xbox360
-cheaper than the ps3, current generation games, technically a weaker system than the ps3, but it is yet to actually show on the current games.  Must pay to play online.  Prone to system failures and breakage.  

PS3
-expensive, very expensive.  Free online play, and best system spec. wise.  

Of these, the xbox360, and ps3 are more game/media systems with large hard drives, streaming media support, video playback etc.  The ps3 is also a blueray player (bonus if you dont already have one)...so, if you want a casual game system, or something for your friends to come over and play, wii is fine.  If you want a media center box, or more hardcore/bigger/more in depth games, then go xbox/ps3.  The ps3 seems to be the better media system, as it tosses in blue ray.
