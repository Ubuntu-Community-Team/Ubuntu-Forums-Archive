### Post by optofonik on 2008-12-23
Around the time of the writer's strike in Hollywood my roommate turned me on the to the PS2. The strike meant no decent paying work so I had to find cheap ways to entertain myself. Now, it looks like hard times are on the way for everyone. After watching my roomie have a blast playing CoD and trying it out myself I was convinced. Since I haven't been playing video games all my adult life there is a vast selection of last-generation games for the PS2 available used. Used and cheap. Cheap is good these days.

Now, you can pick up a refurbished PS2, GC, an original XBOX, and a system switcher from Gamestop for less than any single new console. Then, get some top rated used games (check out Metacritic) from either Gamestop or E-Starland for cheap ($2 - $15 each) and start playing. For cheap.

What! Three consoles you say!?!?!?

Sure. Why not? Even if you owned one or the other "back in the day" you can pick up the other two and see what you missed (pick up several used games for the price of one new game on any of the new consoles). There are great exclusives for each of the old consoles that are great fun and then you can decide which is the best console to buy cross-platform titles for (usually the original XBOX). Check these exclusives out:

**PS2** - "God of War" 1 & 2, "Jak & Daxter", "Twisted Metal: Black", and "ICO".

**Original XBOX** - "Halo" 1 & 2, "Fable", and "Raze's Hell" 

**Gamecube** - "Metroid Prime" 1 & 2, "Eternal Darkness" and "Mario Kart Double Dash".

Unless you own all three last-gen consoles you can't play all these crazy cool games. At used prices *all* 13 of the above games can be had for for about $150.00; an average of about 12 bucks each. All three consoles, a system switcher, and 13 of the best games ever will keep you glued to your controllers for quit awhile and can be had for about 3 bills. Craaazy, man.

Another great benefit of the last-gen consoles is that most last-gen games were designed for SDTV (exceptions are on the original XBOX) so the temptation to get a bigger, better, way expensive TV is less. Also, the PS2 and original XBOX have the added benefit of optical surround sound. If you already have a "home theater" set-up with optical inputs then for a few dollars in cables and adapters you can be surrounded by the din of battle, the racetrack, or whatever environment is on the screen.

Refurbed last-gen consoles and their accompanying used games could keep your fingers twitching till this-gen becomes last-gen and then you can pick-up a refurbed PS3 or Xbox360 for $60.00 and a Wii for $30.00.

Go nuts. Get all three last-gen consoles and you can keep yourself entertained on the cheap throughout the coming years of extended economic depression.
