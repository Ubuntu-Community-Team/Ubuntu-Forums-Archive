### Post by Ozor Mox on 2007-09-07
Just wondered what games people are in to playing on Ubuntu right now, plus a little description and link. It might give others ideas of what to play if they see something they like, and goes to show that there's plenty of games to play on Ubuntu!

Me:

Battle for Wesnoth
Fantasy turn-based strategy. It's very professionally made, a huge amount of fun especially in multiplayer with friends, and has countless maps, scenarios and campaigns produced by the community collected in an add-on server.
[www.wesnoth.org](www.wesnoth.org)

Scorched 3D
Turn-based strategy similar to Worms and Scorched Earth. Great graphics, multiplayer, and the apocalypse mod that I have just discovered for some proper destruction!
[www.scorched3d.co.uk](www.scorched3d.co.uk)

Simutrans
Highly detailed transport simulation game like Transport Tycoon but with more realism in my opinion (passengers and mail have destinations for example)...freeware but should be going open source soon with the release of version 1.0. Lots and lots of community contribution in the form of graphics "paks".
[www.simutrans.com](www.simutrans.com)

### Post by ubuntu27 on 2007-09-07
My favorite games are

[Bos Wars]("http://www.boswars.org/") - A Starcraft like RTS game. Bos Wars is a futuristic real time strategy game (RTS) and it's Open SOurce. In a RTS game, the player has to combat his enemies while developing his war economy. The trick is to balance the effort put into building his economy and building an army to defend and attack the enemies. Can be download a deb at [getdeb.net]("http://www.getdeb.net/app.php?name=Bos+Wars")

[Tremulous]("http://tremulous.net/") - It is considered the best Open Source games by many. Tremulous is a free and open source team-based first-person shooter with RTS elements. ame play is similar to the Quake II mod Gloom and the Half-Life mod Natural Selection. The game features two teams, humans and aliens, each with their own strengths and weaknesses.
