### Post by dave2001 on 2013-04-29
I'm excited about growing gaming support for Linux, and Valve is a big contributor. 

That being said, I also dislike Steam. If you'll read the fine print, you don't actually OWN any of the games you "buy" from Steam. Valve's Steam client is a service, which means anytime they decide to discontinue, they can. It also means that your account can be blocked/banned, and there is nothing you can do about it. If you're determined to be "cheating" at a game, your account can be banned, whether or not Valve's cheat-autodetection software is telling the truth.

I've actually heard stories of people's account being cancelled for no good reason, and Valve being less than forthcoming about why.

If you want to play a game on Steam, buy the game in a retail or digital copy from the actual publisher, and use the Steam Key they provide to activate. That way you have a copy of the game that is actually yours, to do with as you please.
