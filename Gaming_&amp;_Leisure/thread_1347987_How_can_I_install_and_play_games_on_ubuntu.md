### Post by madmax.santana on 2009-12-06
Right at the top of the forum where you submitted this post, there is a sticky post "Ubuntu 64-bit Gaming Guides". You should have a look at it. Also, I have picked a link from the same sticky post:

[http://gwos.org/doku.php/guides:start](http://gwos.org/doku.php/guides:start)

Visit it as well. There is a long list of games and when you click on any game of your choice, you are directed to a page on which a trusted step by step procedure is given to install that specific game. Try it.

Hope it help.

By the way, I am amazed myself to see that Ubuntu / Linux has so much to offer in the gaming section. I am a dedicated gamer and I play mostly windows based games... These Linux games are doing very well competing them especially that developers have made exact counter-parts of Medal of Honor and Counter Strike type games. The effort is very appreciable but sadly yet, these games are not as much powerful as the ones available for Windows. I have an impression that the Open Source software is better than the propriety software. But games are somewhat an exception.

Anyway, I don't mean to say that these games suck. I was very impressed by many of Ubuntu Games and I play them regularly. Be sure to give them a try and I hope you will like them more. Happy hunting!
