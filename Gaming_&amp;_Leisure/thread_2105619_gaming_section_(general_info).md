### Post by oldrocker99 on 2013-01-18
Unfortunately, most retail stores don't sell computer games; you can find every console well-represented, but few computer games are available in most stores. GameStop, a well-distributed chain of stores which has a significant online presence and sells a lot of Windows games online, sells NO computer games in their brick-and-mortar stores. This is why Steam, Desura, GameStop, GameFly, Direct2Drive, and GOG have done so well. Downloading games has become the new normal. You can buy physical copies of games from, say, Amazon, but they offer download, as well.

Even with American broadband (that is, not very fast broadband compared to the rest of the world), some games for Windows can take a long time to download from Steam; an hour or two for biggies like Fallout 3 with all DLCs for $5 (which I got last spring on my dual-boot machine). But, with Steam and Desura, you buy once and download as many times as you need to. I bought Crusader Kings 2 from Steam for Linux, and I can install it on my Windows partition without paying more. I consider that an advantage. Few physical discs are cross-platform (Dominions 3 had all three on one disc, the only one I can think of). Steam supports Mac a lot better than they currently support Linux, but, in a year, things will, I believe, have changed. Linux gaming FTW!
