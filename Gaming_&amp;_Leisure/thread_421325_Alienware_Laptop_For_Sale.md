What panty31772 is tactfully saying is your price is unrealistic. 

No one is going to buy your set-up for $4000 when they can get the exact same thing $3,349.00 brand new and with a factory warranty. You need to lower the price and/or raise the value of the offer. The suggestion of taking anything >$2500 seems much more in line. I'm sure that's not what you wanted to hear but it is the reality of the situation. With that said what you could do is kick in some "technical support" to raise the value of the offer closer to what you want. Sell it locally and give the buyer something like [no charge] delivery/setup, three site visits and ten phone requests over a 12 month period. There are a small group of well off, technically illiterate people willing to go this route.

Whatever you do just make sure you have the cash in your hand before handing over the laptop.
