> ***PlayStation Suite*** is an upcoming software framework that will be used to provide downloadable *PlayStation* content for devices that meet the forthcoming [PlayStation Certified]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Certified") requirements. Currently, this includes devices that both run [Android 2.3]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android_%28operating_system%29") and meet specific unnanounced hardware requirements, and the Sony [Next Generation Portable]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_Generation_Portable").   The framework will be cross-platform and cross-device, which Sony   refers to as "hardware-neutral".   	 		 		 		 		 				 			[INDENT]A little digging and this explains it better. (highlighted in red text)

    	 		 		[INDENT]Sony just dropped a bomb on the Japanese stage -- not a single [PlayStation Phone]("http://www.engadget.com/tag/PlayStationPhone/"),   but a PlayStation Phone experience for everybody. The company unveiled  a  cross-platform software framework called PlayStation Suite, which   sounds rather boring in those words, but what it amounts to is *an official PlayStation Store **filled with games for your Android tablets and cellphone*s. Sound [familiar]("http://www.engadget.com/2010/08/11/exclusive-sony-ericsson-to-introduce-android-3-0-gaming-platfor/")?[COLOR=#FF0000]   Sony's starting with an emulator for existing PSOne titles and is   promising an Android game store later this year, but soon it might be   much, much more: the company's calling PlayStation Suite a   "hardware-neutral" development framework to make games portable for *all* sorts of handhelds, and says that "new and exciting content" is also on the way. [/COLOR]

 Sony will sponsor a first-party licensing and quality-assurance scheme   called PlayStation Certified, and provide the marketplace as well,   likely hoping to attract major game developers to build top-tier titles   for mobile and get a piece of the action too. If your device doesn't   have [a pop-out gamepad]("http://www.engadget.com/2011/01/26/sony-ericsson-xperia-play-playstation-phone-preview/")  handy, it looks like PlayStation Suite will emulate touchscreen   controls, and you won't necessarily need a phone to get in on the   action, as Sony says the [next-generation PlayStation Portable]("http://www.engadget.com/2011/01/27/the-sony-psp2/")  will be compatible with games developed for PlayStation Suite right off   the bat. Doesn't look like we're getting any details on game prices or   compatible devices, but we imagine [one particular phone]("http://www.engadget.com/2011/01/26/sony-ericsson-xperia-play-playstation-phone-preview/") will change all that at Mobile World Congress next month.

 **Update: **Looks like PlayStation Suite requires Android  2.3 at a  minimum, and it's PSOne, not PlayStation Portable titles that  will be  emulated here, despite Kaz Hirai's quote during the festivities.  PR  after the break![/INDENT]
