> **MegaGlest**

         [[IMG]http://megaglest.org/uploads/images/screenshots/gameplay.jpg[/IMG]]("http://megaglest.org/screenshots.html") **The game**

 MegaGlest is an open source 3D-real-time strategy game, where you control the armies of one of seven different factions: [Tech]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/tech_overview.html"), [Magic]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/magic_overview.html"), [Egyptians]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/egypt_overview.html"), [Indians]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/indian_overview.html"), [Norsemen]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/norsemen_overview.html"), [Persian]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/persian_overview.html") or [Romans]("http://megaglest.org/techtree/romans_overview.html").  The game is setup in one of 16 naturally looking settings, which -like  the unit models- are crafted with great appreciation for detail.  Additional game data can be downloaded from within the game at no cost.
 **Gameplay**

 [[IMG]http://megaglest.org/uploads/images/screenshots/game_screens.gif[/IMG]]("http://megaglest.org/screenshots.html") A game of MegaGlest takes place on a map of varying size, such  aslargeplains and fields, with terrain features like rivers, mountains,  seas, or cliffs. Players must establish settlements to gain resources,  defend against other players, and train units to explore the map  andattackenemies. Different resources have to be gathered by  everyfaction. Thiscan be energy, food, gold, housing, stone and wood.  Choose a faction and create its different units and buildings while  developing your factions' unique abilities over the course of the game.  But beware: strategy matters, so chose wisely when deciding which units  to use when and where if you want a chance to beat the enemy.
 **Platforms**

 [[IMG]http://megaglest.org/uploads/images/screenshots/multiplayer_small.gif[/IMG]]("http://megaglest.org/screenshots.html") While the game is officially supported on Linux and Windows,  community contributed builds exist for Mac OS X and FreeBSD. Up to  eightplayers fight in multi-player games (over Internet or LAN) across  all supported platforms and against the various difficulty levels of  AIplayers.
 **History**

 MegaGlest is heavily based on the widely known [Glest]("http://glest.org/"), which it forked from in early 2010. Like its predecessor, MegaGlest is available under [liberal licensing terms]("http://megaglest.org/license.html") and can be downloaded free of charge.
