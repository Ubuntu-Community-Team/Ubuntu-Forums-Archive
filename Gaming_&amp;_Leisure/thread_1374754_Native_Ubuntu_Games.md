### Post by Naegling23 on 2010-01-07
it all depends on what type of game you are looking for....casual games like sudoku, cards, chess, etc should be in synaptic....If your running 9.10, I think there is a software "store" on the gnome menu that might let you browse a little easier for free games (I cant remember, and I'm at work on a windows box right now).

There are also about 63 FPS games, a few RTS, one or two MMORPG, a bunch of standared RPG's, a guitar hero clone, a few platformers, racing games, puzzle games and a few other free/open source games available, some via the package manager, and some available from third party repositories, or the games website itself.  There are a bunch of websites devoted to linux games, and most commentors on this site have some sort of link to one or two of them in their profiles.

And then there is commercial software.  Games like Quake 4, Doom 3, Quake Wars, Neverwinter nights 1, Unreal tournament 2004, World of Goo, and a bunch of smaller indie studio games that have native linux clients....additional games without native clients may be playable through wine (check [www.winehq](www.winehq)), they may require some setup, and tweaking though.  Games like World of Warcraft, Guild Wars, Half Life 2 and others fit in here (not all games will work, and some better than others...its sort of hit or miss)

Your best bet is to browse the gaming section of this website for news, links, and games that people are talking about, then follow up on those...
