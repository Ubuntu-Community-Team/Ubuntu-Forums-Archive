### Post by princedwi on 2006-02-05
Hey there! Glad to know there's another CnC fan in the Ubuntu world =)

Honestly, I would suggest running Generals on the Windows partition mainly because ATI lacks performance linux drivers. Right now, I have a Radeon x700 Pro and I get roughly 5 fps on the game. Until you purchase a better video card,  just let windows be your gaming os of choice. 

To properly get your videocard's drivers working for proper hardware acceleration on Ubuntu, go to this link:
[Ubuntu Installation Guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Installation_Guide")

There are Linux ports of other popular games (Quake 4, Doom III, America's Army), and you can try using Cedega or Wine for some other games requiring less graphical power (Shattered Galaxy, CnC Red Alert 2).
