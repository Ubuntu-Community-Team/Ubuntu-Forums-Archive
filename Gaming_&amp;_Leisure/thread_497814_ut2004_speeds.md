### Post by Contrast on 2007-07-12
Honestly, I never really saw much of a performance boost from running Xfce over KDE, and this is on older hardware we're talking about (800MHz AMD Duron, 384MB RAM). As EndPerform suggested, Fluxbox is a good option. Openbox and Blackbox, among several others, are also extremely lightweight. Just keep in mind that these really are barebones window managers, as opposed to the desktop environment (GNOME or KDE) that's been holding your hand through everything thus far. ;-) You'll have to do a bit of research before you figure out how to use them (e.g., since they usually don't have a start menu equivalent, you'll need to know the command for launching whatever program you want), but if you're running on very resource-limited systems, the payoff in your framerate could be worth it.
