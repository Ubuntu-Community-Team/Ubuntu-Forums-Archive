### Post by yeaitsdark on 2009-07-11
My advice personally is that if you really love the games they officially support, and that you are very content with that list - it's very much worth it.

If you love using Linux, and wish the plug and play aspect then it's great. They have additional support for certain things that Wine (while very very capable of) doesn't automatically implement or is difficult to do so. 

Also note that it is not necessary to indefinitely continue membership with them in order to use the game engine(aka software). For example, you purchase this month (which is 15$ initially or whatever), then that engine 6.x or 7.x whatever, will always be good for you. (If money is your concern, not that we aren't all concerned with money... this is something good to know.)

If you're not an active member you cannot update from them, nor add your opinions to what you'd like to be supported. They do a vote and you can input i.e Fall Out 3 or Dual Monitors etc. They are actually fairly good about this process, but it needs work considering.

Very simply, to answer your question it is not essential or necessary in any way to purchase Cedega. Wine is more than able to run many games very well. It is however and excellent tool if time is a constraint for you, and the time saved is worth the money paid. Hope that gives you some perspective.
