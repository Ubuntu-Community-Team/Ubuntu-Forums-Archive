### Post by rybu on 2007-09-18
Anyone know of a good bluetooth mouse suitable for gaming?  I'm looking to have all my laptop devices be wireless, if possible. Seems like bluetooth might be a good way to go.  If it's known to work well with Ubuntu, that'd be a plus.

The mouse doesn't need to be small (it's not for travel), but I'd like it to be responsive and preferrably be ambidextrous as I like to alternate which hand holds the mouse as the day goes by. Many buttons would be a plus but not neccessary.

I've only found one comparison on-line, for the "Logitech MX-900" and the "Microsoft Intellimouse Explorer for Bluetooth". 

Another that looks promising is "Logitech V270 Cordless Bluetooth Notebook Mouse" at least it's ambidextrous.  1000dpi.  It's not often the DPI spec is mentioned with bluetooth mice.

Kensington Pilotmouse Mini Bluetooth Mouse.  Looks okay, but I have little data on it.

Targus Bluetooth Laser Mouse AMB03CA -- 800/1600 dpi switchable.  This looks reasonable.  Not ideal, but getting better.  I'd prefer it if it had some more buttons.

Microsoft Wireless Bluetooth Laser Mouse 8000 looks okay.  I don't know what its DPI reading spec is, though. Reviews not encouraging.  I do like the looks of it more than the Targus.  Ah, bad review: [http://stuff.silverorange.com/archives/2007/march/lasermouse8000](http://stuff.silverorange.com/archives/2007/march/lasermouse8000)

Ah, a new product.  Razer Pro-Click Mobile:

Ambidextrous, 1200 dpi.  Looks okay. 

The Targus looks like a good option.  I don't have much data on the Logitech V270.  Another option would be to accept a dongle and go for the Logitech G7 Laser Cordless...  if only they had both a left and right handed one... it'd drive one mad to do a full comparison.
