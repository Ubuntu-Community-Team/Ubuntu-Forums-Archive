### Post by cogadh on 2007-11-30
Okay, this is a really long shot, but the forum gang here has come through many times for me in the past, so here goes...

I recently discovered that [Silencer](http://arsia-mons.com/), one of my all-time favorite games from the the late 90's, is back from the dead very unexpectedly. The game was effectively and unceremoniously killed back in 2001 when WON.net merged with Flipside.com, but the community has actually managed to resurrect it and there is now the possibility that the original developer will release the source code under an as yet unspecified open source licensing.

The discovery of Silencer got me thinking about other old WON.net gems that were lost when it folded. There was one particular game that I played constantly and I can't for the life of me remember what it was called. Basically, the game was a cross between chess and a card battle game. Playing pieces were represented by classical and Renaissance inspired artwork. The playing field was like a board game with hexagonal spaces and included different field types (grass, water, sand, etc.) that affected how effective your playing pieces were, depending on your elemental alignment. At the end of the game, the winner could claim playing pieces from the loser to build up their own "deck". I think the game had paid and free versions, with paid users getting some kind of bonus to their deck, but I'm not sure about that.

I realize that the descriptions sound very generic, but if anyone can think of a game that might be this one, let me know, I'd really like to look into whether it not it might still be available or capable of being resurrected like Silencer was.

EDIT - Almost forgot, I realize that these are not Linux native games, but Silencer runs perfectly in Wine and I'm hoping for the same kind of luck with this unknown game.

### Post by cogadh on 2007-12-01
Well, I managed to actually find the game after searching through way too many old blog and news articles about the WON.net shutdown. It is called [SpiritWars](http://www.spiritwars.com/) and while the game and developer do still exist (the game was updated to remove the WON dependency), it looks like almost no one is still playing. There were, however,  game tournaments as recently as this past July. 

A quick run through on Wine didn't work, but I didn't put too much effort into it. I could probably get it running if I really tried, but with no one to play against, I see no reason to bother. I might still contact the developer about open sourcing the game or releasing the source code in some way. If anything comes of it, I'll be sure to update the post.
