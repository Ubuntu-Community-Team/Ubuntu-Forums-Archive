### Post by Glawar on 2009-12-26
I've been searching these forums and the web for some time now, and had little luck...

Anyway, I prefer to play multiplayer games with friends or family physically in the same room/house. We like to play easy to learn, light-hearted, fun games...don't care much about graphics, as long as the game is fun.

Here's a list of games we like:
-BZFlag (simple graphics, easy to pick up how to play, many different flag strategies and a way to counter every strategy; we create our own maps and host over a network)
-Tetris (easy to learn, fun to compete against someone)
-Scorched 3D (we like the smaller weapons, as they call for more skill and thinking)
-Mario party games, Mario Kart, etc.

Note that all those games have great replayability, they're always different because the players have to keep trying different strategies, so you can play it over and over with the same friend and try to think through how to beat them.

Customization, such as creating maps, changing speed, and so on, is also a big plus for us.

But almost every open source game I've found is just not fun to us. OpenArena, Alien Arena, etc. seem to involve nothing but just pointing at the target and clicking a bunch. Not much thinking (or, at least, the thinking is embedded too deeply for someone to grasp in the hour I and a friend might have). I'm sure they're fun for people who like shoot-em ups, but we get bored with 'em.

 What is the game you've had the most fun with while physically beside someone?
