### Post by Naegling23 on 2007-10-17
it depends, if you want to tweak settings, rebuild pc's, download and install mods, customize controls, then go with the pc.  If you want to pop a disk in the drive and play from your couch without thinking about it, go with the xbox.

I find that each attracts a different sort of gamer.  PC games tend to be more in depth and involved, being played for hours straight, while consoles are more fun with a bunch of your friends playing madden for 30 minutes.

Decide what type of game you want to play, and what type of gamer you are.  Then pick the system that matches that.  Dont worry about cost, the pc is multifuntional, so upgrades benefit other things you do, but with the xbox, you dont have to worry about upgrading to play the latest games (although every few years, you have to buy a new xbox)
