No. DRM sucks, doesn't it? A few things do work this way, although it's still a PITA. Examples:
New Star Trek DVD, Collector's Edition contained a free iPod digital download. Of course you still can't play it on your computer without Quicktime or on other portable media devices.
Some commercial games like Doom, Unreal Tournament that are sold at retail for Windows, but the developer's release linux binaries.
Steam now offers games on Mac, and if you buy it once you can play it for no additional cost on either Mac or Windows. Still can't play them without Steam or on linux without wine.

I assume you are talking about games like Peggle or Plants vs. Zombies. I really don't know about the App store since I only have an iPod Classic.
