### Post by eragon100 on 2008-09-19
The battle for wesnoth is a nice turn-based strategy linux and windows-native game. It's not casual, but it has a fantasy theme and is relatively easy to get into, and you can play over a lan network. There is also a tutorial and there is no blood or anything. It requires almost zero hardware. All these things make it great for a family game, if your kids like orcs and elves and draconians and such. It's definitely one of my favorites games. Oh and it's free offcourse!

five in a row, chess, mahjong, blackjack and others are all included in a default ubuntu install, and have network capabilities. I strongly recommend The Battle for Wesnoth tough, it's really a great game!

### Post by sTpny on 2008-09-19
There are lots of games that can be played over your LAN, and even more that you can play together over the Internet. The repos are full of them, and most are very easy to get working. What I would do if I were you is just install a whole bunch of games, and then try them, one by one, to see if you like them and if you can get them working without too much trouble. If not, just unistall it and go on to the next one.  Do that, and you'll have a tonne of games you can play at home with your family in a relatively short time. There are also many games available that are not in the repos, and then there are Windows games, most of which can be run under wine. 

Another suggestion I have for you is to post your experiences with the process so that others can benefit from your experience, because I'm sure their are lots of people out there who would like to do exactly the same thing. Anyhow, good luck, and have fun.
