### Post by tommcd on 2008-12-29
From a linux compatibility standpoint the motherboard chipsets for the Intel Core Duos are better supported in linux than some of the newer chipsets for the Phenoms at present.

If you are in the USA consider buying your stuff from  [http://www.newegg.com/](http://www.newegg.com/). THey have good prices, great customer service, and their user reviews often mention linux compatibilty.

Here is a great website for linux hardware reviews:
[http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=home](http://www.phoronix.com/scan.php?page=home)
Phoronix has a forum also.

Although support for ATI video cards is getting better thanks to AMD making the drivers open source, I would still go for a Nvidia card at the present time. That is mostly just my preference though. Do a search for linux compatibility for any motherboard, video card, or sound card you are considering.
