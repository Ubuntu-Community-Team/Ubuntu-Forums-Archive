### Post by Isaacgallegos on 2012-02-28
Nazghul, running on the Haxima game engine, is the greatest RPG I've ever played. I'm trying very hard to find something better, because there are things that I really hate about Nazghul. However, nothing else seems to compare.
I keep removing this game, thinking, &#8220;that&#8217;s it, I&#8217;m never going to play this piece of crap ever again&#8221;. And about a month later, &#8220;Aww.. hell sudo apt-get install nazghul&#8221;. I get in the mood for some serious RPG battles and nothing else seems to fill the void like this crappy game. I end up reinstalling Nazghul.     I can sit here all day and think of things I hate about it. So allow me to vent for a minute before I mention it&#8217;s positive points. 
For starters, I can&#8217;t &#8220;full screen&#8221; this thing. It&#8217;s always in god damn windows mode! I hate that.  I mean, what programming game engine can&#8217;t do this? Any noob with a game tutorial book does this on day 2!
As you play, you can have many people join your party, but it makes the game play horrible, as you constantly have to go in and out of follow mode. The fighting is turned based, which I like, but everyone has a turn, and you can&#8217;t just specify what you want everyone to do in a single session and watch it all happen. You have to go back and forth between everyone&#8217;s&#8217; turn, yours and the enemies&#8217;. Many NPCs get several turns.  Let me be more clear. Think of what Zelda, the original ones, would be like if it were turn based. You might mistakenly imagine Final Fantasy, but that game conveniently organizes your entire parties&#8217; attack decision making in one session. Instead try to imagine a bunch of random little decision making sessions for each party member&#8217;s attack, with a top-down view.  I always end up dumping the NPC that joins me to simplify battles. 
Some of the caves are fking random with no hits on how to go in or out! Some people on the forums ask if these caves put you in an infinite loop, unable to except, unless you carry a teleportation gate. One of these caves are so near to where you start as a noob, you will undoubtedly loose your first character, if you accidently save while in it, which will happen! You&#8217;ll never make it out. You&#8217;ll not only be lost but you&#8217;ll be attacked in almost every room by all kinds of crazy creatures. Note: The fellowship of the ring had it easy. 
Spiders suck. Spiders cheat. Spiders are the reason I&#8217;m good at casting spells in this game. At first I wanted to play this game like Diablo, where you have the option to run around with a big axe and smash everything in sight. NOPE! Spiders are the one thing in this game that crushes that concept. They will always out number and paralyze you!  There are no immunities to this! Remember how this is a turn based game? You loose dozens of turns when your paralyzed while spiders suck your life points away. Spells work well against them.  
There needs to be more weapons!!!! There are more fking spells you can cast than there are weapons! This isn&#8217;t so bad, because you can hold two one-handed weapons at once, making new combinations of attack per turn, but only if they&#8217;re TWO DIFFERENT WEAPONS. WTF!?!
The dialogue mode, when talking to NPCs, is something I really like this game, but it can end up making you feel more like trying to guess a password, trying different words to gain access to a quest. You see, you have to type words that the NPC can reply to. If you say &#8220;job&#8221; the NPC will tell you his job and you might see some colored text, that are words that they NPC knows. Normally these key words are colored.  If the NPC doesn&#8217;t know the word, like &#8220;apple&#8221;, it will say something like, &#8220;I don&#8217;t know about that&#8221;, or something equally useless.  However, you get a letter from the Enchanter at the beginning with the highlighted word. That key word means nothing to the Enchanter. Neither is most of the other stuff you&#8217;ll say to him. You&#8217;ll have to literally try to make him say everything until he gives you a quest about a thief. Just say &#8220;thief&#8221; to him. Sheeeh. Other characters are just as bad. You can&#8217;t just type, &#8220;want&#8221;, and hear what the hell he wants from you. [/SIZE][/FONT]
The graphics are bad. Almost lazy. When you start you&#8217;ll see yourself carrying a shield and a sword. This has nothing to do with what weapons your holding. You&#8217;ll always see your character holding a sword and a shield because that&#8217;s the only sprite that represents you. It never changes. Note: I never hold a sword and a shield in the game. 
Now that I&#8217;m done venting here are good points. 
The game does everything else perfect! I don&#8217;t even know what my favorite feature is in the game. Everything from the NPC behavior through out each day, to the massive epic spells, to the ninja fighting style, and the landscape are awesome.
The NPCs have little lives. You can follow each one throughout its day and see where each works, hang s out, and goes to bed. The sun actually goes up and down , so you can buy from the shop keeper then take a rest in his bed, until he kicks you out when he comes home at night. Talk about an awkward situation. This even applies to monarch&#8217;s beds. 
Also, if you&#8217;re one those guys that likes to kill the good NPCs then this game allows you to pick a fight, which causes every NPC to come after you. In fact, in a castle/town you can end up fighting all the guards, the monarchs, wizards, bar keeper, Inn keeper, shop keepers, captains, and everyone else that lives there. Doing this is fun because you can get to see some of their abilities. I&#8217;d save before doing it though.
I like casting spells in this game much better than in others, like Diablo, because it&#8217;s faster switching between them. To me, it feels more correct, because you actually say the spell you&#8217;re going to use. Instead of the common RPG way, where you 1) select spell then 2) spam button with spell. Instead you just type out an acronym for the spell you want and it&#8217;s cast. I know a few, that I use a lot, and stick to those. Like &#8220;il&#8221; for &#8220;In Lor&#8221;, &#8220;ifg&#8221; for &#8220;In Flam Grav&#8221;, and &#8220;bp&#8221; for &#8220;Bet Por&#8221;. The spell I.B.X. referred before is an abbreviation of &#8220;In Bet Xen&#8221;, called &#8221;Insect Swarm&#8221;. You just type the letters &#8220;ibx&#8221; to call it. I also like having to mix the potions for the spells before being able to cast them. This means prepping before battle. I also wrote an Excel formula that tells me how much to buy of each magic ingredient, which is priced differently, while completely depleting all my gold. For example , if I have 1430 gold and each ingredient is 3G, 2G, and 5G and my personal inventory already has 10, 3, and 15 units of each ingredient needed, how many should I buy of each to completely zero-out my gold? My little spread sheet does this.  Once I&#8217;ve made my purchases, I can tell my character to make a few hundred units of a spell, ready for casting. 
Another thing I like about spells is having to know them. You pick a few you really like and practice them so you can draw them out quickly in battle. This is more fun than just equipping them like in Diablo. You really get to pick your magic fighting style. 
The environment is big! I spend most of my time just walking around between villages killing monsters and selling stuff to each town. When I&#8217;m into something more involved I might go underground, if I have a teleport spell to get back out.  For about 900 Gold you can buy a boat, which you can sleep in and travel around to different continents. The boat also becomes a battle ground when you&#8217;re attacked by sea monsters and pirates. I like the pirates because you get to keep their ship when you kill them off. I had many ships at once, which I used to make a bridge.
Each town is different. Some are big. Each seems to contain some hidden goodie. Others contain large hidden treasure filled rooms.
Well I&#8217;m tired of writing so I&#8217;ll end with this. Is there a game like this other than Nazghul Haxima? Thanks.
