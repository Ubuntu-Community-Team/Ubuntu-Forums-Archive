### Post by Greendogo on 2009-04-16
There are currently two engine reimplementations in the works for Morrowind.
First and my personal favorite is OpenMW:
[http://openmw.sourceforge.net/jaws/](http://openmw.sourceforge.net/jaws/)
Second is Crystal Scrolls:
[http://crystalscrolls.sourceforge.net/](http://crystalscrolls.sourceforge.net/)

They both are engine reimplementations, so when completed you'll still need the original game for the media content such as textures, sounds, animations, and models.  But they both are being designed with all three major OS's in mind (Linux, Win, and Mac).

I've tested out the working version (.6) of OpenMW and it it looks pretty good so far.

### Post by Greendogo on 2009-04-16
They don't collaborate because they are doing two entirely different things.  One engine will be a strict remake, while the other will add further functionality that the original didn't have.  As for the "Linux überrowind experience" (great name!), I doubt either of them would have progressed very far with a strict Linux approach.  Most of the Morrowind community is on Windows and so far you have to virtualize it to play on Mac or Linux.  So the first engine remakes will cover all three platforms and perhaps later a more specific reimplementation for Linux will be made.
