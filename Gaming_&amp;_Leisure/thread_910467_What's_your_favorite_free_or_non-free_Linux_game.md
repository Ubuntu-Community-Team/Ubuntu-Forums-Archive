### Post by Deathspawner on 2008-09-04
Hi all: 

Two years ago, I [posted here](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=254724) asking for your favorite Linux game, and the response was outstanding. Paired together with threads on other forums, an [article was published](http://techgage.com/article/top_10_free_linux_games/) that immediately received great attention. It was obvious that Linux users want to game, and that article to date has been one of our most popular. That was two-years-ago, though, so what's changed? Lots, I 'm sure, though I admit I don't game much on *any* platform, so I'm out of the loop. 

Since it's been two years since that article, I think it's about time we follow-up, and for that, I once again come here with a similar question. As it stands right now, what's your favorite Linux game, free or not free? Please feel free to list off more than one game, as I'll sort them appropriately. If you have a "favorite" out of your list, please list it first, as I'll assume it's your first choice. 

Unlike most 'top Linux games' lists, this one is chosen by actual fans of the games, not the writer of the article. I don't have much time to devote to gaming, so I couldn't make an accurate list if I wanted to. Hearing the results from hundreds of Linux gamers is a much more accurate method of putting together a top list. 

As a side question, we are also tossing around the idea of including Linux-based GPU benchmarks in our graphic card reviews. Is this something you'd personally be interested in, and are there games or benchmarks you'd be more interested in being included? It's no secret that Linux is lacking games that really push the hardware, but I could easily be overlooking some. The highest-end game I have on hand is ET: Quake Wars... is there anything better? 

Thanks in advance for your participation!
