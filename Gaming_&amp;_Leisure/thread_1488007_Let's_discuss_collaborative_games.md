### Post by Shazzner on 2010-05-19
I was playing Fallout 3 the other day; I had an issue with some of the gameplay so I checked online to one of the many mod sites for gameplay tweaks. I did so easily and along with that grabbed a few quest/storyline mods as well. They integrated seamlessly and added some content to a game the entirely depends on content.

Fallout 3, as with most Bethesda games, and other games rely on a creative community utilizing tools to add content and share this content with others. 

This got me thinking: with the community-centralism of Ubuntu and FOSS why we don't see more collaborative games?

With proprietary games users are limited to the tools released to the community, which are often limited or crippled compared to developer tools, and often need to reverse-engineer the game to get features beyond the tools released. In addition these mods often hold murky legal status and and are essentially owned by the tool-creators.

My point being that these barriers present an opportunity for the Ubuntu/FOSS community. We should work to create FOSS games along with tools to create content, have an easy way to get access to community-created content (perhaps part of the software center), and to upload and share user-created content.

Imagine a simple dungeon-crawler game that with a few clicks expands to quadruple the size in gameplay and content. I think that not only would this be a great entry point for the FOSS community, but I dare say a 'killer-app' for Ubuntu/GNU+Linux.

Any thoughts or opinions on this? Are there examples of this already out there?
