### Post by RIchard James13 on 2008-05-24
Writing a MMORPG is a very large effort. I'm pretty good at programming but I estimate the time taken for me to complete such a game would take the rest of my life, over 50 years. Of course by dropping features you can lower the length of time, even still it will take many years of development. It is not a short term project. 

A much better idea would be to help contribute to an existing MMORPG. That way you can build on the work of others. I currently play The Mana World and I must say that it could be a lot better. There are many ways to contribute to a game.
[LIST]
[*]Programming
[*]Writing
[*]Website maintenance/Wiki
[*]Graphics
[*]Sound
[*]Music
[*]Bug finding
[*]Map making world building
[/LIST]

If you go to The Mana World forums there is a job board and you can ask and find out more information there. Alternately there are several other MMORPG projects out there.

Here is a list of some 
[http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Mmorpg]("http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Mmorpg")
