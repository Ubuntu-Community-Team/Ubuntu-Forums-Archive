### Post by oscours on 2010-08-21
> **dfreer said:**
> What's the difference between this and xmoto?

Good question...

I think about these items :

[LIST]
[*]The rules of open trial are derivated from real motorcycle trial rules (no starberry to collect ;)) (Look this  ["What is motorcycle trial"]("http://www.rocler.qc.ca/ariane/What%20is%20Motorcycle%20Trials.htm") article for example)
[*]There is no time consideration in Open trial (like in old school motorcycle trial)
[*]With Open trial you can use the slow-motion mode to learn trial and improve your technics
[*]Open trial website has quite good [riding tutorials]("http://www.vincentvansuyt.com/ot_Tutoriels.php?strLang=EN") to learn trial technics
[*]X-moto is not a motorcycle trial game. It looks like cross or enduro motorcycle game.
[*]The Open trial game uses vintage and modern trial bikes, and many riders (men and women). You can combining motorbikes, riders and tracks
[*]Open trial use real travel suspensions, proportions, skidpan collisions, clutch, gearbox ratios, tire grips, etc... With XMoto, suspensions(?) have very bad behaviour, for example. It has no skidpan collision, no clutch, no gearbox...
[*]The riders in Open trial have realistic behaviours. They can uptaking and bending, it is not possible on XMoto. The rider look like a simple spring in Xmoto... :P
[*]XMoto is an open source project, Open trial is a commercial project (only the Open trial demo is free)
[/LIST]
Well, there is perhaps more ideas, I am not a Xmoto player, it is not enough realistic for me...

I think you haven't play with Open trial... look at the Open trial [riding tutorials]("http://www.vincentvansuyt.com/ot_Tutoriels.php?strLang=EN") and [videos]("http://www.vincentvansuyt.com/ot_Videos.php?strLang=EN"), if you haven't time to play with. ;)
