### Post by ender4 on 2010-09-09
There are several ways.  If you use Rythmbox you can just pop in the CD and select the CD on the left panel of Rhythmbox, then click the "Extract" button.

You can also open the CD and simply drag and drop the files in the directory you want them in.  I believe they are saved as WAV files, so you might want to encode them to mp3, ogg vorbis, or whatever format you prefer.

There are also several programs specifically made for ripping CDs, including Sound Juicer, Grip, k3b (for KDE), etc.  just install them from Ubuntu Software Center, Synaptic, or from the command line with apt-get.  You could do a search of CD ripping software with Ubuntu Software Center or Synaptic.

You can even rip from the command-line with cdparanoia.  Though this also leaves WAV files which may need to be incoded with lame or oggenc.
