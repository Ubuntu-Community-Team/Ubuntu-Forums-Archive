### Post by spencer the great on 2014-01-19
I don't know about Dungeon Defenders, but Dota2 is definitely a good co-op game, as it relies very much on good teamwork, something that often is not found in public matchmaking.  It also requires a bit of practice, and there's a short (or not-so-short) tutorial at the beginning that is compulsory for all new players (but not the whole tutorial, just the first few stages). You'll always be up with your friends against other players (or bots) if you join a matchmaking "party," or you can create a private lobby and go up against your friends (or co-op with them against bots, if you want). 

Also, as implied by this thread [http://forums.dungeondefenders2.com/showthread.php?110763](http://forums.dungeondefenders2.com/showthread.php?110763), DD is a fairly good co-op game.

Oh, Guns of Icarus Online is a good one!

I don't play many co-op games besides dota2, but there's a list of them here: [http://www.lgdb.org/category/game_modes/cooperative](http://www.lgdb.org/category/game_modes/cooperative)

And, if not satisified, Google (or the search engine of your choice) is also an excellent tool for finding new games!
