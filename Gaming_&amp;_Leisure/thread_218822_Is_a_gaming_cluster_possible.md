### Post by tribaal on 2006-07-19
The short answer: no.

The long one: While you can logically have more computer power by buying several computers and linking them together, the software you run on it *must* be specifically written to take advantage of the parrallel processing... That's where the gaming part fails.

The new graphic cards do parallelize work behing a common set of instructions, so basically... the distribution is taken care of by drivers and hardware. 

And then, to get good graphics performances you need lots of floating point calculation power, and most processors are much worse at that particular job than an average graphic accelerator...

All in all, you probably better stick to "regular" hardware...
