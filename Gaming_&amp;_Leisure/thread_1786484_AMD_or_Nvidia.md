### Post by handy on 2011-06-19
> **CreativeReach said:**
> AMD is slighlty more linux friendly, but Nvidia rocks.;)

AMD are enormously more Linux friendly. They have opened up their documentation on their GPUs & have also helped the open-source community by contributing code. It is primarily due to AMD that the development of the open-source driver stack for their GPUs has progressed so incredibly far & fast over the last 18 months or so.

Their closed-source driver stack has improved greatly as well & will continue to do so.

If I were building a machine to run the games that can be played under Linux I would go with AMD, due to their known & continued support of open-source, in combination with the fact that there really isn't a substantial difference between the output of the nVidia & the AMD GPUs.

What AMD don't have on Linux now, they will have before long.

***[edit:]** I use both AMD & nVidia GPUs, they both serve me well, the AMD product is the more recent of the two. I have to use the proprietary drivers with nVidia, & don't have to with the AMD GPU.*
