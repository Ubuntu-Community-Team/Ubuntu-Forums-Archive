### Post by shaggy999 on 2008-10-29
> **crazyfuturamanoob said:**
> What is DRM? :)

DRM stands for "Digital Rights Management". It's a catch-all phrase that relates to any kind of software that restricts how you can use a piece of media such as a song, movie, ebook, or a game.

Games for a long time have included software that forces you to keep the CD-ROM in the drive or forces you to dial home to a corporate server or something like that to make sure you aren't pirating it. There are a lot of reasons to not like DRM, one of which for me is it is difficult to get many titles with complex DRM to get working in WINE.

In short, No DRM = No Fuss.

Other examples of DRM include Macrovision, CSS, keycodes or cd checks could be considered DRM, anything that limits how you can copy a file, etc.
