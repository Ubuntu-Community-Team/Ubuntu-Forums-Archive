### Post by bg11 on 2006-11-26
I always, and I mean always, hear something along the lines of *one of the problems with linux is that there are no good games for it/I keep windows so I can play decent games/why can't there be good games for linux* as being a major critisism that people have about linux, even from seasoned users.

Emulators are the current solution to gaming on linux systems, I've dabbled with zsnes, mupen64 (N64) and epsxe.  There's even a PS2 emulator being developed.  It's amazing how well they work, even on mediocre computers.  Roms are readily available and for those of us with higher moral fibre, they can always buy the old gen console games for dirt cheap.  I managed to get 10 top playstation games (inc. GT, MGS, tekken 3, etc.) for about 15 gbp.

Using the keyboard can take some time to get used to, but reasonable and cheap usb joypads are easy to get hold of.

Consider the standard of games which are developed for consoles (not that I'm knocking the native linux games), I've got super mario, mario kart, final fantasy, chrono cross, resident evil 3, mortal kombat 3, silent hill, golden eye, zelda: OOT, perfect dark, all brilliant titles which should be more than enough for most people who want something to play on linux (hard-core gamers who must have the latest games would obviously not use linux for gaming).

So, isn't this the perfect answer to the regular, critical questions of the sort which are mentioned in the first paragraph?  Why aren't more linux people using emulators considering how far they've come?
