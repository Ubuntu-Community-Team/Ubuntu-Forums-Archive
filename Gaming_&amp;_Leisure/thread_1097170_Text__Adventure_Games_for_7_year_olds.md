there are these strange people who call themselves "interactive fiction' writers. they write text adventures, even in this day and age, though obviously they call them "interactive fiction" now. perhaps that's what you were referring to. it seems that they've recently managed to find a way to put graphics and sound in their games, though I've heard these are experimental features, and still considered pretty controversial. ^^ anyway, [ifarchive.org](ifarchive.org) seems to be the main lair of aforementioned individuals. it would take a few lifetimes to play all of the games stored therein. not all of this stuff would be suitable for a 7-year old, I'd imagine, because bad literature is bad for everyone. still, it's a safe bet to consider most of it more educational than your average another Super Mario clone.
