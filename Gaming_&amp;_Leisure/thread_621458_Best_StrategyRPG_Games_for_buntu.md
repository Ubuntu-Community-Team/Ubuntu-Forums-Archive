### Post by acoustibop on 2007-11-24
True; in fact, console emulators in general offer a great way to access a very wide range of games.  And there are now a fair number, like pSX Emulator, that will run very well and natively in Linux.

As far as launchers for pSX go, one of the pSX forum admins, Ultima, has written an excellent [Frontend for pSX]("http://psxemulator.proboards54.com/index.cgi?board=news&action=display&thread=1156715263").  Since it works by modifying pSX' configuration rather than only creating terminal commands with switches, it can set up virtually every cofiguration aspect you want to run your game.  Indispensable.

### Post by acoustibop on 2007-12-28
No.  It's still illegal to download Playstation game images (ROMs usually refers to cartridge rips rather than CD ones), stlcoptony, even if you own copies.  But if you have the games, you can rip them yourself - see the [HOW TO: Make Disc Images]("http://psxemulator.proboards54.com/index.cgi?board=rules&action=display&thread=1195746305") thread on the official pSX forum for advice on this.

BTW, talking of RPG games - the topic of this thread - check out [Eschalon: Book I]("http://basiliskgames.com/book1.htm").  It's a superb game, meant as a modern take on traditional RPGs like most of the Ultima series, Might & Magic etc.  There's a Linux version, and it runs beautifully on my Gutsy installation.

**Edit:** BTW Eschalon: Book I is not free; but at $27.95 US, it's not exactly expensive for such an excellent game, and there's a free demo that's better than a lot of full games I've played...
