### Post by Peacepunk on 2008-06-16
People looking forward to play with Digital Legos (Hey, that's cool, you don't have to tidy up afterwards!) should definitely tune to [www.ldraw.org:](www.ldraw.org:) you'll find there tons of links & docs.

So far on my Fedora7 I run LDD (with the same blacked out toolbar issue, never mind), mlcad (easier than leocad), under wine. ldview is your rendering software and runs natively under Linux; all ldraw library of parts are available for linux - and they have much more to offer than the standard LDD stuff. Your next task then is to build Nice Renderings of your virtual creations, and that can be done with povray, still *nix again, but through a very efficient CLI: results are superb!

[compare this:
[http://www.brickshelf.com/cgi-bin/gallery.cgi?i=2985823](http://www.brickshelf.com/cgi-bin/gallery.cgi?i=2985823)
with this:

You'll get an idea]

Enjoy!
