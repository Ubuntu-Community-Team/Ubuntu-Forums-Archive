### Post by theonlyalterego on 2007-06-22
Free:

[Runescape]("http://www.runescape.com") is a decent starter MMO, mediocore graphics at best but it can be fun for a bit, and there's both a free world and a pay-to-play. The p2p is only $5 so it's much cheaper than other p2p mmo's.

You can always kick up some dungeon dust with [Falcon's eye ]("http://users.tkk.fi/~jtpelto2/nethack.html")and play some old school nethack, no multiplayer though ;)

Pay-to-Play:

I used to play [DAOC ]("http://www.darkageofcamelot.com/")a lot, it's very similar to WOW though, level grinding and epic scaled adventures although I LOVE the PvP environment with the ability to seige castles and such, great fun :)

If I were going to recommend any MMO though, I'd have to say [D&D Online ]("www.ddo.com/")is my favorite. The main issue I had with DAOC and WOW is time. Both games have truely great quests, and adventures to play however they can either take 1) a ton of people to accomplish or 2) a REALLY long time. DDO (imo mind you) is much easier to pop in for an hour or so and do a quest, and then put it down with a feeling of accomplishment.

Just my thoughts.
