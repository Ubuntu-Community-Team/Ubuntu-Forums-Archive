### Post by breadbin on 2008-06-12
Yes there is, I had the exact same problem, just trying to remember how I solved it. I think you'll have to change your permissions on the hidden quake4 folder in your home folder. its called .quake4. If in nautilus, press ctrl+h to see the hidden folders. in a terminal type (assuming in your home folder)

```
sudo chmod -R 777 .quake4
```

I'm just guessing the -R part it means recursively change the contents of the folder too. I think its right. Should work now. Let me know if not.
 
Again the 777 is probably overkill and it'll probably work with 644 but someone else might be better able to tell you.
