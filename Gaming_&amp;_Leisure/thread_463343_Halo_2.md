### Post by hikaricore on 2007-06-03
Wine does not work that way yet.
There is a team working on DX10 functionality for wine, but it's going to be awhile bofore they even scratch the surface.

The fact that D3D is a closed source audio/rendering/etc platform means that all of the progress in WINE releating to D3D has been reverse engineered.  This is a long and complex process of trial and error.  If it gives you any indication of how long this can take, DirectX 8 and 9 are still only mildly implemented in WINE.
