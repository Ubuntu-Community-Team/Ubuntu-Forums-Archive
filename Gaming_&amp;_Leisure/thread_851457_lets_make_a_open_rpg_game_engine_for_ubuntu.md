### Post by MaximB on 2008-07-07
Ubuntu is a GNU/Linux distro.
Saying that "let's make X for Ubuntu" implies that :
A. You are a noob.
B. You are a little kid.

First you need to search existing projects , google it, search in sourceforge and freshmeat.
Then if you found something you might want to join the developing team.
Starting a project from scratch is hard.
If you think you are good enough to make something and not just saying "let's make it" and wait for them to make it then just go with it, start it and people will (hopefully) join you.

You can also play "my" role and knowing that you can't program gather a team, but that is very hard.
I've done that but "I" also "gave" my team some stuff to work with and promises for the future (from the original developers).

Just don't say "lets do it" and wait for them to do it for you without you giving anything or being involved with it.
