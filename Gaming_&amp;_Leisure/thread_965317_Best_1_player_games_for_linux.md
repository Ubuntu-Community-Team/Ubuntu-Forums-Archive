I agree about Doom 3 and Quake 4. If you're not into horror games, skip Doom 3 and play Quake 4, since it's got a great action campaign. ### Post by noerrorsfound on 2008-11-05
> **Tux0r said:**
> they work with the windows retail versions.
But the Linux installers aren't included on the CDs. You have to download them separately:
[ftp://ftp.idsoftware.com/idstuff/doom3/linux/doom3-linux-1.3.1.1304.x86.run](ftp://ftp.idsoftware.com/idstuff/doom3/linux/doom3-linux-1.3.1.1304.x86.run)
[ftp://ftp.idsoftware.com/idstuff/quake4/linux/quake4-linux-1.4.2.x86.run](ftp://ftp.idsoftware.com/idstuff/quake4/linux/quake4-linux-1.4.2.x86.run)

After running the installer for a game, you have to copy the **pk4** files from the CD to where you installed the game (I recommend you install it somewhere in your home folder).

### Post by Senuf on 2008-11-07
Hello.

I don't really enjoy those games with LOTS of controls, where you change weapons, load ammunitions, get health packs, etc. For me, an excess of controls, of keys to press and remember, etc., conspires against my joy of gaming. It's a matter of styles, and that's my way to enjoy games.

I'd rather play those games with very few basic rules and as few as possible controls.

That said, it's easy to deduce that I like Snood / Frozen Bubble.

And GWeled.

Sokoban is OK too.

As is Mah Jongg (Shanghai, precisely).

And I like Poker TH (alone against the machine or online multiplayer).

And I also loved Arkanoid (on the real arcade machines), quite some time ago.

And I would like FooBilliard more if it worked OK on my computer.

----------------------------------------------

Now, the **best games** that I enjoyed on a computer were Mac-Only:

**Jump Zampoli**, with a visual design that paid LOTS of attention to the art, where even the different Colour Schemes were obviously designed by ppl. who know about visual arts, and the game was VERY funny. Addictive, even.

The other one is **Barrack**, made by Ambrosia ([customer comments]("http://www.ambrosiasw.com/games/barrack/reviews.html")).

The rest of the games I enjoyed a lot are (most of them), perhaps not strangely, by the same company (I have no affiliation to them). Bubble Trouble, for example, is lovely.
