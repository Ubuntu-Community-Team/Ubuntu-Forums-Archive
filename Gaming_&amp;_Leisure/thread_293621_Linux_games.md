### Post by blendmaster on 2006-11-05
For gaming, it won't have as great of support as Windows did.

Remember, it's not the fault of Ubuntu (or even the developers), it's just that the people creating games see more of a market towards Windows users, rather than Linux or Mac users, and so they don't publish games for Linux.

There are games made natively for Linux, and [this site]("http://gaming.gwos.org/") is a pretty good resource for them.

There are also ways to get some games made for Windows to work on Linux. One program is Cedega, and it is made just for this. [this site]("http://transgaming.org/gamesdb/") has a good list of the games it supports (that's Cedega's site too). Remember though, Cedega does cost money.

Wine is the last alternative, and [right here]("http://appdb.winehq.org/") shows a good list of games it supports (that's also Wine's site).

Remember, Ubuntu is more of an alternative to Windows, not a replacement, and so you shouldn't expect it to work like one.

However, Ubuntu is a great OS, and given the chance, I'm sure you'll come to love it more than Windows!
