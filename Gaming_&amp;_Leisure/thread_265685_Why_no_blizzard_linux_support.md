### Post by leech on 2006-09-27
I'll try to answer this as nicely as possible, even though I consider Blizzard/Vivendi the biggest <insert expletive here>.

Bnetd has already been mentioned, but in brief, it was an open source project to create a battle.net server so that anyone could run one.  The project ended when Blizzard sent a cease and desist letter.  Since the project didn't have the funds for any sort of law suit, they bent down and stopped the development.

FreeCraft.  A project to make a RTS engine, that happened to also be compatible with WarCraft 1 and 2 data files.  Also a Cease and Desist letter was sent to them, stating that *Craft was a registered trademark and they couldn't use the name.  Kind of dumb considering a lot of people were buying WarCraft 2 out of bargain bins for FreeCraft, generating money for Blizzard on an obsoleted product.  FreeCraft is now called Stratagus.

World of Warcraft.  This was the icing on the cake.  Apparently, during development, there was a Linux native client.  It was dropped during the beta cycle unfortunately.

It isn't a "Blizzard only loves to program DirectX" considering the first game they created that even used any Direct3D stuff was Diablo 2 which only used it for some transparency and lighting.  Then WarCraft3, which pretty much had a very pointless 3D.  Then World of Warcraft, which has both OpenGL and Direct3D pipelines.  The fact that there is no native Linux client is only because of either Blizzard or the publisher, Vivendi Universal.  It's not a technical reason.  

Also for those saying they could have an 'unsupported' client.  That doesn't work for an MMORPG, especially since if I'm paying 15 a month for a game, I'd better have full support for it.  What happens if they release a patch that breaks the linux client, but it works still in Windows.  Then the users will say, "Hey, I can't play.  Fix it?"  "I'm sorry, you're playing on an unsupported platform, thank you for your money though."

Ultima Online had an unsupported client for a while, unfortunately it stopped being updated, so all the Linux users were left behind (the guy who was doing the porting had left Origin systems.)

These are the reasons why I refuse to play WoW and any other Blizzard game.
