### Post by Prefix100 on 2008-07-11
[Unknown Worlds]("http://unknownworlds.com/"), a company based in San Fransisco, are the creators of the best half-life 1 mod, Natural Selection.

Natural Selection (to be refereed to as NS from here) is a fantastic team-based RTS/FPS hybrid. Its similar to Tremulous, but actually really, ***really*** good. NS is really quite an old game now, but it is still active, with a league still running for Europe - The [ENSL]("http://ensl.zanith.nl/").

Unknown Worlds are in the middle of creating a sequel to NS, NS2. It is an extremely promising project, and if you want to know more, just hit up their blog, [here]("http://unknownworlds.com/ns2"). Or the NS2 feature list [here]("http://www.unknownworlds.com/ns2/about/"). They are implementing a lot of exciting game elements such as ['dynamic infestation']("http://www.unknownworlds.com/ns2/2006/12/dynamic_infestation"), exciting game physics, advanced lifeforms, etc.

Unknown Worlds have recently just informed their community that they have decided to switch engines, from Source, to their own in-house engine. Now they haven't revealed whether they are using OpenGL or DirectX, but in the short time of releasing this news, 4 people have already asked for a native Linux version.

I think it would be a serious achievement if we were to get this fantastic game on Linux.

If are interested, hit up [this thread]("http://www.unknownworlds.com/ns2/forums/index.php?showtopic=104522"), and post that you would buy a Linux version.

Cheers!
