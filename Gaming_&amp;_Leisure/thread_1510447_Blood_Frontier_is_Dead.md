### Post by HoKaze on 2010-06-15
Yeah, I started playing about a month ago and then found one day that the servers were offline. I investigated, found out about the creators disagreements with the community, the fork Red Eclipse and how the original Blood Frontier died.
Unfortunately last I checked nobody has builds of Red Eclipse for any OS/architecture yet and my attempts at compiling via the svn resulted in a few lost hours and no game. It was a while ago so I can't remember my exact issues.

It was a great game and showed us that the Cube 2 engine could be taken in different directions while remaining an FPS and it's one of the few non-Quake/Doom style multiplayer FPS games I've enjoyed. Their weapon, physics and general gameplay choices were interesting and felt like an unusual hybrid of FPS types. It's more "realistic" than Sauerbraten or Quake 3 but it's very unrealistic at the same time, managing some weird balance of realism and unrealism that makes it interesting. The weapon balances and alternate fire (as well as grenades actually be useful and powerful unlike grenade launchers of most quake-like games) combined with varied game modes, mutators/multipliers and easily editable physics made it a pretty good game.
I probably still prefer more traditional Doom or Quake style games and the art was a bit lacking (as was the size of the community compared to say, Sauerbraten, but that's understandable) but overall it was a good sign of what open source can do and that gaming on Linux is an option.
