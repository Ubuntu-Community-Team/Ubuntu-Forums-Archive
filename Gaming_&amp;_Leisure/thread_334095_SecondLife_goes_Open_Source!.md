### Post by Toxicity999 on 2007-01-08
I kid you not folks, this sort of came out of no where but I'm not displeased, I myself don't play the game. I do however hear good things. What could this mean for us you ask? Well, TONS!

Mark was actually talking about it in a blog post not so long ago (Reference: [http://www.markshuttleworth.com/archives/72](http://www.markshuttleworth.com/archives/72) )

"Second Life of course brings a new twist to the idea of immersion, though for now it&#8217;s immersion on the virtual side of the looking glass. What interests me are the ways in which there is cross-over between the virtual world and the real world. When I&#8217;m walking around town, does my mobile phone alert me to changes in the virtual world? And when I&#8217;m working at my PC, how much can I stay focused on work, say, while my PC also keeps me abreast of what&#8217;s going on with my avatar?"

Now to me it sounds like he's saying something like whats the next step after second life? Well here we now have second life, Let's make that next step.

They recently released a linux native client from what I had heard which was interesting but it seems like all the more people friendly MMOGs do that these days (in general, hear me out.)

This however stepped to the line of friend and myself I would like to see the Ubuntu community manipulate this code, Official Free Ubuntu MMOG anyone? And in that respect we could also easily integrate it well with programs for watching things happening in game, And I'm not sure on what exactly Second life currently handles, but things liek weather, friends online status, etc.

I see a virtual torrent of possibility, we need to jump on it!

[https://wiki.secondlife.com/wiki/Get_source_and_compile](https://wiki.secondlife.com/wiki/Get_source_and_compile)

This could theoretically accomplish exactly what mark was talking about, only now we have a large stepping stone. Make it our own!

Edit: I'd also like to thank Price for just reminding me that only the Client was opensourced, however, this is a small hurdle, MANY games that are closed source have server backends replicated, we have here the client source, recreating a server is a small task.
