### Post by wererabbit on 2006-11-02
I bought an Xbox 360 and I couldn't be happier.  It runs great, Xbox Live lets me chat with my friends equivalent to phone-line quality, most of the hot PC games are coming out for 360 and usually look stunningly good or better than a $2000 new machine.
Plus, it's stable, I never have to configure it unless I really want to, and I just pop the dvd in and it goes.  With the VGA cable, it runs on my Viewsonic 20" widescreen at not-quite-native but good enough resolution - 1366x960 or something like that.  I can play many of my old Xbox games on it too.

I evaluated how much it was going to cost me to upgrade my PC system to play the latest games in Windows at the 1680x1050 resolution that I wanted.  Oblivion was my main target game for cutting edge graphics.  

It would cost me about $70 for a new Epox pci-e SLI board with my existing cpu and ram.  Then I'd eventually want to upgrade to 2 gigs of ram, and there are no 754 socket motherboards that have 4 ram slots on the market (at least that I could find).  Then factor in around $600 - $1000 for dual or quad SLI Nvidia cards.  These would be old news by mid-December with the new 8800 series GPU coming out.  Plus to take true advantage of this power, I'd have to run Windows and I've lost my cd, so that's another $150 from newegg.  Plus, I'd need a new power supply - Antec Neo for $90.  Oh yeah - I'm still on ATA100 - I'd want a SATA 3G 250gb for $75. 

Or I could go for a Crossfire board and use a new Intel processor for around $100-150.  New cpu & motherboard would cost me around $300-$400.  New ram - 2 gigs - would be around $225.  A couple of X1950XTX cards in tandem is around $900.  Windows - $150 Power Supply - $90.  SATA 250gb $75.

It's only $400 for a fully equipped 360 with a wireless controller.  It's $50 for 1 year of Xbox Live.  It's $40 for a vga cable for 360. My usb keyboard plugs right into it and works, so I don't have to thumbstick-type.  Yeah, the games are pricey, but you can rent them and the prices are always falling on them.
Oh yeah, and Oblivion runs better on it than on nearly all non-cutting edge PCs.  

If you don't want an Xbox360, look at the PS3.  Personally, I'll stick with my 360 and I'll get a Wii in a couple of months.  I keep my pc around for WoW/Guild Wars and CS:S.   I use my mac for everything else.  I'll probably ultimately get a mac mini when it gets a beefier videocard, and boot it with windows and ubuntu partitions  if possible.  Then I'll ditch my pc and my big G4 tower and live in small form-factor blissfulness.
-w :D
