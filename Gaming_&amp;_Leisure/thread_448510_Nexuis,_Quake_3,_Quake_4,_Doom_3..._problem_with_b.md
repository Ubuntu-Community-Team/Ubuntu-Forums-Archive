### Post by Naegling23 on 2007-05-31
I'll chime in with how I handle beryl and gaming:

Beryl hurts the framerates in some games, ie frets on fire.  And it also does not go fullscreen on wine games, and mythtv.  Therefore, for me, beryl is not really an option when I'm playing.....WoW results in having the bottom half inch cut off from the screen.  But I'm a big fan of beryl, and since I'm not the only person using my computer, I didnt want to manually toggle beryl on and off everytime I launch a game...I can remember to do it, but someone else wouldnt have a clue.  My solution was to put a small script to turn beryl off and launch the game, when the game exits, it turns berl back on.  It makes everything seemless.  Of course beryl is off when I'm playing the game, but I really really dont recommend gaming with beryl on, it destroys framerates, and introduces a whole host of problems.

Its just another alternative, I dont think its exactly what your looking for, but its another option.
