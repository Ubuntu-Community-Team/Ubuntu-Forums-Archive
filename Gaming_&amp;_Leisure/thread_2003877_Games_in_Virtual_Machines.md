### Post by QIII on 2012-06-14
Each will do some things well, others not so well.

No matter the virtualization solution, the guest OS will be operating on the hardware abstraction layer and will generally be less capable than if it were installed on bare metal.

You may experiment with them all.  You may find that you want to run one for one thing, another for another.  The problem you would have there would be licensing of your OS, since each would be considered a separate machine.

Also, check your license.  Many Windows OEM disks, for instance, specifically proscribe installation in virtual machines.
