### Post by readingboy on 2006-01-01
Here's what I play with: 

For first person shooters, you can purchase Doom3 from idsoftware then use their linux installer [http://zerowing.idsoftware.com/linux/doom/](http://zerowing.idsoftware.com/linux/doom/). This is my current addiction since I didn't buy it when it first came out and have only been playing for the past several weeks. If Doom3 is already old news for you, Quake 4 just came out and has a linux installer on idgames as well ([http://zerowing.idsoftware.com/linux/quake4/](http://zerowing.idsoftware.com/linux/quake4/)). This is almost surely to be my next game. 

Armagetron is an excellent version of the lightcycle game from Tron, which you can find in your apt-get repositories (after you enable the universe repositories). 

Freeciv might not be as beautiful as Civ3 or Civ4, but it's a fun and challenging game, and you can find that in the universe repositories. 

Soul Ride is a fun snowboarding game using OpenGL, and you can find that at TuxGames [http://www.tuxgames.com](http://www.tuxgames.com)

Other games that will work under Linux (perhaps after some configuration/installation fussing) are: Medal of Honour Allied Assault (and the expansion pack Spearhead), Unreal Tournament 2004, Neverwinter Nights (and its expansion packs). 

Robin Hood, Legend of Sherwood is also very good strategy-like game that takes a little while to master, but is completely fun.  

If you're not sure how your machine will handle some of these games or what they're like to play, I'd suggest going to [http://www.tuxgames.com](http://www.tuxgames.com), downloading and installing the demo, trying the game out, then ordering what you like best. 

I'm sure I'm missing a ton of excellent games, but I'm recommending only the ones I've tried and enjoyed. One place I check for info on games is [http://happypenguin.org/](http://happypenguin.org/).

Have fun!
