### Post by Galilei on 2010-04-23
Why, oh why are there no games for Linux?

Common question, isn't it? There are some whom are quick to point out that this is not actually true. While I somewhat agree, it's painfully obvious that Linux lacks great mainstream games. 

Whys is this, then? What could and should be done about it? These are old question, for sure, and most likely will be asked sometime in the future again...unless something is done to remedy the situation. 

Are there other Linux users that feel this way? Are there users that have this crazy idea, that they would like to change this predicament Linux users have been facing for years? Are there any people around these forums that wish they could somehow contribute to the Linux gaming scene in some profound way, but haven't been able to do so, because it's just too difficult to get in touch with the right kind of people? 

I know I'm one of those people and it sucks! It sucks because I seriously feel there's very much that could be done about all of this. I'm a guy who loves games and the game industry. I buy games even though I know I won't have time to play them all through. It's like I'm collecting them just for the sake of having them. Ever since I've been eight -years old I've had a console of some kind in my house. Games were grand experiences for me and ever since my teenage years I've wanted to make one. I want to get a hold people that are like minded. I want to get a hold people that want to make games. People that not only have the passion for this, but people that have some skill in doing so.

The biggest issue with small-time game development is the lack of talented manpower and lack of resources. Those are the biggest issues I can think of. I think most attempts for making a title die, when developers try to do too much with too little. That's why, at least for now, I'll be focusing on making 2D games. The reasons for this are numerous, but to put it simple: 2d games are essentially just as enjoyable (if not more) than 3d games. making awesome 2d games just require a fraction of the resources that a full blown 3d game does. I'll give you some examples:

Advance Wars DS
Ogre Battle (1, 2, and 64)
Cave Story
Megaman 9 and 10
New Super Mario Bros. Wii
Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past
World of Goo

These are just a few examples and I bet you know a few that could be put on this list just as well. As I mentioned before, I'm on a journey to develop a game, and not just any game; I'm so going to make a commercial game. I want and need help.

Think about those games I've mentioned. What kind of resources would it require to build one? Damn, not that much. A lot of work, for sure, but nothing that can't be done with software handily available to any Linux enthusiast!

I can do graphics, animation, and music. I also would like to think that I know what constitutes a good game. I need people to help me though. These kinds of projects are hardly ever done on your own. Besides, this is awesome work! Share the greatness, says I! 

I've been working on a game for a while now and I've managed to build tilesets, game characters, HUD graphics etc. in HD -resolution. What I need is a game engine to take all this content and make it work in a way that kicks *** (just saw the movie, by the way! It was awesome...and no pun intended). I would appreciate help in all aspects of development (sound, graphics, animation, menu design), but programming is the most crucial aspect. 

There's so much more I'd like to talk about, but for now i would just prefer to know if there are people here that would like to get down and dirty in game development. I'm talking about a serious project here that I intend to make commercial as soon as there's something to show to investors. You can PM me or write mail. It's so late that I'm going to hit the sack. Ha det så bra! 

Oh yeah, if anyone gets interested I'll post some of the things I've done so far. Good night...
