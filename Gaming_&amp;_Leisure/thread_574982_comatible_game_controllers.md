### Post by DoktorSeven on 2007-10-13
Pretty much any USB controller should work just fine, since they use the USB HID (Human Interface Device) driver and generally everything is detected and works as it should.

I have seen a few issues on here about getting certain gamepads' d-pad to work properly, and I'm not sure if this is really a configuration issue or a real incompatibility.  Just search for the kind you want to get (both here and Google, adding the keyword "linux" on the Google search) and see if anyone's reported any issues.

### Post by acoustibop on 2007-10-13
FWIW my Logitech Rumblepad 2 works perfectly in Edgy, Feisty and Gutsy - and is an excellent pad, besides.  However, for NES and SNES emulators, as DoktorSeven says, the gamepad is not particularly critical and most USB pads should work Ok as long as the OS picks them up.  I have a couple of cheap no-name controllers that work fine 'out of the box' in Ubuntu.  I also have an old gameport Gravis Gamepad Pro that I did have issues installing, but that works fine now, as well.

For more advanced emulators, such as N64 or Playstation ones, the controller is more critical.
