### Post by Sealbhach on 2010-01-15
These don't come with the standard game and the development is still not complete yet. 

For Linux you need to patch the source and compile  but it's worth it because the work so far is beautiful!

Here's some screenshots from in-game:

[[IMG]http://imgur.com/3SNL1s.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/3SNL1.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/Kvx4Hs.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/Kvx4H.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/HjoNIs.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/HjoNI.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/q5zoCs.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/q5zoC.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/i3Kbls.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/i3Kbl.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/GpMpDs.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/GpMpD.jpg")[[IMG]http://imgur.com/VbHQzs.jpg[/IMG]]("http://imgur.com/VbHQz.jpg")

You may notice that not all of the sprites have been completed yet, so some of them will look pixelated when you zoom in close.

I compiled it for Jaunty 9.04 using the instructions from the OpenTTD Wiki - I'm not sure if you  will need to do anything extra for Karmic. 

I've written up a how-to, although the OpenTTD Wiki is pretty good, but there's a few different things you need to download as well, so I put the whole thing on one page in the hope of making it simpler. [Link to my how-to]("http://faolchu.wordpress.com/2010/01/15/how-to-compile-openttd-in-ubuntu-to-get-the-nice-new-32bit-graphics/").

I think when the 32bit graphics are completed it will bring OpenTTD to a whole other level.
