### Post by lightstream on 2011-06-05
Yes you've obviously put some time into that, and as well as running 3D benchmarks you've collected stats for a good number of Linux games - hey, a good excuse for playing a few rounds of each game ;)

What I liked about the article is that it's comprehensive, starting with a fairly complete overview of what the card offers. I was surprised how much stuff they cram onto a video card these days!

Initially I was a little put off by the number of pages (25!), however they are pretty short pages, and the last 15 or so are charts showing the results of your performance tests, so that initial impression was unjustified. However for a lot of people it might just be a case of TL;DR on seeing that page count!

The results are kind of interesting - they could've been made much more interesting if you'd commented about why the 3 cards might be performing differently, or even if you were just to say how much the results you obtained were in line with your expectations. 

In particular, how come sometimes the 4850 performs way better than the others, but other times the opposite is true?

This is confused by the fact that some chart labels are inconsistent, and instead of having the 6950 at the top, the 4890 is, 

A little info about the 4895 would also be useful, to help understand what benefits we should expect. Why did you choose this card to compare with, what is better about them etc

Now right at the end, you mention the difficulties you encountered getting all the cards running with all the tests - there was no mention of this previously, nor of the steps you took to sort it all out. I think perhaps the article would have benefited from that.

OK now a couple of things about using the web site itself - paging is difficult, especially when you have a lot of pages like that! The problem is with that long list of page numbers is it doesn't show you which page you're currently on.

Normally the current page would not be a clickable link, and would be styled differently (eg in bold or without underline - see this website for example).

Second thing about the website relates to the Flash charts you use for the performance results. I didn't realise these were animated initially, because when clicking a new page link, the chart was often off the bottom of my screen, and the animation had finished by the time I scrolled down. Ideally these animations should either loop, or have a 'Play' button that starts the animation.

So anyway you asked for feedback, you better not just never come back to this blummen post!!
