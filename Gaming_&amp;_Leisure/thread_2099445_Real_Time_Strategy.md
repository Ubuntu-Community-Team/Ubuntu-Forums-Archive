### Post by oldrocker99 on 2012-12-29
While the Ubuntu repos have FPS after FPS, there are precious few RTS titles. Besides 0 A.D., there's 7KAA (Seven Kingdons Ancient Adversaries), an absolute gem of game design by Trevor Chan, which he released to open source a couple years ago. The manual is 182 pages, but is only available if you purchase the game from GOG.com for $5.99, and I personally find the price worth it just for the manual; this game has pretty primitive graphics, but the game design is DEEP.

Globulation is another one, just for its unique gameplay.

One of the most fully-realized (imho) RTS in the repos is Warzone 2100, a late-90s game that never found its audience, but is pretty damned good, with upgrade after upgrade available, custom-designed vehicles, and lots more. It's been in development since Eidos made it open source; graphics have improved, etc.

Widelands is a clone of the early Settlers games.

Megaglest is closest to a Warcraft-styled game, with mages versus melee. Nice-looking 3D, too.

There are a couple more as well. Just Search in Synaptic for "real time strategy" and you'll find some. 

Of course, a lot of the better Windows RTS games are quite playable under Wine. PlayOnLinux is probably the easiest way to configure Windows games, or you can purchase CrossOver, which is how the Wine developers keep being supported.

Hope this helps.):P
