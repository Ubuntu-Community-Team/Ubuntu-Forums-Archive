### Post by Julind on 2015-07-24
Gaming in Ubuntu is possible. Some games are even designed to run on Linux OS's. But for the most part you can use [Wine]("https://www.winehq.org/"). I won't guarantee you that the games will run as perfectly as on windows but it will surely run. Wine also offers something called **Ranking** which helps determine what games can be played with less configuration and a bigger chance of them working out of the box. The only problem with it is the performance hit (unless your PC has some good specs) and DirectX Only games don't work. You can use OpenGL though. Plus there is also Steam for Ubuntu. Tl;dr - you can game in Ubuntu, just not the same experience as in Windows.

### Post by mystics on 2015-07-24
> **joseph66 said:**
> I have been so mentally brow-beaten on other issues that I would honestly for once in my life like something to just be given to me without requiring that I rearrange my brain to understand it.

If you want to keep things relatively familiar, you may want to look into different Ubuntu flavors than the main one that uses the Unity desktop. I, personally, think it is very easy to learn, but it is also not very Windows-like in its design. [Xubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/") is more similar to Windows, and the same could also be said of [Kubuntu]("http://www.kubuntu.org/"), but it is relatively difficult to get into compared to Xubuntu, at least from my (admittedly brief) experience with it. Either way, you'll still basically be using Ubuntu just in a likely more familiar environment.

Regardless of what you choose, though, be prepared: Linux is not Windows. You'll need to have the will to at least learn a few new things.

> So all that being the long short of my story I have seen that most people seem to like Ubuntu and that there is some functional gaming occurring on this OS.

Just to warn you, not every game is supported on Linux. There are plenty of games that get Linux support, and Wine has drastically expanded the list. However, there will still be plenty of games that aren't supported. If you're unsure about a game that doesn't have a Linux port, be sure to look it up on the [WineHQ database]("https://appdb.winehq.org/") to see how it functions with Wine.

And one last tip on gaming: Get PlayOnLinux. It's free and makes the whole Wine experience a lot easier.
