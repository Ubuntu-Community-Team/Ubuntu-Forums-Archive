### Post by ncweber on 2011-06-29
There are a number of games available in the Software Center that work pretty decent on a netbook.  Supertuxkart is a prime example.  Freedroid RPG is another nice one.  For RTS action, Warzone 2100 plays quite well on a Linux netbook.  As do Battle for Wesnoth and MegaGlest.

For a native Linux MMORPG, check out Ryzom at [http://www.ryzom.com](http://www.ryzom.com) It is a "free to play" RPG. If you select Download from the left-pane menu and click the first Download button next to Direct (BeadWindow), it will automatically send you to SourceForge to download their Linux client.  Keep in mind that it's a 1.4GB 7z file.  Register for an account at Ryzom.com and set your graphics options to their lowest settings in game, and it plays reasonably well.

You can also try Spiral Knights, a Java based MMORPG. [http://www.spiralknights.com](http://www.spiralknights.com) Because it's written completely in Java, it works great in Linux, and the resources are quite acceptable for a netbook.

Hope this is enough to whet your gaming appetite.
