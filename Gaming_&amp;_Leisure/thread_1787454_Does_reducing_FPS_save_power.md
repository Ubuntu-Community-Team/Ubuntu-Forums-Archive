### Post by holastickboy on 2011-06-22
Hmmm a difficult question eh.  Technically, your graphics cards power consumption will vary based on the work that it does.  Technically, I suppose that in circumstances where an application hardly uses a graphics card, that will be at its lowest point of power consumption.  Gaming and gpu based transcoding will require more of the video card, and it will then draw more power as a response.

That being said, I don't know if artificially limiting your fps or refresh rate will make much of an improvement.  However, if you are using a laptop, you could turn off the 3D "eyecandy" effects to reduce the time spent rendering unnecessary effects, and increasing your battery life.
