### Post by Naegling23 on 2009-01-12
I've been trying out a few worlds, but I havnt played enough to really get to know them yet.

I just sorted by PW-action, filtered out any password games, and joined the games with the most players.

So far (and, I might have spelled some things wrong, sorry)
-Kharlindale realms - good community, a little small, and very short on quests, at least starting out.  So far, it was the easiest to jump on and play, but I have had to grind in a very small area, having to rest constantly, taking the fun out of it.  Im at level 4 and have to keep trudging through the same map and waiting for the few low level guys to respawn.  
-gondor vs. mordor - LOTR based game, seems neat, but I havnt really gotten into it.  I found two newbie starter quests, but I died almost instantly when trying them.  I got frustrated and stopped playing, but it does look promising
-the three towns - couldnt find any quest, instantly levels you to 4, which bothers me for some reason.  Hard to get into.  Looks like it has a lot of promise though.
-neversummer nights 4 - long introduction, lots going on, lots of customization, ie changes from the original game, complicated story that I dont understand at all.  Basically, its very hard to just jump in and play.  However, on the plus side, the starter area is huge!  No "quests" but there are "jobs" that seem to work the same way.  The game also seems pretty active, and appears to have a huge world.  I think im going to spend my time here for the time being and see if I can get more involved in the story, and figure out some of the gameplay changes.  So far, I have gotten the most help in game on this one too, which counts for a lot, so, I guess they are less RP focused if they are willing to help me out.

Hope this helps a little bit.  I think ill play around in neversummer nights for a bit, and try out the gondor vs. mordor, that one sound interesting once I can get myself started on it.
