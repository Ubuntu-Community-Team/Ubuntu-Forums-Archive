### Post by LillyDragon on 2012-11-11
Can we also plug non-commercial/freeware games too? =P

- Lots of Quake III launched directly with OpenArena's 8.8 native linux binary as a mod, and of course, some OpenaArena to go with that. XD

- Urban Terror for epic online shooting matches mixed with a little parkour, and fun hats. =P

- Freeciv for classic CivII RTS play.

- Sonic Robo Blast 2. Dumb name, but it has great third person platforming and smooth analog controls, great presentation, epic soundtrack, a ton of user-made content, and visually easy on the eyes for something built on top of the Doom engine! [Yes, it has a PPA of a native Linux port for Ubuntu!]("http://mb.srb2.org/showthread.php?t=30779")

- Linux-native versions of all my favorite emulators, and they even run better than their Windows counterparts to beat it all! It's great not having to rely on WINE for classic games anymore. Even Mupen64 and PCSX run nicely on my 64-bit machine, whereas the former stuttered constantly on the 32-bit edition of Ubuntu, for whatever reason.
