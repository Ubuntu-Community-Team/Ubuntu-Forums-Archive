### Post by isaacj87 on 2012-09-19
I bought laptop when it was on sale at Best Buy: [http://arstechnica.com/civis/viewtopic.php?p=21883533]("http://arstechnica.com/civis/viewtopic.php?p=21883533")

The laptop contains an AMD Vision APU. The A4-3300M, to be exact, and I find it to be very pleasant. I should note that I upgraded the stock 4GB up to 8GB.

So far, I have been genuinely impressed with both the CPU and GPU prowess. (If you consider the cost). When I'm booted in Windows, I can easily play TF2 on fairly high settings (I think I have AA disabled). However, everything is smooth and looks nice.

I'm using Kubuntu 12.04 (with KDE 4.9) and everything is buttery smooth. All the games I've tried (basically the Humble Bundles 5 and 6) have been smooth and look great. Other than the watermark issue (which was introduced in Catalyst 12.6, and later fixed in 12.8 ), I haven't had any difficulty with the Linux AMD/ATI drivers.

I will say that sleep didn't work at first, but after I upgraded my BIOS and installed the Catalyst drivers (suspend won't work with the open-source drivers), everything works %100.

EDIT: Also, if you're adventurous, you can take apart the laptop and upgrade the A4 APU to and A8. Apparently, this modification has no serious side-effects (i.e. laptop doesn't get hotter, battery life is still good). You can find that here: [http://arstechnica.com/civis/viewtopic.php?f=9&t=1150450&hilit=hp+g4+1215dx&start=240](http://arstechnica.com/civis/viewtopic.php?f=9&t=1150450&hilit=hp+g4+1215dx&start=240)
