### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-11-08
I'm a rabid free software enthusiast *and* a PC gaming enthusiast. I happily play games with Windows, knowing that it is a really good OS for games, at least until OpenGL gets a foothold (which could still happen). I am very skilled in the art of enjoying things :)

Of course, a big reason here is probably because I view games as a bit of an art form. They are not designed to be needed, so there is no real practical reason why they MUST be open source. I think the platform needs to be open source since people and programs all depend on it, but beyond that it is fair game. (Granted, I would like for this stuff to be open source, but *change takes time and favours those who are patient*).




However, I too haven't found much interest in MW2. I will probably play it at some point, since - like Star Trek - all the odd numbered COD games since 1 (the Treyarch ones) are horrifyingly lame and all the even numbered ones (the Infinity Ward ones) are really great.

I liked how they did COD 4 from the perspective of story-telling, at least in some parts. The cutscenes during loading screens were brilliant, since I never felt like I was waiting for it to load Ever. While they have always done it, they added another layer of depth to the way they bounce the player between different characters. Being in the shoes of the guy who gets kidnapped and assassinated is really a unique way to tell a story and shows off an a real strength of the medium. Similar deal with the sniper mission.

However, I think the "modern warfare" thing is getting pretty tiresome, and COD MW2 really looks like more of the same. (Even down to the multiplayer, which has a lot of wasted potential thanks to that STUPID class system which doesn't work because there is a program you can download that unlocks everything in half a second. Then again, I play Project Reality for my multiplayer shooting, so my preferences are obviously a bit warped).

Once I am finished with Dragon Age, I may give its demo a try...
