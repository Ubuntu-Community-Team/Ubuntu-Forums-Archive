### Post by darundal on 2006-10-05
Why is it so bad? They hire and pay programmers too, and their work ends up in with the rest of everything else a lot of the time. It's not like they sit there and take advantage of everyone...they provide a certain class of product + support to a certain market. A lot of what we use is either something that someone was paid to do that made it's way directly into the free stuff (was immediatly GPL'd, etc...) or was eventually GPL'd or otherwise released freely. Plus, Mandriva Linux IS open source...they comply fully with the GPL. You can download or order a copy of the source code from them, and they also make available free versions of their operating system. When you BUY a copy, you are paying for the packaging, support, and licenses for whatever non-free stuff they might have included...such as an MP3 Codec being installed by default, and in this case, Transgamings Cedega being included and installed by default. Nothing wrong with that. However, personally, I do take issue with Transgaming due to a certain lack of "return". I am aware of no contributions they have made to the community as a whole since going commercial with their product, or any contributions to Wine (which their product is based off of), financially or otherwise.
