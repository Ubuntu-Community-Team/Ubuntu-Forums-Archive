### Post by oedipuss on 2008-11-04
The thing to check for is direct3d support under vmware. If it can do d3d9, then it is possible the game would run. Last time I checked, vmware had some experimental support for d3d**8**, disabled by default, and somewhat temperamental. I don't know what kind of performance you would get, even if it does support d3d9, though.

There has been some success running warhammer with wine, but there is a serious bug with character drawing which renders it unplayable.

Edit: the character bug seems fixed, there's a patch for it! Instructions here: [http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=13139](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=13139) , the patch is in the last comment of the relevant bug. 
Seems you'd have to compile your own wine, but that's easier than it seems. 
Still, judging from the comments, your mileage may vary..
