### Post by Naegling23 on 2008-05-27
My logitech G5 (generation 2) works great.  I had to manually configure some stuff in xorg.conf, but there are enough guides around to walk you through it.

The key is to get one that has the dpi change settings on the mouse (ie hardware) itself.  Since you wont get the software to work on linux, you wont be able to configure anything that is software controlled.

### Post by Seraed on 2008-05-27
The G5 is wicked awesome. The 2007 edition is nice since they added an extra thumb button (something I missed from previous logitech mice).

Definitely go laser. The weight cartridges may seem a little goofy, but if you look at my G5 and then my roommate's they both handle quite a bit differently and we've customized the weight in each to suit our needs to where they handle like night and day.

My only problem with it is out of the box is the DPI buttons. I feel that there is too much variance between the three settings. The middle setting is fine enough for me, but others may want to change it. Like Naegling23 said, you'd have to search for guides to change the buttons.

### Post by Naegling23 on 2008-05-27
for the dpi settings, I think they are fine.  You set the middle one using your mouse system settings to where you feel comfortable during normal use.  From there, you can go up and down.  Very useful for FPS and photoshopping.  As long as your middle dpi setting is set to a happy medium, the other two should be good....it would be nice to be able to tweak them but oh well, I think they are hardwired into the mouse....and I agree, the extra thumb button is worth it.  As is the weights.  You wont go changing them all the time, but being able to customize the feel of your mouse is worth it in my opinion.
