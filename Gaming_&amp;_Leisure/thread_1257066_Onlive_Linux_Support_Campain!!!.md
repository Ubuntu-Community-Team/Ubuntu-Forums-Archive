### Post by Phyds on 2010-11-24
Rerouting the wireless connection of the USB dongle to the Ethernet port of your PC is possible. Windows has a built-in feature that allows this. But with Ubuntu, I don't know if there is a nice GUI feature to make it work. I found [this tutorial]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Internet/ConnectionSharing") that could help you set it up.

Anyway, I think you complicate things a bit. I suggest that you should spend some money on a good wireless router. You'll have an all-in-one small box with wireless and wired access to your network and internet. If you don't need the latest [802.11n]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEEE_802.11#802.11n"), I highly recommend you to buy the [Linksys WRT54GL]("http://www.linksysbycisco.com/EU/en/products/WRT54GL") that you flash after with a third party firmware like [Tomato Firmware]("http://www.polarcloud.com/tomato"), [OpenWRT]("http://www.openwrt.org/") or [DD-WRT]("http://www.dd-wrt.com"). My WRT54GL flashed with [Tomato Firmware]("http://www.polarcloud.com/tomato") is extremely stable and compatible with any wireless device. I think it could be near the perfect router!

 In order to  share an Internet connection, the computer that will do the sharing must  have two network cards or ports. This assumes that you are using at  least one Ethernet port and that it is identified as "eth0". eth0 will  be the port that other computers will connect to you on.

[LIST]

Note: To clarify the above example here is an example configuration that will work -  * 1. You are already connected to the internet using your wireless on port wlan0 *  2. The ethernet port eth0 is connected to the PC that needs to share  your internet connection (or you could wire eth0 to a router for  multiple machines)
