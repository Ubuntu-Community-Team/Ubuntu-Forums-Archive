I've never really had that problem. I'm rather rough with my controllers, and none of my GameCube, Wii, RumblePad, or Xbox analogs have ever had problems with the deadzone. I've actually had to increase the software deadzone on the 360 controller since the slightest touch will register as an axis change.

A lot of times if you're suffering that large of a deadzone it may be a manufacturing defect, or if it's consistent with the controllers a design flaw. Deadzones can also be a feature: they prevent accidental bumps from registering as a controller action.

### Post by The Flying Penguin on 2010-06-28
I'm not too knowledgeable on game pads but I prefer game pads from console systems because they are more familiar to me.

I took the controller port out of a broken XBox and wired it up to a usb cable so that I can plug a normal corded XBox controller into my computers. It works pretty well, even under Linux. ;)

If you want a wireless solution and you have Bluetooth, I believe the PS3 controller works with Ubuntu. I think I read that the 360 controller will also work. You just need a wireless usb adapter so your machine can pick up the signal.

I personally think the PS3 controller is the best choice since it requires no adapters (unless you don't have built-in bluetooth), is wireless, is a very solid design, and if you already have a PS3 then you don't have to shell out any extra money. Even if you don't have a PS3 and have to buy a controller, at least you will always have your own controller when you play at a friends house or something.
