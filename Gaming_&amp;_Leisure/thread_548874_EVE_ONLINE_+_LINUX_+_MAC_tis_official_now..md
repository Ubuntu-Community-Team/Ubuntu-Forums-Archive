### Post by Seraed on 2007-09-11
I played EVE for the trial. It was an amusing game, but I couldn't quite get into it. I had just abandoned WoW when I did and it was a great change but wasn't quite what I was looking for.

I remember when I heard about CCP looking for Linux beta testers, being ecstatic for the Linux community finally getting some attention. Here was a big, if not major, player of the MMOG market finally see the potential for revenue in Linux users. However, after reading that TransGaming article it makes me somewhat sick.  CCP is indeed catering to fans, but I feel it is going about it the wrong way. I was hoping that it would actually modify it's code to work natively with Linux systems. But this is not anywhere near the level I was hoping for. All this is going to do is encourage developers to continue using DirectX, which is just not the way to develop for anything outside of Windows.

Don't get me wrong, Wine, Cedega, and TransGaming are great for allowing me to play Windows games on Linux. I have Cedega to run City of Villains and other games that Wine can't run. But I would rather see a developer take a stance like Id, or Epic and develop games native to Linux rather than support Microsoft and DirectX. Cider and whatever they are going to use for Linux support is just an easy way for a developer to say "Ok, you guys handle it." What will happen if/when they break their partnership? The developers will continue with DirectX and we will all be left in the dust again.

Now, the fact that CCP is taking interest in Mac and Linux is still a step in the right direction, I just wish they'd had followed in Id's and Epic's footsteps.
