license is only valid if you're allowed to read it before purchase. if you have to open the box or run the game/program to read it, then it's automatically invalidated by consumer protection laws. at least that's how it is in many EU countries. but European law is very pro-consumer, for example the game producer is forced to provide a backup copy of the game, if it's impossible to make such a copy by yourself (because of DRM, obviously), even if the license explicitly states that you can't have a backup copy.
