### Post by Toxicity999 on 2006-06-12
I'm confused the title asks about wine, but you're asking in the post about Cedega...

Well first a little backstory (short one, promise.) Cedega is kind of a unifying body of similar projects, rewind, wine, and such. They meshed things together and came up with winex a 'version' of wine geared towards gaming. In the past Cedega has torn the front as far as 'Transgaming' goes.  More recently Wine has actually began catching up! Sure you don't get the unorginized, ugly UI with Wine (We soon will have a pretty and ORGINIZED front for both Wine And Cedega, Wine-Doors.) But wine in essence is about at an equal playing field with Cedega... they all have their pros and cons... cons of cedega mostly being they don't allow the community to better their code. Pro, they have made great progress breaking ground of DirectX reinventing the rectangular wheel so to speak.

To sum up... most hardcore gamers that part of the Tux militia (tee hee) Go with both Wine and Cedega, those even more hardcore say if it wont run with wine I'm not funding the monster demon spawn of it. (don't get me wrong... I like what they're doing but... icky...)

Just try Wine with your favorites... make SURE to read up on the game on the wine app db... [appdb.winehq.org/]("http://ubuntuforums.org/appdb.winehq.org/") because trust me, there is almost always some kind of trick to get it that much closer to playability.

See I think wine is attempting to just now worry about Cedega, they don't want to rush a good product to compete with someone doing a different thing only to degrade their fine work.

It's all the linux way. Enjoy, hopefully you figure things out.
