### Post by oldrocker99 on 2013-03-10
> **ViliX64 said:**
> Thank you for the link :)
And there are not many games on Linux Steam..

No, there are not a lot of Linux games on Steam. Yet. The fact that a major developer like Paradox ported their excellent Crusader Kings 2 for Linux on Steam was a big deal. It has been shown for the last couple of years, mostly through the Humble Bundles, that Linux users are hungry for games. Linux users, just since Steam made their client public, account for 2% of Steam users. Mac users comprise about 3%, after two years of Mac games being available on Steam (fortifying game developers' view that Mac users are just not that in to gaming). This is not escaping the eye of developers who see an unfulfilled market (Linux gamers) that is willing to buy closed-source games if they are good enough.

I have already bought Kickstarter games Project Eternity, Planetary Annihilation, Wasteland 2, War for the Overworld, and another couple I can't remember offhand. All are coming out with Linux versions. Sometime this year or next, I'll see the games I helped fund that way.

While most of the games for Steam Linux are from various Humble Bundles, there have been others that are unique to Steam (Unity of Command, The Book of Unwritten Tales) that are certainly major games.

The main thing I want to say to eager-to-game Linux users is to be patient. Look at all that has happened in the last six months. This is not a conclusion to a process. This is a beginning.

### Post by TazX on 2013-03-23
Put me down as another vote for the Humble Bundle being the best value.  Get on their mailing list, I've got some great games for Linux (and also my Android phone) through them, at less than a dollar a game most times so far (unless I'm cashed up and generous, entirely up to the user how much they pay, though above the average gives you better value).  I've also got Steam on Linux, and have added the Steam Keys through there.  Not sure why yet, though it is nice to see all my achievements, etc lined up for me.  :)

I'm surprised Machinarium hasn't been mentioned yet - gorgeous, and great fun.  I'm loving this renaissance in point-and-click games (though that may just be because I'm old enough to remember the first time) :/.  I highly recommend installing ScummVM and playing through some of the oldies.  If you can get hold of any of the Monkey Island games they're hilarious! Beneath a Steel Sky is available for free through the repos if you want to check out how it works.

If you liked 'World of Goo' I recommend 'Sprites' - a different vibe, but the same level puzzler gameplay - think mellowed out lemmings playing pan pipes.  Limbo is a different, action-based, puzzler (think lemmings after their psychotic break.  In the dark.  With chainsaws. It's dark (in visuals and in mood) but still fun to play with friends or alone.

All three (World of Goo, Sprites, and Limbo) have been in the Humble Bundle in the past, and probably will be again in the future (as bonuses if you pay above the average).  Most of the Linux games I've paid money for have been through Humble Bundle.  Get on their mailing list so you can find out when there's a Bundle on offer.  Thanks for this thread, I have yet to play most of my Humble Bundle games, and will be giving some of the ones mention here (like Torchlight and Supersword Brothers) a run soon.
