### Post by efikkan on 2010-10-01
Two-three years ago the proprietary ATI drivers were hardly usable. Today they are usable for gaming, but still suffer from lower performance than the windows drivers, lack of video acceleration and some bugs in the OpenGL implementation. I suggest you read [my post]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=9910069&postcount=40") in another thread.

Performance wise a mobility HD 5650 will be able to handle Starcraft II, but not necessarily with the best detail settings.

### Post by MrNatewood on 2010-10-01
I have a mobile HD4570 and it works great for native games(including quake iv and all the open source shooters)

Trouble comes when playing with wine. To my impressions wine works a lot better with nvidia cards.

Also, it sucks that only nvidia cards can help with HD playback on linux and it's a pain to get 1080p running smoothly without an nvidia card.

All in all, nvidia are just simply a better choice. Even if you get less bang per buck according to benchmarks(mostly done in windows), adding the level of linux support it's a no-brainer to buy nvidia.
