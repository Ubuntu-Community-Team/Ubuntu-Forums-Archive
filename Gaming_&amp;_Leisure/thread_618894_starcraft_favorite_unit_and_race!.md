### Post by dfreer on 2007-11-21
I really like using Terran, with the brood wars expansion. Early in the game, I can quickly and easily protect my choke points with about 2 bunkers (each with 1 firebat and 3 marines), 2-3 missle turrets (to detect cloak), 1-3 seiged tanks (optional, not really needed), and several SCV's and medics. Get the extra energy upgrade for medics and the extended attack range upgrades for the marines if you have the chance.

The bunkers tend to draw the most attention, and as long as you pay attention and get the SCV's in to repair the bunkers, with medics automatically curing them, the marines inside will cut through almost all wannabe spawn rushers. Most early attackers will focus on overwhelming you with large numbers of their basic troops, so you shouldn't have to worry about air-to-ground attackers, nor distance attackers (at first).
Hopefully, this will leave you time to add 1-3 sieged tanks (making sure that they don't shoot your own troops), and that should take care of most ground based attacks for now. The next thing I focus on is a good set of valkeries, at least a full quick-switch macro group (12, 18, something like that?). They can fly decently fast, and will take out most air-to-ground based attacks.

If I had to pick favorite units, I would choose the zerg's guardian and devourer (they work together so nicely), or the terrans valkeries. My friend thinks the only way to win Starcraft is by playing protoss with a million photon cannons, and then buying as many carriers as possible. Valkeries make short work of carriers as long as you manage them correctly.

### Post by jdong on 2007-11-21
Good strategies. One thing I will point out that people tend to underestimate/overlook: during an attack, **take your infantry out of bunkers, stim them, then put them back in!** It is amazing what a remarkable difference that could make.

Take 2 bunkers and an Ultralisk charging it. Without stim, the ultralisk can easily destroy one bunker and send the other into flames before being destroyed. Stim the marines while it's charging, and the same Ultralisk can barely bring one bunker to red.

> **dfreer said:**

My friend thinks the only way to win Starcraft is by playing protoss with a million photon cannons, and then buying as many carriers as possible. Carriers are vulnerable to EMP, have relatively low HP which makes it vulnerable to strong air-attacking units like goliaths, most fliers, even dragoons. They also have a delay between their target acquisition and attack which easily allows a ghost to lock it down, scourges to hit it, or  a templar to do a psi storm. They also can be battered down by defense structures, lacking a way to attack them out of range with ease. If that's not bad enough they take bloody forever to climb up to on the tech tree and requires you to sacrifice creating many other units to conserve gas.

Bottom line, IMO they are a fairly weak "capital ship" and certainly one of the less optimal Protoss attacks. Against non Terrans, shield battery supported Archons do frightening damage. Usually my dragoon+zealots+templar+reaver strategies leave me with a lot of reserve gas but lack of minerals, so whenever that happens I churn out Templars to merge into Archons. Their mineral cost is the same as a worker unit and archons in groups are difficult to stop without EMP.


As far as capital ship, IMO the battlecruiser is the only one worth massing. A group of 12 battlecruisers is extremely challenging to counter particularly with science vessel support and yamoto gun.

As far as your unit picks, I'd add mutalisks to that list. Attaching 9 acid spores to an enemy heavily armored ship cluster means that the mutalisk basically causes 3 units to kill themselves per hit! Devourers are a fantastic way to counter heavily armored air units!

The guardian was better in Starcraft but worse in Brood War, for me primarily because Goliaths match their attack range now and once a Guardian takes fire it doesn't last long at all.
