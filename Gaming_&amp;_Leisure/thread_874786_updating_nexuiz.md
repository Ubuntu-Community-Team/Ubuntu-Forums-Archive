### Post by detrate on 2008-07-30
Don't bother with the repository for now... if ever.  Nexuiz is an archive that contains executables for Windows, OSX and Linux, just unzip and run whichever one your operating system requires.  In your case, **nexuiz-linux-686** or **nexuiz-linux-686-sdl**, whichever one runs better with your hardware (SDL is compiled with different libraries).

An important thing to note about Nexuiz, is that on UNIX based operating systems such as Linux or OSX, your settings and downloaded items are stored in **~/.nexuiz/data**.  This is a good thing because it mean your settings from 2.4 will be read by your newly downloaded 2.4.2 copy.

You can always find the latest official release on the left hand bar of the Nexuiz website, [http://www.nexuiz.com](http://www.nexuiz.com) but here is a link for your convenience: [http://downloads.sourceforge.net/nexuiz/nexuiz-242.zip](http://downloads.sourceforge.net/nexuiz/nexuiz-242.zip)

For the record, 2.4.2 is muuuuuuch better than 2.4.
