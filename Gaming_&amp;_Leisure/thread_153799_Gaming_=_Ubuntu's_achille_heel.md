### Post by Tommerz on 2006-04-01
For most users I know, gaming seems to be the one thing putting them off swapping to linux.

Obviously, the major hurdle is games not being native, and having to work through different methods (cedega) to get games working.

But it also runs deeper than that - I enjoy playing emulated games, and emulation is much more optimised on windows than linux - for example, the gens port seems to have slightly jumpy sound, snes9x doesn't have as nice an interface, visualboy is slower etc etc.

There's also no Beat of Rage port, despite it having appeared on many other systems.

It also wouldn't be too much of a stress to put mugen with some copyright free characters in Ubuntu's respository. People may claim it's old, but it's the closest you'll get to a fighting game in linux (besides emulation, of course).

Do you think there's something that could be done? Maybe an Ubuntu flavour along similar lines to edubuntu aimed at better (non commercial) gaming implementation (ie: packages such as gens and mugen being available in a repository, joystick support without automatix)?

Any thoughts?
