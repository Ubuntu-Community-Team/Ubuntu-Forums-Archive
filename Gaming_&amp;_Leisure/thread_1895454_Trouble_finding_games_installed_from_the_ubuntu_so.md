### Post by thatguruguy on 2011-12-14
> **timmaaa06 said:**

It would be helpful to know which games you're referring to, and what you've done to look for them.

Generally, if you are using Unity, you can find your games by doing the following:

[LIST=1]
[*]Open the Dash.  You can do so by clicking to button with the Ubuntu logo on it at the top of the launcher on the left, or by clicking on the "Super" key (a.k.a., the "Windows" key) on your keyboard.
[*]Start typing the name of the game you're looking for in the search bar, and it should show up within the Dash. You can also look at all installed games (at least, ones with proper .desktop files) by doing the following while the Dash is open:
[/LIST]

[LIST]
[*]At the bottom of the Dash, you should see 4 icons: one that looks like a house, one that looks like a ruler, a pencil and a brush, one that looks like a document, and one that looks like musical notes. Click on the one that looks like a ruler, a pencil and a brush.
[*]To the right of the search bar at the top, you'll see the words "Filter results" with a little arrow. Click on the words or the arrow.
[*]Click on the word "Games" in the menus that show up.
[*]Beneath the search bar, you should have 3 rows of applications showing up; "Most frequently used", "Installed", and "Apps Available for Download". Click on the words "See [some number] more results" next to the word "Installed", and it will show all your installed games.
[/LIST]
