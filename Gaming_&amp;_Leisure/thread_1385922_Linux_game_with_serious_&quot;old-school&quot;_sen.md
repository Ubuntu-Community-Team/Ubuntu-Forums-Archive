### Post by ucal on 2010-01-20
Well, Nethack may be your thing.  It's got several different graphical engines, from text based, to isometric, so you can take your pick.  It's a rogue like dungeon crawler. 

DwarfFortress is probably one of the most in depth games I've ever seen, but graphically, it's worse than nethack.  There are graphical tilesets for it, but I've never tried them.  DwarfFortress is a city building sim, and also a dungeon crawler sort of rolled into one.  You generate a world, and can build a city in it, or take control of a singular dwarf and explore the world you made.

Oh, the thing about both of these games is that levels are randomly generated.
