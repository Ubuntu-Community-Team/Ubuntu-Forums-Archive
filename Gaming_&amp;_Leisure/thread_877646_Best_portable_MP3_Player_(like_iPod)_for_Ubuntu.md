### Post by spicifer on 2008-08-02
I purchased a Sansa Fuze a short while ago and I certainly wouldn't recommend it without reservations. Getting it to work was [a lot of hassle]("http://spicifer.blogspot.com/2008/07/sandisk-sansa-fuze-8gb-on-ubuntu-804.html").

Now, I'm not saying this player is all rubbish, it just has a lot of rough edges, that shouldn't be acceptable. It will do, but it could be a lot better. So, I would recommend doing your research thorougly before buying an expensive player. It will save you a lot of trouble.

### Post by lkirlin on 2008-08-02
Im sorry you are having such trouble with the weakpoint of ubuntu: trying to make proprietary devices and programs (like a mp3 player and iTunes) work well. my advice to you would be to use the most supported mp3 player on ubuntu; the iPod. Although it is rather expensive, there are a number of programs that can transfer your music very easily. rhythmbox does a good job with this, and it comes standard on ubuntu. when it comes to podcasting i would suggest that you would use banshee, which allows you to subscribe to the podcasts that you would want to listen to.. There are ways to get iTunes running on linux such as cedega, which costs money. And there are a few viable options that i suggest that you try out before dishing out monthly fees for restricted usability.
