### Post by giga+bytes on 2016-08-04
I recommend GOG.com. It is similar to Steam but does not require a client to be installed on your computer or having to login online every time you want to play, and no DRM or questionable EULA. You only create an account to purchase and download games, then all you need to do is install and play like the good old days!

They do not have the vast library Steam does, but they still have a large selection (retro, new, you name it), and more is added every week. For each game there are details about system requirements, and anything parents need to know about possible younger-audience unsuitable content (it will say if there is lots of violence or drug references, that sort of thing). Plus if needed, you can search for a game's ESRB rating online and see what precisely earned it a more mature rating. I know of a few games that did not look ideal for a younger player because of something listed, then found out it was not what I thought it would be and was actually not a concern.
