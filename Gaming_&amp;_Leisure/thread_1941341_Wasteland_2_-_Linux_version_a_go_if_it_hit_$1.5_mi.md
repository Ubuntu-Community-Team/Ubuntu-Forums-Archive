### Post by Sofox on 2012-03-15
[http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/inxile/wasteland-2](http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/inxile/wasteland-2)

The orignal producer of Wasteland (and also worked on Fallout and Baldur's Gate) started up a crowdfunding project to to make a sequel. Well, so far it's been a success, with them breaking through the $900,000 requirement and now over a million, but something else has happened.

Previously to the FAQ "What about Linux?" they responded that they liked Linux, but still needed to make choices about what engine they'd select and couldn't make a commitment.
However, just yesterday, they changed the answer and it now reads:

> Linux is go at $1.5 Million.  If we hit that milestone we will be adding a Mac OS X AND Linux version of the game!!!!!If you want this game on Linux, it's time to [pitch in]("http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/inxile/wasteland-2")!

### Post by zerothis on 2012-04-01
I've noticed a certain resentment from some Linux users about Kickstarter projects that relegate the Linux version promise to 3rd priority (or even further down the list) and also projects, especially Win+Mac+iOS+Android+NaCl+Flash+Java+DHTML+Wii+XBox+PS3+PSP+NDS+3DS but not Linux projects, asking for double or triple funding to add the Linux promise.

There's no need for shock and outrage, low to no priority for Linux is business as usual. What's not business as usual is the opportunity for us to ask for a Linux version _before_ the game creators invest way too much money in proprietary development tools, engines, and libraries that will ensure porting their game to Linux later will be as difficult as possible. Also is the opportunity to use our money _without_risking_any_ to encourage the creation of Linux games even when there is little chance a project will make a Linux version. It works this way, Project X says "for $2,000 we will make our game" (for Windows they mean). And they say "for $100,000 will will make a Linux version". Us Linux users should back them and tell them our investment is for a Linux version. If they don't get funded, we lose no money. If they get $100k, we get our game. If they get $2,000 to $199,999.99, and decide not to make a Linux version, _we_sell_our_investor_rewards_to_someone_else_. They know we wanted Linux and that we gave them money for it and that they chose to ignore this profit potential. We break even, minimal gains in proving our market value, no money out of pocket.
