### Post by Robin T Cox on 2007-07-24
joe88, there's nothing wrong. For Windows Flightgear has a nice GUI. but for the Linux version you are assumed to be able to use your terminal and enter commands. Here are some of them:

> fgfs

- starts Flightgear

> fgfs --help

- shows a list of commands

> fgfs --show-aircraft

- will give you a list of available aircraft

> fgfs --aircraft= (followed by the name of your chosen aircraft)

- will start Flightgear with the aircraft of your choice.
