### Post by PatrickNiedzielski on 2010-03-28
Hello all,

My name is Patrick Niedzielski.  I am the lead developer of the [Humm and Strumm project]("http://hummstrumm.blogspot.com/"), a Free Software project with the goal of creating a 3D adventure game called *Humm and Strumm*.  In this game, two players will collaborate as the main characters, Humm and Strumm, to prevent the evil Dr. Geoff from taking over the world.  Armed only with yoyos, Humm and Strumm will have to travel through various lands until they finally get to his layer.  Players will also be able to unlock the competitive Tag Mode, in which players can play a game of school-yard tag in several arenas.

Our team looks like this:
[INDENT]**Patrick Niedzielski (myself):** Lead developer.
**Ricardo Tiago:** Engine developer, in charge of rendering subsystem.[/INDENT]

[IMG]http://lh4.ggpht.com/_OzmQuyJnaLU/S5-f7qBa0UI/AAAAAAAAAWs/p3z2V3LVSGU/s800/logo-mid.png[/IMG]

The truth is, there aren't that many 3D adventure games out there in the community.  We've a wealth of Free/Open Source FPS games, platform game, and puzzle games, but in the way of adventures, there are surprisingly few.  Furthermore, those that do exist are usually developed only for GNU/Linux, not Windows or other platforms.  There are significant numbers of users on Windows.  Even platforms like FreeBSD which are pretty similar to GNU don't have that many games available for them natively.  Finally, those games that do exist aren't of the same quality as many modern commercial games.  Don't blame it on the platform, because there definitely are examples of games for GNU/Linux that have spectacular graphics and music.  We as developers and artists can definitely do better than we currently do.

Humm and Strumm is being created to address these problems.  We are building the game from the ground up, engine and all.  Our few dependencies are libraries like zlib and libpng, smaller utility libraries, and packages like OpenGL libraries and CMake, methods of cross platform development.  This will give us full control, and it will result in a more integrated codebase in the end.  Each component is being designed to work cleanly and efficiently with all others.  Our process is even similar to that of a professional game development firm: it's a hybrid of the easy-going attitude of FOSS development and the rich structure of modern proprietary development.

At the moment, the game engine is to where most of our effort is going.  The basics should all be out of the way by the 0.2 release, expected in the middle of April.  After that, the development will begin to branch off into the different subsystems.  **We are looking for developers both with expert knowledge in a game engine subsystem (like graphics, networking, or sound) and developers who want to get their hands dirty in any of these fields.**  If you want to help, you can send us an email (as we describe below) and become either a contributor or a full team member.  If you want to play around with the code before you do, either download the 0.1 source release from our [Download]("http://hummstrumm.blogspot.com/p/download.html") page or check out the code from our Subversion repository with the following command:

```
svn checkout http://hummstrumm.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/ hummstrumm-read-only
```

This will download the trunk to the folder *hummstumm-read-only*.  You can also check out one of the branches, *unstable*, *PatrickNiedzielski*, or *RTiago* for more up-to-date and experimental code.

Although our current focus is on developing the engine, we welcome writers who wish to help with the specifics of the game plot and dialogue.  Soon, too, once the game is a little more fleshed out on paper, we will be looking for talented artists and composers who wish to contribute their skills to the project.  **Any work here is on a volunteer basis**--you've no obligation to remain with the project and join the team, but we will mention you as a contributor in both our documentation and the credits.  If you wish, you can always become a full member of the team.  Please see our website's [Helping Out]("http://hummstrumm.blogspot.com/p/helping-out.html") page for a detailed description of how to contribute or become a team member.

If you really have no development or artistic experience, you can still help the project out by spreading the word to others.  Write a blog entry about us, tell interested developers. or even just download and play the game when it is complete.  In the end, we are just looking to make a really fun and playable game.  Any and all contributions are welcome.  Please, though, **no monetary contributions**.  Some projects allow, or even are supported by, these.  Because this project is made by a team from multiple nations, registering us with governments to accept these would be a nightmare.

Our current home page is [here]("http://hummstrumm.blogspot.com/").  On this website, you can find information about the game, recent news, and links relating to the project.

If you have any questions about the project, its goals, or its development, you can either contact me directly [EMAIL="PatrickNiedzielski@gmail.com"]via email[/EMAIL] or email the users mailing list, [EMAIL="hummstrumm-user@googlegroups.com"]hummstrumm-user[/EMAIL], where all developers and users can answer you.  If you want to join the team or make a contribution, sign up for the [EMAIL="hummstrumm-contrib@googlegroups.com"]hummstrumm-contrib[/EMAIL] mailing list [here]("http://groups.google.com/group/hummstrumm-contrib").  We welcome contributors and team members to the project.

Cheers,
Patrick Niedzielski
