So, you've defeated the alien fleet. It's time to fight them on their own turf.

Finity Flight: Fight Back takes place shortly after the events of the original Finity Flight. All alone, you've repelled the invaders of earth, but instead of simply waiting for them to come back in greater numbers, you are taking the fight to them.

Fight Back takes place in space, and adds to the original game with 15 new levels, a new "Boss" level format, four variations of old enemies, and new unlockables (including the "Attack Raptor" skin above).

[IMG]http://i34.tinypic.com/ncb2av.png[/IMG]

[IMG]http://i34.tinypic.com/dww2lc.png[/IMG]

In addition to this, we will be patching the original Finity Flight to resolve some important questions new players have and also add some various polish and tweaks throughout the game. :)

This expansion marks an important milestone for us, as it will be the first game we actually have for sale on MirthKit. That marks an important milestone for games in general, because it will be the first game I know of that is both for sale and has freely accessible source.

[http://www.mirthkit.com](http://www.mirthkit.com)

I'll have more details come release time, but feel free to drop some comments. :)

### Post by curvedinfinity on 2008-09-11
Well, I actually tried the advertising model myself to an extent (though I made open source games). There were two things I didn't like about it: it rewarded being devious in the way you market to people, and it didn't reward good development -- just lots of games. :)

Thus, I switched to making a publishing system that encouraged a lot things I felt were lacking in existing models: quality development, reusable (and sharable) assets and code, and a more open relationship with players and other developers.
