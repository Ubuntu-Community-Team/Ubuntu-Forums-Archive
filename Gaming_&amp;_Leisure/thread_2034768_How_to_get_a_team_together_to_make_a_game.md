### Post by V for Vincent on 2012-07-29
Honestly? For one thing, if you want to start a successful project, you shouldn't start by staking out your own territory and by saying what you can't do. If you only want to do web design and you're not joining an existing project, you're not going to get to where you want to go. You'll need a solid base that already appeals to people and, once you have that, you'll need to actively recruit artists and developers. You can get that base either by starting with friends who share your vision or by putting in the initial work yourself, including graphics and code.

### Post by thnewguy on 2012-07-29
To get a game project started you need one of two things:

1. A working model complete with code, graphics and a website that other people can play and add to.

2. Money. If all you're interested in doing is the website and directing the making of a game which already exists, you're going to need to hire developers, artists and (since this is a clone) possibly a lawyer.

There are a million project ideas out there and not nearly enough developers to work on them. This means you've either got to do it yourself or hire people to do it for you. Capable developers are already working on their own stuff or looking to pay the bills.

### Post by V for Vincent on 2012-07-29
Disagree on the money bit. There are completely free quality games. Not a lot of them, but they exist. If you put in enough initial work and create some good momentum, you'll be able to convince others to join you.

Also, bear in mind that you don't absolutely need *original* artwork when you're starting out. Look around for stock models. Put something together that has solid gameplay and placeholder graphics and sound, maybe you can get some artists to fill in the blanks. In any case, be prepared to put in a realistic amount of time. Learn Python the Hard Way is probably a good place to start. Or you could take the alternative route and learn how to make great artwork. Put enough of it together and make a simple prototype of your game using a user friendly engine like Panda3D - just enough to convince others that it could succeed and that it is worth their time.
