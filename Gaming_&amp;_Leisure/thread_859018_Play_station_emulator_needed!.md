### Post by acoustibop on 2008-07-14
Yes, PCSX2 will require a pretty high end machine to give any sort of satisfactory results.

There's also [ePSXe]("http://www.epsxe.com/") for Playstation games, but like PCSX it's a plugin-based emulator and and difficult to set up and configure: people have produced scripts for this, but nevertheless I find both plugin-based Playstation emulators more trouble than they're worth.  Apart from a recent Windows-only (as yet) update, neither emulator has been updated for nearly 5 years.  PCSX is in the repositories, BTW.

pSX is still in active development, works much better for most people and is much simpler to use: apart from your controller, you don't even have to do any configuration to get good results.  In particular, it doesn't suffer from the constant slowdowns that seem to plague plugin-based emulators.  In addition, while ePSXe and PCSX do seem to work much better playing from images than from your original CDs, pSX plays from CDs as well as it does from images. ;)

**Edit:** incidentally, pSX Author (the pSX developer) is working on including Playstation 2 emulation in pSX.  This will probably take some time yet but, taking pSX as an example, will likely not require as high end a machine as PCSX2, although it seems it will still require a fairly high end one.
