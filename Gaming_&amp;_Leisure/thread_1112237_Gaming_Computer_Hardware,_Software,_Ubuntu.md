**Q/A**
```

Q: Should I get firewire?
A: Mainly used for webcams 'n' stuff (don't bother).

Q: Is water cooling worth it?
A: Tough question - mainly used by hardcore computerers.

Q: I don't want to loose data on my drive - which backup program should I use?
A: There are tons of them, choose for yourself: partimage, rsync, tar, and others.

Q: What does PCIe x1/x2/x4/x8/x16 mean?
A: Number of lanes in a PCIe slot, more lanes means more speed. Different devices use different amounts of lanes - a GPU uses x16.

Q: PSU - Some general info' please?
A: Get a good quality one or a smoke alarm. At least 500W.

Q: 64bit/32bit - Which should I get and what is the difference?
A: 32bit limits RAM usage to around 3GB. 32bit has stability, 64bit has speed. 32bit programs still run on 64bit systems.

Q: Is, for example, a CPU running at 1GHz equivalent to a GPU running at 1GHz?
A: No, because different devices do a different number of 'jobs' per Hz.

Q: Are AMD/Intel/ATI/NVidia all compatible with each other?
A: Yes but you can't use two different graphics cards at the same time and CPUs are motherboard-specific.

Q: Are dual processors worth it?
A: Yes. The more processors, the better.

Q: How should I best make use of my two HDDs, how should I partition them?
A: Simplest way would be to have the OS(s) on one and data on the other (larger) one. Although you may want to look into other methods such as RAID.

Q: A computer gets slower as it gets older: What makes it slow? Motherboard, CPU, GPU, etc?
A: The Operating System; Linux takes far longer to get slow than Windows.

Q: Wine - Is it easy to use, does it create lots of glitches?
A: You'll need a Nvidia GPU to stand a chance, even than you can't expect all games to work.

Q: What is so good about PS/2, PS/3, etc?
A: You don't need to setup your keyboard layout, it just works.
```

### Post by Melcar on 2009-03-31
As said earlier, if you plan to play Windows games mostly best stick with Windows or dual boot.
As for hardware, a good cheap setup would be a AM2+ motherboard, Phenom 9600 (or try a PhenomII tri core), 4GBs of RAM, and a HD4830 (HD4850 is better if you can stretch your wallet a bit more).  A system like that will have little problem with most cross-platform games (Nexuiz, Urban Terror, and all that stuff), and have decent performance in Windows games.  For a PSU 500-600W is more than enough.  
As for your extra concerns:
- Firewire is mostly used for video transfers.  Unless you specifically need it or want it, don't worry with this.

- Unless you plan to do heavy overclocking or run a silent PC, water cooling is too much of a hassle and extra cost to be worth it.  A good "kit" can set you back US$150-200, but you're better off doing it yourself (takes time and may be more costly).  Stick with air.

- Wine "works".  Best not to depend on it for games however.  It's just to troublesome to configure right for certain games, and when it does work it does so usually with glitches.

### Post by directhex on 2009-04-20
> Q: PSU - I know barely anything about it.

First, make sure you buy a decent brand - a "good" 400W supply is infinitely less likely to explode and cause a fire than a "bad" 650W supply. Don't spend less than £50, as a good rule of thumb.

> Q: What does PCIe x1/x2/x4/x8/x16 mean?
This refers to the number of "lanes" (data channels) a PCI Express slot has. Graphics cards are, almost without exception, 16x devices, and need to slot into a physical 16x slot - however, they'll work fine in a slot which is physically 16x but electrically less. This usually happens when a motherboard chipset provides a total of X lanes to split amongst every slot, and the vendors use a physical 16x slot instead of a physical 4x slot for the leftovers (to ensure you can fit a graphics card). Typically, you don't need to worry - any board with PCIe will have PCIe16x, and that's the size/speed you want for a graphics card.

> Q: 64bit/32bit - Which should I get and what is the difference?

32-bit has a limit of 4 GiB of address space, and 2 GiB memory allocation per process. By "address space" I mean all your RAM - PLUS devices which permit "direct memory access", i.e. they reserve some memory addresses which means they can be written to directly, for fast access. The upshot here is if you have 4 GiB of physical RAM, then some of that memory cannot be used with a 32-bit OS, because all your memory mapped devices use up the high-numbered memory addresses (and something's gotta give).

> Q: Is, for example, a CPU running at 1GHz equivalent to a GPU running at 1GHz?
No, not at all. At its simplest, think in two terms:
* How many floating point operations can each chip do in each Hz?
* How diverse is the chip's ability to do those operations?
A GPU generally provides a very large number of relatively low-clock cores - leading to a huge theoretical number of floating point operations. However, the design of the chip means that they are extremely single-task-focused, and are unable to adequately process most tasks. Even within CPUs this can happen, which is how a modern 2.8 GHz CPU will absolutely murder an older-gen 3.6 GHz chip (more instructions per cycle, less "mucking about" per instruction, more focused on important operations)

> Q: Are AMD/Intel/ATI/NVidia all compatible with each other?
Yes. But don't expect proprietary drivers from ATI and NVIDIA to work together at the same time, if you install both ATI and NVIDIA graphics cards. My system has an Intel CPU, Intel motherboard chipset, and NVIDIA graphics card.

> Q: Are dual processors worth it?
Multi-core rocks, and you'd be hard-pushed to avoid it these days. Multi-socket is out of your price range.

> Q: How should I best make use of my two HDDs, how should I partition them?
Well the EASY option is to just use the whole of one disk for the OS, and re-use your data disk for data. But you could do something more ornate if you felt like it.

> Q: What mouse should I use? (wired, ergonomic, no ball)
Buy the most comfortable optical (laser?) mouse you can afford. I have a Razer Lachesis, and it's really lovely. Also consider the Logitech G5. In general, gamer mice are very comfortable. Consider Logitech, Razer, and Microsoft as brands.

> Q: What keyboard should I use? (wired, not ergonomic, backlit)
Backlit keyboards rule for playing in the dark. I have a Logitech Illuminated Keyboard, my wife has a Razer Lycosa. In both the keyboard & mouse case, ask yourself what you gain from the added layout & running costs of wireless.

> Q: A computer gets slower as it gets older: What makes it slow? Motherboard, CPU, GPU, etc?
Yes, it's the OS. As a Windows installation gets old, the registry fills with crap. A reformat tends to make it nice again. Linux systems are much more resistant, but can start to show age (especially a very elderly install's Apt can take ages compared to a fresh install), though this can be cured with shell trickery.
