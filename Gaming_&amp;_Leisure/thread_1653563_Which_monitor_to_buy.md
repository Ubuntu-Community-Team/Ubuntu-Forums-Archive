### Post by QIII on 2010-12-27
I never make recommendations for what is best -- I don't know exactly what you want and I don't want to tell you how to spend your money.

You'll probably get all sorts of opinions, but what other people like may not be what you like.  As you get suggestions, treat them as fact-finding and not as absolute gospel.

My suggestion would be to look at what is within the budget allowed by your gift cards (and maybe a bit more if you want to pitch in).

Then scour the web for reviews.  This is a pretty good place to start:

[http://reviews.cnet.com/monitors/](http://reviews.cnet.com/monitors/)

You might also go to places like NewEgg and check out what their customers are saying about their purchases.

### Post by efikkan on 2010-12-27
I suggest you buy a screen from Eizo (or Nec). If you want a relatively "cheap" screen and possibly for gaming I suggest Eizo FlexScan S2402WFS wich is one of the best TN-panels available and is not glossy. Eizo FORIS have good black levels and great color, but is not suited for gaming, it's more suited for videos and non-professional graphics works. I have both of these screens myself.

If you have more money to spend and want an even better screen, look at the IPS panels from Eizo. (Some panels from HP and sometimes Dell might also be good, but if you buy Eizo, you'll know it's good.)
