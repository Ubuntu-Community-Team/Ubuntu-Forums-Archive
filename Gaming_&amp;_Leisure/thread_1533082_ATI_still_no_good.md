### Post by efikkan on 2010-07-21
The open source ATI drivers will work for desktop usage and some workstations, but is not usable for gaming. The open drivers perform roughly 1/8 of the proprietary ATI drivers (wich perform poorly compared to nVidias proprietary one), does not support OpenGL 3.x or 4.0, is missing real support for Evergreen, have higher power consumption than the proprietary ones and is based on mesa and gallium3d wich is one of the reasons for the bad performance. If the open source drivers ever is going to compete wit h the proprietary ones, the development need to start 6-12 months before the release of new hardware (in other words the developers need to sign a NDA and keep the project closed until release) and mesa and gallium3d needs to be abandoned. In the real world this means a nVidia GT 240 with proprietary drivers will outperform a high end ATI card with open drivers by far.
