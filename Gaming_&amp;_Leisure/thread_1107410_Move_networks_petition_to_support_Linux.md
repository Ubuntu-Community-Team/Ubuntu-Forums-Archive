### Post by taxilian on 2009-08-18
I am an employee of Move Networks, but I am not currently commenting in any official capacity (so don't take it as company policy).

I am currently working on the linux port of our player; it is still awhile off from being release quality.  Most likely it will be at least the end of 2009 before it is done.

That said, please understand that the reason that our player isn't supported on linux yet has nothing to do with a desire to shut linux users out or even a dislike of linux.  In fact, we use linux extensively in our business for various applications and servers.  The sad fact of the matter is that we have finite resources.  The priority of a linux port is driven, sadly, by money; percentage-wise, relatively few people use linux compared to those who use Mac OS and Windows.  Even if there are those who will argue this point, that is the way that our clients see it, and so while we do want to support linux, it hasn't been our #1 priority.

Fortunately, netbooks and other embedded devices are pushing that priority up, and I am very close to having a version of our player running on my linux machine; good things are coming!

Good luck, ya'll.

- Tax

### Post by taxilian on 2009-08-18
Shannon,

You have certainly not offended me; actually, I have been advocating a linux plugin for the last year, since I took employment with the company.  Part of the reason that I chose to respond to this thread -- without any endorsement or even knowledge of the company, so I hope it doesn't get me in trouble -- is that I have seen a lot of negative press in the blogs about the company that I work for.

While confidentiality agreements of course limit what information I can tell you, I can definitely tell you that the company is committed to having a working linux-based player.  While I probably shouldn't give out any details, I will tell you that I am the one currently tasked with the first stage of the moblin project; today for the first time the Move plugin was loaded on a linux box, and I was able to communicate with it through javascript.  The next stage is to turn it over to our another developer so that he can track down some instabilities that I am seeing and then we need to build a renderer for both audio and video.  The core is pretty much there.

Why is it taking so long?  Well, I could go off on quite a rant about this, but suffice it to say that other priorities have taken precedence, despite what many of us would like.   Also, the company has been undergoing a restructuring lately; nothing nearly as dire as many blog posts seem to want to think, but with two significant cuts in personnel there are sacrifices that need to be made.  One of those was a decision that the architecture we were using needed some serious rethinking to address changing times and needs of consumers. The plugin version that I am working on is actually not a port of the plugin currently in use by our major clients.  It is actually a completely rebuilt player that has been designed from the ground up to resolve many concerns and take advantage of the things that we have learned.  One of the primary design goals of the new player is portability; in fact, much of it was originally built on linux and then ported to windows.

I personally have no control over when anything will be released, but as a completely unofficial response to some of the negative press and an attempt from an ordinary employee to reassure a community that I also belong to (yes, I use linux on my desktop -- and mac and windows as well, as appropriate), I promise that I will do everything that I personally can do while I'm working here to try to make that happen.  That might not be much =]  But, there is definitely progress being made.

I can't tell you any of the deadlines, but there should be a demo on the moblin platform coming up; how long it will be from that to an actual product I don't know.

Also, once we have linux support, we will still need to wait for each client to update their sites to use the new player; as you can imagine, we are excited for that to happen as quickly as possible, but large corporations will be large corporations. =]

- Tax
