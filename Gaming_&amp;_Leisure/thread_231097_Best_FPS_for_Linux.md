### Post by saythein on 2008-05-12
No one mentioned Assault Cube. 

[http://assault.cubers.net/](http://assault.cubers.net/)

Pretty much a generic CS:S but then again, CS:S is pretty generic itself. It's by far the best multiplayer skilled shot game. I'm not really into blasting bullets that are the size of the target down a hallway where there's no where to run. That's.... lame. If you are used to playing games where you have to be good to be good, there aren't power ups and crazy things to jump off of, no aliens or other llama features, and you run Linux, here you go. Built in map editor, multi platform resource friendly need I say more. 

If you really want to go through the hassle there are tons of tutorials on how to get Wine to run stuff like CoD-CoD2-CoD4-Medal of Honor-ect.... But PunkBuster will not work so... good for LAN bad for Internet. 

I know this is tough to swallow, but there's no such thing as an awesome open source 3d fps for Linux. I'm sorry, but if you want decent games you're gonna have to buy them. You aren't going to get good FPS or smooth graphics or bug free gaming from a seriously 3d shaded vector environment in Linux, PERIOD! Just don't waste your time trying to take gaming seriously in Linux it's not possible even with Envy and things like Crossover(bbooo) Cedega(what a joke) just don't try it so you won't get mad. I don't claim to be a guru of any kind but I've been around the block a few times w. Linux and gaming, it'll work but you are better off with Windows. It hurts me a Linux loyalist to say that, but it's true. That's how companies make money. Off THOSE people, not us broke Linux people why do you think we look for free software all the time, cause we like reporting bugs?!? We are of no use to game developers, so they just let us run the servers instead of play the game :(

I will say that MMORPG games, namely SilkRoadOnline, work extremely well as long as you have an 8800x or some high end Nvidia card. If you are rocking an ATI, I wish you luck on even getting catalyst to open after you install it. You need Nvidia graphics to game in Linux IMO. Ati has failed me repeatedly with their drivers, Nvidia has never given me a problem which I didn't anticipate from the beginning. Thus concludes my novel of a post. Just had to get that out there :)
