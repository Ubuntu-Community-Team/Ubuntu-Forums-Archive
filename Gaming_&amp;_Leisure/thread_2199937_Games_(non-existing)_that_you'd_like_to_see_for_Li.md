### Post by oldrocker99 on 2014-01-16
RTS games are very few in the Linux world; of course, there's the excellent Warzone 2100, but the only new one is Planetary Annihilation, which I have been playing because I Kickstarted it, but which costs ~$60 on Steam currently. Turn-based strategy is pretty well represented (Arma Tactics, Conquest of Elysium 3, Dominions 3 & 4, Unity of Command, etc.) There are a number of puzzle games (Portal, Rochard, etc), several horror games (The Penumbra trilogy, Organ Trail, Don't Starve), some really good adventure games (Book(s) of Unwritten Tales, The Cave, The Journey Down, KY Route 0, etc.), sims (Cities in Motion, Crusader Kings II, Europa Universalis IV, Euro Truck Simulator, etc.) and lots of roguelikes. One thing (ahem) nobody needs is more shooters, but that's strictly my own opinion.

The game I would love to see someone try to work on is the old 80s game, M.U.L.E., which was an 8-bit masterpiece, and a nearly perfect game design. There is a (sort of) clone called Space H.O.R.S.E., sold by the despised Shrapnel, but it's not nearly as charming as the original design.
