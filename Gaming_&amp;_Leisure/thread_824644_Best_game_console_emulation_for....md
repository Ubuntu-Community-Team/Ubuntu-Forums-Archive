I did have a Sega Genesis myself, as I really never cared for the run and jump side scrolling platform games.  The NES system was famous for that (both in console and arcade games) and I pretty much skipped over all the Nintendo consoles because of that.  I guess at that point in my gaming interests I wanted arcade, arcade, arcade.  I wanted a system that was as close to the arcade as possible.  In addition I did take into consideration the game that came with the systems.   Sonic looked cool, and I never liked Mario Brothers.  So the choice of system to buy was clear.

Yeah, Sega did give me what I wanted.  I had Outrun, Space Harrier, Hang On, etc.  There were a few other cool games as well.  But the Sega Genesis began to favor sports games and I didn't get into those.

The N64 was released at the time I was starting to tire of the Sega Genesis and at first I thought this was going to be my first Nintendo purchase, but I decided to hold off and a computer instead.  That was an extremely wise choice as the Playstation was also released around that time and took off as the preferred system.

While having a computer my gaming interests changed from Arcade and shoot 'em ups to Action and Adventure games.

A little game called "Myst" changed my whole perspective of gaming.  I wanted to feel immersed in the game.  Doom was another PC based game that I thought I wouldn't like, but I ended up becoming engrossed with FPS games.  Finally Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic turned me on to RGP games.

I found out the Sony Playstation 2 was VERY heavy on action adventure/rgp games. Since the PS2 is backward compatible to the PS1, AND it was a DVD player gave me good enough reason to once again invest in a video game console.  

Being the nostalgic nut and yes there are still Atari 2600 games I still play, I wanted to find out what was available on the older game consoles in the form of my action/adventure interests.  Being more open minded now than from what I was in my youth, I decided to revisit the Nintendo consoles.  I knew there had to be more to them than just Super Mario Brothers side scrollers.   Sure enough...I found some cool adventure games going all the way back to the original NES.  Much to my surprise, there was more variety in the adventure games with the NES platforms than even my "back then" favorite Sega Genesis.  Even today with GENS loaded on my Ubuntu system I don't see many adventure games on the Genesis roster.

I now know that the NES had quite a few action adventure games but I am curious to learn more what the SNES has.

So I am going to be a very very busy gamer :).
