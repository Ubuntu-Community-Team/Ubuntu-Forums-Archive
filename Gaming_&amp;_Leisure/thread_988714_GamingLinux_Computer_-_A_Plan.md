### Post by Sammi on 2008-11-22
At the moment the graphics card drivers from ATI are NOT good enough. This may change in a years time, seeing, as they opened up their specs, but don't count on it.

Best bet is to buy Nvidia.

And 1+ for the Nvidia 9800 GT 512 MB RAM. Good bang for the buck.

As for harddrives, ethernet, and optical driver, everything you can find should be compatible with Linux. So go with whatever gives the best performace per dollar. Wi-Fi has also become really great with the latest Linux kernel releases. Almost all wi-fi chip maker do Linux drivers now. Only Broadcom chip are iffy, I think, but they're coming around.

Stay with something safe with soundcards. Everything on board should work. X-Fi has been a pain in the ***, but has just been opened, though it'll take some time to get to a usable state.
