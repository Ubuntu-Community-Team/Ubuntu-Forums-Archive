### Post by loftx on 2006-06-24
Hi All, I just purchased Dance Dance Dance, and thought I'd mention my experience with it and Ubuntu. 

Dance Dance Dance is a Dance Dance Revolution (DDR) clone, which for anyone who doesn't know, is a game which involves you "dancing" by stamping on different pressure pads on a dance mat in time to music.

There's plenty of DDR type systems available, but I went with Dance Dance Dance because it's one of the cheapest. If you live in the UK you can get it for only £10 ($18 ) including P&P from Gameplay.co.uk [http://shop.gameplay.co.uk/webstore/productpage.asp?productcode=RM00658&title=pc_dance_game_and_dance_mat](http://shop.gameplay.co.uk/webstore/productpage.asp?productcode=RM00658&title=pc_dance_game_and_dance_mat). There are also a few floating around on Ebay, though the price is similar so you might as well buy new if you can.

This is the PS2 version which plugs in between your PC and you're keyboard and maps to particular keys on the keyboard avoiding any compatibility problems. There's also a USB version for £2 extra, I haven't tried it but it's likely to be compatible with Stepmania (the main opensource DDR software), though I don't know about the supplied software.

For the keyboard version, the supplied software works perfectly with wine though a crack is needed to get around the CD copy protection. It should be easy enough to find, but if anyone has problems feel free to PM me. It also works fine with stepmaina, but that doesn't come with any songs so you'll need to obtain them yourselves - there are plenty available on the net though downloading most will probably be copyright infringement, so stick with the supplied software if you want to stay legal.

If you can't get hold of Dance Dance Dance then Stepmania should work with most other dance pads and even ones for consoles with the correct adaptor. Have a look in the hardware section  at [http://www.stepmania.com/](http://www.stepmania.com/) to decide what to get.

Please post your own thoughts about DDR type games and any info about alternative hardware/software and any problems you had.
