### Post by tyggna1 on 2007-08-28
I'm an ex-gamer--that's what I call myself.  I switched to Ubuntu because games had become a very small part of my computer experience.  However, Tremulous has reawoken the gamer in me.

That game runs off 90s graphics, sure, but it's well made and fun to play.  I couldn't dream of running a FPS game on my compter in windows--but in Ubuntu I get about 60fps on average.  Whenever I play a game that was packaged and designed for Linux, it always exceeds my expectations.  When I got warcraft 3 working in it, I was ecstatic to find that it ran faster with Wine than with windows outright!  

For those games, my actual gaming experience has been wonderful.  Getting others to work has been a pain--but once they're up and set right, they run beautifully on my 6-year-old IBM thinkpad.

I think, and please dis/agree with me on this, that if more games were made for linux, the gaming experience in linux would surpass windows.

Please post.

### Post by MonkeyBoy on 2007-08-29
Also it could be argued that the shorter list of Linux games means that we have a tighter community.

I remember the fine days when the SNES and Megadrive were the only consoles that people had which meant that all the console gamers played quite a short list of games.  This meant that if you wanted a game of Street Fighter 2 Turbo you could be sure that just about all SNES players would also be capable SF2 players.  The greater choice of more consoles and games has actually made it harder to play socially as everyone has different games.

With the games scene here it feels more like the old days as we all probably play the same big multiplayer games and know a bit about most of the available games.

If, for example, I said that I particularly liked the Manchester Warehouse level in TC:E, I reckon most of you would know exactly what I was talking about.  

I think this enriches the Linux gaming community more than it limits us.
