### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-06-04
I very much doubt you'll get SteamOS running on a netbook. If you can, not sure what games you'd be able to play. The question really is not whether SteamOS will install on your machine but what will you be able to do with it once it's installed? I'm guessing not a lot. 

> What are the SteamOS Hardware Requirements?
Processor:
Intel or AMD 64-bit capable processor

Memory:
4GB or more RAM

Hard Drive:
500GB or larger disk

Video Card:
NVIDIA graphics card
AMD graphics card (RADEON 8500 and later)
Intel graphics

Additional:
UEFI boot support
USB port for installation

From [here]("http://store.steampowered.com/steamos/buildyourown"). Don't know of many netbooks with 4Gb of RAM, but I guess it depends on what you define as a 'netbook' and what kind of games you want to play.

### Post by kostkon on 2015-06-04
Big Picture Mode (the Steam OS interface) does not even load (black screen) on my netbook, so there is no way to use that interface. You could probably disable it and use the fallback Gnome desktop.

A number of indie and some old AAA games can run on netbooks.
