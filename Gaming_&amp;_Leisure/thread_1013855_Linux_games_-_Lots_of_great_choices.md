### Post by kspncr on 2008-12-20
I'm so glad you acknowledge the following: 

"Today, Linux gaming lags far behind Windows and keeps many people from trying out Linux for just this reason." I just have to laugh when people try to argue that games for Linux are actually **better** than the ones developed for Windows.

I've always felt that more games should be ported to Linux, but only recently learned just how infeasible it would be. DigiPen (my roommates go there) says that in terms of cost, **only about 1 in 10 commercially developed games break even**--and these are games developed for Windows. Far fewer than I ever realized. I'm sure you can understand why so few games are ported to Linux (or even Mac).

So where can we turn? To Wine, sure, but at least in my experience the latest games don't work so well under Wine.

So we look at the open-source games. Of course none of them look as good as games for Windows, but at least I can take them seriously. Honestly, I was never into multiplayer FPSs at all (exception: Unreal Tournament) until I played Nexuiz. Nexuiz really did change my opinion of multiplayer.

No, we're not going to get an open source Crysis anytime soon (I know the impossibility of such a thing) but not so long ago I was laughing at games for Linux. To be fair, there are still some things I think are laughably bad, namely the sound Sauerbraten makes when you get hit (other than that, a solid game). I guess what I'm trying to say is that despite imperfections, the gaming is moving in the right direction.

I'll end with one last thing: I think you were too generous with your review of the games' quality of graphics. For example, I would never give Tremulous an 8 (unless, of course, that's only in comparison to other open source games?) but I really did like reading the article. Good job.

### Post by Dedoimedo on 2008-12-20
kspncr,

Thank you for your feedback. I like to think of myself as a realist, not a crusader. And operating systems are like women. Why have to choose only one, when you can have two ... or there :)

As to graphics, well, I might be slightly old-fashioned, but I do not think that the latest games are that good looking. Since Doom 3, there has been a tendency for "real-looking" games, but you end up with a screen simply cluttered with useless detail that does not add much to the feel. Plus, the characters look plastic and dead and I don't like that.

You won't believe me when I tell you that I think Operation Flashpoint looks better than Crysis, but that's me. Besides, what makes it for me is the game play, not the graphics. That's why I still play DOS games like mad.

My grades might be high, but that's just a matter of taste. If you like what you see and can play it well and your eyes do not wish to suicide themselves after 4 hours, then things must be good, eh? No reason to have a game with 40GB footprint just because it has fancy graphics.

Try Urban Terror for FPS, you'll like it.

Cheers,
Dedoimedo
