### Post by cor2y on 2008-07-13
Its easy for publishers and devs to scream its the evil pirates/p2p users who are to blame.
There is no proof that a download via p2p = lost sale, they always claim so but no concrete proof.

It seems lately anytime a game on the pc under performs the publishers and sometimes developers are quick to blame the p2p users , its never that the game was riddled with insane drm that made it unplayable for regular legit users (titan's quest), had too high system requirements (Crisis) , crappy game (hellgate : London) or was just more of the same generic FPS,RTS,Sim,RPG game without any innovation.

Yes P2P use will happen can't get around that despite how much drm you throw on it to shackle it, in fact the invasive drm we see these days on pc games seems to me to more about stopping second hand sale of the product than stopping p2p usage, if a game has a limited number of installs/activations and requires you show irrevocable proof that you bought it in order to get new activations then it will kill second hand sales , we have already seen it doesn't stop p2p usage/downloads.
You can't for example sell your steam version of HL2 or give it away its tied to your steam account as is the other games you purchase over steam, anyone trying to pass off a second hand version of bioshock or the new Bioware game Mass Effect is not going to get that game sold except to the really clueless, heck if you used up all activations for those games you can't even give them away.

Solution to P2P?
Make owning a game worthwhile (note i used the word **owning**) its the little things that count, no invasive/insane drm. Yes i can download game X via bittorrent but if i buy it i get an actual printed manual (remember those) addons for the game that are not micro transactions (something EA loves to do) and discounts on future games purchased via the company's online download store.
