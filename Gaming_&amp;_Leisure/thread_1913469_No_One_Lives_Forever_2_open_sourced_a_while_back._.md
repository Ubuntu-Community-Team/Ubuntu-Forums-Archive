### Post by Sofox on 2012-01-24
Arthur_D, I not quite sure. The blog has very few actual arguments backing up it's stance (the author admits he's just going on instinct) and it doesn't explain why Arx Libertatis or Quake/Doom games were open source successes.

There may be another reason. I looked up NOLF along with other games mentioned in this thread that were open sourced but didn't gain recognition (Rune, Shadow Warrior, MechCommander) and found that none of them are available on GOG.com or Steam.

Contrast with Arx Libertatis which is on both, and Quake/Doom games which have always been for sale in one form or another since the early 90s. If any of us wants the assets to the game, or just play them, all we need is a few moments and a credit card.

The simple answer is that even if a game is open sourced, the assets need to be legally available. Why would someone port an engine when the most people won't have access to the assets of the original game that made the engine valuable? Lack of availability would also limit the amount of people who could potentially take an interest and then try to hack it.
