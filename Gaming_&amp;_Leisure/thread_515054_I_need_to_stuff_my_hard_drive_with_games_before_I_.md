### Post by acoustibop on 2007-08-01
Well, if you want to play Final Fantasy VII, Archoniam, you can't do better than a Playstation emulator.  There are AFAIK three that run in Linux: [PCSX]("http://www.pcsx.net/index.shtml"), [ePSXe]("http://www.epsxe.com/news.php") and [pSX]("http://psxemulator.gazaxian.com/").

PCSX has the virtue of being available in the Ubuntu repositories, but has been out of development for a long time now and is looking rather long in the tooth.

ePSXe was favourite for a long time but, again, has been out of development for some time.  It's also somewhat fiddly to install and configure, and controllers only seem to work in it in digital mode in Linux.

Although ePSXe still has a loyal following, the Playstation emulator that everyone (who uses Playstation emulators, that is) is turning to now is pSX.  This is a new emulator - less than two years old and still fully in development - pSX has been available for Linux for about 6 months now.

It's a pluginless emulator, making it extremely easy to configure.  Installation can be as simple as installing libgtkglext1 (on a vanilla Ubuntu install), tarring the pSX download to a folder, adding a BIOS and running it.

The fundamental difference between pSX and other Playstation emulators of recent years is that it aims at accurate rather than enhanced emulation, and it's very good at that.  After using ePSXe for years (in Windows) it didn't take long for pSX to completely replace it for me.

One of the members here, dfreer, is maintaining pSX packages, as well as those for Ultima's pSX Frontend, a Python-based application which enables a very complete range of starting options for the game profiles you can create with it for starting pSX.
