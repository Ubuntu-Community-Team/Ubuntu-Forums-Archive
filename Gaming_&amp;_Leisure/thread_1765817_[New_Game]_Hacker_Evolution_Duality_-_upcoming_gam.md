### Post by KIAaze on 2011-07-29
Ok, I finished the 2 available levels.
Not being able to save was a bit frustrating, but I suppose it's just for the demo.

Issues:
=======
When downloading a file, if you switch to another one, you get the last selected one at the end of the download. If the download time depends on the size of the first selected file, this might be abused. Also works for uploads and allows having multiple copies of a file.

At one point, while doing a DOS attack, I did something else which caused the DOS attack window to stop responding. The cancel button didn't work and while I could get rid of the window by clicking the DOS attack button, I couldn't run any other attacks. Scrolling of the map also seemed to stop working (while the rest of the interface (messages, console, esc for exit, etc) still worked). After a while everything was back to normal. I haven't been able to reproduce it again, but I think it happened at least twice.

Apart from that, the game started, ran and exited fine. Fullscreen OK, no resolution problems.
Tested on Ubuntu 11.04 64-bit.

I've attached the logs of my playthrough.

Game design criticism:
======================

It's easy to get stuck in the levels.
In the tutorial, you can't win if you buy any hardware. (no way to resell hardware, no other banks to hack)
In the 2nd level (or level 1 after tutorial), you can only transfer all the money from another server to yours or vice-versa. You can't sepcify an exact amount. So if you don't play so as to have the exact amount you need to transfer for a mission, you loose.

All this reduces the degrees of freedom to finish a level and is a bit frustrating.

You can't do anything but wait while an attack is running (firewall, DOS or EMP). All windows appear in the same space and you can't move them.
Some seem to have priority, so you have to close them to see the others. The message and objectives windows could at least be somewhere else.
You can't minimize or hide an attack while it's running, without cancelling it.

Money transfers are visualised with an increasing/decreasing money amount, but it only has an effect if you let it end. So an interrupted money transfer never gives you just part of the money. Realistic maybe, but then why give the impression that some of the money has already been transferred? (I've actually always wondered if hackers couldn't just "create" money instead of transferring it. :rolleyes: )

The game seems to require more fast reflexes than thinking or searching from what I've seen so far. (just like uplink. Something similar to the trace the hacker missions, where you had to follow traces in logs would be nice. Or some puzzles.)

Overall, the game is not very realistic (but neither was uplink). For DOS attacks, you have to position 4 sliders to the correct bandwidth. For EMP attacks you have to stop a rotating satellite at the correct angle.
The voice recognition system was quite interesting. Instead of recording and replaying a signal like in uplink (quite realistic), you have to fit a voice pattern using some sliders (why not play the pattern you have to fit directly? Nice idea nevertheless.).

However, the graphics and general style are good and I liked the idea of "integrity" as some kind of health bar, which can be depleted through DOS and EMP attacks. Because this, coupled with enemy AIs attacking you could turn it into a playable multiplayer game. Just replace the AIs with real players. :)

And my biggest complaint, also valid for uplink: There's no tab complete and no copy/select+paste in the console. :(
