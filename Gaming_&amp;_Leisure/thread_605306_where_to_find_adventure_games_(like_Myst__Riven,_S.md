### Post by acoustibop on 2007-11-07
One good source of low-cost RPG/Adventure games is console emulation, bluepowder7.  There are a number of excellent emulators around for the various Sega and Nintendo emulators (sadly, no Master System one for Linux! :(), and the Sony Playstation.  There's also PCXS2 for the Playstation 2, but you'll need a fairly high-end machine to get good results from that, really.  I'd recommend:

NES: FCE Ultra (in the repositories)
SNES: ZSNES (also in the repositories; Gutsy has the current version)
N64: [Mupen64]("http://mupen64.emulation64.com/")
Genesis/Megadrive/32X/SegaCD: [GENS]("http://gens.consolemul.com/index.shtml"), but find debs [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=290008&highlight=gens")
Playstation: [pSX Emulator]("http://psxemulator.gazaxian.com/")
and [PCSX2]("http://www.pcsx2.net/") for Playstation 2.

As well as games, you'll need BIOSs for some GENS games, pSX and PCSX2.  Easily found but not discussable here.
