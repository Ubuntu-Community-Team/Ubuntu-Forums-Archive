### Post by Giraffemonster on 2011-01-09
I'm quite confused. I haven't done this "Desert Mission", don't know who Kahn is, and I don't know what Forest Harvesters are. I did finish the game though. The ending second last mission is really really really boring. Just some things to point out:

- I never used a ranged weapon, at all, when I completed the game. Maybe it would have made things quicker, but the best weapon I used was an iron bar.

- To save some money, don't buy much armour. Instead, invest immediately in raising your Check System Integrity program. Eventually at revision 9, you can heal 30 health for 26 heat, at 0.5 seconds of paralysis. If you combine that a high level of Emergency Shutdown, you can last for a LONG time in areas with no healing/little armour. This all costs a lot though. So I would suggest you just gather what little money you have to invest in Checksys.

- Again, what I did used a ton of money. There's another program called "Killer poke", which messes up system memory in order to make bots do a large, but unpredictable amount of damage to themselves. This produces a ton of heat (around 100 for first revision, increasing with revision number). In order to kill really powerful bots that would have otherwise taken a long time to kill, this comes in handy.

First you switch to Killer poke obviously. Then use it, which will either highly damage it or kill it in one shot.
Then take cover, and then use Emergency Shutdown. Heal if you need to. Killer poke costs 1200 per book though, so you probably won't be buying it anytime soon.

- Another REALLY useful tip which I wasn't about to post until I read your last post again, is Sanctuary. Definitely worth the 450 circuits. This program immediately teleports you back to the city upon use. It generates about 90 heat, which isn't near lethal. It isn't really useful buying anymore, as all the revisions do is produce less heat. Definitely worth the circuits, get it before you upgrade Check System Integrity. You can use this whenever you want to get to the city, you don't have to be in danger. If you're trapped underground and facing insurmountable odds, use it.

- More about money, all amour and weapons not only cost money, but deteriorate as well. This is why I would suggest hacking/using malformed packet more often if you can. It's better just to use armour though, selling it is useless.

- If you see the blue hunters north of town, they can teach you how to extract parts from bots. 1 ability point, 50 circuts per lesson. 5 lessons available. Each lesson contributes to a revision to a passive skill named "Extract bot parts". You don't need to activate it, you just kill an enemy, and it will have a really high chance of dropping a bot part. These can be used to upgrade equipment (by speaking with Dixon the Mechanic), or sold. Each revision, causes another possible bot part to be dropped(As in item type, not quantity). I believe that the better revision needed for said item, means better value.

This also makes killing massive masses of Acolytes and Templars more worthwhile. All bots have an equal chance of dropping a bot part, so quantity matters here, not quality. The items stack, so don't worry about inventory space, just accumulate a lot by killing weak bots, and sell it all.

This requires 250 circuits in total, so chances are, you won't have a problem with that. 

- To be honest, the game and most of it's missions (that don't directly require combat) can be completed with just invisibility and running skills. When I was doing the second last mission, I used Invisibility revision 2, combined with the running of Tux with only 100 run (Not that much considering I never upgraded Dexterity, only take Dexterity pills to increase it permanently when they are dropped). I just ran through a large horde of high tier bots, and whenever things got too heavy to handle, I would use invisibility to run past them, then maybe clear out a safe spot for me to regenerate health, cooling, and stamina. Of course I really destroyed the bots in my actual run though.  I think a lot of quests can be finished like this, but perhaps you might not want to.

### Post by stinkyfishheadisred on 2011-01-30
I'm one of the people who works on developing this game, so maybe I can help you all out with some pseudo [word-of-god]("http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/WordOfGod") advice :p. We are changing things around a bit in the dev (will become 0.15) version, so I hope this is accurate for what I remember about the older ones:

> 0.14 gave compilation errorsWe try to keep the following post updated for installing freedroidRPG on Ubuntu: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=6127097#post6127097](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=6127097#post6127097) . If you follow that, you should have no problems getting it to work. It is linked to in the README.

> Now how do I get into the armory...You have to join the Red Guard by completing a number of the quests Spencer outlines. There have been plans (for several years now) to have Tux optionally aid a rebellion/resistance to their rule. So there is a bit of an effort to portray them as borderline malevolent. 0.14 and the dev do this more than earlier versions.

> by "Dalek", do you mean The SingularityYou mean "Dalex"? Well, there are more than that. There is one on Warehouse Level 2. And the Secret Area is packed full of them.

Also note, the 0.13, 0.14, & dev all have different takes on the Dixon/Singularity/Maintenance Tunnels mission(s).

> I can't seem to find a walkthrough onlineProbably  because no one has written one yet. We have been discussing doing  something similar so we can keep track of what all the NPCs said and do  (and where they are in the game), although there are variations between  versions. If you'd like to help write one, I think it would be helpful.

> "Desert Mission", don't know who Kahn isIt is "Koan", and we have changed this somewhat in the dev version, because it was way too complicated, and there weren't any hints about it. However here is a (simplified) spoiler/walkthrough:
[spoiler]
To open the town's west gate you need to talk to Duncan at least twice (0.14->without the Red Guard  following you).

Out in the desert there are two places you can go down: Koan's bunker (where Koan is) in the north and the (optional) Secret Area in the south. Koan is pretty self-explanatory.

What isn't (at least in the earlier versions) is the Secret Area:
After entering the Secret Area you talk to a "Dalex" SADD who needs dilithium. In the earlier versions you should request "a significant reward". Note that the autoguns made a small hole in the wall. Bring him back the dilithium (maybe just get the extra that you gave to the Cook?). In the earlier versions you need to complain about the gift, which causes him to turn on the autoguns again (later versions this should happen automatically).

There is a bug in 0.14 & dev: do not hack SADD if you make him your enemy, before you take the Tania quest.

When the autoguns are turned on, the wall should be blasted through. Go through this and talk to Tania on the other side.

0.13 & earlier -> You have a quest to bash through walls and talk to the SACD (I don't know if this actually works, because it was broken by 0.14). I think this was insanely hard.
0.14 -> You have an [escort mission]("http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/EscortMission") to bring Tania to the town.
dev-proposed -> You have both quests, also the bash-walls quest is much easier.
[/spoiler]

If you have questions (or would like to help - we especially need graphics people), we have a [forum]("https://sourceforge.net/apps/phpbb/freedroid/viewforum.php?f=10"), as well as an IRC channel [#freedroid on irc.freenode.org]("http://#freedroid%20on%20irc.freenode.org").
