### Post by tipsqueal on 2007-03-13
I've tried gaming on linux, especially Source Engine games, which is what you're looking at. I have used Cedega, Wine, and the new Crossover Office. My best luck was using Crossover Office, but it still wasn't that great. I could play it at 1280x768 ( lowest widscreen monitor resolution) and I would get maximum 50 fps but about an average of 26-28 fps. This is with water relfections on the lowest setting and all other settings on medium. My setup is an Nvidia 7600 GT, AMD 3500+, 1 GB corsair Value select RAM. With this same setup in Windows XP I can play Counterstrike, Day of Defeat, and Half Life 2 at 1680x1050 with full everything, 4x Anti-aliasing, and 4x Anistropic Filtering, and it runs on average of 60 fps.

Anyways, in a nutshell do what I do. If you have 2 hard drives laying around and your BIOS enables you to select which drive to boot from, then install windows on one hard drive and Ubuntu on the other. You can also do it with 1 hard drive, but for me it was easier to do it on 2. 90% of the time I'm in Ubuntu, but when I want to game I boot into Windows.
