**Short answer:**  Yes.

**Long answer:**  Like all things in life, there is a finite number of gamers.  We'll call gamers X.  Now, take those gamers, and multiply them times .25, that's the number of gamers who use PC gaming as their main forum as opposed to the 75% who use consoles.  (X/4)  

Now, take that 25% of gamers, and realize that 90% of them use a Microsoft based gaming computer, which eliminates them.  Y=((x/4)/10)

Now, of those remaining gamers (x, remember) you have a body who play video games on a PC that is either Linux or Mac, so cut your number in half.  Y=(((x/4)/10)/2)

So we have gamers now, who use Linux.  Now we need to cut that number down to those who play MMORPGs, about 50%.Y=((((x/4)/10)/2)/2)

So, of this minuscule number, you have to remember there are categories.  You have UO, EQ, DAoC, EQII, Eve Online, AO, WoW, D&DO, and many others.  When you realize that UO has roughly 50,000 members, EQ has roughly 65,000, and WoW has 16,000,000 members, you realize that about 90% of all MMORPG players play WoW.  So take your number and divide it by 10.  Y=((((x/4)/10)/2)/10)

So you take that number and you have to realize that very VERY few of the free-MMORPGs are open-source, say 10% You are now down to a tiny population of people. Y=(((((x/4)/10)/2)/10)/10)

If we assume there are 500,000,000 gamers out there (that would be 1/12 of the total world population) and cut the numbers down to your specified usage (Y, remember), then there are 62,500 gamers out there who fill your criteria.

### Post by ZoiaGuyver on 2009-12-05
Well the answer to "am i wasting my time with open-source games" is a little more than Yes or No.

Also Cedega charging is fair, they were "originally" a fork of Wine but they no longer even use the Wine engine (thats left for Codeweavers) Cedega now use's a proprietary engine they created. If someone is to be put on the pedestal of charging it should be Codeweavers not Cedega.

Are you wasting time with open-source, depends a lot on what your looking for, some of the newer games are making use of Open-Source/Cross platform engines. Torchlight uses a modified "Ogre3D" engine. A lot of game's are based around "Open Source" tech. Thing's like idSoft's "idTech" engine's use massivly modified "OpenGL" based tech. This is what we need imo, we need Developers to use "Open-Source" engines to make really great game's. This way they become "cross-platform" and we maybe open up more option's for us as gamer's.

If your looking for community driven projects of the scale you get with Windows games, sorry to say your not likely to find them. Linux user's can be pationate gamer's but they are also not in the majority.

Tbh though Linux is hopefully getting more "spotlight" with game developers now, with a few already making Linux "native" games. idSoft has always led the field with that though. Or atleast from the perspective of "big name developers". 

It's a case of "patience" really. We just have to wait and see where linux take's us. The gap between Windows and Linux as far as most thing's is becoming really slim. Linux has become more of an "alternative" to Windows than it ever was in the past (thanks to people like Canonical!). 

We are yet to see "Linux" in any form as a "gaming" platform though. We just dont have the market share that make's us viable for big developers, smaller developers and community projects are often very "Windows" orientated aswell. When the attitude towards Linux changes to one of acceptance is when Open Source Gaming will take off.

P.S I am talking about "Open-Source" not "free" software. The two although sharing some of the same principals are a world apart.
