### Post by Danfun64 on 2017-07-05
I don't know why it is, but for whatever reason a lot of open source games with multiplayer have lousy communities. In my teens I used to be a Moral Guardian type who didn't like Blood and Gore or the demonic imagery in the various Doom and Quake games. That kind of "disturbing imagery" isn't what I'm complaining about. I'm complaining about communities which for example enable bigoted speech with no repercussions, or in some cases actively encourages such behavior. Some have servers with almost no moderation whatsoever. Others use mods or settings which directly incorporate offensive imagery. Can anybody guide me to some open source multiplayer games with good communities?

Here's a sample of the games I've played that either have bad communities or have some other major flaw. I know of a couple games with good ones, but I'm not sure if I should mention them, as mentioning them might bring in the wrong crowd to "fix" my rating of them.

Cube 2 Sauerbraten and Tesseract: One of the most popular servers for this game, "Pastaland", has offensive automated announcements (the "Girl Facts").
Quake 2 and 3/Urban Terror: Suffer from fake player syndrome, meaning servers are filled with specially made bots that not only don't appear to be bots at first glance but give fake pings.
Paintball 2: The reason this game is on the list isn't due to the community, but 1. Having a code base which is limited to 32-bit architecture and might not compile on modern linux distros. and 2. Strange vague ban on "custom clients"
Wolfenstein Enemy Territory/Star Wars Jedi Academy/Star Trek RPG-X: Any value these games have in terms of community is ruined by a fatal combination of an sv_pure system and using platform and architecture specific .dll's and .so's. At that point I might as well play a closed source game.
Minecraft: Not open source, but some people think the fact that it runs on Java is close enough...unfortunately running on OpenJDK and other architectures is not a priority, and in the case of PowerPC Mac and Java 6 support being dropped (however outdated they may be) means that this game definitely won't fit my portability desires.
Quake Live: Not open source, but some people think that the ability to play its demos with WolfcamQL makes it close enough. However, the popular sounds mod (which hasn't been removed from the Steam Workshop) has some sounds that can be considered offensive.
Star Wars: Jedi Outcast/Quake 3 Defrag: These communities tend to keep to themselves, and have the tendency to thwart newcomers joining in.
Zandronum: Popular mods Complex Doom and MM8BDM-Unholy Bosses contain offensive imagery built in, and in the case of the ladder I'm not talking about the Screamer imagery.
