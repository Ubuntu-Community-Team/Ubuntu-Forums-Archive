### Post by djinnkeeper on 2010-05-07
Always remember that most successful games are made by teams and that the open source and Linux communities are pretty naturally conducive to teamwork.  An existing project might give you the focus you need, to divert that errant attention from Liberty City or where ever you happen to be running amok.

If you're not already somewhat experienced as a programmer and are having trouble focusing, you might find the needed guidance working with others, on a project that already has some traction.  While considering this, also consider that there are many ways to contribute to game development, other than programming.  Perhaps you are not able to focus on programming, simply because it doesn't pique your interest.

I feel that it helps to start looking more critically at your favorite (Windows-dependent) games.  None of them are perfect; if given the chance, how might you improve upon them?

I personally don't like dual-booting - if you have Windows and you want to use it 85% of the time.. don't let some abstract ideology keep you from enjoying that experience.  Linux is great, but it shouldn't keep you from enjoying your computer(s).
