### Post by oldrocker99 on 2015-02-25
XPlane has been the best flight simulator, hands down, for nearly two decades. There is paid content as DLC from Steam, and a **raft** of free plugins.

The distro doesn't matter as much; the difference between Slackware, Debian, Red Hat and Arch is *mostly* how they install software. I know that the CIV V Linux threads have had happy reports from users of the above. It really is a matter if you can easily install Steam, and whether you have an appropriately powerful video card and CPU. Mostly.

Good luck; I have never met a flight simulator fan who didn't praise XPlane to the skies (so to speak;)).

### Post by MikeCyber on 2015-02-27
> **oldrocker99 said:**
> XPlane has been the best flight simulator, hands down, for nearly two decades. 

X-Plane supports Linux only since 2006 or xp8 which was the first to have worldwide terrain/airports.
I had a look at xp7 on win98 but went happily back to FS9/Fly at that time. 

A 4 core CPU is needed to have most features on. And in case you run into problems with your AMD graphics, there are some command line arguments. See the forum section, same link as I gave above.
[http://www.x-plane.com/desktop/system-requirements/](http://www.x-plane.com/desktop/system-requirements/)

Happy flying. Personally I can't wait for a VR device for X-Plane and the upcoming Project Cars.
