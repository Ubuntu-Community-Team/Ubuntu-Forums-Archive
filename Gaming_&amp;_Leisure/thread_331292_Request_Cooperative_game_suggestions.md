### Post by Indras on 2007-01-04
Hey all.

I've been a dedicated Ubuntu user for nearly a year now.  I have a rather busy life, so I don't get much time to browse the forums.

Anyway, I am a life-long gamer.  My drug of choice is RPGs, but I don't mind the occasional RTS or FPS, and often crave a good turn-based strategy game.  Basically, I prefer games where fast reflexes or manual dexterity aren't an important factor in winning.  So, naturally, I prefer Civilization (turn based) to Command and Conquer (real time).  And RPGs lend naturally to this concept, by allowing me to take my time to level up if I run into a boss battle or difficult situation I can't handle.

I've slowly gotten my wife more and more involved in gaming.  She really only played Mario type games while growing up, so she didn't quite know what to expect.

Gaming sessions now basically fall into three categories for me: hand-held gaming at work during my lunch break (I own a Nintendo DS and play Final Fantasy V or Age of Empires: Age of Kings), console gaming in front of the TV with my wife watching (she loves watching me play Final Fantasy X, since she fell in love with the characters, and likes the action in battles), or cooperative play with my wife on our two computers.

My wife's computer runs exclusively Windows XP Pro.  I may change that later, but for now it's actually convenient for making games work for her.  I run Ubuntu 6.10, but I have Win XP on a separate partition, and will occasionally break down and reboot to it when I run into a game that I can't get to work in Cedega, or through a Win98 virtual machine running in VMWare.

Anyone here with a wife or girlfriend knows that competitive play can only sustain itself for a short while.  Trying to play Age of Empires was a joke, because she was immediately frustrated that she couldn't compete, due to a lack of knowledge of the game.  So I tried teaming us together against some computer opponents, which helped, but still got either boring (computers too easy) or frustrating (computers too hard) within a short period of time.  The same was true of Mario Kart DS.

 Cooperative games, on the other hand, we can play for hours on end.  Since there's no competition between us, and endless room to explore, it is always an enjoyable experience.  I've found a few games which offer what I'm most looking for:
Mario Kart: Double Dash
Diablo 2: LoD
Dungeon Siege
Dungeon Siege 2

(and before you say something about Mario Kart: DD, keep in mind that it is a completely different experience from Mario Kart DS.  In Double Dash, you are together in a car, communicating and working together to accomplish a task, while in Mario Kart DS, and basically every other racing game, you're each doing all of the racing by yourself, and just happen to be on the same track at the same time.)

However, we've both gone through most of the Dungeon Siege games, and we're currently on our second time through Diablo 2.  Our first time we got our levels into the eighties before it got boring.  Now, it is happening much more rapidly.

I'm looking for other suggestions.  Here's my options:
PC (DOS, Windows, Linux... I can make pretty much any PC game run on these computers)
Nintendo DS (we both own one)
Playstation 2 (need another controller, though)
Gamecube

You think this would be a common option in games, but looking through my collection, most either have no multiplayer capability at all (the Final Fantasy games sans XI, the Elder Scrolls games, SimCity and The Sims games, the Might and Magic series, etc), or the only multiplayer is head-to-head (Warcraft and sequels, Civilization games, Age of Empires, etc).

I already know that the thing I'm truly looking for is MMORPGs... World of Warcraft or Dark Age of Camelot would offer everything I'm looking for, but I'd prefer to avoid monthly fees if possible.

Can anyone help me out?
