### Post by MattyGabe on 2009-12-09
Hello all,

My name is Matt and I run a (relatively) new website called FOS Games (Free and Open Source Games), which aims to become a Gamespot/IGN/Game Informer equivalent for free and open source games.  While the site itself isn't necessarily "open source" such as a Wiki, I feel having a group of editors writing the articles is the way to go - to ensure quality articles make it on the site.

Here's the URL before I continue on:  [http://www.fosgames.com]("http://www.fosgames.com")

The problem I have is that I am currently the only one working on the site.  This means both in a content creation sense and in a marketing sense.  Also, it means there's only one pair of eyes scrutinizing the site. So, if you're interested in helping, here's what I need (in order of relative need):

1. Editors.  If you regularly play free and/or open source games and feel that you can write reviews on them in the manner presented by the few articles I've already written, please let me know.  While I don't have a minimum article length, the general rule is that you have to be able to clearly explain your reasoning for whatever scores you give a game.

2. Marketing help/advice.  Not only do I have enough time to create content for the site, nor do I have enough time to properly promote the site as it should be.  This is one area I have never been very strong at, and so even just advice as to how to go about promoting the site well would help.  The tough part here is that the budget is non-existent (domain reg and hosting comes out of my pocket).

3. Layout/look & feel/flow advice.  This one is definitely easier to come by, but if you have an idea on how to make the site look or feel better for users, please let me know.  I am always open.

To help out, you can reply on this thread, or reach me at matthew at the domain name above.  Thanks!
