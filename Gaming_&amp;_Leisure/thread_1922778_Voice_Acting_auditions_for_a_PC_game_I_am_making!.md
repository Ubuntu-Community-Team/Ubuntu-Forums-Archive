> Some companies go out of their way to shut down fangames, declaring them [copyright infringements]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright_infringement"). Such shutdown has been believed to have a [chilling effect]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chilling_effect_%28term%29") on free speech, where amateur developers can't predict what a court will find as [fair use]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_use"). The term "[foxed]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company")" is often used to describe these incidents, stemming from the original coining of the term from [20th Century Fox]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/20th_Century_Fox")'s shut down of an *[Aliens]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aliens_%28film%29")*-themed [total conversion]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mod_%28computer_gaming%29#Total_conversion") of *Quake.*
