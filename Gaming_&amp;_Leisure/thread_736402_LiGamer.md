### Post by derekr44 on 2008-03-26
Do any of you use LinkedIn?  It's a great site for business networking with people and friends from all different industries.

Anyway, a new website was launched today called theLiGamer.com.  I'll let the site creator explain the purpose better than I can:

> It all started with a question: Would anyone be up for having a better way of talking shop about video games in LinkedIn? Maybe even a specific channel or category?

I had been consistently frustrated with some of the channel selection and categorical breakdown of the Answers section of LI.

But an even greater frustration was feeling like something was missing; I wanted more interactivity, more chances to really use my connections (and potential connections) for mentoring relationships, career moves, and deeper conversations and discussions instead of the sometimes fire-and-forget Q&A.

The responses that were posted were enough to encourage me to sit down, jot a couple ideas down, talk business strategy with a few friends, buy a domain name, spend an hour in photoshop, and then an entire night in development.

theLIgamer [dot] com was born.

We’re not sure completely where it’ll go, but we’re crazy excited about the possibility. And since we want to service and support the LI community, we’re always open to suggestions, ideas, etc.

So feel free to drop us a line anytime.

IMO, this is potentially a HUGE opportunity for the Linux crowd to voice opinion and excitement because a lot of these guys are game developers themselves.  I'm connected to a few of them through the site (I'm one of the co-mods) and wanted to bring this up to this great Linux community.

So pass the word!  Get on over there and let's encourage those developers and publishers to take a look at the great potential Linux has as a gaming platform!
