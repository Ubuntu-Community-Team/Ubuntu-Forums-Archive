### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-12-02
[Tremulous]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/tremulous") is good, as is [Nexuiz]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/nexuiz") and [Neverball]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/neverball"). I'm all about [Armagetron]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/armagetron"), myself. [LinCity-NG]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/lincity-ng") is fun, but seemed tricky to figure out. [FreeCiv]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/freeciv-client-gtk") is a great Civ2 remake. 

[Anagramarama]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/anagramarama") is good for those who don't dig shoot-em-ups. Mom loves [Frozen Bubble ]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/frozen-bubble")and [Gweled]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/gweled"). 

[Chromium]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/chromium") is *sweeeet*. [Battle for Wesnoth]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/wesnoth") is as good a strategy as most any you can buy for WinXX. 

A lot of the K-series games are good, but I don't have much heart for KDE and don't care to add all the dependencies. 

[Planet Penguin Racer]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/planetpenguin-racer") (and some other similar ones) is good for a kid. I dig [PySol]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/pysol") for a Solitaire timekiller. [Scorched3D]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/scorched3d") is good too, and almost educational. 

[Torcs]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/torcs") is fun, but [Trigger]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/trigger") is weird (in my opinion, anyway). [XMahjongg]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/xmahjongg") and [XGalaga]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/edgy/games/xgalaga") are two more good ones.

There are a lot to pick from. :mrgreen:
