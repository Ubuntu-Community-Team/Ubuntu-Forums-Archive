### Post by Hero_boy on 2007-09-22
Hey Mongo have you tried both? Here are my **Initial **thoughts, I'd be interested to know what you think:

**Installation:** 

Both installations were incredibly easy, mimicing a windows installation. Why can't all installs on Linux be this easy?

**1st 15 Minutes of play:**

**PlaneShift: **When they say 'Beta', they mean it! Most races aren't available when you start. A complicated and some-what confusing character creation process takes place just before you are thrown straight in to the game. A MMORPG of this nature is essentially telling a story, but with no inital cut scene I have no idea what my purpose is in this virtual world or where I am! I really think an opening scence is crucial to getting started initially. Some of the graphics do look good, however much of it is un-finished, un-textured and the cliping is clunky. I immediatly got stuck on tables and walls. The world if vast but there doesn't seem to be a lot to fill it?

I realise that this game is only in Beta, and almost every complaint I have will be fixed in the final edition, however I am looking at playing a game _now._ 

**Regnum: **The game starts with a nice opening cut sceen to establish the game setting. Like most of the players in the game, the cut scene is in Spanish however it has English sub-titles. The graphics and gameplay are well polished, and the best way to describe them is very similar in WoW (in fact... it could easily be called WoW's free cousin). Even though there are reportably only 80,000 players vs PlaneShifts 400,000 but there are actually lots of people in here! However be prepared to find an English-Speeking group if you are so enclined.

Long story short, juding by my 15 minutes of playing each game, and keeping in mind that 15 minutes is definitly NOT enough time to truely review/judge a game, I would recommend Regnum if anyone else is trying to make this decision as well.
