### Post by MaximB on 2008-02-17
**It's NOT another "what RPG games we have for GNU/Linux" , it is more general thread about RPG's.**

Since about a week ago I was sure that Diablo series were one of the best RPG's ever made, but a few days ago I were proven to be wrong by Vince the AoD developer.
He made a good point and even managed to prove to me that Oblivion is not that good RPG as I thought at first.

To explain why those games are not a good RPG's (yes they are great games but not good RPG's) I need to first explain what IS a good RPG.
RPG's it's not all about fighting monsters and gaining levels.
RPG's are about choices you can make to develop your character and it's personality.
RPG's are about good story that you can really influence and change the outcome of the game.
It's about dialogs.
It's about customizing your characters masteries and skills.
It's about good quests that you have multiply choices to solve them.

Why is Diablo series is a BAD RPG you ask ?
Diablo is one of the greatest games ever made but it's more of a hack and slash game then a real RPG.
You don't really have choices in this game.
Yeah you can chose attributes like str, dex, mana, life and yes you can learn and use spells and yes you can in D2 chose skills - but that is all good for a fighting game.
You don't however have a choice about quests.
You just have to solve them all and in only one way.
There is only one ending to game and you can't change that.
You can't influence the story of the game.
You don't have dialog options with NPC's that actually change something in the game.
You can't be evil or good.
All those things are very important for a GOOD RPG.
So yes Diablo is a good game, but it is a very bad RPG.

Now what about Oblivion ? why this RPG isn't so good ?
In short :
You don't have many dialog options
The combat sucks
The persuade mini game is absurd (I've made several "levels" on that alone)
The game is easy
Most quests focuses on combat solutions
You can join and advance in ANY guild even if your skills focuses are very different  (A fighter character can join a mage guild and advance in it).
The game doesn't become harder when you advance as all enemies are always in your level.
All of the gates that you need to close looks very similar, and after the third gate you feel like you done this before.
Long review : [http://www.rpgcodex.net/content.php?id=129](http://www.rpgcodex.net/content.php?id=129)
Now don't get me all wrong, Oblivion had a very good things that I liked, but it's NOT such a good game as I/you first thought.

Anyway, think about it next time you play RPG games.

### Post by eljoeb on 2008-02-17
Fallout 1 and 2, Planescape: Torment, Gothic, and Arcanum (sorta) are all good RPGs.  The Elder Scrolls series was last worthwhile with Daggerfall. ### Post by Nevon on 2008-02-17
According to me, a good RPG doesn't necessarily have to have multiple endings that you can influence depending on your choices. ### Post by kidux on 2008-04-06
I think some of you are mixing classes of rpgs. Standalone CRPGs are not going to provide the amount of choice that you can get from a persistant world MMORPG or PnP, even with downloadable content a la Neverwinter Nights. An RPG, IMHO, puts me in total control of a character or party, allowing me to customize everything about that character. MMOs don't usually have a story to be completed from beginning to end like a standalone, and allow a much more open feeling of play. 

The Elder Scrolls series grants this feeling quite well, with an incredible amount of side quests that can be performed. I think the OP's complaints about being able to belong to any guild and excel is ludicrous as that lack of class limitation opens up so many customization options to be nearly impossible to count. The same with the complaint about leveling up during the speechcraft minigame. This tells me the OP doesn't really understand the mechanics of the game and is lashing out in frustration about it. It makes complete sense that every action you can and do perform be governed by a skill and stat, and The Elder Scrolls captures that wonderfully.

IMO, given the ground rules put forth by the OP > RPG's it's not all about fighting monsters and gaining levels.
RPG's are about choices you can make to develop your character and it's personality.
RPG's are about good story that you can really influence and change the outcome of the game.
It's about dialogs.
It's about customizing your characters masteries and skills.
It's about good quests that you have multiply choices to solve them. it could be argued that Dues Ex is an excellent RPG. In that game, and it's sequel, you can modify your stats and abilities, either through leveling up or augmentation. You also have multiple dialog options as well as multiple quest resolution options. They also had fantastic story that flowed with the choices the character made with multiple endings. Based on this, I would say these 2 games are the epitome of RPG, but sadly they are not. While they include these elements in a big way, the focus of the game remains on strategic FPS combat.

IMO, Planescape:Torment, while ultimately having the story revolve around the PC is an excellent example of an RPG. To a lesser extent, the Baldur's Gate series of games are also outstanding RPGs. 

While genre mixing is becoming more prevalent, I feel it can yield some very good RPGs. For instance Jade Empire is a fantastic RPG, allowing the PC to choose different paths through the story by not only introducing great dialog options, but also through a persons actions and giving multiple endings based upon the choices made by the PC.
