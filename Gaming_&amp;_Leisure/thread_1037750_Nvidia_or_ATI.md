### Post by Epsilon on 2009-01-13
The fact of the matter is; if you go out and buy a new graphics card you'll want to get an nvidia one, because they're the only company that supports Linux with drivers that actually perform and are compatible in most scenarios.

If you go out and get a 4800 card from Ati/AMD you'll have to use the proprietary drivers that have a wealth of problems like: 

Fan control 
these cards have a tendency to overheat, especially the 4870x2 kind

Compatability issues
Try running a 3d application in wine using SM 2.0 and up on an Ati card, it'll crash

OpenGL 
3.0 instruction set is lacking right now in the proprietary drivers, which makes it a pain to use it, when you're coding for it.

Poor compositing support
Kde 4.1 and up will in many scenarios crash your xserver, compiz overlays on top of other 3d application will most likely crash as well on 8.12

Poor performance compared to Windows
The same card will most of the time perform as much as 60% better on Windows, on Nvidia cards the drivers are generally equal to their Windows counterparts.

All of these issues makes buying a new graphics card from Ati a huge hassle and not something you'd want in a production environment.
Ideals means nothing when you want to get your job done or your stuff to work. Same thing can be said about games, when you have time to play games and feel like it, you should be able to do so. And not have to jump through hoops only to have your xserver crash.
I realise that many of these issues plagued Nvidia as well, but that was in the past and this is now.

If you go out and get a geforce 280gtx or even the new 295 gtx, theres drivers ready for it and it'll basically work out of the box, and perform as you expect - that is also how it should be.

### Post by Melcar on 2009-01-13
Both nvidia and ATI have proprietary drivers.  Both also have FOSS drivers, but while ATI cards can count with 2 that offer both 2D and 3D on most cards, nvidia only has 1 that just offers 2D (and I heard vesa is faster for the most part).  Most people that care about 3D performance use proprietary drivers, so all this matters little.
In terms of raw 3D performance, both ATI and nvidia driver sets (proprietary) offer near identical performance when it comes to Windows vs. Linux.  I'm talking cross platform applications here like open source games and the like.  Wine doesn't really count because it introduces lots of overhead and most 3D applications are really a hit or miss when it comes to usage.  Still, nvidia drivers have less problems with Wine, so if that's what you use most of the time, then by all means get an nvidia card.
No opengl3.0 support for fglrx yet, but it should come in soon.  However, this is not a totally bad thing right now since not everything is using opengl3.0.  Average home users need not worry about it that much.  However, if you do work with opengl3.0, then yeah, nvidia is your only choice right now.
As for composition, Compiz works beautifully on ATI drivers (except for mipmaps :mad:), as well as KDE4 being smooth as silk.  I have heard more people complaining about how slow the nvidia drivers are with KDE4 than anything else.
