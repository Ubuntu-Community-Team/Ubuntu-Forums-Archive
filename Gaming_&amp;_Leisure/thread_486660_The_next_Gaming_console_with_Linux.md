### Post by leech on 2007-06-28
It would have been cool if the Indrema had ever taken off.  It was in essence a Linux-based gaming console.  Rather than something like the PS3 or Xbox, where you can just install Linux on it and use it to varying degrees.  But their backing fell through or they never really had much more of a prototype than some PC parts cobbled together to be a 'game' system.  The idea was pretty cool behind it though.  

The problem with something like this though is that game consoles are even more competitive than computer operating systems or video cards.  For the same reasons that there aren't fully open sourced video drivers for AMD / ATI and nVidia cards, there won't be a Linux that is fully capable for the hardware of gaming consoles.  PS3 is probably the closest you'll get without the system being completely an open spec.  I certainly wish we had more players in the video game market.  It's turned into basically two mega-corporations duking it out, and Nintendo trying their hardest to stay in the game.

Though really I think with the exception of the time just before the playstation one came out, it's always been three game consoles.  Back then at least you had the choices of the 3DO, Atari Jaguar, Sega Saturn, Turbo Grafx, Neo Geo, etc.  Now it's basically just Sony, Nintendo and Microsoft.  Personally I think the last one shouldn't even be in the game console market and instead Sega should have stuck around, or even Atari (but of course those damned Tramiels.... )

If you are just talking about a console that works also as a Linux work station (like PS3 can be) then don't worry, some wily hacker will port it to pretty much everything that has some sort of CPU.  They even have Lunix which works on 8-bit computers!
