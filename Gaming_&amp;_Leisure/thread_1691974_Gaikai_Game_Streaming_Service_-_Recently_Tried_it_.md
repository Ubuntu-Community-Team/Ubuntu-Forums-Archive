### Post by gerowen on 2011-02-20
So I "rarely" click those links on Facebook that ask you to play a game, but I saw one today that said, "Play Mass Effect 2 in your browser" so I was like, what the hell, let's give it a shot.  Well it turns out it's a company called Gaikai ([www.gaikai.com](www.gaikai.com)) that is working on a game streaming service.  I'm not advertising for them, but I just found this and thought it was really cool.  Linux gamers might actually be able to participate in PC gaming!  Lol.  You buy games from them and stream them through a Java applet in your favorite web browser.  I played through Mass Effect 2 on a system that can NOT handle the game, and managed to maintain a decent framerate with some nice video effects in game.  I cranked down the audio/video quality in the screencast to keep my computer from overheating, but you may notice some slight graphical hitches caused by me trying to record while I play.  If you manage to get into one of their Beta games you'll be greeted with a test of your internet connection speed, and by the wording on the page you may get told to erf off if it's not fast enough, at least until they get out of Beta.  I know I've got a pretty beefy connection and although I had zero latency, the game was compressed into I "believe" 720p, not sure I didn't do any measuring.

The game ran flawlessly on my laptop with these specs:
AMD Turion X2 64 Processor @ 2.1 Ghz
4 GB RAM
256 MB ATI Radeon 3100 Graphics
Ubuntu Linux 10.10 64bit

Textures are pretty detailed, even if it was compressed into a portion of the window.
