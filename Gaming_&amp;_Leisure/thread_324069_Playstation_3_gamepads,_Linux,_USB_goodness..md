### Post by leech on 2006-12-23
Ok, so just out of curiosity, I tried out my Thrustmaster Dual Firstorm Power 3 USB gamepad on my brother's shiny new Playstation 3.  And what do you know, it works... well sort of.

The odd thing with it is that it behaves the exact same way as it does in Windows when I'm playing a game that is an obvious playstation / xbox port.

For example, in Marvel Ultimate Alliance, all the buttons work, except that the right analog only works with the left / right axis which is reversed (basically instead of moving the camera left / right, it'd zoom in and out which is supposed to be the up / down) and buttons 1 and 2 are reversed.  Fortunately in this game I could still customize my controls, so I could get the up / down to zoom in and out and the left / right to rotate.  But I still never could get buttons 1 and 2 to actually exchange properly.

Now it's the same thing with using it on the Playstation 3.  Except now the disadvantage is that most games don't let you customize your controls quite as extensively.  For example, we were playing Resistance : Fall of Man.  

While the controller worked, I couldn't look left and right, but I could look up and down (going left or right which is really weird.)  I eventually did exchange the right and left analog sticks so that I looked with the left and moved with the right.  But I could only move forward (going left) and backward (going right).  And again, the buttons to one and two were reversed.  

Now I know I took this thing apart at one point, but I'm pretty sure I put it back together correctly, and I think I had this problem since I got it.  Even when I go into the control panel under Windows and the jscalibrator in Linux, I pus the right buttons and it shows which ones I'm pushing, so I don't think it's that.  I'd have to do more tests though.

But this does look promising for several reasons.

Firstly, it means that most any USB gamepad will work on the playstation 3.  Including of course keyboards, mice, etc.  To specify, this actually does work on the Non-Linux installed Playstation 3.  So it uses standard USB HID programming.  This is also enhanced by the fact that my gamepad works the exact same as it does in Windows.  This should mean that you could take any gamepad (assuming it has the same amount of buttons / controls) and stick it into your Playstation 3 and use it.

This also means, and has already been done, that you can use your Playstation 3 controller on your Linux computer without any modifications.  The only problem with this is that they haven't gotten the wireless to work yet.  I think it uses Bluetooth, and most Desktop PCs don't have Bluetooth for obvious reasons.

This is quite cool news.  It means that the PS3 could not only instantly have a lot of various accessories already out on the market.  But it also means that a lot of the PS3 accessories will be easily used on anything with a USB port.  I'm pretty sure just simple driver tweaking for most things will be all that's required.

On the other hand getting the motion sensing stuff out of the Sixaxis controllers might be a bit more difficult, but seeing as how not much under linux even supports Forcefeedback yet, it's probably too early to even bother with that.  

Here's to the possibilities here.  I may just get a PS3 controller to play games on my own computer, plus to play multi-player with my bro.

Leech
