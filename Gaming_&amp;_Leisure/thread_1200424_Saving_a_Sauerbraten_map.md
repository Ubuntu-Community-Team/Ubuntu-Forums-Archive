### Post by Rhubarb on 2009-06-30
I don't do much sauer map editing myself, so I can't say (however I do play it quite a bit).

Have a look here about saving sauerbraten maps:
[http://www.sauerbraten.org/docs/editref.html#savemap](http://www.sauerbraten.org/docs/editref.html#savemap)

It looks like it saves to packages/base by default, you may be able to change the behavoir of this, but I recommend changing the permissions of your packages/base directory to allow write access.

Also, if you installed sauerbraten from the repos, I recommend you to uninstall it and grab the latest version of sauerbraten - which is newer than the version in the repos.
If you run the latest version of sauerbraten locally, you won't encounter any issues with permissions.

Also, if you make your sauerbraten map with the latest version of sauerbraten, you can share your map with the rest of the world at [http://www.quadropolis.us/](http://www.quadropolis.us/)

Happy mapping!
