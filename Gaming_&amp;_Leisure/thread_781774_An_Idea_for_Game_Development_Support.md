Now as I understand it, Canonical supports Ubuntu monetarily.

What if we created a non-profit foundation to support the development of open-source games.  There are many ways to get grants to support the foundation as well as individual donations.

The foundation would then pool the money and select projects to support, i.e. maybe hire developers, maybe one per game.  It might just allow people to work on it full-time, i.e. give them health insurance and a paycheck.

Getting the non-Profit status would be key because then people could donate to it as if it were a charity and take the tax write off.  This kind of thing is done to support artists and they sell their work, the games that are being made Open Source are free for all.
