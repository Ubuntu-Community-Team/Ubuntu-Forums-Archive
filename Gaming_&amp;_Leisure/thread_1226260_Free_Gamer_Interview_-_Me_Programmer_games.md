I conducted [the 2nd Free Gamer interview]("http://freegamer.blogspot.com/2009/07/interview-stephen-carlyle-smith-aka.html"), this time with Stephen Carlyle-Smith aka Me_Programmer games, who gives some good advise on game development as well as introduces you to some of his games.

He has created [20 FOSS games]("http://forum.freegamedev.net/index.php?t=msg&th=2741&start=0"), most of which you probably won't have seen before because he puts very little effort into generating publicity for them.

If you have any feedback for his games, post it in the [Me_Programmer forum]("http://forum.freegamedev.net/index.php?t=thread&frm_id=51") where he'll see it (don't think he'll read it here).

### Post by charlieg on 2009-07-29
Harsh to say uninspired, Passenger and Laser Tactics are pretty original.

Also it is pretty moronic to use the 'Java' as a put down.  There's plenty of very well written Java stuff out there.  Peruse [jMonkeyEngine](http://www.jmonkeyengine.com/) for a sample.  FreeCol is Java.  Or Jake2.  Or... I don't have time to start an exhaustive list.

I do agree however that he needs to make his games run more smoothly and improve on their experiences.  They could be better than they are, for sure.  I think he just needs a bit of player feedback though.  When you create a lot and get ignored it can be easy to shrug your shoulders and move on to something that you enjoy.  But fixing, iteratively improving code can be tedious.
