### Post by blueturtl on 2010-01-07
Randomness is such a tricky thing in games...

Let's look at the two big titles in the RTS genre:

**Command & Conquer:**
Units can fire but miss, guided missiles can hit friendly troops by accident, tanks can run over ground troops... How much damage a unit takes depends on wether it's moving while being fired upon... etc. You get the idea.

**StarCraft**:
Any unit that fires will hit it's target. Even if target moves outside firing range. Loss in hitpoints is a calculation of shields and armor vs. firing power. Big units (like siege tanks) can't run over small units (like zerglins).

As a result of these differences the games play completely differently.
