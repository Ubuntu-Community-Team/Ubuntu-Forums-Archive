### Post by Melcar on 2009-04-04
DX (D3D if you want to do accurate comparisons) is geared towards gaming and other desktop type graphical applications, while opengl is geared towards the workstation market (in short, it's not a gaming API).  You also get the relative "stability" and fast evolving nature of DX over the (in comparison) stagnant opengl; this is due to DX being at the sole mercy of MS while any advances in opengl have to go through a council of supporters before any change can be made (and as we saw with opengl3.0, they are not too keen on rapid changes to the API).
