### Post by lysander6662 on 2017-02-17
The short answer to this is 'yes'. 

The longer answer is that I had wanted to move to Linux for many years but gaming was always a sticking point that prevented me from leaving Windows. I have about 108 games on Steam - and only about 42 apparently work in Steam for Linux [though I think in reality it's less than this] - but the games that it does include are enough to make me move over, Portal, Hotline Miami, particularly Left 4 Dead 2. That game has been so important to my gaming interest that if it were supported I may not have even moved. 

Another reason is that these days I don't game as much as I used to. My academic life has taken ahold and if I play games for any length of time I feel like I should be doing something else. During holidays I kind of have to 'force' myself to relax and enjoy them. Also, these days, with the proliferation of retro/pixel games, many of which are not demanding on graphical hardware, moving to Linux is not a hard choice. I am waiting for the new Tomb Raider to be supported, but seeing as the first one in the reboot franchise was, and they use the same engine [I think], I'm happy to wait.

### Post by ravalox on 2017-03-01
In 2003 I decided I was simply spending a lot of my free time coming home and playing games on Windows and not really learning anything- so I formatted my computer and put linux on it.  If I was going to play games I would have to learn to install them on linux/build them from source.  I became a convert immediately- the virtual desktops and layouts were designed for a guy like me.  I was a gentoo user for a while until I had a hard drive failure- days of compile time were lost, so I switched to Ubuntu.  The rise of steam on linux has been a huge boon for a guy like me- so many games, I don't know where to even start.  I did create a windows partition recently to accommodate the new Doom; I couldn't say no to a game that is part of a series that defines my formative computer gaming years.  The Doom and Quake franchises have a multiplayer component that made them the definitive games of my school days and I can't ignore a new one... and the new one didn't dissappoint, incredible.
