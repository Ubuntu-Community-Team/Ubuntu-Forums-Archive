### Post by cogadh on 2007-09-22
Let's see, "The ATi drivers themselves are better then nVdia ones are"... unequivocably wrong. The current crop of drivers made by ATI may be an improvement, but they only work with a limited number of card models. The Nvidia drivers on the other hand work with every card they make and work very well. The simple fact is, the Nvidia drivers just plain work, while the ATI drivers have never been able to make that claim. Just ask anyone who has tried to do any serious 3D gaming in Linux or run Beryl/Compiz with an ATI card. They have to jump through hoops to get anything to work, while the Nvidia users just have to do one thing; install the drivers. Additionally ATI has historically had the worst OpenGL support, both in Linux and Windows. Just look what happened when Doom 3 was released; ATI had to release an emergency update for the drivers due to the fact that no ATI card was capable of running OpenGL properly and failed to run the game. Nvidia cards/drivers on the other hand had no problems at all with the game.

The simple fact is, the new ATI drivers and release of the source code to Open Source is great news, but there is absolutely no indication **yet** that this will mean better or even usable drivers for ATI cards. On the other hand, Nvidia's drivers, while still closed source, function just as beautifully as they always have. 

Venom, I appreciate your enthusiasm for ATI, but I think your loyalty and exuberance is a bit misplaced and has definitely not been earned by ATI yet. The fact that ATI has made a move towards improved Linux support is a good thing, but until we see some actual results from this move, your claims of ATI's superiority are extremely premature and unwarranted.

EDIT - Oh, and the fact that AMD bought and now wholly owns ATI means that ATI is now AMD. The ATI name only exists as a brand of AMD products, nothing more.
