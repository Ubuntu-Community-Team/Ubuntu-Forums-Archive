### Post by grossaffe on 2009-01-24
well I have no idea about what you are using, but I do know what you SHOULD be using... SNES controllers!

if your computer has a parallel port in it, you can build an SNES to parallel adapter to use SNES controllers on your computer (up to 5 of 'em).  that's what I use for my earlier emulators.

### Post by DirtDawg on 2009-01-24
First, you may want to try [snes9x-gtk]("http://www.happypenguin.org/show?snes9x-gtk"). It may or may not detect your gamepad, but I find it more pleasant to use than zsnes all-around so it's worth a try.

If you want help with your phillips controller, maybe if you post the model number or name it may be easier to find information. 

Otherwise, I use a [Saitek gamepad]("http://www.amazon.com/dp/B000AL703E/ref=asc_df_B000AL703E693319?smid=A2YLYLTN75J8LR&tag=shopzilla_rev_1331-20&linkCode=asn") I found at a local store that was relatively cheap ($20) and works very nice with Ubuntu.

### Post by freddychicken on 2009-01-24
[SIZE=2]Figured it out. Extremely simple.

All I had to do was go into ZSNES, click on the CONFIG tab, click on INPUT, Select device,then click SET KEYS and assign keys by following the message boxes...

In this case, my controller was KEYBOARD/GAMEPAD. Imagine that. :)

The controller specs:
PHILIPS
PC Controller Retractable
SGC2909BB/27
[/SIZE]  [SIZE=2][SIZE=2]
To be honest it isn't Grade A in quality but it is getting the job done. The price was around $15. [/SIZE]
