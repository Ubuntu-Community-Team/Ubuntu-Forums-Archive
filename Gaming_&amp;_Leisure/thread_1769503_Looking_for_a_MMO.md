### Post by Mozai on 2011-05-28
Three Rings Design makes two MMOs that are pure Java, thus they run fine on Linux.  
 - [Yohoho Puzzle Pirates]("http://www.puzzlepirates.com/") umpteen puzzle minigames used for dueling, crafting new gear, and sailing ships.  When you crew a ship, everyone takes a station where they must perform well at their minigames to give the captain the tokens needed for her navigation/tactics minigame.  Once the ships come broadside, everyone duels in a pvp puzzle-fighter game.
 - [Spiral Knights]("http://spiralknights.com/") it's Gauntlet II the MMO.  Action-based, up to four players cooperate to clear out successive levels of clockwork tunnels under the artificial planet's surface, hoping to find the enigmatic core that powers it all.

Both games are "freemium" -- free to play, though you can trade real money for premium items in-game.

I've been enjoying Spiral Knights on Ubuntu 11.04.  High framerate, clear sound, responsive controls.

### Post by brencameron on 2011-05-30
I will suggest [Grand Fantasia]("http://wiki.aeriagames.com/grandfantasia/index.php?title=Main_Page"). If you have your video card drivers enabled, it runs perfectly in Wine (i.e. no GameGuard to mess you up), the graphics are anime/light hearted, and the world seems small but fun nevertheless. It has several things to do, several classes to try out, and is free with an optional cash shop. I find that I can drop in, play around or grind for a few hours, and leave it for days without feeling like I miss out on stuff.
Check out videos on Youtube for it, and if it appeals to you, give it a try.

### Post by Hwæt on 2011-05-30
[RuneScape]("http://www.runescape.com") is pretty grind intensive, but it's a pretty fun game imho. To get it working you'll have to install IcedTea, OpenJDK, and run it while in Ubuntu Classic (No Effects). As Compiz Fusion, the successor to Beryl, will still do a number on OpenGL. There's no installation required other than those Java packages, though. It's all browser based, with the option to pay to upgrade to a member's account which opens the free to play version into a much larger game.

[The Mana World]("http://themanaworld.org") Is a pretty decent 2D MMO. There's a lot of grinding in this one, but it's Linux Native and entirely Open Source. I believe it's in the Ubuntu Software Center under either tmw or The Mana World.
