If this is just for fun great and excuse my doubt of getting anything accurate using wine to do benchmarks or for that matter even if 3dmark ran natively in Linux, 3dmark has had a very shady track record to say the least and mostly due to ati and nvidia trying to improve 3dmark scores by adding optimizations to drivers so either ATI or Nvidia can look like the champ with 3dmark. (this was a long time ago I don't know if nvidia and ati are still playing that silly game with 3dmark)  I could go on and on but I will be alittle more helpful (if really intested in doing some benchmarks)

There very few GPU benchmark utilities for Linux and I know of none that do overall system benchmarks.

The ones I know of are:
UT2003 and UT2004 in game time demo


Doom 3 in game time demo



UMark ut2k series benchmark 
[http://ut2k3botbench.sourceforge.net/index.php](http://ut2k3botbench.sourceforge.net/index.php)
Cons: Have to have UT2k series,But it could be something great! Also they are no longer around

AnandTech framegetter
[http://www.anandtech.com/linux/showdoc.aspx?i=2229&p=2](http://www.anandtech.com/linux/showdoc.aspx?i=2229&p=2)
Cons: Primitive results (things are saved in bmp images and txt files and fps are not averaged) and nobody uses it so finding user results to compare n/a  Not maintained anymore. But it could be something great!

GL O.B.S.
[http://globs.sourceforge.net/about.php](http://globs.sourceforge.net/about.php)
Cons: Limited number of Open GL tests no standard test (have to use the demos, unless you write your own) But it could be something great!
