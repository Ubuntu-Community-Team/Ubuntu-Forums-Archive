### Post by KIAaze on 2010-05-12
While stealth does play a role in Lugaru, it's not effective enough since there aren't any one-hit killing/disabling moves.
In the first levels you can kill some enemies directly by throwing a knife or kicking them while they are sleeping, but after that they start wearing armor or are more resistant (wolves).
In that case, throwing one knife into their back won't kill them instantly.
And there are no up close instant killing/disabling moves like in Splinter cell or Hitman. So unless you're lucky (or good enough), you won't be able to directly punch/kick them KO even if you managed to sneak up directly up to their back.
There also aren't any good ways to hide or distract the enemy and the goal is always to kill everybody.

Here are some other games which might fit into the stealth genre:
OpenClou: [http://sourceforge.net/projects/openclou/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/openclou/) (haven't tried it yet)
Adrian: [http://code.google.com/p/adrian/](http://code.google.com/p/adrian/) (Commandos-like)

### Post by KIAaze on 2010-05-13
Get help here: [http://forum.freegamedev.net/](http://forum.freegamedev.net/) :)

The easiest is probably to use the programming language you know best (if you already know one).
However, if there is an appropriate game engine in another language and you have programming experience, you could also switch to the corresponding language.

For a 2D game, using python might be a good choice (or the Löve engine). It's easy and fast.
For 3D, probably C++, altough 3D engines also exist for others now.

If you're going for games like thief or deus-ex, using an RPG-FPS engine like Cube makes most sense I think:
[http://cubeengine.com/](http://cubeengine.com/)
[http://sauerbraten.org/](http://sauerbraten.org/)
[http://sourceforge.net/projects/eisenstern/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/eisenstern/) (RPG project based on the Cube engine)
[http://sandboxgamemaker.com/](http://sandboxgamemaker.com/) ("3D game maker" based on the cube engine)

More useful links:
[http://freegamedev.net/wiki/Main_Page](http://freegamedev.net/wiki/Main_Page)
[http://freegamedev.net/wiki/Free%2C_cross-platform%2C_real-time_3D_engines](http://freegamedev.net/wiki/Free%2C_cross-platform%2C_real-time_3D_engines)
