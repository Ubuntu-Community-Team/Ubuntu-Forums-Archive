### Post by casperiv on 2006-11-15
I have been reading posts about people being banned for using 3rd party automation software such as bots, but today my wifes account got banned.  She has not been using a bot, all she did every day was log in as her mage, kill people in battle grounds for a few hours, and log out.  That's it.  So, of course I emailed Blizzard, but I started searching.  I came across several posts about accounts being banned for being run under wine with any kind of configuration changes.

This is what I have been able to figure out, if you program your keyboard, or use anything that can directly interface with the application, the Warden system in World of Warcraft will flag you as using a bot.  There is an site [here]("http://infernix.net/wowban/") where another linux uses talks about being banned for a similar instance.

It is interesting that my wifes account happened to be banned after I had loaded her account on my laptop, which I am on right now and is running Ubuntu.  Know, I don't know for sure why her account got banned, but the message said "Third party automation software" and that seemed kind of like this same issue to me.

If by some one in a million chance blizzard responds I'll be sure to post it up here.  In the mean time, I hope you don't piss warden off as well.
