### Post by Vadi on 2008-06-28
I don't know if anyone's heard of this, but one russian company is working on a cross-platform (windows and linux, 32bit and 64bit, even may work on consoles) game engine.

I just heard about it today because it'll get included as a benchmark in the [PTS]("http://www.phoronix-test-suite.com/"), but check out their [features]("http://unigine.com/products/unigine_v0.4/") page. I was really amazed - hope to see some games come out using this soon. Cross-platform engines will really help linux out as they remove the possibility of the game devs wanting to go cross-platform but the engine not allowing them.

Oh, and they have a demo too - if you can't wait for it to get included in the PTS, download both .zip archives, move "data" into the linux one, and run it. Check out all the neat filters they have too.
