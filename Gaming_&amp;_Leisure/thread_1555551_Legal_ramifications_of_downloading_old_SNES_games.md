### Post by ronnielsen1 on 2010-08-18
> **Legal status**

 ROMs themselves are not illegal per se. This section gives a general  discussion of the legal status of ROMs as regards the various uses to  which they may be put, though this should not be construed as legal  advice.
 **[[edit]("http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=ROM_image&action=edit&section=10")] Private ownership**

 In some countries, it is legal for an individual to personally make  backup copies of a game they own. Individuals may make backup copies for  various reasons, perhaps as insurance against losing the game or as  redundancy in the event that the original game's medium becomes  unreadable. See the section on ROMs and Preservation.
 However, in the U.S. it has been illegal since 1983 for a user to  create their own backups of video game ROMs onto other cartridges. This  was decided in the [court case of Atari v. JS&A]("http://people.bu.edu/ebortman/index/atari-js&a.htm").  JS&A manufactured a "game backup" device that allowed users to dump  their Atari ROMs onto a blank cartridge. JS&A argued that the  archival rule allowed for this. The court disagreed, noting that ROM  media was not subject to the same volatility as magnetic media (for  which the law was created). Thus, not being so relatively vulnerable,  ROMs were not applicable under section 17 USC 117(a)(2).
 Some games companies, such as [Nintendo]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo"),  print warnings inside their game manuals that they do not allow users  to make backup or archival copies. Whether or not these warnings in this  specific form can be considered valid [contracts]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contract") is legally questionable. For an overview of relevant issues, see [user agreement (EULA)]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_license"), [shrink wrap contract]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shrink_wrap_contract"), [clickwrap]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clickwrap"), [Fair Use]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_Use"), [Fair Dealing]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_Dealing") and [DMCA]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DMCA").
