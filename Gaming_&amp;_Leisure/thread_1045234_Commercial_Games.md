### Post by Melcar on 2009-01-20
I think the current reasons people are not willing to pay more than a few bucks (or even pay at all) for Linux games are the following:

- Most software on Linux is free.  This gives people the wrong impression that everything *should* be free

- Lets be honest, Linux games don't look even half as good as AAA Windows titles.  Nowadays looks are what sell

- Most commercial games are ports from older Windows titles.  Chances are gamers already have those titles, so why pay extra for something you already have?

- The uncertainty of compatibility.  Even if the box says "Linux compatible", there are many distro flavors out there, most sporting their own configurations.  Making a binary that is certain to work on every (or at least most) distros seems very hard.  Why would people pay a premium if they have no assurance that the game will "just work" on their system?
