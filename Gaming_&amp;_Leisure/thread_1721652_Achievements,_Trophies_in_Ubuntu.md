### Post by donkyhotay on 2011-04-07
There are some practical concerns, first of all where would this be hosted? Also, most linux games are FOSS which means mods and tweaks are common. How would you determine achievements? Beating wesnoth without losing a single unit would seem like a great achievement however if people can just tweak the source to make their units invincible then it doesn't mean anything. Many people enjoy "cheating" in single player but when you involve achievements you can't really do that. Don't get me wrong, the idea has some potential and might help bring the FOSS gaming community closer together but I don't think it would work.

### Post by Furyhunter on 2011-04-08
Personally, I think achievements are a bit of a plague in gaming. Centralized systems are too prone to having achievements that are "too easy" to obtain, and the concept of achievements period eliminates the way traditional games allowed you to set your own personal goals.

Since Linux is a platform of diversity, games are better off left to implement their own "achievements" so that they're tailored to their own needs. I don't particularly see the need for a centralized community in the way Steam, XBL, etc. provide.
