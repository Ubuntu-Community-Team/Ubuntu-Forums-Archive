### Post by JoshuaRL on 2009-10-20
Here's my review:

Well, since 2D Boy decided to follow the lead of Thom Yorke and Trent Reznor, I decided to buy the much-lauded World Of Goo.  I bought it for $5 since I have no money but didn't want to miss out on this.  Anything less seemed like an insult.

I'm not even halfway through it and I have to say it's one of my favorite games.  Almost every level makes you start fresh and question your ideas about how things work or the best way to do something.  Just in messing around in the free building area I've figured out 3 different build designs that all have their own strengths and weaknesses.

It just runs perfectly on Linux, no framerate issues or clunkiness.  I was wondering how the sound would work, as I was reading about the issues they had with it while the port was in progress, but I haven't had any problems at all.  The game just runs excellently.

All in all, I have to say that this game is incredible.  The end level for the first chapter blew me away when I finally figured out the last piece of the puzzle and the cutscene started.  It is easily worth the typical price of $20.  But being as they are giving it to you for whatever you want, go forth and reward cross development.  Let them know you appreciate developers that care about our operating system.  Once I get paid again, I'll probably kick another $10-15 their way.

Verdict:  Definitely buy.
