### Post by LillyDragon on 2012-06-09
There's virtually no effort (On your part.) in getting most controllers to run on Linux, if the system can recognize it. The kernel already has more controller drivers than Windows does naturally. [_You could even just wire up a normal XBOX controller to a USB connector and expect it to run automatically without installing drivers beforehand._]("http://www.redcl0ud.com/xbox_wiring.html") The only annoyance here is finding a Joystick testing app that works for you. Personally, I like how straight-forward jstest-gtk is, and that can be found in the Software Center.

As for controller recommendations, the wired Xbox 360 controllers are a pretty penny, *but hot dang are they ergonomic.* They're probably the most comfortable controllers I've ever held, and the more sensitive analog sticks are definitely smoother than those last gen console controllers. PS3 controllers are pretty nice too. If the price tag of either is a tad steep, even for used units, generic USB controllers, like the kind at RadioShack, feel pretty okay and just work. (Personally, generic just translates into "blank canvas" to me, I enjoy customizing them specifically! :P )
