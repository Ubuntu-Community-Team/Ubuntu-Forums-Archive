### Post by dfreer on 2007-12-07
AFAIK, you can't play any iTunes DRM files in ubuntu. Although you can always burn them to a CD and rip them back as DRM-Free MP3's. 

There is also an option for some songs on iTunes, called "iTunes Plus", to be purchased as DRM-Free AAC files, which work fine in ubuntu. You can even upgrade previously downloaded music to Plus for the price difference (like $0.20?).

You can't currently download music from iTunes Store in ubuntu either, unless you use a virtual install of windows (which really isn't downloading music "in" ubuntu anyways). There are free and DRM-free solutions out there, namely Amazon's new MP3 service, but none with the selection of iTunes Music Store.

My 5th Gen iPod video works great with amarok, although I haven't tried putting videos on it. I do believe it is possible using another program, though, like gtkpod.
