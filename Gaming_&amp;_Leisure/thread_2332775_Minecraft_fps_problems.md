### Post by sgian on 2016-08-19
If you are still around, there are several things to check when your fps is capped at 60 fps.  Minecraft on Ubuntu and Mint can do many more fps than that.

1. video settings in minecraft: turn vsync off and adjust your framerate to more than 60 fps.  Sometimes it is set too low by default on linux for some reason.
2. make sure you use Oracle Java, and not OpenJDK.  This makes a huge difference.  Here is a good way to do it... [https://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/java](https://sites.google.com/site/easylinuxtipsproject/java) or you could use the webup8 repository
3. use the proprietary drivers, I haven't had much success with open source drivers on minecraft
