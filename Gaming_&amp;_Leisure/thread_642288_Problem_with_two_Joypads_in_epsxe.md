### Post by acoustibop on 2007-12-16
LOL!  ePSXe has been out of development for four years, Morientes. pSX is still in (very much - see the [official forum]("http://psxemulator.proboards54.com/")) active development, and is the only Playstation emulator available for Linux that is.

The difference is that PCSX and ePSXe are aimed at enhancement which, nice though it is, tends to impact on performance on all but the fastest systems.  pSX, however, is aimed at accurate rather than enhanced emulation.  In fact, even on very mid-range machines, pSX will run games at full speed with no slow-downs, and the accuracy of its approach makes it much more like playing on the console.

I call this "transparency," because it means the emulator doesn't come between you and the game.  The enhancing emulators, with their constant slowdowns and graphic distortion, tend to produce a playing experience that's constantly intruding on your immersion in the game.
