### Post by AirRaven on 2006-07-20
It depends what you want to run.

Personally, FLOSSing zealots aside, I find Cedega the nicer option. It's a far neater option- everything's contained from one control center. It also runs Warcraft III with the movies, and can handle Starcraft's Battle.net functionality near-perfectly. Which, since these are the two games I play the most, is what I need, and Wine can't perform as well in these respects as Cedega. The price is the only thing that I can complain about.

It really boils down to how much you're willing to pay. Wine's a jack-of-all-trades, while Cedega focuses its efforts on a few games and gets them running *well*.

If Cedega's "theft" of the Wine code offends you, then just go for vanilla Wine. It runs a lot anyway.
