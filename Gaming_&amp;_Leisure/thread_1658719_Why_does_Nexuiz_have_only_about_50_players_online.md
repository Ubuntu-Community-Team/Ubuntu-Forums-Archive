### Post by perspectoff on 2011-01-03
I played Nexuiz for about a month or two and got bored of it quickly. Yes, it's graphically excellent, but because of that it is a real memory hog. Also, an endless deathmatch really isn't that fun for me. I like some variety. Lastly, the power of your machine makes a big difference and players with better machines have an advantage.

I play net games with family and friends over the Internet, but in Nexuiz, play is easily slowed if the individual client computers are not up to the task (and not everyone has an uber-gaming rig).

I ended up going back to Skulltag (based on Doom/ZDoom), which has so many variations it is endless. Further, Skulltag is available for every platform, so my sister, cousins, nieces, kids, etc. can play on their Macs and Windows boxes the same way I play on Linux. Skulltag adapts, to some extent, the gameplay of other FPS games. So, I play Real Guns Advanced, which emulates Call of Duty in gameplay, to some degree.

Yeah, the graphics of Skulltag are nothing like those of Nexuiz (or other current FPS games), but that doesn't detract from the enjoyment for us. Everyone's computer seems to be able to handle Skulltag easily, so we are all on an equal footing (and game lag and freezes are rare). Further, starting a game server from a Linux or Windows machine is integrated into Skulltag and is trivial to set up and run.
