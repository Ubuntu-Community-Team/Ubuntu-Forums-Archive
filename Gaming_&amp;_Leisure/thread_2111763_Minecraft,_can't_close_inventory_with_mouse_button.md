### Post by Horbo on 2013-02-02
I hope this is the right forum for this thread...

If I use a keyboard key to toggle the inventory open/closed there is no problem everything works as expected.

As soon as I assign a mouse button to inventory though (any mouse button) I can open the inventory fine, but a second push of the mouse button doesn't close the inventory as it should (I have to press esc to close it).

Very weird.

Has anyone else seen this?  Have a solution, or even just an idea about it?

---

### Post by Ender Shadow on 2013-02-04
Once you open the inventory, the mouse buttons are used to move items around. That takes priority over whatever you set the mouse buttons to do. Like closing the the inventory screen.

### Post by Ender Shadow on 2013-02-15
Minecraft uses different files that handle input (e.g. keyboard, mouse etc) in Linux OS's then it does in Windows. That's probably why it's happening in Ubuntu and not Windows, and that might be why you can use a mouse button to open the inventory, but not to close it.
