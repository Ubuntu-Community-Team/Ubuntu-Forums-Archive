### Post by Rhubarb on 2006-11-28
There's a promising RPG game called Eisenstern, it's not completed yet though.  - So you'll have to wait 'till it's finished!

Eisenstern is based on the Cube2 Engine, which the FPS game Sauerbraten is currently using.
Sauerbraten comes with some Eisenstern maps.

Eisenstern is just missing some typical RPG code elements, like spells, money, trading and the like.

Everything is open source, free and cross platform.
The Cube2 engine allows you to make your own maps in-game and online with up to 32 'mappers' co-operatively making a map.
So it's got a lot going for it.  I suggest you keep an eye on the Eisenstern project.

[http://www.cubeengine.com/](http://www.cubeengine.com/)
[http://sauerbraten.org/](http://sauerbraten.org/)
[http://eisenstern.com/](http://eisenstern.com/)

### Post by leech on 2006-11-30
You want to talk emulators and RPGs.

Try the Atari ST or Amiga emulators.  They had so many good Role-Playing games, I'm not sure where to begin.

You've got of course the Dungeon Master genre of games (I sorely miss), the top down ones like Ultima, the old D&D games for Dos, etc.

Personally I never got into any of the Console RPGs (though Phantasy Star was pretty cool.)  

As far as Morrowind, I want to mention that Loki themselves did not produce the installer.  

All of the Loki installers are called that because Loki programmed a installer for all their ports and open sourced it.  Google Earth even uses it (though Google ported it to GTK2, not sure why others have not upgraded their installers to use GTK2, since GTK1.2 is starting to get phased out of distributions (hint hint Nero))

The installers for most of the games on [www.liflg.org](www.liflg.org) are non-native (including Morrowind) and they actually just use Wine.

To clarify for those wanting Cedega, you don't really have to continue your subscription.  You have to pay at least 3 months worth at $5, then you can cancel.  Otherwise, if you really wanted, you could fork over $15 and then just cancel at the end of three months.  You then get whatever version is the most current and any updates within that three month period.  Most of their updates lately haven't even really added new games anyhow, mostly they've just fixed problems that the World of Warcraft patches cause....

Leech
