### Post by verb3k on 2007-08-20
Hi ubuntuists!

I've got some topic that I want to share with you.

It's about how supporting non-Microsoft gaming consoles and platforms helps the Linux gaming world become better.

We all know that Windows and the Xbox360 use the DirectX API for games that are exclusive for Windows and/or xbox360 . Other non-Microsoft platforms and consoles like Linux, PlaySation 3 , Wii all use OpenGL . So if a developer opts to develop his game using DirectX , it will be really difficult for him to port the game to OpenGL later and make it available on other consoles. But if the developer develops his game using OpenGL it will be much easier to port later because all gaming platforms support OpenGL ( including Windows and Xbox360 ) . Now it's our turn to support  and popularize the PS3 /Wii who are the two most notable users of the OpenGL ,  becuase when we support them we make it really easy for developers to port games on Linux. Recently, Developers of Unreal Tournament 3 stated that they will release a Linux version of the game, which is  a good indicator and hopefully a start of a new trend that takes the Linux gaming market and community into consideration . I am really interested in hearing your opinion about the topic .

Cheers,
verb3k

### Post by cogadh on 2007-08-20
I'm sorry, but I can't agree at all. The only thing supporting those consoles will do is tell game makers that they can make a bigger profit on console games than they can on PC games. The PC gaming market is already in significant decline due to the now competetive power and abilities of the current generation consoles, taking support away from PC gaming and giving to the consoles will only accellerate that decline.

The only way gaming on Linux will become viable is if we can get more people to try out and use Linux for everyday things other than games, i.e. office applications, e-mail, web browsing, IM, etc. Once you get the general public to accept Linux as a viable alternative to Windows for every day use, then the games will come. After all, was it Windows gaming ability that sold the public on its use? No, it was actually MS Office that did that.
