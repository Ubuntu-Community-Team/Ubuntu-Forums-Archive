### Post by mystics on 2015-08-09
It is probably best not to assume that someone's choice of an OS will mean that they prefer a certain game. OSs and games aren't the same thing. One aids people in using computers and the other is an entertainment product. Yes, you could make an argument that Linux, in general, attracts more tech-savvy people than Windows or Mac, but being tech-savvy isn't the same as not enjoying goofy entertainment. It just means that you've put more effort into learning how to use technology than most people have, but that won't mean much for entertainment except maybe getting annoyed at all the stupid "technical" comments made in movies and TV.

### Post by efflandt on 2015-08-10
Some of us are just not into zombie apocalypse games. I was an old on-line Quake2 player from way back, so I enjoy Team Fortress 2. I do have L4D2, but there was a free weekend and I wanted to see if I could continue, they had it on sale for something like $9.95, and I got an Ellis hat and frying pan (melee weapon) for TF2 out of the deal.

I have also spent considerable time playing minecraft off and on (since it was beta), especially Tekkit mod where besides structures you can build all sorts of machines including a computer controlled nuclear power plant powering a steam turbine for power to mine for resources and oil refinery for fuel to go to the moon and Mars. I could deal with the occasional creeper blowing up and other mobs attacking, but preferred servers with anti-griefing and not PVP servers where other players could destroy or claim what you built up. I would help others build and share resources. Although, when I was on servers with no anti-griefing or inventory protection I learned how to conceal entrances and hide chests. I had to be particularly inventive when someone was using a disallowed invisibility mod, by hiding a chest under lava where I could still access it, but he could not me open it or tell exactly where it was. I have not tried Rust or Ark.

A Steam game with beautiful graphics and really makes you think is The Talos Principle. It is sort of expensive, best bought on sale. There is a free demo ("Download Demo" button) and if you expand "About This Game" there is a free mini-game to learn how to use the Sigils (which can give you some stars to help get into special levels of main game). [http://store.steampowered.com/app/257510/](http://store.steampowered.com/app/257510/)
