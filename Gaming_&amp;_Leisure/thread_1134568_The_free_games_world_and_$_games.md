### Post by Ubuntiac on 2009-04-30
The funny thing is, everyone keeps going on saying "open source games can never be as good as proprietry because devs ae addicted to windows" and things like this. When you look at what companies are *using* (not advertising) though, loads of their libraries are already open source. Ogre is used by many games to render, bullet (used in Blender's Game Engine) is actively developed by Sony and used in their games. If you want to see some real next gen power check out the open source engine driving Xreal. Of course, when these libs are used, there won't be any media fanfare because no company wants to advertise their use of them like they do if they've developed something proprietry that is now being licensed.

No, the main problem isn't on the code side.

The problem (and expense) is in the creation of assets. Sound, video, animation, textures, models, story, lighting etc. Open source has traditionally been made by coders, and as such, largely for coders (although that's getting much better). By not attracting enough contributing artists (of all descriptions including writers, sound people, visual types) we end up with a bunch of libraries, not being used to their full potential and not garnering the excitement to get more contributors and reach a critical mass.

I've seen the Nexuiz guys almost begging for artists to create new models. The Xreal guys who have arguably the most powerful open source 3d engine have exactly 2 gun meshes for their game. Two. (and they only got the second one recently). Why are there no open source games with deep engaging story lines? Because there are virtually no writers in open source! When they arrive, they're usually told to help with documentation or suchlike.

Get the tools out to the artists. Get them contributing on existing projects rather than just trying to do their own thing. Get coders used to working with artists (no, we don't all know how to use SVN, just as you don't know colour theory) and the rest is easy.

Yes, game studio's spend huge amounts to buy their slaves souls and work. Then again Microsoft spends a lot too, and we definitely have them looking over their shoulder. :)
