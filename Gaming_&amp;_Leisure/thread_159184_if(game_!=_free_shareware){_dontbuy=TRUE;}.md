Hi,  I think GW is amazingly complex, & addictive.  I have never been taken by the role playing games before GW.

The world is very large, & diverse, there are a huge variety of non player characters, races & cultures, weapons, armor, skills, dyes, runes, quests, missions. An actual story unfolds through the playing of the PvE (player verses environment) stream of the game.

Then there is the huge variety of players on line.   Some you put in your friends list & play with often.  Some may invite you to join their guild, or you may create a guild yourself & invite others to join.  Then you may help each other out, & enjoy  refining playing as a team.  

Some prefer to go into tournaments against other guilds, & pit their teams against each other.  This seperate stream of playing called PvP, (player verses player) is very sophisticated & has tournaments happening at all levels of skill, between guilds.  There is play-offs going 24hrs a day, right up to the current world champion guild, which changes multiple times a day!  You can observe tournaments if you want as well, with the ability to talk to other observers.  More sophisticated technology that I'm not going to go into here.  (Because I really want to go to bed now :))

GW caters to both types of player all in the one game.  A remarkable feat!

Highly refined technology, very reliable, rarely any down time.  Constantly being improved wherever the developers see that they can, the original GW world has been expanded during the last year, just as a bonus from the developers!

Of course just a few days ago, near enough to GW first birthday, GW Factions went live.  Now you can buy a licence for factions & expand GW,  using any & all of up to the 4 characters that GW allows you to build, & travel to a new country called Cantha.  You can also use characters that you build in factions in GW.
If you buy factions, you get 2 more character slots for a total of 6 that you can build.  You can only ever go online & use one at a time.
Factions also introduces 2 new classes of character, many new weapons, monsters & on it goes, apparently thousands of new things!!

The Factions map, is apparently only slightly smaller in size than that of the original GW.

Oh, & there is even a story going on, that has twists in it & all ( in GW for sure, & apparently it continues in Factions, but factions is also totally autonomous, you don't ever need to own GW to have a complete playing experience in Factions.)

I have not played Factions, but the online feedback in GW, is VERY positive.

So, there you go, a bit of feedback, from a GW junkie, who's only had the addiction for approx' 4 months!

There are so many great features of the game that I havn't mentioned:  The way you can teleport around the map, that opens up as you explore it.  Meaning more towns & villages become available on  your map only after you have been to them.  Once they are on your map, then you can just press the "m" on your keyboard at any time to see "your" map, & just double click on a town & go straight there.

In the towns is where all players & most merchant, crafters & what have you are.  You form teams there, often with a mixture of people on line & "henchmen" of different classes that are computer generated.

When you go through a portal, leaving the town  to start a mission/quest, the map then is only shared by your team.  Nobody can rip you off, by camping where there is a lot of loot, or interfere with your game in anyway.  Apart from whispher, team & guild chat, which you can optionally turn on or off anyway.

Anyone playing, anywhere in the world on any server, can be spoken to via the chat.  You can play with anyone who is playing the game.  Sometimes you just have to choose which town, & what server, eg. European English District - 1
(roughly)  So, you would type in, "meet you at lion's arch, d1, by the hench".  Then in less than a minute you have found the person, who is playing the game on the other side of the world & you work out what quest you want to do, whether you want to use hench or not.  Or, you may decide to go "farming" which means you go out to collect loot, by killing the oposition & picking up what they leave you when they die.
You then take your booty back to a town, & sell it to the appropriate type of merchant, for gold.

You can also go out looking for bosses, so that you can use a special skill called a signet of capture, so that when you kill the boss, you use the cap' sig', to see if the boss has any elite skills (this is the only way to get elite's) If the boss does you choose to use your cap' sig' to capture (only one) of the late boss's elite skills.  The capture signet, can only be used once. 

I just realised, that I should have just said it's a great game in my opinion, have a look at the [GuildWiki]("http://gw.gamewikis.org/wiki/Main_Page"), to find out more.

Good night! :KS
