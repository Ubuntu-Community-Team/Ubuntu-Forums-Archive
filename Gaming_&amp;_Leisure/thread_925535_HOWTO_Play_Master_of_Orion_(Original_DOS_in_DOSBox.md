### Post by Achetar on 2008-09-20
OK, I was bored and reading around some enthusiast sites and noticed how underrated the Darloks are in Master of Orion. Everybody seems to think they are evil Psilons! If that meant nothing to you, read this: [http://wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_Of_Orion](http://wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_Of_Orion). I can't explain it much better.

You can download Master of Orion (MoO) for free from here: [http://www.abandonia.com/en/games/101](http://www.abandonia.com/en/games/101). It is now free software, so this is completely legal.

It runs well in dosbox:
```

sudo apt-get install dosbox

```To start simply right-click on the file and click Open with... and select DOSBox.

OK. So who are the Darloks? The Darloks are the ultimate spies. Shapeshifters. Nice eh? They get huge spying bonuses (my favorite in any game) and their only detriment is that other races they meet are Uneasy by default instead of normal.

So, why are they looked down upon so much? In short: Play style. In order to play the Darloks you must go slow and do nothing. That sounds weird, right? You have to *only* focus on researching Computer tech (more on that later). Below is a guide on how to play the Darloks:

[SIZE=4]Colonization:[/SIZE]
The first thing you should realize is that you need to colonize other planets only if they have 90 or more population limit. Anything less and you're sunk on that world. On every world max out factories and population, then build 2+ missile bases to help prevent takeovers.

Don't try to spread out, solidify what you have. Most games you will have 2 - 4 planets until you are ready to completely annihilate your opponents (usually the one surving race).

Okay, I lied, the Darloks also reproduce more slowly than other races, though you won't find this documented. That is why I recommend solidifying your worlds.

[SIZE=4]Research:[/SIZE]
A unique thing about spying is its position in the tech tree: it has none. How good your spies are depends soley on your **Computer** tech level. With a normal race, if your Comp level is lower than that of the espionage target, your spies will probably get caught. If you are on par with them, you have roughly a 50/50 chance of success. If you are about 1&1/2 times their Comp level, you have about a 40/40/20 chance between Success/Success-Frame Another Race/Failure. Lastly, if you have double their Comp level, you will almost definately frame another race.

Why that lengthy dissertation? Why not just tell you what to do? Because the point of this is to understand the Darloks. Not Ace Impossible on them (although that will likely happen).

You should (as you probably guessed) focus on Computer technology, but not entirely. You should at the very least get **Personal Force Fields**, **Battle Suits**, and **Planetary Shields** before you begin really laying on the spies. These technologies (in accompanyment with weapons, construction and force field technologies stolen from opponents) go a *very* long way to preventing your planets from being taken over.

[SIZE=4]Combat:[/SIZE]
A very defensive and non-offensize strategy is in order until you are ready (really, really ready) to annihilate your opponent with 40 turns.

But that doesn't mean research (steal) Force Fields over Weapons. The Darloks work well with both. I prefer weapons, but I know others who have come close to beating my high score and still used Hyper-X Rockets (which will make sense if you play the game for awhile). (By high score I mean Total Tech Levels + Total Planets Owned + Total Ships + Difficulty Level (Ranked 1-5)).

Simply avoid confrontation at anything other than a world in Revolution (which won't affect Diplomacy) or one of your worlds. You can expand by capturing worlds you set into Revolutions (see Spying below). On your own worlds you should have plenty of missile bases. At the beginning of the game 2-4, mid game 20-40, end game 100+.

[SIZE=4]Spying:[/SIZE]
You should plant as many spies as possible ASAP into every empire you ecounter, whether they're doing something or not. This allows you to quickly mount an espionage assault when the time is right. On Research heavy (Psilon, for example) and Research neutral (Humans, as an example) get lots of spies doing lots of tech stealing. On everyone else, use Sabatage to hinder their normally unstoppable growth rate from taking you over before you get a chance to say "All Your Base are belong to Us!"

When stealing technologies, if a computer tech is available for theft, *always* take it. It will keep your spies one step ahead of their opponents.

When sabatoging your opponents, unless you have a specific need (such as taking out missile bases on a world with no ship-guard to prepare for invasion), go for Revolutions. If you are sabbying the Klackons (the ant guys) destroy factories, as this slows them down and is much easier than Revolution on their worlds (they are a unification, meaning you have less of chance of recruiting rebels).

[SIZE=4]Conclusion[/SIZE]
One more thing before I close: Use the **C** key. Pressing the C key allows you to see how many spies were caught this turn (both your spies and spies you caught).

I hope this guide has brought you to notice both Master of Orion and the Darloks. MoO is a great game. I still play it 10+ hours a week, and it runs on pretty much anything. The Darloks are my favorite race in this game, and their small but loyal following has done little to tell how great both the Game and the race are.
