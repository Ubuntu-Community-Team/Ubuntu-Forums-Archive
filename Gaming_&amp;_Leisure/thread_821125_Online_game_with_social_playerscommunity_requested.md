### Post by a-guitarist on 2008-06-08
As for MUDs, [http://www.forsakenlands.org]("http://www.forsakenlands.org")  FL is a great MUD, which requires Role Playing.  No ooc (out of character) channels after level 15 (50 mortal levels), and only one before that.  It has a great Imm staff, and is Rom based (but massively rewritten and retooled.)  Tons of classes and races, but not so many as you have to just randomly pick what you want.

There are quest races and classes, and a great community.  The community is best observed on their forums ([you can see them here, activation required]("http://forums.forsakenlands.org")).  You can find me on the forums there with the same handle I have here (a-guitarist).

There is Player Killing, restricted by level, and enforced role play, along with clans (called cabals), quest races and classes, along with a number of other great features.

I don't staff there, but I do play.  FL has changed hands a number of times, due to the origional owners getting "real" jobs (one with EA sports), and the next in line just moving on with their lives.  The current ones continue to balance and maintain the game, and the player base is growing.  We're consistantly in the top 10 of the Top Mud Sites' charts.  ([found here]("http://www.topmudsites.com/")).

To be perfectly honest, the learning curve is a bit steep, and a lot of the current players have been playing for six or more years.  But if you have questions, and roleplay, many people will answer them for you.  Look into the Adventurer's class if you decide to connect, as it will allow you to stay safe from PK, and learn the MUD well enough for the first thirty levels. (after which you have pick a class and start again at level one, however, all your skills/spells you've learned will carry over).  Also, read the essay's section on the forum, as there is a new character guide that is in development, along with class specific tips and hints.

I hope to see you there.

Greg
