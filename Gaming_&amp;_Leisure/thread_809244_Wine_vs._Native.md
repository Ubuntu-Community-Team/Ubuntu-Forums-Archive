### Post by wdaniels on 2008-05-27
> **Naegling23 said:**
> Do I even bother complaining that its not linux native?

Sure you do - this is precisely what we need to be doing more of. If you wait for companies to discover or project from other indicators that there is a good market for native linux games, then the whole process will take much longer if it even gathers enough momentum at all that way.

But if your objective is to increase support and investment in Linux as a gaming platform, you don't (in my opinion) just dismiss Wine compatibility as not being good enough and punish the relatively few games developers that take any initiative at all in helping people run their games on Linux; if some gaming company invests (to any extent) in providing some level of support for Linux, but there is no significant payoff plus a load of complaints that it's not native support, then I think they are probably more likely to write it off as a bad idea and not bother in future, than learn some "lesson" that they made the wrong call and should have produced a native version.

The semantics of how you want to label it exactly, as a "Linux game" or not, are much less important. It don't think it has any value to anybody, anywhere, to boil the matter down to those terms.

> **Naegling23 said:**
> I guess the reason I ask is that normally emulation adds some degradation, slowdown, or other problems to a game. However, since Wine Is Not an Emulator, there is no noticable problem with games that run properly.

There are even a handful of games that perform *better* under Wine than Windows, but they could probably perform better still without the Win32 compatibility layer, plus who's to say that they will continue to work at all after the next patch (either to the game or to Wine) so I don't think that really mitigates the case for proper native support.

Personally, I would just be happy to see us reach a point where games developers consider Wine support sufficiently valuable to test on Wine before releasing updates...that's the one thing that really bothers me - I can spend money on a 12-month subscription to a game only to find that it's broken with the next patch.
