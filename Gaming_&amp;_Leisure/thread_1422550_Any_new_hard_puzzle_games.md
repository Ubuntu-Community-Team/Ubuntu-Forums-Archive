### Post by ikacer on 2010-03-05
Could you be more specific on what type of games you are looking for? Puzzle games could cover a lot of different genres, from tetris to lemmings to chess, etc. depending on your interpretation of what a puzzle game is.

Two games worth checking out that clearly fall within the puzzle genre are Enigma and The Fool's Errand.

Enigma is really fun and pretty tough. Just be careful not to confuse it with a different game also called Enigma that uses a marble (that game is awful), the page I linked to mentions it at the bottom. Also, Chroma (which is similar) is also a fun puzzle game. These games probably aren't in the Ubuntu repos, so you'll probably have to compile them. I'm not 100% sure of that though, as I don't use Ubuntu.
[http://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/enigma/]("http://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/enigma/")

Another game you might be interested in is "The Fool's Errand". It is an old meta-puzzle game, which runs well in DOSBox. It's a classic and definitely worth a try.
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fool%27s_Errand]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fool%27s_Errand")
[http://www.thefoolandhismoney.com/01-the-fools-errand/index.htm]("http://www.thefoolandhismoney.com/01-the-fools-errand/index.htm")
