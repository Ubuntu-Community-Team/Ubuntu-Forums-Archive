### Post by detrate on 2008-08-14
Overall good video, I could picture my father enjoying a condensed view of games available for Linux.  Little slow paced for me but you did however introduce me to xmoto, I used to play a Windows equivalent.  Needless to say I had installed it even before finishing that segment.

One critique however, I think it should be noted that Open Arena is actually an incomplete remodeling/texturing project, trying to rebuild Q3A from the released code.
[quote="www.openarena.ws"]
The gameplay is identical to the game it's based on so fans of that other game should feel at home here. We do not intend to change the physics of the game. If you feel there is a change of physics of input smoothness, you can blame the ioquake3 team, not us; we just make MD3s and TGAs, compile the code and put it out there.
[/quote]

And for what it's worth, they are doing a great job.


Also, it's unfortunate that Nexuiz 2.4.2 is not in synaptic yet.  2.4 (the version you demoed) lacks many important changes and fixes.  To ensure you have the newest version of Nexuiz, I'd suggest downloading the archive from the [Nexuiz Homepage](http://www.nexuiz.com).  Just unzip and run either nexuiz*-glx or nexuiz*-sdl (whichever happens to run better on your hardware).


Tremulous seems really good... I really need to spend more time with it.  Thanks for this.
