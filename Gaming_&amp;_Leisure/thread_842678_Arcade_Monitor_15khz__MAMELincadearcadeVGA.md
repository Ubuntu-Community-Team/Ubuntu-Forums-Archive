### Post by miner49er on 2011-01-05
Yes, it is possible I managed it with an ATI Radeon card and a Matrox MGA 200 card. The one extra piece of hardware I purchased was a J-PAC ([http://www.ultimarc.com/jpac.html](http://www.ultimarc.com/jpac.html)), this is basically a device that helps hook up the joystick & buttons to save the hassle of hacking-up a keyboard. It also (I understand) filters out the incorrect monitor frequencies so you don't end up destroying your lovely CRT.

Oh, I had to hack the Matrox driver in order to get it to run at the required lower monitor refresh rate.

For the OS, I used debian net-install, so it's a very lean install. The frontend I use advancemame.

It's the best games machine I've ever owned.

### Post by mips on 2011-01-06
There are places that sell arcade monitors, just google arcade crt.
[http://forum.arcadecontrols.com/index.php?topic=92551.0](http://forum.arcadecontrols.com/index.php?topic=92551.0) this thread has links to a few manufacturers.

You get single sync, double sync, tri-sync & multisync monitors.

If you just want a 15kHz crt many people simply use crt televisions. Depending on where you live go for one with [Component]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Component_video") or [RGB]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RGB#Video_electronics")/[SCART]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SCART") input as it will give you a much better picture than composite or s-video. Get a graphics card that offers the same type off output. My XFX 9600 GT came with a Component Out Y Pr Pb adapter but I'm not sure if it does 15 kHz although I suspect it does seeing it gets connected to the same port as the s-video cable.
