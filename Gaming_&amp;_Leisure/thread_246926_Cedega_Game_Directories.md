### Post by edemark on 2006-08-30
I am not sure if you can share games in this way as you suppose as cedega installs games in your home directory. They can be found in /home/yourusername/.cedega (if cedega is 5 of later) or in the /home/yourusername/.transgaming as well as in /home/yourusername/TransGaming_Drive (if cedega is 4 or earlier)

### Post by handy on 2006-08-30
I suggest you make a /home partition, or even a /home drive if you have a spare drive laying around :) 

[Here is a great link, all about it]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome.html").

The benefits when it comes to Ubuntu upgrades, or recovering from a user or hardware failure are immeasurable...  It's not the whole story as far as backup is concerned, but it does go a long way. :KS

Yeh! I know you got more than you asked for, but it sure beats less! :rolleyes:

**[Edit:]** By the way if you ever intend to let your Cedega sub' expire, make sure you have the most recent Cedega_engine & Cedega_small files, so you can keep using what you are legally entitled to if you ever do have to do a reinstallation!  They are both allways available to subscribers in the transgaming downloads section.

By the way Cedega installs all games in a hidden folder in your /home.  There are options to change this if you have a good reason.
