### Post by gnawingonfoot on 2011-06-25
It looks like a lot of the games recommended here fall within the **roguelikes** category, which isn't all too friendly to newcomers.  These games tend to have very complex roleplaying-styled combat systems, minimal graphics (if any), and no forgiveness for player error.  If you want something to ease your way into the genre, **DoomRL** is a very newbie-friendly game for introducing some the tropes of the genre, and a step up from this is **Powder**, which is a very traditional roguelike that manages beginner-friendly playability and a nice graphical interface without dumbing down the gameplay. **Nethack** always gets recommended, but I've found it to be a very discouraging game that is not particularly well balanced.  Making progress in that game (from my understanding) usually means reading the source code or getting strategies from other people.  On the other hand, if you want a roguelike that is more complex that DoomRL or Powder but without the balance issues of Nethack (and others), **Dungeon Crawl** is probably your best bet.  The Ubuntu Software Center has packages for both ascii and tile-based versions of the game.

**Beneath a Steel Sky** is also an excellent game and much easier to learn how to play most roguelikes.  It was originally a commercial game, but the developers released it as freeware a while back.  Adventure games such as this tend to focus on story and odd combinations of items to achieve a goal.  (For example, use the steak in your inventory to distract the guard dog so you can sneak into wealthy villain's house.)

I agree, Battle for Wesnoth is a great game. Some of the missions with really high amounts of units can get sluggish though, especially on my slow system.

[Chromium B.S.U.]("http://www.reptilelabour.com/software/chromium/") is also a good game. It's a top-scrolling arcade shooter, meant to be played in short bursts. You pretty much pilot a ship and destroy other ships, but it can keep you entertained for a few minutes at a time. Really low system requirements.

[Dungeon Crawl Stone Soup]("http://crawl.develz.org/wordpress/") can also be pretty good if you're into it. It's a roguelike, so if you like Nethack or Angband, you'll probably like this too. It also has a graphical, "tiles version" available on the website if you don't want CLI based graphics.

[FreedroidRPG]("http://www.freedroid.org/") is another good game. It's an isometric RPG where you play as Tux, destroying or hacking robots and doing quests and all that. To be honest, the gameplay is fairly linear despite you being able to do many things right from the start. Hopefully the developers improve on that, which I think they will.

Regarding system requirements, I don't think they have any listed, but it worked well on my system, and apparently they've optimized the OpenGL portion of it.

Hope that helps, maybe I can think of some more. Have fun!:)
