### Post by pseudonym on 2006-09-26
Depending what game you've got, it's probably not too difficult to extract the music files from the game's system files. You just need to get the right viewing/extracting program eg. pakviewer for quake-type .pak* files. It's likely to be windows software, but shouldn't be too hard to run under linux using wine.

Short of that you could output your game's sound to the input of a sound card on another PC and record it.

But I don't know of any in-game capture tools, unfortunately.
