### Post by donkyhotay on 2011-11-10
There are lots of things for you to do, Ubuntu (and a large part of linux in general) is community driven which means things get done when people volunteer to do it. First of all you need to determine what your skills are, and what interests you. Just helping people resolve issues on the forums is a good start. If you want something more technical find a FOSS project that interests you and find a bug in it to fix, then you can either fix it yourself or at least file a bug report for someone else to fix if it's beyond your capabilities. Or read through bug reports from other users and submit a patch to fix it. You could also create your own project if you really want. It's really up to you.
