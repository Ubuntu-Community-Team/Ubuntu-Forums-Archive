### Post by Jovec on 2008-04-06
You'll want to consider Vista if you want to run SLI.  XP and DX9 are limited to rendering 3 frames ahead, while Vista and DX10 can do 4.  I also can't recommend Phenom for gaming and strongly suggest a Core 2 Duo or Quad - they are simply the better CPUs (better IPC and higher clocks) outside of the server market (Where AMD is still competitive).  If you do go Phenom, be sure to get one with B3 stepping to avoid the TLB bug (the 9750 is B3).

The 8800GT is still a good choice (and the best value) as the 9xxx is nothing but minor improvements (and some losses), and the 8800GT still gives you the g92 core.

If you think you might want tri- or quad sli in the future you'll have to go with an nVidia chipset board (unless you want to cough up the $660 for [this board]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16813121330&Tpk=skulltrail")), but then you'll want to switch to a single card SLI solution like the 9800GX2 to free up a PCIe slot for future expansion.

If you stay XP you can still accelerate your Audio with a dedicated soundcard to offload from the CPU, but not so much in Vista.
