### Post by ZylGadis on 2007-08-22
> **ketzerei said:**
> My main complaint here is (and I know its kind of silly but im dirt poor) there really isn't a really bleeding edge free MMO, RTS/TBS, FPS/TPS game for linux.

Supporting game development does not necessarily mean you contribute money. In fact, money is not very important for a Free project, unless there is a continuous stream of it. What GNU gaming lacks is developer time (by developer here I mean programmer, artist, musician, etc).

My point is, you can contribute in an extremely useful way by devoting some time every day to drawing a picture for a Free game. In my experience, any person can learn to do graphic manipulations with the GIMP / Inkscape / Blender (unless they are of the whiny "It does not look like Photoshop / 3DS!" type), and art is probably the sorest spot of Free games because of the natural tendencies of programmers to do what they like - program.

From what I understand of your post, you also think that the art of Free games should be improved (in fact there are hundreds of wonderful MMOGs out there without any art - they are called MUDs). So here is a plan for you: find a Free game that you like, and you think has a huge potential. Find out who is developing it, and offer your help in terms of whatever you can do. If the person is any good, he will be glad to accept your help. And if you (and the person) are serious, in a year you might have a MMOG that looks "bleeding edge." All it takes is consistency.

### Post by deadlydeathcone on 2007-08-23
> **ketzerei said:**
> My main complaint here is (and I know its kind of silly but im dirt poor) there really isn't a really bleeding edge free MMO, RTS/TBS, FPS/TPS game for linux.

This is sadly pretty true - good, free games for Linux are few, and fewer still if you leave out the poorly documented ones that are as a result nearly impossible to play. Adding a "good graphics" requirement makes it even harder, as while programmers will work for free, professional artists almost never do, at least for video games. From my experience:

MMO - None qualify, but I'm probably not the best judge of them anyway. Not my favorite genre.

RTS -  "Under heavy developement" and "uninspired" sum up every one I've encountered. 

TBS - The Battle for Wesnoth! Honestly, If you haven't tried this, do, as it's frankly excellent. I normally play "Paradox" style strategy games such as graphically Victoria, SR2010, Civilization et al, and had a really tough time getting into it as it just seemed stupidly simple in comparison. After giving it a chance it completely changed my mind, as it ends up deceptively strategic, and, to be honest, carefully and very apparently well designed. Unlike a lot of OSS games this isn't a collection of features thrown together because they're cool; everything just fits together so well and simply that there's an odd sort of minimalistic beauty to the thing.

A lot of the campaigns are pretty bad, though, and either stretch on way too long or horribly written. "Under the Burning Suns" is pure awesomeness, though.

FPS - Tremulous, and if you're into Quake 3 maybe Warsow (Savage too, but you've played that). Really, Trem is as good as Wesnoth. Vaguely RTS like, but not to the extent of Savage, as it's squad based, has base building and two different armies - aliens and humans, who don't play anything like each other yet are (almost) perfectly balanced. I'd say it's better than Savage, and equally complex and unforgiving. It also has some of the best graphics I've seen in a free game.

Most free windows games work under Wine, too, like Dwarf Fortress and Anacreon, but don't expect good graphics from them - most free games look terrible, regardless of OS. If you really want to kill your eyes (but have fun doing so) you might also want to look into DOSBOX and abandonware sites like Home of the Underdogs (Master of Orion, Xcom, Darklands), or get into Nethack (apt-get install slashem-sdl for a prettier version) and other Roguelikes. If you want to spend some money, Dominions 3 is another game in the "horrible looking but very good" category.

As for decent looking games - if you want to check out unfinished ones UFO: AI, Vega Strike and FreeOrion all have good graphics and gameplay (what gameplay there is, at least). If you like space shooters, Freespace 2 Open.
