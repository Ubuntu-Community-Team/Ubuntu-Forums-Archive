### Post by forrestcupp on 2011-05-27
> **Arthur_D said:**
> But 3D games are a big no-no for someone new to programming.

It's true that he's not going to come up with a blockbuster 3D game right out of the box. There's way too much work involved and most people don't realize that until they start trying.  But that doesn't mean he can't mess around with it if 3D is his interest. With some C++ under his belt, it's really not hard to set up a simple terrain with Irrlicht that you can navigate around. It's not going to be a game, but it's still cool to see that kind of progress with minimal effort.

You'll also want to learn how to use Blender for creating 3D models and even levels if you don't get too discouraged by how much work it takes. But you'll never get there until you start learning and trying.
