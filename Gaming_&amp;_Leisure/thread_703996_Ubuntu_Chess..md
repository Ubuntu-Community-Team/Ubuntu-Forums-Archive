### Post by jacksaff on 2008-02-24
Playing computers is a crap way to learn to play chess. Most chess programs will play lots of strong moves mixed in with blunders. It's completely unlike how people play. The best way to improve is to join a club where your (human) opponents will happily explain to you how they can beat you so easily. Then get a book or two on basic chess strategy. Once you've done that then join FICS and learn to play FAST (slower than about 10 minutes per game, total, and you'll never get many opponents). Blitz away for a few weeks, then go back and re-read your book and see if it makes a little more sense this time. Rinse, repeat. Chess gets a lot more fun once you start to get a grasp of basic strategy and learn some common tactical themes so it's worth some effort and a lot of beatings to improve a little.
