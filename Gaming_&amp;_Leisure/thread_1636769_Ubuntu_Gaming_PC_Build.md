### Post by efikkan on 2011-01-11
It really does not matter much, but commonly the screws for the motherboard have 'hexagonal head', and the ones for the case (for mounting PSU and brackets) looks the same but slightly larger. The screws for optical drives and hard drives are generally smaller (and weaker), typically with a round head. I would say it's most important to use good screws for the PSU and the motherboard, since these might get damaged (during transport etc.), but hard drives generally stay in place with any screws, but I recommend using screws on both sides so the screws does not get bent over time. I know these different screws got names but I don't remember them. I build and repair computers when I'm not programming.
