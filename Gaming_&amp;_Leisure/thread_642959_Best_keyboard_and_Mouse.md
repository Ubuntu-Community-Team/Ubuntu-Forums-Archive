### Post by Rhubarb on 2007-12-17
By the looks of it your laptop specs are exactly the same as my notebook here, except mine runs 100MHz faster.

Anyhow, I'm also a very big FPS gamer, and use solely ubuntu to game.
(I play sauerbraten and assaultcube).

I have a G11 keyboard here (it's the same as a G15 just without the screen), and a logitech G5 mouse.

The keyboard:
I'm too lazy to find and install the linux daemon that works with the G11 + G15 keyboards.  You can get all the extra G1 to G18 buttons, and the screen working in ubuntu, but you need to hunt down the program that lets you do this (and probably compile it + configure it too).
Hence I don't use the G keys at all, they're (mostly) useless IMHO.
The good news is that all the other buttons work fine on the keyboard: volume, play/pause, next track, previous track, stop, mute, and the back light button (off, low brightness, and high brightness)
The back light is good if you can't touch type in the dark, but other than that it's also quite useless IMHO.

The mouse:
Everything works
You can adjust the dpi setting easily with the 2 buttons on the mouse.
Goes right up to 2000dpi, which is perfect for FPS gaming
It has a weight cartridge, so you can customise the weight distribution of your mouse.
If you want to enable some buttons on your mouse, you'll need to do a small change to your xorg.conf and a small change using xmodmap so you can use the tilt left + tilt right  + back buttons on your mouse.
Have a search on the forums here if you want to know how to do it.
I recommend this mouse, it is very fast and accurate, and has helped me win most online FPS games :D

So, all up the mouse is the most important for FPS gaming.

You can get some very good USB FPS keyboards that just have the WASD + surrounding keys, if you can, I recommend you get one of those.

### Post by Naegling23 on 2007-12-17
since your a linux user, beware of the reviews!!!!

You want to buy the best product that works under linux.  That may be the best overall product, but might not be.  Sure the copperhead might be the favorite mouse of the reviewers, but if it doesnt have full functionallity under linux, its worthless (at least to me).  I think, but im not sure, that the dpi settings on the copperhead are software based, meaning no on the fly dpi changes in linux.  If thats the case, then the g5 becomes a better linux gaming mouse.  Same with keyboards, most of those macro buttons dont work in linux, so those keyboard buttons are not going to be of any use, and will just take up space (I think the g15 is the only exception to this).  Who cares if its a better "review" product, the real desire is how well it works, and how much you like it.  Most reviews are written for windows users, so they dont take into account what works in other OS's, which is a big deal for linux users.  My advise.  Go to best buy or another computer store.  Play around with mice and keyboards, pick a few of each that you like.  Then go online and look for ones that work under linux, eliminate those that dont.  THEN look at reviews to make sure that the quality is there.  Reviews are designed to help you decide, and avoid bad products, not to tell you what to buy.  If you buy based on anyone's decision other than your own, you will be disappointed.
