### Post by IsawSp4rks on 2008-03-18
Substantially.  The two cards are many years apart, ATI is pretty much always slower in OpenGL (while it's normally Nvidia's strong suit) and your card is the 'se' model which means that it was the slowest/cheapest of the 9200's.  

The 7600GT will basically run almost anything you throw at it (within reason) while the 9200se struggles on some of the simpler OpenGL stuff.

### Post by Sammi on 2008-03-29
> **spadewarrior said:**
> The new card should be arriving tomorrow. Is there anything I should do in Ubuntu before removing the old ATI card and inserting the new Nvidia one?The graphical user interface (gnome, xorg) will most likely fail, if you just exchange without uninstalling the ATI driver first.

If you are using Gutsy Gibbon, you should be able to use the restricted drivers management tool, which is in the menues.

Personally I like to use the Envy script for graphics card driver installation/uninstallation, because it it's drivers are way more up-to-date than those in the official Ubuntu repos: [http://www.albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html](http://www.albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html)

Please keep in mind that Envy not an official method of installing, so there's no crying to mommy if it doesn't work out. Envy's homepage has good support info, if you need it.

EDIT: Oh and I firmly believe that upgrading the RAM to one GB will give you a reasonable performance gain, and make the most out of your computer. After that though, you should buy new in stead of doing more upgrades to it.
