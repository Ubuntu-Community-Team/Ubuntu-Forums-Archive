### Post by testbenchdude on 2006-12-22
I've actually read up on the Lumenlab projectors, specifically their DIY Projector. Engadget.com actually made one from the plans and it works pretty well, and they spent something like $800 out of pocket.

Seeing as how most name-brand projectors are designed to project TV and Powerpoints, their resolution is less than what we would consider ideal for gaming. Lumen's DIY and their product support "normal" gaming resolutions (up to 1280x1024 I'm guessing), and they are cheaper, so yeah I'd consider buying one. What would you rather have, a 6' display that can only do 800x600 (low end projector) or a 6' display that can do 1280x1024 for cheaper? No brainer.

There is an easier alternative though, and while I can't remember the site I got this from, I'm going to "build" my own from an old overhead projector and a gutted LCD. No muss, no fuss. It will live in my garage/game room so I'm not concerned about aesthetics. All you need to do is remember to leave a small gap (1/8th to 1/4 inch) between the LCD and the projector glass, and mount a couple of case fans for airflow otherwise the LCD might suffer from the heat of the lamp.

Used overhead projector? $30. Used 17" LCD? Already got one, and the case fans. So this is the option I'm pursueing.
