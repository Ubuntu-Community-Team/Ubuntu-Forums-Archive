### Post by justin whitaker on 2007-03-30
I think  Linux is fine for gamers. Many popular titles can be made to run via Wine, Crossover Office, or Cedega: Hl2, CS:S, WoW, GW, etc. There are a few games that have native linux ports, particualrly titles from Id, Bioware, and Epic (Unreal). There are also alot of really fine open source and freeware games that run on Linux.

Now, if you want to play Command and Conquer 3, STALKER, or anything else just released, you will have to wait, maybe forever. Some titles will never run, some have weird DirectX quirks that may take some time for Codeweavers, Transgaming, or the Wine Team to figure out. 

But I game exclusively on Linux, and I find performance to be better overall on titles that I do get running. 

Decide how much pain you are willing to put up with, and go from there.
