### Post by Grant A. on 2009-01-22
Well, if you are into PvP and player housing you could play [RuneScape]("http://www.runescape.com/"). To get it to work you need to go click 'play now' and when Mozilla tells you plug-ins are needed (you need ubufox enabled), click the pop-up and follow the directions. You need to pay for player housing, though.

Nexuiz is a pretty fun FPS, you can install it by running this command:

```

sudo apt-get install nexuiz

```

Have fun gaming! :)

### Post by Carpentr on 2009-01-24
I found another game that some of you may find interesting ,

I'd add _*[Puzzle Pirates]("http://www.puzzlepirates.com/")_* to the list of MMOs people can play on Linux. I just started playing it again and the puzzles are quite fun , and it has a really stable player-base. It has a really interesting economy as  well ; players can open shoppes and produce goods to be sold to other players. The game also has a political system where groups of players can join together and fight in PvP for ownership of Islands.

Like the other MMORPG I mentioned the client is made with java and can be run on Linux without issues.

If you enjoy puzzles I'd give this one a try.
