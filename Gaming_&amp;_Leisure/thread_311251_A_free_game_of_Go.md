### Post by dbd on 2006-12-05
[Gnugo]("http://www.gnu.org/software/gnugo/gnugo.html") is probably the best free Go AI. But as DiJEH said, writing a Go AI is VERY hard (and I'd know, I wrote one for my final year project at uni, and after a years work it was only about 25kyu!). Even the best Go AIs are weaker than strong amateurs, also if you play against them too much you will probably learn bad habits. However, they do have their uses and are worth messing around with when you are just starting. Also, on 9x9 they can be reasonably good, and since that is where you want to start, they will be useful there.

Playing on the net (when you get the chance) is always better though, so I might as well plug my favourite Go server: [KGS]("http://www.gokgs.com/")! In the Beginners Room you are guaranteed to find people to play teaching games with you, which are very valuable when learning Go. (But of course, games in real life are even better!)

Also, [Sensei's Library ]("http://senseis.xmp.net/") is an awesome site for learning Go. It is a wiki and has pages that will help almost all amateur players, the Beginners Study Section is especially helpful.

While I'm in the website plugging mood, GoBase has a great [selection of Go problems]("http://gobase.org/studying/problems/"), the Korean Problem Academy especially valuable for tuning up your Go skills. Although unfortunately you need to register (for free) first.

Good luck, and have fun! :)
