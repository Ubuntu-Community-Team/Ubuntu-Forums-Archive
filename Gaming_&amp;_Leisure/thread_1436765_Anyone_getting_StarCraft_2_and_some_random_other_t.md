### Post by jedispork on 2010-03-23
StarCraft is one of my favorite games. I'm not a regular player but do go through a few phases per year when I'm into it. The main reason I can still play the game is because I have a cousin and a few friends that still play. Now with sc2 on the way only 1 of my buddies is planning to buy it. He will most likely get bored of the game in a month and not buy the expansions. My cousin who loves sc is interested in the second game but has no plans to buy a computer that can run it. 

What made the 1st game fun for me will be missing from the second which is people to play it with. I know there are plenty of people online but I've always been a solo gamer or real life friends only. 

For the most part I've gotten away from pc gaming in general. Most commercial games are not supported in linux and the state of pc gaming is a mess right now. The drm on newer games is horrible and full or problems.  We also now have pc gaming divided into categories, like steam and other competitors. 

With a nice home theater setup I no longer want to sit at the computer and play games. I agree that pc gaming can have advantages for a power user but the ht setup trumps it for me. So why am I complaining? I don't want to sit at my computer to play games anymore, especially if I have to boot into another o/s just to play it.  We will see if I can over come the force to purchase sc2.
