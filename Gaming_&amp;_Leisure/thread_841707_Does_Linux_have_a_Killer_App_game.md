### Post by Zeotronic on 2008-06-26
First off, it must be understood that any games available for Linux are likely available for Windows as well, at least in one form or another... so as far as I know the term, their not technically 'killer apps'. The only games I have really touched in a long time have been Open Source games (save for Unreal Tournament 2004), so my list will consist purely of them.

As far as First Person Shooters go, I'd advise you to check out Open Arena... or Urban Terror if you have some friends (and some means by which to connect multiple computers, whether it be the internet or a LAN). Urban Terror is kind of glitchy in it's input configuration though, and that may turn you off of it. Open Arena is pretty good, but it simply doesn't compair (or at least yet) to Unreal Tournament 2004, and thus probably some other propritary First Person Shooters. If you want to buy a first person shooter, you should wait to see if they release Unreal Tournament 3 for Linux... I suspect they will (since they did ut2004), but I could be wrong.

If your into Massively Multiplayer Role Playing Games (I left the O out because I dunno, maybe some of them arn't Online), but the only Open Source game like that I know of is Plane Shift... and its kind of underdeveloped. I think someone is making another Open Source MMO, but we'll see what becomes of that.

Battle for Wesnoth and Freeciv are good stratagy games, though I'm not particularly good at either of them, I have to respect just how wide of an array of match options Freeciv has.

Other genres (which are not quite as notable), like puzzle, shoot em up, and whatever Dance Dance Revolution and Guitar Hero are, offer Frozen Bubble, The Ur Quan Masters, Step Mania, and Frets on Fire, respectively. (though from my experiance the Frets on Fire in the repositories handles very poorly, so you might want to get it streight from their site) If your into platformers, the only thing thats free that I know of thats any good is Cave Story, which is freeware. Other than that Scorched 3D is the only other game worthy of note that comes to mind... I'd describe it as an 'artillery shooter', though I don't know if thats a genre.

### Post by Naegling23 on 2008-06-27
I'll second the notion that an above poster mentioned that there really isnt a killer game app for linux since almost all games on linux run on windows too.  Well, I guess except for tuxcart, and Im not going to call that "killer"

There are a lot of free/open source games (frets on fire is my favorite open source game, just make sure to grab one of the mod packages, not the game itself).  Saurbraten, nequiz, warsow, and a whole host of others, in all genres (the big ones are FPS, but MMO's, RPG, RTS, exist too).

There is also a whole host of commercial (ie pay for them) games that run on linux, these are broken into two catagories.  The first is linux native, companies that care enough to make an actual linux version: Savage2, Anything by Id, darwinia, neverwinter nights, UT2004, and a whole host of smaller games that I am forgetting.  The second catagory of games are ones that do not run natively, but run great/flawlessly through wine (some games loose something when run through wine, but some of them you cannot tell you are not running them in windows).  These games are ones like, Half-life 2, oblivion, anything by blizzard.  For the most part, any game that was a big success on the pc will eventually work well in wine.  

So, linux has a ton of games that run well (Not as many as windows, but more than mac).  You just have to look a little bit harder to find the right ones thats all.
