### Post by Inc&#940;nus on 2007-05-13
> **original_jamingrit said:**
> The sad thing is, OpenGL is not being supported in Windows to the extent that it could be.  DirectX libraries are more popular because there is more support for them, and OpenGL (which isn't strictly open-source, but oh well) is having difficulty competing.

Gone are the days of Postcard-ware. =-(

Yeah, but this is intentional and game developers are aware of that. GPU manufacturers are really going to think twice about whether they want sub-par OpenGL performance on their hardware because there are a few upcoming games where it could be a factor - including DirectX games, I don't doubt.

DirectX 9 hits a sweet spot for programmers, and for a realistic chance of change, we will need to see that either supported or rivalled on GNU/Linux.

On the 3D side of things, OpenGL is entirely capable, but it means you're going to spend a lot of time building your own stuff - and I don't see D3 being open sourced for a while. :p

What needs to be done in my completely unimportant opinion, is for someone to speak to developers, to find out what kind of crack they would get addicted to (to coin a phrase from the father of DirectX) and implement it across more of their desired target platforms than DirectX supports - also resting on DirectX where appropriate to avoid unpleasantness - much like SDL does.

A few bits and bobs to aid portability for platforms like PS3 wouldn't hurt either.

Basically, I would like to see SDL-on-steroids.

With such a system in place, developers can still have the "DirectX whatever" sign on the box because it's true, but they don't have to waste aeons porting between APIs when it comes time to get a port for Playstation 3 (or even God help us Wii) out the door.

/2cents
