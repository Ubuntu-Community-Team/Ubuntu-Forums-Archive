### Post by mikedep333 on 2007-12-25
I was going to post something else, and then I realized based on the gamefly website that you are referring to console games, rather than PC games involving heavy DRM for renting them.

Anyway, my advice to you is to only buy (or obtain a limited and restricted license to run) proprietary games that you actually want to and intend to play.

Also, check out open source/freeware games for the PC. My personal favorite is Nexuiz. If you like handheld gaming, I suggest you buy a GP2X, the only homebrew-friendly console.

### Post by mikedep333 on 2007-12-26
The best way to decide whether or not to make a game purchase is to look at reviews. 
Metacritic averages those reviews scores together.
[http://www.metacritic.com/games/xbox360/](http://www.metacritic.com/games/xbox360/)

That and the demo versions should help you make good decisions about whether or not to buy a game.

Oh, and don't expect companies to let you rent PC games physically like gametap does with console games. It is too easy for people to pirate PC game discs.
