### Post by Joshuwa on 2008-07-02
They will never release a linux version. But thats fine, because Wine will run their games perfectly.

When you play a game like WoW, you're not just running the WoW code; it also uses Warden and other separately developed programs, that supplies feedback to Blizzard, updates your game, reports crashes/errors, etc. Heck, when you run WoW, additional software runs that looks at every open window, all your memory, and what else running (borderline spyware, imo...but thats another discussion). All of this is probably where they came to the conclusion of it being unusable with their servers (if no client exists to send them the data they need, thats a problem).

To produce a Linux client would mean that these peripheral programs would also need to be coded for Linux: something that is up to their individual vendors (and thus unlikelky), and would cost Blizzard money to develop, test, maintain, and support.

Additionally, Blizzard has full-time support, for installation, use, etc of all their games. Adding a new OS would mean adding new staff (ie: new training, more time, more people to pay, more work for all involved).

Now, Blizzard isn't exactly hit up for cash, they are rolling in it. 

But for the above reasons, and from a financial standpoint, the extremely low size of the market (in comparison) is not worth the money, time, and staff necessary to make it happen.

Will I buy D3 and run it with Wine? You bet :guitar:
