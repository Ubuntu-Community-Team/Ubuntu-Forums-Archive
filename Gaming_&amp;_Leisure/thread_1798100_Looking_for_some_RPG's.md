### Post by HolgerB on 2011-07-08
@other: Sorry...bit off topic !

> **mateo14 said:**
> This is bad advice:
"I would expect that the chance of sacred 2 is related to the sales level of Sacred."
Michael Simms
[http://blog.linuxgamepublishing.com/](http://blog.linuxgamepublishing.com/)
This is my opinion and not an advice for anyone !
Every company (esp. those developing for Linux) has to do evaluations and strategic planing. From a monetary and success-oriented approach it is at least pretty dumb to port an one to two year old title to Linux and expect anything close to success.

Bad move ! Companies like Frictional Games have proven that game developments for multiple OS works nicely. Release good games on multiple OS (Mac, Win, Linux) with new ideas at the same time and people will buy with enthusiasm. For older titles ported by enthusiasts will most likely only be bought by enthusiasts. 

Example: I just bought a game mag today for 7€ which had the full versions of World at Conflict and Torchlight on their DVDs. Now imagine how "big" the "success" would be if someone ported either of them to Linux and tries to sell either for lets say 40€ :P

Many windows games run fine on WINE which both means that "porting" them is not too hard in the end (think about Gametree Linux) and more companies could / should be willing to make their Windows-only titles more WINE-compatible. Some games might require extremely hard changes while other only might require smaller adjustments.
