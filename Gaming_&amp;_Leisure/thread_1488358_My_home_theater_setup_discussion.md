### Post by Naegling23 on 2010-05-20
Well, I've sort of been down this road before, I've had a few different setups that I have created with varying degrees of success.  As for the actual setup and install, I cant be of too much help, but I can give a few pointers.  I have had the one box to rule them all setup, but truthfully, there were too many sacrificies for my tastes, the system was difficult to use, and it always felt like I was fighting to use everything.  In my opinion, your better off with several stand alone systems that each do something well.

If you want DVR, your going to have to go with MythTV.  There are other options, but they are not nearly as complete.  I've struggled with Myth, it works, but I could never get it set up to be a truly great system...and I dont think mythtv's dvd player supports surround sound...plus there is no hulu, and limited streaming video support.

If you just want media, but no dvr, options like boxee are great, they look and feel much better than MythTV, but they dont carry TV, and thus no DVR option.

I also know that windows media center is an option since you have windows 7.  You could also try that.


In my opinion, game on the wii/xbox, watch DVD's on the DVD player, and set some sort of DVR system to handle your TV....but my preference would be to use your cable company dvr...
