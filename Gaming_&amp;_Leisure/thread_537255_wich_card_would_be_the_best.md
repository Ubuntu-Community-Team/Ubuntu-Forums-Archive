### Post by maltman on 2007-08-29
Keep in mind that it's not just power supply wattage that is important. What's even more important when it comes to high end video cards is the amount of Amps offered on the 12V rail in your power supply.
For example, the GeForce7900GS is recommended to have a 350Watt PS, but additionally with a 12V rail with 20A or more. I can guarantee you aren't going to find a 350Watt PS with that type of 12V rail.
For comparison, the 8800GTX is recommended to have at least a 450Watt PS with 28A available on the 12V rail. That's pretty hefty.
You can check these numbers which are usually printed right on the side of your power supply.
