### Post by Koosti on 2009-10-21
I've had both systems and I really don't think there is one definite winner as to which you should buy. It's all preference...

What I suggest you do is look at the exclusives and see which platform has the most that you really want to play and simply can not get on the other. They're the only real meaningful difference between the two consoles as far as I'm concerned.

Other stuff that might not be that meaningful but I feel like mentioning:

-I found the 360's controller to be a bit more intuitive and comfortable than the PS3's. Then again, I'm sure you've held a PS2 controller, so if you had no problem with it, you'll be fine with the PS3's.

- As 569874123 mentioned, the 360 does have a fee for their online service (Well, the gold package which is what you need to play online) and the PS3 doesn't. It's around $50 a year, and from my experience the Xbox Live community was a bit more social with more people using voice chat and trying to communicate.

The PS3 does have the option to install Linux (or perhaps other OSs as well), so you might want to consider that it could rather easily be turned to a perfect media center PC once it's served it's purpose as a gaming console. ;)
