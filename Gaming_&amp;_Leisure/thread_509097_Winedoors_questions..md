### Post by cogadh on 2007-07-25
You've got things a little backwards, Wine is not the next best thing to Cedega, it's the other way around. Cedega is based on Wine and owes everything it can do to the work of the Wine developers.

Also, Wine Doors is just a graphical frontend for Wine. Wine Doors doesn't actually add any functionality to Wine, it just does the same things you could do on your own with Wine from the terminal. It does make things a little easier, but it is also alpha software and prone to bugs.

The only other real option is CrossOver, but it is not free. It is also based on Wine like Cedega, but it is more geared toward running office applications and only recently added some game support.
