### Post by dbd on 2009-05-13
42" sounds crazily big for a PC monitor, wouldn't have to be pretty far away to fit into your field of view? 22" is big enough for me, with 42" my eyes might fall out! Also the fact that it's a TV would lead me to worry about the response times, so I'd have a look into that first. There is a reason why 42" PC monitors cost thousands of dollars, while TV screens are a fraction of that.

As for the two monitor solution, why do you want it for a gaming system? If you were programming or something then having two monitors can be really useful, but for gaming there is really little point. Also, $130 sounds a little cheap considering the power of your system, you may lose out in terms of color quality and response times. It maybe best to just go for a single high quality PC monitor in the region of $300, and then, if you really must see your email / instant messages in another monitor while playing games, pick up a tiny, low quality $70 monitor to go on the side.
