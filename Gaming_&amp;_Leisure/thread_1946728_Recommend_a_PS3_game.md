### Post by SeijiSensei on 2012-03-27
You might have better success buying him a PS2 game; there's a wider variety of options there.  The only issue is that the emulation method used in recent PS3s to play the PS2 generation games doesn't work well with all those games.  Here's a good reference site: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_PlayStation_3_backward_compatible_PlayStation_2_and_PlayStation_games](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_PlayStation_3_backward_compatible_PlayStation_2_and_PlayStation_games)

I suggest [Final Fantasy X]("http://www.amazon.com/Final-Fantasy-X-Playstation-2/dp/B00005TNI6/") or [Final Fantasy XII]("http://www.amazon.com/Final-Fantasy-XII-Playstation-2/dp/B000F5IH2I/").  You could buy both of these for less than the price of a new PS3 game.  Unless you find the skimpy outfits worn by the women objectionable, it's quite suitable for a twelve-year-old.  My daughter was about his age when she first played FF X. The violence in these games is quite stylized and bloodless, too.  XII, in particular, has a tactical system that your young friend might enjoy.  (The sequel Final Fantasy X-2 has more "fanservice" than its predecessor and isn't as good a game as the others.)  

The first PS3 entry in the Final Fantasy series, Final Fantasy XIII, isn't as good a game as X or XII.  I haven't played the newest installment in the series, the sequel FF XIII-2, but I wouldn't start with that one.  Plus it costs $60 rather than $15-20.

When he's a few years older, I suggest [Dragon Age]("http://www.amazon.com/Dragon-Age-Origins-Ultimate-Xbox-360/dp/B0045ZIENQ/").  There's too much sexuality in it for a twelve-year-old, hence it's M rating.  DA is a lot more gory,too; the characters appear spattered with blood after melee battles.  It's my favorite of the PS3 games I've played so far.
