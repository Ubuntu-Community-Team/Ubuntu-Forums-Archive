Hi : ]

As a great fan of Games, I've been studying Blender for some time now with game development in mind. I'm still just a beginner to 3D and Blender/BGE, but i want to test everything I'm studying. Sooner or later i would make Games, and i feel prepared to begin my first one now. For sure i want to start simple, I'll start with a Platform one, since Platform and RPG are my favorite genres. But like i said, I'm just in the beginning of my learning, therefore, I'll begin with a Platform game, because it's less complicated than RPGs. My motivation to study Blender is to understand it and be able to use it and understand how to do Games with it, but my passion is Administration and Game Design, that's what I'm really good at. Probably I'll never be a great mesh modeler or animator, cause i enjoy Administration more and I'll focus on that. Of course, I'll continue to study and improve my Blender skills.

So, i want to form a team, cause "lots of heads think better and faster than one single head."

My role in the project is administrative, but are not limited to it:
&#8226; Game Designer
&#8226; Director
&#8226; Team Leader

I'll eventually need and want to contribute in mesh modeling, animation and etc as much as I'm able to.

It's very hard to start i tread like this, cause looks like I'm asking "Do a game for me, for free". And that's not the idea. Since I'm here to learn and work together with the team. Administration is not more important that mesh modeling, or shading etc, it's equal, we're a team, every single role is major for the success of the project.

I wish i could start with a WIP tread with a cool game partially done, but it would take me many-many years, that's why I'm seeking for help to form a team, it will be more faster and effective. I'll show my commitment and my goals, I'll describe and show below, everything I've done so far for the game, also the vacancies on team jobs. And of course, my administrative team organization can be wrong, useless or miss-presented/nonsense, but i can change anything and adapt to a better workflow, just say it.

My goals in simple lines:
&#8226; Create the first 3 game levels, focus only on these. They not big, are fairly simple to do. The thing is that they should be at least beautiful. Of course, no need for HDR/Bloom and GLSL for now, we not trying to make the next Crysis, just a good and enjoyable game.
&#8226; The focus is to do these 3 levels, work hard on it and release a DEMO.
&#8226; Continuation of the project will be based on feedback, and if we, developers, are satisfied with the DEMO final result and want to push the game development further.
&#8226; Real time Cinematics/Cutscenes. Again, no need to be a Metal Gear movie, we doing simple, so just good animated characters, good camera angles to make a decent real time animation will do very well.
&#8226; Texture and lighting should be focused.
&#8226; Motion Blending should be dealt with care and attention, for the player doesn't feel like he/she are controlling a brick instead of a character.

The main motive for me to start this tread is that I'm not a good 2D artist, therefore, i can't do proper Concept Art, what is a must to start any game. If i could Concept Art i would simple do the first level of the game and post a WIP tread and ask for help with the subsequent levels. And what i enjoy more is write stories, scripts, organize and work in group. Therefore i need to be part of a team to start this game.

Our Game
It will be Open Source, initially it will be under a Creative Commons (CC) BY-SA 3.0 Unported license to keep things easy and simple to begin the development, but as soon as the project gain body i will license it under a GPL license.

The minimum requirements to work on this project:
&#8226; Love games, understand games as art.
&#8226; Like or at least understand the Open Source philosophy.
&#8226; Believe (Understand) in Blender as a AAA Games capable.
&#8226; Be able and enjoy work in a team.
&#8226; Not be a biased person. We will be making a multiplatform game, therefore, members will be using different Operating Systems, so, taste and option have to be respected, we not discussing which OS is better, we doing a game not a OS. I use only Ubuntu as OS, and only Open Source tools for the game production, i recommend for others to use free (as in freedom) tools too (not OS), but this is just a recommendation, not a must. The most important thing is to do the game on Blender and BGE, which is multiplatform, free (as in free beer) and Open Source (free as in freedom).


Project
&#8226; Name: Lori's Tear
&#8226; Genre: Third Person Platform Game
&#8226; Targeted OSs: GNU/Linux, Mac OS and Windows.


Sythesized Plot

Velt and Lori are very adventurous brats... they once again disobeyed their Clan rules and their parents advices and got into the Forbidden Hari Waterfall (again...). As always, Lori was near a little garden between the forest and the river, searching for Witchcraft Butterflies (explained below). Velt, was lying down on the grass playing nervously with a bush leaf on his mouth, daydreaming of a life of brave and dangerous adventures. Suddenly, he hear his sister scream and promptly wake up to just see Lori been carried away by strange little spherical creatures with strange Armour and glowing orbs in place of it's bellies. Velt promptly start to run to save his sister, to be abruptly stoped by a terrifying creature like the ones that got his sister, but that one is more tall, larger and resembles a lion. The creature get it's spear with a big blade. Velt have only a wooden stick on his left, on the ground... he grabs it...

And the adventure begins.
