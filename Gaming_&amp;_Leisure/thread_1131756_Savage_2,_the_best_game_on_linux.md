### Post by Pasdar on 2009-04-21
The reason I like this game is because I'm a real RTS fan. However, this game combines RTS with first person shooter genre. If you become the commander of your race you're playing an RTS, and if you are one of the fighters, you are playing an FPS. 40 players online, on one map, really awesome.

The cool thing about it is that as the commander you can ask your fighters to do something, but its up to them to listen to you. The only thing you have real power over is what to build, the builders, etc. However, at the same time everyone really depends on you to have good oversight and tell them what to do, make the right strategy, etc. So everything really depends on a good commander to win the game. If your commander sucks, you will lose, if he's better than the other commander you'll win, as simple as that. Of course the fighters (who are the ones in the 'shooter environment' have to be good also of course, otherwise it's useless).

This combination of third person, first person, strategy and shooter makes it very nice to play. As a playing in first person mode you can actually see the commander putting buildings, etc around you or searching a place to put a building, as if hes reaching from the skies... heh.. really cool

Unfortunately on my PC I only have a GeForce 6200, I have all settings at low and sometimes it still becomes slow when there is a lot of things going on on the screen. I'm going to install it on my laptop today, it should run fast. :D

PS: If you're playing it for the first time, you should definitely go through the tutorial first and then try practice mode before you go online. Also when online don't choose the commanders post.. play a day or two in shooter mode first before you try the commander post.

Therefore, the notion that this is a free game or only costs $10 (for the premium account) is false.  I have to give S2 credit for coming up with a clever method of marketing the game as "free" while not needing to use DRM.  Actually, the game could probably cost upwards of $100 or more in a single year (if you want to develop uber stats).  That's about as much as you'd pay per year for a MMORPG.

> The reason I like this game is because I'm a real RTS fan.

So am I.  It has some RTS elements...You get to command several units, work with resources (gold), build and destroy enemy units/buildings.  Since the other players are your "units", they decide what type of unit they'll be.  Also, the players (especially the noobs and jerks) don't always follow orders, which becomes very frustrating...Arguments ensue about who's fault it is for losing a match :P.  This is also an FPS (first person shooter) game so you must rely on the players FPS skillz to get ahead. (see above paragraph)

I'd like to find a good pure RTS game for linux but my search has come up dry...Globulation (lousy graphics), Warzone 2100 (doesn't work with compiz), Spring TA (awesome but has a lousy map downloader and leaves a mess of files/directories all over ~username), Glest (haven't tried it but heard its far from finished), Dark Oberon (not finished, lousy AI and pathing).  Anything that doesn't use 3d acceleration (ex:  the civ clones, Battle of Weznoth) is right out and I'm not a fan of Wine.
