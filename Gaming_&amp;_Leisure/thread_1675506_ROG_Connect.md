### Post by rdfarley2 on 2011-07-23
Sorry for posting on kind of an old thread, I just wanted to point out to cascade9 here and the OP some things about ROG connect. ROG connect allows a direct connection from a laptop to the motherboard CMOS/BIOS. What this does is give you real time control over some settings (albeit a small amount of settings). I may need to update my software I am unsure but that is neither here nor there. My point here is that the temperatures posted by ROG ,since it connects directly to the CMOS of the computer and does not go through the Windows GUI, are more accurate, actually vastly more accurate like a 10 degree celsius difference. Windows reports all of my temps almost 10 degrees higher when I check both ROG and the bios of my PC (after restart) they are much lower than that. Possibly a one time thing only with me but using speedfan in windows i would get a much different temp report than what was coming from the Bios, and later after I got a laptop for my job (lol) Rog connect. 

 This has nothing to do with the original OP question but to kind of help you OP, I LOVE my AMD ROG board, and I am sorry at this point in time I would highly recommend AMD processors and video cards over Intel though...the intel is faster BUT...here is why. The AMD overclocks very nicely with these ROG boards. I got a Phenom II x4 955 BE (unlocked multipliers) running VERY stable overclocked from 3.2ghz to 3.6 ghz per core, I havent even touched the multiplier thats just using the HT base speed (FSB for Intel). I also have this board running the HT link at 2200 mhz which is SMOKING. I was going to do crossfire with two Radeon HD6870s but I stuck with one after I saw the increase upgrading to the 890fx ROG board. I play Rift mostly, and Mass Effect 2 lately, both games, 50-60fps with everything on max, overridden with the ATI control panel. Hopefully you will see this bud and make a decision on if you want to go with the ROG or not. It is a little hyped, the ROG connect feature isn't as cool as they make it seem but it is useful, and it shows better temps than Speedfan in Windows.
