### Post by buntunub on 2008-06-14
1. Silkroad. Best graphics of any free MMORPG hands down. Great fun gameplay. Just have to deal with the massive armies of bots which flood the servers.
2. EQ2. Amazing graphics. Awesome gameplay. 
3. World of Warcraft. Crap graphics for a modern day MMORPG when stacked with the above games, but tons of subscribers. Lots of little kiddies. Fun.
4. Dark age of Camelot. Best MMORPG PVP system hands down. Awesome gameplay. A bit dated engine, but lots of fun.
5. NWN2. Great graphics. A bit spotty in wine, but its getting there fast. NWN pre 2 has Linux clients with lots of fun hacking involved. A geeks dream come true.
6. Warcraft/Starcraft. Runs flawless in wine now. Blizzard game so nuff said there.
7. Civ4. Amazing game. Runs flawless for me in wine.
8. Oblivion. Game of the millenium. Simply incredible graphics and gameplay. Runs flawless in wine.
9. EVE Online. Breathtaking graphics and gameplay. Best PVP system in a space age MMORPG. CCP is very linux/FOSS friendly.
10. Ceasar 3. Runs flawless in wine. Older game but its the king of the historic SIM types.
11. Total War series (Rome, Medieval, etc). Most of them run flawless in Cedega, and some in wine. Amazing strategy game with historic elements.

Now on to the Native Linux riff raff. Not much to speak of here unless your really in to that whole 1980-1990 genre of games. Game development in the FOSS gaming area seriously sucks, but there are rumors of some interest being generated. Enough game engines have been Open Sourced that there is a promising future should some talented devs choose to pursue this. The main deficiency as I understand it is a critical lack of contributing Graphic Artists.
