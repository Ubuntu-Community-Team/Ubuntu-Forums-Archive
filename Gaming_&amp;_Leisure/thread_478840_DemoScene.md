### Post by rc55 on 2007-12-23
You would be right in thinking that the demoscene PC wise is heavily skewed towards DirectX. In fact, you are probably aware of the 4k/64k productions of late being very impressive, but that's due in part to the functionality made in cutting edge DirectX releases.

It is a curious issue however. If you go to any demo party you will find a significant number of attendants using Linux based systems. Well, that and AmigaOS of course!

Paniq of Farb-Rausch is probably the most passionate Linux based demoscener, and he has set up the site [linuxdemos.org]("http://www.linuxdemos.org") - which is the main place for you linux demoscene fix. Bear in mind also, [pouet.net]("http://www.pouet.net/") does have the occasional multiplatform release too.

The attitude towards linux is mixed really. Some demosceners just aren't interested in the political/freedom side of things, and are happy to use Windows as they find it does what they require (whether you agree with that or not). Some advanced sceners say they have tried linux but just struggle to live without Visual Studio's IDE or the DirectX API which are both very mature, and often the demosceners use them as part of thier own jobs.

As with Linux generally, I suppose it will be a growth area, and it's probably a matter of time until more and more demos come out.

Until then, it's Wine for most things!

Ruairi
