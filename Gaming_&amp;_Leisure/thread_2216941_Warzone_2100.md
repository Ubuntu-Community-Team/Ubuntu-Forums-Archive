### Post by oldrocker99 on 2014-04-15
Well, on Steam, there are the Wargame:European Escalation and Wargame:AirLand Battle games, Planetary Annhiliation, and Oil Rush, not to mention Crusader Kings 2 and Europa Universalis IV, which aren't exactly RTS games as they are usually defined. The Spring engine is also (Real Soon Now) coming to Steam. While there are tons of FPS Linux games, both open source and commercial, RTS Linux games are still few and far between. This will probably change in the next couple of years, but this is the situation at present.

Warzone 2100 is (seriously) one of the very best RTS games ever, which got lost in the shuffle when it was released in 1999, and it's wonderful that it was released as open source. I should also mention Seven Kingdons:Ancient Adversaries (7kaa in the repos), which is now open source and was a favorite of mine back in the Win 98 days. It's ever being sold on GOG.com, and I consider it fully worth the $5.99 it costs to get the nearly essential manual.
