### Post by jeepfreak2002 on 2007-05-13
This petition's purpose is to establish the demand for natively coded, Linux based, video games.  Massive multi-player online role playing games (MMORP) such as World of Warcraft, Guild Wars, and Ever Quest collectively represent one of the largest gaming communities in the world.  As such, these communities are the best resource available to speak as one voice to the game developers.

One of the biggest challenges in the Open Source community's acceptance into main stream computing is the ability to play video games without the need for additional software to such as Wine, and the associated difficulties, to make them run.  While web browsing, office suites, graphic design, and email clients have all been produced by the open source community, commercial applications such as video games have lagged due to the overall lack of demand.   

By signing this petition we are speaking as one voice to the gaming development community that there is a large community that wants today's games to be available natively on the open source platform of Linux.

Thank You.

Link:  [http://www.petitiononline.com/lgp51307/petition.html](http://www.petitiononline.com/lgp51307/petition.html)

I am a software developer.  I work for free.  

A lot of the work I do I know will help me in the long run, even if I don't get paid.  For instance, the cursor work that I am doing for Wine has 1) gotten my brother closer to playing games, 2) has made me smarter, 3) has created something that I can put on a resume.

That said, you are correct that *most* software developers don't work for free.  And *all* don't do it without some sort of reward, even if it is the nice, fuzzy feeling that they made someone happy.  Companies like HP, Google, etc. do it because it saves them time in the long run.  They will commit patches to the Linux kernel, for instance, not so we can have the functionality, but so that they won't have to take care of the patches any more if the internal apis change.
