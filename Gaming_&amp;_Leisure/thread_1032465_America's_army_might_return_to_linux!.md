### Post by Polygon on 2009-01-26
here is a list of all the so called  "propaganda" in the game.

1: loading screens. sometimes shows the 'values of the army', which include, honor, leadership, trustworthyness, etc...stuff like that. Not exactly traits exclusive to the army

2: main screen, / game loading screen, has a short video with the army logo on it

3: the game itself puts a flash.exe on your computer that has videos of real life army officers. Optional, i just delete the shortcut when i install the game

4: i belive the game itself now has maps where it shows models of 'real life heroes' and you can load it up and read/hear about what they did. optional

5: training. You are pretty much required to go through a set of training maps, which are supposed to mimick what a real soldier would go through in real training, like medic training (where you have to take notes and take a test, lol!), shooting snipers, going through a shoothouse, special forces training (again taking notes and then a test, this is really hard though =/ ) and then a finally a training mission where you have to sneak around a course and not get caught.

6: the actual game itself. America's army is set up a bit different then other fps's, in the case that YOUR team always appears to be the army, as in all of your team mates have models of people wearing camo, etc, while the opposing team is always wearing ski masks, is wearing darker colors of camo (black as opposed to tan/green), etc. Oh yeah, in the chat it always says [US ARMY] <username> next to people on your team, and [Enemy] or [OpFor] next to the usernames of people of the other team. I really like this setup, as you get used to looking for certain player models, instead of having to adjust what your looking for based on what team your on. Also provides very good contrast between your allies and enemies. 

 Also, the start of each map has a little description of what you should be doing, and has a made up scenario which is supposed to mimick what real life army operations look like. You read it to find out what the object of the map is, and you never read it again. Not even really considered propaganda.....many other games have 'fake military scenarios"


THATS IT. thats all the propaganda in the game. NO WHERE does it flash at you JOIN THE ARMY!!!!. The worse propaganda is completely optional (real life heroes videos) and 99% of the people who play this game dont watch it. Teh second worse propaganda, the training missions are all one time things. once you beat it, you dont have to go through it again. Then after that, all you see is just the word US ARMY and a couple of instances of the army logo, and thats it. 

I have over 100 hours on this game, i KNOW what im talking about. While the game itself might be a recruitment tool, once your actually playing the game, you don't even realize it. The core gameplay is nothing different then call of duty 4, the us army vs the terrorists.  it really pisses me off when people claim that this game is just brain washing, when its simply not true. Like i said i have over 100 hours on this game, im still a liberal and have no desire to join up to any military branch, at any point in my life. Almost all of the 'brainwashing' is optional, and the gameplay itself is pretty fun, and hell the game is free. not to mention a good majority of the players are not even american!
