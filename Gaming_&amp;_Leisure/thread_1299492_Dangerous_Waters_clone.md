### Post by kgsuarez on 2009-10-24
Hi ubudog.

I stumbled upon your post by chance and realized, as soon as I saw it, that you were probably not going to get a very decent reply. Subsims are probably the most obscure genre in videogaming. I wouldn't expext anyone on these forums to have heard of Dangerous Waters. I hope nobody takes offense to these statements.

I'm new to Ubuntu and was just browsing these forums for the first time. I figured there wouldn't be a better time to sign up here than now, to help a fellow subsimmer.

As of yet there are no real clones of Dangerous Waters for Linux. In fact, there are virtually no subsims to speak of that are meant to run under Linux. This is a shame, I know.

There are, however, a number of projects under development that seem promising, several of which will probably be available for Linux. Check out [this]("http://www.subsim.com/radioroom/showthread.php?t=129346") link. It is a list of indi subsim projects, posted on the subsim.com forums. I encourage you to read the list and to take a look at the forums. You owe it to yourself as a fan of subsims to sign up and join the discussions there.

Have you attempted to run DW through WINE? I haven't gotten around to it yet, but I did try to run Command: Aces of the Deep on WINE earlier this week. The results were sub par, unfortunately.
