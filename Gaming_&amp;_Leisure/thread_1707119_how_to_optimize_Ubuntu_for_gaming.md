### Post by Kir_B on 2011-05-15
> **mastablasta said:**
> turn off special desktop effects (compiz). it can make games lag.

Try installing the [Compiz-Switch]("http://forlong.blogage.de/entries/pages/Compiz-Switch"). Works great on my Lucid install. I've got it in a drawer on my gnome panel and it comes in very handy, especially on the rare occasions that compiz goes screwy (my mouse pointer sometimes loses it's mind). I also used it on my old dell, which was rather low on resources and shutting down compiz during a session of gaming was very helpful. Once installed, it can be found in accessories.

Hope thats helpful. ):P

### Post by t.rei on 2011-05-31
I love to game, but I only get to game while waiting for an email-feedback or someone to come online in chat/skype. Usually when done working, I've had enough of screenwork for the day. 

So I do game on linux. Wow runs fine - just too timeconsuming. Some casual Rift, thats not really running well. Used to play eve-online doing some carebearing... that game ran absolutely wonderfully. Steam? I just gave up. Fallout3? Sad story (see steam). Crysis? Don't even get me started... But I did learn a lot while trying to get those suckers running. ;)

What I like about gaming on linux is that dual screen, virtual-desktop goodness allows you to keep your game running while still getting "back to work" with a keystroke. Also fullscreen on one monitor while chatting on the other works flawlessly, while my win7 desktop insists on minimizing the game window if I want to focus anything else. 
So linux allows me to do real casual gaming (or watching a movie) while still being able to work. The transition is smooth.

This might shock some, but really - what do you guys do when that fatefull line is said/written: "Let me check with X and come back to you in a minute..." or simply "one of our trained technicians will be with you shortly, your call is very important to us, please hold."
