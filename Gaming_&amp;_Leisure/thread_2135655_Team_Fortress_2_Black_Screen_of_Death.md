### Post by myromance123 on 2013-04-16
Sorry about that, I just assumed you knew your way around.

Ok, to check what graphics card you have just open a new Terminal and Enter the following:
```
lspci | grep VGA
```
Not sure where to find the Terminal? Just open Ubuntu's Dash (or click the Windows key on your keyboard). Then type in "Terminal". Open that, and type the command above. Copy the contents it outputs, and post it here.

To determine what kind of graphics driver you may have installed, once again open Ubuntu's Dash (or click the Windows key on your keyboard). Type in "Software Sources" and open that. To the top right,  you should see a tab that says "Additional Drivers". Click that. 

There should be one driver at least selected ( the circle will have a dot in it ). Please let me know what driver that is (just type the entire name and post it here).
I hope my instructions are clear, let me know if you get stuck :)

### Post by myromance123 on 2013-04-17
Alright, so your graphics hardware is an Intel Integarted unit.

There are two things I can think of to help you get the latest drivers for it. Which one you use is entirely up to you.

**_Using Glasen PPA [Unofficial]_**
Following 1204's instructions here: [LINK]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2123040&p=12548961#post12548961")
This will hopefully help you install the latest Intel drivers.

**_Using Intel's Installer [Official]_**

Go to the following link, and download the Installer for Ubuntu:
[https://01.org/linuxgraphics/downloads/2013/intel-linux-graphics-installer]("https://01.org/linuxgraphics/downloads/2013/intel-linux-graphics-installer")

After downloading the installer, run it and follow the on screen instructions. Since I don't have an Intel Graphics card myself, I can't test this driver installer.

Even with the latest driver however, I can't guarantee that you'll get TF2 working. TF2 has rather high graphics requirements, and since you're using an Intel Integrated Graphics unit you don't have a lot of horsepower for GPU workloads.
