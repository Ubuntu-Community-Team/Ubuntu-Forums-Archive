### Post by howefield on 2010-03-15
Don't know about online spades or backgammon play but for chess you could try playchess.

This would require running the application software through wine, but it runs perfectly.

There are generally about 5,000 players at most times of the day, so you'll always get a game according to your rating/time control requirements.

[http://playchess.com/](http://playchess.com/)

You can log on as a guest for free and play in the "Cafe" or pay (a very modest sum) to get a registered nickname and rated play plus many other benefits. A free 30 day trial is available. Generally good atmosphere with lots to offer. 

Babaschess (which I have only previously used in windows) by all accounts runs through wine very well, it was very good software when I used it, I'm sure it is even better now. Allows you to connect to Internet Chess Club, (ICC).

[http://www.babaschess.net/](http://www.babaschess.net/)

FICS is a free chess server, which I haven't used for some time. 

[http://www.freechess.org/](http://www.freechess.org/)
[http://www.freechess.org/cgi-bin/Dow..._Interface.cgi](http://www.freechess.org/cgi-bin/Dow..._Interface.cgi)

Have a read also at [http://en.opensuse.org/Chess](http://en.opensuse.org/Chess)
