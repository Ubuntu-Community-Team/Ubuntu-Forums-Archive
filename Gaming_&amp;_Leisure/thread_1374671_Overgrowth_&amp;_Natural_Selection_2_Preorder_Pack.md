### Post by MaximB on 2010-01-07
The indie game studios Wolfire Games and Unknown Worlds have just announced that their upcoming games Overgrowth and Natural Selection 2 are available for preorder with alpha access for both games at the price of one ! just for 40USD !
This offer is for a limited time of 6 days – so hurry up !

Overgrowth will definitely have a GNU/Linux client and although Natural Selection 2 GNU/Linux client hasn’t yet confirmed there is a high chance – and it will be much higher if you’ll preorder now.
In ANY case you will get the GNU/Linux, MacOS and Windows clients for both games (if NS2 will have one – that is…).
You can always post at NS2 forums and try to make them commit to a GNU/Linux client before preordering (if you do this quick, you only got 6 days) , there is also a huge NS2 GNU/Linux thread, so I would say that the chances are pretty high.

### Post by MaximB on 2010-01-07
I feel your disappointment, but things are different with the indies.
UT3 Linux client was already completed long time ago (so I heard), but it cannot be released due to licensing issues.

With indies you don't have this boundary.
Wolfire Games made a Linux client for their first game Lugaru and they promised to me personally that they will also do a Linux client for Overgrowth.
Remember that they will also make a MacOS version.

While there is no 100% guarantee that NS2 will have a Linux client, but their engine is cross platform and there is already a huge Linux community in their forums asking for a Linux client - and the developers are not against it.

The problem with this development method is taht first they develop their game for Windows and then port to other platforms instead of developing it simultaneously on the 3 platforms like Mystic Mine was done.
