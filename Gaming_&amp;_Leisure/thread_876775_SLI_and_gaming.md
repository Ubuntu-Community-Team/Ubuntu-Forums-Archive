### Post by Nostrafus on 2008-08-03
Yeah, SLI is good for gaming in windows, but Linux has yet to tune to the advantages of multiple graphics cards, would it do so, you'd probably see a jump of 50-60fps and lose any annoying graphics lag in most of the newer games.

But unless you're gonna keep a separate partition for gaming in windows, just stick with the one graphics card built with a higher PPS (Polygons per second), possibly an 8800GT unless something newer and beefier has come out and is in your price range, and they also support SLI, so if later down the road the linux kernel is updated to take advantage of SLI architecture, you can always tack on another card for cheap.

I use 2 8600GT's, but I also work with a bit of video, and at the time of purchase, it was the only Nvidia card that supported SLI and HD graphics so I get the best of both worlds.

### Post by geekygirl on 2008-08-03
One decent card will always out perform two mid to low range GPU's in SLI. There are plenty of becnhmarks out there to show you that. (I will admit nearly all those benchmarks have been done with Windows though!)

As far as I can tell there are not too many games that run either with Wine or natively that actually require such a setup, there isnt really a game that demands it. 

It might be an option to consider though when a game like Crysis is up and running under Wine though...

To get something high end performance wise look at an Nvidia 9800GX2 - basically 2 single cards, yet without the hassle of SLI. Cost wise will probably be the same as getting an SLI motherboard, decent PSU to run SLI, and 2 cheaper cards....

No, its not worth the effort to set up SLI currently for Linux based gaming :)
