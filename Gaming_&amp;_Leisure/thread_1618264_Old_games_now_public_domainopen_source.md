### Post by mastablasta on 2010-11-12
> **Spice Weasel said:**
> I always thought that it was only the game engine that was GPLed, and the data was left proprietary as with Quake.
 
Indeed. you still need doom wad to play it, which has to be purchased.
 
Another good old game that is now freeware is Command and Conquer. I am not sure, but i think Dune 2 and Dune are also freeware. another good one would be Open transport tychoon.
 
there are many games that are not realyl released as opensource or freeware, but are so called abandoned software. abandoned, because company that built them does not exist anymore and no one claims the copyrights for it. it's kind of gray area. it's not realyl legal, but on the other hand no one is or will prosecute you if you copy them, because simply the one that would does not exists anymore. so it's not "piracy".
 
you can find these kind of games on Abandonia. they seem to be very clean about it as they have specific rules as to which games are allowed and which ones are not. they also have an agreement with ESA and do a thorough check if the game is really abandoned or not. and if the game starts selling agian it is removed from the site.
 
Also for freeware and opensoruce games you might want to check their sister site called Reloaded because many of those games have review and many of those games also work in Linux.
