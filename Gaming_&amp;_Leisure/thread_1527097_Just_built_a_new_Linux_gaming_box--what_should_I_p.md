### Post by 2Karl on 2010-07-28
I'd definitely agree with everyone who's mentioned Neverwinter Nights - A very fine RPG (which rather rigidly follows the D&D 3.5 ruleset), which you can pick up with both expansions for very little. It also has a plethora of player made content (or modules as they're known), and online play. Well worth it.

Also, two games from a little software house called Introversion - Uplink and Darwinia. Uplink is a rather unique hacking simulation - it's not going to blow you away with graphics, but it has a very cool cyberpunk atmosphere and makes you feel like a contract hacker (If you're a fan of the movies Sneakers or Wargames, you can't pass this up :) ) Darwinia is a very nicely styles action strategy game. Visually, the envioronment looks like the inside of the movie Tron. You control a squad of computer lifeforms around the innards of a supercomputer destroying viruses in a method similar to the old Amiga game Cannon Fodder.

Additionally, you can try Unreal Tournament. Even the original from 1999 is still very playable today (I spent all last night playing it), and like NWN, all the UT games have a host of additional content which will keep you going for a long time, such as the Alien Swarm mod for UT2004, which turns the game into a top down squad based shooter in the vein of Alien Breed (another amiga classic)

If you like retro point and clickers, you could do worse than install SCUMMVM and get hold of some titles for that - IIRC, Beneath a Steel Sky and Lure of the Temptress both work superbly on it and are available for free, I'm sure you'd be able to get hold of some old monkey island titles from ebay (if they're not already freeware).
