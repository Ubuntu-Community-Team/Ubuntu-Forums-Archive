### Post by wilborm on 2009-02-18
I am a beginner learning to play contract bridge. I found a Linux program that allows me to practice at home. XContactBridge is available from [http://www.xcontractbridge.com/xbridge.htm](http://www.xcontractbridge.com/xbridge.htm)

I downloaded the free program. It comes as a binary file that doesn't require any difficult installation that I couldn't have handled. Just open the archive by right-clicking on the tar file and extract the xcbridge file. To run it, just click on it. You can also drag it to the panel to establish a ready link.

The free program lets you play as many hands of random bridge deals as you like, rubber style, and keeps score too. It took me a few minutes to realize that in order to bid you must type the usual abbreviations (1C, 1H, NT, NB for pass, etc). Click on the continue button as needed.

The computer players play fast and correctly, as far as I can judge. When you are dummy you get to play your partner's hand. My only problem is that the program uses several common bidding conventions by default. You should be able to toggle them off with the F-keys, but that does not work in the free program. So unless you are an expert, bid conservatively or you may be in for some surprises.

I have ordered the full program on CD for a reasonable $14.99, including S/H. It comes from Scotland. You can pay for it with a credit card or PayPal.

I recommend it.
