Decided to compare the two awesome games we've gotten recently. If anyone has something to add, feel free to.

Things to keep in mind:
[LIST]
[*]Enemy Territory: Quake Wars has a much bigger budget than Savage 2

Things that are nicer in ETQW:
[LIST]

[LIST]

[/LIST]

### Post by curvedinfinity on 2008-04-21
About the megatexture negative comment, in quake 4, there are no huge outdoor scenes like in every level of ETQW. There are some outdoor scenes in Q4, but if you ever notice, you are always constrained to being "down in" the terrain. That's because its less overhead than if you were allowed to see a lot of terrain at once; Q4 doesn't have a very sophisticated terrain renderer, so that's a limitation of it. -- If you go in a building in ETQW, you should get fairly identical graphics performance compared to Q4. That's not saying you'll get identical frame rates though, because Q4 only keeps track of the room you're in a couple rooms "in front" of you and a couple rooms "behind" you, where ETQW has to keep track of a huge expansive area all at once. -- That's a CPU bottleneck though.

Megatexture, as far as I'm aware, is a texture paging system. Basically, it allows for a huge texture to sit on your harddrive, and the game to dynamically load parts of the texture in to memory. I don't think it really deserved its own fancy name, considering its not a revolutionary idea -- though it may be the first of its kind in this application.
