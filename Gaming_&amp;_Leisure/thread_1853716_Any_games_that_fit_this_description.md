### Post by mastablasta on 2011-10-03
Open Transport Tycoon deluxe (you can also get the original on abandonware sites).
 
Speaking of abandonware there are plenty old "abandoned" DOS games (that are free for download) that do simulation. Graphics are nothing special, but you have stuff that fits into this description: For example Pizza tycoon (pizza restaurant simulation), rollercoster tycoon (themepark simulation), sim earth (earth simulation), sim city (city simulation), MythTV (tv station simulation), Floor 13 (secret agency simulation),various football managers .... and more.
 
As you can imagine there are also plenty mario like games. for example jazz jackrabitt, prehistoric 2, various sonic hedgehog games...
 
A good site for abandoned DOS games is Abandonia, while to find some freeware/opensource stuff you could try their sister site Reloaded.org.
 
Dos games can be installed and played using DOSBox (available for linux). Dos box also has some nice frontends for those that hate command line.

### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-10-03
> **Lucypie said:**
> Honestly, I don't know what an emulator is... What is it and what can I do? :o


Simply put, an emulator allows you to run software (or games, in this case) on a system that they originally weren't designed for - usually PCs. For example, with an SNES emulator, you can play the original Super Mario Bros. on your PC. Or with an N64 emulator, you can play Super Mario 64 on your PC.

There are emulators for other systems, too, like:
[LIST]
[*]Arcade cabinets
[*]GameCube
[*]Wii
[*]NES
[*]Playstation
[*]Sega Genesis
[*]Sega Dreamcast
[/LIST]
I've listed all the systems that I know have emulators available, but even if I didn't list a console/system here, there probably is an emulator available for it.

### Post by DangerOnTheRanger on 2011-10-04
> **Lucypie said:**
> Alright, well do they come with the games or do you have the games on a disk and you put them in your computer?

Unfortunately, pretty much all emulators (MAME is an exception - you can legally get a few for free on MAME's site) don't come with the games, due to copyright law; you need to buy/have bought the games you want to play. Since you already have some Wii games, you don't really need to worry about this, unless you want an emulator for another system.
> **Lucypie said:**
> 
Take the example of a wii emulator, i have about 10 games. With what youre saying, I could use an emulator to play a disk or would i have to DL the game off the internet?

Yes, you can use an emulator to play the disks you already own. That's perfectly legal. Unfortunately, you cannot legally download 99.9% of commercial games off of the Internet, due to copyright law like I mentioned before. Exidy has allowed MAME to legally redistribute some of their arcade ROMs on the Internet for free, but they're the exception rather than the rule.

### Post by 8_Bit on 2011-10-06
For the record, you can't use Wii discs in a PC. They are simply not readable, they are not standard DVDs.
You need a very specific model of an LG Electronics DVD drive to read them. Your regular DVD-ROM reader will not recognize it at all.

Therefore, I believe people have unfortunately led you onto something that might be a bit too complicated for you. Not trying to say you are dumb, but just saying that for the purpose of simply playing those types of games, it may not be worth going through all the hassle. Especially if you're only going to be free for one day as you said in your original post. 

I've had experience making backups of my own Wii discs using a Wii homebrew program that sends the ISO file to my PC through an FTP connection. It gets kind of complicated and takes hours. You may be better off playing Flash games of the genre's you mentioned, or use WINE to play Windows ones. 

There are also actually places on the web where you can play SNES and NES games from within your browser, using a Java emulator. I can't link to them here because it's basically piracy. So I guess, use Google. Here are some great simulation games I can remember off the top of my head:

Harvest Moon (SNES, farming simulation)
SimCity 2000 (SNES, city simulation)

Harvest Moon in particular is good fun because you can get married, have a kid, grow your own animals and pets, etc. It can get pretty deep, especially in the newer titles for the Gameboy Advance and the Nintendo DS. (Both which are emulatable on Linux.)
