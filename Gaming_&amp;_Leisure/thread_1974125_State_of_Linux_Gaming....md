### Post by cajual on 2012-05-05
So it seems Linux is constantly peppered with small time, indie-type games without much polish or promise for longevity. I've been tracking Linux gaming for a decade now, and each year I come back looking for new developments, but it feels as stagnant for big name titles as it was 10 years ago. I understand that the platform isn't that large for gaming, but pigeonholing people into windows for a premium fee is starting to **** me off since Microsoft is releasing new versions as often as Madden.

Here's my question: I've done the research and reading, but is there any real promise of native linux gaming? Don't start spouting off B-titles like Open Arena and Nexuiz and Xonotics, but is there any real push for a triple-A release?

I hate having to play through Wine, because it's as buggy now as it was 5 years ago and game dependencies are growing with each year. I also remember the promises of OpenGL 3, but have yet to see anything born of it. 

I'm just so tired of Windows, though it works well, I hate having almost no control over my operating system.
