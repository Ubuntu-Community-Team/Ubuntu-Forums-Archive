### Post by Jucato on 2006-07-14
> **vem0m said:**
> ahhhhhh yes the free ones and that crappy non working junk of a gameguard :P

Well, both free and non-free. Non-free games that I liked playing were Ragnarok Online and Tantra Online. The free ones include FlyFF (Fly For Fun) and Silkroad Online. 

I was a sort of MMORPG addict before. Ever since I started using Linux, I had to give it up. But like I said, it's a good thing in some ways. 

However, I think the situation does reflect a sort of need or lack of support for such games in Linux. But mostly it's the responsibility of the game makers. There were different responses to this issue coming from game makers and Wine developers. Most MMORPGs made in Asia use GameGuard to "protect" (don't know how efficiently) accounts from being hacked (the bad meaning of the word hack). I've heard that GameGuard is a PITA to support in Wine, and that it also present some security vulnerabilities to the system itself, while trying to protect the user account (ironic, isn't it?). Another reason is that the companies who make these games (mostly Korean) believe there is no sufficient demand for their games on Linux to justify "porting" the games or working towards compatibility. I find this quite ironic, since South Korea is one of the very few countries who have fine MS for anti-trust/unfair business practices. IIRC, SoKor is also one of those countries who recently designated a particular city to use Linux-only systems in government institutions (I have no update on that yet). 

On the other hand, I could probably try to start playing more "Wester" MMORPGs, like GuildWars, Lineage, etc. But GuildWars, so far, is the only US made MMORPG that has hit our local stores. The rest you have to buy online, not to mention that maintaining accounts require credit cards. :D

Btw... Can GuildWars be run on Cedega CVS??? :(
