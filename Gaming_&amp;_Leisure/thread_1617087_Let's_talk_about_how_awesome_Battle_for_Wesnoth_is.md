### Post by KoRnholio on 2010-11-08
So, I knew about this game long before, but never really tried it past the tutorial. A couple nights ago, I couldn't use my TV, so without the PS3 I decided to check out a good Linux game. Fired up Wesnoth, and started the Heir to the Throne campaign because it uses characters from the tutorial.

I did *not* know this game was so good. And runs beautifully on my laptop.  So far I'm up to the fifth or sixth mission. The last one I beat was on your way to the port city that's getting besieged, if you take the sea route you'll get shipwrecked and fight an undead island.

So far, I've beat all of the good objectives. On the island with all of the undead, you can win just by surviving 27 turns or defeat both enemy leaders. If you manage to beat both enemy leaders within the 27 turns, you'll permanently get the services of a very powerful priest who helps you in the battle.

Defeating both enemy leaders in the 27 turns is actually kind of tough, because they are on opposite sides of the large island, and you don't have access to your veteran units from prior battles. The only characters you can recruit are actually quite weak. I managed to pull it off, though. And it's all because of the aforementioned priest, and a Mermaid you get in the prior battle with a really strong magic attack.  Did it one turn before the timer was going to run out. 

So now I have this awesome priest and a level 3 Mermaid Enchantress ready to tear my next enemies up.

In short, this game is awesome, and you can download it for free from the repositories.  It has a crapload of content - this campaign I'm on is 25 missions long, and I'm only on the fifth or sixth battle. And it comes with many, many other campaigns installed from the start.  You could probably play this game for months on end.

Give me some stories about you and Battle for Wesnoth. Just try and keep story spoilers out of it.

A few general tips that work for me, some of which you've probably already figured out :)


Rally around the healers. Even 4 HP back each turn will give you a much better bet at making it.

Protect your guys. The AI will pounce mercilessly on an exposed unit with low hit points. You can't afford to lose levelled up guys.

With your elves, you should almost always rely on bow fire. A lot of orc units in particular have no melee attacks, so you can hit them in your turn, then when they attack you, hit them again in melee. 

The upgraded horsemen (knights and later paladins or grand knights) are absolute tanks. With good support they'll hack their way through a lot of bad guys.

Pay close attention to day and night. During night time, fight defensively as orcs and undead get a damage bonus, while most humans get a penalty. During the day, you can generally risk melee combats better.

Unless fighting the little lizardmen, a staggered line tends to work well. Leave no more than one hex between anything, keep some heavy hitters a hex behind to dart in and deliver the death blow, and be sure you can fall back and replace with a fresh unit.

### Post by koanhead on 2010-11-15
If you are playing Battle For Wesnoth on Ubuntu, DO NOT UPGRADE the Wesnoth package until you have read its changelog first! You will lose all your saved games if you do this. 
Major upgrades to Wesnoth (for example, the recent upgrade from 1.6 to 1.8) break compatibility with the older version for saved games. This does not happen with every upgrade, but needs to be watched for.
See this bug report for details:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/wesnoth-1.8/+bug/674783](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/wesnoth-1.8/+bug/674783)
