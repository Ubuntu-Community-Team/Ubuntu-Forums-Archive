> Wine 0.9.19 released  
 

Summer development is rolling forward. Alexandre released Wine 0.9.19 today and a slew of updates: 


Support for a proper Trash folder. 
Many improvements to the IDL compiler. 
Better FreeBSD support. 
A number of MSI bug fixes. 
Many RichEdit improvements. 

Binary packages are in the process of being built and it may take a few days for them to appear, but the source is available now. You can find out more about this release in the announcement. Check out our download page for packages for your favorite distribution.
 
  
Annoucement : [http://www.winehq.com/?announce=1.123](http://www.winehq.com/?announce=1.123)

### Post by der_joachim on 2006-08-13
A hopeful note to the Guild Wars players out there: every time a new version gets released, I try to tweak wine for Guild Wars. Yesterday, the login screen showed up, albeit with very corrupted fonts. The character selection screen is usable, although not as pretty as the windows version. The music plays at double speed though. After selecting your character, the games fires up and everything looks more or less normal. However, after a bit of moving around, the game just freezes horribly.

For the adventurous: 
- Win 98 Windows version
- DISABLE pixel shader. However, enabling the pixel shader will show you how your character looks without hair. Mine actually improved. I should have gone for a female ranger. ](*,) 
- ALSA sound emulation.

[Edit]
[The Wine App Database]("http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=5386") upgraded Guild Wars support from Garbage to Bronze. It is still not entirely playable, but it does look better.
