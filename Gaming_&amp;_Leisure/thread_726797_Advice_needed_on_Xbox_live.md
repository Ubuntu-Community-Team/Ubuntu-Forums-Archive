Downloads are probably not the issue here, as the others have noted. You really need to pay attention to traffic. 

You need unlimited traffic. 

Most of the 360's titles do not have dedicated servers. Someone needs to set up the host game, and either invite friends or allow public players into the game. 

Now, some games, like Halo 3 and Call of Duty 4 use all of the player's connections to smooth out the game performance by caching some of the game content locally and then using each of the 360s as mini-servers. That makes the game appear fast to all of the players in the room, regardless of who is the host. 

Gears of War and Team Fortress 2 require someone to act as the server. 

It works really, really well, but you need to have no restrictions on the traffic if it is going to work properly.
