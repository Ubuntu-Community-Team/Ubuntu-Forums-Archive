### Post by po0f on 2007-02-22
azan00,

With my first xpad, I just hacked the cables together with some electrical tape and a spare USB cable.  :)

All the wires inside the Xbox controller cable are standard USB wires, **except** for the yellow one.  Just don't connect it to anything.  IIRC, there should be a black, red, white, yellow and green cable.

I can't give any advice about adapters, I've never used them.

The driver for standard Xbox controllers is called "xpad" and is in the main kernel; you don't have to install anything special to get them to work, it's all plug-n-play.  XBCD is the Windows driver for Xbox controllers, don't use it.  ;)

### Post by po0f on 2007-02-23
azan00,

If you go to a pawn shop or used game store, you can usually pick up controllers for dirt cheap.  Practice on one of them.  Also, you can just as easily mod the dongle (the smaller cable where the controller quick-connects together).

It's just me, but I have always preferred the DIY approach to these sorts of things.  Taking something apart and putting it back together is always fun.

Another mod you might want to try is taking out the rumble actuators.  Just disconnect the plug from the PCB and take em out!  It makes the controller *soo* much lighter.  :)
