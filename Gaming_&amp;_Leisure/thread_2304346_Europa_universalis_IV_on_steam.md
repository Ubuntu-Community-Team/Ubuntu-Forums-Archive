### Post by T.J. on 2015-11-26
In my personal experience, people claiming problems with Steam on Linux have not taken the time to configure their systems as they should.  No offense to Ubuntu, but I have had their packages break the video drivers. 99% of the time, problems users have with Steam or Steam games are because they did not install their video driver correctly - Linux or otherwise. 
 
I  have EU4 on Linux  I have not played it much. I  have never had a problem, not once even on an AMD card.  It should be specifically noted that before you buy a game that the companies that port them to Linux sometimes port them only for Nvidia hardware.  Shadows of Mordor and Alien: Isolation would be two examples.  They require Nvidia cards to run.  You can't necessarily blame them. AMD's Catalyst drivers for Linux are crap, and while the open Radeon drivers are very decent, they lack support for OpenGL 4.

If you are considering EU4, remember that you get the Windows version as well for a single purchase.  If that is not enough to convince you, Steam guarantees your satisfaction.  If I recall correctly, Steam will refund you if you have not played the game for 24 hours or waited 7 days - whichever comes first.
