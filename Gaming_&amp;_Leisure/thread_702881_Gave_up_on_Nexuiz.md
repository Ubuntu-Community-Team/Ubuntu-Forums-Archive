### Post by AussieLinuxGamer on 2008-02-22
This thread caught my eye as I used to play Nexuiz quite a bit, and I still use it to make sure 3D is working (as glxgears isn't a true indication on how your system is performing 3D wise).

Anyway, to cut a looooooooong story short, I gave up on Nexuiz and their small but active community. It's fantastic that the game has a 64bit version (along with the usual 32bit), but the fact that it's not multithreaded (it's based of an uber-hacked Q1 engine called Darkplaces) and the gameplay never felt quite right. Quake 1,2, 3 and to a lesser extent 4 had brilliant multiplayer gameplay with graphics that suited - even in Q1 you could easy spot a baddy in the distance.

Nexuiz still lives, though hasn't been updated for a while, but I think that with Quake 3 engine now opensourced there will be a replacement for Nexuiz that captures the Q1/2/3 style of deathmatch/ctf/et al perfectly.

My pick: UT2004 for multiplayer, Q4 for singleplayer.

Still waiting for UT3...
