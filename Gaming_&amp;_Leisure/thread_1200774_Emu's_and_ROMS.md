### Post by thegreenblob on 2009-06-30
There's good native emulators of both GBA and DS.

DeSmuME is a decent native DS emulator, you can get it through synaptic (System --> Administration --> Synaptic Package Manager) or through your favorite method to get software in the repos.

Though it does come with 2 GUIs. I recommend the Gtk-Glade one. They'll both show up in your games menu.

As for GBA, visualboyadvance for the backend and vbaexpress for the GUI, works really well. You can get both of those packages in synaptic as well.

As for roms, I don't think we're allowed to tell you where to get them but google is a good place to start. ;)

Depends on where you live. Some countries allow users to make backups of their own media as fair use. Other countries however declare it to be illegal. Ubuntuforums doesn't allow posting of ROM information because there is no way to verify who it's illegal for and who it isn't. Given as how the USA is one of the countries that does consider the backup of personal copies illegal per the [Digital Millenium Copyright Act](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmca), chances are it's illegal for you.

### Post by donkyhotay on 2009-06-30
> **CharmyBee said:**
> Check among the first pages of a manual of a DS game.

Just because a company prints in a booklet that you don't have certain rights doesn't mean you don't actually have those rights (especially when it's profitable for the company). Some countries have stricken those notices and EULA's, courts in those countries have ruled you can not force someone to a contract that takes away their rights (like fair use), especially in a notice that can't be seen until after it's purchased. As I mentioned, other countries (like the USA) declare those notices to be legitimate and violating one is breaking the copyright and punishable by $1,000,000 per nanosecond the violation existed from you, your family, your dog, and everyone that might have been within a 50 mile radius of you for the past 10 years regardless of whether you know them or not. Ok, maybe I'm exagerating the penalties a little, but [less then you might expect](http://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2009/06/record-labels-awarde). Regardless it's important to know your rights within the country that you live and don't assume they are the same in other parts of the world.
