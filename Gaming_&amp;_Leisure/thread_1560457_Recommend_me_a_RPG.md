### Post by OliW on 2010-08-26
**Dragon Age: Origins** - I don't think an RPG ever made me go "WHOA!" like DA:O did in the first hour. I was completely stunned. It's like playing a particularly beautiful film that you seem to have control over. Good story with a nice rate of progression... And the trailer for the sequel blew my trousers off. Bit of a pain to get working in Wine but well worth the effort once you've managed it.

**Oblivion** - I disagree with the "most game in a box" comment for NwN. You can easily sink 200-300 hours into a single character in Oblivion. In that it's a sequel, it's very similar to Morrowind but also better in almost every respect. The reason it can take so long is it's riddled with side-quests. They're not milk-runs, some of them are as long as the main storyline and (save but one or two) they're all as good. There billions of small addons but there are also a few major expansions which are well worth playing once you're done with the main storyline.

**Fallout 3** - Based on the same engine as Oblivion and in the same vein, as epic as you want it to be in that you can do a whole load of side missions or you can just charge through the main storyline. Differs from Oblivion/Morrowind in that it uses global experience points rather than skill experience. There are lots of smaller expansions to keep you playing though. Get the Game of the year version and you get the major ones for free.

**Neverwinter Nights** - I personally can't fathom how you'd be interested in RPGs and yet have somehow managed not to play this. Epic although a little dated. Play this *before* DA:O or you'll think it's rubbish. Native client FTW. GotY edition comes with expansions but they're not as good as the main game. NwN2 wasn't as good nor does it have a native client and still doesn't work under Wine very well.

**Baldurs Gate (+ BG2, BG2:ToB)** - These are ADnD legends. BG2 especially. These games now look extremely dated and frankly need new art to bring them up to date - but that's all. Interface is mostly solid. Storyline is just amazing, especially if you make it all the way to the end of the Throne of Bhaal BG2 expansion and er.. (without giving too much away) give into your heritage.

**Knights of the Old Republic (and KotOR2)** - Epic Star Wars games. Two of my favourite RPGs and though they're slightly dumbed-down in terms of hiding lots of the ADnD maths, provide a very fulfilling story. I have played these games through at least half a dozen times each.

**Jedi Knight 2** and **Jedi Academy** should also get mention. They're technically first/third person shooters but they have RPG elements... And you get to use a lightsabre.
