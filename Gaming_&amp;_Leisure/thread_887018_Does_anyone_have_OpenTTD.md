### Post by spoons on 2008-08-11
I play OpenTTD all the time. It's like Transport Tycoon, except:

You can change resolutions.
There are trams, and a 4th electrified railway type.
Canals and canalbridges have been introduced
You can have custom train/road/air/ship/infrastructure sets via newGRFs.
You can have 32bit graphics.

Loads of limits have been removed, no longer you get the "Too many trains in game" message.

It requires the original sample.cat and graphics from the original game.
[www.tt-forums.net](www.tt-forums.net) has lots of new graphics, you can find me there as AdditionalData too.

### Post by Ozor Mox on 2008-08-12
OpenTTD is a reimplementation of Transport Tycoon Deluxe, created in C++ and I think partly by reverse engineering the proprietary game. It still requires the original game for the graphics, sound and music data. The original game was created by Microprose, who have been bought by Atari, who seem to care very little about the game giving it the unofficial status of abandonware. You can download it online for free, or buy a copy from eBay for a just a couple of pounds.

The open source game expands on the original's limitations, as spoons says. No one has really worked on improving the rather awful AI in the original game (though there are efforts on this now), and it is now even worse because it can't handle the changes made to the game's engine. You can play without competition on your own, or play on online multiplayer servers.
