### Post by tristan on 2006-12-01
Apologies in advance for the length of what's turned out to be a mini-review!

I recently stumbled upon the Linux demo of Cold War and after playing for a few minutes was impressed enough to buy the game through [www.tuxgames.com](www.tuxgames.com) (quick delivery, non-existent email support it seems). It installed painlessly on my Ubuntu box. For a small independent Czech developer without a multimillion dollar development budget, Mindware have come up with an amazing game which is a hell of a lot of fun to play. Cold War is a 3D 3rd person stealth / shootemup / action adventure which has been reviewed to some extent for PC and Xbox (generally being unfairly compared to Splinter Cell) but really hasn't got the attention on Linux I would have expected. This is a shame because the Linux community seems to be crying out for decent quality native commercial games (well, I am at least :)). Here's my take:

Pros:
- Story: Entertaining story set in Soviet era Russia/Ukraine. The developers have succeeded in creating a deliberately corny and ironic B grade spy movie feel, complete with cheesy dialogue and all Russian characters speaking English at all times. You probably won't really appreciate this from the demo, and it seems most reviewers are immune to the irony too.

- Gameplay: Interesting gameplay which doesn't just involve killing things. While the plot is essentially linear you can approach a given situation in many ways, through stealth, constructing MacGuyver-like gadgets, or just blasting your way through. The game can be played as challenging and frenetic as you like. It can actually be played in complete pacifist mode in which you can't kill people, if you so desire (it's actually a liberating experience).

- Graphics: Unlike many games which licence out Doom3/Source/Unreal etc engines, Mindware wrote their own (MENG) and the result is a very attractive game with detailed models and textures, and interesting lighting effects (especially nice light bloom, smoke/fog/flame and weather effects). Some parts of the largely indoor environments (eg Chernobyl nuclear reactor) are huge and intricate. Even so, the framerates are fast and smooth and you don't need a Geforce 7900 to get >30fps. I get very smooth hiccup free framerates at 1200x1024, with an Nvidia 6600GT and 2.6GHz P4. All in all the graphics perfectly portray the various gloomy Soviet themed areas. The comic-like cut scenes also help convey the B movie feel.

- Sound: Witty yet corny dialogue, generally delivered well. The music is a very appropriate mix of James Bond and ambient. The sound effects nicely convey the feel of the areas, and the positional audio (eg guards walking nearby) helps the sense of tension.

- Controls: Standard WASD/mouse, with a context specific right mouse button menu for interacting with the environment. Generally controls and menus are pretty intuitive.

- Speed: The game loads in just a few seconds, as do new levels. For those like me who are getting sick of 60+ second waits for the latest games to load, this is very refreshing.

- Length: I've been playing for a few hours every night for 2 weeks. I'm pretty crap though, so it's probably realistically a 20+ hour game.

- Trust: No Starforce copy protection on the linux DVD, so you needn't worry about system instability, DVD drive meltdown or spyware.

Cons:
- AI: For those used to the incredible AI in games like Half-Life2, the AI in Cold War is definitely a little lacklustre. Enemies will take cover, but show no tactics otherwise. This is not a huge drawback since most of the time you are trying to avoid engaging them anyway. AI dialogue can get repetitive.

- Animations: Again not up to some recent games, but perfectly adequate. I don't think a huge amount of detailed motion capture went in to them. There seems to be some ragdoll physics though.

- Lighting: It's a real shame that there are no volumetric shadow effects a la Doom3 (or shadows of any kind really) which would have helped with atmosphere to a great degree. Still, it probably does help keep the frame rate so high.

All in all, for a budget title, Coldware is a very impressive game and well worth the addition to any linux gamers collection. Just play it on its own merits and I don't think you'll be disappointed. I give it 75-80% (would give it 90% if it had better AI, animations and shadowing).

No need to mess around with wine/cedega, nor dual boot to windows: just click, wait a few seconds, and have fun with a quality game. Thanks Mindware (developers), Linux Game Publishing (for bringing it to linux), and you for having the patience to read this far :).
