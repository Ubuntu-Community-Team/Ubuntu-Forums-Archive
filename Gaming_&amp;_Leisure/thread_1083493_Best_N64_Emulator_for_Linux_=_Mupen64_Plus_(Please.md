### Post by Yfrwlf on 2009-03-01
Since I've seen others asking what a good N54 emulator is for Linux, and since there are none in the Ubuntu repos, a great one is [Mupen64Plus]("http://code.google.com/p/mupen64plus/"), which is a continuation of Mupen64.

Works great for me, and no annoying compilation needed, though unfortunately being a straight up binary means no automatic updates.

If anyone else has a better one, feel free to add to this thread and help out those eager N64 gamers, but for me it's the best I've found so far.

### Post by Mike3 on 2009-12-19
You should look if there's any link to it under Applications -> Games. Otherwise, enable "View hidden files" in nautilus, navigate to your home directory, and search for Mupen64Plus. It should be in the folder labelled .mupen64plus. It may also be easier to get the binary from the repositories (as of Karmic, it's in there). They're also working on a beta of version 2.0 of Mupen64Plus now. You can check it out at <http://code.google.com/p/mupen64plus/> (without the carrots at the ends). However, you need to compile the tarball.
