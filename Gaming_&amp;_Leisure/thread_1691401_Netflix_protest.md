the reason we aren't seeing linux support for netflix/silverlight comes down to DRM.  linux developers don't want to incorporate DRM, it is against everything the community is based on.  so we have microsoft, who does not care to write silverlight for linux (they'd rather we bought a copy of windows), and we have the linux/open source community whom refuse to develop DRM for anything.  i agree, DRM is total [expletive] and i refuse to buy any DRM-laced product.  i firmly believe that creative commons and the public domain are the key to a healthy future for the arts, whereas industry associations and lobbyists want to retain intellectual property rights on EVERYTHING they invest in.

it comes down to the fundamental philosophical differences between the parties involved here.
