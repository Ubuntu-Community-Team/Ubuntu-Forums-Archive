### Post by QIII on 2014-11-22
Hello!

There is a database of compatibility [here]("https://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/").  Look for *at least* silver, but applications still may not run optimally.  Gold or better, of course, is desireable. Bronze might as well be taken as "well, maybe if you're lucky you can use some of the software's features".

Don't assume that just anything will run.  Be sure to browse the compatibility database for the specific software you want to run before forking over the hard-earned cash.  :)

Cheers!

Hello there,

I agree with QIII 100% - try before you buy and don't give so easily if something doesn't run out of the box. As you can see in the link provided above, your experience with Crossover will depend on what you want to get it to run and what system you are using. In my case, I can verify that Crossover will let you run more games in an easier, faster, more accessible and more reliable way than vanilla Wine or POL.

Why? This has been my **_personal_** experience so far and this is also the reason why I prefer it over vanilla Wine and POL:

**1.** 90% of my Steam library runs just fine via Crossover 14 - with minor or no tweaking at all.

**2.** A lot of games run just as fast as on Windows, some even faster, some slower and some don't run at all. Crossover ships with "performance enhanced graphics" enabled at default, which gives it the edge on other solutions like POL and vanilla Wine. 

**3.** Bottles. You can isolate apps so they won't "collide" with eachother.

**4.** Superior support channels: Phone, Ticket, E-Mail, IRC, Forum... pick the one you prefer most.

**5.** Some apps/games are officially supported and maintained by Codeweavers. Some of the most popular apps and games are supported, including Microsoft Office and World of Warcraft. Downloading and installing games supported via Crosstie is a matter of seconds or minutes.

**6.** Everyone using Crossover can jump in and help by providing information, creating or editing Crossties for other users to use and share or just by providing some basic feedback. 

In addition to number 6:

**7.** You don't have to pay for it, if you are willing to provide feedback from now and then. [Check this out]("https://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/faq/advocate_overview/").

**8. In addition to 1-7, quote:**

See [here]("https://www.codeweavers.com/products/faq/differences/") for more information.

Even vanilla Wine and POL recommend Crossover, if you want to **a)** support Wine and **b)** get the most polished experience of Wine.

**Tipps:**

If something doesn't run out of the box or if the ranking for an app is negative ("won't run at all" or bronze medal) - don't let that discourage you. I have been able to get apps ranked that way to run just fine. If you need help: Post on the forum or use the official support channels. Just like on WineHQ, some ppl tend to rank an app as not working or bronze, just because they were missing a single file or because one or two things weren't configured correctly. Unfortunately, a lot of ppl "forget" to provide more info with their rankings. Keep in mind: Apps supported by Codeweavers will most likely run out of the box, apps supported by the community via Crossties will most likely run out of the box, but might require some tweaking to achieve optimal results.

I also recommed using a single bottle per application and it's also wise to archieve (make a backup) of your favorite bottles before making changes to it. Just in case something goes wrong, you can quickly restore it to a working state. You can do this with three or four clicks in Crossover.

A lot of DirectX 9 apps and games will run just fine. Unfortunately, DX10/11 apps and games are not officially supported yet though.

On a 64-bit system: Crossover supports 32-bit apps only. Wine and POL also support 64-bit apps, but vanilla WineHQ recommends using a 32-bit version for compatability reasons.
