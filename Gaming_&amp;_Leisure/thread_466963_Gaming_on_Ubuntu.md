### Post by the_darkside_986 on 2007-06-07
Wine can be messy. I recommend dual-boot setup to keep Windows but also have Ubuntu. The installer can resize the NTFS Windows drive but it is important to backup all data and defrag the drive first.

Some people use Cedega to play games in Linux. However, Cedega costs money and is a proprietary fork of wine back when wine was under a BSD license, so there is no real advantage to using Cedega over a real Windows installation, especially when Cedega does not promise to play 100% of all Windows games.

There are also lots of good native Linux games. I like to play Sauerbraten. It is a free single/multi-player FPS for Windows, Unix, and Mac.

(Of course, I gave up the ability to play Oblivion and I'll just quit buying games like that until the devs have enough sense to release Linux ports. In the meantime I will devote time to study game development in C++.)
