### Post by bigseb on 2011-01-08
I have worked with many CAD programs over the years and have discovered that Linux does not have much to offer in that regard. Its sad but there you go. 

The only CAD software for Linux that's worth bothering with is VariCAD: 

         [http://www.varicad.com/en/home/](http://www.varicad.com/en/home/)

They have a version that's native to Linux and its not too bad depending on what your needs are (it is geared more towards mechanocal engineers). To be specific their filleting function is not very well developed. Then again at 500 Euros it is very competitively priced. As I am a toolmaker I do tons of product modelling I need to given a greater scope to give my clients the product they need so ultimately I bought Alibre Design Pro (only on Windows, I'm afraid) which I'm very happy with. Do VariCAD's thirty day trial and decide for yourself.

All other Linux CAD programs are a waste of time in my opinion. If productivity is key then go with windows.
