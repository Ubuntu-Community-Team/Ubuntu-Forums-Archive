### Post by kostkon on 2008-04-07
There is a possibility that your TFT monitor has a low response time that cannot catch-up with the fast moving graphics of this particular game. 

The response time is a characteristic of TFT monitors you need to take into account. Although, very rarely a monitor presents such problems of low response times.

The response time refers to the time that the pixels of monitor take to change from one colour to another (althought, the official measurements refer to the time it takes the pixels to change from black to white and then again to black).

A response time of 25ms or less is good enough for games (and pretty much a standard), so I will find it really strange if your monitor has a bigger value.

You could check the manual of your monitor for the response time value.

### Post by Project2501 on 2008-04-07
Cheap monitor plain & simple. The "3ms response time" is more than likely on grey to grey color. Once you bring the full color spectrum into play it raises considerably. That's how all these cheapo companies rate their response times (grey to grey).

I've only recently heard of this Hanns-G company and we bought an LCD for a server and it's pretty mediocre at best. It's fine for a server setup but I wouldn't even think of using it for a desktop, let alone one that plays games and/or movies.
