Just wanted to let everyone about an excellent baseball simulation game that is native Linux.  It is called Out of the Park Baseball, and I have been flawlessly running it on ubuntu 8.10 for over a year.

We should support those you strive to bring gaming goodness to our beloved Linux.

This is a very in-depth game where you can choose to play a the GM of an organization or you can choose to be a manager in control of everything, and even have the game happen pitch by pitch.

The game does not show players actually running but base runners are depicted on the baseball field. I understand the comment about watching a bunch of numbers, but the game is so much more than that, IMO.  There are often pre-order sales or other sales from time to time.  I ourchased my license for $29.99.  I have never regretted it.

The game will run in demo mode for almost an entire season.  You can download the game and give it a try for no cost.  I bet you will see that it has changed greatly since you last tried it.
