### Post by acoustibop on 2008-01-26
Many joysticks and controllers work perfectly in Ubuntu, arkangel - like leo_rockway, I have a Logitech controller - mine is a Rumblepad 2 (wired) - and it works perfectly; setting it up is literally just a matter of plugging it in.

If you already have a joystick, the logical next step is to plug it in and try it - if it works, you're laughing; if it doesn't, then you come back here and we'll see if we can help you with it.

If you don't have a joystick/controller, and are asking for advice about what works, what's best to use, etc, you need to say so - you also need to give us an idea of what sort of joystick/controller you want.

FWIW my Logitech Rumblepad 2 is excellent; I've also tried a number of other controllers in Edgy, Feisty and Gutsy and most have worked straight away.  The only one that had to be set up was an old gameport Gravis gamepad pro - the others were all USB controllers or connected via a USB adapter.

One thing, though, you won't get rumble/vibration to work with any controller in Linux.  Nobody seems to have written an adequate driver for it as yet.
