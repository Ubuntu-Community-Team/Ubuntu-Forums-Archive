### Post by Harleen on 2006-05-21
For LCDs a digital input is always the better choise. CRTs use an analogue signal to steer their electronic ray over the screen. So the digital image in your graphic card will be converted into a analogue signal and send to the monitor.
LCDs don't need that. If you use the analogue input, the signal will be converterd to an analogue signal in the graphic card and converted back into a digital signal in the monitor losing some precision. If you use the digital output, the monitor can display the image in you graphic card without any conversion, making the picture sharper.
Whether you see a difference or not depends on the used A/D D/A converters. Usually there won't be much a difference. Only in high resolutions you may actually see the sharper picture when using the digital connection.
