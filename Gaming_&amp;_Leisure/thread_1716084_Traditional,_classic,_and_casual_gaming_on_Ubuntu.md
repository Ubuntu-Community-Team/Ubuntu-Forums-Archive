### Post by davesalyers on 2011-03-27
Hi,

As an old time "gamer", I'm particularly interested in finding more options for traditional, classic, and casual gaming on Ubuntu - especially card and board games with computer opponents.

Here are some of my current favorites:

1) Cgoban - this is the game of Go - I use it with gnugo for computer AI.
2) Free Awale - this is a version of Mancala (an ancient game of pits and pebbles often found in Africa)
3) Chinese chess (GMChess) - this is the game of Xiangqi which is the Chinese version of Chess. (this is done beautifully, BTW)
4) GlChess, Fltk checkers, Lagno (Reversi/Othello), and Gnubackgammon - nuff said.
5) Kajongg - this is the KDE version of REAL Mahjongg which is a rummy tile game from China. (not the solitaire tile game)
6) Xmille - a clone of the Mille Bornes card game
7) Tali - a clone of Yahtzee
8  PokerTH - Texas Hold'Em Poker
9) Hearts and Aisleriot Solitaire - solid version of classic games
10) KsirK - a clone of the Risk boardgame. Well done.
11) Battle for Wesnoth - this is just excellent.

As soon as I can figure out Pioneers then I'll probably be adding that to my list. I'd love to hear your suggestions. I'm especially interested in finding native versions of such games as Rummy (regular and Gin), contract bridge, canasta, dominoes, Shogi (Japanese chess), and others that I may not be as familiar with - with the proviso that they have a computer player to play against.

### Post by davesalyers on 2011-03-28
I did manage to find some more interesting classic games out there:

1) Pasang Emas - Pasang Emas is a traditional game of Brunei which is commonly played during celebrations of royal weddings and the annual birthday festival of the Sultan of Brunei. Very interesting gameplay which is different from many others. Highly recommended.

2) Xbridge - I was finally able to finally find a version of Contract bridge which runs on Linux. I had to download it and add a launcher to my games menu which was not hard.

3) FreeDoko and LSkat - these are 2 games based on the card game of Schafkopf and Skat respectively which are other types of trick taking card game (like Bridge) and popular in Europe.

4) GSeptica - the GTK implementation of a popular card game, known in Romania as Septica. It quick and simple and quite addictive.

Has anyone else found any other gems out there? Since my background was in cultural anthropology, I love this stuff.
