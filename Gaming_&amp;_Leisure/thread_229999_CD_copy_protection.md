### Post by Steggy on 2006-08-05
One option may be to, while in Windows, download Daemon Tools (search Google for it), then use its disc-protection emulation feature while making an image of the disc* (I'd suggest Alcohol 120% for this, but there are other options out there.) This may work; it may not. I use this method to play Civ3 on Windows without the disc (because I hate having to get the disc out to play the game every time.) I also believe I had to install from the disc image in order for the game to work correctly.

I haven't tried it on Ubuntu, and I'm currently away from my computer so I can't find out if the disc image I made will work on Ubuntu, but it's worth a shot if you really want to play the game.

* The Daemon Tools help files (or online manual) have instructions on the exact process to do this.
