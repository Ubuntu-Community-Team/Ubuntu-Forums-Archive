### Post by rossjman1 on 2008-09-08
How can you be bored with Wolfenstein: Enemy Territory?!?!?!!!!one!!

Anyway there are some good games, but the best ones (besides ET) I have installed are actually Windows games that I run through wine. The ones that are red are commercial games (aquire them as you wish ;))
* [COLOR="Red"]Anno 1602 (windows game, also called 1602 A.D., basically you settle the New World and develop your own colony a la Sim City)[/COLOR]
* BSG: Beyond the Red Line (Linux, very promising 3d space shooter based off freespace - has a story (follows the show) and even has voice acting. There are only 3 levels included in the demo, but the full game will be free when it comes out. You can also download fan created missions)
* FreeCiv/FreeCol (Linux, you probably heard of these already, I prefer FreeCol)
* [COLOR="Red"]Settlers 2 Gold Editions (Windows, an oldie but goodie, look it up at gamespot or whereever)[/COLOR]
* Vega Strike (Linux, an intense 3d space trading game)
* Tile Racer (Linux, seems like a stunts type game, and I just found out about it, but have yet to try it. [http://tileracer.model-view.com/tl/index.php/media.html](http://tileracer.model-view.com/tl/index.php/media.html))

There are also plenty of smaller coffee break style games:
* Gweled (linux, clone of Bejeweled and would fit very well in the Gnome-games collection)
* IceBreaker (linux, clone of Jezzball)
* Icy Tower (Windows, very addicting arcade style game where you jump up on platforms)
* lBreakout2 (Linux, breakout/arkanoid clone)
* aTanks (Linux, Scorched Earth clone)
