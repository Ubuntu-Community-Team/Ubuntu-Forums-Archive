### Post by Mongoose on 2007-04-21
Well, I see some misinformation in this thread.  Too be honest I can't really blame all the hate for the title.  A lot of the problems with the game are due to problems with the production company ATARI, which I'm sure everyone knows the story of the financial issues, etc.  I'm not going to address any of that stuff.  I will say that the engine is completely new, however the rules system is a rewrite of the BIOWARE D&D rules for 3.5, new classes, etc.  

Sure.  It would have been nice if OpenGL was chosen from the start, and then there would have been an OS X and Linux build more than likely.  It also would be nice to have Linux server support, but if ATARI isn't paying you can't really blame the developer.  I was really hoping to get to write a Linux port at the start of the project to be honest, but it wasn't in the cards.  If you never worked in the game industry it might be hard to understand how things work out sometimes.  I personally think some things could have been handled better in terms of the involved parties: WotC, BIOWARE, and ATARI.  You can feel kind of hamstrung on licensed titles to begin with, and ATARI's problems feed into other issues.  Not that the developer is completely blameless here, but there is only so much you can do from one side of the table imho.  I'm not angry at all about any of that, but I honestly can't say I'm not disappointed.  You'll always have a little disappointment with any project, however if you want to improve to take those lessons along with what goes right and build for the future.  That's why game postmortems are so popular.   ;)

This is just my personal opinion, and doesn't reflect the views of my past or present companies.  Sorry for getting a little ranty there.   Please don't email me about any of this.  I'm just telling the other side.
