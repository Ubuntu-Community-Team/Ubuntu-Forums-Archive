### Post by arekk76 on 2014-07-26
there are couple of options when it comes to chess software on ubuntu: if you just want to play against AI then indeed glchess is an interesting option, if you want to save and analyse your games you can use chessx (download stockfish, which currently is the strongest chess engine, and install it in chessx). my personal preference, regardless of whether i want to play against AI or other people, is FICS (free internet chess server). there are couple of native linux interfaces which allow you to connect to FICS but arguably the best option is to run babaschess in wine, it offers best functionality, looks great, and is very customisable. it also runs perfectly in wine with no glitches or anything. on FICS (apart from playing against other people) you can play against chess engines of various strength from very weak to super-grandmaster level. cheers
