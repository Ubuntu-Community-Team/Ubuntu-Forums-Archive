### Post by hikaricore on 2009-03-24
Firstly WINE didn't mess up anyone's system.  Stupidity and not caring to be bothered to understand what they were doing is likely the culprit.
Second WINE installation is quite easy and there is plenty of info out there on how to do it, as well as a database of what works and doesn't work ( [http://appdb.winehq.org](http://appdb.winehq.org) ) we even have a subforum for wine: [http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=313](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=313)
Third there are tons of native games for Linux, even some you may be familiar with such as the [Doom/Quake]("http://zerowing.idsoftware.com/") series and [World of Goo]("http://www.worldofgoo.com/").

Further info about Linux games can be found in several locations:
[http://freegamer.blogspot.com/](http://freegamer.blogspot.com/)
[http://www.happypenguin.org/](http://www.happypenguin.org/)
[http://gaming.gwos.org/doku.php](http://gaming.gwos.org/doku.php)

Some games can be found through the Add/Remove Programs option in your Applicaitons menu or though Synaptic package manager.
You can also check Getdeb.net: [http://www.getdeb.net/](http://www.getdeb.net/) For new and updated games that aren't in the Ubuntu repos.
