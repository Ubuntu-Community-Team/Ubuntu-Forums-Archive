### Post by KIAaze on 2010-02-07
Commercial offerings (where most big mainstream GNU/Linux compatible games are):
[http://www.tuxgames.com/](http://www.tuxgames.com/)
[http://www.linuxgamepublishing.com/products.php?](http://www.linuxgamepublishing.com/products.php?)

There are few "big mainstream games", but lots of "small indie games". ;)
ex:
-Lugaru
-World of Goo
-Cave Story

cf [TIGDB]("http://db.tigsource.com/browse?genre=&platform=linux&max-price=&release=&min-rating=&developer=&publisher=&engine=&code=&content=&language=&tag=&sort_by=name&sort_dir=asc")

Upcoming: [http://lgn.linux-hardcore.com/list-of-upcoming-gnu-linux-games/](http://lgn.linux-hardcore.com/list-of-upcoming-gnu-linux-games/)

For more, look through these sites: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=359842](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=359842)

And apart from Wine, there are also engines/emulators like:
-SCUMMVM
-console emulators (ZSNES, etc)
-game specific engines working on GNU/Linux and requiring original game data (Jagged Alliance, Doom, etc)
which allow playing "old big mainstream games". :)
