### Post by oldrocker99 on 2018-07-31
The best 2018 games, for my money, are the delightful Tower of Time, an action RPG with a unique and totally original combat system, Neverwinter Nights Enhanced Edition, which I have enjoyably played thousands of hours, and was the one commercial Linux game in 2008, when I moved forever to Linux, and which is cross-platform and runs in any resolution up to 4K and, of course, the amazing Dominions 5, which is the **VERY BEST** fantasy turn-based game available. Ever.

NO TBS has held my attention and playtime as much as the Dominions series, which I've been playing since 2009. At least 90 nations, none of which play the same, in three ages, thousands of unique unit s, hundreds of unique spells (not 6 differently-named Fireball spells), 8 types of magic and 8 schools of magic, and battles you only can watch. It is 100% moddable (and there are LOTS of mods and maps available), and is a very popular online game. You have to know the game very, very well to win online (something like DOTA), and it's easy to have your hat handed to you before 10 turns have elapsed. The AI is pretty tough for *most* people, although some grognards complain about it. 

I tried a MP game once, and had the above experience. No matter. The game is infinitely replayable. It costs $40 on Steam, and is Worth. Every. Penny.

Tower of Rime is a $24.95 bargain, and Neverwinter Nights EE is a steal at $19.95. NWN is the most game you can buy, with hundreds of AAA FREE adventures for download at [https://neverwintervault.org/](https://neverwintervault.org/).

That should set you up for a while. Linux has always had a surfeit of FPS games, and still only a few RTS games.

I also like the card game monster-slaying Slay the Spire.
