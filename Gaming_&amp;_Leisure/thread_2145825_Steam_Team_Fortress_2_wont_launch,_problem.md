### Post by King Dude on 2013-05-16
For the record, AMD Radeon video cards are a lot of trouble when it comes to Linux. Even my ATI Radeon HD4350 was giving me crap a while back. I mean, I love AMD's products, but they seriously need to step it up in the Linux compatibility.

Here's what I suggest:

[B]1.) Trade in the AMD card for an Nvidia card.

[/B]I heard from multiple people that Nvidia cards are more reliable in driver compatibility, so this might be the way to go. However, I'm not sure if this is true or not, so be warned!

**2.) Seek support from AMD and Valve's official support forum.**

There are three players in this game, AMD, Valve, and Ubuntu. You might find support if you put help threads in the other two support forums, and remember to routinely check them for helpful information.

[http://forums.amd.com/game/](http://forums.amd.com/game/) -AMD's Forum
[http://steamcommunity.com/discussions/forum/1/](http://steamcommunity.com/discussions/forum/1/) -Valve (Steam) Support Forum
