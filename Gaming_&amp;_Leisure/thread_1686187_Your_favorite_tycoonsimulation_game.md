### Post by gerowen on 2011-02-11
> **whatthefunk said:**
> Im just starting to look into games for Linux.  I really like tycoon games, simulation games, and games like Civilization.  Can anybody recommend a good open source game?  Thanks.

I play Warcraft: Orcs and Humans in Dosbox.  World of Goo has a Linux version in .deb format.  You have to pay for it, but it's an excellent physics game in the style of Angry Birds.  Pingus is an addicting little Lemings clone.  My wife and I spend entire nights playing Battle for Wesnoth against one another between our two Ubuntu computers.  I also like to play Unreal Tournament 2004.  It's a little bit of a pain to get the Linux executables installed and everything, but it's an excellent game.  On top of that I use Gens, Snes9x and Mupen64Plus to play old school games I used to own before my first house burned in 2001.

All of those above are in the repos with the exception of UT 2k4.  I bought World of Goo during a little online competition thing, but I've noticed that it has been added to the software center so you can just buy it from there and I guess it licenses it to your Ubuntu One account.
