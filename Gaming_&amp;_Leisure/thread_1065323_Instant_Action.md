### Post by SlackerTD on 2009-02-10
It's unlikely to ever come to Linux. Take a look at this [response]("http://forum.beta.instantaction.com/smf/index.php/topic,1726.msg69862.html#msg69862") from the IA forum.
> Yes, at the time that article was written,  we were very early on in the process, and indeed it was being considered.  The plugin I believe even had a linux build at one point.  But given the very small percentage of linux users -- who are linux only (a large chunk of linux users often dual boot or have a windows box for gaming), and that we would have to create linux build of every game,  linux support is currently off the table.  Not to say that we aren't keeping an eye on it, or that it is also ruled out for the future, but right now there is a plan to come to linux in the near future.
Instantaction is created by Garage Games. They are not very Linux friendly any more. Most of their tech now is 'community supported' if one wants a Linux version (IE: port it yourselves). Many of the active people in the Linux community with GG have moved on to other engines (as they've gotten tired of GG's lack of help when people post patches, send emails, etc). In other words, IA will most likely be a dead end, even though there are three games I see on there in which I have previous versions of in Linux.

You can however, read this [KB article]("http://support.instantaction.com/index.php?_m=knowledgebase&_a=viewarticle&kbarticleid=79") on how to add yourself to a mailing list about Linux support should it ever happen (perhaps with enough people signing up for it?)
