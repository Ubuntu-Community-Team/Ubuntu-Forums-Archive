> **Rumbl3 said:**

I had thought about guild wars but i was afraid of buying just one verison. Because i was afraid i'd be stuck playing only the quest that come with it and get stuck basicly not being able to play multiplayer somewhat unless i bought all of them.

You can always buy additional expansions later. The story quests take quite a long time to do anyhow so you wont be pissed by buying it and running out of stuff to do.

There two big dissimilarities between Guild Wars and conventional MMORPG's.

The first is that the only place you interact with people in general population is in cities. To go on quests you group up with your friends and head off on quests. Whenever you leave a town it's like entering an instance. Only your group exists in that particular world until you go back into town. This isn't to say that there aren't lots of chances to interact with other people. Last I played the major towns were very densely populated all hours of the day.

The second being that there is one main story line quest that each version of the game centers around. The biggest difference in the versions are what quest you'll be working on. There's some other differences like what areas you can travel to and what classes you can pick, but as soon as you build up some questing buddies to join a guild it feels a lot like any other MMO except that it's quest focused as opposed to grinding your way up the ladder.

Also, there's henchmen NPC's you can take on to quest with you if no one is on to group with so it's not like you cant play it solo. It's just a little bit harder that way.
