### Post by sffvba[e0rt on 2014-04-23
Ensure you have a swap partition of at least 2GB and run as light as possible system (in other words, something like LXDE rather than Unity) and don't have any unnecessary applications starting up with your system (if you are not using Steam you don't need it running).

Other than that the best way is to add more RAM.  Also, if you are really using DDR2 RAM it means your system is a few generations old by now and it may even mean your current CPU/GPU are struggling with the newer high-end games.
