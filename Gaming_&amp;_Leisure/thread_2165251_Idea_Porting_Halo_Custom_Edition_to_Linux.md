### Post by LillyDragon on 2013-08-04
It sure would be nice, since I still enjoy playing it myself, and don't have an Nvidea card to run it through WINE, but I don't expect either Microsoft, Gearbox - the guys who ported it over to PC in the first place - or 343 Studios do that themselves, let alone release the old engine's source code, so fans could keep it patched up through the years, (I guess it's enough to them that they released Custom Edition, and took out the CD copy-protection in the final patch.) and fix some of its problems. I'm pretty sure the PC port doesn't even have basic things like culling for 3D terrain, so optimal performance past 30FPS isn't very realistic for mid-end chipsets.

OpenMW doesn't have that the benefit of the game's source code either, so it's not exactly a port like [AlephOne](http://source.bungie.org/), and is doing everything from scratch. That's probably the only way it could happen for the first Halo, but replicating a decade-old engine isn't easy. Then there's understanding the weirdly self-contained map format of the custom edition. (Which I'm sure more people would want to recreate, than the original retail release.) Even if something like a re-implementation were to happen right now, it would take years to reach a mature, usable state.

Bearing that in mind, it couldn't hurt to start a petition for the Halo PC ports to get some modern support. Would be nice to see them digitally re-released, or the recent Anniversery edition to get a PC port. I understand Microsoft wants to maintain some exclusivity on their XBOX with Reach, ODST, and Halo 3, but c'mon, it'd be crazy not to port the remake of the first game to the PC, when the original was on that platform too.
