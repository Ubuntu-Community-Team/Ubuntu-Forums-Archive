### Post by J.K.Makowka on 2010-11-07
No Intel CPUs have something called Hyperthreading, which enables a cpu to simulate another one... very simplified ;)
i3 and i5 have AFAIK all only 2 cores, but with HT they can run 4 threads at once. Some i7 are real quad core cpus, but not all of them.

Edit... you don't really need quad core, especially not in a Laptop. Be careful with Nvidia's Optimus technology however! That is not and probably will not for a long time be supported under Linux (the Nvidia GPU works not at all then).
