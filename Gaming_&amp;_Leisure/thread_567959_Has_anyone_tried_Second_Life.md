### Post by eye208 on 2007-10-05
> **ROUBOS said:**
> You have to pay for everything.
No.

There are only two things in SL that are not free: land ownership and file uploads. Everything else is free. You can buy items from other players, but you're not required to do so. Your avatar does not need food or a home. If you can build cool stuff or write useful scripts, you might be able to sell them to others. Think of SL as a MMORPG game SDK: Everything you can see in-world has been built from scratch by the residents. There is no prefabricated content. Initially there was (almost) nothing at all.

Their monthly fee is completely optional. I've been a resident since Jan 2007. I never paid a dime for this game.

The SL viewer for Linux is very good. Probably the most stable of all three, despite its official "alpha" status. But you need a good video card to play. And if you want to make machinima (like me), a dual core system helps a great deal when capturing video from the screen.

Edit: And no, it's not about "dressing up". It's about building a world. Besides the things usually shown in the media there are SciFi areas, fantasy roleplay areas, anime areas, artistic areas, music performances etc.

### Post by eye208 on 2007-10-09
> **ripfox said:**
> What is this exactly?
It's an LSL ("Linden Scripting Language") function.

As I said before, SL is not a game but a game SDK. You can build objects and make them do things by adding scripts. You can make interactive buildings, vehicles and weapons that way. So if you want to shoot people, you can either play one of the existing FPS games in SL or build your own one. The interesting thing about it is that you need not do it all by yourself. SL is an MMO environment. There are people who focus on making weapons, and others who focus on making buildings, or vehicles, or character design. By bringing them together, you can set up an FPS game or racing game or any other type of game. SL also has a physics engine (Havok 1, soon to be replaced by Havok 4).

It takes a while to figure out how these things work. But once you do, SL can be very addictive.

### Post by click170 on 2007-10-12
You shouldn't look at Second Life as a game, SL is a platform.  To look at it as a game is to expect something that Second Life won't provide.

Second Life has no goal, no end, no finish, and in that sense it is boring.

At the same time, MSN/ICQ/AIM is boring if all you do is wait for it to do something.

Second Life is a 3 Dimensional chat client, that's how simple it is.  You can build anything you like and you can even script your 'things', or Prims, to communicate with the Internet using HTTP, but if you go into Second Life looking for a goal then don't expect to find it unless you build it yourself first.

Thats my take on it anyway.
