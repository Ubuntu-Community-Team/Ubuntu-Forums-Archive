### Post by Sofox on 2010-10-22
Take a look at the "For Purchase" section of the Ubuntu Software Center (assuming you're on the 10.10 Maverick Meerkat). In addition to the Fluendo DVD player, it now has it's second ever paid app whichwdas   is a video game!
The game is called Brukkon (gameplay vid here: [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qxrUaTguEZs](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qxrUaTguEZs) ) and to be honest it looks a bit low quality, however the fact is that this is a first! No other desktop operating system has an App Store and this is the first game to have ever been released for one.
This makes it a historic moment, much like Chu Chu Rocket was the first (in the western market) game to demonstrate online multiplayer on consoles, OS app stores are only going to get bigger with both Microsoft and Apple planning their own versions.
What's more, with hard sales figures, a guaranteed method of distributing their games, and the assurances of a primary OS to focus on, this could lead to a large influx of native games for Ubuntu that can only help things as a whole.
I think we should bring attention to this through gaming blogs and the like. It's not often a "first" like this happens, and we can use this as an opportunity to drum up interest in Ubuntu and Linux gaming as a whole.
