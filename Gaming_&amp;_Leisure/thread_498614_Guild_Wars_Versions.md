### Post by dorath on 2007-07-11
Personally, I would recommend starting off with Guild Wars: Nightfall.

The major reason is the addition of Heros.  All three campaigns allow you to round out your party with henchmen if you can't, or simply don't want to, find other players.  Henchmen come with an unchangable set of skills, armor, and weapons.  Heros on the other hand, are customizable.  You can choose which skills they'll use, what weapons they'll carry, and you can customize their armor a bit too.

You'll reach evel 20 (which is the level cap) faster in Nightfall than in Prophecies, which I consider a plus.  (I haven't picked up Factions, so can't compare that one.)

One downside of the Nightfall campain is that it has five fewer missions than Prophecies (20 vs 25).  That's not a huge minus though, and it's more than made up for by the Heros (IMO of course).  If you pick up Nightfall first you'll be able to use your Heros for the Prophecies missions.

The Heroes are really beneficial, especially as a new player (though they can be expensive if you try to outfit them with the best gear at all times - but that is unnecessary). You reach level 20 faster in Nightfall than in Prophecies but slower than in Factions - but I consider it a good thing.

Do NOT get Factions as your first one. It moves way too quick for someone new to the game. Depending on how many hours a day you play, you could reach level 20 in a day or two which is, imo, way too quick for a beginner. It is also much shorter than all of the other campaigns.

Prophecies is by far the longest - while it is 25 missions to Nightfall's 20, it goes by much slower. It has much more of basic content (more land, missions and quests) but is lacking in heroes. Also, Nightfall is the newest so there may be more people to play with there.

On second thought I'd say go with either Prophecies or Nightfall. Both would be good for a beginner - just do not get Factions as your first. If you want all three, get Factions as your last.
