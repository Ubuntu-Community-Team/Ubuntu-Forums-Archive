### Post by rizzeh on 2011-02-02
Linux friendliest game developer is iD software, imo. All their games have native linux binaries, at least the ones i've tried. quakelive.com is still fun to play, too :guitar: 
 If you are looking for the latest greatest graphics, windows7  is probably the best platform for that as you'd get DirectX 11 and big money developers. Linux games are often concentrating on gameplay 1st and graphics distinct 2nd.. or not at all :D I prefer it that way tbh. Lugaru is a good example - 34Mbs of data gets you days of gaming while still managing to look acceptable, i'm well impressed by that.

Im sure red alert2 will work fine in [wine]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=252")
