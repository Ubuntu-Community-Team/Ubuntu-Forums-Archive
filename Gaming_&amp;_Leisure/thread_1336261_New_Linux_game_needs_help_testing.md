### Post by pieman711 on 2009-11-24
I just downloaded it and it ran fine straight from the box with no hassle (always a good start). The game was great, really enjoyable. Quite similar to portal with the dark comedy on the screens in the level, and the types of puzzle but still plenty of originality. 
I thought the mask border was quite annoying especially when you are trying to land in quite tight spots but there is a way of turning it off in the menu anyway.
As far as game-play goes it's really easy to get the basic skills but fun trying to master them.
I rushed through the demo levels a few mins and I know that the challenges and level editor help extend the life of a game but I'd be worried that if the other levels in the full game weren't enough of a challenge it might be quite short. From the description of the laser guns et a,l though, this should hopefully not be a problem.

The only technical issue I noticed, and this is a very minor one, is that a bit of writing on some of the in game graffiti had been cut off (see attached image)

The graphics aren't as shiny as the portal but it doesn't look like they're trying to be. Using similar graphics style as seen in Warsow. I can't really comment on the sound as I was running amarok in the back ground sorry. 

Altogether I loved it and am particulary pleased that it ran natively in linux with no hassle!


--- just saw the post from thelions... a disclaimer I'm running this on a intel core duo with a nvidia GeForce 8800GT and 2gb Ram.
