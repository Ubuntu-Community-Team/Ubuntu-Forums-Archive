### Post by darckwolff on 2012-01-13
Overall, our plan is to just make games for everyone to enjoy. Business view is: make games affordable, but make them worth paying for. We plan on distributing digital and physical copies, though the physical will be on USB sticks, each designed special for that game. We decided on that for the entire sake of portability. CDs/DVD's usually end up scratched or broken, so we wanted a more durable medium, and this one can be partitioned to have a Windows/Linux/Mac only partition too. The save files will be on their own partition so you can play on any OS.
Eventually, we plan on releasing our own console, which is in the barest of planning stages, as in we know what we want. Our team currently consists of 4 people, though only 3 will develop, the fourth is for accounting. We have little experience, though none in the coding dept, and that is why I am learning it as best as I can. Any help would be appreciated, and anyone who can help make each game a reality will get a complimentary copy, and/or be hired on to get paid to make the games.

### Post by darckwolff on 2012-02-21
Just a note: From the beginning we decided that all our games would initially be closed source. We are going to somewhat follow the idSoft model, and open source our games based on a Sales over Time model. If the game sells (for the first three I believe is what we set it as) over 25k copies, then they will be open sourced under a custom license, which will basically state do whatever you want, but don't sell the game, the engine for it can be reused though for commercial purposes, and any company/individual who makes over 100k during a fiscal year off our engine must purchase a commercial license.
