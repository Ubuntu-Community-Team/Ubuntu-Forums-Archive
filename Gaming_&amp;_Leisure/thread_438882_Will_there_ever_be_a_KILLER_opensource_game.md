### Post by PrimoTurbo on 2007-05-10
I'm wondering why it's the case then? Closed source games require a lot more time to be created because they often have to create their own engine or license and then modify it.

Open source games have tons of free engines to use.

Closed source games often have to make all textures from scratch or maybe buy some texture packs.

Open source has tons of textures available from other open source games and retexturing projects, which they can use. Same with models on polycount and such.

You can have a team of 500 people creating little bits of the game, compared to 20/30 in a closed source game and yet the closed source game is the one that's complete, functional and look stunning after 3-4 years of development.

### Post by Henry Rayker on 2007-05-11
As far as open source games vs closed source games goes, the problems really do come down to money...game development from start to finish is a very time-intensive process. The professionally made games have all the time in the world to work on them (because they get paid for their work) while the open source volunteer games have to be made in spare time. While some of this time deficit is made up for by adding more people to the dev team, there is a point of diminishing returns. Everything has to funnel through some key person at some time and his/her time is also limited.

As far as whether an open source game will be of the same quality as closed source, if you mention graphics in that same sentence then, in your mind, no; open source games will probably never have graphics and effects that surpass the closed source games. The reason for this is the time/money issue. If you ignore graphics, however, and you're talking about gameplay, then I'd say that it is possible...although not terribly probable. Even gameplay requires a lot of work for the developers...

Additionally, all of the really good talent will already be employed by the industry (and covered in pretty steep NDAs, I'm sure). Except for a very small minority, why would you make absolutely nothing off of something if you are good enough to make a pretty good living off of it?
