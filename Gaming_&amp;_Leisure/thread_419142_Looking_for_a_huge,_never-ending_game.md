### Post by nihilocrat on 2007-04-23
I am completely enamored with the Total War series (namely Rome: Total War). It's not technically neverending, but like Civilization, you have the option to keep playing after your faction is victorious. It has the strategy of Civilization without the micromanagement and (obviously) a much, much more developed combat model and is the first series that I know of that tries to simulate large scale pre-gunpowder combat with a high degree of granularity (as in, each single unit is autonomous and independent although they stick together in groups and are commanded in groups).

Also, still on the RTS front (but not never-ending), the Kohan series (of which the first incarnation is available for Linux thanks to Loki) is an excellent redesign of the RTS genre. The series largely eliminates micromanagement by not having resource harvesting per se and forcing you to recruit and control your units from a company level rather than a unit-by-unit level.

Also, a much more obscure game, you should check out [Dwarf Fortress](http://www.bay12games.com/dwarves/). It's basically "Dungeon Keeper meets Nethack meets the Sims". The game can be neverending (but don't dig too deep!) and is really strategically interesting, though the micromanagement can get really annoying when your fortress gets to 100+ dwarves. Since it's constantly in development, I'm assuming the dev (yes, one dev) will redesign the work queuing system to make it less annoying.
