### Post by Cappy on 2008-06-13
I doubt you are using a motherboard with hybrid AGP and PCI-E, they aren't very common. PCI-E is newer/better.

If it has to work out of the box then get a Nvidia 8600; they are in the $100 range.

The 8800s are about $150 for the cheap ones.

Finally, the 9600s are nice for $150 but you would have to install drivers with a program that does it for you [called Envy].

Those are all PCI-E cards.

### Post by cogadh on 2008-06-13
Don't bother with PCI, you will get far better performance out of AGP. For good performance at a decent price, get a GeForce 7600 GT or if you can't find that for around $100, a GS instead. Its new enough to run everything in the DirectX 9 generation of Windows games and works perfectly in Ubuntu with the restricted drivers. If you are looking for DirectX 10 capability in your Windows games (assuming you have Vista), then you'll need to look at the 8XXX series, but I don't think you'll find any of those in the $100 range. Just one extra note, since you are looking for ease of use across Windows and Linux, stick with Nvidia products only, they have the best driver support on both platforms.

### Post by 1337 on 2008-06-13
The scheme of choosing good video card that will work with Linux is very simple, take an nVidia card with 600 or 800 number in the name like Geforce 6600/6800/7600/7800/8600/8800... etc. the exception from this scheme is 7300 card which is very good if it has DDR3 memory. Avoid ATI at all cost if you are thinking about serious gaming on linux not only native games but also via Wine/Cedega. Ah and would recommend you AGP of course, if you don't have PCI Express slot on your motherboard.
