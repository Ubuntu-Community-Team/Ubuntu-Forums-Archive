### Post by whitewolfshield on 2008-05-09
keyboard and mouse is always more accurate than gamepad gaming (take this from a long time gamer). If you want to get better, play more. Some people just have a niche and/or play more way more than other people --aka no life. If you suck at FPS, don't worry; it just means you've had less time to waist.

FPS advice that they don't tell you:

1. Find your niche: whether it's snipping, grenades, spray and pray, or just running up on a MF and throwing a couple of 16 gage rounds down their throat. Everyone has something their good at in FPS. I personally prefer grenades, which is all about how and where you throw them. 
2. Learn your physics engine: If you know that some explosives go through walls in their damage; where in many older games the explosions lacked clipping --goldeneye *sigh*-- you get a tactical advantage if you've mastered it. Also, things like bullets doing less damage in water is a huge advantage if you feel like being covert underwater.
3. Just because it's a big weapon doesn't mean it's better. In deathmatch, getting very close to someone with a RPG means they lose point (generally) for suicide, either you gain points or they lose points.
