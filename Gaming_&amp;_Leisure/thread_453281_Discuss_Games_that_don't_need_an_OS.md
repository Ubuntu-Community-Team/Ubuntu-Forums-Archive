### Post by tocleora on 2007-05-24
[SIZE="2"]I just bought a new computer yesterday. or parts rather (yes, I got an nvida card! I'm excited cause right now I'm running an Intel card YUK) and being a HUGE Halo fan, I'm excited to finally have a computer that will run Halo 2.  HOWEVER, what I'm not excited about is the idea that I'm going to have to buy [COLOR="DarkRed"]Vista[/COLOR] to play it. So instead of spending $50, [COLOR="DarkRed"]I'll have to spend $150[/COLOR] (minimum).  It took me I want to say a couple of years but at least a year to move from 98 to XP and I'm sure I'll do the same with Vista. [COLOR="DarkRed"]I just don't like supporting Microsoft's Monopolistic tendencies[/COLOR] and try to avoid it as much as possible.  Then I got to thinking, I wonder if a lot of companies are possibly going to start writing games for other OS's based on assumption I have that overall the perception of Vista isn't good.  Then a visual of a game sitting at the store that had "[COLOR="DarkRed"]Works on Any OS[/COLOR]" on the box came to mind.  Wouldn't that be great?  Of course that wouldn't happen easily, because then game companies would have to learn not only multiple Operating Systems but make sure as far as LInux is concerned that it worked on multiple distributions and that just sounds really complicated and it seems it would cut into their game development time.  Then I thought back to the DOS days where you put in a disk or a CD and although it *did* need an operating system, it seemed to pretty much take care of itself.  They have bootable CD's now, how great would it be to be able to [COLOR="DarkRed"]put in a CD and it boot to the game? no questions asked?[/COLOR]  I'm sure it's a lot more complicated than it sounds, but would it be better than trying to learn every OS and every distro?

Picture this... You put in the CD, which boots to a very stripped version of Linux (Ubuntu? with Gnome?).  It detects the partition types on your hard drive, Fat32, ext3, NTFS, whatever Mac is... then looks on that partition for a game folder.  If it doesn't find the game folder, it checks hard drive space, maybe checks system requirements (Sorry ATI users!)... installs the game in that folder (the important files at least).  then it can either [COLOR="DarkRed"]just start the game[/COLOR], or if it needs to reboot for some reason, you reboot the computer, it boots to the CD, the same check happens, it starts the game.  You now can [COLOR="DarkRed"]play the game no matter what OS you are using[/COLOR]... and Game developers can now just [COLOR="DarkRed"]develop for *one* operating system, and hit everyone*[/COLOR].  Linux and OS X users will no longer have to complain about not having the game on their OS's, because as long as you have the drive space and meet the requirements, you're set.  This would also more than likely [COLOR="DarkRed"]force the hardware companies to be more compliant with linux[/COLOR], as ATI won't want all of their gamers to go to Nvidia.  [COLOR="DarkRed"]AND BEST OF ALL - WE WOULDN'T HAVE TO SUPPORT MICROSOFT ANYMORE[/COLOR]!!!! Well, those of us that just use it for gaming at least. :)  I wonder if Adobe could use this...

Obviously this is just a brain storm, I know little about what's involved and I offer this opportunity for you to give me your educated responses on the topic.  Let's discuss. :)

### Post by chrisLACHCIK5084 on 2007-05-24
well here's what i did...i bought a large computer case (was originally a case for some big raid server thingy or something) and i took apart a Mac and a PC. i put the motherboard on one side with two 1tb hdd's and the other one on the other side. i had two powersupplies and they each had a dvd/cd writer for each motherboard. i bought a bunch of fans to keep the damn thing cool. and so on one harddrive on the PC side i installed ubuntu/windows xp and the other harddrive ran vista. on the mac i ran osx/ubuntu 6.10 for mac. i ran off of two monitors and now i have all the systems i need! :)

the total cost of the project was $11,000+ because i had to buy alot of parts off of the internet as well and i shopped around at target and circuit city.
