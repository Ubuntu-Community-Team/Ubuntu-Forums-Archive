### Post by proteusmoteus on 2010-08-11
Migrating to linux as a gamer is a bad idea. However your past with gentoo indicates you have the ability and patience for problem solving. If you got a sense of satisfaction from figuring out the puzzles of getting gentoo working then being a linux gamer is an interesting idea rather than a bad one.

Their is a very small amount of [games that work well on linux]("http://www.youtube.com/user/Jakejw93#g/c/E522AE8F94CA7957") relative to windows.

Getting games that were not purposefully made linux compatible can be [often be made to work]("http://www.youtube.com/user/Jakejw93#g/c/89E71A442563E8AC") with the [wine project]("http://wiki.winehq.org/FAQ"). Accomplishing this requires patience and problem solving. Reporting bugs, sharing your solution with others, and researching each game can give its own satisfaction. Wine has many resources including [forums]("http://forum.winehq.org/viewforum.php?f=2") ([ubuntu]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=313")) and a [database]("http://appdb.winehq.org/") sorted by how easy and well you can expect the [resulting program]("http://appdb.winehq.org/help/?sTopic=maintainer_ratings") to work.
