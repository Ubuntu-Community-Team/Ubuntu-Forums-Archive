### Post by Melcar on 2008-12-30
If you're a gamer and/or are looking for top of the line 3D performance, the proprietary drivers will always be your best bet.  These drivers will be useful for the average user (almost everyone) who simply wants working and stable 2D/3D.  It's unrealistic to think that radeon/radeonhd will ever catch up to fglrx in terms of raw performance; at most one could expect 80% or so in a few years (or even months).  Even with the memory manager on full swing developers will just not be able to match the level of optimization in fglrx.  Stability and functionality will always be the primary reason for the existence of open source video drivers and is the main goal of most developers.
