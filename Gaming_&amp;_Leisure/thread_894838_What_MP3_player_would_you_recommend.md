### Post by IanW on 2008-08-20
I recently bought a [Sansa e260](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sansa_e200_series) for £40 (UK) and I'm very pleased with it.
4GB onboard, micro SDHC slot, and FM tuner, and with a claimed 20hour battery life (I never need more than 9).
After minor tweaks (switch to "MSC" mode), it talks happily to both Amarok & Rhythmbox.

There are 2 versions, v1 can have the [Rockbox OS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockbox) installed on it, but I got the v2.
Don't believe the Wikipedia article above about needing Sansa's Windows software to convert video,
googling around will find a mencoder command to do the job.

### Post by Glucklich on 2008-08-21
Seriously. A PSP is a very expensive and big piece of equipment just to be used as a MP3 player. Let's be practical, for example, if you use public transportation... a PSP is the last device you want in your pocket. In that case, use something small but with good sound quality like a iPod Shuffle or a Creative Zen. And get some kick-a*s 20 euros speakers to keep in your room.
Now, if you are also planing on playing some games. Then you should consider a PSP, because that's what it is... a gaming station. It just happens to have some extras included. 
So, my advice is to do not focus on the extras if you are not planing on using its main function. Find what suits you better.
