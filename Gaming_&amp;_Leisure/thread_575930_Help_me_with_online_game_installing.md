### Post by Dark Aspect on 2007-10-14
There are a few options for playing windows games under Linux.You can try wine,crossover and cedega,the last two cost money. I have all three and frankly I can't live with out any of them as they all seems to playback things different.In other words something that works on crossover may not work on cedega or wine and virsa versa.

Some links:

[http://www.winehq.org/]("http://www.winehq.org/")

[http://www.cedega.com/]("http://www.cedega.com/")

[http://www.codeweavers.com/products/cxoffice/]("http://www.codeweavers.com/products/cxoffice/")

I might be fair to warn you cedega is basically a modified wine and a good bit of Linux user dislike them due to cedega's extreme lack of Support.Wine works with a few games but my major problem with it is normally an application breaks after so many releases.

You can check compatibility at for all of the three compatibility layers at:

[Wine application Database]("http://appdb.winehq.org/")

[Cedega Games Database]("http://games.cedega.com/gamesdb/")

[Crossover's compatibility]("http://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/")

VMware can play a few games back but like cedega I can't really recommend it.VMware for starters is not meant for gaming Nor is crossover but Halo plays back on the latest version of crossover.

Edit:Hm....Last Chaos doesn't look to good with wine [(Check Results)]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=6828")
