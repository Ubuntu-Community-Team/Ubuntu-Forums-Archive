### Post by Eddward on 2009-10-21
Right now, on the Corporation of Goo level I'm building a pyramid.  Before I was able to get a taller tower by building up a little faster than building up.  The problem is you have to be careful.  You do tend to have to narrow as you go up, even if you don't do a perfect pyramid.  It's too easy to overestimate how high you can go without widening more.  If you get it wrong once, there's little chance of recovering in a good way.  

On the regular levels you rarely have enough Goo balls to be able to do pyramids though so you have to learn how sturdy a Goo lattice is.  You can sometimes get amazing support by widening the base of a wobbly tower by one triangle or by reaching out to touch something for support.  Also different kinds of Goo seem to have different properties.  (Strength of bonds, elasticity, length of bonds, number of bonds, breakable bonds, etc)  Sometimes a bit of wobble in  a vertical structure with the green Goo isn't too bad, but don't expect them to handle long narrow horizontal structures without breaking.

Get to know your Goo.  And don't be afraid to use time flies when you have them and something starts to collapse.

Edd

### Post by Eddward on 2009-10-22
That video reminded me of two more tips.  If the tower looks like it going to fall one way, you can sometimes stop it by putting weight on the other end.  Goo balls have weight whether they are in the structure or on it.  Second, you usually on have to reach the pipe.  It's vacuum can often hold you there once you reach it.
