### Post by DoktorSeven on 2011-03-28
MAME 0.92 is very old, and the Linux MAME was called "xmame" back in those days, which is hard to find since previously SDLMame and now mainline MAME have taken over since then.  Even if you do find it, you might have trouble getting it to build and run in a modern Linux distro.

The best thing to do, really, is get updated ROM sets for your non-working games.  I know that's really not what you wanted to hear, but it's just not very practical to use an older version of MAME, especially one that has been long since abandoned like xmame.
