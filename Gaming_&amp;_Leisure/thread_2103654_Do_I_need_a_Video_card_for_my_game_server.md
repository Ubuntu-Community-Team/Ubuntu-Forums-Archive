### Post by holastickboy on 2013-01-10
Hmmm.. it's a little off.  The desktop CPUs with onboard graphics, such as the AMD Trinity CPUs are great if you don't want to fork out money for another graphics card.  Please note that if this is your intention, don't bother with Intel, as the integrated HD graphics in all of their chips is utter garbage compared to the Radeon HD 7670 used in the AMD Trinity CPUS.  The desktop CPUs won't be an issue when it comes to overheating, as might be the issue with laptops (as laptops have smaller fans, heatsinks, and less room for air distribution).  You can get an AMD trinity CPU with APU that will play games like Battlefield 3 on High settings at around 60fps in the one package. You will be lucky to get 10fps with an Intel HD graphics in the same game... 

If you are using the computer for game servers only, you don't even need a display at all (you can run from terminal).  If, however, you intend on using it for a media PC, ensure you get a graphics card with HD acceleration (such as Nvidia and AMD).  Although I like AMd (as they are the underdogs) Nvidia cards perform much better under Linux.  If you plan on Windows install, either is fine.  

For sheer processing power, the Intel Core i5s are great.  They are much cheaper than the i7s, and only slightly slower (and the difference in cost can be spent on a better graphics card, which will make the i7 performance advantage even less important).  If you are on a budget, the AMD processors are great.  The AMD FX 8 core cpus are under $200, which is amazing considering the speed.  I personally use AMD, as the REAL LIFE difference of the processing power is not even noticeable, and you can put a cheap system together that will do a great deal of work, whether it be gaming or video encoding.
