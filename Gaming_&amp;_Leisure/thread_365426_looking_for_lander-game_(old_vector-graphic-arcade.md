While playing an asteroids-clone (maelstrom) my mind was wandering back to one of my most favorite games in old days on C64. 

Its a vector-graphic-game and you are controlling a spaceship that has to navgiate through kind of underground-tunnels, avoid or shoot canons and has to find a ball  which is then attached to the spaceship and one has to get out of the tunnels again.
The fun is that gravity and other physical laws are quite real in the game and navigating a small spacehip with a big ball attached is quite fun ;)

In google I found out that the game is called "lander", which makes it hard to find, cause any lander-game I found is the old moon-lander, where one simply has to land a spaceship on the moon before fuel is running out and if you are a bit too fast, you crash.

### Post by Jussi Kukkonen on 2007-02-19
You want *Thrust* (but remember to check out the somewhat more modern *Luola* also). 

Thrust uses a virtual console for graphics so be prepared for your desktop vanishing for the duration of the game and maybe even some starting problems -- I have to run *thrust --svgamode=G640x480x256* to get anything on my LCD screen..
