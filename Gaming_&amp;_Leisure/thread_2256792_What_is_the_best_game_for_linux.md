### Post by mastablasta on 2014-12-15
that's quite a limit you've set there.

first of all PC games with split screen are rare. there were some in the past that were moved from game machines or consoles onto PC. those had the split but usually it didn't go too well mostly because there is key jamming problem. free opensource games are even rarer and the kind that would have multiplayer online support even rarer.

it might be good to get a used older computer. so you have two at least.

otherwise have a look at playdeb (add the repository to your software center sources and you will get those games in software center). Supertuxcart might even have split screen. good multiplayer game is enemy territory that also have a bot mod (Omnibot). If you like FPS you can look at world of Padman but I am not sure how full the servers are. they have bots built in though. most of these games work well even on 10 year old PC.

if you are willing to spend some money there is Diablo & Diablo 2 that work well in wine. again can be played with very old PC over LAN. no need for internet :) also unreal tournament 2004, though old still fun and bots are descent. no need to go online.

in any case mostly old games (especially dos) would still have split screen (I am not sure if any steam ones do). I liked very much Cyberdogs and Cyberdogs2, space federation (RTS strategy with split screen & coop option), Egoboo also has multiplayer, old Need for speed games had split screen, then those that were moved from arcade machines like Golden Axe, Lotus challenge and similar. but these are really old games. most new ones want you to be constantly online and have no option for you to create your own server let alone to have split screen.

### Post by oldrocker99 on 2014-12-16
What kind of game are you desirous of playing? Platform, puzzle, FPS, action RPG, etc?

There are examples of these and other types of games available for Linux. Many, many Linux games are available on Steam for Linux, and Gaming Goodness is everywhere.

My own favorite game right now? Limited as the laptop I've been using while my PC is repaired, I've been playing my all-time favorite game, Dominions 4, which is a lousy-looking visual interface on top of an extraordinarily **deep** game engine, turn-based only, and appeals to a niche of gamers. The game becomes the most complicated, and best game is to play online against people, not the AI. Playing against the AI is enough for a lot of people. As I said, my most played game.

There are games like Counter-Strike and DOTA 2 which are free from Steam, there are tons of action games, RPGs, RTS (including the new champ, Planetary Annihilation), and several recent hits on Windows: Borderlands 2, The Witcher 2, Civilization V, Bioshock Infinite, X-COM, Wasteland 2 (1 was the the game that influenced Fallout), the upcoming Pillars of Eternity, and many, many more. Steam has well over 750 games for Linux.

Steam isn't the only way to get games. [http://www.desura.com](http://www.desura.com) was supporting Linux a couple of years before Steam did, and they have a large selection themselves with pretty good prices. Be patient with them, they have new ownership which is trying hard to set things right, including a new, **working** Linux cllient.

There's also [https://www.humblebundle.com/store](https://www.humblebundle.com/store). They always have Linux games for sale. A portion of the price is donated to charity. Most of theirare Steam keys, which does require Steam for Linux. 

Steam for Linux is available for free from the Ubuntu Software Center, or from [http://store.steampowered.com/](http://store.steampowered.com/), which for some people is a better way to do it. Post if you need help getting Steam installed, and you can also find out how other people have solved a problem. Search first, before you post; your question may have already been answered.

Good gaming!
