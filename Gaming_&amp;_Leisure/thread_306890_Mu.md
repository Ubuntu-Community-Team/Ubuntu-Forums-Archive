### Post by flugh on 2006-11-25
Do an 'apt-cache search mud' from commandline, and you'll see several packages (servers and clients both). gnome-mud comes to mind for the graphical client, tintin++ for text-based client. Check out [http://www.mudmagic.com/](http://www.mudmagic.com/) for a free python-based client that has both Windows and Linux versions available (the site pooped out for the holiday, I'm sure Kyndig will have it back up in the next day or so). I also like mcl by Erwin Andreasen ([http://www.andreasen.org/mcl/)](http://www.andreasen.org/mcl/)). There's a .deb available (I always compile it from source).

Just a couple things I have found useful in my MUDing :-)
