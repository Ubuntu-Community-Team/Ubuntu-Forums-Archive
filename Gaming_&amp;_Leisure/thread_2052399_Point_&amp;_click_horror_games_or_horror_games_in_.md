### Post by Sofox on 2012-09-03
The Chzo Mythos Quadrilogy: [http://www.lgdb.org/game/chzo_mythos](http://www.lgdb.org/game/chzo_mythos)
A series of 4 free point and click (3rd one minor exception) horror games made by the guy who now does Zero Punctuation, check the instructions at the above link for how to get them working natively on Linux.

Aside from that, just check out the ScummVM list of compatible Adventure games: [http://www.scummvm.org/compatibility/](http://www.scummvm.org/compatibility/) . You'll need to obtain the game data files yourself (some like are freeware and can be found on the ScummVM download page, others can be bought at sites like GOG.com), but once you do, ScummVM should run them on Linux.

### Post by tartalo on 2012-09-03
> **Sarys said:**
> So they don't run natively just like that?

These games (5 days a stranger...) were written with Adventure Game Studio (AGS). 

AGS was originally a Windows only freeware. During certain time there was a Linux engine that later went missing. 

Recently AGS went open source and several ports of the engine have been developed, among them there's a new Linux engine too.

A thread about the Linux engine port: [http://www.adventuregamestudio.co.uk/yabb/index.php?topic=46152.0](http://www.adventuregamestudio.co.uk/yabb/index.php?topic=46152.0)

More adventure games made with AGS: [http://www.adventuregamestudio.co.uk/Games.aspx](http://www.adventuregamestudio.co.uk/Games.aspx)
