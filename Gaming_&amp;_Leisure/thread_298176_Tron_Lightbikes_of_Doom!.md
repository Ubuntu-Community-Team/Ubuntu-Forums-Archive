### Post by 23nt3x on 2006-11-14
One of the things that I absolutely love about Armegatron is that if you have several Tron Light Bikes programs resident on your HD then the AIs from the various Tron games are added into Armegatron.
The first time I played Armegatron was on my first KDE (K-Dutch) Linux install on a 386 back in the 1990's. Having perused the games available back then and finding many to really lack the NintenDo, Doom punch I was used to (back in the 1990's) loading Armegatron up I was ASTOUNDED! 
The rich texure 3-d Graphic and red-blue 3-d support was a boon! Also the support for Virtual IO glasses (anyone remember those?) was MIND-BLOWING! Considering the advanced Nature of Armegatron and the fact that it really is an old game I'm still amazed at its playability...
These days I like to set the speed up to near maximum, and the number of players to whatever system I'm running it on's maximum number, which is usually 80 players or so. At that sort of high speed most of the 80 players explode right away and then if I lose my cycle I just change the cameras around and follow various other players until the next round.
Good times to play, and good times to watch. A friend and I had an Idea to record the video of the Light Cycles and do some Machinima type stuff using Halo or something for the interm bits and the Lightcycles for the "action" bits.
