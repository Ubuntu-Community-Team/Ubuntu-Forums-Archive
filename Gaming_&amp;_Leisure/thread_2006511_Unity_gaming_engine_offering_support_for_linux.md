### Post by natoshabard on 2012-06-20
The Linux port of the Unity Engine came from inside Unity Technologies; it was started as a free-time / FAFF (Fridays are for Fun) effort by a few of the developers at Unity Technologies who are Linux enthusiasts, and has recently reached a point in its development where we decided as a company that we are ready to share it with you.  In particular the success of the Humble Indie Bundle and the success of games in the Ubuntu Software Center makes it apparent to us that there is a market hungry for games on Linux and we need to give that opportunity to our customers.  We will be supporting export options for 32-bit (x86) and 64-bit (amd64) architectures.

We are thrilled about the upcoming Wasteland 2 game and when we start working with the developers behind it, we are certain that we will do nothing but improve the engine.

The decision to support Ubuntu officially comes from Ubuntu's commanding share of the end-user desktop market.

We hope to see many Unity games coming soon to Ubuntu!

[I]**Na'Tosha Bard**
Software Developer for Unity Technologies[/I]

### Post by natoshabard on 2012-06-20
> **Sslaxx said:**
> As per my PM: Would it have happened if Wasteland 2 had not had Linux support? Because being the initiative you make it sound like indicates to me Linux support was a requirement or otherwise required major backing or support from a third party (such as inXile) before it would have been officially progressed.

It is hard to play "what if", but I can say it was really a matter of timing, that made the port go live.  We've had the port in the works for years and at our most recent internal developer hack week, we got the port to a point where it was showing some pretty compelling demos of "real world content".  This, combined with the success of initiatives like the Humble Indie Bundle, success of software in the Ubuntu Software Center, and other developments in the industry that show that Linux users are hungry for games, made us feel that it was ready to ship the port to the world.

We haven't shipped it before now simply because it wasn't ready and we have been concerned about the support burden -- certainly not because of a lack of requests from our customers or lack of developers/studios who were willing to work with us on a port.

It's still only going to be shipped as a preview feature in Unity 4.0 because it will be awhile before we feel it's tested and polished enough to call it "stable", and we're limiting official support only to Ubuntu with vendor-made graphics drivers in order to ease the support burden on us.

We are looking forward very much to giving our customers the chance to publish Unity games for Ubuntu.
