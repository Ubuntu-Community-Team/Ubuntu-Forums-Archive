### Post by MrBlond83 on 2006-10-08
Eve-Online subscription is actually about the regular subscription fee for mmorpgs. Guild Wars is an exception, since it's a one time payment and not a monthly payment. Then again, while Guild Wars is fun to play, it is not a REAL MMORPG. Why? Because Guild Wars (And Dungeons and Dragons Online too, for that matter), is only really MMO in the "city" areas, and inside missions it's just you and your group. While real mmorpgs, like Everquest, SWG, Eve Online, WoW - are much more suitable to being described as Massive Multiplayer games. In regards to EQ2 and WoW, those too have "group areas", but a big part of those games happens in a true MMO world, in zones with a large amount of players, and so on. And as far as Eve Online is concerned, 98% of the game occurs in true MMO environment, where everyone can just hop in and certain areas can have hundreds of players sometimes.

So you can't really expect the same from Guild Wars and Eve Online - it takes a lot more for a company to maintain servers with a true massively multiplayer world, than to maintain a few small massively multiplayer areas with some npcs, and group adventures.

Again, not saying Guild Wars isn't fun, it's just different in that way and doesn't really deserve being described as a MMO, for the exact same reason Diablo isn't a MMORPG either.

Not to mention that the folks at Eve Online have been fair enough, so far, to provide expansions for free. Which is more than I can say for most MMO games, with or without monthly fees :) And I'm not talking about small free add ons, I'm talking about huge additions to content without taking anything except the regular monthly fee :)

### Post by MrBlond83 on 2006-10-09
In Eve, you don't get to choose whether you want PvP or PvE. You can be happily mining asteroids, or killing NPCs, and all the sudden a group of players warps in and starts shooting at you. This is normally how it goes down... And that's why it's so much fun. You don't choose from a convenient menu, "hmm I think I'll PvE now" and then get to kill computer monsters easily :) And the PVP terms aren't always fair - sometimes you and your friends are outnumbered, outgunned, and you have to make use of all your ships traits in order to just get out of the system alive.

The results of losing a battle can be bad in Eve - you lose your ship and everything on it. Many new players have been complaining about this, because they did not realize this is not just like any other MMORPG, where players hardly care about death, and where you can go ahead and use your hard earned money to buy the best gear. In Eve, it's not like that - if you make 50 mil, and buy a 50 mil ship & equipment, and then lose it in battle - you have to start from zero with a newbie ship. So in Eve you are expected to buy gear with just a part of the money, and always leave most of it in the side in case you die.

Which isn't so bad considering the way combat works in Eve, is that you can be very useful in PvP even if you use cheap ships with cheap equipment - The small, cheaper ships are much faster than the big cruisers, and can be a pain in the *** for them, while the torpedos, which are the strongest type of missiles, don't do much damage at all to small ships and sometimes even fail to hit them completely. So a good army uses both big cruisers and small ships, so even someone who started playing a month ago can be useful, and doesn't have to spend day and night to grind for money.

### Post by stuart.crouch on 2006-10-09
You can play EVE for FREE* (* = Free meaning not buying it using cash from the real world).

Eve is the only one that I know of that is self sustaining.  That is, you can use your ISK (ingame credit) to buy more game time.  So if you do well at the game, you can use your success to buy next months subscription - This is by design and implemented by CCP (the creators of EVE)

See this link to the wikipedia for some info about the above - [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EVE_Online#Cost](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EVE_Online#Cost)

I've got a friend whos been playing it for about 8 months and says he's getting close to that.  So you say EVE is expensive, but get good at it, and save some ISK for subscription and the game essentially becomes FREE.

(NB. Other games might be self-sustaining, I just dont know about it.)

Edit:  Just read that stuff above about PvE and PvP so I'll define that a little more too. Eve has the concept of high security space (1.0 sect) and low security space (0.0 sect).  If you attack another player in anything above 0.5 sect (I think - I haven't played in about a year), the police will shoot you on sight and you'll loose your own ship for it.  

So the only chance of really loosing your ship in Eve is to fly into low sect space, and you have to actually plan to do that.  You are then aware that you are potentially engaging in PvE as you are entering a risky area.  Of course a fast trading route, or a big mine field can be found in low sect space, so you start waying up the risks against the benefits :P and thats all part of the fun.
