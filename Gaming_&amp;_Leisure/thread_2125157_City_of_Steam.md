### Post by CoSStephen on 2013-03-13
Hello ubuntu guys, 

First of all I would like to express my thankness to [Artificial Intelligence]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=19") who gives me the chance to post a thread here. 

My name is Stephen. I work for a small developer, Mechanist Games. For anyone interested in a very accessible browser-based, industrial fantasy/steampunk MMORPG with loads of content and a unique world, you might want to check out [City of Steam]("http://www.cityofsteam.com/"). It's made by an indie developer, and despite being browser-based, thanks to Unity 3D it looks quite impressive; the world, for example, is a huge mechanism of gears and spokes, as seen below: 

[IMG]http://i.imgur.com/6Auu7NI.jpg[/IMG]

(To see this in full animation, see: [http://www.cityofsteam.com/world/worldmachine](http://www.cityofsteam.com/world/worldmachine) ) 

For a better picture of the game, check our latest Closed Beta Trailer, a lot has changed and improved since then, but it can still give a good idea for what we plan to make of our game:

[http://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&v=ynmn3DjIDqA](http://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&v=ynmn3DjIDqA)

This world is full of both magical and mechanical monstrosities, clockwork wonders (even dwarves are part-organic, part-mechanichal), and ripe for adventure. Expansive metropolitan cities built on years of neglect and abandon allow untold evils to thrive within the heart of its intricate networks of pipes, gears and towering spires. It is up to the new age of hardy adventurers to aid the Triumvirs, the local governing body, to rid and clean up the depths and heights of the city.

Edit: After reading through the thread my post just seems to be condensing what has already been said in the other posts.

The game uses the unity3d engine which can publish to almost any client platform available.  Their studio so far has only published the game as a browser game and to access the game requires using the unity webplayer which doesn't run under linux.  The unity developers have stated they have no intentions of creating a unity webplayer that can run on linux and it also doesn't seem to work at all in wine.  Because Mechanist games intends to make their game available on steam it means they'll have to release a client version that can be distributed over the steam client; from what I understand a client can be made for a specific platform with just a push of a button in the unity game creator.

There are already some other games made with the unity3d game engine either released or in beta on linux and I've recently been playing one.  From my experience there have been only very few bugs that are linux specific in the game and none of them make the game less playable.  For the most part the bugs that are not windows related also seem to affect the mac clients.  When the game client is released I don't think there will be any reason for Mechanist games to delay releasing the linux client with the other platform clients.
