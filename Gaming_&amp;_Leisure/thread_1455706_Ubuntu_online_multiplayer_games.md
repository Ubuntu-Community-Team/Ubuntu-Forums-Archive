### Post by Aped on 2010-04-16
Neither of these are up your alley at all, OP, but I always plug them when possible:

Puzzle Pirates: About what it sounds like, perform various puzzles which represent myriad piratical activities. Pretty fun, fairly in-depth when you get into it. Thriving economy, buy some real estate, people can actually become royalty and own entire islands, etc. Paying optional for a time, becomes desirable. 
[www.puzzlepirates.com](www.puzzlepirates.com)


Dofus2.0: Quirky, cute, adorable and goofy French mmorpg. English supported client, obviously. Again, pretty rich world. 200 levels, good luck getting past 100 within a few months. Lotta play time. Free to play slightly gimped until, say, level 20 or 30. 
[www.dofus.com](www.dofus.com)


Check em out, or don't, whatever.
