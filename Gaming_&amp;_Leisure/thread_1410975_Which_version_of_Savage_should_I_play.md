### Post by donkyhotay on 2010-02-19
As mentioned previously, story doesn't matter. The story isn't important to the gameplay at all and if you read the stories you'll find that they're actually two different (but similar) continuities. For gameplay differences, savage2 is a bit faster paced and focuses much more heavily on melee combat. Savage1 is more RTS-ish and has greater emphasis on weapons. They're both fun games and I actually play both depending on my mood. My recommendation is to try them both out and see which one you prefer.
