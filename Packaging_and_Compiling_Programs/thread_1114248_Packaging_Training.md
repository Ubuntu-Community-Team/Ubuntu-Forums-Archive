### Post by andrewsomething on 2009-04-02
Short version: if you’re interested in learning what it takes to learn packaging, mark Thursday in your calendar. From now on Thursday is Packaging Training Day.

Based on our experience with initiatives like the MOTU Q&A Sessions, the [Ubuntu Developer Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek") and [Ubuntu Open Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuOpenWeek"), the Ubuntu Developer Community is offering the following training effort starting in April:

    * one 1-hour session every week
    * short demo of packaging techniques
    * rest of the hour: ask all the questions you like
    * in #ubuntu-classroom on irc.freenode.net
    * with translators in #ubuntu-classroom-{de,es,it,fr,…} if you’re not sure enough asking your questions in English yet

We rotate session times to make sure we cover all timezones:

    * 1st Thursday of the month, 6:00 UTC
    * 2nd Thursday of the month, 12:00 UTC
    * 3rd Thursday of the month, 18:00 UTC
    * 4th Thursday of the month, 0:00 UTC
    * (5th Thursday of the month, 6:00 UTC)

Do you want to give a session? Do you have questions? Would you like to request a session? Would you just like to hang out? Do you want to help out with translations? Header over to [Packaging/Training]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Packaging/Training") and add yourself.

This is an effort by the Ubuntu Developer Community, if you can do something to improve it and help others to get the most out of it, help out. This is going to be what we make of it. Get involved now!

Future Packaging Training Sessions can also be found on [Ubuntu Fridge Calendar]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Fridge/Calendar"), which is available [online]("http://fridge.ubuntu.com/calendar") and in [XML]("http://www.google.com/calendar/feeds/j5q85mmi6ujvjtii5s1n3li5io%40group.calendar.google.com/public/basic"), [iCal]("http://www.google.com/calendar/ical/j5q85mmi6ujvjtii5s1n3li5io%40group.calendar.google.com/public/basic.ics"), and [HTML]("http://www.google.com/calendar/embed?src=j5q85mmi6ujvjtii5s1n3li5io%40group.calendar.google.com&ctz=America/New_York") formats.

Stay on top of things and subscribe to the RSS feed of the [Ubuntu Packaging Blog]("http://ubuntupackaging.wordpress.com/").

Note: For help on time equivalents around the world, visit [Time and Date World Clock]("http://www.timeanddate.com/worldclock/")

### Post by andrewsomething on 2009-04-02
(Cross posted from: [http://ubuntupackaging.wordpress.com/](http://ubuntupackaging.wordpress.com/))

So we just had our first Packaging Training session. It was awesome! Lots of people attended and although we didn’t quite manage to hold the session in exactly one hour, we had a bunch of people presenting their patch at the end of the session.

So what did we do? We had a look at a [bug that was still there in Ubuntu, bug fixed Upstream already]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/subvertpy/+bug/323270"). We walked through all the steps necessary and worked our way through a bunch of snags that we hit. [Logs of the session are available]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Packaging/Training/Logs").

I hope everybody had a good time and hopefully we’ll close down the number of [bugs fixed elsewhere]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bugs?field.status_upstream=resolved_upstream").

Great start of Packaging Training. Next up is James Westby at 9th April, 12:00 UTC talking about bzr builddeb. See you there!
