### Post by Bachstelze on 2011-10-03
By default, /usr/lical/lib is already in the ldpath (it's in /etc/ld.so.conf.d/libc.conf), so adding or removing it to/from /etc/ld.so.conf probably doesn't change anything. You have two copies of libmpfr: the one that came with Ubuntu, which is in /usr/lib and does not depend on libgmp.10, and the one you compiled, which is in /usr/local/lib and does depend on libgmp.10. When the dynamic linker tried to find libmpfr, it finds your compiled version first, hence the dependency on flibgmp.10.
