### Post by Asterio on 2009-03-06
Self-explanatory; let's begin. 

I'm 13 as of now, and I've always been fascinated with computers. People would take a fair amount of surprise at the things I could (and still can) make Windows do. 

So I was quite ecstatic on getting my first laptop. It was an old Dell (a model used in public schools!), 512 MB RAM, a 30GB HDD, and an XP ripe for the modding and whatever else I wanted to do. 

And so, that's exactly what I did. I spent a year or so modifying my computer, from looking like Vista to OSX in one sitting, and installing   and uninstalling various games. So much software was installed and uninstalled, my HDD was ripped to shreds. 

Then I got Vista. Even though my humble old laptop didn't have the horsepower for Aero, it still looked nice, and I had it up and running. The same thing happened. Crap and mods and all sorts of things were used. My HDD suffered more.

Until one day, all my networking just DIED. Completely. I couldn't connect to any wireless networks, an ethernet cable wouldn't work, NOTHING. I spent 2 hours on the phone with tech support, and they couldn't diagnose the problem. The only advice they had was to reinstall the system. I wasn't up for that, as I'd lose all my files, which had taken quite a bit of work to amass (I had QUITE the music library). 

Enter Linux. I'd heard about Ubuntu in an IM conversation once, and looked it up. The only thing that stayed in my brain was GOOD.FREE.OS. GET NAO. Burned it to a CD, and a couple hours (much less, had I simply used the boot menu -_-) later, Ubuntu was installed. My networking worked (at least a cable did). Now that I was on the internet, I could look and see if drivers were the problem. And they were. I installed the (lifesaver) B43 Legacy driver, and MY WIRELESS WORKED WOOHOO. 

So I've been a Linux man (kid for now lol) ever since. I tried Kubuntu, didn't like it (KDE crashed a LOT), so I'm sticking with Ubuntu. 

If you're not asleep, that's my (poorly written) story.
