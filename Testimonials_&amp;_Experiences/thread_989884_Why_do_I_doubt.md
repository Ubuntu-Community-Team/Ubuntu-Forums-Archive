### Post by Riffer on 2008-11-22
I just spent the last week tearing out my hair.  You see I was trying to get my LCD tv up as a secondary screen, and only thing that ever worked was using a very low res s-video setup.  Every time I tried to use a different setup (DVI, analog) I would get nothing or I would crash X.

So I did it.  I re-installed XP.  Why?  I thought that perhaps Ubuntu couldn't do the job, perhaps maybe XP could.  Also I wanted to check if there was something wrong with the tv.  Well guess what!  It didn't work in XP either, crashed it, I mean BSOD, which I never seen in XP.  If fact I had to do a hard reboot, which never happened on Ubuntu, at least there is always something you get so you can fix what was wrong.

So once again I had to relearn a hard lesson.  Just because it doesn't work with Ubuntu chances are its the hardwares fault not Ubuntu's.

PS  I much prefer the nVidia server setup program on Ubuntu then Windows.
