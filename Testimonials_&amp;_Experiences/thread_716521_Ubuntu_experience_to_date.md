### Post by L815 on 2008-03-06
So, I had installed ubuntu about a year ago on a desktop and enjoyed it, but couldn't stick with it (feisty). 
I now have a new laptop Sony Vaio-Fz240e, which is built for vista. Xp won't detect my hdd and I have to install it with slipstreamed drivers.

Now ubuntu had no problem recognizing my hdd during install, and most of my hardware is detected out of the box (that i know of).

The only problems I've had are the headphone/speaker issue (i found a fix), the hibernation won't work right, brightness won't change when unplugged, and the power management is lacking for laptops (low, power save, high, etc..), and I can't get my s-video out to work yet.

The rest seems to be working great. I have only logged onto my dual booted vista maybe 2 -3 times for the s-video. I can say I'm becoming addicted to ubuntu :)

I'm really excited for Hardy release to see if these issues were fixed. 
And I do have a strange font issue with Open office and Firefox 3. It looks horrible compared to the rest of the fonts on my laptop. 

Anyway, for now ubuntu has been my main OS and it will stay like this if they keep on improving as much as they do.
