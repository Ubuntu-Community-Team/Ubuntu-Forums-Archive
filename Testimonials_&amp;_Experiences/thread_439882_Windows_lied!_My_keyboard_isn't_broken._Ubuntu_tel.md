### Post by weblordpepe on 2007-05-11
Ok this has happend a few times now. For some reason (unknown to modern science) my normal keyboard (has both USB & PS/2 functionality) stopped working in Windows.
I tried everything...drivers...safe mode...you name it. Nope, didn't wanna work.

As a result I bought a new one. For fun I installed two keyboards at once while I was in Linux and what do ya know - the old keyboard works fine!
[I]
-That was two months ago. And it only sprang to mind when I started typing. Now for what I intended to post about:[/I]

I just bought a new (ok old) Dell Inspiron 8600 laptop. The reason being was so I can install Windows on it, and use software as a soft-synth for my USB-MIDI keyboard during live performances.

'Yay!' I thought, plugging in the USB keyboard only to find out that the USB hub didn't have enough power to power my USB keyboard. Rubbish I thought, it worked fine on the PC. OK maybe its some laptop thing. I'll just buy an AC adapter for the MIDI keyboard, so the USB hub won't be powering it.

Of course being the nerd I am, I installed Ubuntu on the laptop. Eventually I got round to plugging in my USB MIDI keyboard. ....no noise. No nothing. Did it install? Lets find out.

I loaded up USBView and what do ya know. It was listed there. I did a quick search for some basic sequencer software and BAM the keyboard was working.

Now why is it that it works in Ubuntu without any problems at all, yet while under Windows it's the USB hub? I think Windows is lying to me! Driver issues? Who knows. I tried everything. You can't exactly tweak built-in Windows drivers that much.
