### Post by phillipmaloney on 2012-04-02
I would just like to make a comment about my experiences with ubuntu and now xubuntu which I've been using for about 4 months now. It is installed via wubi on an Acer netbook DN255. Plain and simple everything works. I have had no problems to deal with. The wifi worked from day one and update manager works with no issues. I use the ubuntu software center without any problems. I have tried several desktops, I did not care much for unity, but have used gnome classic (that is a good sticky), xfce, and for the moment I am on lxde. I sure don't miss Windows 7 which I don't even use anymore. This system is fast and at least for me foolproof. I read posts here of all sorts of problems, most which lead me to believe that nobody does a search of previous posts to research  their problem for previously posted answers. I am very happy with this operating system. It is as close to perfect as it gets.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-04-02
> phillipmaloney
I read posts here of all sorts of problems, most which lead me to  believe that nobody does a search of previous posts to research  their  problem for previously posted answers.

I think the problem of searching for answers is caused by a number of issues. For example these three:

1. New users may not know what terms to search for.
2. Some users have found that it's quicker to simply ask a question, rather than spend time searching existing threads.
3. Users may not fully understand how to use the forums search functions.

Historically, I've asked very few questions in the support areas -  primarily because the answers have already been posted. In my  experience, it only takes a few minutes to perform an advanced search  and if one knows how to utilize the search functions, answers are  generally available within just a few moments.

For those users who are comfortable using database search functions, the  forums are a wealth of information regarding Ubuntu, Linux and apps.  For some, Google is their search engine of choice and if they can't find  an answer via Google they start a thread here. Regardless, of how threads get started here, I'm always amazed at how  many forum members just can't wait to assist. IMO one of the true  differences between Ubuntu/Linux users and users of other OS's is this  inherent desire to help others enjoy the OS - while simultaneously  projecting a sense of community. Which I find admirable.

I see no reason (at all) to make any comparison between Windows and Ubuntu/Linux. Ubuntu can stand on it's own merits and doesn't need justification, by identifying problems with Windows. Since first installing Ubuntu (7.04) my family & I have been Ubuntu/Linux "only". I've learned that there's no reason to justify or rationalize problems in Ubuntu by comparing them to issues in Windows. Reminds me of pointing out the bad behavior of others in an effort to justify one's own behavior. I hope Canonical never envisions Windows as the standard for OS's. But rather sees the future of Ubuntu based in creativity and imagination - not comparisons.  :-k

Just my $0.02
