### Post by saberz on 2008-10-28
Reading much of the negativity here on the forums I felt like posting something positive. Seeing as I have been off an on the nix platforms for roughly 10 years(by no means an expert but I can get around a terminal if i'm held at gunpoint). 

All this negativity against Ubuntu really isn't warranted. As many others have said, people tend to just JUMP right into the OS forgoing any dual-boot options and expecting a Windows like experience. It would be the same thing as going from Windows to Mac. Many people do not research what it will take to perform a full transition over, and its usually best to take baby steps.

I having finally being fed up with Vista running like ***(well it ran well, but after installing all the must have AV+Spyware removal apps etc) to keep it lean and mean said it was time to switch. I was ecstatic about Wubi, you have no clue how easy it was to install Ubuntu, no longer having to deal with partitions etc. I ran a Wubi install for approx a month to re-familiarize myself with whats available for Ubuntu and Linux for that matter and was surpised at how much had changed since my last foray in with Dapper Drake.

The one issue that kept me from fully switching was not being able to have World of Warcraft install and run properly(yes its my addiction). Well after much testing I was able to get it work flawlessly!. I knew it was time and did the switch.

1 week into a full HDD wipe and running SOLEY on Ubuntu I am very happy with the results. I was able to find all the applications that are counterparts to the Windows variants I had run. WoW is running great, I installed VirtualBox with an XP install ONLY for my Zune so I could synch it with the latest podcasts and music =). All in all, it was a seamless switch, a great experience and I commend the Ubuntu team for a job well done. Hardy Heron has been great, and I am sure Intrepid Ibex will be even better.

All of you downing Ubuntu, give it another try when you fully research what software you need, what matches it, and how Ubuntu will suite your tastes and needs. Always start with a dual-boot, I highly recommend Wubi.
