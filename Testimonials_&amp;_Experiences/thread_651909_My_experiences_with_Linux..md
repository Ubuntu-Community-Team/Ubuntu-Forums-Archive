### Post by sujoy on 2007-12-28
I can only speak for myself and its that I started my linux journey with red hat 8 and ended it there after a short (miniature !!!) stint of 2 weeks. What put me off was that i knew none of the commands not even ls ! and the KDE application names seemed aweful (not that I hold the same view at present).

Then this July (2007), someguys in orkut used to talk a lot about how linux is great and Ubuntu is great and that made me install Fiesy and also grab a book on UNIX system concepts. I enjoyed reading it . As for Fiesty, well it was good except that I had problem playing video. The background applications would be visible now and then. After about a couple of weeks, removed it again.

Later in October I got myself a laptop (Lenovo G400), and ran Vista (the drivers included were for Vista only). Now when Gutsy came out, I formated again and this time I dont know by magic or something, everything worked beautifully, the wireless, the video, compiz. Life has never been the same since.

Now that I have learnt the commands and have a basic experience of Linux (though only a bit more than 2 months), I know that I will never switch back. Infact I am getting accustomed to Slackware these days, and still keep trying other Linux distros in the dual boot. (Will be installing Gentoo 2007 today :) )

So, I guess Linux is all about finding the right distro to go with your hardware (like Fiesty didn't work for me but Gutsy did), getting ready to learn something new and accepting the fact that you can never learn enough, theres always more to know. 

Also, another point is that I had Operating Systems this semester and that made me even more determined to try out Linux, and I am glad I did. Its not only Ubuntu that I admire, but the whole philosophy behind GNU/Linux.
