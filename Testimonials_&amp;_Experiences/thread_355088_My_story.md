### Post by chapake on 2007-02-06
Hi there!

Just wanted to let the whole community know, how much I love this great distro. And to tell you my story.
My first experience: It all started in a couple of years ago when I was in senior high. In order to graduate I had to do a research paper. The subject was actually up to me, so I decided to do a little researh on Linux and it's distributions. So I went to talk to my teacher and when I said that I would like to do the research on Linux, he just asked "Is that a program?" Anyway, I got the magic "Yes" from my teacher, so I was ready to go. The more I read the more I liked the idea of using Linux someday.
The paper was supposed to be a teaching material for the new IT course in school, so I explained the whole idea of free and open-source software,
a bit of Unix basics and the most used distros at that time. So finally it ended up to be a 50-paged ode to Linux.
I also had to do a presentation on the subject, so I decided to make it more interesting for those taking the IT course and demonstrated Mandrake 10.0 at work (installing packages from the package manager, terminal, etc). The whole course was like "WOW! It's so easy! Why haven't I heard from it before?" Even now, several years later I wind up meeting some students from that course saying "Hey, you're the girl who made me use Linux." And last time I checked, some computers using Edubuntu have emerged to the school computer class.

Present time: Time went by and I bought a laptop. The next question was: XP or Linux? Unwillingly I chose XP, because it was the only install CD I could get my hands on. But now I have XP and Kubuntu both happily installed on my box and Kubuntu is workng like a dream come true. Everything is set up just the way I like it and I never boot into Windows willingly. Sometimes I get strange looks from others in my class when I open the lid of my laptop and boot into Kubuntu, but I actually like that.:) My friends have dropped their offensive questions eg. "How's your Linux doing?" because I can tell them at least 10 things that you can't do on Windows. I have also removed that annoying sticker saying "Designed for Microsoft Windows XP" and replaced it with a much more nicer Ubuntu logo:D:D
Even my boyfied is beginning to understand the advantages of Unix-based operating systems. He used to be a total MS freak. When I told him that I was going to install Kubuntu on my machine, he was hopping mad. He calmed down a bit when I said it will be a dual-boot. It's the only computer in our apartment, so I actually understand. I made him an account on my box so that he could make it so Winows-like as possible and installed all the goodies he needed. I succeeded and now he uses XP only for gaming. He still thinks that Linux is ment to be used on servers, but has become more tolerant about using it as desktop computer. My next mission is to get rid of XP permanently. And his next project is to make my Kubuntu boot in 2 minutes.:D

This whole community has been a great help. All the available documentation and tutorials have teached me a lot about Linux and I still think it's great. No degfrag, no viruses, no malware - in other words: no crap thats usual on Windows. I even got some multimedia keys working on Kubuntu, back on Wndows I could only dream of using them. I recently upgraded to Edgy and this distro keeps going better. So - a big-big thanks to the community for all the help provided!!
