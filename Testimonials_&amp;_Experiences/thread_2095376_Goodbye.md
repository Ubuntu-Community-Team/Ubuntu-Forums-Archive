### Post by Janeleaper on 2012-12-16
In reply to the question: 

I've always used Ubuntu on our desktops.  I"m not sure for how long, but I joined this forum in July 2008, so around then. When I installed it I did not know there was any other type of ubuntu, and it wasn't an issue until recently, since I was happy with it.

When I installed the OS on the Asus, I knew there was an issue with space, so I installed lubuntu.  However, this is a side-issue, because the Asus is rubbish anyway, and the problem is the computer not the OS. It was OK for a while, but upgrade is impossible because there isn't enough space in the memory.

The Dell mini, on the other hand, is really annoying. Upgrade resulted in a system that works at the pace of a snail.

For the moment the desktops are OK, but there are still the problems with Libre Office and photo management to endure. 

I guess the central problem is word processing.  Libre office is a pain, and it doesn't get any better. Both of us do a lot of writing. My husband is an academic who uses German, Hungarian and Serbo-Croat a lot.  He loves the way he can switch between country keyboards on the ipad, and wants to be able to do the same thing on his laptop, plus he can use microsoft word on a mac. As for me, I don't know why Libre insists on "recovering" my last document every time I open the programme, but it is an irritating waste of time, and I haven't been able to cure it.  The fact that Libre doesn't show up in the launcher is also very annoying.
