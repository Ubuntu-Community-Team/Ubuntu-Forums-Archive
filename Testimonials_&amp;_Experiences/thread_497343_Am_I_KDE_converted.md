### Post by eentonig on 2007-07-10
I'm a Gnome adept. My first Linux experience was KDE, and it s*cked big time for me.

I didn't like the look and feel it gave. It was windows in an awkward dressing. I really, really didn't like it.

Thank god, when I tried linux the next time, I installed Ubuntu, which had this wonderfull Gnome DE. It was everything I needed. Fast, smooth, simple, customizable, clear, etc... I fell in love and tuned it to my needs. And I accepted the extra burden on my system to run some K apps that didn't have a suitable Gnome alternative (mainly K3B and amarok)

a few months later, I installed yet another machine. A 2Ghz P4 with 2G ram. Not the top of the bill, not a slow machine neither. And I installed the wonder called Beryl (and later yet, Compiz Fusion). Happy hippo me.

Lately, I decided to give the terrible 'K' another chance, so I installed it on the same machine.... And never even bothered to launch it once. Untill today that is.

I'm still not very fond of the 'K'dyness of KDE. I hate the childish way every app needs to start with 'K-something' The menu structure feels strange. But, overall, it feels like my system is running smoother and faster. 

I think I'm going to try this out for a few days. See if I can keep up with the K'dyness of KDE.
