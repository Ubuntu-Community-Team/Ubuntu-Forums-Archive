### Post by jperez on 2009-10-03
Hello all!

Some of you may have already seen me post here, or other forums such at the Maemo Forums.  I tend to drift around a bit.  I'm here to talk about my experience with Linux and how I use it today.  This may get long, but if you are interested in reading, then grab some popcorn and read on. :D

**Pogo Linux**
Okay, well, the first encounter I ever had with Linux was probably in 2001/2002 after my younger sister, mother and I left my father while I was in my last year of High School.  I ended up buying a $300 Compaq Presario 733Mhz with I believe 512MB of RAM.  It was a decent machine, but the OS sucked...it was running off Windows ME...even worse in comparison to Vista.  I can't remember how I happened upon this CD, but I got a hold of a CD of Pogo Linux, which at the time, had no GUI for the installation and was all text-based.  Since this was my first time installing a Linux OS on any computer, the installation seemed alien, but as with all things different, it usually always will be.

It never even got to finish up and I was left with a Linux installation that was unfinished, so I went back to WinME.  I basically gave up on Linux after that, never to mention it again...until I came to Colorado.

**Enter Ubuntu Hoary Hedgehog 5.04**
When I came to Colorado around late mid-2004, I tried to do the "church-going" thing only to find that people that were religious were not exactly my type of crowd.  No offense to anyone, it's just that I grew up around people that were a lot more harsh in demeanor and were basically cursing like a sailor...me included at times.

Anyway, so I met a computer technician among the crowd that worked for HP and he asked me if I ever heard of Linux before.  I told him about my experience with Pogo and recommended me to Ubuntu.  I figured I'd try it out and got myself a CD.  I tried installing it on a separate HDD I had laying around that was in my main PC at the time, another Compaq Presario 5900Z.  I had an nVidia 32MB video card, my SB Live! 5.1 DELL OEM soundcard and the stuff it originally came with.  For starters, the sound didn't work, but that's typical for that card.  Video was sketchy at best and networking was non-functional.  Now, rather than come on here and ask for help, which I had never used a forum before anyhow, I left it to just gather dust yet again.

Some time passed and I skipped over Breezy, which from what I read wasn't that great, and then I happened upon Dapper Drake v6.06.1.  I came back to try it out and where I still had a few issues, I decided to try it out a little more.  That's around the same time I started working at the Boys & Girls Club of Larimer Country here in Fort Collins.  I found out that a couple of the kids in the Computer Lab knew about Linux, one more than the other, and I decided to see what they knew.  One told me his father used SUSE before and thus had some knowledge of it there.  I myself had tried SUSE and only met despair with my networking card, again, not working.  The other one had a nicer laptop than my PC at the time and wanted to try out Ubuntu with Compiz.  I had tried it out as well and loved the effects.  It was eye-candy and I liked it that way at the time.

He installed it and he was on his way.  I don't know if he still uses it to this day, but chances are he's still fiddling with it in some way.  Who knows?  This of course was 3 years ago.  I got Ubuntu only for the eye-candy and to declare myself rid of Windows when in reality, I was attached to Windows more than I knew.  Again, Dapper pushed aside for Windows due to some games I was playing at the time and my limited hardware in regards to multiple computers.

**Enter Jaunty**
When Jaunty was released, I was using Intrepid sparingly and only used it for the eye-candy yet again.  I put it on my laptop to get rid of Vista.  I tried to get XP on there, but due to SATA incompatibilities, I decided that the best route was Linux yet again.  I had used the older releases, but did little, if nothing, with them.  So, when I upgraded to Jaunty on my laptop, with it's Intel integrated graphics chip, you can tell what happened.  Even with the workarounds, I couldn't get the effects to work at all under GNOME.  I got a minimal set with Kubuntu KDE4, but again, it was VERY minimal and the desktop still ran slow.  The specs of my laptop are as follows:

CPU: Pentium Dual Core 1.6Ghz (not Core 2 Duo)
RAM: 1GB
GPU: Intel GM965/GL960 Integrated Graphics Controller
HDD: SATA 80GB

So, it's not a great system at all, but it works out well for what I need it for.  Still, at the time I was upgrading to Jaunty, I was all about effects and what not.  That changed a few weeks ago when I decided that under Ubuntu, the effects aren't everything and it just makes me lose interest in it pretty quickly.

To be quite honest, I also found Kubuntu KDE4 not to run smoothly on my laptop, but this was during Jaunty.  I'm currently using the alpha release (&#945;6 if I'm not mistaken -09/21/2009-) of Karmic and the desktop of GNOME is much faster than it was before, boot time is great and with me not wanting to impress myself with fancy 3D effects as much as I wanted to before (I still use Compiz-Fusion, but to increase productivity), I can actually enjoy my Linux experience.  My experience while using Kubuntu Jaunty was...updating and only when I really felt like it.

I can't stand Windows, but I use it because of the games I want to play such as Sims 3 and an MMORPG that uses GameGuard, which won't work under WINE, Cedega or CrossOver.  Check multiple threads and even on the WINE forums.

So, I am stuck with Windows as long as my favorite games stay on Windows.

I most recently got an eMac PowerPC 1Ghz with 384MB of RAM.  I was, until recently, a Mac hater, but I had no real grounds on which to hate it.  I was just moved by an unknown vendetta that I had formed in my head without even listening to any Apple bashers, Mac haters, Windows lovers, etc.  I currently use it, but not much.  I have it so I can broaden my scope of computing knowledge to all three major systems; Windows, Linux and Mac, of which I have had MUCH more years of experience working with Windows computers.

I love Linux and what it can do/has the potential of doing.  I even have, as my signature states, a Nokia Internet Tablet called the Nokia N810 with a relatively little known Maemo Linux (Diablo-release).  I applaud Nokia for their efforts in bringing Linux to the masses and making it much easier to use in everyday use.  The new N900 by Nokia will be a smartphone supported by the T-Mobile 3G network and will basically be the tablet we've known, only better and with integrated phone features.

So, that's my experience with Linux.  It may not be much, but it's what I've encountered thus far.  I can't say I will drop Windows anytime soon seeing as two major games I play are Windows-only, but maybe one day, that'll change.  Until then, Linux will live on in my laptop and in my heart as my favorite OS, even if it's not the one I use all the time. :p

Thanks for reading and have a great day!

Jesse~

### Post by Lunx on 2009-10-03
Nice story Jesse, who knows maybe Lucid Lynx will see you leave Windows altogether :)

I've been a convert to Linux only 10 months, but never felt the urge for a moment to go back to MS. I've only got a modest spec box, and have to say I've had the odd issue here and there (sound problems mostly), but nothing I've not been able to resolve by working it out myself or asking for help here and on other forums. I've actually have less problems now than when I was running Windows, spend way more time actually using my 'puter than fixing it :P I also find that I know far more about my 'puter and how to accomplish stuff than I did with Windows, 

I must admit that even when I was using Windows, nearly all my applications were open source anyway, so there was not much of a learning curve with software. Now I find that most of the apps I use run far more stable with Linux than the Windows versions, and just work better. 

I upgraded to Karmic a couple of days ago, apart from a couple of very small bugs, everything is running very nicely now (Sound is 1000% better now too, wooo hooo ). I think I'll probably upgrade once more to the next LTS distro, then leave it at that for a while.

I'm also a university student and do all my study on-line. I started out when I was still using Windows, so was a bit uncertain whether the apps I now use would put me at a disadvantage in some ways, but actually found the opposite to be true. An example was that they posted some powerpoint files for us, but in some new fancy format .ppsx. I couldn't open them in Open Office, but neither could most of the other students using Microsoft Office!!! Went looking for the open source solution and found it in about 10 minutes, simply rename the file extension as .pptx and OO will then give you the option to select the correct filter to open it and problem solved. The MS users needed to download and install an add-on, and this can only be done after running updates to Office beforehand!!! :lolflag:
