### Post by RuneGeek on 2009-04-27
About three months ago, I put Ubuntu 8.10 on my laptop (it came with Vista).  I was already a Firefox and OpenOffice user, and the laptop has never been suitable for gaming, so I figured I'd give it a shot.  Everything was great until my wireless quit working a couple of weeks ago.  I hadn't gotten around to trying to debug it, so when 9.04 came out I upgraded and got the wireless going again.

I'm still dual-booting with XP on my desktop... for now.  I have a couple of programs that I don't want to let go of just yet.  I'm going to try some sort of VM solution for those, though.

My fiance uses her comp for the basics... surfing, e-mail, occasional office apps.  I put her on 8.10 about a month ago and she has been very happy with it.

I'm quite pleased with Ubuntu.  Three different machines with very differing hardware, all had 8.10, all have been upgraded to 9.04, all three installs and upgrades went well.  The wireless issue is the only problem I've had (with the exception of a few learning-curve issues... I like to play/tweak!)  ;)

I'm not anti-Microsoft or anything... but as a long-time computer user and an IT professional, I have become progressively less-impressed with the value of their products as compared to their cost.

So I'm a convert now.  My spirit has always been open-source; now my computers are too.  

):P
