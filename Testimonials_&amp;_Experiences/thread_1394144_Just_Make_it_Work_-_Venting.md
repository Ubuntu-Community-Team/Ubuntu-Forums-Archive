Canonical can't do anything about Flash. Even if they wanted to, they can't. Flash is a proprietary codec by Adobe. Canonical can't change the code in Flash, they can't change the format that Flash ships out in, they can't do anything, because it is proprietary and they are not allowed to. I'm pretty sure that Canonical would love to have Flash not have any bugs on anyone's computer, ever, or cause any crashes, but how could they accomplish that? They can't look at, much less change the source code of Flash. If something doesn't work right, all they can do is say, hey Adobe, please please please do something about THIS here, and hope that Adobe pays attention to them. If Adobe chooses to release a substandard, buggy version for Linux, then there is absolutely nothing that Canonical can do to force them to fix it. I guess they could try paying Adobe to make the Linux version of Flash better, but there's nothing forcing Adobe to take the money, and Canonical is not yet turning a profit and they do have to pay their own employees.
