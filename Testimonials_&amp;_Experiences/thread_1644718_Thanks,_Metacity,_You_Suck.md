### Post by rookcifer on 2010-12-13
I am about to ditch Gnome completely after learning that one cannot disable "focus stealing prevention" in the default WM (metacity).  Indeed, it seems the geniuses over at Gnome/Metacity decided that not giving users a choice to turn this on or off was the right thing to do (when most other WM's do allow the user to make that choice).  With Compiz I can elect to turn it on or off.  The same with KDE and Xfce and their default WM's.

So why is the Metacity team so obstinate to doing the right thing?  They claim that they are merely following convention and all the applications are simply not coded properly to handle it.  Well, I'm sorry but that does us end users not one bit of good.  We have no control over the app developers, so why punish us?  

This particularly affects some apps I HAVE to use in WINE.  I am sure those Winblows app developers are not concerned in the slightest with developing their apps to work with Linux (and to conform with the Metacity team's rules), so this is why us Linux users need the choice to disable this horrendous "focus stealing prevention" BS.  The apps I use depend on being able to move forward when a certain action occurs.  Not possible with Metacity.
