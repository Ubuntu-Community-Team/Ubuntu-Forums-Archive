### Post by Duskao on 2009-05-16
I personally find this quite funny. I am a bit of a gamer and I have used XP for years, and it worked great... for the most part, of course with the exceptions of all the virus's, malware, memory leaks, defraggin, and file and registry corruption... ok, well... lets just say it worked and leave it at that :D. But for games it was actually quite good, I had very little in the way of problems and the same goes for most software as well. I completely skipped Vista because I tried it a couple times on my mothers computer (fixing it of all the aforementioned proplems with XP) and found that it was complete garbage. The menu setups were illogical and it didn't run well even with decent specd computers. But when windows 7 Beta came along I figured I would give it a try. At first I thought it was decent to be honest. I still prefered XP over it, but it certainly was a step in the right direction from Vista, minus the lack of a generally logical setup, but still an improvement over Vista. But as I said before, I am a bit of a gamer so naturally I wanted to play some of my favorite games on windows 7 to see how it would go. All I can say is wow. But not in a good way. Of course there were some games that installed and ran completely fine out of the box, but I found more and more that those were quite rare. 

One game that comes to mind that took me about a week to get up and running was Fallout 3. And when I say week, I mean a week. I would work at it a few hours a day. Then I finally got it running and gave myself a little pat on the back... Then my sanity came back. And I realized that it took me the better part of 20 hours to get a fairly new game running on a OS that it is designed to run on... Huh? Is that right? I thought to myself. Now i am totally aware that win 7 was/is in beta but when I had searched I found that even many vista users were having these issues or similar. I then reverted back to XP. I then found that my CD and my key would no longer work (they are completely legit, not OEM). Of course I was not happy about this at all. Basically what this means likely is that while I was using windows 7, someone probably generated my key and is using a hacked version of XP with MY XP key. I could be wrong in this but it's possible. I then went down a bad road... I tried a hacked version of XP. (I didn't realize that there was something better at the time and could not afford a new XP). Anyway, when I installed an AV it caught the hack and said this is a practice is futility and decided to try Linux. Right after the initial install everything worked peachy (Ubuntu 8.10). I then tried wine and it worked quite well, minus a few Ati bugs. Then installed Fallout 3. Between AppDB and the POL forums I had it installed and working great in about a half an hour, plus most other games I play in less time, as easily as putting in the disc and choosing the install or setup.exe. And thats not to mention all the amazing games in the repository within add/remove. 

To be honest, MS could take a hint or two about an OS from Ubuntu, and when there is an OS upgrade, you don't threaten to destroy a decent OS like XP by stopping support for it, and put out trash like Vista and win 7 which actually makes it more difficult to use and you need to learn to use the operating system again from scratch. I actually found it easier to learn to use Ubuntu from scratch then to try to learn win 7 which I should be familiar with because of all the years put into MS products. Different naming schemes and naming directories should not have any part in a OS upgrade, why complicate something that is already complicated enough for the end user? I'm a bit of a geek and I found it difficult to get used to the stupidity. My mother has seen me use Ubuntu and she says "geez that looks like it's easy to use" and I reply "it is". But I am hesitant to put Ubuntu on her computer because I don't want the same thing that happened to me with XP to happen to her with Vista, and I fear that a Dual boot might be a wee bit too complicated for her. 

Sorry for being so long winded but I had to get this off my chest. Anyway, Canocial and Ubuntu deserve a huge Kudos and my personal gratitude. Way to go guys.
