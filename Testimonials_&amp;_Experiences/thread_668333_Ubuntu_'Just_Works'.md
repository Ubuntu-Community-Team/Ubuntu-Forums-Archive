### Post by jackmo on 2008-01-15
Hi all,

I'm a bit of a gamer and have always been a windows nub. I've always known about linux and more recently Ubuntu when my brother would tell me about it but I was always never keen to take the plunge, always a little worried I wouldn't be able to make it work without massive amounts of effort.

After stumbling onto the Mark Shuttleworth blog I decided to checkout the site and download ubuntu. With very little effort I burnt the CD, booted from it and voila! it actually did 'just work'. I thought I would need to do some sort of leet haxing to get my dodgy wireless network card going but even from booting off the cd (not installing) it was just automatically detected and functioning - I could not believe it.

Anyway, to all those involved with the development of Ubuntu: kudos and nice work. I've always wanted to change to linux but have always been a little apprehensive. It was the fact that the CD booted and just worked so well that finally nudged me over.

My next goal is to get warcraft3 functioning online, managed to get it to load but not play on battle.net last night but I think a bit of tinkering and it should be sorted, fingers crossed.

p.s. I'm loving the support / community available - ubuntu FTW!
