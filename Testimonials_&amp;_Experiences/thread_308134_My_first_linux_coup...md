### Post by davidfield on 2006-11-27
Anyone who says it can't be done.. they are talking out of thier behind..

I'm the IT Manager of a UK ISP.. who has about 30 XP PC's in the Office, and by the end of Friday our support dept will be running on Ubuntu Edgy, and ditching XP..

There were a few obstacles, 

our VoIP system, we were using Voispeed, and the Directors didn't purchase the Sip client.. that changed, today, and I got Twinkle working in about 10 minutes to attach to the Voispeed SIP Server.. 

Next was BT...

BT insist on using the worst, and i mean the WORST interface for logging support calls with thier Broadband Support, eCo.. It is an app which will only work in IE6.. with security certificates. It was at this point i went for [IEs4Linux]("http://www.tatanka.com.br/ies4linux/page/Installation:Ubuntu") the perfect installation, for IE on Ubuntu.. It just works.. and runs this dreaded BT software....

Pretty much everything else we do on a day to day basis can be done from a Linux desktop.. infact, things like NMAP and proper NSLookups make it hard to go back to XP...

I will post an update in the next week, to let you know how it goes, and i will showcase this to other depts.. i can think of Sales and Provisioning being able to use this desktop...

Its a saving of £2000 on software alone for the IT dept...
