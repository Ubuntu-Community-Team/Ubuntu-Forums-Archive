### Post by dmizer on 2009-05-19
Packages which come from "the murky past" are not unpatched.

As a general rule, you have one of two options:
1) Rock solid stable to infinity but old packages.
2) Cutting edge software but unstable.

Option number one is most important in an enterprise level server situation where you can't afford to be niggling with a broken network after having upgraded from ibex to jaunty, only to do the same thing again in 6 months when upgrading from jaunty to karmic.

Option number one is also a good idea when you just need to use your computer and don't want to fiddle with it when things break or become unstable.

Option number 2 is for those of you who can't live without the latest release of OpenOffice or Firefox.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-05-19
Dude.

It's what they call a "stable" software configuration.

"Stable" means "Unchanging".

A lot of people want to run systems that remain secure through patches, but don't want to deal with the trauma of being delivered new software versions that may have moved/removed features or new unpredictable bugs. That's the whole point of Ubuntu LTS; that essential security updates and bugfixes come to the software, but the user or administrator does not have to deal with things changing on him or her.

If Ubuntu's developers kept all the software in Hardy up-to-date, it would pretty much just be Jaunty.
