### Post by michaelzap on 2007-09-19
My Ubuntu install definitely boots and performs much faster than my XP install, despite the fact that my XP install is on a SATA drive and Ubuntu is on an IDE drive (and I tweaked my XP for speed quite a lot before I left it for Ubuntu). Just loading Kaspersky (my XP anti-virus) takes quite a while (and I have ClamAV running on Ubuntu). I always feel like I'm using a computer that has a cold or something whenever I boot into XP, and I'm always looking for those missing desktops so I can organize all my windows!

I do boot into Windows from time to time, and usually because there's something that I can't quite do it Ubuntu. Sometimes it's for Photoshop, sometimes it's for Flash. I'm also frustrated by the fact that I can't find a decent multi-subtitle DVD authoring app in Ubuntu and the video editors I've tried just aren't as good as what I'm used to from Windows.

Oh and I hardly ever use OpenOffice anymore since I installed AbiWord and Gnumeric...

I find that although there are some things that I can't do in Ubuntu (yet), they're few and far between and the overall experience and speed makes up for this. There are things that I do easily in Ubuntu that were difficult to accomplish in Windows as well (a friend just had me rip some streaming video that he just couldn't get in Windows, and it was a snap in Ubuntu). There was a learning curve and I had to spend some time figuring out new challenges, but I enjoyed that.

As happy as I am with Ubuntu, the more I use it the more platform-agnostic I become. I'll use Windows when necessary and a Mac when there's one's around and whatever else seems like a good way to do what I need to do. That sense of freedom and focus on the end result may be the best gift I've received from Ubuntu.
