### Post by mattweed9 on 2009-12-04
Right now the only pc I own is a Gateway NV5214u laptop. The first time I installed Ubuntu 9.10, it had just been release. I experienced issues with the network manager. It wasn't holding a connection. I found out through trial and error that Wcid seemed to work. I have since played around with almost every major Distro. I liked Fedora 12, but I decided I needed to stick with one Distro, so I've come back to Ubuntu. The Media buzz and reviews helped, but it boiled down to the time I had spent using it before I wandered off and tried the other ones out. I just felt more comfortable using it and it's interface. I was glad to see after installing the updates, I no longer had to install Wcid. Network manager  is working with out any issues.

Back to my first statement for a moment, this is the only PC I own at the moment. My tower is no more, and beyond worth trying to fix. This is the only thing keeping me from removing windows 7 from my laptop. I really only use it for school. A few on line courses I take. Other then that it is just used to keep me from going crazy in the winter season. I am happy with Ubuntu being media friendly. Being able to watch a video, or listen to music, or surf the net. My only dislike so far is the whole gaming thing. I like many others, like to play video games time from time. And it saddens me that Microsoft has such a hold on that part of the market. Thats why Ubuntu 9.10 and Windows 7 have to live side by side for now on my hard drive.

To focus on my actual experience a little more, I would have to describe it as cluttered. Maybe cluttered isn't the right word, over whelming is probably better. From the perspective of someone who is pretty much new to the whole Linux world, I find it to be a little hard to get rooted. 

There is so much info about this and about that, some more technical then others, some that is no longer correct or applies, that I  find myself getting frustrated. For someone new like me, who maybe is just trying to find a simple answer, it is hard to know where to start. The documentation on the main site, these forums, google.... Dear god, don't ever look for answers about Ubuntu on google. It will make  your head explode instantly. My point being, it's a lot to take in. There don't seem to be simple "getting started" anything anywhere. Nothing like, a little welcome to Ubuntu here is some basic info to get you started and off in the right direction. most of the stuff I found like when first starting Firefox, seems to dive right into the technical stuff like I had been using Linux since birth.

Instead I found myself on google and here, reading hours and hours of info, that to find out, wasn't what I was looking for or it didn't provide any real answers.I am still not sure where to start for things. Like for finding out properly about all those neat cool softwares that are in the software manager. Is there a Linux site that reviews and discusses them? Or, do I have to google each one and do hours and hours of research to find out App A is only a music play and not a Music and Video players like App B. But App B is buggy and will cause things to crash for no good reason, so App C is the one you want. It may sound like laziness, but I have already spent years and years doing all this for Windows, and it makes it harder to switch. And yet, I am still here, still googling, still getting iterated and looking for answers. I believe I have found a good thing, I just need the right direction to help me get going like many other users.

Things like...

Helpful links to trouble shooting 

Helpful Software links - About, how to's, reviews

Helpful links to better understanding the command line

Helpful links to Developers tools for Linux

And so on.

And there could very well be these things, but if your new, and don't know where your going, they can be very hard to find. I myself have found hundreds and hundreds of post and blogs about the same topic's, yet each of them seem almost completely different in answers.


I don't know, to me this seems like the biggest draw back for Ubuntu. From what I have read, it is gearing more and more towards the " Regular Joe" kind of users, and not just IT guys and coders. Yet I know for me and many other new users, the over whelming amount of information on even just one simple topic when it comes to Linux, is enough to steer most people away out of fear that it will be to hard for them to understand and use.

So, other then being completely lost when it come to the right documentation, I really like Ubuntu lol. The UI is great and pretty easy to navigate and use, and the software manager is nice as well as the update manager.


I know that's a lot of crap for one post, but better then a two word response.
