### Post by drawkcab on 2009-05-17
Windows enacts a certain epistemology regarding the relationship between os and applications.  Linux enacts an alternative.  When windows users protest, usually it has to do with the fact that they refuse to move between the two; i.e. they refuse to spend time learning this new way of organization.  

Back when you had to work in the command line or even outside of x, I think this refusal made some sense.  But given they recent direction of distributions like Ubuntu, PCLinuxOS, Mandriva, SUSE and Fedora, I'm not sure the objection "it just doesn't work" holds water.

In other cases there really is a legitimate complaint where the person has made an attempt to execute a specific task and they find they cannot do it.  If you really need to run final or the latest version of photoshop, then yeah, linux just doesn't work.
