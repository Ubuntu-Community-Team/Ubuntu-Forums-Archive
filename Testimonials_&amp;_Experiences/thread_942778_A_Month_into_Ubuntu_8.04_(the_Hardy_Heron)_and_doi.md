### Post by joltvolta on 2008-10-09
... wanted to throw in my $.02 

After my main computer (WinXP pro) went down due to a video card fan failure I switched to a secondary comp I had sitting around. 

When I slapped the power button I was reminded that WinXP and Ubuntu (version 6?) were dual-booted. Both we're fresh installs, and I need to get some work done requiring Office Apps. Knowing that Open Office was pre-bundled with Ubuntu, it was a no brainer. 

So far, its been great running Ubuntu. I've since upgraded to v8.04, and installed various apps and played with the endless desktop customizability (is that a word?) and I'm happy with the results. 

There certainly has been some issues to overcome, and a few hours of frustration when something wasn't working correctly, but I must say its been fun trying to figure out a new OS. 

One trap I fell into when using a new OS is making a direct comparision of the previous OS with Ubuntu. 

Comparing one OS that I have had years of experience with to another that I just started using isn't fair, although its the first thing that I started to do. 

I had to remind myself that I've had years of issues and frustration with Windows (3.1x, 95, 98, WinXP), and needed to cut Ubuntu some slack while I learn and become accustomed. 

Oh, and I'm not saying that Windows sucks and Linux rockerz. WinXP has been a great OS for me. I'll continue to have a dual-boot setup as I still have use for it.

Both have their pro's and con's, but Ubuntu (and Linux in general) has more to offer me and is much more interesting. 

Good work & thank you's to all those who've put in time and effort into Linux and ubuntu.
