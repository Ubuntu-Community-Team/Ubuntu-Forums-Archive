### Post by BigSilly on 2012-03-11
Just wanted to post up a sincere thank you to the developer(s) of [Boot-Repair]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Boot-Repair"). For some unknown reason after updating my openSUSE install, it decided to install its own version of GRUB legacy over my Ubuntu Grub2 without prompting me or asking for permission. This resulted in the Ubuntu install completely disappearing from the GRUB screen and unable to boot. I tried Super Grub2 Disk, Parted Magic, and various commands from a live disc, none of which worked. I was looking at the prospect of a long boring Sunday stuck in front of a monitor fixing GRUB, when I discovered Boot-Repair. It's easily added to your list of software sources, and it installed, initialised, and fixed the broken Grub2 for me in seconds, saving me from a ruined day! Ubuntu is restored, and all is well. And it's lovely out. :D

An absolute God-send of a tool, and further proof, if it were needed, that Ubuntu and its community lead the way for free software for regular users. My heartfelt thanks again. :)
