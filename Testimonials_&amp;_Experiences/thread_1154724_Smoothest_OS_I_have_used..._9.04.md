### Post by AoA Linux on 2009-05-10
I been hanging around here for a bit now, and thought I would post my experiences with Ubuntu.  A while back I purchased a Dell Inspirion  1525 and made the mistake of getting Windows Vista with 1GB of ram.  The computer was nearly unusable for my standards.  A friend of mine said I should try Linux, so I googled Linux and ran across Ubuntu... went to the website downloaded a copy of 8.10 Intrepid Ibex and installed it right then.  To my amazement the computer was fast and everything worked out of the box... like it should have with Vista.  I have since then upgraded to 4GB of ram, but the point is that Ubuntu uses memory much more efficiently.  Thank goodness for Vista being a memory hog, or I probably would not have turned to Linux! 
  
   I  stuck with Intrepid for about a week after Jaunty was released after having some problems with the beta.  I later decided to do a clean install of 64 bit Jaunty and to give it a shot.  I have LOVED it.  Of course everything "just worked".  The new ext4 is noticeably faster on my machine then the ext3 on Intrepid.  Flash and Java have worked flawlessly, it is the smoothest operating system I have ever used.  That includes Windows 95 to the Windows 7 RC and Mac OS X.  I am very very impressed with Ubuntu as an OS!
  
   Sorry for the novel guys, I just wanted to share my very positive experience with Ubuntu!  If people would take the time to experience this OS, I don't see how they could not enjoy what it has to offer... lack of viruses, better file management/permissions, lighter OS, free (in both ways), TONS of software, and GREAT community support.

Thanks guys.
