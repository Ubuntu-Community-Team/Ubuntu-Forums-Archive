### Post by weblordpepe on 2008-07-17
I think translators are under-appreciated. It's all these non-english speaking or third world oooga-booga countries (sorry I am not very literate) that are the next major target by Microsoft.

Microsoft works by establishing platforms, establishing APIs and ground-roots. If you can show linux or open-source applications/formats/protocols are good, then that will help tremendously.

For example- promote Firefox. Promote OpenOffice. Or even SSH!

I am always going 'hey check out this latest awesome XYZ thing'. My boddies go 'wow cool! how do I get that?' and I say 'ohh sorry Windows doesnt support that'.

You'll find that Microsoft not implementing open-protocols is one of their weapons. Try to promote things which leverage open systems and Windows, like applications which are cross platform. They usually come bundled with support for things.

Eg OpenOffice gives you support for ODF's as well as DOCs. Thats an advantage. 


And last of all you can promote the mindset, or different architecture of linux. Eg:

Windows: OS must be secured by using firewall. OS on its own unsafe. Must run anti-virus/security products.
Newer version = slower, costs money. Older version=fast but unsafe, unsupported, apps  wont work.

Linux: OS secure in itself. Applications pre-verified as safe (repositories). Costs no money. The bloat / speed / memory usage has no relation to how new the software is. Slow computer? Can still use modern, safe software. 

Thats my 2 cents :)
