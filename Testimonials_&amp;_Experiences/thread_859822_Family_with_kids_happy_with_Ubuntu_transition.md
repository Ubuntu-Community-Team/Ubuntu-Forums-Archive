### Post by davesalyers on 2008-07-14
My family and I made the switch from Vista to Ubuntu Hardy a couple of weeks ago and we couldn't be happier. Installation on the desktop went smoothly - even recognizing all of the hardware so installation only took a few minutes. My pesky HP laptop was a little harder with the wireless and graphics card, but the fine folks here at the Ubuntu forums were able to help with it - step by step in some places.

As parents of two elementary school age kids (ages 8 and 10) who are homeschooled we have been impressed with the quality of the educational and game software available for free (if you homeschool you tend to be a single-income family). We tend to be one of those "casual games" families (even on our Wii we tend to go for the more casual and party games). My kids also had no problem doing their online homeschool work and favorite games on the web using Firefox.

I even found some great games for me to use to unwind when I come home from work like Battle for Wesnoth, Open Arena, Alien Arena as well as my standard casual games like Hearts (I would love to see some other single player vs CPU card games like Rummy, Gin, Canasta, Rook, Uno, Mille Bornes - hint, hint), Checkers, Chess, Circus Linux, and the various puzzle games like Monkey Bubble and Monsterz.

I'm able to bring my work home from the office in MS Word format - load and edit it in Open Office - and take it back to the office.

My kids were also able to navigate Ubuntu very easily. My 8 year old son much prefers it to our old Vista OS.

Good show!

Dave
