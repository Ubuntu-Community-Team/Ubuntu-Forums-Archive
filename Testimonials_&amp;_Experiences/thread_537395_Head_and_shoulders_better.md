### Post by plwhit on 2007-08-28
I've tried a few other flavors of Linux but Ubuntu is head & shoulders above all the others for breaking me from with windowz.

Installation was a breeze, everything worked! sound, inet, wireless keyboard/mouse (even the sound controls on the keyboard worked)...

Getting my ATI radeon x1300 card working with hardware acceleration took a few trips to the forums, but it's working now.  Getting beryl going was a breeze (thanks again to these forums)...

Performance compared to windowz, best way I can describe it is quite a bit snappier... thats across the board..

The things that bummed me out up to this point with Linux distributions (can't get xyz to work -or- reboot the system and now something else doesn't work the way it used to etc..) is gone.. system is rock stable, video/X never crashes... media plays without the pauses/jerkiness I had with windowz.

Thanks to all of you who made this possible.....

~peter

### Post by michaelzap on 2007-08-28
I totally agree. I was actually thinking about posting my own "Thank you, Ubuntu" thread when I happened across yours. Maybe it's the wine I've been drinking, but I was just feeling sort of happily buzzed and realized that I've been using Ubuntu almost exclusively for two or three months now and I can't even imagine going back to Windows ever again (except for those necessary side-trips - usually via Virtual Box - when I need to view something in IE7 or create a Flash file).

The other day someone told me that one of our USB memory sticks had a virus on it. I didn't think twice. I inserted it into my PC, checked it with ClamAV, found the virus and deleted it (took all of 30 seconds and was as painless as deleting a text file).

There are still a few things that I can't do in Ubuntu (e.g., create multi-subtitle DVDs), but I'm going to stick it out until I can and keep reminding myself of all the new things I can do that never would've been possible in Windows.
