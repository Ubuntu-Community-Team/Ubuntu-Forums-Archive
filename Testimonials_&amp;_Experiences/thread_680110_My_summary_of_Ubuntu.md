I wrote this paper for selfish reasons.  I did not actually write this as a tutorial, but more as something for me to see how much I know about Ubuntu (which isn't a lot).  I know this was written with the Forrest Gump's of the computer world as a target audience, that was intentional.  Anyway, any critiques, comments, conversations or corrections would be most welcome(unintentional alliteration).  Thanks, everyone!

[CENTER]Dies Irae
By: einherier, AKA OMERTA, AKA Ian J[/CENTER]

If anyone owns a lot of shares of Microsoft, I suggest you seriously consider looking for a good time to dump it.  Microsoft's whole world is about to change.  The complete story of Windows reign of **** over 89% of computer users is in its final chapters (the majority of the other 11% being Mac and Linux users).

Back in 1985, when Windows operating system accounted for approximately 2% of the pie (unreferenced).  Most people were using IBM's DOS.  Poor old IBM, RIP... I still remember playing knock the green blip of the red dot with my blue square not to mention discovering pornography for the first...magical time... on my grandpa's old 80/88 machine.  Today, most people wouldn't know what a DOS was if it kicked them squarely in the ****, but back then, if you didn't know a couple of tricks on DOS, you woudln't be able to get anything done.  Today, Microsoft Windows is the world's leading operating system. Which still dumbfounds me, considering that Windows has built a SOLID track record of unreliability and instability, difficulty of use, proliferating vulnerabilites, and hosts upon hosts of other problems.

But the bell tolls for thee, Mr. Gates...

Dell has recently paired with Ubuntu, completely free Linux based operating system aimed at the average PC user, and beginning to market Ubuntu PC's to the general public.  

So what is Ubuntu?  Ubuntu is the operating system that you're going to want to try if you haven't already by the time you finish reading this little article.

I am not saying Ubuntu is the perfect operating system, there is no such thing as the perfect operating system.  Ubuntu is, simply stated, a dramatically superior product.  Let's get dirty:

Windows Vista costs between a hundred dollars and nearly half a grand depending on which version you need.  Compare this to Ubuntu, which is free -- yes free.  I hope you like that word, cause you're going to read it a lot pretty soon.  I will say it again.  Ubantu is 100% free, and the monetary savings is only half of the beauty.  Because it is free, anyone can look at the source code, what this means is that there are lotsa little computer nerds all over the world pecking around on their spare time able to make contributions to the Ubuntu operating system.  Additionally, and unlike with windows, they can spot and rectify potential security holes which can potentially ruin a Windows user's world.  
Ubantu comes with a (free) firewall called Iptables already installed.

Spyware? Gone like yesterday.  

Adware?  Don't get sentimental, but you are about to stop seeing advertisements for V1@gRa and penile enlargement.

Defragging? It's a ghost, baby.

Virus protection?  You literally don't need it.  You can get it (free), but its more of a resource drainer than anything except in special circumstances (scanning an attatchment that you are sending to your misguided Windows-using friends).

Ubuntu is easy to set up.  It's totally a perspective thing, but most feel that setting up, configuring, and making changes to things is much more difficult in Windows.  

You will esentially have a better computer because so much of your computer's resources will be freed up.  For example, Windows Vista REQUIRES (and keeps tied up) a whopping half a gig of RAM (memory; specifically: Random Access Memory).  Windows is so big that it takes a DVD to store it, Ubuntu is small enough to fit on a CD.  This also applies to CPU usage (Central Proccessing Unit, the big chip inside your computer that does all the math that makes magic like what you see in front of you possible)

It looks cooler.  Imagine your friends seeing how you're using another operating system besides possibly the only one they've seen in thier whole life.  People will talk, make way for you on the street.  Women will want you, men will want to be you.

Compatibility can be an issue.  This is the biggest hump to overcome, and a lot is still being done to fix it.  This is also the biggest obstacle that Linux and Mac O/S users have had.  Some programs such as WINE (free) can run some Windows based programs (Note: Some viruses can theoretically operate via WINE, so it may not be a terrible idea to have virus protection on, but your chances of coming to harm are still quite small.)

Here's the kicker:  You can download it for free (or have a copy mailed to you (free) though it takes 6-8 weeks).  The free copy comes with what is called a LiveCD (a.k.a. LiveDistro).  The nifty thing about this is that you can run Ubuntu without even installing it on your computer...  Thats right, you can run it directly from the disk.  How pimp is that, and how could you NOT at least TRY it now?

Another disadvantage is that you will hear computer elitists bitch.  They have bitched and moaned for years about how they wished that the Linux operating system was mainstream, and now that it IS going mainstream, they say it is complete shit; however, Ubuntu is aimed at the average computer user.  If you want something that gives you god-like power over your computer at the cost of making it extremely easy to accidentally foul up your computer, use Debian Linux.  If you want a good operating system that is more reliable, and efficient than windows, use Ubuntu.  But, if you really enjoy a good ol' fashioned ***-******', keep using Windows.
