### Post by mjaytee on 2011-02-02
[SIZE=2]I had an old ThinkPad Z60m that was the slowest thing ever booting and anything else for that matter even after tweaking start-ups and other stuff.  So it made for an excellent guinea pig for trying out Ubuntu.

My first impressions were great.  I did a reformat full installation that went without a hitch.  Everything was recognized, i.e. wireless, touchpad, sound, and what not.  The improvement in boot up, shut down, and restart times are worth its weight in gold.  I like the look and feel of the gnome desktop for the most part.

I've installed a few programs to test things out.  Everything went fine until I attempted to do one of my favorite computer tasks rip a cd to the mp3 format.  Now, I'm cool with trying ogg and would love to go that route but let's face it mp3 is the defacto standard even though it is inferior.  

My first attempts were a complete disaster.  Some kind of way I messed up the various rippers I tried along with  the ability to download anything.  I called myself installing the proper codecs and but no success.  The next day new updates appeared then everything seemed to work out.  I finally found Sound Juicer (yes, I did not know it was Audio CD Extractor I was looking in the S's not A's) was and was able to use it to rip a cd to mp3.  Then came the problem of actually playing the mp3s.  I have downloaded/installed the restricted extras thing, along with all that gstreamer stuff, still no luck.  So back to the drawing board.

So with some good and bad experiences with Ubuntu, here is my assessment.  Ubuntu is good but not ready for prime-time.  Seems great for those curious like me, enthusiasts, hobbyists, and the down with "the man" types but it is too encumbered with some frustrating quirkiness for the general public.  To use a sports analogy, if Windows and Mac were Kobe Bryant and Tom Brady, Unbuntu would be Rudy Gay and Matt Schaub, good players but not consistently money.

I like the abundance of available free software, though not as prim and proper as commercial software functional nevertheless, like why doesn't Rhythmbox have a stop button.  I will continue to play around with it and I have a book coming to that will hopefully help me with getting under the hood.  Well, that's it for now, as I get more knowledge I'll come back with further experiences.
[/SIZE]
