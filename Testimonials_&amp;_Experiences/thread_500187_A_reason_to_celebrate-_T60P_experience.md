### Post by Ancheron on 2007-07-13
This summer I got a Lenovo T60P for college. It came with Windows XP Professional (would never touch Vista) installed.... but being a Ubuntu devotee, I wanted to dual boot ASAP. Popped in 7.04 live cd.... and X failed. After some late-night searching on my OSS-reclaimed XP desktop, with Firefox, OpenOffice, and Eclipse/Aptana, I was able to get 7.04 working via this forum and thinkwiki; although after a system restore from lost MBR, and a complicated install process. 

After a month of playing with madwifi, ndiswrapper, and module loading configurations, it is complete! 

The stubborn atheros card now loads automatically on boot. still have to resolve nm-applet/keyring stuff... but otherwise it works great. The next objective is to get XGL working on some level.

So my advice: even if you have a stubborn laptop wireless card, there is hope out there! 

Finally, I can pull myself out of a slump of XP degeneracy- I have a lot to do, including working with Aptana, learning Ruby and AJAX, finish studying calculus, and doing designs for a website. 

Also, the laptop itself is one of the best computer experiences I've had. lenovo makes a solid ThinkPad. Not to mention the incredibly intimidating matte black styling- I have to live up to it.

Signing off
