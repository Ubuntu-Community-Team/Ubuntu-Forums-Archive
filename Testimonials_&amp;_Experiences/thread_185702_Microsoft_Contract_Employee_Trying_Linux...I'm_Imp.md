### Post by Avenue on 2006-06-01
Hey Kids, 

Believe it or not i've been working at Microsoft HQ in Redmond as a contractor for the past three years (i'm in Marketing for Exchange Server and the new FrontBridge/Exchange Hosted Services team).   I've heard SO MUCH about Linux that I wanted to give it a try for myself.  

I recently installed both SUSE and Ubuntu Linux on seperate hard drives and I have to say, Ubuntu is most impressive.  I absolutely cannot believe all of the software that comes with a Linux install, and i'm frankly quite surprised at the look and feel of OpenOffice.  Of course i'm a total beginner and have not really played too much yet, so right now my experience is limited.

  On Saturday I went out and bought the new book, "Beginning Ubuntu" so I could learn to really get inside the OS and get a feel for it.  I have to admit that without the book I would have been lost, or at least I would never have been able to discover the majority of cool things that I have discovered thus far (simple things like Virtual Desktops and the Office software).  In my opinion a lack of both marketing and published materials have held Linux back, specifically Ubuntu.  However, I do think this will change in the near future.  

Great OS, especially FREE which is unbelievable to me!!!  My plan was to install, play around and then wipe my hard drive so I could install a Microsoft Server but i'm not going to be doing that now.  

I'm sold!!

Ave

### Post by jdong on 2006-06-06
Always glad to hear about a newcomer to Linux enjoying the experience. As others have been saying, check your Ubuntu version. If it's 5.10 ("Breezy Badger"), we'd recommend that you upgrade to Dapper Drake (6.06LTS). It brings usability and performance to a whole new level, as Ubuntu's first long term support release. You can either decide to do an upgrade ([https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DapperUpgrades?action=show&redirect=DapperUpgradeNotes](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DapperUpgrades?action=show&redirect=DapperUpgradeNotes)) which smoothly upgrades your Breezy to Dapper, requiring just one reboot at the end of the procedure while letting you work on your system during the procedure (another Ubuntu strength), or you can try out the all-new live installer CD that boots into an instant Ubuntu system and lets you use it while installing.


Believe it or not, I've been hearing lots of stories similar to yours, and it's still surprising. That is, Microsoft power users / dedicated users giving Ubuntu a try and being impressed. This time last year, I wouldn't have expected them to be able to find their way around a Linux install, so you can see what strides Linux has made to end user friendliness :)




BTW, while you're at it, push Ubuntu to its multitasking limits. Open up lots of programs. If you know how to run an infinite loop through a programming language or otherwise push CPU up to 100%, try it! You'd be surprised at how responsive the operating system is, while under stress. That was the first thing that drew me from Windows to Linux.
