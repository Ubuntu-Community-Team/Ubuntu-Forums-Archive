### Post by xyzzyman on 2012-01-23
Most likely your new laptop will come with Windows. Even if you plan on never using Windows and completely wiping it clear (Or even if you plan on dual-booting), I'd recommend you make sure you burn your recovery DVD's as they most likely are not included by default. You may find a time that you need to run Windows for something, or you need to reset to factory for some reason and you'll be glad you took the time to make them.

If you aren't familiar with making your recovery discs, the system will guide you through it and will tell you to (Just don't click No!)

### Post by Baldrick_NZ on 2012-01-24
What I'd do, is to find a PC with USB support and install the whole OS to an 8g flash drive as a working system. Then, with flash drive in hand, go to your local computer retailer and boot from your flash drive onto your desired PC.

I've found most retailers are happy to accommodate this if they understand it's not going to effect (or infect) their demo machines.

By doing this, you can test the puter you want to buy to see whether its components are Linux friendly 'out of the box'.

It also gives Ubuntu (or Linux generally) exposure to people who may not otherwise been aware of its existence.

Just be aware though that the chances of the screen resolution are likely to be low when testing, especially if the PC has a nVidea video card installed. This is an easy fix, by downloading the drivers when you actually install Ubuntu onto the machine.

Hope this helps.
