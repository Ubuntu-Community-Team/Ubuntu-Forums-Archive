### Post by waspbr on 2008-10-27
unfortunately this story repeats itself a little too often, mostly with people that are fed up with windows and jump right into linux without any experience. 

Since I knew a few of the programs I use are not ported into linux when I started about a year ago, I decided to take things a little slow, instead of installing ubuntu as the main OS, it is often advisable to dual boot. 

As mentioned before, there is no ctrl+alt+del in ubuntu, but there is the kill applet and/or ctrl+alt+backspace. Dual booting either through an alternate partition or wuby is the best way to go for new-comers. 

You can slowly take gain experience in the system and before you know it, you will be using linux more than windows. 

So if you can, try dual booting, take it slow, the cold-turkey plunge into a completely different OS often leads to frustration, specially in a work machine. try posting your problems in the forum, you will find that it won't take too long to find a solution.
