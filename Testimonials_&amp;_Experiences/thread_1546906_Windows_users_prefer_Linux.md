### Post by Nick_Jinn on 2010-08-06
Admittedly my sample size is small, but ever since I started fixing peoples 'broken' PCs by saving their files to external storage via Knoppix and then installing an appropriate version of Linux (Usually Mint or Ubuntu Studio, GOS or MoonOS), I end up customizing it for them, setting up their panels and Docky or Awn, installing programs appropriate for their needs, setting up some of their windows apps with wine while also showing them the linux alternatives, enabling as much 3d eye candy as is appropriate for their hard ware....they love that...I geet OOOOHS and AAAAHS when they see 3D cube effects....They are absolutely amazed by linux and thank me and give me an extra $20 tip.....I work cheap, base rate $40, but I always make at least $60 with tips. These people are absolutely THRILLED by what I show them. It just looks and feels nicer than windows.


Now I realize a lot of people who install Ubuntu or Linux see the default screen and they are like....wtf? Thats ulgy! At least its nothing special. Its not until I turn on the eye candy and customize everything for them that they really start to appreciate it.


I think more PC vendors should consider more exotic installations of Linux. Use some kind of dock. Get some wicked cool themes. Be bold. Enable all of that cool eye candy to the extent their hardware can handle it. People are enamored by Ubuntu eye candy (More so than performance gains and being light weight that a lot of power users prefer more). Looks are a big deal, and then you show them the free apps and they are thrilled. After seeing Compiz effects, Windows7 is kind of ho hum...even mac.


So Im just reporting that I got another happy customer. I am by no means an expert. I am not even that good with CUI. However, I can trouble shoot and make a desktop pretty and useful. Just removing the initial headaches and having them reconfigured makes a huge difference in user response. 


Unfortunately the approach of 'keep it small and let the user decide' is good for geeks, but probably wont sell very well in the show room. They want that immediate flash and bling, which is why I am getting a reputation for setting up awesome desktops for cheap. Im trying to help Linux gain popularity in the process.

If any of you Linux geeks are broke, there seems to be a bit of money in fixing peoples virus ridden computers and installing Linux.....but you have to go all out on the eye candy, rather than keeping it simple for them. They love it. You can even make a little more by upgrading their ram, which is almost too easy to charge for. The hardest part is unscrewing the case panel. 



I really think that if more people saw really decked out versions of Ubuntu with all the eye candy enabled and some more exotic themes, I think people would choose it over Windows even at the same price.

What turns people off to linux is the headache of installation with difficult hardware, the unimpressive default settings, having to add medibuntu manually themselves, and it being kept a little too 'lite' for their needs. They dont even know what Compiz is let alone give it a chance to enable effects they dont know exist. They probably think they will break something.

On this paticular PC I couldnt get it to install at all....I had to physically remove the hard drive and put it in a different PC with a different video card, then swap it back after enabling the drivers and it finally worked.....pain in the *** that I didnt anticipate, but these people would never have given Linux a second chance after an experience like that...but now not only do they have Linux on their PC, but they are beyond thrilled and like it better than windows.
