### Post by lsb on 2009-11-04
I convinced a friend to have me build them a new computer and to go with PC-BSD because of my good experience with FreeBSD for my servers. Well, PC-BSD had too many little problems including stability. Did some googling for Linux desktops and the majority of the people said go with Ubuntu.

Well, I visited Ubuntu site and "oh look, the ISO is only 1 CD instead of 3 for PC-BSD". That's a good start. I downloaded and burned the ISO to CD and placed it into the now, PC-BSD erased computer. This is amazing, its only been 10 minutes and I'm up and running. Sure, I had to click to get the current updates taking another 5 minutes, but it was easier than PC-BSD and a whole lot easier than Windows update process.

Now its been 15 minutes and I need to have Photoshop, designed for Windows, running on Linux. My friend said, you can do that? I thought I needed Windows? No, I said, you don't need Windows. I installed Photoshop under Wine and it works faster than it did under Windows. Now my friend is Microsoft free and happy as a clam especially when I said you don't need to defrag or run spybot anymore. And don't bother looking for anti-virus software either.

Ubuntu is amazing. Now I'm going to build myself a new workstation running dual Xeon processors on the new Asus motherboard. Ubuntu will be at the top of my list as the Linux Distro to use and I'll be Microsoft free at last, kinda, as I live in Redmond, WA area of Washington state. Does Ubunta have t-shirts with their logo or Linux? When I go to lunch, it is nothing but Microsoft employees around here with their Windows 7 and MS-Office shirts.

Thanks,
Larry
