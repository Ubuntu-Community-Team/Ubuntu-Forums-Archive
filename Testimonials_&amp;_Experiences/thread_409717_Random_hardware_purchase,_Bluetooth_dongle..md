### Post by msgyrd on 2007-04-14
Alright, I've been a linux user for going on a decade, I've had my ups and downs with it, and if there was one thing I've learned over the years that I hope you learn the easy way (as opposed to my way, the hard way), it's that you should research your hardware purchases before you buy.  

Proprietary hardware and drivers are a raw deal for us linux users, but I wasn't thinking clearly and bought a usb -> bluetooth adapter on the spur of the moment.  If you're interested, it's an "IOGear Bluetooth to USB Adapter", model GBU221WM.   I only gave in to my purchasing weakness because it advertised Windows and Mac compatibility so I figured that it might adhere to some standards and I hoped for the best.

(Side note: I've upgraded to the Feisty beta already, because I like new kernels and I'm too lazy to compile them myself)

So I get it home, plug it in and...nothing.
No worries, I've been in worse situations, so I found this thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=75978&highlight=bluetooth](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=75978&highlight=bluetooth)  I followed part 1's instructions, I also installed obexftp (it's in the repositories as well), and poof, I was transferring files to and from my LG CU500 via bluetooth. I used obexftp, bluetooth-applet, and customized my bluetooth config.  Bluetooth connections still have a little polishing that needs to be done before the average user will 'approve' of it's ease of use, but I was impressed nonetheless.

From my years of linux usage, this experience was a decent surprise, and I figured someone might enjoy yet another success story.

### Post by DarkStarAeon on 2007-06-22
I use the same Bluetooth USB Adapter on Ubuntu, Vista, OS X Tiger, and XP. It's the **IOGEAR GBU221**.

After plugging in the Bluetooth USB Adapter I just went into Synaptic Package Manager and installed just about everything that mentioned Bluetooth. lol

Then I went into System > Preferences > Bluetooth Preferences and edited my settings.

Then I went to Internet > KbluetoothD and opened it, then added it to my start up programs (which you can do in  System > Preferences > Sessions) and then I went to Applications > Accessories > Bluetooth File Sharing and opened it, then also added it to my startup programs.

Now I transfer files to/from my Ubuntu desktop, Vista laptop, XP laptop, Mac desktop, Nokia N800 and other devices.

I can plug my Nokia N800 into my Samsung Home Cinema System, then via Bluetooth send files from my computers to the Nokia N800 which then shows/plays the pics, movies, or mp3s on my tv and home theater speakers.

Bluetooth rules.
