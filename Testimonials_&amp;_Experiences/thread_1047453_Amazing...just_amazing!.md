### Post by Old *ix Geek on 2009-01-22
Last week I started upgrading all of my computers from Feisty to Intrepid.  (Yes, I know I skipped some versions!)  The desktops (HP and Compaq) were a piece of cake, each installation taking 20 minutes or less.  Everything--and I mean EVERYTHING--worked effortlessly.  For example, my HP Deskjet F4140 All-in-One printer, which I previously had to find and install drivers for, tweak, etc., just worked. :)

But I dreaded doing my laptop, an HP dv6000--with the infamous Broadcom 4311 issue!--so I saved it for last.

Yesterday, I braced myself and took the plunge.  I never was happy with the way I'd partitioned the drive before, so I re-did that and then began the install.

When I had tried the disc I'd burned last week, I realized that if I had a wired connection during the install it would be able to download/activate the drivers needed for the Broadcom card. So I plugged into my network and fired away.  PIECE OF CAKE. :D

Good grief, when I think about all the work it took 1-1/2 years ago when I bought this laptop and installed Feisty on it, and had to find drivers, read tutorials, do the ndiswrapper thing, etc., just to get wireless working...that was a nightmare compared to this!  (I actually didn't mind it, really, but wasn't looking forward to doing it again if I didn't have to.)

Everything has been painless.  Just now I installed Flashplayer in SeaMonkey effortlessly by clicking a couple of items.  Before, that involved finding/downloading the right files, putting them in the correct locations, etc.  Now? Click, click, done!
 
I just can't give enough kudos to the Ubuntu team for their great work.  Thinking back to Hoary, my first Ubuntu version, and comparing it to Intrepid, Ubuntu has come a LONG way in a short time.  GREAT WORK!  And truly appreciated by this longtime, old, CLI-using, *nix geek. :)

(PS  Now if I can only figure out how to mount network shares so they're usable from a command line. :confused: My saved fstab files [from the same computers before upgrading] aren't working, nor are my attempts at mounting manually from a command line.)  ETA: Got it! :D
