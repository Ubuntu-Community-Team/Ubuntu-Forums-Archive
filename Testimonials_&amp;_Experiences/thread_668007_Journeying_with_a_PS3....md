### Post by ninjaschwa on 2008-01-14
Hi, 

This is my first post (and visit) as a registered member, however I've used this place so much for help along the way with most of my problems; you guys rock with all you Linux wisdom, keep it up! I thought I might share my journey with my PS3 with you all and let you know of the hurdles I encounter... if you care to read!

Well having Windows all my life, I just dealt with it (the lag, the spyware, and every other annoying Window related thing you could think of...), and to be totally honest I'd never even heard of Linux ever until I was told you could use it on PS3 as an alternative OS, which intrigued me. I wanted a media centre and my laptop was wildly outdated so I thought I'd take the plunge...
At the time I didn't anticipate how much of a plunge it would be, of course I did my research first in basic things, like how to install the OS, which OS, and whether my programs essential for my uni course could be used with Linux too... everything checked out...

So I chose Ubuntu (yay!) Feisty Fawn 7.04, and I backed everything up then once I got the PS3 and myself a nice little 19" screen I installed Ubuntu...
Everything just seemed a bit... wrong... I knew it wouldn't be the same of Windows, I could deal with that, but I, like others encountered numerous resolution problems the PS3 seems to have, adjustments to the kernel and compiling from source, the wireless kernel ordeal... and eventually I gave up on Ubuntu...

So I then chose Yellow Dog, resolution setup was automatic so I could get straight onto customising my new toy! Sure, Yellow Dog has its many perks, wireless configuration without the messing with the kernel, the resolution ease, and the eye candy was quite nice! But after about 6 months of usage it just wasn't everything I hoped for... I was at Uni, I was using a wired connection now, I could only get .ogg to work, the distro refused to recognise my CD-drive other than for ripping pure music CDs, all my data back-ups were useless... and for some reason, no matter whether Noatun was on ''realtime'' setting or not, it would jam and skip when I opened even the most simplest of files (I feared this was the PS3 lack of RAM)... so when coming in drunk one night last week I decided to switch back to Ubuntu so I cracked out my Live CD...

And now here I am, happy as Larry himself...

And everything satisfies me perfect, I've discovered Synaptic (no more compiling from source for everything!), I used Synaptic to get all my math programs I need, all my music and video files work, data CDs can be read easily, VLC doesn't skip my music (a massive achievement in technology I'm sure you'll agree!), the resolution was easier to fix than I first thought! 

In short, I'm loving rediscovering Ubuntu and Linux as it should be... and every time I encounter a problem, the solution usually lies in a thread right here! In fact, moments ago my FireFox had some font trouble, the answer was right here as usual! So thanks guys =D> you're awesome!

Josh
