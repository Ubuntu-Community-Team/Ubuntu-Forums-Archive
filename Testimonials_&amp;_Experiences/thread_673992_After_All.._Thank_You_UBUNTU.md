### Post by Kspirit on 2008-01-21
Hello

Let me start by saying i got introduced to the whole Linux experience back in the summer of 2006. Before that all i knew about Linux is that you have to type a 6-line command to open a file ( Kind of like the Matrix thing).

I tried different kinds of Distros., mostly the ones that are easy to set up and have a large support community. I spent a whole year just downloading and installing different Distros. and also re-installing the system just 3 hours later for many stupid reasons, Example: once i had PC Linux 2007 and changed themes but i couldn't go back to the Original theme , so i re-installed the system!!!! I can't say a sticked with one distro for more than a month, i kept going back and forth between each. 

I can sum up my little experience with the Distros that stayed long enough by the following:

Ubuntu 6.10: Really good!! stable and did what i wanted 

PC-Linux 2007: Really good, but i didn't like the KDE environment and the software management

Sabayon (3.something): just to try out Beryl at the time.. didn't last a day!!!!!!

Fedora Core 7& 8: a good distro but i had some problems with certain hardware and didn't  like the Font rendering

Linux Mint Cassandra: Liked it very much, stayed with me a good three months, very stable and everything just worked ( chose it over Ubuntu 7.04)

Wolvix Hunter : i would have sticked with it but it was only a Live CD ( HD installation didn't work correctly as the Live CD did)

Now comes the good part 

Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon 7.10 , just installed it a month ago and all i can say is after a year of frustration, what i got in the end is worth it!!!

Every single detail of hardware works. 

Every single need of mine is fulfilled.

My PC is really, really, stable and everything i ever wanted is up and running perfectly! I am positive this time i will stick with this release a really good time.

I highly recommend Ubuntu in general for beginners or anyone looking for a stable system with excellent community support. I myself am just a beginner till now and find it really easy to get your system running Ubuntu with all the features and Software. The main key is good hardware support and a little sense of courage to try something new!!!!! 

In the end i would like to thank the Ubuntu people for a job well done
and would like to thank every single one in this forum who contributed with a Guide, solution, tip, and testimonial for others to benefit from.

Thank you again and sorry for the long post!!

PS. English is not my first language, so sorry for any grammar errors as i know how to speak English not structure sentences!!!!
