### Post by Vereinfachen on 2012-05-17
I use linux for 5 years now and ditched Windows completely one year ago so you could say I have a lot of experience with linux. When I first started out using Linux I quickly came to Ubuntu. Used it for half a year but it didn't feel right at that time. So I tried some more distributions and eventually found Arch Linux.

Arch Linux is my big love, I love everything about it. The rolling-release model, the package management, the KISS principle, the community forums. But then I looked back to Ubuntu a few months ago and Ubuntu changed a lot.

Now everything is tightly integrated, Unity is awesome and superior to Gnome 3 and everything works nice. This is the biggest pro about Ubuntu: You install it and it works good. Fedora looks ugly by default, on Arch Linux you need a lot of time to customize it to your needs.

That's why I will stick with Ubuntu now. I still like rolling release more but you can't have everything ;)

### Post by BigSilly on 2012-05-17
It's really won me back too. I've always thought the Unity desktop was very nice, but I've always ended up returning to Gnome Shell on other distros (I didn't care for the implementation on Ubuntu) as I just preferred it slightly over Unity. With 12.04 however, though I don't want to speak too soon, I think I may be back for good. You simply cannot beat the consideration for the every day user and the system polish that you don't get to quite the same degree with other distros. All my personal stuff that I like to use has installed and works with minimum fuss, and the desktop is fast and stable. It certainly uses less resources generally than say, my Windows 7 install, though obviously it's no longer the lightweight OS it was a couple of years ago.
