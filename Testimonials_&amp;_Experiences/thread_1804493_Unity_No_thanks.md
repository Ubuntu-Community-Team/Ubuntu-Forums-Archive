### Post by Juan Largo on 2011-07-16
I believe Unity is intended to attract new Linux users, although many seasoned Linux veterans may not like it.  Windows and Mac users tend to dislike Gnome initially, but Unity might make more sense to them because it looks similar to their smartphones and tablets.  

The fact that Unity isn't as configurable as other DEs may actually be a benefit to newbies because they could be overwhelmed by all the choices that they face with standard Linux DEs.

For people who prefer Gnome or KDE, it's simply a matter of knowing where to look.

BTW, I think the Ubuntu 11.04 installer is miles ahead of previous installers.  The developers have removed the "gotchas" that used plague installers of previous versions.  (I'm not going to get into those here, but I think many of you know what I mean.)
