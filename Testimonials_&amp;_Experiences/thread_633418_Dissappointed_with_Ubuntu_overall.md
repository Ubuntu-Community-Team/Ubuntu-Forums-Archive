### Post by don_m on 2007-12-06
System: IBM T23 Laptop (256mb RAM), 3com 3crpg175 wireless card

I first intalled 6.06 on this laptop a couple of years ago. My biggest dissappointment with Ubuntu is why the 3com wireless card was working, and then not working. I also had problems getting the modem to work. It worked in 6.10, but the wireless card would not. With 7.04 and 7.10, neither the modem or wireless card are functional.:confused:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=342632](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=342632)

So I don't understand why the wireless worked, and then after the 6.06 upgrade last year, stopped working. I recently tried ndiswrapper with my 7.10 upgrade, but it locks up the laptop. 7.10 will not make the built in modem operate either, but did a version or 2 ago. The ethernet connection has always worked with whatever version of Ubuntu.

On my desktop, at some point it lost my Brother MFC 9700 driver install and I have not been able to reinstall. Recenlty, with the 7.10 upgrade, I can no longer read my 2 fat32 partitions from Ubuntu, but reading the drives is okay on my laptop with windows partitions.
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=623060](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=623060)

I still find the installation of Flash and Java to take far more effort than it should. I've got this to work, but again this should be fairly automatic after 10 years of linux.

I do have some 3rd party card reader on my desktop, and Ubuntu is much better functionality here than in Win2k.

I"m not advocating that Linux from an interface perspective "be like windows". However, this stuff with the drivers needs to work out of the box when installing if Linux is overtake Windows. Yes, I know some companies are stingy about driver info and such, but I purposely bought an IBM T23 laptop so I could switch between win2k and a Linux distro and I knew that the IBM Thinkpads could mostly work with Linux.

I'll still use Ubuntu sometimes, but it is not ready as an OS for everyone.
