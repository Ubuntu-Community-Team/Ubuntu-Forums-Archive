### Post by poofyhairguy on 2005-10-18
I totally agree with this:

> 
Let's compare this situation to young children, children are known to learn different languages much more quickly than adults. To most adults, learning new languages is very hard unless the language is very similar to their native one. When you are learning to speak new languages, you will start from the very basics.

Now lets return back to the Windows <--> Linux scenario, these are two completely different operating systems, which with luck may have similar desktop environment that can ease the pain of transition. A new user should start from the basics and not jump into advanced stuff in the very beginning.

Most people who are trying to jump to linux from Windows, don't get this fact. They are trying to get EVERYTHING to work in the first few days, which will prove to be frustrating and difficult. Right, so what these people should do then? My suggestion would be to get two different hard drives and install linux or another alternative operating system to the second hard drive. You can then dual-boot between Windows and your alternative operating system of choice. While you are learning the new operating system, you can still get back to the one you have pretty much grown up with and where "everything just works".

Right on. I personally advocate Windows Power Users to just use the Live CD at first.
