Praising or complaining about  Ubuntu/Unity here, thinking that your somehow in direct contact with  Canonical or it's staff is unrealistic and false.

The only disparity, is that it "appears" acceptable to praise  Ubuntu/Unity here and keep a thread open. However, I am aware that for a  period of time (in the recent past) this section was inundated with  anti-Unity threads. Which to be honest - got old. See Elfy's response (#5) in this [thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2123544&")   - I think it pretty much defines the attitude  towards anti-Unity threads. The staff probably got tired of trying to keep  them in line with the COC, while minimizing confrontation in  the T&E. It's been my observation that for every member that posts a  negative or anti Ubuntu/Unity thread in the T&E, there's going to  be a defensive responses intended to argue or diminish the OP's point of view.

Personally,    I'm a big fan of making the T&E "read only" which might eliminate  the need for the staff to constantly monitor this section, beyond  watching for threads that violate the COC. This would permit members to  share their experiences (good or bad) without worrying about trolls or  confrontation. As the thread would basically be closed immediately after  posting and would drop rapidly as others posted. I see little value  here in arguing with anyone that has no desire to see Ubuntu,X,K,L/Linux  improve. I've never accomplished anything, by arguing with someone that  already has mindset.

 For those that come to the forums with the primary  intent of trolling or bashing the OS - I suggest they seek an  alternative site and that the staff take whatever steps necessary to  assure that. But, I also think that negative feedback can be just as  valuable as positive feedback, if collected and used specifically for  the purpose of improvement. As a moderated product forum, it's my  opinion that the staff "tends" to be extremely patient and endeavors to  assure the forums remain open to all. Except, those trolling or with an  anti-Ubuntu/Linux agenda.

But that's just my $0.02.
