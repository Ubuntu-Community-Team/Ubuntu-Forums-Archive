I have to admit that Ubuntu had no bigger problems with hardware till now. Every random printer worked :-P Although not every wifi worked. Had problems with the net at friends' house. (Win 7 worked then). What was unusual sometimes it worked without any problems. 

What makes me irritated is the software. Oh no, it's not again the lack of Photoshop or etc. For years already (and switched my mum too) I use Open Office, Mozilla's products or GIMP. No, I have problems with software not working too well.

First problem was with my Airline Tycoon Deluxe. I installed it but it crashed a lot (bye bye compiz) and I couldn't save games. Even tho I installed it as a user its ownership was root so I had to do sudo nautilus. Now, on 10.10 it was the same again. I have to check Compiz again. 

Regnum, installed twice and still has problems. If not lack of possibility to write the name of character then lack of game.cfg... >< Seriously... How about doing a game that works? 

Next irritating "feature" was with Rhytmbox and other programs using Musicbrainz. They refuse to read tracks of my Chinese cd. I couldn't even select tracks for ripping, not talking about listening to it. Had to install Banshee. Till that day I was a great Rhythmbox fan... Well, no longer. 

Another problem is reading Japanese in programs. Even tho Ubuntu reads well names of files in different languages regardless of the language in the given time (Windows Vista generally has problems with this and treats it like "no such file" and refuses to open it) I was rather displeased lately when various music/movie players had problems with meta data in Japanese. Arrrgh... 

Yet another problem is the lack of really good Japanese ime. As everybody on the net suggested, I instaled Anthy. But it's not enough for me. Although the shortcut is handy I need more advanced tool with emoticons and kanji writing pad, etc. Yup, I do use it often. Windows Ja IME is better here. Having to use online handwriting is bad enough.... Tried it wih Chinese. :-|

Installing from Internet needs to be made easier. Really. I hate it that everytime I get something from the net I have to find how do I install it.

Today, Ubu 10.10 was really picky about closing at once all the sessions of multiple logged users. Shutdown was one user at a time... ><

And... Last but not least... Community. Well, it's not about helpfulness but about floss. Open source is your friend, closed source is your enemy... Or sth like that. Personally, I don't give a damn about philosophy. I would prefer less philosophy, more action. I'm not a person who wants an open source to make my improvements in the app, nor do I care if the app is free (like in free beer) or commercial. Although I started to learn Python to do my dream app.

Generally, if I hadn't practice my patience with Windows, I wouldn't be so patient with Ubuntu. It still lacks a lot even in just inputting/reading text. And contrary to the usual beliefs my Vista isn't a bad system, althouh the UAC pisses me greatly.

Take a look at the Apache license, and also take a gander at the FreeBSD license and Mozilla license as good examples of other strategies.

In general, the GPL is toxic and intolerant of commercial software, but the gist of it is that if commercial software statically links to a GPL library, then that commercial product must be released under the terms of the GPL.  On the other hand, if you take an LGPL library, you can link to the interface and use either a commercial or non-commercial library which implements that interface.
