### Post by jamesatmaisonverre on 2012-12-05
Hi everybody,
 I wanted to do this post not for social aspect but for general necessity. I have played with linux as a rule over the years but predominantly used windows. Fortunately I have had the knowledge to rarely have to pay for the privaledge  of doing so. I expect more from my operating system than doing what it says on the tin as I get board easily and like a challenge. This is what compelled me to try linux. I tried over the years to make linux work for me as it scratched an itch of curiosity but always fell at the final hurdle. As like many users, I started with mandrake for its user friendliness, but could not get everything working. Two years ago I started on a large campaign to test as many linux distros as I could. I started with mandriva 2010. I managed to get this working perfectly, even customised it to my liking with sugar candy software. Unfortunately I had the Broadcom chipset for wireless and was dependant on ndiswrapper. Yes, this I got working on wep but as for wpa2 it sank like a stone. Security lead me to abandon it which I was disappointed with considering the near perfect state of what I had created.  
 I then turned to suse 10, I loved the sugar candy and wanted this to work, alas command ndiwrapper was to allude me on this one, so that ended on the scrap pile.
 I had looked at ubuntu but to be honest I just hated gnome, could not bare to look at it. Ubuntu was not even an option for me as well as mint. I tried another few distros including debian, red hat ect…. but did not have any sparkle. I remained deflated and abandoned the project.
 Then….. I started again a couple of weeks ago. I tried mandriva and was very disappointed with the interface, suse, was not happy with the stability under virtual and its printing capabilities. I downloaded and installed ubuntu 12.04 and my god!! Wow! What a transformation. I was so excited after 3 days testing on virtual I installed it on my pc at home as a dual boot and I was even more wowed. 90% of stuff worked out of the box. Unity, what can I say about unity, wow! What a great interface. Ubuntu is now in the right direction. After install I had problems with grub but was easily resolved with the boot cd. Then there was my printer, the notorious x4650, also now works. Everything works! I am really happy with ubuntu and all the free stuff including antivirus. What a great os. I would like to thank everybody on this forum for putting there input in getting my issues resolved with this os. What a gem.
 It was the recent release of windows 8 that prompted me to do all this again because I ran virtual rtm and what a hideous beast it is. I think I can safely predict the downfall of Microsoft from the mass market to the confinds of the commercial. I can now go forth in confidence.
 Thanks again
 James
