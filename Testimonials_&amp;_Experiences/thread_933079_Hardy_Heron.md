### Post by spawn. on 2008-09-29
After tonight, I will never return to another Windows OS (be it Vista Ultimate, Home Premium, or XP Home/Pro). I was hesitant to make the switch because I assumed there was a significant learning curve associated with Linux. In actuality, there is no curve. Ubuntu installed in about 13-15 minutes. It's pretty much point and click. The interface is *that* intuitive. The Synaptic Package Manager usability is a godsend to a new user. It makes the transition easier because most first-timers aren't accustomed to typing command lines. 

I already customized my ThinkPad to my liking and I'm blown away. The only regret I have is not making the switch sooner. Donating a few bucks to support the developers is a small price to pay for the stability, speed, and power you have with 8.04.

What's amazing is the community support  for this distro-. This forum alone has helped me resolve all of my questions about E-Mail configurations, IM clients, audio and video, wireless configuration, you name it.

Laptop specs:  T61p, 2.6 GHz, 2 GB Ram, FX 570M et al
