### Post by Yossarian on 2006-07-07
I thought I'd detail my experience with this operating system as a way of repaying, in part, the ubuntu community.

There's alot of guys like me out there, technical people who use windows but are curious about linux, who download a distro, install it, and uninstall it due to some unsurmountable technical problem.  For me it was always getting a piece of hardware working properly, video config, or installing new software.  That was me in 2002 with Mandrake 7 or 8.  I kept periodically trying different versions of debian, red hat, and mandrake/mandriva.

Eventually I tried breezy badger with gnome.  I could tell right away it was the best OS I'd ever seen.  Setting up software and multimedia support was very reasonable, once I found this board and the wiki.  I had one or two hardware problems, which I solved by searching through the forum.  I only tried posting for help once.  I didn't get an answer, and found the solution on my own.

Overall I'd say this forum and the wiki were the tipping point for me.  I couldn't overstate how useful I found them.  I now have ubuntu dapper on my main pc (although I now rarely use it, and probably use windows xp more) and xubuntu dapper on an old pentium 2 machine (which works great).

That's my bit there.  Hope someone poking around will read this, recognize themself in it, and decide to try ubuntu.  That's how I got started.

Captain John Yossarian.

### Post by Boomy on 2006-07-08
I've tried a bunch of distros throughout the years and they all had a "unfinished" feel to them. I would use them for a few days and the wife would complain that she can't do this or that and I would revert back to the Windows partition. Ubuntu is the first one were she didn't complain. She can open all of her Word documents in Open Office and they actually open properly and it doesn't crash. She can't play her Yahoo card game, but she likes the Ubuntu games even better anyway. I must say everything it pretty easy in Ubuntu. I wanted to rip a CD, so I popped it in and wouldn't you know Soundjuicer pops up automatically, I set the directory and press a button. Works perfectly! My digital camera and removable drive all mount automatically without problems. Importing digital photos is easier with Ubuntu that Windows. thumbsup!
