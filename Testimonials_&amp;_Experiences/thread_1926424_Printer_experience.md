### Post by armandh on 2012-02-16
10.10 scores a win over mint

for those of us who have to generate reports for meetings
or flyers for local politics, printers over cloud still prevail

my well used Data Graphics DDS-32 started getting 
electronically flakey in both the parallel and T10 connection

Still for a 32 page a minute printer it had its limitations.
the last factory supplied driver was Win 2K beta which worked in xp.

with Ubuntu a generic post script or Hitachi, or Talley driver worked.

in win-7 an IBM infoprint 32 PS driver worked.

the same innards were used for several brands.

and along the way I picked up two IBM infoprint 40 printers
the dds-32 has become a parts donner


SETTING UP THE IBM PRINTERS IN UBUNTU WAS EASY
Type the IP address into search
probe the printer
use the Hitachi driver

in mint I could not make it happen
