### Post by snowpine on 2012-01-21
ps To give a historical footnote the reason why the default install is the way it is (rather than minimal install being standard):

In ancient times, Linux took hours to install. The user was asked dozens of questions and needed to be intermediate or better to even get a basic system running.

Then Ubuntu came along, it made sane assumptions about what the typical user needs, streamlined the installation process to about 15 minutes, and thereby became the #1 most popular beginner-friendly distro, ready to go "out of the box."

FYI there is no actual benefit to removing the default applications other than saving a little hard drive space and maybe making your menus appear less cluttered, if that is important to you.
