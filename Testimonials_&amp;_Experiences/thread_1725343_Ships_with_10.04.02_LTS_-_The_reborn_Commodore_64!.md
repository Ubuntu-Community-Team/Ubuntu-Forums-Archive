### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-04-09
I'm having some serious (digital) flash backs here...

For those old enough to remember, the Commodore 64 and Amiga lines of computers were a major milestone in affordable personal computers.

Commodore USA is been reborn: **[http://www.commodoreusa.net]("http://www.commodoreusa.net/CUSA_Home.aspx")**

They are offering "it's all in the keyboard" computers again. The tradition Commodore OS is installed, and Ubuntu 10.04.02 LTS is shipped on CD with each system.  They can also run Windows (as the site says: "If you *have* to"). They use current technology, and are a nice mix of the ahead-of-its-time design from Commodore with modern processors and GPUs (and VGA/DVI/HDMI outputs).

The Amiga line is still under development; but the larger Amiga 2000 case has room for 6 hard drives and an optical drive.  They say it will support the current "Video Toaster", too.

It nice to see a computer system that includes Ubuntu as the preferred 'current' OS.

***The Hedge***

p.s. Who ever thought I might be buying a Commodore Computer again?!?!  The Amiga 2000 would pick up where my old Amiga 1500 left off.....
