### Post by minivan on 2011-02-16
Forgive my impertinence, I am not a tech savvy person - I am a middle aged mom with 3 teenagers. My experience with Ubuntu has been great. First of all, like most families with bunches of kids, we had a crappy laptop laying around that seemed to have picked up some sort of virus. Frustrated by the whole Windows thing and the lack of tech support for a 3 yr old laptop (plus the $ it would cost to fix or replace), I googled free OS and stumbled upon Ubuntu. I have been using it for about 6 months. I love it. I think as a selling point one could point out most families just use a computer that your kids can write their papers on and use the web. None of my kids do more then that - we're not computer gamers (they have whole host of other gaming systems for that) . Since I found Ubuntu I have converted at least 4 of our friends computers over as well. They all had the same problem as our family. 

We have had no crashes, we've recycled a laptop that was either going to be trashed or worse, and the best part is that I haven't spent a dime. I've found your forums to be great resources. We have 4 computers in our house, 1 Ubuntu, 1 Mac, 1 Windows 7 and Windows XP (work computer - can't really change anything on it). The 2 that get the most work is the Ubuntu and the Mac. 

I can't thank the Ubuntu people enough.
