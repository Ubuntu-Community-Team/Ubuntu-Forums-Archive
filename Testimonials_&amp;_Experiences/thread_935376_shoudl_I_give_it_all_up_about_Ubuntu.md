Isaque,

First of all, since your native language is Portuguese, your English is very good. Congratulations. Secondly, I think you have answered your own question. You sound determined to make Ubuntu work for you, but right now Compiz-Fusion and Emerald are causing you some problems. Congratulations on not giving up.

I don't know your hardware configuration, but perhaps I can offer a few suggestions that you could try before giving up on 8.04.

If you don't have it, install the "Compiz-Fusion Icon" using the Synaptic Package Manager. The actual file name is "fusion-icon." After the installation, go to your top panel, click on Applications, mouse down to System Tools, then click on the Compiz Fusion Icon. This will place the Compiz Fusion Icon just after your name on the top panel.

Right click on the Compiz Fusion Icon and mouse down to "Select Window Decorator." You should see Emerald and whatever other window decorator you were using before selecting Emerald. For instance, if you see "GTK Window Decorator" click on it. This should take you out of Emerald, and restore the title bars to your windows. 

At this point I would suggest going back to the top panel, clicking on System, mouse down to Preferences, and click on Appearance. Click on the Visual Effects tab, and then click on none. This gets you back to a starting point were you can find out just how much in the way of visual effects your system can tolerate. You can experiment from here.

Also, on the Compiz Fusion Icon, you can right click and look at the different options that are available.

You are correct that installing Emerald just gives you a theme manager. One place to get Emerald themes is [www.gnome-look.org](www.gnome-look.org). After the web page loads, click on "Beryl" in the artwork section. When you click on any theme you should see the word "download." When you click on download it will download the theme file to your desktop. Then open up the Emerald program.  Click on the "Import" button. Emerald will see the file on your desktop. Select it, and then click open. Emerald will install the theme into it's list of themes. Then if you should get back to the point of using Emerald again you will have the title bar for that theme.

I hope you find this helpful. 

Darrell
