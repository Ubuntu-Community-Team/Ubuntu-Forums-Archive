### Post by Thomar on 2007-08-28
I've had Ubuntu for about 5 months now, but I've never really had a "special moment" like this until now.

Today, I was reconfiguring my hotkeys after seeing touchkeys 2 fail to get my extra buttons and fn- keys to work, and I suddenly realized I could bind Shift+Alt+Left to Previous Track, Shift+Alt+Right to Next Track, Shift+Alt+Up to Play/Pause, and Shift+Alt+Down to Eject.  I mean, wow!  My laptop's little spring-loaded CD tray pops right out with a few keystrokes!  Isn't that cute!?  (okay, I've seen cuter things, and a laptop CD drive ranks pretty low on the cuteness scale, but the fact that I don't have to reach around to do it is very satisfying)  If some brigand tries to steal my laptop while I'm using it, I might be able to mildly bruise his knuckles with the Eject command!

Anyways, one reason I love Ubuntu is that Rhythmbox can use the exact same folder that iTunes uses on my Windows partition, and music is a big part of my experience on Ubuntu (I consider its lack of decent games to be a benefit, it keeps me focused and I can still use emulators with a little effort).  Having these hotkeys work in any application at any time is marvellous (as I was right-clicking on the system tray icon before), so this is a great time-saver for me.

Yeah.  Just had to get that out.
