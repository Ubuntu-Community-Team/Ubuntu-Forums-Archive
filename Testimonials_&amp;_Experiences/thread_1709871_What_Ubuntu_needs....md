### Post by usagiakumu on 2011-03-18
I am going to voice my opinion, it is educated but an opinion

 

 I installed Ubuntu on an unused machine. It has become very apparent that you need an active Internet connection to get any joy out of using it, at least until you get some software, drivers, and codecs installed. In fact it looks like it would be impossible to use should you not have Internet. This could be an Achilles heal.
 

 Everything just works and attention to details seem to be lacking more in some applications, especially games. The whole &#8220;it's free so why complain&#8221; notion kicks in here. I have not paid for a single thing. Discoverability seems to be the theme here, I especially like Software Center.
 

 On a surprising note, there are more applications then I originally expected. The argument of it not running applications is kind of a moot one because they exist, just not what I am used to. Most applications &#8220;just work&#8221; without the hassle of cd-keys and activation.  
 

 Video editing tools and DVD tools are easy to come by. Codecs installed without hassle without forcing me to go elsewhere and look for them.
 

 Everything seems streamlined and in a word better than Windows, and Mac when it comes to delivering you the things you need. Little details seem to be missing in some applications and it becomes very easy to forget that it is all free.  
 

 I had to go to the terminal a few times to do a few things to get some things to work such as playing DVDs. It was as simple as googling it and copying and pasting into the terminal, I minimized it and went on with my day until it was done.  
 

 I made the mistake trying the IRC #ubuntu channel for help only to find it not very helpful at all and after a few tries it became very apparent that most of the people didn't know a thing and googled my queries, which I could have done, and in fact did. I found out that if I wanted to go any further I was on my own to seek out and see what I could personally do about it.
 

 With my intellect I was quickly able to resolve all problems and issues. Googling it worked a lot of the time. Most of my issues, a lot of people wont have though. I had a problem displaying proper resolution that I was sure supported. Windows could see it. I had to buy a video card since there was no on-board solution.
 

 Another issue came about where I absolutely needed Sun Java JRE and Open JDK just was not cutting it but it is somehow untrusted. I don't believe Sun, a multibillion dollar industry, would be trying to poison my system with a standard that has been around since the dawn of the .com boom. I keep running into political garbage which is really turning me off to the whole experience as a whole. Nothing illegal or out of the ordinary is trying to be done, and yet because of some political garbage, my desktop has to suffer. I have to go out of my way to install what should be standard in a way that should not need me to invoke command prompt. Earlier versions it was a single click and it was installed. Sure I &#8220;could&#8221; use another distribution, but the mentality should be that you want as many users to use Ubuntu as possible. Not everyone cares if something is open source if it can't get the job done they need to accomplish. This really got my goat. Ubuntu isn't Windows, but come 11.04 I won't be so sure its Linux either. This shouldn't be some hyper emotional roller coaster, it is software. Really good software at that, but good software can quickly die when someone loses their heads over personal beliefs, especially when your targeted group, desktop users, needs to use standards that EXIST on Linux but are ignored. That is a quick way to make enemies and therefore shoot yourselves in the foot and not only never gain developers but lose some major players too.
 

 Don't be Microsoft. Microsoft puts their beliefs before the users, please be different and cater to users, include drivers and go out of your way to make certain things legal like playing DVDs and BluRay. You have come this far, you are nearly to your goal. Heck you are even going as far as to make your own desktop brand, Unity. That is bold and sexy and will win new users with a fresh perspective, possibly turn some developer heads to your favor, I would even go so far as to say Photoshop on Ubuntu, this will in turn give it to those who use other distributions who need that one killer application they haven't been able to get which keeps them away. Go for the gold baby! Keep the vision alive. Listen you may not contribute to the already incredible kernel which never breaks, works perfectly, and needs no fixing, you are contributing to other developments which are long overdue. I give you major Kudos for that! From the bottom of my heart, thank you.
