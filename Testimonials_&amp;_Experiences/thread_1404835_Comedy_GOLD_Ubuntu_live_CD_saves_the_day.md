### Post by fisheater on 2010-02-11
Hi,

This reads like a movie plot.

My colleague has been working on a power point presentation for ~ 3 months now. He was to present this AM at our AGM, he was the main event. 

Last night at 12:30 AM he calls my house and says, 'I'm in trouble'. His WinXP laptop crashed and he was unable to reboot it. He tried a dos boot disk, but had no luck. He called a 24hr mobile IT service 250$ to come to his house, $150/hr with no guarantee of repair. Then he called me and was on my doorstep in 30 mins.

I put in 9.04 liveCD, it booted. I was able to see his HD and copy his files to his USB drive. He finished his revisions in the instance from the liveCD and presented this AM as if nothing had happened.

+1 for linux.

Thanks.

FE

### Post by Methuselah on 2010-02-14
I had a similar experience in the past as well.
Friend's computer wouldn't boot and I couldn't even get to run chkdsk.
He had no recovery console installed.

So I used an Ubuntu LiveCD and ran ntfsprogs from the live CD.
Then windows was able to boot so I could eventually let it run its own chkdsk.
Finally, the computer was usable.

So I guess it's a case of Ubuntu saving windows' ***.
