### Post by MoxDex on 2006-06-17
My father and I both collect what is known as geek garbage by family and friends. We have a few old computers laying around. One of which was mine, a compaq Presario that I fried, numerous times. Eventually I broke down and shelled out for a dell with XP. XP was easy for me not because I knew windows all that well but because it was bright and shiny. I mean, I watched a few of my friends use it before. But a year later, I remembered all the good times I had when I used Linux on some of the older computers, it's where I learned to play Tetris after all.

Well, A buddy of mine recently converted to Ubuntu and my Dad had talked a little about it. I wasn't ready to give up the nice gaming zone I had for windows. So, I figured sometime in March that I would push my luck at the Presario. I installed 5.04 on it. It was nice, I got a nice theme to match my angsty little mood, something I couldn't do with XP. I was starting to fall in love with the distro, and it had tetris on it. Well, I never really bothered to update anything. My friend who I got the 5,04 disk from informed me of 6.06 coming, so I figured I would give it a try. And, since I installed it (only hitches being a few idiocies on my behalf dealing with the ISO burn) I've learned the full power of Ubuntu. I have grown to love Synaptic, IMO so much easier than scouring the web for .exe's with windows.

And, the upgrade to 6.06 came around the time of my Dad's new computer purchase, so instead of a slowly dying Presario, I got another testing ground, an HP Pavillion with a few little kicks that were top-notch back in 2000 when we got it. IMO Ubuntu is a great OS, this opinion formed not only because of countless re-installs of windows and even more innumerable crashes, or my burning hatred of MicroSoft. Nay, this opinion is because Ubuntu is a nice OS for someone feeling adventerous. I currently use my computer with XP only for storing files and listening to some of my music.

Thus ends my humble, little story of my use of Ubuntu and a little of my biography.
