### Post by wmn on 2008-04-18
Ubuntu Forum and especially to "logos34"
Finale.. after a full year and about $200(on social security) of trying to install and use a Linux product. I think it may be time to call it quits, I have used Firefox for ages, won't use anything else, and silly me assumed and my intuition told me, Linux must be better. 
 Throughout my trials I have been called a quitter. stupid, any number of things by Linux users. I can guarantee you none of those things relate to me. I'm 72 years old, have been with every upgrade of Win from 95 through XP. Built computers from scratch and repaired many for others.

One vendor (ebay) sent a LinuxCD that would not do anything, then bad mouthed me for complaining, another, (Gearhart & Yeager Company), would not send the product or a refund. A third, (On-disk.com) was good enough to send the product(Ubuntu) and offer to replace if not satisfactory.

I am hoping for instructions on returning my computer with Ubuntu installed(still no modem connect), to it's original Win XP so that it becomes usable again.
 From what I have seen of Ubuntu, it is intriguing but simplicity it is not. 

I would suggest that the designer? of Ubuntu or any form of Linux, make sure it is compatible with some brand and model of modem and also PnP, tell the user what modem to purchase and where to purchase.

These products(Linux) truly need a disclaimer for those wanting to try for the first time, especially the modem problem, but not excluding the major change from Windows. For me, it has been and so far still is, unsurmountable. Old people are not comfortable with change you understand.

At 72, I question that I'll live long enough to have it function and on-line. I do appreciate all your(logos34) help and hope you (logos34) can see the return of XP for me. 

Bill Noyes
