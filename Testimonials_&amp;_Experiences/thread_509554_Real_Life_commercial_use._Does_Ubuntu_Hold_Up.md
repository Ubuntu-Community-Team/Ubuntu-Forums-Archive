### Post by theorganloft on 2007-07-25
In previous post, I pointed out that I use Ubuntu and Ubuntu Studio (US) in my commercial endeavors where my installations include Mepis 6.x and Ubuntu Studio.  My commercial customers see it as the top of the line server software that will not fail them. They run their systems hard and for long periods. They are so glad that they do not have to license a MAC or Windoze. My machines use AMD processors.

I did run into configuration problems with Ubuntu Studio (US) where the problems were actually minor.  I use 2 sound cards. One for playback and one for record.  I had to configure US to boot with the on-board sound card as default.  This was easy to fix and that part of the system works great!  I like to use Audacity for live recording therefore I had to configure Audactiy to output Mp3 files and to play and record to the proper sound card.  There again. It works flawlessly with the US Alsa configuration.  Most of my systems use the SB-Live for live recording. My personal studio uses a M-Audio 7.1 Delta.  All work Great! The other US software gives my customers options that they otherwise could not afford. For example, the Rosegarder sequencer and Hydrogen.

The Mepis computers (ubuntu deriative)  are  basic office production workstation/systems that work without any non-sense.  Updates are easy. No symantec antivirus to get in the way.  I do not worry about spyware and my customers and kids are well protected from myspace and other junk by the firewalls.  When my kids grow up, they can move out and join MySpace without restrictions from Dad.

Every day I discover new features and uses for the softwares.  My current computer upgrade will move my windoze XP machine to the background by installing US on a brand new AMD based music production center with video.  My windows machne has my Dreamweaver package on it that cost me allot of money. Other than that I have switch to Ubuntu. :guitar:
