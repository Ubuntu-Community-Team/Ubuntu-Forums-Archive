### Post by kosmoskid on 2008-11-13

He's not what you would consider a "linux type," but loves the fact that he doesn't need norton, and that he's got all the software he needs at his fingertips through the package manager.

We're also lucky at my best buy to have people who are interested in, and excited about linux in general and will easily encourage unsatisfied customers to make the switch.

### Post by ITAndrew on 2008-11-13
From experience of working at the Geek Squad for six months, they are more than happy to install any operating system for you, for a price. Also, I am very sure if you run into problems most will not know how to fix them. As far as I know they only support Windows (maybe OS X now but I doubt it). Just dont run into installing an OS for a customer that you or your co-workers do not know how to support, or you me be in for some fireings or seriously angry customers.

Oh and also, how are you supposed to attach all sorts of software goodies to your new PC sale if you don't have any software to sell them & no new PC setup package. I'm sure Best Buy would be unhappy about that fact as well.

Don't take cash from a money hungry corporation or you may end up losing a hand.
