### Post by studiesinsound on 2008-04-23
I just wanted to chime in with my own testimonial.

Granted I am somewhat of a noob, but I struggled a bit with a few things in Gutsy
Not so with Hardy RC.

the grits:
Hardware: Dell Vostro 1000, 2.0 Ghz AMD 64 x2 processor, 2 gigs ram.

my ultimate purpose with Ubuntu Studio is to have a rock solid guitar looping station.
To that end I am running jack, and sooperlooper.

Install of the OS from the Alternate DVD: smooth sailing.
The system looks slick, seems to run faster too, maybe that's just in my head though.


What has impressed me the most is how smooth configuring jack to use my m-audio fast track pro sound card was and how easy it was to get everything to talk to each other, and run sooperlooper.
I also disabled my onboard sound card by blacklisting it.

Under Gutsy all of those tasks were lots of trial and error, and even after months of messing with it I still had the following audio issues:
1: xruns, and lots of em.
2: weird audio crackle even with no xruns
3: periodically, and not at even intervals, the audio would "glitch" can't think of another word for it, and the audio coming out of my soundcard would be horribly scratchy and distorted. I began to suspect bad hardware.

Under Hardy, the first 2 issues were resolved.  runs like a truck!
oddly the third issue remained.  Last night I finally narrowed this down.  I was running jack, sooperlooper and timemachine (the sound recorder app).  I wanted to use timemachin as a lightweight app to capture all my audio output.
However, sometimes when I hit record in timemachine, the aforementioned glitch happens.  the only way to get audio back to normal is to close all apps and relaunch them. 
So I took timemachine out of the mix, run the laptop for 2 plus hours, with jack and soooperlooper running, putting everything through it's paces, no glitches.

so I guess time machine doesn't play well sometimes.

in any case Hardy on a dell vostro 1000 with an external m-audio fast track pro, running jack and sooperlooper = win!

rock on!
