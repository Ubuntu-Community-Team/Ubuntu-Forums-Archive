### Post by SomethingXP on 2010-03-30
I have used Ubuntu quietly since 6.04 Dapper Drake and stayed current with every release upgrading without hassle, switching motherboards without hassle. At first I was sad that you went with Gnome and not KDE until KDE 4. I got used to Gnome and all it's simple beauty and cannot live without Ubuntu now. I have had issues but none I couldn't resolve on my own with Google or reading something here in the forums. I seriously just installed Ubuntu as a joke because I hated Gnome and really disliked other Linux distros outside of Mandriva. Well life got busy and I never had time to sit and reinstall Windows XP. We had a baby and we are having our second child. The Internet came and went but Ubuntu was there with F-Spot handling the pictures and movies of our daughter. 
     I never thought about Ubuntu when using it other than I turned on my computer and there it was doing what I needed it to the most. Windows Viruses came and went, I never got hit by a single one. I love how I never had to de-fragment the hard drives, not once. Well life started to cool down, I backed up all the pictures and all my parental junk, and I tried to go and use Windows XP and KDE. I forgot I needed an anti-virus, I forgot to de-fragment, I forgot I needed anti-spyware, and I forgot how to use it. It lasted a whole week and before I could get my bearings and re-learn how things worked and it died. I went to try Kubuntu and man KDE just kept crashing out on me after about a half hour's use.  Of course I went and pulled out my 9.10 disk and re-installed it. It was just as snappy as it had been before. Not once did Ubuntu ever slow down during all the heavy use and abuse. 
     I just wanted to let all the developers know what an awesome job you are doing and it is highly appreciated. I look forward to the beautiful purpleness of 10.04.

-In Christ
Christopher
