Ubuntu has not taken away choices. In 10.04 you had the choice of Gnome 2, KDE, XFCE etc. In 11.10 you have the choice of Unity, Gnome 3 shell, Gnome Fallback Session, KDE, XFCE etc.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-18
> **EdWh said:**
> I will look at the the alternate install of Gnome-fallback-Session but do not see under Ubuntu home page where to get info about it.  Can you direct me??

I'll just point out, I'm not a moderator :-)  The person who posted above me is.

Go to the terminal and type:

```
sudo apt-get install gnome-session-fallback
```

(or otherwise install gnome-session-fallback from the repositories, your chosen way).

Log out. Before logging in again, click on the cog icon next to your name on the login screen. Choose Gnome Fallback. Continue logging in and you'll be on Gnome Fallback Session. Note that the panels can be customised, you just have to Alt-Right-Click them (or something like that).

> I get very upset when someone attempts to force me to use something I do not approve of.

Unity is just the default, the officially-supported desktop. The other desktops have been available since the start of Ubuntu and will continue to be maintained by community as part of Universe. Ubuntu isn't trying to force Unity onto you, only suggesting it as it's the offical desktop of Ubuntu.

> You people have done a good job with Ubuntu and I would hate to leave it and I have brought people into it but finding documentation on usage if very lacking.

You raise a good point. Documentation on open-source is traditionally very weak, and probably more so for a young project such as Unity.
