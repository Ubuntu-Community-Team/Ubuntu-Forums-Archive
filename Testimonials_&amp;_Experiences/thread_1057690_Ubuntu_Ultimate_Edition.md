### Post by Montblanc_Kupo on 2009-02-02
Well... from the link you posted... that's just compiz. I have all that installed on My default Ubuntu install. The thing that would keep Me away from "ultimate" was somewhere in the back of My head I remember reading that they didn't update very often... so a lot of the stuff installed was old, including the core components. Not a big deal... but it does mean a lot of updating. *shrug*

I installed a base Ubuntu 8.10 and have gone about installing compiz with emerald for themes, tons of iconsets, window themes, startup images, made My own backgrounds... etc. 

Personal preference I guess. I chose Ubuntu simply because it was the "base" for everything else. I stuck with it because I've been really happy with everything so far... the more you stray from the "source", so to speak, the more likely you are to have problems.

So... if you're new to linux... I'm thinking stick to just Ubuntu... if you're familiar already... give it a shot if you want.

If you would like to just play with the 3D effects without installing anything, Sabayon Linux (based off gentoo I believe) comes with a LiveCD with compiz already installed... you can just pop the cd in and test drive compiz.

[http://www.sabayonlinux.org/](http://www.sabayonlinux.org/)

So far though, I've tried several distros and have really liked Ubuntu. It's a very forgiving distro to get acquainted with linux.
