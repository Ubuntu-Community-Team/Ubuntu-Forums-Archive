### Post by MetalMusicAddict on 2006-10-11
Thank you Mark Shuttleworth. Thank you Canonical. Thank you MOTU. Thank you Ubuntu-geek. Thank you every tinkerer who likes to put some usefull little app out there and gets it into Ubuntu. Thank you.

In 99' [SIZE="1"](gotta check on that)[/SIZE] I first saw FreeBSD running xwindows. It blew me away that you could install this on a PC and you didnt need windows. It opened my eyes.

Sadly, Linux/BSD was not ready for my desktop then. I played with every distro I could get my hands on. I had HW problems usually and GNU/Linux just didnt click for me. I was still stuck in a windows frame of mind. With any success I got I still tried to make it look like windows. Icons, wallpaper, layout and such.

I had given up on Linux for about a year [SIZE="1"](I got sucked into the whole "Make your XP look like OSX scene)[/SIZE] when I heard about this new distro called "Ubuntu".

I went to the site and the release was called "Warty Warthog". I thought "Thats weird". I started downloading the .iso and looking at screenshots. "Ick! Look at all that brown." I thought. At the time I was very much still in that "I want something that looks like OSX" thinking. Still, I knew I could switch things around and I would be fine. I also looked around for a place for info. I found Ubuntu Forums and got over the brown. :)

I got the .iso and installed it. [SIZE="1"](Im not gonna bore you with the particulars)[/SIZE] I booted it up and everything worked but my scanner. (a problem to this day) This was a first for me. Usually some crucial piece of HW failed.

I get into it and have ALL the n00b questions. "Can I play my mp3s?" "Can I play my videos?" "Can I connect to my servers?" ALL these questions had answers on the forums without me having to post 1 question. I actually lurked for a good bit before I joined. Searched and read. Most things were spelled out very well.

Within no time I had a working multi-OS network via SAMBA. Had mp3s working and most of my viedo played. There was a Ubuntu guide page someone created. It was a HUGE collection of how-tos that worked to a "T". [SIZE="1"](someone help me with the name :))[/SIZE]

Now here we are almost 2 years later. I learn so much everyday due in LARGE part to these forums and the collection of people it has. It is a true community. All it took was some reading. :)

Ive learned enough and got together with the right people to even start work on a Ubuntu offshoot. With this new development I have spent the past 3 weeks sitting on IRC. Watching conversations. Chatting with people and watching how it all works.

You know what it has taught me? That Ubuntu users have **NO** clue how close to Ubuntu they *really* are. If you want to steer this OS, YOU, *really* can. Im watching it happen. Many of the people that do it are on this forum. Hi MOTU guys! :)

Since this revelation I have become increasingly angry at users who say [ignorant]("http://www.dictionary.net/ignorant") things about Ubuntu. It strikes as a slap in the face to the HUGE ammount of people who give up their free time [SIZE="1"](often at the expense of other things)[/SIZE] to really make this the best GNU/Linux there is. Not just because of its functionality but *because* of its people. Please, new users, think of this first before you post negative things. You want something fixed you have a problem with? Get involved! You dont need to program to do it either. Submitting bug reports is a big help. Talking about them here wont always get it fixed. It needs to be in a proper place where someone who handles those things can see it. [HERE]("https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+filebug")

The ride Im currently on is wild. Ive gone from user to project lead on a new version of Ubuntu in 3 weeks. All because I had the interest, willingness to learn and people to help. This would NEVER be possible in a corporate OS or without all the help I get from the awesome people on IRC. Thank you.

Ubuntu really is a community [SIZE="1"](built by its people)[/SIZE] OS. I just wish more people knew.

Thanx again to everyone who positively contributes to Ubuntu. Im done. :)
