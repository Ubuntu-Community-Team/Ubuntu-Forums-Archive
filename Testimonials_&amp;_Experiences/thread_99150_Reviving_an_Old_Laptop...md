### Post by Ffats on 2005-12-05
I own an old Compaq Presario 17XL260 that had Windows 95 on it back in the day and was a horrible pain to use.  Always sluggish, completely buggy and prone to display random error messages on just about every start-up.  I looked into upgrading it a little bit as a small web/e-mail/word workhorse and decided to install Windows XP on it, just to see how it'd go on bare-bones.

I installed XP (after about 3 hours!  SHEESH!) and logged in.  The scroll buttons didn't work, audio was inoperative and random drivers were either missing or fubar.  Not a good experience at all - I would have imagined that Windows XP would have been a little more compatible, but it "worked".  Not well, but it worked.

That laptop got set aside a couple years ago in favor of DIY desktop and a very recent purchase of an excellent Powerbook.  Well, I opened up the closet TODAY and decided I'd check out what this Ubuntu hype was all about after one-too-many diggs all about it.  I burned an ISO and popped it in.  The install was the easiest I've ever experienced - I don't consider myself a neophyte, but not much of a linux poweruser at all.  I installed SuSE when I was 15 but definitely didn't know what I could do with it.  Ubuntu's installer explained EVERYTHING so well, the stress I usually associate with clicking the "Continue" buttons when installing a fresh OS was totally gone.  The option to use the installer menu was so cool - if I made a small mistake it actually TOLD me, and offered me a list of places I could go.  SO COOL!

After a fairly quick install, I logged in for the first time.  Man, a beautiful logo on a free linux distro?  Cool, but I wasn't expecting much else.  I log in.  My audio works with a beautiful start up sound that I wish was on my Mac (hello live CD...), I open up Firefox and I can scroll, my wireless card worked without me even googling the manufacturer's driver site.  What the heck?!  Something's got to go wrong.  Nope.  Nothin'.

If Ubuntu runs this well on a 450-some mhz laptop, I can't wait to build a cheap 1ghz and see how that gets torn through!  Sweetness, and thanks for making a once dinosaur machine a surprising force to be reckoned with.

As kooky and as cheesy as this may sound, Ubuntu really has gave me a sense that people are ultimately good - a huge billion (if not trillion-dollar) corporation failed to beat out a group of people who have a little thing called PASSION and BRAINS.  Money can't buy everything!
