### Post by tslawinski on 2008-11-21
I'm impressed with Ubuntu. Over the years I've experimented with different linux distro's but never fully moved away from Windows. With Ubuntu it was different. It set up like a charm. Virtually everything worked perfectly on initial set-up (except for my modem). For me, the benefits of Ubuntu (stability, performance, ease of use, software base, updates) far outweighs the use of that one device. I recognise that driver issues in a windows centric world will likely be an issue for a while yet. I now make it a point to look at linux hardware compatibility before purchasing new hardware. Just wanted to say thanks to: Mark Shuttleworth (and his vision), all the developers and support staff, the OpenSource community, and individuals who provide technical support to us who have switched over. I love the freedom...

Thanks

Ted Slawinski

### Post by johnkzin on 2008-11-21
I'm of a similar opinion.  I started out using BSD unix in the '80's, then NextStep in the 90's.  As a result, I've always been rather spoiled about the quality of Unix GUI's -- if it wasn't as good as NextStep (and, later, Mac OS X), then it wasn't worth using.

That has been my opinion of FreeBSD, NetBSD, OpenBSD, and all Linux based GUIs.  They simply lacked the proper integration, attention to detail, etc.  Even attempts to "look like a Mac" or "look like Windows" were typically pathetic, because they were overlooking the fact that the look isn't the important part, the important part is the feel, the things that are underneathe the look.

A year ago, I got a Nokia N810 and started using it, and was pleasantly surprised about how well they did with fully integrating the GUI.  It was amazing.  When I mentioned that to some one, saying "if only there was a desktop Linux that was this polished", they suggested I try Ubuntu.

I finally got around to that earlier this month.  There's still some rough edges, still some places where I feel like I have to look under the hood, or hack together some things to fill in some gaps, but it's orders of magnitude better than the state of Linux GUI's from just 5ish years ago.  And it's almost to the point where I feel like I could start (_start_) migrating off of Mac OS X.

And that's what I plan to start doing... setting up an Ubuntu VM guest at work, and one at home, and start seeing how far I can go in shifting over to Ubuntu entirely.
