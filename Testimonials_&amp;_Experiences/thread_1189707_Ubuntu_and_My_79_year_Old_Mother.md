### Post by undecidable on 2009-06-17
One year ago I installed Ubuntu 8.04 on my 79 year old mother's pc.  My brothers, cautious men, advised against it.  

**Upsides**

1.  My maintenance of her pc is massively reduced.  I used to unplug and take her machine to my apartment for maintenance (checking for rootkits, checking for filesystem issues, wrestling with the a/v,...) once a month.  Now I just run a hdd checking utility (spinrite) while I am there for lunch.

2.  The annual renewal pain for s/w that expires (eg a/v) has been eliminated.

3.  Her life is much easier.  I can download and use lots of software to see what works for her without worrying about licenses.  eg she used to have one player - itunes - that did everything and confused her somewhat, plus because it updated podcasts automatically she would go over her bandwidth limits.  Now she has separate players for podcasts, music and audiobooks (gpodder, banshee, rhythmbox), each has an icon on her top panel and she can select which activity she wants.  

4.  I worry less.  She does her banking on that machine and knowing it does not run Windows is comforting.

5.  It is an old machine (2003) and now runs much faster.  This makes her more patient, so she is even nicer to me.  

6.  The amount of damage that can be done by various derelict siblings and demented nephews, who mess with it when they are there, is limited.  I have not had to kill anyone for 12 months.  Have now planted cabbages over the spot where I used to bury them.  


**Downsides**

1.  My brother bought her a Lexmark printer which doesn't work under Linux.  He bought it because it was very cheap, not realizing that it was very cheap because the ink cost is ruinous.  I unplugged it with prejudice and bought a low end HP laser printer which does work with Linux.  

2.  I use Kubuntu myself, and always have to scratch my head when configuring Ubuntu.  It is like being in a playpen with padded walls, when the problem you have is snakes. 


**Transition and Training Issues Windows to Linux**

1.  I had moved her to Firefox, Thunderbird and Open Office over the previous year,
so in fact the bulk of the work was done prior to installing Ubuntu.
In fact none of these was a particular effort.

2.  When I installed Ubuntu, I moved across her data, set up her key apps in the panel, took the box to her place, connected it,  and said a quiet prayer.   It would be like taking news to Josef Stalin - you hope it turns out to be good news.  

3.  She gave me 15 minutes.  I did what I could (booting, nautilus, the apps I had put on the panel, where her data was).  Her patience, never her strong point, then ran out and so we had a cup of tea and I left.  I left the key points typed on one and a half pages beside her machine.  She never read it.  

3.  Turned out though that the only issues she had in the first month were:

a.  I had installed using a different screen.  So rarely when she booted the screen came up blank.  I told her to do c-a-bksp, which fixed it.  The problem went away after about 2 weeks.  I never solved it.

b.  The HP 1005 printer is a firmware loading printer that would toss an error that would lock up the queue after printing the job.  Searches suggested it was caused by the 'trial toner' that came with the machine.  I told her just to delete the job in the queue window - easy as it was on the panel.  However she did have difficulty remembering this.  

c.  My dark-side sister tried to logon without knowing the password.  After 20 tries she rang me to say the machine was broken.  I pointed out to her that brute-force, while it worked well on her husband, worked unreliably on the computer.  

We had far, far more problems when I first gave my mother Wz XP a few years before.  Within a month she was using Ubuntu exclusively (I had set it up as dual boot) and has been booting to Ubuntu exclusively since.
