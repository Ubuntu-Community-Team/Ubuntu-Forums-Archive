### Post by ddhwc on 2008-04-18
Hi guys!

Five days ago I installed Ubuntu, my first use ever of a non-windows OS. I'm a professional programmer so I'm pretty good at computers overall and learning new stuff.

I use a HP Pavilion dv9540. The installation of ubuntu (gutsy gibbon) was no problem at all. Once it was installed it took a couple of hours to get the wifi, gfx card and headphone jack working. Of course all the new commands and stuff is a little overwhelming at first, but a little googling explains most of it. I noticed most problems can be solved by searching "problem + ubuntu" or something like that.

I actually like all the command line stuff. And I'm still learning all the commands of linux. I installed compizconfig yesterday. Waaay cool stuff! Not sure how useful it really is though :).

I recently bought a Wacom Bamboo Fun and after 20 minutes or so I got it working with gimp using a few guides.

I haven't tried any games yet. I mostly use my 360 for gaming. I'll probably try some games in WINE soon though.

I did try to install KDE, but once it started my screen faded and it went completely black. Even after a reboot, and entering BIOS/Vista it went black. I took out the battery and started again. I tried starting KDE a couple of times, and reconfiguring xserver, but no luck. So now I'm back to using gnome.

Overall it's a very nice experience so far. I have no intention of switching back to Vista (I can still dual-boot to Vista though if I need to use any applications not working in Ubuntu).

I know many people (my friends are among them) think linux is very difficult to use. I guess it was more difficult before, but now, with Ubuntu, it's no problem. It's a shame though that most of the difficult/non-working stuff (the drivers!) is not actually Linux's fault but the developers not supporting Linux. So thanks to all people getting most hardware working anyway.
