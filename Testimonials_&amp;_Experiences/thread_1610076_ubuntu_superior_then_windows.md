### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-10-31
> userm3
I have fully Migrate everything to Ubuntu.  Our home (5 systems) has been Ubuntu/Linux only for >4 years and I  can say without hesitation it was a brilliant move. It's been an  extremely pleasant 4 year computing experience.

With just a little creativity and investment in the learning curve, one can enjoy the OS and appreciate it's unique qualities.

The only recommendation I'd make is to always (always) test new releases  prior to commitment. I use a spare USB HDD to test all our systems,  assuring compatibility and functionality prior to installing on the  primary HDD's. A simple yet effect way of assuring upgrades are not only compatible, but beneficial.

A simple testing procedure is used prior to the upgrade decision and all our systems are upgraded to the new release upon successful testing. Have no desire to have different  versions running on different systems. Either the upgrade passes  testing on all systems or they all remain with the previous version (at  present 10.04). Stability and reliability are critical in our home and  following a basic set of guidelines keeps all our systems fully  operational without constant vigilance.

I hope your Ubuntu experience emulates our own and wish you the best.  I'm also certain there will be those individuals that (for what ever  reason) feel the need to respond endeavoring to point out potential Ubuntu/Linux  weaknesses. My suggestion is to simply ignore them. As I'm a firm believer  in making once own choice, instead of relying on the opinions of others.

Good Luck and thank you for sharing your experience(s).
