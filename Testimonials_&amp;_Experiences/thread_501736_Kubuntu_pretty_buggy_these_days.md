### Post by homeslice on 2007-07-15
I've been using various flavors of linux for about four years now (currently Kubuntu feisty on my laptop and desktop), in part because of it's supposed rock-solid stability. Although there are many things about linux that I love, I can't help but wonder: Is linux getting increasingly buggy? By linux I mean not the kernel, but a good share of the apps I commonly use. Here are just a few examples: Konqueror crashes at least once a day on me (most often when attempting to load some web page, but every day it's a different page), Xorg often spontaneously begins to utilize one CPU core at 100% until I force it to restart (I don't have any proprietary video card drivers installed), OpenOffice crashes pretty frequently, and, when closing it after a happy no-crash session, it 'believes' it has crashed, restarts, and attempts to 'recover' whatever document I was working on, I always have to start Firefox twice before it will successfully open....
 I could go on. I realize that open source software sometimes means putting up with a few bugs, but these are some of the most commonly used apps. Is my machine cursed? Do other folks have similar experiences? Are there simple things one can do to avoid these common, frustrating, errors?  If this stuff is typical, then is the linux reputation for system stability getting more undeserved these days?
  

Dell 6400 T2300, Kubuntu Feisty, KDE 3.5.7
