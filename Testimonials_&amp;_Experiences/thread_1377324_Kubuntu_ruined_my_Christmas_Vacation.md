### Post by Kver on 2010-01-10
During my holidays, it's routine for me to be the computer repair guy for the family whenever I visit home. This year, I happened to have a Kubuntu CD on me, as I love the system for daily use. Without bringing a laptop, I was pretty much dependent on whatever computer I was either fixing or cleaning to check my junk online.

My parents are technically literate; They know roughly what marks to hit and mostly how to get there; Defragmenting on occasion, burning CDs. They also miss now and again; Deleting bookmarks because firefox is slow, instead of clearing the cache or disabling toolbars, for example.

My mom was, as per usual, asking me to speed up her computer; It was an older XP machine 1.6ghz single core, 768mb ram (odd number I know); it did have 2 hard-drives I had set up the year before, one dedicated for my stepfathers pictures, the other for software. When I would drag a window, it would literally grind. Opening a program also meant making a tea. Firefox had more toolbars than I knew it could have - and I knew they weren't put there voluntarily.

So I made the offer to give her computer a "split personality". Windows if they really need it, Kubuntu for regular use.

In about half an hour, everything was working but sound. PulseAudio failed to install, but it was quickly fixed. I ran a custom brew of scripts and tools I made for quickly bringing a machine up to my personal standard. 3D acceleration was respectable.

I transferred Windows outlook accounts to Kubuntu Thunderbird, bookmarks, etc. I toured my parents through Kubuntu, and it seemed good.

Here's where my vacation went downhill.

The next morning I wake up expecting to answer a few questions - it's a new environment, there's a learning curve; But I'm eager to read online comics and drink tea. My mother is doing her banking online, disappointed, but I still have my tea.

Next time I try to see when I can get online, my stepdad is using Digikam. He's pouring though hundreds of pictures doing things I didn't know you could do. Correcting horizon lines, contrast, colour and focus.

Finally, I get to the computer; The desktop wallpaper, from a classy picture of a nebula, to a slideshow of animals. I never taught them this! I wanted to give them class, but they chose animals over me! At least, I have my tea.

Several attempts to use the computer for personal gain are thwarted, internet, email, printing! I forgot to install the printer, but they got it working without even asking me! I'm becoming obsolete. Lonely. Kpatience got more love than me.

My stepdad moved an RSS widget I set up to the panel because mom didn't want his headlines. I just told him it was a widget, I never mentioned locking, unlocking, anything else! I didn't even know you could move existing widgets to the panel. An analog clock was added, and weather too. The desktop felt so alien.

Then they started changing the themes; I've always loved air, but I guess they like oxygen more. No style, pah!

By the end of the vacation, their computer wasn't even mine anymore. I barely used it. I only managed to tweak the settings for a little more speed, but otherwise, I had no fun at all. The only satisfaction I got was the odd question.

Thanks alot, Kubuntu. The computer works great, but my vacation was ruined. I believe KDE 4.5 should be made less-friendly again, so I can needed again.
