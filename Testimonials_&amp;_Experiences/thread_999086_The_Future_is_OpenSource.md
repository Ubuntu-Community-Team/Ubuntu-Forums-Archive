### Post by tslawinski on 2008-12-01
I believe some of the issues/problems described here will become moot points down the road as Ubuntu matures. These are growing pains resulting from a worldwide trend towards open-source solutions. Just as Microsoft underestimated the impact the Internet would have on computing, I believe they've grossly underestimated the impact OpenSource will have on the future of information technology. The writing is already on the wall. The FLOSS (Free/Libre/Open Source Software) initiative is already well under-way in Europe. HP, Novell, OpenLogic, and Google are just a few of the growing number of  corporations jumping on-board. OpenSource is where the future will play out.

### Post by Glucklich on 2009-11-02
Well... the problem is that Open Source, only in very few cases, is self-sustainable (forgetting projects made as hobbies or sustained by other interests). And maybe you've already noticed that sustainability, in most cases, is obtained by corporations that have or created something significant aside. When the momentum you talked about ends, if the business (and yes, it is still a business underneath) is not sustainable, things as we know will end. And there's a very practical reason for that... developers are human! Let's face it, you need money so you can trade it for goods such as food, for example. Simply put: No money, no development.
I'm not saying the project will die, because anyone can grab it and continue. But taking things out of a professional sphere will definitely slow down considerably the rhythm of its development. Unless, any corporation sees potential or functionality in it and decides to support it, obviously.

So, I guess what I'm trying to say is that self-sustainability is one of the biggest problems that Open Sources faces.
