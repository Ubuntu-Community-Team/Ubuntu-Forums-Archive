### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-02-14
A buddy of mine at work has a rather large blended family.  The sheer number of kids means some waiting in line to use the Windows PCs.  This wait time has increased just lately as the internet &#8216;backbone&#8217; in his neighborhood is undergoing replacement, and network speeds are substantially reduced until construction is complete.

This weekend the youngest daughter of the family had a gift card with an unknown amount of money still on it.  The line at the Windows PCs was so long that she said (sadly) &#8216;I will check the card tomorrow&#8217;.

This same weekend, her step-dad (my coworker) had just loaded the oldest PC with Ubuntu 10.10. He directed her to that PC to use the internet.  At first, she was not so sure about the new OS.  But after clicking on Firefox, it felt familiar.

While they waited for the slow internet to load, he asked her to go &#8216;to&#8217; and &#8216;from&#8217; full screen in Firefox (the system has an old NVidia card that is doing compiz Wobbly Windows).  

The young lady giggled with delight at the Jello-like behavior of the windows.

Then he had her try the &#8216;peek&#8217; behavior, stretching the window and pulling the edge to see the screen underneath it. This too was a big hit.

He said that even 30 minutes later, when he looked in to check on her, she was still giggling.

Ubuntu has yet another fan.  She likes &#8216;cute&#8217;, and was very happy to find that on a computer.
