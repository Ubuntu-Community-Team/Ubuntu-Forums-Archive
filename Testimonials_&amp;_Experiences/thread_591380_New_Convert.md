### Post by chimay on 2007-10-25
I had been toying with various Linux Live CD's and had previously used Fedora Core 3 several years ago. Last week I decided to bit the bullet and format my laptop and install a linux distribution and use it as my primary computer, keeping my Desktop (with XP) as a fall back in case I get stuck with trying to do something. This time it came down to Mandriva or Ubuntu. I decided on Ubuntu simply because you do not charge money for any of your distributions (Mandriva while it seemed like a good deal, isn't anything I couldn't learn to do/get myself). 

After finding out I had a bad hard drive sector and getting it replaced under warranty, I was off with Installing Ubuntu. Install of course was easy. Adding software was not. For some reason none of the software package type options (the multiverse or whatever) boxes were checked under the add/remove software app, so it was always trying to reload the list even though I could see the apps, just not check them to be installed. After feeling my way through that and getting the xorg ati driver whatevers, taken care of, it was easy sailing. I had my extensions installed in Firefox, Thunder Bird getting my e-mail and Ubtuntu automatically finding and installing the codecs needed to watch the new popular flash video format. ANd compfiz working great. (I still need to figure out how to get the backgroup behind the cube working... gotta find some more how to's, but the rotation works great and the wobbly and water effects). Short cuts and commands in the gui were easy to pick up (practicallly the same as windows). 

I know that was a bit long winded, but the point is I am loving Ubuntu so far. My next big project will be figuring out how to get my windows games running (can't give up a few of them). But that doesn't look too hard from what I have read.

All in all, great job!
