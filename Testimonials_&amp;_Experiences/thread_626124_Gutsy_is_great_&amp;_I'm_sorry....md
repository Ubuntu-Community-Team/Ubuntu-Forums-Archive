### Post by RebounD11 on 2007-11-28
...for what I said about it. After failing to install the 64 bit version of Gutsy (awful first encounter) and after having update problems with openSuSE 10.3 and KDE problems with Fedora 8 I decided to give Gutsy another chance... the 32 bit version this time. After a flawless and fast installation, a quick upgrade, enabling restricted drivers, and installing everything I wanted/needed in less than half-an-hour, I found the first ever system to recognise all my hardware(from scanner and wireless to mouse and monitor) and have perfectly working desktop effects out of the box. 

To bad I couldn't install the 64 bit version but I'll do it once I get the DVD version, and I'll see in a couple of weeks just how stable it is, if it doesn't crash at least once in 2 weeks it's as stable as openSuSE 10.3, and if it lasts a month (a month with me is an eternity for a distro :D) I declare it the most stable ever... and further more if I get the same from the 64 bit version (provided I can install from DVD at least) I will donate to the Ubuntu project from my very limited budget a month's scolarship (that's about 60-65 euros or 80-85 USD). 

PS: If anybody knows what might be wrong with the 64 bit installer I welcome answers, for starters it isn't a bad burn for I have tried at least 6 burns at less than 8x speed (CD 8x - and that's very slow) without any other apps running and on 3 different computers/burners. The error is sth like device 0 cannot access PCI location 0:0000:0000 or sth like that... it happens when I try to boot from the CD (before installing), right after the loading screen finishes the left-right movement of the yellow short-bar on the black long-bar, that's when the screen goes black and a cursor keeps blinking at the top left corner. I have to press Alt+F1 to see what it's doing and there it gives that error I told U about, and it says Loading... and keeps loading even after 2 hours (I came home from class and it was still "loading"). 

Any way the bottom line is that Gutsy is a great distro after all, at least the 32 bit version. It also has great artwork... and respects my personal colour code for distros (Fedora - blue, SuSE - green, Ubuntu - brown, Sabayon - red etc :D)
