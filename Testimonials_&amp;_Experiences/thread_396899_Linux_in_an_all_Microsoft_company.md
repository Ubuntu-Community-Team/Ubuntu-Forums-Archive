### Post by Cirdan7 on 2007-03-30
Well, my dad's company who does all things Microsoft had to move to a smaller building because of prices(why do I find that slightly ironic?) Their computers had to stay at the old building while they move, but my dad has to work at the new building(don't ask me why) so instead of installing Windows on a spare computer to use for Skype, AIM, VPN, and web browsing he decided to install Ubuntu. I had Dapper on my laptop, and Edgy on my desktop. He wanted to see if he could VPN to his computer and server at work.

So we hopped on my Edgy desktop and tried to setup VPN. That failed because supposedly VPN is broken in Edgy. So we tried it with Dapper and it worked fine. But, just to try it out, we installed Feisty Herd 5 on his spare computer and did all the updates. Setting up VPN on Feisty was VERY simple compared to Dapper and Edgy. So he was quite happy. So after that I installed Beryl on his computer which only has a built in Intel video card, and beryl works wonderfully(the only spare monitor we have is only 1024x768).

So till he gets his main desktop to the new building he is using Ubuntu to VPN to his computer and the servers, along with running skype, gaim, and firefox. He was very surprised at how quickly changing themes was. You click on a theme and it changes instantly, even in Beryl. So I showed him around gnome-look.org and he picked out a cool theme and had his computer tricked out. And this is a guy who has used Windows for as long as I can remember.

He defiantly won't switch over permanently because "having to use the command line is annoying." And this is coming from a guy who sets up networks, and sells computers to people. Among other reasons, Windows only programs, knowing how to use it, etc.

Anyway. A slightly annoying and off topic point is, Ubuntu has yet to work flawlessly on my desktop. It keeps randomly freezing, hardware isn't bad because Windows works fine, video card works fine(you can search my threads to find where I aks for help.) So Linux works fine for my pro-Microsoft dad, but zilch for me. So either I get a new PC, or buy a Mac. Yay. :frown:
