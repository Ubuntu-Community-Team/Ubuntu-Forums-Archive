### Post by psismondi on 2010-08-29
I have finally had enough of linux after all these years. I just removed Ubuntu 10.01 from my machine (dual boot with Windows 7). Good riddance.

I have used linux off and on since 1996. I started with Caldera, moved to Redhat, and the Fedora.  I struggled constantly to find device drivers over the years. Back in 1996 it was difficult to find an ethernet card that would work with linux so that I could use my broadband Internet connection. Also, I do a lot of music composition using high end audio devices. Virtually none of these are supported under linux. I have had various tablets over the years for use with Photoshop. Also often not supported, or badly supported.

Yeah, I know: it's those nasty hardware vendors who are to blame. I JUST DON"T CARE.

For the past three years I have mostly been using a Mac. OS X is a dream for the kind of thing that I do.

Recently I decided to try Ubuntu again about a week ago, setting it up to dual boot on my Windows 7 machine.

Here are the problems I had:

1. parted could not recognize the partition table. The partition table was created by Vista (prior to my upgrade to Windows 7), so I have no idea what the problem was. I backed up Windows and redid the partition table. Naturally, I had to reinstall Windows.


2. My wireless adapter did not work under Ubuntu. The wireless card uses a ralink chip. Apparently ralink wireless chips  have been a problem for linux for some time. I have neither the time nor the inclination to build a driver as supplied by ralink or whatever the vendor's name is. There is no wired ethernet on the second floor of my house, so that is not an option.

3. Once Ubuntu was installed it would not recognize my DVD drive. I have no idea why, and don't care.

4. An hour or so ago I booted up Ubuntu with the intention of giving it one last try. For reasons unknown Ubuntu failed to see my Logitech wireless kb and my Microsoft wireless mouse. These were working the last time I booted Ubuntu. I have no idea what happened in between; nothing changed as far as I know.

That was the final straw for me. The last vestiges of my patience, my interest in open source, and my willingness to waste lifespan farting around with an operating system has been extinguished.

I have a GREAT *nix-like OS in the form of OS X. I don't care that it is proprietary, Moreover, I am finally reasonably happy with MS. Windows 7 works pretty well compared to their earlier junk.

Get off of people's desktops, linux, and stay in the server world where you belong.

So long, thanks for all the fish, and all the *@$#* grief, linux. 

- Phil -
