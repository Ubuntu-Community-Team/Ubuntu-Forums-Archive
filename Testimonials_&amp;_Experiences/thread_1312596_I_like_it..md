### Post by Incubus9 on 2009-11-03
I had 2 major problems with previous releases.

1) I could never connect to my school's Wi-Fi network in 9.04, after hours upon hours of tweaking. In 8.10 I had it working, but it took forever.

-- in 9.10, it worked on my first try. I'm using it now.


2) Some external hardware was a bitch to set up.

-- in 9.10, I plug in my PodXT (usb sound card / guitar amp modulator), expecting to spend hours searching for a driver that works, but Ubuntu RECOGNIZED it exactly for what it was. I thought that was pretty incredible. This tells me that there is a big selection of pre-installed drivers.


Overall, I am very impressed by the completeness of this OS. The only thing that really bothered me was having a hardwired internet connection in order to get a wi-fi driver.  How about throwing in a big folder of wi-fi drivers in the next one? I'm going to read some of the posts where people are complaining about the OS, because I have no complaints. I'm dual-booting aside Windows 7 and I must admit, I choose this hands-down. Being an engineering student, I need to have Windows because of certain programs (circuit simulator and pcb design). I do not doubt that I could use similar programs on Ubuntu, but I need to use specific ones. This is too bad, because it's the only reason I keep Windows. I may give Wine or other such programs another try.
