### Post by rubboferraro on 2008-01-23
*“just wondering, have you tried any other distro's than ubuntu? If you havent you might soon get the disease known as "speed distro swapping" *

Yes, I've looked at various incarnations over the last ten years; Live CD's as well as dual booting. I've got about 7 different distros on cd's (all a bit old – except for Ubuntu). Usually I would play around with them for an hour, get frustrated having to type command lines constantly, then go back to Windows. So you can imagine how amazed I am seeing ubuntu. As wormser says, “My "speed distro swapping" disease was cured by Ubuntu.”

I'M IN ABSOLUTE SHOCK!!! To tell you the truth. 

I can't begin to describe how pleasant the experience has been – there's just so many awesome, intuitive things about the OS. Just now, for example, I needed to send my paperwork to the office, the secretary likes it in PDF format. After I fresh installed Ubuntu I naturally needed my Canon scanner to work and a PDF converter. I was naturally very nervous about the whole thing (a Windows hangover). Anyway I went on the net and found out that all I need to do is add XSane. So I found XSane in the package manager, clicked on it and installed it in about 5 seconds. I then opened Writer and scanned my documents. Then “Wholly Crap!” what's that: “convert to PDF” ao I converted it to PDF and sent it to the office. Wow!

Now let me tell you what I would do with Windows after a fresh install. 

Firslty I would have to install the scanner drivers from the cd, wading through option after option of whether I would like to install bloatware or not and where I would like to put it all. This would take several minutes. Then I would install MS Office, entering serial keys, personal information, nags to register etc. and waiting a long time more. After that I would need to install a free PDF converter (which were hard to find until fairly recently). Eventually I could mail my work. What a nightmare.

I want to sleep with the Ubuntu dev team.

And, continuing with installing other distros – sure I'll check out Live CD's out of curiosity but I aint in no rush to take this little baby off. Besides, I think it's the Ubuntu philosophy that really pulls my heart strings. I'm all for those in developing countries getting the best that I as a Western pig take for granted. The great irony is that we now have something completely free that far exceeds anything all our multimillion dollar capitalist companies are capable of. Ubuntu is the Wikipedia of operating systems.

Thanks again all you spunky geeks.

- Lorenzo.
