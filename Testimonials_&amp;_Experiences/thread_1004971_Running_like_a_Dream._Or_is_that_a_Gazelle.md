### Post by xarte on 2008-12-07
So yesterday was a bit frustrating. I couldn't get the wireless card to work and spent hours trying to edit stuff in terminal and messing about.

So then I did a clean install, enabled the ATI and Broadcomm drivers in Hardware Drivers, installed WICD wireless driver with Synaptics Package Manager (following instructions on Ubuntu community page) (rebooted, inserted information, rebooted. worked.) 

Turned off graphic effects in 'Appearance' to remove the flicker on Tux Racer.


Ran the updates. Installed Skype. Installed Flash player - OMG - you just double click the packages and they self install just like on windoze ...

Browser works great.

So far... brilliant. I've installed Wine and will install World of Warcraft though I'm not sure that this  poorly-supported graphics card is going to cut it - if not, that's okay. All the rest of my net browsing, chatting and writing I can do right here in a lovely clean Ubuntu install.

No endless popups telling me my identity manager is ready for use and my wireless is on. (duh, there's a blue light....). No security or update programs suddenly activating when I'm in the middle of something else.

My advice to new users is to SEE WHAT UBUNTU DOES FIRST before you go trying to edit and compile and all that good linux geeky stuff - it seems to me that Ubuntu is a very noob-friendly interface.  

I think the wireless connection may even be faster than it was on windows...

Happy camper! ):P
