### Post by NFblaze on 2010-02-02
Alrighty, then It's been about a year-ish since I sincerely started using Ubuntu. This is my summation in that time, quite long. The summation is that overall Ubuntu has been a positive experience.

The person who introduced me to Ubuntu or at least prodded me to try it was a friend of mine while I was overseas. We both were apart of an IT department. I will admit (at least here), he was more literate around computers than I (though not by too much) and that created a personal rivalry.

 Somewhere down the line he mentioned that he used Ubuntu. I became a bit curious Ubuntu isn't that some sort of Linux? I had had very limited experience with Linux before hand, really, a very short single time use with a version of OphtCrack, a short 2-day IT training course using Unix, and an even shorter one using Solaris. but I was aware of Ubuntu Linux, as I saw it mentioned quite alot in various blogs and comment sections of sites.

I decided that day to google it. I was shocked to see that it was a fully featured desktop Linux (so much so that I discounted my friend and said that's not *REAL* Linux, it has too much of a friendly desktop). He stated to me that that indeed was Linux, and that he doesnt even use Windows at home (Dont know the veracity as he ran Windows on his laptop with no Ubuntu)

Eventually, I perused the net and learned more about it's mission, it's goals, and beliefs. I eagerly purchased a CD from the Amazon in order to discover more. 

After sometime I received it, it was 8.04 Hardy Heron. I had before hand printed off a rather large tutorial on how to install it. I followed the guide step-by-step and completed it successfully on my laptop which at that time was relatively new.

I eagerly rebooted, now being that my laptop was relatively new. I was greeted by the terrible surprise that my screen resolution was wrong and I didnt know how to change it,codecs issues, work, Xorg kept eating my CPU, and Wi-fi didnt work. Terrible. I still managed to skim it around a bit. I noticed that it was zippier than Windows, but I was completely a fish out of water when it came to navigating it.

I came to Ubuntu forums, and ask questions on how to fix it. I received no replies, I assumed it was because my hardware was too new, so I chalked it up that I was assed out. I wouldnt use it. I did take note that being that it was September a new version would get released in less than 30 days. I could wait.

8.10 Intrepid Ibex arrives. I manage to stay awake enough to go to the internet cafe and set it to download overnight. It finally completes! I must say I was reading the reviews about 8.10 and they were favorable, so I was anxious to install it. The installation process was rather deja vu, it completes. I restart and instantly amazed by the improved loading bar. I'm thinking this will get good. 

I login I notice that my WiFi works! Yay! The screen resolution had automatically fixed itself. I was truly amazed at it. The new look, the speed, the functioning of the essentials. I had issues with Totem and Brasero. Those were minor.

Although, the system is now second nature to me now. I still didnt understand which programs where which or how in general the Linux system worked. Luckily, the internet was there.I utilized the internet to downloaded guidance books from various sites and some torrents.

I think I must've dedicated 3 months that it took me to overcome the main obstacles in the learning curve. After that, I was comfortable enough that the Ubuntu suddenly started eroding into Windows usage monopoly it had over me. I even used it to repair computers and bring back some life into old ones.

I used it more and more I realized, that I was running out of the small space I had dedicated to Ubuntu. So for the past year, when Im using my computer Ubuntu is 9 out of 10 times the OS I boot into (that is when I have to reboot...). It managed to gain more hard drive space on my limited hard drive. I eventually bought a bigger SATA hard drive for it, though haven't installed yet.

More importantly than that Ubuntu has taught me, and continues to teach me more about computing and information systems in general than I would've have ever learned had I stayed solely a Windows user. I understand that the power of the terminal. It broadened my horizons about the opensource software movement and the merits of collaboration. 

Anyway that's my long story about my introduction to Ubuntu and Linux in general. It's been great fun, hopefully one day I will have explored even more Linux distros. I want to say to those who are eager to try Linux and it's not working. Don't give up! Remember Linux isn't Windows, things are done differently. There is so much you can learn. Have a little patience and who knows what you can do! Utilize the strength of the Linux community and the internet to improve your experience.

[SIZE="2"]*In The Future: I will continue to use it more. I will eventually branch out into different distros. I plan to make a media server (like boxee or xmbc) instead of use cable if I move to MA.*[/SIZE]
