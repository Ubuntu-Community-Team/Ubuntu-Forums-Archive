### Post by keithg on 2006-05-01
Hello,

About two years ago, I learned that there was an alternative to Windows.  I immediately dug up as much information about it as I could find.  I became very interested, but alas I did not have a second hard drive to install it on, and I had no way of burning to cds.  Well time went by, and I eventually purchased a second hard drive, and a burner.  At the time, I was running Win2k Pro, and the burner didn't even come with the drivers.  I tried to get the burner to work, but eventually had to give up on that.

	One day I was in Borders, and I came across a magazine with a Mandrake CD that came with it.  Well, I jumped at my chance to get a install CD of Linux, and I installed it on my second hard drive I mentioned earlier.  I was then delighted at the chance to test out this mysterious Operating System.  I was delighted that I was able to burn CDs out of the box, which would come in handy for later distros.

	Well, I migrated to Ubuntu after that, and I couldn't be happier.  Over the course of having this computer, I have had trouble with Windows too many times to count.  Eventually some time after having installed Ubuntu, Windows crapped out.  Well I haven't installed it again, and I plan on reformatting that hard drive, and installing some other distros on it for testing.

	I really must thank you the community that keeps Linux going for what you do, and I wish that I had the technical knowledge to help out.  I am very happy with Linux, and I have yet to have any problems with it, and I have had a lot of fun learning along the way!

Thank you very much,
Keith   :KS
