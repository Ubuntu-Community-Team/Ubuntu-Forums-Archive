### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2008-05-06
Hello.

I have spend considerable time these last few days reading these forums. Particular threads with topics like "Hardy sucks" and "Hardy is the worst ever" etc.... which makes me sad.

Granted, i'm the new kid on the block, err.... newbie Hardy user (newbie LINUX user to be precise) but as of yet, i can only say, and i'll be honest - i LOVE Hardy.

No problems installing it. No problems getting the graphics to work (TV-out issue - have a workaround, not a real solution but it WORKS) and no problems getting Wine to work, Samba configured (phew!) and a static IP configured. My bluetooth works out of the box, i can send files wirelessly (!) thru the WiFI bit of my router to my boyfriend's  Windows-laptop (!!!) and my torrents download AND UPLOAD way faster than they did under Windows. Oh, and i got the thing personalized to my liking, AWN works like charm, it doesn't crash or hang and the only remaining quirks are a non-working IrDA thingy and some network-manager related textual error messages when shutting down, probably because some services that network manager needs are closed BEFORE network manager itself. 

So i really don't understand why people speak with such hateful words about Hardy. yeah, there are bugs. but, folks, there are bugs in EVERY SINGLE PIECE of software that has ever been released. And this is an operating system, the most major piece of software that can be installed on any given machine.

Hardy is a lot like Vista. Seriously. And not in a bad way at all. Vista was a MAJOR overhaul to the Windows platform, as is Hardy to the Ubuntu platform, many features new from ground up. Bugs are to be EXPECTED, and yes, they were there in Vista, aplenty. Now, a year in, Vista is pretty good. I never had a problem with Vista in the 15 months i used it, apart from bad/none video codecs the first couple of months and software incompatibilities, by now all largely ironed out, either by updates to the OS or updates to those applications, "Vista compatible". 

And that is commercial stuff, mind you, the developers actually make money with that stuff and it's in their wallet's interest to have it compatible.

Does anyone make serious money developing open source software? Not really, me thinks. Still, they do it. And i am 100% convinced, a year down the road, Hardy is as stable as can be, even for those who now face problems with bugs (of which many seem to be focussed on laptops and, specifically, wireless networking). 

What i think funny is how people complain about Windows "features" yet happily do use same in Ubuntu. Oh my god, i haven't heard of anyone NOT complaining about UAC in Vista (asks for passwords or confirmations all the time) but in Ubuntu, i need to enter my password to update or install stuff, too. People also whine about the need to install updates to Windows all the time - well, Ubuntu does it just the same. So in truth - the two are very comparable, except for two things - Vista did cost me two months of my low Thai salary while Ubuntu cost me a few hours download time and one blank CD. And Vista takes almost two hours to install a basic system, while Ubuntu was up and running with all the nice things included (!) in less than half an hour. I am talking fresh install to a virgin, unformatted HDD here. 

Yeah, some things could be improved vastly - 3D to name one particular, we still need proprietary drivers in Ubuntu to get even basic effects working.... in any Windows (well XP and Vista at least) i can run the stock driver and play games or run Google Earth. Heck, Vista even gave me Aero, which requires quite some "oomph", out of the box - on a somewhat low-endish graphics card, that is. Ubuntu wouldn't do anything without the proprietary driver, a version of which i got from the manufacturer's site and that badly hosed the system.... yet a version off the repos works just fine. That's not Ubuntu's fault, mind you, if those guys at Nvidia can't be arsed to code proper drivers or release their sources to people who DO code proper drivers for free. 

I'm now using Ubuntu Hardy as my sole OS on two computers already and i couldn't be more happy with it. I was happy with Vista, too, i have nothing to bash or rant - no crashes on that one either. Switched to Linux for the sake of learning Linux, gaining knowledge and experience. My support questions here for a few issues were mostly ignored or simply overlooked (there are just too many coming in a short time i guess) but just googling things lead me to these very sites again and again and i was able to find solutions and apply them myself, and i am really no geek in any way, shape or form, yet - i got it going.

Now i will keep my system updated but the very day these error messages upon shutdown disappear because some update has fixed that, i will disable updating because - never change a winning team and - if it ain't broke, don't fix it. I DO tend to keep my OS way longer than 6 months and if i am really happy, even way longer than 3 years.

So, Ubuntu - convince me to get a newer version, otherwise i'll be with Hardy till kingdom comes :)

Edit: Maybe i'm just plain lucky with this one - many people are complaining about the FF3 beta. Well, for ME this is the fastest browser i ever used, everything "just works" including YouTube, Flash etc. As i download quite a lot of stuff from the web i had to get one extra add-on tough - the one that restores the "Clean Up" button in FF's download manager. I wonder why that isn't there out of the box.

Oh, and T-Bird downloads my Hotmail better than Outlook under Windows! Now THAT's a funny one :) 

Best regards........

Thanh
