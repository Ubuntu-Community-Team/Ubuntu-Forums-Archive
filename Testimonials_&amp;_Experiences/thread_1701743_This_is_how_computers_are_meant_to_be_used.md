### Post by u-noob-tu on 2011-03-06
Using linux has simply been a revelation for me. My career goal is to be a programmer, though I had never tried Linux (or even seen it) until 4 months ago when my dad brought an ubuntu live cd home from his office. It was meant to recover the password (which my dad forgot) from my vista laptop, but I ended up installing ubuntu over vista completely (looking back, I don't know how I didn't go insane from vista). Immediately, I felt like I was experiencing how computers were meant to be used, freely and without restrictions. Words cannot express the fantastic experience I have had with ubuntu/Linux. my 18 month old Dell studio boots in 30 seconds compared to 2+ minutes with vista. I can count the number of times Linux has crashed on me with one hand. And my favorite thing about Linux is the staggering amount of options you have when doing anything on your computer. Don't like GNOME? try KDE. Don't like either? Try flux box. Literally, no two Linux computers are exactly alike, and that's a very good thing. One windows does not fit all sizes!!!
