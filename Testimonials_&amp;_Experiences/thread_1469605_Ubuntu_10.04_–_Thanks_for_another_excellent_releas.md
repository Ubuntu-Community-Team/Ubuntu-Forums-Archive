### Post by Ad@m on 2010-05-02
Ubuntu 10.04 is another excellent release.

Out of the few distributions I have used, Ubuntu has always been the most stable, reliable and usable.

I believe this is because the Ubuntu team are more committed to delivering a working product and not a test platform to be used to develop a commercial product. OpenSUSE and Fedora springs to mind.

Ubuntu 10.04 is no exception. What is evident when Ubuntu is concerned, is the devs are listening to their users.

A lot of people flame the idea of making the operating system boot up / shut down quicker as pointless but fact is there is an ongoing perception that a modern operating system should be quick.

I like Ubuntu's continued efforts in this area, it demonstrates to me that the devs like a challenge but more importantly they respect the views of their users. 

The two new themes Ambiance and Radiance are also a pleasing change. Just as with boot / shut down speed, these may be minor and insignificant changes but when you start adding it all up it is having a big impact.

Admittedly I was a fan of OpenSUSE even though it has always felt a bit sluggish compared to other Linux distributions. 


 My first encounter with Ubuntu was with 8.04 and I have tried every other release since, 8.10, 9.04, 9.10.....

Each release has always been a good experience and I don't know why I have stuck to OpenSUSE for such a long time.


 I wish to express my continued support for Ubuntu and like to thank all the developers for their efforts.
