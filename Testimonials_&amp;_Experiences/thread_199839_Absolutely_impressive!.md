### Post by PenguinMan on 2006-06-19
[COLOR=Sienna]Having tested Ubuntu 6.06 (Dapper Drake) for a while now, I have really become fond of this new version. It's absolutely fantastic IMHO! I have been testing it in VMWare Workstation 5.5.1 (it's fast in VMWare!) and on our computers. Ubuntu 5.10 was close to being this good, but it did not quite reach the level that Ubuntu 6.06 has achieved.

I started using Linux a year ago with Linspire Five-O. I still use Linspire, but I have also been running PCLinuxOS since the start of 2006. As you can see, I am used to KDE, and I never thought I would like GNOME until now. Actually, I am liking the newer versions of GNOME very much. [/COLOR][COLOR=Sienna]:)

Yesterday, I was blown away with a test I did with an AMD AM2 motherboard/processor combination for a system we plan on launching - the first Linux OEM to do so. The new AM2 processor platform is important for me, because it allows an OEM to decrease their costs - very important! With an AM2 motherboard I can use the slowest Sempron 64 right up to the fastest Athlon 64 X2 processors. The problems of 754-pin vs 939-pin systems will be eliminated once and for all. AMD AM2 gives me huge flexibility in configurations/pricing.

Just running from the Live CD mode of the Ubuntu 6.06 CD-ROM (AMD64) I burned, audio, LAN, and video were working perfectly. Quite impressive, since sound was not being recognised by Linspire Five-O (5.1.427). PCLinuxOS worked a bit better, but only with the newer 0.93 minime version. The full version of 0.93 is forthcoming...

I am quite impressed with what has been achieved with Ubuntu 6.06. Keep it up, everyone at Ubuntu! :) I am going to install Ubuntu 6.06 on my HP AMD64 notebook this evening, since it has impressed me so much. ;)
[/COLOR]
