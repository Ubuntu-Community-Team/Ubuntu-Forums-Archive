### Post by 73ckn797 on 2011-11-12
Ubuntu user since 8.04 Hardy Heron. 

I was not impressed with 11.04. Completely skipped 10.10. Have been  checking out 11.10 on 3 computers. I have adjusted to Unity and do think  it is a good DE. I did not feel this way initially but a few days and  several tweaks make me think I can live with this.

I have not had any  problems with 11.10 and installed it on a Toshiba L300D laptop today.  Everything is working on first boot. This computer is for my wife's use and she is not a computer geek. It works for what she wants. The only issue, which is common across all of the Ubuntu releases is that certain websites require Internet Explorer/Windows to accomplish some tasks such as printing coupons. Virtualbox is the solution for that issue.

I have it on 2 desktops and another Toshiba laptop. I have been running 10.04 but believe that I will install/use 11.10 as the main OS. 

The improvements over the initial Unity release, in my opinion, are worth making the move. I likely will not use any other Linux distribution. Change can be something many will resist (there are a multitude of threads addressing other peoples issues) but, I can adapt and must if I am to continue using Ubuntu. Ubuntu has generally worked "out of the box" for me. The exception being 8.10.

These are my experiences and observations and your results may differ. If you want to complain, please use a thread where others are ranting. 

I await 12.04 LTS. It will be my next update on all of my systems.
