### Post by podunk on 2006-12-01
I understand the developers seldom come to the forums, and that's to bad. If they happen to drop by tell them I said thank you, they done good!

I installed a thing called "rutebook" from the repositories. It's a Linux tutorial/handbook. I've just started but it looks like I'm going to learn Linux by the time I get to the end.

It's 600+ plus pages in PDF format. It uncompressed instantly. I opened it and expected it to take forever since it was so big.

Less than 5 seconds. Jumping around in the book, I expected the huge lag I was accustomed to in the other OS - but clicking a thumbnail jumped right to the page.

I'm getting used to the applications I've chosen. I find that it is easier to organize, the apps are faster and I get more work done in less time.

It's true that I have to kludge an accounting system for my business because Quickbooks doesn't have a Linux version, but it's proving much simpler than I would have thought.

More and more I'm simply amazed at K/Ubuntu.
