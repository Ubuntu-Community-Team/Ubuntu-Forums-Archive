### Post by Nick King on 2008-04-12
I was in the library the other day using an iMac just to see what it was like.  I didn't like a lot of things about it, but it did have one thing that struck me: simplicity.  I loved how (seemingly) every program seemed to be linked with every other program.  I liked that.  But knowing Apples software licensing policies, the prices for iMacs, the contempt for the intelligence of the average user, I simply knew I was never going to use one.

I got home and remembered hearing about Ubuntu being a user-friendly Linux distribution.  I looked at some screen shots and started thinking about the idea of open-source computer.  I was suddenly struck with passion.   I was a slave to the Microsoft corporation.  I personally like their products, even though I've had every single one be DOA whenever I bought one.  My Xbox360 was DOA, and so was the replacement they sent me!!  My Zune died, XP was horrible at launch, as was Vista.  I still had faith, though, because I like their warranty plan, customer service, and ability to fix problems OVER TIME.  But it's not enough to keep me satisfied.

Linux has been right under my nose for years.  I just never liked the idea of going without Windows programs altogether.  Ever since VirtualBox came out, that's not much of a problem anymore (VMWare won't run on my AMD64 machine)

So I decided to switch and try Ubuntu for the first time.  i couldn't be happier.  I love everything about Linux.  The GNOME interface is simple, like Mac, but the OS is powerful and technical.  It's just the thing to keep the computer fresh every time I use it.  I love the thousands of FREE programs available, I love the surprising computability!  The only issue I had was my Nvidia GeForce 7200GS, but I enabled a restricted driver and it works fine now.

I still have an HP laptop, so I can install my Zune software on it (I know how badly MS plays with competition)

I can't begin to tell you how excited I am to be a part of this community!
