### Post by blitzd on 2006-07-22
Perhaps they aren't simple problems to fix? Perhaps there are no developers interested in working on the problem (the vast majority of them probably don't get paid for the work they put into Linux you know)? Or maybe the problem is with your hardware itself? Either way, you're going to be disappointed if you're expecting *any* software to be 100% bug free - whether it's free of charge or not.

Anyways...

Have you tried updating the firmware for the card?

Have you tried installing using a wired connection? 

Once the install is done you could switch to the wireless and at least have a chance of errors being logged to diagnose the problem.

Maybe the problem has even been fixed since the .ISO you're using for the install was made? ...and is just waiting on an update server somewhere.

Maybe there is even another driver you could be using? ndiswrapper maybe?

I know that support for wireless has always been sketchy for many cards. That mainly seems to be caused by the companies who manufacture the products themselves not wanting to share the information the devs need to write the drivers...

1) Manufacturers share the hardware specifications with the Open Source community.

2) When more attention will be brought to the matter.
