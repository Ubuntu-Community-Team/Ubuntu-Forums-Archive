### Post by nb1980 on 2009-03-31
I had been "playing" with Ubuntu for some months on an old laptop that I use mostly for P2P; but recently I decided to move to Kubuntu in my main computer and try to leave Windows behind for my main tasks.

The experience has been horrible and I want to share with you the reasons with I give up Linux. I respect the work that the community does and I wish you the best. I don't mean to criticize but help understand what are some of the problems that the regular user faces when trying to move to Linux.

My 2 week experience on a HP Pavilion with AMD Athlon 64 X2 5000+ and Nvidia GeForce 6150SE/430:

:!: LOTS of problems with the GPU (happens on every version of Linux I have tested since I was a kid).
  - At first, blank screen as soon as I tried to change something in the graphic configuration. Didn't manage to return to X. Had to reinstall the whole thing.
  - Once I installed Nvidia drivers, it worked but the performance is lousy compared to Windows on the same HW: bad resolution, weird-looking, unreadable fonts, SLOW transitions, display corrupted often. The memory didn't purged appropriately: when maximizing a window, for a fraction of a second I could see a window that I closed minutes before. Even something from a previous windows session!

:!: Almost everything new you try to do, come with lots of problems and hours of troubleshooting.
  - Geeks and IT people might have the time and motivation for that. For most of us, the OS and the computer are the tools, not the purpose.
  - When you look for solutions, all you find are forums with lots of unrelated or outdated comments and unresolved issues. Not a centralized problem-solving guide. People who try to help are great, but finding the right solution for each problem takes sooo much time.
  - Firefox crashed every few minutes. FF default theme looks like garbage. Amarok failed to send to trash. Some Plasma widgets stopped working. DEB packages don't install on AMD64. System didn't find DejaVu font. Unreadable menus with dark font on dark background (I didn't change the default desktop theme). Can't have the file manager to open documents on 2 clicks and browse folders in the tree on 1 click. Plugins not available for Gwenview. And some other problems... (I am talking of only a 2 week period).

:!: Software out of date or not available.
  - Why do I have to cope with the problems with OpenOffice 2.4 or Amarok 1 to later find out that OpenOffice 3 and Amarok 2 are already out there? The update manager didn't tell me anything. "How do I install those updates myself?", "Will I mess something up if I update the software myself, out of Adept?"
  - When I open the manual for the software I am having problems with, it corresponds to a much older version of the software.
  - Drivers, drivers, drivers. The 3 main problems with Linux.
  - Adept offers hundreds of pieces of software, but what is left when you leave out the "geeky tool"? Lots of types of applications are not available in Linux. For example, what if I need to operate my GPS?

:!: Which brings me to the next: I will always need to pay/use Windows anyways:
 - For those applications that are only available or only work well on Windows.
 - It is almost impossible to find a good computer at a good price that doesn't come with Windows installed. If I want to save the money I pay for Windows, I have to pay more for the (unsupported) HW.

:!: TIME. The purpose of computers is to save time and make things easier. Does Linux comply with that?

:!: Security. I hear that Linux is great for security. I love that. But I never had a security/virus problem in Windows (that I know of). 
  - It was so easy for me to start Kubuntu in safe mode and, from there, go to the shell as ROOT!... no pwd needed.
  - I though that, by default, other users can't see and read documents in my home ...but they can!

:!: And what finally made me say NO MORE:
  - I find out that Linux has been constantly doing some kind of read/write operation of a SD card that I have inserted in my computer. I can't see the LED indicator from where I seat. No warning or notice from Ubuntu. Now the SD card is dead thanks to Ubuntu. $$ to the garbage.
  - I wrote some very important documents on OpenOffice 2.4, saved them as Word XP and sent them out. The recipient could only see the first 3 lines of the document. That makes me look so unprofessional. I don't mess nor joke with my work documents and professional relationships. That's it, Linux does not work for me.   

What would you say if Windows had half the problems I had with Ubuntu? Ubuntu is free and I love the concept but why use something that gives me so much trouble when I have something that performs better and is already paid for?

Like communism, Linux is a beautiful idea. It simply doesn't work for most of us. Linux is for IT people, not for the general public.

As I see it, Canonical will have to realize sooner or later that their business model won't work. Linux is great for IT professionals and things like servers. Not for the general public.

I really wish the best for Linux and want to congratulate the great Linux community. Great people. But Linux is not for people like me. Maybe in another 15 years...
