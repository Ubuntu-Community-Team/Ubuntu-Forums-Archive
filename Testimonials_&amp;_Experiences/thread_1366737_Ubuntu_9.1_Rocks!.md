### Post by carbonbased on 2009-12-28
I first installed Linux on my old IBM 386, which had a 133Mhz processor and a 1.6Gb HD and came with a nightmare OS called Windows 95. I installed Red Hat Linux. I've toyed with Linux off and on since then, sometimes for months at a time. I've installed OpenSUSE, Mandrake, Fidora, earlier Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Red Hat, and FreeBSD. So far, Ubuntu 9.1 is the only one I've found that seems like a top drawer, A+, easy to use, ease of install, and even beautiful desktop OS, easily capable of replacing Windows.

I have been very highly impressed with Ubuntu 9.1! I just can't get over how easy everything is to set up and what it's capable of doing right off the CD.

Kudos to Canonical and its developers everywhere! Ubuntu 9.1 just rocks!
