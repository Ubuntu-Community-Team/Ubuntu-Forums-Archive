### Post by Quicky on 2008-07-21
Bought a new laptop this week after my previous stalwart finally kicked the bucket. It won’t turn on anymore, which is a shame. It’s also a lie - sometimes it does switch on, boot up, and reach the desktop, but it will invariably power off at random and without warning. Its wasn’t the only hardware issue,  but certainly the most obvious.

It was while searching for a new machine that I began questioning whether I wanted to run another Linux system again. Its been good, for the most part, but is it time for a change?

Dapper was good to me - for two full years it was a flawless system, capable of anything XP could do before it was uninstalled, but not without a lot of reading, configuration, and trial and error. Hardy, on the other hand, was not so friendly. Sure it fixed a couple of minor issues I had with Dapper, but it brought with it a whole host of others. Things that previously were not broken. Basic stuff. 

I’m aware that I’m a single user, with only my own experiences to use as a yardstick, but with the upgrade to Hardy I encountered an inordinate number of issues with aspects of general everyday computing that I wouldn’t just expect to work out of the box, but demand. These weren’t unreasonable requests, given their functionality in its two-year-old precursor.

Still, like many on here I enjoy a challenge. Getting something to work that previously did not brings with it a great feeling of accomplishment. I like that. Except…except when the desired function is so menial and integral to general operation that its resolution is no longer a challenge, but a chore.

I love my Xbox. I may have mentioned that in the past. I’ve always been a big fan of Forza Motorsport - I played it incessantly for months. Kind of. What I actually spent most of my time doing was playing with the custom livery editor, trying to replicate my favourite movie cars within the game. Then one day, I stopped.  I realised I hadn’t actually raced a car in weeks, and that made me question my motives  - was I actually having fun with the game? Or was I working?

The same has happened with Linux, I’ve spent a lot of time over the last few years tweaking, customising, and struggling with various (non)functionality - time which my wife might call ‘wasted’. I wouldn’t be quite so blunt, but I was obviously working towards some goal, the motives for which became blurred during the procedure.

I’ve enjoyed aspects, it’s a great community - everyone here knows that. Probably the best tech support department in the world. Never having to worry about security in the way that Windows users do provides a phenomenal sense of relaxation and freedom. I suppose it just doesn’t offset it enough.

I’m writing this on my new Vista laptop. I like it. Its familiar and new all at once. It emanates productivity, but its filled with things I will immediately hate. For every shiny new application I’ve discovered, there’s a bloody great Norton Security suite.

Like many things, choosing an OS has become an exercise in picking the lesser evil. Time will tell, and my choices will probably never be right. After all, its never the right time in the IT world. A brighter future is always just around the corner. I’m sure I’ll see you there.

Quicky

We do not agree, I find Hardy to be the best Ubuntu OS ever.  It is stable, works with LOTS of hardware.  I bought a new laptop (Toshiba A205-S5859) and EVERYTHING works OOB.  No tinkering, no .conf file editing.
