ok, here's the situation:

i've got one of the tip-top boxes (ok, not the best [-( ) that winXP can dream of (DFI LanParty nF4 SLI-DR, AMD Athlon 64 X2 Manchester 4200+, 2x MSI NX6800GS-TD256E, 2 GB Samsung DDR 3200 etc.) and i've tried both signed for XP drivers and betas.

the installation is ok, my winblows box is up and running in 1,5-2 hours (the way i need it). everything is fine for about a month, and then it begins freezing during boot.. that happened a few times. re-formatting doesn't help, i have to boot it in safe mode once, after restart it boots normally..

i had issues about symantec client security v 3 (goddamn tampering control), and my logitech keyboard and cordless m$ mouse.. i can't run m$ intellipoint and logitech setpoint together (there's another program doing my job????? - that's the error).

in breezy i had minor problems, but it wasn't complete for me from some angles (i don't know how to explain that), dapper is much better (except for the printing issues and sli-xgl problems). but even for an os under heavy development, it kicks winblows' a.. hands down.

what are people at m$ thinking about? being the first in the marketplace gives advantage, that's ok, but as far as i see ubuntu will be much better than their products in the next 2 years or so. and it's not the only successful distro..

yes, gnome sacrifices too much for the sake of simplicity, and kde is slow and breakable (my experiences), but they will find the mid point, for there's such a great community. driver issues are coming to an end also, yes there are too many different kinds of hardware, and linux devs cannot cover all of them, but firms are (slowly) beginning to support linux.

i now have win server 2003 standard and dapper on my disk, and actually the win partition is for urgencies, for i'm an active directory admin, and not-so-experienced in linux, yet ;) 

all-in-all i'm not even thinking to go back to winblows, for i'm sick and tired of firewalls, anti-virus progs, stupid errors, meaningless m$ articles which simply say "well, sh.. happens, learn to live with it" (as for the 2 GB memory limit in xp sp2), etc.
