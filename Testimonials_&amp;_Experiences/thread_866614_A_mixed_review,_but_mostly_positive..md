### Post by brokenLockpick on 2008-07-22
I have to say that when I first installed I was disgusted, I was stuck at 800x600 resolution on a monitor that I'd used just an hour prior in Windows at 1280x1024. No problem I think, I'll just get the proper Nvidia driver and all will be well, nope, say hello to a choice between 640xblah and 320xdouble-blah. A whole lot of messing up the xorg.conf file and head scratching later I tried a VGA cable rather than DVI and it worked beautifully.

From that point on it was all golden. (except for one small issue with blender). My printer was a breeze to install the drivers for, though the suggested driver didn't work, the manufacturer had linux drivers on their website. Digital camera worked instantly. All the media keys worked on both keyboards (have a multiscreen simultaneous htpc/desktop thing going on). A quick download gave me at least clock functionality on my G15 keyboard's lcd (and the software developer's kit looks promising to add additional functionality). The MOST amazing thing was that my DVD burner worked, it never worked under Windows. Updating the firmware and drivers yielded nothing, and no matter what software I used it just made coasters. Score one major point right there for Ubuntu, I was about to drop 40ish dollars US on a new one because I was convinced that the hardware was just faulty.

In closing, there was one other benefit. Ubuntu gave me some familiarity with the bash shell, so some quick shell scripts to be run at login finally fixed the problems at work with the handful of Macs we had that didn't like to recognize the network drives on a largely MS network.

All in all, aside from a snarl or two, pretty good.
