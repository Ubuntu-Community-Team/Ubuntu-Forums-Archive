### Post by cheapie on 2011-05-20
Just about every piece of hardware that I throw at Ubuntu has worked just fine. Here are some of the things that I've tried and it has handled perfectly. (As in I just plugged them in and they worked)

[LIST]
[*]VIA VT6421 (I think) RAID Controller (Some Windows users have trouble with it, but it works fine in Ubuntu)
[*]Seagate ST-157A-1 45 MB Hard Drive (20 Years Old!)
[*]Unknown Atheros Wireless NIC
[*]Cheapo OEM Motherboard
[/LIST]

As for that hard drive, yes, it is 45 MB, not GB. Also, it doesn't support LBA and only operates in PIO 0. Interestingly, Palimpsest (Disk Utility) even displays the correct model number. As for the motherboard, Windows wouldn't read any SMART data at all from any of my hard drives, but Ubuntu read it just fine. I also like how Ubuntu can boot from a hard drive with a GUID partition table without EFI. Speaking of GUID partition tables, it's nice to be able to use them without creating "Microsoft Reserved Partitions". I like actually having control over how my hard drives are partioned, or as Microsoft sees it, "all my storage are belong to me".
