### Post by cdtech on 2008-01-23
Linux desktop users tend to be students (undergrads or graduates), full time workers tend to be System Adimistrators. Most consider themselves experianced but without technical skills. Most can set it up the way they like and keep it updated but don't think of themselves as experts, more like "power users".

Stability and Security is why Linux is so sought after as a primary OS. Linux could use more work on the hardware support and usability, we all know that. The least important is Technical Support, we tend to not like having our hands held while we're setting up our systems. The best thing is the price.

Most users do not contribute to the development of the OS. A very small part of the users acually do work on bettering the distribution, and most of them are busy with the bug reporting on mailing list and even a smaller group work on the patches.

"Just over 1/3 of the 2.6.20 patch stream was written by volunteers. The real number will be lower, but it still shows that a significant portion of the code we run is written by developers who are donating their time."(Who wrote 2.6.20?, Corbet, 2007)
[COLOR="DarkRed"][http://lwn.net/Articles/222773/]("http://lwn.net/Articles/222773/")[/COLOR]

So when I see so many users just throwing thier hands up just tells me that thier primary goal to use Linux is just to get away from another OS that they can't understand as well and want someone to hold thier hand to set it up the way they want it.
