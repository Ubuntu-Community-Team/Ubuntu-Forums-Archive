### Post by Kappity on 2008-12-27
I've been using Ubuntu on an old IBM laptop for about a year now.  Although the old hardware wouldn't let the OS reach its full potential, I gained some confidence, and have just bought a new Dell Inspiron with Hardy Heron pre-installed.  It arrived 4 days ago, and I've got things set up pretty nicely.

I still generally prefer the extreme user friendliness of Mac OS X to Linux, even an easy to use release like Ubuntu.  With Mac it's all very intuitive, with Linux, even after I've learned to do something, I have to make notes, because I may not remember the command line stuff next time.  Something as simple as renaming a thumb drive, for example.  So why this decision?

Well, I decided I need a new desktop computer.  My Mac Mini, the one relatively inexpensive Mac, had a fried hard drive after less than 2½ years.  I actually have it working again using an external firewire connected hard drive, but it seemed like time to get something new.

Look at the prices on Mac desktop computers for yourself.  The Mini is still pretty cheap, but I no longer trusted it.  The others seem very nice, but I was able to get the Dell for little more than half what the cheapest iMac costs, and only a little more than another Mini.

Ubuntu is easy *enough*.  I've had the Dell for four days now, and I've got all my major programs set up and running nicely.  Okay, I haven't yet gotten Google Earth to work right, even after reading quite a bit of online advice, but that's minor.  Everything else is working just the way it should.

I still have the Mini as a backup computer, and my laptop of choice is my MacBook.  If I have to buy another laptop in the next year, I might get another Mac, as I don't want to fiddle with a lot of problems getting power saving and WiFi to work properly under Linux.  For home use, though, it just doesn't make sense to pay Mac prices, when I can do everything I need with Ubuntu.

So there you have it, another Ubuntu convert, at least partially. :wink:

Of course I *could* go back to Windows. :lolflag:

### Post by pataphysicianu on 2008-12-27
> **Kappity said:**
>  I have to make notes, because I may not remember the command line stuff next time.  Something as simple as renaming a thumb drive, for example.  

There is no need for command line, though it might be quicker, just use the Partition Editor(gparted) under the System>Administration menu, it's for managing all your storage devices. Just switch to the device that is your thumb drive, unmount it by right clicking the partition on the thumb drive and choosing unmount, now the Label menu is no longer grayed out, so right click on the partition again and choose Label, now give the thumbdrive a name and hit the apply button. Now remove the thumbdrive and put it back in and Nautilus will show it with the new name.
