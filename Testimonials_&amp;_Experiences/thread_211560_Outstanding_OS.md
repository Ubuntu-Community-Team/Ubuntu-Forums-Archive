### Post by mtcook01 on 2006-07-08
I wanted to spend some time with Ubuntu Dapper before any comments were made.  A little background about myself.  I am an IT Manager for a metropolitan 911 agency and have used linux on and off for the last 10 years.  I started with Red Hat 3 and fought with it for months.  Then moved to Mandrake and finally Libranet. 

I use Libranet and Windows servers at our current organization and to be honest the Libranet server is much better behaved and requires little downtime.  We have a schedule to reboot our windows server every Friday to avoid memory allocation errors and runaway exchange processes.  I am not saying this to bash MS, so please refrain from flaming.  Its just that way.

Anyways, I have been playing with Dapper for about a week now on a dual booted laptop.  I find myself spending more time in the Linux boot more every day.  I must say Ubuntu is a fine OS.  Its refined, easy to use and appears very stable.  I have been a VB programmer for years as well and am very interested in the REALBasic product as it provides very good cross platform builds from the little that I have tested.

Also the community support is second to none. The Ubuntu forums have answered every question, concern and complication that I have encountered.  

Although there are some annoyances I find that Ubuntu Dapper is outstanding.  Thanks for all the hard work.:-D
