### Post by mthmchris on 2007-10-28
As soon as I installed Ubuntu, I was immediately very, very impressed.  Suddenly it seemed as if my 3 1/2 year old hardware was brand new.

Me and a good friend of mine are studying abroad in Hong Kong.  In preparation for the lack of good TV, my buddy bought an external hard drive and loaded it with over 600 gigs of movies and tv shows.  It was a very nice collection, I must say.

But one night he had the hard drive connected to his computer, and during his sleep he mistakenly ripped the power cord from the wall.  Not good.  This completely ****** up the hard drive, and it didn't read on his Mac anymore.  Plugged it into my computer running XP, and while it recognized the hard drive, it said that it needed to be reformatted.

We tried getting someone we know to fix it, but no luck.  He found that the filesystem, for some reason, was change.  He tried changing it back, screwing around with the Master Boot Record (just fyi, I have no idea what these things mean), and nothing worked.  The hard drive was dead.

After I switched to Ubuntu, my friend had the idea of plugging in the hard drive to my computer just to see if it would would.  Even though I love my new system, I was intensely skeptical.  After all, hardware support is supposed to be Linux's weak point.  But to my surprise, when I plugged it in, my computer read the hard drive and everything worked perfectly.  I have no idea why it worked.  All I know is that the thing worked, when OSX, Vista, and XP just couldn't do the job.  Interestingly, after this thing worked out, my buddy is deleting his OSX partition on his MacBook Pro and putting on Ubuntu (he, unlike me, likes doing work on his laptop, and needs his Vista partition for Office).

I'm sure this is a rare circumstance, but I just ******* love this OS.  I am so damn impressed.

That's a cool story.

A friend of mine had a flash drive which was unplugged before unmounting, and it wouldn't read on Windows or Mac OS X. I plugged it into my Mac OS 9 computer, and the Mac OS automatically fixed the flash drive :-)

And my father had some trouble with his flash MP3 player. It had mysteriously become read-only, and he couldn't even format it. Until I chucked it into my Ubuntu computer (the same machine as the Mac OS 9 computer in the previous story, lol) and ran Gparted to format it back to FAT32.

After that, he had no problems, and I think this particular incident made him think very positively about open-source. He saw it more as "open-source software being better than proprietary software" when I think it was probably more a case of "different code works differently".
