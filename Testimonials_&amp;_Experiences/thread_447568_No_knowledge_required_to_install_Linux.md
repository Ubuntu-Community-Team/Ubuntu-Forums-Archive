### Post by suziequzie on 2007-05-19
"Just like the OS from Redmond..."

Yes, I agree. When I built my first computer 5 years ago, I didn't know about linux, so I bought a second hand (but legal) Windows 98 CD, thinking "I'm gonna need Windows..." and prepared to install that. 

By prepare, I mean I searched online on my boyfriend's computer about the necessary steps to installing an OS. I made sure to have ALL my necessary hardware driver CDs ready (mobo, vid card, sound card, etc). His computer (an NEC system with bundled software from Future Shop) relied on a rescue CD, so "re-installing" to factory defaults was simple. But installing a fresh OS? On a custom machine? I knew I had to do my homework.

When I discovered Linux, it was in the Mandrake 8.0 days, and researching the OS was still a very necessary step. Mandrake didn't last long (looked nice, but... I dunno...). 

Now, this is my second computer, and I followed the same preliminary steps for installing Kubuntu. I first got a free Ship-it live Ubuntu CD, loved it but hated the interface. Did some more research on Desktop environments, fell in love with some KDE screen-caps, and downloaded Kubuntu. THEN, after a little reading, installation.

Success. Never looked back.
