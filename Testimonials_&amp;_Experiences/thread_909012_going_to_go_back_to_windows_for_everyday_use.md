### Post by irrdev on 2008-09-03
> **hyper_ch said:**
> Install a fresh retail win XP copy and try to play a dvd there... it does not work ;)
That's not all that **doesn't** work on a **clean** XP or Vista install! My XP box crashed 1 year ago, and when I finished reinstallation, I discovered that my Networking, 3d Graphics, Sound and Automatic Updates didn't work. Absolutely lovely. I had to download drivers onto my other computer (running Ubuntu, BTW), copy them on my external hard drive, and then transfer and install them onto my XP machine. I did get most of the problems fixed with new drivers, but Automatic updates never worked for me at all, while my Network connection was constantly lagging behind at about 20%. That experience finally convinced me to switch completely over to Linux, and I haven't turned back.

If you haven't had a good experience with Ubuntu, I recommend trying out other Linux distributions. Specifically, I recommend that you try out either LinuxMint, or openSUSE 11.1 that is coming out in December. :wink:
