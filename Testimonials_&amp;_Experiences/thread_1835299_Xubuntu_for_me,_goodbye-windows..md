### Post by goodbye-windows(tm) on 2011-08-29
Hi All,

I'm a newbie to ubuntu, although I have followed the linux movement for years. I would not be running linux if it wasn't for the ubuntu (and derivatives) advanced (current) state of readiness.

I had my share of technical issues, and the major ones are now resolved. My laptop has the dreaded black screen video issue, which means I can't use a screen saver and I can't allow the sleep or hibernate modes. But, other than that, the punchbox's performance is outstanding despite the fact that the processor is old and tired. Earlier this evening, the last major issue was resolved, I now have my wireless running!!!!

Many in this forum helped to train me and offered help. I want to say thanks to them and to all the other volunteers who run the forum and those who make linux run and those who write software.

For me, I'm here because I got fed up with windows, mostly because of its many back doors and security issues and other assorted pitfalls. Also, I also couldn't send my daughter to school with a windows based computer-again because of the lack of security.

The open sourced operating system and software means I don't have to wonder about back doors and malware built in to the aps. I don't need a virus scanner and don't have problems and overhead needed to scan for viruses. The performance of xubuntu with this old and underpowered processor rivals that of my daughters last computer-which was a high end Mac. These reasons alone justify the time I invested to convert to linux. As far as basic computer operation, converting from windows was pretty painless. My daughter commented that xubuntu is more similar to a Mac os than to a windows os however.

In closing, I look forward to the day when the linux community has enough clout to approach a computer OEM and have them build a computer from the ground up-one that will be 100 percent linux compatible without quirks and without the need for special measures just to make linux run.

I'm 99 percent windows free now, and very much look forward to the day when I can put a sign on my front house entrance that says 'no windows allowed'.

GL to all.

Art
