### Post by wbee on 2009-10-30
Hello,

The development and testing team should take enough time to pat themselves on the back for this one.

--9.10 (Cups and Xsane) recognized my printer/scanner and both work perfectly without workarounds,which was not true with 9.4.

--The nVidia driver loaded perfectly and every time I have booted(so far) it has loaded the correct screen resolution.

--One of the things that drove me nuts last March when I first installed Ubuntu was getting the sound card portion of a TV tuner card to work.

This time,the system recognized both cards,allowing me to switch between those,and also *it converted the second one from a bit rate of 33.5(?) to 44.1 without my having to follow the directions in the Pulse Audio documentation to get it to work correctly.

--Over all,the audio portion is more polished and cogent,perhaps even intuitive to a newbee user.

--The extension four,grub,and log-in are fast accurate,and it even put in a Linux swap without my having to go into GParted to do it myself.

--The new Gnome desk top(or the improved nVidia driver or both) has a better feel. I've read some of the comments elsewhere about the direction Gnome is going. That said,this desktop,at least on my computer,if MUCH better than the one previous.

--The two gigs of cloud space is useful.

--The Software Center seems to be an excellent abridgment of the Synaptic Package Manager.

-----------

****Developers and all,thank you.

### Post by Duncan J Murray on 2009-10-31
The things I noticed were:


Everything just seems slicker - maybe something to do with my graphics driver

Music plays without skipping  a beat - hurray!

It boots faster


I've had a couple problems though..

When I did a fresh install, Grub wouldn't boot from my harddrive.  I had to use the alternate CD to edit grub to get it to work properly.

I used to have a problem with flash on 9.04, in that it was v slow and skippy, especially full screen.  This all resolved in 9.04, but in 9.10 it seems to have taken a slight step backwards.

There is the occasional crash (not sure what's caused it - could be any of the several things I've been running)


But, on the whole I'm very much enjoying it!!    Great job!  I hope my little niggles get ironed out sometime soon, but it still doesn't detract from what is an awesome OS.

Great job guys.

Duncan.
