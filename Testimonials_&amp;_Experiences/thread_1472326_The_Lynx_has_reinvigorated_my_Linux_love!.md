### Post by BigSilly on 2010-05-04
Not that I needed a great deal of invigorating! :D

But seriously, I just wanted to post up and say a massive thank you to the Ubuntu team for this brilliant release. For me, it's a massive step on from Karmic Koala, which had certain problems that I was unable to sort out. Out of the box, Lucid Lynx has proved a much better experience, and with a little tweaking (and I really mean a little) it's proving so far to be the complete experience I want from Ubuntu.

I'm finding that the audio problems I had to just put up with on Karmic have completely gone now. Video is much better, with no tearing at full screen etc. I have a few games (emulators and other Linux native titles), that suffered all sorts of problems under the Koala, that now work flawlessly on the Lynx with no input from me. All my favourite Compiz effects and utilities are running beautifully and trouble free. I am absolutely stoked. :)

Outside of all this, I'm just finding that I just love the look and feel of the distro. It feels like business this time. I like it. :)

I do now dual boot with Windows 7, which I bought around launch time at the pre-order price. I wouldn't have bothered with it usually, as I've been an Ubuntu user for years, but the price was good and the press was positive. During the six months I used Koala, I found myself more and more on the Windows install. But now, with this release, I'm back to using Linux more and considering selling my W7 retail box. I really didn't expect that after the Koala, but it's true.

So thanks again for re-igniting my passion for Ubuntu and Linux with this fantastic release. Best one yet!

### Post by dnguyen1963 on 2010-05-04
This is a classic case of a product life cycle.  As Ubuntu (Linux in general) is getting better and better after each release, the expectation from the general public is getting higher and higher.  Most people expect Lucid Lynx to work flawlessly (with their systems...considering the hype that Ubuntu has gotten in the press lately)right after the installation.  Unfairly, (well, life is not fair) they vent their frustrations when they run into problems.  Some of us, including myself, post the problems that we have observed.  We don't post any solutions because we do not know how.  Unfortunately, some developers and contributors take these venting as insults to their efforts.  I wish that everyone would take a step back and be patient.  Let's give the computer experts some time to work out the problems.  It has been less than a week since the release.  As far as I can tell most contributors do not get paid to work on Lucid Lynx.

Thank you to the Lucid Lynx team.
