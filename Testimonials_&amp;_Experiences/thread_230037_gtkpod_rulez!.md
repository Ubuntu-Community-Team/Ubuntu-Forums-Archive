### Post by hellfried on 2006-08-05
i had a pleasant surprise just now. when i was using breezy i never managed to transfer mp3s to my ipod mini or nano using either gtkpod or banshee. for some strange reasons they just refuse to write the damn things to the ipods.
 
luckily tonight i had some time to kill and i decided to install gtkpod on dapper and hooked up my ipod video. lo and behold gtkpod worked!! just transferred some songs over and they are playing flawlessly on my ipod. i did not have to do anything at all.

while researching my problems with gtkpod on breezy earlier on i have had advice from wellwishers suggesting that i reformat my ipod and it just sounded too scary for a relatively expensive piece of equipment. mind you this ipod came right out of the box and was hooked up from within my winxp partition. all the songs on it so far has been written to it from either winxp or mac osx. this is the very 1st time that it has met dapper and everything turned up hunky dory!
 
i wonder what has changed. either gtkpod was refined after my failed attempt to get it working with breezy or maybe there is some piece of code within  dapper that did the trick. or maybe apple just made sure that the 5th gen ipod video was also supported by linux.  either way i am just very glad and i thought i would share it with you guys. one step closer to deleting that winxp partition!

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-08-08
I've loaded an iPod Nano from GTKpod on Breezy, and it worked like a charm.

I've got another iPod-with-Linux story. My Mum got a Shuffle, so I tried to load it with music on iTunes (Windows version on Windows). Long story short, it copied most of the songs over but the music wouldn't play on the iPod! GTKpod and AmaroK reported the songs being on the player, but nothing we did convinced the iPod to play them.

So I reformatted it and reloaded it with the same music, this time using GTKpod instead of iTunes. Now it works perfectly.
