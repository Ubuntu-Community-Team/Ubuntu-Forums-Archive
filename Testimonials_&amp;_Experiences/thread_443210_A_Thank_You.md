### Post by Knowles on 2007-05-14
Thank You Ubuntu Team/Community.

For many many years I kept to the proprietary software on the sole argument of 'standards', Linux and in fact the whole 'open-source' movement scared me. I was comforted that an operating system like Windows would hold my hand and embrace me rather than require me to perform any sort of configuration. Though as I expanded my horizons and my requirements (functional and non functional) changed and something about the free and open publication of software pulled me in. Initially my conversion started with Firefox, at the time I wasn't aware that Firefox was open-source it was just quality free software in my eyes. Slowly, in some ways I like to think I became more 'ethical' in how I chose what to use, I would only choose applications which published their entire source code. Anyway go forward a few years and I am a full Linux/open-source convert the fact that software can now be openly tailored to meet 99% of peoples needs is fascinating.

What I mean to say is thanks to the strong community behind the development of this software and the time and effort people give without receiving anything apart from a digital pat on the back really puts faith into the work of open-source.

Thanks for giving more than you receive, ubuntu and all the other variants (xubuntu user here) is something I wouldn't want to be without.

Thomas Knowles.
