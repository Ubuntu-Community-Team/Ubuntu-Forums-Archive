### Post by anotherusernametoforget on 2011-09-10
After years of DOS, then Windows use with a little Apple use (art school) I decided to take the plunge into Ubuntu. Let me say that the reason was to escape windows into what I thought was a shortcut to bypass Virtual box, which needed to get it's affairs strait- but that's a different story.

First impressions were a mix of excitement manifested in the nuances of an alien OS with accents of aimless discouragement from not being able to do anything that I had before.

What a change of atmosphere from the suburban standard Windows environment, warm and familiar, yet underachieving and bumbling at times, to the barren tundra of an 'it's-what-you-make-of-it' OS just waiting for someone who's already an expert to take the wheel and launch it off the line. I felt both overwhelmed and underwhelmed simultaneously. It was like walking into a surprise birthday party with no balloons, cake or streamers, instead finding gray scale suits and satin ties adorning bitter bodies with faces seemingly waiting to be impressed. 

Less time booting? Great! More(slightly) liberty changing basic aesthetics? Awesome! Command functions to achieve what used to be simple mouse-navigation? Coo....l...? Thousands of pages of reading to be done before finding answers to what would be common problems in windows? uhh... The prospect of a varitable powerhouse of function with only little glamour to trip over? *TANTILIZING*.

I don't like not being able to access the files in my ntfs hd, and downloading software a billion times before being able to run an application, but I see promise here. Hard work, and promise.

Closing notes: Be ready to read a lot of newbie posts from me in the future. I'll do my absolute best to keep calm accept that this is going to be excruciating for a while.
