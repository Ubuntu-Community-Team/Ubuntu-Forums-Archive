### Post by ReverendJ1 on 2007-07-12
I know there are probably a million threads just like this one, but I feel compelled to write my own. I have been a Windows Power-User since the days of 3.11. I have tried various Linux distros (RedHat, Mandrake, Fedora, maybe a couple more) over the years, and have given up on them for one reason or another (I couldn't do this or that, ran in to dependency hell installing programs, didn't have time to stop trying things 'the Windows way', etc.). I tried them out because I love the ideology of Linux and FOSS and the security Linux offers. 

   I had been hearing people rant and rave about Ubuntu for a few months and decided to try it out myself. Especially because I REALLY didn't like the way Microsoft was going with Vista. Apple/Mac was also out of the picture, because I don't like the way they handle things either.  I don't hate either of them for it, they are a for profit company, and what they are doing makes sense for them. But if they want my dollars, they need to do what makes sense for me. Which is what Linux is all about. 
   I made a conscientious decision this time to stick to Linux about 4-5 months ago and installed Ubuntu. Aside from a couple hiccups getting everything running, it has been a dream. I mean, is 'Add/Remove' and Synaptic not the coolest thing ever? Since I did my install (I also did a dual-boot) I haven't looked back. I only boot into XP on VERY RARE occasions (less than a handful in the past 4 months) and the community surrounding Ubuntu makes me actually WANT to keep using Ubuntu. It is almost like a family. I really love the behavior of everyone on these forums. It is SO nice to not see any RTFM!, 'search google n00b' type comments. I spend a lot of my time on various forums and everyone here seems a lot more civil. 

   I have also been trying to help out a little bit with the open-source community, finding/reporting bugs, and attempting to help people on forums when possible. Once I have enough money that I can actually afford rent AND food I will start donating to projects that accept them, or buying merchandise from them.

   This post really has no point, except for a big THANK YOU to everyone involved in Ubuntu for creating such a wonderful product and human-friendly atmosphere! All of your obvious hard work is greatly appreciated.
-Johnny5
