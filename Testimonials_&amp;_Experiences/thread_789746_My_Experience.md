### Post by Hellweek on 2008-05-10
Well the day has arrived.  I am no longer tied to Microsoft products.

For the last 2 weeks I have been converting my day to day computing needs into Ubuntu&#8217;s distro of linux.  While I freely admit I had many issues to overcome do to my computer configuration and the pure number of years I spent in Microsoft OS&#8217;s.  


While at times some of the challenges were daunting and frustrating they also were refreshing and allowed me to better understand this new world I was in.

Some of the challenges issues I faced I will list here as I am sure many of you will nod you head and say yep been there did that.

1.	Video just purchased a new video card. NVIDA 9800 GTS.  Wanted it to work in this new world and I wanted both monitors to work.  I am kind of funny about that.  

Thanks to a great utility mentioned in a post (Envy [http://albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html](http://albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html)) I was able to quickly get the drivers installed correctly and configure my screens.  It&#8217;s great to be king.

2.	I have a large Music and movie collection that reside on NTFS disk that are on RAID arrays.  I did not wish to try and move the data it was to large and I had no where to move it.  So it was a requirement that I was able to remount my Raid Arrays.  

After reading the MASSIVE debates about software raid and hardware raid and reading the pursuits thoughts.  

I decided to just try it.  It seemed that UBU saw my drives it saw my mirrored drives and mounted them (As 2 drives grrrr).  And it saw my other raid drives.  

DMRAID was the saving factor as was this post([http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-629459.html](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-629459.html)).  It sorted the disks out and confirmed the drives were correctly configured.  So all I needed to do was mount them.  

What a cryptic command that is prone to error, however the forums here were most helpful as someone wrote very simple commands that explained (This was the most important if I don&#8217;t know what a command is I don&#8217;t want to run it) in good detail what I was doing.  Thanks to This link([http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Linux-NTFS_FAQ#Common_mount_options](http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Linux-NTFS_FAQ#Common_mount_options).) post I am a mounting fool and was able to get my Arrays up with the data intact.    

You should have heard the shouting in the house scared the wife silly. On a side note I am not sure if I have configured the mirror part correctly still looking into this issue 20 years of source code is on these drives must be sure must be sure. 

3.	VPN ugggg this one sucked a big hen egg.  
While there was a good add-on to network manager that allowed you to somewhat configure it easy it is still not 100 percent.  I can not use a static IP address.  I personally don&#8217;t want a DHCP address on my home computer as it do have ports forwarded (Luck for me I have a Linksys wrt54g v2 reflashed with custom firmware).  So fine I accept that VPN support for a windows machine is not simple in Linux.  

I was however, able to connect to my company network but was unable to access anything by hostname I had to settle for IP.  This works as I only want to connect to my desktop at work once on it I can terminal serve to any machine.



4.	Let&#8217;s face it.  There are still games (World of Warcraft) and other things (My compilers, Ventrilo, etc..) that do not work in the Linux world yet as such I needed a way to run them.  (While I was willing to let this pass and resign myself to being forced to duel boot.) These were overcome by some clear programs.  WINE and VMWARE for Linux.  Problems solved and to be honest WINE runs WOW better then Windows XP 64.  Microphone support was a bit dodgy to work out but was able to get it working.

5. N52 game pad.  Well still working on this.  It appears someone is or was working on a driver however, I still have not been able to build it or use it.  The funny thing is for the most part the Nordstorm is working the keys I use them most seem to be some sort of default layout.  My jump key is located in a different spot but meh I will get over it.

5.	News Group reader software.  Well still looking however it seems PAN is my best choice.  Still looking.


I have been windows free for one week now.  To be honest I have a lot to learn It reminds me of my old dos days when everything was done via command line.  I look forward to learning more and hope that this begins a new era in computing for me.  

While I used Linux in the past Way before home routers were common.  I used them for firewalls and nat routers to share internet connection.  However, I did not consider using it for any other real reason.
