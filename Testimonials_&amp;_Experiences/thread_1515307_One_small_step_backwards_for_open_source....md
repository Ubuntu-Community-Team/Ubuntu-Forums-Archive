### Post by ktat on 2010-06-22
Gosh, has anyone else had a disappointing experience with Lucid Lynx?  After reading all the hype/reviews I thought I was going to be in for a real treat (particularly regarding the prospect of having a working video editor to play with).  I have been using Ubuntu for about 4 years now and am relatively used to some ongoing problems with my hardware and Linux not agreeing with each other, however, I must say I am quite frustrated with the situation I find myself in now having done a fresh install over Karmic.

1. No longer is my sound working, I can't even play .ogg files (this has NEVER been an issue before)

2. I can't print.  When I checked the printers my Epson TX100 was there (but not working), so I removed it and tried to reinstall it.  I was able to select it from a list of possible printers, however it could not find a driver (seems I have to be connected to the internet for that). Again, rarely in the past have I had significant problems with printing. **OOPS! Not a problem with Ubuntu, my printer cable decided to break same time as install!!**

3. NO GIMP!! One of my favourite Apps gone, was it sacrificed to make room for Pitivi??  Now I know, if my pc was connected to the internet, this would be relatively easy to fix, however, I'm not and a quick check of the GIMP web page doesn't seem to have a .deb package that I can upload to usb and install onto my pc at a later date :(

4. and on the subject of Pitivi... a great addition to the OS, however, most clips wouldn't load and came back with the error
> "Problem:An internal error occurred while analysing this file: Your GStreamer installation is missing a plug-in. Extra information:gstdecodebin2.c(2"
The ones that did load, didn't have any audio.

What I am wondering, though, is this - could the fact that I have a separate /home partition be a part of the problem.  I've noticed in my /home folder there are some folders such as ~/openoffice.org2 and ~/gnome2. I imagine they have been created rather than overwriting the old ones that went with the Karmic OS I had installed previously.  Could this be the source of my problems??

I have tried the "Computer Janitor" but it opens with an error message > "essential package dash is missing" and then closes.

This experience has left me feeling somewhat deflated as, in the past, Ubuntu distros have demonstrated overall improvement from one version to the next.  Now I'm having to back to re-discover old tricks to get things back on track again and wondering "...is it all too much effort?".  I believe strongly in the principles of open source, so I guess I will persevere.


I am using Lucid Lynx i386 with AMD Athlon(tm) 64 X2 Dual Core Processor 5200+
