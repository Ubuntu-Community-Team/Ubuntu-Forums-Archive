### Post by AirWreck on 2008-09-09
Just wanted to come off the wall for a moment and say thanks to such an open community.  I installed a dual boot Kubuntu/WXP on a whim Friday.  My laptop is dying a slow death and I'm not a big fan of Vista so I figured I'd check out the options after hearing some Ubuntu chatter hear and there.  I'm Windows savvy but command line/geek impaired.  Not scahhhhhhred, just impaired:)  

I was pretty lost a few times, but managed to navigate through the dual boot hurdles and onto figuring out how to install programs.  I was surprised to find that it was pretty point and clicky.  Every time I hit a stumbling block, the threads and good info here and there and everywhere eventually led me down the right path.  Well except tarballs, I picked up one of those for a cool widget I wanted but it got on my feet and I can't figure out how to cram it into the computer in any way that it likes...yet.

Four days later, the surfboards didn't get patched and the wife's bicycle didn't get tuned.  But I do have a pretty workable rig that shares my T'bird and F'fox profiles from WXP (still working on whether I'll go with a /home part or not).  I beebled my way through the first day or two and loaded the things I know I wanted/needed.  I had just hoped to netsurf, email and see if there was a decent music player.  Today I found a neat site that showed an example of a Kubuntu setup that would stand in for a typical WXP install.  Did all that and I find myself staring at a desktop I like a lot more than the one over on the WXP side of the fence.  Pretty cool.

I've seem some posts where you folks are trying to demystify Ubuntu for the general public.  So just wanted to let you know that this Johnny Q Windows user (ex?) is psyched.  I haven't had this much fun on the keyboard in a looooooooooong time.  Nice to be able to download all the similar programs until you find one thats just right.  Gmusicbrowser rocks!

Just as an FYI - I was pretty lost a couple of times.  But I see you have a nice sticky that probably would have helped my ADD rear end out so not sure how it could be easier.  The info is out there, you just gotta surf for it.  Thanks again.

Aloha,
Eric
