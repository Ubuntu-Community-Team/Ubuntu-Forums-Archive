### Post by seachnasaigh on 2007-11-04
Sorry you feel that way.

I've been using some flavour of Linux since 1999; I was a Solaris and Windows person before that. I've used Linux on servers, primarily, and not as a desktop environment, and been a huge fan. 

I've tried various variants of desktop Linux since then, and been amazed at the progress. It does take some patience; we're looking at an emerging environment, and one that's not wholly corporately supported (as Windoze is), and really, the progress in small and large things has been impressive. I think with the last few iterations of Ubuntu, we're really getting to a Linux desktop environment that can be handed off to the non-geek. Of all of the distros out there, I've been impressed most by Ubuntu, really. I think, were I you, I'd give it another go.
