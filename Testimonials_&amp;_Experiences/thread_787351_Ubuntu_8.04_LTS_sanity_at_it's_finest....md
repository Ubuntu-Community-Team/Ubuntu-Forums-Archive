### Post by rabbitnightmare on 2008-05-08
Well my experiences with Linux have been shotty at best in the past with distros. I love the new 8.04. Been experimenting with Linux for quite some time. Heard of Linux in 1998 and tried it only to be dismayed. Back in '02 with RH9 until now I have ran along side XP. 8.04 I made the jump to Kubuntu and installed the full KDE and did the downgrade to FF2 and couldn't be happier. Windows is only ran partially as I copied the dll's over to wine to get my 3 or 4 needed apps. I loved watching Linux grow. People now are coming to Linux faster than the Niagara Falls can produce water. Microsoft has a huge problem on their hands. But now so do we. What to do with all these new people. Which leads to my next point.
In this next part the list is in no general order but numbered to keep order. I know I have heard it a million times. "Its free so you can't complain."
Few complaints:
1. 8.04 related only: Firefox 3 beta 5 in a LTS release? Why couldn't FF3 wait for 8.10? It doesn't need bleeding edge software for a slated "stable" release.
2. The whole attitude here in this forum when people become so flabbergasted they want to leave, I keep seeing the "Ubuntu is not the only distro, try distro X." For most new users it is Ubuntu or bust. Wal-Mart doesn't send people to K-Mart so why do we as a community do it to them. To a new user this can be insulting and make them not want to stay. Letting them know other options exist is not the insulting part, but really the *buntu's imho are the best that desktop Linux has to offer right now. A lot of people agree with me and point new users here. To them they think if this is the best it has to offer then why does it even exist. For these, as hard as it can be, show tolerance and patience and it will pay off. It isn't that people don't want to deal with a too geeky to be good os, it is they don't want to try 30 different distros before they find the right one for them.
UPDATE:
To those who haven't sent people else where I commend you!
3. Elitist attitude open source but closed minded. (no flamewars on this one please too many exist @_@)
