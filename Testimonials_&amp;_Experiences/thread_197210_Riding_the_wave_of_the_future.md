### Post by Upochapo on 2006-06-15
I will try to make this as short and sweet as possible, but can't make any promises hehehe ;)  .

I have been using computers before there was even an existence of windows, only DOS.  Since then, I have watched windows take the world by storm and eeking it's way into every small corner of the world.  Meanwhile, sitting quietly like a sleeping dragon there was Linux.

I have been a windows user for a long time now.  I have messed with linux before and found the operating system to be quite cumbersome.  That was all before Ubuntu arrived on the scene.  I found out about this distro right on the threshold of the Dapper release.  I expected the usual Linux experience.  Needless, to say, this is not your 'typical' linux experience.

I have installed Dapper on three different systems without a hitch.  Two older systems and one newer one.  All three installations was a breeze.  Everything just worked.  With the exception of installed graphics cards on the older machines that had an onboard graphics card.  This cannot be contributed to the Ubuntu distro as the problem was with the computers themselves.  I.E., couldn't turn off the onboard graphics card.  Suffice it to say, I eventually figured out how to get the good graphics cards to run.

This is the kick in the pants the Linux community needs to be taken seriously and as a real competitive force in the OS market.  That of course, is just my opinion and is open to debate.

I would like to take this opportunity to give the Ubuntu developers/community a great big "THANK YOU!".  You have made it a challenge to find a reason to switch back to windows.  I never thought Linux could be this much fun.

Oh, and currently I'm using the Kubuntu version of Ubuntu, for the record.  Which of course, means nothing, just my personal preference of the KDE desktop.  And with all the great customization you can do with this distro, it looks like it's microsofts turn to play catch up.

Take care.
Upo.
