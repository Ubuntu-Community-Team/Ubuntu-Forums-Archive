### Post by yuku-aki on 2007-09-23
I work at the Wedge Co-Op in Minneapolis, MN, and thought I would make a suggestion to the IT department: If we're all about sharing and support, making the world a better place, etc, then why don't we use open source software in our store?

Well...

Turns out, we do!  The computers in the breakroom (which I failed to notice for a while, just dismissing them as some normal public IE terminals) turn out to be running Ubuntu, with access to all the initially offered software on the CD, and a nice big Firefox logo for those who just want to go online.

I believe M$ products still lurk on some of the machines and equipment, but it's encouraging to know that places like the grocery co-op think to support the flow of free information for all.

Death to copyrights!
