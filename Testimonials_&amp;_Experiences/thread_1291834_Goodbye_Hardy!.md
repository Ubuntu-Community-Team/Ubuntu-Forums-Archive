### Post by coolbrook on 2009-10-21
It couldn't wait.  To make a long story short.  I switched from WEP to WPA security on my router.  I have no idea why my laptop's wireless completely broke. The (hardware) WLAN indicator wouldn't even light up.  It worked perfectly on (the dual-boot partition of) Vista.  I already had a Jaunty Live CD to test behaviour.  The connection to the router worked during the session and a clean install was the most logical, and a painless fix to the solution.  I wasn't about to scale back the network security just to re-connect to WEP and troubleshoot.

I toyed with the idea of upgrading from Hardy directly to the next LTS and so on, but didn't feel like waiting anymore.

N.B.:  If you're running a wireless printer, change its security before you change it on the router.  It's another time-saver.
