### Post by Predator106 on 2008-09-10
What can I say, my experiences with Ubuntu, and Linux since July-ish have been perfect. It just works, that's exactly what it does for me, I did have a little messing around with my sound. I absolutely love the freedom aspect, and am extremely passionate (yes, a fanboy :) ) towards it. At first moving to Ubuntu, I was scared, was trying a dual-boot, am pretty efficient with software, being a very young computer programmer, so that helped. But after it took a while to get used to (a month or 2), I felt quite a bit comfortable with it. I did ALL kinds of customizing, from sounds, extra packs, application-trying-out. Getting things setup, moving files, downloading every good GNOME theme known to man, and I even wrote a bash script to automate the install of said themes, was all the summation of what I have done.

I really love the idea of the OSS, and you won't be seeing me going back to Windows, I still have it there, may boot into it once a month or two, but that's about it.

After trying Ubuntu, prior-to, I kind of liked Microsoft, now I despise them and their bloated OS. I find their Operating System extremely annoying, it purposely gets in your way to warn you about something nobody gives two shits about(security updates). I even had it one time, when I was playing Oblivion, I just installed the updates that it had bugged me over for a few days, and after installing, I choose not to reboot (naturally you have to reboot with EVERY one of their update's), played Oblivion for about an hour, BAM their useless update manager gets in the way and crashes Oblivion (because it minimized it). It got in the middle of my work, to bug me about installing. That is just something that really ticks me off, I don't want to be bugged. Not to mention with Windows, you fire up a few applications and the entirety of explorer freezes.

Oh and I absolutely love their 20 instances of svchost </sarcasm> which is commonly exploited with viruses, because nobody knows how many there should even be, lol.

I feel that Mac OS is the same way, I was on a Mac computer at school (oh, this happens on all the Macs up there), and these computers are good (intel core 2 duo, etc. which I have, as a PC). After starting up Word, Firefox, and trying to log into my server, the entire OS just locked up and all I get is the stupid spinny wheel. After about 20 seconds, I can use it again. Mind you, their menu bar at the top, and bottom also freeze up. I feel it's the same way with other OS's, that they're horrible at multitasking, I understand with Mac's, the hardware is a little different compared to what I have (favoring me in spec's), so the results should be a little different, but I imagine it will still be the same. Not to mention their hardware... the Monitor breaks down, the entire computer has to be shipped back....bad idea IMO...

With any Operating System, I expect to be able to do things while it is working, I don't want to have to wait for it to fire this program up before I can even try to fire up the other program, it should be near seemless, and Linux brings that to me. I also LOVE the feature that Compiz has, to aggressively (or at diff. settings) prevent focus from being stolen. Why would I want to be interrupted in typing something such as this, by the program I fired up, just so that I have it a few minutes from now. I love that ability.

I also hate the way Microsoft always screws people, $200 for an OS...wow, and a pathetic bloaty one at that. The only times they don't charge for things is when they are trying to get good PR i.e. XNA, etc. The Xbox360 is a sorry piece of hardware and Gears of War is the best game it will ever see, it's already maxxed out after about a year or whatever. They charge for their online service. Who wants to do that, they have internet bills, PLUS their microshaft service charges. They built the system horribly, it overheats, and the problem is still not solved. It's a complete lack of freedom, I even heard you had to PAY for themes, the PS3 you can just make your own and pop it on there. Microsoft, before launch (and the heat problems) was going to have a 30 day warranty. WTF is that? I can get a 90 day warranty from Wal-Mart, the former isn't worth anything.

I find Ubuntu smooth, completely customizable, and fun to learn, I love the terminal (even though I am relatively new to it), it's at least 10x better than DOS, which is also terrible. 

I hate Windows, and won't buy anything from Microsoft ever again, hell I won't even pirate anything of Microsoft. Everything I need is in Ubuntu. I'm so passionate in fact, I stopped gaming on Windows altogether, I now use just my PS3(before I did have one, so I used the PC).

I love how Ubuntu is always on-time and has their releases planned out, I can mark it on my calendar. With Windows, they might as well say "it's done when it's done", but they don't... no, instead they say it will be out September and push it back multiple times until it is next November. Microsoft only adds security functionality with updates, Ubuntu actually adds features and bug fixes. I also love the package manager. And with Windows, if you want any new "features", if you can call them that, you have to fork up another $200 for a "premium" version that gets "awesome features!" like Bitlocker... and that's about the full extent of paying another $80 as opposed to the non-premimum. Microsoft patents everything, they even patented the Page up/ Down keys. What the ****! What next, letters A-Z on the keyboard?

With Ubuntu I screw it up, I can fix it, with Windows if I screw it up, it generally ends up in a format. One time I got this nasty-as-hell, gay porn virus that would, when you log in, go into a forced-slideshow mode and make you see all the nasty pictures. Gawd that was stomach-wrenching disgusting stuff. I tried all kinds of things, but it had things as tough to get rid of as DLL injection into WinLogon.exe, which is the starting process of Windows, along with other things. Not to mention trying to customize Windows a little bit, and end up with things like the start menu being blank for an entire minute. Irreparable.

I also find that with Ubuntu, I love having the power of the terminal, able to do all kinds of stuff, it's certainly a good task to do, once learned, it's extremely useful, bash scripts also.

Well, I'm sure I could write more, but I think you get my drift :). I won't even buy a Zune, I use the Apple iPod because it's the closest thing to a big MP3 player (that's small in physical size). Plus I can run Rockbox on it (also, a free, open source media player firmware for many media players([www.rockbox.org](www.rockbox.org)).

I also like the intelligence of everything, it's intelligent by design, not defective by design :). For instance, the file system, Ext3 has not much fragmentation at all, and it's not a worry for people like me (non-media-nuts people :) i.e. HD movies).

Also, there is a distribution of so many flavors, you're bound to find exactly what you like, that's what's great about Linux, it's an excellent kernel, and is a great starting point for distributions. A common anti-Linux argument is "But there are way too many distributions", it's not one-size fits all, do you complain about having too many shoes to pick from, i.e. Nike, reebok, would you prefer one; Would you prefer one size also? No such thing as too much variety, the only thing is when they start to diverge from one another too much i.e. Incompatibility greatly increases, which could eventually be a problem, but I'm sure somebody would point it out (like freedesktop.org did, about something else, I believe).

Not only all this, but with Ubuntu, right when I boot my computer up and log in, it finishes doing it's stuff like right away and the entire thing is quiet, no network spamming, hard drive scratching, or CPU hogging (as it is with Windows). I love how in Ubuntu right when it boots up and logs in, I know that it's ready to go right when I log in, I don't have to wait for the OS to finish what it's doing (which is just plain irony in Windows...). Heck, I don't even have to worry about when my brother is playing his PS3, for it to lag him up or anything because it's hogging the pipe. Like I have played the PS3 before online, and then it got extremely laggy and we were like "what the heck is this from" (because our DSL connection needs to be reset sometimes/needs to have a service request), and then I found out that my mom just turned on her XP computer. Ha, typical, and Vista is even WORSE at that. I don't know what they do, they must send all of your personal information (credit card information, etc.) on top of checking updates and making sure your copy is genuine :) no wonder why so much bandwidth is used, heh.

And with Ubuntu, I am fascinated by the idea if you don't like it, you can improve or change it. You can create your own themes if you want to (which I think I will look at doing, even though I am not good at artistry or creativity/originality (in my opinion)).

I love freedom, and will fight to protect it.

These are my experiences, and opinions, take what'chya will.
