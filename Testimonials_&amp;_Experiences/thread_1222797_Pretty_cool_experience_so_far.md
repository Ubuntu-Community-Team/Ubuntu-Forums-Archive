### Post by s3a on 2009-07-26
I would highly recommend using a *home partition* for everyone. This way if you ruin your system (for any reason including a mistake you could potentially make), a reinstall would not require any backing up of files or anything of that sort, it would be a quick re-installation procedure and it'd be all set with all your files and configuration settings intact.

Make you sure you mount the home partition instead of re-formatting it though on the re-installation. Alternatively, you could use the live cd when you're trying to learn by doing "crazy things."

Basically, if you ruin your system, it is most likely your fault and not Ubuntu's. This is why I suggest home partitions. They've been and still are very useful for me at least. It's when you use root (administrative privileges) that you can damage things if you do something wrong.
