### Post by 1roxtar on 2011-08-19
My brother gave me an older IBM Thinkpad R40 (Released 2005 I believe).  He told me it didn't work and I could have it for parts, if I wanted it.  I never say no to free computers. ;)  

Specs:
Intel Celeron processor 2.0GHZ
256 Megs Ram (Can handle 2Gigs)
20 Gig Hard Drive
Missing Mini-Pci adapter for wireless.
Windows XP

After inspecting the laptop, I found that it had simply had a busted hard drive.  I had an older Thinkpad laying around that still had a working hard drive, so I swapped it out.  Afterwards I added a 1Gig memory stick and Voila! I had me a working laptop. For wireless I am using a Belkin N300 USB Micro adapter (works great with Ndiswrapper).   

I decided to try out Ubuntu 11.04.  It couldn't use Unity, so it fell back to Gnome 2 shell.  I wanted my Unity so I installed Unity 2D.  I couldn't believe how fast it is working.  I went through my usual tweaks...turning off unnecessary start up programs, replaced Banshee with Guayadeque and installed the extra codecs for media. 

I text my brother and he was like, "that's why I gave it to you, cuz I knew YOU would make it work."  This laptop has gotten a new lease in life and unlike having Windows bringing it back to a crawl, I know Ubuntu will keep it working snappy for a long, long time.  Now I plan to get a bigger hard drive, add another Gig of memory and test out Ubuntu 11.10 when it lands in October.
