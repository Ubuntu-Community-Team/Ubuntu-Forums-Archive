### Post by dardack on 2007-06-07
I just want to start off saying, I'm considered the "techy" guy in my circle of friends/co-workers.  But i do know enough to know i don't know anything.  I've messed around with linux since 1996 or somewhere around there.  Was never up to my liking.  Plus i was a big gamer.  I finally had a linux all the time box about 5-6 years ago for MythTV, but of course that's a whole nother ball of wax and not really desktop related.  (BTW that has such WAF, that she can't live without it and prepared the way for what i'm about to write).  So recently I've been hearing a lot of good things about Ubuntu.  I decided to try it out.  I don't want to upgrade to Vista (ever).  And let's just say not every single piece of software should have been on my desktop.  My only concern was I only play WoW and wanted to still be able to do that.  Well I can say that I haven't booted into windows in 4 day now.  I raided kara with vent the other night, and I actually get more FPS in Linux that in Windows (BTW thanks to Sammi for his/her's WoW/Wine howto).  I've actually convinced my wife to blow windows up on her laptop and go with ubuntu ( she only needed web, picasa, and office suite and OpenOffice.org has made great strides that she actually likes it better, just had to make sure it's default save is M$ Office so her friends can open).  So just wanted to say as of right now i'm very happy and don't know if i'll ever boot into windows again.  I hope that the community and companies can keep up and make more drivers avail for linux (no WoW on the laptop cause SIS chipset, anyone got a cheap Laptop with a NVIDIA chipset for sale? hehe)  but just wanted to say thanks for all the hard work and help.  I don't think i've ever had an easier install than with Ubuntu (don't know about Vista never tried, well bought).  And whoever came up with NDISWRAPPER for the wireless cards to work with windows drivers, I seriously thank you.  Made getting my PCMCIA Belkin F5D7010 working without problems.

-MacNean
