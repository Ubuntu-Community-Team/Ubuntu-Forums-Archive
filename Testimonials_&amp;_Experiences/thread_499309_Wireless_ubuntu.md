### Post by Paul820 on 2007-07-12
I have been using Ubuntu for around 3 months now, in that time i had been using a BT Voyager 105 modem connected to my laptop. Now i have got me a new shiny wireless router Netgear DG834G v3 and a WG511 v2 PCI card.  I was not looking forward to setting it up at all after reading all the horror stories about ubuntu and wireless. Well i plugged it in and read the instructions about connecting to netgear website to set all the options. I used the lan cable ( i think thats what it is ) to do this. After setting my username and WEP encryption i logged off and then rebooted Ubuntu. Here we go i thought, this is where all the horror stories come true. When ubuntu had loaded, it asked me for my password for the router, gave it and it connected first time. WOW, i am amazed. Not one single problem. I have just realised i don't even need the PCI card, i have wireless built in. So it gets better everytime. I am glad i moved to Ubuntu those three months ago, everything has just been plain sailing for me.
