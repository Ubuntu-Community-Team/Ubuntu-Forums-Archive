### Post by StarLab on 2009-11-13
Greetings All! :)

Yup! Exclusive Ubuntu for 2 weeks now! And I survived fine with no Wine. ;)

I spent the last year of my life thinking I was in bad need of a hardware upgrade. My poor P4 2Ghz FrankenPuter had seen it's best days, or so I thought. Things ran slow, swap file activity was constant and reinstalls were only a temporary fix to these problems. 

Yup. Need better hardware, right?

Wrong! Turns out I only needed better software. 

Exactly two weeks ago tonight I'd finally had enough. Ubuntu was my first stop on what I thought would be an exhaustive evening of searching and evaluating. I have a small hobby business installing CMS's for startup companies needing websites on a tiny/non-existant budget. I needed to ensure I could carry on without missing a beat. 

(Note: Karmic was only one day old at this point)

Five minutes running the Live CD indicated that yes, I could just pick up and carry on. (Right out of the box I might add!) I migrated to a Dual Boot, then a Native install within 24hrs. My goal at this point was simply to carry on. I gave myself two weeks to either be happy, or to move on. 

Both myself and FrankenPuter are very happy. I really had NO idea my computer actually ran this fast! FrankenPuter barely breaks a sweat anymore and the mark on my forehead from banging it on the desk is healing quite nicely. 

Granted, I'm suffering from the infamous ATI Radeon issue described elsewhere in the forums but I'm not going to bitch about my decade old hardware not being 100% compatible. The default driver installed by Ubuntu works fine for what I need. I'm not much of a 3D gamer so the high end stuff isn't overly important to me. If it was, I'd update my card. ;) I still get the wobbly windows, so that's enough splash for me right now. 

Overall, the move to Ubuntu has been pleasant and enlightening. I haven't yelled at my computer for two weeks now. Previously terms like, "Stupid Computer" "Hurry Up!" "What the #$%^ are you thinking about" rang loudly from my apartment. Neighbors are now knocking on my door to see if I'm okay. :shock:  

People visiting this forum to evaluate Ubuntu should keep in mind that this IS a support forum. A lot of the posts here will be about problems and should not in any way discourage you from taking Ubuntu out for a spin. If your running semi-ancient hardware like me, I think you'll be surprised. 

I think my sig sums it up. In fact, we're buddies now. :) (Pics of us at the beach to follow ;) )

### Post by Dude-PWB- on 2009-11-13
Nice post/thread.

I used to muck around with linux way back when windows 3.1 and 95 were all the rage. Somehow, I allowed myself to get sucked into the Windows craze and other than a few boredom induced weekends, I never really got back to it.

About this time last year, I broke my leg and was stuck at home with an increased amount of boredom. One of my friends dropped by one day to see how I was doing and handed me a Ubuntu 8.10 live cd and told me to check it out.

Well, the rest is history. I liked it so much I actually dug around and slapped another drive in my system and went with a dedicated dual boot system. After a couple months I realized I wasn't even using windows anymore for anything other than a few games that don't play well on wine.

Long story short, about a month before 9.10 came out I had really messed something up with HAL and had trouble logging in and getting the GUI to start. No matter, I toughed it out for a month, downloaded and burned 9.10 and set off for a resurrection.

Since my initial windows drive was actually the larger of the two, I backed up my pertinent info to another drive, and wiped it clean for a complete full install of Karmic. The old (smaller) drive now only has a small partition on it for my windows xp for playing my few games that I still play...nothing else. It's stripped down and locked up and that's all it gets used for.

I have a daughter that is mainly a graphics freak. When I told her that the program she uses for most of her graphics is native in Ubuntu (Gimp) she marched her computer right to me and told me to get rid of windows and install Ubuntu. She hasn't complained about a thing since.

I have a son that is a huge gamer, so he will unfortunately be stuck with windows for a while, but I hold out faith that the linux community is going to finally get a breakthrough with some of the gaming community with regard to linux ports of the more popular and upcoming games.

I can't say I'm as happy with Karmic as I was with Intrepid or Jaunty, but it is still as comforting and interesting as it's predecessors. 

A huge thank you goes out to Canonical, Ubuntu, and the great community out there that supports this operating system. Without it, I'm not sure I could ever show an actual interest in computing as I have over the last year. My experience is still quite limited, but I plan to give and receive as much knowledge as I can to this community while it still allows me to do so.
