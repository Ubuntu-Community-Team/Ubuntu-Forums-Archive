### Post by empcrono on 2008-08-20
My first thing I ever installed was Red hat linux. I did not even get X installed the first time. I had a friend with a Computer science degree and he even knew a little about linux so he said. "Try typing startx or start x or start gui etc etc" even he did not know. Well that marked the end of that for him for me I was determined to have somthing that worked so I went through it all over agian.

The next one I installed was Mandrake back then it seemed like a thousand times better then Redhat but today I think of the two as clones of one anthoer.

Then I tried ubuntu it got me back into the swing of things. It was differnt it was easy it worked. From Ubuntu I went to Kubuntu and then to debian. So I relized that all debian systems for the most part were all easy all worked nice with little need to do much to the system. 

Then I tried Slackware I was told slackware was hard. While I used Ubuntu I never even thought of being a poweruser. I never felt that I was getting to that level. While the way I felt is some what not so true. What I mean is if it were not for Ubuntu I would not have learned so much that I have later took for granted. Still the fact is with Ubuntu you always have your hand held while blindfolded. Slackware did somthing the others did not. Slackware holds your hand in a sense by providing you with the info to get the job done. It takes the simplist apraoch to this that I have ever seen. So all the while you are becoming a power user and are learning to think for your self!!! If you do not know what the "man" command is and you want to really learn linux then slackware is for you. If you never care to understand why something hangs or breaks for no good reason then as far as my exp goes then (K)ubuntu is the best.
