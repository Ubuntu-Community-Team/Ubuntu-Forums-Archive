### Post by armandh on 2009-12-15
here I go again 
previously my son moved up to a PCIe buss faster computer for his gaming and his old 2ghz wound up on my work bench. slower equipment moved to lesser jobs. the problem came when I went to install Ubuntu along side his licensed XP. 

the GTX video card was a no go. in goes a known compatible card and the gtx goes in the parts bin. all is well with the world.

a few months later I think I will try that GTX card again and B4 X even tries to start I get text about available restricted drivers.
I click and in a few minutes wow it just works.

now I think I've done it again
the newly put together family room media player atom/ion itx has a recognized hdmi out sound driver problem. one look at the search and I opt for a hardware solution. from the parts bin again, an adapter box converts SVGA and analog audio to hdmi. presto we are doing 1080p.

much like the wait for the BCM wifi drivers, maybe in the next Ubuntu this atom/ion HDMI audio gets fixed. 

some experiences are like a reoccurring discussion. when the hardware is compatible Ubuntu is desktop and noob ready. when the fix goes on for pages probably not. well set out cut and paste such as medibuntu or the 1 line edit in the x11 file to get the sound working on my mini 9 with 8.04 is about my noobish limit.

for now I've got a hardware work around, and waiting I can do, easily.
