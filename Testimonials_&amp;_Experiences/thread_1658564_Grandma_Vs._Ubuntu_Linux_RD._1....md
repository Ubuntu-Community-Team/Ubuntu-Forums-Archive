### Post by trinitydan on 2011-01-02
So my grandma showed up earlier with a scanner (CanoScan LiDE 60) she found at a garage sale.  I was not too sure what exactly it would take to get it going for her, but I was encouraged when I saw it had a USB cord.  So I plugged it in. Nothing. No noise.  No activity on the screen.  So I thought the best thing to do would be fire up the super easy software center and see if they had a program that might help me out.  Turns out Ubuntu comes with Simplescan installed and I didn't even know since I haven't scanned a document since the '90s. Ok, so fire up simple scan, maybe I can configure it to work with this scanner that was made for Windows and Mac and came with no software.  I hit the scan button and it starts scanning.  Needless to say my grandma and I were very happy and impressed with the capability and device recognition built into Ubuntu 10.10!  I know not everyone with a random scanner gets so lucky, but in this case you can chalk one up for Ubuntu!
