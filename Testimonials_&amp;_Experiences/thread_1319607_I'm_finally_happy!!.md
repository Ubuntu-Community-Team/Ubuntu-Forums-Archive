### Post by sgtmcc on 2009-11-08
This probably is not the correct place to post this and those that read most wont care, and the others will already know what I am saying. 

I have been using Linux around 10 years, started out with Mandrake and was a pretty faithful user of it. Over the years I have tried MANY distros and always went back to Mandrake. Here lately I have been more and more unhappy with Mandriva(the "evolved" Mandrake). The update process was a nightmare, downloads would constantly time out and fail using their aria2c, and more likely than not the drake programs would lock up and not respond unless you left it alone for 10-15 minutes. These are of course my personal experiences on my computers, others will vary. 

Once KDE4 hit I knew I had to find something else. I wont get into the KDE3 vs KDE4 debate. Gnome was a viable alternative but I soon realized I was still having to put up with little quirks and inconveniences of Mandriva. I thought that was what Linux was, a computer geeks way of always having a problem that needed to be fixed. 

A few days ago I tried Xubuntu in order to fix an Eve gaming issue with framerates. It was only supposed to be an experiment. Once I installed it, every problem I had I could google and within minutes have a detailed explanation of how to fix it. The update process is so much nicer than driva. I never could get virtualbox to work on Mandriva, however it installed and ran like a charm, I was impressed. That is just one of my positive comments.:p

The heart of this post is I am actually happy with my computer. Things work, things work good and as expected. I am extremely happy with this distro and wish I had tried it sooner. I still have some things in my head like rpm vs deb but I am already getting the hang of sudo apt-get. Thank you Ubuntu team, Ubuntu geeks on the forums. Again I know this is probably not the right place to post this but I had to say it somewhere. I get to actually enjoy my computer again.

):P
