### Post by Mr. Matt on 2006-11-07
Hey all,

I installed GNOME Dapper this past Saturday and have been amazed with things so far.  Not only are things running generally well but I'm also learning more about how computers work.

The biggest amazements for me have been:

- my Netgear MA101 USB 5+ year old wireless adapter worked straight away!  I had spent hours on the phone with their technical support whenever I changed Windows to get it setup properly
- I've switched to KDE because I was very impressed with the slick look and graphic transitions.  I had disabled all transitions in Windows XP because it slowed down my Duron too much.
- I love the multiple desktops.  It makes organising work so much easier and productive (but I guess this isn't standard just to Ubuntu)
- there's so much choice - which can be a downside at times and frustrating when starting out.  But I'm sure I'll be grateful for it in the future.  Man, one could spend weeks just figuring out all the tips and tricks that are available.
- it was suprising to me just how ingrained I was in my Windows thinking.  I had taken some courses in University where we used UNIX but it was a big shock at times now using it on my primary computer.  I tried to keep away from thinking "why can't it just be like Windows" but it came up at times :)

All in all, it's been a great choice for me to switch over.  I'll be sticking with Ubuntu for a while!
