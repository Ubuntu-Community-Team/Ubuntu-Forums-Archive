### Post by tyggna1 on 2007-08-23
Just as a note:  Battery service life also must be taken into account--a battery that's 2+ years old won't hold as much of a charge as one that is brand new.  Lithium-Ion were designed to be a moderate-power drain rate.  

Here's the likely reason for such a huge power saver:
Most power is eaten up by RAM.  10W per 128mb is a safe power ratio.  Vista idles at 512mb--Linux idles at around...I don't know, but I know it's less than 50.  Already, there, you're using 1/10th the power.  XP has a very slow memory leak (meaning that the memory will get filled up with junk over time).  If you've ever left a windows system running for more than two weeks straight, you've probably noticed a gradual decline in power.  Linux has no memory leak--not even a slow one.  

Because of the chemistry of a lithium-ion battery, the more you drain from it, and the way you do it will effect the over-all lifespan of the battery.  If you gradually use more and more, it's less healthy than a steady stream, or even than a few sudden jolts in power usage.  The slower the change-the worse--with the exception of no change in power draining.

Hence:  Ubuntu will likely make your battery last a month or two longer than it would otherwise, and will definitely increase your battery life if you're switching from Vista.
