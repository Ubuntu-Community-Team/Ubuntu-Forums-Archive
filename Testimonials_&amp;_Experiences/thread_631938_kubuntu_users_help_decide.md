### Post by linuxlizard on 2007-12-05
256 not enough for kde?
Why not?
I put kubuntu feisty on my mother's computer with an amd 1800+ cpu with 256 mb ram and an nvidia card and the thing works great for everything. It's quick, responsive, and even beryl runs fine on it with the cube and 3d effects and emerald. When the grandkids were coming to visit last summer I even loaded it up with a bunch of games - even urban terror runs great on it.

Has gutsy's requirements really jumped that high that fast since feisty?

That said, the guy asked for a comparison-

Your going to find pclinuxos runs faster than kubuntu. Quite a bit faster with lighter requirements. So, that may be the way to go if speed is everything. Dare I say things like codecs and multimedia are going to be easier to set up as well, most likely. 

That said, it's not hard to set them up in kubuntu, and if compatibility is what you are after, kubuntu has many times the number of packages available in the repository and more and more websites have .deb files for (k)ubuntu users when downloading software. pclinuxos is a handful of people who try to appear like they are as large as something like ubuntu or fedora. They have a decent sized repository compared to most, but even so, ubuntu's got more stuff in theirs.

Attitude wise I really respect and enjoy (k)ubuntu's community  attitude. I run pcfluxboxos on my laptop because any ubuntu spinoff is too heavy for it to run with speed (haven't tried fluxbuntu yet though). I really dislike the attitude I get from the pclinuxos community leaders- possibly from the developers on down. If you download their community magazine it's full of negative comments about ubuntu and other os. It's like the kid with a social problem and a chip on his shoulder trying to put others down to build himself up and to me, that just ruins it. It makes their community magazine seem very unprofessional and even unintelligent, and definitely shades the way I look at the distro.

That's just me though.
