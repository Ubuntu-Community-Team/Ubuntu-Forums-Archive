### Post by chefmac on 2006-03-02
I have been a long time Windows user.  Over the last few years I have tried dabbling in Linux but I always ended up getting fed up with issues (wireless, database server, etc) which I could easily resolve on my Windows box.  A couple of months ago I acquired a hand-me-down Dell laptop and thought about giving Linux another go.  A coworker suggested giving Ubuntu a try because he had heard many good things about it.

I installed Ubuntu and within an hour I was up and checking email and surfing the web on my wireless network.  But...... I couldn't just stop there.  I wanted more.  I demanded a WPA secure wireless network and a LAMP development environment using MySQL 5, PHP5 and Apache2.  I had to get stuck in.  I have to admit getting the WPA network working was a b*tch, but thanks to a very helpful poster I was able to get it up.  I also compiled MySQL 5 from source by reading through many of the helpful posts.

Yes, I still have a Windows laptop for Quicken and gigs of Ipod mp3s and digital photos.  But tonight I am on my Ubuntu laptop building my first non-MS based website and listening to my favorite music streams on my WPA secure wireless network.

Baby steps.
