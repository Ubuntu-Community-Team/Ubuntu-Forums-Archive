### Post by DennyF on 2011-04-05
Once upon a time, I had a nice clean interface on 10.10 that made it very easy to get to everything i needed really fast ...
A nice little panel bar at the top where i could set my preferences, do admin chores, launch aps, open folders, drives, networks and install new aps, all within two clicks. I could even monitor the system, monitor the weather, etc. etc -- whatever I wanted ...
At the bottom was a clean customizable dock (Cairo) where I could set up a launchpad with anything i wanted. It had a really cool, unattached block that held 4 squares corresponding to my workspaces -- they were just one click away ...
AND THEN IT ALL WENT TO HELL WHEN I INSTALLED 11.04 WITH THIS UGLY SIDEBAR THAT SOMEBODY DECIDED WOULD BE "EASIER" FOR ME TO NAVIGATE!!
Now it's three, four clicks to switch my workspace ... provided the obtrusive sidebar cooperates and flys out when i need it (most the time, it flys out for a second, then goes back in just about the time i decide what I need to click on to do what i want to do!!
Now my admin chores and preferences are way down at the bottom, below the screen and I have to "fool" the bar into showing me what's there .... more time wasted!
So I decided to disable the Unity interface, restore the Cairo dock -- but guess what, It's real shaky in this release ... and I still cant figure out how to open Ubuntu Software Center (remember, that was in the nice top panel??)
So the moral of this story is ... THIS INTERFACE SUCKS AND WASTES MY TIME ... WHY DON'T YOU GUYS GO WORK FOR MICROSOFT IF YOU WANNA DICTATE HOW I SHOULD USE MY COMPUTER?
This release, although faster in its core, sucks in its presentation and accessibility, ease of use and user friendliness and goes against the whole reason i switched to Linux in the first place.
So, anyone out there have suggestions on a stable distro that'll be easier to use than this? Please let me know.
In my opinion, this is a flawed design that never should have happened. Should be named "Natty Not Worth a Damn"
