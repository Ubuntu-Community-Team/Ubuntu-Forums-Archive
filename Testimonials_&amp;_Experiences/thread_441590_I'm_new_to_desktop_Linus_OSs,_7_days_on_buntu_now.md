### Post by PerlMountain on 2007-05-12
Well, this maybe [size=1](probably)[/size] doesn't belong here so move it if you like, or set it on fire :)

So, we've got about 7 days on Linux OS. My wife is a designer, I'm an admin and our daughter is coming along well, training for both jobs ok.

I am SO sick of 10 years on M$ and though I work in shell a little almost every day on our boxes, but I really did think that Linux (desktops) was run from cmd with text browsers. Well, that's about what I was commited to agree to when I found several Linus desktop OSs last week.

We burned and tried several OSs and kind of settled down on Kubuntu. Since my wife liked Kubuntu and since I don't care, we all decided on Kubuntu so we could keep things more congenial between all of our machines.

Now, we both just reinstalled Xp Pro x64 last night and our daughter is on Xp Pro 32. We have Linux on 3 computers here for testing. Right now, I'm setting up a dually on my laptop and then when that goes well, on my desktop. My wife is next, then my daughter (who's boyfriend has tried every Linux OS out there).

We're still a little new about what we're doing but I feel really comfortable diving right in after a week of messing with it.

I've got to say that this community has done an excelent job with this project. I see stuff every fewf minutes that I'm like "Hey wow, they're thinking of me!"

Just for example: My PC was down for reformat last night and I had to work on something real quick. I jumped on my Kubuntu laptop .. now .. I need PuTTY, let's see if I can get this installed.

Well guess what? I didn't need to install it, you put it there for me to use. I was in the box in 2 minutes :)

So thank you for the tools that are right there .. before I need them.

Maybe I'll be able to go straight Linux one day, if I can find all of the tools I need, because that's what I ultimately want.

My daughter is excited, loves Kubuntu and is already going to help 2 friends set up. 

I set up my 2 computer parts guys yesterday.

A friend is having me build him a new Linux box for his home-office next week. 

I have a client in Cancun who would like me to help him switch his 5 office machines over..

A client in Bangkok is having me help him set up a dually tomorrow OTP.

So, this thing is kinda viral huh? I think we'll get along pretty well. :)
[size=1]*I hope we don't wear out the .iso giving it out to the whole neighborhood.*[/size]
