### Post by wh33t on 2009-11-07
I'm not what you would call an 'avid' linux user. But I have been very interested and kept a close eye on the linux movement ever since Red Hat was the norm. I have tried many different distro's and versions of linux but I have always returned to windows because I haven't yet found that Linux does for me, what I need it too. Here is my review of Ubuntu 9.10, take it for what it's worth.

**The Good**

It installs so fast, and sooooo effortlessly. I also popped the install cd into my laptop and it loaded the test/demo version of Ubuntu and I was ASTONISHED that my wireless card worked and connected right up. I then went onto the web and went straight to youtube to see if the flash player came with it. It did not. But after a few minutes and a few clicks Flash player was installed, and I was streaming video and audio into my laptop effortlessly. The test/demo environment did eventually freeze and I was forced to reboot my laptop, but I was very impressed. So impressed that I chose to grab a spare 30Gb drive I had laying around and install it on my pc with a fresh copy of Ubuntu.

**The Bad**

The bad really isn't so bad. It's just more like... minor-medium annoyances. The system booted up as expected, I logged in and everything was good. I turned on some graphical enhancements and played with some of the settings, nothing too extravagant. And then some strange things happened. 

I was setting up evolution to use IMAP from Gmail and for the life of me I couldn't find a way to change the port numbers from the default in Evolution. Then Firefox stopped responding and I couldn't for the life of me figure out how to force it to shut down... simply right clicking on the task bar and choosing close did nothing, I even waited for a minute or so.

So I logged out, and back in and that seemed to take care of the Firefox hanging problem. Then I played with some taskbar settings (like turning on translucency etc), I set the translucency back to default and then my task bar didn't stretch across the full width of my screen, it was now centered at the bottom. So I right clicked it to play with the preferences again and to my surprise the Preferences option was gone. 

**My Conclusion**

I have now taken my linux drive out and thrown my Xp drive back in. A part of me is sad that with in the first 15 minutes of using Ubuntu I discovered so many things that challenged me and made me feel like I had to head to these forums to ask for help. I definitely think Ubuntu is now leading the Home Linux movement, and I totally think it's going in the right direction. But for me, sadly, it is not quite there. There seems to be too many things I am used too seeing and using in Windows for the switch to Ubuntu to feel comfortable. 

I just wanted to say "Great Job Ubuntu team" and to personally extend a thank you to all those involved in developing it. I welcome any comments or suggestions or questions from the community.

Happy Linux'n
