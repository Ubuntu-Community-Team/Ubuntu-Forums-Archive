### Post by Gvarelov on 2009-05-04
I have been playing around w linux for years, but have never felt comfortable enough to part from my win machine. However, after finding out about the Wubi installation option when installing Jaunty, I decided to give it it a try. No need to burn a disc, just download a file from the net and install it just like any other file you download. It looked almost too good to be true.
To my utter amazement, it went absolutely flawless. What was even more amazing, the wireless USB stick was recognized correctly and immidiately, my USB printer was also seen and used, video card as well. Having played w linux before, I must say this was the smoothest installation ever. I was up and running in minutes. On linux.
Here are the hardware details, to give you a hint at how dated this machine really is:
-AMD Duron processor 1.2 GHz (AMD discontinued Duron more that four years ago)
-NVIDIA GeForce2
-1GB DDR RAM
-2 HDD, one around 40 gigs, the other nearly 80 gigs.
-19" flatscreen Envision
-USB 1.1
I have put this PC together 7 years ago. 
Kudos, cheers, hugs and a big round of aplause for folks that put the effort into Jaunty and Wubi. Wubi is a great idea! :guitar:
