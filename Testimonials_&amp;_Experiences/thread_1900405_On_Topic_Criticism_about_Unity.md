### Post by Old Jimma on 2011-12-26
Hi Ubuntu Community:

I've noticed that there is alot of criticism about the Unity Desktop. Some of it is constructive... but alot of it is downright rude, in my opinion. There are a few things that Ubuntu users should always bear in mind. 

**Ubuntu is Free.** One thing that Ubuntu users must fix in their minds is: Ubuntu is free. Over the years, I've read the explanation about what the linux philosophy means by "free." I'm such a very old, wizened, and crotchety geezer that the only thing I understand is that I don't dip into my pocket to pay for Ubuntu! 

A major problem with looking at it this way is that people who become accustomed to getting something for nothing over a long period of time  [LIST]
[*]become dependent on it,
[*]take that good for granted,
[*]feel that they are entitled to it, and
[*]feel no responsibility in sharing in the maintenance of that freedom.
[/LIST]

How long do you think such a good can exist with many people sharing this view? Share in the responsibility of the development and maintenance of the free thing you value, or loose it!

**Knowledge advances because of innovation and things change.** Over the past 20 years, knowledge and inovation has lead to the Apple iPhone, and Apple iPad. Garnering acceptance of innovation isn't easy. Sometimes people aren't ready for it. Remember the Apple Newton? I'm old enough to remember when most people thought that anybody who worked with computers must be cold, heartless people with no milk of human kindness in their veins! Well, things have changed, haven't they?

**Ubuntu is on the right track.** Regardless, the touch-screen user interface is the wave of the near-future. I, for one, was delighted by innovation that led to the extinction of the Hollerith punch card. I'm not going back, either! :wink:

**Get involved.** If you want things to go your way, get involved. It is that simple. You can't expect somebody to act on your behalf. Be a part of the Community, help out in the forums in one way or another, and be a part of the solution!

We'll it is the end of another year, and as always I express my appreciation for Ubuntu. It works for me and I'm grateful for it!

Sincerely,
Phil de Duluthistan

### Post by cwklinuxguy on 2011-12-30
Here's the thing: I do not think that innovation is necessarily a bad thing--it's a necessary thing for a vibrant, healthy, and evolving software project such as Ubuntu. However, you can't make a MAJOR change with almost no transitional phase and expect that everybody's going to be quiet about it. There are going to be some early adopters, there are going to be some people that won't adopt it as quickly, and there are going to be some people that will never adopt it. The problem is that I feel like Ubuntu has developed the Unity interface largely based on their desire to be able to run on touchscreens. Yes, most tablets are incapable of running Ubuntu, but that's due to a lack of support for the devices, not that the interface doesn't work. To my way of thinking, this interface belongs on a tablet, and when you try to use it on a desktop platform it's just not well suited. I think THAT is the core of this problem. That said, I am not per se a fan of Unity, and I don't like how the community has been shut out of the process to a certain extent, but it is an interesting endeavor and it may eventually be worth considering as a main interface for my uses. For now though, I'm a Gnome 2 and LXDE user.
