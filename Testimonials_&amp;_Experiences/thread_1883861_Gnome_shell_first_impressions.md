I feel the same way. I jumped ship to Kubuntu just before Natty was released and stayed there until a few weeks ago. I'm now trying both Unity and Gnome Shell during my week off work. Unity works once you get used to it but is prone to crashing. Gnome Shell seems much more solid and reliable but I can't get used to the fact that I'm not presented with a continuous display of which applications are running. I see lots of people here leaving due to Unity but not many ever saying they've tried Gnome Shell. May be they consider them both to be too similar when to me they feel quite different.

### Post by PaulW2U on 2011-11-23
> **pete04 said:**
> And I miss the minimize/maximize buttons.

These can be put back by going into Advanced Settings and changing 'Arrangement of buttons on the titlebar' to 'All', you'll find this option on the Shell tab.

If you don't have Advanced Settings then you will need to install gnome-tweak-tool. This was one of the first settings I changed.

### Post by Roasted on 2011-11-26
> **werewolves said:**
> The only thing that bugs me about Gnome-Shell is the message tray.  If you aren't sitting at the computer when a message comes up, it's easy to miss notifications.

Double edged sword there, because some people prefer the lack of being "bothered" with continual notification messages, whereas I'm with you and I would like some way to be notified about them. It'd be nice if we could have a reoccuring popup, like if I get a new instant message and I stepped away and missed it, it'll at least re-popup in 60 seconds or whatever I set it to. If this feature exists, let me know, but this type of feature is one I'd really like.

Quick tip: Some of you may be like "well duh" when I post this but I only discovered this a matter of days ago which makes using any DE much easier, including Gnome Shell. If you single press on the scroll wheel on the title bar of a window, that window gets backgrounded. This works on laptops too with a 3 finger "tap". This really negated me missing the min/max buttons. Why do I need to minimize anything if I can just center-click to kick that window out of the way, and why do I need to maximize anything if I can just drag a window to the left/right/top of screen?

I hated it at first, but now I see the light. I'm still up in the air about what to use as my default DE. I like Unity a lot, but Unity's dash bothers me. In GS, if I click Applications, I see EVERYTHING in one shot, whereas in Unity I see 3 tiers. Recent apps, Installed apps, and Apps available for download.

Uh. No. Just give me everything in 1 shot. I'll filter it if I see fit.

I also have to wonder what the future is with Unity and Gnome developers. I hope they play nice. I would love to see solid GS integration into upcoming Ubuntu releases just as much as I'd like to see Unity on Fedora or OpenSUSE. I can only hope that everybody plays fair and keeps one goal in mind: We're all in this together.
