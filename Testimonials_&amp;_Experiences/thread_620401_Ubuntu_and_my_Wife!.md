### Post by ARhere on 2007-11-22
I wanted to take a breath and give my testimony.  I have been using M$ products since the DOS days of 1981.  I grew up as a DOS Prompt boy and aways feel comfortable with a black screen and letters!

In my house, my wife and I each have our own PC, a shared laptop, and a file/print server.  Up to this point they all have been running Windows 98/ME/2K/XP, whatever.  I know it would be good for my carrer to learn more about Linux as all the *cool* engineers seem to be making hardware now that runs Linux instead of home grown code.

I tried Linux for a while, Red Hat 8/9.  It took a VERY long time to figure out how to do the most basic stuff and the temptation to just reboot into Windows was almost unbearable.  I finally gave it up when my Red Hat 9 server crashed, and in an attempt to recover my hard drive, all data was lost.  It was at that point I threw in the towel and just figured Linux was not for me.

Six months ago, I read on [/.]("http://slashdot.org") about Ubuntu and how it has suddenly gained popularity over Fedora Core.  Just for fun I downloaded and burned a live CD and tried it out on my laptop at home.  I was **amazed** how it **just worked** and I was able to jump onto the internet and perform basic admin with tasks with the built in gnome tools.  I have never seen a Linux that I did not have to put a pot of coffee on just to get all the hardware working.  I started playing with it more and soon ran into trouble with xorg.conf and closed source ATI driver.  I thought it was over at that point, but I was amazed that the recovery mode was....well.... **useful**.  All PC&#347; have problems, and with windows, if the driver or GUI tools did not work, I was usually SOL!  It was frustrating and a lot of work, but **never** did I hit a point were I hit a brick wall with Ubuntu.  If I could not find a man page, and no one wold answer my post, then it was really easy to search through the forums and find someone with a similar problem with a fix.  So far, my opinion when I hear a person POéd at Ubuntu, it seems like they get in too much of a rush and don't RTFM, or a piece of hardware they want does not have good Linux support, neither of which is Ubuntu's fault.  (*it is Ubuntu's responsibility to make this better I would agree, but not Ubuntu's fault*)

I have now given my wife a new Dell PC with Ubuntu loaded, and I was amazed.  She **liked it!**  The install automatically identified her iPod, camera, scanner, and printer and finding programs for her to use was really easy.  Personally I like Banshee far better then iTunes, and so does my wife.

I had to reload Windows XP on my game box just recently and something hit me like a ton of bricks!  When I finally got WinXP loaded, i sat there and stared at a blank OS with no useful software and a device list FULL of stuff that I needed to download new drivers for.  I still need to reload a text editor, remove addware, and configure the browser.  It hit me that all this time, Windows has been far more work while offering a whole lot less, but I have always put up with it because I did not think there was a choice.

I truly believe that if everyone that complained about Ubuntu made a list comparing the problems with Ubuntu and Windows, they would see that Windows was far more troublesome but have just gotten used to those problems.  For me, when I stared at a fresh install of Windows verses Ubuntu, there was absolutely no comparison.

The future is definatly with Ubuntu.  What is needed now is to get a major organization with muscle ***WINK WINK GOOGLE!!!!!*** to promote Ubuntu so more third party people make support for the OS, and M$ will slink away to its XBOX.  

I am not a Windows hater as it is a good OS as well, I find M$ business ethics lacking and competition is _always_ good for the customer.  Look at Intel vs. AMD, NVidia vs. ATI, and the hard drive market.  If you like Windows, you should support Ubuntu to make M$ work harder with their product, and not just their lawyers!  :)

Sincerely,
A full Ubuntu house!  -ARhere
