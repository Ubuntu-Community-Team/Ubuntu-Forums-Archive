### Post by cro_409 on 2010-08-20
I have tried probably a dozen times in the last few years to designate an entire computer to Ubuntu, but every single time I try that I experience a problem that is so devastating that I can't find a reason to tie up a computer with it.

It's a lot like my relationships, there is always that gorgeous girl that you have always dreamed of being with, and you keep trying and trying, but in the end, you always end up going back to the simple, so-so attractiveness, and functionality that is Windows.

I know a lot of you absolutely hate Windows, probably more than I hate Mac, which anyone who knows will tell you is difficult. But in the end, Windows has better support and in my experience fewer problems. Of course, I grew up on Windows, but I hate the fact that nearly every thing I want to do on Ubuntu takes 20 to 30 minutes of research to ensure it is done properly and that the right codes and procedure is used. I like the Download, Save, and Apply features on Windows. It's simple. I hate having to Terminal, Run, Terminal, write code, Extract, Terminal things that I could sometimes Right-Click in Windows. Granted, Windows has to be babied a lot, you can't go on a downloading spree and expect not to get any viruses, and it needs cleaning all the time to run quickly. A messy Ubuntu boots faster than a brand new Windows, but it doesn't matter when your system crashes every 5-10 minutes requiring a hard reboot with a finger to the power button.  

Originally I loved Ubuntu because of the massive amount of customization. But whoops, all of a sudden Log-On Screens, Password Prompt Screens, Splash Screens are all gone. Don't tell me it's for simpler use. The people capable of more complicated things should still be able to do those things. I can see where this is going. As soon as Ubuntu and Linux start *taking away* features, they are no longer open-source. Sure it's still free, but that feels like the OS is going into lockdown. I promise you this, Linux, you take away the customization, and you will lose a very very important part that looms over Window's head.

As for me, I feel compelled to crawl back to Windows, despite the fact that every part of my being wants to stay with the gorgeous Ubuntu, but in reality, I can see that right now, Ubuntu has no potential to help my future, other than bringing her around and making my friends jealous and looking pretty.

Maybe in the future, Ubuntu and I can come to an understanding once she has a little more maturity and experience.
