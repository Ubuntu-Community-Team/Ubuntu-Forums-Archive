"I was a windoze user, I spent years learning that OS dude, like I was a geek extrodonaire with like super powers and everything, but like you know, they made windows suck with Vista so I wanted to use Linux but it was like hard all that stuff and there was a terminal and all and like dude the six hours I spent trying to learn it was hard, so I am going back to windows. As windows becomes less and less functional in a modern age with its relentless attempts to adhere to digital copy protection schemes that make simple file copies slow to a crawl and as the world moves more and more to netbased (netbook) computing that either uses an abandoned MS OS or Linux, the number of users that have become OS agnostic and simply function oriented grows.  My daughter dosn't care what the OS is, she cares what functions she can get out of the OS.  These are the Linux users of today and tomorrow.  The first class of user mentioned above above may never be Linux users and simply want windows without all the bloat and I suspect they are simply doomed to endure it.  

I guess I am tired of the comparisons and the complaints.  Yes, Linux is not windows and yes some of the people you bought hardware from don't want to see an open OS take root and therefore they don't help make our OS support your hardware.  Either throw away the offending item and buy one from a company that has a clue or move on.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-06-06
I recently installed Ubuntu 8.04 on a neighbors super slow Windows machine (he's a retired senior) and since that time he's been learning how to use it. He's figured out how to e-mail (including attachments), internet activities, a money program, photo manipulation, etc. He's excited at the speed and performance, not to mention free applications.

Having used Windows all his computing life, this was a tremendous step and it appears he's advancing rather quickly. He wasn't an IT guru or required to use a computer in his career. He didn't go to Community College or any other school and take courses on how to. He simply had an open mind and sat down to learn the new OS.

Granted, I installed it (without problem). But when put in perspective he didn't expect the new OS to be Windows. Now, I doubt you could force him to put Windows back on his system. Every time I see him he's got a new question and it's getting tougher to give him a quick answer. I did not get the opportunity to spend gratuitous amounts of time helping him learn, as we left for a trip shortly after installing the OS. I was amazed at how far he had advanced upon my return.

If a retired senior can sit down and learn the OS in a matter of days, why all the complaining? What is it that inspires some to believe that Ubuntu is beyond their capability. Is it that they expect Ubutnu to be a Windows clone or perhaps expectations of Windows like activity impacts their ability to comprehend. I realize (for some) complacency is an issue. However, I also suspect there exists an attitude of - if it requires more than a few minutes of my attention span - it must be junk. As in instant gratification. Some even exhibit an attitude of self righteous indignation. As if the OS should have been designed specifically for their system and/or compuing habits.

Whatever the motive, I doubt seriously that they come here seeking help. But rather seeking quick and simple resolution and should they not receive it - Ubuntu must be garbage. For it could never be the result of their own ineptitude.

I'm 58 years old and have been using Ubuntu for about 3 years. I'm not an IT person nor do I have a back ground in computer science. No one helped me install, learn or identify issues in Ubuntu. I did not post many questions to this forum, as most were readily available by performing a simple search. I do understand that some systems and applications struggle with Ubuntu. But it's been my experience that this is the exception not the rule. Perhaps, that is why I have little sympathy for those that come here only seeking that which they expect to find. Instead of accepting that which is different. Our minds will adapt if given the opportunity.

I refuse to argue or become involved with those types of testimonials anymore and have learned that it's simply a waste of my time. For some (it seems), only wish to complain and demean that which they can't or won't spend time learning. 

Just my $0.02
