### Post by hotrod6657 on 2008-08-18
I'm a college student and every time i come home it's the same thing.  The computer my parents use, an aging 3.2 ghz HP pavilion running XP, has slowed down significantly, it's showing odd error messages, it takes about 5 minuets to boot up, the whole 9 yards.

Now, for ages I've been more than happy to run the spyware and adware removal software and search Window's forums for solutions to their problems.  I had played with Ubuntu quite a bit before but always on my hardware.  I had also made a few attempts to completely switch but usually came back  to windows for one reason or another.  

That was until recently.  I decided to build my first computer this summer and figured "hey, why not give Ubuntu another shot for real this time."  It all began when i started to get frustrated that my fresh Vista install was giving me problems...  That's a good way to annoy people and make them second guess their computer building skills...  haha  So, i executed the triple boot and haven't left Ubuntu for probably a month or so.  I might as well free up that extra space on my HD.

Spending much of the summer on Ubuntu and growing tired of the Windows box sitting in my parent's house I had two options.  1.  Do a factory reinstall of XP to take care of all the things that are bound to get messed up after years of service or 2.  See if i could get my parents to make the switch to Ubuntu.

The real kicker is knowing that XP will loose support from Microsoft soon.  Not angry about that in the least, it's been a great OS and always served me well.  That being said it lacking support really left us with three options.  Keep XP and prepare for the onslaught of trouble associated with using something that doesn't get regular security updates, upgrade to Vista(which would mean getting a new computer because the pentium D just doesn't need to try and run Vista), or switch to Ubuntu.  I can't bash Windows at all on this machine (the majority of the errors are user problems ;))  It's really served us well considering I haven't had to fix anything major in, in think, 4 years.  Not too shabby but its time has come and Vista is out of the question.

Neither of my parents are terribly computer savvy.  They manage, but I wouldn't expect to see them doing anything more than office documents, web surfing, and a little online radio streaming.

I threw Ubuntu on an old laptop we had sitting around and showed it to my folks trying to get a "yay" or "nay"  I had to verify that a few things would work, namely a quickbooks service my mother uses that proved to be a bit of a pain.  Longs story short, it wanted Internet Explorer to access the page, no problem right?  Just install user agent switcher and I'm good right?  No, it also insisted on having ActiveX running so IE4Linux is it :)

That all checked out so this afternoon I set them up to dual boot.  I set a very fast timeout on the bootloader screen to help curb any desire to pop back in to windows.  I wanted to be sure that getting back to Windows isn't too hard though because if something were to happen while i'm at school I want to get them up and running without much hassle (and in that event i think they'll be more comfortable with Linux if i can keep their down time minimal)

Everything is installed and configured as of right now and I added a dock and a very cool but simple background image.  Visual effects are on "easy on the eyes" because i think the cube might be too much for them haha.  Frankly it looks stunning, especially considering the rather blah and ordinary look of XP that they were used to.

So far they seem to like it.  They're not nearly as impressed or excited about stuff like the visual effects or the dock as i am but none the less they like it.  I'll try and post a screenshot of the desktop tomorrow when i'm back on it.

I just wanted to post this as a success story and to say thank you to the whole community.  This community has had a hugely positive impact on my Linux experience and if it weren't for that I certainly wouldn't have set them up with it.

Thanks again to everyone.  I'm glad that the install went without me needing to post any problems on here but having the community behind me is the most reassuring feeling in the world.

Happy computing,

hotrod

PS sorry if it's a bit long, but it's a lot of information to try and squeeze in to one post.
