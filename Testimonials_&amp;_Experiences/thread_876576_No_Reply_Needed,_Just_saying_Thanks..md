### Post by Blonddeeni on 2008-07-31
Gidday.
Not having used computers in any great detail for 8 years, and before then it was always microsoft, which I disliked, I'd like to say "THANK YOU" to Linus Torvald, and **The Ubuntu Team especially**.
At the beginning of this year I decided to have crack at it again, and am now running a variation of Ubuntu on three seperae machines.
Mythbuntu Gutsy dual booting knoppix on a m2npv-vm 1Gb ram 500G hdd amd 64 5200. (Think it has faulty ram - see kmalloc error)
Hardy (dual booting knoppix) on a Dell Latitude D800.
Hardy (dual booting knoppix) on a ... wait for it ... PIII 450 Mhz 20G running an **old** bios. (Cracks me up that one.:D)

The only real problems I've hit have been trying to get dual displays working on the m2npv-vm Mythbuntu machine, (and the Dell), and trying to login via samba to Hardy.

Any other problem has been self inflicted due to "Making It Run The Way** I** Want".

So although I've been frustrated to the point of wanting to smash the screen and declaring jihad on the entire computing world, after some food and sleep, usually found a solution to the problem. Which brings me to the three big strengths of Ubuntu, and indeed all linux.

The support.
The dedication of the development team.
The ability to get in via the command line and get dirty. (Usually do a partimage via knoppix of the system's partition first though).


So Cheers and Thanks Amigos.
