### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-03-23
If it's stability you're looking for, and it's Ubuntu you want to use because of it's vast repositories and compatibility with *most* (but certainly not *all*) hardware, then I suggest you stick to the LTS releases. I'm enjoying Xubu 10.04 (Lucid) to the utmost. Starting with 10.04, the LTS releases are built from Debian Testing (the others in between LTS releases are built from Debian Unstable). This means the stuff that Lucid was built from soon became Debian **Stable** (and Debian Stable is as stable as it gets). The next release of Ubuntu being tested right now, is also a LTS release. By the time Lucid reaches end-of-life, Precise will be as rock-stable as Lucid is now.
