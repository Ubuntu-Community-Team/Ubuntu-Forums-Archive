### Post by knowledgeispwr on 2008-04-24
There are so many negative threads in this forum, so I'd thought I add a positive one.

I decided to try Ubuntu last year after computer issues forced me to reinstall everything.  By early this year, I was using Kubuntu Gutsy Gibbon, as I prefer KDE and a RAM upgrade makes everything super fast on my nearly 4-year old Thinkpad R51.

I decided a couple of days ago to upgrade my system to the Hardy Heron RC, thinking there should be little change between it and the final release, and to beat the rush on actual release day.

My upgrade, using adept, went off without a hitch and didn't take too long at all.  Everything still worked after the upgrade except for Vmware Player (I run XP in it), which was quickly remedied.  I have more up-to-date software, some of which are faster than before.  I wanted to dedicate more space to Linux, so by using a GParted Live CD, I shrank and expanded the appropriate partitions with no trouble.  Kubuntu Hardy gave me no complaints afterward.

So now, I'm a happy Kubuntu Hardy Heron user, with no issues, everything running fine.
