Just a quick note to say how Fantastic linux really is. I returned to XP for a while, and all was well, I would have stayed quite happily. But The Apps On Linux! People say they drive away. No, I submit that, once tried, they really have no match.

It is rich. Like velvet, Audacious, unobtrusive yet unflappable, sits in the corner. My custom icons and docs shine, watched by conky. Chrome is open on Ubuntuforums, and as I zoom out, emacs, OpenOffice, VLC and a myriad of others flash past, one great passion after another.

Oh where to begin!

Last week I had 3 HDDs fail, on 2 computers.  They were all old so wasnt a surprise.

in about an hour and a half I had both systems up and running Ubuntu.  My Wifes was easy I plugged in the HDD from an Everex laptop and Bobs your Uncle she is up and running.  mine was a bit more difficult, I had to shift files around to clear a hard drive to use.   Once that was done  I was up and running in about 45 minutes.

The rest was a bit more time consuming.  Consolidate back up files from 2 drives to 1 160 Gb drive after doing rescue and rejuvination on it.  BTW i do recommend the Heirens boot CD.  has great tools on it.

Onward.  I have a 120gb HDD i need to set up dual.  Much as it pains me i have two apps i have found no replacements for, both of course corel apps.

Install winblows ( 2 hrs) run NSW, defrag (1 hr) reboot w/ ubuntu live CD
winblows has knocked off an additional 7.5 GB partition for god knows what
no recoverable.   Gparted will not format new partitions. try preformatting, no go.

Nothing will format new partitions on this drive after winsucks is installed.  Preformatting results in winsucks wont/cant read last segment install terminating.

end result 3 days later, install Ubuntu on 120 Gb drive with 40 gb system partition 2gb swap and remaining as /home.  install winblows on old 20 gb drive so i can throw it away asap.

and to top this all off I dont have email in windows because i refuse to use the windows virus detecting app ( that is virus detecting in that it gets them all)and I have yet to discover how to change ports in thunderbird or incredimail.

in a recent post i read that somebody thought that we Linux users wanted our distros to be replacements for windows .  I am content to let windows be the best , most effecient virus detection software available.  it hasnt missed one in years.

For my self I will dump it just as soon as i can.
