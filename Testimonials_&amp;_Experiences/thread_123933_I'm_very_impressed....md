### Post by ectospasm on 2006-01-31
I had heard a lot about Ubuntu Linux, and I had been dying to put some form of Linux on my three-year-old IBM Thinkpad R32.   So I decided to try it.  First I booted off the LiveCD to see if the hardware detection and everything else would work.  Everything I needed (display, mouse, wireless connection) worked out of the box (I'm not sure if sound was working since I had the sound turned off).  After playing with it for a few hours I was satisfied;  I decided to take the plunge and wipe Windows off the hard drive completely.  I even deleted the IBM restore partition,  which probably was an unwise move, but anyway....  Installation of the full system went off without a hitch, and even sound worked without any manual configuration.  The only thing that I needed to configure manually was setting the SSID of the WAP, since there are a couple of WAPs in the area and at least one of them doesn't work. 

There is one very minor issue, but I wouldn't call it a deal breaker.  Suspend (or sleep mode) doesn't work, but hibernating does.  I also don't know if the onboard wired ethernet controller works, but I suspect it does.  One thing I can't really test is the presentation display, so if I ever get a chance I'd like to try it out.

The biggest plus with this is that, at least for now, I'm not the primary user of this laptop.  The primary user is my 87-yr old grandfather.  All he needs is an internet connection, an e-mail client, and a web browser.  Ubuntu fills those more than adequately with the working Prism drivers, Evolution, and Firefox.  I'm stoked that I've just made my grandfather into a new Linux user!

I do have some questions, which are probably better answered elsewhere...
