### Post by Tweedicus on 2007-04-21
My 57 year-old mum is switching to Ubuntu next month. Why?

Because she's fed up of her brand new (albeit low-end) Dell laptop slowing to a crawl. She's fed up of having scan for spyware and viruses using three different programs every month. And, significantly she's not used to Windows.

One of the things you read on Absolute Beginner Talk a lot is along the lines of: "why can't Ubuntu do this like Windows?" or "why can't I just double click on a file and it will install?" etc. People often seem to confuse usability with familarity. That is, Windows might seem easier if you're new to Ubuntu, but only because your mind has been trained since the age of six to use Windows. Now, if you'd been trained to use Linux from the age of six...

So, as my mum came to computers late in life and fairly recently she was never programmed by Microsoft to use Windows and get used to its commands. And as such she finds Ubuntu just as easy, if not easier.
