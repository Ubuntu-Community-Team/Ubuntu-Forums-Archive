### Post by pitanu on 2008-11-19
I installed Ubuntu 7.10 on my children's computer about a year ago, though I still left them the option to dual boot, but after playing with Ubuntu they never turned back. My two children of 7 & 9 years say that "Windows is slow like a turtle". 

I also tested Ubuntu on one of my office PC's round about the same period,  though all other machines were still running Windows XP, I wasn't ready to commit to Ubuntu just yet because of concerns about losing my email records plus the software needed for  my business, eg. Dreamweaver, Adobe Photoshop, etc. 

One day out of the blue I finally called it quits for Windows, backed up everything onto a disk, installed Ubuntu on my notebook and copied all my files back on to the hard drive. Of course there were some issues, for example it doesn't recognize my onboard web cam, but in total honesty I haven't searched for a solution as it isn't a priority issue for me. One major concern was the Wifi, Ubuntu couldn't find my wifi card which was a major pain ... a notebook without the mobility of wifi!  But this was a problem other's were facing and therefore it was quickly dealt with via a regular update. 

As for alternative software, I found Gimp to be more then what I needed and it was way faster then Photoshop. Open Office I would say is more intelligent then MS Office, though Quanta Plus can not compare with Dreamwever (yet), but I've learned to live with it for now.  There is a short learning period for everything that comes with Ubuntu and I see no problem with people making the move, even  my wife who's computer knowledge is limited found that it wasn't a problem making the move which I had forced upon her because her PC was struck by a virus. 

Our company is 100% on Open Source, even our website which sits on a Linux server, whilst running Joomla for CMS, back of the house is powered by Ubuntu and Open Office.  There is no turning back. I've put links and banners on my website, and will continue to promote Ubuntu amongst friends, at the same time I will look into possibility of promoting it in schools in Thailand.   

I would like to thank everyone involved in the open source community for giving us an excellent software.  

Pitanu Boonyaratvej
All Travel Network Ltd, Pte.
Bangkok, Thailand
[http://www.bangkokian.com](http://www.bangkokian.com)

ps. I feel so so sorry for those who paid money for Windows Vista!
