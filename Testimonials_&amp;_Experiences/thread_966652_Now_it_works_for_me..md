### Post by WASHAD on 2008-11-01
Long time subscribers to this forum might remember a post that I made about a year ago. It was titled "Ubuntu - it just doesn't work". In it I detailed my woes as a new user in trying to get the simplest things to work. I suggested that if I, a fairly experienced PC user, couldn't get it to work, what hope did the everyday user have?

Needless to say, that post got a lot of response. Some took it for the contructive feedback that it was meant to be. Others, well - not so much. 

Anyway, I just wanted to say that I've returned to try the newest release, 8.10. I am really impressed with the changes that have been made. Finally, I've been given the drivers that I need to make my wireless work out of the box; I can see my PC files from within Ubuntu, my graphics card works as it should, and I can see the same web pages that I could from my PC. 

I've even noticed that more applications are coming out that support both Linux and PC. 

The Ubuntu team has really done a good job over the last year or so. My congratulations to you. 

SteveJ
