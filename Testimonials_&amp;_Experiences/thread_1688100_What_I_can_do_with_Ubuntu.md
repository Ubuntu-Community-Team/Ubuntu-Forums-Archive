### Post by jumpgroup on 2011-02-15
Or "How I Learned to stop worrying about Windows"

First, I want to thank both the amazing work of the Ubuntu Developers and most useful contributors to these forums for the new obsession that's taken over my life the past 4 months.  Here's what you've helped me put together:

Running on a Dell Studio 15 i7 8GB 640GB - wiped Windows on day one.  Ubuntu loaded and took one reboot to find the video card and wireless adapter drivers.  So far so good.

- Fast, excellent, Smooth OS
- Highly customized the desktop with Compiz.  Windows looks and acts paltry in comparison.  All my friends envy the "Cube" and multi workspaces.  Got rid of the bottom status bar and replaced it with Docky.  Moved the few things I needed to the top bar (multi-workspace, multi window, trash)
- Set up a fileserver on an older laptop with two 1.5TB USB drives (one for backup, copies nightly using LuckyBackup), which I use to backup all my other machines and store music and video files
- Set up a LAMP server (with one click!) and loaded Ampache, which streams my entire audio/video collection anywhere - even to my phones! (Palm Pre has the best app.  Android has a couple as well) 
- I am not a musician, but I can play like one with Linux Multimedia Studio.  I'm in the middle of a music battle with my son (who has some paid software on his Windows box) and I'm kicking his butt!
- Sure, I have to use Windows sometimes, mostly for work.  So I loaded Sun (Oracle) VirtualBox.  Now I have Windows 7 64 bit, Windows 2008 R2 Server, Windows 2003, and Solaris 10 running one physical machine.  Note you also need Win/IE for NetFlix cause of the Evil DRM monsters.
- I'm a photography nut, and with a little practice, I've been able to do just about everything in Gimp I was used to doing in Photoshop (with the Raw converter plugin).  Pleasantly impressed.

Really, I could keep going.  These are just the highlights.  Using the 64 bit version of Maverick, I did have some initial issues with connecting wirelessly to my Canon i860 printer/scanner, but that's all resolved (proprietary drivers are evil).

My next effort will be to figure out how to stream all my cable video throughout my network for a full-fledged PVR, if I can figure out a way through the HDCP demons (other proprietary nonsense). Here I come, Mythbuntu!

You guys absolutely rock.  Thank you for all the new found joy you've brought to my life!
