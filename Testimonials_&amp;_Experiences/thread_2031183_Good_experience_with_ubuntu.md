### Post by Kelvari on 2012-07-23
I'm reminded of why I use Ubuntu every time I have to re-install Windows for someone. No matter what computer they use, you need a driver for SOMETHING. And it's usually something important, like the network adapter to download the rest of the drivers and the updates.

Ubuntu, on more systems than I care to count, has been the savior of systems. It doesn't matter if it's a laptop or a desktop; Ubuntu gets the computer into a usable state far quicker than any other method I've seen (even when compared to official recovery media), and seems to have about a 90% chance of getting things working out-of-the-box (curse you Broadcom, though you are getting much better about it).

Even if they want to just have XP/Vista/7 re-installed on the computer, I still bring an Ubuntu disc with me to help me navigate Driver Hell.
