### Post by mmcmonster on 2006-04-27
Leaving the windows world was a process for me:

It's not that I really wanted to unshackle myself from Microsoft.  I just felt that I really needed to have access to all my documents and upgrade at my own pace.  I didn't want to _have_ to buy MS product to read all my documents.  I wanted choice.

For me the first step was Firefox.  I never used Internet Explorer much (I was a Netscape fan), so going to Mozilla and then Firefox was easy.  Convincing my family to do so was doubly easy, since they didn't want the hassles of pop-ups and drive-by spyware installation.

Step two was OpenOffice.org v2.0.  I made the conscious decision to use it for my documents, with very good results.

I tried various Linux distributions over the years: RedHat (v6?), Fedora Core 1 & 2, Vector.  None of them worked for me.  I even had Ubuntu 5.04 on my computer but didn't really delve into it much.

Ubuntu 5.10 is where things changed.  I _learned_ Linux using 5.10, and have been pretty much MS free since October.  I still boot my PC into Windows to sync my Palm to epocrates every month or so, but that's it.

The interesting thing is, when I started using Ubuntu v5.10, my dad's Windows XP box crashed and wouldn't load windows anymore off the boot disk (It wouldn't even load windows 2K, which I had lying around).  I got him to buy an apple iMac, and I reformated the computer and installed Ubuntu v5.10 on his old system and gave it to my brother (Who's Pentium 3/450 was spy-ridden for months now).

So chalk me up for converting 3 households away from MS.  We're all happy (Me more than ever - I don't get any support calls anymore!).

Ubuntu has a way to go before any new user can just pop it in and have everything setup perfectly, but it's getting close.  Real close.

Indeed, it is a process. I was all over Apple a few years ago and wanted a Mac for my next computer because they looked so sexy and had such an interesting OS. At the same time I developed an interest in open source software (not anything political, just exciting open source applications). It started with Firefox, then FileZilla for FTP, OpenOffice eventually replaced Microsoft Word.

Then, almost  2 years ago, I thought "Hey, why not get a Linux system to play with until I get my Mac?" (up till then all I'd used was LIVE CDs) and I got Ubuntu Warty Warthog. After about a month of using it I realised I wasn't booting into Windows anymore. A few months after that the whole open source mindset had grown onto me and using OSX wasn't even an option anymore :) not that I needed it because I'd found my new favourite apps which were all brilliant open source projects and some were Linux-only (Muine for example). I've also grown to love Gnome, it's a great DE.

So you could say I went from a Windows user envying Macs to a full-blood Linux user by accident.
