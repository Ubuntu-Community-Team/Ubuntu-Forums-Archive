### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-10-10
I flirted with LXDE for a few months last year and really liked it's speed and simplicity! But it was buggy on a minimal Ubuntu install (sound issues, shutdown issues). Always "under heavy development" (which to me means the same thing as "Beta! Beware!"), I went to Xfce and was delighted with it. I have some li'l applets on the panel (desktop weather, analog clock, stuff like that) and it's pretty fast on my old Dell.

It's about time I revisited LXDE I guess. I'm still on Karmic (Xubuntu) because of huge issues with Lucid (Plymouth, PulseAudio - neither of which is used in Karmic Xubuntu). When karmic reaches end-of-life I'll experiment with Lubuntu as well as Xubuntu just to see how they run on my modest machine.

With the speed at which LXDE was evolving when I left it, I imagine it's alot better now, and maybe my old issues have been (or *will* have been) addressed by the time Karmic's support ends. I liked it before because of it's extreme simplicity! I'm looking forward to revisiting it next April.
