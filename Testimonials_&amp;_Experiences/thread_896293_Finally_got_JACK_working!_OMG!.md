### Post by surfsunadam on 2008-08-21
I was just about to give up on ubuntu and dual-install XP. I use my computer primarily for audio recording - and I just could not work out how to do it. The only reason I went with ubuntu because a new PC I got came with no OS and the laptop XP installation CD I installed hardly functioned on my computer. 

I use a ZOOMH4 as my recording interface. It works fine on windows. Initially with ubuntu, I could just get it working with ardour with choppy playback and recording. JACK was a nightmare to configure.  Finally I stumbled upon a configuration that seemed to work with ardour, but not audacity. I like audacity better because of the direct access to the waveform. 
Eventually I worked out I could set JACK:system as the I/O settings in audacity and hoorah! It worked just like it used to in windows.

So I think I'll stick with it. Now I just have to worry about how my formatting in openoffice comes out all screwed up in office 2003 at my uni :(
