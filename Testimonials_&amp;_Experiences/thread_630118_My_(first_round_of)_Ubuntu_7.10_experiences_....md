### Post by daviddehoog on 2007-12-03
Hi all

I've used Ubuntu since Warty and a range of other distributions since 1999. Ubuntu is very impressive - slick, intuitive and human-focused. As a desktop or laptop distribution it's a very solid citizen.

I recently installed Ubuntu 7.10 (Gutsy Gibbon) on my ThinkPad T42 with a view to a shiny desktop environment in which to develop a couple of simple web application to be the interface for a small MySQL database.

I've seen a few posts suggesting that 7.10 is not quite as stable as 7.04 ... Is there any indication as to what was different in the most recent development cycle ?

- The installation was trickier than I expected, including a hard lock-up during the detection of my hardware (at 90% completion)
- The system takes almost exactly 2 minutes to boot from GRUB to gdm.
- I've had two other hard lock-ups while undertaking routine tasks (like clicking on the desktop to create a new launcher)
- The two networking management systems (network-manager and interfaces) don't seem to want to play well together with any failure from network-manager leaving me unable to connect to a network without a reboot

I have to say I am surprised. I've previously run (and shamelessly abused!) Ubuntu desktop systems for long periods without these sorts of issues. In the past, the only issues I've had have been programs early in their life being bundled in without sufficient features or lacking the necessary interoperability. Now it seems as though some of the core packages aren't very happy. 

Ubuntu was certainly a mould-breaker in the development of real "just works" configuration for very diverse hardware. I hope to see it continue down that path into the future !

Apart from posting my thoughts here, I'm about to step up and jump into the bugsquad to see what I can contribute.

Cheers
Dave
