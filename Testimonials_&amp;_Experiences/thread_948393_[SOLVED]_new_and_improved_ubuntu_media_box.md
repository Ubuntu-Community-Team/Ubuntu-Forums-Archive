### Post by armandh on 2008-10-15
My son would deliver .avi files on a memory stick and we needed to play them on the family room TV. I moved an old 933 mhz P-III with a fresh 8.04 Ubuntu from the basement spares to the family room. the picture was fine for NTSC via S-video on a 35" tube.
 
then I connected to the lan with the basement server. with a wireless mouse and keyboard my wife inquires about her e-mail ... hard to read in ntsc and over fills the screen if one makes the type font big enough. 

With Feb fast approaching it is time to boost the retail electronics economy. after measuring the cabinet 4 times and gaining 2 inches by removing the doors, which we never closed, a 42" from sams club just fits with plenty of room top and bottom to stay cool. swap video cards and add a DVI/HDMI adaptor plug and Ubuntu's resolution choices fill the page right out of the box save only the restricted drivers.

not all is well though. while it is picture perfect, Firefox and Utube and Flash are not playing well togeather [Hard Lockups] and only since the new video card. [BFG brand nvidia chip]  I would sub a different card first but it worked with out lock up on another basement machine. Maybe I'll try a different browser first.
