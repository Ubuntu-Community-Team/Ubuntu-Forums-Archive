### Post by SupaRice on 2008-06-04
So I've finally bit the bullet an gone completely Ubuntu! :)

My laptop, home workstation, and home server all are Ubuntu now.  I'm using 8 on my home workstation and 7.10-ALT with software raid on my two HD laptop.  I've got to say overall I'm very happy.  My machine seems faster, definately more stable, and far more comfortable to use.  There are only a few things I've not found to be very easy to use.

**Wireless**
There really needs to be an "out of the box" GUI with WPA / WPA2 / Personal & Enterprise support that just plain works well without a fuss.  I'm able to get wireless to work, but it's fussy and having to use iwconfig is a pain at times.  I've tried wifi radar, but it didn't really work well.  I move around a lot and need something that keeps profiles and works with all the security flavors ex. LEAP PEAP etc...  Any suggestions?

**iPod / iPhone Support**
It's not crucial, but it'd be really sweet to have USB cable support for the iPhone and a way to sync your contacts and calendar without having to jump through so many hoops.

**Dual Monitor**
I have a different second monitor for my laptop at home than I do at work (Nvidia card), and it would be sweet if there were better support out of the box for that.  Although I've not completely researched this topic as of yet so any suggestions are much appreciated.

Other than those small things, UBUNTU freak'n :guitar: !!
