### Post by RayJo on 2011-01-27
Hello all!

Just wanted to poke in and tell you of my recent experiences.

Over the past few months, I have been fighting with Windows Vista on a few year old laptop.  Been an MS user for years since getting out of the .mil (all unix shop) and was never comfortable with it, but working for large companies, I used what I was issued.  In house IT supported it and it was a tool for work.

Left the big companies and have become my own de facto IT department.

About 10 days ago, I had enough.  Did a recent back up and upgraded the OS to W7 and added Office 2010 (trial ver, of course).  Took a little hit in the wallet for the OS and a RAM upgrade and got my machine to a happier place.

The night after that, in a caffeine induced haze, got my hands on an older laptop that had supposedly crashed.  Nothing important on it and it sat on the desk gathering dust until I packed it for a move.  It has since sat idle in a box since moving to my current address 18 months ago.

I tried booting and upgrading to W7 and failed miserably.  I didn't want to spend any money on this thing (though I did upgrade the RAM), so I started poking about and came up with the idea of playing with a linux distro.  Fought with it for a few hours that night (early morning) and got Ubuntu 10.10 running.  The time getting it running was no reflection on the actual OS but rather fighting with some hardware problems on the old laptop.

This Ubuntu machine was supposed to be a "back up" laptop as when my "primary" (now W7) machine went down, my productivity took a nose dive.

For the last week+, 10.10 has been the most frequently used, all my office work has been done on this machine instead of the W7 machine.  Half the RAM, slower processor and still faster and more user friendly than W7.  So, here I am thinking about dumping W7 altogether, wiping the drive and going to 10.10 on the newer laptop.

Where I work now, I am one of 5 FT employees, so naturally, our IT budget is tiny and everyone is pretty much self-support.  My employer is not even closely related to the tech industry.  The last company I worked for had somewhere in the neighborhood of 62,000 employees...  IOW, MS was forced upon us.

A little about me...

I am in my late 30s and spent my entire adult career in high tech.  Installing, troubleshooting, selling and marketing for telecom manufacturers; I am not adverse to tech but never saw a "need" to migrate away from MS.  Until recently.

I can say that the OS does everything I need for the small business where I am a principal and Ubuntu lacks very little in regards to what I need for work and recreation.

A few milestones I must reach to detox off MS:

1.  Business travel with only my Ubuntu machine and sever the safety net of having a MS machine next to me.

2.  Let my Office 2010 Trial License expire.

At that point, I can eat the cost of the W7 OS upgrade and go all in.  I have loved the wealth of information here and elsewhere for assistance with my migration and interesting tools out there...

That's a little story of a new linux user.  If my future with this OS is reflective of the previous week, I am a convert for a long time.

Thanks, community!

-Ray
