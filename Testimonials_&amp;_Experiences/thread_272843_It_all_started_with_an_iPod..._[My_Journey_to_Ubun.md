### Post by drFUNK on 2006-10-07
I didn't mean for it to be this long, but here is my story:

**The Microsoft fan boy stage:**
My journey to Ubuntu was an interesting one indeed.  One year ago today, I was one of [*those*]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=307823") Windows power users.  You know, the ones who couldn't be converted for all the gold in the world.  The ones who believed that Microsoft could do no wrong.  I had booted a Live CD of Breezy for all of 5 minutes and thought to myself "This is crap, Windows is so much better" - simply because it was [not Windows]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm").  I even hated apple and all of their products for no good reason.  However, over the course of a single week everything changed.  My father had purchased my sister an iPod for her birthday, but she had no idea how to use it, so it was my job to learn and teach her.  The device's simplicity and intuitiveness  impressed me.  It impressed me to the point of questioning my blind Microsoft loyalty.  Conveniently, around this time the [OSx86 project]("http://www.osx86project.org/") (now [InsanelyMac]("http://www.insanelymac.com/")) was gaining steam, so i bit the bullet and installed OS X on my Dell.  Initial Response: Wow!  Everything ran so smooth.  I never even knew my little laptop had such performance capabilities (of course, nothing on par with a gaming rig, but still impressive compared to Windows XP).  Over the next few months, life was great with OS X... except for the lack of Wi-Fi.

**The Dapper Impression:**
In early May, a close friend of mine began to experiment with Breezy Badger.  He expressed some frustration, but seemed to enjoy the experience overall.  I decided "Why not give it another try?"  I saw that Dapper Drake was on it's way, so I decided that I would delete OS X and install Dapper on June 1st (Dapper's release date).  Upon boot, I was greeted with fresh icons and other GUI improvements from Breezy.  It took me a while to get everything in working order, but it was very satisfying once it was all setup.  I kept the install for about 6 weeks, but eventually ditched it for OS X again.  OS X was a familiar environment - I had already become accustomed to the Mac way of things and didn't want to change.  I also had a longing for my expose and eye candy.  So back to OS X I went.  Once again I was in bliss - sans Wi-Fi.

**The Compiz Effect:**
Then in September, something happened.  To this day I still don't know what pulled me back to Ubuntu.  This time it was different though - I came with a completely open mind.  It was almost as if I was returning to a former place of residence.  Everything seemed familiar.  I had become a bit rusty on command line navigation, but I quickly relearned everything.  Within a week I had everything configured to my satisfaction (+ Wi-Fi! :biggrin: ).  Enter Compiz.  Oh my, where do i start with Compiz?  Compiz/Beryl is what finally weened me off of my OS X addiction.  It gave me my beloved expose functions with even better eye candy than OS X in a much more customizable environment.  I finally have an OS that i can call ***my** own*.  Everything can be customized without worrying about killing the OS (Window Blinds + Windows XP = ](*,) ) or breaking some stupid EULA.  And of course, being a compulsive OS hopper, I *had* to install Edgy Beta 1.  Even though it's still a work in progress, it has already become my primary OS.  One of the most fascinating aspects of the beta software is watching the communication on launchpad and testing the patches as they roll out.  This community is definitely where I want to be.


To all Ubuntu Forums Members,
I wouldn't be anywhere near where I am today in terms of knowledge without your help.  Even though I don't have a high post count, I've used these forums as a resource **many** times.  Every time I have searched for a topic, there was someone else who had the issue and usually a resolution/ helpful work-around.  Even when I searched Google, it pointed back to these forums.  During the few times I have posted, I always received prompt replies full of helpful suggestions.  Thank you all for everything and I will contribute where I can.
