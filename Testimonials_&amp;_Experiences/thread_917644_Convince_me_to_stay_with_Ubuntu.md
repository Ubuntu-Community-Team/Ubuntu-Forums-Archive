### Post by bmac on 2008-09-12
Why should anyone need to convince you to use one OS over another? You seem to have been provided with enough feedback to assist in the decision.
> 
Armed with what you'll all said I'm in a much stronger position to make a decision for here on in.

Unfortunately, feedback from others with respect to re-enforcement, cannot interrupt individual perspective. Prior to installing any OS, logic would suggest an in depth investigation into that OS. Assuring a more positive outcome.

I continue to express concern regarding the lack of investigation into Ubuntu prior to many individuals switching from Windows. Recently an argument was made that somehow Ubuntu was responsible for Windows users that attempt to switch and find that their hardware isn't compatible or that Ubuntu isn't a Windows clone. Of course they become extremely frustrated upon realizing  a learning curve is required or their system is incompatible. 

My recommendation is that prior to considering any new OS - spend time reviewing all information linked to that OS and prepare yourself for a potential time consuming effort. Don't just assume (bad word) it will function OOTB or meet/exceed your expectations... Make the effort to accept responsibility/accountability and you might be surprised by the result... I truly believe many people would not make the transition if they performed their own research and made an enlightened decision. Of course one solution to this issue, is to purchase a system that already has the OS of choice installed....

Just a thought.....

### Post by Marmidon on 2011-10-21
Well it's three years on and I'm happy to say that switching from Windows is the best thing I've ever done.
I've come miles in those three years in my knowledge of Ubuntu (and other linux systems) but i'am only scratching the surface.
I've been liberated and it's allowed my to explore things like setting up my own web and mail server with a small desktop bought off ebay, I'm enjoying programming in PHP, (something I would never have conceived in my Windows days) controlling my websites through ssh and producing my own graphics using GIMP and Inkscape.
People sometimes ask me questions about their system, a windows computer, but I struggle to answer as I can't ask them to open up a terminal!
I am now 100% Linux and Windows seems like a distant memory. I'll never knock Windows, they've helped many people (like my mum and dad) use their computer in a way that suits their lifestyle but as far as I'm concerned, I'm on a one way road to Linuxville!!! The weird thing is that I'm in a mind set where I want to do everything for myself and it's all because of Ubuntu.
Example: I don't want to take my car to the garage, I want to fix it myself. I don't want to call a carpenter to fix my door, I want to do it myself. This is the Linux way, anyone can do anything!
A mention has to go now to all you guys on the forum. Without the online help I'd be nowhere and my only regret is that I'll not be able to reciprocate the help and advice I have been given over the years. 
So, anyone else thinking about quitting Linux and returning to Windows? If you've got a bit of time and don't expect life to be handed on a plate, stick with it. Someone out there will guide you through.

Cheers guys.
