### Post by bradford72 on 2007-02-26
So, I switched to ubuntu about a month ago, and so far, I love it!  I had a little trouble at first in getting on the internet, but found out about modifying my modeprobe.d by reading the forums here, and since then, smooth sailing!  About two weeks ago, I updated to feisty, which is working great, however, I think that I would have done well to wait for the official release.  Anyway, I'm in the position now, that I don't really 'need' my computer to do anything official at this time in my life besides turn on, and be there for me to learn linux, and being a linux noob, I find that ubuntu makes linux very accessible and pleasant to learn!  
 
[COLOR=black]_Some things that I find really nice_[/COLOR]:
 
Open Office .org--I really like the fact that the transition from MS Office to OOo feels so smooth, and the fact that I can open MS files with OOo, and that I can save my OOo files with MS extensions and use MS to open them (as school and work are still MS environs)
 
The forums--The idea that there's a bunch of people who have all come together to share our experience with ubuntu, and help each other figure how to use it more effectively...and for free....that's great!
 
The philosophy--the general idea that science is for everyone, and that anyone who wants to put in the time and effort to learn something should not have to pay and pay and pay is a beautiful thing...enough said!
 
 _Random Thoughts_:
 
I really like ubuntu so far, of course my whole goal is to learn linux, and run it.
 
I like the idea of having total control of my system, and the idea that I can now make it do whatever I want it to...now to figure out what that REALLY means...:)
