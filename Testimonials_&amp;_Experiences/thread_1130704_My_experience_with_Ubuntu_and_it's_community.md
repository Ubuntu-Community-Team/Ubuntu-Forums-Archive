### Post by Svensk023 on 2009-04-20
I am a 19-year-old male nursing student from California, and I have always been a person who likes to solve problems. I like to solve problems for sheer enjoyment, knowledge enrichment and ultimately to attempt to help others to the best of my abilities.

   So naturally when I first saw a Linux magazine (about a year ago) while in a book store I was drawn to the promise of a change and a **challenge**. I quickly went home to my rather beat-up laptop and started my download of Ubuntu 7.10 (I had no clue what an .iso was at the time... haha) after tackling the new ways of burning an image to a disk in windows I got pretty excited at the completed live CD I now had.

   After poking around with the live boot, I was pretty impressed, and needless to say, hooked. So I took the plunge and re-formatted my trust laptop with Ubuntu. I had quite a kick juggling all my nursing classes, hospital clinicals and trying to understand what was going on under the hood of Ubuntu.

   Now about a year later (and a nice new computer) I am still finding much to learn about Ubuntu and Linux in general. I have gained a lot of knowledge and experience in the process (but by no means am I a pro, or anything close to it) and I attempt to reach out to others who have problems that I feel fit to triage and properly give support to. So on that note, I am very impressed with the Ubuntu community. there is a very big support structure here, and getting help you need is pretty painless.

   So I encourage those who are taking the time to read this, please keep on helping our brothers and sisters in the Ubuntu community. Enrich your knowledge whenever time allows you to and let us try and keep Ubuntu welcoming, stable and enjoyable to use and be a part of. I have noticed a lot of fan-boy behavior that pop's up here and there, and being passionate about something is perfectly ok, but let's not lose our purpose with over-zealous behavior. 

  The great know who they are, and their meekness is what allows them to rise to the top. So let that be our community: diplomatic, empathetic and wise.

For those reading, thank you.

-Svensk
