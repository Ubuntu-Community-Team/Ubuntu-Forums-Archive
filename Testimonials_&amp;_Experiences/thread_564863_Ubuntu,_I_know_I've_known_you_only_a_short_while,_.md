### Post by lightstream on 2007-10-01
:-({|= ..would you marry me??

cuz I love you!!

I've just installed a new motherboard, nothing special, dual core AMD 64, but still I never thought it would go without a single hitch. Ok, on first boot, X would not start, but all I had to do was hit Ctrl-Alt-F4 to get a command line, find my way to xorg.conf which told me the dpkg-configure command I had to run.

I restarted the X-server from the command line, and bang I was in! 

The USB mouse however wasn't working - OK, I thought, it's the new motherboard's USB drivers, I need to install them. How to do this without the mouse? I plugged in a USB stick to see if there was any activity on the USB, and to my surprise the drive popped up in nautilus. I browsed its contents with the keyboard - some music - and hit enter to play one. No surprise there - no sound. Audio is on the motherboard, drivers again.

I double checked the audio connection - doh! I'd plugged the speakers into the wrong socket! I corrected that, and woah, sound!! I then noticed that I had not plugged in my mouse at all, but had mixed up the cables and plugged in a different peripheral. Connecting the mouse, and voila - it works great! 

I am shocked. Stunned. I was so used to the rigmarole of upgrading hardware on Windows that I was so quick to assume drivers were the problem, when it was me just wiring things up wrong!!

Yes Ubuntu, you are my true love now, I have been M$'s bitch for far, far too long.....

yeah it does just work. Windows on the other hand just ~doesn't~ work. I've just rebooted into Windows for the first time since the upgrade - the pain the pain!
