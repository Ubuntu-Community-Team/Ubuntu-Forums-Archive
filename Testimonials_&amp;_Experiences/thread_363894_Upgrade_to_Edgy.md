### Post by kevinlyfellow on 2007-02-17
I had bought a laptop with dapper preinstalled, so I've been reluctant to upgrade to edgy (the company I bought from would fully support the upgrade of course).  I didn't want to go through the hassle of backing up all my data and do a clean install, nor did I want to do a dist upgrade because of bad experience in the past.

But I got the itch

I had downloaded and burned edgy, tried the live cd, and the itch turned into a rash.

I decided to go with the dist upgrade, even though I feared things would go wrong.  After I downloaded all the packages and rebooted, sure enough, there was a mess.  But I did the upgrade a few more times and eventually got everything to install.  I booted into the new kernel and let's just say "The WOW starts now!"

Rhythmbox has finally got its act together!  I like the way they now handle plugins.  I enabled the lyrics and the album art.  More lyrics need to be supported, but I'll look into submitting lyrics to the site they get them from.  I also enjoy the new screensaver preview, though its still laking the configuration that people have complained about for a long time.  Having Baobab installed on default is nice as well.  Everything seems a bit snappier than dapper and I noticed that the animations were better over all.

Then I installed beryl.  It was not hard at all!  While I think the window manager needs a bit more work to get very nice compatibility with gnome, its frikin' awesome.  I especially like how I can get a view of all my windows at once and select which one I want.  I configured xine so that I can play movies while its on the corner, which gets me gitty! 

I really like where linux is heading.  If we could get more oems to install and support this software on their desktops, then we would be on top for sure!  Things move so fast in open source these days its impossible to keep up.  I've been thinking of my win95 days and how I hated how software would get updated, because that was synonymous with it becoming worse.  Now I can't stop myself from installing the latest versions of software!

Ok, so now my complaints section (I'll work on solving them).  The bootloader doesn't tell you whats going on in the background.  What if it hangs somewhere?  Next is with nm-applet, on startup it tells me I'm connected to the network, but it tells me near the applications menu, since gnome is still starting up.  Then there is is nautilus.  I like how they have made it easy to use the location bar instead of those annoying buttons, but not I have a button to switch when I know I'm not going to use it.  Then there is the new emblem artwork.  I do not like the art because it looks bad and they are not very distinguishable from each other.  The point of having emblems is for quick identification, but when the emblems look to similar, you loose the point.  Gnome-screensaver still doesn't have a customize button.  Beryl has too many options for gnome ;-)    None of these complaints are nothing thats a big deal, just annoyances.  

So keep up the good work canonical, ubuntu foundation, all developers that contribute to open source, and the communities that surround open source.  Thank you!
