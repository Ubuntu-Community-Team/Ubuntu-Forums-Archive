From [http://www.marcelgagne.com/node/535](http://www.marcelgagne.com/node/535)

Two Communities Collide

Since 1954, Christian Life College has been educating students.  Christian Life College celebrated it's golden 50th anniversary the same year Ubuntu made it's debut to the world in 2004.  Part of the school's mission has been to equip and prepare it's students for the future.  Ubuntu's Mark Shuttleworth has seen Ubuntu, among other things, as a tool to better equip schools that lack the funds for quality software.  On November 6th these two communities took another step closer to fulfilling both their missions, together.

Patrick Green, longtime Linux advocate and President of Chicago based Silver Strand Solution (a Linux migration consulting firm) began teaching computers at this fully accredited four year university.  He has incorporated Marcel Gagne's Moving to Open Source Software into his curriculum, exposing the students to the robustness and value of open source software.  Noticing that the students were benefiting from the class and the tools, he wanted to expand the benefits to include the entire student body and faculty.  

A clinic was announced to occur in the student lounge on November 6th.  The students would come with concerns, needs, and questions and a team of experts would help resolve those issues.  Among some of the IT volunteers were three representatives of the Ubuntu Chicago Loco Group.  Chicago Ubuntu founder and president Richard Johnson, Jim Campell (of ADP), and Robert Stolorz (of Feratech).  

Between them, an Ubuntu install was repaired and two other installs took place. Richard got a tour of the campus and an opportunity to meet with the Dean of the college, Wayne Wachsmuth.  By the end of the night plans were made and solidified for the two groups.  Christian Life College has a troubled server running Windows and a network that is problematic.   The Ubuntu Chicago group will replace the server with Linux and have said server run the network more effectively on Linux.  In return, the Ubuntu Chicago LOCO will have access to a classroom and the computer lab for their monthly meetings.  The Chicago Ubuntu team will meet on the campus for their first foray into the Christian Life College servers on Sunday, November 25th.  

Less than a week after this event, more students have had Ubuntu loaded on their laptops and still more are getting it installed on Monday November 12th.  Currently, more than 10% of the student body now has Ubuntu on their laptops.  

As this project continues, all progress will be documented and recorded for use for white papers, case studies, migration maps, and even video documentaries.
