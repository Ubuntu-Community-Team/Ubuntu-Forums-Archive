### Post by gdonwallace on 2009-10-28
First a little background. I have been using Ubuntu since 8.04, so a little over a year.  I worked for a company that supported accounting software written in BBx on SCO OpenServer 5.  I got into the beta for Lindows (Linspire now) and messed around with a couple other distros.  I'm no noob when it comes to Unix/Linux.  

What I was, was someone tired of having to reinstall Windows every 12 months, and reboot everyday because something would freeze up.  I knew that there was something, had to be something that was as good as (or almost as good as...that's where I was) Windows.  

My boss told me about Ubuntu (He runs Fedora).  I started out with the LiveCD and ran it for a about 2 weeks.  I did the wubi install and booted directly into Ubuntu for almost a month to get everything the way I wanted and the programs set up.  (Did this so I could make notes of settings, IP address, etc for the complete install)  I finally installed 8.04 over Windows on my work PC, that was over 1 year ago and have not looked back.

My wife and I recently got laptops, and the first thing I did was download 9.04 and install that on my laptop.  Sure there are problems, name me one OS that never has a problem. (at least I don't get stuck in endless reboots when trying to install it:lolflag:)  But thanks to the community and my own persistence, everything worked out.  

Just wanted to say thanks to the community for the help, and thanks for putting out a product that allows those of us looking for a windows alternative something that we can use and never have to worry about he problems and issues with Windows.
