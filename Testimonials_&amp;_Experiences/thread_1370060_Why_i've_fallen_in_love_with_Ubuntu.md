### Post by Arla on 2010-01-01
First the negative,

I wish Ubuntu had more, and better video editing software, editing HDV video in Ubuntu just doesn't seem very possible right now, and is why I have Windows 7 still on my new laptop.

On the flip side, everything else is FAR better under Ubuntu, I think the biggest thing I LOVE is that it starts up so well, what do I mean by that? Well, Windows starts reasonably quickly, it comes up with the desktop, I click "explorer" and then, then I wait, I twiddle my thumbs, while windows figures out what else it still has to load, and how to load it and... and this is a relatively clean install of Windows 7, only anti-virus, and Sony Vegas video editing.

Ubuntu on the other hand, it just responds, when the desktop comes up (and it comes up quicker than Windows has ever done) it's ready, I click firefox and bingo, there's firefox ready to browse the net, I click on places and my data drive, and there it is, ready for me to find a file, NO MORE FREAKING WAITING!!!

I think for a good 99% of users, Ubuntu would actually be a really good option, it's fast, it's responsive, and it really works well. I think the only issue is the always irksome hardware non-interations, but I think if we could get it more used then hardware manufacturers might care more if there hardware worked under Ubuntu, vs the normal "it works in Windows, why would we need to care about Linux"

### Post by 3Miro on 2010-01-01
Microsoft supports no hardware, manufacturers support Windows, by making windows drivers for their hardware. That is why, if the manufacturers do not think they can make money, they do not make drivers. For example when Vista came out, manufacturers did not make new drivers for the old hardware.

Linux supports huge amount of hardware and that is why when a new version of Linux comes out, all the old hardware automatically works.

BTW, check more carefully for the HD software, you might not have found it yet. Also, consider Virtual Box vs dual boot. I don't do video editing so take my advice with a grain of salt.
