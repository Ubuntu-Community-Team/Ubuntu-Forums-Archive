### Post by xavier_r on 2007-10-19
For a quite while I have been upgrading ubuntu from one version to another...

But this time i decided to break that chain, and make a new fresh install...

So instead of downloading the Alternate CD, I burned the LiveCD iso
I booted into it...

Performance was ok... and the installer was pretty sleek... I mean if new users were migrating to ubuntu, they would feel much comfy...

Anyways, when install finished... and i made the first boot...
the dreaded thing strikes...

Apparently the default splash screen is in some resolution that my monitor wont allow... it hanged...
So i booted into rescue and edited the /boot/grub/menu.lst file

Booting went fine... But this time GNOME wont start...
So I again booted into rescue mode and did init 5 on the shell...

Whoa... it started
My first impression of the desktop was pretty neat...
Then when I poked around a bit, i thought it was awesome...

The change desktop background takes me directly into a panel, where one can change themes and set styles too... :D
Also switching workspaces looked great...
I am still exploring 7.10...

Thanks UBUNTU Team for an even better and greater release :lolflag::lolflag:

I am surely enjoying this... :D
