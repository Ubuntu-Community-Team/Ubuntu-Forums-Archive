### Post by ostrm on 2009-04-14
I just wanted to thank you for the amazing work everybody involved in ubuntu is doing!

I've been using linux for about 5 years, started with Gentoo and never looked anywhere else ... 
... until last week where a disk crash on my Thinkpad T60 and not much time on my hands forced me to look for alternatives. 

*Every* single detail works out of the box, dri on fglrx, wifi, suspend/hibernate, power management, I'm really impressed!

Though I will keep on using Gentoo on my computation servers, where having full control and a powerful distribution and compilations system of my own sources is crucial, I have got the impression that ubuntu is *the* choice for the desktop.

Have a great day!

### Post by ostrm on 2009-04-15
> **Armor Nick said:**
>  Btw, why don't you use Ubuntu Server for your server?

Mostly for "historical" reasons, I think. When we chose to run Gentoo on our computation servers, it was with the intent to easily push and build the software we develop on those boxes on a daily/weekly basis. Moreover it was important to have full control of needed dependencies, from external packages as well as our own libraries. We have packaged our different software packages in ~80 ebuilds and use portage for syncing from a central server. 

And of course we follow the "Never touch a running system" - paradigm :-)
