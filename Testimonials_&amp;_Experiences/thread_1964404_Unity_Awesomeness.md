### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-04-24
I first tried the Unity desktop in Maverick. I couldn't see where they were going with the desktop and I hated the execution of it. Aesthetically it was okay but otherwise I really didn't like it.

During the 11.04 release cycle, after they ported Unity to Compiz, I tried it. Much better. Still not great, but it was usable and there were new improvements every week. In fact I never went back to Gnome Classic. I especially liked drag 'n' drop to applications in the Launcher, and the search function, and the progress bars on Launcher icons. Recently I encountered a bug in Update Manager that caused the actual update progress window not to appear; but I was able to see the progress bar in the Launcher and so I knew what was going on.

11.10 was when it actually became a good desktop, even if the dash speed left something to be desired on a netbook. I started to understand where the developers were coming from and appreciated their design (and their implementation), especially after trying Gnome Shell.

12.04 doesn't seem that different to 11.10, except for Dodge Windows no longer being an option :-(  and a bit of a speedup for the Dash. The best new feature for me is the Video lens: I was pretty blown away to see ABC iView videos listed on it, and being able to search iView from my desktop is RIDICULOUSLY convenient compared to wrestling with the iView website.

There's still a couple of things that Unity needs. Dash calculator. Files able to be pinned to the Launcher. Web search from the Dash. Touch-friendliness in default applications. Expose'ing the windows from a launcher button during a drag (it was in 11.10 but no longer in 12.04 as far as I can see). No more "mystery meat" icons at the bottom of the Dash - they should be moved and labelled. But everything is usable and actually quite nice to use. My 65 year-old father is also interested in moving from Gnome Classic to Unity in the near future after I gave him a demo.

If you go around saying things like "Unity is crap" then you're really not doing yourself any favours.

### Post by Squonk07 on 2012-04-25
I'm kind of in the same boat as many folks here. I didn't hate Unity but I wasn't blown away with it, either. The weird thing is, to me at least, the basic interface hasn't changed a whole lot since Natty. I still need things like MyUnity and CCSM to keep me happy, but I think what's happened is I've sort of adapted myself to it. Not to mention it's gotten a lot more polished. Some things I asked for, like little progress bar overlays on Launcher icons (like when Nautilus is transferring files), have appeared. It's little stuff like this that doesn't seem that big, but it adds up.

Overall the whole thing just feels better. The Dash is still kind of sluggish--in fact, I could swear it worked better earlier in the dev cycle (maybe I should do a clean install, except I've got everything nested and I'm happy with the setup). It's not a huge deal, though. I don't actually use the lenses much. Mostly I just type what I want in the search bar and it comes up (albeit with a bit of lag). I tried using MATE on Mint 12 and it was like going back to the dark ages. I can't believe I moused my way through those drop down menus every single time I wanted an application and didn't manage to go nuts in the process. Yeah, I did pin some shortcut icons to the Panel, but not for every single program.

In a weird way (longtime Mac users will know about this), Unity is a lot like OS X. It started off as a clean break from the past, began with a lot of functionality missing, users and developers filled in the gaps along the way, and the official product has gotten better with every release. KDE 4 was the same way, and it's come a LONG way since version 4.0. I've got a partition devoted to Kubuntu 12.04 that works wonderfully, and I spent months using Kubuntu 11.04 when it was current. But this cycle I keep coming back to vanilla Ubuntu 12.04--this is definitely my favorite release so far.
