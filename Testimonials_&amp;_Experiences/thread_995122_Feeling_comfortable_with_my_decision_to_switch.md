### Post by tslawinski on 2008-11-27
I've been running 8.10 for a couple of weeks now. I'm still quite enamoured with Ubuntu. I've got pretty much everything set up that I wanted to set up on my machine. I'm very impressed with the stability and performance compared to windows. It just works. No major hang-ups to speak of. A bit of an adjustment switching to Linux equivalents of window's apps (but nothing too overwhelming). I do a bit of web site development and found a really nice site development IDE ([Aptana Studio]("http://www.aptana.com/studio")). It comes with a very rich set of plug-ins. Gimp meets my image editing needs quite nicely. Blender modeling is awesome (but takes some time getting used to). The compiz stuff (ie. desktop enhancements, cube desktop, etc) were neat but I don't really care much for bells & whistles so I got rid of that. OpenOffice is more than enough for office productivity. Each of my two boys have windows boxes. Having seen my machine, they are interested in switching over but are concerned about World of Warcraft performance in wine. My gut feeling is Ubuntu is going to make great strides in drawing in more disgruntled windows users. I hope this is the case and then maybe we will see developers like blizzard port their stuff over to this side.
