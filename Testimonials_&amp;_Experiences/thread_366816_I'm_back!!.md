### Post by thoffland on 2007-02-21
I never actually left, since my desktop has been running Ubuntu for a little over a year, but when my wireless wouldn't work with Feisty Herd 3 I figured I'd try out a couple other distro's on my laptop while I waited for a fix. I only had to wait a few days before I saw that Herd 4 came out and the live CD's wireless did work... so that put an end to my tinkering. But here's my opinions on 2 other distro's which I probably didn't give enough of a chance:

DreamLinux: I had to use ndiswrapper to get my wireless working, and it wouldn't hold my wireless configurations and I had to re-enter my wep and everything each time I rebooted.  It's also probably not the best for a laptop since it doesn't have built in wiress monitor or battery. Of course these can be downloaded, but it was just one more thing that makes Ubuntu that much more attractive. I think it's a cool distro, if you like XFCE it's a pretty cool setup, but still needs some work in my opinion. It froze on me a few times and the only way out was to reboot, and setting up the panels caused a few problems too. 

SimplyMepis: Seemed ok, aside from the fact that I dont really like KDE. The wireless did work, but was a pain to setup and for some reason I couldn't figure out how to set a static IP on it. Everything seemed to run smoothly until I used a "recommended" list of repositories that I found in the Mepis Forums and after updating and upgrading, I lost my internet connection and other stuff just wouldn't open. It wouldn't even shut down... and I had to do a soft shutdown to turn my laptop off.

That was late last night, and this morning I installed Ubuntu and I'm back up and running again!!! 

I love my Ubuntu!! It's been a solid OS since the day I started using it, it always works, the support is awesome, and I'm always recommending it to people who even mention the idea of trying something else.  I've satisfied my curiosity for now, and it's just re-confirmed that Ubuntu is the best. 

Thanks Ubuntu Team!!

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-21
I think the forums are the real reason people come back to Ubunutu.  Besides a solid Linux OS, there is growing support for more hardware and clever work-arounds for stuff that is broken or annoying.  In the enterprise world there seems to be a consolidation of about 6 Linux distros--based on what high-end servers are supporting.  In the desktop/user world there are still perhaps 15 flavors of Linux that consistently rise to the top.  I'm not sure where Ubuntu is in that stack, but the forums act as the magnet that keep people coming back after trying other distros.

thoffland's testimony is typical.  I went through a few distros my self, starting back with Redhat 6.4 (old but works) and until I discovered Ubuntu a couple of years ago I have tried other distros and yet I seem to gravitate back to Ubuntu.

Bottomline:  If you have a working system, stick with it.  If you have a new/old machine that needs an OS, Ubuntu provides a nice balance between reliability and cool stuff.

Oh, help someone with a problematic Windows machine--that's what Ubuntu is all about.
