### Post by solwic on 2009-01-15
Although I do want to add that I think sometimes the forum is a bad thing.  It's a great source of information, and everyone helps each other and that's nice, but the downside is that when a person asks for help on the forums, they're not learning what's wrong, or why they're fixing something.  They're just being told the answer.  

With Windows, when something broke there wasn't a forum to go to (at least I never did).  I experimented, turned options on and off, ran through menus and the like on my own, trying stuff out until I fixed my problem.  Because of that, I learned - a LOT - about Windows and how it worked.  I would bet that I learned more about Windows in a week of fixing all its problems than I have in seven straight months of using Ubuntu.  

So the forums rock on, don't get me wrong...but at the cost of not figuring stuff out yourself.  The problem, of course, is that Linux tends to be so counter-intuitive with simple things.  For instance, it might be nice that all programs are installed to one area (I don't even know what folder that is), but I sure do miss Windows' control panel when I want to uninstall an app that *didn't* get installed through Synaptic or Add/Remove Programs.  

Not complaining, just making an observation.  :)

EDIT:  I see that Gnome has a Control Center, but it's not quite the same thing.  Close, though.  :P
