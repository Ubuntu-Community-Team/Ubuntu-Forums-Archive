### Post by AnimateDeath on 2007-10-02
I figured that I would explain a little about why I switched from Vista to Ubuntu. 

I have been a Windows user from the very beginning and have used it solely as an operating system. My first computer was a trs-80 and then I got a Tandy-1000, My first computer that had Windows 3.1 on it was a home build thing that did what I wanted it to do. My current is a Toshiba Satallite A135-S4467 and I love it (far cry from the trs-80 which I still actually have and it still runs like a new machine).

When I bought this laptop I was a little leery of it at first because of the Vista operating system on it, but I remembered when XP came out and the problems it had. I am in no way a windows noob so I decided that any snags I might run in to with Vista, I could handle (sometimes I think that I am a lot smarter than I really am).

Day one with this laptop went great. I installed all of my programs and everything worked just fine. Aside from the nagging pop up asking me if I really wanted to do an action that I initiated, there was not much that bothered me. I turned off the UAC so that I would no longer need to verify that I was, indeed, the administrator. SO far so good.

Day three of vista seen me putting my recovery disk in and re-installing back to factory settings. Why did I do this? Because I was using Photoshop CS3 and in the middle of a picture edit everything froze. Not just PS, but Vista as well. I had to do a "hard" shut-down and when I started it back up again I was informed by my laptop that I had no operating system installed...  WTF? 

Ok, no big deal really, just a time consuming proccess of re-installing vista and then all of my programs. My program list went like this: Adobe Photoshop CS3, Adobe Flash Pro CS3, Adobe Dreamweaver CS3, Adobe Illustrator CS3, Adobe Premier Pro CS3, Adobe After Effects CS3, CoolEdit 2.1, utorrent, M$ Office 2007, Adobe Lightroom, Oblivion, Doom3 (old but I still like it), various other games and about a dozen other progs that I used a lot. So like I said, no big deal, just a time consuming ordeal.

I ran for a week after this with no problems and during this time had put a lot of Photo's on the computer (I am a photography nut) and a ton of personal files that couldn't be replaced. Add to this all the music and movies that i had on the system and I was back to being a happy camper. Vista was running good, and my programs was running good. I was once again smittin by the "Microsoft Love Bug".  Then tragedy struck once again.

What was I doing at the time? I was surfing the internet with Firefox. What happened? I closed Firefox (as I was done surfing) and then when it closed I got the blue screen of death... WTF!??!  Why did that just happen? I restarted the laptop and watched as vista checked my harddrive and then started up. I was told via popup that my system had just recovered from a serious error. What error was that? Closing Firefox? At this point I was settling back down as I found that everything was ok. I decided that this would be a good time to back-up all of my stuff so i wouldn't lose it. I grabbed the external HD and plugged it in to the firewire port and turned it on...
BLUE SCREEN OF DEATH.  Now this F(*&^%ing rediculous. I restart the laptop and what do I see? My laptop telling me that I have no operating system installed. Now i am no longer upset about it, I am mad.

I talked to a friend of mine that brought over a live cd of Ubuntu and loaded it on the laptop. From here I was able to get in to the Windows partition and get all of my personal things out of it. He asked me if I wanted to go ahead and install Ubuntu on the laptop and I just had a little laugh and told him "no thanks". I am a windows users and have been for years and years. Vista having a few bugs was to be expected and I would overcome. (Once again I think that I am a lot smarter than I really am)

So, once again, insert recovery disk and bring it back to square one. Install all of my software again and then load all of my personal stuff back on it again. This time the proccess took all day long. I went to bed and then the next morning I felt good. i grabbed the laptop, spare battery, and charger and went to the park.

It was a nice day out and this park had open wifi for people to use. I was about an hour in to surfing the internet and checking out the girls as they walked past when my eyes went from a girl's backside to my laptop where I noticed that everything had frozen. Now she had a nice rear, but not good enough to make a laptop freeze up. Fearing the worse I restarted the laptop and was relieved to find that Vista started fine. I opened Photoshop and went to load a picture to edit and found that all of my pictures was gone. Not just one or two, but every single one of them. I quickly checked my documents and found them all there. Trying to stay calm, I restarted Vista to see if maybe something had just glitched and was greeted by an old friend. "No operating system found"

I say BS so loudly that few of the girls I was admiring started walking a little faster away form me. I decided that this couldn't possibly be Vista's problem. There was no way that it could have problems like this and be released to the public. It had to be the laptop. I packed it all back up, went home, and checked the HD on the laptop 3 times. There was nothing wrong with it. I ran to a computer shop and had them check out the laptop. Everything fine and dandy. i told him what had been happening and he just shook his head and laughed. i left the shop in a foul mood and went home. 
Once again I recovered, re-installed, and put personal things back on. All day long again, and I shut it off after this. The next few days I spent using my collective Windows knowledge to figuring out the problem. Well, needless to say, I had nothing. I decided that maybe my lack of doing a Windows update had been at fault... Yes, that must be it. Something had been found that was doing this bad things to the operating system and windows update would take care of this problem and make it go away. A quick run of windows update and a restart and... a freeze on restart. A reboot. A "recovered from serious error". A freeze. A reboot. and finally..... "No operating system found"   That was it. I was laughing so hard when I called my friend that he couldn't understand what I was trying to say to him.

He came over with Ubuntu 7.04 in hand and handed it to me. i told him that there was no way that I could install linux, I knew nothing about it. But he insisted and sat down next to me as I installed it. WTF? Did that really just install that quickly?

Not all perfect though, I had no sound. I asked him about it and he told me that I would never learn if he walked me through everything and then he left. I was left alone with a new install of Ubuntu 7.04 and no sound. i had no idea where to start.

I looked at everything I could find for sound and had nothing. I picked up my recovery disk and almost put it in the drive when i decided that I would give it a little more time. Firefox took me to the Ubuntu Forums and my sound came on directly after that. What other problems did I have? None. There is alot that I don't know about linux. Actually it would be more correct to say that I know absolutely NOTHING about Linux, but I am learning quickly as I have always been a computer junky. I have had to abandon all of my Adobe CS3 stuff for now (which makes me sad because of the price paid for it all) but I have found free replacements to almost all of that software that has been doing everything that I needed it to do. FREE I said (just in case you didn't hear that part).  

Ubuntu 7.10 is now running on my laptop, which seems to have a love affair with Ubuntu, and I have found a renewed love of computers that I had appearently lost in using window so much. I still haven't found a replacemt for After Effects or Premier Pro, but I can do without those things for a while. 

Has Ubuntu crashed on me since install? Yes it has. What was I doing at the time? I was doing things that I had no clue about. Was the re-install a pain? What re-install? i just restarted the laptop and everything was fine. 

I've had a lot of things come up that I didn't know how to do, but a quick read of the forums and I find my answer (unless i accedentally forget to search on purpose and post first).

I've been a windows user since 3.1.

I've been a heavy Windows user since '95.

I've been an Ubuntu user since september of '07 and haven't looked back since. I've thrown away the recovery cd for Vista that came with the laptop. I've gotten rid of anything that had to do with windows (except my adobe stuff, maybe one day I can use it all on linux), and I will never again be an advocate of windows. It's Ubuntu or nothing.
