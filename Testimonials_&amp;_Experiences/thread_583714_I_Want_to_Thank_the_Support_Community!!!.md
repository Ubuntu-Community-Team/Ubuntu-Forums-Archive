### Post by besseriym on 2007-10-20
I have been an Ubuntu user for less than a month, but I felt like I had to make this post, and I'll explain why...

I was a frustrated Windows user for a LONG time (not due to lack of experience or knowledge, but frustration with the terrible OS), and after seeing the price of Vista when compared to the problems that seemed imminent, I was hardly enthusiastic.

I had heard about a new Linux distro. that was getting a lot of hype and decided to give it  a shot. Having never used Linux before in my life (despite over 14 years of computer experience) I can honestly say I was very skeptical at first. I heard stories of how complicated and ugly Linux was from a number of friends back in school (although, that was a number of years ago), but I was NOT going to give Mr. Gates any more of my money for his mediocre software.

After a completely painless and straightforward install from a Live CD, I was up and running within minutes...literally. I was impressed with the look of the system and was eager to see what it could do. I can say that never having used Linux before has not really been an issue so far....but that is LARGELY due to the help and dedication of the Ubuntu/Linux community.

I have never before seen so many people willing to help each other online....ever. I was absolutely floored with the amount of help I could find on ANY issue I was dealing with, no matter how specific. The responses were in plain English and very easy to follow, and even more impressive was the sheer amount of responses for each issue. And these are people who are not getting paid a dime to give service that is better than I have ever experienced with a supported product in my life. Saying that I was impressed would be an understatement.

I would like to say, on behalf of the growing community of Linux newbies...

THANK YOU !!!...to all of you advanced users who took the time to help the rest of us....and thank you for being so nice about it, lol.

Without you guys, I would have been completely lost in the world of Linux and probably would have wasted many an hour trying to figure out what I was doing :).


As a side note, I am completely amazed by the stability of this system and will never return to Windows as long as Linux exists. I am currently running Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon 7.10, with a mass of open source software that is growing on me by the minute and eye candy that makes Vista look like garbage...all without a single crash or OS error to speak of. I have found a new home :)
