### Post by slinkey1981 on 2008-10-22
**[SIZE="4"]People who complain about FREE things[/SIZE]**

I find myself sifting through post after post on why Ubuntu is either The BEST OS or why it is absolutely HATED. 

I am annoyed by the people who complain about a problem they are having with some piece of OPEN SOURCE software, and feel the need to try to beat their point of view into my head. You don't like this movie player? Use a different one! There are options! Amarok doesn't work for you? Try a different one! Your (insert hardware name here) doesn't work out of the box? Look for a tutorial, but don't start complaining that you don't want to read and/or sift through multiple pages that someone has spent their time documenting just to help someone else who is stuck with the same problem.

>  That dosnt help. I sed i dont wana read 3 pages to find an answer 

That was an actual quote! Someone asked how to do something and they were pointed to a tutorial that listed EXACTLY what they wanted to know, but they couldn't spend the time to actually read and DO IT FOR THEMSELVES!

If you WON'T read, and CAN'T follow instructions, don't blame Ubuntu (or any other Distro) it's your own laziness. Don't expect everything Open Source to work exactly the way you want it to. It won't, all you are doing is setting yourself up for disappointment. The people who write the Tutorials aren't paid, they don't OWE you ANYTHING. 

Ubuntu is free, I could say that you get what you pay for, but, with Ubuntu, you get much more than that. 

You get freedom. If you really want something, write it, code it. Figure it out. If you can't/won't, then don't complain when something doesn't work exactly how you want it to. By all means, tell the author of bugs, but don't have the audacity to expect or demand that they tell you how to fix it. Too many people have this mindset that they DESERVE things to work the way they want.

Did your credit card get charged when you used apt-get, aptitude, synaptic, whatever package manager you use? I didn't think so.

Linux is definitely not for everyone, will it work out of the box? Maybe, mine didn't.

Will it work flawlessly, without any real work? No.

Ubuntu is like a garden. You can't grow watermelons unless you plant the seeds. 

I see so many posts in the beginner's section, and they typically seem to be the same, Compiz, nVidia... you all know what I mean. Why do people feel the need to just have their question answered, it seems like a bunch of kids pushing to get to the front of the line for a piece of candy, when there are people handing it out throughout the line. It makes no sense, all you do is clog the forums with more of the same. Search and Read. If you can't do that, then please, please, please, do NOT complain when people don't answer you. It's not anyone's fault that you can't use a search function. Don't demand answers either. It isn't Bobby's fault that you can't get a spinning reflective globe in compiz. 

When you make demands of people who aren't paid to help you, you just look like a booty-head.

[SIZE="4"]**People who say that Linux is too hard:**[/SIZE]

How much you get out of Linux is directly proportionate to how much effort you put into it. You might think that you were born with a Windoze mouse in your hand, I am willing to bet my left leg that you had questions about it. You didn't see the screen and know the intricacies of the OS. You most likely had YEARS of learning at home, school, work, wherever.

Learning anything takes using your brain. I know that sounds redundant, but I feel like some people are missing the point. 

There is usually a reason that people pick up a Ubuntu (whatever distro) CD. Mac OS cheesed you off? Windows got infected (again) with a virus? You WILL have problems with your OS, I don't care what you use. None of them are THE best at EVERYTHING.

Windows has games.

Mac has Video and Audio editing apps coming out of it's little apple ears.

Linux has FREEDOM, use it. It may take some (a lot) of tinkering, but you CAN get it to do what you want, IF you put the EFFORT into it.

Some people don't want to invest that amount of effort. That's not Linux's fault. It sits there ready to learn and be molded into shape. You lack the time, energy, knowledge, or (HA)aptitude to make it realize what you want it to be.

Anyways, that's my rant.
