### Post by Kellemora on 2008-10-26
Hi LB

Congratulations and WELCOME!

I'm an old (and I mean that literally) WinDOZE diehard!

Messed with other Distro's a little and stumbled across Ubuntu by accident.

But in less than two months it has entirely changed my life and my business for the better!

Within only 5 weeks of learning a little bit about Ubuntu, I began the process of converting my entire office over to Ubuntu.

It now runs more efficiently and with a whole lot less problems.

Some things might seem daunting at first, but as soon as you pick up on them, you'll find that EVERYTHING in Ubuntu is much more LOGICAL than in the DOZE and the software designed for the Doze too......

Here is a very simple example, so you know what I mean.

If you wanted to change the format of a text document, where is the LOGICAL place to look to do this?  Under A. Format Page, or under B. File System?
Out of a hard learned habit, if you used msWord, Page Setup, which is a Formatting process is located under File System.  In OOwriter, it's under Format where it belongs.
So, if you're stuck using a new program, stop and think Logically and you'll find it quickly!
Normally that is!  Some of us old geezers don't know how to play with the fancy toys yet, as depicted by a few of my recent posts.  One would think I don't know how to read either, hi hi......

Have PHUN with Ubuntu
