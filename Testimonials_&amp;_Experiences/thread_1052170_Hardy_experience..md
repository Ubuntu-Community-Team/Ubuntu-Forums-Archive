### Post by chupys on 2009-01-27
I will write down in short what bothered me in the first hour of working with Hardy. I'm used to Suse distros and this was my first time using ubuntu.

Anyway, the main problem I encountered was the 64bit 4800 Linux driver from ATI for my 4850. (This is after I tried out the 32 bit one, which someone told me is already bugged). Anyway, I Installed the 64 8.12 driver through the terminal.

When using the driver in combination with compiz it bugs the ATI driver and it makes every video MPEG/AVI/whatever flicker constantly. The only rememedy is manually switching of compiz each and every time you want to use a video, aka I've stopped using compiz completely now, it's permanently off.

Rebooting got me a driver warning and it tossed me in 640*480 without any driver support. Manually loading the new driver again did nothing. Reinstalled ubuntu and didn't bother to load up a new driver again and probably won't because the driver is completely unsupported and untested.

I wish someone from ATI or someone experienced enough would actually get these drivers working. This will be the first reason why people not familiar with Ubuntu or with a terminal will go straight back to Vista, it's driver support.

Anyway, I still have Ubuntu on my system and will keep using it. I actually like it a lot, but the drivers need a lot of work. <3

### Post by solwic on 2009-01-27
Just an FYI to the OP:  the flickering is with every ATI card, not just yours.  It's a combination broken driver/broken Ubuntu that kills 3D and lags the ^%$#! out of your CPU when you run desktop effects.  

While nVidia generally works better, there are issues with them that ATI doesn't have (a bug in the driver, for instance, that makes certain movies play with screwy colors and a thick green bar down the left-center of the screen).  It's really a case of pick your poison.  No desktop effects with ATI, or only able to watch half your movies with nVidia.  :P

Good luck, whatever you decide.
