### Post by Zzooommm on 2006-07-23
OK, I'm pretty new to linux. My first encounter with it was when I decided to set up a family server to share our net connection (firewall, NAT, storage, stuff like that). I went thru every singe Windows version and in the end I realized it completely sucks. For simple desktop usage, listening to music, playing games it's perfectly OK but when you actually try to do something serious it fails on every front.
I've decided to stop the madness and decided to get linux. I just picked a distro out of the random at the time (it was mandrake). So in three days I had my server running on linux, something I before thought impossible. Ever since, it has worked perfectly. It takes a lot of time to set up, but once it's done it does it's job.

Today I installed ubuntu on my comp and I must say I'm impressed. A lot. It's so smooth, organised, it has excellent GUI response, and it's compact. 
I freaking love it.
