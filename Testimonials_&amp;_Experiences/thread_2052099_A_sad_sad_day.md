I've considered that myself. The only conclusion, I've reached  is that it's for "entertainment purposes only". The forums don't provide  Canonical with any data or feedback from this section and many of us  already know that the devs don't live here.

Historically, it's  entertained many a member and has been (in the past) a busy section of  the forums. From the forums perspective, I can envision that the T&E  provides members with another motive to continue returning and  participating. Major rule changes in the T&E have only been  implemented within the last year or so. IMHO these rules are  specifically designed to minimize the number of repetitive arguments and  eliminate certain undesirable behavior characteristics that  could reflect poorly on the community as a whole.

For example,  it's now not uncommon to read numerous response posts to a thread that  imparts "the use what works" theory. This is a true deviation from the  historical norm in this section. There have always been a few  individuals that responded that way, but (based on my observations) the  number has increased dramatically. Which in my view is a positive, as  it reflects a level of tolerance that our community should convey.  Assuming, we can accept the basic fact that not every user will find  Ubuntu/Linux acceptable - for whatever reason.

Just my $0.02 and of course I could be wrong. ;)
