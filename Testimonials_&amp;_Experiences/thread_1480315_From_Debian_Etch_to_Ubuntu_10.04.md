### Post by sparklet on 2010-05-11
Hi,
I have been a long time user of Debian - 9 years or so.
I think it is definitely the best Linux system around. Two things make it outstanding... it is rock solid and has the best package management system around.

I tried Ubuntu some years ago, round about the time it was first getting started, it didn't tempt me away from Debian.

I read a review about the new Ubuntu version that had just been released. It sounded good and screen shots looked attractive. A CD was duly bought and used in the "trial" method without installing. I was very impressed and went ahead and removed Debian Etch and installed Lucid in it's place.

I am very pleased with my Ubuntu Linux, it has the advantage of being Debian based and uses the Debian package handling system. It is however easier to get things working, I was impressed by the ease and speed of setting up my network printer. Everything else just works from networking to mounting cameras and USB sticks.

I think the desktop is very nice and everything just looks very good. I am very impressed and I reckon that Ubuntu has come a long way and has now got a very good product, both visually and also it is so easy to set up and use. It has persuaded me to switch entirely to Ubuntu Lucid.

It's a long way from the early days of Linux when you had to be a "brain surgeon" or astrophysicist to try and install it, having got it installed then the fun of getting anything to work involved hours of research and fiddling..... Oh! the joy when you finally had it set up and working !!!
