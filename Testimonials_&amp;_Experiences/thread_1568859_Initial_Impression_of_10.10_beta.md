### Post by thefish123 on 2010-09-05
Hi everyone,

I have just installed the 10.10 beta and I through I would share my initial impression with you.

The first thing I want to mention is that much to my delight the “snappiness” appears to be back.  Sometime ago after upgrading to 10.04 I wrote a question on this forum about my perceived lack of speed in 10.04 versus 9.10.  You can read that original post [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1473857").

Anyway, I am glad to say that with 10.10 it just feels quicker again.  Applications like gedit and gcalctool open immediately whereas on 10.04 there seemed to be an almost imperceptible “lag” (although obviously sufficiently perceptible that I noticed it).  I have no idea what might have caused this but the hardware I am testing this on is exactly the same – as in the same physical computer.

The other thing I want to mention is that my 32” Samsung TV comes up with a clean viewable resolution when I connect the external VGA cable.  You can read about my complaint about this from 10.04 [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1483744").

Now, just for the record, as with 10.04 my 32” Samsung still comes up as a 40” Samsung TV but I don't really care as long as I can set the resolution to something that works and can watch my Internet-based TV ([www.casttv.com](www.casttv.com)) on my actual TV (instead of being forced to either watch it on my notebook or reboot into Windows).

So, so far the 10.10 beta is a MUCH better release for me then 10.04.  I guess I can say that 10.04 just wasn't that good for me sines this is the first time I have upgraded to a pre-release.

There are a few things I was a little surprised at and perhaps a little disappointed with.

1) I didn't get a chance to enter a hostname for my notebook.  I like to name my computers something short and memorable like LGS1 (my notebook is an LG and is the S1 model).  Now my notebook is called myname-laptop which is a bit longer then I would like but... OK... no biggie and I guess I can change it after the fact.

2) The other thing I noticed was that upon initial login I got “Canadian English”.  Now this makes sense because I live in Canada and I set my timezone to “Toronto” but I am sorry... I can't stand seeing “Color” spelled with a “U”.  So I was pretty happy to noticed that I could change my language before logging in.  Oh, and don't get me wrong... it is pronounced “zed” not “zee”. But seriously, who spells center with the “er” backwards :-)

Anyway, good work on 10.10!

The Fish.

### Post by argile on 2010-09-06
I love this Ubuntu 10.10 beta who is installed perfectly on my fakeRAID hard drives. Grub was installed just fine (I just have to specify the partition where my linux was installed) and that was not the case on Ubuntu 10.04.

I installed flash and moonlight (silverlight) without any problem at all inside firefox.

The installation of my Sharp LCD TV was not a problem. I installed and configured (NVIDIA X Server Settings) the nvidia driver without any bugs with this tool : System -> Administration -> Additional Drivers. Even the sound that came out from the HDMI jack of my video card works : I installed the Realtek High Definition Audio Codecs (on the web site of Realtek) by followed the instructions in the Readme.txt and I installed Gnome ALSA mixer from the Ubuntu Software Centre on witch I marked IEC958 to unmute the HDMI sound.

Ubuntu 10.10 is great!
