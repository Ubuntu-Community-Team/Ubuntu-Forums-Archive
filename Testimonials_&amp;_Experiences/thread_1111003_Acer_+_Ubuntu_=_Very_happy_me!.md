### Post by Xyphoid on 2009-03-30
I bought an Acer Aspire One with XP on it last week.
I turned it on, filled in my name and then it started to auto install. After 10 minutes it was ready and XP booted. I clicked on Internet Explorer to see if I could check my mail....And IE crashed and froze XP!!!!!! ](*,) Deep in my heart I was expecting it to crash, but not on the third mouse click!

I didn´t even bother to check why it crashed. I downloaded Ubuntu and burned a cd (I was already planning to install Ubuntu, but I needed to check if the laptop was working, so that´s why I booted XP first). After half an hour Ubuntu was up and running. Had some trouble with the wifi, but with the help of [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/AspireOne) that was also working within half an hour \\:D/

I read stories on the internet that there were trouble with the wifi, cardreaders, webcam and sound on the Acer One. But I had to get rid of XP and am not afraid to tinker with Ubuntu or ask help in this forum. But after the installation of Ubuntu almost everything worked out of the box (except wifi as I told before).

Still having some trouble with the battery indicater which sometimes doesn´t change status from batt to AC and back. If anyone has a solution, please post it here.

I´m not a Linux guru, but with Ubuntu and the help of this forum I feel like one :lolflag:

And I´m not saying that Windows is bad, because I have used it for many years (never had any virus. At least not one I have noticed or that bothered me). But Linux just gives me the feeling I am really using my pc and have control over how it works. At the moment working on trying to get Ubuntu to boot a little faster and some other tweaking stuff.

I am so in love with my Ubuntu Acer :P
Well, I just had to get rid of my 2 cents. Hope you enjoyed it :)
