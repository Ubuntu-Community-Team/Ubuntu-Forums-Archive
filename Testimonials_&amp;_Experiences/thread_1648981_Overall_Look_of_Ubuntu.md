### Post by BugStalker on 2010-12-19
Let me be blunt - it is my best virtue.

Ubuntu is functional, even popular but it has one major defect.

Its overall look is, well, cartoon-ish at best.

I can NOT be seen in public, using it in a professional (read: Business) environment using Ubuntu.

I use SUSE regularly at work.  I will NEVER use Ubuntu in a workplace as long as it does not have a business look and feel.

I get it - there are sub-versions of ubuntu that can have a more business like look.
I also get that those are SUB versions - not the main version, and therefore does not have the same support that the rest of the ubuntu community has.

Until Ubuntu becomes more business like in its look and feel, it will NEVER be accepted in the average workplace, and therefore will NEVER take its rightful place as the leading, most used operating system/distrobution in the world.

The most used operating system at home, will be the one most used in schools and workplaces.  Schools will teach the ones most used in workplaces, and the one most used in workplaces will ALWAYS be the one that appears most businesslike.

I would hope that the Ubuntu community would take this information in the spirit it is meant.  Glitz and gloss is nice, but I would prefer to have linux rule the workplace - which it will never do until the workplace in general accepts it as what it is - a superior operating system.  It already IS a superior operating system - so why isn't it accepted?  Because when my boss looks over my shoulder - he wonders what GAME I am playing - because Ubuntu looks like a game, a toy, a cartoon.  Not like a business machine designed to perform work.

Thanks for listening.  Hopefully you aren't offended, and take my comments as they are intended - you've got good things going - you simply need to make them LOOK more like good things.
