### Post by adl80l on 2007-09-13
Greetings,

 About a month ago my wife found an opened box deal on an HP vista machine.  While she was making the purchase I began preparing for an alternative OS.  We havent had any problems with her machine yet, other than slow file transfers.  I think she may have bought Gate's personal copy of vista from listening to others experiences.

 I use a few Linux based apps at work, other than that,  I have very little experience with it.  I have contemplated taking up Linux as a hobby for a couple of years anyway.  Despite the lack of problems I anticipated with vista,   now that I had already downloaded Ubuntu and read about it.   Not playing with it was not an option for me. :)

  I ran the live cd one of my pcs and liked what I saw.  After using it for a few days, my plan is to install it on 1 of my 2 xp pcs that are wired.  The wired ether connections are in my basement, every other machine in my house uses wifi.  Being able to use Ubuntu wirelessly will determine whether we have several Ubuntu machines and one Windows, or vice versa.  

 So far, I have installed Ubuntu on one old laptop that was destined for the trash at work.  Its a P3 700 mhz w/ 256 mb ram. The laptop doesnt have a cd drive but I found link here that allowed an online installation that worked flawlessly.  :)

  The laptop has a netgear ma521 wifi card that I was able to get running with ndiswrapper, but it only worked for a couple hours.  While I was following everyone's instructions on how to set up that particular wifi card I noticed on the manufacturers web site that a set of drivers for Fedora are availible.  I havent seen anyone mention this set of drivers, but I will probably test them out anyway. 

 Wifi aside,  if I measure difficulty in the amount of time consumed setting up and troubleshooting something I think Ubuntu is at a min equal to windows and great for salvaging old hardware.  Its just a matter of learning different commands and options.  With the all the support here its been a breeze so far.  

 I look forward to tinkering with Samba and Beryl over the next couple of weeks.

adl80l
