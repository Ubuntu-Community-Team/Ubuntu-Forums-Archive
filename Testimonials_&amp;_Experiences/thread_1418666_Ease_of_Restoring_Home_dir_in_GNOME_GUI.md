### Post by Anzan on 2010-02-28
I've just set running a copy of the home directory of a friend from a backup on a server to a new box just using Nautilus. The GUI is completely friendly, relatively fast as it's not CLI but showing pictures of stuff, and will be done in 3 hours. A restart and their home dir (on its own partition) will be just as it was. (I'd saved the packages list and reinstalled that already.)

I am amazed and very grateful.

Thanks to all: Mark and Canonical, Ubuntu, Debian, GNOME, GNU and all whether upstream or downstream.

I've been using Ubuntu since 7.04 and the contrast between the drawn out agonies of over two decades of using Apple and then Microsoft products and the sanity of Linux in general and Ubuntu in particular strikes me several times a day.

But this is really just extraordinary. It used to take a week or so just to get a Windows box more or less to what it used to be with pushing in and pulling out dozens of CDs, finding registrations, fiddling with settings and so on.
