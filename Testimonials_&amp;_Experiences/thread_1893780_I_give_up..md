Ubuntu isn't for everyone, because not everyone really enjoys computers as much as they simply enjoy using them.  However, that means you need to pay for support, which is the reason why Ubuntu is free.  It is a business, not a hand-out. Ubuntu provides commercial paid support.  If these particular kinds of users do not have the necessary mad skills or know-how to work with computers or learn how to use Ubuntu on their own, by themselves, well then, they have to pay for help, just like any technological business, which is really what this is about.  Users sometimes forget this very simple fact, either due to some strange idea about entitlement or whatever, but there is a reason why the OS is free. If you can't afford to pay for support, and you can't learn how to use it on your own, then you guys are going to have to pay for it regardless of whatever operating system you eventually settle on using. Everything is a trade-off, but not everyone understands this sadly. You can be the biggest idiot, and get paid support to login remotely and fix your computers these days for a very small fee, just like everyone else who needs computer support but can't fix it by themselves. Complaining about having to pay for support when the OS is free is kinda unreasonable, imho.
