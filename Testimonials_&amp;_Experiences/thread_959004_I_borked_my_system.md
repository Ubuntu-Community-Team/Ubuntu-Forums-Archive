### Post by Riffer on 2008-10-26
So I did something silly, what I not quite sure, but it caused a complete lockup.  And after a hard reset I couldn't get onto my desktop.  GDM came up fine but after that just a blank orange screen.  I was able to restart my session with a failsafe terminal, but what to do?  I sat there in panic, confused, unsure, I didn't know what I did so didn't know where to go or what to do.

It was then that the geek god of linux "Torv" whispered in my ear.  
"Upgrade, you've got nothing to lose, it'll be fine."
Quicker then good sense I typed in that faithful command, "sudo update-manager -d" and without a seconds ponder I hit ENTER. Well with over a gig of download I was able to take sweetie out to the big craft fair so that when I got home setup was almost done.  20 minutes and a couple of prompts I was done, time for reboot.

Blank screen, grub and then the familiar loading screen with the OK's flying by.  Oh my custom GDM screen is the same, ok name and password put in.  Now to see if I get to a desktop, any desktop, I can reconfigure a desktop, oh please just get me to a desktop.  And there it is,

Wait a minute its my old desktop!  Way cool.  Its all there way to go Ubuntu.  Hold on something's different, its the same but not quite.  I try out some programs and they all work fine but something is change its not quite the same as Hardy.  I know its the theme, its consistent.  No little differences in FireFox, or in any app that I've tried. 

So in my silly way I just wanted to say thanks.  Not only did the upgrade fix my system, but the improvements while subtle is IMO a major step forward.  My desktop while the looking the same has a more polished look and feel.
