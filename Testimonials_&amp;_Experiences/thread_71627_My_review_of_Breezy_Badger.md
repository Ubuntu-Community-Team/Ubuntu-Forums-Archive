### Post by mstlyevil on 2005-10-03
I have to say that now I have installed 5.10, I am very impressed with it. I had tried a few weeks ago to install it and was dissapointed that I was unable to find the extra repositories I needed to get the programs and codecs that I wanted. That has all changed now. I found a thread listing the extra repositories, and now I am able to get all the programs that are featured on the Ubuntu guide. Almost everything works except Realplayer will not play and neither will lime wire.
 I am sure I will find fixes for them soon. 5.10 just seems to run faster and I did not have issues with setting DMA on my drives as I did in 5.04.  DVD's play in xine flawlessly and firefox seems to load faster. OO.o 2.0 is a excellent improvement on the previous OO.o. It has more eye candy and it has more of a Word feel to it.  The add applications tool is one of the best improvements for noob's. Instead of asking a dozen people how to find a program they want, they now may be able to find it right on their desktop ready to install. Moving the terminal from the system tools to the accessories menu does not make much sense though. However, it is less confusing for noob's now that the terminal is just one icon instead of two. Overall Breezy Badger is a great improvement over Hoary Hedgehog. Thank you Ubuntu Development team and Mark Shuttleworth for the free gift. (Free as in speech and free as in beer.)=D>
