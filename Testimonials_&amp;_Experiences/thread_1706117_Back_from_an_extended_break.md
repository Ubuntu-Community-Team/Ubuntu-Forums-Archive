### Post by guru1ofatl on 2011-03-13
Our house has been running Linux as the sole OS for several years now. I dabbled with Ubuntu early on and thought it was nice but too rough around the edges.

For the past few years I have been running PCLOS starting with 2007 up to the 2010 version. It has been a great OS, possibly the easiest to install but relatively slow compared to some of the other distros.

Recently I happened to watch a few videos on youtube that covered Ubuntu 10.10. I was impressed by the speed and how well put together it seemed. I spent the next few weeks reading up on it before finally making the switch.

Now that it is installed and I have had the chance to play with it for a bit I must say that I am impressed. Ubuntu has come a LONG way. My boot time is half of what it was with PCLOS E17, the OS seems a bit faster in some respects, and it simply seems to fit me a bit better.

PCLOS is still more configurable (KDE/E17) and easier to install on some hardware (laptops specifically) but it looks as if Ubuntu will be my OS of choice. Now to install it on the rest of the PCs on my network.
