### Post by mukiex on 2008-05-04
All right, so let's get the bad out of the way first :

- My Geforce 8600 doesn't auto-detect for the hardware driver thing. This card's already a year old, and there's been a driver available practically since day one. Not a big deal for me 'cause I use Envy, but just to be kept in mind. UPDATE : Scratch this, doesn't show up in the LiveCD, DOES show up once installed.
- I think autocomplete in the terminal got ravaged in this version. I remember it was non-existent in alpha and it doesn't seem to show up in Mplayer when it used to.
- I'm not even gonna try my wacom tablet. Wacom support's been **** in Ubuntu since day one for no good ******* reason. There's zero reason not to turn it on by default in xorg.conf and LinuxWacom's latest stable should have been part of Ubuntu from the get-go. Ubuntu "Studio" not supporting it is borderline retarded.
- Wine still looks like poo. Not Ubuntu's fault.

And now that that's off my chest, the good :

-A2DP support may just be the best on Hardy. Bluesoleil (doesn't *exist* on Windows XP otherwise) doesn't support double-pairing (if you attempt this it kills the Windows audio manually even after you're done with your cell phone). Leopard is way-easier, but I'll take going into the dark dungeon of text-edit/terminal land over a delay I can't do anything about. The audio delay (at least using mplayer so far) is practically non-existent in Hardy. For someone with bluetooth headphones this is a Godsend. Resuming from turning off a phone seems a little flaky at the moment tho. We'll see how well this turns out overall.


* A2DP is a bluetooth option that lets you listen to full stereo audio on bluetooth headphones. Some also come equipped with a mic, and let you do something called "double pairing". This lets you pair the stereo audio part to a computer or music player and the mono/mic headset part to your phone. Whenever you get a phone call, the audio cuts off from the computer/player so you can take the call, and when it's done, the audio from the computer/player comes back. There's also next/previous track buttons on most A2DP headsets, which is also convinient.
