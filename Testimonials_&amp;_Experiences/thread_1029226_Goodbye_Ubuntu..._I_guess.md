### Post by blueridgedog on 2009-01-03
Based on your post, your chief objections are that the installation of applications was tedious, that the graphical display was buggy and that the overall performance was not up to your expectations.

I would wager that the graphics and performance issues are one and the same.  My PC is very fast running Ubuntu, fast as measured by its ability to do work. The fastness is not translated into fast window drawing as the support for my graphics card in Linux is still immature (ATI).  Load up Vista Ultimate on your PC and get back to me :-)

Others have commented on the installation of software and I can only add to it that finding a software program on the net that you want, then clicking System -> Administration -> Synaptic Package Manager and searching for it and clicking it is pretty easy.  Or clicking Applications -> Add/Remove.

A broader point at play is the nature of Linux.  Linux is the global OS.  Linux is the OS of the future by virtue of its openness.  It is a way for all people to have a computer and tools to run on that computer.  Consider the other reality.  Assuming you do not pirate software, a windows system with an office suite and a collection of four or five other programs will cost $1,000 in software alone, not including the cost of the hardware.  Linux empowers people;  It gives them the power to be part of the information age.  It is not competing with Windows just as a clinic in a mission doesn't compete with a hospital.  Its core mission is about the people who use it and I think it is beautiful.
