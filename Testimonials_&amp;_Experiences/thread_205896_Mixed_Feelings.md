### Post by cas194 on 2006-06-29
Generally, I'm a fan of Ubuntu but I do have some reservations. I was irritated by the apparent absence of a proper Superuser login, but this is less of a problem since I discovered how to work around it. (Many of the irritations can be overcome through experiment, but why should the user have to?)

Two worries remain:

(1) Successive releases seem to be moving closer to the M*cr*s*ft philosophy: "You will do things OUR way." Why, for example, do what MS has done with the command prompt by hiding it behind menus-within-menus?

(2) I find the slogan "Linux for human beings" really offensive. What exactly is being suggested? That other Linux distributions are not fit for humans to use? I can understand the urge to encourage Ubuntu use, but is Canonical helping the Linux cause by reinforcing its reputation for being difficult?

I'm happy to use Ubuntu for now, but I can foresee the day when the petty annoyances lead me to dump it in favour of a distribution which is closer to the original UNIX approach.

CAS

p.s. I forgot to mention that ghastly brown splash screen!

My understanding is that the *human* in **Linux for Human Beings** is based on the idea of *ubuntu* meaning > Humanity toward others

More of #2 than #4, in other words: >    1. Of, relating to, or characteristic of humans: the course of human events; the human race.
  ** 2. Having or showing those positive aspects of nature and character regarded as distinguishing humans from other animals: an act of human kindness.**
   3. Subject to or indicative of the weaknesses, imperfections, and fragility associated with humans: a mistake that shows he's only human; human frailty.
   4. Having the form of a human.
   5. Made up of humans: formed a human bridge across the ice. You can read more about this issue in [this thread](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=70603).
