### Post by sillyxone on 2008-05-30
background: Ubuntu user since Dapper, using Gutsy on Dell D620 (laptop provided at work)

My 1420N came with Gutsy pre-installed. I've been having a few bugs on Gutsy now and then but the D620 is my production machine, didn't have a chance to upgrade yet so I want to try Hardy. Beside, Intel X3100 on 1420N is not supported under Gutsy so no Compiz.

It took 20 minutes to install Hardy on it, another 20 minutes to install the updates. Wireless and Compiz work upon installation finished (Intel 3945, no proprietary driver needed). Spent 2 more hours customizing it (font, theme, Compiz, Firefox, printers, keyboard shortcuts, install more software ...)

Hardy is very impressive. Webcam, sound, and bluetooth just work. My HP All-in-one printer just plug-and-work, including its scanner (Xsane is way faster and lightweight than the bloat HP scanning software on Windows). The 1420N is very similar to the D620 but yet I feel it's much smoother with Hardy. I noticed most of the bugs in Gutsy are gone. Suspend works really well, both suspend and resume are faster than Gutsy on D620 and more consistent. Firefox 3, although beta, is a huge improvement. All in all, Hardy is much better both in performance and usability. I think Dell should start shipping with Hardy pre-installed instead of Gutsy.

BTW, this laptop is for my wife, who only used Ubuntu on my D620 a few times, but I believed that she would have no problem making the switch. For me, Ubuntu is way more superior than Windows, but a few buggy things in Gutsy kept holding me back from installing it on my relatives' machines. Heh, from now on, if anyone asks me to fix a Windows machine with spyware/virus on it, either it will be Ubuntu or just take it somewhere else.

At work, I'm in a mix environment of Windows and OSX on AD, but yet haven't had any problem with Ubuntu being the only OS on my laptop. I wasn't so sure about switching completely two years ago, but now, I know for sure that I'm walking on a wide open road ahead.


1420N specs: Intel T7500, 2GB 667MHz, 160GB SATA @ 5400, 8x DVD-RW, 14" 1400x900, Intel X3100, bluetooth, WLAN

D620 specs: Intel T7200, 2GB 667MHz, 80GB SATA @ 5400, 8x DVD-RW, 14" 1400x900, Intel 945GM, WLAN
