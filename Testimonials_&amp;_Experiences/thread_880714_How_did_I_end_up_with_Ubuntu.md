### Post by bytor4232 on 2008-08-08
> **powerpleb said:**
> It's more like GNOME than KDE. Only it's a bit simpler and bit more lightweight, plus it has some important (for me) features that GNOME lacks, like flexible key bindings. And they are not hidden in a labyrinth of settings windows like KDE.

You can get the flexible keybindings in Compiz.  Look in the general options, you can assign anything to the 10 commands, and bind said commands to any key combo.

I've been using Linux since 1998.  Back when KDE was new, it was where its at.  KDE1 was tons of fun, but I dropped it when KDE2 came out all bloated and whatnot.  I started using Debian way back in the Potato days, but I decided to drop Debian due to personal reasons, I couldn't get behind their renaming of Firefox to Icedove.  So I was on the hunt for a new Linux distro, and Ubuntu seemed to be taking over the world ( it still is ) so I welcomed our new Linux-based overlords with 7.04, and its been Nirvana ever since.

I use both Ubuntu and Xubuntu.  When I have an older machine, like a K62-500 that I use as a terminal at my work or my old 500 Mhz Compaq notebook, it has to be Xubuntu.  It breaths new life into such old hardware.  Or if I want to put a gui on a server, and want it preserve resources for the busy services, its definately Xubuntu (XFCE).

However on my modern Laptop (1.8 Celeron M with 1 gig or ram) or my desktop (Multi head dual core 2.8 with 2 gigs of ram) I definately treat myself to Ubuntu, Compiz and all its glorious and tasty eye candy, bells, and whistles.  Hey, if you got the resources, use em, and treat yourself to something nice.

Besides, Ubuntu with Gnome has a tendency to get a lot of features that Xubuntu misses, like power management features and multimedia keys.
