### Post by Alex566 on 2009-10-11
I recently bought and built a box for my personal use and was going to install OSx86 onto it. While I was going through the (painful) process, I typed something wrong and that prevented it from booting. So I grabbed the nearest LiveCD to fix it, and it was Ubuntu 9.04 64bit. While I was fixing the file, I remembered how awesome Ubuntu was and installed it instead of OSx86. Being daring, I just had to upgrade to 9.10 Beta, an I LOVE it! It was a super easy install and the new graphics on boot-up are very nice looking. My nVidia's driver was found and I was notified; very cool. While I most likely will try to install OSx86 again, I will dual-boot with Ubuntu.

Specs:
Mobo: Gigabyte EP45-UD3P
Processer: Intel Core 2 Quad 3.0 gegahertz
HD: Western Digital 640 GB

And just a question, is it just me or does it feel like the Ubuntu update servers are sssssllllloooooowwww. I changed to archive.kernel.org and it seems a lot faster.
