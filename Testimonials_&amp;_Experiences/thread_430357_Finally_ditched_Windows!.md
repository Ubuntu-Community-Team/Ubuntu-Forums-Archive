### Post by Ubunted on 2007-05-02
I've been on-and-off Ubuntu ever since the Hoary RCs, but it has not always been smooth. While Ubuntu has always served me well on my Thinkpad laptops, as my primary desktop OS it has been found wanting. Flash, Java, buggy Azureus and crummy ATI drivers kept it off my main desktop.

When I built my current PC, I made sure to keep Linux-compatible as possible. This meant nForce chipsets and Nvidia graphics. It all worked extremely well under Windows.

Just this past Saturday I decided to give Ubuntu another shot. Feisty has just come out - why not try it?

To make a long story short - it is four days later and Windows is completely gone from both hard drives. I sold all my old games a few weeks ago and do most of my gaming on my DS now. The only PC game I play - WoW - actually runs *BETTER* than it did under Windows - load times are less than HALF of what they used to be! Ingame play is indistinguishable from Windows. The only bugs are well-known and minor and don't affect gameplay at all. Imagine if Blizzard released a native Linux client!

Flash works. Java works. Both were stupidly easy to install. Bittorrent - well I don't use Azureus anymore and am currently hunting for a uTorrent replacement, but so far the built-in client is working well enough. I even got all the buttons on my MX510 mouse working with just a few new lines in xorg.conf - a BIG step from the series of flaming hoops it was a few releases ago! The automatic codec installations were a real godsend. I find myself spending less and less time in the command line with every release. I didn't have to fight with the Nvidia drivers and the i386/k7 and SMP kernels to get optimal performance. Fonts are better on my LCD. Themes are nicer. I could go on and on.

Now my only regret is spending $150 on an OEM CD of XP Pro x64 that I am now stuck with. Ah well, I suppose when the time comes to sell this rig I can reinstall it.

Next stop - 64-bit Ubuntu!
