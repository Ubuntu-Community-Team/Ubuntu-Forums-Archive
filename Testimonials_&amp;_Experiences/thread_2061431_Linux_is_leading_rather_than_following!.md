### Post by exploder on 2012-09-22
For all of the complaints I see about Unity I am completely thrilled with it. Linux is no longer trying to play catch up with other operating systems. Ever since Gnome Shell came into the picture things have changed for the best! Because distros did not like the direction Gnome Shell was taking, Unity and Cinnamon have come to exist and it's the best thing that ever could have happened.

I look at Unity today and see features being introduced that no other user interface has. Linux to me is now leading the way to the future of computing for the masses and I never would have imagined all of this a few years ago. I see people I work with switching to Linux and they are doing it all on their own with no arm twisting or Linux evangelism. People are liking what they see on their computer screens and I think that it is because distros like Ubuntu and Mint are giving them choices that they really want rather than just Windows clones.

These are the most exciting times I have ever seen! I am thrilled to read or watch videos about new features in Ubuntu and Mint. It's great to see distros coming up with new ideas and new ways of doing things and I hope this trend keeps right on going. These are the good times we have all hoped for and have patiently waited for.

### Post by exploder on 2012-09-22
I look at omgubuntu.com most everyday now and see good things happening. I used to feel like Ubuntu was getting a lot of press over nothing but since the introduction of Unity I feel Ubuntu is doing something that deserves recognition. 

Since the popularity of Android devices, everyone is trying to re-invent the desktop. Unity has introduced some very creative things and it just gets better with each release. I have tried Gnome Shell and it is not bad but Unity seems to be developing at a much faster pace and with more innovative ideas being introduced.

My step-dad liked Unity when he used my computer and went out of his way to install 12.04 on his high end laptop. My step-dad has always been a Windows fan and I was amazed to see him so enthusiastic about Unity. Also, a 60 year old woman at work installed Ubuntu 12.04 on her computers at home and said she loved the interface. These people chose Ubuntu 12.04 all on their own and are genuinely happy to be running it and that says a lot to me.

I will say that I liked the out of the box accessibility features that Gnome Shell has because I recently suffered from some very serious eye problems but Unity by default was also a good choice and was usable for me under the circumstances I had to deal with. 

Seeing game developers and other software developers taking interest in Linux because of Unity is also very encouraging to me. Unity has inspired much of this interest and it benefits all Linux users. I am seeing more positive things going on for Linux than ever before and I do not think that it is a passing fad. I think developers are seeing Linux as a leader rather than a follower now and they want to cash in on the success they are seeing.  

Windows and Apple are always going to be around but it is great seeing Linux heading toward being a real mainstream choice. I have a very positive outlook on where the Linux desktop is heading.
