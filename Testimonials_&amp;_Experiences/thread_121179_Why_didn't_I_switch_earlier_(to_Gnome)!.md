### Post by el3ktro on 2006-01-24
I was using KDE ever since, on Mandrake, SuSE, Gentoo, then Kubuntu. I just loved it, I loved it's configurability, it's features. I got used to it, and I know a lot about it. I never really came to try Gnome, I had Red Hat installed 2 years ago for a few days, but didn't like it (I HATE RPM's you know).

So now it happened that I decided to try out Dapper Flight-3, and actually accidentaly I downloaded Ubuntu instead of Kubuntu. Well I found this out after I burnt it and booted from it, but then I just thought oh well let's install this one anyway (I always have a spare partition for such things). The install went fine, and wow Gnome really impressed me. I don't know why, but it was just somehow so much cooler. It was really slim, easy & clean.

I always thought (as many KDE users do) that Gnome was just too minimalistic, but I think - after playing with it - that this minimalism is in no way too minimalistic, but that it just reduces the desktop to what you actually need. As an example, I plugged my Digikam in, a window popped up, I just entered a name for this "film" and all my photos got downloaded. I was impressed. This was easier than ever. All the apps are just reduced to what you need. I instantly liked gThumb, it has all I need, and no unneccesary stuff. I can view & manage my photos, I can batch rename them, do some little manipulating stuff - perfect. On KDE, I always switched between Digikam, Showimg and Gwenview - but they none of them really ever was right. Showimg's interface is a nightmare, Digikam - though it is intented to be an app for digital cameras - has the photo-downloading function hidden somewhere between all the menus.

It continues with other apps: Why did I never try Totem earlier! The perfect video player! I open a file, and there's just a play & pause button and a position slider. The only thing I miss (on all video players) is a button to put the video on the TV screen with Xinerama etc. On KDE, I tried Kaffeine, KMplayer, KPlayer and lots of others, but they've never been right. They where to complex, buggy, or something else. But Totem is just right.

Well I could continue, but you know what I would write ... ;-) Gnome actually has enough configurability imho, the panel layout is pretty much what I always used on my KDE desktop (large application buttons at the bottom, taskbar on the top, desktp switcher on the right, clock on the left), and I chose some other colors, icons & metacity themes, and now it looks really wonderful (and xcompmgr does the rest :-) )

So, welcome Gnome world, you have a new user. I now installed Ubuntu 5.10, just to be on a stable basis, and Gnome got me convinced of itself within just a 30 minutes or so. I love it!


Tom
