### Post by Elzar on 2008-12-02
A long-time (and generally happy) XP user, I began my journey with Ubuntu about seven months ago, when I bought a new laptop. I made sure that I wasn't buying any hardware that would cause me problems with Linux, and set it to dual boot XP and Hardy (64-bit). Now, the only reason I boot into XP is to test out websites I've designed work okay in IE.

My experience has generally been good. It was a bit disorienting at first, but now I'm completely comfortable with, for instance, using the terminal.

I upgraded to 8.10 soon after its release, and I've experienced no problems at all. 

The only intermittent problem I've encountered has been due to Flash on Firefox, but now even that is fixed.

And I've got used to using GIMP instead of Photoshop to the extent that I now prefer GIMP.

If any developers are reading this, I just want to say a big thanks. Escaping Windows feels like what I expect escaping a religious cult or escaping a drug addiction must be like. The sense of freedom is really exhilarating!

Cheers!

### Post by Elzar on 2008-12-02
I forgot one other reason I used to boot into XP was to make backups of DVDs I buy. My program of choice is DVD Decrypter, and although I had heard that it's possible to run this in Wine, I just never got round to having a go because I don't buy many DVDs.

Anyway, I just installed Wine, installed DVD Decrypter, made one very minor change to the comparability settings, and ripped a DVD. As simple as can be!

I also installed WinDirStat, as I prefer it to the equivalent Linux programs I've tried. Again, worked as well as it does on XP.

{sigh} I love Ubuntu.
