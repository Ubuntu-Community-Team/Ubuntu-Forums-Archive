### Post by bobbo85 on 2008-07-24
I wrote a while back about my first time using Ubuntu ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4199120#post4199120](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4199120#post4199120)), and in so many ways it was miles ahead of Windows; but there were some very basic things that just killed it for me.  

So after a few months of using Windows, I installed Kubuntu KDE4 to give that a shot this week.  Unfortunately, I have had more, and scarier, things happen here than I ever did in Windows!

My Very First Kubuntu Startup:
After a few updates installed, I managed to fix the clock to be 12 hours:-)

I then went to system settings and checked "enable desktop effects" and poof!  Nothing but a black screen with a mouse pointer.  Restarting was no help- I could see the log in screen, but then back to the black screen.  This time the forums, google searches, and even the IRC Channels were of no help.  I tried setting "compositing" to enabled in some file, I tried reconfiguring xserver or something - no luck.  Finally, someone told me I could just delete my .kde4 folder and it would restart as if I'd just clean installed.  This probably wouldn't have been a great fix if I'd been using the system for a while!

I got into an IRC chat using konversation, which worked just fine, and I asked if the youtube problem had been fixed in the last six months since I left... "yes it has" - great!  I opened up firefox 3, started up one of the featured videos on youtube... *facepalm.  Full screen still stutters horribly.

Oh well, I heard flash 10 was supposed to be a huge update for linux eventually.

Next thing I tried to do was install "samba" and "printers" so I could see the shared folder and printer on my office network.  In adept, there are 3 versions of each - this is confusing, but I'm guessing one is the back end for terminal stuff, and the other two are gnome/kde GUI's... Anyhow, I tried to install one of each, and without warning Bam!  The screen went black, none of the keys responded, no mouse cursor... I had to just hard reboot with the power button.

So as much as I love Linux, the community, the ideology of it all, it just kills me to have what amounts to a BSOD (blue screen of death) twice in the first day, especially considering this is a work computer- all I tried to do was change my minimize/maximize window animation, and be able to "see" the shared printer and folders at work.


To be honest though, I'm kind of like a wife in an abusive relationship...  Linux will kick my *** and ruin my day, but I still love it... and I'll probably be back in again tomorrow.

PS If anyone can help me figure out the youtube and/or printer thing, that would really make my day.  Thanks in advance!

### Post by Methuselah on 2008-07-24
Sorry about your problems.
If you take each individually and post a topic on the help or absolute beginner forum you might get more assistance.
Be sure to use a descriptive title so people will be quick to notice.

With certain kind of hardware there can be issues.
ATI's driver just doesn't work for me.
I have had everything from a white screen to sudden reboot on logging in.
The open source drivers are fine except that the hardy version doesn't support 3D.

I have never had a problem with fullscreen flash in linux.
However, there do seem to be problems and there have been requests to adobe to fix them.

It's a recurring theme:
Quite a bit of instability in linux comes from proprietary code that no one but the companies that own it can fix.
