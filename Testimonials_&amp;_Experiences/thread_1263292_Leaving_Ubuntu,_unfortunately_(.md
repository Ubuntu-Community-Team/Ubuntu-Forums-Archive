### Post by Jimleko211 on 2009-09-10
Honestly, I've about had it with Ubuntu.  I tried out KDE on here, and tried updating to KDE 4.3, and it just completely screwed up everything.  If it was just isolated to KDE, sure, that's fine.  But something about it makes my GNOME user switcher applet just stop working.

So I was in the shower when I had an epiphany.  On Windows, especially 7, I never had these crashes.  Never had instability, never had broken updates.  Yeah I may hate the proprietary nature of the OS, but in the long run, that only affects developers (practically, not ideologically).

I love Linux.  But I love playing my games, speaking to my girlfriend through teamspeak on the server I'm hosting on windows (actually able to do it there, haven't been able to figure it out on Linux), love the rock-hard stability of Windows 7.  Yeah, I realize that I have it better than most in that department because I basically know Windows like the back of my hand, and I know what to avoid.

But for me, I think Windows 7 is the best thing.  It may not have the ease-of-installation that Linux Repos might have.  It may not have Compiz.  But it has the latest games (which is more important to me than I like to admit to myself whenever I just run Linux), it has support of products that most of my friends use.

I don't think I've become friends with anyone on this forum, but I love the community here, I love the amount of developers here, the sanity of this community over most internet forums.  So I don't think I'll leave the forums, and maybe I'll even run Ubuntu in a virtual machine, to ease my urge to come back when it inevitably comes.

"Why not dual boot?" you might say.  I've tried Dual-booting.  When you plan on hosting a teamspeak server for a guild once SWTOR comes out, that becomes extremely impractical.  Plus, it's kind of a pain in my behind to have to reboot just to play KOTOR2.  For those who just have one or two apps holding them back, that they use sparingly, it's the perfect solution.  It isn't for me.

I would like to say thank you to this entire community for helping me with most of the issues I've had with Ubuntu.  I'd like to thank Canonical for making such a great product that has given me great educational opportunities, such a great OS that it makes me kind of sad to leave it.

Most of all I'd like to thank my girlfriend, who has given me such great moral support over the low points of my Linux run, when I didn't know wtf I was doing (relatively to now), when I had such problems getting Compiz to work correctly, to make pidgin make sound when using KDE on Arch.  And for getting me to think straight during this whole ordeal, before I punched a hole through my moniter.  She's not a member of these forums, or even a Linux user, but she's been the wall on which I've leaned on many times, and will so for many more times in the future.

For all who have no had problems with Ubuntu, or had only minor problems, I hope that you never go through the infuriating things I went through.  On paper it might not sound so bad, but in my head, it's the spark that caused the fire.  I hope that one day, Ubuntu will be a significant OS in terms of overall marketshare, and that I'll hear my neighbors talking about the latest Ubuntu release.
