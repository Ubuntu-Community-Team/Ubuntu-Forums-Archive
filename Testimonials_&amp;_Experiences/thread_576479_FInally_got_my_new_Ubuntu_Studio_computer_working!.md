### Post by theorganloft on 2007-10-15
It has been months to get back to this. It is not because there is any wrong with Ubuntu Studio, but because I needed more time and resources to put into the project.  

I built a new computer using a Biostar MOBO, 1Gb Ram, 120Gb IDE HD (I did not have a SATA available on my shelf), Dual Sided DVD, and a M-audio Delta Revolution Sound card.  No video capture in this system.  The other Ubuntu Studio computer will be dedicated for that.  This is my audio system only.

I disabled the on board VIA sound card and loaded the Studio.  I works well. I wanted to set up the system to record from the Delta and playback through the VIA but  I just do not like the quality of sound from the on-board sound card.  I compromised by using my external Mackie mixer and a compressor limiter to regulate the incoming sound and can monitor the incoming sound externally.  

I played with Ardour, Rosegarden, and Audacity and they all work great. You have to start Jack for Ardour,Hydrogen,  and Rosegarden but it works good except that I crashed Ardour playing with the zoom features and attribute this to limitations of the on-board Bio-Star video and/or drivers.  I will work on that later. BTW, Hydrogen also works great. I test MIDI today.  I will use a USB M-audio Midi interface with this system.

I advise that you get familiar with ALSAMixer and set your outputs correctly or you will not hear your recordings in Stereo.  Let me tell you that this M-Audio card sounds fantastic and ALSA drivers know how to talk to it! KUDOS to the team that did the drivers! I dumped my Sound Blasters for this Delta. I can see all seven +1 (sub) outputs channels! This opens up some great possibilities for my audio recordings. 

This week I will produce a couple of podcasts in Audacity and try to use Ardour to help produce some tracks.  :guitar:

It all seems to work. I intend to test the Video recording next week.

My windoze XP system crashed last week again due to a spy ware attempt.  It was my fault for trying to download some free .wav files that carried nasty spy ware.  I have all the virus scanning that I can tolerate and I still get hit with nastiness! :(I intend to rebuild that system with the new version of MEPIS.  Windows is about to be booted permanently FOLKS!
