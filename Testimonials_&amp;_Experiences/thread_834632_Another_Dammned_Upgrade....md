### Post by BigSilly on 2008-06-23
Updates are a good thing from my point of view too, simply because of the constantly evolving Linux landscape. Luckily, Ubuntu is pretty much at the forefront of that, so you get first dibs! I think I'm right in saying that Ubuntu was the first distro to get Firefox 3 at launch. 

It's also in the name of security too. I look at it this way, if you do a fresh Windows install, when you first stick on your anti-virus, then your spyware and adware programs, you have to wait while they bring themselves right up to date with the latest definitions etc. Depending on when you do your fresh install, there can be thousands of files to download. It's the same for Ubuntu in many ways. There are many eyes in the Linux world seeking to ensure the system remains strong from a security standpoint.

I look at it as a healthy sign, anyway. The distro is busy, alive and constantly new. Long may it continue.
