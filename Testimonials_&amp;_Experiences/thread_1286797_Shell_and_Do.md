### Post by ibuclaw on 2009-10-09
[COLOR="Sienna"][SIZE="4"]New Autumn Look[/SIZE][/COLOR]
So I've taken the upgrade route straight to Karmic after something went horribly wrong in the pinned upgrades I've been doing (am getting lazy at managing package dependencies ;))

And, whilst I'm at it, might as well do my bi-yearly overhaul of the desktop.
Except, this time I thought I might try switching to Gnome-Shell, rather than just a new theme, icons and background.

[COLOR="Sienna"][SIZE="4"]Gnome-Shell[/SIZE][/COLOR]
Two hours later after a 'do-release-upgrade -d' and tweaking I finally got it all setup, and my opinions of Gnome-Shell are - it is quite good, not a standing ovation, but like KDE4, the beginnings of something big.

The biggest put off is that to start an application feels like more effort, than in compiz/metacity. It is very mouse oriented, and I'm not the biggest fan of the "point-n-click" revolution of desktops. To be frank, half the times it feels like I'm starting to get RSI from using mouse devices.

[COLOR="Sienna"][SIZE="4"]Gnome-Do[/SIZE][/COLOR]
I've heard of Gnome-Do before, and my understanding was that it was a bit like [Ubiquity]("http://labs.mozilla.com/ubiquity/") but for the desktop. But I thought that it was worth a try.

Five minutes later, and I am an instant convert! It was exactly what I am looking for and has already sped up my productivity in the desktop.

So, I'm just putting a small word out, for those who want to try out Gnome-Shell when karmic comes out, install Gnome-Do. It is the perfect companion.
