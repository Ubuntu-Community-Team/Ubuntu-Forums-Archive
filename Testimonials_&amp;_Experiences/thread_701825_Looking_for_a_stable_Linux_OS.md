### Post by Mustard on 2008-02-23
Just a thought I have regarding the OPs question.  If a stable version of Ubuntu was the ultimate goal, wouldn't it be best to use something like the 6.06 LTS version of Ubuntu?  From my perspective the non long term service versions of Ubuntu are always attempting to incorporate the 'latest features'.  Inherent in incorporating these features is the possibility that they will have some stability issues.  There is a tradeoff between stability and bleeding edge software.  You can't necessarily have both.  Each user needs to decide for themselves which they prefer.

I had been running 6.06 LTS for quite a long time, because all I wanted was a system that booted up and did the job, not a system that has lots of bells and whistles.  Lately I have reacquired the taste for bells and whistles, so I have upgraded to 7.10.

The same situation in Debian.  You can use stable, testing or unstable, depending on where your  preferences lie with regards to stability or bleeding edge.
