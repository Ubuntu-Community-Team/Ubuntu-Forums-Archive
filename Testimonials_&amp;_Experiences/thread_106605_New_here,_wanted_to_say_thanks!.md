### Post by ipaidforthisname on 2005-12-21
Hello everyone,

I've been lurking for a little while now, and I've also been constantly searching the forum to find solutions to any problems I have had, which has been amazingly successful. 

Computers have always been a big part of my life. Many years ago, I dipped my toes into Linux, but I considered it a dismal failure as far as desktop operating systems go. The hardware support was basically nill, and I just felt like I could not get over the learning curve. I continued to use Windows for many, many years after those experiences.

Within the last year, I decided to play with FreeBSD on my main file server, and was floored by its performance and ease of use. It outperformed Windows so much, on every level, that it really piqued my interest in other *Nix's again. I beat that BSD server to a pulp; it only has 256 megs of ram, but it transfers 30+GB a day, and is always running ctorrent and GTK-Gnutella, and it runs like a fresh install of Windows while doing it. 

I finally decided to bite the bullet and give Linux a try once again, but this time on my main computer: My laptop (A Dell Inspiron XPS). I knew Laptops were the hardest when it came to hardware support, so I went into this with absolute trepidation and doubt. I decided on Kubuntu because apt-get seemed like a must have after enjoying BSD's pkg_add. I ran into serious problems post install; Xorg kept locking up after about 5 minutes of use. Disabling renderaccel in Xorg fixed it, but it ran awfully. I was about to give up totally, when some serious searching on the forums turned up a solution. Now, I am running Kubuntu on my XPS with full 3d support on an ATI MR9800. Everything works flawlessly, and that learning curve that seemed to exist before is completely gone. I don't know how, or even when, it happened, but Linux is an amazing, easy to use desktop OS. I haven't looked back since ditching Windows. 

I just wanted to say hi and thank the entire Ubuntu community. This forum is an amazing resource. I look forward to learning more.

Cheers,
            Julian
