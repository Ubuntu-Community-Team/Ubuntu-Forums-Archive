### Post by jermza on 2011-11-30
There is a lot of hatred fired at Unity and my guess is that it's mostly techies. I've become a fan and here's why.

I started using Linux about a year ago. As a total newbie, I found Ubuntu quite inviting, and I liked the Apple-esque interface of Gnome.

I then installed 11.04 and wasn't sure I liked it, so I moved to Mint 11, which I stayed with because it was fast and everything worked. Then Mint 12 came and I didn't like the way it's trying to keep Gnome 2 users happy as well as Gnome 3 users; it now feels like it's lost a bit of direction, with two panels and bits of pieces of addons to cater for those who are resisting change.

I tried Fedora 16 for a day and, while it's very sexy, it's just too much hassle. Nothing, just about, works because of their strict free-only policies. I couldn't even get my fonts working properly.

I then installed 11.10 and have to say that it's a lot better than 11.04. It feels snappier (other than Banshee, which hangs every time it starts) and solid. I've added launchers to the launcher and I use workspaces and my workflow is faster than ever. My Wacom tablet even has its own options in the System Settings, which Mint (or others) don't have by default.

I even like the left-hand side launcher, and the Ambience theme has grown on me (although I'd like to see more integrated themes).

For all the hate fired at Ubuntu, at least it has a clear direction in which it's going. Sticking to its guns must be given some credit, whether you like it or not.
