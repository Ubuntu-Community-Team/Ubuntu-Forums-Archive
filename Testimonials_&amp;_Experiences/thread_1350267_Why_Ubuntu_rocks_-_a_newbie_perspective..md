### Post by MontyFromSingleMinds on 2009-12-09
For all you "power users" out there, here's a relative newcomers story that may make you smile - or wince, or maybe both.

Over a year ago, I switched to Ubuntu after an unrecoverable Vista kernel crash after an AUTHORIZED update. Needless to say, that was the last straw. 

So for a little over a year now, I've been running Ubuntu, and it's been a great experience. Now and again perhaps a sound problem, but overall I can still use my computer - and that's what's important to me. 

When some error occurs, not often, I boot into the grub menu and it's fixed in a flash. 

So this weekend, I'm a little rushed, and while removing a portable hard drive (and getting the application using it prompt) I ....turned it off to remove it. 

I went for supper, started up my computer, and .....got a system error. 

No problemo.....grub......recovery mode....and ....WHAT ?

Multiple claimed blocks in inode..... WHAT ? Error probing smb2 ? 

It's NOT fixing it ?  The unthinkable.....

So now, shaken, I try going to a command line level and seeing if I can resolve it THERE. 

(flash forward about a day and a half, frustration rising, my faith in Ubuntu shaken to the core over it's inability to protect my computer against myself....) 

And then...sitting there... it hit me.

Maybe, just maybe, if I drop to ROOT and run a FSCK.EXT4DEV there ? 

Hey....it finds the same inode problems, but THIS time it asks me if I want to REPAIR them one by one ?

All it takes is pressing "y" perhaps ten times ?    

And then..... SYSTEM FIXED ?  

REBOOT ? 

And it WORKS ? 

You know that old story about how many monkeys it would take at typewriters to produce Hamlet ? 

Right now, I'm starting to think only one, if those keyboards are connected to a computer running Ubuntu. :D

(Again, I apologize to those "power users" out there, who are sitting there going "Ummm....D'uh" right about now after reading this....)

Anyone else have any stories about how Ubuntu empowered them by giving you the tools to fix a problem that certain other OS's (no names) never offered you ? 


Time to share those small "beginners victories" with the rest of us.

### Post by b1lancer on 2009-12-09
I have a Dell Inspiron 6000 laptop that's pretty old by many standards. I was running Xp on it just fine but gradually, I've seen it's speed slowly fade, despite no spyware or other malware.

A friend at the time told me about Ubuntu, saying it should be easier an older systems and I would do well to install it and see if performance peaks a bit. After an intense q/a where I kept looking for reassurance in that it's easy to use and all my files would work, I decided to give it a go. This was a year and a half ago. Needless to say, I'm a convert.

Ubuntu has saved me money by not having to take it to a repair shop or get a new latop. ALl my files work just fine (if not, there's a way to...*make* it work) and the ease of use was just astounding. The fact that it's free helped too...just a bit....

Here's another point-- back with the old os, updates would scare me. Now, updates tell me that I'm getting things fixed, not making things working fine break.
