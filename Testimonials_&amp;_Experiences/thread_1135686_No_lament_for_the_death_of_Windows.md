### Post by stwschool on 2009-04-25
Actually a sensible move to take on a really well-proven and established version of linux for your first (it was my first ubuntu too after I tried a variety of different setups on virtualbox). That said, you're missing out on a few nice new features, especially in later versions of gnome, such as the nm-applet (really sexy network manager introduced in 8.10 which makes mobile internet a breeze) and tabbed browsing in nautilus (the file manager). I'd at least upgrade to 8.10 as that's rock-solid, and 9.04 is looking pretty tidy now (though intel graphics are a bit of an issue presently).
