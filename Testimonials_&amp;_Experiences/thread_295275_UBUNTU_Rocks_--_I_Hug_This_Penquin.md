### Post by node42 on 2006-11-10
I've been all over the board with OS's. I started using Linux in 94 I believe (jeeze I'm old...) but have switched between OpenBSD, various Winders, and most recently OS X.

I recently had a hankering to see how Gnome and Linux had progressed since staying pretty loyal to the Mac camp for the past four years. As a test I tossed Edgy on a friend's desktop and didn't run across *any* problems! I was so impressed I sold my MacBook Pro and picked up a Dell XPS M1710. Compiz absolutely flies! I haven't gotten too involved with the Ubuntu/Debian packaging scheme yet - so I don't know how I like it compared to RPM (which I have worked with almost exclusively under Linux). But comparing Fedora Core 6 to Edgy - man - Ubuntu has just nailed it!

I was pretty impressed with Vista RC2, but after Compiz it suddenly looks pretty dated. At any rate I am amazed at what the Ubuntu developers/packagers/testers/etc have managed to put together and I am hooked! The opensource community is really making inroads to the desktop.
