### Post by Rumor on 2005-11-29
I've been running Ubuntu-Breezy for a couple weeks now. I've been windows "free" for a week. :) 

Windows was a continuous source of aggravation and headache. And to think I paid so much money for the "privilege" of using M$ software . . . 

I don't recall what I paid for a DOS upgrade or two way back when.
Windows 3.11 came with a computer I bought, so I can't really guess at its cost.
Same with Windows 95 (shudder)
Windows 98 ran somewhere around $100 if I recall.
The upgrade to Win98 SE was $100.
I managed to skip Windows ME.
I bought Windows XP Pro on sale for $100. I know I got off cheap.
Then there were the various incarnations of MS Office which you simply HAD to buy since the older versions could not open the saved files of the newer versions in their native format.
So let's ***very*** conservatively say $200 for the various MS Office products.
Thus we have  avery conservative estimate of $500 for various MicroSoft operating systems / Office Suites and other sundry software over the course of 10 - 15 years. 
And last year I was on a freeware kick, having decided that my spending on software was getting out of hand. But I was still connected by the Windows umbilicus to MicroSoft.

Then someone tells me about Ubuntu. I don't wait for my disc to come in the mail. I go to the website and download an ISO which I burn (using Nero which cost another $75 or so). I have a HD in my computer that isn't really doing anything important so very shortly after burning the disc, I have a dual booting system. Total cost to me at this point is 25 cents or so for the CD I used.

Once Ubuntu boots, I find there is a nice office suite, one that I've been using for a while. Oh, there's a nice CD burning program already installed and set to go. Oh, and look at this, I don't really need WinDVD (what's it go for these days? $60?) since xine works so nicely. Then there's the FTP server I use to send files home from work. That wasn't too hard to set up . . . took a bit more work than Bulletproof FTP Server (which only cost $20 if I recall correctly). There's even a nice newsreader, Pan to replace Agent - the very first piece of shareware I ever registered, back in the .99d?e? days. 

In the end, I have a PC that does what my windows set-up did and so far I am only out 25 cents for that CD because I was not patient enough to wait for my discs to come in the mail.

_Ubuntu (just works) $0_ versus _Windows (Just let me reboot) $500_ or so. Even ***I*** can do that math.
