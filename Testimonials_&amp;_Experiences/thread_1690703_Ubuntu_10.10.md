### Post by Maddjokerz on 2011-02-18
Over the last 2 years of switching over to linux, I have been kicking myself as to why I hadn't switched earlier. I have read on a lot of different forums and helpful postings on how hard of a switch it is to move to Ubuntu or Linux, I found the move very easy and the learning curve wasn't as hard as most people put it out to be. With the biggest concern being the CLI (which the way many places made it sound was the only way to operate Linux) I have become more familair with termianl and it has become less scary and more fun. The fact that ubuntu is so customizable has to be the greatest selling point for me, not only can I download unique themes I can customize and create something that is uniquely mine, and didn't have to pay anyone extra to make it. One big thing that has been great is the feeling of community I see in ubuntu everyone is so willing to help with the littlest problems no one ever seems to turn a blind eye to the us N00bs and for that I am greatful for everyone who has answerd a post that I have placed on here.:guitar:
