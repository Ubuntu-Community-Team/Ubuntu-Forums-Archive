### Post by LucyDog on 2010-12-09
Hi Folks

Two or three years ago, I heard one of Fiat's top executives being interviewed on BBC Radio 4. He  was asked why Fiat produced such unreliable vehicles. The gent replied  that the cars were beautiful to look at. The interviewer repeated the  question several times, and each time the fiat executive would give the same answer,  "but the car is such a beautiful car to look at." The chap just  didn't seem to understand that a driver wants to get from A to B safety  and without a break-down.

I've been a Windows user since 1987 (since version 1.something I think).

it's been a couple of weeks since I loaded Ubuntu on my desktop pc, and this is what I've noticed:

1. Ubuntu is fast to start up, very fast.
2. The Dell pc it's installed on no longer hangs or crashes
3. Evolution email is fast, very fast
4. Evolution is stable
5. Open Office is fast
6. Open Office is stable and so far has done everything I've wanted it to do
7. The sound system works a treat
8. My headset works fine
9. My Xerox Phaser printer works perfectly
10. There's no problem with my external hard drive, used for backups
11. Skype works fine
12. Networking is a dream - no configuration was required
13. i feel like a weight has been lifted from my shoulders - what a sense of relief

I own a successful business, a small one. We engage the top people to perform the vital tasks that need to be done. We use the services of the best shipping company in the UK - they haven't made a single mistake in 8 years. we use one of the top marketing and graphics guys in the UK. My business partner is probably the top sales guy in our market. We are the number one business in our industry.

By moving to Ubuntu, I feel like we've eliminated a weak link and that we've added another strong partner to the team. Technical support is first class.

Someone once said, "The task of education is to make a person feel so uncomfortable that he or she actually considers change".

I guess i finally got to the point of being very unhappy with the pain caused by Microsoft and the damage it was doing to my productivity. I'm glad that I've now moved my business critical application requirements onto the Ubuntu distribution of Linux.

I'll keep Microsoft for a while for some of the less important stuff like the Babas Chess interface for playing on [www.freechess.org]("http://www.freechess.org") or for Adobe Acrobat (until I find an alternative for that one). But I certainly don't miss the thorn in my flesh. It's gone now, and I'm starting to heal.

And soon, I'll take the unfit-for-purpose Fiat of operating systems to the scrap yard.

Kindest regards

LucyDog
