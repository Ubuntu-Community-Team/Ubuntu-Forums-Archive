### Post by smilingralph on 2009-01-09
This is just a quick thank you note to all in the Ubuntu community. I'll try to make this short as  I have the attention span of a two year old. 

I was prompted to try Linux after reading an article which asked a simple question: "How many applications are you paying for whose main purpose is to compensate for the inadequacies of your OS?" In my case in my case the answer was close to 50% of the applications: anti-malware, defrag, registry cleaners, etc.

So I went the usual route WUBI-dual boot-exclusive Linux. Finally settling on Xubuntu as my preferred OS.

In this conversion process what has truly amazed me is the quality of the support community. Whenever I've had a problem a quick search has led to a solution a workaround or at the very least an acknowledgement of the bug. This is marked contrast to my previous OS where solutions were rare, workarounds rarer still, and acknowledgement of bugs practically nonexistent.

Specifically, but not exclusively, I would like to thank the following:

All those responsible for the Community Documentation: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community](https://help.ubuntu.com/community)
