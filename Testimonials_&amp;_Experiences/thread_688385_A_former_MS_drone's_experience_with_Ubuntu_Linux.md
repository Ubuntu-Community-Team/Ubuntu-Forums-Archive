### Post by wheredidrealitygo on 2008-02-05
What can I say?

I'm in love.

I've been with Ubuntu now for a while. Several years ago a friend of mine gave me a disc with Dapper on it, but I couldn't install it at the time for reasons that I was travelling and needed a working laptop for several months. I got home, forgot about it for a while, then found it and decided to give it a go. Booted into the livecd, everything was interesting, I couldn't see myself getting into it though. 

So I started working for MS at a Call Centre, and I kept hearing all these people reactivating because they “Dual-booted XP with Linux” or because they “installed Linux but put XP into a VM”. More often than not it was Ubuntu when I started asking, so I got interested, and asked around the office if anyone had tried it.

It turns out a good friend on my team was using Feisty as his only OS, he offered to burn me a livecd and let me try it. It worked a lot better on my only computer at the time ([toshiba satellite a100]("http://209.167.114.38/support/TechSupport/productcontents/satellite/PSAA8C-0FH00E.htm")) than Dapper had

I dual-booted that laptop for a while, and found myself using Feisty more and more than XP.

I went through the whole “I'm sick of Linux” and “I'm sick of Windows” phase, reinstalled/dual-booted both often because of one thing or another that would work well or better on the other, and have finally settled with my current config (in my sig) both only booting into Gutsy for more than two months now, and it looks to stay that way. Both work very well without any major tweaking (except the wireless on the desktop, rt73, which will be fixed in Hardy).

I can see Ubuntu being one of the, if not very best, OS's out there with all of the amazing support behind it, and this community contributes an unbelievable amount to that, and I'm glad to be a part of it.

I'm hooked!

### Post by ph1 on 2008-02-07
Likewise here!

I've basically used MS all my life--I can remember way back to Windows 95, which was the operating system on the first computer I'd ever used.  I've used every single Windows OS since then, up to and including Vista.  I'd been quite complacent--Windows worked; I could find plenty of stuff to make my computer "fun" and functional; I could get rid of some of the useless stuff that I would never use (but not all--that always bothered me).  But once I learned that there were other operating systems besides Windows, my dream for a long time was "to learn enough about computers to use Linux."  

Finally, I was out of town for two weeks--and my Vista installation pulled a stunt on me and stopped working.  No network connections, no internet, no "WMI,": no Half-Life 2.....after basically an entire weekend talking to tech support (the guy was terrific, not his fault), the only solution was to reinstall Vista--and my DVD was sitting 1000 miles away.  

Refusing to have a brick for two weeks, I used the occasion as an excuse to dual boot Gutsy--and I've never looked back.  It was by far the best thing I've done to this computer yet.  I love it because Gutsy is conservative with system resources; it's relatively great about "working out of the box;" it's easily hackable/customizable; it's fun and functional; any software I could ever need is basically a few clicks away; and the community is full of people who want to learn and to help people like me learn.  (WIndows seemed to be more and more targeted toward forcing the average user to sit there and let Windows work/not work without really having any power to do anything, or to even know what was going on, especially in Vista....)  Plus I don't know how I lived without the terminal or multiple desktops....

I guess all I'm trying to say is that I was afraid of Linux--and a little envious of all those dashing terminal hackers, I suppose--for a long time, and now it feels like realizing a dream to actually run it--and to be able to get my hands dirty tweaking.  Linux is, in my opinion, already more or less the best operating system--and only getting better!
