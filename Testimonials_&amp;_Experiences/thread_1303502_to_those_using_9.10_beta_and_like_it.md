### Post by longtom on 2009-10-28
I don't think anybody here can advise you on which distro or OS will be the very best for your personal circumstances.

I am, however, glad to see that you obviously did some research and tried different distros.
I always have a distro (the ones I am interested in) running in VirtualBox and check them out that way.  By doing that I get a pretty good idea what I like about it and what not.  Only than I install it on my hdd in a separate partition and use it, if it works with all my hardware the way  want it too.

That is the wonderful thing about Linux.  So many choices...

So, if yum is better than apt (or Fedora better than Ubuntu) or not is not so much a question of absolutes but more a question of opinion.  I personally tried both and after weighing up the pro and cons I made the decision that Ubuntu has the most to offer for me and for what I want to use it for.

### Post by ctc26 on 2009-10-28
I completely agree with the above post.

I couple of things to keep in mind:

- Ubuntu has the largest user base of any other linux distro. Because of this, development of the distro itself and subsidary projects occurs at a much greater pace. Applications are designed with Ubuntu in mind, as it is the most substantial linux market.
- Ubuntu has shown steady progress over all releases, demonstrating a clear direction in development.
- Ubuntu has dedicated corporate backing that is begining to forge relationships accross the marketplace. Money talks, and whilst it is a free OS it still has to compete in a proprietary world.

I'm not saying that the other linux distros do not compete in these areas, but whenever I weigh up the pros and cons of changing from Ubuntu, I ask myself who has the brighter future.
