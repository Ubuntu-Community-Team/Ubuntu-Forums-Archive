### Post by bigseb on 2010-05-26
In February I tried Ubuntu for the first time ever. Had a couple of disks lying about - Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Fedora - and decided to pop one in. Turned out to be Karmic Ultimate Edition. 

I was hooked!

Now, I am no fan of Microsoft (due to personal reasons) and to see what a Linux distro had to offer just BLEW MY MIND!! "Never ever going back!" I said.

Alas there is one problem. It's a biggie too :( 

I use CADCAM software for my job and all software that I could find that runs on Linux just doesn't do the job. This isn't just about freeware either, I'll gladly pay if it does the job. So far I've tried: VariCAD, BRLCAD, Medusa, FreeCAD (both of them), QCAD, SAGCAD and Synergy Solids. These are all supposed to do what I want but unfortunately do not. VariCAD came very close but lacks the detailing capabilities I need as a toolmaker. To give you some idea of what I need just think Solidworks and Rhinoceros.

So, I have had no choice but to dualboot with Windows. I decided to purchase Alibre but once VariCAD fine tune their software's capabilities I would like to switch. 

And to all software developers that might be reading this: PLEASE CONSIDER US LINUX USERS!!
