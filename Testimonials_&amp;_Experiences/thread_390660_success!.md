### Post by MrWashy on 2007-03-22
I have to share a small success here.  While Ubuntu is new to me, OpenOffice is old news.  OOo is a fantastic (albeit somewhat compatibility hindered... thanks to M$ not OOo) application.  

Well, after updating to OOo2.1 (by following some excellent direction posted on the forum) my next concern was getting all of my settings from XP into Ubuntu.  Luckily the settings can just simply be copied from one to the other.  

Now I could have just done a click and drag but wanted to use the command line.  And guess what?  It went incredibly well!  Now that may be simple to those whoa re old hands at Ubuntu and linux, but as a newb it is actually kind of intimidating - a slight fear of losing/screwing up something.  

Well, after getting the original files backed up the copy was fantastically easy!  (Go figure, all I did was copy...) it was also extremely satisfying, kind of like a getting a small taste of a really good really huge buffet.  Maybe one day I'll be up to sitting at the kids table!

Hang in there fellow newbs...  small successes are out there and no doubt they'll lead to bigger ones!
