### Post by Dragonbite on 2006-10-23
I am happily surprised at how well Kubuntu worked on my old Sony Vaio desktop system.

I've used Ubuntu before, on our Dell Dimension 4100 desktop and was pretty pleased about it but it never ran on my Sony system before (something about the video drivers and not being able to start X).

I had instead installed CentOS (built from RHEL 4) and it worked fine thought not too swiftly and while KDE is available it runs even slower and CentOS is built for a fairly no-nonsense Enterprise Workstation/Server.

A few nights ago I tried the Kubuntu live CD on it just to see if it would work.  

I was surprised first that it booted up into KDE after not getting Ubuntu and other distros to run live very well.

I was surprised second by how well it worked;[LIST]
[*]Detected my hardware (haven't checked the winmodem yet but fully understand if it does not).. I have full video and sound right out of the box!
[*]Responsiveness is much better than even KDE installed on that system with Gentoo
[*]Not a lot of hang time (searching the CD) despite not having a ton or ram (256MB)
[*]More pleasant look than basic KDE 
[*]Not bloated with dozens on dozens of programs with little information as to what it does
[/LIST] I know that some of the credit should also go to the KDE crew for their improvements on KDE from the 3.2 and 3.3 that I've used before!

Kudos for the Kubuntu (and KDE) crew! You've impressed me!
