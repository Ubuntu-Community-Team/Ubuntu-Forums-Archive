I am sadly disappointed because this reminds me of MS Windows 98 and ME.

### Post by WinRiddance on 2010-05-28
Seven upgrades, 2 clean installs with newly formatted ext4 partitions on 4 PCs and 3 laptops with ZERO problems that would prevent me from using Lucid.

Can't tell this for certain but I know that Ubuntu officially recommends and supports the use of the ext4 file system for every Ubuntu version beginning with Karmic. Perhaps that's where some of the problems are evident since I've come upon a few threads now by Ubuntu 8.04 users who've upgraded to 10.04 with their old ext3 file system ???

Other things to consider are, well umh pardon me, but umh ... laziness. I'm also coming across a number of threads where people are clearly either too lazy to read and work at a solution, or annoyed that a solution needs to be worked at in the first place (can you believe this is coming from a "die hard Windows user" and not a Linux junkie?)

Last but not least, the age of the machine is something to consider as well. Just in the past few days I must have read over 100 posts by people with machines, laptops and PCs alike, which are 4+ years old. Well, the older the machine, the more likely you are to run into problems. Geeez, these days you can get very nice used systems dirt cheap on eBay, and I do mean dirt cheap.

Ooops, one more ... if you make proper backups of everything that's important before doing an upgrade then a downgrade to a previous version of Ubuntu would be a no-brainer, something that can be done in apx. half an hour of time. Considering the agonizing situations that I've had to deal with on windows machines for so many years, there's nothing at this point that Ubuntu "could do to me" which might have me complaining about it. When you live with Windows for 18+ years, to include having built 100s of custom systems and discovering the occasional "overnight problem" that simply couldn't be fixed without reformatting a disk ....

**.... need I say more?**
Just grit your teeth, read the posts, hunt down your solutions, and be happy as a clam when you do. If there's one thing that I've learned to love about Ubuntu and various Ubuntu forums, then that's the fact that there's always a solution out there, **always**, as long as you're willing to look for it. Good luck everyone!
