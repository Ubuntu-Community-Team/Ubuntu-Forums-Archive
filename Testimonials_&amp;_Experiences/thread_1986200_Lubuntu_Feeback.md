### Post by daisyflower on 2012-05-24
Overall assessment: _very poor _

Lubuntu simply doesn't offer enough to make it a sustainable operating system.  I am not just talking about programs, but basic necessities are not met.

**PROBLEMS 1&2**

On a netbook, window sizes are too big.  The submit buttons hang below the bottom of the screen.  You have to move the panel to the left or right, and then you can click an edge of the submit buttons.  There is nothing in the center of the window, so obviously it is too long vertically to be suitable on a netbook.

Also, you cannot use the Fn key to change the brightness.  I am using Xubuntu and there are no problems with these two issues.  The windows fit nicely on the screen and I can click buttons without playing games with the panel.  The brightness can be adjusted so you don't use up the battery. 
[B]
PROBLEM 3[/B]

Flashing is another issue.  I don't know why, but Lubuntu will flash often.  It always flashes with the Lubuntu logo at the desktop when it initially loads.  Xubuntu hasn't done this at all.

**PROBLEM 4**

After installing Lubuntu, it won't load up again unless the USB stick is connected.  I must have installed Lubuntu a dozen times now.  Xubuntu installed immediately without the need for a USB stick to be connected when it booted after installation.  The installation steps were EXACTLY the same.  I didn't do anything different to tell Xubuntu not to install to USB.

**PROBLEM 5**

I have an issue with my netbook not rebooting properly.  It will just turn black and I have to shut it down or press the power button.  In trying to resolve this issue I had to use "GEDIT" commands for the grub.  In Lubuntu there was no response from the computer.  I installed it several times.  I updated it each time.  I removed it from Windows several times.  I then reinstalled it.  There was no sign of a GEDIT in sight.

In Xubuntu, I got a message it was not installed.  At least I know it is missing.  Now I am trying to get it installed.  Perhaps it could be installed on Lubuntu too, but with the issues mentioned earlier  I am not going to re-install to find out.  I'll give Lubuntu another shot when 12.10 comes.

**PROBLEM 6**

After installing Lubuntu many times to troubleshoot, it stopped loading properly.  I got a mesage about the filesystem not being correct.  With Xubuntu, no such error message appeared.

This thread is essentially resolved as it pertains to Lubuntu.  I will make an Xubuntu thread if I find a solution to the rebooting issue.  If you have an Acer Aspire One 257, don't bother using Lubuntu on it now unless you want a week of grief and headaches.

3) Find GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX="" and change it to GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX=&#8221;reboot=efi&#8221;

4) Save changes and close gedit

5) Update the grub with the following command:

6) Power off your machine and then switch it on again. Once it is booted, try to restart it to see whether it is working. If this is not working, try following commands instead of efi (i.e.: reboot=efi).
