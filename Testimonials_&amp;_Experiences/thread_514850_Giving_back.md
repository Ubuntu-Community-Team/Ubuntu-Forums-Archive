### Post by Shib on 2007-08-01
Hey all,

I've been using Linux since sometime in 2000-2001... during this time I've kind of been going back and forth, depending on my needs at the time, between OS X, *doze and various *nix's.

In this time, it has come with experience that Debian wins out on pretty much every single application that you'd want to do with Linux. This is pretty much due to apt, but also the logical structure and layout of the distro.

More recently, I've become certified with Comptia twice (A+ and Linux+) and Cisco (CCNA) and have been using Debian systems all all my servers and firewalls (two web servers, two DNS servers, file/backup server, snort IDS/IPS boxes) and I thought that it was time to use my knowledge and experience and give back to those who have helped me get where I am with my career. I've been referred to this site on more than one occasion from Googleing various issues that I've ran across, so today, when I was digging into how to give back to Debian... it sort of made sense to come here, again.

My days with Ubuntu, while I'll admit, are not that extensive, started with it's initial release announcement on slashdot. I ordered a few CDs to be sent my way for myself and people I know... saw the ugly brown gnome look, and that's pretty much as far as it went. Now that Kubuntu is out though, it was a little more enticing to go with the prepackaged route instead of Debian with no packages, then build the rest from there (kde, etc...). So, that's what I'm running on my desktop nowadays.

My laptops and servers are still all on Debian... but I figure that helping out here where I can, introducing people to the logic and ease of a Debian based system, would be the most valuable and time effective way of giving back, as changes in Ubuntu seem to be making their way into Debian.

In closing, I'd just like to take a little bit of time to say thanks to everyone out there who has been contributing, and I hope that my stay here will be appreciated as well.

All the best,

Shib.
