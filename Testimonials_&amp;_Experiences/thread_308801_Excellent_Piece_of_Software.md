### Post by jdq997 on 2006-11-28
I've been using Ubuntu for 6 months now and it is the OS I boot 95% of the time.  I don't use Linux for any ideological reasons -- in fact I am absolutely opposed to most of the various "free software" and "software freedom" philosophies.  I think that my reasons for using Ubuntu can be broken down into two categories:  

1.  I use a small number of applications a lot and I want my OS to be stable.  Linux provides both of these criteria very well and Ubuntu makes Linux easy to use.  I don't use any specialty software and so the various free alternatives do a great job of accomplishing everything I need.  It will be nice in the future not to have to purchase (or pirate) versions of Microsoft Office, because Open Office is just fine for my needs.  There are also a lot of nice little apps that do common tasks that are very well written and a pleasure to use.  Amarok, Gaim and F-Spot come to mind as standouts.  

The OS is fast on my hardware and it doesn't get bogged down after months of consistent use like Windows does.  For some reason I am not very good at keeping my Windows system free of various kinds of viruses and spyware and with Ubuntu I can avoid this problem altogether.

2.  I like the geek factor.   I like the fact that a lot of Linux is the work of the common geek fiddling around on his computer.  I like how Ubuntu is customizable down to the very fine details.  I like switching between Gnome and KDE whenever I am in the mood to do so.  I don't have any problems with corporate, proprietary software, but with Linux I can take more commmand of the user experience if I want to and I can fiddle around with more stuff.

So in any case, to end my rant I want to thank everyone (both paid and unpaid) who has worked to create Linux and Ubuntu and I want to thank Mr. Shuttlesworth for putting his time, money and energy into making this excellent piece of software work so well.  I look forward to future versions.  Thank you very much for the free stuff guys.

 - Jason Quintana
