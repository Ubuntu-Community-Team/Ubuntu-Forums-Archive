### Post by Scarlett on 2008-07-20
I am continually impressed with how much better each release is to install.  I've sort of made a hobby out of breaking Ubuntu to the point of no return so I end up installing a lot.  The last distro I used was 7.10 and it was completely problem free for so long that I got bored and upgraded some hardware which caused it to have a graphical meltdown.  So I got the latest version of Ubuntu, and aside from some driver issues caused by bleeding edge hardware, this has been the most painless install yet.

To whomever made Flash easy to install, I wish you much material wealth and many karmic rewards.  Seriously, BIG THANK YOU!  That's always the part I dread most and this was quick and simple.  It was nice that I didn't have to comment out the CD-ROM lines to get updates to work.  VLC worked without the codec voodoo I've had to do in the past.  Azureus just worked.  It's never worked for me, much less "just worked".  The back and forward mouse buttons work.  They've never worked before.  My resolution was correctly detected and the refresh rate worked.  Overall, everything was very easy.

Except for the nvidia drivers everything has been perfect.  (Always gotta be something, right?)
