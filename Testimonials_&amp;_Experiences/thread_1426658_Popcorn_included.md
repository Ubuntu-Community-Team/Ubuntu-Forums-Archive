### Post by arophous on 2010-03-10
Hey everyone,

A few months months ago I installed 32bit 9.10 on my girlfriends laptop as she was constantly getting malware.  I have probably kept asking her every couple of weeks how she is getting on with it and she has not had to ask for any help with anything at all!!!

Fast forward last week.   I had the RC test for windows 7 and was using that as my main OS since it began (it was refreshing but not groundbreaking and certainly not worth £140!).  Due to running out a week or so ago I knew I could not go back to the slowness of Vista.  It was stable but didn't quite have that kick, maybe it needed espresso...

So I spend a few days researching finding out what kinds of things are now possible in Ubuntu...Skip to today and I am sitting with:

[LIST]
[*]A 64 bit ubuntu system utilizing all the parts I paid for;
[*]BBC Iplayer desktop installed along with Adobe Air;
[*]Skype fully working;
[*]Eve online(just got into it but too much work going on to play alot);
[*]Spotify;
[*]Chrome browser, with extensions;
[*]VLC player with enough codecs to shake a stick at and a very smooth playing full screen, just needs the :popcorn:;
[*]Customised desktop (avant bar, themed etc);

[/LIST]

And this took all of an hour to setup after install!

As I have been working from home today I found myself enjoying using Ubuntu for working :p, the multi desktop feature is something which I believe all OS users have a right to!

Ubuntu is great and can only get better.

Cheers for all fun,
Aro
