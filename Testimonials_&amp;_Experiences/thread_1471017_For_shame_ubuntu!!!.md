### Post by snowpine on 2010-05-03
> **cludwig said:**
> 10.04 is an LTS version of Ubuntu.  It should be more stable than other Ubuntu releases.  It should concentrate on bug fixes and pre-launch testing.  It not push the OS to the bleeding edge.


Actually, you have it backwards. :) The "S" in "LTS" stands for "support" not "stable."

Regular Ubuntu releases only have to be fresh and cool for 6 months until the next release, so they can afford to be a little conservative.

LTS releases have to stay fresh and cool for *2 years* until the next LTS, so Canonical has to look into the crystal ball and guess what people will want in 1 year, 11 months. If they play it safe and only include conservative, stable choices, the LTS releases would get really stale real quick. LTS releases *become *very stable towards the end of their 3 year cycle (as they've received many bug fix updates), but are no more stable than regular releases at the beginning.

Remember that Hardy still has 1 year of support left. A certain group of "I need stability" users will no doubt choose to wait until spring 2011 to upgrade 8.04->10.04.
