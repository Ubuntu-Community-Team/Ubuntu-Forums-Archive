### Post by christopher.wortman on 2011-10-30
I started Linux with KDE, always used it as my main desktop. I learned on KDE, and learned to love Linux with KDE. My hatred of GTK and it's bugs that even persist now make me wonder why people cling to it? If an application kills a dependency you take out the entire UI... E.G. the stable version of Wine has a tendency to mess up with some Visual Basic calls. This is a non-issue in KDE because it simply invokes KDE crash handler. In Gnome it wipes GTK out, even in GTK3.2. You are stuck with your entire screen frozen requiring you kill X. Ubuntu thought it cute, to kill CTRL+ALT+Backspace requiring a full reboot. Again I would ask why, but I just don't care at all. It is enabled in Kubuntu along with everything else I grew to love about Linux.

Canonical take a hint, STOP USING GTK!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

It is a veritable pile of rubbish. 30 year old bugs exist in the new GTK 3.2 code. 

<snip> It is so full of GTK related bugs along with a list of annoyances as long as my arm. 

Global Menu
The bar is anchored to the left
GUI non-uniformity
-Only some applications have a real scroll bar while others use the handle thing
-Only some applications support Global Menu
-No GUI based way of changing some options, I am forced into terminal to make changes
The side bar has a tendency to lose the icon to my applications requiring I remove it and place it back
Unity 2D is not the same as 3D, and is even buggier
Forget the name of your application? You are stuck searching almost 3 times as long as a small simple list from a menu.
It looks like a phone UI gone wrong.
It is clunky and a pain in my butt to use and I can't get it to work half the time, E.G. I have to right click an icon like trash more than once to get the menu to pop up, and Unity seems to be slowing down over time.

KDE has literally none of these issues... Even during 4.0~4.3 it was alright, just missing options and some of the features were unstable. Nothing a random old QT 3.5 app couldn't fix for the time being.

I am not saying Unity sucks, but it has a long way to go and I doubt very much it will be ready for a 5 year stable tag within 6 months...
