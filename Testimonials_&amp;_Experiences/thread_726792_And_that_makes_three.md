### Post by fetisha on 2008-03-17
So, last week I installed Ubuntu 7.10 on my best friend's PC and the only complaint he has is that Rhythmbox doesn't look [I]exactly[I] like iTunes, but he's happy with everything else. And it just so happens tonight a friend of mine came over with his laptop and I installed Ubuntu 7.10 on it. I don't know how to connect wirelessly to the internet (I have my computer directly connected to my router) so that's the only thing I couldn't show him how to do. Last month I helped my friend install Ubuntu 6.06 on his OLD eMac, but he's since opted for Yellow Dog. My boyfriend has also installed Xubuntu on his laptop.
Suffice to say I'm very proud of myself. In about a month I have converted three of my friends to Ubuntu, and all of them opted to completely remove their original OS from their computer. Two of them, the ones with Windows, had problems with viruses and their computer took AGES to start. Just goes to show, if people who have no idea what a Linux distro is or have never touched one before are willing to install one on a whim, there's just something wrong with Windows.
