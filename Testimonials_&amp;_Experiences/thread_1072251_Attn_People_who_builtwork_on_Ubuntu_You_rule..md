### Post by Motorhead Kaze on 2009-02-17
THANKS!

I suppose this is as good a place as any to write this. I have been using Ubuntu since Feisty. One day, when my computer was all messed up and I was tired of hating my computer, a really good friend said something about this Buntu thing he had been playing with. Since my computer was already in need of yet another fresh install of XP, I just said screw it and installed Feisty over the top of Winders and quit using it flat. There seemed not to be any reason not to just jump into Ubuntu (Feisty) with both feet, and I have been here nearly 2 years now.

There were some problems switching of course, and I swore at the computer a lot, but I had been doing that since Windows 95 anyway. The difference was that every complaint I had, I could actually DO something about, or it was fixed in a couple days with an update or in the next upgrade. Sweet. First was the automatic NTFS external drive recognition in Gutsy, then Hardy automatically recognized both my printer and my ATI vid card. Thanks!

Because of the forums, and the way Ubuntu is built I have actually become much more computer literate and feel that using Ubuntu is a good way to make oneself smarter. 

A year ago my wife and I became freelance translators, and we trust Hardy so much that we use it (and Open Office) on every job we do, as well as for our personal needs. My wife and I each use Hardy on our respective, dual-head machines. (And once I learned how to set those up, getting multiple monitors going is easy now too)

Basically, I just wanted to share my enthusiasm for this operating system you have provided. For years I shook my fists while I demanded that "computing should be free!" and was so frustrated that the only people who were qualified to be help me out when my computer had problems were guys who would always talk down to the poor slobs who didn't "get it". And then Ubuntu came around and make computing a positive experience. So, thank you for your effort, your time and for making computing a fun and educational.

Way to go.

Motorhead Kaze
Osaka, Japan
