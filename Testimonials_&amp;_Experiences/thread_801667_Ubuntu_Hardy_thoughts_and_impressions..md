### Post by exploder on 2008-05-20
I followed Hardy all through it's development and from time to time had serious doubts concerning the outcome.I reported bugs and submitted any requested information.There are people hard at work on the problems, the "Hardy Proposed" repository demonstrates the dedication of the Developer's.

I installed 64 bit Hardy on my new Compaq and am more than satisfied with it's performance. Sure, there are remaining bugs but thanks to the forum I know how things are being handled and how things are progressing.  

Here are some of the things I have thought about.

- Ubuntu provides "Daily Builds" during development. Who else does this? No other distribution provides this valuable resource.

- Hardy will have "Point Releases" every 6 months. How many distributions provide this needed service?

- ShipIt will mail you a very high quality copy of Ubuntu, free of charge. Ubuntu is the only distribution to have such a program in place.

- Launchpad for reporting bugs and tracking them. Also, access to mailing lists and generally all information regarding development. Everything is open to view and participate.

Ubuntu has a great deal to offer! The Ubuntu slogan "Humanity Twards Others" is actually more than just words, it is what the distribution stands for. 

Some other things to consider. Ubuntu provides the source code for other distributions built from it's releases! Ubuntu repositories are used by distributions that are built from Ubuntu. The Ubuntu forum provides a section to discuss other distributions! 

Who on earth could match all of this and I have not even mentioned the technology Ubuntu development has contributed. 

These are some things everyone should think about. Sure, we all get frustrated from time to time but Ubuntu really is putting a lot of time and effort into making you the best OS they possibly can. "Humanity Towards Others", is much more than a catchy slogan, it has real meaning.
