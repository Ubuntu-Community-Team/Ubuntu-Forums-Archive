### Post by Sean Moran on 2009-09-24
After 'umm'ing and 'ahh'ing  for the last few hours on whether to let this thread rest in peace or not, I figured it  the honest thing to do to finish off what I started on a positive and hopefully on-topic note re: testimonials.

Tonight at 11:30 gmt (19:30 local here) It willl be five weeks solid 16-7 since I first decided to take the standard 699Mb Ubuntu 9.04 Live CD and make it better.  (rather foolish in hindsight)

I have learned quite a lot over these past five weeks, about apt and synaptic, and burned quite a few CDs and DVDs in that learning process, and taking into account the sorts of applications and generic sorts of desktop configurations that the people I know around this neighbourhood might be likely to use as a standard sort of setup, I have come to the conclusion that I cannot make it even the slightest bit better than it is already as bog-stock standard.

I've taken out gnome-games and put them back, added apache and decided to remove it for the sake of authenticity, got Xubuntu going too.  Epiphany, SeaMonkey, Firefox, all the same if the end-user wants a choice for the sake of a few megabytes of a doanload.  Not much use for a web browser if you aren't on the Internet, is there?  Thus just add the packages to try out and pick the one you like the best.

Putting together a repository on a DVD along with the standard LIVE CD .iso willl help those like myself with restricted monthly ADSL quotas (I get 5Gb on peak and pay $100/Gb for excess so that's a concern).  I will be giving away a few DVDs to my friends now, so as to save the excess downloads and their time,  but there is a rather significant use-by date on anything that comes off a CD in comparison to what can be downloaded over the Internet anyway, and in the end, I have come to the conclusion that there is absolutely nothing I can do to make this any better than it already is.

I would like to end this rather disjointed personal account of a thread by testifying that the original Live CD seems about the best I could come up with after five solid weeks of messing around with near-perfection.

I feel rather silly now. :lolflag:
