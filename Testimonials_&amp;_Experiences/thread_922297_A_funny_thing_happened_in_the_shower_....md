### Post by WeddleWebguy on 2008-09-17
Now before you all get hot, bothered, and excited, I should point out that this is not that type of forum, and this is not that type of thread.

I would like to discuss Zen moments and when I came to the discovery that some South African Millionaire held more of a grip upon my future than some guy from Seattle that spent millions of dollars building a bunker he calls his rec-room and home.

I would like to speak about when I came to the realization that Linux, and Ubuntu in particular, is far superior to Microsoft Windows when it comes to my needs.

I had been holding out switching to Linux on all my computers for the simple fact it was little more than a curiosity of mine and not really something I had considered.

I hadn't yet come to the realization that I was powerless when it came to the hold Windows had on me.

I had used Linux for remote shared hosting before, but had never really gotten much experience beyond the apache realm of the Linux world.

Without a doubt, as an end user, I was a cybernetic Morg Drone, under the command of Gatious of Morg.

I had experimented with Ubuntu on a spare machine and came to the conclusion it was interesting but never really considered it as a primary operating system.

That was until LeadLight came out and Java mysteriously stopped wanting to be able to be installed on Winos Cheesta. (An operating system I shucked out $239 US for).

I noticed some other grim little tidbits and came to the conclusion that the Morg are in full invade mode and Gatious of Morg will destroy us all if we don't make our stand here.

The line must be drawn.

I was in the shower, and I asked myself, "I wonder if Ubuntu can play DVD's?"

It would be a very deciding factor in my eyes.

After a quick search of this forum, I had an ubuntu distribution fully set up and able to play my entire DVD collection.

My mind was made up, I would switch to Ubuntu and not look back.

Open Source is the only protection we have to the corporate giants who would quash innovation and liberty.

I say, Viva La Ubuntu, and I will never turn back.

~That Weddle Web Guy
