### Post by usagiakumu on 2011-01-16
I started with 6.04 and I jumped around a bit with Kubuntu, back then KDE 3.5.x was still lovable lol. I held onto KDE 3.5 as long as I could, then made the dive into Gnome which held me over until KDE 4.5 which was outstanding, and now with the advent of 4.6 RC2 I am once again acquainted with my love. I don't hate gnome, just prefer KDE so please no racial desktop slander wars. 

I run Kubuntu on my main desktop
I have acquired a popcorn hour for my TV
I run Android on my phone
I run Kubuntu with KDE netbook interface on my netbook
My router has Linux on it
My TV came pre-installed with Linux on it
My cable box runs a basic Linux kernel from Time Warner Cable

I will admit I have Windows 7 sitting over on my iMac 4,1, but I only turn it on to play with those legacy diehard apps that I just keep needing every so often.

It is really scary in a way to see Windows so phased out. Windows 7 refuses to play nice with the rest of the Linux network in my house, but it does play. I really didn't have to put that much effort into de-windowsizing my house but I couldn't find a decent Windows Media Center machine for under 200 bucks, and the iHome just plain sucks. I had Windows mobile 6 on my phone and after the migraines of dealing with it's hassles I went with the only other smart phone option on Verizon, Android. I mean I could have went with a blackberry, but, it only made sense to get a third Linux device. 

It is odd since I don't have a hate for Windows 7, I really like it, but ever since the Vista fiasco I became accustomed to Linux in a rather short period of time. I didn't set out to de-ice my home of Microsoft products, but Microsoft failed to deliver anything but XBOX which sits unused and I don't own a 360, it just sort of happened. I even play World of Warcraft quite successfully in Wine. If I needed to do something, there was a fix for it or the device I wanted existed where there was no real competition.
