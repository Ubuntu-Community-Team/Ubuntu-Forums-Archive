### Post by suziequzie on 2006-12-26
While visiting my sister this Christmas, her best friend Lara came by to visit, bringing her 8 (or 9, I can't remember) year old daughter Christina. Denise (my sister) wanted to show off her computer to Lara and Christina. Why? Because Denise loves her Kubuntu desktop that I installed for her. 

As Lara and Christina ooohed and aaahed at the features, games and graphic interface, (they found the xmas tux wallpaper to be extremely cute), my brother-in-law Mike piped in "I haven't logged into windows once since this was installed... this never crashes on me. I can surf the net for hours, play my pogo.com games and never have to reboot." Denise echoed the sentiment. 

I showed them how mozilla is just as easy to use as netscape, "Looks very familiar, don't it?" and played some music on amarok and showed them open office. 

Lara said "Oh, can you fix my computer like that? My sister got our computer 'fixed' by some guy she works with and it doesn't work. It's unbelievably slow and keeps having porn popping up... How much do you want?" 

I said "Uh, nothing. This is completely free software. Just make sure there's lots of coffee for me and maybe some dinner while I work on it." Lara couldn't believe all that software I was showing her was free. Not a penny paid for it except the bandwidth used to download and upgrade, and cds to burn.

Christina really liked the tuxracer game, and frozen bubble, but what really got her excited: "Oh my god Mom, it's Super Mario, but with penguins!!!!" I still think Tux is the coolest mascot ever made. I'll have be sure to install supertux for her. We left Christina to play some of the games, while the adults talked and got caught up... Kubuntu, so easy a child can use it.

So, some weekend, maybe two weekends from now, I'll be going to Lara's to fix her windows AND install Kubuntu. To make sure her problems are software related and not hardware, I gave her Denise's Knoppix 5 live cd to test out, after showing her how to boot into it and instructing her to test out the internet, gaming, multimedia and office apps to see how well they ran. 

If all goes well, I'll be providing tech support for another friend of mine.  
And, I can show her how to set up remote desktop so I can troubleshoot from my home computer (she and I live 3 cities apart from each other). 

From talking with her, I have the feeling that she'll be probably booting into Kubuntu more often than windows. She's used open office at work, so there's a selling feature right there. What she described as her average computer use activities can easily be taken care of by Kubuntu.

I'm gonna bring Xubuntu as well, just in case her machine is a little too old and slow for Kubuntu.

### Post by suziequzie on 2007-01-14
It is done!:KS

She has a dual boot system now: Win98SE and Kubuntu 6.06. 

I was very fortunate. She only had a driver disc for her ethernet card, but she had the original receipt from 2000 listing every component by name and model.  All good name brand parts (Asus mobo and Asus vid card and Soundblaster 16 PCI) so I was able to get those drivers online.

Kubuntu installed without a hitch.  It took a bit longer than I thought, because her processor is older and slower than mine, but it went smoothly.

HD: 8 GB Windows, 7 GB / and 7GB /home partitions, 1GB Swap and 16GB shared Vfat drive.

The only hitch was with tuxracer/planet penguin racer: her vid card is too old for it to run, it crashes X. I'm gonna see if I can find a better one on ebay cheap.

And, they were so pleased I got a huge chocolate bar, and an afghan that I was admring (their house is filled with handmade doilies and afghans... they all crochet...)  I also had my shoulders and scalp massaged. Very much needed after all the time I spent in front of the computer.

And Christina has her penguin theme wallpaper and her favorite kid friendly links and her games (except the tuxracer).  I'm going to ask them in a few weeks which OS they prefer to use.

They were blown away by amarok and the album art showing up the soundmania karamba applet. And the lyrics script. 

I think they're pleased.
