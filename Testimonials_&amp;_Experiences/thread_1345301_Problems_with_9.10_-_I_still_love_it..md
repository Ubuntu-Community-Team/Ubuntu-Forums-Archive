### Post by bshosey on 2009-12-03
OK. I have made several posts about having problems and getting frustrated. So I distrohoped. I like apt-get and synaptic so I went to Debian. It is real nice. I really enjoyed playing with it. I tried Stable ( too out dated for my taste ) Then I tried testing much better ( I thought ) Now this is not to insult to Debian but I found out, as buggy as 9.10 felt to me. Debian Testing was a problem child for me. I installed Debian on Virtualbox and on my Dell Inpiron 1721. The Inspiron has an ATI video and Broadcom wireless and NIC. OK. So this machine really is not the best machine for linux. That is also why I picked this machine to test Debian with. Debian needed time and work to get network up. Sound never got to work. ( did not try real hard ) So you may ask what is this all about.

Here it is.
30 minutes. I have ubuntu 9.10 installed, wireless working. Sound out of the box working. So believe me ubuntu may have some bugs. So does any other operating system. Just in the 3 days of playing distrohoping I realized what made me go to ubuntu in the first place. Keep up the good work ubuntu community. You truly have a fine distro.

### Post by bshosey on 2009-12-05
You know I run a server with some little web pages on it. It also is a DNS, DHCP, and VPN Server. I stream videos to xbox360. Stream videos to Windows XP, Windows 7 and other ubuntu boxes. Heck I even have an old computer hooked up to my stereo so I can use pulseaudio to play audio through it from any ubuntu box. Most of my systems are older dell laptops latitudes and one inspiron. Every thing works great with the exceptions of Cross fading of songs on same album in rhythmbox. And the one issue that is driving me crazy is the networkmanager icon in the notification area. So really that is pretty darn impressive when you think what all I am doing with ubuntu. This would cost me a fortune with most other oses.
