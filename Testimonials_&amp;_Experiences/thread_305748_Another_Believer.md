### Post by gjtoth on 2006-11-23
I got weaned from Windoze about a year ago being lured away by another distro.  At first, it was a little shaky but I managed.  The distro was rock-solid and really user-friendly.  But, over time, it started flaking out on me (repeat characters from the keyboard, screen savers working intermittently, little stuff like that).  Then, one day after I had to reboot to get my sound back, it failed.  I used to frequent the forum of this other distro a LOT and there was a lot of praise being bantered about over Ubuntu.  Even the diehard other distro users freely admitted Ubuntu was a great distro.  So, I burned the ISO and loaded it up.  WOW!

I am so impressed!  I'm running Edgy-Eft and freakin' out over how much easier it is than the other distro!  I've burned a copy for a couple of friends of mine and can't wait til they install it.

To say this is an outstanding product would be the understatement of the decade!

I'm off to make a donation to Ubuntu.  I grudgingly ***PAID*** for the other distro.  I'll GLADLY donate to this one!

Kudos! :KS

### Post by Spin Doctor on 2006-11-24
Great story.

I moved over to Ubuntu on my laptop for like 2 weeks ago, and after a few hickups getting my wireless networkcard to work, it is working amazingly well. The things that does not work as well as you would hope are some of the drivers to certain hardware such as printers and so on, but that is really not Ubuntu's fault, when the manufacturers refuse to distribute Linux drivers for their hardware. Many times however, I find many creative solutions around this.

But I feel as the Linux community and the installation base of linux systems to homeusers increase, the manufacturers will be plain stupid not including drivers to Linux, so I feel this problem will go away when Linux becomes more mainstream on the desktop. It would really be a competitive disadvantage to any manufacturer of hardware not including drivers. And you know companies these days dont want to have those disadvantages compared to their competition =)

So considering Ubuntus young years and that is have become so powerful allready, I am really looking forward to the future of this amazing OP, and the communtiy that stands behind it.
