### Post by KdotJ on 2011-12-30
Hey all,

I used Ubuntu for a while, since Karmic 9.10 which i loved and the introduction to linux. I had then played around with Arch in virtualbox for a while and then in April this year I wiped Ubuntu off and installed Arch as my real OS. It forced me to learn about linux and how to configure things, and more importantly, learn how to fix problems via a console tty when things went bad and the desktop environment was broken. Pacman is without a doubt by far the best package manager I have ever used, the AUR is superb and the wiki offers a huge wealth of information. 

I had my perfect system setup on my laptop, with both KDE and gnome-shell as my DEs. Ive just now got a new pc, a desktop, and I installed Arch on it. I was fiddling with it all when I realised, I just literally couldn't be bothered. Setting up X, the desktop environment the way i wanted, the modules, daemons, getting the right drivers etc. So after about 10mins of tinkering I rebooted and installed Ubuntu. 

I'll see how it goes, Im enjoying Unity more than I thought. Its nice to have it all working so easily, and the software center has come on leaps and bounds since I last saw it. I do miss pacman, apt just doesn't look neat. But its nice to be back, how ever long it may be. It goes to show that sometimes its nice when things just work.
