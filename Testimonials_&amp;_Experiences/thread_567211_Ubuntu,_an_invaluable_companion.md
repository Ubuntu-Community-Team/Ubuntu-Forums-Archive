### Post by Kowalski_GT-R on 2007-10-04
Hi everybody,

not much to add in terms of technical discourse, but I thought I'd share my experience: I guess that's what this section of the forums is for.


- Jumped into Ubuntu this April: faultless installation (see pc specs in sig.)
- It took some time to learn the basics (search for "the costs of Linux"....): CLI, mounting, multimedia, etc. It was all enjoyable nevertheless...

- Had some fault-ish moments: ATI woes and (related?) Open Office problems wich ended up reinstalling two times (without loss of data, which I keep on a second hd), mainly due to the fact that it was quicker for me, instead of trying to recover in that period of inexperience.

Now the good bits: 

a long period of faultless operations,
I have been applying to some jobs all day-long, and Feisty Fawn just flies on my pc....
lots of apps and windows opened, the whole screen cluttered (I like it that way, not a fan of workspaces, which are useless for me most of the time) and not a slowdown, not an hesitation.
Very, very satisfactory!


Overall I think Open Source is the way to go. Ubuntu is getting there as a VERY good desktop OS. 
Can't wait to verify Gutsy performance on my pc. The future is bright....

ciao,

Andrea

### Post by Steve1961 on 2007-10-05
Pleased to hear that initial problems have no been overcome.  When I first starting using Linux (about 3 years ago) I seemed to spend the first 4-6 months constantly reinstalling and struggling with what seemed like insurmountable technical problems that were beyond my ability to fix.  However, it's amazing how you soon get to grips with the basics and I now think Linux is much easier to use and fix than windows (and I fix windows PCs for a living).  Haven't needed to reinstall because of a problem since then, and I've never looked back.  New users just need to be prepared to climb that learning curve for a few months, and stick it out.  The effort is certainly worthwhile :)
