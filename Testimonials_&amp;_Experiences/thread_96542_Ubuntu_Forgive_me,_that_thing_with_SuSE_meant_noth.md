### Post by geokker on 2005-11-29
I am a long, long time user of unix and linux systems. I've been using desktop gui's ever since they were usable i.e. quite recently. I hacked and tweaked early Red Hats and Mandrakes to attempt to de-hideousify them. I've had an on/off relationship with SuSE and the YAST tool. 

When I recently installed Ubuntu Hoary and perused the extremely comprehensive and friendly websites, it was a genuine pleasure. I got all those buzzy, enthusiastic feelings back - the ones I had before WiFi came along to derail most Linux distros. Things 'just work' with Ubuntu. With a tight integration with Gnome, I enjoy using it as much as OS X Tiger. 

Some of the things that make me happy: Snappy windows, 'always on top', Gedit, Evolution (I prefer it to Thunderbird now), general speed, Milosz Wlazlo's icon set, Abiword, system sounds, gThumb, inkscape, gimp, gaim etc. etc. Not to mention reintroducing me to brown.

In the past couple of weeks I had been having a connection problem at the office - I was being frequently disconnected. This didn't happen with Windows. It was hugely frustrating. I tried various things but in the end, I installed opensuse 10. The problem didn't go away. I replaced the WAN router and then everything was working again. Something to do with their not being 'keep alive' pings through Gaim and ssh or something. However, I struggled to get suse to work with wifi among other annoyanced. I reinstalled Breezy and now everything's perfect. I have found my distro!

So, if I read that Google or Sun are buying the project, I'll take it personally.
