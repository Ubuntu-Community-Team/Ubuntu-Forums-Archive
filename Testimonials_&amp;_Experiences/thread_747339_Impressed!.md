### Post by siryuhan on 2008-04-06
I have avoided switching to Ubuntu on my laptop for some time. Previous releases did not support wireless, graphics cards, etc. out of the box, and I didn't want to go through the hassle of disabling a perfectly fine Vista install. 7.10 was different. In about 45 minutes my entire system was functioning under Ubuntu, and I could still access my old files in the NTFS partition! That NTFS partition has been shrinking every day now...

Here are several of my initial reactions. First, almost everything worked perfectly out of the box. Evolution for e-mail, OpenOffice for my documents (I was already using OpenOffice in Vista), RhythmBox for my music, Firefox for my browser-- this was already 99% of what I used my computer for. Graphics card, sound card, even the custom buttons and shortcuts on my laptop such as auto-screen dim were working! The one quip I had was the broken Suspend, which just doesn't work for me. On the other hand, the entire Ubuntu startup/shutdown is actually faster than Vista's suspend/boot for me...

Thank you for a job very well done. I am convinced that I can now order both desktops and laptops without preinstalled operating systems without sacrificing any convenience. All of the computers at home are now running Ubuntu. Now if only major software developers started offering more Linux software then I could follow suit at work as well. I look forward to that day. Keep up the amazing work!
