### Post by Zlatan on 2011-08-04
Hello team, just wanted to share my Kubuntu experience with you.

I'm using Ubuntu since Warty and currently I was on natty after short and pleasent ride with Debian Squeeze. I got back to Ubuntu because of restricted-extras goodie- it is so much more easily done than in Debian that it was enough  for me to switch back.
I've found Unity OK, no problems with it.

And today I've tried Kubuntu. Earlier I've had wireless, gmail and KWin problems here but current KDE (version 4.7) is so much better than previous versions (although I've found 4.6 wonderful as well but not enough for me to switch from Gnome) that I'm considering a switch to Kubuntu this week.
What I like about Kubuntu (or KDE):
- activities (most of us do not use them but I think these could be actually pretty useful
- amarok is definitely better than banshee (defaults matter), at least it has pre-tuned radio stations, I don't have to look for a nice d'n'b station to listen myself
- I love Koffice because of it's consistency and layout (I'm using widescreen notebook so layout matters)
- Kwin has became lighter and it works on my 2GB with integrated Intel graphics ==> pleasant looks
- KDE has a VERY nice netbook layout configuration
- rekonq works with gmail nicely, I don't know probably there will be problems with docs or whatever, but none so far (I prefere native apps in a DE)
- 4 upgrades in a year (2 coming from ubuntu and 2 from KDE), I'm a semi-cloud notebook user so no problem with upgrading for me, I actually find it exciting to see our distros getting better and better;)
- etc&etc, could go on but have to do some work right now;)

Only thing to solve yet is- how to get all my google calendar reminders onto my Kubuntu desktop...
[THIS]("http://www.theirishpenguin.com/2010/02/19/getting-google-calendar-to-work-with-kdes-korganizer-kontact-and-kmail-on-kubuntu-9-10-karmic-koala/") works very good:) Even easier than in Evolution under Ubuntu...
