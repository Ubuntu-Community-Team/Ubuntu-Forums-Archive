### Post by rasteenb on 2008-04-06
My recent installation of Ubuntu 7.10 marks my return to Linux after 10 years away.  It's good to be back.

I installed Slackware on my 486 in the mid 1990s and used it without a GUI for a year.  I finally gathered up the courage (and knowledge) to configure X and FVWM.   I had a blast with that system and it allowed me to run some heavy-duty numerical models as an undergrad.  Unfortunately the machine died in 1998.

Inertia kept me from installing Linux on subsequent PCs until now.
I recently gave my son an ASUS eeePC running Linux for his birthday..  I was hooked again.  I started looking around for a distribution to install on my old Dell Dimension 2350 and settled on Ubuntu.

I was extremely impressed with how far Linux has come in a decade, and what a pleasure the Ubuntu distribution is.  It retained all of the features of Linux I loved in the 90's and added some particularly cool enhancements.  Installation and configuration were so simple I felt a twinge of disappointment in not having to troubleshoot as much as I'd envisioned.   I eventually did have to contend with the "no splash" problem on my Dimension 2350 with the Intel 845G video chipset.  But thanks to a robust Ubuntu community, I was eventually able to cobble together a solution.

I salute everyone who has been involved in the Ubuntu effort and encourage anyone who's thinking about Linux to give it a try.

Rob
