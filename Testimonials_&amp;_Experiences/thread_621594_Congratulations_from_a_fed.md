### Post by gov cheese on 2007-11-23
Congratulations from a fairly senior officer at a federal agency.  I do IT for a living.  Unfortunately, as I moved up the ladder into management, my hands-on IT skills declined - it's a fact of life for many managers and I've lost my patience for command line configuring.  I'm very much a "suit" in other words.  As I went from one version Ubuntu to another, they really didn't make my grade as a completely user friendly OS.  

This Gutsy however, is a different matter -  kudos to all of you who have played a role.  My laptop installation was flawless, painless and fast, and unlike your previous version, discovered my wireless hardware without need for user intervention.  I was especially pleased with the ability to make personalized thematic changes without degrading the responsiveness of the OS, and impressed at the ease of adding and removing applications. 

The Ubuntu human–computer interaction design is very well done, and lacks the klunky design one might expect from a collaborative project.  

While I appreciate your philosophy about freedom, I believe there's room for both proprietary commercial OS's, and free OS's like yours.  Not only room, but a need:  the industry and consumer benefit  from the healthy and vigorous presence of both, driving excellence through competition.

There has long been a recognition regarding the security of Linux backends in the federal government, and perhaps one of these days I'll see Ubuntu as a supported client side OS in the federal agency where I work.  The taxpayers deserve it so keep up the good work.  Again, congratulations for the hard work behind the vision.

### Post by matthewcraig on 2007-12-02
As a taxpayer, one of many in a world of government taxes, I heartily encourage your exploration of Ubuntu.  Using Free Software solutions will save on licensing costs and allow lower cost software configurations.  Additionally, I hope you will consider how the government's role to facilitate infrastructure can be used encourage additional open source and Free Software.  When you evaluate quotations for technical work and for software programs, you can help pay for increased, non-proprietary skills in your community and additional Free Software that can be used for all citizens.  Isn't paying for technology that will ultimately help all your constituents more effective than paying for technology that will only help your governmental organization and the organization that provided the sale?

In short, welcome to the community.  Organizational shifts to Linux solutions require people like you who have an understanding of Free Software and the authority to create change.  Also, look to your local Linux clubs in town for resources to help you meet those goals.  (Links to these clubs in sig below!)
