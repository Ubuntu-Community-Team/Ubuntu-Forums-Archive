### Post by spawn. on 2008-11-05
I've been reading through some of the posts here about those who had or are having bad experiences with Ubuntu. I notice most of the complaints have solvable issues. Too many people run into bumps in the road and then panic without being proactive in finding a solution. As a result, they give up quickly and move on. The only way you can enjoy Linux distributions is to tinker with it more and more. Crawl, Walk, then Run.

I've had good and bad experiences when I first started booting Ubuntu (8.04). Within a few months of using this forum and raping Google searches, there isn't a thing I can't do on this distribution that I couldn't do with Windows. I use my laptop for just about everything.

So before you decide to change your current OS (like Windows) or call it quits, determine how dedicated you are in learning more about a "new" OS. Distributions like Ubuntu aren't necessarily loaded with everything you'll need to make it a productive system. (e.g. I added new repositories to acquire more packages.)

Just giving my 2 cents.
