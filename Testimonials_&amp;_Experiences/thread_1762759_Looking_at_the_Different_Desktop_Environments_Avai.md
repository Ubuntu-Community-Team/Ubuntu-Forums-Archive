### Post by davesalyers on 2011-05-19
I don't quite get all of the Unity and GNOME3 bashing. I have been using Ubuntu since 8.04 came out. I am currently using 10.10 and using older hardware. As I know my hardware will have difficulty with the requirements for Unity and GNOME3 (and I'm not really interested in all of the bells and whistles anyways), I have been experimenting with some of the other desktop environments that are available before I upgrade to 11.04 (probably at the end of summer).

I tried KDE which looks great but slows down in performance with my system a little, but Kubuntu may be a great option for those who do not want Unity or GNOME3. I know this has been repeated but have any of them thought about trying another desktop environment to see if that better suits their needs - as each individual has different needs.

I particularly like the XFCE (Xubuntu) and LXDE (Lubuntu) desktop environments which are more lightweight and probably will be my distros of choice for my machinery from 11.04 onwards. I especially like the menu and taskbar setup in Xubuntu, but they both run great on my machine. My wife and kids acclimated to both XFCE and LXDE environments rather quickly.

We have had no complaints with Ubuntu and we had a relatively seamless transition to a Linux system for home use. I have had only a couple of bugs and got prompt assistance from the helpful community here on the forums. My wife is comfortable with OpenOffice and AbiWord. The kids like the great selection of games (we have a ton of the more casual variety of games on our system) - PLUS all of the casual games they can play through the web browser. And my daughter spends hours using GIMP. We even had a seamless use of WINE to use the kids school textbook CD-ROM (we homeschool).

Thanks Ubuntu developers and community!

I have both Gnome and KDE on Lucid at the moment but after trying PClinuxOS I rather liked enlightenment or E17 as it is called. I had a scary moment when I had both the Ubuntu version (yes it is in the universe repo) and the E17 PPA and there were conflicts.

Whatever your choice - the good thing about Linux/Ubuntu is that you do have a choice - enjoy it and Good Luck.
