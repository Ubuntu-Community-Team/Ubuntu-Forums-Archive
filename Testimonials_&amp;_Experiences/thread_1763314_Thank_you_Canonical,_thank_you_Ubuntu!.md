### Post by dniMretsaM on 2011-05-20
Changing distros will get you absolutely nowhere. You will still have to pick from the available desktop environments no matter what distro you pick. It works vice-versa. Any DE you can get on Fedora/OpenSUSE/Debian/Linux Mint you can get on Ubuntu. Some of them (KDE, XFCE, and LXDE) have their own Ubuntu derivative (Kubuntu, Xubuntu, and Lubuntu respectively). And if you want GNOME 2, switching distro will not change the fact that it is dead. You can still use it (you can still use it on Ubuntu also with Classic mode or an earlier version), but it's still dead.
