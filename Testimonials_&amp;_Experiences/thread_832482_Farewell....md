### Post by jonathanhayward on 2008-06-17
I've been using Ubuntu since at least Dapper Drake, and I am bidding it farewell.

The anvil that broke the camel's back came when I was trying to find a workaround for a major program inexplicably crashing... and I tried to use slocate, and when updatedb wouldn't build a database, getting rid of the package altogether, downloading slocate from source... and finding, with apologies to Arnold Schwarzenegger in *Kindergarten Cop*, "There is no slocate." I remembered, with dismay, that I had seen a note saying that a technical user had failed in his attempts to get slocate or locate working. (And this was not an isolated problem; if I explained why this was the anvil that broke the camel's back, this would be a much longer note.) I realized that I was spending most of my time at work solving system administration problems rather than the programming problems I was hired for.

And all the time through I couldn't help thinking that Hardy Heron was the Linux equivalent of Vista.
