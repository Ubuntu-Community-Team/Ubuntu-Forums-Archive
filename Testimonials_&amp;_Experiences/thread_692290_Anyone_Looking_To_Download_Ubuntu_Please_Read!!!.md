### Post by khensucat on 2008-02-10
[*]This is NOT recommended for people that get frustrated easily.(Always in development)

This is true for any operating system, even Windows. XP gets updates and service packs. Newer and newer versions of software, dll's, etc... ANY operating system will make someone easily frustrated if it doesn't do what the user wants it to do, *easily*, with *easily* being a qualifier relative to the user. 

In relation to the GNU/Linux world, you'd be hard-pressed to find a distro with an all-around more user freindly approach. Off the top of my head, you're talking about maybe the *buntus, Mepis, PCLinuxOS, Mint, and maybe 1 or 2 more. From there, you scale upwards in difficulty, leading to likely frustration if you don't know what you're doing *before* you attempt something.

[*]This is NOT recommended for first time computer users.

Relates directly to the above. Again, it's an easier install than not only Windows, but most other distros. What do YOU recommend?

[*]This is NOT good for computers that don't have atleast 10gb.

I'm going to assume you're meaning magnetic storage, HD space. If you're referring to RAM, I've seen Ubuntu 7.04 run quite snappily on a K62-266 with 196 megs of RAM. So, onto HD space. This is more than adequate, unless you have a specific need in mind, such as storage space for ripping CD's, videos, etc... in which case 10gb is not going to be sufficient for any OS on earth. Although with Windows alone demanding 15gb just for the OS, I'm guessing that at an average size of about 2.5gb for OS, DE, 1 or 2 office suites, 3 or 4 jukeboxes, gimp, 2 or 3 DVD players, 2 or 3 dvd rippers, an instant messenger or 20, a couple of corporate-strength mail programs, and the oodles of programs we all install and play with, that's quite lean and efficient.
