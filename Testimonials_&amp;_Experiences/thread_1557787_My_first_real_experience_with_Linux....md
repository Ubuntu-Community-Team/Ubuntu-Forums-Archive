### Post by littelbro14 on 2010-08-21
...was actually quite some time ago. I believe it was Hardy. I still have the disc around here somewhere. I tried the live CD, decided it wasn't right for me (though I downloaded and used that rough-leather looking background for some time after that), and didn't look back for...well....however long ago that was now.

But then I got into college, and I started taking CS classes, as that's what I'd like to do with my life. I started looking into other OSs. I'd known Unix was there, and Linux, and OS X, but I didn't have any experience with them. What actually qualifies as Unix still confuses me slightly, and I'm too poor to drop a few k on a system from a company I so thoroughly despise, so I picked up Ubuntu again, this time with Lucid Lynx (I considered Slackware, or Fedora, or about a hundred other distros, but I had experience with Ubuntu, however limited).

It's been a jolting experience. I suppose everything that I feel I've learned about Linux I'd known all along, I just wasn't aware of the extent of it all. Such as the good, like the community. I often hear stories of Linux elitists who take every chance they get to point out their system is superior, however, just a few moments on the Ubuntu forums shows a more live and let live attitude. But also the bad. Such as Gnome (I seem to share the same criticisms toward Gnome as Linus), the fact that I'm in a minority as a linux user (which I feel is a direct result of most computer users not knowing wth linux is), and poor support for gaming (which, at this point, is really the only thing keeping me tied to Windows). On that last note, being the kind of guy I am, I've decided to embark on a crusade to harass gaming corporations until they release clients for Linux (I'm looking at you, Blizzard and Valve!).

On a side note, I'd love more swag available in the shop. I'd be happy to support the project monetarily (at least until I can make a contribution in the form of code), but I think Ubuntu needs to incentivize harder, perhaps with more t-shirt designs, some Ubuntu Cola, and for the love of whichever deity you worship, some freakin' case stickers!

Anyway, for what it's worth, here's my testimonial.

tl;dr: I <3 Ubuntu!
