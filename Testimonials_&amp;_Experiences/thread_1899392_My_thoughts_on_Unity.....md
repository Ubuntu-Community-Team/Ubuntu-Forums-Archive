### Post by wazupwiop on 2011-12-23
Many of you who are reading this have probably seen my other posts.  Anyway, my family and I had Xmas on Wednesday, so I got my brother's old laptop.  It is an HP Entertainment PC DV6-1030us.  It has an Intel Core 2 Duo processor and 4Gb of ram.  I am dual booting it with Win7, but this is about Unity.

Lately, I have been using Xubuntu.  For those of you who don't know, Xubuntu uses XFCE.  The desktop has a dock at the bottom of the screen, and the menus look similar to Gnome 2.

Over the last few days, I have been playing around in Ubuntu 11.10 with Unity to see if I like it.  Overall, I am nuetral, but I will explain my thinking.

Unity is meant to impress with its looks.  One side affect of this is increased processing power is needed to run the desktop interface.  While I am not opposed to that, if you have an older computer or what more RAM and processing power for your applications, Unity may not be for you.

Unity is meant for keyboard users.  Coming from Xubuntu, I like the dock and the ways I could customize it to suit my needs.  I think if people would use the dock more, many of the complaints about Unity could be solved.  I have no real issues with Unity, but I can understand how point-and-click users could be put off by the OS.  My thinking is that I can type in what I want into the search bar and get the apps and programs that I need to use.  If I use a program often enough (like LibreOffice), I can attach it to the bar and it is there when I need it.

That said, the desktop environment was a little iffy for me at first.  I expected it to be a little more like Gnome 2, but once I figured out what the Dash Home was, I was okay.  The docking bar has all the basics that I need, Libre Office, FireFox, and the Software Center.  Those are all 1-click and there is no need to go to the Dash Home to scroll through several menus.

It all really comes down to preference.  I kind of like Unity.  It looks nice and runs great on this 2 1/2 year old computer.  While I can understand some of the complaints against Unity, I think it is a usable desktop environment once you get used to the search bar and the dock.

That said, it is really easy to switch back to Gnome 2.  I wish that the install would let you select the desktop environment that you want instead of forcing Unity by default.  I have used XFCE, Gnome 2, and Unity now.  I think XFCE and Unity are tied.  I like how XFCE looks similar to Gnome 2 but has a dock.  Unity is definitely more modern looking, but I wouldn't run it on an older computer.  I really think Gnome 2 is a little dated, but it still looks really nice.  I would like to try Gnome 3 in the future, but for now I am content with Unity, but I wouldn't be heartbroken if I had to switch back to Gnome 2 or XFCE.  I think that using XFCE was a good transition to Unity because I was used to a dock, which Gnome 2 users may not be used to using.
