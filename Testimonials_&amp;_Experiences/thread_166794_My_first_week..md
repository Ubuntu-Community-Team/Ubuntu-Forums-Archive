### Post by eentonig on 2006-04-27
Well, actually it's not.

Few years ago I tried Suse... and ditched it because I couldn't get it to work.
a few years less ago, I tried Knoppix... I didn't ditch it, but it's certainly not my OS number 1. allthough I love the LiveCD opportunities for recovery purposes.

And then, two months ago, my brother mentioned the Ubuntu word to me and gave me a couple of CD's. I installed it on an old laptop to play with, and continued to have it installed as a web-, file, file- and sshserver. 

Next step was to prepare my current Laptop for dual-boot. And every night after work, I booted in Linux and tweaked around, read the forums, googled for help.
And now, one week ago, I went to work and booted in Linux and have been using Linux ever since. So this is my first week of living without Windows.

I still have some gaps in my setup. 
- For the moment, I can't live without the companies Citrix server as I have a strong need for Visio and Project. So I would be delighted if anyone could point me to their linux-counterparts capable of doing the same AND opening the files created by those apps.
- SecureCrt I can substitute by Putty and gtkterm, but they're not as intuitive as 'the real' thing in remembering, organising and automating connections to several different routers & switches.
- Photomanagement: I'm still looking for some apps (or one) like Picassa, which can help me in organising my distributed photo's. (moving them to one location, batch-renaming, insert tags for easy reference, simple editing features for red-eye-removal/cropping/...)

I'm pretty confident that those gaps can, and will be, filled (most of them are probably just me not knowing the correct alternative). so overall, I'm a happy user.

And for all those "Linux isn't ready to replace Windows on the Destop"-adepts. You're quite right. It isn't! I crashed my X several times in the process of tuning and tweaking my system (But hey, I didn't have to reinstall the whole system for this), I spent a lot of time looking for information, configuration and so on.
But on the other end. For my mother, this would be the perfect Desktop.

If you NEED recent games, the latest Messenger, specific Apps, ... or if you're too hung up on the windows way of doing it. Don't botter changing, you'll be disapointed.

And on a brighter note.

I succeeded in getting VanDyke's SecureCRT and Picassa working with wine.

Picassa won't stay on for long, Gthumb and F-spot seems to do the job as well. But SecureCRT was a app I really missed. I need several remote ssh/telnet sessions towards different customers. So an SSH/Telnet/Serial app which allows me to organise these different sessions in a practical way, and allowing me to automate certain tasks, is really something I'm in need of on a day to day basis.

Allthough I'm not a big fan of running WinApps through emulation, I must say that wine has improved a lot since the last time I gave it a try.
