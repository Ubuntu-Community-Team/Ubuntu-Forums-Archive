### Post by foxconn2012 on 2011-10-14
Good morning everyone here, our beloved Ubuntu users, it's developers and Administrators of these forums.

The day, that we all were waiting for, has passed. Every Ubuntu user on that day was feeling deep glory, amusement and general happiness inside him. Even i was happy to see, that our very anticipated Ubuntu 11.10 Oneiric Ocelot was available for download. The announcement of the new version of an Operating System made of pure art with help of Canonical Ltd.'s finest coders was like a balm for the heart. People have downloaded it and installed through ISO'd CD's and upgraded their old natty to the new version. Some of them were satisfied like a child getting his first candy or looking up his dad's hawt adult playboy magazine, but as usual, some 11.04 Natty Narwhal or even 10.10 Maverick Meerkat veterans were not and have just found in this release...ONLY new bugs and useless features, that bring a new meaning to the word "unstable". It is understandable, that someone was crying crocodile tears all over this system, because it is now full of new bugs and other stuff that nobody cares about...well, the only excepts were some SERIOUS bugs, but we are going to talk about that later. Screaming the hell out like a little BAAAWWWWWWW-er won't solve your problems with this release anyway.

So, up to the business.

I wasn't really interested in installing the release at first. All i was thinking about it, was "Okay, there is going to be a new release, but i am not participating onto it. New releases are usually full of bugs and they will be later useless, because people from Canonical Ltd. will "hype" this new version the hell out, but they will really hype out the new bugs and many removed features in that new version system." - And this opinion stood in my mind for five hard months. Some days after, I have looked at it's Changelog... - "Oh wow, Linux Kernel 3! GNOME 3! GTK3! Extreme Security Updates! Cool! I'm on it!" Yeah, it was pretty much like this. Since the moment when Mr. Joseph Robert Gillotti has recommended this operating system for me, I extremely respect the work of the open-source community and Canonical Ltd. Their hard work on the 11.04 that i have used for these six months was extremely appreciated by me and others, despite being one of the more buggy and less stable releases. Few days later, I have downloaded the new release and the ISO file to write it on some blank CD's, install it on our computers, test it's stability and effectiveness in programs such as LibreOffice or few games among my comrades to pull out our final statements about it.

The installation didn't took me so long. On my 750 Kb/s internet connection i have downloaded all the files in a hour or 3/4 of it. Installing it all took me 3 or 2 times more, but it was OK. Satisfaction to see my running new version of Ubuntu was Bigger than any worries about it. The first thing we have liked in this new release is the new Login Screen. Everything in it was performed nearly perfect. I was seriously happy when i saw it's style. It's something i haven't ever seen before on any raw Windows install. I have typed in my password and i have noticed the new version of the Unity interface. This time it was really hard to get used to it, because the dash button is not located on the task bar, but on the app dock. This was annoying for a hour or two, but later i got used to it and now i don't have anything against it. Something that i haven't understood is removal of font options. In Ubuntu 11.04, if you were bored by the standard ubuntu font you could change it to whatever you want with just a few clicks. I was using FreeSans on Natty and when i have updated my Ubuntu version it was actually FreeSans mixed with default Ubuntu font, that is not so aesthetic. Changing fonts on Ubuntu 11.10 is now only possible through an app, that is called "Advanced Settings". Why on earth the developers haven't already included this in the raw package? Something that really drives me crazy, are those buggy window shadows. Every time i was moving a window everywhere and on everything, some "artifacts" appear on the shadows. This is really an awkward bug that hasn't been fixed yet. Maybe for me. Other than that, Firefox 7.0, LibreOffice 3.41, Thunderbird 7.0, updates, updates and updates, New default theme (Adwaita is a neat theme for those who like white, thank you very much, contributors and devs.) and lots of improvements, that could be VERY useful for some people. The integration of these programs into the system and it's standard skin is very deep and there are no problems at all in using them. 

There are no roses without thorns, like folk should say. Startup and Shutdown times are really slower than before, on our beloved 11.04. There are still some features that need to be improved or even replaced by others. For example, Banshee, the standard music player. Why you have replaced Rhytmbox with that almost useless broken iTunes clone? It crashes itself very often and it is unable to sort music through organizing files. Yes, i understand that downloading Clementine or Rhytmbox is the quickest solution and it doesn't take so much time to do this, but i think that Banshee and Ubuntu developers are a bit lazy over this. Yeah, basic strategy. Tell a lazy person that you have a problem, because it will quickly find the simplest solution instead of contributing to the program by fixing something that needs to be fixed. Random crashing, Unability to work with iPods, Difficulty in adding radio channels, i can stand here and give you examples like these without a littlest pause. Before, Banshee was an OK program, but now we can say, that it looks like a drunken woman - It's hard to control it, It's annoying, It looks really poor and it is shameful to be with it. It is also worth of notice that among with new GNOME, GTK and KERNEL versions (mainly 3.x), a slightly visible increase of minimum system requirements. It states, that getting it to work on some weak laptops can be a seriously hard procedure, without being rude. Actually, if you do own a decent PC, Ocelot will run pretty well. Ubuntu still has problems with the Flash Player, because making a stable 64-bit release of it is still a big problem.

Ubuntu 11.10 has a huge base of drivers. It's a topic that is scaring the most of users, because some legends say, that some outdated computer equipment can be hard to make it working properly. For example, dot matrix printers. They are still popular among a little group of users and some of them are trying to make, or at least trying to port drivers for Ubuntu. Their clicky sound was something that i have listened to for years. Since my childhood. Oh, nostalgia. And about the "normal" equipment, there are still no drivers for Canon Pixma IP2700, and i am not even going to talk about S3 Chrome 540 GTX graphics card.

SUMMARY:

When i am looking at Ubuntu 11.10 and think about it's features, i feel happy and i feel that i can die happily and in peace. When i am looking at it and taking a look at the bugs, i am starting to have a tough dillema. Windows 7 or Ubuntu? Aero or Unity? Microsoft or Canonical? Comparing these can be like comparing a M16A2 to the AK. This month we have got a decent release with some bugs fixed, some improvements added, but still, there are some driver problems and startup times really grind our gears.

ADVANTAGES AND DISADVANTAGES:

+ New login screen;
+ Better Unity interface;
+ New theme (Adwaita);
+ New versions of Linux kernel, GNOME and GTK;
+ New versions of Firefox, Thunderbird and LibreOffice;
+ Bug-fixes, improvements;
+ Still theoretically virus-proof;

- Startup and shutdown times;
- Lack of drivers for old machines...
- ...and the newer ones;
- Slight increase in minimum requirements 
- Stability issues;
- Slight decrease in interface modding possibilities;
- Weird shadow bug;
- Weird placement of the Dash button;
- Flash Player is still buggy;
- User-unfriendly interface in Banshee;

**OVERALL RATING: B-**

Thank you for reading this review. Feel free to provide some feedback.

### Post by DeMus on 2011-10-16
To the OP:

My story about 11.10 is just the opposite. I hate Unity and refuse to waste any more time on it. It's an interface for gadgets like smartphones, not for a desktop or laptop computer where serious people try to work on. Having said that, Gnome 3 interface is not much better.

The bugs being present is because Ubuntu wants to release a new version every 6 months and don't have enough time to finalize it. There is an alpha version, a beta version, a RC and still the final version is buggy, very buggy. If they only would change the schedule for new releases things would be a lot better. Do we really need a new version every 6 months? By the time the bugs are ironed out, a newer version is ready to be released and the whole cycle starts over again. Make it one year before a new version is released and finish it before it is released. Would be much better, less people complaining, making more people use Ubuntu since it has become a steady and stable Linux version.

I love Banshee and hate Rythmbox. Why? Because Rythmbox is too heavy. It uses too much of the CPU, interfering with other programs. Banshee on the other hand is a nice lightweight program with which I can do all I want to do, which is playing my MP3 collection and listen to internet radio stations.

To avoid the new look and feel of Ubuntu, in April I moved over to LinuxMint 11 Katya. It's based on Natty but I feel it is more stable and faster. Main reason however is the wonderful neat, slick and shining interface Mint uses. I really hope the developers of Mint stay with this interface so people can continue with this great distribution, without the pain of Unity and Gnome 3 shell.
