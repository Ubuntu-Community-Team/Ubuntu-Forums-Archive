### Post by fuzzyworbles on 2009-06-05
I would just like to say that today was the day that I realized that I have a computer where absolutely everything works. With Windows there was always something awry, be it unexplained slowness, viruses, mysterious errors, etc. With various Linux distributions there was always some unsupported hardware or missing functionality.

Today I'm happy to say that for the first time in my life, EVERYTHING WORKS AS INTENDED!!

Naturally, some tweaking has been involved to make it as such; I don't want to mislead anybody into thinking that I'm a benefactor of the "it just works" cliche.

With the help of forums, experimentation, and patience, my compaq CQ50 116LA now does everything that it was designed to do -- and fast! This includes uncompromised wireless functionality, standby, and full Nvidia support. Furthermore, I don't have to pay the price of gnome/compiz/linux awesomeness with fewer available applications because of the advances in wine. I play warcraft 3 TFT as well as use ms word, excel, powerpoint, and photoshop without incident (not to mention the occational off-brand application). If for whatever reason I can't use wine, I just launch a slimmed-down version of XP under virtualbox.

Thank you debian, canonical, and most of all, the linux support community.
