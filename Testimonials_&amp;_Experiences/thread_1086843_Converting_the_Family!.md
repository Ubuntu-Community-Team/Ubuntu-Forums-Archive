### Post by K7522 on 2009-03-04
Last night I finally figured out exactly what the issue was with the laptop I was working with. So there I was, with my sexy little laptop newly Ubuntufied. I had plans to spend some time with the family, so last night I drove down to my grandmother's where my mother was as well. I brought in my machine and started tweaking, setting up compiz and all the fun toys of Linux.

My mother was the first to notice the OS, and how different it was. I explained to her how easy it was to install programs, showed her Synaptic, and all the cool toys of Compiz.

The conversation went to computers in general, and my grandmother has an ancient Gateway machine that takes at least ten minutes to boot, and more for the desktop to fully load. I pulled out my disk, threw Ubuntu on that computer without telling her, and everything worked flawlessly. (Still don't know what her response is going to be...)

Later that night I went to my mother's to install Ubuntu on her machine, she loves it. I set up her email in Evolution and set up the cube in Compiz, which is what she really wanted I think.

So two more machines that run on Ubuntu. One is really happy, the other I apprehensively laugh at every time I think of it. I hope she likes it and doesn't die of a stroke!

Go UBUNTU!
