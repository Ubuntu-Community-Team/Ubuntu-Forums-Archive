### Post by mistypotato on 2009-02-20
About 2 weeks into my new voyage into ubuntu and one thing I have noticed is that it is extremely stable.

Things that I'm sure would have crashed my WinXP boxes are shrugged off by Ubuntu.

Like loading up the CPU and simultaneously maxing out the memory.
Ubuntu dutifully continues churning rock stable when I load the cpu to 100% and keep going.   It just doesn't want to break.  :D

It's really refreshing.   Like others new to Ubuntu, I get frustrated sometimes when things seem to be umm....not cooperating....but it's usually that I don't know something (like permissions) that is the cause and not a problem with Ubuntu.  Frustration with learning something new is the nature of the beast.  I remember back in 1982 learning Dbase.  It drove me nuts...but once I grasped it, I used it extensively for many years and it seemed so easy.

I think the vast majority of people have no clue what's available here (Ubuntu).  I've been a Windows user since 3.1 and a MS patron (DOS from the beginning) since before the world knew who Bill Gates was and I can honestly say that Ubuntu and Open Source has finally rid me of my MS addiction.  Sun's _Open Office_ is awesome for example.  

I use computers HEAVILY.  I run servers, program in several languages and have at least 5 computers running in front of me at all times so I'm not a "casual" computer user.."Geek" is more like it.  But Linux is still new to me.

If Linux / Ubuntu is new to you also,  I hope you can see that this is actually a great opportunity and resource made possible by the hard work of many, many people and while it's not for everyone,  there's really no point in complaining ....complaining should be proportional to how much you paid to use it.  If you paid zero...complain zero....or just move on.

But I've found that Ubuntu and all the Open Source software has risen to the point that Linux is truly now a viable alternative to Commercial products and this has got to worry the people at Microsoft.  The sudden loss of all my Windows boxes to malware brought me back to Ubuntu.
The choice was to pay $200 - $300 per box for an OS (minimum) plus licenses and all that jazz....ummmm....I'd rather spend that money on a new box or movies or anything actually.  I think MS shot itself in the foot by not offering Windows for a really affordable price and making up the difference in productivity software and commercial licensing rather than trying to bleed the average end user bone dry.

One long term concern I have is that Ubuntu has raised the excellence in computing so high that it's likely that Linux / Ubuntu will be seeing a substantial increase in usership and as the Linux pie slice increases, so does the incentive for hackers and hoodlums to write intrusive and malicious software.

A second is that as that same pie slice grows, the "CABLE TV" effect will kick in where things that were once taken for granted (do you remember when Cable TV was commercial free?) will change and the larger market will cause more and more of the once free and open Linux software to become.....ummmm...."not as free".  I don't know all the politics behind Ubuntu or Open Office but I'm not convinced the motivation is not so much to offer free this and open that as it is to limit the power of Microsoft.

So this post was just a bit of an early morning, coffee time, discourse on Ubuntu and Linux.  I hope you enjoyed reading it and that it's relevant.

And I pledge to try not to get frustrated and vent in my posts :)

just my 2 cents

### Post by Armor Nick on 2009-02-20
It's a good post and you're right, but I think people are allowed to complain.
HOWEVER, this does not mean every Windows addict, as you beautifully call it, should go on a rant when they find out Ubuntu is not a free Windows clone. The people of ReactOS are working on that.
Making complaints can sometimes be a big help in fixing bugs, since someone has to complain before the developers know there is a bug.
And about your long time concern, don't worry. Linux is secure by default. As long as you don't install apps that you don't trust (which is harder in linux than in windows) and you don't run as root, you should be fine. And even if you get a virus (as a normal user), you can just create a new user and delete the old one and the viruses should be gone automatically ;) .
