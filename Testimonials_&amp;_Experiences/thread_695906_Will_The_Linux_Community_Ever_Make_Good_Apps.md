It depends where the bug is introduced. If it's in the source of an application then yes it will be seen in every distro. If there's a packaging mistake or a problem in  the way a disto implements a library or a daemon/service used by that application (for example) then the bug will be distro specific. Many applications are customised by the distros, and not just to suit their distro structure, sometimes features are removed/altered too because there are worries about distribution of certain technologies in some places. This is most obvious in multimedia applications where mp3 or aac abilities are removed in apps like k3b, easytag and so on. You can also notice that the versions of amule available from many distros repos are compiled without support for protocol obfuscation. Different distros have different policies, some leave the applications basically as the author intended and some do a lot of modification.
