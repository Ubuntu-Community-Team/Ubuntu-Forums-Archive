### Post by mister_lister on 2008-04-11
I have good news to report after 6 months of continued Ubuntu use.

I have:
*550 mhz AMD K2 processor
*327 mb of RAM
* nvidia Geforce first generation video card with 120mb video memory
* A random generic, mostly unknown mother board (a FIC mother board)
* Sony CD-RW
* A windows network with one 98 machine and a HP 612c printer shared from it.

This machine has been in continuous use with various operating systems and battery changes since 1998. It is still my work rig. You would think it would be as slow as molases, but it is not. I can do all of my home computing with easily. Here is an example of what I do with this machine DAILY:

* Surf the net with Firefox 1.5.0.12 eol. I jumped up the flash and java. The only thing I don't get is Active X type controls but that is the same with all firefoxes.
* Full email, both with Evolution (pop mail from gmail) and online email sources. Not one hitch thus far.
* Full office compliment of applications (write, spreadsheet, presentation, and database) compliments of OPEN OFFICE 2.0. I do tons of things with it from writing a book, papers for college, do my finances, compile holiday mailing addresses which I can easily integrate into write for form letters and labels.
* I burn cds of stuff regarly.
* I watch movies on youtube and other sources.
* I back up and print to windows shares with samba, no problem just a little configuring. Back up to a USB drive weekly. Back up to floppy daily (some key files). USB works great for camera too.
* I edit photos with Gimp.
* Instant messenger

Mind you this is with a PC that is more than ten years old. Most applications take a few seconds to load but while I am working everything works smoothly.

IT is my home rig. Sometimes I use my dell laptop with vista installed but rarely, only for sites that are microsoft centric (some radio online).

WHAT I DID TO MAKE IT ALL HAPPEN:

I came here and posted and searched for solutions, from integrating a floppy on my desktop to getting all the liscensed software that doesn't come with FREE Ubuntu. I configured samba after asking a couple pointed questions. I regularly update, whenever that update icon appears I update immediately. I also opened up my librarys, from resticted to universe. I installed pidgen and some other programs the hard way (which is easy after a few times).

I highly recommend LTS 6.06, conical has been really good about updates and the system I have is running tits to the wind. It is good for older systems that have fairly popular components, maybe even some new systems (I don't know I haven't tried).

Thumbs up Ubuntu! The best Linux source I have come across in ten years. I have tried almost all on this system and had no where near the luck I have had with Ubuntu. I will keep on plugging along with this system until it dies or the updates stop comming.

UBUNTU IS THE FIRST REAL ALTERNATIVE TO WINDOWS THAT I HAVE DOWNLOADED FOR FREE!
