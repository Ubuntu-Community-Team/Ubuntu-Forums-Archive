### Post by morgonhed on 2010-09-25
Hi,

after having used Ubuntu for about 2 years I am now leaving it for Debian.

At the time I was very happy with 8.04 (coming from SuSE) but I am extremely disappointed with 10.04 and I don't see any indication that things will get better (for me).

I have had a lot of issues with 10.04 (I have posted a few of those in this forum) and what really is making me leave Ubuntu is that I did not get any (useful) support from the community.

Every issue I was able to solve I had to solve pretty much on my own.

Canonical has done a lot for Linux, I am grateful for that, but for my Ubuntu is becoming less and less stable and it is getting hard to find people who really understand how to configure things differently when you are not happy with the "standard" way (e.g. notifications, power management etc).

Ubuntu is the best distribution for beginners, but I want more control over my system.

Goodby!

### Post by Hexster71FD on 2010-09-27
Yeah, I've had problems with the notifications and trying to configure the Gnome desktop into something I liked. For me it was just a matter of using Mint instead of Ubuntu. Mainly because I like having a single bar on the bottom instead of two bars and having to play around in-between them. Plus all the goodies come pre-installed (flash, mp3/DvD codecs, etc.) on Mint. The only thing I had to do was install the MS core fonts and I had a desktop OS that I could use every day.

I'm not sure about the stability of the Ubuntu system getting worse, but Linux in general is a work in progress, so I expect to run into bugs that may cause me to re-install or choose a different distro.

There's plenty of distros out there so go ahead and try them all. It will broaden your knowledge of Linux and will help you find whatever it is your looking for hopefully.

Good luck :D
