### Post by j.bell730 on 2009-07-07
A while back, I had Windows installed on my machine. From research I had previously done about Microsoft, I knew that they were a very untrustworthy and devious company, so I decided that I would switch from their operating system. I wanted to switch to Linux, but, there are so many distributions to choose from...where to begin?

I heard a lot of good things about Ubuntu, so I decided to give it a try. Immediately, I didn't like the way that the GNOME desktop environment looked (I was inexperienced, and didn't know you could change it), so I decided to give Kubuntu a try. I really liked it, so I used it for a while. I felt that there was something missing though. I didn't seem to be content with Kubuntu, and I wanted to be completely suited to my operating system. I tried Mandriva, and I thought it was really nice, but I absolutely couldn't be without my favorite package manager, apt. I tried PCLinuxOS...again, I liked it, but, I couldn't stand rpms, and I couldn't be without debs! I tried Gentoo, but I couldn't even get that installed! I tried Debian, which, I liked the most of all non-Ubuntu distributions. But, there was something missing...there weren't a lot of communities as active as this one for Debian. So, back to Kubuntu I went...there was no better choice for me.

Eventually, I missed playing my PC games on Windows, so I decided to reinstall that over top of Ubuntu (as I have little hard drive space). While installing Windows, wouldn't you know it...my mouse and keyboard weren't working, because they are both USB input devices, so, all I was able to use was an old, non-USB keyboard which I had lying around, until I had Service Pack 2 installed, which provided USB support. Other than the installation problems, I was doing just fine on Windows...until about a week later. I tried to install something which required a reboot, so, I rebooted the PC, and I couldn't log in, there was a required file missing which is responsible for logging in on Windows. I didn't know of any way to get the required file, so I tried to repair my installation. Repairing did nothing to help me and my problem. So, I tried to reformat my computer...as I was reinstalling Windows, again, my USB devices didn't work. I wasn't about to do this, to only have it happen again, so I just said skip it.

I lost my Ubuntu disk while cleaning, so I got on my dad's computer, downloaded, and burned Kubuntu, popped the disk in, and...ahh, how refreshing it was to have working USB devices while installing an operating system. After the installation, while looking for things that I had missed while I was on Windows, I discovered...working Creative X-Fi drivers, and they're easy to install! Finally!!! I played around with KDE 4, which I liked, but, it was too complicated for me I decided to give GNOME a try again. I installed GNOME on my machine, and thought I'd keep KDE there just in case. In a matter of minutes, I found out how to customize GNOME to my liking, then uninstalled KDE 4 off my machine. In GNOME I'll stay, as I don't think I've felt this content with any operating system I've ever used. My own operating system, for me to do what I want with, with no limitations. Never will I go back to using Windows. Often, I'll find myself really impressed that much of this, higher quality software, was created by a community of people working for free.

Thank you Ubuntu.
