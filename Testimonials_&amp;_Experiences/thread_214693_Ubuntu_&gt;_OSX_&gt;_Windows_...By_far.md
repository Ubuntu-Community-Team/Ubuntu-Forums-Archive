### Post by FurryNemesis on 2006-07-13
I'd have to agree with you. In my house we have an XP desktop, my laptop running Dapper and a new Macbook Pro.

I'm my family's tech support. I was a windows power user, and then one day my  brain went *ping* at the creeping degredation of XP on my laptop and I switched. 

We recieved the desktop a month or two earlier, before I switched.
Needless to say, I leapt on it. It was a fresh XPSP2 install on a nice shiny 3.2 Ghz P4 with a (then) fairly decent budget graphics card and it went like a rocket. 

My laptop, at this point, was dying by degrees. I researched linux, decided it might save my laptop, and after a botched FedoraCore4 install (not sure why I went for that, really) found out about ubuntu. At this point, it was Breezy.
In terms of start up times, my laptop outstripped the desktop. I was blown away. Then I got into the process of customising it and it was like scraping the snow off your car and finding a Ferrari where you thought you'd parked your Ford.

Programs flew. Evolution was what I'd desperately wanted Thunderbird to be. Firefox was back and faster than ever. (I'll be looking into swiftfox at some point). I realised that underneath all the chug and fluff of XP that I had had a very good laptop. It just took ubuntu to bring it out. I had everything proggy-wise at my fingertips through the repos. It was all free and discless. I loved it; it was a breath of fresh air for me in terms of delivery.

Now it's several months later and I've upgraded to dapper. Wireless works out of the box, hurrah. My hotbuttons work and so does my media control panel on the front. I still, however, can't mount my Nokia N70. That's annoying, and it looks like gnokii doesn't support that generation yet.

Now, the Macbook. I helped to set this thing up, and there was a tiny niggle at the back of my mind. 

Back in high school my best friend had been a mac fanatic. He had one of the first IPods and was hooked on his lime green mac desktop (OS 8.6) and how great it was. There were days when he literally wouldn't shut up about having no viruses, malware etc. 

Until one fateful day.

I'm sure the two are unrelated, but the coincidence was sublime. First, his cat territorially marked his tower casing. Copiously. 

Five minutes later (and I still don't know how this happened, unless he was hacked), his hard drive spontaneously dismounted, wiped itself and then for want of a better word, *caught fire*, probably due to cat-pee seepage. Gutted wasn't the word. You know those days when you feel like the gods are personally screwing with your life? It was like that.

I couldn't believe the damage. Ever smelt burnt cat pee? It's *horrific*. :-# 

Anyway, back to the present. Installation was ok, even though there were several teething problems with the DSL modem, even with drivers installed.

Now, if I chose to, I could conceivably do everything on here via the command line. I choose not too because I don't want RSI and really can't be bothered.

Mac OSX seems to be the opposite. If possible it seems even more GUI-oriented than XP. There's a wizard for *everything*, and the command line is very hidden.
But due to the build-it-idiot-proof approach of OSX, I couldn't easily customise. Codecs were a bitch, and it was back to the old windows method of scour the net and download proggys. That said, it looked very nice and was very shiny. But I wasn't feeling it. That said, for a computer newbie, which the actual day-to-day user is, it was perfect.

Now, the XP machine is at this point about 6 months old, and it's only in good condition because I've been looking after it. 

BUT 

I can still feel the chug setting in. It's gradually slowing down. I'm not looking forwards to the day it packs up and having to tell everyone else here that the OS needs re-installing, possibly the entire system due to a 1-partition setup by the OEM.

And if they upgrade to Vista? I'll run a mile.

That or viciously install something CLI-only on it and hide the manual, just to teach them a lesson. :D
