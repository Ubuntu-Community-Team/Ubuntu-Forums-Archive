### Post by Gorlist on 2012-01-27
Im not sure whether this is the correct forum thread, but here goes!

We've been using Ubuntu in the office since 8.04 with multiple machines, and have in general been very pleased - however since late last year, 11.10, we've now moved over to Xubuntu. 10 x better than unity.

I understand the need to progress, but in an office environment, when you have people complaining about the interface it shows that perhaps Ubuntu has gone in the wrong direction.

The general consensus was, it simply isn't conducive to good work! over the course of day we found it waring to use, having to wait on menus to pop out, buttons to appear, and previously simple operations of swapping between windows had to be undertaken as an epic mission!

I think in general the major underlying problem has been the relentless search for more screen space, and it now effects user performance. We need a task bar, we need application menus to remain the same, we need it be targeted for PC users with large screens, not mobile phones and Ipads.

### Post by jal301 on 2012-01-28
I believe that the OP's complaint is the same as mine, and if so, I hope that there are enough people out there to create the pull necessary to keep *everything* from going this way....

I am one of the few who aren't desperately tied to their mobile device 24/7, so I am really not in tune with recognizing somebody's logo for every action that I want to take.  That's nice if a company can come up with what they believe to be a flashy and recognizable logo to associate with their product, but I'm not committing my life to remembering the program associated with every logo just because somebody decided that we're going to move away from including WORDS in a menu.

I am open to the fact that this change may be wonderful to somebody else and I am not saying that this option should not be available for an OS.  But for those of us who are not so enthralled by the mobile device world, please keep the option available of what has been the standard in the past.

The 'mobile-looking' environment has it's place, but not on my PC when I'm attempting to actually do some work - an attempt which becomes quite poor and frustrating with these new environments which some consider to be 'enhancements' but are actually only forcing me (us) to work with what are truly just somebody else's **preferences**.

Some, above, have suggested that Xubuntu may be what I'm looking for...as of now I'm happy with 10.04LTS.  Perhaps I'll need to make the change, but I'm hoping that everything doesn't go the way of the mobile-look and way of working.  If it works for you, great - but please don't completely take away the current way of working from those who will not be living that kind of life on a PC.

Thanks for your consideration.
