### Post by fredfelter on 2008-10-24
I'm going to get roasted for this but, in my humble opinion, Linux/Ubuntu will never reach a significant level of success until it changes its ways. I am a reasonably intelligent person with a number of years of computer usage (Windows) and, in fact, began to enjoy working with computers once I got past the command line DOS OS and the unstable Win 98 to a truly people functional OS in Win 2K (which I still use on one of my laptops). 

Based on some enticing information I decided to take on Ubuntu. Supposedly I would be able to substitute a Linux app. for about any equivalent Win. program and that the latest versions of Ubuntu were becoming so user friendly they are being sold installed on consumer computers.

The reality for me has been far different. After hundreds of hours of effort, mostly by googling or soliciting forum help, all I now have is no more than a handsome desktop and a web browser (FF) that works almost as well as the Win. one ("already running, need to close that version" what?). I have attempted to use Skype, and at one point it was functioning but soon died and I've never been able to resurect it (mic doesn't record), streaming video works at times and not others, I can't sync my Palm and I can't get my GPSr to be recognized. 

Now I realize that there are a lot of you out there that have all these capabilities, and more, up and running but if you knew what I've been through you wouldn't say I hadn't given it a good shot. And, judging from the forum activity, I'm not an exception.

Like DOS was, I don't want to have to learn the language to use the system and like Win 98 wasn't, I want it to be reliable. That hasn't been achieved yet and I don't have the energy to keep hitting my head against the wall.

### Post by estyles on 2008-10-24
Some people have problems with Ubuntu where the time/difficulty to fix is either beyond their ability or just not worth it.

Some people have similar problems with Windows.  I switched to Ubuntu because trying to deal with my wife's Windows problems were not worth it, and Ubuntu "just works" on her machine. 

At this point in time, there are probably more problems, as a percentage of total users, with Ubuntu.  At least with Ubuntu, the community seems (to me, at least) to be more helpful.  And hopefully with time, the people with major problems in Ubuntu will be a smaller ratio of total users than those with major Windows problems.  Only time will tell.

Unfortunately, if you have problems with Ubuntu that aren't worth the time and effort to fix (or if they're impossible to fix, which I find hard to believe), and if Windows works for you, then there's nothing anyone can say that will change the fact that, unfortunately, MS may make the best OS for you.  Again, hopefully that will change with time.

edit: Oh, and if you're still willing to try a Linux distro, Mandriva, from my experience, has excellent hardware support.  It's not my favorite distro, just because I'm comfortable with Ubuntu, but it was actually easier for me to configure my hardware in Mandriva than anything else I've tried.
