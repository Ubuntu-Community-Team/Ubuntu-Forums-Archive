Welcome! How long have you been with Linux in general? How long with Ubuntu? Just curious.

Yes, I couldn't agree more. I'm also finding it far easier than ever to get relatives on to Ubuntu because when I visit them with their Windows PCs, they're slow zombies with popups everywhere, a copy of limewire that actually is their enemy and they don't know it, with unupdated virus scanners (kind of like the old "set the clock on the VCR" problem) and the firewall turned off. So then they ask if they can have their Quickbooks, and I point them to several online versions on the web. They ask for a word processor, a spreadsheet, a slideshow presenter, and I show them a sped-up OpenOffice that I have configured. They ask for an image editor and I give them Gimp and Inkscape. I get them on Thunderbird Mail, Firefox web browsing, and Gaim chat. I configure multimedia to work completely on their system for DVD playback, DVD burning, audio CD burning, and MP3 playback and conversion. I bookmark this website for free tech support, along with the Ubuntu pay support site, and I make those bookmarks easily available. I also bookmark reddit, Yahoo news, Yahoo movies, Wikipedia, YouTube, and Gmail. I give them the ytr script and the yt2mp3 app that lets them extract simulated stereo MP3s from YouTube videos. In case they need to VPN, I give them gvnpc and tsclient and configure and demonstrate those. Last, I have an iptables firewall script that I've reused over and over and it works well.

I mean, I show up, show them how bad their Windows PC is, explain that identity theft is no laughing matter, give them a dual-boot with Ubuntu or boot up on the live CD, offer them that I can backup their stuff to USB drive, upgrade them to Ubuntu, and see if they like it. And if they don't like Ubuntu, I explain why getting a Mac is 10x better than going with Windows Vista.

So far, so good. They like free. They like Ubuntu.

And when I send an email, I got tired of people's hotmail accounts putting a signature line bragging about Windows Vista, so all my signature lines brag about Ubuntu.

Microsoft can stuff it for being so insensitive to its customers.
