### Post by karellen on 2007-09-07
I’m leaving Ubuntu. In fact, it just happened, some weeks ago. I’m just tired. I don’t want to learn ‘new things’ about this matter, I don’t want to hunt hardware that would definitely work with Linux, I’m tired of workarounds and I want to be able to use all of my gadgets at 100% of their capacity. I want my documents to be decently printed and to use my scanner and webcam. My girlfriend will leave for studying abroad for 5 months and I’ll need a webcam to stay more easily in contact – and that’s where yahoo messenger enters the stage. She’ll take a lot of photos whom she will share with me fast and easy with one click – photo sharing, the same old yahoo messenger. Not to mention the file-sending. There have always been windows-native applications that I’ve missed in Linux….and I realized some time ago a very simple fact: why should I continue living without them, searching for workarounds and limited functionality?  I don’t want to get to know every inside of an OS, I don’t have the time nor the willingness. I’m studying history and international relations, so why to make my life harder?...I’ve used Linux for more than 3 years and Ubuntu for more than one…but now I feel the need for a change. So one night I took the turn and I installed Vista. After I disabled aero it runs just fine, as fast as xp was. Plus I happen to like the new interface, even without aero. I use it for almost a month and still no problem, crashes or freezes. If something bad happens, there’s always XP waiting in the drawer. With a decent firewall (zonealarm) and with a decent antivirus/antispyware (nod32, avast, bitdefender) +some basic common sense I hadn’t had any problems with malware and virus in windows for ages. I’m not a fan of windows per se, it’s all about the software ecosystem that it’s built on top of windows platform.
Ubuntu is still there, on its partition, I can make it to dual-boot again but honestly, I feel no need for that, at least until now. Maybe someday in the future I’ll come back, maybe not. for now I’m comfortable with the way things are. I’ll continue playing with live cds (I’m waiting for gutsy gibbon) and probably I will be curios to see how things will evolve with Linux. Hardy Heron is a good candidate to be installed on my rig. So does opensuse 10.3. I know, I really know. I’ve used linux beginning with suse 9.3, so I’m pretty into this matter; I've used fedora core 5 and 6, opensuse 10.1, sled 10, ubuntu 6.06, 6.10, 7.04. I know the causes and consequences: drivers, lack of them, blame manufacturers  etc etc etc. but now I don’t care anymore. “why?” became less important to me then “can I do this or not”. I appreciate the ideas (and idealism) behind Linux, and especially behind Ubuntu. Humanity towards others I believe it’s one of the noblest and greatest endeavors that I know about after 2000’s.  The community is fantastic, very helpful and friendly and willing to help and give advice. I’ll certainly miss all these things. It’s not like “Linux is not ready for the desktop” and bullshit like that, it’s just I’m not ready for it. I want things done the easy way and with as little hassle as possible. And I have no problem in admitting my own indolence regarding computers and the time I am willing to give them. After all, they’re just tools, I want to live using them, not living for them and in front of them…

It’s just the way things are, I find time to be very precious…&#61514; that’s it for now, I’ll continue to hang around these forums as I’m kind of addicted to them and overall I find them an interesting and complex experience
keep in touch :)
