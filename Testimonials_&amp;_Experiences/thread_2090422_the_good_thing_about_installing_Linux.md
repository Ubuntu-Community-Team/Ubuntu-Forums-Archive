### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2012-12-02
This is a nice feature!  By running the 'try Ubuntu' first and then selecting 'install Ubuntu', your computer is useful even during an install.

I know how and why it works that way - but it still feels a little magical to me, too.

Another nice behavior is when I boot from a Ubuntu USB stick to rescue a Windows PC; I can run 'dd' or 'dd_rescue' and copy one hard drive to another, and still be able to surf the web or listen to the MP3s I keep on the USB stick while the hard drive is being copied.

Most folks are blown away by that.
