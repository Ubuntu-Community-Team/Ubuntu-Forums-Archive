### Post by tom66 on 2010-04-23
Today at school (which I shall be shortly leaving - 3 weeks until study leave), our English teacher was trying to play a DVD. On the first laptop, he could not get it to recognise it, and the second laptop would recognise it but Windows Media Player refused to play it and VLC would play it but it was garbled and jerky. Tried for 30 minutes to get it to play; tried every player - QuickTime, VLC, RealOne, WMP - none worked. (Both laptops ran Windows XP Professional.)

It turned out that it was an encrypted DVD. I had never played an encrypted DVD before on my laptop, running Ubuntu 10.04. It wouldn't play it either in VLC, but looking in dmesg I could see it said something about not being authorised or having the encryption key. So in a hunch I installed libdvdcss2 (which was easy, from Medibuntu) and popped the disk in. It played fine. I plugged my laptop into the projector, used System > Preferences > Monitors, and turned on the projector (mirror screens), which worked immediately without an Xorg restart. So we got to watch the last 15 minutes on my laptop. Though the audio was a bit quiet out of the audio jack I think it was because the line level was set too low, setting the amplifier louder helped. At the end unplugging the monitor and setting the resolution was a cinch. So thanks to Ubuntu and Linux. 

He said he'd be getting a standalone DVD player for £25 - much easier anyway, but he loved how Linux (he pronounced it Ly-nucks) just "worked". 

I'm now using 2.6.34-rc5-generic on this laptop which has dynamic power saving for Radeon cards built in. It works much better. Resume from standby is less than two seconds in most cases, 3D works perfectly, and startup/shutdown is quick.

### Post by mcduck on 2010-04-23
> **Dragonbite said:**
> "SysRq"?

Alt+PrtScr+k, if you prefer. :)

It kills all processes on the current virtual console, so if you do it while in X you get exactly the same result as with Ctrl-Alt-Backspace, but as a bonus it works in the same way in text TTYs, in case you ever end with some program freezing the whole console.
