### Post by x33a on 2009-08-22
ishan, the scenario you mentioned exists all over india (in fact, i feel almost the same as you do, about it). but, i wouldn't rant over it (because that would be endless and boring), so let's focus on the seminar.

well, as dixie said, first focus on security. even microsoft slaves don't like getting infected every time they plug in a pen-drive or connect to the net :D.

then, there is wine (for apps like photoshop, ms-office, adobe acrobat which people are addicted to). though if you can manage to convert people to at least openoffice, that would be awesome.

you can also speak about wubi or dual booting, so that people can try out linux for a few days (apart from the live cds).

and of course, mention ubuntu or mint or even pclinuxos as the distros for starters.

Important: make them aware about piracy (most don't know what piracy is and that the windows they are using is stolen)

and in case people wonder, how linux is free, then you can make them aware about the free software foundation and the gnu/linux philosphy.
