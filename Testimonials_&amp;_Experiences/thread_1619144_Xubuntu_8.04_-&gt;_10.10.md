### Post by viMitch on 2010-11-11
My first successful installation of a Linux distribution was back with Xubuntu 8.04 with Wubi. Soon after that I moved to GNOME and never really turned back. But, just recently I tried out the xubuntu-desktop meta-package, the results were amazing! :)

  The only thing I did not like, was the fact you cannot edit the menus without editing XML files (When installing multiple desktops on a single install the menus get full of rubbish.) The new theme for Xfce is great, and Google Chrome looks gorgeous on Xfce (In comparison to Ubuntu's Gnome where it looks a bit out of place.) Xfce is lovely to work with on my Dell Mini 10v, performance of the desktop isn't even a worry on GNOME, but Xfce suits my style better. :)

Two and a half years of change is sure worth the wait. ;)
Thank You, for making Xfce feel that bit more polished! :P
