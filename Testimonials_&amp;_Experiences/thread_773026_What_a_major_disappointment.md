### Post by nikkiana on 2008-05-13
But who's responsibility is it to find the bugs and report them? Dare I suggest that it's everybody's responsibility? 

The way I see it, Ubuntu (and other open source projects) are community projects. At the heart of it, we have the code that makes it possible... Written by tons of men and women who have varying motivations for doing it but all have a desire to share fruits of their labor with the larger community... but the thing is, developers aren't and shouldn't be the only people who contribute to a open source project. 

Contribution is beta testing. Contribution is making a bug report. Contribution is writing documentation. Contribution is helping someone you know in your community install Ubuntu on their computer and teaching them what a package manager is. Contribution is answering someone's question on a forum. Contribution is even asking questions about things you're not sure of. You don't need to be technical to do ANY of that stuff, all you need is the willingness to give back to the community and help things be better. 

I'm not trying to be preachy, but I find it extremely empowering as a user to be offered that sort of responsibility in exchange for a free and open operating system...  It means that in the grand scheme of things, I matter. I never got that sense when I handed my hard earned money to Microsoft or Apple. 

That said, I don't think you're wrong. Bugs happen, and if people... especially those among us who perhaps aren't the most techncial of the bunch... beta tested we'd stand a better chance at having a more stable product. But I guess my question to the entire non-techncial side of the Ubuntu community is this... Why not you? 

As for the Firefox thing... Since the development cycles of Firefox and Ubuntu aren't in sync, I personally strongly prefer the possibility of being slightly inconvinenced by my browser's beta-ness now than to not be able to get Firefox 3 through the package manager when Firefox 3 comes out of beta. Look at it this way, any bugs you find in Firefox, you're contributing back to the Firefox community. Yay for contribution! You're making a difference!
