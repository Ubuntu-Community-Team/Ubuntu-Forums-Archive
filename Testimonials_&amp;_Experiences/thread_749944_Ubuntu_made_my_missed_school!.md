### Post by aimpau on 2008-04-09
I'm a definitely first time linux / xubuntu user.

It was break time and I have 3 whole hours of nothing to do so I went home. As I logged in to Windows, I remembered downloading a xubuntu ISO file. So, I said to myself, why not try it out?

So I got to the page where everyone must have christened it with the name "GRUB". So I started to load the kernel, and oops, I dropped my pen on the floor. I picked it up and when I looked into my monitor, I thought it crashed. Everything was already loaded and my cpu seems idle. What the? IT'S THAT QUICK TO LOAD? And I've heard countless times how slow it is to load in the LiveCD. But that was oh so quick!

To make a long story short, I was tinkering these:

1. printer - i already enabled my epson c90 printer using the CUPS c68 driver.
2. i played around the composite window tweak making my desktop cool
3. themes and what to do with them
4. the terminal - i know the command exit and shutdown -P
5. workspaces and what they are
6. Abiword - printed my first 2 documents
7. I just found out that you can LITERALLY edit everything that you see. However, just a quick question, what code do you use? I have knowledge about C++ but where to begin?
8. Started using Gimp.
9. Accessed the Synaptic thingy and found out that I have hundreds of software ready to be used.
10. Played a simple AVI movie (non divx/xvid)
11. Access almost all features in the System and settings menu.
12. Played World of Warcraft and StarCraft 2...kidding!

Tinkering with all these things actually made me missed my class! LOL! I'm using it for the 3rd day and while im typing this post...all in LiveCD!

xubunutu/linux all the way!

Semper Fi!
