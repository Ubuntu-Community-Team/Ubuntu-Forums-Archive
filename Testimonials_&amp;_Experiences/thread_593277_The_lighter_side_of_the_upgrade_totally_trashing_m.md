### Post by ctothej on 2007-10-27
The bad...
I also had an unsuccessful upgrade from Feisty. It might have been because I started Feisty with Kubuntu (KDE) and switched to Gnome afterwards by installing ubuntu-desktop and GDM (following ubuntu wiki howto). In any case, after the upgrade, I had no sound and things were a bit slow. This all led me to back up my home dir and reinstall Ubuntu Gutsy from CD. 

The good...
Even though I had to re-install, it was worth it. Gutsy is awesome; a great leap forward for both Ubuntu and Linux. So many things just work. I have it installed on both my laptop and desktop. Package management is incredible; almost anything I need is right there in synaptic. Having the latest Compiz integrated and working well/stable is just an awesome feat. And it is not just eye-candy, as I find much of it increasing my productivity. I could go on for a while about all the great things in Ubuntu.

A word to those who are new to Ubuntu (and Linux in general)...
Yes, Ubuntu has a learning curve, but the experience is well worth the time spent learning. I've been an Ubuntu user since Edgy, and i am still learning new things every day, but have become quite proficient at installing/customizing/configuring due to the vast amount of help available. When you install Ubuntu, you become a part of a [community]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community") supporting a [philosophy]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/philosophy").  There is no support better than the forums and the irc channel. Best of all, any bugs relating to your problems get direct notice through [launchpad]("https://launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bugs"). Your problem submissions get handled by the developers. Where else do you have such impact on the direction and priority of the software you use? 
Additionally, you cannot think of Ubuntu/Linux as simply a Windows replacement. Yes, there are the standard equivalents to being productive with E-mail and Word Processing and the such, and those ideas are the same no matter what system you choose. But, Ubuntu/Linux takes a different approach to how work is done on the computer and how computing is done in general.

Finally, a big THANKS to the Ubuntu dev team for such an awesome Linux distribution! Take pride in the accomplishment that is Gutsy, keep up the awesome work, and know that it is much appreciated.
