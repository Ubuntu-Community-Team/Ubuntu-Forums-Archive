### Post by Angeliq on 2008-05-04
When I got Ubuntu I was thrilled,being able to experience a new kind of system,I was amazed by the multiple desktop system,the synaptic app manager,no internet explorer anywhere and the general practical improvements of ubuntu compared to other OS,as well as the idea of a free community driven operating system.

It's been a few months since I had Ubuntu and I really did enjoy it,however,the lack of drivers,command line necessity and a number of small problems that pile up make it somewhat of a no go for my family but also myself.

I installed it first to try it my own PC,then onto the PC at my sis's and dad's office,hoping they would be as happy as I was but the opposite was the case,from the first day they wanted me to turn it back to Win XP and even though I forced them to use it for more than 2 months since I wanted them to try it instead of buying XP and going with the crowd,I was conviced they could do all the office stuf on it since there's open office and all,but they insisted I install XP.The reasons were scanner,usb and printer drivers,lack of native support for media such as mp3,mpeg,avi...and many other small things that ultimately slowed down her work...
My father is not a patient man and doesn't like when things don't work as he's used to,he just got used to XP and there is no way in hell he's learning how to use something that is more complex than clicking on a button with text even if it is better.

I did find open source replacements for most of the programs I needed and I was willing to learn and I did learn a lot while using ubuntu.I learned that no matter how evil and disgustingly bad something is,if it is backed by ridiculous amounts of money,people will favor it before free quality stuff such as Ubuntu.My 2 main reasons for not continuing to use Ubuntu:

- I couldn't find a driver for my wizard pen genius that worked (did get it to work but couldn't calibrate pen sensitivity,lots of bugs,etc...) which I NEED to work ( I am a graphic designer and digital artist [http://angeliq.deviantart.com]("http://angeliq.deviantart.com") )
- I couldn't find open source programs that can open and edit commercial graphic software file formats.
- Inability to find USB drivers for my ADSL modem (to enable pure bridge mode which is necessary for downloading torrents with my ISP)
- Missing USB 2.0 drivers for flash memory sticks and cards (though I'm convinced there are drivers and that I just didn't search enough or did something wrong)...

I believe that Ubuntu has the potential but lacks the funds to become a real threat to Microsoft for the time being...I might install it again some day,until then I am forced to use Windows like it or not.....

:KS Jovana
