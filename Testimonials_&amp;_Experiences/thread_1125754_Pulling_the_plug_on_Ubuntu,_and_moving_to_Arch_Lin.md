### Post by masterkoppa on 2009-04-14
After adding a countless amount of PAA's and third party repos in an endless attempt to mantain a stable and up-to-date system I've finally decided to move from Ubuntu into Arch Linux.

The main reason being the rolling release methodology they use which I find very usefull, allowing the user to have the latest bleeding edge software. The other reason that made me switch is that while reading through these forums and troubleshooting I've opened my eyes to endless amounts of customizations and optimizations available to linux. Ubuntu tries to hide these features from the user with the ease of use in mind. I find that the Ubuntu devs have done a very good job on this regard.

But after using Ubuntu for so long and seeing the possibilities on a Linux System I think that its my time to move to a more technically complicated distro with performance as a huge priority.

So before I leave Ubuntu I want to give my experience on Ubuntu.

Ubuntu was the first drastic change I did in regards to technology. I did this while still in High School and in all honesty it was a huge mess. I could easily say that I spent more time troubleshooting the computer than the amount of time I spent using it. That was a couple of years ago and it was mostly related to wireless drivers and video drivers. You could probably add to all that some very newbie errors.

After taking a break of around 7 months I returned to Ubuntu but started using Kubuntu. After working out the KDE 4 and its crashes I finally started enjoying what Ubuntu and Linux was capable. But I've always struggled with one problem lack of updates in certain packages. Call it either bad luck or ignorance but around 80% of all the software I looked at, it was outdated, some even outdated by some years, with bug reports going gallore complaining about the lack of updates in the repositories. Some examples of this is Eclipse IDE and the recently update Battle for Wesnoth(Great Game BTW, the OSS Game out there). Whatever the reason for the lack of updates, I has to go to third party PAA. This sometimes brought incompatibilities because libraries were updated and software from the official repos broke. This once even made my system unusable.

So the rest of my experience has been working out all the third party updates and fixing dependencies problems. So im dumping Ubuntu as a whole and making a drastic change to Arch.

Thanks to everyone in this community that has helped me out and I hope the Ubuntu community keeps groing until it conquers the world making the fix to issue #1 in launchpad.

PS. Sorry for my bad english, not my primary language.
