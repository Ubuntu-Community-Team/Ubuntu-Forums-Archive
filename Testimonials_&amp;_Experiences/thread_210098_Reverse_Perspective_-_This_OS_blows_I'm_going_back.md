### Post by richbarna on 2006-07-06
Noob to windows.

I just decided to have a go at installing this new-fangled-windowsXp thingy.
I'm 1337 on Ubuntu, but I can't get the hang of THIS OS Lol.

It took about an hour to install, and guess what!? Where did ubuntu go? It's got a menu a bit like GRUB but it didn't find my other OS ! More forum searching to fix that later.

Well it booted, and the lay out is really bad (only one tool bar !!). I decided to go for an update but couldn't find Synaptic, or any package manager for that matter, so I decided to do an "apt-get". 
No terminal, it's got a different one called "command prompt", so I opened that and did "sudo apt-get update" and got an error.

So I went looking for the sources.list, did a search and everything, no sources list?? that's strange, back to the forums. I got told that there aren't repos, nor synaptic, nor apt-get (What kind of stupid system is this?!)

Apparently when you want stuff, you have to search the whole internet until you find a program that you want, Oh yeah windows has got other versions called 98 and Me, so I had to make sure that i got the right ones.

Here's the shocker !! I was expected to PAY for the software!! no seriously, I kid you not.

I wanted to write a letter to complain so I went to start/all programs/.....
What do they mean? ALL programs! There aren't any! There isn't even an office suite. I went looking for it on the internet and I nearly fainted, they want 500 bucks for it !!!!

I'm sorry to criticize, I know the developers work hard and there's a nice community, but this OS is NOT "out of the box" OR "ready for the desktop".

There is some box that keeps popping up telling me that it's found something, anybody know what a virrus is? I had a better look and it tells me that I have got a trojan or something. What is that? Maybe it's the only program on this OS that comes preinstalled. Sorry for the stupid questions, I'm a n00b to all this.

It's only got one media player, (funnily enough it's called "media player" LOL)
I tried my divx, avi and xvid stuff but it just won't play anything, god I'm sick of this OS. When I post for help on the forums, it's "gui" this, and "click" that, I know that they are all experts but I'm a n00b, could some give me anything "command line" until I get used to the Gui click'n'point?!

I tried to plug some USB stuff in, but it won't run my dongle or multicard reader etc because it doesn't recognize USB 2, evidently i need something called a service pack.
Found one!! yay. Damn 100Mb just to get USB 2 ? I'll check it out.
Ok apparently on the microsoft website, there are lots of add-ons that they give away for free. They are called patches (my aunty had a dog called patches LOL).

So I let the website tell me how many patches I needed, I nearly passed out!!!
267Mb of updates for things called "exploits" and "vulnerabilities", cool !! that must be something new that we don't get as standard with Linux.

Anyway..... oh hold on, another message box, "RPC has shut down unexpectedly, system will reboot in 30 seconds".

What a load of Rubbish !! I'm going back to Ubuntu !

### Post by bonzodog on 2006-07-13
I actually have to say that attemting to install and use/configure windows was possibly the most frustrating experience of my life. I had used windows before in the late 90's, I had win98se, but I kept on GPF'ing it - normally an install lasted anywhere from a week to less than an hour before needing to be done again - I actually destroyed 2 hard disks from over installing windows. It seems windows didn't like me hand editing it's system.ini files.  Also, I learnt that you should never just delete shell32.dll. Windows WILL refuse to boot if you do that.

Not so long back, I got myself a new computer with the central objective of using as a slackware box, thus I bought an AMD/Nvidia all-round set-up. Sure enough, on installation of slackware, it Just Worked. 

Then, for fun, i decided to try WinXp.
BIG Mistake.
It took nearly 3 hours and 4 attempts to get a clean install- It very rarely recognised or even wanted to communicate with any of my hardware. 
When I did get a successful install, the GUI/Windowing environment wouldn't configure correctly. Being of almost pure linux/unix upbringing, I went looking for a config file to manually correct. I really couldn't find one, and it turns out that it was because I had (GASP!) an NVidia set-up. 
Yup, it wanted mobo drivers....*sigh*. 
An hour later, I had them installed...even that wasn't easy. I have NEVER in my life seen such a convoluted install process. 
honestly. 
There is no straight forward way of finding drivers for any hardware for windows, no central software sites, and then having to click about 20 times through various questions....what??? why???.

Just install the goddamn thing. 

Don't ask questions.

I did in the end, with some help from a few friends, and my brother, who seems to know something about windows admin, get a stable install with a reliable anti-virus. (AVG) The first thing to go was windows internet explorer (my brother had to stress to me how important explorer.exe was - I seriously thought it was JUST the web browser!)
I also removed Outlook express - it was an install of WinXp SP2 Pro. 
But my video card proved to be one massive uphill struggle, and it got to me very quickly. 

I decided to clean wipe the Hard disk and made it all ReiserFS, and went back to sole Linux, where at least I knew what i was doing, and my hardware Just Worked.
