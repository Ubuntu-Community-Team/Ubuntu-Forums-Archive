### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-02-15
I wrote a lengthy reply earlier, but I lost it when the Ubuntu forums went into maintenance.

Basically, Ubuntu supports as many wireless network cards as it possibly can. Unfortunately, every card works differently enough to require different drivers, and many manufacturers won't provide Linux drivers or enough information to Linux developers for them to create Linux drivers. We're reverse-engineering these cards as quickly as we can.

My motherboard's wireless card works out-of-the-box on Ubuntu 8.10, and new users are encouraged to seek out hardware that has official or reverse-engineered drivers for Linux. I'm assuming you have an Atheros-based wireless card, and that this is why you were told to use the "madwifi" driver. Atheros is in the process of changing its driver from a closed-source to an open-source, and it looks like you may have been caught in the middle and having to compile bleeding-edge drivers. This is not a good situation and hopefully the transition will be finished quickly.

The ideal solution would be for the wireless card manufacturers to sit down together and come up with an open specification for communications between a computer and its wireless card. That way, as soon as the specification is implemented by the manufacturers and by Ubuntu, all future cards will work with Ubuntu out-of-the-box. Just like how all USB flash drives and hard disks work with all operating systems "plug 'n' play".

BTW, Windows is an unpopular operating system that comes preinstalled on most computers.
