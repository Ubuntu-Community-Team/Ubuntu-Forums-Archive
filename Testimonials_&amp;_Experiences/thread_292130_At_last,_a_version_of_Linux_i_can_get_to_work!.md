### Post by Hjaltlander on 2006-11-03
Hi all,

Just thought I would stick my 2 pence worth in here.

I have been mucking about with PC's since the very early ninties, and I have never liked Windows in any of it's versions, so I have tried various Linux distros over the years but could never get one to work properly, for various reasons. Mainly me not having the time or inclination to look at them properly.

Last week, I thought to myself, well let's try again, so I found the Ubuntu website, and liked the fact there apeared to be an active forum, which could help any of my installation woes, and set to downloading the .iso file, which I burned in Windows.

After repartitioning my drive using Partition Magic, and backing up my personal files, I set to installing Ubuntu for the first time. It went without a hitch, but I still wanted to know I could do the things I did on windows on Ubuntu.

I know it's not much, but every small step for me is a milestone...

I downloaded and got Opera working

The same with ktorrent, I tried Auzerus, but it kept throwing errors up

Amarok rocks! It works great, and even detected and updates my 512mb iPod Shuffle

Instant messaging with Gaim is instant, pity I can't get my webcam to work though?

Synaptic is easy to use, I can install an app, if I am not happy, off it comes again.

So, after 1 week, I am farther ahead with Linux, than I have ever been before, I for one will be staying here, and if i can get a few more things to work, well, that Windows partition will be no more!

Thanks for listening, Hjaltlander

### Post by Bragador on 2006-11-03
Welcome to freedom !

As for UchihanoKonoha, you should try to install it on a part of your hard drive. That's what I did on my laptop and I was very afraid to break something since I use it for university. Partitioning my hard drive was what got me nervous, especially since there was so much partitions on my IBM thinkpad. I thought I'd only have one for windows but I actually had 2 or 3 plus a hidden partition.

So I can say you need to keep your C: and that's it. You can keep your hidden partition if you want to if there is one. It's for the factory guys if your laptop needs to be fixed. Scrap your D: and the rest and give it to Linux.

Take the plunge !

ps: When I did all that I had no prior knowledge of partitioning. I used Google and wikipedia. I may sound "hardcore" now but it sounds more complicated than it is in reality.
