### Post by DennoCoil on 2010-12-24
*/Rant/*

I have been working with Ubuntu since Monday and I have  to say, Ubuntu is one of the most laggy and disappointing OS's I can  remember in a long time.  The lag might be a part of it being on a USB  key since Ubuntu doesn't use a file system that would help my empty  solid state drive from being inefficient, and my 2 other hard drives  being almost full.

I have been trying to get Wine to play Team  Fortress 2 for almost a week, trying to create a Idle farm with several  spare accounts, with no success and using every available option and  tweak to try to make it playable.  It wasn't just that though, but a lot  of things either don't make much sense or seems a little too  complicated to try to use.  It would be very helpful to have a tooltips  option on at all times that can tell what vaguely defined box, icon,  label means in this thing.

At first it was easy, the CD worked as  expected, loading everything, even if Ubuntu didn't ran in total memory  like it should.  It was a pain to find out I had to load TF2 and Wine  in a directory it could recognize, ie in a Linux space.  So after trying  to install it through Windows with Wubi and trying to make it be  friendly, it was slow and still had little space.

Then came  installing it to a available drive.  I can't access Windows 7 now  because of a BIOS error saying "VAORX IS MISSING" or "VAORX IS  COMPRESSED".  I've looked up Google.  There is no part of the internet  that knows what the **** that means.

The use of the terminal was  something I have not grown accustomed to when DOS and the Commodore 64  went out of style.  The words of barely definable tech labels\initials  and the foreign command line prompts will not be something that people  would like to do in this day and age of click box menus.  Why are we  still using it again?  Or anybody for that matter to download and  install programs?  Shouldn't that be merged into the Software manager or  have its own little RUN box next to the labels on top?

I have  never had Firefox or the file system be this laggy before.  It could be  the USB, but it's a brand new 16GB key that was rated as having enough  speed and space to run Xbox 360 games off of.  Videos would sometimes  stutter when either installing a program or opening up a new window.   Most of the time I had to refresh the window just to make the video show  up again.  Flash is picky and crashy anyway.

Wine  itself was okay, if not for the lot of documentation related to it, but  that doesn't make it any more workable.  I've only tried 2 games, Super  Meat Boy being the other, and both of them crashed on startup.  It seems  like a lot of the options needed don't come with Wine itself and needed  to downloaded separately, like most other programs on Linux, as well  the main focus seems to be making Microsoft Office workable...  Don't  you people have Open Office for that crap?  Or one of the dozens of  writing apps available?

Linux will always be a curiosity to me,  something that can stand up against the 2 giants of the computer world,  but it will always just be something to **** around in until it becomes  more stable, user friendly, and accepted among the world.  The amount of  Linuxes is too much, there shouldn't be over 300 variations of the same  OS.  Even Windows had 6 different kinds for W7, unless you count XP and  Vista, and they were easily definable.  And the TYPE of environment it  has is very vague as to WHY I should be using either KDE or GNOME.  A  lot of you linux programmers either need to sort out your differences,  squish the smaller and more useless distributions, and focus on a more  friendly and stable system with more focus in Wine development to even  BE close to something Windows users might abandon for.

Right now,  it's just something for many nerds and businesses to use when the other  2 big OS's don't meet there needs.  I may come back to Linux in general  in the future, but until then I'm gonna go back to Windows 7 and also  try my hand at a Apple OS sometime in the future.

*/End of Rant/*

Sorry for the long list.  Goodbye.
