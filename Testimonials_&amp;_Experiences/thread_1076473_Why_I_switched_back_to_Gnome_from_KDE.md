### Post by yonkman on 2009-02-21
Ever since I started using linux I have always used the KDE desktop.  Initially it was because of the ease of transition, but then it became comfortable.  However, after 6 years I have switched back to GNOME.

I just did not like the default 4.1 gadgetry and found it less than optimal.  Now, I know one can configure it the way they want, but I decided if I had to do that then I might give Gnome a try again.

Well, I first tried XFCE which I really like, but moved to Gnome for a little richer experience.

I am happy and don't intend on switching back, but here are a few things I miss.

1) dolphin It just seemed easier to use
2) quanta (and other development tools)   Much better than bluefish
(However, I now do all development in eclipse so that point is mute)

That really is it.   

So, anyone else switch back from KDE like me and, if so, why?
