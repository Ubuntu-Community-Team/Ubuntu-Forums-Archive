### Post by 8jwong14 on 2011-05-03
When I compare Ubuntu, Windows 7, and Mac OSX, I noticed one major thing.  Mac OSX and Window s7 look far more professional than Ubuntu.  One thing I don't like about Ubuntu is the appearance of a fresh install.  Windows 7 and Mac OSX look pretty yet professional while Ubuntu's purple and orange colors don't look that great compared to those other OSes.  IF Ubuntus wants to gain more popularity and market, they should make it look mroe on par to Windows 7 and Mac OSx in terms of the professional looks while still beign pretty.  Yes I know there are themes.  But when a person is introduced to Ubuntu, they don't see all those themes that are available.  They just see the fresh desktop that comes default.  Sure there are some really nice themes but what Ubuntu needs is a new default theme.  One that looks professional yet pretty something like Elementary but the scheme reminds me of Mac OSX.  I've asked many friends and they all say Ubuntu even 11.04 Unity looks the least professional and pretty overall.  Unity was unique but it still didn't look as good as Mac OSX and Windows 7.  

Sure some Ubuntu users don't care because they theme Ubuntu after they install it but for getting new users and more popularity, it also has to be on par in terms of looks.  We can't simply appeal to those already using Ubuntu but we should also appeal to others to gain more popularity and market share.  

The new look should be something that is on par with the more professional looks of MAc OSX and Windows 7 yet still maintain the Ubuntu identity as being different than the other 2 OSes.  To get more users, we can't simply show all our features despite how good they are getting.  We must also make good first impressions with the appearance.
This is solely my opinion.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-05-03
One must assume your talking about the default desktop. It's been my  experience (I can't speak to unity - 11.04, as it won't be installed on  any of our systems) that few people I know run Ubuntu in default. Most  of the users I'm familiar with modify the desktop as an expression of  their own creativity and to increase productivity.

IMHO it's one  of the true benefits of the OS. Not to mention, it helps significantly  with the learning curve. New users who invest time learning the OS, are  more likely to remain in the environment. I believe experimenting on the  desktop helps new users become comfortable with the OS and improves  their confidence level.

Rather than worry about the default  desktop (theme, icons, etc.), perhaps you might consider investigating  the amount of bloat that's often associated with all that glitz and  glitter. Using unnecessary resources and slowing the system only for the  purpose of appearance. Many Ubuntu/Linux users realize the difference  between speed/performance versus non-essential (what I refer to as)  garbage.

Thanks for sharing your feedback and good luck.

Just my $0.02
