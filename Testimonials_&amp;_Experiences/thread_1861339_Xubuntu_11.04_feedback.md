### Post by Isaacgallegos on 2011-10-15
***This is a review, not a help thread. ***:)


Hello Linuxers, 

Here's my first week's experience with Xubuntu 11.04!

*** How I ended up with Xubuntu 11.04***
I was using Kubuntu for a while and thought, what the heck is Xubuntu? Tried it on a usb and immediately saw this was a fast and_ normal looking operating system_. I loved it! So, basically content with Kubuntu 11.04, after I had tried Xubuntu for about 5 minutes I had to switch.


*** Why love Xubuntu!***
I have a small netbook and Xubuntu lets me pile on tons of apps, streaming feeds and checking email every five minutes. This is while I stream videos online and install stuff from the repos. This thing packs a punch unlike any other distro I've used. **This is great because I want to rely my web browser less, because it's very distracting and easy to waste time!** So I rely on lots of apps getting stuff for me instead. 

Basically, Xubuntu looks like it was made by someone that had asked me, what do you really want in an OS? 

* Favorite apps so far, which can be used in any varient of Ubuntu: *

1. RSS feeds, **Liferea**. 

2. Email, **Thunderbird** (It took like half a second to setup and sycs perfectly with Gmail).

3. Note taking and school project organizer, **Keepnote** (probably the most important program on my computer). 

4. Journal entry organizer, **Redbook** (This is where all my notes from anywhere first go before being put into a calendar or a project file. It's a great starting point for all information because you enter data based on when you heard it. It also uses tags. Where Keepnote is focused on class notes, reading notes, and assignments,_ redbook is for everything else_).

5.  Task organizer,** Getting Things GNOME! **(I almost removed it because I thought it would be redundant between thunderbird, Keepnote, and Redbook, but it's not. It's very good at what it does and better at task management than the others.)

6. Calendar, **Google Calendar on it's own window in firefox with Stylish mod** on virtual desktop #3. (Google Calendar is really the best calendar system ever made. It integrates with so much and I dump so much info into it. I also like to know that if I put something on my calendar it will stay put 3 years from now. The stylish mod takes google calendar and removes the crap at the top so it fills the whole page.) 

There are more apps I could mention, but I thought those were the most important. 




*** Here are the problems I plan on fixing later.***

1. On the file manager, any shortcuts you put on the side bar don't stay. They vanish. 

2. "Debconf on Milo" window pops up during installation of a program. It's a non issue but I might look up the problem later.  (milo is my computer's name)

3. power-button on my computer won't respond. This would be a deal breaker if xubuntu wasn't so dang good! It makes up for this problem x30 over in its speed. 

4. info "mouse over" boxes block actual button sometimes. Like when you mouse over a button, an info box might appear, to explain what the box is, and it will keep you from clicking the actual button. Kind of a weird problem. 

5. Ctrl+Alt+D applies to all desktops instead of the current one. Easy fix, but wish it wasn't the default. 

6. keyboard commands that execute scripts don't work. Go to Settings Manager> Keyboard>Appliation Shortcuts, nothing in this window works. Nothing! I tried also adding a hotkey to disable my touchpad, which works in the command line, but not in this window. Not sure what's up. 

7 system seems to be confused if my battery is full or not. - worried this could do some damage.  Example. While it's charging, it will periodically say "BATTERY FULL!!" then a few seconds layer it will change its mind and say "BATTERY 60%... " It charges normally so I'm guessing it's the notifier that's buggy. This has never happened on any other distro I've tired and I've gone through quite a few. 

8. No clear way to disable the "roll-up" feature when you scroll over a title bar. Keep doing it by accident. Drives me nuts. Roll-up actually could be completely removed and I'd be happy.

9. Bluetooth not supported by default.

10. Audio controls don't have hardware switching as an option. 

[I][B]Conclusion:
[/B][/I]There were a lot of compiz effects I really used the heck out of in Ubuntu. I see that some people can get desktop effects enabled in Xubuntu, but I don't think I'll do that. I feel that the purpose of Xubuntu is to give more spotlight to the applications, rather than the OS. 

Xubuntu feels like this invisible thing in the background and I tend to forget what OS I'm on after hours of work. I think that's how it should be. When I used Windows XP I didn't care that I was on, while I was working on an assignment. There were no fancy desktop effects or themes to make me feel good about my OS. Though, I must admit, I felt different when XP was new. 

Xubuntu reminds me of this. It's not the OS that makes you feel good about it. It's the OS that stays out of the way while being easy enough for general users like me.
