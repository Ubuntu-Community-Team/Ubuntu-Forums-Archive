With Natty I had issues when I tried to use an alternate display (login) manager like lxdm or slim - leave gdm in place for now.
If you like a lightweight classic desktop, lxde or xfce4 are worth trying.

BTW I don't know why anyone says unity or gnome-shell are 'user friendly' at this point. They require hardware acceleration for acceptable performance and most of all, they lack easy customizability.
Big freaking icons on the launcher!
No organizable 'drawers' of pinned apps - one long list.

No thanks, I'll stick with the thinnest panels and set them to autohide if I want them 'out of the way'. Also, I can't imagine trying to teach a computer or smart phone newbie with physical challenges how to use anything with hiding widgets. This is progress?
