### Post by TwiceOver on 2008-07-02
So it has been a month since my buddy went off on me about using Microsoft products when I proclaimed IE to be a better browser than FF.  He is hardcore against Microsoft calling them evil and generally scoffing at the idea of using their products.  I on the other hand am indifferent.  I don't go around saying "Winbloze", "M$", "Steve (blow) Jobs"...  I mean c'mon, right time, right place, right product, what else is Microsoft supposed to do?  But I decided to take his rant to heart.

I had distro shopped a few times, usually ending up with Ubuntu and then getting disappointed by something simple.  The last three releases had problems with backlighting on my laptop (primary machine).  I still lurked the forum from time to time and right about the time my buddy was ripping me a new one I found a "How-To" post on manually setting screen brightness levels for laptops.  Awesome...  Here I go again.

I installed 8.04 leaving a partition for Vista Basic (Even though I am "indifferent" as stated above... Vista is BY FAR the worst POS software just beating out iTunes for the top slot).  The Hardy install went perfect as it always had with my previous Ubuntu installs.

Something felt different this time.  Kinda like quitting smoking, I think this time I was "ready".  I worked over the screen brightness How-To with success and went out to get my standard apps that I like.  With help from RedDead over at the Ubuntu 1501 blog I was able to get my wireless working (expected this) and Compiz worked perfect right out of the box.  Spent some time doing some desktop customizations, Cairo-Dock works great, Gnome Color Chooser is good stuff, screenlets kick ***...  I'm happy.  

Today (and my reason for posting this) I tested out the suspend feature of my laptop.  I don't know why, just hadn't used it till today.  I pressed the fn+esc (hibernate) and the laptop shutdown.  Hrm...  On reboot go through bios -> Grub -> Enter password and system resumed!  Cool, but that was too long.  I shut my laptop lid...  No suspend.  DAMN!!!  So I Googled around, searched the forums, there are so many people with suspend problems.  I read through a couple How-To articles on fixing known issues and I thought this was going to be that "simple" thing that kicked me back into Windows mode.  Depressed I started roaming around the system panel.  Power options.  "On Lid Close: Blank Screen"?  Could it be this simple?  Had I just read an hour's worth of articles just to be tricked by a drop down box?  

The simple answer... Yes, I had been duped.  My previous Linux experience had pointed me into the hardest direction.  I changed the setting to "Suspend" and cautiously closed my lid.  After a few seconds my power light went to the "Slow Blink".  A good sign, I gave it a few seconds and opened it back up.  Good to go!

Now I can honestly say that everything "Just Works".  And to defend my IE statement above, It still is the best but ONLY because many websites are formatted for IE.  FF + Adblock usually gets me around the formatting problems.  Yes I know "W3C Standards" blah blah. Some day... Some day.  

Sorry for the long winded post, I hope it reads as well as it felt to type out.

EDIT: Ubuntu 8.04 on Dell Inspiron 1501
