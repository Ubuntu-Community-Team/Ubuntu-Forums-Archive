### Post by CompactDestruction on 2007-06-25
I've been on Ubuntu for three days and reinstalled it as many times due to various niggling problems... but now I am kicking back, listening to music stored on my iPod through Banshee, with a rock solid wireless connection, Gaim and Evolution ticking in the background, feeling really good about Ubuntu and Linux apps in general.

Ubuntu is running very stably for me despite my 'hax' to get my wireless working and also despite having to use fglrx drivers as the 'ati' ones don't work for me... it just works!

I won't bother going into any nitpicking details about what Ubuntu has over Windows or Windows has over Ubuntu. They are very different operating systems with different strengths that will appeal to different people. The apps I find usually have different strengths and weaknesses compared to Windows counterparts. For example Banshee can't buy or play iTunes Music Store music, and can't play gapless, but what it can do is store my music lossless then sync to my iPod in a lossy format allowing me to get all my music on it without needing two copies of every file on disk... Not to mention Banshee's resource usage is so good compared to '30-seconds-to-load' iTunes.

But apt-get/aptitude/add-remove programs... bloody hell I can't believe Windows doesn't have something quite like it. I just installed Audacity in thirty seconds flat... in Windows I'd be lucky to have started the download by now.

And not having to worry about viruses/malware/file-fragmentation, the maintenance that takes so much time in Windows... oh look I started nitpicking between the OSs :D

Oh and I especially like the fact that in Linux, 64-bit users are not treated as second-class-citizens.

Thank you all ;)
