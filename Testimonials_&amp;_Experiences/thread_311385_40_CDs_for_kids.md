### Post by macdo on 2006-12-02
I was running a geek camp at the beginning of November - and guess what? Canonical sent me 60 CDs (and some complimentary stickers :-)), and I was able to distribute 40 of them... I know that quite a few (not all, unfortunately) have been used.

Funnily enough, what convinced most kids that K/Ubuntu was better than Windows was the wallpaper - I've got it set to change every 10 minutes or so in KDE, and that was a real wow factor :-)

I ran a workshop on FOSS, with 14 year olds, and I was quite surprised by some of the questions they came out with, like: "How do FOSS companies make money / how do programmers who program GPL'd code make money? and so on. (no they weren't focused on money, that's just the easiest question to remember :-))


Thank you Ubuntu!

### Post by AusIV4 on 2006-12-03
To elaborate on my answer to the poll question, I think kids should be introduced to multiple operating systems as soon as they're exposed to computers.

When I started on computers I was 2, and I was exposed to Dos and Windows 3.1 shortly afterwards, but I (regrettably) was stuck on Windows for about 15 years. Last year I started as a CS major, and I was completely unprepared for the work I'd have to do with Linux - which was incredibly minimal. I think exposure - even very little exposure - to other operating systems would be beneficial to any kid that is going to be around computers.
