### Post by Shobuz99 on 2008-09-06
I installed Ubuntu 8.04 (Hardy) about 5 months ago. GRUB setup
to dual boot on separate drives with XP.
Tried to learn how to use it and wean myself from Windows.
I'm still learning and am still trying to find some programs
that operate the way that my Windows stuff did..(i.e, Dreamweaver MX 2004)

But I am VERY glad that I found Ubuntu. Ubuntu saved my sanity.

Last week, Windows required an SP3 update. I wasn't wise enough to ignore it. I suffered the consequences. 
After a 3 hour SP3 update/install, the program told me "Access Denied, click OK". I did, and then it began to uninstall itself. 
Next, it crashed in the middle of restoring SP2 files.
It sat there for 5 minutes. I restarted...I felt there was no choice.

Now I have no more XP drive. It's just a drive with all my files (20GB)
of websites and work data files. XP will not finish booting. It says "autochk failed, skipping autochk" and restarts, looping forever.

I know this is an Ubuntu forum; but I'm trying to relay here that if it wasn't for Ubuntu, I'd be completely screwed. 
I can at least access my XP drive from Ubuntu (as local) and get to files
that I need to move over to my Ubuntu drive (80GB)

It just goes to show you that Windows will be the black hole of OS's
and Ubuntu (Linux) will be the future for anyone who wants to pull the MS needle out of their arm. I'm working on it.

For all you MS bigots: Microsoft took me where I didn't want to go....
Where have You ended up today? 
Thank you Ubuntu!
Rick (shobuz99)
