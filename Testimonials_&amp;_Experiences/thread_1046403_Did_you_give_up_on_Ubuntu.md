### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-01-23
While the poll numbers are interesting, polls on these Forums are notoriously inaccurate representations of the userbase. 

People who vote in polls have something specific to say about the issue(s) in the polls. Those who were having satisfactory experiences possibly (probably?) didn't think about or feel the need to cast a vote about their experience. Those who had a negative experience seem more likely to vote because they believe it will help things change, more quickly, to their benefit.

For example, somebody makes a poll "Are Emacs and Vim too complex?". The possible answers are "Yes. I like nano/gedit, who needs C-x M-c M-butterfly?" or "No. Once learned they offer more than the simple editors". Those who enjoy using Vim/Emacs see the poll and decide that they don't need to get involved in another editor war, while those who have been burned by either decide its time to commiserate about their woes. The poll gets skewed toward simple editors like nano/gedit.

Just my two cents. Cheers!
