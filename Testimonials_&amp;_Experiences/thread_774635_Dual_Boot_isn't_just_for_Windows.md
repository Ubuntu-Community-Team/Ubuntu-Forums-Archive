### Post by azimuth on 2008-04-29
Alternate Title >> Dual Boot isn't just for Windows

Judging by how busy the Ubuntu mirrors were last week, there has to be a lot of folks using or at least have tried Hardy Heron. The distribution has been properly maligned in this forum. The number of failures that have been chronicled does not seem large compared to how many have to have tried Hardy. Many who haven't actually installed it have added to the FUD. This type of behavior has to be detrimental to folks wanting to try Hardy. Some want to wait for a month or an incremental 8.4.01 version. I say "Why wait". "Why deny yourself the chance to use a new version of your favorite OS". Use the same advice that we and probably even you have given to die hard Window users, Dual boot.

My personal experience with 8.04 has been stellar. That does not mean that yours will be, but you'll never know till you try it. Gutsy was working satisfactorily on my Inspiron 1501 Dell laptop, but it wasn't easy getting it there. I would have spent several hours alternately crying and cursing while I was trying to rebuild a working system if I had replaced it with something that didn't work. So, I dual booted. I needed to do away with the windows on it anyway, You know, the one that is never used and will never be used again.

Hardy is working out very well, much better out the gate than Gutsy, and after I have become completely comfortable and confident with it, I will recover the disk space that Gutsy is now using. I am sure glad I didn't wait, I am loving all the new and almost invisible improvements. It would have been a shame to have missed out on an early adoption.
