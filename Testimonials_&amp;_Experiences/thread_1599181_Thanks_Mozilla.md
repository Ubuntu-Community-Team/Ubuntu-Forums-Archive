Yes.. I had tried Firefox many , many  times on the (other) OS , but, to no avail. There would be the same types of hangs as with IE. I do note, however, that Firefox is the default browser for Ubuntu. Some of my most important pages that used to lock up with a MS OS are now very fluid with no down time. Especially  home pages, home ISP pages and e-mail servers. It was never ending , no matter how validated and authenticated I was or how fast my PC was .. it was always the same kind of hangs (personally) , but , this only scratches the surface of those testimonials of the other PCs that I had to service.

  And no matter what firewall, AV, Malware Guard, Spyware  Guard .. etc...it was just a never ending logjam of hangs and crashes.

  I also note that there is not an MS Internet Explorer for Ubuntu but that Internet Explorer can be run in Windows in a Virtual Box (which I have accomplished) and have commented on how well IE 6 works in the VBox.

 Also  I have worked on machines where IE has worked extremely well but for the most part  Firefox and IE did  not fare well with performance and reliability on an MS OS as Firefox does on the Ubuntu 10.4 OS.


  It is quite a relief to have a browser that does not have to have constant toolbars taking up precious space, resources and memory.

  I have also learned how to program network connections to add the DNSs for Comodo which will advise me if I am entering a site that may have active threat delivery of malware but for the most part I am doing my browsing with a lot less worry these days.
