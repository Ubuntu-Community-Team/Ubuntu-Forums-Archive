### Post by ztrange on 2009-04-14
I wanted to tell my experience about changing into kde with the jaunty beta release.

I first made a backup of everything I got. (Thats basic even if you're not going to destroy your partitions)
Then, I formatted my hard drive with new partitions using the ext4 file system and installed kubuntu jaunty beta

I got some trouble with configurating the sound and the volume wheel control but finally got everything working.

I still feel kde a little sluggish with my Intel 945 chip but I hope they'll be gone as uxa is finished.

Finally, I got some great tips form here: Its a very good resource for ubuntu linux.
[http://kubuntuguide.org/Jaunty](http://kubuntuguide.org/Jaunty)

I'm also still suffering from some system freezes but I think they have something to be with the sluggish performance of my graphics. I need to do some more research.

But mainly, I wanted to tell everybody that kde4.2 is great. It still looks a little unfinished in several places but everything is fully usable. The interface is a little different from gnome, thinks work sometimes very different but it is still great. And its beautiful too.

Anyway, if someone wants to know more, just let me know.

### Post by ztrange on 2009-04-15
I will tel you what I love about kde.

-The almost perfect integration of the desktop effects compared with gnome + compiz
-Plasmoids are great in functionality, design and usability.
-Control panel has almost everything I need.
-There is a lot of congruence in the places I need to find things like configuration options and functionality. More than in gnome.
-Trash can always empties whenever I tell it to do it.
-Plasmoid to control plugged in devices.
-Easy to configure Panel in height, width and behavior.
-Dolphin... What can I say?
-The alt+f thingie, I still dont know its name, altough I've read it somewhere.
-Netbeans looks awsome in kde compared to the way it looks in gnome.
-Kde recovers from screen resolution changes when plugging and unplugging external TV monitor without cropping my screen as gnome used to do. (That was also fault of compiz).

Need I keep going?

Well, in the other hand, not everything is great. It still lacks functionality and is not as configurable as gnome but sometimes i am thankful of that last one thing. I hope Kde 4.3 is ever greater, I can't wait to see it.

I also have to say that I had tried Kde 3.x and I thought it was ugly... But kde4 is relly great. You shuld give it a try. I also would never use Windows, thats a fact.
