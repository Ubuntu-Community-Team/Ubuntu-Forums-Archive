### Post by smartalecks on 2006-06-07
"The Great Transition"

As I like to call it, haha. The story: One day I became extremely fed up with Windows. Not just the OS, the company (Micro$oft) as well.

My friend had been telling me about Fedora, so I decided to give it a try. I downloaded all the live CD and had a go. It was pretty awsome. I took the CD out and rebooted, back on Windows. I went to Google to find out more about Fedora, when I stumbled across an Ubuntu Breezy review. Needless to say, it was glowing. So glowing, in fact, that I went to Ubuntu.com and downloaded the Live CD. Stuck it in the drive, and rebooted.

It was bliss! I would guess that even off the Breezy Live CD, (supposedly the slowest), it was 30% faster than Windows. Immediately I wrote the Install CD, made backups of all my files, and did a clean install. The transition to Ubuntu was seamless. Withing a day I was up and using Ubuntu as if I had all my life. It seemed so *natural*.

The install had taken care of all my hardware, including my connection to the internet. No extra configuration, except for my printer, which I was able to find clear instructions for [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=105703&highlight=Brother+MFC") on the forums. I was also able to find FREE replacements for all of my old Windows programs:  Bluefish for coding, Opera for browsing, GAIM, Rhythm Box, GnomeBaker, GFTP, Thunderbird, Azureus, XChat IRC, Open Office... All except Photoshop, because the GIMP, in my opinion, is just terrible. I've paid $32 for Pixel, which is as good as (maybe better) than Photoshop. Here I am, a month later, upgraded to Dapper, got XGL/Compiz runnning, and have had very few problems with Ubuntu thus. I've handed out nearly 100 Ubuntu CDs to friends and colleagues, with at least 95% of them trying the Live CD (a few dozen have switched, I think). I still use Windows a little bit, but for testing under VMWare Player. 

That's my review of a month with Ubuntu. The most remarkable thing to me so far definetly is that I have not had to run a single Spyware or Virus scan. I will continue to reccomend and use Ubuntu for the far forseeable future. 


Thanks Ubuntu! Keep up with the great work!,
-Alecks
