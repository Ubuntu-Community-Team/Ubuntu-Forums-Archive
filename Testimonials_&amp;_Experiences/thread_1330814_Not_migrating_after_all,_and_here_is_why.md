### Post by VulcanTourist on 2009-11-18
I'm not going to be migrating from Windows XP to Ubuntu after all, in spite of having strong incentives to do so (hint: LICENSE hardware restrictions: GPT drives, 4 terabyte volumes, and 8 gigabytes of RAM).  The reason is simple: I've discovered (what for me is) a show-stopping bug in Ubuntu that has persisted for years, in spite of people repeatedly calling attention to it and even filing official bug reports:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=495131](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=495131)
[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/gnome-mime-data/+bug/185165](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/gnome-mime-data/+bug/185165)

It seems that an insular us-versus-them mindset might be at work here, but whatever the motivation it has been deliberately ignored and sidelined for a very long time.  During this same period the Ubuntu community has been openly courting Windows users and encouraging migration.

Do you know what this bug would have done to me if I had been swayed by all that courting, damned the torpedoes and incautiously waded into a migration with both feet?  I'd be livid and screwed, since I have many hundreds of these .URL files and depend heavily upon them.  As it is, I'm just disgusted.

The fact that this bug has persisted for years and was denied "papercut" status tells me that Ubuntu is really not yet ready to accommodate people who need to MIGRATE rather than start all over from scratch.  I will NOT do the latter.  The fact that 90% of a solution for the bug exists is further galling to me, not comforting, since that solution has been ignored by core developers and left out of the distribution, left for people to ***find in a forum*** and try to figure out how to implement fully themselves, and probably well before they are experienced enough to know how to do it.

Now I'm off to learn whether the recent releases of OpenSUSE or Fedora have been more respectful of the needs of prospective immigrants.  It may be that later today I'll once more be putting more money in Microsoft's fat wallet, an unthinkable act that I had declared I would never do again.  Maybe Ubuntu will finally be ready to truly welcome immigrants in another 5 years when I've outgrown Windows 7.
