### Post by 0per4t0r on 2009-04-14
There are always good and bad things about every OS, a lot of them you can ignore, but some you can't.
Here's what I think, basically.

I think that Ubuntu is great, free, open software that is far superior to Windows. It has everything you need built-in, but you can still expand Ubuntu to everything you'd ever want. I love the compiz graphics, the lack of bloated software, and the list goes on. But, there's compatibility issues, crashes, and a little bit of bad to go along with the good.

[B]Here's the good stuff:
[/B]
1.It's Free!
It's free of charge, and it's opensource, so what more could you ask for?

2.Easy to use, pretty much.
 Installing packages is alright, and everything is pretty clear.

3.Extended Graphics
I can't get over the compiz graphics! I love the desktop cube and wobbly windows, but sometimes it crashes or gets laggy.

4.Customization
You can customize it any way you want, and everything that you can do on this OS is pretty much limitless.

5.It's not evil.
It's not made by some huge corporation centered on money, like apple or microsoft, it actually endorses free software, and it doesn't get as annoying as windows with constant errors, and security breaches.

6. Security and how it does things for you.
It's very secure, and it auto-defrags and updates easily.

7. Compatibility with devices
With ubuntu, it doesn't take forever to install USB drivers, mice, or printers, which makes it a lot easier.

**The Bad **:(
1. Crashes/Freezes/Slowness
It sometimes crashes, or freezes when you're on firefox, and it can get pretty slow at times

2.Incompatibility
Even with Wine, it's still not compatibile with a lot of programs and features because of the microsoft/apple-centered society. Most people don't bother making linux versions of programs because of the "small" market share, even though a lot of people use linux.

3.Somewhat hard to use at times
Even though it's "Linux for human beings," sometimes it depends what experience the human being has. To fix things, you often have to reinstall or use the terminal or other advanced settings, and if you don't know what you're doing, you can get in big trouble.

4. Slow internet/flash
you know what I mean here.



Basically, ubuntu is great, but lags just a little.
