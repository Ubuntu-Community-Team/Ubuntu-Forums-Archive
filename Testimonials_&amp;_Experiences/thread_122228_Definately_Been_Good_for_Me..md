### Post by Rev. Nathan on 2006-01-27
I'm a geek. But very conservative. I'm extremely social, am into fine arts and all of that stuff... but damn do I love nerdy nastiness. And I think Linux is really what I needed. Unfortunately, XP really dragged Windows away from DOS, and I never got to use it, I think, ever. But the prompt/terminal is fun. XP was just... boring. I couldn't wait to blast spyware or help someone out with a virus... boy I needed to get out of there.

So I widdled my choices down; SUSE (openSUSE to be exact) or Ubuntu. I was told to go Ubuntu back when it was version 4.10, but I never did. So now, I did it. And I'm really glad I chose Ubuntu over SUSE. Debian rocks. Plus, suprise! Everything worked. And this community is absolutely amazing. I dedicate myself to do at least two HOWTOs a week (and I've posted a HOWTO!), and am now *really* informed with Linux. I am always checking DistroWatch to see what's up... but I don't see me ever switching from Ubuntu. Plus, it's #1 on the charts.

Soon, I am getting a new HDD, and I'm putting Ubuntu on that (right now it only has a measly 10GB :( ). Then, in Summer I get a Mac (Don't want to, but need Final Cut Pro for college). Since I will have all my Adobe software on that, I will nix Windows, and be completely UNIX'd.

I was thinking about making the Mac the main computer in the house... but dammit, I'm having so much fun with Linux! I don't think I've ever been so attached to computing. And don't worry... my nerdiness is still very conservitive.
