### Post by Bucky Ball on 2009-08-24
Hi all,

I've been lurking about these forums for quite awhile now and 99% of the time I'm looking for help or looking to help somebody. I have only ever posted curios I have found along the way in the Community Cafe (very rarely) which I thought the community may find interesting or amusing in some way. This is the same in many ways but a bit closer to my heart I guess. Part HOWTO, part testimonial and part water cooler I figure it belongs here and mods feel free to move if not. Just thought I'd relate this story, always nice to talk to someone who understands!

My wife and I recently befriended a family, mum and five kids ranging from less than a year to thirteen years old, from Ethiopia, originally arriving as refugees about five years ago. They're happy enough but struggling along.

First time we went to visit I noticed their computer shoved in a corner collecting dust. "Does it work?" I enquired. A miserable 'no' the reply. So I booted it up and after some whirring, the old familiar MS blue screen and a message about the battery among other things. Put a new battery in and got a blue screen with a new message. So I asked if I could take the machine for a couple of days when I had some time and I'd fix it up for them (no charge). You know where I'm heading with this .... 

The kids had been using XP for a bunch of educational disks they had, so I figured they would want that back on there. So first thing, bung in an XP disk and see if I can retrieve that. Go to 'Repair' and at the cursor 'chkdisk' 'fixboot' 'fixmbr', reboot the machine and whappo, XP comes out of hibernation no problem. 

In goes the Gparted Live cd so I can see what's going on there and resize the XP partition (it is the full 80Gb disk). Now, this bit should have been simple but I started the shrink then realised I had set off two jobs, one shrink I didn't need to do so unthinkingly I hit 'cancel'. WRONG. Then I set the resize up the way I wanted and resized but the damage was already done. I figure the 'cancel' had left the XP MBR in total dissarray as I couldn't get XP back up with anything I tried.

Minor setback. I'll just reinstall XP. So I do and that boots fine except I don't have the CD that probably came with the machine with the drivers for the hardware: USB, sound but most notably video. The desktop GUI is there but not really useable. Ten hours later at about 2am I have finally tracked down all appropriate drivers and XP is working normally. Phew.

In the meantime, once I'd loaded Windows and knew I needed to find the drivers, I put that on hold and installed 9.04 and the Edubuntu add-ons for preschool, primary and secondary. That took all up about an hour to install everything and Ubuntu picked up all hardware right from the start. I didn't need to tweak a thing. 'It just worked'! I obviously needed to tweak and download other stuff to get the machine purring (the computer not online at its usual home so chucked on what they would need), but overall this was just a great, easy install.

I dropped the machine off and the kids were overjoyed. The effort was worth it just to see their faces! I showed them a few things and left them to it. My wife went around after a couple of weeks last weekend. They haven't touched XP pretty much since the first night I dropped the machine back! They reckon why do they need to when they have Edubuntu!

This whole experience has been really wonderful and I have to admit has moved me a little. It really has been a case of Ubuntu/Linux sharing the freedom and knowledge and spreading a little love and goodwill in the community. A crappy computer doesn't perhaps mean a lot to many of us with 2, 3, 4 computers lying about, a home server on the LAN, but for these kids, a Compaq EVO with 512Mb of RAM and an 80Gb drive opens up the world. (They are going online soon.) :)

ps: ... And possibly not just the kids. While mum's English is pretty good, Amharic is spoken at home and she is most comfortable with that and what do you know, the language pack is available! I might post about this separately but if anyone knows how to get the whole machine into another language and not just what you type (if you follow) any info much appreciated.
