### Post by RuneGeek on 2009-04-27
I've been in the IT field for quite a while, and much of that has been spent in positions where I made hiring decisions.

Degrees are helpful in a flooded market; you can use it as an extra selling-point.  However from a "can this person do the job?" standpoint, they prove nothing.

Applicable certifications on the other hand tend to speak better to a person's ability to grasp the important concepts relating to a technical positions.

Of course nothing beats experience, but we all had to start somewhere.  :)

Honestly, if you have a degree in *any* subject, I wouldn't worry about going back to college.  As previously mentioned, given the interests and aptitude you've described, an A+ is a good place to start... then maybe a Windows desktop cert (since they have the biggest market) followed by a Linux cert (less market, but consequently more demand for good support people!)

Good luck!
