### Post by stchman on 2008-03-04
Hello all, I am here to commemorate my 1 year anniversary of Ubuntu bliss.  I started  with Edgy and have been using Feisty for some time.  I have never really looked back.

The forums here are incredible as I have learned so much and now author a website dedicated to Ubuntu usage.

I skipped upgrading to Gutsy as Feisty works so well for what I do.  I eagerly await Hardy this April.

As I toyed with Ubuntu over the last year I was amazed at how powerful it was.  It definitely was the best software installation and is a whiz with printers.  I found that Ubuntu was far better at sharing printers than Windows ever was.

I do however wish that Ubuntu was better at Samba configuration.  I am playing around with Samba with only limited success.  Video editing also lack a little bit but I am not a hardcore video editor.  The GIMP does take a bit of getting used to but I can muddle my way through that app.  I do wish that Adobe would fix Flash for Linux.

Rhythmbox is absolutely a fine music organization softare app.  I really love using it.

Open office does my office duties very well.

CD/DVD burning duties are handled very well by K3B.

CD playback and ripping done using Sound Juicer.

DVD playback is handled by VLC flawlessly after codecs and css package installed.

Email duties handled well by Evolution.

FTP done using FileZilla.

IM handled by Pidgin very well.

Fast loading, small hdd footprint, efficient apps, no viruses or spyware are big for me.

As you can see I am the average PC user that has no huge need for highly specialized applications.  I find that as long as you stick to mainstream hardware (Nvidia, ATI, Creative, Realtek, etc.) that all your hardware will work out of the box.

I don't ever forsee myself going back to Windows and have never regretted the switch over.

FYI, I do dual boot occasionally when I want to play a Windows (DX9) game but that is about it.
