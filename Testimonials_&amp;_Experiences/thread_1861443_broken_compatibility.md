### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-10-17
> **christopher.wortman said:**
> Actually I can, yet another feature people use, developers deem useless.

Er... no.

Indicators do the same function as the notification area.

The notification area technology is ancient by Linux terms. It's inflexible and usually leads to inconsistent behaviour (such as Qt programs rendering their notification area icons in Qt, even when the desktop environment is GTK). It needed changing. The way these icons behaved was often highly confusing, even for old hands; "minimise to tray" and "left click shows one menu, right-click shows another" etc.

It's not a change for the sake of change, it's a usability concern.

Every operating system depreciates old APIs and moves to new ones when better ones become available. You won't avoid that on Windows or Mac OS X. In fact, Mac OS X depreciates an API in one release and then removes it in the next, usually.

It took four releases for Ubuntu to explicitly remove the notification area. It still maintains a "whitelist" of programs that are allowed to draw notification area icons, that haven't made the switch and are too important to miss. If your favourite program hasn't been updated, hassle the developer AND add the program to the whitelist so its notification area icon gets drawn.
