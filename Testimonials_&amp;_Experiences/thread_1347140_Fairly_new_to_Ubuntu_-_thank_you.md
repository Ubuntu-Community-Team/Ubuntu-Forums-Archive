### Post by ScottinSoCal on 2009-12-05
This isn't a request for help, this is a thank you for the resources available here and elsewhere on the web.

I've had Ubuntu installed on a laptop for about a year. I like it, but my e-mail program kept me tied to my WinXP laptop. Well, today I found out how easy it is to install WINE, and I transferred my preferred e-mail client (Forte Agent) over to my Ubuntu laptop with all my old e-mails. About 15 years worth of old e-mails. It works beautifully. Next project is to figure out how to get the launcher moved to the Applications menu, but in the meantime, I'm doing a final backup of my WinXP laptop and preparing to mothball it. I love the interface, I love the ease of getting information, I love the accessibility of software, and I love the fact that I'm no longer worshiping at the church of Microsoft. Thank you for Ubuntu, and thank you for all the documentation and assistance - even if you didn't know you were assisting me when you wrote it.
:D

### Post by sgosnell on 2009-12-05
If you truly want to get away from Windows, you might want to give [this program](http://www.aid4mail.com/) a try.  It's shareware, so you can try it out, and maybe not even need to register it.  I haven't tried it, no need for it, but it claims to be able to transfer email between most common email programs, and specifically names Forte Agent and Thunderbird.  If you can get your email into Thunderbird, you should be able to get it to Evolution, but you may prefer Thunderbird.

I did use NoteTab Pro, one of the company's products, for a number of years when I was using Windows, and it's a solid product, and I have nothing bad to say about the company.
