### Post by Intel91 on 2009-11-05
I have been dual booting linux for awhile now, and have never been more pleased with any other distro or version than I am with 9.10. It just works, almost. I was amazed at the pre-bundled support it came with. My laptop hardware used to not be supported by ubuntu, but this is the first release that I've tried that naturally supports it and will actually install completely (Asus F8SA laptop). The only tweak I had to make was adjusting my resolution via xorg.conf. My wireless NIC was immediately recognized, even my Verizon Broadband Modem was plug-and-play, unlike windows, and that just made me smile. I like the new looks, new wallpapers, and I think it is extremely easy to modify as you wish, anyone can get almost any look they please. It seems mouse gesture work better than ever as well, just much smoother than ever before, it feels like you are controlling the workspace by thought alone.

Onto what I don't like, or rather I wished for:
1. Bundled with Wine, plus patches, plus extra .dll, directx. I don't know the legalities, but to me, I would stop dual booting if a more perfect wine were bundled (I say this because your programmers are better than I am, and can do a better job distributing it ready to go than most anyone can modify to work.
2. Themes, not for myself so much, but for converts. A Mac OS X theme and a Windows 7 theme. I, again, don't know the legality of this. But it would rock some socks.
3. Small thing, but I wish changing the size of a panel icon was as easy as right clicking and saying resize.

I am yet to test a game, but I know what my computer can do in Windows, and typically Linux doesn't perform as well (the only area), but Windows is the king of the gaming market. This is really why I want pre-bundled wine, not only so any user can pop in a crysis disk out of the box and play it, but so even veteran users have a better application with better compatibility and power at their disposal.

Thanks for reading, assuming of course you read
Intel
