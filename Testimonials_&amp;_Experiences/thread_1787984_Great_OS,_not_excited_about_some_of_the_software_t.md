### Post by ynneb on 2011-06-21
I really love Ubuntu and some of its derivatives like Mint.

If email, web surfing, and media playing was all you wanted to do, then you would be a dill to pay for any other OS. Ubuntu/Mint do these with ease and little setup required by the user.

Ubuntu networking, works beautifully. Unlike the tricky XP I also use.

In reality that person might be having a really bad time in their life.

Because nobody wanted to help, and there were no activities to help troubled youth, he was forced to leave. The problem was not this boy, it was the fact that nobody wanted to help and because there was no incentive to do so, he committed suicide later that year.

Not because of obscurity of many distros, but it is missing a key factor, market pressure. Without market pressure, there is no way anybody will (outside of foss developers) develop high end applications.

Please don't take me wrong, but I am not sure who you are fighting against (honestly). I think we all have jobs and earn money to live. No one is telling you we should go back in time and exchange carrots and potatoes. Although some of us are quite "fanatic" supporters, and the reasons to use Ubuntu/Linux may vary, most of the Ubuntu users are rather open minded I would say. I believe the idea of Ubuntu and open source is more than just "cute". It proves another kind of motivation exists and can be powerful too. Let's just see what happens in the future.
