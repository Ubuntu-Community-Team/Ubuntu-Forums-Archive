### Post by Upochapo on 2008-08-29
ROFL!  "Twirks"

I like that word.  Bought a laptop for casual computing and some light gaming.  I wanted to like Vista...had to force myself to like it. But, in the end, I just couldn't hack it.  I still have it installed mind you but boot on the rare occasion for that game that just will not run on Ubuntu.

My absolute favorite thing that Vista does to get the twirk ball on a roll is that...we'll both be sitting there...cohabitating quite peacefully.  I sit there quietly minding my own...nothing going on on the pc...

No software running.
No music being played.
No internet connectivity.
No virus scans being done.
No spyware scans being done.
No tasks running in the task manager tab.

Then it happens...out of the corner of my eye, my hard drive light goes on.  I sit there mystified just staring at it contemplating what it could possibly be doing.  After about 15 minutes of this I get even more twirked.  Because it simply will just not stop.

And, I will say this...I swear I thought someone was being cute when I clicked on a link that had a graphic of a vista message successfully installing Unidentified hardware.  That was until it happened to me.  That message is definitely real.  Boggles the mind.  Apparently, Vista is smart enough to successfully install a hardware device without identifying it or cluing you in on to what said device could be.  The only thing I can tell you is that I'm glad that this unidentifiable hardware was successfully installed.  Otherwise, I don't know where I would be today.

But, yeah, Vista gave me the excuse I needed to look at Ubuntu, again.  And, I have to say...loving the heron.  Intrepid should be fun.
