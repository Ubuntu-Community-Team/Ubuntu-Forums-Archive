### Post by mister_lister on 2007-11-10
I am basically a linux newbie. I have been using Ubuntu Dapper Drake 6.06 LTS at home strictly for the past month. I have it on a 9 year old computer. Here are the specs:

* K6/2 AMD 500 Mhz
* 327 MB RAM
* 20 GB PATA Hard Drive
* Nvidia Geforce 128mb Video Card
* Sound Blaster AWE 64 Sound Card

The video worked immediately with no configuring. The sound card and floppy required me to put entries for them in /etc/modules. After that I was able to have sound and also my floppy.

I got around the codec issues so I was able to play MP3s and MPG and other files after posting messages about the issues. 

I am pretty happy with this distrobution of Ubuntu. There are still somethings I can not do even after posting messages and recieving  some help. They are minor usage issues, for parity with my M$ Windows experience. I may get more serious about resolving these issues but I do not want this thread to become the place for the resolutions. I will start other threads for those issues.

I tried Linux a couple years ago: SuSe, Mandrake, and Knoppix. I had all sorts of issues and abandoned the effort. I have always believed in the idea of Linux. I have seen a marked improvement in the experience of Ubuntu in comparison to my previous try with other distributions.

I am not ready to complely abandon Windows. It has served my needs well. I wil admit, it has dictated my needs as well. Learning a completely new and different OS requires time and effort. I will keep fiddling with Linux and specifically Ubuntu. It is my official "hobby horse" OS but until I get real secure in administrating and using the OS I will still buy Windows and Support Microsoft as well as Ubuntu. I am waiting for some things to develop with myself and with Linux, before I totally abandon Microsoft products.

To show my support for Ubuntu, I bought a few items from the Canonical Store.
