### Post by sajro on 2007-10-12
Windows. Looking back, I feel a bit sick thinking about it. Until about 6 months ago, I thought the only alternative was Mac.

Even back then, I completely despised Microsoft. I worshiped Apple. But then the iPhone came and disappointed me. I searched for an alternative to my alternative. What I found was Linux.

I searched for information about it. The feeling I felt after I saw the [later proven] promises was amazing. Fast boot-up (my Kubuntu rarely takes a minute), security, free apps, and all the other things. Amazing, I thought. I believed I'd have to live with 15-minute (up to 30) startup times (XP Home never reinstalled), but not anymore.

I ordered a CD through ShipIt. I put in the Ubuntu Feisty LiveCD and went goo-ga for the beauty and functionality (meaning I liked it). I loved it, but was wary of installing...I needed a stable computer for my students' work and I could *survive* Winblows.

Then, it happened. Windows just froze. Reboot. Works fine...go on the web and 5 minutes into Firefox...froze. "Forget this," I thought. I decided to dual boot then. I still needed Windows apps not WINE-able, but didn't want to pay antivirus and such. I wanted a stable, useful, efficient OS.

I decided on Kubuntu instead of normal Ubuntu. I liked the KDE but I was too newbish to install through GNOME, so I burnt the CD from the torrent I downloaded.

After a 7+ hour stint on Google and the Craigslist Forums, I finally resized partitions and got into install. Well, here's the thing: The NTFS was too spread-out in the hard drive space, and I--well--*whispers* destroyed the XP system files. *[SIZE="1"]What was that?[/SIZE]* [SIZE="4"]**OKAY! I DESTROYED WINDOWS IN THE PARTITION RESIZING! **[/SIZE] Geez...

So, now my Winblows doesn't boot. What do I do? GET SMART! I mount the ntfs partition (for some reason needing ntfsfix...) and rescue everything I needed, especially my students' work. Then I installed XP again, installed needed programs, and gave it 1GB more space than that required. The rest of my HDD is for Kubuntu!

So, did I screw up my original intentions? YES. Did I get the best OS I have ever used in my life? CERTAINLY. Would I even dream of buying a Windows (or Dell [product], see below) computer again? *_**NO.**_*

Thanks Ubuntu/Linux community. Thanks Linus. And most of all (ironically enough) thanks to Bill Gates for making a crappy enough product I switched. 

Thanks.

To System76...or ZaReason...or HP..or another preinstalled Linux vendor of laptops.[/COLOR]
