### Post by scottuss on 2009-04-17
Howdy everyone! Just had a month or so with Arch Linux as the OS on my main box (switched from Ubuntu) And I've decided to come back. I love Arch, the Arch Way and the idea behind it but for me it's just no good right now.

I consider myself an advanced user and so had no problem configuring Arch but with so much University work on (and other people starting to use the computer a bit) I just can't have such a "tweaking necessary" OS running.

I was finding silly little things that Ubuntu did for me "out of the box" needed sometimes hours of work to achieve in Arch, and whilst I did like that finer degree of control, it was mostly for speed, RAM usage and bootup time. I thought "hmm when I get some spare time over the Summer I'll probably go back to Arch." 

And then I experienced Jaunty's boot time. Kick's Arch's you know what! It's crazy.



So I'm back on Ubuntu, 32 bit (even though all other releases I've used 64, but I need some 32 only software right now)

Just thought I'd add a "hey I'm back" to make up for the usual "goodbye" threads we see here.

Hope you're all loving 9.04 as much as I am!


P.S More than ever before I'm a strong believer in the "use whatever works for you" camp, as opposed to the "you're too stupid to use X or because you couldn't use it you needed to go back to Y"
