### Post by JaradT on 2010-12-19
My first Ubuntu computer was a Compaq Presario PC, SR2125-UK. After Vista decided not to use my mouse, I decided to go "screw it" and install Ubuntu 10.10, and I enjoyed it!

Now, my second computer, which was a Compaq Preario Laptop, a v5000, was running Windows XP until I corrupted the NTOSKRNL.exe file. My cousin and I were trying to change the bitmaps, and it crashed. So don't change the bitmaps in Reshack! :p

I had problems trying to install the Broadcom wireless card (the BCM4318 AirForce One). Tried ndiswrapper and it gave me a "firmware missing" error on GNOME's network applet...

Now, I managed to fix it with [this tutorial]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/bcm43xx") (no internet, I would use Ethernet on my laptop or my xBox 360 >_>) and now I'm updating the OS.

Oh, and I tried Kubuntu, but I'd probably leave that to my VM, unless I dual-boot, but I want to dual-boot Windows XP and Ubuntu, as I can't use Windows 7 with my main computer and I hate Vista.

Anyone had trouble with the Broadcom wireless cards, by the way?
