### Post by sirdilznik on 2007-12-19
Recently I have been finishishing up my media convergence project at home.  Basically I'm bringing all my media types together in one unit (or series of units).  Everything runs through my computer now (Computer, TV, Music[CDs, ogg, etc...], DVDs, even my old PS2 I don't really use anymore) and outputs video to my monitor and outputs sound (via spdif out) to my 5.1 receiver and surround setup.  It's a pretty sweet setup and I'm pretty proud of myself.  Installing MythTV was a snap and everything works awesome.  

I got an idea to add a wireless keyboard and mouse so I can have something to control my computer with when I'm watching a movie or video and sitting on my couch 10 feet away.  I did a little research and found that pretty much any keyboard I get should work, what's more the exact brand and model I was looking at was mentioned by name to work.  Good enough for me.  So I get the items today (1 - 2 days earlier than expected, thanks Newegg!) about an hour or so before I had to go to work.  My idea was to keep my existing (corded) setup as is and use that when sitting at my computer desk normally and leave the cordless keyboard and mouse on my coffee table (or underneath for storage) for use when sitting on the couch.  I had no idea whether this was difficult to set up or even possible (well anything is possible in Linux with enough programming) or what it would entail.  I planned to look up a howto after getting home from work.  I had some time though and I couldn't resist so I opened up the package.  I popped the batteries in the keyboard and the mouse (It came with Duracells!) and noticed the small red "connect" button on the bottom of each unit.  I plugged in the IR receiver via USB and noticed the small black "connect" button on top.  On a whim I pressed the button on the IR unit then the buttons on the keyboard and mouse.  Stuff flashed on the IR receiver.  I set the mouse down and moved it around on the table.  The pointer moved.  I smiled.  I opened up an Eterm window and typed some stuff with the wireless keyboard.  It showed up on the screen.  I walked over to my old corded keyboard and mouse.  Work just like they did before.  Woohoo!!!! :KS

Now that's Plug n Play baby.  Absolutely zero effort and preparation and 100% successful result.  Now my only question is, what to do with the useless Windows driver disc that came with the product?  Frisbee or coaster?
