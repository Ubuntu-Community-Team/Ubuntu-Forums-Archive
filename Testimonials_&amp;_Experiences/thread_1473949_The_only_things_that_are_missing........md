### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-05-05
If you have a 32-bit or 64-bit installation, you can run several versions of Photoshop, Dreamweaver, and Flash in Wine with good speed, and you can always use VirtualBox if you have a computer with 1 GB of RAM or more and an XP disc.

Thing is, if you're ingrained into any OS and its niche programs, you're going to see something in Linux as lacking.

However, even though it's open source, there is a ton of Linux-only software. Sure, there's coLinux and Ubuntu Portable, but I have the reverse situation- Windows just doesn't have Banshee, Rhythmbox, AWN, doesn't support Gnome applets, and there are a few games I play that haven't been ported, yet. Also, the Windows packages for some of my software are outdated or have less features under Windows.

So really, the only thing that new users seem to dislike about Ubuntu is that it's not Windows. Those people should get VirtualBox if they have enough RAM, otherwise they should simply dual-boot and stop trying to force Ubuntu to be the end-all be-all just because they want it to be that so much.

We're coming along, if Ubuntu doesn't suit your computing needs as it is, you don't have to crack out on Linux. Just dual-boot. It's a simple solution that gives you a broader range of applications.

But yeah, you're right, there's not much Ubuntu's really missing.
