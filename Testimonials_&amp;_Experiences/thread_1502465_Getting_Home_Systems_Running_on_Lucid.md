### Post by SteveGoTex on 2010-06-05
I have installed Lucid  in a dual boot XP Pro. The computer is an ancient Dell Optiplex 210L. I placed Ubuntu on a second SATA drive. It is wired to my router, then to broadband cable.

I also have an Inspiron 9400 with Vista, connected via wireless. I set up Ubunto on that via Wubi.

On the network also wired in is a NAS (Buffalo Linkstation).

The big hassles were:

1. Getting the computers to browse the network and share printers.
2. Getting the Inspiron wireless to work.

However, I found all I needed to make these work via this forum.

I really like the way Ubuntu works, and I only have to boot back into XP to update the accounting books on my home based business, and downloading the occasional DRM'd audiobook from Audible or my local library onto my Zen Mozaic. The player works ok with rhythmbox for MP3's, though

I will probably wait until next accounting year to change the business books over to a Linux app, and I guess I will just live with the MP3 player situation for now. I can be in and out of XP in just a few minutes, and I don't have to do that every day.

I have an MSI Wind netbook waiting in the wings to be my next challenge for Ubuntu. I tried the Netbook edition on a pen drive, and no Wifi... I hear that is a challenge, but I am confident someone here can help. (That unit has Win 7 Starter on it, and I plan to make a new partition.

I am enjoying this project of moving over to Ubuntu. I really thank the community.

And, on my ancient Dell Optiplex, Ubuntu beats XP hands down for responsiveness and ease of use. On the Inspiron, it is great, too. My wife can click on a launch button, bring up Firefox and check her gmail, and is happy too.

Thanks,
Steve
