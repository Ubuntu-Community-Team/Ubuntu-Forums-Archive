### Post by ipatt on 2010-09-25
I received a request from an acquaintance to help him install Ubuntu on his machine. I had only an 8.04 CD and decided to request a 10.04 CD from Shipit. I made a donation to Shipit and then entered my request. It was denied "because I had requested too many CDs" in the past. I have requested and received about 10 CDs over the past 5 years for myself and to distribute to friends to help propagate Ubuntu. In most cases this entailed some hours of handholding for installation and introduction to Ubuntu/Linux.

 So, I started the "free download" of the 10.04 iso. I have a high speed ADSL connection capable of 5MB/sec downloads. What do I get from the Canonical server? A speed varying between 14-20 kB/sec: estimated download time for the 696 MB is ~ 10 hours. I got faster downloads when I had only dialup.

 If I had known this would be the situation I would have bought the CD from the Linux Store ($10.57 incl shipping) and not donated $10 to Canonical/Shipit.

 This is mnot the way to encourage prosetilization of Ubuntu. For my installation I could do an upgrade to 10.04 but to for a new user I prefer to give a bootable / install CD.

 Call me disappointed.

I'm sure you're an experienced enough computer user to realise that this is most likely to be a temporary problem with the server - either overload or maintenance. While I accept that this could be off-putting to someone who doesn't understand this, on this page...

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/download)

... you can click on alternative downloads, which takes you to...

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/alternative-download](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/alternative-download)

... on which no 4 in the list is "Complete download location list", here:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/alternative-download#mirrors](http://www.ubuntu.com/desktop/get-ubuntu/alternative-download#mirrors)

... and you have the whole world to explore in search of a server that is not overloaded.

I have very rarely encountered such a slow download, except in that few-days period after a new release of Ubuntu. So don't try again on 10th October or for a couple of days thereafter! :wink:
