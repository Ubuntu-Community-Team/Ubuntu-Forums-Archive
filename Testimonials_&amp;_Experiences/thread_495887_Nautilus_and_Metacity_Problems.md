### Post by merlinus on 2007-07-08
Upon booting up yesterday, I found new drive icons on my desktop.  The three buttons usually at the top right of windows appeared in the center of the title bar.  Clicking any of them caused the window -- and program -- to close.

When I tried to to run gconf-editor, a message came up that metacity was not installed.  This obviously was not the case, and reinstalling via synaptic was useless.  Nautilus also was problematic.

Nothing had changed in the usual configuration files either, and so I suspected the recent ubuntu updates.

Even booting from the older kernel did not solve these problems.

So I reinstalled ubuntu, rather than spend endless hours going round and round with little or nothing gained.

In the process, I discovered that one of my cd drives had problems, and my first attempt at installation failed because of it.

Fortunately, having a sparate /home partition made the process much easier, although obviously I needed to install a few extra apps such as ntfs-3g and ntfs-config, thunar, skype, and thunderbird.

But all of my preferences, settings, emails, bookmarks, open office config, etc. carried over smoothly.

And risking installing the updates again presented no problems.

My best guess is that somehow some basic system files got corrupted, but exactly what caused the problems will remain a mystery.  Not a bad thing at all....

-merlin
