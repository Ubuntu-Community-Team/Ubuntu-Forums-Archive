### Post by tuebinger on 2008-11-20
Ubuntu, for me, has been a rocky roller coaster ride.  

I've had a love/hate relationship with it for a while.  The last three months have been hate, because of some difficulties I had which now seem minor.  They were things like webpage compatibility, upgrade, and sleep issues. They were enough I felt driven to give up Linux in favor of Windows.  

But I now realize that was a premature thing to do and I've adopted a much more realistic view of oeprating systems.  I realize there's no 'one' OS that will fit everyone's expectations.

Ubuntu has become my primary OS again, but I haven't gotten rid of Windows.  I keep it on my hard drive just for those instances when I need to run a game or a piece of software that is Windows only.  This seems like the most practical solution.

I'm glad to be using Ubuntu again, mainly because of its stability and responsiveness (by comparison to Windows, which has become sluggish on my machine, and unstable with some programs crashing or refusing to start at all; i.e., Outlook <-- seems horribly buggy and slow, and just seems to have mysterisouly vanished from my machine as of last week).

Anyway, count me as one who came back after wandering the proverbial desert for three months!
