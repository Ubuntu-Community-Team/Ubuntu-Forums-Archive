### Post by eveningsky339 on 2010-04-25
The other day, I decided to try out another Linux distro.  My Ubuntu installation was being sluggish, and I was getting a little frustrated.  A website advised that openSUSE was "friendliest" for laptops, so I decided to give it a shot.

Well, to put it shortly, it was a mess.  KDE4 was confusing, installing new software took some research as to where to even find the package management system, and bootup was a heckuva lot slower.  About the only thing I liked over Ubuntu was KDE's flexibility in terms of customization.  Otherwise I was practically going to the wiki every ten minutes to figure something out.  To my dismay, the openSUSE community was utterly lacking in the spirit of Ubuntu.  

Don't get me wrong, I think openSUSE is a solid distro.  Installation was a breeze and it was fairly easy to use, compared to some other distro's I've tried.  But its claim to being the most beginner-friendly Linux is off the mark.

About a week after picking up openSUSE, I deleted its partition and went back to Karmic.  Like slipping on an old pair of shoes, it was comfortable and familiar.  While I give openSUSE props for being a nice distro, nothing can beat Ubuntu.
