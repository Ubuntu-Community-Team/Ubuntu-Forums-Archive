[INDENT]Well its more of a case of why use Windows over Linux. MS is the biggest
software company in the world and their marketing strategies are hard to
compete with.

Linux isn't one OS. It's an industry-supported framework for operating
systems. There are many different choices available for different
computing situations. The kernel is the constant between them.

So far the only real reason to actually purchase Windows is gaming. Even
that is starting to fade with projects like WINE. Most of the
down-points to linux are either myths or caused by someone else - eg
something which doesn't cooperate with open-source.

Moving to an open licence system pretty much releives the burden of
constant hardware upgrades of Microsoft & paying for software when its
released every year.

Most people have gotten used to the most rediculus things in computers,
due to Windows.

The majority of 'new' things in Vista are old features of Linux. From
videos on the desktop to 'gadgets' to UAC to disc encryption
to..well...basically everything. Its always how its been.

The huge variety of software to do tasks etc is basically all free and
that goes for codecs as well.

Linux doesn't suck up all your memory.
Linux don't have spyware (that's by design, not by how popular it is)
The Linux kernel has been available to every programmer around the world
to improve on for the last 17 odd years.
Its not proprietary.
It runs on almost any type of computer. More computers are able run
Linux than all MS OS's put together. From high end servers to embedded
devices.

There are no real restrictions. All software by default you're allowed
to redistribute. There is no licence keys etc.
And the support is better than Windows. Most linux users will vouch for
that.

MS is only one product and it is incompatible with everything else
unless it is dictated by one company. ALL OS innovation is capped by MS.
Not so with Linux.

And it shows - Windows is almost constantly behind with every software
innovation.

That's all the benefits of the end-user that I can think of off the top
of my head side from the obvious things like how the OS isn't made by a
company which competes with everything. EG you're encouraged to do stuff
in Linux. There isnt anyone trying to force you to use software that
came with the OS. And it works. There is no broken standards: Eg MS HTML
or Jscript.

As far as the industry goes..Well costs for maintaining the system drop
through the toilet. Companies don't suffer from vendor-lock-in. The core
internet applications are UNIX/linux anyway. Eg Apache, mySQL, PHP, rah
rah.

Plus you actually have a choice. You don't have to choose any particular
operating system since the majority of operating systems are based on
UNIX/Linux. The compatibility issues with Windows are by design of MS.
Its about all they have left for reasons to purchase an OS.

Theres plenty of Ubuntu Vs Vista things on the web. Do a search for
'Aero' on youtube. They're more or less neck & neck aside from the fact
that Vista won't be changing much aside from security patches, but a new
Ubuntu comes out every 6 months and its free to upgrade.

The high players in the industry are viewing MS as halting innovation
with the constant restraints and propritary standards. All those Mac Vs
PC adds are right, ya know. People actually look forward to the next OS
release unless its Windows.[/INDENT]

I haven't had a response yet and I won't post his response because thats not very fair. But let me know what you guys think. I'm not entirely sure on the bit about codecs but I haven't paid for one yet.

I noticed a few things wrong with your post, which i have quoted above.

WINE will always be behind Windows.  This is because of the nature of the project, due to the fact that they have to reengineer added features and such (see DX10).  They may not be far behind, but they will always be behind.

The new features of Windows are the old features of Linux.  Is this because Linux is more innovative or because Linux has a much faster release cycle.  IE you can't say that beryl was release before Vista just because technically it was, you must look back to when development was started. Remember that Beryl is stll beta while aero is released.

Codec's are not free.  There is a reason that they are not installed by default with the standard installation.

Most software is free to redistribute (all under the GPL, but not all software is under the GPL).  You cannot redistribute VMware Workstation.

Other than that, it looks good

:EDIT:

I should add that both Windows and Linux have different philosophies.  Windows starts with everything open and you must close things off to make it more secure.  This allows it to be easier for the end user.  Linux on the other hand starts secure and you must open things to allow functionality.  This tends to be harder for the average user.
