### Post by hambone79 on 2007-05-16
...of course I probably shouldn't be putting Ubuntu in my mouth, but I have definately had some bad experiences with it in the last 6 months. 

My experience with Ubuntu started when I installed it on a spare machine I had sitting around in my apartment. Everything went fine with the installation and all the hardware was detected properly, but a few months later a "security update" rendered the machine completely useless because I couldn't get X working.

At this point I decided that it must have been something I did wrong, so I decided to give Ubuntu another try, but this time I installed Kubuntu just because I wanted to see how the KDE desktop ran. I really liked Kubuntu because everything just worked. It worked so well that I decided to dump Fedora Core on my laptop and install Kubuntu.

I should point out that the laptop is actually my work laptop and I run Pro/Engineer on it. When I dumped Fedora and installed Kubuntu, I had to work through a few minor dependencies to get it working, but eventually I got Pro/E up and running flawlessly. After about a week of use, Pro/E began to act very weird...when I atempted to pan/zoom/rotate a part it wouldn't do anything until AFTER I let go of the mouse button. This problem started completely out of the blue and made the laptop useless as my mobile workstation. I ended up having to reinstall Fedora (on the road) to get my work done.

I gave Ubuntu another chance a few weeks later when I used it to build a MythTV box. The installation and setup of MythTV went very well and after a few hours of copying my media over and setting up some things, I was up and running with a DVR on steroids. That is, until today...for some reason the MythTV box decided to stop detecting the DVR card and extra TV card I had installed. I have been trying for the past 6 hours to get this thing working again, but have just decided to give up on it.

While I believe Ubuntu and all of its derivatives really have alot of potential, I think they have a long way to go to really be considered stable. For now I have dumped Unbuntu on all of my machines and switched back to Fedora. Hopefully I will be able to give Ubuntu another try in the future.
