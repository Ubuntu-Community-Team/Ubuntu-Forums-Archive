[LIST]
[*]Laptop
[*]Processor : T6400 2.00GHz,Intel Core 2 Duo CPU
[*]4GB RAM
[*]Sony VGN-FW390
[*]HDD : TOSHIBA MK2552GSX
[*]Dual Boot with Vista Ultimate 64-bit, Ubuntu 8.04Desktop LTS
[*]ATI Mobility Radeon HD3650, 16.4" display
[*]250 GB HDD
[/LIST]
I recently bought my laptop with the above config, 1200USD, being a fan of SONY.

I thought that I could do partitions by myself, install VMs and play with Windows/UNIX

I later found that OEM based licenses from SONY on Vista cannot provide partition.I have to buy a retail version of Viata to do this naturally.
Somehow, I defrag the HDD, used freeware partition tool to consolidate free space ( a lot of them, none could create partition, Vista did it after defrag), shrunk the volume, able to create a separate partition and installed Ubuntu, Whew!!!

Then, I did a research on T6400, and found that it does not support VT (virtulization technology) and feel very devastated by this. Although, I believe I can do virtualizaion wtih perf degrade on host and the VM.

Now, I relaize I should've done some research on laptops (to check what I wanted to do) but ignored it due to hurriedness.

I originally wanted to buy a MacBook or MacBook Pro, and could've installed Ubuntu separately, but I was afraid of going out of my comfort zone (Windows).I'm a techie by profession and very ashamed of myself.

Thankfully, Ubuntu saved me and I installed Ubuntu 8.04 Desktop on my other partition and have dual-boot with Vista. I wish I could go back in time, and ordered Mac-Pro.
anyways, trying to forget the past and enjoying with what I have now!!!

Also, I recently, bought a DSLR - Canon Rebel XSi based on reviews and am happy with it now, and never had one before.

I think I spent most of my time in researching DSLR than laptop....

Posting this, so people can learn from my mistakes!!!!
