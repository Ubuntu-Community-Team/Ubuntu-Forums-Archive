### Post by Guilden_NL on 2010-08-15
I understand and agree.  I've been running Linux on my desktop since 1995, and still understand what you are trying to communicate.

It is getting better; Ubuntu has made the install of the OS much easier than ever before, and unless you have a very new laptop/PC, it installs easier and performs better than Win 7.

But, having people like you keep telling all of us what the reality is, will help keep making Linux progress.  The biggest improvement I've seen over the past 15 years is the development of the .deb and .rpm packages for ease of installation. I architect very large corporate systems using RHEL 5.x, but still prefer .deb over .rpm packaging.

Keep trying, it's always getting better.
