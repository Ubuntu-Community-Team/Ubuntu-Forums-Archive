### Post by mivo on 2008-07-16
It's not about the costs, there are very good, free AV programs, but it's about performance. ;) Persistent AV programs tend to eat memory and processor cycles, so they slow down the system. Not needing any is really quite an advantage and improves system speed. On my gaming computer (XP), I don't use an AV program precisely for that reason (but I also don't download suspicious stuff, open file attachments or visit obscure web sites on that computer).
