### Post by Cathhsmom on 2010-01-11
I started using Open Office in Windows because I did not have the money to upgrade my MS Office.   I liked what I found in Open Office and I noticed that it used heck of a lot less of resources to run it.   Then I began to wonder what else was out there in Open Source Software. 

 I used Linspire many years ago and it was flawed to a point that I could not use it full time; I had to rely on MS Windows still.   I went looking for Linspire and I noticed it was taken over by another distro.  So instead of trying the distro that took over, I decided to research all the distros, and I ended up with Ubuntu.  

At first, I had printer issues and sound issues; but with all the support on this forum, I was able to fixed my sound and printer issues.   I have dove into many Ubuntu books and I learned the basics... basically enough to be dangerous.  I have done some things that would or should crash the kernel, but it didn't.  MS Windows would not be that forgiving. I am continuing to learn more and more and simply cannot contain myself in talking about it to friends.   

Many thanks to the Ubuntu Team!
