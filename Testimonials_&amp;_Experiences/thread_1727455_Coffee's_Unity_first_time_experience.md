### Post by coffee412 on 2011-04-12
**First I would like to say that I approached the switch over to unity with an open mind.** I know change is a hard thing for some but that is life. As they always say "The only thing that doesnt change in life is - change".

I ran 11.04 install on a clean drive, Basic Dual core AMD with a 8400 nvidia video card. Nothing special about the hardware. I like it that way.

Here are my thoughts on the new desktop interface.

1. Where the ell is the terminal window? Or perhaps we are not suppose to go there anymore? - Ill keep looking. No biggy, Ill find it. But thats the main problem. Where is everything and why do we need to move it all around? 

2. So, We cannot any longer add applets to the task bar (do we still call it that?) and we loose the lower one. Humm... No thanks. I really preferred right clicking and adding applets and working with properties. So, That is a minus in my book.

3. The side bar is really something else isnt it? I think I enjoyed it better by just putting a short cut link on the desktop instead. Now we have this thing hanging off the left side of the desktop. I guess I need to buy a 16:9 monitor for Unity now eh?

4. Im still looking for the term window - Dont worry, Ill find it...

5. Geez. Where did preferences and settings go?? OH, Hid them over at the "shutdown" button. That was tricky. Guess you have to hide them somwhere right?

Im sorry, Dont take all I say in a bad way. But I just want to be honest here. You wouldnt want me to lie would you?

This whole Unity thing kinda reminds me of when MS went from XP to ViSTA. What a big mistake. "Now easier than ever", "Where do you want to go to today?". 

Stepping back and looking at the desktop in general I get the feeling I'm on some romper room playstation thingy with the BIG BUTTONS in MENU SIDEBAR thingy. 

Honestly, This is a bit much of a change for anyone to expect. I think it would have been alot better if you just provided Unity as an add on in the Software Center rather than force it on users as the new and improved Ubuntu desktop. Or perhaps having the option to turn it on/off in the Appearance menu. I dont think I will get used to this at all. Im sorry.

:rolleyes: Well, I think it is safe to say that Im gonna load up on 10.10 or 10.04 for the next 2 years (or less) and after support stops on that I will have to bail. But in todays world I guess Unity is the new term for progress and change - Have at it. 

Best wishes,

coffee
