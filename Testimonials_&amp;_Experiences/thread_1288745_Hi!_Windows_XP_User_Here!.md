### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-10-14
Good to hear an enthusiastic experience from a Windows loyalist. Of course, before you or any of your friends switch over, you should take a good look at osalt (open source alternatives) and the WineHQ AppDB so you can find Linux equivalents to your commonly used software on Windows, and if it doesn't have an equivalent, see if it works with Wine on the appDB website. Always a safe idea.

Of course, it would be a mistake to switch to Linux thinking it should run Windows applications, since it was never really meant to do that, although Wine does it quite well with all of my work applications (advanced game design and 3d modelling stuff), along with many of my favorite games.

But still, I think it would be wrong to judge Linux based on the commercial ports that haven't yet been made for it. It's a valid issue, but doesn't relate directly to the OS.

Again, great to see someone enjoying themselves. Have fun with Linux- there's no reason you can't use as many Operating Systems as you want at the same time.
