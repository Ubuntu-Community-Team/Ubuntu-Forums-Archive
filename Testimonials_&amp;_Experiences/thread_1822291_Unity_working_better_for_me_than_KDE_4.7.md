### Post by marl30 on 2011-08-10
I had used Kubuntu 11.04 for the first two months, from the beta stage until the release stage. I then tried Unity after it was announced that they had fixed majority of the bugs. I've been using Unity for the past two months and have really loved it, as it was bug free and everything just worked. 

I've been coming across a lot of praises for the newly released KDE 4.7, and since I had a great time with KDE 4.6, I reluctantly left Unity to give KDE 4.7 a try. Now I'm sorry I did. I'm also sorry to say that KDE 4.7 is a more of a ram hog than 4.6. The performance I was getting with 4.6 was on par with Unity. 

Now I tried to play my favorite Linux game, Urban Terror, and the performance was very poor in KDE 4.7. Never had that problem in 4.6. The computer even kept freezing. 

So I went over to Psychocat and got that command to get back to pure Gnome/Unity. After I did it, and then load up Urban terror in Unity, the game played smoothly, and Unity was using almost half the amount of ram KDE 4.7 used. I really love KDE, but I'm disappointed with this release. I thought it was getting better on the resource it uses, but it seems like a step backwards, in my opinion.

I'll just continue using Unity because for the last two months, Unity and 11.04 has given me the best experience I have ever had with Ubuntu, in that I had no issue whatsoever. Don't remember anything crashing.
 

 I'll still try KDE in the future, but for right now, I'm going to stick with Unity.
