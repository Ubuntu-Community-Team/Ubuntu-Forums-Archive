### Post by tpg on 2007-08-25
Dear OP: You seem to have had either an exceptionally bad experience with Ubuntu, or an unusually good one with windows. Here are my own thoughts comparing ease of use:

I play around with programming, so like python, ruby, gcc, qt libraries, opengl libraries etc. lying around. I use latex, so need a full installation. Also, I like having a music player that lets me read the tags on all my files, including OGG and FLAC, and organise them nicely. It's also nice to have all my hardware working after an install.

I've tried setting up these environments on both windows and ubuntu. The difference is obvious. On ubuntu, it's easy, on windows, it's almost impossible. Installing everything from separate packages downloaded from websites is such a pain after you've used apt/synaptic for a while. And getting it all configured with paths etc. is very awkward and time consuming. This is one of the many problems I found when setting up windows for development.

I'll also mention here the real value of apt/synaptic: it'll upgrade all your software for you. If I wanted to upgrade from Qt 4.2 to Qt 4.3 on windows, I'd probably have to uninstall, reinstall, reconfigure... On Ubuntu, I do nothing, other than click "OK", then it does it all for me.

Then take LaTeX. MiKTeX is what I've tried, and it works. However it's cumbersome, and again, you have to set paths by hand. Do you honestly believe that typing
```
sudo apt-get install texlive-full
```
is harder than downloading several package files by hand, clicking through installers, then setting environmental variables by hand?

Finally, I am unable to port my music collection of OGG & FLAC to Windows Media Player or iTunes, as they won't recognise the tags, even if I can persuade the files themselves to play with (sometimes poorly supported) codec packs.

Finally, I spent several hours hunting down all the windows drivers for the components in my laptop. On Ubuntu, everything worked out of the box. (for the record, it is an ASUS A3000N).

I'm afraid I just cannot agree that windows is easier than Ubuntu. Quite the opposite.
