### Post by solwic on 2009-03-16
> **nelskurian said:**
> Since it s global movement against propritery softwares.

Not to be a killjoy, but therein lies the problem.  There is the reason that FOSS will likely never take off, at least not in the sense that proprietary software has.  

Rather than being a movement *against* something, it ought to be a movement *for* something.  As long as the movements goals and objectives are cast in a negative light, it'll always be underground, always be small.  

That's what I believe anyway.  The outer affects the inner, and I, for one, am tired of Linux users being cast as anti-establishment.  I'm not *against* Microsoft; I'm *for* Linux.  To be perfectly honest, **** Microsoft.  They aren't even a blip on my radar.

As for proprietary software, same deal.  If I can use free software, I will.  If I have to buy something, I'll do that, too.  You can't take down the system just to replace it with another any more than you can take down one religion/government/idea and replace it with something else.  

I guess what I'm saying is that the FOSS philosophy has to coexist with proprietary ventures, or it's doomed to failure.  Humanity towards others is a grand, lofty (and ultimately good) idea, but it will never beat out the philosophy it has been set against, which is the philosophy of money.  

Just my .02 (pardon this small pun).  :)

As many, I've witnessed ideas that started with good intentions only to be corrupted by the profit seekers simply to line their pockets. I have no arguments with some proprietary software, problems arise when we attempt to specify limits and integrate the two concepts.

I have no desire to watch Linux/Ubuntu become another Windows, whose sole purpose is to make a profit - period. I truly believe this tends to limit creativity and increases the natural instinct to profit (at any cost) off of enhancements or updates/upgrades, etc. MS has already displayed a propensity to either buy off or purchase competitors who represent a threat. With the amount of resources available to MS, I'm amazed that FOSS projects even exist. 

I firmly believe that many would not have the ability to enjoy a modern, quality, fully functional OS and associated apps. based on funding. This motive alone I believe generates significant pirate activities that occurs with Windows. I also believe that is why Linux is perceived by many as a threat to MS and it's profit mongering exploitive associates. I've often wondered how much malware was generated in support of a cottage industry that has grown rich from this activity. 

Anytime, we consider proprietary software, it should be kept in check (check & balances) with regards to the primary objectives and ideologies of FOSS. Again, I wish to reiterate that my own personal views are -  that business and potentially personal (especially games) proprietary software could find a home in the FOSS community. But we must control and appreciate the environment.
