### Post by irv on 2011-10-15
Ubuntu with Unity is not for everyone, and that includes myself. I am not a fan of Unity but I believe in giving it a chance. When 11.04 came our I download it, installed it, and used it for about a month. I still didn't like it so I kept Ubuntu 11.04 and tried Gnome 3. After a while I found Gnome 3 to much like Unity so I formated and went with Lubuntu and xfce desktop. 

Long story short, it wasn't Gnome 2.3 but it did the trick for the rest of the year while I waited for 11.10. The day it came out I install it at 2am in the morning. I used it one day and broke it.

All I did was change the size of the icons in the Dash and made them smaller. This went well, but then I went back into Compiz manager and setup the cube and rotate and the OS locked up. I had to reboot and when it came back up I lost the Dash completely and without the dash Unity is no good. Next I went into recovery mode and when I came back to the desktop I had the Dash again but couldn't change anything.

From that point on every time I started in normal mode I had no Dash, I had to start in recovery mode to get the dash to work. So I dumped everything and re-installed 11.10. This was a fresh install with a format. The same thing happen again. I did this 5 times and could repeat this same problem. This is a bug I could not live with. Besides I still didn't like Unity.

Well I went with Kubuntu with the KDE desktop. I tried KDE a few years ago but liked Gnome 2.x much better. Well now that I don't have Gnome 2.x any more only Gnome 3, I think I will stay with Kubuntu and KDE and see where things go in the future.
