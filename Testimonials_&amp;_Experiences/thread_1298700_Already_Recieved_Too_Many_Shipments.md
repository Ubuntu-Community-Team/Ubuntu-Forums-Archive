### Post by fourtyseven on 2009-10-23
I did not realise that there exists a limit on how many times one can request a new version from shipit. Working in IT education, I have been teaching Ubuntu for a few years now. I work with previously disadvantaged people in Africa and have been a promoter of open source software. I have lived by the Ubuntu philosophy 'umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu'. Being in Africa where internet acces is very limited and due to the fact that I dont have internet access at my home I have religiously requested a new version of Ubuntu every six months when it becomes available so that I can keep in touch with the latest developments.
That being said, this morning I went onto shipit to preorder 9.10 and I get the following message: > Demand for Ubuntu CDs is very high, and we're trying to ensure that we have enough CDs for those who really need one. We've noticed that you've already received CDs of several previous Ubuntu releases. You can help us ensure the continued availability of Ubuntu CDs by 
upgrading to the new release without a CD
downloading your own CD for free
becoming an Ubuntu member by contributing to Ubuntu, and thereby becoming eligible for more CDs
Thanks for your support of Ubuntu!
Now I can understand if I was ordering 50 copies of the same version but I only request one copy each time a new version comes out. I then go out and buy literally hundreds of blank discs, copy ubuntu and distribute. 
I have attempted to get freedom toaster up and running in my area but all attempts to contact them have resulted in nothing so I have tried to take it upon myself to distribute open source as best I can.
While this is more of a comment than a complaint, Im slightly disappointed in ubuntu or canonical or whoever for telling me that I have 'already received too many shipments'.
> 
A traveller through our country would stop at a village, and he didn't have to ask for food or water. Once he stops, the people give him food and entertain him. That is one aspect of ubuntu but ubuntu has various other aspects. Ubuntu does not mean that people should not enrich themselves but the question is: Are you going to do so in order to enable the community around you to improve? ~ Nelson Mandela
