### Post by Molydeus on 2009-03-22
Hello Everyone!

This is my first post to the Ubuntu forums and I thought I would let everyone know how Ubuntu works for me.

I am Running Ubuntu 8.04 (Hardy)  on a Gateway  700S  pc. Intel P4 2.40 GHz and 128mb Nividia GeForce 4 MX 440G Graphics card. Audio is a SB Audigy SE 24bit 7.1. Monitor is a Dynex DX-LCD26-09 HD TV. 

I bought my first computer in 1986 and it was a XT IBM clone with a 30 MB hard drive and good ole ms dos C: desk top :-). Since then I have operated windows based machines. I would describe my computing ability as a competent operator with enough knowledge to keep my machines running well and to be able to get myself out of most problems that I create for my self.

In early January 2009 I had just pulled out a nice 120g HD from a old Satellite receiver I was taking to the dump. Thats when I installed the HD in my system and had to find something to put on it. That is when I remembered about Linux. I figured I would leave XP on my 80gig HD and install Linux on the 120gig HD and have a dual boot system. 

Now since I knew almost nothing about Linux other than there was a multitude of flavors to choose from I  went to Borders and started looking through their Linux books. I settled on Ubuntu for Non-Geeks 3rd edition by Rickford Grant. I liked how it was put together in a format easy for me to follow. It also has a live CD so I can test my system before I jump in to the ocean that is Linux.

Well, I got home and tested my system and it seemed to work so, I jumped in and did a full install on the 120g HD. For the most part everything went smoothly. Once done, the dual boot idea was not working and I had no sound. I figured the dual boot was my fault because I do not always read all the instructions. :-)

Now that I have been playing with Ubuntu 8.04 for 3 months. I have fell in love with the OS. During my 3 month journey I have resurrected my friends dead (due to viruses) Gateway pc and I almost have my system running perfectly. My last few issues are minor at best. 

Every one I have showed my system to has been impressed. I have Compiz Fusion and Emerald working well with a cubed desk top and all the animations working. Wine is working great for running the game Torment and UT2004 works great as a Linux install.

As for the few things I would like to get working here is my small list. If anyone has any ideas please let me know.

1.)Sound: initial problem turned out the card that came with my system had no Linux drivers. So I changed that to the SB card listed above. Sound works great but the digital input for microphone does not due to the current driver has not got that part working. I will probably swap to a card that every thing works when I get around to it.

2.)Dual Boot: After reading a large part of the grub manual and looking at my current grub file it looks like my system should be able to dual boot. My issue is for some reason my monitor / TV does not seem to receive a signal after the BIOS step and before Ubuntu booting. I can hit Esc and halt the boot as if I am in the grub menu but I have no output on my screen. I will test it with a borrowed monitor when I have a chance. I also have already turned off silent boot in the BIOS.

3.)Streaming music and video to my Xbox 360: I have tried a few things but have not figured that out yet. I keep getting distracted to many great things to learn about Linux. :-)

4.)For some reason when I exit UT2004 my sound does not work until I kill the program UT2004.bin that seems to linger around after I exit the game.

5.)Last but not least I have a 120GB external HD with all my music files in WMA format: I can play any of the files on Real Player but I do not find it to have a friendly interface. I really like Song Bird and I currently am listening to one of the streaming rock stations with the pictures of the band scrolling buy. However, it only seems to play a hand full of the albums I have in my library. Any suggestions to a good media player that will play all my WMA songs before I change the format of several thousand songs? 

My review of Ubuntu 8.04 at least for my self: 2 thumbs up! Nearly a flawless system except for the operator :-). As a last note I have never had as much fun with a computer as I am having now since I switched to Ubuntu.

Regards

Molydeus
