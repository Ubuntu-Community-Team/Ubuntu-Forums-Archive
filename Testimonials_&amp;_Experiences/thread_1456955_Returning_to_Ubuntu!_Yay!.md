### Post by Buddy's Pal on 2010-04-17
Hi folks. I'm back after an absence of about 10 months.

I ran Ubuntu exclusively for about 1 1/2 years but returned to Windows because of a few work related programs that I wasn't able to run in Wine. I Couldn't go the VirtualBox route because I didn't have a Windows disc. I Couldn't dual boot because my recovery discs didn't work! So I had to _pay_ :( to have Windows Vista put back on.

I later upgraded my laptop to Windows 7 which is leaps and bounds better than Vista. I thought I'd be happy forever. But the urge to mess with Linux returned and grew stronger. So I considered dual booting. But I thought back to my first experience with Ubuntu and realized that I hadn't experimented or learned all that much in a year and a half because I was afraid of messing up my main system.

To solve the problem, I bought a cheap second hand Dell Inspiron 1520 that will become my "Ubuntu toy". I've installed Hardy on it but can't really use it much yet as its missing 2 key caps and has one key that's not working. I've ordered a replacement keyboard and should be up and running in a few days. There'll be nothing important on this Dell, so I'll be free to tinker!

Forum experts prepare to answer lots of newbie questions!
