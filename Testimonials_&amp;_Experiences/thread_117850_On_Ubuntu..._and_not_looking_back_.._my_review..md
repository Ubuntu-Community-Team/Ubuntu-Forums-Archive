### Post by DigitalDuality on 2006-01-15
Ok i guess in alot of ways i'm your typical Windows convert.  Networking and Computer Mgmt B.S., 2 IT jobs..one being a systems admin, built at least a dozen small business websites in my short existance, a music and movie addict, who does all his financial stuff online or on a computer.  I've been on Windows for about 10 years until i recently about a Mac Mini.  I have always embraced the OSS movement, but loved the user-friendliness of both Mac and Windows systems.  But one day i just decided, i can't support these business models anymore.  I have to finally quit playing with linux once every other  year and just make the switch and learn it.

Now i'm still an amateur at best.  The only real experience i've had with linux before this was using Red Hat 6.0, Fedora Core v2 for about 2-3 weeks, and i setup and manage a Debian based Censornet proxy content filtering appliance.

My installs of Ubuntu on an old PII, 400MB Pc didn't go so smoothly.  I had video card problems out of the rear and just got really frustrated.  But i decided to try again.. on my main PC.  THe one with 300GB of all my files on it.  The one machine i've always been hesitant to try it on.  I installed it..and wow. Smooth.  The first thing that i loved right up front is something pretty petty.  The sound scheme, the color scheme and the default background were all very eye catching to me and soothing.  I don't think any linux distro has as good of an interface in terms of graphic art..as say Windows or OS X (no.. i don't like KDE..sorry.  I just don't like Konquerer).  But Ubuntu has seemed to make a few minor changes here and there with stuff that was completely configurable on all other distro's and created a wonderful "human theme" and i am truly in love with that for some quirky reason.

It noticed all my hardware.  I was particularly impressed my Logitech webcam, my HP 320 Digital Camera, my iRiver mp3 player, and my PNY thumbdrive all worked upon first install (well the webcam needed software).

I've fallen in love with the Synaptic Package Manager.  What a great concept, especiallly for alot of windows converts.  Those who feel so very used to the double click and crappy menus of "next next next", this is much better than what even Windows or Mac can provide.

I'm also quite impressed with the Art Manager application and it's attachment to the Gnome library of themes and backgrounds.  Very cool.

I had problems mounting my 2nd hard-drive  but that was my inexperience with linux more than anything.  I had some problems getting video files, and dvds to work properly but with the help of such a great online community that pointed me in the right direction, these problems have been eliminated with minimal frustration.

I'm currenlty working on a website for work using Quanta Plus and have made several brochures using Scribus.  I still have problems getting Nicotine to connect to a soulseek server, but i got bit torrent so that's just a minor inconvience i'm sure i'll figure out of make a thread requesting help when i feel like messing with it.

I almost don't know what to do with some of the spare time i have now.  (As i did on my mac as well).  I set up firestarter, but.. i love the fact that security maintenence takes me no time now.  No anti-virus b.s. no anti-spyware b.s. b/c i simply don't have to worry about it all that much. (though i'm not arrogant about it).

I loathed the default icons for Open Office that come on every linux distrobution, I do like the default icons they use in windows.  They're much easier to tell apart. So i changed those, but it would be nice if these icons came standard.   The one thing i always hated about both Linux and Mac was no replacement for MSPaint.  Sometimes i just need to do really really basic stuff that doesn't require a slow loading application like Photoshop or Gimp with complex art menus.  KolourPaint has been a great replacement in this department.

Rythembox.. not the prettiest application out there. But it does everything i need.  And the data organization on it is great.. even superior to iTunes  It has definately helped me navigate through the 7,000 ogg files i have with much greater ease.

I do love the fact that the system just upgrades.  I've seen this on Fedora too if i'm not mistaken and don't know about other distros out there, but that's a wonderful feature.  Beagle has been a great replacement for the desktop search apps i've tried and used on Windows boxes and comparable to Spotlight.  

I've grown to like the menu organization on the upper panel quite better than menu layouts on MS and Mac. Quite nice.   I'm still having problems trying to share a folder between my Ubuntu box and my OS X box, but that doesn't seem like it'll be too bad of a problem.

I do not like Totem.  It doesn't seem to render the video quality i want from my files.  VLC has more than made up for it though.  I think it was good i familiarized myself with apps like VLC, GAIM, Gimp, OpenOffice, and Scribus before coming on board, that definately didn't make me feel like i was in such an alien environment.

So basically.. i'm sold.  I found my distro.  Not just b/c i've enjoyed the experience, but b/c of such a wonderful community that's been more helpful than any other online community i've seen.  Synaptic has also sold me as well.  I thought i'd list out my problems and complaints along with my praise to paint the full picture and not just do some pointless ass patting.

For all that's on this board that have helped me out, and for those that participate in the development of this software, thank you.  It's a great product and a great experience.. and i finally get to do all i need to do on a system that embodies my beliefs.
