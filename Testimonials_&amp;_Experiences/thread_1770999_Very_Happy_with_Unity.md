### Post by pythagoreanmetronome on 2011-05-29
I started using Linux with Ubuntu 9.04.  The compaq desktop I was using at the time came pre-installed with Windows Vista.  I wanted to try something new.  I became disillusioned with 10.04 and switched to Fedora 13.  Fedora got on my nerves because of the rather byzantine security built into it and also the fact that each release had such a short lifespan made it feel like I was on a gerbil wheel testing features for Red Hat.  So, I started using Debian 6 and I am actually very happy with it.  All of the hype and controversy around Ubuntu Unity made me want to see what 11.04 was all about so I recently gave it a try.

I have to say that the installer makes it so easy to use that I feel like this version of Ubuntu really does represent the first example of desktop Linux that actually could work for the general user.  I ticked the relevant boxes in the installer and lo and behold once I had booted up my installed system I found that my nVidia drivers were already installed and the computer would actually play mp3's, flash video and a dvd without any additional work at the command line required.  The Unity interface is really cool.  I especially like how when you hold down the "super" key (which is the windows key on most keyboards) it makes little numbers appear on the icons on the desktop.  So, for example, to open firefox I just push super+3 on my keyboard now.  I also like how Ubuntu has made a clear and bold step to differentiate itself from other Linux distros on the desktop.  When I was using Fedora and then Debian they all seemed basically the same because they all used the same gnome desktop.

Thanks to everybody who works on Ubuntu.  If ONLY there was voice recognition software that works as well as Dragon and also some sort of Video editing that was as easy to use as the Windows versions, I would totally just switch to Ubuntu.

### Post by pythagoreanmetronome on 2011-06-01
Well I have been messing around with Unity/11.04 and I really just can't be bothered anymore.  Its too much.  I suppose that the overall idea of Mark Shuttleworth to invent a new operating system for free for Africa is a laudable goal and everything, which I support as part of the whole laptop-for-every-child concept but...  after using Unity and trying to configure it over the last couple of days I realized that its basically just good old gnome with compiz and a cairo-dock, which is hardly much different than just running debian with compiz and a cairo-dock...  
 
The most striking difference is the work that the ubutu team has put into the installer.  The installer is very nice.  sort of.
 
What is most objectionable about what is happening with ubuntu/unity is evident in the installer and unity in general: the fact that MARKETING AND DESIGN professionals seem to have taken over your computer.  They are using the operating system as a vehicle for testing out logos and color schemes and various design concepts but overall they are adding very little of quality or content.  Its like they have a few people who write code working for Mark Shuttleworth and all of those people really wish they went to art school so they read Wallpaper Magazine and suchlike longing to be graphic designers.
 
Anyway...  the way forward with computing is not to follow the starbucks model.  The starbucks model is this: take a cheap cup of coffee and make it 10 times more expensive by making that cup of coffee into a vanilla chai latte double pump pumpkin spice.
 
Even typing this into the void which is the internet makes me feel like a tiny bit of my soul is slowly oozing out of my fingertips.  This is not why I use computers.
 
Marketing.  This who goofy enterprise is a vehicle for a marketing team.  I am sure of it.
