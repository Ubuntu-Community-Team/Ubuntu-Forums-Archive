### Post by alienexplorers on 2007-02-22
This is the first operating system I used where I did not have to reload it every week or so.  When I ran Windoze, each update killed my system and I had to reload from scratch.   With Ubuntu I loaded it back in March of 2006 and have yet to reload a thing.  I am so happy with the reliability of this operating system that I never will go back to Windoze.

I have been running 6.06 since it came out and before that 5.10.  The upgrade process from 5.10 to 6.06 was so easy and everything worked with no problems.  Since 6.06 is a LTS project that I plan to keep using it.  I see no need in updating to Edgy since everything is working great and I would hate to mess something up.

I might update when the new LTS operating system comes out to replace 6.06.1.

The only problem I have had that had me stumped was upgrading from a old ATI video card to a newer Nvidia video card.  After spending sometime in the forums even that problem was easy to correct.

Keep up the good work and LONG LIVE UBUNTU..........

### Post by draynes on 2007-02-23
I've spent the last several days thrashing around with my old dumpster-dive PCs to get some Linux systems up and running.  In years past I tried various Slackware, Red Hat, and freeBSD distros and never could get any of them up and running with a GUI - terrible problems with video card incompatibility, random crashes and reboots during the install, etc, etc.  I decided to give Linux one last try.

First, I was able to get SuSE 9.2 installed and working on an old AMD K6/2-500 box with no problems other than not enough horsepower in the box.  It's very sluggish, but it works fine.

Next, I downloaded ISO's for current versions of freeBSD, Debian, SuSE 10.2, Ubuntu 6.06, Kubuntu 6.10, and Xubuntu 6.10.  The installs of freeBSD, Debian, an old Red Hat 5.2, and Xubuntu wouldn't work with my funky old hardware or just crashed off into neverland during the hardware detection phases.

SuSE 9.2, SuSE 10.2, Ubuntu 6.06, and Kubuntu 6.10 all installed flawlessly on either separate drives or on partitions on a W98SE system.  I now have three fully functional dual- or triple-boot boxes with: W98SE/SuSE 9.2,,,,, W98SE/Ubuntu 6.06/Kubuntu 6.10,,,,,SuSE 10.2 KDE/SuSE 10.2 Gnome.  A variety of boot managers were used, including System Commander 7.05 on one of the W98SE boxes.

Whew.  I lost a lot of time thrashing around with Debian, Red Hat, Slackware, and freeBSD while the Ubuntu and SuSE installs were smooth as glass except for my occasional ignorant stumbles.

Ubuntu and Kubuntu run quite noticeably faster on these old clunkers than does SuSE, and I am amazed at how much stuff could be packed onto one CD.

Great work, team!  My earlier experiences with trying to install those crude early distros *almost* made me skip this rehab project as a lost cause.
