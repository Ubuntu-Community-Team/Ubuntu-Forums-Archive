### Post by HappyFeet on 2009-10-29
I have tried kubuntu on and off since the beginning, and always regarded it as the proverbial redheaded step-child. This *may* be changing. I have tried KDE 4 in the past, and found it lacking in stability and would not put up with it. But I have to admit that the devs have done a great job in refining it, and making it something I would *consider*. Granted, I've only been using it for a few hours, but so far the experience has been pleasant. (minus my learning curve as a gnome person)

I was immediately STRUCK by the visual aspect of KDE. Holy crap Batman, it looks good. It actually puts gnome (in it's current state) to shame. It can definitely keep up with with vista/win7 and mac. That being said, stability is THE most important factor for me when it comes to OS's. We, afterall, can make our OS's look like almost anything with some tweaking. KDE (Kubuntu) so far has passed my stability tests. Mind you, I've only been using it for a few hours. Many tests remain.

Besides the nvidia-settings fiasco going on,(which is not kubuntu specific) I think the Kubuntu team and KDE team in general, have done a great job so far. I'm not quite ready to wipe out my install of 9.04 (gnome), but I will continue to use kubuntu and explore it. Who knows, I may actually be reporting Kubuntu bugs soon. (which I would)

It seems KDE4 has grown up a bit. It's about time. Welcome aboard.
