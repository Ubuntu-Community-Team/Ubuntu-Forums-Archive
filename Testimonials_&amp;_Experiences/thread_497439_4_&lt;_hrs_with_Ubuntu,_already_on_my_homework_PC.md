### Post by BRow86 on 2007-07-10
I've been a Windows guy all my life.  Recently, I've decided to hope over to the Unix/Linux boat.  Why?  Well, for one, I installed Vista (dont' worry--I didn't pay) and *WHAM* everything sllooowwweedd down.  My CPU was constantly at 100%.  I also hopped ship from Windows because I want to eventually set up my own web/ftp server.   My friend suggested trying out DragonflyBSD (what he uses on his server).  I did my best for about 3-4 weeks with it.  It was EXTREMELY difficult to only go with command-driven prompts.  Even with X installed I only had window terminals.  I tried putting KDE on there, and it eventually filled up my /usr.  I said "screw it" and tried an old version of redhat-- which literally exploded my laptop.  Later, I moved to Ubuntu because I thought this was a good place to start.  I installed XP, and then started installing Ubuntu.  I wasn't too worried about getting dual boot because I had a CD with GAG handy.  But, crazily enough, after I chose to use the driven install on the nearest open available free space, GRUB had automatically set itself up for dual boot.  Needless to say I was pumped.  Everything was working too-- sound, video, wireless.  I was amazingly surprised since I'm used to everything blowing up.  I then went home and tried it out on my main PC.  Now I'm dual booting WinXP 64 and Ubuntu.  Let me just say this: if I can get my web stuff going (Apache, OpenSSH, and I want to start making flash sites) and get Half Life 2 running, I may just give up Windows forever.  Thanks a lot Ubuntu, you've given me a fresh look at computers, and since computers are a good deal of my life... well, I think you know where I'm going.  Thanks guys.
