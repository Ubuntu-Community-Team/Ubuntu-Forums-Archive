### Post by shaft350x on 2007-05-11
So I opted to not compare Ubuntu to a virus in the title for this thread because I figure people would get upset about that.  However, I've been using Ubuntu nearly exclusively for about 6 months now (dual-boot, use XP for Oblivion and Video editing only).

I was thinking of buying an older laptop just to use as a play system, and after discussion with my wife we decided to get her an ultra-light and then I would take her old (1 year old) laptop as my play system.

Enter System76 Darter.  Beautiful little machine, got Beryl working on it in record time.  A friend of ours who is a computer aficionado commented, "That is a sexy little box."  I personally don't know why people complain about it being a white case, because it sets it apart.  So my wife, who was not even a super adept Windows user, is now on Linux.  I highly recommend System76 for anyone considering purchasing a laptop, pre-installed is the way to go for non-power users who just want it all to work, right out of the box. [Side note, woo! Dell!]

Next my friend and next door neighbor was talking about how his system was lagging, I said try Ubuntu, it has a LiveCD you don't have to install it, just play with it and see if you like it.  He brought back the CD the next day.  I told him I had copies, to which he replied that he liked it so much he installed it.  He is now thinking about putting Feisty on his laptop as well.

Then my other neighbor, knowing that I was using Linux, started asking about it because his system is getting a little bogged down, so I told him about it.  I gave him the LiveCD for Feisty, I don't think he has installed it yet, he may wait until school is out.  However, he seemed excited by the idea, and hopefully it will work well with his new external harddrive.

When I went to drop off the Feisty LiveCD to this neighbor his in-laws were visiting.  I was telling him how it came with Open Office, and Firefox so he would have everything he would need, and that Ubuntu could see his files in XP, and read them.

His father-in-law perked up, at the mention of Open Office.  So I told him about Ubuntu as well, which he said he'd have to look into.  Although his wife uses Microsoft Publisher, which Open Office doesn't have an equivalent for, and I've seen no alternative programs that read them for Linux (yet).

I also gave a copy of the Feisty LiveCD (I burned 3-4 of them just so I could give them out) to two other classmates, one with an older laptop that she hopes to extend the life on for another year or two (until graduation), and the another friend who had tried Linux before, but never knew about LiveCDs (he works for IT at our school too.)

I recommended to all that they join the Ubuntu forums, and being Windows users, that they should dual-boot as well.  Just in case they run into problems.

With the exception of my neighbor's in-laws, none of the rest of us really rely on programs that depend on Windows, mostly music, videos, web-surfing, and word-processing are our needs.  So it is easy to recommend. 

It hasn't always been easy for me, I've broken X several times, but I learn.  I admit that to people right from the start.  However, Ubuntu is pretty darn stable if you aren't trying to tweak it (Beryl anyone, though it's gotten easier).  Maybe Ubuntu will be right for them as well, maybe it won't.  

Works great for me though!
