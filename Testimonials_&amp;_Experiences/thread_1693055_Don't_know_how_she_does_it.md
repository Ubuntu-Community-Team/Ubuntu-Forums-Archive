### Post by sprangalang on 2011-02-22
My daughter, bless her, has this strange ability to kill Win7, either that or Win7 has a "feature" that kills itself.  I have had to re-install it 4 times, and each time, it ends up slowing down and doing stupid things like forgetting restore points (after 197 updates perhaps?), hanging on it's own menus, slowness, and not working.  Gahhhh.  Finally I told her she should try Linux, as our 2001 desktop has had Mint in it for years with nary a problem.  She reluctantly agreed, and off I went to Distrowatch to see what's what these days.  Ubuntu still leading the pack.  I see Mint there in second, and Puppy still in the running.  I decided to try 10.10, since it is so popular and it has mission critical capability, ie ipod compatibility.  I was pleased to see that U10.10 is gracious in that it does not demand to take up the whole drive, and can live happily among other OSs.
Right off the bat no wireless internet.  I tried in vain for hours to correct with mouse clicks.  This cured it: sudo apt-get update && sudo apt-get install bcmwl-kernel-source.  Thanks to the great users here, that annoying bump was hurdled in seconds.  I guess it would have went smoother, had I installed with a cable to the router.  Oh well, next time.
I believe she is impressed, by the lightning fast boot-up, quick navigation, software manager, and the fact that Dad is not blowing steam from both ears from "Winitis".

Cheers!
