### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-03-08
Compiling isn't really something that is recommended for newbies to do. Until you know your way around a bit more, stick to what's in the repositories. The stuff in the repositories is guaranteed to be high quality and mature. Compiling actually isn't difficult, it just involves getting into the correct directory in the terminal, running ./configure, and then finding -dev dependencies in Synaptic whenever the configure script complains.

Literally 95% of computer users work without a "dock". It's probably not a "Linux-desktop-blocker" :-)

If it's any consolation, I'm about as much a Windows newbie as you are a Linux newbie. Heck, I owned a copy of Windows on my previous computer, for about a year and a half, yet I still never got my head around it. I didn't use Windows often though - the first day, I installed Ubuntu.

Infamous true story: On my first day with Windows, I wanted to convert some MP3s to WMAs. Windows Media Encoder's GUI seemed to want me to do them literally one at a time. Very impractical since I had 120 files. After looking in the help file for a way to batch-encode them, I came across the documentation for the Windows Media Encoder command-line version. Two minutes later, the command-line was happily encoding files while I went to the supermarket.

Sometimes the Linux way is the better way to do things - even on Windows!
