### Post by xdunlapx on 2008-12-27
I totally agree with all of you. I've been using linux since late 2002 and can honestly say I do not miss Windows at all. I really enjoy the freedom linux gives and the almost innumerable ways to configure it for your tastes. On that topic Windows only provides a 'theme' manager. Ooh, one theme that looks the same as all of the others, with exception of the high contrast ones. That is one thing that Windows lacks. Actually it lacks everything that Linux is and stands for. Over the course of a few years I've experimented with almost every distro of linux. I always come back to Ubuntu. 

I would love the smaller footprint distros but the main things I find are lack of support for my intel wifi built-in card and the widescreen 1440x900 resolution. 

Ubuntu seems to be the best for newer and higher-end systems, but I think that is because of the massive community support and development that most other distros lack.

If I want to install flash support on any non-debian systems it's near impossible to install via CLI as the package names are so unorganized.

Thanks to everyone who uses and contributes to Ubuntu!! Woohoo!:popcorn:
