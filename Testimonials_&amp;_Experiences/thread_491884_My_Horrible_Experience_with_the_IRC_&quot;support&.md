### Post by Jamesinator on 2007-07-04
First of all, I love Ubuntu. I've used the IRC several times before and found it full of kind, helpful, users and excellent mods. I had several questions answered there and helped several other people with their questions.

Not so anymore.

I was just about to give my elderly mother (who uses Windows 2000) a tour of Ubuntu to try to get her to switch, so I came into the IRC to ask about addons aiding a Windows-to-Ubuntu switch, and was instead met with totally oblivious users who didn't even bother to listen to my question, and a completely out of bounds operator who kicked and banned me because I told him he wasn't listening.

Unfortunately, it's only now that I realize I hadn't checked "enable logging" in GAIM, so I guess I'm stuck with this rant.

I'd just like to thank n2diy (who sarcastically suggested that I give my mother a terminal and have her learn Linux), the several other self-centered, unhelpful users there (who told me that Ubuntu was already wonderful and easy to use. "Yes, thanks, I know, but this is a bit of different case, can you please help me out with answering my question? Yes, it really is great, isn't it? Yes, I know it can import most of my Windows fil-- YES, I know it doesn't need a theme that looks like Windows to work grea-- Can you *please* answer my question?"

And finally, to tritium (if I have his name correct) who kick/banned me because I told him he was making me pissed and being oblivious.

So I'd just like to say thank you to everyone who "helped" me on the #ubuntu IRC channel at 4am when I was already stressed enough and expecting the kind, helpful community I had found before.

Bye Ubuntu, your software is great, but your community can be abominable. Until you can manage your abusive operators and actually focus on weeding unhelpful users instead of attacking helpful ones, you can stop expecting me to ever try to help people in the ubuntu support again.

~Jamesinator
