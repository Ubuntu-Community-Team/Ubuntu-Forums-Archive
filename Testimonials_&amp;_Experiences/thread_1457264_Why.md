### Post by TBABill on 2010-04-19
Well I can give a "why" perspective...
 
Kids used the wife's Win 7 laptop for a couple hours and I spent the following hour tracking down a  hidden single .exe file that was a trojan posing as antivirus software that "found" many viruses on my machine and led me right to the credit card input screen to "activate" the software. Of course I did not, but the threat was real. I had to boot into safe mode and track down the file via command line (like my old DOS days) because it was contained in a file I could not find via GUI (must have been an attribute I didn't take enough time to find).
 
Second machine is a 25 inch screen HP touch with Core 2 Duo. This machine is nice when it works, but I have had more crashes on this single machine than any other. Never a hardware failure. Always a Windows or app issue. Yesterday I could not get to the OS at all after one of the kids was on MySpace. I never saw them click anything but pictures of friends and the chat box or navigation buttons. I was right there with them when it crashed....locked and nothing but a cold restart would do anything. But the cold restart stuck at the half-way point of booting and never would go past there, even in safe mode. I tried everything and I am certainly not a novice Windows user.
 
End result...reimage back to factory default. That took OVER 6 hours!! I do not have the disks but the image is on the D: so I just did a system restore. Why it took so long is beyond me. I finally went to bed and finished this morning. That machine is the fastest I own and was still painfully slow to install. I can install Ubuntu in 15 minutes...windows in 6+ hours. 
 
I am betting I had another virus on the HP, but I cannot confirm. It could have been a glitch. But I had just reimaged back to factory after a game my son used kept creating issues with Windows (Wizard 101 I think). My "fresh" install lasted about a month and now I reinstalled again last night. 
 
I have 6 computers in the house and feel like I am fighting Windows on one of them almost daily. Print issues (spool crashes), viruses (I just love when my kids click "yes" without a clue!), malware, update failures and on and on and on. I should get IT pay at home for all I have to keep going.
 
But my Linux machine just works. They have yet to infect it, crash it or harm it. They use it whenever it's free because it is a laptop. My wife's laptop on Windows....good times and constant work to keep it going without viruses and the other stuff Windows machines are so susceptible to.
