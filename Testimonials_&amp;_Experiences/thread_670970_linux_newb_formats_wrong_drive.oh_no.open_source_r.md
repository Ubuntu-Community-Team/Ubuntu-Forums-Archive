### Post by daveoftheworld on 2008-01-18
Hello. I just got serious about switching to linux.I downloaded several live cd distros including gutsy. Then I bought another hard drive and installed it.Knowing nothing about formatting or partitioning,I did some reading.I then booted up gutsy and started installing.Not to far into this i realized that i formatted my other drive, full of years of touring the web.I remained calm but my heart sunk.Right after that i got the hang of partitioning and easily got ubuntu fully installed. I searched google about data recovery and linux and learned about Testdisk.I'm a newb but i got my files from the formatted drive copied over to my home partition.I'd like to thank the people who spend so much time developing all this software.I have had no trouble at all with ubuntu connecting to the internet or peripherals or anything. I've burned many cds and dvds on windows but never on linux.I burned a forensics distro called fire today and it only took about 5 minutes to figure it out.
         Thank you very much for all the great software i've used already and more i've yet to use
