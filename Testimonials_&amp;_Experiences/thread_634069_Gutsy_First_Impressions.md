### Post by FRuMMaGe on 2007-12-07
Well, after 9 months of Dapper, I felt I was ready to update my system. This thought was cemented after I was greeted with a merry little message on bootup explaining that an "Unfixable hard drive failure has occurred".

So I inserted the Gutsy disk and booted up. The first thing I noticed was that they login sound had been changed. Next thing I noticed astounded me. Gutsy had managed to automatically detect and configure my internal usb wireless card, my external hard-drive, and even my built in card reader! It had taken me weeks to finally sort out my wireless card in Dapper, and months trying to sort out my card read but to no avail.

Obviously I was impressed. The general feel of Gutsy was much more polished than that of Dapper, particularly the Vista-esque window management system.

Anyway, I have been using Gutsy for about 1 week now and I have not experienced any crashes, freezes or software errors of any kind. Good job!
