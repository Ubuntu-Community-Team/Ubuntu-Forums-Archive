Market saturation is happening. Many people are content with the OS that shipped with their computers. They are not shopping for something better, they are happy, and I am happy for them, and the world is at peace, and suffering is lessened. Ubuntu may grow again, if one of the other OS developers blows it big time, and causes the remaining userbase to be so dissatisfied that they must look for something else, then we may see another growth spurt. Keep in mind, if Ubuntu ever hit 100% of all computers out there, then the graph will flatline. The reality of the situation is, there are X amount of people at any given time that will use Ubuntu, when X is achieved for Y time, then the graph will flatline.

The myth of constant and eternal growth is one of the biggest broken philosophies of our time. Products, Companies, Operating Systems, or whatever it is your trying to market, will have a saturation point, when that is achieved you may either accept it, or deny it and attempt to push your goods onto someone who neither wants nor needs them. Then you get to start losing money as the returns, support service expenses, and other problems associated with selling igloos to Eskimos begin to surface.

Lack of growth is NOT a bad thing, it only indicates a current market saturation.
