### Post by [darkfire] on 2007-05-07
I never thought it would happen, but our main computer is now 100% linux. How this happened is quite interesting.

Being the only one my dad trust to fix his computer, he asked me.  We had just got netzero dsl, and the spyware that was installed during our dialup days awoke. I spent about 1 month cleaning, going through the registry, and any method of cleaning spyware. It came to a point that our internet would no longer connect. After talking with the trouble shooting guys at netzero (They have competent staff, or a real good instruction manual)  They informed us that we had a socket error and had to get sp2 installed.  Now theres a problem. I had to download it. The internet didn't work.  Then I remembered, I could use linux.  I was looking for my slackware cd... but found a release candidate for dapper drake first.  So I used it. My dad walked in and saw that the internet worked now.  He asked if I fixed the problem. I said, "No, this isn't windows. Its Linux". He looked at the screen and said "oobuntu". Yes, he pronounced it right. (not to amazing since he is Nigerian, but even I still say Uubuntu). I explained that it was free, and satisfied the familys basic usage. Word pressing, internet, his mp3 player and such.  I also explained that with Crossover you can still use your specialized windows programs.  He loved it and now we have it installed on our computer. Updated to Feisty fawn. 

So whats the point. My dad fell in love with ubuntu in 10 minutes. That was the first time he saw linux.  Of course I ignored my windows loving brothers plea.  I have yet to switch...After Effects is holding me back.
