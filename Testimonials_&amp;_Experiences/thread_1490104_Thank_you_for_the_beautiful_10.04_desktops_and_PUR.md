### Post by thrice_loved on 2010-05-21
[COLOR="Purple"]Dear Ubuntu design team & artwork contributors,[/COLOR]

[COLOR="Indigo"]About three years ago I posted on your wiki about the fact that there were no really feminine themes or default desktops in the Ubuntu install. The first thing I always did, upon installing a new version was google pretty themes and pictures![/COLOR]

[COLOR="Purple"]This wasn't a big problem, but it always made me feel a little excluded that none of the default themes or desktops used pink or purple or stars or flowers - while I'm not quite so stereotypical, you get the idea![/COLOR]

[COLOR="Indigo"]But Ubuntu, you have outdone yourself with 10.04 - Purple by default!! Thank you, thank you, thank you!!! In addition, the last two versions have featured some very pretty desktops (I now flip between the meadow, butterfly one from 9.10 and the bright daisy from 10.04) - I love the experience of falling in love with default desktops! - for the first time I feel like the desktop really suits me![/COLOR]

[COLOR="Purple"]I don't feel excluded, but privileged AND I dare say it's going to result in more female users - When I showed my flatmate 10.04 netbook edition on my eepc she said, word for word: "That's gorgeous, I will use that!"

Thank you again![/COLOR]
