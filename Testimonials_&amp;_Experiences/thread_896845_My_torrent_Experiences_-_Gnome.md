### Post by ctarwater on 2008-08-21
I'm still fairly new to Ubuntu but have been trying out a few torrent apps to see which one I like and I figured I'd post my opinions. 

I've tried to keep them non-fanatical, please understand these are merely my opinions about these programs.

OK, that's out of the way.  I'm running Hardy 8.04 with the Gnome desktop, and an ancient p4 1.4 Mhz with 1 gig of ram,  so that's where I'm coming from here.


 **Deluge** 
  I tried multiple versions of it (from the one available in the repositories, to the newest RC)and it seems ok but it had a habit of moving my .torrent files as well as the actual data being seeded.  Not cool.  Also, with the newest RC release it would just stop all traffic after a bit.  However with it's constant development I will continue to test it occasionally.

 **Transmission**
  I'm a control freak and like more options.  But it works well, it's easy to use, and I use it on my N800 without issues.

  **Ktorrent**
  Probably my favorite of all the *nix based torrent apps, but running it under gnome just seemed to slow down my computer.  I love the interface (I know it's not that different from Deluge, but I like it better for some reason).  I tried multiple versions and the most recent one (3.1.2) is my favorite by far, but for some reason right-clicking anywhere in the app caused it to crash.  Once I've got a better understanding of running kde4 apps under gnome I'll definitely try this one again.

  **Utorrent / Wine**
  For now though I'm sticking with this setup (most recent version of wine and uTorrent 1.8 )   I'm used to it, it works without any problems at all, takes very little resources, and does everything I need it to do.  

  **rTorrent**
  I know, I know, fanboys.  It's the best...etc. etc. etc.  Real men don't need GUI, etc.
