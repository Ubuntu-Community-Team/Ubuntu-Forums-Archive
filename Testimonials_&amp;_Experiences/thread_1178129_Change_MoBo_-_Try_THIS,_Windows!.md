### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2009-06-04
Hi.

So the day finally came..... i had all the parts together to soup up my computer. My trusty every-day production computer that's been running flawlessly (more or less) under XP, later Vista, even later (and for over a year) Ubuntu Hardy. It was still fast enough for my every day stuff, but sometimes the lack of USB ports (one MoBo USB header didn't work) and the slow video converting was a bit annoying. It was a Sempron 2800+ socket 754 with 2 GB of DDR-1 RAM.

Now i've gotten a slightly used Athlon 64 X2 5000+ and a matching AM2 board and 2 GB of DDR-2. 

Prior to doing the swap i did a similar swap (i.e. MoBo, RAM and CPU) at my office machine, running XP Home. Here, too, the newer MoBo was from the same manufacturer (Asus) and the graphics, HDD's etc all stayed the same. I even carefully uninstalled all related drivers before shutting Windows down and swapping!

On boot - BSOD. 
Safe Mode - BSOD
Last Known Good - BSOD
Recovery Console - not working.

Ended up with a new install of XP, re-activation via phone (convince them that it is really just a new board and not a second computer... the online activation didn't want to accept that fact!) and finally i was up again. This software-only operation took little over three hours including installing the other software (Office etc). Luckily no data loss - everything on separate physical HDD.

Now my Ubuntu box. Shiver. Shutdown, power cord out, taking apart. Old MoBo out, new MoBo in. DVD drives changed as well (SATA instead of IDE), checked cables, close case, power cord in.

Hardy Live CD handy, push that power button.

About 40 seconds later i was looking at my desktop, all my stuff was there and - WORKING! Wait, all of it? No, AWN was missing. Aha, no desktop effects. Aha, no Nvidia driver. Hmm but still installed, just click "enable" and AWN appeared magically :)

And the internet didn't want to work either! Hmm. Aha, something about "roaming mode". Nope, i want my static IP back. Hammered it in, started Firefox again - and there it was :)
 
Time needed for the software-only operation? Hmm three minutes..? Maybe a little less. 

And the thing is FLYING now!! Watching four different videos (different formats even) in different players at the same time generates a load of about 10% on each of the two cores..... i have yet to convert the first video on this system but i have the feeling it will take less than half the time it took before :)

Ubuntu, i'm loving it!

Kind regards....

Thanh
