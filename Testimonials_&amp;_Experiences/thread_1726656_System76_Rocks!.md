### Post by Echo35 on 2011-04-11
This thread is more of a System76 praise than Ubuntu itself. I've been using Ubuntu for quite some time now (I think I started back on version 5.xx or 6.xx) and have been very satisfied. By far the best, easiest to use Linux distro out there, which is a large part of the reason I heard about System76 in the first place, since they were at the time, the only company that shipped Linux machines OEM (And as we all know, setting up Linux on a machine, particularly back then, is a pain sometimes). I had always looked into getting myself a System76 at some point, but I never really had the opportunity. Fast forward to last month, and such an opportunity presented itself.

I had been using an HP Pavilion DV-7 for about a year at this point (After moving up from an OLPC ;) ) and it was, to say the least, a problem child. Seems like the very day the warranty ran out a slew of problems popped up, mostly little nagging ones, like the screen becoming scratched to hell (Seemingly by itself, as the only place it ever was was on my desk or in the case), the logo LED burning out, dust getting under the screen, the SD card reader unseating itself, and so on, but at least it worked. Well, I was playing some video games on it (Big time gamer, which is the single thing I DON'T like about Ubuntu. You just can't beat Windows in that department, which is why every machine I own is a dual boot) and the laptop just shut off, never to power up again. I was a little pissed, but at least I had a good excuse to drop some cash on a new laptop :D I was looking around, since I'd been out of the laptop market for a while, and remembered System76.

I figured, being a small company, that they would be more expensive than traditional offerings, but boy was I wrong. I got myself a Serval Professional, nearly maxed out, for much less than anything Dell, HP (Not that I ever want another HP again), Toshiba, or anything else I looked at offered. I couldn't even find too many laptops with an nVidia 485M in it, much less not many available yet with second gen Core i7 Sandy Bridges (Since the recall, supply has been low) so as a gamer, this was incredibly attractive to me, and at a stunning price point. I ordered the laptop, and due to the afore mentioned Core i7 recall, I was expecting a slight delay on my order. What I was not expecting was System76 to be so quick in responding to me, and their overall transparency in the entire order process. I really felt like they cared about the customer, and making sure I knew exactly what I was getting and when I would be getting it. No mucking around with "special offers" and extra crap during the order process. They didn't try to hide system specs and try to make themselves look better through bogus technical jargon. They didn't have a billion unnecessary options and things I didn't want. Very straight to the point and professional. Needless to say, I am very happy about the entire process and impressed with their level of professionalism. I am an IT guy, so I'm quite used to getting the "could shoulder" as it were from a lot of computer companies, practically pulling teeth just to get any support from anything. In my field, just getting a replacement part from a computer under warranty is suprising ;-)

As far as the machine itself, I couldn't be happier. Looks gorgeous (A lot of "gaming" laptops out there are incredibly flamboyant and gaudy. This laptop is simple looking and very slick) and runs very well. The 1080 15.6" screen is incredibly sharp, and the hardware itself is impressive to say the least. I've dual booted it since I got it last week, and have been throwing game after game at it, as well as various other high performance software, and it takes it like a champ. Far and away, the best laptop I have ever purchased, and System76 rocks! You guys definitely have a recurring customer from me!
