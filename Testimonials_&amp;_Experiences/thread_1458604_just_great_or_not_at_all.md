### Post by armandh on 2010-04-20
there is a lot of hardware both new and old that will just work with Ubuntu and, of course, some that just wont. [IMHO] it is the owners of the first group that become the successful converts. the second lot owners become the hit and run flamers.

some of both have valid opinion and it is some times obvious that a few have no skill when it comes to installing an OS.

as easy as an Ubuntu install is, some hardware isn't.

now the experience part

NO ONE should be doing their first OS install on a mission critical computer, even the OEM copy over [if they want to save any files]

here is where the win box that died from a virus last year comes in handy [as long as it did not really die from dust bunnies in the fans.] still one has to know enough to understand that a box throwing off beep codes is not a good first choice.

OEM Ubuntu may not be the answer either. my experience with a Dell Mini 9's dellbuntu resulted in a mini 9 with OOTB real Ubuntu.

after just a few years I still think of my self as a noob when it comes to ubuntu experience, but I do know how good it can be.
