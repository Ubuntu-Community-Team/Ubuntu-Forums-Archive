You've arrived. There's no real reason anyone needs windows at home anymore.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-05-01
I truly believe that using Ubuntu has nothing what so ever to do with age. But rather a simple desire to learn.

I have a seven & one half (don't forget the 1/2  :))  year old grandson who can install - setup - and use Ubuntu. So obviously Ubuntu isn't rocket science. I suspect 99% of all the problems with new users is a lack of understanding and no desire to spend time learning the OS. They expect a Windows clone and when issues arise they get frustrated.

For example, this incessant desire to install the newest release without doing any investigation into bugs, compatibility, etc. Then start ranting when a failure occurs. In most of their rants they compare it to Windows, instead of addressing it as it's own entity. When reading many of the complaints in this section. It immediately becomes apparent (to me) that the majority of these types of post insist on comparing Ubuntu to Windows. They are unable to identify and/or address issues in Ubuntu without that comparison. Which in my opinion says it all.

If I were to give anyone a recommendation or advice on Ubuntu, it would be prefaced by the words "patience", "tolerance" and a need to realize that learning is a requirement. As it was with Windows, yet apparently some believe it unnecessary. Then base their expectations on previous experiences with Windows.

Your grandma understands the need to spend time learning the new OS. It's instinctive and she is obviously an intelligent lady that comprehends why learning is necessary. I hope the new OS meets all her requirements/expectations and wish her well.

Enjoyed reading you post and look forward to reading your (and hopefully your grandma's) future posts.
