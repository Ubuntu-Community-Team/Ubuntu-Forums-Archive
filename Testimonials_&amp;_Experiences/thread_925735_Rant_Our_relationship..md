### Post by 311005901 on 2008-09-21
My dearest Ubuntu,

We go way back.
I knew you when you were 4.10. I gathered your pieces on BitTorrent over a six month period on my speedy dialup, borrowed a friend's external drive to burn you, waited hours for installation on my first computer, and finally, after some time, I was able to see you fully.
At first sight I was captivated. Pretty mahogany interfaces over a strikingly fast interior, you were the system for me.

Though I was new to Linux, you cradled and nurtured our electronic relationship. 
Did you know?
You were my first.
I had never compiled before I met you.

In a matter of months, I knew your innards inside and out. I learned how to do everything I wanted to with you. Watch movies, listen to music, send emails, do school stuff, etc, but you had a few hang ups: you liked to use text inputs and the terminal whenever something went wrong. I know you like communication, but geez. Come on. Do I have to interrogate you whenever something goes wrong?

As in every relationship, I realized that we needed our space. Maybe over time, you would smooth over some of the hang ups. I asked a few others about you. They said that the "hang ups" were simply nuances. I didn't know what to believe, so I decided to give "us" a break.

A few years later, I picked "us" up again. You were still beautiful. Nice curves. You had some shiny effects and were quite the looker. But, I realized that you had not grown up. To my surprise, our conversations on the terminal had not gotten easier. My simple questions like, "Can you find where 5 gigs of my music went?" and "[What happened to the printer we installed together]("http://img140.imageshack.us/img140/3734/printbb2.jpg")?" were replied with "Bash."

I always felt inadequate when you answered me that way. What were you thinking? Was that some kind of interjection like "psh" or you want to "bash" my head in?

A simple "I don't know" would have satisfied me...

So I guess this is the end between you and me.
At least for now...

If any developers or entrepreneurs are reading over your shoulder, this is the part that they should pay attention to:

[LIST=1]You still don't have a way for me to buy and rent popular [music]("http://www.vorbis.com/faq/#fan") and [movies]("http://www.theora.org/benefits/") legally in your famous format. Of course, I could go with other [formats]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mp3"), but isn't that cheating?

[*]You still aren't compatible with some things that I have or that I might buy for you to play with, you know, graphics cards, scanners, cameras, the wifi that I still can't get to work. Ubuntu, you have to learn that nobody will like you if you can't play well with others.

[*]You have no common executable with any body else. I know a lot of studs that have experience with you tell me that it's fun to have it different ways, but I'm pretty traditional. I like it one way. I thought .deb  and .tar.gz was good enough because you shared it with your cute but older sister Debian, but you can do so many: .sh .tar .bz .bz2 .ymz .bin .pet .slp .rpm... Your list goes on and on, and I can't keep up. (You're beginning to look a little "open" with how many foreign programs you let run on you.) :(

[*]I see that you made a big [step]("http://news.softpedia.com/news/Canonical-To-Offer-Multimedia-Codecs-and-DVD-playback-93845.shtml"). But babe, if I have a DVD drive and a Windows license taped on the underside of the laptop, I am legally allowed to download and use a DVD reader program for free. I'm sorry, but I don't need to buy another one.

[*]Finally, none of the stores in and around my city know how to talk to you when you have a breakdown. I have to talk my [significant other]("http://josearroyo.org/files/page1_blog_entry67_1.jpg") just to get over the differences between you and me.[/list]

And so, Ubuntu I have come to the conclusion that we are too different.
You attempt to meet my needs, but I'm sorry. I like movies, music, editing graphics, designing HTML and CSS, writing papers, checking my email, browsing the internet, listening to audio books, and transferring music to my iPod.

I've realized that I'm too old and tired to tinker with you any longer. Let's end our relationship, it feels more like a hobby. We can always hook up, but as of right now, my needs are not being met.

It was nice to know you.
You were always faithful.
Love,
A former Ubuntu user.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-09-22
Buying music is easy - use Amazon.com.  The music is in MP3 format. Legally.

There are lots of printers, scanners, and wifi cards that work easily with Linux. There's a massive collection of graphics cards and cameras that work easily with Linux. If you want to buy some hardware, just check that it works with Linux beforehand. Mac users have been doing the same for years. Vista users do this. It's reasonable for Linux users to do this.

There cannot be a "common executable" on Linux. It runs on more than one architecture, and CPUs of one architecture cannot run programs from another architecture. I'm sorry, but you should get over it. RPMs can be converted to Debs, if you don't feel like compiling from source you shouldn't, and if something has a binary installer it's sure to be made by a proprietary software company.

You don't need to buy a DVD reader program to play your DVDs. Libdvdcss is a free library. Besides, buying software for one platform does not automatically give you the right to have it for another platform - you should know that!

I don't see anything in your needs that Linux cannot accomplish easily.

It's a creative rant, but not exactly a correct one.
