### Post by pataphysicianu on 2012-04-07
> **Thrasyllus said:**
>  To mention one detail, the size of the Xterm terminal has been reduced to that of a large postage stamp so I need a magnifying glass to read it comfortably - and character fonts don't look too good at those sizes. 

You must have accidentally reset the font size in xterm, it's not hard to do accidentally. Most people don't realize that xterm has a menu, you get to it by holding down Ctrl and pressing the Left mouse button for main menu and the right mouse button for the font menu, it's a sucky menu that operates by having hold the mouse button down and whatever menu item is selected when you let go is chosen. This means that sometimes you can accidentally choose something, especially when trying to cut and paste, using more traditional methods that don't actually work in xterm. 

Xterm has several choices of size on the font menu, one is called unreadable, and is just as you describe, the size of a postage stamp. I am fairly certain you inadvertently selected this, it's really easy to do accidentally.

As far as cutting and pasting, xterm is kind of strange so it is much easier to use gnome terminal which has always been ubuntu's default terminal. In xterm, you paste by clicking the middle mouse button, and cutting require holding down the left mouse button, highlighting the test, and while still holding down the left mouse button, hitting the middle button.
