### Post by armandh on 2008-04-15
so far 8.04 beta testing seems great but I have not pushed all the buttons yet, and I will probably wait for the release B4 putting it on the main workbench machine.

just looking at the posts one can see that Ubuntu 8.04 is almost ready for my desktop as I am not likely to try hardware or software seriously lacking support, or that I have not already figured how to get running.

I do see trouble ahead as the complex nature of one OS fits all grows exponentially. those who's hardware and software are at the far edge of the map will have those "fix it" questioned to be resolved in greater numbers.

nor am I sure that there is a ready fix for the ever growing complexity.
the good news is that "where there is the will" to fix something it can be done in an open OS. I was able to get an obsolete peripheral working with Ubuntu, not so with vista. 

So to paraphrase an old adage, I will work for the ability to know what can be changed and hope for the wisdom to avoid what cannot.
after all; with my limited ability and advanced age there is no use banging my head it to something I can avoid.
