### Post by Remkoh on 2010-11-10
Hi all,

I always wanted to try a Linux based operating for various different reasons.

- curiosity
- the added security and stability (even over Windows 7 - which I still think is a fine OS)
- The fact that it's open source and community driven...
- Free

The only thing stopping me was the fact that not many games work natively on it. And I was a bit daunted at how to start at getting all my games to work on it. 
I personally refuse to create dual boot systems for gaming.

But now that i'm following a course as a network administrator (almost finished btw) and I had to use both Windows and Linux for this. Now was a good a time as any to convert to Linux on my Home computer. I chose Ubuntu because well; it's the most accessible and best known Linux distro (in case one needs support this is handy).
And I happy to say I'm very glad I did. Up until now Ubuntu and Linux in general have not failed me once. From the ease of installation, to the plethora of software... it has been a very fun and educational experience for me.
Currently I'm learning the ins and outs of the terminal and it's various commands. And so far it's going ok-ish. Mostly thanks to my knowledge of dos and the huge amount of how-to's available on the internet.

I even got some of my games working again under Linux, not that i'm a big gamer anymore but it's always a fun distraction :D

So, one more happy convert here... Goodbye Windows for personal use. And since i'm the goto guy for most of my family when it comes to pc's. I'm also recommending Ubuntu/Linux to them. (saves me a lot of reinstalling their fubarred Windows Installs) :guitar:
