### Post by wombat20 on 2005-10-18
I recently built a PC for my mother out of the leftover bits when I upgraded.  

She was previously using Win98 which was just about serving her needs, although a few things were playing up.  She used it for email, web browsing and word processing.  As she didn't have a huge amount of Windows knowledge, I thought she'd be better off with Linux, so I installed Ubuntu 5.04 and got it working before delivering it to her and talking her through the basics of logging on/off and using Firefox, Thunderbird and OO.org.

I also wrote a 20 page guide talking through her most common tasks in case she forgot.  The whole experience was made more pleasant for her as it's a faster PC with an LCD screen.

Anyway, two weeks on she told me, "I love my new computer" - which from my technophobic (almost tin-foil-hat-wearing :)) mother is great to hear!

I'm still having my own trouble getting games running as they should, but for the simpler stuff (which is 90% of desktop computing), Ubuntu is the answer!

So a big thanks to all those who contributed to this great distro.  :)
