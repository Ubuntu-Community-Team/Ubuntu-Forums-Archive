### Post by M_Mynaardt on 2012-04-13
Hi!

Just for my own Ubuntu experiences.

I first got into Ubuntu, like you do, because of Microsoft Windows 7.  The thing is, I had a  desktop on XP and a Toshiba laptop on (gag!) vista.  I found out an upgrade to MSW7 would cost about C$100 and _might_ work; *maybe*.  No way was I going to spend a hundred bucks a pop for something that _might_ work!

Someone suggested I try Ubuntu and see how it goes.  That would be your basic version 10.04.  First thing I noticed was that when my computers ran on Ubuntu, they started up much, much faster.   It took me less than a week to decide that Windoze just had to go!

Anyway, since then, I did a bit of messing about between Ubuntu and Linux Mint.  I gravitated to using either Xfce or LXDE and finally settled on Xubuntu for my PC and Lubuntu for my laptop.  I'm quite happy with both.

It was a bit of a tough having to learn a whole new OS from the ground up.  But I'm glad I made the switch!

### Post by M_Mynaardt on 2012-04-14
One thing I do rather like about the Ubuntu family is how the updater can install the newest version of your Ubuntu OS without having to reinstall things like data and such from scratch.

Unfortunately, distros like Mint make it necessary to start from scratch with a new version.

Mind you, best to wait until the latest stable version with the bugs fixed sort of thing.  And it's a bit of a slow process.  **On the other hand**, it's not as slow as having to recopy all your data and settings all over again as well.

I just wish it were easier to get people I talk to, live and in person, to try something like Ubuntu...
