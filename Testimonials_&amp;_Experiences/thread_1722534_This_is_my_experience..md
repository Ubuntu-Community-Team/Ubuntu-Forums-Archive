### Post by DrSeemann on 2011-04-05
Hi all, thou this is my third post (i'm trying to help). I'm a complete newbie when it comes to Ubuntu, even Linux of free software.
Im a 21 years old "Computer Technician" going for a "superior" degree and teachers never talked to me about Linux.
Tech aspects:
I'm running Ubuntu 10.10 Netbook on a Grundig A4PS notebook, wich is **useless** for my mom, brother, sister, nephews, and  pretty much all the world. It's really a **slow motion **computer, you can't have fun unless it's able to run warcraft V diablo 8 and lineage 10. What i mean is, it isn't funny not having the leastest games (OS) from the market. That's what they argue and i never wanted to believe in that.
I had hard times running WXP SP3 on it, in fact it became slow, full of system problems wich took me hours to solve, just because i dont like to **format** the HD every 3 weeks.
Now with Ubuntu this Dual Core 1.73 1GB Laptop runs faster than my brother's AMD Athlon64 3.2 *2 2GB 256MB GForce. 
Life "philosophy" aspect: 
Well, i came out guessing that i've been always running behind the technolgical advances. Always using a 5 to 10 years old OS (illegal). Using "free" AV wich seems to work fine until your Windows blows up. So... what can i lose switching to Ubuntu?
I started with the wrong foot, Sis Mirage 3 isn't supported on Linux. I came to find that SiS doesn't want to release drivers on linux. OK im switching back to the "dark side of the force"... NOT AT ALL.
And even if it's really hard for me "unlearning" the Windows way, and learning Linux way. I feel the same way i felt when my dad bought the old 486 with DOS 6.22. I was 6 years old, and spent hours learning how to run some games on that "Super Spaceship" Computer, i mean i was like OMG IM SO HAXOR just because i was able to change directoy :lolflag:.
What i mean is that im really happy, im feeling the same curiosity for the computer, that i used to feel when i was a child (not too long ago). I really find hard to guess how in the h*ll i was using Windows.
Now i feel that all my studies are "paying me". Not money (yet), just because i find really lame that a technician don't know how to fix something. The common answer for everything is "REINSTALL FORMAT DESTROY" a soft to make it work.

In a few words: Im so happy with my "old" netbook, that i can't even imagine why some people complains about their new pcs running slow.
