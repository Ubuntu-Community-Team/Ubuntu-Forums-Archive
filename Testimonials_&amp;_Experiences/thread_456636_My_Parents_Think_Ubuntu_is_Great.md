### Post by 13thMonkey on 2007-05-27
I finally decided to very generously donate my old Ubuntu server to my parents (I'm too kind :D)

My old Ubuntu server is still my server and I changed nothing, apart from to set up accounts for them (plus email etc) and install NX so I can log in remotely to it plus some security stuff. There were a few teething troubles until I told them in no uncertain terms that they weren't allowed to just switch off the computer anymore (and I put a blinkenlights poster over the power button to reinforce the point after they discovered that was the only way they had left to actually switch the PC off  ;))

Neither of them were computer savvy, but over the few weeks that they've had Ubuntu they've been fighting over who gets to use it. The cynic in me says it's because it's a faster PC with Thunderbird/FF instead of the MS crap on their old box, but since it's still my server they don't get to see much of a speed increase overall really.

The reasons they like it?

1) They haven't broken it yet.
2) They've tried to click on everything and haven't broken it yet.
3) Everything works, despite their best attempts at breaking it.
4) Frozen bubble :roll:

I like it because the only question I've had from them so far is "why doesn't this thing I saw on the internet work?" The answer is of course, because my life is made eaiser if they can't sudo anything :D But isn't it great that they're so excited by their PC that they're even trying to? And using the command line too! Heh! Compare that to Windows when they never even tried to install anything but still managed to screw things up and you can see why I love Linux even more than they do!
