### Post by ranch hand on 2009-04-17
I just wiped 1 drive and have been repopulating it.

I a linus noob, I need to learn.  I was pretty good at MS (isn't MS a noted ailment).

The need is to become that good on linux.  It looks like Debian based OSs (so far) are what I am after.  I really like the Ubuntu based ones.

UE is interesting.  I downloaded UE gamer (I am not a gamer, can't even figure out how to play them).  It takes as much space as Vista did.  WOW

I am running Intrepid on a 10G HDD on another box, / and /home.  UE would fill the HDD.  That box, by the way, is running as fast under Intrepid as this one (see sig) did under Vista.

Linux in general and Ubuntu particularly are in danger of gaining a "fanboy".

Playing on the box has become fun again.  The only time in the past that comes close is the transition form MSDOS (with doss shell even) to W98 (missed 95).

I just screwed ssh somehow on the other box.  With MS all that I would have had to do was reinstall the whole thing.  With Ubuntu I had to go to the dreaded command line and wipe my /.ssh/trusted_hosts file and let it rebuild itself with use.  Geez, how can anyone enjoy this.

This is just a lot like fun.
