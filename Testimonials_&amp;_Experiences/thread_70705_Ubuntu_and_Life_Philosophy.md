### Post by Emerzen on 2005-09-30
Ubuntu changed my life.  For years my heart has been on the side of wanting what inspired the '60s movement to be possible...That humans could genuinely lead a happy and free life, and in so doing also nourish their fellow man.  But this "idealistic" desire has always foundered on the practicality that profit is the true engine of society, w/out it the system tends to degrade to the lowest common denominator.  When I looked through history and recent history--the failure of communism/socialism, the widening gap between the upper and lower classes, etc...--I was disheartened, to say the least.  I have tried to think of a workable system, one that could motivate people to excellence and retain freedom while also maintaining our liberties.  And while I could imagine a system based on "excellence", agonomics, as I call it...empirically it seemed an ivory tower dream, and nothing in the real world corresponded to it. 
Now, here's a community that produces tangibly excellent products.  Ubuntu/Linux/OSS, in my mind, is driven by people having fun, striving for excellence and w/ friendly competition.  We are there for each other, and it's a blast.  
What does this mean?  Here, in the real world, is a community that thrives w/out profit...that not only does it as well as the same system driven by profit, but does it better.  And, throughout all this, doesn't cut each other down, but builds each up.  Now, here is something to point to, it can work, it can happen!
I'm not trying to say OSS is going to change the world into some Utopia.  Who wants a Utopia?  We want to strive.  I'm not even saying that OSS is going to change the world, although it has to some degree and I hope will continue to do so.  I do believe that OSS is evidence that there is more to life than that based on greed.  And, therefore, it evidence that we don't have to resign ourselves to a "transnationally corporate run world."  It works for OSS, it can work elsewhere!
That's what Ubuntu has meant for me.  Thank you all who are and have been involved.
