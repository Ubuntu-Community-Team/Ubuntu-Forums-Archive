### Post by ReedMace on 2008-05-01
Just to say, having installed 8.04, I am stunned!

Am completely new to Ubuntu - this is first ever install from downloaded ISO. I spent about three weeks solid struggling with Fedora about 6 months ago, trying to get wireless network working and gave up. Abandoned Linux and went back to XP.

Heard about Ubuntu in Permaculture (UK) magazine, sounded as if it might be easier than Fedora, so decided to give it a go.

Having easily re-partitioned HDD with the installer and installed 8.04 on laptop, (as dual boot with XP) found where to configure network parameters straight away (WEP shared hex key plus static IP/Gateway and DNS). Then discovered and ran the "Hardware testing" wizard and, apart from sound, everything works, including wifi network (Intel 4965AGN)! Connected straight to the internet and then to desktop running Windows XP, both over wifi, and it finds and opens files on the desktop!! Amazing!

This is a seriously good piece of software. Thank you. Thinking hard now about converting all my other machines to Ubuntu.
