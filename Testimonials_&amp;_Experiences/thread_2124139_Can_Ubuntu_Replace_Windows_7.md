### Post by pqwoerituytrueiwoq on 2013-03-10
zip compression is weak, other formats are much better (higher compression ratio)
lots of updates cause it was afresh install (ever done a clean install of windows and spent hours doing updates)
there may have been a driver installed by default for your printer, i was worried about a printer working once and it came up with a installer in the add printer section
yes it will detect floppy disk, but have a floppy drive (5.25" floppy disk that is even before my time and i am in my 20s)
i prefer gimp for image editing (i know know much about image editing either)
you may want to try [kazam]("apt:kazam") for a screen recorder (last time i used it it made a 2.3gb file in avi format for 30 seconds, but that was in ubuntu 13.04)
steam is here:
[http://store.steampowered.com/about/?snr=1_230_linux__11](http://store.steampowered.com/about/?snr=1_230_linux__11)

it is not your OSes fault if a server has slow upload (driver download from cannon)

look in the top right of the dash there is a thing to filter to category feature

i386 is 32bit
amd64 is 64bit
the install.sh was supposed to handle the deb files so you don't have to

dont forget to move your printer/speaker

using a usb 2 port for the hdd was really hurting the video editor, an modern internal hdd will run about twice the speed (give or take 10mb/s) as usb 2

windows go gray when they stop responding

Sounds like you've uncovered a different reality from most people. Congratulations.

Whether Ubuntu (or any Linux) can or should replace any other OS is a highly personal question.
The strengths for 1 OS are not usually the strengths of another OS. The same applies to the weaknesses.

The Ubuntu team has done amazing things, but there are lots of other F/LOSS teams doing many amazing things too AND letting Ubuntu be "downstream" to leverage their work.

As I read your task list, I didn't see much that I have a desire to do myself. I'm weird.
I would like to point out that your task list missed most of the strengths that Linux and Ubuntu bring to computing.  It really does require a** complete thought shift **to make the most of UNIX-like operating systems.  Here's an article that explains that [http://blog.jdpfu.com/2012/02/15/beginning-linux-thought-shift-needed](http://blog.jdpfu.com/2012/02/15/beginning-linux-thought-shift-needed) .

I still use Windows daily, but 99% of my computing is performed on Linux systems - Ubuntu systems specifically.  I try to avoid point-n-click as much as possible. That simply takes too much effort.  I want computers to work FOR ME, not have me pushing their buttons all the time.
