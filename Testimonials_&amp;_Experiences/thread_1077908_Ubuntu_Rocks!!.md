### Post by SVEN1 on 2009-02-22
After rebuilding my computer, I could not load Wxp..errors and bad key codes. My orig. disc would no longer work. as it was a copy of my HP computer from 2001.

Was hoping to do a dual boot XP-Ubuntu. No such luck.

Had to go all the way with Ubuntu as my OS. Didn't feel liking paying for MS again.

Played with it for a week solid, about 4-6 hours a day. Started learning the workings. Not too hard at all, just have to play around with all the features. Had a few minor problems that I would post help for but, eventually figured them out for myself. I have been using Ubuntu now for about 3 weeks or so.

Just one headache, can't get my Lexmark x5150 printer  working.
What's a good all around printer that will work with Ubuntu?

I forced myself to learn how to use Gimp, still miss my Paintshop Pro. It's not that hard to use for editing ebay pictures. Still need to learn if there are some shortcuts rather than clicking  a few extra times to do an edit.

Using Firefox browser for the most part since I have been using it for the past 3-4 years. Also, using Opera, tailoring it to mirror Firefox.

No problems playing Urban Terror or any other games.

One of the nicest thing about Ubuntu, I'm not worrying about  a firewall and virus programs driving me nuts with alerts and asking for permissions. Felt like I was always on guard duty whenever I was using windows XP, cleaning files, dumping this, dumping that. Lots of maintanence to keep it running smoothly.

Ubuntu is like a breath of fresh air, just turn on and go. Like  a Sunday drive in the country.

Not sure if I ever go back to Windows, unless I have to for certain programs.

By the way, Ubuntu has loads of free Bells'N Whistles you can download that you would normally have to pay for if using Windows.

Still learning this Linux thing, eventually I get around to playing with the Terminal. 

Time to go back and have some fun...........:p

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-02-22
HP is one of the few printer mfgs that truly supports Linux. I've actually sent them a note thanking them for there participation in Linux. I recommend we all acknowledge them and provide feedback expressing our appreciation.

With all the hardware mfgs out there that refuse to write drivers for Linux or produce poor ones, it's nice to have a company step up to the plate.

I have two all in HP Inkjet AIO 5610's that function perfectly, including printing, coping, faxing and scanning. Delivers exactly what it says on the side of the box. One additional benefit of the 5610 is the cost of cartridges. New < $20, rebuilt approx. < $15.

With all the different makes and models of HP printers available,  it's hard not to find one that will fit both your budget and requirements.

Enjoyed the read and welcome aboard.
