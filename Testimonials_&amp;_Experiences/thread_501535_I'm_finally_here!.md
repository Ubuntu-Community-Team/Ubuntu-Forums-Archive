### Post by Norst on 2007-07-15
Well here I am. I waited for about a month before making a post like this to see if it was real or just a dream. I've tried Linux off and on for many years now. An install never really lasting more than a week or so because I just got too frustrated trying to get things to work. 

  My 1st try was Mandrake way back, then various versions of red hat and fedora. I'm kicking myself for not trying Debian back then because I didn't realize how different the package structure was. The biggest issue in the past was dependencies. I would thumb my way through installing things, only to find out that what I wanted needed something else, and that thing needed some other thing... ARG! 

  Ubuntu has been the 1st one I've installed that worked right from the start (other than some messing with some 3D support). The help that I've had for any questions has been outstanding from the community here on the forums. It's actually been better than support I've had to pay for in the past. 

My main reason for writing today is because of what happened last night. I was reinstalling Windows XP for a friend of mine. After the VERY long install process we had a ton of driver issues. No network, scanner was a no go, and we still had a long way to go because he needed office and such installed still. I mentioned to him how I bet that Ubuntu would have seen this stuff from the get go. So I reach into my bag and grab my Ubuntu CD, reboot the box from the CD and bingo bango bongo, the machine was up and running with network and all. He was amazed. He asked the most obvious yet chilling question of all. "If this works right off the hop, then why aren't we using this?", I had no logical answer for him. He began to run down a list of check point questions for me:

Can I get on the web?, yes
Can I see my photos?, yes
Can I open and edit documents from work?, yes
Can I get my email?, yes
If I get stuck can you help me?, I'll try, but if I can't I think I know a few thousand people who may be able to help us find answers.
(his last question was gold!)
How much is it going to cost? Not a fu$%#@! thing.

Do it..

So we smoked the drive and started all over. The install was done in well under an hour. We had his email running, the scanner scanning, the printer printing and all is well. (The big thing here is that he is not a gamer in anyway. I think that as more of us come over to Linux we will see the companies take note and make *nix versions). I had a phone call or two today from him with some minor questions, but most of it was helping him get windows out of his head. Things like "Is there MSN for Linux?", no but theres gaim, and we got that going for him. 

All I can say is this OS (Linux in general) has come of age for the desktop. And I think Ubuntu has done allot to help that (note again that I never tried Debian). So thank you, thank you for opening my eyes and seeing that there is a way to have all the things I want and not feel trapped by a license or by lack of funds. I will do my best to give back to the Linux community by helping others on the forums.

Thank you!
