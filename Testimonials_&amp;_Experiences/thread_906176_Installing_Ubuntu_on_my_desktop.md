### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2008-08-31
after making the switch to Ubuntu on my laptop and being very happy with it. I have it dual booted right now with Win XP pro which I really only use for playing games and recording music with Adobe Audition.  I'm in the process of learning Audacity, and if I ever get Ubuntu to where I can play my games on it I'll probably just get rid of windows entirely.
But anyway, I'm in the process of installing Ubuntu on my desktop computer right now, which is actually turning into a bit more of a chore than I had anticipated.  The install went so smoothly on my laptop that I wasn't really expecting any complications.
The main problem I've run into so far was that when I'd run the install disc, Ubuntu wouldn't recognize my hard drive.  At first I thought it was driver problem, but after a little bit of research here on the forums I was able to determine that the cause of my problem was the IDE to SATA adapter that I used to hook up my HDD.  It wouldn't have been my first choice to do it that way, but the adapter came free with the mobo when I replaced my old one, and the DVD drives were taking up the Ribbon connector.
So it took me a little bit, but I swapped some things around and now the hard drive is hooked up to the motherboard in the conventional IDE/IDE method.  I was able to resize my partitions and get Ubuntu to recognize my HDD during installation, but I keep on having problems during install.  The system keeps on crashing partway through at random intervals, so it's probably not a faulty CD.  besides I've got three different copies burned; one that I burned at a fast speed, one at low speed and a copy of the alternate install, and every one of them has failed at one point or another.
Anyway, I'll be keeping my fingers crossed. I'll post again when I make some more progress.
