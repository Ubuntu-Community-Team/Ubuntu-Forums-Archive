### Post by Algus on 2012-04-28
Well I've got 12.04 up and running and I have to say that I am really pleased with it so far.  11.10 was solid for me though it introduced a number of problems that I was never able to resolve (lockups, issues shutting down, and the like).  So far I've been experimenting with 12.04 and it seems like most of the problems that I've had have been resolved.  

I have to say, I'm really pleased by some of the GUI changes, particularly the soft browns coming back into the basic design.  This is the first version of Linux since Karmic that actually looks like Ubuntu to me.   Unity seems to be working much better now too, to the point where it is actually servicable instead of quirky eye candy.  

Many kudos out to everyone on the team.  It seems every distro I'm amazed by the work that has gone into Ubuntu.  I'm planning to stick with 12.04 for awhile since I'm getting tired of upgrading every six months (and my machine is getting older) and it looks like the team was really committed to bringing a good version of the OS to this LTS.   

I am also very pleased by the decision to maintain 12.04 for five years across the board.   For those of us with older hardware, its great to know that we'll have something stable for as long as we need our systems.
