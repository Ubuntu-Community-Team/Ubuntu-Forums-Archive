### Post by Radioman991 on 2008-09-07
Hello all:

I usually only browse the Testimonials forum for entertainment usually....because its either someone loving Ubuntu, or hating it for whatever reason.  Well, I debated for months on whether to post here, and finally decided to do so.  Forgive me, it's a long one :-)

OK. I would not classify myself as an expert on anything in particular.  Spent 20 + years on the Broadcasting business (Radio), and the last few years in Engineering, and more recently, installing automation systems in radio stations.

After dealing with Active Directory at Cox Radio, SQL for accounting and traffic applications, I would be more of an appliance operator, and not an in-depth tech....kind of like "Hey Marketron Support, the traffic system ain't working right.......OK I'll run this SQL script".  Oh, and that joyous 72 hour period when the Kletz (sp) virus clobbered the radio station...and getting called in from vacation to assist with complete reinstall of Windows on 76 workstations with 3 people...including my lovely wife...assisting.

In more recent times, installing automation systems...all various Windows products...and supporting same with customers all over the world...via phone and on site.  Include in this...the usual upkeep of my OWN windows boxes, and my 2 daughters, and my father, and father in law's.......

Last August, I decided to try Ubuntu after finding these forums.  Went to set up a dual boot system on my home machine.  Have 2 HDDs in the unit.  Well I managed to hose my MBR..oops.  With the help of the forums, I was able to repair, and set the project aside until Christmas.  Picked it back up and began playing again, and started to LOVE it.  Tried getting the wife interested, but she was not interested..she just wants stuff to work...email, internet, and office apps (she's now in nursing school).  She'd come in, restart the machine, and boot to XP for whatever she wanted to do.

The clincher was the weekend in March 2008....when I spent the entire weekend imploding 3 Windows boxes that had been eaten alive with spyware...and my frustrations with getting all the updates...everyones stuff backed up, etc.  I THEN planted the seed "If you'd just use Ubuntu, and start using it, I wouldn't have to do this stuff every 6 months".  She finally relented, I sat her down, created launchers (icons in her world) on the desktop for her, and I cannot remember the last time she booted into Windows.  Now some of her nursing studies CDs required Windows to work, so I kept my old Dell D610 with XP.  With Gutsy, I could never get the wireless to work with that unit.  I had to use an old Linksys PCMCIA card to get wireless on it to work.

Installed Ubuntu on an old Dell Optiplex and gave that to my father-in-law.  He's 79.  He gets on the Internet...with his DIALUP, about every 3 months.  Thats all he cares about.   I did fight the old Dell included slot modem...it would work for a while, and then not work.  Cautioned by the forums, I guess I should have known better.  Once I went and got him a serial external modem, I cannot remember the last time I went over to fix his PC.

No way will I get the daughters to switch...BUT I was smart enough to put a dual boot system on their units...so the next time they get larded up with garbage, I can boot into Gutsy to get them KINDA working...till I can get Windows reinstalled for them.

Now I'm working for a rather large company...with a big search engine, and who just came out with a new browser last week......(you figure it out), and still dealing with a plethora of Windows support for automation systems.  Now I decide to try installing Ubuntu on an external USB drive, so I can boot to Ubuntu when not using the work laptop to run dedicated Windows apps.  ...uh well, that required a crawl on my knees to tech stop...because I killed my MBR on the company drive...."uh please send me another drive  :-)".  Moral of THAT story...if you wanna install an OS and boot from an external drive, and want to make SURE you don't kill an encrypted company HDD, pull the drive OUT of the laptop FIRST! 

I have now been running and playing with Ubuntu, by this time, for a year.  Learned to love it for the simple fact "It Just Works!!!!!"  I have a need for XP in a virtual machine to run my traffic and music scheduler systems when using the test lab at home to assist radio stations, but even THAT just works well.  Other than 9-5 when I am on support all my PCs run Ubuntu...including NOW the Dell D610 mentioned above.  Wifey got hammered somehow with that Antivirus2008 malware.  Finally just kicked Windows to the curb, and tossed Hardy on it...no dual boot.  and much to my surprise, I was able to get the INTERNAL Broadcom wireless working...no more Linksys card!  After a few unsuccessful tries in the past using Wine, I managed to get her nursing school CDs from her textbooks to work with Wine on the D610.  WOO HOO!

Its now September, 2008.  I only use Windows when I have to.  Mainly for Avaya IP Agent (work phone system), Log Me In Rescue (supporting customers), and our platform-specific apps that interact with our automation products.   My next task, and I MIGHT be asking for  help, is to get an app we have for a product, that happens to be written in JAVA, to be easily ported to Ubuntu.  I am no programmer or software developer, so look for future posts on this  :-)

Sorry for the long story...and those were the Cliff's Notes...

My opinions.....Is Ubuntu the "Magic bullet" to replace Windows?....well I think it ALMOST is.  Once vendors really start properly creating linux drivers for hardware, it will be even closer.  I have yet to have an HP printer not work out of the box, but Lexmark a different story.  However, I was able to get a driver to work with the help of the forums.

With the exception of some specialized products such as Windows based radio automation systems, and things like Turbo Tax, I have found suitable apps to replace everything I ever ran or needed to run on a Windows box.  I am of course excluding games...if I wanna game, I light up the Wii.  Everyone uses their systems for different tasks.  If you can't find a suitable app for your needs, then maybe you NEED to stay with Microsoft...or Apple....there is no shame in that.

Thanks for reading.  Hope you enjoyed it as much as I enjoyed spell checking, and typo checking it.

Radioman 991
