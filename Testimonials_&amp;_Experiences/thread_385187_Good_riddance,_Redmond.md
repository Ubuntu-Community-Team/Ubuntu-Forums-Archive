### Post by ScotchontheRocks on 2007-03-15
I recently lost *yet another* Windows partition due to corruption. This time it was the TCP/IP stack preventing the machine from booting. I got an Ubuntu disk through the mail with the intention of building a Linux VM and "playing" with Linux, but sticking with Windows as my primary OS. I had a spare 80GB HD and I just rebuilt my machine a few months ago (losing 17K MP3s in the crash). I was livid. I installed the new HD and loaded the Ubuntu disk. After installing it, I went ahead and jumped on the web with Firefox (taking on-line classes). I was able to get my homework done!! Then, I opened my homework docs (Word 2000) in OpenOffice. I purchased CrossOver office and installed MS Word 2000 and Excel 2000. I also set up GNU Cash, Evolution mail, and a couple of music apps. The automatic updates was really impressive, and without the "Genuine Advantage" crap! :( I also love the Add/Remove feature. It makes it a snap to search for and install new programs. 

As far as I'm concerned, I have no reason to go back to Windows.
Thank you!!!
