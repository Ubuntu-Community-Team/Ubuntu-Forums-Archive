### Post by Enopal on 2011-09-27
Hello there!

Upgraded to Oneiric beta yesterday.

Actually I liked Narwhal a lot. Obviously 10.04 was far from "ready" but wanted to proudly present Unity. Okay. I kept waiting the months since and thought to myself, soon everything will get a loooot better, and then it will be really REALLY good.

I do know, beta is beta. I do hope there is a lot the official release will have that the beta still doesn't.

But I'm getting worried, forgive me! I'll try to express my concerns.
So please let me lament. Otherwise I would have to blame myself for never even trying to name the things that bug me.

Well, then. Unity may have gotten nicer or faster, but I still keep waiting for the things that seem really, really important to me. Here's a small list:

I desire a panel (the bar up there in Ubuntu - menu bar, system tray, not sure what the fashion is) that I could configure decently, which means: I want to put things in it and let them disappear when I want it. I don't want to see Gwibber, I don't want two battery icons neither of which seem to be of real use. I want to configure this "user account"-thing, I want a brightness control, I'd love to get back the clean shutdown-icon because I don't like the hedgehog that is sitting there in the corner now. (Though it doesn't matter for the time being as a giant Pidgin icon and a giant Parcellite icon - btw why don't we ever get a built-in multiple clipboard? - try to squeeze into the beta panel ^^)

I'd love to configure the notify bubbles that try to inform me occasionally - size, colour, font, location.

I crave for Pidgin (or an even nicer messenger, but don't get me started with this) in the very place of Gwibber in the panel aka the ugly envelope one still cannot configure yadda yadda...

I hate the new switcher because it hurts my eyes trying to figure out which area might be the highlighted one. No offence, I just would love to configure the backlight-colour-highlight-whatever issues. Not much that I could do about the switcher in compiz.

I could almost die for a real clean system configuration panel instead of (at least) two, one of which (system settings) doesn't seem to be able to do much about anything) and the other one at hand (ccsm compiz ...) constantly crashes itself or even the system or wouldn't keep half the promises it makes or maybe it is just too wicked for the average Joe to dig.

Will we ever get this far? I am worried that the gentle people designing and programming Ubuntu will rather proceed to create the next very fabulous thing (to replace Unity) than see to bring to perfection the promising things we already have now - the latter I would very much prefer.

Hope I can get some consolation here, maybe some hope or advice!


LLAP, Enopal
