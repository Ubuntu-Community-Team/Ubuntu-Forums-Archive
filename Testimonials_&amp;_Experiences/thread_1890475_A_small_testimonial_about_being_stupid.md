### Post by Henkdroid on 2011-12-03
Today, I was getting annoyed by the constant xkb configuration error I kept getting so I deleted the xkb folder....

Now most of you who know  a bit about X will just have facepalmed pretty hard about now. Thankfully I was dualbooting Joli OS which runs on top of maverick, so I was able to copy the xkb folder over and it worked.

So from me and all the other people in the world who like their computers working. **_Don't delete anything unnecessarily, especially things to do with keyboards._**
