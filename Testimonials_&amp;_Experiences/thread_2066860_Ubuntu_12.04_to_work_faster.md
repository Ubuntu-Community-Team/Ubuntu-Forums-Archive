### Post by Chdslv on 2012-10-05
I found one Ubuntu 12.04 derivative that work very fast.  It also uses very little memory. What it doesn't have are apps, but when you look deeper, you find that the dev had stripped Ubuntu from lot of bloat. I won't say devs, because it was one man's hard work. His Ubuntu derivative is very popular these days.  

I installed Sunflower and compared his derivative and the vanilla Ubuntu and found how much could be easily uninstalled from the vanilla Ubuntu! By the way, Sunflower is like Gnome Commander, only prettier.

I left the main useful apps, such as Libre Office, added Gimp, etc and deleted everything including Unity, some Gnome as that dev had done.  I ended up with a lean Ubuntu 12.04, still with default useful apps, but working very fast in a 3 year old P4 laptop.

That's what I found. do you find lot of bloat in the vanilla Ubuntu too?

By the way, if you install Deepin Software Center--you can uninstall Ubuntu Software Center--you could see, which apps, even those supposed to be base apps, you could uninstall without harming your Ubuntu 12.04. I am sure it would be the same with 12.10, but right now I am using it with the Elementary desktop and Cinnamon. I've already uninstalled lot of bloatware from it, including Unity.

Now, don't flame me for considering unity as bloatware. Sure, it looks pretty, but not for me. At the end, I'd turn to Cairo dock for easy life.  

It was interesting to put the Cinnamon panel to the top, autohide it, so I won't be clicking it, and have Cairo dock at the bottom.
