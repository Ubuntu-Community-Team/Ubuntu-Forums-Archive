### Post by rabi.fettig on 2007-08-17
I first used Linux as an undergrad working in a CFD (computational fluid dynamics) lab. It was fantastic once it was up and running, but as a casual user I was never too interested in putting the time into learning how to set it up myself. I had an install on my personal computer that I used less and less until I finally reformatted the partition so I could use the disk space.

A few weeks ago, I found my self frustrated with Windows to the point where I was ready to throw my computer into the Charles and buy a Mac. I decided to give Linux another try instead, since I had nothing to lose.

Fedora is just as cryptic (to a neophyte like myself) and time intensive as Red Hat was all those years ago. I was Googling some problem I was having when I came across something on Ubuntu. I figured it couldn't be any worse and installed it where Fedora used to be.

That was possibly the smartest decision I have ever made. I have now installed 7 versions of Windows and 3 versions of Linux, and Ubuntu is the only one that has ever been up and running with no extra effort or driver installs.

I decided to test what it came with out of the box, so I started playing media files it couldn't possibly have support for. A dialog box popped up volunteering to fetch all the necessary plugins for me and install them without any effort on my part. Quicktime files that wouldn't play *in iTunes* play beautifully, and without any of the glitchiness or gluttony I've come to expect. I did have to peruse the forums to find out how to enable playback of encrypted DVDs, which took a whole 5 minutes of my time.

To make a long story short, I am in love. I have always liked the concept of free and open source software, but have never had the patience for the implementations. Until now, that is.

I have set up an installation of Ubuntu for my mother and my 9-year-old sister, and they love it too now. Actually, I feel guilty even saying I set it up. I put the CD in the tray, and it set itself up. My roommate only has a laptop, and regularly uses my desktop which is in the living room of our apartment. He just installed Ubuntu on his laptop. I was showing Beryl to one of my Mac user friends, and now she's decided to get Ubuntu.

I still have Windows as a dual boot, and I find that I only use it to watch movies online from Netflix. I've written them an e-mail and signed an online petition to complain about their lack of support for Linux.

My laptop is still running Windows, but grudgingly. I have a tablet PC with hardware that has known issues (Toshiba Portege M200), and I regularly use my BlackBerry as a tethered modem. I have faith that the community will develop solutions for the hardware, handwriting recognition and BlackBerry support.

I know it's only a matter of time now before I'm ready to completely shake Windows like a bad cold. I couldn't be happier.

Yes, thanks to Mark and Canonical, and thanks to everyone who contributed to the OS and the thousands of free applications.

I've been reading as many bad things as I can about Ubuntu, just to prepare myself. Everything I've found so far seems to boil down to one of two things:

  1) My hardware isn't supported
  2) Windows/OS X is better optimized to run Windows/OS X applications

As far as the second one goes, given the choice I would rather run Linux native software with the same functionality. If it's all free and open source, that's a welcome bonus.
