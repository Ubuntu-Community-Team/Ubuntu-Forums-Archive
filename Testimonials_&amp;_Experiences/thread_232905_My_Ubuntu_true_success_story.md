### Post by locoHost on 2006-08-09
I wanted to post this in the "Beginners" area so that right people will see it. I'm pretty much still a beginner myself so I'm thinking this is Ok to post here. 

Everyone seems more than happy to post and post endless complaints that elaborate on why they feel Ubuntu (Linux) sucks. Well I'm going to say something you wouldn't expect to hear from a -very- long-time Windows user. Linux and Ubuntu certainly do not suck (you already knew this) and I'm now prepared to convert all five of my Windows PCs to Ubuntu. My first install on a Dell Dimension 4600 went pretty good. Most everything worked "Ok". The display was a bit low res which I eventually resolved. Getting the wireless going was a -nasty- bit of work. I got kinda pissed about it so I yanked the wireless card and hard wired up directly to the router. Now it works great. Nothing to cry about I guess. I decided last week that I wanted to try again and install Ubuntu on my Dell Inspiron 8100 laptop. I thought I should be proactive and investigate a bit (here) to find out what wireless card would be my best choice. I decided I would remove my Belkin card and go with the Proxim (Orinoco) Gold "G" card because the info here says "it just works". Sounds like exactly the card for me right? So I ordered one from Buy.com and it arrived yesterday. I installed the card then proceed to copy all my important files off the LT in prep for the Drake 6.06 install. I put the Drake CD in the drive and began the installation process. When everything was finished, which was not very long, guess what happened... 

IT JUST WORKED! 

I mean -everything- looks fantastic and works perfectly. I have never had an easier install in my life. I realize this isn't breaking news to a lot of you, but I'm just absolutely thrilled at how easy this happened. I wanted to share a great Ubuntu experience.

Mark :-)
