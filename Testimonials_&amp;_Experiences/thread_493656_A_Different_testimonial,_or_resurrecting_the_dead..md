### Post by Scruffynerf on 2007-07-06
Hi all.

Long time windows user (3.11 -> XP) and relatively new to *nix (8 months now). I just thought that it might be time for a different testimonial - one regarding ease of repairing when everything goes bad (from both OSs)

My setup involved 2 IDE hard drives. The primary drive is split into 3 - Windows OS, Ubuntu OS, and Linux swap. Secondary drive is a storage drive, also split between windows and linus (ntfs/ext3) (No FAT partition- i've got ISO files up to 16gb)

Windows story:

Recently, 2 interesting issues occurred. As a part of testing some software, I nuked the windows partition. Purely my own damn fault. 

So, whilst I had all my user data files, all my windows preferences, other installed applications and whatnot, including such niceties as Firefox bookmarks and rss feeds.... all went.  Re-installed windows xp, and had the driver disks to enable adsl and NIC functionality.

Nevertheless, all my personal settings were lost. Most also were not documented. So, whilst I had an os, and my data files, most of my preferences went the way of the dodo. Took about 30 hours to get my system back to approximately where it was. Oh, also about 30 reboots due to MS Update.

Yes, I know about imaging and backups and system restore, but a) I don't have the software, and b) I have issues with system restore turned on.
 
Linux Story:

A couple of days ago, I decided to upgrade my Edgy install to a Feisty install. The os went away and downloaded all the updated files, and began installing them. With a third of the way to go, everything just.... stopped. All drive activity, all everything. Left it for 20 minutes. Nothing. Went to make a cuppa, came back to find that my computer was off. Not rebooting, but off. Odd, especially since we did not have a power failure.

So I rebooted. Bunch of odd errors, then Xorg really died in an ugly way. Couldn't back up from my old xorg files either. I reasoned that I've experienced OS failure that I couldn't readily fix.

So, back in windows, I download the Feisty DVD, and install that, using the same partitions, and not formatting the home partition. Eventually Feisty is installed and running. Add in my third party repos that I use, and begin updating/installing stuff.

Pleasantly, I discover that there are a lot of my settings and preferences saved. Even though the old installed programs do not work (versioning problems probably) once the programs from the Feisty repos are installed, the preferences are already working. 

About the only real loss/pain that I didn't get back was some custom icons and metacity themes. All up, in 5 hours I went from broken OS to functional and pretty os. Only had 2 reboots.

TL:DR: Scruffy broke everything, linux easier to replace from first principles than windows.

### Post by teet on 2007-07-06
Most all the settings for ubuntu and your programs are stored as "hidden" files/folders in your home directory.  As long as you have your home directory on a seperate partition, you should be good to go.

I've updated from breezy to dapper to edgy to feisty by doing clean installs of ubuntu.  All my settings and preferences have been saved and all I have to do once I upgrade is reinstall a few programs/codecs.  I like this method better than dist-upgrading because I know everything is fresh.
