### Post by Efrain Valles on 2006-10-25
No.. IGLURBE is not an operating system...

UBUNTU led my to IGLURBE ... my university's Linux user group... I was so infected with the fact that I wanted to be part of ubuntu that I headed to my user group... and guess what... I maa the new president of the user group. ME, of all people. :)... wel on to the how to... if you feel like a marching ant and you want to star a local linux/ubuntu movement. here are some pointers.

-first find out: If your university has a Linux User Group. in my university there was one :), probably they know how to get around your university in terms of organizing stuff.

- start simple, find followers: Look for people that use linux in you local university. gather them and think of things  you would like to offer the linux community. let them say what the community wants.

here are some of the stuff IGLURBE is going to take on:

-Documentation on how to install different distros, customization tips. You may be the wisest of all linux users but that knowledge is only in your head. if you leave. all that is gone with you. it does not stay at the user's group. set up a wiki where you can keep record of all the learnings of the user group. BE RESOURCEFUL... more than a bragger :)

-Set up a linux lab. if you find the space to do it take time to install linux on a laboratory and let people use them. you can even invite some teacher to teach a class in your laboratory. my database teacher will use mysql with his students YAY :) in a linux lab... if you find 1 lab with 50 computers then that is something. PROVE that there is an alternative ...

-SAY IT AND SPRAY IT... have a bulletin board to post your linux events.  

UBUNTU brought me to linux... and I will forever cherish that...

keep on rocking in the free world...
