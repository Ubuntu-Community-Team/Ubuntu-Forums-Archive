### Post by wwwade on 2008-10-12
Ok, made the big switch from XP earlier this week and, with today being a Sunday, I thought I'd register here - and, you know what, here's my (brief) story.

I'm a student, using a 2-and-a-half year old Dell laptop. Not the most technically minded person out there, especially when it gets to programming, but enough for my friends to call me a geek!

So, back to the actual switch. I'd heard of Linux and toyed with the idea of installing it before, then last week (probably whilst closing lots of error messages and "this is out of date, pay loads to renew your subscription" notifications), something clicked and I decided to make the switch. I did a bit of research and spoke to some people I know who know more about this stuff than I do, and decided that to start with, I'd **dual boot XP and Ubuntu**. Fine.

So, I backed up all my files, spent hours defragmenting my hard drive, downloaded the ISO image, burned it, got *everything* sorted out ready, installed it all, following the easy instructions and *viola!* I'm working in Ubuntu. So after half an hour of messing around, customising things like getting that desktop cube thing going, Megan Fox as a wallpaper (the usual stuff), I thought everything had gone well. Maybe too well. I mean, the only problem I found was the internet didn't work, and that was only because some idiot forgot that the wireless settings didn't transfer over - 2 clicks and a password later, sorted. "Hang on," I thought, "*nothing* goes this well."

So, I booted back into XP.

Except, I didn't. At some point - and I'm quite sure I can picture it in my mind - instead of partitioning the hard disk, I clicked the wrong option and accidentally wiped it of XP and all my files. Oops.

I panicked. Briefly. Then I remembered I had all my files backed up, and focussed back onto what I actually thought I'd need XP for that Ubuntu may have problems with. First thing, iPod? A quick google later, and thats sorted out. Second...erm...recording music? Ok, there I'm a bit stuck. Then I realised I hadn't even done that on XP for nearly half a year, and got worried less. Third thing - there is no third thing. I still have the XP disks lying around somewhere if needs be, but I haven't even looked for them yet. I'm not going to. Hence why the title is "the big switch", not just "the switch" - by accident, at home I'm now exclusively a Linux user - and it's fantastic :D

1 week in, it's all going good...
