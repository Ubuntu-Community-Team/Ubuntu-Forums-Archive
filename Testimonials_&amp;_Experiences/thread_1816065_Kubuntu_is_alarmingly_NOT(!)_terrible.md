### Post by FormatSeize on 2011-08-01
I take back most of the stuff that I've said about Kubuntu, and KDE in general. Actually, it's not terrible. As a matter of fact, it's so not terrible, it's scary. 
 
In general, I tend to say bad things about KDE. It's bloated, it's slow, it's just not right for someone like me. Secretly though, I've been running it over that past two weeks on the new computer I've built. Don't tell anyone, but its pretty much all that I have been using, and it has been satisfying. Its a guilty pleasure, so to speak. But I'm actually not hating it. 
 
Now my old system could not handle KDE 4.X in any form. I'm used to using 1 Gig of RAM and 40 Gigs of HDD space. But I've gotten with the times, moved up to 500 Gig HDD space (which I still find is incredibly large) and 4 Gigs of RAM. And it runs Kubuntu like a champ.
 
Don't get me wrong. My reasoning behind not liking KDE before is not because my hardware wouldn't play nice with it. I had other reasons as well, but it turns out that I was wrong. I didn't really give it a chance.
 
Just as an aside, Fedora Core 15 sucks. A total let down from 14. No GCC by default? Seriously. This wasn't well thought out.
 
But anyway, back to Kubuntu in all it's lack of terribleness. I've been liking it more and more, but this past weekend has solidified it for me. Generally, I'm one to say that people that aren't computer savvy aren't cut out for GNU/Linux. That's all changed now. Here's the story:
 
A transformer explodes Friday evening, knocking out the power in my apartment complex. This happens when I have my box open trying to do something, though I don't recall exactly what. But anyway, I go outside to see what's going on, and I meet my neighbors for the first time. We get to talking, and somehow the subject of one of my neighbors computers came up.
 
Viruses. Everywhere. So I tell her that I can probably fix it. She has no Windows install CD, and I don't either. Generally, I won't just throw anyone into GNU/Linux. I've been vocal about being completely against that. Especially if someone doesn't know crap about computers. I guaged that this person didn't know anything about computers because when you ask "What OS are you running?" and you don't get an answer, but rather another question, it's pretty obvious that they aren't the savviest of computer users. It wasn't incredibly shocking that there were more viruses than I could count.
 
But all of this is a testament to the awesomeness that is Kubuntu. At first, I thought, I would just try Wubi. I've never used it before, but I thought that whatever the heck it is that Wubi does would be fantastic for someone that is absolutely clueless. After thinking about that for a few minutes, I neglected that idea because I have no experience with Wubi.
 
So I search around my house in the dark looking for OSes that would be nice enough for someone to not shout "THIS ISN'T WINDOWS!", and could take full advantage of all the nice things that come along with a $700 laptop.
 
ASIDE: Why is it that people are willing to go to Best Buy, spend $700, and not have a clue as to what it is that they are buying? Why is it that they aren't required to give you some sort of insight into what the heck you are paying your money for?
 
Anyway, I went with the Kubuntu installation on this person's computer. What the hell is the difference? They aren't even going to know that [X] (Windows) is gone and is replaced with [Y] (KDE) if they didn't know what was there in the first place. 
 
Turns out, she loves it. I didn't tell her that it was Linux. I didn't show her a terminal. I didn't even cut it on when I took it back to her the next day to show her what the DE looked like. I just handed it back to her.
 
Of course, after the installation, I did all the necessary things. I activated the wireless card, installed Flash player, created her as a user (I did create a traditional root account and left her as a sudoer as well, but I highly doubt that she will use the terminal), and put some normal looking icons on the desktop (things that I generally don't use). 
 
The reason I was so sure about this is because it was clear that this person was what me and my friends call "A Typical PC User". You know, just internet, music, Facebook, and that's all. I felt that Kubuntu would be perfect. I would make it all pretty and shiny, and perhaps pink. I would install Audacious because Amarok is a crash-fest, and is just terrible in general. I could install Firefox, because it's more familiar than Konqueror, and I even went to the length of making sure that the camera thingy worked. 
 
It was a home run. And after all of this, even now, I am using Kubuntu. It's, in general, incredibly not terrible. I might even go as far as to say it's usable. Definitely though, it does not suck.
