### Post by thehessian on 2007-06-06
Wow.

All I can say is that things are definitely heading in the right direction.

I installed Feisty for my Dad originally, as he was constantly getting Viruses and Spyware on his XP box.
He is 56, and never really got interested in computer until a couple of years ago, but he says that Ubuntu is easier to use than XP.

There is a tiny bit of a learning curve for him, but I configure everything and set as many things as I can to auto-update themselves, if the option is available... less confusion for his sake ;)

He is your basic computer user: Checks email, reads the news, watches some video etc...
It's just that once he posted his email address in a public forum, the virus filled emails started coming into his inbox, and due to his lack of experience, XP would soon become overloaded with Malware.

I have since spent some time with him going over basic internet security protocol and how to watch out for Phishing tactics and the like, and it's great to see him catching on...

To anybody who thinks it's just a corny story or whatever... it's not.
I actually get to spend time with my Dad and help him, like he helped me when I was younger.
I am grateful.

Now, on to my computer!

I am running Ubuntu Studio (Feisty), on an HP a1104x machine, but have the grub set to boot the generic kernel so I can install and use certain applications that require the generic kernel instead of the low latency kernel in Ubuntu Studio.

I am pretty let down that Line 6 is completely ignoring the Linux community when it comes supplying compatible drivers.

The TonePort UX2 that I have is the only thing that I miss, as it allows me to plug in my guitar and use my pc as an amp/recording studio.

I have a good feeling, seriously, that within the next six months, we will see a shake-up in the audio production industry where more hardware and software manufacturers will make their goods compatible with Linux.

Setting up Azureus was a snap after I figured out the correct ports to open.

I am using Keepass effortlessly, although I wish that the auto fill and open url features were more precise, but it's nothing that I've been sweating over ;)

Setting up pretty much any application is a breeze, now that I've spent some time with the command line.

Since I'm still fairly new, I don't know the intricacies of installing from source, but I would like to learn!
I know about ./configure, make and make install, as well as sh... but many times I download a source package and there is no makefile, or if there is, I do not know how to run it.

The community is awesome, the outlook is great and I am just keeping my fingers crossed in hopes that Line 6 will get it together and send us some drivers! ;)

Take Care and Talk Soon,
Hessian
