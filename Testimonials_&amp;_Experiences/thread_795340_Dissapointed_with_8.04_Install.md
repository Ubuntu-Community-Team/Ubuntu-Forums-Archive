### Post by YenningComity on 2008-05-15
I am thoroughly disgusted with the current release. I have used Ubuntu in the past and have recommended it to friends. I stopped using Linux for a bit and decided to come back and toy with it over the summer. I can pretty much sum up my thoughts by saying releases like this are why Linux is not mainstream. 

Specs
AMD Opteron 148
1 Gig 3200 DDR
6800 GS
NFORCE 410 Motherboard
250 GIG Sata HD
1280x768 23" HDTV VIA VGA

The installer is broken. I had to load it in safe mood so it would not set the resolution beyond what my monitor could do. The Partitioner is awful. I am reinstalling Vista anyways, and getting rid of my XP partition. It would either format half my drive *didn't say which half*, format all of it, or let me do my own thing. I couldn't even see how the drive was partitioned until I chose manual. Sorry guys, but really now if this is supposed to be a mainstream os, you need to make it easy enough that manual isn't required.

I was pleased that my wireless card drivers were installed and working upon first boot. I was very displeased to find the system had not loaded up my gfx drivers. They were in there, but I was unable to enable them. I had to then find out how to add repositories, finding which ones worked for 8.04, before I could even fix the resolution. Update, restart, no biggie and they work! That is, until I rebooted again and now it has gone and borked itself again. 

I didn't pay a dime for this os, and I will continue to use said os, but if Ubuntu wants to actually help move Linux into the mainstream it needs to get its act together. This version, bites out of the box, I had alot less trouble with older versions.
