### Post by TigerEar on 2010-05-08
Hey all,

I run a Dell XPS m1330 and fell in love with Ubuntu. I really, really tried to make it work, but it just wouldn't run properly. This is what did it in the end.

ubuntu Lucid Lynx:

Idle GPU temp - 65 degrees celcius
Battery life - 1 hour and 10 minutes
Battery discharge rate - 35000v (!!!!)

windows 7:

Idle GPU temp - 54 degrees celcius
Battery life - 2 hours
Discharge rate - 19000v (:))

I tried undervolting my GPU in lucid, doing all the tricks suggested for power savings, but it still turned my computer into a hotplate with no battery life.

In Windows 7, all I had to do was set the battery meter to powersaver and install the GUI of i8kfan. The Linux version couldn't actually control the fans for longer than half a second at a time - this keeps my lappy cool and happy.

So just some advice (if Ubuntu creators actually want a bigger uptake), better notebook support would be dandy.

Apart from that, I really liked Ubuntu and it's certainly the best looking OS out there in my opinion. Hope everybody here is well.

Cheers
