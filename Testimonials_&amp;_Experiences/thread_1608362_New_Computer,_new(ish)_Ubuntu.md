### Post by Grez on 2010-10-28
I've just bought a new computer. :)

Specs as follows:

CPU: AMD Athlon II X4 640 Processor
Case: CiT 2206 (Black)
PSU: Powercool 80+ Dual Rail 450W
Motherboard: ASRock M3A785GMH/128M
Memory: 8GB Corsair 1600Hz (4x2GB)
Optical: Sony Optiarc DVD RW AD-5260S
HDD: 2x SAMSUNG HD103SJ 1TB
Card Reader: CiT Internal Card Reader with SIM Slot
Graphics: PALIT 512MB GeF 210 PCI-E 2.0 DVI RET
OS: Ubuntu 10.04 Lucid Lynx (I'm an LTS fan!)

It's the first PC I've bought for home use that hasn't had windows installed or supplied with it, and although initially Ubuntu was having problems with my graphics card and monitor (which is a 22" widescreen TV), having installed the nVidia driver, it's worked fantastically well.

My previous experience of buying a new computer nearly got me to take the thing back after spending hours fiddling around trying to get the OS to work with my hardware, software and getting it to read my files from the HDD from the defunct machine it replaced. The OS was Windows Vista, and I never got it to work successfully for me which is why I migrated to Ubuntu.

I'm very pleased with the way the new machine is working.



For those of you who are interested my old machine was:

CPU: Athlon 64 X2 4000+ AM2
Case: Generic Minitower Black
PSU: 350 Watt ATX/BTX Delta 120mm AC-out
Motherboard: MSI MS-7253 mATX K6M890 AM2
Memory: 1GB DDR2 667MHz Elpida QC
Optical: LG DVDRW+/- GSA-H44N
HDD: Samsung SATA II HD160JJ 160 GB (80 for Ubuntu 8.04 Hardy Heron, 80 for 32 bit Vista Home Premium, pre-installed)

It's still going strong and is now being used by the children for homework and surfing the net. They like to use Ubuntu as well and dislike booting into Vista!
