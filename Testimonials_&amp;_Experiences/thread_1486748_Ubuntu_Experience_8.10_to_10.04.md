### Post by wishingstar on 2010-05-18
Hi all,

Just wanted to blabber a little about my ubuntu experience, including the various ups and downs.

First of all let me say this: the Ubuntu Team are doing a phenomenal job with the desktop, they have covered huge grounds since 8.10, and 10.04 finally looks like Ubuntu is dropping the "made by geeks for geeks" motto :)

90% of my hardware works great out of the box with 10.04, 8.10 couldn't get me connected to my home WiFi, and now it's just a click away, I was finally able to fully use compiz with all its effect in 9.10, and 9.10 started to automatically realize that i plugged in headphones and turns off the speaker output. Nautilus has finally matured and is now a lot faster and less buggy that its 8.10 counterpart, Empathy is great, and OpenOffice...well i had even used that on my windows machine before the switch so..nuff said :) overall, i'm in love...

On to papercuts - and there aren't many now with the release of Lucid:

1- I am missing the ability to know folder contents visually, as in "folder.jpg" showing up as folder icon, I've had some success with cover-thumbnailer, but it still requires better integration with nautilus.

2- My microphone still doesn't work! I spent hours upon hours after every ubuntu release hoping to find something that makes it work, but to no avail. (I have a Realtek soundcard with two ports in the front of my laptop, one headphone and the other mic).

3- Voice recognition and/or dictation in openoffice. It was a nice feature that i used to play around with in XP, but from what i hear, ubuntu is not planning on adding that accessibility feature anytime soon.

I finally made the full switch with Lucid :) and I gotta say, i'm very happy with it...(the app that finally tipped me over to the Linux side was Guayadeque Player, that app is awesome!)

Thanks again Ubuntu!:guitar:
Wishing Star

### Post by warjowuch on 2010-05-18
try this:
GO to your volume-prefs.
Go to tab input
Switch back and forth one time through your microphone-input choice-list, so that you end with the original again. (but you must have switched 1 time)

Now, talk to your mic and see if the input-level-meter moves to your voice/noise.

Works for me, although it seems to be a bug
