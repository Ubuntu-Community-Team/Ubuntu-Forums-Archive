### Post by solwic on 2008-08-08
This is tough to do.  I'm sitting here writing this post from Windows XP while Windows Computer Management utility formats the partition my Ubuntu used to live on.  It's a sad day.

Some will ask why I bothered at all to come on these forums and say, "So long," and I guess they should be answered.  I can only speak for myself, but I've tried almost every version of Ubuntu only to go back to Windows, and the decision to give up Ubuntu always comes back to one thing:  hardware support.  As for why I come to the testimonials and experiences forum to say "good bye", well, I always heard the squeaky wheel got the oil.  And I know I'm not the only person who has left Ubuntu because of shoddy hardware support.  Maybe if we all come on here and let the community see how many people it's losing to these problems, they'll be fixed.  

(And please, spare me the lecture about having to download drivers for Windows.  Yes, that is required, but at least the drivers are there, and they work.)

Yes, I know it's not Ubuntu's fault.  But, to be quite honest, I don't care whose fault it is.  When I'm watching Youtube video and screenshots of people with all the "eye candy" turned on in Ubuntu, and I know that I can't turn it on myself because I happen to think ATI is a far superior video card to nVidia, I'm not bothered with thoughts like, "Damn ATI!  Wish they'd get their crap together!"  Again, to be quite honest, they *do* have their crap together if I use Windows.  My ATI X1300 PCI-E card works wonderfully in XP.  Granted I don't have the eye-candy or customizable desktop, but I didn't have that in Ubuntu either, unless I wanted to give up playing video games, using 3D screen savers, and watching full-screen videos.  

I'm not complaining, either, though it may seem like it.  I love Ubuntu.  I think it's a great thing.  I think keeping it "free" is severely limiting its potential, but I agree with the overall principle of the project.  I just wish it was a little farther along on the development scale.  (Let me be clear that I think Ubuntu is more than ready for desktop use, and I think Gnome is far more user-friendly than Windows is.)  

I'm hoping Ibex will improve on this video issue which, frankly, should have been fixed years ago (though I must admit great improvements have been made; I remember having to manually edit xorg.conf just to get a working screen and the proper screen resolution).  

Either way, I'm 12% through formatting my Ubuntu partition, and that makes me sad.  I wish I could've stuck around, but rest assured I'll be back to try Ibex.

So good luck.  I hope Ubuntu is around for a long time.  :)
