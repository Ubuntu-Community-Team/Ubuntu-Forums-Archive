### Post by Soapmech on 2013-02-11
I've been an on and off Ubuntu user since around 9.04, maybe earlier or maybe later, switching between Ubuntu and Windows (alternating, not dual booting). I've always had a whale of a time in it, can never remember why I went back to Windows. Eventually I permanently installed Xubuntu. I never liked Unity, but that's the great thing about Ubuntu and linux as we all know, we can change it. And not only that, it's encouraged!
Though, I decided Ubuntu may be too small hat for me, I wanted to try Arch, and I did, with great success actually. Other than learning some neat things about linux, I learned an even more valuable lesson, I really like my system to be all set up and user friendly, no matter how much I know. Really trying my hardest not to say "just works".

And I have to say, with all it's default software, all the great software one can install, the massive support for it, the user-friendlyness, the forks, and the customization, I really can't think of a better operating system, much less a better distribution than Ubuntu (except Xubuntu but that's a fork and very much opinionated). I've always been a fan but now I've just gone all-out fanboy on it. I can't wait for Ubuntu Phone, but more importantly, I can't wait for the impending Ubuntu World!

And one other thing I'd like to note is, I'm very happy with Ubuntu's success as it is. At this point, I don't care who does and doesn't use it, it's userbase is huge, it has OEMs, companies like it, it has tons of programs, it's pretty much an established giant now, abit a very small giant, but I bet that'll change soon. I'm very confident I'll never have to touch Windows as long as I live!

Though of course nothing is actually perfect, I absolutely despise the automatic update checker with the passion of a thousand flaring suns made of crows and black liquid sorrow, but thankfully that's easily shut off.

Long live Ubuntu!
