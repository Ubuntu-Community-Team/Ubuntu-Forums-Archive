### Post by wyliecoyoteuk on 2012-10-14
I'd ask for your money back if I were you.
Seriously, if you really are a web developer, you need paid for software with paid for support.
Linux is free, most of the software is free, but if you want support, you have to pay for it, rely on volunteers, or do it yourself. It's called "choice".
Windows and OSX have support because you pay for them, commercial software likewise.The hardware manufacturers support them because that is their main market, and if they didn't, pretty soon they would have no customers.

I use Ubuntu Linux for many things, but my company uses RedHAt, and pays for support.

Expecting Linux to be developed like Windows or OSX means expecting to pay for it.
At the end of the day, everybody needs to feed their family..
