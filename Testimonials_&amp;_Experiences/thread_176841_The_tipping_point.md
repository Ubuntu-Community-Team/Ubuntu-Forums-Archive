### Post by mcalwell on 2006-05-15
Is Ubuntu the tipping point for desktop Linux? I'm a former (and some might argue still am a) Debian person, but much as I found it an incredible system, I never felt comfortable recommending it to people with high expectations and limited technical ability.

But I feel now with Ubuntu/Gnome, assuming a machine with fairly standard hardware and of reasonable power, I can tell someone who needs generic applications to have a go with Ubuntu.

It's a gorgeous, sophisticated, clean, rewarding distribution. I run dapper and it just gets better and better.  I've had a few problems with more obscure network drivers but on the whole IT JUST WORKS. 

It's really only a few months away, I think, before one will unhesitatingly be able to recommend it to non-technical users.
