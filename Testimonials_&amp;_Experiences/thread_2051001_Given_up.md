### Post by etheral1022 on 2012-09-01
Well, I've been at this for years and I gotta say after tonight. I really don't know if I can use Linux again seriously or even recommend it to anyone. As I've said.. I've been at it for years. Using both Ubuntu and Windows. Minus driver issues which I know are mostly out of Ubunut's hands, my linux experience has been pure pain. I won't outline details, just my most recent issue. I updated ubuntu and wow, it just won't boot anymore. I'm writing this on windows, I'm so tired of having to toil through forums looking for solutions. I'm so SURE there's a workaround, there's some reason for it.. but damn, I just want to get some work done... I'm really not in the mood to invest time or effort trying to figure that out. Why won't it boot? I dunno, and I really don't care to know. Windows 7 has been awesome to me, I just get my work done there. Don't get me wrong, I really love Linux and I truly home that someday these issues are not issues anymore. And when/if that day comes I'll happily return, but right now.. I just want to get some work done and battling the OS to do so is simply way too much. I didn't do a major update mind you, just some regular things. No idea what went wrong. It hurts me but I think I'll just stick to windows from now on. Yeah I have files and other important things on Ubuntu, but I'm just going to ignore all that. When these issues become non-issues, I shall return. Tired of battling Ubuntu. Peace.
