### Post by kendals on 2006-08-30
Awesome.

It took about 10 minutes to partition and install Ubuntu 6.06 Dapper which was sent to me free, from Launchpad/Ubuntu, on my Toshiba M50 Satellite, but took a couple of days to understand how to get WPA to play nice with Ubuntu (vice versa).

After flicking my network to WEP so I didn't have to keep restarting to XP to get answers/software, I grabbed Network Manager, and haven't looked back. Switched the network back to WPA2 and it works so beautifully. At first I was worried I'd have to wait for Edgy to be released in October some time, but now I can use Dapper perfectly.

After updating easily, and grabbing some of the software I want, customising and configuring, Ubuntu is perfect.

I am seriously going to be removing XP from my laptop once I get VMWare installed so I can use my Dreamweaver and Office 2007 apps (need them for work/uni), which should be within a week if I can find time.

Anyway, just want to thank everyone who graciously helped me on the forums, and to everyone who has put in countless hours developing this brilliant philosophy, community, and OS. Without all of you, I'd still be using XP until I could afford a Mac. But with Ubuntu, I can wait until I finish uni to buy a Mac to run OSX/Ubuntu.

THANKS, UBUNTU! :KS

### Post by Roelski on 2006-08-31
To all of you out there: Here's my adventure & a free MBA-lesson :-) , in "roadbook style"

1996: experimented with Suse... had very few successes
... (nothing really interesting happened in this period of my life) ;-)
Beginning of June 2006: heard something about Ubuntu
During June 2006: got awfully interested in Ubunty
End of June 2006: started repartitioning my HDD for dual boot
Early July 2006: first attempt to install Ubuntu
Bit later in July: second attempt to install Ubuntu
Even more later in July: third attempt to install Ubuntu (all good things come in three, so does ubuntu!) - attempt concluded successfully !
Somewhere in August: messed up my xorg, hence lost GUI
2 seconds past Somewhere in August: remembered there's something like "Terminal" and "Ubuntu Community"
Halfway August: figured out how to manage my xorg mess-up without re-installing Ubuntu (Hey, don't laugh - try this in WinXP !)
End of August (Today): trying to compile some packages!

Months and years to come: learn a lot about Ubuntu, its terminal, the meaning of the word "free", etc...

Just to tell all you newbies out there that you should jump in, open your rusty Win-whatever mindset and say a hello to a new and intriguing world!  

As quoted before: Linux is not for everybody, but see it as a challenge to develop your self and start to think out of the predefined box  (it takes you a 2-year MBA course to learn this, so that's my contribution for today !)

Enjoy!
