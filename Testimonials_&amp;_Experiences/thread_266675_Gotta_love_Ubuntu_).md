### Post by izalac on 2006-09-27
I'm one of the rare type of Linux users. I've been messing around with Linux ever since I got my first copy of Slackware in 1996., and I've been through Debian, Red Hat, Mandrake, and several derivatives since that time, always having a secondary Linux partition on my hard drive and dual-boot up.

My last distro was Fedora Core 2, which I managed to get into the most workable state so far, and I finished my university graduate paper on it.

I heard about Ubuntu around the time of Hoary Hedgehog, and several months later /. was full of Ubuntu fans. So I decided to give it a try, to see what's it all about myself. A couple of days ago when I had a few hours to kill, I downloaded the ISO image, ran live CD, liked it and overwrote my aged Fedora. I got dissapointed a bit since it didn't detect my MSI's WLAN stick, and I wasn't really thrilled with the software and options included in default install. (I liked quality but not quantity)

Something kept bugging me about the system, and nagging me, so today I dug out an old Ethernet card from a carcass of an old Celeron 433 box (my on-board network card died a few months ago in a thunderstorm accident), and I stretched a Cat5 UTP cable to my router. I ran Ubuntu again.

I got blasted. And I mean, blasted to bits. Everything simply.... worked. I ran an update manager and synaptic... At that point I pretty much started shaking :)

An awesome piece of work. Only time I got blasted before was when I saw Knoppix, but where the excitement of Knoppix died once I actually started using it, Ubuntu simply runs amazing, smooth and without any hassle.

My father is just starting a company at the moment. After some talk and a few demonstrations... well guess what OS did he decide to use on his future company computers :)

I'd switch myself completely to Ubuntu... if it wasn't for that damn WoW ](*,)  Hope they make a linux port in near future :)
