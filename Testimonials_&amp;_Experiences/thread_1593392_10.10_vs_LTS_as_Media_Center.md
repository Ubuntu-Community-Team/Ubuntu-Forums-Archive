### Post by tslawinski on 2010-10-11
We are an open source family and are currently running Ubuntu exclusively on three machines in our household. I've been faithful to try new Ubuntu releases  on their inception for several iterations now. This particular release didn't quite stick for me (at this point in time).

Our primary machine functions as a Media Center. I use wireless keyboard, mouse, and 40” flat panel display as monitor (using the ATI/AMD proprietary FGLRX driver). This center is used for playing dvd's, music, and other web based media. Video quality is mission critical for this application.

I noticed that on installation, I could not attain desired frequency response (at High Def resolutions) as achieved in 10.04 LTS. And video was a bit choppy when playing dvd's as compared to the previous LTS release (using restricted extras packages). I realize that this will likely be ironed out in time. 

Nevertheless, I am grateful to the Ubuntu community for maintaining a Long Term Stable version of its operating system in their release cycle.
