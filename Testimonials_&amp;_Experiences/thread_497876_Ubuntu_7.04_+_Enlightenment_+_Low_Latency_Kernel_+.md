### Post by adampyre on 2007-07-10
I thought I would just let it be known my current Ubuntu experience.

I've been using Linux for a year now, Ubuntu 6 since February (tried Knoppix. openSUSE, Fedora and didn't dig as much as Ubuntu). I started out with the default Gnome, liked it. Tried KDE, liked it, and they are awesome for a computer with very capable hardware. But I have been using Ubuntu on an old laptop and both Gnome and KDE have been bogging down it's limited resources (see my auto-sig below for my dinky laptops stats).

So I have been experimenting with different desktop environments and came upon Enlightenment. At first, I didn't like it. But after customizing it to fit my needs, I am really falling in love with it. I performed some file system, boot and internet tweaks. Customized Enlightenment and installed the low latency kernel. I won't be playing any 3d games, which is fine on a laptop, but I must say that after all the tweaking plus Enlightenment and the new kernel, this computer probably runs as fast as my desktop, which has a Pentium 2.4GHZ, 512 MB video card, and a gig of ram.

So, if anyone is using Ubuntu, doesn't play a lot of games and needs more speed for there mid to low end computer, I really, really recommend this setup.

I will admit Enlightenment has a long way to go before being an intuitive desktop. There are some things missing and glitches, but thanks to the innate modularity of Linux, you can find and install programs to fit the needs. Sure, this isn't ideal from a standards point of view, but I like how it makes your OS unique and fits itself to your needs. That is one thing that keeps me using Linux and loving it is that I can bend it to my will.

Ok, my rant is over. Thanks.
