### Post by Orixyu on 2006-06-20
I have to say, for a freeware linux distro, Dapper was amazing.
My first linux experiance ever was with Dapper, and although I did enjoy it for the short-time span, I have decided to switch back to Windows Xp.

The reason being is of 3 perfectly valid reasons:

1. Windows XP is easy to use, vast driver range and terminal free (99% of the time).
2. Linux fails to provide adequate software packages for my situation.
3. 64bit Dapper on a k8 kernel ran slower than Xp 32bit, 6channel sound wasnt working properly and there was alot of fuss involved, although I had tried alot, also the ATI drivers for linux are extremely poor, i would experiance wierd laggy situations where my computer would jerk up, although I had everything installed correctly...

I did enjoy Dapper, I must say I love the GUI, the interface is amazing, the sound effects are good, and it is a very classy operating system. 
Perfect I believe for server type situations, but not for complete domestic and sound productive use.

I hope you guys can somehow improve on any future releases of Ubuntu for these issues to be looked at.

Fairwell, and thanks for the help you have offered me during my Linux experiance.
