### Post by Ascenti0n on 2009-04-30
I have used Ubuntu on and off since 7.04, I have been using Linux on and off on a dual-booted windows pc for over a decade.

I was just about to blast Ubuntu/Canonical in the cafe forum, when I suddenly realised.....

SO, I've had some major system freezing problems. These particular ones, on investigation, maybe due to EXT4, nVidia Graphics drivers or even the current kernel build.

But here is the thing I realised.

[LIST]
[*]I freely choose, in this case Ubuntu, because I want freedom from lock-in
[*]This OS is provided to me FREE OF CHARGE
[*]Canonical is NOT resposible for all the elements that make up a Linux OS, ie X, Kernel, Gnome, closed graphics drivers etc
[*]did I mention it's available to me FREE OF CHARGE?
[*]Ubuntu has an amazing community
[*]I am free from nasty viruses
[*]don't forget people left Windows Vista in droves because of major issues, and that's when MS controls 90% of what goes into it's OS
[*]I AM free from lock-in - I HAVE freedom - and it's great
[/LIST] 

So, other OS's including Windows and OS X, have their own security and stability issues. Linux IS massively, hugely and every other similar word, more secure than Windows, if only because it is the focus of 99% of attacks, but that STILL means Linux users have a more secure desktop.

So, the next time you, or anyone else is ready to rip into Ubuntu or any other Linux distribution, please pause and consider what you are getting and for how much you are getting it.

Oh yes, and after searching the community forums and a bit of action on my part, my desktop seems stable once more.
