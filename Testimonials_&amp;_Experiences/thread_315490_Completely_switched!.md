### Post by cookfromfrozen on 2006-12-09
I can now say that I now run Ubuntu exclusively on both my laptop and desktop machine :)

My first experience with Linux came a few years ago when I first saw Mandriva (then called Mandrake of course) running on a machine in school.  I tried it on my desktop and didn't have a great deal of success with it.

The next time I used Linux was when I first heard of Ubuntu.  At the time, 5.04 had just been released and I decided to give Linux another try.  But it was not until Dapper that I really started to consider it.

At first I removed XP from my laptop and replaced it with Dapper.  It was a bit of a struggle getting WPA support working, but after a while I looked back and realised that it wasn't actually that difficult.

A couple of months and I'm now running Edgy on both the laptop and desktop and even after two weeks of Edgy on the desktop, I couldn't bare to go back to XP now.

With Ubuntu on both machines, I can effortlessly SSH into my laptop to copy some files over or use VNC if I want to do something on the laptop from my desktop.  With XP I had to battle with Windows file sharing and it was pure luck whether it worked or not.

I'm not a gamer but I do enjoy the odd game of Unreal Tournament and UT2004, and they both work flawlessly and natively.

Another thing I like is that you can basically just set it up and Ubuntu works out of the box.  There's no silly regime of installing drivers, then Service Pack 2 and then patching before you can even get onto installing all your apps.

I don't know I haven't become a Linux user sooner, but better late than never and there's no way I'm going back to XP if I can help it.  In the unlikely event I ever run into problems I can't solve, I know you guys will lend a helping hand.
