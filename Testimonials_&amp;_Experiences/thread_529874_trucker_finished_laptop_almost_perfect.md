### Post by buzzmandt on 2007-08-19
i have a acer apire 3680 with one of those wireless cards (those meaning the ones that are a bit** to set up).  

Tried almost every wireless "how to" until i found the one with the poll did this work for you.....that one worked, it had recommended wifi-radar and I just figured out that it works great.  :)

I can keep up with miles, starting point, ending point, etc with openoffice and databases....good :)

it's almost perfect because I couldn't find any good linux graphical maps (like MS streets and trips), gpsdrive almost fits the bill but couldn't figure out how to use it without a gps hooked up, could get the maps i needed only after the simulation gps randomly floated over an area of interest.  Not ideal  :(

Tried MS streets and trips in Wine, no good.  wouldn't get past the initial "set up is setting up .net framework"  wouldn't even install :(

Only one shot left.....Virtualbox with winxp and then install MS S&T in that....and it worked.  :)  Not an ideal work around, 2 OS's at once....but it's actually pretty good, and actually looks better in the VB xp than it did running in actual xp.

Beryl is installed and working like a charm and i have an image of linux with ubuntu on the front of the laptop to show off that it's linux when I'm on the internet at our trucking terminals.  Then when someone asks "what's linux", I can show them the wonderful world of linux first hand.

So, except for running VBox for MS S&T, it's a perfect conversion for this truck driver.  Thanks and props to all those with the "how to's" in this forum that made my conversion a possibility.:guitar:
