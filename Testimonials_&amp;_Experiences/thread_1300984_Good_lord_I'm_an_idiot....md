### Post by L0tech on 2009-10-25
First off, hi folks, complete Linux greenhorn here. I've got years of novice-level experience with Win XP, mostly a home user, but I'm very familiar with replacing my crumbling (or virus-ridden) OS with a shiny new one. I think I'm almost going to miss that... almost.

> [Linux users often find themselves learning bits here and there, but rarely hit a wall of incomprehensible geekiness. This can be harder at first (where Windows wouldn't make you learn), but it pays off in the long-run (where Windows wouldn't teach you the skills).]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SwitchingToUbuntu/FromWindows/Philosophy")This might just be the most profound mini introduction to Linux I've seen anywhere. I'm definitely a "jump right in" first time installer, and even with a few false starts, I'm really surprised at how easy it is to pickup the basics. I'm going to agree wholeheartedly that Ubuntu has been a steeper-yet-much-friendlier learning curve than Windows ever was. 

On the other hand though, for someone making the switch, the hardest part has to be that Linux is a victim of it's own success. My biggest slowdown hasn't been a "wall of geekiness" so much as sensory overload. Having 5-6 highly reccomended fixes for any given issue, and Ubuntu's enourmously helpful community sems to make creating a stable, self-tailored install of Ubuntu a very legnthy process. Don't get me wrong, getting to that level in Windows takes just as long, the first few times. But if Linux wants to take its rightful place at the top, it has to get a lot more aggressive at stealing MS' customers :twisted:. The Million Dollar Question for me is (and I think will always be, for a brand new convert) "Ok, this is awesome, but what's the best way to make my Microsoft (or Apple) programs work?

Anyway, back to me being an idiot. Thanks for creating an awesome OS, and I think I've found my new home. I should have done this long ago :)

### Post by timjohn7 on 2009-10-26
Explore OpenOffice before before you go down the wrong route!  It reads and writes all MSOffice files (pre 2007) and the Save As default can be easily set to doc, xls and ppt files.  The learning curve to become comfortable with the differences between the 2 suites is minimal and for the majority of users (I generalize!) it is fine.  OpenOffice lacks certain MSOffice features, but they are the highly-advanced, VERY rarely-used ones.

Visit [www.osalt.com](www.osalt.com) for a handy & comprehensive list of OSS equivalents to commercial packages of all types.

I was where you are 2 years ago... I am still learning daily but will never look back.  I doubt whether you will either.
