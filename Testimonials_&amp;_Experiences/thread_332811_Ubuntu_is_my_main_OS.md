### Post by RPG405 on 2007-01-06
I was reading these forums, and noticed at lack of positivity towards Ubuntu and Linux in general, since many people come to the forums to gripe or whine. I really like the idea of the Testimonials forum, for people to share their postitive experiences with Ubuntu. I am one of thoe positive people.

My Ubuntu journies started with Dapper. In June of 2006, I decided that when Dapper was going to be released, I would at least give it a try. Windows had been paining me for years. I went so far as to split my hard drive into two partitions, and install two copies of Windows. It'd be commonplace to discover that one would stop working, and then I'd have to jump to the other copy to use, while I reinstalled the copy that went bad. All this hassle had been pushing me away from the Microsoft experience (if you can even call it an experience).

Upon installing Dapper I made myself a promise -- to try to convert to Ubuntu and never go back (unfourtanately due to logistics reasons, mainly for my line of work, completely ditching Windows is impossible).

I don't have much to say after that, because mostly everything just worked! I enjoy tweakign different things to discover how they work, and cusomizing everything to my specification. However, the #1 feature of Ubuntu (and Linux in general) to me is the fact that everything is... FREE! I'm a poor student, and previous had satisifed my software craving with pirated copies. Now I can proudly say that all those pirated programs were turned into Ubuntu 1's and 0's. I couldn't enjoy more the serenety of not being plagued by "Buy this!" or "Evaluation trial expired!" messages in Ubuntu.

When telling others about Ubuntu, the point I stress is the package manager. When the multiverse repositories are enabled, thousands of pieces of free software are listed for you to choose from. Every piece of software I've needed on Ubuntu has been in the package manager. Here are some of my favorites:
**Music:** Amarok
**Web:** Firefox
**AIM:** Gaim
**BitTorrent:** Azureus
**Code Development (PHP, C++, and Java):** Eclipse (with a few extra plugins)
**Word Processing:** OpenOffice

I will forever be a Linux user!:KS

I'm a student too (at the University of Arizona), and I switched this summer also. I'd always wanted to give linux a try, and my University's decision to "mandate" spyware (you can get around with a VM or a few other methods) for windows kinda crystallized my mind and caused me to act instead of just thinking about it. 

I knew a guy down the hall (lucky) who was really into linux. He helped me install gentoo and ubuntu 64 while leaving xp intact. Linux partitioning seemed kinda weird to me at the time, and I was pretty glad he was around. (Also, I was lucky that I already was booting 2 OSes, win64 and win32, cause it made the partitioning easier.) (Otherwise I probably would have had to nuke windows, being a noob and all.)  Aside from partitioning, I probably couldn't have managed a gentoo install on my own (I knew how to do command line stuff because I had limited experience SSHing into a unix server to write simple c programs for an engineering class, but that was no prep for gentoo). Anyway, I've been on my own since then, and I've wiped gentoo (too much compiling and researching command line entries and appending things to emerge and such, plus we'd forgotten or overlooked compiling CPU scaling/power management into the kernel, (big issue, I'm on a laptop.). So I wiped gentoo, eventually (rather quickly), since I didn't want to deal with learning all the intricacies of gentoo (my friend's favored OS), I wiped it and replaced it with 32 bit Ubuntu because I wanted the web to just work without having a chroot ( felt it a little too much of a pain. )

Since then, I've come to use Edgy32 bit as my main OS, almost never use windows, installed multiple times because I always play with the thing until it breaks and I can't fix it. (I'm probably going to do fresh install in a few days, I've broken my themes, and the look annoys me.) I have to admit, because at school with fast internet I can do a full reinstall in less than 2 hours (maybe even more like 1 hour) I tend to just reinstall and save bits of my home folder rather than try to fix a problem I can't find the answer to quickly.

I've done a xubuntu install, it's pretty quick, and I love the clipboard on the dock or taskbar or whatever it's called because on gnome the clipboard seems to disappear whenever you close an application. But I still prefer gnome. HATE KDE, probably because it seems too crowded and full of what I consider to be cheesy looking graphics, not to mention 50bazillion menus with stuff scattered in what seems like the wrong place). (It might help that my first computer was a mac, too.) 

Anyhow my favorite apps:
OpenOffice AND Abiword (opens a lot faster than writer )
Wings 3-D (used it even on windows as a quick and easy poly-modeler.)
Inkscape (LOVE it. I prefer doing vector art, maybe because I used to do a lot of 3-d)
Evolution (I think it's better than outlook, though I haven't managed to get the alarms to behave as I like.)
Ryhthmbox/Banshee (haven't really centered on one, rythmbox didn't used to support ipod sync, but I'm using it more now that it does.)
Xine (movie player preference) though I also use VLC
Gaim 
Wifi Radar
GNUCash (I love this app. )
Genius Math Tool (it's kinda like having my ti-83+ on my computer. really easy to use.)
SuperTux
GNOME (my favorite desktop of all time. Combines the things I like about the other OSes I've used. I get SO frustrated using windows, even macOS, now.)

Also, my top "subtle" features:

The date/time on the desktop says "Sat Jan 6, 2:33 PM," NOT 2:33 PM and it says it in 1 line of text, not  two lines.

Synaptic/aptitude ( Proprietary OS people don't know what they're missing, though I'd also hate to be running ubuntu on an internet connection slower than about 128 kb, at least if I did, I wouldn't tinker as much. )

Desktop Customization ( So easy )

Consoles (tty1-7)

Rebooting gdm without the whole computer

Clicking on the date and time to go to my calendar/appointments

nano+lynx ( just in case.)
