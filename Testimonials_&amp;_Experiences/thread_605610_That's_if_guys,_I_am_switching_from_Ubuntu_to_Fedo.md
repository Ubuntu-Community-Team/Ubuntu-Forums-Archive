### Post by kojakk00 on 2007-11-07
That's it I have had enough. You might say I hate Gutsy's guts (pardon le pun)  even though it was love from first sight to start with. There are two things that I am now absolutely fed up with. I won't go into many details because both problems have been widely reported and described by many people on this forum - unfortunately I have seen no solution, just a lot of random advice from good people whose guesses are just as good as anybody else's, 

The first one is the login screen. The Gnome version that came with Gutsy just ignores whatever is in xorg.conf and comes up on my LCD Philips 1600x1200 60Hz with something like 1280x780 at 85Hz. My LCD can't cope with that. All I could achieve is to bring xorg.conf to the stage that I can at least see the portion of the login screen which allows me to login. But if I lose that, I will probably waste another two days just trying to guess what to put in xorg.conf  to make gnome happy.

The second one is more annoying. It's the slowness of the network driver. The log file is being flooded with error messages for about 5 mins after each boot before the network adapter on my Dell laptop eventually comes to life.  Unfortunately, although quite a few people reported this problem on ubuntuforums,  I have not been able to spot a solution. 

I know, I know. A lot of people put their effort and time into the development of Gutsy and  have not been paid a penny. I appreciate that. But all this good will was put in jeopardy by what looks like a premature release. I just can't stop wondering how these two problems, and a number of similar others,  could go unnoticed before the official release.  Sorry.

### Post by jamespi on 2007-11-09
every time there is a new version of ubuntu released, a lot of people who encounter problems/regressions assume that the problems are due to some kind of irresponsible negligence on the part of the ubuntu developers. 

the sad truth is that they are kind of shooting in the dark with regards to hardware support, since they dont have access to every possible configuration of hardware, or the necessary manpower to test those myriad configurations. In fact everything is based on a very tiny selection of hardware, supplemented by bug reports from users, which often are difficult to fix because all they have to go on is the user's report.

for every disgruntled user who says "gutsy is crap, they got it right with feisty so i'm sticking with that", there is another user who said 6 months ago "feisty is crap, i'm sticking with edgy" and so on right back to warty.

the truth is gutsy is no more "crap" than any other release it's just you were one of the unlucky ones this time round. not that that invalidates your dissatisfaction in any way, just that i dont think it's fair to claim gutsy doesnt work for you because the developers are incompetent, or have pushed out a sub-par release. it's just one of those things. the sheer magnitude of the project means that for a not insignificant portion of the userbase it is going to have issues.
