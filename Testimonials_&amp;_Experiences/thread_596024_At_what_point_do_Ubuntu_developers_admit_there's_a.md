(I started doing this back when I was using WinXP too - so its not a special thing I started to do when I started using linux).

I use dd to make an exact clone of the working HDD (includes system and all my documents and data) and put it to one side. (Back in WinXP days of course I used norton's ghost to do the clone)

If ever my system turns to custard I got a bootable clone drive waiting to go.  Every now and then I plug in the USB clone drive and use grsync to do an incremental backup of /home.  /home is also backed up to corporate windows based fileservers whenever I am connected to the corporate LAN.

With just a little attention to that kind of detail I have a very safe and pleasant experience when its time to think about upgrading.  I am one of those people who just cant wait to have the latest.  Even to the point of installing a late beta, or release candidate.

So when I just cannot wait any longer, I do a final backup to the spare HDD (remember this spare can boot up... I can pull out the internal drive and replace it with the spare in minutes, or just boot the spare in its USB case as an external drive)..   Then I do the distribution upgrade thing...

I guess I have lucky hardware cos I had no issues at all going from Edgy to Feisty to Gutsy (Beta).   The Gutsy (beta) evolved into Gutsy (ReleaseCandidate) after a number of days/updates....and the Gutsy (RC) evolved into the Gutsy Release version  some more days/updates later.

I gotta say that even when running the beta the only problems I saw were minor (suspend/resume failing after some updates, but working after others, some issues playing fullscreen slideshows/movies after some updates) but thats to be expected with a beta thats in active development.
