### Post by zxee on 2006-08-12
1st off I've installed many distros. I could list them all but with some exceptions those distros are the top 20 on distrowatch. I also installed freebsd and lunar but didn't actually use either very much-it takes time and I have others things to do once in a while.
I've used ubuntu since hoary and even had a ppc install of hoary on an imac G3. Oh, and I currently have 3 installs of ubuntu on this homebuilt 
sempron-breezy, dapper, & edgy. (+ slackware, foresight & frugalware)

My big handicap with using computers/the internet is that I'm on a dial up connection, and I can't get broadband-just not possible. So generally upgrades are out of the question. Tying up my phone line for a couple of days is not practical. 

With the point release that's just been made available (I can go out to a wifi cafe or the library and download the iso) I was able to actually do an upgrade. That's a first for me. 
 
I had some problems with my dapper install-several things didn't work including dial up. I was able to get dial up working but it was less than satisfactory. I remained a ubuntu fan in those dark times of trouble with dapper, and as a fix I tried edgy. Of course edgy is a test release but it's very promising and didn't have bugs in ppp.
When I saw the annoucement of the release of 6.0.6.1 I had to try it and I'm glad I did. I was able to upgrade the dapper install, and although I haven't checked everthing it does seem Great! :D Gnome-ppp which just refused to work is now fine. I'm typing this from my new improved dapper. Thanks ubuntu; ubuntu devs and community too. You keep getting better all the time! :D

Update: After another little while of using the system I am even more impressed. If this sounds too much like an advertisement-I apologize-and I really am pleased with how well the upgrade to the point release worked.
Even modem monitor, which I don't actually need to use now that gnome-ppp works, is working. Before the upgrade I could use mm to connect but it often balked and if the connection dropped it wouldn't work-I then had to use the terminal. Now it works as it was suppose to and even shows when I'm connected-it didn't do that before either. Everything I've tried works very well and my system seems new and spiffy!
Much thanks to all that worked on and made the point release happen.
