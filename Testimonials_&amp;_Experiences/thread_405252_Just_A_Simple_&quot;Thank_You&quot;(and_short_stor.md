### Post by Sup3rkirby on 2007-04-09
I would very much like to thank EVERYONE who helped in any way to the development of Ubuntu(and its siblings(Xubuntu, Kubuntu, Edubuntu).

I've never used Linux.  I have been plugged into the Windows OS since I've been using computers.
Recently, I've been helping several people out(family and friends) with their computer troubles(repairs/fixes).  Well, two computers I recently fixed were old computers.  I was nice and decided to help make these computers(that had XP installed on them(to try to be up-to-date) and shouldn't have had it) run a little faster.  So I found some RAM from some of my old computers and added it on.  Those computers already had some RAM, and so I just took, the old ones, just to have spare parts.

Well, after demolishing my old computers, I ended up with 1, 128 Mb stick of RAM and 2, 32 Mb sticks of RAM.  The only shell(as in the big box with the motherboard in it and all) I have left has two slots..... so I can only have 160 Mb at the most:( 

I didn't want XP on there.  Too many updates(and my internet connection is slow) and it would just be too much of a hassle to try to get that on there, just to have one extra PC(in my room).  I wouldn't really have to share a PC anymore and could do a lot more development and such.

So, I looked into Linux.  An OS you can download for free.

I downloaded several versions of Linux(DSL, Feather, SLAX, Puppy, the Ubuntu(and siblings)).  So far, I LOVE Xubuntu!!  It ROCKS:guitar: (lol, had to add that in there, he is just rockin' out on the side...)

I am very new to the Linux environment and all this, but honestly, I think I am liking Xubuntu better than and Windows OS.  And to be really honest, I would like to think of Ubuntu as a very nice alternative to Vista.

In many ways, they are quite similar.  The only difference is anywhere from $99-$259(as in Vista is expensive).  Vista is pretty, I'll give it that.  But whatever.  Customizing any OS is not that hard.

And one HUGE thing I LOVE is that I don't have to be over cautious about viruses.  If you go a couple of days on Windows w/o an antivirus program, you are probably in some trouble.  Linux doesn't have that problem really.


So Woot Woot for Ubuntu and Linux!

Awesome job guys!
