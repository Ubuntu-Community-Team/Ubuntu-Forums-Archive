### Post by fremantle on 2011-06-12
i just dont like the concept of unity or gnome shell. both seem like bloat to me, because i always prefer simplicity and usability. why bother using something like unity when xfce has to offer a stable solid and extremely lightweight/superior alternative? And whoever says xfce is not "good looking" enough surely never tweaked around with it :)

xubuntu boots up in less than 25 seconds on my dell mini 9. which is considerably a pretty old system, although ssd is a plus point. second, unity does not save space for me. it just gets in the way of my work. look at my screenshot, except the 25px top xfce-panel and the small title bar i losing only less than half inches of horizontal space. and i can always set hide like i did with the bottom panel (which btw is amazingly handy).
owh, and about my desktop customizations:

1. I installed the popular faenza iconset from equinox ppa (google, and after installation set it from appearance)
* The browser is opera, and i selected my desktop wallpaper as the speed dial background thats why it seems transparent.
2. The darker panel is because of the equinox-evolution theme from the same ppa
3. the kinda osx like window border is done by selecting agualemon form the window manager styles (settings>windowmanager)
4. Also, i really recommend using the orta theme, (google it, also install orta xfwm decorators) it looks magnificent in xfce.
