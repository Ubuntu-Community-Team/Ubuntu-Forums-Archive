### Post by markux^Hs on 2009-06-13
I have finally gotten Ubuntu working. Shame about the hair, though: I'm only 18. Ah, hair grows back... right?

Anyway, about 5 months back, the online IT community I moderate (won't mention names - not sure what your rules are on that) were discussing Linux. After the discussion, they had me convinced: you're not a _true_ geek until you're running Linux. And, by God, no one was going to take away my geek status and shiny badge! I was pointed in the direction of the Wubi installer. It was awesome and completely without error. It seemed as if I may keep my (geek)pride, afterall. 

Booted into Ubuntu. *Man this looks awesome*, I thought. *Well I'll be damned, it plays some cool ***^H^H^Hmusic when you boot, too!* All in all, I was feeling pretty pleased: I had a sleek, user-friendly interface to work with, and, amongst other things, I'd *become a man*.

The road to manhood was paved with golden bricks; lined with beautiful, cheap women; and as straight as it was short. 

After walking for only a few minutes, sun gently beating down, I stood on the (tr|f)ailing bit of cool, rebelious personality I had left. *Damn shoe laces*, I thought. Pushing myself up from the ground, I saw that my shoes had scuffed and torn the deceitful, yellow painted paper from the floor. The floors were not gold at all. They were regular bricks. Thunder blasted through the clouds, and the winds picked up the long, scraggly, untamed hair of the diseased witches that stood where blond, full bosomed women once posed.

[B][I]Where was my internet connection?

[/I][/B]As suddenly and as swiftly as I had found happiness in this new Operating System, it was taken away from me. *No internet connection.* It's like taking the mouth away from any animal: they cannot access the internet. And when they cannot access the internet, what good is life?

[I]Restart. Windows.

[/I]<concise>
Months passed before something in me said it was time for another shot. I tried again. Different wireless drivers this time, though. Internet works, and here I am, the only living boy in New York. 
</concise>

Why not join the community? Hello everyone. My name's Mark / Markus. It's a pleasure.
