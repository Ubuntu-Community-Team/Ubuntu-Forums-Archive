### Post by Erja on 2008-03-05
Just thought I'd add my positive Ubuntu experience to those that others have listed.

I have toyed with various Linux distros in the past (mostly with Slackware), but never had the desire or need to really "make it work" for me.  Recently, however, I've become more and more frustrated with my Windows experiences - primarily the ridiculous difficulty I had in upgrading my new computer from XP Pro to Vista Ultimate (Vista wouldn't detect my wireless card, dual monitors, and was slower than XP -- all common criticisms of Vista I don't need to go into here).

A couple of members of my family are currently using Ubuntu so I thought I'd try it.  In no particular order, here are my overwhelming positives:

1) Wonderful, friendly, helpful community.  I was able to find all the answers to my Ubuntu questions on these forums with a little digging.
2) Fast, easy install.  Easily 2 or 3 times less time to install than Windows.
3) Fast-as-lightning boot-up.  I'm running XP and Ubuntu in a dual-boot setup and Ubuntu boots twice as fast as XP does.  I hate waiting for XP to boot now that I've been spoiled by Ubuntu :)
4) Lots of fun and pretty eye-candy with Compiz-fusion (who doesn't love a cube desktop?)
5) Being able to find all the free and quality software I'd ever need with a simple click of my mouse.

Put simply, _for zero cost, I am running an OS that boots faster, runs more efficiently, and looks prettier than Windows latest offering._

I love Ubuntu and couldn't be happier with my Linux experience.  The only thing I go back to my Windows boot for these days is a few games (such as Oblivion, and Civ IV), and I'm contemplating trying to setup Wine for those in the coming weeks.

Just one unfortunate thing on the whole experience:

1) Using an ATI card has made for some difficulty in setting up video effects.  The biggest headache I had was trying to configure my xorg.conf file properly so that it detected my video card (X1950) and getting Compiz-fusion working with no glitches.  I never was able to figure out how to get my second monitor working properly, but that's more of an inconvenience than anything.
