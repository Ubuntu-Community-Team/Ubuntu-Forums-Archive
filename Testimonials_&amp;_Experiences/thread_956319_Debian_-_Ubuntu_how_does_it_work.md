### Post by taqkavar on 2008-10-23
Debian is a community driven distro which supports a wide range of platforms. Its releases are fewer but are very stable. Ubuntu, based off of Debian, has a smaller scope and supports I think 3 of those platforms while doing its best to be a desktop distro. Ubuntu patches and bug reports are submitted back to debian devs.
Now those patches are either accepted by debian devs or rejected/ignored. Ubuntu devs then choose to either keep the change while maintaining compatibility as much as they can, or drop it. So I would say it is both cases.
