### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-04-30
> **thefirstbruce said:**
> installed on Dell D610 notebook. 
- fonts are horrible. my eyes hurt looking at the blur. 


Change the settings in System > Preferences > Appearance > Fonts to suit your screen. If that doesn't work, then find out the native resolution of your screen and set Ubuntu to use that.

There is nothing wrong with font rendering on Ubuntu. If you have your screen set up wrong, then of course fonts will look blurry (as will everything else, but it's more apparant with fonts). 

> - wpa wireless didn't work out of the box. had to spend 90 mins messing with settings in Hardy and my router for a solution.

Unlucky, but I imagine it's either:

a. A misunderstanding on your part about wireless networking
b. A wireless chipset whose manufacturer will not release specifications to the open-source community.

Probably the latter. The best solution is to buy a wireless card that's compatible. Not a difficult task - I should know too :-)
 
> - sound doesn't work in evolution or thunderbird

Not sure what is meant by this.

> - anyone serious about computer prioritizes scheduled backup more than anything else. where is it out of the box. is this just a kid's OS that focuses on eye candy rather than serious productivity usage?

Places > CD/DVD Creator

It's how most people, even many businesses, do their backups. I mean, Microsoft doesn't give you an office suite out of the box - is that just a kid's OS rather than intended for serious productivity usage?

On a more serious note, there's limited space on the Ubuntu CD. If you feel the need to have automated backups, there's heaps of backup software available through Synaptic. Try "sbackup" if you want something easy-to-use.

I've been using Ubuntu for over 2 years now, and never lost any data from any Ext3-formatted drive. Although backups are still important to make, you're probably more likely to restore from backup due to fire damage rather than due to filesystem damage.

EDIT: I've just realised, *rsync* is preinstalled. This is a classic flexible program that can perform backups across local disks, networks, or even the Internet. Chuck it into your anacrontab and it becomes an automated system. So, there's your preinstalled backup program, and as a bonus it's designed to be useful in other situations too.
