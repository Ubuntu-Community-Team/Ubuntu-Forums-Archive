### Post by b9k9kiwi on 2008-10-29
'Magick' being the Middle English (often mistakenly referred to as Old English) way to spell magic :).

Having purpose built a reasonably high spec PC as my music machine and installed (largely as an experiment) Ubuntu 8.04 rather than a spare copy of XP, I was more than impressed by Ubuntu's look, feel and performance (although 5.1 sound configuration was a bit of a headache) and decided to stick with Ubuntu.

Anyway, the machine is not mission critical and is backed up twice a day (incremental) and once a week (full) to a second hard disk - so I decided to take a risk (my concept of 'risk' being configured by past Windows upgrade experiences) and upgrade via the internet to 8.1(RC).

Everything went smoothly (save for a couple of opaque questions as to whether I wanted to replace a .conf file or not - who knows :confused:) and, less than 40 minutes later, the PC restarted without a hitch.

Experience varies of course - but in my case I confess to being somewhat surprised at such a trouble free process.

Not new to PCs but new to Ubuntu/Linux - just thought I would share this with you all.
