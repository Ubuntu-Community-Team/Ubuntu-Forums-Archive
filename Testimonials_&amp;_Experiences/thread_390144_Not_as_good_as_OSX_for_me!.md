### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-03-21
I've been using Ubuntu for a couple months now. First on my MacBook (terrible experience, wouldn't hibernate, suspend; kernel panics on boot, etc) and now on my ThinkPad (going much better).

I'd take it over Windows in a heartbeat, but here's what I still need:

Reliable codec support, video and audio: m4a and others simply won't work although I have every gstreamer under the sun. Totem seemed to adopt them well, but Rhythmbox ignores them. I resigned to using LAME MP3, but that makes my iPod run very slowly.

A music manager as good as iTunes. Banshee has failed the test, as has Amarok and Rhythmbox. I've resigned to a combination of SoundJuicer (rip), EasyTag (ID3 tag) and gtkpod (moving files to my iPod). I need a solid environment that can play music, rip CDs, go to online Radio, and with excellent music management. I also need a player that has seemless support with my iPod (let's face it. They have over 70% of the market share, so I'm not being ridiculous), never crashes (apps crash in Ubuntu all the time, especially audio ones) and has support for all codecs.

There's more, I suppose I'll post back later. Oh, and I'm sticking with Ubuntu until I can make it do EVERYTHING I complain about. When Apple has its next major OS release, I'll see if it can wow me out of $129-300 + new hardware...

### Post by Schalken on 2007-03-24
MPlayer, Xine and VLC lead the way of video on Linux. Any will do (Xine is just a backend for something like Totem-xine), but I use MPlayer because it comes with a Firefox plugin that actually works: mozilla-mplayer.

With regard to codecs, if you followed [http://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats](http://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats) then you must be using some pretty exotic stuff, because I am yet to find a song or video I can't play, and I just found out what a Matroska video is. :)

But if you want the professional polish and a hole in your wallet, you'll find yourself upgrading to Leopard. ;) I hear VLC fills in the gaps QuickTime leaves on OSX as well anyway.

And for your iPod, Amarok leads the way, so if it didn't satisfy you in terms of functionality I'm not sure what will. Since I in particular *hate* running KDE apps on my GNOME desktop, Banshee is *supposed* to sync well with an iPod (even though I don't have one), but Amarok remains #1 for Linux music.

Hope that helps.
