### Post by grunge09 on 2009-12-10
I have ben using Ubuntu since 6.06 LTS (12/2005) and have ben pretty happy.  Did the upgrades via the Update manager every time and all was well.  Until....4 days ago.

My rig is a AMD 6000+, 2 GB RAM, 250 SATA II WD Drive, ATI 4650 PCI-Express video card, DVD RW, DVD Rom drives.  

I did by backups (and am darn glad I went the extra mile besides Sbackup and copied all my directories over from my home folder.  

I ran update to 9.10 via update manager and it downloaded all the new packages and all seemed well.  It began installing the new versions and got about 50% through and it died.  everything stopped, system was locked, mouse stopped.  I left it alone for 2 hours.  Finally I had to push the rest switch, and as you mught guess my system was hosed big time, would not boot, could not use recovery mode, or any of the older kernels either.  

Ubuntu does not have a re-install over itself like Bill Gates' OS does.  I could boot off Ubuntu live 9.04 CD, and downloaded the 9.10 CD and burned it to a disc.  Tried the ALT 9.10 CD to re-upgrade and it only allows ya to drop to a shell.  

I searched Ubuntu Formums, ran dpkg --reconfigure -a, and several alt-get updates type commands.  At least at this point to would boot on its own to a text login screen, and drop me to a prompt but that was all it would do.  prompt was not understanding basic commands.

So I took my backup machine, installed 9.04 fresh on a 40 GB Drive, and did a Sbackup restore of everything after installing the aps from synaptic package manager, then did the update manager updates.  Then used ALT 9.10 CD (burned at 4x btw) and ran the upgrade (no internet connected yet).  It seemed ok, fineshed and told me to reboot.  So I did, now during the boot it just flashes at me, and won't let me login at all.  

Needless to say I got ticked off AGAIN!

So I went out and got a NEW HD (as mine was like 3 years out of warranty) since I did not want to loose my data in case I did not backup everything on my USB external drive.  installed 9.04, all my apps, used sbackup to restore my web browser, e-mail, virtualbox and ran udpate manager to current 9.04 updates.  

So I am back up and running again.  Copied my data over off External Drive.  When I copied the data over I had done it as root (gksudo nautilus) so I had to change file and directory permissions on several directories.  Now my ATI Drive flickers the screen when I try to play a video or mpeg.  

I ASSUMED at after a few months the bugs would be worked out of the new release.  but NOOOO.....

Bottom line, do your backups, read the update ubuntu forums 1st before you update.  have your live/alt CD's ready.

Even fresh installs of 9.10 after update manager updates are wreaking havoc on systems, read the forums folks.
