### Post by aufan19 on 2009-03-06
I do not use Ubuntu as my primary operating system (Yet-I hope to some day. I have a MacBook right now) but I must say I am blown away by the quality of it, how useful it is, and how much I enjoy using it, even if it is through the live CD. Though I don't use linux on a daily basis, I keep a live CD around just in case I need it.

 The other day a friend of mine gave me a flash drive with very important information on it that I needed to look at and make copies of. Initially, I did not think that the flash drive being in a Windows format would pose a problem. I was rather dismayed when I plugged the drive in to my Mac and it got rejected, saying that the flash drive was in an unreadable format. I was about to send my friend an e-mail saying that my computer could not read the flash drive and asking him if he could print out the information and give it to me later, which I knew would inconvenience him. 

Then I thought "Before I do that, I should grab my Ubuntu CD and see if it can read it." So I grab the CD, boot my Mac from it, put the drive in the USB port, and it reads it perfectly. I was also able to print from the CD because I have one of the printers supported by linux. Were it not for Ubuntu's usefulness and linux's ability to run off of a CD I never would have gotten anything done. Ever since I learned about Ubuntu, I have been truly amazed by it's quality.
