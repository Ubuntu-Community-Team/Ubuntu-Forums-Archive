### Post by cautiouschris on 2009-02-24
Just wanted to drop a note to the Ubuntu community. I’ve spent my entire adult life using Microsoft products, 95, 98, XP, Vista, and various versions of the MS Office suite. I’m in the financial services business so I anticipate I will continue to use these products, at work at least, for the rest of my life. I’m not a Microsoft hater. That being said, I’ve recently had issues with Vista on my HP laptop, which ultimately lead me to find Ubuntu. I spent a couple of days researching the different distros, tried a couple on liveCD’s, and finally decided on Ubuntu. I am VERY impressed by the system right out of the box. The dual-boot options are easy to follow and setup and I am now happily running Ubuntu/Vista on my laptop. The setup was painless, installing the proprietary drivers needed to run my wireless card and graphics card was simple. There is no bloatware, adware, all the apps I need are included. There is a bit of a learning curve on different apps, but that has become part of the process I actually enjoy. I’m just scratching the surface of the Linux world, but so far I am amazed that all this is out there, open-source, free, and easy to use. Thanks to this forum and its users for providing a helpful resource to get me started. Ubuntu has totally amazed me and I plan on being a loyal user for life. Who knows, maybe there will even be a day when I run a machine w/o a single MS product on it!
