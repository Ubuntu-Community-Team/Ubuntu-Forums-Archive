### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-02-22
> lotusalive

Your not indebted to anyone. The whole concept (IMHO) of Ubuntu and this forum is humans helping other humans.

Rather than feel indebted, feel that your part of a community whose purpose is to help others enjoy the benefits of Ubuntu/Linux and improve the FOSS experience. There will always be some who (for what ever reason) demean or endeavor to force controversy into the decision of which OS to use.

I view the community as a group of individuals sharing the same goals. Some will get the picture, while others will invest time in attempting to reducing the communities value and intent.

 If asked, I suspect many will tell you they help others in an effort to improve the overall experience and they willingly share their knowledge. No financial or person gains/rewards are associated, only people helping other people. FOSS has the potential for involvement and one can certainly find ways of getting involved (testing, art development,  writing help manuals, donating, etc.).

We can all make a difference. Rather than waste time arguing over which OS is better (which IMHO is foolish). Logic might suggest, that if we all get involved in the community - that question will only be asked by those who support other OS's.

Good Luck and thank you for sharing your experience.
