### Post by trak87 on 2007-11-01
It's a user supported thing. People respond as they are able, have the knowledge or feel so inclined. I find the support great, but then I'm wondering if you do what I do and perform the essential search first to see if your problem has already been addressed. There is an 'advanced' search page. Check it out, it may help you hone down the solution. 

Second, I often use Google to get even better search results. Just do a google.com search like this:

```
site:ubuntuforums.org <insert your keywords here>
```

And all the results will be from this site. If you want to broaden the search remove the "site:ubuntuforums.org" string. The chances are great that you'll find what you're looking for!

### Post by Johnsie on 2007-11-02
Well, I'm glad it's just people you have disappointment with and not linux itself.  The problem with Humans is sometimes we're not always nice to people and sometimes we dont help people when we probably should. That's just humanity. No matterwhere you go in the world you will find good people and  bad people. I've found that the way you word help requests can go a long way in getting help. If you're nice and friendly about it then generally people will be nice and friendly back, but if you whine or appear to have a bad or demanding attitude then they wont want to help you. The linux community is no a corporation so there is nobody setting the standards for free techinical support. If you were speaking to a tech support person from a company then you expect high standards and for the staff to be respectful. Anyway, I hope you can find support for your problems. There's plenty of places to look and I'm sure you'll find some friendly people out there who can help you.


In relation to your technical problem. If Ubuntu doesn't work on your machine then it might be better to try a different Linux distro that is more supportive of your hardware. I would also submit a bug request on [http://launchpad.net](http://launchpad.net) so that the actual Ubuntu developers can try and improve Ubuntu to work better on your hardware.
