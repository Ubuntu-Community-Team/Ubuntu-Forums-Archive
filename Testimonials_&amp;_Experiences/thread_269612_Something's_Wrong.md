### Post by bodycoach2 on 2006-10-02
I don't get it. I fairly new the the Linux game -just over a year. I started with Red Hat/Fedora. Nice distro, good intro to Linux. Difficult to update, but still good. 

Then I tried Ubuntu. It just worked. Worked better than XP on some equipment. Recognized many devices without needing to install drivers. XP didn't do that.

So, with my Ubuntu encouragement, I loaded Ubuntu and Xubuntu on many desktops - twelve now. Even a 233 Mhz laptop - though I might try nUbuntu on that one (Fluxbox version of Ubuntu). It works.

I found that the Belkin 54g USB Wireless Network Adapter works with Ubuntu. No extra scripts to run. Just a few tricks with Network Manager, and it works. Out of the box. On ALL my boxes.

My Desktop and Laptop are both dual boot: Ubuntu/XP. Has to be this way since I'm in school. I hope the educational world eventually catches up, and I can get rid of Windows. That, or Linux dudes come up with something that will run any Windows program. Wine works, but barely.

I read so many problems on these boards. I've had a few, but not nearly as many as other. I just ain't that good with Linux. At least, not good enough for it to be this easy. 

So, I'm trying to figure what I'm doing right! How am I ever going to really learn anything when Ubuntu is this easy??

Maybe, I'm just Linux Lucky.
