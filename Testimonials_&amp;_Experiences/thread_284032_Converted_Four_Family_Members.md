### Post by Alex_Perry on 2006-10-25
Don't know why I'm only posting this almost a year later, but meh.

Myself, I have personally used some form of Linux for roughly six years (the last version of Windows installed on my computer was Windows 2000 5 years ago). I started with Mandrake, and then switched to Ubuntu when Breezy came out. It's easy to use, and my wife and I can do everything we need to, generally web browsing, music, and banking.

Anyway, last Christmas Holiday I converted several family members to Breezy, and since then they have been upgraded to Dapper and soon Edgy. The main reason was since I haven't used Windows for so long, I was not able to help them with any questions they had. They all use their PC's for email, web browsing and word processing.

My Brother, Aunt (high-school guidance counsellor), and Mom all use Kubuntu, and my Sister is using Xubuntu. The only reason for Xubuntu is her computer is slower than the rest. 

Generally, everything runs smoothly for everyone. They can do anything they need to, but as I said, the main reason for the switch was to be able to assist them when needed. 

That's pretty much all there is to say. With no problems, there's really nothing to talk about!
