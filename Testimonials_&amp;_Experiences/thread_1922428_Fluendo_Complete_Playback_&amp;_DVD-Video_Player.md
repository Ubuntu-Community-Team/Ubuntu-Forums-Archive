### Post by Welly Wu on 2012-02-08
I bought the Fluendo Complete Playback and DVD-Video Player for my Ubuntu 11.10 64 bit operating system about one week ago and I am very happy with my decision. For those of you that don't know, Fluendo is a company that sells these software packages for Ubuntu users that allows you the legal right to play back various audio and video sources by giving you the DVD-Video player and the codecs necessary. It is a high quality product. I watched a couple of DVD-Video movies using the ubuntu-restricted-extras package and VLC and I compared it to the Fluendo solution. In every case, the Fluendo solution was far simpler, easier to use, and the quality of the DVD-Video or other video source playback was far superior. Fluendo plays back video and DVD-Video sources with a very smooth, analog film-like quality and the Dolby Digital decoding is superb to give you a home theater surround sound experience. I own a high end high fidelity audio system and I use top of the line headphones. I own the Resolution Audio Opus 21 full stack and a pair of Audio Technica ATH-W5000 "Raffinato" headphones along with the Monster Cable Turbine Pro Copper Edition IEMs and Ultimate Ears Ue-10 PRO custom IEMs plus a Ray Samuels Audio Emmeline HR-2 headphone amplifier. I also own a custom Balanced Power Technologies BPT Jr. II Ultra power conditioner and Cardas Golden Reference power and interconnects along with custom DNM Reson interconnects. I connect my ASUS N61JV-X2 notebook PC to my Resolution Audio Opus 21 system using a USB 2.0 cable. The picture and sound quality are especially fine using the Fluendo software packages. I can notice the improved picture and sound quality instantly. There is a lack of coarseness or grain when using the Fluendo solution for picture and sound quality. It makes me feel like I am watching a movie in the theaters.

I can recommend the Fluendo Complete Playback and DVD-Video software packages without reservations to any Ubuntu user that is looking for the ultimate in picture and sound quality. Buy the software packages and the installation is a snap. All you have to do is to put in your DVD-Video disc or load up the movie off your solid state drive or hard disk drive and you'll be enjoying the movie with greater picture and sound quality than the open source solutions currently available. I was skeptical about some of the claims made on the Fluendo website, but they are absolutely true.

I watch a considerable number of DVD-Video movies so I thought to treat myself to the Fluendo solution as it made sense to me. I wanted a legal way to play encrypted DVD-Video movies without the hassles and the legal problems associated with ubuntu-restricted-extras. As a matter of fact, I re-installed Ubuntu 11.10 64 bit without having to install it and Fluendo takes care of the rest.

If you have a home theater system in your home and you want to make your Ubuntu desktop or notebook PC the center of your media hub, then you have got to get the Fluendo Complete Playback and DVD-Video player. It is really dead simple to use and it provides superb picture and sound quality especially if you have high end and expensive audiophile or home theater components in a good system installed in your home. I also have a HeadRoom Total BitHead portable USB DAC and headphone amplifier and the Fluendo DVD-Video player is especially nice since I can play DVD-Video movies that I transferred to my Kingston DataTraveler HyperX 128 gigabyte Super Speed USB 3 thumb drive and I have my own portable home theater system and an audiophile listening solution that I can take with me anywhere.

See the Fluendo website for more information about these products.
