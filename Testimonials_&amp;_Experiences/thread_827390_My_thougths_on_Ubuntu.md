### Post by TimX on 2008-06-12
Well after about a week of exploring all 3 variations of Ubuntu I will have to say that this is the best distro (hardy) I have seen yet. I have always tried out distros for the past couple of years and always thought they were a joke. Not for the average consumer. But this one comes closest to being one that might be actually usable. 

The pros are the community. There is tons of software and the Symnaptic package manager and the add-remove program as well as the debian installer are making things easy to download this great software. However a lot of that software is buggy. It's hit and miss. But the community is what makes Linux attractive.

Time for the cons. There are way too many things that i find to be annoying. If u want this to be a everyday OS there is a lot of work involved. I understand because of licencing u have to install alot of things just to get ur DVD player working as well as other codecs, but it takes a lot of work to get everything working. Most people don't want to work that hard just, they want it to work out of the box. I still don't have my DVD player working even after doing everything I've read on this forum and elsewhere. That right there makes this OS useless. The network manager is iffy at best. I still have manually add my wireless connnection after coming off of suspend. It works when it wants to work and i have no control over it. 

Windows. Remembering windows placement should be easy. I find it highly annoying to have my windows pop up all over the place randomly. I opened 5 pdf files (not at the same time but seperately) and they all opened up  in different places in different sizes. Oh my Gawd.  This should be simple but i am sure there is tons of work wih compiz and yes i have tried the center position, but still, they open up everywhere.. How do u people deal with that.

What about little things like video thumbnails in Gnome. If u forget to install the codecs before viewing a file they will all be icons. Oh yes then u have to find the command to delete the thumbnail cache if u want to view them as thumbnails....arghhh...lol

Are catching my drift here? Linux is for people who like to fiddle with computers, not do any work on them because all the work IS fiddling with them.

After restarting my computer and booting into Vista i feel like I'm am going from a Ford Maverick to a BMW. The reason MS is so wealthy is because there are people like me who would rather pay $130 to have everything work than deal with a poor mans operating system.

That being said I am going to keep it on my system, because i like to fiddle with computers too...lol. But when i want to work, I'll do it in Vista.

TimX

### Post by Sef on 2008-06-13
> You are entitled to any opinion about Ubuntu.

Opinions are (I feel) are formed by the following:

1) Hardware: Is it GNU/Linux compatible? If it is compatible, then less tweaking, if any, is required to get the hardware to run as it should.  

2) Software: Is it free (open source) or proprietary?  There are almost always less problems with free software.

3) Laws: Certain software cannot be bundled with the system because of it.  Thus adding more steps to an install.
