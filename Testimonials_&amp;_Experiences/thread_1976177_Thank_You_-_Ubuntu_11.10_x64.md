yes I'm a old tyme Windows user and finally made the leap to Linux. My first experience was CentOS Linux it was rather hard to make anything work right. (Network card issues) I fell back to windows trying to keep the computers running. Then a friend suggested try "Ubuntu" Well I did started stumbling around asking question and learning. About that time Norton's in Windows notifies me that its coming due again I figure time to kill the Windows network card and use Ubuntu. Now I use Ubuntu about 95% of the time the other 5% is just to fire up a game on Windows. I'm impressed with the power of Linux, security, etc. Hands down has Windows beat. So now I got Ubuntu linux installed on my desktop computer, Mom's computer, my old Sony Vaio FX215 laptop (Xubuntu), and I got one more to convert in the shop.

I'm also truly surprised on how much information is out on the web for Ubuntu. Just type a question into Google and add Ubuntu somewhere sure enough I end up back here for the answer... :lolflag: 

So once again Thank you to DEv's and the wonderful people here on Ubuntu Forum that help us noobs out!
