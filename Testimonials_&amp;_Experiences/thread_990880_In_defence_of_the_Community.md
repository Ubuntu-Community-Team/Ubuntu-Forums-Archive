### Post by stalkingwolf on 2008-11-23
I decided to post this here because this forum is where I see the highest
volume of these complaints.  Those complaints being unanswered posts.

IE: we all have to work,pay bills, and do all the things necessary to sustain 
our lifestyles.  Each of us has areas that we are familiar with.  We all chose Ubuntu for the same reason, It gave US choices.

read or conversely not posted in proper areas.  So for instance this morning I read 5 pages of unanswered posts and was able to reply to only
one.

This situation will only be "fixed" by questions being posted in the proper areas, and/or a general search for unanswered posts.  

Here comes the problem. To assume that a new user will know intuitavly where to post questions is ludicrous.  To assume that the volunteer admin staff of this board has the time to move posts to the appropriate areas is also ludicrous. So what is the answer? I don't know.  What I do KNOW is that this Community
provides more support and answers than any other that I am aware of.

All I can say is IF you have a question , use the board system and email
me @ [email]tech@dandmkastner.com[/email].  In the subject put "Ubuntu Question". I will as time permits try to answer you request.  And YES there is an alterior motive.  In trying to answer your question I will learn.:)

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2008-11-23
I use the advanced search and select:
1. The appropriate prefix (Ubuntu)
2. Find threads with - at most - 0 or 1 replies
3. Find posts from - Your last visit - and newer or any date
4. Search in Forums - Search all open forums

Then save search preferences. I found it simpler than searching through each and every section of the forum.

I do however agree that to many titles are incorrect and often listed in the wrong section of the forum. I think people get confused as to which section their question should be posted for example -  Absolute Beginners Talk versus General Help versus a more specific section. I could see where this could be confusing for a new forum member. I wonder if it would be beneficial to list a description of each section and what posts should typically be listed in that section - in it's header. As for titles - good luck.
I have found that titles that are brief and descriptive generally receive quicker responses than titles that are vague or don't actually contain any pertinent information. Probably for the exact reason you've stated - time constraints of the readers.

I personally endeavor not to respond to posts that have derogatory or inflammatory titles, posted in assistance sections. These types of posts often annoy me and when ever I'm asking for assistance, I don't preface my question with a derogatory remark. Why, because it tends to irritate people, which could result in a less than desirable response. Something IMHO users should keep in mind.
