### Post by Stew2 on 2006-05-14
Hi all,
I am a newcomer to Ubuntu and I must admit that I have become frustrated with it and deleted it off my systems numerous times. The strange part is I always end up reinstalling it again, sometimes on the same day. It has some sort of addicting attraction to it... as soon as I deleted it I missed it. Strange huh? I finally built a system dedicated to Ubuntu so I didnt have to worry about screwing up one of my other machines and my whole attitude changed. Now I wasnt worried or frustrated that the OS wasn't doing what Windows would do because there is no Windows install on this box. Once you figure out a few things with Ubuntu (Linux in general) and realize that what it does makes sense, it suddenly becomes fun and exciting. You still get frustrated at times but if you give it an honest shake and be patient with it you will find it is an amazing and rewarding OS. Add to that the fact that the people on this forum are absolutely amazing and always trying to honestly help. How could you go wrong? Kind of makes me wonder how many other users there are like me out there... install, remove, install, remove, repeat :D . Its on my machine for keeps now and if I get good enough with it, it will be on my other machines as well. I dont have any problems with Windows but Ubuntu feels so much friendlier and real... human I guess you could say. Happy Ubuntuing everyone!
Regards,
Stew

Edit: Upgraded to Dapper the other day and it is absolutely fantastic! Its like a breath of fresh air! I'm going to have to change the title to "The more I use it, the more I **love** it!" :)
