### Post by randomnumber on 2006-10-30
I was talking to my professor recently about computers and the topic that came up was how painful it was to be reverse compatible. He was of the opinion that the more backwards compatible software was the harder it is to create and maintain. This was a general discussion but I thought of how MS comes out with upgrades and then though of how Ubuntu/Linux upgrades overtime. MS seems to have a brutal upgrade that happens over very large leaps which has many more problems with users then the gradual upgrade implemented in Linux.

I have witnessed major changes in Ubuntu but it was nothing like an upgrade from 98 to XP or from what appears to be the upgrade from XP to Vista. Just the time for users to get use to the modifications would cost companies a day of work from employees. 

How much software will be compatible with vista. Will games play, will you be able to use CAD applications, will accounting software work? Companies add and replace computers all the time and have no desire hassle with upgrade issues when they purchase a new computers that come with Vista. Compare this compatibility to the last upgrades of Ubuntu and how much software was compatible from one version to the other. Please do not consider unstable or beta releases, I am only considering complete versions.

Please understand that I not saying Linux has no problems with upgrades but Ubuntu releases unstable for the bleeding and cutting edge to users to find problems with. Companies would keep stable versions. Vista releases beta versions but I think the idea is very different.  I was curious what you think and how this topic relates to the future of software. I welcome you disagree.
