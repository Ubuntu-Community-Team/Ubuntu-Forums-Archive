### Post by larjan on 2012-02-02
I've been using linux as my main OS since 8.04. My time with it has been very good and very eye opening. I had most of the same issues everyone else has at first, due to being use to windows. I simply chose to immerse myself into it in hopes of speeding up the learning curve and that worked pretty well.     I find some of the complaints in these forums kind of sad, because I have found solutions to every issue I have ever had in these forums, without having to rant and rave. This is without doubt, the best support community I have ever found for any OS.   Like many of you I am not a big fan of unity or Gnome3. I don't think they are horrible, just not what I like. I do not however feel the need to scream and yell about them because there are just to many options out there for it to be that big of a deal. There is someone who's signature says "you reap what you tweak", I could not agree more. Several times, I have borked my system due to my doing things I did not know as well as I thought I did. But in that process I have learned how to get my stuff to look and work the way I want it too.   So, I say to all that have issues with ubuntu or any other distro. Take the time to learn it, it will payoff in a big way. Open discussion about the good and the bad is good, but ranting doesn't accomplish much. Try some different things, there are alot of distros out there, and alot of de's. I have played with only some of them and have found that I can get almost anything I want with a little tweaking. I also can't remember the last time my system crashed that I didn't cause. There is no other OS that I know of that can be configured as much as linux can.   Bottom line, if you learn it, the sky is the limit, if you don't, you are just limiting yourself.
