### Post by jtreach on 2008-09-24
Well I called them up a few days ago and explained that I wanted a refund etc. The man was quite helpful and said that he would call me up the next day after speaking to his superior. 

Predictable however he didn't get back to me. I then called Dell up again and after having a different person at Dell tell me it was not possible to have a refund as I had bought the laptop online (I asked whether that meant they had accepted the EULA agreement for me)! I asked if there was somebody else I could talk to about this. He told me that he could get the legal department to call me the next day.

The next day came and I recieved a call from somebody from the legal department. They tried their best to warn me off the idea using excuses about the warranty being voided (but only if ubuntu caused the problem). She then asked if she could call me back after talking to someone else. Five minutes later she called back and told me that they would give me a refund, all I had to do was email her saying that I accept that if Ubuntu causes a fault with the hardware then the warranty is void and I had to promise not to use my copy of Vista (they didn't actually want the disk back or any other evidence which is very trusting of them).


Long story short. Don't give up, keep quoting the EULA and ask to speak to somebody higher up if the person on the other end obviously doesn't know what there talking about. Good luck!
