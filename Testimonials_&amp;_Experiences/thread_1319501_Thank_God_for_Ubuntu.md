### Post by envalin on 2009-11-08
My roommate has a mock trial competition today, fairly big. Last night he texts me that Windows Vista was not working and wondering if when I got back I could load up Linux for him until his Windows 7 disk came, because he needed his documents for today. When I looked at it, Windows was indeed pretty corrupt, it got a massive virus that got through our university's security system, which is supposedly top notch compared to most universities.

Long story short, ATI cards suck, Acer partitions their drives in a completely nonsensical way (which meant I could not dual boot because they used up all 4 of the possible partitions), so he is completely Windows free for a bit after wiping the hard drive and putting a nice clean Ubuntu install on it with all his documents on it. Before leaving, I showed him the basics of how it works, as he is not very tech savvy at all (not knowing how to even make an excel spreadsheet yet in college). He loved the interface and how easily it connected to our network, running Linux automatically gives you an extra 3 bars of Wifi connectivity on campus because there is no need to run the extra security programs on it. Add on the wobbly windows effect and the multiple desktops that are there by default, and he has said he's not even sure if he'll want to be running his free upgrade to Windows 7, especially based on the 24 second boot time for Ubuntu.

For someone that "just needs something to be able to type with," Ubuntu has been working great, and he has been throroughly impressed with even the color scheme present in 9.10. So thank you for Ubuntu and its ease of installation to save the day!

### Post by Zoot7 on 2009-11-08
@envalin good work; your friend is a lot better off now and thanks for sharing! :)

I had a similar experience with my Dad. He's very computer illiterate, and is the classic example of someone who just wants the machine to work when he turns it on. He just uses his machine for Word processing, skype, internet browsing and movies/music.
XP strangely enough kept doing odd things, I ended up getting phone calls and in in one case actually having to drive home to help him rescue pretty critical files shortly before they were really needed. But the problem was I'd no sooner fix it and it'd act up again. The oddities were stuff like Services refusing to start, applications suddenly refusing to work, wireless randomly ceasing and refusing to reconnect without a reboot.
Anyway, enter Ubuntu. I installed Hardy on his Desktop, I was a little hesitant to do so since it involved me using ndiswrapper to get his wireless PCI card up and running. But I set him up and installed everything he needed. Everything worked pretty much flawlessly including the printer which surprised me, and also the wireless despite having to use ndiswrapper.
Anyway fast forward 6 months, and I haven't heard from him for IT problems since I let him loose on Hardy. He's now working away with Openoffice, playing his DVDs on vlc, playing his music with Rhythmbox and using skype away fine.

He's one of quite a few people I've converted.
