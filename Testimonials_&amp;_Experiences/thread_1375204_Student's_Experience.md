### Post by kjah on 2010-01-07
Due to some bad weather I was unable to bring my main computer back to university with me, however, I did have an old Advent 7087 laptop that I picked up used a couple of years ago, it was running Windows XP Home Edition, albeit not very well. I've played around with Linux before and decided I would give Ubuntu a try this time around.

So far the experience has been good, the only downside being that my wireless doesn't work which really isn't that much of a problem as I normally use a hard wired broadband connection.

What I like most about the system is how easy it is to add in missing functionality. The most recent example being that I couldn't play store bought DVDs (District 9 to be exact, good film btw), a quick search on Google brought up the necessary steps to resolve the issue and after installing VLC and the css package (can't remember the specifics) I was able to play the movie. Same thing with MP3 playback, but the system found its own plug-ins and I'm now able to listen to music from my iPod (the collection is from my main computer).

Another thing I like is that Ubuntu has effectively breathed new life into this archaic machine, it runs like a champ now whereas before it ran like a crippled dog and took ages to do, well, anything.

In conclusion I'd just like to say a big thank you to the Ubuntu developers and I hope that this excellent software continues to improve. If my course didn't require me to use propriety software I would probably use Ubuntu as my main OS.
