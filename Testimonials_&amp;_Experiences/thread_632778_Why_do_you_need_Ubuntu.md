### Post by Seti on 2007-12-05
Not so long ago I had work as a student computer-labs and help-desk attendant, and part of the deal was they let me use a nice newer Dell laptop during the contract. The first day we got them (my collegue and I) we were supposed to image them (with Norton Ghost) with XP on them. We did that. The next morning, the first thing I did, right under the boss' eyes, was install Ubuntu. 
(I must say here that he had already hinted it might be OK when I talked about it. )
So anyway the laptop (with linux on it) quickly became an invaluable tool in my job around the labs. It was even frequently used for network troubleshooting, communication and even data archiving during the job. Pretty much two days later my collegue also installed Ubuntu on his identical laptop. The other technicians in computer-services quickly had their eyes popping out of their heads at stuff like Gnome and (at that time) Beryl, to the point that a couple of them actually came to me privately and sheepishly asked about how hard it was to install. 
Our manager laughed at us. We covetted his mighty XPS.
An older guy working in backend teased us, calling us open-source hippy freaks. (he later became useful at giving advice and assistance at setting up the mysql stuff).
Before long the head manager came to us one day if we'd be up to setting up a server for him; he needed something that would serve a single college website. The side was designed to be a questionnaire of sorts for new students. We were given the choice of a new machine in storage, given some ancient Red Hat discs, and told to go to it.
Well as you can imagine, Ubuntu was promptly installed, along with apache, PHP... a basic LAMP. The machine was updated, security was set up, and before long it was given a permanent IP address and put into production. 
I actually felt a little bit sad when finally one day I went to ssh to it and found they had changed the password on me.
So you see, from the start linux was a success at my workplace with me using it. My boss quickly saw me being productive, its usefulness was obvious, and I didn't have to agree to any weird (and possibly illegal) EULA's along the way.
Cheers
