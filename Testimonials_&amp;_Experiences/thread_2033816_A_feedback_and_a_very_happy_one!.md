### Post by Chdslv on 2012-07-27
I've been using Ubuntu for years, and never had any problems. I visited these forums, but haven't asked a question yet. I didn't find any problems with Ubuntu. Everything worked. Of course, I checked all other distros, installed some, played with them, but returned to Ubuntu. It was my main distro. Few years ago, I discovered Ultimate Edition, then Onyx 64, later Black Opal 64.  All these had eye candy and blings.  
 
One day, I thought I'd play with my Ubuntu 12.04 download in April. I installed Compiz-Fusion, and then Cairo Dock. like Cairo dock, so I usually install it. Once the Cairo dock was installed, there were extra DEs *automatically* installed - Cairo with Gnome+effects, Cairo with Gnome, but without effects and Cairo with Unity+effects, Gnome and Gnome Classic. Then I used a ppa to install Cinnamon. Every DE worked like a charm. I also found that Ubuntu 12.04 is snappier than the above mentioned distros&#8212;I'd say remixes-- and Linux Mint 13 Cinnamon.
 
I filled the necessary boxes in the Compiz Fusion Settings Manager, and I got the eye candy and the blings. Do I need to distro hopping to find the best remixed Ubuntu, or is it better to search around and add icons, themes, applications, etc., and get my Ubuntu working the way I liked, I thought. Well, it is good to go distro hopping to learn something, but one can really make one's Ubuntu, the way one likes it. 

CCSM gave me all the eye candy I need and the blings. Then I went to Gnome-look org and Gnome Art and searched for the themes and icons. I have a liking for the Onyx 64 and its theme/icons. I found them there, and now I have Ubuntu 12.04 64bit with the Onyx 64 look.  

What I found interesting is that a user, practically a noob, could get where he wants with Ubuntu 12.04. I did all those installations, while doing my work in Ubuntu.

Now, I use Cairo (with Gnome+effects), Cinnamon, Gnome and Unity from time to time, so I won't lose contact with the known DEs.  
 
Looking back, I find there are energetic people, who make Ubuntu remixes by either deleting some applications, icons, themes, wallpaper, etc or adding some, making other distros out of Ubuntu. Good for them! There work to be appreciated.

I think of Ubuntu as a operating system, not as a distribution. What's underneath Ubuntu works without troubling the user, and gives him freedom to work without a hitch.
 
Thank you, guys for the well done operating system!:D

### Post by Chdslv on 2012-07-28
I was wondering why Zorin OS 6 Ultimate is given for money. Zorin 6 core was free, so I thought of checking it out.

Zorin comes with a look changer and Zorin Desktop, which cannot be downloaded free from its repos, so they are of commercial value. But, how to change Ubuntu to look like Windows 7 can be found in the Internet, if one is dying for Windows 7 look. I am not asking anyone to change Linux to look like Windows, so I won't give the link here. Linux is Linux and Windows is Windows.

The bottom panel in Zorin 6 is AWN dock.  I installed Cairo dock, which pulled out the Gnome-shell from within. Logging out and opening Cairo with Gnome+effects gave the so-called MacOs look. The so-called Zorin desktop was also there (with AWN dock) and the Windows 7 looking menu. Maybe for some it might look nice, but there are *so many* menus in Linux world, so why go for Windows 7 menu?

Of course, Unity is taken off Ubuntu 12.04 to make Zorin Os 6, so there isn't Unity and Cairo with Unity+effects. One can, of course, install Unity to get that back. *But, why go to that much trouble to get a Windows 7 look on excellent Ubuntu 12.04?*

Ultimate Edition and the Black Opal 64 are the most eye-candy and blings remixes done with Ubuntu in the back. They haven't taken Unity off, and Gnome is just underneath and would come out by installing Cairo dock. 

Can I install Cinnamon to Zorin Os 6? Yes, I can. Can I install E17 to Zorin Os 6? Yes I can. They are in the PPA.

Clem Lefebvre had done a marvellous job by making Cinnamon DE, and it is given free to all. Ultimate Edition 3.4 and Black Opal 64 are free and have all the eye-candy and the blings. 

Ubuntu 12.04 is such an excellent OS, you can install all the DEs in it and all the eye-candy and the blings too, [I]thanks to all the developers and the community of the Linux world.

[/I]So, I wonder why Artem Zorin cannot offer the "look changer" and the "Windows 7 menu" to the Linux world as Clem had done with Cinnamon DE. I have not checked, but someone might've already made a ppafor those.

Cheers!:D
