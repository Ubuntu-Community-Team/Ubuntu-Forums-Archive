### Post by Antman on 2008-08-18
In April I recall when I came to the rescue of my friend (they call me for PC help at least every other month). Their XP machine was taking 20 minutes to boot, 8 minutes to go from one window to the next, pop-ups on the screen, etc, etc.

The last time I was there (sometime in March) I reinstalled their copy of WinXP and removed admin rights for the normal accounts. Well, looks like that wasn't good enough since the machine was virus and spy-ware infested again.](*,) 
They have since lost their original XP CD which was good since I really didn't want to install it again.

Well, Hardy was just released at that time and I received my CD's in the mail so I could pass them out to friends and clients as needed. I asked if they needed anything on the HD and they said no... so I blew away WinXP and installed Ubuntu Hardy. 

I gave the family a quick run down on how everything worked. Scanner, printer, and internet all worked. The only thing that didn&#8217;t work was their floppy drive. 

This was at the end of April and I haven't heard a word from them as far as computer issues go.... :guitar:


PS: Even though they only had 512mb of ram, Ubuntu was running fine. If I had Xubuntu on me I would have loaded that instead, but I think Ubuntu looks more user friendly (although Xubuntu is designed almost like it).

Edit: I would have loved to let them try Zenwalk on their machine. I just didn't have any spare blank CD's in order to burn them a copy at the time. :(
