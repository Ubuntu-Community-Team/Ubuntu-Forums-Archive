### Post by Ubunted on 2006-09-27
My first laptop was an IBM ThinkPad A22m. 14.1" screen, P3 1Ghz, 512MB and an 80GB hard drive after upgrades, no onboard wireless. 2000 Pro and XP both worked reasonably well, detecting and installing pretty much everything via Windows Update, then the IBM System Update utility for the IBM-certified drivers and such.

I sold it a while ago, and today I picked up its successor - a much smaller ThinkPad X30. 12" 1.2Ghz P3, 512MB upgradeable to 1GB, a 20Gb drive (getting the boot ASAP) and an onboard 802.11g card of unknown make.

XP Professional refuses to detect the wireless card, the video adapter (Intel integrated, nothing special whatsoever) and a number of other things. Furthermore, the IBM updater cannot even detect what kind of laptop it's on to GET the software and I can't see the model type anywhere to even manually download them. So, fed up with WGA, an SP2 install that took over an hour and no wireless, I stuck in the latest Ubuntu Dapper.

Not only is it faster overall, it installed quicker, updated in a fraction of the time and detected everything, including the wireless. Even the dock works perfectly, which is good because the thing is so small there's no optical drive in the unit.

Another win for Ubuntu.
