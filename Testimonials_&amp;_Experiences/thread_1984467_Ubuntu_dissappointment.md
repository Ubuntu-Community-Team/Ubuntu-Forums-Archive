### Post by Radioman991 on 2012-05-21
My 0.02

Running various flavors of Ubuntu since Gutsy, has made me a full convert.  I have generally found each release has been an improvement....until now.

I do not have the latest and greatest of hardware.  I despise the Microsoft plan of "forced obsolesces" that seems to happen each OS release..   I am running a Dell Latitude d610  lappy, and old P4 desktop with 2 gig of RAM, as well as the wife's old Dell Celeron machine, and toss in a Acer netbook or 2.  After playing the distro-hop game, and then finally just sticking to the LTS releases, until 12.04 came out, each release either improved on issues, or fixed things that didn't quite work as intended previously.

This is no longer the case.

1)   I have tried to like Unity.  Its "dumbed down" the system in my opinion.   Aspects that used to be available to tweak, or customize, are no longer there...or buried in such a way as to make it impossible to locate the settings.  Unity makes me work slower...as I cannot easily get to things that used to be available.  In fact, I think it's garbage.

2) To avoid Unity, I have tried various flavors of Ubuntu....such as Xubuntu, or Lubuntu.  I simply cannot get a unified version to play nicely on all my machines.  I would rather strive for version and platform consistency as I support my family's machines.  I am having no luck with <insert name here> 12.04.

On my Dell D610, I cannot get dual screens to work properly....either with Xubuntu or with Lubuntu.   My only recourse is to remain on 10.04.  On my old P4, I can get dual screens to work with Lubuntu and my PCI nVidia card....but no other derivative of Ubuntu 12.04 will work properly.   This is frustrating and insane to have to deal with. These issues have forced me to abandon "upgrading" my netbooks and my wife's PC to anything beyond 10.04.

I cringe to think of "upgrading" my server.

So I remain in the land of  10.04....until the end of time it would seem, as the 12.04 versions I have tried seem to be more of a  downgrade.

Regards,

Radioman991

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-05-25
Unity is not "dumbed down", it's just a radical change from the classic desktop you know and obviously love.

Unity gets rid of a lot of the old cruft (Gnome panel, notification area, etc) and redesigns aspects of the desktop to generally make more sense. Sure, there's a period of transition and relearning for existing Ubuntu users, but if you keep your mind open you'll probably come to appreciate Unity. It has some very, very helpful features. If you remember the bewilderment you experienced when you first used Microsoft Office 2007, then you'd probably think HUD is the best thing since sliced bread :-)
