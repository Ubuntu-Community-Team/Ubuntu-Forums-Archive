### Post by php_penguin on 2007-06-24
Well, i tried Fedora 7 first, and my ethernet (wired & wireless combined) card wasn't detected at all.

So i tried Ubuntu, and i got my wired in straight off and did the updates and installing loads of free stuff (who can resist?). NTFS shares worked straight off, as did my monitor, and evrything else.

My wireless took me a while to figure out. I was so used to windows that i kept right clicking the icon in the taskbar, and that wouldn't let me add networks or connect to them. Took me a full month to figure out that left clicking was the answer!

So anyway, the overall experience has been great, and the keypoints are as follows:
 - There are lots of programs out there that work, and work well, and for the times that it fails you, WINE works most of the time. I don't like GIMP (but i don't like Photoshop either), so i use my trusty Paint Shop Pro 7 disk.
 - For me, most things work, and those that don't, don't bother me. My touchpad's sensitivity can't be adjusted (it zooms about the screen), but i have got used to it now.
 - Ubuntu is a good Family Desktop. Notice those Mac vc PC ad's recently? Windows is good for accountants and such, and Ubuntu is like a free Mac without the $100 upgrades and the fanboys going on about how much they find their machines sexy.

Their seems to be alot of complaining about f all, and it mostly seems unjustified. That said, I have windows on the dual boot in case of emergencies. Haven't used it for the last 2 months.

That's my experience anyway, Painless all the way.
