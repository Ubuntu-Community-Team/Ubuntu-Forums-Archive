### Post by blazemore on 2010-02-04
After using Karmic for a week, I had noticed a number of problems:
My printer wasn't detected out of the box (It is in Hardy)
I couldn't listen to sounds from 2 apps at once (I can on Hardy)
X would just freeze up occasionally, forcing me to reboot (It doesn't on Hardy)
Annoyance: When watching a fullscreen Flash video, and changing the volume with the keyboard, it would come out of fullscreen. (Different notification system on Hardy doesn't have this problem)

Not that karmic was a bad distro, I really liked it. It's just that there's nothing I can do on it that I can't on Hardy, and it doesn't crash as often.

I also have Lucid running on bare metal (not virtualised) and I boot into it occasionally to see what's been going on there. Lucid looks like it's shaping up to be a really good release. I haven't even had to install proprietary (ATI) graphics card drivers; everything just worked out the box! So I'll probably do a fresh install on release day, overwriting the development release.

I do like to do a lot of distro hopping, but I think I'll be keeping my 8.04 around for a long time, especially since I use it for Java, Haskell and Python development for my degree and don't want to mess around too much on it.
