### Post by kevCast on 2008-10-01
I left the Ubuntu camp after being a member since Edgy. I made a trek across the Linux landscape. I installed Debian, then Slackware, then Arch, then back to Debian, and now I'm here again. I went to Debian because of the instability of the first 8.04 release after months of anticipation. Disgusted by the huge amounts of RAM usage, it was nice to see Debian using half the memory as Ubuntu. Out of nostalgia, I came back to Ubuntu to see if things had improved. I downloaded the 8.04.1 CD, installed it, performed the updates, and booted into my new system. *Wow!* I forgot the polish of this system. My RAM usage was actually *less* than in Debian. No need to compile emerald, troubleshoot the YouTube plugin for Totem, hunt down proprietary (but necessary) software, etc. All there, just simple desktop usage. No headaches. Perfectly balancing FSF, OSS, and user restrictive software, and prompting competition between the various idea camps.

From an appreciative user, good job Ubuntu team.
