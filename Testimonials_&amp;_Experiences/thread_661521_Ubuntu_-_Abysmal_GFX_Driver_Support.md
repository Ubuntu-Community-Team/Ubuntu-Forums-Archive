### Post by Nytron on 2008-01-08
So I decided to give Ubuntu a chance a couple of months ago.

The first computer I tried it on was my Dell XPS2 laptop, with a 6800 go Ultra graphics card. I could not get 3d effects to work on it to save my life. At first I thought it was user error, but searching around on google (seems I have to do this too often with Ubuntu) confirmed that indeed there is absolutely no way for this card to run in 3D under Ubuntu, period. It was stuck with the default drivers. I was absolutely furious. This card isn't particularly old or uncommon. Reformatted and installed XP SP2 and haven't looked back.

Fast forward to tonight. I decided to give Ubuntu a second chance on a brand new machine I just built. High end Asus mobo with an e2160 overclocked to 3GHz, 4GB Ram, 500GB HD, and a Radeon HD 3850, etc. This is all up-to-date hardware, there shouldn't be any issues with it on Ubuntu like I had on my laptop, right?

Wrong. Turns out there is no way for my modern Radeon HD 3850 to run in 3D either (which I confirmed after extensively searching the internet, again). Yes, I tried Phoronix.com's method with the beta radeonhd drivers. Didn't work at all. Reformatted and installed Vista x64 Ultimate (which I thought was probably the worst operating system on earth in the "bad driver support" category, but Ubuntu takes the cake).

What I would like to know is why in the name of all that is holy would anyone use Ubuntu on anything but a server? As a main everyday machine, this operating system clearly has a long ways to go.
