### Post by drowner on 2007-05-30
So, I installed Linux to my laptop, over a year ago.

I switched for philosophical reasons.

I enjoy Linux. I KNOW more about computers now than I ever did. This is good.

Until about 2 months ago i switched back and forth, between XP and Ubuntu, cursing my inability to fire up my wireless card, play a particular internet stream, couldnt sync my iPod or play m4a's, and various other hiccups.

Until, I decided, to take Linux seriously. I upgraded to Feisty (and, for some unknown reason, my wireless started flashing!) i made a serious attempt to sync the iPod.

And I got it all working. I was fully converted.

But gnawing away in the back of head, was this sneaking suspicion that windows was easier still. Easier for the general user, you know? That windows just worked, Linux needed work.

Until Sunday. I visited my dad, who knows nothing about computers. He and my mum have a laptop, they just use it to mess around. I'd downloaded some clips of our favourite soccer team (Napoli!) to show him. They were .mov files.

They wouldn't play.

So I had to try and download a program to play them. Quicktime was something ridiculous, like 30 meg, and i had to install iTunes too.

And then i decided to try and convert it to another file.

A whole heap of non-free software was available to download, only a few that were free.

Installing them WAS NOT easy. And most of them didn't work. And i was worried about viruses.

And the machine CRAWLED. On a newer computer than mine.

I went home, and booted windows, first time in 2 months. It was so slow. I had to wait for 10 minutes after it booted to do anything. And apparently windows had downloaded some updates for me (which i apparently am supposed to tell it not to). I intend to send some of my internet bill to Gates.

Apparently if i had tried to install it, it would have been worse.

And you know what? Linux is no more hard or difficult than windows. Its perception. If someone plugs a printer into a windows box, and gets no response, they say 'stupid printer'. If they plug it into a linux box, and get no response, its 'stupid linux'.

This is a long-winded rant, but I'm converted. The best thing for me?

I run my computer. Im not scared of it. I can tell it what to do, and not the other way around.

Thankyou to this wonderful community for making it easier, and letting me see the light.

I am trying to convert as many people as possible to Linux, with mixed results. Usually i get a 'oh, i need windows, for word processing' or 'nah, i dont understand computers, so i just use windows' when, in recent experience for me, the opposite would appear to make more sense.

Long Live Linux.
