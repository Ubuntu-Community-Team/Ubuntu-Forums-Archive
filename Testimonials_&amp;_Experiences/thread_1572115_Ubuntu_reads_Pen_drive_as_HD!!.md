### Post by ventrical on 2010-09-10
I can't believe it but Ubuntu installed itself on an 8 GB pendrive , (fully readable and writeable), upgraded and with most of the bells and whistles.

  This leads me to believe that the days of the 'hard-drive' predominating the data-storage hardware market are numbered.!!

  So , as for Lubuntu installs (on a HD) which can be run on most any PC can most likely be also run on any PC from a pen-drive (no doubt) without the config mess because Lubuntu seems to do a fresh reboot of all drivers when the system is reboot.

 just amazing.  I heard about the 'Windows in your pocket' but I never thought it possible that it could be done  and here I am writing this message  with Ubuntu reading my pen-drive as the main hardrive.

### Post by ventrical on 2010-09-10
The Ubuntu 10.4 pendrive worked  starting from an AMD 2600+ 512MB  to an Acer Aspire to a Compaq Pro 550MHz to a Sony Vaio (2.4 MHz)512MDram to and IBM PL 300 550MHz 320 RAM and then back to the AMD 2600+.

  I installed the compiz manager and programs and Ubuntu will deliberately ignore the graphics settings on slower PCs with no graphics card. So, basically, one can carry a virtual Ubunutu persistent OS on their keychain - and boot practically any PC, read/write and transfer files .. etc..! There were some audio driver issues on the Compaq  but no major glitches seemed to appear.

  I don't think I'll look at hard-drives the same again.
