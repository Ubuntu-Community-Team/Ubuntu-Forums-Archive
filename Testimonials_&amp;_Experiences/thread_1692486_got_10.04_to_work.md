### Post by vwbug on 2011-02-21
10.04 required much more work than previous releases! Played with it on a separate drive until I got it just right.  Thanks to the forums I was able to get it fixed :-)

I have an old Nvidia MX4000, so that was really the problem I guess.  I had to do the nomodeset in grub.  Got rid of the speaker/envelope and replaced with just the speaker. Can't understand why they made it that way. The open source video drivers worked much better than the Nvidia. Got all kinds of crazy things to happen when I used the Nvidia drivers. Like my panel disappeared.  Using the open source drivers set it up too high a resolution and VLC would lock up. What I needed was 1080X960 which setup didn't offer. Found somewhere on the forums how to use Xorg-configure, and it worked!!!!

Well, thats my upgrade story. Not looking forward to Ubuntu 11.
