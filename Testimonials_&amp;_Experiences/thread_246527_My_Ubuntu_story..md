### Post by PirateBob on 2006-08-29
I can only sing songs of praise for Ubuntu. I have installed virtually every Windows Os. ever, from 3.1, to a stolen and very non-functional pre-production version of Vista. I have even Installed IPod Linux (Pretty cool actually). Every single one of them has had some problem along the way. (I once had to return and re-purchase Win 98 cd's 3, yes, 3! times because they were faulty).

**_And then I found Ubuntu._**
At first I was a little sceptical. I had always known about Linux, but being the avid gamer I was, had never thought of it to be convinent to make a swich. I knew I would have to mess around with my harddrive partitions. Dividing up my already limited space. At that time, I didn't realise that Nero had made complete copies of every single movie (50+) I had ever decided to burn. So after sorting out my harddrives, I had approximatley 50 gig free across all of my partitions. My instant thoughts were something along the lines of "Boohyah! Bring on Linux"

My friend had told me about Ubuntu, told me that it was very good for Linux noobies, and that I shouldn't find too many problems with it. He was right on the money. After some research, I decided to order the CD's (8 for pc, 1 mac, and 1 64bit. I have lots of techie friends who would enjoy it). I don't have a single blank CD in my house, so I knew I would have to wait.

So after a few weeks of waiting, I recieved a white paper package in the mail, addressed to me, with the word Ubuntu in big bold letters. I ran to the elevator, and didn't even wait for the doors to close before ripping off the top. I got to my house, and  began scouring my CD folders for Partition magic. I created 3 new partitions: My swap (Suggested to be twice your RAM, so I made it 1.6gig.) My root directory (5 gig, to be safe), and a FAT32 partition for sharing with Windows (6 gig). 

I popped in the LiveCD, and within 2 minutes Ubuntu had booted. I played around for a little while and decided that this was going to be installed on my system TODAY (actually, yesterday). So I double clicked the "install" icon, and within 30 minutes I had a fully functioning copy of Ubuntu on my computer.

I've only had one problem, and even then, it's not that bad. I can't connect to the internet.

This will surely be a major part of my computing life. And I will , from now on, try only to use Windows for gaming (Or for those pesky IE websites.)

Cue corny catch phrase "Thank you, Ubuntu"
