### Post by jslmsca on 2006-09-05
I first tried FreeBSD 6.1 and had a major problem with a DMA controller.

I switched to Gentoo and almost got it working but had problems getting linux to work with an extended partition I created.

Since this is meant only as a single-user laptop for browsing, e-mail and C/C++ programming, someone suggested Ubuntu.  I burned the CD and popped it in.  Much to my utter dismay, EVERYTHING was configured properly - touchpad, mouse, USB drive, NIC, DVD_ROM, printer, video and sound - on the first go.  All I had to do was put in my name, password and timezone.  The most impressive thing was Unbuntu automatically finding the free space I setup for it and installing itself on that partition without me having to worry about re-sizing, formating and mounting.

If anyone is scared off by the installation nightmares of other Unix/Linux distributions, give Ubuntu a try.
