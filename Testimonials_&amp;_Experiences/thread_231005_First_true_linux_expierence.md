### Post by Boomy on 2006-08-06
For a photoshop replacemnt, try The Gimp. The interface is a little different, but after spending some time with it I love it, it does everything I need, and is much cheaper. ;) 


Unfortunately you won't find a replacement for Premier. Probably the closest thing is Cinellera. I can't stand the interface and it won't even work properly on my system. 

VLC is a good WMP replacement. I really like Amarok to listen to my music collection, it's sort of like iTunes, but much better imo. There are quite a few players avaialable. 

Installing software is quite different than in windows, but using Synaptic to install things is a breeze. Unfortunately not everything is avaialble in the repositories, but you can add more to the list. I prefer searching in synaptic to tracking down programs over the net. 

Printing seems as easy for me in Ubuntu as it was in Windows, I can even print from my ibook over the network to my Ubuntu machine's printer with ease. I had to install some additional drivers to get the scanner of my all-in-one working, but overall it was pretty easy. Are you having an issue with drivers or something?
