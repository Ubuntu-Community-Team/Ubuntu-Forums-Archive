### Post by conorsulli on 2008-07-12
OK here is the thing, when I first started using Linux full time and taking it seriously was about when I was 16 (now 18.) using a variant of slackware known as slax (lightweight but incredible). I fell in love with it and installed it fully on an old 330mhz win98 box that we were going throw out and really gave the machine a new life and made it speedy. I have messed with many distros including opensuse linux-xp, dsl, fedora, gentoo, and undoubtedly Ubuntu. I ran dsl and gentoo on my xbox (xdsl and gentoox variants). I reckon as far as Linux goes I know enough but wonder why I have such bad luck with Ubuntu compared to other Linux distros. Everyday it crashes and in the last few months I have had to perform 3 fresh installs of hardy- It is not stable at all for a desktop environment, and I believe the only reason it is popular is because of the marketing (one cant deny that having a trip to the moon doesn't stand out). Now you heard me say "everyday" and wonder , but its true. Today for example on a completely updated h-heron here are the things that crashed/bugged.
1: Firefox closed suddenly each time when I visited (removed by user in 2011).
2: Frostwire went as far as 100% loading core components and then the splash just sits there looking dumb... no ui (it didn't happen yesterday)
3: Couldn't hibernate...switched off instead.
4: Firefox displayed a load of text instead of a web page, yet Ie on a win box could display it and Slax also.

Now that's a lot of stuff to put up with on a single day, and other things have occurred that I have had to completely reinstall the system. I moved to Linux because it was secure but I'm slowly learning that security and stability are different things,

Now I feel that this is happening because of the rapid rate of ubuntu's evolution with its 6 month cycles of bringing official upgrades but the result is things are being forgotten and passed onto the user to be discovered . 

The reason I have this major gripe is because I am after getting really used to the ubuntu layout not just the gnome one. and I hate kde on most distros except for a few exceptions.

Now I could harp on, but that would leave nothing for others to say and I would really like a few opinions, I noticed most issues with ubuntu8.04 which for some reason is a LTS version, The most sturdy ever was 7.10 towards the end of its cycle in my opinion. 

So what do you think? am I unlucky? and perhaps would a DVD sized distro be more reliable? but then I mean slax is like 400mb.
