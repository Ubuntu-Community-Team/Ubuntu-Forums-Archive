### Post by cudds on 2007-08-27
I tried fredora on my lil laptop about a year ago. I wasn't impressed. It was my first outing into Linux and it didn't really make the experience enjoyable at all.
That put my linux fun on hold for a while.
A friend then recommended Ubuntu. I installed Gnome and wow! It found my wifi card (all that wasted time with ndiswrapper!) outta the box.
A bit of tinkering and I got my display up and running.
Whoohooo.
However, this didn't win anything over XP for me. I installed KDE and found it pretty cool with lots of features - but again - no win over for me.
I tried in vain for weeks to Beryl it up. Nothing :(
Then I read about Wubi - I thought 'what the heck' let's try this Xubuntu for a laugh. Wow! It boots up for me in about 25% of the time XP does. Now it was getting interesting!
I installed Beryl from synaptics. WOW! It works straight out!
I'm sooo happy now with my lil xubuntu box. It wins over XP because it does all I want it to do but about 4 times as quick.
Just goes to show that just because you have problems with on flava don't mean linux is not for u!

My only nag with Xubuntu is it's too lightweight on the apps.
I only have wifi access so even though it found my wifi card - I didn't have the GUI tools a rookie needs to connect to a WPA network. I would recommend including a few more apps outta the box whilst still keeping the light-weigh XFC desktop.
