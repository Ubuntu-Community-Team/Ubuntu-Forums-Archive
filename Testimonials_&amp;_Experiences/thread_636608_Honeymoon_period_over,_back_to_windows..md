### Post by inversekinetix on 2007-12-10
I installed Ubuntu about 3 months ago, after a few problems getting it installed and running everything was great. &#12288;Very impressive, compiz is amazing, much better than the windows cube, got all my media working no problems, everything was great for doing everyday things.  Then I wanted to start doing some of the things I regularly do on windows,  I like emulators, love them to put it mildly, especially old machines.  I have loads of roms from all kinds of machines and often just play them for 10 mins then go back to my work.  However, trying to get these things set up on ubuntu became a nightmare, just for old hardware emulators, for new games it was an absolute nightmare.  I spent hours and hours, free evenings and weekends trying to sort things out to get them working, the new games I just gave up on.  I soon realized that I was spending all the time I should have been using my computer on searching for answers
to problems I was having with simple tasks in ubuntu.  Spending lots of time trawling through forums and irc looking for answers I started to feel a little let down / disenchanted when I had to boot into windows rather than ubuntu, it seemed so plain without the cube/effects, I started to think it was terribly inferior.  Then I bought myself a new 24" monitor and played some new 3D games in windows, I was booting into windows more often than ubuntu (because of the games) I ended up using the windows setup I have for things I had been doing to great satisfaction in ubuntu.  It dawned on me that I had been swayed.  I had been using windows quite happily for years, of course tweaked to the maximum I can do.  I sat down and asked myself, "why am I using linux/ubuntu?", "what are the actual benefits to ME?"
and to be honest, I couldn't think of any, other than super_pi runs slightly faster (.7seconds to be precise) under ubuntu.

Of course I have read the myriad posts slating windows and praising linux for myriad more reasons.

Other than the price I paid for an oem version of windows and games, I havent paid for any software, everything I use is free (free as in money is not required).  I have never had a security problem with windows, nor any virii, the only BSOD I ever see is when overclocking my hardware too much.  I couldn't find a reason to keep me using ubuntu.

Now that I'm back to windows full time (not even dual boot to tempt me) I am doing all the things I enjoy doing, quickly and easily.  I was going to say it's unfortunate to have to drop ubuntu, but it's not, it's unfortunate that I can't do what I want to be able to do in ubuntu without spending a lot of time that is better spent on other things.  If I could I would use ubuntu, for no other reason than I like it. As it stands it's just not right for me just yet.
