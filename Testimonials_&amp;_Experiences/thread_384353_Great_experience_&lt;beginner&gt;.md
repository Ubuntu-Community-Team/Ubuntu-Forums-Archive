### Post by poconnell2005 on 2007-03-14
I just wanted to post my experience installing 6.06 lts on my 4 year old Compaq 2190us laptop. I am by no means highly skilled when it comes to computers. I am not a complete layman, but I can easily find myself lost when things get beyond my experience. I have traditionally been a MS user and it has only been in the last 4 years or so that I have begun to use a computer in my day to day personal life. Two years ago, I decided to buy a Mac when the Mini first came out and have thoroughly enjoyed that experience. Seeing that there was another option out there besides Windows, I wondered what else there was. I had been hearing more and more about Linux and was intrigued but reluctant to "give it a go". I came across Ubuntu specifically last year when 6.06 lts was released and decided to order a CD through ShipIt. I ran it as a live cd and found it interesting, but not very practical to use as it ran slowly from the disc. I did not want to install it as at that time, my wife and I were still somewhat dependent on our laptop and I did not want to "mess around with it" (as she would say) and screw something up.

Last week I came to the conclusion that we were no longer using the laptop for anything other than surfing the net as we had switched over entirely to using the Mac for our computing needs. Then I remembered the Ubuntu disc that I had saved from last year.

I completely removed Windows and installed Ubuntu on it this past Sunday evening. Not sure what to expect, I had set aside a bit of time to do this, but found that within 40 minutes to 1 hour, I was up and running and surfing along on the net. I had read quite a few comments about incompatibilities with wireless cards, etc so I was quite concerned about this. I can only speak for myself, but I have had absolutely no issues and was able to connect to my home network without any issues what-so-ever. In fact, it's actually a better experience than with Windows. Under that OS, I would need to start my PC (which took a bit of time) and then pop the external wireless card into it's slot so that it would be recognized. Under Ubuntu, it automatically detects the card and I don't need to actually pop it out and then pop it back in. Not a big thing, but a better experience none the less. The lack of the need for things like virus software and spyware detectors also help with how speedy it feels compared to the year old install of Windows (I had re-installed Windows last year on the laptop which was a several hour process).

After a couple of minor hiccups around what I need to do to get MP3, flash, etc running smoothly, it has been so easy as to almost be boring :) 

Last night I decided to upgrade to 6.10 and that also went without a hitch and everything still works. It has only been a few days at this point, but so far I have very much enjoyed my experience with Ubuntu. I have purchased "Ubuntu Linux for non-geeks" and am looking forward to learning more about how to better utilize this tool that is now at my disposal. 

I am sorry for the long winded-ness, but I really wanted to express my happiness with what has to this point been a very smooth transition to Linux. Perhaps someone like me will be looking through the beginner's forum, wondering if they should give it a try but be too concerned to do so. All I can say is that for me (and my hardware) it has been a very good experience.
