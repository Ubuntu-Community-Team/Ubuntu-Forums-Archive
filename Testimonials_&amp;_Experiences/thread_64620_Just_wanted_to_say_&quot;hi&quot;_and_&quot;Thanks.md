### Post by TwiceOver on 2005-09-11
I've been using Windows now for a long time.  I must say it is an excellent operating system, however I find it quite predictable.  Yes it is nice to know that what you have learned in the past will be applicable to future Windows releases, unfortunately I find myself bored.  For a while now I have wanted to try one of the many Linux variants.

In the past I have tried Suse 9.x, Red Hat, Fedora Core 3, Mandrake, Debian, and of all things Linspire 5-0 (no I didn't pay for it).  Anyway, Ubuntu is the only one that worked with all my hardware right off the bat and from there all of the installed apps worked right away also.  I like the idea of a free OS and applications to support it but if they don't work easily you are only going to convert 1 of every 10 people (this is the way I see it).  

I am glad to see such an excellent community forum available for a Linux distrobution.  In my previous experiences Linux has been like a fraternity in which new members aren't really welcome or they are welcome but must learn the secret handshake on their own via Google.  It doesn't seem to be the same here.

Anyway, enough rambling.  Sorry if I ask stupid questions in the future, I try to search for the answers in advance, but sometimes overlook the obvious.

### Post by jdong on 2005-09-11
Windows isn't too "predictable". Imagine using Ubuntu, only upgrading once every three years. That's Windows.

Sure, on the outside, the whole Start Menu concept and basic GUI have not changed in over 10 years, but on the inside, Windows is also a fluxuating mess.

Ask any developer that has to interface with lower-level Windows features (such as the authors of Nero), ask a system administrator that has Windows 98, NT, 2000, and XP all on a network. They're completely different beasts to deal with!

Sure, Linux changes and evolves, but so do the manuals and documentation. A google search produces meaningful information with Linux over 90% of the time, but try to search up why WinProcEventA() no longer functions the way it did before SP2, and you end up with Microsoft's paid support line ;)


Anyway, welcome to Ubuntu. Please make yourself at home. Ring us up whenever you have problems :)
