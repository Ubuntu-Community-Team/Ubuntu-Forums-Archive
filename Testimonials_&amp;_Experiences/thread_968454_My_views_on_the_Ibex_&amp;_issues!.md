### Post by TimboUK on 2008-11-02
Firstly Im going to probably bore the pants off anyone reading this, but im going to type it anyway.

I came to Ubuntu in August with an installation of 8.04 Heron. I found it the simplest distro Id ever used.  I loved it.  I loved the updates, I loved the community (who are all very helpful) and loved the ability to do many more things at once than I ever had with a MS platform.

Yesterday I installed Ibex.  Or did I? The installation was no more difficult than getting an update from update manager, my system was cleaned, my packages updated, GRUB updated and the whole process was automatic.

I still forget that I have actually upgraded my system as it was all so seamless(as per a previous comment)

The improvements Ive noticed:

1. Slight speed increase (im not sure how you've done it but welldone!)

2. Smoother performance (sort of linked to above)

3. Updated packages.

4. Quicker bootup/ and shutdown time.

I have had no crashes, no configuration problems.

I just wanted to take this opportunity to say thanks, thanks to Ubuntu developers and thanks to the Ubuntu community.

I was a little nervous of upgrading, but certainly when Jaunty comes out, Ill have no hesitation.

Kind regards.
