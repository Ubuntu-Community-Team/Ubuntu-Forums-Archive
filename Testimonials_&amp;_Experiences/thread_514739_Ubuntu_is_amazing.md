### Post by strider_mt2k on 2007-08-01
I am a new Ubuntu user and new to the forums. HI! :D

I built a very modest machine from spare parts for experimenting and playing with. My goal was to see if I could make it work just for basic tasks like surfing the web, office apps, etc.

Under WInXP is was so sluggish even after extensive tweaking that I considered getting rid of the thing because of it's limitations.

I installed Ubuntu after I saw how well it ran from the CD ROM version.

What a difference!
Not only does this machine run good enough to use on a daily basis for surfing, but it can do so while running Slacker Radio in the background or even playing a divx movie at the same time!

As a long time WIndows user with a DOS background, I expected a steep learning curve with Ubuntu, and I have to say that at the terminal level I do have some learning to do.
That being said however, I am also blown away at how Ubuntu and it's applications have gone out to the web and grabbed whatever is needed to do the job.

-and then it just works.

I have yet to NEED to go to the terminal level for anything thus far.

It all just works! I'm still getting my head around it!

(the machine)

900Mhz Duron CPU
EliteGroup K7SEM Mainboard (built-in everything)
600+MB PC133 RAM
40GB HD
16X CDR
USB 2.0/Firewire card
Multi-format flash card reader drive

If my explorations continue to be this positive, I'm probably going to set my mom up with Ubuntu next time her WinXP setup goes afoul.

Thanks for giving me room to gush about my first impressions!
I hope to continue here as a fun and useful member of the community.
