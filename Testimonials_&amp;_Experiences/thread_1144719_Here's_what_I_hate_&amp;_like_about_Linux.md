### Post by GuiGuy on 2009-05-01
Presume high end hardware, generally found in the "Linux Approved" lists:

[*] Firefox can be a PITA after prolonged use. 
[*] Getting sound to work seems to be a lottery.

On the up side, my mythbuntu based home entertainment system is a joy. Sure, to burn DVDs I have to transfer files to a Windows computer, and I only notice the slow SATA performance on large file transfers. But everything else is great.

All I can say is I have absolutely none of the problems you mention on any of the three machines we run at home.

Have you just installed 9.04? If so, install 8.04 LTS, stick your 9.04 disk in a drawer for a month and then try again. It has just been released and there will be/are teething problems as usual with any new OS. By the time you try again, the new updates may fix some of your issues. Are you still getting the same problems with 8.04? 

LTS, BTW, stands for Long Term Support, so 8.04 is not out of date, as some people mistakenly imagine. Supported for the next 3 years or so from memory. Everything that comes between 8.04 LTS and the next LTS is practise for the next LTS! (Well, not quite, but ... 8.04 is at least very stable now.)

It would be a good idea to be specific about your hardware, as specific as possible is good. Because it is a powerful machine does not mean all the components are going to work seamlessly running Linux Ubuntu. (You may find different problems with another distro.) :)

### Post by Frak on 2009-05-01
GuiGuy is absolutely correct from my view. It's bad advertising on Canonicals part to advertise Ubuntu as having "a full set of productivity, internet, drawing and graphics applications, and games." It somewhat implies that it is a substitute for whatever the user is currently using. If I tell you "I have a ring just as shiny as yours", it may look and act the same, but it will break faster, lose it's luster quicker, and the fake diamond (or substitute gem) may break from an impact. It is true that they are both visually the same, but one is an incomplete clone of the superior other.

And yes, I think of Windows as a superior desktop operating system over Ubuntu. Mind you I said desktop. I think OS X is the best developing platform (Textmate and Coda wewt). I think GNU/Linux makes the best server OS.

Also, an Operating System is what helps you run your programs. You do not use your Operating System, you use the apps on top of it.
