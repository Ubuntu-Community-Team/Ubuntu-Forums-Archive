### Post by Kn1v3s37 on 2008-09-30
The thread title pretty much reveals all I have to say about Ubuntu.

I was a pretty normal Windows user, I liked computers, I loved games, but I wanted more. I had a friend at school that was into Linux and the sort, so I talked to him about getting into Linux. He recommended Ubuntu, because of how user friendly it is.

After my first install, I was a bit overwhelmed. I frequented the forums, and had to ask a couple questions. And then, I discovered something that made Ubuntu totally awesome: WINE. I could play most of my windows games!! (the ones that mattered, at least. WoW, GW)

So, I used Ubuntu for a while, and then discovered the K Desktop Environment. I loved it! I instantly went and installed it, and enjoyed it very much. A year later, my junior year in high school, I took two programming languages VB, and Java. Alas, Visual Basic 6 would not run on WINE. I had 2 choices: dual boot, or reinstall XP. I chose the former. After another great learning experience (learned all about GRUB), I was ready for XP.

Fast forward a year later, this year. After many computer problems, mainly with hard drives crashing and power supplies (quite literally) going ZZZTTT and halfways blowing up, I decided to go back to windows. All the time and work I put into my Linux install... I didn't want to have to work on all that again.

Fast forward a month ago. Windows was being... Windows. I was really tired of Windows XP. Sure, I could customize the look, but I couldn't customize the feel. I decided to go back to Ubuntu.

Now, It's all set up, I have all my games, my music, my look, my feel, my computer.

Thanks, Ubuntu Devs and Community, for making this the best experience I've ever had with an OS.

-Ryan
