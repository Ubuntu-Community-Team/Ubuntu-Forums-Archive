### Post by Futant on 2012-05-07
Hello, I got my first computer in many years in January, with Windows 7 pre-installed. A friend recommended I dual boot with Ubuntu, which I did, although I didn't use it much at first because I was so used to Windows. I liked the ethos and planned to get more involved in the future. 
 As it turned out, I didn't have much choice in the matter - one day Windows messed up big time (a problem that I did a lot of research on and found many many people also suffering from. Not anything I'd done, a Windows Update problem. No real help or solutions from Microsoft). Of course Ubuntu worked fine (after a couple of little tweaks - I was delighted to find that nearly everything worked straight after the install) and I've been using it happily ever since. 
I have always wanted to get a bit more involved with computers and never really knew where to start. Now I find myself learning without really trying, and it's so much fun! Someday soon I will be able to get properly involved (programming etc) but until then I am telling everyone I know to dual boot - people might not switch to Linux straight away but when their Windows messes up they will be so glad to have an OS that works! My Windows is now sorted but I've grown to love Ubuntu so won't be switching back.  
A huge THANKYOU to everyone who has anything to do with Ubuntu, Linux, and the open source movement generally. It's a beautiful thing.  
 Dell XPS L502x, core i5, Ubuntu 11.10

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-05-11
It's a lot of fun to learn Ubuntu. It's even pleasant just to use Ubuntu, even after 6 years.

After a while you'll try using someone's Windows machine again and be disgusted by the parade of sound effects when you plug in a USB device, and all the notifications for every single updatable program. You'll be angry when you install a piece of "shareware" and get hassled for money at every launch or see ads filling up the windows.

In short, you'll find the Windows user experience to be *terrible*, and this will surprise you because you'd never thought it was bad. Then you realise that you really appreciate Ubuntu much more than you did before :-)

Welcome and enjoy your journey!
