### Post by nSTER on 2007-05-18
Linux is great and has a huge potential but it tend to be very weary of External Drives. Ill tell you why and the experiences I've gone through with it. The first day I installed linux I dualed booted with XP and I had a 250 gig external ntfs drive. I heard that you can write/read on it with linux so I decided to switch as a lot of my work is on that drive. Everything went as planned and it was working perfectly, but then after a while the problems popped up. Corruption is the word that describes it, I would write on the disk and it suddenly the drive would crap out on me. Folders Files everything started missing, gibberish files and folders popped out in the place of actual files. So i restarted in windows and windows could not even read the drive and linux could not even mount it anymore. So all my data was lost if it wasn't for the hours on end spent with data recovery software and I ended up not being able to recover a folder somewhat critical too. So I decided to give linux another chance I ended up reformatting the drive to FAT32; great everything was working as planned until again another 2 weeks later the SAME problems persisted this time the drive completely crapped out and I had to reformat it, again hours spent with data recovery software to get the data back. So now I have completely given all hope on linux and external hard drives, reformatted the drive back to ntfs and now it is working perfectly. And to the people who think the hard drive is defective I bought it a month ago..... and everything was working BEFORE linux was installed. This is a side issue but also linux needs to improve its usb hub abilities, I understand that  my headset are not supported by the manufacturer on linux (Plantronics DSP 500) but the headsets would be distorted on the hub I had to plug it in directly to get rid of the distortion which is inconvenient for me since my laptop has only 2 usb ports. Well thats all I had problems with linux, if it wasn't for those specific problems linux would be my premier choice but right now these problems are stopping me from going all linux. Sorry for the huge life story.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-05-19
I bought my external hard drive a couple of months ago, formatted it into 3 partitions (NTFS, vfat and ext3) and never had a data corruption problem. Not had any problems, actually.

You heard that Linux can now write to NTFS? I'm surprised that you didn't hear people say not to use it - although it's technically a final release, it's been "unstable" for such a long time that I'd be very wary of using it for anything work-related. I don't write to my NTFS partition on Linux, and for similar reasons I don't write to my ext3 partition in Windows.

As for hubs, every single manufacturer of any USB device says that you should not plug their product into a USB hub. It's the nature of the beast - some things will work fine on some operating systems with a hub, other things might not. Is it a powered hub or unpowered?
