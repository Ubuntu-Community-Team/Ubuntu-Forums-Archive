### Post by d4m1r on 2011-10-31
And thats an understatement 8) I have been building my own PC's and enjoying them since I was 13 or 14 years old...Ultimately, I didn't go to university for computer science but maybe I should have ](*,) For years, I enjoyed gaming, video/photo editing, running various things off my server, all within Windows environments and I thought that is where it was at, but I was wrong (still loving Windows Server 2003 R2 though!).

Well, much to my surprise, 1 month into this "experiment" and I haven't booted into my Windows 7 partition more than 2 times...I spend at least 8-10 hours a day on my PC (for work and leisure) and I actually look *forward* to time spent doing so within Ubuntu. It has done more than gotten me interested in the PC scene again, it has slowly begun to advance my career. I tried Ubuntu for the first time in years (ordered some version 8 Live CDs years back) because that is what the majority of the java developers at my work place run....I make *very *good money now for a student but I am learning more and more about Ubuntu and linux/unix daily which will hopefully help me advance within my company as we deal heavily with Solaris based servers...

I have already donated $ to the project and have been trying to be as active as possible in helping other new users on these forums, or posting fixes I've found for issues listed on launchpad. Can't believe how something so profound was hidden from me for so many years...

/rant
