### Post by Lord_Alda on 2007-06-12
I have used Microsoft Window's systems for my entire life (15 years of experience on the platform -DOS Days).. My first experience using Linux was Open Suse 7.0.1 I was amazed by the customization options (It looked, and felt, greater then Vista even now) - I may have even been a little bit shocked- it detected all my hardware immediately (Just like that, no driver hunting required and no user modifications needed). It came with an intriguing set of applications (Packaged OS games that were actually quite attractive) and showed me a new found joy that I had never felt before. Then that was when everything fell apart: program installation was a pain in the @$$, the files I needed support for, had no support, and then my computer fried... literally the power management was such a load of bullsh*t that my motherboard caught on fire and melted (albeit the whole unit was custom built).

            Today, however, everything changed... I found Ubuntu; I installed it on my Alienware m7700 (not a gamer, but media enthusiast) with 2GB RAM, a Centrino Pro at 2.33ghz and 7950GS GO nVidia Graphics Card, and my old Gateway 7330GZ with 512MB RAM, a Pentium 4 at 3.06ghz and a Intel 950GM graphics card. I then discovered something... BOTH OF THEM HAD SERIOUS DRIVER RECOGNIZABILITY PROBLEMS!!! THE GPU DIDN'T FUNCTION PROPERLY IN MY m7700, and the SOUND, WIRELESS CARD, and LEXMARK z705 PRINTER IN MY GATEWAY 7330GZ.... BUT... I REPEAT "BUT"... I resolved it within a day of installing it... my resolution? Well I just hit the forums (this place) and hit search.... I learned about "sudo" instantly!!!! lol. I can now say a week later this is an awesome OS in terms of leisure and office work.... I LOVE IT!!!... My Desktop looks amazing, and any program I want can just be searched up here and installed! INSTANTLY! (Note to N00bs look into Wiki for hardware support).

LEXMARK Z705: [HERE]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters/LexmarkZ705")

Broadcom 4318 Wireless card: [HERE]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=197102")

Intel Chipset SOUND CARD Drivers (all): [HERE]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsSoundCardsIntel")
NOTE: Must look similar to this (WHEN ENTERING INFO IN CONSOLE)

> $ Icpci:
Multimedia audio controller: Intel Corporation 82801DB/DBL/DBM (ICH4/ICH4-L/ICH4-M) AC'97 Audio Controller (rev 03)
$ aplay -l:
**** List of PLAYBACK Hardware Devices ****
card 0: I82801DBICH4 [Intel 82801DB-ICH4], device 0: Intel ICH [Intel 82801DB-ICH4]
Subdevices: 1/1
Subdevice #0: subdevice #0
card 0: I82801DBICH4 [Intel 82801DB-ICH4], device 4: Intel ICH - IEC958 [Intel 82801DB-ICH4 - IEC958]
Subdevices: 1/1
Subdevice #0: subdevice #0

      And then install GNOME ALSA MIXER to permute everything... Viola sound... or you could refer to [THIS]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DebuggingSoundProblems").

      Thank you Canonical and the entire Ubuntu family for giving me the opportunity to use such an awesome operating system. From now on I will use linux.... Learn everything I can of it (Like I did Windows, albeit it just took me a month till I could start hacking it) and contribute to the Ubuntu cause... Thanks again Mark... you showed me the light...
