### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-02-24
Sorry to hear about your problems, but I guess I wasn't expecting the Sudoku problem to be put on the list with those more important problems.

Evolution does remember passwords for me, but you just have to uncheck the Remember Password box, check your e-mail, and then re-remember the password. Easy fix.

As for Unity, try it for a week and really make the most of its features. The search function isn't perfect but it works much better than any previous Linux-based search function; and Linux has had a lot of them :-) Incidentally it can even find and install programs you haven't yet installed and music you haven't yet bought! You can install extra Lenses to provide additional search functions.

Dragging and dropping files onto the program you want to use them with is an excellent feature I've been missing since Mac OS 9. You can double-click an icon on the Launcher to show all its windows. Programs can show a progress bar and/or an emblem on their icon in the Launcher to give you more feedback about what's going on even when the program window is not visible. You can right-click program icons in the Launcher to access Quicklists of quick functions you can perform with these programs.

If you're reading a document and scrolling slowly downwards through the document, you can just click the Down arrow in the overlay scrollbar. The advantage with Unity though is that you don't have to move the mouse very far to get to the Up arrow - great for laptop users. Speaking of laptops, with Unity you now have more space to see your windows and content.

After using Unity for a week, you won't go back to Gnome 2. You might even find Gnome 3 claustraphobic. With Unity it's still better to have a big screen, but it's not as bad to have a small screen. I love Unity on my 22 inch monitor but it's workable on my 10 inch netbook with trackpad. And I hate trackpads. A lot.
