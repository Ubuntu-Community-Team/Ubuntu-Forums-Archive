### Post by Welly Wu on 2012-01-08
Holy cow! Where do I begin? Today has been one of the most frustrating days that forced me to make a drastic decision: I switched to Ubuntu. I own an ASUS N61JV-X2 notebook PC and I installed Microsoft Windows 7 Ultimate 64 bit with a ton of software applications such as Microsoft Office 2010 Professional Plus 64 bit and lots of security software applications. I purchased just about everything for it. It turns out that my Event Viewer and Reliability Monitor indicated that my Windows installation had become highly unstable and unreliable. Windows kept freezing and locking up randomly up to 16 times per day every day for the past 8 months consecutively! In fact, I had defective device drivers that needed to be updated, but I ran out of money to purchase Driver Robot for an additional $39.99 USD today.

Out of frustration, I decided to chuck Windows permanently. I installed Ubuntu 11.10 64 bit. Almost everything works right out of the box except for my nVIDIA GeForce 325M GPU because it relies upon nVIDIA Optimus technology which is not supported in GNU/Linux officially. I really do not care at all. I stopped playing PC games months ago when my Windows 7 operating system became too unstable and unreliable to use on a daily basis.

Ubuntu has certainly changed quite a bit. It is a bit easier to use with the new Unity interface. I am using Unity 2D right now. I like it a lot because it helps me to simplify which software applications that I want to include within the launcher and it is easy to navigate.

I am having problems with the suspend mode. It does not work. I am going to research the solution to this problem tomorrow on the forums.

One thing that I noticed right away is how Ubuntu uses up very little resources. My notebook fan is quietly humming away and the keyboard, touch pad, and the palm wrist area are quite cool to the touch. When I was running Windows 7 Ultimate 64 bit, the fan would always kick into high gear which would make my notebook PC noisy and my laptop would get hot to the touch.

It is a welcome change. Everything is so damn reliable, dependable, and stable under Ubuntu. What I am really looking forward to is Ubuntu 12.04 LTS. I will stick with the LTS versions in the future because I am sick and tired of system freezes and lockups. I plan to use my ASUS laptop until it dies which means that it probably has another 3 more years of useful service life.

I already know that my next computer purchase will be a System76 notebook PC. I am looking at an ultra expensive and ultra high end setup in excess of $5,000.00 USD. I strongly prefer large, heavy, desktop replacements so I am looking at the System76 Bonobo Professional 17.3" notebook PC with almost all of the highest end custom options.

I am really happy that I decided to switch to Ubuntu. I was afraid that Windows 7 would cause permanent damage to my already expensive ASUS N61JV-X2 notebook PC as I upgraded the RAM to 8 gigabytes of Crucial PC-8500 SODIMM DDR3 SDRAM and an Intel 2nd Generation MLC X25-M 160 gigabyte solid state drive. So far, the hardware has been absolutely reliable, stable, and dependable. It is Microsoft Windows 7 that is unreliable, unstable, and not dependable at all.

Alright. It has been several months since I last posted here. I thought that I would chime in this new year and wish all of you and your families a happy new year.
