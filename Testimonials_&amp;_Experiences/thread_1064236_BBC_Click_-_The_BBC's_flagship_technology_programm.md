### Post by Trackieman on 2009-02-08
The BBC News Channel 11:30am Saturday has a weekly tech news show.
I have watched every episode and every time I get annoyed watching it.
On a recent episode broadcast on 7th February 2009, Kate Russell in a slot called Webscape reviewed the free for 14 days application called Deskspace software for windows.
It's a Compiz clone for windows. I fully expected her to add: This feature has been available in other operating systems for quite a while. But no.
What made it even worse was just 52 seconds later Kate Russel said "As you know were big fans of free stuff here at the Click offices".
Well Kate, it certainly doesn't look like it.
Linux is barely on the Click radar at the BBC and [[COLOR="DarkRed"]_the message board for the Click programme_[/COLOR]]("http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/programmes/click_online/inbox/default.stm") seems to have heavily filtered comments only.
But that didnt stop the Beeb from allowing Lawrence McIlhoney, Cheltenham, UK from posting a poison letter about Debian, Suse and Ubuntu.
    (I then found a little bit about this bloke [[COLOR="DarkRed"]_here_[/COLOR].]("http://www.helium.com/users/427234"))

The Click programme must feature Linux more. I don't expect them to feature any one particular disto. But so far It's a very poor technology programme when it comes to open source and Linux.
