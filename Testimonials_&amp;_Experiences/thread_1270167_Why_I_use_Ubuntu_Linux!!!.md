### Post by noelvh on 2009-09-19
:P

I have been reading forum posts for years on how Windows is better than Linux, and vice virsa, so I wanted to write up some this to share WHY I USE UBUNTU, as my default OS.

I started late in life with computers, at the age of 33, in 1997 with a win 95 machine. I quickly move to Win 98 and was faced with a ton of issues, that turned out to be a lemon from IBM. Not only were there hardware issues but the IBM build was bad. I was determined to fix this dam thing. After a year I had it all working just the way I liked it, and found that fixing computers was a fun challenge for me.

As time went on I got into Win 2k, and my wife thought I should got back to school for computers and see if I can get a better job out of it. So in 2001 I did just that I went to Chubb inst. This is ware I got my first taste of Linux (Red Hat) I don't remember what version but I liked it. The only problem was the instructor did not know much about it and we ended up spending 2 days creating users and groups. I asked allot of questions and got we will go over that later. On the 3 day we went right into Novell, and never looked back to Linux. 

During my time at school I found a deal for a cheap desktop for $200.00 that came with linux on it, so I got it. I was playing with it but was not to happy. I came with Lindows installed on it, but it was not a very good distro. 

Well I graduated for school second in my class and quickly got to work as a deployment tech working with XP. So XP was my OS of choice for the next few years. As I moved up in the ranks as a tech I ended up in a desktop support role, and was having fun. I was getting board whit XP so I looked back into linux. I gave Freespire a try, and found it to be allot of fun, but it was still a toy to me. One day I was working on a dead hard drive, and had it connected to a dual boot machine. I was not able to mount it in XP no matter what I did. This drive had a ton of company data that needed to be recovered at all cost. I was just about to give up when I decided to reboot one more time. During the reboot I was called away, for another issue. When I returned to my desk I found the pc was in Freespire not windows, and to my surprise, the hard drive was mounted and I was able to see all the data on the drive. I quickly copied the data and gave it to the user. He was very happy!!! I then became the recovery guy for the hole company, this included 3 sites, and I was getting drive to recover all the time.

This was the turning point for me with Linux. Linux was a valid OS with real value!! I was now looking at every distro I could find. I ended up with Kubuntu 7.04 as my distro of choice. I have moved to Ubuntu after the release of KDE 4, with all its bugs and quarks I felt it was just as much work as XP.

I am now been promoted to a new position, and was asked to help my replacement setup a Linux machine to help with recovery work, as I was taking my Ubuntu machine with me. 

Now for my home use I was all ways looking to get the latest and greatest software, and I am sure you know the routs people take to do this with out buying them. This was not sitting well with me, and I had heard of people getting in trouble doing this. So I move to looking for open source software and that was my goal. This was a good goal, and I was able to find every thing I needed in open source. Now when I made the switch to Linux, I was even happier as most every thing under Linux was open source. No now I am on the up and up and have every thing I need under Ubuntu.

To sum it all up I am very happy to be using Linux as my personal OS at home, and a great tool at work.
Thanks to every one involved in the Linux community
 

Noel
