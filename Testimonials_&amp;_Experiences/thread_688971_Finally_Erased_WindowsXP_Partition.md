### Post by Antman on 2008-02-05
Well, it took awhile, but I finally removed WindowsXP from my desktop PC.

I noticed that, while I still had a WinXP partition taking up over 100gigs of space, I haven't booted into it for about two months. So I said to myself, "Why the heck do I still have it on my hard drive?" -- so, I took it off and used Gparted to reclaim the space for Linux. \\:D/

If I need to run a Windows app, I use either Wine or WinXP via virtualbox. 
Also, since I no longer have any WindowsXp machines in my house, I can just use SSH or NFS to share files instead for messing with that clunky samba. 

Hmm... I still have to move the data off of my NTFS external 320gig USB drive and reformat it to ext2/3; but one step at a time.
