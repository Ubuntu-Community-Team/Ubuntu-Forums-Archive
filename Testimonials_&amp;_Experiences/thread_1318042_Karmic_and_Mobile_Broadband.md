### Post by wangsuda on 2009-11-07
My comments on Karmic after doing a clean install on a six-month old Acer Aspire 4730Z. It works. In fact, it more than works, it supports a certain peripheral better than any other version of Ubuntu I ever used. Ninety-nine percent of the time I use mobile broadband for internet. Karmic is the first Ubuntu that that let's me choose between my phone's modems. Karmic is the first Ubuntu that let's me run mobile broadband through Buetooth. Karmic even lets me bridge mobile connections. I cannot heap enough praise on those who developed NetworkManager Applet 0.7.996 and the fine job they did. Thank you! Thanks also go out to those who developed Bluetooth Properties 2.28.1 with the addition of the bluez file for mobile broadband.

To sum it up, it just works. It's that simple. No problems, no crashes, no nothing. All right, the boot time is a bit long (1:05) but I can live with that!
