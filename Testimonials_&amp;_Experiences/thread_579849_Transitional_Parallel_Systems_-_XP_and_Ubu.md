### Post by bill45 on 2007-10-18
My first excursions into Linux several years ago were not productive.  After the noise about Ubu filtered down to my corner of the MS Win community a year ago, I took it up as a trial on an old Dell P3.  So far have experimented with V6.06, 7.04, and now awaiting my 7.10 disc.  7.04 is solid, reliable, fun to use.


The recent jump in usability has been phenomenal.  One by one the obstacles that have blocked full usage by non-adepts have been resolved.  I now have full native use of LCD monitors and I see that dual monitors are generally supported.  Far more hardware drivers are supported.  7.10 will facilitate an easy printer install, a major hangup.  I have more multi-media codecs working in Ubu that my XP box, mainly because it is safer.  Fantastic!


All the basic productivity apps are generally covered with some inconsistency and sometimes odd interface conventions or lack thereof.  This will improve over time.  


I see WUBI installer has been perfected and may become a standard method of Windows usurpation (excellent!).  A beautiful strategy. WUBI is an Ubuntu Installer that runs in windows and creates a Linux VM.


On my system the Gnome graphics look a bit crude but they grow on you as would a loyal companion.  This may also improve over time. See wish list below. 


WHAT I WOULD LIKE TO SEE
A total comprehensive AV multi-media scheduler recorder converter with a single integrated interface.  I have this in XP and I need it to abandon windows.  Perhaps Myth TV could also be used as a stream audio and radio station recorder.  So far I have not adventured into the myth.


FANTASY WISH LIST
A CSS style GUI substituting simple drop menus for Icons.  I think Icons are ugly, obtuse, childish.  I speak and write in English and wish my Linux PC used simple English menu commands.  No I do not want to be forever typing in the terminal editor.  I think the old Lotus / Norton style alpha based key-stroke command menu system was the best thing ever invented. It is the easiest quickest most productive screen interface ever and I see echoes of this in CSS WEB development. 

Bill
Indianapolis
