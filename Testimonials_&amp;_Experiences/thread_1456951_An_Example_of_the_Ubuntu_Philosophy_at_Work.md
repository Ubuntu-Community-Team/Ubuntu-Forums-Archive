### Post by tuebinger on 2010-04-17
After hours and hours of trouble-shooting problems with my mom's 6 year old Dell computer, and after explaining to her the concept of 'built-in obsolescence' (as in Microsoft codes its software with 'built-in obsolescence' in mind), she finally swore off windows and agreed to give Ubuntu a try.  

She's thrilled to be using her computer again, especially since I've shown her that she can do pretty much everything she used to be able to do with windows, which is mainly check her email, keep up on Facebook, download pictures, and watch videos.  

Ubuntu runs amazingly fast on her Dell.  Time will tell if she will continue to enjoy it, but she was amazed and a little excited when I told her that with Ubuntu she probably would never need to buy another computer (provided the hardware holds out and she doesn't decide she absolutely must have the latest bells and whistles).  

For a grandma who relies on a small pension, who can't afford to throw another thousand dollars on a new computer, Ubuntu has been a good thing.  Thanks to all who work tirelessly on Ubuntu to make it available to people like her!   :)

### Post by Buddy's Pal on 2010-04-17
I love stories where 'old' hardware is resuscitated and made useful again by a relatively lean OS such as Linux. Even though prices have dropped and some people do processor intensive work (or play) Most people's computers are beyond their needs...kinda like soccer moms driving Hummers. People could keep machines much longer than they do.

Same applies on the hardware side. I bought a two year old Dell laptop (2GB RAM, Core 2 duo etc...) for $150 because it was "broken" according to the seller. It needs a new keyboard! I ordered a refurbished one for $29. This machine will be my Ubuntu toy.
