### Post by Sepero on 2008-07-17
Let me briefly introduce myself. I've been using Ubuntu for about 1 1/2 years, and before that I used Debian for about 4 years. Linux has been my main Desktop since about 2003. I don't frequent this forum often, and when I do, it's usually to let everyone know if I've made updates to the Modem Drivers I keep packaged for the Ubuntu community.
536EP Modem [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=471503](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=471503)
537EP Modem [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=552090](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=552090)

I'm Very disappointed in this latest Ubuntu release. My first taste of it was when I tried to apt-get upgrade from Gutsy. Problems started almost immediately with my sound going out.

"No problem", I thought, I'll just burn and install fresh from CD. Apparently I was wrong.






I'm now on my second burned CD (Ubuntu 8.04.1) and second fresh install. The audio is still screwy. It loads up correctly about 1 out of 4 boots. Here's just the list that I could come up with quickly, I sure there are some I have forgotten:

-On the LiveCD, it wastes about 30 seconds after the kernel loads searching for fd0. I have no fd0 (floppy drive).

-The LiveCD default terminal does not show unless vga=771 is entered. Personally, I think that is Bunk. The terminal should always work by default. Higher resolution terminal should be the option.

-As stated, audio doesn't load properly. (This is the biggest deal breaker) Ensoniq 5880 AudioPCI (rev 02)

-After selecting "Quit" from the Applications menu, it takes several, several seconds for the "Restart/Power off/Standby" menu to come up. I'm surprised this bug is back. I believe it was the release before Feisty that also had this problem.


(I KNOW THE FOLLOWING ARE NOT DIRECTLY ATTRIBUTABLE TO THE UBUNTU TEAM, BUT I'M LISTING THEM FOR FURTHER EXPLAINATION.)


-After installing ATI drivers, the terminals (Alt-FX) are completely black, and blink on every couple seconds. Making them nearly impossible to work on.

-"mplayer" will not play videos without using a needed "-vo" option.

-Firefox 3 is sluggish and buggy. I think the thing that makes it slow is it accesses the HD too much. The reason I say it is buggy is because some of the options in the "Preferences" menu do not seem to work (ie. "warn when closing multiple tabs"). And the search bar GUI seems to want to clobber Bookmarks Toolbar if I put them on the same line. (Yes, I know FF2 is an option. You can trust that I will be going back.)




And just something annoying, the LiveCD default resolution is tooo high! (I know it can be adjusted with F4 at boot. Even then, it's still too high! XP got it correct, start with less, then go up.)





I'm not writing for sympathy, just out of frustration. Hopefully someone that puts Ubuntu together will see this and make some things better. Please don't bother writing in to help me fix this or that problem. I haven't the endless hours, days, and weeks it would take to fix them all. I guess these just seem like newbie mistakes, and I just expect more from Ubuntu, since it's been around a few years now.

For now, I will most likely be downgrading back to Ubuntu Gutsy.

Thanks for reading-

Sep
