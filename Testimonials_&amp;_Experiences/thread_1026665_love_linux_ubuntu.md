### Post by monkeybritt on 2008-12-31
i am not completely new to linux. i have tried many flavors. but was having a hard time kicking the windows addiction. finally after a year of constant fighting to keep vista going and realizing that the only thing i was managing to do is keep a astheticly pleasing desktop that couldnt really preform any real functions other than change the way it looked i was ready to switch. in the last year i must have restored my complete system with loss of files five or six times no joke. it seems that the pc i bought that came with vista was ill equipped and unsupported. i have checked out the latest distros via wubi when possible (ubuntu, linux mint, bsd-i know that is not linux, suse, linpus, blah blah blah) any way i noticed something very important, that is the strength of these forums as opposed to all others. ubuntu is welcoming and understanding of new users and that is awsome. yes it takes a bit more intelligence to run a linux machine as opposed to a windows one, but just because you dont know what to do now shouldnt mean that you can learn to be a more productive user, i use ubuntu inrepid exclusively and have been very pleased. the only few issues ive had i was able to find solutions here and fix the issues within ten mins or so. try doing that in windows without multiple phone calls. any way just wanted to give my thanks to developers and those that help the lesser knowledgeable folks like me. i want to contribute and plan to when i can and wish i could program so i could really help but i will leave that to those with bigger brains than mine. thanks keep up the awseome work.
