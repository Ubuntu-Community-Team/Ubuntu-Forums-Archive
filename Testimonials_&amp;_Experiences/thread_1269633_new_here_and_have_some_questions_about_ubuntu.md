### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-09-19
> **webdevelopment said:**
> 2) how does Canonical Ltd. makes its money?

Canonical makes money through these streams:

1. Customising Ubuntu on request to meet the needs of various organisations

2. Distributing commercial software like LinDVD, Fluendo codecs and various other software

3. Hosting commercial "gratis" software in the Partner repository

4. Selling e-mail/phone support contracts for big businesses - if you're running your business on Ubuntu, you want the makers of Ubuntu to be available for you to call if you have a problem

5. Creation and franchising of training programs and certification exams for Ubuntu use - if you're going to hire someone to work with your Ubuntu systems, you really want someone who has a formal qualification in Ubuntu

6. Subscriptions to the Landscape service that allows a system administrator to control all the systems in their care, remotely, securely and easily.

7. Selling Ubuntu-branded merchandise

8. Recently Canonical has started selling support subscriptions for individual users too. I'd be surprised if anyone has taken out one of those subscriptions yet, but at least it doesn't cost Canonical anything to offer them, and they would help at least one of my Ubuntu-using friends if I got hit by a bus. *ammends will*

Canonical accepts donations too, but they go directly to funding development work of course.
