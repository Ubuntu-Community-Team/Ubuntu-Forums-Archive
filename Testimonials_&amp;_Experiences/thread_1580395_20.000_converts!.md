### Post by LinuxAdm on 2010-09-23
[FONT=Arial]I am the leader of the It department of an organization with some 20.000 computers. All of those computers have been running Windows XP. But since Windows XP is a virus and spyware riddled piece of junk from an evil company in Redmond, it has been a nightmare to maintain.[/FONT]
[FONT=Arial] [/FONT]
[FONT=Arial]Yesterday I was working late and I felt like doing something really crazy. So I thought: What the hell, I’ll either get fired or get a raise, and rolled out Ubuntu Lucid on all the computers, all 20.000 of them.[/FONT]
[FONT=Arial] [/FONT]
[FONT=Arial]When I got to work this morning the boss called me into his office just as I was entering the door. He asked: “What the hell have you done with the computers?” I only made it to “well, I…” thinking I would get fired, before he said: “I love it!” It turned out he loved the desktop effects, and he loved the purple color. I just said I was happy he liked it, before he started calling me a genius and offered me a fat raise. I responded that Mark Shuttleworth is the genius who invented and wrote Ubuntu, but he is poor AFAIK since he doesn’t make any money off Ubuntu, and suggested he send an equal amount of cash to Mark Shuttleworth personally. He said he’d do that for the next 15-20 years, plus an equal amount of the licensing fees to Microsoft![/FONT]
[FONT=Arial] [/FONT]
[FONT=Arial]I also got some feedback from the regular employees. Some didn’t like it, calling it “gay” and so on. They even complained of bugs, when the screen froze and they had to restart X. Well, I wrote an email saying that the OS is free, and unless they file bug reports and contribute to the development, they don’t have a right to complain and are nothing short of little Microsoft slaves.[/FONT]
[FONT=Arial] [/FONT]
[FONT=Arial]Others loved it, especially that we now have many more games.[/FONT]
[FONT=Arial] [/FONT]
[FONT=Arial]So finally, thank you and hail our genius and great leader Mark Shuttleworth! [/FONT]
