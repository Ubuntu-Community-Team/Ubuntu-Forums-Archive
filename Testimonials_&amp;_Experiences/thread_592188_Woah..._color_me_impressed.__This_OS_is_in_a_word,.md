### Post by mthmchris on 2007-10-26
I have an old Toshiba Techra that I had bought about... three and a half years ago, right before I first headed to college.  It wasn't a bad laptop at the time, and it's definitely gotten its fair use.  To Toshiba's credit, it's held up decently well for being so damn old.

Lately however, the system had been running **slow as balls**.  It didn't matter what I did with it.  I reformated it a few times, defragmented it weekly, ran little RAM boosting utilities... Windows was just plain slow.  If Firefox and iTunes were open at the same time, the system would grind to a halt.  If it was just running Opera (my preferred web browser), the system would grind to a halt.  And this was only a matter of days and weeks after I had reformated it too, mind you.  I thought maybe it was a matter of security, so I had a friend that is a bit more computer saavy than me try to install a bootlegged version of Vista on my computer for me, and wow... if XP was slow than Vista was is some sort of vegetative state.

The other day I got a virus and was forced off of the university internet.  Tired of reformating over and over, I decided to try Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon.  I had kept on seeing raves of Linux making the front page of Digg, and while I've always been curious, I had always been kind of skeptical (e.g. social media views xkcd as the greatest comic of all time and has annointed Ron Paul as the second coming of Jesus).  But I figured, **** it, my computer blows anyway, and I burned Gutsy off of my friend's computer and we were off and running.

Now, in no way would I consider myself computer illiterate.  But at the same time, I'm not exactly a Comp Sci major.  To use an analogy, I'd compare myself to someone that can drive their car pretty well, but has no clue what a damn carborator or how to fix a flat tire.  I can navagate Windows like a champ, but then again, I've been using it since I was 6.  During some of my internships, I can generally use a computer smoother than some of my collegues and teach them a few tricks in Excel, but I wouldn't consider myself computer saavy in any stretch of the imagination (although, if you read my resume, I tend to paint a different picture ;).  So the thought of running Linux, an OS that by conventional wisdom is to the computer geeks, by the computer geeks, and for the computer geeks... gave me a little pause.

So after some hiccups with the partitioning (thank god for this forum), Ubuntu was loaded and ready to go.  My initial impression: "is this **really** *my* computer?!?".  The OS was snapping back and forth... everthing was running way, way faster than I was used to.  It was like I had a new computer.  I had assumed that there was no way that I could run the Compiz desktop effects, seeing as my video card's memory is painfully small.  But the OS was responding so well, I just had to try.  I had another hiccup attempting to enable the desktop effects, even after I downloaded the driver I was getting an error message.  I had my first experience with terminal - which, although it seemed intimidating, was just a matter of copying a pasting lines from the internet (*phew*).  Desktop effects now on - the computer was still running well.  I decided to do "Extra effects", still very fast.  Again venturing into the command line, I downloaded the Compiz utility, have started to use to whiz-bang plugins... and the computer STILL doesn't lag.

Downloading programs was a little bit of a hassle.  Adding repositories, etc, was a bit uninuitive for me.  But at the end of an hour or so, I was able to get Google Earth and Picasa, import my photo, download AmaroK and import my music, install the AWN dock, and start using Opera as the default browser.  Again, this wasn't the easiest thing in the world, and sometimes I had to browse the internet for different ways to install certain programs (I couldn't download Google Earth from the command line, but then the .bin package for Picasa wasn't working.  wtf?).  Anyway, this isn't an activity that I would want my Mom to try and do, but for someone with at least a moderate amount of computer knowledge this was very doable.  The internet and these forums were my best friend through this process.

And you know what?  On my three year old laptop, I can have open Opera, Pidgin, AmaroK, a game of Majjong, Google Earth, and the AWN dock all open, with an enormous amount of desktop effects, and only at that point does the system start to drag a little bit.  I honestly cannot be more impressed.  I am amazingly happy with my computer now.  My buddy, who has a brand new Macbook Pro, is a touch jealous of my shitty little Toshiba.  My desktop has more eye candy than his, and you know what?  This OS is faster on my computer than Vista is on his.  I'm just so... shocked how this OS is, fundementally, superior.  

So yeah, in term of usability, it isn't quite there yet.  I would cringe at the thought of my parents or maybe some of my *less than intelligent* girlfriends trying to copy something to the command line.  But at this point, (to be... stereotypical) I would say that a definite majority of college aged guys would be able to easily navagate Ubuntu.  

I just see this getting better and better.  I'm just amazed at the whole community here - the support on these and other forums, and the software that equals and at many times exceeds their commercial counterparts.  Good, good job.  I'm excited to see the future of this, and I think I would have a hard time envisioning myself using anything besides Ubuntu Linux at home from now on.

Definitely impressed.  Brought my laptop back from the dead!
