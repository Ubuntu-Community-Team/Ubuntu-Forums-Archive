### Post by Wastedyouthfx0 on 2008-03-25
Hello,

It must have been at least 8 years since I took a peek at Linux, and I always enjoyed it but couldn't get much use out of it.  I used to fiddle around with a shell account back then.  I grew up on a Commodore 64 and worked my way up to an Amiga, and Microsoft pretty much pried that from my fingers over time.  I hated Windows, but over time it grew on me and kept me employed.  I ended up at a company running a small sized network.  I was happy with Win2000 on all my machines, and server 2003 seemed to get the job done.  Then one day a hack attack and some root kits had me fuming.  I replaced my server with Debian, and I was quite suprised.  Thankyou Linux.  I didn't think I had the experience with Linux I needed to pull it off, but I had a webserver with php in a few hours, running my companies timeclock, database, and website and then some.  Its hard to believe it was even easier.

On my workstation I ran vista for a while just to get a feel for whats ahead.  I don't hate it, but it doesn't seem to be going anywhere for me.  So, I took a chance and thought I'd try ubuntu on a dual boot.  A day later I had it on my laptop.  There was only one serious problem though, the dual booting was taking up precious linux space... I wiped my linux partition and have XP for a backup now.  

Within an hour after I had Ubuntu installed, people were banging on my office door needing things to be done.  I'm amazed... I can print to PDF's out of the box?  I needed to scan something... oh wait... it already works?  Any software I need is there, easy to find, and free for the taking and keeping the system up to date is easy as pie.  My windows network was available to browse, and I could remote into all my windows servers.  I'd replace the whole company network if there werent a few applications tying us down to windows.  Linux has come a long way.

Not only can Ubuntu seem to handle anything my office can throw at it, its also brought back the fun in computing I had been missing.  It seems to do everything I want, except force companies to port our apps over to it.  

The color theme was a turn off for me, but other than that I'm loving it.  I've got my XP in a virtual machine as a backup, but I don't seem to be needing it.  I appreciate what the community has done.  Its not much of a learning curve for me at all, everything seems to make sense and is easier to work with.
