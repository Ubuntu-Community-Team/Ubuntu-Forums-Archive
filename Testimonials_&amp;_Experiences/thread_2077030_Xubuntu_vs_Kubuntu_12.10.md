### Post by llanitedave on 2012-10-27
I had gotten used to Unity 12.04 on my Toshiba laptop, which is my main computer when I'm away from home.  (Which is most of the time).  It was very stable on my home desktop, and while there were some freezing problems on the laptop, I didn't necessarily attribute them to Unity per se, but to the laptop itself and its Nvidia Optimus graphics card that Linus hates.

On the other hand, I'd always been tempted by both Xfce and KDE, and although I'd been frustrated by the bugginess of Xubuntu and Kubuntu in previous releases, with 12.10 being released I figured it was time to give them another shot.

So I threw caution to the winds, and wiped the Ubuntu partition completely.  I then downloaded and installed first Xubuntu 12.10 and then Kubuntu 12.10.  I've been going back and forth between them on my laptop for the past week.  Here's my verdict:


I can't decide!  I like them both!  Both have improved tremendously in stability.  Both look really nice on my screen.  Both are customizeable in the most important things. Both perform with lots of snap.

OK, in reality I can decide.  KDE gets a slight edge for me.  It has just a few more options, the biggest for me is the ability to give each of my desktops a different background picture.

And if that's the biggest difference, it really shows how close the two are in performance and productivity.  I make heavy use of separate desktops, and that aspect of Unity in 12.04 never seemed to work satisfactorily for me.

On the other hand, Xubuntu is definitely a bit simpler to get to, and I like the no-nonsense arrangement of the desktops and the lack of clutter.  KDE always seemed a little "Busy" to me.  It also takes longer to figure out what the different options mean.

Another glitch I see in Xubuntu is that in running multiple partitions, the system seems determined to display each one twice.  That is the case both on my laptop, where it shows my two Windows partitions (one of which I use for shared documents) twice each, and my desktop, where it displays my three different hard drives twice each.  It does this both on the desktop and on the side panel within Thunar.

KDE doesn't have this problem.

Neither system is as "pretty" as Unity, I must admit.  Unity has a certain polish, and the Nautilus file manager provides a more attractive display. 

The two systems coexist with each other, but I don't think they are completely free of the other's influence.  My login choices don't work in KDE -- probably because I installed Xubuntu first, and that's the login screen  that is presented, no matter what.  Also, I think the appearance settings in KDE that define how GTK programs behave seem to override some of the settings in the Xubuntu system settings.  Either, I think, would be better off if the other were not installed alongside it.

As for freezing -- it's happened once so far in KDE, kind of confirming my impression that it's not an Ubuntu issue.  Still, it seems to be happening less than it did before.  I've not had any application problems with either system, and both actually seem to run quicker than they did on Unity.

So overall, I will give a slight nod to KDE as my main desktop for future work.  But I don't think you can go wrong with Xubuntu either.  Both are very impressive pieces of work.

Eventually, I'll give Unity another shot.  But not for a while.

### Post by lancest on 2012-10-27
Times have changed KDE/Kubuntu is really the deal.

File managment:
Dolphin folder and file viewing is quite helpful for keeping organized. Files are easy to distinguish, Dolphin has a slick built in terminal called up by F4 key. 

Tasking:
KDE task bar makes managing applications easier when using dual screens/projection. Unity gets me confused there- especially if I'm tired. 

Oxygen theme is really beautiful  - a nice metallic look that I enjoy.

I no longer compare current KDE to Windows so much but rather OSX

Kubuntu 12.10 is quite stable, and after years of testing it I'm finally there with my machines.

Kwin really exceptional- Window scaling wow! Customize for cube whatever you like built in.

HP printer driver allready installed.

Simplicity, beauty, managment, slick features.
