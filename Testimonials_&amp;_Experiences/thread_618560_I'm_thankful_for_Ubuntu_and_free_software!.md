### Post by gratefulgibbon on 2007-11-20
With Thanksgiving right around the corner it seems like an appropriate time to tell everyone that has worked on Ubuntu and the many open source apps I've come to love: THANK YOU! It is obvious that many people over many years have poured countless precious hours into these engineering marvels. To the Ubuntu team in particular I believe you are living up to your motto of "Linux for human beings." For anyone who is on the fence about giving Ubuntu a try you should read my story...

I've been using Ubuntu on my Darter Ultra laptop that I purchased from System76 for a year now (picture below). Although I suspect linux works on all modern machines, going through System76 put me at ease because they ensure the hardware is compatible with Ubuntu, they keep the hardware drivers up to date, and take customer support seriously. And why pay for a bundled OS that you're going to overwrite anyways? Is Ubuntu perfect? Probably not. I had some hiccups at the beginning with my wireless network but one of the regular system updates fixed that. I leave a CD in my drive all the time to avoid occasional 30 second freezes--I'm confident an update will fix that someday too. But, I have not encountered a single problem that a little time on Google doesn't solve, in most cases leading to the very lively Ubuntu community sites. As you can probably guess from my first paragraph I absolutely love Ubuntu! It has put the joy back into computing for me.

To be fair and balanced you should know that I have not tried any other Linux distributions and I'm sure there are some other good ones out there because they probably have a lot of the same source code as Ubuntu and are fueled by similar time-giving individuals. Also you should know that I like Windows just fine. I make my living doing web programming with Microsoft products and keep an XP box around for Visual Studio 2005 (I'm incredibly impressed with dotnet and C#). I will have to dual-boot or get another system running Vista one of these days when Orcas drops (Wine is too hardcore for me). In case you're thinking "no wonder he likes it, he's a programmer" you should know that I haven't typed anything into the command line that I didn't get from somebody's post, I hate dealing with hardware and OS issues, and emacs makes me feel inadequate :) so I still fit Ubuntu's "human being" definition. A year ago I was choosing between buying a new laptop with Vista or a new laptop with Ubuntu. They are both on par in terms of hardware support, networking, and browsing. So why am I so happy I went with Ubuntu? Applications!

I spent about $1600 on my box (includes some upgrades) and another $40 a couple days ago on a Logitech V470 bluetooth mouse (also in the picture) that I am very pleased with. That's it! If I had gone with Vista I would also have to buy Office and Visio and some other apps. Worse yet, I would settle for lousy scaled-down software because even though I lust after PhotoShop I can't justify it for "playing around." Instead I'm playing around with first class open source and free applications like GIMP and Inkscape Vector Illustrator. I even blundered around in Blender the other day. For work I make heavy use of OpenOffice, vpn, and terminal serve into Windows boxes. Quick and easy access to quality software programs (there are a ton of them) is what brought the joy back for me.

Tonight I'm taking my Ubuntu 7.10 CD over to my friend's house because something hosed his Windows box and he "just needs the internet." Someday when he decides he wants more than that, it'll be waiting for him! So go do what I did before I made the jump. Download and burn the 7.10 CD and boot it up in your current computer--then treat yourself to a new laptop this Christmas from System76!  

And be sure to express a little gratitude this Thanksgiving to the countless folks that make it all possible! Keep up the great work, you're all rock stars!
