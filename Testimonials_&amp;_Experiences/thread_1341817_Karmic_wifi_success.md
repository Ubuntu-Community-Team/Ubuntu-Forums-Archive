### Post by frncz on 2009-11-30
I spent a lot of time trying to get wifi on my Compaq Evo N400c laptop (256Mb RAM), with little success. I tried with my Actiontec PCMCIA card and with a Edimax USB wifi stick. The latter nearly worked, but never quite made it in spite of getting temporary connections. I also bought a new PCMCIA wifi card (ath5k), with no success. I did try to follow the many threads as obviously there is a problem with laptop wifi connection for many.

Yesterday, I did the following:
1. Install the new PCMCIA card (ath5k, WEP security)
1. Fresh Karmic install from CD, reformatting the disk.
2. System> Administration> Update Manager to get the system up to date
3. Reboot system
4. WiFi worked after entering the WEP code.

So, it may well be that the Karmic updates now include the patches to get WiFi to work.

Anyway, I am delighted that it works, and I am enjoying Ubuntu.

Thanks to all
