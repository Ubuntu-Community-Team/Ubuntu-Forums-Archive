### Post by lebihan1 on 2007-10-16
After using windows for about 13 years and upgrading to Vista and seeing how badly it managed resources, I decided to give Linux another shot. I tried Redhat way back when and I had gnome but the desktop looked terrible. Both Linux and gnome have come a long way. My Gutsy 64bit looks really slick with the latest Compiz from git (I really wanted the screensaver, and I love it).  I started off with fiesty, but after having problems with sound I gave gutsy tribe 5 a shot, and everything worked pretty well out of the box. I also broke Gutsy several times trying to get everything working just the way I wanted (typical noob), but every time I had to reinstall, I learned something new and now it's working like a champ. Best of all is the community, finding fixes can some times be frustrating, but if there's a will there's a way, and with Ubuntu, there's always someone willing to help out. So here's my thanks to all the developers, GREAT JOB, and to all those helping others along on the forums, THANK YOU. I look forward to being an Ubuntu user for a long time. :)
