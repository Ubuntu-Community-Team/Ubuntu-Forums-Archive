### Post by marcgh on 2009-12-03
First, let this be clear: I am NOT looking for any help about my system. My system is working put I just can't produce with it.

I installed Ubuntu exactly 1 year ago and for 1 year now I did no see any BSOD (blue screen of dead) but people, how misguided I was!

When I have a BSOD, I Google the stuff and get mostly 10,000+ possible answers to my problem and I know that before page 3 of 10,000+ I will be up and running again.

I was misguided that this free stuff (it's not really a OS) will allow me and the small company I run to save money on software and be more productive. 

For 1 year I am going from tweak to tweak, from problem to problem, getting some help at the forums and putting a lot, really a lot of evening hours and week-ends just to have something done.

Imagine, working with some few staff. Opening the workplace at 8.00 am and not getting any WiFi working till 10.00 (yes, meantime everybody is checking out the free stuff games)

Imagine, me as owner and responsible telling whoever wants to listen to me that we are at version 8.04 and that 8.10 will be so much better

Imagine, me, after upgrading from 8.04 to 8.10 to watch 4 systems that are supposed to produce, crashing down to a black screen ( except 1 sytem that whent to 640 x resolution). Well the other 3 systems did not have BSOD.....it was just BLACK with no indication!

Don't give up men...just Google and find out that the updates or upgrades are not working with ATI RADEON proprietary drivers.
Again, no problem... trow away ATI and buy NVIDIA - it is supported but not working in dual screen unless you set it up at every restart or never shutdown.
Again Google and ask at the forum and...get nowhere!
even my last cry for help ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1343206](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1343206)) was read 11 times with no answers ( a golden tip here: title your thread as BEGINNER OR NOOB NEEDS HELP, success guaranteed).

This is for me the final halt. I admid I was wrong. If I don't want my small business to collapse and want to go back to where I was 1 year ago I will have to pay and that is exactly what I did.

I paid for Windows 7 on 2 machines, MAC OS X on another one and installed W 2003 server on the 4th since ending October and I and my assistants are up and running and providing full service to our customers from early morning till closing time of business.

Of course I had to pay for a decent AV ( in this case NOD32 multi license) and I had to download and install Microsoft security essentials (free if genuine) but I just enjoy again working on my PC and I also have again time to spend with my wife and kids (sorry kiddies for all the time we lost)

I switch the thing on, wireless is working as if it is as simple as a round wheel, my windows are opening where exactly I have closed them last time and my 2 screens are displaying at 1440 x 900 and 1024 x xxx and I just feel I AM IN CONTROL.

I can edit files, don't even write about GIMP its nowhere close to CS3, I can write a memo and mail a PDF or spreadsheet and every computer worldwide will read it and open it.

I will try LINUX, any version maybe in a few years - I still remember the thrill I had 1 year ago - but till proven able to produce without trouble I will maybe or not only install it on my home PC where we like to Skype or watch family pictures.

For the moment this Ubuntu family is for me just like a joke or a way for the kids to get some free (American Army - what the F..K) games and play and expect the others to work, and make money, on W Xp, W7 or OSX so that we can keep playing and working all evening till late night to keep it somehow running.

Please, all of you, don't play the missionary. I was converted and misguided and needed help when I was looking for it - no more.

Good luck to all of you who like this 'thing' and happy to be back in the club where things 'are working'

Sans rancune et a bientot peut-etre.

Marc

### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2009-12-04
Well, honestly, once again, I feel the OP is just frustrated because of sheer ignorance.

Absolutely no professional IT department in the world will jump into a new or different OS without a certification, proper testing, etc.  

A company or business is no joke and risks implied make everybody be very very cautious about what changes are applied.  I work for a big american company and we are still using IE6 as the core internet browser!  We are running Windows XP SP3, laptop models are standard for years, Oracle DB versions are equally old, and updates are always tested before applied.

Honestly, if you had problems is because you managed it terribly wrong.  I don´t mean to be mean, but your approach was very unprofessional.

First off, you should have ensured before hand that all your hardware was fully supported.  That´s easily done by using live CDs, no change on your systems implied.  Second, you as the owner could have run a second PC in parallel and try a progressive transition to Ubuntu, and only if everything worked perfectly for you after weeks of parallel use, then you would be prepared to migrate.  These are just a couple of ideas, but they would have saved you from lots of problems.

Don´t be confused, we did just as exhaustive testing when moving from Windows XP SP2 to SP3, so this is not a Linux specific task.  Such care applies if you want to migrate smoothly and with no business impact.

In addition, of course, I think it makes all the sense in the world for a business to have official support.  From your post, I get you never bothered investing in Canonical support, so blaming your frustrations on the forum is unfair, to say the least.  Canonical or RedHat support companies all over the place, and they are  good at it, and they would have been a perfect fitting solution for you.

I believe in critical thinking, and I think Ubuntu is far from perfect, but I can´t agree with your comments when I see so many flaws in the way you ran things.  A business, as you very well put it, is no joke, and one cannot dive into a sea of unknowns, or the experience will be a nightmare.  

You want perfect computing with no planning, no knowledge and no testing.  That´s not an Operating system, that´s called a miracle.  The only reason why you are getting it to work with Windows is because probably your machines have backwards hardware compatibility, which is something that can´t be taken for granted with Linux.

I hope you don´t face those problems again, but please look at things critically and don´t blame Ubuntu for something that you are responsible for.  Ubuntu can´t and won´t close that gap, so don´t try it in some years hoping it will perform miracles, or you will be disappointed as well.  

And lastly...  Lots of companies successfully run their business with Ubuntu and other Linux distros.  They just know what they are doing.

PS:  Again, not meaning to be harsh here, but I honestly think your post lacks any perspective.
