### Post by dai_vernon on 2008-08-05
I'd like to share my story with Linux in all it's forms.  Forgive me if it's long, but I feel compelled.

My experiences with Linux started in November of 2007 when I put the open-source jukebox firmware Rockbox on my iPod.  I had recently learned of DRM and platform control in its various forms and was rather disgusted.  I was extremely nervous of attempting to install anything unfamiliar, so I put off installing it for quite a while. When I discovered that I could install it via an installer app in OS X rather than needing to use a CLI, I took the plunge and did it.  The install didn't work immediately, but it was very painless and Rockbox has been solid ever since.

I didn't venture into the OS territory until I discussed getting a laptop.  Being a lifelong Mac user and having always disliked Windows, I knew I planned on axing Vista as soon as possible.  I wasn't going to get a laptop until July, but I began playing with Linux in April.  I, in a week-long, sleep-losing process (while in college as a Chemical Engineer, no less) managed to non-destructively repartition my hard drive on my PPC64 iMac G5.  My first choice for an OS was, interestingly enough, openSUSE 10.3.  Why?  I'm not sure, I guess I thought it would be a learning experience.

What it was was a rather hellish 5 days of trying to get an OS to work.  Going over forum posts I made on the Ubuntu and openSUSE forums from the time make me realize how disadvantaged I was, and how in over my head I was; I was trying to get an obscure (to me) Linux to work on a) a Mac, b) a 64-bit PPC, and c) with absolutely no experience.  I had some people trying to help me, but I never quite got the hang of it.  I eventually swapped SUSE out for Kubuntu, and the problems I had were (mostly) solved in less than an hour.  It was still a supplemental OS at that point, since OS X did all the things I needed to do, and didn't require any messing with, which, as an engineering freshman, I couldn't spare time for.  It is still sitting on my computer, taking up 35 gigs of space without benefit, as I no longer use it, especially now that I have my laptop.

I began my Linux journey anew when I got my lappy, a modestly powerful Compaq.  I shrunk Vista down as much as I could and installed SimplyMEPIS.  Again, I needed 5 days of hell to realize the obvious choice.  Wireless, Java, and Flash (the three (unconquered then) problems that dogged me in SUSE)), were solved within 2 days.  Other problems have been solved promptly, easily, and with minimal stress.

I've not got the energy to finish this the way I wanted to, but suffice to say that Ubuntu is one of my favorite things I have encountered since I began using the internet.  Thank you, open source community.
