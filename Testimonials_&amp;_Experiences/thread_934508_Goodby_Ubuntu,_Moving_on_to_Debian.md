### Post by the real omni on 2008-10-01
A couple of thoughts:

1) I lost a lot of sleep and a lot of hair trying to get ubuntu working properly with my old ATI card, but when I finally bit the bullet and spent $60 on a used nvidia card all my troubles went out the window. ATI has historically outright refused to support Linux with drivers - I understand they're getting better, but for Linux you really want to be buying nvidia. (8.10 even has an official nvidia system administration applet)

2) Debian won't really offer any extra stability or relief if you are indeed using an ATI card.

3) If you really want to "graduate" from Ubuntu, have a look at [http://www.archlinux.org/](http://www.archlinux.org/) cause holy moly is that distro ever FAST! Also Gentoo is a great distro to move onto from Ubu - the main advantage to Gentoo being that it allows compilation of binaries custom tailored to your hardware so there's no extra cruft. With smaller, more tailored binaries, the system runs super fast. (Not as fast as ArchLinux, but it's also more full-featured than Arch)

On the note of speed, having updated from 8.04 LTS to 8.10 alpha 5 I can say that I've noticed HUGE performance increases so if nothing else, hopefully when 8.10 gets through the alpha and beta testing stages and the LTS version is rolled out on Oct 30th it will be a bit more stable for you! :)
