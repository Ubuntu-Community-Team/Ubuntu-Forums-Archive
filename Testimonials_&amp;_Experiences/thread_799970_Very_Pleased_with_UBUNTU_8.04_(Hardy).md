### Post by uboom on 2008-05-19
I'm a new guy in Linux, and have tried some flavors in the past month, including SUSE, FreeBSD, GNOME, FEDORA and DEBIAN.
I found for my personal and business situation that the UBUNTU has the best Installing and Desktop Environment, and recognize almost any hardware in the computer, including a wireless USB adapter from Trendnet (I stated the brand because I think Trendnet is not as recognize as CISCO, 3COM, Linksys).

I'm trying to change where I work to Linux every machine, so we can save in licensing and because I believe that Linux to be a more stable and trusted operating systems than Windows.

The only thing keeping me from doing this is that we use Quickbooks Premier 2007 Multicurrency edition, and need to find something like Quickboks or better to handle the Accounting in my company.

Already at home I have two machines with Ubuntu 8.04 (Hardy) and I'm very happy about it.

This Post is to let everybody know that you have another person entering the world of Linux, and that I won't give up so easily like other peoples that throw the towel because of some compatibility issues with Wireless Card.

Thanks
