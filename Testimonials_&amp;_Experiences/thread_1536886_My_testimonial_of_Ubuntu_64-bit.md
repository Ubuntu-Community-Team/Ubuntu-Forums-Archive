### Post by elendryst on 2010-07-22
I installed Ubuntu 64-bit single boot on an early 2008 Macbook Pro. The drivers were on the CD, however, installing them proved to be a bit of a challenge. Hardware Drivers would prop up errors saying the debian package needed was not found on the CD despite it being in my drive. I navigated to the package, double clicked and installed them individually, and at that time the wireless worked. I installed the video driver and then things worked... sort of. The touchpad refused to recognize I had checked "ignore trackpad whilst typing." 

Sound did work out of the box, but on one bootup did not work at all. There were dotted lines next to the sound icon in the top right. A reboot fixed the problem. 

Then there were the intermittent keyboard and mouse issues. I posted a thread on the Apple Users board that appeared to show USB problems with the internal keyboard and trackpad. This problem was very reminiscent of the keyboard/mouse problems that plagued OS X 10.6.3, except much more frequent. I returned to Mac OS X because of this reason. 

Some of the keyboard hangs would happen so bad Ubuntu would go back to the login screen. Chrome and OpenOffice were able to reopen tabs and recover documents. I have found what appears to be a bug report filed, so hopefully it's fixed. I was quite impressed with Linux otherwise. I may try out another distribution or an earlier distribution later.
