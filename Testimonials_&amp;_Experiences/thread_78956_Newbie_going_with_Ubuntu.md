### Post by Darrin on 2005-10-19
A couple of weeks ago I got a free copy of Linspire and decided to try it. Ive always wanted to dabble in Linux, but found it to be too confusing. I found Linspire to be extremely easy to use, great start to Linux. Reading more on linux distros I came across Ubuntu, Suse, Mepis. I tried all of those. Ubuntu was the one that stuck out for me. I really like how clean the interface is and from what I read it can be pretty easy to use also. I wasnt sure if I was going to get too involved with any other linux besides linspire because all the tech talk in other distro forums was too much and caused me to think linux was out of my league. I never got much help in plain english, I was going to give up. I made a few posts in this forums while trying ubuntu and was immediately impressed with the fast, courteous responses from other users. Even to my dumbest questions. With the support from this users forum, I think I may actually have a shot at learning linux, and maybe, just maybe even get rid of windows as my primary OS. 
Thanks to all of you here who help in this forum. Your help with newbies like me is extremely valuable and appreciated. :smile:
