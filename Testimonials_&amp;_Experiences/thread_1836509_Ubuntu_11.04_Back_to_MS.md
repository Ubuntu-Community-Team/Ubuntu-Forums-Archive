### Post by SPARTAN-118 on 2011-08-31
Don't hate on Ubuntu, nothing's wrong. That toolbar you see? That's the Unity desktop environment, which has replaced GNOME, the former default desktop environment of Ubuntu. To get back your applications menu and the like, and the Panels/toolbars that have been the standard for years, simply install GNOME, and, when you log in, tell Ubuntu to start in the GNOME desktop environment instead of Unity. You can probably find guides for that on this very forum, so I'm not going to tell you how to do it here. 
Installing GNOME will also "fix" the "problem" of all the menu items being integrated into the top toolbar. This is actually a feature of Unity that streamlines the whole experience for those without a lot of screen space, such as a laptop or netbook. It also guarantees that the main focus is on the application you have open, and not the surrounding Panels/toolbars.

Now, to what seems to be the only actual thing wrong in your post:
What do you mean, you can't find any programs?
again, Unity does things a bit differently from GNOME. While you *can* have desktop icons, most things get put in the Launch bar, or whatever that scrolling list of icons on the left is called. To get more programs, or to see what you have installed, click the Ubuntu menu icon (shortcut key is the windows/Super key). Use the search bar in the pane that opens to find any files/folders/programs, etc. that you have on your computer, including Ubuntu Software Centre, Synaptic Package Manager, and the Terminal (all useful tools in getting new programs, or uninstalling ones you don't want/need).

I'm sorry that you don't like Unity, but again, there's always the option to go back to GNOME (or even 10.04 LTS, aka Lucid Lynx).
