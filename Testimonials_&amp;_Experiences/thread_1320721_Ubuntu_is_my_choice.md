### Post by samigina on 2009-11-09
Hi community!

I want to share my experience with the Koala.

I have a laptop Sony Vaio VGN-N130G, I had used Ubuntu since Hardy Hearon, almost 2 years learning a lot of new and wonderful things about freedom and computing.

In my laptop, Karmic Koala is working like a charm, close to perfection. All my hardware work out of the box, some little bugs (sound playing at the same time in external and internal speakers) has gone; the boot time is amazing, and i see the system very stable.

Im so happy with Ubuntu (and linux in general) that actually this is my unique OS, my hard drives are Ext4 (one partition for /, and another for my files), and im doing all my work like Graphic Designer without too many problems.

I see Ubuntu and Linux like a real alternative for productive machines; its true that theres some walls to pass over; but with a community like this, and so many people working together the walls seem smaller. Mmmmhh in fact, many of the barriers are no Ubuntus faults but hardware and software companies faults.

Thanks to the Ubuntu and Linux developers!
Thanks to this wonderful community!

### Post by samigina on 2009-11-09
Well, I use a combination of many tools, Inkscape for vector designs, Scribus for editorial designs, GIMP and Krita (real cmyk) for image editing, and Kompozer and Amaya for web design.

The common problem for every graphic designer that works with linux is the CMYK and prepress support, but if you are a little creative you can solve it. I use to create my design in Inkscape, generate a PDF with the "rasterize effects" option, and open it with scribus (that really support CMYK) and save as a new pdf in real cmyk. Another way is (if you have a Adobe Illustrator 10 license) use Illustrator 10 with wine (the newer version dont works), open the Inkscape pdf and make a "Flatten Transparency".

The only tool I really miss is Adobe Flash, but FL8 runs very fine with wine... is just that i dont want to emulate windows; I hope to see native software for create interactive designs.

Until now i has sent many works for cmyk printers with no problems. Even with the Pantano palette and scribus we can send documents with spot colors.
