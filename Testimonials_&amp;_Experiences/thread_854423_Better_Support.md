As bodhi.zazen said, random lockups are often an indication of hardware problems.  In fact, it may not even be hardware problems in the traditional sense of the word but, a BIOS problem that is causing the hardware not to work properly (this is common with Toshiba laptops).  

My last laptop was a Toshiba P105 and, without manually fixing the DSDT (think BIOS), the GPU fan wouldn't turn on, the sound didn't work, etc.  These were not linux problems, they were a lazy (and apparently deaf) vendor using a non-standard BIOS implementation that would only work on with Microsoft software (not-surprisingly Microsoft has even used Embrace-Extend-Extinguish when it comes to the way the BIOS is implemented).

When the vast majority of users using a fairly popular OS are not having the very dire problem you are having, it points not to the OS but to the hardware you are using.

### Post by weaver4 on 2008-07-19
I can't say it is fixed for sure, but I was having lockups once a day or once every two days.  Now I have gone a complete week without a lockup.

I installed 2.6.24-19-rt (run time) version of the Kernel.  Now my machine works flawlessly.

It is easy to install too.  Just open System->Administration->Synaptic Package Manager find  "linux-headers-2.6.24-19-rt" and "linux-image-2.6.24-19-rt" and install them.  Reboot your machine and pick that kernel in Grub.
