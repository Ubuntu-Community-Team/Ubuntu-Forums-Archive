### Post by ajgreeny on 2006-10-11
As an ex, now retired, UK civil servant, who is still a member of the Civil Service Motoring Assn. (csma) I was amazed and delighted to see a full page article in the monthly *Motoring and Leisure* magazine about Ubuntu as an alternative to Windows.  It talks about the differences, but says it is fast, secure, stable and a good choice for many.  It goes into a lot of detail about the open source philosophy and freedom and at one point says  "--- in spite of Windows having the lion's share of the market, Linux is catching on and could well be the future."  The final paragraph sums it all up brilliantly.

"Whichever way you look at it, Ubuntu is an amazing OS with a fantastic ethos.  Given that it's free, has a marvellous online support community, appears to be the technology of the future and you can run it alongside Windows, until you're completely sold on its capabilities, what have you got to lose?"

I couldn't have put it better myself.  What an accolade from a magazine which is not always truly up to date with its computer information, and which is not mainly for that subject at all.  Well done "Motoring and Leisure"
