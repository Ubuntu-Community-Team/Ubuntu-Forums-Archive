### Post by ram2500 on 2009-09-28
Congratulations on choosing Linux to use on your computer! I am sure that at first, it's going to be rough using Linux, but as you use it, you'll quickly find that you are in calmer waters. 

Always, always, always check to see if a particular peripheral or device that you've got your eye on works before purchasing by just keying into Google the device name and model and then perhaps "Ubuntu". I have found that to be really helpful and it let's me know if 1) the device is basically "plug and play", 2) if it will work with a little tweaking, 3)it will work if you're really into dedicating 3-4 hours into getting it to work, or 4) it won't work at all. You'll find this information on either this forum or other forums, or maybe just a blog someone has written about Ubuntu, if the device requires extensive configuration (something that you're liable not to be comfortable yet), then move on to another device and see if it works. It is much easier and less time-consuming then having to go back and forth to the retailer or having to mail back the product. Let me tell you this: you will get 99.99999% compatiblilty with HP printers. (They are also great printers, too!) And, if you are considering adding a wireless card, carefully pick the card and investigate before buying.

Also, you mentioned that you can't do any real work other than web surfing. There is a plethora of alternatives to Windows counterparts, and my favorite list to refer to other people interested in switching to Linux is as follows: [http://www.foogazi.com/2006/11/10/alternatives-to-windows-programs/](http://www.foogazi.com/2006/11/10/alternatives-to-windows-programs/). This will introduce you to popular software packages available for Ubuntu that has usually has some or even 100% compatibility with Windows' counterparts.
