### Post by R00tKid on 2008-02-22
After a couple of weeks on Ubuntu, I am switching back to my old XP Pro install.

I think Ubuntu/Linux is a fantastic OS, much more powerful and elegant than other desktop OSs out there. It just works, and works very well, included applications made for it.

But there are apps in the Windows world for which the equivalents in the Linux world fall short.  (I am not criticizing, it is what it is!).  Yes, there is Wine, but many apps require configuration and tweaking to get them to work and unfortunately I don't have the time (a real job, a wife, two kids, a dog and a vegetable garden get in the way:)) to do this in one stretch - in the meanwhile, my needs are going unfilled (tax prep time is here:evil:).  So I am going back to XP -

But I plan to get another desktop soon (for my son) and will make it a pure Linux install and configure it over a period of time to get my apps all working.  It is a matter of time before I am on the forced Vista upgrade path (when XP becomes unsupported) and I want to be completely Linux literate then and have all apps working (in a way I understand).

I think VMWare was very very good - it ran my XP install very well, albeit slower than XP pure (understandably).  The apps I need are:
- Quicken 2008
- Tax software
- MS Office 2003
- DGIndex, VirtualDubMod, SubRip, IFOEdit, DVD Rebuilder, DiVX Author, Divx 6.x, - etc.
- Newsreaders like Grabit, ParnRar - I know there are some Linux apps for these but again the learning curve is something I will attempt over a period of months...

Just wanted to post my experience - keep up the good work, Ubuntu!!
