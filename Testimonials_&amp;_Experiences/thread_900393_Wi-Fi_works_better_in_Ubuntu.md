### Post by Dragonbite on 2008-08-25
I appears I'm back with Ubuntu (on my primary computer) once again!

I just recently got a Linksys wireless router and was having some trouble getting it up due to PEBKAC*

At the computer club meeting, one of the members helped me get it set up and filled in my "mental blanks" on it works (thanks Dave).  He was running Ubuntu and had no issues with connecting to it while I had a couple problems. Happily I took it home and plugged it in and it worked. I had wireless running and could access it via my laptop.

My laptop was running openSUSE 11 (KDE 3.5 & 4.1) and I found it kept losing the signal and not able to reconnect. This would happen regardless of if I was doing anything or not. It never showed a strong connection even when I was practically next to it (or directly above it on the floor above).

To test Ubuntu I started up the LiveCD and installed the proprietary drivers needed. Once connected I opened up some IRC channels and Gmail for some back-and-forth internet activity and left it on while I went and watched a movie.

When I checked on it afterward it was still connected.  That sold me, and so I started putting Ubuntu on my laptop.

After running through 3 CDs burned from a faulty ISO image (despite passing the md5sum and scan disk) then downloading the ISO again and burning it on a CD I got it installed and connected!

Last night I lost connection for a moment only once and was able to reconnect!

Kudos to the Ubuntu team!

PEBKAC = _P_roblem _E_xists _B_etween _K_eyboard _A_nd _C_hair
