### Post by rgb1701 on 2011-03-28
> **kamaru44ken said:**
> Isn't Ubuntu designers trying to make a user interface that will make it different from Windows and Mac? Based on my observation, the Unity interface or the modified Gnome for Ubuntu is what makes it different from other OS (though there are similarities that can't be avoided as seen between Mac and Windows).

Exactly.

Canonical (producers of Ubuntu) are in a damned-if-you-do-damned-if-you-don't situation.

If the default UI is just a clone of Mac or Win7, everyone will bash them for being unoriginal and just ripping off the others.

If they try to do something different and innovative, pushing the UI paradigm forward, they are bashed for not being familiar enough.

Guys, this is 2011, not 2004 anymore.  Lots of people are getting tired of the menu bar (top or bottom of the screen) with drop down menu approach.  Tablets and smartphones and their touch enabled UI's are the Future.  Desktop PC's need to accomodate the tablet/phone paradigms.

The naysayers and UI Luddites can always go to KDE, XFCE, LXDE, $WHATEVER_OLD_STYLE_DE, but some of us WANT to move forward and try new UI's.  Some features, designs and options will fail, some features will succeed and move forward.

It's a chicken and egg situation- some distro has to try new desktop UI's in the default configuration, get feedback and improve.  We need to see what works, what is an improvement and what doesn't work.

I think the dock on the left works- we all have 16x9 screens now.  The days of the 4x3 desktop UI are over.

Move on.

The bar is being raised re: distro differentiation.  The days of putting out a distro with just a stock Gnome/KDE/Xfce/LXDE load (yawn) are numbered.

Agreed re: wait and see.

Too many people rush to judgement on UI changes.

One warning sign is when someone tries a new UI and posts a negative review the next day.

That's WAY too premature.  Human psychology dictates that familiarity, muscle memory, and similar issues tend to result in negative reactions and poor first impressions to change.

I wouldn't take anyone seriously until they've used a new UI for AT LEAST 2 weeks straight, or more.

Also, these kinds of software changes take time to "settle in" and debug.  Unity on 10.10 NBR was basically "beta" or even "alpha".  Unity on 11.04 could be considered "beta".  Perhaps after a few rounds of Updates and/or in 11.10, we'll see what Unity can do.
