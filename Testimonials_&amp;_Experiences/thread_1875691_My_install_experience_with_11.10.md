### Post by detroit/zero on 2011-11-05
I'm no stranger to Ubuntu or Linux in general. My first Ubuntu install  was in the Winter of 2006/2007 - Edgy Eft - after the Windows XP install  on my desktop crapped out. I lost tons of valuable data when it  happened, and I vowed "never again". The first couple weeks were rough,  but within a couple months, I was compiling my own NVidia drivers (way  back when it still had to be done manually, usually after walking  somewhere and back, barefoot, through the snow, for ten miles, uphill  both ways), troubleshooting my own problems, and learning the finer  points of terminal use.

I used Ubuntu exclusively for two solid  years, until 9.04 came out. Jaunty just didn't cut it for me, and I  ended up distro-hopping for a year. I got tired of seeing the same-old  same-old in every distro. I ended up going back to Windows, but only  because a new PC I bought came with 7 pre-installed. The first couple  weeks were rough, but I managed.

I always keep my eye on  Distrowatch, just waiting to see something intriguing or exciting. With  all the flap about the Unity interface, and what I'd seen in  screenshots, I decided to give it a run. The livecd worked flawlessly  and I was eager to see how things would work out on a full install, so I  clicked the big Install icon and off I went.

Download a couple updates, install some restricted drivers, reboot, bongos, reboot, bongos, and there I was.

I  was pleased to see that almost all my hardware was properly detected  and working right off the bat. My 6-core AMD processor, my HD6970 video  card with dual displays, my sound card, ethernet and wireless, my USB  hub and all the external drives... Everything was there but my printer,  but I knew that was going to be the case, so I was not at all  disappointed. 

It's been a solid week now, and my carefully considered impressions can only be summed up in one single word:

Meh.

M. E. H. Meh.

I  can't say I'm all that impressed. For the most part, Ubuntu is just as I  remember it. Wait.. did someone move my window controls to the opposite  side of the windows? WTH?

The disaster that was Pulseaudio seems  to have been ironed out nicely, and a lot of the kinks that turned me  off of it a couple years back appear to be a thing of the past. I  personally believe that PA is still an unnecessary layer sitting between  ALSA or OSS and my speakers, but whatever - my music and movies all  sound good with no pops or crackles, so that's what really counts. Good  job on getting that up to snuff.

The GDM (if that's what it actually still is) is nice, though the purple/pink color scheme is not my cup of tea. 

I  see a number of the default applications have been changed. Thunderbird  replaces Evolution, and I was quite pleased to see that. Evolution was  always one of the very first packages to get ditched on a fresh Ubuntu  install for me, and as tightly wound as that was into the Gnome desktop  at the time, it always ended up being a hassle to track down every  orphaned dependency in Synaptic and uninstall them. 

I can't say I  really care about this Gwibber thing, as I'm not at all into Twitter or  Facebook and the like, but I see what it does, and while not my thing,  the integration is well done.

The notification system, while  not-too-different than what I remember, somehow seems to work a little  better than it used to, so good job there, too.

Firefox, yeah.  Liberoffice, ok... Mplayer, Totem, VLC, check check.. Banshee, cool, I  never cared for Rhythmbox. But, since software can come and go real  easy, enough about all that. Let me just bring up my terminal here,  and... wait, where's the terminal? Well, whatever, I'll just use  Synaptic. Let me just... wait... S... y... n.. There's no Synaptic? The  terminal is buried? What's this Software Centre thing? And why did they  spell "Center" that way?

I know everybody involved is real  sensitive about the Unity thing. I saw the exact same lines drawn in the  sand when it was Pulseaudio. But come on. A giant, completely  unorganized slab menu that I have to search box to find what I need?  Where's the customize-ability? How do I get rid of that bar at the top  if I want to? How do I add things to or take things away from that bar?  Why is the menu on the left? Why can't I move it to the right? Or the  bottom? Why can't I get rid of it entirely, attach my most-used programs  to hotkey combos, and still retain the ability to have and use multiple  workspaces? How can I force certain programs to launch on certain  workspaces? Compiz is still out there, you say? Well, I don't have  Synaptic, my terminal (hands down the single most used application on  any Linux system) is buried somewhere in a giant slab menu, and for some  ungodly reason, my misspelled Software Centre is showing me ads for  various things based on what category I'm searching through.

All  in all, while I don't find Ubuntu completely unusable as some people  have suggested, I do find that it's no longer to my taste. Within a few  releases things could get back on track, but as it stands now... no  thanks.
