### Post by effenberg0x0 on 2008-01-28
After using computers professionally for the last 12 years, today I am celebrating exactly 2 years of Ubuntu. 

In the last year, specifically, I have not needed to turn my XP desktop on at all. All my Office (word, excel, ppt) needs were fulfilled by OpenOffice with eventual WINE+MS-OFFICE episodes. 

Yesterday I upgraded from Gutsy to Hardy (Alpha 3). Something happened with my Video Settings (X would only start in low-graphics mode), the desktop had no icons and I couldn't start Nautilus. 

All those problems were solved in minutes with simples searches in Ubuntu Forums and everything is working just perfectly now.

Xorg performance seems better, with faster graphics responses and smaller CPU and MEM usage. 

There still are some pitfalls, like using external projectors (I have never managed to make it work - problems with weird resolutions, probably VIA Graphics chipset fault) and some others, but still this is an amazing OS.

Regarding VIA Graphics driver problems (the company simply won't produce decent drivers although it claims to support Linux), I have started a petition which is rapidly gaining a lot of support (see [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=676143](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=676143)). 

That's it, thanks for this amazing OS and please keep up the good work.

Regards,
Effenberg

### Post by effenberg0x0 on 2008-01-28
I am not a fundamentalist MS vs Linux kind of guy, as I am in IT to make a living... However, I'd like to point out some benefits of Linux and Ubuntu usage. I know these are obvious for more experienced Linux users, but maybe newcomers might read this.

- I haven't paid for Ubuntu.
- I haven't paid for software for the last two years.
- I haven't paid for Norton Antivirus for the last two years (before that, I used to pay them every year)
- I haven't paid for support for the last two years.
- Instead of paying for all that, I have donated money to Open Source projects I like, spent more money on me and my family and on social projects I admire.
- I have not seen a "Fatal error", BSOD or anything like that for two years.
- I truly don't know what a Virus is like anymore.

> 
[...]how you like and being able to avoid booting to windows for so long (I have to feed my addiction so I can't dodge it much *shakes fist at gaming industry*)

When I have to use PCs from family, friends, etc ("please fix my PC"), I really don't know where the stuff is anymore. I find myself trying to PS, LS and KILL stuff at MS-DOS prompt quite frequently.
