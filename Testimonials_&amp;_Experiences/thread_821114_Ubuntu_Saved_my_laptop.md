My desktop, on the other hand, has been slowly upgraded to Gutsy (not yet Hardy). It's now my play machine while my laptop is my work (and play ;)) machine and I just haven't had the time to mess around with making sure I've got everything backed up and able to be found when I do a clean install.

Thankfully, apart from one or two games that I play irregularly, I'm no gamer, so that's not a problem. I am forced by a short-sighted government department and our tax software people to use Windows for a couple of tasks. Most other things I have found Linux alternatives or workarounds.

I guess open-mindedness is the biggest weapon you can bring with you to Linux. Some things aren't as good in Linux as in Windows....I can't think of any, but it has to be the case, but if you're willing to take a couple of steps backwards the leaps forward are well and truely worth it.

Next exercise is to work out how to get my Windows OEM to install in a VM on Ubuntu....then I can do a clean install and do away with dual-boot.

Also, I'm sure I'll see huge improvements between Dapper and Hardy/Gutsy.

I love the variety of software available in the repositories. Things I couldn't find for Windows without paying a couple of hundred dollars for them are freely available.

I am an amateur star-gazer and had a program called skymap in Windows. It was a demo. The full version cost $$$. I'm no professional or even an astronomer. I'm just an interested observer. In Linux, there's KStars.

I was looking for a PIM (personal information manager) to replace Outlook in Windows. It's all I needed Outlook for. I couldn't find anything that came close and didn't cost a couple hundred dollars. In Linux, there's Evolution or Kontact.

And that's scratching the surface.
