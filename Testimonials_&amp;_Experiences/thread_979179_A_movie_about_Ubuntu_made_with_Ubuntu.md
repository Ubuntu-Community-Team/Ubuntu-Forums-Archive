### Post by einfeldt on 2008-11-11
Hi,

I am making a documentary film about how Free Open Source Software is changing global culture.  I am using Ubuntu to do it.  CiscoSurfer suggested that I might want to start a new thread to talk about it.  I mostly have been using this forum for support, but it just occurred to me that maybe I should tell people about the film in this forum.  I mostly wanted to avoid the appearance of spamming this forum, and so I want to start small.

The name of the film is the Digital Tipping Point, and we are giving away all of our footage under a Creative Commons Attribute-ShareAlike license.  We have interviewed lots of cool people like Mark Shuttleworth, Linus Torvalds, Richard Stallman, the Mayor of Munich, the Culture Minister of Brazil.  The idea is that we want to make it a community effort, so that people can feel like they own the film, the same way that we all own the code.  

We do have about 80 hours of footage posted on-line at the Internet Archive's Digital Tipping Point Video Collection (IA DTP VC), which I will be happy to link to, if the long-timers here on this forum feel that will be appropriate.  Again, I mostly do not want to spam the list, and I want to make sure this is something that people here on this list will be interested in.

I am thinking of just posting links to recent footage that we are working on as we post video to the IA DTP VC.  But if people do not think this is a good idea I won't post stuff here.

Thanks in advance for your thoughts.

c u

Christian Einfeldt,
Producer, The Digital Tipping Point
