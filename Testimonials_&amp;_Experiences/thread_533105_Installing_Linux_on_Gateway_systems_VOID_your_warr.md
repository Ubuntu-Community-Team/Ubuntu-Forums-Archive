### Post by wray.justin on 2007-08-23
Hello all:

Well, let me explain in a bit more depth, and fill in a bit of back story as well.

For starters, let me be clear up front not to miss guide anyone.  Installing Linux doesn't really void you warranty at least not indefinitely, as long as you pay for any repairs during your Linux use (including hardware) and reinstall Windows, then your warranty will be restored.

**Backstory:**

[INDENT]A friend of mine at work, has an increasing interesting in Ubuntu and Linux in general.  He has a tendency to stay "cutting edge" and always attempts to stay ahead of technology.  Linux is obviously his next step forward has he becomes increasingly unsatisfied with Windows and more so Vista.  

So at his request I gave him some pointers on specific hardware and even Laptop Manufactures.  The end decision was his, and one day he comes in to tell me he has purchased a Gateway laptop, and much to my surprise has installed Ubuntu and has everything (including Nvidia drivers installed and running).

Well almost everything, he was struggling with the Realtek wireless and the Sigmatel sound card.  I helped him setup his wirless with little to no trouble, and began to troubleshoot his audio.  The sound seemed to be a real problem, I couldn't even find Windows based drivers online let alone Linux (at least not from Gateway).  I did the normal troubleshooting, and still hadn't found a way to get audio working.  Online it seemed hit-or-miss some people had the chipset working, some did not.

So I thought I would see how Gateway responded to the Linux world.  With the recent sale of Linux based system by Dell they have to at least support the idea of Linux, even if they don't sell it pre-installed.  (As a side note, I build all of my Desktops, and buy a small set of semi-linux friendly laptops.  Therefore I rarely deal with "out-of-the-box" retai)l[/INDENT]

**The Question:**

[INDENT]My question was simply, does Gateway support laptop hardware if Linux was installed, and what sort of driver support was provided if any.

I assumed that there would be no driver support, in fact I dove into the support conversation with that in mind.  But what about hardware support, for failures, etc.[/INDENT]

**The Verdict:**

[INDENT]In case you do not wish to read the entire conversation, I'll lay it out and qoute one line...

Linux is not supported, period, and no support will be provided for systems with Linux installed.  Any software of hardware issues that arise while Linux is installed will not be supported by Gateway in any form.  After all issues are resolved and Windows is reinstalled the system support and warranty will be reinstated.

> Yes you will need to pay for the part replacement or for the repair for that issue. However that won't totally void the warranty of your system. After the system is fix , then you reinstall windows operating system and in case another hardware issue occur. The parts replacement or the needed repair for that issue will be covered by the warranty of your system.

He is responding to an incident where Linux is installed during a hardware failure.[/INDENT]

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-08-24
Illegal, at least in my part of the world.

If the warranty is provided, it is under the condition that the product be used in a manner that a reasonable person would; i.e. following the instructions and not doing anything dumb. If you install Linux onto your computer, and the hardware develops a fault that cannot be linked to your Linux usage, the manufacturer must still stand by its warranty.

Or think about it this way. You buy a coffee machine which comes with a complimentary bottle of descaler. A month in, you use the descaler. A month later, you buy a generic brand descaler and use it in your machine. 5 months later, one of the buttons on the electronic control panel stops working. The manufacturer cannot announce that the warranty has been voided by the use of the 3rd-party descaler -  the descaler has obviously NOT caused the fault.

I would check the local laws and see if the manufacturer has a leg to stand on with this claim.

### Post by Blahah on 2009-01-11
Just to clarify for anyone still curious about this issue:

Installing Linux does not void any hardware warranties or guarantees, at least in the USA or in the EU.

In the USA, the Magnusson-Moss Warranty Act ensures that post-purchase modifications cannot be used as an excuse by manufacturers to deny you access to repairs under warranty.

In the EU, the 2002 Warranty Directive (which came into force in various EU countries over the next year) also ensures that there is a minimum 2 year warranty with any product. It states that the product can be submitted to any reasonable use for which the type of product would normally be used (i.e. with computers, this includes installing software, and linux is software). In this situation, the manufacturer is required to fulfil their original warranty obligation.

The EU Directive can be found here: **[http://eur-lex.europa.eu/smartapi/cgi/sga_doc?smartapi!celexapi!prod!CELEXnumdoc&lg=EN&numdoc=31999L0044&model=guichett](http://eur-lex.europa.eu/smartapi/cgi/sga_doc?smartapi!celexapi!prod!CELEXnumdoc&lg=EN&numdoc=31999L0044&model=guichett)**
