### Post by nerd0795 on 2009-01-10
I was in band class three days back and the teacher asked me if I knew how to repair laptops.  I said kinda, I know how to fix stuff but if it's hardware related I can't help.  So the next day he gave me a picture of the BSOD.  Saying when he boots up the computer, vista says that it can use startup repair to fix the system.  He said he selected it and he was stuck at a black screen with no activity and stuck there for hours, so he shut it down.  He retried same results.  If he selected start window's vista normally.  Still no luck, this time it gave him a long loading time then a BSOD.  Saying UNMOUNTABLE_BOOT_VOLUME.  I thought it was because of a BOOT SECTOR virus *oh noes*.  I thought this because he said "I don't use an anti-virus.  I do use torrents and limewire though."  He didn't even have a back-up.  He also told me he had precious photos of his new born baby.  The only copy was on his computer.  So I heard about using linux live CD's to back up files when Window's needs to be reinstalled.  So I burned a new copy of 8.10.  (It's nice to have a new live CD because my last one was 8.04 and was a bit scratched and I have a new laptop that I heard works good with Ubuntu).  So I installed uTorrent to make the download which would take a day to 3 hours.  So I burned it with NTI since Acer locked the CD/DVD tray to only work with NTI.  I tried 2 programs and it says the computer is not letting access to the CD tray.  So then I finally burned it hooray.  I also burned a Avira Recovery Disk to get rid of the viruses.

The next day at lunch, after I ate I went and put the Ubuntu live CD in to back up his precious photo's before anything.  After a successful boot up.  Hell broke loose.   It turns out not all the ISO was properly burned.  Some of the cylinders (it said) were corrupt.  Then I said "No, I spent 5 hours to burn the CD so it better work"  I waited 5 minutes and it booted up.  Then I felt the power of Ubuntu.  My last machine is not good with the hardware.  (it has a Pentium D, ATi Intergrated Graphics with bad driver support and less then a gig of ram).  So I forced the logfile of the hard disk to be reset since, the computer didn't shut down right the hard drive was locked.   Following a tutorial I managed to put his precious photos on my thumb drive.  Then he took the photos and deleted them when I said "Keep them on my flash, just in case."  Did he listen?  No.  He put them on the school server.  Then deleted them off my flash drive.  I can understand if he's not comfortable.  But in a situation like this he should of kept it.

I tried everything but I found out that the hard drive was dying.  I spent 3 hours.  Trying everything.  But I was talking to a tech guy after I gave up and said "The hard drives probably dead, send it in to Dell since it's still under warranty."  So he was a bit disappointed since that's his only computer.  But he was happy to have his photos back.  So Ubuntu has saved the day.  

The funny thing is this is the best experience with Ubuntu I have ever had.  Even on a computer with a failing hard drive and missing code Ubuntu managed to save the day.

EDIT:  There was lot's of viruses and spyware on the machine when I was browsing for window's files to see if there was a deleted file.  Seemed there was a folder called "System Important" which I have never heard of as well as "Downloaded Program Files" filled with 3 random named smc files.  Even one saying "Important System Files"
