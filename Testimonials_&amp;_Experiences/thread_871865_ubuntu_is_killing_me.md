### Post by marktribs on 2008-07-27
I played with Ubuntu 8.04 for about 2 months.  I have now finally ditched it, and I'll tell you why.  There are many things about Ubuntu that I like, particularly the security around installing software.  I also like the fact that I did not have to worry too much about viruses.  Ubuntu is probably a great OS, but the one thing that it is not, is that it is not user friendly.  Again one doesn't want to compare it with Windows, but my goodness, Windows is a heck of alot easier to handle.  The terminal stuff reminded me of the time when I got my first pc in 1994, using Windows 3.1.  I simply do not have the time and the patience to go back 15 years in time.  I'm also a visually impaired computer user and have tried out what Ubuntu has to offer in this area, Orca and the other stuff is simpy not up to standard.  Ubuntu might be free, but for me it's simply not worth the hastle.  I will check back in two years time to see whether Ubuntu has become more user friendly, but untill then, XP is what I will use.
