### Post by exploder on 2010-10-10
First off, I am running the x64 version of Maverick and am very happy with it! Maverick is the first release since 8.04 that has not given me any kind of problems with my hardware and works "out of the box".

I like the improvements to the themes and the artwork. Radiance is my theme of choice because I wanted a change from dark themes for a while. I like the default choice of colors used in Maverick, everything is blue and green in most distributions, not a bad thing but it does get a little boring. The use of purple and orange in Maverick has been done in good taste and in my opinion is very attractive.

I like the way that Maverick has a very good light and dark theme included by default, the system is not cluttered with too many themes. The default wallpaper is attractive too, not too busy and it does add elegance to the defaults.

The Ubuntu font is a nice addition and demonstrates further that Ubuntu is looking ahead to the future. The Software Center is a big step forward too. The Software Center looks native in Gnome now and is quickly gaining features. It is clear at this point in time that the Software Center can become a one stop shop for package management and that is important where new users are concerned. The addition of software for purchase opens new doors and opportunities and is a welcome addition.  

I like the Me Menu because I think it will appeal to younger users, I say this because young people seem to always want to be connected as far as social interaction is concerned. The Me Menu keeps everything grouped together and it is an original concept not found in other distributions at this time. I am not suggesting that older users might not like the Me Menu, just that younger users are probably going to put this feature right to use. 

Ubuntu One is an awesome feature, 2 GB of free space is not only appealing but a very generous thing for Ubuntu to do. The way Ubuntu One is integrated into the system makes it easy to access contacts, music and data from anywhere, this is quite a big innovation and it's very useful.

I have read a couple of reviews that have said that Maverick was not much different than Lucid, I have to disagree completely with this. Maverick is much more stable, the look and feel is much more refined, the Software Center is so much better, hardware support is better and you can see where Maverick is looking to the future.

I hope Ubuntu keeps heading in the direction Maverick is going. :)
