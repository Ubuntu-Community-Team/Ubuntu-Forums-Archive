### Post by ddv on 2006-07-19
I installed breezy on a surplus GQ3111

[http://www.fryssupport.net/gq3111.cfmhttp://www.fryssupport.net/gq3111.cfm](http://www.fryssupport.net/gq3111.cfmhttp://www.fryssupport.net/gq3111.cfm)

with a "mere" 10 GB disk swapped in. Suddenly the machine became a far better platform than what was shipped to me with a 40 GB disk and Linspire (the disk I erased and cannibalized for use in another system!).

POP/SMTP Email with Evolution configured easily (still need to create some filters and figure out how to leave the downloaded messages on the server.) HTTP/Web worked automatically. Yesterday, upgraded to dapper online, and the operation went off without a hitch, which very much impresses me with Ubuntu's user-friendliness.

Next up: see if I can learn to play audio files, try to access other machines on my LAN, and learn a little bit more about the boot sequence. Once I learn the ropes, I think I will give this system away to a local school.

I am also a Mac user, but one who has become disillusioned with the direction that Apple seem to be headed presently. If I can figure out how to work with the data I use most often, and if a decent development environment emerges (GNUStep, hint! hint!) I will switch and not look back.
