### Post by PorkyPie on 2009-10-29
[SIZE=2]About two years ago, I decided to switch to linux. I was getting bored with Windows, slowing down all the time. So after some searching, I found Ubuntu. I downloaded it. "Whoah! That's a lot smaller in size to windows!" I was saying in my head, as the iso downloaded. So, the iso downloaded, and was ready to burn. I got a CD, and got roxio/nero (cant remember which!) to burn it. So, after some head-start pointers from ubuntu's handy help site. Actually, to be honest, I didn't really need the installation instructions, I picked it up really quickly. So, i went through it's user-friendly installation wizard, and, before I knew it, ubuntu was installed and ready to go!

So, I rebooted my PC, and, surprised, I saw the login screen really quickly. So I logged in, and my desktop came up. I was a little surprised when I saw two "taskbar's", or panels.[/SIZE]

[SIZE=4]WOW!
[SIZE=1][SIZE=2]
So, I had a messed around, and found my way around it almost instantly. My first, and last problem was that I couldn't connect to the Internet. I figured out that I needed to get Gnome-PPP. After that, things went smooth, and I really liked the idea that ubuntu (or Gnome) had two panels, and that you could resize them, delete some, and add more. What's more, ubuntu had everything I needed, a mail client, like outlook, (although, I switched to Thunderbird ater a while :)) one of the most famous browsers in the world, Firefox, and an office suite, OpenOffice.

So, the overall verdict:

Ubuntu is a revolutionary linux-based OS, and comes with everything the end user needs. It's secure, fast, small (compared to Windows), and is the coolest OS on the planet!

(By the way, my Internet problem was fixed in the next release)
[/SIZE][/SIZE][/SIZE]
