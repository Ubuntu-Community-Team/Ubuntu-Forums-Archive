### Post by harisund on 2010-03-10
As you can see from my profile, I used to use Ubuntu a long time back, around 2005-2006. Ubuntu sucked, Linux sucked, and I just preferred Windows XP. I used to go hunting for plugins from various places, there used to be a "automatix" and what not. 

Fast forward 4-5 years. A geek friend of mine encouraged me to try Ubuntu again, so with a weak heart, and the geek in me decided to "accept the challenge", so to speak. 

The following are what I typically use / want / have come to expect in a OS installation, and Ubuntu's responses as well - 

1. **Skype video chat**. Skype now has a beta client for Ubuntu that does video chat. Awesome. 
2. **Java, Flash, Acrobat Reader plugins for Firefox**. I want to be able to see applets, play Flash games / YouTube etc and be able to read PDFs within the browser, instead of having to download them and use evince. Medibuntu now offers just about everything! 
3. **Hibernation and sleep**, or what I believe are called Suspend to Disk and Suspend to RAM. They seem to work fine on my EEE PC so far .. 
4. **Google Talk Video chat** .. . Earlier I used to use Yahoo! Messenger, but eventually that was dumped and now it is Google Talk using the Gmail chat page. I am surprised and pleased to see Empathy actually supports video chat through XMPP / Google Talk! 
5. **Google Chrome**. Now I have it on Ubuntu as well :) And opera browser. (I like multiple browsers since I log in to the same service with different accounts often) 
6. **Multimedia**. VLC is what I use on Windows, mpd, player etc are pretty awesome here. 
7. **Tablet PC**I am impressed with the way Ubuntu 9.10 automatically recognized my touch screen and stylus (and screen rotation using xrandr) on my old Toshiba M200 Tablet PC. (More on this later though). Win 7 is awesome, but runs kind of slow and getting drivers on Windows XP is a pain (for the Wacom tablets I mean) 
8. **My university wireless** uses an obscure WPA2/ TKIP / PEAP  authentication/encryption, and back in the day I had to edit some crazy config files and hope wpa_supplicant connects. Now it is much easier using NetworkManager, but I still use wpa_supplicant and it works fine nevertheless :) 
9. **A2DP** - Oh my GOD PULSEAUDIO rocks. Switching is seamless. Even Windows doesn't do it this good. I am now using my Jabra 620s to talk, even on Skype and Empathy ! 
10. **Tethering** Nokia e71 through bluetooth. For Windows, Nokia offers PC Suite, but still it would break often. After some searching on the web, I was glad to get it working. Really, glad is an understatement. More like super thrilled, or something. And it feels really geeky too :) 
11. **Startup and shut down times**. Sometimes I wonder why I need to hibernate at all when I can startup and shutdown faster !!!!

I don't know what, but Ubuntu 9.10 totally rocks. I have pretty much ditched Windows now. Everything is just sooooo much easier, especially for a command line junkie (I won't say my command-line fu is awesome, but I am quite fluent at it .. let's just say I have tons of personal scripts with regexes, seds, awks, perl and bash commands :) )

Now, I really feel I should start getting more involved with Ubuntu. What are the channels? I love doing tech support (yeah I am the guy who people around me bring their computers to, irrespective of the OS), and I hang around the IRC channels, and should probably start doing that on the forums as well. But  other than social ways to get involved, what are technical ways to get involved? I really really feel I should give back something to the Ubuntu community for making a rocking distro that brings the fun back into computing .. 

------------------------------------
Only gripe - There's remotely no equivalent to Microsoft OneNote. The note taking abilities are awesome. The nearest to that, Xournal, is ok, but it can't do lasso-select, only rectangular selects :( If Basket could implement some inking capabilities, and if Ubuntu can improve its virtual keyboard capabilities ... maybe I should try and help towards that .. ?
