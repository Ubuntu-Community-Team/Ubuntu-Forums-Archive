### Post by Kourosism on 2007-10-28
I was trying out the Ubuntu Beta LiveCD a few weeks ago, and liked what I saw. For the first time, WiFi on my Advent 7113 worked out of the box, and the whole system seemed to have taken a giant leap forward.

I had previously been dualbooting between PCLOS and Vista - Vista shouldn't have been on the laptop in the first place, because the machine isn't powerful enough for it, but that's what it shipped with and there were no options. PCLOS is OK, but it required a lot of tweaking to get it how I wanted it - I'm a Gnome fan.

I decided to install Ubuntu on the PCLOS partition, live with it a few days and then if I still liked it do a clean install and transfer all my files over from Vista. By mistake, I used the entire harddrive (my only excuse is I had 'flu at the time and wasn't paying attention).

I've now been living with Ubuntu since the start of October.... and I really, *really* like it. It's fast, pretty, and just works. I'm seriously considering installing it on my desktop PC over XP, but I have to convince the better half first.

The two drawbacks:-

I am currently a Napster subscriber, and would like another music subscription service. Sadly there isn't anything, yet, in Linux that does the job. I'll go back to buying CDs.

Very occasionally my WiFi connection will drop for no apparent reason, and not reconnect. This is an inbuilt MSI 6877 on Gutsy with all updates applied and WEP protection (I know...). The router is still up and running - but the laptop just loses the signal, from 90% to nothing. After that it won't connect to anything. Any guesses?
