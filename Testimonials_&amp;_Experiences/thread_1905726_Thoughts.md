### Post by gwjvan on 2012-01-07
I'll start off by saying I have decided to stick with Ubuntu:

**a) It works.** Fundamental things don't randomly fail like they do in other distros.

**b) Unity is great interface design.** It needs work, but most criticism has been knee-jerk reactions from people resistant to change, I believe.

**c) It is still popular and a wise choice for software longevity.** Even if Mint gains, still Ubuntu.


Initially I wanted to support Unity completely. However, there are a few things which have slowly become apparent through experimentation and letting things settle:

**1. Unity needs more customization.** You can tweak a few things at the moment, but it doesn't consistently extend to Unity 2D (which is what I need for my hardware at the moment). Customization seems like an inevitability, however.

**2. An app menu is not backwards, it is efficient.** If you have more than a couple dozen applications installed, instant and hierarchical menus significantly reduce the time taken to open an app (without having to memorize every program's name).

**3. Unity with effects is slow, for some reason.** I don't know why- but xubuntu with similar effects runs more smoothly. I'm guessing they didn't develop this, because they intended for Wayland to take care of this issue (therefore, this will probably be a non-issue within the next couple of releases).
