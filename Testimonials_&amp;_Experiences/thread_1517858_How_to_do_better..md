### Post by Neobuntu on 2010-06-25
I lost some long-term Kubuntu users, this week. A desktop power supply started intermittently failing and their solution was to just buy a $400, Win 7, ultimate system, from Best Buy instead.

The problem: They needed Ubuntu installed (and finished) and that cost money (for them). They'd been confused; about printer compatibility. They were tired of their dual-boot system. They want slightly more current hardware, anyway. Even though they know better, they went BACK to a malware, time wasting system. The problem is, they considered that, the lesser evil. Why?

Why? Because they felt safety in numbers, and calling on their daughters vague ability to help, with their "Windows", and for free. Claiming only the need, for "basic" uses.

MAINLY! This is because, the easy 20 minute install, of Ubuntu STILL requires experience, skill and flexibility to ferret out numerous "fixes" to bring it up to (extreme user friendly) par. THE BAD THING ABOUT THIS, IS TIME! As far as we have come, and understanding how complex systems really are, and even all the politics of closed devices, and non-free tools (that aren't our fault), we have not yet made Ubuntu ready for "real people". Even though we're the poster boy, as such.

Now, I know Ubuntu leads the charge, is ever increasing and actuality beats the Windows world, in many major areas. Yet, we have not won the minds of the public! We HAVE to come up, just like any other underdog has come up, before they were dominantly used. We have to NOT leave any stone unturned; when compared to what and how easy people can do it, on current Windows. 

Plus, and most importantly, we have to go MUCH further; with real world benefits, in order to just, get peoples attention, start the snowball effect, of word-of-mouth (and more real "advertising") and all this, against the well funded FUD.

Therefore, why in the ^$#&, are we still patching up a new "release", so darn much? I mean, auto upgrade fixes are expected; buy WHY are we still playing the game of going to third party guru sites, to get things to "just work"? I'm not even talking about JUST non-free stuff, which is deem to be best tacked on later; for legal reasons. I'm talking about SHOW STOPPERS. Release, after release, after release! SLOW DOWN, on the new release additions, if we have to. Anything; to keep stability, at a maximum. Is the new push for a more polish, working?

If we have too many show stoppers; that aren't quickly and automatically fixed by upgrades, then all the "advancement" in the world, is still a public failure, by in large. I mean, that's a guaranteed way, to always be in the shadow, of closed systems, and largely then marginalized.

We are stagnating, even with all our new abilities. Think about it. We have got to ramp up the so called user-ease, sane defaults and polish! It's a moving target and one that we need to be excellent, at any give time. That, is the challenge. Can we step it up?

It's always darkest before the dawn. Are we going to quit now? Status quo, heel no!
