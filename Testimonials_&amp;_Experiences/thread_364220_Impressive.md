### Post by niteshifter on 2007-02-18
I had been running Edgy from a LiveCD for a couple of weeks and a few days ago took the plunge and did an install - even bought a new 120GB drive for the occasion. I had written a lengthy post for here, but making a long story short about putting Edgy (dual boot with XP) on a Dell 8600 laptop:

I am impressed. Very impressed. And I've been around computers - all sorts - for a very long time, I don't impress that easy.

"It Just Works" - pretty much, Yes. A bit of a hitch with the installer doing the partitioning. I'm old school Unix - I want /, /root, /boot, /tmp, /var, /usr, /home and of course swap all separate. Methinks asking installer to do that all in one whack may have been the cause of it balking, but setting them by hand, one at time, with Gparted worked. After that, things went perfectly.

Even setting up wireless with WPA wasn't a problem once I settled down and actually read things ;) Also, the one thing I thought I'd have the devil of time getting work, setting up to use my cell phone (RAZR V3) as a modem proved easy, thanks to wvdial. 

Like many another poster here, I've my Windows horror stories and the impending doom known as Vista were the prime motivators for my need to find an OS I can work with.  I've got
one now! 

To the folks that made Ubuntu possible: Outstanding job. You should be proud. You've my eternal gratitude.
