### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-04-10
One thing that Mint has going for it that really makes a difference is [COLOR="DarkGreen"]MintUpdate[/COLOR]. It allows the user to ignore updates; to install only certain ones. Updates are categorized according to safety, with Level 1 being "Mint approved" - meaning helpful, not harmful; won't b0rk your system... all the way to Level 5 (untested, potentially hazardous, certainly unnecessary). For novices (and semi-novices like me, lol) who may not know how to determine if an update is dangerous or not, this is a very valuable tool. **All one has to do is count the number of "update b0rked my (fill in the blank: Video, audio, wireless, boot-up, whatever)" threads in these forums to know why this matters so much.** Many new users will simply upgrade everything that is upgradeable, supposing that "newer must be better." But many times the newest thing will cripple a working system, and Mint adds an extra "layer of protection" to help prevent that.
