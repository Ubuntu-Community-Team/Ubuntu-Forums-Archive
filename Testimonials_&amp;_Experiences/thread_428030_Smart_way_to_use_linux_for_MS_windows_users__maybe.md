### Post by Atmosphere on 2007-04-29
Greetings all!

As you can tell from my bean count i'm fairly new to these forums and just as new to Linux (using) and Ubuntu. I say using linux because i've been buying linux guides to the last 10 years or so but just haven't understood grasped the whole concept till now (i was very young 10 years ago hehehe). 

Spending alot of time in the "uber noob" section of the forums i see alot of people throwing in the towel on ubuntu/linux, i can understand why, though some times i do wonder why they switched in the first place. I've finally tried out linux/ubuntu because i was just really bored and decided it was time to learn something where the community is extremely helpful (to be honest i thought almost all forums for any topic in the world was helpful till i came here, ubuntu beginner forums i think is probably the closest one could get to a utopia of helpfulness).

Anyway like many users out there i was brought up on DOS 5, then moved on to Norton Commander with abit of X-tree on the side then the progression through the various MS Windows version. so being very used to the Microsoft way of thinking linux was certainly a hurdle if not a mountain. So how does someone who still believes there is a place for Microsoft Windows (oh i do love my Battlefield 2) makes the transition to linux. Answer is fairly simple. I kept my gaming machine dedicated to windows as is my file server, however i have a laptop which i use purely for my business which i don't do anything out of this world with (email, internet etc)which i installed plain old ubuntu on, this means i still need to use a computer with linux on there every day if only for a couple of hours, this ensured i adjusted into the gnome environment easily. 

Then i also aquired a old server box (P4 2.8, SCSI drives, 2gb ECC ram) which i now use to install various distro's of linux to play around with and to try the more outlandish experimental stuff with so that if something does go wrong... it really doesn't matter. It's gone through fedora, Xubuntu and is currently sitting on Kubuntu right now, though i'm thinking of trying out Dreamlinux tonight or give Compiz a go.

I find this a really good mix as Microsoft windows is still my primary OS though i get my "5 cups of ubuntu" maybe not a day but at least a week. For all those who are thinking of moving to linux/ubuntu from windows i strongly suggest reading this website first (nothing technical just food for thought before making a big move). [http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm](http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm)
The first few paragraphs are without a doubt what people need to consider before they move from windows to linux.

I know my solution to acustom ones self into the linux world maybe a little heavy on the resources (space in the house or financially) but i believe it's definately a great equilibrium for any of you who are serious about your computers to try. In todays modern environment your OS is really as much part of your everday life as is your car/clothes/house (thats if you've found yourslf on these forums).

Also i believe linux is only as useful as the amount of troubles you have. The worse experience you have the more you perservere the more you learn.

/end preach

### Post by Hendrixski on 2007-05-02
Cool.  Welcome to the Community!  The best thing about Ubuntu is not so much the software, it's the community.  So the best way to learn is not to suffer, it is to come across something unfamiliar and either read a document (wiki.ubuntu.com or anything on Google) or to ask someone in the community (the forums, the loco team, friends, etc.)

Check out the loco team for your area.  It's a lot of fun, and you'll learn a lot of cool stuff in addition to meeting other people who share some interests with you.
