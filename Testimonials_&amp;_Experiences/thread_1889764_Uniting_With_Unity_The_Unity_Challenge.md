### Post by directhex on 2011-12-06
Christ on a bike... [SIZE="4"]**_*VLC CAN NEVER BE A DEFAULT PLAYER AND REMAIN USEFUL*_**[/SIZE]

This keeps coming up, and people keep ignoring the reply because they don't like it.

VLC needs to be built with every single supported codec bundled in. Some of those codecs - useful ones you want - cannot ship on the CD without placing Canonical under insurmountable legal risk (i.e. they WILL get sued out of existence). You can build VLC without those codecs enabled... but you can't re-add them later. That's the design problem with VLC. It's great strength - supporting everything out of the box - is also its biggest failing.

GStreamer players are popular as defaults because gst is modular - you can ship "safe" codecs out of the box, and if the user plugs more functions in later, that's their problem. Hell, there's even a legal MP3 decoder for GStreamer in the archive, which is offered for install nowadays straight from the CD. There's no VLC equivalent.

By splitting the codec license issue into per-codec issues, you can overcome it. VLC doesn't have that luxury. And can never be on the CD as a result, without making it useless.

### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2011-12-06
I don't mean to rag on VLC, I find it to be a particularly handy piece of software.  But the fact of the matter is, it has an ugly interface and it is a QT app.  Not only that, but it's a QT app that doesn't use the standard QT tool kit.  It doesn't fit in well with Ubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu/Lubuntu.  At one point, VLC was pretty much the only way to handle multimedia on Linux.  But the native multimedia libraries have matured to the point where they equal or surpass VLC in functionality.  If there is an effort to properly port VLC to GTK and QT, then it may stand a chance at becoming the default player.  But the fact of the matter is, by default it handles restricted formats that are available under a different licensing scheme than the rest of Ubuntu, so a "VLC Lite" or "VLC Open Source Edition" are made, VLC doesn't stand a chance.
However, I see no reason why Deluge isn't the default.  Virtually the first thing I do on a fresh install is remove transmission and install Deluge.
