### Post by speedkreature on 2008-02-11
Not all video formats or flash based apps will work "out-of-the-box" because they aren't free (as in freedom). However, getting them to work is hardly work at all and with all the support available on this forum, there's hardly a problem that could be encountered that couldn't be solved.

The short answer to your question of whether or not Ubuntu (or indeed any flavor of Linux) is right for you is: A bsolutely, if that's what you want.

If you have a particular internet poker site you use, there may be specific steps required to get it working but that shouldn't be a problem unless it relies heavily on ActiveX. (Other members feel free to correct me at any point)

There are at least half a dozen office suites available on Linux, and a very good one (OpenOffice.org) comes pre-installed with Ubuntu.

Dell or System76?  Take your pick.  

One thing I will say is that System76 watches and responds to their section of the forum with diligence...Though honestly, I've never monitored Dell's forum.
Another thing to note is that you don't need as much raw power with Ubuntu vs. Vista.  I'm not about to get into that debate but I will say that Linux, in general, is more efficient with it's resources.  That means that unless you are determined to have the absolute best you can squeeze out of your budget, there is no need to strain your wallet.

Case and point:  I have a Dell 5100 Inspiron notebook that I did a processor swap on to increase battery life.  It has a 1.7GHz Pentium-M with 512MB of DDR-400 and a 7200 RPM drive and it is every bit as fast as my work notebook running Vista Business (32-bit) and it's specs are: Lenovo T61, 2.6GHz Core 2 Duo, 2GB of DDR2-667, 7200 RPM hard drive.  Both notebooks perform as they should:  Smooth, consistent and with minimal delay.  I have put Vista Business on the Dell and the result was dismal.

I'll correct you, then. The hassle with Internet poker is that they need a client and/or engine to be installed before you can play the game on the site. Unfortunately, this software is never available for Linux. So out of the box, no, you won't be able to play online poker on Ubuntu.

Conceivably, you may be able to get it to run under Wine. I'm not sure, since I haven't tried - I quit internet poker when I made the switch to Ubuntu (my girlfriend seems to think this is a good idea :))

Best solution I think, would be to run it from in VMware or VirtualBox. A Virtual environment won't handle 3d graphics, but if you play on a low-graphics site it should be no problem at all.

### Post by lespaul_rentals on 2008-02-13
I would recommend either Linux Mint or openSUSE 10.3 (if you have a good computer I'd go with the latter).  Both have codecs pre-installed so you can browse YouTube, listen to your mp3 collection, and  the like pretty much out of the box.  Working with spreadsheets and word processing, no problem.  Both are very newbie-friendly as well (especially openSUSE, you will pretty much never have to go into the command-line with it).

They do have desktop icons like Windows.  One of my favorite things about openSUSE is its menu.  Vista did something right when they added the search function to the bar, and openSUSE implements that same idea.  If you lost track of a file or application, just type it in the search bar and the menu will help you find it.  openSUSE is **very** newbie-friendly as I said before.  My recommendation if you want to install a distro to your computer.

Let me know if you have any questions.  Welcome to the forums.  :)
