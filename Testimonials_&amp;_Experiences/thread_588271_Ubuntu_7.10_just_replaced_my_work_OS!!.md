### Post by taonari on 2007-10-23
I finally broke down and installed Ubuntu 7.10 on my work desktop, replacing Windows XP Professional.  The installation went well and took just about 30 minutes and I was up and running in no time at all.  

Our new helpdesk trouble ticketing for the agency I work for is web based so I can use Firefox right away to get to work.  Gnome-RDP is a must, for it lets me remote in and do maintenance on our Windows 2003 Servers.  I also use Pidgin to log into our company's jabber server and of course I added my MSN and AIM accounts as well.  :-)

Samba was a breeze to setup and I have my CDROM and public folder shared so I can access data from remote locations for reinstalls at different workstations if needed.  This computer is quite old but runs Ubuntu well.  Below are my specifications.

Intel 1.8GHz Pentium 4 processor
Asus P4T-E motherboard
512MB PC800 Rambus memory
eVGA Geforce 6200 128MB AGP video card
300GB Maxtor IDE HDD
32X Liteon CDRW
Soundblaster Audigy PCI sound card


While I have used Gentoo for a couple of years I will say overall that I am quite pleased with Ubuntu's ease of installation and the speed of which apps can be installed since they are not compiled from source.  I feel confident in recommending Ubuntu to anyone that wishes to try something other than Windows or that can't afford Windows.

Well done Ubuntu...
