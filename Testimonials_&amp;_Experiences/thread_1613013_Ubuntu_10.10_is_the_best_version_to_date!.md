### Post by usagiakumu on 2010-11-03
Things are a little slower than previous versions and sure they aren't snappy, but it is finally rock solid. It feels like a desktop OS rather than a sum of FOSS software. I have read through the forums, and to those who get a blank screen, make sure you do the updates, they contain fixes for this well known issue. Blame nautilus. I have even switched my younger sister Kim, my younger brother Matt, and surprisingly enough my dad is soon to follow after XP needs its umpteenth reinstall.

I decided to test out a SSD I had lying around with Windows 7 Enterprise 90 day trial to see what all of the hullabaloo was about. I made it to day six before I started having issues. All I did was leave it pristine, did the updates, install drivers, install Firefox, a few FOSS applications: LibreOffice, Gimp, Pidgin, and the OGG codecs. It had to go into this "recovery mode" and it would only boot to the "recovery mode" and not let me to my desktop. Obviously the HDD was fine because I booted to Linux and did a full scan of the disk overnight and everything was kosher. When will Microsoft learn that you need stability. I installed EXT3 on it and put it in my Popcorn Hour. It has 30+ movies on it and runs great.

I booted back to my 500G Ubuntu drive and everything was as I left it.

My other computer has gone through every single upgrade since 6.04, and is happily running 10.10. Never needed to do a clean install ever. It is nice to know I can rely on Ubuntu.

My two systems are:

Pentium 4 1.89GHZ
256 MB RAM
40 Gig HDD

AMD Athalon 5000+ X2
3.5G DDR2 533
500G Sata
EVGA GTX 260

### Post by usagiakumu on 2010-11-04
Yeah my Pentium 4 machine runs very well, obviously no OpenGL supported, so some applications and desktop effects do not work, but they never did. I mainly use that machine with a 1tb HDD as a catch-all for the files I need. I will use that machine to rip a movie, then transfer it over the network to my Popcorn Hour. I use it for all my pictures and music. 

Most of my hardware is new-ish, all new when I bought it. 9.10 worked beautifully while I had ALSA issues and screenlet issues with my dual core with 10.04. The Pentium 4 chugged along nicely and runs 10.10 without issue. All hardware I installed 10.10 on did not have issues on before I installed it either, maybe that's why it has been the best experience for all of us.

My dad has a Gateway with a Celeron 2.44Ghz, 1gb DDR ram
My brother Matt has a Dell Optiplex 4400 1gb DDR2 RAM
My sister Kim has an old HP with 768MB DDR ram with an Athalon 1500
My Landlord has a HP Pavilion g60 laptop I installed it on and he loves it

Obviously a wide range of hardware, all of which work perfectly without hassle with 10.10. Yet I keep reading how other computers have this black screen. I had a hiccup with Nautilus and my TV Tuner card, but I expected the issue as I had to do some funky junk with each and every Ubuntu install, except for 10.10. It was not working and I was not going to go compile a specific kernel and mess with the files just yet. If I tried enabling it it would black screen. Then an update came, I sat down to enable it to get the error code, and it simply worked without a single issue.

I have external HDDs that work. I have cameras that are and have been notoriously "Windows only" that seem to work now. Everything works over my lan and I have yet to configure a single thing. 

I agree, that while some computers thrown in the trash don't need to be there, but most do. If it doesn't work, throw it out and get another.
