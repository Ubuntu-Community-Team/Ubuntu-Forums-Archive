### Post by TurboRush on 2009-01-03
Quick history... have been running Ubuntu 7.10 exclusively on my main machine for over a year now and have been extremely impressed with the performance I got out of my dual core with 3gig of ram vs Vista. It currently runs 2 VMs (that support my website) as well as my media server, etc.. while still being lightning quick and with my only restarts coming from kernel upgrades or just power outages... 

Now, on to the purpose of this review. I've recently finished my "theater" to the point of usability. I've been enjoying a good bit of football downstairs and realized I needed a laptop in the area so I could surf and read while watching movies/games... the solution... a crappy old dell laptop with a p4 and 500m of RAM which had since long been retired and collecting dust because it couldn't handle Windows duties anymore + Xubuntu. I am happy to say in less than 15 minutes of pre-game + 1 quarter of the Indy v SD game we are up and running. 

Updates are going as we speak, but everything else seems to be in good shape.

For those movie / videophiles, these are some shots of my setup... nothing overly impressive or expensive, but damn I love watching fights and sports on it :P. I still have quite a few loose ends to clean up obviously; Connections at the receiver are pending arrival of wall plates, need a permanent mount for the center channel, and the screen was a cheap unit to ensure I was happy with the fit and size.. it'll be replacing it with a permanent non-retractable wall unit shortly.
