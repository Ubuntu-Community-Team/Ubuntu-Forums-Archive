### Post by donato roque on 2010-09-09
I just finished installing Fedora 13 side by side with Ubuntu.  I use Ubuntu 10.04.  I am not a linux power user but the command line is just fine with me.  I just want to say one thing.  Ubuntu is just a world away from the rest of the other user friendly linux distros.

The first time I tried to follow howto's in Ubuntu back in Ubuntu 6.10 Edgy, I successfully installed my nvidia driver.  I've been consistently hitting success whenever I follow instructions in this forum or the documentations.  I've never spent more than one afternoon when I install Ubuntu, that includes downloading and burning the image.  

I never had persistent problems with 3D accelerated driver installations, proprietary formats and codecs and adobe flash installations in Ubuntu.  At first I thought I was just lucky.  That I should not be bragging like this fearing I might be throwing away my luck.  

But here see, I can't get the nvidia drivers to work with Fedora.  I've been at this since Fedora 11, 12 and now 13. 

You guys must be doing something different.  Take a bow.  You deserve it.

PS.
Fedora 13 has a bug with YUM, their package manager.  The initial update after installation doesn't start. It wont update.  Go figure.  I have no problem with Synaptic nor APT.  I read that Software Center is proceeding fine but if it ain't broke don't fix it.
