### Post by dave0109 on 2011-10-17
I've been using Ubuntu since 8.04 and have used every release in between. When I upgraded to 11.04 I stuck with Unity for a while but eventually ditched it because it was slowing me down.

When I switched back to Ubuntu classic desktop, I found out that 2 of the things slowing me down weren't really part of Unity.  They were: global menu and overlay scrollbars.  Sure, they save a few pixels on a netbook, but on my 1920x1200 display, I cared less for that than how far I have to move the mouse to get to the menus, or that I had to move the mouse to just the right point to pick up the scroll bar control.  So I got rid of them, and rode it out for another 6 months.  In that time, I suffered 5 complete system freezes.  Something that I hadn't experienced for years.

This weekend, I "bit the bullet" and upgraded my netbook to 11.10.  Obviously my 2 main gripes were still there and now the launcher seems to have a mind of it's own.  I've set it to Dodge and Autohide, but when a window is maximised (like they mostly are on a netbook - and the only way to make sense of the global menu), the launcher doesn't seem to want to unhide... but wait, an hour later it's OK.  Then later it doesn't work, then it does.  <sigh>  Yeah, I suppose we should all be power users and stick with the keyboard shortcuts.  You try telling my Mother. :D

So where to go for my main system.  I tried Debian, Linux Mint, Kubuntu and finally Xubuntu. I also use RedHat and CentOS at work, so I didn't really go there.

Wow.  As soon as I had Xubuntu running up I had a great feeling about it. It's fast for a start.  It looks clean and smart without resorting to lots of animations, shadings and shadows.  It's massively configurable without needing extra packages.  It has a good set of default apps - there are some changes for me to deal with, like the music and video players, but they look fine at first glance.  And, as I discovered late in the day, it has a dock that works.  I didn't see it at first because it was hiding at the bottom, but it shows when it should and hides away again. Brilliant.

So I'm happy that I can stick with the overall Ubuntu distro and (for me) the Xubuntu team have done a fantastic job in creating a clean, crisp and responsive desktop.  Well done all.  I'll be installing it on the netbook later as well.  Then my Mother's next week. :)
