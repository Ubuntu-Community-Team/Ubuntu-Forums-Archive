### Post by germclown on 2009-10-30
*This is *not* a "not ready yet" rant*

Just installed UNR 9.10 and, while everything under the hood seems to be just fine (big kudos to the team for that :KS ), the superficials left me feeling a little miffed, even a bit alienated. I know the tiny-screened EEEs are a fairly small (and shrinking) segment of the netbook hardware out there, but isn't it still worth testing the UI on these things?:confused:

Boot graphics are totally fine. More gold stars for those: :KS

But what's with the new desktop UI? The 9.04 one fit on the screen at least. It handled all the default categories fine, and all the Places too! 9.10 inflates the icons, buries folders and settings further into the UI, and still I have to scroll down to see the System super-category which is now so full of stuff that my edge scrolling hopped over some icon rows (scroll amount seems to depend on how much is in the window, since it isn't that jumpy in other categories). Gripe #2 about the new desktop is that it **almost** (but not quite) fits 3 rows of icons on the 701. So I get the chopped, non-rounded edge at the bottom, and about 20px of scroll. You couldn't pull even a handful of pixels out of the icon spacing to have this fit properly? Very sloppy. And that reminds me: why is the icon window cut off at what look like 5-10px margins instead of scrolling right to the screen edge and top panel? The gap looks very strange.

I gave the Software Centre a go and noticed some more 701 unfriendly design. It isn't nearly as ergonomically or aesthetically offensive as the desktop, but it is about icon spacing again. I see 3 columns of app categories and a giant, empty gap on the right. Move the divider 10-20px to the left and BAM! All sidebar text is still visible, and I now have 4 columns of icons evenly filling the rest of the window. So: why isn't the divider to the left a bit by default? Or (assuming that the position is chosen automatically) why can't the icon spacing be shrunk by just the tiniest bit?

And the biggest complaint of all, not just because it's a pain to me, but because smart, Masters degree-earning people like my wife can't figure it out, has to do with the very large number of default apps/dialogues/whatever that **don't fit on the freaking screen**! Many do (which is awesome) but I'm getting a stronger and stronger feeling that those work mostly by accident. Here's a quick list from poking around a little: Appearances, Display, Main Menu, Keyboard, Sound, USB Startup Disk Creator, Movie Player (which should be picked up by Maximus), Network Tools, Software Sources. That doesn't even include the various in-app dialogues that escape the screen. Why is this a problem? Besides the fact that it looks awful, it's extremely confusing and hard to use because all of the Confirm/Cancel buttons are nowhere to be seen because they're off the screen! I knew about the alt-drag way to move windows, but my wife didn't, and was vainly trying to resize or drag the titlebar upwards. #-o

I wouldn't think it was so hard to fit a UI onto a screen, even if it is a really small one (but still more pixels than I had for Win3.1). My 9.04 memories are already faded a bit, but I'm pretty sure I found at least a few windows that have been fixed for 9.10. But there's a lot of cleanup still to do. As far as the 701 is concerned, the UNR GUI is still beta quality.

I'm loving 9.10 desktop and it looks like all my gripes would vanish on even a 9" screen, so I know the Ubuntu teams can pull it off. And if anyone knows what a non-programming (web layout, that's it) graphic/industrial designer type could do to help out, point the way!

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-03-10
Unfortunately, 7-inch screen owners are left out in the cold a bit. Only a small number of machines shipped with such a small screen, and the majority of netbooks have a 9 inch or larger screen.

The EeePC 701 was pretty much a 'beta' netbook as the hardware itself doesn't look a lot like what netbooks quickly became.

I guess the feeling is that these computers will vanish before too long, their owners buying 9 inch or 10 inch netbooks. An effort to optimise the interfaces of programs to fit onto 7 inch screens would therefore be an inefficient use of resources.

Sorry!

(I had one of the SSD Aspire One machines, which was fairly much 'beta' itself, so I know how it feels).
