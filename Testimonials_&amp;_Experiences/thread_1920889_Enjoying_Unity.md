### Post by Version Dependency on 2012-02-08
> **pbpersson said:**
> Okay, so a question for people who love Unity.  

I am attaching a small screen shot showing how I find games in the old Gnome menu.  I click on games, I see what I have installed on the machine - it takes two  seconds to find anything I want.

In Unity I had to click a special icon, then I had to tell it I wanted games, then it showed me dozens of screens of icons with no descriptions.  It would take me at least ten times longer to find anything I wanted.

What am I doing wrong? I would like to instantly see everything I have installed in one screen with descriptions.  Is that a configuration option?

Sifting through the dash for an application is rarely needed.  The only time I really need to scroll through all my apps in the dash is if I don't know the name of the app...or I simply want to see what all is installed.  

First, add all your commonly used apps to the launcher.  

Second, for apps that  aren't on your launcher but you know their names...hit the SUPER key and type the first 2 or 3 letters.  Then click the app with the mouse or, better yet, continue with using the keyboard and just hit the down ARROW key to highlight the app and hit ENTER.  Takes no more than two or three seconds to do all that.

BTW, if for some reason you just need to have a traditional menu, there are Indicators that provide this: [http://askubuntu.com/questions/30334/what-application-indicators-are-available](http://askubuntu.com/questions/30334/what-application-indicators-are-available)
