### Post by RebelwithoutaClue on 2008-03-10
...IT ALL WORKED!

Having used and supported PCs for decades and being uncomfortable at my brief encounter with Unix I had avoided it for years.  Through Digg I became aware of Ubuntu and eventually my curiosity and a front-paged Ubuntu stuff article got the better of me and I downloaded Gutsy.

In fact I downloaded it several times, the first download not working, the second and third having the wrong checksums - but I burnt the fourth and ran the Live CD.  I had sorted out a 'spare' HDD for my laptop and thought that would be the best way to test it.  Though I accepted after doing a little bit of research that I would have to expect nic, video and/or sound driver issues.

Pawing my way through the Live CD I got to the point that I was trying to install stuff.  This is when I realized I was still running from the CD and it wasn't a proper install!  So I did the install.

It took longer than I expected, mostly because everything else had been so fast.  I'm no lover of the beige skin, but I knew it would be customizable and soon changed it - rushing for the Compiz eye-candy - particularly to show off to the kids!  NEW TOY!

The simple fact is that on my IBM T41 with a third-party PCMCIA wireless card everything worked.  Sound and Vision and Wi-Fi connectivity with minimal configuration.

Having used Ms Office forever I see it being a long time until I could live with Open Office - I tried it on XP, but simply lost patience.

I was sorely disappointed that there isn't a current Winamp for Linux - grip is ugly and clunky for ripping, Amorok couldn't play my files from the server and Rhythmbox does but again it is drab and lacks gloss.  I tried to install XMMS2 but it didn't come easy and as a tourist still, I just made do.

I still chuckle when I check the disk usage - compared to Windows and I love the performance - though startup is slower for me than XP.

I'm sure Open Office will get better and sooner or later there will be a really good music player for Linux.

That said, for an average user I think this has got to be the way forward.

All I can say is my own experience - it was trouble-free and everything worked.  It wish it was shinier, but I do like it.  :KS

### Post by julian67 on 2008-03-10
There's no shortage of good music players for linux. For a winamp type player you might like audacious, it used winamp skins, is lightweight and easy to customise with OSD, tray icon menu pop up, cover disply etc. There are some other nice ones like gmusicbrowser which is similar to.....err...nothing, it's just its own thing, takes a while to get your head a round it and then it's great. 

CD ripping: Asunder is great, it isn't in the official repositories but you can download a deb and easily install click click. It's simple and effective and looks good.

[http://littlesvr.ca/asunder/]("http://littlesvr.ca/asunder/")

Also soundjuicer is fine. As long as you have a modern optical drive which doesn't cache audio data then the linux rippers will rip every bit as well as EAC (the pedant/audiophile's favourite in Windows).

Anyway it's nice to hear you're having a pleasant experience. The world of free software is huge and expanding, it will probably take a while to start to realise what's out there and available to you what with unfamiliar names, a whole other universe of websites, projects etc to be discovered. Be prepared to be happily surprised repeatedly!
