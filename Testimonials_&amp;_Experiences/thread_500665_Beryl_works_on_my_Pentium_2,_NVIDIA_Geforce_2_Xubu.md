### Post by FSHero on 2007-07-14
Hi all,

I don't know if this is the right place to post this, but I **had** to spread this news:

OMG! I have got Beryl working on a:
Pentium 2 333Mhz
384 MB RAM
NVIDIA GeForce 2 MX 400
Xubuntu desktop (added to a Kubuntu installation)
... comp!

I thought minimum specs were a GeForce 3, 1.7GHz P4? Therefore I am astounded, shocked, and delighted!

The performance is what shocked me, though. Everything is smooth! (Except resizing windows... more on this later.)
That includes:
Fading in/out menus
Dragging windows
Multi workspace cube
Multi window view (when you move the mouse to the top-right-hand corner)
...!

The only thing causing problems is resizing windows: it is quite unresponsive. For some reason, when I drag the mouse quickly during a resize (to size it around the window's centre), the performance is better.

So, what does everyone think? Impressive, eh? :guitar:

### Post by FSHero on 2007-07-14
Solution:
(to the window resize problem)

I went to Beryl Manager, --> Window Management along the top, --> Resize window on the left-hand side --> For resize display mode, I selected Outline. This causes almost no problems. (The title acts a bit laggy, though. But I don't care! Beryl works!)
