### Post by Enmi on 2008-06-23
I know there have been quite a few posts about how bad Hardy has been in someone else's personal experience. I did my research, as I've usually done before upgrading or diving into a new OS, even if it's just a new version of one of the best (at least in my opinion) operating systems available today.

I remember reading over those "[lockup warnings]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=768200")" posted everywhere about hard locks no one could figure out what causing. Trust me when I say that after becoming comfortable with stability, it's one of the things I've come to expect and look for in any operating system more than any other feature. As much as I like the newest and fab.

So... after much deliberating and contemplating I decided to take the plunge and do a complete full install of the new OS. 

Having downloaded the ISO, and [prepped a handy 2GB flash drive]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=811397") instead of a CD for installation media, I booted into the Live version.

I was impressed, even when running off the LiveCD/USB version, the OS seemed to be very responsive. And yes, I'm comparing this to a 7.1 LiveUSB I have tested and used with the same laptop, and I've been using Ubuntu since the first major release of the OS, 4.10 "Warty Warthog", and before then, Fedora and Peanut linux, and before then Mandrake... and a few other flavors I was sampling out.
__________________________________________________

Two days later, having found the time to do so, I found myself backing up my files and starting the install.

For informational purposes, the laptop I'm using is a [Gateway MX6708]("http://support.gateway.com/s/Mobile/Q106/MystiqueC/6244sp3.shtml") with a Dual-Core Centrino Duo, Intel Pro WiFi chipset, a replaced internal WD 320GB SATA HDD, along with an ATI X1400 dedicated graphics adapter. I've also outfitted the laptop with two gigs of ram to boot. Something that helps when fiddling with Adobe Premiere, even under a stripped down XP install. :)
__________________________________________________

The installation was equally impressive, everything running very smoothly. It finished without any hickups, and even properly mounted my NTFS partitions, and autoconfigured the built-in card reader and modem, something that Gutsy Gibbon (7.10) couldn't do at first. Something else that Hardy did, again to my astonishment, was prompt me to download and update my graphics driver *correctly*, even if it was running quite smoothly under vesa, considering my setup.

Again, the update ran just as smoothly as everything else, so far. Getting Compiz effects was equally easy, and I can now run 3d and opengl apps without causing serious harm to my eyes and ears as Gutsy churns whatever is running into some sort of paste. Then again, that could have been a configuration issue on my part... nevertheless, the process was incredibly easy to do, and I didn't even have to do any modifications or hacking on my part till after I ran all system updates!

I added a few other repositories for multimedia, my dock, and installed / replaced firefox with the current final release, which again went without issues, and I'm about to install the graphical grub like I had before.

So far, since I've upgraded / reinstalled, I haven't had any freezing issues, no hangs or systemwide errors. The laptop's been on for the last 36 hours straight since the update after install, and it's all been quite good. Except for the very small bother of it not auto-mounting my NTFS 16GB flash drive like Gutsy used to so after bit of hacking.

One thing to add that I've been shocked, and almost did a double-take on:
Since having used Ubuntu, I haven't had a single version that managed to suspend on my laptop at all, let alone smoothly.
I just hit the suspend key on the laptop, and after a few seconds the screen went black, cursor on the top left and went dim. Yes, the laptop had gone into suspend mode, since the power light slowly went on and off... I pressed the power button and after about... 3 or so seconds, Ubuntu prompted me for my password.
__________________________________________________

Yes, I am most definitely impressed. In my *initial* take on the OS, I am very happy with how hardware compatibility has gotten much better, as well as performance of the operating system itself.

I am sorry to hear that many other people are having problems, but then again: Since when HAVEN'T there been problems with installing an operating system on many different system configurations? Every major release of DOS, Windows, Linux distributions of all kinds and flavors, heck even with the fabled Mac OS there have been troubles here and there, and I haven't even gotten to server OS yet.

No **one** operating system will be suitable for all, just as much as no one medicine is suitable for all illnesses. 

However, from a power user who takes using a computer and turns that concept into power testing instead of simply using for day to day, personal and business: This is a great OS for just that, and then some. It may fit some, it may fit you, but like me: till I tried it out and saw for myself the kind of potential it had, I was just hesitant and curious as to what the hype was about.

Thanks, Cannodial for your work in releasing this, and the community with which I wouldn't know what foot I'd be standing on for *your* hard work in testing, playing with, and sharing the good and the bad alike. God bless you all.



Enmi M Rivera,
Ubuntu user and Linux geek
