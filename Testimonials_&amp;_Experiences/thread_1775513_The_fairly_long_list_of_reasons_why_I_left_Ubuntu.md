### Post by twtgd09 on 2011-06-04
So I found my vista installation disc today, and without hesitation, re-installed windows. I am currently on the edge of just grabbing my files from ubuntu, and wiping the whole partition, here's a list of reasons why.

Installation -
Installation in ubuntu can be confusing with all of the various means to install, only works about half the time for me, and was frequently a frustrating and overly involved process. I don't want to screw around with ppas and source code and synaptic, I just want to download the file, double click, and then start the install process. Is it that much to ask for?

Interface -
Either you use the default interface that hogs screen space, you adjust the settings and risk your panels never coming back, or you set it up to look like a mac or pc. I haven't seen a single screenshot that didnt look like one of those three things. Also, creating launchers is a royal pain, i should be able to type the applications name, and the system should recognize it without worrying about spaces and dashes and dates and initials in the command line. Ubuntu has no clear edge over either os in this regard.

Media -
This is the biggest problem I have with ubuntu, I use my computer primarily for three things, Internet and Internet Messaging, Work, and Media. If the os cant handle any one of those three, it wont work for me. Ubuntu barely does media, having no one fluid system for managing music and video except for banshee. I _hate_ banshee. It not only uses an ugly browsing system with multiple panes, but it doesn't organize anything in a way that makes sense. Also, I cant hide subtitles on movies. There is no compatibility for apple drm, and its slow. Major hit

Internet - 
I'm a big fan of safari, chrome is faster, but safari works better for me. Nuff said

Now on top of these major problems are all sorts of annoying bugs and glitches that i experience that really wreck the experience. Seriously, I've crashed in windows less than I've crashed in ubuntu.

### Post by twtgd09 on 2011-06-05
I supplied a list as a critique for the devs to consider while working on future OSes, thats what this entire section of the forum is here for, no?

By "browser" I mean the navigation within banshee. I've looked into the issue and there are no other media players than banshee that combine a grid art view and support for video. Of course, banshee doesn't organize video by movies and tv shows, and it doesn't handle metadata gracefully at all. But the biggest problem was that banshee sorts albums alphabetically. This is plausibly the worst imaginable way to sort music shy of sorting by genre. I just can't use a text based interface, I have too much music for that, and the wall of text factor is bad enough to warrant a switch somewhere else. The "simple" players all seem to use a wall of text, and many don't handle metadata. Seriously, do these designers expect us to happily turn back the clocks 10 years to windows media player 5 (i dont remember for a fact which version it was, 5 sounds right) where you had to open songs in windows explorer? Things like the gauda player just sound bad (banshee sounds pretty bad too). 

As for the whole "manual" transmission thing, it seems to be a fairly broken manual transition, half of the ubuntu specific apps I find don't run properly, even from reputable sources (foxit, adobe, etc.) Seriously, compilation has ended up breaking programs that I was trying to upgrade or creating folders and not allowing me to launch anything. I needed to re-install compiz when I screwed up compiling because the instructions they give you don't work. My ppas section has turned into a broken mess and I haven't been told by anyone how to fix it because it appears nobody knows how to fix my problems. I found patches for banshee to fix the godawful grid view problem, but nobody tells you what to do with these things! Some other problems involve sampling rates for audio, I've got a fairly nice usb audio interface that supports up to 96/24, and there's no way to quickly and easily adjust the settings up to 96/24 for my flacs without going into terminal and typing out some bizarre code and "saving" it to a file that I can't find. There are too many options on printing, and none of them ever seem to be set where I need them, I try to print on a color printer, the default is monochrome with weird settings that make it come out as just orange even when I select color. I admire this aspect of mod-ability, but windows, tbh, is more practically mod-able with the advent of regedit, a handful of extra utilities, and two or three guides on the internet. 

And as for the interface, I've tried gnome panel, docky, avn, and glx dock, all with varying set ups. I didn't like any of them. Gnome panel doesn't have a proper hide function, so when I just set it to "hide" and I try to open a facebook chat window, my panel opens up and i minimize the window. That was when gnome panel still behaved right, the thing is, on every computer I've set to auto-hide gnome panel in the past few months, it never unhides itself. The docks and such were better in this regard because I could leave them under my windows, and I could combine both launchers and a convienent place to minimize a window to, but they were too resource intensive. I tried various flavors of ubuntu, xubuntu is ugly and a pain to customize the menu in. Kubuntu was actually pretty good, but in my experience, other flavors in general don't consistently work right.
