### Post by bwitherell on 2010-06-20
I have been using Lucid for a few weeks now and have just really had the time to actually sit down and dive into it recently. I just felt like I should stop and take the time to say what an awesome release this is.

the last few previous releases were great as well but i feel like this release just took Ubuntu to the next level of usability. I'm not a stranger to Linux or Ubuntu at all. I have used many distros and i know my way around. But its just nice not to have to mess with anything to make things work. Everything in this release seems to work out of the box.

The other thing that has greatly improved is the default gnome theme. It looks much better than the same tired old human theme. I know everything is customizable but that takes a lot of time to get things to look just the way you want them too. I actually like the lucid default theme so I don't have to spend any time changing it.

Also, one of the biggest things in my opinion is the native iphone support. This was one of the things holding me back from completely dumping windows. Now i can! I hope the iphone 4 OS will still be compatible with the current iphone software in Lucid.

Thank you to everyone involved in the Ubuntu project for another great release (the best one yet by far!!!) Also, a big thank you to everyone involved in creating the applications that get shipped with Ubuntu.

### Post by bwitherell on 2010-06-21
I do agree that it can be a bit of a pain when installing broadcom wireless drivers but this is only because Ubuntu cannot legally ship the entire driver with the OS for the broadcom wireless cards. This is why you must first connect to the internet and download the bit that interacts with your wireless card's firmware. They actually call the bit you have to download firmware (this though does not really make sense to me because if that was firmware it would mean you would be flashing your wireless card. which you are not doing when you download that software.) 

Anyway, that part of Ubuntu has gotten better anyway. Those of you who have been with Ubuntu/Linux for a few years might recall a time when we once had to use ndis wrappers to the broadcom and other wireless cards going because there used to be almost zero support for wireless cards.

Then came a time when the ndis wrapper was not needed for most cards but for the broadcom's even if you didn't need the wrapper getting the drivers and installing them used to be a huge hassle.

To me it is amazing that in Ubuntu we can now install the broadcom wireless drivers just as easily as we can in windows. Its a great thing that Ubuntu has reached the level of usability where people actually expect it to be easier to use then windows and are a little disappointed when its not easier but only just as easy as windows.

I'm glad there are a few people here who like the release as much as I do. :p
