Well from what I've read about the Apache (about a third of my ancestry, I'm a mix of English, Apache and Cherokee), it was a thing of honor.  For example if you were from a different tribe and came to our village and killed someone, they had the right to kill you, or to capture you and turn you over to the family of the one you killed for them to do with as they pleased.  If you could not be found a member of your family could substitute, or even just a member of your village if you decided to take your family and hide.  It was more about the honor of the one killed than how bad the act of killing was in itself.
