### Post by etomic13 on 2011-11-06
I have been running Ubuntu 11.10 on my MacBook almost exclusively throughout the weekend. I have decided to take up learning Java and wanted to get away from the contrived experience I was feeling with the latest iteration of OSX. I decided to give Ubuntu another try full time, I was impressed by the advancements in Oneiric particularly with the Unity interface and I decided I wanted to leap back into Ubuntu and begin learning Java.

I needed to tweak a few things like changing a value that makes the touchpad respond after resuming suspend. I have also noticed that I lose sound control periodically, I dont actually lose sound but I the ACPI keys dont respond and if I go into sound hardware it shows no selectable devices. A log off and log back in seems to resolve this issue that I've only experienced twice. I experienced a strange issue with Firefox when I took the Macbook to a coffee shop. I connected to a network that forces you to accept terms on a browser page before you get Internet access. Accessing the page went fine but I was unable to select a check box and the cursor wouldnt change from the "I" beam even when I wasn't near text. I closed the browser and attempted to re-open it but no amount of clicking would open the application, from launcher or Dash, I even tried a restart and Firefox would refuse to open until I got back to my home network, so finicky!

Other than these few hiccups I have been impressed with the experience and I feel more efficient with the latest iteration of Ubuntu and I plan to run it on one of my machines almost exclusively for code work and general surfing. I still feel like I need to have a Windows box around for true Exchange support and other various applications and games but for the majority of my work I plan to stick with Ubuntu and try to work out the few bugs, I realize I have learned to live with the other bugs that are present in other OS's, the difference is with Ubuntu I feel like I can actually alter the OS to possibly fix a bug instead of learning to live with it.

I really want to love Ubuntu but I still feel that it has a way to go in a few departments such as hardware support, I realize its not fair to fault Ubuntu for hardware manufacturers using proprietary drivers but it seems that other Distros do a better job lately at supporting a wider variety of hardware. I still notice random little bugs that remind me that the OS was developed by many different human beings all engineering the best solution they could for a particular instance. Its charming in a way and I feel like I'm using a solution that was designed as a platform by a community with a wide variety of needs. It still holds a place in my heart and I will still smile every time I boot into Ubuntu, for that one bit of my day I feel free.
