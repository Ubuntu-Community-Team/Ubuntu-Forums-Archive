### Post by shaggly on 2007-08-18
I don't suppose you've read any of the support documents about restricted formats ???

MP3's will play in almost any sound app, providing you read a little before throwing your dummy out of the pram. I use Amarok flawlessly for all sound formats, and Kaffeine works for all visual media formats. However, I did have to do additional setup work and READING. You should be used to this if you used Debian, as you stated. In fact Kubuntu is a lot easier to customise than Debian in its native format.

Also Kubuntu, and Ubuntu for that matter, are not (fortunately) like Windoze. Neither are 99% of other Linux distros. You don't simply install them and use them as you would "that other O/S", but then again you don't PAY like you would with that "other O/S" either. Therefore, certain proprietary formats like MP3's, DVD's etc etc simply cannot be available "out of the box" for legal reasons.

To initially get things working with all the bells and whistles like your old O/S consult the K Menu > Kubuntu System Documentation Index which was install as part of your help docs. Then have a dig around [https://help.ubuntu.com/]("https://help.ubuntu.com/") which will get you everything you need. If it's not there, then bounce across to the community docs link at the top right and search for it.

There's also the Kubuntu forums, as opposed to the Ubuntu forums, which contain some more K flavoured answers.

I've got both Dapper and Feisty installed and working just fine for everything that I might have done years ago in "that other O/S". All media formats, email, browsing, torrents, video editing, DVD & CD ripping, etc etc are all working fine. Moreover, this is also with using a Radeon X1950 Pro hooked up to a TV w/ remote control. All as a result of a little research, patience, and a lot of posts by the great people at these forums.

If you need any assistance, feel free to drop me a PM or reply here. I may not be a guru, but I've been fiddling with my Kubuntu incarnations for 2 years now, and I've kept pretty good notes as I customise.

Lastly, the best way of getting help is to ask for it, rather than give off a "it sucks" attitude. Things may seem frustrating at first, but believe me it's a lot better than sticking with the money draining and restrictive "other app".
