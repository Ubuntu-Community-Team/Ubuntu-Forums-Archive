### Post by Jacobonbuntu on 2011-04-14
....Strange that people always tend to call “their” system "the best". I used to work on Mac, later on Windows; both good systems in their own way (now) I think. Every now and then I installed Ubuntu, and I saw many Linux programs "grow up" from buggy and ugly looking experiments to mature programs. For me the lack of good software was the issue, until now. Although Filemaker, Photoshop/Illustrator/Indesign are giants, I found the alternatives under Ubuntu working fine now, and doing the job very well. Also Musescore (I need a music notation program) is very nice and flexible, once you get used to it!  
Why I am attracted to Ubuntu? I am not sure, but I believe it is the possibility to make it look, feel and work exactly as I want it (and the right to mess it up completely :D), besides the sympathetic idea of open source software, and open source thinking in general :). 
This time I am here to stay I guess. Thanks to everyone who made it possible!!!
