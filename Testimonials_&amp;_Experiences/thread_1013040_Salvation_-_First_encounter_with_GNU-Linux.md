### Post by krisstaar on 2008-12-16
So I recently bought a used Acer Aspire 5720Z with Intel T2310 Dual Core, 2GB RAM, 250GB harddive and Nvidia 8400GS graphics.

I've always been compelled by the open source mentality and have recently begun emerging from proprietary software to open source solutions. I started off by switching browser from IE to Firefox some two years ago. Secondly, I started using OpenOffice in replacement of MS Office, due to a complaint from an acquaintance saying he couldn't open the .doc-files I sent him and urged me to send them in rtf format.

I've wanted to switch to a GNU/Linux based OS for some time and now that I finally had aquired my own laptop I could do whatever I wanted with it without having to worry about complaints from the other tenants, so I decided to go for it. (When I think back, it appears to me that I might have bought that laptop just so I could trow windows out the ... well window)

I made a live-cd (I actually tryed making a OpenSUSE live cd first, but I accidently burned the .iso file itself to the disc, so I thought maybe some divine force wants me to try something else and I ended up with Ubuntu Intrepid Ibex ^_^ )and prepared for a lot of work. I figured I had to learn a ******** of programming just to get it up and running. You could say I was pretty damn pleased (and a bit releaved) when nearly everything worked out of the box!

Problems encountered:
-My Atheros wireless network-card didn't work
-If I used a lot of CPU my laptop overheated and shut down
-I couldn't get my microphones (both internal and external) to work
-The built-in Crystal Eye Webcam didn't work

Solutions:
(I think I found every single one of them here on ubuntu forums =D)
-installed linux-backports-modules and the wifi worked like a charm
-installed a fan-control script; no more hard shutdowns due to overheating
-haven't quite figured this one out yet... But it's not all that importent to me. Nevertheless, suggestions are appreciated
-For some reason it worked when I lowered the resolution. Fix so I can capture with higher resolution is also appreciated

All-in-all, switching to linux wasn't even close to the hassle I had imagined, and I'm sooo happy I chose Ubuntu and doubt I will find anything better for me in the forseeable future. 

Special thanks to everyone at ubuntu forums for very usefull (and life saving) information! =)

Best regards,
a freshly converted Ubuntu-user
