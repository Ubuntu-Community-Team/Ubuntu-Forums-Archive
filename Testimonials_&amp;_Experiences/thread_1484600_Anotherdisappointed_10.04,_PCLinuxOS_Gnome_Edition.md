### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-16
My big brother uses PCLinuxOS and loves it. He really wanted to "convert" me so I tried it out. It's definitely pretty and very fast, but on my poor old hand-me-down machine it has all kindsa hardware issues that my brother doesn't experience at all. 

The only way to know which distro is "best" **for you** is to try it on your own machine. Hardware compatibility is still a trial-and-error thing I guess. What is needed, seems to me, is a centralized database, ala Distrowatch or something, of which distros work best on a Dell Dimension B110, an HP whateveritscalled, etc. A place where you can look up your hardware by manufacturer and/or other categories and find a list of distros comparing their use on each kind of computer. As far as I know, OpenSUSE has the best hardware compatibility page on their site.

My big brother still swears by PCLinuxOS. I liked it too but *on _my_ hardware* it just doesn't run half as well as Xubuntu does. Everyone's mileage may vary.

I think I would worry, if I was a PCLinuxOS user (or Mepis, or Mint, or any distro that is basically developed and maintained by a single individual) about *long-term* use and support. There is less concern about that with corporately supported distros like Fedora, Mandriva, and Ubuntu. 

I'm glad you found something you really like. Use it in good health!

Happy right where I am,
Robin
