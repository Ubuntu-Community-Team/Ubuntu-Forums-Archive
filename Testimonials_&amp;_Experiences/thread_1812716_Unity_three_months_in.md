### Post by Cloggsy on 2011-07-26
Just thought I'd share a few thoughts about Unity having used it almost exclusively for the past three months - hoping this might in some way contribute to the usefulness of Ubuntu.

It seems as if Unity is a development from the old "Netbook Edition" of Ubuntu; having used this for a while the new Unity desktop wasn't a complete shock on completion of the distro upgrade. 

First, the good bit.
For basic simple need-to-get-on-with-it using your regular applications the permanent dock is easy to customise and gives one-click access to applications like e-mail, internet, documents, Skype and/or anything else we might use frequently. 

Now the downside.
Obviously, most comparison would be with Gnome as the predecessor and alternative desktop. With this in mind I would make the following observations:
[LIST]
[*]Other than the applications immediately in the dock, applications are fiddly to find. Yes, it's possible to right-click on the applications icon to get a menu, but that's not immediately apparent. Clicking on the icon itself brings up a fairly meaningless selection and a second opportunity to access the same menu. The same is true of files without a menu immediately available to find common locations.
[*]The desktop switcher now works in two stages instead of just the one. One of the joys of Gnome was the simplicity of desktop-switching allowing for the simple creation of workzones. These days I tend not to bother and just pile everything onto the one desktop - which isn't very satisfactory.
[*]Abducting the top window toolbar menus and displaying them in the top panel is confusing when you're trying to work with several open windows.
[*]The desktop doesn't lend itself well to the use of a projector. The inability to hide the top panel is annoying - that, and the cumbersome desktop switcher cause me to revert to Gnome ("Classic") when I am using a projector say, in church, where the best method is to cue up a number of applications each in its own desktop and simply switch between them.
[/LIST]

It seems to me that some of the clarity and simplicity of the Netbook Edition were lost when the desktop grew up to become the default. Although we still have Gnome available for the time being, I've noticed that some applications have developed small visual glitches when used with Gnome under 11.04. Please don't read the above as a rant, rather an honest appraisal of the my own experience. I do try to champion Ubuntu vs Windoze at every opportunity but I feel that Unity in its current form is a small step backwards in the instant use-ability stakes where Ubuntu usually trumped Microsoft's golden calf.
