### Post by Paradoxfox93 on 2008-11-10
Although Ubuntu is my only linux experience (And when I get a desktop I will stick four hard drive and multiboot 7 different versions for S&G's) I must say I enjoy linux.

Sure, it's frustrating when something doesn't work.  I'm a recent convert to Linux with experience in windows dating back to DOS black & yellow screens.  I've used 3.1, 95, 98, ME, XP, & Vista.  I'm no stranger to a computer that has problems and doesn't work!

I think one of the things that improved my linux experience was that I never expected it to be flawless.  My only expectations were that it lessened the paranoia I experienced from an increasingly difficult to modify OS that seemed to control more and more of my computer without my permission and gave me less and less permission to stop it from doing so.  I didn't like the constant tracking of my computer by windoze.  I didn't like the 'file indexing' that bogged down every operation except for file searching which I never use because I always know where my files are.  All this and I'm expected to shell out $400 for every copy (ONLY HALF FOR AN UPGRADE!!!).

I suppose with free one naturally has less expectations.  So when my wireless caused my first experience to lock up for the first dozen or so boots (Gutsy...then I went to feisty) I wasn't complaining.  In two sleepless days Linux was running like a peach (And so was my manic depressive brain!).  I found cool applications, encryption modules, games, educational resources and all of it was FREE!  The only thing I was mad about was not having discovered this wonderful world of GNU sooner.

In fact, that's fairly depictive of what I have come down to determine the greatest thing about Linux IMHO.  If something jacks up, it's my fault.  It's because I changed something.  And NOTHING stops me from fixing it or just turning it back to the way it was (Or reinstalling and reconfiguring with scripts I learned to write).  Personal Responsibility!  I guess that also explains why windoze is so paradoxically mainstream though, eh?  

The second greatest thing about linux is no system registry.  IMHO...my computer isn't a person....it shouldn't have a backbone.  It doesn't put out, so why should I have to argue with it over simple tasks?

Sure, some say the downside is gaming compatability....but seeing as I'm a recovering video game addict....this isn't really a bad thing.  I finished my twelfth step by reformatting without windoze and installing AES-256 encryption after my shadowbane clan went to AoC.

Just my two sense after about a year of using Ubuntu.
