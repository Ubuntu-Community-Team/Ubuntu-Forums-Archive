### Post by SniperSlap on 2008-04-28
Since installing Hardy Heron on three of the computers I have access to, I've been noticing a fair bit of instability as compared to Gutsy.

The big few are things like Nautilus and VFS where SSH mounts can cause nautilus to permanently freeze and seemingly take my entire X session with it.  It also appears to like duplicating mounts for no reason when opening files rather than using an existing mount.

Overall I'd have to say I'm it's somewhat disappointed with this release.  It drops a notch or two for Ubuntu in general.  I have a friend who is experiencing issues with Pulse - thankfully I'm not.

The Ubuntu project needs to make sure that it doesn't get so concerned with an outward focus that it loses the ability to introspect and value itself.  

The big headline feature of installing from within Windows was great & all, but you don't have much if you sacrifice the entire OS's integrity for just that one feature.
I also know that the KVM functionality is less than production ready and has a long way to go.

In the end, it's all stuff I'd like the community to have access to.  But let's keep a stable base going guys!
