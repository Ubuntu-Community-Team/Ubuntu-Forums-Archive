### Post by Chdslv on 2012-10-17
Tomorrow, Ubuntu 12.10 would be released. Most probably, it won't be much different from 12.10 beta 2 with daily updates and dist-upgrades. When, it comes tomorrow, some would start whining about Unity desktop, and Amazon etc.

Of course, there is a lot of bloat in Ubuntu, 12.10 or 12.04. Canonical needs to place that bloat in anyway, so it is up to us to take them away and make Ubuntu work faster. If you don't like Amazon lens, just delete it.

Like I mentioned in an earlier post, there is a derivative of Ubuntu we could look in and learn from. (It is pretty easy to find, so I am not mentioning its name.) What I did was compared directories and files of Ubuntu 12.10 beta 2 and the lean Ubuntu derivative and deleted all the files I could, matching the lean one, but left all the applications, and even Unity. *The 12.10 started responding faster. *

It didn't matter, if I kept Unity and installed Gnome 3.6, it still worked faster. Tomorrow, the 12.10 would be officially released. Then, I'd compare my Beta 2 and the new one and delete the bloat. 

For those, who might whine over Unity, I'd like to tell that it is only a DE, just like any other. If you like it, you can use it, and if you don't like it, there are plenty available. One way or other, 12.10 is going to work fast and its remaster would most probably be within the CD size, after the bloat taken off. :p

I find 12.10 much nicer than 12.04, as the Ubuntu devs had enough time to take as much as possible bugs off, and using 12.10 would be a better experience. I almost feel it, as I had been using 12.10 Beta 2.

PS: By the way, when you are installing 12.10 tomorrow or later, disconnect all your Internet connections and install it without any downloaded files. You can update them later, *after* the install, and if you want. That's another simpler way to keep away the bloat.
