### Post by tribaal on 2006-05-08
Well as for the details:

Basically two friends came to my place with their laptops (a vaio and a dell, plus my own dell inspiron 9300), and we installed Dapper beta 2 on them.

They were totally unconfident in installing linux by themselves (they are both professionnal IT engineers though), so they wanted their "linux friend" (me) to just sit by and help them out if something funny happened.

After half an hour of installation, the only thing that was left for them to say was "is that it? It can't be that easy?" :) Ubuntu picked up *all* the hardware for every single machine without anymore hassle (including SD slots, wireless cards, multimedia buttons, and graphic cards, even ATI).

One of them admitted it was much easier than installing a copy of windows XP.

The rest of the time was spent sipping beers while they started fiddling with their new system. Of course, multimedia support was one of their first concern (PLF repos were a breeze to use), so we set that up together as well.

They were both annoyed that the screensavers were so un-configurable (no flamebait, they really said it), but thought it was a fair tradeoff for the security, stability, ease of installation and maintenance and good looks :)

Very positive experience overall, I'm thinking or organising such a party on a larger scale (around 20 people I guess, so that it fits in a classroom)... I'll give you feedback on that too :)

Great experience!

- trib'
