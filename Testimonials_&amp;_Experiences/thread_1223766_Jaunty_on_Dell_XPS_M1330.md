### Post by harishg on 2009-07-26
I did a USB stick install of Ubuntu 9.04 on my Dell XPS M 1330 Laptop. It went well for most parts with some minor glitches. 

Good:

The installation from the live USB was done within 20 minutes. Most of the hardware were detected automatically and the broadcom driver for the wireless card was automatically installed instead of having to download and compile it using the b43-cutter. Hibernation worked without causing any problems, which is an improvement over my previous experiences. 

Not so Good:

The intel graphics driver was blacklisted due to known issues but then again its not a Ubuntu problem. The success in detecting the inbuilt cam depends on the software. Skype detected it without any issues while fspot photo manager could not recognize it. The in built microphone was resulting in low voice capture. A workaround in the Ubuntu forums solved the issue and I could do audio conferencing on skype without any issues. I guess they need to use just one default audio system instead of so many of them.

Need to test:

I have not tested the fingerprint reader and bluetooth yet. 

Overall my Ubuntu experience has been becoming better and better (I started with Dapper Drake) and I would happily recommend the current version to all the people who want to give it a try.
