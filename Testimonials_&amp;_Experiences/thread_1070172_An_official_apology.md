### Post by solwic on 2009-02-14
Quick post here...I want to apologize to everybody who has ever told me to use the "man" command or "RTFM" and received a reply from me in the general form of "thanks for nothing you lazy, arrogant, elitist ^&%$#^%$#!"

I'm learning how to use rsync, and I posted here on the forums and got a link to a decent site.  

HermanAB, on the other hand, suggest I read the man pages.  I hesitantly typed ```
man rsync
``` and was happily surprised that the manual wasn't that bad at all.  It had a lot of information and all of the options I could use.  If there's something the man page doesn't say, like what -v verbose means (I know what it means, but if you didn't know what it meant, you might be confused), there's enough information given to easily find more on Google.  Usually the hardest part of searching the Web for answers is knowing what to search for.  The man page gives all the commands and options you ever need, as well as very clear examples.  

So...I'm surprised.  I would recommend that anybody having trouble with using a command throw those three letters in front of it and see what there is to see.  Be patient, read through them.  The answers are all there.  :)

Cheers, and thanks to HermanAB and cdtech for the help.  :)

### Post by malspa on 2009-02-15
Excellent post, jrock612!

Also a reminder to users to check out **man man** for info about using and getting around manpages.

And, occasionally there's no manpage for a particular application, but I've had some luck with Googling **man (program name)**.  You can often find a manpage that was used in another distro that way but be careful because all of the details might not apply for the distro you're using.
