### Post by AngMohClay on 2008-10-22
At the risk of making my first Ubuntu forum post a drunk one, I'd like to add my semi-coherent testimonial.

Ubuntu is like a beautiful woman, you gotta give her some time.  I bought the Ubuntu book with the OS DVD, and when I got my new computer, I decided I'd partition 75 of her precious gigs to Ubuntu.  My reason: **** Microsoft.  Although I'm an excel junkie, I hate everything else about that company.  

But I digress...

I installed Ubuntu, and it arrogantly took the #1 position on my OS boot menu (which of course I didn't know existed until after installing ubuntu).  Well, at first, I hated Ubuntu.  I've been a windows user for a long time, and although my systems ultimately get bogged down with all of those vicious add ons microsoft makes you feel like you can't do without and my computers get progressively more sluggish, Windows was at least the dirty trollop I was accustomed to.  And Ubuntu was weird, man.  

For a full week I'd press the start button and walk away to pick my *** crack or look out the window or cure cancer (well, two of those are true), and seconds later realize... "OH ****, IT'LL BOOT UBUNTU".  I cursed this beautiful OS, but now I see the light.

After a week or so of seemingly forced Ubuntu usage, I started to get used to her, and then, like the good looking guy in the 80's movie, I started to realize that the nerdy girl wasn't just free, she was also kind of banging, in that Molly Ringwald circa Pretty in Pink sort of way.

I think the light really turned on for me when I needed to use LaTeX to write a reference sheet for a math test.  I literally couldn't get LaTeX to work in windows.  In ubuntu, all I had to do was add the AUCTex package via that beautiful synaptic function for Emacs.  I fell in love that day, and I don't intend to fall out of love.  Getting dual monitors running has been a pain, but I'm cool with the little things.  It's the big things that matter and Ubuntu is funtastic (that is not a mispelling, it is both fun and fantastic, although I did misspell "mispelling").  

So, I thought I'd add that I'm off the MS, and loving Ubuntu.  

Now to explore this bizarre Kubuntu thingy...

Anyway, take care,
Clay

You don't need to install kubnuntu separately. Just go to applications > accessories > terminal and type in this:
```
sudo apt-get install kde4
```
(It's normal for your password not to appear.)

After that, before you log in, go to "options" and "select session," and choose between GNOME (standard Ubuntu) and KDE (Kubuntu.)
