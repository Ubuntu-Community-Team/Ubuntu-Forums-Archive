### Post by KillerSponge on 2007-09-24
I have tried several distro's in the past (including Suse, Fedora, Freespire, Kubuntu and Ubuntu). I liked Kubuntu the best (I still prefer KDE over Gnome), but there was always something that I couldn't get to work (mainly because I didn't know anything about linux), and I would constantly switch between Windows and Kubuntu because I wanted to play games. I would try every new version of Kubuntu, to see all the new stuff, and to check if the hardware support had gotten any better.

Two weeks ago, I completely switched to Kubuntu. 

Because of the lack of new good games for PC, I noticed I was only playing games on my Xbox 360. After trying Feisty, and noticing that (almost) everything worked now, I decided to ditch my Windows partition, and copy all my data to an ext3 partition.

In the past two weeks, I have learned A LOT, and customized the hell out of Kubuntu. I must say, I absolutely love it. I like the way I can customize it to fit my needs, and I like the whole community behind it. 

The best part is, three days ago, my mother saw me working on Kubuntu, and asked me what the pro's of linux are. I told her that is was free of virusses/spyware/etc., and it is (in my opinion) easier to use for people who just want to surf the web, check their e-mail and type a letter. I said she could try the live cd if she wanted. I gave her the Ubuntu live cd (as I think Gnome is a little easier to use, and Ubuntu looks nicer by default), she tried it, and loved it! 

She picked up Ubuntu a lot faster than she did with Windows, and she loves the way it works. And I must say, if an OS can convince my mom to switch, it can convince everyone to switch.

I just wanted to say: Thank you developers, for making such a great OS :)
