### Post by Extreme Coder on 2006-12-23
(Just so that you know, I am 13.)

1) Lame introduction:
I live in Egypt, and I'm constantly moving between Cairo(the capital) and my home town. In my home town and the environment I live in, I am regarded like someone who has been working in the IT industry for 20 years(even though I am not). Whenever someone has a problem, it is always call Ahmad(my name) for help. Got a virus? Call Ahmad. Got spyware? Call Ahmad. Got <insert random windows problem here>? Call Ahmad. I even opened a computer shop and a fixing service!(Not on my own of course, with the help of my older cousin)

2) Discovery of Ubuntu:
What was better is that my opinions were highly regarded. So anyway, enough bragging about me. 2 months ago, I discovered this wonderful distribution called Ubuntu, and saw how popular it was, appearing in most tech-related sites. So I decided to give it a go, as I was very frustrated with Windows. After playing with the LiveCD, I immediately decided to install it and try it for a longer time. As I was even more amazed with it, the next thing I know is that I'm reformatting the H.D.D and installing Ubuntu ONLY:D 
I was just amazed at the driver detection, my HP printer and scanner work better than they did in Windows!

3) Friends amazed:
I had a party 1 month after the discovery, and as with all my parties, my friends came to check my PC to see what cool stuff I had on it. 5 minutes later, I could see lots of jaws dropping ;) They at first thought it was Vista because of Beryl, but I stated the hardware of my PC (not strong enough for Vista), and how linux is better and stuff. Everyone wanted it on their PCs. I told them it had a learning cure and such, but that didn't stop them. Next thing I know, in School they talk about who found out the coolest stuff in Ubuntu :D


I also want to add that now I know the difference between a Windows poweruser and a Linux poweruser:
A Windows poweruser spends his time fixing Windows.
A Linux poweruser spends his time discovering and improving Linux ;)


Thank you everybody, for being a great community, and being a great distribution!
