### Post by perspectoff on 2011-01-01
I like Konversation for IRC. It's the most like mIRC.

Ubuntu and Kubuntu are very easy to get started. It's the second phase that is difficult -- seeing how far you can go. 

I rely upon

Ubuntuguide.org at [http://ubuntuguide.org](http://ubuntuguide.org)

and

Kubuntuguide.org at [http://kubuntuguide.org](http://kubuntuguide.org)

for detailed advice and tips, all in one easy-to-digest location. I also refer from time to time to Ubuntu Geek (but it is pretty dense, not written in the best English, and not always easy to find specific advice). I always refer to the Ubuntu Server Guide first for server topics, and then to the Ubuntu Community Help pages whenever I can't find something on Ubuntuguide or Kubuntuguide. 

Ubuntu forums is invaluable for very specific or arcane questions, but, as you noted, there are thousands of threads and people repeat questions many, many times without first searching to see if their problem was previously solved. Also, there is a lot of overly-complex and just plain wrong advice given here, and there is a certain, shall we say, "political correctness" here that causes some quite sensible answers to be filtered.
