### Post by bcschmerker on 2011-08-04
As a fourth-year user, I am able to do much with Ubuntu® that would require some expensive software tools in Microsoft® Windows® 2000, XP and 6-up.  Originally getting a roundabout introduction to Ubuntu® Forums via Good OS LLC (as gOS® 1.0.1 was directly based on XUbuntu 7.10 Gutsy), I took a real liking to the Forums concerning specific hardware and software resources.

Unlike Windows® (which sticks with one kernel until either a Service Pack or a new major version is released), Ubuntu® can "major-upgrade" a Kernel within an LTS release.  I am already running Kernel 2.6.35 on a 32-bit Generic-PAE install of 10.04.2-LTS (which shipped with 2.6.32) and doing preliminary survey of what Packages will work better with Kernel 3.0 once the Development Team has it packaged.  Microsoft® may have an advantage with manufacturer support of bleeding-edge video hardware, but the open-source community has stepped up to the plate to get older video gear up to top speed in Ubuntu® with few hiccups.
