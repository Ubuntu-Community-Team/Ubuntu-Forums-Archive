### Post by motion parallax on 2008-01-27
I purchased my laptop, an Acer Aspire 5610, nearly a year ago.  Pre-installed with Windows Vista.  After the first few months things starting going haywire, and I began to really dislike the way computers were going.  No control over its behavior, no way to fix problems without massive research and work, and all-in-all general frustration with the entire computing world.  

A few days ago I decided to give Linux my first try.  I chose Ubuntu.  

Since then I can't describe how well things have been going.  My computer runs more quickly and efficiently than ever before.  Installation was a breeze.  I now have the power to make my computer into the machine I've always wanted.  

For the first time in my long life of computer hand-me-downs, I'm truly happy with my machine.  

I wanted to thank all of you for the massive help you've given me in fixing problems and tailoring this system to my needs.  This forum is amazing, and my experience wouldn't be this great without you.  

I'm going to get back into programming, something Windows discouraged me from staying with years ago.  

Thank you all.
