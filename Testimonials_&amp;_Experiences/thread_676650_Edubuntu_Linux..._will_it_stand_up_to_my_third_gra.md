### Post by tuebinger on 2008-01-24
Ask most teachers about student computers in the classroom and they'll likely heave a heavy sigh and tell you about all the freeze-ups, crashes, etc., that occur on a daily basis.  One of the reasons I don't like setting up computers for the kids to use in the classroom is because the machines and programs inevitably freeze up, and I, as the teacher, spend more time trouble-shooting, restarting, etc., etc.  I have a three-year-old classroom  Dell running windows that is sitting in a closet right now gathering dust because it freezes once a day at least!

So, as an experiment, I have installed Edubuntu 6.10 on a much older Dell and set it up in my classroom today.  I was a little concerned about how my students would react, but they love it! Gnometris is their favorite game so far.  Anyway, the point is... it didn't freeze up once!  i could put students on this rickety old computer and go about teaching, not having to worry about rescuing a frozen computer, and I'm sure it's because it's running linux and not windows.  I had twenty different kids clicking,  dragging and dropping with it today, so I think it's a good testimonial to the ruggedness of Linux. 

But will it survive the rest of the school year?  Only time will tell (but I think it just might!!!)
