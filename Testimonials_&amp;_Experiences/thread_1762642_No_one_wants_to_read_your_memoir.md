### Post by sYtrus on 2011-05-19
Hello everyone, my name is sYtrus. Long time lurker, first time poster.

I just wanted to come on the boards today to say that I find it quite disturbing how the Ubuntu community has been evolving as of late. I wanted to give my two cents based on the experience I had with Ubuntu from 9.04 until now with 11.04. 

But then again, doesn't that contradict the title of this thread? Well, yes, yes it does. However I'm writing this with no bias, no fish to fry, and no sense of self-entitlement or importance (i.e. I don't expect my opinion to outweigh everyone elses). I am writing this to not get lulz, or to get YOUR opinion on what I'm saying because like you... I could care less about what some stranger has to say. This thread is not for spinning wheels, causing mischeif, or flaming the creators of Ubuntu, or for praising other distros, it is simply to state an observation based on experience.

I toyed around with Linux ever since I was 13, but never took a serious interest in it or the functionality until my sophomore in college. This was mainly attributed to the fact that I'm a huge PC gamer, and what good is an OS if it can't run games? For me back then it was key that I was able to play Unreal Tournament and Quake with no issues what-so-ever, and so I decided that Windows was the place for me. 

When I got to college pursuing a degree in Information Systems I decided on branching out and trying a Mac. I loved it, and after taking several classes on Unix and Solaris administration I could not part from the terminal ever again. The optimization of Apple's OS was incredible, and despite all the naysayers it's features were sleek, smooth, and incredibly efficient. I honestly don't feel like I would have been able to get through college without my Macbook and later my Macbook air (graduated this month so this isn't a history story)... that and my noise cancelling headphones. Seriously, can't you stfu in the library people?! 

But I still found myself torn between my hobbies and my productivity. Games were on Windows, none were on my Mac's because that is where work got done distraction free (aside from Reddit of course). But after spending my entire childhood and early adult life on Windows I was growing weary. I wanted the option to have a simple workhorse environment that I fell in love with in Apple's product and still have the ability to do what I do most on my Windows machine (if you've been paying attention you know what I mean). 

In the past I had tried many Linux distros, but settled on early adoptions of Ubuntu. I tried loading it on my Macs and ended up having to reformat, I decided to wipe Windows and run Ubuntu primarily (around 9.04 time) and ended up with an unsupported video card and wireless card... and a whole lot of headaches. I removed everything and went right back to Windows and OSX. 

Now, here is the key part of the story. After taking classes at my University in Unix adminsitration and learning about the history, structure, etc.. of the Unix/Linux world I recently decided to come back to Ubuntu on my Windows machine. 

This decision wasn't based around open source philosophy (which I agree with), it wasn't based on wanting something new, it was just an experiment. To see how my abilities changed within a strange environment, and to see if I could overcome past failures and being able to efficiently run a Linux distro on a commercially built Windows machine. 

While some may scoff and say "ha, what a newb, that is so easy", it wasn't for me when I knew absolutely nothing about Linux aside from knowing what it was... and not how to use it.

Now at present time I still love and use my Macbook Air daily for a multitude of things, but I also have a second, secure, and powerful workhorse running my current game of choice (Starcraft 2) at the exact same FPS as Windows 7... on a two year old laptop.. using Ubuntu 11.04.

What is the moral of this story? :confused: Well, it is simply that you all have become jaded. It is not necessarily a bad thing, but it is an evolution that has ocurred none the less. Ubuntu has become so easy to use that many newcomers expect it to be the equivalent of OSX or Windows in it's usability. But make no mistake, while Ubuntu does allow hand holding IT IS STILL A LINUX DISTRO! Which again, is not necessarily a bad thing.

 Looking back to 9.04 that I put on my old laptop, there were SO many problems I personally ran into. The installation of 11.04 on the other hand was dramatically streamlined, and the proprietary drivers were already waiting for my approval when I came in. With minimum configurations I got SC2 running on wine, and with minor changes I got my dual monitor set up running perfectly.

There are many now who have only problems with Unity, and to them I say, look at the notes of 11.04, if you don't like Unity (like myself) then use classic! The fact that the option is even present is a testament that Unity was implemented but it was known it wouldn't be widely accepted. People don't like change, but don't sit here and bash the entire distro based off of an experiment by the devs. 

I have read two threads today that inspired me to come out of lurking both by the same user (who will not be named) that claimed he is leaving Ubuntu for Linux Mint because he was "old school" and was "pro" and was leaving because of the Unity changes. This user then proceeded to remain on the board trolling the negative posts trying to pat himself on the back and reassure himself that this was the right decision by instigating responses friendly to his views. I am of the opinion that when someone says they are leaving the distro they should then stop trying to convince others to do the same (thereby convincing themselves it was the right choice). 

This distro is buggy, my point is that it always has been, and always will be. No one, and I do mean no one, will ever release any piece of software without something wrong with it. That is the way it is. In fact some of the distros you all are talking about switching to have caused more problems for me than 11.04. But quite frankly I like that the devs tried for a dramatic change, I like that they are trying to entice new users to Linux and teach them the philosophy behind it.I think anyone who even even remotely cares for the Linux community (not just Ubuntu) should be happy that more and more users are becoming interested because of these changes while leaving the core functions alone. This happy median will allow future growth, expandability, and tenure amongst the community. 

Just remember that if you are an avid user of Linux you are a part of the entire community (meaning every distro), and we are all here for simliar reasons. Don't turn this into a pissing match between distros, just embrace the fact that there are so many to choose from and move on. 

TLDR

Stop bashing, do research before you make the jump, if you're going to leave the community do it gracefully.
