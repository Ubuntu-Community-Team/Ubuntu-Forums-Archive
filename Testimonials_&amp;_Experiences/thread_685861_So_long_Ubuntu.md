### Post by mivo on 2008-02-02
Sorry it didn't work out for you. As others have said, trying different Linux distros is an option, but the real issue is with Windows-specific hardware and some manufacturers not releasing Linux drivers -- or at least opening their specs so that (unpaid) Linux developers can write drivers. It's easy to blame Linux for this, but responsible are really the hardware makers that only consider Linux. I understand that this is of little relevance if you already have the hardware, but it may be worth remembering the next time you get a new laptop or other hardware.

Thanks for giving it a try, though! :)
