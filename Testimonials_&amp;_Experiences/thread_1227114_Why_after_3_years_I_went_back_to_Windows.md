### Post by Johnsie on 2009-07-30
I used Ubuntu as my main operating system for 3 years.  It was fun and Ubuntu is good if all you need is a simple operating system to do simple things. I'm not blaming anybody, this is just the way it is and there is nothing any of you can do about it... There were some things that really bugged me:


**Video conferencing over the major IM networks**

Most people use MSN or software like that to communicate with audio/video. While AMSN does support this it is very buggy and the front end is quite tacky.


**New Big Apps**

Over the last year we've seen the release of Google Chrome and Spotify. Both of them were primarily  designed for Windows and work better on Windows. Yes, I can get them to work on Linux, but it's a lot more work and the performance hasn't been great. Linux always comes last.



**Keeping My apps up to date**

On Windows the developers control when application updates are made available. Quite often this is an automatic process and you can have the latest versions very easily. With Ubuntu you're really supposed to wait until the official repositories are updated. Sometimes the update doesn't happen until the next version of Ubuntu! Yes, it's possible to manually update programs outside of the repo system, but that's inconvenient and there is always the danger that you might break your system.


**Visual Studio**

I'm a full-time programmer. Sometimes I have to work with web services. Visual Studio is much better at working with WSDL's than any open source software. Eclipse is an ok IDE but Visual Studio has alot more packed into it and is alot better at many things. I try to use open source stuff as much as possible, but sometimes Visual Studio is simply better at certain things.


**Phones and other hardware**

Some phones are good and some aren't. But with Linux it doesn't matter. You need to get a phone that is compatible with Linux. This really restricts my choice of hardware and limits me to a small number of top of the range phones. It is the same when buying other hardware too. I always had to ask, "will this work with Linux?". 


**Getting the most out of hardware**

I have HD speakers on my netbook. They are capable of all kinds of sonic trickery. The software that comes with it allows me to apply special effects to my sounds/music live. I don't see anything that does this on Linux

Printers often have tools telling you how much ink there is left etc. 

Scanners can be used within Microsoft Office to scan something directly into a document


**Many websites don't seem to work**

This is one of my biggest frustrations. I find myself unable to access features of many websites because they are incompatible with Linux.


This is not about blame or anything like that. I don't care about the reasons for why things are the way they are, I just want my computer to work with everything. Linux is good at some things, and bad at others. I still use Ubuntu on my servers, but on my desktops I mostly use Windows.
