### Post by tdawgf on 2009-01-18
Hello all who read this,

I started using Ubuntu with 8.04 (well i tried one of the 6.something versions a long while back), and I want to say this has greatly changed my outlook on Linux as a desktop/laptop option. I had used different Distros off and on for a few years now. A friend of mine got me to try Mandrake 9.2 a few years back (I had heard of Linux at the time but never used it). Thinking back I messed up my system at least 10 times that year, but it was a learning process. I eventually began to learn but never really did anything besides browsing the internet on it, and I still used Windows for everything else. 

2 years later I got a laptop and used it for school and pitched the desktop. Since I was taking some basic programming classes I tried to give Linux another try. I had fairly good luck but since my laptop had an ATI video card and Integrated wireless my lack of true in-depth knowledge got the better of me. Since I couldn't figure out how to install video drivers for anything other than Nvidia chipsets, and the lack of drivers for my wireless card, I hit a stand still and eventually went back to XP.

Well about 6 months ago with my laptop aging (I also don't really play alot of games anymore, which used to consume my life), I decided to give Linux one last shot. This time I chose Ubuntu 8.04 and it didn't shock me when my integrated wireless card wouldn't work off the get go. This time however since I didn't really have a need for my laptop on a daily basis I decided to use my Windows equipped desktop to search the forums and take my time figuring out what was wrong. After about 2 weeks I found that alot of people had trouble with integrated wireless cards across all distros, and then I discovered the Ubuntu community. Following a guide from the forums I got my wireless card working (I dare say it works better than it did in windows, but there are a multitude of factors involved so maybe it is my imagination), and low and behold Ubuntu tells me hey we have drivers for your video card.

Well after a short post on the forums here asking people for software recommendations for software for specific tasks, I was up and going. Now the better news comes along. My poor quad-core desktop is feeling abandoned ever since. Ubuntu has breathed new life into my aging laptop, as well as changing the way I see my operating system. If I said for me Windows just works, I would also have to say with a tiny bit of work Ubuntu works better. I now spend 90% of my time on my laptop with my Windows machine getting a small amount of game time and occasional media use since my laptop just decent size hard drive (by today's standards).

I am thinking of picking up a new wireless card for my desktop (one that I know will work with Linux without much effort), since it only has a usb D-link range booster wireless card, and moving it to Ubuntu as well. The forums users made all the difference in the world. Ubuntu is now my OS of choice.
