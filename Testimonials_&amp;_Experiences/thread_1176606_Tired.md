### Post by Bert Mariën on 2009-06-02
I'm just a guy who found Linux a few years ago.
I can tell you: I ran and tested more than a hundred versions of it.

I'm not a pro, I just like to fiddle around with stuff.

But after 2 years, testing, fiddling, being involved with an excellent community, I feel tiresome.

I already felt that way with the latest Ubuntu LTS, but the developers and the community kept asking for testers, and so I did. 

At this moment I have 8 distro's on my computer. And I must say: I'm a GNOME guy.

But, doing what I do (I mostly test about 5 to 6 distro's a week, depending on new releases) I'm getting tired of it all.

Bare in mind: I DO love Linux, and I do like fiddling around with OS's.

But testing a new version of a distro every 6 months (for a new release every six months) is tiresome.

I cannot imaging how the developers must feel getting it all together. They must be tired as well.

Luckily we have the community to help them solve bugs and other nasty things. But... The help of the community does not write the necessary code. That is what the developers do!

At this moment, I am tired of installing and testing 5 to 6 releases a week. Ubuntu, Xubuntu, Mandriva, Puppy, Mint, Arch Linux, CentOS, DreamLinux, Fedora, Igelle, KateOS, Knoppix, Kwort, Mepis, Paldo, Parsix, Pardus, PC/OS, Ubuntu Ultimate, ZevenOS, Slax, and still a lot more....

I like Linux.
I like the community.
But it is, even for a testing user, hard work.
