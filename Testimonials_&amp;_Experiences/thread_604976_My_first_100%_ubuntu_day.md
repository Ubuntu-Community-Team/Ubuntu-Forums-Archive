### Post by Vorticon on 2007-11-06
Here's a little testimonial.

While i've had ubuntu on my PC for a quite a while, it has (and still is) always been dual boot with some other expensive OS. I'm quite a fanatic gamer and as you all know, directx and linux don't go along very well. I game usually at least once a day so then i have to boot the other OS, since a lot of cool games are made with directx. 

Today was special though!

Today i booted up, put on some downloads and went to college. Made some tests here and there and i came back and put on pidgin to chat a bit over some IM network made by the guys of the other os, I firefoxed around a bit to check mail and to keep up with some blogs. 

Then I wanted to game again, so guess what i did. NOT booting up the other OS! Recently i bought a nice game called Enemy Territory:Quake Wars which i'm a bit addicted to now and they made it linux compatible! Hurray for opengl! Wonderful. It works pretty good too. Haven't had any crashes so far! I wish there were more OpenGL games like that.

Then i watched a movie and played around a bit with the nice GUI that comes with 7.10 ;) And here i am browsing the forum realising this is the first time i haven't booted the other OS this day! And i'm not going to either cause i'm going to bed now and dream about how cool my compiz fusion settings look.

Bye!
