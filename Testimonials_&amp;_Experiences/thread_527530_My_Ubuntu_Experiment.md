### Post by b52doc on 2007-08-16
I installed Ubuntu on my Dell Desktop that was running Windows XP SP2. Love the free software that I can get from the repositories and clean, bloat free interface. But there are a lot of problems I found that still makes me dualboot. But rather than just leave it at that, I am going to list them and see if anyone agrees.

1. Wifi/Hardware support - one quick search on the forums and you can see the countless problems people have with wireless cards and the frustration it provides. Yes I know that the problem lies mostly with companies not making open source drivers, but geez getting the wireless cards to work is something I wish on no one and I can understand why people end up giving up. My epson printer is still MIA.

2. Too many choices - Don't get me wrong, choice is great but looking through the software available in the repositories is dizzying. So many different variations between programs that do the same thing can be overwhelming. A site with a list of all the software with   some screen shots, descriptions and list of features would be fantastic. Being able to compare programs with a feature list would be a huge plus.

3. Command line - Yes, command line is awesome, allowing you to delve into the OS and tweak anything you like, but a novice who is used to GUI fixes will likely be scared off. And while copy and pasting instructions works, I don't understand what I am doing and how I can apply it to later problems. 

4. Bugs - I didn't encounter too many but the bugs are annoying. Some of my favorites was some applet asking for my keyring password over and over or my computer continuing to run even after  shutting it down. The solutions to alot of these are way over my head, editing files, confusing use of command line. From my personal experience with Windows, which I hate, I never encountered any; just software conflicts.  

All that being said, I am sticking with Ubuntu. Why? The support forums are amazing. Almost any problem I have, I can find someone who has had the same on the forums. Whether or not the solution is clear or easy to do is a different story. I can see the potential for the OS once I get all the kinks worked out. 
Mainstream Desktop OS? No yet. But worth a try if you don't mind tinkering around a bit if you enjoy a challenge. 

PS. To everyone who had it work perfectly with your hardware. I envy you :KS
