### Post by weblordpepe on 2010-02-16
Hi guys. Im not new to linux by any means but I figured this is a good thing to share.


I donate many computers, but I've just donated my first computer preloaded with Ubuntu. I've deemed it appropriate in this instance for the first time.

The person I'm giving it to is well..certainly not leet with computers. I was asked by my friend 'hey would she know how to use it?' and it made me think for a moment. 

Basic tasks on the computer these days in 2010 are identical on any computer you look at. Its only the finer details which vary between systems.  I have trained people to use Windows and the various configuration options etc - and I have realized - it could be any bloody system that I am showing them.

And people who use computers as tools (the **real** end-users) typically have no real desire to get under-the-hood. And in the same token - if she does have a need - she will not in a million years phone Microsoft or Canonical for help; she will ask me or her friends.

It really comes down to whats easier for **me** to support. And thats stock Ubuntu :)

Its been two days and the only question so far is "Would it work with my ipod? Can I download itunes?" And it worked out of the box with rythmbox + gstreamer codecs. I've thrown on Wine and VLC as a precaution.


I'll keep you fellows up to date with any events.
