### Post by jso2897 on 2007-09-12
For a while now I have been running Ubuntu on my older machines, and had kept mey newest machine, a 3800Athlon 64x2, 2G Ram, Nvidis 7600gt, on Windows XP, for gaming. I was getting increasingly tired of Windows, and all the BS associated with it, and decided to dual-boot the machine. But when I did, something broke in the Windows install, and it wouldn't boot anymore. So I tried to restore it, but, of course, Windows restore just broke it some more. And at that point, I just said, screw it - and loaded Ububtu to the whole disk. Now, I am running Ubuntu on all my machines, and it only took it a couple hours to get everything working. At this point, I have only tried two games - Diablo II and Tombraider Legend - and they both play GREAT. Legend actually plays faster and smoother than it did in Windows, and the controls are more responsive. So, the restore disks for XP are still in a drwaer, and if I ever feel the need, I guess I can re-install it. But why?
