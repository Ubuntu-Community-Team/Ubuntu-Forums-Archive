### Post by Sorivenul on 2009-02-20
At work today I had a coworker call me in to fix a computer-related issue. It turned out to be an IE related issue regarding JavaScript. I got the problem resolved, but it lead to an interesting discussion about security, operating systems, and philosophy. 

My coworker said she wished there was something out there that would just let her check her email and compose her papers for her graduate courses. Something free and something secure.

Since she was still logged into the computer she was working on I pointed her to the Ubuntu website and said, "Look around, and come ask me any questions you have." 

An hour later she came across the hall to my office and said she had two questions:
**1.) Why haven't more people heard of this and decided to use it?**
My answer was, "Basically, because it's Linux and the word scares most people. Ubuntu makes Linux a relatively painless experience."
**2.) When can you install it on my computer, and can I keep Windows , too?**
My answer was, "Absolutely, you can have the best of both worlds. Today, even. Go home and get your laptop on your break. I'll set it up before you go home."

4 hours later, mostly due to a lunch break and performing my job tasks, Ubuntu was installed, media was playable, and one more Ubuntu user was added to the tally. I will obviously be offering personal support, but have supplied my coworker with information about the Forums and the other excellent Ubuntu documentation.

Cheers!
