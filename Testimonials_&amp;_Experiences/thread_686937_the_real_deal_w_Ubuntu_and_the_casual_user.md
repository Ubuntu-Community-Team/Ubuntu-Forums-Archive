### Post by bradmayne on 2008-02-03
I first became interested in Ubuntu in the late spring of 2007.  I was suprised by how easy the install went (im on a laptop) and how easy it was to setup the wireless (i have an internal atheros chipset).  When i did have a problem the people on the forum were quick to respond and fairly supportive.  On a scale of 1 to 10 (10 being the best) i would say that i would give them a 7 for the help.  When feisty came out i updated and it went fairly smoothly.  The only complaints I have are with nautilus and browsing the windows computers.  With feisty it worked pretty well.  When I updated to Gutsy Gibbon though nautilus worked for about a week or two.  Then nautilus just refused to work!  I looked everywhere for a solution and it seemed that all i could find were other people that had the same problem as myself.  There were no solutions!  I finally figured out a workaround ( I downloaded dolphin) and that solved that problem)  Then the only other problem that I had was with setting up a network printer with samba.  That was a real hassle!  All I got was people giving me the most basic advice that didn't solve my problem.  Finally I went to synaptic package manager and searched for "network printer" and downloaded any packages that seemed relevant.  I also downloaded the old "gnome cups manager" ( I think that's what it was called)  The new printer manager program that came with Gutsy Gibbon was to confusing for me.  So, I used the old one from feisty fawn.  After I downloaded those packages ( see my tutorial or how to for the packages I used) as soon as I typed in my name and password for the computer that had the printers attached (it was a windows vista pc which made things a tad bit more difficult) the computer showed up by name as well as the printers that were attached to it.  It was kinda tricky because the printers were attached to a vista computer from what i read if the computer with the printers was running XP or better yet linux (any flavor) it would have been alot easier)  Aside from that I am very pleased with my whole ubuntu experience.  Ubuntu is alot easier than Fedora, Suse, or Gentoo (I have used both)  Also,  Ubuntu is the only linux OS that worked right "out of the box".  All the other linux OS's had to be configured and have madwifi installed to work. So all in all I would have to say that I would recommend Ubuntu to anyone that is interested in learning linux.  Whether they are new to computers or have been around for awhile I would have ot say I give Ubuntu two thumbs up!
