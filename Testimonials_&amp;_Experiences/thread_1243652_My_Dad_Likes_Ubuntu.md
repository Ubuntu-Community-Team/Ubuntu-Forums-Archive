### Post by Ms_Angel_D on 2009-08-18
So my Dad and I always had one thing in common, we both love computers. He got our first computer when I was about 12 or 13 years old I don't remember the specs but it had dosshell on it. I remember playing little games on it and we even had a dot matrix printer. I fell in love with computers from the first moment I used one, and My dad has always loved tinkering with them.

He's in Cincinnati and I'm in Seattle we've been this far apart for about 6 years now, but of course this never stopped the tech support phone calls where I would spend the day helping him or my sister on the phone walking them through fixing this or that windows problem. 

About a year ago I discovered Ubuntu and soon after fell hopelessly in love with it. He called me up as he had yet another windows problem. So I told him about Ubuntu and how it didn't have the same virus issues as windows. I told him he didn't have to concern himself with running a ton of extra antivirus and anti-malware programs on Ubuntu. He was intrigued but not quite enough to run out and download it. A few support calls and conversations later (approximately a month had gone by), he was finally to the point where he asked me about how to burn Ubuntu to cd. So I gave him the links and showed him some walkthroughs and by the next day he had his very first copy of Ubuntu 8.04.

He installed it on an old system he had laying around to play with it. He wasn't quite ready to expose my poor completely computer illiterate mom to it yet. He really enjoyed using it, he loved learning about how things work on Ubuntu and loved that his old computer seemed faster and better. 

When Jaunty came out I went to ship-it and ordered a disk to be delivered to his house, it showed up about 4 weeks later. He then installed it on the family computer. My mom uses the computer for pretty much only email, paying bills and banking, so the transition for her was quite easy and she loves how much speedier the computer has suddenly become.

10 minutes before this post I got off the phone with Dad and he was in the process of wiping any and all signs of windows from his house. The old man is 60 years young and I'm proud to say he is completely satisfied with Ubuntu. I am so glad I was able to help him learn about it and be apart of his transition.

As for me, I really enjoy the lack of windows support calls these days...
