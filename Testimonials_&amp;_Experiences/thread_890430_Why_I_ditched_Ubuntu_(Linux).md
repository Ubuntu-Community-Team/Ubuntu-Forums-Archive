### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-08-15
Video editing: Fair enough. Basic editing is possible and easy, advanced editing is impossible, but video editing software on Linux seems to crash a lot in their current versions.

Media to CD/DVD: You haven't looked hard enough, honestly. I use Tovid when I want to create menus. When I don't need menus, I just put the file(s) into Kdenlive and set chapters, and use the Export To DVD function. Some people swear by DeVeDe or Avidemux, too.

Dual Monitor and PDA: Never tried either thing. It depends what PDA you're using.

Why was multiple desktop environments a problem for you? KDE programs run on Gnome and vice-versa, so just choose one that you like or can customise to your liking, and use it.

As far as multiple distributions go, you're out of the ball park. The distributions all target specific circumstances and different sorts of people. Windows tries to be everything to everyone, and fails. With Linux, you have versions for enterprise desktops, home users on good hardware, home users on old hardware, embedded platforms, servers, enthusiasts, mega-enthusiasts, penetration testing, music production away-from-home, etc.

With that last complaint of yours, you're really too much into the Windows mindset. Unless you know what you want from Linux, you will quickly find an easy distribution for you to learn; and if you know what you want from Linux, you'll already know what to choose.
