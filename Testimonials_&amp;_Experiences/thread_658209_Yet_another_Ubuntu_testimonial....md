### Post by mike-laptop-dot-local on 2008-01-04
Hi all,

I suppose Message #1 should be my testimonial. I'm not new to Linux, but the last Linux install I did was 6 years ago - Mandrake on a PII 233 laptop. On a related note, here's a quick tip: don't install an OS on battery power while in a moving vehicle. :)

Since then, WinXP was my 'path of least resistance'. But as so many of you know, maintaining XP became more and more of a hassle. Automatic updates, service packs, patches, incomplete uninstalls, restart to complete installs, hung restarts, registry garbage, spyware, and what seemed a constant march towards bloat and decreased performance. I got sick of it.

There were two specific incidents that put me back on the path to Linux. First, my mother's PC fried and - you guessed it - her new one came loaded with Vista, and she absolutely struggled with it. Second, I bought a cheap 2GB USB drive and felt like getting geeky. :) With minimal effort, I d/led Ubuntu onto it and booted.

And not to sound fanboy-ish, but it was an epiphany. Many people comment on how everything 'just works' in Ubuntu, but for me it was how everything 'just makes sense' in Linux. When I screwed Ubuntu on my USB drive, it wasn't "the OS did something stupid" (as it almost always seemed with XP) to "*I* did something stupid. In a warped sort of way, that felt totally liberating. Dual-booting on my laptop came next, but as like many others I found myself only booting to XP for games, and once I learned to use Wine - and got the Orange Box running - I had no regrets about wiping the XP partition.

Since then, it's been Ubuntu bliss, punctuated by the ever-decreasing "*I* did something stupid" incidents, and now my daily computing routine is rock solid. Whenever I recall those Windows days, I run 'uptime' in a terminal and instantly remember why I switched.

That permanent switch was about 3 months ago, and with the enormous help found in the 'search' function of these forums, I've built the *exact* system I want, the interface I want, the software I want, etc. Right now there's a compiz-powered snowman in my desktop cube and both he and I scoff at Windows. :)

A month ago my mother called (again) with Vista problems, and growing ever-tired of my position as family IT consultant, I convinced her to let me build an Ubuntu partition. And what may later become a "HOWTO Build Your Elderly Mother a Rock Solid Ubuntu System", I built her exactly what she wanted - a desktop that made sense to her, most of her favorite Windows software running under Wine, the rest in a WinXP VirtualBox machine, etc. - on exactly what I wanted: an account with no administration privileges. For her (and for the family IT consultant), it's perfect.

Three days ago, we wiped away her Vista partition and that's that. :)

If there's one thing I've learned, it's that these forums are the absolute best source of Ubuntu support. If you don't find anything with a search, ask (verbosely, with enough relevant information) and there's a long list of people willing to help. And now that I've finally written this intro/testimonial thingy, I'll soon add myself to that helpful list.

And on that note, BIG thanks to everyone for giving a long-time lurker the answers to his many questions. *You* have made Ubuntu and Linux a perfect choice.

### Post by ljd65536 on 2008-01-06
I have been running Linux for years on a computer that I have upgraded one component at a time since the 1980's. The only thing left of the original is a keyboard that still has the Packard Bell logo and the floppy drive. Since I haven't upgraded anything for a few years, I began looking at prices for new stuff and somehow came upon the Dell/Ubuntu/Insperon page. I found I could buy everything I wanted for less than the price of a few new parts. And I could buy it without paying the Microsoft tax. There is some ironic humor while ordering the Ubuntu computer on web pages with the "Dell recommends Vista" banner. But I understand Microsoft makes them do that.

The computer was delivered today. I've played around with it for a few hours now and I've seen enough for me to recommend Dell Ubuntu computers to all but the most obtuse. Everything works perfectly. DVD's play, I can capture video from my Canopus ADVC-110. I imported my evolution data from my old computer, Audio CD's rip. 

I suspect that Windows has as many problems installing on arbitrary hardware as Linux. But very few people buy Windows this way. Most people buy Windows installed by an OEM. The Dell computer I bought shows that Linux installed by an OEM can be just as problem free as Windows.
