### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-05-25
I think this is a problem of "I can't go to the trouble of learning something new".

FYI, Ubuntu's window buttons used to be on the right-hand side; almost everyone who's been using Ubuntu since 2009 has gotten used to them being on the left-hand side. I started off on Macs and then switched to Ubuntu in 2005, so I've gone from left-side to right-side to left-side.

Ubuntu is different and trying to get it to act like Windows is going to result in frustration and failing. Ubuntu is an alternative to Windows; not a drop-in replacement. It's not trying to be Windows, it's trying to be an operating system for its users. For instance, on Ubuntu, the desktop is not the place for program or disk shortcuts.

Your complaints about the lack of GUI for GRUB are founded, but then I'm sure it's possible to get a GUI program to customise GRUB. I'm sure I've heard of a couple, actually. As they say, "there's an app for that".

I must admit I'm a bit surprised that you're getting along with Windows 8 Beta. I expect you've probably just turned off the Metro desktop.
