### Post by Da_Gopherboy on 2011-03-28
So this is just an experience I wanted to make mention of.  With the movement toward Ubuntu and other free GNU operating systems are picking up, I just wanted to take a moment and mention an experience that truly has cemented me in Ubuntu.  

I've been using Ubuntu on an almost full time basis now for about two years.  Before then I'd dabble in this fancy and "beyond me" operating system known as *nix.  With Microsoft products, I have always been trained to view them as being the highest of software evolution.  A frustrating experience today with the Microsoft corporation has given me pause in the day to day bustle and I've caught myself reflecting on the history of my life inside this little box known as my computer.

So for the past few weeks I've been noticing Window's Vista constantly crashing while trying to run the accounting software for my business (Inuit's Quickbooks - Great Program *Plug*), this time of the year is NOT when you want these types of problems.  With Tax season here and me working very hard to make sure my books all add up I made the decision that I should do a fresh install of Vista to get back to the basics so I can at least function.

The next thing that happens is something that if you've been around for a while, your stomach develops a pit and you almost want to puke.  I pull out my hard plastic case that houses jewel cases with all of my expensive and proprietory software from the big names, only to find that my Retail Vista CD is missing! How am I going to make this work?  Well being that I'm under the gun, I need to call Microsoft to even see if I can get a replacement CD.  But I really don't want too sit on hold, let me check their website first.

First things first.  This is a legendary multinational corporation, surely they have dealt with this before and will provide me with help and understand my situation!  So I went first online, did a search on Microsoft's website.  Great there is the information I need!  I knew they wouldn't let me down!  So now they want my product key and product id information.  No problem, my key is here, and even if its not I can still recover it from my Control Panel.  So you enter the Key and... This is not a genuine windows product.  "Well that is weird" I thought, so I punch the key in again and guess what no dice.   

Well lets double check that key with the one that windows has already authorized.  Looks the same, says its activated and genuine.  Lets not waste anyone's time, especially my own, and see what Microsoft says.  I'll just download the Microsoft Genuine Advantage Diagnostic Tools to make absolutely sure I'm not wasting my time or theirs.  "Wow their software agrees with what I'm seeing".  After thinking about it for a few minutes, I'm thinking well I had better just do it and call them.

So I call them and get someone who asks me basic questions, full name, phone number, email address, etc.  No problem, here you go!  I appreciate you guys taking my call so soon, I thought for sure I'd be on hold for about ten minutes before I even got to talk to someone.  

Me-
"So here is my problem, Microsoft says my product key isn't good, however I got it installed on my system. I'm trying to reinstall Vista because it isn't working very well.  But I can't find my CD and when I look online about getting a new Disc I'm told my product isn't a valid or genuine product."

Customer Service Rep-
"Ok so let me read this back to you, you can't install Windows Vista because you don't have a valid product key.  In order to help you out, since you do not have a valid product key, we will need to transfer you to our technical department.  And because there isn't a valid key, there will be a change of 60 dollars for support."

Me-
"Um, no.  My product key is valid, and by using Microsoft's products, including my installed Vista and Microsoft Genuine Advantage Diagnostic Tool I've verified that my product key and install are good.  What I'm looking for is to get a copy of Windows Vista for a clean installation."

Customer Service Rep-
"Ok well give me your product key, product id, computer brand."

Me-
"Key, Product Id, Acer Aspire 4730z Laptop"

Customer Service Rep-
"Ok well I see that your product key is invalid, and that you'll have to contact Acer for further assistance.  Thank you for calling Microsoft, and have a good day." *Click*

Me-
"Umm wtf?"

So long story short.  This proceeded for about two and a half hours, until finally I was able to convince someone to allow me to speak with a tech in regards to the bad key in there system.  He told me that yeah it happens, sometimes the networks don't keep as good house keeping as they should and occasionally this happens.  So he agreed to issue me a new retail key so that I could get a new disc should I want one.  But I'd have to call the sales team in order to get that request out, and he couldn't help me with that.  Also that I should upgrade to windows 7 as they aren't going to be supporting Vista for much longer, he could give me a waiver to give me a discount if I would talk to the sales team via him.

I mean this tech Hakey Muldong, was the man and the only one willing to cut through the flake and actually ATTEMPT to help.  I mean out of all the customer service and tech support I've had, this guy just did what needed to be done.

It just made me realize how far we've come.  For those of us that used Dos back in the day for a professional tool, and even when the huge leap into Windows (especially 3.0) there was no crap.  You got a problem, you call the company and they send you a disk no questions asked.  Got a problem?  We don't care if you bought it from us or not, it needs to work, lets do what we can to make it work!  Those were the types of attitude that were prevailing in the world of computing, even in the business world.  Yes time is money, and with certain programs that involve so much time and energy to design and build.  Sure you should be compensated in some way to provide for your efforts, no question!

But this brings me to the point of this rant and sharing my experience.  I upgraded to Ubuntu 10.10 from 10.04 a few days ago, had a few problems.  I posted here on the forum's and was specific in what I was trying to do and I received help from people who actually care more about the function and less about profit.  Less than 10 minutes later from my post, I had a full detailed report on what could be wrong and how to fix it in all possible scenarios'.  Its this type of humanity and support that put function over profit that is going to have Ubuntu living up to its named meaning "humanity to others".  So much thanks to all of the developers whom have had even a small part in the continuation of this project.  With that type of focus to functionality over profit, its unlikely that in the long term, the large companies whom provide the other type of view point will stand.

So again in conclusion thanks to all whom are contributing to these projects/forums, your work, energy, time and effort are very much appreciated!  So in hindsight I like the idea of being able to loose my USB thumb drive, and not worry about reinstalling my OS a year or two from now.

-G
