### Post by ATL_Braves on 2008-05-05
Hello all! This is my first post on these forums.

I've been an avid computer user since I was a child learning DOS command on our 8086 and programming in basic. All through those years I only used Windows products...until now. I purchased a Lenovo laptop, and a week after it arrived "Hardy Heron" was released. I set up the laptop to dual boot with XP, and then went back and installed it on the desktop I had built several years ago. 

I have been amazed by this OS, and by the community and support around it. I am infinitely happier with my Ubuntu setup than I was with my Windows systems. Now with Wine I look forward to the day when I can completely toss Windows. I don't know what I was thinking before.

Anyway, I'm excited to join the ranks!

### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-05-06
For most people, Soundjuicer or even command-line stuff like mplayer/mencoder works more than fine. EAC makes a difference only if you're an audiophile (I'm not saying this in a pejorative manner, nothing wrong in being one) or you constantly need to rip discs in bad state (for which I admit EAC is unrivaled). If you do not fit in either category, you might want to try linux options.
