### Post by juil on 2010-09-04
I've been using windows all my life up until a year ago. My dislike in paying for anti-virus programs and other software has led me to find Ubuntu evenutally. I started off with karmic. Installation went smoooooth. After I upgraded my PC to AMD Ahtlon II, which the guy at canadacomputers told me that AMD works good with Ubuntu.

After a year of using it, I've NEVER ran into any major problems and a very few minor issues. It's a joy to customize your desktop environment and everything else you want to. It's MY computer and MY space, not something that someone else has customized and pressured me to buy. Also, not paying yearly subscriptions for anti-virus programs which take up your system resources is a HUGE relief. 

This may be my personal opinion, but I realized ubuntu Linux is VERY limited when it comes to gaming. I've been an avid gamer most of my life and the games I used to play was only compatible with Windows. Vast majority of PC gamers play online games, and most of the BEST games are for Windows. So if you want to play World of Warcraft or games like Lineage by korean MMORPG giant NCSoft, you have no choice but you use Windows. This is not a minus for me since I quit playing games, but for people who fall under the MMORPG gamer category, it definitely is.

I also realized that Linux takes you out of you comfort zone, and for me personally, motivated me to learn Linux. And it's great fun. But it takes a little more motivation for Windows-comfortable person to want to switch to Linux IMO. I had a friend who was extremely interested in Ubuntu. After installing it and using for a few weeks, he said it was pretty good, but went back to using Windows.

I, on the other hand will continue to use Linux and not intending to stop. Thanks for reading my experience.

### Post by Stray Wolf on 2010-09-04
I started Ubuntu the day Karmic came out as well.  Then, Lucid.  Yeah, there's a learning curve that can be intimidating.  The more I learn through Ubuntu the more overwhelmed I become with how much I don't know.  But I figure experience is the best teacher.  I've been able to clone my hard drive (thanks clonezilla),  organize my huge photo database logically (thanks digikam), make my desktop appealing (thanks compiz and cairo-dock) and learn some basic terminal commands in the process.  I still feel lost occasionally but have gotten a better intuition in navigating for solutions in the forums and on launchpad.
