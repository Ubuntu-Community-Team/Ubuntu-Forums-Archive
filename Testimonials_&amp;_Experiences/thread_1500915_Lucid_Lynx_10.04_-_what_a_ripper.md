### Post by charonred on 2010-06-03
I've been using Windows (3.11, 95/98, 2000, XP SP1/SP2/SP3) for a number of years since getting into PCs in the late '90s. All versions were a hassle with BSOD, lockups etc, and eventually 'bloating' until  reinstall became necessary just to have a 'functional' PC again - and all the time shelling out $ for this 'latest, greatest, best version Windows' ever.

Over the intervening years I had looked at various distros of Linux; Mandrake/Driva, Redhat and others, but nothing seemed easy to use, or could give me the apps I needed for use on my PC.

In Feb '08 I built my first new PC, and based it on a Gigabyte MA790FX-DS5 board running a dual core AMD 2.8Ghz with 2Ghz RAM and a 512Mb graphics card supporting 2 x 19" (4:3) Samsung LCD monitors; and it seemed to handle XP fine. But in June '08 I decided to try Ubuntu 8.04 LTS (32 bit); I was reasonably impressed, it started faster than XP, and shutdown quicker too; but I still dual booted to XP-SP3 ... until Windoze got to it's 'bloat-point' and became increasingly slower to start, respond and shutdown - what a pain in the proverbial! 

So I decided to keep using Hardy, and gradually got it to take over most the jobs I'd been using Windoze for; a couple of apps still required XP-SP3, but I could run them inside Virtualbox, so I had no need to dual boot the PC anymore - this was excellent!

This machine kept running beautiful up until mid '09 when I installed a new model AMD Phenom II 945 3.0Ghz Quad core; there were a few little hassles, but after contacting Gigabyte, they had a new BIOS for that board/CPU, which I promptly installed and it pretty much fixed things, except for some of the Gnome CPU applet stuff. I also installed more RAM giving me a total of 4Gb. 

Then late '09 I upgraded the graphics to a new ATI (Sapphire) HD5770 card with 1Ghz DDR5. I hit a few snags there with ATI driver support in Hardy which I couldn't seem to resolve, so early '10 I migrated across to Karmic 9.10 (32 bit) and that seemed to sort things out nicely; but as I used this PC for business as well as personal, I had wanted to stay with a LTS version.

When Lucid 10.04 LTS was released this year, I really took to the new 'darker' theme and features, so I grabbed a new drive and installed the 32 bit version, moved my docs and apps across to it, and couldn't be happier.

Lucid is fast; from post to a ready & waiting desktop across 2 monitors takes just 35 seconds flat; and shutdown is 6 seconds - damn, that is so fast! and that's using ordinary WD 7200 rpm SATA drives (I wonder how fast it would be with an SSD).

Lucid Lynx ... gotta love it !

And just 3 days ago I just got myself a new Asus K52F SX074V Notebook (Win7 Home Premium 64 bit) - Intel i3 350M 2.26Ghz Quad core, 4Ghz DDR3 (1066 Mhz) RAM with Intel GMA HD graphics. Damn this is one nice spec'd notebook; it runs fine and fast. So I thought I'd see if it would run from a Lucid CD, and it did fine. 

For the short term I thought I'd just do a WUBI install of Lucid (64 bit) and see what problems I hit, before going to a dual boot setup -  guess what .. none! Everything works, including Advanced Desktop Effects without downloading any additional proprietary drivers. It even handles my '3' mobile broadband USB dongle without a hitch - so now I have a new Notebook as well for home, and on the road, that runs either Win7 or Lucid (and Lucid is still faster) - what's not to like ?

:D

ps - I did a clean install of Lucid 32 bit to my old 2003 IBM r40e laptop and sold it, the bag and all accessories to a friend & his wife for a very cheap price - and it outperforms XP Home that came with it - she loves it; she's also got a fairly current Gigabyte desktop running Lucid and that is fast too, he's running a slightly later Gigabyte PC with his own tweaked Debian/Karmic version, but is thinking of moving to Lucid.

### Post by NUboon2Age on 2010-06-06
For the longest time I've wanted to use Linux.  ABMW = Anything but MS Windows.  I wanted to break free of the shackles of malignant corporate control.  But the installation and running of Linux was over my head.  I especially didn't understand or want to tangle w/ Lilo, Grub, partitioning, fdisk, potentially losing access to an OS, and recovery.  

So mostly I went to Mac and had the ease-of-use and aesthetic beauty but still corporate controlled experience that provides.  If my apple hardware failed I was kinda stuck out on Apple island.  Moving data around and between different proprietary programs was often challenging.

I tried and liked Correl Linux in about 1999.  The user interface for the installation was straightforward and user friendly and I liked the beautiful and functional desktop.  Very nice.  Then the company went under and poof goes the support.  Bummer.

Back to Mac...

Then last year I got adventuresome and I managed to install Xubuntu Jaunty Jackalope on an old laptop w/ 238M of ram.  It was charming but glitchy.  I had to go through the whole wireless not working on Broadcom hassle.  The panels disappeared from time to time for no apparent reason.  I didn't feel it was ready for prime time and so I kept it 'in the closet', not showing it to others.

I upgraded from Xubuntu to Ubuntu Lucid Lynx when it came out and Wow!  Now, that's what I'm talking about!  Gorgeous, mostly very functional and user-friendly, an awesome support community.  Wow!  Prime time! Now I'm showing anyone interested how great this Ubuntu thing really is.
