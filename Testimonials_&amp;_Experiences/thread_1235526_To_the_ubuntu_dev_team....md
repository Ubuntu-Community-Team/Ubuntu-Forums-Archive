### Post by rullingen on 2009-08-09
May I just say...

CONGRATULATIONS

I've always admired the linux community, way of thinking and general quantity of effort put into something which is completely free! However I've never been able to get it quite right for me. I've tried previous distro's and things like the opengl issues with the ATI cards, lack of drivers for DVB adaptors and software compatibility have always held me back and I've always ended up back with XP.
However, I decided to give 9.04 a try and I cant fault it. All my hardware works perfectly, I can even run COD4 if I want to, I can write without issues to my NTFS drives and access my wireless networks instantly. I'm still running a small XP partition for Photoshop and Illustrator, which is a shame but I'm sure that the wine project will come through at some piont but other than that I am very impressed.
Cheers and keep up the good work.

### Post by Methuselah on 2009-08-10
I find Ubuntu fits my needs perfectly as well, even moreso than you!
I don't possess any of the Adobe Suite or the newer Microsoft Office Suite (last was 2000 which came with a computer) as they're too expensive and I have no independent employment to necessitate their use or justify the cost(I use Office/Acrobat at work). 
So for me, Open Office and the Gimp slot in perfectly for any minimal document creation or photo editing that I have to do at home.
In fact, I only use a tiny fraction of their features so I'm not likely to outgrow them anytime soon.

The other thing I often do on my PC apart from accessing the internet is programming and linux has a plethora of tools for that.

Additionally, I have a few games from when I ran windows (Sim City 3k/4k/Roller Coaster Tycoon etc) and most work near perfectly in Wine so if I'm in the mood those options are still open. 

So since all my computer use cases are satisfied more cost effectively and with less hassle with Ubuntu it turns out I only used windows because my early computers came with it and I didn't know any better.
