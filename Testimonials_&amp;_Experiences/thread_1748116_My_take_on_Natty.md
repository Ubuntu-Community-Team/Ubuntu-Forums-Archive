### Post by exploder on 2011-05-03
I think Canonical did a pretty decent job with Unity. Yeah, some are complaining and that was pretty much expected from the start. It's funny, people complained that Ubuntu was not contributing enough, they do something completely on their own and people still manage to complain...

My take on Unity is that it is more pleasant to use than Android. I have an Android based tablet and it is not near as user friendly as Unity. Ubuntu did a decent job making Unity usable and for it's first serious release it has a decent set of features and has a certain look and feel of being organized.Multitasking takes a little bit of getting used to but it does do the job and I can see the benefit to it's design.

Unity would be fairly easy for someone with little or no computer experience and I do like the way software that can be installed is made clearly visible in the menu. People new to Linux seem to have trouble understanding package management and Unity does a lot to overcome this.

I like how the proprietary graphics drivers can be installed strait from the installer, this is a step forward in my opinion. Plymouth is the biggest issue I see with 11.04, it just does not work worth a darn and it is something that holds the release back from it's goals. There has to be a way to make the boot experience work easily for people.

Unity itself has great potential when you view it from a new user or oem's point of view and this is the audience Ubuntu is trying to reach. In my opinion, with a little more refining to the gui and a more reliable boot experience Unity stands a very good chance of success with manufacturer's.
