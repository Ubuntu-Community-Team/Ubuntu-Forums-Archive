### Post by Tomosaur on 2006-08-30
While that's all very nice - Microsoft actually subsidises the cost of the hardware if the retailer agrees to have Windows preinstalled. Removing Windows, especially on newer PCs, is just as likely to drive the prices up as it is to push them down. That, and Microsoft FUD tactics to lock the retailers into Windows - they've been known to threaten to reduce their prices if a retailer wants to opt out. This would cause all of the other retailers to sell at a much lower price, thereby pricing our non-microsoft retailer out of the market.
