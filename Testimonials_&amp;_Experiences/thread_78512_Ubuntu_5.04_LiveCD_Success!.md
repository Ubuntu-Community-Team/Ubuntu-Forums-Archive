### Post by strongmantim on 2005-10-18
I just received a bunch of CDs from Ubuntu yesterday and couldn't wait to test them out. The PowerPC Live CD went well, but I would find out that the boot process isn't quite as smooth and streamlined as the x86. I popped the LIve CD in my Dell laptop (Inspiron 700m) and waited for it too boot. 

Much to my surprise, when I opened Firefox for the first time, I tried going to google and it loaded! I wasn't even plugged into my home network! Ubuntu was smart enough to configure the built-in wireless and even connect to a network in my area. (It wasn't my network, of course... I at least have some form of security... but one of my less tech-saavy neighbors is kind enough to "share" their high-speed connection.) I was elated.

I was also impressed with the touch-pad scrolling that was automatically configured. For those without a laptop, it is possible to use the right-hand edge of the touchpad as a virtual scroll-wheel. This was something that had to be set up in Windows, but worked automatically with Ubuntu! Very impressive. It's these little things that make me a happy computer user.

And to show how much I like it, I will be clearing off some space on the laptop and installing it this weekend. :D
