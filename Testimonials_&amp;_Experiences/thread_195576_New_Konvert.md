### Post by JairunCaloth on 2006-06-13
I'll start by saying that I've tried out Linux several times over the past few years, and have had experiences range from horrid, to pretty friken awesome. My first attempt to use linux was RedHat 6 and I never even made it through the install, it was asking me questions that I had no idea how to even start to answer. There was also a lot of compatibility problems with my (at the time) brand new hardware. However Redhat 7 came to the rescue maybe a week or so later and I managed to get it installed *relatively* easily. 

I casually poked around with my new dual boot system for a few weeks mainly lost and confused, until I somehow completely trashed my HDD. (This was some time ago, and I really don't remember how I did that really...) Lessons learned I re-installed back to a dual-boot setup and kept linux around mainly as a 'toy' to show off to people. After a while Linux was pushed to the wayside and I didn't really pay it much attention with the exception of a couple more redhat installs just to take a look. I think I may have installed redhat9 and fedora at some point, however neither of these were very long lived on my system.

Fast forward to the time of Ubuntu, Hoary Hedgehog.
I was pissed off at windows for some reason or another, and I had been playing around with astonshell trying to get a better look and feel for my windows XP installation. However I didn't really want to shell out money just to add functionality to windows that I felt should come with it. After some more research into alternate shells and such, I ran across some screenies of some linux desktops and I was captivated. So, I started pestering a couple of friends that I knew ran linux as to which distro was the best.

I poked around and finally downloaded an Ubuntu Live CD. I really liked the idea of being able to just pop in a CD and be able to check out an operating system with no commitment (Sounds like a one night stand). Within a few hours of using the live CD, I was hooked and I was burning the install CD. (Now Ubuntu sounds like a crack dealer, one hit, and it gets you hooked). I was planning on formating my windows drive anyways for a reinstall, so I went ahead and let Ubuntu take over my HDD completely. I was completely *astonished* at how far Linux had come since the last time I used it and I didn't get around to reinstalling XP for about a year.

For the first time in my life I was pure Linux, and it felt damn good too. The path wasn't without it's pitfalls and complications, but with a little help from a couple books, and an awesome community, I managed to get the system working to my liking. I installed Starwars Galaxies with cedega, and found that it ran **better** than it ever did in windows. However, despite my success with SWG I was really missing my small library of games, most of which I had no success getting to run in either cedega or wine. So begrudgingly I went back to a dual-boot setup. The only reason I keep windows around though, is for games, and a couple of other programs I just love. Like photoshop, and a few things I need to have around for school.

OK, I know this post has gotten long and drawn out, and I haven't even touched on the whole reason I started this post. However, I'm going to go ahead and get to the  point of the thing. 

I just upgraded to Dapper, and since I just upgraded my hardware to a 64bit setup I went with the 64bit version. Also, this time I went with Kubuntu instead of the Gnome version. I must now proclaim to everyone: 

I LOVE KDE W0000t!!!!!!!!!

(and that is what this whole post was started for, however I ended up going into the progression of linux through my life lol)

(really though KDE roXXors my soXXors)

(I would have it's babies if I could)




(OK... not really.....)


(KDE FTW!!!)
