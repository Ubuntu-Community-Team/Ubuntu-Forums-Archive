### Post by animaniac on 2008-11-01
Been using it for a day now and have tested it on two machines, my first impressions:
[SIZE="3"][B]
Bad news:[/B][/SIZE]

1, The upgrade process is messed up, i upgraded on two computers, both 100% hardware compatible, the upgrade broke both of the installations to the point where i just had to make a clean install. Major clusterf*ck on the people who are new to ubuntu/linux in general with the 8.04 release and tried upgrade to the latest release. Bugs here need to be sorted out ASAP.

2, Sometimes the graphics can be a bit buggy, and you need to go into system>preferences>appearance to make it nice again.

3, The new xorg.conf file works differently, and the current known fix for making the middle mouse button into a scroll (instead of default paste) doesn’t work any more, to the annoyance of TrackPoint users everywhere.

4, Apt doesn’t seem to be able to get hold of some packages in the repos, had to install from .deb, maybe its fixed by the time you read this.

5, A bit disappointing that they have not added OpenOffice 3.0, I’m not buying the excuse that they had no time to test, they were just lazy (although thats something i can relate to:P).

[SIZE="3"]**Good news:**[/SIZE]

1, There is greatly improved driver support, i booted a live cd into a desktop that did not play so nicely with 8.04, especially the wifi, now it works perfectly.

2, I love the new bootable usb drive creator.

3, The new session menu in the systray is nice and intuitive, but I’m not too keen on that the same options are divided in the System menu, it should only have a “End Session” option instead of logout and shut down.

4, The improved support and configuration of multiple screens will definitely be useful.

5, 3G modem support is appreciated, im looking forward to better bluetooth internet support in future versions of nm though.

6, Using new versions of alot of upstream software really makes the whole experience more smooth, Flash 10 for example is excellent.

Well, that’s my $0.02.
