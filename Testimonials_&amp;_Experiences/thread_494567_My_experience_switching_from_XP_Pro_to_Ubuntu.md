### Post by Enkay on 2007-07-07
I work at local computer store and consider myself pretty computer literate. One of the techs I work with installed Ubuntu on one of our work horses because he was also curious about how effective it would be compared to Windows at doing what we needed to do at our job. After 2 weeks using it and seeing no problems at work I decided to try installing it on my laptop.

I went into the installation knowing some pretty basic dos commands, but with a will to learn. After installing of the live CD I was VERY impressed that my wireless card was recognized and worked perfectly with no tweaking. Everything else worked perfect as well, except my Intel i945 chipset. My widescreen resolution wasn't appearing under the desktop resolution options. After searching on the forums for about 5 minutes I found a fix and away I went. 

Next was running through a simulation of what I expected myself to do on a basic day. (And making sure that if I got used to this over the summer that I wouldn't be S.O.L. once I got to school) After visiting a few websites I realized I needed to search once again for basic plug-ins such as java, flash etc. Once again after searching on the forums for another 5 minutes I was set. 

Those two problems were the only speed-bumps I ran into whilst installing Ubuntu. Now using it for almost a month I have uninstalled my XP Pro, and never plan on going back.

In Conclusion:

There were a few things that were quite daunting when I first converted to Ubuntu-

-The structure of the file system, I had NO idea what was going on until I did a bit of reading and searching, but now I am as used to it as Windows.

-Having to manually configure some of your hardware. (This is not that hard, as long as you can find some help on the forums, If i hadn't found help there I probably still be on XP :X )

-Getting used to using the terminal, and learning a lot about a new OS. I atleast grew up using windows, from 3.0 to Vista, and changing from that to something totally different has been fun and interesting.

There were also MANY things I loved about switching to Ubuntu- 

-Very Secure
-Tons of free apps to substitute for comparable Windows programs.
-Beryl/Compiz (just cool)
-It does everything I want it to do, but for free
-Customization,Customization, Customization!!!
-The fun of learning new things about computers.
-My CPU on a mild workload is only using 10-15% of its resources.
-The Great Community

I must say the community is amazing, and without it I would be lost and still using XP. This has been my experience, and Honestly, if you are anything more than a novice, its pretty easy to get used to and learn.  And defiantly fun.
