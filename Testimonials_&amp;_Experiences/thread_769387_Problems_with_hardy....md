### Post by Exershio on 2008-04-26
Well, I got excited and decided to download and install the new Hardy Heron 8.04 LTS release. I checked the CD for errors, none found. All good, I thought. I install everything without a problem. Everything was looking good.

However, once I booted up and logged into my GNOME session, my computer crashed. Just locked up and me and I had to shut down the computer with the power button (ctrl+alt+backspace didn't work). So, I tried logging in again. Crash. I gave up and decided to use failsafe gnome. Worked fine. Turns out Compiz + Open source drivers is what was causing the problems. So I enable the proprietary drivers in failsafe gnome, get them installed, reboot back into normal GNOME session.

Woot, it works! However, Compiz was agonizingly slow. Firefox would take ages to scroll down pages. There was a lot of garbage on the screen when moving windows with Compiz. Moving windows was even a little slow. So, I disable Compiz. Everything seemed fine so far.

I wasn't really satisfied with the way things were running, so I'm going to look at other distros for a little while. Hopefully things get sorted out soon.
