### Post by Soccrmastr on 2007-01-08
*couldn't think of better title 0.o*

I found an old laptop laying around the house a while ago, and booted it up. It has win2k on it, 64MB RAM, 420Mhz processor. I was already pretty experienced with Windows, and knew many friends who used Linux. One suggested I try installing Xubuntu on the laptop since it is so lightweight. I wasn't scared/nervous that something might not work because I didn't depend on it for school/family. That is a major reason I haven't done Linux on my computer before. 

I got a Xubuntu ISO, burned it to a CD, and put it into the laptop. It had to install in low-memory-mode, which was at first quite scary, until I got used to moving around with the arrow keys. Well, I installed Xubuntu, restarted, booted off the HDD, and everythign staretd up. EXCEPT the windows environment. I was stuck just with command line. I tried many commands to try to get eveyrhting working, people tried to help me on IRC but no luck. Even after several re-installations same thing. 

I decided it was a bad ISO, downloaded it again, burned it, and installed. Sure enough XFCE started up, and I was very happy. 

I noticed right away that all hardware was supported, connected to my wireless network flawlessly. I was happy. I messed with themes, customized to make everything feel better, used Easy Ubuntu to get some basic things done that I'm used to having in Windows. At first the terminal was a little creepy, scary, and turned me off a lot. Then I realized Synaptic, and many apps were much too resource-intensive to open on a daily basis on the laptop. I started using terminal more and more, most frequently apt-get. I started reading documentation to help me understand workings of Ubuntu and Linux. I soon got very familiar with Xubuntu, and felt even more accomplished that I had it working on an old laptop, perfectly including wireless (I have heard many reports of things not working so well in this area.)

Just recently, my sister came to me and begged me to help her reformat her computer after screwing up the OS with tons of viruses/spy ware/ad ware. I had an Ubuntu CD already for some odd reason, thought I might use it one day. I said to her, "Here, I have this Ubuntu CD, lets run this first before we re-install/format Windows." She had heard of it, and liked the idea of a penguin mascot. 

I saved her files on several 700MB discs (she has a lot of school work/pictures. shes a college student LOL.) I booted up the Linux install CD, it erased the existing windows partition, and installed Ubuntu 6.10 successfully. When i booted up, the built in wireless worked, built in microphone worked, screen worked, EVERYTHING worked flawlessly. I ran Automatix2 for her, got pretty fonts that she liked, all basic things she liked from Windows.

She went back to her apartment the next day, and boom here starts some problems. First, she couldn't connect to her Wifi there. I told her to run sudo apt-get install wifi-radar, she did, and then her wireless worked. Then, she has a blue tooth Hp all in one printer/fax/scanner. she couldn't connect. I tried for a long time to find drivers, then realized there were already these drivers installed with Ubuntu. I told her to open the printer manager and she connected fine. I under-estimated the user friendliness of Ubuntu! 

I got her crazy themes she likes, and she was all happy. She started using it for her school work with Open Office, her social networking and web browsing with automatix2 plugins for firefox which were easily installed. She right away said she liked it much better than Windows, and said it was also much easier!

When I first put Xubuntu on my laptop, I was 13. I am now just 14, and was helping my college-age sister set up Ubuntu. And people still complain today that Linux isn't user friendly!!

The only reason I don't have Linux on my main computer is for gaming. I have a LOT of video games that I play often, and too much work is involved in getting some games to work in Linux, and even then most don't work at all. Maybe one day when I'm done with gaming Ill get Linux on here, and not enough space on HDD to dual boot.

Well, I LOVE UBUNTU! Its easy, and even a kid my age can use it easily. I hope that more people keep focusing on making Ubuntu even more user-friendly, because then there will be no reason to keep Windows. Ubuntu runs MUCH faster, and works much better. Thanks to everyone who contributes to Ubuntu and the Ubuntu community!

Mario "Soccrmastr" Viscovich
