### Post by zilog32 on 2011-04-04
Hello,
Unfortunately we, ( my colleagues and myself ), have been instructed by our bosses to remove Ubuntu from our test facilities and begin using openSuSE. This saddens us greatly as we have enjoyed using and exploring Ubuntu Lucid. This is due to the fact that we cannot get Ubuntu to connect to any Windows 7 machines. The enormity of this major problem of networking with Ubuntu is apparent by simply Googling 'Failed to retrieve share list from server'. We have tried contacting the development team and received no response.
After many weeks of trying to resolve this problem and costing much monies we have simply given up.
Hands up to DMIZER for his efforts but he cannot solve the issue all by himself. Maybe one day we might return but once a decision of this gravity has been made then I doubt it.
Incidentally, openSUSE has no problems whatsoever and takes hardly any Samba configuring at all. If anyone eventually solves this issue then please announce your solutions to all others suffering from the same problem.
I agree with the bosses that it is, in these uncertain financial times, too much of a financial risk to commit developments of our satellite receiver products to such a platform, especially when it is sometimes so crucial to contact the Canonical development teams.

Thanks to all involved in these forums for the help they have posted covering numerous issues.

My account is now closed.
