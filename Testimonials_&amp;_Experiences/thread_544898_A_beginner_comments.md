### Post by Mumbutu on 2007-09-06
I have been using every versions of DOS and Windows except Vista.
I have decided to see what Linux is all about. And I decided 10 days  ago to study this OS with the greatest humility.
Obviously I did not know where I was going.
I am truly, highly, and definitely frustrated. But I have decided to stay  mum(butu), at least to a certain point.
I have several remarks to make.
First and utmost Linux demands a lot of patience and resilience if you are the average home user brainwashed by the easiness of Windows use. Linux is more a terminal oriented OS than a GUI because Linux - derivative or sub product of Unix - appeals more to a community of experts in Computer science or, at least, a level higher than the average household user.
Those who have a curious mind ask me if they can use Quickbooks, Quicken and TurboTax under Linux. I try to explain that Ubuntu is an open source "program" while the software they mentioned is proprietary. They do not want to keep talking about Linux.

Second, I have read, and I still do, a lot of posts.
Some members are so frustrated that they express their views in a clumsy way. It is not a reason for those who have - or pretend to have - a higher knowledge to be rude, insulting ,and ill mannered to say the least (remember: I decided to stay mum). Some users really want to use, even move, to Ubuntu and they need help not a lecture by a big ego, so big that the personal comments take precedence to the technical aspect of the issue. The big ego of this Forum would be better off when they include the words humility and compassion in their vocabulary. Fortunately it seems they are not the majority.
An exemple of this category of persons ( the big ego) can be found in the Corvette  Forum where I am a member. Do not say anything, I mean anything, negative about a Corvette unless you want to be flamed. It looks like we have some of these species in the Ubuntu Forum.  It crossed my mind that  the Corvette big ego are using Linux as their primary OS unless it is the other way around: Linux big ego are driving a Corvette.

Third, from my own and very recent experience some issues are dealt with quickly and efficiently. Some are not dealt at all.
After a week I am still waiting for a genuine help to fix my no sound issue. Luckily I solved it,  by myself, last night.
As I said I am a newcomer, totally open to learn, use, and take advantage of Ubuntu. I have no illusion,  because it will be a long and painful journey. I am prepared for it. But it is obvious that I need the help of the experts and their experience.
Like a child I need a hand to cross the streets. I feel that on every issue I will be facing I will need a step by step help. 
I am ready to learn. I hope the Forum members are ready to help me.
