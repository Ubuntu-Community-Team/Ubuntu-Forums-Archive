### Post by iowa_wildcat on 2011-09-15
I ordered 10.10 disks several months ago out of curiosity so I could play with Ubuntu without ever intending to install it.  Last week my 3 year old Toshiba laptop with Vista refused to boot.  After 90 minutes of frustration and and futility, this senile old man slammed a 10.10 disk into the Toshiba and made Vista a bad memory.  Since then that disk has been slipped into my 12 year old grandson's 98 laptop and a 67 year old female friend's  XP laptop.  Both had received their laptops as gifts because the original owners had considered the junk.  The good news;  all three of us are delighted with the performance and stability of computers that were considered unusable and ready for recycling.  We appriciate the OS more every day for how easy it is to use for 3 total newbies that are not exactly computer geeks.

I've assisted numerous individuals with installing Ubuntu and can say without hesitation that it's user friendly (especially 10.04 & 10.10) and with just a small learning curve a user can truly enjoy the OS.

The only issues I've had with new users (I've assisted), is some may attempt to circumvent the learning curve and expect it to be like Windows. Which it's not. Take your time and play with the OS, even if you manage to crash it - the lessons learned will benefit you.

I hope you continue enjoying Ubuntu/Linux. Should you have any questions, I believe you will find the help sections of the forums to be extremely beneficial. Most answers can be found with a simple search and if not just start a new thread.

Good Luck  ):P
