### Post by shakabra on 2008-06-11
This weekend my buddy asked me to take a look at his computer.  He had several viruses.  Someone else had tried to fix it but only made it worse by installing 4 different anti-virus programs.  I'm sure these programs would work great separately but together they made the machine pretty much useless for anything other than scanning for viruses.  On a side note the person who installed these programs is an IT in the U.S. military.  Anyway I took his computer home, installed Ubuntu, which went very easy despite all the proprietary drivers it uses,  and returned the machine to a very pleased friend.  Then he made me a steak dinner!  Great deal if you ask me.  After a few quick lessons I encouraged him to explore on his own.  The only problem he has had was with ALSA and sound from more than one app.  Everything turned out fantastic.  The next day the IT asked me a question about the computer.  They were trying to locate the Windows files I had backed up and he asks me "where do you find the C:\ drive?"  I thought that was pretty funny.

### Post by stchman on 2008-06-11
I also did the same thing indirectly.

I had a laptop with no Windows CD (no biggie to me) and I installed Ubuntu on it.  Everything worked.  Well I decided I did not need 2 laptops so I was going to sell the other one.  A friend of mine was wanting a cheap laptop and he said he wanted to buy it.

I told him that it did not have Windows and I had to way to put XP on it.  I said if you want XP then you will have to out it on there.

He said he wanted to try Linux and was curious.

He started playing with the laptop and said that it pretty much did everything he wanted with Hardy.

All he does is surf internet, check email, IM on AIM, YIM, and MY Space, some office apps, etc.  Ubuntu does all this OOB.

The wireless and Compiz worked OOB so he could use his wireless router.

Needless to say he is Ubuntu'ing it up.
