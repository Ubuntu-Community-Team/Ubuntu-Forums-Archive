### Post by dpj23 on 2007-09-02
O.K., I have always been a Linux student but never really committed to running Linux 100% of the time till this year.  So, after months of reading and planning I was down to 2 distributions: Fedora or Ubuntu...  I had to decide which one to use...  So, I started with Fedora Core 6...  It preformed well, and seem to be the answer.  However, after 2 months my hard-drive crashed and burned.  I had to decide what to do, when a friend of mine showed how he was able to use VMware real easy on Ubuntu compaired with Fedora install.  That did it for me...  I have been running Ubuntu 100% of the time since and plan on never changing.   

If there something to be said, Ubuntu with VMware is the only way to go...

### Post by markonhismobile on 2007-09-04
Another nice point of the Ubutu/VMWare pairing is Ubuntu is light on system ram leaving more free for your VM's! I recently got a new laptop sporting the now standard (it seems!) 1Gb of ram, required of course for the OS that comes loaded - VISTA! 

Whilst using that beast the system monitor always shows around 50% ram usage! I haven't been using it unless i really have to - mainly for watching movies on the TV-Out as it's easier to config then Ubuntu for the time being (roll on 7.10!) and I'm sure I've never seen below 40%

Running Ubuntu, even with Beryl active and Thunderbird and Firefox running I'm still not getting past 350MB ram usage and my swap space has only shown a 1% usage! I could comfortably hand over 256MB or even 512MB to a VM with little or no performance hit :-) I have yet to try this but Virtualbox is installed and awaiting to be tested when I have time :)
