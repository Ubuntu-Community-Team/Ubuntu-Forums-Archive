### Post by cloyd on 2009-11-21
I recently bought a netbook . . . thought I'd get the best one available, the one with 2 gig RAM and it was running Vista home basic. As I got to using it, I was wishing I'd bought one of the cheaper machines with XP because Vista was just to much of a hog for the little machine. Got to talking to a step son about Linux, and he encouraged me to try linux. As I searched the web, Ubuntu looked like what I wanted, and, in the various tutorials, I found the best instructions on how to install from a flashdrive. Used a live USB drive for a week or so, then decided to install permantlly. I am not a geek, but I managed to shrink my windows hard drive so that I had 40 gig for Ubuntu, and then installed Ubuntu Netbook Remix as a dual boot system. Lo and behold! . . . it still booted to Vista if necessary. As yet, I can't print on Ubuntu with my printer, but Ubuntu is worth the hassle to go to Vista when I must print. No crashes, no freeze ups, it just works.
 
My wife told me she'd let me "work the bugs out" before she switces. She'd really like it if she tried it. She used to work on a help desk for UNIX systems, but as yet, she isn't ready. But I bet the day will come . . . 
 
I love it.
 
I really appreciate the fact that people have worked and made something like this available to the general public. My thanks to all who developed and put this all together.
