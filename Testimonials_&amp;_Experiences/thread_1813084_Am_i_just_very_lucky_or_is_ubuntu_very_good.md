### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-07-27
It's generally pretty simple to install Linux on most vanilla desktop machines. I've done it dozens of times over the past fifteen years using various distros on a wide variety of equipment.  Laptops/netbooks pose a more difficult problem because so many peripherals, like wifi adapters, are integrated with the motherboard.  On a desktop machine, you can just install a different card or attach a USB device.  Most people want laptops to be completely self-contained, which means having to deal with the hardware as it comes out of the box.

It's also the case that manufacturers care little about Linux compatibility.  Even a company like NVIDIA who has been a stalwart supporter of desktop Linux in the past has now decided it doesn't care about the OS on machines with its Optimus technology.
