### Post by Bruce M. on 2007-12-09
**July 2007**

My "double-U" OS system had it's final crash on my computer.  I refuse to ever go back to a "double-U" OS when as long as there is a single "single-U" OS CD in my possession.

I spent a couple of days without my computer because I was adamant that I was NOT going to put that virus laden, security lacking, mal-ware magnet back on my computer.  But I had no idea what I was going to do.  Then I remembered an email that a friend sent mentioning "Ubuntu" because we were talking Linux.  He only mentioned it once in passing as a "user friendly version" of Linux.

**Day 1**

I told my wife I was going out to a cyber-cafe to look for "Ubuntu".  She had a look of terror on her face.  "What's Ubuntu?" she asked.

"Linux, I'll be back as soon as I get it.", and off I went.  It took three cyber-cafes before I found one that could burn a CD.  A quick Google search brought up the Ubuntu site.  I was surprised the name had stuck in my memory with the correct spelling.  Love the name!!

I tried to get the latest version at the time (6.10) but for some reason it wouldn't come.  Don't ask me why, I have NO idea.  So I tried the LTS version of 6.04.  Just over an hour later and I had it.  The ISO file was burned to a CD, as an ISO file of all things.  I had no idea what an ISO file was and obviously neither did the guy at the cafe or he could have used Nero to burn the proper CD.   :(

While the file was downloading I also ordered the 7.04 Live CD to be delivered (in up to 6 weeks) to my home.

I had to waste the rest of the day installing that other "double-U" OS in order to get on the net and do some research.  That really put a sour taste in my mouth.  OK; so I found out what to do with an ISO file and installed NERO that came with my Sony DVD R/W drive and then I burned the 6.04 LiveCD onto another blank.

I installed 6.04, wiping out my freshly installed "double-U", and was very pleased to see that out-of-the-box, Ubuntu was up and running in no time.  Not a single hitch.  It was GREAT!

By this time my wife reminded me that we needed "double-U" because our ISP doesn't support "single-U".  "OK!", I said and after some reading and looking around in the forums I had an idea of what to do.

**Day 2**

Unplugged my second 40Gig HD and cable modem, took out my "double-U" DVD and did a clean install again onto a 10GB partition.  Installed some needed software: ccleaner, AVG freeware anti virus, Comodo Firewall Pro (freeware) and ZonedOut, that added 20,000+ sites to MSIE's "Restricted Site" list.  OK that's it, it's up and running.

Rebooted with the 6.04 LiveCD, and installed that on the other 60Gig of the of my 80 Gig HD.  Again a fast and flawless install.  Plugged in the second HD, with all my important files on it, and then the cable modem.

**The next few days ....**

Over the next few days I allowed AVG to update, as well as the Comodo firewall had a new version.  And I allowed "double-U" to get it's updates as well.
During the same time I got all the updates for 6.04, installed a few extra programs and started using Ubuntu seriously.

**First Upgrade!**

One night shortly after all this I told my wife not to turn the computer off when we were going to bed as it was downloading a new version of Ubuntu.
The next morning I had to wait an hour or so but finally I saw 6.10 configuring itself.  Approximately 14 hours later ( 100Mb/s connection ) and 6.10 was running perfectly.  Including the few programs I had added.  Gee, I was really getting to like this!  I was visualizing all kinds of nightmares and "discussions" with my wife.  No glitches at all.

**Second Upgrade!**

Confident now that this would work, I didn't wait another day, a few hours before going to bed I started the 7.04 upgrade.  Again, shortly after breakfast, and 7.04 was setting up!  Flawlessly.  Everything worked without any help from me.  I'm beginning to wonder, "Why isn't Linux a major pain for 'double-U', it works so well!"

By now I am so totally hooked on Ubuntu it would take a small army to get it off my machine.  Oh, and this was the day I received my Live CD in the mail! The six weeks turned out to be less than two.  Now that's service.

**_Now the fun starts!_**

I don't like the looks of Evolution but need to hotsync my Palm.  It was ugly to say the least. Oh, it worked fine, just looked ugly.  So I got J-Pilot, a few touch and go problems with that but it finally did it's first hotsync, and has never failed me since.

I even set up the ability to watch DVD movies, although I have to change my screen resolution to 800x600 to do so.  Just because I wanted to see if I could.

Got the program and setup needed to read/write to my second HD.  (BTW: today it has an ext3 format)  I can also read/write to my "double-U" partition.  Yea, things are looking good.  Which reminds me, as I write this, it's been over a month since I've booted to that system, I should at least let it get it's upgrades, in case I need it for my ISP support.   :)

Downloaded the 7.04 Alternate CD one night and burned that to a CD the next day.

**The Next Upgrade - Disaster Strikes**

Well, disaster struck, when I did something I had learned not to do in the "double-U" system.  I downloaded the upgrade for 7.10 "beta"! Not the CD, but the "upgrade online" It was a disaster, and even using the 7.04 kernel option in GRUB didn't work.

Well, OK, no problem.  Everything was backed up, thank goodness for that.  Out came the LiveCD again and within hours I was downloading the few programs I had earlier.  All without a single problem.  Can a system really be this good!  Yea - "single-U" - Now that's GOOD!

I told myself, "Self, we're waiting until Jan 2008 to get Gutsy."  But I have to tell you, I'm getting ants in my pants waiting.  I know that the "cube thingy" isn't going to work here, my motherboard, Intel - D815EEA, is old and without a graphics card, it's built in, and only 256MB RAM, it just won't handle it.  But I still want to upgrade to try out the other Canonical stuff.

Which takes me to my thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=623320](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=623320)

**Decisions, decisions ....**

See: **The Next Upgrade - Disaster Strikes** and you'll have a good idea which way I'm leaning.

Yes, I'm a "single-U" person from now on!

Maybe I should start writing:  Br**U**ce from now on!  :lolflag:

Here's a huge **Thank You**, to each and every Linux **programmer** who spends their time and effort making this system work.  To all of you, **the Ubuntu community members**, who spend time helping us new to the "single-U" OS.  And last but not least to **Canonical** for being there and making all this possible!

**THANK YOU!**
BrUce

### Post by information_entropy on 2007-12-10
Br**U**ce

On your motherboard there should be 4 or 5 white PCI expansion slots,
and one black AGP slot.  The AGP slot is for a graphics processor card

The Intel D815eea support 1x or 2x or 4x AGP Video cards.
Those are speed ratings 4x is four times faster than 1x

A quick price check  showed these to be selling for 5 -10 US dollars
That is probably 2- 4 Canadian dollars by now.;-)

On ebay there is currently a lot of 25 2x AGP video cards with
a buy-it-now price of $45.  These are not expensive.

If you get one somewhere, post a support thread for how to install a video card,
or read the already existing ones,** before you install the card**.
