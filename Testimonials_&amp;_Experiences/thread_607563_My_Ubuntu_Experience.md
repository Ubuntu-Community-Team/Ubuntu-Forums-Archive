### Post by etharis on 2007-11-09
3 amazing weeks...
Ubuntu.... Here is how it all started.

I heard about ubuntu from friends and looked into it to see what it had to offer. I had played with previous linux distros in the past and i was never able to get them working the way i wanted to. I started with Mandrake (Mandriva) about 2 years ago. I had it installed on my laptop and it worked ok at best. I couldnt not get my wireless drivers working and the 3d hardware was shot and i was very very frustrated with linux as a whole, then about 3 weeks ago a buddy of mine handed me a burnt copy of the new ubuntu 7.10 and told me it looked really cool. He was going to try to install it on his intel mac through parallels so i figured i would give it a try too at least there would be some help this time if i failed again.... Well its 3 weeks later and i have repartitioned my drive to eliminate all but the most basic windows install, i dont use it anymore and i hate it. I have completly fallen in love with this lady named Ubuntu. Keep reading if you want to hear about my experience setting it up and playing with it over 3 weeks

 Install:
-------------------------------------------------
I started out by loading acronis disk doctor to make a blank partition, i have installed multiple oses in the past and this is just how i like to do it. Not that i dont trust the ubuntu tools just a preference for me. So I now have a 10 gig partition of nothing. I fire up the Ubuntu cd and choose install... It asks me a few questions and detects my windows install in imports my background and a few other settings. I am very excited at this point. As i moved on i choose manual for the partition editing. I made an ex3 partition for my system and my swap partition and answered a few more questions and hit install. I came back in about 30 min and there was a message telling me to remove the disk and hit enter to reboot, so i did....

First Impression:
-----------------------------------------------
So my system boots up and i get to GRUB, look at all the nifty choices and just to make sure i picked windows xp pro worked fine so i rebooted and chose Ubuntu the second time around. Login at the pretty screen and i am at the desktop. The first thing i checked, being on a laptop is do i have wireless card support.... and YES! i did :) it was awesome. Never got it to work with another distro.. didnt have to touch a damn thing and it was working. It asked me for my WPA key and boom there was google. Well so far very impressed... 

Software and Drivers:
---------------------------------
I then get a warning about restricted graphics drivers... I am very confused so I read the description about it and it seems pretty safe to me. I click use and install and it worked like a champ... hell 2 for 2 so far... So I enable the advanced effects and play with the wobbly windows for about 15 min... I figure now is the time to install some apps that I like so i start with thunderbird... and it failed. It gave me an error about the vendor not supporting my system or me not meeting the requirements, or something like that. I figure its freakin thunderbird i know it has to work in ubuntu and i have full 3d hardware enabled on this so it cant be my requirements... its a mail client.. So i look into the settings for the add remove and realize that its not actually searching ALL available software sources... so i check the rest of those off and boom thunderbird installed super fast and was up and running with my pop3 in about another 10 min.

Filesystem:
------------------------------------------
I like having control of where things go and how they work. So this filesystem still confuses the hell out of me sometimes. I dont know where things are or where they install to. I dont know where firefox is installed or what gets run when i click on the icon i made for the top panel (also easy as hell) but thats just my ignorance not a problem with the OS. One thing that impressed me a lot was that it picked up my iPod right away, and my NTFS partition. hell it even let me write to it.... Yeah i know i probably shouldnt write to it but i had to try and it worked like a champ...

Lasting Impressions
--------------------------------------------
Over the last 3 weeks i got wine up and running (stuff i need for work), my system updates smooth as ice and i installed Compiz and began messing with the 3d effects. The only thing i have had any trouble with is dual monitor support. At work i can hook up to a second monitor and it makes my job so much easier but my questions and issues are for another thread, i have made some progress and the community has been awesome (thanks guys and gals) I have not had to use XP for anything. Not a damn thing. I am so happy and impressed that i have repartitioned everything to use about only 20 gig for windows which is a base install and a few games. Hell just today i installed the EVE online linux client. Took me 20 min, and most of that time was downloading the software client... Everything has just been so amazing and smooth that i love it. I plan on moving all of my computers over to Ubuntu, and you know whats nice? NO FREAKIN CD KEYS OR A COMPANY TO CALL EVERY TIME YOU HAVE TO REFORMAT YOUR DAMN DRIVE OR CHANGE SOME HARDWARE....

sorry had to get that off of my chest... I have not had to do any maintence on this system at all. I dont need to scan for viruses or crap like that. Ext3 is robust enought to be resistant to fragmentation and when there are updates i click the little icon, let them download and they install. No sloppy reboots or anything like that.

End:
--------------------------------
Well this has been a pretty long rant but i thought it would be nice for the community to hear how happy i have been. Seriously i never thought i could get this excited about a freakin operating system... you have a convert for life.
