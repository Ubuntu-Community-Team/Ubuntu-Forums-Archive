### Post by robertknight on 2006-02-27
Hi,

This is just a little thank-you to everyone who contributed to the performance improvements in Ubuntu Dapper (and particularly the included GNOME apps).
I installed Flight 4 on my laptop this afternoon, and for the first time I witnessed a 'normal' Linux distro starting up in the same time as Windows XP on the box (actually it is quicker to get working in Dapper because XP seems to delay a lot of work until after logging in.  Dapper is ready a few seconds after entering the password).

Warm log-in time for GNOME is only a couple of seconds or so and OpenOffice.org actually starts up reasonably quickly for a change.  In other places Dapper just feels very fast generally.  The service selector is also very useful, I wish XP had something as simple as that.

I think this bodes very well for the future considering that the competition (ie. Windows Vista) may have fairly hefty system requirements.
