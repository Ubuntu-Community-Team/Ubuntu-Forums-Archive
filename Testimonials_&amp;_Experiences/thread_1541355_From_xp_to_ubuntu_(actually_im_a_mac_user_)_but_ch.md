THE CHANGE FROM XP TO UBUNTU:

Ok so my dad left for houston (AGAIN) cause over here in Mexico the economy just isn't great , he left me a Mac Book Pro 2008 intel core 2 duo with snow leopard 64 bit, and i was very happy with it. You know how mac's are so fast and all perfect. So he decides to come back and once he does well he decides to take back the Mac because he needs more ram and processing power for working on a magazine called LLU (Leisure Learning Unlimited). Yeah hes a designer so he needs power for photoshop and preps etc. 

He brought an old pentium 4 hp pavilion laptop that his friend gave him. He left me wih the pc laptop, it had windows xp home on it and I erased everything and reinstalled windows. After 2 moths of very slow processing from the windows i decided to make some clean up's. I defragmented it, i scanned and deleted some viruses and i deleted some unneded software but it was still slow not as slow as before i cleaned it but it was slow, even my dad said that this laptop was good for nothing "LOL". He recommended Linux Ubuntu OS, i thought about it because i thought it was going to be even slower. But he downloaded open solaris virtually for the Mac and it runned great. I thought that that linux software was running great even on virtual use. So i decided to use Ubuntu and try something else. I had previously used Linux Red Hat when i was like 5 years old. My dad had replaced it from windows 98 because he thought it was frustrating, he stayed with red hat because he thought it was much better. Then we changed to mac's my dad had bought an old G4 Mac and it was the best computer ever from the previous pc.

THE EXPERIENCE:

Anyways, i downloaded ubuntu and burned an image on a disc, then i erased everything and installed it i didint want to try it out first i was anxious for using and installing Ubuntu. So once installed, my very first impression from Ubuntu was the fast opening of the Log in window i meaned it opened briliantly fast and once i logged in (even the log in was super fast) i saw the nice layout, so i started familiriazing with it. I thought that the processor was the one responsible for slowness on windows because of it's oldness and now i know that i was foolish trying to excuse windows from the slowness. So now i have over a week using Ubuntu and i think its a super Os i mean its great in almost every way, not as great as snow leopard, but i mean its cuztomizable it has tons of open and free software and I think the Ubuntu Software Center is great any plug in or software missing i can just go to the center and download it with any excuse. Theres alot off themes for the GNOME desktop, jaja ive only been able to install 2 because i still dont know what you need for most of them to run. But i mean Ubuntu surpases windows 7, xp and vista in every way, well win 7 is great its fast and all but i just made Ubuntu my new hobby untill i can get another Mac. Even if i gain a new Mac i will buy an hp with intel i7 and Run Ubuntu and Win 7 at the same time.
