### Post by oldefoxx on 2009-11-10
This is an absolute warning to most to just stay away from Ubuntu 9.10 for at least a half year.  It is full of unresolved faults and pitfalls, and I would not attempt a move to it again until maybe June of 2010.  Go with the nice, easy to use and much more attractive version 9.04 instead.  Some have even elected to stay with earlier versions. even as far back as 7.10, since they are all solid, easy to install and easy to use.

Am I kidding?  Absolutely not.  I am not a Linux or Ubuntu expert by any means, but I've had my share of PC problems and issues with getting them up and going for many years, and I really like Ubuntu, but 9.10 is so bad that it ranks right near the bottom from my 30-plus years experience.

What some examples of what I mean?  Just read some of the many postings made by people that have run into all sorts of problems getting 9.10 installed, upgrading to it, or just trying to get through some period of time with no further issues.  And you will find many of my own complaints out there with them.

Want an example?  After some struggle, about five times more than I would have expected, I got 9.10 up and running on a Toshiba Satellite Notebook.  With that going for me, and that experience to guide me, I figured a Gateway Desktop would be even easier.

Boy, was I wrong.  I had to begin over and over when something would hose up and give me no other option.  After over a week of days and evenings trying to nurture it along, I worked out some hard rules about what might work and made some progress, so much so that last night I thought I finally had it done.  Not that it really  rewarded me or anything, because compared to earlier versions 9.10 looks drab, even ugly to me.  And it does not come with the full array of preinstalled applications that they managed to work onto the earlier install disks.  Now you have to spend a good bit of time going through a long array of likely applications and elect to download and install them yourself.  That takes time.  But before I went to bed after 2 am, I felt I could work with what I had.

Only that was not to be.  My login screen is different this moring, and while it correctly identified me and accepted my password, after about 5 seconds it would cycle back to the login screen and put up a warning message in the upper right corner.  This is basically what it said:

- Ubuntu has detected an inconsistency in the options selected for power management.  Please contact your Computer Administrator 

What options?  This is a desktop that I am doing, and I never looked for or made any designations related to power management!  Must be some screw-up on the part of the installer.  But to just tell me that and refuse to let me go ahead and login?  That is totally  uncalled for.  Power management is its own thing, what it should do is first let me in, then advise me when at the desktop level to address what is really a minor problem, or to work out the best power options itself and just notify me of the change.

I mean this action on the part of the 9.10 release is plain stupid, but as I found out over a week and more, all too typical for what has gone wrong with Ubuntu 9.10.  it's like this great concept and developing operating system to be had by all had suddenly been turn over to a committee of politically correct and agenda-driven people to completely mismanage.

No wonder three technical writers have written articles that indicate that Windows 7 and Ubuntu 9.10 are now equals.  Microsoft had to struggle to raise itself slighty from the pit that Vista had cast it into. and Ubuntu 9.10 had to tumble badly in order to pass each other in flight, but so they did.  Want to see if Ubuntu keeps falling of begins to rise again?  Give it six months or more and check back.  Or go with an earlier and far better version of Ubuntu.

Not my finest posting, but sometimes you have to call them as you see them.  Get it right with Ubuntu, and don't fail us now when you've been getting so close to the ideal!

### Post by QIII on 2009-11-10
You can and should share your experiences so that we may all learn.  Those experiences should also be communicated through launchpad so that the developers can address them.  Your input in that regard is very important.  I would encourage you to take part in testing Lucid when the Alphas are released, so that the issues you encounter can be addressed early, rather than having them persist into the final release.

If Jaunty works better for you, then I agree whole-heartedly that you should use it.  Use what works.  That is the great benefit of choice!

But I counter your experience with this:  I started with the Alphas, the Beta, the RC and the final release.

The problems I encountered were what is to be expected.  The final release was a flawless installation.

You have to understand that my experience is anecdotal, as are the individual experiences of each person who posts here.

Remember that people do not come to forums like this to complain about things that are going right.  So what you don't see are the millions of installations that went well.  Nor, on the other hand, do you see the installations that did not go well but the user was able to rectify.

Assuming that Karmic is bad based on the fact that people come to this forum for help resolving issues is assuming several logical fallacies.

There are Windows forums aplenty.

There are Solaris forums.

There are OSX forums.

The fact is that every OS and version has its pros and cons; there are always a subgroup of users who have significant issues.  These forums exist to address them.  Your input is important -- even critical -- to making Ubuntu better.

See, the problem is that your GUI does not let your user have root rights, so the profile process at the start of seamonkey is thereby hindered.  After the chmod process, then seamonkey was able to create the profile and get on to the task at hand.
