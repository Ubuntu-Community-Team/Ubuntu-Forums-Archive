### Post by xxPHAROSxx on 2010-01-24
Back in early 2000, I was playing around with Slack and BSD. My friend and I dared each other to remove Win2K which ran everything we did and learn from trial and error. We chose Slackware. I was a bit so so with KDE, but lived with it.

A few times this guy would get so frustrated at his new learning experience that he'd shout "PENGUIN!" to people out his window. xD

Over the years, I realised that manual installations and terminal brought back my old DOS days and the command like being on a BBS. I got familiar with procedures and it grew on me.

I later moved to Debian and found the .deb packages slick. I was also loving gnome a lot. Nice and slim. Ubuntu finally came around the corner and although a bit buggy in the beginning, it became home. To this date, the soft touch of it will keep me around for awhile. :)

Ubuntu and WINE run what I need simply and without hassle. The odd thing I use which WINE has complications, I have a dual-boot of XP for that. No internet access on Windows. Just there to run the odd program, or game. I'd still love to see Rollercoaster Tycoon 3 run as Platinum in wine. :D

I was a bit discouraged with 9.10 due to one thing. I found it to load slower. A friend said it's faster now, so if I ever get bored, I could try it again, but atm, I'm on 9.04 and I feel welcomed everytime I load up my pc.

:popcorn:
