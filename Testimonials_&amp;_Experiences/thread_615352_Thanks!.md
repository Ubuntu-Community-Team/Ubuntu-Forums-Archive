### Post by keskew on 2007-11-17
I wanted to say thanks to the Ubuntu developers and everyone who helps make the Ubuntu forums such a great community.  I can’t tell you how much help I have received from this site.

I consider myself an advanced Windows user.  I get a lot of requests for tech support from the people who know me.  I have always been fascinated with Linux but had the impression it was for “experts” and that I didn’t have the time to spend learning a brand new OS.  This semester the professor of the class I am taking (Numerical Methods) required us to write code in Fortran90 using Linux.  I decided to use this opportunity to learn Linux, as I always wanted to do.

I did some research and decided Ubuntu was probably the best distro to begin using since I was new to Linux.  I used the live CD for a few days, which was long enough for me to know that I really liked it and wanted to continue to learn more.  I installed Ubuntu as a sort-of dual boot using wubi.  I kept that configuration for about a month or two.  I fell in love with Ubuntu (a true sign of my dorkness), bought a larger hard drive and installed it in my HP Pavilion laptop with full intentions of doing a true dual boot with Windows XP and Ubuntu.

I did, and the result has been that I have been using Ubuntu almost 100%.  In fact, since I got VMware working properly, I haven’t booted out of Ubuntu.  As I type this message (on my wife’s laptop) I am shrinking the partition Windows XP uses to give more space to Ubuntu.  The only reason I boot into Windows XP (through VMware) is to use Quicken.

I do believe that over the next few years Linux, thanks to distros like Ubuntu, will begin to capture more of the OS market share.  I don’t think it will dominate MS, but that’s OK as long as those who are technically conscious have an alternative that is on par with Windows.  In my opinion, Ubuntu is there, though the average PC user may not be savvy enough to work through the initial set-up issues.  I am sure over time those initial set-up problems will decrease.  

Anyway, thanks again for the excellent introduction to Linux.

### Post by erginemr on 2007-11-17
Welcome to Ubuntu forums and to the Linux World then! =D>

My experience has been pretty much the same as yours. First just to take a peek in Ubuntu, then resize hard drive to give more space to it, getting used to the command line, witnessing its sheer power, using Windows less and less, to the point of nil. And I have enjoyed every bit of it, especially learning the ins and outs of my new system, facing and overcoming problems, and last but not least, being part of the Ubuntu forums family.

One of Ubuntu's forte is its amazing community, which in my opinion, is even better than most technical support departments of commercial software.

Before I started following Ubuntu forums, I was told that Ubuntu forums are full of conceited people, who are full of themselves and will despise n00bs most of the time, and I admit I was a little intimidated by the idea. However, after many months spent in the forums, I can see that this prejudice was completely wrong and the situation was quite reverse.

By making the switch, you will also feel the invaluable support of Ubuntu forums on your back.

Welcome Again! ):P
