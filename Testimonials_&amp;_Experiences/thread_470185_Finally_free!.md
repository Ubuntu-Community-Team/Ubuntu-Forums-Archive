### Post by Al Fairclough on 2007-06-10
A few days ago, I decided to try Ubuntu 7.04 from the Live disk, just to see if it would play nice with my P965 chipset. Previously, I had tried five other distros to see if any would work, but no joy. Much to my delight, Ubuntu recognized all of my hardware and even set it's resolution to 1280 x 1024 for my monitor. So far so good.

Based on the smooth sailing with the Live disk, I decided to dual-boot Ubuntu with my XP Pro. I installed Ubuntu to one of my smaller 160G drives and dedicated the entire volume. Once again, everything worked just great and one heck of a lot faster than XP. I was/am amazed at just how intuitive everything is in Ubuntu. Burn some music? No problemo. Just put a blank disk in the tray and click a button. As a bonus, it burns faster than Nero as well. Simply put, Ubuntu did everything I needed, faster and better than XP.

After a couple of days, I had occasion to go into my XP drive. I clicked on one of my files in which I stored my more important programs. Looking around, I realized that virtually every program was for some maintenance or security on the Microsoft OS. I had dozens of reg cleaners, anti-spyware, defraggers and a boat load of other junk, just to keep the OS alive, and I am not even counting the AV programs. It was at that point, that I discovered just how much time I spent keeping Windows running in a manner that wasn't near as good as Ubuntu!

Today I am M$ free! I now have all of my drives in ext3 and Ubuntu 7.04 is the only OS I now use. It took me 30 years, but better late than never.;)
