### Post by Ksho on 2008-02-13
Hello!

Last friday 2008-02-08 I tried to install aMSN from getdeb. They said the 7.04 version was not being updated, only the 7.10 was. So I installed the 7.04 RC1. The app was cool but I was receiving error messages so often that it was unusable. So I deleted it.

Then I thought: 7.10 has been out for sometime now. I think it is stable now. I going to upgrade. 900 MB and 2 and a half hours later the upgrading process started but then: lots of &#12452;&#12531;&#12473;&#12488;&#12540;&#12523;&#12391;&#12365;&#12414;&#12379;&#12435; messages and at 60% the upgrade window just closed.

I knew I was in a big trouble. I rebooted to windows, backed up my files, then rebooted to Ubuntu.

A pretty kernel panic message appeared just after grub. (I took a picture of my first kernel panic :D)

I tried to boot using a previous kernel version but I only got the text mode prompt. 
Tried to fix X. Nope. No X installed.
I tried fixing the fstab problem with a Live CD. I wasn't able to mount the root partition. 

Then I decided to backup my Ubuntu home and reinstall the system.I tried with a Flash Drive but I did not automount. So I burned my Home to a CD.

I put the Ubuntu 7.10 CD and clicked on install. After 30 minutes I had  ubuntu reinstalled again and no Windows lost, and 5 hours later everything was as before.
I restored my bookmarks and files from the CD. I lost nothing. I was very happy. 

Here is the good: 
- Ubuntu 7.10 is the ****!! Is better than 7.04. 
- The hardware support is awesome. I have more devices working in Ubuntu than in windows. I now have 5.1 sound working correctly!!
- The flash and java plugins in firefox were installed automatically without any problem. The nVidia drivers too.
- The codecs for Totem were installed automatically too.

Here is the bad: 
- I feel 7.10 a little slower than 7.04. Both in boot and daily usa. And a little bit more unstable.
- Average users would die of fear after the kernel panic message. Ubuntu need to verify that upgrade program. (Or maybe was my 1.8 GB of free space that caused the problem)

Now a little of history:

I have tried GNU/Linux from some time ago. The first time was with RedHat. Didn't install. At the time I had a very low quality hardware. 

Next was a 5 CD Suse. It did not like my hardware so I lost my Windows partition. Actually it was not lost, only the MBR was deleted. But I didn't know that at the time.

Later Mandriva, It installed correctly and detected all my hardware. But installing apps was a pain. I downloaded RPMs for 6 hours without success. I just wanted to install firefox!!!

Then Ubuntu. I'm not sure how do I discovered Ubuntu. I read somewhere it was the easiest distribution.
I downloaded 7.04 last August and installed it the day I was upgrading from 2K to XP. I installed both OSs the same day.

What I liked the most about ubuntu is the dual bar desktop Hahaha. 
It feels so cool. That's why I always carry a Xubuntu CD and an Ecolinux 1.0 CD in my backpack. :P

Why do I keep Windows? Adobe Premiere Pro.
Cinelerra is not good enough yet.

I will later post another message with my thoughts about broad linux adoption. There's nothing wrong with linux, the problem is with the users.

Now I need to fix the multi app sound problem and the intermitent cannot-type-nothing-in-windows problem.

PS. Today I received my shipit Ubuntu CDs!!! My CPU now features a nice Ubuntu sticker in adition to a Linux Inside sticker and a Red Mushroom sticker.

**** = onda
