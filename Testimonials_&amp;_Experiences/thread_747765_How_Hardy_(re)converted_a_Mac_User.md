### Post by Scheater5 on 2008-04-06
After using Linux exclusively for two years, I needed a format for writing music.  So, I bit the bullet and bought a macbook for Finale, Sibelius and Pro Tools.  The initial intention was to use the mac for those applications and nothing else.  
However, I found myself seduced by how nice Aqua looks, and soon I was using OSX almost exclusively.  I overlooked my issues with it, eventually figured out enough of the convoluted file system to accomplish what I needed, and kept my life on iCal.  
And then, I installed Hardy Beta on my other laptop - the one that was *supposed* to be my workhorse.  I took what I had learned about usability (and the lack thereof) from Mac, as well as a few things I did like very much, and began gutting and rebuilding my Gnome desktop.  I replaced the panels with Avant on the bottom and a panel of shortcuts on the top - leaving only the clock to indicate the default.  I altered compiz to work like the desktop-switcher on OSX.  An OSX-esque icon set, heavily modified.  I then turned on the ring-switcher, a-la Vista - giving my the aesthetics I liked from both OSX and Vista, without the aesthetics I didn't like.  I actually installed GlobalMenu, and removed it.  I find it more efficient to have the menus with their respective windows.  
Yes, I'm aware that the dock is highly inefficient - I like it.  And efficiency is at least party based on being comfortable.  Speaking of efficiency - the AWN  applet "terminal" is the coolest thing since sliced bread - saves me alot of time.  
But what finally "got me" was when I throughly had Thunderbird configured - with Lightning synced up to Gmail.  And I also have Gmail synced up with iCal.  It's brilliant.  No matter where I am, I have access to my calendars - and if I'm lucky enough to be at my Hardy laptop or my macbook I have my nice shiny interfaces.  
With Hardy, finally, suspend and hibernate work (and more importantly, return to normal) on my laptop - and the energy saving on OSX was one of the things I loved about it.  I could close the lid and suspend between classes, and the laptop would last all day.
I kept the default Hardy wallpaper, and altered AWN to look dark orange - just to remind me always I am on Ubuntu.  
So, the macbook is my music machine again.  And the Ubuntu laptop is once again my workhorse - which means I get to spend more time in Ubuntu.  It's been lovely tinkering to get it just right.  And I had to do alot "by hand" - change one icon at a time, install avant from svn, drastically alter the panels - but in truth, that's what I love about linux.  The ability to make it work however I want.  It took alot of work to make Hardy work just like I want - and I suppose it looks relatively the same.  But I know it works different - and it's exactly how I want it.

### Post by Scheater5 on 2008-04-06
> What kind of music format are you using on the Mac and what do you find lacking in Linux in terms of music software that you remain with the Mac? You mentioned Pro Tools, so I'm assuming that is your primary recording/mixing tool that is not portable to Linux?

I needed the proprietary programs more than the format of OSX.  I would personally drastically prefer to use Linux exclusively if Finale and Pro Tools had native Linux installs.  There is nothing on Linux that comes close to them, and running under WINE is impractical because music production by necessity can't have lag.  
Ubuntu Studio is quite powerful, and as a matter of fact I use it for some of my recording - but it's also quite complex to configure JACK and get everything working just right.  
However, for typesetting and note writing, NoteEdit and Rosegarden just don't compare to Finale and Sibelius.
