### Post by DrHenley on 2012-06-09
Thank you Ubuntu for years of computing freedom.  It's sad that you took that wrong turn and lost your way.  I wish you well, but since you can't take me where I want to go, we must now part ways.  

10.04 was, IMO, the best Ubuntu ever.  The netbook version was great on my old Acer Aspire One.  I'm running Firestarter and MySQL on several 10.04 boxes at work, and they have performed like clockwork.  I only use the LTS releases, so this Unity thing wasn't a concern until now.  

I had a major WTF moment upon installing 12.04.  Ditto with the Windows 8 preview.  It seemed that the whole computing world had taken leave of its senses...with the exception of Android which appeared to be going the right direction with Ice Cream Sandwich.

After pulling my hair out for weeks trying to get things working the way I wanted in Ubuntu 12.04 (I tried Gnome 3/Gnome Classic/KDE/Unity), I discovered today that Linux Mint is going MY WAY, so I'm jumping on that bandwagon as of RIGHT NOW.

At first I was angry and wanted to lash out at the Gnome and Ubuntu teams, but I'm over that now that I have an escape route.  I wish you luck!  (you're going to need it ;))
