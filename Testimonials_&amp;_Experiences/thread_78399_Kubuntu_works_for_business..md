### Post by orev on 2005-10-18
We are currently running Kubuntu 5.10 (upgraded 4 days ago from Hoary 5.04) on 5 desktops in our businesses.

Kubuntu is rock solid, offers great business software, and easy for all of us to use - and most of our employees are not computer saavy.

Unfortunetly, we still have WindowsXP on several computers, because of our need to access some of our distributor portals which are built to run inside Internet Explorer.  (Internet Explorer inside Kubuntu was tried, but crashed alot and did not work completely.)

Our business sells products and services retail through stores and online.

If you have any questions about solutions we use to tailor Kubuntu to our business, just ask.

### Post by ltmon on 2005-10-19
Nice to hear.

Have you considered running a VNC solution for the employees to use IE when necessary.  That way you just have one Windows machine, which you can log into via VNC whenever you absolutely _need_ IE or MS Office.

The other ways could be:
- To buy a supported version of Wine (such as CrossOver from CodeWeavers)
- Buy vmware and run a Windows virtual machine when necessary
