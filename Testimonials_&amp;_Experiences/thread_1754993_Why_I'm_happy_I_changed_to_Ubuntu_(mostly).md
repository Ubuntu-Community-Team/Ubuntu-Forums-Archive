### Post by sea_dawg on 2011-05-10
Reasons why I'm happy I changed to Ubuntu (mostly)

1 - This forum.  I have recieved quick, cheerful and accurate help whenever I have needed it.  With a bit of patience I can dig around and find most answers without posting a cry for help.

2 - Ease of software installation.  It is almost always totally painless. 

3 - Ease of updates.  Most of the updates I have needed in Ubuntu are handled automagicaly by Update Manager.  The only problem was with an incompatible USB wireless adapter during initial installation of Ubuntu.  A but of study in this forum and at Ubuntu Wiki and in a short time I had it up and running.  Update Manager works well, works quickly and lets me choose when I want to update if it is not convienent for me at the moment.  In contrast Windows driver updates take hours and the process is prone to problems requiring much research, multiple downloads, endlessly reading documentation and reboot after reboot before my machine is running again.  I'm taking a break from a Windows drive update to write this, I have been at it for 2 hrs and not done yet.

4 - Ubuntu is highly customizable.  I like to make my OS my own.  I like to tinker with it.

5 - Hardware runs faster and cooler.  

6 - Software runs faster.

7 - Startups are much faster.  

8 - Shutdowns are faster.  Something I seldom see mentioned here is that shutdowns compared to Windows are instantaneous.  Yes, I know I can leave Linux running for ever and not experience OS melt down.  But I don't like to leave the hardware on when I will be gone for a long time.  When I'm leaving for days or weeks I like to make a last minute email check before I shutdown, lock the door and leave.  I swear with Windows if it has been up and running and working hard for a while I can mow the lawn and have lunch while it shuts down.  Ubuntu shuts down in seconds.

9 - The mostly part has to do with specific professional software requirements.  I haven't yet found any marine navigation software for Ubuntu that is up to the professional standards of Nobletec's software or Rose Point Navigation's software.  OpenCPN is good, but not professional quality.  Given how important that software is when underway I'm not willing to try it under Wine or a Virtual Machine.
