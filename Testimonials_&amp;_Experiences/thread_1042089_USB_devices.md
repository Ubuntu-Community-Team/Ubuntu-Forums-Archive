### Post by phlembob on 2009-01-17
A tidbit of info.  When USB devices are continually plugged in sometimes build oxidized spots.  If they have been in the same USB port sometimes pulling and replacing isn't enough, because the oxidation is on both the male and the female.  You may need to clean or plug into a different USB port for everything to work again.  I found this out today as I shifted OSs to see if one OS wasn't working correctly.  When both OSs failed in the same manner I had to remove replace and clean and shift ports for everything to back on track.
