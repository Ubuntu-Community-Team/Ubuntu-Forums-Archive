### Post by kensmith on 2007-01-03
I am new to linux as I started with Dos and Tandy back in 1980. I went up thru windows from 3.0 to 2000 pro. However my computers are 8 years old and had to up date them I purchased 2 Dell computers with XP Pro on them and started out looking really good. Then problems in one machine. This was 2 weeks before Christmas as these were to be my Christmas presents. The one machine was not downloading right. It would stop in the middle. Looking at others peoples problems, I found that a lot of people was having the same problem and microsoft was unable to help them. The other machine started sending messages to microsoft on its own and then I got mad. I decided to use linux as I was checking into the Ipod a month earlier. I went on the linux site and started looking at what was being used. I found that the most downloads was Ubuntu and they were having great results. I found the Ubuntu main web site and read on how to download burn a CD and check the results.I backup most things on my computer to another computer as I had not completed the final downoads from the old one yet. I sorta got paniced by formating the new XP hard drive so I went out and got another 40 Gig Hard drive. This way I figured if nothing would work, I would just put the XP drive back in. Anyway after the new drive install I followed the directions to install Ubuntu on the hard drive. It came right up with no problems. I then tried to find the network and it came up. I started getting impressed. I then checked the Microsoft spread sheets and word and they transferred into the linux machine with no trouble. Ok on to the MP3 players. Found them and they installed and worked. I used firefox before and thunderbird so installed them. No problem it all worked. Now I'm really getting excited. Checked out the internet and it came up fast. Oh.. now I know that all the McAfee virus protection was really holding it back. Now all I needed was some good system tools. I found and download Automatix and Easybuntu. Gave me the java flash and disk checking tools I needed. I also got Debian which comes with a lot of programs and programming tools. I did use Ubuntu 6.06 as they stated was the most stable one. To date I will never go back to Microsoft. The only problem I am having is with Lexmark printers and drivers. But I will just get another printer that will work. 
I want to thank the Ubuntu team also for there work. I also want to contribute to the work force as soon as I understand Python. Ubuntu also auto updates programs and will show a icon to install the new ones. I'm about ready to move on to the servers and setup one of those. The Ubuntu team has really came a long way since I tried linux red hat about 5 years ago and never got it working. But now this is a new era and its time to move on. When you see your computer doing things fast and no dll messages, you know you are in the ballpark.
