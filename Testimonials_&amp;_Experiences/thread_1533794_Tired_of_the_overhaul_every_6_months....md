### Post by BMWDriver on 2010-07-18
Just a word on why I left Ubuntu altogether. I've quite enjoyed it, it's been a great experience, but I've had to give it up.

Every 6 months, there's a major overhaul. Not doing the overhaul makes me feel left behind. Every six months, there's a lot of changes made that make it difficult to adapt - though many are seamless. And where I used Gimp, the latest version of the latter was never available until someone somewhere made a package for the now derelict version of Ubuntu I was using - tired that I was of taking risks or having to forgo an upgrade as things weren't perfect yet - and I had to pray the Heavens it would happen, let alone find that package. The repositories are just not updated with recent software except when critical. **There seems to not be any backwards compatibility.**

So I find upgrading the OS every six months to be a major risk, experience proven, and extremely tedious and quite frankly, cause for great anguish and anxiety.

I've had to keep two hard drives, one to test the upgrade, and the other to keep a working ability until I was satisfied my data was not at risk, and that the system was indeed stable and all the hardware functional. Then would I transfer the files, work with the new, and keep the previous handy, just in case. 

I've reluctantly moved to Windows 7 (first back to XP), but I can at least have a peaceful mind that there are no major overhauls to be done for a few years yet, and that I can upgrade Gimp without much trouble, any other than a simple download with install.

I miss it somewhat, nice as it was, but I just can't spare the trouble anymore. The emotional cost is just too high.
