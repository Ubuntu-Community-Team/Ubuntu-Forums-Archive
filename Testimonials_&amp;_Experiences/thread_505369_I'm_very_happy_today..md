### Post by Lozz on 2007-07-20
Yesterday I finally managed to convert my brother to Ubuntu after quite some discussion & today I installed it on his ageing Tiny PC (British forum members may remember these from the 90s). I wasn't quite sure how well the install would go but I decided to attempt it anyway. There were basically three areas I thought would be problems.....

1) The dreadful spec of the machine slowing the system down. After all it only had a 1ghz Processor & 256mb RAM as well as having no 3d video card.
2) The particularly annoying little hardware issues such as getting his wireless network to work (something I have never done before on Ubuntu, as all the other installs I've carried out have had wired connections).
3) Actually getting him to feel confident in using it, so that he didn't go back to windows.

To my surprise not one of these areas proved to be a problem, though a little slow from the live CD  the install still worked perfectly & once Ubuntu was on his computer the wireless card (linksys with a broadcom chip) took literally two minutes to sort out. Following this I began to show him how everything worked & he loved it, I've actually never seen him so enthusiastic about his computer. Furthermore he insisted that he'd never had an operating so fast & I must say that I too was impressed by how well Ubuntu performed given the poor hardware.
