### Post by plurworldinc on 2011-05-07
I wasn't sure if I would like Unity because I have gotten so use to my Gnome interface. The only major downside I have ever had with Ubuntu 10.10 was my laptop ran slow, really slow. But as I am trying to get use to Unity I have discovered my laptop is alot faster and fuctional it feels like having a new computer all over again. 

   Right now I am enjoying an ice cold glass of tea while surfing the net and writing this post in my backyard. I am thrilled with Unity because it does something that a lot of people seems to be forgetting, it works. I had a little trouble with wifi when I first started but that was cleared up in a few minute. 

   I thank I shell be keeping Unity on my laptop and maybe try out Gnome3 on my desktop for I can get a better feel of the difference. I would have loved to see an option to have both when you log in like Gnome and KDE, but it can be installed and I believe it really comes down to the end user to figure out what they want in the end. 

   Ubuntu is a wonderful product for all. :KS
