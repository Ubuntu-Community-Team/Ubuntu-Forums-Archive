### Post by Strider42 on 2007-02-18
I know this might sound mad, but I don't think I have been so excited for bloody ages.  I now have Ubuntu 6.10 and VMWare running XP on my Dell laptop, with everything working (well my Broadcom wireless doesn't work, but the Belkin PCMCIA does).

Being very new to Linux this has been a hard learning curve, but after about 3 weeks, several re-installs and reading nearly every post in this forum :lolflag: , I now have the perfect setup for my situation.  I can boot into Linux, but still get access to crucial programs I need under XP to do my job.

Although I can't claim to have given Bill the flick altogether, I do feel I have made a big step in the right direction.

I think to sum up, all I wanted to say is, that if you're new to Linux, don't be disheartened when it doesn't go to plan first time around.  It's a totally different way of doing things but the support available here is second to none.  I think 3 weeks from being a complete n00b to having a setup that actually does what I want is a decent time frame.  So stick with it.  You'll feel good at the end of it all :guitar: 

Happy days...
