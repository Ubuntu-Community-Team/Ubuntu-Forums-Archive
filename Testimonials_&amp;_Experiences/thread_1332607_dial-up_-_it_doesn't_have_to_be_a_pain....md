### Post by longtom on 2009-11-20
I am gobsmacked...

Some of you might remember that dial-up is a MAJOR issue with Ubuntu, especially when one has a win modem.  I had to jump through a countless amount of hoops to get it eventually going in 8.10 with the help of a lot Ubuntu helpers from this forum, especially a member with the name of lkraemer, who was a tower of patience and strength.

I saw puppy before and fiddled around with it, having it in a VB as well as running it from a CD.  I did put it now on an old 512mb flash drive and it works sweet.

Just for the fun of it I used the connection shortcut on the desktop, selected dial-up.  After 2 windows, were I did what was recommended, I was asked for the account details.
I typed them in and....was online.  Just imagine - just like that, no hassles, no wvdial tweaking, no special files and websites to get the modem detected - nothing like that.  I couldn't believe it.

Now - what does puppy have what Ubuntu doesn't do to get that right?  It certainly can't be a space problem on the iso file - puppy is 50mb...:P

No - I don't need really an answer but I found it interesting enough to share it with you.  
Especially in the light that Ubuntu from 9.04 onwards wvdial is also not part of the distro anymore and the dial-uppers get somewhat of a raw deal...

This is typed from Puppy with dial-up - and I still can't get over it...:P

### Post by Irihapeti on 2009-11-20
I'm no longer on dialup, but I also found that the most recent version of Puppy activated my modem card with nothing needing to be done.

I don't know for sure, but I suspect that it's because Puppy contains restricted drivers that Ubuntu doesn't.

Being 100% unrestricted software does have a downside for some people.

I also wish there was more awareness of the need for dialup. I get annoyed when I see posts exhorting someone to get broadband. It simply isn't available to a lot of people, either entirely, or without considerable expense.
