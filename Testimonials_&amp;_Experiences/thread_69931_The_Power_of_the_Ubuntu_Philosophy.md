### Post by Kyral on 2005-09-28
I figured it was time for me to share my Ubuntu story, which has reached beyond the desktop and into my personal life.

I've been using Linux off and on for about 3 years now. Started on Slack 10 on a Dell Inspirion 1100. Not a good choice for a beginner in retrospect, but I had always been rather fluent with computers so it was no biggie. I remember having to compile NDiswrapper from scratch using instructions I printed out from LinuxQuestions.org, having to search through the various websites to find the refresh rates for my screen (because Dell didn't have those on file, the 1100 was discontinued shortly after I got it), and compiling the 2.6.3 kernel from scratch with only the help in the menuconfig to guide me on what and what not to touch. 

As I graduated high school and left for college, I was still bouncing between Linux and XP. Once in my Freshman year (last year for those who have visited my Ubuntu Wiki page) I discovered Gentoo and tried it. It was good, and made me realize the value of a good package manager. By December of last year, I was dual booting XP and Gentoo, and my old 1100 was getting old and underpowered for anything. So I built what would become my current system ( I mean a fried mobo, a couple additional HDs, new CPU, and a new GFX card later ) and slapped Gentoo on it. Thing is, Gentoo didn't seem to like my SATA HD much, so I reluctantly went back to XP.

This is when one of my CompSci TAs suggested Ubuntu. Keep in mind this was back before Hoary came out. I tried it, but it was using the old Debian installer so I was presented with a choice of Warty, Hoary, Woody, or Sarge. I had no experiance at all with Debian, so I just randomly picked one and was spat out at the command prompt. Again, trial by fire. I didn't know how to use Apt, and with no GUI either, let alone a net conn. So I returned to Gentoo.

A little while later (enough time for Hoary to come out) the TA again suggested Ubuntu after overhearing my bitching about Gentoo one day. This time the experiance was a complete turnaround. The GUI fired up quite quickly and pointed me to the ever helpful UbuntuGuide (Return I say! Bring it back!). Within' minutes I had 99% of things working. And the next time I reformated (for whatever reason, I believe I added an IDE HD and that borked the boot order) XP was not invited to the party.

That was 7 months ago. Since then I have learned Ubuntu (almost) inside and out, and have sought to help others with it, and advocate it. Now you may be asking, how has this impacted your personal life? Well, the community. Early on I asked some pretty stupid questions, and I never took a flame for it. The UbuntuForums community truly embraces the Philosophy "Humanity Towards Others" and this has spurred me to try and give back in some way other than helping in the Forums and on IRC. This should become evident shortly. Recently I signed the Ubuntu Code of Conduct. In addition to trying to uphold it online, I decided that it is also a good way to conduct yourself in real life, and so it goes that from this point onward I shall do my very best to uphold the philosophy of Humanity Towards Others in my real life. Now if you excuse me, class calls!
