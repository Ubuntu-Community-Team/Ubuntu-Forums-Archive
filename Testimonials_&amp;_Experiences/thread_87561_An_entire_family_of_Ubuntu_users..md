### Post by zerhacke on 2005-11-08
My wife's a die hard Windows user.  She never could stand it when I put other Distros on the family PCs.  She'd refuse to even use a computer that she had to go through a GRUB menu to get to Windows.  She mostly uses her computer for voice chat on Yahoo Messenger.

Now my wife uses Gyach enhanced, and even has written her own applications menu for Enlightenment.  She created her own theme out of parts on the net for Gnome.  And, she's a Same Gnome lover (though she can't beat my score!!).

My fifteen year old stepson, my wife's son, uses Ubuntu EXCLUSIVELY when he's at our house instead of at his fathers.  He loves the customization of Gnome and Enlightenment.  Usually he uses Gnome and is impressed with downloading new parts for his themes.  I even have him using Evolution.

My ten year old step son recently asked me to create a login for him on Linux.  He's the current high score holder for Robots in our family.  He'll be learning about themes the next time he comes for visitation.

Get this.  *Even our three year old daughter is in on the act.*  She uses Ubuntu to go to Nickjr.com to play her little Nick Jr games without me having to cancel "printer setup" for her every ten minutes.  Most of the games at NickJr.com have a button you can press to print your little game out.  She's only three and can't read, but she can navigate very well with a mouse and has figured out almost everything on the site.  Now that I have her playing the games on Ubuntu instead of Windows, she never has to call for me to undo the printer setup wizard if she clicks it by accident.

My entire family now uses the computer without me yelling at them for infecting us with spyware or virii.  I no longer have to worry about my daughter going to ill-advised places on the net by clicking things she shouldn't have to.  I no longer have to worry that my stepsons will download new active-x components or browser plugins that will ruin system performance.  Ubuntu has made my life easier - we've not had to reinstall since I put Hoary on here.  With Windows, we were forced to do a reinstall every time my stepsons came to visit.

Ubuntu's made the computer fun again.  Now I don't have to go "augh, let me fix it again" every time someone uses it.  Now we can just concentrate on how fun the computer is again.

### Post by jdong on 2005-11-08
I hear ya there.... It's typically the younger ones that adopt a new OS the fastest.

My 5-year-old sister uses the computer for barbie.com or nickjr.com or those flash-based gaming sites, and Ubuntu works very well for it. My parents are now beginning to use Ubuntu for banking and other high-security tasks.


When I used to share the family Windows XP system, my parents were absolutely spoiled. I was the security freak making the inpenetrable system, and the word spyware never every entered their vocabulary. However, as soon as I stopped spending all that time locking down the computer, spyware was a rampant problem on the system, and my parents no longer felt safe using the system for confidential matters...


Currently, Windows is only used at our house for those proprietary apps that require it.

### Post by Mgcross on 2005-11-17
My family has used Ubuntu exclusively since 5.04. No windows in our home. When we moved to South Korea, I brought my tower with me, and have since upgraded to 5.10. We recently purchased another system from an ex-pat who was going home and the first thing I did was nuke XP from orbit and repave with 5.10. The systems share files, and the printer, was no fuss, and firestarter made sharing the dsl connection easy. I've added clamav to scan our email, only because I don't want to pass on virii to our friends back home.

My son was a little upset about not having XP for games, but then I introduced hime to ET, Need for speed Underground 2, and Quake 4, all but one running natively. For NFSU 2 I had to resort to cedega, but it runs perfectly. We've haven't looked back.

I've tried this before with KDE centric distros, but my wife HATED KDE. She loves Gnome though.
