### Post by User_Program on 2006-10-27
Another happy Edgy user!  I was kinda hesitant on installing Edgy due to reading alot of posts giving it low praise.  Though I'm glad I did.  

Everything that is suppose to work by default did. Things like nvidia graphic drivers and wintv prv-350.  I used automatix to install some needed software and the nvidia drivers and this wiki entry for the wintv card [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Install_IVTV_Edgy?highlight=%28ivtv%29](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Install_IVTV_Edgy?highlight=%28ivtv%29)  BTW I love this feature of not having to build the mod everytime I install a new kernel, Good on yea man! 

I am a longtime KDE user and thought I would give Gnome a try after a day of using it I there were a couple of features I missed that were in KDE, Thinking I would have to do a clean kubuntu install due to problems I had with installing KDE from Gnome in Dapper but this wasn't the case this time a simple sudo apt-get install kubuntu-desktop worked great. I also liked the Hardware Database I hope to see more development  added to this.

There are a few things I didn't like.

One was it didn't recognize my Compactflash Jumpshot USB drive. I had to edit the fstab to get it working. No Biggie though.

Two in Kubuntu it seems that the Systems Settings UI needs alittle work or things need to be added to it.  Adding a Launch Feedback tab would be nice also some settings when ticked and unticked don't work properly. So Kcontrol seems to be a more effective way of adjusting settings.  That area of Kubuntu looked more like a downgrade instead of and upgrade.

Three  I had faster boot times with Dapper then Edgy but again I don't reboot that often so no biggie.

Over all I love it and have no real complaint as far as stability and ez of use.  

Great Job!!
