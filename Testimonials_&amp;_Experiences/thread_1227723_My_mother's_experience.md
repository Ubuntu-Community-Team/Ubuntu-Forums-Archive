### Post by LinuxFox on 2009-07-31
To make a long story short, my mother kept having bad luck with viruses on her Windows computer.  So she gave her computer to a friend to fix, and used mine with Ubuntu (though I dual boot Windows).  She got her computer back and she gave me the ok for Ubuntu.

Well I was in a discussion with her, she was telling someone about what I can do with computers.  She mentions Ubuntu.  She said "it took a few months to get use to, but it really is better than Windows".

It really surprised me since she didn't like changing to Ubuntu at first.  Now she has it as a dual boot too.  It's not deep, but it's nice to hear someone say this.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-07-31
It's been my experience, when sharing Ubuntu with others they either love it or hate it. Very few people I've interacted with fall in between.

I've also noticed that those that love it, tend to be open to change and not fixated on the status quo. I provided an older neighbor with a Ubuntu live cd and spent about hour showing him how to run it from the live cd and the basics. A few days later he contacted me and requested I help him install it. Turns out he had not used Windows since receiving the live cde and found Ubuntu extremely intuitive. He's almost 80 years old, yet he's open to change that either improves his life or hobbies.

Guess, this is another example of why we should never assume anything with respect to how someone else might interrupt something.

Good Luck and hope you mother continues to enjoy the experience.
