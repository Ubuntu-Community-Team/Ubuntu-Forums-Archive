### Post by GoS_ on 2008-07-11
I installed Ubuntu about two and a half months ago alongside Vista on my computer.  I'm glad I did, but I'm also glad I chose to dual boot.  So far I've had a very mixed experience with Ubuntu, but my time using it is always getting better, so I believe that those that get rid of it after a couple weeks really should give it a chance.  
     The main thing I like to use Ubuntu for is programming.  I'm just learning, but I've found programming on Vista to be a bit annoying.  The build-essentials package makes it very simple.  The full-screen terminal is another thing that Vista badly lacked, so it's nice to use one again on Ubuntu.  Aside from programming, I found Ubuntu to be very well suited to basic tasks.  e-mail, internet surfing, and doing office applications work just as well as Vista's equivalents.  They are also all ready to use upon installation, so there really isn't any hassle to do basic things like that at all.
     I found the installation of Ubuntu to be very easy, which was a very pleasant surprise.  It took me probably all of ten minutes to have it on my hard disk, and the recognition of my wireless card was very impressive too (As I remember Vista had a little trouble with that).  So getting Ubuntu up and running was a very easy task, and I found it to be on par with Vista's installation.
     Despite being a very solid OS all around I do have a couple of minor complaints.  Support, the obvious issue, really was a bummer.  The Nvidia drivers for Vista are very good, but not so much on Linux.  And gaming has proved so difficult that I just boot up Vista for it (again a support issue, but I think worth noting).  Although the installation was easy, I am finding it hard to get used to the .tar .gz .rpm things.  Even after over two months, I just try to avoid downloading anything outside the package manager, which is a very nice feature by the way.  I've also experienced several graphical glitches, nothing serious but a little annoying.  Vista just feels a little more stable to me.  But the strangest thing is that Vista beats Ubuntu's boot time by 5-8 seconds.  I'm sure I could fix it somehow, but I think for the average user that would prove somewhat bothersome (I personally don't mind, both of them boot fast, I have a WD raptor).  I realize all my complaints have been many times stated, but I think it's only fair to point out Ubuntu's flaws instead of ignoring them and pretending that Ubuntu is perfect.
     Overall I think Ubuntu is a very good operating system, and I keep finding myself opting for it when GRUB brings up my choices.  Although it feels a little buggy at times, it can serve any of Vista's basic purposes and if your interested in programming, go beyond them.  Considering that Ubuntu is free, I'm amazed at the quality of it.  For those who want to get rid of Ubuntu after a few weeks, I would reconsider.  If you're like me, your Ubuntu experience will just keep getting better.

Thank you.
