### Post by buster2209 on 2011-05-11
I upgraded from 10.10 to 11.04 on pretty much the day it came out. Yeah, I'm one of them....

Anyhoo, Unity absolutely frustrated me and had me writing post after post venting my frustration.

After I had sufficiently thrown my dummy out of the cot, I went about installing the Gnome3 shell in 11.04.  After 2 days, I had gotten over the 'ooh, this is pretty phase' and came to the conclusion it was actually a pain to navigate quickly.

Next up, Kubuntu. As pretty as the new plasma is, I just didn't like it.

After that, Xubuntu. I tried that for several days but found it to be the same speed as Gnome2 but lacking the features Gnome2 has. I found this was because it used a lot of Gnome2 apps thus required Nautilus hence there was no increase in speed.

After that, I tried a variety of different distros; Opensuse, Mint Xfce, etc but none of them stuck.

Then, just the other day, Mint released Katya 11 complete with the Gnome2 shell which looks like they will be sticking with for the foreseeable future. Seing as Mint is based on Ubuntu, I thought my prayers were answered so I quickly got to installing Mint. 

I like the mintmenu, the theme and the general overall feel of the UI....... but...... it feels old and somewhat clunky compared to Unity.

So here I am, about 10 days since I first installed Unity, several temper tantrums and a weeks worth of distro hopping later finding myself back with 11.04 Unity.

Once I got the idea that the UI is a beta version, I began to enjoy and see the *potential* it has. By 11.10 I imagine many of the bugs will be worked out and the ability to customise will be a lot higher and by 12.04LTS, I imagine it will be pretty much rock solid.

So there you have it, I guess I'm now one of the 'Unity Fanboyz'...... lol
