### Post by CrazyCanuck on 2008-05-26
Wow.

I _WISH_ I had done this a while back.  I would have more knowledge about Linux commands had I realized how robust, compatible and fun the Linux World is.  Allow me to tell you all about my experience.

For the record, my first install was a total failure, but I will get to that later.

To install Ubuntu 8.04 from CD took me aproximately 10 minutes.  I started the install, then went to make a coffee, then stepped outside for a cigarette.  I return, and there is Ubuntu, CD happily telling me to take the disc out of the drive.  Then the dramatic boot to the desktop was a thrill!  EVERYTHING connected to my computer was recognized (USB drive, USB modem et al) and was happy to be there.  I then chose to update my machine, and was told that 89 patches were waiting for me to download.  All patches downloaded fine, and I was even more impressed.  So, I decide to peruse the Add/Remove section looking to finalize my installation.  Of course, I found many things I needed, including some wonderful space simulations and Bluetooth technologies.  After spending about 5 hours looking at everything, and choosing perhaps 30 or so packages to download, I decided to check if I was in need of another system update.  Sure enough, 19 patches were available to be downloaded and installed.  Once again, worked flawlessly.

I intend to open up a few businesses, and decided to look at Linux Ubuntu to help me do the administration thats necessary to run my businesses.  I have a whole lot of reading to do online and in-system!  Oh, did I mention I am very very happy?  HAH! :)

Now, back to my first attempt to install the OS.

I am in Canada, and made the mistake of selecting Canada for my keyboard layout.  I lost a few keys, such as my question mark, and asterix and ampersand.  So there was One Strike against me.  Then, I was trying to update my system, and 88 patches were made available to me.  During the patch cycle, I encountered a duplicate entry error and was not able to patch anything, nevermind download my packages to finalize my installation.  So, I decided to scrub the first attempt, and chaulked it up for being the Essential Super Noob To Ubuntu.

....and now, I am here in the forums!

I had asked for many assists in the form of messages, and got wonderful responses from several people.  Thanks to all those that responded to my plea for help.

To the New User out there that is having a hard time with their installation, I say be patient, and endeavour to persevere!

Thanks to the warm Ubuntu Community!  (deep bows)

CrazyCanuck
