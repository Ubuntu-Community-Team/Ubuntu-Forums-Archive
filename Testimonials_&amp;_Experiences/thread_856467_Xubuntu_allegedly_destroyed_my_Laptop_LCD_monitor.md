### Post by aimpau on 2008-07-11
One night, my GF's uncle decided to use her laptop (made 1999;xubuntu). He was shocked to find that the magical Windows XP loading screen was nowhere to be found and said that the laptop had a virus loading named xubuntu. Yep, I found it funny but suddenly, the screen went white (unsurprising to the guys who made HP Omnibook 900). 

Being the "learned" electrician he is, the notebook cried for help when it was being sliced up. Upon CSI-like inspection, he concluded that the flex cable was torn-the very flex cable that only had a max life of 5years. Without a shadow of any doubt, he quickly turned to me and blamed me for installing xubuntu in it (which actually fix the old thing) and lectured me about electronics. According to him, the "xubuntu" os was SUPER incompatible with the old notebook. He told me that I was a stupid (im an ECE student) engineering student and that I failed to notice the laminated sticker on the palm rest reading "Made for Windows 98". Also, he stated that since xubuntu wasn't compatible, its program architecture differs from Windows. That xubuntu changed the voltage flowing through the flex cable, thus catalyzing the oxidation state of the said flex cable. Since the oxidation was faster, the flex got crispier then torn. He lectured me that installing other than windows damages computers specially the hardware. He also commented that I was lucky that the laptop didn't blew up since xubuntu also configures the battery would have probably fed too much current to the LCD.

At the end of his "seminar", I was actually quite hurt since he said those things to me with personal attacks. My GF wanted to defend me but I held her back.

Well, what I want to ask is that is there a terminal command that can restore the broken flex cable?

Will this work?
```
sudo repair flex-cable-that-costs-$50-or-just-buy-a-new-eeepc-for-$400
```

Is it possible that this is some kind of kernel bug?

Is there a Windows program alternative?
