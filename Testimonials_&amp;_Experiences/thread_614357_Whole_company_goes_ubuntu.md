### Post by richardc1 on 2007-11-15
My hatred of windows has been growing for years. I switched to FreeBSD...that gave me a crash course in *NIX. I learned how to do all kinds of things, but couldn't play Flash, games were limited, and I had to hack the snot out of anything that wasn't bundled. 

My associate, a FreeBSD guru himself, mentioned ubuntu to me. After I poked and prodded him with the propeller hat jokes and called him a traitor like 500 times, I found myself downloading it and burning it to a cd (with FreeBSD, kde, and all and STILL had to do it from the command line...I don't know how many disks I messed up). 

Here's the latest: My 900Mhz box running ubuntu 6.06, my associate (well the owner of the company) has it on two desktops at home, his laptop and his desktop at the office. [Digital Focus is all ubuntu]("http://digital-focus.us/2007/11/14/new-production-environment/"). We love it, cause it just works!

### Post by digitalfiz on 2008-01-21
Hi! I'm Richards associate :) was searching around ubuntu forum just getting an idea of whats going on and noticed richard was on here posting around. It is true our whole company has gone ubuntu and plans to stay that way. I only have 1 box with windows on it for photoshop cs3 and a few of my games which I hardly ever get to play anymore but I still got the capability :D I've always been a *nix fan and liked the bsd distro the best mainly for the ports system and I became very familiar with it. The last couple of years I've bounced around from different versions of windows including vista(puke) and FreeBSD trying to get something stable enough for me all around. A buddy of mine mentioned the new ubuntu(7.10) had beryl capabilities built into it and it was very stable. Me being a web designer and a person who loves eye candy decided to give it a shot. So I loaded 7.10 up on a spare drive on my box to play with it. Withen a few days I found myself not rebooting back into windows and had pretty much found all my replacement tools in ubuntu and they actually worked! Quanta anyone? That program only half works on FreeBSD and thw only close alternative for it on windows is Zend Studio which I love but Quanta is better ;) Anyways its been a few months since I went completely ubuntu at home and at the office and I am very happy. I've almost got my parents up for switching to it but I'm having to offer full support hehe. They live in florida and I'm in Texas but thats not a problem because I can just ssh in and do updates or whatever :)
