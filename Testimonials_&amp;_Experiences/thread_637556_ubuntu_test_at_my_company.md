### Post by mbubb on 2007-12-11
Hello All,

I have been testing ubuntu at my company for the past 6 months. We need a replacement for Win2k for our 700 users in Europe, Asia and US.

We (IT dept) have been simultaneously testing it in all 3 regions. For my part i have felt comfortable to move over completely for the past month. I have it running pretty well on an HP 6510b dual core Pentium laptop. A few Samba issues that I am working on and vnc weirdness occaisionally but overall fine. And better than Win2k for my purposes.

For me there is every reason to go with Linux in general and ubuntu in prticular - 7.10 feels ready 

I need to submit a report by next week to the CTO on the status of this project. It won't be a hard sell as he has over the years migrated almost all servers over to Linux and has weaned the company mostly off of the MS Office/IE/Outlook use to OO/Mozilla.

Which is one of the reasons I came to work for this company. The downside is that we are a small IT dept and I have observed over the year or so that I have been here that we tend to do things in an unorganized ad hoc way. We have diffuse roles, I am roughly in charge of tech support and do intermediate Sys Admin stuff. I am not high enough up the ladder to really change this and do not have much in the way of managerial skills to do so anyway. But it is in my best interest to have a good overall migration plan as the brunt of this will fall in my lap.

And I would rather be trying to fix Ubuntu issues than Vista ones...

My question is if there are good forums/mail lists to join for this situation. I doubt that I am the only one in this situation but I do not see alot of posts on this. I see people using Ubuntu for small startups but nothing of the size and complexity of what we are looking at. I have been using Linux and BSD for a few years and would say that I have a good general knowledge.

But a migration like this is above my head.

Thanks for any advice 

yrs


Michael
