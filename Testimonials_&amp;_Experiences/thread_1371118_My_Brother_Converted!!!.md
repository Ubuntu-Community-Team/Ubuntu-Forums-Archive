### Post by 1roxtar on 2010-01-03
A few weeks back, I had posted in another thread that I let my brother borrow my netbook with Ubuntu 9.10 (not Remix) as my OS.  He was impressed with how snappy it ran on my machine.  What really got his attention was that his Sprint 3G card only got about a 30% signal in his apartment, while it got 100% signal on my netbook.  

If fact, installing the software on his laptop, which ran Windows XP Media Center Edition, took nearly 20 minutes to install and set up.  My netbook immediately detected his broadband card and I was on the internet in about two minutes.

Tonight he called me completely frustrated and said, "I want you to install what you got on your laptop NOWWWW!!!"  I went over and asked if he wanted to keep his XP and have me do a dual-boot until he got more familiar with Ubuntu.  He said to just do a full install and I obliged.

He was impressed that the install only took about ten minutes. I ran the updates, restricted extras, Java, Ubuntu-Tweak, Google Chrome, Limewire.  I was completely done in about 30 minutes.  I gave him a short tutorial and showed what was where and he took it from there.  

He didn't even mind that the taskbar was on top, unlike Windows which is on the bottom.  I didn't change it because he WANTED it to look different, so his friends could notice it.  Even though it took a little playing with it to familiar himself to it, he found it EASY to navigate through it.

I always get a kick hearing anyone ask about antivirus, firewalls, spybot, etc.  I also love the look on his face when he was feeling relieved that he could just get back to what he did the most, surfing the internet and downloading music.  I know his needs are pretty basic, but I told him I would teach him more about tweaks and customizing as he becomes more comfortable with Ubuntu and Linux.

I'm a happy guy today.

### Post by dearingj on 2010-01-03
I had somethat similar experiences with a couple of my friends, one very computer literate and the other attempting to become so. I installed Ubuntu on my computers, they noticed it, and eventually they both ended up using Ubuntu for day-to-day tasks, keeping Windows for gaming.

@not2sure: I'm a gamer, with 36 games currently attached to my Steam account. I've only tried a few of them on Linux (Portal, Doom 3, and Quake 3 Arena come to mind) and they've worked well under Wine. Portal actually runs faster than under Windows XP on the same computer. Just make sure you run the [latest beta]("http://www.winehq.org/download/deb") of Wine.
I don't see myself buying another copy of Windows again - by the time XP and Vista (I've got multiple computers) become outdated enough to warrant an upgrade, then Wine and/or [ReactOS]("http://www.reactos.org/") will certainly be good enough for my needs.
