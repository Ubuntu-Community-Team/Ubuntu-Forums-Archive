### Post by 5hark on 2007-05-04
I was born and live in Russia, Moscow. Anyone here can go to the metro station located near the KGB office and buy Windows Vista/XP, Autocad, Photoshop CS or any other software for 3-5 US Dollars in small shops called "palatka v metro". Of course this is not only one place where you can buy software, there are a lot of such places here. Police officers buy software in such places too, nobody will put you in jail for this.

Over 5 years I was working in IT and tried all Windows versions, FreeBSD, SUSE, Debian Linux. Finally I have chosen Ubuntu Linux for my home PC, and Open Source software. I am web developer, and I find a lot of great software that I use every day at home and at work. My company use Linux also (Debian and Gentoo). I don't use Zend Studio now, Kdevelop replaced it for me. Actually there is no stolen software anymore at my home box.

Maybe Ubuntu is now not that kind of operating system that I will install for computer of my Mom, but for experienced users it is the best choice. The main problem now I think is auto detection of monitors and video cards. Ubuntu did not recognized my Samsung 215TW monitor with 1680x1050 resolution, and I have to configure X.org by editing /etc/X11/xorg.conf. While doing that I make some mistakes and X.org failed to start, in console session I installed links2 and find a solution in a minute with Google, after restarting gdm I have working operating system. Any Windows users can find solution to OS errors such fast? I don't think so.

My printer HP LaserJet 1200 started to work just after I plug it in, no installation CDs was required.

I tried the latest Windows Vista Enterprise (or how is it called, the most expensive and full version). After it just loaded about 3-5 minutes I saw CPU load at 20-30%! Intel Core Duo 2 6300 with 1 Gb RAM and Seagate Barracuda (10 000 rpm) hard drive. MS wants >300$ for this? No thanks, I will not use this even for 5$, I will better donate more money to Ubuntu developers for their hard work and making this great operating system. Thank you Ubuntu developers and supporters.
