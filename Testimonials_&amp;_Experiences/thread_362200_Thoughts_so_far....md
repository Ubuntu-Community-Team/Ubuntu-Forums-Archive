### Post by wv25m on 2007-02-15
Just started using Ubuntu/Linux last week...  just some thoughts about the switch from XP. 

Background:
Long time windows user, much like everyone else... and I always hated it.  I like Macs and have been using a Mini Mac for a year and a half.  I got a laptop (wanted to get unchained from the desk! and decided to get a Windows based unit because it would have cost 1.5x more for a comparable Mac.  Laptop is great (HP DV9030) but XP sucks.  I put up with it for as long as I could stand and started looking at alternatives and that is what brought me to Unix/Linux.
I am sick of the windows bugs/viruses, big business mentality, high cost, DRM disaster, inability to really customize my system, living with the knowledge that I was using and working with an inferior OS, and I wanted something new... and after seeing how "great" Vista really is... umm yeah, my free "upgrade" disc is going in the trash.  Microsoft and their DRM scheme can kiss my a$$.  

The Switch:
I have dual 100Gig HDD's so I decided on a dual boot system, keeping Windows on my first drive as a fallback while learning Linux.  The install was effortless and grub got everything configured perfectly, hda is Windows, hdb is Kubuntu.  No issues.  I love Kubuntu and am very happy with it thus far.  

Issues:
Wireless card works perfectly from the get-go.  Nvidia setup with no problems.  However my Nvidia settings that are supposedly saved are reset to default everytime I reboot.  My built-in webcam is not setup.  My audio jack is not setup.  Fonts look grainy - in general.  Had Beryl setup but it was buggy; open windows had no borders and there were some stability issues going on, so I wiped it all out and re-installed.  And it doesn't seem like my new Canon MP830 all-in-one printer is supported... that is very disappointing.  If anyone has any suggestions I am all ears.  

Software:

Overall the software is much more customizable and powerful, but not as slick or polished as Mac or Windows apps.  I personally like Windows Media Player/iTunes and was disappointed to find that a nice media player like Amarok does not have video support... it's an absolute shame.  Other apps may be powerful (Mplayer/some of the IM apps etc) but they have zero eye candy factor... they look like they're straight out of 1985!!!  
Amarok would be perfect (for me at least) if it had audio/video capabilities, podcast/video podcast, and a nicer way to display all of my albums with pictures of the albums like WMP or iTunes (it does it, but not very well and not automatically).  I don't want to use 5 different applications to do these things, they are all very closely related and one player should be able to do this.  Open Office is great.  The graphics/photo management programs are great.  

Overall: 

Love Unix/Linux and the open source movement
Love the community and support that is available
Love most of the software
I'm 100% converted from the dark side and will be digging into this a lot deeper... although as an aside, OS X x86 is extremely attractive as well... although the lack of customization on the Macs is a huge negative and their capricious views on DRM is as well. 
Now I just need one of those "designed for Kubuntu" stickers for my machine...
