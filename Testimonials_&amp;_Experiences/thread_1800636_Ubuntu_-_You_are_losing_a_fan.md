### Post by Actuary on 2011-07-09
I've been in the Linux field for over 10 years now, I think I can say I'm an advanced user. I absolutely loved Ubuntu when it came out with a fresh new perspective. I loved the simplicity and ease of getting things done instead of doing every single thing in the terminal. I loved the huge support in forums and simply the whole atmosphere.

I feel that Ubuntu's philosophy of **simplicity** is changing towards **restriction and lack of freedom** with that being more obvious in 11.04. There is a big, big difference between **hiding** advanced features and** removing them!** I'll give you a few examples:


[LIST=1]
[*]**Notifier: **Why can't I change anything about notify-osd without having to re-compile it. A simple thing as time-out, location on screen, etcetera
[*]**Launcher:** I can't change position, I can't change the menus, I can't control / modify the applications menu and I can't even apply changes to application icons and propagate them to the launcher
[*]**Panel:** Why not provide me with a right click menu, give me the freedom to add / remove whatever I want. Integration does not equal restriction
[/LIST]
With all respect and admiration to the hard work and many hours into making this great distribution, I would humbly ask the Ubuntu team to think about some of their users. The reason why many of us left proprietary systems and switched to Linux is the freedom! this is the one thing that makes this whole experience sweet.

I tried adopting the new system, but after a good while of trying, I'm beginning to give up. I hate to move to another distribution, but I feel like I'm forced to because although Ubuntu is taking brave steps towards change, this change doesn't have to be restrictive turning away users like myself.

Perhaps I'm mistaken and the future releases are going to surprise me, but with the way things are headed right now, I don't think my next desktop installation will be Ubuntu.

On the positive side, I LOVE ubuntu-server. It's really fantastic!
