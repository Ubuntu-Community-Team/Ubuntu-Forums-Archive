### Post by alex_ndc on 2008-09-28
I don't think this is a revolution.

When you're talking about open source, as in a pragmatic development methodology, then it works, but it is not good for everything. I mean, open-source products tend to have good and modular implementations, but user interfaces are really user-hating. It is great because of all the knowledge shared, but to give you an example, GIMP will always be behind Photoshop.

I used to believe in Free Software. It is really seductive to be a part of something, to change the world. But then I started thinking about starting my own company, and the notion of Free Software stopped appealing to me, because you need a business model to pay your bills, and frankly selling support just doesn't cut it. Selling consultancy services doesn't cut it either, because to increase revenue you have to hire more developers. 

Free Software doesn't work because of basic economics. Normal people will pay you good money for your software if it solves their problems and don't give a **** about its license as long as it works. Incidentally this is the biggest problem with Linux. The commercial ecosystem isn't really there.
