### Post by milanvora2 on 2008-04-17
I have now Ubuntu running since a week.

First the bad stuff

I downloaded the dvd hoping it would have all versions. For some reasosn there was only an installer for 1 version.
- I installed it a couple of times to try various partition setups. Twice, the installation completed but did not boot and gave a grub error. 
- Under kubuntu (I first triêd out kubuntu), my usb ext drive would'nt auto-mount, and my network printer wouldn't work
- The installation of the ati drivers was a pain. It took a lot of reading and trying till I finally got it to work
- Getting compiz to work was alos not easy
- I would have hope that it would have automatically recognized my logitech webcam (a recent model)
- Removing software doesn't automatically remove the config directories

Now the good stuff

- It is very stable
- Now that i got the ati drivers and compiz running, it's lovely
- Installation, upgrades, adding new software has never been so easy
- All the apps are same if not better than the ones under windows
- Customization of the desktop offers amazing possibilities (however a bit too many options for plain vanilla users)
- I'm very happy with the virtualbox solution and wine in case i have to run windows software and happy also with the .nzb automation

The first week has been intense in terms of trying & configuring. But once it works it's worth the efforts. I know some of the unix commands, so it was a bit easy for me to get adjusted. I then tried to get my wife and daughter to use the system. A year ago, we tried the same experience with opensuse and they were very unhappy and returned to windows. This time, they have tried it for 2 days and they are very impressed.

We don't play much games, so we don't really miss the 3d stuff.

Overall, my wife uses it for (Web, mail, skype, word processing, photo, and tel directory search). All these work well.

I'm specillay pleased with the nzb automation, the compiz stuff and the virus free environment.

I'm still in the trial phase and will experiment the upgrade to the new release, the backup and restore of my hdd and other stuff. But the first week has exceeded the promises.

Thanks to all the people who have worked hard to compile all the help and howto descriptions. Without them, i'd have given up long back.

I'm actively promoting ubuntu everywhere and hope to convince many others to join the bandwagon.

### Post by StooJ on 2008-04-17
ATI drivers are much easier to install in Hardy, and once they were enabled for me Compiz worked straight away. (This has never happened before; I've always had to go down the XGL route).

The fact that you can change absolutely every part of interface with fonts and themes and metacity and compiz and GTK1 and GTK2 and on and on and on... It can be very daunting at first. It certainly was for me.

And, for a lot of users leaving Ubuntu with the default theme is not an option either (although I kinda like it). The first thing people ask me to do is take the brown away. Every bit of it, from the window bars to the splash screen on boot.

Once you get into the habit of installing themes rather than trying to change everything manually, and once you have a (basic) understanding of the difference between GTK and Metacity things really start to come together.
