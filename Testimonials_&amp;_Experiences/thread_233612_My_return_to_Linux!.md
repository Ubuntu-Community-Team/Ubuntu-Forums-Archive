### Post by DavidAHughes on 2006-08-10
Well, I thought this would be a good place to detail my history with Linux and my recent return! Don´t worry, my departure from Linux was not a negative on its part, merely a condition of job I took.

I started using Linux a few years ago when a Graphical Installer was unheard of, i think it might of been Suse 7.2, but then moved to Caldera and back again. Stuck with Suse through until 9.1. For some reason or another the laptop i was using couldn´t support any further versions of Suse, and since 9.1 was running perfectly for my work I thought I might as well stick (why upgrade if you don´t have to or support runs out?)..

Anyways, with the help of Suse 9.1 I managed to get my Degree in Physics. And I took up a doctorate in Biomedical Engineering where a free laptop was given. Unfortunately by taking on this laptop (which had oodles and oodles of power) I had to abide by the institutes wishes and stick with Windows XP. Using a full range of open source software on that platform I manged to satisfy my linux tastes for a while up until a few days ago when i discovered Ubuntu..

I am now running Ubuntu 6.06 off a liveCD with persistent mode using a 1GB USB Key, and I´m happier than that pig I heard of who likes faeces!

I again have access to all the programs I loved, Lyx for writing my papers, Scilab for crunchign my numbers, a multitude of development tools for my hobbies.. Suffice to say I will never leave linux again!

Ubuntu is a breath of fresh air after using Windows for 6 months, I missed the community spirit, the fun in trying to get Firefox to play sounds in Flash documents, the excitement of finding that my scanner works out of the box, the fact that Evolution handles my University exchange server better than Outlook does (I don´t know why this is the case but I don´t care!) and the fact that finally my PocketPC can sync with Evolution with ease..

I have noticed a magnificent jump in usability of the platform in the gap I´ve had, I finally beleive that linux isn´t just ready for the desktop, it  is needed for the desktop.
