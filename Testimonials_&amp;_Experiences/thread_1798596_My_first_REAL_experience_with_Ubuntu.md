### Post by derekeverett on 2011-07-07
Mostly I've found Ubuntu is a pain in the butt and a major source of frustration.. but it's fun as hell too to learn new stuff and figure out how to make things work for ya.

There is no question that it has it's place in the world.. and a valuable place too. But a replacement for everything it's just not. I'm an advocate of dual booting if you can afford to do so.

All that being said.. to me it seems to get a little better every release. Things get easier and more stuff works properly and more betterer. Yes, betterer is a word now.

I have to agree though that hardware support is the one single most frustrating thing. And many folks blame Ubuntu for it when they should be pointing fingers at hardware vendors and making sure they spend their money only on Linux compatible components. That's what really makes Ubuntu frustrating.

Amazing that it as come this far really. A lot of people have put a lot of work into this. And I'm thankful.. I've resurrected a couple "dead" computers now and given them to folks who couldn't otherwise afford one and now they are online. That alone is worth a lot of praise to everybody that contributes to this project. So thanks!
