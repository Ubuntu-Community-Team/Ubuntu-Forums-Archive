### Post by nautilus on 2008-07-03
Hey all.  I'm really sorry to have to do this, but I really need to post this little rant.  I want to put this out there as some input from me to the developers of Ubuntu as feedback and views from one long-time and faithful Ubuntu user.

It isn't really constructive, but I hope that my opinion is taken into account for some future considerations, even a little bit.

I'm wiping my partition of Ubuntu once this is posted, as well as reinstalling new distributions on all of my servers and workstations both hobby and production at my company in preference for a yet undecided distribution.

My experience has taken a gradual decline with each new release, and this last upgrade was such a mess and screwed my system up so fiercely that I've decided I've simply had enough.

Even my simple patches to fix ifup have sat unapplied in launchpad for nearly a year without any indication that they would ever be applied, forcing me to manually rebuild each upgrade of it with my patch applied to have sane IPv6 on my network.

I realize Ubuntu is a majority-rules distro, and that the needs of many outweigh the needs of one developer, but this will cost at least my own contributions.  I won't be packaging my web server, television broadcast channel, web applications, libraries, point of sale, or work order management software in .deb format anymore, and if this trend continues I'm sure I won't be the only one.

[http://symphonyofblades.net/~doneill/ubunturant.txt](http://symphonyofblades.net/~doneill/ubunturant.txt)
