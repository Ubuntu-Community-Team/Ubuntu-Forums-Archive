### Post by harisund on 2006-08-01
After having been a long time Ubuntu user, participating in the forums, IRC .. advocating .. networking yadda yadda.. I thought maybe Ubuntu has become too easy and I should try something else on my spare machine at home 

So I went and installed Fedora, and so far here's my experience. 

1. Install using 6 CDs. General pain. 
2. After installing, tried yum update. aptitude update is miles ahead. This yum seriously sucks. First it takes so long to figure out what it needs to do. Then initially it had some gpg keys screwed up. Ask on the forums and after a day (sooooo slow) they say do yum clean all. Ok did that and did an update. Took a day nearly.
3. Wanted to install GNU screen. yum search screen and after waiting for while, it gives me a huge list that I can't even read, and scrolls down super fast. WTF?
4. pirut is nowhere as near convenient as synaptic and makes you think the package repositories are very small. 
5. xfce version is 4.2? hahahahah.. dapper has 4.4 beta. Neat.
6. smbfs support is deprecated for cifs. cifs sucks real bad. can't hvae different uids. When is Ubuntu switching to cifs? 
7. Use pirut to get rid of some unwanted software (for lack of space) and immediate complains of software dependancy. RPM hell? AAaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa ... 
..
..
..
And plenty more gripes. 

How do people even use Fedora? 

Seriously. I feel soo much comfortable at Ubuntu. Whoever said Fedora Core tries to get the users to stick to the GUI and not learn the command line is absolutely correct. Ubuntu may be a n00b friendly distro, but that does't mean you can't customize it to the extent of Gentoo, if I might. 

I wanted to learn to system admin the fedora way. After all, Fedora is being used in plenty of places, including universities,and RedHat too is an industry standard. And a lot of software that you see online has nice .rpm packages for Fedora (but recently, .deb support is increasing too. But then again, as we all know Ubuntu doesn't play nicely with Debian and packages compiled for Debian will not necessarily work in Ubuntu). But the more I try and use Fedora, the more I want to get back to Ubuntu. 

Ok, coming up next is perhaps the second on the Distrowatch list. OpenSuse.

My only complaint is that the distros are all so different and that the fact that you might be very good with the command line of one distro means nothing in general. I am talking about simple stuff like the /etc/network/interfaces file, which doesn't even exist in Fedora! It is in /etc/sysconfig/ifcfg-eth0 or something like that. And location of commands, sometimes in /usr/local/bin, /bin/, /sbin and other such trivial inconsistencies. And of course, the systemV init scripts. Atleast /etc/init.d/service {stop|start} works, thank God !

Why is that? Why can't we all be consistent and move along nicely?
