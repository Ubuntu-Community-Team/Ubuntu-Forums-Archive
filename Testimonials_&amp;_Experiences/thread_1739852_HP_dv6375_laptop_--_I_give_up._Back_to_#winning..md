### Post by dookiebowser on 2011-04-26
Ubuntu 10.10

Had 4 problems that I would consider crippling:

Cannot use ethernet port due to some EEPROM problem. The drivers fail on probe, so I have no ethernet. This is a well documented but obscure issue that was never officially resolved. I even tried editing the driver source and commenting out the part where it checks for this specific error that prevents the probe from succeeding and recompiling.
[https://bugzilla.redhat.com/show_bug.cgi?id=459202](https://bugzilla.redhat.com/show_bug.cgi?id=459202)

Wifi is extremely slow. I pull about 80-150KB/s on any given site or download. On win7 it's pulling as much as my Windows desktop hardwired 1.6MB/sec.

Microphone doesn't work. Presumably something to do with ALSA options. There's a forum post on the ubuntu forums with a ragtag database of options, and mine isn't listed. I'm not going to try the 50 combinations of HP laptop options to get my mic working. 

Ubuntu 10.10 fails to estimate battery time remaining. 
[http://askubuntu.com/questions/7205/battery-indicator-is-stuck-on-estimating?tab=active](http://askubuntu.com/questions/7205/battery-indicator-is-stuck-on-estimating?tab=active)


Granted these problems are mostly out of the realm of Ubuntu specifically (except maybe battery time), but it's a hard reminder that linux is still at the bottom in ease of use and therefore mass appeal. Rather, it's the best for professionals that benefit from using it, and computer hobbyists.

I'm not a programmer, I'm not a linux expert. I do although have pretty good troubleshooting skills, patience, logic, and experience with command lines. That should be enough to get hardware up and running, in my experience with this laptop it is not.

I just wanted to get edubuntu up and running and capable of what I needed it to do before traveling. Since I'm going to be on an airplane, I want to know how much battery time I have remaining. I'll be away from home and wanting to use Skype to speak to/see my wife. Having the ethernet port and not only relying on the wifi is a nobrainer. 

Don't remember having these problems in earlier versions of Ubuntu. I do remember a problem with PulseAudio and Skype where I had to use a special OSS version of Skype. I do remember the ethernet issue that I was able to fix somehow and can't now.

Seems like it's [Ubuntu] getting worse with new releases and adding frivolous things AKA bloat, rather than focusing on and maintaining the things that made it popular in the first place, namely ease of use.

Just my 2c rant. Don't need explanations. It is what it is.
