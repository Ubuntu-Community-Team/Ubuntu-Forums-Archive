### Post by jdrago_999 on 2008-05-28
Well it's been a month and I have to say I am very impressed.

I've been using Linux as a server environment for 10 years, but have never successfully made the leap to Linux on the Desktop.

I'm running an HP Pavilion dv9000 laptop (webcam, 802.11g, microphone) and once I got past installing the prerequisite wireless drivers, everything has been very easy.

The package manager works great.  It is really easy to search for and install new software.  Pidgin IM and Skype *just work* and I haven't had any problems with the beta version of Firefox that came installed.

Ripping and burning music with Rhythmbox Music Player is also straightforward and simple.  Even Google Earth installed easily and runs just fine.

It took no time to get Apache2 and mod_perl2 set up for development and testing locally.

For those few times I need to run Windows (i.e. someone sends me a *.docx or *.xlsx file) I have WinXP running under Ubuntu via innotek VirtualBox.  Somehow it seems that WinXP runs faster under Ubuntu than it does on its own.

In a few weeks I look forward to upgrading my wife's laptop from WinXP to Ubuntu also.

"THANK YOU" to everyone who contributed to make Ubuntu Hardy Heron as good as it is.

Best Regards,
A very happy user.
