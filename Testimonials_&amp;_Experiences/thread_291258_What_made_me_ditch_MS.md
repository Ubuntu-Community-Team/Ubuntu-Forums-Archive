### Post by gerowen on 2006-11-06
Yeah I ran Fedora Core 5 for a long time, but switched to Ubuntu for a couple of reasons.

I uninstalled my nvidia driver, ntfs module and a few other things in preparation for a kernel upgrade I saw in yum(FC package manger like apt-get).  Too bad I didn't see that they had just released FC6 and all of the repo's would be down and I wouldn't be able to re-install any of my stuff.  I "was" going to download FC6, but even though I've got 1MB/s up and 1MB/s down available bandwidth, I thought to myself, "I do NOT feel like downloading 3.9 GB of operating system", so I downloaded Ubuntu, one of the few 1 CD distros, I had it in about 5 minutes.  I chose Ubuntu because it was only 1 CD, because it had good reviews on linuxquestions.org, and it had an official forum site which appeared to have quite a bit of traffic, which meant lots of help for the new convert, :p.  I chose to keep Ubuntu because even though I like and respect the Linux command line and all its power, having a user friendly, time saving method of updating software and getting support is a really useful tool, and while there's no question that Fedora Core and Yum were useful, there was just too much to it.  5 CDs is just too much for a single operating system.  Ubuntu is quite small compared to a lot of other distros, and very user friendly, and has a great community.  I attribute part of Ubuntu's success to its small size, because like me, sometimes people just don't feel like waiting for 4 GB to download, regardless of your connection speed.
