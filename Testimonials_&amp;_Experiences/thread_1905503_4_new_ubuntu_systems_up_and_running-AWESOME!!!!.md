### Post by goodbye-windows(tm) on 2012-01-07
Hi All,

I continue to be amazed with the performance of ubuntu. After years of following the linux progress, I decided to take the plunge in June of 2010. 

I started with xubuntu on a non-linux compatible Dell laptop. I managed to make the thing run despite a black screen video problem that is intermittent.

My second conversion was from an XP desktop with a P4 processor. I built this system from scratch back in 2006. Ubuntu ran so well that I bought a new hard drive and gave windows xp the boot. I haven't used XP in months...AND I DON'T MISS IT AT ALL.
 
Next, I brought my daughters new Dell 15R (i5 processor) to life in ubuntu. When she saw how well the thing ran, she asked me to remove windows 7 permanently...which I was only too happy to do>>: The Dell 15R runs like a champ, my daughter is happy and now prefers ubuntu/unity over her Mac system.

Grandma bought a new Dell 620 in order to run ubuntu/unity. It took me 4 days to fine tune and customise it and to train her in ubuntu. She started working in computers in the days of the Eniac and she's a tough customer. But, she seems to have taken to ubuntu like a fish takes to water. The windows 7 partition has never been used, and she plans to use windows 7 only in an emergency.

Granddad might be interested in a ubuntu system too, and if so, he is likely to want ubuntu only (no windows). Time will tell.....

Both the Dell 15R and the Dell 620 systems are certified for ubuntu compatibility, and both systems ran from the beginning with very few bugs.

I hope ubuntu continues to develop and grow and look forward to the next version of ubuntu.

Regards,

Art
