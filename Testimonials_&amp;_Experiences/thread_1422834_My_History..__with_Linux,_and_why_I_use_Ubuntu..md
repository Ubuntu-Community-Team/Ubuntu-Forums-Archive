### Post by GeeLo on 2010-03-05
Hello.. Here is my story, I have traveled a pretty awesome "IT" road so far, but it's not the destination, it the journey right? ;)

I started using Linux way back when RedHat was at version 5.2 Apollo... I've tried different versions throughout the years. What I liked about Linux was not just the community that still surrounds the O.S., but how always since day one Linux could run on older legacy workstations and servers. 

I started out as a hardware technician, building and fixing workstations part time by myself for a few years, learning more about software and networking as well. After a few years, I was able to get a full time job working for a major retail computer company as a hardware / software technician. Then I was able to land a job with a very large company, and what attracted them to my skills is not just that I had experience with commercial software, but also that I used Linux as a operating system. Honestly, that was the "main" reason I got the job. 

I use Ubuntu (started with ver 4.10) and recommend Ubuntu to everyone, because of all of "YOU" out there supporting it, as well as it is the easiest linux distribution out there to install and use. Ubuntu was also the first linux distribution that worked with the WiFi cards that I had (and still have). And my company "uses" ubuntu now, how awesome is that? Very Awesome! 

Currently, I am a Network Engineer, on a very large Wide Area Network. Even with my all my experience, I do not know "everything" (one of the joys of IT) but I do help people on internet forums and IRC, with Linux and more, giving back what was given to me.
