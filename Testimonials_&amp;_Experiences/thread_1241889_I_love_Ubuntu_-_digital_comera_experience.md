### Post by Cowchip7 on 2009-08-16
I have been 100% Ubuntu for the past 4 months. I previously dabbled with versions starting with Dapper Drake but never made the complete switch. As you can see from my signature, I bought a Dell Mini with lpia pre-installed... I am loving it.

I just tried hooking up my Canon Powershot SD600 camera to my netbook via usb. My first attempt was unsuccessful, as Ubuntu failed to mount the camera. Until then, I had not run into this problem. So far Ubuntu has been able to mount everything I stuck into the USB port.

So, I did 15 minutes of internet research and installed gphoto2. Worked beautifully; however, it was command based and a little cumbersome. While skimming the man pages, I found a reference to gtkam. Gtkam is a basic gui version of gphoto2. Voila! With only 20 minutes of research, I was able to access my camera. Most importantly, I did not have to install Cannon's junk! :):):)

Thanks Ubuntu... and the Linux community!
