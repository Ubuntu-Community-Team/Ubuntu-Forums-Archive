### Post by hatstand on 2008-11-11
Well, I have had my ups and downs with Ubuntu, but I love it.

I was an XP user, who needed to buy word etc. I downloaded openoffice, and my free software adventure started. I started with Mandriva in 2004, then Mepis, then Ubuntu. Ubuntu was the first Gnome distro. I resisted, and stuck with Kubuntu for a while. Now I have used Ubuntu and Gnome for 3 years. I have to use XP for work, but all my home computer use for work and for personal stuff, is done on Ubuntu.

I am not going to offer reasons, though in the last 4 years I believe I have really seen Linux become truly user (GUI) freindly. My reaction is purely emotional.

I just love "stickin it to the man". I love being one of the brave adventurers beyond the norm. I love being able to be a functional computer worker without buying in to corporate shite. I feel a smug superiority when people complain about upgrades to vista. I feel part of a community. I feel like "one of the few".

It's great. It works. It has problems (sound cards, USB devices etc), but to get the wholly wonderful feeling about computing that I have, it's worth it. I love showing it off. I love showing the MAc theme, or disguising it as windows and saying "guess what - it's all free!". I love it when it just works. I get frustrated when people won't even try it.

I feel cool.

Why pay massive amounts, when there really is a good alternative out there? OK, it's not for some people. But the way it is developing, these some people are becoming fewer and fewer.

I'll go now before I get teary eyed.
