### Post by bbqsandwich on 2008-01-15
I dual-boot Ubuntu and Vista. I have a [Flip video camera]("http://www.theflip.com/products_flip_video.shtml") that I love to use for its simplicity and portability (fits in the pocket nicely).

I tried to connect it to my laptop on Vista to get the videos off it and Vista did not recognize it. Camera maker's customer support said install a Microsoft hotfix that is supposed to correct errors in recognizing external devices -- I had already installed it for a hard drive that Vista also didn't recognize. The hotfix made the hard drive work, but not the camera. This is a $120 camera and my OS doesn't even recognize it?

Guess what the camera maker's next suggestion was? Ship the camera back to them. So they can "fix" it. Ship it at **my** expense. The problem is not with the camera, guys, and you should know that because it's **your camera**!

You can probably see where this is going. I didn't ship it back. I switched to Ubuntu, which, of course, recognized the camera right off the bat. I didn't have the codec, but it automatically popped up those helpful boxes prompting me to click "OK" to find the codec and install it. Two minutes later and I'm watching my video.

So screw you, Vista. Shiny windows aren't fun anymore when I can't play my home videos.
