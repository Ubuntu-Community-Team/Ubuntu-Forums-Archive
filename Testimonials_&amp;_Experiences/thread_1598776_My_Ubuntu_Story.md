### Post by Artemis Fowl on 2010-10-17
I first heard of linux from a game I used to play called ROBLOX. Today, I absolutely hate that game, but it's really what got me on the internet. One linux user had made something called "Roblox4Linux", which would allow roblox to run on Linux. One user of the program, linuxkde, told me about linux. I did some research on ubuntu, and decided it looked cool. Linuxkde suggested a virtual box, which I never did for whatever reason, and dual booted using wubi.

I used 9.10 Desktop Remix and the first thing I did was try and fail to get the desktop cube working. After a bit of messing around, I decided I liked ubuntu. I started always booting the ubuntu partition. I tried creating many, many livecds and I cannot tell you how many CD's I destroyed with my laptop's weird burner. I accidentally uninstalled ubuntu when trying to put wubi on a cd(don't ask), and by then 10.04 had been released. My mom had apparently been telling the IT guys where she works what she had been doing, as she came home one day with a message from an IT guy about how he thought Ubuntu was much better than Windows and what I was doing was great. He also gave advice with using the operating system in general.

At this point, to developments occured that led me to believe I was destined to use ubuntu; 1. My system32 folder was corrupted, I think because of a batch file antvirus I had been making and 2. I finally succeeded with a ubuntu livecd. I immediately installed 10.04 and the only things I lost from Vista are some pictures of my and other people's cats and games. 

That, is my ubuntu story. What's yours?:KS

### Post by plurworldinc on 2010-10-18
I remember my first time!!!  I was with my daughter in NYC and on the way home we decided to buy something to read on the train ride home. So she got some teen magazine and I picked up my first copy of Linux Format. Now my father in law has been using Linux for years , but I never really asked him about it or had any idea what Linux was. I know it was an operating system that you could use to fix a broken computer with ( Knoppix).

 When I got home I discovered my Windows laptop was running really, really, slow and was infested. I was pretty sick of my laptop not working right so I thought I would dual boot my system so I could learn something new. I have no idea what I did, but the dual boot ended horribly. 

 By this point I had nothing to lose so I dove into Ubuntu 8.04 and never looked back. When I first started I wasn't happy with Linux because nothing as I know it worked right, I could listen to my music or watch my DVDs or anything. I couldn't even install all of my Windows software I wanted to use. I was a very sad noob! But after freaking out for a few minutes and taking the time to learn the OS I was using , I was able to learn and figure out what I was doing well beyond what I thought I know about Windows. 

 With Linux you really can learn how to use a computer and all the ends and outs of it. I still have a copy of Windows laying around somewhere on my desk and my wife is a Windows7 user because she needs it for work. But for me Linux has been a wonderful breath of fresh air.
