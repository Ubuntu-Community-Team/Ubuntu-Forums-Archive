### Post by thommango on 2007-05-10
I was really looking forward to making the switch from XP.  I have four computers in my house and I thought I would make a clean break from XP on all of them.  So far I'm 1 for 3  - and even the one that now has Ubuntu is basically limping along without it's wireless card.  I've tried low end machines, high end machines, I've tried Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu, Dapper, Feisty, alternate installs,  I've messed with configuration files, bios settings and read countless advice postings on this issue and that issue.  

Overall, I think it's a cool operating system with definite potential.  I think if you're doing some very vanilla computing on a very vanilla computer, it's worth a shot.  But, in my experience, if there's anything remotely non-standard about your computer, expect troubles. 

I look forward to future releases, but I won't bother trying again, unless I believe that there is better driver support than there is today.  

I found this community very supportive and friendly, and I will miss it more than Ubuntu itself.

Cheers
