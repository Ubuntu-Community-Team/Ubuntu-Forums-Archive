### Post by webcoded612 on 2008-04-30
Today is a beginning for me.  For a couple of years now I've tried intermittently to install Ubuntu in lieu of Windows.  I love the concept and ideals that Linux embraces, I love the open-source programs available for Linux, and I think the community is awesome in the way it comes together to solve problems and help new users with tough questions.

But I've never been able to shake off Windows.  Even now I'm somewhat tenuously tethered to it, but I can live with that.  Thanks to a wonderful program called Wubi, I'm now able to *use* Linux after I install it, instead of *fix* it.  

I've never had an experience with Linux that didn't involve several hours (usually several days) of troubleshooting, swearing, and frustration.  In that sense I've never really used Linux - as I said above - and so when Wubi installed it perfectly the first time (with absolutely NO problems from download to bootup), I find myself at the helm of a strange ship; a Linux distro that *actually works!*  And I have to tell you, friends, that I'm liking it very much.  Right off the bat, my video card worked (something Ubuntu has never gotten right, even with the latest release), streaming video, audio, mp3's, mpegs...all of it.  It is identical to the experience one would have installing Windows:  you click a button or two, and voila!  You're good to go.

The difference, of course, is that Linux is far superior to Windows.  So much so that I'm not sure they should even be compared anymore.  

Anyway, I wanted to say thanks to those who have stuck with it, fixing problem after problem and bug after bug.  And a very special thanks to those wonderful people who created Wubi; without you, I wouldn't be here, running the latest Ubuntu, writing this testimonial.  

Thanks, guys!
