### Post by trivialpackets on 2011-10-28
Starting off.  I really like Ubuntu 11.10.  I find the flashy Unity interface very cool.  Likewise, I am really impressed with the flashy Gnome-shell interface.  It's glitz, it's beautiful and the boot time for me on my system if phenomenal.  Bravo to the Ubuntu team as far as I'm concerned.  I think that Unity has a little ways to go yet, but it's progressing nicely.

With that said, I am really a fan of the simplicity and lightness of Lubuntu as a desktop environment.  I'm not sure how it will perform as a fresh install, or the sole install with regards to wireless drivers, but I am really liking it overall.  I connect a lot using NX remotely to my computer, and even over NX, things are just snappier on Lubuntu. At this point, I have only done an apt-get install lubuntu-desktop and have run it over NX, but I am liking it.  I may try the install fully so that I can see the full experience of installing it.  I had previously only used the environment on an ancient PIII laptop that I used only to NX to my main ubuntu box, but who knows, I am kind of liking what I'm seeing.  Great job Lubuntu devs.

### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-10-28
I have Lubuntu 11.04 running as the only OS on my oldish laptop with a celeron cpu and 512 Mb ram, and on my netbook with atom 270 cpu and 1GB ram, and it is brilliant on both.  It took a bit of searching to figure out several configuration methods for such as turning of tap-to-click on their touchpads, and how to get the suspend/hibernate to work properly by replacing gnome-power-manager with xfce4-power-manager.

I am not like you with regard to unity, which I detest, so am looking at probably xubuntu, or now it might even be Lubuntu, on my main desktop machine.  I find the DE does all I want without getting in the way of working, and with the ubuntu repos available, and the ability to install just about anything I want, just like all the other ubuntu family of OSs, I can not see what there is to stop those who can not manage unity from sticking with the ubuntu family of distros.

Many users have become so proprietorial about the gnome version of ubuntu that they think Canonical has lost its way.  Whilst I shall probably not be using gnome or unity once 10.04 has gone, I still believe that the *ubuntu OSs are the best for me for the forseeable future at least.
