### Post by MARP1961 on 2011-05-16
I recently installed Natty Narwhal and have been getting used to Unity (2D version). Everything seems to work fine except that I just don't like the appearance of the desktop. If I could customize the panel successfully by changing icons,colours and position of side panel etc. I would be happy. The top panel seems useless, nothing can be added or removed. I realise these issues might be addressed in future releases but I don't want to put up with this desktop for even six months. Gnome 3 seems to have similar problems and needs 3D support so I started looking around. The release candidate for Linux Mint 11 was out so I downloaded it and tried it on Virtual Box. It looks fantastic and some claim it will be the best Gnome 2 experience around. I have previously stayed away from Mint because of their founder's political views and a post he made and later retracted regarding Israel. However, I have now decided I will try Mint in the knowledge that his views do not represent Mint's position. I will keep an eye open for Ubuntu's future direction and will try it again when my 7 year old desktop looks good running it.
