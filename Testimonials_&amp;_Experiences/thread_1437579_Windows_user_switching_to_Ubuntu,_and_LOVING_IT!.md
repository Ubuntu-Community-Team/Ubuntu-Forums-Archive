### Post by opethfan89 on 2010-03-24
Hi all,

Reading through the testimonials of other users has inspired me to write my own, and I'm sure there are many users who are (or were) in my current position.

My first computer was a mac, wayyyyy back in 93/94, when a 300mhz with 30mb of ram cost upwards of $2000 (yes, my dad was crazy). After that computer, I've used windows in some way, shape, or form for the majority of my life (I was born in 89, so suffice it to say I was VERY young when we used that mac), and recently decided to switch to Ubuntu.

I have dabbled with ubuntu in the past, in fact I still have some 6.10 discs lying around, but I had abandoned that because I still wasn't as big of a power user at that time to really get into things. I've been tinkering on/off with Linux for about the past year, first with Kubuntu (love the look of KDE but it wasn't right for me), then Fedora 11, which worked flawlessly, and Feodra 12 had far too many bugs, so I abandoned that in favor of Ubuntu, so at least I would be comfortable with the already-familiar gnome interface, but have the popular support that Ubuntu has.

I have to say, I spent about 2 weeks customizing everything to my liking. I recently (November of 09) bought an HP Pavillion dv7 laptop, installed with an incredibly BLOATED Windows 7 install (I mean, 800mb for "AOL homepage protection, REALLY!?"), and after only 1 week of owning this laptop, I decided to dual boot with Ubuntu 9.04. There were a number of quirks to work out before I was satisfied with my install on this laptop, the primary complaint being the sound issue (which, I still get a loud BEEP on shutdown), but everything else (display, wireless, touchpad keys, webcam, even the built-in remote control) works flawlessly.

As of late, I decided to finally make the switch over to Ubuntu, full time. I still have a small 50GB partition of Windows 7 (which, even with a vanilla install, takes up almost 30 out of 50GB) just to satisfy my time-to-time gaming needs, but I am using Ubuntu for good now. This morning (maybe 2 hours before posting this) I finally got the Pandora ONE Desktop app working, and that just solidified the fact that this is my OS to keep. I have been working for a few days and I was able to customize Cairo-dock to my liking, sticking with a Mac OS X-like theme, and adding my own launchers with custom images (I really love the guitar one for Audacity, although I use that image for my TuxGuitar launcher instead). I even took the time to post an album on my facebook page, just showing off my compiz-fusion effects, and all the eye candy that this laptop is capable of.

I think that about wraps up my testimonial. I love Linux, and I have a few friends who I'm converting over (I've already wow'd them with the compiz-effects so its hook, line, and sinker now ;) Until I discover a viable solution for gaming on my linux install (Yes, I've used PlayonLinux but its not working for me), I will still be forced to keep a windows install on any computer I use, or at least run it inside a virutal machine. However, I am very satisfied with Ubuntu, and I really want to thank every single user of this forum who spends their personal time answering the repepative questions of noobies like myself. Never have I seen such a patient and helpful forum community in my many years of computing, and you guys have truly demonstrated the Ubuntu philosophy.

Just 2 side notes:
1) Is there a sub-forum dedicated to posting screenshots of our desktops, just to show off a little bit? I love the widgets I have, and would love to show off the bling bling a little bit :)

2) Anyone tried out 10.04? I'd have to say I do like the dark purple theme, but beyond a few updates in programs and whatnot, I don't see the big deal behind it. I know its an LTS release, and there might be some great fixes behind-the-scenes, but I don't think a purple theme is enough to convince me to switch. And its interesting to see Linux incorporating some OS X-like features, in the switching of the maximize/minimize/close buttons to the left side.  This should be interesting to see!!

Thanks for reading through this novel, and thanks again for being such a great community.

Yours truly,
Opethfan89
