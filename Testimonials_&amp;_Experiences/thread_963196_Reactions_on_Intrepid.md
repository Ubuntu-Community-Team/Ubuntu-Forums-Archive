### Post by CloudFX on 2008-10-30
I've been running Ubuntu 8.10 Intrepid Ibex since the release of Alpha 1, and it has progressed quite a bit in that time.  There are some very notable software upgrades, even more bug fixes, and all in all, a more stable, secure, and purely awesome system.

Some notable updates to packages in Main include Pidgin 2.5.2 (much better MSN support!), Network Manager 0.7, and on the Kubuntu side of things, KDE4, which was available in Hardy, but not the default.

So, unless you require the stability of an LTS, I strongly recommend upgrading!  You may not notice it, but the amount of bug fixes are HUGE, and you'll just be able to do so much more.

### Post by CloudFX on 2008-10-31
> **alwez_loner said:**
> Which one would you recommend to install the Alpha version on the Final version? 

Is it better to update or run a fresh installation?

Install the Final version; you will not have to do so many updates, and there's a much less risk of something going wrong in installation.  As for using system updates or a fresh install, it's really up to you.  There's a risk of something going wrong when upgrading via Update Manager, however it will be much easier on your part.  You won't need to redo all of your settings and install all of your applications.  However in both cases, making a backup of your important files is essential.
