### Post by Serpher on 2010-04-15
I've been using Ubuntu since 8.04, as my first Linux distribution. Since then I've gone to college for IT and learned a lot about other distros and Linux in general. I still prefer Ubuntu for my home computing, and I've recently installed the 10.04 beta. Ubuntu has always looked ugly, like every default GNOME theme, however this new beta just wants me want to cry. I've customized everything to my liking and moved the buttons back into their old spot, but I'm really kinda now scared to show people what Ubuntu is like through a live CD, and also why the hell is Ubuntu getting like this anyway?

I'm talking about a lot of things, and I'm sure a lot of people feel the same way:
1) Buttons on the left hand side: Yes, you're trying to be different to appease the hipster OSX douchebags. Thing is everybody is used to having it on the right, and we have been used to that our whole lives assuming you haven't ran OSX for a sizable portion of your life. Every-time I want to close an application, and I'm not in a position to easily use a hot-key, my reflex, along with almost all of you I'm sure, is to go to the top right hand corner, and click that button closest to the edge of your screen. Now it's on the left side and I'm sure the majority are finding it fairly annoying.

2) Purple: My first look at the GNOME theme was it seemed a little bit too flamboyant, but I didn't mind it to much. The black bars looked kinda nice and the purple matched it fairly well. However I opened a terminal window and I couldn't believe my eyes. I have some stupid purple box staring back at me. I couldn't even do anything with it and I just quickly downloaded a new GTK theme, wallpaper, and changed the profile on my terminal window, as well as modifying metacity to un-hipsterdouchebag my buttons. To make things worse this is a LTS release? I don't want my personal favorite distro for home/desktop use to be known as the flamboyant queen of operating systems.

3) Boot ain't so fast after modifying the GUI: The first time I booted up 10.04, I was amazing at the speed of the whole boot process. If this isn't the cause, I still want to say I feel the same way, but after changing my metacity data and my GTK theme, I got to my login screen just as fast, but when I logged in, it hangs there for enough time for the whole process to be just as fast as my 9.10 instillation on the same machine. I don't want to have to compromise between looking like a flamboyant queen, or a fast boot.


So please Ubuntu, please stop trying to appeal to look cool, cause honestly, it feels like Ubuntu is some 60 year old trying to feel like a kid again, it was just better when you dressed like grandpa.
