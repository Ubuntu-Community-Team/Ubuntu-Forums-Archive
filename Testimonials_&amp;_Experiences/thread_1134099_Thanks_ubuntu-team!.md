### Post by apostate on 2009-04-23
Indeed.

I am going to honest here. I was getting worried. I honestly felt that Hardy was a dog. I mean it. It sucked for me quite a bit. Lots and lots of bugs, glitches and foibles...it just didn't seem as fully baked as Gutsy. When Intrepid came out, it added a few nice new perks. But in some ways, it seemed like an even bigger step backwards stability wise. Just weird stuff. Java apps ran like crap on my box, sound would randomly stop working, the digital camera import fiasco....I was starting to look at OpenSUSE with great interest.

So, just because the performance on my Intrepid machine was soooo bad, I upgraded a few days early to Jaunty.

Wow. This is the pleasant computing experience I remember. It just works. It is polished, professional looking, fast, stable on on my rig, even the RC was almost trouble-free. I had the issue where the updated killed the network during the Flash upgrade, but I found the answer on Google and update Flash by hand. No problem. Firefox runs better, Java runs better, the new notification infrastructure looks way better...all in all this is one was a winner. If you haven't upgraded yet, I'd say don't walk, run to grab Jaunty.
