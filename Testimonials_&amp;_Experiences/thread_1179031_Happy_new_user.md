### Post by MoleStrangler on 2009-06-05
Well I was deciding between a Mac Mini or building a Ubuntu box for a general machine at work.  To do some admin work on also some seti number crunching.

So I get an ok CPU with the AMD Phenom X4 9550 at 2.2GHz, ok I thought.  An ok Video card in the Nvidia 9500GT and a couple Western Digital green HDs not so fast but low energy and minimum heat., ah also a single 2gb memory stick.  All with a low power 400W power supply.

Just for a general machine for doing 'stuff'.

After just over a week, and finally getting desktop visual effects working, a real pain!  I got another 2gb memory stick cuz I loaded Virutal Box 7 Windows 7, I found myself thinking about the 3GH AMD Phenom X4 and a faster video Nvidia video card.

I'm very impressed with Ubuntu!

1. Virtual Binx runs Windows 7 kinda ok, hence looking for faster CPU.
2. I can SSHFS bcak to my MacPro at home just nicely.
3. The dekstop GUI is plesant, but I still prefer OS X, I still don't like app menus in the 4. window, I still prefer a menu bar at the top of the screen.
5. Adding apps is easy through the GUI, eventhough I have 10+ years with bash.
6. My two optical drives from my old PowerMac G4 work just fine, no issues.  All the hardware i've thrown at it seems to be recognised and works.

Anwyay its turned into more than just an admin and general machine under my desk.

My only complaint is not Ubuntu but PC hardware, it makes too much noise to keep cool. My two year old MacPro at home is wisper quiet, even filly loade with RAM and HDs.
