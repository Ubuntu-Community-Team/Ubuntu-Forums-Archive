### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2006-10-28
Alright I will begin this post with my experinces on the computer so far.
I started out on a no name brand compy back in 2000, did not know the first thing about computers or anything of that sort outside of the little bits I learned in school.
My first OS was windows 95, gawd it was horrid, with 95 about to die it wasnt long before we got our first name brand computer.
We got a hewlett packard with XP home edition but sadly that was a bad year for HP, we had two faulty machines in a row from them.
Then came what I call "the miracle machine", my good ol Emachine.
Now normally Emachine is not the best company to buy from but I tell you that Emachine was the best computer I have had so far despite the issues I had with it.
It came bundled with XP, a DVD player and a CD recorder, not much mememory or hard drive space but she really packed a puch.
She was a good computer, but XP was a lot less cooperative.
Our first issue was getting XP service pack 1, service pack 1 was a nightmare as it corrupted our system.
We had to re install XP, then re install service pack 1.
The second try with service pack 1 was better then the first but it removed our DVD drive and we could not get it back.
We spent about a year under XP service pack 1 and that was enough to get us irritated, XP really turned us off from even using a computer as it was constantly under attack.
We got antivirus, anti adware, anti spyware then we got firefox...
Firefox was a godsend on XP, even when it was still firebird it was a major deliverence from all the problems we had on IE.
Then came service pack 2, service pack 2 was more merciful on us then service pack 1 but it was still an unsecure mess.
It was around that time I started to experiment with linux.
My first try was with Yoper.... it really sucked.
Went back to XP, then came on to Suse linux.
Suse was a far better experience for me then Yoper but still very hard to use.
I still primarily used XP but then came the linux distro that got me into linux full time... Mepis linux 3.3
Mepis was wonderful to me, incredibly easy to install and use... got my media, my MP3's... EVERYTHING!
I liked Mepis 3.3 and i used it for about a year before my emachine finally gave up on me.
I then used a loner PC for about a few months and once more on to XP.
Then I got a new compy, in that time I tried other distros:
Mandriva: Yuck
Linspire: Ehh
Then I first tried ubuntu, but I honestly HATED IT.
It was Ubuntu breezy, and I thought it was the worst piece of crap i ever saw.
I hated its brown color scheme, it detected NONE of my hardware and I found it a pain to use.
I tried many other things before I made my way back to Mepis.
I installed Mepis 3.4 but it was not as smooth as Mepis 3.3.
Then came Mepis 6.0 and its betas, I was actually livid when I heard it was going to be ubuntu based.
At that time I only had experience with Breezy, Dapper was still in the works and I didnt like the news of the switch.
After a bit i gave in and tried Mepis 6.0 beta 2 and I kinda stuck with it for a bit and then I decided to give dapper a shot.
I downloaded ubuntu dapper beta and I was actually impressed, dapper really read my system and recognized everything... my saitek controller, my DVD drive, my router...
Dapper was much cleaner looking then breezy, gone were the ugly browns and there were more pleasant orange colors instead.
Dapper really impressed me, it honestly did.
I watied it out and when dapper was finally out of beta I gave it one more try and Dapper was far better then I expected...
I finally gave in and Dapper is now on my computer full time.
Dapper is really awesome, I was really touchy with it at first as I said i didnt like Ubuntu there for the longest time... Dapper proved me wrong :D
