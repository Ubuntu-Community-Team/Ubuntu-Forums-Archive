### Post by LemursDontExist on 2011-03-25
I get the sense you might be judging a little early.

I don't know what stuff you're missing from Adobe.  Acrobat is certainly available for Linux (though I prefer the built in gnome pdf viewer).  I personally prefer Inkscape to Adobe Illustrator, and I've heard Scribus compares reasonably with Pagemaker.  Many people *do* prefer Photoshop to GIMP, but I think that's mostly a matter of what they're used to.

I can't say much about the sound quality except that I've never heard of anyone else having that problem.

As for yahoo video chat - have you considered using Skype, Google video, Empathy or Pidgin?  All of them allow video chat.  If you're really set on using Yahoo video, (why, I can't fathom), 5 minutes on Google turned up [GYachI]("http://gyachi.sourceforge.net/intro.shtml"), which supports Yahoo video chat.

I'm with you that open office sucks, but if you're like me and mostly use word processing and the occasional spreadsheet you could try installing Abiword as your word processor and Gnumeric as your spreadsheet.  Abiword is very lightweight fast, and easy.  If that's not what you want, there are a dozen other options.  Gnumeric, in my opinion, is flat out superior to Excel.

If you need Powerpoint, you're stuck with open office, but at that point, that's the least of your problems!
