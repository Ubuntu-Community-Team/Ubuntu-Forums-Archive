### Post by marin123 on 2012-04-10
I have been using Ubuntu as only OS on my laptop since Natty and kept the beast in the bottle (XP in VirtualBox), but I know of Ubuntu since Hardy and I always had it somewhere on my HDD.

But yesterday I backed up my files and installed Windows 7. And I'm keeping it... There are 2 reasons to do so. First, my battery life has almost tripled (from 1:10 to 3:00) and I can put laptop in my lap without worry I will get burned (it was really heating - normal work ~75 C).
And second, I have decent graphics drivers (Mobility Radeon HD 5xxx).

The thing is, I like Ubuntu, but battery life and watching movies like normal people do (without stuttering (radeon driver and catalyst) and tearing(catalyst)) is more important to me.

But I have learned a big lesson. Now I'm using as much open-source programs as I can. Libre Office, VLC, gedit, Thunderbird, evince, LMMS etc...

And not to finish in depressing tone, I will be using Ubuntu on my home desktop because when compared to Windows, there's not much difference and I can say, Ubuntu is a bit better.
