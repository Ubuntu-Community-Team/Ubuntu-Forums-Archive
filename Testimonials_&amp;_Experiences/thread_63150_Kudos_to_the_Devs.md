### Post by Grey on 2005-09-07
So I bought a laptop a couple of weeks ago (Toshiba Sattelite M50), as I was going on a journey, and needed a PC with me.  I didn't have time to customize it whatsoever, other than installing Firefox in Windows.  The last couple of days, I've spent a lot of time getting the laptop whipped into shape.  I began with a complete repartitioning via the Windows XP CD.

I installed Windows, and by some miracle, it didn't require a special mass storage driver at boot (as I don't have a floppy drive, this would be difficult to work around).  But once installed, there were about 10 devices without drivers, and I had to find and download appropriate drivers on another computer and bring them over on a flash drive to get it working.  After downloading about half the drivers, I discovered that Toshiba had a page set up with all the software I needed, so it probably didn't require as much time as I spent, but it probably took a good 4 hours to get the base system up and running. 

Yesterday my project was Ubuntu.  I installed Hoary, and encountered a full system freeze when running glxgears... fair enough, I had seen the same thing happen from the live CD, so I assumed it was a problem with the OS somewhere.  But then I figured that since Breezy is due out in a little over a month, I might just try the beta.

So today I downloaded and installed the Breezy Colony 4 beta.  I had heard so many horror stories in these forums about Breezy that I was expecting huge crashes, and all manner of problem.  But to my absolute shock, nearly all of my hardware works out of the box (there's a bit of stuff missing... built in ethernet, and a few other things according to the device manager, but nothing I'll miss).  All in all, it took me about an hour to get the base Ubuntu system installed.  Which is simply outstanding.  I spent a few hours trying to find laptop specific utilities to install... stuff to reduce the CPU speed, dim the monitor, etc.  I felt like a complete tool when I realized that too was working out of the box.  There were hot keys on my keyboard to dim the monitor that were working just fine, and CPU scaling was already enabled.

I've been working with it all day, customizing and tweaking it.  And I really only have a few comments to make.

1)  Everything works.  It's incredible.  No crashing, and all my favorite software is humming along beautifully.  I've already recommended Breezy to a friend.

2)  Stuff that DOESN'T work is a fairly short list.  But I really wish that my laptop would suspend to ram  when the lid closes, and the hot keys to suspend to ram doesn't work either.  (Anyone have tips on how to accomplish this? :))

But overall, I am just absolutely ecstatic about the new Ubuntu.  I didn't think it was possible to get this much better with every release.  :)
