### Post by CongoJim on 2006-05-15
People here are amused by my using an op system whose name is an actual word in Tshiluba, the language spoken here. It basically means "the spirit of a collective humanity", or Karma in other words.

I love using UBUNTU. I have partitioned my hard drive and I almost never use the other partition anymore. With the help of the US embassy, (their money, my labor) I am installing a computer lab in a school that will be shared by 6 high schools during the day and open for public classes and use until late.  Who needs sleep? 

Two things I've yet to locate, a document translating program ala Systran and a Cyber-cafe control program. We have to charge for use in the afternoons and evenings if the kids are to study for free, our internet connection is 500 euros/month and the diesel for the generator runs $8/gal. A cyber-cafe program would come in real handy for us, anyone seen one?

There is no doubt whatsoever as to what op system we will work in most of the time and all of the time on-line. UBUNTU wins that job hands down. (I'll still have to teach that other system because the kids will need to know how to fix it when it screws up).](*,)
