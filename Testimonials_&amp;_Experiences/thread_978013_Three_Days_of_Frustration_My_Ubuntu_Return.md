### Post by Unanimated on 2008-11-10
Only six days ago, I started a thread called [Why I'm Leaving Ubuntu]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=970514"). Three of those days I actually had access to my computer, and I had access to all the frustration the three other operating systems I chose had to offer: OpenSUSE, Mandriva, and Fedora 9. Let's start with a run down of my experiences with each of these operating systems.

OpenSUSE - I wanted to try this because I wanted something different. I read that this was a good operating system, so I downloaded the ISO and burned it to a CD. I ran the LiveCD,and... it didn't detect my video card right. There were white and purple lines throughout the screen, the mouse wouldn't move, and the screen itself was too big for my screen, resulting in laughs.

Mandriva - UGH, this was the most horrid Linux experience I've ever had. I probably installed this horrid thing about 5 times before I decided to give up. I ran the LiveCD and it detected everything perfectly. I took this as a good sign. I ran a few programs, then I ran the installer and installed it. When it said it was finished, I rebooted to my shiny new Mandriva install. It took me to a survey screen, which I thought was actually kind of nice. (One of the things under "Why you chose to use Mandriva" was stability - more on that later.) I finished the survey, typed in my designated root password, and logged in. I used it for a while, trying to get used to KDE (I've been using GNOME ever since I started using Linux), and I decided that I wanted to change a few settings. It asked me for my root password. I typed it in, plus about three other possibilities, and it rejected me every time. I went to their forums to ask about it, and a very rude user was giving me hell about the whole thing. I decided to reinstall Mandriva. I went through the survey again, then I made sure I remembered my root password this time. I started using it again. I installed a few updates, then I realized that during the install, Mandriva didn't do my partitioning right - my other Mandriva installation was still on the hard drive, on a separate partition. I went back to the Mandriva LiveCD, and wrote a new partition table. It claimed that this would remove all data. Great. I reinstalled Mandriva. Again. I used it - again. Once again, it didn't do my f*cking partitioning right. I went back to the LiveCD. Again. And reinstalled Mandriva. AGAIN. I started downloading the massive Fedora 9 ISO and burned it to six different discs, leaving me with a single blank disc left. Yay. When I went to Fedora's installer, it claimed that the ISO was corrupt. I thought I was going to scream. My hard drive was wiped clean of any operating system, so I installed Mandriva. AGAIN. This time, it got my partitioning right. I installed all 79 updates, removed the orphaned packages, and rebooted. At the login screen after the reboot, it wouldn't let me log in. I chose a bunch of different sessions and typed in my username and password countless times. After each try, the X server would reset itself and take me back to the login screen. Even failsafe failed - how ironic. I gave up and here I am again, a faithful Ubuntu user - again.

I've decided that I'm going to try and look past (if they even show up again) Ubuntu's issues that made me decide to switch.
