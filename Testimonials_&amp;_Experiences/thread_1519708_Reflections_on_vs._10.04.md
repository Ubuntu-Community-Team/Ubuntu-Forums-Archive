### Post by jawkyn on 2010-06-28
For the past four years or so, I have ordered an installation CD of each version of Ubuntu as it comes out and have installed it on a spare computer.  Although I have Windows on my main computer, I like Ubuntu and like the idea of having it up and running.  Every year it seems to get better.   
 

 But I don't know about 10.04.  I like the fast boot up speed.  I like the “Getting Started with Ubuntu” PDF users manual.  And unlike some users, I have always liked the brown color scheme that Ubuntu is known for.  But I don't care for Apple computers and I absolute detest the purplish color that 10.04 has adopted.  Also, I don't like the button arrangement that 10.04 has introduced.  The buttons that manage windows don't seem to work as they should.  As installed on my computer, the left and right button close the page and the center button sizes it.  None put your open work on the bottom panel until you want to restore it to the desktop.  Also, since I sometimes transfer files from Windows 7 to Ubuntu, Dropbox works for me and Ubuntu One doesn't.  All-in-all, I have a problem with version 10.04.
 

 I also experienced some glitches in installing this version.  I finally got it installed and working properly on the third try.  Could it have been because all the previous versions have been ext3 and this one is ext4?
 

 Even though 10.04 is a LTD, I'm thinking of going back to 9.10 and this post is more of an attempt to reach a decision than it is a putdown of 10.04.  How do other users feel about this version and does anyone know if the Ubuntu developers are going to make the 10.10 version more Linuxy and drop these imitations of the Apple?  Sorry if I appear to be an Ubuntu basher.  I'm not.  As I said earlier, I like the distro.  But my installation of 10.04 just doesn't seem to “cut the mustard”.
