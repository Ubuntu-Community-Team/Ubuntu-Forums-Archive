### Post by Mazate on 2011-06-22
Just wanted to throw my $.02 with linux.  I've had in the back of my mind for probably a year that I wouldn't mind trying linux sometime.  I'm not a big Microsoft fan, only because it's my belief (based on no evidence whatsoever) that windows is a fantastic way to gather personal information about people and transmitting it over the internet to some server somewhere could happen and I'd never know it.  I want to be "off the grid", if you will.

In March or so I ran across an article on tomshardware.com about Gnome 3.  I had no idea what gnome3 was at the time but I did see that it was something new for linux.  I read about it and ended up at the openSUSE website.  I saw that I could download the .iso disk and install it on my computer.  Ironically my wife's computer's hard drive died so I installed openSUSE.  I wasn't a big fan only because I didn't have any idea what I was doing.  To make a long story short (if that's possible at this point) I ended up also installing Fedora (which I really liked) and then finally Ubuntu.  Ubuntu is clearly the most user friendly distribution.  I loved fedora but it was just a little too much work for someone with virtually no experience with linux.

Anyway, my hard drive died about 3 weeks ago and now I'm 100% Ubuntu on my computer.  (The wife went back to XP.)  I love it.  I like unity.  And best of all I like that my operating system is FREE.  I'll never have to shell out another dime to a large corporation to have a computer OS.

I work for a hosting company that has a bunch of linux servers (including Ubuntu) and I'm now beginning the process of taking classes and getting linux certifications to work on the servers.  See there?  Linux may have opened up a whole new world for me, all because of an article on tomshardware.com.
