### Post by cobolt01 on 2011-03-09
I first started playing with Linux when I was about 14. I was just curious and after fiddling with Mandriva, Fedora and SuSE, I got a copy of Ubuntu 7.04.

The operating system was easy enough to use not to frustrate me and I set it up on my mother's machine and my own for a while (I find dual booting pointless and I spent a lot of time playing games). With a question here and there my mom easily got the hang of doing all her basic tasks on ubuntu that she kept it for nearly 3 years. 

At the same time I convinced a new friend at school to try Ubuntu. Being quite a computer guy himself he immediately saw the benefit of the software and he kept it as his only OS for nearly 2 years. 

In 2009 I got a laptop and immediately threw Vista off and put initially Kubuntu 9.04 and eventually Ubuntu 10.04 on it.

After years of showing interest in ubuntu and linux to my best friend's dad (who is a software project manager) he actually now runs 4 Ubuntu machines to develop linux based cash register software and host a master server and web server.

Over the years it has always been my favorite operating system and the rate at which it is improving is really impressive. It also makes me proud that a fellow South African (Mark Shuttleworth) is behind the project. I just wish I didn't have to use Windows for running games. Sadly I think it's going to be a long time before games are all OpenGL and come with a Linux version.
