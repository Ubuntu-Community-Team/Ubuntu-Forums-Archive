### Post by Jacobonbuntu on 2011-11-06
I think it was a about a month before Unity was introduced that I switched to Ubuntu for “full time use”. As soon as it was released I installed 11.04, and as many others I was a bit confused. After a few months I found out the left side was the only right side for the launcher to be, given the fact that scroll bars are on the right and most screens are wide. Amazing that Ubuntu is the only one that came to the idea (as far as I know). At this moment I have the most efficient desktop I ever had; the launcher organized with a number of quick lists; “chapters” of  functionality, directories, groups of applications, and in some cases with separate functionality within applications, scripts, links to system files to edit now and then etc. The complete “organization” can be transported to the next installation in a minute! You want your notes to be synchronized? A simple right clicking the data folder and choose “synchronize with Ubuntu one” is enough!
 Also the 6 month cycle I actually like a lot. In the past I re-installed my system once or twice a year anyway, and now with a bit of adventure to see “what is new”. In spite of some testimonials I must say Ubuntu is rock solid on all my (six) quite different computers. Seriously, I believe I made the switch on the right time. I am no Ubuntu marketeer, but I believe I have good reasons to say thank you for this elegant and efficient environment.
