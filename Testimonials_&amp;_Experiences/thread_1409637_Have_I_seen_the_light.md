### Post by scottjr on 2010-02-17
Hello, Ubuntu community!

I have been a Windows user nearly all my life (from 8 or 9 yrs old on) and never really considered the possibility of another operating system other than Mac (which I do not like).  Perhaps growing up with it conditions one to just accept it as the only option.  Only within the past couple years have I even begun to take notice of Linux, having seen Ubuntu magazines on the shelves at a newsstand where I used to work.

The day before yesterday, I installed Ubuntu 9.10 (dual booting with windows, just in case) and must say I am thoroughly impressed so far.  I've had only 2 issues: 

1) I had one hell of a time figuring out how to get Flash working so I could use Pandora, and
2) I was so impressed I immediately popped the disk in my Acer netbook to install there as well.  Installing the grub update from the Update Manager, however, locked the netbook up and upon rebooting, nothing would work; it wouldn't boot at all.  Fortunately, I was able to repower with the liveCD inserted and reinstall the OS.

That second issue bodes not well, and has me slightly uneasy, but I'm keeping my hopes high, because I'm loving everything else about this OS.

I'm euphoric about all the little things I keep finding and saying "Wait, what?... It just DOES that? I don't have to download or buy anything?!" Like archive management, for example.

So far, I <3 Ubuntu Linux :)
scottjr
