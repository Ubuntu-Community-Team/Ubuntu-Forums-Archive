### Post by Ghost|BTFH on 2011-04-28
I have to say "a biased opinion" because, let's face it, we're all biased to one end or another.  Myself, I'm biased towards Gnome and how it's looked for many years.

With that being said, my next statement may come as a surprise:

Unity is revolutionary.  As I told a friend who asked me what it was like, I said, "It's like Gnome meets Mac and the two lay out their best features and merge them together." and that's how I see it.

The icons on the side are fantastic.  Keyboard shortcuts are slick.  Finally a good use for a Windows hot key on a keyboard other than being annoying.  Having one window as your key focal point and doing away with even a few more pixels at the bottom to give us more viewing room?  I love it.

It's smooth, graceful - I get it.  Is it *the* way to go with Ubuntu?  I don't know.  Gnome 3 is pretty slick and I have no hate on for it, but minimizing things horizontally instead of vertically just doesn't make much sense anymore with widescreen monitors becoming the defacto standard.

I wholeheartedly agree with the step Ubuntu has made with Unity and as one voice among the many nay-says, I'd just like to go on the record as saying...

"YAY!"

Cheers,
Ghost|BTFH
