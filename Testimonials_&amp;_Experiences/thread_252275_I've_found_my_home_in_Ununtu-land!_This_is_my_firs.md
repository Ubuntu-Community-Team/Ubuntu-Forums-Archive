### Post by MonkeeOfEvil on 2006-09-06
Howdy y'all! Well my name is Steve, and I'm a second generation computer nerd. I grew up with computers.. in fact, I don't remember NOT having one. I've used everything from Apple's Lisa to DOS, almost every version of the Macintosh OS, and of course...all incarnations of Windows from 3.1 through XP. I even fiddled with a copy of Vista ( Longhorn build 4800 ) which I uhhh *cough*aquired*cough*. I also knew of *nix systems because my father worked with them while he was in the Navy. 

My first encounter with Linux was in 1998. Dad brought home a copy of Slackware from the office and I watched him install and configure it. It damaged my teenage psyche. No GUI installer? No GUI configuration? Pain in the *** to get drivers working! Why use such arcane methods... Those were my first thoughts. Fast forward a couple years, I had become a reclusive, anti-social computer nerd.. I spent my days breaking Windows ( intentionally or otherwise ) and trying to figure out A.) WTF I did, B.) Why it broke, and C.) HTF to fix it.. I also become quite interested in programming and web design. I had heard from a few friends that Linux was supposedly great if you were a "programmer". Wanting to be all hardcore 1337 I decided to install Linux.

I chose slackware my first time round...not very n00b friendly at the time.. hell I didn't even know how to make the boot floppies. I never did get it installed. Later on I tried SuSe 8ish, which seemed to install fine and work the way I wanted it to. I spent HOURS configuring KDE to my liking, tweaked XMMS and other apps and then rebooted. To my absolute horror the OS had completely disappeared and I was left with a RAW hard drive. This happened about 5 times. This turned me off from SuSe completely, and to this day I will not given it the time of day. 

I later tried RedHat it's variants but never really fealt like i could replace windows. I tried Mandrake and other Debian variants and I disliked them even more than I disliked XP. I went with RedHat for a while, but always ended up going back to windows. 

About a year ago I began to hear people begin to rant and rave about Ubuntu. I said skrew that, I won't touch debian based distros. So I began using Fedora Core 4. What a pain in my ***. I STILL can't get my wireless drivers (WUSB11 V2.6) to work for my desktop on any other distro, and it took me 2 months to get my Belkin F5D7011 to work on the laptop. Finally said skrew it and went back to windows. 

About a week ago I picked up a copy of Linux Magazine which included a live boot of Dapper Drake, so I gave it a try. Tried it on the laptop because i've had more success with linux on that particular machine than on my desktop. Had to boot into safe graphics mode, compile ndiswrapper, load the driver, and configure my networks SSID. It worked splendedly. I was VERY impressed, roughly an hour to install and configure a distro, not bad. Replaced Windows completely on the lappy. 

After a few days of playing with Dapper on the laptop I decided it was time to get my hands really dirty and install it on the desktop. I figured I would spend at least a week wrestling with my wireless adapter, if I even got it working. I boot the live CD and HOLY SHIT! It already had drivers for the network adapter. The ONLY thing I had to do was tell the network config util what SSID to connect to. 

I am VERY impressed guys. This is by far the best distributions I have ever used. I will keep Windows XP on my desktop, just in case I can't get something working with WINE.. Ubuntu is now my primary OS.

I can not stress enough how happy I am with this distribution. I mean, I like it so much that I plan on looking into making a livecd variant of it for my daughter to use when she is old enough to start using the computer.. Aurora is 7 months old, and already loves the computer. Right now she likes banging on the keyboard and watching visuals from winamp/wmp/xmms/whatever media player she's in front of. The basic idea will be for there to be plenty of educational software, big colorful menus and so on...more on this later though..

Anyway, That is my rant about how I came to Ubuntu.. I will be here for a while and if anyone ever needs help learning OOP techniques please let me know..I may not be a grand master 1337 c0d3rz0r, but I know enough to help people in the freshmen and sophmore level university courses. 

Cheers!

- Monkee
