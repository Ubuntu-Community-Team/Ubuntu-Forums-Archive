### Post by FootySr on 2009-05-07
1992-2003 --- Mac OS7, Mac OS8, Mac OS9, OSX
2003-2009 --- XP Home, XP Pro
2009 Forward --- Ubuntu!!!

Tuesday April 28th I picked up a second hard drive because I really wanted to test out Ubuntu and I didn't want to mess with my XP drive in any way, shape or form, just in case.

Wednesday April 29th I installed the second hard drive and loaded up Ubuntu 9.04. On boot up I was surprised I had sound and a working network. Hmmm, no drivers to install? 10 seconds into it I get a notice to install my graphic card drivers, ok thanks? Can it really be this easy! I played around testing different things out. Nice, real nice!

Thursday April 30th I decided to try setup of the printer that is connected to my wifes Mac. To be honest it was such a pain in XP that I didn't think I'd really have a shot at this working via Ubuntu. Lets see, System --> Administration --> Printing, once open, Server menu --> Settings --> Check box "Show printers by other systems --> OK. Looky there! There's the printer! Test print shot right out. I handed to my wife and she read it... "No way this is going to work but if it does I'll delete my XP install tomorrow!"

Friday May 1st I played around with Ubuntu, researched Ubuntu a bit deeper and Linux in general. 

Saturday May 2nd I deleted XP and installed Ubuntu on my primary hard drive and deleted the new second drive and made it available as extra ext3 formatted space!

On Sunday and Monday afternoon I ripped 78 CDs, 4.5 Gigs, 1042 songs using Rhythmbox in Ogg Vorbis! Oh I'm in with both feet now! :)

That said... I do have a couple of concerns. 

I'm addicted to Photoshop! I'm liking GIMP but it does seem to be missing some of the key features that made graphics really pop in Photoshop. Between GIMP and Inkscape I'll figure it out. Learning curve thing. No I refuse to run Wine or Virtual anything! I told you I'm in with both feet already! ;)

I also have many iPods around the house between me, the wife and kids. Now I could piggy back with any of the other 4 computers running XP or OSX but it won't be my favorite 78 CDs I just tossed in here. I realize by jumping from mp3 to Ogg I've created yet another hurdle but I'm willing to jump that one too.  
 

 I'm not against getting a new music player that's what it takes play well with Ubuntu and Ogg. And if a new player is needed I'll do that, don't think I haven't researched it to death already. :) I have 2 iPods, one is a regular nano which I primarily use in the winter months with I'm stuck in a plow truck for 16 hours at a time (I hate snow) and the other is an iPod Touch, which is nice but I'm afraid to get a scratch on it and to be honest I don't need all those features. It was a heck of a nice gift though Honey! Maybe I'll research Jailbreaking it so I can get my Ogg files on there.  
 

 One more thing. I like having a few movies on my hard drive. I did copy 7 of my favorites over from my XP install before deletion. I'll use HandBrake to encode them into m4v or mp4 but I'll have to research how to decrypt my collection. 

Thanks for all your time! I'm absolutely loving Ubuntu!

### Post by jasonsjunk on 2009-05-08
I am glad you have had such a good experience with Ubuntu.  As for me my wife's laptop is the last Windoze box left in my house everything is now running Jaunty Desktop of Server Edition.  

With regards to your music collection why not rip to mp3 to keep it compatible with your ipods.  I know mp3 is a restricted format but you can install mp3 support for ubuntu although its legality is a grey area depending on where you live.  

As for photoshop Wine isn't evil many Windows programs work very well in Wine.  Here is the link to the Wine information page for photoshop  [http://wiki.winehq.org/AdobePhotoshop](http://wiki.winehq.org/AdobePhotoshop)  In addition there is another application written for Linux but not free called Pixel, I personally haven't used it as Gimp and other applications have met my needs so far.  

As for the movie conversion thing I would recommend WinFF for converting your movies into MP4 or another suitable format for your Ipods, WinFF is very quick and easy to use.  WinFF is a graphical interface for FFMPEG and already has built in presets for IPod and many other devices.  I use it quite regularly to transcode movies and tv shows for my Blackberry. 

To rip DVD's check out dvdrip, k3b, acidrip, k9copy.  I personally think k3b is one of the best burning programs around but that is my personal opinion.  You can also install FabDVD or DVDDecrypter in Wine they both work just fine.  However I will leave the legal determination up to you....
