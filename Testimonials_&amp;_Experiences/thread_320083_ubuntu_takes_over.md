### Post by x64Jimbo on 2006-12-19
You don't know how right you are. Linux distros have to be competitive if they want to keep their users. They have to be feature-rich and standards-compliant to keep their strong userbases. Look at Fedora Core. They used to be the top players when 1 and 2 were around, but they've been losing ground to Ubuntu because their package management blows (among other things). So now they're being forced to innovate and revamp their package management system. Hopefully this makes them a more viable player again. Just like Intel needed AMD biting them in the arse all the time to have the motivation to move away from the hideous NetBurst architecture, Linux distros need each other's fierce competition to force them to think of new, faster, easier ways to do things. And Microsoft, with no real competition is poking along - barely making any improvements. Sooner or later, probably in the next 3 years, *nix will be able to run any Windows program (thanx a million to the Wine project) and there will be no reason for people to stick with Windows.
