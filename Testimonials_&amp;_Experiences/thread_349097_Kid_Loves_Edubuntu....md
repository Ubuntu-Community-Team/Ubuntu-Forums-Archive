### Post by sstakes1 on 2007-01-29
I have dabbled with linux in the past and have had dual boot options off and on since RH5.0. However, I had only one computer at home running W2K as my primary portal on a home-built PC. I constantly tinker with my PC, changing video cards, memory and HDDs with other PCs belonging to my brother, I was not sure if XP was the right solution for me as I have heard some complaints regarding activation being a little unfriendly towards such activities. So with Microsoft officially dropping support for W2K soon, and the need for me to create a PC for my 4 year old son, I was seriously forced to consider Linux solutions. 

I tried several LiveCDs. When I tried Ubuntu, I knew that it was the OS for me. What blew me away as the earthy default theme and good support for all my hardware. Mepis was a close second.

Being reasonably proficient with Linux/BSD, I was comfortable with the installation. The first steps were crawling (dual boot setup with W2K). Once I had the ATI video card working comfortably, I gradually weaned away from Windows so that within 3 months, I have entirely switched to Ubuntu. I blew away my W2K partition. Unfortunately, I have W2K under VMWare for 2 purposes: my tax software, and the Logitech Harmony Remote Control client. I did not bother with Wine as these are the only two programs I need from Windows. In addition, I am an avid photographer. But I have always been a Gimp user even under windows. So that switch was painless.

The biggest surprise was that my wife liked Ubuntu as well and found the switch painless for her Web surfing/e-mail activities.

Emboldened by this success, I put together an Edubuntu PC for my 4 year old son on a small Antec Aria case. I was really surprised when the installation recognized without any issues the built in card reader. I have GCompris, Childsplay and Google Earth on it and he has so much fun with it. I really crack up any time he says "Edoobuntu". Since then I have tried a Ubuntu Server, running Squid and DansGuardian. I have also upgraded my system with a HDTV capture card and MythTv. All of this funding was justified as funds obtained from the savings on Windows Upgrade/ new Windows Licenses.

I have since then encouraged three of my friends to switch to Ubuntu. All of them were considering PCs for their young kids. I have also put together another cube case PC for my neice as her birthday gift.

I am reasonably happy with my switch over to Ubuntu. Granted, it was not entirely painless. However, the excellent support of the community (past forum posts mostly) have made it tolerable for me. 

Thanks a bunch to everyone who has contributed towards making Ubuntu a very viable distribution.

### Post by sstakes1 on 2007-01-30
Thanks. I am aware of those. I currently play TuxKart (the kids watch it transfixed!)  and FlightGear flight sim. My 4-year old still does not have enough coordination or reflexes for TuxKart yet. My 2-year old is currently training to use the mouse using games designed for that purpose under GCompris. Both of them also enjoy flash based animation/games at kid web sites. There is an amazing depth and variety of Software for everyone in the family.  

The greatest benefit of this is that my kid uses Windows based kids games in his pre-school and seems to easily switch between the Windows desktop and Gnome Desktop without any issues. 

The greatest obstacle in more widespread adoption of Ubuntu and other distributions in my opinion is that it is not available pre-installed, which eliminates a lot of people. Once it is on the system, most people would not know/care as long as it works....
