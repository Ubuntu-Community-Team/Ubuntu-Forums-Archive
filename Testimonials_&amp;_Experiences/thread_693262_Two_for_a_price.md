### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-02-10
Good work!  I hear you loud-and-clear about the patience part.  I find most people have a hard time getting past the "It's legally free ..." part.  They're like, "There's gotta be a catch," or "How can it possibly be free and continue to be developed?"  They just don't get it ... but I have found one way to explain it to them that almost always works.  It relies on the notion that they respect my overall skill with computer hardware and software in general, and will not work with people who do not trust my computer advice yet.

"Linux, and Ubuntu in particular, is free because it's developed by thousands of computer geeks like me on our free time.  We have nothing better to do than eat burritos and write code ..."

Of course, I hate burritos, and I can't code anything to save my life, but *they* don't need to know this LOL.  As long as they understand that it's developed for free and that it actually works ... many people will be more open to it, particularly once they have seen/used it.  They're often amazed, "It's easier to figure out than Windows!" ... hehe, very satisfying.

Anyhooo ... didn't mean to hijack your thread.  Good work, and keep it up!!!
