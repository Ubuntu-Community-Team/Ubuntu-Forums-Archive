### Post by Foaming Draught on 2005-09-23
I'm IT literate but completely non-technical (you guessed it, I'm a salesman  :razz: ) and I've been put off Linux on several previous attempts by an incomprehensible command line environment. Then as distros got friendlier (Mandrake of blessed memory comes to mind), critical hardware wasn't supported, or not without recourse to the aforementioned incomprehensible command line. In my case, Palm synching and wi-fi support were killer points.
Windows XP Pro is the best (perhaps the first stable) iteration of Windows, and if it weren't for a moral distaste for the Redmondites I might have stuck with it. I'd already switched to OOo, Firefox & Thunderbird, so the OS was my only concession to MS.But I had one more go, at Breezy Community, and voila, it just installed flawlessly, detected my wi-fi card, made putting the Win32 driver into ndiswrapper dead easy, picked up all my other h/w and updated itself across the Net. And it carries on updating itself, day by day, it's magic.
I love the GNOME DE. I love Synaptic, it really has nothing to apologise for compared to MS app installation. I like the look of Evolution's calendar, but it won't synch with Palm (it won't, and no-one can honestly say that it does for more than a couple of days on end). Current Evo mail is much improved for my IMAP server over previous versions, but not as fast as Thunderbird. So although JPilot is not as elegant as Evolution or even Palm Desktop, it works and is lean and mean.
I've removed my Win partition altogether, running only Breezy. Sometimes, after I've booted and before I load apps, I just sit and stare at the screen, it is SOOOOO beautiful. I've saved an enormous amount of money, not just by not forking out dosh to Redmond, but by getting a very great performance boost out of my 6 year old Dell which will put off for a year having to get a new machine (unless I win Lotto of course).
I can't begin to express my gratitude and admiration to the creators and developers of the Ubuntu project, and to so many other community contributors at Debian, GNOME, OOo and others.
Ubuntu is ready for the desktop, says this absolute non-techie, and it's ready for the world. Go team!

### Post by Foaming Draught on 2005-09-24
"May I ask how that happened? (I have breezy but I already had my wifi card setup lol)"

Good question. We're going back 2 months, at my age I can't remember how I did things 2 days ago.
I installed Breezy Community using an ethernet cable. At some stage I was presented with an opportunity to configure other network devices, including a wireless card (Breezy had detected that I had a card but not what it was, a Belkin F5D7010au). I had a choice of using a native driver or ndiswrapper. I plumped for the latter, and used a second CD drive to choose bcmwl5.inf from the Belkin install CD, I didn't go to Sourceforge. Lights flashed on the Belkin card, I removed the ethernet cable and haven't looked back.
