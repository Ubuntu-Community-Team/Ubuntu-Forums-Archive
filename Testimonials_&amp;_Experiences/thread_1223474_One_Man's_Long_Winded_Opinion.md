### Post by derekeverett on 2009-07-26
I felt like ranting a bit so here I am. I'm not looking to spread hate and evil or anything so please nobody take it that way..

I just felt like sharing an opinion or two about Ubuntu and all the other OS's in my life.

I've been using Windows almost daily since about 1993. I bought a mac in 2006 and another in 2007. I discovered Ubuntu in 2007 also and used it exclusively in my home for a year ending a couple weeks ago. So I thought after many many months of using these three operating systems daily I thought I would share my opinions for anybody who cares and isn't sick to death of such discussions yet.

First, Ubuntu is the most fun.. from a learning how computers work and getting around a command line perspective. Unfortunately it is hands down the most frustrating when you want to actually get something done other than google around. Webcams are a nightmare, I've had and continue to have audio problems, it's hit and miss when you install new apps from the repository - some just plain don't work, If you need a VPN connection good luck. I haven't been able to establish one since 8.04 that suits my needs. I won't even talk about the email problems with Evolution cause I'll throw my computer off my balcony.

But all that being said I keep truckin' with it. I've even got the t-shirt. lol. 

But I no longer consider Linux a reasonable alternative for people depending on their computer to get work done. 

Windows is windows is windows. It's slow. It's full of spyware etc. and it gives me a headache.. but XP anyway is stable for the most part. Vista frankly even works for me as long as you don't download pirated stuff. But it gets the job done. I just hate what microsoft has allowed the OEM's to do. You get so much clutter and garbage straight out of the box now.. it's terrible.

OS X. Works everytime. If I could have only 1 computer frankly it's a no-brainer for me at this point. For the regular desktop user an imac has most everything you will ever need. If you buy office 2008 and download the windows media codec than your pretty much fully compatible. I get frustrated with apple's bs lots. They are as corporate as they come even if Steve Jobs wears jeans and I would sooner eat nothing but tofu for the rest of my life than buy an iphone but OS x rules man. If you need it for office, work, or play it will get er done for you with style to boot. 

Not sure if I have a point here. Guess I don't. Just wanted to say something.

I guess if there is another poll I best be honest and vote for the mac. I am addicted to this linux biz at this point but if I am truthful with myself I have to admit it's just for fun. It ain't ready for primetime yet. Not for my needs anyway. I would love to get my hands on a commercial version of linux one day and see if it's better. Any suggestions? I'd pay the $300 or so it costs for Windows to give it a whirl.

### Post by raronson on 2009-07-26
Nothing wrong with ranting or honesty.  That's been your experience, and I never get sick to death of hearing other people out regarding their adventures in Linux.

As for me, I downloaded Slackware Linux in 1997 from Walnut Creek's FTP site onto something like 16 floppy disks and installed it over an OEM Windows 95 Packard Bell.  I've been using Linux since then and watching the changes that come year and year that make it better with each new iteration.  

A lot of people who have joined "this show already in progress" have a hard time appreciating Linux from that perspective.  Too many times there are unfair comparisons to commercial offerings which have certified hardware vendors that cater to them.  The fact that Linux can support the amount of hardware that it does is nothing less than amazing--even if the odd webcam or bleeding edge video card takes some time to get support (or just falls by wayside altogether)--double this if you're talking about a laptop.

Linux has long been ready for primetime for a lot of individual and business needs, it's just that we all have a definition of primetime that slightly differs.  If you like Linux though, the best way to see* your* primetime come into being is to get involved, report bugs, and provide feedback.  If you don't want to be bothered, that's totally understandable.  Your primetime version will be here soon enough.  Keep checking back :)

Edit: this comes to you from a macbook running only Ubuntu and Arch Linux (OS X is not for everyone either).
