### Post by towsonu2003 on 2006-12-05
Wow, time goes so fast... ~1,5 year ago, back when I was trying to set up Slackware to my laptop, I got stuck with a non-working winmodem. 

When my antivirus scanner subscription (Norton) expired, I started looking around for another OS, one that I saw about 6 years ago with Knoppix and forgot about after booting it once (was ugly, games didn't work, but sooo many software.. wow). I googled for "free operating system" and there it was: Linux. 

I spent 3 months to set up my winmodem, trying to decipher my modemdata.txt, understand the instructions I got from linmodems.org, and so on. It didn't work... But I still liked it (Slackware). 

Right when I lost all hope and stopped trying, I noticed the Ubuntu CD on my desktop. I didn't try it, because, well, I don't like to try popular stuff. For example, I don't read best-sellers...

I booted with the Ubuntu liveCD, set up the driver for my modem as usual, tried to dial out, and... It connected!! Still don't know why... I just couldn't believe my eyes, using Firefox in Linux to check out the web... and no virus scanner!.. Wow... You wanna click on a link, go ahead, no need to think twice about the malicious Windows code it may have... Amazing!

But I still didn't want to use the popular thing (that's how I am). So I posted my first thread here ever: [this one]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=73662"), asking how to get Ubuntu's kernel with its config file to Slackware. Fortunately, no one answered.[1]

Next, I asked why I was having [this stupid problem]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=76065") with my winmodem. No one knew the answer, so I learned to live with it (even though I later filed a bug for it). 

Now you know the cause of my obsession with winmodems... 

And finally, trying to settle down with a best-seller, I started reading the forums. And I learned so much. It was fun giving and receiving help. The community was (and still is) great. And the OS is easy especially after having some (great) experience with Slackware. 

Now, 1 year later, I did not boot to Windows for the last 7 or so months. It's so ugly, so badly designed... It doesn't come with almost any bundled software. The ones that come with it are either adware, or second-grade quality software. You have to find and install your own programs. If something is wrong (software bug), you don't know whether it will be fixed or not in the future. You have to have a subscription to an anti-virus provider if you want quality protection. You have to stay tied to a firewall (zonealarm) that will bork your installation during updates. And your installation may bork itself without your input from time to time. Not to mention the filesystem maintenance (check this, defrag that). And you certainly can't email Bill Gates or his team when you are worried about stuff going on around... Bla bla bla... Did I say the interface is ugly? 

Thanks everyone in the community for making this a great journey. 

Oh, and, knock on wood :)

And, as usual, visit the links in my signature :mrgreen: 

[1] It wasn't that difficult: now I know that the kernel, the patches, and the config file were all at packages.ubuntu.com... ;)
