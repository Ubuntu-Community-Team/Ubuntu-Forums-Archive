### Post by zcal on 2006-08-15
Was I ever bored with computing!  I've used Windows ever since 1999, when the family Mac Performa got replaced by a 450 mhz HP loaded with Windows '98.  After having always experienced the power of Windows over at my friend's house, I was extremely happy with the switch, especially since it was away from a four year old Mac that I obviously couldn't play the latest games on.  Well, seven years later, and onwards to Windows XP and my own custom built beast of a desktop, I found myself wanting a laptop that I could haul around and type papers on.  Being at school, I didn't always find it comfortable to type papers at my desk in my dorm, so some poking around on eBay landed me an IBM Thinkpad R40.  Though it's certainly not as powerful as my desktop, it gets the job done.  I could even play Quake III or Half-Life mods on it.

At any rate, I was getting rather bored with Windows, and had been thinking about undertaking a Linux project for myself, and when I say 'project' I really mean just getting a distro up and running that I could play with for a hobby.  Anyhow, I finally took the plunge, using my laptop as the Linux machine, I could always fall back on my more powerful (and vastly more spacious) Windows desktop.  I tried everything from Ubuntu to Gentoo to FreeBSD (Unix-based, yes I know), but always found myself coming back to Ubuntu.  I suppose at heart I was looking for more of a fight but could never stay away from the distro that just worked.  I think with other distros I was spending way too much time fighting with the install process rather than with application issues.  So, I left Breezy on my laptop for the remainder of the schoolyear, even entrusting perhaps 4 papers to it.

With a summer job away from home and the rest of my family still riding it out on that aging '99 HP, I left my Windows box at home for the family to use, and took my laptop along with me.  Of course I've upgraded to Dapper, and being without Windows for some time has really brought me to appreciate this distro.  I'm planning on installing Ubuntu on my desktop once I get back to it, which will be several months from now.

I do miss Windows' somewhat easier accessibility, but I also like the fact that Ubuntu puts up a fight now and again.  It gives my computing a little bit more excitement.  I'm always discovering new things, and I feel like I'm back with something a bit more quirky and somehow amazingly charming (like my old Performa, looking back).  Windows was just feeling a bit too bland, and Ubuntu's been such a breath of fresh air. =D>
