### Post by SE7EN-LOCSTA on 2011-02-24
i have never been a programmer/coder/hacker, and i cant design webpages. i first started using computers at the age of 19, using windows me for a bit, then moving to xp. over the years, ive taught myself (trial and error, google, etc) what i need to know to fix stuff. i am the person these 'oh my god i got a virus cause i checked my email!!! what do i do???' people around me come to when they have a problem.90% of the time, i can fix it, and after fixing it, i give them some a/v, and tell them stuff not to do... though they do it anyways, and i see them again  AAARGH!!!

  ive always hadda a p.o.s. computer (not terrible, but i'm a gamer, and a p4 with 1gb ram and a ge force 5200 just didnt cut it) but i have never had the problems these people had. ive never gotten a virus (that i know of). if ive reformatted, it was of my own accord, and i had my old program's install files sitting in a backup. i just do it every so often to kind of cleanup what i have. 

about a year ago, i bought a new computer, spent every dollar i had. i consider it a beast (maybe not to some serrious computer people, but im poor and it runs like a champ) specs are in sig. i added a 2tb hd and a new cpu cooler this year. been running windows 7 only until a few months ago. i have never had a problem with it. i bluescreened a coupe of times, but that was my fault. my cpu cooler and power supply were not adequate, and i knew it, but i still tried to pump out the newest games at ultra graphics and max out the AA. 

 then along came the mac people. use a mac, its soo much better, they told me. ummmm, no. but curiosity took hold. i had only ever used windows. could other be soo much better, and i just didnt know? i stumbled across this other os, i had heard about before, this linux. 'linux is for people with elite computer skills' i saw. than i came across ubuntu... linux for human beings??? hmmm. i installed it, gave it a lil spin, but because i am a gamer, it sat there alongside win7, not being used. so simple to get these programs from the software center tho, i kept it, and booted into it a few times, trying to get the 'feel' of it. 

  well, what if i want something not in the software center? so i searched for some info, and got me a lil tarball of some lil program i didnt really want (it would aggravate me less if i couldnt install it that way) done the command line extract and went to make.. makefile not found... ummm, im looking right at it, wth? im going back to windows. i still kept ubuntu installed.. telling myself, one day, ill learn this 'elite computer crap'.

i moved. the house we are at now has some electrical issues, where not enough power is flowing thru the wires. bad news for my beast. the ps3 runs fine, but my big bad computer... well it bluescreens if i try to watch a hd movie on it. not enough power to have everything at full, the fans spin too slow, the cpu heats up, and boom, i crash. my poor beast went from a gaming/multimedia center to a *^%*^ web browser, that still gets to hot after about an hour. i got to thinking... if it stays on just enough to be on, but overheats for hd movies and gaming... what if perhaps there was some way to lower the power needs? i turned my starcraft 2 to low graphics, and increased my time before crash by @10 minutes. 

needless to say, a lightbulb went off. 'i bet ubuntu needs less to run on then windows 7 with all the extras and aero view and stuff'. i was right. i no longer have @ 1hour time limit to my computer on... and if i keep it on low graphics, and keep the computer case open, i can run starcraft 2 from wine without overheating. 

about a month has went by, and ive installed all the programs i might want, gotten kubuntu, xubuntu, and lubuntu packages (just to check them out) and have only logged into windows a couple times for photoshop. (i know linux has gimp.. but im familiar with photoshop.. i wanted to do picture work then, not learn a new system, and then get to work) because i cant play games anyways, i dont miss windows.

i dont know what will happen once i get the power issue straightened out. ill have to be on windows for my games, but i like linux, and want to learn more. ubuntu is great for computer illiterate people, who only check their email, get on facebook, and use instant messengers. they wont get the virii and spyware from 'you won click here!' stuff and altho they probably wont be installing many programs for other stuff, they werent going to anyways. linux is also for the 'computer elite' those that know their way around source code and c++, the kind of stuff that stumps people like me. linux is really not for that middle class, the gamers, the people who know enough about computers to not get into trouble, and can fix what does go wrong... the people like me, but i have that thirst for knowledge when it comes to computers, that makes me want to join the 'computer elite', and i know that i will be using ubuntu for a long time.
