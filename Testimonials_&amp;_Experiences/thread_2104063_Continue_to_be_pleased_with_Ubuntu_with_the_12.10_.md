### Post by davesalyers on 2013-01-11
I'm been using Ubuntu since 8.04 with the Gnome desktop environment and subsequently migrated to Xubuntu when Unity first came on board. I just recently upgraded from Xubuntu 11.04 to Xubuntu 12.10. This was a clean install and I had no problems or issues with the installation. 

While we still primarily use Xubuntu as we are used to the efficiency of the XFCE desktop environment and have older hardware, I really like the changes that have been made to Unity which have won me over from being a former Unity "hater". I've converted my parents into Xubuntu users (this helps as my mother was getting her computer inundated with viruses and other malware when they were using Windows) so I will be upgrading their system up to 12.10 as well when I go down there.

My kids have been exposed to both Xubuntu Linux OS at home as well as Windows (mostly at school, but I do have a dual-boot system). It warms my heart when my kids (ages 13 and 14) tell me how much they prefer Xubuntu over Windows especially in terms of speed and ease of usage.

Xubuntu not only saves us money on the cost of OS and application software as well as extended the life of our computers by being able to use lighter desktop environments which require less resources as our hardware got older so we could save for that purchase. Applications like Abiword and LibreOffice have allowed both the kids and I to do work at home and save it in a format which is usable at school or the office.

I have noticed some issues with installing Flash this time around for Firefox (Adobe did not like the Flash installer) so I had to go with Chrome as Flash is already integrated. I'm sure those bugs will be worked out over time anyways or I'll find the solution.

Overall though Xubuntu/Ubuntu continues to meet our needs and usually pleasantly exceeds our expectations.

Kudos to all of the developers and others who keep Xubuntu/Ubuntu alive!
