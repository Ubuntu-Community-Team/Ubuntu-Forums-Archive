### Post by navic101 on 2008-06-15
I've been an experienced windows user since W98 1st ed, and have never thought of ever changing. I have stuck with XP for 2 Years+ now. Have been on the whole happy but for the ever increasing installation of EULA and infringement of what I WANT TO USE on the OS and the ever increasing requirement on hardware resources. I had heard much of Linux and the inprovements with the new " Hardy Heron" issue of Unbuntu mainly from computer mags. With the use of try before you commit virtual CD.

I gave it a try and dipped my toe and here are MY personal findings as a "newbie" just less than 1 month in. I tried puppy Linux 1st as it was so small and was quick to load and burn. OMG very impressed. So my progession to try Unbuntu.

1.There are inital issues with the display settings but these are easily sorted and a download ( which is automatic) sorts this. Forums are excellent and this issue comes up about every 2-3 hours lol. Just ask someone and they will help. It helps if you are polite! they are not PAID technical support. Remember this IS FREE!!

2. I had to get over the fact you don't need virus and firewall software. This does kinda take a leap of faith! Its all built in and i think the only real risk is cutting and pasting script lines into terminal ( its like DOS for you newbie XPs) If you are unsure of a script ask for another member to check its OK. Thats what i do!

3. Its very stable. No BSOD for unbuntu.

4. The layout is quite different and it does take abit of getting used too. Also there are more hands on do you want to run this and with what?
This intially seems like why won't it work but actually its MORE control to you as YOU decide what is what. Terminal 

5. Firefox. I LOVE! its so fast and easy. Once you get to grips with the add-ons. See my post toady. I am still learning! Its a pleasure to use.

6. Open office (its XP office but free) no bells and whistles. Kinda like office 98 but with add-ons. Quick, capable, and no BSOD

7. Updates are automatic and YOU decide what is instlalled. You don't like the sound of an item. Unlclick it. 

8. Last but not least the forums. An indispensible guide to any problems. Do not hesitate to post. These guys and girls ARE here to help. Remember though your software IS FREE _and so is your support_ so be patient and polite. You get far with these rules. 

9. Patience. The OS is not perfect_ and it is different_ so you do need to persevere and learn! I am still doing so..slowly!

Hope this may have been some use to some complete newbies as i once was 4 weeks ago
