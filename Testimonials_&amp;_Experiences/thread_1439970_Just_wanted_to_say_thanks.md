### Post by XPM on 2010-03-26
Having not been able to use ubuntu, or any other linux distro that is, to it's full capacity for over a year now due to my ATI hard lockups (Opensource and FGLRX) and my DWA-160 not working at all no matter what the tweaks I applied to it and custom editing.

Ubuntu 10.04 supports my 5770's in crossfire (Previous 4850X2, different motherboard and the lot) and my Phenom II 955 with 300 FSB running at 3.6 GHZ is running ubuntu so fast, It barely manages to refresh the screen before I'm automatically logged in.

My DWA-160 worked without a hitch after a kernel update (Updated all packages through synaptic) and my 5770's finally works with ATI's own driver (Included control panel).
My Widescreen monitor also works (It didnt before want to rise above 1440x600) and is properly identified, instead of being shown as UNKNOWN.

All running at 1920x1080.
Computing has never been this good :)

Thank you for 10.04 Beta.

Please don't screw it up now :)
It's running smoother then Windows 7 :)
It's so snappy it's unbelivable!
It runs off an SSD for it to be this fast ofcourse..

Video starts buffering/playing instantly as I press on any video! Good going team!
