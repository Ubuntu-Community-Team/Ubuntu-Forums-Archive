### Post by dmizer on 2009-04-27
While I understand your frustration at damaging a monitor, a screensaver is not a necessary function of your computer. You should have simply left the default "blank screen" setting in the screen saver, especially after having experienced several previous lockups related to your screensaver.

The screen savers have always been a problem (even in Windows). This is why default screen saver settings are configured for "blank screen". Fixing screensavers is hardly a priority compared to other reported problems.

Also, if your monitor burned in after only a half-day, I suspect it was already on its way out, or the burn in may be cleared by degaussing the monitor. Monitor burn in is cumulative and usually takes about at least [week of non-uniform usage](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phosphor_burn-in).

I find it odd that you would denounce a perfectly useful OS simply because a gimmick didn't work right. While this doesn't excuse the faulty behavior of the gimmick, it shouldn't be used as a gauge by which you measure the entire OS.
