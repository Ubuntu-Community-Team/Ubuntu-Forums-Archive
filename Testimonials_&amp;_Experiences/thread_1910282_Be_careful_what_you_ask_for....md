### Post by 37fleetwood on 2012-01-16
Remember the old saying, "Be careful what you ask for, you just might get it."?
Well, when I switched to Linux from Windows, I loved the OS but Gnome 2 was a bit plain. I wished for someone to spruce it up, make it look better. Well I guess I got my wish... sort of...
Now I'm not sure what the future holds for me. I don't like either Gnome 3 or Unity. I never have liked KDE. LXDE and Xfce are decent but again unspectacular in what you can do with it. Gnome was my favorite. Why couldn't they just improve on what already worked so well, spruce it up a bit. make behind the scene upgrades. instead it's totally different, not a progression but a jerk in a different direction that is clearly not working as well as what was available before. now everyone is scrambling to patch and hack Gnome 3 to make it work.

so, what am I going to do? for now, I don't like Ubuntu with Unity, all the hacked up alternatives are just that, hacked up junk. my temptation is to move back a step and do a clean install of the current release of Debian. many people are moving to Mint, but I was thinking, isn't Mint built on Ubuntu, and isn't Ubuntu built on Debian? why not cut out all the middlemen and go straight to Debian and wait out all the drama. eventually things will settle down and Gnome will get straightened out... or not, but at least I'll have a better idea what to do.

if anyone cares what my opinion is, Please work on things that make the WM better rather than trying to come up with novel ways of doing things that make no sense. it's a desktop PC, not a smart phone.
my advice to the people at Gnome, work on getting Nautilus more fully functional. Windows has been able to sort files by artist, album genre, and a whole host of other criteria.  once everything works, then make some eyecandy upgrades, but make sure you're not replacing chocolate cake with boiled cabbage.

### Post by 37fleetwood on 2012-01-17
well thanks for the responses...I guess.:confused:
I'm more of an end user and I figure that if I think of something someone else smarter than I am has probably already addressed it. as can be seen in the post by mastablasta. why this wouldn't be preinstalled is anyone's guess.
as to what I expect a de to do, I guess Gnome 2 is the simple answer, I like to be able to find the open programs, and files easily without having to hunt them down. I like having the web browser running on one desktop and a media player on another and I want to quickly click on a switcher and watch the desktop spin around to the correct one in one click. I want to find the program I want without having to have 25 things pinned to the side panel, and I want to do it quickly. my fear is that I still don't know where this is going. at least with Debian, things seem to move more slowly maybe all the drama of Gnome shell/Unity/Mate/MGSE/Cinnamon...etc... will be over before I'm forced into one I can't stand.

### Post by keithpeter on 2012-01-17
Hello 37fleetwood and all

Gnome Shell and Unity are aimed at commodity devices and 'casual' users I suppose. The various design documents focus on uses like music, photos, web surfing, e-mail, social.

Basically its your choice: 

1) wait it out until something to your taste gets built on the new libraries

2) join in with the development, in the right places, and keep reminding people that there are a significant number of users who have larger screens, more than one screen, and desktop PCs

If you are going for 1) then Debian is ace on a desktop PC. CentOS 6.2 is nice as well with support for the probable lifetime of your current hardware.
