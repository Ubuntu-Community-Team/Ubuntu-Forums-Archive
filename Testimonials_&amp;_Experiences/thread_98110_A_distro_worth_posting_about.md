### Post by sgacity on 2005-12-02
With all of this talk in the news about Ubuntu being good for newbies, I felt compelled to post my most recent experience with Unbuntu.  (Specifically, the Breezy version of Kubuntu).

Now, let's set a little context here, first.  I've been in the field for 20 years now, the vast majority of it spent working with various Unices.  I loaded Linux 1.? on my PC, way back in 1994.  I've loaded more versions of Mandrake, RedHat and Gentoo than I can comfortably count.  I even have a machine running SoL.  I'm not exactly what you would call a newbie.

And I have a new favorite distribution.

A few weeks ago, I inheritted an old machine with some hardware issues from a friend.  I thought I would repair it, and reload it as a Linux box for my kids to use for homework.  (We have a machine that they use for that, this would be a secondary to accomodate congestion. ;)

2 weeks.  For 2 weeks I spent my evenings loading, recompiling, reloading and so forth Gentoo.  Mind you, I *like* Gentoo.  Finally, it came down to the video driver.  It's an old machine with onboard video.  After going through the stuff, I couldn't get the video driver right.  I went online, apparently, lots of other people were having the same troubles.  Just wonderful.

Well, I'd heard about Ubuntu recently in the online news and thought I would try it.  I mean, it couldn't hurt, right?  So, I plugged the CD in, told it to install, and went to get myself some dinner.

15 minutes later -- less time than it took me to cook, let alone eat, dinner -- I was staring at a happy little KDE login screen.  The system was fully functional -- video and all -- and with all of the software my kids would need installed by default.  So, I admit that I spent another couple of hours after that carefully tuning and upgrading the installation.  (Hey, I'm an old geek.  I tune things. ;)

I went out and bought a cheap wireless network card.  Silly me, I forgot to check the comatibility lists.  Plugged the card in.  It didn't seem to work.  Again, I went online looking for answers.  Come to find out, it was an RT2500-based card.  Every other distro I saw, used ndiswrapper to let you use the windows driver.  Nope, none of that here.  Ubtuntu supports it out of the box, and the wiki had ever-so-proper step-by-step instructions for how to get it working.  Followed the instructions.  It worked.

That's it.  Done.  It just worked.

Folks, I am impressed.  And, that is something that doesn't happen easily.
