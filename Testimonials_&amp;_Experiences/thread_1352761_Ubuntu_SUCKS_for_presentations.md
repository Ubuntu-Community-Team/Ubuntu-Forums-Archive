### Post by Doughy on 2009-12-12
I love Ubuntu, it's the only OS that I use.  But, it really really really sucks when it comes to doing presentations on external projectors.  I'm working on a PhD, and over the course of the last 2 years, I've had to give some presentations on my research.  Here's what went wrong.

My first presentation would not send a signal to the projector.  I plugged it in, pushed all the buttons I knew about, etc... nothing.  I finally had to log back in with the cable connected to get a signal.  At the end of my presentation, the video I had embedded in my OpenOffice presentation crashed.  Great...  The whole thing was a joke.

At my next conference, I THOUGHT I had everything running perfectly.  I had the projector working before it was my turn, and I thought I was past the problems.  When it was my turn, I couldn't get the full screen to output to the monitor.  I had to make the window a smaller size so it would fit on the projector and the slides were not even being run in "presentation mode."  OK, this really irked me.

Today, another presentation.  Actually this one was a demo.  I had the video all pulled up, everything set up, ready to go.  Then, I plugged in the cable, and everything froze.  I couldn't move the mouse, couldn't switch to another TTY, nothing.  I had to hold the power button down and reboot. I tried again and again, it never worked.  Turns out, Ubuntu 9.10 has a known bug on this.

This is absolutely pathetic.  I wish I was in a position to contribute to fix these bugs, but I'm not.  I just don't have that kind of expertise. I HEREBY DECLARE THAT UBUNTU SUCKS FOR PRESENTATIONS.  BAD.

Frustrated,
-Doughy

### Post by Glucklich on 2009-12-13
Actually, it is better and more reliable when connecting to a projector when you know what to do. I've seen many Windows computers fail this test.

So, here goes your answer you've been waiting for...
First, I only know the trick for computers with nVidia graphical support. If you have an ATI, well... I'm sorry but I won't be able to help you.

Open the 'nVidia Settings' (wherever that is on your Operating System), then go to 'X Server Display Configuration', click on 'Detect Displays' and now you got two screens on the map. Select the new one and on 'Configuration' select 'Twin-View'. The wallpapers will be different but don't worry about that. Put the new device's screen on top of the regular computer's screen (and center it if necessary), click on 'Apply' and voilá, you should have image on both and able to do your thing. I might have skipped some steps or made a mistake somewhere but the general idea is there. You know... because sometimes we don't want to reboot. Or because we're in a worry or because we have some external pressure to get on with it.

You can test this procedure at your home with an HDMI cable and your television. That is, if both your computer and television are equipped to support it.
