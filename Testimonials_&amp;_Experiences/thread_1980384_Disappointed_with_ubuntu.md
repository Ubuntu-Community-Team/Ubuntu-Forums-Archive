### Post by samwillc on 2012-05-15
Hi everyone,

So the high hopes are slowly fading away as I discover more and more stuff just doesn't work in ubuntu. I love the look of the UI, unity is superb IMO, it's fast, but the cracks are appearing. Namely:

1) Printer drivers, I have nowhere near the number of options I had with windows. I can just about duplex but no advanced settings whatsoever, kind of makes it pointless having such a good printer. **edit** and this is using canon downloaded linux drivers, not generic ones.

2) No graphic equalizer, realtek HD audio drivers with equalizer was so simple to install in windows.

I mean it's not all bad, code::blocks runs fine, gedit, terminal, firefox, libreoffice, I don't really use much more than that. I wish there was a way to get the above fixed so I could enjoy my linux experience.

I'm really so disappointed because I can't stand the awful win7 UI either with those horrible window gradients (and equally lame transparency) and sh*tty 80s looking maximise/close buttons. I see lots of happy users, do they all just 'do without' decent printer software or decent sound? How do users overcome the clear software difference?

Any suggestions are gratefully received! Windows is dragging me back to hell with my tail between my legs!

Sam.
