### Post by Kemono on 2008-03-27
A while (about a month) ago I installed Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon. I don't remember exactly when I did it and how I found Ubuntu (forgive my 14 Kb memory but I just don't pay attention to dates much). On my first installation I managed to partition my hard drive ( I'm still stuck with Windows XP because of a 56.0 Kb file) and successfully installed the System, the Swap and the Home to separate partitions (although I followed some instructions, they weren't very clear about some parts). This proves that even someone like me, who's only heard of the Linux word through a Red Hat installation book that scared the s**t out of me, can install a modern Linux distribution without a problem. I do have some basic knowledge about software (only on the Windows side) and hardware which I think also helped a bit. Funny thing is, I never even checked if my system was compatible with Ubuntu. I just put the disc in my DVD drive and installed it. 

Next thing I know I'm at the boot screen looking at some options that say Ubuntu *something* Generic *something* Kernel *something*. I figured &#8220;Well, it's got to be the first one. I mean... the second one says recovery mode and the third one doesn't look like an OS (don't I just have a great judgment?). Besides, there's no logic in letting the computer boot into something that's not an OS after 10 seconds when started.&#8221; Fortunately I didn't forget my password. Ubuntu detected all of my hardware(boy, ain't I lucky?), so I was good to go. 

Of course, not everything was working out so smooth. I've had some serious problems with the video drivers. Once I (thought I) f**ked up so bad I reinstalled Ubuntu **within the first week**. I've never installed an OS twice within a week(except that one time when I installed Windows four times in one night. Boy, was that weird and scary). I really wanted those cool Compiz effects. On a side note: I wasn't attracted to Linux by the eye-candy of Compiz. I didn't know about it until after I installed Ubuntu. Anyway, they're all working fine now. Sort of. Well, I have an ATI card, so you do the math. Whatever other problems I've had, I found the solutions on these forums and the internet easily. Sure, it's still a little rough around the edges, but the development of an open-source OS or application is much faster. In a couple of years, with the support of  governments and agencies all around the world, it will be even easier. In my opinion, a Windows machine is easier to setup (mainly because of the better hardware support) but harder to maintain, where it's the exact opposite with Linux. Most of the time I have reinstalled Windows is because of broken libraries and/or registries. To this date I still don't download updates (they have a way of messing up my system).

I wish I could help with the programing of the software but I don't know any languages whatsoever. Yet. Currently I'm trying to learn Blender. The plan is to contribute whole training kits one day.

I have one question though: Where can I see my video memory? I recently installed POP The Warrior Within. It was a no go though. Right after the system detection window a black screen and my system freezes. What attracted my attention was in the detection window. See, I have an ATI Radeon 9600 with 256 Mb of memory (unless my friend, the PC vendor and Windows are lying), but in the window I saw 128 Mb. Is this what Wine sees or is it Ubuntu?

I see a bright future for Ubuntu and its community.
