### Post by cariboo on 2009-01-11
I learned a long time ago, save regularly and often. I worked for a market research firm back in the mid 80's when a 5Mb hard drive was half of the price of a $2000.00 computer, so we didn't have a lot of computers with hard drives. Of the 6 computers in the office only two had hard drives and the rest had 2 5¼ floopy drive. I was covering for one of the Data entry clerks and had been working for an hour entering data without saving, and I somehow managed to kick the power cord out of the wall socket. Of course everything was lost. After spending the next hour re-entering data, I went off to lunch, came back nad did exactly the same thing again.

Since then I always save at minimum every 2-3 minutes and have at least 3 backups of every thing.

Jim

### Post by mesmith on 2009-01-17
Thought I'd follow up.  I have spent 40 years in IT.  Mainframes (IBM, Honeywell, Univac, etc.) right on down to micros.  Xubuntu is small, fast and, for me, stable.

I made some changes to the desktop display via panels.  Added a nifty datetime plugin, went with K3B for CD burning and VLC for multimedia.  Added System Profiler and Benchmark (missed SysInfo on Windows), and a few other odds and ends.

I just love this thing!  Highly recommended.  Running on an old Dell Dimension 3000 with 1GB memory and an old Western Digital 20GB HD.  Dell had delivered this machine with a single bay for HD's, but a pal at Google told me that they use velcro tape for stacking HD's in servers.  I tried it after getting a ribbon cable with two connectors.  Works just great, with no discernable vibration or noise.  Also added a new 350w psu (StarTech).

Mike

### Post by albinootje on 2009-01-17
> **jrock612 said:**
> Ctrl+Alt+Backspace didn't work, mouse, keyboard...all dead.  Had to hard reboot.  

Well.. that's possible, 

A friend of mine told me that you can check with caps-lock or num-lock key whether the keyboard control is really completely gone..

But.. before you do a hard reboot (holding power button for > 5 seconds rather than pulling the electricity cable), you can try the Magic SysRq key combination in the future.

In recent Ubuntu releases you will have to adjust the "Alt + Print" in the Keyboard Shortcuts first before being able to use this.

[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-use-magic-system-request-keys-in-ubuntu-linux.html](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-use-magic-system-request-keys-in-ubuntu-linux.html)

Perhaps not needed for a desktop user, but for a sysadmin in some office this could be useful since i can imagine that possibly more information in the logfiles will be saved by this.
