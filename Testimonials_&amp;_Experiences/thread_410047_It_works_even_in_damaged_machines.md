### Post by emmerc on 2007-04-15
Hello everyone, 

Let me share my experience.

In september 2005 I had a real crash in my Desktop (Clon, PIV 2.0 GHz, 160GB HD, 512 ram) The hard drive got physical problems as well as the memory stick. Later in november something similar happen with my laptop (Toshiba Satellite Pro 6100, 60GB HD, 512 ram), the hard drive got physical problems. I was in the middle of my semester (PhD). 

The machines were not able to even load windows nor install it. So a friend of mine gave me an Ubuntu 5.10 CD. I insalled it smoothly in both machines and I used these machines by months until I was able to buy the HD's and the memory stick. I was able to write my papers in Open Office and the machines were fully functional.  

Three weeks ago a friend on mine had problems with his laptop and he got a new HD but still WinXP gave him troubles with his 5 years-old Laptop. Then we installed Ubuntu 6.10 and the machine is fully functional and my friend quite satisfied. 

I really recommend Ubuntu for old, defectous and even new machines,

Hello!!! Let me give you some updates about my machines.

I sold my Clone PC and my toshiba Satellite Pro 6100 is working fully and exclusively with Ubuntu 8.04. Recently its cd-rom broke.This machine will boot only from HD, Cd-rom and network. I needed to install something on it so I took the HDD out and installed it on another Laptop (This was an Acer Aspire, old model) then I installed Ubuntu 8.04, modified the /etc/X11/xorg.conf to match the Toshiba 6100, shut down the Acer, took my drive back to the toshiba and it is working perfectly at full throttle! :-) Two thumbs up for Ubuntu!!! I installed wine and BibleWorks 6.0 is working quite well. Bibleworks 7 is buggy. 

Now I am buying an Acer AS6920-6621 and, most probably, it will run fully on Ubuntu 8.04.
