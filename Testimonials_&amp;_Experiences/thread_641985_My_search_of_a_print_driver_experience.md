### Post by azimuth on 2007-12-16
I received the following e-mail and just had to share it with the community.


"Thank you for contacting Canon product support.  We value you as a  Canon
customer and appreciate the opportunity to assist you.

We regret that we do not support Linux with the IP6210D printer.  While
considering the desire to provide the best possible support for Canon's
products, Canon must make decisions on which products to support when 
new operating systems are introduced.  Currently, Canon has decided to 
support only the Microsoft Windows and the Macintosh operating systems.
 
We understand, and sincerely apologize for any frustration you have 
experienced if your are using another operating system, but we hope that
you understand our rationale.

We hope this information is helpful to you.  Please let us know if we 
can be of any further assistance with your IP6210D.

Thank you for choosing Canon."


I am sure it would come as a great supprise to Linus Torvalds that Linux is a "new operating system".

My first first reaction was anger (even if it was roughly the answer I expected). It took all of 2 minutes for the anger to be replaced with laughter. These folks don't understand that for the price of ink in this thing, I can replace it with a printer from another vender that will not only come full of ink, but will also support Linux. I hope they "understand MY rationale".

Try contacting their customer support, not technical support.  Be ready to give them the make and model of the printer you are considering purchasing, due to their competitor's Linux support.  Give them a fixed time to resolve the problem.  After that time, make your purchase and email them again to let them know their competitor's solution worked for you.  When customer support gets emails indicating they are losing paying customers to their competitors, then they may take action.  Technical support simply just doesn't care about providing support outside the product's stated guidelines.

	I am more of a passive personality. It is not my intent to persuade a manufacturer to support any OS. Big companies are like politicians, they do not respond to individual comments, but they sure as hell pay attention to polls or in the corporate world, market share. 
	Printer manufacturers work on the same business model that made Gillette famous and rich. At the beginning of the last century, Gillette gave away his new safety razor and then got rich selling the "expendable" blades. With the exception of maybe the very high end printers, the printer is a profit loss throw away and the  over priced ink is the real product.
	Just like I don't care who built my throw away printer, the printer company doesn't  care who uses it, as long as I keep buying their ink. My old Epson Stylus C80 prints along very happily on Linux. Take a guess as to who gets to sell me ink? The big companies have armies of market analysts watching ink sales data. When the data becomes apparent that a competitor's Linux friendly models are gaining market share, they "get it". No amount of my bitching and moaning  or threats can produce the results that yours and my dollars can. 
	If you really want to make a difference, I say only buy Linux friendly hardware and software. That's the new policy in this household.

Disclaimer: This is just my opinion, based on 6 decades of observation.
