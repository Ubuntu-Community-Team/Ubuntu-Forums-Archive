### Post by ghandi69_ on 2007-04-13
Ok, I have been reading posts on this site for a while, and one of my pet peeves are when users have come on here and basically state how their 3 day trial of ubuntu didn't go as planned, then also detailing how much linux sucks and how great windows is.  Unfortunately, I'm in some ways going to do the same thing on this thread, but I'll try to do it in a more constructive way.

I had been using Ubuntu, windows free, for about a year and half.  I had gotten a few of my "necessary" windows programs to work under wine, and had gotten the system pretty muchtweaked to my liking... However, there were a few issues that I just never got over, and last night I gave my free version of vista my school game and a try, mainly just for something different.  

Issues that I think are difficult to overcome with linux, that at least held me back and hope to get irons out in the future.

1.) Wireless... I had the same wireless card for about 1 year and half that I just was never able to get up and working.  Could an expert have done this?? probably, but it just should have started much easier

2.) Updates... Now, I know windows has just as many updates as ubuntu to download, well, not quite as many, but a lot, and I've never had one that crashed my system like ubuntu updates do.  Whenever my computer seemed to not have any problems for a month or so, sure enough some x11 core update would come along and render my computer completely gui free until I was able to go in through the terminal and fix the issue.  I don't think a recommended update should EVER be able to do that to users system, especially not once every 3 months.... Now I'm guessing thats not the norm, but I had a new nvidia graphics card that was pretty well supported, so it should have worked better than it did.

3.) Stabability ....  Supposively this is a strength of linux, but for me, whenever I would leave my computer on without restarting for a couple days, it would start to act haunted.  I would have some issues where sometimes it would act like my mouse was constantly "clicking", and would open up whatever I would scroll over, making it very difficult to even open a menu.  Restarting would fix that issue.  Sometimes, no matter what window I opened, I would not be able to scroll down using the right hand side scroll bar it would constantly try to scroll back to the top....., and sometimes, my sound would just quit working after being left on for a couple days, and no "alsamixer" settings would be able to bring it back.  All of these things seemed to only solved by rebooting, and they would always creep up if I left it running for more than 2 or 3 days. 

4.) Speed.  I know vista is supposed to be a hog, but it runs WAY faster on my current rig than ubuntu desktop did.  I experimented with fluxbox and all those window managers, but windows manages to look slick and be extremely fast, so I can have my cake and eat it too.  

5.) Games... enough has been said on that issue.
I feel icky inside

Overall though.. I still love all the free software, the development features, and no worrying about anti-virus software.  And I will be back to linux I'm sure in the next few months.. but just some things I thought I would vent about.
