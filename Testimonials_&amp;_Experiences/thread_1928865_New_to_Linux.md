Words of wisdom.

Anytime Linux has an opportunity to attract new users, IMHO it's a good  thing. The gaming user has been on Linux's back burner for quite some  time, perhaps it is time to invest in attracting more of them. From a  standpoint of marketing I think it makes sense, assuming Linux truly wishes  to compete.

From my experience there's quite a few gamers out there. I've even  noticed that many PC sales sites have been offering high end gaming PC's  for quite some time. Which potentially may create additional issues  with respect to high end hardware compatibility and driver performance. Investing in this particular group may actually benefit the community, by placing emphasis on improving the over all performance and stability of Linux distros.

Of course this also requires an investment in creating substantially more high quality, competitive games for Linux. Or a much simpler and convenient way to port Windows games (assuming the DirectX issue can be over come).
