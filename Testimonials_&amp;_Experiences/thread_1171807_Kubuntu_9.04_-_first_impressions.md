### Post by Rechinica on 2009-05-27
Hello,

This is my experience while trying to change from Win to Linux. Downloaded the x64 version of Kubuntu 9.0. The install went smooth, i was quite pleased.

The video
I have a nvidia 8800GTX card, so i've enabled the recommended proprietary driver. After restart "hardware driver" reported that the nvidia X driver was in use, but nVidia settings disagreed. No luck in enabling desktop effects. Several drivers uninstalls and activates later i've give up. I won't be using desktop effects and that's it.

The sound
So i've downloaded the creative driver for my X-FI. Everything went smooth, and the card installed just fine. But no sound. After a couple other restarts i got a startup sound. WOW! victory at last! But no, i've got no sound from Amarok. Exit and startup sound, but that's it! Tried to play a couple of movies, different formats - of course no luck here either. Blank screen or not starting to play at all from that dragon player.

Mouse
I have a Razer Lachesis mouse. While i wasn't expecting to have all the features from Windows, i hoped to have at least a normal sensitivity. But it was insanely high, and setting it to minimum values had almost no effect at all.

Network
Everything went ok here, no complaints.

Bottom line is IT SHOULDN'T BE THIS HARD!
Yeah probably there are fixes for all the problems above, with internet searches, file editing and console commands, but we're in 2009! 

I give up on desktop Linux once again, and i would never recommend this to another person.
I'm writing happily from my Win7 OS, hoping that the next time i will try Linux things will be different.

Sorry if this post bothers someone.

### Post by Neo Adventist on 2009-05-28
Nvidia graphics:  did you try install the newest driver directly from Nvidia's website, or did you use the driver that came with Ubuntu?  Please explain your situation.  ATI Radeon cards have well-established open-source support, and RadeonHD 2000-4000 chipsets should have full 3D hardware acceleration in the next 3-4 months.  In general, proprietary graphics drivers tend to cause headaches.

Creative X-Fi:  until VERY recently, X-Fi cards have had ZERO support for Linux.  New driver source-code has been added to the ALSA driver package, but I don't think its in a usable state yet. Sorry.  If you want an excellent audio adapter for Linux (and Windows, too!), I recommend the HT OMEGA Striker 7.1

Razer Lachesis:  I use a Logitech Trackball mouse, so I'm afraid I can't comment on your Razer.  

It seems all of your Linux woes are hardware related, and a quick search on compatability would have helped tremendously.  

Forgive me if I sound harsh, but it seems like you posted this in order to whine, rather than actually ask for help.

Linux is not Windows, but that does not make it hard. In my experience, good hardware compatability erases 98% of all problems. And since Linux occupies only <2% of the desktop marketshare, some hardware vendors refuse to support Linux.  This is unfortunate but sadly unavoidable for now.

We at ubuntuforums are more than willing to help you in any way we can.  Asking for assistance before becoming frustrated is a great way to keep you blood pressure down and you sanity in check!  No one will judge you for keeping Windows, but going around complaining, "Linux sucks, I can't figure it out" after doing zero research tends to irritate.
