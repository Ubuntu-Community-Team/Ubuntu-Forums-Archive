### Post by Jimleko211 on 2009-07-20
I recently installed Ubuntu onto my grandma's computer (keeping Windows on it so she felt secure, so she has dual boot), and she absolutely loves it.  Open Office solves all her poetry writing woes that she had on Windows, and she loves how the Aislerot Solitaire has so many games in it.

She loves how it's simple to launch applications, and she was really happy that she's able to customize it (mostly her "customizations" were making sure that her name was the username, but that's besides the point).

Overall, Ubuntu is simply perfect for an old woman that needs to be walked step by step to access AOL.  Once I beat that into her head she'll be good to go.

Thanks to the Ubuntu Forums for teaching me so much so that I could make her move possible!

[AHEM]  Ubuntu is easy for a grandmother with an Ubuntu-knowledgable grandson who knows how to open a terminal.  Does this grandmother you speak of understand this line, which is quoted from medibuntu, which is basically required to use your computer?:
sudo wget http://www.medibuntu.org/sources.list.d/`lsb_release -cs
`.list --output-document=/etc/apt/sources.list.d/medibuntu.list;
 sudo apt-get -q update; sudo apt-get --yes -q --allow-unauthenticated 
install medibuntu-keyring; sudo apt-get -q update

I'll bet not.  This kind of gibberish and gobbledygook is essentially required, since you can't do any media without it, and it takes some Geek-horsepower, since the people that put this operating system together don't feel it is necessary to make it any simpler under the hood.  

My wife, who is quite savvy about things digital, hasn't ever opened a terminal yet because I fix all that stuff.  If I got hit by a flying Moon rock tomorrow, the Ubuntu computer would swiftly go in the trash, as it wouldn't work for very long for someone who isn't Terminal-literate (I hardly qualify myself).  

A Geek was talking to us the other day, I described a problem my wife was happening, and he said to her "Simple - just type sudo whachamahigger whambam -R -Deadfrog"  She looked at him like he had antennae sprouting from his head, and I explained that she wasn't ready to sudo anything, let alone that stuff, having never cracked open a terminal yet.   

Ther learning curve on the graphical interfaces is not too bad - a few things are prtty wierd, some things easy.  It's the errors ("you don't have rights to do that!") or things that just quit working before the comoputer is set up, the stuff that doesn't work right out of the box that gets Gramma, unless Grandson is there in the background.
