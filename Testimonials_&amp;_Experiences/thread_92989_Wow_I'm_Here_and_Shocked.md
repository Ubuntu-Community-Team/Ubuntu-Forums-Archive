### Post by aysiu on 2005-11-21
I think you have some legitimate gripes, and I'm coming from a similar place--Mepis was what got me into Linux to begin with. I really didn't see what the big hubbub about Ubuntu was.

The us.archive.ubuntu.com gpg errors are annoying, and I have to troubleshoot those in these forums just about every day (see my sig). And Breezy in general has not been pleasant--all sorts of automount problems in both KDE and Gnome. Hoary ran perfectly fine from May to October of this year. I don't know if it had similar kinks in April when it was released.

Bottom line for me (and why I use Ubuntu instead of Mepis): the support at the Ubuntu forums is top-notch--people are friendly, helpful, and almost immediately responsive. I get answers within **minutes** most times. Sure, some 0-replies slip through the cracks, but that's bound to happen, especially for some more obscure problems.

To those that are straight from Windows, I actually recommend Mepis as a first distro, but Ubuntu is an excellent second distro--not too complex, not too difficult, but also highly (and easily) configurable for people who aren't afraid of copying and pasting a few commands into the terminal.

### Post by Brunellus on 2005-11-21
[QUOTE=mephisto56]Do you think ubuntu is a hype and draw users fast because of it, but with a lacking product to match this interest?

And what about mepis that is better for you?[/QUOTE]
There is a lot of hype around ubuntu that is legitimate.

The biggest draw for me was the .deb/apt packaging system.  I was coming from SuSE and I was fed up to the back teeth with RPM and trying to resolve dependencies myself.  I tried getting apt4rpm to run, but it never seemed to go right for me.  

I thought about Debian, but was deterred by the fact that "stable" Debian was very old.  At about that time, Ubuntu 4.10 (Warty) was released, and I jumped on the bandwagon.  Here was a distribution promising semiannual releases, reasonably current packages, the GNOME desktop (which I had wanted to try out)...pretty much everything I needed at the time.

Installation and setup was remarkably easy--as easy as SuSE, for me--and I was up and running in short order.  

I think Ubuntu definitely has the goods, but I do feel that it's being sold as a "newbie" distro somewhat unfairly.  If I wanted to give a newbie with little or no linux or computer experience a distro to try out and play with, it would be Knoppix or Mepis.  Ubuntu is an excellent distro if you already know a bit about what you want/need.

As to the OP's "barren moonscape" comment.  Your desktop aesthetics are not universal.  I like the default ubuntu setup.  My windows desktop is full of icons I don't want, and doesn't have enough stuff I actually *do* want (e.g. the iweather gdesklet) on it.
