### Post by bug67 on 2010-01-29
I recently purchased a new HP Pavilion DV8T laptop.  This thing is LOADED! :D

However, as I was quickly reminded, if you install an application in Windows and then, for whatever reason, decide to uninstall it, the deleted program leaves remnants of itself all over the place.  I spent 2 days trying to rid my new shiny of crap files left behind by Window's less than stellar uninstall process.  I finally found an article on-line that walked me through a registry edit process that did the trick.  You can read all about it here if you feel so inclined:

[http://social.answers.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/w7files/thread/1e5c64c3-7ead-4e0c-bb23-d3fe05a52f17]("http://social.answers.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/w7files/thread/1e5c64c3-7ead-4e0c-bb23-d3fe05a52f17")

The one person who tried(?) to help (if you can call it that) came off as very snooty to me.  I have never gotten that kind of attitude in these forums.  You guys have always been friendly and extremely helpful.  A testament to the kind of people who use Linux perhaps?  This kind of stuff (registry hacks and such) is unnecessary with Linux.  You delete something and it and all it's parts are gone!

Anyhow, This new 'puter of mine has two hard drives in it.  I will be installing Ubuntu X64 on one of them for a duel boot as soon as I get  Windows 7 to kneel before me.  :D
