### Post by andrew.co.za on 2008-01-31
My perspective:

Major Pros of Ubuntu:
- Far more growth potential for Linux in general due to the amount of contribution.
- Ubuntu is already leaps and bounds ahead of Microsoft's latest. (Features, performance, security, user efficiency. Yes i said user efficiency, it's even an improvement on windoz' major selling point).
- Ubuntu is an excellent entry point for Linux due to it's interoperability.
- Major customisability (limited primarily by how far you're willing to go to achieve it).
- Security blah blah blah (Microsoft really tripped over their own feet trying to catch up on this one, AND THEY FAILED!).
- And of course it's free, and will always be free.

Major Cons:
- getting even the most common hardware to work sometime can be a nightmare (but it is usually always possible)
- Simple everyday tasks in windoz can be seemingly impossible to figure out (But once you know how, the problem's over. It's finding out that might leave you with a few less hairs on ur head)
- There are bugs! (not unlike windows but a different set of bugs. You've come to be able to work around windoz bugs now you're faced with figuring out new ones)
- Getting support is not as easy as ubuntu forums makes it out to be
- And if you're a gamer, you really don't get much variety (i recommend warsow (google it) for Quake 3 lovers, yes it's free).

Conclusion:
You're going to be faced with a lot of new problems, some seemingly impossible to solve and no one to help you. It all comes down to the type of person you are. If you're willing to learn/teach yourself then go for it. It's taken me a good two years but i'm finally full time Ubuntu, other than playing the odd game or two in my windoz boot. Anyway if you want a silver platter (windoz, OSX), it's there but what you see is what you get. With Ubuntu, you craft your own experience and there's great potential.

Note:
You can run any OS through Ubuntu using VirtualBox (google it) but the virtual graphics card it gives you is kak (poo, for those unfamiliar with Afrikaans). Now this might just be psychological but i really think XP runs better through virtualbox than it does on it's own, probably psychological ;)

### Post by p_quarles on 2008-01-31
I largely agree with your assessment, but would make a couple points:
1) Vista is actually a pretty major improvement over previous Windows' versions in terms of security. So, they did a fair job with that. The problems are that the security features add some amount of user frustration, and closed-source software is prone to having its vulnerabilities obscured.

2) Many people will never have a single notable hardware difficulty. I have Linux on three separate machines, and the only hardware fix I've ever had to run was installing the 915resolution package. Obviously, it varies a great deal from person to person, but the hardware problems are not a guaranteed obstacle.
.
EDIT: Oh, about Virtualbox: XP boots up *way* faster when virtualized, I'll agree with that. It only takes a little longer than something heavy like OpenOffice.org Writer.
