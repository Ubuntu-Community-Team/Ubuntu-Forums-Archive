### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-05-04
So, I was having some confusing issues with Gnome, but they were infrequent- turns out I kinda' dashed through GParted too fast when I was installing the RC. I already left some opinions here, but that was before I got my disk errors sorted.

Man, what can I say? Battery life has increased, fan's hardly ever on even when using OpenGL (this is the open ATi driver, I'm using), and Flash is actually pretty good this release.

I thought everything had gotten faster BEFORE I cleared up my disk issues. But man, it must be the drivers, Gnome 2.30, the removal of HAL, Kernel Mode Setting, or SOMETHING, but everything is so instantaneous, even without preload, that I'm seriously doubting installing preload, since it might cause my computer to jump up and eat me. This release is really that good.

Holy CRAP, it's amazing. All the little tiny tweaks you notice that make things even easier... it's like a desktop that does stuff FOR you! Like, a computing companion as opposed to some system you feel like you're USING, and grinding through. Sure, Karmic wasn't bad, but this release has such polish (not talking about the theme), it just feels smoother to my mind. It's broken some kind of threshold... to the point that, even though Linux excited me to use my computer again and made it all such a pleasure to do, I feel like this is really something else.

It's almost too good.

Thank you so much Canonical! You're really pushing it to the limits, here, not just on features, but the usability of those features. Keep up the awesome work, Canonical, the upstreams, and don't let the success get you slackin' kernel devs! Now that we've got the hardware support, we needa' really refine it to perfection. We're all here behind you, keep rockin' that IDE!

You're making all our lives just a little bit better every time we get on the computer.
