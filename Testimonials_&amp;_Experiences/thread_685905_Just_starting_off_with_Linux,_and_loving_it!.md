### Post by Jason2gs on 2008-02-02
Well, that's not entirely true. A few months back I tried Darn Small Linux, and I can't say I was too happy with it, except for the fact that it was so small. It didn't offer much, and I didn't like that I couldn't get wireless working.

But here I am again, with Ubuntu as my main OS, and very satisfied so far!

Actually, the story of how it came to be my main Os is kind of funny, if you want to listen.

A couple days ago I was about to put Ubuntu on my external HD. I wasn't too happy with the idea of an operating system dangling off the site of my laptop, but I was going to try it out anyway.

I downloaded Partition Magic on Windows, and started using it. My external partitioned fine, so I decided to take the 7.8 Meg of unallocated space on my main drive and give it to the recovery partition.

*Sigh*

Safe to say, two hours later I was on the Gusty Live CD, copying my corrupted Windows partition onto my external, formatting my main disk, and starting fresh with Ubuntu.

So all-in-all, I didn't LOSE anything. If anything, I fixed my laptop by getting rid of Windowz ^_^

So far, Ubuntu is great. It went online a LOT easier than I thought, but I am trying to find a way to get monitor mode working on my wireless card. I believe it's coming along, though.

Kk, I've rambled on enough. My dog is whining to go out.

Thanks for reading, and wish me luck with Ubuntu!

-Mike
