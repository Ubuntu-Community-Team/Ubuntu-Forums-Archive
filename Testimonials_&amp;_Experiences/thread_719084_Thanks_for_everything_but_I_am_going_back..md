### Post by Bargeek on 2008-03-08
Well, I wanted to thank everyone for all the answers, and the support, but I think I am going back to windows.  As much as I hate to say it, I just don't think Ubuntu is for me.

I know that given time, I could PROBABLY learn to use this OS adequately, the learning curve is a bit steep, and my frustration level is at an all time high.  I can find fixes after hours of poring through the forums, but by the time I get my issue fixed, I am either too tired or too frustrated to do whatever it was I set out to do.

The biggest issue for me thus far has been with audio.  One minute, I have no mic, the next, I have it, and then I have it but it isn't doing what it is supposed to do.  Then, all that will playback is mic input, and no other audio.

It is a shame.  I love the otherwise intuitive updates, and the overall philosophy behind both the software and the community.  But, if I can't get it to do what I need it to do, then obviously it is not right for me, or maybe I am not right for it.

I will keep lurking about, and maybe when there is a build out that has more stable audio, I will dip my toe in the pool again.

Thanks again, and happy computing!!

### Post by scottro on 2008-03-09
Even if one wants to buy a computer that should be Linux compatible, it's often difficult, especially if one tries to save money by buying online. 

I chose a laptop that had been sold in Asia with Linux on it.  So, I figure I'll have no problem.  However, in Asia, it was sold with a different wireless card--here it has the infamous AR5007EG.  Although I now have it working with the MadWifi patched snapshot, the point is, I thought it was going to work out of the box. 

Then there is System76.  I think they're doing a good thing, but  I would have had to pay more.  

(Tihs was a $500 laptop.)  
As for Dell, it's interesting--I note that they have different hardware for the MS and Linux versions, using an Intel card for the Linux laptops and their own Dell something or other for the MS ones.  (That's not thoroughly checked, this was for a low end one that I looked at a couple of months ago.)

However, I agree with Antman in this case--that's not the best answer in this sort of situation.    For better or worse, for Ubuntu to handle its stated #1 bug, Ubuntu has to adapt to what is. 

I have this vague idea, that I still haven't decided if it's brilliant or idiotic.  Work on the manufacturers,one at a time.  First have all Linux users boycott, say Acer, with their AR5007EG on Core Duos, making 64 bit wireless completely non-trivial.  Get an online petition, and have people do it--when Acer has a sale for $500, write them and say, sorry, I signed this petition, until you get completely Linux compatible out of the box laptops, I'm not buying your machines.  

Then, if it was successful, go to the next one who uses one of these troublesome cards, say HP.  

It seems as if it's feasible, but I suspect I"m overlooking something .  :)

Still, I would think there are enough Linux and BSD users out there to represent customer pressure, it's a matter of figuring out the most intelligent way to use it.
