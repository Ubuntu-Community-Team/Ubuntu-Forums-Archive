### Post by Morty007 on 2009-02-09
This is a bit unusal. 

I use to have Linux Mint 6 installed, but went back to Ubuntu. Well I went to transfer my files back using the restore feature in "flyback", which is a great backup utility by the way.

Flyback quickly restored my data. To my dismay though, it also restored my Linux Mint toolbar, along with some of the mint tools and wireless utilities! Even my shutdown screen was the mint logout, and the wallpaper changer was restored too. As a result, my computer seemed to be running  Mintbuntu instead of Ubuntu. My computer appeared to go back in time to the way it was about a week ago. It must have backed up the files that I have in the toolbar folders (if there is such a folder) instead of just my home folder. 

Well I didn't want that, and rather than try to find out how to get my ubuntu back I just did a reinstall. Because Linux installs so quickly I was soon back to where I was before that botched restore.:lolflag:
