### Post by Crispin_101 on 2009-11-03
A technique I use makes an upgrade pretty much bullet proof. I make a copy of all the files/folders in my old Home Directory that I want to keep. 

Since I use Firefox and Thunderbird for my Internet needs I make copies of their hidden folders too. When in your home directory, Go to View and click on View and tick "Show Hidden Files". Now you will see myriads of folders you never knew were there. Look for and copy .mozilla directory and also .mozilla-thunderbird directory. [Note the . preceding the folder name].

Now go ahead and install a NEW copy of the latest release software. Forget about using the Upgrade method, never quite as good as the replacement route. Install your favourite programs and copy over the files you copied earlier. Don't forget to replace the hidden folders you made before.

Now when you run Firefox, all your favourites, passwords and other addons will all be there. The same is true for Thunderbird. All your old 'stuff' is there just like you never changed over to a new installation.

This process might be a little more messy than ideal but not that much bother in order to achieve a seamless change over.

I just changed over from Mint 7 [Ubuntu 9.04 variant] to Ubuntu 9.10 with PERFECT results. I am delighted with the way the software is running on my Samsung R519 LapTop.

Hope the above is of interest.

Cris.
