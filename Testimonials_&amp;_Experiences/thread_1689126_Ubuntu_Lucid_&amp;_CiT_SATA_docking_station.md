### Post by Tony Flury on 2011-02-16
Just bought a CIT SATA HDD DK02 docking station - expecting to have some challenges in getting 10.04 to recognise the HDD and read the data. The dock was labelled as Windows and Mac compatible - typically no mention of Linux.

Imagine my delight then when i turned it on - and within seconds, every partition on the HDD got mounted automatically under "/media", and the full data set was completely readable - no downloads, no config, everything just there.

Amazing - well done Ubuntu, and well done CiT - not bad for £19.00 including P&P and VAT.

### Post by coffeecat on 2011-02-16
It's good when things just work in Ubuntu - especially when there is no mention of Linux on the packaging.

A few years ago I managed to get Ubuntu running (I should say crawling!) on a vintage 1998 laptop. Which was fine, except that I couldn't connect to the internet - no ethernet port and no wireless. I don't think wireless had been invented in 1998! :)

So I bought a usb to ethernet adapter from Maplins - there was, at least, one USB1 port on the machine. When I got it home and unwrapped the adapter, the Windows installation CD dropped out together with strict instructions to install the driver before plugging in the adapter. In my haste I hadn't even considered the need for a driver and I thought for a moment I had made a wasted purchase. But I plugged the thing in anyway and - guess what? - it just worked. But, of course. :wink: That was with Dapper Drake 6.06.

Marvellous.
