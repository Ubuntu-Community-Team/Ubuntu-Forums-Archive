### Post by Foxman2k on 2006-09-22
I must say,  I am extremely impressed with Ubunutu.  Here is my experience.

I've been using Linux since ~1996 on and off,  so I'm quite well versed.  I think the earliest Redhat I installed (off of floppy's) was version 2.0?  

I've installed Mandrake,  RedHat, Suse, Gentoo,  Linux from scratch etc. etc.  

I've had experience with BeOS, Solaris, IRIX, HPUX etc. etc.

Anyways,  I always dual booted back to windows with any of the above versions of Linux,  as they were always "quite nice" but not good enough.  Something was always missing.

Enter Ubuntu.

I was bored a few weeks ago and decided to download the ubuntu live cd.  I fired it up and played around,  seemed pretty nice.  Now I have just upgraded my computer and have a 2nd hard drive,  so I said,  why not,  let's install it and see how it performs without the burdon of the cdrom.

WOW.

Speed is great.  And everything just works.

XGL + Compiz is probably the nicest interface I've ever worked with.  Nicer, IMO,  than Max OS X.

Plugged in my digital camera - Up pops a window "would you like to download your pictures"

Plugged in Ipod,  it appears on my desktop.

I am now running Ubuntu almost exclusively.

Areas I still need windows (either through vmware or native).

1) MSMoney - Nothing open sourece is quite as robust as this app.

2) Games - Game support is very limited in Linux.

Overall,  my hats go off to the dev's of ubuntu and everything else that is linux!
