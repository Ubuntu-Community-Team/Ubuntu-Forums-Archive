### Post by coolglobal on 2012-01-29
We decided as a family to go with Xubuntu, so I've just finished installing the latest Xubuntu on three home computers. Over the last week I tried a number of new distro releases and found Xubuntu comes pretty close to the mark for a standard install. It seems to be the most user friendly release with Xfce. In stars, I reckon it's around about a 3 and a half out of 5, as a home computer in general. With 4 stars as a xfce distro.

It was a very easy to install and the overall feel of the system is quite coherent, it kicks *** over the other distros. With Gnome 2 in its death throws and Unity in a shambolic state of user unfriendliness, the choices are getting slim on the ground, so Xubuntu it is.

This is a dirt sandwich post. Nice bread on either side with a touch of dirt in the middle. So here's the dirt. The startup is darn ugly, it's an out of focus mouse and a blurry neon loading bar. No good amigo, for a decent sized screen. I just need to work out where to delete this monstrosity altogether. Random code scrolling past would be preferable.

Thunar. OH MY GOODNESS. Someone has attached a large heavy sack of potatoes to Thunars foot. Never have I experienced such straightup lag in a file manager. Damn, it has to be experienced to be believed. Scary. My solution was to install that great unsung rock of linux beautifullness, Pcmanfm. What a relief it was to do that. To complete the install, I just turned off desktop icons, so I don't mistakenly open the lumbering Thunar. Crumbs. There's gotta be a fix in the works for this one, cause I like Thunar and always have.

Why two image viewers? Gthumbsomething and Ristretto. I could not work this out, so had a play with both of them. I'm guessing it's something to do with their interaction with the file manager. But what? Haven't got a clue. I have to say that Ristretto, I think it was, brought up diced images. What the? Mate, I just removed both of them altogether and installed Mirage. All I can add here is, try Mirage and see the difference, it's like chalk and cheese. The design and theme even integrates better with Xubuntu.

Parole could not grab my entire, small, music collection from just one directory and present it, just part of it. No time for that nonsense, instant uninstall. The video player worked fine. Even still, uninstall, and went with VLC. The funny thing about VLC is that it just works and it has a strong support base. Why dilly-dally around the bushes, with the others, it's a fragmentation and duplication, it doesn't make sense. All VLC needs is a skin and some plugins, too easy. Perhaps complexity and confusion is the end goal, whatever.

Firefox. Thankyou, thankyou, thankyou. Works everytime. To my knowledge not a single mainstream distro ships default with anything meaningful to blank out the great, ugly, ocean of ads, and devious, batallions of trackers taking over the world wide web. Harrassed and stalked everywhere on the world wide web, no thanks. Thankfully its only a few clicks to abomb the hawkers and stalkers with Adblock and Ghostery. Will they ever ship default? Perhaps in a world where the privacy and safety of the end user takes presedece. Suppose I better settle in for a long wait. There's reds in the beds!

That's about it for the dirt, which is pretty darn good going if I say so myself. That is to say, everything else works GREAT. Hardware detection, printer support, composting, speed and snappiness, themeing, system tools and so on. All good. We've chosen Xubuntu, over every other distro on the go, for our three home computers. We're all very happy with Xubuntu.
