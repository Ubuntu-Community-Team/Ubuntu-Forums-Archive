### Post by ursus262 on 2009-07-25
From the very beginning, I used Kubuntu on my laptops rather than ubuntu as I preferred the visual appearance of the desktop, and once installed gnome so I could evaluate both environments.

Everything was OK until KDE 4.X.X was introduced and things haven't been right since then.  In fact, it's so bad that I've had to move over to Ubuntu. Why?  Because KDE simply isn't very good.  I've lost count of the number of times I've lost data, and haven't even been able to watch streaming video (something that I can do in Ubuntu).  Even with 4.2.2, the system just isn't very stable and I am very disappointed with it.

Recently, I lost patience with it, and completely reinstalled Ubuntu with the Gnome interface.  I've had no problems since.

Ubuntu is completely stable and rock steady.  I've lost much of my music collection as a result of Kubuntu.  Why this is I am not sure, but the support is not very good.
