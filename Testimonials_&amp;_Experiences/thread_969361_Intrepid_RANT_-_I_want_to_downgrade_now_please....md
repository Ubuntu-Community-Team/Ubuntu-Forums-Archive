 That is true to some extent.  
 However, often improvements are made in areas that are unused by the majority, but improve functionality for some. These improvements may be packaged with an upgrade or simply added in an update.
 

 Sometimes improvements are:
 
[LIST=1]
[*]Enhancements to existing packages
[*]Ascetics
[*]Speed
[*]Functionality
[*]Etc.
 	
[/LIST]
 Not all improvements are beneficial to everyone and may even have a negative effect for some. With all the existing hardware and software - what one may view as an improvement doesn't necessarily equate to an improvement for you. If an auto company were to improve the tires on a car that doesn't necessarily mean that the car's steering will improve. Other problems impacting the steering may not have been improved. Please do not use this analogy to spoon feed responses – it's just late and I couldn't come up with anything better right now.  
 

 I think that is one of the primary reasons Ubuntu has regular updates. Endeavoring to satisfy the masses is often riddle with problems and complaints (just ask Windows). Yet Ubuntu continues to support the releases and I truly believe it's in an effort to improve on the product.
