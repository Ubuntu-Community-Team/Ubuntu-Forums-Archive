### Post by thorbs on 2012-02-02
I have been exclusively on ubuntu and ubuntu based os for three years. But I just said goodbye two months ago and went back to win world.
I really liked my time with Ubuntu. I loved the philosophy and I discovered after leaving that it tought me a lot about computers. I am gratefull for that. 

The reason I left was that I was not able to find any writing software that I was satisfied with. Being at the core of my work, it just became to much hassle. One can say what one wants about office 2010 but it works pretty stable. As I gave in to office so many other programs followed and I must admit it has been really nice having them all again.

If I should make a conclusion from this, I feel ubuntu and linux is lacking on the software side. I do not know if there is a remedy for this, I was for some time thinking that maybe linux needed to encourage a for pay sector. It would be nice if those of us who can afford, would support those who can not, by bying products which has a free version.

But lately I have been thinking that the solution would be to create a whole different approach. I could deffinitely think off a writing tool that was very different and much lighter than office. 

My point is that the present strategy just does not seem to work. Offcourse it does work for all those who have no other choice than linux because of they cant afford anything else, and for the enthusiast.

Maybe linux just need to be the os of choice for the very IT inclined and for those who are not but feel a bit adventurous. I deffinitely enjoyed my time with Ubuntu.

I do not know if you can use me feedback, but I thought I offer it. 

Thanks for everything

### Post by thorbs on 2012-02-06
I am no expert on UI or programming but I am happy somebody at last ask me how a word processor should be. That is probably every writers dream. 

The thing that struck me so much going back to win, was how it was close not to be a computer. Everything is done for me, choices are made. But not only that, I am not allowed myself to interact with my computer. Very strange. I can only wonder how a mac would be. 
Also win try to be everything for everybody, which I have to say it does deacently. But the problems is that if you do not fit your simply lost. Even though you can find other people with the same problems, there is no  solutions. And then offcourse this be everything for everybody approach make it incredible ressource hungry. 

I think a Linux wordprocessor, should have the same core functionallity that makes Linux as os great. Do few things really good, and do not try to be everything for everybody. Allow for people to choose themselves directly. And give opportunity for people to customize to there needs.

So how would that look at as wordprocessor. Define the basic functions of a wordprocessor and stick to making them work, and stable. Make the writing area fill the most, and do not use to much space or energy to ui, instead make it more direct(maybe something like gnome do, sidepanel or what do i know =)). Give access to formating etc. (I have read that wordperfect has this feauture).Dont try to do everything for the user, instead be transparent about how to fix things yourself.  Avoid falling in the be all for everybody trap, stay lean and fast. Open up for somekind of api, so people can customize.

The offcourse there is the problem with interaction wit other programs, I have been reading about the history of wordprocessors, and many write that the way word beat wordperfect was to close of the format and making word the standard by giving to little information for other application working well with windows. 
So I think some energy should be put in this translation of document type to document type. 

That is my idea of a new approach to wordprocessors.
