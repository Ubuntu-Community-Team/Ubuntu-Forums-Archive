### Post by maxtheitpro on 2009-12-02
I recently purchased an excellent quality used Toshiba L305-S5944 laptop here in Nairobi and I couldn't install XP because of the stupid SATA hard disk thingy. I had ordered a Jaunty Jackolope CD a while back, which incidentally took only ONE WEEK to arrive to me here in Nairobi, KENYA. I was SHOCKED!!!
Anyhow, I tried out the Live CD and went straight to the full Monty (er install). Suffice to say, EVERYTHING works like a charm. I am soooo tickled pink. 

I've used Linux (Corel, Libranet, Puppy, Peanut, Xandros, and Mandrake) back around 1998 to 2002 -- just distro hopping when I was doing my Computer Science internship at a bank in Toronto. Boy has Linux changed over those years!!! My hats off to the fabulous Tech elves over at Debian along with the Ubuntu community for moving Linux forward in a BIG way.

Oh, I downloaded and installed the latest version of VirtualBox to run XP for 1 or 2 minor apps and it runs FAST as hell. My system is a Dual Core 2.16Ghz with 2Gb RAM, 250 Gb HD and I just can't believe how EFFICIENT it runs XP as a guest while I run other apps on the Ubuntu side. CPU load is usually below 10%. AMAZING!!!

Last week, I decided to update to Karmic Koala (9.10) using the Update Manager and, surprise, surprise, EVERYTHING worked flawlessly. So, you can add the Toshiba L305 series as 100% Ubuntu (Debian) "FRIENDLY." 

To conclude, I will be whoring Ubuntu Linux down here in Africa like a parrot that can't shut up!!! 
:popcorn:
Thank you all for ridding me of Windows Updates, viruses, worms, MacAfee, Symantec,Kaspersky, NOD32, AVG and constant rebooting. :-)

Peacies!
