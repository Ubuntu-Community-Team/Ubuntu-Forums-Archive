### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-04
Well, it sounds as if the disappointment comes from your expectations. You were essentially expecting Linux to be a free version of Windows.

Linux isn't Windows, and you have to come to it with an open mind. Nevertheless, I think some of your problems are just... wrong. You can edit .doc files in OpenOffice, and there are plenty of good FTP clients and web browsers in Linux. And Matlab has a Linux version as well.

If you're going to migrate over, ask yourself why you're migrating. Do you, in fact, just want a free copy of Windows? Are you willing to explore applications that may be better than the ones you use now but not exact replicas of the ones you use now? I think a big hang-up for you right now is that what you're progamming VBScript/ASP is extremely Windows-centric.  So much so that it is what MS uses as a serious form of lock-in to the Windows platform.  No C# dev is going to jump from doing .NET for a living to suddenly go to PHP/python etc just because they're OSS.
