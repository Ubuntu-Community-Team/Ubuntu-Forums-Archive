### Post by HittingSmoke on 2008-09-18
I figured I'd share this as I found it interesting. Maybe someone with more insight into it can shed some light into it for anyone else who might have these problems.

I've only been using Linux for a month or so and Adobe Flash has been by far my biggest pain.

When I first switched to Linux I went with Kubuntu. Linux for human beings, how convenient, thats me! The transition went pretty smoothly, I'm a resourceful guy and anything I couldn't find on Google I was helped with here (thank you all, you've made the transition much easier).

Right from the start I followed all the "beginner" guides I could find. I installed Flash player 9 and for the most part it worked fine. All the sites I normally used regularly worked great, except Hulu.com (a flashed based TV show streaming web site). It would crash as soon as it loaded and it was the only site I tried that would crash Firefox.

Sometimes I could go in through a back door by using the about page which contains no flash, and use the search page to look up a specific episode and get it to play but more often then not it just crashed.

It may sound like just one web site but I use it regularly to keep up with the TV shows I'm too cheap to subscribe to cable for. After a while I did some research into Flash 10. After Flash 10 RC1 was released and I read the reviews from people who tried it, I was ready to believe.

After upgrading to Flash 10 RC1 (after a bit of grief getting it properly installed and Flash 9 properly removed) my beloved Hulu.com worked flawlessly. And to put the cream in my twinkie, every flash video ran much **more** smoothly and I could run them in full screen with out any choppiness. I love Linux. Windows what?

Then more trouble started. After installing Flash 10 RC1 I noticed a small handful of pages that would crash my browser. Not normally crash like when the page loads it crashes, but only when I scrolled the page. One example is a friend of mine updated her myspace profile layout. The page would load fine, even if I waited to see "Done" in the status bar. Yet if I scrolled down the page till her friends module showed on the screen, the browser would crash. I saw this problem with several other pages. I miss Windows.

My workaround was to disable page styles in Firefox for sites that would crash on me. Then at least I could view most of a page albeit hard to navigate. This worked well for me and most everything worked great, except Myspace video.

Myspace video is my Youtube. No limits on video size means I can watch something without having it split into parts. Plus there is cooler clips there in my opinion. After install Flash 10 RC1 Myspace video pages would give me the "You need flash player installed to display this...". Oddly enough I could watch them if they were embedded in another page, just not on the native page. This wasnt a big deal, I'd gladly trade myspace video for Hulu.com.

After a frustrating bout with trying KDE 4.1 on my Kubuntu Hardy install I thought maybe I had made the wrong choice with KDE in the first place. Everyone said KDE had more options and was more user friendly, but if thats true how come so many more people use Ubuntu with Gnome.

After weighing some options to install it along side KDE and give it a try I just settled on the fact that this install was made when I was a total noob, and It couldn't hurt to do a fresh install of Ubuntu as all my documents/vid/pics etc were saved on a separate disk. Plus, if I installed Gnome and wanted to stick with it, it would be a pain to clean up all the KDE apps that install with Kubuntu.

So here I am. I did a fresh install of Ubuntu Hardy. With Flash 9 installed on Ubuntu every site I've tried works great. I do have to put up with some choppy frame rates at full screen some times but the stability is worth it. The only thing that is different between my installs are the desktop environment. Maybe KDE doesn't play as well with flash as Gnome? Maybe it's Firefox that prefers Gnome over KDE?

In summary, everything seems to work a little more stable and solid with Gnome vs. KDE. I don't see where the whole user friendliness comes with by using KDE over Gnome. With Gnome my menus are better organized. Standard apps like file browsers feel better integrated. Themes looks just as good on Gnome (although good ones seem harder to find). All my favorite things about Compiz still work flawlessly. My Cairo dock still runs great. And most important of all, Firefox behaves better.
[I]
Winwha?[/I]

I don't know what relevance any of this really has but this sums up the only real grief I had switching to Linux from Windows (other than gaming, which is to be expected). If anyone is trying to make the Linux switch and web browsing is an issue for you like it was for me, I hope my experience helps.
