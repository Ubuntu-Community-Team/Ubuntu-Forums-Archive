### Post by Irihapeti on 2009-08-02
I've had Ubuntu on this computer since July 2007.

I was getting along sort of OK with Vista, no major problems, but somewhat sluggish. I'd heard of Ubuntu in a number of places around the internet, and being the kind of person who likes to use unusual things, was interested in trying it out. Eventually, I came across a computer magazine with a software DVD which included Ubuntu 7.04.

Lots of googling followed, and then I backed up all my data files, took a deep breath, crossed my fingers, and installed Ubuntu as dual boot. Hooray, everything behaved.

Actually, I was extremely lucky on two counts. One, I had an HP printer; two, I had a softmodem that has a Linux driver. Dialup isn't ideal, I know, but I found it beat the heck out of being stuck with Vista.

I found that I could do pretty much everything I wanted in Ubuntu. True, I don't do gaming or high-end graphics/video editing, and the last thing I want to do is tell other people what they should or should not run. I did try telling my son to switch, but then I was getting sick of him complaining all the time about Windows misbehaving on his laptop. Eventually, I wised up and just let him be.

After two months, I realised that I was only booting into Windows to do the updates. I decided at that point to delete it altogether. No regrets on that score.

I've had lots of fun learning the command line, compiling stuff, breaking things etc. At times I was tearing my hair about stuff I couldn't get to work. Some issues got solved and others never did. Those that didn't I either lived with or worked around. Played with Puppy Linux and looked at one or two other smaller distros but kept coming back to Ubuntu for my everyday working environment.

At present, I have two laptops in addition to the desktop machine, all with 8.04 on them. My son eventually got totally fed up with Windows glitches and decided to install 8.04 on his laptop and on his wife's computer. We've pretty much decided to stay with 8.04 and upgrade next year when the new LTS comes out.

Is Ubuntu easy enough for a grandmother to use? Well, I'm not a grandmother but I'm old enough to be one, so maybe that answers that question. :)   Anyway, I'm having fun with it, and that's the main thing.
