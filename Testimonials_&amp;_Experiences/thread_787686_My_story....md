### Post by Monstroxus on 2008-05-09
I have been reading these testimonials for a long time here, which I enjoy very much... so I thought its time to put my little story here as well. Oh yeah, one thing I cannot stand is that some people here are posting something like: "hardy heron is bad, blabla, is the worst so far blabla" they better say... hardy heron doesn't work for me, or doesn't really go along well with my hardware. Anyway that said... my story starts a few years ago when I tried out Red Hat (don't know which version, but it was in 2003) I quite liked to play around with it, but didn't have a cue how to install things, I guess it was quite difficult at that time to do so. It was running ... but boring, not much to do with it. Bought a linux book as well, but it was boring as well (for me). Then Red Hat decided to focus only on servers and such... so I left the Red Hat distro... looked around and thought SuSE should be nice... played around with that for a while but it still wasn't really interesting for me. Anyway, my linux enthousiasm was dorment for a while... went back to XP as a result. Fast forward to 2006... had nothing to do... was browsing the net and thought... hey lets see if there is any linux news... discovered then that Ubuntu was quite popular, so I tried out that one. (Dapper) I really liked it, it was a BIG improvement over the linux distros I tried earlier. (I only had to get used to the name "UBUNTU", it really sounded strange at that time for me, my first thought "a distro from Africa???) I installed it as dual boot with XP, played with it and like it quite a bit. At that time my wife and I decided to buy a laptop. (lenovo 3000 c200) I told her, "just to let you know before we buy it... I really want to install Ubuntu on it" informed her that it would be a bit different than windows, but she didn't have any comments... luckily... so we bought the laptop.. brought it home. First thing... switch it on... XP starts, waiting... all the usual preinstalled stuff loads as well... norton and whatever stuff. I literally had XP running for 2 minutes, switched it off and booted the Ubuntu Live CD, most things worked... so I clicked install. (use whole harddisk) Originally I wanted to dual boot but decided at that time to go linux only, and I am glad I did. A pure linux laptop, you know that feeling. Its nice. After the install... most things worked... but sound / wireless didn't work right away. I didn't mind that because I expected it, and I always told myself and my wife, sooner or later there will be a fix, or a how to. I think its very important to have this mindset, anyway, indeed a little while later and I got sound and wireless running. Of course thanks to some copy-paste commands. IMHO getting things to work is basically a matter of simply searching the web for a solution and literally to copy-paste the commands in the terminal, and hit enter. You don't need to be a "geek" for that, just stay frosty and follow the instructions IMHO without giving it too much thought in the beginning.  Next I installed Beryl and Kiba Dock... then installed Feisty till one week ago, when I made a clean install with Hardy. Sound and wireless didn't require any tweaking, it just worked right away FOR ME. I am very happy and have everything running nicely, for me Hardy is the best so far. Thank you very much for reading... and if you are a newbie that might encounter some obstacle with (ubuntu) linux, don't despair, sooner or later it will be fixed... but you have to put some effort in finding these fixes/solutions sometimes. It will give you a lot of satisfaction. You can say; I did it myself! Its my computer, its an unique... fun learning process. Thank you, all those who made this possible!

PS my wife doesn't have any problem using it, she is browsing the netf. She makes very complicated spreadsheets on open office as well, emails with Evolution, and is now getting used to the useful compiz settings (extra stuff) like expo, magnify, ring switcher and such. I am glad to see she likes it too. She never says she likes ubuntu, but I don't hear any comments either so I take that as a compliment.. heheh.. you know.. wives. :)

### Post by armandh on 2008-05-09
in the early 80s my company had an IBM 38 in the early 90s we were using a 386, wise terminals, and unix soft ware. a lot of stuff was done with dos. 

I retired in 93 and at home I was a M$ user from 3.1 through 98se, then in this century XP. my wife's laptop came with vista snail. throughout my retirement I have made side trips in to the Linux jungle. if I was able to get a GUI I was happy but none fully worked, none lasted. 

Knoppix looked interesting and my DB manager/brother in-law suggested Ubuntu. my first try was a mature FF-7.04 WOW everything just worked. GG-7.10, after about 3 months, seemed better, and so will HH-8.04 

two house hold computers run dual boot. 7.10 with XP-SP2 and 8.4 with vista. four boxes are Ubuntu only [two 8.4, two 7.04] and two more are XP-SP2 only. gone are the Greatly Disadvantaged XP-1s and I have spread Ubuntu to two other households.

I have not experienced any major grief that was not of my own doing, and the repair was just a reload anyway.

I do try other distros but I have found none better for me.
