### Post by murderslastcrow on 2009-10-06
So long as you don't have an iPod Touch or buy music from the iTunes store, you should be fine using an iPod in Linux.

However, if you have a touch, there are ways to hack it. Depending on what you use your iPod touch for, a zii-egg (google Zii) may be a perfect match for you, and a bit cheaper than the equivalent iPod Touch.

Also, there are ways to strip DRM from iTunes Music if that's the issue, although I'm not sure if that's an acceptable topic for discussion here.

I just know because a friend asked me to put Linux on his Intel-based Mac (64-bit is wicked with Linux). There are many great solutions for all of your music and multimedia needs, and 7music, amazonmp3, eMusic, and many other vendors will give you DRM free music cheaper than iTunes can offer.

Then there's Jamendo (legally free music).

If you want it enough, there will always be a solution within reach. And it's true- use Ubuntu and show it to your friends, without mentioning it's not Windows, without mentioning the security, speed, etc.

They'll be enticed to try it themselves, and all the little nuggets of goodness with manifest themselves along the way. It will get them a lot better than fanatic ranting, I can tell you that.

Currently I have my immediate family, my Grandma on one side, my Uncle, several cousins, and my best friends and their families converted simply due to seeing it in action in the first place.

For the majority of intermediate users, it's a perfect solution. I'm a professional designer and it's still a perfect solution for me. I'm glad you've seen how beneficial it can be to use open source software.
