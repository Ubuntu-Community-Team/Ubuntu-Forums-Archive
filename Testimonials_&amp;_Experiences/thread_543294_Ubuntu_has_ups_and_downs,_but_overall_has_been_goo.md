### Post by SuperDuck on 2007-09-04
I've been running a dual boot with XP and Ubuntu for over a week now.  I've had two major problems with Ubuntu so far - I had trouble setting up my dual boot system and I couldn't get Java to work for the life of me.

The first I am willing to chalk up to my inexperience.  I've never done anything like this before.  It's perfectly normal to obliterate Windows... twice in a row.

The second problem had me stymied, but it had to have been something that was my fault.  I followed all of the instructions and FAQ's and How-tos.  I saw thread after thread of people trying various fixes and leaving satisfied.  Finally I had to create my own thread, and had my answer that morning.  I'm not sure what I was doing wrong, but eventually it worked.  Huzzah.

So really, the problems haven't been with Ubuntu, I just need to get my **** together!

I haven't needed to boot Windows for pretty much anything.  I am keeping it for some hardware and software that I need for my business, but haven't done any work lately so it doesn't really matter. ;)  Yet.

Like many other yutzes, I have started a blog with my impressions on the transition thus far.  Feel free to read it, or feel free not to.  I just though I would write down how I felt now and the problems I've been having, so hopefully I can look back and giggle at what a n00b I was.

Cheers to Ubuntu, so far!
