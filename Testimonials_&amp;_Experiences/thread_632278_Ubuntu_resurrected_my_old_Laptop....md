### Post by ubuwatson on 2007-12-05
Well, I have a Dell Inspirion P4 with 256 m ram and a 20 g hard drive.  It's not a bad laptop by any means, but XP has always been too slow even for just casual web surfing.  A friend of mine has been trying to get me to switch to Ubuntu for quite a long time, but I have always been reluctant to switch - afterall I have been a happy windows user for some time.  At home a few months back I switched from XP to Vista, I built a new dual core machine and I run Vista 24/7 without any problems.  My old dell laptop has always been a sore spot for me (as I love to surf in bed or on the couch), however every time I crank up IE or Firefox on it, the laptop begins to crawl after about 15 minutes of use.  XP takes forever to start up, it's takes a few minutes to load the wireless drivers and connect to my network and you can't really do much more than web surfing as everything else seems to swap to the rather slow HD.

I decided I can do two things at this point either 1) ditch the laptop (donate or sell it) or 2) Install Ubuntu and give it a shot.  Well I decided to give option 2 a test using the latest release 7.10.  To my suprise the install was rather fast and intuitive.  All of my hardware was detected, and I was on the net in little time.

I really feel ubuntu resurrected my old laptop and here is why:

- it boots quite fast, at least 50% faster than windows
- upon login, my internet is ready to go, whereas windows took a long time to load the device drivers and establish an IP from my router.
- using swiftfox, internet is blazing fast - no slow downs to speak of even after 5 hours of heavy tabbed browsing.
- i just can't seem to slow this thing down.  Even with multiple application s open, I never hear the hard drive swapping, Ubuntu does a fantastic job of keeping overhead low.  I feel like I am using a brand new machine.
- no more defrag ?  I used to defrag my XP laptop almost daily because of heavy surfing.  After several weeks of Ubuntu, the laptop is just a fast as when i started.
- all the apps I need are pre-installed - everything is right there in front of me.
- i can easily remote into my Vista machine when I need to run a windows app.  Yes I could go the WINE route, but remote desktop via linux is fast (and better IMO than the windows client).

If anything is a testimonial to ubuntu is the fact that Ii don't use the dual core machine much anymore.  I don't have a problem with Vista, but Ubuntu has me hooked.  While the Vista machine will not be going away anytime soon, Ubuntu has a definite future with me.

Thanks Ubuntu and fellow community members,

Daniel
