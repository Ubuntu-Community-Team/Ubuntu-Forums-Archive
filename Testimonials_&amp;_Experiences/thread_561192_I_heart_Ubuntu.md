### Post by TedGarvin on 2007-09-27
I've been using Ubuntu for about a month now in a dual-boot with XP and I am amazed.  I keep xp around in case I need to do something in windows that I can't do in Linux.  So far, I haven't found anything.  I've been able to burn DVDs, watch videos online, get my podcast, and do all my business activities. 
 The only letdown is games (I like the Sims2 and Neverwinter Nights 2, neither are do-able in linux.) But even with that, I have the original Neverwinter Nights and UT2003 to play around with.  The only issues I had was with getting compiz-fusion working, and getting my network card going in a second computer I installed Ubuntu on, but both were solved easily with forum members help.
It's like a bad 80s movie where I've been dating a popular girl with a ton of baggage who demands I buy her expensive things all the time and comes home with STDs and then I fall in love with the cool, quiet girl who after you let her hair down and talk off her glasses is a knock-out.  
I don't know where I'm going with this, but I just wanted to thank everyone(programmers, developers, and of course, all the Ubuntu community) for all their hard work.
