### Post by Rylin on 2008-04-29
Though my computer experience started off with the apple 2e(my first personal computer), I've been a windows power user since then. Using it so much, I downplayed other OS's as inferior and not as widely supported as windows. Mac OSX was becoming more and more enticing to me after having seeing my friend use his macbook pro over the past year or so and the ability to use windows with their 'boot camp' software. That curiosity led me to take a quick gander at what linux has been up to -- mostly because I couldn't try osx for myself unless I bought a machine from them(linux was the next best thing). That's when my Linux experience started to take shape..

After watching a few youtube videos of various linux distros and what emerald (now compiz fusion) was giving users, I knew I wanted to give it a try. Doing a bit of research into various distros, Ubuntu looked like the obvious choice as far as ease of use and a great distro to introduce myself to linux. At the time, Feisty was the current version and to my suprise, it installed itself and was up and running alot quicker than I had thought. 

After the 'wow' wore off with using emerald effects, it was a fairly tough learning process when it came to installing other software/drivers and getting accustomed to the terminal and its commands. The important thing I learned from this and as a note to anyone wanting to give linux a try -- is to have the 'willingness' and slight persistence to learn linux and the freedom/power it can reward you with if you stick with it. Another point worth noting, is to use sites like ubuntuforums.org to the fullest. I can't stress how important this forum has been(among others) in aiding and providing the knowledge I've gained over the past year or so as a linux user. 

Ask questions and ask them often. the community that sprung around Ubuntu and linux in general, is nothing short of astonishing. Windows, being as popular as it is, doesn't have that 'community' feeling that is offered here and the amount of support people are willing to give to aid their fellow linux users. With all that said, my experiences have been very positive, though not without its early pains and trial&error.

If you take the linux plunge, your in for a world of enjoyment and satisfaction from the freedom and customization it gives you. Just be ready to get your hands dirty -- and keep an open mind in regards to learning something new. :popcorn:
