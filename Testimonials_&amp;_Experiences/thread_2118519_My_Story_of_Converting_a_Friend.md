### Post by throese on 2013-02-21
Well, my friend Mark had received his laptop as a Christmas gift. I don't know what he did, but when he handed it to me and I turned it on, Windows loaded and let you mess around for a few minutes, then blue screened. So, after a month of tinkering and getting NO WHERE, I showed him this video:

[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aTuoFDQXH2Y](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aTuoFDQXH2Y)

After watching it, he said, "Sign me up." So, I loaded it onto his system, and configured the basics. Afterwards, I installed Wine and PlayOnLinux to try and solve his problem of IMVU not working. So, I installed and configured Virtualbox for him and put Windows XP on it. Now all that is left to do is to set it up so that he can use IMVU via his Windows XP VM. He's rather happy with Linux over Windows, but is still learning.

I also recently completely switched over to Ubuntu. I have my Windows 7 OS backed up on an external hard drive via CloneZilla if I ever need to boot from it again for whatever reason, but I main Ubuntu. Will post screenshots of my system at some point today.
