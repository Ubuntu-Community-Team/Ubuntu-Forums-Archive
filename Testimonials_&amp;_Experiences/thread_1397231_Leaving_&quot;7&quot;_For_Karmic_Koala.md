### Post by 1roxtar on 2010-02-03
Last week, I bought a brand new Toshiba Satellite L455D-S5976 from Wal-Mart for about $400 (with tax included).  

Specs:
AMD Sempron SI-42 Processor
2GB DDR2 RAM, 250GB HDD
ATI Radeon Graphics
Windows 7 Home Premium 32-bit

I love this laptop, but after having been using Ubuntu for the better part of a year, Windows 7 just didn't feel right for me.  I wanted my Koala!  I wanted to hear the drumbeat as my laptop booted up (I love Ubuntu for lots of other reasons too, by the way). I've become an avid supporter of Ubuntu, Linux and Open-Source software in my local community.  

I ran with 7 for about two days and then I couldn't take it anymore...I didn't install a dual-boot...I erased Windows 7 and did a full-install of Ubuntu 9.10 after testing it out on my flash drive.  Everything ran smoothly, so I made the leap.  Afterwards, I slapped on a shiny "Powered by Ubuntu" sticker and hugged my laptop with glee.   ;)

On several posts and threads, I have stated that I love Ubuntu and Linux as a whole and that I have given up on Microsoft and Windows.  Call me a fan-boi or whatever, but I am flying the Ubuntu/Linux banner proudly.  So far every friend and family member that I have installed Ubuntu 9.10  on their machines have been happy and surprised at how good it really is.

These forums rant so much about what's wrong with Ubuntu that they lose sight of how good we have it and it's only gonna get better in my opinion.  I am on pins and needles waiting for the final release of Lucid Lynx in April.  I don't want to play with the Alphas and Betas, just yet.  I am happy reading about the positive reviews, so far.

As always....Thank you Ubuntu! Thank You Canonical and Mark Shuttleworth!  Last but certainly NOT least, Thank you Ubuntu Community for helping me learn so much in so little time.   XOXOXO
