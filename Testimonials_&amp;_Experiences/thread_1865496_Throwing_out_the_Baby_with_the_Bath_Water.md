### Post by tsikamtanda on 2011-10-20
I have been a loyal user of ubuntu Desktop since 6.04, and have  upgraded to the latest version whenever one is available. On the whole, I have liked the obvious improvement throughout (i even like Unity, although i still use it in conjunction with cairo-dock). 

One gripe i have, however, is that the progress often breaks, for no discernible reason, apps and utilities that I find enhance the whole ubuntu experience. Take, for example, wallpaper-changing utilities... Up to 10.10, i was happy to use wallpaper-tray, till 10.10 refused to work with it. Fair enough... there are many other fish in the sea.. so I changed to Desktop-Nova, which worked with that and 11.04. As some may already know, it no longer works with 11.10. Why, oh why, Canonical?

I do not think i am the only person who thinks this way, so i am encouraging all ubuntu users to post to this forum utilities, functionality and apps they lost with the change to the 'latest and greatest', in the hope that someone has an alternative or Canonical pays attention to retain the Most Wanted. I will start off this list with (off the top of my head):

1. Desktop-Nova wallpaper-changing utility no longer works
2. Nautilus no longer remembers its last opened desktop position (useful for users, like me, who tend to open the same windows for the same tasks/environment)
