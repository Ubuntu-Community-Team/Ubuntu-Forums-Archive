### Post by plurworldinc on 2010-02-18
My wife is staying with Linux to my surprise. Now my wife is not new to Linux since she has been using Ubuntu and Ubuntu Netbook Remix on her netbook for about a year now, but now she has a new used laptop preloaded with Windows XP. I really thought she would stay with XP since it just works out of the box. 

   What I didn't expect was having an OS that works right out of the box that is very buggy is not the best idea. So she asked me install Ubuntu on her laptop. She loves it because she can do everything she want to do right out of the box with out spending an arm and a leg to get everything done. 

   I took about an hour to install her new OS and add a few drivers and codec in and she spent the next 3 hours tweaking her laptop to the way she wanted it to look and feel. 

  See everyone another great example of Linux just working for the average user.

### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-02-19
> **Methuselah said:**
> I think it is relevant that she had a tech (you!) to configure everything to her heart's desire.

That much is true for every standard (not OEM) installation.  After all, what is an OEM installation but a tweak on a standard OS to install the required drivers, add a few proprietary tools, etc?

I think someone with zero technical skills would have a hard time trying to install windows on a machine with devices not recognised out of the box.  Even for a technical person it can become a pain.  I recently installed a standard Windows XP SP3 on my wife's (3 years old) HP laptop.  I was expecting it would pick all drivers, since it is a very standard model which has been in the market for a long time and comes from a huge manufacturer.  It didn't.

Luckily, HP provides very good support in that regard.  I went to their site and downloaded (with a different PC, of course) a bundle executable, which contains all drivers required for that machine-OS combination.  Installation went fine and that was it, but I can anticipate it would have been a nightmare if that kind of manufacturer support had not been there.

Anyways, I have the feeling that most people tend to benchmark an Ubuntu standard installation against proprietary OEM installations.  I think it is obviously not a fair comparison.
