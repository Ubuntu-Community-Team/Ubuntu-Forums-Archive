### Post by plurworldinc on 2010-02-21
Imagine no learning curve! Imagine being able to sit at your computer and with a few clicks of the mouse you have a ton of new free games on your computer to play with. Thank about how wonderful it would be to install an OS onto your computer and in a few short hours have all your software installed. Wouldn't it be great to have a computer system that was customized to look and feel the way you have always dreamed it could be. This is the joy my kids are having with Linux.

  What makes me such a proud father is that my kids are learning new things everyday, ever a few things I never thought of. My daughter were sharing a laptop together but figured out a way to get a second computer in there bedroom with little to no cost to me. My oldest found an old desktop in our closet that was just collecting dust and plugged in two monitors, keyboard, and a mouse. I saw her moving stuff around but I thought she was just playing with the old computer and trying something out. She tried booting up but nothing happened, we not until she though in a LIVE CD.

   A few hours later the kids manage to set up on there own a dual monitor system of there own in there bedroom running Ubuntu 9.10. I was shocked and amazed. I asked them how did they know what to do in the first place and they told me this is all basic now. "How hard is it to just plug everything in and play a CD" my oldest said. Plus she watched me do it so many times it wasn't really that hard.

   Now i did have to help my kids finish getting it set up with internet and download all of the codec needed to get there music and videos up and running.But for the most part its simply amazing. Now my kids are trying to figure out how to hook up there television to there computer for they can watch there videos on a larger screen.I must say when you give young minds the power to create and let there imagination run free, the skies the limit...:lolflag:

### Post by Docaltmed on 2010-02-26
Wow, I'm jealous. My daughters hate Ubuntu. They want their Windows and only their Windows. Same with their mother.

After spending the better part of a year trying education, various forms of electronic bribery, etc, I just said the heck with it and replaced Windows with Ubuntu in the "family" computer. Daughter #1 has a computer with Windows that was given to her, I hooked it up to the network, made sure that she had access to the music and picture folders, and loaded Ooffice. Told her if she needed/wanted support for anything beyond that, she was free to pay for it, as the in-house tech (me) was only supplying support for Ubuntu boxes. Also told her if I found pirated software on it (e.g. a copy of MS Office from a friend), the computer was going in the trash, as we don't tolerate theft around here.

Daughter #2 got an old laptop which barely limped along under XP. I threw on Ubuntu and the apps she needed, the thing got sparky again. I told Daughter #2 to suck it up and get with the program.

Harsh? Yup. But anyone who is the father of teenage daughters knows exactly where I'm coming from. And I was sick and tired of always having to massage XP to get it to do what I wanted it to do, and cleaning virus crap off the thing and having constant network problems...My time spent keeping the family computing environment functional has gone way down since I dropped the hammer.
