### Post by AnnaMary on 2007-07-30
:redface::redface:  

Hi everyone, I am a newbie.  I have had Ubuntu for all of a week or so.  I am far from being the computer geek that I aspire to be, but Ubuntu is awesome!  After you all helped me get up and running with streaming radio, I am in business.  I am the average home user who does nothing fancy.  (Just word processing, web browsing, cd-playing, and once in awhile scanning photos.)

Ubuntu, Ubuntu, where have you been all my life?  My favorite features are:

-  The workplace switcher that lets me have more than one desktop going at a time, plus I can move windows across desktops!

-I am no gamer by any means, but I am thoroughly enjoying many of the games that came with Feisty Fawn.  The concepts are great and the graphics are better than the standard MS games I have seen.

-My computer runs like the wind on Linux/Ubuntu with the firefox browser loaded with extensions.  Wow!  

-I feel safer using Ubuntu.  I didn't realize how worried I was about viruses and worms and spyware and any other vile creatures while using Windows XP.  There is no firewall sounding unnecessary alarms frequently.  

-Ubuntu saw my XP folders, documents, pictures, desktop, etc and ported it all in.  Amazing.  How convenient can it get?

-The desktop sticky notes are fantastic.  They come right with the OS, and I can use them right away.  I tried several times to download such programs with Windows, but it never worked for me.  (Remember, computer non-geek here, so I know it may have been "operator error").

-I still don't know the difference between Gnome and other Linux graphics/desktops, but I am getting along great with Gnome.  I have been easily able to select all my colors and settings for the desired desktop.  For me, this is a big deal and makes using Ubuntu more fun.

Well, that's about the end of my love letter for now.  Thanks for letting me babble!

I found Ubuntu by googling free OS when I crashed a Windows computer and ruined the old OS.  I was so bummed out.  This was one of the best mistakes I ever made.  Now I can keep learning Ubuntu, and get a better pc without having to spend lots of money I don't have for MS products.  

I am using a Compaq Evo P4, 2.4 GHz, 512 MB RAM pc with 120 GB HD.    I dual-boot, and that was simple to get going.  I still need Windows for a couple of programs (mostly Dragon Naturally Speaking, but I can change to LInux compatible ViavVoice I think), but my computing life doesn't absolutely depend on MS anymore.  I am free! 

I am well aware of the learning curve factor.  I am declaring my love after 10 hours of pulling my hair out yesterday trying to get introduced to the command line and synaptic package manager.  At least  when I generate errors, Linux usually gives me details and some idea of what I am missing.  Then I can post here and get fast, descriptive assistance.

After my rough Ubuntu day yesterday, I booted into Windows, and quickly switched back to Linux.  I guess this means I'm hooked.  I am enjoying the challenge and look forward to what comes next, headaches and all.  (As if I haven't suffered MS headaches for YEARS!)

Like I said, I think I'm in love!

Cordially,

Anna

### Post by Redache on 2007-07-30
I'm glad that you have found Ubuntu and have grown to like it.

About the desktops thing (If your at all curious) you can install the 3 other variants of Ubuntu with Apt.

Just copy and paste the following commands into a terminal (Although which desktop you choose is entirely dependent on you) 

```
sudo apt-get install kubuntu-desktop #For KDE - K Desktop Environment.
sudo apt-get install xubuntu-desktop #For xfce - Light weight Desktop
sudo apt-get install edubuntu-desktop # Good for children as it contains a lot of education programs.
```

Then all you need to do is when you first log in there should be a "sessions" option, click on that - change session - choose the desktop environment of choice - click "Just this time" in the dialog box and give it a try. To change it back to the default just repeat and select GNOME.

it can be fun to experiment, KDE might be THE desktop environment you've always wanted!.
