### Post by thaelim on 2011-11-27
I have to say that I am immensely enjoying using Gnome Shell on Ubuntu 11.10. It is extremely smooth and fast on every machine I've tested it on so far. I'm loving the 'no distractions' philosophy of the design, and I'm already addicted to the overview mode. I love being able to create virtual desktops on the fly. The multi-monitor support is excellent (one monitor that changes, one that doesn't for the stuff you want to see all the time - what could be better!)

Anyway, this is just my small attempt to counter the negativity bias when it comes to people writing about their experiences. To the the Ubuntu and Gnome devs: keep up the good work guys, and ignore all the naysayers out there who really just don't want to change.

### Post by thaelim on 2011-12-09
For me the killer feature of Gnome Shell is its performance. Over a range of different systems with different hardware, I've never been able to get Compiz (which is part of Unity) running absolutely smoothly, especially on dual monitor setups. And this is not for lack of trying. Whereas Gnome Shell (with one or two minor tweaks) runs perfectly smoothly on every system I've tried it on so far.
