### Post by SteamDonkey74 on 2013-01-11
I dabbled just a little bit in Linux about ten years ago and decided that I was not enough of a tenth level magic user to really thrive in that environment. I had used various UNIX machines in college and done fine on those, but I have to say I am kind of GUI oriented for most things.

Recently, realizing that a budget for a brand-new whiz-bang anything sort of computer just wasn't materializing I bought a second hand laptop with Xubuntu 12.04 LTS pre-loaded. I love it. I love how well thought out so much of this is. The only really frustrating problem I have had is with a particular JAVA based program with which I seem to have problems any time I put it on a new computer, but that program has its own yahoo group and there are people there helping me get it sorted out. 

I have now gone and put Xubuntu on two old Dell desktops and I will be transferring one of those to a friend of mine who has found herself without a working computer in 2013 this weekend. 

Since this is testimonials & experiences I am not going to ask questions here or whatever, but I want to say that I am enjoying this quite a bit, and transferring from a Windows 7/Windows XP/Mac OS X based computing life to a new world of free and open source software has so far been very enjoyable, and I really appreciate all the help I have gotten online so far from various Linux users on various questions.

:guitar:Rock on!
Adam
