### Post by agurk on 2007-04-21
Yep, definitely a thank you from me as well. I am particularly impressed with the way Feisty handles networks; it's now a breeze to assign a static IP to my wireless card whenever I need to, which wasn't that easy before.

I think I'd better touch on the few upgrade issues I've had as well though, for any possible future reference.
My computer is a Lenovo Thinkpad T60 with Intel chipset. It has previously been running stock Edgy without much hassle. Normally, I'm a clean install guy, but this time I decided to dist-upgrade since I'd heard so many praises about it, and indeed it was overall a pleasant experience. The upgrade script, however, ignored the fact that I was using linux-restricted-modules to enable my wireless (Intel 3945). Luckily, I had access to a wired network that I could use for downloading this package, and a reboot later, the wireless was up and running again.
Trying to play an ogg sound file, I noticed that the upgrade had changed my "Open with..." preference from XMMS back to Totem. Ok, no big deal. Worse was the fact that the sound didn't work at all, it was allegedly "blocked". Some quick googling indicated that it was because I had disabled the built-in modem in BIOS (heh, wtf?). Sure enough, another reboot later and the sound was back.
Overall, as I said, a pleasant experience, though the wireless issue has some grandma-killing potential.

Anyway, thanks again! :)

Update: I just noticed that hibernation unexpectedly works again, that's another pleasant surprise.
