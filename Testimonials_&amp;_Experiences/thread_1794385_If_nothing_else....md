### Post by jonathon6017 on 2011-06-30
About a couple weeks ago I wiped OEM windows 7 off my laptop. I installed ubuntu linux. I had to uninstall Ubuntu (and many other distros in my search for linux to work) because I had some hardware difficulties not to mention some games that only worked with Windows. So After I got a Win7 iso and installed it (was more frustrating doing that then dealing with the limitations of linux on my machine) I updated drivers and everything just so that I could get everything working (network wasn't working, graphics would go no higher then 1024x760 After I got that settled I would leave my computer alone and it would produce weird noise on the screen (one guy having the same problem called it 'artifacts') So after about two weeks of putting up with it I figured I would get it solved. After being in the linux crowd so long I figured I would just find a windows 7 forum and ask for help there (the same forum that the other guy posted help for his problem at) So I sign up for the forum, Post my topic asking for help...I come back 8 hours later still no reply. The other guy that asked for help on his thread, (I told him I had the same problems and told potential helpers about my thread with photos and screenshots)  got no replys after I posted.

I checked some other threads on that site some people posted questions and two weeks later still haven't got an answer.

If I hated linux (which I don't) I could at least say that with linux you get help with your problems. I'd post a question and within two hours I typically had some sort of reply and alot of times it only took about 20 minutes or less to get a reply. 

That is one **BIG** difference between linux and windows. If it wasn't for those hardware difficulties and one game that I wanted to play, I would still be with linux I think overall even though it needs some work its about 85% better then windows.
