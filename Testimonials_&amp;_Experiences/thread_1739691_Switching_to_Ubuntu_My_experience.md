### Post by Ken UK on 2011-04-26
Hi everyone,

Let me tell you a story about how I switched to Ubuntu recently, much sooner than I thought I would. Please read bearing in mind Im a new Linux user!

I joined this forum years ago and between then and now have dabled with Ubuntu on and off but never had much luck with it due to stability problems, hardware incompatibilities and support/software which was missing that I required. I also tried other distos but never found one significantly more adequate to run than Ubuntu for my desktop.

Over a year or so ago a family member who was completely new to computers bought a Dell laptop with Vista pre-installed and thus he started to learn how to do basic things, mainly browsing the internet. Previously having no exposure to computers at all he had to learn basic tasks like understanding how the mouse works etc. and out of all the people I have had to teach at many different levels this was the first time I have showed someone with no exposure what so ever.

He used the laptop on and off but found it complicated and confusing to understand, even I had never seen Vista before when I first showed him so even to me it seemed inappropriatly complicated for beginners doing basic taks with very limited flexibility (I had been mainly an XP user at that time).
Sometime after 10.10 came out he had aquired a serious virus that rendered his machine near useless. I managed to clear it up after alot of hard work but later it came back again. When I had a quick go at the 10.10 liveCD I just decided to finally test it on him to avoid the viruses and give him something more simple to use. The clean, simple, no fuss interface was ideal, all he really needed was the power off and the firefox icon most the time.

I installed it alongside his Vista and he's never used Vista since, hes also never phoned me for help (which he did on a regular basis before). I just sat him down one night, installed it, showed him the basics and hes had a play with it without being able to brake anything.

My parents where very happy with the results infact so impressed that they agreed to allow me to install it on their windows XP desktop (which was very slow and clogged up with rubbish, they where very unhappy with it). I did the same and showed them the basics and they have never needed help since, internet, emails, basic word processing, spreadsheet tests etc. They where capable of figuring out alot of the baiscs on their own with little computer knowledge.

With the family happy with it I decided to install it on my Macbook to test it and give it a real world run for my work. It went on great with little problems and I can now boot into both easily.

Most recently though my hard drive aquired the click of death from a simple crash of Windows and force restart (I can't complain though, I've had the computer over 5 years with no hardware replacements) but the problem is that I had an OEM copy of XP so lost it. I bought a hard drive yesterday and decided to install 10.10 straight on it. Its the same computer I have tried before and earlier versions had failed but it seemed to work and be stable (ignoring some non-fatal problems such as the very high fan speed on one of my graphics cards).

So thats how I've found myself moving to Ubuntu, I'm hoping to learn more about it and become more capable in its use and that my next computer will be better suited for it, until then though Im happy to manage like this.

My opinion on using Ubuntu is that it is the leader in Linux and has the potential to really make the difference between just developers using Linux and everyone using Linux. Thats one of the biggest problems with Linux, developers develop it for developers. Sure if you know what you are doing it can be stable and reliable with no problems but most computer users do not and without that knowledge its often not stable or reliable at all, as soon as you come across a problem even a minor one often its "No problem just type this into the terminal then this and this then replace this blah blah" which is too complicated and fiddly for average users, its enough to scare most ordinary people straight off, the terminal needs to become one way of getting things done, not the only way.

Although I like more things than I hate about Ubuntu I must note also I hate the fact often there can be dead ends such as being stuck with open source graphics card drivers meaning flash might not work correctly or wine (I think thats the only flaw to the way parts of Ubuntu are updated so regular mixed with poor support from big companies). I could list a number of problems I've come across, many that don't seem to have an obious solution but I've had lots of problems with Windows too.

I do come across problems when usng Ubuntu such as stability problems and software crashing etc. but no system is perfect and I've just decided to keep with it until it gets better. I believe that once it catches on more and big companies give it better support (especially hardware ones) it will be much more consistent and reliable. I am impressed with many parts of it for sure and it can only get better.

I am a power user who developes Java desktop based software (only as an interest) which is luckily easy on any platform and work in Civil engineering design (infrastructure such as roads, serivces etc.) using specialist software highly reliant on Windows which is a big blockade especially AutoCAD so not much good for that. Im also a student but all the none specialised software I used on Windows were open source programs available on Linux and Mac anyway.

Anyway thanks for reading, I just wanted to give my insight into my experiences.

Ken
