### Post by Flymo on 2011-10-10
We first had a crack at Ubuntu with Breezy, and whilst there's been the odd hiccup in the glorious progression of Linux for Human Beings it has never lasted longer than one release for us.

LTS is the name of the game - we use LTS releases for 'production' day-to-day work, and I install LTS releases for our posse of n00bs (some have since graduated in style) as they enter the fold.

In between LTS releases there is huge fun to be had from having the current intermediate release on a small partition.  Good for getting used to new stuff, and usually another step forward in some directions.

So we (and our n00bs) are all on Lucid Lynx! :D

It's speedy, reliable, regularly updated, and it does the job.

What's worrying me is that nobody I know likes Unity, or that Gnome thing.
I've tried to grasp it, but it seems so completely pointless.  Before anyone suggests that I'm against innovation, hang on a bit.

Back around Ubuntu 8.04 I came to the conclusion that todays letterbox screens (neat way for a manufacturer to give you less pixels per nominal inch, btw) needed the panel(s) at the sides, to give a space between them more suited to the average web page/text page/whatever. 

So I reconfigured Gnome to give vertical panels on L & R - but the right one camped in the middle of the screen after a reboot - had to learn the mysteries of Gnome and twiddle some bits before it worked. Huge fun!:)

Not saying it was much good.  Gnome 2.x Panels didn't like standing on end that much, text  and tooltips misbehaved too. But when the first Netbook remix appeared it was a perfect realisation of the idea in my head, only better.

UNR worked well for us on laptops/netbooks with screens up to about 14", at 15" the interface somehow seems to become too much in yer face, in our view.  Remember, I have the posse of n00bs available for feedback, and they are used mercilessly!  The same GUI also worked pretty well on a TV; not as good as XBMC but better than vanilla Gnome at the distance we view TV.

KDE4 has been less than a success in our community. The only instances of acceptance being those where it most accurately mimics KDE3. <sigh> 

We wanted to like Kubuntu, but....  something about that boxed-in menu and the heavy load on our ancient hardware means that  nobody in our group has stuck with it.  I'm just saying, right?  People like and dislike things for their own reasons.  Doesn't mean Kubuntu isn't ideal for someone with a more recent CPU and more RAM.

Our lot favour Xubuntu and Lubuntu as well as Ubuntu.

Unity is a conundrum.  None of us can fathom what it is for, unless it is attempting to do a Netbook Remix thing for the bigger screen?  Not that successful, it seems.

Really have tried hard to get to grips with it, even installed Natty on a spare partition, but it would not suspend/resume reliably and was therefore wiped.  OK, I admit it, I enjoyed wiping it.  That's not entirely rational, but it is a human response to something that (still) annoys me - and it seems that it annoys all our posse, in varying amounts. 

The kids thought it was a joke!:lol:

Giggled at it and treated it like a game, became bored in ten minutes and wanted 'a proper computer' back again.

Oneiric Beta 2 live CD still won't suspend and resume reliably on our Lucid-happy hardware, and it's got some niggly little changes to the UI that are the subject of rants elsewhere in this forum.  You moved the controls! Just as I was getting used to them!  Understandable, in a frustrated Human Being wanting Linux.  

It's really, really sad that Unity has been so poorly received.  It's even sadder that it should have been released far too soon.  The KDE 4 lesson seems not to have been learned.  

And more. 

How infuriating to be driving along in car - only to find that the controls are on the other side!  OK nobody dies when humans scream at computers, at least not often. But it need not happen with better planning/design.

When whingeing on about anything it is reasonable to offer improvements in a spirit of "It would be wonderful if...."

Wish I could oblige.  :(

Unity is (gulp) even less desirable than that Gnome Shell thing.  Tried to like that, too.  But they both seem to assume things that do not apply to us.  Bling is OK, but only if it does not get in the way of using the machine.  Counterproductive  interface, unlike LXDE, XFCE, JWM, KDE3, Fluxbox, E17, etc.

This is a show stopper for us.  Do hope Unity is fixed (but I cannot explain how) before the lovely Lucid expires and we switch to the next LTS.  Or better still, abandoned for a better approach. 

Back to Fluxbox, anyone?....:guitar:

PS we do love Lucid!
