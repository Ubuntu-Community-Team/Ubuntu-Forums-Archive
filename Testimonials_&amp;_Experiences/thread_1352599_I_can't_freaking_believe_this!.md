### Post by mivo on 2009-12-13
> **cguy said:**
> How did Windows XP acquire ANY share of the netbook market??

Because it actually works on most netbooks. My HP Mini 2140 does not work well with any flavour of Linux. Hibernate and suspend do not work (which is essential for a netbook), microphone does not work, the LEDs do not work properly, and the Broadcom 4122 wireless chipset is just a complete and utter pain in the butt (not the fault of Linux, but doesn't change the situation, especially since most netbooks seem to come with Broadcom).

XP on my netbook is very snappy and responds & boots fast. So, no complaints here. I would use a Linux flavour on it (probably not Ubuntu, but either Mint or Arch), but I didn't buy a netbook to use it like a desktop or to have some of its features not fully working.
