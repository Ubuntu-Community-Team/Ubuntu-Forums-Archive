### Post by dodle on 2008-07-30
For a long time I felt that the Linux community was overly biased against Microsoft.  A couple of years ago I started messing around with OSes and have had at least one installation of Linux on my computer since.  When I first started out I thought Linux was going to be impossible.  recently, I have been feeling just as comfortable with it as I did with Windows.  Even more recently, I have discovered how much more I like it in comparison.  I have been helping my dad with his XP machine and I am going insane!  Here are some things that I love about Linux:

- I have never had a runaway process that I couldn't kill immediately.

- The open source community.  Developers from all over spending their time to make software that runs smoothly and efficiently.  (Rather than a company that is marketing their product and want you to pay $25 to call their support center.)  

- Freedom of customization.  Hundreds of themes that I can download or even create myself, instead of having to patch uxtheme.dll.  Which, even afterwards I don't have the right software to make my own themes.  And if I want it, I've got to buy it.  

- The Linux community itself is wonderful.  The friendly people of the forums have helped me one-hundred times more than any call to a support center.  

- Just plain freedom.  My dad's E-machine broke down and we had to rebuild it.  After installing windows with the product serial number that came with the computer, we couldn't activate it over the internet.  So, I decided to give the company a call.  After battling it out, and jumping from one support center to another, we were finally told that we couldn't legally use the serial because the serial was meant to be distributed to an E-machine computer.  So a perfectly good copy of Windows, which was legally purchased by my father, went to waste.  Ubuntu, and many other linux distributors, will allow me to use a single cd to install on a thousand computers.... or more if I had that many.  

- The linux community works to make open source software work right.  Problems in Windows are blamed on open source software.  

I am tempted to delete my XP partition completely and permanently, but wine still has a way to go and not all of the open source-win32 apps work well on it yet.  Please Linux community, free me of my bonds and break through the "windows" of my suffering!

(Thanks for reading through this all the way.  I just got done working on my dad's computer and really needed to rant)
