### Post by bitserf on 2010-04-28
For kicks, I decided to try installing it on my MacBook Pro.

I haven't used Ubuntu for a while, I spend most of my time in Windows 7 or OS X, and my last Ubuntu foray on the MacBook ended in tears.

Must say I was pleasantly surprised.

Running BootCamp to repartition and installing rEFIt was probably the most complicated part.

Everything else *just worked*.

Compared to my Windows 7 experience on the MacBook (I had to download Windows 7 64-bit Apple drivers from BitTorrent, since Apple refused to provide them for my rev of MacBook!!!)...Ubuntu was seamless.

* Sound works.
* Wireless works.
* Sees my Mac Partition, and mounts it.
* Two fingered scrolling required only to go and tweak a system preference
* SMB network support works fantastically well out of the box, compared to OS X Snow Leopard which is pathetic at it.
* MacBook function keys work out of the box (e.g. Eject)!!
* Media codec support is nice, open the file on SMB-mounted NAS, confirm download, play.

I'm at a loss.

Usually I'd budget a day or two spent trying to get everything working, but I've got nothing to bitch about.

What now? :)
