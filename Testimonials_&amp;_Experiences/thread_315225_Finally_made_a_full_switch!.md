### Post by izalac on 2006-12-08
10 years of using Linux on and off...
...and today I finally made a full switch.

Entire procedure actually started yesterday. I started the painful job of cleaning up all my old data in Windows, and moving the things I wanted to keep to one partition. Then I did it. Windows got nuked and partition erased. Moving partitions around proved to be slooow...
Installed Ubuntu this morning. Decided to use my old and trustworthy Dapper CD. Switched my clock to UTC. Got my system up and running...

Got all my data from NTFS partition on my new ext3 partition. NTFS got nuked, as a last trace of Windows ever being on my computer, and ext3 gleefully spread over its new space.

And now I got my system updated, customized and running full speed :)

The funny thing is, it's still dual-booting, as the second operating system on my computer is my old Ubuntu installation on a 7 gig partition :) I don't know whether to keep it or to remove it - a rescue partition could come in handy, and I've got enough room so far :)

Everything works like a charm.
And I love it.

As I started to use Linux more and more recently, I learned a lot of its logic and workings. I used to realize its power before, but now I learned to utilize a large part of it. This amazing OS and its even more wonderful community encouraged me to finally do the switch.

So cheers to Mark and Ubuntu! Cheers to Ubuntu forums and the community.
