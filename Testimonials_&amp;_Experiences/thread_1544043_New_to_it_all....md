### Post by modelck on 2010-08-02
My love story with Linux OS's actually started from a run in with a cleverly placed CD distro of Ubuntu 6. From then on out I was intrigued at the open source community. 

I pretty much took a chance to dual boot my older Asus 3GHz machine just to see what Kubuntu Lucid could do to give me the edge I was looking for in an OS. I was not disappointed! From the very get-go I was impressed with the look and feel of the OS. Not only did everything work out of the box, I found myself falling in love with that computer all over again. I built it from scratch 7 years ago and now it operates better than my wife's brand new Sony VAIO. 

My favorite feature is the command-line terminal. I find it compelling to work around the OS in a text-based way. I'm eagerly discovering new ways to operate the basics as well as still learning how to do more advanced things.

My other favorite feature is the plasma desktop which is part of KDE's workspace. From widgets to widgets and even more freakin' widgets, I just can't get enough of 'em. 

Besides some of the drawbacks with using newer hardware (i.e. ATI Radeon HD3650) I'm still very impressed and a have become a Linux Lifer. I've been so impressed with it, I've convinced 20 of my friends and family members not to throw that old laptop away but to refurb it with an acceptable alternative. So far all of them have continued using the free and open source software provided by the illustrious members of the greatest community on planet Earth. There are some M$ programs I need to use as I work from home but I still find myself operating in Lucid far more often. In fact, only 3 of the friends have grimaced at the difficulty of a new OS because they are hardwired M$. Even though I'm a noob at Linux and it's derivatives, I find myself troubleshooting all their computer problems via ubuntuforums.org. This was long before I decided to register as a member.

Whether I contribute to the troubleshooting or not, I will continue to show people another way to computing that doesn't involve saving up for the biggest and baddest (unless that's your thing). So far, everything I've thrown at this OS has yet to disappoint me.
