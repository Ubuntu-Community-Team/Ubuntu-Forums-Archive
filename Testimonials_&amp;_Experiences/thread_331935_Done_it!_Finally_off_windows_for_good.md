### Post by drewwa on 2007-01-05
Just wanted to express my joy and thanks at finally being off Windows. :p 

I managed to solve my last remaining issues (printers) by use of turboprint. Not quite ideal (in terms of free and open source), but better than being on Windows.

Can report the whole household is delighted with the better speed, access, stability (big things up compared to WinXP) and the impressive collection of software available. My 6 year old has an old Tecra 8000 and is loving SuperTux, Barrage and KTurtle. My wife is simply enjoying a stable machine without antivirus, firewalls and windows update phoning home every five minutes.

Me, I'm just happy not to have to be a system administrator anymore! Everything just works... extraordinary!

Thanks community... and thanks Ubuntu.

Cheers,

### Post by stalker145 on 2007-01-05
> **forgetso said:**
> this is what i want to do also.  but i was wondering can windows be run in a window on the desktop from ubuntu?  i need it for some games i have

Windows itself *can* be run in a window on Linux. You would use either VMWare Player, VMWare Server, qemu, or something of the sort. It is *not* advisable to use this method for running hardware intensive games, though, as running the guest operating system (Windows) causes a big hardware hit (RAM, CPU cycles) and does not take advantage of any good video/sound card you might have. If you have any games that do not work on Linux or through Wine, you would be best to keep a dual boot setup until you lose interest in the game.
