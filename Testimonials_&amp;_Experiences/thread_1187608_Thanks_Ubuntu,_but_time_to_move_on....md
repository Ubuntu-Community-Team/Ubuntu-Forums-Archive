### Post by adam.ec on 2009-06-14
I've been using Ubuntu since Hoary because it was a distro that just worked out of the box. Apart from the silly Intel HDA sound problems, which incidentally still don't seem to be solved, Ubuntu has been a solid stable platform for my programming development for almost four years.

Then came along Jaunty. Having been given two projects that required Python 2.6 I upgraded immediately, fully willing to take on the Intel chipset problems that have been documented by Canonical. After a fresh install everything seemed to be working okay. I haven't had any of the video lag problems or slow boot times that everyone else has suffered.

But after a week the 'real' problems started to become apparent:

USB memory management is a nightmare. I save files to my USB keys (fat16 and fat32) and they don't show in Windows. I bring them back to my machine for checking and I have to go through changing all the permissions before I can touch them. They are all there though. 

Gimp has horrendous white blocks all over every graphic I create. I have reinstalled, rolled back a version, tried the Intel video solutions to no avail. This version works flawlessly in Fedora and Arch (which I'm moving on to).

Files transfered from other folders to the Desktop don't appear. According to the shell, the files are there but some of them suddenly appear after some, as yet, unidentified event. The rest never appear.

Firefox has got terrible problems. Apart from going greyscale and locking up my machine for 3-4 minutes randomly I have lost the history list that appears on the address bar. I click on the dropdown button and nothing appears. Sometimes I will get it after seven or eight clicks, but usually nothing. It also randomly loses all of it's settings including the homepage. The flash plugin also keeps failing completely and alerts me that it needs to be installed. I've installed it three times now using the solutions on Ubuntu forums.

I haven't been able to burn a single CD without Brasero crashing. I thought coaster CD's were a thing of the past.

Bluefish doesn't remember any of the preferences I set it. It always reverts to it's old directory paths for my files and gives me the starting message every time it opens, even though I click 'Do not display this message again'.

At least 50% of the applications I have installed so far I've had to create manual menu items for. Gnome just clearly doesn't add new apps to my menu.

Openoffice 3.0 seems to ignore large numbers of mouse clicks. Sometimes the mouse pointer jumps across the screen and selects text on it's own.


I thought originally that I had a hard drive problem on the machine. I've run diagnostics on it and it appears to be fine. Arch and Fedora both operate without problems on here as well. I have installed Jaunty on another machine that has a more up-to-date Intel chipset and still have the same problems.

With the mainstream applications I am running it is hard to think that all of them have become unstable and as they work on two other distros it seems to point the finger at Jaunty. It is unfortunate that Linux is just getting its chance to break into the desktop market, particularly since Vista has punched a few noses and the best mainstream distro provider launches Jaunty. 

At least for the next few months I will shift over to Arch as my main platform until Canonical apologises with 9.10. It is not as straight forward as Ubuntu but it still offers up-to-date packages and more importantly, a stability that just isn't there with Jaunty. It's also the only distro I've used that my Intel HDA sound works with too.

I'll keep up with the Ubuntu news on the forums. Just in case Canonical release 9.10 as an emergency replacement for 9.04. Good luck in the meantime everyone.
