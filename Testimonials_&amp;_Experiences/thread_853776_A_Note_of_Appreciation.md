### Post by Jay Car on 2008-07-08
Here's my testimonial...

Over the years I've followed news about Linux avidly, the progress has been astounding and fun to watch.  But for every encouraging article, or blog, or news story I'd read there were always either comments or other articles explaining why Linux was still too difficult and still not ready for "grandma".  It was disheartening...

...did I mention that I am a grandma?  :)

The release of Vista, the despicable behavior of Microsoft  over ODF, the patent threats, and last but certainly not least, the SCO litigation(s), all made it very clear to me that I needed to stop playing with Live CDs and stop with the dual-booting, and just get away from Windows entirely.  

Also, because so many of my friends and family began asking about whether to get computers with Vista, I had to find something better to recommend...in other words, it was time for grandma to stop talkin' about it and take the plunge herself.

So I did, as of January 2008, my main work computer has Ubuntu installed, no Microsoft product has ever touched this computer and never will.  

In the following months I've managed to convince several other people to either let me set up Ubuntu on new "naked" computers for them, or to buy Macs, or at the very least to stay with XP.  I'm rather proud of doing that. I'm sure Microsoft won't miss those few thousand dollars they didn't get this year, but it's satisfying just the same. 

So, to all those who have declared Linux to be "not ready for" and "too difficult for" Grandma, I'm here to tell ya, Grandma is up to the challenge and she is definitely happy to learn (annoying though she may be at times).  

What was especially interesting to me was, after tearing apart and cleaning my old Win2k machine, and reinstalling Win2k at the same time I was installing Ubuntu and Mandriva on two other computers, the difference in the degree of difficulty between the Win2k install, and installing the other two, was amazing. 

By comparison, the win2k install was like a root canal, and the Ubuntu and Mandriva installs were like a drive through the park in a chauffer-driven limo. Even having all the correct drivers for that old machine (a VP6 with dual PIIIs), it took time to get them all set up and working.  

The other two machines just worked.  Yes, wireless worked, and even the dialup modem worked for the friend that doesn't have access to cable or DSL. I kept waiting for the difficult part, but it never showed up. Installation is definitely NOT too difficult. 

Of course, I've learned how to make sure of the hardware first, so that made things easier too.  But still, it was a great experience.  

My friends have simple needs for their computers, so I've been able to help them with learning their way around (so far).  But I have been working to gain more understanding so I can help with more complex things later on. 

In the process I've done some crazy things to my own computer, skunked the sound and did some other dopey things, but it was all fixable, and I have truly enjoyed the learning experience.   

I am also learning new software so I can do my work without ever resorting to the old Windows machine again and I'll admit, that part has been more difficult, but it just takes patience.

Every week I've used that old VP6 less and less.  At this point it hasn't even been turned on since mid-June.  I'm trying to figure out what to install on it when I remove Windows.  Ubuntu doesn't seem to work well with it, but someone suggested Puppy Linux (any other suggestions?), so I'll probably start on that this weekend.

Anyway, it's the "Learning" part that brings me to the point of this little testimonial (yes, I promise, I'm almost finished)...

The Ubuntu forums helped make adjusting to a Window-less computer much easier.  Reading through the forums these last few months I've been so impressed at the patience, encouragement and genuine kindness that's shown to those of us that ask questions.  

I've also seen some of the rudest, most impatient and disrespectful, demanding posts from people claiming to want help, but who really just seem to have a need to complain.  Yet, I have not seen any of them treated disrespectfully.  

My own experiences on this forum have been very positive and I want to say thank you for that.  What an incredible amount of time and work all of you contribute to us newbies. All I have to pay you back with is a lot of gratitude, just wanted to let you all know how appreciated you are!! 

Okay, I'm finished! 
:)
