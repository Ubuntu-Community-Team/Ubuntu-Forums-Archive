### Post by Juan Largo on 2011-06-12
I installed Ubuntu 11.04 on a virtual machine. I'm impressed by the look and feel of the Unity desktop.  Although Unity isn't as configurable as KDE (my current desktop), I think it definitely has a niche for a lot of users.  After installation, everything is all set up and ready to go OOTB, which is great for newbies.  (This reminds me of OS X in that regard.)

The installation process was very simple and straightforward, although it took a bit longer than I'm used to with other distros.  I'm guessing that a lot of stuff was being downloaded from the Internet during the installation process, and my broadband connection isn't the fastest in the world.

It's very easy to switch back and forth between Unity and Gnome, and the overall response is snappy and smooth.  Although I prefer KDE, I would definitely recommend Ubuntu 11.04 to people who want to try Linux for the first time.
