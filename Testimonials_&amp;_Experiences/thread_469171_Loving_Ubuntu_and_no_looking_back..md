### Post by drascus on 2007-06-09
I use to Run Windows XP on My Compaq Laptop. It was fine for a while I was young and foolish. I soon learned the awful truth. My computer began to slow. Firewalls, Virus scanner, Spyware blocker, Defrag, Registry cleaner. I don't know if I was ever productive on my Windows computer as I spent most of my time fixing it or trying to regain a bit of performance on it. Then I hear of Ubuntu on Cnet.com TV. I installed it on my computer had a bit of a problem with Wifi. I promptly fixed it with the help of the community. Then everything just worked. I was liberated, I joined the free software foundation. I helped on the Forums. I installed Ubuntu on my moms computer and she learned how to use it in about 15 mins. I like some of my windows apps and found Wine the perfect match to run them. I tried windows for a little while about a week ago as a Virtual machine.  I hated it so much I gave up pretty quickly. Now when I see people running long and dull scans on their computer or complaining about performance. I just laugh. Windows has made the world complacent to PC's which don't work. People expect that they will get viruses or that their computer will crash. So they don't complain. Ubuntu has offered an OS that shows what good computing is all about. Its about fun and productivity. Not about worrying what may happen every time you press that little on button. Thanks Ubuntu and all the community members I am humble and grateful to be a Ubuntu user for life. 
~Mike~

### Post by flewis7777 on 2007-06-09
I have a Compaq presario which came with windows XP. 

One day microsoft sent me an update wanting to install spyware to ensure
my windows OS was "legal and genuine." Microsoft saw me (their customer)
as a potential threat. I said to hell with this!

I reached for my Ubuntu CD and did a full install, no partitioning. Wiped it
all clean.  I have Edgy and I have been relying on it for 100% of my home
computing needs for 6 months.

Bought some books, replaced all (now erased) windows software with Linux equivalents.
Learned how to use GCC, Allegro, Synaptic, command line, replaced notepad with
gedit, use firefox web browser (better than explorer IMO). 

I would not go back to windows now. I consider windows to be inferior. Popular, yes, 
but it's general motors compared to BMW. Which would you rather have?

Windows still has the edge with games, but Linux will catch up.

:popcorn:

### Post by mikeua89 on 2007-06-09
I, too, am having a great experience with Ubuntu, but I haven't jumped in all the way yet.  I'm still dual booting my laptop between Ubuntu and Windows XP - just in case.

I've been trying to switch to Linux for a couple of years now, but until I heard about Ubuntu, I didn't have much luck in finding a flavor that I thought was user-friendly enough.  A co-worker told me about Ubuntu a couple of months ago, and after a some starts and stops, I'm on my way.

My advice to anyone thinking of making the switch from Windows is keep at it, continue researching and experimenting, and sooner or later, it will all come together.

### Post by voxman69 on 2007-06-10
It just gets better and better! Got rid of Vista pretty quickly after installing Ubuntu (april 19th) and what do you know? Not once, I repeat, not once have I found myself needing it! Every little (and big) thing I used to do in Windows I can do in Ubuntu too, and then some. 
I even understand some of the lines I type in the Terminal now! :-)

In Windows I had to reboot quite frequently to get back performance, in Ubuntu it's up and running for weeks and the reason I reboot is just because some of the updates requires it. I can live with that! :-)

Right now I'm learning and enjoying Nautilus scripts. I have found a few scripts for queing songs in XMMS, resize multiple images, open File Browser and Terminal as root.
Being (used to be anyway) a web developer my next goal is to learn how to write my own scripts for my own specific needs.

Oh...and I'm still VERY impressed with Beryl!
