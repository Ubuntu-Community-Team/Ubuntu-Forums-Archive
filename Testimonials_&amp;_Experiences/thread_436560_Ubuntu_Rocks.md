### Post by cricketshadowolf on 2007-05-07
I am a 47 year old grandma who used to struggle with Debian. I have been using Ubuntu for over 2 years now.  My very first computer was a pile of parts a friend of mine gave me. He dumped them on the floor and said "Build!" then once I got that done he handed me three Debian disks and said "Install!" then he left for three  months.  Boy was that a exercise in stubborness and trial and error. Mostly error! LOL! I could barely do email at the time. Well, that computer got lost in storage after a move. So, I got another couple of old computers off an ex that he gotten from a dude that sold him all his excess computer junk. Chet thought he got a deal. When he found out he hadn't he dumped it all on me. He thought he was being a jerk. Of course I built a couple of good working computers outta them all. Then they were running Windows 98  and such. So, I asked my friend for some Debian disks and he said he would burn me some but never got around to it. So, I purchased some Linux disks off a website. In the mixed lot was a Ubuntu Hoary disk. I have been a faithful Ubuntu user since. Ubuntu isn't perfect. But nothing made by humans ever is. It sure beats Windows any day of the week or any other time. All the support on this forum and on others keeps me computing happily. Many of my friends now use Ubuntu. I posted a thread on [www.hipforums.com](www.hipforums.com) and got a lot of other folks to try Ubuntu. They haven't posted any complaints back at me yet. There is a large Ubuntu fan base on that forum and we all help each other. Isn't that what Ubuntu is all about! I am using Xubuntu on an old Dell deaktop at the moment. And it runs just fine. I would not trade my Ubuntu for all the Windows in the world. Even if they gave me all the software for free and supported it as well as the Ubuntu folks. Ubuntu has saved me lots of gray hairs. Now I can surf from my rocker with ease. And the new PySolfFC at sourceforge is way cool. I got it loaded with all my golden oldies tunes and I beat old Sol to the tunes of the 60's,70's, and 80's. Now that is cool. Now if I can keep up with you young'uns in the computer world with the help of Ubuntu and its awesome support systems then I guess any body can. 

UBUNTU ROCKS!  Hooray for Penguin Power! 

A very satisfied customer, Cricketshadowolf
