### Post by gjtoth on 2007-05-04
I got my first taste of Windows Vista last evening.  A friend of mine got a brand new eMachine with the Home Edition "supposedly" already installed on it.  Lemme tell ya -- that SOB had to restart no less than EIGHT times before it settled in.  THEN, it automatically loaded some AOL crap, the McAffee Security Suite (free for the first 30 days -- then ya gotta pay them a monthly subscription), and a bunch of other crap.  I figured I'd be in & out of there in an hour.   Right.  This is a BRAND-NEW 4 GHZ with 1 gig of RAM and it ran like a snail!  Of course, McAffee in their infinite wisdom and marvelous programmers decided it was a great idea to run a FULL SCAN on a brand new machine that had never been on the web!  Nice touch.  Anyways, after I got all the crap uninstalled, it did run SLIGHTLY better... not much.  I had to really search for the free version of AVG.  They don't just pop it out there like they used to.  Oh yeah, the layout for EVERYTHING is different so I had to resort to the help feature just to locate stuff!  
 
Anyways, after I finally got it set up, I muttered something like, "I'm glad I don't use this crap."  To which my friend replied, "Oh? There's something better?"  He is now QUITE happily running Ubuntu at blazing speed.  Of course, he and his wife only want to surf, check email and occasionally do a word processor thing... I showed them how to get photos directly from the memory stick on their camera and their chins hit the ground.  So, did a quick lesson on how to burn 'em to CD/DVD.  Wanna know how many times the system had to restart after I installed Ubuntu?  Once.  Vista balked at his old Lexmark Z52 printer -- needed drivers.  Ubuntu picked right up on it.  Oh yeah, the "new" desktop cube that MS is touting?  He didn't have the horsepower to do it under Vista!!  Ubuntu has absolutely no problem doing it!

Vista?  No thanks.
