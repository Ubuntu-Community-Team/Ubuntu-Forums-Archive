### Post by oldsoundguy on 2011-03-24
a tip on installing wireless .. any desktop or laptop.
Install your operating system and HARD WIRE the computer (through the network connection) to the internet.  Do your install and updates.  
Shut down and disconnect from the internet.
re-boot, and the network manager should appear in the panel if your networking set up is a valid one (many are now).
Open the manager .. fill in the blanks .. and you should be connected.

I have had nothing but problems when I do NOT use this method.

### Post by TBABill on 2011-03-24
Ok, to be fair, some devices are supported with open drivers (Realtek) and still won't work out of the box sometimes (blacklist the rt2x00 driver series to get a rt2860 working, for instance). It really can be a pain in the rear to get them going. However, it should be a pain one time. Get it working, keep notes and enjoy. Even if you require proprietary drivers like my Broadcom....no big deal on any distro.
 
I think the perspective is skewed. Windows 7 just works....well, duh, when you have it preinstalled it darn sure better just work. And you get a nifty backup just in case it gets borked. And if you buy from certain companies you also get a reinstall disk that also has all the drivers on it for that machine. But here's the test....go install from a Windows 7 disk from Target, Staples or some other retailer. Basic OS. See how fun that is to get going on all the hardware and how much time it takes. For mine it took over 2 hours plus another bit of time for Office plus updates.
 
Then take one of your machines, search the forums and vendors for info on your driver, take the path of least resistance and keep a record of how you did it. I bet it's still a shorter period of time to install Ubuntu, research, fix or install whatever drivers are needed and start using the machine.
 
It sometimes is a pain. It's also NOT Windows. Not all hardware is supported as well as others, and some not at all, and that does suck. However, I have yet to own a machine I cannot configure now in minutes to a full ready desktop. I can get 6 Ubuntu machines up and running in the time it takes for 1 Windows machine if both are from a fresh install. Having the drivers is surely more convenient in Windows, but the rest is such a pain that I'd rather deal with drivers.
