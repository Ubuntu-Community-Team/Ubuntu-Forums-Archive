### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-05-05
> **migs73 said:**
> Always I read through these types of threads and the same patterns develop. One person praises the new version (everything just works etc.), the next says it horrible (this broke, that broke, etc.). 
Both of you are right, there is no argument to be had. I have had a flawless upgrade from Karmic, and therefore think Lucid is great, but I am sure if it had broke all my stuff I would think it was rubbish.

I think you are completely right, most people praise or complain based solely on their own experience, which really means nothing.

In my case, though, I don't like Lucid because of other reasons, which have nothing to do with my specific personal experience.  My main issue with Lucid is that I see lots of decisions being taken, but mostly in the wrong direction.  The implementation is also far from good.

**1.- Social desktop and the ME menu:**  I like the idea of such integration, but not the choice of applications.  I personally don't like Gwibber, so I would uninstall it and get tweetdeck, which simply does not get the integration offered.  Same goes with empathy.  I think social tools are probably most suited for "petpeeves", they are a very personal choice, so trying to get everybody to accept the same schema is pointless in my opinion.  I think only people who start using Ubuntu from scratch may like this feature and use it as is.  

In other words, this feature is a waste of time and effort, I doubt it gives Ubuntu any competitive advantage over any other Linux distro or OS.

**2.- PiTiVi in / GIMP out:**  I know you can easily uninstall/install, but that's not the point.  I just don't understand the reasoning behind Canonical's decision.  GIMP was supposedly a tool aimed at professionals...  How many of those who found GIMP complicated and overpowered will actually be interested in video editing?  I just don't get it.  If they wanted to free up LiveCD space, then just do so, don't stick a video editor in!

**3.- Branding change: ** I really think this change is not up to scratch.  It's not just about changing colors, this branding change was a declaration of intentions, a way of saying that Canonical was raising the bar and was up to the task of playing the major leagues.  

Simply put, if I compare Ubuntu 10.04 branding to either Win7 or Mac OS X, it sucks.  Canonical should have waited until the branding update was completely finished, polished and mature.  On top of that, it makes more sense to release the new branding changes on 10.10, so they are 100% perfect by the time they get to an LTS release.  10.04 users will now be stuck with the current incomplete branding for 2/3 years!
[B]
4.- Removal of HAL to enhance boot and resume times:[/B]  This is a good feature, I reckon.  However, while boot times are indeed faster now, it seems shutdown times are slower than they were in Karmic.  Was this intended?

**5.- Application updates:** I won't mention Firefox, GNOME and other application upgrades, because they can easily be obtained in other older Ubuntu releases or other distros.

Now, on to the things that are _plain misses_:

**1.- WHO DECIDED TO GET ALL THESE CHANGES INTO AN LTS RELEASE?!?!?**  It makes absolutely no sense.  Corporations couldn't care less about branding changes, a social desktop or a ME menu.  For the most part, those companies probably keep IM ports and social networking sites closed anyways!  

They do care about consistency, they want a very solid and conservative approach so that they don't have to spend insane amounts of money fixing upgrade/install problems.  They do care about users not wasting a minute adjusting to window buttons moving to the left.

In my opinion, Canonical made a suicidal move on the corporate desktop arena, probably losing the little credibility they had gained.  They should have concentrated all efforts into fixing old bugs and making 10.04 as good as possible in terms of performance and stability.

**2.- BUGS!**: Ubuntugeek has published a number of articles about bugs and workarounds that apply to Lucid:

[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/known-ubuntu-10-04lucid-lynx-issuesbugs-with-workarounds.html]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/known-ubuntu-10-04lucid-lynx-issuesbugs-with-workarounds.html")

[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-firefox-slow-problem-in-ubuntu-10-04lucid.html]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-firefox-slow-problem-in-ubuntu-10-04lucid.html")

[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-ubuntu-10-04-lts-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup.html]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/how-to-fix-ubuntu-10-04-lts-lucid-blank-screen-at-startup.html")

There are more that could be considered issues, but these are the ones I consider most important.  The last of those articles was published just 4 days after Lucid was released...  Come on, people, this is an LTS, supposed to be rock solid.  I can't help but feel like a Microsoft user again!!

Btw, before anybody goes there, I did test Beta and RC releases, and did NOT have any of the issues above, while I do have some on the Final release now (W T F?)

In short, I think Canonical failed in how they positioned their LTS release, making several choices that are difficult to understand, to put it mildly.  I think it would have been smarter to play it conservative, release an almost bugfree version of Karmic with application updates.  Once released, start working on branding changes, social integration, etc., from 10.10 on.  By approaching it that way, they would be fully ready to smoothly incorporate these changes on 12.04, instead of rushing them in at this stage.
