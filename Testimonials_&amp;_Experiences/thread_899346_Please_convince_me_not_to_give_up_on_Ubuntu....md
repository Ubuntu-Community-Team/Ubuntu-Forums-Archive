### Post by Stikky on 2008-08-24
Alright, everyone, this may seem like a strange post but I have reached my breaking point.. I have had Ubuntu and, recently, xubuntu on my loyal little Sony VAIO for well over a year now and the time has come, I am going back to XP. As much as I wanted Ubuntu to work for me, I just think it's not ready yet and here are my reasons...

1. The most important one - Ubuntu is NOT, despite what people would like to believe, user friendly and it is FAR from idiot proof. I'm not the biggest tech-head but I find the inability to do the simplest of tasks without entering a password or something is beyond frustrating. I log in, using my username and password and then as soon as the desktop loads, I'm asked for my "default keyring" password. Why it can't just remember which network I wanna connect to is beyond me. Then if I wanna install a program? I need to re-enter my password. It's just frustrating.

2. On the note of installing programs, I cannot, for the life of me, work out how this works. I open the Synaptec Package manager or whatever it's called, mark something for installation (if I can find it through the useless search feature) it installs, then the screen goes back to how it was and I am given NO choice or even information about where it's installed to. If it doesnt show up under "applications", I'm screwed, I have no idea what it's called or where it went, it is just ridiculous.

3. Dual monitors.. Wow, don't get me started on this.. I like to use my little laptop to do uni work, I'd rather do it on my 19" LCD than the 12" low res screen on my 4.5yr old laptop or, maybe I wanna watch a movie on my projector using the VGA input? Nope, not possible, apparently. I have spent countless hours trying to get this working and it used to spaz out under ubuntu and under xubuntu, there isnt even a damn option! I have read the tutorials, I have tried my hardest and I just don't know what to do, is it THAT hard to include this kind of thing?

4. Open Office. I'm sorry, I like to support the open source community (even though I know NOTHING about software development) but open office is just terrible, it really is. It saves things in stupid formats so I can't print them if I don't remember to change the extension..

5. Printing.. Heaven forbid I wanna get anything on to paper from linux, it won't see any of the printers on my network (which all the XP computers share quite happily) so my best option is to print to PDF and then put it on a thumb drive. Hardly ideal..

I think that's about it for now, a fairly short list but all things I have been putting up with for over a year now that I just can't take anymore. I write this post not because I hate linux or because I hate ubuntu or xubuntu, but because people out there are saying that ubuntu is now a viable alternative to XP when I wholeheartedly believe it is NOT! No one can say I didn't give it a chance, no one can say I didn't try to learn to use it because I did. The fact of the matter is that even with all the online support and information, a lot of it is in a language most people do not understand, command line interface means NOTHING to me, I just don't get it and every time I see someone say "oh that's easy to fix, just type "sudo apt get something T@&((@*Y$" it may as well be gibberish to me.

So, for these reasons I am considering leaving ubuntu behind until it really is ready for the average person, unless someone can convince me otherwise...?

### Post by adult swim on 2008-08-24
i agree with owenll.  you might not be the biggest tech-head but if you look the answers are out there.  the first time i installed ubuntu, i wiped my windows partition because i didnt read what i was clicking.  i was mad and reinstalled windows.  when i figured out how to partition my hard drive 6 months later i installed ubuntu again.  my wireless card didnt work and my video card wasnt working like it should.  this time, however, i wanted to find out why things werent working.  i searched google for my problems and sure enough every problem that i had was cached in this forum.  all i had to do was read what people had suggested and everything worked after following their directions.  in the beginning i was just typing random stuff in the terminal and it fixed things.  i realized that instead of just typing in what others had suggested i should see if i couldnt learn something about linux while i was troubleshooting.  now i feel naked without having the CLI to fall back on.  in most cases, if you do not want to use CLI there is no need to.  in many cases, the CLI does what you want it to much more efficiently and way easier than a GUI would.
you think that your computer asking for a password is a bad thing.  if your computer was in a mesh wire cage and it wasnt connected to the internet you would be right.  there would be no need for passwords for any reason.  the truth is, if they want to, people can get to your data.  passwords arent a bad thing.  
as was mentioned earlier, if you want to have the default options, with no room to make anything how you want it, then windows or osx is the desktop environment for you.  that will keep you "nice and tidy" and do most of the things you want to do.  when you are doing them though, you will not learn a thing about your computer.  that might not be a big deal to you, but to me and most people running linux, learning is where its at!
