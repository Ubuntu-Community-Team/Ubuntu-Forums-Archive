### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-11-27
I have an old P3 system (8.04) & an P4 system (10.04). Both are  running without issue. Granted the P3 tends to be slow due to the single  core speed, bus, video card and max volume of ram. But it still plugs  along and does it's intended function. It's our finance system and is  dedicated to investments, checking, billing, printing envelopes, etc. We  don't consider it when talking about our 5 systems, as it basically  performs one function. Be it an extremely important function.
 
 The P3 is rock stable and since installing Ubuntu - has never failed. A rose by any other name is still a rose. I  believe it has an old NVIDIA card, which has never been changed. I did  replace a power supply in it once (before installing Ubuntu), but other  than that the P3 just keeps going - like the energizer bunny. :smile:  Of course we keep the system backed up regularly, but would do that with any system that maintains our finances.
 
 I've rebuilt a number of older machines (installing either 7.04 or 8.04)  for individuals that have limited resources and they are still using  them. They don't expect it to fly around the net or preview photos with  lighting speed.

IMHO "wants" are significantly different than "needs" and should only be  consider based on available expendable income. Never credit. Indentured  servitude, should only occur based on "need". If one can't afford a  "want" then logic might suggest they should save until they can. :wink:

Obviously, lancest is taking responsibility for no unnecessary spending  on wants - based on living within their means. I believe that's not only  admirable but intelligent.
