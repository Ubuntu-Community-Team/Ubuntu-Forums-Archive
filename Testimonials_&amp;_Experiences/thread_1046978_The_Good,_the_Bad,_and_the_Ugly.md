### Post by solwic on 2009-01-21
So I got a new ASUS/nVidia 8400GS video card today, and replaced my ATI X1300 with it.  Thought I'd give the experience a rundown here, of switching hardware with Ubuntu:

THE GOOD:  Desktop effects finally (FINALLY!) working on my system.  Doubled the video memory (256mb to 512mb), so things are much faster in that regard.  Also the new card led me to a reinstall (will explain) which gave me the chance to finally separate my /home to its own partition (by the way, is 92GB too much for "/"?).  PC is quieter and cooler (think the fan was going on the ATI) and a little peppier in the video department.

THE BAD:  Had to reformat after realizing this game I want to play (Oblivion...got a weird urge to play it...I'm not even a gamer....) won't load up in Wine.  Also, I'm out $65 (not complaining) because I suspect Canonical might be in bed with nVidia.....

THE UGLY:  I swore I'd never do it.  I swore I'd chop off an arm, shoot the computer, or run away to Pango Pango before I ever did it again...but I did it anyway.  I re-installed Windows XP and gave it 100GB of my 500GB drive.  I'm running Oblivion on it, and maybe GTA:SA.  I hate it there...just knowing it's on my drive makes me nauseous.

Now...I assure you I'm writing this from Ubuntu.  The first thing I did after the updates/virus/spyware/video drivers/firewall/firefox/googlepack install was pop my Ubuntu CD in and reinstall.  But still...I feel dirty, used...like a piece of meat.  I even had to call Microsoft to have my key activated.  I had to *talk* to them....

I think I need counseling.  Or an exorcism.  I don't know.  But I didn't have a choice...Wine wouldn't do it, and VM doesn't do DirectX....

Still...new card is rockin', and my 'Buntu is lookin' kickass.  :)

P.S.:  The hardware change didn't cause the format.  I deactivated the ATI driver, shut down, switched cards, and activated the 177 nVidia driver and rebooted...no problems at all.  The reformat was my stupid *ss.  Ubuntu handled the switch just fine.  :)
