### Post by nuvo on 2006-04-17
Hello.
I've been tinkering with Linux off and on for about 2 years now.
I haven't really taken much on until now as I've always had problems with the major distro's.
I started out using Mandrake (tried 8.1 and 9.2), but it never managed to set itself up properly, leaving me with either a messed up display or no sound (both versions had one of these issues) and getting my USB WiFi working was, well, impossible (it was an old Actiontec kit reccomended by NTL:, my broadband provider at the time).
As that was a failure, I returned to XP for a while and dabbled in LiveCD's a bit.
Knoppix was nice and it was the only distro that I managed to get my WiFi working with as it had an old version of NDISwrapper (it was a fairly old LiveCD that I forgot I had), but newer versions of it no longer support it and I've noticed it can be pretty touchy about changes to your hardware (I installed a second 40Gb HDD that's almost identical to my origional and Knoppix wouldn't boot unless I burned off another disc).
SuSE just annoyed me as it lacked some things and I can't say YaST is something I'm a fan of.

Not too long ago, a few friends from another forum started using Ubuntu.
Having looked into Debian, but never installing it due to the apparent slowness of updates, I thought I'd take a look.
I burned off a copy of Ubuntu 5.10 and set about my HDD once again.
I removed SuSE and put Ubuntu in it's place along side XP without any problems (the installer may not be as pretty, but it's fairly simple to use).
I'd previously used KDE since that's what the other distro's came with, but it was a nice change to boot Ubuntu to find a less irritating desktop environment in the form of GNOME (KDE has shadows and such, but I wanted something clean, functional and easy to customise).

Initially, I was stuck offline due to the annoying WiFi dongle, but when I chose to get a VoIP phone from BT (my current broadband provider), they sent me a new modem \ WiFi router \ bluetooth VoIP box and my dad swapped the old one for a mobile phone.
I chose to throw in the USB connector and to go and buy a PCI WiFi card from PC World (two actually as they were only £20 and I have 2 PC's).
Ubuntu picked it up straight away and I got online in no time.

I prefer Ubuntu to Windows XP as GNOME suits me better, Linux doesn't run like trash on my system and Synaptic \ apt really makes life easier.

I'll admit, along the way to getting my system up and running, I've broken things and reinstalled (I added a new graphics card and hooked up a 17" HDTV, but had to reconfigure the display properties as it didn't detect the right resolutions, but I messed up), but now I have a pretty good system running and Ubuntu's even outdone XP at detecting Microsoft's own hardware (I have a Microsoft wireless optical mouse and XP didn't find it even though it was plugged in before booting while Ubuntu detected it even though I plugged it in when the system was alrwady running).

I can't say I moved to Linux through fear of viruses and such because I've never got a virus (my dad has, but I suppose being behind 2 hardware firewalls, 1 software firewall and antivirus software helps), but the overall idea of it just appealed (especially not waiting a month while the developers work on a patch to close a bug that could cripple your system).

I've been pretty much non-windows since February (unless you cunt the xbox 360) as I do a bit of web and desktop programming (Delphi) and I didn't want to give that up.
The only piece of web design kit I didn't have was fireworks, but Inkscape isn't that bad and once I get Lazarus running properly, I'll have nothing to go back for and I'll end up runnig Dapper, not Vista.
