### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-03-27
I just installed Ubuntu 11.04 Alpha 3 on my computer, over the top of 10.10.

The installer now has a new option: Upgrade from 10.10.

This option was also kind/smart enough to note what software I had installed on the old 10.10, and start downloading and installing the newer versions on 11.04. Very impressed. I think it could do with giving a bit more feedback on exactly what it's doing (for instance, what package it's installing) and provide a "skip" option for those who don't want to wait hours for the software to download and install, but it's a great feature. You can, actually, force-quit the installer while it's downloading packages.

When I booted my new system, no Nvidia driver was available in Additional Drivers; so no Unity. Or so I thought! I enabled the experimental Nouveau 3D driver and it works brilliantly. A couple of years ago I couldn't even get 2D Nouveau to work properly, now I've got a 3D accelerated desktop with Unity on open-source drivers, that works almost perfectly.*

I was also impressed to find that Unity had picked up the launchers on my old Gnome panel and on my desktop, and added them to its own launcher. How convenient! And when I used Software Center to install a program, it asked me if I wanted to add the program to my Unity launcher.

I'm really happy with my new system, it absolutely rocks. Thank you!

*It crashes my computer when I use popup menus in Chromium, and the computer won't start up again until I make sure all the power is drained from the motherboard. That's not so good on Nouveau's part, but I'll make allowances for that because it's experimental.
