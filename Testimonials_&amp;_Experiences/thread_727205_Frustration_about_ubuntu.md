### Post by SpongeBob SquarePants on 2008-03-17
Hi Joseph,

I understand your pain. However, I do have a few thoughts on the matter. Perhaps Ubuntu isn't the distro for you. Have you tried any of the others? Speaking as a Linux user of over ten years......some distro's definitely suit different hardware configurations better.

Case in point, my own. On my newly built machine Ubuntu won't run at all without all manner of faffing about. I need to insert cheat codes to actually get the live CD working, and even after installation I have sound and graphics problems. Can they be resolved? Yes they can. But not without time, trouble and learning on my part.

On that very same machine PCLinuxOS loads without any problems at all, installs without any problems at all, and detects every bit of hardware I have. My graphics work superbly, my sound sounds fine, and it even detects my wifi card. With Simply Mepis, apart from choosing the vesa boot option and using the "noapic" cheat code, it all installs fine. Even my printer works without any tinkering.

But try SUSE andI I have problems. Try Mandriva and I have problems. Some distros I can't even get to boot. Some fall apart during installation.

My point is, different distro's have different capabilities when it comes to detecting hardware. Just because Ubuntu is giving you a headache, doesn't mean that if you try a different distro things won't be better. Of course, they may be even worse. But my experience is that with a bit of patience, you can find a distro that likes your hardware, and suits your needs. Ubuntu is a great distro....but people do have problems with it. I have problems with it. I am quite happy to work on those problems...because I like tinkering with computers. Others may not feel that way...and of course that's valid too.

But if you truly aren't all that jazzed with Windows, and want to try the Linux route, why not just download another couple of distro's and give them a shot? Most are free....so there's not a great deal to lose.

And I know people that have a terrible time with Windows. It always crashes. It never runs smoothly. I personally have no problems with XP. But for some systems it absolutely stinks as  an OS.
