### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-08
I'm saddened that people feel the need to hype up Ubuntu. It doesn't "just work." The only operating system that "just works" is OS X (because the software and hardware are designed for each other), and [even that has problems sometimes.](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-80341.html)

Worse yet, the recommendation of 64-bit Ubuntu for a new user... not the smartest recommendation. As you can see from [this poll](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=403064), most 64-bit users use the 32-bit edition of Ubuntu.

I generally start people off with [Is Ubuntu for You?](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=63315) and try to take them through baby steps instead of offering Ubuntu as a "just works" panacea for all Windows problems.

As for a knowledgebase, here's a good start:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport)

If you do decide to give Ubuntu another shot, I hope you don't believe the hype, you do a bit of research first, and you take baby steps and ask for a lot of help... or buy a preinstalled Dell Ubuntu computer when those computers become available.
