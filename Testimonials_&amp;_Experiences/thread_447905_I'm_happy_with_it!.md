### Post by Tsu Dho Nimh on 2007-05-18
I even wrote about it, and will be writing more "How to" for that site. Their typical audience is outside the technical community, just ordinary users.

[http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/233123/migrating_to_ubuntu_linux_from_microsoft.html](http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/233123/migrating_to_ubuntu_linux_from_microsoft.html)  

Some comments:
The Ubuntu download page led me to download the 64-bit version, because I knew I had an AMD64 cpu, which made it hard to get FLASH and Adobe Acrobat Reader working.  And Picasa was borked!  Apparently they are not well-supported under 64-bit Linux. Second Life, on the other hand, intalled with no problems and workd better than the Windows version.

Please add a statement that lets people know that 32-bit will be easier to install. I will need the 64-bit because of Cinelerra but that's unusual.

*********
I had NO problems getting Nvidia drivers to work ... I had a working display from the start, activating restricted drivers worked, and the Community Documentation gave me the command I needed to get to the Nvidia setting screen.

*************
Getting DVDs to play ... Automatix had everything I needed.

**************
GNOME ... absolutely HORRIBLE legibility for its desktop icons. Forced white text with a dropshadow is '"NOT the solution (bug report filed, but please nag them for me)
