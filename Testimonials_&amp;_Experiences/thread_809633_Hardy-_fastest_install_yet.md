### Post by arfink on 2008-05-27
Yes, lots of people love to hate Hardy. Well, as a sensible computer user I noticed that Hardy is not a "lean-n-mean" kind of OS. But my laptop is only 1 year old, so it doesn't need a lean OS. Edgy was the first Ubuntu I tried. I liked it, but could not get wireless or graphics working. Feisty was better, I got wireless working after a big fight. But with those two I was still dual booting with XP because I wanted games and I had no 3d drivers in Linux. Dang Radeon 300m. 

So then I tried Gutsy. Still a pain in the rear with the wireless (ndiswrapper) but the video drivers FINALLY arrived. Not great, but they did work pretty good. Then I got rid of XP and went whole hog with the Linux. I dabbled in some lightweight distros, like Slax, Slackware, Zenwalk, DSL, even Xubuntu and Fluxbuntu, but kept coming back to Ubuntu for it's clean and appealing design and, against all odds, pretty dang good performance for the range of features it incorporates. (Gnome, DesktopFX, loads of extra modules, etc.) 

And then Hardy. I like it even more. The wireless was painless, the 64 bit support has finally come of age, graphics drivers faster than before, and a cleaner, less buggy interface. And I got a full install (plus drivers plus my personal data) running in less than a half hour flat. 

Hardy is no light weight distro, and not for the faint of memory or processor power, but if your computer has enough muscle to run XP it will have more than enough to run Ubuntu, and it will soon become your favorite heavy-weight OS. Now to wait for Fluxbuntu to update to 8.04- then my old P3 can fly with the rest of 'em!
