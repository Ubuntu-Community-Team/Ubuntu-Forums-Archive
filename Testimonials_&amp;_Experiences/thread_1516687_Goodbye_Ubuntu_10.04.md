### Post by swappa on 2010-06-24
I really tried my best. Gave you time and a lot of second chances, but you failed me on my laptop at work and on my private workstation. Things like dual monitors, switching between wired and wireless networks,  in and out of docking stations and resume from hibernate/sleep is obviously to hard for you to pull off. 

Fair enough. But I cant use you because of it.

You've been a good friend on my private workstation at home. But, this morning you failed me in a big way refusing to boot because my Ipod was plugged in my USB port. When I removed the Ipod you booted  up, but the resolution was then screwed up. I didn't apply any updates to the system since the last time I used you, so why did you do this to me? Why did you screw up my Mp3 collection just because some of the folders contain Scandinavian characters? I didn't mount the fire-wire drive holding the Mp3 collection using a custom entry in /etc/fstab either. It was all you. I really hate you for doing that to me. Took me a week to fix it. 

And then it is the smaller annoyances like Gwibber not updating the timelines, the Nvidia driver starting in "adaptive mode" when running on battery ...stuff like that. I can't blame you for all of it. I know that. But, I find myself spending too much time hacking things than using the OS like it is supposed to be used. I don't have time for that anymore. And, quite frankly, I don't want to either.

All the stuff I mention here is working flawlessly (I emphasize flawlessly) on Windows 7. When I start Windows 7 I do not worry about if stuff will work this time or not. I trust Windows 7, I don't trust you. Trust has to be built over time. It is not something you automatically get. You still have a long way to go I'm afraid.  

So, All the best to you and your friends. I will be watching you. Maybe we'll hook up again sometime in the future. I do hope so. 

Until then, take care.
