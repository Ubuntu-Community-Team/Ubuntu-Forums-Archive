### Post by fridayxiii on 2006-06-17
I'm a new user to Linux that just saw the light 48 hours ago.  I was solely a Windows user at home & work since 95 or so, and I must say that my computing experience had grown stale.  It was "log in, check email, read news, burn CDs, logout".  I did everything as fast as I could; there was no more love for the OS or real excitement or joy found in being on the PC.  XP is pretty (w/help from Belchfire themes) & my environment ran pretty well, but still....

Then I read a Digg article on Ubuntu, and my curiosity was piqued.  "Why not try someting different, that's free, and can be run from a CD or dual boot?" I'm thinking.  I had hada long-running underlying urge to try Linux but the right set of circumstances had never existed 'til now.  I spent a couple of hours reading the forums before getting going, and before I even installed the OS I was hooked.   

I was very lucky w/my install experience.  I'm running a PC that's about 18 months old, and the install was nearly flawless.  My only issue was trying to figure out how to setup the desired set of HDD partitions (took some careful deliberation as I'm keeping my XP paritions for my fiancee).  But once the install was completed my Internet connection worked ("out of the box"), and my sound did too.  The only thing I had to do was tweak my monitor settings a bit.  I almost feel guilty that my install went so well!  :D 

Now I'm embarking on the journey of installing & using new app's like FrostWire, Gnomebaker, etc. & tweaking app's I already love (Firefox, er Swiftfox), and learning a really cool new OS.  The use of command line & challenge of learning a new OS is great & one I've needed and have fully embraced.  Installing & using Ubuntu has totally renewed my interest in computing & made the time on my home PC thoroughly more enjoyable.  I've been spouting about Ubuntu to some coworkers, and even have one trying Puppy on his (much) older laptop, and another considering setting up dual boot too.  

I gotta give credit to everyone that posts in the forums - I could not & maybe would not have undertaken this venture w/out such a strong support resource behind it.  In my years of using online communities for wireless (PCS) hacking & tweaking, advice about my dogs, and endless computer questions, I find the Ubuntu community here to be w/out peer.  Simply the best.  

Everyday is a great day using Ubuntu, and there's more to do & learn all the time.  I foresee a day in the none too distant future when I fully leave Windows behind & become a total Ubuntu/Linux convert w/a dedicated machine.  Thanks to all for opening a new frontier in computing for me.  :KS
