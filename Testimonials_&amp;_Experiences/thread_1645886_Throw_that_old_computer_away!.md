### Post by MartyBuntu on 2010-12-15
Okay...

:p

...so my thread title is a playful take on another similarly titled thread extoling the virtues of running Ubuntu on older machines.

Seems like there's much emphasis on having Ubuntu "play nice" with Windows...getting WUBI installs to work...using Ubuntu on ancient, second PC's...but...

Why not run Ubuntu on your *best* machine?

I couldn't give a darn about Windows compatibility. Aside from my wife's dual-boot XP/10.10 machine (guess which install I have to "clean up" every Sunday morning over a fresh pot of coffee?) I am Windows free and have been since 2003 when I first installed Mandrake Linux as my only boot.

I use 10.04 on my Socket 775 Gigabyte Intel board with E7300 proc and 4GB RAM as well as NVIDIA graphics card. Next year, possibly moving up to AMD quad core and 8GB RAM...No problems that slow me down.

It's tough convincing some people that Ubuntu or some other *nix can be their only boot. Old habits die hard, I guess.


[B]For the record, I am somewhat idealogical about software and hardware.
If it doesn't get along with Ubuntu, I don't get along with it.
Until more manufacturer's include Linux compatibility, a little research into purchases goes a long way. I will continue to support Hauppauge, NVIDIA...etc... [/B]
