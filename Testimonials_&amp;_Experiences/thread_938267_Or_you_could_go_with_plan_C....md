### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2008-10-04
Let's say that you are John Q. Public. You're moderately familiar with computers. You use them to write papers, check e-mail, waste time on youtube, and occaisonally play games. You are a starving college student and you need a laptop computer, STAT. You need one that you can do all the things I mentioned earlier, and it absolutely HAS to have a wifi card. Like I said, you are a starving college student, so price is the main factore here. You shop around and you can't find a decent laptop that won't be obsolete in five minutes and that has a wifi card installed for under about $750, and for the laptop you really wanted cost $1100. So you look on craigslist and you find that someone is selling a laptop that they've lost the factory install discs to and unfortunately they've messed up the hard drive by getting a virus. This laptop is almost as good as the one you saw at BestBuy for $1100, but it's a little bit older and has a slower processor and less ram, but aside from the operating system being DOA with a virus, it's in perfect condition. Well, aside from the "Creed" sticker that the owner put on the lid anyway (and even God hates Creed...). So this guy is selling his laptop for $350, but you call him and you're able to use the inoperable operating system and no recovery discs as leverage and you talk him down to $300. Sure, that was all the money you had in your account. You're not going to be able to make your cell phone payment on time this month and you're probably going to be eating a lot of Ramen noodles for a few weeks but you knew that if you didn't act fast you weren't going to find another deal like that again.
So you are now the proud owner of a laptop. Now what? First thing's first, you've got to get rid of that Creed sticker. A little WD40 will do the trick. So now your laptop is Creed free. Well, there may be some MP3s on the hard drive, but you're going to be formatting the disc. De-Creedification almost complete. next order of business, figuring out what to do about the operating system. You ask your room mate if you can use his Windows install disc to see if you can fix your laptop. Yeah... that didn't work at all. After about three more hours of messing around with it, you haven't gotten any closer to having a working computer than you were yesterday, and now you're broke to boot. You're definitely having some buyers remorse.
You call it quits and get some sleep. The next day between classes you head to the internet cafe on campus and you start out by looking around the internet to try and figure out what you can do. First you hit amazon.com. You're shocked when you see that the SAME exact operating system that came free with your laptop originally (Windows Vista Home Premium) costs almost $300! That's highway robbery! It's unacceptable! It's just not right! You're appalled that Microsoft is charging so much for a product that comes FREE with every new computer! How can they get away with this? Oh yeah... they're Microsoft and they rule the world. And then you find out that it doesn't even come with Microsoft Office (which is why you bought the computer in the first place) which costs another $100+. And then you still need Antivirus. So at this point Windows is starting to look like its a no go. So you look at Mac OSX. Oh, what? you can't install Mac OSX on anything but a Mac? That sucks. What now?
You need an operating system bad. Windows costs an arm and a leg, and it doesn't even come with the software you need anyway. You're at your wits end and you've made up your mind that you're going to just download a copy of windows from Limewire or something. So you ask your room mate if you can use his laptop to download Windows. "Isn't that illegal?" he asks you. You don't really have a good answer for that, being that technically since you own a computer with Windows on it and it's supposed to have a copy to install in just such a situation, shouldn't you have a legal right to get another copy? Don't you have the right to make archival backups or something like that? Anyway, you finally convince him to let you use his computer. You search around and then download the file that says it's a good copy of Windows Vista. As soon as you finish downloading the file, you realize that you have no DVD-Rs, you have no idea what an ISO is (neither does your room mate) and on top of that you have successfully infected your room mate's computer with a virus, which promptly causes his computer to crash. Needless to say, your room mate is not very happy with you as he begins to reformat his computer and you STILL don't have a copy of Windows. Not cool. You are now the proud owner of a nice new laptop paperweight.
So what if someone told you that there was another option? What if someone told you that there is an operating system that you can install on any computer, that comes with an Office pack of software, is completely virus free, and doesn't cost a penny? You tell them to put the crack pipe down. But just for shits and giggles you google "free operating systems". To your surprise, google turns up like fifty two million hits! You find out that there isn't just one free operating system, but there are literally hundreds of them available for download right from their websites! So you start reading up on them and after doing a little research you decide that you're going to do it! What have you got to lose? You have a laptop that doesn't work and no cash to spend on fixing it. It sounds like a win win situation. You find out that the most popular flavor of this "Linux" thing is called Ubuntu. You check it out, and it even kind of looks like windows. It seems pretty legit, so you decide to take the plunge and get this Ubuntu thing. You can't figure out how download it from the website, and you're not supposed download stuff onto the hard drive at the Internet Cafe. You're room mate isn't going to let you touch his laptop ever again, and besides the file is one of those ISO things again. ISO is bad juju for you... Then you see that Canonical will actually mail you a copy of Ubuntu for free! Sounds good. So you order a copy, and a couple days later you get it in the mail. As you sit at your laptop, you turn the power on and put the disc in the drive. Here goes nothing. The computer starts up like it normally would, but instead of dumping you off at DOS prompt giving you some system error message, it actually boots the computer up. your guided through a very simple installation and in less than 20 minutes, your computer is up and running. Your thrilled when you look in the corner at the network manager icon and it tells you that there are like 15 wireless networks in range, and a few of them are unsecured. Thank you Windows for making your wireless network setup tool retarded! You noodle around with it for a few minutes and find OpenOffice, GIMP (like Adobe Photoshop) and all kinds of cool software that came free with the computer. Windows doesn't even come with Microsoft Office, much less Photoshop! You look around for an antivirus program and you can't find one, so you go online and check the Ubuntu website only to find out that there are no antivirus programs for Linux operating systems because they aren't needed. This is too good to be true.
But can it hook up to my iPod? Why, yes! there's even a program that kind of looks like iTunes already installed. This is getting kind of creepy. It's an operating system that's free, and you can install it on any computer, it's completely virus free, and it comes with all kinds of software that does what people want to do with it. And all the programs are all designed so well and they're so functional. How is this possible? It's simple. Linux was made by the people who use it, for the people who use it, so everything about it is custom taylored to suit the end user. And did I mention it's free?
And you haven't even discovered Compiz yet... :D
