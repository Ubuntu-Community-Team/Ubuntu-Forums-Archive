### Post by Goober on 2005-09-27
Well, since I am bored, and procrastinating on HW, and in a grumpy mood because i am being forced to use Word today, here is my Ubuntu Story.

Ever since my Dad got our first computer in '95, I have been experiencing, well, Windoze at its best, I guess.  I have been putting up with BSODs, Windoze not working properly, randomly shutting down, being slow, open to viruses, worms, etc., being full of holes, just generally being a pain.  I thought this was normal.  Yes, I am very naive at times.

I thought it was normal that we had Win95, then got Win98, which was a bugger to install, and produced more BSOds then we could count.  The came WinME.  That is the worst OS, along with Win2000, that I have ever seen.  Personally, I would never go back to them.  Perhaps it was because the old computer my Dad had was old, but, whatever the case, they were collectively crap.  Win98, with all its BSODs and other problems, seemed like heaven in comparison.  I remember the old Computer not working twice as much then it worked with WinME/2k.  

Then I got my Computer, and it came with WinXP Home, for 160 bucks.  It was a fairly decent OS (after I put another 512Mb RAM in it, since XP won't work well with 256Mb RAM), but dang did it suck up resources.  And still does.  But, compared to Win98/ME/2k, it is much better.  But still Windoze.  but I need to tell you guys why I got Ubuntu.

I got my Computer almost exactly 3 years ago, and have suffered with XP ever since.  I had to re-install XP this June after it horribly crashed.  XP has always been slow for me, I probably had more on it then I should, and expected too much, but it still just never worked properly.  After initially getting it, and installing lots of stuff on it, I realized I needed to slim down.  So I did.  And I realized you cannot multitask with XP.  Not well, anyway.  

Things went ok, until this May/June.  I was up late at night, 11PM, finishing my final English Essay, which I was doing in Word.  It was about 4 pages long, I was doing the final edit, and I was about to print when Word randomly closed.  Literally, it closed, and I was staring at my desktop and that stupid "start" button in mute shock.  It didn't save anything, just closed, taking 4 pages of my English Grade with it.  Needless to say, I was pissed.  Then, to add insult to injury, about 2 weeks later, XP's MBR somehow deleted itself.  I guess a Virus or something got inb, but XP refused to boot, and I couldn't use the CD to fix it.  So I stuck in my Ubuntu 4.10 CD, which I got from somebody on Freecycle, God Bless them, and I had been meaning to install, and see what this "Linux" stuff was all about, and, maybe 20-30 minutes later, I had completely wiped clean both Hardrives, and had Linux working, which was amazing, since I had literally just had XP die.  Then I installed XP, and, well, have used XP for nothing but Games and necessary M$ office stuff ever since, and Linux for everything else.

So that is my Linux story, and also why I vehemently hate Word, and don't particularly like Windoze.  Linux is now my primary OS, and might be my only OS, if I got Crossover Office and Cedega, for which I need a Credit Card, which I don't have.  

Anyway, enough of my rambling, on to HW.

### Post by Goober on 2005-10-05
(Mods, not bumping my thread, just updating it for Breezy)

Ok, i figured it is time for me to ramble on a bit more about my Ubuntu story.  It has continued!  I have managed to successfully partition my 160 Gig HD, with one 40 Gig for Hoary, one 40 Gig for Breezy, and another 80 Gigs just sitting there, collecting dust, leading me to wonder yet again why I bought it . . . well, ok, it was the price . . . 

Anyway, I successfully have partitioned the 160Gig, and installed Breezy, all in 27 minutes!  I did this mostly because I was ensuring that the CD worked because I was trying to install Breezy on my Laptop (see Sig), but, well, it didn't work.  Anyway, Breezy is, simply put, awesome.  It is a fair bit faster for me then Hoary, probably because I dist-upgraded Hoary from Warty, and had loads of programs on it that I didn't need (like Firestarter, ClamAV, Nessus, etc), and taking up space.  Also, Breezy detected everything perfectly, and even automatically mounted the Hoary Partition!  Needless to say, I am extremely impressed, plus I absolutely love the new Add/Remove Applications thing.  Awesome, simple awesome.  I have been adding stuff, and, upon thinking about it, should start to remove stuff I don't need.

But, there is one thing that is not making me happy about Breezy.  My Printer.  I have an HP PSC 1400, and it was automatically detected by Hoary, so installing the Driver was a Breeze.  Not so in Breezy.  It is not detected, and the driver that worked in Hoary (which is for the PSC1100) doesn't work in Breezy.  That means I need to go to Hoary to print.  I am wondering if I could just unplug the printer, plug it back in, if that will make Breezty detect it, and allow Hoary to continue to detect it.  It might mess up XP's ability to detect it, but I frankly don't care about XP.

Anyway, that is the updated version of my Linux Experience for Breezy.
