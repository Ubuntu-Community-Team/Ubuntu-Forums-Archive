### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2009-08-18
I wanted to make Ubuntu "user friendly" because a lot of kids use this computer, and I wanted them to be able to just sit down and use it without any "coaching" or advice.

For me the answer was the super-simple (and ultra-lightweight) [LXDE]("http://lxde.org") desktop environment. I just dragged and dropped the most popular applications from *usr/share/applications* to the desktop, and re-named the icons (Firefox became "Browse the Web," OpenOffice Writer became "Word processor," MPlayer became "Music player," etc).

Kids that have only known Windows or Mac can just sit down and use this old computer effortlessly. They don't even know it's Linux unless I tell them (and I don't, unless they ask or unless they compliment me on how simple and fast that old dinosaur computer is).

*My* Ubuntu *is* user-friendly! Very much so. Still, Gnome wasn't hard to learn at all. It's *different* from Windows (thank God), but not harder to learn nor to use.

I'm a n00b myself (only started last March and only knew WinXP before that). For this little n00b, Ubuntu has been far more "user friendly" than Windows was.

-Robin
