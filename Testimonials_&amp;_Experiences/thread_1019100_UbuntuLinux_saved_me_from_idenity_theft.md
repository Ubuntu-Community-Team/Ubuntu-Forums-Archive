### Post by doas777 on 2008-12-23
> **CraigPaleo said:**
> I thought Vista solved that bug by making the user push "okay" one or two more times.

thats exactly the problem with UAC (and gksu for that matter). In order to make an informed decision about whether an binary should be allowed to perform an action, you need to know who is trying to access the resource (the exact binary), what resource they are attempting to use, and what they are trying to do with it. UAC/gksu displays only the name of the app package that the binary is running under. the other flaw of course, is that they both involve process elevation rather than elevating the specific action. 

I used the Kaspersky IDPS, and set it to ask me about most everything, except signed system components. between it an processexplorer I learned a ton about how my system works, and can be reasonably sure i know what is running at all times (unless KIS itself is being attacked).
