### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-01-03
You will not believe this. So, since all of my professional 3d editing software works well in Wine (very well), I've been using it for a while. Cinema 4D for the majority of modelling and UV Mapping, since it has many functions that improve and facilitate sculpting a new model.

I use 3ds max 7 for its plugins and animation/rigging set up, since it's more straightforward and simple than Cinema 4D and more compatible.

I was considering buying ZBrush as well since it performs so well in Wine, and it would be great to lift and depress features into my models.

Also, I was looking around for a good open source or cross-platform 3d programming kit that supports python, since I've only programmed 2d games up to this point, while 3d is my true passion.

So, I decided to download and use Blender, since it's one of the only programs that fully supports my 3ds max plugins, and I figured it's more modern than 7 and I don't wanna' use old software the rest of my life.

Wow, was I blown away! Blender's just as easy to model in as Cinema 4D when you get the hang of it (much faster since it's easier to navigate AND work with its one hand on mouse one hand on keyboard method), it has a great rigging mode that supports vertex painting, as well as AUTOMATIC weighting, it's easy to modify several bones at once, it has a great rendering device for OpenGL, and so many other things.

It also has a sculpting mode that's highly reminiscent of Zbrush, making that purchase entirely unnecessary. Also, I found out it has A GAME ENGINE that's simple to use without any programming knowledge but ALSO HAS BINDINGS FOR PYTHON!!!

WTF OPEN SOURCE!!? WTF!? (why the face)

This is amazing. I mean, I don't mean to focus on just this one project, because it's obvious throughout this entry that the work of many different open source projects has greatly enhanced and sped up my work flow, including the ability to use what might have been necessary software with improved speed under Wine.

I'm thinking of buying some Wine compatible games, since I'll have no use for Wine at all soon. XD

But to the developers of Ubuntu who like to make things simple and improve usability, to the Gnome and KDE developers, to the kernel developers, to the creators of all the professional and commercial quality design software available for Linux, and especially the designers of Blender, thank you so very much.

Linux has improved my life more than I care to recognize some days. I know it's not perfect for everyone, but I truly believe it's perfect for 90 percent of people, if it can satisfy my very distinct and relentless needs.

Only a system like Linux can leave me thoroughly satisfied every time I turn off my computer. It's simply amazing. This is technology that empowers people. 4 different tools I was seeking in one program, so now I can improve and streamline my work even more in a native program.

I plan to give back by releasing anything I create for GNU/Linux, FreeBSD, Solaris, etc. distributions for 'beta testing' at least one week before having an official release for Mac OSX and Windows.

I think someone should show preference to Linux users in that industry. If nothing else, I will release all versions simultaneously (looking back, my comments were a little biased XD).

Before this gets too long, just allow me to say once more, thank you for everyone in the community who have brought me this beautiful and outrageously fast computing experience, which has empowered me in my work. I think it would be worthwhile to stop for a moment and think of just how fortunate we are to have high quality open source software.
