### Post by w4rr3n on 2010-07-10
I have been using Ubuntu on and off for several years now. I love the OS, but there has always been one little quirk here or there that I have not been able to resolve (or had to endure some tedious workaround), and I inevitably wind up back on Windows. However, Ubuntu is sticky and damn near impossible to drop all together. So... Lucid was released and I could not help myself...

Since day 1, I have done nothing but gush over this release. The install was painless and without mishap. I immediately started trying to find the flaw. I hit all the previous snags on all the previous versions, but I was pleasantly surprised by success at each trial. And I love you guys on the forums... but I am happy to say that with this release I have not had to come groveling for your assistance ^_^

Lets hit some high points:

-Rhythmbox... It picked up my music library (on a 2nd internal drive) without a problem. Easy ipod management, and integrated Last.FM... sold.

-Empathy... i used Trillian on windows. Empathy is more aesthetically pleasing and more functional (Trillian liked to tell me EVERY TIME GTalk changed resources.... which quickly became irritating).

-Language Support... I study &#26085;&#26412;&#35486; and Ubuntu's Japanese support (Anthy) is easy to set up and, frankly, is more user friendly than it was on Windows. (I know it has nothing to do with the release, but I also love that Anki (a SRS) has a version specifically tailored to Ubuntu).

Random happiness... 
-The weather indicator built in to the calendar was quite the gem upon discovery. Saved me from trying Conky again (I know you guys can do some crazy awesome things with it, but i can't...and really only wanted weather). I'm also not a big fan of little widgets all of the desktop, so the built in weather function appealed to the minimalist in me.
-The Ubuntu Store... I haven't tried it out yet, but it's cool as hell that we have our own now.

And it of course comes with all the Linux goodness of doing what i say, when I say. None of this Window's "yes yes, when i get around to it" or "after I close X, Y, and Z, we'll see."

I'm sure after posting I'll think of 10 things I could have raved about, but whatever, this is getting long. I'm thoroughly satisfied with Lucid. Great job :D

(oh, and a small side note... why did the GIMP get no love this go round? :(  poor guy. least you kept him in the repos)

### Post by simosx on 2010-07-10
GIMP is a tool aiming more advanced users, so that was the first justification in not including it by default inthe 700MB CD, as WinterRain mentioned.
GIMP is quite big in terms of package size.
I believe that a simpler and smaller image editor may be added in a later release. 
A message that we need to pass to the new users is that there are many cool programs available in the Software Centre, such as GIMP, that can be installed easily later on.
There is a tremendous amount of open-source material available to try out.

You mentioned your ipod.If your ipod is supported, you can install the Rockbox firmware and gain lots of new functionality. For more, see [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockbox](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rockbox) Rockbox is based on a cut-down specialised kernel, which is open-source.
