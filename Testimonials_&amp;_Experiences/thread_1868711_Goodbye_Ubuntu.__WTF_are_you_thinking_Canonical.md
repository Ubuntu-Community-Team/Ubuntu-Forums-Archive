### Post by subliminalurge on 2011-10-24
Been using Linux in commercial environments for a very long time.  Since before Slackware came out, and before RedHat.

Moved to Ubuntu for my desktop systems a few years back, since they finally produced an out-of-the-box installation that was significantly better than my Fedora systems that I'd been running.

In the years that I've been using Ubuntu, I've been highly recommending it to many other people.  That will not happen anymore.

This insane, ill-advised push to cram a tablet UI onto laptops and desktops without providing an easy way to switch to something more suitable is just ridiculous.  I understand the reasons for developing Unity, the tablet market is going to be huge.

But why attempt to force it on people still using actual computers with keyboards and mice?  Somebody in charge is trying to save money on the developer payroll at the expense of the end users.

Well, sorry guys, you've taken a great product and needlessly turned it into a steaming pile of ****.

I'm wiping my Ubuntu partition this evening and installing either Fedora or CentOS.  I'm advising those who turn to me for advice to do the same.

I'm not asking for advice or solutions.  I need neither.  I have the knowledge to customize this Ubuntu 11.10 installation to suit my desires.  But I won't.  It's too much work when I can acheive the same result much faster with another distro.

I just wanted to leave a "goodbye note" to the powers that be at Canonical so they would know what's going on when they see their dominance in the Linux marketplace start to fade and wonder what went wrong.

The answer is simple.  Unity was a bad idea, and on top of that, it was poorly executed.

It's a shame, because there was a time when Ubuntu could be described as a great idea, well executed.
