### Post by Oranges10e on 2011-05-03
**Ubuntu 11.04** &#8211; *THE best Linux Distro I have EVER used.*

In order to keep things easy to read (for Canonical, if they ever read this), I will make a short list of what I like and dislike, with pros and cons. I encourage anyone to join me on this and keep it simple and easy to read too. 

I have been using Ubuntu 11.04 the past few days and before that the Beta. I have been an Ubuntu User since version 8.04 and I actually came from the Windows/OSX camp. Up until now, I always had to dualboot, even just to watch a simple movie, without tearing. Usually, I stick to LTS releases, but always try out newer versions on less important computers (always native, no Virtualbox/Emulation stuff going around here) Let's go:

**Positiv:**

+ Unity and Unitydock in general: Fast, simple and sexy. 

+ Works great so far. Sure, a bit buggy at times, but it's getting there. I realize, that this isn't a LTS and that over 90% of stability should only be expected in a LTS version, since the &#8222;normal&#8220; releases have a way too short release schedual to stick to. So, to be honest, this has been a pritty good release (so far) - for me.

+ I didn't like Gnome-Shell with the new Gnome (3), that I tried out from a USB stick. I kinda got lost with it. To me, as a user coming from Windows and OSX, this feels "right" and somewhat similair to what I am used to. Anyway, you do have the freedom to choose what you want, so it's all good anyway. Don't like it? Change it.

+ Something fresh. I know a lot of ppl (mostly the geeky kind on those tech-savy forums) don't like the idea of Canonical not using G3 and Shell, but I do. A lot of ppl were against having prop. software on Ubuntu or being able to download it from within. I liked the idea. A lot of ppl don't mind all the tearing Compiz has and how buggy (in a dozen cases on my testing side) it is with certain hardware. I do. So, I am often the opposite, but that's fine. I represent the "normal" user, switching from other mainstream OS's.

+ 2D version of Unity. THIS is nice. A good way to ensure everyone can use it and not only the lucky ones.

+ The prop. Nvidia drivers were activated and installed @default on my machine - AWESOME! So, Install Ubuntu (while you're installing updating right after or at it) and you're ready to use it! No more trying to find out how basic things work.
(This one has made me go NUTTS, I really mean it, over the last few years, using Ubuntu) Unity is using Compiz, what I don't like that much (I see Wayland as the way to go), BUT Vsync is actually working so far! I was using Unity on an Intel GMA so far, where Vsync was working out-of-the-box, too. I figured, okay, this has been working for some time now, since newer Intels drivers support KMS (?). But how shocked was I, when I figured, that it is working for my Nvidia Laptop as well ____ out-of-the-box____ ! THIS is awesome and EXTREMLY user friendly. To be honest, this alone makes me use Ubuntu as my primary system once again. At least now I can watch videos and online stuff, without videos tearing to hell. At least NOW I can do those full-screen presentations, without having to let the ppl watching run out, because of the bad tearing. Actually, just because I spent months trying this out: WHAT was causing the problem and how did you guys fix it?

+ Reviews for Apps in the Software-Centre, nice!
+ Loving the Music-Store.

+ Crossover has finally made it in to the Software-Centre. While I see "Wine" (in general) as a "bridge" for what Linux - really - needs (that would be more native games/apps via the store or even some already very good and stable Open Source apps pushed, supported and polished more by Canonical and implemented in the store, since they're so good at this anyway) it helps knowing that it is there. I hope Crossover/Wine gets some stronger support this way. Canonical: Don't forget Crossover Games, though! ;D 

+ I love the fact, that I can update Ubuntu, while installing it. 
+ I love the feature, that I can choose to install codecs, before installing the main system. If you combine theat, with the fact that, if you choose to install, update while install, install codecs and if you realize that the Nvidia/Intel GMA drivers are working out of the box (still have to test it with ATI later) after a fresh install (vsync included), you get a very nice and easy to set-up system. Not even my 7 is as easy as this. It's fast and easy.

+ I LOVE the new website. It really is growing on me and it has gotten so much easier and user-friendly with howto's, faqs, pictures (the simple mind likes pictures &#8211; a picture/slideshow can mean a thousand words) and even a section to ask the Ubuntu devs some Q&A. THIS is AWESOME. Period.
Loving the wallpapers.

**Negative:**

- Actually, not much. Our Netbook Remix (of 11.04) is a bit buggy at times and crashes now and then, but it's nothing major to worry about.

- I really wish I could move the Unity dock to where my heart desires.
- I really wish the Unity dock wouldn't always hide and that I could easily choose if I want to hide it or not (like; right-click on it and change).

- Ubuntu Support is only available in English for now. There should be commercial support for more languages right now. A lot of ppl would NEVER even consider looking in a forum for a solution and just like the direct and personal approach at things. This kind of support is a must-have, at least if you are targeting the mainstream. The next Ubuntu version should start supporting this change.

- The Ubuntu website should be translated in to the most important (start a vote?) used languages. It's good for ppl to have ONE place to find EVERYTHING they need BUT have the CHOICE to look somewhere else, if needed.

- Quicken for Linux? I saw this on the website. This WOULD be awesome, but since it's not, what gives? Or do you guys mean Kmymoney, Homebank or even GNUcash, that can import Quicken files?

**What I wish for future releases:**

**ã)** There are some wonderful games out there, that should be in the Ubuntu Software-Centre. Some like Aquaria for Linux, the Penumbra Series, Doom 3 and a few more. These are just an example. 

**b)** I had this brainstorm idea: [http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/27171/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/27171/) (example: [http://img141.imageshack.us/i/ubuntuintroductionvideo.jpg/](http://img141.imageshack.us/i/ubuntuintroductionvideo.jpg/) )- Most of it is actually part of the new Ubuntu website and a few things are going in that direction. Video FAQ's or tutos could be in English (on the website) and subtitled in other languages (in ordner to save some money). This, in combination with translating the Ubuntu website in to various languages, plus adding full commercial support in different languages = MUST HAVE. 

**c)** Canonical could work with partners to push support for more scanners under Linux. This is a major issue for a lot of ppl I know. Support more scanners, please.

**d)** When buying Ubuntu from the Ubuntu shop, you could bundle it with (at least) a short manual in three or four languages. Just a short &#8222;getting started&#8220; kind of quick-guide-thingy, ya know? ;D

**_Final words:_**

Well, that's it, for NOW. If there is anything else to say (which is very likely), I will update/edit my own post. So, this is just a short way of showing my gratitude and love for this version.

**5/5 stars on this version**. 

*Way to go, Canonical/Ubuntu.*

Oranges

### Post by chinamike on 2011-06-12
Well, I have always wanted to try Ubuntu, and I tried Natty for a month and today I cleared it off and installed 10.4 instead.

I just read too many of the same error problems and install add on issues that seemed to reflect what I was having trouble with.

It seemed really great at first, but on reflection, I realize now I was wanting it to be just Ok rather than great. As I had issues, I kept coming across comments from other previous version users saying things like "I'm hearing too many issues, I'll stick with my 9 or 10 version". It kept coming up enough that I thought maybe Natty was just too robust and complicated for my laptop.

So to give anyone a heads up if they have an HP Pavilion type laptop, this might give you some pause and reconsideration on Natty and go with//back to 9 or 10 Ubuntu.

First off, my system, although six years old, is really a power horse. It has a dual Intel Centrino processor, 1000 mb of RAM, two large 90 gb HDs (yes, 2 of them!) and generally pretty good graphics card and what-not.

After the first week, I reinstalled Natty, kept hanging up on me at times for no apparent reason. A week later, I did it again (not full reinstall, just putting in the live CD, going through the steps to repair/upgrade).

Also, I had real issues with trying to integrate Transmission torrent program--I had to keep adding various missing "necessaries" and it still never truly performed like it did in other Linux OS systems I have used. Also couldn't get certain DVD burner/convert programs to work without installing tons of various plug-ins and so on. I kept seeing references to version 9 and 10 using the very programs I was used to but they were also having trouble using them in Natty.

So today out of disgust and anger, I quickly erased and installed Ubuntu 10.4 and really--what a pleasant experience from the get go! I could tell within the first six hours that I had made the right decidsion. It runs really smooth, uses all my favorite programs (Transission already built in! Yeah!) and so I am a happy camper. I did like some of the cool features in Natty, so I hope to try it again after all the kinks get worked out!

So if you have an HP Pavilion dvXXX number (mine is a dv8000) you may be better off using Ubuntu 9 or 10.

All the best!

China Mike
