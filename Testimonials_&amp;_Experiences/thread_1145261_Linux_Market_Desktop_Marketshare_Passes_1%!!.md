### Post by irrdev on 2009-05-02
I think that 1.5% is probably an accurate mark. 4% as reported by W3 is misleading, as the majority of visitors to W3 are website designers, who would naturally be more likely to use linux.

I don't expect that Linux's market share will grow significantly anytime in the near future. However, remember that the number of computer users in the world as a whole is growing all of the time. I believe that this will be a key to Linux's success: actual number of users is more important for future development than shot-term overall market share. I also expect that Linux will grow in popularity in emerging economies where users simply can't afford to pay $$$$ for a computer with Windows or the Mac.
