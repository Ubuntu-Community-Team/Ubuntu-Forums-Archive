### Post by gtrtx on 2009-02-15
Every so often I like to peruse the Testimonial & Experiences forums. Usually is a fairly even mix of good and bad. 

I just want to share my very positive experiences with Ubuntu installed on a number of computers. Mine and others. 

In my circle of friends and family I'm pretty much the "go to guy" when somebody is having issues with their computers. Actually seems pretty common amongst linux users on this forum. Anyways, most of the systems I work on are using XP. And most of the problems are virus/malware related. Most of the users are, for the most part, not very computer savvy. On three systems I've worked on, I've wiped XP and installed Ubuntu. 

I find out what the users want to do with their computers and I set them up to do said tasks as easily as possible. Usually give them a rundown of things they need to know and let them go. On those three systems I rarely get calls regarding computer problems. When I do, I usually can remote in and straighten things up in a matter of minutes.  All three systems have been running now over a year and the users are very happy. 

I also use ubuntu on my personal computers; 1 desktop and 1 Sylvania G Meso netbook. I just recently acquired the netbook and it came with Hardy installed. I upgraded to 8.10 with no issues to speak of. The wireless worked out of the box. I was able to set-up mobile broadband using my cell phone and a dongle and now have Internet access anywhere I can get a signal. 

I just wanted to share my positive experiences with Ubuntu. Granted I've been a linux user for far longer than Ubuntu actually existed(starting with Slackware back in 96) so configuring, tweaking and trouble-shooting are rarely an issue. Thing is, with Ubuntu I rarely have problems and when I do, I almost always find a solution in a timely manner. Back in my Slack days I would spend countless hours modifying config files, issuing commands in a terminal and so forth just to get things to work. In Ubuntu things mostly "just work". 

I understand that there are cases where users have issues with certain hardware or configurations, but that's going to be the case with any OS. I remember a tv tuner card that I had that wouldn't  install correctly in XP. It was designed for XP. It took a lot of time and effort, but I finally got it working. Of course it wasn't very stable. The same card in Ubuntu installed and worked with no user intervention...none. So it goes both ways. 

I suppose I've been lucky. Just about every Ubuntu installation that I've done has worked great out of the box. Every release seems better and an improvement over the last. 

I just wanted to share. I guess I could have done it in a more succinct manner but I'm trying to get used to the tiny keyboard on this netbook and this rambling is good practice.:P

regards
