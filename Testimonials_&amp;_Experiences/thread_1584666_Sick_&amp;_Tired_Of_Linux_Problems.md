### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-09-30
I doubt it matters which OS your using.
The first rule is always: Backup critical data, drivers, etc.

I don't know any company that doesn't have a backup for critical  systems, regardless of which OS they use. Even the mom & pop shops.  Just to many potential problems.

Some companies I'm aware of run continuous backups, as they deal with  regulatory groups, etcetera. Making the preservation of data mandatory. 

Production facilities (I'm aware of) rarely update or upgrade without  motive and serious consideration with regards to impact. Time  constraints based on production needs and schedules often negate  software improvements. Unless said improvement(s) prove necessary for  increases in productivity or security.

Prior to any changes, a system backup takes place and rarely (if ever) are all systems updated simultaneously.

Might I suggest staggering system updates/upgrades starting with the  least significant system. This eliminates the potential of all systems  failing, regardless of issue and puts control back in the hands of the  administrator(s). Should an issue arise during the update/upgrade  process of the initial system, cancel the rest until testing can assure  positive results.

Success increases with consistent hardware (systems). This minimizes the opportunity for failure based on hardware diversity.

Just a thought.
Good Luck
