### Post by aaargh486 on 2008-03-05
I live in [Belgium]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium"), a very small country in Europe. My native language is Dutch, so please forgive me my horrible English contortions. :oops:
And here in Belgium we have an organization called OCMW (Openbaar Centrum voor Maatschappelijk Welzijn) which translates to Public Centre for Social Well-being. 
It's our social welfare provision, I don't know if anything like it exists in the USA. In French, it is CPAS and in German ÖSHZ.
I hope you understand what I'm talking about.

But I'm drifting off. One of the tasks of this organization is to help the poor and deprived people of the society. 
And because a computer has become an indispensable asset of every household (I.T. is very important everywhere in the industry), they also help people work with computers and give them very basic ones for free.
And here comes the good part. Because the computers I'm talking about are about as expensive as Windows Vista, they made the obvious decision to learn them use Linux instead. Of course, I'm convinced none of the computers are capable of booting Vista properly, looking at the system requirements.
This means that my government is actively helping spreading Linux, I see this as a powerful signal.

The reason I'm telling this is because they need people to install the computers. And because my mother works in the administration department of a nursing home (another task of the OCMW), she mentioned my name, which means I'll be spending my Easter vacation installing Linux on the PC of the OCMW office in Beersel, Belgium. :lolflag:
Paid of course :lolflag::guitar:

I'm looking forward to this. Any reactions?

Kasper




PS: When I mean socially deprived people, I'm not talking about hobo's or pickpockets. I'm taking about people that lost their job, or never got the opportunity to study or immigrants etc... The people that need help to integrate in our society.

PPS: For the record, I'm a 16 year old boy, going to high school. I'm no sysadmin or whatever, I trained myself to do this.

PPPS: Sorry again for my bad English.

To the non Belgians here (and maybe some deluded Belgians too ), the OCMW is the "last line" welfare and gives the famous "minimex" which is the small survival income given to indigents. Normally, it is now given along very restrictive lines. The rules are set by the federal state and any form of aid which is given outside these lines are not paid back to the municipality by the state. For example, one has to surrender any license plate before  even asking help. If you're rich enough to own a car (even a crappy old one), you don't need help. Basically, you must be a jobless worker who for some reason hasn't got any unemployment allowance (hasn't contributed for six months to social security, just got out from school, etc...).Anything else is a "gift" from the municipality. Since full-time students are not " available for the job market", their aid can be suspended anytime. That's an efficient way of pushing people to drop their studies and become available for the crab jobs market and/or an endless strings of phoney "formations" which will lead them to nothing but are generously paid to the "formator" who is often tied to the mayor/political party ruling the municipality/administrative workers or their family :biggrin:

You get the picture i think. Sorry for the annoying off topic but too many people believe welfare is given like candies all around Belgium when it's not the case. That's why i am very surprised.
