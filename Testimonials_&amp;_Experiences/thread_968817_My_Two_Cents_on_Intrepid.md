### Post by SirSigma on 2008-11-03
I performed the upgrade before going to work. I left my laptop (running 32 bit Ubuntu 8.04) running the upgrade process. I figured that long after I got back it would be ready and running, so I left my laptop plugged into the AC adapter.

Approximately 5 hours later I returned home. And I saw a message asking if I wanted to erase or keep some files. I chose to keep them since they were from my OpenOffice.org 3 installation (the REAL OpenOffice 3 installation; not the half-assed Softpedia one). I waited about 30 minutes longer as I got a chance to relax while the computer continued to upgrade.

Soon the laptop finished and began the restart process. The screen sat there with a blinking white underscore over a completely black screen. I waited for the computer to really restart. I waited what felt like 10 minutes until I pressed enter to see if anything would happen. Nothing did, except that the blinking underscore moved down.

Now here's where everything starts looking up. I had to uninstall a few things that were obsolete, but I was able to get by anyway. I was a little sad to find Exaile was gone, but I just decided to go back to Amarok (I'll check later to see if Exaile works on 8.10).

I was originally told by some prompts during the upgrade that my Evolution Mail preferences wouldn't carry over in the upgrade, but the strange thing is that they really did. All the way down to the last downloaded emails. All my contacts and settings are intact.

I tried to reconnect to the Internet on Ubuntu using my USB wireless WiFi connector (because ndiswrapper never worked for me; I guess I was doing it wrong at the time). But I had a few problems trying to connect. But then again, I had the same problems when I first used 8.04 solely because I wasn't sure if I was using a 128 bit passphrase or 64/128 bit hex. But my jaw dropped when I soon found out that my laptop's wireless card was not only recognized, but worked! I stared in awe at the fact that Ubuntu now supported my internal wireless card now.

I checked to see if OpenOffice.org 3 carried into the upgrade, and it didn't. I went back to the page that originally taught me how to install OpenOffice.org 3 and followed the instructions again. It's all working just as well as it did on 8.04.

The only thing I hadn't liked at first was the speed. 8.10 seems slightly slower than 8.04's lightning-quickness. But it's still lightyears ahead of Vista, and it's still my OS of choice.

To be honest, I'm very satisfied with the upgrade. I love it. Keep up the good work guys!:)

And the reason I posted this? I noticed a lot of negative testimonials on this, with fewer positive ones. In a way, I wanted to show that Ubuntu is on the right track.
