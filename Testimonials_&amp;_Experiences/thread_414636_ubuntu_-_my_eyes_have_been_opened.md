### Post by adampyre on 2007-04-20
Hello,

First, sorry if this isn't the proper place to post it, I'm gnew (knew)...... (ha get it?)

anyway...

I am 27. I have been using computers in some capacity for pretty much my entire life. I remember when I was in kindergarten, '86, we were playing learning games on what I think may have been commodores or apple IIe's? I don't know, too young to remember or know. In grade school used the same things pretty much. Didn't see my first PC or Mac until I was in freshman year highschool, '94, '95. My school of course had nothing but Macs and my father was slowly building a 386 pc piece by piece from the power supply up. I remember when he finally got it together, we had Dos and Windows 3.11 running on it. Played some pretty cool games, flight sims at the time.

That computer broke frequently and I learned how to fix it. Thankfully, in hindsight, it broke so much: as it taught me so much about how computers work. At first, of course, I had no idea what I was doing. It was a learning process, trial and error. I spent hours of what felt like futility trying to get something to work.. even when the information I had said that it couldn't or wouldn't. Alas, after hours it would work! The elation I felt that with a little determination anything could be done on a computer! So of course as time went by I kept upgrading to different versions of Windows up until recently.

So a few weeks ago I decided to try Ubuntu. Maybe it was because we were learning some unix commands in a operating systems class I am taking and maybe it's because open source, Linux, open office and things of that nature have been in the news. I decided to see what the hype was about. After a few weeks, I haven't felt this way about computers and technology since I was playing around with the computer my dad was building, trying to get things to work. It feels strange, that I am back to being a beginner not knowing what I am exactly doing, but it also feels good. Already I am pleased with what Linux has been doing for me and I am excited to learn what I can...... sure, sometimes not everything works the first time....but that is how I learn. It may be tedious to get my wireless internet going, but it forces me to understand the inner and out workings of Linux and Ubuntu.

I have officially purged Windows XP from my laptop and now leave it solely open for Feisty Fawn. I am excitedly running down the road ahead........
