### Post by keiichidono on 2008-06-12
My Ubuntu story:

I bought my computer preloaded with Vista in August 2007 and i was content. I was a little experienced so i installed Firefox and went to a tech site called Notebook Review and joined their forums. After a few months i get to learn a lot about Windows and computers in general there, so when my tech buddies told me about Linux i was open minded enough to try it. I got LinuxMint 4.0 and booted into the LiveCD as they had already instructed me how to burn it. It didn't recognize my screen resolution properly, and at the time, since i didn't know that i needed to enable proprietary drivers (which needed a restart so it wasn't going to happen anyway) and just thought Linux was a pile of crap. Skip ahead a few months from December 2007 to March 2008 and you'll find that i'm trying out Linux on my old computer to make it run fast. 

I got rid of XP and got Xubuntu, i didn't like it because of it's simplistic look but my resolution was just fine and everything worked pretty nicely. I went distro hopping for a while looking at Mandriva, OpenSUSE, PCLinux OS, and eventually found LinuxMint once again. I tried it on the old machine and it worked marvelous! The resolution was just fine and all the applications i needed were at my finger tips. Along with the MintMenu and it's already included NDISwrapper coupled with a GUI to get wifi working nice and smooth in no time i was impressed beyond belief. After working with Mint on that machine for a little while and getting used to how everything worked i decided to dual boot it with my Vista machine. I kept working with it this time and found i absolutely loved it. 

After reading many articles on how Linux was superior and inferior to Windows i was sure fire set on installing it as my main OS. I waited until the LinuxMint 5 RC2 came out and then i moved all of my files to Mint and deleted Vista. 

I was quite content with it as everything i needed to work worked and so when LinuxMint 5 final came out i quickly downloaded it and installed it. I still had the same loading screen being too small problem as before with Mint but i was able to put up with it. I also had a problem with my Firefox scrollbar that i absolutely hated but was able to live with. When i tried to install Blubuntu on it, i had some problems and i was pretty fed up by then. I was contemplating if i should use Ubuntu or go back to Vista for a few days but i decided that i love Linux too much to stick with something inferior. 

I decided on installing Ubuntu as soon as i finished 7 episodes of the last of my anime left for me to watch so i could put the rest on my USB stick and get it down quickly. After finishing said anime i went and booted up into TinyMe and Fedora one last time as i didn't plan on popping them in ever again. Now, time to install Ubuntu. 

I booted into the Ubuntu liveCD, now with more knowledge i set my resolution to 1280x1024 instead of my native 1680x1050 so i could get visibility in the LiveCD and installed it using my preferred partitioning scheme. 

I booted into Ubuntu and enabled restricted drivers, then i went and added all the programs i liked/needed and updated my system. 

It was very quick and painless to get it set up how i liked it and OH MY GNU, the scrollbar and loading screen issues were fixed! Amazing! This alone hooked me into Ubuntu and even though i still recommend Mint for beginners, i love and will gladly recommend Ubuntu to anyone. BTW, this is only about 2hrs after install. :D
