### Post by aphexdash on 2007-01-18
Well Hi all, new to the forums, and wanted to give my Testimonial (Warning, Lengthy).

A little preface, i have used nearly every MS OS, starting at Dos 3.00-6.22, win3.0-me, nt4-xp.  I also worked as a freelance computer tech (Windows).  Always mucked around with linux, starting with mandrake 8.0 (i know, not very far back) and tried a few, Mandriva, suse, red hat/fedora and was not really thinking i could seriously consider ridding myself of Windows.  But on one faithful day, I was saved, my names AphexDash, and this is my story...

Twas a harsh summer afternoon in Australia, and i was knee deep in a game of Unreal Tournament 2004 on Windows XP, just about to nail the final flag capture on CTF-Face III, for the win! But alas, Stutter, Stutter, nothing!  BSOD.  Well thats just dandy, I restarted my Computer (A Laptop, ASUS M6Ne, Pentium-M 1.7Ghz, 1024mb Ram, Mobility 9700, Centrino Stuff, etc..), to XP had started a disk scan, and found a few Cross-Linked files, and continued to load windows.  Then once in windows I was trying copy over an Audio cd to OGG, but the computer kept giving me, CRC errors on my main Laptop's HDD, 2nd partition.  OH YEAH! :(

Everything i tried after that gave the same result, CRC BAM!, so i formatted and reinstalled XP, and as soon as it loaded, CRC Error.  I thought my HDD was a goner, but it was odd because i was listening closely to my laptop, and I've found usually if a computer gives you constant CRC's the HDD is dead, and it makes a repeating "Whhhhiiiirrrrrr, CLICK".  So i thought, i'm on holidays, i've got no real important stuff on the hdd, lets once again, bugger around with Linux.

Upon entering "Windows to linux for a n00b" into google, one of the pages popped up with this strange distro called "Ubuntu".  I was intrigued, so began downloading the dapper drake live/install CD (Luckily I had a 320gb external usb2.0 drive to save it to).  Once downloaded, i burnt the CD and rebooted with the CD in the drive.  Upon boot, Pressed Enter to start the LiveCD.  a few mins later, was presented with a clean/crisp Gnome UI, i was impressed.  So i mucked around with it for a while, And installed it to my HDD without XP, i expected the install to fail when formatting my hdd with swap/ext3, but BAM, perfect! No errors, and prompted me to either restart or continue using the LiveCD, i promptly restarted.

Wow, boot was fast, and my login worked.   I didn't have the net, my Wireless LAN card was setup, but i wasn't sure how to set it up to join a Open Shared WEP network.  So a little digging i found IWCONFIG page in "System>Help>System Documentation", within literally 10 mins i was up and browsing google to find out what else i could do, I felt invincible!

After only 2 weeks with dapper, i've updated to EDGY, installed the ATi drivers (which seem to work well for me), installed Unreal Tournament 2004, Neverwinter Nights Diamond (with all the premium modules), fell in love with Amarok, and most of all, been freed from Windows.

I'm not sure what i can do to ever repay anyone/everyone involved with Ubuntu or GNU/Linux, but just know, I've been saved!

Yours Faithfully AphexDash.

p.s: Sorry for the Length :/

p.p.s:  Thank you "Extreme Coder" I truly believe your saying:
"A Windows poweruser spends his time fixing Windows.
A Linux poweruser spends his time discovering and improving Linux"

Thats something i truly believe now.
