### Post by Donshyoku on 2006-06-21
I have been a Windows user since we got our first computer in 1992.  We started with Windows 3.11 upgraded to Windows 95 and then there was a large gap.  One year for Christmas, I got a brand-new Compaq with WinME.

The computer was great, the OS sucked ever for its time.  I waited for Windows XP and upgraded to that with my stepfather's promotional discount (he worked at a university).  Alas, I used the operating system happily.

Then, Microsoft took too long.  As of now, the OS has been out for 5 years with no major updates.  Vista is on the way, but doesn't look at all inspiring.

Going from Red Hat 7 to Fedora to Ubuntu... I have finally found something to hold onto.  I used Hoary infrequently with a dual-boot, the same for Breezy, but now feel all too well on Dapper.  Today, I installed Windows XP again right over top of my Dapper install.  Since Dapper's release, I have installed Windows twice and both for the same reason.  Support...

A lot of websites use ActiveX... a lot of sites use DRM-protected audio or video... i had to use Windows to access this content.  But after installing Windows, I was in a drudgeland.  It was slow, buggy, crashy, and needed to restart every few minutes after getting gunked up by a few websites or errors.

Now, I am installing Dapper again with a new resolution.  If it doesn't work on Linux, somehow or some way, I won't do it.  I won't support a site that wants to stream me a DRM-protected music video.  I just won't watch it or find it somewhere else.  If someone wants to show me a site with ActiveX and no other option, I'll avoid the site.  

Microsoft is in the position they are in because consumers put them there.  No longer in Windows a good consumer product for me, and it's time to leave it behind.  In all fairness, Vista may turn out better than I think, but I always say...

As a consumer, I am going to consume the best product I can afford and obtain.  Right now that is Linux, more specifically Ubuntu... I am going to consume it along with everyone else and dethrone Microsoft or at least make them think twice about my business...

### Post by dimatrod on 2006-06-29

The key with this situation is patience and to sit down for multiple hours, reading tutorials and looking for the linux ports of everything.  Oh, one more thing, let's not forget that if you want to use windows+office, you'll need at least $300, whereas if you do it the linux way, you'll need patience and probably $30 for Cedega.
