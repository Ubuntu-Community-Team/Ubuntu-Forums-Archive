### Post by milehigh on 2006-06-19
I would like to send out a great big THANK YOU to everyone involved in the development of Ubuntu. I'm now running 6.06 LTS as the only operating system on my primary computer, and I couldn't be more pleased! 

Over the course of the last year or so, I tried out a number of distros and had pretty much settled on PCLinuxOS. Then I bought a new Dell Dimension E310, but I had trouble getting it to work with my Viewsonic VX710 flat panel. I tried a few other distros from my collection of Linux discs with similar results. Dapper Beta was available, so I thought I would give it a try. Once I booted in the “safe” mode, it displayed perfectly in the monitor's native 1280 x 1024. I decided to back up my files and install the beta version. Every day I downloaded the updates, fully aware that things could break. And most everything kept working just fine! I followed the instructions on the Unofficial Starter Guide to get all the multimedia working. And I ran into a few things:
1.Slow internet – from previous experience I knew to disable IPv6 in the “aliases” file. I also found a post on the forum on how to change the settings on Firefox to speed it up.
2.My printer wouldn't work at all. I found a post in the forums that directed me to a how-to for downloading and installing the correct driver – problem solved. 
3.I found another post that directed me to the necessary files to install to get my scanner working. This is the first time I've ever gotten a scanner to work in Linux! It's kind of slow and noisy, but it works.
This is what's really special about Ubuntu - the support. If you can't find something in the user guides or wiki, chances are there is a thread in one of the forums with the answer. This is a very knowledgeable and helpful community. I'm going to install Dapper on my laptop tonight and dual-boot with Windows, since I have hard-drive space to spare. At this point, though, I don't think I'll be needing the Windows partition much because I can do everything I need to do with Ubuntu!
