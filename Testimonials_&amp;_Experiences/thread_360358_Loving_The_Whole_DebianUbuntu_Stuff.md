### Post by SuperKillar on 2007-02-13
Heya People,

About 2 years ago (when I was 11) I encountered many spyware/malware/virus/security issues with our family windows xp computer. It puzzled me why it was so unsecure and so slow nevertheless my favourite online games that I had spent alot of time on kept getting hacked which was making me mad. The second thing was that the response from everything I did, also another thing bugging me was it was a very new/fast computer at that time. This slowness also was being caused by the firewall and antivirus. December that year for christmas I reveived an Acer Computer (Athlon XP 2600 1.9ghz , 512mb RAM , 40gb HD, Radeon 9550 ) and of course wireless :) that was one of the best christmas's for me :lolflag:   

So I started searching around the internet and asking around places. My dad also works for Sun which uses solaris so he said maybe use UNIX, So that is what I did, Solaris was put on to my computer. I used a guide off the internet which taught me alot about partitioning and many other things to do with Linux/Unix. It was very fast, I was really pleased with it but software was very hard to install for me so I started looking for an alternative. I later found SUsE which however felt bloated for what linux should be (so I removed it) I then found fedora core 5 but it did not recognise my wireless (rt2500) I tried to use ndiswrapper but nothing worked.

Now this is the cool part ):P 
I googled for about the millionth time it seemed, about a good linux distro, I was starting to get fed up on my windows that I had to put back on because I had no working linux yet :rolleyes: so I had to find something good. I stumbled upon [http://www.distrowatch.com](http://www.distrowatch.com) and looked at the top distro. Ubuntu Seemed to be the one with a massive 2k (Approx) of people visiting that page. So I thought okey we will check that out. Was amazed at the screenshots and how big the iso was (1CD :D ). 

I put the cd in my drive and booted from CD, The install was really friendly and I had it installed in no time, I like the way I could do things while it was installing, ('coz it is a  live CD) Also My Wireless card showed up :KS Also It was fast which I liked. So now it was installed I was prepaired to boot into my fresh system. The beautiful login screen came up I entered my username and password and It loaded like lightning. I then went to setup my wireless (took a while as I could not find the option :P ) but when I configured. I never experienced any drop outs like windows would do. the next step was to add some eye candy, I liked the way it was so customizable withought needing 3rd party programs. It has stayed with me all this time on my 40gbhd. It has on edgy now and can not wait for feisty in April. Also I finally got used to synpatic manager recently even though it was fairly easy to use anyway. ^^

Throughout all this time, I have learned alot of things, which also helped was that recently I received a free computer from someone very kind that was wanting to get rid of it (Pentium III, 384mb RAM, 20gb HD). I thought this would be a great idea for testing linux distributions and their different desktop enviroments just incase I found a better one or the same. It has been really good for doing these things, I even plucked up the courage to install gentoo the other day but I got stuck somewhere and gave up.

I have tried many distros on that box such as:

ZenWalk (XFCE)
Gentoo (Was Going To Be KDE)
Debian Sarge/Etch (GNOME)
Debian Etch  (KDE)
Debian Etch  (XFCE)
Ubuntu Feisty Fawn Herd3
Xubuntu (XFCE, FluxBox, GNOME)
Kubuntu (KDE)
Arch Linux
Suse (New one)(KDE) ~ Nothing had really changed ^^
Mepis (KDE)
Mandriva (GNOME)
PCLinuxOS (KDE)

They have all been very enjoyable but not the right ones for me (maybe debian etch though :) )

Overall My Linux Life has been pretty awesome, AND.....

















.... I plan to continue :guitar: 

Thanks
SuperKillar/ Robin
