### Post by warped6 on 2007-07-16
I just wanted to say Thank You to everyone involved with Ubuntu.

I initially had my laptop set up  dual boot  with Ubuntu on an external drive. Well something happened to the MBR so I couldn't boot either. Decided to get a new hard drive and go cold turkey.

It's been great! I use my laptop for work and have found native versions of everything I need except for 1 program. (IBM Websphere Development Studio Client  With that I just installed VMServer and a very stripped down Windows install. Works great. )  I can connect to VPN's, remote desktop back to my office (XP back there), wireless connections. Everything. 

My internal wireless card crapped out so I went and got a PCCard wireless card. Plugged it in. Ubuntu went "OK cool I know what that is, here ya go!" and it was up and running. No reboot. No driver installing. Just plug it in and it works.

2 other guys I work with "Upgraded" to Vista. They have had all kinds of problems. I just look at them and shake my head.

Anyway, it's been great! Next step is to get my computer at work set up as dual boot!

Rock On! :guitar:
