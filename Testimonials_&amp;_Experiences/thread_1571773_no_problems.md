### Post by skythunder on 2010-09-10
I finally installed Ubuntu 10.04 a few months ago and have no problems to speak of. A minor issue with Skype, but I have a workaround. I'm running dual monitors off a oldish Sony Vaio.

I also installed Ubuntu on my netbook for travelling, again all works.  

I've learned to work with Terminal and Sudo and all that and while a bit like when I first used mnembler assember and trashed the bootstrap loader. But that was then, and this is now and I just want software to do what it says on the tin. Fortunately, many developers understand this and are producing superbe functionality, with sensible defaults and customisation options.  The bulk of potential users aren't going to 'look under the hood' any more than most people maintain their cars anymore. 

I would add that end-user ease is an important driver of adoption and diffusion, which in the end will improve quality, which benefits everyone. The more 'ambient' the technology is, the more likely people will use it. I think one test is to make computers that from a user's perspective don't boot but just turn on and off (that is, they work like using the telphone or turning on the television).
