### Post by Kearin on 2007-04-02
I decided to switch to Ubuntu after our family's computers' with various windows installations continuously crashed necessitating reinstalls. My personal computer was running Win2k, and it did everything I needed it to. However, I was growing worried that my computer just might be next, so I decided to take the plunge. I took an old Ubuntu 5.10 install disk and tried it out. There were no snags during installation, and I quickly grew to love the system.

Post installation I quickly found Synaptic and grabbed the various packages I needed, and things have worked without any problems I couldn't solve in a few minutes. There are many options that I have yet to explore, and I need to get to a faster internet connection so I can update everything (I am currently living off of 56kilobits/second at best over a LAN.), but even without them I am happy with the current functionality.

End result? My computer has never run as fast, I have better control of my computer than ever before, and there are so many new things I am amazed. I have successfully converted two other people to Ubuntu, but I am still working on my family. My only beef is that I have seen the light, and yet I am still forced into the dark and dank Windows caves at school.

I thank thee, members of the Ubuntu Community, for building such a marvelous OS and creating this welcoming community.
