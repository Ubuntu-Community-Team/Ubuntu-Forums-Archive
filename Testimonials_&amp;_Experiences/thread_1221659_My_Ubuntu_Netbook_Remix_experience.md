### Post by groeswenphil on 2009-07-24
Hi Group,
Bought an Acer Aspire One netbook back in April.
Lovely little device.........comes with the Linpus distribution.

Well, it worked OK but quite honestly, the interface looked awful; more like a Fisher Price toy than a computer.

I installed Ubuntu Netbook Remix. Now, it looked and felt like a real computer but after a few days I noticed that it was running much slower than under Linpus. It took much longer to boot up. Also, it couldn't deal with BBC IPlayer content at all. Eventually I gave up and went back to linpus.

A couple of weeks ago I thought I'd give UNR another go.
This time, things worked much better. Faster boot-up and BBC IPlayer worked.
My netbook looked and felt more like my standard desktop computer.....especially when I changed from the UNR desktop to a standard Ubuntu desktop.
I was indeed happy.

Then one day I started it up and showed just a blank desktop. No top panels at all. Fiddled around a bit but got nowhere. Eventually I re-installed.

Eventually I got the machine back to starting point.....then after a few days, exactly the same thing happened......no panel at the top of the screen, consequently very hard to get anywhere.

Again, after fiddling I went for a complete re-install.

Things I've learned:-

1) Ubuntu is far better supported than Linpus.....far nicer to look at BUT does it actually work? I'm not so sure.

2) DON'T EVER install Ubuntu Netbook Remix onto an Acer Aspire One netbook that has an expansion SD card installed to the right hand port. If you do then UNR installs itself onto the SD card.

3) Linpus is as solid as a rock even though it looks like a Fisher Price toy. Who was it that said, "If it aint broke, don't fix it."?
