### Post by bobwdn on 2010-03-03
My Linux Story



Circa 2001 - I am fed up with Windoze blue screen of death. Time to try anything else. I cannot afford Win2000, heck, I just cannot justify spending my hard earned morning on junk. So . . . I tried Redhat (I think it was v7.0, but I do not remember, so don't call me on the correct version.) I didn't know anything about dual boot, so I just installed on my second computer to see what would happen. It worked!!! Then after a few months it began to run funny and finally hung up. My primary machine was still running (barely) Win98. What is this I read? Free updates with Redhat. Surely, they charge for these, I think!! But, read more on the INTERNET and no Redhat does not charge for updates. So, I update and get a new kernel and a bunch of other stuff and WOW!! My old second hand Classic Pentium 200MMX with 64Mb Ram works again with Redhat whatever version.



Circa 2002 - I am discovering that I get more and can do more with Linux. Let's see, what is this LTSP thing? I found an old Classic Pentium 75 Packard Bell machine at a garage sale for $25USD. Change this, do that and wow, again here this old computer thing runs my Redhat via LTSP and it's as fast as it is on the 200MMX machine.



Not long after this I dumped the Windoze 98 OS in the primary machine (another Classic Pentium 233MMX w/128MbRAM) and installed Redhat something (I think 7.3, maybe.) and Windoze is gone for now.



What is this Knoppix thing? And what, it runs entirely off the CD!!!! I experiment!!!



Circa 2003 - Windoze is becoming a distant memory. The more I read about Linux, the more I like it.



I stick with Redhat 7.3 for a while, then decided to try Redhat 8.0. This was about the time that Redhat became Fedora and that made me a little nervous that they might be getting ready to demand money for updates. (See, still skeptical and sure that this open source, free of charges thing is going to go away.) Then Redhat v8.0 began to run strangely. (Later, I would learn that it was due to a hardware issue and had nothing at all to do with the software.)



After my hardware issues were resolved, I decided to try SuSE 9.0. So, I bought (that's right, spent some money) a copy for $80USD. I liked the YaST control center. There were differences in how configurations are handled between my previous Redhat versions and how SuSE does things.



I stuck with SuSE for a while.



Circa 2004 - Windoze is now long gone from my home.



Circa 2005 - More Linux reading and learning.



Circa 2006 - What is this!! Microsoft and SuSE Collaboration Agreement!!! Scared the H__L out of me!! I need something different, again. So, what is this Ubuntu thing. Hum, v6.06LTS and I can try it with a live CD to see if I like it, cool!!



And the rest is history. I have been Ubuntu ever since. At my work, we use Ubuntu Samba servers to store our design (CAD) files on a hardware RAID5 array. We have had hard drives fail and the array rebuilds over to the spare drive. We go on line and order a new drive, install it as a new spare and all is good. Unfortunately our workstation software will only run on Windoze XP. The good news is that Windoze XP is the only software we own and have to have properly licensed. Our proprietary CAD software is frustrating piece of software junk and we (as a business) have no other option. Our vendors supply us with catalogs for this software and without it we would never sell anything. But ! ! ! Ubuntu servers saves many many dollars and cut our total cost of ownership. Open-source software including Firefox, Thunderbird, OpenOffice.org, Spybot, TightVNC, WinSCP, CCleaner and countless other programs that help keep our workstations safe from most viruses. Oh, and I almost forgot about the IPCop firewall that runs on a dedicated (old) computer to keep prying eyes out of our system. VPN tunnels to push and pull files to and from two different locations. The list goes on and on . . . . 



So, Circa 2010 - Ubuntu Lucid Lynx 10.02LTS is due soon and the future looks very promising. I may have found a possible replacement for the expensive proprietary CAD software we are forced to buy. This possible replacement runs under Java on a MAC and currently on Windoze. Hum, I wonder if it will run on Ubuntu? Time will tell.

Thanks to the Linux community and most certainly THANK YOU to everyone a Ubuntu!!!! :)
