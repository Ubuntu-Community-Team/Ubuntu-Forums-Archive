### Post by fl1pper on 2005-10-04
First things first, after being on ancient IBM gear (60s & 70s), Macs and SGIs, and Windows, what a gas Ubuntu Linux is. I did an install, my first Linux install, of Debian, a few weeks ago, on an Aluminum Powerbook. Went okay, got to the KDE desktop on the 2nd attempt, with zero support of any kind, and zero background, but I trashed the install after having tons of iptables issues (say wha?), and all sorts of hardware stuff that seemed to be recognized by the sh script on load, but didn't really work in the runtime end of things (where it matters, in other words).

Then comes Breezy, and the install was slightly simpler, I guess, not a problem. A lot more of the Mac laptop's buttons and whatnot are operational, and the interface itself, and included apps, are just great. I haven't booted into OS X in a week. A fine collaborative effort, and I'm impressed. I'm giving up a ton of access to big Windows and Mac suites. (Office Enterprise, Adobe CS Pro on both platforms, Final Cut, a boatload of audio sequencers and the entire Native Instruments thing, etc). It's a sacrifice, but the stuff's still 'over there'. Heheh.

Meanwhile, the only flaw I see, is a real marginalization of the ppc after-market thing. Example: Flash plugs, Java Runtime, MPlayer, VideoLAN VLC, ffmepg....and on and on. And yeah, I know, all that stuff is available...but it's all in i386, AM64, etc... what's up with that? I d-loaded/installed IBM's Java runtime but the Synaptic app (there's a great one), says, my java-ppc-50 is deprecated or something. Really? It just came out. It also keeps asking me to trash the gnome-java-dummy thing...why? On a ppc CPU v. that's the only way to 'tell Gnome' that there's a Runtime happening. Inconsistencies are part of life, no big deal. 

And the external monitor thing. What goes on with that? I mean if the device manager can pick up on the volume keys on a 3rd-party (Matias) keyboard, what's so bloody tricky about an external monitor? Anyway, I don't want to sound like an old bitch, because i love the ubuntu environment and approach to things, it feels really right. But I'm concerned about altivec support/implementation, the seeming 'bias' against IBM and the Power architecture, including, specifically, the ppc chips in a lot of Macs, and what will happen when Apple goes to the intel side. What then? I doubt very much i'll go with them, and I bought an Apple in Mtn. View in 1978, so I go back a little farther than the dot com boom.

Anyone out there interested in getting a ppc/Mac/Big Blue-centric bbs or LISTSERV, web site together, let me know. It'll be a way to give back. As great as this desktop Linux distro is, it looks like we'll have to watch out for ourselves.
