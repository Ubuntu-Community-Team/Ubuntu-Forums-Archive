### Post by ugm6hr on 2009-08-03
I've been an active forum user for a while now, and an Ubuntu user for a little longer.  This is my latest story...

I taught my dad to use a computer (and the internet) some years ago (on a Windows 98 machine), and he is now set up at home with a limited user account on his desktop.  My brother and I maintain his machine for him between our visits.  He only writes the odd letter and email, and spends a reasonable amount of time online on cricket and news sites and listening to internet radio.

He bought a new Compaq Mini 702EA Netbook (essentially the same as most 7xx series and HP Mini 1000 series) a few weeks ago with XP Home.  Not a bad spec (and outshines my Dell Mini 9 with its larger screen and keyboard) with Atom, 1GB RAM, 60GB HD.

Within 2 weeks, he was complaining about it being slow, and asked me to reinstall Windows (as I often do for his home computer at intervals when necessary).  While I am perfectly capable of googling with the best of them, installing XP without the aid of an external CD / DVD did not seem like a straightforward task.

Hence, I let him play with my Dell Mini 9 (Ubuntu+Xfce) for an hour, and he agreed to give it a go.  I saved his XP with GParted, and installed a dual-boot.  While doing this, I found 2 executables in his C: drive (from the LiveUSB) named something like gfawhyr78934tr34wgtf79.exe (which I just deleted) - googling that string found them to be identified malware of some description, but with no instructions for removal other than advice to buy some software to protect yourself.  More reasons to avoid the MS world at the moment...

Anyway, after a little setting up (multimedia and streaming predominantly), a couple of well-documented fixes (sound and ethernet do not work out of the box on this device), he was up and running with Ubuntu + Xfce within an hour.  His Xfce is similar to mine, albeit with a bottom panel resembling XP - even with the menu button labelled as "Start" :(  I also set up Firefox 3.5 to open at boot, since he uses it 99.9% of the time on the Netbook.

He seemed thoroughly impressed with it, and I think he especially likes Rhythmbox's ability to open a radio stream directly, without requiring a browser (presumably there is something open source for Windows that does the same?)  I've left him to it, so will hear back about his progress in my absence over the next few weeks.  I'm interested to know whether he will persevere with Windows (which appears to be running somewhat normally after a bit of simple disk cleanup / defrag / chkdisk and deleting those executables), or if he will become a new convert...
