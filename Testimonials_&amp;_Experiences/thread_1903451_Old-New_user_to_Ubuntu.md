### Post by kensum on 2012-01-02
I started using linux with 6.10 and the gnome de and I thought ubuntu had an easy to use distro, but I found myshelf having trouble setting up new users and getting them to the point they no longer needed hand held. To make a long story short, I left ubuntu and found many good and some not so good distros. The one thing that I have always felt and expressed is that ubuntu has been a major mover in the linux field and deserves much credit in bringing linux to the everyday users. Whether intentional or not, this can be seen in the development of the other distros. 

A few months back I returned to ubuntu to see what all the fuss was over the unity de. Installed 11.04 and 11.10beta. The beta was so I could watch and use the changes being made from 11.04. The first thing I noticed was Ubuntu was still ubuntu, a good solid linux that time had not changed. Good job devs. When 11.10 became final, I felt this was a vast improvement over 11.04....so 11.04 went away. To be fair to myself, I have always installed or used two of something and after a month or so, the one I preferred would come to the top; so I replaced 11.04 with a pure gnome3 shell os (sabayon7) to use in conjunction with Ubuntu's Unity. After roughly a month and a half here is what my conclusions are:

I did not like Unity and found I would avoid it in favor of the gnome3 shell. I did not count what I call development issues against Unity; such as slow launcher response,which I'm sure will be fixed. My main concern was dash. I don't get it. If you are going to make a "better dash" then do so. I found the gnome3 dash much faster and easier to organize and switch from workspace to workspace or app to app. 

I set auto login to off and switched Unity to the gnome shell de. I have gotten rid of sabayon, for other reasons, but am still on ubuntu and the pure gnome3 shell. Ubuntu has given me no problems of any kind. Ubuntu continues to be an outstanding os in both use and stability and with either gnome shell or unity it will return to the top of my lists of distros to offer new users. Kudos to Conical and the Ubuntu devs. thanks:P
