### Post by deformated on 2009-07-17
First, I'd like to thank the community for their time putting together mounds of great software. It is much appreciated!

So... *deep breath*

I'm the resident tech guy among my circle of friends and family. I like to help out wherever I can but after repeatedly cleaning their systems of viruses and malware I began to get really pissed off! We're talking 5-30 times some of them trashed their system which was quite easy for them to do. Each time they did this I'm sure they weren't concerned with the hours, no days, I've spent fixing their mistakes.

After years of this going on I realized none were ever going to learn what it takes to be a responsible computer operator. I needed to either tell them to go somewhere else or I needed to adopt a different policy. I opted for the latter after and did a few experiments.

Step 1.) Bring their computer to my house so they can't bitch.
Step 2.) Install Ubuntu!
Step 3.) Bring the machine right back over and drop it off.
Step 4.) Haul *** out of there and make them give it a try.

The first thing they notice is that it looks tight, organized, and simple. After playing around, with my guidance or not, they all realized things just worked and quickly at that. None ever mentions the best part: no crap chewing up their system, stealing their information, or getting in the way of their work. They don't have the downtime and I'm able to be a son, brother, and friend instead of their angry elitist computer bitch.

--------
Note: I really expected to be met with more hostility. Any complaints were solved by showing them where to get equivalent software to what they used. Most of it is in the default install anyway.
--------

Do all of them know what Ubuntu is? No.

Was there anything they did before that they can't do now? Netflix and a few games.

Cost: 1hr each for install AND training!

Success!

Thanks again for your time and I look forward to the future of the project.

:D
