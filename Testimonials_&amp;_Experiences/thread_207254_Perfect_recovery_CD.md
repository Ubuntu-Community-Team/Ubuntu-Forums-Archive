### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-07-01
A friend of mine has a laptop computer. Somehow, Windows got corrupted and the computer wouldn't start up. She had a "recovery" CD for the computer, but that just wiped the disk clean and restored it to factory settings, and my friend needed the files.

I tried running DSL, Puppy, Dynebolic, and Knoppix to either copy the files to a USB HD MP3 player or to my Ubuntu computer using Samba. None of them managed to accomplish that (e.g. Konqueror crashed on Knoppix, couldn't mount my MP3 player on DSL, etc).

So I tried Ubuntu Live CD. It didn't mount the Windows partitions by default, of course, but that was easy enough for me to do. Copying to my MP3 player was flawless and impressively quick! The laptop didn't want to get along with the other distros, but Ubuntu ended off being the only one that didn't crash. I was very impressed.

(Pity my friend still needs MS Money and MYOB, or I'd suggest to her to convert the computer to Ubuntu, it ran that well! Still I got the opportunity to install some open-source onto the computer anyway.)
