### Post by hal8000 on 2008-06-03
My first experience of Hardy was not good, dropped to initramfs shell and general ata errors.
At this time I had 2 IDE drives sharing the same IDE cable and IRQ. A (side effect) of libATA emulation in the kernel renames all all hdx drives to be sdx.
I wasn't too happy with the performance of my UDMA100 drive so decided to be a cheap Hitachi Deskstar SATA2 drive.

Now having a SATA drive on its own cable, a single UDMA HD on its own cable has removed the problem of shared IRQ's and Hardy runs like a rocket, under 30 secs boot time on a Pentium 4 2.8G CPU (not dual core).

I have to say that the problems I had on Hardy were also common to any distribution that uses libATA, always a string of ATA errors, and it may be due to bus mastering, or whatever, but having the freedom of separate drives on separate ata and ide cables has eliminated my source of problems.

For me, this is the fastest Ubuntu at booting as well, Gutsy and Feisty were around 40 secs, still faster than windoze...yawn
