### Post by Old *ix Geek on 2007-11-08
Last night I finally thought up--and implemented--a solution to a dilemma I've had.  Because it involves a windoze drive...I was never quite sure how to solve this [since windoze sucks as badly as it does].  So anyway, here it is.

Although I rarely use it, there's a 'doze partition on one of my computers.  I had it because I've been unable to get Paint Shop Pro running **correctly** and **reliably** on any of my *nix boxes.  My most recent attempt yielded what looked like a successful try--I mean everything looked great, and early testing seemed fine, and I was really thrilled--but then reality hit.  It would crash randomly, certain tools failed to work, and so on.  (I need PSP for my [web site's](http://www.smartassproducts.com) designs--yes, I've tried the GIMP, yes, I use the GIMP, but there are certain features PSP has that just make *MY* needs so much easier than the GIMP.)

Since I rarely boot the windoze side of that box, I tend not to think about it--in fact, there have been times I've totally forgotten about it and proclaimed this a windoze-free household!  Last night I decided to TRY using some sort of remote desktop method of accessing it, so I booted up 'doze and started looking for solutions.  I already use KRDC and KRFB on various Ubuntu boxes, but hadn't thought of it for the 'doze box.  I tried...and tried...and tried various methods; I scoured the web and these forums looking for a way to **control** an XP box **from** a Linux box.  I was at the point of pulling out my hair after running back and forth between the windoze box and the laptop.

And then, like magic!, something worked. I actually got a reaction from the windoze box.  I entered the info it asked for [on Ubuntu] then walked over to 'doze to answer its question and, voila!, I had a connection.  I tweaked and tweaked various settings and ended up with what LOOKS like a reliable connection.  I've been running PSP and it's working like a charm.

So now I have a 'doze box--with no monitor, keyboard, or trackball--that I'll leave booted up all the time (its drives are networked, so if I need something on the *nix side, it's always available).  Then I'll feel okay because I don't have to dual-boot and/or look at 'doze periodically. The laptop I'm typing this on is now connected to one of my other Ubuntu boxes and the windoze box; both connections are fast and reliable.  I'm absolutely thrilled. =D>
