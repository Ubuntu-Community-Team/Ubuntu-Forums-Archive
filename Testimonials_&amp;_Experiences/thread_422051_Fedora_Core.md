### Post by Jerry Wolczanski on 2007-04-24
I had Ubuntu running on an old machine and it did fine.  But I've had nothing but troubles with newer versions on my new (AMD64) machine.  With a new computer dedicated to Linux, I've had the ability to bop around and try various distro's before I really commit to one.  I love the Ubuntu community but I tell ya, Fedora Core 6 was the smoothest encounter so far by a long shot.

Started out by immediately recognizing my graphics card
Best of all, it immediately recognized my nice Viewsonic 1680x1050 resolution screen
(no diddling the config file!)
and it recognized my m-audio sound card, and did a cool little sound check

Quite a nice experience.  Ubuntu should dig into the Fedora code and find out how they were able to do it.  In fairness, the Fedora install is a 32-bit....and my recent Ubuntu installs were 64-bit.  Maybe therein lies the difference - I dunno.  I just know the Fedora install was without a hitch.  I even did it twice, going back to 7.04 (it kept crashing during the install) before I gave up and went back to Fedora.

I'll stay in the community - I'm a fan of Ubuntu and still recommend it.  If I had stock in Microsoft, I'd be selling it.  I think open source is the way of the future.  

Interesting to see all the notes/experiences in this section.  Folks love Linux/Ubuntu or hate it.

For the record - I have a Mac G4, dual processor as my home computer.  My intention is to slowly port everything I do over to open source, mostly internet, mail, photography and music.  We'll see about that!  I've got no real time schedule....I guess when 10.3.9 ain't supported anymore, I'll pull the plug on Mac if the open-source experience has matured.  

I absolutely refuse to use Windows, so that option isn't even on the table.

Cheers
Jerry W
