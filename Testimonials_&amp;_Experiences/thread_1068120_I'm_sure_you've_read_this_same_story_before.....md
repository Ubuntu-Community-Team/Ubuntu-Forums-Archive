### Post by Sspankyy on 2009-02-12
Well,

   OS's on my system:
 
 Ubuntu-  My primary OS. General purpose, powerful, and pretty darned easy... It was, up until recently, getting a 50/50 share with my XP time... Until "IT" happened...  <explained later> In the beginning, I would barely use it, I kept it as a novelty, with intentions of "tweeking" it to my machine, but with every new version of Ubuntu, I would use it more because of the increased functionality.  The newer versions are WONDERFUL because they are easier to convert people with... 

   Puppy- I have never seen better quality video playback on my laptop PERIOD...  How can you not love puppy for that. I also keep it around to help people with older computers convert... "This is how it looks/feels"

 XP- Not used much as of late, in the process <right now in fact> of removing all pics/music/videos/whatever so I can delete it for reinstall with a smaller partition. <still keeping it around for "special occasions">  Recently I had some nasty virus disable my virus scanner and firewall..... While I was away from my computer.... for a couple days.... in MY account as Admin..... I'm going to stop there because I'm sure you get the point.

   I said all of that to say this: When I was on XP, I always knew that I would have to be worried about what I'm doing, and I'd even log into Ubuntu to do any kind of online buying or banking.
   Now that I am primarily Ubuntu, I have that safe feeling all of the time on my computer.  I know that <as long as my wireless traffic is not being monitored> I am pretty safe.  I don't have to worry about "should I click on this" or any stuff like that.  I still practice pretty safe internetting, but when one of those windows pops up telling me that I must download WinAntivirus2009, I just smile, I don't freak out any more.

  I will also mention the other reasons that everyone mentions, because they apply.  I can Tweek <fun once you get the hang of it>, I can find pretty much Any software I need, for free, most of which in Synaptic Package Manager <I just love that program.... Yes, I could command line it, but I can't help it>.  You know all of the other reasons, they were likely some of the reasons you are here...

Anyways, I'm going to stop typing now, I only meant to post a couple sentences...

Goodbye for now

 Scott
