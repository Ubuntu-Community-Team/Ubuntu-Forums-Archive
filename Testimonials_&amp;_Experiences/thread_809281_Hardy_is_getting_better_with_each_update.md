### Post by Antman on 2008-05-27
Ok, I admit, when Hardy first came out I was disappointed with its performance on my hardware (major freezing and locking): IBM Lenevo T60, and Dell XPS 400 (older model with a PentiumD processor), and some other laptops.
So I went to Fedora 8/9. While I love the Fedora experience, I really wanted to run a LTS type release just so I could lock down the machines and stop tinkering for a while. While running Fedora I always *wanted *to tinker, play, tweak, and configure. lol

So a month after Hardy's release, I decided to give it another try. I installed it on my test laptop, applied the updates, and tried it out for a while. So far, I haven't had any issues with it (unlike the first time I tried it).

I installed it on my main desktop too and it is doing very well. So, it looks as though the LTS is starting to live up to its name. :popcorn:

I still run fedora on some of my systems, but Ubuntu has found its way back to my heart.:guitar:
