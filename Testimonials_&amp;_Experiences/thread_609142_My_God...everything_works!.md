### Post by Krovas on 2007-11-10
I recently purchased a modern laptop with a 64 bit dual-core AMD, and I decided I wanted a 64 bit flavor of Linux to go with it. I kind of assumed that finding and getting such to run would be a long, hard slog. Lo and behold, barely 24 hours later, only a few of which were spent actually working on the thing, I have a permanent installation of the AMD64 version of Feisty Fawn up. It networks. It reads my thumb drives. It has full read/write support of the Vista volumes. And it runs like a flaming cat. I have to hand it to Ubuntu; I never thought I would settle into a new distro this quickly, and enjoy doing so this much.
