### Post by level9 on 2012-06-19
Unlike the majority of my counter parts and colleagues, I have never been a "Mac Fan-boy." I had an iMac for a while, I bought it because I thought the environment was pretty, I guess you could say I was naive then, going with the flow and following the trend set by larger design firms.

After being out of school for about two years I opened my own studio and decided to sell the iMac on eBay. I was sad to part with it but got over it almost instantly when the money hit my PayPal account. I took the money I got from the iMac and added about $3,000 to get a cheap laptop with Windows 7 and Adobe Creative Suite Master Collection CS4. 

I've designed or developed most of my portfolio using Windows, but as the version numbers increase, reliability and responsiveness decreases. I have to say now that I hate Windows7 and from the videos that people are posting on YouTube, I'll probably never adopt Windows8.

I installed Ubuntu 12.04 on my work computer alongside the included Windows7 installation. I've installed Ubuntu on a laptop before but hadn't used the OS since 11.04, so Unity is a new experience for me.

I love it! :D

After taking a couple days to explore the Software Center and the general experience I'm proud to say that I'm going to format my hard drive at work and re-install only Ubuntu, getting rid of Windows7. I've already done this with my desktop at home and on my laptop.

I thought I would be lost without Adobe's Creative Suite, but I've used programs like Net Beans for development before and found an awesome open-source WYSIWYG editor that I can use alongside it. (Both programs together replace Dreamweaver, brilliantly and obviously cost nothing.) GIMP is just as good, if not better than Photoshop, and I've also found excellent open-source alternatives for Premiere Pro, Soundbooth, Illustrator and InDesign.

I look forward to participating in the forums and sharing my experience with Ubuntu as a creative professional. Adobe has lost me with their constant updates that cost thousands of dollars. I'd much rather be a part of an awesome open -source OS community...Ubuntu!

This is my first post so I apologize if it isn't up to par, thanks for reading though. Please comment if you have any software suggestions or tips.
