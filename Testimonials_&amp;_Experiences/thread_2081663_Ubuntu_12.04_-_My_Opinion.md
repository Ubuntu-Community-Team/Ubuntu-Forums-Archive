### Post by Javelin Dan on 2012-11-07
Running Ubuntu 12.04 Ppc on an e-Mac G-4 (1-Ghz processor, 40 GB hard drive, 1GB RAM), and 12.04 i386 on a Compaq Presario laptop (duo core processor {don’t know specs off-hand}, 200 GB hard drive, 2GB RAM). Also running Lubuntu 12.04 Ppc on an old Mac G-4 “Graphics” (450 GHz processor, 20 GB hard drive, 1256 GB RAM).
   
   

  Guess I’ll weigh in on this just because I can. I started out dabbling in Linux about seven years ago strictly as a curiosity. At that time I had absolutely no idea what I was doing (some would make an effective argument that I still don’t), and was nothing but a “point and click” guy – still am as much as possible. I got more serious about Linux about 4-years ago when I bought the Compaq that had Windows-7 “Starter Edition” which had the Office Suite which has a time bomb that self- destructs after so much time unless you pay extra.
   


   I started on a mission of downloading and burning every single Linux distro I could that was advertised as “user-friendly” – dozens and dozens of them. I had a long cup of coffee with Puppy which still gets my vote for being the overall fastest, slickest, most fully-featured-in-a-small-package distro around. But ultimately, I walked away due to the fact that Puppy is not as intuitive and has a far crappier software management system compared to Ubuntu and its derivatives (sometimes Puppy programs work after installation, sometimes they don’t). I also found the Puppy forum community to be rather crabby and short of patience with true beginners…and yes, I know they are over worked and under paid. In contrast, I’ve seen the Ubuntu community bend over backwards to be polite to everyone – including some people who really don’t deserve it. 
   


  As my very modest skills *slowly *grew, I realized that for an idiot like me, Ubuntu was the closest I could come to the GUI Windows / Mac experience I was accustomed to. And everything just worked! I’ve never had a piece of software out of Synaptic fail to load or work as I have many times in Puppy. I also had a brief interlude with Zenwalk which I rather liked, but found their package management system rather confusing.
   


  For me, Ubuntu 10.10 (sorry, my ADD brain just can’t get all the animal names straight) was the Holy Grail. I found it pretty much flawless and very fast. I was horrified when I got the message sometime in March that my version was no longer supported – I thought I’d have till the middle of April at least. I could have upgraded to 11.04, then 11.10 to stretch things out, but I was lured by the siren’s song of 5-years LTS. So I bit the bullet and went for 12.04. I must say the installation was flawless (on all three machines) and overall, rather un-dramatic. I’ve actually found 12.04 to be as problem free as 10.10, though admittedly it is noticeably slower than 10.10. 
   


  I do want to weigh in on the “Unity” thing…*hated it!* After experimenting with Lubuntu, I installed the Lubuntu desktop on my two Ubuntu machines and like it much, much better. A much simpler interface for a knuckle-dragging troglodyte like me. And yes, I do realize I could have opted for the “Classic” desktop I had in 10.10.
   


  I have to believe this is the last iteration of a full-blown Ubuntu I’ll be able to install on my antique junk. Maybe Lubuntu will work, but with the constant resource creep we’ve all noticed in the newer kernels, I doubt that in 5-years, Ubuntu will even boot on my old stuff…if it’s even still running by then. No complaints, my e-Mac is approaching 10-years old (that’s like 70 in computer years!), and my G-4 Graphics is older than that! It’s actually pretty amazing that Ubuntu is able to revive this old crap and make it run as good as it does, especially on the powerpc platform. Thanks, devs!
