### Post by Fenris_rising on 2009-03-08
Hi all

Yesterday I took on a PC to sort out for a friend. Basically the windows install was dying from all the malware/spyware and viruses it had contracted. Heres a rundown of the steps I took from the start to it's glowing dual boot functionality.

Received PC early morning. Upon booting XP fell over with all sorts of messages and windows popping up.

[COLOR="Red"]INTERMISSION: Open PC up and choke to death on the fur ball inside. Strip down and clean it up.[/COLOR]

This looks bad! Reboot with gparted disk in drive and blasted the partition into oblivion.

Re-install of XP begins, Longer than 2, or even 3, Ubuntu installs later XP is is booted up. Now begins the hunt for the drivers once I have Installed my USB network adapter.

Hours later all drivers are in and everything is working. All that is missing is the software that gives the PC purpose. IE Nero burning rom, DVD movie player, Office suite of some description. This is due to the bundled software having been lost in the midst of time.

[COLOR="Red"]INTERMISSION: Run the windows update service for hours.[/COLOR]

So Anti virus first. Avista in this case. Followed by Firefox with Adblock and NoScript as addons. Divx Codecs, java, and Adobe player, Open office follow. That's about it for the Windows install.

Now the fun part.

Reboot with my HH 8.04 disk in place and setup a dual boot. The drive is 300GB I partition a 50GB area for Ubuntu and away we go. This is done and dusted in less than 30 minutes. Everything works right out the box of course.

Apply restricted hardware drivers for the GPU, Bluetooth mouse works OOTB the multimedia keyboards plethora of extra buttons all work OOTB.

[COLOR="Red"]INTERMISSION: Attempt ethernet connection to router to facilitate updates and additions. After much trying and using other machines it is determined that the wireless router has a problem with the LAN cable ports.[/COLOR]

I get round this by grabbing my X over cable and hook up to my backup Semi retired XP machine. I connect instantly via it's wireless connection and DL Ndiswrapper first. With this I set up the wireless USB dongle on the PC and it's all go.

Medibuntu archives are raided for DVD functionality, tested and found working. DVD's, AVI, WMA and MP3 are all ago. 

Other installs are Amsn, a selection of 'choice' games added to the standard. Amarok, DVD Ripper, Remastersys, Compiz :) Emerald themes, Deluge, a choice of alternative background images. Oh and the Adblock, Noscript addons to FF.

Well hopefully I have covered all the basics based on his needs and a bit of sparkle thrown in as a bonus. Everything works a treat and after all that I went back in with my gparted disk and shrank the windows partition to 80GB and gave the rest over as an NTFS partition that both OS's can use.

Demo's and explanations and demonstrations ensued when my mate came to pick it up and I can say he's happy that the loss of the windows software has been mitigated by the glory that is Ubuntu. 

The thing to top this off is when he arrived he had brought his wireless USB dongle for me to install. So windows first which by some happenstance demonstrated how god awful it can be by buggering up the install :D .

Ubuntu next.......uninstall the driver I had used for my dongle........ Put his in the slot, and before I even put it's driver disk in, Ubuntu had found it and fired it up fully working \\:D/ \\:D/ \\:D/ 

Fingers crossed I have all bases covered and he can settle into using it before being faced with the 'complexity's' of CLI and synaptic. First impressions seem very positive.

The PC has the following specs

Compaq pressario SR1629UK

3.06Ghz CPU
2 Sata HDD 1 @ 300GB 1 @ 250GB
1 DVD RW
512MB ram
Nvidia Geforce 6200A GPU

regards

Fenris
