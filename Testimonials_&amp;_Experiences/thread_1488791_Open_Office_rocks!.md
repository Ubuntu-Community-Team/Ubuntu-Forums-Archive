### Post by aa4bb on 2010-05-20
My biggest fear in switching over to Ubuntu from WinXP was that I would have compatibility problems between MS Office and Open Office in getting my work done. For 6 months, I did all my "playing" with Ubuntu but returned to my XP computer for work.

Finally, I bit the bullet and decided to use OOo to prepare a major grant proposal last month. It involved working with several other colleagues on Macs and Win machines, all other than me using MS Word. I made sure I had installed all the TrueType fonts I needed. 

There were some early glitches regarding use of .docx vs .doc for transferring files back and forth. Once those were ironed out, everything went smoothly from there on. I was pleasantly surprised to find that working with tables and figures seemed easier and more intuitive to me in OOo than in MS Word. The project was completed on time and looked great.

I now have the confidence to finish transferring the last of my work - spreadsheets - to Ubuntu and break loose of Windows forever! Oh, sure, I'll keep a version caged up in Virtual Box for when I get sentimental, but I'll no longer be having the antivirus software taking over my machine and bringing it to a grinding halt several times a day when I'm in the midst of getting something crucial done.

Thanks OOo, and thanks to the Ubuntu team for making all the rest possible.

### Post by lancest on 2010-05-24
> **SyAu said:**
> 
Please let me know how i could create a file in Ubuntu Oo(.doc or .odt) and make it compatible, so that it opens properly in ms office. Since i don't want to provide my resume in improper format to job agencies and ruin my opportunity.


[URL="http://news.cnet.com/8301-13880_3-9864262-68.html"]Make sure Word,
OpenOffice.org Writer play
well together[/URL]

This part is critical: 
double-click OpenOffice.org Writer in the left pane, choose Compatibility, and check these options:
[LIST=1]
[*]Use printer metrics for document formatting
[*]Add spacing between paragraphs and tables (in current document)
[*]Add paragraph and table spacing at tops of pages (in current document)
[*]Do not add leading (extra space) between lines of text
[*]Add paragraph and table spacing at bottom of table cells
[*]Consider wrapping style when positioning objects
[*]Expand word space on lines with manual line breaks in justified
[/LIST]paragraphs

Most of the time this will make you pretty compatible. 
For backup run Windows dual boot or Virtualbox. (as mentioned-for insurance)
99% of the time Windows not needed for me. 
PDF is also great option for resume. (as mentioned)
Any place that can't take PDF format is amateur in my book. 
(to bad PDF is a security risk lately in Windows - an unsafe platform)
