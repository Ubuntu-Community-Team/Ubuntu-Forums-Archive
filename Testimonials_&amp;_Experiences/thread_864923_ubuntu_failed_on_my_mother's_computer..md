### Post by bmac on 2008-07-21
Being a member of the >40 but <60 group, both my wife and I have been converts for a while. Of course my wife resisted initially, as it isn't Windows and doesn't look the same. However, after a few months I noticed her M$ machine was collecting dust, I couldn't help but ask (not always the smartest thing to do) and was informed:

1. I'm tired of waiting for you to clean up my machine - Virus's, spyware, defrag., etc.
2. The Ubuntu machine is faster.
3. I can browse the net without negative feedback from you regarding response #1
4. All my friends at work are using it.

Shortly after asking, I was questioned as to when "I planned on converting her machine". Oh, and by the way my friend has a new puter and I was wondering - "Yes Dear". At my age it's just simpler to say "Yes Dear". Now shes seems happy and since installing an OS isn't usually a life changing experience, we've moved on to the more critical aspects of life. Like which shoes go with this outfit or does this make me look heavy. I'm certain that most married men in my age bracket already know the answer to both those questions....:lolflag:
