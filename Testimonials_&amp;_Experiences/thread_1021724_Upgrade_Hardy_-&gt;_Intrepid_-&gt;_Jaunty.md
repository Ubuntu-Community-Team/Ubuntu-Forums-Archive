### Post by L815 on 2008-12-25
I had a Hardy cd laying around. Picked it up, installed it and was pleased there was no video flicker. Sweet, I'll stick with Hardy for now then. Hours later I found out about the sound issue that I had forgotten about. The one where only 1 app can use sound at a time.
I upgraded to Intrepid knowing the issue didn't exist there.
-> Upgrade flawless.
Having used Intrepid, I kept getting annoyed with the video flicker, and the solution wasn't much better either (causing flashes before video play, with or without compiz)
Oh, I thought, let me upgrade to Jaunty and just play with the Alpha for now and just ignore the video flickering for now.
-> Upgrade flawless.

Contrary to what this post sounds like, this isn't about the issues I've come across, or issues that annoy me, but to put emphasis on the great job the developers have done with the upgrade process. Not just between stable releases, but to an alpha version as well.

All I have to say is, hats off (no pun fedorians ;p) to the Ubuntu Devs, because they are doing a great job!
