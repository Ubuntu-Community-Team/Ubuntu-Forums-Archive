### Post by getmeoutofhere on 2012-07-16
I've been running 12.04 for just over 24 hours, so it's early days.  However here are my first impressions.

Obviously I knew that 12.04 would come with Unity, so my first job was to remove this, and replace it with Gnome.  After a bit of googling, I found out how to do this.  It was very straightforward.  However it would be nice if 12.04 shipped without Unity, to save people the bother of having to remove it.  Or at least had an option on installation to remove it.  I have absolutely no idea what the point of Unity is, except to clutter up your desktop and slow you down.

Anyway, once Unity was gone, I was very, very impressed.  So far, 12.04 strikes me as being the best release of Ubuntu.  Wireless works brilliantly, and my USB sound card was instantly recognised, and now I can run a microphone and speakers through it.  Finally!

12.04 seems clean and fast.  Also, it's amazing how my laptop, which refused to take Windows properly, even after a clean install, took to Ubuntu like a duck to water.

So overall, I'd give 12.04 very high marks - though it is early days, and of course things could go wrong later.
