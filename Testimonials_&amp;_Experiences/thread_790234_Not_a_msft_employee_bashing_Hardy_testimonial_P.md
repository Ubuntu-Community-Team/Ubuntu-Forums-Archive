### Post by srossnz on 2008-05-11
I tried Linux desktops 2yrs ago, they drove me nuts -way over my head and after spending days trying to configure 1 little thing I gave up.  I then continued to use winxp, and eventually moved to Vista.  I found Vista to be a living nightmare, I literally got so p'd off at msft over this OS I vowed to never use a msft product again.  So here I am now running the latest Ubuntu.  I am thoroughly impressed so far.  If someone like me can get a linux desktop up and running, tweaked to look purdy and find replacements for all my windows apps.. then I suppose the linux desktop has practically arrived.  And backing up my entire system is a breeze and free with 'simple backup'.  I also discovered a dozen or so great apps and tons of support for any issues I have in these forums.  I have and still have 1 or 2 glitches but why complain when I have a great setup full of apps for free which is for the most part rock solid!  I think firefox beta on Ubuntu is buggy and glitchy.  while using firefox my system has spontaneously logged off 2 times over the last few weeks, very odd.  I also get a lot of firefox crashes but i think this is because I have not installed flash programs properly.. for example I'll go to a video streaming site and the page will freeze up so I'll need help there.  Internet worked automagically, as well as video, printing.. screenlet widgets are cool and I love having multiple desktops that i can mousewheel between (using gnome and like it, the file browser is also super).  So..  good job Ubuntu teams!  I think things will only get better and look forward to the next release.
