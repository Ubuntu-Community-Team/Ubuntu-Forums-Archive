### Post by DustyRider on 2009-07-27
There is definitely a learning curve involved with Linux, that is because of all the P&P we were used to and GUI under windows. I found Ubuntu a seamless transition

I have been running Ubuntu since the summer of '07 and I have not looked back. Oh, don't get me wrong as a neophyte in this operating system I had my share of frustrations, but they were nothing compared to the frustration and BS operating a Windows machine.

Here's a tip. If you find a distro that likes your machine and is operating correctly (as most of the time it is not the software but your particular hardware or hardware config that causes problems), then leave it be. Why does everyone want the newest and latest gadgets?

I had a bitch of a time switching over from 7.04 to 8.1 to get some settings working. It was worth it. The longer you hide under P&P and GUI, the longer it will take to transition.

I have found the community helpful and most issues can be resolved if you are but a little computer literate. For those who aren't well there is always Vista, and the latter is what motivates me in learning the ropes.

I haven't had a serious crash or infection for 2 years now, I guess that motivates me as well. Allot of folks are jumping on the Win7 band wagon. I don't like their marketing.
