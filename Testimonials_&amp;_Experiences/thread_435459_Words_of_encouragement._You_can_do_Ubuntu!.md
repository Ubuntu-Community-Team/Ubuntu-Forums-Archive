### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-07
Sound not working. Video not working. Internet not working. No codecs for playing DVDs. No decent programs installed.

All the times I've had to install Windows, I haven't had the drivers readily available for all my devices and didn't even know what drivers to look for.

The ideal situation for Windows (apart from it just being preinstalled, which it is most of the time) is to have all your CDs and activation keys in one place--you never lose them, and you know exactly which CDs go with which computers' devices. I haven't always been in that situation.

One time I installed Windows 2000 on a computer my dad had built, and I had no idea what his peripherals were or where to get drivers for them (he'd built it a long time ago). I ended up having to disassemble the computer to find out what the video card brand was. And Googling for drivers gives you a lot of false positives and sketchy sites (Am I downloading a virus or a driver?).

The other major time I installed Windows (XP) was after my laptop had been infested with spyware. I hadn't kept track of the driver CDs Dell had originally sent me, so all I had was the XP installer CD. After installing XP and all of Service Pack 2, my screen resolution was off, and I had no sound. I also couldn't play DVDs since I'd lost the InterVideo WinDVD CD as well.

Same laptop with Feisty--everything works out of the box: sound, screen resolution, resume from suspend, multimedia keys.

As I said before, though, that's just been my experience. I understand there are some Windows users who've never had problems with Windows. But I've used Microsoft operating systems since MS-DOS (3.1, 95, 98, ME, 2000, XP, etc.) for over two decades and Ubuntu for almost two years... and I still find Ubuntu easier to install, configure, and use.
