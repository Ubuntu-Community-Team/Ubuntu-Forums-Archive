### Post by ryanparrish on 2008-06-15
I have been using Hardy Heron, for a while now. I am going to say I have no experience at all with computers. I got tired of Vista that came on my Toshiba A135 I had wanted to toss it out of the 4 story floor at my university, till I stumbled on linux.  I will do a quick SWOT on Ubuntu from my prospective. 

Strengths 

easy to use/install stable 
highly compatible with multiple devices and hardware
fairly fast 
highly customizable 
many software packages 
Banshee is great 
Great support nice people easy manuals

Weakness 

Firefox 3.0 is a load of rubbish it crashes  a lot 
Gnome power manager is not that great, I have tried to get it to work but not exactly how I wanted. Sometimes when it is on battery it will be at full brightness despite specifying it to be at like 10 percent brightness. 

Opportunities

I think Ubuntu could really take over the desktop market from Windows as more of the lose ends get tied up this is a great OS better then some of the linux distros of yester year as I am told 

Threats 

Some of the quirks and bugs could drive users away looking for some more stable piece of software. 

On the whole I think Ubuntu has been great better then vista nothing can be perfect love the software thanks a lot to everyone that makes it great

### Post by powerpleb on 2008-06-15
I don't think anyone would deny that Ubuntu needs to evolve if it is to threaten the MS hegemony. Fortunately with open source, the more people who use it and contribute in whatever little way they can, the better it gets. Unlike Windows, which is created and fixed behind closed doors with very minimal input from its users and if there is a problem you just have to cross your fingers and hope that error report you sent will be addressed in an update someday.

It's still not final release so if you want something more stable you can get FF2.14 like this in a terminal:

### Post by powerpleb on 2008-06-16
> **issih said:**
> Ubuntu's biggest problem is people believing that it should just work with all their hardware, because windows does.


Very true, although the raft of former Vista users who have recently downgraded to XP will have experienced this.

Personally I find that the Windows install is a lot more annoying than the Ubuntu one. I have to insert several CDs to install drivers for everything from the video card to the motherboard chipset and I have to restart my computer several times. I bought a laptop with vista pre-installed and I still had to restart the computer 3 or 4 times before it had sorted itself out. When I installed Ubuntu on my desktop I only had to restart once after the initial install, to enable the nVidia drivers.
I do acknowledge however that Ubuntu has the potential to be a bigger pain in the ***, especially with wireless drivers. I basically had compile them from source. Most windows users probably think source is something you put on your chips.

As far as Firefox goes, the only problem I've has is this bug: [https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/firefox-3.0/+bug/206295](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/firefox-3.0/+bug/206295)
