### Post by Opsidian on 2006-08-25
Non-profit organizations usually tend to have the same problem, lack of money to do things. I currently volunteer at a aviation museum and wear many hat's there. 
Recently we received a donation of 10 computers for a youth program that was getting setup. After finding out the requirements the machines had to produce we decided to go with Edubuntu. The board approved of it of course, 0 cost verses around $2200 to set it up the MS way. 
I've been dabbling in different distros for years now but never found anything I really liked, to much conditioning of doing it the Windows way. I got Ubuntu awhile back and have been loving it. Soon it will be the only thing I boot up to. 
I've been sharing my views on Ubuntu to almost everyone and I've got at least one person to switch over and foresee many more making the change as well.
A big thank you to Ubuntu and to the users of this forum that contains every answer that I have wondered about.
