### Post by numerodix on 2005-10-20
I know Breezy was released ~last week, I checked back then and there were no upgrade docs. That was puzzling, I decided to wait. Today I found the upgrade documentation, it was short and concise. I did the upgrade, _everything_ worked :shock: :shock: :shock: The only anomaly was ant not playing nice with the upgrade, so I uninstalled it, resumed the upgrade, then re-installed it. Took about 20 seconds of effort. Otherwise, the fonts, the applications, the configuration, hell even the gnome menu is complete with icons for every application!!!!!

The single most problem free upgrade I've ever had. Upgrades in linux are nice in my experience, but there's always SOME problem. Today on my gentoo server, postfix couldn't find mysql after upgrading the latter, I reinstalled postfix. Big deal, took 30 minutes, happens maybe once a month on average. But the Ubuntu upgrade, I'm speechless. I just wish RedHat et al would catch up.

Meanwhile, the last 3 months I've been recommending Ubuntu to every linux newbie, saying that it has the best out-of-the-box compatibility I've ever seen and I was never quite sure how painful the upgrade cycle would be. Call me relieved.
