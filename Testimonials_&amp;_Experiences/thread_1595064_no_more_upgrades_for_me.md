### Post by efflandt on 2010-10-13
Sometimes you never know what is going to work or not until you try it.  Starting with 10.04 I installed new distributions to a USB hard drive first to test if there were any snags and how to resolve them before messing with my existing version.

And it was a good thing I did because my early Athlon64 3200+ from 2004 with Asus mobo did not like partitions aligned on MB boundaries (the default for Lucid), so I had to use partman/alignment=cylinder kernel boot parameter.  And its old ATI X1300 did not like the new KMS which made 2D video slow and interfered with suspend/hibernate (video would not turn off, so it would not complete).  But nomodeset or radeon.modeset=0 resolved those issues.  64-bit 10.10 does not have the 2D video lag, but nomodeset significantly speeds up glxgears by at a factor of 10+.

But when I got someone a new laptop, I acquired a low end Compaq laptop from maybe 2005 with Sempron 3300+ (basically an Athlon crippled to 32-bit with less cache) and it worked awful with 9.10.  It would drop keypresses (making login or especially sudo difficult), the mousepad was laggy and jumpy, and it refused to come out of suspend/hibernate with no clue in pm-suspend.log.  But 10.04 just using nomodeset for its old ATI video works great.  No dropped keys, minimal mousepad lag, and suspend/hibernate worked.

My new desktop with i5 650 3.2 GHz, 8 GB RAM, nvidia GT 220 has had no issues at all with 64-bit 10.04 or working up from 10.10 beta to release.  There was a segfault issue with flashplugin-installer for flash 10.1 r85 that I did not even notice except from /var/log/messages in 10.04 or crash report in 10.10 beta.  But real 64-bit flash 10.2 r161 resolved that.
