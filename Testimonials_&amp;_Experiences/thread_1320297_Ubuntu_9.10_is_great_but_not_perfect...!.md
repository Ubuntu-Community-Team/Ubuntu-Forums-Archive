### Post by kiomkids on 2009-11-09
I have been using Ubuntu for just only a month, and I like the system so much expect one thing...!

Ubuntu 9.10 is really great and user-friendly, I can do everything I want with a computer on it. However, the only thing that's getting on my nerve is the Sound & Keyboard problems!!! It's a very BIG BIG problem for me.

Everything works out great before I update the system. Once I installed the recommended updates, the system starts to going down. My sound would turn down by itself to mute while I am typing on the keyboard, sometimes the sound would even interfere with my typing process! I have googled this problem for the past few days, it seems a lot of people are getting similar problems with Ubuntu 9.10. And this sound problem has been existing since 8.10...I was really surprised how Ubuntu didn't seriously try to improve the sound problem when publishing a newer version of Ubuntu.

In fact, I was really disappointed about the interependent sound & keyboard problems. 

Ubuntu 9.10 could the the perfect Linux system...but without solving the sound conflict, it will just make people more frustrated, especially to a newbie like me!

### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2009-11-10
Sounds like the two things are related. Your volume decreases gradually as you type? Could be that Ubuntu is (for some reason) seeing a press on the "lower volume" key event when it shouldn't be. It has nothing to do with sound, though; that's a keyboard problem. People may be able to help you better if we attribute it to the right place.

On that topic, there is absolutely no reason for this thread to be in testimonials (and I don't see how that will help you). Those other sections in the forum are here to help you!

Okay, my attempt at a solution.
Check out System -> Preferences -> Keyboard Shortcuts. At the top, there is a Sound section; Volume mute and Volume down are the two actions we are interested in. To the right it should say which key / combination triggers the action. Try clicking that and hit Backspace to erase it.

See if your problem is solved.

If not, check out System -> Preferences -> Keyboard. Maybe you need to select a different keyboard model.
