### Post by Telekinesis on 2009-02-19
Alright, sthrnpagan, hear me out for a minute. This is going to be honest and heartfelt advice. First, take a look at my registration date. Second, take a look at my post count. Judging from that, you might think I don't visit the forum often or that I'm not very active in the community. That couldn't be further from the truth.

The truth is, some problems are harder to resolve than others and often the users viewing your threads/posts might not have an answer for you. Over many years, I've come to understand that Linux has and continues to be an OS of research. Utilization is key. These days, and I know it sounds cliche, but Google is your best friend. Chances are, someone else has had your problem at one time or another. Use quotations on specific keywords for your issue on search engines. I've followed this path all the way from the old RedHat 5 and it still works today. Research is a must.

Most of the time, I'll ask for help with a problem and then resolve it on my own by seeking out other forums/posts around the internet while I'm waiting for an answer. If you just wait and never get a direct response, you'll become even more frustrated than you were before you asked your question. The Ubuntu Community has been especially excellent, but even they can't solve every problem 24/7. Linux can sometimes be an operating system of trial and errors. Every now and then, you have to take a risk. Seek out what seems to be the best opportunity, input those terminal commands or edit those files and pray for the best! I've been there.

If you're worried to death about breaking the system (been there, too) then research the best way to restore it. You'll start to notice that you're doing more tweaking and fixing of your own than seeking help. That feeling of accomplishment in itself is worth all the frustration. At least, it has been for me. That's when you can begin to help others instead.

Sorry for the long-winded rant. I just wanted to try to lend a shoulder and give you my two cents, since we've probably all been in that same boat at one point or another. Good luck and never give up! :)
