### Post by Wastedyouthfx0 on 2010-05-14
Several years ago, I took a pretty deep plunge.  I wiped windows from my computer completely and stuck it in a virtualbox on Ubuntu and tried it out for almost two years in a work enviroment.  Forum support was just awful, but reported the bug directly to the people responsible got them fixed... which was very impressive.

There was game killing bugs for me, but I waited for promises of new versions to fix them.  The new versions fixed them, but added new and worse once.  Over time, more things stopped working, more things became unstable... and incident with a printer sent me over the edge.  

I wiped it and installed Windows 7 and, I liked it.  But I missed the wobbly windows.  

I decided to give Ubuntu another chance.  I see you've added a purple screen of death to the installer.  Once I got that out of the way I spotted my first bug... the installer said it was easier and I'd enjoy my Ubuntu experience.  It isn't and I didn't.  

This was the worst install experience I've ever had, and I've been computing since I had a vic-20.  Not being able to install without an hours of work doesn't make me look forward to this experience at all.  

I've got it up and running, and I've noticed its even uglier than before.  I want a minimalist bluebuntu...

But hey, I was able to get my printer working, which is what made me throw Ubuntu away in the first place. 

If it didn't have so much potential, I wouldn't care so much...
