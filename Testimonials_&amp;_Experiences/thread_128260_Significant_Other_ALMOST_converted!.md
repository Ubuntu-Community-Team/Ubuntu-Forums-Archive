### Post by Puptentacle on 2006-02-11
My girlfriend and I represent both ends of the computing spectrum. For her, the computer is a tool; for me it's a hobby. What she wants from a computer is word processing, some websurfing, email, research and browser games. In spite of being online since the mid to late '90s I have had to teach her a lot of computing basics. Best example: she was AMAZED recently when I showed her ALT-F! 

Being around me is making her aware of the problems I have with Microsoft in security and privacy issues, business practices, etc. She's been picking up my various magazines laying around, reading about Microsoft and Linux, open source software, etc. She has been watching and listening to me becoming more and more enamoured with Ubuntu. FINALLY, two nights ago she let me put the live CD in her computer and she has started playing around with it!!! Her quote, "Considering what direction Microsoft is going in on privacy, I'm ready to make a change. Will I actually be able to use Linux?" The answer, thus far, has been YES!

I haven't installed it on her machine as of yet. Instead I'm easing her into it. I installed OOo and Gimp on her machine so she can start to get a feeling for the programs. Those are the two she will most likely be using the most. I'm fairly sure that soon we will be "All Ubuntu" around here. 


Just proof that a little evangelism will convert even the most MicroSet people!
