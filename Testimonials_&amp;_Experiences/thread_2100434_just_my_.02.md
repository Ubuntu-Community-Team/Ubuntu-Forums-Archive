### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-01-01
If it was too easy, newbies would choose it and then set up their partitions with useless sizes, like 100GiB for / and 10 GiB for /home. When users know enough to do their own partitioning, then they will also know enough to use the partitioner.

The trouble is, there are still too many people here who think (and advise others) that you need a separate /home in order to keep your data when you do a clean reinstall. Ubuntu is perfectly capable (since 2009!) of preserving your /home when you do a reinstall or upgrade when it is only one partition.
