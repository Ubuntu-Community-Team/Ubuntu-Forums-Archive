### Post by LukeCarrier on 2007-10-22
Hello everybody,
I've just formatted my Windows Vista partition and installed Ubuntu 7.10 Gutsy Gibbon. And I like it. A lot. Here's my story.

I've used Windows all my life, and found ways of exploiting it, had hundreds of viruses and seen the blue screen of death many a time. But, like most people, I spent hours troubleshooting problems and got the system working again. I saw Linux as a nice toy, but never really found it a serious operating system, probably because any attempt I made to use it resulted in broken graphics or no wireless internet capability.

For example, when I installed Feisty Fawn, I couldn't use my wireless card. I played around with drivers I knew to work on Windows with NDIS Wrapper, but never got anywhere.
I tried Gnome, the graphics were dodgy.

But in Gutsy, it's a whole new story. I booted up from the CD to see a nice desktop, with loads of effects. I installed it and loved the way it only took ten minutes. I didn't need to fiddle with my graphics card drivers to make the resolution 1280x1024. I was extremely surprised when I connected my wireless card to see the system recognize it instantly and that the wireless configuration was more logical than Vista's. My list of praise for this masterpiece in engineering could go on and on.

These are just a few of the reasons I want to thanks to the amazing contributors to this operating system. Not just the amazingly talented developers, but to every single person involved in the Ubuntu project. I'm sure I speak for many, many people when I say:

**THANKS FOR MAKING UBUNTU PERFECT!**

--Luke
