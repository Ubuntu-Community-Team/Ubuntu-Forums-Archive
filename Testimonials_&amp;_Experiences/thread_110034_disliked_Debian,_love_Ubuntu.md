### Post by unixxstar on 2005-12-29
I had two reasons that I disliked Debian, both of which I feel Ubuntu has solved for me.
#1 The package management system always seemed over-complicated and limiting when you wanted a newer package. I got addicted to apt-get, until I couldn't get what I wanted [ though I used the testing release ] :( 
**To give Debian credit**, I was lazy and found the whole package thing just too complex without in-depth study which I wasn't willing to do.

#2 I never liked having to choose between "stable" and "testing" 

#3 I always had a lot of things to fix, post install. I will say after I fixed everything, Debian ran quite nicely for me, etc.

Ok, that's three things! So far I've been using Ubuntu for a month and like it very much. So far I haven't had to make any packages from source. All of my peripherals except the mouse wheel ran perfect from the install. Mouse wheel - big deal. 1 minute fix. Very nice distro with a super-clean Gnome desktop that runs pretty smooth even with only 256M of RAM. 

Great job, Ububtu developers! I am recommending Ubuntu to all my Linux friends.
Here's a very biased take on the distros I've used personally [ and they all are really quite nice except for .....]

Gentoo  - love the idea, takes too long to set up and maintain. Great package management, not sure the small speed-up time is really worth the effort, at least on my machine. Very clean system, though.

RedHat - what are they smokin' over there?! Used to be a die-hard fan till Fedora came along. To my knowledge [which could be wrong] they STILL do not have complete dependency checking for their package sytem. YUM sucks! And who wants KDE and GNOME to look and act the same? Madness !

SUSE - very nice ! 'Specially since they open-sourced it. But both KDE and GNOME run V E R R Y slowly ... couldn't fix it. Perhaps just too many services running amok.... YAST at least works now.

SLACKWARE - my second choice, after UBUNTU. I still have it and run it on a second hard drive. You're a little bit on your own, but once things are set up, it stays that way. Runs pretty fast, for me. No package management to speak of...
GNOME not officially supported, but you can get dropline GNOME with updating and it works fine.

Actually, I always wanted a Debian system, except for the afore-mentioned reasons. I've been using Linux for a long time now [since 1998], and I have become more willing to write my own scripts, and less willing to deal with distribution quirks [ I won't call them failures since 95% of the "quirks" are fixable if you are willing to spend the time]. 

Sorry such a long post! UBUNTU is working just great for me.
KW
