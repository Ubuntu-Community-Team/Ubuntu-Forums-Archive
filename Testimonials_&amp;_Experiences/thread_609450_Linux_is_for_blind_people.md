### Post by Exershio on 2007-11-11
Well, after messing with Ubuntu for the past week, I've decided to go back to Windows. It was so frustrating to also find that it ran slow as hell on my computer. Just clicking in the URL box in Firefox took 24% CPU. Loading a web page took 100%. Opening any program took 100%. Yet the system idled at 0-5%. That's pretty rediculous for a P4 2000mhz processor in my opinion. I rarely hit 100% CPU doing everyday things in XP.

So, goodbye for now Linux. I'll give you another shot in the future. =)

I am sorry to read this, hope you will come back quite soon.
Now to the subject, what is "nice" depends on personal preferences and is highly subjective. Everyone is free to choose what suits them better. That said, my preference clearely leans toward Linux. I actually find the KDE and Gnome interfaces to be better realized with more attention to detail. You can modify quite a lot of them and adapt them to your taste.

You have many alternatives as to graphical interfaces, e.g. KDE, Gnome, XFCE, Enlightenment, etc, something you are not free to choose in the Windows world.

You also have a lot more of control over your system and the internal workings of the operating system is much more transparent.

The proprietary ATI drivers did not provide 3D acceleration for my card.

It might be far fetched, but have you checked the temperature of the CPU on your computer? P4 CPUs have protection mechanisms that scale down the frequency (and hence performance) quite a lot in order to protect themselves from damage. This can happen in case of a faukty heatsink or fan.

Other than that, your slow environment experience in Ubuntu could have been caused by a number of causes, maybe misconfiguration.

Nevertheless, I would agree with a previous poster. If your graphics driver did not provide 3D acceleration and you were using software rendering to run 3D effects, such as provided by beryl / compiz, the CPU will be doing most of the work. That is a huge performance penalty.
