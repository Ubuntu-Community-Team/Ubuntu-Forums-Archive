### Post by theonlyvlad on 2006-06-26
When I first installed Ubuntu I was, 99% of the way linux illiterate. 99% because one other time, about 3 years ago I tried installing RedHat on my system and de-installed it after I couldn't get anything to work. So here I was, installing Ubuntu because my friend suggested it, saying this is “linux for humans.”

My perspective is that of a windows user. I've been using Windows ever since I can remember (I'm a sophomore in college right now), and I am very used to the way it does things. That said, I enjoy using Gnome, and I enjoy this operating system. Sticking Ubuntu on my T43 Thinkpad, a lot of things worked. My NTFS drives mounted automatically, GUI loaded with no problems, my wireless worked out of the box, and even my volume buttons actually changed the volume. I was impressed.

And I am also so greatful for these forums. This is where I came for to get help on all those things that didn't work. ATI drivers, my Logitech mouse, XGL, and Samba, from the top of my head. And these forums greatly helped, and now all of those things work, except Samba which I'm still learning how to configure.

So, in my excitement I wanted to share this OS with my friends. The big response I got is, “I would try Linux, but it just seems like such a project” I wanted to protest, but couldn't, because I think that is very true.

I've been generally lucky with my windows install. I know a lot of people have problems with Ati drivers, I just installed and typed aticonfig –initial and it worked. A lot of other things worked too. But it still took massive, massive amounts of time to google every problem I had up, and try to solve it. A lot of people don't see why they have to invest all this time, to get to a point where they are already, only with a different OS.

Because here's the thing..... for me windows XP never crashed, it never bugged out. Ubuntu? Crashed many times. Most of the problems, true, came from OpenGL, things like games and XGL. But I do these things on XP, and XP doesn't die. So, let's leave absolutism behind for one second, and just let me say “in my personal experience, XP proved to be much more stable than Ubuntu”

I'm using Ubuntu now. I'm happy with it, I'm going to keep using it, but largely because I want to. From a usability point of view, Ubuntu is only better than XP because my wireless works better in Ubuntu (go figure). Everything else, I would say, is on par (well the stuff that works). Of course there's the virus/spyware issue that I don't have to worry about as much which I enjoy too.

So why do I keep XP on my hd? Well games. You knew that was coming. Right now BF1942 won't run in Cedega. And Savage is bugging out. What besides games? Well, to be honest, only two programs: ACDSee and Photoshop. I have not found any app on linux to match the power and speed of ACDSee, and Photoshop CS2, and neither of them work in CrossOver Office, or Wine, to my current knowledge.

Ubuntu is a giant leap, I think, to a usable desktop linux. But it's not there yet. The time spent on google.com looking for solutions is still too long for those people who want to use the OS not for the sake of using an os, but for actual tasks that the OS allows them to do.

### Post by treris on 2006-06-26
Right now I've been using kubuntu for about a year, and I have spent a lot of time looking up problems I have had or stuff I wanted to improve. And sometimes people (friends, relatives etc) ask me why I bother. 
I must admit I'm not really sure.....
I can afford to actually pay for software, support and everything, I don't even really mind, IF I get value for money and windows XP just didn't do that for me anymore.
Considering the amount of time I had to spend working through Windows Update and browsing the internet looking for updates for software I needed to have running just to keep my computer running, it just wasn't worth it. 
Nowadays I run Synaptic (I prefer Synaptic over Adept) once in a while (a week or so) and I'm done updating my system. I don't have to worry (as much) about virusses, hackers and spyware anymore and KDE has some very cool functions that most of my friends and relatives running windows haven't even heard about.
Adding to that the fact that I can do a whole new kubuntu installation including updates, getting multimedia support (thanks to Automatix) and doing most of my customising in an hour, hour and a half, whereas I have to spend a full day (of mostly waiting) to get to same result from a new WinXP installation makes my choice for OS even simpler.
Last but not least, I actually llike being involved with the OS I'm running. I spend some time a week (not a whole lot due to a busy working schedule) browsing through the ubuntuforums and answering a question if I can. It just makes (k)ubuntu feel so much more personal, which I really like.

Now if only I could make some of my relatives understand that, so they don't have to bother me whenever their windows boxes get screwed up somehow and I have to spend the better part of a day to (try to) fix it again. That would really help ;)
