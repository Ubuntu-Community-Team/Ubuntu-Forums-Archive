### Post by tja on 2006-06-01
let me tell you where i come from ...
i'm in the IT business for about 25 years now. as anybody else with this long record i've seen much things appearing, rising to the top and vanishing again.
this is true for various *nix flavours and linux-distris too.

for the last years i exclusively worked with gentoo, because i didnt get the things i needed/wanted with other distributions.
the so called "commercial" distris (f.e. SuSE) always forced you to hard-upgrade but were not current because of the pre-ship development time. updating from one version to the next was a pain mostly so you had to re-install anyway to get a clean system.
the "free" distris (f.e. debian) were even more behind the current developments.
both approaches forced you to shoot from-behind-thru-the-chest-into-the-eye if the maintainers/distributors hadnt built a package.

thats the point where gentoo stepped in.
gentoo has a big repository, would allow various tweaks and optimizations via the build system and will give one a totally customized, small and fast system.
the drawback is compile time and quality at times - both things could not be blamed to gentoo itself as the first ist inherent and the second a problem of manpower and diversity.

i tried ubuntu several times in the past and it was clear to me that for the desktop this would be the future someday. the effort to concentrate on the best apps to just "do the job" and make it easy at the same time was promising.

for me it is here now with dapper: its current, complete, elegant and works flawlessly. its well-thought and all fits.

as you see from my praises, i'm extremly happy with ubuntu/dapper. i have it installed at 4 machines, even my wife (non-IT) installed it on her machine and didnt need much hints.
for the servers etc., which are heavily customized, gentoo will stay for now - but i see no need to invest much more work at the desktops - ubuntu (nearly) has it all.

so thanx
- to the developers to make it all happen
- to the community for being here and helping
- and last but not least the donators and financiers
