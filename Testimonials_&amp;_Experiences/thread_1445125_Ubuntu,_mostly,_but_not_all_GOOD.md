### Post by armandh on 2010-04-02
mostly because it just works on most hardware

Ubuntu runs on most x86 hardware, unlike mac, which mostly runs on mac only hardware. 
or windows which leaves the drivers to the hardware manufacturer with varying results. 

there are the "winmodems", the occasional bits of hardware that require a window only driver. when my daughter got AT&Ts all in one deal she got a windows only XP or better wifi usb dongrel for her second floor computer.

I got her a wifi card that was recognized by both sides of her dual boot computer. some might have tried to pound a square peg in a round hole to make the free usb wifi work. why buy trouble when buying the right hardware just works!

then there is the software side. Some write only for win. some only for mac, some for both, and a rare few for linux too.

my netbook goes to meetings and dual boots XP only for Intuit QB.
all the suggestions of virtual box or other software are just not as practical as dual boot. [IMHO]

when ever I hear of some one getting a new computer I suggest ubuntu for the old one. it is the no pressure way to learn.

I've pretty much converted one die hard windows IT guy by putting Ubuntu or Puppy on his "broken windows" hardware.
