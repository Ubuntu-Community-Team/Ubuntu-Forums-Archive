### Post by freeridr on 2006-08-27
About three or four months ago, my Windows XP became so infested with spyware/adware that I downloaded a bunch of software that was supposed to get rid of it. I soon realized it was just more spyware/adware junk that slowed my computer down even more.

I forget exactly how I came upon Ubuntu, but I downloaded it and installed it without even trying it out. I installed it on my 30gig slave drive to test it out, leaving XP on my 60gig master drive. Like many non-computer savvy folk, I had some issues installing, all of which were quickly resolved by searching this excellent forum. I actually enjoyed figuring out the solutions to the problems I encountered during the install. Learning is sweet.

Well, I dual-booted for a couple of months. Every time I booted into my XP desktop from the Ubuntu drive was like going from driving a tricked out BMW M3 to an ugly, gaudy, gas-guzzling pickup truck with a bunch of engine problems towing a fifth-wheel that I never used but couldn't unhitch.

A couple of days ago, I backed up my music from my master drive and installed Ubuntu. I love it. LOVE IT. I'm so psyched I could just crap my pants. Hopefully, that won't happen but if it does, I'm sure I could just download a package that cleans my underwear. AWESOME!

Ubuntu rocks my dome so hard I can't stand it. A sincere thanks to all the folks who put in work on this fine OS. Also, to all the folks who help out with noobs like me by contributing to this forum.
