### Post by drcoracle on 2008-08-31
Ok, after many hours wasted of this holiday weekend, I have come to the conclusion, it just ain't worth it. Period.

1. Having no CD-R's and not wishing to drive 100 miles to get one, I attempted to install from my HD to a empty HD. Yea, I have a lot of extra HD's installed. After trying Netbootin, InstLux, finnaly tried WUBI, which one of the three left me with an unremovable boot selection in my boot menu.
1. Windows XP Pro
2. Ubuntu Installer 6.06 << Huh?

Even FIXMBR won't get rid of it.

2. Finally had to drive into town, got some CD-R's came home and discoverd that the highly recommended InfraBurner, insisted that my new blank CD-R would only hold 588MB. Ok, lets get another one. BurnXP, gee Mr. you got no CD-R's installed according to it.
Bah. So went looking. Tried the 10 pak from CNET, a zip file which has 10 of the most popular burners.. Only ONE worked.
PowerISO and my 29.95 is on the way to those guys.. job WELL DONE!

3. Burn the Live CD. Boot it up. Play around, ...wow..look.. it even saw and used the wireless network. Cool. Bring up Firefox.. 
](*,) system lock. system crash.. not the blue screen of death but a nice black screen of death and reboot.

4. Okay, not one to give up, having installed and blown away windows more times that Bill Gates has money, figured.. it happens. Let's try again.

5. Reboot. Live CD up and here it is again. Linux in the form of Ubuntu. ...okay.. easy now.. lets try email.. Up it comes. Configure...nice..it worked...received messages from my POP account... 3...2....1.... Crash.. lockup...black screen... reboot... 

6. Hmmm.. maybe a memnory problem.. Run memtest.. nope all is well.

7. So, one more time.. You know what.. what the heck.. lets install it on this nice 40GB blank HD that is just sitting there wasting electricity..

8. Up comes the Heron. Use the first selection.. Guided..
Hey you... the partition is too small... WELL HECK.. you (the installer in Guided auto mode, made the thing why did you make it to small? So try to back out.. OOPS... lockup... crash, black screen of death.. and reboot.. Oh YEA!

9. Okay another sip of Diet Dr. Pepper.. and lets try this again. After all we are bored. Wife is at work.. kid is at work, tired of the Porn collection.. so here we go again.

10. Try again. Rezize the partition it wants.. makes partition 5 and 6? what the heck, 5 & 6??? where is 0 thru 4? No where I can see. Oh well, who cares.. let it install.. it seems happy. 
Of course while getting another Diet DP, LOCKUP at 32%. Ahhh!!! a Windows style lockup.. Nothing but a hard power off and on reset will do for this one. Nice! Reminds me of Windows 286 :lolflag:. Oh yea, I can still laugh... 

11. Ok, blow it away. Use FDISK, clear out all the extra partitions, reformat. Make it FAT32 hoping the Coffee can get along with it instead of NTFS. And once again emabark on a trip to a closed Starbucks. See 1 through 11.. and repeat.

12. Well that's enough for me. My poor little plain jane, generic simple 1000 Mhz AMD, 1GB RAM, Radeon 7200 video card, just can't seem to accept the intrepid coffee.

So tell me folks, all of the, "Yes, try it, you will like it, it is so much simpler to install and use than windows folks," want to drive to the middle of nowhere and show me how simple?

Oh, and I am not a noob to these things. My first hard drive cost 1500.00 if tells you anything.

Ubuntu.. bye.. you are a bigger pain in my rear than Vista.
(no i did not try Vista on this machine... but your Ubuntu is suppose to be the perfect OS for my little plain jane AMD puddle jumper. After all, all my wife wants to do on it is play POGO.
