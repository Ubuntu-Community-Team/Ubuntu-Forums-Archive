### Post by StarLab on 2011-04-29
I'll be honest here. For the first hour or so I had 11.04/Unity running, I simply hated it. "How am I ever going to get used to THIS?" was the thought pounding in my head. 

As I continued to explore and tweak, I noticed that somehow I'd suddenly fallen into the 'grove' of using Unity. I just stopped, sat back, and let out a, "Wow! This is really easy to use!" Although miles away from the finely tuned Gnome desktop I spent the last 18 months refining, I quickly found myself preferring this simpler desktop.

Later I found it's actually quicker to launch apps by keyboard than clicking through menus. For instance: Super+gi+enter launches GIMP in less time than it takes to reach for the mouse. Granted, as I add more menu items I may have to expand to three or four letters of the app name, but it's still quicker. 

Only had one extra step to perform after install. The video drivers needed to be manually installed. After that all was up and running.

Also only have to minor glitches. a) My tray notifier for Thunderbird doesn't run, b) there is an (unknown) error while saving when trying to export spreadsheets to XHTML in LibreOffice. I installed the needed JRE for the function, but no joy. Other than that, my dual core workstation is purring along just fine. 

My workstation was a fresh install, but also took the upgrade route for my media machine. All went well for both. I have one more machine here running 10.10 but am leaving that for now as I need that ability to export spreadsheets to XHTML. Once I get that fixed it will see the same upgrade. 

I hope your upgrade/install went as good as mine. (Yes, I've read the threads-o-hate here) Remember: tar is your BEST FRIEND! ;)

Anyway, a big thanks to the community around this project. Been a fan since 9.10. :)
