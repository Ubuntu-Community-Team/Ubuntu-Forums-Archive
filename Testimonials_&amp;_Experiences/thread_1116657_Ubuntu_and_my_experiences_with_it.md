### Post by dmillerw on 2009-04-05
I've been using Ubuntu for about 5 months, and, of course, I've had problems with it, but its to be expected, its not a super popular OS, so there isn't as much support for it as other OS's. However I have stuck with it, why?

Because when I first booted into Ubuntu, I felt home, I know it may sound weird, but it just seemed like the OS for me. I'm the kind of geek who likes to tinker with things and change them around so much that no one else could figure out how my computer works, and I've been able to do that with Ubuntu, something I never could with Windows.

Sure, Flash and some other plug-ins were a pain to get working, but I worked at it because I wanted to make Ubuntu work as well as it could, no matter how long it took.

I've been running my current install for about 2 months, and I still don't have it working the way I want, but I'm sticking with it.

I rarely boot into Windows anymore, unless I'm playing games or recording windows related YouTube videos. I feel that if people keep working on Ubuntu, it could soon become a great OS, with companies that support it, and make commercial software for it, but until then, I'll keep working on  it.
