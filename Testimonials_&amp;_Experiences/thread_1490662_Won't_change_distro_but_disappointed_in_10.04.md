### Post by josephellengar on 2010-05-22
So far, I think that 10.04 is the worst ubuntu release that I have ever used.  I've been on Ubuntu since gibbon.  Gibbon was okay, and only that low because I just switched from Windows and was learning.  Heron was good but not great.  Ibex was incredible and I used it on one of my machines until it just expired.  And the koala was even better.  However, I am disappointed in my experience with lucid so far and likely will not use it on my desktop replacement.

<rant>

There are a number of problems with lucid.  First, I think that the integration with social media is a little over the top and should be optional.  Just as an example, the new notification icon in the gnome panel has a gwibber icon, even though I have completely removed gwibber, that is impossible to remove.  When I did the upgrade I can't count the number of pieces of social networking bloatware that was installed.  I do understand the need for canonical to make money, but installing rhythmbox by default, with the music store?  You know, all of that software was installed by default and was really irritating to get rid of.  The increasing amount of bloatware in Ubuntu was the only thing that I didn't like about intrepid/karmic.

</rant>

In terms of the actual functionality of the system, I am blown away by the speed.  This release definitely reaches the 10 second boot, even on my netbook.  However, the upgrade process was easily the worst I have ever gone through.  When I upgraded my camera, audio, wireless, bluetooth, and microphone all stopped working.  It took me hours to figure out that for some reason, lucid had disabled them in the bios.  Weird!  Easy fix, but very annoying.  Also incredibly annoying- moving the buttons to the left and the incredibly obvious copying of the mac theme/style- ugly as hell.  Also easy fix but annoying.  I hope that AWN doesn't become default son.

Anyway, my two cents.  I probably will be skipping this release, at least on my main computer, and possibly in a few releases changing distros if there is not an easier option to opt out of all of the social networking, ubuntu one, etc that Canonical and Shuttleworth seem to be moving towards.  And if the volume manager isn't rinstated as its own applet so I can get rid of the gwibber icon.  I can't stand that thing.
