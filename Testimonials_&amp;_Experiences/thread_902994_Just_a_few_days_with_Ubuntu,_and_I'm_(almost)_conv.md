### Post by DanJC on 2008-08-27
Hey everybody.  I'm new to Ubuntu, and fairly new to Linux.  I thought it'd be fun to post my story too. :)

I first became interested in Ubuntu about a half a month ago.  I first tried to put it on a USB flash drive, but the tutorial I found didn't work.  I moved on to something else.  

Flash forward to just a few days ago.  I'd been using Fedora 9 on a key up until now, when I remembered I had Ubuntu on a Live CD.  I hadn't really given Ubuntu a good look, and I wondered if it was any better that Fedora.  Needless to say, I was very impressed.  I can't really point at a specific detail that impressed me, the OS as a whole just did.  I decided to dump Fedora 9 from my flash drive and try the tutorial again (now that I had more experience with the terminal), a risky move, since Fedora 9 was pretty finicky, and would only reinstall to my flash drive 25% of the time.  After the tutorial, I had a working Ubuntu with persistant memory.

Hours later, I began having problems.  A settings daemon stopped working, certain programs crashed upon running them, and the system as a whole began to collapse.  It eventually forgot GNOME and the desktop, and ceased to function.

I was confused.  Ubuntu is probably the most popular Linux distro.  If the system was as unstable as I had experienced, why were this many people using it.  That's when it occurred to me that the problem was not the OS, it was that I was trying to jam Ubuntu onto a USB key.  An article on Linux.com focusing on USB key distros confirmed my fears; Ubuntu on a flash drive would not work well, and would eventually break.  I then tried to put Fedora 9 back on the USB key, but it wouldn't work.

So I was rather disappointed.  There I was without any form of Linux distro, with only Vista on the computer, which I really do not like using.  I couldn't just dump Vista; though I would have loved to do that, my grandmother (who I live with) was and is convinced that Windows is the greatest thing ever because more people use it than anything else.  She used it for Boggle and Solitaire, and I use it for a bit of gaming.  So I was stuck.  That is, until I stumbled upon Wubi.

An install with Wubi and some updates later, I now have an amazing Ubuntu desktop.  Vista sits almost unused, save for my grandmother to play Boggle.  I have all the functionality of a hard disk install, and I didn't have to kill Vista.

I've also decided that, should I ever get a laptop (or my own computer), the first thing I'll do is dump Windows and install Ubuntu. :)
