### Post by k2t0f12d on 2008-02-07
> **azimuth said:**
> And thank you for posting your positive experience. It's always nice to hear from someone who isn't just throwing an insult over their shoulder on the way out the door.

I have overseen just as many problematic Windows installations as I have seen complaints by folks here about installing GNU/Linux.  Usually, if you get a full version of Windows on disc instead of some "recovery" garbage, the Windows installation media is designed by the OEM and slipstreamed with specific driver software (as well as some good ole spyware/adware/bloatware).  If you try to install from a non-OEM disc, getting any of the hardware, or *even* being able to tell what the ID of the unsupported device is, at best, a chore or, at worst, completely impossible.  There have been times that I have booted the Ubuntu LiveCD and probed the hardware configuration to be able to find out what hardware I was supposed to be looking up drivers for.
