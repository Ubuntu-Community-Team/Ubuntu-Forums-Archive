### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2007-10-18
Ok, I had this PC that I didn't have immidiate use for. I had been quite happy with Windows 2000 the last 8 years, yet never made the switch to XP and certainly would not make the switch to Vista.

Also, I always wanted to try a desktop linux, so I decided to use this PC for exactly that. And the distribution would have to be ubuntu x64. 

After waiting for ubuntu 7.10, today was the day.

Knowing Debian, I expected massive problems with my Adaptec 2130SLP and most likely my ATI 1800XT. Or perhaps the "Nocona" Xeon processors as they had been causing some troubles when setting them up under Debian 3.1.

Much to my surprise, the Ubuntu splashscreen sprang to life without any error messages. I was amazed when the raid card and the attached drives were recognized without any problems. Ubuntu even realized the drives were set up as a raid 5 array. Endless hours of trying to teach Debian that trick came to my mind.

Once Ubuntu was installed, it instantly pointed me to the proprietary  driver I would need for my ATI card. Then there was a reboot needed... and my monitor went black, blinking "frequency out of range". After some modifications to the xorg.conf (which I had been pointed to by a thread somewhere in these forums) the problem vanished and my need for at least some kind of problem during installation was satisfied.

While Ubuntu so far proves frustrating from a techie point of view (nothing to fix... :) ) it will certainly serve the typical end user, even though my monitor problem shows it's not entirely there yet.

During the next weeks, I will gradually move one application at a time to the Ubuntu machine, intending to do a complete switch by the end of the year.

Even though there will certainly be problems on the way (thinking of games and typical windows software...) I am confident that, perhaps with the help of the community, those won't make me go back to windows.

Good job, Ubuntu dev's. And cheers to us, the Ubuntu community.
