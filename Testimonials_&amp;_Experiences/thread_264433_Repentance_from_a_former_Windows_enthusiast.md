### Post by alcamus on 2006-09-24
After nearly 3 months working/playing with Ubuntu and various other flavors of Linux (including Linux from scratch, so I'd learn a thing or two), I've come to the conclusion that my initial infatuation is indeed love. The clincher occured yesterday evening when, after upgrading my system's CPU, motherboard and video card, I fired up the computer, logged into Ubuntu and everything just worked -- I mean it just freaking worked. My XP partition gave me the BSOD and required a complete reinstall (including searching several hours for an obscure driver upgrade), but I'm writing this testimonial on the very same install I used before I went from a raggedy 1.2 Celeron processor to a shiny new 64-bit AMD processor. How in the world does that happen? I'm still blown away by it.

It confounds me how many Windows XP critiques of Ubuntu begin by negating the latter's strengths -- when folks begin their arguments by saying, "Let's not talk about security, stability or cost. Now let me tell you why Windows is superior to Linux." And then there are those who have been using Windows from 3.1 days who toy an afternoon with Ubuntu and decide it is inferior to what they are using. My challenge to them is invest a month in Ubuntu, learn as much as you possibly can and then decide.

I keep a very small Windows partition on my machine (in spite of getting rid of it at least twice), for one reason -- but whenever I go over there these days, I'm always itching to get back to Ubuntu. I've customized the desktop to look the way I want it to look, not the way dictated to me by someone else. I edit config files to suit the way I work. I've never had a problem that wasn't solved either in this forum after a brief search or in xchat after talking with a very friendly and equally enthusiastic Ubuntu user. Along the way I've learned a lot -- not only about Linux, but also about how my computer functions. For that I'm more than grateful.

I have no grievances against those who want to continue paying the Microsoft toll and are willing to be subject to all its shortcomings -- it is certainly a decent enough OS -- but it's no longer the OS for me (even after being dazzled by Vista's brilliant eye candy). Like an old saw says, you can put makeup on a pig, but it's still going to oink in the morning.

I'll stick with the OS that celebrates humanity, not corporate greed.
