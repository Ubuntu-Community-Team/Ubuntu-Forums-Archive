### Post by madscientist159 on 2009-04-14
> **wolfen69 said:**

To some users, the following comparisons might hold true...

KDE2: VHS cassettes
KDE3: DVD video
KDE4: bluray

Now, everyone jumped over to DVD from VHS because it vastly improved the viewing experience (I might add without radically changing how the user watched the movie).

The question is, are the new features and benefits in KDE4 worth the radically different UI and workflow?  In my bluray analogy, for most people they were not.  The same holds for KDE4; there is a group of users that found the perfect desktop *for them* in KDE3.  I imagine they may switch over when (and if) KDE4 can do exactly the same things that KDE3 did, but until then it really is a matter of personal taste.

I don't like to see the KDE2==KDE3 comparison, as KDE2 kinda stunk.

Just my $0.02... ;)
