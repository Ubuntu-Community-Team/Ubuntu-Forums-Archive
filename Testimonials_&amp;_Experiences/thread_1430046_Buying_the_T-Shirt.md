### Post by lump532 on 2010-03-14
Just wanted to let everyone know that I have seen the light!

I have been considering trying Ubuntu as an alternative to [s]Microsux[/s] Microsoft but have lacked a good machine to do it on.  When my friend offered me a deal on a recycled ThinkPad T41 I talked to him about dual booting with Ubuntu (He's much higher on the geek food chain than I!).

So it came loaded with a fresh install of XP and the ISO file for KK.  I burned it to a CD and installed it with no problems.  After tinkering for a few days I finally jumped my last two hurtles, backing up my Blackberry and anxiety over switching from Office to Open Office.  I am surprised how similar it is to Office.  I just need to get used to the menus.

So I am ready!  I am going to keep XP for at least a few more days to make sure there isn't anything I am forgetting.  Then I am going to do a fresh install of KK without XP.

Oh, I almost forgot.  I will be ordering a t-shirt and stickers!  Who knows, I might start trying to convert people.

### Post by derekeverett on 2010-03-14
A friendly piece of advice... from someone who has learned the hardway.. and bought a couple t-shirts himself...

Don't get rid of Windows entirely.. you will probably need it sometime...

And be careful trying to "convert" people...

Unless your willing to help them through what could potentially be hours and hours of problems getting there apps & hardware working...

Well I'll just leave it at that. It's a good way to make people mad at you.

I once installed Ubuntu on a friends laptop because her XP wasn't working anymore and she didn't have a recovery disk. I spent hours configuring it to her liking. In the end she bought Windows again because I couldn't get her webcam working with messenger. Hours of my time and she wasn't even that grateful in the end.
