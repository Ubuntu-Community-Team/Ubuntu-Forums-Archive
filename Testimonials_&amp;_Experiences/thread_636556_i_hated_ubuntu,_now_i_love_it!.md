### Post by soulmatic09 on 2007-12-09
long story short, this is what drove me nuts:

installation headaches with dual booting 
customizing themes is VERY cumbersome
too many new programs to keep track of (aren't GNOME, KDE, & nautilus basically the same thing?)
the tv tuner driver automatically mutes ANY station that has a slight bit of static in audio (technically not an ubuntu issue, but annoying as hell)
multimedia support doesn't work all that well.
mounted partitions disappear for no reason, forcing reboots


why i like it:

after fiddling with it, i realized that some of (actually **all**) of dual boot partition issues were due to windows, not linux. 

customizing themes is a pain, but you can do ANYTHING with them... it's total control once you understand it.

the software that comes with it (i.e. GIMP, openoffice) isn't bloated like the windows products. i actually like those two better than CS2 and office now

much more stable, and have had fewer crashes. no downloading patch after patch after patch after patch....

oh, and the windows genuine advantage has a bug in it, and won't let me log on! what a POS. i had to reinstall xp 3 times today to get it to work.


i'm converted! linux is the future....

### Post by tiachopvutru on 2007-12-10
If you want to set a wallpaper, you can drag a picture while holding the left button of the mouse to the desktop, press and hold Alt, release the left button of your mouse, then click on "Set As Wallpaper." Alternative, install a package called *nautilus-wallpaper* if you prefer the Windows way of doing it. It adds a "Set As Wallpaper.." when you right click on an image. Some other useful nautilus extensions are *nautilus-open-terminal*, which allows you to open terminal within a certain directory by simply right clicking and choose "Open In Terminal." There's also *nautilus-image-converter* that allows you to resize image(s). Simply right click an image to see the option.
