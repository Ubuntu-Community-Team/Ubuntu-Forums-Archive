My family & I find Unity totally unacceptable (for any of our 5  systems) and will simply continue using 10.04 LTS. No reason what so  ever to upgrade, as you state if it ain't broke why fix it.

Nothing mandates we immediately upgrade and since 10.04 is supported  until 2013 (I believe) - what's the rush? We've decided to start using  XFCE on a couple of our systems and no longer worry about Unity. This  way when the support for 10.04 runs out we should be comfortable with  XFCE or there may be other options available at that time.

Simply investigate  the options and pick the one that works best for you. We test (spare  HDD) every new release prior to upgrading our systems and should one  fail or be found unacceptable we just don't install it on the primaries.  No harm - no foul. We've found that patience really is a virtue when it  comes to upgrading a newly released version of this free OS.

Just my $0.02
