### Post by PatrickMay16 on 2005-09-28
This is my story of how I came to be an Ubuntu user.
Since March this year I've had an interest in Linux. It all started while I was reading a discussion on forum about Sonic the Hedgehog which I visited at that time. People were discussing Linux, speaking of their experience with Linux and other things. Eventually, through this, I came to be looking around the (then) mandrake linux website. After awhile, on the 7th of April, I had Mandrake installed on my computer (after a lot of effort and odd problems during the installation).
It was an interesting experience. Sadly, my computer that I had then died ( on the morning of June 7) and recently I got this new computer. 

About a week ago I got a cheap second hard disk so I'd be able to install a second operating system without getting rid of windows (I still had some interest in Linux after my experience with Mandrake). Then I installed Ubuntu on this hard disk.

All my hardware was detected and set up properly without problems. I was able to find applications to do the things I do the most on the computer (text to speech, listening to and making midi files, playing music, browsing websites, speaking over instant messaging...) and so on.

For something which cost nothing at all, Ubuntu is absolutely great. Though I've still got to keep Windows for some things, like gaming and so on, I don't mind that. I like Linux, and I like Windows. I came to linux for adventure, not to escape from anything. 

Ubuntu is a great Linux distribution.
