### Post by waspbr on 2008-11-27
my advice to you is to try to dual boot. As an engineer Í can relate to the CAD issue. I have been dual booting with vista and XP for over a year now. I use ubuntu 95% of the time. There are some great free engineering tools in the linux world that may be worth your while (google CAE linux for example, but that may be too advanced for you now, but it's something to look forward to).

look for youtube videos on "dual boot ubuntu", you can either make a ubuntu partition or use wubi (with a slight compromise in performance but still good).

Also there are plenty of people in this forum willing to help. 

if this is your working computer, remember to back up your essential data, you may want to keep/burn your vista recovery disks just on the unlikely event something should go wrong. like  you should do when fiddling with any operating system.

Remember to try things at your own pace, don't force yourself to learn everything in one go, if you haven't figure out how to do something in ubuntu, then boot into windows and do it, eventually your are going to discover more and more uses for your new operating system. 

enjoy the ride
