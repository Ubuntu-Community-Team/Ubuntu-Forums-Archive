### Post by mistypotato on 2007-12-07
I have a Linux Ubuntu Machine I set up last year and it serves as a web browsing machine for my Girlfriend.

It seems many people hear about Linux and decide to "test drive" it and quickly become disillusioned when they encounter problems.

My experience with Ubuntu (as a fairly experienced computer tech) was a bit frustrating at first, but that turned into joy after getting a basic install working.

Early on I learned how to use Mondo and created a set or Recovery disks and that saved me from losing faith because when I crashed the system, instead of starting ALL over, I just reloaded from my trusty Mondo disks and at least I could move forward.

As I made successful upgrades to my install, I created updated re-install disks.  Trust me, these have saved me MANY times.

I have to say that I like the look and feel of Ubuntu better than XP and that for 75% of my computing needs, the Linus machine is superb and rock steady.

Maybe Ubuntu needs one of those "FixYa" type areas where people have an especially hard time can pay a small fee to have a dedicated helper to fix their problem.....but then I may have said the "bad" word when I said fee....If so....sorry.  I certainly would not have hesitated to pay $10.00 to get past a really difficult point.

Anyway, Ubuntu is great.   If your experience is bad early on, try again later.  It see,s to be very stable and reliable.
