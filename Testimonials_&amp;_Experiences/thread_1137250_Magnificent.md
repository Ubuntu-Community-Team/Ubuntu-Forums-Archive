### Post by Einsamkeit on 2009-04-25
I've been thinking about switching to Linux for quite awhile, but when Microsoft announced that they were slowly ceasing support for XP, it pushed me over the edge of *open-sourcedom*.

I was very nervous of installing a new OS as I didn't have any simple means to go back to XP (120g *AspireOne* with XP, didn't come with a CD), but after a few tries with *UNR LiveUSB* I made the leap of faith and installed it.

Everything I need worked out of the box, I was kind of worried about the wireless not working in the previous version I had tried (8.04 on Wubi), but apparently it has been cleared out.

The only real "uh-oh" moment I had was when I partitioned the HDD, but *UbuntuForums *was a source of valuable information and I got past that step in no time.

It's even better than Windows for the kind of work I'm doing, absolute and instantaneous satisfaction! If I had to rate it, easily an 11 on a scale of 10.

M.
April 25th 2009
