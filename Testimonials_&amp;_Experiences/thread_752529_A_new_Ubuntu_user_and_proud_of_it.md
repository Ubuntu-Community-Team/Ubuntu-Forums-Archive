### Post by agriffin74 on 2008-04-11
Well, I have done it. After months of being annoyed with the slow boot and login times with Windows XP. Also, the awful long times that it takes to be able to get to the apps that I want to use after logging in, just pushed me over the edge.

I installed Ubuntu 7.10 on my Toshiba laptop and wow, what difference it made. Goodbye to daily updates of the anti-virus program and spy-ware detector. Bye, Bye to your firewall is not installed on your network when I had it turned on for over 3 years. Hello Ubunto, 25 minutes is all the time that it took to do the install. Okay, it did take an 30-45 minutes to download 200+ updates after the install. But, I should not have to do that again for awhile.

The most critical problem I had was getting wireless working. But, that has been solved also. I just need to find replacements for some applications. Mostly a good news reader that can handle binary downloads. I have been trying a few, pan, knode, thunderbird, knode. Pan and Thunderbird like the winners so far.
