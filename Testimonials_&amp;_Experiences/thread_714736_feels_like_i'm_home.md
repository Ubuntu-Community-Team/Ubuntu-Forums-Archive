### Post by mcsimon on 2008-03-04
I am 19 years old and a student at university. 

my first GNU/Linux experience came several years ago when mum and dad upgraded the household pc and so i was left with a spare machine to play with. I decided i'd like to set up a web server, and since it was going to be mainly a project for fun, i decided i'd give linux a go. After searching what some of the main  distros were i settledon debian. Sarge 31R2 i believe it was. I had the best time configuring that thing and setting everything up until after a month or so that machine came to it's demise due to a power surge :(

and so linux was out of my life for a few years. 

Then i decided to buy a laptop to take to uni with me. I was looking forward to vista and thought it would be a great experience...... I was wrong.

Vista was unexplainably slow, ugly, filled with bloatware and the tv tuner i had also bought wasnt even compatible with the vista version of media center. I thought there must be a better way.... and i remembered how well debian had worked on my old play machine and thought it was wortha  shot.

I decided to have a look round for what distro to use and whether my laptop would give much trouble. i came across these forums and the size and quality of this forum is what made me choose ubuntu as the distro to use. I installed the then relatively new feisty in a dual boot setup and sorted out the several problems i came across with the excellent tutorials and help that is on these forums.

After everything was working, i fell in love again. 

I'd already been using open source apps like firefiox, openoffice and the GIMP on our family xp machine for years so it really did feel like home when these were all installed by default along with many other great open source apps either installed already or extremely simple to download from the repositories. Everything was snappy, boot ups took about half the time and there was no crappy norton360 trial asking me to install it.

open source apps have been readily available for everything i have needed to do over the last 9 months and i haven't needed to boot into my vista partition once. The only thing stopping me from deleting vista altogether is that I couldnt bare to throw something so expensive away - no matter how overpriced it is.

Ubuntu has given my laptop a new heart and i would like to thank the developers and sponsors for making such a wonderful (AND FREE) operating system available. 

Thanks,
Simon
