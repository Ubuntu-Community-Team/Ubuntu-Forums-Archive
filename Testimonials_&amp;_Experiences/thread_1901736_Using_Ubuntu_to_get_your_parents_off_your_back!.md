### Post by cobolt01 on 2011-12-29
Being the 'computer guy' in my area, I'm often emotionally blackmailed into providing tech support for my family. This usually involves fixing the problems they have caused on Windows and explaining how not to cause them again. Obviously this is futile because they will continue to destroy the frail and impossible to successfully maintain 'commercial' OS till I of course have to re-install. :'(

Love it: 
[IMG]http://blogs.walkerart.org/newmedia/files/2008/07/too-many-toolbars1.jpg[/IMG]

I decided to force my mom to use Ubuntu 7.10. With only minor complaints about OpenOffice.org (which really does NOT have good enough .doc and .docx compatibility) she used 7.10 for 3 years. My lack of support actually left the install too old to update eventually xD

In 2011 my mom bought a MacBook but it had a fatal hardware flaw and broke in the first week. xD

Back to Ubuntu, she's been using 11.04 with gnome 2 (she's quite used to that) and although she insists there are problems, she can never remember what they are. (Desperate attempt to get me to come visit).

Ubuntu is quite idiot proof and of course easy to use. If you're worried just don't give your relative or elderly friend the root password.

### Post by Trapper on 2012-01-02
Yeah, a while back I finally got my Lady to convert over from XP to U1004, rather than go up to Windows 7 and she just loves it. It's easy for HER to use, SHE doesn't get slowed down with anti-virus, file management is so much easier for HER,  it's a breeze for HER to back up. For ME ... well I'm her almost eliminated guru thanks to Ubuntu.

Also, there was a time where everyone and their brother would call me regarding problems on their Win box. That's just about non-existent anymore. The last Windows OS I worked with was XP. Almost everyone I know now has Vista or 7. When they'd call with their problems I'd simply get to tell them I have no experience with Vista or 7 so I can't help them. Those that were calling regarding XP were told that I haven't used XP in many years and have just about forgotten everything about it. Haven't had calls in a couple of years.

Life is good, thanks to linux!
