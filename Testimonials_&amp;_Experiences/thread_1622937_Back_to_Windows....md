### Post by bigseb on 2010-11-21
Just a little update (and backstory):

Both my wife and I used Ubuntu, although I dual-booted with Windows. I do CAD for a living and some of you may know about the hassles of CAD on Linux. Then there's the gaming... Beyond that I use my PC for the usual stuff ie music, movies, browsing, etc. My wife uses her PC for office work (reports, budgets, mail, etc) and also entertainment.

Ubuntu ran perfectly on my machine but since I use my PC for work mainly I was using Ubuntu just for entertainment... small stuff really. In the end I decided that since I can do that on Windows anyway why bother dual-booting just for that.

My wife's PC is a different story. Nothing ever worked properly. Sometimes (half the time actually) it wouldn't even boot up. Other times it would freeze/crash!?!? Yes, you read right!! Then all the little issues, it was just way too much. I have lost count of the number of times she was typing out a report and halfway it'd crash and all her work would be gone. a right mess.

Earlier today I tried, as a last resort, Ubuntu 10.10 on her PC. What do you know.... it works beautifully!! Straight 'out of the box'!! No tweaking, nothing. She is very very pleased. It really has breathed new life into her PC. Unbelievable!!

I will probably still play around with Linux a bit. It has its ups. But Windows as main OS will still be the best solution for me. Also, to answer a question asked above regarding using a virtual OS: no unfortunately CAD systems don't work well in that enviroment.

Now you'll have to excuse me, I have to watch Top Gear.
