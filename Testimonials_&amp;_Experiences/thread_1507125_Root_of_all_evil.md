### Post by Istrebitel on 2010-06-11
Greetings.

I am a newly migrated user. So far i've tried Kubuntu and Ubuntu, sticking with later. 

Recenty i was able to solve almost all of my questions and now i can work on my computer and play on it and relax watching films, with no problems, and i am happy (my wife even noticed i "was very unhappy" two days before and now i'm "better", heh).

But i spent like two weeks of studying the matter, of digging, googling, and searching, and asking, and debating and stuff... I thought Ubuntu lacks newbie guides, quick start guides, getting started guides, documentation, guideline or something..

I love to analyze, everywhere, be that a game, a social situation, or an accident. And i think i found what is the root of all evil in migrating to Ubuntu Linux.

**They only say UBUNTU!**

90% of solutions you find dont work or are outdated because the VERSION is wrong. Ubuntu is there, but solution is for another version, and it no longer works like that! 

You see, when you have windows problem, you google (never heard of free customer support, ever... dunno whats the customer model ppl are talkin about, here in russia even if you buy legal windows its your problem if it's bugging, go google...). When you find a solution, 99% of time VERSION will be specified! Like "Windows XP" or "Windows Vista". It sticks! Its easilly distinguishable. And ofc solution for Windows (Vista) wont work for Windows (95)! 

Would you ever ask "hey i got a problem with my windows"? Never, you would mention "Windows XP" or "windows 7".

But when learning Ubuntu, that is not the case. You see, first of all, google and search engines have problems with googling numbers. They might find nothing or find those numbers somewhere close, even in reverse orders. Second, numbers are not so distinctive! Really, Windows XP differs from Windows Vista better than Ubuntu 9.04 from Ubuntu 10.04. Third, confusing long name of the system (Ubuntu 10.04 LTS Lucid Lynx) makes it hard to spell it and makes you OMIT! Omit the version. Omit the name. Speak Ubuntu, everybody gets you. They do. Right? WRONG!

By doing this, the effort is wasted. Why is the "solved" model created on the forums? Because it helps finding answers! You search, you see solved and go read - there must be an answer. BAM! There is none! Because they speak of Ubuntu, and you have Ubuntu, but they have DIFFERENT operation system! Why so? Version!!!

Most of frustration, problems with understanding and tweaking and using of Ubuntu came from this **root of all evil**, now i see it! If there would be a simple name advertised everywhere, on the website, in the distro, everywhere, like Ubuntu Lucid, Ubuntu Karmic. If it would be promoted on forums and sites, used in the system (You may continue running Ubuntu Lucid, wecome to Ubuntu Lucid) and branded as this exact Ubuntu, people would use that too. ANd when asking for problems, pepople would write "Hello i have a problem with Ubuntu **Karmic**". Or Ubuntu **Lucid**. This would help later, when you google for it, make this easier to use! You would instantly expect it NOT to work if its different (still might, but not really good hoping for it) and you would search for correct version! 

Right now you cant search for answers for your very system (noone uses the name or the number in posts) and thus you have to dig through outdated, not working and overall wrong suggestions! This undoubtedly makes you frustrated because you are in trouble, you find the solution: Here, here it is, and its working, people confirm it does, but IT DOESNT for you. Now you understand you suck, because everybody else seems cleverer than you, it worked for them, and for you it doesnt... and you get frustrated. You keep looking for a typo or blame a bug, or think "am i too idiot to write 20 letters into a terminal?", when in reality its DIFFERENT OS they speak of.

This occured to me just now, but i think, it is TERRIBLY important. If version name would be PATCHED into people's minds by every mean possible, then overall technical support and problem solving experience would be up TENFOLD! Because searching for guides, clues, solutions would become SO much easier. 

But for this to happen, Ubuntu sould become Ubuntu (version)! In the minds of the people, not on paper. It should be advertised so, it should be spelled inside the system and in man pages so, it should have different wallpaper each time with words "UBUNTU VERSION" on it, to paste it into our heads that this time, its Ubuntu Lucid, not just Ubuntu. And then Ubuntu Maverick. ETc.

### Post by Spr0k3t on 2010-06-13
Perhaps a little more search-fu is required to receive the answers you are after.  Here's a few tips I use to isolate the exact asnwers I need.

1. Instead of using the sites search feature, use a dedicated search engine such as Google.com

2. Most search engines can limit the site to search through by using the indicators in the search fields.  In this case: site:ubuntuforums.org

3. Using keywords will give better results than using full phrases.  A phrase will be filtered of the small words such as "it", "as", and "a".  Sometimes the small words generate a boolean function such as "and", "or", and "not".

4. Modifiers help zone in on the exact information you are looking for.  Use of "+" or "-" before a word or phrase-in-quotes will add to the search or remove it from the search completely.

Examples
**site:ubuntuforums.org ubuntu 10.04**
This searches for ubuntu, 10, or 04 on the site ubuntuforums.org

**site:ubuntuforums.org +ubuntu +"10.04"**
This will return anything with both ubuntu and "10.04" on the site.  You can remove any results containing 9.10 or 9.04 by adding -"9.04" -"9.10".

**site:ubuntuforums.org +ubuntu +"10.04" +solved +grub2**
Find anything regarding grub2, ubuntu, and 10.04 as well as solved.  This could mean the thread may or may not be solved, but it would point you in the right direction.

I have a 2nd degree black belt in search-fu (use of regex in the search feilds).  Learning some of the small tricks like that will help yeild a much better result.
