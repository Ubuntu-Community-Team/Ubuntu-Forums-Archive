### Post by rdd on 2008-06-13
It's one of my 'well done, community' posts. My mum wanted an iPod and she wanted a nice one. So she ordered a brand new iPod classic 80GB in black. 

I was a little concerned whether it would work with her Ubuntu machine. 

It arrived today, she unpacked it, plugged it into her computer. I grabbed gtkpod from the repos, and lo: After a painless little setup procedure I could add music to the iPod without any trouble at all. 

I like Banshee so I tried it with the newest 1.0 version. And wow, even the cover art stuff is done automagically. 

It's one of those days where I just want to yell: Everyone, we're doing a great great job with this.

### Post by The Cosmic Hobo on 2008-06-13
Ubuntu is a far cry from the very first time I used Linux... which was a Mandrake distro from about October 1999. That came with a lot of different desktop environments, very little information on support (I didn't even have home internet access at the time); the installer wasn't unfriendly but it was confusing.

With dipping in and out of Linux over the years, I have to say I'm still very much a newbie. But Ubuntu, especially in its current incarnation, is the most amazing user-friendly distro I've ever seen - and I'm sure that in the other apps out there, there are plenty which are no less amazing.
