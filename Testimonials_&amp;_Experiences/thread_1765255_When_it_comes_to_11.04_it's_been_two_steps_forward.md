Done a lot of upgrades and fixing problems in Ubuntu since 2005, but 11.04 has been a challenge and a disappointment. 

I can't believe all the problems and issues I had with this release. First I had so many issues with getting wireless to work. After that I had programs that wouldn't work, Of course I did some DE changing. Went from Unity to Gnome 3, and I did break some things along the way.

To make a long story shorter, I finial went back to 10.10.

### Post by Flymo on 2011-05-23
Had problems with Live CDs from Alpha through Beta-2; just could not boot into Unity on a variety of hardware (see my sig), but the Natty *release* worked OK live on both Acer laptops (Celeron 540/intel graphics, T5600/nVidia), so I've had some time to play, even followed the tutorial pages on 'omgubuntu'. But...

No I'm not keen on Unity.  It seems a retrograde step from Ubuntu Netbook, imho.  The 'metaphor' is confused, although I approve of vertical panel desktops  for todays widescreens - even built my own in Gnome for 8.04. Took me days to get it done. No, it was not that good!

But I'm happy for Ubuntu to experiment with *intermediate* releases - we stick to 10.04 LTS for daily duties on dozens of family and friends' machines. So, no problems with bleeding-edge desktop metaphors there.
 
Experimentation is a good strategy for Ubuntu in particular and Linux in general, in my view.  I'll be watching with interest.

Maybe I'll catch on to the Unity metaphor, maybe Unity will improve (you can bet that the devs are working on that right now, bless 'em), or maybe I'll continue to enjoy *boxen desktops more than the many others that I  use cheerfully, like Gnome 2, XFCE, LXDE, Enlightenment and KDE 3. 

So, apart from not loving Unity, my live experiments have found no holes in the items tested - games, music CDs, surfing, torrents, **un**encrypted DVDs, the office stuff, GIMP and some other stuff I forget.

Natty Live seemingly just works - for me, but am not motivated to install it yet - but that might change.

Your mileage may vary, of course.
