### Post by rhh7 on 2011-12-19
I have dreamed of using Linux for years.

Today I took the plunge. 

 I downloaded Ubuntu 11.10, burned it onto a bootable cd.

Then I edited my bios to boot from the cd-rom first.

I rebooted my computer, with the cd in the drive.

After a couple of minutes, Ubuntu came up!

Within 5 minutes I had connected to my WiFi network, and was on the internet!

I am so excited!!

Now I am planning to build a new desktop, running Ubuntu exclusively.

I am presently running an AMD Sempron 2800+ system at 1.6 GHZ, circa 2005.

### Post by stalkingwolf on 2011-12-20
my first install of ubuntu was 6.06.  In the intervening years i have loved it , hated it, cussed it and praised it. But still use a variant of it as my primary OS.

Most of the issues i have had over the years have ended up being hardware related.  Ubuntu has helped me to identify situations that were critical and could have resulted in major damage or data loss.  Other times hardware that just didnt play well.

I dont like unity. primarily because it wont run on any of my desktops. Again thats hardware, i just dont need to upgrade hardware to run an OS.  Thats windows mentality.

I use SuperOs at present, simply because things are already there i dont have to add them. That may change.

In short ( hahahahahahaha) use what works for you.Have fun with your computer and doing what you want to do, not what the system tells you you can do.

One of my biggest bitches with windows is that it is always preparing to do something instead of just doing it.  " windows is preparing to copy", "windows is preparing to shut down" oh wait we have to do updates first. you cant do anything else until we tell you its ok.
