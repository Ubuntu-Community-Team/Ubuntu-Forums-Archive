### Post by Käptn K on 2008-04-27
At first, no I am not a Linux or Ubuntu hater, I am a big fan and long time user of Ubuntu (started with Edgy). 

But after half a year of Gutsy (which runs perfectly on my rig) I could not resist to make a clean install of Hardy. No problem so far, I got a back up of everything just in case the s**t hits the fan. 

I was hoping that the bugs which first appeared in the RC were fixed, but I was asking for too much. After suspend-to-ram (S3) and/or suspend-to-disk (S4) sound and network are broken, and there is no way to get them back to life but to restart the machine. 

This was the first time a Linux (and especially an Ubuntu) made problems with a fundamental function I can not live without. 

Shocked by this experience, I am still thankful for the Ubuntu devs for creating such a great OS, and I am hoping that there are some nice bugfixes around the next weeks so I can enjoy Hardy to its fullest. 

Till then, I go back to Gutsy, which is running like a charm again on my rig. 

Greetings Käptn K :)
