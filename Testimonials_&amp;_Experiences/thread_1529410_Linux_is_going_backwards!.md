### Post by macroshaft on 2010-07-12
Not so long ago I noted with interest that the introduction of netbooks had resulted in linux being offered out of the box on systems here in the UK. This was a great step forward as many people who otherwise would remain ignorant to it's existence had the chance to give it a go and see how they feel.

Today I have decided that my poor old Acer has reached retirement age and thought that it was time to show my support by going out and buying a system with linux pre-installed. Sure, it's bound to come with a distro I don't use and so will be wiped immediately in favour of Ubuntu but at least this way I'd be adding some points to the linux scoreboard. To my surprise not one of the four major PC vendors in my town now offers any linux systems at all! I had to ask and eventually after much head scratching one system with SUSE installed was found, which is out of stock. How is it that after having made a break onto the High Street, linux is now once again banished and not getting any shelf space at all?

If distro's such as Ubuntu are ever to be accepted this one is a must.

### Post by julianb on 2010-07-12
It costs money to make sure the hardware & software work together. 

So for a given model, computer-maker has to spend some money (staff time is money) figuring out what hardware and drivers they will use in order to have everything work. 

The price of the computer reflects that money - all of the work the computer maker does, the customer pays for through purchase price. 

If you sell a million computers, though, you don't have to charge very much (per computer) for the work it took to make sure the hardware & software work together.

Would be neat if manufacturers would just offer us a computer with no OS.
