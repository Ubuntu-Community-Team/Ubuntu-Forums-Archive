### Post by tomski on 2007-09-27
I thought I'd share my experience of using ubuntu & other linux distro's

I have been using ubuntu since the warty release & i have seen it grow to what it is now.
When i first started tinkering (really doing something with my) PC i tried various different types of Linux: Suse, Red Hat/Fedora, Slackware, Gentoo (bit of a mind feild!!) but then as i learned & read quite a few articles on it's history since Unix i decided that to me the true linux OS was debian and i began using knoppix & Kanotix live cd's just to get a feel for things and to safely play about breaking thing's and trying to fix them with the safe knowledge that nothing bbad had happened as i was using a live CD with a static e2fs home partition on a predominantly windows PC.

When i felt comfortable i took to the high diving board and installed pure Debian sarge on a pc i obtained as a test pc i started out quite unskilled and declared my self an ubernoob!!

As i learned more and more & then relearned more & more then forgot some & learned again i decided to dual boot my main PC with suse 8.9 and Windows XP this was a good experience but it did not feel homely to me and just hardly ever used it, i dipped in a couple of times but i always had so many problems with getting things to work and when i compared it to my experience with Debian i felt more comfortable with Debian and found that i still had problems with hardware & drivers & the odd software compile it just seemed to be logical, now what i mean by this is that the placement of files within the system felt easier to find & i also noticed that when i was looking for help on the web a majority of how to's & tutorials were aimed at debian systems. So i decided in my small but infinite wisdom i would drop the suse install & replace with Debian.

Then 2 weeks down the line my friend turned up with a cd that contained warty & yes i laughed when he told me " ere' Dude! av' you heard of warty before?' i said no & he went on to tell me about how good it was & that it was based on debian! (all good distro's seem to have this as a base)

So i thought hey i'll give it a go and wham!! it felt even more comfortable than pure Debian & i loved it & i installed it 2 day's later.

I was so happy how easy things were and i started to look for games and other little bit's and bob's for it & had a great time too & it started to get better as well.
I would sit there infront of my pc waiting for the next update & yes along the way we have all had the occasional nightmare when the simplest of hardware (gfx mainly) would fail after an upgrade but i would keep plugging away trying this and trying that, i even increased my use of lynx (text web browser) whilst in the console mode with no X running trying to find a way to fix my poor pc just so i could see what the latest upgrade would be like for me.

By now i had started to use ubuntu a lot... hell i was even using nerolinux & a few other apps to get my cd's & dvd's burnt off as well as editing sites using just gedit and i began to leave windows but i had to keep it as something i could not figure out where or how todo certain tasks (old habits die hard)

and here i am now in a similar situation eagerly awaiting the release of the gibbon to lollop into my humble dwelling and bless me with it's wonders but by now you are probably wondering what all this is about well when i sit here and think of my linx/ubuntu life and compare that to windows i feel good that i stuck with all the troubled times i've had when it feels like you need a digital shoe horn to get something to work & it turns out that just a string of commands & a few edits to a file makes things work because i think there is a whole shed load of people out there that have become scared to touch linux because it's hard to understand or just happy knowing that rather than getting digital oil on there hands to fix something or to do something they can just throw some money on a counter & have some software do it or a guy like you or me behind a counter fix it for them.

I started with the same thoughts as that guy with the cash but i got curious and up until today i have not been aware that just by USING ubuntu i have learned more about how computers work & how hardware communicates to software & how networks really work.

Windows is good for businesses because you can get things done but for sheer knowledge that feels like it get's put into your brain just through using linux/ubuntu without you knowing it is worth so much more... great things can be done with just time, effort, broadband & a free (not pirated) OS.

I have been on a training course that has not cost me a penny! and it's all because ubuntu makes thing logical (well to me anyway)

You all may have a different view of some of the comments i have made & fair play we all walk a different path but just by reading this there is a very good chance that you too walk like a penguin but with a slightly different waddle but the main thing is that you have learned what you know by using this OS and you continue to learn more by using it & this my freinds is what makes linux because unless you pay for software or training the learning path of windows is slower because you are not forced to think as much as you are with linux

well that's what i think anyway

so thanks canonical & the ubuntu team when i have some spare cash i'll donate some but in the meantime i wish you all every success with ubuntu & i can only hope that now DELL are shipping this great OS with their PC's & lappy's that others may follow to.

Thank you for making debian & linux in general even easier to use
