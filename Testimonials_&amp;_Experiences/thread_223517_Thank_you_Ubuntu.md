### Post by timmahhny on 2006-07-26
First I am a newbie to Linux; I tried Linux about eight years ago with Madrake 7, installed but could not get any drivers, no real help on the internet as there is today.
 I got tired of my HP laptop coming up with Mickey Mouse (Microsoft telling me that my windows os was not valid) and crashing. I once again tried Linux, using Red Hat 9, then Mandriva 2006 and finally someone told me about Ubuntu. 
 I have an old Dell Latitude 600 laptop that I installed Ubuntu on and with one exception the wireless card, it ran fine. With help from this forum and a local linux group, I got the wireless card to work, downloaded a bunch of packages and the more I use Ubuntu the more I am convinced that I am going to switch all but one machine over to it.
 I "fix" computers as a hobby; a neighbor went to a Big Company to get their three year old HP fixed, they were told it was bad, buy another, which they did. They had problems got in touch with me, and I fixed it, they gave me their old HP PC, and I installed Ubuntu on it with no problems. (Nothing wrong with that PC). It connected to the internet, I used the network manager to connect to my home network and to my windows PC and now am looking for drivers for my HP printer, which I will install.
 It has been a joy using Ubuntu and I spread the word every chance I get. 
 When I do my computer help classes I am going to include the idea that people at least try Linux. Ubuntu is almost as easy to use and set up, with a few exceptions and as been said many times, it may-not be for everyone, but you won't know until you try. 
I personally believe that if all people are doing is surfing the internet and using email, Linux is the perfect answer. If they are doing more database and other similar tasks, well maybe Windows is better for ease of use, as I have not gotten into using the database as of yet in Ubuntu. 
 I truly love it and all the help you get here at this forum.
Thanks Ubuntu.
Timmahhny
