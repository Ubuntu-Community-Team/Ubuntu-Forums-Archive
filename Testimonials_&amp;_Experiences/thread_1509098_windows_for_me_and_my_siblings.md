### Post by steve19137 on 2010-06-13
Well, after a year or two with Linux, I've gone back to Windows. I have a few reasons why, and I'd like to share them. 

See, I've recently gotten back into gaming after moving to Linux. It seems that I'm not able to keep interest in more than two hobbies at a time, and my one constant is contributing to the iPhone/iPod Touch jailbreak community. So gaming just left my mind. Recently, I found my copy of Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic. I immediately tried to install it via WINE, and found that it didn't work. Oh, well, I thought, wasn't important to begin with. Still, it kept nagging at me, and I researched it to its death, and I still couldn't get it to work. By this time, I had been seriously considering a WinXP/Lucid dual-boot. I finally did setup the dual-boot, and have happily been playing my Star Wars games since. Until I discovered all the compatibility that I had been missing out on. To make a long story short, I deleted my Lucid partition, and I've been pretty happy. The only thing that i can't get in Windows is the usefulness of a command line, but then again, it's Windows, and command line is needed one in every 6 months. So I'm leaving Windows for the issue of games. But! If developers start making well marketed Linux ports of their games, then I might set up a dual-boot again. But I will still use Ubuntu in a virtual machine, because I'm pretty pumped about the Unity desktop being developed. But thanks for reading. I hope that some day Windows apps in Linux, most likely in Ubuntu in particular, will run better, especially games.
