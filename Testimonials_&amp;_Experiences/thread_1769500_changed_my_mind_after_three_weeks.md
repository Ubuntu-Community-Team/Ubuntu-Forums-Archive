> Can you list some of the things that you discovered?

here it is:

                                 A few examples:
(forgive me the translation from Dutch in some occasions, I am not really good at the Ubuntu jargon yet)


[LIST]
[*]What I really like is the fact that when you click on the icon of a  program you are using for multiple instants, all windows of that program  are shown  in expo, works also with the shortcut super +...  (but across all workspaces, very well thought!), and you can  pick the one you need. Nice when you work with 4 or 5 programs at a time. This is a refining and big improvement of the  Compiz switcher I used to use, where *all* windows were exposed.
[*]Same thing with the workspaces, when you choose super+s, all workspaces  are shown, and you can even drag windows from one workspace to another,  superb!
[*]if you press the super-key once, the dash window appears, I like the shortcut. I think it could use some development on the &#8220;find-file&#8221; side, but it certainly takes away some &#8220;work resistance&#8221;.
[*]In the beginning I missed the usual way of choosing programs, but the funny thing is that after some days I also started to use the &#8220;find program&#8221; utility on my Xubuntu computer, that works more or less the same way, but less polished. Now I find the &#8220;old way&#8221;of choosing programs a bit tiring :)
[*]keeping the super-key down makes the left bar appear, no need to wait for the mouse-pointer to do the job.
[*]If you are in a directory, simply choosing &#8220;add to shortcuts&#8221; (&#8220;voeg toe aan bladwijzers&#8221; in Dutch) is enough to add it to the places-menu.
[*]Another one, not new to Unity, but I just learned on this forum :P is the tabbed folders, when you select a folder and press the middle-button of the mouse.
[/LIST]
 To some of you these features may be obvious, but they were not to me, as a (fresh) ex-windows user :)

  Just a few of the things I discovered the past weeks, no doubt there is a lot more to discover, but these features I took into my routine so far and it feels as a natural thing.
 

 All the best!
 Jacob
