### Post by Tristan Schmelcher on 2009-10-14
Not long ago I decided that my current computer wasn't powerful enough to handle what I was demanding of it. These days I often find myself wanting to display 1080p H.264 content with my computer, and my old 2.0GHz Core 2 Duo laptop just couldn't handle that. I also happen to do coding work at home in a "hardware description language," which is a programming languages for describing hardware, as opposed to software (I use VHDL). These languages are very different from software programming languages, particularly in that compiling them is very CPU-intensive. My current project took two and a half hours to compile on my laptop, even when only one file had been changed.

For my new computer, I chose a System76 Leopard Extreme (a desktop). System76 is a small computer company that only sells Ubuntu computers. I had never bought from System76 before and the Leopard Extreme had only been out for just over a week when I placed my order, so obviously there were no reviews of it yet, but I ordered it anyway. I guess you could classify me as an "early adopter." I've now had my computer for over a month and there are still no reviews of it on the Internet, so I've decided to review it myself.

The short review is "pure ebony awesomeness". The full-length review is at [http://randomtristan.blogspot.com/2009/10/my-review-of-system76-leopard-extreme.html](http://randomtristan.blogspot.com/2009/10/my-review-of-system76-leopard-extreme.html).
