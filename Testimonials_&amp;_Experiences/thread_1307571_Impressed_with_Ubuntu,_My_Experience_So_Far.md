### Post by alexk554 on 2009-10-31
So as a "regular Joe Soap" Windows user, here are my thoughts on Ubuntu.

I have tried Linux a few times before, about 4 years ago or so I was interested in seeing if it really was any good, and I will be honest it was beyond me. If something didn't work, I had to edit configuration files or use the command line, which made my head spin. I eventually gave up using it everyday, but decided to keep an eye on things.

Long story short, I had an Ubuntu 8.10 disc in my drawer. Was bored Thursday night, I noticed WUBI installer, and decided I had nothing to loose. 

After logging in, I didn't have any serious hardware problems (it didn't pick up the FN keys on my notebook), and I was able to do everything I wanted, browse the web, chat to my buddies on IRC/MSN, etc.

Needless to say, I saw that 9.10 was out last night, so I grabbed it, and once again, installed using WUBI.

9.10, wow!. Ubuntu is really starting to look suave. The new pop-up notifications, new login screen. It really does look professional.

I'm going to keep Ubuntu on my notebook, and I'm going to use it unless there is something Windows specific that I need to do. At this point in time **I feel the fast boot time, responsive desktop, ease of use, and stability I experience in Ubuntu surpasses the Windows desktop experience, something I have NEVER experienced after about 4 years of trying out Linux.** Congratulations Ubuntu team, you've taken the "geek's toy operating system" and made it human! You have my thanks.

I had some more things I wanted to say about Open Source in general, but this is perhaps not the time or place. Suffice to say, Ubuntu is making an impact I never thought Open Source would. I cannot wait for 10.4 next year, as I read there are even greater improvements on the way!

Now I just have to find out why my notebook's numeric keypad worked in 8.10, but not in 9.10 :/..............
