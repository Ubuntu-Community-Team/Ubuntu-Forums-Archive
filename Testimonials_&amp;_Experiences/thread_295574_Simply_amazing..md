### Post by destruego on 2006-11-08
Okay, so I'm a Linux newbie pretty much, I've been using it for almost a year now, and I have to say first that this operating system is truly awesome. I remember the first time I used it I absolutely hated it, but it was my first experience with any Linux distro. After I finally made the hop and said goodbye to Windows I found myself in something infinitely better. Not only did Ubuntu recognize every bit of hardware, save my Broadcom wireless card (I'm running an HP zv5000), it set everything up so I didn't have to. No sound or Ethernet or any sort of driver to install to get complete functionality. Ndiswrapper isn't a problem at all, I keep it and the bcmwl5 drivers on my USB key, and the minute I installed it I could do everything I usually do on Windows immediately, no additional setting up or installing. I couldn't believe it was so simple. And it runs smoother in Ubuntu, I have full 3D acceleration from the get-go, and XGL/Beryl work flawlessly.

I wish I had heard about Linux a long time ago, it would have been nice to learn early, but better late than never. And I can say that I'll never go back. Things are so much simpler, and I find everything I've come across as intuitive somehow (kudos to whoever added the scroll wheel/volume taskbar icon functionality!), and it doesn't seem clunky. And the software available, I mean, pardon my French but holy ******. I can do almost anything possible on a computer if I want to without having to look anywhere except in Synaptic (yes, I'm still clinging somewhat to my GUIs ;) And the versatility amazes me too. I can tune *anything* on my system now, and every time I come across something else I can do I kind of just sit back and marvel for a second. You guys and gals on the Ubuntu team, you guys frickin *rock*. I've been using it since Breezy and it just keeps getting better, and I'm liking the changes in Edgy. Heh, now I just wish I knew how to code so I could jump in. Ah well, eventually.

Anyway, sorry about the monologue, I just love this operating system. I've ordered a handful of CDs to bring to work with me (cross your fingers that the IT department gives Ubuntu a try). Oh yeah, and if there's anyone reading this from Montreal, drop me a line, I'd like to learn more about how the guts of Linux work.

So yeah, hi Ubuntu community, I hope at some point I can contribute something useful. :-D Thanks again to the developers and Canonical for making something that is truly awesome.
