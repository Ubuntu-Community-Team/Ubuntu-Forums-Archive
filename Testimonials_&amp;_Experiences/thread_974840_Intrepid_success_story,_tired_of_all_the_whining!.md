### Post by 73ckn797 on 2008-11-07
I upgraded my desktop from 8.04 to 8.10 and just finished a fresh install of 8.10 on my laptop which was running 8.04.

Things went without any problems on both machines. I know there are those I read about who have all kinds of issues. I am not one who could explain why but my experiences with Ubuntu have been great in 90% of the experience. Yes, you will see several posts from me about ATI video problems but those were on a different machine than what I use for myself.

Being an Ubuntu/Linux user since only August, I had several issues but attribute that to the newbie experience and the initial learning curve that goes with using a "new" product.

To the dev people, thanks for all the work you have put into the product. I try to help others from my experience and find the forums to be, for the most part, a source of good help and reference.

I look forward to many years of using Ubuntu.

### Post by jerrylamos on 2008-11-08
The end result of Intrepid Ibex on this IBM Thinkpad R31 is good.  With Compiz removed, i.e. boot rescue mode then sudo apt-get remove compiz commpiz-core the laptop's apparent responsiveness is better than any release starting with Dapper.  The laptop has a new lease on life, not bad at all for a 1 gHz 32 bit Celeron 512 mb, even dual boot Ubuntu on 20 gb.

I first reported Intrepid Alpha 3 failing with a black screen after login on August 19.  Much kudo's to fellow users on the Ubuntu Forums I was able to get Beta to run almost 2 months later with a lot of sweat.

Development finally put out a "fix" which is to disable compiz for pc's with certain intel graphics chips.  I.e. it's not a fix, it just avoids using compiz.  This "fix" didn't make it into the release code so there are many posts of people searching for the problem.

End result, I'm running great, I even have the very early version of Jaunty Jackalope running as I enter this post - with compiz removed, of course.  It's running O.K. which means Jaunty hasn't been tweaked enough.  Alpha 1 is due Nov 20, I'll hold my hat.

Jerry
