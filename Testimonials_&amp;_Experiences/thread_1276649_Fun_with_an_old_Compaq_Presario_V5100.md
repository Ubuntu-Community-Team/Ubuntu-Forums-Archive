### Post by megahurts on 2009-09-27
I've been an Ubuntu user on and off for several years - since 6.10 or thereabouts - but have usually had to stick with XP as my main OS due to the combination of software and games I use/play.  Recently however I inherited a 3yr old Compaq Presario V5100 from my son, who's "upgraded" to a Tosh running Vista.  Its nothing special, a AMD Semperon 2800, 512mb RAM, 80Gb PATA drive and ATI Mobility300 graphics.  XP ran ok, but as is its wont got slower and slower as time went on, usually getting a reinstall every 6-9 months or so.

I figured it was time to get back into Ubuntu again, so being short of blank media did a google for USB stick installs.  Five minutes later Unetbootin was pulling down the latest ISO and about 30 minutes after that the base install was complete.  It took me a few more minutes to get the Broadcom WLAN card working (enabled the restricted drivers) but apart from that everything worked out-of-the-box.  I installed various bits and bobs including Screenlets, Chromium, and FBReader as well as the ubuntu-restricted-extras package and vlc etc for my net-surfing, book reading and movie watching needs.

Getting Gsynaptics to work properly took a little fiddling and a lot of googling for a solution but thats all up and running now too.

Overall Jaunty seems to be running quite a bit quicker than XP ever did, even after a clean install, and I honestly can't see any reason I'm going to need go back to anything related to MS any time in the future.
