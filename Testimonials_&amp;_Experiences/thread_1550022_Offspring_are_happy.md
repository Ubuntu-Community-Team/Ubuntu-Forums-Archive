### Post by pricetech on 2010-08-10
I recently had a problem with the "kid computer" at home and rather than reinstall XP, I decided to install Lucid, for more reasons than one.  I wasn't sure at first how the kids / grandkids would react, but I knew the worse case scenario would be reinstalling XP if they complained enough to upset my Wife.

Anyway, after booting to the live CD and making sure everything worked, I installed, updated, and installed the applications I knew they would use.  I then turned them loose on it.

I needn't have worried.  My oldest child is 35 and my youngest grandchild is 3.  Nobody had a problem adapting to the new interface, and the younger ones were more than happy to help the older ones find what they were looking for.

I still have Windows 7 on my work laptop at home, since I still support Windows at work, but my home computer is happily running Lucid.

### Post by Old_Grey_Wolf on 2010-09-08
I have children and grandchildren that use both Linux and Windows. 

It fascinated me when the 3 to 9 year old grandchildren used whatever OS and applications were installed on a computer. They didn't seem to care or notice that there was a difference. 

However, time has passed, and one of my grandchildren is old enough to start growing armpit hair :). I am starting to see signs that he is becoming accustomed to a particular version of an application that is only available in a specific release of an OS, It doesn't matter that it is the same program on all releases of the OS. The fact that its interface is different from one OS release to another is enough to make him not like it.

It will be interesting to observe this progression in behavior/preference over time.
