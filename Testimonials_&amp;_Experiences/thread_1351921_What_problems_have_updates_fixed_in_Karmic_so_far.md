### Post by michaelzap on 2009-12-11
The kernel updates are definitely an improvement (and the final Lucid kernel is also available now, which is what I'm using). Personally I still cannot suspend or hibernate on my Lenovo Y530, and I have an annoying pulseaudio memory leak and other minor annoyances. But I wasn't all that happy with Jaunty either (I have integrated Intel video on my main laptop), so Karmic is an improvement in my case.

If Jaunty is working well for you, you might be better off waiting for Lucid. On the other hand, if you have the time and inclination to do a test installation and debug any issues that come up, give Karmic a try. You probably do need to install to another hard drive if you want to preserve your Jaunty system untouched, since Grub 2 could make your life overly complicated if you install Karmic to the same drive as Jaunty.

If, for example, you find that your system will not suspend reliably in Karmic and that's essential to you, then I'd say go back to Jaunty without wasting a whole lot of time beating your head against the great Karmic wall. But if you find most everything works well and there are workarounds or fixes for what doesn't, then it's probably worth switching to Karmic if only to be on the leading edge of the Ubuntu wave. Don't jump to Karmic blindly or without being prepared to invest some time and energy into it, however.
