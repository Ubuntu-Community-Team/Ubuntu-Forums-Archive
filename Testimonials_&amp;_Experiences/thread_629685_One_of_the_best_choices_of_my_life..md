### Post by Meiscooldude on 2007-12-02
Let me give you a small background of myself. I am a junior in highschool, planning on going into computer science as a career. I've always been really good with computers, mainly Windows. But since i started to do Computer aided design with iMacs, my love for Windows has dwindled.

I have tried out Solaris 10, which was the OS i was actually wanting to use, because it was made by Sun Microsystems. Personally one of my favorite companies because of JAVA (my favorite launguage.) But i had alot of networking problems on it, It also was not compatible with alot of my hardware. So i had to decide against it. From there i went back to Windows for about 6 months, and during those six months I looked around at various linux distros. I was pretty sure i was going to chose Fedora, because of how clean its site looked (this was before i found ubuntu.) But i chose not to get it because of the 3-4 gig download (i have limited bandwith, and im not patient.) So then i quit looking for a while... 

Then one day, while just surfing the web i found Ubuntu. I noticed that they offered to send you the cd via mail (FOR FREE.) But i setteled with the 700mb download. So i burned the iso to a cd, poped it in, and ran from the live cd. I FELL IN LOVE! It automatically connected to the internet (one of my biggest problems with other OSs.) So I installed it. I found many amazing fetures that alot of commercial software doesn't have today. But the thing that really got me, was the Add and remove app. A compilation of free software, easy download and install, won my heart forever.

Now, as i mentioned before, Java is my favorite launguage. I wouldn't even think of using any OS if it did'nt support Java. Luckily i found that Linux "pwnes" windows at developing applications in Java. Although, the installation of the JDK could have been a little easyer, I still love it.

And the community here is amazing, full of people willing to help you out with every posible problem.

Thanks for the Amazing Operating System.

Ubuntu > Macintosh > Windows
