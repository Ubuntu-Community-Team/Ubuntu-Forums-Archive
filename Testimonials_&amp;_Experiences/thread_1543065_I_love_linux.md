### Post by justinsn95 on 2010-07-31
Well I have been playing around with ubuntu for roughly a week now, and I have decided that I love linux. I had tried to get into linux a few years ago back when Ubuntu 7.1 Gutsy was released. I was still fairly new to computers in general at that time, so I don't think that was the time for me to start. Now that I have a lot more confidence, Ubuntu is working out a lot better for me. I am starting to see all of linux's appeal. And, I love learning. I like the fact that I have to learn something every time I boot Ubuntu. 

There are still a few kinks to work out, but I like working out kinks. I am going to enjoy trying to get a bunch of new modern games to work in Wine. Stuff like Dead Space and Gears of War 2. I don't think linux will be behind when it comes to gaming for long. Rumor has it that steam will soon be on linux. Once that happens, I bet you will see a lot more developers releasing for linux as well as PC and mac. I mean look at starcraft 2. 'Ol Steve Jobs has got mac so popular these days that they made starcraft 2 for mac. Prime example right there. 

Although I will say that I can see myself still using windows for a few more years. I am already very familiar with it, and if there is somethign that I just can't do on linux for whatever the reason, I'll likely be able to do it on windows just cause that's where my experience lies. But the more I learn linux, the more that will dwindle, until one day, I won't need windows for anything. I just wish they could smooth out a few of the quirks with Ubuntu (and linux in general I guess) so that it would be very easy for me to convert others to linux. 

I mean I guess I could use Linux Mint to help convert them, cause you don't have to download the restricted extras and all that. From what I hear it comes with them installed. But if noobs cant just go to youtube and watch, or watch some dvd, or whatever it may be, they are going to have the same initial reaction that I did. "WTF?? This doesn't work!! GRRR!!" That and the themes could be a little slicker. That theme they released ubuntu 10 with is just pathetic. My still living great grandmother could have come up with something better than that made out of wood. Its an eyesore. And I know they can do so much better. Aesthetics are *very* important to people, especially noobs who are trying to come over to linux, and they see this ugly crap that looks like its from the year 2001. When they are used to the glossy polished shine of the windows themes. And you don't even have to use up a whole lot of RAM to make Ubuntu look oh so pretty. I am really at a loss as to why the Ubuntu team did that. Anyway, just my thoughts.
