### Post by winjeel on 2009-12-06
Simply stated: Thanks. :)

I'm ordinarily very careful about what I do, and the choices I make. I want things that work, and don't frustrate me. I'm really busy with work and research, so I don't want to mess around with peripheral things. Which is why Windows ME really destroyed my like for MS operating systems. Though, I did move onto XP.

I first experienced Ubuntu on 9.04 six months ago, and that was good, but I didn't want to move to 9.10, yet. On Friday I just received the Dell Mini v10 new, with Ubuntu 8.04, and was amazed at the difference. That prompted me to consider upgrading my main computer sooner than later. Despite the advice of others saying it's quicker to download and fresh install 9.10, I used the Updates Manager to upgrade, and it was all done easily yesterday inside of 2 hours, and it works flawlessly. Thankfully, that annoying shut down beep is now gone.

So basically, after about 6 months giving Ubuntu a go, I am glad to say that I'm really, really satisfied, and that is shown in me being happy to buy a new Dell mini laptop with it pre-installed. The other great advantage for me in Japan (and I'm an English native speaker), all new laptops come with monolingual (Japanese) Windows... Ubuntu was an easy choice for me. Now I'm looking forward to the next LTS for the Dell Mini. :)

Keep up the great work. :D
