### Post by Envergure on 2009-05-30
One of these days I'm going to use the same OS for a whole year...

I've been dual-booting Ubuntu and XP for a while because I have FL Studio (lags badly in Wine) but I just plain like Ubuntu a lot better than Windows.  The latest drama has been that I ordered Vista restore disks from Acer because FL Studio works a lot better in Vista than XP (WAY lower latency, fewer underruns at higher loads).

The disks Acer sent took a week to arrive because, for some reason, they had to ship all the way from Texas to my home near Toronto.  When they got here, I found not a DVD as had been promised, but five DVDs and one CD.  The DVDs were named thusly:  1 of 2, 2 of 2, 1 of 3, 2 of 3 and 3 of 3.  I put in the CD since that's what the instructions said, and it asked for the "first restore disk," so I tried 1 of 2 and 2 of 2.  The restore worked, and Windows welcomed me back with:  *Bienvenue a Windows Vista édition familiale premium.  Veullez attender que Windows configure votre ordinateur.*  Aparently Acer thinks everyone in Canada speaks French.

Oh, and the guy who signed on the shipment at the Texas end is an illiterate guy named Dickie Sanchez (signed on the order with a "|".  Yes, I'm serious.

On first run, I downloaded AVG Anti-Virus (almost forgot about that!) and deleted crapware while it was downloading.  The crapware amounted to over half the programs in the computer, the rest being drivers and Windows components.

I never did figure out what those other three disks were for.

As if that wasn't enough, my " key is now a dead key.  To type a " I have to hit " and then Space - unless it's followed by a word beginning with a consonant.

In spite of my general displeasure with all versions of Windows since the late mid-90s, I have decided to keep just Windows, and not reinstall Ubuntu (Vista erased it during installation).  I haven't lost any data, and my French is pretty good considering I haven't spoken it since grade 11.
It's too much hassle to be rebooting (or *redémarrer*-ing) every time I want to change tasks.



As a parting thought, I want to thanks the community for their work, which has gotten me through high school with few hiccups and no viruses or crashes.  I'll certainly be back at Ubuntu before long (maybe a PS3 mini-supercomputer is coming?)



EDIT:
Turn out Vista didn't erase Ubuntu, it just hid it really well.  I accidentally dug it up when trying out DoctorFreeware.
The other 3 disks turned out to be an English version of Vista which doesn't work very well.
