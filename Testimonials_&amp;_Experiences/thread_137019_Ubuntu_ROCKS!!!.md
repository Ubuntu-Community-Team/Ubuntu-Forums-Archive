### Post by kwas101 on 2006-02-27
Hi all,
I am a Windows user from waaaaayy back (I'm talking Windows 3.0 here) and have been Win sysadmin/support for almost as long - well just got sick of the whole thing and wanted to try Linux and see what it was like. I had given Debian a try back a few years ago, but I seem to remember quite a few problems getting it to work...so...whilst viewing /. forums I read someone extolling the virtues of "Simply Mepis" and so I thought, well why not? So I ordered the CD, and waited, and waited.
Finally it came one week later! With baited breath I opened the envelope - and out tumbled a "brown" CD case with UBUNTU written all over it! Damn, they had gotten my order wrong! Well I thought, I'll install this *ubuntu* anyway and see what happens...
Talk about painless! Easy to install...none of the horrors that I'd had before.
Well I used Ubuntu for a few days, and even managed to get Opera to work, when (lo and behold) I was about to throw away the envelope I'd gotten delivered when (shock horror) here was the Simply Mepis CD after all. Well! If Ubuntu could be easy to install and use, how much easier could SM be??
Wrong wrong wrong - I don't much care for 800x600 at 60Mhz. And it didn't even boot off the CD the first few times - had to screw around with the options - ugh. I finally got it install and then persisted for approx 45 minutes. With a sigh I reached for the Ubuntu CD and I have not looked back one week later.
Ubuntu is not only easy to use but fun to use. I find that it is intuitive and "just works". The sheer pleasure that I get from just browsing the Synaptic package manager is indescribable - all those lovely programs, which shall I download next?
Although I am a Windows sysadmin as I said I am not by any means an admirer of Bill and his cohorts - in fact I flatly refuse to use IE I have only used Opera for the last 18 months (and T bird for mail). I always recommend Opera to people after they have been burned by IE. Imagine my joy when I can now recommend an *OS* that is also spyware-free?
If it sounds like I am gushing, I am! I am a true convert! Ubuntu ROCKS!!!

Stephen
