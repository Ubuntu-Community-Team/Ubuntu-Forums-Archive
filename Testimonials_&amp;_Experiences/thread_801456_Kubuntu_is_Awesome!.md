### Post by rocky5051 on 2008-05-20
I used to use Ubuntu (with Gnome and all that good stuff), and have since version 5.10. In fact, I made a full switch over to it when Feisty came out. However, Gnome was too simplistic for me, so I switched to Kubuntu (I prefer KDE, not just because it has better visual appeal off the bat, but it's more like Windows in appearence and functionality so anyone that I let use my computer is less frustrated and doesn't have to guess what certain things are for).

Synaptic makes it so convienient to install programs, and the scripting abilities with linux itself are so helpful (like for operations that might take 10 steps, they can all be put into a script and run in one click or a few keystrokes in the terminal).

My computer has 768mb of ram to work with (only counting the physical memory alone, not including swap), and an AMD Athlon 1.4ghz cpu. On XP it took about 10 minutes to start up the computer. On Kubuntu (including Ubuntu) I can power the thing up and the desktop and everything is loaded in under a minute, with my linux substitutes to my favorite Windows apps, and everything was ready to work without delay. Basically I'm saying that it's far more efficiant.

My only real let-downs so far is printer support (and for a friend it was wireless...it took me a week to figure out how to get it going before I finally figured it out for him), but other than that I couldn't be any happier.

I see alot of people bash Ubuntu, but really, they don't seem to understand that Linux can only be so convienient as it's geared toward customizablilty and not simplicity of use. Those people should look into themselves and consider that learning how to use Linux first might be helpful. It took me a full year before I understood how to use the terminal in full, and about a month before I could compile programs successfully.

Anyway, I love Ubuntu and Linux, and I don't plan to switch back any time soon. :D
