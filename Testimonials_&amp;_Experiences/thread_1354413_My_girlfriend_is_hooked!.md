### Post by Bigtime_Scrub on 2009-12-13
My girlfriend's computer was slowed down from viruses and spyware. She wanted to know why it always got that way. I thought to myself, well, for what she does with it, Ubuntu might work so I installed and configured it for her. She has been loving it until she dropped her laptop. Her motherboard was broken. She sent it off for repair and got a loaner with Vista and she has been complaining about Windows to me nonstop for 2 days now. She can't wait to get Ubuntu back so, and I quote "My computer will be fast again." and so she can theme it  the way she likes. 

Now she installed her first distro by herself, Fedora 12, and she is now distro hopping. I never expected this. I didn't think she would ever care about the internal working or even what OS she was using. 

Ubuntu has another convert and I am happy to say she will be on the boards helping others when she feels a little more confident in her abilities.

### Post by Bigtime_Scrub on 2009-12-17

> Debian? Is that easy to install? I have been using Ubuntu for quite a while now and really like it. Is Debian a better distro than Ubuntu? Easier to use and maintain? Where can I download it to install on my PC?

You can get Debian here. Debian is the mother of Ubuntu, without Debian, Ubuntu as it is today would not exist, but then again, many many distros are based off of Debian. I don't like to say a distro is better than an others. I will say Debian appeals to a different kind of user. Debian is more stable than Ubuntu but at the cost of older software. It is as easy as Ubuntu to install.

You can get it here [http://www.debian.org/distrib/ftplist](http://www.debian.org/distrib/ftplist)
