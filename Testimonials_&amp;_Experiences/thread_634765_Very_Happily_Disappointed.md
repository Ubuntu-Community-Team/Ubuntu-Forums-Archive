### Post by apothecaryaaron on 2007-12-08
I finally have gotten enough free time to get back into computers for fun instead of work.  I have always been under the impression that Linux and it's different forms were for hackers and geeks.  This was my reason to install Ubuntu.  I assumed that it would be a command line with shortcut icons back to the prompt.  I was looking forward to customizing from the ground up.  

Needless to say, I was completely wrong about Ubuntu.  I am thoroughly impressed with the completeness and readiness of this OS.  I've had it on 3 different systems so far, each with varying hardware.  Yes, some of the setup required some tinkering, but so has every XP and Vista setup to date.  Ubuntu was so much easier to fix, because all of the files were not only easy to find, but I could change them and configure them to my needs.  Much much easier than trying to find a weird patch from a company because I have 64-bit XP.  

This forum is full of very helpful people, and I wanted to say thanks to the developers of Ubuntu and the helful folks here.
