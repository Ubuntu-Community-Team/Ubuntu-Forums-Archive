### Post by tbay007 on 2008-08-17
I am somewhat new to Ubuntu.  I tried it 3 or 4 years ago when Ubuntu 6.06 was out.  I had a lot of issues with it.  Like it took me 4 hours to figure out how to run World of Warcraft and had problems with running youtube media and all that mess.

Now i have wiped out microsoft windows and replaced it with Ubuntu 8.04.  What made me decide this?  Well, Windows has been slowing down and i was getting sick of all the nonesense that comes with Windows.  

Ubuntu has come a long way since 6.06.  I enjoy the fact that we can read and write to NTFS file systems without too many problems now.  I enjoy partitioning my hard drive a lot and when i did this under windows i had all NTFS partitions.  So before when i installed Ubuntu in 2003 or 2004, NTFS config tool was still in developement and wasn't as reliable.  Now, i seem to have no issues.  I know this isn't ubuntu's fault, but all i am saying is this is a lot better now that that type of support has been added.

World of warcraft took me a half hour to setup.  I was shocked by this.  Normally i have issues galore when it comes to gaming with linux.  But using wine and using this guide [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WorldofWarcraft](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WorldofWarcraft) made my life easier.

I am really happy with Ubuntu now.  The team has come a long way, and i would like to thank everyone who is on it providing this neat software and support!  I hope i stay an Ubuntu member 4 life now!
