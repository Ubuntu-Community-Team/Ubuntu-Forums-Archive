### Post by Cycledoc on 2012-08-16
I'm a 70 plus year old dabbler.  My computer a Dell XPS 15 now, though I had an elderly dell laptop previously.  

About 3 years ago I stumbled into the Ubuntu world, trying the release active at that time, 9. something.  It worked fairly well, far exceeding the capability of my old laptop.  I moved onto a lighter release, and used it for about a year.  I never got it working quite right .  My alternative at home is an IMAC.

About 10 months ago I purchased the  PC laptop, quickly repartitioned and tried the then active Ubuntu release.  It seemed OK but the Unity desktop was awkward to me.  I then went to Linux Mint.  I found the desktop cleaner but there were a number of quirks which slowly eroded my satisfaction.  Using Cinnamon for example, the bottom panel is microscopic (my aging eyes) and not enlargeable.  The wireless failed on a regular basis having to go through a procedure of turning it off (hardware switch) and on again.  And finally everything seemed to slow--a minute and a half to boot for example.

Now I'm on my second installation of Ubuntu Unity (12.4) and am finding it pleasing.  Easy to work with and lightning fast--to me and in comparison with my previous Mint install.  

I realize some of my Mint problems may have been self inflicted and that release does have many strong points.  But the future of LInux for those with limited technical skills is an out of the box release that works well with little adjustment.  Ubuntu for me, on my current computer, seems to meet that goal better....at least for now. 

Thanks to everyone for their hard work on these releases.  They are a kick to play with.
