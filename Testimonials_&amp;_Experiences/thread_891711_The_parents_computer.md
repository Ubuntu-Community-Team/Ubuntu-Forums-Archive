### Post by Kver on 2008-08-16
I've been a long(er) Ubuntu user, ever since the end of college jut over a year ago. My time with it has been ripe, even to the point where I've been developing themes for the art team to give the girl a little more spruce.

My parents had a computer that's roughly a year and a half old - two years oldish, running XP. When they first got it, it was one of those "It would do" computer, for the internet, mostly.

For the first year, it was good, but then it turned into a problem child. Crashes, severe speed issues, inability to browse the web with any vigor, and my stepfather would be forced to wait do red-eye removal at a speed only blazing to a frozen slug.

Every visit, from coming home from college for a weekend, to when I joined the Canadian Forces and would visit between training; a managed to keep it churning forward. Every visit would involve adware, spyware, malware, turning off useless processes which would only be reinstalled when some program updated.

And every visit, I would offer to install Ubuntu, telling them that they wouldn't need to worry about this stuff. Of course, they turned down all offers - understandably, they know I can run a computer through it's paces and they don't want some weird program they wouldn't know how to use.

After moving back to my hometown for the next round of training, my services were called on to fix my Grandmothers computer. Again, Ubuntu was declined, but after cannibalizing an old computer to upgrade her machine and giving windows a complete tune-up, I earned some cache with my mother (who had never seen me work) and earn enough trust for what happened next;

"The internet was cut-off, I'm distributing spyware", my mom said. Her computer, despite my best efforts, had gotten eaten alive by all manner of spyware, malware, adware, etc... a day before service pack 3 was released. The malicious programs had turned off and corrupted all antivirus programs, replaced her wallpaper with ads, disabled menus, and regularly bluescreen'd the poor box.

All I could say at this point, was "It's pooched." Never in my life have I been able to fix that much damage without formatting. My mother was devastated. The options were no better; Buy a new hard-drive, install windows, and scan all the family pictures to copy them over. Keep the old drive as a backup. Money was the issue, and no Windows CD either. Try to fix it again - although I said it would never work nearly as well. It wasn't happening. Get a new computer (she looked at a mac, we check out hard-drives at futureshop, and some lower-end computers aswell); She knew that any windows machine she got, would need to be fairly expensive because Vista requires it - Mac was an option, the small mac minis, but they were still expensive. She would not be getting a new computer anytime soon. Then I once again, told her "it's just pooched", and then it sunk in, and she knew what I was going to offer next.

"Shrink Windows", I started, shrink windows, put Ubuntu on the space left over, and at least use it until you can get a new computer. She keeps windows, so no options are closed, keeps the family photos, and boots into Linux. I also explained Live CDs, so I could show her without installing it. And the way I explained how it all worked, she finally agreed.

I happened to have 2 live CD's on me (after a friend expressed interest), Ubuntu and Kubuntu. We tried Kubuntu first, I was curious since I was running sans K. She thought plasma widgets were neat, but mentioned she liked my laptop better (Gnome). I plugged Ubuntu in, I showed her I could lay it out like Windows, explained that it could import her settings, and she was almost excited. I was happy because Ubuntu running off a CD was still faster than windows - a very good sign. I also accessed her Windows photos, showing she still had them even with this new system.

She headed off to bed, and I got to work on it. Started Ubuntu, and it installed like a dream. I decided not to install through windows, since eventually if her and my stepfather get so comfortable, they could just format windows and keep the extra space.

Then I started the updates, which again, went swimmingly after re-connecting the internet. moving onto the printer (which I had never hooked up before on Linux in my life) was surprisingly easy. We had one helluva time getting the printer to work on Windows when we got it.

I changed the wallpaper to one of the family pets, and organized everything to be more familiar to windows. Surprisingly happy, I went off to bed. I was spending the night so I could have a BBQ, so I was ready to fix anything should it catch fire or grow tentacles.

When I woke up, all I heard was "It's AMAZING!", "I called your grandma, and I put it on facebook, telling everyone your a GENIUS!". She had gone on, hooked up Thunderbird, set up her email, ran firefox, did banking, and apparently in some sort of record time.

I explained the features of it, from the tracker search I enabled, to adding programs. Very happy, I also made her well aware that she won't need to replace it, at least until the computer physically breaks - and at that point I could probably just cannibalize another computer.

From considering spending hundreds (thousands?) of dollars to using Ubuntu, she has nothing but glowing reviews for the system. She just wished she had taken my offer sooner!
