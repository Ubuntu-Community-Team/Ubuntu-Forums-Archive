### Post by Upochapo on 2006-08-07
Ok, I have to share this with everyone.  I helped a coworker fix her home computer that had windows xp.  Her old computer was sitting in the corner collecting dust after several months of not being used.  She said that I could have it cuz she knows that I tinker with computers and she was never gonna use it again. It was in decent shape and could have used it for myself, but she had three kids and I wanted to experiment a little.  I suggested to her that I could fix it up for her kids.

So, I slapped in a little extra ram, and a spare low end 3D card I had lying around.  I knew that if I was gonna set this thing up for wee little ones, windows was definitely out of the question.  So, I downloaded the edubuntu distro to see what that was all about.  Well, I put together a smaller version of edubuntu based on plain ol vanilla ubuntu.

Got the system back to her place and boy did eyes light up and the laughs roll!  It was priceless.  I was afraid that the little ones wouldn't take to the computer.  But gCompris was a smash hit!  Mom absolutely loved it cause it was fun,educational, and FUN.  The little three year old kept laughing hysterically as he rolled the ball around in Neverball.  The kids were fighting for turns.  They were painting in Tux paint too and playing with the stamper.  All kinds of activities they tried.  And they didn't even get to half of what gCompris has. 3 kids, 1 computer maybe not such a hot idea.  But, I'm sure that they will work out computer time.  My coworker had company at the time I dropped off the computer and she was amazed and said, "Oh, I have to get so and so here to look at this and play".

So, to all you mums and dadders out there, that second computer makes a great kids computer with edubuntu!  The edubuntu distro has TONS of great stuff as the children progress in years.  A computer that will last them for a good couple of years!

So, on behalf of a happy mom and three little kids who have their own computer, thanks goes out to the Ed/Ubuntu team(s) and the developers of gcompris and all the other people who make kids software for linux.  

Take care.

Upo.
