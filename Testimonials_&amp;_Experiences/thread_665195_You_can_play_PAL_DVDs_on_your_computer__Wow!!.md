### Post by lisati on 2010-04-17

> whats PAL:confused:

Long, potentially boring, jargon riddled explanation that's way over my head cut short: The way a TV signal is put together is different around the world. The two main ways are referred to as NTSC (used in America and some other places) and PAL (used in Europe, New Zealand, Australia, and some other places). A third way that I'm aware of is "SECAM", but isn't as common. 

Although the basics are similar, there are enough differences present to trip up the unwary. There's stuff like frame frequency, picture size/shape and the way colour is handled.

It's too early in the morning for me to try to come up with an accurate yet easy to understand answer that goes into it further.... bring on the coffee!

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-04-18
PAL is definitely superior. NTSC gives you a darker, fuzzier image. The frame rate is a bit better (NTSC has 29.97 frames per second, as opposed to PAL's 25 frames per second) but film-to-DVD transfers are easier to do when you're going to PAL, because the difference is only 1fps, instead of 5.97fps.

### Post by DeMus on 2010-04-18
> **days_of_ruin said:**
> whats PAL:confused:

PAL is the improved NTSC way of transmitting TV signals, it's the system we use in Europe a lot.
PAL stands for Phase Alternating Lines. It has a build-in way to correct deviations to the signal because of transmitting it by air.
NTSC, the American way of doing things, is called here: Never The Same Color, because the signal is disturbed while transmitting it and it does not have the build-in way to correct it.

SECAM was developed in France and is/was also used in some African countries (mostly former French possessions), the former Soviet Union and a handful of other Middle-Eastern countries. A common sarcastic acronym expansion is "Surtout Eviter la Compatibilité Avec le reste du Monde" ("Above all, avoid compatibility with the rest of the world").
