### Post by duckgoesoink on 2008-04-08
I heart Ubuntu. It's beautiful, fast, easy to use, and powerful. I love:
[LIST]
[*]the visual theme possibilities (I'm obsessed now with making all my windows and programs look beautiful)
[*]the graphical package manager with thousands of free programs available at the click of a button
[*]not having to reboot after installations
[*]not needing to run and update anti-virus and anti-spyware software twice daily plus a firewall asking every 10 seconds "are you sure you want to allow this?"
[*]that it only ever uses 6% of the CPU and 35% of the RAM when I have 6 programs running simulataneously
[*]and I'll never have to pay for upgrades...
[/LIST]

I have previously used XP (was fine to start with if you don't include the countless reinstalls - first computer in 2003). Then bought a nice shiny iMac in 2005 when I enrolled in a Computer Graphics degree, and fell in love with not worrying about anti-virus software, anti-spyware software, firewalls, etc.

Then moved to France last year, sold the Mac :-( and bought a PC laptop. Suffered with Vista for 8 months - I have 1024mb of RAM and dual processors, so technically Vista should have run better than my iMac, but it's just too hungry. 


Now I dual-boot with Ubuntu, but I find I never boot into Vista - it's too painful after the beauty and speed of Ubuntu.


I also find people are rarely helpful on Windows forums. The Ubuntu community is full of lovely helpful people ready to help with any problem if you ask nicely - hopefully one day I can be as smart as the people here.

Thanks to all the developers and contributors for *giving people a choice*.



And to all those who say Kubuntu is nicer looking and better for ex-windows users - I have to STRONGLY disagree - KDE is an overly-shiny, super-cluttered visual mess. Sorry Kubuntu, but I love your brother Ubuntu. :-)
