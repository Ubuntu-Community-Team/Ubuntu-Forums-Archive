### Post by RuneGeek on 2009-08-13
It was in April that I first posted here that I'd started using Ubuntu exclusively on my laptop and in a dual-boot environment with XP on my desktop.

After installing Ubuntu, I rarely used XP.  Over the months, this turned into "never".  Occasionally I'd boot the XP side and download patches, but the comparative speed of one versus the other made me cringe at the thought of hitting the XP side.

So a couple of nights ago I reformatted the Windows drive to ext4 and claimed it as fresh disk space for Ubuntu.  (Although I've kind of been considering installing a LAMP server to that drive to play around with instead...)

I do a lot of office-apps work and web development at home, plus I have to remote into work sometimes... Ubuntu is solid, fast, and - bottom line - just *better* than Windows.  Not because it's free (although that helps -- a lot!) but because it's flat-out, hands-down just a better OS.

I've become a bit of an Ubuntu evangelist these days.  My computer is the single most important electronic tool in my house... more important than the TV, the DVR, the gaming systems, my cell phone, etc.  Ubuntu puts it miles ahead of what it used to be on Windows, and I love to tell people how good it could be for their computers, too.

Anyway... I just wanted to share the joy.  Months after making the switch, rather than going back, I'm going forward full-steam and loving it.  :P
