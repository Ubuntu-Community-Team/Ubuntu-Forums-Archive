### Post by DeusExM1 on 2009-11-16
I switched from Ubuntu to Vista months ago due to the fact that gaming is very weak on linux. Some desktop programs were operating poorly (open office / firefox / a few others). So i got fed up and ditched Ubuntu.

There was also a lot of crackpots who only read the Testimonial page to flame and worship their sacred OS, instead of actually admitting some things are wrong with the OS. I won't  miss those ********. What I did appreciate were the few people who offered constructive insight into the problems i originally presented.

However, now that i think back, i believe that Ubuntu is not a bad solution to Desktop computing. It has its limits (some pretty damn big ones, although i guess any OS does), but overall it cant be beat for security and some other features. (I love some open source software like Rhythmbox and cd burning was a lot faster in linux than in vista). There is other stuff that i liked but im not going to bother to list it here. I plan to build a new PC soon, and i was wondering what OS i should put on it. I plan to build a gaming rig so getting Ubuntu as a primary OS makes no sense. But i figure ill dual boot and just dedicate maybe 100 GB to windows and the rest to Ubuntu. Windows could be gaming-only OS, While ubuntu could take care of everything else. 

I Wish games ran on Ubuntu but i guess thats not going to happen in any near future. (By games i mean stuff like Crysis, open source games are not good enough... but thats natural). 

What i wish companies did was attempt to sell a game for the linux community before they actually make it for linux. If they poll enough users and manage to collect ($) what they believe will justify the costs, they could then move on to make the game. Call it an insurance policy if you will. Considering Ubuntu has over 8 million users, even if just 1/8 of the people sign up that would probably make it a good enough reason for the company to make a professional-grade game for linux.

just my .02c. 

Hater ******** are always welcome in my threads. I love you guys.

### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2009-11-18
> **DeusExM1 said:**
> But i figure ill dual boot and just dedicate maybe 100 GB to windows and the rest to Ubuntu. Windows could be gaming-only OS, While ubuntu could take care of everything else.

Way to go, I think you can´t fail with such set up.  However, considering the ridiculous prices for hard drives nowadays (500GB under 40 euros), I would consider dedicating a full drive for Windows, then getting a second drive for Ubuntu.  That set up is cleaner, it allows you to use a recovery disk from your manufacturer if required (not risking your Linux partition), and you can even partition your secondary drive and getting two flavors of Linux.

I would recommend Fedora 12 to be installed first (I love it), then Ubuntu to be installed second.  Fedora is a bit tricky with multiple booting, so be careful.  However, installing Ubuntu last should do the trick, as it is great at coexisting with other OS.

I think that way you can get the best from the GNOME world and the KDE world, and have fun learning Linux while keeping your Windows clean for gaming.

Good Luck!
