### Post by Wesseli on 2007-02-28
What's good about it, the All MIghty Plus From The Basic Caveman:

- Easy to use
- Stable
- Community (hopeless for noob without it)
- COST!! :) (dude with a wife and kid does not have THAT much extra cash...)
- Enough programs for basic user (can do all tasks for university courses etc.)
- No virus/adware/firewall-crap that drove me nuts with windows (and still does with my dad's computer...)
- Easy to change the looks, fonts etc. useless time wasting things. But nice.
- Ran out of the box with my Dell laptop, no hussle
- Easy to send pictures from my cell phone via bluetooth
- Wireless mouse (Logitech MX1000) worked without tweaking
- Internet install at home took about 15 seconds (after waiting for about 1 month for the providing company to "plug the cable in" to our house)

What's not good and drives me nuts:
- Wireless. OK at home  BUT: Our school has wireless network. Everyone else but me. I have a user name and a password for the network, but no can do. Remember, I am not an expert. With windows, just click and open explorer, put username and password there and there you go.
- Support for webcam. Once I finally had the damn thing working, some system updates, or what so ever, and installs and it is all ****** up again. With windows, plug it in and chat with skype or messenger in just a few seconds. WIth ubuntu, voice through Gizmoproject and picture through aMsn (to the point it stops working)

When I have the money (some distant time in the future :-)), I think next step is Mac... Or internet-tablet/palm device...

Peace

### Post by matt_risi on 2007-03-01
I second the vote for using wifi-rader. To install, go into terminal and type:

```
sudo aptitude install wifi-radar
```

And of course enter your password. It'll show up in the menu under "Internet". VERY useful app, I use it to access my university's wireless.
