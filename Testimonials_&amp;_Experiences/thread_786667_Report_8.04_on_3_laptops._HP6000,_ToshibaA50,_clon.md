### Post by JC Cheloven on 2008-05-08
Hi, I'd like to share my experience with Hardy after 2 weeks of use. So far I installed it on 3 laptops: 

- Toshiba: SA50-110  /  Pentium M (0.09) 1596MHz  /  1.1 Cache =64K, 19704 MB/s  /  1.2 cache=2048K, 8674 MB/s  /  Memory=1007Mb 1039Mb/s
Chipset: Intel i855GM/GME/F SB  99MHz Mobile platform  /  Settings: RAM=165MHz (DDR330) / CAS=2.5-3-3-7

- An old clone: Pentium III x86, 1GHz  /  256Kb RAM  /  64Kb video  /  Matshita UJDA310 Cdrom

- HP Notebook 6000, dual core... standard configuration.


In average, Hardy works smoother than gutsy for me: 
- In the Toshiba I don't need to start with "nolapic" or "routeirq" (I had with gutsy).
- In the HP, sound & usb ports does work (In gutsy randomly didn't). Moreover, I had to wipe gutsy from here because it didn't start at all, after some complicated (although otherwise successful) wireless printing and scanning setup. Still working at doing it with hardy (no problem using wired networking).
- In the old P III clone, hardy is a charm compared to gutsy: no startup parameters needed, no 5-min spent to startup (actually only 1'30''), no screen problems...
- Nice utilities for installing, and short startup time are strong additional points in hardy.

But I have to say that some things seem to be worse in hardy. Remarkably, it hangs (total kernel panic) some times when opening a video file, or similar tasks. Perhaps I'll go back here to post a more specific report on this.

TOO SUM UP: I would recommend hardy over other ubuntu versions, and also over other distros I know (well, I use fedora sometimes :-). But I look forward stability problems to be fixed. Perhaps would be wise to wait a few weeks before shipping friends and clients in the "hardy adventure" :KS
Regards

### Post by JC Cheloven on 2008-05-11
Hi, some more report about hardy in these laptops:

- In the Toshiba A50, random crashes (nothing works but shut down button) occur when trying to play mp3 music or wma video. Only in these situations, so far, and not everytime.

==> by the way, no proprietary drivers are being used in either machine.
