### Post by nickle on 2008-02-17
Shortly after Dapper was released, I installed it on an ex-computer for my ex-wife and kids. None of them had any prior computer experience whatsoever. They simply wanted to browse, email, listen to music, watch videos, write/edit documents watch/burn CD's DVD's etc. All the stuff most home users do.
Apart from a few sessions in which I had to explain the very basics of opening applications (which icons to click) and saving/finding files, there has been no difficulties whatsoever. I also explained how to perform updates. It should also be noted that I am not hugely technical myself; I am a user.The amazing thing is that the system is still running fine and stable and I rarely get any questions any more.
So it amazes me when I still hear people saying Linux is too difficult for "Joe Public", especially when I think of the number of friends who have continual difficulties with MS: viruses, malware, bugs, crashes, system getting progressively slow with time etc.
Linux has come a long way and I now believe firmly it is in fact the most stable, easy and cost effective way of delivering what most home users need for everyday stuff. Not only does it "just work", but it also keeps on "just working", even in the hands of the technically naive.
