### Post by mpace965 on 2011-05-27
So a little background; I have been using Windows all of my life. I was introduced to Linux around 2 years ago. I am 15 years old. From then on, I toyed with various distros on VirtualBox. Then in January I finally worked up the nerve to make the switch. Now I have Ubuntu dual booting with Win7 on my laptop. I almost never boot into Windows anymore. After installing, using Ubuntu was like a breath of fresh air. It was like computing, untethered and unrestricted, safely. I love how customizable everything is. I also love FOSS and am looking for ways to contribute. I have also been beginning to use the command line more often, seeing at is infinitely more useful than that shameful relic on Windows.

_Things that I have noticed coming from Windows (personally)_

-Doesn't use my laptop fan half as much- doesn't need to. This really got to bug me when I was using Windows, because the fan couldn't decided to be on or off and would constantly switch. Now I have 100x the power, and its still quiet.

-Boot time; simply amazing.

-Time from log on in which an application can be launched; almost instantly.

-Security; amazing, never really worry about viruses any more..

Thank you so much!! I have also made bootable flash drives for a bunch of my friends, and they loved it.
