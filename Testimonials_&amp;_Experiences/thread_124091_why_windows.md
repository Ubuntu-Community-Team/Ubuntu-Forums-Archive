Alex, I still say that Linux has bad hardware support ... not from the community, the community does damn well with being given black boxes.

The bad hardware support is from the hardware manufacturers themselves. Yes, it's getting better, but it isn't as good as it is in Windows (XP).

If you compare Windows to Linux, you will see that Linux is moving at a much faster rate than Windows in every department.

Linux is 15 years old (counting from 0.1 beta released in 1991( compared to Windows being 21 years old.

I would say that as far as eye candy in the gui (gnome/kde), Linux is going at about MacOS rate, so in a year or two, I am sure there will be a really nice dock that is native to the enviroment (gDesklets don't count, neither does the KDE parallel if there is one).

As for hardware support, if you rewind windows line 6 years, there will be Windows 2000 (which was new) and Windows 98 (I am not touching Windows ME). Windows 2000 has damn good hardware support ... after 4 service packs? Windows 98 has good hardware support as long as there are drivers.

Whatever version of Windows comes out after Vista, I am going to say that at that time, Linux will definately be known to Joe Sixpack users and it may even become a real competitor to Windows.

I think that in the next 5 years, Apple will eat away some of Microsoft's market share on user desktop/laptop systems, but Linux will take a much bigger bite.

Some numbers (pulled out of thin air) are 98/1/1 (Windows/Linux/OSX) then in 5 years it will become more like 85/10/5 (Windows/Linux/OSX). Of the 10% of Linux marketshare, 99.9% will be Ubuntu-type distributions (that are going the way of serving the user who wants a working system without messing around too much).

As for Linux on 64bit ... it was faster there than Windows ... I give Linux about 6 months or the next major kernel (2.7? 2.8?) to have 32bit compatability down as how Windows 64bit claims to be. (Absolutelly transparent to the user).
