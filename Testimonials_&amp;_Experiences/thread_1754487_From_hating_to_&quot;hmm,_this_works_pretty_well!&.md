### Post by Resdayn on 2011-05-10
Hello Ubuntu community!
I thought I'd share my thoughts about Ubuntu here and introduce myself a little.
I'm a student from The Netherlands who has picked up Ubuntu again first since Ubuntu 8.04
Ubuntu 8.04 was the reason I became anti-Linux because in my opinion, it wasn't user friendly at all, and what about that terminal? I always said: "If I wanted an OS where I have to type commands 90% of the time, I'd use a freakin' Commodore 64!" Several years passed, and I decided to check the Ubuntu website to see which version was out by now. I decided to give it one last shot, so I installed an old SATA hard drive from my previous into my new pc, and installed Ubuntu 10.10. For those who are interested how that went:

I first installed a 64 bit version. After installing Skype, I heard very strange 8-bit like noise when I started Skype. The sound was completely distorted. A friend of mine who has been using Ubuntu for years told me that I should switch to 32 bit because 64 bit was rather buggy and still experimental. So I wiped my disk and tried installing the 32 bit version of Ubuntu, which failed. Apparently my hardware was too new or something. So I installed the 64 bit version again and used it for a while. I must say that after getting it to know a little, I was quite satisfied, but the sound was bugging me though. My headset microphone did not work (huge latency for some reason) and after I messed up a few times, leaving Ubuntu in a working but still messy state, I switched back to Windows. 

After hearing that 11.04 was out, I hoped that installing 11.04 would fix all of my problems, so I did. I was a bit disgusted by Unity, and switched back to GNOME as soon as possible. After a few days, I read that it's possible to install kubuntu-desktop from package manager and that I could switch between those two desktop environments whenever I wanted, so I installed kubuntu-desktop. That's where I really started to like Linux in general again. For some reason I like KDE better than GNOME, not because it looks more like Windows though. Everything a user could possibly want to do with his pc, is possible in Kubuntu. I even managed to get a Source engine game running on Wine. Basically, I do not have to use Windows anymore unless I want to play game. The only thing that has been bugging me is the sound, but I've already made a topic for that.

So what are my thoughts in short? I really like Kubuntu, because I can do everything I want like in Windows, but the typical little Windows-annoyances do not exist here. For some reason, Steam (Wine) loads faster in Kubuntu than in Windows, and that is rather funny I think, since Wine is just an emulator.
I think Ubuntu in general has definitely improved since 8.04, because that release really put me off and started my anti-linux feelings. Ubuntu is not perfect, but it's made by a group of volunteers and it's free, so I think it's unfair to "sue them" if something doesn't work. 

You pay for Windows, so you have the right to expect it to work as it should, but it doesn't, and that's a major difference. I do not understand why the average computer user (13 year old msn-addicted girls, 30 year old house-wives and those other stereotypes) couldn't use Ubuntu/Kubuntu, because you can do everything you can do on Windows, it's free, it has better support than Windows, it requires no antivirus and it runs on the Linux kernel which is more efficiënt. Also, Ubuntu/Kubuntu treats me like an adult as opposed to Windows who still treats me like a newbie in my opinion. This post is a little long and messy, but I hope reading it wasn't a waste of time. Anyway, I'm glad I freed myself from the Microsoft enslavement. :)
