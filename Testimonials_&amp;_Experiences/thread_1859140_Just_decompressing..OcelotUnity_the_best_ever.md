### Post by ventrical on 2011-10-13
> **MartyBuntu said:**
> Haven't tried it.
What are the differences from 11.04 that you are enjoying?


With my Nvidia card the graphics are much more snappy. On my ATI cards (laptops) Oneiric is smoother in it's graphical rendering of backgrounds and, by default, has a larger variety of backgrounds. Unity is also smoother with a few small differences as opposed to (natty) and during the alpha/beta testing there wasn't  half as many crashes with compiz as there was with  (natty) alpha/beta testings.

  I'm not sure that this review could be considered the kind that would lead one to believe that Ocelot is a solid graphical user interface that would make for a sturdy buisness platform, but, as the cards lay with compiz, Oneiric is working through compiz much more efficiently as compiz is  the Ubuntu hallmark graphical user interface. 

  I must confess that I haven't tried to resize the launcher icons in Edubuntu (11.04) (will try in just a bit) but this is an option that is unique, passing more control of the desktop the the end_user.

  I also get a sense that some of the security features and rock solidness of Maveric Meerkat have been embedded into Ocelot that may not have been incorporated into (natty).

  I'm not going to slam (annty) because I really like it now that it has coime into it's own, but, I have been on the record as reporting that I had a very difficult time getting wireless drivers and graphical drivers to work. Ocelot is a walk away upgrade that solved a lot of problems (at least with my terminals) that (natty) did not.

  For the most part one may propound that Ocelot is just a mere theme upgrade of (natty) but if you study it just a bit one would see a more solid response in installation and stability than that with (natty).

So .. just to list a few ... :)
