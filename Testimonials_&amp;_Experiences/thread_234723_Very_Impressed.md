### Post by jagaholic on 2006-08-11
Been using Linux mainly on the server level since red hat 6.0   Started trying Linux on the desktop with red hat 9 but never liked it.  With red hat moving to fedora later, I kept on messing with a desktop replacement, using fedora (never liked it) and suse 9+ (nice, but would always run into issues.)  Last few years, have been using centos with ok success.  Heard about ubuntu from a buddy, but like an old dog that I am, was not sure to try something to new knowing more about red hat and suse.  So I decided to fire up my spare pc and install ubuntu 6.  The install was very nice and easy.  After checking out the ubuntuguide and adding the xtra repo's, installs were simple.  Added everything I needed in no time and even ati drivers that can be a pain in the rump sometimes.  Only issue I had was making a raid drive as my system has 5 drives in it and I wanted the other 4 drives to raid for storage.  Had to revert to the terminal way to get it working. Wish it had more of a suse easy raid after an install, but if that is the only thing, heck I am happy as a clam.  Even have vmware running thanks to this forum.  Between the forum here and the guide, this has been very painless for a worn out IT guy who just likes things to work after a 10 hour day of work.
