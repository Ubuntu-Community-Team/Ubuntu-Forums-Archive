### Post by Vignesh S on 2010-04-25
After having so many threads in this section, I thought I would never write a post here, thinking that I would never leave Ubuntu. Little did I suspect that the tables have totally turned fairly recently.

I started out with Ubuntu 2 years ago when my friend came over to my place to install Ubuntu 8.10 on my desktop. I didn't really give too much attention, until I wanted Ubuntu on my sister's old netbook too. The wifi refused to work, and that's when Ubuntu started to grow on me. It took me forever  to get it to work, but I did it in the end. And that's when I realise that I actually *liked* to solve computer problems like this. 

And so it continued. I removed XP on my desktop, my ancient IBM Thinkpad R31 also got a dose of OSS software and operating system, and as the problems came, with the help of this friendly community, I blasted through them.

I also learnt a lot about computers in general. Things I had no idea about, like how drivers actually worked, and how (somewhat) oblivous a lot of Windows users are to this kind of things that went on in the background. And it was also nice knowing that I wouldn't be affected by Windows viruses in Ubuntu :P .

However, despite the help of this awesome community, it wasn't the best in getting the job done. Publisher in Crossover refuses to work, and that was pretty important. In the end, I couldn't afford to fail a major outcome in school as a result of things stuffing up in Ubuntu. Time also became a valuable resource, and I didn't quite have the time to fix any unexpected problems that might have come along in my way during my final years of High School. 

So in the end, I removed Ubuntu off my desktop, and put Windows 7 onto it. My laptop still has Ubuntu though, because it will take forever for the recovery software on it to just reinstall Win 7 on it. 

This is me saying farewell to Ubuntu, though not to linux as a whole. I know it will have a growing place in fast-booting operating systems; something Windows doesn't do so well in. 

However, there was a reason why I temporarily quit Windows, and that was I wanted something better. Linux didn't quite make the cut for me, and there is only 1 OS left out of the "big three" which are Linux, Windows and of course, Mac OS X. Maybe it is now coming down to whether I am a Mac or PC, but that will take some time to determine. But that linux experience could come in handy on the Mac :)

Before I go though, I just to let you guys know that the Ubuntu community has been very helpful in all of the struggles I have been through, and I gained enough knowledge to even help people that needed help too :) . 

It has been a positive experience on the whole with my time with Ubuntu, and I wish Canonical, Mark Shuttleworth, this community and all of the users of this operating system all the best for the future, because it is going to be interesting what will happen to the OS market over the next 10-15 years.
