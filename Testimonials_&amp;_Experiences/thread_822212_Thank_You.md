### Post by carlolovearianne on 2008-06-08
Browsing the forums I noticed that there is a considerable amount of posts about going back to Windows, saying goodbye to Ubuntu (for now) and how their experience with Ubuntu has been hell so I take it upon myself to even things out even a little bit.

First of all I would like to say "Thank You" to the people in Canonical for sending me the Ubuntu cds I treasure so much. It was almost a year ago when I chanced upon the website mindlessly browsing and reading something about requesting cds for free. I had to create a Launchpad account very quickly and honestly paid it no mind. I wasn't looking for an alternate OS to my Mac OS X. Back then I couldn't really care less if the free cds were a scam or not. But then it arrived (in about 3 weeks) and it still took me more than a month to try it out on my brother's pc (which has been crippled by Windows XP). 

The interest hasn't waned since then. I bought me a laptop and its exclusively running Ubuntu 8.04. There are minor nibbles, though. I haven't made the webcam to work. The Gstreamer extra codecs freezes the system everytime I play mp3 files. That prompted me to convert my 3000 plus mp3's into .ogg files using a wonderful app called Soundconverter and got rid of the mp3 codec in the system. No more freezing afterwards (don't ask me why, I'm no expert :)).

Going back to the subject of "thanking", I would like to thank the Ubuntu developers for bringing to the world an operating system that is free and beautiful and so full of promise. Well, its still not a "just works" OS but its getting there. I'm looking forward to Intrepid Ibex and the much touted "new look" that it will come with. Hopefully, the mockup which can be seen in [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=784692](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=784692) will be incorporated.

I would also like to thank and commend the creators/developers of some of my favorite applications. People who work hard as them can't be thanked enough. So, in no particular order:

Thank you Rhythmbox authors. The simplicity reminds me of iTunes before it became too big and too bloated and too resource-hungry. 

Thank you Open Office.org.

Thank you Mozilla, for Firefox.

Thank you Evolution Team.

Thank you Pontus Ekberg for Comix.

Thank you Bastien Nocera for Totem.

Thank you  Adolfo González Blázquez for pYreNamer.

Thank you Gimp Development Team.

Thank you Scribus Development Team.

Thank you F-Spot Team.

Thank you Alex Graveley for Tomboy Notes.

Thank you Gautier Portet and Lars Wirzenius for Soundconverter.

Thank you Gnome Team.

Thank you Linus Torvalds.

Thank you Ubuntu Community.

And of course Thank You, **Mark Shuttleworth**. You are a hero to us all.
