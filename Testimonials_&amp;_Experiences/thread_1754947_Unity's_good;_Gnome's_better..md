### Post by parthgadhia on 2011-05-10
Well, it's been about 3 days since I switched to Ubuntu. Always been a Windows Vista user, was fairly happy with it, so much so that I didn't really mind formatting my PC every 6 months, had pretty much turned immune to the virus attacks, come what may, knew that my PC will be affected despite all the antiviruses. 

Then one day I read the word "Ubuntu" somewhere, got a little curious, googled it, and decided to give it a shot. And boy! am I glad I did that. I loved Ubuntu, and everything that came with it. Unity was pretty awesome, my laptop, a Dell Inspiron 1545, 2.00 GHz Core 2 Duo, 3 GB Ram, responded very very well to it. All in all, I was really really with Ubuntu and Unity. Seemed like a perfect DE.

Till I decided to switch over to Ubuntu Classic just to see what the whole fuss was about, since everyone kept cribbing about Unity. And I'm really glad I did. My laptops become lightning fast, and neat, and much much much much better than how it was with Unity. Just looks and feels cleaner, and responds beautifully to everything, and it is extremely user-friendly as well. Strange part is everyone claims Unity's supposed to be more user-friendly, but Gnome's wayyyyyyyyy more user-friendly. 

I'm a little upset Gnome 2 is going to be supported for another 6 months only, because it's a lovely desktop environment. And Ubuntu's a lovely OS. Danke! :)

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-05-11
> **mastablasta said:**
> a lot like but not not quite.
 
For example it does have two panels but it has only one menu (functions are not separated clearly as gnome is).


There is an applet that gives you a Gnome-like Places menu. Other than that, only System is merged into Settings int he menu.

> 
Gnome is also known to have much less settings and options via GUI so that they don't confuse a beginner user. XFCE has a bit more options as i see it.


This can be argues both ways, "Gconf-editor" can definitely be dangerous. In XFCE, novice users have much more control without the danger of messing up their system.

This is a notable difference.

> 
XFCE has a nasty bug (which i am still not sure if it was fixed or not, but it's definatelly still in the long term "*support"* version) that is that the panels might dissapear. 


This usually happens when you turn composition on/off. You can right-click and adjust the transparency of the panel or sometimes just click on the panel. For the record, some of the biggest issues of Gnome2 are associated specifically with the panel (problems with auto-hide, issues with multiple monitors and/or changing the resolution and dependence on deprecated technology).

Overall XFCE panel is much better.

Bottom line is: XFCE is not Gnome2, but it is pretty close.

### Post by murderslastcrow on 2011-05-16
Xfce is faster (OH SNAP!). I'm a pretty extensive tester, and if you get Xfapplet (allows you to use Gnome2 applets in Xfce), the only real difference between Xfce4 and Gnome2, functionally, is that you can't just scrub through menus and indicators without clicking them individually. I'd like to see them implement that feature.

So far as the settings go, you can see them all from one place- it's really a whole lot like OS X's settings manager, or Gnome 3's (but a few more options). Nothing compared to how many options KDE presents you with (which I love).

So unless you really need to scrub a lot, Xfce4 may be a good option when 10.04 is unsupported. I'm seeing an interesting similarity between people who dislike new cell phones and people who can't stand Unity/Gnome3/KDE4. They tend to be the same people. XD
