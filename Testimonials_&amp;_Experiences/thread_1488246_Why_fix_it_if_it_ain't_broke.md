### Post by John Bean on 2010-05-22
> **WinterRain said:**
> Actually, I have seen more glowing reviews of 10.04 than bad ones.

Yes, and that despite the fact that the vast majority of "testimonials" are actually motivated by dissatisfaction of some sort; satisfied users are far less likely to post their experiences.

As to the OP's point, it's double-edged: "if it ain't broke don't fix it" is often synonymous with "no progress". A reliable and well-used tool always seems better for the user than a new one, and there is no need to replace it with an *identical* new replacement unless it's broken - but that simply doesn't apply here, 10.04 isn't just 8.04 (whatever) with go-faster stripes, it's a significantly improved version in almost all respects.

We could all still live in caves and paint ourselves blue if we wanted to, but most people given the choice prefer to embrace positive change rather than having to be dragged screaming towards it ;-)

### Post by oldsoundguy on 2010-05-22
I have a couple of machines that are set up SPECIFICALY to run the hot new item.  Old machines that have been around for several years.  IF the new release functions on them, then I consider updating.  I have one machine that goes from LTS to LTS with no modifying in between.

Fixing what ain't broke is a good idea most of the time.  But the newer stuff does work better and faster and has more bells and whistles. 
The Choice is yours.

Right now I have virtually flawless installs of 10.04 running on 3 machines.  The 10.04 version of Mythbuntu, however, leaves a bit to be desired on legacy video equipment and does NOT work flawlessly. But NOTHING has worked flawlessly on that box since day one.

As pointed out above .. when an install works, you don't come on to a forum and ask for help.  The amount of "help" cries on 10.04 is VERY noticeably LESS than for previous releases .. and the "this build is JUNK" posts REALLY are not there in comparison to times past.
Still moans about laptops, but what else is new  about those proprietary pieces of crap that are foisted off as "great" in the bargain stores!
There seem to be little complaints from those using quality laptops in the first place.
I have yet to see a complaint from someone with a Panasonic Tough Book, for instance. (that could be because they are designed for the US military and the military runs Linux/Unix.)
