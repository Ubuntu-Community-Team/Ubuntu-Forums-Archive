### Post by llcooljsl on 2008-04-21
Hi this is kind of linked to [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=760727]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=760727")

I was basically feeling disheartened at not really get much success from my linux install.

I found getting internet via a cable was very easy. 
If you have this option, use dhcp and connect to your router and it should autoconfigure.

I then ran all suggested updates.
This then showed my wireless adaptor in the network window where before it was missing.

I clicked configure and clicked the tick and clicked close. 

The problems started when in the bottom right hand corner the wlan0 was not visible in the network manager.
I went back to system>administration>network.
Everything looked fine, then I noticed a bizarre phenomenon, if you make changes to an interface such as wireless and instead of close, you click on another interface, it says changing interface or something similar.
After this I saw the signal strength green bar appear and the ability to choose wireless wlan0.
Then I was pretty much instantly online via wireless.
There is also no obvious wireless utility that I could find, I went to synaptic package manager and downloaded wifi radar but didnt really get too far.  
I set most things to AUTO, typed in my WEP key and click save and then connect.
It would not receive an IP address so had I not found the weird interface change phenomenon then I would have been struggling.

Also linked to my other thread, I am no longer experiencing system freezes after the updates.
I am scared to tempt fate but the system seems stable and functional...lol
