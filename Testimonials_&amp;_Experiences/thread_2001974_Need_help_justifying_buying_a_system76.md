### Post by TBABill on 2012-06-12
Only thing I would suggest is a lot of research into consumer stories about the Dell "R" series of laptops and not just this particular model. I have 2 Dell 1545 laptops, which appear to be precursor models to the R and are nearly identical in fit/function/hardware. What I found the 2 laptops to be is pretty much garbage. On Windows 7 they operated well for quite a few months, but over time and with updates the machines screeched to a crawl, they run hot and the screens are very low quality with visible lines within both screens before the first 12 months was up. 
 
I've reloaded them from scratch and then updated, I have used the Microsoft antivirus software, then uninstalled it and run AVG and Avast (separately, never 2 antiviruses running simultaneously) and the machines are clean of malware and spyware. They just seem to run hotter over time and they've both been cleaned internally (and neither had hardly any dirt inside and the vent tubes are clear). The keyboards are not very durable and we've replaced 1 power cord for failure for unknown reasons (transformer). 
 
I do not know the quality level of the System 76, but the lower end Dells are average at best. I don't mean lower end in terms of processor, HDD, etc., but rather in terms of which model starts at the lowest price point and moves up with better specs. The R series seems to be a low end line for Dell and the quality of Dell laptops is questionable in some models. The Inspiron line just feels and behaves "cheap" so your "lower" priced Dell may not actually be if, like me, you end up having to replace it due to hardware issues so quickly (less than 2 yrs for me).
 
I'm not touting any brand, just offering free advice to do some homework based on others' experiences with the Dell "R" line before deciding...could help you decide with some confidence once you know how others perceive the quality of their machines over time...well beyond the initial "wow this thing is great" post when someone gets home, fires it up and finds it works as expected on day 1.

I'd say it's a toss up. Just pour over the details and see what works for you.

I have a Dell and a system76. My Dell was a really nice deal -- Inspiron 11z with an i3 and nice battery life for $325. It's become kind of a favorite for me because I really like the keyboard and the 3.5 lb weight. I can stick it in a bag and bring it anywhere.

### Post by 3Miro on 2012-06-15
The two machines do not have the same specs.

- Gen 3 CPUs will give similar CPU performance, but for a laptop you will get much better battery life.
- Gen 3 have big improvement on the GPU performance.
- The screen resolution is also different, the Gazelle has higher pixel density than a Mac Thunderbolt monitor. I haven't done the math for the Dell, but it is lower density.
- If you are planning on running Ubuntu, then you have to consider that there is no guarantee that the current or any future versions of Ubuntu would work on the Dell. System76 offers support even for their older models on newer versions of Ubuntu.

I have little experience with Dell, but System76 has great long term reliability. I have two Pangolins and I am pretty happy with both.

Ask yourself why is the Dell so cheap? The reason is that Dell wants to clean their inventory from the old Gen 2 laptops, this is older technology, while System76 gives you the newest available hardware. If you are planning on using this machine for a while, the Dell will start you one generation behind.

On the question of Gen 2 vs Gen 3 CPU as well as the Ubuntu support, I would pick System76 any day. If cash is of big concern, I would sooner go for Pangolin or Lemur with Gen 3 CPU and extra battery life, than gamble on Ubuntu support with the Dell.
