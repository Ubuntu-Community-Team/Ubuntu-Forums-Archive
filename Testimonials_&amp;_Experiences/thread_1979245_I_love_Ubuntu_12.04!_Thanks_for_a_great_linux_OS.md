### Post by nleibert on 2012-05-13
I'm a long time Windows user but I have always messed around with different linux distros over the years and I finally wanted to make the switch and have linux as my only OS. My first experience was with Slackware, then Debian and Arch Linux because I loved to tinker with my system and learn about linux...but then I stopped wanting to mess with my system and just have an OS that worked out of the box and I have found that with Ubuntu! I love the font, the themes, and everything has just worked except for a minor issue I figured out with my video card.

The only criticism that I have...as many others have said, is Unity. I used Unity for a few weeks in the 12.04 beta and then also after 12.04 was released and I actually kinda liked it...but then I realized that while it worked well for me when I was just viewing youtube videos or doing some minor things, it wasn't very effective for me when I needed to get some real work done. I own a lot of websites and always have a lot of applications/windows open at all times which include Filezilla, Bluefish, Leafpad, Gimp, several web browsers, and more...and I just couldn't manage all of them effectively with Unity like I could with Gnome Classic or even Gnome 3. Right now, I'm using Gnome Classic (No Effects) and have it setup exactly how I want it.

Anyways, I just wanted to say thanks to all of the Ubuntu developers, keep up the good work!
