### Post by RKCole on 2007-05-20
I have been meaning to write a testimonial for quite some time, but I just have not had the time to do so.  For anyone reading this, please forgive me in advance for the length of this post, as I have a bit to say; I also have a few points I'd like to make.

I started using Ubuntu (and Linux in general) back in 2006 or so when Breezy was the latest release.  Needless to say ti was unusable for me back then.  The system worked great, but accessibility support was lacking.  Needless to say, though, (and this is not to start any type of flame war) I could hake just dropped the idea of using Linux altogether and stuck with Windows...which I really did not want to do.  I could have made a post here on the forums and bashed the system to the ground, and then I could have left the forums never to be seen again.  But you know something...I saw the principles and mission of Ubuntu...and ti was heart warming.  
Things got better and better with each release, and now I use Feisty and I am very happy with it.

If any of you here at the forums have read any of my posts, you may know that I am a partially blind computer user.  I won't get into the specifics of my visual handicap (I won't say "disability" as the prefix "dis" puts the word into a negative context meaning "unable"...and well...just because I'm blind does not make me "unable" to do things in my life.); let's just say that I have VERY limited vision (20/2000 in one eye, no vision in the other).  What I am trying to get at with this is that when Breezy was released, accessibility was much lacking in the department of scree magnification.  With each new release things got better and better, and now the magnification (with gnome-mag or with Beryl's Zoom plugin) is comparable to the commercial magnification software for Windows that costs hundreds.  Instead of giving up on it altogether, I kept my faith, and I am glad I did.

So...here is what Ubuntu is to me...

**A Way To Stay Connected With My Family**
I moved from Ohio to California this past February.  It was a tough decision, but I figured I would have a better chance of finding a job here.  Many employers in Ohio were not too willing to work with me because of my vision; that is beside the point, though.
It was a hard decision as all of my family lives in Ohio.
Last month my mother asked me to work with my sister to install Ubuntu on their home computer, as Windows was seriously corrupted (as it typically is, right?).  My family LOVES Ubuntu.  The computer runs so much faster, and they are so ecstatic about ti each time I talk to them.  And now that they use Ubuntu, I found that I could play LAN games such as KBattleship, Atlantik, and Armagetron with them.  It has helped me to feel a little bit closer to home by this type of interaction.  I am very grateful for this.

**Freedom**
I still dual boot because I have a Lexmark Printer (X2470) that does not work with Ubuntu.  I plan (soon) to buy a new HP printer.  But anyway...I hated booting into Windows.  Each time I would run the OS I would be reminded of how my data was always being sent to M$, how the screen magnifier I use in Windows costs around $600...and a lot of other things.  I hated that it would take at least a minute before my desktop would be fully accessible...because I would have to wait for my anti-virus software, my anti-spyware software, and my firewall software to load just so I would have protection.  I hated being constrained as I was.  When I start Ubuntu and login, I can open my magnifier right when I see the icon...and I instantly have screen magnification.  It feels nice...like I have broken out of some prison...and I can take in fresh air that I never knew existed...I know my metaphors are in the plenty...I've always been the writer-type...so my apologies.

**Community**
This one is also equally important.  if not for all of you here in the Ubuntu community, I don't think I'd be where i am today.  Each time I've posted here, even if an answer was not yet known, there was always someone trying to help.  I never felt as if I was being judged for my ignorance when I had a problem.  I cant' say that about some of the Windows forums I was a member of in the past.  So I am very grateful for this community...I am just really glad to be a part of this.

I will say that I have had problems with Ubuntu in the past, such as my wireless...but after research that problem was solved...and I even was able to write a guide to help others with the same issue.  Sure...I was frustrated...but the community stuck through the problem with me to the end.  But you know something...there will be problems with any software that a computer runs...any OS.  There is no perfect piece of software...but the Linux community strives to keep things up-to-date and secure.  I don't' care of I have daily updates...as I know that things are being worked on and fixed.

Well...I don't feel that I have described how I feel about Ubuntu in the words I've written...but I hope this gives even a tidbit of justice to it.

So...in short...thank you.
