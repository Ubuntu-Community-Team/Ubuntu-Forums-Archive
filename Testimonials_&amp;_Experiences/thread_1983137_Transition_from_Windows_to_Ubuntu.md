### Post by oslub on 2012-05-19
Hello everyone.

I am an Ubuntu user since 11.10 version, not very skilled or experienced at linux in general but getting to know the basics. I also use Lubuntu regularly since 12.04 version on an older desktop. I am posting here about one of my biggest concerns at the moment (I believe the testimonials & experiences thread is the most adequate for this post).

Windows XP support ends at early 2014. Although Windows 7 is getting more and more users (Microsoft is doing everything to force upgrade, just look at Skydrive Desktop example, or IE9 or WLM 2011, which I consider very stupid and disrespectful for its users), XP was without a doubt the dominant OS and is still present in tons of PC's. However, many people, as me for example, and also institutions or schools, will still have that older PC that still fulfills the basics and they won't or really can't throw it away. There is an economical crisis all over Europe. 

So I consider this is the perfect time to raise awareness about free software. Until the end of MS support to WinXP, I want to find the perfect distribution that fits in my older desktop. For that I have chosen Lubuntu, for being inside Ubuntu/Debian family and because of its low resource consumption. On my laptop I use Ubuntu 12.04.

My goal is to make sure that by the time I have to drop Windows XP, I have an alternative OS that can entirely fulfill all my needs. In my opinion, Ubuntu and it's affiliates (Xubuntu, Kubuntu, Lubuntu) is the most prepared and intuitive distribution to receive someone migrating from Windows, making people feel comfortable about using it. But there are some things that still keep most people away from Linux (I'll say some of my own later) Thinking about that, I had an idea that I would like to share with you.

I couldn't find a place where people could discuss specifically and uniquely the difficulties and problems they have when trying to move from Windows to Ubuntu. I went to askubuntu and this forum. Rather than discuss Ubuntu own tweaks and bugs, in my opinion there should be a section, a sticky, some sort of dedicated area where people would discuss about what they need and don't get with ubuntu, having to use Windows to do it. So this wouldn't be discussing ubuntu errors or failures but instead the incompatibilities that arise from the migration of one OS to another (I hope I am making it clear so far).

For example, MSOffice is probably the most notorious case of an application that leads people back to Windows. I mean, it's not ubuntu failure, we know that it is hard to get 100% compatibility between MSOffice and Libre Office suites, since one is free software and the other is proprietary software. So in this case, we could provide, in this hypothetical section, some improvements that could be made (for example use doc or odf format, installing MSCore fonts). It's not that LibreOffice is bad, but most people use MSOffice, there would not be any problem if there is no need to open the document on Windows. On this section, we would talk about free and Linux-suitable applications that could replace what other applications that are made for Windows do (and there are many). Also the big problem that is Flash Player on Linux, we all know it just doesn't work well and now it won't get any more support, once again it's not a bug or a failure from ubuntu side, but still we need a good web experience.

I have already tried to convince some people to try ubuntu, not replace entirely but just try, and Ubuntu is in fact the best, not only to enter the Linux world but overall, the best distro. It is becoming more and more the responsibility of Ubuntu to raise awareness about free software, Ubuntu is the only one with enough resources and support to accomplish this. In my opinion, most people who join Ubuntu come from Windows and their main concern is that they feel comfortable and don't miss anything from Windows. But in most cases, people don't feel that and are little confident that they can turn to Ubuntu as default OS, because let's admit, if MSOffice does what they need, why change? Unfortunatelly, many people just refuse to change or try new, and buy licenses because they believe in myths that are not true about Linux world, that everything is complicated and don't feel confident.

But with a section as I described, people would already know in fact what problems they would face when migrating, we would make them see what is true and what is false about Linux experience, we would demystify many wrong beliefs, for example that you can't have an important paper done entirely on LibreOffice, we would address the particular and most important doubt that hovers in their minds - Will I be able to have an easy, graphical, intuitive experience and still get work done on Linux? Will I have the equivalent of some application I really need?

Any feedback, suggestions, questions about this post would be very much appreciated. Think about it :)
