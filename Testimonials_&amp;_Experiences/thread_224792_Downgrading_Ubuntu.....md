### Post by paullinux on 2006-07-28
Today, after hours and hours of desperate attempts getting my ATI-driver to work in 3D, I decided to downgrade (yes DOWNGRADE) to Breezy.  I installed the previous driver (8.14.13) 'et voila' I can use penquin-racer again...

I figure that, after a month with Dapper, there isn't so much difference between its predecessor so I installed Breezy again. I have upgraded the installed software to it's presentday versions.  So now I have a fully functionally working OS and software!

For all the hype I cannot come to the same enthousiasme about Dapper.  I realise that ATI has something to do about it, but I cannot accept that in a 'next generation'-version of Ubuntu things do not work anymore! While it did in its previous version!

Hence my conclusion: What's next in Ubuntu Edge? No sound?...

### Post by agger on 2006-07-28
FWIW, I myself am running Xubuntu Dapper on a machine I bootstrapped from a Debian Sarge base system (since Ubuntu has no floppy install), which means that practically everything was downloaded and dist-upgraded over the net.

It runs absolutely sweet. If there's problems with Dapper as installed from the CD's, I suspect doing a complete upgrade and dist-upgrade should solve a lot of these problems?

Apart from that, Dapper seems to run clearly better and with better hardware support than Breezy as far as I can see. But maybe I was just lucky not to run into the issues you mention.
