How did I find out how to do this... well, you're right, I had to think about it.

It can be unsettling to get used to a certain "mode of thinking and understanding" when it comes to a computer, and then try to switch completely over in one fell swoop.  For a comparison, if you learn to write with your right hand, but then try to switch to your left hand, it's not going to be automatic -- even if you've been writing for years with your right hand... your left hand doesn't have the "feel" for writing yet, even though your brain does.

Nobody ever wants to move from their comfort zone.  It takes planning and something like a "pre-flight" check before you can go ahead with a big change.  

What I suggest if you ever want to try linux on the desktop again, is do your planning ahead of time: what application do I need to burn dvds?  what can I use to make movies?  etc.  If you create your own "pre-flight checklist" and then follow through with your plan after installing linux.  I'm sure you'll be much happier with the result.
