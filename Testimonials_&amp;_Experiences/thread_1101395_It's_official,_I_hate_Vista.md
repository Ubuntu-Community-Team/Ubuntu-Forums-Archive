### Post by jbysmith on 2009-03-22
I'm a Vista hater myself.  It's not the performance, as it *is* running a lot of things in the background by default.. you don't get something for nothing and all that.  Bloat aside, patched to current it's not that far behind XP in speed.  I do like DWM.. it's no Compiz but it's still pretty snazzy, aside from the occasional app that forces it to shut down.

It's just the little bugs and new "features" that hurt more than help that drive me nuts.  

Oversimplified example.  This is definitely a case of "RTFM", or "just don't do that nub", but I've run into this once from a client.. not sure what he was trying to accomplish but it pointed out another quirk in Vista that I really dislike.  Take your "Pictures" directory in your home directory, click it, and give it a new name.  Say "Pics".  Ok, fine.  Now open up a command prompt and look at the directory, you'll still see "Pictures".  Now for laughs, type "Ren Pictures Pics".  And presto, Explorer loses the directory. It's still there, but you can't see it.  Even showing hidden/system files doesn't show it.  Console will see it, Windows itself won't.  Just for laughs, tried it on the latest Win7 build in a VM, still has this behavior.  I can live with Windows *still* not having proper symbolic links (junction points don't count as near impossible to use and very inflexible) but not being able to see what's really in a directory is just downright silly.  XP may not be all shiny and loaded with bling, but at least I can see what the hell is in a directory.

Fairly disappointed with Win7 so far too.  Yes, it's definitely improved on the performance end of things, and I do like *some* of the new features they added.  The library spin on indexing is a neat concept.  The Superbar on the other hand is a huge "meh" as far as I'm concerned, neat yes but just feels clunky compared to the old style taskbar/quicklaunch.  But the whole thing just feels like Vista with some bugfixes, and a few additions to justify making people throw down a few hundred bucks on an upgrade.
