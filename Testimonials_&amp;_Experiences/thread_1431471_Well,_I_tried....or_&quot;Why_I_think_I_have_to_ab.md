### Post by caruso81 on 2010-03-16
(I need to preface this by saying I'm really not trying to start a war here....I'm genuinely interested in a real discussion on what I am posting...)

I have played with various Linux installations in various configurations over the years, but I never made the commitment to really go to Linux, mostly because most of my hardware was PPC Mac and the 10% of Linux that could not be made to work on a Mac was a deal-breaker...wireless and the like. Fast forward to now...

Karmic seemed to be the distribution that could change all that. So many things worked out of the box, and so I installed it to both my Macs in a dual-boot setup. Even with minor issues that involved the usual problems with bootloaders, permissions and the like, I got a pretty decent working system on both my desktop and laptop and was ready to make the "Linux 100% of the time" commitment.

Four weeks later, I am typing this as I get ready to boot back into OS X.

Until today, I have never been able to articulate why I feel like Linux is this amazing idea that will simply never become dominant as it stands now. It is fast. I mean, crazy fast. It looks good. Compared to OS X, there is so little bloat, and compared to Windows...well, no need to elaborate there. So what's my problem?

In the end the final straw was, of all the stupid things: mailing labels.

[INDENT]Step 1: Need to make mailing labels from a spreadsheet in OpenOffice.
Step 2: Discover the 1999-type interface for mailing labels in OpenOffice.
Step 3: There's an app for that....off to Synaptic...OK...there's 75% of an app for that.
Step 4: Back to OpenOffice.
Step 5a: Discover that the "Ubuntu version" of OpenOffice does not include a menu option to enable a data source.
Step 5b: Figure out that I need "full" version of OpenOffice.
Step 5c: Download and start the install of OpenOffice.
Step 6a: Discover that certain dependencies with regards to JRE Runtime are required.
Step 6b: Attempt to download and (re)install JRE.
Step 6c Discover that it doesn't seem to matter to OpenOffice that JRE is already installed.
Step 7: Figure out that I need to uninstall the Ubuntu version of Open Office and install the core version, all in Synaptic.
Step 8: Find the menu option for labels in my new OpenOffice.
Step 9: Spend two hours getting the labels I need.[/INDENT]

Of course, most of this is about OpenOffice, and none of these problems are insurmountable. Right now, most everyone is yelling, "OPEN A TERMINAL AND TYPE...", but that is the point....the solution to printing labels in 2010 cannot be "Open a Terminal and type..." Not if Linux is to become a day-to-day OS for those of us who not only like to tweak but also have to to get real work done. And yes, I get that jumping to Step 7 is really not that big of a deal, but the thing is -- and here is the dealbreaker -- in the end it took me an order of magnitude longer to do the labels in Linux than it would have in either OS X or (*gasp*) Windows, and while I made the commitment to stick with Linux exclusively over these past four weeks, I have literally gotten nothing done in that time. My productivity has been zero, because every time I needed to do something, I spent the rest of the day addressing the process of getting ready to do that something. This is understandable near the beginning, but four weeks in, I need the OS to work...OR the OS has to be intuitive enough that my days are not an endless string of resolving dependencies and 1999-ish user interfaces. Is that the fault of Ubuntu? Maybe not, but the OS is only as good as the apps it can run.

When I want to participate in the process of computing, Linux is a blast, and I have gotten pretty good at finding solutions, either from other users or on my own. On those days, I love the idea of solving problems and getting my system "just right." I can hear all the hard-core Linux users out there solving my problems along the way with a few simple commands in the Terminal, but you guys are the "5-percenters." You enter commands in the Terminal from memory, and no one is denying that you are incredible in your willingness to help the rest of us, but making labels cannot require a web-browser and a terminal open alongside the word processor -- I can do that if I have to, but what happens to the regular user who doesn't know how to do that? That's the audience you have to capture if you want to take over the world....you can't just say, "tough, they need to learn to use a computer." What a lot of extremely skilled Linux users don't get is that some days, work just needs to get done, and most people can't afford the time it takes (or are unable) to configure this or tweak that each time something needs to happen. 

What Ubuntu Linux is now is an operating system for 1) experts who work from the command line, compile their own hybrid kernel just to see if it can be done, and simply know command syntax or 2) the novice appliance users who need to get to their Facebook pages and forward pithy sayings and cat pictures to their friends. But everything is a bell-curve, and most of us are semi-experienced, not-quite-power-users who need to do quasi-complicated things with a degree of efficiency. At some point, the never-ending state of installing Linux needs to end and the computing needs to begin.

(6 hours later, the labels were done. It should have taken 30 minutes.)

I use Ubuntu for a while now and I have the same need as you in regards to data. So there's 2 way around this:
