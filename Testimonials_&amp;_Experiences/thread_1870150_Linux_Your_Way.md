### Post by makitso on 2011-10-26
I have been a Ubuntu user since the 8.04 days.  Quite frankly, I love my 11.04 gnome 2 desktop system.  It's customizable, it suites my purpose and does exactly what I want.  Am I a heavy duty developer, nope.  But, I have a LAMP installation and do after hours development on a open source project.   My desktop has icons, folders and yes launchers on it that are not on the dock or panel &#8211; surprise.   

 So, I viewed 11.10 with some trepidation.  But, to give it the college try, I have been using Unity and Gnome 3 on my laptops and in a VirtualBox environment.   
 
 Can I get 11.10 to work for me, yes but only the Gnome 3 shell.  You see, I use an app dock for several releases and my choice is AWN.  I adopted the dock after drooling over the Apple dock and having my wife tell me how sexy it was.  However, Docky or Cairo would suite my purpose just as well.  Point here is that each dock is customizable so I can do it my way.                             

Canonical and the Gnome developers seem to be moving away from what make Linux so enjoyable and fun &#8211; you can have it your way. 

 PS. In my journey, I discovered Lubuntu and LXDE. It is wonderful and fun and takes me back to linux roots and what it was all about.  You can "still" have it your way.
