### Post by crag277 on 2006-08-14
I have been running Dapper on my laptop since its release.  It took a couple weeks in the beginning to get everything working properly, but it came together very nicely in the end.  Then, a few weeks ago Ubuntu told me that there was a new Kernel availible.  This scared me because I knew it would mean that I would have to reinstall some of the software over again.

I installed the update, but continued to use the older kernel because I didn't have the time to configure the new one just yet.  I finally got around to booting up the new kernel the other day and to my surprise everything except the ATI 3D driver was working.  Even ndiswrapper.  I checked the ATI website for a driver, and found the newer 8.27.10 driver.  I tried to use the pretty GUI automated installer that came with it, but it didn't work, as I expected.

But I was able to use the automasted installer to generate Ubuntu packages of the driver.  Then I scoured these forums for the same tutorial that I had used to installer the older version of the driver on the older kernel.  Once I found that I realized that the process was exactly the same.  Only the file names were different.  After some mistakes, some new kernel restricted modules, and about 30 minutes I had the new drivers installed and was running XGl on the new kernel. :D 

Now obviously the process isn't perfect.  It is still much easier to install a new video card driver in Windows, but Ubuntu has made TRMENDOUS strides in the right direction, due in large part to the people on these forums.  I think the largest reason it isn't easy on Linux is because of the proprietary nature of the software and it just takes more time to get things working properly.

Great job guys!  I now have full faith the Ubuntu is here to stay on my laptop.
