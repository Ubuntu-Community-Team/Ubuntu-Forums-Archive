Majority as defined by what - Windows, Mac? When put in perspective an  OOB experience that is unalterable (or minimally) could be defined as  that which the masses have come to expect. By permitting all to select their theme of choice, one eliminates that  OOB experience. It also eliminates the need to make a concerted effort  to determine the masses choice, which is finicky and always in flux. Basing their choice  on that which they believe is the choice of the masses.

Knowing that tomorrow the  masses may change their proverbial mind. Should Ubuntu/Linux conform to  that of the OOB experience of Windows, or should it set a new standard  of creativity and imagination? One that is defined only by the user and  not by the so-called masses.

Ubuntu/Linux is not a Windows clone and when one decides to try Ubuntu,  IMHO the Windows experience should be left at the door. As it simplifies  the learning curve and opens one's mind to new concepts.
