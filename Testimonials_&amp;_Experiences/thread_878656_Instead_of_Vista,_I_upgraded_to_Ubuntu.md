### Post by comwiz7 on 2008-08-03
I've been using Windows for as long as I can remember. I work at a high school as a system administrator for a Windows XP network. I used XP at home for around five years. I felt like I new that operating system inside and out.

Then Vista came out and I felt the pressure to upgrade. None of my apps worked well in Vista and I knew XP so well, most everything in Vista pissed me off. Plus, I was confronted with hardware/driver issues and my machine became slow as dirt. The worst issue was that I couldn't get the wireless to work because the manufacturer decided not to support Vista on older cards.

At that point my only experience with Linux was OpenSUSE version 8 or 9 and it had been an _extremely_ unpleasant experience(Dependency Hell). Despite this, I went online and downloaded Ubuntu 8.04 and booted it. It automatically recognized all my hardware including the wireless adapter and I was online immediately. I hadn't planned on installing it but I was so ticked with Microsoft that I blew away my disk and installed Ubuntu. 

I have since used Ubuntu mainly while booting into XP every once in a while for proprietary apps. I am making an effort become an expert in Linux like I am on Windows. I have to say, I am extremely impressed with the open source community and I really hope that this is eventually Microsoft's downfall.
