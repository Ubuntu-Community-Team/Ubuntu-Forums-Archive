### Post by nzjethro on 2011-06-19
While you might not recommend 11.04 to your mother, I'm sure she or any other newbie (and I apologise for assuming your mother is a newbie :p) could handle 10.10 or 10.04 both of which are absolutely rock solid. 11.04 was released two months ago, meaning it is still having all the bugs ironed out. Believe me, it was a lot buggier a month ago, and I'm sure that a couple of months from now it'll be fantastic. 10.10 is just starting to get solid now. For a newbie to Ubuntu, it is probably good to recommended one upgrade old (i.e. use 10.10 now, and then switch to 11.04 when 11.10 comes out in October) to give the bugs time to come out, or even run the tried and true long term support (LTS) release which comes out every 2 years (so 8.04, and 10.04, with 12.04 being the next proposed LTS).

As for stopping bugs in 11.04, make sure all of your drivers are up to scratch, and if bugs do arise, either find a fix from the friendly UF team here, or else report the error to the Ubuntu development team so they know that it is an issue. :D

### Post by Shippou on 2011-06-19
Hello. 

What exactly are those problems? You only said that you have random crashes and hangs, but that description does not offer really much about the problem(s) you are having.

For example, maybe it's because of Flash? I have experienced Firefox (and Arch hanging) on a low-spec machine due to 2 flash videos simultaneously playing. Or maybe because you have just upgraded the kernel and forgot to reboot? Though not necessary IMHO, it can be a cause. Or maybe you've just executed sudo rm -rf? I've done that once; it was fun. You get the idea.

Now, I am not an Ubuntu user; I've abandoned it long ago in favor of Arch, which is geared towards what you call as "power users", and what I call as "intermediate to advanced users". However, that does not mean I don't experience problems; in fact, I was unable to use it for the first three days due to network configuration errors.

You have mentioned that you recently upgraded your box, and I think that's the cause of your problems. Ubuntu is not a rolling-release distro (as of the moment), thus upgrading rather that installing from scratch in order to get the latest version can, and IMHO will, cause problems. This is not FUD, but one of the most common problems encountered not only by newbies such as yourself (considering your bean count; sorry if that denotes otherwise) but also by longtime Ubuntu users. That's one reason I've moved over to rolling-release distros, specifically Arch Linux, because I do not mind running bleeding-edge software and I hate reinstalls just to get the latest version of my operating system. (BTW, I am typing this using Firefox 5 beta.)

Also, may I ask where are you getting your answers from? You mentioned you are editing system files just to fix things. Now, isn't that, considering your bean count, a bit dangerous and overkill? Maybe editing those system files is what have caused the reboot thingies you have been experiencing. Yes, Linux is also well-known for its cryptic command line and config stuff, but I think editing system files is not a must for Ubuntu. Believe me, I have messed my systems more than once by editing/eradicating system files. As of now, it is the ALSA config, and I haven't been able to simultaneously use sound from more than one application because of that.

Again, considering your bean count, I don't want to think of you as a troll, but I don't, and maybe cannot, know. I just hope that your problems will be solved whichever way you prefer.
