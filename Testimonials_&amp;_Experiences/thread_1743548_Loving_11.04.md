### Post by Pirate Zoro on 2011-04-29
First things first: I couldn't stand Unity when I started with Maverick NBR. It was ugly, non-responsive, and took up WAY too much space on the screen(no autohide). I always used GNOME, it just worked better. About a week back, I started using Natty beta2, and while it was buggy, I was surprised how much they had done with Unity. It looked better, worked better, and I loved the fact that you could hide it. Yesterday, I upgraded to the final release, and despite some trouble (upgrade failed TWICE), I love it. I still prefer GNOME, but have resisted the urge to use it so far. Unity has come a long way and I can't wait to see what they do with it from here. Judging from the posts on here, it looks like I may be in the minority, but I just had to say it. I love Unity now.
