### Post by rmccutchan on 2010-02-20
I just built my first computer today!  I was using an old stock Dell with a Pentium 4/2.8ghz.  I now have an AMD Phenom II 3.1ghz on an Elitegroup A785GM-M motherboard, 2 gigs of ram, and a Thermaltake 430w power supply, and a Western Digital 500 gig blue series hard drive and a Rosewill case with 3 fans, and the front fan has blue led lights.  Total cost:  $300 USD

All went smooth, and I plugged in the Ubuntu disk, installed it, updated it, turned on all the goodies (rotating cube, etc....) and had absolutely no troubles.

I have installed Ubuntu on about 10 old machines in the last couple of months, but this was my first ever build-from-scratch system.

I'm sure for many of you this is just normal.  But I was a little anxious when I started.

Maybe Windows would have been just a smooth, but it was INCREDIBLY simple with Ubuntu.  Ubuntu noticed I needed a driver for the on-board graphics system, so it asked me if I wanted to install it.  I said "yes", and a minute later, done. Ubuntu recognized everything else, and all works flawlessly.

Again, many thanks to the Ubuntu team for ANOTHER painless Linux install!  It is so simple, even a caveman could do it. :-)
