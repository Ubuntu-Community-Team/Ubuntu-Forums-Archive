### Post by Comhra on 2008-10-31
I really think that the developers should consider giving people a more attractive out of the box GUI simply because newcomers do not realise that they can customise everything in Ubuntu (right down to the kernal, for God's sake).  The default colours are not the most appealing and I'm sure that they put some people off, esp. those coming from a Windows or Mac background.  

Maybe it's time that those who do such a fine job fostering Ubuntu took a look at some other well known Distros that look really great, such as: Mandriva One, Sabayon, GOS, and PCLOS to name but a few.

I know people will say that everything including the fonts in Firefox can be changed but the first time user generally doesn't know that.  The 'Dark Room' theme in Ibex is a step in the right direction but it doesn't go far enough.

Surely it wouldn't take that much to include one or two well designed themes & Icon sets?

There are plenty good ones available and many capable Ubuntu enthusiasts who would be willing to do the job, indeed there are loads of people who would love to be associated with the advancement of this superb Distro but who lack the programming skills.  Some of these have excellent graphical talents.  Why not put that talent to good use?

I know Mark Shuttleworth is thinking along those lines judging by recent statements.

It would appear that the priorities of the Ubuntu team are to get things right under the hood and that's very laudable, it's what makes Ubuntu great.  This is the philosophy that has brought us to the point where we now have the best release of all: Ibex.  With a little extra effort they could make a great distribution more inviting.  Ubuntu and indeed the Linux world generally would greatly benefit.
