### Post by Jouke74 on 2008-03-19
It's interesting to see the connection of "64 bit" computing and the two major problems that people have, are always mentioned in two sentences: Flash and Java. I know these are essential to many people (you really can't live without internet games and youtube I guess :-) ), but those two plugins can't be the only thing that define whether people are ditching 64 bit, can they? To me (yes my opinion) it means like not buying a car because the windscreenwipers are bad... seriously. I would simply not go out and drive on a rainy day for a while, and then replace them.

The 64 bit "problem" remains as long as some developers, company or not, don't want to write their software to 64 bit. They probably would given time and cash, however these are not free, *the software you use is*. If you look at the marketshare of 64bit Linux users in computerland it is also easy to see why this is not Adobe's main priority. Furthermore, for now 32 bit is not limiting most users in any way (you also don't really need 64 bit to surf the web do you?) so progression is slow.

With linux there always will be bugs & upgrades. People should realize how difficult it is for developers to keep track. E.g. if Flash is updated then the wrapper for Flash needs to be updated as well, this simply takes some time, and it's not like the Flash developing team is going to help those few Linux wrapper developers to get their act together. 

To be honest I am shocked that things are working so well, given the amount and various groups of people that have developed my Ubuntu system (32 bit or 64 bit). The true strength of GPL I guess. We may need to be just happy with the things we have..
