### Post by PapaGary on 2011-06-21
First let me say that I am not now a power user. I bought my first PC in 1991 (DOS & Win 3.0) and have owned several since. I started messing around with Linux ten years ago and have tried Puppy, Red Hat, Mandrake and now Ubuntu. I no longer play games and I don't do video or music editing. 

My original trial of Linux was to have a laptop that I could take on vacation to access the internet and get email. At the time I was a moderator on a couple of forums and I wanted to keep abreast of everything going on. My major frustration with Linux was that I was unable to get a wireless card to work with any of them. And then I bought a magazine that had an Ubuntu 10.04 disc attached. I tried it with two Dell laptops and it worked on both. I installed 10.04 on the used Dell I bought for $175 and felt I was golden. The reason it was so cheap was that it occasionally wouldn't boot (XP Pro) but on Ubuntu it seemed to work most of the time. 

Then I saw a Toshiba laptop on sale at Best Buy for $399. Investigation revealed that it was built for Best Buy to be marketed as a kid's PC and had a MSRP of $581, Best Buy sold them at Christmas for $499 and since they had a lot left over dropped the price this Spring to $399 (they are now down to $349). A visit to Crucial found memory for $22 for a 2G chip and $93 for two 4G chips. So, I bought one, had a second 2G memory chip in it within a week and played with Win 7 HP for another week (taking all of the kids programs off of it). I then made a pendrive installer for Ubuntu 10.10 64 bit and did a dual boot install... Wait a minute! Wireless didn't work and I had no sound. RATS! I tried installing a driver for my RealTec wireless with no joy and was getting ready to get rid of Ubuntu when I noticed in Update Manager when I was on my cheap Dell that 11.04 was available . So, I thought I would give it a try on the Toshiba. I fired up the Toshiba (hooked to an ethernet cable) and went to Update manager and clicked on 11.04. Well, sometime later it finished and when I booted it up... a miracle happened! I had WIRELESS! I HAD SOUND! Now I've even gotten my wireless Canon printer to work. CompizConfig Settings Manager allowed me got the size of the Unity bar reduced and I now really like Unity. Ubuntu forever!

(Sorry this is so long.)
