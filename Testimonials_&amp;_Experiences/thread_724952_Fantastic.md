### Post by oli888 on 2008-03-15
I've been tempted to switch to Linux for years. I've tried LiveCDs and even installed it, but the bugs, lack of applications and poor hardware detection have been real problems. A few years ago, my copy of XP came broken beyond recovery, and as it came preinstalled, I don't have a recovery disk. This meant that I would have to pay the M$ toll again.

I had tried previous Ubuntu releases, and because the computer I had tried was by an obscure manufacturer, hardware detection was poor at best. I bought a new laptop, and I fired up Gutsy. Although it involved a lot of work, I got it working thanks to the *incredible* support that you provide here.

After everything was working, the system has run like a dream. It's been stable (except one crash, but that was beta software I installed), the desktop is extremely easy to use. The package installation is perfect, and I love the idea of the menus being categorized by the type of application, determined by the software developers.

Personally, I think hardware detection is the only obstacle remaining for Linux's widespread desktop adoption. Once this is up-to-scratch (which I imagine that it will be soon, considering the amount of progress Linux has made in the last couple of years), I can't see anything preventing Linux on the desktop.

Thanks for providing the great OS - out of all the ones I tried, this is by far the easiest to use.
