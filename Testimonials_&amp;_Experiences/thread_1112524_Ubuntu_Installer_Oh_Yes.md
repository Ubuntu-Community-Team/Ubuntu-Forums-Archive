### Post by WatchingThePain on 2009-03-31
Diary of a Distro Hopper:

Hello,

Just want to say that I have tried loads of Distros and I find that the Ubuntu installer is the absolute best.

As an ex windows user I highly rate the Ubuntu installer as it usually works out of the box, leaving very little configuration to the user. I like the options for easy Hard drive partitioning. A lot of installers fail on this as they are hard to fathom or manipulate. I think other distros can learn from the way the Ubuntu installer works.

I really love the restricted hardware drivers menu in Ubuntu. Is that the most easy graphics configuration or what?.
Last week I tried installing a graphics driver on freebsd (or Belinix can't remember) and had to abort lol.

At present I use Ubuntu but also Arch Linux. Setting up the desktop environment on Arch was not really easy. The thing I find with Arch is that they have good documentation.

Ubuntu 8.10 is one solid OS.
