 Thank you for sharing you experience/feedback. You should be given  credit for your efforts and persistence. As for Kubuntu (KDE), I'm not a  fan. However, IMHO that's one of the  true benefits of Linux - Choice. If you've found the present  version of Kubuntu unacceptable, then I would suggest you remain in the  environment in which your comfortable - until a future version of  Kubuntu can meet with your expectations. 
 
 I have no idea how many people investigate Ubuntu's hardware  compatibility prior to downloading the iso. With users now doing their  own install, I believe if hardware compatibility  is a mandate (as some in this thread have implied) that potential new  users should be informed in advance. Let  them know that hardware compatibility may determine individual system  performance.
 
 For example, [this]("http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/why-use-ubuntu"):
 >  **It’s easy to trial and install**

   **You don’t have to be an expert. Ubuntu’s quick and easy to get started.**

  

IMHO there should be a disclaimer at the start of the installation,  notifying users that hardware compatibility may impact their success.  Should a user continue with the install they would have been warned and  made aware that perhaps further investigation is warranted. This also  applies to confirming vendor warranties. If one doesn't need to be an  expert, then logic might suggest they at least be notified.

I think it's foolish to tell a member posting a thread in this section  that - it works for me. How does that improve their position, help them  cope with or understand their issue(s)? IMHO when a potential new user comes  here to ask for support or share their experience, the community should  welcome them - not dismiss them. I'm just glad they're at least open to  trying Ubuntu/Linux.

Just my $0.02
