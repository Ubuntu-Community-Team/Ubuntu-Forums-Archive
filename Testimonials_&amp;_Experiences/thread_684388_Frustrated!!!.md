### Post by mzembe on 2008-02-01
I have the opposite problem, windows works with current hardware often. When people bring me their old computers, I will try 95,98,XP and still can't get the video or sound to work. The companies won't have the drivers or won't exist anymore and ubuntu generally has a working driver. Right now I have an issue with a newer laptop, nvidia provides the latest video drivers for linux but offers no drivers for XP on mobile chips - you have to get old drivers from Dell. Two laptops actually, both nvidia. Video works with windows, but I'm playing CoD4 and can't turn up all the settings! 
Wireless issues can be a problem though, the wireless cards can be like software controlled radios and the companies don't want to be liable if someone tries to boost their range or otherwise violate FCC regulations so we have to load firmware to operate them in different modes (usually a binary file located in /lib/firmware) - it's not always available as a separate file for linux, but... intel does, for their recent cards *and* for the cpus (you can now get microcode for linux from their site) and many other companies warming up to linux so maybe all that will change soon.
