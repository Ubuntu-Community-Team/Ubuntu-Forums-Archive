### Post by Nightstrike2009 on 2010-06-24
I recently tested out Kubuntu 10.04 after using Ubuntu 10.04 (Happy with Ubuntu but was seeking a new experience). I have since returned to Ubuntu 10.04 and will never return to Kubuntu.

I found that my 3G Vodafone Modem didn't show a set up wizard, forcing me to perform manually set up (not good for noobs), and generally had a bad experience with it, programs installed but could find no sign of a launcher anywhere.  I left again for Ubuntu 10.04 (Within one day)

I am not trying to bash the Kubuntu guys but work needs doing on their system in my opinion (and I agree with Linus Torvald's comments on it now, after trying it out), its supposed to be a different GUI but my personal experience of it was terrible it was more like learning a new OS (apart from terminal or "Konsole" as they call it).

On the plus side they have some ace programs but after this I will be using Ubuntu 10.04 to run them, and whats with all the "plasma" widgets it reminds me too much of Windows XP/Vista the way its laid out.

*PS: Any opinions expressed are my own, these KDE guys definately need user feedback Kubuntu 10.04 is an LTS and my personal experience was a nightmare, it was too complex for no good reason. Xubuntu seems to have it right though I like their Distro, simple and powerful (but I am sticking with Ubuntu 10.04).*
