### Post by feenstrovski on 2008-03-10
Hello!

I'm new to the Linux world, and here is my story how i was a convert to Ubuntu.
I currently have an HP Pavilion dv9428 laptop with only 1gb of ram (booooo) and a crappy 256 graphics card (not dedicated) which was pre installed with Windows Vista premium 32 bit. Lets be to the point about it Windows Vista looks very good, and has not much else going for it. Vista would crash every time I booted so i gave up and installed Ubuntu.

_Installation_ 10/ 10
The installation process was incredibly easy to manage. Booting the live CD was tricky and slow but was easy enough for my self to eventually figure out. The installation took a matter of 22 minutes, and I was surfing the internet during the mean time too. For me the installation may have been a little easier because I did a clean installation.
I found the LIVE cd very nice because it let me have a glimpse of what Ubuntu was without ruining my already ruined system.  ;) Overall I found the installation process to be perfect

_Desktop_ 9/10
I found the Desktop to bee very nice looking and easy to use. Gnome looks great, and although i have not given KDE a chance it does seem to me to be the simplest safest optionn for first time users.

One issue I did have was when i minimized a program it was not seen on the task bar (or whatever you call it) tray, but I fixed that thanks to this forum. The installation provided all the necessary programs  that you need right of the bat; such as OpenOffice, media players, Firefox, Evolution mail and the list goes on.

_Drivers_ 7/10
I love how Ubuntu installed the majority of the drivers very quickly. The Restricted Drivers Manager automatically detected the proprietary drivers for my hardware and installed them. I did have several issues with the proprietary drivers for my Broadcom Wireless card. The Restricted Drivers Manager claimed to have the driver installed but in reality it didn't work. I eventually figured it out with a lot of searching.

_Installing Programs_  6/10
I do not think that installing programs was difficult to do, but it was difficult to understand at first. The concept of packages and shell files which are hidden to the average user. These concepts were ones you needed to understand before you can see the full potential power of Linux systems. The Synaptic Package Manager has become a very nice tool to use kinda easy to use if you know what you are looking for. 

In terminal the apt-get command is one I avoided unless I had a full tutorial in front of me. 

_Issues_
I do however have issues still but it is more likely out of my limited understanding of Linux systems that these problems exist.

1. When I plug in USB key it does not mount at all. I do have *mount removable drives when hotplugged* checked but still nothing. It will mount if I boot with it plugged in. I  need a fix or at the very least a command i can use in terminal to mount the drive.

2. Synaptic Package manager and Firefox both have a tendency of freezing on me. They do that grey screen fade thing. Any fixes would be great!

3. My wireless connection is little on the slow side. Generally it works great when I connect but after 20 -30 minutes it gets very slow and then it dies.

4. MPlayer flickers when playing a DVD or mp4 file. It will only do this on full screen but is annoying.

5. I suck at Linux. Well I guess i have to fix that one.

_Overall_
I am glad i made the switch to linux. The stability and the speed are so much better then that of Vista on the same machine (1/3 rd of the ram usage). Unfortunately i made the switch at a critical time before a major project was due for my Architecture program for University, and thus compounded the problems  of me needing things to work soon because of a deadline. I have come to appreciate what the Linux community is about, and what open source really means. Now I need to find a way to contribute to this community!

Dave Feenstra
