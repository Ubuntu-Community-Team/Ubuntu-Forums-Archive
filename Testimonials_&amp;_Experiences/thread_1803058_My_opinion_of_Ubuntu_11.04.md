### Post by mystika1 on 2011-07-12
Hi,
I am not new to linux but I am no expert either. I have been using linux for approx. 2 years now and have used several distros and all of them have been KDE(PCLinuxOS, Kubuntu, Debian, Aptosid, Chakra, Arch) Some of them were pretty easy to set up and others...not so easy. I have read lots of hateful remarks against Ubuntu on the forums of the other distros and it really doesn't make a lot of sense to me but that is not what I am concerned with here.

I was looking for something different as I have used KDE for so long that I thought I would give something else a try. Also, at the time I was using Chakra and just could not get my printer to work correctly. I use my printer everyday and I need it to work flawlessly. I don't have time to troubleshoot. I was a bit stand offish of ubuntu because it is touted as the noob distro and almost didn't download it. I am no noob and I am not a beginner either! I thought about opensuse or mint and even downloaded mint 11 and tried it out in virtualbox........something about it just wasn't right for me so I tried Ubuntu. It only booted in classic and I liked it alot. It was fast. I decided to install it. I had no problems but this time when it booted I was greeted with Unity instead. Yes I could have rebooted and changed over to classic as I was used to it but I guess I like to live dangerously.  It is very different from the classic desktop I saw before the install but.... I wanted something different so......

Yes it is slower than the classic desktop...no doubt about that but everything works. My printer installed in less than five minutes and prints better than ever. I even got boxee to work.(and it works great) I have been fighting to get boxee to work on my rig in debian...aptosid and chakra with no luck at all. No networking issues at all and my video card drivers installed in minutes instead of hours(I really had a hard time with drivers in debian) 

Then came the tweaks. I made the icons much smaller on the sidebar so they were not so obnoxious and replaced them with the things I use most. If you don't do this and try getting to your favorite programs via the applications icon on the sidebar you will find it takes alot more clicks/time to get to the things you want. It would be aggravating and quite frustrating. You have to tweak the bar to fit your needs. It is not as configurable as KDE or the old gnome but this desktop is in the infant stage right now and will get better IMO. Things also got better once I read about the shortcut keys. ctrl Alt T solved the big miystery of where is the terminal. :) 

I have been trying to get my husband to use linux to no avail.  This Unity Desktop Environment has cought his eye. It reminds him of his beloved iphone. This just might do the trick. I am going to be very happy the day I can delete Windows 7 from his machine.

All in all I.... am happy with my move to Ubuntu. I know some people are very frustrated and that is understandable considering that they lost their fav desktop environment as default ubuntu.... but I feel that if people are set Gnome users then pretty soon we will see Gnubuntu(a new gnome distro)born from this change to add to all of the other flavors of buntu's available. Only time will tell. 

Ubuntu runs on my rig better than all of the other distros so I will stck with what works. I like tinkering and getting into the guts of my machine but I need reliability. The buntu's work for me. I am sticking with it.:D

Sincerely,

Penny
