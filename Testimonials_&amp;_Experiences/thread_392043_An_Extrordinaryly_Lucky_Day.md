### Post by LermanetDOTcom on 2007-03-24
I bought an old Dell Latitude 610 laptop on Ebay for $250 a few days ago... it came with win2000... and  I installed Ubuntu and the dang thing worked!!

Everything worked!  But I did not have a wifi nic for the machine...

I did have an old laptop nic, a D-link DWL-520...

And i saw the weird little mini pci plug inside the old dell 610... for the internal Wifi card..
which was not there..  

Then  i looked at this old DLink desktop PCI card and saw from the circuit board pins that it looked like another card was plugged into a slot  and being a very old electronics tech.. I took the cover off... the D-link... and it contained a Wi-Fi INTERNAL LAPTOP CARD that fits old Dell Laptops!

If you have an old D-Link Air Plus  DWL-520+...
Wi-Fi card for a desktop.... and you have a needy old laptop...  
 take the two screws out and pull the D-Link Air Plus
 Logo cover off and throw it away

 Pull the card out of the mini PCI connector and
 Guess what?

  Pull off the antenna connector, and cut the wire
 ( it comes from the power supply )
 close to the circuit board with finger nail clippers

  I took the bottom cover off my old dell latitude 610
running Ubuntu, plugged it in,  and the dang thing WORKED!!

 Ubuntu ID's it as a Texas Instruments ACX 100 Internal Wireless Interface card and it WORKED in my old Dell Latitude 610

  PASS IT ON!
   It WORKS!

  I'm still blown at my incredible luck this evening

  apparently the old d-link pci card accepts and coverts
  laptop mini internal pci cards for desktop use..

  and Im blown away by Ubuntu, this was a very lucky evening 
  nothing has ever worked so well..
