Users parading around saying "linux is great" can, at the most, be classed as "hear-say" and nothing more. I'd like to think that people such as us who use and enjoy using Ubuntu shouldn't have to "preach" because we should realize that if you don't enjoy using Ubuntu, you probably won't use it for long.  I myself have converted a few people to Ubuntu, but usually they come to me to ask after having played around on my laptop, or on a liveCD for a bit.  Also, preaching isn't really marketing, as it's just often aimed at putting down Mac or Windows, and it all comes down to perspective.  If you'd ask me if Ubuntu was better than Mac, I'd say yes, not because it's easier to use, or if it runs smoothly, but because i disagree with mac's closed-source policy, the effects they sell off as a major part of their OS are easily done with compiz, and the reason that mac software costs so much.  Also, what do you define as "just works"? Every OS i've ever used has always required some tweaking (in Vista it was disabling the UAC and setting up Samba support in the registry), in XP it was the annoying tooltips disappear behind the taskbar thing, and a few other issues, in Ubuntu it happens to be getting the drivers and such.  But once you get the drivers, Ubuntu keeps running for a long time, since i've had XP i've had to re-install it every year, since something broke and wasn't worth fixing, but in Ubuntu i've had it on my PC for years and only installed it once with 6.06 and been upgrading since then, that, for me, classes it as "just works".  Also, the repos are packed with free software, which "just works" when you download and install it.  And the major issue is, Ubuntu does offer what it preaches, because it neither preaches nor markets itself. That's like saying "I'm a genius" and then being blamed for you then not being able to figure something out.

Last point:  Hardware that no longer works in 8.04?  Well, i'm one of the unlucky people who have these problems, my ATI card has decided to lag terribly if i use anything but the restricted driver without direct rendering, the wireless card in my laptop was supported by the b43 driver in 2.6.24-12, but lost support after that because of the patch removing support for other cards, etc. etc.  So the problem is that hardware advances so fast that to keep support for newer hardware, sometimes you have to remove support for the older, since the reverse-engineered drivers often conflict, why is this? it's because manufacturers don't see Linux as a worth-while OS to build drivers for.  Want support back for those?  Install Debian, it supports hardware from ages ago, or try to get the manufacturers to release drivers for their hardware that will get rid of reverse-engineered driver conflicts.  As for the hardware that doesn't work anymore, there are probably ways of getting it working again (e.g. use ndiswrapper with the windows driver for my bcm4311 rev 2 card).  Sorry if this seems like a flame, but i'm frustrated at an essay i'm trying to get done, and these kinds of threads annoy me to no end, because people never seem to be happy to try something new when they realize they will have to learn if they want to use it...
