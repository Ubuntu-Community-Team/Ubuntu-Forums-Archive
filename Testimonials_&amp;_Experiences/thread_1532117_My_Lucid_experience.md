### Post by Screwdriver0815 on 2010-07-16
Hi,

first of all, I have to admit that I have done a Kubuntu Rant already in the Cafe, but I think it is worth it, to write another Testimonial about Lucid. Maybe a more polite one... ;)

Okay... so here it goes.

I am using Ubuntu and Kubuntu since 2008. Actually I started the first trials back in 2006, but at this time I did not succeed. 
Anyway, my first Ubuntu, which I actually used (instead of just trying) was Hardy Heron (8.04). 
I switched over to it after a huge effort on collecting information and solutions for my existing hardware. The result was simply great! Hardy worked like a piece of cake. It ran like nothing else I have seen before. And, this is the topic I am targeting, it was stable like a rock. Not just stable, it was unbreakable.

After that, Intrepid came out and you clearly could see the progress. Intrepid was as stable as Hardy, but faster and had more features and a better Hardware support. At this time I thought: nothing can stop the development and when it goes on in this pace, we (the whole Linux community, not just Ubuntu) will leave everything behind us.

After that, Jaunty came out and I had some kind of mixed feelings towards it. It was not bad, but it was something like a slow down. Of course it worked great and was even faster than Intrepid. But it had some things I did not like at all: notify-osd and the change to the update manager, which causes it to automatically open and when you close it, it is gone in a way, the normal user does not understand. Anyway, Jaunty was a good one too.

Then Karmic came out and with it the "styling over usability" attitude at Ubuntu. I mean, a nice looking system is priceless. That's for sure. But when something is just styled to look nice, and at the same time the value for the user drops in the same level, the styling factor increases, then something is wrong, in my opinion. 
In my opinion the real challenge is to style something nicely and AT THE SAME TIME to enhance the usability and the value for the user.
In Karmic this was not completely the case. For example the bootscreen did not show the progress of the bootprocess anymore. This some kind of important feature was sacrificed for some styling...

So now we switch over to Lucid...
In Lucid even more practical things were sacrificed for the styling. Even the stability. 
Lucid has a completely new bootscreen. This screen is just black and at the end of the boot process a pixelated picture shows up. This heap of pixels shows "Ubuntu"... In the meantime the normal user is scared like hell, because he does not know whether the system is booting up or if it is stuck somewhere! The old "ugly" bootsplash's of Hardy, Intrepid and Jaunty at least showed that something was happening! This is the goal too: interact with the user, show him whats going on. 
Lucid also has new themes. These themes are designed to have the window buttons on the left side. No problem at all. If you don't like it, just switch them to the right hand side. But when you switch between themes, you always have to switch back the window buttons. This is annoying. Annoyance, grown on the field of styling. No enhancement of the user experience, just styling which comes along with annoyance. 
All this is then topped up with no more stability.
Recently I have done a performance test, which I also did with all the other Ubuntu versions before. Its simple:  copying 20 Gb of files, installing some software and watching a movie at the same time. 
With Hardy, Intrepid and Jaunty the system did not care at all. It just did what was to be done. Karmic showed a jerky movie, but at least it worked. And in Lucid all this ended up in an epic crash. Congratulations!
I also experienced some other crashes in Lucid: Evolution during fetching mails, Rhythmbox during reading the music folder... simple things... not acceptable. Kubuntu is even worse than that. Refer to my rant for more ;)

So my result is: Ubuntu has sacrificed the most essential parts of the user experience, which means: get done what you want to get done!! ---> this is the most important task for an operation system!!! <--- on the battlefield of styling. 

I mean: I like all these new things, Mark is thinking about and I also like the new notification area and so on. But this shall not lead into a situation in which the focus is lost. The focus in my opinion still has to be on the system itself. The system is the thing which helps the user to get things done. It can do that while looking ugly, and it can do that while looking average and it can do that while looking nice. But it HAS to do that! It absolutely needs to do that! 

You can not say: "I style the **** out of it now and I brush the remaining stuff aside. I don't give a damn about stability and usability, I just wanna style now". Of course you can do that. When you are a hobbyist. But when you want to be the best, you can not act like that. You need to do both: styling and stability/usability. 

In my eyes, Lucid is not the right direction. Ubuntu has to find a way which combines looks and stability again. 

So I am deeply disappointed from Lucid and I wish Intrepid was still supported... I am normally not a guy who always looks back. But this mess, called Lucid leads me to that. It's a pity.

So please, you guys at Canonical and Ubuntu: please think about the future of Ubuntu! Do you want it to become an fragile Diva, or a strong beauty?
