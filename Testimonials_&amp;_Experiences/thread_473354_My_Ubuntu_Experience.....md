### Post by james_2002uk on 2007-06-13
Well, I'm pretty new to Linux, in fact Ubuntu is my very first distro - I did try out a knoppix live cd a while back but my laptop is seriously old and I couldn't get some stuff like audio, video etc to work.

About 3 weeks ago I was just minding my own business, playing solitaire on XP having finished my last exams ever and feeling ever so contented when all of a sudden... Blue Screen!... What... My hard drive is corrupt... What do you mean I can't get back any of my files!! Yes my computer totally melted and to make matters worse my cd drive has been busted for months so no chance of reinstalling XP.

Then I had a bright idea (well for me), using my knoppix live cd (on another computer) I got the network boot image of Ubuntu and extracted it to a USB drive, back to my computer, installed Ubuntu (with downloading all the files) in about an hour and my computer was totally fine (I say fine, I'm missing well over a quarter of the keys on my keyboard - I did have one problem, the menu.lst file still pointed to my USB stick so had to change a letter). 

So I upgraded to Linux by virtue of it being the one chance to salvage my computer, I still couldn't get my files back but I'm still pleased.

Ubuntu is really refreshing... I was initially a bit confused by the two panels but a few weeks later and I really love the layout. The menus are less cluttered and more organised and actually make XP look a bit messy. I've tried KDE via kubuntu-desktop, KDE vs Gnome is tough, KDE is way more customisable but I like the default look of Gnome better, but really, why are there so many apps starting with a K, It just means that when I'm looking through the menu I have to take more time to make sure I get the right one, and also my girl friend would know i'm a geek (for some reason KDE feels more geeky... i don't know) and i think she already suspects it anyway. Anyway... The best thing about Ubuntu is...

apt-get - it is the single most useful program I have ever used - Its actually turned me into a software junkie, there just isn't an analogue in the windows world, its brilliant... all the software you could ever need a command (or a click) away! There are exceptions of course, some software I actually had to google for, but it seems so old fasioned doing it the windows way.

I suppose to balance out my semi-review i'd have to find something i didn't like about Ubuntu... umm.. the defualt brown looks better in pictures i've seen on the internet then it does on my old laptop screen, gnome is more difficult to customise than KDE (I had to write - well copy - a script to change the panel font colour to white to look good with my dark background and transparent panels), and thats about it - I don't play allot of games and I don't use photo shop so none of that worries me. Everything works fine and I didn't have to do a thing - except get audio/video codecs but i'm aware that is more a legal thing than a technical failing

Ubuntu is really good and more than ready for the Desktop (as far as i can tell)!
