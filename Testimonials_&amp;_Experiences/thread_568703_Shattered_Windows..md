### Post by Adamaeus on 2007-10-06
I have been using Ubuntu for a little over two months now; I first installed it on an older computer (the very one I'm using at this moment), to see what all the fuss was about. 

And surprise, surprise, Ubuntu 7.04 runs as well on an old computer as Vista runs on a -new- one. (Well, almost. It runs more steadily, at any rate.) It has been almost three weeks since I had to restart my system, and everything I did on my Windows box, I can do in Ubuntu. Internet, word processing, a few Flash games here and there; it all works, and easily so.

There's some tinkering to be done when you first start up Ubuntu; but it's easier than with Windows, because there's the massive, beautiful community that keeps Ubuntu going strong. Microsoft cannot rival this, because you guys at Canonical and the users generally are so devoted to making Ubuntu a fantastic OS. And you're doing a wonderful job. 

I have no desire to go back to a Windowed world; I am keeping a Windows machine only because my family needs one. I myself, do not, and have no inclination to go near that security-hole-ridden cesspool of an OS ever again. 

Thank you, Canonical; and I thank the many thousands who contribute to Ubuntu to make it what it is. 

I requested and received the free CDs; and I'm going to be passing them around to friends of mine, and insisting that they pass them on once they're finished with them. 

I am a basic computer user; and so are most of my friends. I don't need a complicated OS. What I need is simplicity, and Ubuntu gives me that; and it still has enough flexibility to become as complex as I would ever conceivably need it to be. I love the security. I love the dependability. I love the community. 

I love Ubuntu.

### Post by kshane on 2007-10-06
I will join in on this...  I have been using computers since about 1984.  I learned BASIC at that time and wrote a few programs for the company that I worked for.  Nothing fancy, just some inventory control stuff.  Then I started a computer BBS.  Through this I met a lot of really terrific people.  My first OS was DOS(3?).  When I bought my first PC, it came with Windows 3.1.  I played with every version of windows since then up to Server 2000.  This past February(?), I bought Vista.  I was excited and was looking forward to the "***Wow!***" Bill Gates promised.

Well, I was *NEVER* "**Wow**ed"...  From the moment I tried to load it, *Vista was a problem*!  Most of the games my wife and I had bought (yes, all paid for, **no pirated stuff**) would **not** run under Vista - not without a lot of tweaking, if at all.  My video card (the most expensive I have ever owned) wouldn't work right.  This particular computer was very fast, had a ton of ram, a fancy video card, cool wireless mouse - everything I wanted.  ***But Vista brought it to a _CRAWL!_***

In May I found Ubuntu.  Ah!  What a breath of fresh air!  ***It just worked! *** **Everything**!  Only thing I had to do was to click on the place to enable my ATI drivers.  It was smooth and fast and ***ROCK SOLID! *** I called Microsoft and told them I wanted my money back for Vista and that they had no business selling that mess until it was "ready for prime time", which it most certainly is not.  The young lady on the other end of the line told me it would be no problem to refund my money.  Shock!  Disbelief!  Then she asked when I had first contacted Technical Support.  I told her I had *never* contacted technical support.  *In twenty years they had never been able to help me*, so I never bothered.  I was far more adept at dealing with Windows problems that any of their tech support people were.  She then informed me that ***there was nothing they could do for me! ***

**THAT** was the final and complete end to any relationship with Microshaft and the beginning of a wonderful relationship with Ubuntu Linux!  I have tried several other flavors of Linux (Mint, Suse, Slackware, Mandriva, etc.), all of which were okay, but ***Ubuntu just rocks!***

**So, thanks, Ubuntu!  I am here for the long haul!**

Kevin
