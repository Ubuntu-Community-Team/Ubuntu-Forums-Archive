### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-13
At least on some systems, **[COLOR="Blue"]PulseAudio[/COLOR]** kills sound, and the only way to have sound in Xubuntu 10.04 - for some of us anyway - has been to completely remove PulseAudio and use only ALSA.

It's too bad. I've read that PulseAudio (when it's ready for old hardware like mine) will offer some pretty cool advantages. I can appreciate the rush to include it in a long-term-support version because of the advantages it has when it works. Just like Grub2 - when it works, it's great; when it doesn't, it's a major hassle even for experienced users.

I really think that the default software in the LTS releases should be more stable at release time, but that's just my opinion.

And the new [COLOR="Blue"]**Thunderbird**[/COLOR] is so completely different from the old one! As soon as it had my G-mail account info, it almost instantly created a half-dozen new folders and began a huge download to fill them up - including "spam" and "trash!" What, downloading the trash?!? I stopped it, went to g-mail in the browser and cleaned up the mess without downloading it. I liked my *old* Thunderbird! Can I get it back somehow? In the meantime I've found SeaMonkey (configured to open into the Mail & News window on start-up) and it works very much like Thunderbird 2.0 except for the Lightning extension.

Replaced Brasero (which always made coasters) with Xfburn, and all is well. Xubuntu is nice again, with PulseAudio removed and a few applications exchanged. The best thing about it is that I notice **no discernible difference in speed and performance between Xubuntu and Lubuntu** on my old hand-me-down Dell Dimension with it's scant little 512 megs of RAM! I get all the way-cool advantages and features of Xfce (that awesome panel with it's applets and opacity) with no loss of speed. Xubuntu rawks! After a few changes.

Your mileage may vary,
Robin
