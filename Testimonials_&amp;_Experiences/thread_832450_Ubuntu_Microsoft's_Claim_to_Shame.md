### Post by daltonlaffs on 2008-06-17
I've been using Ubuntu for two or three weeks now as my primary Operating System (with Windoze Vista still back there in the tiny 3rd partition just in case), and I've got quite a few things to share about my experience so far, as well as why I never want to touch Windoze again.

[SIZE="6"]1)[/SIZE] The Internet

When I first installed Ubuntu, the built-in Broadcom wireless card my laptop had wouldn't function. I thought "whatever" and pulled out my old PCMCIA card, which is quite old and speedcaps around 100kbps. But it worked.

Then today, I found out about Ndiswrapper. I installed it successfully and loaded the Windoze driver for my Broadcom card, then took out my PCMCIA card and restarted. To my surprise, the integrated wireless works perfectly now... and it's FAST!

Here's a speed comparison:
Windoze with Broadcom: ~400kbps
Ubuntu with Zonet PCMCIA: ~100kbps
Ubuntu with Broadcom: ~550kbps (!!!)

So there you HAVE it! I looked at my download stats in disbelief as a 30MB file downloaded in less than a minute.


[SIZE="6"]2)[/SIZE] The Stability

This is probably the best reason to switch from Ye Olde Windoze to Ubuntu. Ubuntu almost NEVER crashes. The only crash I've had was when I accidentally pulled out a mounted external hard disk and tried to remount it (with a slightly malformed command), and since there were no problems caused, I still love Ubuntu.

Comparison:
Ubuntu crashes to date: [SIZE="4"]1[/SIZE]
Windoze crashes to date: [SIZE="4"]&#8734;[/SIZE] 

OK, so it wasn't infinity, but it might as well have been. I'd crash every few hours, and quite often while doing something important. And yes, I DID defrag once weekly. I DID run scandisk every other time I crashed. I DID use a half billion error fixing programs. But it wasn't enough for ol' Windoze.


[SIZE="6"]3)[/SIZE] Wine

Here's the really funny part about Ubuntu: It runs Windoze programs with the help of Wine, the "NOT AN EMULATOR" Windoze emulator, and if they crash, 99% of the time they just close without harming the stability of the system.

Wine: WIN
Windoze: FAIL



Of course, there are way more reasons! I just don't want to keep typing, I'm lazy like that.

RAWK ON, UBUNTU! :guitar:
