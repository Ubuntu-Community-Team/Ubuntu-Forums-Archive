### Post by jwbrase on 2009-03-26
> How do you pronounce Ubuntu?

Ubuntu, an African word from Zulu and Xhosa, is pronounced "oo-BOON-too".

Well, if you really want to get it right, you've got to transcribe it in IPA or X-SAMPA. In which case you get, for the original Zulu word, according to Wikipedia, [ù&#595;úntú] in IPA or [u_Lb_<u_Hntu_H] in X-SAMPA. (IPA is more human-readable, while X-SAMPA sticks to ASCII characters and is thus less likely to run into encoding problems).

The first syllable has a low pitch, and the other two have a high pitch. The voicebox is brought downward during the pronunciation of the b, creating a bit of suction when it is released. (This isn't an unnatural movement for English speakers, you do it whenever you sip on a drink, but we aren't used to doing it while speaking).
