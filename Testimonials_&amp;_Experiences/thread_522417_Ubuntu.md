### Post by Glake on 2007-08-10
**.... No it doesn't. But I got your attention didn't I ;) lol.**

Seriously, What do I think about Ubuntu? First I have a habit of being a bit direct at times and annoying people. So if you have another main distro that is your favorite you have two choices. Don't read beyond this point or read and respect where I am coming from.

When Ubuntu first came out, when it was very new, I was one of the people in a couple of other forums that said this was worth keeping an eye on. But at that time I was bouncing around from distro to distro.

My main favorites before Ubuntu where,

I started with SuSe (Before OpenSuSe, back in the 90's, can't remember who owned it then.)

Red Hat (Before Fedora.)

Mandrake (Stayed with this for awhile, Before Mandriva.)


Now I do like Fedora and OpenSuSe, but Ubuntu is better. I am trying Mandriva again on VMwareserver. Works fine but I am not impressed with it.

I also tried Peanut Linux (Before aLinux.,) College Linux, Puppy Linux, Arch, Feather, BLAG, CentOS, Debian (Could never get that to work right on my old PC,)  Xandros, Freespire (Also never worked out well on install,) Slackware, Solaris, Berry..... and the list goes on. Recently I tried DreamLinux and a few others.

Now my favorite live cd's are Knoppix, Damn Small Linux (Which I was very fond of when it first came out, and I still am. It is by far my favorite live cd.) and Wolvix.

I also like Gentoo and LFS (I have had success with installing Gentoo, but I am still learning with LFS.)

My all time favorite as of now, and hopefully will stay the same unless something stupid is done ;) is Ubuntu. But why???

In part, this is why.

1- Stable (I know, duh, it is Linux.)
2- Easy to set up 
3- It is based on Debian
4- Synaptic Package Manager

One thing I hope to see in the future is Automatix becoming standard with Ubuntu. When I tried Ubuntu CE before going with plain Ubuntu I had my first experience with Automatix. I decided to give two things a try. CrossOver and Vmware server (tried Virtual Box, to buggy for my taste.) I am currently using XP on vmware and I bought crossover (If it wasn't for Ubuntu AND Automatix I would have never bought it or it may have been a long time before I did.) Then comes Beryl..... It is just fun to play with, lol.

So while I like Ubuntu and think it is the greatest Linux Distro out there,  Vmware server, CrossOver and Automatix also played and equal part in me making Ubuntu my main OS. (Ubuntu, find a way to work more closely with these three.) Also you need better and more HAM packages :)

I have no fear of playing around, learning, etc, with Linux. But there are some things I just want to work right out of the box. I already got someone else interested in Ubuntu. In a few months it will be on a couple of other peoples computers as well. They are, or will be, duel booting but VMware server is great to use if they don't have any 3D graphic needs in XP (Games mostly.) The VMWare, Automatix, CrossOver, Ubuntu combo is great.

Software, as well as hardware, companies would be smart to work with CrossOver and Automatix. I am more then willing to buy software for Linux or at least software that works on CrossOver standard. For Ex. World of Warcraft works great on CrossOver (though they offer no official support for this at Blizzard.) I bought the Burning Crusade Exp. and I will buy the new Exp. when it comes out  in 2008. I bought Doom3 because it worked on Linux (though I was not happy with Doom3 as a game,) I bought the Platinum Edition of RTCW because that worked on Linux. I am more then willing to buy and pay, but I also believe some things should just be free. I don't have to spend an arm and a leg on an OS right now, or to join a club for software that is free, etc. Ubuntu just mailed me CD's for free (Though it did cost them for shipping.) What other distro does that, all of this? With the money I am saving   I can spend it on other software (for ex. Instead of buying Vista I bought CrossOver, cheaper and works great. Vista is expensive and barely works.)

So these are *some* of the reasons why I like and will keep using Ubuntu. With Automatix, if it has commercial software available, if I like it I will buy it. (Hopefully games companies see Automatix as a way to get their demos out there so people can try them before they buy them.)

I am running out of time. There is so much more I want to add but I have a few things to do and then I want to play around with Gutsy on the VMwareserver. Hey, Ubuntu team. How about extending support for 7.04 Feisty for another 6-12 months?


For security *some* of what I use is,

Rkhunter
Chkrootkit
Avast anti-virus
Firestarter
NmapFE


In short, **[COLOR="red"]Good Job Ubuntu![/COLOR]**
