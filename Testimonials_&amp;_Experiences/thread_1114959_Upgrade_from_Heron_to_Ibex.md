### Post by Hauk on 2009-04-03
I'm more of a lurker than a poster, but I just thought I'd throw this here.

I had not updated my desktop running Hardy in a long time, and now that I got some time I decided to update this machine.

So I updated Hardy Heron as much as I could. One or two restarts later and everything is still going smoothly, and I can't update the system any more.

That went perfectly. :)

So I set the software sources to update the Distro, and then loaded Update Manager.

I clicked 'Update', and it had 1.5GB to download so I went and made coffee and watched TV.

The download took about an hour, but it went great and started installing the new packages. The software installation took about 30 minutes but went smoothly.

Following this, it ran the clean-up on all the old programs installed and went perfectly.

I restarted the computer, and was met with my regular login screen.

Then I had to update the video driver to the Nvidia 180 version, but that was fine.

Restarted again, and I was back to my regular desktop with all the Intrepid Ibex features waiting for me.

A very painless update, which I am very impressed with how easy the Ubuntu team has made the upgrade process.

Very good work, and another reason why I love this OS.

Well done Ubuntu team :)

Hauk
