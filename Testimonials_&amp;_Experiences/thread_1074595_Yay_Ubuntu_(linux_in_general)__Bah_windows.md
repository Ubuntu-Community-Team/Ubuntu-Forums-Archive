### Post by TombKing on 2009-02-19
So the power supply on the spouses computer went toes up. At first we thought it was the monitor (could still be as it wont give us a no signal screen) then after getting a new one still nothing. The box is at least 6 years old so we are off to finally get the replacement for it that she has been pondering on and off for several months now.

Anyway I have a nice little SATA/IDE to usb adapter so I can pull all the data from the old drive. For a bit I couldn't even get the drive Vista kept hanging on reading the drive or just copying the files. I have read where it has issues with big files so I wasn't totally suprised. I gave up and booted to my linux drive and voila!

It still takes awhile as the drive was overdue a few years ago for a total wipe and reload but I can see and copy all the data needed without issues and I have more drive space on the linux drive as well. I can then move the needed files back to the vista drive and boot back to windows share out the folder and copy over to the new machine (note to self get out cables and use wired network next time).

I am pretty much OS agnostic and really have had no real beefs with Vista yet outside of the poor implementation of UAC or lets make an annoying version of sudo so far. I had read about the issues with large file transfers and figured that's what was biting me. Anyway it was nice to have the option of getting at the data with a second OS.
