### Post by goettlek on 2009-04-02
Well I'll start off by saying, I'm an avid musician. I like recording my own music, making drum beats and experimenting with synthesizers and stuff. However I was never really satisfied with it; I felt like I had to jump through too many hoops just to make a simple song. Then, I was trolling the Wikipedia page for Ubuntu when I spotted Ubuntu Studio. Bingo!

I downloaded it and burnt it straight to a CD and booted it up. After testing it out, I figured why not partition my hard drive and install. I'd wanted to do it for a while and was excited to finally do it. 

I'm not really a huge fan of GNOME, I'm usually more leaning toward KDE but it was easy to navigate and use so I figured it could stay. The best part was that most hardware worked right off the bat, no hassle with drivers or any Windows crap. However, I realized the internet wouldn't work. Nothing led to solutions after I searched the internet, and then I found that if I installed Kubuntu, I could type```
sudo apt-get install ubuntustudio-audio ubuntustudio-audio-plugins
```. That may not be exactly right but something to that effect. I whipped out my Kubuntu 7.10 disc and slapped it in the drive and installed it right away.

It was good for a while, but then the audio wouldn't work. Once again I tried for hours and hours but couldn't get any of the drivers to work or anything; everything was a dead end. Bummer. That's when I figured I'd try Kubuntu 8.10. Downloaded it, booted it, liked it, installed it.

I've been using it since and have been OK with it. KDE 4 is nice, but it seems too much like Windows Vista with a few wasteful features and eye candy (widgets for one, lack of typical desktop for another). Anyway, I liked KDE 3 a lot better so I'm thinking I might switch back to 8.04. I'll probably at least wait and try 9.04, even though it does have KDE 4.

So there, the end of my Linux **install** journey and the beginning of my **Linux** user journey!;)
