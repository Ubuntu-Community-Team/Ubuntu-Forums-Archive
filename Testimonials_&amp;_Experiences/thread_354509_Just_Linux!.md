### Post by DimitrisC on 2007-02-06
I finally took the big decision. I am switching all my computers to Linux.

I never thought I would do this but last night was the last chance i gave to Windows.  I always read about people in forums like this that have something bad to say about the Windows OS and always thought to myself that these people just like Linux and were just exaggerating making their problems look bigger than they are.

As far as i can remember, everytime i installed Linux it was always on a dual boot system with Windows XP.  Slowly i found myself booting to Windows less and less.  When i bought my laptop i thought that it was a great opportunity to install Linux and see how it does on its own without booting to windows for certain tasks.  Everything went great and i now have a beautiful Dapper & Beryl installation set up the way i want to.  But (yes there is always a but) i kept a Windows XP installation on my Desktop (No Linux there.....YET) for certain things not able (at least me) to do in Linux (if you really need to know, i am talking about easily copying dvds bypassing protection {always for legitimate reasons ofcourse ;-)}, webcam support in msn messenger and skype).

That was until last night.  Without doing anything that could potentially break an XP installation (install drivers and such) my PC just rebooted.  I thought it was just one of those sudden reboots we all have everynow and then (even in Linux i had a couple).  But when i tried to login to Windows i found out it was more than that.  I got a strange message something about the registry had been restored succesfully.  I clicked on OK and then i logged in.  In my suprise i found that all of my settings, favorites, documents were gone.  It was just like a fresh install of Windows XP.  All of my applications were there but not my settings.  It did not recognise me as the administrator but as a new user. Before i could even try and find the cause of the problem the pc rebooted once again. No errors, no messages, nothing. I have to mention here that i had NOD32 antivirus installed with all updates and a firewall and i never had problems with viruses in the past in case you are thinking that.  Then everytime i got to the login screen and tried to log in the pc would just reboot.

And ofcourse once again linux came to the rescue.  With a livecd i managed to save my files and with Terminal client tried to figure out the problem.  I could login the pc remotely with linux and use it fine but when i tried to login normaly it would just reboot.  

Under different circumstances it wouldn't bother me so much. What's frustrating is the fact that i did nothing to break the system and i got no errors or messages to at least help me identify the problem (not even those windows error messages that only the microsoft guys can read ---> you know error caused on 2349234-98404-2=cxxvxlkhs2-30942340- thing hehehe ;-).

Well i am writing this post from work and i just can't wait to go home and have a windows free computer at last.  I may have to make a few sacrifises but i don't care any more. I am done!
