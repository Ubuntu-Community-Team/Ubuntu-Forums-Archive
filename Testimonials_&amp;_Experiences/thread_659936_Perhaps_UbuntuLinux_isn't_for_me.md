### Post by carusoswi on 2008-01-06
Ok, I've read this sort of thread before from the perspective of one still positively engaged in making things work in Ubuntu.  I started with version 6.10, upgraded to and solved many problems in version 7.04, then, upgraded and lost most functionality in version 7.10.

The "community" has, apparently, succeeded in convincing developers of Automatix to toe the line, so that fix (which worked to get my wireless adapter working in 7.04) is no longer available.

So far, my wireless capability is nil in Gutsy, wine refuses to accept MS Office under Gutsy, and, without functionality in those areas, it doesn't make sense for me to continue trying to solve issues relating to my sound cards (that never worked properly in earlier Ubuntu implementations, my digital camera (Sony A100), and various attempts to achieve multimedia functionality in Gutzy (never achieved it in earlier verions, either)).

I am a tinkerer by nature, feel I have been patient waiting for functionality and interoperability to materialize.  I understand and appreciate that, in most cases, Ubuntu is a volunteer effort.  I appreciate the added security and stability of Ubuntu.

. . . but, if I cannot count on this OS to get my work accomplished, it becomes nothing more than a very well developed and secure web browser.

Although it is more bothersome, I can accomplish the same in XP.  While I embrace the open source concept, it becomes painfully more apparent to me that I simply cannot accomplish my work in Ubuntu.

I do own the software (ok, the license to use the software) I need in XP, and it works, and I rely upon it to accomplish my daily work.  I seem to be getting no where in terms of being able to successfully port my work to Ubuntu.

So, with regret and utmost respect towards those who constantly strive to make Ubuntu viable, I have decided to scrap the Ubuntu experiment and, reluctantly, return to the onerous world of exclusive XP use.

Good luck to you.

Perhaps at some later date . . .

Caruso

### Post by Yoke &amp; Chung on 2008-01-06
It is unfortunate to see you are facing so much problems with your Ubuntu installation. Just want to share some of my experience:

Though I am a MS hater deep inside me, I have never used another OS, and never used another office suite since 1995. I started to look at Ubuntu only a few months ago when Vista gave me some problems on some software. I strongly urge new users to install Ubuntu or any other Linux on another old computer/ laptop before going into full scale or even dual boot where you might get into trouble and affected in your work productivity. I started by installing Ubuntu on an old P3 computer, played with it, tested applications and settings, before it finally get installed on my main work laptop.

The P3 system is now retired, but I still continue the same concept on another desktop. I do not install anything onto my laptop unless it is tested on the desktop. Today, I would say I use 95% Ubuntu and 5% Windows Vista on the laptop. I use native Linux applications instead of running WINE. To be frank, I am more comfortable working with OpenOffice than with the expensive Office 2007 that I paid, at least OpenOffice still have the familiar Office 2003 kind of look. My work PC in my office is running Windows XP and Office 2003 (which I could not change), and I have no problem so far in documents created at home by OpenOffice porting to Office 2003 at work. 

Regarding hardware compatibility, it maybe painful at first, but nowadays I am "well trained" to research before I buy anything especially latest product. Ya, sometimes it still does give problems after research, like my recent nVidia 8600 GTS graphics card. (To be fair, all 3 OSes in my test box including Win XP could not boot properly with this card which made me think it could be a hardware issue at first, that's another story.)

One final tip is I installed an additional old hard disk ripped out from another even older computer to my "test box", this keeps images created by Partimage/ Clonezilla, and if there is anything wrong I can just get back a working system in less than 30 mins. I always make an image before I install any applications/ drivers even on the test box. You can buy a cheap 2nd hand from ebay, and this does not have to be large, I started with only a 9GB hard disk.

Finally give it another try, perhaps when hardy comes out. I never thought I would be using Linux too, and well, I am one now.

Good luck.
