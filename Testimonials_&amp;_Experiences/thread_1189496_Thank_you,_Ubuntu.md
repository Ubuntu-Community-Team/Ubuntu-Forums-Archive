### Post by imagenius85 on 2009-06-16
Hello all,

I normally don't join forum sites. I'll read them for the information I need, absolutely, but then be on my way. However, I'm making an exception. And the reason is because I am thrilled with Ubuntu (8.10 at first, and now 9.04).

About 4 months ago I felt I needed a change. I was tired of XP. Vista (no surprise here) was just a $300 fail. I even beta tested Windows 7 which seems slightly more promising but still packed with the same ol' Windows BS. I was tired of license keys and (cough) buying (cough) software. I was tired of feeling like $Bill$ was staring into my hard drive every time I connected to the internet.

I had very little Linux experience; the few times I had tried it would lead me down dark paths of formatting, installation, epic fails, reformatting, and back to XP. (Lack of graphic card and USB wireless drivers' support) I then tried Ubuntu 8.10. It installed... flawlessly. It even recognized my USB wireless device which enabled me to download the correct nvidia drivers... which enabled me to use all the wonderful little gadgets in Compiz.

When 9.04 came out I was worried to upgrade because I had just got everything the way I wanted in 8.10. But after a couple weeks hesitation, I decided to do the upgrade. Again, flawless!

I, a complete noob with linux months ago, am now zipping around Ubuntu trying all sorts of crazy things like recompiling wine for the hell of it, editing samba config files for file sharing, downloading gtk themes, toying with screenlets, toying with clamAV/Firestarter, and laughing at those sites that claim I have a malware infestation. I'm like a sponge with Ubuntu - soaking up all this knowledge and wanting more! 

Needless to say, I have a very professional-themed OS on not only my Laptop but my Desktop now as well.

More than all of that though, is it's FUN. I haven't had this much fun playing on an OS in forever. So I have to say to all those in the Ubuntu community: Thank You. Thanks for all your work. I know Ubuntu is free and I hope it remains that way, but I'd sure as hell pay money for it! I'm sold. :)
