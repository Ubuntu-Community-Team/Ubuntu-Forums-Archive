### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2008-05-05
Hello :)

Well, did you hear that "thud" sound? That was me, i forgot to hold the doorknob on the way in, and your doors close quickly here :) Sure everyone, once inside the Ubuntu home, won't want to leave......

Little history: Since about 1997 i have been with Microsoft's stuff, and have quite always been happy. Granted, i got '95 borrowed from a friend, '98 that came with my then-boyfriend's new machine but installed on mine as well, ME from the friendly pirates at Panthip after moving to Thailand, 2000 a couple of weeks later (ME sucks!) from that same place, XP much later from that same place as well and only Vista, the star of the stars, actually contributed to Bill's personal paycheck. I was probably the first (or at least one of the first) buying Vista Ultimate, at it's full price equaling roughly two months Thai salary, in Bangkok the day after it was released. 

In that time i did several attempts to get friendly with Linux, just for fun - SuSE 6.4 was the first, i still have all 6 CD's which at that time cost about as much as Windows 98 in Germany, my native country..... never got the thing to work the way it should, it's VERY difficult to get a Linux working when you have no mouse for lack of mouse driver and can't download the driver for lack of modem driver........ further attempts with some old RedHat, EasyLinux, YellowLinux, Mandrake etc all failed with similar problems........ 

My first Linux success came with SuSE 10.0 like three years ago, so well in fact that i had it going on my office workstation and used it daily - for a whole week before it suddenly decided to go haywire. Back to Windows then.

Ok some further attempts in virtual machines (which led me to my very very first discovery of this forum) brought me to Ubuntu. 6.06, then 6.10. Still problems. 

Well, recently i have started playing with it again. Ubuntu, 7.04 and 7.10. Real installs, on a second computer. Still trouble - the onboard graphic (SiS) doesn't support 3D and Ubuntu doesn't support my spare, an old TNT II-M64, which has perfect 3D under XP.... none in Ubuntu and crashing with the Nvidia driver. 

Tried PC Linux OS Gnome, and decided - THAT'S IT. Planned to put it on my main machine. And so i did - on a separate HDD which i swapped with the Vista one. And really, i actually got it to work! Well.... but wasn't really happy. I need that dock on the bottom, and never got AWN to work on it. Their repos are rather limited.

So i tried Ubuntu again, 8.04. Beta. And - it worked, including AWN! So, the day the LTS came out, i downloaded that and made a clean install on that second HDD, still swapping it with Vista for production. Got "my" Ubuntu customized and tweaked (remember i am a n00b - i had to learn the command line, hours and hours of googling commands...... and the better i got it, the more i liked it.

And then, yesterday, Sunday May 04, 2008, i did "it". "The Step". 10 AM - i opened my computer once more. Pulled the cable from the Vista HDD. My computer probably expected the Linux HDD to be plugged in for a few hours, but - this time the screwdriver came out. Out went that Vista HDD. In went the Linux HDD. The case sides closed, the power button pressed.

And here i am, Ubuntu 8.04 Hardy Heron my sole OS on my main machine.

Now i hope i am welcome amidst you experienced guys, even with the little preparations i did i still kind of dived in head-first, there are still minor issues (how do i get my IrDA adaptor to do something? I can see it there in "lsusb" but it doesn't send or receive files....... and how to stop these network-manager errors when shutting down?) but i have made some stunning experiences in that short time.

My printer/scanner/combination thing which doesn't print anymore but is still used as a scanner - a 160 MB software suite wants to be installed in Windows to use that thing. I have read about the "printer hell" in Linux. So, upon my first try to search for a driver, i switched that machine on and it immediately (!) showed up in "lsusb". So i just fired up Xsane to try - and guess what, the thing worked, and not only worked but scans better and faster than under Windows!! 

I have since found that pretty much everything i plug into USB just works - be that my old Nokia 3230, any of my newer Samsung phones, my Phillips mp3-player and even that good old Bluetooth thingy! NO DRIVERS INSTALLED!! 

Gotta love Ubuntu!

A few apps which i need daily (uTorrent - sorry, no native Linux app is as good for torrenting), Magic Video Converter which really IS magic for it's simplicity converting anything to everything (and for which i have a legit fullversion!) as well as ImgBurn (again, plenty of native apps but i haven't found one allowing me to tweak it like ImgBurn) run perfectly (almost - small uTorrent issue) in Wine so i really don't miss Windows - tuly, Vista is amazing (if the machine has the horsepower, which mine, a home-build, does) but Ubuntu is just as amazing and further (and easier!) customizable. 

I have just read that long-ish thread about people suffering from lockups under Hardy, so i touch my wooden desk here and hope mine won't break........ because, frankly, i don't WANT to go back to Windows, but also wouldn't want to downgrade to an older Ubuntu - i love Hardy :)

I am truly looking forward to a long and happy relationship with my new boyfriend, who's name is "Ubuntu", the handsome African (hope my real boyfriend, a handsome Thai, won't get jealous) :)

With kind regards......

Thanh
