### Post by wadelewis4 on 2008-04-29
Ubuntu is awesome - most of us think so. It's not going anywhere - it's here to stay. So with that said, here's what I think about Hardy Heron. 

[B]
Visuals: [/B]
No real curb appeal. It just doesn't look like a modern OS should. I mean, honestly a lot of us immediately set up compiz/beryl/emerald anyway as part of the ritual of a new install. (Now that compiz has been pre-installed it's just a matter of activating it & possibly installing proprietary video drivers). Then, of course, there's gnome-look.org so we can all go out and customize to our hearts content.
But, what about the "out-of-the-box" experience? I personally would like to see a different color scheme. The orange/brown thing has run it's course with me, and actually I never really liked it.

[B]
Performance:[/B]
Speedy Speederton. (In comparison to 7.10's performance.)
Boot-up speed has significantly improved for me. Programs open and respond quicker, even with all of my eye-candy enhancements set-up. OpenOffice opens with no wait time, which deterred me from using it previously. Bonus: I've had no errors or program crashes as of yet (knocks on wood). 

[B]
Hardware Support:[/B]
Big improvement. Really, ever since the Restricted Drivers manager came into being (earlier releases) I've been a lot happier. I don't even have to install my printer anymore - it's automatically there and works like a charm. 

[B]
Default Applications:[/B]
Not really a big deal since there are thousands of alternatives out there just waiting to be installed via Synaptic or apt-get. 
Personally, I like the initial program line-up and I'm really glad they decided to make Brasero the default burning app. But this is an area of personal opinion, so I'm not too focused on it. 

**Summary:**
Overall, I am happy with Hardy Heron. It feels rock solid below that familiar (and getting old) orange/brown paint job. But because of the fact that it's so customizable and dependable - all while being simple and ready to go directly after installing - it gets a big fat A+ from me. 

Share your experiences with the new Ubuntu.

### Post by igoddard on 2008-05-16
[B]
Visuals: [/B]
No strong feelings.  What it does matters more than what it looks like.  Using KDE I didn't particularly care for the background colour but that's easily changed.

[B]
Performance:[/B]
Big problem.  Sitting there running KDE and one terminal with top running shows that its eating almost all of a 1Gb laptop's memory.  Trying to run VirtualBox results in more spectacular swapping than I've seen in years.  I'd upgraded straight from 6.06 so I've no idea if this is an 8.04 thing or whether it's been bloating in stages.  But bloated it has and that's why I'm writing this from 6.06. 

[B]
Hardware Support:[/B]
It found the wireless H/W OK but for some reason it had to be told manually via Wireless Assistant to reconnect after each boot.  Annoying and probably easy enough to sort out if I'd stayed.

[B]
Default Applications:[/B]
Not a Ubuntu issue as such but... I need one particular app under Wine and Wine has been broken with that app for several releases.  The last working Wine version isn't available for 8.04.  If I'd stayed I could build Wine from source (I know what to fix & have told the developers but it's not been done in releases.  This is fair enough because it's H/W dependent and what works with me might break someone else).

**Summary:**
The memory bloat is a show-stopper.  800+Mb to do nothing is ridiculous! I've gone back to Dapper.  I might look round at other distros in the longer term.
