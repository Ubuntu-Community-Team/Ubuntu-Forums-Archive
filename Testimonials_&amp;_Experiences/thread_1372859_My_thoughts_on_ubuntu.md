### Post by ownasaur on 2010-01-05
I am what you call an average computer user. I do web browsing, downloads here and there and of course, games. I am by no means expert in any computer related subject, I just think I know enough to get by with windows.

I was first introduced to ubuntu about 3 years ago in a CIT class (basic computing, or I should say basic MSFT office learning). Anyways, I was pretty impressed back then at what was being offered online 100% free. I decided to download an ISO and give it a shot using a live cd.

I remember it being called feisty fawn, compared to what I had running (winxp) this seemed to be faster, but slow since I was running the live cd but never had the courage to do a full install because:
a) dell didn't bother including a recovery cd, and their recovery cd maker kept crashing midway and lost many disks that way
b) the drivers weren't being offered at their website in case I needed to reinstall windows.

I was disappointed I couldn't figure out why the wi-fi and the video were not working properly, so I gave up on the whole linux thing.

Recently I decided to check the ubuntu website to see what was new and found they had a special netbook release, so I decided to install in my hp mini 1030nr. Like I posted on a related thread, everything (except for the mic and the card reader) worked out of the box, with the exception of wi-fi. After plugin in and getting the necessary updates the wi-fi worked. I am real happy with it now!

I then decided run the regular 9.10 live cd on my old dell computer to see if there had been any improvements on compatibility issues. Again, everything worked out of the box. I was so happy that I did not think twice about getting rid of windows and installing ubuntu in it. My computer which is about 5 years old runs faster than my girlfriend's hp pavilion (she bought it in may '09) running vista. 

I'd like to thank everyone at canonical, the ubuntu team, and all those who have contributed one way or another on the making of such a great operating system that just works!

Thank you all!
