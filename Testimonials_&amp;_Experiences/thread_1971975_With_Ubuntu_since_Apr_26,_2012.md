### Post by paulkiss on 2012-05-03
Hey, everyone, I dreamt of "having Linux" on my computer for years, one thing that stopped me from migrating from Win was PC games and my lack of some technical things, but several days ago I finally did this. 1st my friend migrated to Ubuntu, and then I followed him. 

I'm happy I have a Lin OS, an officially free OS, user friendly and stuff. 1st I set up 11.10, but then I upgraded.

There're some minor bugs, of course, but as I saw most of the problems have their answers via googling and getting to Ubuntu forums or related places.

When I 1st booted from LiveCD I was shocked and impressed that Ubuntu found my hardware, my modem, I even browsed internet... from an OS which was not installed at all! For a Windows slave like me that was impressive and unbelievable. No CDs with drivers. It was all found "by itself". All those HDDs, everything. 

And of course I was pleasantly impressed by all that GUI stuff. All looks friendly, cute, nice. 

I'm still suffering, however, that I don't have exact analogues of Total Commander and IrfanView :) That makes me being have to learn new stuff, like Gimp, for instance.

I didn't quit Windows world, however. At work I have Windows, I have Win7Starteron my netbook (just see no reason to take down Win 7 since it's clean and works ok so far), Win 7 on my laptop (my mom uses it). But sooner or later all my devices will be set to Linux. Coz there was actually one thing that held me within Windows - pc games. But since I've not been playing anything for a while already, I think I'm done with playing, and why don't I just migrate completely to a normal safe OS.
