### Post by Lunx on 2009-10-03
Upgraded yesterday evening from Jaunty and everything went very smoothly. Been using Ubuntu  (and Linux generally) since January this year, and so far the experience has been excellent. Started with Intrepid, switched to Jaunty beta and now here with Karmic and loving it.

Everything worked for me apart from needing to open the settings manager for compiz and fiddle with the bindings for rotating desktop cube. Sorted within seconds :). Other issue was playback of some mp3 files in VLC, but once again, I opened the control panel for sound and tried a few settings, and voila. Sounds 1000 times better than pulseaudio ever did, so very happy with that. 

Haven't posted on the forums for a while as I've been tied up with university study, but wanted to let others know I'm studying on-line here in Australia and was a bit worried before commencing that I may have problems using Linux and FOSS, but haven't had a moment's problem. Funny thing was some files we needed to access were .ppsx files and so many people (MS Office users) complained they couldn't open them and had no idea what to do. Yet I simply renamed the file extension to .pptx, then when I went to open it with OOo, it was simply a matter of choosing the right filter and away I went. All the Windows users need to download other crap, and before they could do that they needed to run updates first. When people have compatibility dramas with proprietary software and need to jump through hoops, it makes me chuckle to myself when many still look at FOSS being somehow inferior LOL
