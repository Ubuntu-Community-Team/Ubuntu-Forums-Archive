### Post by zero244 on 2009-01-03
Ive been using Ubuntu for about 2 years. I dont want to discourage you but there is a definate learning curve.
If you dont want 3d graphics or special effects on the desktop, it will be fairly painless to use.
I would say there is about a 6 month learning curve for Linux just to get the basics down.
It took me three fresh installs to get Hardy running correctly.
Then I realized that the nvidia drivers were causing a random hard lockup.
Im not completely sure its the video drivers but Im pretty sure.
I just removed them and will see if that solves my freeze probs.
Of course I dont have video acceleration, so I have no compiz or no gaming.
In my mind stability is top priority.

Give it a try, but unless your one of the lucky ones, it wont be as painless as installing os\x.
Dont get me wrong I think Ubuntu is a nice OS.
Im considering installing Fedora or possibly going back to Edgy or Feisty with XGL.
Hardy was running smooth as silk, if it weren't for the lockups I would been very satisfied.
Good luck keep us posted.
