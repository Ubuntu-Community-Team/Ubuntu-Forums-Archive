### Post by Russell Burrows on 2010-09-30
I used to be a Windows fan until I noticed that I was daily running anti virus scans, antispyware scans and antimalware scans with the result of still getting problems every two months.

One day I said why am I spending all this time virus scanning and reading posts trying to fix Windows goofups?

The breaking point came when I purchased a then new laptop two years ago when it was either deal with Vista or pay good money to a computer shop to have them wipe Vista and downgrade to XP.

For some strange reason I was using Altavista search that day and on one link it said Vista versus XP or Ubuntu and I said Ubuntu? eh! must be some African cpu shop but curiosity had me press the link.

I read about Ubuntu and went well its free so lets try it and if not then theres always those money grubbers at the cpu shop that can install XP.

I downloaded Ubuntu 8.04 and tried it out and had so much fun customizing my desktop that I forgot about trying XP.
And I only update once a month or hardly bother since if my Ubuntu laptop is running great then what do I need an update for? right so I only do the update thing a few weeks after they have been out or most of the time I just say nope my stuff is running great and no updates needed.

A month later after that I saw a thread on virtualization and I went oohthefoxthat? huh??
So I said virtualbox? yeah lets try it since its free.

After that it was fun to have folks over and sooner or later some geek talks about what the best antivirus for Windows is and I said Virtualbox.

I then showed folks XP running on Virtualbox and with one button press off Windows goes with the laptop still on and running Ubuntu LOL.

Some folks were unable to process what Virtualbox was doing since the folks were but,but Windows was on and a button press later its off but, but the laptop is still running!!!!

Today marks two years that this laptop is running Ubuntu with no reformats or other problems endemic to Windows.

The only small hassle I had was permissions for removable USB drives and two days later I went DOH! when I saw what System>Administration>Disk Utility does then I went dude! thats so simple just select mount and permissions problem solved.

Oh and my system running Ubuntu 8.04 was using 525 MB of ram and todays 10. Ubuntu version uses 370 MB running a lot of stuff like firefox, vlc, ktorrent and youtube all at the same time so its faster, better to use.

Today my wife asked to have me fix her Compaq Mini 110 netbook with XP and I DL Ubuntu Netbook Remix and my wife said its much, much faster than before and it looks so nice.
Hey whatever keeps the wife happy and lets me spend my free time on things I want to do.

My final words:

To mess up a linux laptop you really need to work at it, to mess up a Windows laptop all you have to do is work on it.
