### Post by Dirka on 2009-11-17
Ok, so I've tried many Linux distros over the last 9 years. Too many flavors to care anymore. I would download the ISO, burn to disc, install the OS, and install the apps i wanted. On earlier distros it was more of a frustrating attempt at installing the apps i wanted. I usually finished my Linux romance with the always fun "driver install/reboot terminal screen - now what?" With Ubuntu however, i would like to say - I only got that terminal screen once! It was because of me - dumb end user didn't know what the hell he was doing. No worries, i reinstalled the OS and carefully selected my video card drivers, rebooted, THEN installed my apps. ANYWAYS long story short, i really enjoy using Ubuntu, I've got everything up and running the way i want, and it is a joy to use. It's funny how i now think of my Windows OS as an "XBOX" as the ONLY reason i need it is for games.
