### Post by rmcellig on 2010-10-24
I have been a die hard Mac user since 1988. Over the years I have found that I had been paying a lot of money to Apple for their products. While I am happy with Apple's successes, I felt that I couldn't go down this road of always paying. When I discovered Ubuntu a couple of years ago, at first I didn't quite take a liking to it but over time, I found myself working more in the Ubuntu environment and less on the Mac side.

I also found out that there was way more to Ubuntu aside from it basically being free. I learned how valuable the open source community is as well as the way people can really modify their systems to their liking. To me that meant freedom and the reason I first started using a Mac.

I am happy to say that with each release of Ubuntu, I am happy with the steps taken to build an even more robust system with great applications and tools.

Right now I have a couple of issues that I am working on in Ubuntu that don't work at all. I am really hoping I can get these resolved. After several posts to not only the Ubuntu Forum but other Linux forums as well, I still don't have a solution.

As a side note, my mother who is 75 years old started off with an iMac circa 1999. She now has a Dell laptop running Ubuntu and LOVES it!! She is telling all of her friends to use Ubuntu because you can customize it to your liking. That's my experience with Ubuntu so far....
