### Post by Ariya243 on 2012-09-22
Looks like I had been neglecting a very good distro - Lubuntu. I have Ubuntu and Xubuntu in many flavors in few partitions. I decided to install Lubuntu and do the same things, I do with Ubuntu. I don't really use Unity until I could get the launcher on the bottom, even though I like lenses and the ease of finding applications, documents, etc.

Installed Lubuntu 12.04 and what a quick distro!

I went on remixing Lubuntu. Installed Marlin File Manager, made it default. Then Compiz, CCSM etc, and the response to Compiz in Lubuntu surprised me. Much nicer than in Ubuntu and Xubuntu, snappier. In Ubuntu, when you enable Animations, Wobbly windows,etc the desktop dies off and comes back, but in Lubuntu it didn't. All I had to do was compiz --replace and Compiz WM was there without any bugs and with desktop cube, fire particles, magic lamp etc. Everything worked the first time. No log out and log in or restart. Just worked! Add Fusion Icon to start up, you have a Lubuntu Compiz WM.

Tried to uninstall Leafpad, but it would take Lubuntu-desktop with it, so I installed Medit and Scite--very nice text editors, without DE dependencies. Tried to install Gedit, but that tried to pull so much MBs, I dropped that idea. Medit and Scite are very good. 

Installed Libre Office and that went smoothly. Uninstalled Abiword and Gnumeric. Installed Wine and then Sketchup. That too went smoothly. I had Sketchup working with one regedit tweak. Installed Gimp 2.8.2 from Noobslab. Installed Inkscape too. Installed Thunderbird and uninstalled Sylpheed. Everything works very smoothly. Kept Xfburn as I like it better than Brasero, which is a destroyer of CDs.

I wonder, why the Lubuntu developers think that this is a distro for low-spec computers! **It is not!** It is a pretty good distro for any computer.

I have a thin bottom panel in Lubuntu, which has everything in it, and Compiz working quite smoothly, wobbly and other eye-candy, I just can't believe why the Lubuntu is not that popular as Ubuntu and Xubuntu. Lot of people talk about XFCE desktop as a substitute for Gnome 2 experience, but I find Lubuntu is much better and snappier. 

I can understand now why Knoppix uses LXDE as its default desktop.:):):)
