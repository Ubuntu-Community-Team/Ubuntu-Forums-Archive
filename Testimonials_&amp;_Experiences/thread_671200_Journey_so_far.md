### Post by Yoke &amp; Chung on 2008-01-18
I was frustrated with Windows Vista and Office 2007 when I took delivery of my new Dell laptop, and took one bold step forward-- downloaded and installed Feisty on an old computer to test it out :) I have been using Ubuntu for 4 months now, and I have not look back since, though my office PC is still running XP which I can do nothing about it, my home computers are invaded by Linux!

My desktop & laptop are now loaded with Ubuntu Gutsy, and I have just installed PCLinuxOS 2007 on my daughter's PC :-) The only PC that is running on pure Windows is my wife's laptop, which I need to solve her printing to Canon Selphy photo printer. Anyway, anyone tried Sims 2 on WINE? She is a big fan of that too.

Though not a smooth journey with numerous hours spent on configuring software and hardware, I would say I am a happy user, and thanks to Ubuntu, I am finally almost free from MS and eliminated the dilemma inside me: on one hand despise MS for its business conduct and almost abusive pricing; while on the other hand I continue to buy their products...  

One of the major improvements I would like to see on Ubuntu is more out of the box support of hardware especially wireless. I have had a hard time configuring 2 wireless adapters, 1 PCI card with ACX 111 chipset, and another USB with Atmel chipset. Spent countless hours checking the net, trying different ways to configure them, and still just able to see the networks and not able to connect.

I was about to give up installing Linux on my daughter's PC which depend solely on wireless to hook onto the internet, then I saw the PCLinuxOS 2007 CD in my CD pockets, boot it up for one last try, and it just work. I am going to try the PCI wireless card (ACX 111) again on PCLinuxOS and see how it handles it.

I am not saying Ubuntu is inferior to PCLinuxOS, but there is a lot to learn from PCLinuxOS on the ease of use and installation especially if Ubuntu wants to  win over more Windows users.

All in all, I would say the experience is great so far and I am especially thankful to everyone in Ubuntu Forums, the community is what makes the difference other than features of the distros.
