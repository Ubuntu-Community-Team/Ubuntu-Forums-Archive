### Post by armandh on 2009-02-14
my first Ubuntu [7.04 in june of 07] was a slam dunk
I won the compatible equipment lottery. 
the prize, a perfect install.

but since then I have run in to the same problems as the average noob, and almost always worked around it.

here is my list of the biggest noob blockers and pit falls, assuming a good CD

1] bad CD drive, of the sort that mostly works but not without error, and other bad or intermittent hardware, too little mem.

2] unsupported hardware and bizarre attempts to get it working. simple is better leave the square pegs and round holes for the experts. buy a video card that works, you'll be glad you did.

3] unreal expectations. dusting off an unused machine with problems #1,#2 and expecting new performance. it was set aside for a reason. fix it first.

attempting something new on a mission critical machine is a noob headache #4


add your observations and save a noob willing to read and learn

### Post by stalkingwolf on 2009-02-15
I like you have discovered that flaky CD drives can be a major source of Hemoroids .  another that I have recently discovered is flaky RAM.  The Ram may be perfectly good but the board dont like it.  Or when adding RAM the
board does not like the combination.  There is also the density factor.
Some boards dont care , others will only accept low density.

My suggestion here is if you are unsure use Low Density, Non ECC, Unbuffered.  It is almost 100% compatible with everything.
