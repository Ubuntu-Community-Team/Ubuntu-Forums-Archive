### Post by kurushi on 2006-07-07
Ever since I started studing computer sciene, I tried to use Linux without success. All distributions were difficult to administrate, and use, with too many aplications. But one year ago (more or less) I discovered Ubuntu linux for human beeing, and this is real, is for humans and Dapper demostrates it.

The other day I was showing some weekend pictures to one friend, who is completly out of computers. He saw Ubuntu and asked me for that (windows skin), then I told to him this is not a skin, is a completly different operating system ... He felt some interest about Ubuntu and specially about Gnome simplicity, he loved things like Beagle, the applications menu, or the add/remove option (what can I say ...). He felt really comfortable with ubuntu and is thinking seriously to change, he thinks ubuntu is more than suitable for his daily computer use. The only lack for him is wireless compatibility, and a good gnome messenger (he don't like amsn stetics), I'm still convincing him :P

For me this was quite surprising, because I'm usually the nerd who use linux (from my friends eyes), and this is a new attitude from them. They really don't perceive it dificult, and only two friends who use MacOs don't felt curious about Ubuntu.

There's another surprising thing, for first time my faculty is full of people using Ubuntu, this is new because students (even computer science ones), don't like to change their systems (the majority of university applications are based on Windows) and was not usual to see people using linux before Ubuntu. Now is different, I can count 3 of 6 people using Ubuntu in the studing room (from my point of view), and I think this is really successfull.

I'm very happy with Ubuntu, of course it must be improved, but I'm waiting for next release (hope will integrate Xgl with only a couple of clicks). For first time in history, I survived more than one year without malware, virus, ... And that's really great.

Now I decided than I'll try to evangelise as much friends as possible.

Thank you very much to everyone who makes Ubuntu possible.

Albert
