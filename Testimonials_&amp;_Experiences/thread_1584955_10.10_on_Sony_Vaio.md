### Post by manzdagratiano on 2010-10-14
I have Sony VAIO VGNAR520E running Maverick, which has had its hardware fully supported on Ubuntu since Karmic - no issues at all!

Regarding the fact that the XP install was borked on a few machines, all I would say is this (aside from secretly thinking that it would be good for these machines in the long run! Yes... I am evil, but not as evil as Winblows!) - it is usually an unsafe idea to tell Ubuntu to automatically partition your drive for you when you have Windows - with the way the Windows boot partition is set up with its stupid ntldr and other stuff that a mere copy-paste and a toggle of a flag cannot fix, you would always be better off to first shrink your partition as much as you can after defragmenting to death (for Windows, defragmenting the MFT is the issue, and PerfectDisk - the trial version - is the only thing that I know works for that; you do it once in your life anyway); fix your boot sector using the install CD if you have XP and you resized using GParted - Vista (and 7 I believe) can shrink the volume themselves - and then install Ubuntu in the free space. Unless you have issues with Grub2 - which I do not think there are any anymore - it should work flawlessly.
