### Post by ofb on 2007-10-18
This is a thread for experienced users to report what the LiveCD does on their machines. The purpose is to familiarize the people who answer troubles in Absolute Beginners with what our new batch of New Users is seeing.

Because it's a little different from previous LiveCDs, as it turns out.

So, to begin:

----

I've just run Gutsy on two boxes.

Both started up at 1600x1200, which is absurd for a 17" CRT.

This is quickly fixed in Screen Resolution, but interestingly enough the desired 1024x768 is the next available option -- there are no intermediate sizes available.

Also, you can select and get a real 85Hz now - not the misprint 50/51/52Hz that we used to see when getting an actual 85Hz. Yay team.

After that, one box seemed to check out fine, but the other couldn't find the internet.

Fired up Network and switched from Roaming to straight DHCP with no luck. Checked Network Tools and found the desired card wasn't showing up there. The LAN card was listed, but not the internet card.

Poked & prodded for a while but no luck.

So, that's new. These machines had no resolution or network issues with the 6.10 & 7.04 LiveCDs. The spurned card is a Realtek RTL8029(AS).

[Also, the new default wallpaper will make you crave coffee. Be prepared.]
