### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-02-25
I'll just point out that Ubuntu doesn't force you to use Unity. Gnome Shell is easily installable from the repositories.

It was not possible to stick with the old Gnome 2 desktop for a few reasons:

1. It was no longer maintained by its developers and is no longer the current version of Gnome (even though MATE now exists, it only came a year after Unity started development, and the future of MATE is not certain)
2. If we stuck with the same old ugly 2D desktop for the next few years, we'll be the laughing stock of the computing world. Gnome 2 was certainly ugly, it looked like the illegitimate son of OS X and Windows, it wasn't touch-friendly or TV-remote-friendly and it doesn't scale to smaller screen sizes. It's also a highly inefficient desktop with a distinct lack of modern features such as pervasive search. Unity attempts to address each of these rough points of Gnome 2.

Ubuntu would have needed to have changed UI at some point in time. Better now than later. If the switch to Unity is painful now, it would have been twice as painful to accomplish in five years time.

I agree with you about video editing and how flaky the programs are. At least now they work in between crashes; back in 2005 when I started with Linux, you needed several days of prayer and incantation to make them export video at all, and even then you'd usually find that the audio was five seconds out of sync with the video. Maybe in three or four years the programs will no longer crash.
