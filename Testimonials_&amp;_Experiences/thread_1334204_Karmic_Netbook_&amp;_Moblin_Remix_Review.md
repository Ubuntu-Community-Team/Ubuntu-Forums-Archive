### Post by scourge on 2009-11-22
Just bought myself an ASUS EeePC 1005HA netbook for my travels, and of course replacing Windows XP with something usable was the first thing I wanted to do. After having pimped my trash can by attaching the "Designed for Windows XP" sticker to it, I decided to install the recently released Moblin 2.1 because the colorful stick figures in the introduction video look so cute. Unfortunately the official Moblin distribution doesn't have any decent package repositories, so installing applications, and especially audio/video codecs, was a huge pita. Moblin made me feel like a total Linux n00b, which I definitely am not.

After the first disappointment I still wanted to belong to the always connected, ultrasocial group of twittering 21st century hipsters. I checked out Ubuntu Moblin Remix, which is based on the now outdated Moblin 2.0 and doesn't have the improvements and bugfixes of Moblin 2.1. But it does have Ubuntu's excellent repositories, so I had all my favorite apps installed in no time. However, both UMR and Moblin 2.1 had serious trouble staying connected to my wifi network, often dropping the connection several times in a minute. And this is with one of those few netbooks that were extensively tested by the Moblin team. I guess they forgot to test the wireless drivers.

The unreliable wifi was unacceptable to me, so I was left with Ubuntu 9.10 Netbook Remix. This time everything worked perfectly: installation was smooth, sleeping and hibernation worked like a charm, wireless was reliable, it lowered by heightened blood pressure, and it took very little effort to install all the stuff I need. There are also many design differences that tip the scale in favor of UNR (when compared to Moblin or UMR):
- The icons are more informative. Moblin has beautiful, modern-looking icons, but they're more cryptic.
- The applications dialog/view is easier to navigate
- It's faster to switch between applications (via Alt+Tab or by clicking the icon on the task bar)
- The battery applet tells how much time I have left (based on current power consumption) or how long it takes until the battery is fully charged. Moblin just shows a percentage number, and in the case of UMR, sometimes it tells me that my laptop doesn't even have a battery.


Conclusion (there must always be a conclusion):
Moblin 2.1 is not ready, except for serious freaks who like serious pain. Ubuntu Moblin Remix is not ready, except for moderate freaks who like moderate amounts of pain. Ubuntu Netbook Remix is frickin' fantastic. Go install it on your netbook now. Then install it on your friends' netbooks while they're not looking. They'll thank you later.
