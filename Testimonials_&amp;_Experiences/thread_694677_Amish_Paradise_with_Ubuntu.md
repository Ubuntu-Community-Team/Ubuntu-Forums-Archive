### Post by 91004 on 2008-02-12
Since this is the area to post our experiences here I go....

I have used various flavors of Linux over the years including earlier versions of Ubuntu, plus like most everyone else have used Microsoft products as well.  I'm not a "programmer", nor a "coder" by any strech of the imagination, althought I have been a tech geek for a 22+ years.

Anyway like I said before I have used Linux since the late 1990's starting off with Red Hat.  Maybe I was not experienced with it or maybe it just wasn't compatible with my hardware.  I don't know and I don't care but I moved back to Windows 98 & Windows 2000.  I was frustrated because I couldn't get Linux to work right on my system.

Well I kept up with the different Linux flavors throughout the years and installed them every once in a while, and then one day Ubuntu came along.

I started with I believe 7.04 and had success with that, and although I had to create drivers for my sound system by following the instructions on this forum I actually liked linux.

Well as the story goes my wife & son didn't like Linux so I was overruled.  My theory is if you are going to be with a Operating System stick with that system and dual booting was just a waste of space.

Anyway step forward to the end of January 2008.  I've become sick and tired of the troubles I have with Windows XP.  Programs would install and then restart windows without my permission because you weren't given a choice, it just did it with certain software.  That pisses me off.  Also the Network would become unstable as far as line speed.  I have a 6 MB pipe  coming in and I just wasn't getting the line speeds as what I could.

I had a friend that wanted Vista taken off his computer and a operating system placed on that wouldn't eat 2 gigs of ram just to run the system.  Since I had been playing around with the idea of installing Ubuntu anyway I told him I was going to install Linux, and he responded that he doesn't care what I put on as long as he doesn't have to wait a long time to load and he wanted to have multiple desktop environments.

So I made a Ubuntu 7.10 disc and installed it on his system.  Guess what!  The system installed all of the drivers on his Dell 1501 without me having to find drivers, except for the Wifi package.  So I found the site [http://www.ubuntu1501.com](http://www.ubuntu1501.com) which specifically covered this particular Dell laptop.  I can't believe the support that I had from this website. Everything that you needed to install from Mediabuntu to installing the wifi was listed on there, plus general hints & tricks for 7.10/7.04/6.06.  Man I couldn't believe my luck.
