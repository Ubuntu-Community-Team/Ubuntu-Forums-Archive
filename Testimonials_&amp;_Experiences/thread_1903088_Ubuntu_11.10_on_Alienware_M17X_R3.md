### Post by redlinethecar on 2012-01-01
Hello everyone,

Just wanted to share my experience with Ubuntu. I've been using Ubuntu since version 7.10. It was the first version of Linux that I tried to install and actually had drivers for almost everything on my laptop. It was fast, clean, and an easy learning curve. After loading it for the first time it's been an endless learning path ever since. My computer transformed into a fully customizable, no rules workspace of infinite possibilities. With that said I knew once I got my Alienware laptop (6 months ago) Ubuntu was going to be a speed demon. I loaded Ubuntu on I7  2.2GHz - 3.1GHz w/Turbo Boost, 6MB Cache, 16GB Dual Channel DDR3 at 1600MHz, 1.5GB GDDR5 NVIDIA GeForce GTX 560M...... The only two issues I have with Ubuntu on the system is battery life (which in windows would last 2.5 times longer) and Video Card. Ohh and, it appears that GPU's with Linux in general tend to be a huge pain in the you know what. I feel the power issue has to do with the high end video card. On that laptop there is an integrated card that is supposed to activate and switch to when on battery power but, haven't figured that one out yet. That aside Linux has been a great benefit on my life and computer problems all together. One thing I love is how I can surf with no worries of malicious software since most is specifically made for Windows. I know that is a bold thing to say but just from my experience I've surf the internet since version 7.10 and not once did have to reload a system for malware, viruses, spyware, and all the other ware's that leech on to Windows boxes. Huge relief! Ubuntu barely uses my system resources. I have 16 gigs of RAM and only about 1 is in use. If your using Windows and want full control of your system I would suggest Ubuntu. But in my opinion if you use your PC twice a week to check emails and that's all you know how to do and all you want to know stick with what you know, Windows.
