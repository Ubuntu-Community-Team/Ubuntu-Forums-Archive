### Post by yourwhiteshadow on 2011-03-04
I had to analyze some scientific data and was considering Kaleidagraph or SigmaPlot, but I wasn't interested in downloading illegal versions. SigmaPlot also doesn't look very great, same with Kaleidagraph. After about 5 minutes of research, I decided to give QtiPlot a run. WOW. Had my data analyzed within 2-3 minutes, and had figures ready in a few seconds. By the way, these were publication quality, super high quality plots/graphs.

To top off the story, later that day IT was in our lab cleaning up computers that are hooked up to our instruments because they had been infected with viruses a la USB drives. i think the only thing holding back the science community from fully switching to ubuntu is the lack of support from instrumentation manufacturers. we just can't use our instruments in ubuntu. desperately trying to get 10.10 on my MBP so that i can use QtiPlot.

### Post by Simstud on 2011-03-07
I am a phd student in chemistry. I find that all our older instruments run unix or linux, and the newer ones run windows. Some of our instruments (from bruker) run linux with windows in a VM! It's not programming or interfacing the instruments that holds up the science community, these things are EASIER in linux, it's the preferences of the sales team.
