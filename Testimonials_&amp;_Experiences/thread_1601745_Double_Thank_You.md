### Post by CJ56 on 2010-10-20
So

The wife's Dell Latitude D630 (on Win Xp) which she uses for work finally got chomped up by malware & viruses & God knows what & she said in desperation, could I put Ubuntu on it? And thank you no 1 to the Ubuntu team for making it a (relative) breeze. I've put Ubuntu on a number of machines before but they've been pretty much standalone devices (my own included) but here I had to get it working with a wireless networked printer & file-sharing with a desktop PC (still on Xp) & get Wine to run a tricksy little application she needs for her dictation machine. And it worked...I put on 10.04 & it was brilliant. Wireless drivers, printer drivers, desktop effects, the whole lot, virtually out of the box...

And then

I awarded myself an eMachines M250 (mainly for carrying my music collection around) which now seems to be made by Acer. I kicked off Win Xp  & gave 10.10 + Unity a whirl on it, & I liked where it was going a lot, but too flaky at present & some inconsistencies in the way it handles files & folders, so back to 10.04 for netbooks & again, straight on there, no messing about. Everything works...

This is wonderful stuff from the Ubuntu team & so two big thank yous for all the work they've put into this wonderful OS, making it so easy to install & such a pleasure to use. Congratulations!
