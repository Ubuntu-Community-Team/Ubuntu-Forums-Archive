### Post by Hugh Mulqueen on 2009-11-05
This is the first time I did a complete wipe of the computer and installed Ubuntu. I have been using Ubuntu/Linux for the past 2 years. I finally got all my hardware working under Ubuntu only recently. Been very happy about that.

Now Karmic, I know that karmic has just been released but I did not find it very stable at all this time. A few bad things:

1. I don't think Devicekit is a viable alternative to HAL just yet, too many issues. I'll describe my own issues as follows...

2. After I upgraded my box to 9.10 my huawei E220 modem acted very erratically. Sometimes it wouldn't connect to a network and after a lot of fiddling it would decide to connect and then would be very slow to load up pages. It disconnects from the network without warning. Devicekit would recognize it as a Mass storage device aswell. for some unknown reason.:-?

3. This is probably something for 100 paper-cuts but in Ubuntu 9.04 when you wanted to automatically mount a ntfs partition all you had to do was to check the box to auto mount the partition every-time. Now with the new DeviceKit mount manager it asks you for your password every time you want to mount.

4. For some reason I can't burn ISO disks any more. Brasero just stagnates, It doesn't do anything left it there for a half hour still just sitting there doing nothing. I've got a sneak'n suspicion its got something to do with you know what.:-\"
EDIT: know what happened its definattly a bug; when you eject a CD or DVD the OS forgets to unmount the volume. I am reporting a bug report.

Things that I like about karmic:

1. I love the new icon set. Very well polished...

2. The little things like when you left click a USB stick, Safely remove device option was added.

3. The default wallpapers are fantastic...

4. Integration of the IM client Empathy and evolution. very cool.

5. Overall look-and-feel very very nice.

6. OpenOffice.org 3.1 automatically recognizes UK dictionary for me. Everything is set up and ready to go.

7. Automatically recognized my big screen resolution of 1680x1050 without the official NVIDIA drivers installed!!

8. Ext4 not too shabby either.

Overall I think using DeviceKit was just diabolical. The other yoke, the GUI and all the little changes was fantastic (100 paper-cuts brilliant idea;)) Really polished up the desktop.

The only question left to ask is do ye think that I should downgrade to Ubuntu 9.04 or what??
