### Post by afmGM on 2010-05-15
Hello, I've been getting frustrated with Windows lately.  Almost making me late for classes because I have to wait for the Vista to shut down before I can pack up my laptop.  Even having it take 3 minutes to open up GIMP, and I've decided that's enough.  It's time to try Ubuntu 10.04.  

**Previous Experiences With Ubuntu**

8.04 - Installed sucessfully, ran, and everything... it's just that it didn't work out so well since I added outdated repositories since I had no idea what I was doing.

9.04 - Installed on Laptop through Wubi and didn't work.

9.10 - Worked with Wubi, but Wine wouldn't work.

I know my way around the terminal now in Ubuntu, and everything but I was never able to fully switch, and keep Ubuntu.  But this time, I think Ubuntu will prevail.  I will update this thread for a full week, with my experiences.  

**Needs**

Run Lunar Magic, emulators, type notes, type Japanese, program with pygame & python, watch YouTube and chat on MSN.

**Table of Contents**

Day 1 - The Beggining (DOWN BELOW)

[B]Day 1 - The Beginning

[/B]*Deep Breath*, let's pop this sucker in and boot it up.  Let's see if it works.  First of all from the Live CD, I found it much improved.  I liked how it started a GUI asking "Try it" or "Install it"... it seemed much more better that way.

Ok, I have tried the live CD before to make sure it worked, so I'll pretty much said, worked perfectly and everything worked out of the box.  (I also wanted gbrainy, it's fun :P).

One thing I liked, was definitely the new look, I hated Ubuntu's old orange look.  For me, I'm very picky about default looks, and the previous look just made it feel like it was going to break and was obsolete.  But no problems with the new look what-so-ever :)

Well it's time to install.  The installer was good, I already had an unallocated partition ready, so all I did was used continuous free space, which was quite simple.  It even recognized my time zone too.  The slideshow it had was also very professional and looked quite nice.   It actually amazed me of how fast it was!  Only two things that bugged me during the install, first of all it got my Restore Partition called "Vista Loader", and me real vista partition called "Recovery", so it got them reversed.  Now if I was to access my restore partition, it instantly overwrites the MBR, just in case a glitch or something (which was nice when I screwed it up)...   Though I can easily remember, so I should be fine.  Maybe I'll fix it for another day.  The final thing, which really bugged me, is when it ejected the CD when I shut it down.  It made a huge wave of errors.   Talking about input errors, now I was familiar with take the CD out, and press enter.  I know it could be a hardware glitch though, as it barely worked in 9.04.  It actually kinda scared me as I thought the install failed or something and that my computer was hosed.

Ok, it's time for the moment of truth.  *crosses fingers*... I saw a flashing underscore and then it boot up GRUB.  Now I pressed enter, and more flashing underscore.  I really don't like having a flashing underscore in a boot, it kinda weirds me out a bit.  I think something's screwed up.  Though I get it a few times with Vista.  So I saw the nice new logo, and boom, it was at the login screen.  WHAT THE HELL?!  That was freakin' fast.. too fast.. it kinda startled me actually XD  Well first of all Wi-Fi, graphics, etc... worked out of the box.  When I connected to the internet Ubuntu nicely told me that there was graphic drivers available.  I downloaded the recommended one, restarted and low and behold my logo was all ugly now, I don't mind it, but I think I do wanna fix it later though.  I found an article at the forums about the issue anyways.  But that's for another day.

So far, so good.  I downloaded flash and all that, everything works fine.  I definitely like the Ubuntu software center!  It's very elegant now, and nice to use.  Though, when I pressed install I had a 6 second hang, it's not a big deal... but I'm impatient, and clicked it again a few times.. and had it download a few more times.  Though now it doesn't seem to do that for some reason.

Now for the moment of truth, I need to run Lunar Magic on Ubuntu.  I do a lot of Super Mario World modding and, I heard it worked on WineHQ.  Now in Ubuntu 9.04, for me it didn't work.   But with Ubuntu 10.04, I decided to download the beta one (since the other said dummy package..).  What's that apposed to mean?!  Does that mean it's an old version, unstable, stable, incomplete, stupid, or what?!  But.. after letting it install, I plug in my flash drive.. and it recognized and mounted it extremely fast!  But does it run?? *drum roll* YES!!!  It was so nice to see the level rendered correctly :P

So I started using Empathy IM, and I gotta say I really like how the speech bubble look is, it's very nice.  Even better then Windows Live Messenger.  Very good.  I chat with a few friends, until one wouldn't to voice chat with me... uh oh.   Voice chat doesn't work on Empathy, so I tried aMSN to no avail... I had to reboot and go into Windows.   But after that I was glad to go back into Ubuntu.  I hope I can find a way to get MSN voice chatting to work, but that's for another day as well.

So, today's done, I'm finishing the day off by writing this, and I'll be off to bed soon.  I wonder if this will be finally be the time that I will fully manage to switch.  It's looking good.  Just a few things to fix, and I'll be glad to remove my Windows partition.  Tomorrow, I'll setup and customize my Ubuntu install and install a lot of stuff.

Until then, cya.
