### Post by markmcc on 2007-12-05
For the last few years I have (like many others) continued to use WindowsXP on both my home desktop & laptop computers.  It has served the purpose I require.

I have however every 6 months or so tried one or another distribution of desktop Linux to see whether I could possibly replace my Windows desktops with it.

Every time has been an interesting, and generally more impressive experience, but for one reason or another there has been no need to not revert back to Windows:

* Lack of essential hardware support (ie. network cards, USB drives)

* Lack of quality replacement applications for standard desktop tasks (ie. Internet browsing, DVD/CD burning)

* Lack of easy access to installing/trying out applications (who wants to learn how to build an application from source... just to try it out!)

However, two weeks ago I took my first look at Ubuntu.

Basically, to cut a long story short, it's ticked ALL the boxes that previously were not getting ticked.

It installed first time will 100% hardware support on my desktop and I even managed to get it installed on my laptop eventually - after learning how to manually setup Intel video drivers and how to use ndiswrapper to get my network card to function under Linux.  Thankfully, all the info is out there... most of it in the Ubuntu Forums!

OK, so I had two working Linux desktops, but now I needed software (the usual nail in the Linux-desktop coffin for me in the past).

No problem, not even a need to go searching on the internet.  The "Add/Remove" option in Ubuntu is a fantastic idea.  In fact, the whole experience is pretty much 95% positive.  A few things are a bit rough around the edges, but nothing serious.

In summary, I'm writing this on my laptop,, Linux laptop.  I would get rid of the Windows install but I don't think my wife is really for Linux.  (Not just yet)

So will I be "upgrading" my XP desktops to Windows Vista?

No.  By the time I would need to go to Vista I am totally convinced that the Linux desktop will be even better than it is now.  And it's already good enough _now_!

Hats off to the people behind Ubuntu.

You've finally convinced me that the Linux Desktop is here,

Best Regards,
Mark.
