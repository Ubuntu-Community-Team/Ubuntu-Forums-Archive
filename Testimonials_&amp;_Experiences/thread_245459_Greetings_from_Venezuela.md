### Post by odiseo77 on 2006-08-27
Hello all. As you can see this is my first post. I've been a linux user since like 3 years ago or so. I've tried many distros in this time. I started with SuSE 9.0, then I switched to FC3, then to FC4; then I bought a bigger HD and started playing with it, so I installed Debian Etch, FC5, Ubuntu, Slackware, Elive and recently PC-BSD (I installed it yesterday and I starting to dislike it). 

What can I say, Ubuntu is a great distribution. When Ubuntu Dapper was released some months ago I decided to give it a try and now I'm loving it. I joined ubuntuforums.org by that time and I haven't had troubles so far (other than the broken X update last week that I solved easily by reading some guide on internet). There's so much (and good) documentation on the net about Ubuntu that I even haven't had the need of asking any question here. I'd even say that I'm getting bored because everything is so easy (just kidding :D). I mean, there are so many packages available for ubuntu that I don't have to compile anything; simply apt-get or synaptic do the job of installing stuff for me (the same as with debian). Updates are as easy as one click on the orange notifier on my desktop. I even started to like GNOME and now is my main desktop!! 

Well, that's all I wanted to say.

And to the Ubuntu developpers: keep up the excellent work you've done.

Greetings from Venezuela. ;)
