### Post by cYbercOsmOnauT on 2011-05-03
I have a Dell Studio 15 with 4GB RAM and ATI Mobility Radeon 4500 and use Ubuntu 64Bit.

After I upgraded from 10.10 to 11.04 fglrx with 2.6.38 didn't find any fitting resolution for my monitor. Lucky me that I still had 2.6.37 installed and with that I could get to the GUI. 

First time I got there I forgot to change to "Ubuntu Classic" so I saw Unity. For beginners it may be fine and maybe even great. For me to work with my Ubuntu as a web developer it was pure junk. So after about 2-3 minutes of looking at it I logged out and relogged in with the classic gnome. What a relief.

Back to gnome I realised that Compiz doesn't work 100% and I also didn't want to stay at 2.6.37. I needed a long time to fix this by changing back to mesa and reinstalling/reconfiguring fglrx. Same with compiz. It had forgotten almost all settings and I had to set them back.

All in all my upgrade was nothing a beginner could solve easily. But other than my upgrade from 10.04 to 10.10 I could do the upgrade without reinstalling Ubuntu from the scratch. So even with these problems it was better (but still not really good). Ubuntu is still far away from an upgrade without big problems.

Not 3 days and some updates  later I have almost everything fine. Just my workspace switcher at the bottom right only shows the first workspace and not all 4 and Plymouth is not showing up on a boot I just see the grub background until the logon screen comes.

Positive things:

[LIST]
[*]Network Manager is now holds my WLAN connection without losing it time by time. It also connects faster.
[*]Wakeup from Sleep seems to be faster now.
[*]Clicking on a link in Skype or Pidgin doesn't start the wrong browser anymore. (That was annoying)
[*]Sysinfo doesn't crash anymore when I click on "System" (wow :D)
[*]Nautilus now also opens the documents of my iPhone. I don't have to boot Windows anymore when I want to put a movie into my VLC playlist on my iPhone. (yay)
[/LIST]
Greetings,
Tekin
