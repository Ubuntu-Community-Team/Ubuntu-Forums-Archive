### Post by Tsboncompte on 2009-10-25
I'm not an IT guy, I'm not an informatics mayor, I'm not a programmer, a hacker, an open-source guru. At most I'm an open source enthusiast who doesn't particularly likes Microsoft and that would really, from the bottom of his heart, like a world where you could get a free, easy-to-use, and powerfull OS for nothing, and without breaking laws or stuff. I'm a sociologist, and thus my informatics expertise is SPSS, Atlas.ti, and being an intermediate-level windows user.

So I gave Ubuntu a try. It promises to be easy, and free, and powerfull, and totally compatible with the rest of the world (you know, office and such), and I get to keep my Windows installation. I think to myself: awesome!

I'm very sad to say this, because I've been a win-ubuntu double booter for many years now: I'm using Ubuntu since 5.10, and it fails. It's supposed to be easy to use, it's supposed to be quite usable for a non-IT guy like me, **it's not.**

My relationship with ubuntu has always been a bit rocky, but never any problems that were critical. It's just that there's just so much to deal with. It's like those relationships in which your girlfriend just keeps nagging and nagging and nagging you. she wants more sex, then she feels like a sexual object, then she wants you to meet her parents, then she hates that you and her parents get along. She wants to meet your friends, and then she's angry that you don't spend quality time, then she wants to get out more, then she wants you to buy things for her, and tuck your shirt in, and keeps busting and busting your balls non-stop. Ubuntu has always been that kind of thing for me.

And when I ran a "sudo apt-get udate" then "sudo apt-get upgrade", restarted, and found that the operation had deleted the settings file of grub, which means that I now have no ******* idea how to boot using windows, I mean that's it. **** Ubuntu, I'm through, this relationship isn't working. I'm dumping ubuntu for her older sister Debian, or maybe for Mandriva, I haven't figured that out yet.

So Ubuntu newcomers beware, unless your're smarter *and* more tech savy than me, you're goings to spend more time fixing **** that's broken in Ubuntu than your'e gonna spend actually getting stuff done in it. Really, either don't waste your time or become a tech-geek, otherwise, it's not worth it.

The update manager will not run "sudo apt-get upgrade", it will run "sudo apt-get dist-upgrade" which is safer on Ubuntu (to use marketing-speak, it uses a wholistic approach to upgrading the system, rather than just trying to push as many packages as possible to their latest versions).
