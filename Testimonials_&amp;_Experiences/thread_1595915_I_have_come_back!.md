I'd put it as the gateway drug...

You start with Ubuntu and think "this is good enough for me". Then you get the idea to try Fedora. You switch to Fedora for a while and say "oh this owns ubuntu" but then you find faults with Fedora and give openSUSE a go. You then toggle KDE with GNOME and vice-versa. 

You then progress to Gentoo and Slackware. You get obsessed with compiling (useless!) everything and resolving dependencies on your own.

You realize you wasted weeks of your life doing useless crap so you decide to go out and buy a Mac.
