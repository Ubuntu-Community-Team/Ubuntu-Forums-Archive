Angel, sorry it didn't work out. Linux doesn't yet have the same level of compatibility of Windows, though the issue is really with the hardware manufacturers that support Windows, both with drivers and cooperation, but don't pay much heed to Linux and compatibility. Linux by itself isn't much harder, only different, but if you have a computer system that uses hardware or a setup that isn't well supported (the result of a lack of open specs or the manufacturer's support), things can get fairly hairy.

With  a lot of help and compromises, you can sometimes work around that, but if it does not work, or if the time factor is just too high to be reasonable, it may be best to delay the switch to Linux until you buy a new computer. These days, I only buy pieces that I know will work well in Linux -- and I feel that's the best strategy. Buy from those companies that actually realize that Windows is not the only OS in town. :)
