### Post by hopstah on 2006-08-15
I used to be a criminal.  Not in the violent, prison-time sense of the world, but I used to steal every piece of software that I used.  I guess the argument could be made that I'm still a criminal because I do download the occasional song here and there, but as far as I'm concerned, the jury is still out on that one.

So I used to have an estimated $50,000+ of software on my computer.  I didn't use even a fraction of it, but I had it anyway.  Over the years I would hoarde every cool program I could find, try it out, and keep it.  At the time I didn't have a moral dilemma with it, it was fun - but I'll get back to this later.

Over the years I had heard of this thing called Linux.  First it was in a Best Buy ad, when Red Hat made the dismal decision to sell boxed copies of their software through retail outlets.  I scoffed - as much as a 13 year old can really scoff - and thought "who would use that when there's Windows?"  I even tried Red Hat once, and it was painfully slow.  Waiting over a minute for a browser or word processor to open?  Please - there's *no way* that this is better than Windows.

Then in January of 2003 I decided to give the Gentoo distro a try.  I knew plenty about computers, and had read the (lengthy) install document, and managed to get through an install in only 3 days!  Compiling everything from source because it supposedly made for the fastest binaries seemed cool to do back then, except it made for an operating system that felt like a patchwork of crap all thrown together.  Samba didn't feel at all integrated, not to mention that hard drive access was noticeably slower than in Windows (I even benchmarked this one to prove it.  Later, I was told about this DMA stuff....)  So I stuck with Gentoo while dual booting to Windows for the programs that I needed for my field of study.  That only lasted for a couple of months until Gentoo managed to invade my unmounted WinXP partition and screw something up.

Back to Windows.  Eventually Longhorn became the big talk, and I became more and more frightened of being locked into Windows, and also developed a conscience about the software that I had been stealing.  So I decided that I was going to make the plunge into Linux.... right after graduation.  Biding my time while working on my final project using AutoCAD at home (which I had stolen) along with 3D Studio Max (ditto) along with plenty of software most people haven't heard of, I mentally prepared myself for Linux.  I had been planning on going with Gentoo again, to see if things had improved, but then I heard about Ubuntu.  I decided to give it a try, and haven't looked back.  I even managed to convert my 160GB music library from the (failsafe) fat32 in case I needed to run back to Windows, to ext3 once I decided to stick with Ubuntu.

I have since deleted all of the illegal software I had, because who needs it?  There are open source alternatives for almost everything, and non-open source but free stuff for everything else.  I love it and have since made one and a half converts to Linux.

Thanks for the great software, and keep up the good work.  I would love to contribute, but I majored in civil engineering, not electrical. :)
