### Post by Darktrax on 2010-09-03
There is no doubt about it. Audio on Ubuntu can trip you up easy!
The basic audio - Rhythmbox, VLC, YouTube, whatever starts out OK.
You only have to succeed in playing back a MIDI file for it all to stop!
Timidity, RoseGarden, the required presence of jackd, patchage, et al seem to block audio from all familiar routes.

There are enough ways to play - all complicated.
Timidity from command line (Hmm..) does it - if that is all you are doing.
There are things called "modules" :|

For me, who likes to sometimes playback downloaded MIDI (.mid) music, the choice is TWO Ubuntus! One Ubuntu Studio, hacked to lose PulseAudio, and the other - the new upgraded 10.04. Its not that it cant be done. It just that one gets into reading major manuals quite quickly.

Not that I have strayed into a Windows machine recently, but I understand it opens a player that "just works". Yes.. we know that the player probably comes with all sorts of baggage to profile your preferences, but the point is, it manages to make the noise without too much fuss, and has done for a decade. There are so many very skillfully written components to making audio happen. They overlap. They can conflict. And there is a load of very skillfully written stuff that tries to connect them all together. Front-ends, back ends, modules.

Maybe its just me, but I found it difficult, when I just wanted a stop-startable midi player with a play duration indicator that did not also knobble the other audio while it was busy.
