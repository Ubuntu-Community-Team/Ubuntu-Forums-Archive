Couldn't agree more! Life is too beautiful to be hung up over software! Imagine we could very easily have had a world composed only of proprietary, costly software; in fact we did have such a world till about the start of 90s I suppose, then along came the GNU project, BSD, MINIX, and finally Linux, all of which combined to give us the variety we have in computing now!

The great strength of Linux (as compared with say Windows) is the relative modularity of most of its applications. I can have several desktop environments installed side-by-side, use applications written for one, with another (though you do incur a penalty of loading extra shared libraries), and use drop-in replacements for just about every component in the system!

Ubuntu has done more than any other distribution towards the ideal of tighter integration, and ease of use, but I do hope we don't end up emulating Windows/Mac/Android so much, that Linux' strengths are obscured or lost.

Desktop environments must adapt to applications. Applications should not be forced into being tailor-made for any on DE. I hope Linux err on the side of too much variety rather than consolidate too much around a few frameworks which could turn out to be an Achilles Heel later on.
