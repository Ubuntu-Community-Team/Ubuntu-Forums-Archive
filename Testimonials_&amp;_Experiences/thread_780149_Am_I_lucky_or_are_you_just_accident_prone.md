### Post by Delever on 2008-05-03
I was able to solve my problems because, well, I am me.

I was watching my sister the other day to stumble upon random error message by Internet Explorer 7. Her reaction was quick and simple: "wtf", close down IE, fire up firefox, and continue. That actually made me laugh a bit, because she always used IE, even if there was Firefox installed by me.

What I am getting at is this: average user who want to get the job done, won't care to solve some software problem, if they know another piece of software which works. They may be angry, but not enough to get to the forums, register and post in testimonials and experiences. And they may be disappointed, but not enough to care to file a bug report (or even to begin to understand what launchpad is).

That's why, even after solving my lockup problem in some lucky way, I am vocal about it. This particular type of bug worries me alot, because it says "this system may freeze. at any time. if you are unlucky." I don't say that, but that's how it would translate into the mind of average user. And if that user needs to get job done, he would avoid any possibility of such thing happening, therefore - avoid ubuntu.

You may say "we don't need users who are not willing to learn", but that is not learning anymore, that is risk.

I agree that this particular section of forums have already driven huge number of upcoming ubuntu users away. Just look at number of guests who will never register ;). But fixes are coming. I already installed proposed updates on other system, and everything seems to work fine, including fixed kernel.

I am willing to invest more than little time into ubuntu, because I see great future for it. And I am vocal about it. I am doing that because average user won't. Therefore it's like my responsibility to help in any way I can, and not hide any problems.
