### Post by 73ckn797 on 2009-08-10
I have now been using Ubuntu for one year.  There is only one minor issue (flash applet quit working for 2 sites I need to use after a recent update) that I cannot remedy at this time. I can still upload photos but not in bulk. That is "MultiPowUpload" which does not work. Another flash uploader (Arigma Image Uploader) for other sites is still working great.

I no longer have Windows installed on my desktop and only keep it on my laptop for one use. 

I have come to despise having to wait for Windows to load up and be ready to go. The same amount of time in Ubuntu I am already doing my tasks. 

I am still working on getting the wife to use it. If there was a good checkbook program similar to Quicken, she would switch. The available checkbook programs for Linux are just not there yet with a simplicity equal to Quicken. I have looked into too many that are out there and they are just not easy to get up and running with.

The only draw back that I feel is a hinderance to more people using Ubuntu/Linux is that too many apps are written in such a convoluted way and not very intuitive for the average user.

The video editing programs are not easy for the average bear. Kdenlive is the one exception that I have found to be more easier to get up a running with.

These, of course, are just my opinions based upon my level of expertise in adapting to a different OS and programs. I am no expert but know what has worked for myself.

Ubuntu is still my choice and I really have enjoyed it over-all.

Thanks to all who have contributed to the current state of the art in Linux based systems and apps.
