### Post by Nesaskewatch on 2010-05-15
Catchy headline no?  I sat down this evening, after a long day ramming heads with a 95 Jag XJR, and having just finished cutting the grass before the coming rain, and thought, hey I am so glad I can flip up the lid of my shiny new laptop and fire up Linux for a quick troll through the light fantastic.  Recently, I had to suffer through a few agonising days without my new electronic friend, as she hadn't quite gotten her virtual head around having Linux wrapped around her just yet.  Much coaxing and gentle cajoling was needed to sooth her surges and calm her unease.  But after a time, she has settled in to her new role as an open source companion.  I found this labour to be one of love, as I consider myself more than merely privileged, rather honoured that I am able to enjoy such a wonderful union of machine and source code.  Linux is fantastic!  And so is *my* virtual life, now that I am somewhat familiar with Lucid's pleasant demeanor and almost divine intricacies.  No more need I wonder what secret bits of my life are being transmitted south to Redmond; all completely unnoticed but surely expected.  Having to pay for each little piece of something that should have everything I need considering how much I shelled out for it.  Gone are those days, and not soon forgotten.  Now should I need some little gizmo to make something work, all I need do is delve into Linux's capacious reserve of wonderful things and select it.  No need of credit cards.  No need for licenses.  Just a desire to explore, and the patience to learn.  So much, for so little.  Truly a blessed thing.

I just want to express my gratitude to all those who have helped me make the switch, and also to those who unwittingly helped by merely asking for help with their problems and taking the time to provide the solution here.  Mostly I would thank the tireless folks that spend enormous amounts of time developing such a magnificent work of art; for all of us to use and share; never receiving much aside from the occasional thank you, and could you please fix my computer.  

Thank you all for helping make living with Linux such a pleasant experience.  The next several rounds are on me.

Cheers!
