### Post by JDelmoso on 2008-02-26
I attend meetings at Youth First Texas, a non-profit organization for GLBT youth. At the YFT building in Dallas, we have a small lab of twelve x86 computers of varying age, all networked to a router (3 wired, the rest wireless). None of them do a very good job of running WinXP.

I met up with the guy who gets stuck maintaining all of them and asked if he'd be interested in trying out Linux on them. He gave me permission to install on one; I installed to a wired PC as I didn't feel like screwing around with wireless networking. 

I got feedback from one person even before the installation was completed. This guy was quite nearly aroused by the sight of "Line-ux" and proclaimed that he'd never be dragged away from this computer. 

I'll be returning to YFT Thursday to finish configuration and set up a short survey about Ubuntu for people who use the computer (what's the point in installing Linux if you can't gauge its reception?)

I'll post later on the progress at YFT.

Cheers, thanks for a great distro.

(I've been using Ubuntu on and off since Breezy).
