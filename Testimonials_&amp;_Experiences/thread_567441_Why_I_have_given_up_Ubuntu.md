### Post by bep on 2007-10-04
I would love to use Ubuntu, but I have given up.

I have it installed as a secondary boot on both my desktop and laptop.

On the desktop I used it as a main OS for a long time, despite a problem I spent a great deal of time trying to fix: Crackling sound.

On my laptop I finally got my wireless network working, but no sound. My onboard sound chip should be standard (a modern Intel chip that is inside a large percentage of modern computers). I read hundreds of pages pointing to others trying to solve the same problem with the same hardware, so I spent 20 hours compiling and tweaking alsa drivers, no luck.

And I am a software programmer. What about the grannies out there? This should just work.

I know It is hard to support all kinds of hardware, I also know I could buy a "known to work with Ubuntu" computer (but then again, I suspect a kernel upgrade would crank it up), but I would suggest the developers of Ubuntu focus more on the main problems in this OS: Hardware compability. Compiz is nice, but hardly essential.

I have no more time to fiddle around with this. I want a Unix based OS, so I will go for Apple.
