### Post by ejw2076 on 2007-12-06
For the past few years his xp machine has been so clogged with spyware and viruses, it would take 20 minutes to boot up and get into word; then a pop-up would cover the entire screen and wouldn't come down.  So today I spent about 4 hours backing up his pictures and going through a linux install and configuration, then showing him how to use 'his new computer.'  It went mostly like:

"So when do you enter your password?"

"only when logging in and when it says I have updates."

"correct, how do you get your email?"

"click the red fox?"

"no, that is internet, the blue bird is email"

I kept his xp install just in case he needs anything from it, but everything installed without too much trouble.  I expect this install to last years without any problems.
