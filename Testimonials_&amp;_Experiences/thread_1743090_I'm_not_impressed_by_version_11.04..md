### Post by hvysmker on 2011-04-29
I realize it's a little early.  I only installed 11.04 yesterday but, so far, I can't see anything I like about the new GUI.

1. I don't trust “cloud” storage.  It's too easily hackable by individuals or government.  It depends on ISPs.  Mine slows down and goes out regularly.  
2. After an update, I had spend hours reinstalling my favorite programs.  Such is Abiword. I have over a thousand files in that format and the new Libre system doesn't open or save in it.  Also, VLC.  I don't like that music program.  It's too much like Windows.
3. I never did buy into that system of “MY” this and that.  My Documents.  Bull.  I use many partitions and don't like downloads and all my storage on the Systems disk.
4. I'm old-fashioned and don't use those social network programs or the idea that I can't seem to get them off my computer.
5. All those icons make it tougher to find little-used programs, much harder than simple written pull down menus.
6. I do appreciate that I can avoid that GUI, though, and intend to do so.
7. I haven't gone over it extensively yet.  It may be possible to customize a lot of my complaints, but I don't like to contemplate all the time that will take.
8. I especially don't care for that bar down the left side of the screen and that I apparently can't, at least easily, customize it or the size of the icons.
9. I also noticed all the advertisements for Ubuntu products.  Too mercenary compared to past versions.

I hope there are a lot of improvements that I haven't seen yet.  So far, I'm not impressed.

Charlie – hvysmker

compizconfig-settings-manager package is your friend; you can change the size of the icons from ccsm, and turn on the "Dodge Windows" behaviour of the launcher. You can customise it, too - just run the program you want to add, and then when it appears in the launcher right-click its icon and choose "Keep In Launcher".
