### Post by samh785 on 2009-10-24
In my high school here (even though we only have about 1200 kids) there is a group of more tech savvy people who know a bit about computers and Linux. These kids (and 1 teacher) generally have a very high respect for Linux (the teacher even installed Ubuntu on some older computers that were lying around). I've only met opposition to Linux on one occasion in which the person declared to me that Linux was the "ugliest operating system" and that it looked like a cheap rip-off of Mac OSX. I then explained to him that you can customise the way Linux looks very simply (even making it look exactly like Mac OSX if you want to). But once again, that was an isolated incidence.

One thing I've definitely noticed is that people don't seem willing to take the plunge like I did and install a Linux distro as the main OS. I think most of this can be attributed to people being intimidated by the new, and by the efforts that both Microsoft and Apple have made to keep people from switching from their particular OS (iPods certainly weren't *designed* to work in Linux, and Zunes can't at all).

All in all though it's a very encouraging thing for me to look around and see regular people holding Linux in high regard.

### Post by blwizard on 2009-10-24
I'm a computer science undergrad student. Here in my university in Melbourne, it seems like most people around me use ubuntu. Every time I'm in a tutorial room or lecture room, what I hear is always the login sound of ubuntu. The other day, I went to one of my lecturers' office, who invented the Mercury language, to have an exam review, he showed my marks on his ubuntu terminal on his personal laptop. Last week we had a guest lecture from a guy who's part of our university's IR and machine learning research group, he did his presentation on his ubuntu 9.04 remix. It just seems to me that everyone uses ubuntu, and I really like the feel of that. MS doesn't have a market among us computer science students, cuz I only know three people around me who use Windows regularly.

Since the servers in our department are all solaris, and most of our project work has to be done and be assessed on them, so people have gotta learn using command-line first year they get into uni. So I think it's easier for people here to be converted completely to Linux/Unix than people from elsewhere. And I think that's probably also the reason why ubuntu is so popular over here.
