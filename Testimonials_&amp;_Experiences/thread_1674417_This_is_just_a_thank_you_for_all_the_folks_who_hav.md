### Post by Hedgehog1 on 2011-01-23
:D :D I wanted to give a general thanks to the community for your ongoing support, particularly for new users. :D :D

Thanks to your previous threads, when I moved to my new desktop PC and loaded Ubuntu to dual boot with Windows 7 (yeah - I know - but my Sony Vegas video editing and Photoshop run best there), I was able to quickly resolve:

1) The lack of sound on my NVidia GT430 out of the HDMI port

2) VPN setup to reach the office (I cannot tell you the joy I feel running remote desktop from Ubuntu and controlling my Office Windows PC - it feels like cheating, somehow)

3) Updated GRUB to put Windows 7 first in the list so when my wife turns on this PC it goes to the OS she knows.

I moved to the NVidia card to resolve Photoshop/GPU issues.  I found myself laughing that, of course, the audio on HDMI needed a little help for the card. It all averages out.

I use Chrome/Chromium for web browsing, so both side feel the same web-wise.  I use VLC for playing video files, again both OS's feel the same.  There is no iTunes for Ubuntu, but RhythmBox take up a lot less resources and plays all the same music files.  I do prefer the volume/current song display from RhythmBox.

Mostly I just wanted you to know what an awesome job you are all doing.  I found everything I needed without having to post.

But I did want to tell you all THANKS.

As nice as Windows 7 is - I spend most of my 'free' time on the Ubuntu side of my PC.  Thanks all!

:D :D Listening to Beatles 'Hey Jude' from RhythmBox as I type this! :D :D
