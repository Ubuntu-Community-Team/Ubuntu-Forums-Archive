### Post by stinger30au on 2008-08-15
My eldest daughter has an IBM thinkpad laptop model 2628-TTA

im not sure of the cpu speed but it is an intel cpu and not very quick by todays standards and had 256 meg of ram and 30 gig hdd

it had had xp pro on it for some time and it was very slow and no matter how many times you defrag it, it still went slower then a snail and my daughter was ready to smash it to bits with frustration it was that slow.

i have been telling her for ages i was going to put ubuntu on it.

tonight she gave me her laptop and said "dad, can you please stick ubuntu on this, but only if you promise me it will run faster then it currently is"

i said , yes of course it will and i will have it back to you in an hour or two.....

*wrong*

8.04 would not start properly, but i know its ok as i used the same cd to upgrade my desktop machines. same thing with 7.10. only 7.04 worked

and about 300 meg or so of updates later, its working. so quickly burn the updates to cd with APTonCD cos i dont know when Canonical is going to turn off updates for 7.04 and set it up so it prints to lan and add a few extra apps and hey presto...

ask my daughter to inspect machine and see if there is anything else she needs...

i left her in her room to tinker with it i went back about 15 minutes later and she is playing one of the card games on it and tells me everything works fine and minimises the game and shows me she has created links to a few of her favourite apps and games on the desktop and raved about how fast it was and asked if i have dropped some extra ram in to it for her while i had it .

i assured her i had not done so and she was impressed.


So, thanks Canonical / Ubuntu / Mark Shuttleworth for Ubuntu you have saved me and my eldest daughter some major frustration.

no to get my little celeron 400 lethal weapon sorted out and it will be awesome :lolflag:
