### Post by Borbus on 2008-02-15
That's why you should only buy hardware that you know will work with Linux. Things that use a standard interface will always work like SATA disks, most USB storage devices will work (they usually implement a USB IDE or SATA controller with a disk on the other end). Things that might not work (fully) are proprietary things like some MP3 players (but even then, iPod and Sony players all function as standard USB storage devices, even though by default you can't actually get music on to them).

To be honest, if a piece of hardware doesn't work with Linux then it's probably a bad piece of hardware (or highly specialised) and if they don't write drivers for Linux then they don't deserve your money.
