### Post by svref on 2012-05-27
There comes a point in every desktop distribution, when the people contributing to it lose sight of the fact that their users are less capable than they are, and instead of trying to lift them up and empower them, they get distracted by rarified improvements that matter little to the end-user. When that time comes, it&#8217;s time for the user to find a new distro.

For me, Ubuntu is now joining Debian and Red Hat in that pile of discarded distributions. 

The irritants are just too much. What are they, you ask? Here&#8217;s what&#8217;s annoyed me in my last hour of computer use:

**I have to outwit the computer to turn it off**

Last night a friend logged in on her account, then I also logged in. I forgot about her login. When I went to shut down the computer, it instead just logged me out. When I selected &#8220;shut down&#8221; from the login window menu, it just silently failed. For whatever reason, C-A-f1 wouldn&#8217;t get me to a virtual terminal so I could not do &#8220;sudo shutdown -h now&#8221;. Obviously pressing the power button to do a soft shutdown doesn&#8217;t work - this is Linux! So in the end I decided not to <snip> with it and literally pulled the plug. I can see failing a power down if someone else is logged in, but silently failing is <snip>. I can see reinterpreting &#8220;shut down&#8221; as log out, but asking a user &#8220;do you really want to shut down the computer [Ok] [Cancel]&#8221; and then reinterpreting the [Ok] click as &#8220;log out&#8221; is <snip>. Empower your users to make decisions, don&#8217;t lie to them!

**Play DVDs? In 2012? How about a cryptic error message instead!**

What&#8217;s the single most common cause of dvd-playback failure? Obviously, it&#8217;s not having decss installed properly. Given that its 2012, do you think we could have an error message that actually leads the user to the heart of the problem? Something like &#8220;You&#8217;re trying to play an encrypted DVD, but this program does not have the decryption properly installed. Maybe you want to run the script &#8230;.&#8221; would be a whole lot better than just hanging or giving a cryptic error message. Empower your users to do the stuff they clearly are asking to do. 

**&#8216;Start&#8217; button broken**

For whatever reason, when I click the &#8216;start&#8217; button - or whatever Ubuntu calls it - it has no effect. No window-like thing pops up. I guess something isn&#8217;t working somewhere. It seems like this is the lynchpin of their UI, though, so I&#8217;d expect it to be bulletproof.
 
**Why don&#8217;t you...**

I suppose I could file three bug reports per hour of use, but I have better things to do with my life, I don&#8217;t have the expertise, I don&#8217;t expect it to be fixed. I certainly can&#8217;t hack the changes myself - long past are the days when a non-pro human can understand a fraction of the workings of a desktop computer. 

**Conclusion**

I have great respect for anyone who develops free software. It&#8217;s a great service when its done right, and even when its done wrong, I still give props for trying. 

Sadly, Ubuntu seems to have fallen below the critical mass of usability for me. It&#8217;s time for me to leave you, Ubuntu. Hopefully there is a distribution out there that works with me, not against me.
