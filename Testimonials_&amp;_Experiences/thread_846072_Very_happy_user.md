### Post by tsger on 2008-07-01
I am generally very happy using Linux, specifically Ubuntu 8.04 Hardy Heron.  I, like many others here, have gone back and forth between Linux and Windows for years.  I currently dual boot with Windows XP.  Some days I am so frustrated with one OS, I boot into the other one just for a change of scenery, so to speak.  Lately I am spending almost all my time in Linux.  On the occasions when I do boot into XP, I notice  a definite performance hit.  Even after a clean XP install, after applying updates, and getting the antivirus and spyware apps running, and tweaking what I can for performance, Windows just seems really resource hungry and lethargic.  

I do enjoy learning, and Linux has given me lots of opportunities to learn how to do things.  Needing a solution, and researching and finding it, are part of what makes my computing experience fun.  I do agree with others that there are times when this gets tedious.  

I am also a musician, and need a robust, dependable, and flexible environment to do recording and midi sequencing.  I am slowly learning my way around the complexities of Ubuntu Studio, and sound in Linux in general.  At this point I would not say I am familiar enough with this aspect of Linux to be comfortable depending on it alone for professional studio recording use.  I hope this will change in the future, because this is the main reason I keep XP and Cakewalk's Sonar around.
