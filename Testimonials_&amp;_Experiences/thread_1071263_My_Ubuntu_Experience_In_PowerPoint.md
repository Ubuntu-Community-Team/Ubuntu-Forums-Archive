### Post by simonbanyard on 2009-02-16
Slide 6.
"Both the Apple *cooperation*..." It was a *Corporation*, but now Apple refer to themselves as Apple Inc. People that have Windows and Mac also "own" their software. What makes Ubuntu open source is that you can get the *source* code and change it under the auspices of the GPL at no extra charge.

Slide 7.
Open-source software is **not** always free of charge. A good majority of OSS is released at no charge. From [http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html]("http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html") "Free software is a matter of liberty, not price. To understand the concept, you should think of free as in free speech, not as in free beer.". It's an important distinction to make.

Looks like an interesting presentation, hope this helps.
