### Post by Orval on 2006-08-03
Since the end of the 80's I have been using PC's quite a lot for various tasks. Word processing, DTP, spreadsheet calculations, graphics, sound manipulation, Web publishing etc. etc. First using DOS, later nearly all versions of Windows.

Because I don't use the apps I needed professionally, I refused to (or even couldn't) pay for them. So I always had to hunt for pirated versions, including the risk of malware.

Sick and tired of these practices, I tried Suse exactly one year ago. Suse just wasn't my cup of tea. Nice distro, but installing apps more often than not led me to a dependency hell. And I don't like to be dependent. So in October I tried Ubuntu Dapper and I got hooked. Deb is so much better than rpm.

In (K)Ubuntu I just found (almost) all the apps I needed and for many tasks there were alternatives to choose from. No piracy, no fees, no registration codes, perfect. Getting used to my new OS and the new apps wasn't too difficult. Even my computer illiterate wife now uses Kontakt, OpenOffice, digiKam and FireFox without any problems.

I have one big problem though: I cannot choose between KDE and Gnome. Gnome is faster on my system and I like the looks and feel more, but I can customize KDE more, I love the KIO-slaves and the integration of KDE apps. I like KDE apps more than their Gnome counterparts (Konqueror is light years ahead of Nautilus, I prefer Kontakt over Evolution, K3B has more options than GnomeBaker, only Gnomad2 works better than KZenExporer). KDE has some glitches on my system, though, Gnome runs perfectly. So mostly I run Gnome with KDE apps, but sometimes I log into KDE. Depends on the weather, I guess. People who can't choose, shouldn't use GNU/Linux. Maybe that's why MS apps are so popular.

Anyway, computing has never been so much fun. Which is dangerous, because I spend way to much time behind that screen. I can make the system look different every week if I choose to. I have the feeling that, instead of my OS, I am in control of my computer. And I have never had a virus, spyware or even spam since my switch. I have the feeling that most open source apps are more user friendly because they are open source. Everyone can contact the author and request features or add features themselves. Who said Linux is communism? It's ultimate democracy!

Last week a friend of my turned to me in dispair. Her (Win2000) computer had been infected by adware. I tried whole evening to get rid of it, trying all anti-malware app I could get my hands on, but alas. Now she seriously considers installing (K)Ubuntu. Well, that is to say, let me install (K)Ubuntu for her. And I would be very happy to do so.
