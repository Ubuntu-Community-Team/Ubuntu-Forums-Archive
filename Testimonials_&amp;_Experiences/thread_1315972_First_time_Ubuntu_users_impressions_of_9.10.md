### Post by Aaron49 on 2009-11-05
To be honest ive always been a windows loyalist. Why fix it when it aint broken? As this was my first year in college, i decided to buy a netbook, for the simple fact that they are great to have for taking notes, working on papers on the go, etc. The netbook came with windows xp, and i happily used that. 

But when windows 7 came out, and all the netbooks started switching to that, added to the fact that windows is going to cancel extended support for xp, i decided it was time to look into upgrading. Now i didnt exactly have the money to drop on a Windows 7 copy, but a friend of mine did inform me that a new Ubuntu had recently been released. I had the linux stereotype in my head: A confusing command based OS with little to no support for programs and a community the size of an abandoned island in the middle of the ocean.

But i was (and still am) a poor college student. So why not? I made the flash drive and loaded it up, and voila, the installation went off without a hitch, with the exception that i had to redo the flash drive since many people seem to be having a problem with some certain type of formatting on USB drives, but nothing that couldnt be fixed.

I Have been using it for 3 days now, and i can say i am very impressed. The whole using a new operating system is a little different, but nothing i cant adapt to. I don't feel like i can give the OS a review since 1. I have barely used it and 2. I have no experience with the more advanced or powerful capabilities of Ubuntu.

But i can say this: The next netbook or laptop i am getting will most certainly have Ubuntu on it.
