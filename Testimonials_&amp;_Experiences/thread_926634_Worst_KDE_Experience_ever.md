### Post by MarlonDean on 2008-09-22
Hi,

I would like to share my experience with you guys, and maybe have some courage and sense talked into me.

I have been running Ubuntu 8.04 since its release in April. Being new to linux, I was timid at first, but with the help of all you community members, I quickly started booting into Ubuntu only and almost forgot my Vista partition.

What impressed me of Ubuntu was that the installation of my hardware was easy. I heard horror stories of networking with Broadcom, and granted there where problems in the beginning, but i got it sorted. I even got Kismet installed and working on a Broadcom, which some people told me was impossible.

On Friday, I was having a slow day at work, so I formatted my laptop and installed Kubuntu 8.04

Installation went rather smooth, but as soon as it booted I started having chills. Kubuntu was nowhere near as responsive as Ubuntu. My movies and mp3 refused to open with a simple double click, I had to right click and select the open with option. I couldn't get my Nvidia card enabled in Restricted Hardware, no matter how I clicked it would just say 'not enabled' even if there was a tick to it or not. Worst of all, My broadcom wasn't even visible in Restricted Hardware. I had problems with other apps too. I couldn't get Konqueror to use my Huwaei e220, Open Office refused to open word documents (which worked previously), and my bluetooth didn't detect my phone (which it did in Gnome).

After a weekend of frustration, I decided to format and reinstall. I installed Ubuntu 8.04 again, but when I went to restricted hardware, my broadcom card was not visible. So I  kinda freaked out (thinking of the weekend I had with Kubuntu) and booted into vista so I could at least get some work done(My wireless still works in Vista).

It seems that the evil of Kubuntu has soured my computer and that even sweet gnome is now destined to give me problems.

I just want my computer to be the way it was before the horrible Kubuntu incident:confused:

I'm going to jump to the defence of Kubuntu here. It doesn't always feel as though it is as polished as Ubuntu but I think it is enriched with the sheer number of applications provided by KDE. I can almost guarantee that you are going to have some new frustrations with another Linux distribution. You are always going to have some type of problem so long as your hardware is going to require proprietary drivers. Some are available- for example, I have a Broadcom card too but the proprietary drivers have installed correctly- but there will be times when they are not.

A Linux distribution is going to require that you get your hands dirty once in a while. If you aren't prepared to do so then maybe it's best to stick with what you know best. There's no harm in that.
