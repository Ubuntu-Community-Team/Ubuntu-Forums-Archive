### Post by Tyche on 2008-01-08
> **altariel said:**
> will you post any further comments on the computer now that you've used the system for a while?

I apologize for the delay.  I didn't put a watch on this thread, and I should have.  I see that now.  In fact, I've put a watch on any threads that I start or respond to now, so requests like your shouldn't slip past me again.

The 530N is a good, rugged machine.  Not top of the line, but since I'm not a rabid gamer (I've had my shots :)  ) it more than fits my needs.  I selected the 19 inch flat panel screen, and am glad I did.  Wide screen movies work very well on it.  Also, some games give me a larger field of vision (for an ex-truck driver, the wider the field of vision the better).  

As soon as the machine was unpacked, my wife insisted that I get speakers.  And so we did.  Relatively inexpensive Logitech speakers with a sub-woofer.  The sound that comes out of them, for the small room that I'm in, rivals that of a theater or concert hall.

Speaking of concert hall, my "music of choice" is Classical.  Primarily symphonic music.  Using such programs as Amarok, I am able to set up an equalizer that maximizes the sound of an orchestra to the point where one can actually feel that one is right in front of the orchestra, say in the position of a conductor.

In short, I'm enjoying this computer a great deal.  Especially as I just read an article on BoingBoing about the Cray-1, and realize that I have more power and ability with this machine than that "big iron" machine did.  Rather humbling to an old man, to realize that I've got what used to be the power of a main frame computer sitting on my desk.  I live in a Science-Fiction world.  :lolflag:
