### Post by xbleekx on 2010-07-23
My take On Ubuntu is that if your a tech type person it's probably a lot of fun to play with. If your like me and just want a system that works "out of the box"...better stay with windows for now.
  I recently purchased a refurb m6300 with no os. Seemed like a good time to try linux so I downloaded ubuntu 10.04 using my windows machine. I chose Ubuntu because I read it was the most popular version. The download and install on the m6300 went off without a hitch and was easy even for me. 
  I'm not someone who gets excited about discovering the ins and outs of an os, I just wanted to get online. Unfortunately, Ubuntu didn't find my wireless. I poked around a bit but it was a no go. I don't know what ssd is or vpn and I'm not really interested, I just want it to find the wireless on its own.
  I plugged in the ethernet and I was online. I did what I normally do when I go online... I clicked on Firefox. It looked different. Things were out of place but I figured I could get use to it. It only took a couple minutes to realize that this weird (to me) version of the browser was slow to load pages and not slow like a turtle...I'm talking snail slow. Not as slow as my webtv of old but you get the idea. "Maybe this refurb computer is no good" I thought, so I typed the problem into info.com. Sure enough it seems to be a common problem and it was suggested to use Chromium. I don't care for Chromium but I tried it and it was indeed faster but also very buggy. It wouldn't save my preferences and after using it for a while it also seemed to slow down.
  I wanted to see how good the screen looked on the m6300 so I popped in a dvd. Nope. It wouldn't play. The pop up said I was missing something but after clicking and clicking...I couldn't find it, whatever it was. I was frustrated so I shut it down and turned on my Vista machine. It may be the most hated os ever but it works. 
  The next day I did some research and people who seemed to be in the know suggested using an older more stable version of Ubuntu. I down loaded and burned a cd copy of ubuntu 9.10. and again, it was an easy install and I'm using it right now to write this. 
I'm happy to say that my trusted and loved Firefox seems to be back to normal and is working fine. Sadly, the wireless still doesn't work and I still cant play a dvd... I'm tired of messing with it.
  Note to Ubuntu developers... Why are you spending your time messing up Firefox when you haven't got the basics right yet? I like to surf the net, stream videos, watch dvds and use email and Skype. Pretty much everybody I know is the same (okay, I know dvd's are old school to some of you young people but I like them).
   I don't need built in photo editing software. If I can retrieve pictures from a memory card and email them without jumping through hoops I'm happy. I don't need video editing software I just want to be able watch a dvd. I don't need a built in email client. Myself and everyone I know use Hotmail or Yahoo or Gmail. 
  If you develop an os that does the basics and works "out of the box"  I will happily support your efforts the best way I know how...by giving you money for your product... whether I have to or not.
