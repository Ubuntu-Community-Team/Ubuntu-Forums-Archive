### Post by magnoliablossom on 2006-08-06
I'm a dual-booter.  I still have XP and I believe I will probably have it for a while until I and my family become more comfortable with Ubuntu.

Ubuntu is my first experience with any type of Linux system.  It has been frustrating at times which is why I decided to wait until I had all the kinks worked out before I gave my honest opinion of it.

It's funny really how my views of Microsoft and Linux have changed over the past few weeks.  I actually had no qualms at all with MS and tried Ubuntu only because I was curious about Linux.  The Linux following may be small but it's growing.  I was hearing more and more about it from the people I'd talked on the internet and some of the other forums I went to for certain applications talked about Linux as well.  I decided to give it a try.  I asked a question or two about which distros were good for beginners and the name Ubuntu kept coming up.  People were in love with it.

I first checked out the Ubuntu main site so I could get a look at it.  I thought it was beautiful.  I've never really been a fan of brown but they actually made it appealing.  Then I browsed the forums for a few days as well reading over some of the problems people were having with their systems.  It sure seemed like a lot to me.  But after a little while I figured out which problems might apply to my system and started looking for tutorials and solutions before I even installed it.

I had some major problems at the very onset.  I bought a new hard drive because my original was rather small and I wanted to give them both, XP and Ubuntu, plenty of elbow room.  My problem started with the fact I didn't update my XP to sp2.  Once I did that though it was pretty much smooth sailing in setting up my dual-boot system.

I've found that most of my problems come from errors on my part and not Ubuntu's.  I believe that if most new users were honest they'd say the same.

There are a few guides and tutorials I have found that made things easier for me.  I'm going to give their links here in the hopes that other Linux newbies will find them helpful.

For setting up my modem I used this link and it works well.  You have to follow it exactly as it says and by all means CUT & PASTE it.  Don't type it in because sometimes that O might be an O or a zero.

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=198730&highlight=lucent+winmodem+install]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=198730&highlight=lucent+winmodem+install")

To set up your codecs for mp3s, videos, whatever, as well as java and flash in your browser use this:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_dapper#How_to_use_Easy_Ubuntu]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_dapper#How_to_use_Easy_Ubuntu")

And by all means bookmark this link here because it will keep you from having to ask the same how to questions over and over:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_dapper]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_dapper")


I think now I can safely say I love Ubuntu.  I did have some problems in the beginning but as I said earlier, the majority of them were my errors.  But the things I've noticed in Ubuntu verses my XP are undeniable.  My Ubuntu boots a lot faster.  My internet performs better.  I haven't had any error messages while working in Ubuntu.  I rarely go into XP now.  When I did I couldn't believe how sluggish it felt moving around in it.  

Maybe I'll eventually get rid of XP.  I don't know.  I don't use it myself anymore.  But still...until I become more comfortable with Ubuntu, it's nice to know it's there *if* I need it.

~* Ash
