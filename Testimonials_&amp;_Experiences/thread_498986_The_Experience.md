### Post by tcoffeep on 2007-07-12
So, it's been a full month now since my complete switch from Windows XP to Ubuntu. It's been long and tedious, and I've had to ask alot of questions, but in the end, it worked out. I look at my friends on Windows, and how they complain on how slow it is, but the second I bring up Linux, they blank out, and ignore me.

I don't understand it, but I pretty much did the same thing, 'til a magazine published a straight-forward guide to installing Ubuntu. I was scared to try it out, being a Windows user for my whole life, but I leaped. Eventually, I took another step, and deleted Windows, along with the secret partition for the back-up, and haven't looked back since.

The only problem is the compatibility with ATI graphics cards, but by the end of the year I hope to switch to a better graphics card to get full support. Anywho, just wanted to say, with enough learning, I hope to become a tester, and even more, I hope to be a help in the community.

Wish me luck all, for my journey has only just begun.
