### Post by almigi on 2008-12-03
Well, I think Ubuntu has won another full time user.  That user, of course, being me.

This is not my first attempt at converting to the Linux lifestyle, however.  I tried years ago to "go Linux" on a Compaq Presario Laptop.  After going through a few distros, and learning a little bit about Linux, laptops, and Winmodems, I decided that I wasn't ready to embrace Linux just yet.

Fast forward to present day, I recently bought a new computer, and I decided to give Linux a whirl again.  I decided to give Ubuntu a try.  A couple of weeks ago, I downloaded and burned the amd64 version of 8.10.

Not wanting to wipe Windows from my system just yet, I decided to set it up as a dual boot.

My first day using Ubuntu was a good one.  I had a few questions, which I was able to find the answers for on various websites (including Ubuntu's own help section).

This is the computing experience I want.  It does just what I want it to do.  The system is stable and fast.  It's as easy or hard as I want it to be.  If I want to only play it safe and just use the Gnome to do everything, I can.  If I want to get my hands dirty, I can do that too!

I really am in control.  I look forward to learning more about the heart of the system.  I want to see what this baby can do.  I can't foresee me booting into Windows anytime soon.  As a matter of fact, I was considering reinstalling Ubuntu, this time using the entire hard drive and saying goodbye to Windows for good.

I am finally ready for Linux!

- Alan
