### Post by blippy on 2007-10-20
Ubuntu is my favourite OS. Here's some random thoughts about what I like:
* The fonts. Nice, small, and attractive. Many distros have really bad fonts. The fonts in Ubuntu are in the same league as OS X
* The ATI drivers. It makes the difference between a screen that looks like unprofessional garbage, and one that looks attractive
* Flash - indispensible for visiting YouTube
* Gnome versus KDE: I used to like KDE better, but now I think it just looks ugly. Also, I think Gnome does a few things better than KDE. 
* Gnome versus Xfce: I used to think that Xfce looked worse, but now I think Xfce can be tweaked to look positively beautiful. I prefer Gnome, though. Although it is slower, I don't have to mess around as much. It has a lot of things that work just as I expect them to (date appearing on the panel, volume control correctly mapped to my keyboard, and so on)
* OS X: a good OS, but I find that Ubuntu actually does usability a bit better. Plus, I prefer the simplicity of Ubuntu as opposed to the "black box" approach of OS X.
* repos: a big repository of packages is important to me, and Ubuntu wins out over many other distros.

I've tried many distros. This year I have tried PCLinuxOS, Mint, Mepis, Zenwalk, OS X, XP, sidux, Belenix, and probably some others which I don't recall. Going further back, I have tried Mandrake, Slackware (good distro!), Suse, Fedora, Red Hat, and lots of others. Of them all, I keep coming back to Ubuntu, for one reason or another (e.g. sidux is quick to install but is often broken, takes up too much time to administer, Slackware is fast and solid but becomes a pain if there are lots of dependencies involved, and so on). I'm still keen on trying one of the free Solaris's, though.

Some quibbles:
* my UK iMac keyboard still has problems with some of the keys - grrr
* I don't like the theme. Fortunately, I have quickly switched to a OS X look - which is waaaaay better
* Firefox crashing on YouTube
