### Post by keljaden on 2010-04-07
Well it has taken two years, and I am happy to say that with the 10.04 release of ubuntu and mint 9, I will be officially removing Windows 7 from my desktop.  My biggest setback for the longest time was that my games would not run.  Now as and advocate for crossover I run/test everything just like in Windows (okay i have to give up shadows in MW2, but I can live with that).  I decided to boot back into Windows to starts saying my goodbye's to it and I was immediately disgusted by how it looked.  I set a goal for myself to not boot into Windows for a week and after that week I was unhooked from my Windows addiction.  There have been and still are some bumps in the road with linux, but I truly feel much more at home here.
I am really thankful to the community for all the support they have given me, and another thanks to the developers of Both Ubuntu and Linux Mint for creating the most wonderful OS experience I have ever had.

For other users thinking about switching, if you give Linux an honest chance (like not running Windows for at least a week).  You will see why you need to not infect your system with Windows ever again.
