### Post by FormatSeize on 2011-10-30
11.04 this, and 11.10 that. All the complaints. All the "this is buggy," or "this is slow" or "my computer boots in 10.00000000000000007 seconds when it should boot in 9.99999999998876545 seconds". 
 
All rubbish. Ubuntu is good. This is good, quality software that the devs may or may not have thought about before they released it to us. I've got no complaints about this. Even if they didn't think about it.
 
Mostly, it's a hardware error or user error (or a combination of the two) that causes bad things to be said about Ubuntu. So choose one: I made a mistake; My hardware isn't appropriate.
 
Anyway, there's a point I'd like to make. And it's mostly about the whole "I'm going back to M$ Winblows Mi(r0$0ft" thing. From my personal experience, just as many things break on Windows "out of the box" as they do on Ubuntu "out of the box". So if you go buy a Windows system, or Mac system, of course things are going to work well because *somebody already did the thinking for you*. That is, until you try to do something that that particular person did not care to think about. 
 
Along comes Ubuntu, and no one did the thinking for you. You have to do it yourself. A computer user thinking? Unheard of. You have to think about your hardware. You have to think about your software, in addition to having to think about your hardware drivers. 
 
Will this work? Will this break something? Will this make my computer a brick? And this is not to say that people that ask for help with GNU/Linux don't think when they encounter problems (well, I actually don't know... most of the time they either Google or are told to Google (whatever happened to figuring things out?)). 
 
This is getting lengthy.....
.... I'd just like to say that "working out of the box" is overrated. And I just broke one of my computers when trying to install something. On another machine that has Ubuntu 11.10, everything went well. 
 
I like this OS. It's good.
