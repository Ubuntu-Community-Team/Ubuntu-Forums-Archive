### Post by BuddyThirteen on 2011-05-28
I have not yet "upgraded" to 11.04. But I thought I'd go ahead and start   trying out Banshee as a sort of "easing in" idea. My Ubuntu comp is   really the main entertainment center of the living room. So it needs to   do only a few things really well: Hulu (and other internet TV), Play   DVD's, and play music. (Obviously not Netflix, cuz Netflix doesn't   support Ubuntu -- Gotta use my Wii for streaming Netflix. Ugh)

Rhythmbox has worked great for me up till now. It has a few issues, but   nothing that really hurts my user experience. For example, it seems   completely incapable of loading album art. But I've been using Picard   for tag editing lately (I'm kinda obsessive about that sort of thing,   and Picard works great), which also finds album art about 95% of the   time. Rhythmbox is a both good and bad for podcasts (why won't you play   the next one without me manually having to click it?!), and I'd love to   use them in my MP3 player, but Rockbox doesn't seem great for that (it   mixes them in with the songs, mostly).

Anyway, my first thoughts after opening Banshee and loading in my music library:
"This interface is horrible!" But it wasn't really. That was just my   brain saying "This isn't what I'm used to!" On further inspection, the   interface is actually almost identical. Artists on the left, albums on   the right, songs at the bottom. Only it's got cool album art instead of   text. I almost hated that--because it was different--but I learned to   love it quickly. The majority of my music collection is ripped from   physical CD's that I own, so it's great to visually see the CD there.   Much faster than reading text.

The row of controls at the top I find a bit lacking. I really like   Rhythmbox's buttons. Very functional. Banshee is missing a bunch of   really useful buttons. But that's not a super problem, since I usually   keep my music minimized to the sound menu anyway, all I need is back,   forward, and play/pause. Which is exactly what it has. But what's with   the tiny microscopic miniscule slider for playback? What if I want to go   to a certain part of a song? You've got literally three buttons up   there, don't you think you could maybe stretch that slider to fill the   space? I kinda like the "now playing" display of the album art and   title/artist/album, it's better than the purely textual one in   Rhythmbox. But again, it sits there next to the slider, there's a ton of   wasted space, and then the volume icon. Stretch that slider!

The left menu. I do like it better than Rhythmbox. Except it has video.   What? Why? I needs you for music. I have VLC for video. And as far as I   can see, there's no way to remove this. Otherwise I like the menu.  And,  before, when I plugged in my music player, Rhythmbox would display  the  generic mountpoint name of the device. But you know what Banshee  says?  "Rockbox Device" -- I don't know why, but that strikes me as  awesome.  How'd you know I put rockbox on there? Time to sync! Oh,  you're deleting  all my music from my music player.

Ratings. Rhythmbox doesn't save your ratings to the actual file, which   is a bit of a peeve for me. I sync my files based on rating (only four   and five star music make the cut--why would I want to listen to music I   don't like?) But my music player (not mp3 player, since the majority is   ogg, duh) couldn't show those ratings because they weren't part of the   file. This also creates a problem for me switching over. Banshee sees   only 130 songs that are "Favorites" because I must've rated those ones   back in Windows when Media Player saved the rating to the file. So now I   have to go through my entire collection and re-rate things. Not a huge   deal, and more of a Rhythmbox problem than a Banshee one. Only peeve   here is the lack of star outlines. I don't like that they're invisible   till I mouse over them. I literally have no target until I hit said   target.

This, by the way, is why it emptied my music player. Rhythmbox loaded it  with all my top-rated songs, which Banshee now sees as unrated, so it's  removing them. But that's okay, I'll start rating and re-filling that  thing.

Also podcasts. I had about 50 podcasts subscribed and no easy way to   import that list into Banshee. So I'm guessing (I haven't tried yet)   that I'll have to manually "Add Podcast" and copy/paste the URL of all   of them one at a time. But I do look forward to using the Miro guide, so  I can subscribe to a bunch of podcasts that will update too much and  I'll get overwhelmed and then never listen to any of them until I find a  better way to put them on my music player (in Rhythmbox, I tried  syncing music to the player, and podcasts to a a microSD card, which  sort of worked, but then Rockbox stopped seeing the files on the card  for some reason).

In the preferences for Banshee, it also has the option "Sort albums by   year, not by name" which is BRILLIANT. In Rhythmbox, when I ripped from a   CD, I sorta cheated by doing the Album Sort Order as "YYYY - Album   Title" so that I could get this same effect.

HOWEVER. What the hell is up with the skippy audio? This is really   annoying. I know that the windows alpha has this as a known bug, but is   it there for linux too? I even bumped the nice to -10, and when   damn-near any other process needs CPU, the audio cuts out. Even when   Banshee itself is doing something (like loading the library or syncing   my player) it overpowers its own audio.

Actually, beyond just the audio thing, the whole app does seem a bit   sluggish and unresponsive. Move my mouse over a button and sometimes   take a full second for the button to respond. The window greys out   regularly, which is something Rhythmbox only did on startup (while   spinning up the external drive where the music lives).

A minor thing. When I first started trying out new audio players, I   tried one that had song ratings in the sound menu. I think it was   exaille. I liked that. Then I could play my music that was unrated, and   just open the sound menu to rate it, instead of bringing the whole app   back up. That would be nice, especially now that I have to re-rate   almost my entire collection. Not a big deal, though.

Rhythmbox is a GREAT lightweight app that does exactly what I need it to   do: Play music unobtrusively. It has some minor issues that I mostly   overlook.

So, all in all, I love Banshee so far. The interface and options are   wonderful. But the audio cutting out is a HUGE issue. Is there anything I   can do to fix this? Or do I just have to make sure that Banshee is   pretty much the only running process using CPU?

I'll probably stick with Banshee for now, I just hope the issues go away as time and updates go on.

</ramble>
