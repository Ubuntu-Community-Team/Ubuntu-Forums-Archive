### Post by aaronp on 2010-05-30
Hi guys,

Just thought I'd share my new printing experience with you all.
I'm running Lucid and have been running Ubuntu since Gutsy.

I have preivously owned a Canon Pixma ip3300 and whilst I've been able to get it to work 'ok' with Ubuntu, it's been an ongoing struggle and the setup + ongoing problem resolution has been annoying to say the least.

Well, it finally died so I decided to buy a new printer, looking for the cheapest I could find with some evidence of good Linux support.

I took a look at D1ck Smith Electronics and found a HP Deskjet D5560 (with wireless capabilities) on their website for $29. Surely a mistake? $29?
No, I called my local store and they said they were $29 and that they had heaps of them. Usual price $89
I went straight down and snapped it up. It's a basic inkjet with a black and tri-color cartridge and it pretty much just prints.
From what I had read, setting it up (especially wireless) was going to be tough. 
Opening it up I found the instructions just told me to 'put in the setup CD and read the on-screen installation instructions'. Luckily, I had my wife's laptop which has W7 on it so was able to do this. Otherwise I may have struggled to set up the wireless.

However, once it was in, it told me how to set up the wireless, plug it in briefly by USB so it could get the wireless settings from the laptop and within 5 minutes it was set up with my wireless LAN. I went into my router, gave the printer a static IP address and then connected my Ubuntu PC and laptop to it via the WLAN. Within 60 seconds I had connected to it via the WLAN and printed a perfect test page. No mucking around, nothing extra to download, no issues with the print alignment etc.
I'm amazed. I can't believe how much time I've wasted with printing over the past few years.

And, on installing hplip-gui (in repos) I've also got a full GUI toolbox for the printer.

Anyone in Australia, if you're looking for a printer that just works straight away on Ubuntu (disclaimer - I've had it less than a day so far!) and an absolutely awesome bonus of wireless printing, get to D1ck Smith and grab one.

I'm not entirely sure how you would configure the wireless settings without a Windows PC from which to run the install CD, but I'm sure there's some smart people that would know how.
Even if you simply connect it via USB to start with, the hassle free setup and <$30 price is worth it for me.

Looking at the list of supported models with hplip driver, I'm pretty sure I won't be getting a Canon again unless the support for Ubuntu gets much better
