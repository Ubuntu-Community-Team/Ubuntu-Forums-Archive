### Post by AureiAnimus on 2011-07-10
hiya everyone,

I now have my laptop for nearly two months, which means I've been using Ubuntu (11.04) for that time. Before that I ran 10.10 though VirtualBox on my (dad's) desktop. I came across ubuntu through the Google Code-in ([http://code.google.com/opensource/gci/2010-11/index.html](http://code.google.com/opensource/gci/2010-11/index.html)), where I discovered how though "make && make install" is on a windows machine. 

I can't say it's been a breeze, but it is certainly fun. While I wouldn't recommend Ubuntu (or any distro for that matter) to people who don't like tinkering with their computer, I do recommend it to people who like computers. I got to test a live-cd in the store before buying the laptop, so I knew it was at least workable. However, it turned out not to be that simple. The touchpad being recognised as mouse annoyed me to no end, the Nvidia issues i could live with for now, since I usually use AC power. I managed to fix my touchpad somewhat with a python script to enable switching it on and off and disable it while typing. However, as awesome as the open-source community is, within 2 months all the solutions have found their way to me. I now have 3-4 hours of battery life, two-fingerscrolling and the fn-keys work. 

Ofcourse, there are also purely postive things: The update-manager:awesome, Software center:awesome, workspaces:yay (while at first I used them very little). And then there's unity. Though I think it needs improvement, since it's not very intuitive at this time, I feel the basis is promising. I'm hoping I don't need windows for my studies, because I can't really imagine going back to it. (In that case: dual-booting is my friend)

Well, I might start contributing to ubuntu some time in the future, if i have the time, but if you contribute to it already and you read this, give yourself a pat on the back. You've earned it! :D
