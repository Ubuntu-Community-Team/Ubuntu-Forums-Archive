### Post by cdekter on 2009-08-23
Yup that's right - last night I finally lost patience with Jaunty and installed Hardy on my laptop. Compared to Jaunty, I can hardly believe how smooth and responsive it is! With the current version of my favourite apps installed from PPAs (OpenOffice, Firefox), I have everything I need on an extremely stable and fast operating system. 

I have a Dell laptop with Intel GMA965 graphics. I don't think I'm the only person who feels that everything has been getting slower and slower since Intrepid, and I don't just mean the graphics. But, with my Intel graphics, I'd pinned my hopes on the new drivers coming in Karmic. Alas, I just tried alpha 4 of Karmic and the graphics performance is, to put it mildly, abysmal. In its current state I would have to say it would be utterly unusable on my laptop. 

I guess I'll be sticking with Hardy for the time being, and hoping that by the time the next LTS release rolls around, the problems with X and the Intel graphics drivers have been resolved. And hopefully some fixes for kernel performance regressions (they do exist, as Phoronix benchmarks demonstrate).
