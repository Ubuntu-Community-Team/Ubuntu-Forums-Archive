### Post by LT1Caprice57L on 2007-09-06
Alright, here goes.

My mom just bought herself a new laptop, a TOTL 13.3" MacBook.  She in turn gave me her old laptop, a Sony Vaio PCG-K33.  It came to me with one of the nastiest viruses I've ever seen.  I may be a newbie to Linux, but I am certainly "not" a newbie with computers.  Been using them for years, I've used DOS 3.0, 5.0, 6.0, 6.2, 6.22, Windows 3.0, 3.1, 95, 98, 98SE, XP home, XP Pro SP2, IBM OS/2 2.0, Warp 3.0, and on the "other side" of the hardware world, Mac OS 8.5, 8.6, 9.0... You get the idea.  I've built computers, fixed them, you name it, but nothing was fixing this aside from a wipe & reinstall.

I proceeded to do this with XP Pro SP2.  Upon install, graphics chipset was not recognised, screen resolution was not recognised, wireless was not recognised, and the fan control was not recognised.  I had to go back to my desktop unit, download all the drivers from Sony's website, burn them to a CD and install them one by one.  What a PITA.  When all was said and done, my video worked at the proper screen resolution and I had wireless, but performance left something to be seriously desired.

It was at this point that I'd remembered about the days I wanted to try out Linux, back in '99 or so, but was hampered by 33.6 dialup and was too much of an impatient SOB to wait 6-10 weeks for a free CD.  NOW, however, I have 6MBit down speed and CD burners and what-not.  Did some googling, found that Ubuntu seems to be the current leader for compatibility and ease of use in the Linux world, downloaded the Feisty Live CD image and burned it.  Loaded it up on the laptop, to find that it recognised everything without issue.   Wireless, graphics chipset, screen resolution and all - it knew exactly what was going on from the get go.  The fan was running at low speed unless otherwise needed, like it should.  I wasted no time installing it.

It's been *exceptional.*  This is honestly the first OS, aside from DOS, I've ever installed where I didn't have to mess with -ANYTHING- to get it to work. (and you see the list of OSes I've used over the years.)  Performance is like it should be, and blows my much slower desktop out of the water. Webpages load at lightning speed and remain usable even when Flash is present on a webpage.  Windows couldn't boast the same.

I'm here to stay, guys.  Helluva job you're doing and many thanks for the efforts taken by the developers and everyone else in the community.
