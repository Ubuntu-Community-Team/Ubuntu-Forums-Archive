### Post by j7%&lt;RmUg on 2009-12-14
About [six months back]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1212958") i posted about my first impressions of using ubuntu, this is just a big update to that post really.

My first use of ubuntu was when i requested a liveCD of 8.10, my first impression of it was 'wow' it was awesome and even though i had my fair share of issues with it the first time around i stuck with it and fixed many problems such as flash, java, sound and net connection.

I then just mass installed many, many programs to see which ones i liked best, then when jaunty came out i installed that and found that my sound worked much better and java and flash had vastly improved. While using jaunty i tested out many DE's, window managers, themes and desktop setups.

I now currently use ubuntu 9.10 with emerald + compiz, i am also hosting a project on launchpad and regularly participate on these forums, in irc and on mailing lists. I also now run an ubuntu based server and its current uptime is 127 days.

Sure i still have ALOT to learn but im optimistic about it now. No longer is there any need for me to download cracked proprietary software, scan for and remove hundreds of viruses a day or even spend much time maintaining and administering my systems, i now find i have time for so much more and i feel that this is just the beginning for me.

No longer do i go "oh damn, i have to do that assignment" i say "yes! i can use open office".

In conclusion, ubuntu has changed my life, i really mean this too. Not only has it made my computing activities WAY more interesting and ALOT easier, i now enjoy the challenges it presents me with.

P.S. Just to clear up any rumors: no i do not sleep with my karmic liveCD.

P.S.S. Although i did think about it at one point.
