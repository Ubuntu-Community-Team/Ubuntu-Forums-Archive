### Post by sohosources on 2007-11-02
Hi, Gang:

Been a PC weenie for 25 years...Windoze expert...Linux newbie (but not clueless) :)

Been wrangling with Gutsy this week and mostly have it tamed, running VMWare Server, VLC, behaving nicely on the XP network, etc.

Thought I'd try Fluxbuntu cuz I like speedy systems. My existing lightweight speed champ is Puppy Linux, which is nicely intuitive and super fast. It's not as nicely appointed as Ubuntu, however, so I wondered whether Fluxbuntu 7.10 might be a workable middle ground.

Nope.

It's pretty much "Suxbuntu.' 

It got my 1680 x 1050 screen resolution set up right out of the box, which is swell...but the wallpaper is revolting...and the distro, which is designed to be easy, fast, intuitive, yadda yadda yadda, is so intuitive that I (and others, judging by the posts I've read), can't even change the Desktop wallpaper without invoking some esoteric part of Rox Filer or typing some crap in a terminal command line! The same goes for adding icons to the "start" menu equivalent!

How lame!

As I type this, the text on my screen is a pale blue that I can barely read. I did change the overall theme to something that made the menus readable...and it must have made my text boxes tootie-fruity.

Lame, lame, lame!

There's a "kernel" of ingenuity here...but unless you're a Suxbox fanatic, you won't be able to make it play in Peoria without enduring the pain required to simply manipulate and understand the GUI, let alone the guts of this "lightweight, easy to use" distro...

Good luck.

My search for an Easy But Not Bloated Linux Desktop continues...

--Kirk

### Post by rxKaffee on 2007-11-05
What is all this about desktop wallpaper commandline business? Any so called "windows expert" should be quite quick to hit their right mouse button and look for a properties menu. While you won't find "properties", right clicking on any of the desktop icons gives you an option to change the desktop background. It even has a filebrowser to choose the image!

And for adding entries to your program menu (this is whats meant by "start menu" right?), it is handled automatically when you install most programs from synaptic/apt-get. If you find some program who's package doesn't support ubuntu's standard menuing, then guess what? Fluxbuntu has a nice click-through for you here too. Bring up your program menu(right click on desktop, not on desktop icons), Apps>Tools>fluxmenu. Its quite a shame though that you actually have to typeout the name that you wish to appear in your menu...:roll:
