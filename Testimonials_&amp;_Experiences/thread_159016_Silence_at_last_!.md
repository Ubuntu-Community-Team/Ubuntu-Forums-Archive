### Post by cophi on 2006-04-12
I've been enjoying ubuntu for over a year now. Ubuntu was my first real contact with linux, and I can say it has been a wonderful experience from the first Live CD I inserted in my pc to the installation of Edubuntu yesterday!

At first it was a humbling experience, having being used to knowing my way around windows and being used to cleaning my system, re installations, the works... 

And then ubuntu, from windows poweruser to a user in a flash! I loved, everything "just worked". and when I did have a question, there  was a great community. 

I tried to do something back by distributing cd's amongst my friends (all 3 of them :D ), my family, and just about everyone else I know... I noticed some people really seem to be hooked on the struggle with their windows machines... go figure? 

Anyway... back to "the silence". I was trying to find a really silent pc for my livingroom (the girlfriend doesn't like being in our small computerroom, didn't like the noise of the windtunnel, etc...)
I looked at the mac mini, loved its silent working, didn't like the price (all it should be able to do is just internet, mail and office.So it seemed too much money for underusing the mac) 

Then I looked at Edubuntu, at first underestimated it a bit, thinking it was just fun and games (and educational). But then I read about its use of thin client / server infrastructure. I looked how hard it would be to transform my existing Ubuntu install to do the same. And I chose the easy way out. :D 
And just installed Edubuntu instead! First I tried it with another (but diskless) fat client (PII) (connected through a hub, should be a switch, I know.) Installation went flawless! Booted the (fat) thin client, worked great!

Now I'm looking for a real thin client (around € 199 for a real small one) (or a real cheap laptop), install it in the livingroom and enjoy the silence!

Thank You Ubuntu, for making it so easy! (and dare I say, cheap?) :D 

And thank you guys, for all the times you helped me , without even knowing it.
