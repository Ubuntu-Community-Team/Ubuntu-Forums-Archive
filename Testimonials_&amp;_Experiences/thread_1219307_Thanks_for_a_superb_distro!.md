### Post by Marflus on 2009-07-21
I've been a Windows user since the first computer I ever owned, and I have to say it always served me well enough, since the vast majority of problems I came across I could solve, being fairly capable with computers.

 

 I was never *quite* satisfied with it, though, the gradual slowdown of my system always annoyed me requiring regular defrags and reinstalls, and I hated the restrictiveness.
 

 The first time I tried out Linux was for the wrong reasons and so predictably I didn't last. I'd seen a friend using Compiz, and although I was fully aware of what Linux was and how it was different, I installed Ubuntu (Hardy) for Compiz and Compiz only. As I said, I inevitably got bored of Compiz soon enough, and since I'd not taken the time to worry about what the other aspects of the OS could do, I just saw the negatives. So I removed Ubuntu, because it was taking up space on my Vista drive that could better be used on random software/games I wanted. Sigh.
 

 I lasted a little longer on windows, then, and it was only about 2 months ago that Ubuntu entered my head again. I was tired at last of having to pay for everything on windows, and of the lack of control I had over my own computer. So, for the right reasons this time I downloaded Jaunty, the latest release.
 

 Having one of the Intel chips affected by the driver issues that Jaunty had, Compiz wouldn't work when I first installed. A blessing in disguise, I feel, since it meant I had time to explore the system properly without the rabbit-in-headlights attitude. Eventually Compiz started working again, and I couldn't be happier with the OS. I still have a Vista partition taking up most of the space on my fairly small hard disc, which I've used once in the last two months, but I'm working on shifting stuff over from that bit by bit so I can finally wipe it out sometime. Only thing is, I've done so much customising to Ubuntu I'm not sure I want to reinstall it, and since it's installed inside windows there's no alternative I'm aware of. Ah well, it'll give me something to do over the long summer holidays, I guess.


I know there are faults, and for every one there seems to be a user determined to run Ubuntu, Linux, and seemingly the whole Open Source ideal into the ground on the basis of that fault alone, but I've not yet found a problem that I can't happily ignore or organise some kind of amicable fix for. Well, apart from my iPod touch, but hey, I'll think of something.

 

 Thanks to everyone, for altogether a superb alternative to the standard PC. You're all doing a great job.

I always try and keep in mind that eyecandy does little for the actually computing experience (outside of the visual feedback). Similar to others I customize my desktop, but always keep it minimal. Hate wasting memory just to support eyecandy.

The ability to install various applications (for free), settling on the one that meets the majority of our (family) needs or expectations - is truly remarkable. Having dealt with proprietary software for most of my computing life, it's a refreshing change and one we really appreciate.
