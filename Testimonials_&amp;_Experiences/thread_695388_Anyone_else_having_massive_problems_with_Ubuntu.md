### Post by tashmooclam on 2008-02-13
I experienced two problems, but not too massive. The first is if your computer has a screen resolution of 1024 x 768, you must make some changes in the startup manager, or it will not boot up normally nor in a reasonable time. Fixing that was crucial. 
My second problem was wireless. The solution was removing a Broadcom mini card and replacing it with an Intel card. Since the Intel wireless minicard drivers are in Ubuntu 7.1, I had wireless working very easily after swapping the card. The cost was $25 on ebay. 
I made my Ubuntu 7.1 disk on an Apple laptop and tried to burn it slowly. If you experience severe problems, try making a new CD, and/or try reinstalling it. 
In a few weeks you can forget your troubles and buy a nice Everex laptop running gOS Linux for $400 at Walmart. 
Good luck.
