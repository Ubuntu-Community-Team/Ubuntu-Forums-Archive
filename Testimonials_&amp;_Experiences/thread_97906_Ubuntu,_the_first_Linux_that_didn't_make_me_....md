### Post by kadymae on 2005-12-01
... run screaming in homicidal rage.

I tried Corel.  But after a week, I gave that computer to a friend whose redhat machine died a horrible death.  I wasn't really that impressed with it.  A lot of what I wanted from a system wasn't there -- like a basic word processor.

I tried Yellow Dog.  I paid for a copy because I believe in voting with my dollars.  -->Insert white hot incandescent rage here<--  Plus, the newbies mailing list was full of a lot of RTFM jerks.

On a whim, I figured I'd give Ubuntu 4.10 for PPC a try.

OMG.  

Gnome.

Better hardware support for PPC.

One application that. just. worked. for each task a user needed to do.

I'm up to 5.10 now, and other than some problems getting Opera 8.51 installed, I'm liking it better than ever.

OS X is still my main OS, don't get me wrong, but when it comes to sitting down, surfing the web, writing something for my website(s), and coding some HTML, there's no reason NOT to grab my  "Ubuntubook".

(Besides, I love Gnome +Nautilus.  It's like somebody read my brain to see what I wanted from a file browser.)

The people at "Team 'buntu" have a great idea.  The coolest most bleeding edge idea in Linux distros in a long time.

It's not "everything but the kitchen sink"

It's not "bleeding edge"

It's not "server in desktop clothing"

It's  " A computer should be simple and easy to use."  A computer should "just work".

And that's why I think there will be a 'buntu box in my house for years to come.
