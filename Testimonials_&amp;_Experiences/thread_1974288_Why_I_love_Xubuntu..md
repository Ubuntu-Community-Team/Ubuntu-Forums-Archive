### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-05-06
I "inherited" a computer from a beloved friend who was my "Linux mentor." The computer had Xubuntu 10.04 on it. 

We had toyed with Debian, Crunchbang, PCLinuxOS (too much for this old box), Mepis 8 (awesome but KDE is too much for this aging Celeron with 1/2-gig of RAM machine), Mint, Mint Xfce, SalixOS (really awesome until the latest version which seemed a big step backwards), Lubuntu, and finally Xubuntu. 

Truly a *community-developed* distro (the mailing list was full of ideas and suggestions from the community of users, who actually get to VOTE on stuff!), Xubuntu is absolutely the fastest, best-hardware-support, best-balanced, and as my mentor would say, [COLOR=Purple]**"bestest and most awesomest Linux distro in the history of ever!"**[/COLOR]

Xubu is the king of Xfce distros, because of it's minimal Ubuntu core and because it is truly a community project.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2012-05-12
> **Rebelli0us said:**
> Standard Ubuntu 12.04 has a startup option "Classic". Is that the same thing as Xubuntu?

In software centre, search for and install 'xfce4'. Logout, choose 'xfce session' from the 'Sessions' pop-up menu, login. That is the desktop environment used in Xubuntu. That will give you a taste of at least one aspect of Xubuntu. Give it a go. A full install of Xubuntu has different packages to Ubuntu, is more lightweight (faster) and pared back.

Follow the same procedure to get back to Unity or Gnome (or any other DE) by logging out and choosing one or the other from Sessions. ;)
