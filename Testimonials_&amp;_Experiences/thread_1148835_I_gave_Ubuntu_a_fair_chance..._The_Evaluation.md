### Post by DeusExM1 on 2009-05-04
So i got tired of Vista because its a resource hog, and wouldnt let me do anything with my system. I was itching to try linux. The first distro i ever tried was kubuntu and that turned out to be a disaster (but that was a while ago, so it was an old release). After that experience i had the general notion that linux sucked. The terminal is not user friendly, it takes a lot of effort to install certain software, etc...

So After a lot of googling to see what my hardware might be compatible with best I decided to try Ubuntu. So what are the results?

System Specs:

Vostro 1500
Nvidia 8600M GT
T7250 Processor
2 GB Ram
Ubuntu 64 Bit

If i could sum it up in one word I would have to say: Amazing. I honestly did not expect this. I thought i was going to uninstall it and go back to Windows, but thats not happening now. I can not believe Ubuntu developers did such an amazing job. I am simply at awe that a free project can be this good.

Things that i love about Ubuntu:

1. I booted up the computer. The sound worked. The driver for the wireless adapter got installed after i clicked on the wireless icon and messed with it a little ( Found new hardware wizard). The Dim buttons work, the media direct button works out of the box (would not work with vista out of the box, as it does with Ubuntu), the DVD drive works, the touchpad scroll works (both horizontal and vertical scrolling) ! I could not even get that to work in XP or vista with the appropriate drivers. That alone is KICK ***, and had me taken aback. 

2. The system comes with EVERYTHING one might need. My favorite apps included (Pidgin, Open office, Firefox). 

3.It is absolutely linux for humans as advetised. Granted, the use of terminal is necessary in some cases, but is not extensive - which is great for a beginner. Not to mention that after using it a little by little, i am now convinced that it is a more powerful way to use a computer. 

4. System is quick, its responsive, the boot time is quick. 

5. I Like the whole environment - the desktop is cleverly designed, the layout is NOT confusing as Kubuntu was when i had it. Also it is nice to be able to switch to another desktop on the bottom right.

6. Printer support (cups i think its called). What can i say about this? I spent 30 minutes configuring an HP C7200 printer in Vista with drivers installed. It would not print over the network no matter what, until i reinstalled it. Half an hour gone right there. 

What about Ubuntu? 2 minutes of work. System - administration-printing - new. Found the hardware, the driver was loaded, worked right away. Need i say more?

7.  .deb packages are a windows users dream come true ! makes me very very happy. Although i am learning how to open other formats as well now using the terminal.


Things that could be improved:

Honestly i dont have much to add here. I think the Ubuntu development team did great. The only thing that i could recommend is making GUI (point and click) completely independent. The terminal is a great tool but some people are just not technically inclined to do this stuff, despite the fact that it is a VERY powerful tool to communicate with the computer. I will be relying on it as much as necessary, but like i said some people dont know how to surf the internet so the terminal alienates them. 

Thats all, tell me your thoughts on the review. Dom:guitar:

### Post by DeusExM1 on 2009-05-04
> **thedaemon said:**
> Not too long ago, all a computer had was the terminal. I grew up on DOS, so I guess having to use a terminal feels fine to me. I am curious, are all the people that want GUI only teenagers?

I dont think that is true. I am a teenager and i know a pretty good amount about computers. Did i want to learn how to use the terminal? Not exactly, but once i started using it, it turned to be just another tool to get things done.

My parents on the other hand can barely check their email, so they need to rely on GUI completely. The same goes for most elderly. I had trouble teaching one old lady how to check her mail and browse the web... Some young people are equally inept (most teenage girls imo certainly wont bother to learn CLI)  So CLI is out of question for such people. So to answer your question,  I think that those who do not want to use the terminal spans across all age groups. 

The thing to consider is that the other 2 OSes (Windows and OSX) have completely independent GUI. So many users learn to use JUST that. So then who wants to switch several years later to something that _appears_ to be more complicated? Many people just sit there thinking they have no alternatives, and many just dont have the time to learn new stuff. The good thing about GUI is that a user can guess his way around a system completely without a manual, while CLI requires the user to know what they are doing and to actually know the lines to input. (at least somewhat). 

Once again, now that i have used the terminal i see that its quite useful. But for people who simply cannot master this, maybe we should offer another option? 

Thats just my .02
