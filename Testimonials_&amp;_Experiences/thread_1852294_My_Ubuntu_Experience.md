### Post by daniel.p on 2011-09-29
I first discovered Ubuntu a few years back, I think it was 2007 or so. Somehow I had gotten a "stickin' it to the man" attitude towards Microsoft and decided I was determined to get away from Windows. I knew Linux existed but knew nothing about it. I did a search for "Linux download" on Google and clicked the very first link. It brought me here, to Ubuntu. 

I downloaded the ISO and burned it to a blank disc I had lying around, and before even testing it to see if I liked it - I chose to install it, completely wiping out my Windows partition. Everything worked from the start, but I wanted more. I lurked around the forums (unregistered at the time) just browsing various topics until I came across how to install "The cube." I was fascinated by it and went ahead with the installation. I began showing all my friends when they'd come by to visit. They were also amused by the cube and most went home to install Ubuntu, whether it be their only OS or dual-boot.

Finally, I began reading all that I could. I bought numerous books, read countless guides online, etc. to learn my way around Ubuntu and Linux in general. I wouldn't consider myself an expert or even close to it. In fact, I consider myself to know very little still. However I enjoy the learning that comes with Linux and since installing it for the first time years ago, every computer I've owned since then has had Ubuntu. I currently have an HP laptop running 11.04 and Win7, HP netbook with 11.04 and BackTrack 5, and a desktop I built myself running 11.04, BackTrack 5, and Win7. I doubt I will ever fully return to Windows. I've been sold on Linux since day 1 and it's all thanks to Ubuntu.
