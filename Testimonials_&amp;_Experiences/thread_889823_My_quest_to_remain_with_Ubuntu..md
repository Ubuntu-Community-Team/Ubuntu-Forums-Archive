### Post by ZachTorpy on 2008-08-14
A long time ago I was in an IRC chat with some friend from the Halo community. We got on the topic of this strange thing called "Ubuntu" (I was on Windows XP Media Center Edition), I became immediately intrigued. I remember asking "what the hell is a Ubuntu?" and getting many laughs (I wasn't trying to be funny). Back then I knew very little about computers, and I had only heard the word "Linux" mentioned once or twice.
Thankfully somebody had the courage to let me know that "Ubuntu is a distribution of Linux". I was thankful that he told me the definition, but I had absolutely no idea what that meant. We then proceeded into an hour-long conversation about Linux, why it is awesome, why Ubuntu is a great choice, etc. Back then I had really no reason that I should stay on Windows, so I came to the site and ordered 5 discs of 6.06.
A month or two later I checked the mail and was surprised to see a package for me (it had been awhile since I ordered the discs). I quickly ripped open the package and ran downstairs to my computer and popped in the Live CD.

I was amazed.

This "Ubuntu" was running my computer faster than ever, and it was being run off a **Live CD**! So I bit the bullet and wiped my HD clean of Windows, and installed Ubuntu.

Now, it hasn't been all jolly since then. Because I'm so deep into the Halo Community, my friends and I make "Tricking" videos for the Halo series (see: [HighImpactHalo](http://www.highimpacthalo.org) for more info) and I was always the guy chosen to edit these movies. On Windows I had grown very fond of Sony Vegas, and there was no way to run it in Ubuntu yet. So I told myself "Hey, I'd much rather be able to edit my movies on a crappy OS than use a nice OS without the use of my program", which ended up being a big mistake. Within a few days of using mu newly pirated copy of Vista, I couldn't stand it. It was clunky, slow, and nowhere near as user friendly as Ubuntu (which had now been upgraded to 6.10).

So, I switched back to 6.04 using the Live CD.
And again, I was in love. I stayed with Ubuntu for awhile, upgrading it to 6.10. I also ordered a Live CD of 7.04, in case I ever had to switch back to Windows, which turned out being a smart move.

After a few months with Ubuntu I switched back to Vista for the same reason as before. And within a few days, I had to switch back to Ubuntu. I know some of you are thinking "wtf, just dual-boot nub", well I would but my HD is way to small, and I prefer only having one OS.

It is here where I have remained for some time now. I used 7.04 for a long time, later upgrading to Gutsy for the sole reason of upgrading to Hardy, and I was disappointed. My stuff worked so well in Feisty, but everything was seeming to fall apart in Hardy. Not to mention the install had hung at "installing new locales" and I had to go into the root shell to "dpkg --configure -a", which ended up leaving my distro somewhere between Gutsy and Hardy, that I became fond of calling Hurtsy. So I downgraded to Gutsy, and have been happy ever since. :]
I've started going to my friends house to edit movies (he has a much nicer PC anyway), and there is really not a great reason for me to switch back to Windows.

tl;dr: Ubuntu is awesome. <3

Thanks for all the support you guys have given on these forums, I have searched the crap out of this place whenever I run into a problem, and you guys always have the answer. :)

### Post by Comhra on 2008-08-14
I installed Hardy the day before the official release.  PCLOS had been on the laptop for 4 weeks before.  Straight away, I had kernel panic on the first reboot.  It happened on the second go as well.  

I reinstalled, this time devoting the entire disk to Hardy and the laptop booted normally.  Though stable, Hardy was using a lot of RAM and CPU, a bit more than Gutsy and I wasn't best pleased. A far more serious problem involved a hardware issue which caused the start-up to 'timeout' at boot-up.  This happened a lot, maybe as many as 50% of start-ups involved this 'timeout'.  On those occasions, there was no recourse except to use the power button.

I went back to Gutsy and when I returned this week, I found that while CPU usage and consumption of RAM were way down, the start-up issue remained.

I made a determined effort and found a solution in the the record of the discourse between a number of testers who had the same bug as me and whose discussion I found in Launchpad.  We were all on Sony Vaios.  

After applying the remedy, I found the Hardy was behaving normally and that the start-up bug was gone. Hardy continues to be perfectly stable and I'm very happy.

I take it the upgrade from Feisty went well?
I tried the same thing twice and failed both times.  This was just after discovering Ubuntu.

The upgrade to Hardy didn't work properly for you, it seems, but you do have a functioning OS.

Try installing Hardy from the CD, this OS is worth it.
