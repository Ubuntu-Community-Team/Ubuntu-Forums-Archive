### Post by WinRiddance on 2012-02-15
Thanks for the reply. I've used Ubuntu (without KDE or XFCE) for 3 years, until Ubuntu with Unity was released. Tried that for about 3 months and just got sick of the lack of freedom. By lack of freedom I mean to say that I'm not a programmer of apps nor part of the Ubuntu development team, so for me (and apparently many others) that old Gnome2 was perfection pure since I couid pretty much do with it whatever I wanted, things that I simply could not come close to doing with Unity. Tried Gnome3 too, wasn't thrilled with that either ... like it better than Unity though. I heard so much about Unity for being practical & cool for "today's user" but from my point of view it's more of a step in the Microshaft direction. Linux is about pure freedom and Ubuntu is about Humanness. To me, Unity does not fit that bill as well as Ubuntu with Gnome2 or Gnome3 for that matter does/did. Gnome2 DEFINED perfection ... But ah well, that's just my opinion.

So then I tried Debian for a month or so but really missed the *buntuness which then got me to go with Mint 11.04 for a couple of months. Problem with Mint is the user forum though, which I can't stand. Total lack of *REAL* support, plus ads all over the place, plus slowly loading pages quite often. Still missed that initial *buntu feeling too, to be perfectly truthful about it. When I saw how similar XFCE was to the older Gnome 2.0 after installing Xubuntu on my Dad's older laptop, that's when I decided to start working with Xubuntu instead. Installed my Compiz, Nautilus, Gimp, and other choice packages ... never looked back. Granted, it's only been a couple of months with Xubuntu, but **so far I like it just as much as Ubuntu 10.10 with Gnome 2.0** ... as close to perfection as I can hope for, and less fiddling too! :D
Unless XFCE radically changes, I'll stick with it for a long time to come.

### Post by ivanchic on 2012-02-22
I have recently installed Ubuntu 11.10 on my office desktop comp and Xubuntu 11.10 on laptop. To be honest, both are better than Windows and it is only a matter of habits and aesthetics to choose one of DE (don't forget Kubuntu, Lubuntu, Mythbuntu, Mint). The great thing with *buntu family is actually that abundance of possible choices. There is always one or two DE that fit your needs and you can switch in no time!!
