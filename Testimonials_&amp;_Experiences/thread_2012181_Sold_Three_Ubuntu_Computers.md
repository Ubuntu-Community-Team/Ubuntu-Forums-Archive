### Post by 1roxtar on 2012-06-28
Recently, I refurbished three computers (2 Laptops & 1 Netbook) and sold them pre-installed with Ubuntu 12.04 LTS on them.  I operate a home-based computer repair shop and got curious if any customers would like something new like a Linux-based computer.

Two of them I had painted the back lids orange and placed Ubuntu branded stickers from the Canonical store on them.  I also placed Ubuntu stickers over the "Windows Key".  So far, they have sold quickly and with each customer loving their new and unique operating system.

The netbook was an Acer Aspire One and the other two laptops were Toshiba Satellites.  Each time I posted an ad on my Facebook page, they sold within two days.  One thing that is working for me is that I have a good reputation in my small community concerning my tech work and they trusted me when I recommended them an Ubuntu Powered computer.  

I have already installed Ubuntu on many of my customer's computers and the word is starting to spread around.  I even had a work-mate (at my full-time job) tell me that he was considering buying a Mac because he heard about it's security.  He told me, since Macs are pretty expensive, he could just buy a regular laptop and just have me install Ubuntu instead.  

I let him know that I am in the process of acquiring another laptop and I could pre-install it and sell it to him.  He liked the idea.  So you see, it's not hard for people to use a Linux computer or buy one with Linux pre-installed if they are just properly educated.  I have had nothing but great results with Linux/Ubuntu.  It's definitely done wonders for my own business.
