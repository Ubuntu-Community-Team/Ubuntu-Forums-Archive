### Post by scotcella on 2008-06-21
As a computer lecturer, I encourage my students to keep an open mind when it comes to different types of technology and software we have available in our hands today. I hope that my teaching methods I use on my classes will give them the incentive to fly away experiment using  different things, see them turning to open source products if they choose to. Many of them think that all “free” software means poorer quality.

Being a long time Windows user because it was there, my world into open source software started with Open Office- from being stingy and not wanting to cough up money one tiny software I rarely use. From then on, Firefox for stability and many others followed and which eventually lead me to Ubuntu. For many years I wanted to try out Linux but wasn't game enough to take the gamble or was time poor. Eventually I felt ready and I spent a lot of time reading on different Linux OS's and decided on Ubuntu to start with. So far I haven't tried other Linux OS, I will eventually when I have the time and energy. Now, I don't use Ubuntu because of the price factor ($0) but rather because it's something to play with and learn something new.

My personal experience with Ubuntu has been positive, starting with Fiesty (on and off for 2 years) and now using Hardy. My first installation with Fiesty (dual booted XP) was on a Dell Inspiron laptop. All features worked out of the box pretty much, except a some minor tweaking with the screen resolution and something else. Unfortunately, I was never able to get the wireless going on a secured channel, but unsecured worked really well but obviously I don't want someone scabbing one luxury I pay for. At the time it wasn't a big deal as my laptop was always stationed in one spot and I went travelling overseas.

When my partner got a new Sony Vaio laptop which came with Vista, she didn't like it (not into computers and vista was too unfamiliar compared to XP) so we swapped laptops. Around the same time Ubuntu started promoting the latest version- Hardy. I downloaded and tried the live CD, and was amazed the wireless on a secured channel worked using WPA keys on BOTH laptops. I was really impressed with some of the improvements and wasted no time installing Hardy into the Sony laptop (Dual booted Vista/Hardy). 

Everything works flawlessly!! Some minor tweaking and adding new applications, doing this and that I am learning more than I ever did after a month of exclusive use. I haven't booted into Vista since, and don't think I need to for a long time. Pity I still to keep it as I need it for work just in case- so far no need. 

The only thing that doesn't quite work is my laptop to receive images from my mobile phone via Bluetooth but my laptop can send. I believe this very minor issue can and will be fixed at some point when I am able to find a way! 

The community support for Ubuntu- it's amazing! If I have a little issue, it's fixable by doing a search in the forum or googling for similar issues. Through this, I have been able to find wonderful applications that I would have never thought of using and by far a lot better than a lot of software made for specifically for Windows. 

The community support and the philosophy behind Ubuntu, makes this a wonderful distro to use. These reasons alone are the reasons why I will choose to stay with Ubuntu, not discounting its ease of use. 

I am free to come and go, free to do what I like, free to decide what I want, free to do things when ever I want! Because of this fanastic environment, I have decided to build my own media centre using only Ubuntu- no dualbooting. When I build I will use it for work purposes  for video editing and making screen casts for my classes at work. When the time is right, I will get started and I can't wait!!
