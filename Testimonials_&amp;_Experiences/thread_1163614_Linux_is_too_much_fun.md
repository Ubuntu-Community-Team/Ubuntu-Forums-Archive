### Post by ethan brand on 2009-05-18
I'm at a good place with Linux at the moment:

- Upgrade to Jaunty (Xubuntu) from Hardy using the alternate CD went very smoothly

- Found out Rosetta Stone works perfectly under Wine

- Wireless connection is established with a wrapper under Linux *much* faster than Windows using proprietary software (Also a major headache with permanent connection drops in Windows is avoided in Linux)

- Recently tried virtualization with VirtualBox and successfully installed W2K and WXP. That means no more dual-booting necessary to run my windows applications.

- Virtualization of other Linux distros is god damn fun. 

All I can say is that it's really fun to use my computer nowadays.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-05-18
It's like to much money or a car to fast, there's no such thing. ;)

9.04 really delivered a significant increase in speed and functionality on our systems. Three years ago after installing Feisty Fawn on my system, the entire family requested that Windows be removed from their systems (5 total) and replaced with Ubuntu. I was thrilled with their decision, as it meant I would get my computer back - they spent more time on my system enjoying Ubuntu than their own Windows PCs.

Since that time we have been Windows free and to say we're satisfied with the OS would be an understatement. I can say without hesitation that I'd rather throw my systems in the dumpster than re-install Windows.

Some have insinuated that I'm a fanboy, but in reality I'm only a user that is completely satisfied with the product. Should the product become corrupted or unusable, I would seek a different product. However, it's been my experience that Ubuntu just continues to improve.

Hope you continue to enjoy the OS and find the same level of satisfaction with the product that we have.

I wish you well and look forward to reading your future posts.
