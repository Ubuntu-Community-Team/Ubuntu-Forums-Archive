### Post by faizal525 on 2010-08-17
Hi All..:D

I knew vaguely about Linux when Linus Torvalds first created it back in the 90's. Was interested but didn't give it much of a thought then. Had my first PC about that time and was diligently fiddling with the DOS command lines and Win 3.1. Had to, because my employers (a Bank) was using it. Then it was the natural progression thru the rest of the Windows family and all its inherent problems, especially instability and viruses, to say the least. I thought to myself, isn't there something better for us regular Joes?

Linux was about a couple of years in the making and I decided why not give it a try? My first encounter with Linux was Red Hat 5 (If I can recall) It was quite a challenge (read : fun)installing and I liked it! I decided to switch back to Windows because it was 'mainstream' at the time (read: ALL my employers were using it!). Moreover, I am an avid gamer and most games run on Windows..:(

So, time passed and I went thru all the injustices in the computer world that Microsoft has to offer..sigh. It was sometime in June this year that I came across Ubuntu. It was really a surprise to see how Linux has grown in leaps and bounds from all the good Linux folks out there. It was a far cry from when I installed Red Hat. What bowled me over was the simplicity built into the Distros nowadays. And its sad to think that there are many of us out there are still being fleeced by Microsoft.

So, I had Ubuntu installed into my Windows XP thru WUBI. It was a breeze! Amazing! It was smooth as silk. Although I still have to go over the learning curve with Linux (it's quite steep for me), I am managing. I make it a point to spend a few hours at night to read through the threads in this forum and try out some of the suggestions. And there ARE a lot of helpful people in the Linux community!

A few weeks back, I decided to do a proper installation of Ubuntu in my Notebook. Did it through USB boot install and as usual, it was a breeze. Only problem was the Nvidia drivers but the good news is, I have this forum to turn to..:)

I have since been dual booting with my Win XP and learning more about the beauty that is Ubuntu/Linux. Hell, I am booting more into Ubuntu than Windows. I have my own business now and we are still running Windows as the main OS. All that will change. The first challenge is to persuade my staff how easy it is to use. They are intimidated by it..hehe.

So, what I would like to share here is, Ubuntu and all the other distros out there have grown leaps and bounds. It is easy to install and use. Any problems encountered can be dealt with easily in this forum. It is just a matter of learning a wee bit more on How Linux works. There are tons of reading material out there, most of it free! What else could a person ask for?

And a small advice for people who has switched and plan to switch to Ubuntu/Linux. Quit complaining! This software is free and is supported by great people who sacrifice their time and money to make this world a better place for us. Give Ubuntu/Linux and the people behind it some understanding and respect. Learn to say PLEASE. It makes a big difference..:D

What we have here is something powerful..PEOPLE POWER!

'Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts' - Winston Churchill
Cheerio!
