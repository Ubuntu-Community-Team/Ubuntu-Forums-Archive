### Post by VoodooLoveDog on 2009-10-13
I'm not as impressed as some of the posters here. I know, I know it's a beta, I'm giving feedback. 

Because I haven't messed with the software for that long, my comments mostly center around the eye candy and my general first impressions.

I've loaded the software on 3 computers now all Dell (Optiplex GX620, 745 and a 960). On the GX620 the x64 software doesn't run. It complains about an out of memory condition (Yes, I know there is Launchpad for bugs. Last time I submitted something, it took someone 4+ months to respond to me). 

The splash screen(s) on start-up are silly. The first one I see is a white Ubuntu sign. No movement, nothing. On the GX620 it sits there for quite a long time, then a black screen for several seconds (is it hanging?). Then I get to the chocolate colored spotlight splash screen that looks terrible because of the resolution and the fact that the OS hasn't loaded video drivers. 

On the 960 I get to see the white logo for a few seconds then a 3 seconds of the chocolate spotlight screen. 

Why two splash screens? Why the chocolate spotlight screen that looks horrible at low resolutions? Why spend time revamping a part of the OS that has worked correctly since 8.04? A simple Ubuntu logo with some way of determining OS load progress is fine. 

Now I have the login screen. Yuk. I'm presented with the same chocolate spotlight screen with a white bar to login. The color scheme looks awful. Once I update, you change the white login box to a black one. Slightly better.

I login and I'm now faced with a brownish-sunburstish background with white panels. Not bad looking, but boy does it not go with the splash screen. 

Second. Why am I being force-fed evolution and empathy? The little notification icon is along with the sound and the network icons. For one, I purge evolution on any fresh install (I'm a Thunderbird man) and two, I don't even use an IM client. Why not on initial start-up ask the user to add these pieces of software to the notification area? 

I realize these are all aspects of the OS that are easily changed. But as far as the GUI colors and start-up colors are concerned, it looks amateurish (Which it didn't in the past). How can I convince Grandma, Uncle Bob, Next door neighbor Frank or otherwise that this OS is better than Windows when it looks out of of the box like HS OS project.

I love Ubuntu and want it to have a larger audience. That is why my comments may be a bit harsh.
