### Post by seeker5528 on 2008-09-22
Not only have I not gone Linux only, I actually added Vista into the mix, bringing me up to XP, Vista and 3 Linux distributions.

I am in that position where repartitioning is impossible due to all the partitions being full enough that I can't move enough files around to free up partitions and create different sized ones, even if I delete the ones the OSs are installed on.

I have resized partitions many times though with [System Rescue CD](http://www.sysresccd.org/Main_Page). Originally using QTParted, and more recently Gparted only for FAT filesystem. As the support for NTFS has matured I've come to trust Gparted for NTFS now too. More recently the tools dealing with ext2/3 have gained the ability to resize partitions of those types, and I have used GParted for that a couple of times now.

The only time I have had an issue resizing is if there was a pre-existing oddity in the filesystem or partition, but there is a reason it is always said you should back up your stuff before doing things of this nature. 

Later, Seeker
