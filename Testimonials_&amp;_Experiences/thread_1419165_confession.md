### Post by everytimeref on 2010-03-01
I work in a Development centre for a government agency.

We were recently setting up a wireless network. Eager to prove how brilliant Ubuntu is I installed a version on a 10 year old Dell laptop to show how it would work with wireless.

It didn't work. A Sony erricson mobile, an Iphone and 2 Windoze laptops (7 and xp) all found the network with no trouble, but my Ubuntu laptop was proving very confusing  and impossible to get working. Oh the shame.

Then I had an awful realisation: I'd been right clicking on the network icon instead of left clicking. when I finally left-clicked, there were the Networks sitting happily and ready to go.

I then had a moral dilema, Did I blame Linux and not admit my stupid error or did I take the hit and admit my stupidity.

You'll be pleased to know that I didn't let Ubuntu down and admitted responsibility:oops::oops:.
