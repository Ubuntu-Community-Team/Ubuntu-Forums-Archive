### Post by mac71 on 2008-09-27
Ok, a few months back (while i was still dual booting) i received a BIOS upgrade from lenovo. 
This seemed to have no real affect on either windows or ubuntu .............

Until a few weeks later, when i plugged in a removable usb device while using ubuntu. Nothing was recognised unless it was connected at boot.

After lots of scouring through the forums and google searches i realised it was going to be some time before this problem would be resolved.

It was then that I decided to dip my toe into the vast expanse of available distros. Don't get me wrong, it was not due to dissatisfaction with ubuntu, rather the inconvenience of what *seemed* to be a kernel or hal issue.

So, off I went searching for a suitable substitute.

Linspire and Freespire worked fine, but it felt like a big stepbackwards and everything seemed a bit haphazard.
Knoppix looked good and felt familiar, but had the same issue as ubuntu.
Fedora had the same issue, but didn't feel right.
Mandriva felt good but seemed to be far from straightforward.
OpenSuse looked and felt good, but again, had the same issue.
Then I tried PcLinuxOS- felt good and looked good (and everything worked). It seemed to have the best of everything. My only issue was that it was only *really* supported by donations, and, right or wrong, I'm not sure I want to base my Linux future on a distro that *feels* like it could just fold at any time.

Once i put my brain in gear, i downloaded an older version to re-flash my BIOS and all was well again.

I used this opportunity to try out the big guns that "didn't work"

Didn't bother with fedora- I don't know why, it just isn't for me.
Mandriva felt good and was well polished, but some apps just wouldn't work properly ie; skype (which is an issue for me), and the forums seemed a mess.
Then it was over to OpenSuse. It is IMHO a well rounded and highly polished distro. Once I got my head around YaST everything felt nice and "comfy". My only issue was that it was a hardship trying to get dvd codecs installed. Vlc seemed my only option. OpenSuse is no ubuntu, but a very close 2nd.
It then felt like it was time to try out a fresh install of hardy.
For me, ubuntu really is the cream. It's got the apps., the codecs, the hardware support etc, etc. Windows has completely gone. Ubuntu looks good, feels great and just makes sense. The experience was invaluable, but I am sorry I ever left you. **Ubuntu is now my only OS.**

No question about it, ubuntu really is superior.

Ubuntu really does reign supreme.
