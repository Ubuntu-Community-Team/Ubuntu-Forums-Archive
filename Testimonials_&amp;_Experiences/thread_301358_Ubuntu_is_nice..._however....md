### Post by ordinarystar on 2006-11-17
I forgot how I bumped into Ubuntu. About a month ago, I suddenly had the interest on venturing into Linux, and scheming to dump Windows. Partially is due to copyrights issue (well, almost every IT user here has copyrights problem. Software, movie & music copyrights infrigement problem is in rampant in this country. You can get pirated cds anywhere, even Ubuntu.), and partially is attempt to weed out antivirus and spyware problems, finally is because of interest.

It really gives a new addiction into my life. There's much to discover about Linux, when I eventually bumped into Ubuntu as my first distro, instead of the famous ones like Red Hat or Mandriva. Ubuntu really is riched with all kinds of supports: software supports, hardware supports, community supports. One using Windows (...and serious bugged by the Windows Genuine Advantage message everyday when he starts his computer.) could try his luck on this alternative which can be competent to Windows, and much much more competent in terms of security and stability. (No thanks to Windows firewall, I am still bugged by spywares anyway. The firewall is instead a paperwall.)

The experience of using Ubuntu is nice... the appearance and stability of Ubuntu really amazed me. Yes, THIS is the answer of next generation computer users. However, there's still a lot of things that don't make me 100% comfortable with Ubuntu. It takes a lot of time to learn to master the whole thing, as it's not functional totally out of the box. For example, the multimedia codecs, flash plug-in, and so on. I might prefer everything out of the box, wanting to squeeze everything I can get on the hardwares that I have invested with real money. It would not be that pleasing if I can't unlease the machine's power to be as good as Windows (will try out Freespire some other day, not done with Ubuntu yet).

[B]Hopefully someone will create a package, specially dedicated to noobs that want to get every multimedia essentials work at one off installing. Then I believe Ubuntu can even beat Windows Vista.
[/B]

I love Ubuntu... I wil definitely laugh at anyone that spend RM158 (about $41.5)to buy genuine Windows 98. Let's forget about it, when you can get Xubuntu on your old computer, and unleash the stability of new computers with Ubuntu.

### Post by gerowen on 2006-11-17
The reason proprietary media codecs are not included by default is copyright laws.  If you pay attention when you start Automatix there is a big warning saying that you have to own a legal copy of Windows in order to use their codecs.  By making them available in this way Linux companies leave the users to suffer the consequences of violating copyrights if they choose to instead of the company itself.  That's why so few Linux distributions come with win32 codec support out of the box.
