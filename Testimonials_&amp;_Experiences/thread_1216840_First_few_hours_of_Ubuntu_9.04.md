### Post by Distorted Humor on 2009-07-18
Back in 2007, perhaps early 2008, I had tried ubuntu, and while I played around with it, I wasn't really impressed at that time.   Now, July 2009 and I burned a CD, installed 9.04 in my preset desktop linux space (2 gig swap, 20 gig root, and 50 gig /home so that as I play with new distros, I never loose my documents - this co-exists on a Vista system, and I have a laptop that is a pure Linux system (Suse) 


So far, I am quite impressed, a professional and pain free install, reconizing my hardware without trouble, and then installing a Gnome Desktop onto my desktop.

First impressions

- Fast, both in boot up, and in actual speed, it is a very nice setup, and the  defult desktop is very nice, and of course, since I am not a complete newbie to Linux, I was able to get a desktop that works for me easily enough.   

- Nice minimal install, does not have too many applications cluttering the task bar.

- if you can google-fo, you can quickly tap into the mindshare of Ubuntu.

-  It is a personal thing, but Gnome is nice and clean, but I am a KDE guy

-  The package manager is very usuable, and now testing Synaptic by installing KDE.

- I prefer how some other distros handle Open office.

- Fast- stable- and a very good implementation of Linux, and I think of it much better then I did two years ago.    Currently Synaptic is downloading and installing KDE, and I will play with Ubuntu with KDE to see how I like it before I make a final grade for my personal use.   

- Overall, not bad, and I will make sure it stays on my list of recommended Distros for people to try, even if it might not be replacing my install on my laptop.
