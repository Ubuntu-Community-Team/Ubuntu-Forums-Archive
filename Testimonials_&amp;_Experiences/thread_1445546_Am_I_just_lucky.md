### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-04-04
> armandh
My number one of the install killers is a worn out cd drives.
good enough to play tunes but not good for an install

Might I suggest one slow the burn speed down to 2X. It's been my experience that even with a new drive, trying to burn an Ubuntu cd at high speeds almost always resulted in failure.

To many times, I disposed of a high speed burned Ubuntu install cd. One of this forum's wiser members (HappyFeet - no longer with us) advised me a long time ago to slow my burn speed down to a crawl. Since that time, I never again had trouble burning a live cd. I hated to see him go, for he shared his knowledge openly and for users such as myself he provided us with an endless source of information. All one had to do was listen. ;)

* Of course this is only my $0.02 and based solely on my own experience. I refuse to debate the experiences of others in this section of the forum.
