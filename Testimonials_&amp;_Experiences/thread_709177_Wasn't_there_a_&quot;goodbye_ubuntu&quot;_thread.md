### Post by gheywood on 2008-02-27
Well, time for it to go. I was running two Ubuntu machines at home. 

One was running as a host for my VMWare Server 1.03 install. It was a pig to install, but once running worked well for a while, but then every so often I would come home and found that the host had locked. It got to the stage where it was happening every week or so. A shame, because although it was a pain at times, I really enjoyed the experience. I need something more stable though and so far, Windows 2003 Server 64bit has been it. 

The second machine was going to be used to capture video and record to DVD. I tried for ages to get this working and just ran into problems at every turn. When I first considered Ubuntu for this, I was assured that it could do it (I am still sure it can) easily (not so sure on this). A big part of the issue here was the regular failure of the wireless config. It seems like every few weeks, I woud have to spend a couple of hours trying to get it work to again. It would just stop working. When I was trying to get the real problem sorted (the capture, or editing, or writing of video), it was just too much. That has been reformatted as XP. 


At the risk of sounding negative, I have enjoyed the experince with Ubuntu but it just doesn't offer what I want, as easily as I want. 

The biggest dissapointment is that I looked at linux in '99 and thought that it would be easier by now. It isn't in my opinion. The days where you could justify the difficultly because of x% performance increase are over. It is no longer an issue in 99% of cases. 

This is a great board though, and I had lots of support from the people on here. Hopefully I will be back in a couple of years to see all the improvements!
