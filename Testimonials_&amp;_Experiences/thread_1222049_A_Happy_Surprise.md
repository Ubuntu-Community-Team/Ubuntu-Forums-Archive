### Post by solwic on 2009-07-24
I was at work yesterday (I work at a Pizza Hut), standing around pretending to be busy, when I glanced over at my manager's PC and got a pleasant surprise.  She was writing a letter to somebody, and the first thing I noticed is that she was using OpenOffice Writer.  That brought a smile to my face, naturally (I love OOo).  It was only a couple seconds later that I looked at the taskbar, and realized it wasn't Windows...it was KDE 3.5, complete with the default military style clock (it was 18:36, if it matters heh).  

That blew me away.  Usually her PC runs a full screen app that is solely designed for the store.  But when I clocked out on another machine, I noticed at the bottom of the screen it said "Linux Terminal version:" and then a string of numbers.  

It doesn't really mean anything, I guess, other than making me a little happier at work, now that I know we use OSS.  I do realize, however, that in the time I've worked there, the system has never went down.  

Now I know why.  :)
