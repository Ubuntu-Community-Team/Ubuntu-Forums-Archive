### Post by shaft350x on 2007-11-01
I've been using Ubuntu since Edgy Eft.  I've made a couple switches (64 down to 32 EE down to DD back to EE, up to FF.)  Plus I've tinkered with it and Studio on a laptop.

I opted to do a fresh install because I wanted to do a /home partition and I wanted to see how well things would work on a fresh install compared to the laptop I upgraded.

So far, DVD was a pain to setup, I ended up booting up my wife's laptop (system76 Darter1 running Feisty Fawn) and checking the DVD settings in in Synaptic and changing to xine instead of gstreamer.

Desktop settings were a bit sketchy, thankfully installing the advanced desktop settings was pretty easy, but the window behavior was off (I'd click to drag the window and it would maximize or restore I couldn't move them.)  I did manage to get it to an acceptable behavior but I was seriously frustrated by the odd settings that it setup with.

I had the mysterious "title bar going grey on mouseover" behavior, don't know if that will continue (I believe this is something to do with compositing and NVidia cards)

Still tracking down how I'm going to make Flash work, then I'll get to my other tweaks.

Now I've set all this stuff up before so I'm no stranger to it, and I'm not complaining about it just to gripe.  I just haven't had much success with the automatic tracking down of codecs and such.  This was one of the things I was looking forward to in both FF and GG.

Just so you know I really like Ubuntu and I'm sticking with it, but this was kind of a reality check for me.  In a good way because it makes me realize some of the short comings (why some of my friends would be turned off of it) so I don't become something of a Ubuntu-fanboy (like an apple-zealot).

I'll keep you all posted on how the rest of this goes.
