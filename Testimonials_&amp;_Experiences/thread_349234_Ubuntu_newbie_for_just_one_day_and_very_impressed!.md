### Post by Stevo22 on 2007-01-29
I've heard all kinds of horror stories about Linux - how hard it is to configure, immature drivers, etc.

I just installed Ubuntu 5.10 last night and have had a chance to experiment with it more today.
I'm very impressed. For several reasons. The OS is easy to install - although Linux non-experts (like me) should stay away from using the 'expert' mode for installation. 

I'm surprised and pleased at how quickly the OS recognized my Canon scanner and my USB memory key. No futzing around with driver disks - the drivers for each peripheral are already there - right in the OS itself. Even setting up my Canon S600 printer was super easy. It literally took but a second for Ubuntu to set up the driver once I had opened up the printer selection applet and selected the driver. But what was even more amazing is how much speedier the printer works under Linux compared to Windows XP.

Finally, I'm very happy with the price of Ubuntu - FREE! and tons of FREE! quality software already there or readily downloadable. Although I wish Gnome had a more intuitive installation system.

I decided to install Ubuntu because, with a price tag of zero dollars, I had absolutely nothing to lose. I'm also trying to get out of the Microsoft box. I won't get into why here - that's a subject for another post. 

The people at Canonical, Ltd. and everyone who has helped make Ubuntu a reality deserve a Nobel Prize, in my opinion. Finally, a humane OS made by humane people for humans everywhere.
