Hi

right now, I use both Windows Vista and Ubuntu and it is working very smoothly. All the hardware work so far. I have not tested the printer yet, but sound, internet connection, and other work perfectly.

I am planning to download LAMP so that I can start designing webpages. Unfortunately, there is not Enigma Game already installed, but I can play it from Knoppix so no problem.

I already tested the email and I am happy with that. Now, I am going to test all the software that are already included.

Your help was very helpful and beneficial.

However, preparation and being informed is very important, but the most important is patience. We rush to do things, but are not prepared in the end and then we criticize everyone but ourselves. I know a person who came late to the airport and there were long lines. When he saw that the time is running up before he can reach the plane he started complaining about everyone except himself.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-08-15
I agree with the original poster. I took things very slowly; I booted the live CD every day for weeks before installing (that's patience for you, this computer had 128 megabytes of RAM!). After installing, I didn't throw in the towel even though nothing interesting would install (Multiverse and Universe weren't enabled). It was about a month before I plucked up the courage to install graphics drivers. Flawlessly.

Of course, Ubuntu is much easier these days. But Linux is so different to non-Unixy operating systems that I still encourage people to take it slow and learn, rather than try to compile bleeding-edge software on their first day...
