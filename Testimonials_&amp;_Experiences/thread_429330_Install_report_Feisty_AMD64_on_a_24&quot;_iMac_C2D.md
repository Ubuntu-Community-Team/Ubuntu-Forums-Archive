### Post by Slackpacker on 2007-05-01
For your amusement & edification:

This is an experimental setup; I'm quite happy with Tiger, and I run Xubuntu on an old Dell lapitude notebook for work.

-used the [lpj boot option]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBook") as per [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=408940").

-NVIDIA drivers detected and installed without a hitch.

-reconfigured the X server, and damn does Ubuntu look good at 1920x1200!

-hit the [alsa sound bug]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=418681") and solved it... by turning up my powered speakers.  The last guy claims to have compiled a new version of alsa, with luck it will soon be updated.

-Ubuntu auto-detected my third-party 3-button mouse!  That's more than OS X ever did!

-mashed the clear key, which X thinks is num lock, to activate the keypad.  How often do you find a one-keystroke bugfix?

-hacked xorg.conf to display [window borders]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=418837") with desktop effects.

-Beryl.  Now I know what the fuss is about - I never thought I'd like Beryl that much.  Heck, I even love wobbly windows!  Yes, I'm still a Mac user at heart.  There are a few sporadic black windows though, and I wouldn't use Beryl if I wasn't experimenting.  Tweak, tweak, tweak... whoa, it's 3 AM already!

-Google Earth a la [medibuntu]("http://medibuntu.sos-sts.com/repository.php") works better than the mac version!

-mounted my [mac partition]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=311476") as per this thread.  I can also mount the Linux partition with [ext2fsx]("http://sourceforge.net/projects/ext2fsx/").

-codec-detector got all gstreamer packages in two tries, can play AAC in Rhythmbox.  Realplayer works fine with 32-bit libraries.

-Java seems to work fine in Firefox.  I'll wait on [Flash]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FirefoxAMD64FlashJava").

-HP printer works!

Haven't gotten to and probably won't ever:
iSight, wireless, Bluetooth, iPod, video codecs

So, is Ubuntu ready for the desktop?  Is it grandma-friendly?  I dunno, this ain't my grandma's desktop.
