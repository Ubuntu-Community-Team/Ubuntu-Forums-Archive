### Post by daengbo on 2007-11-05
Jake,

Boot into Ubuntu. When the screen messes up, hit 
CTRL-ALT-F1. 
Log in. You'll get a terminal. At the terminal, type
sudo dpkg --reconfigure xserver-xorg

Go through the screens, selecting nv for your driver. Everything else can probably be left at the defaults.

When you finish, type
CTRL-ALT-F7
You'll get your gibberish back. Then hit
CTRL-ALT-BACKSPACE
to kill the graphics layer. You should have a normal screen when it comes back up.

If, for some reason, it doesn't want to come back up, type the following:
CTRL-ALT-F1
sudo /etc/init.d/gdm restart

You will definitely get the login screen then.

Best of luck
