### Post by zbyszeksz on 2010-10-30
Hi all,
first of all I would like to thank all the developers of Ubuntu, it made my life easier. I really enjoyed it.

Unfortunately, there is one big problem with it, ie. versions. Ubuntu 9 is better than Ubuntu 10 - mainly in user interface and being userfriendly - and it is not only my opinion but also all the people I know. Let me explain it.

I involved myself in distribution and promotion of Ubuntu between anyone I know and anyone who asked me for advice (I am IT guy). It started to be a matter of ideology to me, like "be a free man and leave your Windows for Ubuntu, it's simply better".

Sadly enough, when I have installed them newest version of Ubuntu, people started to give me negative feedbacks and they were really confused with it's inteface (eg. "macish" close, maximize/minimize button of a window). 

When I have showed them my Ubuntu 9.04 all of them told me that it is more userfriendly then version 10 (the one they had) and they have been asking me why I installed them "worse Ubuntu". It is more sad that most of them have switched back to Windows as they did burn their fingers on Ubuntu 10. Even when I have convinced them that Ubuntu 9 works fine, they didn't want to listen about any Ubuntu again. They simply installed Windows in its place.

So I really found myself sad that version 9 is no longer supported. I, myself, will have to go back to good old Debian and stop promoting Ubuntu, as it only puts people against any Linux as a good system (ie. better than Windows) - not because I no longer like Ubuntu (I'll still be positive about it as a project) but because it is counter productive ie. not efficient.

Sorry for giving this negative feedback to Ubuntu 10 as I think you really do good job but my experience with non-IT users shows that Ubuntu goes wrong way. Reactions of people show that they are willing to switch to Ubuntu 9 but not to the version 10. I know that they can use version 9 anyway but when the version 10 is not supported and not promoted it is far more difficult and confusing. 

I think going a new "macish" style wasn't good way. I know, I grumble but I thought I just need to give you this feedback as I care about taking people out of dirty hands of Windows.

### Post by darkhelmetchris on 2010-11-03
I'll happily add my 2 cents.

I have been using Ubuntu for several versions now, going back to about 2005.  For me, even back then, it was a treat to use an OS that was designed for everyone and everything.  I have recently installed 10.10 and have been (mostly) very happy with it.  There are a few things that are a little broken, but I'm sure that will get fixed in the updates.  It is certainly faster than previous versions, and it really does wonders with some of my hardware.

I admit, I did have to change the window manager buttons back to the right side.  No, not immediately.  I really did give it a few days to see what I would feel like.  After the test period, it just didn't sit right with me.  I didn't mind that they changed them to the left, as long as I could still set them back to the right - and that's what it's all about I think: You can do things YOUR way.  That's the spirit of it.  You can't change them to the left in Windows, at least not as far as I am aware.  You can't do what you want over in the blue zone, and you'll regret trying.  Yet, over in the orange zone, you can stand, walk, sit, run, you won't get called offsides, and your teammates really ARE part of the team.

Ya, so I had to customize; so what.  That's no big problem.  It's a collaborative project and I respect that.  Overall, it's a heck of system.  You can change it to your liking.  It's hands-down faster than Windows, by a LONG shot.  It's for everyone, even if they don't understand it yet.  No worry.  It will still be here when "big" blue messes up their next one or four revisions (without me, thankfully).

So far, Ubuntu 10.10 has been a pleasure.  I'll be keeping it.  I'm sure that 11.04/11.10 will bring some more custom changes.  It's all good.  I'll still look forward to seeing them.  I certainly won't abandon it based on cosmetics that I can re-apply.
