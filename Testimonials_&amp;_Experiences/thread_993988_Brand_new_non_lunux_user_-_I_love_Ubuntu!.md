### Post by poetstorm on 2008-11-26
Hi, brand new here to the forum. About a week ago I was a solid Windows user. Me and my husband had rtied Red Hat years ago but it was about as east to use as a nuclear power plant. Well ,last week Bill Gates in his infinite wisdom (LOL) decided to install some sort of Genuine Advantage update. I do have a genuine install but apparently the update was not compatible with mylaptop hardware and it wouldn’t boot all the way and kept freezing. Tried a reinstall of XP b but discovered that SP2 was no longer supported! I thought I was up the creek when my husband suggested Ubuntu.

Let me tell you it did take a bit to install especially my wireless card but once it is working I am very, very impressed. I don’t know if I will ever go back. The gui interface is just as user friendly as Win XP with some cute new features. Mounting a USB drive or viewing a network one seems pretty straightforward and I’m not typing any commands into a terminal. Installing supported programs through Add/Remove is a piece of cake. For a non linux user, without much instruction by my husband I was able to figure out how to do everything I needed to. We even got Photoshop CS2 to work through Wine and I would have really missed that program. The only thing I can’t get working is Flash but I am sure with this forum it can be figured out. So I just wanted to shout it to the rooftops. Ubuntu rocks!

EDIT: Flash problems have been fixed! Thanks everyone!

Laura

### Post by BigSilly on 2008-11-27
Thanks for sharing your tale. It's much the same as my own. I'd had Windows XP installed for an absolute age on my desktop, and then one day whoosh, it locks me out and tells me I'm using an illegal copy. I couldn't believe it. My mate told me to give them a ring, but without any kind of receipt or key on the side of the unit, I had to just presume that I did indeed have a pirate copy, in spite of the fact that it was bought from a good local store.

My thoughts were alerted though. How come this company has this kind of control over ***my*** computer? I just thought it was a worrying state of affairs. I managed to successfully remove the malware (for that is what it is) known as Windows Genuine Advantage, but my relationship with my PC had changed now.

Luckily, a work mate at the time had been espousing the benefits of open source, and thrust all manner of Linux discs my way. It was a bit tricky at first, trying to figure out how repositories worked and whatnot, but boy am I glad I stuck with it. I started with Mandriva 2006 Discovery, then moved on to distros like Freespire, SuSe, PCLOS....then found Ubuntu.

I removed Windows altogether a year later, once I'd discovered how complete an experience Ubuntu is, and have never looked back. I much prefer to use Linux all the way now. I feel quite sorry for those who feel they are trapped with Windows, and only hope that, like you (and me), they find their way to Linux and have a positive experience.

Thanks again for sharing your story.
