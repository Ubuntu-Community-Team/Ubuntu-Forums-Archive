### Post by Boomy on 2006-07-15
Well I'm trying to get Fink workin on my Mac, so I can run Amarok, which is 10X better than this Itunes hype BS (which sux in comparison), and I need GCC3.2 or something like that as a compiler. So after alot of searching, I discover there is no download for it other than the Mac developer BS site. Damn I hate my freedom limited by a corporation! So I register for it, and find out that I need OSX 10.4 to install it. Oh no, I have 10.3.9!! I get tired of searching around, because I know that in an advanced operating system, you have tools like apt and synaptic that do this chore for you. 

I have to say usability wise, Ubuntu is better, Mac has the cool Expose' feature that my buddy thinks is the bees knees, and yes it does work better than compiz, but navigating OSX is still much more of a hassle than Gnome. I don't need eye candy, I need results. 

I won't even get started on the hardware. My Mac is 3 years old, but the speed feels more like a Pentium I. My PC desktop is 3 years old and is powerful as a mofo in comparison. There is a reason Mac finally switched to Intel after lying for years about how their CPUs were superior. No comparison, Intel and AMD slay IBM. 

Stability wise? Well I have had 1 freeze with Ubuntu after installing the software synth zyanaddsubfx, which was totally buggy for me in Windows as well. It's a shame becuase it is the best sounding softsynth out there. OSX stability wise? It required hard reboots more than once, but when you have a dinosaur CPU, that is to be expected. 

Where does the Mac shine? Well it can run Photo$hop, but I'll take the Gimp because it's like $1000 less than photoshop, and meets my needs just fine. However, the audio production apps for Linux do not compare to those available for OSX or Windows. I can not get Ardour to even run on my system. It gives me some midi error, which I attribute to my onboard sound card. I have a big $ pro soundcard, but it is not supported by alsa, so Linux unfortunately cannot fulfill my needs for music production. It cannot run Ableton Live, Propellerheads Reason, or any other commercial pro audio app, which is a shame, because that is the only thing keeping me from being 100% Linux. 


But I have to say, audio production aside, Ubuntu blows away OSX and Windows XP. I think Ubuntu is easier than OSX for general use, and imo, it "just works" better than OSX.

### Post by Derek Djons on 2006-07-16
Nice to hear that you have found purpose for Ubuntu Linux! It's also interesting, reading your story. I also have a Mac. An Apple Mac Mini G4 to be precise.

I use Ubuntu Linux on my portable. I browse on the machine, check my email and do some writings. I love it, because I can take it to any room and keep working. If there's a app. that needs to be installed, I just quickly throw the apt-get commands in the terminal. Very easy!

But I won't forfeit the Mac for all the gold in the world. I know it's personal but I use the Mac in the same way you do. My mac is here for Photo editing, web design and the more serious work.

I love Mac OS X features like:

- Spotlight
- Automator
- UNIX based
- Installation / Removal ease
- Relative speed through the years

For me, I don't see any reason why I should totally switch to just one OS, whether it's Mac or Linux.
