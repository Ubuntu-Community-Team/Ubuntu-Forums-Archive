### Post by nathandh on 2006-07-25
After noticing my main pc (running Xp Prof) was getting bloated and slower I knew the next format wasn't far anyway anymore.
After finding out of yet another severe security hole that will probably takes weeks for em to patch.
After that final explorer.exe crash.

I knew I had to make the full switch to Linux.
I've had some experience with Linux before, namely red hat but at that time I had some problems configuring it and for some reason I didn't bother enough and leave it there for a few years.

So I decided it would be best to start of fresh and Ubuntu seemed perfect for that.

Like I expected I ran into some problems at the installation (x server was struggling to start up and Gparted acting weird) but I didn't take long (10 minutes to be exact) thanks to the awesome and fast help of some individuals on this forum. (it's so nice being able to connect to the internet with the LiveCd whiles you are installing Ubuntu on the same system).

It didn't take long until I managed to install my ati drivers thanks to these forums again and all my other hardware got nicely detected (even my WiFi card worked staight out of the box!).

With the help of Automatix and the build-in add/remove function installing software has never been easier. In no time I had replacements for all my software I used on Windows. A very nice end user experience.

The only thing I still wanted was to transfer alot of my music and movies from my windows partition, I just searched the forums again and in around 10 minutes I mounted my ntfs partition and could start copying the files.

It's an awesome feeling that I won't have to boot up Windows anytime soon!

Nathandh
