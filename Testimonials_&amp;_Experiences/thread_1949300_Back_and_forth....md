### Post by cuppsy on 2012-03-29
Just wanted to toss out a little hat-tip to the developers and everyone behind Ubuntu for the past few years. There's been lots of backlash and lots of frustration since the switch to Unity (much of it justified). And I just wanted to throw a little praise in the mix, since my latest go-round with Ubuntu has been better than I expected.

I first used Ubuntu in 6.06, then missed a few released and came back at 10.04/10.10, which were the best examples of a complete Linux desktop I'd used to date. Then came 11.04. At first Unity seemed neat, but like many, many other people, it impeded my workflow and was too drastic of a change. I did the requisite distro hopping before trying 11.10.

Oneiric was definitely better, but was still getting in my way a bit, and most of all: it was super unstable in my experiences. When tinkering with Compiz, I'd lose Unity entirely. I couldn't use Launcher icons for WINE programs. It was a mess.

I have to say... I've spent some time away, and even a bit of time with 10.04 again, and now I'm back. The updated GNOME Classic in 12.04 was compelling, and it led to me giving 11.10 and Unity a second glance. And I love it now.

Most importantly, it's been stable. No more glitches, no more icons or configuring things just not working. It's been responsive. And I spent some time (something I know many don't want to or shouldn't think they should have to do, which I understand) watching some videos on Unity, learning some of the shortcuts (recently found out about ALT-~ for switching between multiple windows in one application, awesome) and... it's great.

It's a different workflow than virtually any other system I've used. It's taken some tweaking and some getting used to keybindings (which, really, was the same when I used GNOME 2, KDE or even just a WM like dwm). And once I've gotten everything set, it's glorious. I'm a web developer who typically runs nine workspaces with Firefox, SQLyog (in WINE), GVim, a couple terminals among other things daily... and I'm efficient.

Anyways, that's enough rambling. And perhaps this is pointless, but for myself (having bounced between love-and-hate for Ubuntu) and to contrast the crazy amount of disdain for Unity, I thought I'd share a little hat-tip and say good on ya. Ubuntu's back on top in my book, and 12.04 looks to be even better.
