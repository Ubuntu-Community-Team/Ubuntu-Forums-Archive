### Post by Izobalax on 2007-05-19
Firstly, let me explain a little of my Linux history.....

I made the proper switch from Windows XP to Ubuntu Dapper 6.06 in Nov' 06. Frustration with a buggy, corrupt XP machine, the price, and my moral objection to Micro$oft flipped me over the edge. My father gave me an old Apple Mac G3 of his. I bought Mac OS X Tiger, installed it and ***loved*** using it. My only problem with it was that it kept having a kernel panic after every hour or so..... I figured, after research and experimentation, that I needed to change the RAM in it. I've yet to do this, but one day, when I do, I'll have an Apple Mac machine next to my Linux one. :grin:

So anyway, I got a new PC, it's specs are listed in my sig. I installed Dapper on it, in much anticipation, and ***immediately*** fell in love it. The ease! The look! The stability! The freedom from spyware and viruses! I had died and gone to computer heaven.....

After joining to the forums, and tweaking my system to fix a couple of things (*mainly the screen resolution*) I started "distro-hopping". This seems to be a common thing, I've noticed. :grin: I had a ride on Freespire v1. I liked the KDE interface, and it was very user-friendly, but after installing fonts on my system, it would kick me out and wouldn't recognise my login password anymore. This happened after many re-installations. So, I put away Freespire, deciding to keep eye on it for future versions. 

I then tried Sabayon v3. I ***loved*** it and stuck with it for a couple of months. In-built working Beryl and MP3/codec support and a sexy KDE interface? Marvellous stuff. 

Just before Feisty got released I tried out OpenSuSE 10.2. This is another ***great*** distribution as well.

However, once Feisty got released, I decided it was time to return my origins. I could no longer avoid Ubuntu's ***massive*** community and ***huge*** package database. At the beginning of May I installed Feisty on my PC and it's been like a dream since. Initially had problems with my screen resolution again, but previous Ubuntu and other distro experience enabled me to fix that one. A couple of other small issues popped up as well, but posing the questions for the minds of the Ubuntu community meant a solution was usually arrived at within a couple of days. :grin:

I've decided, now, to ***stick*** with Ubuntu permanently and contribute. I can't code much, or programme, but I ***can*** produce graphics and art. This is my contribution to the Ubuntu community. I have an art.ubuntu.com album, I've submitted stuff to gnome-look.org and kde-look.org and I've added my entries to the Ubuntu forums gallery as well. I also post all my contributions on my DeviantART gallery as well. Check it out, by all means -----> [http://izobalax.deviantart.com](http://izobalax.deviantart.com)

And this is where I am now. I contribute art and graphics to Ubuntu, to pay back the massive work that's gone into Ubuntu, and I try to help out new Ubuntu users wherever I can. :grin:

Let's keep the ball rolling people! :grin:

/izo
