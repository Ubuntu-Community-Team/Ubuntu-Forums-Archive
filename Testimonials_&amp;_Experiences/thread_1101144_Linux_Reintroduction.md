### Post by LADmaticCA on 2009-03-20
Hi everyone!

I recently found myself in the position of having 4 gigs of ram in my main XP machine but only 3gigs usable. I also had a 64bit AMD processor I never fully took advantage of, so that got me curious to look at some other OS's. In studying for my A+ certification I was reminded of Linux, which I had used briefly some time ago, and decided to see how far it's come. 

After reading lots of positives about Ubuntu I decided I'd start with a simple wubi install. Wow! What a speedy and gorgeous OS. I love the Add/Remove installer as well as commands like apt-get and the repository system. The amount of quality free software is pretty amazing! Most importantly all of my hardware worked just fine, but I did struggle to get the nvidia drivers to work. After about 3 days I finally discovered I had to add a line to my xorg file to use the DVI on my monitor. The worse part was the answer was right in the official documentation :oops:. I shall definitely look there first from now on. 

I guess I've been using Ubuntu off and on about 2 months now. I've gone on to do an actual install on my older box and have been enjoying tinkering around with it as well. I've done lots of digital painting in Photoshop, but I'm curious what I could produce with Gimp. Anyways, I haven't made a full switch from Windows but I'm officially very interested in Linux and Ubuntu, and I look forward to learning more. And Jaunty is looking really good :D!

You can probably tell I'm a lurker. I felt it was finally time to register and post my experience. Also, I did refer to these boards many times to find solutions, so thank you to everyone that helped me even if you didn't know it.
