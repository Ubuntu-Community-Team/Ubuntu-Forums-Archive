### Post by Dodger73 on 2008-05-07
A friend (long time Windows user) has a Toshiba Satellite that simply decided to stop working. It had Windows XP pre-installed, but at some point simply decided to blue screen during boot, and reset itself. The same happened when trying to boot in safe mode or even from the Windows CD.
He was out of ideas and ready to bring it to the shop or replace the hard drive, as there didn't seem to be a way to get it working.

Yesterday, I (Linux user since 4 years ago) downloaded the Kubuntu Hardy CD and booted the live setup on said laptop - without any problems whatsoever. Wireless and Audio worked out of the box, everything seemed to be going smoothly. I was even able to rescue his Documents folder from his Windows partition, which left him happy and quite impressed. Windows, afterall, didn't even want to boot.
The first installation attempt failed because the installer froze up when loaidng the partitioner and trying to scan the hard drive (which points to some issue on the hard drive itself). That was quickly remedied by wiping the partition table with fdisk, after which installation completed smoothly. After the reboot, with a little bit of guidance from me, he installed firefox, set up his IM accounts on Kopete, played some music in Amarok and was changing the desktop settings, wall paper, window decoration and widget styles to his liking.

The first comment after installing firefox through adept was 
"That's it? That was easy.". Getting his yahoo messenger account set up in Kopete, he was delighted at not having to do more configuration and setup, when after three mouse clicks, his contact list populated from the server. After about 20 minutes he was able to change system settings like mouse acceleration, look&feel settings, use GetHotNewStuff to install new wall papers, Kopete emoticon themes and Superkaramba widgets, look for and install new software, and generally comfortably play around with the system.

The only problem came up when we installed the restricted ATI drivers and ended up with a bunch of garbage (but a working mouse pointer, nonetheless) on screen after rebooting X. Relatively easy fix for me, but he most likely wouldn't have been able to fix this on his own - this is bad, and more work is needed to give users a failsafe graphical environment to fall back on.

I'll try that one again tonight to see if I can get it working. I can only imagine how much fun he's going to have with Compiz.

Other than that, this experiment has been pretty successful so far, and there have been a few 'oooh aah' moments when he discovered a cool feature. I'm sure there will be some issues down the road to get certain things working - but, and this is the critical point of this post, the general base line setup - meaning, getting the OS installed, booted, connected to the internet, and necessary software installed - I can say with full certainty and confidence, is now much faster, easier, less frustrating and simpler than it is with any version of Windows I know of. His last remark of the evening was "After using this, I honestly don't understand why people pay hundreds of dollars for Windows."

Great job, Ubuntu devs. Keep it up, and keep ironing out the remaining kinks. :)
