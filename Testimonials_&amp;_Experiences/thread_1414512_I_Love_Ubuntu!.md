### Post by Chris_cur on 2010-02-23
I tried Linux for the first time back in '06 when my Dell ran into issues. While I was stationed in South Korea my HD ended up getting wiped, and a buddy put a pirated version of Windoze. Once they caught on to my shenanigans I tried to switch over to OpenSUSE. Epic Fail!!! 

Fast forward to October last year, I bought my first laptop an Asus Ux50 with Vista. It was supposed to have 7 on it but it didn't, I tried to upgrade it and that was like jumping through hoops in a 3-ring circus. I didn't like how half of the programs were only trial versions and upgrading to full versions cost $$$. So, I started looking back into the Linux community.

I had issues installing Ubunut 9.10 on the laptop but it installed perfectly on my tower, which is now a dual-boot (but this shall soon change). When installing 9.10 on my laptop I would get the live disc menu to try or install etc. Whenever I installed I got a big black screen for long lengths of time (each time varied from 15 minutes to 5+ hours), then on occasion I would get the glowy white Ubuntu logo, then if I was lucky, I would get scrolling code. It would scroll and scroll then stop. After the code stopped I would get the nice little blinking curser. This was the extent of my 9.10 progress. I was quite perturbed by this but I continued on.

My wife told me about Linux Mint. I tried out Mint 7. It installed without a hitch and I was happy with finally having a working laptop and in the open-source community. When Mint 8 came out I tried it out and surprise, surprise I came across the same issues I had been having previously with 9.10. After some research I found out that Mint was based on Ubuntu, so I found the version of Ubuntu that Mint 7 was based off of.  All Kudos to the Mint people but it was not for me. 

I burned myself a 9.04 Jaunty Jackalope, threw it in my sad laptop, installed it successfully and I have not turned back since.

Even with my issues with 9.10 I am completely pro-Ubuntu. I love the philosophy behind the distro! I enjoy the ease in updating my system and adding new programs. Every once in awhile I have some installing problems but nothing worth mentioning here. I have upgraded to Ubuntu Studio and have yet to really DIVE into all the recording things. I keep telling myself that I cannot learn everything at once.

My terminal skills are getting a little better. Don't ask me for advice though, I am useless, as my wife is figuring out. (I installed Mint on her Dell laptop, she likes the "elegance" of it better than the Ubuntu feel.) I am doing the little changes and fixes for her because it just easier for me to them them than try to explain the processes.

My next step is setting up a Ubuntu server on my tower. I am also looking for Linux programs and apps to help out my legal work. I was so happy to find a FOSS time keeping program for my billing.

I feel like an Ubuntu stalker, I am prowling the Internet looking for sneak peeks to 10.04 and wishing I could work for Canonical or Ubuntu. Sadly I am merely a Legal Professional and no computer guru.  

I highly suggest Ubuntu to anyone that it considering the switch!
