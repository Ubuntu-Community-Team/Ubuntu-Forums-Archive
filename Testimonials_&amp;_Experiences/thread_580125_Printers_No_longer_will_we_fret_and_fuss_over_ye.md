### Post by MrChonks on 2007-10-18
In honor and extreme respect to the Ubuntu community and developers who made Linux and printers finally play nice.  Here is a portion of a blog entry about the Gutsy release and that which I found unbelievable.  Forgive my excitement if anyone else out there has already A) stated this and B) found this to be true for other releases.

The unbelievable:

Under Administration, I found an option for “Printing”.  Anyone who has ever used Linux before knows what a pain in the *** setting up printers has always been.  I have normally opted to simply post a file on a share or email it to another OS so I can print it.  Much easier than pulling your hair out.  But, since I vaguely remember mention of new and improved Printer capabilities with this release, I decided to try it out.  When the options window appeared, I only had “PDF” as the listed printer.  Above that is a button called “New Printer”.  I clicked this and it immediately performs a search for attached printers.  Given, ours is a networked printer, so I didn’t have much hope, but was pleasantly surprised when it was listed in the devices window.  From there, I selected my printer, clicked “Forward”, selected the appropriate HP driver specific to my printer model and then ran through the rest of the guided setup.  Once completed, it provides you with a “Test” button.  Again, with trepidation, I clicked the button to send off the test print job.  Again, in the past, I have experienced issues with printing from Linux where you will print one half page of data and walk out to a ream of paper sitting in the tray with nothing but gibberish atop.  This time, however, I could see the Ubuntu logo in full color and the test colors being printed out in various percentages.  Once complete, I sat there and just reveled in the fact that they finally got it right.  I can now print from Linux and not have to wonder if it will **** up along the way.

Thanks, Ubuntu, for giving me my printer back and leaving no reason to have Windows on any remaining system.
