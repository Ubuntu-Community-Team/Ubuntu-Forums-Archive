### Post by dlegend on 2007-12-24
**Introduction**
I'd like to start off by saying that I have been using Ubuntu for about a good month and a half now and it has been like a roller coaster ride with its up and downs. Having said that, I have tried one other flavor of Linux in the past -- using the PCLinuxOS 2007 distro. I did a decent amount of customization on that OS but found it to be too slow (with KDE) on my Dell D600 Latitude laptop. It wasn't until about a month and a half ago until I decided to try Ubuntu (Gutsy).

> 
**Note:** The actual specs of my laptop are as follows (this pertains to my experience with Ubuntu): 1.4 ghz processor, 1.25 gb of ram, and a Radeon Mobility 9000 FireGL RV250 32 mb graphics card.


I might add before going to my experience that I started web design when I was 10 and started programming in Visual Basic when I was 13 -- most of my programming experience being in the Windows operating system. Much of my fascination for Linux (as you will read below) derives from my love of customization and writing applications.

**Experience**
So, after installing Ubuntu for the first time I found GNOME to run much faster then its KDE rival and from the get go I started customizing my OS following this guide: [http://tuxenclave.wordpress.com/2007/11/23/ubuntu-customization-guide-v2/](http://tuxenclave.wordpress.com/2007/11/23/ubuntu-customization-guide-v2/)

Being familiar with basic commands and the general feel of Linux from my past experience, customization didn't take long. However, along the way I did run into issues:

For example, trying to rename my mounted drives but not knowing how and accidentally formatting my whole machine (Note: Creating MSDOS label in gparted does NOT rename a mounted partition)! But that didn't stop me. It only took me about 5-6 hours to reinstall Ubuntu and get everything back to where I had it originally.

But anyhow, during my customization experience I gained valuable knowledge in the Linux filesystem and learned things about the GRUB and how to dual-boot to Windows XP for my gaming habits. I also realized how awesome Linux was in terms of graphical effects in that it takes relatively low processing power to do all the cool things that it can (using compiz-fuzion).

I also found out that Linux and its community can be pretty entertaining as well. Such as the discovery of an easter egg in nautilus which I posted on my websites forums here: [http://forums.programming-designs.com/viewtopic.php?id=1128](http://forums.programming-designs.com/viewtopic.php?id=1128)

In addition to those things, I've learned how to connect to my Windows PC using samba, use fstab to mount partitions, use several bash scripting/terminal commands, configure the xorg.conf file and other system files, restore settings from the terminal, working with rdesktop & VNC, and I'm sure several other things that I can't remember off the top of my head right now.

**Current Desktop**
I feel it is necessary to show you guys where I'm at, at least with the customization of my Ubuntu desktop:

My desktop appearance is heavily inspired by Windows Vista and Mac OSX -- trying to combine the best features of both into the ultimate desktop.

**The Future**
I plan on eventually getting a quad-core machine with Linux as my default OS and hope to do some more advanced things such as VMs and virtualization, setting up boxes dedicated towards specific tasks, getting more applications to run under Wine (currently my laptop isn't powerful enough for games/high-end graphical applications), and to just push the machine to the best of its capabilities using Linux/Ubuntu.

**Verdict**
I'd like to say that my overall experience with Ubuntu and Linux in general has been awesome -- having all the special effects and things set up the way I do and using only around 300mb of ram is great. If only Vista could be as memory efficient and customizable as Linux! I'd like to add that my experience with Ubuntu has led me to love Linux in general all the better and has sold me to the open source community. As of now, Ubuntu isn't my default OS (currently using Windows XP) but it will be on my next machine :)

I'd really like to see how Ubuntu and the Linux community advance over the next few years and I'll be sure to ride along with the rest of the community as well... The ride is just too good to not experience!

This post isn't as in-depth as it could be in terms of my whole experience, but I just wanted people to get the gist of it and to not fall asleep reading through my entire experience :). Thanks for reading! Feel free to leave any comments.
