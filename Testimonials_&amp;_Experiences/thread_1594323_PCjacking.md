I'm just curious - what do people here think of PCjacking? ([http://www.manucornet.net/pcjacking/](http://www.manucornet.net/pcjacking/)) Wikipedia defines it as 'the act of putting a Linux Live CD in a public computer, such as one located in a department store or public library, and rebooting it into Linux'. There might be issues due to your not having permission from the host of that computer. However, it might be gratifying carrying around your operating system with you in a USB stick, thus obviating the need to lug around a laptop, and surreptitiously log into the operating system from a public terminal, if permission is not to be had, so that you needn't use Windows - not to mention the benefits of security. I don't know which the best distro to use would be - maybe Ubuntu, maybe Knoppix, maybe Puppy. At any rate, the idea is appealing on the face of it. Has anyone tried it?

### Post by coffeecat on 2010-10-12
> **Robert Buckmaster said:**
>  There might be issues due to your not having permission from the host of that computer.

This is the central point. If you do not have specific permission PC-jacking would be wrong. Besides, there might be terms and conditions for the use of a computer in a place such as a public library. You might be contravening them.

Much as I might like to use Ubuntu on a public computer, I would hope that the administrators have been foresighted enough to disable booting from CD and USB in the BIOS and have password-protected the BIOS. **You** might want to use a live-CD or live-USB for entirely innocent reasons, but there are enough technically-able but irresponsible people out there who could use a Linux live CD to do something malicious. This is why I hope the BIOS would be attended to.
