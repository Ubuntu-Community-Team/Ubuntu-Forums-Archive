### Post by JamesV on 2009-06-09
I recently picked up a Compaq laptop on sale. I ran Jaunty on it and ran into the frequently reported problem with the Atheros wireless chipset. So far, I've been unable to get the wireless to work right despite trying madwifi and running the windoze drivers under ndiswrap.

So, I thought I'd contact HP customer care to report my difficulties. I expected zero help but I wanted them to know that they have customers who want Ubuntu.  Maybe if enough people ask, they'll come up with an Atheros driver.

HP/Compaq's response?

"If you have an HP Pavilion or Compaq Presario consumer notebook PC that shipped with Windows Vista, you **should not** attempt to downgrade to linux. Changing the operating system (OS) will cause severe operational problems.  ...HP does not provide linux-compatible drivers. I regret for inconvenience caused to you."  (Bold is HP's emphasis).

Wow, they have it backwards. Putting Vista back on the machine to get wireless working again felt like the downgrade to me!

Just lettin' my fellow Ubuntuans know that **HP is not Ubuntu-friendly**.
