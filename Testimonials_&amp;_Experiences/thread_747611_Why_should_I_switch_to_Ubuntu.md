### Post by azredwing on 2008-04-06
Hi all,

Right now I'm running Fedora 8. Fedora 9 is going to be released in a few weeks and I'm most likely going to upgrade. But..

I have been thinking of switching to Ubuntu to give it a shot, instead of upgrading to Fedora 9. There are a few issues that I never got resolved when running F8 and I'm interested in seeing if these issues are solved/better in Ubuntu.

_What I **can't **do in Fedora:_
I am running on a Sony VAIO VGN-SZ110 laptop. Since I switched from Windows to Fedora, I have never been able to get the Fn keys working; the screen does not dim when an external power supply is removed; and sometimes has trouble sleeping/hibernating. The webcam works after installing a driver I found (r5u870).

Skype doesn't let me use any other programs with sound (no sound from Rhythmbox, say) when running. I found several fixes online, tried them all, and nothing worked. This may very well be a hardware/driver/didn't configure ALSA correctly problem, but I don't know and quite frankly don't have the time to troubleshoot. 

I never did get my Logitech wireless mouse's buttons working in Fedora. I found how to reassign those buttons, but it never worked like I wanted it to.

_What I **can **do in Fedora_:
I've got a dual monitor set up -- the monitor on my laptop and my external 17" monitor, running Xinerama. When my laptop is docked, it detects my monitor and runs Xinerama immediately. When it's not docked, it detects that it's not connected and keeps the 1 screen. (This is when the computer is shut down, anyway -- if I tried to undock the machine while it's running, everything just freezes and I have to remove the battery.)

My Logitech keyboard buttons (multimedia, scrolling, zooming) all worked out of the box, with the exception the "F-keys" mode where the Fx keys become keys that open stuff like MSWord, Excel, PowerPoint; or print, or save. Not that big of a loss to me. I'd rather hit \cS to save a document instead of F8. Whatever.

I got my printer to work easily. My network connections (wireless as well as ethernet) all worked out of the box. 

I switched to Fedora because I was interested in running Linux, and my work uses RHEL -- so I figure that Fedora would be a good step since it would run something close to work, so I wouldn't be unfamiliar with it all.

I am not a Linux noob who just came over from Windows recently (okay, fine -- December -- but I'm good with computers, and at my work I write code exclusively in perl and bash, and I'm pretty handy in C). I know my way around /. (not the website.) I only run sudo and never root. I own SELinux (although sometimes with SAMBA it returns the favor.. but that's another post.) I don't need GUIs (although I use them because it just makes life easier to deal with) to play with SAMBA, CUPS, video card settings, etc. (I may need some help from the internet sometimes, but nobody knows everything after all.)

I enjoy Fedora a lot, and aside from hardware/driver issues I've never had a problem.

But I have seriously given some thought into trying out a new distro since I will be upgrading to F9 in a few weeks anyway.

I am a poor, starving college student who is either in class or at work all the time, so I really don't have the time to go out and try a new distro of Linux. I have read several threads on Ubuntu vs. Fedora, but most of the stuff I can see either very little difference or the differences really don't apply to me that well. It's pretty much a coin toss for me.

So, good people, please convince me that when I reinstall Linux, I should reinstall Ubuntu and not FC9. I've done my own research and so far I am indeterminate. 

Thanks for your time.
