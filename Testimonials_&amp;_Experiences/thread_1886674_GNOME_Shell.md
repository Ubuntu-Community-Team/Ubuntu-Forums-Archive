### Post by r.darwish on 2011-11-25
I started using Ubuntu 10.04 LTS while 11.04 was available. I really enjoyed the experience, but wanted to get more updated versions of softwares, so I switched to 11.04. I didn't like Unity at first but no problem, I could just use the classic GNOME interface. 

When I upgraded to 11.10 I saw the the classic interface is not available anymore, so I decided to install GNOME Shell and give it a try. After I installed GNOME Shell I saw the classic interface is available once more, or so I thought.

I decided to give GNOME Shell a shot, despite some bad reviews I read on the Internet. After using it for a couple of weeks in my PC and my Laptop I must say - it is awful. It's unintuitive and inconvenient when working with a lot of windows. It preforms smoothly when the CPU is idle, but when the computer is under medium load the animations aren't smooth anymore. It's even more horrible on my EEE-PC Laptop. 

I don't understand why the minimize button isn't there anymore. 
I don't understand why there's no visible bar that resembles a taskbar.
I don't understand why every time I want to switch windows using the mouse I have to go to the "overview" mode, which is stupid when I work with a lot of windows because the windows thumbnails become very small.
I don't understand why the tray icons are hidden at the bottom of the screen.
I don't understand why the overview mode is activated when the mouse hits the top-left corner, because the mouse might get there by mistake.

The thing that really broke me was the fact the "File operations" dialog was hidden from the overview window. That means the if a copy files I need to manually move all windows to the screen edges so I can find my "File operations" dialog again.

Some of my complains can be solved by using extensions, but it doesn't make sense that third-party extensions are needed to make the interface usable. I also wanted to try to "fit myself into the designers' head", but I don't understand what did they think.

GNOME Shell looks cool at first, but it's unproductive in my opinion. There are some people that use Linux which do not adapt to new interfaces very easily, and if I, a programmer, had a hard time getting use to the GNOME Shell, what are they supposed to do?

Also, trying to remote control my computer using x11vnc from the Internet was a horrible experience because of the "overview window".

"No problem, I'll just stick with the classic interface" I said, but I turned out the the classic interface in GNOME 3 looks like someone hacked it to work under GNOME 3. The theme is ugly and many features are missing. It looks as if GNOME developers would kill the classic interface completely if GNOME Shell didn't require 3D acceleration, which isn't always available.

I think that GNOME is going in an bad direction. They had a good and simple interface for a long time, but I think someone there just said "Let's do something drastic in the new version" instead of thinking how to improve the good old classic interface.

Finally I moved to Unity and I'm quite happy with it compared to GNOME Shell. At first I didn't understand why Canonical are detouring off GNOME's mainstream and developing their own interface, but if that's where GNOME is going then I'm glad I'm using Ubuntu and not Fedora, otherwise I would had no choice but to switch to KDE.

I will be glad to hear other opinions

### Post by Roasted on 2011-11-25
When I started testing out Gnome Shell many months before it was ever released, I hated it. I passionately hated it and began to throw rage fits every time I tried to do something in it that I couldn't accomplish.

But then, it began to dawn on me exactly where they were going with it. The lack of minimize and maximize icons are actually rather nice. I mean, why do you need them? Where will the window minimize to? There's no Win 95 esque taskbar. 

At first I began to wonder how in the world I can swiftly switch from one window to another. Once I realized I could click down on the scrollbar and that window would become backgrounded.

The more I use Unity and Gnome Shell, the more I like both of them, however I think Gnome Shell takes the cake as of now. I hope that'll change in the future. I would love to see Unity become amazing and widely adopted. But so far, I just don't see it.

Not to mention, Gnome Shell runs great on my Atom powered netbooks... while Unity still gives me some lag hangups even on higher powered machines. Unity has come a LONG way since 11.04 though, and I'm sure future editions will be even better. That said, so will Gnome Shell. So we'll see what happens. :D
