### Post by VeeDubb on 2011-10-28
I'm going to be 100% honest here as well.

Your post is absolutely right if you're talking about 4 years ago, and absolutely wrong if you're talking about today.

It has been a very long time since I had the slightest trouble with flashplayer on Ubuntu.  It's been equally long since I had any reason to try gnash, flash-aid, or any of the other work-a-rounds that are floating out there.

I have a strong suspicion that many of the people who have had recurring trouble with flash have had those troubles because simply installing flash from the repos was not their first attempt.  Rather, they tried to install from a tarball, then tried some workarounds, then tried the repo (which didn't work because of all the other stuff) and finally ended up in a loop.

The last time I ended up in that loop, I got out of it by purging every package related to flash, then searching my entire harddrive for files with flashplay in the name, removing them all, and installing from the repo like I should have to begin with.  Suddenly, everything was working fine.
