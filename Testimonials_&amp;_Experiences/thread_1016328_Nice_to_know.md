### Post by mtbsoft on 2008-12-20
Well I've just read through all your posts and threads and think you're being grossly unfair.

You seem to have received plenty of help, despite trying to "dance a tango before you knew how to crawl" - you've thrown yourself in the deep-end and have received plenty of advice, you seem to simply expect to have everything handed to you on a plate.

You need to remember that all forum users are volunteers, providing free help and advice in their own time.  So a couple of posts suggested you need to cough up some cash, so what!  Most of the posts have been constructive and helpful, despite what you say.

Have a little patience and be selective about what advice you follow; just give yourself, your computer (and other users) a bit of time to settle in to your new regime otherwise you could miss out on a great opportunity to learn a whole new world.

My best suggestion for you would be to reinstall Vista but only use about half your disk for it, then install and dual boot Ubuntu on the other half - that's essentially what I did 18 months ago (read XP for Vista).  This way you can use Vista for those tasks you cannot yet get to work in Ubuntu while having Ubuntu available to gradually get used to and get working the way you want.

In time you can gradually use Vista less and Ubuntu more and eventually even get rid altogether.  You need to remember that some things are not designed for Linux and simply may not work - sometimes Wine is the solution, sometimes a VM (Virtual Box, VMWare, etc.) is needed and sometime you just have to use raw Windows.

Stick with us mate, we'll try to get you there.
