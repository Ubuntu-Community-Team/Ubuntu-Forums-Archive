### Post by wolfen69 on 2008-07-01
after about 5 years of trying every distro under the sun, and still falling back on windows, about 15 months ago i went all linux. i guess i could be considered a power user by most standards.(35,000 MP3's, many videos and dvd's, some video and audio transcoding, bittorrent, cd/dvd burning, printing/scanning, photo editing.....) and linux has allowed ne to do these things with relative ease. when i discovered linux, i found home. 

i demand full streaming web content. i play an occasional game, usually with the LinuXgamers DVD. (dont tell anyone, but i have a hard drive with xp (not connected) that has never been online, and stripped. but i hook it up every once in a while to play 2 games that we love.) ubuntu allows me to be creative and free :guitar:

ive had my own pc repair business for about 2 years now, and love it. it's nice to get paid to do what you love.  and i'll never profess to know everything, but what i do know, i know very well. the one thing i feel i excel at is knowing operating systems , install methods and what will work on any configuration.. i feel it is the 1# thing you should learn if you're going to get serious about computers. but linux has actually added a new dimension to my business. i now have 6 customers at least dual booting. and they love it. the trick is to know who is "linux ready". i think you would be surprised how many people are ready to use linux. yes, you have to set it up for them, but once they get used to the simple goodness, they are hooked. anyway....


i advertise for old and unwanted computers online, and you wouldnt believe how many people *give* me real nice computers. the average person has no use for something that could be fixed with a simple re-install. instead they buy new computers. but then again, that's good for me. i had one guy give me 8 computers! i made some good coin too. 

lastly, sorry for the run-on sentences and so forth, but that's just a quick (personal) testimonial.
