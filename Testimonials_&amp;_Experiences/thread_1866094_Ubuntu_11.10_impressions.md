### Post by pablolie on 2011-10-20
I have an avid user of Ubuntu since the 7.x release.

I raised my eyebrows some in 11.04 - I did think Ubuntu had taken a turn to a degree of user-friendliness and dumbing down that, in my opinion, does not match the people likely to use it; or underestimates the amount of learning people are willing to do.

With 11.10... sorry, I find it utterly unusable. I can no longer make many choices, the menu fights to hide advanced user configuration options... this is where I will have to bury it and go back to Windows. Oh heresy.

Go back to 10.04 guys. That was the best Ubuntu release, shame about the hardware support. The wannabe Apple UI and the urge to put a a dumbed down interface on an OS that will always require some willingness on the user to learn are a step too far in a direction I can no longer use. It requires too much fighting to tune Ubuntu to my needs.

I'll stay on 10.04 and 11.04 with some machines. I loathe the 11.10 UI that no longer can be easily changed (I was able to figure it out in 11.04 easily enough, now Ubuntu forces the silly left hand strip on us).

I have to take another train. Please, please get back to basics. 11.10 is absolutely not what I need. But hey, if gadzillions of people now drop windows and apple (ha!) it'll be worth alienating the average Ubuntu user (which I regard myself to be).

Truly sorry about this message, but I truly feel this is headed the wrong way. If it's just me, ignore it.

### Post by W.McL on 2011-10-24
> **mastablasta said:**
> Like Xubuntu, Kubuntu and Lubuntu are not an option. it's either Ubuntu (GNOME) and Windows in this world isn't it?

KDE is very unlikely an alternative to people who liked Gnome 2, because it's completely different and cumbersome to use and the lack of integration of the typical gnome applications won't make that better.

Xfce and Lxde are lacking much functionality, which might be an OK trade off if you're running them on weak hardware, but usability wise is a step down compared to Gnome 2 (although Xfce is catching up slowly)

The Gnome 3 fallback session is a step down as well, because of several reasons, I'll list only the 3 which annoy me most so far:
[LIST]
[*]The indicator applets aren't ported to it yet
[*]The "System" menu was replaced by a cumbersome configuration dialog that takes more clicks to achieve the desired task.
[*]The User menu lacks the possibility of directly accessing shutdown, hibernate and suspend, costing me another additional click where I don't need it. 
[/LIST]

The often suggested Linux Mint is only an option for people who can live with a dumbed down GUI installer, RPM based distros are a matter of taste as well, not to speak of the configure and/or compile everything yourself approach of some more obscure distros.

Gnome 2 in my Opinion was and still is the best choice when it comes to a productive and intuitive yet still customizable desktop environment.

I'll give the [Mate]("https://github.com/Perberos/Mate-Desktop-Environment") fork of Gnome 2 a try until (hopefully) the Gnome 3 fallback reaches the usability of Gnome 2, or Xfce catches up, whatever happens first.
