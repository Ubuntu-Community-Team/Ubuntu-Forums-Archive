### Post by Clayton South on 2008-01-29
Hi Everyone, 

Here's the story:  my wife has had a sony vaio laptop for the past 7 years, and it was running XP, but very painfully slowly. I installed Ubuntu on my older Dell Inspiron 6400 machine, and now on my HP machine, but I couldn't get Ubuntu to install on her machine - no matter what I tried.  She suggested just throwing out the laptop, but I refused, saying that any linux install is better than the best microsoft OS, and that I would take her "slow" PIII 1.6ghz laptop and, with linux, make it work the way it should. 

It took me a long time of searching, but given my conversion to Ubuntu I simply refused to give up - and I finally ended up installing 6.10 on her machine as 7.04 and 7.10 wouldn't run. 

I post this because I'm very, very proud of our community - we're free people making free choices to use an amazing operating system that we can tailor for OUR exact individual needs. And, each one of us is an innovator in a movement of software freedom and CHOICE.

I'm proud now that my wife is using Linux and is starting to really "get it" that linux in general and Ubuntu specifically is not only a superior OS to Windows, but also a superior CONCEPT - one of freedom and innovation. My faith in Ubuntu was rewarded - and with the help of our community here. 

So here's a big THANK YOU to everyone here - let's keep helping each other and making the difference - one computer and one mind at a time.

Thanks for listening!

-Clayton South
