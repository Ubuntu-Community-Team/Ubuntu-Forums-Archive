### Post by Orval on 2006-06-21
It took a while, but finally I have Dapper all set up and everything rocks. I experienced more problems with Dapper than I did with Breezy, especially getting the fglrx drivers to work.

With the new release I also changed from KDE to Gnome, just to give it a try. And I must say I love Gnome. It is much faster and stable on my machine. Considering that Dapper is faster than Breezy, you can imagine how happy I am.

I always had trouble running gnome programs in KDE (gtk-qt engine issues, random crashes), but I have not experienced any problems running KDE programs in Gnome so far. So, I now use a mix of KDE and Gnome programs from within Gnome. Konqueror, [Listen]("http://listengnome.free.fr/") (great app, better than amaroK and it doesn't crash), digiKam, GnomeBaker and GIMP live happily together.

A great challenge for the near future is to get XGL or AIGLX and Compiz up and running, just to look what the fuzz is all about.

One thing, though. I don't think Dapper is ready for the masses yet. Too many things don't work right out of the box. I wouldn't recommend it to my parents, unless somebody else installed it and set the system up. If everything works, it is a great OS for even less experienced computer users.

Nevertheless, I am impressed and happy. =D> Even my wife likes Ubuntu now. \\:D/
