### Post by MKR. on 2007-04-29
My first foray in to the world of Linux was in 2000 when I tried Slackware. I had experience using a command line and figured most of the criticisms of Linux's difficulty were from hardened windows power users. Oh how wrong I was...

I installed it on my massive 6gb secondary hard drive so I wouldn't interfere with windows 98. The install went well enough (it even added windows 98 to LILO for me), but beyond that I was at a total loss. Aside from a handful of useful articles and helpful acquaintances in an IRC channel I frequented, finding information on how to do little things like get a window manager running, or access windows shares on other computers that would be useful to a beginner were nowhere to be found. It wasn't until 2006 when I tried again...

Flash forward to June 2006. I heard good things about this new distribution called “Ubuntu”. I'm a fan of anything with an unusual name, so I gave it a shot. Dapper installed without a hitch, and I was in a classy looking gnome environment. I was surprised at how far Linux had come in so little time.

I figured I would use it until I had gained more experience, then switch to a “serious” distribution. I was a month in to a Linux class, so I knew the experience would come. Now I'm 9 months in to the course, and still loving Ubuntu (it has only gotten better with each release). The irony is that the more experienced I became, the more I realized that having the guts of the system in my face all the time would be a pain in the butt. I've stuck with Ubuntu partly because of the great community, but mostly because it has abstracted the guts without hiding them, so I only have to look at them when I want to.
