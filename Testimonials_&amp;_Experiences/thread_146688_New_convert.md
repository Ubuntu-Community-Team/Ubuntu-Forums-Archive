### Post by cordaen on 2006-03-18
Hey all,

I'm new to the forum, new to Linux in general, and new to Ubuntu.  I've been a Windows user for a very long time... decided I'd give some linux distros a whirl.  Tried alot of them.  First distro I tried was Red Hat 9... screwed up a good 40gig drive.  Tried Mepis, Xandros, Knoppix, Puppy, and a couple others... but so far I like Ubuntu the best.  Nice GUI-Integration, diverse driver-compatibility, great security/stability... combined with (what appears to be) a good community support-base & regular software updates... I dig it.

Ironically, Ubuntu 5.10 was one of the most difficult distro's to implement.  Goofy keyboard errors on install, wrong display/resolution, refusal to accept network drivers, and installation progress freezing at 83% on 4 different machines.  However, after some tinkering & forum browsing, I now run Ubuntu on all 4 machines dual-boot with WinXP, networked wi-fi, and even tweaked to support REAL Nvidia drivers.

One main thing I've noticed on this forum is the amount of unanswered questions... most of those being the same stinkin' questions over & over again.  For cryin' out loud, do some searching in the forums... I got this distro working on 4 machines, each with random errors on install/config.  I helped myself, not asking a question once...and I've only had experience with Windows 2000/XP.   

Well, I'll get off my soapbox & just say thanks to the community.
