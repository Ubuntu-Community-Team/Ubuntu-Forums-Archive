### Post by BruisedQuasar07 on 2011-05-17
Puppy for tablets would be a fantastic project!

After the train wreck new version of Ubuntu. What a huge step backward, team Ubuntu took with release 11.04 What gets me is you would think the team would have learned from beta testing to run back to the design table and start that release again from scratch. I suspect either Ubuntu ignored complaints from average Linux users or more likely Ubuntu has allowed beta testing to fall into techie hands. That is, beta testing has become to in house, to mutual admiration society like.

Gone from the desktop is Ubuntu's famed user friendliness. That version should have come with a manual, a desktop schematic & a warning "for oddball linux enthusiasts only." I stress oddball because I'm a Linux enthusiast of several years and if my only exposure to Linux was Ubuntu 11.04, as much as I hate microsoft products, I would be praising Windows even as i hate it. It would be love-hate because the love for Windows would only be because the alternative is far worse. To top it off Ubuntu 11.04 is too big, too slow.

As I continue introducing newbies to Linux, I'll be sure to show them PuppyLinux or Ubuntu 10.XX, certainly not 11.04 and I hope I can hook them before they get wind of the Ubuntu 11.04 upgrade circus.

User problems with the 11.04 upgrade is out of sight. This version disaster could prove to be worse than the Windows Vista clown show. The sad thing is Ubuntu is so big, so popular that 11.04 will impact the entire Linux community. That and Google Android's sad performance in the tablet world darkens things for Linux.

Team Ubuntu picked a very bad time to turn over its desktop to extreme techies (people OK with investing more time & effort tweaking and mastering an O/S than actually using a computer as a tool for work or fun). Did Ubuntu forget that most of us have work to do and lives to live. that most of us see computers as a tool, not an end in itself? This year Microsoft will be moving to dominate the new tablet world. Android leaves much to be desired in consistent functionality and user friendliness, even with release of Android 3.0, and the first Tablet with Windows Starter 7 is out there. We have been here before remember the first Asus LINUX netbook, the one that caused the netbook explosion, the Eee PC?

Asus launched netbook with Linux and Linux community let the netbook opportunity slip away into the hands of Windows XP. The issue was user friendliness and once Intel entered the scene, portability. Letting Google Android carry the Linux water with the initial Tablet explosion will waste another opportunity for explosive growth in Linux use. For once can't a Linux team be Face Book and not Napster or Myspace?

It would be a brilliant move if team puppy left the Linux pack to its reinventing the desktop and laptop PC and trying to retake netbook and skipped forward to work out a Puppy version for tablets. As a successful investor, I can tell you mobile devices are the future, home desktops will continue to fade out and laptop sales will continue to drop. Intel is making great strides in designing a powerful processor for netbooks and I am confident Intel will turn out powerful dual core engines for tablets. 

With a little vision Ubuntu could be the face of under $300 tablets and the coming $150 to $200 Internet netbooks. First, this entire Unity Apple wannabe desktop must go right into the trash can.

If I was the founder of Canonical, there would be some Ubuntu heads in the unemployment line, namely the entire Unity management team. They belong at born again Commodore Computer reinventing the wheel.
