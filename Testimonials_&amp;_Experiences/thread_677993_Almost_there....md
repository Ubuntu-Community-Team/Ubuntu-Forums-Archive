### Post by saunders on 2008-01-25
My experiences with Ubuntu - and they are positive, despite what you may think from the thread title...!

I decided to investigate Ubuntu just over a year ago after finding out that a recently bought, brand new XP-loaded laptop would probably keel over and expire if I put Vista anywhere near it. I didn't like the idea of having a laptop with an unsupported operating system once MS withdrew XP support, and didn't want to have to fork out for major upgrades to a new laptop just so it was able to run the current OS! I mean...what is it, 20Gb for the OS alone?!?

So, I chose Dapper over the then just-released Edgy because of the LTS (not fully realising how the upgrades work!!) and installed as a dual boot, just in case. All went well. A noticeable improvement on speed, I wasn't constantly having to virus or spyware scan, I was impressed with the variety of available software, and there were a hundred little things that just seemed so obvious I wondered why MS hadn't implemented them. Tinkering with the system was pretty easy, to get stuff sorted it was mostly just Synaptic or at a last resort copy-and-paste into the terminal and Bob's your uncle. I upgraded to Edgy a few months after.

There were a couple of bugbears that wouldn't go away though. Firstly was getting my Creative Zen recognised by the system. Both Gnomad2 and Amarok just wouldn't see it. Then there was reading my windows partition...for the life of me, I just couldn't get NTFS-3G to work. I found myself pretty much using Ubuntu 50:50 with Windows.

A few months ago I decided to try a fresh install of Gutsy.

All I can say is WOW!

GOne are my driver woes...it really goes out of the way to be helpful, offering to sort out the drivers from the start. I can see my windows partition out-of-the-box. My Creative Zen is now easily recognised. The eye candy is superb, available from the start and even works on something that was straining to run XP. I can see how far Ubuntu has come in a year, and it's damn impressive. I'm now almost 99% exclusively using it. Before I get labelled as a fanboy, I'll say there are some things that I have to occasionally fire up windows for...for example some applications that won't run in Linux, or the odd website that still requires you to use IE, and there are things I just can't get working - Rosegarden/Jack for example. 

Basically, what I'm trying to say is if you don't get on with Ubuntu/Linux at the moment, keep trying the upgrades. It's coming along in leaps and bounds.

Er..maybe I am becoming a fanboy...!
