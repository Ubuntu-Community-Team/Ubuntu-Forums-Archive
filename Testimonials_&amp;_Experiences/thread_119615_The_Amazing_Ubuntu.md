### Post by browndog on 2006-01-19
You've all heard the story countless times...someone who worked with and supported Windows operating systems for years finally got tired of mediocrity, and discovered the lighter side of life under Linux.  Well, that's my story as well, but I must say that I'm not only happy but truly amazed at what Ubuntu has done.  I installed Windows XP professional on a computer with what I thought was a malfunctioning integrated sound device; I say I thought it was malfunctioning because try as I might I just couldn't get sound out of this thing.  I tried bios updates, bios settings, chipset updates, different driver versions, nothing.  So guess what happens...I install Ubuntu, and this wonderful operating system detects the sound device just fine, and even as I write this I'm rocking out to my tunes.  

I don't care what anyone says about tens of thousands of Windows titles this, and hardware support that...history is replete with examples of how things get easier when you go with the status quo, but get BETTER when you invest sacrifice and hard work for the sake of something truly unique and good.  As of right now I'm systematically working toward the day when I will be Microsoft free, and quite happy about it.  I wholeheartedly support the open source / Ubunu philosophy, and think that things like Ubunu and OpenOffice really are a gift, obtained by the work of some very talented individuals.
