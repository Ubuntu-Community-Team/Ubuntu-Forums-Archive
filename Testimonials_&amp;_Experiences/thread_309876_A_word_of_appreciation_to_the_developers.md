### Post by davesmith on 2006-11-30
I have been using 6.10 Edgy on a tosh satellite A30 laptop now for a month. I tried Ubuntu out first with a live CD in the summer and was suprised at how well it worked, considering. 

I also thought that the forums were very friendly and helpfull, and if I did have a problem, so long as I had internet connectivity someone would help.

After giving it considerable thought, i took the plunge and reformatted my laptop h/d and did a clean install of Edgy. No dual-boot. To my suprise, it worked immediately. I had internet with Firefox, Evolution email and the firewall up and running straight away. Later that evening I was importing documents and reading them with OpenOffice, using Gaim and burning CD's.

The only problem I had was writing to memory sticks etc, but having learned from Taurus how to mount a new external drive and format it to ext3, I was up and away.

The nice thing about the forums is that the how to's etc are there to follow, once you master using the terminal and synaptic. Very quickly I had my printer installed and an external DVD burner working. Automatix2 solved the codecs issues and the internal DVD player started up with a better quality display than before!

So, a month into the installation, how do I feel about Ubuntu?  Well, I cannot imagine ever going back to the old OS.  Edgy does everything that I used windows for, but without the security issues. It's rock solid, the tosh loves it and so do I!

One last point. I'm not an IT professional, just a guy who was  fed up with windows. I think the reason it went so well for me was because the developers have got it nearly right with Edgy in the first place, and because of the community spirit here in the forums that helps you. Thanks!
