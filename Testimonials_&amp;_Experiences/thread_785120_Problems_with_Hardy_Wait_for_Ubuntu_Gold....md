### Post by zoubidoo on 2008-05-07
Many people are disatisfied with Hardy and are having problems. But then, many people are happy with Hardy.  So who's right?

Both of course.  It's just a question of user expectations.  The problem is that some of us are expecting too much.  We were spoiled with Dapper and are expecting the same very high standard from all Ubuntu releases, especially LTS ones.

When I voiced my disappointment with Hardy, another user suggested: "if you want a rock-solid system you shouldn't have upgraded but waited a while until the bugs are out".  That's  good advice.  So it's pretty clear that beta-testing and the release candidates are insufficient testing for some of us (very) demanding users.

So I'd like to suggest a new label for LTS releases: **Ubuntu Gold**.  After an LTS release has been released to the general public and the number of bugs has dropped right down, say after 6 months, the ubuntu release reaches the Gold status.

Then when people download Gold, they know that they are getting a rock solid system.  And if they download a non-gold system, they won't be disappointed.

GNU/Linux and Ubuntu developers are doing a fabulous job, some of us are only complaining because we are upgrading too early.  Give us an "Ubuntu gold" label and we won't be so negative any more.

Z.
