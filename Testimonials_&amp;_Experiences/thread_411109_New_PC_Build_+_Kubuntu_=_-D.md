### Post by Tanker Bob on 2007-04-16
I just built a new PC based around an Intel E6600 Core 2 Duo.  I used an MSI P6N SLI Platinum Motherboard, EVGA GeForce 8800GTS 640MB video card on an PCI Express x16 interface, and 2GB of OCZ Platinum Rev 2 DDR2 SDRAM.  To keep the E6600 cool, I installed a Zalman 9700 heatsink/fan with Arctic Silver 5 Thermal Compound.  Linux compatibility was one key criteria in choosing the hardware.  I carried over my LG DVD writer, 3.5" floppy drive, 250MB Zip drive, 320 GB Western Digital and 60 GB IBM ATA100 hard drives, and a modem card from my old machine.  I packed it all into a Raidmax Smilodon case.  You can read more about the hardware and the assembly process on [my blog here](http://www.mobiletechreview.com/ubbthreads/showflat.php?Cat=&Board=tankerbobblog&Number=27344&page=0&view=collapsed&sb=5&o=&fpart=1).

I was already running 32-bit Kubuntu Edgy smoothly on the 320 GB drive in my old machine.  After powering on the new PC and ensuring the BIOS found everything correctly (which it did), I simply booted the same hard drive through to Kubuntu on the newly assembled PC.  It booted like there was no change at all, finding all the new hardware and activated it.  Kubuntu assigned a VESA video driver until I installed the newest NVidia driver for the 8800GTS with Envy.  To say that the transition was effortless would be an understatement--no drama, no troubleshooting...it just worked on the first boot!

The 60 GB drive has WinXP SP2 on it for a dual boot arrangement.  I have yet to get it to boot successfully on the new PC even in safe mode.  So much for Windows' highly-self-touted hardware compatibility.  I'm not sure that it's worth the effort to get XP to work, as I'm running XP in a virtual machine under Kubuntu as well.

A friend who works across the hall tried to get Vista to run on his PC, which he just built two months ago.  He had so many hardware issues due to the lack of driver support that he took Vista off and reverted to XP.  Plus, he's had serious problems with activations because of all the hardware changes he makes to his machines.  He's totally fed up with Micro$oft.  I just told him of my experience and recommended that he move to Linux.

To rub salt into the Windows wound, I loaded Beryl on my new PC last night.  After fiddling with the xorg.conf file per the HOWTO and setting the NVidia driver to the best picture rendering quality, it works great.  Oh, and I'm also overclocking the system by 10% using the BIOS settings.  When Feisty releases later this month, I'm going to load the 64-bit version to take full advantage of the new hardware.  Life is good.

The end result is a screaming Linux PC sporting a beautiful 3D desktop environment, and a very happy Tanker Bob.  Congratulations and my heartfelt thanks to the entire Ubuntu/Kubuntu team for a superb OS!

### Post by Tanker Bob on 2007-06-16
My system is working great under x86_64 Kubuntu 7.04.  I've overclocked the E6600 to 3.125 GHz and the memory to about 900 MHz.  It's blazingly fast and very stable.  Beryl is still beta and has its problems, especially a bug that it brings out in the nVIDIA driver.  K/Ubuntu 7.04 doesn't support most scanners anymore because of a poor implementation in the kernel, so that's an issue but unrelated to my build.  Outside of that, I'm a very, very happy camper.

For the record, WinXP totally choked on the new system and required a rescue reinstall plus all my driver disks to work at all.  Kubuntu booted the new system as easily as if there were no change at all (except for the nVIDIA driver which I had to load for the new 8800GTS card)!
