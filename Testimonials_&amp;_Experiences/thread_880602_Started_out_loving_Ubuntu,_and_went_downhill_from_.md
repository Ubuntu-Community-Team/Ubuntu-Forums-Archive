### Post by Kila_Bite on 2008-08-05
In the 3 weeks I have been using Ubuntu, I have had it refuse to speak to wireless drivers on a Toshiba laptop, the Ubuntu GUI refusing to load, the sound randomly pack up for no reason, windows opening for no reason, unexplained closure of windows for no reason, links on websites clicked on for no reason, applications opening many many more times than they're supposed to, this morning I woke up to find that Compiz has decided it doesn't want to work anymore for no reason.

The thing is, I didn't instigate any of these. Ubuntu just keeps messing up. It seems to work as it should half the time and you spend the other half in the terminal scratching your head as to why things randomly decide to stop working. Don't get me wrong, the support and community is great. The OS on the other hand seems to be very unpredictable. At least with XP I could rely on it to boot an environment when ever I turned my computer on, at least with XP I'd know the sound would always work, at least with XP, there was no nasty surprises. I didn't get malware, ad-ware or viruses because I was savvy enough to avoid them.

Ubuntu is full of nasty shocks, it isn't dependable. When I start my computer in the morning, who knows if I'm going to see a desktop today?

I'm sorry to say this guys but from a user perspective, I can't rely on Ubuntu to do the job I want it to do and I'm in split minds about going back to XP.

### Post by Kila_Bite on 2008-08-05
Ah sorry, perhaps I didn't explain myself correctly. I started playing with Ubuntu on a Toshiba laptop. It was one I was borrowing from work to play with. I actually own a Sony Vaio. The Toshiba never worked but I didn't really put this down to Ubuntu. It had a realtek wireless card with an unusual configuration (it was plugged in to an internal USB port inside the laptop.) I forgave Ubuntu for this when I put it on my Vaio. The install was a breeze and all drivers and configuration worked first time around.

Since then though, I've been having problems that occur usually after updates that seem to mess up the GUI or various settings. I turned my laptop on this morning to find Compiz wasn't working and had to reinstall Nvidia drivers again. This sort of thing wouldn't usually bother me but its just the fact that different problems seem to crop up every week and I have to keep fixing them.

I have decided to stay with Ubuntu for now. I absolutely love everything about what this OS stands for and (when it works) how it operates and feels. I'm just getting a little annoyed at having to keep fixing it.

### Post by Methuselah on 2008-08-05
Compiz stopping after an update and having to reload the nvidia driver probably has something to do with an update to the kernel.
Binary driver blobs and new kernels can sometime hate each other.

My ubuntu installation as been ROCK solid.
However, certain bad behavior manifested itself only when I decided to test the ATI binary driver.
Now I've had the desktop freeze when a flash video is coming out of fullscreen mode or the proper resolution failing to be set after vmware leaves fullscreen.

The installation of the ATI proprietary driver has definitely resulted in a more unstable machine in my case.
My guess is that many problems users face can be traced back to these non-free driver blobs which run with privilege.

### Post by coolen on 2008-08-06
Sounds like a pain.

You might look into downgrading to Gutsy. In my experience, Hardy wasn't the strongest release. I've resolved the main issues, and stability is generally high enough for me, but Gutsy was much more stable.
