### Post by demolishun on 2011-08-06
Here is my experience with 11.04.

First some background:
I am a veteran Ubuntu server, I have developed kernel modules for the Linux kernel, and have used Linux in one flavor or another for 12+ years.  Almost exclusively at home for 8+ years.  I know my way around the system and how to get just about anything I need done on the system.

Here is my experience:
I had an installed Ubuntu that was at 10.10.  So I figured it was time to upgrade and went ahead.  I get it installed and log in.  First thing I look for is the main menu: gone.  Okay I see this new menu filled with HUNDREDS of items with just icons: ?.  I am actually trying to get some work done and had no idea that 11.04 was going to put a new user interface on the machine: ?.  Now I have to spend time to figure out how to even be productive: frustrating.  So I find my way into Compiz settings (not sure how I did it, just started clicking crap).  I go in and try to adjust something. All title bars, menus, and any widget on the desktop: gone.  My reaction: WTF.  So, I am staring at a screen with absolutely no widgets whatsoever and not even a way to logout.  

I again am an experienced user, programmer, developer, etc.  I obviously figured out how to fix it.  Mostly by somehow getting a terminal up to search for what the heck is wrong with 11.04.  I find out I need to log into old Gnome interface to make it even work.  So why am I posting?  Because if Ubuntu truly wants to get the masses to use this, crap like this should NEVER happen.  At least it should bluescreen and reboot.  That has more appeal than a completely hosed desktop.

One major thing I don't understand is the obtuse nature of the responses from people on these forums.  The main theme is: If you don't like it then go away.  If it were just a matter of "hey we upgraded this, how can we make it better in the future." I could respect that.  But saying either like it or hit the road is just stupid.  Or the best one is: go make your own distribution.  

Perhaps there should be some warning or message saying this has been changed and here is some info to learn how to use it.  Also showing how to revert would be good too.  Making the assumption that people should just know how to use it, and "my kids run okay what is wrong with you" is lame.  They probably know how to program the VCR too.

So I can try and be productive:
1. Apple is not a target or definitive interface to be modeled.
2. Ubuntu does have a user base.  Their opinion does matter and their user experience does matter.
3. If you are doing something like moving the buttons around on a window or radically changing the UI then there should be a warning and a solution for reverting.
4. The developer DOES NOT know better than the user.  They may know the technology, but their view of the interface is very different from that of the user.
5. I should have stayed with the LTS version...  

Note: most of these issues are for upgrading users.  Not new users.  Let them be the beta testers!  Muahaha!
Thanks for creating, maintaining and providing Ubuntu.  

Sincerely,
Frank
