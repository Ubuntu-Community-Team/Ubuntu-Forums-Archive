### Post by johnman145 on 2008-11-25
Well, i looked and downloaded all the mentioned distro's and decided to give mint, mint-kde, and opensuse a try. My experience:

Mint was really cool. I was just looking around... tried some stuff and even my konica minolta 2300dl laserprinter (an oldy) worked like a charm and i didn't even had to reboot. All the settings and stuff were nicely centralized and on the whole it just worked. Its also ubuntu based (which on its turn is debian based) so that was cool to. The mint guys really understand what i as an OS user want. The only negative points for me were that the icons in the main mint were kind of ugly (not a real problem) and the mint team is a bit small. Someone asked for a custom installer (or booter, i dont exactly remember) but they said they have only limited resources and that was sort of to much trouble. That is a major issue since that means they will always ubuntu+ some extra and never can address the real weaknesses of ubuntu.

Mint kde was also cool, better looks, but not as wel developed as mint imo. 

Then i tried opensuse. I read somewhere that opensuse somehow, someway... managed to get the font right. For me that is really important. I do a lot of work on my computer and i dont want to have to put to much effort in reading from the screen which is ergonomically very bad. (NB with good fonts i mean good READABLE font. Vista i.e. has good looking fonts but they are less easy to read then the but-ugly winxp fonts so therefore i prever winxp for working ). The first time i booted suse everthing looked well, particularly the font. I browsed some, tried to get the printer working (did't work... no biggy but mint did without any trouble). Everything seemed okay. The kde (3.6-ish, not 4) did have a problem thought... the taksbar kept on crashing and crashing and crashing and.... So the first thing i did was a total online update and i figured that would surely solve the problem. Stupid thought... the kde bar kept on crashing like crazy. From my experience with linux i know that if the developers weren't able provide me with an OS that after a clean install isn't working properly... its arrogant to think i can get the job done as a linux noob.
Im still not sure why 3.6 kde is working perfect on my server and is doing awful in opensuse, but for me its just a "fact of live". The opensuse guys did their voodoo, as did the KDE team, but apparently that was not enough. One minor annoyance also was the fact it took forever and ever to boot opensuse. Even vista isn't that slow, which imo does say a lot. The lessons i learned:

- There is still not a single distro which van provide me a stable platform with dualscreen and native resolutions support and some basics like a decent config panel, good browser, readable fonts etc. 
- If you mix up 1000 programs which have much more functionality than any windows, you still haven't got a proper OS.
- KDE guys are crazy... i thought kde4.1 was horrible, but i read kde4.0 was even worse. To make thing even worse, a repo for ubuntu 8.10 was taken down by some kde dude before supplying an alternative. Shipping a bad product is one thing. Forcing us to use it is a whole other thing. And when some blogs say KDE is a big no-no, some guys are getting touchy because the KDE team worked so hard on it. I respect their work, honestly, but a piece of cr*p stays a piece of cr*p, no matter how much effort went into it. I preferred kde over gnome, but in my very humble opinion they need a reality check and i'm not gonna waste my time on it until 2010 (which is the year kde4 is estimated to be usable).
- Its seems you need a commercial company like novel or ms or ibm or whatever to get behind an OS before it can reach any level of usability. Else everybody is doing its own thing and you get something that doesn't  work very well with its own parts (like i.e. a graphical packet manager which you should better not use :P)

I don't want to sound trollish (not really at least), but i keep wondering why there is not even one linux distro which is as about as usuable and stable as my 10year old winxp. Thanks for all your help guys, see you again in 18 months when i try again :lolflag:

PS i made a joke about it, but a self respecting OS maker would NEVER ever supply a packet manager if it could break the system. For me that is a sign of bad priorities (but im aware that is a not really a fair conclusion since every OS has its problems).
