### Post by RESID3NT on 2008-06-25
I mean no rants, nor is my point "Ubuntu sucks". I just want to make this an healthy critique.

I use Ubuntu 8.04 because I want, and I like it, however (like in every software), there is some space for improving.

I started my Linux experience with Slack 10.1 . It was fast as hell in my 2.66GhZ Laptop. Everything worked with some effort and I has positive experience. However, due to software being only native in Windows (games, lol), and lack of patience I abandoned Slackware.

3(or some) years later I started having interest on linux again, mainly Ubuntu. I heard it was stable, simple and fast. Installed 7.10 and had a positive experience.
Everything worked out of the box or with one or command lines. Lots of support. It wasn't as fast as slackware on my old computer, but I guessed I could do some configuring and make it faster.

8.04 went out, everything worked out-of-the-box again, however it is noticiably slower. Boot takes 10secs longer, compiling is a bit slower. I understand and accept that updates will, in fact, improve the overall speed of the distro, but in my opinion, 8.04 seems "un-oiled".

I probably can have better performance with a WM instead of gnome, I know. 

RESID3NT's Hardy 2cents

Cons - 
Slower 
X.org is **** with dual screen

Pros - 
Large number of programs in reps
Customizable

### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2008-06-25
Hi :)

For me, i noticed that Hardy isn't the fastest OS to boot - but it is still a LOT faster than Windows either XP or Vista (with some softwares installed) on the same machine.

Only a plain, fresh installed, XP beats Ubuntu - by mere seconds.

BUT....... Ubuntu takes like one minute from "Power ON" to "End of HDD activity" and it is "fully on", i.e. usable! That is with auto-logon (i HATE passwords and am the only user of my machine). 

Windows Vista, to compare, took like 2 minutes (still reasonable) for the desktop and all icons to appear - BUT i still couldn't do anything as it kept hunting for files on the HDD and loading background stuff and services and whatnot, so in the end i had to wait slightly over 3 minutes before i could start an application and it would actually start right away (i could also start it the moment the desktop appeared, but the app wouldn't open until Vista was done with it's hidden boot procedures). 

Maybe one of the recent kernel updates would have made it even faster (Ubuntu that is) but i religiously avoid updates for fear of breaking something - as my system runs 100% perfect for me, fast and stable, i don't want anything to change, except for my own knowledge of Linux.

Oh, and i am NOT dual booting - i did the "cold turkey" switch - Vista off, Ubuntu on, a couple of days after Hardy was released, and never looked back. 

Best regards......

Thanh

Very honest - if Vista boots faster on your machine than Ubuntu, then you have a seriously knackered Ubuntu.

### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-06-25
Interesting point.

I guess it's an inevitable trade-off. Some bloat is the price Ubuntu has to pay to ensure that the default install supports more hardware and has more features. Ubuntu follows an "opt-out" policy instead of the "opt-in" policy of more minimal distros.

Of course, it's not as bad as Vista because removing an unwanted application or service it's usually an apt-get remove away, while Vista does not allow you to unininstall a bundled application like IE without requiring the sacrifice of two virgin goats.

It might be a good idea if an "official clean-up guide" is made available. A guide written by the developers that explains to newbies how to optimize your system step-by-step. "Don't have bluetooth? Do this the stop the BT service:", "Don't have the wireless card XXX? Do this to delete the firmware you won't need:", and so on.
