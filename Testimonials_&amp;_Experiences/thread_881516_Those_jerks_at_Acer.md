### Post by powerpleb on 2008-08-06
So I sent my Acer laptop off to get fixed. It was a strictly hardware issue, headphone jack wouldn't work on the left side unless I jammed in the cable as soon as I let it go it stopped working again.

On Acer's support site there is a questionnaire about your computer, one of the questions is about the operating system and whether it came preinstalled on the system. I found this reassuring, if they are asking whether or not the OS is factory default, surely that means that if it has nothing to do with the reason the customer needs support and the customer doesn't specify that they want the OS removed, they will leave it alone. My heavily tweaked Xubuntu installation was safe.

I get the laptop back, switch it on. Windows Vista graces my screen, followed by a waiting period of about 5 minute while it struggles to get its **** together on my measly 1gb of RAM. 

Now I understand why Acer has reservations offering support on an OS they didn't configure, but at least have the decency to ask before going off and erasing all my data. Especially when the reason they have it is so obviously unrelated to the OS installed.

Luckily I backed a lot of stuff up.

### Post by bmac on 2008-08-06
A good policy when returning a PC is to securely attach a note directly to the unit describing the problem and any references you may have including OS user name, password, etc. Also mention your preference as to maintaining the present HD unless failure is directly related to the HD....

I've received numerous units for repair and had no idea what user name or password to use, which required contacting the client prior to evaluating the system.

Unfortunately, many techs prefer to troubleshoot with windows diagnostic software and don't think twice of wiping the HD. So don't forget to backup the drive (if possible) prior to sending the unit in and always expect the worst - you probably won't be deceived....

I do however agree with [dca]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=142868") that many units go through a quick evaluation and it's determined to be more cost efficient to simply replace or shotgun....
