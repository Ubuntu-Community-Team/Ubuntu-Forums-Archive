### Post by rgb1701 on 2008-05-17
> **jrusso2 said:**
> I don't have a clue why people bother with the hassles of 64 bit.  At this point there are few advantages unless your running over 4 gigs of ram forget it.

Agreed.

I have no idea why people keep getting lured into 64bit OS's at this time.  There is zero meaningful benefit to most computer users- at best, ~15% speed improvement in real world apps (not compilers).  "Most" computer users don't compile lots of code- occasional make-installs, maybe.

I do tons of video encoding and processing, and don't use more than 1-2GB of RAM on dual core machines.

Yes, some day 64 bit will make sense- basically when /if there is a need for most people to have over 4GB of RAM.

That may happen as touch interfaces, speech recognition/synthesis, visual pattern recognition, and other goodies become embedded in OS's as the norm, but thats many years off.

99.5% of PC users should just stay away from the 64bit Linuxes- after all, 99% of retail Vista loads are 32bit, and ~95% of XP loads in operation among consumers are 32bit.  These represent most of the PC market (for the time being ;) ), so where this expectation for 64bit comes from when people move to Linux, I'll never know.

So, unless you have a need for >4GB of RAM- drop the chalupa, and step away from that 64bit distro :D

The real kicker is, most people's motherboards can't even *install* more than 2GB, 3GB or 4GB of RAM!
