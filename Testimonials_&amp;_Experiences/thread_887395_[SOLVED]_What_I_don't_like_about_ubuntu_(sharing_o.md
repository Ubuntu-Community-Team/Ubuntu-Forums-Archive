Hi guys,

I just wanted to share my opinion and maybe some of you will have some hot tips how to fix things that bug me.

(BTW I own HP Pavilion tx1320us, dual boot with vista, mainly use ubuntu)

1. I have to turn off and on the wi-fi switch to make the wi-fi work (same goes to bluetooth). What's interesting is that I can do that already during the booting process (the orange progress bar). I use ndiswrapper and my wi-fi works fine (after the trick).

2. Icons - it bugs me that gnome has no regular and standard layout of icons. By that I mean - the "keep aligned" function on the desktop doesn't work for me. Like the align grid resolution was too high. I'd like it to align in rows and columns, like in xp/vista, not to align to some micro-grid stuff I can't even see. So, the align and layout thing first. Secondly, the size of icons which concerns the mentioned matter. The pdf/jpg/other thumbnails are generated in non standardized size, this looks ridiculous (before I thought I could put up with it, but I'm already thinking of getting in touch with the developers, I'm too little experienced in linux programming). It's a usability issue, see how it works in xp/vista, and you'll know the difference, you'll get what I'm talking about :)

3. cheese doesn't work for me (hp pavilion webcam (uvc)) - I've just submitted a bug, but I've got a feeling it won't change much.. FYI the webcam works with gstreamer or skype..

4.  .. although I don't know how to brighten it :D The picture from webcam is shady, it's dark. And I know (from some listings, console output, etc) that there is actually a 'brightness' function, but I don't know how to get to it.
