### Post by the_darkside_986 on 2007-07-10
I think all technology suffers problems. For example, automobiles have lots of problems. They eventually have to be repaired and they rely on non-renewable resources (most of them). LCD monitors have a problem with having "dead" pixels that cannot be fixed.

I highly doubt that Macs are perfect and flawless. I've seen quite a few examples of Mac problems, not that they are terrible but they are not invincible. Also, I believe hardware choice is far more important than minor issues. With lack of hardware choice, the manufacturer could decide to restrict the user's ability to run unsigned code. (e.g. iPhone, PlayStation 3).

Problem-free OS's can be boring. I never did have any problems with Ubuntu 7.04 so I installed FreeBSD on a separate partition and attempted to get 3D acceleration and openGL working. It was very difficult and I had to install the OS twice but now it works. I am still trying to get a gnutella client to run on FreeBSD but with no luck. I don't think I could even get the text-based mutella client setup properly. I used to have lots of fun trying to fix openSUSE and Fedora Core 5.
