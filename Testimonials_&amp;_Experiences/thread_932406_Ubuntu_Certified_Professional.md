### Post by Darrell Lawrence on 2008-09-28
I've decided to become a Ubuntu Certified Professional. I've already registered for the first course.

That said, I'm 61 years old and on full disability. I suffer from multilevel degenerative disc disease and peripheral neuropathy a debilitating disease of the peripheral nervous system that has symptoms similar to multiple sclerosis. I get social security disability as well as private disability insurance. Prior to going on disability I was Manager of Industrial Engineering for a medium-sized manufacturing company, and I hold a masters degree in operations management from the University of Arkansas.

So why in the world would I want to become a Ubuntu Certified Professional?

I strongly feel that open source software is the future of computing, and I believe that Ubuntu will play an ever increasing roll in the choice of Linux as an operating system. I want to play a part in contributing to the community and promoting Ubuntu as a viable choice of operating system for both the desktop and server.

Perhaps I'll become a developer later on - I program in C and C++. And last, but certainly not least is the sense of accomplishment - that you are doing something worthwhile that is beneficial to society.

So it's back to school after a long break. Wish me luck.

Darrell
