### Post by waynemcl on 2008-09-01
I started with ubuntu 5.10 and was impressed. It was the first distro that correctly shut down a buggy VIA Mobo and also configured a nasty KTX monitor. I had moved over from SuSE because it ran snappier.

Upgrades went well, and every version got better and better. Now with Hardy, the browser's broken, sound's broken, flash's broken, pidgin with SSL to Jabber is broken. I've spent way too much time trying suggestions and trying to fix basic stuff, and it's still broken. I know this is from the change over to pulseaudio, and more secure compilation of pidgin, and versions of players needing to talk/be configured for the new sound server, not the fault of the ubuntu distro particularly, but the end result is, it no longer "just works".

I bought a mac instead. I love the FOSS concepts, but the execution just wastes too much time, and I need to get on and earn a living, or just play a movie to relax.

Perhaps something in the usability testing process needs addressing?

Cheers. Box-on!

### Post by mdsmedia on 2008-09-02
I only opened this particular forum because I was going to write my own testimonial, and I still am, but I found a couple of threads that peaked my interest.

This one was far more polite than the other, so I'm not going to have a go at the OP. I can't agree with his logic but at least he's polite about it.

I've been running Dapper on this notebook since its inception. I haven't upgraded for various reasons and had my doubts about upgrading.

My desktop is far less mission critical and so I've upgraded it from either Edgy or Hoary or maybe Dapper, through to Hardy. Though there are no real problems it's really slow and some things need a workaround.....until I have time to fix them or find that I can't, so upgrading to Hardy on my notebook seemed problematic. I've tried Xubuntu on both computers and quite like it. It still seems slow on the desktop though, so I was concerned about upgrading the notebook.

On Monday I backed everything up and hit that upgrade button. A few hours later my notebook was running Hardy, and I LOVE IT!! It feels like I've clean installed and everything's new again, but I didn't. I ran the upgrade directly from Dapper to Hardy, and it's running like new again.

After Dapper started to feel like it was labouring, I thought maybe a lighter Linux was going to be the only way my 3yo laptop would breathe again. I thought it might not be only Windows which needed updated hardware to run properly. It's restored my faith in Linux. I'm running Ubuntu Hardy...Gnome, not Xfce....and it feels like new again.
