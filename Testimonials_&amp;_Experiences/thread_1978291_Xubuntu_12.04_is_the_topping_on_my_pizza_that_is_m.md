### Post by ufugu on 2012-05-11
After reading so many good reviews on the net about the latest Unity, I tried it. Again. I love trying new things, and new desktops and interfaces. I want Ubuntu to succeed. And I am pretty easy to please.

But: I just couldn't enjoy it.  The reasons are already exhaustively detailed by others in these forums. Yadda yadda.

But after a fresh install of Xubuntu, I wanted to bump my old post of appreciation. It's fantastic. But the old post is[closed]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1767896").  So I'll just put it here again, because it still says whay I mean, and even moreso now:

> I'm I huge Xfce fan. I have never been a Xubuntu fan, but I have always kept an eye on you. I've used Mint, Zenwalk, Wolvix, Sidux, Debian, Fedora, Dreamlinux, Crunchbang, all with Xfce desktops. With 11.04, you have blown them away. 

Thank you for all of the thoughtfulness you have put into the release. It is perfectly executed. No lineup of applications is perfect for everybody, but the selection you chose is pretty inspired. Thunderbird. Pidgin. Obvious choices, and the right ones. gmusicbrowser, unusual but appropriate. The presentation with the faux-dock-panel is lovely, and shows off the power of the Xfce panel brilliantly. Suspend works on my ATI equipped laptop for the first time in years (probably not your doing, but yea!) and the Elementary theming looks very good on you. 

Ubuntu has it's own vision of the future, and that is Unity. But you guys are doing something that seems like real craft to me. You are putting the parts together and making things work in a refined way. It is awesome.

I've only used desktop Linux for five years, which is a long time to some and not long to others. But this is the first time I've installed a distro and not felt like I had to start tinkering right away. I'm not sure what a raise looks like for a group on volunteers. Beer? Pizza? Whatever it is, I hope you get it.

### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-05-12
I have read old reviews of Xubu (going back to like 9.04 and 9.10) that described Xubuntu at the time as "bloated and every bit as cumbersome and slow as it's 'heavyweight' parent."

Perhaps it was, back then. But I have tried other Xfce distros as well, just for fun: Debian Xfce, Phoenix (that's PCLinuxOS' Xfce spin), Crunchbang Xfce edition (Statler), Mint Xfce, SalixOS, and a couple of others my "Linux mentor" and I toyed with since he "adopted" me as a student. 

Compared to the *"lightweight"* Xfce distros (Crunchbang, SalixOS, and Debian), Xubuntu had more features and was easier to configure. The cost of that convenience was paid in speed, I guess, but hardly noticeable to me. I was used to Windows though! I was enjoying the ludicrous speed of *any* Linux compared to that!

But compared to other "*standard*" Xfce distros, Xubuntu 12.04 is NOT "bloated," nor "cumbersome and slow" even on this 8-year-old Dell with it's ancient Celeron processor and a paltry little 512 of RAM. Some applications are slow (Firefox), but they have counterparts (Seamonkey, Midori, Chrome) that are wicked-zooming-warp-speed-ultra-lightning quick! The Xubu devs have chosen most of the quick stuff. Wanna see a bloated Xfce distro? That Phoenix mixture from PCLinuxOS ran slower than Mepis (KDE, super-Mario-bloat) did on this machine! Xubu has been on a serious diet since at least Lucid. Now it's lean, mean, screamin' fast Xfce awesomeness that - on this old machine at least - runs no slower and no less responsively than even Lubuntu does!

Best Xubu ever!
