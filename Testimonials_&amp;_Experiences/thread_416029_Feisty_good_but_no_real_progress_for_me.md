### Post by don_eros on 2007-04-20
I'm a fan of Ubuntu. I really love that baby. Like any form of love, this particular fixation is deeply irrational, but today I'm a bit unhappy with my favourite distro. So this is either a rare moment of lucidity or I'm falling out of love, you decide.

I download the new Feisty Fawn as soon as it appears, burn it and immediately try the live version on my Toshiba Satellite A100-317.

It boots almost without problems (just a strange error message saying it failed to allocate memory, but it's just a flash before the splash screen). A nice balloon tells me I need to use restricted driver to take advantage of my wifi card and of my Nvidia graphic card.

Cool, I think, I just click on the checkbox next to the nvidia driver and the system says it will download it. Except that, I realise in horror, the Internet connection is not working. The network driver is already loaded and apparently working, but there's no signal. I don't have access to a wired connection at home, so here I am, in the dark of a world without Internet. The show is over already.

I reboot into my trusted edgy (here obviously the network works) and, against my own good judgment, I go on and do the network upgrade.

Everything goes rather smoothly, I reboot and, as gnome loads the session, I wait impatiently to see if the weather applet shows a question mark or the yellow bright sun that's the first sign of a working Internet connection. YES, the sun is there, everything will be all right.

Except the volume of the welcome sound is a bit too low, everything is set to 100% and yet it's really faint. I google around a bit and find a quick fix for that. Then I try the desktop effects: no joy there, the title bars of windows disappears as soon as I turn them on. Another few minutes on the Internet and I find a solution for that too, I just have to add an option to xorg.conf, not a big deal really (and to be fair it clearly says that desktop effects are experimental), but the fix has been on the beryl/compiz forum since 2006, why didn't they incorporate this in the official release I ask myself?

Even after that, I have to turn the desktop effects off because the close button at the top right corner doesn't always work and it drives me insane.

OK, last thing I really miss is hibernation. I hit the hibernate button and wait. OK, no graphical feedback, I can live with that. For some reason the screen saver starts, I move the mouse and enter the password to unlock the screen and see what's going on. Ah, OK, it's working. No it's not. No maybe it is. Oh yes it is, look the led has gone off. A few seconds of silence. Now let's see if it wakes up. I push the power button. The progress bar stops very soon and a dismal black screen looks at me. Then a very long line of "BUG BUG BUG" appear on the screen. Maybe it's nothing, let's just wait. I grab my toothbrush and go to the bathroom. But when I come back five minutes later, despite my minty breath, the laptop is still saying "BUG". So I first try to reboot it and after a while I just give up and turn it off.

I turn the computer back on again, I have a look around and I don't see a big difference in how things works: applications are still a bit slow to start up, the graphical interface still doesn't feel snappy. Nothing really new. It feels just like old edgy (not that edgy was bad, mind you). 

To be honest I hadn't expected anything really ground breaking, what I had hoped for was something that just worked, almost out of the box, but sadly we're still not there. For me (but maybe just for me) Feisty is not at all different from edgy. 

I upgraded mainly because I wanted to, but also because there were a few thing that didn't work in edgy. But  none of them actually work in Feisty either (not for me at least).

But then again, maybe I was just expecting too much.

### Post by HotFoot on 2007-04-21
I think it's very important for people to keep posting their issues and dislikes about ubuntu.  I'm a huge fan, and I've switched to using it on all my household's computers: a desktop, a laptop, and a media centre.  There are, however, some issues.

My main problem with updating to Feisty was server load.  I decided to just download a new .iso using torrents and do a fresh install.  I keep /home on it's own directory, so this method is a piece of cake for me.

The second issue I had was that my screen's extremely common resolution of 1280x1024 wasn't available by default.  I used the restricted drivers manger to install the drivers for my nvidia 6600 graphics card (awesome addition over Edgy), but this action didn't make the proper resolution available either.  I still had to manually edit the xorg.conf file, which puts getting things working into the experienced user's realm.  Then again, even with Windows most inexperienced users I've known never set the proper resolution for their screens.

To the good side, it took me very little time at all to do a fresh install and get my system back up to 100% functionality.  I've sucessfully enabled desktop effects, and I like it.  I've used Beryl before but I had some stability problems: after running for a while I could no longer use the keyboard/mouse for input, so my system was basically frozen other than tasks already running.  I hope not to encounter these problems this time around... so far so good.

I could not get desktop effects working on the media centre, which isn't of huge importance, but worth noting.  Maybe it just doesn't want to work for wxga resolutions.

Anyway, I'm sure that as long as complaints are made in a constructive manner, they will really help direct development for the next version. :)
