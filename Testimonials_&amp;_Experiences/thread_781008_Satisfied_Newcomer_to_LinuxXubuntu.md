### Post by kwebster on 2008-05-03
This forum is great. I research the internet extensively and this forum is one of the best I have found. There is a wealth of information and some truely dedicated people who take the time to answer any and all questions.  

Overall my somewhat limited experience with Xubuntu has been great.  I have an old Dell Inspiron 5000 450M PIII, 128M RAM 6G HD that I got for free.  It originally had 98SE and I wanted to be able to use it for web and email when on travel. I did alot of research and tried various live CDs and installed Vector.  While it installed and worked without problems, I didn't like the limited software and slow upgrading of what there was so back to the internet for more research.  I found this forum and here I am.

Xubuntu 7.10 installed without a hitch.  All my hardware was supported and I was up and running in no time.  XFCE is a bit of a resource hog with only 128M so installed Fluxbox. Back to the the forums to learn how to confiqure and set it up.  Didn't have wireless so found a cheap ($1) wireless card at the local swap meet, plugged it in and had wireless.  

Upgraded to Hardy. The only problem was the slow RT2500 wireless speed.  Researched the forums, found the solution and fix it.

I like Xubuntu so well I installed it on my desktop (dual boot with XP) and again installation went flawlessly.  I find that I use XP less and less.  

Xubuntu is a great distro and this forum makes it even better.  Keep up the great work.
