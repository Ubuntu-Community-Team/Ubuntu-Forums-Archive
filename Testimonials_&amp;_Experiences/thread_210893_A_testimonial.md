### Post by CheeseEatingBulldog on 2006-07-07
I have been using Ubuntu for just over a year now, and thought it may be fitting to praise a little.

From an old post I happend to stumble upon, it turns out I was one that was most hard to convert to Ubuntu. I worked in IT support for four years before quitting that rat race and going back to uni, where I now reside. Anyway, I was pretty handy at windows and the whole infrastructure, seeing as I was an IT runner techie for most of the time, having to solve every windows grievens you can think of in various setups. 

My home machine was to state the least a bit jury-rigged with a pirated windows version and the usual hacked bits for the updates. Aside from that I had an assortment of downloaded programmes to do the various small things that you do, be that photoshopping to gaming. 

Last year my machine kept on crashing and acting quite strangly after I had been given some faulty memory, this unknown to me at the moment, or better said moments of anger. So, in a foul attempt to smite windows after trying to reinstall from scratch (in all about 8 times and it crashing each time at different steps) I tried to install ubuntu, which didn't work either. Yes, the memory chip. This error I did eventually find,  but after doing so,it didn't change my mind and I installed ubuntu anyway to reap vengence on 8 failed windows installs, and have been running it ever since.

Now, I must add ubuntu was a jump in the dark for me, and one that I don't regret I had a Debian geek help me). Since then I have even successfully installed it on a vaio laptop which didnt have an internal cd/floppy drive. The install involved screwing the thing open and using an ide converter to install the drive using my desktop and putting it back in my laptop.(If you are interested I gt my infor from ([http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/sony.html]("http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/sony.html"))

Ubuntu got me to stop pirating software and start using opensource, and reinvented the way I look at computers. I am now starting to really enjoy linux and all the modding and ...well freeness about it. is:
I have some problems with setting up dualhead(please someone nick this for ubuntu![http://fedora.redhat.com/projects/config-tools/redhat-config-xfree86.html]("http://fedora.redhat.com/projects/config-tools/redhat-config-xfree86.html"))
and have had some issues with figuring out how to share folders between wireless laptop and desktop, but no rush. (Shame about the lack of gaming though, lucky I have a playstation)

I would never go back to windows now, I mean c'mon, one desktop? Besides, using free software to do =almost= everything other people pay for is a novelty by itself. 

Thanks for the great product, to everyone who participates. I am sure it will only get better in bounds from here on in :)
