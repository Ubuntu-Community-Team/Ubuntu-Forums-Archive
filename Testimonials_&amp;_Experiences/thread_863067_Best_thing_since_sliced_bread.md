### Post by flogger on 2008-07-18
Just wanted to say hello, Love my new unbuntu/linux OS, and relate my conversion...

Until last week I was a Power XP user. I could find the most obscure settings, had a flash drive with 100s of useful free programs, set up Windows Explorer with tabs, knew a few DOS commands, set up computers for other people etc...

In 2006 after destroying an XP installation I decided to I try Kubuntu before reinstalling windows. It is fair to say I did this without really understanding linux, kernels, distros and desktop environments, other than it was free and not Microsoft. 

I installed and booted up and waited.... and waited ... and waited. After a full 7 min (this happened every time) I finally got to play on a PC without networking or proper screen res. Then I got turned off by KDE which seemed more cluttered than XP.

Fast forward through many frustrations with Vista and a downgrade back to XP I decided to give Ubunutu another shot. This time I did a lot of reading beforehand so I was mentally prepared.

At first had a few problems with my broadcom wireless chip, which just about caused me to say "nothing changed" and go back to XP, but I powered through and found the solution.

Then I got to play. First installed missing/restricted software which was pain free, then KDE and Xfce. Followed by some other nifty programs.

So after 1 week I love it and here is why...
- Free
- Not MS
- Live CD so I could 'try before you buy'
- Choice of different desktops
- Repositories (what a great idea) with cool software
- No constant updating
- No AntiMalware programs chewing up RAM in the background
- Easy install
- Any problems made me learn faster the ins and outs
- Great forums with friendly people

My views on the two most common metaphysical questions posed on these forums...

KDE vs Gnome vs Xfce

Right now Gnome has won the battle on my laptop but the lighter Xfce will probably win the war. I like fast, simple, lightweight and don't need fancy cubes or hundreds of settings to tweak.

Is linux (read Ubuntu) ready for the big time?

It is already there. Anyone who is a power XP user can easily work through the issues. Technophobes can go to Dell or could probably buy a computer without Windows and have the store install Ubuntu.

Also the time wasted getting Ubuntu off the ground easily matched the time it takes to update and install programs on a new XP installation (which you have to do one at a time). Plus it is a good way to stick you head under the hood and see what makes Linux tick.

So now I am off to learn how to make a backup image of the installation, spruce up the fonts, maybe add a little flair to the desktop, investigate some other distros and generally thumb my nose at XP/Vista.

Two thumbs up to the Ubuntu team and community.

Cheers
flogger
