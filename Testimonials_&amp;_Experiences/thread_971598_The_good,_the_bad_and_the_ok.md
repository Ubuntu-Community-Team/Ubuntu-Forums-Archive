### Post by meep_meep on 2008-11-05
Just thought that I'd come on here and talk about my experiences with ubuntu & variants and opensource(but mostly to vent my irritation).


A couple of years ago my parents were having problems with their pc again....reinstalled windows for like the 4th time so not cool.  So I install firefox and thunderbird and told them if they ever wanted to fix their computer again they werent allowed to use IE or outlook ever again.  Since then I havent had to reinstall win xp, bonus!

This year my dad wanted a new computer (our other one is like 6 years old) so he went out and brought a mac....following all the hype.  My parents dont like change and every1 said that OS X is really easy to use...but my mum wants to go back to win xp now lol.  Anyway I put all their music on, setup their mail and then copied across their documents...whenever i tried it would get stuck and wouldnt tell me why, so i had to restart finder and pull out the external harddrive ^^ grrr.  Just so happens that some picture files didnt have the right permissions and i had to work out which ones by process of elimination...this took me a while.  Before I did that I tried to change the permissions of the files in the terminal all to no avail.  This really annoyed me...but the good part of the story is that I got my dad to use openoffice.org 3.0!!  i downloads the RC2 and installed it on bro's macbook and he was so impressed when he went to buy the imac he didnt get office 08.  i also got my sis using adium instead of msn messenger!

anyway i started using linux last year.  I installed ubuntu it was great but took a bit of learning.  It keeps getting better apart from my wifi....which is supposed to be supported straight out the box which it never is.  rt2500 pcmia where i have to compile the driver and everything every time and then edit /etc/networks/interfaces file to get it to connect to anything.  thats not too bad now cos i can install my wifi on a new install in five mins but if I was new to linux that would be a pain.  The rest of my experience has been really good.  I've used ubuntu, linux mint and xubuntu which all have be awesome and quite stable...then i used KDE 4...


I've been reading about KDE 4 development since like september 07 and it looks amazing.  so i could wait to try it out and i tried kde 4.0.1 or sumthing like that on live CD but i knew that it wasnt quite there yet from reading reviews so i left it.  Then i tried openSUSE 10.3 with a build of KDE 4.1 where I couldnt get my wifi to work at all which sucked even tho I had installed the driver correctly.

So i waited for an official release of KDE 4.1 and that was Kubuntu 8.10.  I installed it and it looked nice.  I set it up how i like it but then the problems started.  Random freezes mid use then occasionally while booting it would go to a screen full of yellow and blue rectangles.  Now it does that all the time or it would go to a black screen with just a mouse.  Also whenever i had to shut it down it would go to a black screen and stay there until i pressed the power button again.  I really like KDE and the apps and it has potential but it just doesn't quite work yet and needs to be more stable.  I dont know if it was my graphics card because i know that it can be really anal at times, its a ati radeon mobility 9700 128mb.

so anyway thats thats I dont expect anyone to have got this far...rant over *deep breaths*
