### Post by FraggedLocust on 2009-04-01
I've been using Ubuntu exclusively now for about two months, and I'm pleased to say that I've been able to find everything I needed to drop Windows from the laptop. Open source programs are definitely exquisitely built for the amount of money put into them (usually almost none) compared to some commercial products. 

I will still be running Windows virtually for school purposes, but open source solutions have taken my heart. I love the freedom and choice and customization available to me. At the moment I'm fiddling with my emesene python scripts to clean up the grammar and output to notifications.

I had started my Linux adventure sometime after April 2008 with Linux.org and found Debian. It was hard for me to understand as I didn't pay enough attention to how the system worked and messed it up countless times. I then moved to Fedora 10 and had problems with my wireless drivers. That distro was fairly short-lived as I learned about compiz and moved onto my first Ubuntu distro, 8.04.

This was exactly what I was looking for in Linux as I hadn't needed to mess around with any of the configurations. Though I do tend to, I like to start with a working system. I dropped Linux for a while and returned in February when I saw my roommate using 8.04 and remembered how much I enjoyed Ubuntu.

Since then, I've installed 8.10 and have now moved onto Jaunty beta. 

I'm eagerly awaiting the final release and seeing what else this amazing community has to offer.
