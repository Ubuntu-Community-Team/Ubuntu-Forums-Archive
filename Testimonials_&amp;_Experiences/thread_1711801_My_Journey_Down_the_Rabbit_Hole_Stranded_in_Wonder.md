### Post by solution123 on 2011-03-21
Hello everyone, long time listener, first time caller—yada yada yada.  My adventures in Linux began last summer when I decided to give Ubuntu a try.  I had been a break/fix tech for 7 years, but I mostly dealt with windows clients with the occasional Red Hat install.  So, in the interest of learning new things I setup Ubuntu 10.04 along side my Vista install (hey, it came with the machine, ok? lol) and things were jammin'.  Within 4 days I learned to do everything I use Vista for on Ubuntu.  I even set up Steam and all my games for it in Ubuntu via WINE.  It was fun—exploring new frontiers and what not, except there was one minor detail that it turned out to be major for me.  I had my Office 2k7 set up in Ubuntu through WINE, but there were issues whenever I would bring up documents like my resume for example; I could never get it to come up correctly.  All the layouts were screwed up and fonts would not display correctly.  I was looking for work at the time (still am actually :[  ) this was very important to me. I scoured the interwebs for a solution but couldn't come across one.  So I had no choice but to pull on my rope and be whisked away back to the world of M$ until I could find a fix.

     Seven months later, a buddy of mine brings up the subject of Blackbuntu.  He tells me all about it and I figure “Why not? Sounds like fun.”  I go to install Blackbuntu and I select “Install alongside other operating systems.”  The next window presents me with a cool little slider graphic and shows how much of my Linux partition I want to give to Blackbuntu.  Along the bottom of the menu I see a option that reads “Use entire partition”  I decided to use it all since I hadn't used 10.04 in a while, figured I'd start fresh.  When my PC rebooted, something terrible happened.  The Blackbuntu install not only wiped my 10.04 partition, but EVERY partition on my drive including my Vista recovery image partition (I can hear some of you cheering lol) and decided to make my entire HDD its new home.  I had a backup of all my critical documents, which wasn't much, so I was okay in that respect.  But now I can't go back to Windows.  I called my PCs manufacturer and they told me since my PC was no longer under manufacture warranty they wanted to charge me some obscene about of money to ship me out a new recovery DVD.

My rope has broken: my lifeline is no more.

     A great comedian once said: “If you're ever lost in the woods, f!ck it—build a house.”  Stellar advice.  So, here I am, stranded in Wonderland, armed with a copy of Ubuntu: Unleashed and a ubuntuforums account; building my new home.  This is my story, I wanted to share. I'm excited about all the new stuff I'm about to learn and I apologize in advance for any extremely noobish questions that I may post.

### Post by marl30 on 2011-03-21
Sorry to hear about your accident. If you choose the option that says to use the entire disk, you're telling Ubuntu to format your hard drive and be your only OS. Going with the side by side option would have preserved Windows.

You did mention that you were able to do everything in Ubuntu that you were able to do in Windows, so you'll manage just fine.

As for your issue with Ms Office 2007 in Ubuntu. I have had Office 2007 installed for the one year that I have been using Ubuntu, and I have never had the issue you had. I even used it to finish a school project which required me to create a graphical brochure and a bulletin. And everything displayed just as it would in Windows.

I suspect you didn't install the Microsoft core fonts for Wine, nor did you enable Wine's font smoothing.

To do this you'll need to install them from Winetricks. In addition you'll need to install Tahoma font too.

### Post by cipherboy_loc on 2011-03-21
1) Stop using the hard drive. The worst thing you can do is to keep on using it.
2) Boot from a LiveCD and run the boot info script in my signature.
3) Start (from a LiveCD and/or another computer) reading up on testdisk. If you really nuked your hard drive, this should recover it.
