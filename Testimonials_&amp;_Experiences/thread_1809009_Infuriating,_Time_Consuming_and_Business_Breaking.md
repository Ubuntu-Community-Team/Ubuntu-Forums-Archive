If you need them that reliable, not unlike a production machine, why are you using 11.04??? You should be using an LTS release. They are designed for stability on production machines and servers. Everything in between is a little more 'experimental' and what works winds up in the next, stable LTS.

Get an LTS up, tweaked and stable then screw around with whatever other releases you like, would be my advice. ;)

### Post by zero244 on 2011-07-21
Sorry your having problems.
I realize this wont help you now.

1. Using a long release is recommended for critical machines.

2. After I install a version of Ubuntu, I will do one or two updates. If everything is working right I stop doing virtually all updates. People are going to gasp but I stop doing kernel updates once my machine is working good.
It appears to me that once you have the peripheral drivers installed any kernel updates could break some of those devices.

3. On a business machine, once it is setup and running like you want be careful about over tweaking it or trying something that you are not sure of the outcome.

Ive been using Ubuntu since 2007 and I have found that Ubuntu will run very reliably day in and day out for as long as you don't have a hardware malfunction.

### Post by snowpine on 2011-07-27
I think you've overlooked a couple of important points, KafkaIan (unless I in turn have overlooked your reply):

1. If your problems were caused by a kernel update as your original post implied, you can simply choose the old kernel from your GRUB boot menu. Did you try this?

2. In the future, consider *testing* updates on a non-critical machine before you apply them to your production machines. 

A final thought, Ubuntu is but one of many Linux distributions. It is a very fast-moving distro, based on Debian's "unstable/testing" branches, designed to showcase the latest innovations, often to the detriment of its stability. I'd encourage you to try some stability-oriented distros (Debian, Slackware, Red Hat, Scientific Linux, etc.) before you go back to Windows 7 as one poster suggested. ;)
