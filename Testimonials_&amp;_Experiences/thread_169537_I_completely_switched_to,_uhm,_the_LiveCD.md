### Post by towsonu2003 on 2006-05-02
Ok, so I got curious and wanted to try Dapper. Downloaded the LiveCD, and booted to it. Everything is nice (a few bugs, I reported -and keep reporting), the themes is much nicer etc. 

I am now booting to the LiveCD for the last 2 days, and booted to Breezy only once for a couple of minutes to make some changes in my thesis (stability, just in case). 

I have a fat32 partition that I mount and move some of my configuration files here and there (.liferea, .gnome2/deskbar-applet, .conkyrc, xorg.conf -[conky bug]("https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+source/conky/+bug/42467")-, .mozilla, /var/cache/apt/archive/*, desktoppicture.jpg, wvdial.conf -[wvdial bug]("https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+source/wvdial/+bug/41679")-) and everything is going nicely. It takes me about 10 minutes after boot to get everything to work like I want (export some of Breezy software configuration to the live CD), restart xorg (see [conky bug]("https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+source/conky/+bug/42467")) and another 10 minutes to connect (as usual, with my imbecile slmodem -thank god it's working!-). 

This is very nice. They did a good job!

PS. MOZ_DISABLE_PANGO=1 firefox --> this is very useful...

Edit: and... my wireless (broadcom) works after like 2 commands, without ndiswrapper! Special thanks to bcm43xx makers...

Edit: They really need to make the documentation about networking offline... It keeps referring to the wiki. See this: [https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+source/ubuntu-docs/+bug/42454](https://launchpad.net/distros/ubuntu/+source/ubuntu-docs/+bug/42454)
