### Post by jbysmith on 2009-01-12
> **stchman said:**
> I don't think 2.8GHz P4 and 512MB is considered high end.  I would upgrade the RAM to at least 2GB seeing RAM is so cheap these days.

Ditto that, I can order a Big Mac and get a side of fries or a couple gigs of memory, my choice.  No reason not to.

Depends on your needs too of course.  On a desktop that'll be running a full blown desktop environment, Compiz, browsers, Office, blah blah absolutely want to give it more memory just for the sake of performance. Gnome and KDE3 will run just fine with 512; not sure on KDE4 but I would suspect it'll want more to run comfortably.  But with more RAM you'll be running without touching swap, which is always a good thing for performance.

On a server, not so much.  I'm running one machine, purely console, with Apache, MySQL, Samba, and a mail transport, and it's fitting very comfortably under 200MB.  I've read of installations that fit on a single floppy and takes almost no memory but thats just masochistic heh.
