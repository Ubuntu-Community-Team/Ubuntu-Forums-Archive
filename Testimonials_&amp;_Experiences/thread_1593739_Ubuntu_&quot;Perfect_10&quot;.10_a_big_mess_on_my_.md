### Post by Kupfer on 2010-10-11
First of all I want to say that I am a big fan of Ubuntu. Since I switched to Jaunty I never looked back. It introduced me to Linux and even after experimenting with other distros I always came home to Ubuntu mostly because of Canonicals decisions regarding usability. In the opinion of this average user "simplicity" isn't a swear word, it is the most essential factor (besides stabilty and innovation) in broadening the big tent of an operating system and winning over more and more average users. I applauded the creation of the Software Center, which will ensure Ubuntu's dominance for years to come, I enjoy the art of even reinstalling it given how obscenely easy it has become and love the fact that most of my annoyances come from little bugs instead of the whole roof falling on my head as with Windows.

That said, for me, Ubuntu 10.10 is a big mess and and even bigger dissapointment. Where do I start? With my netbook - an Asus Eee Pc 1005PE and Ubuntu Netbook Edition 10.10 with the new Unity interface. If I may kindly direct your attention to this bugreport I filed:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/658380](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/unity/+bug/658380)

Here you can read all of my many problems with UNE 10.10, none of which are minor, in fact they are pretty big problems for me.

My bug is a duplicate of the following, where you find other people suffering from the pretty severe problems in UNE 10.10 and Unity:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/mutter/+bug/657976](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/mutter/+bug/657976)

Let's leave my Asus netbook and move over to my Dell Latitude D620 laptop and Ubuntu 10.10 desktop edition. These are relatively small issues (compared to my experiences with UNE) but unfortionately they are big annoyances:

Installation: Good, except it takes much much (for me it feels three times) longer than before. It has something to do with packages it downloading from the internet. Of course, I don't have to click "download updates" while installing, but most average users would, as it sounds like something you would obviously do.

I know it downloads language packs as it says so, but also some other packages. What packages are these? Are these still language packs? If yes, why doesn't it say so? Or, what other packages are these, what kinds of updates? What kind of updates does the installer download for approx. 4 minutes on the day of the final release, when the image should be mostly up to date?

The installer doesn't inform the user about what it is doing for so long - it should communicate every move in a simple, understandable way, which would logically prevent user frustration - especially during an installation this long.

Login sound:  This is a small thing and maybe I'm misunderstanding how this works, but when I'm trying to turn off the drum sound at the login window in System -> Administration -> Login Screen, the Play Login Sound option is not even ticked by default. Even though I hear the drum sounds at login. I have to tick the Play Login Sound box and then untick it again to disable the sound - which is strange. Little details can be make or break for new users.
Again, this is a small thing bothering me, maybe it was like that in Lucid too I just didn't notice it, because everything else was fine, but it does bother me.

Software Center: The most frustrating aspect for me. It takes long (not that bad, but not satisfying either) to start on my laptop but definitely too long to load on my netbook. Even on UNE: VERY long.
Installing deb files is the really painful affair, as it takes very long for the SC to load - rendering quick usage of the OS impossible. Really, you have to wait long seconds for SC to load a deb.
This may just sound like me complaining, but really, it is like pushing the brakes when trying to work fast. Everything comes to a screeching halt.
Starting an installation itself from within the SC seems slower too, but I may be wrong on that one. But SC definitely feels slower for me in every way.

Also, following smaller issues: If you select a software from the SC you do it with an orange colored bar and the sofware name and information is white, but after installation the bar turns grey/whiteish and you can barely see the letters. No big issue, just aesthetic.

Sometimes, when I click on an application it says it's installed, even though it isn't and shows "remove" - if I click on it again, it shows correctly "install"

Sometimes the fonts of the softwares in the list change and look out of place. I don't know what's going on but it looks like something might be broken and might make a bad impression on new users.

I know, these are relatively small things but in effect they give the impression of an unfinished and unprofessional product. Lucid felt finished, professional and even exceptional, therefore I will revert to using it on my laptop as a choice and I will revert to using it on my netbook, because I have no choice. See the UNE bugreports for the very severe problems of Unity.

In light of all this I have to say, that sadly, the slogans on ubuntu.com "The perfect 10 is here" and "better than ever" seem like to me.
