### Post by ephraim on 2007-01-12
Greetings,

I've mostly been a Windows user, but have been experimenting with, first, Fedora Core, and now Ubuntu.

I got a laptop last year (emachines, AMD turion 64, broadcom wireless card and ATIIXP sound card) and last week I decided to install (dual boot on new partition) Ubuntu 6.06 with the intention that if it worked out and I liked it, I would keep it on the laptop.

I am very new with Linux. I've learned a few of the commands in bash, learned to navigate a bit in gnome, etc, but for the most part I get lost when I comb through how-tos.

Everything ran fine EXCEPT for three issues...3d graphics, wireless card (broadcom), and sound card (ati ixp).

The first issue was resolved rather easily. I have a nvidia card and found some simple instructions in Ubuntu Unleashed just for that card, which worked perfectly.

The wireless was a bigger problem. I have the troublesome Broadcom card. I went through several attempts based on various how-to's, until I finally implemented ndiswrapper which uses the driver from Windows...and miracuously I got it to work.

The biggest headache of all was getting sound. Ironically, it ended up being a simple fix, thanks to the following thread and the two fellas there...

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=244382&highlight=atiixp]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=244382&highlight=atiixp")

But, prior to this, I went through all the steps in the comprehensive guide to sound problems found elsewhere on this forum. This guide was an extremely well written how to, but unfortunately I didn't need to go through all that. The next day I found the above link in the forum, and all along it was a simple procedure of adjusting the settings on the sound icon in the system tray!

I felt so good when I heard that first sound coming from my laptop.

So, I have succeeded in getting a fully functional Ubuntu installation on my laptop, and Ihave to say that it's more than I could've expected from a non-Windows OS. In fact, I like it better than Windows.

I can now consider myself to be a member of the Linux user community, and I feel that I'm here to say.

I would just like to conclude by expressing my gratitude to all the Linux and open source developers. I owe a great deal to you all, even at the early stage I am in, but I feel bad that I got all of this without paying for it. You guys and gals deserve alot for all that you do. Since you don't require money for your labors, I'll send along my gratitude. Not much, but it's something.:) 

Later,
Eph
