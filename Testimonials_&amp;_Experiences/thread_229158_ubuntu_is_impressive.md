### Post by kake26 on 2006-08-04
Hi all,

My first post here. So let me give you all a bit of background on me before I tell you why I will stick with Ubuntu. I have used Linux for years, yes my faith in windows permanetly vanished a long time ago. I started with Mandrake 6.5 Delux I think it was. Not bad for what it was at the time and not perfect. I stuck with Mandrake till about version 8.0, having seen much improvement during the time I realized Linux as a whole will evolve far faster than dare I say Microsoft. I then went to Slackware for several years after that dicovering FreeBSD. However, to make a long story short I got around the distro block trying everything from Fedora to Gentoo. Ending up with SuSe, which I stuck with because it was good. From 9.3 Pro to 10.1 which is still on my fileserver btw, I used it. It should be noted my years of using slackware where a huge help using and seeing the real difference in allot of distros. This was in my quest for the perfect desktop distro one that would allow me to lazy and still totally customizable. SuSe fit the bill except the packages where never updated. Once again the quest for the perfect distro went on. I have been monitering distrowatch.com like usual and noticed, you guessed it ubuntu. My business partner had switch over a while ago, yes I run a business off nothing but Linux. Have for over 2 years now. He raved about it so I said hell, lets try it. I burned and booted the live cd and messed around with it. Within 30 sec I knew I found what I have been looking for. Despite my deep seated fear and dislike of Debian and GNOME, I liked it. I've customized my Ubuntu with a bit of effort since I'm a bit of a tweak, but I love it. I may never need to use another distro again. This very well maybe the lasting answer to my quest for the perfect distro. I am impressed, and comming from me thats, well high praise. Finally, the Linux distro has evolved into what it should have been and I see why Ubuntu has exploded in popularity. Well, I'm looking for words and not finding them so I will leave it at that. Ubuntu might as well be the next FireFox, if you all get my drift on that.
