### Post by MrHigh on 2010-12-24
Hi everybody,

I installed wibu two days ago, I just wanted to give Ubuntu a try, I was really sick of windows with all the errors, and the big amount of resources needed. 

Well, when I first installed it, I was kinda confused, since it was the first time and I didn't know where everything's placed. Fortunately I had someone helping me out, and show me the most basic stuff about the consola, the commands and so. I'm amazed, honestly, Ubuntu is way faster with everything, I could say atleast x3 times faster. I notcied this while I was playing a game (Minecraft), when I saw the FPS. On windows, I run Minecraft with about 30-50 FPS, and on Ubuntu they raise up to 200-300FPS, that's amazing.

Now that I'm getting kinda used to it, I have to say it is really easy to use, once you get kinda used to it you start to find out how things work. Another great thing is Wine, since I can install some software I use everyday such as Fruity Loops. I can enjoy of free, fast and amazing OS, and at the same time keep on producing and recording.

I already unistalled Windows from my notebook, and I won't even go back to that, oh no, Ubuntu is now my best friend.:p

I will be reading this forum, I need to learn more about it; the commands, the packets wich I do not understand yet, and some other good stuff.

[http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/)
And for dual booting with Windows, plus a lot of other great stuff:
[http://members.iinet.net/~herman546/](http://members.iinet.net/~herman546/)
There are a lot of beginner friendly linux tutorials on How to Forge: [http://howtoforge.com/](http://howtoforge.com/)
Also, the Ubuntu forum's founder Ubuntu Geek post lots of great tutorials on his site:
[http://www.ubuntugeek.com/](http://www.ubuntugeek.com/)
As for learning linux commands in the terminal, here is what I used to get started with that:
[http://linuxcommand.org/learning_the_shell.php](http://linuxcommand.org/learning_the_shell.php)
Learning how to administer a linux system from the terminal is the foundation for using linux. 
(Keep in mind that you don't need to learn all this stuff in one day. It will all come together in time and as you gain experience.)
