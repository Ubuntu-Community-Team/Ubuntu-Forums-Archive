### Post by Polish Rifle on 2007-09-11
I really really wanted Linux to work out for me.  For years I've heard about how awesome Linux operating systems are and I love what Linux stands for with freeware and the strong community.  But over the last 5 days I have spent countless hours trying to get a right fit for my computers and I have to go back to XP.

I have a 1.2 Ghz 128 MB RAM laptop that I was sure I could bring back to life with Linux.  I tried burning an Ubuntu disk but it wouldn't install.  I tried this for a week.  So then I sent away for install disks, and they wouldn't install either.  I finally burned the alternate-text iso and got Ubuntu installed.  Unfortunately, it was very very slow.

I uninstalled Ubuntu and tried Xubuntu.  Everything was fine until I loaded up Firefox.  It killed my RAM.  So then I got Epiphany, still took up too much res.  Xubuntu didn't find the drivers for my wireless card, I couldn't tell how much juice was in my battery, I couldn't uninstall certain programs.  I tried to install Damn Small Linux but Xubuntu wouldn't let me boot from the CD anymore.  I had enough.

Before this week I had Windows XP on my laptop and it was stripped down to the bare bones just to browse and word process.  It worked great, but I heard I could go faster with Linux.

I know I only gave Linux a month or so.  But when opening a web page takes 30 seconds, or I have to go to a command line to install something, Windows just becomes more convienent.  I know I'm retreating to what I feel is comfortable, but I don't remember the learning curve with Windows being this difficult.  I love to learn, but maybe I just expected too much from Linux for my crappy laptop.  

I love these forums.  My questions were answered very quickly and I even got on instant messanger with a couple people to help me out.  I'll still support Linux but I guess it's just a personal preferance and I'll be returning to Windows.

Now I have to get on the boards and figure out how to get XP re-installed since Xubuntu won't let me boot from CD anymore.  


Thanks Linux community
