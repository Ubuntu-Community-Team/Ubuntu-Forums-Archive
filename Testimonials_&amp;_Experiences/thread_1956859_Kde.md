### Post by Gregory Lee on 2012-04-11
This is just a little advertisement for the KDE desktop.  I hadn't used it for some years, but in Ubuntu 12.04, when the Unity shells and the Gnome shells started crashing on me, in desperation I tried KDE.  It's working well.  If any of you find yourselves in a similar situation, or just feel like experimenting, you might want to try it.

I got it through the Software Center when I downloaded the "Gnome Desktop Environment" -- KDE was listed as an Add-on, and I checked it off.  (Why KDE should count as an Add-on to Gnome, I don't know, but that's where it was.)

I've been using KDE for about a week.  Here are some good points:

[LIST=1]
[*]KDE doesn't crash on me.
[*]I think it's functional and attractive.  In the last few days I've seen a lot of KDE-related updates come in, so some people are working hard on it.
[*]I can get the workspaces-on-a-cube eye candy to work on KDE, while I couldn't do that on Gnome (back in the day when Gnome didn't crash).
[/LIST]
