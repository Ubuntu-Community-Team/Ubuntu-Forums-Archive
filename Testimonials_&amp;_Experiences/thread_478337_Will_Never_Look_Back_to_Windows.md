### Post by GMU_DodgyHodgy on 2007-06-19
I am a fairly average computer user.  I have a business (Accounting/Finance) and an IT background (Infor Systems/CS).  However, I mostly have used my home computer for average home user things: Internet (surfing, banking and investments), Email, Office Apps, home finance, photos and music.   With Windows XP I was always re-installing my OS every 9-12 months because of a registry error or some other bug that kept me from updating my Windows image or prevented me from running some application.  I never felt like I had control of my system or could rely on it to maintain all my data, documents, and files.  

I have tried Linux in the past and was turned off by the difficulty in getting internet access or wireless to work.  However, in my frustration with Windows - I gave Ubuntu a try because of all the press on its ease of use.

Well that was it. Ubuntu installed flawlessly, found my wireless network, and got me system updates in 40 minutes.  

I know have all my photos, music, documents, and files migrated to Ubuntu.  I even had Windows XP run in VM to run MS Money.  Now I have migrated my Money data files to GnuCash.  

I started with Edgy Eft and am now on Feisty Fawn and could not be happier.
