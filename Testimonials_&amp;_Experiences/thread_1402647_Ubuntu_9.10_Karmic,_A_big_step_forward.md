### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-02-10
This has been discussed many times, but here it goes...

Ubuntu, just like any Linux distro, has (unfortunately) very little testing support during development.  This results in some rough edges when it is released, and the first 2-3 months are a bit of a bumpy ride.  This is specially true for a release as ambitious as Karmic.

Having said so, I very much doubt that people would find that many issues if they installed Ubuntu 9.10 now (Feb 2010).  Karmic is very much a strong and solid release only 3 months after it went live, and that is something that Windows can only dream about.  Windows 7, for example, which was released around the same time as Karmic, with incredibly larger budget and resources, is still showing issues (lots of complaints are being raised now about it not managing laptops battery life correctly).  In fact, Windows releases historically become solid after Service Pack releases, and required constant security patches (Internet Explorer 8 security HOLE anyone?)

To be honest, all of those people who are sourly complaining about Karmic should put things into perspective and do 1 of 2 things: Either they wait until new releases are tested and solidified by others, or they simply shut up and help in testing.

I think the community can do just as well without people who don't help and only make noise.

And to the OP, glad you bring a little positivity, thanks a lot.
