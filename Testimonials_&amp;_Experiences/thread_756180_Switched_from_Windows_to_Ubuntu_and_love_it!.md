### Post by mike21091 on 2008-04-15
Before I start, let me introduce myself. My name is Michael, I am 17 years old, and have been using computers since I was 10. My first computer was a Tandy with MS-DOS 3 and two 5.25" floppy drives that my grandparents gave me.

Well, I have switched over to Ubuntu from using Windows. I have dabbled in Linux, BSD, and some other alternative operating systems (ReactOS to name one) and there were three main things that kept me from switching to Linux.

The first was that I had virtually no knowledge of how to maintain, install, or do anything in Linux. Second was that I was used to Windows and fell into the 'It's too hard to switch' mentality. Third, I wouldn't be able to play my games.

Well about 6 months later I decided to try a few live cd's to get a feel for Linux and was surprised to realize that it wasn't as hard as I thought. (Out of about 4 or 5 distributions, Ubuntu was the one I liked) 

I spent the next year just messing around with Linux on various computers I had and reading various articles related to Linux.

One week ago I was ready to install Ubuntu on my desktop and hopefully rid myself of Windows.

I am happy to say that so far I can't see myself going back!

My games work great under Wine, I am confident using the Terminal (Reminds me of the days of DOS 3), and I'm able to use all my hardware without any problems!

I'm glad to be using Ubuntu!

Here is my setup.

Ubuntu 7.10
AMD Athlon X2 5200+ (2.6GHz)
2 GB DDR2 533MHz
320GB SATA Hard Drive
DVD+/-RW with Lightscribe (SATA)
NVIDIA GeForce 8400 GS 512MB Video Card

Games I play.
Half-Life (Source and Non-Source)
Half-Life 2 (Including EP1 and EP2)
Counter Strike Source
Opposing Force & Blue Shift

So far I am getting better performance under WIne that I ever did in Windows!
Averaging 80FPS in HL2 and over 120FPS in HL.
