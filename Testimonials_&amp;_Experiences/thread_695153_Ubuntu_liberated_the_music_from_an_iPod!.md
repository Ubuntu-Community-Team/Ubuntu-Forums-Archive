### Post by rockin_goliath on 2008-02-12
One of my dorm mates just got a new laptop (an Apple Macbook) and she was trying to get herself set up. She is not the most computer savvy person ever, and was on the verge of buying the trial version of MS Office that came on her system and reformatting her iPod because she couldn't get her music off. She came and asked me if I could help her out. After she explained her problems, I immediately installed Openoffice for free instead of buying Word, and set about to rescue her music collection from her iPod. I realized that you can't view hidden folders in Mac's Finder (:mad: ), so I brought the iPod to my room, plugged it into my Ubuntu desktop, and it popped right up in Rhythmbox. I just selected all her songs in the library view, dragged them into their own unhidden folder, and offloaded them back into iTunes on her laptop. Without Ubuntu, I wouldn't have known about Openoffice or have been able to rescue her music! Thanks Ubuntu!

PS. You can also drag music from shared libraries on a network into your own library with Rhythmbox ;)

### Post by mrsteveman1 on 2008-02-12
You can actually get to any file in OS X finder, theres a setting that shows hidden files, but normally it doesn't do it.

As for recovering the music, rhythmbox can do it, there are other programs too. Basically the files are just renamed, but the ID3 tags should still be intact, so if you use something to rename the files according to their tags you will have your files back.
