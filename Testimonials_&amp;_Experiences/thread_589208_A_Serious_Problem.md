### Post by MatthewPlanchard on 2007-10-24
Guys, I have a problem.

I first installed Ubuntu a couple of weeks ago. Since then I have learned a ton about linux, working in command line, open source philosophy, and now I am starting to work on bug and package management.

I have had a lot of problems, many self-inflicted, but each problem has taught me how to better interact with the system and how to make it even better in the process. Each problem also sent me deep into the archives of the thousands upon thousands of posts from users just like me detailing their problems and discovering how to fix them, with the help of other users just like them.

I have learned how to back up my system and keep my home directory on a seperate partition, so even though Ive had to reinstall three times since upgrading to gutsy due to excessive fiddling, I haven't lost any information.

Today I swapped the two gigs of RAM in my laptop with the 1 gig in my dad's laptop so he could run vista without constant lag.

I can run a music player, an audio converter (for a project converting FLAC's to .ogg's to seed online to spread open source :) ), a web browser, a file manager, a terminal, dual monitors, an automatic wallpaper switching app, an e-mail client, and an image editing program all at once, and yet I still cannot use more than 60% of available RAM, even after today's switch.

With nothing but a few clicks in the packet manager as soon as I get the hardware, I can have a complete music recording and editing platform on my portable computer, and the same goes for video.

So here is my problem. I just don't know how to thank you all enough for making such an incredible community for such an amazing piece of work. I really have never seen anything like it, and I can say honestly that the open source community has inspired me to try and spread the word about Linux, Ubuntu, Debian, and all things Open Source.

So I would like to say thanks for letting me finally drop Windows once and for all, and thanks for providing an alternative that is not a substitute but a true replacement, with ten times the potential and a million times the community.

I am proud to be a part of the Open Source community. :KS

Thanks, Ubuntu, and Rock On
