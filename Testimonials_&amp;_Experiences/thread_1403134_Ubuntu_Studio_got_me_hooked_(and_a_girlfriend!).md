### Post by Toadinator on 2010-02-09
Back in 2007, I became addicted to what is known as [YouTube Poop]("http://www.youtube.com/results?search_type=search_videos&search_query=youtube+poop&search_sort=relevance&search_category=0&page=") (sorry if these videos are extremely immature; I was only 13 at the time :P). So after a little while, I wanted to make my own, preferably using clips from Homestar Runner (a flash cartoon). My video editor that I used once or twice before, some generic "Ulead" thing, was extremely glitchy and didn't work. After giving up, I looked up the PS3 on Wikipedia and it showed a neat picture of it running an OS named "Ubuntu". I thought it seemed cool, since the only OSes I really knew of that were "professional-looking" at the time were Windows and Mac, and Ubuntu looked rather professional to me. I looked it up and it seemed great, and as I was looking it up I found Ubuntu Studio, and it seemed perfect for my needs!

After spending two days defragging and installing it, risking my Windows XP partition in the process, I finally got it working! After exploring around and getting everything to work correctly, I tried the extremely-crashy Kino video editor out and made my first video (not showing the link because it's terrible). I later switched over to regular Ubuntu and got used to the terminal. Then I found out all about the Free Software/Culture movement, and I became an advocate of it :D! Ubuntu is wonderful, and while there are a couple weird issues here and there, it's nothing I haven't seen before on other OS's (especially windows ;)). Thanks everyone for being such a wonderful help over the years!

...Related, that video that I made introduced me to my current girlfriend! She liked it on YouTube and we started talking on Jabber (via pidgin :D). Then I went out with her friend for a while over the Internet. I broke up with her later, and I found out that she never really liked me. That's when I found out that the girl that liked my video had a crush on me for about a half of a year (almost all of the time I was dating her friend), and I started dating her. I showed her Ubuntu (which she now uses :D), we're very very similar, and we're the same religion too (Roman Catholic)! If Ubuntu can get me a girlfriend, then that's a perfect reason to never switch away from it again.

Thanks to everyone that helped make Ubuntu as great as it is :D!

PS: I don't use Kino anymore; Kdenlive is a MUCH better video editor, and I've been using it for a little while now.
