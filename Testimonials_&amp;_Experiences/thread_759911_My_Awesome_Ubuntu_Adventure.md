### Post by kestal on 2008-04-19
I will admit it, im brand new to Linux. (Especially Ubuntu). In a span of 3 weeks, I accomplished a lot. I tried Mandriva 2007, 2008.0, 2008.1. I tried Gentoo. I tried Ubuntu 7.10 Gutsy. Finally, I am on Ubuntu 8.04 RC, and man am I ever impressed.This is the first time I have tried to use ubuntu (or any linux distro) without trying to forcefully crash it or have way too much fun with it.

**Things to Note:**
[**[COLOR="DeepSkyBlue"]X[/COLOR]**] I have experienced no lock ups, freezes, halts, or crashed applications as of yet.
[**[COLOR="DeepSkyBlue"]X[/COLOR]**] 8.04 RC loads up much quicker than Gutsy on my computer.
[**[COLOR="DeepSkyBlue"]X[/COLOR]**] Sound, Wireless Network, and Restricted Video Card Drivers worked 'out of the box' (no sweat, no pain)
[**[COLOR="DeepSkyBlue"]X[/COLOR]**] Opera 9.5 works incredibly well/fast in 8.04. I wonder why it was removed from repository? It works much better than firefox 2 or 3 [COLOR="Red"]([/COLOR][COLOR="Silver"]in my opinion[/COLOR][COLOR="Red"])[/COLOR]

**Effortless Installations/Addons:**
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Compiz Fusion
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Emerald Theme Manager
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Wine (w/ Photoshop CS2)
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Installed a package that had all the Windows fonts I have grown accustomed to.
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Automatic Logins with no keyring problems.
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Smooth shutdowns with no network errors or errored verbose screen. 
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Mac-like hover 'mouse-in-corner' to show desktop/hide desktop.
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Opera 9.50B1 (With Flash!) [COLOR="Sienna"]+++ This Works Incredibly Well! Fonts Look Great! Streaming Video is Smooth!! +++[/COLOR]
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] Custom Splash Screen ([link]("http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Usplash+BlackChrome?content=60249"))
[**[COLOR="YellowGreen"]X[/COLOR]**] No lag Login with custom background -- No default background image/color, just my desktop background.

**Somewhat Effortless:**
[**[COLOR="SandyBrown"]X[/COLOR]**] Sunbird 0.8 (Standalone, compiled from Mozilla).
[**[COLOR="SandyBrown"]X[/COLOR]**] Modded Firefox (its much quicker, modifying about:config) with Flash

**The Things that wouldn't work.**
[**[COLOR="Red"]X[/COLOR]**] Gcursor. There is a current bug with it, with 8.04... I hope they fix it soon. I was really looking forward to using it.

**My goofs:** (I fixed after)
[**[COLOR="DarkOrchid"]X[/COLOR]**] Used Ubuntu Tweak to accidently get rid of my logout sequence. ie) "Quit" command logged out, without options.
[**[COLOR="DarkOrchid"]X[/COLOR]**] While getting rid of Evolution, I accidentally removed gnome-panel (understandable that a mail client is connected to core files? *gasp*)
[**[COLOR="DarkOrchid"]X[/COLOR]**] While manually installing Sunbird 0.8 (standalone), I forgot to remove the one I installed from the repository (0.7).


---
**[COLOR="DarkRed"]The only reasons why I think that I'd use Windows right now:[/COLOR]**

**Reason 1:** Shaiya. I love my online games. This one is free and protected by some form of gameguard. I read around saying that I'd have to edit the executable to bypass it all together. im not sure if I am comfortable with doing that as of yet.

**Reason 2:** Photoshop CS2. Though yes, I have it working. Wine is brilliant and I love it. However, it does not feel quite the same. Perhaps with some tweaking, I might be fine with it.

**Reason 3:** Webcam Support. Though yes, aMSN provides webcam support, I still like my MSN messenger. Emesene comes pretty close to a smooth feel that I like (especially over aMSN), but it lacks webcam support.

[http://forums.aeriagames.com/viewtopic.php?p=384533](http://forums.aeriagames.com/viewtopic.php?p=384533)

[http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=9998](http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=version&iId=9998)

I then read a few other posts (can't find links at the moment) about creating a copy of the game exe file, and basically removing the whole gameguard as a whole. (the idea behind copying it and renaming it is so that you could basically download updates from the original client screen whenever needed - this was tested and it seemed to work for updates, which is good news).

Not saying that I would personally attempt this until its been tested by someone else, but it would make my dreams come true if I could play Shaiya on Linux. Hell, it doesnt even work on ME/2000, yet if it could work on Ubuntu that would be wonderful ;) It would be one step closer to finally ridding myself of any Windows OS ;)
