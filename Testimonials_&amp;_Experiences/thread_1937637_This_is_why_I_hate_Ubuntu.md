### Post by Zill on 2012-03-08
> **jan.petras said:**
> It released three versions over the span of 1 and a half years without fixing a simple yet vital issue...
What you have to realise is that FOSS, such as Ubuntu, is not a "product" sold by a mega-corporation.  It is actually a collaborative effort produced by many thousands of, generally unpaid, developers who freely offer their time to produce amazing software.

Because they are volunteers, developers are free to choose what they work on and may well concentrate their efforts on things that are important to *them*, rather than *you!*

Ranting on forums and demanding someone else works to resolve *your* problems demonstrates that you may be happier to simply purchase your OS from Microsoft or Apple and then use their paid-for support services.

If you wish to use FOSS then I suggest you try to actively participate in the community, rather than simply criticizing others.  A good start would be to raise bug reports for any problems you experience and then to assist resolution, such as by testing patches.
