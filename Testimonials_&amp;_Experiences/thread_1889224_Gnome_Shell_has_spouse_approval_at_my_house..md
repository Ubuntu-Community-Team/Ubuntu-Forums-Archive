### Post by Johnny Buffalkill on 2011-11-30
I actually like both Unity and Gnome shell, but I really like Gnome Shell.  The important thing is that my wife also likes it.  This is good because she sometimes gets frustrated when the computer looks completely different every time she uses it.  My wife is *not* a geek - she is a former cheerleader.   Here is a typical computer conversation between us:


**Wife:**  Wait . . . what's going on here, what is this?  Why is the computer doing this?

**Me:**  Oh, sorry.  That's KDE, its a desktop environment I'm checking out.

**Wife:**  WTF does that mean?

**Me:**  Never mind.  Here, I'll find Facebook for you.

**Wife:**  Why can't it be like it used to be?

**Me: ** You mean Windows?  Don't go on the internet with Windows.


Despite this, she got the hang of Gnome shell right away and even told me she likes it.  When I first started using it I thought it would be a really easy setup for a new user, and I think it will be a good move for targeting new linux users.  I understand people's frustration with losing Gnome 2, and I am a little sad to see it on its way out too, but there still plenty of DEs available, and of course there is always the terminal.
