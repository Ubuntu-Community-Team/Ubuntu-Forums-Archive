### Post by swiftsam on 2009-09-22
I just wanted to say thanks to Canonical and all the developers for making an OS that makes my job of supporting my parent's computer needs from 500 miles away so much easier.

I've used Ubuntu exclusively for 2 years and love it, but I'm a computer guy so it doesn't seem like a stringent test.

A year ago, I replaced my mom's ancient computer and decided to try an ubuntu install.  She always had problems with so many programs prompting her to do things she didn't want to do.  With Ubuntu, she has been using her computer 10 times as much because the environment is stable, fast, and free from software trying to tell her what to do.

My dad just called me last week saying that his computer was unusable because after windows started there was an endless stream of "your computer is infected" pop-ups.  I mailed him a 9.04 cd and was able to walk him through a dual-boot install over the phone in about 30 minutes.  He (and I, honestly) were amazed how easy it was.  The hardest part was getting him to find the [Del] key on his keyboard so that he could boot from the CD.  

These great experiences have convinced me that the perception that linux is for computer experts is actually the inverse of the truth.  Ubuntu makes it effortless to maintain a computer that works for you.  Windows requires so much more expertise to stay out of trouble and get the most out of your hardware.  Thanks to Ubuntu, both of my parents are happily back online again focusing on the important things: emailing, receiving the pictures I send them, and playing fantasy football.

Thanks Ubuntu!
