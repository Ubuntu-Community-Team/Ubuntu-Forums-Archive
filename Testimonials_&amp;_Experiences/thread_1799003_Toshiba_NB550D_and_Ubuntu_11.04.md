### Post by toumpas on 2011-07-07
Two days i bought myself this mini laptop, the Toshiba NB550D netbook with 1Gb Ram and the "multimedia" dual-core APU from AMD running at 1Ghz. The little computer also features an integrated ATI video chipset for 3D graphics.

The netbook comes with Windows 7 Starter edition and loads of crap toshiba software to hinder furthermore the small machine.

After a couple of hours of usage i figured out that enough is enough and is time of some innovation: good ol' Ubuntu.

So i download the new version the 11.04 with the updated desktop and voila! The machine runs so much better already!

The 11.04 graphical shell comes bounts and leaps since my last venture in the Linux domain some odd 2 years ago with SuSe.

Now i can right click a file and choose "open with" on a file to play it with a different player or open its properties and set it to open with any player i like, like i would do in windows so many years now.

The Ubuntu 11.04 feels quite nice on the netbook and the resolution is just perfect for anything, be it browsing or open a console, the keyboard is layed out nicelly for such a miniature of a computer.

Edit: A day after and I bought a 2GB 1333Hz DDR3 Ram, and there is a significant switch in speed on both Linux and Windows. 

The "rig" runs now like a REAL computer should work. There is no slowing down when opening Firefox or a player or file-browser. I really love this little toy.

Highly recomment this netbook with a 2GB Ram!!!

### Post by m0n4g3 on 2011-07-14
I bought this netbook about 2-3 weeks ago now. It has been running beautifully since then on any flavour of ubuntu i've put on there (xubuntu, ubuntu both 11.04).

The only real issues i've had with it is the bluetooth not working on ubuntu, but works on xubuntu.

I've stuck with ubuntu because i like unity, but really wish i could figure out why the bluetooth is not working.

Apart from that, proprietary drivers work and highly recommend them over the latest opensource gallium drivers. Performance is double and it allows proper frequency scaling of the gpu with the proprietary drivers.

Battery life went from 4-5 hours to 7-8 hours.

Most of the hotkeys work, bar the volume ones. Suspend works and so does hibernate for me.

Hope that helps
