### Post by allegedlyyours on 2010-06-11
I wanted to express my giddy joy at my very first experience with Ubuntu (or any other OS save Windows, for that matter.)

Short story:

A few years back my husband and I bought a brand spankin' new PC with WinXP Pro on it. It was my favorite machine ever, I loved it so much. And then, about 6 months ago, I got a wicked virus. AVG didn't catch it and it spread through my whole system pretty quickly. I'm not a genius, but I've never found a virus I couldn't beat, and this one totally kicked my butt. I ended up wiping most of my drive to do a clean install of XP, and all of a sudden couldn't find my partition that held my backup. 

We tried everything we could think of. Tried three different versions of XP until we found one that sort of worked. It didn't have any of the correct drivers, though, and so I spent days trying to find them for all my extras. I ended up finding my original OS cds, and popped them in, installed, and ended up in a forever-long boot cycle. 

At this point I'm sure you can understand my frustration with Windows. I decided to try Ubuntu because, well, I couldn't really break the thing any worse. 

I downloaded it, threw it on a flash drive, popped it into my PC and...it just worked. That's it. I wanted to tether my HTC Hero and I just plugged it in and hit "share internet". Amazing! Took me an hour to find the right drivers for Vista the other day. 

Everything is so easy to find. The UI is intuitive and simple and clean. It's a beautiful thing. Even the few issues I've run across have been really easy fixes just by searching around in these forums. 

I just wanted to say THANK YOU to all the folks that gave up their free time and parts of their sanity (i'm sure) working on this project. It revived a 1200 dollar paperweight, and all of you have my eternal gratitude.
