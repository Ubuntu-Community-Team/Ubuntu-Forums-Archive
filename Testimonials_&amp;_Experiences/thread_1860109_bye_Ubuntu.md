### Post by elimarcus on 2011-10-14
I love Ubuntu, and have been using it on my laptop at home at least since version 6.04, and I admit I have had my ups and downs with the changes over the years. I was pretty happy with 11.04, and today I just upgraded to 11.10. 

I must say that I am disappointed in the apparent philosophical turn in the design - I don't want my desktop to be a bloody browser interface! I don't need my laptop to start resembling a stupid iPhone!
I would like to be able to access all the inner settings that were open to me in previous versions. Why is it all hidden away? 
How do I access a terminal? 
How do I change the general interface configurations? 
If the installation failed to mount certain packages, how do I repair it?
I'm no programmer, but I have plenty of experience getting around Windows nonsense and  configuring it as I like, but I would like to also be able to do the same here in Ubuntu.
