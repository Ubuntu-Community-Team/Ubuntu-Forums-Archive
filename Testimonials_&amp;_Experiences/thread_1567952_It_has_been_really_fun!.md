### Post by usagiakumu on 2010-09-04
I have always been a bit of a nerd. Being newly single, and with the yearly reinstall of Windows, I figured the "family computer", now being my computer, I would borrow an Ubuntu CD from work, and try it out for home use. After two months, here are a few thoughts, praises, and annoyances that just got under my skin.

1. Thoughts:
Remove the included games and include a photo editing program on the install disk, I really appreciate Gimp.
The default color scheme and button placement is a little unsettling, but a quick theme change fixes it. I am not sure if I like the default theme and button placement or not.
The desktop effects are incompatible with a few things like Wine and Flash, so I never use them. Might I suggest simply removing them off the install CD to save space for other more useful apps?

2. Praises:
I really love Gnome. Not so much KDE, as it has too many issues. I wish I could take Gnome with me to Windows. I really get along well with Gnome and the GTK application set. Free or not, Gnome and GTK applications are in a league of their own. Thank you for using Gnome and not KDE!
Network stability is WAY better than it is in Windows.

3. Annoyances:
When I open an MP3 file, it opens Movie Player even if Rhythmbox is set to default. I am unsure how to change this, but it really peeves me off.
When playing music or movies, the sound jumps and stutters when doing ANYTHING else. I have read around that some have this issue and others don't, but it is due to a 30 year old bug from what I gathered. You would think someone would have fixed this by now.
Not including closed source drivers. This caused a real headache for me. I was unable to boot normally because of an issue with NVIDIA. I had to install it with a text interface, boot to command prompt, get the driver off another computer, and run the .run off of a CD. Is it that big of an issue to make a DVD version with the drivers and applications people use and need daily? No the DVD version had the same issue as the CD version as the drivers weren't activated.

Now I know complaining about a free product is like a homeless person complaining about the decor of the hotel they were put up in, so please don't take what I say to heart. I know some people are in love with the OS as they have staked countless hours into making it as wonderful as it is. I simply can't find the words to describe how awesome it really is, but, like OSX, it is just something I can toy with. While fun, there are applications I need, and use daily. I enjoy playing video games, and even roms seem to lag in Ubuntu vs Windows. While this may or may not be directly an Ubuntu issue, more a Linux Kernel issue in which the developers here have no stakes in.

To the devs of Ubuntu, thank you for organizing Linux into something usable. Thank you for the user interface. I have used Linux distros in the past, and none can hold a candle to what you have accomplished. Alas, my package with Windows 7 and Office 2010 has arrived and I am going to be installing it, but with a heavy heart.):P

I will be back with each release to keep up to date on your awesomeness. I appreciate the time put into this.
