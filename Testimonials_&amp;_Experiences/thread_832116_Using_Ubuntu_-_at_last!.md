### Post by Gannett on 2008-06-17
I'd been trying to get Ubuntu to work for at least 2 years, and yesterday finally did it, though with some rather hair-raising moments.

I am using my wife's 4-year old Dell Dimension 3000 PC with this spec:
Pentium 4 2.8GHz
512Mb RAM
40Gb internal hard disk (25Gb Windows partition, 15Gb Ubuntu)
200Gb external USB hard disk with 30Gb FAT32 partition.

so not the latest, fastest, well-endowed machine by any measure.  It all seems to be working fine, Windows works fine, which keeps my wife happy, though it now takes ages to get going.  

I did use Debian for 6 months last year, and though I loved the KDE desktop, it was a headache getting simple stuff like sound to work.  But Ubuntu is great, sound works, internet works, printer sort of works!  

My wife wonders why I bother with Linux (and why I jeopardized her Windows installation!).  I couldn't really give her a clear answer, but I just love Ubuntu.  It's fast, good looking and clean.  I notice it does use alot of processing power on my old Pentium 4 (just painting a straight line in GIMP pushes to processor up to 60% for some reason!), but you have to pay a price somewhere.

And the Ubuntu Forums are excellent, cannot praise them enough.  Hardly worth starting a thread if you have an issue, chances are there's one about what you're interested in already there!
