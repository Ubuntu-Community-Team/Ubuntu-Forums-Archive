### Post by solwic on 2010-05-06
I'm breaking my "No T&E" rule to post this, but I wanted to say that this is the very first time since 7.04 that I've installed Ubuntu and only had to use Google for one bug fix.  My installation was smooth sailing - even the Ubuntu CD actually burnt properly the first time - and setting things up was a breeze.  

The catch was that desktop effects is still (still!!) buggy; if they're on, you're not getting Flash controls on Youtube.  Turn off DE, and the controls are great.  Flash isn't even buggy fullscreen this time around.  

And 10.04 is fast.  I'm using the 64bit version on a 2.8Ghz quad with 8gb of RAM, and it's five times faster than 9.10 (which was actually a little slower most of the time than Windows 7).  Still the same old Ubuntu look (when will Gnome3 and Gnome Shell be implemented?  Ever?), but I'm mightily impressed.  

I know the Devs don't use the forums, so I'll just throw this "good job" out into the void.  10.04 isn't perfect - far from it - but it's the best to date.

### Post by WinterRain on 2010-05-06
Solwic, to get flash controls to work properly, do the following:

Remove any flash you have installed, and get the 64bit flash file here [http://download.macromedia.com/pub/labs/flashplayer10/libflashplayer-10.0.45.2.linux-x86_64.so.tar.gz](http://download.macromedia.com/pub/labs/flashplayer10/libflashplayer-10.0.45.2.linux-x86_64.so.tar.gz)

Then extract it and put it in /usr/lib64/mozilla/plugins and restart firefox. Works perfect for me.

If you are unsure how to do this, to extract the file, just right click and "extract here". Make sure it's on your desktop. Then do in terminal: (you can copy and paste it)
```
sudo cp ~/Desktop/libflashplayer.so /usr/lib64/mozilla/plugins
```
Restart firefox, done.
