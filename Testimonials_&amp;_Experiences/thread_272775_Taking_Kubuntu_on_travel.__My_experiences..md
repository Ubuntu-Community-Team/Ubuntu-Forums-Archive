### Post by pahbi on 2006-10-06
My job requires me to travel several times a year.  On this last trip, I installed Kubuntu to see how well it would hold up.  Below are my experiences.  I am using a Dell INSPIRON 8200 laptop.

DISCLAIMER:  A lot of this review using Kubuntu while on travel are not related to this Distro, but are related to more to what software is currently available for Linux distrobutions.

General functionality

In general, I had all the tools I needed to complete the work I needed to do.

Kmail and Kontact (from KDE) worked well, allowing me to keep in touch with co-workers, provided I had an online connection available to me.

Open Office worked well, allowing me to create documents to share , and it also worked well at being compatable with Microsoft Office.

Using Automatix I was able to install codecs and software that allowed me to watch DVD's, play MP3's and a variety of multi-media formats.

Synaptic Package Manager made it a snap to get new software if necessary.

The hardware of my laptop was handled adequately by Kunbuntu.  I was able to plug printers, digital cameras and memory keys into the USB port and everything worked well.  Although I was unable to find drivers from the manufacturer, Kubuntu seemed to have drivers built into the system that allowed me to interface to most anything I plugged into the laptop.

The big exception was using a wireless adapter.  Initially I had a Belkin USB wireless adapter, but I was unable to get it to work well.  Eventually I broke down and purchased a PMCIA wireless adapter and that worked with no problems.

Sound and Video also worked well on my laptop, although I ran into trouble getting 3D acceleration to work with my ATI mobility 9000 video card.  But following some instructions I found on these forums resolved problems I had with 3D acceleration.

My desktop is easy enough to use and configure.  With a little research I was able to install new icon sets that were extremely readable, but more importantly, I was able to make shortcuts to put on my desktop that provided a link to commonly used directories or files.

I think, at this moment, the one thing that I *REALLY* missed from my WinXP operating system was Microsoft Streets.  While I don't get lost very often, being able to put in a street address and find directions to all the restaurants, gas stations, atms or whatever, that are nearbye is invaluable.  This is probably the one thing that will tempt me to go back to WinXP.

WINE didn't really work to well in letting me use Microsoft Streets and Trips.

So, there is my dis-organized experience with using Kutuntu while on travel.

It will be several months before I have to go on travel again, if in the meantime I can find some software to fill the gap in the mapsoftware hole, I'll definately continue using Kubuntu.  If I can't find anything, I may have to go back to WinXP.

- Pahbi
