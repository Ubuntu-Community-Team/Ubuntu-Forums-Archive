### Post by uRock on 2010-05-12
This is not a rant towards Debian nor Ubuntu. I am thankful for the end product that is happily running on my system.

For the past few weeks I have had the wild idea to give Debian Squeeze a try, again. But this time I wanted to make it the promary OS on my desktop so I used my Netbook's hard drive space and, via the network, I backed up all of my data and .mozilla files. 

Next I booted the awesome Lucid LiveCD and used its GParted to shrink W7, then erase all other partitions. I then created the extended partition with three partitions in it for /, /home and swap.

I restarted and threw in the Debian Squeeze Netinst CD and started the install.

All was good, it utilized the precreated partitions without complaint. It let me create the user and root accounts, then started installing.

As many may know, after Debian has made its partitions it will then offer to choose a server to run the rest of the install, sure, sounds great, but it did not recognize my NIC. After an hour of running commands from the nice guys on the Debian IRC I still couldn't get the NIC working, so I let the minimal install run. Once it was done and the restart was complete it recognized my NIC, sweet!

Next I had to enter the repos by hand, because the installer couldn't get them earlier. No problem, one of the guys on the IRC gave me a few repos to add and all was good.

So, now that I was able to install gnome, gdm, and xorg, which took an hour, I played some Wii with my kid, which was fine.

Now came the fun part that had detered me before, getting nVidia installed. I found the documentation and did everything it said to do, but for some reason after an hour of troubleshooting, the driver still would not completely install. At this point I had realized that It was not worth wasting one more minute.

Within 45 minutes I had reformatted and installed Lucid, completely with nVidia drivers and all my favorite programs.

Lucid now runs better than before. I think the clean /home had a lot to do with it. The previous /home had more than 10 installs of various*buntus on it with all of their settings folders in the home folder trying to have their way with the next install.

My lesson learned is this. The folks developing ubuntu have worked their asses off bringing this great product. They have made all of the configuration to Debian's build to make it work on my system with very little thought input from me. I am sure that with a few more commands and an ounce more of patience I could have gotten the nVidia driver installed and would have been happy, but my realization said, "Let Ubuntu do it for me!" So, I did.

Thank you everyone who has put any work into Debian and/or Ubuntu to make this end product what it is. To include, but not limited to;

Developers,
Testers,
IRC & Forum Volunteers, 

Cheers & Beers,
uRock

Edit: This thread is a testimony about how easy ubuntu is to install as compared to its father, Debian. I should have worded the poll better as I wanted it to be a + or - for giving thanks to all who make Debian and Ubuntu possible.
