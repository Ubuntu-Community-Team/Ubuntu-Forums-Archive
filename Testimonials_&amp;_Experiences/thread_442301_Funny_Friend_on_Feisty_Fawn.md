### Post by Rab22 on 2007-05-13
I get a call about 6 to 8 months ago from a friend who says she installed Ubuntu 6.06 on her computer (which she has upgraded now to Feisty). She told me she did it because she really enjoyed the way my desktop looked and because I talked it up so much.  I asked her how she obtained Ubuntu (since she didn't ask me for it) and she told me she talked to some friends at school about wanting to try Ubuntu and when one guy heard her say 'Ubuntu' his eyes light up and that he not only gave her an Ubuntu CD but a stack of Ubuntu stickers (which she has posted all over her wall).  She said she was impressed by the way 'Ubuntu people' get all excited when others are interested in Ubuntu. 

Now for the story. 
Last night, I received an IM from my friend about how she messed up 'her Ubuntu Linux'.  This time, she -- not knowing anything about video cards, video drivers, etc -- decided to install Beryl.  She followed a tutorial for Beryl installation using Nvidia's latest drivers on Feisty Fawn.  I figured she messed up her xorg.conf file which, as everyone around here probably knows by experience, causes X to crash. 

Our convo went something like this, 

Me: "You are getting an error that says X can't start?"
Her: "Yes"
Me: "You are on Windows now then?" 
Her: "No.".  
Me (shocked): "Well how are you on here then?".  
Her: "I used the LiveCD, I hate Windows".
Me: "lol, Ohhh"

Anyways, she doesn't have an Nvidia card, so all we needed to do was access the correct video driver...and she is back up and running 'her Ubuntu Linux'.

Another interesting thing about my friend. About a month ago she went from Yahoo! DSL to Road Runner.  When the cable guy came out to hook it up he was impressed at her computer.  He started asking her what she was using and she told him it was "Ubuntu Linux".  She gave him a CD and he said he'd look into it. 

I think it should be a goal that every Ubuntu user spreads Ubuntu to at least ONE other person.  I have found that every user I get hooked on Ubuntu usually spreads it to another.  If this trend stays consistent -- the results could be amazing! :)

-Rick
