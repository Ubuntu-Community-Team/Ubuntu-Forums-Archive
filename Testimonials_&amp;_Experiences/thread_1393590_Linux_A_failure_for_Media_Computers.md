### Post by beastrace91 on 2010-01-29
First off I'd like to say I've been using Ubuntu for almost three years now and for the most part I am decently happy with it. My netbook runs Ubuntu 9.10 32bit and my gaming laptop runs Ubuntu 9.10 64bit/Win7 64bit (I use the latter for DX10 games that do not run under Wine as of yet).

Earlier this week I purchased a PC to use as a dedicated media center, naturally I  formatted Win7 off of it in exchange for my favorite operating system - Ubuntu 9.10

Now I knew going in that my netflix and certain TV sites like Fox and the WB would not function on Ubuntu - and I was willing to work with that. However the issues started when I attempted to get the audio for the system to work through HDMI output, six hours, three walk troughs, and two unanswered forum posts later - still no digital audio.

Alright - fine. I have an 1/8 to RCA cable, I can live with analog sound if it means I don't have to deal with M$.

Next: my Gateway remote I got with the PC is non-functional as well. Again not a big deal, I have a wireless keyboard and mouse...

What good is a computer if I can't get online with it? My router however is tucked away in a corner in my house and all my PC's access it via wifi, I planned for the media center to be no different. I acquire a USB wifi card, plug it in - the network manager detects it and sees my network. Can the card connect? Of course not. Several hours yet again and three posts on different Linux help forums all of which have 0 solutions or even ideas on how to fix the problem later my media center is still offline.

Needless to say I am VERY disappointed. Currently in the process of moving my 362.3gigs of media files to my external hard drive so I can format the internal drive to Win7 ultimate. Such a shame.
