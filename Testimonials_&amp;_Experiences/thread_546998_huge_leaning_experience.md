### Post by savagenator on 2007-09-09
hello everyone. I just wanted to share my linux experience over the last year

the first time i used linux, i couldnt figure anything out at all. THe second time i used linux i started learning about packages and automatix. Also i learned about how many hours it could take to fix the xserver when you know nothing. Then it took me at least 8 hours to fiture out that i need to have only nvidia-glx installed and to edit the xorg.conf i need just to do "sudo dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg" (now memorized). To redo the same thing today in a reinstall, it took me 15 minutes, including adding a second monitor in twinview which i never did before.

Grub used to be a mystery for me. I didn't know how to work it and what sda(1,0) was i had no clue. Then i spend an estimated 8 hours straight just tryin to edit grub learn its commands and trying to get it fixed. The problem was strange, it just wouldnt get menu.lst to show. To fix it at the end of 8 hours, all i did was go into the live cd, copy the grub and boot files for the kernel, and put them in the boot and grub files in my HD, for some reason they werent there and the problem was fixed. Today i accidently had the wrong order of drivers, and i installed ubuntu on my secondary boot drive. after 15 minutes of troubleshooting, i got it to work after a simple grub edit and boot after making the change for the drive to be first.

what else? i think i am as educated in ubuntu as some of the higher techs now, i can actually fix stuff. And it took me a whole year... but it was fun, and its still fun to make my desktop user friendly in every way.

Thanks ubuntuforums for helping me and giving me lots and lots and lots of guides.
