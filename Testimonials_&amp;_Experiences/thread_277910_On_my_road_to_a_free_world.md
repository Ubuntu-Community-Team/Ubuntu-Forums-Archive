### Post by Gokudan on 2006-10-15
Well, first of all i say hi to all of you!

     My name's Martim and i am from venezuela, i work as an IT consultant and most of my life i worked as a computer technician, in the last two years i've worked more like an IT Administrator on corporate environments than a computer technician and i've been more involved with Servers, Networks and comunications administration, so i've worked with the MS Server family and very little with Red Hat ES and SCO Unix.

   All of my life my behavior was more like sorta of a windows fanboy rather than an IT guy (i think it happens to most IT guys in a major part of their live), since ever i've felt some attraction to discover and work my way around and being different on my approach to the job, i always felt an attraction also to unix/linux environments but always felt some kinda fear to make that giant leap, mostly because i'm also a gamer.

    Lately i'm kinda dissapointed with MS customers policy, they do not seem to care at all for their customers, and that's because now i am responsible por 5 critical mission servers and i'm also responsible to lend support to 80+ users with Windows based workstations (99% of them belong to a domain) and i call a lot of times to ask questions now and then.

    I dunno remember when but some months ago there was an event id error related to our domain and i asked a very technical question and the first thing the phone support guy said to me was that i had to pay USD$100 to microsoft and then i'll get the support i was asking for, and i hung up on him on the most polite way i could found at the moment.

   Geez, i mean isn't the customer already paying for an expensive license? why should i pay for support when i already paid a USD$1000 (Win2k3 Server Standtard w/ 5 CAL's) or even a UDS$3705 (Win2k3 Server Enterprise with 25 CAl's) fee for a server OS?  Shouldn't support be included with the license fee? (i guess there was a time where ms applied this rule, support was free) If i'm gonna pay USD$100 i prefer to pay an IT Specialist not from MS.

   I recently upgraded my PC from an Athlon XP 2000+ cpu to an Athlon 64 3500+ cpu based box, and i'm not even thinking about installing vista on it, right now i'm dual booting with XP and Ubuntu 6.06 LTS. I'm turning away from ms on whatever aspect that does not involve my job, i still have XP installed because as i said i'm a gamer (altough i have a PS2 haha :D) and i still need windows to run some of the latest games.

    "Isn't that nice?" i say myself when i know that my just upgraded pc became obsolete thanks to gaming and MS industry, i mean, i just upgraded my pc 6 months ago and it's almost obsolete, at least in therms of cpu and graphics.

     To be honest i tried first with Gentoo 2006.1 x64 AMD64 but it's installation is yet far too difficult for me (altough i was able to even compile my own kernel) so i decided to try Ubuntu because it was highly recommended from some friends.

   And oh well *sighs* here i am, trying to get away MS from my PC :D, i installed Ubuntu x64 altough i've seen some negative stuff about it, but  it's ok, i downloaded automatix and my java problems went away, right now i just have some sound glitches, like there's some times that sound doesn't start right away, but well...i can live with that. What i see is going to be the major problem is getting over some differences between ms programs and linux programs, i.e. microsoft office vs open office, and getting used to the apt-get and all the other differents commands i need to input to make things work and install programs (i need to dust off my old ms dos skills hehehe :D).

   And well, i think ubuntu is a good place to start (maybe stay?) my way to a ms free PC, it's almost sure i'll need a lot of tips and suggestions       from you guys in order to adapt myself, but so far i'm very happy with my experience.

Cya :D

Gokudan
