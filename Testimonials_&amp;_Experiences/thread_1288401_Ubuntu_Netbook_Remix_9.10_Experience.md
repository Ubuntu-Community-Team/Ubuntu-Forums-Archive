### Post by s.fox on 2009-10-11
Well,  I decided to upgrade to UNR 9.10 on my Acer Aspire One and I thought I would share how I am getting on with it.  Main points are as follows:

1)  The new login screen isn't quite what I expected and to be honest I prefer the one that came with Jaunty.  I suppose the biggest thing I don't like is the fact that it lists my username.  I imagine this could probably be fixed though, so nothing major :)

2)  The new kernal means my inbuilt microphone finally works with programs like Skype.  Extremely happy about that.  :D

3)  The user interface has been given a face lift and I am happy to see that the files and folders have been added into a menu item on the left hand side.  For me this makes far much more sense than Jaunty especially given that I only have an 8.9" screen. 

4)  A minor problem I have found is that the main user interface seems to have a short cut to my favourite applications twice.  This is perhaps better explained by the screen shot.   I am not sure at the minute how to fix this,  though again I imagine it won't be hard.

5)  I am also happy to see that I can now set a desktop wallpaper that is visible.  A nice feature that allows me to personalise my computer that little bit more. :)

Over all I am very happy with the way the system is performing.  Of course I have had one or two system problems,  but that is to be expected with a beta.   I 100% believe that this version of the remix is better than the old one and I am already looking forward to the next set of updates available for download.   Thank you to all involved in this development,  you are all doing a brilliant job :D

### Post by DigitaLink on 2009-11-30
Okay, there's a mar on my UNR experience.  USB hard drive performance.  It's horrific, and apparently pretty common.  I was getting sustained 17-20MB/s out of Windows 7.  I'm getting flaky speeds on Ubuntu.

The transfers start out okay.  I'll get a couple hundred megabytes at 17-21MB/s, then it halts for almost exactly 40 seconds.  Then it will continue at around 5MB/s for around 10-12 seconds.  Then it halts for another 40.  Then goes a little bit.  Then takes a 40 second rest.  The 40 seconds is almost clockwork when it pauses.  Then eventually it will stall out altogether, unmount, and re-mount.  Then I'm good for another short burst at near full speed ... then 40 second breaks ensue.

This makes is REALLY tough to get about 100GB of backed up files off my external drive.  Heck, I wouldn't even care about 5MB/s transfers ... if they didn't stop for 2/3rds of every minute!  I've searched forums and bug trackers and other distros ... the bug is out there, it doesn't appear to be Ubuntu specific, but none of the kernel devs seem to care about what would be a showstopper for a Windows convert using a USB drive.  Tried everything out there I could find, including manual mounting/unmounting, editing fstab manually, etc.  That bought me what speeds I AM managing right now.  The 17-20MB/s starting speed wasn't happening at all before.  And the 4-5MB/s in between pauses used to be 700KB/s.

Not sure what else to try, but it's a sour note in an otherwise fine symphony of computing joy.
