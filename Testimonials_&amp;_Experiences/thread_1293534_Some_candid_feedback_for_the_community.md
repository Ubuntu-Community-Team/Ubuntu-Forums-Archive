### Post by winjeel on 2009-10-17
I took my laptop into work and a colleague noticed my OS. He said, "so you're using Ubuntu". I was surprised that he both spotted and identified the difference straight off the bat. He's a mac user, but has tried Ubuntu. I asked why he doesn't continue to use it, and he said that he didn't like Open Office, pretty much for the same reasons why I hate Open Office: the formatting style doesn't abide by professional style format, and Open Office is just generally annoying to use. Then he asked how I can keep using Ubuntu and get my documents properly formatted and why I seem not to have any troubles; my answer was 'Wine'.

The point is, he assumed that Open Office was naturally apart of Ubuntu. We're both REALLY busy people, so we don't want to spend much time playing around with software packages and things, so trialling gets limited time, so it either works right off, or it's out. I did admit to him, though, that I still have XP for iTunes (iPhone) and for LightRoom.
