### Post by loudnlownoma on 2008-03-14
I know I have dropped a few snippets of my experience in various threads, but figured after yesterday, I needed to make this post.  I know that it may not mean much, but I can't stop telling people about what a great time I have had with Ubuntu, and people at work have been giving me "shut up or die" type stares most of the day now...lol.

Have been using Ubuntu(Feisty and Gutsy) for a while now.  Started as a virtual install of Feisty on my HP laptop.  I had messed with a few other distros before(Knoppix, FC5, and Linspire mostly) but had strayed away for a few months when a buddy handed me a Feisty Kubuntu CD.  Really impressed with how well it seemed to work in the VM and the LiveCD, I decided to install.  But I prefer GNOME over KDE in most cases, so I grabbed a copy of Ubuntu 7.04 and went to work, splitting my laptop hard drive and dropping about 20 GB to Ubuntu to get my feet wet with it.  Don't get me wrong, I had some of the same issues people see around here consistently - Wireless not working(the dreaded BCM4xxx), ATI driver issues, etc.  But I chugged away at it, scouring the forums and spending many late nights in IRC trying to get it running exactly how I wanted.  After a few failed attempts with the wireless drivers and a whole mess of trouble with Video drivers, I was getting discouraged, and wiped for a fresh install.  Started with the restricted ATI drivers off the bat, gave up wireless on my laptop for a while(since I don't really use it that frequently anyway), and was putting together a pretty nice setup - playing with the themes, etc., and, through the help of WINE and IE4Linux, was able to keep enough familiarity around for those programs I just couldn't get away from.  Was very happy with this setup and was getting happier and more confident in it's abilities every day I used it.  Also found myself using Windows less and less.

Fast forward a couple months, still a happy Feisty user, seeing Gutsy coming out and looking to get a new desktop any day, I had a friend burn me a copy of Gutsy.  Soon enough, I get my desktop (an HP m9040n) and pop in Feisty(slow friend still hadn't gotten around to burning the disc for me and dial-up is not the way to go for a download like that).  Played around with it and was having fun, but there were a few things not working correctly, so I decided to wait for the Gutsy disc.  And boy was I surprised.  Finally got it from my friend, installed, and right off the bat fell in love with it.  Everything just seemed to work.  It picked up most of the hardware right away, enabling my Nvidia card was a couple simple clicks, and things were going great.  I boot into Vista so little now, I might as well get rid of it(and will be as soon as Hardy is stable, since it should have the updated drivers for my TVTuner).  Again using Windows less and less, and finding new things working or new useful apps.  Even found out my HP printer(which Vista required a 40 MB driver to download and  use) works right out of the box, without changing or adding anything.  Another plus for Ubuntu!

So a few months later still, and I finally decided to upgrade my laptop to Gutsy, to see how things improve for it.  Install was a little rough, but I had backed up everything already and decided it was time to take the plunge - I formatted the whole drive and let Gutsy have at it.  Luckily, the install went smoothly after that and I was up and running, with only a few little quirks that I have not yet had time to look into.  But these are far outweighed by the improvements that the update allowed.  My wireless card now works!!!  Two simple clicks in the Restricted drivers manager and I can use my wireless card again!  On top of that, Compiz works flawlessly with it.  This was one I had experimented with in Feisty and found all kinds of complications, and was happy it worked so well on my desktop.  Now I have it on my laptop too!  There are just so many things that either work now and haven't before, or work better than before, that I have no idea why I didn't try the upgrade sooner!  

Just wanted to share a little bit about my experiences and joy with Ubuntu, and in case any of the powers that be(devs, forum staff, people that have offered help or kind words along the way, etc.) happen to see this - THANK YOU!!!
