### Post by Jeramiah on 2009-11-10
Hi, I'm Jeramiah, kind of new to Mr. Penguin, but not so new as to not know how frustrating it is to see BASH YOU CAN DO THAT HA-HA! Like many users, it seems, I made the conversion to Ubuntu via Windows shortcomings (the many... many shortcomings). I enjoyed the learning process so much on my Ubuntu Laptop, that at the first sign of blue screen terror I installed in on my Desktop as well. Sure, running video games is a bit more difficult (or almost impossible as per the given game title), but the feeling of actually getting something to work on something so alien to me as Ubuntu is makes my freaking day!

After the post-nuclear war with windows, I launched my first attempt at customizing the desktop, I use pictures from the Pipboy from Fallout 3, and made little icons to look like the buttons, so I basically made a somewhat functioning Pipboy 3000A! It was very fun to take my laptop to college for notes only to see the person sitting next to me staring at my laptop screen with a curious look on their face. "You playing Fallout 3?" my peer asked. I said "No, that's my desktop." After adding little bits like sound effects and nifty little widgets for the "Pipboy's" clock my awesome transformation was complete. Then I started another project to make my desktop look like an old scroll like from a fantasy game or something.

So, after jumping on these fine forums for the occasional tech help (most my questions were already asked for me, so I've only just started posting on here), I chose to join this fine community as most of my answers were already here! Ha! 

All in all, my Ubuntu experience has taught me a great deal about the thought proccess, how to over come technical problems, and the right methods to seek a solution. Windows never did that for me. My XP would catch virus from myspace, or too many cookies here, or some game just hated my harddrive and I would simply back up and reinstall. Back up and reinstall. With windows, I got so used to the idea of it crashing I bought a 320 GB external harddrive just to back things up. Every 3 to 6 months my XP would hiccup, choke, then die. Almost like clockwork. With conspiracy running in my head like "Microsoft is trying to destory my computer", I finally gave up on Windows altogether. I may still use a different computer with windows, but only for video games. I just won't chance the data lost from the variety of viruses out there that I always stumble upon. 

Anyway, I just wanted to say "Thank you!" to those with the know how to answer the difficult questions about Ubuntu like "Why isn't my wireless working," or "Wine isn't playing this game, or that game". Those of you how asked my questions before me, and answered them made the scare world of Ubuntu seem like a purring kitten.

Ubuntu is like this awesome hacking game. It's frustrating to learn. Scary to think what you can do with so much power. It can seem so overwhelming. Yet, the payoff of getting a program to run, with only the help of furoms, or through trial and error, is far greater than any operating system I've used ever. No only does one build self confidence through it's application, one learns through the experience.

Thank you Ubuntu Community
You make Ubuntu wonderful

### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2009-11-11
Glad to hear the OP great experience!

However, I gotta say that you make it sound as if nothing worked out of the box and a standard user would have to wrestle to get anything moving.

In case someone thinking of trying Ubuntu is reading this, please understand that´s far from being the case.  Ubuntu usually works flawlessly out of the box.  

Many people confuse the OS with potential problems coming from a given application.  It is important to understand that many of the available applications out there are developed solely by community members, so it is understandeable that sometimes errors are found.  Those kinds of errors should not be blamed on the OS, but they often are.

I don´t want to deny, though, that there is a definite learning curve when you start using Linux from Windows, just like if you started using Mac.  Perhaps it is more intense for the Linux experience, because it is less of a controlled environment and because the user can dive as deep as s/he wants.
