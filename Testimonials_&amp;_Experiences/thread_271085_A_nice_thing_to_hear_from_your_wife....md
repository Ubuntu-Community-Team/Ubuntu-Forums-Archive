### Post by encompass on 2006-10-04
I have been an advocate of linux for ALONG time.  7 years now, and love it.  I married a wonderful Finnish woman and am not living in finland.
I was recently having a meal with a relative who was a programmer, not just any, but a game programmer, he made engines that are used in Tony Hawk Proskater, Prince of Persia, Splinter Cell, Max Pain and much of 3d Max software titles.  The guy knows his stuff.  He now programs for Xbox and PS3.
I hadn't tried to mention linux to him figuring he knows enough that he would give it a try in a while.
But while eating my wife begins to ask if he had ever heard about linux.  EEEK!  What is she doing!  She then talked about a recent seminar I organized and said that she learned alot about it and talked to him about how powerful and amazing linux is, how she uses it at home, and how much she doesn't like windows now that she has learned linux. (mind you she has only ben using it for about a year now.)
I was scared, and he even looked a little scared.  I think the reason he was scared was the fact that she is a simple person, not advanced in linux knowledge but able to use it well and often.  He couldn't say anything technical to here he had to keep things simple and he couldn't, why?  Because he never spoke to someone so simple about something that is so technical.
Needless to say it was funny in the end. :)
But none the less he told that he can't use linux at the time but will always keep it in mind.
Just goes to show how linux is something that even a simple computer person can be avid about.  I will never forget the look on her face.  She was so proud to share something a millionare programmer didn't know the first thing about.
And by the way, the seminar tesimonial is next... but not fully complete.  Stay tuned for that this month.
