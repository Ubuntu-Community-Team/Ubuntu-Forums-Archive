### Post by 1roxtar on 2010-03-11
I go to a small local college and using their school computers is such a pain.  Except for two computers that run Linux Mint, all the rest run Windows XP and are so full of viruses that I practically have to place a condom over my USB flash drive so it won't get infected when I plug it in.  

The other day, I had to use a computer to do some quick research, but I couldn't access anything because virus pop-up alerts (mostly fake alerts) rendered the computer useless.  I had enough and reached into my bag and pulled out my SuperOS LiveCD (Ubuntu 9.10 w/restricted extras and other stuff), popped it in the cd tray and rebooted.  I heard angels singing as Ubuntu appeared on my screen.

I finally got my research completed, but not before my instructor noticed that my desktop was now different than the others.  He asked how I did that.  I told him that I was running Linux straight off of a cd because the computer was unusable because of the viruses.  He gave me this wide-eyed look and said, "That's pretty cool" and moved on.

When Lucid comes out in April, it will also be my one-year anniversary of using Ubuntu and Linux as a whole.  I am thankful and privileged to have been set free.  I have since boarded up my "Windows"!!!
