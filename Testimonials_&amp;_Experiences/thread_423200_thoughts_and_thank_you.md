### Post by papercuts on 2007-04-25
After two months of tinkering with all Ubuntu editions and some other Linux distros I have today settled on my desktop configuration. Its really solid, looks great, works like a charm, and does everything I need.

At times I got really fed up though, and felt like dumping the whole thing. "That stupid Linux" was going through my head at those times. After working with and around some things I noticed that most problems were caused by a lack of understanding and trying to work against the system provided for you. For instance I always wanted to get a dock for Gnome. It looked cool in OS X and did cool stuff. I screwed up and in the end realized that a Mac Dock is good to use with a MAc because that's how a MAc is. Now I really get why Gnome panels are the way they are. It makes a lot more sense to me now and I feel like I am really in control of my desktop with well utilized Gnome panels. I also had the time to test and see my favourite apps. Mostly it was tiring to try everything but I realized I don't have to try everything. Take a bunch of tested and liked apps and only try those. You can't go wrong. 

So in the end I realized that everyone's trying to find a desktop that works for them and until they do (we do), they will not stop complaining. Linux offers possibilities and I don't blame people striving to realize them. Though it is true that we as newbies get rude from time to time. Sorry for that :)

Another thing I realized is that Ubuntu is just a solid distro. I also know now that community and a continuous solid resource backend is the major component of Linux, not some shiny starter eye candy. I might change some of my components but I don't think I will switch to any other distro than Ubuntu. 

I am truly amazed to think that my great Ubuntu desktop is not a commercial product but the result of cooperation and sharing. The fact that such great work could come from such a difficult working environment is good news for the future. I don't know if Linux is ready for the desktop but I can safely say Ubuntu surely is. 

So thank you everyone who worked on this and shared their expertise. You have built something great!
