### Post by kleeman on 2005-11-04
So I just bought a notebook which had hoary preinstalled. The wpa encryption for the wireless card didn't work (everything else did). So I rather incautiously decided immediately to upgrade to breezy (using an ethernet cable which worked perfectly). After switching my sources.list file I went

sudo apt-get update && sudo apt-get dist-upgrade

and after two 

sudo apt-get -f install

commands EVERYTHING just worked. This was a shock, it has never happened to me with an upgrade in Linux before. There was always some nagging problem that took several hours of mind numbing effort to fix. Not this time. And then the wpa encryption worked once I manually configured the ip address (dhcp did not work unfortunately which I will lodge as a bugreport). 

Today I needed a usb mouse and a usb flash stick so I went to Bestbuy and rather foolishly (or so I thought after leaving the store) just bought the one that looked the best off the shelf.  I was worried because there was a big warning about having to download Windows 98 drivers from their website.

Took them home plugged both into my new breezy notebook and both worked absolutely perfectly. The flashdrive even thoughtfully opened a nautilus session and showed me what was on it. I dragged my powerpoint files over with a mouse then clicked on them and my screenshow very cleanly and nicely opened and worked exactly as expected. Boy does all this just working really reduce my stress levels.

If breezy is this polished I am thinking dapper is potentially going to blow Vista out of the water next year.

By the way I bought the notebook from LinuxCertified who I can thoroughly recommend. The notebook is a little ripper!
