### Post by Nolroz on 2009-04-17
I thought I would write a quick tutorial on how to get Kubuntu to work on the Toshiba A205.
[LIST=1]
[*]Burn a Kubuntu install disk
[*]install Kubuntu
[*]turn off ivp6, if you have a crappy router like I do
[*]do a dance of joy
[/LIST]

Folks, I have literally been through dozens of versions of Linux, I have never EVER had it work out of the box EVER. After installing Kubuntu, I had sound, wireless, graphics, mouse, and even the freaking volume control on the front of my laptop working.  Not to mention, I can play the music and edit the images that are still on my windows partition...brilliant.

Kubuntu 8.10 is the best damn Linux distro out there (I like GNOME too, don't get me wrong) it beats the living pants off anything I have tried.  I can't wait for 9.04 to calm down a little.

I just wanted to glow a little bit.  We have a great OS at our disposal folks, it's mindblowingly awesome that I can migrate from windows in essentially one step.

Thanks to everyone and anyone out there who has contributed.  It feels great to own my computer again.

### Post by Nolroz on 2009-09-26
Sliiice, I'm no car guy, but pcdoctor is right on this one.  KDE and Gnome largely give you a different look and feel, and a different set of natively compatible applications (you can get most programs from either desktop environment to work on the other) 

I am a web developer, and have had the best luck with KDE for a usable work environment, although I like the general look of gnome better.

Since I initially posted this thread, I have been through a couple of versions of Ubuntu.  I discovered that there is an issue with the graphic card and the current version of Ubuntu (9.04) so I am currently running 8.10.  You can bypass the issue by installing one of the release candidate kernels, but I would wait till 9.10, as the fix should be included in the install, along with some other great enhancements for laptop users.

Welcome on board!
