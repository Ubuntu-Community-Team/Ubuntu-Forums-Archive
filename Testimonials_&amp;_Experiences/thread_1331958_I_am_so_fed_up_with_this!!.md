### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2009-11-19
..... said my boss when his Vista Ultimate did no longer boot after installing SP 2 for the umpteenth time as it kept nagging him to do so despite MANY previous attempts that ended in identical failure to boot.

And he asked me if i could do something about it.

He is my boss. I love the guy - one can't have a boss more cool than him. Lets me play online games all day long if i have nothing else to do, and ever since i put Ubuntu on pretty much all computers at the office there IS not much to do for me, the computer guy. 

Those computers don't hang or crash anymore. No anti-virus or Windows updates force reboots in the middle of work. They run like 200% faster than before, and STAY that fast. And every employee that's sitting in front of one had no problems whatsoever using them... despite leaving the office on Friday evening after shutting down Windows XP and coming back Monday morning to boot Ubuntu Jaunty. All i had to do was telling them that "Microsoft Word" is now called "OpenOffice.org Word Processor" and that creating pdf files is now built-in and much faster. And that they CAN now open their private e-mail and click on whatever they want - if it doesn't appear to do anything, like viruses usually do, it will at least also not do any harm. 

Now three computers remained on Windows - my boss' because he didn't trust himself to be able to use Linux, my admin station because i have all the important stuff on a pgp encrypted removable drive and i didn't find a Linux-PgP yet... and the one that serves a a monitor for our security camera, and that one does nothing else - Windows XP and the cam software, that's it. Running 24/7 for months on end (until a power outage is long enough to bring the UPS down).

That number is now down to two - as my boss also faced the dilemma of coming to the office one beautiful sunny morning to boot his machine into - UBUNTU. 

No, he was not upset. He was eager. Live Messenger is now called aMSN. Yahoo Messenger is now called Pidgin. Doc and xls files still open by double-clicking them. To take the flash drive out you need to right-click the icon on the desktop and chose "unmount volume". No, you don't need to hunt that little icon in the taskbar that always hides itself. His Firefox bookmarks were all there, as were his stored passwords. 

That was on Tuesday this week.

Yesterday he brought in his Logitech webcam. "I want to try something, can you help me please?" Plugged it in. Terminal, lsusb. There it was. aMSN. And it worked, no driver CD required! 

And then he said the magic words i was hoping for him to say. "Be at my place next Monday 8 am. I want this Ubuntu on my machine. Screw Vista!"

Another convert, and a happy one at that. A great boss, one that tells me to "make it work" and doesn't in any way dictate HOW i "make it work". 

Oh, and did i mention that Ubuntu ROCKS..? No? well, i do that now.

Ubuntu ROCKS.

Best regards......

Thanh

### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2010-01-28
YEEEEEE-HAAAA!!!

Thursday, January 28, 2010. I did it. Yes.

Yesterday, actually. But today i finalized it :)

What?

I used a period of slow business to unpack that screwdriver and got busy on my admin machine here at the office. First, i cleaned up that Windows install - there was still some junk installed that nobody ever needed, got rid of it and managed to get the whole thing down to 14 Gigabytes. I then installed VMware vCenter Converter and converted the actual install into a VMware virtual machine which i then saved onto an external USB hard drive.

Then i shut down Windows, started the computer from my Ubuntu CD and selected "install Ubuntu"!

And i gave Ubuntu both of the internal 40 GB hard drives - one for / and swap, the other for /home. After no more than 15 minutes that computer restarted into beautiful Ubuntu :)

And now, one day later, the system is fully customized, tweaked and working. It looks the way i like it, it has all applications that are required for work (and some for pleasure - Planet Penguin Racer for example!) and all hardware works perfectly - what took the longest to get working was the Canon all-in-one printer "Pixma MX318" that didn't want to scan, however now i don't ask it anymore, i just scan :)

The network is up and running as well, file sharing with all the other computers at the office works, printer sharing works as well (the second, laser, printer) and the computer is faster than ever.

Oh, and should i ever need it, the previously virtualized Windows is at my fingertips as well, two clicks and i'm in Windows (or more like "two clicks and a cigarette break" because it takes just as long to boot as it took when it was the sole OS) thanks to VMware Workstation which installed and runs flawlessly. 

So now i have one single computer at the office that is still running XP..... and i believe that one i'll keep on that because it does nothing but show the security camera :) 

Feels good to be free...... 

Kind regards......

Thanh
