### Post by terry_gardener on 2008-08-04
As the title says i have been using ubuntu for the past year and i am loving it. I have learnt so much with regard to the linux OS and how bad windows actually was for me. I have been using ubuntu since 7.04 and then upgraded to 7.10 and then to 8.04 hardy.

When i first started using ubuntu i have to admit i had problems adjusting to the new OS but when i used it for a few days i loved it. I have had my fair share of ups and downs with the OS but i have learnt so much by searching these forums and using google, if it wasn't for these forums i would have given up without a doubt as i knew windows pretty well. I have since migrated my parents pc to ubuntu and my nephew is using it aswell . 

I still use windows xp in dual boot environment only because i have a piece of hardware that uses serial port connection to upgrade its firmware and it is not supported in ubuntu or have never found out how to do it even after endless searches.

I have tried lots of different distros Kubuntu, pclinuxos 2007, mandriva, dreamlinux, fedora, opensuse and i have always come back to ubuntu seems to be the best all rounder for me and my hardware configuration. 

I would certainly recommend ubuntu or linux in general to anyone and have done plenty of times, but i dont force it on them because it is up to the end user what they want to use. 

I have know got my desktop looking just how i want it and i change the theme regularly aswell which i love about this OS. I use my pc for a wide range of tasks tv (tvtime), surfing (firefox 3), email (thunderbird), IM (pidgin), VOIP (skype), Music (amarok), Videos (VLC), Downloading (hellanzb), Devede (video converting), K3b (cd/dvd burning), Quickstart (backup/restore and installing codecs), Ubuntu Tweak. 

I also think it is brilliant that certain companies are starting to give there customers a choice of what OS they can have or even sell the computer with no OS so people can install what they want on it. I think that we need even more companies to do this then more hardware support for ubuntu can be achieved, I have a creative soundblaster X-fi and i have had trouble getting it to work with ubuntu properly so i am using the onboard HD audio device instead which works perfectly with ubuntu.

Just like to say thank you to everyone on the forums for your help in the past year and to all the devs for creating a great OS.
