I have been on Ubuntu for two months now and I love it. Customization of anything. Fast. Stable. Fun. I also get to be in these forums.:popcorn:One day when I was bored and looking for a Linux Distro for some iMac G3s, I came across this thing called Ubuntu. I saw it would not work with the iMacs, so I decied I could use it on my laptop. I posted with the internsions of using Kubuntu, but nightwishfan and some others told me and guided me to use Ubuntu. I burned it and tried out the Live CD. I loved how oveis everything was, so i hit install and gave it 60GB. I got to the desktop from GRUB in about 10 seconds, and then went in to every menu there was and customized. I asked how to customize and started threads, and kept getting help. I then though I needed to give back. So now I have gone through these forums, skimming for zero post threads and answering. I now have an advanced user desktop, friends, social groups, guides, and answered posts under my Ubuntu "Belt". Remeber this was all done in two months. ### Post by cariboo on 2010-09-06
The best way for me to learn Linux was to keep breaking it, and then fixing it again, instead of re-installing as many people do. Once I was fairly familiar with the commands, I started picking things I didn't know, like setting up a web server, working with mysql, NFS file sharing and many others. It helps if you have at least a second system to play with, that way if you break the system you still have a working one to look for solutions on the internet.
