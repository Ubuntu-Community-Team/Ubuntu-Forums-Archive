### Post by sderrick on 2009-07-25
A couple of years ago I installed Hardy and was ecstatic about how well it installed and performed. Everything worked, it was rock solid. I became an Ubuntu convert. 

I upgraded to Intrepid when it was released and was somewhat disappointed that there were glitches with the boot up process and I started to have intermittent OS hangs. There were some minor video issues also. I eventually found a work around for the boot and video problems.  The intermittent OS hangs were never solved. 

I upgraded to Jaunty hoping for the kind of product that Hardy was, and was deeply disappointed to find it worse than Intrepid. Network issues, hangs during shut down, video was totally f**** up. I finally fixed the network issues with the latest kernel, three months later.  Teh video and hangs are still here.  

I think Ubuntu has dune a great job with the installer and eye candy packaging but its just not a stable system. I don't know if its because they use the Debian SID(unstable) release or just don't have time to test all the packages they include. 

As the old saying goes Form follows Function, and Ubuntu has more and more put Form first while sacrificing function. 

Going to reinstall the latest Debian stable release and say good bye to Ubuntu for now.

Scott

### Post by sderrick on 2009-07-26
Actually the LTS releases are just as unstable out of the gate as normnal releases, see 

[https://blueprints.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+spec/server-karmic-lts-policies](https://blueprints.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+spec/server-karmic-lts-policies) 

for a discussion on the issue. 

All Ubuntu releases are based in the unstable Debian release repository. The stability if any of a LTS release is that the cycle length is longer, thus after you have fixed(if you can) any problems with the new LTS release they will stay fixed for a longer time.   Not get blown away by the next 6 month normal cycle, which possibly introduces the same and new problems.

### Post by HappyFeet on 2009-07-27
> **pcdoctor said:**
> I wonder why Canonical only uses unstable Debian (SID)?  Why not go with Debian stable?

Because Debian stable uses older apps and won't support new(ish) hardware. The goal of ubuntu is to work on as many hardware configurations as possible, and going with Debian stable would not allow that.

But ubuntu does a very good job at stabilizing Sid. Case in point: Hardy Heron was based on unstable Debian, and is now considered rock solid.
