### Post by anomalousclouds on 2009-11-27
The story about how I finally have a dedicated machine running Ubuntu 9.10 is quite a funny one, actually. Much earlier this year, I had just gotten dropped off after a little Sunday night tradition at work where we'd usually head out to a pool hall and down some pitchers... being that I wasn't driving, I probably had a little much and came home quite intoxicated. I had this computer (Acer Aspire 5315) out on the table sadly running Vista while I answered a phone call and cracked open a cold bottle of Newcastle and began walking around conversing. I must have waved my arm without knowing, and PLOP... the nearly full bottle of beer completely drenches the machine, it turns off and I figured it was done for. I had read about methods of "drying it out" such as putting it in rice, or in a sealed container with silica gel packets, but nothing worked. I figured the beer had killed it, and kind of ignored the situation and purchased another laptop. Then only a couple weeks ago, I happened to come across it, and it mysteriously powered up!?! Everything still works, and all the data was intact... so I knew what I had to do. Get whatever data I wanted to off the HDD and wipe it clean of Vista. 

I was a little nervous, I had come across problems with Ubuntu and the Atheros AR5007EG wifi adapter and even some with the sound drivers, but the install was cake, and it's been so nice getting used to the way Linux works again. My other Laptop (a Compaq CQ-60) has nearly twice the specs of this Acer, but still runs slow and bloaty as I've kept Vista on it. :mad: I'm gonna keep that one as my machine for games and such, but this for everything else. It's only got a 1.73Ghz dual-core and 1 gig ram, but runs super-smooth and starts up in seconds, the polar opposite of how it ran with Vista. 

The last time I flirted around with Linux was wayyy back in the day when I had Red Hat 3.0 on a dumpy Packard Bell desktop. I was still only in junior high at the time, but getting drivers to work at that time proved to be a huge, huge headache to me. I'm glad everything is so much more streamlined now. 

But yeah, there's my intro. I've also got an IBM desktop that's a little older which I think would be great with Ubuntu. It's got a single-core Pentium 4 and about a 1/2 gig of ram, and plenty of HD space. I just need a machine for when my grandpa wants to surf the net, haha.
