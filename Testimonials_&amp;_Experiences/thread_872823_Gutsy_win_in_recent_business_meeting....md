### Post by inanutshellus on 2008-07-28
I was in a sprint review for my project a few days ago with 4 windows laptops, one mac, and one ubuntu pc. 3 of the windows laptops couldn't connect to the wireless network, the mac and the 4th windows laptop that could connect weren't developer laptops so didn't have the software loaded... so we couldn't demonstrate our software changes to our users. 

My ubuntu box could connect fine, but I'm a backend developer, I didn't have any of the visual projects checked out! 

So I connected to wireless, switched subversion branches, loaded, recompiled, ran the software and.... *hesitated*. Do I tell the people in the meeting I have it up, then have to disappoint them all over again when I have issues connecting to the overhead projector? After all, it never did work right in Hardy Heron (I'm now on Gutsy). 

But I go ahead and tell them and hook it up anyway and HOLY MOLEY the laptop's function-f8 display switcher actually works in HH and it shows up on the projector. *Boggle*

We showed off the software changes and made the customer happy. 

(And in defense of the 3 windows developers, they had checked a few days earlier and could connect fine to wireless, so they were caught unawares when their PCs wouldn't let them on)

Either way, some very high level execs got to see ubuntu save the day :-)
