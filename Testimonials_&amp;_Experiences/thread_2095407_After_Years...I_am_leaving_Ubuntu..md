### Post by HighCommander540 on 2012-12-16
Hello Rest of the Ubuntu Community,

**tl;dr Leaving Ubuntu for privacy concerns.**

I makes me sad to type the words I am about to type. I am leaving Ubuntu as my Linux OS for good. I was a HUGE supporter of Ubuntu when I first came across it. Super easy to use (that really hasn't changed) and I like control. I always tried to get anyone that would listen to use Ubuntu.

With some of the newest releases I have become more and more disappointed with Ubuntu. Unity is lame honestly and its all about money. With each release I find that the things I liked are being removed and made so mainstream its annoying. There is no access for the power user anymore other than the terminal. When I started many of the "applications" built-in to Ubuntu also were made for power users not just the n00bs. I don't see that anymore.

Although, you want to call your new OS theme Unity the problem is that there is no Unity in the Linux word except for with Linus himself. Linux is about control and I feel that Ubuntu taking as many "risks" as it has...Really did nothing to help Linux as a whole. That's my opinion though.

Last, the thing that is actually making me leave. The new amazon and privacy crap. One thing that I loved Linux over M$ is that I, ME, MYSELF was in control of the system. Whether or not Ubuntu is giving my information away anonymously or not....I don't like it one little bit. Any company that I have no requested to keep my personal information for one reason or another should never do it as part of the base system. Not even Windows does that. You have to opt-in to submit your usage statistics.

As long as Ubuntu has something that is taking ANY amount of my personal information without me opting-in I will not use it. IDK, how secure you think you are or how well you protect my data. I DON'T WANT YOU TO HAVE IT. Especially just to get ads or better searches for me. If I wanted that I would use Chrome instead of Chromium.

@Mark Shuttleworth: Whether or not I trust you with building the OS that I will run later. I DO NOT TRUST YOU WITH MY INFO. You don't have automatic root access to my machine once I have installed in. That's the point. Someone can take my info from you by force if they wanted to. Privacy's main concern is the user not putting their info in multiple places. The more copies of it the easy it is to get.

This is the end for me. I have already removed all versions of Ubuntu I have from all my machines. Desktop and Server editions. It's a matter of feeling safe. If you do this on the desktop version...What is next for the server version? Tracking my databases? No thanks.



**[COLOR="Blue"]I do have this to say....Thanks, for introducing me to Linux.):P[/COLOR]**
