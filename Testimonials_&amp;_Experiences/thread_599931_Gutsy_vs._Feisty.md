### Post by getmeoutofhere on 2007-11-01
Just a few quick remarks.  My computer is a few years old, and I'm using a CRT monitor on it.

I did a clean install of my Feisty, and put Gutsy on it.  Problems:

a. When I activated drivers via restricted drivers, my screen went permanently black.  In fact, as Gutsy booted up, it turned the monitor off.  I could still log in, because the computer was responding to my typing of the username and password, but the screen remained black, the monitor remained turned off.

b.  Never mind.  Just don't activate the drivers!  Computer seemed OK... I changed the screen resolution... but after a few boots the screen resolution remained locked, in a resolution I didn't want.

c. Virtualbox was difficult to work with.

So I scrubbed my installation and went back to Feisty.  Everything is now wonderful, seemingly even better than before.  So I'll be sticking with Feisty... what's after Heron?  Maybe I'll stick with Feisty until Illustrious Iguana comes out - or will it be called something else?  Can't think of other animals and adjectives beginning with 'I'.

### Post by Johnsie on 2007-11-02
The new graphical configuration is in its infancy. I assume that's why people need to play around with it a bit to get it right. I would expect that after a few generations of the program it will improve. Did they make the right decision forcing it into the distro at this point? Yes, because it put the software into a great test bed and allows people to upgrade and improve it ahead of the LTS. It would be nice if it 'just worked' the first time they integrated it into the OS but with the diversity andd variance of monitors and graphics cards available this kind of thing will need to be released to the public and improved before it will work with all setups. The good thing about the way they released it is that you can still configure Xorg from the terminal. So, even if the GTK configuation tool doesn't work you can still use the old method. Hopefully this new method for configuring X will mature and become a great piece of software.

If you are still having a problem with your hardware on Ubuntu then I suggest you use the techinal support forums on this site or submit a bug to [http://launchpad.net](http://launchpad.net) so the nice Ubuntu developers can fix it.
