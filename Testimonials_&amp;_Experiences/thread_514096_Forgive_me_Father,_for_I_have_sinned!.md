### Post by MadMardigan on 2007-07-31
I ask forgiveness, for too long I walked in the dark. I was a habitual (dare I say it here?) Windows user. I even jumped to Vista! I know, I'm so ashamed, please forgive me!

In all seriousness, I've always used Windows and I have tried linux in the past (I think it was Ubuntu) and always had a hard time understanding what was going on. So until recently, I was an avid user of Vista. While I liked Vista, it had a lot of limitations that XP didn't plus I didn't want to go back to XP. I still have Windows on my desktop PC mostly for games and certain apps, but I just loaded Ubuntu 7.04 on my lap top.

At first, I was skeptical. The reason I switched back to Windows from Linux before was the lack of hardware support (ie: WiFi card). I decided to be patient this time and find the solution (which I did, wifi is fine now). There are a lot of things I don't know about (like where installed programs go - no nifty Program Files folder) but I'm getting there. These forums are obviously a great resource and I appreciate the level of knowledge here.

I also enjoy using CompizFusion (though its in development) and the themes I've gotten are great. It adds a little more eyecandy to the bland default desktop and I'm really digging it now (better than vista aero - though I could get that theme too).

So I hope you all will forgive me for not trying to learn Ubuntu sooner. If anyone has anything to help me out or cool things I should try, just reply! Thanks Ubuntu team!
