### Post by iheartubuntu on 2008-03-01
Recently, my mom was diagnosed with lung cancer and my parents headed about 1000 miles away for alternative cancer treatments for the next several weeks. My mom loves card games on her XP, as well as Mahjongg.

I have two old laptops I dont use anymore (PIII-900mhtz, 512mb RAM, 20GB hd) and installed Gutsy on one of them and it works great. I added in a few more games besides what Ubuntu comes with, made big icons on the desktop for her, added in some bookmarks in Firefox I know she likes (mail, news, comics, etc) and decided to drive up to see her last weekend and give her my old laptop.

She was obviously surprised to see me :) and very thankful for the laptop with some great games and access to the internet on it. Her spirits have been down when she got the bad news a month ago, and her cancer doctor wanted to do surgery right away which would have meant her being bed ridden for 10 weeks at least (not something she wanted at 75 years old) and then they wanted to do chemo and radiation on her, so she told them to fly a kite (all three of those mainstream choices only help 10% of patients anyways).

She is feeling a little better every day since she left for alternative treatments, the cancer is actually decreasing in size, and her spirits were really lifted when I showed up and with a computer to keep her busy. She is basically holed up in a hotel room now for the next month.

I just want to thank everyone for helping me with my Ubuntu growing pains these past 6 months. It took just a couple hours to install Ubuntu on my old system, update it, and get it prepped to give to my mom.

Thanks everyone!
