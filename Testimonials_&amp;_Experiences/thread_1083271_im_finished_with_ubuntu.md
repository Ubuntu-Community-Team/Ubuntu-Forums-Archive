### Post by jbysmith on 2009-02-28
As has been said all over the place, no one distro works for everybody.  Plenty of good ones to choose from.  But Canonical does add a fair bit over the vanilla packages, and I do have to agree lately there's been more bugs than usual.

Didn't say which one you were using; did you try 8.04 LTS?  Pretty solid now really, and some stuff gets updated via the backports repo. 

I've had very little issues with Arch.  There's the odd glitch but that's to be expected with a distro that's constantly in a state of flux, and there's no shortage of help to fall back on. On the plus side it's a rolling release, and updates come very quickly.  It's also very "vanilla" too, so you pretty much get the software as intended from the original developers without extra fluff thrown in.

Debian Lenny is rock solid, not bleeding edge but it'll (in theory) run forever.  Sid is fun, but you'll get the occasional issue of course; you'll definitely want to use the apt-listbugs package to hopefully prevent major breakage.  You could always track Testing too, a fair in-between road that isn't quite as rough. Debian is also fairly "pure" to the source, the stuff they add is rather minimal.

Have a love/hate relationship with openSUSE.  Very high quality distro for sure, but YaST irks me to death sometimes.

I liked PCLinuxOS too, even though I'm not a KDE fan.  Rolling release, but not super up to date.. stuff doesn't get updated till Texstar is reasonable sure it'll be solid.  Excellent tradeoff between current and stability, and personally the only RPM based disto that just doesn't suck (Mandriva's ok but meh for me), and as a bonus uses Apt and Synaptic for a package manager.

But whichever you choose, good luck with it.
