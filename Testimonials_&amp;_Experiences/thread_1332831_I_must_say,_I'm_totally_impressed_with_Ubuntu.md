### Post by Land Rover Series 3 on 2009-11-20
Looking through these forums (fori?), one could get the impression that Ubuntu, and Linux generally, is riddled with problems and obstacles that newcomers like myself are struggling to overcome just to get a half-decent, working system.

It's obvious from looking through all the posts that lots of people have problems of one kind or another. However, I'd guess that the vast majority of people starting out with Ubuntu/Linux have no really big problems at all, and just get a superb system that works like a dream straight off. I'm fortunate to be one of the latter. Yeah, I've had a few minor upsets, but I think that most of it was just down to the fact that I'm a complete novice on Linux, and once a few things have sunk in the problems are quickly overcome.

So let's hear it for Ubuntu!

I've only been using it for a few days so far, but I can honestly say that compared to "other" O$s I've always used to date, I have found it to be absolutely superb - completely stable, a joy to use, simple, reliable, powerful and packed with features I'm only just coming to understand and appreciate (the desktops, the integral "selection buffer", the total absence of nagging reminders, the total absence of potential intrusions, and so on).

Will never go back to using Window$ again now.

Thanks to all. It's been a real eye-opener.

### Post by Dvdre on 2009-11-20
My feedback as a long-time Windows user, and a Mac user at work (never really fool much with that one) is that Ubuntu is extremely easy to install and then use, within minutes. The proof of that is the fact that you can run it right from the Live CD.

In the last two months, I have gone from a complete Ubuntu virgin to installing 9.04 36-bit  on a 4-year-old Dell laptop (my old throwaway laptop) ... then dual booting a brand-new Sony Vaio laptop with Windows Vista and 9.04, and later upgrading to Windows 7 and reinstalling 9.04 36-bit. And today (today!, after work, in 45 minutes!) I set up 9.10 64-bit a dual-boot on my major, really important Dell XPS desktop, my real home machine. 

So why?

Because Ubuntu boots so quickly and is so fast doing the basic computer tasks I do 1,000 times a day. Check e-mail. Check Websites. Watch a YouTube video. Edit a Website with Web-based tools. When you are done, shut down. Bingo.

Startup in 50 seconds. Shutdown in 10 seconds.

That is super appealing.

I will still use Windows on both the laptop (but rarely) and the desktop (often) because I have particular Windows programs that work well on that platform. Otherwise, I use Ubuntu 75% of the time on the laptop and I predict, 50% on the desktop.

And I am still a two-month newbie.
