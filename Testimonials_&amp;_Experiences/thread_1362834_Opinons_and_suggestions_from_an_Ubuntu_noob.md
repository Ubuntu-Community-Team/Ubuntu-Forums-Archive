### Post by Kaner on 2009-12-23
I`ve been dual booting Ubuntu and Vista for almost 3 months now and here are a few of my observations. First of all the installation of 9.04 and the upgrade to 9.10 couldn`t have gone more problem free, everything including my Nvidia GTX 280 vid card worked from the get go. I`m really enjoying learning the ins and outs of Linux  and now even using the terminal doesn`t seem so mystifying. I also love the way I`ve been able to set my desktop up. The one thing Ubuntu needs to do to make me a full time user though, is make it less a pain in the neck to play games and by that I mean mainstream high end games. I don`t want workarounds or secondary software like Wine, I just want to install it and play it. I know the main reason is the publishers themselves aren`t interested in supporting Linux, but it would go a long way in popularizing the OS.

### Post by linux4life88 on 2009-12-23
> **Kaner said:**
> I know the main reason is the publishers themselves aren`t interested in supporting Linux, but it would go a long way in popularizing the OS.

This is the number 1 reason in my opinion why games are not released for Linux. The game companies want to make money. The easiest way to do this is release the game for the operating system that is used by the most people, which is sadly Windows. To a game company there is no real reason for them to care about Linux simply because of how few users there are compared to the all "mighty" Windows. Don't get me wrong, I don't like it when companies ignore Linux users and only focus on the Windows side of things. Or even worse, they give us an inferior product and tell us to deal with it. But the only thing we as a community can do is to constantly contact these companies. Let them know that we would gladly use their products if they would simply support Linux. Let them know they are losing business. This is really the only answer in my opinion. Will companies listen...I really don't know. I would think they would want to listen to their prospective or current customers but then again I'm an optimistic person. Anyways, thanks for giving Linux a try, I'm glad you like it and I'm sorry (I will apologize for the game companies since they won't) about the inability to easily play computer games on Linux.
