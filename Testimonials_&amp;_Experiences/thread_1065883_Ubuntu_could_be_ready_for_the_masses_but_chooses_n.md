Make Flash videos play smoothly, movies smoothly, speed up performance (it honestly needs improvement), you know work on making it absolutely beyond perfect. Instead Ubuntu turns out to be like the rest of the distributions out there. Gets all philosophical yet in doing so you annoy the "human being user" and steps on the freedom to basically get things done. I have to search and research and ask to know. I get about half of what I want, I still have half to go and I am tired of doing it. I go over to my Mac or Windows machine get it done and get on with my life. 

If the GNU guys want to worry about philosophical idealistic BS that isn't going to ever happen, let them. Why not make everything compatible, smoother, and faster. Performance is a real issue. I also have a PC-BSD machine. The thing flies. I guess what I am trying to get at is, be newbie friendly and make it newbie friendly, leave the philosophical idealistic BS out for those who actually care about it. Chase the legal system and fight it to get codecs legal where they are not. Make it simplistic and faster. Make it for someone who doesn't know how to use a computer. Wasn't that how Ubuntu started out to be?

I for one am sick of Linux. Not for the code but the idealistic, hypocritical mindset surrounding it. My experiences are very bad due to all of this. If I, a power user, can't deal with it, then it isn't ready because the people who make it choose it.

A tool to install onto a computer so I can enjoy doing things again. I miss the old days where things were exciting, no one cared about legal code, if it was closed or open it was accepted and loved. ### Post by stchman on 2009-02-10
I personally think Ubuntu is ready for the masses.  On a machine that is pre-configured it runs just as well if not better than an XP/Vista machine.

I use the term pre-configured as 90%+ of the people that use a PC have no idea how to install an OS be it Windows or Linux.  So one cannot use installation difficulty as a criteria for user friendliness.

For the average person that uses a PC.

- Web surfing
- Email
- Office apps
- Digital camera photo download and image viewing
- Media duties(CD ripping, DVD watching, etc)

Ubuntu can practically do all these duties OOB.  With the installation of the Medibuntu CODECs all can be done.

Can Windows XP or Vista do all of this OOB?  No, you have to go find numerous apps or purchase them to make their OS usable.

Now there is of course some proprietary stuff Ubuntu has problems with like iPod and Zune management.

Lets not forget that the Ubuntu user is safer from viruses and trojans that try to steal your identity.

Flash 10 is far better than Flash 9 in Linux.

Just my $0.02.
