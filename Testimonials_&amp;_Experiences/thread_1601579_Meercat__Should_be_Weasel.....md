### Post by TBABill on 2010-10-20
macman, I would like to suggest maybe trying 10.04 instead of 10.10 for now. There are serious problems with devices in 10.10 and until they resolve you may perceive the entire Ubuntu experience to be a failure. Most bugs in 10.04 are worked out and it runs beautifully now on anything I install it on. 
 
Also, when you download a .iso try to make sure you verify the checksum (in Ubuntu you perform a md5sum nameofiso.iso) against the alphanumeric on the web site you downloaded from. It's not always easy to find for Ubuntu on their site....makes no sense to me.
 
Anyway, performing that check will verify the integrity of the downloaded file. Then when you burn it to CD or DVD, make sure you burn it as an image and always at the slowest speed possible. This again minimizes potential for error.
 
It's odd that it ran poorly, or not at all, on 2 different machines, which leads me to believe the media or download was defective. I have literally made over a hundred CD's and a few did contain errors and were throw-aways. It happens and is not surprising considering the size of the download.
 
Since you are downloading from Windows and burning the disk, do some searching for recommendations to download and burn that seem to work best. You'll be glad you did once you get a proper disk and hopefully have good hardware compatibility.
