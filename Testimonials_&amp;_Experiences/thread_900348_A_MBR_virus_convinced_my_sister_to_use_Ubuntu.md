### Post by phenest on 2008-08-25
I got a frantic phone call from my sister saying her laptop has failed to boot. It looked as though she had a virus that wiped the MBR and the partition table. So, out came my trusty Ubuntu Live CD, and restored the partition table. I then used an XP CD to restore the MBR.

Just when I thought my work was done, she asked about Ubuntu and whether I get problems with viruses. "No", was my answer, "and I don't run AV software because Ubuntu has the security that Windows lacks". That was a good enough answer for her to immediately backup all her data (which took several hours), after which I installed Ubuntu and gave her a quick tutorial and installed any software she required.

Several weeks later, she is loving Ubuntu. She has reported that her laptop runs faster, and says that the software selection is fantastic. She is into genealogy, so GRAMPS was the choice of software, not to mention the full Open Office suite. She can't believe all the money she wasted on software for Windows when it's all free in Ubuntu. And the fact she doesn't have to maintain the OS, and it's non-restrictive (e.g no DRM).

Now my mum wants it (pending an upgrade to her wireless card)!
