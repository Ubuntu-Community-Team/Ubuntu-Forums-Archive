### Post by Tamlynmac on 2010-11-05
> armandh
went to a fresh install of 10.04 LTS and added the first of my goodies and it is A-OK

If 10.04 works - use it.

I always test (our 5 systems) prior to installing an upgrade. If an upgrade fails testing, said upgrade is simply not installed and I wait for the next release or an update that resolves the issue(s). Then I retest.

With a release occurring every six months, it just makes sense. We prefer stability and reliability over newest releases. Unless the newest release proves (through testing) that it will enhance the experience and is compatible. Use a spare usb hdd, slave hdd, stick, etc. and assure that the new release is compatible & fully functional. Before you install on your primary hdd overwriting your existing version.

Just use what works and test new releases prior to installing. Another new release - is just around the corner.

Good Luck
