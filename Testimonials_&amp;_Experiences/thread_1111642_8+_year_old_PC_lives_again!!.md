### Post by wgarider on 2009-03-30
I had to share.......I thought this was pretty cool.

My wife picked up an ancient PC over the weekend, at a garage sale, for $10. The manufacturers date on the back was 11/20/00. The specs on it are: 700MHz Celeron, 192 MB of RAM, 20 GB hard drive and it had a modem. 

I stripped out the modem, added an old 3Com 10/100 NIC, added some RAM to boost it to 384 MB, loaded 8.04 and off it went. It took about an hour to add the 265 updates to it, but after 90 minutes total, this hardware will get a new life.

I'm donating it so someone will have a decent PC that cost them nothing......gotta love Ubuntu....

### Post by jbysmith on 2009-03-31
Linux is excellent for old hardware with the right distro.  

I'm a hardware pack-rat.  I have a bunch of old systems that I just don't want to part with.  Some of them I've turned into little servers and with a light Linux installation they actually run *very* well.  Some of the really old ones just run a terminal only, but do it very well.  

Some I do it just for fun; I have an original XBox for example, when it's not feeding movies to my big screen via XBMC, I'm occasionally using it as a MySQL server.. just because I can.  Would I use it to host a web site? Hell no.  But it's still fun to show off.  I have an original TRS-80 Model 1 laying around; one of these days I'll have to research doing something with that.

My husband argues with me "wellllll you could use just Windows 2000 or 98".. sure that's fairly light-ish too by today's standards, but my Linux installations will be completely up to date and still supported.   Is Windows 98 still getting security updates?  Can I run anything even remotely current on it?  I didn't think so.  Shaddap and go do the dishes, drone.
