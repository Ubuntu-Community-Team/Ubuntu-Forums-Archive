### Post by FunkyCat on 2006-08-30
Namaste!

This is my story and I'm sticking to it:

So one sleep-deprived night, I was poking through my files on WindowsXP (hereafter referred to as "You Know What"* or "That Which Must Not Be Named") trying to figure out the source of the slowdowns and weirdness that was going on.  I deleted some old files, emptied my recycle bin, all that good stuff. This was after running a couple virus checkers, and at least 3 different spy-ware checking goodies.

It was when I was about to remove a program that I hadn't used in ages that You Know What decided to randomly pop up a window delivering the message that caused it's own demise; the blasted thing wanted my serial number.

To which I replied, with a resounding "WOOT?!"

Confused, I try to tell it that I'll do it later.  All I wanted to do was finish what I was doing, then I'd deal with the # later.  I thought my request to "register later" was a polite one - alas! My request was shat upon, and I was tossed into the oblivious state of Rebootland.

...

>.<

So, I try to log in, but it wouldn't let me.  It couldn't get past the "derrr, what am I?" state... it kept asking for my s/n.  So I find the number on the bottom of my laptop (an Averatec, but that's a whole 'nother story), write it down carefully on a piece of paper so I don't get it wrong.  I stick the damn number in, and, gee golly wiz... the s/n is invalid.

To which I replied with fantasies of sending the laptop into orbit.

Anyway... You Know What sucks.  I took it up the yang.  Luckily, the s/n window had a convenient link to open *gasp* an IE window so I can *gasp!* BUY A NEW S/N! But I took advantage of said IE window to get to my files, back them up, then I promptly called my dad, asking for some Linux suggestions. (as it was I who turned him on to Linux, years ago)

Several hours, a day, and some more hours later, I had Ubuntu installed.  That was a pain in and of itself.  I tried to install Dapper Drake, but was having issues, but the version before that worked just fine.  You know what the funny part is? I never have to reboot my computer anymore, so I never get to see the splash screen that tells me what the hell I'm running.  So that's why I can't remember which version I'm using.

I've had experiences in the past with Linux.  None of them were good, from a technical "Things Should Work" point of view.  Every linux I tried was worse than what I was using at the time (win98).  I even lost some very important documents because I had trusted it, due to it's popularity.

So this Ubuntu is really the best thing since computers.  Ubuntu has features that I've been telling my friends that Windows needed, all those long long years.

Things I like about Ubuntu:

-It installs without asking me to first pull down my pants and bend over.
-It's zippy.
-It's lightweight and bulk free.  (smaller IS better!)
-It has just about everything I could ever need for software, for free, available at a couple clicks of the mouse.
-The seamlessness of which It Is.
-It doesn't demand rebooting at every turn.
-It doesn't force me to do the pants/bending routine.
-When I click on a html file, or a link in an AIM window or e-mail program, it *GASP* opens in Firefox instead of IE!
-The GIMP! Need I say more?

Things of that which I Ain't Likin':

- It doofs with my Wacom a little.  Don't know how to explain it.
- Didn't recognize my NetGear wireless card.  So, I've basically been demoted in that aspect.
- Latest Flash not supported.
- Doesn't play my DVDs, music CDs, mp3s or videos (i.e. I probably need to download codecs)
- Can't play my Sims games.  If I'm not mistaken, you have to have You Know What to play games via Ubuntu with WINE, yes? This is a paradox to me.
- I'm scared spitless of repeating the events of my Linux-related past of eating my files, thus, I've been using my computer less, thus, I haven't got to research my questions and concerns regarding Ubuntu, thus, I r iGnORanT.

I'd apologise for rambling, but it would be a lie.  Bwa ha!  Now, I must be off to see if I can solve my codecs problem and maybe even have my "Mommy, where do programs from from?" question.

Angela "Got Root?" Englund


* = I only capitalized the letters of You Know What so that it could be more easily seen, so that you may prepare to advert your eyes, or mayhaps break out a really big thumping stick.

### Post by Anonii on 2006-08-30
Ubuntu will surely not tell you when the next DirectX is ready to install, because thats Wine's responsibility :) Check this page out:
[http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=1942](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=1942)
And this one: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Wine](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Wine) for more info about Wine in Ubuntu.
But I must prepare you that any game in Wine, wont install itself flawlessly. Get ready to configure wine and spend some time in the Wine IRC channel before installing any complex games like Sims :)
It will possibly be easier with that VMware things, but I have no idea.

Automatix and EasyUbuntu are programs that allow you to easily configure your system. Its terribly easy to install them, and they can help a great deal. More info on Automatix here:
[http://www.getautomatix.com/](http://www.getautomatix.com/)


Good Luck :)

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-09-04
If you add the WINE repository to your /etc/apt/sources.list, then every time you do a sudo apt-get update you will be able to see if there's a new version of WINE.

The WINE repository entry should read:

```
deb http://wine.budgetdedicated.com/apt dapper
```
