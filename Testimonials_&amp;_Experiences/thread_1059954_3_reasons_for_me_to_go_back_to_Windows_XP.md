*** I can't use my favorite PC-To-Phone services (Yahoo voice) on Ubuntu. There are some other services working on Ubuntu, but they are expensive (like Skype,...) or still cheap but not good.
*** I can't install my Nokia PC Suite on Ubuntu. With Bluetooth File Sharing in Ubuntu, all files from my PC go to SMS Inbox of my phone. I don't like this.
*** Firefox in Windows is much much better than Firefox in Ubuntu. I don't know why people argue so much in this [thread ]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=997919"). For me, obviously FF in Windows is better. Just open many tabs with many flash videos and you can see the difference.

### Post by apo00 on 2009-02-05
Thanks for all your wishes ;)
I just add something more:
+) When I say I go back to XP, it doesn't mean I uninstall Ubuntu. I'm using dual boot (well, actually triple boot: XP, Ubuntu, Vista but I rarely use Vista). I used Ubuntu / XP in the ratio 90%/10% before. But now Yahoo Voices becomes my daily task, Nokia connection becomes weekly task ... Then I've switched to use Ubuntu/XP 10%/90% or maybe 0%/100% later.
+) Yahoo voice: I don't say Yahoo voice service is the best for all. But in my case, to call to a phone in specific country, Yahoo voice service is very good when compare calling rates/quality to other services. Moreover, it is very popular service of my friends, so I can connect to them more convenient.
+) Nokia: I don't see any Nokia PC Suite for Linux. PC Suite has many functions, but I often use only two: Phone Explorer and Address Book Synchronization. 
With Ubuntu Bluetooth File Sharing, I can only send files from PC to SMS Inbox of phone. And because of limitation of my phone's firmware, only some file formats in SMS Inbox can be saved: image, video, ... It is impossible in my phone to save unknown format files from SMS Inbox to other folders. And I can't use my application on phone to open those files stored in SMS Inbox. Results: they are unusable. 
+) FF Linux may be good in your cases. But again, in my case, maybe hardware/driver problem or something, I see FF in Windows is much better, especially for the pages with heavy flash video. I use FF to watch video, TV, and listen to music very often, and most of them are flash. Actually I love FF and I don't want to change to other browsers if not having critical problems. FF in Ubuntu was one of the main reasons I switched to Ubuntu before, because I saw it was nearly the same with Windows version. However, now I see the big different.
+) Every reason of mine is personally, I just want to share them after having some experiences. So, if someone don't (often) use Yahoo voice, don't (often) use Nokia PC Suite, don't (often) open too many web pages with too many flash in FF, he still can be happy with Ubuntu :D
