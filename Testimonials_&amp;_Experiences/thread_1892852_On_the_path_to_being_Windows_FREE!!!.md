### Post by wazupwiop on 2011-12-08
Hey Guys,
     Some of you might remember me from a few months ago.  I posted on here a while back about my experience with Ubuntu, so I thought I would comment on my further explorations of ubuntu after 4 years of using the OS.

     I'll start off with a little bit of my history.  Basically, I have been using Ubuntu for 4 years now (since 8.04), and I am a homeschooler.  I have been using Ubuntu to complete my school assignments for 4 years now, and needless to say I am very impressed.

     Recently, I got a printer set up and working with Ubuntu.  My parents bought an new printer, so, unlike the last printer that we had, our new Brother MFC printer is linux compatable.  I ran into a little error, but I ironed it out, and I am know on my way to being Windows free.

      I am currently using Ubuntu Studio 11.04, but I am going to upgrade my laptop to Xubuntu 11.10 over Xmas break.  I wasn't pleased with Unity because I was unable to run it on my 2005 laptop, so I am switching to Xubuntu to increase performance a little and try out the XFCE desktop.  I did a little bit of recording in Ubuntu Studio, and it was just like using Windows for the most part, just error free and faster.

     After using Ubuntu for 4 years now, I would like to say "Great job!" to Canonical!  When I first started using Ubuntu, it was just an experiment to see how my laptop would respond to the changes.  Needless to say I was impressed back then, and I still am today.  I am sure that many of you who are reading this were around when 8.04 was out, but here are my thoughts.  Back in 8.04, I felt like the installation process wasn't quite as easy as 11.04's install options.  I felt like the installation was super-easy, and I felt like the bootloader options/configuration has improved majorly since 8.04.

     That said, I am going to start gaming on Ubuntu.  I am going to get Minecraft for Xmas, and I cannot wait to try it out in Ubuntu.  I already fired up a small Minecraft server experiment, but I want to try actual gaming before I start handling bigger projects.

     Needless to say, my Ubuntu experience has been pretty smooth.  I was extremely disappointed in ubuntu with the release of 9.04 and 9.10 because the upgrading was really rough, but 10.04 came out and I was really impressed.  I am planning on trying to host a Minecraft server with CentOS, but we will see how that goes....

     If you are looking for a reason to try out Ubuntu, you now have a reason.  Ubuntu is pretty easy to install, and it is pretty user friendly.  If you run into an issue, you have an entire community to support you and help you out.  Any issue that I have had with Ubuntu has been fixed by a simple look through the forums.  If you haven't tried it out yet, give it a spin and post your results.
