### Post by fleaaccela on 2009-05-20
Brand new Ubuntu user.

I decided to try out Ubuntu with a 40 gig hard drive I had laying around. No harm, no foul, go back to Windows when I'm done, right?

I've never felt so wrong.

The install was quick, no frills. I was blown away the moment it booted for the first time. The very first thing that impressed me was the fact that I didn't have to scour the web looking for drivers to install. Even my pro audio card (the kind you buy from Guitar Center) was functioning! It was immediately inviting and the interface looked absolutely lovely. How could I not try it out? I mean, Firefox is already installed. Let's give it a test drive!

Browsed the web a bit. Pages were flying. Seems great so far.

I decide to give it the first official "push". I decide to try to play a .flac audio file. Low and behold, it plays! No downloading codecs, it's already there! Ich bin ein IMPRESSED! Tried some video files. Looks pretty good - good enough anyhow. Tried videos with subtitles. NO PROBLEM!

With a bit of searching on google, I start stumbling on extremely easy to understand directions on the greatness of what is "Synaptic Package Manager"! I try out Audacity, the wave editing program. I write electronic music. I recently purchased an outboard AKAI sampler, so basical audio editing is absolutely essential for me. Though it may not make use of VST's, it certainly does basic operations that are absolutely essential to my workflow.

I'm losing my mind at this point.

I start wondering how to do this or that. Google searches lead me to use of the terminal. Though I may not know what in the world the commands mean, the fact is that the community is extremely patient and helpful to sort you out with commands. "sudo" certainly is key in Ubunto, isn't it? ;)

Either way, the community itself is beyond beautiful. And I do mean beautiful. I've never spent time browsing the internet and came up on pages for support where negative attitudes and the whole "you're a big noob, FAIL" are completely absent. I'm sure it happens, but I definately can see the universal love is strong in the UBUNTU community. Big open arms. It's very welcoming.

GIMP? Forget about it. Brings tears to my eyes. How dare Microsoft offer Paint in their package? That alone makes me feel gipped. It's gonna take a little getting used to, but it's highly efficient and I can do basic things that I need to do in it.

I could keep going on, I really could, but you get the point. My dad has been running and programming UNIX machines since the days of the Digital VAX. The mid to late 90's, he had me trying out FreeBSD and RedHat which made my life a nightmare. Obviously, times have changed to the point where an average-joe computer user can use a sick GUI on a Linux OS and no problems with it. I wish I could wave a magic wand and send a plate of cookies to every single person who has worked on Ubuntu and all the open-source programs that I'm using.

I'm absolutely obsessed and totally in love with Ubuntu! Gnome is frikkin' awesome. I had problems going nuts upon the first day and installed KDE and figured it wasn't really my flavor.

I'm KNOW I'm just preaching to the choir, but seriously. I feel like I've washed away my sins since I switched over. I'm completely grateful of everyone's current and future contributions to this monster of a project. 

AND FOR FREE????? ARE YOU KIDDING ME??? Never in my wettest dream could I ever imagine something so pure in the digital world. The only thing I will be using Windows for is for occasional work in audio. But I assure you, I will hate every second of it.

So thanks. Seriously. These words do no justice for the gratitude I feel right now.


- A Ubuntu user for life! Repping hard to the fullest!

### Post by starcannon on 2009-05-23
@OP
I'm gonna be a bit of a buzz kill, but not in a negative way.
Please, please, please, keep in mind that "sudo" is an admin tool. When one is copy pasting commands into ones terminal, one should be in a place where one can have a reasonable expectation of trust. Please take a few minutes to read:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/security](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/security)

I'm happy your experience with Ubuntu is an excellent one. I write this post not to be a wet towel, but to help you keep your experience excellent.

Be safe out there on Google.

Welcome to Ubuntu!
GL and Have Fun!

P.S. one can always prepend the "man" to a command before running it, for example:
```

man sudo
man rm

```
If somehow a man page does not exist, then google the command; know what the commands mean, you don't necessarily have to memorize them, but take a moment to look them up before you run them.
