### Post by surelock22 on 2009-04-25
I'm at work, so I'll make this as short and sweet as I possibly can:

My machine is a 64-bit core-duo with a GeForce 6800XT and 2 gigs of DDR3 RAM.

I noticed that there was a little "a new version of Ubuntu is available" "click to upgrade" button on my updater, so I had some time on my PC's hands, so what the hell. UPDATE.

It gave me a nice little warning that my "non-free" drivers wouldn't work after the new version is finished downloading and installing, but with the ease of installing these "non-free" drivers using Terminal and the fact that I haven't had any problems since my first month of using Ubuntu for the first time, I felt comfortable enough to do it.

I started at the beginning of my nightly sleep in my stand-up coffin so that way in the morning I could dittle around with it and have it up and running by sunrise. It said downloading the 1200 or so packages would take 3 hours (than 1 day, than 2 hours..), so I just let it run while I slept. I woke up at around 1AM, checked on it, and my install ran into errors when my connection dropped. I restarted the process, and low and behold, the installer started off right where it left off with the packages. I only had 200 to go, and within minutes, it was ready to start the real "installation". It informed me that about 300 packages would be considered obsolete and would be deleted, and Ubuntu knows what it's doing, so what the ****... let it ride ************... let it ride.

The process... was almost.... finished....DONE. Reboot. Nvidea 6800XT Bios? CHECK! Rest of the hardware recognized? CHECK! New Ubuntu loading screen... a little on the basic side, but what the hell, it's loading. Username works... password works..., drum roll... HOLY ****, IT KEPT MY DESKTOP?! Eat a dick Windows! (this is my first time upgrading my Ubuntu, so forgive me for being a bit on the virgin side). Surprisingly, my videocard worked without any new tweaks, something I was expecting to have to update.

Loading up FireFox, I immediately noticed no more "glitchy" starts where it makes my screen look like crap for a sec while FireFox loads. Flash didn't work. This was simple. GO TO UBUNTUFORUMS.ORG, LOOK UP MULTIMEDIA SECTION, READ UP TO DATE TUTORIAL ON INSTALLING ALL THE PROPER CODECS TO PROPERLY DISPLAY ALL TYPES OF MEDIA, including flash, mp3, mp4, AAC, etc support. As simple as copying and pasting commands from the tutorial into Terminal. After about 4 commands, a reboot, loaded up FireFox, went to YouTube and wah-lah! No problems. No problems with Myspace Apps either. Runs nice and smooth.

So Jaunty isn't a crazy leap into the unknown like I felt Vista was, it's a normal Ubuntu upgrade it seems. From this PC dummy: no problems to report... yet.
