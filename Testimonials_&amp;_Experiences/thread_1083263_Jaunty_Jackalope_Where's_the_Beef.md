### Post by jbysmith on 2009-02-28
Toying with it myself.  Visually, there's (so far) only two things that really stand out.  The new login screen, and the notification system.  Otherwise, not a whole lot to stand out.  No dramatic Gnome changes and the like.

But, under the hood it's much improved over 8.10.  I just loooove the EXT4 file system now; even on mediocre older IDE hard drives the performance is much improved.  Boot time is significantly faster, and I can't say for sure but the whole "feel" of the thing seems faster as well.  

I wouldn't call 9.04 revolutionary, but a very healthy dose of refinement and optimization for sure.  If they're downplaying 9.04 they obviously haven't tried it yet.

Well, I do have to agree 8.04 and 8.10 have been a little questionable in the reliability department when they first came out.  There was some pretty significant changes in them from various sources.  8.04 and 8.10 have never worked just as nicely as 7.10 has on my hardware, one of the reasons I'm currently using Arch at the moment.  And that's just my rig; I know plenty of people who have had zero issues with it, lucky buggers.

I wouldn't say that Ubuntu is out of date though; just the nature of a six month release cycle.  When each particular build comes out, it's pretty current.  (Look at Distrowatch for 9.04's package list as an example, it's very current.)  Just need to keep in mind that Ubuntu doesn't make the kernel, Gnome, and all the other applications that come with it, that all comes from Debian, and that trickles down from the original developers upstream.  

Plus they need to stop introducing new stuff at a certain time for each build so they can get the kinks out and try to make it as perfect as possible for release day.  There's a bajillion packages that the Ubuntu team needs to make sure all work properly with each other, a daunting task I would think.  One of the downsides to a rolling release is that once in a while you can get major breakage from something new that doesn't play nicely with the existing packages.. doesn't happen often but it comes up from time to time in say Arch or Debian Sid.  Not something I'd want to see in a distro that's designed for the 'average user'.  I've had one Arch update that completely made my system un-bootable, had to do some recovery work with a live CD, fun fun.

To be honest I'm kinda glad there's no "major" updates this time around, lets the Ubuntu guys work on the performance and reliability end of things, which seems to be *much* improved.  From what I've seen so far with the alphas, 9.04 is very very fast, I'm liking it better than even Feisty Fawn in the performance area.  Some glitches and issues that I've had in the 8.04/8.10 builds seems to be fixed as well.  I'm 99% convinced I'm switching back to Ubuntu as my main OS once 9.04 goes final. As I understand it, 9.04's focus is performance and reliability, 9.10 is going to build on this with a bunch of new toys.
