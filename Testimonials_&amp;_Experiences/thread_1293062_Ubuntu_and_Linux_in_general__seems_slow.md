### Post by cjnkns on 2009-10-16
First, I do not want anyone to take this as a bash agasinst Linux and/or Ubuntu.
I love my Ubuntu lappy and enjoy using Linux. So I am not here to start any flame war, just would like to share my expereience.

I've been using Ubuntu for a few distributions now and I have used Fedora a bit.
Honestly, I am no expert, but I can stumble my way around the Linux environment.

My pc life consists of Windows running at home on a desktop, at work on a laptop and Ubuntu is on my personal laptop.
I have noticed that my Windows laptop and my Windows desktop are MUCH more responsive than my Ubuntu or any Linux has been.

This could be due to hardware issues, graphics card issues, blah blah blah.
The thing is my laptop is Dell and runs perfectly with the exception of the responsivness issue.

For example when I click the firefox address bar (arrow) the menu pops down right a way in Windows. On Ubuntu it
takes at least a second or two.
The menus in Gnome do seem faster than I recall so I can't gripe to much on that front.

But, minimizing and maximizing and just all around routine pc work seems sluggish compared to my windows experience.

It is not something I can't live with, but just a bit annoying when I think about it.

 I have been thinking of getting an Ubuntu laptop from System76, but I am 
not sure I should spend the money if I am going to be frustrated by the sluggishness. Or maybe that is the problem. 
Maybe I need to buy a PC with Ubunt pre-installed..... 



Does anyone else have issues like this?
