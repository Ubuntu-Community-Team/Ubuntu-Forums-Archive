### Post by S0m3th1ngw13rd on 2009-02-03
Been running Ubuntu for about two weeks now and it never fails to amaze me. This forum is a goldmine of info on this OS.  Discovering new things everyday   Conky, alias, downloaded the kubuntu-desktop package to check if I like it(can switch to it pretty easy if I choose). can install packages without a restart. The desktop effects are pretty sweet. After the kernel upgrade the other day a few things weren't working anymore but do this, do that, change this file, get this package, etc  and its back strong again.  The level of customization that is possible here is something that microsoft will never grasp. The day I discovered Ubuntu I was looking to upgrade to a 64 bit OS and almost purchased WIN vista ultimate 64 bit, decided to wait another day or two while searching for something a bit less expensive. Told my son about it also and now he's running it on his machine that used to have vista. This post might sound a bit cheesy, but I feel like a kid with a brand new toy. Thanks to everyone who lends a helping hand on this forum, the how-to's and guides, advice and thanks to the linux community in general. Gonna go and play with conky a little more make it the way I want it. cya all later.

For the most part, ditto that.  I occasionally get the minor quirk, usually with experimental stuff (Wine and Compiz) and once in a while a 3rd party application (same with any OS).  nVidia drivers are a little touchy yet, but meh the bleeding edge nVidia drivers actually caused a hard reboot on my Winders partition yesterday too.  There's the occasional niggle with stuff that Canonical throws in, but usually corrected pretty quickly.
