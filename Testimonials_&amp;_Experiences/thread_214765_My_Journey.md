### Post by phantomreaper on 2006-07-13
My first encounter with Linux was 5 years ago when I was 11. I went searching through my Dad's Programming Books (He's a mathematician/programs on the side) and picked up this book called Idiots guide to Linux. I had never heard of Linux up until that moment. I put the CD in and wiped my Windows 95 OS completely off the HDD. I somehow managed to install Caldera OpenLinux 1.3 onto my Pentium 1 166Mhz, 32MB RAM, 4GB HDD computer, that cost my parents roughly 2-3 grand (Its amazing seeing how far technology has come) and then I moved back to Windows. 

I stayed a Windows User, Really Liked Windows until it started crashing everytime I opened a certain program, RealPlayer,JetAudio, Alchol 120%,Nero, it didnt crash on my games when they started though :D. I would have got Microsoft's help, but me being a naive, young fellow, had as you would say "illegal" version of Windows XP Professional on my P2.Then I was searching again and found low-and-behold a Knoppix CD (I said to myself "What's Knoppix" and shoved it in my CD-drive) I was in heaven for 10mins, until I tried loading a game, I had 64MB RAM then, so I got Lag. Then I went back to Windows. 

Then at the end of last year roughly December, Lightning struck me. I was at my local newsagent when I saw Ubuntu *What's Ubuntu*. brought it home, looked at the CD, looks pretty and chucked it in the corner.Okay now you're all thinking man this guys a yo-yo, Cant keep on one system. Well I couldnt find the system I liked, sure Windows was great, but Linux, I did'nt understand Linux. Then my sister brought home from Uni-She's finishing off her degree this year, Ubuntu 5.10 to try out. So I shoved it in a computer and formatted the entire HDD, and from the fancy loading screen I was hooked....And that's it so far..
