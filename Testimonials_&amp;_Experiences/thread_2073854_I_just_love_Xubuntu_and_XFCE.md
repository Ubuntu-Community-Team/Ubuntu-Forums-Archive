### Post by u-noob-tu on 2012-10-20
I've been using Xubuntu for about 5 months now, and it has easily become my favorite desktop environment by far. It's simple, it's clean, and it's elegant. No flashy animations or transitions; minimal, just the way I like it. I decided to try Xubuntu because I didn't like the direction vanilla Ubuntu has been going lately (I think Unity is kind of weak for a desktop environment, though it is getting there). Currently I'm downloading Xubuntu 12.10 and am anxious to see what's new. Just thought I'd share my two cents on Xubuntu and XFCE because I like them so much. :P

### Post by llanitedave on 2012-10-23
I had tried both Xubuntu and Kubuntu in previous versions.  I wanted to like them, but they were both even buggier for me than Unity.

For 12.10, I decided to give them another whirl.  Bottom line, both are much improved.  There's a lot I do like about Unity, and I may return to it at some point, but for now, KDE is about 95% what I want and Xubuntu is at about 90%.

Unity at 12.04 was 80%, so it may also have improved since.

As point of reference, Windows 7 is about 70% for me.

My main caution is that Kubuntu seems to interfere with Xubuntu's setup choices.  I downloaded Xubuntu first, and played with the Appearance settings -- changing themes and Icons around freely.  Since I installed Kubuntu, however, the Appearance  utility doesn't work quite right.  It doesn't seem to affect my KDE settings, though.

Amarok still sucks, though.  Annoying bug in playback.  I didn't like either of the included media players, and went back to Rhythmbox.
