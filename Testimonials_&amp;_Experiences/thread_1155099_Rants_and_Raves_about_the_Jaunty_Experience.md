### Post by vav on 2009-05-10
Here's my version:
Lets get the Rants out of the way:
1> Multi monitor support still sucks on my ATI mobility X300 - corruption on the second screen and what not
2> Default settings battery life on my laptop (Inspiron 9300) is still 66% of what WinXP gets!
3> Openoffice 3.0 is SLOW while showing presentations, although the feature set/ .doc/.ppt compatibility is now better than ever before! Overall my satisfaction with MS Office **2002** is much more than Ooffice **3.0**

4> All of 1> 2> 3> mean that I **cannot** use Jaunty for presentations... still need a dual booted barebones XP + MS Office 

Now for the thumbs up:
1> The interface is AMAZINGLY snappy! And I'm running a 3 year old ATI card with full compiz, gnome-do docky, semi-transparent windows, all kinds of screen edge sensitivity, pop up terminals, Virtualbox virtual machine and background music! Everything from firefox to panel animation is quicker!
2> The new gnome-do docky rocks... :guitar:
3> Hardware recognition/printing is much better now!
4> Dual-screen sorta works for non-critical presentations! The new config dialog and its back end is a great step in the right direction.

I have yet to figure out this need to compare Ubuntu with anything. All OS's have different quirks and comparing one to the other can only be accomplished in a controlled environment. With duplicate systems designed specifically for the individual OS's. To simply point out one particular function of an OS over another is meaningless. Different hardware reacts differently in each and is dependent on drivers, different implementation impacts functionality and of course individual user requirements/expectations can lead to negative results.

Even comparing one version of an OS to another is irrational. With all the variables and potential implementations, results can be somewhat unpredictable, IMHO it's just illogical to attempt making  comparisons, yet some continue to pursue that endeavor. It's often the first words written when a new user experiences a problem with Ubuntu. "I didn't have this problem in Windows", "My system worked with Widows", etc.

I think one of the best pieces of advice we can provide new users with is that Ubuntu is not Windows and comparing them is a waste of time. If Ubuntu works then use it, if not I suggest seeking an alternative. The learning curve exists and all that wish to use Ubuntu need to understand - that it does exist. IMO, patience and time significantly improves the experience.

Just my $.02 for what it's worth.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-05-12
> **3rdalbum said:**
> You want to switch to Windows 98 - you'll have 40% more battery life than XP! Or even better, use Windows 3.1 and you'll have DOUBLE your battery life!

Sorry, I should have been more helpful in my post.

You can try installing Intel Powertop (sudo apt-get install powertop; sudo powertop) and taking a look at the suggestions it gives you. Ubuntu has a couple of apparently inefficient settings by default like lack of USB autosuspend and VM writeback time. Powertop will detect any inefficiencies and give you the option to apply its suggestions. You will also be able to see the most power-hungry tasks running on your computer, and you could take action by switching to more efficient alternatives.

I doubt you'll get as much battery life as Windows XP for the reason I mentioned earlier, but you might get more life.
