### Post by Lidamyk on 2009-10-01
I dual booted for about 2 years in the late 1990's, enjoyed it, but drifted back to the Windows world.

	Last week, while surfing aimlessly, I came across a reference to Ubuntu Linux. Curiosity got the  better of me and very shortly I possesed a Live CD.

	Wow...

	Partitioned a small chunk of one of my hard drives and did a quick and dirty install. Nothing fancy, just wanted to check it out.

	Wow...

	Moved a bunch of stuff around and freed up an entire hard drive. Did a proper install.

	I couldn't get my wireless networking working. I booted up XP and found this site. I searched the forums, found the answer, rebooted into Ubuntu. I haven't booted into XP since then, and I never will again.

	After a couple of days lurking and searching I was able to get everything I needed or wanted up and running smoothly.

	Today I wiped XP and am single booting Ubuntu.

	I would like to use my first post here to thank the members of this forum and the GNU/Linux community for all that they have done to allow me to do this.


	Wow...


	Lidamyk
