### Post by romansky on 2008-07-09
I've been using Ubuntu the last year or so and was looking forward to the new LTS release. My first impression with 8.04 was poor, though:

> **romansky said:**
> OK, I've used ubuntu since the 7.04 release, and both 7.04 and 7.10 worked well on my hardware (Intel 1,83 GHz dual-core, 1 GB RAM, NVIDIA Graphics, Intel broadcom network drivers). 8.04 being a long term, presumably stable release, I thought upgrading would be smooth, so I wiped my HD and did a fresh install of Hardy. Unfortunately it was anything but smooth:

-Major sound problems (impossible to adjust volume; the problem is adressed elsewhere on the forum. Only ad-hoc solutions available as far as I can see)
-Most of the time, Ubuntu would hang while booting, requiering hard reboot (this problem is also adressed elsewhere on the forum; no satisfing solution exists at the moment, apparently)
-FF3beta5 seems unstable and buggy, and many add-ons don't work.
-The system seems much slower and less responsive than 7.10, and my hardware isn't THAT outdated.
-VMware won't work with the newest kernel (I realise that this is not the Ubuntu team's fault), and IMO VirtualBox isn't a good enough replacement, due to, among other things, problems with screen resolution.

So, for now I've reinstalled 7.10. Guess I just have to wait for 8.04.1 in June, and hope some of these problems will be solved by then. I must say this is dissapointing, given Hardy is being labeled LTS.

When I installed 8.04.1 the other day, I thought most of these issues would've been sorted out through patches and updates. That was not the case, though, and I had to go through several command-line-driven steps to work around these issues:

The sound problem:
[http://kubasik.net/blog/2008/03/31/sound-problems-in-ubuntu-hardy/](http://kubasik.net/blog/2008/03/31/sound-problems-in-ubuntu-hardy/)

The slow boot problem:
[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/213796](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/213796)

FF3 is a marked improvement to ff3beta5

Installing VMware:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4868919](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4868919)

Now, most things seem to work, and Hardy is running really well, meaning it is now my primary OS. I'm still a bit disapponted that it's been this cumbersome to make the LTS-version of the wannabe most-user-friendly distro work right...
