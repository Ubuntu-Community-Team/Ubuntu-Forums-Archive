### Post by DOD1951 on 2006-10-02
About 3 weeks ago I picked up a magazine with a Ubuntu 6.06 distro DVD attached.
I had a spare disk on my Windows XP Prof machine and decided to give Ubuntu a go. Apart from a few initial dumb mistakes on my part it was up and running within an hour. Every single bit of hardware was recognised. I didn't have to do a thing. I added a new USB 2 (4) port hub and it worked. Windows XP the mouse froze and it spat the dummy. I loaded a card reader to the USB 2 hub and Ubuntu recongnised it and the XD card I inserted from my digital camera,read the contents and copied to the hard disk, no effort.

It takes less than 2 mins to reboot Ubuntu where as I can go and boil the kettle and make a cup of coffee in the time it takes Windows to shut down and come back up again and even then I still have to wait for all the virus updates to complete. 

Now I just hate having to go anywhere near windows to do anything and there is less and less reason to do so. What's really impressive is that there is almost a solution for everything and what's more its all free to use. But most of all its thanks to the countless number of people who contribute their time and skills to making this possible which impresses me most. 

One happy Unbuntu user here!
