### Post by deanjm1963 on 2008-04-29
Being a long time linux user I am very impressed with Hardy on its release. I was a tester for Dapper and enjoyed it greatly until it became a little "out-dated". I then switched to Kubuntu 7.10 for something different, and then onto Debian Etch. The last few weeks I had been trying different distros, Debian Lenny - which I found good, but still a bit too "unstable" for my liking, Dreamlinux, which appears great on first boot, but updating was a pain, having to save config files (which are done by an applet) to keep their version of certain packages was a pain, especially if there's no mention of having to do it in the first place, and then Mandriva 2008.1. Mandriva (Mandrake) was my first ever distro, it was great, but I felt something was missing. 

I had completely given up on Edgy and Gutsy, nothing seemed to work right with my hardware, which is not that archaic - p4 3.06gz with HT, 1gb ram, nvidia 7600 with 256 ram, liteon dvd/cd burner and a 160gb hd., but I thought I'd give Hardy a go.

I waited for the RC and did a fresh install on a clean hard drive. Worked like a charm. I downloaded a few of the dailies until the final release, and again, they worked great. 

On each of the different "daily" installs I chose a different file system and different partitioning layout, they all worked flawlessly, tho my only gripe is that using reiserFS tends to kill the boot screen after 30 seconds or so due to its long mount time, but ext3, xfs and jfs all work like a charm. I've settled on jfs, great speed and quick boot time and no "forced checks" every 30 boots or so.

The nVidia drivers installed easily, tho I did have to install the nvidia-settings to tweak my card a little bit to get video playback just right. Brasero is great, I haven't had a single coffee mug coaster yet, I wish I could say that about GnomeBaker ;) Compiz is working great and far better integrated than I thought it would be - after trying to get desktop effects working in gutsy I thought I'd give it up completely. I've had no problems with Firefox or Flash or Java or any other applications.

Above all, Hardy is GREAT!

I suggest to new users a few things, do a bit of research, read the forums, check your hardware for compatibility and don't be afraid to "play around". I personally prefer the alternate text install, it can give you a little more control over your installation and it's very quick.

Hardy can only get better with updates over the next months, so persevere! It will be on my desktop for a long time to come.
