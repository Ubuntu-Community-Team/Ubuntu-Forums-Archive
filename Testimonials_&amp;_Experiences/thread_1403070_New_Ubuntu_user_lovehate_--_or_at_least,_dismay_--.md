### Post by mpemburn on 2010-02-09
Hi all,

I recently acquired a serviceable Toshiba laptop and decided to make it a Ubuntu machine -- and my wife's next computer.  She's been using my seven-year-old Dell with, you know, that *other* OS and hating it for quite some time.  After installing 9.10 Studio on the Toshiba, I handed it over to her to play with.  She likes it so far but I have to tell you, it's a good thing I'm a very experience computer geek or this would not work out in a good way.

Exhibit A: Like a significant portion of the Western world, she's on FaceBook, and uses it a lot.  One of the first things she wanted to try was uploading some photos since the Win-something machine wouldn't do it for some reason.  When she clicked on the link to do this, it told her that she needed to install a plugin.  Now, in Mac or Win-thing, this is a simple click-here-and-maybe-restart-the-browser kind of operation.  In Ubuntu, I had to download a .gz file, un-archive it and then move the .so file into a "plugins" folder -- after figuring out what was needed by reading the code in the non-working shell script.  Not too hard for me; a world of impossible weirdness for her.

Exhibit B: Since we are troglodytes who don't own a TV or a DVD player, I thought it'd be cool to use this laptop as our player.  Nice 17" screen.  I discovered that even though there was a built-in DVD player in Ubuntu, it couldn't play commercial DVDs.  I was eventually able to make it work but it took *days* to figure out with help from a more experienced friend and numerous half-working solutions from Google-ville.

The bottom line is that I love Ubuntu for what it does but I just despair over the notion that it could ever become a replacement for the dominant players in the industry, despite all the hard work of the community.  End users can't deal with the kind of problems I outlined above and there are just not enough Linux geeks *per capita* to leap in and fix-ya-up with their marvelous and mysterious command-line wizardry.  What is needed is more scrutiny of the kind of things people actually *do* with their computers.  I know it'll come and it'll be glorious when it does but please do not kid yourself that it's ready to slay the giant yet.

Best Regards,

Mark

I agree with some previous posts, I just think your comments show you are a bit new to Ubuntu, for both exhibits, as you call them, are simple to fix.

Ubuntu is a free open source OS based on Linux, and it is important to understand that concept.  

Open source:  While Ubuntu incorporates some mono snippets (Tomboy), it is generally only using open source software, so including propietary DVD codecs would not be a wise idea.  They leave it a the user discretion to add them if so s/he wants, and I very much agree with that approach.  By the way, any of the thousands of "what to do after installing Ubuntu" lists published out there will tell you how to install those codecs in a couple minutes.

Based on Linux:  Ubuntu does not and should not work like Windows.  I am sure a Windows user would require some adjusting to Mac and viceversa.  Each OS has its own style, and it is unreasonable to expect them to work in the same way.  

The fact that facebook is Windows oriented and expects Windows users by default, that just makes it a lot easier for Microsoft users, but does not demean Ubuntu or Linux in any way.  Please understand that Windows users would have the same problem if facebook provided a Linux installation by default.

In my opinion, instead of coming into an Ubuntu forum to complain that Ubuntu is not ready for facebook, you should try to contact the facebook developers and raise this issue to their attention, so they can improve their support for Linux.  If they see a potential in our community, they will make an effort.  As long as we are silent, we don't count.
