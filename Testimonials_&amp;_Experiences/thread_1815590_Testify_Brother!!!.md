### Post by zero244 on 2011-07-31
Ventrical: I am very impressed you saw the possibility to res-erect hardware you found by the side of the road.
And then was able to get it up and running.
kudos for your efforts.

So much computer hardware gets trashed that could still be used.

Windows and Windows Games are part of the problem.....both keep pushing the hardware envelope which forces people to discard hardware that works but is not powerful enough.

I still have several Socket A cpu's and 184 pin ram, but the motherboards are scarce.
You can only get ide hard drives in smaller sizes. And even the sata drives are moving to newer versions that may not be compatible.

Then if you buy the latest greatest hardware, it doesn't support Windows XP. So its Windows Vista or Windows 7.

This is where the choice to go Ubuntu comes in.
If you want to get off that hardware upgrade conveyor belt Linux is a good choice.
