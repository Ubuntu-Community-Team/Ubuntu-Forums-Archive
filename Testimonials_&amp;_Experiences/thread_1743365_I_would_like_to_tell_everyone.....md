### Post by collisionystm on 2011-04-29
That I love UNITY. 

I have been using Ubuntu since 8.04 and I have gone to many distro's. I equally love Fedora and I have always loved OpenSuse ( even though it never works). However, when I fire up Software Center and I see easily accessible software just pop up right in front of me, it makes me happy. I hate reading each detail of  the package in Fedora, it becomes a nuisance. 
I think we can all learn to become accustomed to Unity. If you are a true Ubuntu fan, you might as well stick this one out and make the best of it. Just because Unity is default does not mean you cannot switch to Classic gnome with the click of a button.

The Unity App bar does not take up that much space, and besides when you full screen, the damn thing disappears anyways, so whats the big deal? 

If you are missing  having neat custom gnome panels, check out omgubuntu.co.uk

[http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/01/the-omg-guide-to-must-have-indicator-applets/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/01/the-omg-guide-to-must-have-indicator-applets/)

I installed mine on a Dell Vostro desktop with no problems what so ever. 
I also installed on a Vostro 1520 laptop, and the only problem I had was with Broadcom drivers.This was fixed in 5 minutes with my Live-CD thanks to this forum.... 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=10736018](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=10736018)

I followed the instructions, ran the Additional Drivers app and after a reboot... happy happy.

Call me crazy but is there anything wrong with making Linux Simple? I admire Canonical and their effort to make Ubuntu attractive to the entire world, not just geeks in  their basement that love compiling drivers. ( Although I have been there =)  )

Stick with Ubuntu and this great community we have. I believe we can work together to make this thing great.

-- me  :D

### Post by tubunu on 2011-05-05
I am starting to warm up to Unity and I do like the eye candy and all that glimmer. After all, it's not so bad. BUT I am talking about my netbook, it's been made for netbooks and it should stay that way. I can't imagine having unity on my PC because it takes a lot more actions to perform one action. 

I've used UNR for almost a year and only now I've installed the new 11.04 with Unity and I can't say it's bad, it's just different. One thing that still gets to me though is the Dash. I can't understand how the file manager in the Dash is supposed to be superior to good old Nautilus. You can't do anything with the files in Dash (or am I wrong and haven't discovered how to copy, change name, permissions, etc in Dash yet? please enlighten me :))

I also don't like the additional application bar on top - I think it just wastes space and accessing application properties, etc from it is just simply wrong. Take Pidgin for example. It has a very small window, which I like to keep on the bottom right. Why would I want to navigate away from bottom right to top left of the screen to change the preferences or add buddies? Sorry, but this is also very wrong. 

Can I also moan about one more thing? (please bear with me)
I dislike window management in Unity. I would like to be able to select from multiple windows (of eg Firefox) which I want to open and I can't see that option. :confused:

All in all Unity is a great step forward, and I do hope it will get much better with the next release. As of now, I do not intend to upgrade my 10.04 until it reaches the end of life. (or until they release a more intuitive version of Unity) :)
