### Post by erginemr on 2007-10-30
Free software developers do this as a hobby, but are also more dedicated. If I were working for a commercial software company, I would do my job during the working time, but leave at 17:30 (or whenever we call it a day) and would not ever look back until the next day. On the other hand, if I were to develop a program on my own, I would see it as &#8220;my child&#8221;, would dedicate all my time (day and night) to my program and would do my best to perfect it. 

Besides, free software comes out when a user with programming skills is stuck somewhere in his/her work and feels that current programs are incapable of handling the task in question efficiently. So, such a program would be written first-hand by a user's viewpoint who knows what exactly is needed, not by a commercial company whose main objective is to make money, and would be &#8220;to-the-point&#8221;. Besides, each new version of most commercial software brings minor real innovations, most being just cosmetic (new toolbars, new icons, and that is all). Free software may not be as visually eye-pleasing as some commercial software do, but they do what they are supposed to do much better than those.

All in all, I feel like we should be thankful to open-source software developers for taking their time and provide us with those great programs for free, rather than despising them as being dull and inefficient.
