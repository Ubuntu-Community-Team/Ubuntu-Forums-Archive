### Post by ShadowSong on 2010-02-17
So back during the time of LTS 6.06 I went in search of alternatives to windows. It was really an experiment more than anything and I happened on Ubuntu and while I thought it was nice I was turned off by the (what felt like) massive amount of work I have to do in terminal and I upgraded my hardware (built a new desktop) and found that I couldn't trick some of it into working. Recently I started thinking about venturing away from MS again and started thinking about a Mac (Expensive!!) and found the 0sx86 movement and decided to try to Hackintosh a netbook.So I got a HP Mini 110-1033CL - It worked but long story short I am NOT a Mac guy - I find the doc and expose to be a crappy way to move between tasks (Sorry Mac peeps no offence intended). So I tried a couple Linux distros. UNR and KNE are both nice but I think for all the work they put into working better on a netbook - to me it didn't so I tried the regular Ubuntu 9.10. All the hardware worked and I was able to get most of the multimedia and stuff working. Screenshot:
[IMG]http://www.mark-shields.net/screenshot/finalnbscreenshot.jpg[/IMG]

I did a little work on the interface to get work better on the tiny screen. It handles all the social networking, music listening, video watching, and light image editing tasks you can throw at it. I actually really like it. 

I have two other PCs 
my DIY budget gamer desktop
Gigabyte G31 SL2 motherboard 
Intel 6750 OC'd to 3.1 stock cooling at 34c - (need a little more airflow in the case i think but it's nothing to be worry about)
2 GB DDR2 800
XFX Geforce 9800 GT 512MB PCI-E 2 
Creative X-fi Xtreme Gramer
It finds all the hardware and I have it dual booting with Win 7 
Screenshot:
[IMG]http://www.mark-shields.net/screenshot/desktop.jpg[/IMG]

Also I have an Acer Aspire 4520 Notebook - it finds all the hardware including wireless on it too - I decided to leave it with win xp though. 

I'm impressed by the hardware support on this release - it just seems way more mature as an OS too. I think I'm going to leave the Netbook as Ubuntu all by itself.
