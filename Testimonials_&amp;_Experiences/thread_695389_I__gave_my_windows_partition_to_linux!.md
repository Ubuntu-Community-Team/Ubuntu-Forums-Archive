### Post by adult swim on 2008-02-13
I first was introduced to ubuntu about a year ago and i thought i would try it out.  I used the wubi to install it from the internet and somehow deleted my windows partition.  I definitely started out on the wrong foot but i thought i would stick with it.  After getting all of my hardware up and running i used ubuntu for about a week before i wiped my hard drive and went back to windows.
Fast forward to a couple of weeks ago.  
I installed 7.10 and was dual booting it with vista.  I kept my vista partition huge and left the linux one around 10gb.  After playing around with my ubuntu setup and all of the open source software that is availabe i decided to use ubuntu exclusively but save the vista partition just in case i needed something or for a safety net.  today i realized that there is absolutely nothing that i can do in windows that i cant do in ubuntu!  (im not a gamer)  Ubuntu gives you the freedom to make your computer exactly the way you want it.  you can make it run or look the way you want it to, to meet your specific needs.  you dont have to be stuck with what some proprietary developer has already decided what they think you need. The best part of the  ubuntu/linux experience is these forums!  If you ever run into any problems you can find the answer or find someone who is willing to help. 
If you read all of this, long story short, i am a linux guy :cool:
