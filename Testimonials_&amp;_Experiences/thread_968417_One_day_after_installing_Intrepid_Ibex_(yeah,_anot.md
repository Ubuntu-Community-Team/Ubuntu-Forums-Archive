### Post by ubun_two on 2008-11-02
After a day of tinkering around with Intrepid, I figured I share my impressions.  I did not touch Intrepid before the official release.  Also, my installation is 64bit, and it was a clean install, not an upgrade.

What I like:
- Installation went without any issues on my PC.
- The restricted driver for my nVidia card was readily available.  Previously I manually installed it whenever a new one came out, but I guess it'll be managed now.
- Compiz!  Few additions to the effects, like cylinder.  Also, it seems more responsive in general than with Hardy.
- My scanner, which worked fine in Hardy in the beginning, but stopped working at one point, now works again.  I hope it stays that way.
- Wacom graphic pad works without tinkering in Gimp (have to have it plugged in before starting Gimp).  Eraser doesn't seem to work, at least out of the box, however...

What I don't like:
- Pulse audio is better than in Hardy, but not perfect.  When I was running Java applet, sound disappeared after a while.  Then I couldn't even play any music files.
- Skype doesn't work properly...related to pulse audio??!!
- Strange issue with DVD drive where it closes the door immediately after opening it.  This seems to be a known issue.

I think I'll keep Intrepid around for a while and hope for updates that correct some of issues, like DVD drive door and pulse audio/Skype issue.
