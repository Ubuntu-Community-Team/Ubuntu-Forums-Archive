### Post by 1roxtar on 2009-11-02
I am a computer tech and last night, I decided to try something and use my kids as guinea pigs.  I recently restored an HP Pavilion tower and installed Ubuntu 9.10 Karmic Koala as my OS.  My kids use a similar tower in the family room.  I switched towers on them so I can see what reaction I would get.

When I got home from work today, I saw my 10 year old daughter on the computer and she was already surfing the net and playing some games.  Her first question was, "Why does the computer look different?"  I asked her if she was having any problems using it.  She said that she noticed the menu bar was on the top and not on the bottom, but was able to navigate through it with no problems.  

Is that "user-friendly" or what when a ten year kid can know that something was different but it wasn't too hard to find what she needed to do her thing?  I'm going to let my kids friends use this same computer and share my results here.  At work, I take my Acer Aspire One netbook dual-booted with Windows XP and Ubuntu 9.10 and when my co-workers want to use my netbook to go online, I boot into Ubuntu.  I get so many looks with it and find that lots of people are intrigued with it.  

Their eyes get big whenever I am looking for new programs and then download them using the Ubuntu Software Center.  I get the biggest chuckle inside whenever they shockingly ask...."and they're all FREE????"  Gosh, I love Ubuntu and Linux!!!  Thank you for setting me FREE!!!
