### Post by MlDean on 2011-07-11
I have Had it , get this out of my HD , this is the most un-user-friendly OS on the planet , nothing works ..

LOL just kidding ..
but that can be used at times to describe how i feel about the Windows OS that i affectionately call "Bill's Brat ".

THE BEGINNING  .
My start in personal computing  was in school in the late 70's to early 80's , remember those days , apples , IBM's and  commodores , there was no OS , well not like we have today just a basic BIOS or Unix if you were lucky  ,  it came with a bible full of codes, it was command prompt or nothing  , and the floppy drive was king , our PC teacher had no qualifications to do so except the fact that he had one of his own ,  but we had the choice at study hall to stay there or  go to the library and learn to use a PC ,our teacher was a smart man , he said one day everyone would own one , it would change the way we live and one day they might all be connected to together to form a system , little did he know how right he was ..LOL 

skip forward to the mid 80's in high school , our teacher bought a copy of a windows OS , i don't remember what it was but i think it was Windows 1 , and he said it would revolutionize computing , it came on several floppy discs , the cost was astronomical and it caused his PC to run like molasses LOL, but it was a wonder to behold anyway just point and click  ..

FAST FORWARD 
I have owned Windows 95 , 98 ,Xp pro , Vista and 7 , and liked them , but all have faults that cannot be fixed , i get tired of fixing stuff and the never ending update , god forbid if you get a improperly configured update and it screws up your framework , i find some aspects of windows unbearable ..LOL   

LINUX ..
when i saw the news that Linux was on its way, i was very enthusiastic ,but i kinda forgot about it as time went on ,then in 2006 i had a Pc with XP pro Installed , that the HD went south ,  someone suggested Ubuntu , it was free  ,i tried it , played around with it a couple of years , found it a bit daunting , but kept it around to give it the full test drive , divorce happened , the ex got the PC , now here a couple of months ago i had a extra HD laying around , so i installed 11.04 64 bit on it and i will have to say i am quite impressed in the strides Ubuntu has made , i searched deeper and found Ubuntu studio 11.04 64 bit  , and decided to see if i could build a virtual studio around it , to supplement my current recording setup ,which is a pair of 8 track tascam hard disc recorders , a 16 channel mixer and a variety of microphones   , things look very promising , i still have a few months of work on the studio project , but hey anything worth doing is worth doing yourself ,i find myself using my standard Ubuntu more than windows ,i guess you could say i have been bit by the Ubuntu  bug , LOL   i love Ubuntu , you all keep up the great work .

Keith
