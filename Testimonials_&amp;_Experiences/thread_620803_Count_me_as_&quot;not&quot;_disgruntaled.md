### Post by azimuth on 2007-11-23
Wow, who would have thought an old dog like me would have so much fun learning new tricks. A year and a half ago, my tax man handed me a Dapper Live CD and told me to go play with it. Not only did Ron save me a bundle of cash, that the government would have wasted anyway, but he gave me a whole new way to use my computers. I love not having to deal with the arrogance of the OS. A couple of computers in this household are too old to run supported versions of Windows. Not a problem with Xubuntu. Dapper LTS gave one of them a whole new life and I have great expectations of DamnSmallLinux for the other one. 7.10, both 64 and 32 versions, are running well on the newer computers. I expect them to stay that way, if I stop fixing them before they are broken. Even Granny is talking to me again, after finding I didn't repair her broken Windows, I replaced them. ### Post by azimuth on 2007-11-23
My experience with Ubuntu has not been without pain. I haunted these forums for months before I joined. The first thing I noticed was that I didn't know enough about my problems to even ask valid questions. By the time I'd researched my problems (search the forum and Goggle), I not only knew enough about my problems to ask intelligent questions, I had them fixed. It seemed like a waste of everybody's time to keep asking questions that were already answered. Something about reinventing the wheel comes to mind here. The user base is large enough that it is pretty hard to be the first with an unheard and unsolved problem.

My experience with home computers started out with a STEEP learning curve. Picture plugging in your shiny new TI-99/4A and all you get is, what I learned later is called a command prompt. That is not exactly Plug-n-play. I would have guru status by now if I'd had a forum like this one from which to learn.

To the bitchers and moaners, all I can say is thanks for giving it a try. Here's a crying towel and it's time for you to go back and let Bill or Steve control your computer and your life. (Did I really type that? That is so out of character for me. Oh well, the truth can be brutal).

To the forum volunteers, thanks for being more patient than we have a right to expect. Keep pointing us in the right directions (don't you just love cut and paste?)
