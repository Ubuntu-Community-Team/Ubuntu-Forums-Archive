### Post by MikesGuitar on 2008-09-27
I recently installed Unbuntu on my laptop and love it. I was using Vista and had to bring my machine back to factory setting because of various problems. I never download music or share any files at all, just surf the web and do school work. After the third restore I was on the lookout for something new. I can across Ubuntu on the web and was astonished at how cool it looked and its capabilities. I also was surprised that it is free. When I first installed it I had a few problems with my graphics card and getting my flash player work with online video. These forums provided me with great information and I was able to fix all my issues. I love the dual boot option because if I need to use any windows stuff I still can. I may however get rid of windows all together eventually, but I am still getting a feel for Ubuntu. Some questions though that I still have pertain to security and virus protection. On Vista I have PCillen Internet security and some other virus protection stuff. I have heard that I do not need any of this with ubuntu, but my experience with Windows has left me skeptical. If anyone has any info on this please feel free to let me know. All in all I am very pleased with Ubuntu. The desktop cube is really cool and fun but I have yet to have a real reason to have it (besides using it as a conversation starter hahaha). It is an easy OS to get used to and I have had no problem with transferring work I do in it to other computers. I have not had any problems downloading updates either, however I am not much of a techie so I tend not to DL many optional updates because I simply have no idea what I am downloading. In conclusion, Ubuntu is great and I am really happy to find an OS without all the extra stuff I dont need that come standard with Vista. Great community here and I love posting and getting help from all of you.
