### Post by Mike54 on 2007-09-30
A couple weeks back, a member of a forum I co-admin asked if any others were using any Linux distros.  I watched the thread with some interest, as I've always wondered about the Linux mystique.  However, at 53 years of age, I didn't hold much hope for this dog to be learning any new tricks.  Change operating systems?  Gee, isn't an operating system just 'in there' and not meant to be tampered with?

On a near-dare, I grabbed a Kubuntu Live CD download and played around for 3-4 hours, which was all it took to set up a dual-boot on this machine.  As I began trying my new wings and discovered all the available K/Ubuntu resources, I started playing with different distros.  I installed Ubuntu onto a work computer and managed to work through the issues it presented.

After twelve days of a fairly steep learning curve with Kubuntu here at home, I decided to try an Ubuntu Live CD on for size.  And it was a pretty good fit, so I switched over on Friday evening.  The Gnome environment just 'feels' better to me.

I'm still dual-booting, but I've spent considerable time installing packages that will allow me to do everything I want/need to do in Ubuntu.  I'm going to make every effort to not drop back into Window$ for anything and see how long I can make it last.

I want to thank the members of this community for their participation.  I've picked up so many helpful hints and instructions here.  When I couldn't get the screen resolution I wanted, a two minute search pointed me in the right direction.  I logged in here this morning and saw someone asking for help with DVD playback.  I tossed a DVD in the drawer and sure enough, it wouldn't play.  I followed the instructions I found in that thread and within 5 minutes everything was working as it should.

I hope the time will come when I will be able to help other forum members with my own Ubuntu experiences.  But for now, I will try to help by saying I was wrong and an old dog ***can*** learn new tricks.  If you're reading this and wondering about whether or not to try Ubuntu, let me encourage you to take the plunge.  If I can do this, anyone can.  I hope everyone will get an opportunity to experience everything Ubuntu has to offer.  Download some Live CD's of different distros and see which one of them seems best to you.  Then give it a go.  I like how it feels so good to just download software packages without having to prove I'm not a thieving pirate.

As I've said before, I'm not looking to topple any empires with this change.  I'm quite sure Bill Gate$ doesn't miss me.  But I'm not missing him, either. :lolflag:
