### Post by dgray_from_dc on 2008-09-25
I rescued an old Sony VAIO PCG-505JL with a 733Mhz CPU, 128MB of RAM from my aunt.  She was ready to throw it away because of its age and a corrupt 10GB hard drive.    I swapped out the HDD and took my new piece of free hardware for a spin.

  This machine crawled when it was running Windows because of all of the anti-virus/firewall software required to run it safely.    I figured if I was going get any *real* use out of this thing, Linux (particularly Xubuntu) was my answer.  I decided to install Xubuntu to see whether or not I could coax a few more years of use out of this little puppy.  Not Feisty nor Edgy nor Dapper would install because they couldn't find the CD/DVD-ROM drive built into slim dock after booting.  I never got around to trying any other media and instead settled for a crappy Windows laptop for several months.

  Fortunately, not long after trying Feisty, Gutsy was released.  Gutsy installed without a hitch.  After a few tweaks on the screen resolution, I had every bit of hardware (even a few things Windows couldn't find) configured and ready to use.  To my suprise, I was able to simply plug in my off-brand 802.11b PCMCIA card and it "just worked."  There was one problem, I couldn't play any video.  I installed VLC and attempted to play DVDs and various video clips, and all I got was choppy snapshots accompanied by jumpy audio.  I was still able to use this machine for web browsing and other every day computing tasks, so I kept it around for when my wife was hogging our desktop Ubuntu install.  I was reluctant to upgrade to Hardy because the laptop was stable and functioned well and I didn't want to risk losing a descent general purpose laptop to a release that might be even more power hungry.

  After several months of staring at the upgrade button in the upgrade manager and wanting more from my laptop, I decided to give Hardy a try.  After upgrading, I noticed a few things had changed.  My install had gone back to some of the default apps and taken away some of those I preferred i.e. Konsole for Xfce-terminal and a few more.  Other than that, the laptop was in pretty much the same shape as it was with Gutsy.  Last night, just for the heck of it, I tried to play a DVD and to my suprise, it worked fine!  Just to be sure, I went to my music video collection and threw every type of video codec I could find at this thing.  They all played normally!

  My VAIO has just gone from being a general purpose laptop to the all purpose laptop I had originally hopped it could be and I expect to use it for quite some time to come with this 8.04 LTS release.
