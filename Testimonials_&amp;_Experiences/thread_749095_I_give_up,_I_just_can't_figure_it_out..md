### Post by azimuth on 2008-04-08
Hope nobody minds if I make a short story long. After nearly 2 years, my daughter-in-law returned my old Celeron 550 laptop. I wiped the nearly broken XP off of it and after 4 or 5 false starts, I got it up and running with Xubuntu 6.06. I no more than got it setup just like I wanted, when the wife's old Win98SE desktop finally plugged up solid. So you know who got the lappy. Naturally she had to have nothing newer than Win98SE so she could use the obsolete scanner and it had to be Windows so she could use her Cannon Pixma (not Linux compatible). So here I am with an antique PII 450 that dual boots Xubuntu 6.06 and Win98SE. I wipe the Windows drive and start playing. I had Xubuntu 8.4 Beta, that I had planned for the laptop, before it was commandeered. Not Good! It installed and ran rather well until I downloaded the updates, broke xorg badly and I didn't feel like fixing it. I played with DSL, but didn't have much luck with the wireless. Puppy turned out to work fine on it, so I installed it over the Xubuntu 6.06 (which had lost it's Panel and I didn't feel like fixing it either). Now that I had a usable Linux computer, I felt I could go back to playing with the Beta. 

The Xubuntu Beta won't boot. The Puppy's install had torched the boot sector for the Beta. I started a recovery of the Xubuntu Beta and stopped about the second screen into it. The Xubuntu Beta was in bad need of an xorg repair, besides the boot repair. As long as I had a working Linux on it I could just keep playing with distros on the second hard drive. Out comes the Ubuntu 7.10 LiveCD.  No go, hangs before it ever gets to the desktop. I ain't through yet, I still had a Ubuntu Beta alternate install disk that is destined for my main Desktop. What the h3ll, I can't no more than break it. I installed the main Ubuntu Gnome desktop over the Xubuntu. Install went well, everything worked. Video, audio, wireless, everything worked! It was fast too. A lot faster than a computer of that vintage is expected to run. It even worked after the 400+ updates that it got at the first try, that was amazing. Not only was it working and fast, the Gnome 2 desktop was even faster than the installed Puppy, which was faster than Xubuntu 6.06, which was faster than Win98SE.

What I give up on, is trying to figure out why the Ubuntu devs are trying to destroy the computer industry. Don't they know that each new version is supposed to be bloated, slower and hardware hungry so we will buy new boxes and more memory. Why, if the final release of Hardy is no worse than this, my 10 year old PII is good for another 10 years. The hardware vendors are going to starve. The folks in Redmond don't do it that way.
