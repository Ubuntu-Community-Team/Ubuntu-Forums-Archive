### Post by Objekt on 2010-05-11
Over the weekend, I gave my mom an Acer Aspire One netbook (model D250) loaded with Ubuntu Netbook Remix 9.10 as well as the Windows XP Home install it shipped with.  The default is to boot Ubuntu Netbook Remix.  As it's been such a success, I don't think the Windows XP install is going to see much use.

Mom is, to put it kindly, not technically inclined.  Even so, she had no trouble figuring out UNR.  I had to show her one or two things, but she seemed particularly appreciative of the way UNR's "menus on the left" GUI makes use of the limited screen space.

I set up user accounts so that she gets logged in automatically.  I'd rather she logged in manually each time, but I have tried it before & it is just impossible to get her to understand any concept of security.  Too many years using Windows systems, I guess.

I also have my own account on the netbook, with full admin privileges, just in case I need to fix or change anything in the future.

So yeah, Ubuntu Netbook Remix was a rousing success.  

The other win was on Mom's other computer, a Toshiba Satellite U405D-S2850.  It came with Windows Vista Home Premium.

For the life of me, I could NOT get the cursed thing to connect to my wireless router at home!  Vista claimed various causes, from "insufficient signal" (bollocks, as I was less than 10 feet from the router) to "unknown reason" (gee thanks, very helpful).

Then I popped in an Ubuntu 9.10 live CD.  It "just worked."  I simply added the notebook's MAC to my router's access list, entered the access password, and a connection was established.  I was astonished at how quick & easy it was.  

I don't know what Vista's problem was.
