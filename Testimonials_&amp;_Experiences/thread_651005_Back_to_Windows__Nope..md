### Post by PacShady on 2007-12-27
I've been dabbling in the Linux world for years since about 1999, when it first dawned on me that Microsoft was crap and, sooner or later, I'd have to ditch it if I wanted to remain sane. My first taste of Linux was Red Hat 6.0, and since I had no clue on what to do with it, plus driver issues and complete lack of Google (and internet at all for that matter!), I gave up after about a week. I tried it again in the next couple of years, with Red Hat 7.0 and Caldera OpenLinux 2.2, and from then I learned that I preferred KDE over GNOME (although, after a week or two, I still gave it up, mostly because my internal software modem wouldn't work).

Over the years I tried various other Linux distros including Linspire, MEPIS, etc., but none of them would ever stick with me. It was just too much work for me to start off with, too much of a shock to my system how different Linux is to Windows. Plus I hated having to try and compile programs (I never seemed to have any luck with it). But last year, I stumbled across Ubuntu, and gave it a try. After swapping to Kubuntu for my preferred KDE desktop, I started with a dual boot between Kubuntu and XP, focusing on XP but booting into Linux every so often to get the feel of things. It was just so much easier to sink into than previous distros I tried, I didn't have to worry about root, and while some of my hardware didn't work to start with, most of the stuff that mattered worked well. The help I received in this forum also greatly influenced my staying with Linux. I had XP and Kubuntu dual booting for about a good 10 months before even my computer realised how awesome Linux is (it eventually refused to boot Windows!). When I upgraded my system about 6 months ago, I knew Windows wouldn't even work (it never does like working on new hardware without a reinstall), but one tweak to GRUB's files and Kubuntu was running like it always had. I only bothered to reinstall Windows about a month ago when I wanted to play a couple of games that flat out refused to run under WINE. But besides that, my desktop system runs Kubuntu 99.9% of the time (except for the very odd time I want to play those not-working-in-WINE games), and when I bought my laptop, the first thing I did was wipe the Windows partition and replace it with Kubuntu.

Now that I've got into Linux thanks to Kubuntu, I feel more comfortable with other Linux distros (I use DSL for a few server computers at home). But for a while yet at least, Kubuntu is still my favourite. Thanks Mark!

'Shady
