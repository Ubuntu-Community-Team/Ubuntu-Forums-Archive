### Post by arvevans on 2010-02-08
We can all hope that with removal of Gimp from the next .iso, there will be plenty of room to make the tiny wvdial available as part of the live CD release.  There are still valid situations where dial-up is the only way to get a new installation up and working.  The most prevalent of these is in needing a way to access your ISP's help desk if their DSL or Cable modem does not work out-of-the-box.  Also, some ISPs still offer modem access as a back-up method in case DSL or Cable modem goes down.  How else are you going to work with the help desk to get your high-speed systems back on-line if you don't have a dial-up alternative?

In addition to possibly needing dial-up capability, there is the use of dial-out for support of SMS origination and reception via landline, and many of the landline based functions of VoIP systems.

I know that some are going to say that you can always use your cell phone as a data line, but many currently marketed cell phones still do not support Internet access via a USB port on the phone. Dial-up may be not very heavily used, but it is still a relevant tool for those who have the skills to use it.  I'm fairly sure that the Ubuntu people are aware of this thread, and are looking at a way to make room for dial-up software on the live-CD.
