### Post by gnomeuser on 2009-09-28
> **hoppipolla said:**
> I think the man makes a good point.

However, I'm not sure if Fedora is designed to be friendly, is it?

It's not really unfriendly but it's also not always a determining factor for a first cut of some given technology and usability bugs are still bug so they will be worked on if they are filed. The Fedora people tend to prefer pushing a technology out there so it can prove itself and expose problems. They also aren't afraid of acceptable regressions at the expense of technological progress. 

Overall though I think it is a fairly usable desktop, there are a few rough edges here and there but efforts are being undertaken to polish those up. I would rate it second only to Ubuntu on this front but the underlying technology is much newer and the developers working on it are the upstream maintainers. This is one reason I strongly prefer e.g. the Fedora kernels to the Ubuntu ones.

I must though warn people to not install Fedora 12 yet, it is still in development and this cycle has been especially rough in my experience and from that of my testers. Fedora 11 should be solid though and a very good base and I am sure that in time Fedora 12 will attain the same stability.

Fedora are also very good at fostering distribution agnostic projects when they need to replace a given set of technologies. E.g. there was a desire to replace the initrd tools recently and it was acknowledged that every distribution basically builds their own. Thus in the process of replacing the aging Fedora tools Dracut was born as a project that would be suitable for every distro. This is one of the things I love the most about Fedora, they try to approach new solutions with the aim to let them apply to everyone. Prior examples include smolt, plymouth and PackageKit. I think this is the road ahead to furthering Linux and I am sad to see Ubuntu so uninterested in joining such efforts consistently.
