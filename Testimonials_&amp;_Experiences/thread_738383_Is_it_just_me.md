### Post by unknown03 on 2008-03-28
I've noticed that the Linux/Ubuntu community and the Windows community are like night and day.

I've been using Linux off and on for a few years now, never really plunging into it too deeply -- mainly because of Cakewalks Sonar and a legion of games. 
But I've noticed that I've yet to see someone flamed in the Linux community who has a problem and asks for help. I've yet to see anyone called a n00b if they make a mistake with programming/gaming. And I've yet to see anyone threatened by immature snot-nosed brats..

Thats when I realised the great deal of respect I have had for Linux. Its powerful enough to do whatever you desire, and just complicated enough so that 80% of sheep cant use it.. which is a topic in itself..

It takes a while to get use to, and I'm learning every day -- but the rewards are knowledge and power (not literally) - and I'm willing to waste the time..
