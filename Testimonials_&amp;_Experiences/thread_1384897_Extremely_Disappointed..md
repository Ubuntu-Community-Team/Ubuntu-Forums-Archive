### Post by Ironman1965 on 2010-01-18
I've dabbled on and off with Linux for probably 10 year now.  I love the concept behind it, and it's potential is most definitely high.  I was attracted back this time after seeing a Compiz demo on YT.  I figured I'd give it another shot as a home theater frontend.  I have to say that after a week of trying to get a single piece of hardware functioning (USB-UIRT) I'm ready to give up.  I keep getting the checksum error and the device won't read remote codes, period.  It's not like I haven't given it an honest effort either.  I've read soooo many woeful stories of users trying to get their IR's working with no success my eyes feel like they are going to explode.  I'm not even going to bother asking for help either, because I figure if the hundreds of other posts for help that I've read looking for answers, haven't found one, I highly doubt I'll be any luckier.  I'm truely amazed at what Linux "can" do.  I actually had 2 HD movies playing on the cube at the same time.  That's a big WoW!!!  It's all for nought though if a user can't plugin and use "common" hardware.  I've read over the last couple years what a great Media Center Linux makes.  Rubbish!  From what I've read, and it's been quite considerable, Linux has had nothing but problems with IR devices.  Once a HTPC is set up the ultimate goal other than maintenance is to store the keyboard away in place of a remote.  I know I'm probably on a bit of a rant but after 2 weeks 4 different distro's and all but one version of Ubuntu and both Karmik and Jaunty in that I've earned my voice.  I'd love to be a full time Linux user, but I cant commit myself to something where a problem can't be resolved between major releases.  I'm going to wind this up on a valuable bit of advice to the Linux developing community.  You're doing an awesome job at developing a beautiful and powerful OS.  Light years ahead of the rest.  I have to tell you though a Lamborghini is a beautiful and powerful machine, but if it has just one flat tire that never seems to get fixed, it's nothing more than a real pretty chicken coop.  I'd love love to see this OS reach it's full potential, but I really don't believe that will ever happen if it can't strengthen it's base, which is the I/O devices that people are going to try plugging into it.  Video, Hot Swap, IR, Touch Panel.  These need to be more intuitive.  Sorry for the rant but I was starting to feel pretty crabby, and I tried to be constructive as well.  Maybe well see you all in another few years.  Thanks for your time.

### Post by Methuselah on 2010-01-19
If your hardware is supported it is usually just plug and play.
But manufacturers of certain harwdare do not provide drivers.
Sometimes there are drivers written by the community and sometimes not.

Basically, the hardware that does not work with Ubuntu (and some that does!) is hardware that the manufacturer did not intend to work.
Since I've committed to my OS choice a while ago, I just make sure to support only those that cater to me.

In a few years, the hardware you have now might be supported but what you'll have in the future might not be.
So, for the best and most painless experience, it will still be necessary to be selective or buy Ubuntu pre-installed.
