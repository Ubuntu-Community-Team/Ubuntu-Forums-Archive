### Post by ScottDeagan on 2012-11-03
I left Ubuntu for a while for various reasons, but decided to give it a try again after 12.10 was released. So far, my experience has been mostly positive!

I say "mostly positive" because the first thing I did after installing was try to install the proprietary nVidia drivers. This was an absolute nightmare. I didn't know why it wasn't working, Googled around, found out I needed the Linux headers, once installed my TTY consoles were not working (CTRL+ALT+F[1-6]), messed around with /etc/defaults/grub to try and get them working, installed v86d to try and get consoles working (etc).

Having said that, from the bad came good. I decided to perform a fresh install after reaching "the point of no return" (did not know what I had installed/modified, had no way of getting the machine back to a usable state). This time, however, I decided to try out the Nouveau drivers, something I had never contemplated doing in the past.

WOW! I cannot believe how well they work now!!! I don't know about "raw performance" and I have no desire to measure the performance. What I do know is that the user experience is (in my humble opinion) much better than what the proprietary nVidia drivers deliver. For example, with the proprietary drivers in 12.04, I used to get "stuttering" and "lag" every so often when dragging windows around. Now, using the Nouveau drivers, everything just "feels" more responsive and snappy. Things  like the desktop cube, for example, just "feels" smoother.

For anyone else out there who experiences problems and frustration with installing the proprietary nVidia drivers, I would suggest you at least try the Nouveau drivers - they may pleasantly surprise you - as they did me :)

Only one complaint - 3D Windows (for the Desktop Cube) doesn't work... It's certainly not a show stopper, but I do like the 3D effects provided by 3D Windows.
