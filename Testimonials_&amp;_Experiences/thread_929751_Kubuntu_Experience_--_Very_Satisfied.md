### Post by JazonEsti on 2008-09-25
I have been using computers since the time of DOS and currently I am using Kubuntu 8.04.

It all began around 8 months ago, when I tried to scan my friend's USB stick with my antivirus scanner in Windows XP. Suddenly the antivirus shut down and my PC stopped responding. I thought my precious documents would be lost in a viral infection. Thankfully, my files are intact and though I can't attribute the sudden malfunction to a virus, I thought I had enough. 

Since Ubuntu 5.10 I have been trying the Live CDs so I am familiar with Ubuntu and I have been researching about Ubuntu as well, but I have always been afraid of installing and configuring an unfamiliar OS. 

But then I took the plunge and bought a new hard drive and installed Ubuntu 7.10. It took me a while to get used to it, and I marveled how easy it is to adapt to it. All I had to give up in Windows are the games, but since I have little time to play anyway, it mattered little. 

The only thing I didn't like about Ubuntu is that things seems to be scattered everywhere, it's rather difficult to be familiar with and that the interface is lacking in aesthetics.

After several months of using Ubuntu 7.10, I reformatted and installed Kubuntu 8.04. And I have little regret about the switch. 

I found Kubuntu to be swifter, more functional and more attractive looking than Ubuntu. KDE wins over GNOME for me. Everything looked stylish and pleasing to the eyes. Not only that, but I found that setting up the system is a breeze, probably because settings are in one place and are easy to set up. KDE's similarities with Windows, like it or not, is a plus factor for people such as me who do not want to dabble much on the system, rather, we want our system to be up and running ASAP without us having to delve deep into the system first.

And so for months I have been using Open Office (and sometimes Google Docs!) to type and edit documents and presentations, Firefox to browse the web (I hope to try out Chrome too!), Amarok to listen to music, Kaffeine to watch videos, Korganizer for my tasks, KTorrent for downloads, and KPDF to view pdf files. That's all I do with my PC and Kubuntu helps me accomplish those tasks as easily as -- if not easier than -- Windows XP.

Overall, I am a satisfied Kubuntu 8.04 user. Perhaps in the future, they can improve it with automatic installation of printer drivers (it detected my printer but didn't install the driver), easier means of applying themes, less painful installation of video drivers (I had to use Envy NG) and means to synchronize with Symbian mobile phones (because Nokia is too lazy to release a Linux version of its PC Suite, but hey, I hope Android will make its way into mainstream phones).

To anyone who just edits documents, sufs the web, organizes tasks, reads ebooks, watches videos and listens to music, I highly recommend you switch to Kubuntu.

### Post by JazonEsti on 2008-10-17
Here's another Kubuntu experience:

My printer used to be Epson Stylus C67. I must say that **the Linux drivers are better than the Windows drivers**. Linux drievrs allowed me to be flexible in printing: I can choose draft, economy, standard, photo, etc. The economy prints are actually readable, unlike in Windows where if you print in economy mode you can only see faint traces of ink, so I was forced to print using standard and of course, this wasted a lot of ink when I just want to prints drafts! Linux offered me printing flexibility that enabled me to save on ink.

Now, after around 4 years of service, my C67's magenta nozzle just broke down. No amount of cleaning could clear the clog. And from previous experience, asking Epson to replace the print heads means that I have to spend as much as I would on a new printer. So I decided to buy a new printer.

My first choice was Epson T10, but I cannot find documentation that it works already in Kubuntu. So I opted instead for an HP D2560. I must admit I was more than a little anxious about my purchase because the printer is relatively new. But a quick search in the forums and download of the proper HPLIP enabled me to print no hassle (so far!). And there are even more printing options here, so I expect to save on ink.

I just love Kubuntu! I am looking forward to 8.10.

### Post by JazonEsti on 2009-02-04
It's been a year since I made the switch from WinXP to Linux (Ubuntu Feb 2008, then Kubuntu Sep 2008 ).

I'm glad to say I'm still using Kubuntu and for a year of using Linux, I have experienced an amazing peace of mind that I never felt with Windows. No more worries about viruses. No more need to defragment. Updates are a breeze. Old hardware is still alive and kicking. 

About the only anxiety I experienced with Linux is when I bought a bluetooth adapter that according to its box is supported only by Windows. I plugged it in a USB port and -- presto! -- it was automatically detected. No need to install drivers, unlike in Windows. 

I wish that within a year, there would be an easy to set-up program for synchronizing my Nokia E63 and Kubuntu, and that Windows games would be Linux compatible. 

I look forward to years of using Linux.
