### Post by CydoniaRaven on 2006-08-15
I have been a Windows user since 1993.  I was absolutely convinced, after using Win3.1, Win95, Win98 and WinXP that I was going to be a Windows user forever.  I was about to hunker down with XP -- no way was I going to upgrade to Vista -- and ride out the storm until something else came along that I would be able to use just as confidently.

Well... my computer had other ideas.

It died.  Horribly.  Messily.  I had to Frankenstein it together with another box I had lying around that was similarly dead.  And when the Creature rose from the table, it wouldn't boot into WinXP no matter what I did.

So, in frustration and anger, I grabbed the nearest non-Windows OS I had available.  It was an Ubuntu 5.10 Breezy Badger disc.  What the heck, I thought.  I'd tried SuSE 9.X a while back and while I couldn't wrap my mind around Linux's filesystem, it at least had some good GUI stuff.  I know people who use Ubuntu, and they swear by it...  I threw it in and installed.

What a difference three days make.  I upgraded to Dapper Drake on Day One.  By Day Two I already had my old directory structure set up (I never believed in phantom My Whatever folders "above" the computer, even in XP) and had my stuff back on the drive.  Today I solved the remaining issues I had thanks to more help, and I'm using all my stuff and doing all the things I used to do in Windows.

Three days, folks.  That's all it took -- and I was the most diehard Windows guy around.

I might use Windows on other computers, but I think this one is staying with Ubuntu (or maybe Kubuntu... haven't decided yet).  I'm learning all kinds of things and my confidence is high.  I don't think I could have done this with Fedora Core, or Mandriva, or even the Gentoo or Vida/VLOS a couple of my friends use.  Thank you, Ubuntu. :KS

Nice story, welcome. I had about the same feelings a year ago. Unfortunately, frustration got the better of me during my first attempt to convert, when I - a proficient computer (but Windows) user - couldn't get the simplest things done without having to resort to spending hours on forums and wikis searching for tips and hacks. In short, I realized I wasn't able to Linux my way out of a wet paper bag even if my life depended on it. And it bugged the hell out of me.

So, I took a time-out and went back to Windows. The urge, itch and fascination was still there though, so, after mustering the strength needed for a renewed attack, I decided for a more zen-like approach this time, accepting the fact that "Linux is not Windows" (duh..). Though I still have my gripes with some aspects and my own inabilities, I can't see myself ever going back again.
