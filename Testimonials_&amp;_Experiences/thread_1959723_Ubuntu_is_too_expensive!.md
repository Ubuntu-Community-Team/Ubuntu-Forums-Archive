### Post by andersvinther on 2012-04-16
I have been using Ubuntu for some years now, and I love the whole open source movement and have been advocating it to everyone who has been willing to listen.

Unfortunately I have now come to the conclusion that Ubuntu is too expensive for me to run.

I depend on my computers to do my work, and I need to be productive.

The first victim was my netbook. It was a lot more responsive with Ubuntu than Windows, but every six months I had to go through the Ubuntu update dance and waste at least one day to get everything to work.

In the end I gave up because my wireless network never worked 100%. I use an EEE 1000H and usually when the hardware gets a bit dated Ubuntu just works... but not in this case. I finally resorted to installing Windows, and - sorry to say - since then it has been running fine (albeit a bit slower).

On my desktop I have deliberately been holding off with updates. I was running 10.04 which is soon to be out of service. So I updated to 11.10.

Although the technical process worked quite smoothly I am now faced with numerous challenges caused by design changes.

I use dual monitors and depend on those for my productivity.

I am absolutely stumped by the decisions made by the UI team in the 11.x releases. It's a topic lively debated already so I am not going to re-iterate the problems here. (But what on earth was the value of moving the window buttons to the left?? I know every user has been frustrated but what did anyone get out of this change???? Apart from lost productivity...)

To get everything working again now I can probably spend two days tweaking Gnome Classic (or whatever it is called today) to get my productivity back...

And I can repeat this process every six months dreading whatever fancy design changes might come next! 

Or I can spend two days moving back to Windows. As much as I loathe Windows at least I know that if I tickle it the right way I will have a stable platform the next 2 - 5 years. Not so with Ubuntu!

I had a quick look at Kubuntu and liked the look of it... but again: why spend a week learning a new Desktop Environment with all it's quirks to end up in the same six month upgrade dance, when I can spend two days to get a stable platform?

I am fully aware that Windows has it's own challenges.

**But four days of lost productivity per year is simply too expensive for me...**

The purpose of this post is not to start a heated Windows debate... Rather it is my hope to highlight a problem with the Ubuntu releases and shed some light on the real costs of the design choices made...

Sorry, it has been a great ride, and I have loved Ubuntu... but it's time for me to check out...

All the best!

Good testimonial. :) I think you would benefit from either a super-stable distro or a rolling-release distro. Ubuntu is a cutting-edge, fast-release-cycle distro. If that's not what you want, then you could use a stability-first, slow-release-cycle distro such as Debian. Ubuntu is based on Debian so some of what you know can be easily applied back to Debian.
