### Post by ThomWilhelm on 2011-11-19
Finally I have had enough of Unity in 11.10. I actually quite liked Unity when I first used it, but I find it very clunky switching between windows, and I feel 11.10 is actually worse than 11.04 in terms of stability on my system.

I had a lot of major annoyances with 11.10 that just meant things didn't work intuitively (for example gedit would open each document in a new instance) and for a long time my system would freeze with Unity 3D so I was stuck using Unity 2D, not to mention the poor performance I had with 11.10 once I could use Unity 3D, in relation to the following bug which still isn't fixed:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/803943](https://bugs.launchpad.net/unity/+bug/803943)

I've become tired of this whole "wait for 12.04 LTS before Unity will be stable". I've been using Ubuntu since 8.10 and each release was an improvement until 11.04 was released. I think Unity has some potential, it provides good layout improvements and I like the lense search feature (that's the only thing I miss when now back on 10.04). However the launcher is VERY difficult to use when you have multiple windows open and the whole system just isn't stable.

So rather than moan (like a lot of users I see on this forum), last night I took the time to backup my whole disk and switch back to my favourite release, 10.04 LTS. Today has been fantastic and everything just "works" again.

I might consider using 12.04 LTS once it's been out for a few months but I will definitely not leave 10.04 again lightly. :)

I guess the moral of the story here is that you don't have to leave Ubuntu just because you don't like the Unity interface. Also a good piece of advice (I have learnt from this experience) is that if you are not sure about a new release then why not test it in a virtual machine first? Rather than upgrade then say you hate the new interface.

### Post by Scott Baker on 2011-12-01
These are the steps you should have gone through to resize your launcher. Open the compiz settings manager. Scroll down to the section labeled "desktop". In that section, you'll find a listing for "ubuntu unity plugin", go ahead and open that. From there, you'll have 2 tabs, click the one labeled "experimental". Halfway down the resulting list, you'll find "launcher icon resize" with a slider to adjust it. That will allow you to resize your launcher. Once you've done that, click "back" then on the following screen, click "close" When you restart your system, your size changes should be in place. :cool:
