### Post by dlmoak on 2008-06-20
I started out on DOS.  The thing I liked about it was that you could write batch files to modify things to make the computer work the way you wanted to work.  A 386 with 4 megs of RAM and a 40 Meg HD just screamed! (1988 or '90 I guess).  You could often choose how much of a program to install if it offered modules you weren't interested in.  The largest programs came on 5 or 6 360K floppy disks.  You could even change the way the earliest versions of Windows worked.  All that changed as time went by.  Programs got more bloated and the user had fewer and fewer options, and the MAC was already locked as far as user control goes.  I know that doesn't matter to most computer users, but I enjoyed tinkering and making things happen.  I didn't like the fact that one had to upgrade programs to keep their data compatible.  I hated that things got slower and more complicated.  I went through all the Windows versions up through XP.  I forgot to mention that I built and serviced computers for several years.  Prior to that I was a music teacher (I've had a long and checkered career).

I don't recall how or why I heard of Ubuntu, but last February I downloaded Wubi and set it up on my computer.  Being an old DOS user, the command line was no threat to me.  I had to check the forums to see how to setup my wireless card, but the answers were there.  I had to find out how to set up my DVD player, but the answers were there.  I never actually look at DVDs on my computer, but since I had the hardware I thought I ought to be able to get that to work:).  It took about 10 days to decide to do a complete install and get rid of XP.  I realized that for the first time in many years I actually enjoyed being at the computer.  I had stopped reading computer mags years ago - I just wasn't that interested. I bought a new computer to load with Ubuntu, my first in 5 years.  I bought a Shuttle with Core 2 Duo E8400 abd 4 Gigs of RAM.  From my reading on the boards here it seemed to me that the Intel chipset and onboard NIC, video, etc. should work fine.  Ubuntu 8.04 64-bit recognized everything and it all works great.  I have no problems with Compiz or anything else, though I had to check the boards to see how to install Lightscribe (again, I have the hardware so I wanted to see if I could get it to work).  I have had a great time.  I have everything working just the way I want it to.  I have a wireless network setup using WAP and a wireless print server so my wife can connect with her Windows laptop.  I check these boards often and find out other options that were unknown to me.  I need to spend some time learning more Linux commands and how Linux is structured, but I foresee a long happy future for me and Linux.

(1) get a overview of the most used commands:  [http://fosswire.com/2007/08/02/unixlinux-command-cheat-sheet/](http://fosswire.com/2007/08/02/unixlinux-command-cheat-sheet/)

(2) learn how to write some bash scripts:  [http://www.linuxcommand.org](http://www.linuxcommand.org)

(3) read the man pages - they contain a wealth of info
```

man COMMAND

```
or if you prefer
```

man COMMAND > ~/Desktop/COMMAND.txt

```
