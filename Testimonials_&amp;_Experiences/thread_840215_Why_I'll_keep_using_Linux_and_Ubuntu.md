### Post by KonaBean on 2008-06-25
I tend to think of myself as a nooB, as I have no real programming skills. However, I've been using linux distros since 2000. Like many people, I have Microsoft to thank for the initial impetus that drove me to start tinkering on the PC back in the dark ages: since Windows 3.0, I've had to constantly learn how to fix what wasn't running smoothly. What has been the maintenance of annoyances in windows has become a hobby. 

The truth is I have hit the wall in terms of hardware (pc rebuilds are old news), and I refuse to upgrade to Vista and its privacy headaches. The undiscovered country is the linux universe and linux code. I'll admit it, at heart I love to solve puzzles and to tinker. And while the bugs and hiccups (some of which are self-made) can really piss me off, I like the fact that in sorting out some problem I have learned something new about how linux (in general) and Ubuntu (in particular) work. I have an infant at home and far less time for tinkering than I'd like, but I'll never give up the buzz I get from it. 

The bottom line:
The open access philosophy is a winner, the software repositories are amazing, the Ubuntu support documentation / network is very thorough and its all free! Beautiful.

### Post by Greyed on 2008-06-25
Why I'll keep using Linux - I am productive in it.  Since I use open programs and open formats what I work on now will either be supported in 10 years time or, being open, will have a clear method of conversion or upgrade to what is in use then.

Why I'll keep using [KU|XU|U]buntu - It is Debian based and geared to reducing the constant hassles of basic hardware operability.  Or, put another way, it is Debian with just the perfect dash of pragmatism.

Why I'll keep using Windows - Games.  

Oh how I wish I could just drop that last statement.  :)  Recently I have found that I have rounded another curve in my long journey on Linux use.  I started off dabbling in Linux.  Then using it for server stuff I wanted to keep under my control.  Then I dedicated a laptop to nothing but Linux and it has been that way for years.  That laptop became where most of my long-term productivity migrated.  Recently I installed KUbuntu on my game machine to keep tabs on how well Linux runs my games.  I am finding that I prefer booting into KUbuntu.  No, not prefer, I take some joy in booting my game machine into KUbuntu for those times when I am not gaming.  Firefox 3, Thunderbird, Pidgin are all available on Windows but it just feels right under Linux.  Snappier.  More at home.  Oh how I wish I could just drop Windows and know, just know, my games'll run.  I don't need them to be native, Wine/Cedega will do just fine.  But it's not... quite... there.  :(
