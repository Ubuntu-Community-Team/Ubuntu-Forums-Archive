### Post by Bluecircle on 2007-06-11
Well, I've been on Ubuntu for about a month and a half now, and I will never be going back to Windows. I've had a slight bit of Linux experience before, I've tried Mandrake and Red Hat, and used Ubuntu Warty and Hoary way back when they just came out.

First of all, I love messing with my computer and operating system, so anything going wrong is no big deal. Part of my job at work is to fix broken computers so I'm used to doing this all the time. I installed Ubuntu 7.04 with absolutely no problems at all. I then installed all the programs and packages I wanted and found that Ubuntu offered everything I wanted.

I run my own web business, and transferring all my emails, spreadsheets, and documents from Windows to Linux was as easy as it could possibly be. I use gFTP, Bluefish Editor, Thunderbird, Firefox, gnuCash, and Open Office for all my business needs.

 I had a little bit of trouble installing my HP LaserJet 1020, but after about 20 minutes of messing around I got it printing perfectly. My scanner was detected and works perfectly, my 1TB external hard drive and USB drives mount and unmount with no problems. All of my computer's hardware was configured and running right from the installation. I did have a slight problem with the i810/intel drivers where the i810 did not support my 1920x1200 external LCD, and the intel drivers did not support my 1366x768 internal LCD. I wrote a shell script to automate the switching of drivers when I docked/undocked. When I upgraded to Gutsy (yeah I did it, I put Gutsy on a production machine! I'll expect breakage, give me your best ;)), this driver problem was fixed and now works automatically. I also had a slight problem with my sound, where only one program could play sound at a time, but I resolved that quickly with a howto on this forum.

I now have a free, customizable, fully functionally OS that supports all my hardware, has the software I need to run my business, and has tons of features and stuff I can mess with in the terminal so I can break and fix more stuff. In a little over a month I've gone from barely knowing anything about Linux, to being able to use the terminal and CLI programs to mess with my system and diagnose problems myself.

Also, beryl is great. ;)

To everyone who thinks XP or Vista is better: Either you got screwed over with unsupported hardware, need to use proprietary Windows software, or are just crazy! I could never go back to XP/Vista, I would miss my multiple desktops, beryl, the power of the linux terminal, all the networking tools for linux, tab support in practically every program, and all my customizations way too much.


Thanks for all the hard work put into this great OS!
