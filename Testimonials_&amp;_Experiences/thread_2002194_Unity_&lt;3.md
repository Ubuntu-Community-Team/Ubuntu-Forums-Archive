### Post by overcast on 2012-06-12
This is sort of "eat my own words" type of post. I was heavy gnome 2 user and after upgrading to the 12.04 I was forced to use the unity interface. I heard enough of the negative stuff about unity but tried very less of it in earlier version. This time I wanted to try the unity at it's best. I turned on the HUD and never looked back.

I now search for files from the dash menu, use HUD while running apps and also use the dock. I also kept the global menu in top dash. To sum up I am now used to this interface. I had 0 percent of productivity loss. In fact I found gnome 3 and gnome 2 type of interface more annoying. 

I know there are many negative rants about unity but after using mac and gnome 3 I found unity more productive for myself. I don't need those ugly menus of gnome 2. 

This is not a rant post but Let me say this to developers of Unity. Keep doing what you're doing. I don't want to hold back when there are new changes. I kept myself from using new stuff and realized what I missed. I don't want to be that old guy again who rants when changes come. I do understand bugs and broken stuff but not going to rant against big changes. 

Anyway, Uniy <3 ya. :)

### Post by Roasted on 2012-06-12
When Unity was released, I was an instant hater.
When Gnome Shell was released, I was immediately in love.

The more I used Gnome Shell, the more it just grabbed it. Everything it did, I loved. Once Unity came out with 12.04, I gave Unity another shot. Whether I love or hate something, I'll still use it for a while to really get a good idea of how it works. Nothing bothers me more than people using a desktop environment for 10 seconds and reporting bad things about it on every nearby forum, much like it also bugs me that people use products for 10 minutes and review it as being amazing or terrible on Amazon, NewEgg, etc. 

Once I got "settled in" with Unity on 12.04 (which didn't take long) I began to realize that Gnome Shell had some shortcomings I didn't even fully realize until I got hooked up on Unity. Don't get me wrong, Gnome Shell is absolutely my #2 interface and I have a lot of respect for what those guys did, as it's simply amazing... but the more I use Unity, the more I just... "get it"

I'm a fan.

### Post by odysseusjak on 2012-06-12
I've been a fan of Ubuntu for a long time. When Unity was introduced, I immediately saw the potential and where it was headed. I could see the "big picture" of how all of the Ubuntu interfaces would have a similar look and feel. And, to top it off, I really liked the interface. It was so simple and sleek and intuitive. It was very easy to use. I've been in IT for roughly 15 years and I've used just about UI out there and Unity is, to me, the easiest to use - *once* you get it figured out. There is a learning curve. People who've never used it before will need some hand holding. But, once it's figured out (it doesn't take that long), it's *very* easy to use.

With that stated, I keep trying different distributions and, like others have stated, I keep coming back to Ubuntu and Unity. It absolutely spoils you! It's snappy and doesn't eat up resources. For example, this last weekend, I tried three other distributions and part of the test was to see how streaming Doctor Who worked on my big screen TV. Every one of those distros took too much resources and made the show dodgy. But, with Ubuntu and Unity, the show streams flawlessly.

So, I'm sticking with it and will continue to let other people know that value of free software!

### Post by BigSilly on 2012-06-15
One of my favourite new features is the changed Dash Home page. I've read arguements saying the menus and filters are too buried and whatnot, and this is true to some degree, but if you use your Ubuntu regularly, before long your Dash Home has populated with all your most used programs, meaning you don't have to go through your menus or type in their names. To me, this is really something, as I can just click Dash and there is my program with only one more click under "Recent Apps".

I really enjoyed Cairo Dock on Mint recently, but again the Unity launcher is better for me, mostly because it's out of my way on the left side and is always visible now with 12.04. Sure you can set up Cairo to do this (Cairo Dock is awesome after all), but it's really cool that Ubuntu does all this out of the box and the user doesn't have to do anything.

I dunno, I'm pretty glowing about Unity of late... :D
