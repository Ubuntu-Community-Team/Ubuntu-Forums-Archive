### Post by philipbakerclarke on 2006-07-27
My Ubuntu Experience.

My interest with a linux distro started with Mepis, it worked very well. It picked up all hardware and drives. But it just lacked “polish” I am not slating it.

Then I bought a 2nd hand G4 running panther, what an amazing OS from a user perspective it very well indeed. This old G4 running panther would make my Pentium 4 2800 running XP, look wet. I could multitask beautifully, I could backup a DVD and look at photos, whilst listening to iTunes. 
But,I really hate the fact that these Operating Systems think America is the only country in the whole world.

For one reason or another I had to sell the mac, we have a Toshiba Portege 4010 now. 
I use XP at work because I have little choice and it is installed on my laptop to satisfy my other halves work commitments.

I have had to install ubuntu 5.10, because my laptop doesn’t like 700mb disks.
I like ubuntu, there is just something about it that just feels right, and it works. I have upgraded to 6.06… FANTASTIC. I can now install debian applications just by double clicking on them. I have “googles” picassa installed which is great for pictures.

Rhythmbox picked up my IPOD that was plugged into my laptop via a usb2.0 pcmcia card. GREAT

Everything I need is in the USER GUIDE with the ability to copy and paste into terminal, it’s a breeze. To be honest I don’t like using the terminal, its black and white and boring.

I have had to set up my Speedtouch USB modem manually, but thanks to the forums it hasn’t been that difficult. I have noticed a project in lauchpad to sort this out. This is a major issue. There is always the argument of buying a router, but why should I when the speedtouch is free. It is perfect for us, we only have one machine. The majority of ISPs are dishing out USB Modems anyway.

The future:

I walk into my local computer store, is this case PCWORLD.
Salesman “Hello Sir, How can I help you?”
Me “I am looking to purchase a new computer/laptop”
Salesman “What do you intend to do with the machine?”
Me “ Set up my Music, Videos, play with my camcorder, Surf the internet and read emails”
Salesman “OK Sir, We have a machine for you”
Salesman “It’s a beautiful sleek black Sony number”
Me “WOW, that is nice”
Salesman “Now Sir, What kind of Operating System would you like?”
Me “What”
Salesman “We have a number of Operating Systems, Ubuntu, Suse, Linspire to name but a few”
Salesman “They all based on what we call the Debian System, You can use the same programs on each, It is YOUR CHOICE”
Me: “Fantastic”
Salesman “Tell you what, take these Free disks home, put them in the drive and boot the computer”
Salesman “If you like what you see click on the install button, if not choose another”
