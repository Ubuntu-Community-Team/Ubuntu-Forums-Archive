### Post by LiamWilson on 2009-11-10
I had an eMachines G520 running Ubuntu 9.04 when I recently bought myself a Dell Mini 10v (With Ubuntu, of course), instantly upgrading it to 9.10. This left me with a spare laptop, which I have decided to give to the girlfriend and family after doing a fresh install of 9.10.

Now, 9.10 actually installed flawlessly. Wireless drivers downloaded, Codecs downloaded, samba installed, we were good to go. So I gave it to her the other day, with a bit of scepticism about how she'd find it, with her only using Windows machines in school (This was also her 1st home PC). 

So I gave it to her, and basically left her to play with it. When I returned some hours later, I found her Ripping CD's with Rhythmbox, Typing up documents, and looking at pictures - all without any help from me. (I did have to show her how to create user accounts, but that was all.)

Today, she let her brother use it for the 1st time, and I, like her, left him to play with it on his own. When I showed up later, he had plugged in his phone, and was copying files such as Pictures, Music and Video . He had changed his Background picture, and even made his own theme! All without any help from any of us. Later, we were crowded around it watching The Mighty Boosh on DVD, all without a single flaw.

The only problem we've had is installing the 3 Mobile Broadband USB dongle, but I'm working on it. 

So yes, I really do think that Ubuntu IS Linux for human beings! Especially for those who have very little computing knowlege! Well done Ubuntu :)

right click NM >> Edit Connections >> Mobile Broadband (tab) >> Add

Just follow the steps, you can choose 3 at some point :)
