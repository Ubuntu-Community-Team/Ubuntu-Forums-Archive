### Post by doron387 on 2011-12-31
hey everybody.
**this is a thank you letter for the developers of ubuntu.**
(in my language we don't have capital letters and it drives me crazy to add them so please forgive me for not using it and just read the content)

i have been playing and using ubuntu dual booting for the last 3 years.
recently i have been travelling for 3 months in india and took my asus 1201n netbook with me. 
it is dualbooting win7 & ubuntu 10.10 32-bit.
except for small flaws ubuntu 10.10 served me beautifully, released me of fear of viruses and also helped some travellers that could not access the files on their external hard disks or on their laptops. using ubuntu i could access the files, show the people that they did not lose their stuff and that was the best way to spread the linux message.
the only 2 flaws were that
(1) while copying files from my netbook to external hard disk or vice versus, sometimes, in the middle of the action or in the end of it the windows closed and i had to open them again. very not comfortable.
(2) it is not easy to catch the borders of windows in order to resize them as the effective borders for that action are too thin.
but
this is not the reason why i am writing this post.
i am writing it to thank the developers team for ubuntu 10.10. 32-bit and 64-bit.
when i came back home, i have realized that my other notebook (gigabyte w566u intel core2duo winxp) is too slow. so i decided to give ubuntu 10.10 64-bit a try using usb thumbdrive with live session. i was so surprised of the speed that i installed it in dual boot with my winxp a week ago and since then i went into winxp just twice :
(1) to burn a dvd as brasero/gnomebaker make a mess of hebrew files names, and k3b keeps on working for too much time after it finishes to burn and on my xp the nero works flawlessly.
(2) to use universal-usb-installer to check out linuxmint 64-bit but i was dissapointed from it. 
except for these occasions i am sticking to the ubuntu install. 
and i would like to say that it works really fast on this 3 years old dual core cpu.
and i am very gratefull for your work. keep on.
if you had in your mind the question why is he using 10.10 when we have already released 11.10, well, the answer is that it is stable, it works quite flawlessly, i know it and how to tweak it and for me, in the last 2 years, since ubuntu bacame very graphic and doesn't require too much using the terminal,this "every 6 months" rythm is too fast. it takes time to get used to a distro, to learn to tweak it to my needs, and then, a new distro comes up, some things work better and out of the box (wireless is offcourse the best thing in the new releases, the 10.10 software centre is beautifull, i  wouldn't changed it for nothing) but so many new things that are not necessarily needed (unity for example - though the intention was good offcourse).
so, i had to thank somebody and here it is. i am still with ubuntu beacause it is fast and the most beautifull distro that i have seen.
be strong and keep the excellent work.
doron 387
israel
