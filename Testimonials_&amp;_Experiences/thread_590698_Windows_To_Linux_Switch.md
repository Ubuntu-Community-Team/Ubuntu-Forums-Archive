### Post by abstractcoder on 2007-10-25
I have been a windows user all of my life (well aware of its' weaknesses) 
I recently setup my computer for dual boot Windows/Linux on a**Dell C521**. 

I love the feel of the operating system, It's a beautiful system. I can tell just how powerful and stable the system is compared to a windows system.  Being on windows is like being fed baby food -- when you want steak!(Linux). 

Once you get over the minor inconvenience, such as hardware compat. issues, or the 64-Bit flash issue,etc.  Regardless of that, it's still a beautiful system and that didn't bother me much.

When I first installed, I had some problems with my mouse freezing after a period of time(apparently a lot of people had mouse issues), but after updating my bios, it fixed it. (Someone else told me to update my bios because they had mouse problems and that solved it)


Overall, I love how everything is done differently, the file system is nice, and it's so secure. Although. I'm reading up on how to write good iptable rules.

Right now my version is Ubuntu 6.06 LTS, but I downloaded Gutsy yesterday.  I love how everything is so free...in every sense of the word, FREEDOM!

I can't believe what I was missing all these years!

--Mike (AbstractCoder)
