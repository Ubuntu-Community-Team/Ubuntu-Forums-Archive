### Post by brow on 2009-01-01
So I am a bioengineering student right now and have been using Windows my whole life. I now have to admit that screwing up my current Windows XP OS was the greatest mistake I have ever made with my computer. 

To be honest, the first thing I tried was getting a Vista torrent and that was good for about 5 hours.  So I looked at the prices of some OEM and Retail versions, and I looked at the reviews and decided to take the plunge. 

The things I am enjoying about Ubuntu are endless. Let me express some of my greatest 'likes'.  

-First off, the two workspaces is awesome.  I love the fact that I can set one with music, instant messanging, and whatever else and actually be doing work on the other space. 
-The biggest reason I have never switched earlier is that I thought there would be compatibility issues with documents, and that would be pretty bad since I use my desktop at school and most papers that I submit are done electronically. The openoffice.org app is way above my expectations of a good Office 'suite'.
-The fact that everything you need is included, or it can be accessed from the Add/Remove app. You mean I dont need to pay $200 for openoffice.org?!
-It is also a learning experience. Granted if you are a basic user, there is not much learning needed. It provides an extremely intuitive interface, and sometimes you have to 'dummy' down to think about where things are, because thats where they 'should have been' all this time.  
...But I enjoy messing around with new programs and changing preferences and options so indeed the fearful Terminal has been used. 
-Above all, the community is great. Very helpful. Very accepting. I have even seen some websites saying 'we encourage new linux users coming from the Windows OS, and will be the most helpful to them'.....


There is _one_ downside I have come to....
-I enjoy 3D gaming, and I understand that it would take a huge collaboration to get the compatibility issues with modern Windows 3D games to work flawlessly on linux.  That being said, down the road I may end up having another Windows machine for that reason. 

Overall I just wanted to thank all of the contributors that made this happen, and provided a very pleasing transfer to the other side.

-Mike

### Post by phlembob on 2009-01-01
Great Story,
For the first time users which need to protect data for the semester course or longer Hardy 8.04.1 LTS has proven itself to be data safe.  What happens is people using Ubuntu will get the urge to upgrade with kernel upgrades.  Those upgrades for 8.04.1 will come at intervals you should accept.  Novice Ubuntu users should hold off on upgrades that change the LTS status, and stay with 8.04.1 LTS unless terminal functions are your preferred operating modes.  You see, the upgrades do fix many things but the overall effects on other operations may not have been totally diagnosed.  In Hardy Heron upgrades are well checked out before you receive them.  You may need to correct things with others such as Intrepid or Ibex.  Of course, if you have a second computer or a second HDD, and you want to work with it there is no problem.

The Open OS is less expensive for the money wise.  You do get many perks such as the Open Office.org.  Any student or new business should investigate cost effectiveness vs many aspects of the user/s.

You haven't lost every MS programs.  Linux/Gnu - Debian base has many programs for hobbyist.  The forums have great people with experience with a few bum-dopers.  I am not listing programs because Linux is command sensitive to capital and lower case lettering.  I don't want to say "wine" without checking its use of capitals and lower case lettering.  I do not want to mislead you unintentionally. Good advice turns sour when people try something that is rejected or loses time.

Good Luck --- I was a money wise student years ago.  Education is the only solution to the crises of the ignorant.  later

### Post by matteojg on 2009-01-02
I'm a first time user as well.

Confronted with WindowsXP Blue Screen of Death I decided to give Ubunto a try. Using a friend's computer I downloaded and burned to cd Ubuntu 8.10.
Getting movie players to playback dvd's and getting my favourite internet chess interface to work (using Wine) took some leg work, but i think it was well worth it--I got the computer running again for free! Some things even seem to work better: Boot up is faster, Ubuntu seems to find and establish wireless connections better than windows, and Firefox is performing better for me (not sure if this is because the browser works better or because an improvement with the wireless connection).

The installation was relatively painless, although I ran into a couple of problems:
1) I wanted to use the 'live' option first (try Ubuntu without changing your computer), but it would freeze after loading.
2) I then tried to install Ubuntu to a partition but the Install Wizard crashed and afterwards Windows would no longer boot (I forget the exact error 'file batch dirty" or something)
3) When I ran the Ubuntu Install a second time I think it just automatically reformatted the drive...I was a fool for not copying the hard drive prior to install and I have no way of knowing whether these problems were due to Windows, Ubuntu, or (the most likely reason) my own stupidity.

Overall I am very satisfied with Ubuntu and the next new laptop I get, I will purchase sans OS and just install Ubuntu.
