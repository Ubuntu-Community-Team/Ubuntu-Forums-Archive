### Post by nateperson on 2008-09-19
I loaded Linux for the first time after I purchased a new laptop from dell back in January.  I chose ubuntu cause it was the most popular at the time (prob still is, haven't checked) and dell was giving some support (not for my model).  I've enjoyed it not had many issues I couldn't figure out or find an already answered question in the forms, and learned quite a bit about linux.

After the past few weeks I'm begun to feel that Ubuntu is to easy/userfriendly...  why? I started a new job.  They gave me a brand new PC with a fresh load of RHEL v5.2 on it.  I have honestly learned more about linux and using its cli in the last two weeks with redhat than I have in the past 9 months with ubuntu as I've had to figure out how to get everything set up properly on this system.  I'm not talking about I've had to manually set up and configure things in RHEL that ubuntu was doing automatically.  

So yeah... that's my 2 cents...

Nate

Gee Wally! What is wrong with it being too easy?
