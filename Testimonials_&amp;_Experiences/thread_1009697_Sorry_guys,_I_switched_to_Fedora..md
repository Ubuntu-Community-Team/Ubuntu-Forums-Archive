### Post by Envergure on 2008-12-13
Well, after a day in Fedora here's what I think:
Whilst Intrepid has a lot of little problems, Fedora has just a few big ones:  Most hanously, it looks like there's no Flash for 64-bit users *at all*.  Other problems include the difficulty in installing nVidia drivers, the very strange file browser and the general lack of stuff in the default repos.

What Fedora does better than Ubuntu:
-- PulseAudio is implemented bug-free
-- The shutdown menu is drawn the first time!
-- "System > Preferences" menu is divided into sections instead of having on long list that goes from the top to the bottom of the screen
