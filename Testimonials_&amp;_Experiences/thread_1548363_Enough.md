### Post by tripower on 2010-08-08
First I have to say that after 6 solid months of using Ubuntu I can honestly say that Ubuntu is not ready for prime time. It is just a desktop GUI sitting on top of Linux command line OS.

75% of the stuff that I need to do at home I can get by with using Ubuntu (web, email, OpenOffice, etc). If that is all you are doing this OS might work for you. But I have other apps, apps that just don't work with WINE and that I need to run; eBAY TurboLister, OBDII software, tax software, etc. and I had to endure not running this app or that app and the list just kept growing. I remember one solution I got from a guy on this forum was to just run the Windows emulator...buddy if I am going to run a Windows emulator ....I'm just going to load Windows and be done with it.

Second the OS is buggy as hell. I was always having to fix something with the printer or some other problem and the solution was either another (daily) software update or some ridiculous terminal command.

If you EVER hope to have Ubuntu become attractive to a wide audience you are going to have to lose the command line stuff. There is NO WAY that ordinary folks are going to use that to solve their problems. Ubuntu was billed as the Linux OS for ordinary people...IT IS NOT!

Here is what you need to do:
1: Lose the command line solutions, not saying drop the command line all together but there must be a graphical solution for every problem.

2: You need a product (like WINE but better) that can literally install Windows executables just like in Windows.

3: You are going to have to fix some of the more glaring bugs in these version before they are released to the public.

And as an aside I have to say that Evolution mail SUX! I know it is not directly a part of Ubuntu but it is the default email program (Thunderbird is no better). Here is a little tip when making an email program, please provide an easy way to export and save off my emails and contacts. Outlook has this functionality, it's called a PST file. It's easy, it's quick, it's convenient. IT WORKS! When I saw the bizarre backup routine for Evolution, I just said ENOUGH!

Sorry if this comes off as a slam but there are just things that need to be addressed before this OS becomes anything other than a geek OS. Now I am back to XP and I am home.....

### Post by Error403 on 2010-08-08
Hi,

I don't think you understand what effort is put on Linux here. Is it Linux's fault if your tax software has not been made for Linux? Apple has that exact problem with software that has been written exclusively for Windows. The nice part of Linux over Mac OS X is that you can dual-boot to Windows without software like Parallels. 

Let's have a club sandwich: Are you going to order it, at a cost (like buying and installing Windows), or make it yourself (like installing Linux)? If you choose to make it yourself, you have to consider buying the ingredients like the bacon, the mayonnaise, the bread, the cheese, the salad and the tomato (consider them pc parts). If you have the advandate of having a garden (knowing at least some of the IT field), you can grow your tomato and maybe your salad yourself and not have to buy it (some people salvage old parts or make other stunts with odd hardware). If you're a farmer (are a pc manufacturer) you can get all the ingredients yourself, maybe even MAKE them yourself. Then, you have to assemble your meal (knowledge). You'll have to follow some recipe, do some research on the web, ask a relative (follow howtos, ask on forums, etc.). Also, don't forget you might also come in some obstacles, like missing a good cutting knife (terminal) and having the twist of cutting the sandwich nicely and cleanly just like they do it at the restaurant (mistakes, obscure command lines, troubleshooting). In the end, you'll probably have your club sandwich, maybe not (discouragement, incompatible hardware). But the fun part is that you can customize your club after you know the basics of it (customisation, binaries, little projects)! You can even make one to a friend or show him how to do it (copying, distributing, etc)! Have you ever tried asking a food chain clerk for the recipe of their principal meal? They might refuse to give it to you (proprietary software, proprietary knowledge). However, your mom will be more than happy to give you her secret pie recipe (open source, freedom to know what's under the hood)!!

All in all, Microsoft just wishes everyone ate at McDonald's.

I might be good in computers and linux and customising my sandwich (linx), but god do I suck at plumbing! However, I still can buy a commercial solution and ask a plumber to come and do the fix, or I can try to do the fix by myself... I know I can search for howtos and if I'm really stuck, I'll call my handy-know-all brother in law ;) The same goes for any other field that has profesionals for, like car mecanics! Also, in most fields, there's consultants that will be happy to help you have the job done, without doing it themself, just like Ubuntu's pay-for support. At least Ubuntu gives you the CHOICE to pay for the support, or ask for it on the forums, and hope someone knows the solution and answers back for free.

I know the sandwich example might have issues, but I just hope you get the general idea between a whole closed-source solution and an open-source solution. If you feel like always eating at Mc Donald's or some other food chain, then open source is just not for you. I, like many others surely, opt for the middle: I like to tinkle with linux and free apps, just like cooking my own meals, but sometimes I boot to Windows to play my Steam games, especially on delivered-pizza fridays!  ;)

Who's hungry?
