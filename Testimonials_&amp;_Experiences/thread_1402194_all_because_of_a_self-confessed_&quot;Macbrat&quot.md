### Post by ubunterooster on 2010-02-08
It was Aug.13,2008 when a Mac-using friend was trying to covince me to ditch Flashy-Slow-OS. I mentioned my dislike of Apples EULA, the lack of technical feedback...
At the end he showed me how all of my points led me towards "linux" "As you see,you are really inconsistant"he said folding his hands behind his head. 
I had only once heard of GNU/Linux before, but that once had left my father with a copy of SUSE that had gotten promptly thrown into a drawer. Not long after the Apple user left I had my first taste of freedom. But the interface was bewildering, YAST confused me, and I knew nothing about what I was doing. I got some magazines and discovered Kubuntu, then Slackware, then Knoppix. I liked Knoppix but using an IE based dial-up service, I knew no way to update. 20-something distros later, I found Ubuntu and loved it. When I found out how I could save money and get 6mb/s internet speed to replace our dial-up, I dumped the old bloated OS I'd been using, completely using Ubuntu. A few NTLDR errors later, and I conviced my entire family to go opensource.
  It has been at times been a rough and bumpy road, but as it has been said before "Freedom is more precious to us the more we work for it".
I like this road, I like where it goes, and I will not go back.
