### Post by febuntubum on 2010-10-31
Hi all,

First of all I would like to salute all the staff and ubuntu community the last version is outstanding. Now I will give some feedback on my install.
Recently I acquired a Intel SSd and installed a Vista on my old Inspiron 1501. I really was impressed by the gain of performance.
Since I was short on space (its a 80g) hd I let only 7 gigas to my dear Ubuntu (Vista crap take too much space).
Considering I'm a newbee, it took about 4 hours to get the ubuntu desktop installed. I got a corrupted cd and was too lazy to check it before burn. After loosing grub, and panic a little bit, I decide to go with Unetbootin and the desktop amd64 image.
Before that I tried the mini.iso install and got stuck at commnand line stage with no graphical mode. :(
The Unetbootin install was pretty forward. In 20 minutes I was running but with no wireless support :( (that b43 are disgusting)
Download the cutter and some drivers and I'm in heaven.

Something that was not clear for me was the minimal requirements for the pendrive installs. I thought that I would need a 2 giga pen drive and a 1 gigas was enough. That was the main reason I did not go for a USB install at the first time.
Now I have a 13 seconds boot time system(could be improved of course since I got a modprobe warning at the start) against Vista 33 sec boot time and the really nice interface.

By the way my Kids love ubuntu and the call it Handy(because of the Gcompris software) 

Congrats Canonical and keep the marvelous work.
