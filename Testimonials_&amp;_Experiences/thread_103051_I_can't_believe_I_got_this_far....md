### Post by jdong on 2005-12-13
(1) Ubuntu is not as anal about Free FREE Free (*) whatever else as Debian or some other Linux projects are. However, since Ubuntu is commerically backed, what Ubuntu is doing must be LEGAL. I spend a lot of my time making mini derivative distros for other projects (i.e. robotics club), and like knowing that it's LEGAL for me to be redistributing these CD's as long as I take care whenever I pull from restricted or multiverse.

### Post by Brunellus on 2005-12-14
The relevant passage from the PINE license:

> Redistribution of this release is permitted as follows, or by mutual agreement:
(a) In free-of-charge or at-cost distributions by non-profit concerns;
(b) In free-of-charge distributions by for-profit concerns;
(c) Inclusion in a CD-ROM collection of free-of-charge, shareware, or non-proprietary software for which a fee may be charged for the packaged distribution.

Redistribution of binary versions is further constrained by license agreements for incorporated libraries from third parties, e.g. LDAP, GSSAPI.

Note the final restriction.  Elsewhere, there is also an encouragement, but no obligation, to make the source available.  I am not a lawyer, but this isn't quite GNU.

Anyhow, that's just so many angels dancing on the head of a pin.  I rather like PINE, anyway--I used it for years at college.  mutt and nano provide the same functions to me now as PINE and PICO did then, so, again, ceteris paribus, I choose GNU.  

Let me again congratulate you for getting your mail server online, though.  It's always really cool when you find out that something is *possible*...and that you can make it happen.
