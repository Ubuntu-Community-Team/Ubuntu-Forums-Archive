### Post by Buddy's Pal on 2009-03-01
Title pretty much says it all.

I've been totally Windows free at home for one year now.

Started with 7.10 and moved to 8.04 back in August. Any problems I've had have been minor and certainly far fewer than the frequent issues and frustrations that I used to have with Vista.

To restate the tired cliche: Everything just works. 

I'm running a pretty much plain Jane Toshiba Satellite A200. A friend of mine got curious enough to install 8.10 on an older Dell laptop. I helped him get his dLink wireless pcmcia card going. Took about 45 minutes. He's had clear sailing since.

I used a live CD of 8.10 to rescue my wife documents when her Toshiba A100 running XP gave her the dreaded "Windows could not start because the following file is missing or corrupt" message. Maybe now she'll take the trouble to back up regularly to our external HD. I spent hours and hours restoring her XP. I'd forgotten how much I hate having to reboot for every little change or update made to the system.

She was more comfortable staying with XP although she can certainly use my machine effectively and is envious of the speed and responsiveness of Ubuntu.

Anyway don't want to drone on too long. Just want to say that I'm a **VERY VERY** satisfied user - probably a member of the silent majority. I imagine that most of us happy sorts don't take the trouble to report in. I know I've been checking these forums regularly but not logging in.

If the frequency of problems seen here was representative of users in general, Ubuntu would have ceased to exist by now. 

I am very grateful to all the talented programmers that produce Ubuntu, all other distros and open source software in general. Your generosity is appreciated.
