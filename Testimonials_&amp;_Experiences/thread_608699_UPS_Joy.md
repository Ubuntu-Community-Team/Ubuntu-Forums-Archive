### Post by badrunner on 2007-11-10
I get a slightly dodgy power supply out here in the countryside, it often cuts out for a few seconds, normally when im in the middle of work and as i'm not using a laptop, everything goes down. I just picked up a nice UPS for £60 to alleviate this, plugged it in, and before i even got as far as an "apt-cache search ups" i noticed the gnome power manager icon in my system tray, already set up to to exactly what i would want when mains power goes down. 

Fantastic. Seeing as it took about 2 hours of fighting just to get something simple like wireless working on my brothers new vista laptop, this made my day.

Now if only compiz, tracker and displayconfig-gtk hadn't been pushed well before they were ready, gutsy would be the best release every. At the moment its definately a close second behind fesity out of the box, and best once i can convince it to boot on amd64 without a black screen, and turn off the afformentioned "features". I dont even know where to begin with reporting bugs against displayconfig-gtk, it seems so unusuablly broken.
