### Post by arunvir on 2008-09-07
I really liked ubuntu from the moment I had been using it on my PC as my second OS. It's really as good as it can get. 



Openoffice -> for office stuffs.

vlc  -> for videos.

xmms -> for music.

Inkscape -> for Image editing.

Aptitude/apt-get - >one of the easiest way for installing softwares.

Firefox(till 7.10) -> simply the best.

Transmission -> for torrents.

wine -> for running any executable programs not available for the platform.

and all the programming stuffs I need from (simple java SDK to Apache mysql netbeans..........._

Really, the best I could get for my PC.


But I just don't feel the same with my laptop.


Problems came for me with my laptop(Acer Aspire 4720z) with Linpus Linux.


I did manage to get my wireless device and sound working with ubuntu and that in no case is my problem.


My problem lies with the usability.

I feel the wireless disconnects far too many times for comfort.

I did use madwifi, but I don't think it works in the same speed as would in a windows environment. I did find one thing interesting when I started working with the signal strength issue. I believe ubuntu takes all the wireless access points transmitting speed to be 108mbps by default. Mine is 54mbps and it always shows very low signal strengths.I tried understanding the coding for WiCD(HEHEHE........ Brain suffering from temporary BSOD when reading that code). I couldn't even get DHCP configured to get automatically a wifi address. Each Time I had to assign some ip manually in that range. When I tried refreshing WiCD..... boom.... wireless network connection lost........I have given up wireless for the time being(if no useful solution then permanent wireless suspension....... if anyone asks, "my laptop doesn't come with any wireless card".... will be the easiest answer)....



I tried to tweak the touchpad settings as much as I can. It doesn't allow me to resolve to a flimsy usage that I do on others laptop(same Acer and Dell with other OS's). It wants harder single and double clicks. I have tried every tweak(except for gsynaptics I didn't manage to get it installed on my hardy), yet couldn't get the feel I wanted. Well, I tried my level best to get the acer acpi settings up and enable my other keys. Even went through several forum postings here to do that. 

Never succeeded even a bit!!!:)

My battery is just giving up, not even able to last upto 2 hours and it has really annoyed me. I do believe power management could be further well developed as it is one of the must when using  laptop.

My experience of ubuntu in my laptop was also slightly taken aback by the new Firefox that was more than bloated and suffered heavily to get up and running.Sufferings were magnified with flash.


I feel ubuntu has become my defacto for my desktop with the 7.10 gutsy but still has to do much for my Lappy.


I loved ubuntu as my first experience of linux. It gave me much curiosity about linux itself. That's why I have decided to try other distro's for the time being in my laptop and returning back to it with an attempt to get wifi working with ndisgtk or madwifi in the concerning distros.


Recent attempts will be ubuntu based FreeSpire and non-ubuntu based Mandriva one. Ubuntu has encouraged me to try out all of them. I'll start with these 2 and go for a spin in the linux world. I believe ubuntu will still be the best.


For future, I think the developers must be able to provide advanced support for wifi connectivity and automatic configuration of devices or atleast provide an internet link to a web page with a manual concerning installation of features/drivers for a laptop( not like a forum, but one that contains only the consolidated steps involved in installing each feature/driver with respect to each hardware). This will be super cool and help others to migrate much easily to ubuntu.

I thank the ubuntu society for all the works they have done and appreciate each and every one of them for their concerned contributions and hope one day(no way soon going by my brain), I might be able to do something useful too.

well I decided to post one of my present screenshot on my PC too to just show how much I love it..........

I feel I got to find it by using it......... I always liked tweaking whatever I get .... from my laptop,PC to my mobile).....
