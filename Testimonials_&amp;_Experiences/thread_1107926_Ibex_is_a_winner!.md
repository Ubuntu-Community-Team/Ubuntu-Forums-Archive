### Post by Mehashi on 2009-03-27
[COLOR=Magenta]Hey Guys[/COLOR]![COLOR=Black]

I have been using *nix on my main computer for a while (Quad Core, nVidia 9500gt setup) and am always loving it! Still, I have always had troubles getting ubuntu to work (quickly) on my (Single Core, Ati x1950 pro) system. The main problem before (specifically with 7.10) was the Ati graphcs card. Drivers wouldn't work at first and I repeatedly lost the ability to see anything at all! I continued with gusto and made through it! 

When my 'Home Hub' was delivered to me today I expected the same day of pain and suffering. Downloading 8.10, Burning, Wiping, Formatting, Installing Ubuntu, wrestling with 'BT' equipment, and configuring dual boot on the server. Sounded like a day with lots of caffeine involved. 

So i'm not sure if I was disappointed or surprised when I popped in the disc, installed and rebooted. It works. *Just like that*? It cant be. Even with the awkward Ati card I could immediately use advanced effects. I could happily connect to my network and external audio amplifier. Just like that? I understand that this is brilliant for new users, but has left me wondering what to fret over for the rest of the day?!

Overall since I started using *nix this has been the most user friendly experience I have ever had!
