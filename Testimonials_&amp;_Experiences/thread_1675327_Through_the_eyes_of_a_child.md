### Post by cprofitt on 2011-01-25
I converted my eldest to Ubuntu from Windows 98 years ago. My other two children used Ubuntu as their sole OS at home. Each child got their own computer at the age of three. What I find the most interesting is some of their 'comments' and some of the stories I have gotten back from teachers.

eldest child comments:
> 
Why are the computer so slow at school?


> 
I just got a fake virus pop-up... it told me Windows had a virus, but I run Ubuntu


> 
My friend wants an Ubuntu shirt like mine; can we get her one?

yes, Ubuntu is 'cool'.

middle child comments:
> 
I don't like the computers at school.

note: they use Macs

youngest child comments:
> 
I love my computer!


----

teacher comments about the middle child:
> 
Your son logged in to the computer, browsed to a website (game), logged in to the game before I finished explaining how to login to the other kids...

note: I have been told be educators that children that age (4) can not login.

----
To summarize my kids all love Ubuntu and apparently there are many other children who do as well.

I think a few of us have experiences similar to yours.

Children can "tell it the way they see it". If they don't like something they say so. They know nothing about operating system fanaticism. 

Children seem to be very adaptable when it comes to computers, as your experience shows. 

My grandchildren live with me. When they moved in, they only had one vintage Windows XP computer that was infested and didn't have restore CDs. I had some Frankenstein desktops each with a Core 2 Duo CPU of some version, 1 GB RAM, and HDD in the 100GB to 150GB range. I set all three of them up with their own computer running Ubuntu 8.04 at the time (now running 10.04). 

They never questioned logging into the computers even though the vintage Windows XP computer ran an Admin account all the time with no password. On the identically configured Ubuntu machines, I only had to show one of the grandchildren the password for the children's' account, and the other two got the info from the first one. 

Now that the grandchildren are all in school, they go to a school that uses MACs. Yes, I guess my school Taxes are paying for those MACs. :)

The grandchildren also get exposed to Windows 7 as that is what our multimedia computer connected to the big screen TV uses; because, of Netflix.
