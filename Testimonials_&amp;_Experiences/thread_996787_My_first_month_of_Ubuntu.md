### Post by .zolder on 2008-11-29
[size=3]History[/size]
My first experience with Linux was back when the first Knoppix first came out (2000 I think). I liked the idea of having something else than Windows on my PC, so since then I installed and tried a few different distro's I could potentially use as my main OS. I went from Mandrake 6 to Debian 3 (couldn't get past the commandline installer lol) to kUbuntu 7.04 to Ubuntu 7.10 and every time i disappointedly went back to ucle Bill. A month ago however, on the releaseday of 8.10, I installed Ubuntu 8.10 and I must say that, to me at least, Ubuntu and with it Linux finally is mature enough to run on my desktop.

[size=3]Why Linux[/size]
I guess there isn't a very convincing reason for me to ditch Windows, but I hate the fact that with an experienced user like me, Windows still manages to spontaneously fall apart at least once every 2 years. Turning off every service I don't use, having a strict antivirus, strict firewall, Spybot immunization, altered HOSTS file, not installing bogus apps, etc etc etc doesn't help if Windows has the tendency to inevitably destroy itself with no particular reason whatsoever.
Enter Ubuntu with the promise of never having to format again and have the option to just keep upgrading into eternity. I still am a bit skeptic towards that, cause I've read about a lot of cases in which major upgrades kill the OS, but I guess I'm going to have to wait for an upgrade like that to occur and see how the Linux gods like me.

[size=3]Dualboot[/size]
I still have a dualboot setup, because I need my Cubase/VST(I)'s, but if it wasn't for that I would probably have deleted Windows and format everything to ext3. I haven't booted into Windows since the very day Intrepid was installed on my box. Tweaking Linux has higher priority than making music at the time. First few nights i kept tweaking all night long up until i noticed every sane person was awake again and heading to whatever they do.

[size=3]Ubuntu experience[/size]
Besides me creating music, my computerusage is pretty much limited to surfing, mediaplayback, streaming to xbox360, hosting an FTP server, occasionally opening an Office document and downloading torrents. Eventhough I still am used to the behavior of my Windows apps (Irfanview, MediaPlayerClassic, Foobar2000), I feel more at home on Ubuntu every day I use it and all the counterpart applications do their job pretty well. Setting up everything so I could do my everyday things was a fairly easy job and every time i ran into a "problem" setting something up (FTP server per example), I could either check these forums or google for the fix in the form of simple commandlines.

I have the crappiest GFXcard you can think of, so I don't have any FX running. I like a clean, fast experience anyway, so I don't need wobbly, translucent windows that catch fire when you close them. What I did do however was getting rid of the horrible stock colors, creating my very own Iconpack (by ripping the best icons from different packs, occasionally adjusting the colors of said icons) and install MacOS Fonts (way better than mscorefonts!)

[size=3]Conclusion[/size]
All in all, I'm really happy to finally have a proper alternative to Windows. I want to thank everyone involved!

**Pro's:**
No spontaneously combusting OS
Nice community
Looks better than Windows
Tweaking is fun!
Scripts!
NTFS support
No mandatory restarts after app or even kernel upgrades
No sudden reboots out of the blue
No develish WMP11 for x360 mediastreaming

**Cons:**
Slightly more sluggish than XP on my box (Ath64 3200, 1GB RAM)
Can't get RDP (VNCserver) to work
No Foobar2000 (WINE doesn't allow for global hotkeys, or drag & drop from Nautilus)
Keyboard behavior slightly different from what I'm used to
Dutch terminal output makes googling for solutions to problems more difficult than it should be
Scrolling through the new Nautilus compact view is plain stupid compared to Windows Explorer
From time to time, Flash stops working in Firefox. A restart of FF fixes that.
Doubleclicking a file in an unfocused Nautilus window doesn't open it.
