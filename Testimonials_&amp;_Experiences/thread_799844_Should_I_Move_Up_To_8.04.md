### Post by omegamike3 on 2008-05-19
The one thing I would say is that, at least in my experience, there have been a few little things that didn't go well switching to 8.04. Nothing terrible, but just quirks that annoyed me after the upgrade. Mostly being on my laptop where I had suspend and sound working beforehand and then they broke afterward. Most of the initial bugs, things that were missed before the final release, have been dealt with now, so it's more just a judgment call on your part. I would say that if you're comfortable for now and there's nothing really making you want to jump to 8.04, stick with it until support runs out then make the move to have the LTS release that will keep you happy for much longer. Just my two cents. :)

### Post by stefangr1 on 2008-05-20
As they say: If it ain't broken, dont fix it / never change a winning team!

You can easily replace all applications with their newest version by running apt-get update apt-get upgrade. I would say install a new version only when you're having problems, when there are some new features that you really want to use (functionality) or when the installed system is really getting old (security+functionality).
