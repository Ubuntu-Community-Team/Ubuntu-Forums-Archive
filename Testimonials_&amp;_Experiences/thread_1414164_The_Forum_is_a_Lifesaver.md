### Post by plurworldinc on 2010-02-23
I just wanted to say "Thank You"!!!

 When I started using Ubuntu I was a total fish out of water with very little to know idea what I was doing. I was not one of those users that heard about Ubuntu for years and decided to give it a try on the recommendation of my friends, family, or co-workers. I just randomly found a magazine in a tiny bookstore in New York City that said on the cover in short, " Dump Windows Forever".

   Since I had an hour long train ride back to Philadelphia I thought it would be a good read. I sat in my chair with my cup of coffee and learned something new on the way home. I thought it would be nice to try out Ubuntu from a LiveCD or better yet dual boot my laptop for I could really get the full experience I would not be able to get from a LiveCD. 

   Now the comedy of it all was when my Vista laptop just did not work with most of my XP software. I just couldn't understand why, it's all Windows right. So i tried to dual boot, but something went wrong and I never tried to fix it. I thought I would just install Ubuntu and that would be that... yeah, a new happy user of Ubuntu, for five minutes.

   Nothing worked right out of the box. Since I was so use to Windows, I had no idea I wouldn't be able to listen to my MP3 or watch my videos I saved on an external hard drive. Worst of all my WIFI didn't work. This was a huge problem because now my laptop is no longer portable and I couldn't even get on the net to find out what was wrong. Lucky for me I was able to plug into the Ethernet a day later where is came across the Ubuntu Forum.

   I must say with out the forum I would have ran back to Windows because I didn't know how to fix anything. But when you ask questions, answers are always quick to follow. I was able to turn my laptop around and make it work for me and what I wanted to do. Even better then just making it work, most of the people were willing to help me understand why a few things didn't work out for me and give me not only a solution but a reason why. 

   At least after two+ years I am still using Ubuntu as my primary OS on all my systems now. I feel like I at least know what I am somewhat doing and I understand why certain things are done the way they are done. So once again Thank You to everyone that has made Ubuntu and Linux a welcoming home for me...
