### Post by PantaRhei on 2007-05-01
Although WindowsXP is running without major problems on my computer, I started to feel uneasy about the Microsoft-policy some time ago. In 2005, when I started to use Firefox in 2005, I couldn't no longer obtain Windows-patches on the Microsoft-website. (I got a notice that my “IE” was out-of-date). So I was obliged to use IE for downloading these patches. This event triggered an unhappy feeling about using Microsoft-products.

I started to look into some books about Linux in the public library, but most of them were dealing with entering magic spells in a terminal-window. I don't believe in sorcery, so Linux was no alternative after all. Recently I heard about Ubuntu and I decided to give it a try.  As I was running out of disc-space, I bought a new disc, ghosted Windows on the fast new disc and installed Unbuntu6.10 on the older, slower disc. Ubuntu installed pretty well and the responsiveness was OK too (although it was running on a slower disc). The major problem I encountered was that I had to learn all kind of new applications: Synaptic, OpenOffice, GIMP and that you're quite busy in finding alternatives: how to backup your files, finding an alternative for FrontOffice (NVU is a good alternative for me), and so on. During 2 months I learned to work with the new system and I got used to it (people are often reluctant in changing their habits!). Only when I wanted to print out something, I switched back to Windows (There are no Linux-drivers available)

Last week I downloaded Ubuntu7.04. I burned it on a CD. I prefer installing from a CD, because if the installation fails, you'll save the 90 minutes download time, and if later on, you need to reinstall the software, you'll have a copy immediately at hand and last, but not least, my intention was to use this CD as a demo for the friends and the family. However, I could not start updating the system from the CD. Ubuntu added this CD automatically to the software-sources, but the installer was always trying to download the packages from the internet again? I didn't find a work around and so I made a bold decision: I ghosted WindowsXP back to the slower, old disc and made a fresh installation on the new, faster disc (I made a backup of Ubuntu beforehand, of course). I have some remarks on the installation : 1. It is slow and as nothing is happening during a long interval of time, you are inclined to restart the installation all over again. 2. As I was using Firefox on my WindowsXP-build, I expected (as stated in the release-announcement) that my preferences would be automatically imported, but no users where detected?

After all, Unbuntu7.04 is running fine and hereby I would like to thank the Unbuntu-people and community for the efforts made. A nice job done!  Recently I installed Wine and was able to play chess with my Fritz8-program. 

Personally I don't think that Ubuntu will spread fast. My friends and family are not interested. Their comments: Ubuntu? Never heard of! We don't encounter major problems with Windows, we have paid for it (pre-installed on the computer), we are only using our computer for sending/receiving emails, so we don't care about an OS. Yesterday I visited the public library and I estimated the number of books dealing with computers, ... I counted approx. 500 books (Books on one shelve * number of shelves). I searched then in the database: books on UBUNTU : 0, books on Linux : 6 (youngest one dates from 2003), books on OpenOffice : 1 (2005). I went to a popular book store. Books dealing with Linux or OpenOffice : none!! (Yes, books upon Windows Vista were all over the place). I checked the contents of popular courses on ICT offered in the neighbourhood: There are all dealing with Windows and Office!

So if you want to tackle a giant, don't hope you'll beat him with a frontal attack. WindowsXP and Office are working fine! See for his weak points: the price! For many organisations (schools, non-governmental organisations, ...) the price of software-licences is a point. Up to now schools are spreading Word i.s.o. the word of informatics!
