### Post by hellion0 on 2009-10-31
I've had a soft spot for KDE from the beginning of my Linux journey. It's just that I've preferred Gnome over it, then Xfce over it when I became jaded to Gnome. Then I started hearing about how good the new version of KDE is supposed to be.

So, I decided to give Kubuntu 9.10 a test-drive using the LiveCD. First, I tried it in my desktop machine, having to reboot it anyway after a kernel upgrade.

Surprise, surprise, X was messing up. I'm guessing the Intel graphics bugs are still floating around.

So, plan B. I booted my laptop with it, one with ATI graphics. That one booted like a charm.

The first thing that caught my eye, insignificant in the grand scheme as it was, was the Usplash. I think it's Usplash still with Kubuntu, anyway. The way it faded in and out, almost as if it was breathing... awesome. Great intro.

X didn't start, of course, but at least it dropped me to a prompt this time. A quick "startx", and I was on my way. It was just a minor speed bump to me, someone who deals with a CLI-only system on the side, but to a newbie that alone could be a gamebreaker.

KDE started, and I'll tell you, I liked the theme right from the start. Gnome devs, take notice. That's how you do "pretty" at first sight! Also, Plasma is maturing well. I especially liked the microblogger right on the desktop... nice touch! I was tweeting all the while. There may be eye-candy, but it felt as light as air even after ticking on the desktop effects.

First thing I always do with a system is open the hood by opening a terminal. Konsole is still as bloated appearance-wise as ever. Considering I keep a terminal open from boot to halt, that's enough to be a gripe. Xfce4-terminal doesn't take up nearly that visual space with the same font and size. Still, there's other things to consider than just the terminal emulator. 

I then opened Konqueror. Let's just say this - it's far from dead. The scrolling was smoother than Firefox on the installed system, even beyond that of IE in a fresh Windows install. Can you say "like butter?" It got me where I was going just fine. I'd strongly consider installing it in a non-KDE system on its own if not for the dependencies.

I decided to give the new Amarok a whirl after that. No sound ruined that experience quick, but I wasn't about to give it a black mark for that. I'd had problems with the sound in an alpha install as well, which forced me to roll back to Jaunty. I mean, at least it "saw" the soundcard. Turned out I was having sound issues in the installed Xubuntu as well... removing Pulseaudio fixed that for me. If only there was some way not to run Pulse in the live environment but have it work on the installed system...

I didn't have a lot of time to try much else. Considering the way I had to start X, rebooting was a little painful... easy as "sudo reboot" for me, but again, possibly a problem for a newbie.

And the way the usplash faded to dark as it powered down... fitting end.

I'm almost tempted to install kubuntu-desktop on my Karmic system now. It's that nice.
