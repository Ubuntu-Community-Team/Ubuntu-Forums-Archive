### Post by nintendude7cubed on 2010-05-29
I am in the process of recovering files that I thought I'd never get back using ubuntu.. Heres why..

     A few weeks ago my Western Digital Mybook started clicking randomly while i was on my computer (using windows xp at the time.). Much to my dismay my harddrive eventually went south and decided to stop working alltogether.. I took the drive out of the enclosure (I was disappointed to find it was a Green Power drive which are more power saving than reliable). I put the drive into my computer and tried it as an internal using windows xp.. It sort of did a little moving that i could hear so i was a little hopeful, but i had no such luck when the boot finished. Windows didn't recognize the drive..
     Disgruntled, I brought the drive to school where I had a guy test it in a Thermalteke Blackx docking station.. I knew it didn't work when i found the drive sitting at the bottom of the garbage can.. This drive had all my precious data on it so thankfully I didn't wanna give up and dug the drive outta there (there wasn't anything gross in there).
     I brought the drive home and shelved it for a while. It sat there collecting dust for quite a long time.
     Between then and now I installed ubuntu on my computer.. It is awesome. There is a ton of software out there that makes this thing just as capable as windows xp.. Suprisingly enough though I don't have any of that software to thank for the retrieval of these files.
     I randomly thought about my drive collecting dust on my shelf today and thought, maybe somehow if i boot into ubuntu with the drive in, it'll work.. Sure enough. It did! I just had to share this while waiting for my gigs of movies, pictures, and games to be transferred to a safer place. 
     The two main things I am excited about having back are my collection of Touhou games, and I also have this folder that I transferred from my very first computer which is now over 10 years old. About 120MB of pure nostalgia that I thought was gone, is now back in my life. I think of it like a bunch of pictures that create memories of my life (which btw i also had some of those on my HDD and retrieved those):biggrin::biggrin::biggrin::biggrin::biggrin::biggrin:
