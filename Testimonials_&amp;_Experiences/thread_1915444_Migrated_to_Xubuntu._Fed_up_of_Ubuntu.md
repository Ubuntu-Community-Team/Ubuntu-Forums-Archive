### Post by subehsharma on 2012-01-26
I tried to make myself familiar and to like Ubuntu' Unity/Gnome-shell but no matter what i do i.e. use lighter apps alternatives, installing proprietary drivers, preload and other tweaks, I just couldn't make it work. The sluggishness in opening apps, folders, switching tabs in browser was just too much! I tried Unity 2d as well which hardly changed anything..On top of that, making tweaks often left me with a broken dependency, broken system and ultimately I had to re-install it.

Then one fine day, out of frustration, I experimented by erasing Ubuntu and installing Xubuntu and I was blown away! That was exactly what i have been longing for all this time..And it has been more than 2 months and the system hasn't crashed even once or slowed down(Like Ubuntu) after tweaking alot with the system. System doesn't slows down when a second screen is connected (unlike Ubuntu which showed weird issues like unity failing to load up on either of the screen and the only workaround was to plug out the second monitor and then once unity is loaded, plug it back). There are so many more about Xubuntu which i could write that has really made my life much more convenient.

Its sad that this amazing piece of software doesn't get much-deserved recognition whereas unstable systems like Unity/Gnome-shell is ruling the charts, so to speak.

### Post by TBABill on 2012-01-26
If you're a gnome person who switched due to Unity/Gnome Shell, perhaps checking out Cinnamon will bring some confidence back that you may be able to function in Ubuntu in a similar way as before. It's another UI with some cool features, but Clem, lead developer of Mint, still has it as a work in progress. It's coming along nicely and it gives some snappiness back to your desktop that you may have felt you lost in Unity.

Xfce/Xubuntu is always a good choice, particulary if you enjoy the look and feel of the DE. You really can't go wrong.
