### Post by lubrio on 2006-08-08
In Venezuela, although there are some laws promoving migration of the government platform to Free and Open Source platforms, the fact is that very few steps has been done to migrate. Some members of the venezuelan community of Free Software toke the inniciative and installed GNU/Linux (Ubuntu) to the laptops of 17 representatives (deputies) of the National Parliament of Venezuela. The parlamentaries got fascinated with the installation.

You can check photos at: [http://lubrio.blogspot.com/2006/08/realizado-segundo-festival-de-software.html](http://lubrio.blogspot.com/2006/08/realizado-segundo-festival-de-software.html)

Unfortunelly, we are a bit disappointed with the rumours coming from Mexico's DebConf, about Canonical making a new, propietary distribution that will replace Ubuntu in the meantime. 

Bye!
