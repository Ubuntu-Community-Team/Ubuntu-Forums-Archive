Well said.  For people new to Linux, I strongly recommend only staying with LTS releases.  10.04, 12.04, 14.04 (when avail).  Canonical strives to have stability as Job-One for LTS releases. For non-LTS releases, things are a little more *_experimental_,* to put it nicely.  I know that my 80 yr old Mom doesn't care about experimenting with newer features at all. She just wants a PC to work.

Upgrades from LTS versions is supported too, so we can skip all the odd year and .10 releases.  That is a bunch of system changes when we just want to use the PC to get things done.  

For people that want the latest and bleeding edge, I'd suggest they go with a continuous update distro like Arch or gentoo that provide little updates all the time - not a large "distro" release every 4-6 months. This method seems to be much easy to maintain over many years.
