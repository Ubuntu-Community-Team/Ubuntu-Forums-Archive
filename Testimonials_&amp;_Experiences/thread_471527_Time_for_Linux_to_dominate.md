### Post by MattDTownsend on 2007-06-12
Meh.  This isn't the best article I've read about Linux, by far.  Some of it is really pedantic, and other parts are so poorly translated that it's clear the writer didn't know what he was writing about.  Who calls gnome or KDE a "front end," anyway?  Who outside of the computing world would even know what a frontend or backend is?

I'm not sure that there's any need to declare "the time" for Linux.  If Linux is ready to dominate, it just will.  The best thing we can do is keep using it, improving it, and passing the word (gently) along to others.

Keep in mind that people have been declaring "the year of Linux on the desktop" since, what, 1999?  And now, some say that 2008 may just be the year of the desktop.  Which is news to all of us, who are using Linux on the desktop now.  And it's news to some of us, who have been using it on the desktop since before '99, anyway.  This kind of language is bad for business -- it makes us seem like a geeky minority.  Okay, we are a geeky minority.  But still.

So, I guess my question is:  why does Linux need to dominate, and why now?  As I see it,the fact that the Redmond Municipal Garbage Dump continues to produce, well, garbage, and that MacOS is striving to create perhaps the fruitiness interface known to man. . .this hasn't hurt Linux at all.  It's helped it.
