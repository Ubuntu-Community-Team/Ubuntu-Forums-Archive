### Post by IMELucky on 2006-11-02
Well, let me just start out by saying that I'm a huge fan of the Ubuntu Distributation and that my comments here are my personal experience. Also, there could most certainly be workarounds to my problems, and as I find time I will search the forums and do some hacking to figure things out. After all Edgy Eft has only been out for a week...

So here I am, one week with Edgy Eft, and I'm right now downloading the Install CD to Dapper.

When 6.10 was released I did a good old gksuko and used it to upgrade my distributation. Everything worked great. It didn't crash my system or break my install. Ubuntu started right up and all my documents and music were still in tact. So far so good...

But everything was not. I left to go to class, and pressed the sleep button on my laptop (which was setup for hibernation). The computer did not hibernate. Since I was in a hurry, I used suspend and that worked.

A while later, I investigated the problem: the upgrade had corrupted my swap partition! I tried to use the Gnome partitioner tool (in system->adminstration->disks). The launcher was still there, and I was propmted from my password, but the tool wouldn't launch.

Another thing really bothered me...All the icons in my application menu had disappeared. This had happed to me in Dapper too, so it could be a left over bug, nevertheless, it was very annoying.

Java quit working too, until a reinstalled the JRE

Lastly, when I resumed from suspend, I couldn't get sound from my speaker, only through the headphone jack. I had to reboot the computer.

So after this frustration, I downloaded the Edgy Desktop CD. It was my hope that a fresh install would be the cure to my problems. Instead, it only introduced new ones.

After installing without a problem, I ran into a different set of difficulties. Since I'm not a huge fan of brown, I downloaded a MacOS GTK theme, only to find that none of them drew the widgets correctly! So I picked a different theme, but it isn't one that I really wanted.

I finished installing all of the codecs, Java, some other non-free software that I use. Then I went to finish a presentation that I was working on. OpenOffice did not show the background of my slides! No matter what theme I applied, there would be no background.

Now here I'm starting to get into a hurry and didn't have time to look at the problem. I saved to a MS Powerpoint file and (I want to cry here) had to reboot to Windows to finish my work!

Well, in case anybody's concerned (which I doubt anybody is, lol) I did finish the presentation. But I'm so bothered by the fact that I had to use Microsoft's tools, that I decided that for now, I will continue to use Dapper. It is an excellent release of Ubuntu and I never had a single complaint about it. 

As for now, since I am a pro-active person I will install Edgy onto another computer that I'm not using for work, and will begin to troubleshoot my own problems. After all, it doesn't pay to complain about something if your not willing to try and fix and contribute knowledge back right?

If anybody else has had these kind of problems (or similar), solutions, comments, or would like to share their experiences; I am really interested in reading them. Please post them below and I wish everybody else good luck with this release and future releases!

--Pat
