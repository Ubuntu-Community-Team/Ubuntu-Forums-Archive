### Post by anotherdisciple on 2008-09-19
I just realized that I never wrote a testimonial!

I will have been using Ubuntu for 1 year as of sometime in October. I have been on the forums for exactly 11 months today.

Last year I bought the very first computer of my own. I was 20, moving out and getting married, so I thought I better get a computer for my new place. I bought a Dell E1505 (just weeks before the E1525's that let you choose the color came out!!!) and it had Vista on it. I used Macs from when I was little until I got into high school, then I used Windows. I was a "Windows Power User" and liked to tweak the registry like mad and do everything I could to make it faster.

So, searching the internet over to find out how to make Vista faster, I stumbled across a lot of forums. Almost every forum I came to had someone who suggested to dump Vista and use Linux. Up until then I had never even heard of Linux! So, I tried looking it up. It sounded cool, so I thought I would try the most popular version of it... which ended up being Ubuntu. Well... at the time they had Ubuntu 7.04, which wouldn't work with my computer... Xorg was all confused and I didn't know how to fix it. So, I tried the beta of 7.10.

I dual-booted for a while and then, about 3 months later, I realized that I hadn't touched my Windows partition in a long while, so I just reinstalled right over the windows partition!

Since then I have been asking a ton of questions on Ubuntu Forums, and I've even been able to answer a few, which is kind of fun. I waste a lot of time on these forums! I've even been learning a little about bash scripting and stuff like that.

I've tried a few other flavors of Linux, but I like vanilla Ubuntu. I'm a big GNOME fan. I like KDE, but it's not for me.

Linux does EVERYTHING that I want it to do and more. I like the freedom,the security, the ability to customize, all the free software, and the awesome community. My only complaint is that suspend and hibernate can be sketchy, but I'm just sticking it out until that gets solved.

I've even been able to install Ubuntu on my step-dad's laptop, my older sister's computer, one of my friend's computer, and a neighbor of my sister's computer.

It's been good. Thanks to all the people who help out on the forums, your work is greatly appreciated! :)
