### Post by halfbakedntx on 2006-03-08
[COLOR="Navy"]I am now officially a Linux Convert. Been running Ubuntu now for almost 3 weeks. Just installed Ubuntu on my last Windows PC (BuBYE! heh) \\:D/  . Before I was like running WinXP and various linux distros in vmWare such as Fedora (ok :-k  ) Mandrake/Mandriva (UGH it sucked :-? ) Suse (wtf  :confused:  ). Tried a few other nonames. Ubuntu is where I have officially landed. And now today I installed Ubuntu on my last WinXp box. Now get this vmWare is offering its GSX server and Vmware player for free. So I am running WinXP (what few apps I must run in Windows left) in Vmware! Muhahhaha! :twisted:  I will try some freeware Linux virtual apps later.

I needed a media box in my living room. So the other day I bought a cheap Fry's 1 day special GQ PC for 99.00 LOL.. Updated the Ram to 392 megs for 30 bux. Bought an Airlink 101 AWLH4030 Wireless Super G PCI card for get this $17.99 (again onsale). Ubuntu detects it immidiately on install. SHWEET! So this GQ pc is plugged in behind my entertainment center. I VNC into it and have the sound card plugged into the RCA input on my stereo. I use StreamTuner to listen to ShoutCasts through the Stereo and record them to Mp3 with Streamripper. Create playlists in XMMS and basically have a jukebox I VNC into from the laptop at the coffee table or Kitchen to play tunes. I plugged in my 250 Gig Maxtor loaded with MP3s and add to the collection recording Shoutcasts.. SWEET! I plan to order a touch screen monitor add on and build a touchscreen interface at the entertainment center to make playlists/downloads there. Gotta find a nice Mp3 jukebox heh.. Ontop of all that I plug in my Xbox into the built in NIC on the 99.00 GQ PC and followed your instructions to route it to the wireless Netgear gateway (NOTE: This link is very valuable instruction for this : [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=91370](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=91370)
I have a verizon FiOS Fiber ISP connect 15mbs down 2mbs up. The damn Xbox wireless nic was 99.00 in itself. So by not having to buy that POS I paid for the Cheapo PC Jukebox with that savings \\:D/ 

Needless to say THIS IS A SHWEET setup. Has everything I need from filesharing between my 4 PCs , media stuff. Again for that little bit of media that needs it I have WinXP running in a Vmware session ;-).. I dont PC game anymore thats what my Xbox is for (Yeah I know still a MS product LOL) But then again MS is now left as a game platform for me. These forums and online community is the Shizzle.. Good info and instructions which is icing on the cake. Again thanks for the help and a great Distro!! :mrgreen: 

PS: Donated 20.00 to the forums fund. They saved me too much in time, money and uneeded/supported hardware not to.. Thx again!
