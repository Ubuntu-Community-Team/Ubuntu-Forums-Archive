### Post by jromer on 2007-10-28
I just installed 7.10 on my system as a triple-boot. (I also have XP and Ubuntu6.06) I kept balking at trying another version since the LTS 6.06 had been so time consuming to get working right. (Automatix, NVidia drivers, Flash, all the other driver and codec issues, Compiz/Beryl.) I didn't know if I could go through that again.

So I was very surprised at how easy 7.10 installed and how intuitive it was to add the drivers and codecs I needed. I would try to run something and a box would come up and ask me if I wanted to install the additional software...very easy. 

It's been 1 day and I have as much or more functionality with 7.10 as i do with 6.06. Oh, plus I really like how they integrated Compiz. (Especially Winkey+Alt+Tab.......very cool!)

One more thing, I saw some people disappointed in boot times? Well, I was pleasantly surprised. Actually my boot times were much faster than with 6.06...and comparable to XP.

My comments are based on me looking at this as an Ubuntu to Ubuntu comparison so I'm not commenting on what might need to occur to have Ubuntu be more viable competition in the desktop space in general. (I don't know enough to comment on that.)

 But I see much improvement so far. 

Job well done.
