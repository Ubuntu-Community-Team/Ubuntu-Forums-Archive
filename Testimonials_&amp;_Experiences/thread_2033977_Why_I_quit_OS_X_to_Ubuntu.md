### Post by Juan Largo on 2012-07-28
Apple products do have their places.  They provide a reliable, consistent user experience (for the most part), requiring very little effort on the user's part.  When problems arise, Apple generally tries to make things right for their customers.

I prefer to poke around under the hood and get dirt under my fingernails, so Linux suits me better.  But I can understand why OS X would appeal to some.  What I don't understand is why so many customers feel compelled to "upgrade" OS X to the next version within a microsecond after Apple releases it, or why they camp out over night at Apple stores to purchase the next great Apple gizmo on Day 1.  That kind of psychology completely baffles me.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2012-07-30
> **Juan Largo said:**
>  What I don't understand is why so many customers feel compelled to "upgrade" OS X to the next version within a microsecond after Apple releases it

Former Mac user here.

The problem is that users ARE compelled to upgrade to the next version. The new versions of their favorite programs, especially Apple programs, are made using new APIs that are only in that latest version of OS X. So, in order to use newer versions of their favourite programs, Mac users must also upgrade their OS.

It gets really bad when you are using a computer that Apple decides to obsolete. You get stuck on an old OS, which means only your existing programs and no new versions or brand-new programs. Oh, and no new Apple hardware either, unless it's a whole brand-new computer running the latest OS.
