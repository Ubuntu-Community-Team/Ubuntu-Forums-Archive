### Post by duckfeet on 2008-08-05
I feel the same way, absolutely.  I spend a lot of time on Windows, and around my town, in my crew, I'm the guy they come to when they get malware and stuff, and need minor problems solved, and I usually carry some "fixit" tools on a USB necklace...and also have a Ubuntu set-up on there...but I also tell them that linux is not for people who want to come to *me* to work on their computers, but for people who want to sit up late at night working on their own stuff, but that it is so rewarding...

I spent three days, living and breathing this forum, reading old tutorials, just to get my WG111 usb wireless insert device to work, but I got *so* much help, especially from people who won't even know about it, since these are old threads, but I owe such a debt to them, and hope I can do the same for the people who follow me.

Here in San Diego, they have a crew called Kplug, who meet twice a month, just to help newbies install linux, on their own time, for nothing, even got the donuts for free ;-).  

I've met so many nice, helpful people online and off, just as a result of this operating system...how many people can say that about Windows?
