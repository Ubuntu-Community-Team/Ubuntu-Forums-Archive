### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-02-02
I think the quality of our software is already pretty high, but further functionality and improvement of newer projects like the video editors would be a good idea.

But yeah, the only thing Ubuntu really needs is for people to get the word out and have more options for preinstalled hardware. Most people ask someone else to reinstall Windows for them, so they're not likely to install Ubuntu, even though it's insanely easy to do.

If some extremely benevolent company wanted to throw money at some television ads for Ubuntu, or even Linux in general, that'd be great. But the simple fact is that it's unlikely.

So Linux will grow, albeit slowly. If it's presented correctly, however, it will be a very appealing choice to new users, and more people will be able to enjoy it together and support it into the future.

I just think these people saying that, "oh, this one thing would make everyone jump on over to Linux" are being a little simple-minded about it.

Even if Wine were perfect, do you really think people would just up and install Ubuntu like mad with Wine on the side? Maybe after a few years, when Microsoft spews out another high-cost OS.

The simple fact is that tons of people don't know about it, or if they do they have no idea what it really is.
