### Post by raydar on 2007-12-02
After upgrading to Gutsy, I was on the lookout for subtle UI improvements here and there; e.g., I'd heard drag-and-drop functionality was to be increased.  I came up with a few small items, some or all of which may not be new with Gutsy, and I just wonder what anyone / everyone thinks about them.

GOOD
1. Icons: If I create a new file by right-click on the desktop and then get distracted and click something else before I name the new file (which I concede I do blind most of the time; add some kind of translucent visibility for that, maybe?), it's still sitting there waiting for me, text selected. 

2. Icons: I also like the default selection of all but the dot-suffix on rename, unlike MS' selection of the whole filename, 'cause I caught myself frustrated at work the other day wondering how many times I've needlessly gone shift+four-left-arrows as a mindless routine.


BAD
1. Icons: When I want to see what image file a shortcut's or launcher's icon is set to, opening its properties box and clicking the icon changer button puts me at a file dialog in my home folder instead of the folder containing that image file.

2. Keyboarding responsiveness: I got used to going alt+F S really fast in another OS, in any app with the usual file menu. Some key combinations I can do as fast as I can and the gui keeps up, but with alt+F S, I always get stuck with the file menu hanging there and have to hit S again. (Maybe it's just this machine--is anybody else an alt+F S 'er?)

3. Window closing: Double-clicking the icon/button/whatever-you-call-that in the upper left-hand corner of a Gnome window doesn't close the window, unlike in that other OS.  (Is this a settable option whose control I just haven't found yet?)

4. App launch speed:  I have Ubuntu & Kubuntu installed on different drives in the same machine.  In Kubuntu, I launch an application and bang, it fires up.  In Ubuntu, I always have to wait several seconds, sometimes as many as 10, before anything happens.  This is without Compiz running.  Why the lag in Gnome?

### Post by raydar on 2007-12-03
Re: 2. Keyboard responsiveness:
True, I can certainly adapt a little and use what's obviously a more direct key combo.  I just like the feeling being able to type/click commands as fast as I can and still have the computer waiting on me, not me waiting on it. :)

Re: 4. App launch speed:
I think the apps I notice in particular launching silently and slowly are gedit and the update manager . . . EDIT: and Nautilus . . . those are what come right to mind, anyway.  Some others take time to load but do at least show up in the task bar as "Starting ____" etc.  Somewhere just the other day I ran across "Qt" in the non-Quicktime sense, and as opposed to Gtk+, for the first time; so from that and what you're saying about different libraries already being loaded depending on Gnome/KDE, maybe I'm starting to understand where a difference in app start time could arise (more Linux 101 remedial needed! :) ), even if I don't know why cause-and-effect-wise. 

As for why separate installations on separate drives, the reason I do it is so that I have two utterly independent systems--one being my main/day-to-day one and the other being a guinea pig I can play with fearlessly.  (I also regularly back up my main/day-to-day system's data onto the guinea-pig drive just for the heck of it, so as not to completely waste that drive's space, even though I may wind up obliterating the backup data from time to time--it's older, only 30GB, so I don't consider it much of a waste even so.)  

One other that I forgot to mention:
Icon opacity:  When I drag & drop one icon onto another (on the desktop or in a Nautilus folder in "icon view" mode), the one I'm dragging gets in the way of seeing precisely what I'm dropping it onto.  There are clues, like the fact the "receiving" icon gets selected/highlit when I hover over it, but if the one I'm dragging is bigger than the one I'm dropping it on, it's harder to tell (e.g., a video file thumbnail onto a folder icon or, especially, the recycle bin icon in the panel).  Is there an icon opacity-while-being-dragged setting, perhaps right under my nose that I haven't found, that I could decrease?
