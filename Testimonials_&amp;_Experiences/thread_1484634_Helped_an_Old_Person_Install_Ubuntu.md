### Post by dadgetboy on 2010-05-15
It was the usual cry for help: "My laptop is so slow now!" Indeed, it was.
It had many useless programs starting up, too many processes running,
and worst of all it was Vista on 1024 MB of RAM.

I started by removing McAffee A/V, but then I realised, what do these
folks use their machine for?

So, that's exactly what I had asked them.
They answered "You know, e-mail, Google, check the weather.. not much more than that."

I then told them about Ubuntu. They were willing to try something new, and
we prepared a LiveUSB. They loved how fast it was (even Live). I then explained
the philosophy behind Ubuntu, open-source, and a few other things Linux strives for.

They were shocked at how fast it installed (~15 mins).

When it booted (even for the first time) they were astounded by once again,
the speed at which it booted. They were used to their bloated Vista taking ~2 min. to boot. 

Updates installed smoothly, and then we had to reboot.
Once again, shocked at the shutdown/boot-up speeds.

Then came the problem: they were used to Vista's layout. Ubuntu's is obviously
for different for a not-so-computer-savvy person. The Vista Ubuntu theme proved very annoying to install; however they were OK w/ XP's layout

Installing an XP theme was simple, I just found a simple script that did everything.

Then came the Compiz animations, virtual desktops. 3D Cube... astounding!

(Forgot to mention installing ubuntu-restricted-extras, ubuntu-tweak, a few other common things to do on install).

The last thing they wanted to do was watch DVD's/movies. Easily done w/ VLC, and libdvdcss.

Ubuntu is truly "Linux for Human Beings".

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-16
I have an old 'puter with Xubuntu set up in the dance studio for the other kids to use in between dance classes. They do homework on it, check their e-mail, listen to music, print dance cue sheets, stuff like that.

They expect it to be really reeeeeally sslllowwwww, because it's an old dinosaur. But it's more responsive than much newer machines. It flies along very fast and does everything the kids want it to (except some MMORPGs) much faster than they expect at first. When I get compliments on it's speed and ease, then (and *only* then) I tell them it's Linux. "Impossible! I'm just pointing and clicking, not typing commands," the parents say. Wow.

Anyway, several other dancers have replaced Windows with Xubuntu (I hand out discs if they ask). One whole family switched entirely! It's very cool. The only downside has been that I'm their "tech support," but it's usually just a quick e-mail exchange ("install xubuntu restricted-extras," for example).

**I never "preach" Linux** except "to the choir," so to speak, *after* people have expressed their admiration for the simplicity and speed of the studio computer and tried out a LiveCD at home. Then they get to hear all the really cool stuff: No registry (so no registry errors)! No viruses! No searching the web for software and installing it from dodgy web sites! No cost, ever! 

It's so cool when people just run up to you with a big hug of thanks for turning them on to Linux.

Still getting lots of hugs,
Robin
