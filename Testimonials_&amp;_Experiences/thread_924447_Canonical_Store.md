### Post by aaror on 2008-09-19
Thinking the idea of a canonical store is a tad off topic (not in a bad way, but in a maybe we should start a new thread and post a link way)...
That said, how "Pure," would it need to be?

(remember I'm a business major, so try to follow me, and tell me if I get too technical, we nerds have a different language just like the geeks do).

Instead of launching a bunch of new stores, we use the franchise concept. Canonical looks at what hardware configurations work best with Ubuntu, then finds local computer stores that are interested in Ubuntu. Canonical puts together a nice logo and a small version of the franchise staff and layout team. Local computer stores put up the logo and theme, and can get discounted advertising, in exchange for a percentage of revenue.

The other secret here is, we don't kill the core business of the franchisee. So what if they also sell Windows, heck, maybe they sell computers with your choice of windows, Ubuntu, or both pre-configured to dual boot! If they sell games, they also install Wine and will offer 5 hours free tech support to install games with the purchase of one of their computers. Follow this logic and you could have 1000 Canonical stores in the US in two years, and maybe even have a significant market share increase for Linux...

Thoughts?
