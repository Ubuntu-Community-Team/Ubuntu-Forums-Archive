### Post by scottuss on 2010-04-23
Well, I have to say, Ubuntu has got me AGAIN! :D

Turns out, every time I distro hop and say to myself, "right, that's it, use this instead of Ubuntu", I end up crawling back.

I've recently started installing Fedora on pretty much all my stuff, but I got a new Samsung laptop and for love nor money it won't behave itself with Fedora.

I think it's something to do with the Wireless, I've submitted gazillions of Kerneloops reports, but I can't really wait for it to be fixed. It occurs in both 12 and 13 BETA.

One other thing that I find a problem in *every* other distro: fonts.
Ubuntu's fonts just **can't** be beaten. They're the smoothest and nicest looking.

So, here I come again Ubuntu! I ditched you before for a stint with Arch, and I did it again with Fedora, but I'm crawling back!

From now on, distro hopping is purely VM based for me! :D

Anyone else had similar experiences?

:guitar:

P.S I really hate people that say I must be a noob because I "can't use" Arch or Fedora (it was said elsewhere) but I'll be honest with you: I have a life and don't want to spend it tweaking millions of files for something to work. I could do it (I really do know how) but I can't be bothered. With Fedora, it's more like Ubuntu in a sense that it just works, but fonts and other niggly things just keep me from going. Keep up the great work!
