### Post by Jakemc on 2010-10-22
I've been a PC user since they used 8 inch floppies. I've always thought that Windows products got a bad rap from everyone.

Recently I installed Ubuntu on a separate drive in my PC just to see if I could do better. 

I intentionally wanted to leave Vista around cause, well first of all, I paid good money for it so why not use it. Anyway, something free couldn't be better... Could it? And anyway, there are things I do in Vista that might not work in Linux.
 
At first there was a lot of playing and experimenting to get things to work the way I liked. After a few hours or so I thought OK so it works. Pretty cool. And it is free. That's nice but really, what's all the fuss about? I could do most of this in Vista. 

Then I started customizing with stuff like Compiz. And I said OK so Vista isn't this beautiful or fast and it nags you all the time about this or that, but it gets the job done.

Over the last week Ive been visiting Vista to get playlists or other things that I left over there. But I've been booting back into Vista less every day because I am find how abysmally slow and clunky it is.

I just got done with a process that should have taken me 2 minutes to: 
go to Vista, open iTunes display and print a playlist and leave. That's it. 

- It took 5 minutes to boot up, 
- 4 minutes to open iTunes as it wanted to "scan" my library, 
- then deal with Norton (who wanted me to re-subscribe), 
- Did I mention that all of this ran slower than cold molasses? I guess because since I haven't run Vista for 24 hours, it was busy doing background tasks associated with bug and virus patches that I apparently have fallen behind with (in 24 hours I've fallen behind???)
-Then 1 minute to shut down. Yes, it took over a minute to shut the damn system down so I could re-boot Linux!

Bottom line is- I've discovered that I wasn't happy with Windows. I was numb to it. Spend some time away from it and you will see how really bad it is. 

Don't wait, try Ubuntu and see what I mean.
