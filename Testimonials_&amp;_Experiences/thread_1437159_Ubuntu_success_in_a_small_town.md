### Post by Revolutionary101 on 2010-03-23
Today has been a great day for me. One of my friends that is having computer difficulties went to a computer store (this after me already trying to get him to use Linux, to no avail) in my small town of 5,000 people. When he went there he asked to have a Windows XP disk and if they could install it for him. To his surprise, the employee at the store said "Sorry, but we only install Ubuntu Linux on computers". Now this made my friend see that Ubuntu Linux wasn't just one thing "crazy" me was suggesting him to use, that it was also used by other people. Due to this my friend has come to me, apologizing for judging Ubuntu by its cover, and has asked me if I could help him try it out.

There is yet another success story that I have to share about my town. A few years ago I went to the local public library. When entered the library, I looked at signs on the computer lab windows that said "Check with front desk to see if your Flash Drives will work with our computers, because we do not use Windows on our computers." I thought "Oh, this will be cool, they have Mac OS X on their computers" (This is before I had seen Linux on desktop computers). When I sat down at the computer, to my surprise, the computer used neither Windows nor Mac OS X. The computer turned out to be using Kubuntu as the OS. After this I became more interested in Ubuntu. This tiny experience has led me to become a dedicated Ubuntu user for over 1.5 years.

Although these both may seem like small and insignificant story about my town's use of Ubuntu, this is big for me. My town has a population of 5,000 and to have the public library and one computer store (out of 2) using Ubuntu is great! I hope that other people will experience the spread of Ubuntu in their towns, cities, or neighborhoods.

### Post by 3Miro on 2010-03-23
This is a great story. I don't think this to be a small achievement at all. The number of computers in the town may be small, however, there is also the density factor. There are many Linux users around, but most of them are sparsely distributed. A small town dominated by Ubuntu is far more likely to attract attention, both by other people and by companies. It would be great if large companies took notice and started working with Linux for better drivers (printers most notably) and third party commercial software (i.e. games).
