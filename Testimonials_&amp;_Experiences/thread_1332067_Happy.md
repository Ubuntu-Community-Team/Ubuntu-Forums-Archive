[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]Well here I am having a week ago installed 9.10..... and at this stage I can't find anything to whinge about.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]Installer worked fine; found all my hardware. Had no trouble in gparted setting up the partitions. Grub installed my copy of suse 11.2 on another HD in the boot menu... no probs.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]Ubuntu made it really easy to install the naughty bits so I can open all the priority files and Firefox is setup to play all the pages properly. [/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]The Ubuntu community are wonderful. All-in-all a very satisfying experience.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]I still run my copy of XP pro in Virtual Box as here in Aus the taxation department won't port their tax program to linux. Bah humbug![/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]I have been a linux convert for some years now. I started off dual-booting XP and suse 10.2. I found 11.0 to be wonderful and KDE3.5 to be very mature and easy to use. Never had a crash or problem. Unfortunately suse's insistence of using KDE4 in it's latest versions is disappointing. KDE4 is immature and buggy. I had KDE3.5 setup on 11.1 and was very happy and could not wait for 11.2 to come out. :( Not good. Could not get my nvidia card to work with the composting. Could get it to work in Mandriva 2009.10 so KDE4 seems not to be the problem.[/FONT][/COLOR]
[COLOR=black][FONT=Verdana]Then I discovered ubuntu 9.10 (yes I had heard of it for a long time but was never temped to use a program based on Gnome.) What a wonderful experience. There are some things I will have to get used to in Gnome. It does not seem to be as flexible as KDE......... but there you go. Thanks to the folks who work hard to supply the computer uses of this world with a mature, stable and workable system without being tied to big business. Don't get me wrong; I don't mind buying software..... but on my terms only. Thanks again :D[/FONT][/COLOR]
