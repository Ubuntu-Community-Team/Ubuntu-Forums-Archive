### Post by Jammin80503 on 2008-01-05
This thread is a story for people thinking about switching, or wondering how easy it is.
Let me start off this story by stating that I still don't officially own this PC, so I'm still using it because my parents let me.

below is a rendition of my story that is, for the most part, poorly written. however, its 4 in the morning and it gets the point across.

My dad is a computer geek, a web coder, and yet he never even looked into linux. Thereby, I had a childhood without linux of any kind :( now, as I grew older, I grew to like computers more and more. I slowly took over the best computer in the house, and it migrated closer to my room. After I had it in my room for a while, I started messing around with it as any geek should do. Seeing as this computer had windows, that road soon led to the computer crashing all the time (I didn't know what i was doing at that point) now, admittedly, I might have had... ulterior motives, seeing as I had just discovered linux. The computer, as all windows computers I have seen do, slowly declined into a irreparable mess. Games wouldn't play, and it was running really slow. Eventually, it just died. At this point, i was getting quite geeky, and knew what was wrong with it - Windows. I reloaded the system, and it still didn't work (Well, it might have. But don't tell my dad.) . Not only that, but it died again after 3 days due to circumstances completely out of my control*. I had no computer for nearly a week (very painful for an internet addict).
now here is where the story gets happy. I received permission from my parents to nuke it and load ubuntu, which was done within 20 minutes of permission being given. Now, I was very excited, but I still didn't know if i could do as much with it (sorry guys, photoshop is better than GIMP). this is when I was faced with another happy event - a new iMac, ready to go. this solved any problems I might have had with ubuntu, like wireless access and professional applications. Now i have a happy little hacker-den and everything functions in prefect harmony. w00t.

if all you want is the good and bad points, ignore the above.
*ubuntu* (gutsy)
good: everything is free, it works well, no crashes, awesome programs and GUI
bad: problems with wireless, no internet until i shared the internet over a LAN from my iMac, not compatible with a lot of stuff (like my old webcam) and confusing for new users.
*imac* (in working with ubuntu)
good: shares internet, runs most of the things that gutsy can't, and works better for my ipod
bad: i cant get it to see my other desktop, for some reason.

so after all this, my advice to anyone is to go for ubuntu. i like it better than OSX, and thats quite a statement. also, if you can, get a setup like mine. Its really nice when all your stuff functions in harmony. :P 
May Tux be with you.
