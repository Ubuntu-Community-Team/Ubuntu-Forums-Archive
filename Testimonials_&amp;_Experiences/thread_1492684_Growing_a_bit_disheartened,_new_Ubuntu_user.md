### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-25
I, too, find it alot easier to just [COLOR="Purple"]re-install[/COLOR] if I've messed up stuff by playing around with stuff I didn't understand.

For me *that is one of the great strengths of Linux* - and the 'buntus in particular because the installer is so easy - a complete reinstall is effortless and takes only a few minutes.

I'm one of those kids that prefers to run *applications* rather than the operating system. The only things I really change in my Xubuntu are the panel settings and wallpaper! 

[COLOR="Purple"]Hardware compatibility[/COLOR] is one of those things alot of people don't think of when they're first checking out Linux, but I could have saved myself a few headaches if I had simply asked, "how will this run on my old Dell Dimension?" Many other Linux distros won't run well on it, but Xubuntu ran perfectly on it right out of the box (until Lucid, that is - no sound - fixed by removing PulseAudio, which Xubuntu never shipped with until Lucid). Almost all the better Linux distros have a hardware compatibility list on a web page or wiki entry, so be sure to check your own 'puter against the hardware listed on the distro's compatibility list. That helps alot.

[COLOR="Purple"]Just marked my first year as a Linux user[/COLOR] and love being Windows-free,
