### Post by dale_nx26 on 2008-05-04
So in my house we have 2 Macs, 1 Windows, and now Ubuntu. To get to the point, in terms of usability Mac is #1, Windows #2, and Ubuntu is #3. HOWEVER, I must say Ubuntu has been very good to me and sometimes I feel like it's tying Windows for 2nd place. I've read many issues people have in this forum and I guess I can say I'm lucky my problems were/are not major or I can eventually find a solution to them.

I really like how I can customize to my heart's desires in Ubuntu. It makes it more personalize, something I can't easily accomplish in Windows. I dislike the use of the terminal. I prefer Window's way of handling installations, but I can bare with command lines. I used to dislike Linux because I've tried Feisty Fawn and often got aggravated. Hardy seems more stable and I am now more patient in searching in Google for solutions. Once again, suspend and hibernation do not work for me. But that doesn't matter because I really like how fast Ubuntu boots up and how it continues to be fast during usage (programs open up snappily). Everything I do in Windows I can do Ubuntu. Ubuntu shares a similarity with Mac in that programs are simple and to the point (most of the time), and also the panel in comparison to the dock. Compiz is very nice. I like how simple it is to burn an ISO to a CD and how I can listen to music in my NTFS partition without copying them over.

Truthfully, my Ubuntu experience is very similar to Windows. I actually don't see how it can excel Microsoft's offering; both are very nice systems to use (I rarely get viruses). What I can say is Ubuntu keeps it's performance unchanged while Windows for some mysterious reason progressively slows down (my school's PCs has more programs and weaker specs and still trump my PC in terms of speed!). However, to me Ubuntu has much more bugs. In the end, I still prefer booting and working in Ubuntu, but I don't know if that's because I am eager to try something new (a reason I wanted to try Linux--a new OS!) or if the experience is actually better. Both Windows and Ubuntu has their good and bad points, but not so much as to make either one superior.

P.S. From reading messages here and everywhere, I realize and confirmed what I think about fanboys: they are not beneficial to society and are very hard-headed.

system spec (I hardly saw anyone post this): Asus p5l-vm 1394, Seagate 320GB HDD, 3GB ram, celeron (conroe-l) 420 1.6gh, nvidia 7600gt
