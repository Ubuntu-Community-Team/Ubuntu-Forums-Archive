### Post by MindRiot on 2007-04-25
Hello.

I wish to thank Canonical and the Ubuntu community for providing me with the best overall Linux distro available. Furthermore, I want to extend my gratitude and appreciation to the individuals responsible for the Unofficial Ubuntu Edgy Starter Guide. If it were not for that amazing document, I wouldn't be using Ubuntu or Linux.

I realize this sounds crazy, but the single most important contribution Canonical and the Ubuntu community can provide Ubuntu users is documentation on how to actually use Ubuntu.  The depth and quality of organized information available to peruse is astonishing compared to what I have experienced with other distro I've tried. My choice to use Ubuntu over other Linux distros was decided by the clarity and empowerment I found in such documentation.

I'd like to share a brief word about the reason which  compelled me to reinstall Ubuntu Edgy. Unfortunately, its a device driver which is preventing me from moving to Feisty, and the blame is with ATI.

Unfortunately, both the ATI driver 8.36.5 and the ATI driver in the repository don't work properly under Feisty with my ATI Radeon 1600 series graphics card.  Nevermind beryl.  I cannot watch commercial DVDs, or stream videos from the  internet, or play video games I was able to play on Edgy.  It doesn't matter whether I use the vesa driver (no 3d rendering), the driver provided by the Restricted Driver Manager, or the latest ATI proprietary driver.  This puts the ball in ATI's court, and I'm anxious to see how long it takes ATI to provide a driver that works.

For the meantime, I have reinstalled Ubuntu Edgy with ATI driver 8.35.5 and everything, including xgl/beryl, works again.  

Of course, the only way to determine if future releases of the ATI driver will work under Feisty on my system is to reinstall Feisty with the new driver and see if things work. I'll rue the day in May when ATI releases the next driver, since I don't look forward to undergoing another installation routine, yet I'd like to use next release of Ubuntu.
