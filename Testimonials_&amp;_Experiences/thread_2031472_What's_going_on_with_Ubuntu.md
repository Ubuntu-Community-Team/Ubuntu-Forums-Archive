### Post by psixpsi on 2012-07-22
I've been a Ubuntu user and proponent among my friends for quite some years now. But the last Ubuntu releases, esp. 12.04 are simply horrible. I'e installed recently 12.04 on my HP 2760p. Everything (+/-) worked straight out of the box, but it keeps crashing and crashing. Mozilla, window manager, etc etc. What the heck?? It is much worse than the parallel Windows 7 on the same machine: less stable, SLOWER, and, what's even worse, completely closed, just like commercial OS. For example if I want to change something more advanced than simple settings I have to search the net for magic rites, because Ubuntu developpers assumed probably that Ubuntu users are idiots (like Windows and MAC OS system developpers assumed which is humiliating IMHO and has been the driving force for me to go into Linux some years ago) who have to eat the cake they've ordered without complaints. Last but not least, I feel that the new Ubuntu window manager is not simply inspired by MAC OS but is a copy on a border of being a plagiarism. Why they've missed their chance to create something own and new?
I'll probably take the effort and learn Debian, replacing Ubuntu as unstable and useless.
