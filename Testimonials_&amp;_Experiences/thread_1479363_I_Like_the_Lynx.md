### Post by pricetech on 2010-05-10
So far I've been happy for the most part.  I do have a few annoyances though.

At boot time I had to hit escape to bring up the menu to check the integrity of the disk.  Not intuitive for a first timer. (I'm not)

Software Center is not, in my opinion, any better and possibly not as good as the Add / Remove programs option under Hardy.

Why did the minimize, maximize, close buttons move ??  OK, I can adjust, but I'm just saying.

Samba didn't work until I ran the smbpasswd command, then it works perfectly.

Virtual Box required me to install more than I recall having to install under Hardy and the Guest Additions ISO was moved from its location under Hardy.  Not sure where they put it, but it's not in the same place.

Where's the Lynx on my desktop ??  Hardy had the Heron, but Lucid has no Lynx.

OK, enough whining.

I burned my disk at a very high speed, forgetting that speed was an issue before.  Both the 32 bit and 64 bit disks checked out just fine.  An improvement that will save newcomers some time I'm sure.

Synaptic still works just fine and installing the stuff I add on was actually quicker than it was in Hardy using comparable hardware.

VirtualBox is really improved over the version I used in Hardy.  One thing that always annoyed me was having to escape from the virtual OS to use my mouse and keyboard elsewhere.  I no longer have to do that and it has made my life easier already.  I also noted that maximizing the window gives me just less than a full screen, which is exactly the way I want it.  I do a lot of remote administration so I'm a lot more at home than what I was with it under Hardy.

Overall the OS seems faster and more responsive than Hardy on comparable hardware.  Both my desktops are identical and the one I'm using as a server has a slower processor with much less RAM, but is still performing admirably.

A word of praise to the community as well since the few problems I had were quickly solved with help I found on the forum.

In summary I have to say I like the Lynx.  I just wish I had a picture of him. (her?)

### Post by ionospheric on 2010-05-10
I like the Lynx, too.

One of the improvements is that the AT&T USBConnect Mercury card now works out of the box. Previously, you had to manually enter the DNS but not any longer. This means that you can get on the Internet wherever there is cell phone reception.

The only reason I am still having to use 9.10 is that I have a Wacom Bamboo tablet where I have to compile the device driver myself, and I haven't figured out how to configure it properly under 10.04.

Other than that, everything I have tried works as in previous versions, and I am sure I will switch to 10.04 completely very soon.

What I like about the Ubuntu philosophy is that they never change anything drastically, so most of the solutions from previous versions still work, and it takes relatively little effort to upgrade to a new version. This has been my experience with Lynx so far.
