### Post by labrador on 2005-04-11
Well, as I learned from Gentoo, having community forums is a big bonus.
You can't get that from Debian or Suse - perhaps with Fedora, but I'm
done with beta RedHat land.

An active and helpful online community with forums should be seen as
part of the features of a distro.  It solves problems that readmes
and FAQs can't.

I've had 2 problems like this already: getting my audigy 2 soundcard working
with newer alsa driver package, and fixing the broken /etc/init.d/apcupsd
script.

All distros have imperfections, but it is an important feature
to be able to fix problems very quickly.

Looking at the bug reports on sound from March and the responses that
nothing can change that impacts the release date, I humbly suggest that
Ubuntu switch to a "it ships when it is ready" method rather than
"it ships on a gold date" method.  Either that or have a gold date
that can change.  Basic hardware functionality out of the box is
an important feature for new Linux users (included in the
set "humans") and reviewers (also in "humans") alike.

### Post by MasonM on 2005-04-13
Committing to a release schedule is a good thing as it provides a goal to be met. When there is no goal things just don't happen. Look at Debian proper. Sarge is STILL in testing, mostly due to politics and the lack of any sort of release schedule.

While I really like Debian, I moved away from it because of the out-dated software and politically-related slow releases. Debian has far too little focus on the user and user input is neither headed nor welcomed. I see these things as the biggest reasons for the rise of Ubuntu and other Debian-based distros.

People like the idea of Debian but their own politics and lack of user focus is killing them as a viable desktop OS and they are becoming just a base upon which a good distro is built. Sad really.

Developers are of course important to any Linux project, but when the developers become THE focus and users are pushed out of the picture completely, the users will move to a distro that focuses on and meets their needs.
