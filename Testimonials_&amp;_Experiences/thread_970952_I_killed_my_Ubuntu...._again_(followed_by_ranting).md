### Post by slinkey1981 on 2008-11-04
So about a week ago I killed my Ubuntu 8.04, not a big deal as it seems to happen to me a lot. LUCKILY, I used a guide on backing up to USB Flash Drive, which should have helped me to quickly restore everything to the way it was before.

I am sorry to say that it didn't work. Not that the tutorial was badly written, or anything that anyone could have foreseen, except for maybe me.

If I had done just a little bit of research into my own cmos/bios features, I would have quickly seen how it was not possible for me to boot from USB (a problem that has since been corrected with a newer bios.)

I have a bad tendency to not let my PC restart after installing recommended updates, I don't know why, it really takes about 45 seconds to reboot to my login screen. I just got used to it in Windows, where my system (not saying everyone's) was so clogged and bloated that I had more like 5 minute shut down times. Who wants to wait 5 minutes while reading emails and just surfing the web? Not me, it's not like I am doing anything that really needs those updates.

I probably had +/-40 updates, and didn't restart my pc so it could do it's little thing.

Then I lost power and God help me, my computer was DEAD. It would say "Grub Loading..." and then the Demons of Linux would print things on my pc too fast to be read by me, or anyone without the power to slow down time.

Did I mention that I gave my brother my Ubuntu cd? Yeah, not the smartest move, but I thought "Hey, I have my stuff backed up on this USB drive, so it's all good. Take my cd."

So I go into the bios and look under boot order... and nothing from USB can be booted. AND, after telling my brother just how great Ubuntu is, I didn't want to call him up and ask him for my cd back. I mean come on, how dumb would I look then?

So I did what anyone would do with a pc with no OS.

I installed XP, talked to the person on the phone, and downloaded 8.04 again. Thanks to all of the people who were downloading 8.10, It took me a while. Haha at me.

So I reinstalled Unbuntu and have been having a ton of fun tseting it up the way I like it, which is actually a lot different than how I had it before.

**************************************************************************

This is where I get into my metaphors and start annoying some random people.

I really see Ubuntu as putting together a puzzle, only I get to decide how the picture looks. There are thousands and thousands of pieces, but I don't NEED them all to have what I want. 

I love how I can customize it (which usually causes breakage, but I'm new) and whenever I think I am done, I see some different thing that I thought was dumb before, until I started messing with it (conky for now).

I know I have sounded overly critical of people in the past for criticizing OSS developers. But, the way I see it, those people (OSS Devs, not Ubuntu Devs) are NOT making money off of support, they aren't doing it for any reason other than it's what they want to do. 

By all means, fill out bug reports when you encounter problems, it's how you help to make it better, but saying "this sux. i hate it and you. i will never use linux again." doesn't help the community, and it actually makes people wonder why the hell they waste their time giving something that they spent their time on, just so some people can scream and yell when it doesn't work.

Also, if you bought a Support Package from Canonical and your OS just won't work, that's one thing, and you DO have a right to complain.

But, if you download randomguy1234's Amarok Library plug-in and it's supposed to give you detailed bio's of the band, and it doesn't work on all of your music. You really DON'T have the right to tell him that he sucks and should die a violent death by being thrown into a fiery pit.

See what I mean? If users piss off all the people who spend their time developing software and then  GIVE it away, we will be left with a very sparse community where people only develop things for themselves and don't release it to the public because they don't want 5 million emails asking the same questions, over and over again.

I am going to make a gross assumption that EVERYONE who comes to this forum can read, and as such, should be able to find, and use the search function. Please use it, many times (Not with 8.10 though, so ignore this all you early adopters) your question has been asked and answered. If you can't follow directions, or they seem too technical for you, ask someone for help in that thread.

If you search here and can't find things, there is always Google. Just remember to use keywords that pertain to your problem. For example, "my screen resolution sucks" might need to be searched more like "Ubuntu 8.04 nvidia gforce 6800 800x600" and you will most likely find something that can point you in the right direction.

I am not saying "Don't Complain" I am saying "Don't jump up and down and get red in the face, screaming at everyone while spittle flies from your mouth" because all that does is make you look like captain a-hole, and not many people feel like helping out a-holes.

I don't personally know a single person who has had a perfect experience with Ubuntu, or Linux in general. It really does take work, some time. But there is a reason that you switched, you took the time to read about Ubuntu and decide to give it a try, I'm just asking that you continue in that manner and search for the answers you need.

I you are polite, I am sure that people will do their best to help you. I see lots of people who scour the web for links on things that might help. But please don't get annoyed or start taking it out on other forum members (who are typically not paid to help out on the forums anyways) when the answers they try to give don't help, or, when they just don't have an answer for you.

I read the forums for fun, I try to help out with what I know about, which isn't very freaking much, and it really just makes me mad when I am trying to have fun, and get the equivalent of someone screaming in my face.

I guess I am just saying that I wish people would try to learn more, there is no end to the things you can learn, if you don't want to try to advance your knowledge, then don't be surprised when things go BOOM in your face. 

I would be more than willing to help anyone with any problem (that I can learn about) saying that things are "too technical" is a cop out. 

Learn about it, ask for help. Let's try to be less short tempered with people who DO post a question that everyone has seen asked, give people a link to an answer.

Let's make Ubuntu Forums the best community possible.

That's all I have for right now.
