### Post by indycar1979 on 2007-11-20
I had a Dell Optiplex GX270 that I was going to dualboot with XP and Ubuntu. I put XP on and spent a couple of days loading all the drivers that I needed to get everything working. Of course, this didn't work very well, as usual with Microsoft, and I still ended up working to get my video driver right after three days. After more frustration with XP, I began to install  Ubuntu from the live CD after trying it out (great feature)!  Amazingly, everything I had plugged in worked immediately (monitor resolution, printer, external HD, camera). I "upgraded" to UbuntuStudio and even my recording interface (Lexicon Omega) worked perfectly! On a side note, the  music produciton  programs packaged in UbuntuStudio are incredible. You would pay thousands to have anything close in XP, but it wouldn't work as well, and XP would crash right in the middle of a recording session (I know that from experience).

Also, after a quick download I even had DVDs and MP3s playing perfectly! I could even convert files with absolutely no frustration.

So here is the rest of the story:

After a few days, I had to restart my computer. What a disaster when my XP partition comes up corrupted, ruining GRUB and my MBR in the process. The XP problem basically took down everything! 

So I thought I was in for a long night, reloading Ubuntu (and only Ubuntu)! Not at all!!! I was up and running in about 20 minutes. 30 minutes later, after getting all the extras loaded in, I was back too normal with Ubuntu.

_Let me make this clear_, I have little experience when it comes to computers and a total "noob" with Linux when I started this whole process about a month ago.  I still don't feel like I know everything, but I am so much happier compared to when we were running XP. Even my wife loves Ubuntu and hasn't had any frustration to date. She is a little bit frustrated with ABC.com and their decision to not support linux with their video player, but I am getting around that with Firefox and Wine (the videos aren't perfect yet, but we are getting there).

If you are considering switching, or just trying out Ubuntu! Seriously, give it a chance! You won't regret the change. I might even set my Grandma up with a new computer running Ubuntu! Why? Because, ubuntu is a linux distro that even my Grandma can run!!!
