### Post by thirddementia on 2008-06-19
About three months ago I first stumbled upon Ubuntu, when one of my classmates raved about it to me. Now lets face facts, my friend while being an excellent human being, I had dismissed 3 years ago as a penguin smooching Gates hater. I have since learned that such dismissals are at best short sighted.  
Ubuntu has been fantastic, and yes there were some issues. Mainly I didn't read the damn manual and I slept through Unix in school. (Sue me, I'm a father of two with a full time job, I sleep when I can).I have spent a pretty good portion of life on Windows. From 3.0 onward.  I have to say that it has never been very good to me and in fact, I spent so much time learning ways to fix it and keep it running, that I had very little time to enjoy the OS.  This is not a stab at Microsoft. It is just the truth.
But I have gotten tired of exorbitantly priced software and the forced hardware coercion that exists between Microsoft and Intel, to name just one.
Ubuntu (Heron) has been fantastic for many reasons. I dont waste my time or space on virus and spyware protection. I dont waste time on foolish things and since I have started using ubuntu, I now have more time to program software, because I spend less time upkeeping an OS.
Ubuntu is eyecandy. Gorgeous operating system with multiple power user features and simple users features. No it will not play your iTunes perfectly when you are sucking down that 6 dollar latte while hanging out with friends. It has better players and options and it offers more usability.  Ubuntu doesnt treat me like an idiot. It treats me like a power user. In Ubuntu, I have almost anything you can imagine installed. Firefox, email,office docs, amarok,comix,netbeans,eclipse,robocode, you name it. It is there. A grand total of 4 whopping gigs used for all the junk I installed on this thing. :)
Free of charge.  I will never go back to windows. I think I will stay right here. I will code and drink my home brewed coffee and smile at an operating system that I think looks great. Is not stupid friendly. And was made with a lot of dedication, love, time and commitment.  
It takes something special to make an OS for no charge, that may still have some things to be worked out.  It takes nothing  special at all to develop a half assed operating system, that relies on consumer bullying tactics to be sold to end users, all in the name of something we are all quite tired of, corporate greed. 
Dont get me wrong. I think that software engineers should be paid for their work  , but not at the expense of monopoly and gestapo marketing tactics.  
Ubuntu has its issues ladies and gentlemen, dont get me wrong. But 95 percent of the time...its my fault not the OS.
Ubuntu I love you for a million reasons... efficiency, community, pretty eye candy, and every integrated development environment I can ever lust for. Hey Ubuntu guys? In case anyone didnt say it.
Thanks. A million.
