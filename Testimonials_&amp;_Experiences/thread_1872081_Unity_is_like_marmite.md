### Post by JHorrocks on 2011-10-30
Maybe this is not the right place to post this, but I just want to comment on the new Unity desktop environment.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Unity is like marmite, you either love it or hate it... or so I thought.

I tried unity back at 11.04 and gave up after only a few hours and reverted back to the original gnome classic. I hated the new interface purely based on my working environment requirements.

Ubuntu 11.10 got released, I ignored it at 1st to continue with my work until I realized mono was shipped with it (I am a .NET developer trying to transition over from Visual studio to monodevelop) so I decided to install 11.10 on a new pc....

Again, I hated it...!!!!

Why?

19 years of being a web developer using the same tools day in day out using the same menu shortcuts etc.. lead to frustration and hate towards the new unity environment, but wait.....

I didnt want to use this desktop environment at all, infact I was 100% against it but realized if I want to stay with ubuntu I needed to stay current with their direction so decided I have to give it a few days of using before commenting.

Its been 5 days actually


........its amazing.


Why the turn around?

It is very intuitive, once you understand the super key (windows key icon on your keyboard) and its shortcuts along with the launcher icons and "STOP" thinking of the bottom taskbar like in windows. I started to realize all the menu items that I once wanted are just a keyboard click away, also that the screen layout is great for wide screen and also for laptops.

There are a few issues, regarding personal taste (ie. I would prefer the unity launcher to be workspace dependent instead of global but maybe this could be an option in the future).

Just want to say a huge thank you to all helping develop ubuntu.....
