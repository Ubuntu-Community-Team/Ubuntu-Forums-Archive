### Post by Puptentacle on 2006-01-24
Back in the middle of December, after a couple of years of trying various live CD distros, settled on Ubuntu. After installing it I absolutely loved it. Everything about it is quite well thought out, the repostitory method of doing installs is wonderful and I was quite happy and going on my way learning all I can about Linux in general and Ubuntu in particular. 

Well, this past weekend, while walking through Barnes and Noble, I spyed on the magazine rack a nice little package with a full five cd distro of OpenSuse 10 and a magazine of pointers and tutorials. I decided to purchase it and install it on my machine to get a taste of another full distro. Well...

I can't STAND it! The install was drawn out and difficult in comparison to Ubuntu. Difficult to add apps to, amazingly little help online in comparison to Ubuntu. And whats more, it was just...slow...in comparison. 

That was Saturday. Last night (Monday) I reinstalled my beloved Ubuntu, ran Automatix, and spent a happy hour or two setting everything up the way I had it before. It was definitely a learning experience, and I'm not saying I won't install Suse on another machine to play with at some point but as far as a "daily use" distro, Ubuntu seems to win HANDS DOWN!

I'll never stray again. Please for give me! [-o<
