### Post by thecapsaicinkid on 2011-09-11
I got into Ubuntu (and Linux in general) from Hoary Hedgehog back in 2005. Loved it, recommended it to all my friends and colleagues. I'd spend time helping people get it set up and switch them from Windows.

I have 2 systems, both are Ubuntu and Ubuntu alone. A media centre and a laptop.

7 years later and it pains me to say it but, I really can't stand it any more. It has turned from a joy to use to something that's a constant irritation. Granted, it's rare something major goes wrong but the little annoyances are numerous and constantly replenished as quick as I can squish them.

Now, I know I'll probably have my technical ability put in question and probably a whole bunch of 'it works fine for me' but rest assured, I am extremely competent at resolving issues (I'm a programmer) The thing is, I'm tired of resolving issues, I just want something to work and STAY working. Every batch of updates brings more niggles, it seems new features have crept up the agenda over stability over the years and I'm not enjoying it.

I'm not going to go into great detail of the numerous issues but off the top of my head, just recently:

Chromium stopped remembering my pinned tabs
All video playback has a block of flickering, offset pixels and playback judders
Removing an option from startup programs sees it come back on reboot
System won't try and reconnect to the wifi if it drops
Spotify fails to start often
Shortcuts to network folders take an age to open
Grub won't auto select an entry (started today)
All 2ch audio is upmixed to all speakers (awful)
Unity is a constant source of annoyances
Various websites (Twitter) peg cpu at 100% (fine on windows)


I've been hoping it will cease becoming such a time sink and I can become a fan again but it's just not happening. So much of my activity is web based so ChromiumOS seems to be my logical next stop. Sorry Ubuntu, I think we're done.

### Post by gardnan on 2011-09-11
Honestly, there are a lot of problems with Linux. However, I would like to point out that a lot of the problems you mentioned are specific to GNOME, Unity, GRUB, and Chromium. 

Also, you have failed to mention how Ubuntu or Linux has caused any of these problems, as they work fine for me, and several others. Now, granted, Linux in general has a lot of multimedia problems, mostly because of proprietary formats and companies that don't want to write drivers or codecs for Linux. However, my opinion is that when it comes to adaptability and customization, Linux is king. Don't like the DE that comes with Ubuntu? Get a new one. Don't like any of those? Write one. Don't like the lackluster kernel support for your keyboard? Patch the driver. Yeah, sure, Linux has a lot of problems, but the great thing is that there is nothing you can't change or fix. 

Also, don't expect Linux to be Windows or Mac OS X. They are both proprietary operating systems developed over many years to be a user-friendly experience. Linux is an open kernel that has been developed to fit the needs of server administrators, programmers, desktop users, and nearly everything in between. People complain about Linux distributions not being the best desktop operating system, when it was never meant to be. Linux was meant to be morphed and changed for different purposes.

### Post by sanderd17 on 2011-09-16
I didn't read the whole thread, so sorry if someone mentioned it before, but ARCH is the way to go for you.

Arch is very suited for tech-savvy desktop users. Ok, it takes some time to set it up, but once you have set it up, it stays working like you want.

I haven't had any little annoyances with Arch (only big ones, like me being stupid enough to erase the MBR etc).

And if something would go wrong, the number of recently changed packages is usually small, so you can locate the problem without problems.

Off coarse, Arch is not suited for everyone (to become a member of the forums, you need at least be able to execute a terminal command without help), but for persons that use their computer heavily (like programmers), Arch is perfect.

Arch also doesn't have the most friendly forum. If you ask something that is explained in the manual, you get an RTFM pretty quick. But yes, as said before, if you know your way around in Linux, there are not many distros better than Arch.
