### Post by dan l on 2008-02-03
So, a few months ago I bought a compaq F730US.  It's a budget laptop - decent specs for a few hundred bucks.  It's a nice putz around machine - unfortunately it comes pre-installed with. 

Need I explain any further why I decided to give Linux a shot? 


Anyway, first couple of install attempts were complete disasters.  I blew up windows enough times and eventually gave up on the Ubuntu install.  A bout a month ago, thoroughly frustrated with Vista, I decided to give it another shot. 

Magically it worked. 

The good:
-I love the speed.  This laptop is heavily taxed by Vista but in Gutsy - it flies.  As a matter of fact, I don't think I've ever even had a hiccup. 

-Compiz Fusion/Beryl whatever-it's-called - I like eye candy.  I hate useless eye candy.  Like, when I started playing with Compiz I figured it would be some dumb Comet Cursor like ap that would be pointless to run.  I can honestly say that with only a few weeks of usage, my habits have already changed.  I group move and tab windows together.  I use the scale function religiously.  Even the cube has a certain amount of utility to it.  And...it doesn't even seem to make the computer wince when I run it. 

-Battery life has gone up significantly.  I'd guess that it's doubled, but that's hardly a scientific conclusion.

-Need a program?  Use the manager.  Obviously, it's not guaranteed that you'll get the best tool for the job and there's plenty of programs that extend past  where the managers look, but I'm not going to complain about how most of my needs are met within moments. 

-OO has come a long way.  Maybe more a testament to those folks at Open Office, but wow...

-Wine - Remarkably easy to get working and shockingly simple to get it to run a game.   Still not 100%  for a few games I like though, so I might leave the vista partition just for gaming. 

The Bad:
-Configuring a wifi card (the much maligned Broadcom card, none the less) was a pain.  I had a lot of trouble with NDISwrapper, but I eventually had a trusted friend remote in and take care of it.

-Queer issue with firefox:  I can't figure out how to make java work right in the default Firefox in Gutsy 64.  On the other hand, swiftweasel's java works correctly.  At least, I think it's a java issue.  LogMeIn is where I have most of the problems - and that's something I need for work almost constantly. 

The ugly:
-Chronic lock ups.  I randomly get total screen freezes.  This would be bad enough, if it weren't for the fact that the like <a href="http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=587905">50 pages of documentation</a> astoundingly provides few definitive answers.  Even worse:  I'm too much of a n00b to even begin to isolate what the problem may be. 


Overall:  I love the new OS and I'm sticking with it.  The lock ups do raise my blood pressure by 30 points, but I assume I'll eventually get it solved but the good here far outweighs the bad.
