### Post by Waskar on 2007-06-12
[COLOR="Blue"]Greetings All,

Where to start?  I am taking time-out from my busy schedule to express my deepest gratitude for the latest release of Ubuntu 7.04!  Since the early nineties i have been a Blue Screen, Crash & Burn junkie.  That's Right, junkie is the best term since it's self demeaning, the experience that is.

One can only imagine the number of times i have had to re-install because of, faulty drivers, buggy software, hardware conflicts, viruses, spam, phishing, oh, don't let me leave out Rouge Updates.  VAR's telling you to call OS support since they broke it.  The need for VAR's to keep all the holes plugged, which of course slows everything down to a crawl.

That is well on it's way, behind me, now that Feisty has been released!  Yes, i have spent better part of (free time) last couple of days configuring my GeForce6 card, dual monitors, intellimouse, themes, e-mail accounts and the like.

Sure, i crashed a handful of times, due to human input errors.  Still, i was/am able to recover, and with the help of the Ubuntu Team, which includes it's Members i have been guided through all the work rounds. Thank You All!

I cannot articulate enough, what a wonderful liberating feeling it is, to have a computer with all the bells and whistles i need, up and running, not to have to dread, 'Will It Float'.  An honorable mention here:  When i did encounter boot failure do to human input error, my gut wrenched and words spewed forth, thinking i would spend many more hours starting over.  HA! recovery works like a charm, if your willing to get comfortable at the command-line.

In Closing:
I have been waiting patiently for a system that provides me a hassle free environment to focus my attention on my work.  Ubuntu's Feisty Fawn provides just that workspace, does so with all that i am accustom and expect from an operating system.  Now all i have to do is join a Windows detox group!

I am pleased to be an (New) Ubuntu Member, will spend newly freed up time supporting and promoting Ubuntu on the Desktop.  

Can't Thank You Enough![/COLOR]

Best Regard,

Scott L Ayon
[Formerly] MS VAR Round Table Member

PS:  I Have Been To The Top Of The Mountain And IT's Open Source![/COLOR]
