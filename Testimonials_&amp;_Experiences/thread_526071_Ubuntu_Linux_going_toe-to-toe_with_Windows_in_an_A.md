### Post by n00buntu NJ on 2007-08-15
Hello all!  

I have been using Ubuntu (primarily) and a few other flavors of GNU/Linux for about 18 months now (since a few months before Dapper Drake)...  
When I first tried Ubuntu I immediately found replacements for everything I used in windows (or gave up on things I couldn't immediately replace), and never looked back!

I work for a Microsoft Partner software-development company in the Pacific Northwest that runs entirely on M$ technologies for all its needs (e.g. Windows / Windows Server OSes; MS SQL, IIS; MS Office 03/07; Windows Mobile; MS CRM; developing in .NET; etc)...

I've recently been fortunate enough to have the opportunity to wipe out XP on my company laptop, and use Linux for my daily production needs.
Since I'm a "Specialist" in our Company's Tech Support department I have quickly gained the attention of the other "computer geek" type people, and have even impressed some decision makers in the company to the point that I was tasked to perform an implementation of Ubuntu Server Edition to manage a 2 TB disk array via a Samba share in a $50,000 - $75,000 mobile processing unit!

Now two computers have been setup in our IT department to use for common IT tasks such as "remote desktop-ing" to (windows) workstations for support, and disc duplicating (they love the easy .iso burning/mounting/etc compared to using Nero in Windows as they had been).

The ease by which I was able to operate within the pure M$ environment made a strong impression on my coworkers - enough that one of them has completely switched 3 of his home computers to Ubuntu, and is contemplating migrating his corporate laptop as well.
Our lead developer has even setup an Ubuntu linux environment at home where he has been playing around with tweaking our latest .NET application to run in Mono.
 
The crazy part is that all of this has happened in the span of only three weeks!  I should have tried this a long time ago!

Needless to say it has been an all-around great experience for me, and I wanted to share it with the community who makes it all possible.

Everytime a potential show-stopper would come up (e.g. "Aha! I'll bet Linux can't do this [input some application or interface here]"), a resolution would quickly be found - most often that resolution would come from these very forums.

So, for anyone who hasn't had the opportunity to share their Linux and/or Ubuntu love with someone who would appreciate it - you can take part of the credit for the successes of others!


Here's to an exciting and successful future for Ubuntu, and/or any other flavor of Linux!  

Now pat yourself on the back!


Best Regards.
