### Post by Kopachris on 2009-08-12
I upgraded my ancient desktop machine to Ubuntu 9.04 a month or two ago.  It was previously running 8.10, but not very well, so I never used it and stuck with my PowerBook G4 with Mac OS X.  In 8.10, the graphics driver (proprietary ATi driver) was the main problem.  It would sometimes lock up entirely and I would have to reboot.  Sometimes, I'd have to drop into recovery mode to reconfigure X.Org.  I also had fits of USB problems.  USB ports would turn on and off unexpectedly, and I couldn't fix it.  The USB ports worked fine in Debian (which was installed alongside 8.10), so I knew it wasn't a hardware problem.

But, I wiped my hard drive clean and installed Ubuntu 9.04.  At first I had a little trouble because I wanted to set up my drive with encryption during install.  I used the alternate install disk, but it always failed.  Deciding that I wanted ext4 more than disk encryption after all, I just stuck with the live installer and installed onto ext4.  The actual install only took about half an hour, and booted twice as fast as before.  Even after setting a GRUB password and turning off auto-login, I can boot and login faster than a faster dual-core laptop with Windows 7 on it (my desktop has an Athlon XP 2800+) that logs in automatically.  Right away, I noticed several differences in performance.  Not only was hard drive access snappier, but audio and video worked perfectly out of the box (well, not OpenGL, but that's an ATi problem).  And on top of that, my USB ports work perfectly, too.
