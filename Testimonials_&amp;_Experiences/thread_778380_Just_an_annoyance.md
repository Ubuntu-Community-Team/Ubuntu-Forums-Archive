### Post by Seti on 2008-05-02
"I had to do a "sudo apt-get update" for it to let me install the restricted NVIDIA drivers."

Yeah, you should ALWAYS do ```
sudo apt-get update
```
as one of the first things you do after an install, especially before installing any new software.
You raise a good point though. You can never please everyone. No matter how polished the finished product is, someone is always going to have a problem with it. Of course the reasons why individuals have problems are of a great variety. One user may have problem with a particular wireless adapter, and another will have trouble installing the right video driver. And then, you have to factor in all the people out there who really don't understand what their computer is doing at any given time, or don't really know how to admin their system properly. How many times do errors originate between the seat and screen? Come on guys, stop blaming Hardy for so many of the things that are just caused by something else.
