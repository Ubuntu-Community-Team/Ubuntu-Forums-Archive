### Post by Aspiring_Failure on 2011-01-14
I'm frankly stunned. I was hesitant at first, -having read a lot about it being God-awfully slow compared to 10.04. But the results are incredibly pleasing.

I've always loved Uuntu's low ram consumption. 10.04 used just about 180 MB after a fresh reboot. Excellent, compared to XP's 260 MB.

My disappointment was the CPU load. An unsettling 4-6%, vs XP's 0-1%.

But after the upgrade, things have gotten a lot better. The ram consumption dropped down to a mind boggling **126 MB** for me! Wow! I don't know how or why it did, but I love it. The CPU load has fallen only slightly, down to 3-4%. It's still better than before though, and I'm extremely pleased.

I also like the revised theme. It's simpler, and feels somewhat more "Ubuntu" than 10.04's, which felt a bit more "Mac".

I'm hoping the 11.04 release is great!

### Post by uRock on 2011-01-16
I am glad the upgrade worked out for you.

RAM loads are different in newer OSes as compared to XP. Ubuntu and Windows 7 reloads many of the programs into RAM, so that you click to open them, then they are readily opened, instead having to be read from the HDD the way XP was designed.

That said, while the idling RAM and CPU usage may be a bit higher, the performance when programs are opened is better.

I prefer a low CPU usage for the simple fact that I am a tree hugger when it comes to saving electricity, which also explains why I don't buy a gaming system to use for browsing and other light load tasks.

There are programs/services that can be removed from Startup Applications that may lower the idle statistics of your machine.
