### Post by trinitydan on 2010-11-30
I have been running Ubuntu since 6.10 on a dual boot with Windows XP.  I am basically a casual home user.  I have no preference attachment to Windows, it was strictly a software thing.  The main one for me was my iPods.  I have an iPod touch and a 160 GB iPod classic.  Back around the time of 8.04 or so, I did something with my 160 GB that messed up the file system which made me have to restore the thing, which on a 160 GB iPod is a major PITA.  After that I was pretty timid of ever trying to sync my iPods with Linux, and it kept me tethered to MS Windows and Apple's iTunes.  Fortuneately I finally gave it a shot again just tonight, and after successfully syncing photos to my iPod touch, I was able to wipe the last remaining copy of Windows under my roof.  :D  A lowly copy of Windows 7 Starter.  I hope you all realize what a great thing Windows 7 starter is for us Ubuntu enthusiasts.  It is utter garbage.  You  can't even change your desktop background  :D  It doesn't even have dual monitor support, on netbooks that are fully capable of handling dual monitors.  With it coming on all these new netbooks, and netbooks making up a huge growing portion of the computer sales, this is going to be a great boon for Ubuntu and Linux in general.  But I don't want to get to far off track, my point was to say how impressed I am with the smooth new look and feel of Ubuntu 10.10.  Even my mom loves it (she had Vista on her machine prior to Ubuntu 10.10 and her computer is vastly more usable now).  Thank you to anyone who is reading this who is participating in the development of Ubuntu or open source software!  I am amazed at how far Ubuntu has come since 6.10.  I am thrilled to be able to use solely Ubuntu Linux to meet all of me and my families computing needs!  Good riddance to bad rubbish!:D
