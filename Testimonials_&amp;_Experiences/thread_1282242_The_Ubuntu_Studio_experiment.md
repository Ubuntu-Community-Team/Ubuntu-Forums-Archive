### Post by Jestersage on 2009-10-04
While using Ubuntu is great for one who is willing to learn, I do wonder how it would do in terms of business, especially refurbishers who sell computers for around $50 to 200.

Thus, begin my experiment — to build a Ubuntu Studio system using used parts that the end product can be sold for $200 or less. However, the real limitation are of following

[LIST=1]
[*] User will be a musician. Even if they claim they will learn, by waving that guitar magazine that features all those "setup and forget it" stuff and amplifier emulator  (which is no-no for real guitar player), it makes me wonder how well he will do when Ubuntu fails at something
[*]  I cannot provide technical support. Well, only once a month.
[*]  the first customer (who will use the test type) will have a tutorial, but in the future, I may not be able to do that, couple with rule no.2, they are basically on their own.
[/LIST]
Despite this, even though I foresee this may fail in the long run, I would still try.

First, I tried it on my Core 2 Duo. 8.04 64 bit. It works fine... occasional freezes, but only during setup. Then I tried it on my friend's computer, a Athlon 64 3800. Works greta until we decide to swap graphic card (forgot which one) and it freezes catatrophically, forcing us to switch back to Ubuntu 8.04 vanilla. In that light, the final test type is going to be 32bit LTS Ubuntu Studio.

So after installing from the alternate disk, I start to change the background and panel setting so it will be more user friendly. Then, seeing the fast-user-switcher-applet was not installed, that was also downloaded. Lastly, stuff that are in vanilla Ubuntu, such as OOo, evolution, pidgin, and Transmission are put in (Why are they left out?! if it's DVD, may as well as go large!)

One of the requirement is the use of midi, and since I have m-audio uno, I ended up using it. Eventually find out that the firmware is available on repository, I downloade dit — but still doesn;t work. In the end, after reading [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96506, ]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=96506,"), I got it recognized.

Now I can use Rosegarden with my midi drums to make a recording of drum beats, but now I need to see if it works with other stuff. Also, I need to figure out how to use ti to control stuff, whatever that means (can someone set up a test to use midi drums as control?)

I maybe missing some other stuff, of course, but as of now, I definitely feel satisfy of Ubuntu Studio... but IMHO, I do not think it is ready to be used for sale in our corporation. Why? Well...


[LIST=1]
[*]  Ubuntu, in the end, is still not Mac or Windows— there are still some userfriendly issues. (I always say that  Ubuntu is a Stick-shift AE86,  Windows is a caddilac with auto, and Mac is a Limo with a chauffeur that works according to the company's rule.) Unfortunately, that customer, and many musicians I know, would like to have a chauffeur in their computer— which as "Linux != Windows" pointed out, is not really for them. Waving a guitar magazine and talking about all those "cool gadgets" is not helping the image.
[*] As of current policy, I cannot provide technical support, not physically or through telephone/online. I can do it about once a month, but that's it. I also do not believe the policy will change, unless the one that give grants to the corporation I work at required technical support.
[*] Customer may not have internet. Real weakness of Ubuntu is that you need to have a working internet connection. Yes, I know it's the same with Windows and Mac in a certain aspect, but With Windows and Mac the drivers come in the disk. Recall rule 2), and you will realize that if there are stuff missing after the computer go out, the you are screw.
[LIST=1]
[*]Aside from drivers, you also have codecs. While you may say "We can just download it or use Linux mint!", We cannot due to possible legal ramification (DMCA, even though I am in Canada)—thus we cannot install it for them. (we cannot even install the official Adobe Flash... don't ask why, not allowed my company)
[*] Customer do not read instructions. Many times, when people phone back to us and ask for the sudo password, we simply need to ask them to look at the document right on the Desktop. same thing with DVD codecs (which we also included the instruction right on the desktop)  I am doing more and more facepalm nowadays.
[/LIST]
[/LIST]
 
No 2) may be able to assist by the ubuntu loco group... maybe. No 3) will require a a policy change of the company. In the end, I find that the real obstacle for Ubuntu as a Desktop replacement could actually be a physical/phoneline technical support, which in turn is due to politics.

However, it's not too bad here. We figure the machine can be sold for $200, monitors extra. In any case, All I can do right now is wait for the customer to pickup the machine, and hope that I got everythign set up... so you may see some updates in the future.

As for me, I will get a used Duo core machine and install Ubuntu Studio... maybe 9.04/9.10 64bit. (Unless I can upgrade from 8.04 to 10.04). Far better than trying to compile Hydrogen on mac.
