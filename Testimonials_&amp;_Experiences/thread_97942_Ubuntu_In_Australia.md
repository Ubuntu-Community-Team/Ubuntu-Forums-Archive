### Post by Phrostay on 2005-12-02
I just switched to Mac OS X (iMac G5) after being a longtime windows user. Was extremely pleased with OS X and my new Mac machine. I had a quick look into Linux when I was about 12 and found it slightly difficult running Redhat on an older computer but never though much about it, seeing as OS X was running off UNIX as the kernel I thought I might as well take another look at Linux again, so I stumbled upon Ubuntu and was very impressed with the principle behind it and the community base that it already had. So I ordered my ShipIt discs (2nd best invention created, No.1 is sliced bread :p) and installed it onto my brothers old, un-used intel laptop. Ubuntu 5.10 breathed new life into it and now I am discovering and trying to learn everything there is to Linux and Ubuntu. I was very impressed with the ShipIt program, as I live in the Northern Territory, Darwin, Australia and I didn't believe the discs would arrive at such an obscure location marked on the globe ;).

### Post by atoponce on 2005-12-02
The Shipit CDs are flat out the best thing ever, including sliced bread.  Since discovering Ubuntu myself, I have managed to hand out over 100 Ubuntu CDs, converting many.  I merely order the CDs, go to my local university, and sit them on a table in the computer labs with a note saying: "Free.  Install.  Enjoy."  They disappear like cotton candy at a fair.  Even my family has shown interest (although they are a bit tougher to convert).
