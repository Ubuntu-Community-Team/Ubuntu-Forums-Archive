### Post by Zireth ZH on 2010-05-07
Well ive been dual booting windows OS and Ubuntu for a while... and used other distros too on diff machines... im still NOOB but ive learned alot in linux...

Im pretty happy with ubuntu, i wanted to let others know about my experiences with ubuntu and other distros being a noob.

Ive read tons of reviews, top lists etc .. u can see everybody claims that the distro they use is the best... but it looks like it all matters on ur taste and how much experience u got...

This is what i think about Ubuntu, comparing to other distros ive tried... 

-The GUI is more polished IMO, and it seems that everything u need is there... For me Ubuntu gnome desktop feels more complete compared to other distros.

When u use Fedora it looks like outdated... and most of the time ull be asking urself where the hell is this, where the hell is that, why it doesnt has this or that... 

OpenSUSE is ok, it has everything well sorted but it seems that they were lazy with gnome GUI look... although i know their "default" desktop enviroment is KDE...

-Ubuntu is easy to use. Software, updating, everything u need is so easy to get compared to other distros.

Debian packages are so easy to install, synaptic "has it all".. alot of packages and software available...

I had a hard time with OpenSUSE...

Ivent tried YUM .. I ran Fedora under liveCD so i dunno ... But YAST was kinda USELESS for me. I had to download tar files all the time.

-OpenSUSE's help and manuals were complete, Ubuntu's help and manuals are not that easy to understand. When u look at it u feel like u wanna run away from it.. its just too much info and too much commands in one page lol. 

-Ubuntu's community is bigger, I could get help from others and find the solution to my problem from other posts easily. In OpenSUSE, dont even bother of waiting for a reply.

I wanted to type alot more stuff but i forgot... 

This is my experience with these 3 distros as a NOOB.... 

Post what u think about ubuntu and other distros...

Sorry for my bad english
