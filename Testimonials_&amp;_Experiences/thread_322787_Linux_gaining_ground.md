### Post by OttifantSir on 2006-12-21
Today I was as at Elkjøp (a translation would be El-buy, a consumer-electronics chains) here in Norway, and lo and behold. What did I see?

A Linux PC!
It was running quite nice,
A version of Linspire
Out of the freezing ice
Now we can't retire

Sorry for the bad rhyme.

Anyway, this is the first time ever I have seen a Linux PC for sale in any such shops. Or a Linux-PC at all for that matter.
However, I believe they are trying to make it less attractive. The specs? Celeron 850 Mhz, 512 MB RAM, 200 GB HD, 64 MB shared graphics, scart-port, optical and 5.1. NO MICROSOFT WINDOWS! (IKKE MICROSOFT WINDOWS!), English Linux-version

It didn't have any fancy box, I didn't check the back for more ports, or ask anything about it, except: Why does the Linux computer only have 850 Mhz. The answer was, that the person I asked didnn't know.

I believe I know. Coupled with the price. A computer in Norway usually costs from about 5.500,- brand new to what you wish to pay for it. This box costs 1.995,-
In other words: Inexpensive.
