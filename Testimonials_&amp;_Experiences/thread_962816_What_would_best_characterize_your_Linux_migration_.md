### Post by aysiu on 2008-10-29
I tried Blag Linux in October 2004 and had a miserable experience (not quite as bad as my experience trying to reinstall Windows without the driver CD and InterVideo WinDVD, but at least Windows offered me familiarity), especially since package management in Fedora-based distros back then wasn't quite so polished, so I ended up in some serious dependency hell.

Eventually, I gave Linux another shot in April 2005 with Mepis and loved it. Having a book walk me through the partitioning process was a huge step in getting me over the unfamiliarity hump (remember, this was in the days before Wubi). In May 2005 I discovered the Ubuntu Guide and the ability to copy and paste terminal commands, and that pretty much moved me over to Ubuntu permanently. I've played around with other distros since then, but I've kept with Ubuntu for the past 3.5 years because the community here has been a good combination of supportive and knowledgeable (I won't name names, but there are Linux forums that are supportive and not helpful at all and others that have really knowledgeable users who are less than helpful to new users).
