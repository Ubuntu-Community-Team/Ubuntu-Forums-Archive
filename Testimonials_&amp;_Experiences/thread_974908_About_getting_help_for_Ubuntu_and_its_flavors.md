The most prominent is the ATI issues people have (which I am not omitted from) sure, I'd LOVE to get an NVIDIA card, but I've seen many support issues for NVIDIA as well. I have a 300W Power Supply, and I don't wish to switch it out. I have ONE x16 PCIE slot, but I don't know which one it is. Even if I did install a new card, it may not work with Ubuntu when I first start using it; and on top of that, my computer often gets above 40 degrees Celsius with all the integrated stuff I use.

A few notes:
-A 300W PSU is tiny compared to any current standard or processor or GPU pairing etc.  Just sayin'.
-ATi drivers stink IMHO-though they HAVE gotten better all around (as they noticed how their drivers were driving away customers), for a while I futzed around with an ATi 1900XTX-it put out a great picture, but the primitive state of their drivers, especially in comparison to what they supply for Windows, caused my to vote with my wallet and get back to Nvidia.
-40C is quite cool for a processor temp (many Intel CPUs are rated for upwards of 75C before they cook)-if with a stock heatsink, for a system temp reading it is a bit high for my liking....of course there is margin for error in readings as well as what you are reading.  But the lower the thermal power rating on your processor the more room your processor has for handling heat (my 125W thermal power AMD6400 is only rated to 65C, Intel and AMD 65W CPUs can go upwards of 80C before slowdown or emergency shutdown)
-PCI slots are keyed, as with everything else on a motherboard.  If you don't know which one is your x16 it is easy to find out-as a GPU will ONLY go in a x16 slot (and only go in one way ;) )


Ubuntu comes with generic enough drivers to read off a new GPU from the get go--you may or may not get native resolution, but it should at put out a picture long enough to install drivers.  I've had this same experience with both ATi and Nvidia boards.

ALSO, with modern cards, I always advise getting a 2-SLOT board with an exhaust blower on the card.  Yes newer GPUs put out more heat, but 2 slot cards are very good about preventing excess heat build-up, so you don't see that much of a difference under normal operation.
