### Post by armandh on 2008-12-21
Those Linux distros I tried in the distant past were a world of frustration, but looking back I can see a lot of my problems were hardware related. They had things I might have been able to work around if I had any clue at all. but looking back I see those things as still out of my depth. 

Then a year and a half ago I tried Ubuntu. It was the first distro that the basics worked out of the box for me. sound, picture, apps, and internet. here was a distro that I could use. 

Now I must admit to a few rough spots. Printers etc, whose names have gone the way of Packard and Studebaker, whose win 2K beta drivers limp along in XP, will never install with the ease of an HP laser jet 5, if at all. I got over that.

Apps that are such an effort to switch from makes it is worth keeping the dual boot. Sure there are programs that might work, but if you have 15 years of data, picture how useful Oo would be if it could not open a .doc or .xls 
I wish they would port to Linux.

So much for the recidivists who write of their [and my] frustrations. on to the good news.

every day it gets better. medibuntu.org solves all those pesky codex problems. a grudging acceptance of restricted drivers has made some of my problems a thing of the past [bcm wlan]

as for my un safe surfing brother in-law.......
a give away box with ubuntu in it never comes back to be reloaded, and if it is a hardware problem [other than HDD] just toss the drive in another junker. gone in 60 seconds.

All the talk of how much better Linux is does not help the frustrated step one user with incompatible hardware and not enough ram.

we will loose a few backsliders for whom the hill is just too steep, but often, just as it happened to me a year and a half ago, the "better Idea" light goes on when, with another try, it just works.
