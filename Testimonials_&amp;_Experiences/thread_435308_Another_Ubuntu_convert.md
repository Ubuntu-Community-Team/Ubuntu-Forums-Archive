### Post by aschueler on 2007-05-06
First, let me say that I have been dabbling with linux for a few years now.  I have tried SUSE (first try) then on to debian (nice, but at the time used an older kernel than any of my hardware liked), then gentoo.

I really enjoyed gentoo, but it would take 1-2 weeks for me to putter on it enough to get it installed.  I really liked the installation instructions, and it taught me tons.  However, almost every update seemed to lead to something getting borked.

I currently have the 32 bit Kubuntu installed on my AMD64 3800+, mainly to give it a fair chance instead of wrestling with 64 bit stuff.  I can't believe how quickly and easily it set up -- I have a dual boot (two hard drives) system, and I did a manual partitioning scheme.  The GUI installer was the first I have used that actually worked.  I was surprised to find it put grub in the correct hard drive with the correct settings, without me having to do anything!

My ATI 1900X works on the first try, my printer works without really needing manual installation, etc etc all out of the box.  The only major thing I have done really is to change to Kubuntu.

This is a linux my parents could use!

I have a few questions that I will post eventually, but just wanted to congratulate the programming community on this distro.
