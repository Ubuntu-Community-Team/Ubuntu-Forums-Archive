I was just reading an article in the Nov 6, 2006 edition of eWeek titled [**Making Web memories**]("http://www.eweek.com/article2/0,1759,2054062,00.asp"). It's about the [**Internet Archive**]("http://www.archive.org/index.php"). You may have used their WayBackMachine at some point to view old web sites from the past.

**[SIZE="5"]The Internet Archive houses over 4 petabytes of data and they use UBUNTU![/SIZE]**

*[SIZE="4"]"Nagios isn't the only open-source application used at the Internet Archive. The PetaBoxes run Canonical's Ubuntu distribution of Linux."[/SIZE]*

Cool huh?
