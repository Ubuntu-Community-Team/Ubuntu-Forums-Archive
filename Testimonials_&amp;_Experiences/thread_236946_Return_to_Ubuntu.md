### Post by pessimist on 2006-08-15
Tried an earlier version of Ubuntu, can't remember which one and found it distinctly "clunky".

Have been using Fedora as a standard for the past year or so and have been happy with it.

In the last couple of weeks have acquired a new laptop with an internal wifi card, Fedora didn't want to know.  I've also had big prblems with Fedora5 and ndiswrapper.

Have now installed Ubuntu Dapper on the laptop and have to say I'm impressed.  It recognised the internal card "out of the box" and installing ndiswrapper for an external USB device was a breeze.

Had a few problems with the display driver (nvidia) but they weren't hard to sort.  Many thanks to nvidia for actually providing drivers.

The only things not working are the sound card and TV card and I'm not too bothered by that. 

I was impressed enough to install on another (less bleeding edge) laptop and was even more impressed.  Everything (including the sound card) worked "out of the box".

Ubuntu has clearly moved on in the last year.

I think I'll stick with Fedora for servers, but I think Ubuntu gets the vote for desktop machines.
