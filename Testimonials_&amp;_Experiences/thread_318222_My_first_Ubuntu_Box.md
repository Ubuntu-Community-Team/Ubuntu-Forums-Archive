### Post by stanjam on 2006-12-13
OK.  I have a lot of computer experience.  Most of it is Windows related, some Linux, some Unix.  I am currently going for my MS in information assurance and hold a lesser degree in network security.  I was drawn to Linux because of its wonderful security attributes and the good selection of security programs.  I also teach Linux at a local College.

I started out with Fedora Core.  In fact I have a Fedora Core server running downstairs.  Nice distribution, but definitely not for everyone.  I recently decided to put Linux on my laptop instead of running it through VMWare.  I decided to weigh Fedora against Ubuntu for this purpose.  In my experience I have found that often one distro may work better than another on any given machine.

Fedora went on but I could not get the wireless networking going.  Neither my internal Intel IPW2200 or my D-Link pcmia card would work without a whole lot of effort.  I tried the Live CD of Ubuntu Edgy and the wireless cards were both recognized immediately!  Well that settled it I installed Ubuntu!

Now my laptop I am using is a barebones Asus Z71V.  I love this thing (I really enjoy building my own computers).  It has an Intel Pentium M 2.0 Ghz processor, an Nvidia go 6600 vid card, 2 gigs of ram, etc etc.  What truly amazes me is that everything pretty much worked flawlessly OUT OF THE BOX!  Sound, video, headphones, wireless, touchpad, usb trackball, flash drives.  Very good stuff.

I installed the latest NVidia drivers and also Beryl.  Beryl was a pain to install on this system.  Compiz and Beryl both gave many errors.  I wish I had known about Automatix2 Bleeder then!  I got it working and love the results!

Then I found Easyubuntu and Automatix.  These programs are really nice.  Now I don't mind digging around and following install guides but the fact that someone has gone ahead and made all these scripts for Ubuntu Edgy is a wonderful thing and they have gone a long way in making Linux easier for the average user.

So I must say I am very impressed with Ubuntu.  While I don't think I will be replacing my server with it any time soon I do believe it has found a place on my laptop for the long haul.  The only thing I don't like are the default color schemes and graphics.  The brown really turns me off and has been a major reason why I have not tried Ubuntu earlier.  I am changing all that though!
