### Post by Jekshadow on 2009-12-24
I started out using a computer at a young age, 6 or 7 I think, and I immediately liked the idea of a computer. About a year later, my family purchased a new computer, running Windows XP and I got the old one running Win98. I played around on it like any child would do given admin privileges, and enjoyed it until I was given a Dell Inspiron 9200 laptop for the Xmas of 2003. The laptop ran WinXP and I started noticing how unstable of an OS Windows really was, but I just dealt with it, as I had not really heard of Linux and I was not going to get a Mac. Then, in 2005, I was tasked with building a server for my dad's business, and installed XP on it. I had planned to run IIS on it, but it just sat around and never was used, so I kind of adopted it for my own computer. It worked well, and I upgraded it to Vista when service pack 1 came out, only to realize how horrible Vista actually was. I had heard about Ubuntu from a friend's brother who is a few years older than me and I decided that if it was free, and will run on my Laptop, it must be better than Vista, so I downloaded it, burned it to a CD and installed it on my Laptop (I think this was 7.04). Instantly, I was amazed at how much faster and smoother it ran than Vista, on a machine with a quarter of the RAM and a much slower processor. I used it more than the desktop actually, and it worked very well until the Xmas of '08. This is when I got my current laptop, a Dell XPS M1330. I booted it, and instantly realized why I had been using my Laptop with Ubuntu so much more than my Desktop with Vista. After about two weeks of having Vista on the Laptop, it started refusing to boot. Every time I would boot it, it would give me a blue screen of death. I installed Ubuntu 8.10 and it worked great, and ran at an unbelievable speed on my new laptop. My previous laptop became a Debian server, along with my desktop until the motherboard died for an unknown reason. I do not have Windows installed on any computer, and I am trying to convince my parents to at least look at Ubuntu.
