### Post by cipher_nemo on 2007-11-28
First, I'd like to thank the development teams who've worked on 1.) better integrating access to closed-source nvidia drivers, and 2.) better integrating MythTV 0.20. over the past couple (or few?) versions of ubuntu. All of you contribute so much for so little praise and next to no compensation. Know that so many ubuntu users are grateful for your investment into what should be the ultimate Vista-killing O/S.

More specifically, I've noticed an amazing difference between when I first tried and bombed with ubuntu (Dapper Drake), and when I more recently installed and kept ubuntu (Gutsy Gibbon and Feisty Fawn) on two HTPC systems. My first failed attempt, with 6.06, was mostly because of a lack of integrated mythtv package support and unusual issues with ubuntu and ivtv on my first HTPC system. Now it seems like all of that has changed with 7.06 and 7.10.

Installation of MythTV in ubuntu 7.x now makes the process in all other distros seem like a nightmare of obfuscated chores.

Ubuntu has now won my loyalty. I've switched from Fedora Core on my HTPCs to ubuntu, and I'm not looking back. And when Windows XP is no longer supported by Microsoft, my desktops will run ubuntu as well! Microsoft killed any inkling of brand loyalty with me when they released Vista, pushed it on consumers, and forced it with PC distributors. The whole fiasco of Vista suddenly instilled me with a whole new respect for the open source communities, and the one distro that sought to free people from Windows. Thank you ubuntu!

The massive efforts and community-driven model behind ubuntu has certainly inspired me. In time I hope to give something back to the community when I transition from .NET, C#, and MS-SQL to LAMP and C. I'm stuck with an employer who is integrated in the Microsoft world. Hopefully I'll find time to transition by helping the ubuntu MOTU team.
