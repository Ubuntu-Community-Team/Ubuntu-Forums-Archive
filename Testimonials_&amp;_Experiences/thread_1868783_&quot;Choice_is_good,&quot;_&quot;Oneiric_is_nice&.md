### Post by Viman on 2011-10-24
I recently upgraded to Oneiric after much doubt and thought about doing so. And the end result is...

**Ubuntu 11.10 is nice, and you people hating on the new desktops need to chill.**;)

Keep in mind that this is coming from a user who ditched Unity first thing when upgrading to Natty and praised Compiz like a god window manager. And even then, I have to say that Canonical et all did a pretty good job in this newer, more mature Unity Shell. 

I feared that by upgrading I would not be able to get the two-panel standard desktop ever again and regret my decision, but I in fact liked it.

Personally, I feel that people in charge of advertising Ubuntu need to emphasize the importance of **choice** here. Many people (Ubuntu users too!) go around thinking that if Unity isn't good, they will have to ditch the entire OS. But hey, isn't the good thing about Linux the freedom of choice? I personally chose, for example, to stick with the GNOME Shell because I liked it more than Unity. But you still can choose LXDE, Xfce, KDE and a zillion more DEs that may please you!

I don't get why there's so much flaming in this section, when all people need to do is install GNOME 3.X and run it without the shell! Compiz works flawless there.

Anyway these are my two cents. I don't get what Linus T found so bad about the GNOME Shell...

Happy computing!
