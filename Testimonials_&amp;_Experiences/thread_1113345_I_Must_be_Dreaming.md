### Post by malfindin on 2009-04-01
I WAS a MS certified on-site computer tech working for a company in Las Vegas, and now my biz is as a full-time Linux tech doing Windows to Ubuntu conversions. Has my experience with Ubuntu been a positive one? Why yes! Have there been any speed bumps? Well of course. Here is the balanced and true story of my origin tale (and at no time did I fall in a vat of radioactive waste).

A friend service tech at my hardware vendor gave me an Ubuntu 8.04 disk to check out. I got back to my company's shop and popped it in a new-build PC. During the install process I was distracted by a client's tech-support phone call. After getting off the phone, I walked back to the sys and the only driver missing, was the video card. 1 reboot later I was done with the install. I spent the next 2 hours trying to figure out which one of our other techs had searched for and downloaded all the drivers for this computer; I was mad someone had messed with MY PROJECT--and I think you know how this ends--only to find out no one had touched it. It was just that easy!!!.!!!



I then spent the better part of the day searching for VISTA drivers for a single PC and it wasn't long after that, that I decided to leave MS behind me and become a full-time Linux tech.

Since then I have done about fifty conversion, and I now have 20 or so clients that think Ubuntu is the greatest things to come along since the wheel. One such client was so grateful, she is now my girlfriend and continues to “thank  me” to this day.

It took me about 2 months to figure out my way around Compiz Fusion. I still set it up from scratch on all my installs and I would like to hear if anyone knows a faster way???

I was in charge of the used sys rebuild program at my former company and out of the 50 or so systems I have tried to install Ubuntu (Kubuntu/Xubuntu) on, I have only failed on 2. I wasted no time on these and simply tossed them in the trash. For all I know they had bad hardware and it wasn't even a Linux incompatibility issue.

My latest thing is to install a mirrored version of my clients systems to a USB-thumb-drive, so they are not only backed up, but can live boot their computers (or install them) on any computer they might find themselves in font of. This is too cool for words of course and could take up an entire post.

I have read some very different experience tales in the Forums and am mystified as to how anyone could have anything, but a totally positive experience with the miracle that is Ubuntu.

This is a legit question and I encourage people to respond.

PLEASE LIST YOUR SYS SPECS AND TROUBLE SHOOTING STEPS!

Or else your post will teach me nothing. I might then also be able to steer you in a less upstream direction and help you to have the same kind of experience I have had. Can't promise you a girlfriend though! Lots of  love.

Malfindin

### Post by malfindin on 2009-04-01
No it was not more profitable, though the type of service I now offer is very different. My old company actually banned me from installing Ubuntu on clients systems because of the drop off in biz caused by the lack of virus removal and windows instability issues. Their complaint, "Ubuntu is TOO good".

I tried to tell them that we could do just as well by generating new clients through word of month (new Ubuntu users like to show off their systems), but they lacked the advertising budget and vision to try anything new.

I now get as many clients as I can handle during my morning coffee at Starbucks with what can only be described as walk-byes. Remember the Field of Dreams, "If you build it they will come."? It seems that with Ubuntu, "If you DEMO it, they will want it!".

My current business is to install Ubuntu for free and only charge for support, training and "extra services". I think Canonical would be very proud of me.

I also charge for resizing partitions if people want to keep there MS Windows and have Windows/Linux dual boot.

The real trick is to offer the "extra services", one of my best sellers is backup to Live-bootable USB-Thumb-Drive. It's like having your whole computer on your key-chain and it will run on nearly anybody's computer as long as you can live without desktop effects working perfectly every time.

As long as we opened up this can of beans. Can anybody out there think of really cool Linux/Ubuntu related things the average new user might not be able to do for themselves that they would pay to learn???.???

Oh and by the way. I never just do a service with Ubuntu (like I did with MS)...I always offer to teach the client how to do it themselves, so every time I visit one of my clients it's for something different and new.

My goal is to make myself go away--this is by far counterbalanced by new clients and a level of customer satisfaction that Bill G. will never know. I have gotten as many as 3 referrals from a single client and average at least 1 or 2. Do the math! Not too hard to start a biz if you are good.
