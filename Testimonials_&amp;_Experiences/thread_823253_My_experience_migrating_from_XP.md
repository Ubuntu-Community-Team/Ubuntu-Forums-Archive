### Post by 843 on 2008-06-09
I've been hearing about the ups and downs of Linux for quite some time, but I can't really be bothered trying to learn a new OS after 6 years of having XP. I know well enough about XP to keep my desktop spyware and malware-free, but compromising speed and performance, so I don't see a reason to migrate to Linux, you know the saying 'don't fix what's not broken'. With my experience using Windows, that is a very true saying. Yet the trigger eventually came when I got myself a laptop, with Linux already installed.

First of all, I live in Indonesia. Piracy is so prevalent that even police officers and government authorities use pirated softwares on their desktops, else they use Linux. I've never heard of an Indonesian buying genuine softwares unless they're filthy rich or just want to brag. The natives would even be considered rich if they own a computer.

Being poor and stingy, I refuse to purchase a genuine XP. No offense, but who the hell wants to pay an insane amount of cash to get a problematic OS? Don't get me started on Vista. I have a DVD copy of Vista sitting on my desk, collecting dust. Of course it's downloaded off the net.

At first, I reformatted to my XP. Making the copy genuine is a piece of cake if you know where to look, but I have to bring the laptop overseas somewhere in September this year, and I heard the security at a local airport have finally started checking for pirated softwares on laptops, though I'm willing to bet they use pirated copies at home, and that they are in it for the money (the fine is about US$1000, who knows how much of it goes to their pockets.)

Here's where the problem lies. Placing the laptop among the baggage is not an option. There have been cases where locks have been broken and valuable contents stolen (usually jewelry or electronic goods). Shipping laptops overseas is a risky gamble. The only choice left is to carry it in my backpack. My laptop, of course, doesn't have the license sticker, so I'll be in trouble if I can't produce them, in spite of having a (modded) genuine XP.

The sensible option left for any rational man is to migrate to a free OS, namely Linux. I got a Linux magazine with a copy of Ubuntu and Mandriva in DVD (OS from a magazine with no extra cost!?). I know I can just reformat to Linux and reformat back to Windows when I'm overseas, but I don't really have anything against Linux, so might as well learn to use it. The result? I'm very pleasantly surprised.

I know close to nothing about programming, but I know my way around XP. Aside from incompatibilities from my games, everything works flawlessly. *Everything* is faster and downright more stable. I expected slowdowns when I noticed the dynamic desktop theme, but they never come. I'm also shocked that I can still run new applications, surf the web, and use the computer normally when I'm running multiple applications. It feels as if my 1MB of RAM is suddenly doubled.

Installation? No problem. Getting my way around Ubuntu? Unexpectedly easy. Network sharing? A few clicks do the job nicely, without the need to reboot. Repositories and Synaptic? I didn't know it was even possible. I no longer feel the need to save my documents every few minutes in case the system crash. Goodbye spywares, virii, malwares, trojans, dialers, activex, registry, and piracy. I won't miss you.

Bottom line? I have no regrets. I'm still not used to browsing files and folders, but I know I'll be browsing smoothly in time. I still keep XP in dual boot for games and specific programs that don't work yet with Wine, but the need to use XP will eventually dissipate with Linux gaining more and more support.

Speaking of support, the support for Linux is astonishing. With XP, my only choice is Google and some technical help forums, but response is slow and these don't always fix the problem. Technical support by phone is avoided if possible. With Ubuntu, support comes within *minutes* of posting, and I'm surprised at how active and helpful the community is. I guess it proves that without the support from community, Linux is not possible.

Sorry if it's a bit too long, just something I wanna share. :)
