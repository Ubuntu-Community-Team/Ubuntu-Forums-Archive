### Post by thulefoth on 2010-02-06
cwalker1960 makes some good points, and sketches a perspective that works to Linux' & Ubuntu's benefit.  

But Windows is not without it's GRUB-equivalents - and then some!  The Windows Registry, for example.  Like GRUB, it's not intended that 'the casual user' is going to have much - preferably nothing - to do with it.  But like GRUB again, sometimes things go wrong with the Registry, and then one has to study arcane info and deal with an 'unfriendly' system-component.  

It appears that something interesting is going on with the introduction of a partial implementation of GRUB2 on Ubuntu 9.10, in beta form.  Something fairly important - and close at hand - must have motivated this decision ... else Ubuntu would simply have waited for GRUB2 to become, if not mature, fully-functional and out of beta.  

For another example of Windows 'user-hostility', consider the long-standing existence of "DLL-hell".  Libraries are a malignant curse on Windows OS products ... but the - very closely - comparable 'Nix library system is a model of 'good citizenship'. 

Microsoft Windows is in decline.  Whatever they're doing, we should let them continue, should not bother them while they are at it.  Linux, under these circumstances, should just continue their good work, and let time take it's course. 

It's like when one's political opponent is digging himself into a hole.  It's not necessary or even a good idea to attack him!  Just let him continue! ;)
