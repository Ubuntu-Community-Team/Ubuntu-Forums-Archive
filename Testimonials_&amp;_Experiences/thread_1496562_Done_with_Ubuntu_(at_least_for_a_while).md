### Post by kaldor on 2010-05-29
I've loved using Ubuntu since 7.10 and never had many problems at all. I just found that over time, it got worse and worse.

7.10: Solid, stable, fast.

8.04: Solid, stable, fast, but crashed with compiz all the time.

8.10: Fast, but froze on occassion.

9.04: Skipped

9.10: Buggy, annoying, slow boot, audio problems, graphics problems, freezing more common.

10.04: glitchy bootup screen, 1 minute boot time, shutdown taking ages, nvidia graphics issues, unstable feeling, a wine program I used didn't run properly anymore, slow and worst of all... overheats!!

Everything took so much longer than needed on 10.04. I put 10.04 on my desktop with 512 RAM and 3.2 ghz intel processor (weird balance, I know but it was given to me) and it's incredibly laggy and slow to boot. I can't even drag a window or open anything without it being overly choppy. This is even without any desktop effects. 

For now I have switched to openSUSE where I feel like I'm running an older version of Ubuntu again; 15 second bootup, very clean even with desktop effects, doesn't get hot anymore. Lucid kept getting to temperatures of 85 degrees (185 fahrenheit) and causing my laptop to shut off. openSUSE usually doesn't get higher than 55 degrees. The desktop PC also works flawlessly, no choppyness or waiting up to a minute for a terminal to load. 

So what is happening to Ubuntu? It's getting more and more unstable as it's getting more popular. Hopefully it won't give Linux a bad name!
