### Post by e^(i*pi) on 2007-10-24
My experience with Gutsy didn't really start out so well...

I tried to upgrade shortly after it was officially released and about half way in my computer freaked out.  Everything totally locked up and I was not able to reboot.  I'll admit, I was a little angry over the weekend.  I went so far as to install PCLinuxOS on my computer, thinking that what I needed was a different distro,  After two or three days though, I really missed Ubuntu.  I realized at that point, that I had never "missed" an operating system before.  Every time I upgraded to the newest version of Windows I stopped thinking about the old version, and when I ditched Windows for Ubuntu a year ago I never missed it at all.  After calming down from my initial bad experience, I decided that maybe it was just one of those things that happens with computers from time to time, and decided to try a fresh install.  I just finished installing tonight, and WOW!!!!  I fully expected to have to battle with my computer to get my widescreen monitor to display correctly, but there it was, perfect 1440x900 as soon as it booted up.  That has never happened since I switched to Linux.  Then I was ready to battle with my wireless card, and what do you know, it worked too.  After getting over the shock of all my good fortune, I took the time to really look at the new system, and I must say that it is beautiful, really a work of art.  I was foolish to think that another OS could replace Ubuntu.  It's nice to be back.
