### Post by Juan Largo on 2011-09-27
I've been using Linux since I bought my first computer 5 years ago when I retired.  I did a lot of reading before I made the purchase, an it seemed that Linux was the way to go.  The computer came with Windows XP on it, but the only time I used it was when I downloaded and burned my first Linux Live CD.  I'm an old guy (age 67) and am not a computer programmer in any way.  I worked as an editor, and only used computers for word processing and other easy tasks.

After using other KDE Linux operating systems, lately I've decided to make Kubuntu my primary operating system.  I downloaded the i386 version Kubuntu 10.04.3 because I want a safe, predictable operating system, and I don't want to upgrade it every other week.  I could use Gnome or Unity, but I really prefer KDE with Folder View and the Classic KDE menu.  In short, I like to keep things simple, and I look at my computer as an appliance and not a hobby.

The things I like most about Kubuntu 10.04.3 are the long term support and the large amount of software it offers.  I was unhappy with my other Linux operating systems because many of the packages I desired were available for Ubuntu, but not for the systems I was using.  I use a lot of software for an old guy, but I just don't want to be bothered with compiling it.  I just like to click and install it.

Once installed, Kubuntu 10.04.3 is very stable with no surprises.  I like being able to turn the 3D desktop effects on and off with the click of a mouse, and I can use 3D effects without causing any problems with my system.

I don't want to spend time programming and fiddling around with my computer.  I had a minor problem getting Kubuntu working, and the people in the support forum were very helpful in solving my problem without talking over my head or referring me to a manual I can't understand.

My wife (age 63) uses the computer to email our friends and relatives, and to do web surfing.  She doesn't know anything about computers, and leaves those details to me.  So she's going to be using Kubuntu whether she knows it or not, but I'm going to set it up so she won't be able to tell the difference.  

Yours truly,

Juan Largo
