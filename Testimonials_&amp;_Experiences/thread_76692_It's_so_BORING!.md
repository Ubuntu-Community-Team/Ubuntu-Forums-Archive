### Post by Ubunted on 2005-10-15
So I stuck in the 5.10 install CD, booted to it, wiped the drive and installed. Less than an hour later I've got all my software installed (codecs mainly and Firestarter and GnomeBaker), the nvidia drivers, k7 kernel, my desktop the way I like it, and I'm done. What kind of "experts-only" OS is this?

When I install Windows it takes me on average three to four hours to format, install, update, reboot, update, reboot, update, reboot, update, reboot, install video drivers, reboot, motherboard drivers, reboot, reboot, reboot, USB drivers, reboot, Audigy drivers, reboot, printer drivers, *monitor* drivers, ***mouse*** drivers, ***KEYBOARD*** drivers, a disk defragmenter, a page file defragmenter, reboot, Firefox, Antivirus, openoffice, a registry and temp files cleaner, Acrobat Reader and an IM client, JUST to get the same functionality, speed and reliability that I enjoy in Ubuntu.

Seriously though, it's VERY nice to escape the daily updating of antispyware, AV, Firefox, Windows, Acrobat, Gaim, Java and pretty much EVERYTHING ELSE through its own update solution. I just love sitting back with a stupid grin on my face when news of the latest Windows worm hits the mainstream media.

### Post by megamania on 2005-11-21
Ubuntu works. It's my only OS at the moment, as I permanently wiped Win off of my hard disk.

Saying it is boring is a different matter though. Linux can be quite frustating from time to time.

Examples:
- pendrives: a lot of people has problems (writing 100Mb in 4 hours!)
- palm support: it is there, but can't be compared to the "native" programs for apple and win.
- wireless
- video cards
- ipod support
- sound
- skype
- cd reading speed, cd burning

As I said, it is my *only* OS at the moment and that means I definitely love it. But saying that Linux/Ubuntu is boring and easier than Win Xp is just misleading in my opinion...

I see Linux as a Formula 1 car - hard to drive but powerful and highly rewarding in the medium term, while Xp is a sports car (ok, maybe a city car) for everyday use - easier to use at the beginning but frustating in the long term.
