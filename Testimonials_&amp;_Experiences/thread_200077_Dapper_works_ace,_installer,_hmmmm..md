### Post by WeldingMarathon on 2006-06-19
I've kind of been dabbling with Ubuntu for a year or so on a second partition and through various Virtual PC machines but never really thought I would change completely. For work I have to use Windows with Visual Studio, MSSQL, and even the dreaded IIS. I've also played with various other distros and thought I preferred Fedora until I tried 5.10 and played with apt-get. Once you see that working you don't want anything that doesn't include it.

I installed 5.10 and I found I was using it more and more but still not enough to switch. A couple of weeks ago the update icon appeared and presented me with the option to switch to 6.06. OK, I thought - its late - I'll let it do it overnight. 

Would it upgrade my whole OS ?

Nah - it died. Just stopped, no messages - nothing.

I just put it down to this notebook not being too reliable (it overheats and dies) so I downloaded and burned a CD. 

The new graphical installer is, well, kind of OK I suppose. I really resent having to wait for the "Live" cd to load and I found the text mode installer slightly easier - especially when it came to partitioning. After a few attempts and loads of Tetris - so not a complete waste then - it installed. 

And its really nice. I join and browse the forums and - whoa - VMWare is available - not just the player but a server beta, and a whole load of info on how to install it and XP. So I decide to switch - no beer was involved in this decision, although it was involved in the subsequent attempts to get it all working.

I copied the stuff I needed onto another PC and then installed from scratch. Around 20 mins of Tetris later I was logging into the Ubuntu only machine - everything running fine. 5 mins later EasyUbuntu was installing everything else   I needed. The VMWare / XP dance took a little longer than expected but that was mainly XP's fault, but I have a working XP with VS, MSSQL etc - for when I need it. I had to fix VMWare when Ubuntu updates broke it, but that was OK - the VMs were safe. 

I now have XGL etc running too - wiggly windows, rotating desktop, transparency - wow. I've seen loads of demos of Vista but this seems far more intuitive and easy to use - the F12 window thing is my fave feature. The only downside of this is the fiddling required to get it working but the depth of info on the forums is amazing and usually helps you through, though not always.

The bad news ? Well I was so impressed I wanted to swap my desktop (AMD 3000, 1GB Ram, SATA) as well and it works, but I can't get all the nice XGL stuff to load and run at an acceptable speed, but the machine, OS and VMWare all run really well. And I suppose the fiddling to get this all to work is a little annoying but I can see this all getting installed as part of the default OS very soon given the speed of developments here.

Oh - and I did use this switch from Windows as my gaming platform to justify spending some money on a console instead :cool:
