### Post by imperius69 on 2008-03-19
Hello
Im was IT professional, all my life worked with windows, but never did like it hihihi, but i was always scared of linux, did try sometimes, red hat and other distros, but was only install see the OS and the problems and them format. I had pretty mush given up linux in this last 5 years most couse of work related and also couse linux was very time consuming. Then a Friend of mine told me last year about ubuntu 7.04, i was like wuuoo looks nice, but stayed like that. Well just arrived home from abroad and was bored so lets see this ubuntu (now 7.10) thingy is.

Heres my experience.

I tried  ubuntu, first in a very old machine (P2-300MHz, 384 Mb Ram) and was slowwww, but i expected that, had a lot of problems, i expected that couse its a old hardware PC, came here to foruns and corrected them all, but, i wanted to see the full power of ubuntu, namely compiz and stuff, so i setup a new hard drive on my laptop and intalled it, for the ppl who want to know, my laptot is an Acer Aspire 1604LC with 1G Ram, ATI Mobility Radeon 9000 64 Mb, and gess what ... all system worked out of the box, even the ATI, compiz works like a charm, wireless (PCMCIA card) works better than in XP. The main problem i had was with slowwwwwww boot, well, forum time and i found the solution, was the splash size, corrected and now im using it (40second boot time :) ). Not saying i didnt have other problems (suspend/resume dont work), but for the rest of hardware related, all hardware detected and working.

And the more i use it, the more ubuntu is growing on me, i like it makes me fell like i did on DOS time (when i was a kid discovering a computer), something that is new and that is always right so I as the user have to dig and see what's the problem. Made me like computers again.

I need to change back to the XP HD drive (work related), but i dont want to, so im thinking and doing a dual boot.

So thats my story, not totally converted but getting there.

Im happy :) so a BIG Thank you to the developers :) and keep up the good work :)
