### Post by spoovy on 2010-10-22
I had used Ubuntu in the past but stopped in favour of other distros, mainly Slackware, CentOS and Debian. But..

My laptop bust two days ago - completely kaput.  I really can't do without at the moment so I had to buy another.  I bought a cheap asus for £250 new.  1Gig RAM, very basic spec.  I naturally went for a minimal Debian install first. I tried to install Squeeze but couldn't get the installer to encrypt no matter what I did.  I needed something installed, encrypted, fast, so I grabbed an Ubuntu Lucid CD I had lying around and installed that, cos I remembered it had a simple, reliable installer which also handled encryption.


I honestly can't believe how good the result is.   A couple of hours  configuring and I have a near-perfect desktop.  the GNOME desktop is fast (better than I dared hope - I tried out icewm as well and the difference is literally insignificant); and every package I needed was available in the repos, and installed quickly and easily.

I'm very ashamed at how much the anti-Ubuntu, and sometimes anti-GNOME, badmouthing has obviously affected my views.  I am massively impressed with this release, and knowing that it is supported for another two years is really stunning.

Thanks to all at Ubuntu, particularly those of you who aren't getting paid! :)

### Post by Spice Weasel on 2010-10-23
Linux Torvalds (creator of the Linux Kernel) on GNOME:

> "This 'users are idiots, and are  confused by functionality' mentality of Gnome is a disease. If you think  your users are idiots, only idiots will use it. I don't use Gnome,  because in striving to be simple, it has long since reached the point  where it simply doesn't do what I need it to do."

I use a combination of FVWM, Awesome WM and Blackbox by the way. (wouldn't recommend these for newbies, try LXDE,  XFCE or E17)

You won't see many differences between a lighter desktop and GNOME on Ubuntu because it uses lots of background processes and is built and optimized for GNOME.
