### Post by chavon20 on 2008-10-14
After learning about Ubuntu a year ago from a free CD on a magazine, I became quite interested in it and the whole open-source world as I had never heard of it before.

I looked it up on the net and was attracted to how it looked and its features and how it was based on open source software and also because it was non-Microsoft.  However I didn't consider switching to Ubuntu from XP then only because I had recently bought a new laptop and I had paid for my copy of XP, so I thought since I had paid for it may as well get my money's worth.  Anyway it had stuck in my mind and because of this and doing research on the net, I came across a whole swathe of open-source applications that I could use on my computer.  I had downloaded firefox, openoffice, an adobe acrobat replacement, cd burner software, etc and bit by bit I replaced some of the pre-loaded software on my XP system with open-source applications.  

A few months ago I came across an Ubuntu dedicated magazine at the newsagents so I thought, I'll buy and try the live CD that came with it.  Got it home and popped it into my laptop, it ran a little slow (as it was running from the CD) however everything just worked, the touchpad, the card reader, controls to adjust the screen, the volume controls on the front of the machine (I have a Dell Inspiron 6400) everything worked.  

After a few days thought I decided hmm maybe I should add Ubuntu to my system, if everything works fine, why not?  So I backed up all my data, put the Live CD again and clicked on the install button intending to partition my hard-drive.  Then I thought to myself, why would I want to do that?  I don't have anything or need anything that can't run under Ubuntu, so I removed Windows entirely from laptop and have been enjoying Ubuntu Hardy Heron ever since!  Everything is soooo easy to use, from adding music to my phone and my girlfriends MP3 player to fixing up my photos and creating new documents, it also fixed my blue screens (memory leaks) as well!!.  As the website says 'everything just works'... except for my printer, ha!  However that was easily fixed, I did a little research, ditched my existing Dell printer and found a good cheap HP one and I can now print and scan too.  

I am totally in love with this operating system, I have found everything that I need in an OS (and also some pretty cool games too), though I feel that I should give something in return (as it is free), shame I can't really contribute something really useful since I can't program.

Thanks for reading to the end of my testimonial and I'd like to thank everyone in the Ubuntu community and all of you that help out the newbies (and others) in the forums with their questions, queries; I have read a lot of them and I think all of you are doing a truly, outstanding job!! :)
