### Post by u-noob-tu on 2011-08-18
Im not new to ubuntu, but I've never tried kubuntu, and recently I felt the urge to give it a go. I'm really glad I did. It is really quite pleasing to use. I've really had no significant trouble with it. Wireless worked out of the box, runs great, looks great. I had heard that KDE is more resource hungry than GNOME, but when idle, kubuntu still only used between 2 and 5% more CPU than GNOME, which is still less than 20% CPU load on my laptop (Dell studio 17, 2.1ghz core 2 duo, 4gb RAM). The only thing that seemed odd to me was that, by default, the standard repositories were turned off (Canonical Partners being the most important). Other than that, I've had a great experience with Kubuntu. I might just have to switch it for ubuntu as my primary (right now it's running off an external drive). I <3 Kubuntu!
