### Post by ethan brand on 2008-10-05
I remember years ago when I installed my first Linux distro--Mandrake--which forced me to get my feet wet with the world of Linux. Well, after abandoning Mandrake due to specific Windows needs at that time, I have once again decided to see whether Linux would work for me. Xubuntu seemed like a good fit, so in it went into my hard drive.

I can't believe how easy things have become. Not only in the ease of package installations but hardware support as well. And it's FAST! Why does *everything* run faster on my Xubuntu setup than Windows? It's incredible; I feel like I've been stymied all these years with Windows. PDF, web browsing, and even remote desktop (through rdesktop) seems to run faster on Xubuntu. Openoffice can open my word and excel documents. I really don't think I need Windows anymore except for occasional gaming.

Well, just wanted to share my positive experience.

### Post by L815 on 2008-10-05
I recently decided to go over to Xubuntu 8.10 Beta to see what has been improved, previously playing with Ubuntu. Ubuntu is great, but Gnome with metacity without compiz is a bit ugly, and the speed is altered with compiz enabled.

Xubuntu is fast, even with desktop composition enabled. I do have a pretty strong laptop aside form the gfx card (thus slowdown with compiz), and xubuntu works just great.

The only things I wish were different were the desktop handling of multiple objects. I like to click and drag to select multiple things, while xfce requires you to hold ctrl and to click individual objects.

And the second being the volume manager. The default one is ok, but I prefer the way Gnome's works because it is a bit more inuitive.

Besides those 2 personal gripes, I would say Xubuntu is awesome. If you want Ubuntu without much of the bulk of Gnome & a bit more speed, I say go with Xubuntu. The team has done very well!
