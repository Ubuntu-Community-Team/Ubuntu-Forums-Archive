### Post by Private_Ops on 2009-08-04
I've used Ubuntu on and off since the 5.04 (well, something around the 5s) version and mainly on laptops (its a main stay on my current one).

Well, I acquired an old gateway P4 board from a buddy. 2.4GHz, 1GB of RD-RAM.

I had everything else I needed (HD, DVD, PSU, Video Card) and decided to throw it together and see what it could do.

So I grabbed an old ATI X800 card I had lying around and plugged all the hardware up. Messed with the BIOS for a bit to get things going and put Ubuntu 9.04 in the DVD drive.

Rebooted and waited for the live disk to load to a usable desktop. Once it was loaded I was amazed at how fast it ran but, was more amazed at the fact that the open source ATI drivers had automatically been loaded!

I proceeded to install Ubuntu on the hard drive figuring it couldn't hurt since it was just an old spare drive anyway and my curiosity was getting the best of me.

Once installed...well... it ran beautifully, I've never had an Ubuntu install go so smoothly... especially without me having to do anything other than updates!

All in all, I'm very pleased with it. Even though I'm not a complete convert (I have windows 7 on the main desktop and actually like it very well), I plan to make use of Ubuntu one way or another for a long while and down the road may switch completely.
