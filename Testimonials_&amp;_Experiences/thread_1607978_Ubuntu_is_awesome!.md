### Post by Santaji on 2010-10-28
Hello, I just thought i'd share my great experience with Ubuntu!
The first time i used Ubuntu was about a year and a half ago, I was a happy Mac user but i had heard a lot of Ubuntu Linux so i decided to try it out on a Windows PC that i had. just out of curiosity. I played around with it for a few weeks and really didn't like it much so i went back to using my Mac.
Until a few months ago, When my MacBook suffered a major hardware problem and i was out of warranty/AppleCare. I then started using my brother's 5 year old HP laptop which he no longer uses.
Originally the Laptop ran Windows XP, but after replacing the old hard-drive which died after i used it for about a month, i decided to try out Linux again as Windows XP was very slow and crashy with just 512 mb RAM. 
At first i installed Crunchbang, Which ran amazingly fast on this old laptop, but i felt it was too "un-noob-friendly" for me, I then added 1 extra GB of RAM and installed Ubuntu 10.4 which was great, Now i'm running 10.10 which is even better!

Installing 10.10 was super easy(i did a live usb install as my cd-drive is broken) and quite fast too. 
The new default theme is very impressive and high-quality. The design team at Canonical did a excellent job!
10.10 Works very well too, hasn't crashed or frozen up even once!
It's amazing how fast it booths up on this old laptop. it's about as fast as my brothers brand new MacBook which has more RAM and a faster processor!
I really don't know what else to say. everything's working so good and i'm very happy with Ubuntu. I use this laptop mostly for web browsing, listening to music, storing/editing photos, playing SNES games, and some website designing. Ubuntu does everything i want it to do very well and i didn't have to pay anything for it at all. That's just awesome.
I am really looking forward to seeing what Canonical does with the next versions of Ubuntu. i'm sure Ubuntu is going to keep getting awesome-er and awesome-er.
Canonical also seems to be taking usability and design very seriously and i believe Ubuntu is getting very close to Mac OS X quality-wise. :)
