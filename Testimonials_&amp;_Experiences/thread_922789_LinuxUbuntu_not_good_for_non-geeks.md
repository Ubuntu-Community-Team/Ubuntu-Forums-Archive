### Post by blakjesus on 2008-09-17
Well im not trying to preach anything here i just want to share an experience i had with Ubuntu. Well actually, it wasnt technically my experience. Just read ;)

Well one day my friend called me one day and told me that she let her sister use her brand new laptop (with vista installed) for a few days and now there were a ton of viruses on it and she asked if i could fix it. I reluctantly accepted, so she came over to my house and brought her computer for me to fix. ](*,)

While i was "diagnosing" what needed to be done with her computer, she told me that she wanted all of the viruses off.  So i politely asked her if she wanted to never have to worry about viruses and to try out something besides Windows. At first she was surprised because she had no clue that there was anything besides windows, so (with her permission and after her files were backed-up on my hard-drive) i reformatted her computer and installed Ubuntu. Of course i had to get it completely configured with internet, her favorite IM client, and a few other small tidbits that were surprisingly all available through synaptic (took me about an hour including issues with her wireless adapter). Afterwards she tried it out and was thrilled about how easy it was (in her own words), and then i broke the news to her: NO MORE RANDOM VIRUSES! :shock: She almost couldn't believe that her computer was now practically virus free indefinitely. She thanked me and took her new computer home.

The only problem she ever had was when she was trying to get her printer to work (which i was able to help her with over the phone), and the only things that she couldn't do were easily teachable to her boyfriend (also a friend of mind) who is an avid windows user and he took on pretty quickly now she has never called me for help at all on her computer. Nevertheless i offered to help her if she ever ran into any problems.

My thoughts on Ubuntu:
Although i came to the realisation that Ubuntu still isn't ready for the mass public (couldn't get a few software alternatives for Linux so now i dual-boot), i also realised that for the average user, Ubuntu can be as simple as any other OS. I really think the only ones who have problems with Ubuntu are the ones that demand so much from our computer, but for non-power users its as simple as the initial install is, if there happens to be someone who knows how to configure Ubuntu then it is very easy to catch on.

My point is that Ubuntu is ready to be a main OS, but the only thing holding people back is the ease of install (including things that must be added after the initial OS installation to get a fully functional suite of applications, codecs, plugins, and tools)

Please feel free to hate and/or criticize what i say its just an opinion. :popcorn:

EDIT: She also liked the idea that she could change the system's theme easily and that she could get new ones if she wanted to. :D

### Post by xarsted on 2008-09-17
I actually had a discussion very similar with one of my buddies, a self-described Linux "sage." I'm in IT, I do mostly database administration stuff. For the most part, I've worked primarily with the Windows and OS X operating systems. After some nudging from my buddy and coworker, I picked up a second laptop and finally got around to actually trying Linux, this time "for real."

I'm really happy to say nonetheless. I decided to go with Ubuntu, because of all the Linux distributions it's the one that caught my eye and kept it. Anyway, my friend the "Linux Sage" was upset because he was hoping I was going to jump on the openSUSE distro ship, which obviously I did not... 

He grilled me as to why, and I basically summarized that as a "novice" Linux user, I'm not a power-user and for now, Ubuntu gives me what I need and I am very pleased and satisfied with the result. My computer is very customizable and a lot of fun. 

So I completely agree with the point you raised- for the average user, Ubuntu is awesome. It can only get better.
