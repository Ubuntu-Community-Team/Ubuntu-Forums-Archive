### Post by smbtol on 2008-01-29
Ubuntu just works out of the box. All the hardware is recognized. This was a surprise for me. When I started with linux in 1998 it was a lot of trouble to install the sound card on my computer:( .
Coming back to my experience with Ubuntu, yesterday I managed to crash Ubuntu on my laptop. I tried to connect my laptop to a video projector and I changed the video settings from System>Administration>Screens and Graphics . I spent two hours trying to get my display to work back to 1440x900 resolution without any luck. I had to reinstall Ubuntu. It seems that I should have used Nvidia Settings to do this job, but now I am afraid to try that.

Anyway my experience with Windows 95, 98, XP or Vista was not always smooth. The reason I decided to give Ubuntu a try was the Vista I had on my desktop computer. Its Windows Explorer crashed when I downloaded a file with Firefox on my desktop. That file was not even completely downloaded or opened. The Windows Explorer just wanted to restart all the time and never recovered until I found on the internet the cause and I deleted the file in Safe Mode.

I will stick with Ubuntu. I am impressed about how everything is integrated. For example I like how my google calendars are imported in Evolution and I can see my appointments just clicking on the date in the panel.
Another point is that linux now is a "cool" operating system with compiz visual effects. Linux community should hire somebody like Steve Jobs to market this:biggrin: . On youtube everybody is impressed by the clips with compiz/beryl in action.
