### Post by kprowell on 2007-06-20
I couldn't think of the best way to title this post so I think I captured this experience pretty well.  It started a month or so ago (I even posted a question here for help on GRUB issue not allowing it to boot into XP) when my mom asked me to install Ubuntu on her PC because of all the talking I do about it.  I was surprised and took advantage of the invitation.  Understand, my mom is in her mid to late 70's so change is not always welcomed.  Anyway, the install went smooth but as I mentioned above GRUB would not allow the PC to boot XP so she was stuck for a few weeks until I got back in the area.

During that time she initially moaned about her "Microsoft" is missing (she uses the M-word for everything on her PC.....Bill Gates would love that).  I had configured Ubuntu to do everything she was doing before (browsing, email, photo's, printing, etc) so she truly had everything she needed.  It was just different.  After a week or so she called and I asked how it was going and she said things were good.  Then around the 3rd week she really wanted XP back.  Why?  I don't know.......maybe because she was familiar.  So when I arrived, I fixed the booting problem and I even removed Ubuntu completely (per her request). Now she had her XP PC back and she was happy again..........  But, and here's the funny part.......

Since then (two weeks now) she has been complaining about all the pop-ups, application crashes, spyware, you name it.  Oh, and how slow it was.  LOL  So, long story short, she indirectly gave Ubuntu/Linux a compliment by complaining about all that is wrong with XP only a few weeks after removing Ubuntu.  I guess she was so used to the problems with XP that it seemed normal.   Maybe I can get her to go back.....
