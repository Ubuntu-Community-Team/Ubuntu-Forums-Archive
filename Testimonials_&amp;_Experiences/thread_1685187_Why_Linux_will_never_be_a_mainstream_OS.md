### Post by P4man on 2011-02-10
The forum is having issues, it was down a few minutes ago and its still a bit flaky. Nothing to do with ubuntu really.

As for your thread title; Linux already is mainstream. It probably powers your (next) smartphone, tablet, it runs on your router, it will be in your next tv and/or settop box, it works behind the scenes in your car. It didnt get there by mimicking windows, which has tried but failed to get anywhere in those same markets.

As for setting the primary display. You probably have an ATI card with the ATI catalyst drivers. Why AMD doesnt provide that option is beyond me as well, but if you have an intel or nvidia card, you dont need a cli:

[IMG]http://bp2.blogger.com/_DPRpWaKQ8NQ/SBQFljNq1lI/AAAAAAAAAGE/DCLWuNRJduI/s400/arange_displays.png[/IMG]

Hardly Linux or Ubuntu's fault if AMD's control panel omits that.

Lastly, about the CLI. As you get to know the OS better, you will begin to realize why that is actually such a great asset. Not dependant on what GUI you use (KDE, GNOME, Fluxbox, whatever), not dependant on UI language, personal settings, OS version. Besides nothing is faster than copy/pasting commands and their output, thats why its used so often. Pretty much everything can be done with a GUI but explaining how would involve use cases for every language, every desktop environment, every distro. 

Use the GUI to explore by yourself, use CLI to get stuff done fast.
