### Post by TerdRatchett on 2008-09-13
I understand your frustration and feel your pain!

I learned about this one the hard way. Our fellow posters are a bit too quick to jump on the bad cd/burn bandwagon. While some issues may be attributed to bad burns, too many people are having this problem for it to be shrugged off.

From my experience I have discovered two types of motherboards will induce this exact failure during the Ubuntu 8.04 CD test and during the actual install. The boards I have discovered this issue with are the PCChips XCEL 2000 and the Matsonic MS7308ET. There is nothing wrong with the motherboards, they work perfectly during a Windows install and other versions of Linux, for example, DSL. Both boards appear to use SIS chipsets....

I simply replace the MB with a different type and the install works fine! If you can find another PC you might want to try again....

Best of luck,
TerdRatchett
