### Post by pirast on 2007-11-02
> (Fedora 7 does have the same issues as Gutsy, but it die come out over half a year ago.)

Fedora 7 should work, also. Kernel 2.6.23 is being shipped in the updates repository of it. You just have to remaster the dvd with up to date software, and it should work flawlessly. (two commands, or download the updated F7 dvd from fedoraunity).

I also use the Nvidia RPMs from the Livna repository, but the point is that you do not have to just do one click in order to be able to use it. This makes users buy graphics cards with open source drivers. And this makes vendors of closed source drivers (ATI/NVIDIA) to think about their politics. Just think of ATI. Do you think that they would have released specs if the had known that their proprietary driver would be included into every distro?

Same applies to Atheros: If no distro would have included the HAL, they would probably have re-released it under an open license.

Lastly, shipping binary drivers is problematic just because the distro has to support it (security / bug fixes). I remember the NVIDIA driver having a security bug. You can't imagine how happy the Ubuntu security team was that they only fixed the bug and did not add new features which would probably have broken some users' desktops.


Ah and by the way: The Fedora developers are much more	responsive than the Ubuntu devs are. And I have the feeling that much more Fedora patches get back upstream.

APT is no argument for Ubuntu anymore, as the YUM being shipped with Fedora is as fast and as good as APT IMHO. Also, delta support is being developed (you just have to download the changes of updates).

Just received an update to my F7 installation: I now have OpenOffice.org 2.3 on a linux distribution that is 6 month old :)
