### Post by PariahVayne on 2009-12-03
It's just so buggy. I'm not willing to put up with it much more.

Here are a few examples;

>Disabling Kwin before playing any fullscreen game results in a black screen if I re-enable it after I'm finished playing said game.

>For some reason the mouse has just started lurching around like there is repeated heavy spikes in CPU activity, but there isn't. Even resetting xorg.conf doesn't work.
XP on the same machine with the same mouse has no problems.

>Trying to connect to another PC on the network crashes Dolphin.

>The desktop folder gets changed from desktop view to folder view randomly on boot.

> Resizing of the desktop folder will result in it getting auto resized if the PC is restarted.

>The Kickoff 'snapback' bug that happens if you open it too soon after installing software.

>A font smoothing bug with Trebuchet MS that goes back to the days of KDE3. Sooner or later it's going to start distorting. I've seen this over & over.

>On bootup I randomly get told that I have removed my audio device, and would I like KDE to forget about said device. Even telling it to not show again doesn't work.

>The above bug causes a bug with KNetworkManager that stops it from connecting to the WIFI network unless I restart the PC.

>I get random 'hangs' (that is the best way I can think of to describe it) where the network/internet will just stop dead for a random amount of time, and then carries on like nothing had happened. XP on the same machine works fine.

>PCM gets randomly set to 0% on startup.

>Sometimes clicking dropdown menus results in a black space where the menu should be.


Problems like this just shouldn't happen, even an unpatched Vista is nowhere near as broken. It's sad to see people waste their time coding something that results in such a poor and painful result.

I will wait for KDE 4.4 before I make a final decision, but unless there is a massive improvement, and I mean a **MASSIVE** improvement, I may give up on Ubuntu altogether.

> **Ms_Angel_D said:**

I have tested a bunch of KDE distros myself, and in my experience Mandriva and Fedora are the best, at least for a standard user type of activity.  OpenSuSE is fine, but nowhere near those two as far as I am concerned.

@PariahVayne:

Kubuntu has never been a worthy KDE distro in my opinion.  I tried Kubuntu 8.10 and Kubuntu 9.04 and both were big disappointments.  I have read that KDE and Debian are not the best of friends, and the fact that most good KDE distros are no Debian based makes me think there may be some truth to that statement.

Fedora 12 is an awesome distro.  Fedora is always at the cutting edge, offering the latest versions of almost every application soon after they are released.  I have already been using KDE 4.3.3 under Fedora for many days, and they will not wait to get 4.3.4 in there.  In addition, I think they get lots of their own technology, and you get very cool features that other distros don't have.  Plymouth is a good example, but there are tons more, as you can see from their announcement here:

[https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Fedora_12_Announcement](https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Fedora_12_Announcement)

Mandriva 2010 is also very good.  It also gets the best out of KDE, and I can tell they are making an effort to update application versions more often, much more than they used to.  The best thing in Mandriva is its control center, which simplifies many tasks that may be cumbersome or require manual effort in other releases.

If you like a KDE desktop, these two can't be beat IMHO.  And btw, I started using KDE when version 4 was out, and I didn't like it at all.  On top of its bugginess, it felt bloated, short in features, and not even that good looking in my opinion.  However, I have to say that I am very impressed with KDE 4.3.3, and 4.3.4 should be even better, as it fixes many bugs.  My KDE desktop feels light and responsive now, and everything feels like it behaves the way it should.  

Go for it, it's cool as long as it's Linux!  :p
