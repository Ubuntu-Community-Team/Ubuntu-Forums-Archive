### Post by tomplast on 2006-08-06
Linux have always been there in some kind of way, the first period of my life (and the longest) Linux was only some kind of thing that the real 1337s used, later on (I guess the last 2 years or so) my interest in Linux have grown and grown as my dispise for closed systems (I can't help to mention Windows) and bit-slavery. 

After I had tested a number of various distros Debian, Mandrake/Mandriva, SUSE to mention some of the most known. At last the stabillity, security and the functionalliy in Debian got my attention and I was drawn to Ubuntu (how I found it I don't remember) but the first version I tested was Breezy and it was fascinating to see how Linux had progressed in functionalliy AND user-friendlyness at the same time.

Still Windows was a part of my computer and it was used quite often. It wasn't really until Dapper Drake came out officially as my usage of Windows was dropped more or less to ZERO. All the talk that Linux can't replace Windows is totally bull, and even if it means some sacrifices you get more than you sacrifice, that's what I have learned at least.

It was mainly because of my mother that Windows was still on the HDD and her only need was beeing able to write documents and e-mail, that wasn't so hard to fix and thankfully to a HOWTO on this forum I was able to get my mother's Hotmail working :).

And today came the freedom to my computer, after some thinking about whether I would need Windows or not I came to the conslusion that I didn't.

So that's the story, today my computer is free :mrgreen:
