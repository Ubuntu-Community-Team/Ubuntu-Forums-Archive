### Post by xenton on 2010-12-09
I installed Ubuntu 10.04 months ago now, had a few issues, worked through them all with the help of the wonderful community. Had a few niggles, worked through them all apart from 1 : syncing my mobile.

There is a floor with the way a windows mobile device handles sync partnerships in my opinion. I would of liked to use both windows and Ubuntu, at least initially. However, once data belongs to one sync partnership on your mobile device you can't sync it with another, and if you delete a sync profile then everything that was synced with it disappears. Then there's the nightmare of trying to get a winmo device to sync with Ubuntu. You need a minimum of 2 different programs to communicate between your phone and, for example, evolution. I never got it working properly, whereas windows 7 has windows mobile device centre, which just works. This is probably the only issue that has forced me back to windows as my main OS.

I'm sure at some point someone will come up with a brilliant piece of software that looks good, is easy to use and just lets you sync your winmo device to Ubuntu as easily as windows mobile device centre allows with windows.
