### Post by travissparks1307 on 2011-09-17
I first started using Ubuntu Linux about a year ago. I loved it. But lately, I find my faith in it has been shaken.

 I am anti-Microsoft. Do not attempt to rationalize this with me. I am anti-Microsoft, and I always will be. As far as I'm concerned, they are The Enemy. Period. You will not change my mind on this, don't try. 

I have watched Ubuntu Linux increase its dependence on Mono, through various projects. i have seen it disregard the larger GNU/Linux community by becoming an ever more exclusive club. It seems that its always referred to as Ubuntu, rather than Ubuntu Linux. It uses an exclusive interface that is not used by other distributions. The community itself seems to want to "play nice" when it comes to proprietary competition. I have seen Ubuntu Linux exclusive websites such as OMG! Ubuntu advertising Microsoft and Apple products through its articles. 

The technology behind Ubuntu Linux is sound. Don't get me wrong. But here's the thing: The technology behind other distributions, is just as sound. Other distributions have automatic updates and regular, predictable release cycles. They have up to date, modern technology. All the things that make Ubuntu Linux great, can be found in other distributions. 

But without the things that have made me question Ubuntu Linux. Fedora has adopted GNOME 3 (face it, it will be THE interface for pretty much every other major distribution of Linux.) As well regular predictable release cycles, automatic updates, and everything else. The difference is, Red Hat threw down the gauntlet against Microsoft YEARS ago. They REFUSE to include Mono in any way, shape or form. Red Hat has it's target reticles aimed squarely at Microsoft. Same as me. It makes me question why I gave it up all those years ago for Slackware, and then Slackware, for Ubuntu. I've been questioning a lot of things. 

I think I will be switching to Fedora. At least Red Hat and I can see eye to eye on one thing, Microsoft is The Enemy. They don't play nice with Microsoft, or hump the Mac's leg the way Ubuntu does.
