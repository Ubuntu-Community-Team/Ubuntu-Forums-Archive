### Post by juan9847327 on 2010-09-28
Well, I tried Lucid for a couple weeks on my Inspiron 1545, and I got to say I liked it. I installed it through Wubi, and had also Windows 7 installed. 
I noticed a change the minute I logged into Ubuntu. It made way better use of my laptop's resources, so it was amazingly fast. Installing programs was not hard, and everything I needed was there for me: word processor, music manager, etc.
However, I changed backed to Windows for some reasons. Here are the pros and cons of my Ubuntu experience:
Pros:
[LIST]
[*]Better resource management
[*]Most needed apps are available
[*]True freedom to work your way around your pc
[*]Not much security needed, contrary to Windows
[*]Nice looks and effects (with Compiz)
[*]Great support
[/LIST]
Cons:
[LIST]
[*]I read many people complaining about Inspirons heating up, and mine was no exception. You could fry eggs on the touchpad!
[*]Some apps are not available or not as developed as their Windows equivalents. (Eg: Windows Live Messenger, GuitarPro, iTunes)
[*]Some drivers are not available. Even less for the 64bit version.
[*]Installing the wireless card was really confusing.
[/LIST]
Anyway, I liked my Ubuntu two-week experience. I think I'm not ready to abandon Windows yet, but there's no doubt that Linux is developing really fast and it's growing every day.

Maybe someday, I hope, I can use Linux exclusively. Until then, I remain in Windows.

I can't comment on your particular model of computer, but this is not normal behaviour even for laptops. I've run Ubuntu live and installed on a number of different laptops with no great cooling problems.


> [*]Some drivers are not available. Even less for the 64bit version.

What drivers are available for 32-bit Linux that aren't available for 64-bit?! Linux runs on eleven different CPU architectures; at the source code level drivers for one architecture should run on another architecture as far as I know.

> [*]Installing the wireless card was really confusing.

Most cards do work out-of-the-box, it's a good idea to research a particular wireless card before buying it. In your case it already came inside your existing computer.

### Post by colin.p on 2010-10-06
Just to add that my 1545 actually runs 5-10c cooler in 10.04 (2.6.32-25 generic kernel) than win 7. I have a low end 1545 with a T4400 cpu, standard intel graphics and a bcm wireless card. I use this thing exclusively as a desktop, never on battery, so I have added a cooler that has brought the temps down into the low 40s, so the cpu fan rarely comes on anymore. Again in windows, with the cooler, the fan still comes on at 55-60c and then when the temps get down to 40, the fan shuts off. It just keeps cycling like that. But in ubuntu the fan hardly ever comes on and the computer is actually cold to the touch.
