### Post by Crick on 2008-05-04
Ok, so I have been very eager to try the Heron since before Gutsy. I've been using Ubuntu since Feisty, and I want to see what has evolved since then, something I can upgrade to and leave "as is" until the next LTS. That would be Hardy.

Seeing all the tales of woe in testimonials held me back. I always wonder whether they are paid MS astroturfers or genuine. I'm usually skeptical of those who have just joined with a couple posts to their name. It would seem a no-brainer for a company with MS' asset base to throw some FUD at their most feared competitor. OTOH, chances are some of it is genuine.

And so I thought, why not wait another 5 months until the bugs are worked out and HDD prices are that little bit cheaper? (I always upgrade the HDD at the same time, that way I have the old HDD with the previous working install if anything goes wrong. Often warrantees are not far from going as well, so it makes your data that bit safer. And one thing I'd like to do is wait for SDD prices to drop/reliability to be more clearly ascertained so that I can have faster loading in my laptop.)

But curiosity got the better of me; I wanted to look beyond the livecd. I had an old HDD long past warrantee date I would not trust with actual data, so I thought I'd give it a trial run.

After a couple of hours, I managed to figure out how to get my video card (GeForce4 420MX) working properly, compiz enabled and a decent resolution. The key was to do a search for nvidia from synaptic and go from there. I had to google to figure out that setting resolution via *sudo displayconfig-gtk* and put in my specific monitor. Playing around with Admin->Hardware Drivers and System->Preferences-> Appearance got me the wobbly windows.

It looks fantastic, so far so good. I don't have any desktop effects enabled in Feisty, and I have to admit they are fun. They also seem to take a minimum of overhead to work.

I thought I'd try the other thing that cost me nearly a week of my life the last time I tried it in Ubuntu, that being to connect to a MS PPTP server. I googled *hardy pptp*, came up with this [link]("http://tipotheday.com/2008/04/29/connect-to-windows-vpn-server-pptp-with-ubuntu-hardy-heron/"). Literally five minutes later, I had connected to the network. OMG!!! So easy! Connecting to the windows server via Terminal Server Client was a cinch as well.

Overall, I'm very impressed. Impressed enough to want to upgrade now. Yes, I know there is no real pressing need to upgrade, certainly no business case. I suppose the only benefit would be that I could get used to things now so that any improvements I make now I will still be using 3 years later, and not have to change in 5 months time.

Congratulations Hardy developers, from what I can see this looks like a home run. In a lot of ways, it feels like moving from 98SE to XP in that the networking is more seamless and user friendly, and also prettier. Compiz is just so aesthetically nice. Things certainly don't seem any slower either.

Well done! If there is a distro to install on your friends' computers, this is shaping up to be the one!
