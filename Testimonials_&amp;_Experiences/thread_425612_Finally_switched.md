### Post by bobbybobington on 2007-04-27
I'm pretty much ecstatic right now, and I'll tell you why. I've been without linux for about a year... until today. I had a dual boot of opensuse10 and xp, until I borked the install of suse. The partition got corrupted (I think:confused: ), and at that time I had no clue how to fix it. In the mean time I was stuck in windows land, but I couldn't shake the awesome experience of linux. So I investigated it more online. I heard about ubuntu, got involved in the forums here, and read ubuntu planet often. I still couldn't do anything with the main family computer without easily backing up data and  setting it up confidently, so I did the next best thing. I used our old spyware ridden win98 computer as a playing ground where I could mess up without much damage. After going through several distros I ended up with dapper, and I learned a lot along the way. 

Fast forward to a week ago. The family xp install was starting to get bogged down despite my best efforts. Several files got corrupted  after  a hard reboot and thankfully it booted up, but I knew it was a matter of time before it wouldn't. So I told my mom we needed an external hd to save our stuff, bought it and backed everything up. I changed the partition table, and literally an hour ago finished a feisty install that took less than 20 minutes. What could be better? _There was not a single hitch_ It even set up compiz with no issues with my ati card causing psychotic exclamations of amazment.

 Over the past months I've been slowly acclimating my family to what ubuntu would be like by modifying xp, and now I'm actually in the final stages of that transition. I would like to thank the ubuntu community/devs for bringing me this far, and for making some thing so frickin awesome. It may seem silly to some of you that I'm ranting about a good install, but this means a lot more to me than just that. Well, now I have no excuse to start converting :twisted:
