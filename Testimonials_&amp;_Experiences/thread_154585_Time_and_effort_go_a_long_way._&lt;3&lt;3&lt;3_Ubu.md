### Post by superchar42 on 2006-04-03
I figured my relevations yesterday deserve a testimonial. 
I totally got my Canon Pixma MP130 printer to do the things it was supposed to do (I don't really care about scanning, just printing and copying really), and got my mp3 player to work as well (which was a little more of a challenge). 

So I've toyed with linux for a few years now, I started on Slackware because I found it to be the most Unixy. (yum) though I didn't get any instructions before I loaded in the installation disks and didn't find exactly what I needed (startx) before I gave up about a month later, thinking I was just really good at getting through the installation but had something really bad with my box. Last Distro tried: 10.2. 

Fedora I tried, but it was SO buggy. Worst bug ever: when the power cord of my laptop would come out, the system would freeze. Last distro tried: FC3. 

Then I found Ubuntu. Installation was sleek and painless, I was able to use everything I plugged in automatically, plus it automounted my usb drive and my digital camera (if I remember right). Still hooked on Windows though. 
I did most things in root, and screwed everything up all the time, just reinstalling as a good fix. But I learned a lot!

Fast forward a large chunk of time. I'm on 5.10, and just determined to make this work. Again, I've taken home my windows PC but now I have win2000 and breezy on dual-boot, though I hardly ever use it. 
The biggest thing with my mp3 player was that it was MTP as in WinXP+MediaPlayer10 ONLY! That stunk. I was able to do a firmware flash that made it UMS though, and it was cool. 

Okay, enough rambling. I'm just superhappy about it all though, and Linux is cuddly, whether we get cranky after 9 hours of programming the darn thing or not!
Though I'm nowhere near experienced, through this resource and my best friend google, I've been able to get just about everything to work, almost got my USR 5410 wireless card to work, too. That'll be the final step. Then I'm done with Windows. 
Then I get to figure out Ubuntu+Dualbooting+RAID on my main box. haha.
