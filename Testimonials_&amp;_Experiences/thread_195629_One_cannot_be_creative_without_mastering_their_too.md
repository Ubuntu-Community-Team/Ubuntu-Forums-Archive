### Post by tribalpoetry on 2006-06-13
The best of art and creative expression require scientific research and further advances in technology 
“One cannot be creative without mastering their tools"
Late last year, the package I have been expecting from Ubuntu made its approach to my doorstep.  And like a kid with a new toy I didn’t wait to give it a try 
Looking at this little technology on my hand, the thought of our ancestor comes to me, the reflection of them with their tools, the beauty of such recollection brought tears to my eyes and suddenly the memories of my inherited culture were born in my line of thought

The torchlight; I rewind it up as the instruction says, to fully recharge the battery, and I pictured myself using the tools our ancestors had invented over a period of time, I pictured myself winding a twig, trying to kindle a fire,

I pictured myself using such tools alike. I thought of the objects or procedure that they use to adapt to change, Utensils that allow them to modify or operate things in their surroundings for their own benefit, and I think of them as an extension of my arm holding the product

I moved to the compound, and as I rewind up the manual operated radio, my mind was elsewhere as I do it, I was with the natural habitat of our surrounding, and I was one there with the herds, the birds and the open sky, and all the tool users from ancient time were born into this author’s creative realm, and I thought I can hear the products telling me…"I am what I am because of who we all are". (That is what the word ubuntu meant)

When I think of this beautiful technology, I think of one of the most primitive tools that were most likely used on plants and animals, when I think or use the charger, I think at some stage of our ancestors using local tools to make other tools. 

As I handled the charger attached to a cell phone battery, an important innovation that occurred in the Middle Stone Age also came to mind, rewinding the charger thought of the earliest of humans, attaching a stone tool to wooden handle by binding them together with rawhide

Working it manually with its battery, hearing the modern nuance, hard rock and roll music at its best, I went back in time, in the Iron Age when metals were smelted from rocks and hammered into tools. memories closeting in which began about 2000 years ago in Africa, and I felt the warmth creeps into my palm finding it way up toward my mind, recalling the early humans hafting of tools, hitting two stones together, chipping of fragment to accommodate or create a more sophisticated technology. 

I am really impressed with these products more so because I like the need to identify what distinguished Africans from other nationalities, I am really impressed with our ability to create and think as our own, it will, I believe in time allowed us to progress 

I feel Ubuntu offers a measure of refinement that's not current in similar products handled down by other sponsorship industries and I think its entrance into the West African market ought to be celebrated by all, because it suggests that we have a past to be proud of. What Africa really need now, is something of it own, something identifiable with its own belief and heritage
Ubuntu is an ancient African word that means 'humanity toward others,  Many of us have been brought up to uphold values based on old African adage. it is beautiful when we name our product with our visions
“Through this technology Ubuntu have ensured that our families and communities are grounded on the African idealism."
