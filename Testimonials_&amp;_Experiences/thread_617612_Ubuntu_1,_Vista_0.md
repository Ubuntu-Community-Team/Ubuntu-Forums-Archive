### Post by Tristam Green on 2007-11-19
Bought a Dell 1501 in late September, and have been running Vista Home Basic on a 45gb partition of the 60gb drive it came with.

I was intent on installing Feisty on it initially (I've been dual-booting XP/Ubuntu on my desktop since Hoary and ran Xubuntu Dapper on my POS IBM 600x), but after a coworker told me the Gutsy Beta was very stable, I decided to wait for the Gutsy release.

I nuked the Dell Recovery partition (10gb) and installed Ubuntu Gutsy on 11/5 after running LiveCD Ubuntu/Kubuntu since release.  I must say, after my initial configuration for wireless, Compiz-Fusion, and my essential apps, I am VASTLY impressed, this being by far the best Ubuntu release I've encountered.

I had a major snafu with Power Management (I attempted to solve the hibernation issue and f'd my machine to where I needed a rebuild), but I was able to rebuild in about 3 hours' time, whereas Windows would have definitely been an all-day project.

To the Ubuntu Community, Canonical Ltd., and redDEAD's Dell 1501 Blog, I salute you.


--Tristam Green



P.S.  Yes, I still have Vista on the 45gb.  I use it ONLY to run Windows Games (I have never been able to get Wine to work as it should).  It's not a bad trade-off as 10gb suffices just fine for Ubuntu (my favorite part: it's not a space-hog).
