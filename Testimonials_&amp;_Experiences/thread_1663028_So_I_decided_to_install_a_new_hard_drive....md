### Post by Irihapeti on 2011-01-09
I had three hard drives in my PC. Two of them were older IDE drives and starting to get a bit noisy. Common sense suggested that I replace them now instead of waiting until something terrible happened. For once, I listened.

There were two installations of Ubuntu, a server and storage partition, and an install of Vista.

One Ubuntu version stayed where it was, on the third (SATA) drive that I wasn't replacing. I archived the other Ubuntu version, plus the server partition, and replaced these quickly and easily after the new drive was installed. One or two config file tweaks (grub and fstab) and everything booted nicely, no problems. Probably took about 1/2 to 3/4 of an hour.

But as for that other OS - well, I could say lots, but I won't. This is a family-friendly forum, after all, and I wouldn't want to run the risk of getting banned...

Let's just say that I was reminded *very* strongly of why I prefer Ubuntu. :)
