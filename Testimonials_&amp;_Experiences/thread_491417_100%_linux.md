### Post by moredhel on 2007-07-03
I have used linux for a /long/ time. well long in relation to my age :D

I have used it since I was 8, and I am not 15, so nearly half my life.

However I have always duel/triple/quad(xD) booted. (***dows XP and various linux distros)

One day I decided to upgrade XP to Vista because I was bored. this failed, and told me to restart while it restored my XP system. This failed as well.

great.

So I gave in, after all of these years of struggling along with windows because of a few games and software holding me back. The software was easy to use alternatives in linux, but games not so.

So now I use cedega, which covers most of my top games. The rest I will probably never play again :\

I must say that I think I would of benefited from jumping in the deep end instead of dipping my toe in. Forcing myself to use 100% linux for 2 weeks made me appreciate it in a light I never had. 

All of this, free, and done in good nature, by the people, for the people.

Long live linux :3

To make it perfect, we need software companies to make their applications for windows,linux & mac. For games developers to swap to opengl and make games for all 3 systems :D And for hardware developers to make drivers for all 3 systems. I mean it really can't be that hard, when other people without half the resources the company has can reverse-engineer and make some pretty damn good attempts.

That'll be the day :)
