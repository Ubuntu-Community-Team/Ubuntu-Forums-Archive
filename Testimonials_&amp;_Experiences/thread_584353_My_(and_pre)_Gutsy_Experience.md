### Post by ryanVickers on 2007-10-20
For the longest time, I had been very reluctant to upgrade to gutsy, but for some reason - maybe it was the effects, maybe my current system was just too slow, maybe it was inevitable and sudden drop of support for feisty - but whatever the reason, I decided to go for it!

First, I had to download the CD of course.  Well, this was no browse in the file transfer protocol [SIZE=1]*[/SIZE], it was a lot of work! ;)  First I had to re-download it like 20 times and it kept failing or was just too slow, but I got through it in the end...


Once I had it downloaded, I had this idea that I was finally going to jump on the separate home partition bandwagon, and so I also downloaded the gparted live CD for the extra power it provided.  Of course that didn't even boot, so oh well, I can do it from the Ubuntu live CD, but it won't be pretty... ;)

Getting the live CD to work properly wasn't difficult, but it wasn't the easiest thing either.  The first time I logged on, it just wouldn't get past the #DAB082 screen, ;) and so I had to kill X and try again - this time in failsafe mode.  It worked but I had no panels.  It dawned on me how to fix this eventually, open /bin and run a terminal and then killall the alleged already running panels and make new ones.  This worked like a dream, so I continued.

Getting the wireless internet was a bit of a pain - much harder than before, but still no "real" challenge. ;)  I just did what I had done in the past, and kept repeating the commands, and eventually it just worked... :???:

Once I had partitioned and re-partitioned and moved my ~100GiB worth of data back and forth a couple of times, everything was set - just do the install!

It installed perfectly, but there was a few problems getting it to boot.  Naturally, the first thing to do was to use super grub, but that didn't completely fix it either.  Although it did give me enough info to point me in the direction of the numbers being wrong.  Sure enough, it had put the wrong numbers in the menu.lst file.  So, I re-booted the live CD (and for some reason there was no problems at all this time :???:), and changed the file.

After that it all worked perfectly, and I'm quite impressed with all the features, fixes, and speed improvements!

I would really encourage everyone to upgrade!  That is, **re-install**, I never would do just and upgrade... ;)

[SIZE=1] *You know, like that old saying "no walk in the park"? :p[/SIZE]
