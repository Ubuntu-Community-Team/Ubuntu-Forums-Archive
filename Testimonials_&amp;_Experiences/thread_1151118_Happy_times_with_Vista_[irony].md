### Post by Te Kiwi on 2009-05-06
Well here´s my testimonial and a prediction:

Soapbox Testimonial:
Because I was sick and tired of the amount of crap that was getting built up in XP which slowed down the computer I formatted it, and installed Ubuntu [mainly because I couldn´t find my xp install cd].  This thing is brilliant! 
The setup was maybe 15 minutes I could see where everything was going and knew exactly how much space it took up.  At only 60Gb so space is at a premium.

So fast forward two weeks and I´ve bought a shiny new laptop with vista already installed.  but no discs came with the machine, I asked the shop and they said ¨No people don´t get discs anymore.  It´s all on a nice little partition that you can restore from¨
This nice little partition by the way eats up about 60Gb!  Anyway, I get home and plug it in, when vista boots up and plays its little piece of music I always imagine the computer saying ¨You are using Windows, be happy in your day¨ in the same toneless voice as the robot from Wall-E ¨B is for Buy N Large, your best friend¨

Looking around the thing I note that it´s fancy looking but still comes with so much crap blundered in with it that reformatting it straight out of the box is a definite option,  Media player won´t play my AVI´s saying that it needs new codecs which would be understandable if the movies themselves weren´t several years old!  So I try to connect to my internet but there is no luck to be had there either! [Compare this with Ubuntu which played everything and connected effortlessly right from the word go]

The other thing that gets on my wick is the system notifications.  Even something as simple as deleting a file needs to be be confirmed 3 times.  Once by you, once by the ¨owner¨ and once more just for the heck of it.
Finally and this has carried over from XP, I really hate the way the whole system conspires to keep you out of important files.  
¨Did you know that altering these files might change your computer?  Can you say com-pu-ter?¨ 
¨yes I did now let me delete minesweeper damn it!¨ 

I know there are workarounds for these and the many other problems I had but I´ve just lost all patience with it.  Let the sheep of the world stick with their increasingly hungry and virus filled OS. I´ll be riding free with Ubuntu from now on. [as soon as I get around to formatting my new one]

Prediction:
My prediction as to what will finally topple the Windows OS is the rediculious amount of effort they put keeping the damn thing from being pirated.  They already make you jump through many hoops with 100 digit long activation codes and 30 days time limit periods.  Vista will regularly send data back to Microsoft to confirm that it is still a legit product!
Windows has gone through the expansion period of it&#347; Empire building and are now in the Stagnant part where they stand around looking confused and saying ¨What?  Really?¨  :)
I´m putting the fall of windows as the dominent operating system at about 10 years
