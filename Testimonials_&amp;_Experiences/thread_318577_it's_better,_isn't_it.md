### Post by martymc on 2006-12-14
i took a sick day a couple days ago thanks to a migraine (sp?) - thank heaven i don't get them very often.... ](*,) 

after i slept it off, i spent the day on my home pc fooling around with ubuntu. i installed it (dapper, then a downgrade to breezy for wireless purposes) about 2 months ago just to see what it was like.

i've slowly been learning the ins and outs of it like getting the fstab set up for mounting a vfat partition, using sudo nautilus to enable writing to folders for themes and other fun stuff and using the coolest programs like open office, blender, gimp, etc....

the next day i went back to work and my xp pro dell laptop. it's supposedly faster than my athlon 2000+, but i don't see it anymore. everything feels so slooow and looks  - well - boring. i had to "brown out" my appearance settings and put ubuntu wallpaper on my xp rig just to take the edge off.

sure, i know my way around xp as good as most i.t. people. and it's familiar. aaaaand i had a LOT of frustrations with ubuntu for the first while. but it's getting familiar. i know i can type "ubuntu (whatever)" into google and most of the time i'll find what i need to type into the terminal or fiddle with in the settings to make ubuntu dance for me. :twisted: 

i told my wife about my day with xp pro after an afternoon with ubuntu. she said, "it's better, isn't it?"

yeah, it's better.
