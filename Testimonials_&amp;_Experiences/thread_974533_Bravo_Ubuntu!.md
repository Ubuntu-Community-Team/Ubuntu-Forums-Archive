### Post by Rabbiddog on 2008-11-07
I must say, I was quite pleased with this latest version. I had been trying several Linux flavors off and on for years and the last version of Ubuntu I tried was 6.something. 

The biggest problems I had with pretty much all flavors of Linux (Ubuntu included) was the wireless devices\network on my laptops. I decided to try the newest version of Ubuntu and a few other flavors just recently, and found Ubuntu to install easily and much to my excitement the wireless setup was no more difficult then Windows XP. Ubuntu saw my wireless device right away, and I was able to see my wireless network with no interaction on my part. I entered in my wireless security key and viola! It attached flawlessly. 

I am very much impressed at the strides Ubuntu has made with Linux. But it doesn't end there, as pleased as I was with the ease of attaching to my wireless network, I also found I could go in and see my other (WinXP) systems on my wireless network. I could access their shared folders, including my storage drives. Yes, I could copy to and from them and have full acces, all without having to do any extra work on my part. I am now considering migrating a number of my WinXP machines over to Ubuntu. 

I realize this might not be the scenario with all users, due to the vast array of variations in systems and hardware out there. But for me I must say, Bravo Ubuntu!  =D>
