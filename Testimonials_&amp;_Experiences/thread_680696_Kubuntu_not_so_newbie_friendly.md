### Post by tubunu on 2008-01-28
I am not a Linux newbie, but was trying to help a newbie friend by installing an easy to use and a good, solid distro on her PC. Having experience with running the last 3 Kubuntu editions and only recently switching to Ubuntu Gutsy, I must say my opinion of Kubuntu being the easiest distro to switch over from Windows turned out to be a myth.

Don't get me wrong, I used to be crazy about Kubuntu and I still like it very much (now that I know my way around). So here goes the story...

My friend, a complete NEWBIE (read: not knowing how to save a document in a text editor) got Kubuntu installed on the PC - I believe it was Edgy. First off, not knowing English, she spent a week attempting to install the language pack, but couldn't get past the password stage in Konsole. Yeah, we tried graphical Synaptic too...

Then, came the burning issue of saving a file to a floppy. To guide her, I even installed kubuntu-desktop on top of my Ubuntu install. Although I found it to be pretty straightforward on my machine, I couldn't explain it in lay terms how to find the mounted floppy on her PC (using Konqueror) because I couldn't do it on mine either, only in Konsole, which wasn't something she wanted to hear of. 

There was also another issue of Firefox not displaying JavaScript properly after a default install. And the thought of guiding her through a fresh install of Firefox sent shivers down my spine. 
[B]
CONCLUSIONS[/B]
From my experience Ubuntu is more straightforward and newbie friendly than Kubuntu. I wish I had advised my friend to go with Ubuntu. 

There are still a lot of bugs which should be fixed to maintain the newbie friendliness *buntu's are known for. Say automounting floppies or installing a chosen language pack during the install process. (Yes, it's got much better with Gutsy because you get the menus in your chosen language at least to find your way around to installing the language packs for your programs)
Still, it needs to be more straightforward. I like the idea of suggesting restricted drivers for installation, which makes newbies' experience with codecs so much easier. Why not add more clues like that for other newbies' questions? (i.e. how do I save to a floppy?)

I am very happy with both Gutsy Ubuntu and Kubuntu, but I don't think newbies will agree.. :confused:

### Post by DMK62 on 2008-01-28
I would agree that in most cases Ubuntu with Gnome is the best place to start for newcomers. Although you can get to tinker to your hearts content with settings etc with Ubuntu it by default gives you a simple and well laid out desktop. The System Preferences and Administration menus groups things together well, whereas in Kubuntu its not always as obvious ( System Settings, System, and Kcontrol etc ). Although I prefer Konqueror over nautilus it takes some getting used to and diving into the settings the first few times can be daunting to a newcomer. I have heard some people recommend a KDE distro for newcomers as the desktop resembles what they are used to with Windows but I think that besides the K menu placement and the quicklaunch area it pretty much ends there. 

The Gnome release cycle is also more predictable than KDE. How long have there been rumours of when  the full version of KDE 4.0 will actually be released. It has to add to the difficulty for the Kubuntu devs as they are working on 3.5.x but also having to look towards integration of 4.0. 

I currently run a dual boot with separate installs of ubuntu and kubuntu. I got my feet wet with Ubuntu Breezy and learned my way around with it and the experience gained there and with other versions after Breezy definitely helped me with Kubuntu.

I don't have a floppy on this compter so I can't really add to that point. A standard Firefox install on Kubuntu doesn't include ubufox which is installed by default with Ubuntu 7.10. It does make plugins etc more easier to deal with on Ubuntu than Kubuntu. I would like to see Firefox included with Kubuntu by default as a large amount of windows users coming to liunx have used it or have knowledge of it.
