### Post by darkod on 2013-01-05
Have you called Logitech and asked them if they have developed linux driver and gave it to Ubuntu?

It's easy to say things just work in windows when manufacturers race to create drivers and send them to MS, so it really looks like it simply works out of the box. They don't do the same for linux.

The dual screen issue can be related to the video driver also. Not all drivers can be included in the live cd of 700MB and especially if you have nvidia card, nvidia doesn't release the drivers too. So it's a bit tricky since you first need to install, and then activate the nvidia driver.

You might find the same works for the bluetooth if you install using another keyboard temporarily.

I have Logitech wireless keyboard, but not bluetooth, just the simple 2.4GHz wireless and it works just fine including in live mode and during install.

At the dn of the day, use whatever works for you, but don't blame linux for something that some times hardware manufacturers don't do.

### Post by ScottDeagan on 2013-01-05
My experience differs somewhat to yours. I received a brand new laptop for Christmas, couldn't gel with Windows 8 (in my humble opinion, Windows 8 is some kind of bad joke, but that's just me). I tried to install Windows 7, and I can assure you it does not work "straight out of the box". You have find and install about 23 different drivers (quite difficult if you don't have another PC as the WiFi and LAN don't even work without drivers on this laptop).

While not everything worked "out of the box" when I installed Ubuntu 12.10 on the laptop, all of the important stuff did - like LAN, WiFi and even the graphics driver (Intel HD 4000).

If you want a seamless out of the box experience with Linux/Ubuntu, you'd be better off buying Linux certified machines from the likes of System 76 (and a handful of others). System 76 are particularly good as they even customize BIOSes to ensure Linux compatibility.

Be it Windows or Linux - it's most unlikely you'd be able to plug in and installation CD and have a perfect install come out the other end (unless you have truly blessed hardware). In my experience, Linux will detect and install drivers for much more hardware than Windows.
