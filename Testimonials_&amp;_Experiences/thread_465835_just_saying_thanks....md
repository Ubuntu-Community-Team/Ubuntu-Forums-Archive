### Post by rickdog on 2007-06-06
First of all, I had been a hardcore windows user since the early 90's and 3.1. At one time I even scoffed at Apple wondering why they didn't just give up and go out of business already. It was mid to late 90's when I began to hear stuff about linux, but I didn't give it much thought. The unix-like environment and the command line seemed to much of a pain to deal with and it just didn't look good to me. That and with 98 and then XP, I figured that microsoft might just be the end all!

However, having payed a little attention to the news surrounding microsoft and hearing about anti-trust issues, I began to realize that there was something wrong with the os market and that microsoft had pulled the wool over my eyes for some time.

Eventually, circumstances would find me back in college and working on my senior project. I had been using a software package called ArcGIS, from ESRI. Now the software is pretty cool, but uses a lot of resources and eventually, totally crashed my good ol' Toshiba laptop. So much so that earlier this year, trying to resolve issues and replacing the hard drive, I ended up going through at least 3 all night re-installs of windows XP to get things straight.

Don't ask me how, but some time in either late February or early March, I stumbled upon a website describing the linux os and displaying some screenshots. Wow! I thought, this has really come a long way! So, with the old hard drive from my laptop, I began experimenting. I admit, that I knew NOTHING about linux command, but dove in anyway. I first tried Debian (not even sure which distro), but failed to get that running. Then came Gentoo, and I actually got the GUI going, but it was still pretty tweaked. So, I went to Fedora 6, and that was a little better, I was really stoked about the compiz deal that they had (this was pre-beryl for me of course). Now, I'm sure all these distros work fine, but it was me that didn't know what the heck was going on, so I probably screwed something up.

Anyway, then I found Ubuntu. Immediately I was up and running, I learned a little more command line lingo and I found this forum. The rest is history!

I admit that there are occasional issues and it's not perfect, but I love it! I have been in situations where I started to panic, but going through this forum has totally brought me from the depths of despair on many occasions. When I upgraded from Edgy to Feisty and my desktop wouldn't even boot, I freaked, but using the forum and noticing that Feisty changed the hard drives from hda to sda but somehow didn't update my fstab or menu.lst, I got through it. Then of course there's the kernel upgrades and the nvidia card on my desktop. After going from 2.6.20-15 to -16 losing the xserver, then getting around that, I was expecting it and conquered it when I installed Studio and the lowlatency kernel. Then of course, I lost madwifi somehow on my laptop when I added Studio to that, but no prob, got through that.

Nevertheless, I still have windows (dual boot) on my laptop. I look forward to the end of this summer, though. That's when I plan on finishing my senior project and my usage of the ESRI software (it's a timed student copy). Once that is complete, I will then vanquish the last remnant of windows on my computers once and for all! Then I will focus my attention on working with GRASS which does similar stuff as ArcGIS, but is open source.

So I would like to say thank you to everyone that has helped in the past and in the future as well! Hopefully I can return the favor to someone down the road as I continue to learn more about linux

rick

--even though no one hardly ever responds to anything I ever write, question or comment
