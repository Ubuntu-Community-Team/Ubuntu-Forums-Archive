### Post by alexis44 on 2011-03-29
Why do people stay with Windows?  That is a good question.  I suppose different people have different answers to that.  It may be that they're just familiar with it and don't have the time or inclination to learn a new system.  Also, maybe because you can download so many programs because they were made for Windows.  Of course, that's where part of the problem with Windows is.  

I had no thought of changing until recently.  My computer was at Death's door, literally.  It had several Disk Boot Failures.  I was about to just write it off.  Then, I remembered that about three years ago, I made a Live CD of Ubuntu.  I think it was 7.10.  I was able to access the Internet, and that's all.   That gave me hope.  With some luck, I was able to get the Computer to boot up again.  I thought the best thing to do was to find the newest version of Ubuntu and make another disk of it.  Soon after, I installed it with a Partition for Vista.  

The Partition didn't work so well.  The Ubuntu side worked just fine, but not the Windows side.  I had error messages with Windows and the Computer wouldn't shut down from that side.  The answer seemed obvious.  I put the Ubuntu disk back in and decided to format again and just install Ubuntu as the OS.  I installed all the normal codecs at the OS installation as well.  

I've still had glitches, however.  That may have been my fault.  I have this habit of downloading things such as Pictures, Video clips and music from various sources that may not all be clean.  The vast majority of them are fine, but a few may have not been.   That's a hold-over from Windows.  The glitch goes away when I re-boot.
