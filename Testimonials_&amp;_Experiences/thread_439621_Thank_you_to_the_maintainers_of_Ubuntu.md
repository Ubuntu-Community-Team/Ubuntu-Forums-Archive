### Post by h4ku on 2007-05-10
I recently decided to move from Slackware (sorry Mr. Volkerding) after the whole flash 9/ glibc2.4 issue and one week into using PCLinuxOS, upon a friend's recommendation, I got discouraged with getting wireless support working in PCLOS TR4 for my LinkSys WMP54G V4.1 card (which uses the rt61 driver for it's native RaLink chipset.)  PCLOS didn't like it when I diabled the avahi daemon to get it to stop trying to DHCP with my router (I have static IP addressing setup) Since Ubuntu was ranked at #1 on distrowatch, I decided to give it a shot.  It turns out that someone had posted a step-by-step on how to get my wireless card working with Ubuntu, taking static IP config into account.  All I had to do was uninstall avahi and NetworkManager for this to work properly.
Getting my wireless card to work meant package upgrading and the like.  Next was getting my GeForce2 with 64MB DDR to work.  It turns out that another helpful soul had a howto involving the nvidia 8776 package and a patch for the 2.6 kernel.  After editing the monitor section of my xorg.conf and adding the proper refresh rates for my NEC Multisync 97F, Blender works.
The Wacom driver for my Intuos 3 has me drawing with pressure sensitivity in The Gimp and smoothly using the Inkscape illustration package.  Additionally, LMMS and SCIM/ANTHY for Japanese input worked perfectly while, with PCLOS, were available but didn't work correctly (SCIM never activated and LMMS was missing plugins for the core sound generators--tripleoscillator...)
Also, I was more than pleasantly surprised to find that some odd packages I depend on were in the repository and work perfectly after installation: ZynAddSubFX, Seq24, Hydrogen!

Thus, I would like to extend a ginormous thank you to everyone involved, from the maintainers of this incredible distro, to the generous users who posted those helpful guides (without which I'd probably still be stuck) for making this truly the best Linux I've ever used.

I've found that I love Ubuntu because while it sets up most everything on its own, it won't break if you want to do some manual configuration of your own.  I was afraid to leave Slackware because it didn't do things on its own.  I was afraid to stay with Slackware because of the upgrading headache and breaking packages that relied on a certain glibc major version.  I'm overjoyed with Ubuntu!  Not too many processes running (ps -He), not too much ram for the desktop; session.  Ahhhhh!   I can finally breathe a sigh of relief!

And I have to comment on the incredible selection the repositories (Ubuntu, World, and Universe) offer.

Thank you all very much and keep up the excellent work!
