### Post by gerowen on 2008-03-29
I've been hooked on Linux for a couple of years now, I've always enjoyed using and working with it, but there was always an issue somewhere.  The wireless has been a big one, over all these years I've never found a Linux distro that, out of the box, picked up the wireless card in "any" of my 3 laptops.  (Mine, my wife's and a spare).  Well recently I've adopted the "spare" (a Dell Inspiron E1505) as my personal laptop because I ordered the official Ubuntu 7.10 CDs from Canonical.  I'm pretty sure there are some bundled updates in these CDs because I've tried Ubuntu 7.10 on this machine before with no wireless success.  However when I used these commercially produced CDs I got in the mail, wireless, sound, display driver, everything worked like a charm right out of the box.  I'm finally able to ditch Windows once and for all with no worries about having to go back because of needing to use one thing or another, it all just works.  Ubuntu is pushing the envelope in the world of Linux, and I appreciate all of the hard work you guys are putting into this distribution.  In my job, information assurance/security is very important, so on my personal machines I feel that running Linux is the best and most secure and efficient way to keep my personal information safe.  Ubuntu 7.10 has made this a reality, again, THANKS!

**Edit:**On my other laptop though, it was not quite as successful, it's a newer Compaq, on which even the BIOS is tailored with a custom "Windows Restore" hotkey and for the first time ever, my on-board NIC didn't even work with a Linux distro, so I just figured I'll stick Windows back on it and use it as my spare.  HP (The makers of Compaq machines) offer no Linux support on their desktops even though they heavily advertise their willingness to work with Linux in the server roles.  I have what I've always wanted though, and that's all that matters.
