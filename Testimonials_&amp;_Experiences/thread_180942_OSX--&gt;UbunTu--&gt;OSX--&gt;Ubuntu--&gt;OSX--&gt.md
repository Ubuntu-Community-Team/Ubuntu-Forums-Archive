### Post by Rxke on 2006-05-23
I finally made up my mind, after the nth switch back and forth.

First of all: before people start asking 'why would you want to replace the sleek OSX experience on such an expensive machine yaddayaddayadda...' My machine is officially 'obsolete' by Macintosh standards, despite me thinking it is *still* a very capable machine. 'nuff said.

I have two old macs, one being a Clamshell iBook, that's running 24/7 as an Ubuntu-machine, and it gave me less headaches than running OSX 24/7, which already make me think all kinds of good things about Ubuntu, but there were some programs on my desktop machine that I couldn't figure out how to run/replace under Linux. Now that I figured out the most part of them, I switched...

Yes, again, but this switch is for real. Plethora of good reasons.

What eventually made me decide to get rid of OSX once and for all: I couldn't find a reasonably good, and free scanner and OCR program. That really frustrated me very, very much, untill I said, "okay that settles it, I'm outa here!"

Also, I learned to love Kile, the LaTeX editor, and missed it quite badly. I'm a Gnome user, but not a fundie re: using KDE stuff if it's good. ;) 

But above all: there's no community like the Ubuntu community. 
[COLOR="SeaGreen"]Ubuntu may be free, its community is truely priceless![/COLOR] (Hmmm... I think I just made up a slogan :mrgreen: )

I mean, this thing really works; the sharing ideas, the helping eachother out of deep or shallow troubles, the tips, the HowTo's, the pointers to new nice software, the jokes,... The feeling we're all witness -and contributing in our own ways- of something greater than the sum of all parts.

Rock on, boys and girls, Rock on, you're the best!
