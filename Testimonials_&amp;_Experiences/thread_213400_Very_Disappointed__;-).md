### Post by Max Roswell on 2006-07-11
I just put Ubuntu on my new Acer laptop, and I must say, I'm very disappointed.  I'd read a lot of threads here, did my research, Googled the make and model to death.

I was prepared for problems with partitioning.  I was prepared for the video resolution to not work.  I was prepared for wireless to need a lot of tweaking.  I had reams of printouts for every possible contingency.  I had a pot of coffee made.

I even chose to do it at the All-Star break, so I'd have 3 whole nights without a Red Sox game that I'd have to miss.

And then...it all just worked.  

Partitions?  Easy peasy.
Resolution?  Perfect.
Wireless?  I'm posting this here, ain't I?

Dang it, Ubuntu!  How am I supposed to feel like a tech genius if you don't give me any problems to solve?  Put in a CD, click some buttons, and be done with it?  HOW AM I SUPPOSED TO VALIDATE MY MANHOOD THAT WAY?

(Seriously - awesome.  Works great, looks great, super easy.  Good job, everyone!)
