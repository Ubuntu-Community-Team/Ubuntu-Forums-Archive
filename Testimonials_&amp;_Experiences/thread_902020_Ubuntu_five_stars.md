### Post by Comzee on 2008-08-26
Ubuntu is the easiest Linux distribution I've ever used. I started out with edgy, the first Linux OS I'd ever worked with. Since then, I've tried  numerous other distributions, just a few include, Mandriva, Gentoo, Slax, Fedora, Suse, Parsix. Every Linux distribution has it's place and it's purpose, but for user friendliness, software support and availability, driver support, a massive community, and a clean interface, Ubuntu is #1. For anybody wanting to get into the Linux scene Ubuntu is a great way to go. I only wish more people knew of this incredible Operating system!


Thats just me, post if you think I'm wrong. I like seeing what people think.

### Post by Comzee on 2008-08-26
I've always been a huge fan of GNU project, I wouldn't want that to change. What I would like to see is the Ubuntu people, whoever they may be that deal with this, cut a deal with retail companies, I.E. Best buy, to carry Ubuntu Desktop Edition without that much of a retail price increase. I'd like to see Ubuntu single CD's in stores for about 5-7 USD.

I hate commercialized Linux distributions personally. I guess it all comes down to greed. Thats why I stopped using Windows. I hate when people code something, then mass market it at zero expense to them. Software can be copied over and over and over again with no cost to the maker. It's not like selling other things like TV's or computer hardware, that take raw materials and man hours to make.

I really appreciate what the GNU project is doing and has achieved.
