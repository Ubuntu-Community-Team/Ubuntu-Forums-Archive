### Post by furigimp on 2005-12-06
so i had food poisoning.

i'd like to think that at my moment of uttermost sickness...i had my best revelation.

i had been sick for a week...and the only thing i could do without retching was to lay in bed and surf the net.  invariably, this led to /. and Ubuntu came up.

i thought, "hey!  what really is stopping me from going completely OSS?"

in a word, nothing.

i surf the net, talk on IM, listen to music, watch videos (which means torrents).

it dawned on me that such things could not possibly be that difficult to accomplish with OSS.

i already knew of some alternatives.  there was firefox, which i used already.  there was xmms, luk, amarok, etc. for music.  there was VLC media player for encoded video.  i used azureus on windows - and it being java based...no reason i couldn't use that in linux.

but linux is hard to use right?

well, yeah, it was when i decided that amongst term papers and final exams during my 1st year at college i would try my hand at redhat.  that went miserably...and i crawled back to gates and ballmer with my eyes cast downward.

but it's half a decade later, and i currently have a lot of free time on my hands.

soooo...i downloaded the dvd package, burned it...loaded up live for about 3 minutes, decided that Ubuntu was it - and did a complete install.

install was really simple.  so was redhat, but you couldn't seem to eff ncurses up if you tried.

once installed, came the fun stuff.

most directions online came with command line help.  apt-get, sudo, and other weird linux commands.  Ubuntu came with Synaptic which is both a blessing and a curse.  took a little while to understand how repositories worked, and how universe and multiverse worked as far as getting packages.

to quickly interject, getting the wireless working on this dell 6000 was a breeze on breezy.  no hassle whatsoever.

once that was settled, i went about recreating my computing needs.

downloaded xmms, but i had no sound.  ah, i needed gstreamer stuff.  okay, got that done.  sound worked through xmms.

downloaded vlc media player.  had video, but no sound.  hmm, i need a specific gst-ept-plugin or something.  i love google, and the ubuntu community for having information so easily accessible.  got what i needed and vlc worked fine.

then i rebooted, and the volume control on the systray was all effed up.  for the next 5 hours i didn't know wtf to do.

finally, and stupidly i admit, i realized i had to gst-register-0.8.  durrrr.  everything was peachy after that.

so now i have all the "essentials" i had gotten with winxp (firefox and gaim is a given).

er, no i don't.  there's two things that i'm still working on...well three.

i can't get skype to dial out.

i can't get azureus to work properly.  it connects to trackers, i have opened the ports with firestarter - but the downloads won't work right.

and i'm just now scouring the net to see how i can get my s-video to work.

but i love Ubuntu.  it's everything i hoped it was going to be.  linux has re-invigorated my love of computing...and reawakened my love of the command line.  i didn't realize how much i missed using the command line on a regular basis.

it's like being 10 again and discovering computers and playing with them.  :smile:
