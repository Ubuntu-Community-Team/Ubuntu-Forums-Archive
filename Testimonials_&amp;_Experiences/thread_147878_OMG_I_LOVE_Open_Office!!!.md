### Post by glug101 on 2006-03-21
Ok, I wouldn't normally write about something like this, but I just had another one of those moments.  You know, one of those moments when some small part of a piece of open source software just jumps out at you and COMPLETELY justifies it's existance.  

Tonight I was editing my resume with Open Office and I reached a point where I wanted to do columns.  However, I only wanted to use them for one existing section, and didn't want them to continue before or after.  Now, I support Windows software at work, and one of my least favorite phone calls is the 'I screwed up my MS Office formatting beyond resonable limits, could you help?'.  The person calling is often some sweet old lady who is completely befuddled as to why Office will just not listen to reason. (well, I'm often in that situation too, so I don't blame them one bit)  But when I tried column formatting an existing document.... IT WORKED AS I EXPECTED!!  No funny business with the columns wanting to continue to the end of the document, no bizzarre instance of the document going to all caps for a paragraph or italics or bold.  The program did what I told it to do, nothing more, nothing less.

Now, I'm not the typical user.  I practice 'clean' formatting and try everything I can imagine to remove strange hangovers that don't actually print (like the crazy 20 blank lines that seem to show up on so many documents), but I must admit that I've been using Microsoft stuff at work for so long that I suspected that what should have been a simple bit of formatting would blow up in my face.  

Perhaps someday this cleanly formatted resume will get me a job working for a reasonable employer that will listen to my rantings about open source programs and at least give them a chance.  I mean, they don't even have to give up the Windows security blanket, they could just use the best bargain* in office suites out there and install Open Office on their Windows boxes.)

Keep up the wonderful work!!  Open source developers really do deserve a national (international?) holiday!

*Note that I said best bargain and not the best office suite.  I have the personal prejudice that WordPerfect is still superior to Open Office, but it's still a close call, and ODF is a concept that alone could make up the difference when it's better adopted;)
