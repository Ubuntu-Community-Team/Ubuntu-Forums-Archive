### Post by mhpathfinder on 2009-05-19
Echo the Love.

I've installed Ubuntu Jaunty on three of our five home computers (two desktops, two laptops and an ASUS EEE netbook).  The Asus netbook is an entry-level 2-gig surf model, so will not accommodate any version of Ubuntu at this time, no matter how stripped down it is.  My wife's laptop is running Windows XP and Microsoft Frontpage, on which she is dependent to keep her clinical psychology web site running.  She has done well to learn so much about computers and software through me, and doesn't want a drastic change to her computing routines, so I've provided a used Gateway E2100 desktop with Ubuntu Jaunty for her own experimentation, should she be interested to change.

I use Ubuntu exclusively on my Gateway E4100 desktop and HP Special Edition L2005US laptop.  I made the complete move away from anything-Windows early this year, although I've been experimenting with Ubuntu since its early distributions.  In past years, I've tried other LINUX distributions, such as Zenwalk, Red Hat, and Novell's Open SUSE, but nothing compares to Ubuntu for ease of use, hardware compatibility, and personalization.

Much of my professional work is comprised of programming entry systems and computer-based camera/DVR systems for commercial customers which, unfortunately, demands Windows-based software for access.  I can usually get by with using the customer computers for programming, thus no need to install Windows on my own equipment.  However, I experienced a minor panic last week and replaced Ubuntu with Windows XP on my laptop.  It took me half a day to reinstall the Windows drivers, programs, antivirus, anti-malware, anti-spyware programs and run the security scans and updates.  Then it was done.  I had forgotten the intrusive pop-up windows in XP, constantly nagging me for individual software, antivirus, and system updates.  I had also forgotten the predatory techniques of profiteering commercial companies who ride other software programs on top of other programs being installed.  To make a long story shorter, my Windows installation didn't last through the day.  It was just too much out of my own control.  I stripped down the drive and put my Ubuntu system back on, with a deeper appreciation for its stability, MY control over it, and how simple the update process is, all at once for software and operating system, with a single mouse-click.

I also gained an appreciation for the Ubuntu Community and how we help each other to learn so much about Ubuntu, our programs, and code through an atmosphere of cooperation and mutual respect to make Ubuntu our system, personally tailor by each of us according to our own specific needs and desires--free and open source.

What's not to love?

Amen
MH Pathfinder
