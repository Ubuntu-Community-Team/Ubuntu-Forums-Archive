### Post by mivo on 2008-06-19
> **Daggo said:**
> Everytime I hear 'why can't ubuntu be more like windows' it sounds like 'why can't my screwdriver be more like my can opener?' It doesn't make sense to me at all.

The analogy is a little off (mine are usually too ;)).

For most computer users, an operating system is merely the base for running applications on. Both Windows and Linux, as operating systems, are meant to do the same thing. They just approach the goal differently, but the purpose is identical.

It's like can openers. There are can openers with a spike that you have to push into the can. Then there are can openers that have a handle to operate a wheel that drives a blade. Other can openers are electrical. But they all do the same thing: they open cans. Linux and Windows are both can openers, not screwdrivers.

So a Windows user may be more like someone who is used to electrical can openers, and they are used to needing no force, not having to turn the handle, etc. A mechanical can opener may be more powerful and allow for more independence (no dependency on electricity to open your cans), but it's *different*, and many people don't like *different*.

I understand people who are frustrated, though. If you have always opened your cans with an electrical can opener, and now you are handed a different type of can opener and you just can't get the can open, it's annoying. Especially when you are hungry and really want to get to the yummy stuff inside the can. The stuff people have been telling you about. The fantastically delicious food that you really want to taste.

So, be understanding of hungry people who have never been exposed to anything but electrical can openers.

### Post by 23meg on 2008-06-20
> **stormbolter]It is important that we have whiners. I prefer to have people telling me what they don't like that only have people that tell me what they like.
[/QUOTE]

No, it's important that we have people who know what is what and tell it like it is, in the correct form and place. It's important that we have people who know how and where to criticize, and get at least partially involved in fixing what they criticize (i.e. filing good bug reports). They are not necessarily whiners. People who point to shortcomings of free software are not necessarily whiners, and characterizing all of them as such will produce disastrous results. There are lots of people out there providing good and sane feedback, and it's their work that's improving things, while whiners just shout around in a vacuum (i.e. this forum). Free software cannot mature and grow without peer review and critique-driven iteration, and it's the critique of thousands of non-whiners that's improving it every minute.

[QUOTE=armandh]How could those actually writing the code know what needs work without the whine.[/QUOTE]

Correction: how could they know what needs work without good criticism, in the form of bug reports, or sane, constructive discourse (for broader issues where bug reports are not the correct form)?

Note that to a very large extent, this forum is not read by those writing the code. It's not a mechanism for providing feedback, and there's no promise that things posted here will cause any progress. If anything, there should be a disclaimer that this is a "by users, for users" place.
