### Post by christopher.newell on 2008-01-18
A few months ago, I decided to try Ubuntu, and got very frustrated, blamed my frustration on the software, and stopped using it in favour of Windows.  I'm not sure what prompted me to try again, but I'm glad I did.  Two days ago, I deleted my Windows partition.  (For the anti-Microsoft people, don't pat me on the back yet--my wife's laptop still has XP on it, so I use that for synchronizing my PDA and for CorelDraw.)
     I thought Ubuntu was so much harder to get used to until I realized that I've been working with DOS, then Windows for well over a decade, and I just expected right away to be able to do in Ubuntu everything that took me years to figure out in Windows.  I still don't like having to go to the terminal so often, but I'm dealing, and I'm not looking back.  I installed Wine, but I don't have any Windows software installed in it.  I also installed VMWare server, but I haven't actually installed Windows to it.
     It's not easy getting used to a new system when you're used to being able to do whatever you need and fix it if things go wrong, but if you stick with the community, you'll get through.  Thanks to all the people who posted problems that have helped me out, and thanks to everyone who has answered them helpfully!
