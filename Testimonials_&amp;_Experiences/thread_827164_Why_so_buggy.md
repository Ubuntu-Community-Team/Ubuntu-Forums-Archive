### Post by claschxtreme on 2008-06-14
I can only confirm your opinion Sink, i have also 3 machines running Ubuntu, my whole family uses it and lots of friends too.
Why oh why did they put some really buggy release just after the most stable and pleasant Linux i have ever experienced. I thinking of renaming it Floppy Heron, don't get me wrong, i love it but this things annoy me very much :
Things that worked perfectly in 7.10 and now don't

-Compiz settings, unable to change or edit animations, no caps, etc
-Sound mixing, multiple sounds playing simultaneously, or muted or just paused
-Games playing with same resolution as desktop in fullscreen mode, not windowed
-Games that use copy protection CD's or DVD's, none of them work anymore (before and after Wine 1 update)
-Secure java applets, they just don't load, no matter what.
-Xine, it sometimes produces one hell of an "unkillable" Totem process in fresh Hardy installs. Only solution : Remove.
-Changing frequency for some screen resolution.
-Quiet BOINC, now 100% and going. Had to disable it.
-Display recognition, works fine with all new, flat ones but somehow my old Acerview is gone.
-OpenOffice, i guess this one is related to Java too.
-Amsn, in some configurations, with Compiz enabled, trying to change display picture restarts X, random issues.
-Auto-hiding panels, now they want to stay up.
-Playing movies without flickering

And many more ...
I just spent the whole week searching for fixes, workarounds or solutions. I'm tired and betting on the next release. I wish my first post here had been more positive, Ubuntu has been a great experience for me.
