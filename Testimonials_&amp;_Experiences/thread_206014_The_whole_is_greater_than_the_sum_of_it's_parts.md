### Post by lasal on 2006-06-29
First time posting here, thought i would share some thoughts; 
(hope its not too boring)

I started out with SUSE Linux 7.2 back in 2000 or 2001 in grad school; couldn't do a damn thing except browse the start menu, looking back it really seemed foreign. But like all things that intrique us, you keep going back. 

I went to Suse 8.x in 2003 and bought myself a book ("learn linux in 10 minutes"); now I really didn't expect to learn everything in 10 minutes but the book had all the terminal commands. From that point (and with some help from forums like this one) I was able to configure my modem with Wvdial, edit files with VI, kill a process, and compile programs (mplayer was my first success story). All the while my knowledge of the underlying logic of the operating system was growing. 

Fade out and back in to 2005; Suse 9.3 and continued to advance with configuring wireless adaptors and compiling programs, (xine, etc). I have always been focused on multimedia and Amarok really caught my eye and I continue to use it. 

Fade out and back to 2006; so very tired of windows; keep hearing about Vista right around the corner; Install Suse 10.1; what a regression, cannot get the wireless to work, zydas 1201 (probably the easiest chip for Linux). Firmware is no help once I figured out that these files were no longer included. I searched around and decided to kick Suse to the curb; tried Mandriva (no luck), Fedora (no luck, hosed my MBR); 

Then I came across Ubuntu; couldn't believe I had overlooked it all this time. The philosophy jives with my social and political views, so I decided to give it a go. Unbelievable; this is what I have been waiting for. Everything is understandable, logical and easy to get the basics working with minimal effort. Wireless worked as soon as I copied the firmware files to the correct folder. I have spent the last month (and most waking hours) with this OS and cannot get enough. 

I weaned myself off MS Money; and have all my finaces in GNUcash; great program after some tutoring. When I get some time, I am going to finally delete the windows partition and set up a better Ubuntu (want to have /home and /swap on a separate drive). It has been fun and frustrating at times learning linux over the years, but well worth the effort. My praise and admiration goes out to the developers of Debian and Ubuntu.

-joel
