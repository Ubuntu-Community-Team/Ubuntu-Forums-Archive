### Post by cybertron3000 on 2008-01-22
As a newb of 2 weeks to Ubuntu, I can say I found my new home.  It is quicker and seems to show sharper and better pixelation of graphics than my XP.  Uses an avg. of 50% less RAM from when I've looked at both.  My slow but happy Ubuntu discovery is after about 15 years of Monoposoft lethargic products.  I installed more 500 MB more RAM into my old HP Pavilion 521c, 1.47Ghz, it helped a bit, but still didn't make it seem "alive" again.

To help other newbs who ask the same question, I can give you my own feedback of trying the 3: Ubuntu, Kubuntu(kde) and Xubuntu (Xfce).  I've got to say for me Ubuntu is the best.

Kubuntu (transformed through Ubuntu - not a "pure" Kubuntu install): for me was slower and was hungry for RAM, which is what turned me off of Windblows in the 1st place.  Perhaps a pure install of it might be different?

Xubuntu: Again not a pure install but done through Ubuntu OS.  This was not the speed demon I read about - though it did use the same minimal 15-20% RAM as my Ubuntu does.  Interface and menu though are not as intuitive or detailed enough as Ubuntu for me.

Keep in mind I'm still fresh to this, but this is my take so far.  I'm definitely staying with Ubuntu OS (any) for the long haul now.  :KS

### Post by DMK62 on 2008-01-22
Glad to hear that you are enjoying your Ubuntu install so far.

Although you can install the metapackages for other DE's such as Kubuntu ( KDE ) on top of Ubuntu it can lead to some pretty messy menus and I noticed a small performance decrease. You may also run into some quirks with programs. You do however save on drive space by having them together.

I tried the kubuntu package on ubuntu and ended up going dual boot with separate installs of Ubuntu and Kubuntu which is chainloaded through grub. If you decide to do separate installs of them I am sure you will get assistance here on setting that up.

I actually found Kubuntu runs faster on my computer than Ubuntu and I have similiar hardware specs. Kubuntu / KDE places as much as possible in cache ( ram ) and that may be why it seemed to be using so much memory. Strigi can also take up system resources and it can be removed and you can use Kfind instead.

Feel free to ask as many questions as you need.

Dale
