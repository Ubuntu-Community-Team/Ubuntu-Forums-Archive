### Post by spindrift on 2006-07-05
So several months ago, after much agonizing over which distro would hold the keys to my heart, I installed Kubuntu.  I had previously tried Fedora, which was too clunky, and Gentoo, which I would have expected to do my homework, sexually gratify, and make me breakfast in the morning for how long it took to compile.  Instead, it had the same feel as Fedora.

Kubuntu and I hooked up, and things were awesome.  Hardware support was fantastic, menus were nimble, Dapper and I were frolicking hither and yon.  Occasionally amaroK would come over, we'd have too much Wine and get a little kinky.  (We always used LaTeX though.)  Time passed, however, and after much usage I begin to wonder if I was being completely satisfied.  True, I had customized the system to my heart's content, but one thing had eluded us - the magic of Xgl - and it put a strain on our relationship.  Additionally, I noticed KDE's memory usage was awfully high, and I personally felt that I deserved better for myself.

I had heard talk that Gnome could give me Xgl, and that it was so much lighter and speedier in every way.  Feeling ashamed yet compelled, Kubuntu and I "took a break" and I took a spin with my new mistress.  Ubuntu had all the amazing hardware support of Kubuntu, but it felt even quicker!  Automatix performed an hour's worth of configuration in about 10 minutes, and one of the many, many (many) tutorials on Xgl+Dapper worked the first time, unlike on Kubuntu.  Suddenly I had an amazing 3D desktop with all the codec support I wanted.  Surely I must be thrilled!  And, indeed, at first I was.

It was later that I discovered, while fiddling unsuccessfully with full kicker transparency, that along with Gnome's speed came an extremely spartan approach to customization.  Dialog boxes that would be full of interesting little check marks were replaced with a couple of radio buttons.  Functionality I took for granted in KDE was nowhere to be found.  Not to mention the unflattering comparisons between Kopete > GAIM, K3B > GnomeBaker, KOffice > -- Hmm, Gnome doesn't even have an integrated office suite, does it?  Suddenly, my mistress became a whore - I expunged her from my system, waving a bittersweet farewell to my rotating cube desktop and vowing to find it again on another system.

Rather than come crawling back to my former partner right away, I decided to play the field. Mepis promised me everything I had had before, and more: Great hardware support, speedy operation, the might of KDE, a version of Automatix, Ubuntu repositories, and pretty pretty pyramid wallpaper.  But would I be able to trust again?  I wiped my system once more (thanking the Linux Gods for transportable /home folders) and loaded it up.

At first, it seemed my search was finally over.  Speedy installation and a lightning-fast boot gave me hope.  The first kink came, however, when I discovered that I had to mess around with dhclient to get my wireless up and running - the only distro to not "just get it".  It's okay, Mepis, I'm sure you have some baggage too. I don't mind rebooting my router three times for you to detect the signal.  I loaded up Automatix Mepis, and let it rip.  About 5 times. Eventually it was able to completely download all the selected packages.  It's okay, Mepis, I know that your Automatix is still in alpha.  I don't even mind that your Control Panel seems unpolished and your menu options don't always make sense.  You come with a ton of apps!

Then I discovered that the speedstepping daemon was not loaded either, most of my WMVs wouldn't play, and libdvdcss claimed itself inoperable - two balls dropped in the hardware department, and failed codec support.  This simply would not do.  Where was my promised happy ending?  Finally, more research taught me that my former high memory usage was partially due to using too many Gnome apps, which loaded Gnome libs alongside KDE's.  An easily avoidable choice, as KDE has more than enough apps without touching Gnome's.  Oh god, what have I done?

My heart broken once again, I made one last request of my fallen angel - fire up KTorrent and download the Ubuntu Server ISO, where I'll patch my kernel and then install kubuntu-desktop, begging her to forgive me.  Sometimes you never appreciate what you have until it's gone.
