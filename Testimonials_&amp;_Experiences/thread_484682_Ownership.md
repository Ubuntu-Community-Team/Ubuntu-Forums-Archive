### Post by Sunforge on 2007-06-26
I've been running Ubuntu exclusively for about four months now and barring a hiccup upgrading from Edgy to Feisty I like the fact that I feel like I own the system. If there's something that you don't like: change it. If there's something you don't understand: there's always something simpler. If you want a challenge: you'll find one. If on the other hand, you just want to boot the system, surf the web, check your mail and get some work done, you can leave it as it is and things tick along just fine. 

The only problem that I've really encountered that I can't quite get right is printing to an Epson R1800. I can print but colour management isn't as good as a Mac or PC equivalent. For now though, I'm quite happy to leave printing where it is as I do have other machines to do the scary stuff. I've heard that there's something called Gutenberg which makes printing a little better for the R1800 but this is still in development. Am I right about this?
