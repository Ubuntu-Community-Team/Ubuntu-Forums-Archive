### Post by em007a on 2007-08-20
Hello Ubuntu enthusiast!

I just wanted to share an experience with you. Ubuntu edgy was my third attempt (over the last 5 or so years) trying out Linux as an alternative to Windows.

Shortly after installing Edgy and realizing how far Linux has progressed, I started talking about it to just about anybody that would listen. Working on the computer was suddenly "fun" again. I started to post about it on my blog, telling anyone who wanted to read it how impressed I am.

My father-in-law was one of the people that had been reading my blog entries. One day out of the blue, he called me and asked me to build him a new computer and install Ubuntu on it. 

I started ordering parts and thinking to myself "*is he a good candidate for Ubuntu"? *I mean, he has had several computers over the years but never spends much time on them. He is one of those people that just never appeared to know what he wanted to do with a computer, but he sure wanted a good one!

I was talking to my wife while assembling the parts and it dawned on me that he may be **the perfect candidate for Ubuntu**. He had no real experience with computers. He really didn't know his way around except to check email. Believe it or not, his favourite game to show off was the original Wolfenstein. (which he didn't play) :)

Anyway, I installed edgy (no dual boot, just Ubuntu). Changed the wallpaper and added a couple games to the panel, etc. 

When I delivered and set up his new computer  he liked the "feel" of it right away. He called a couple times to ask questions, but otherwise has been on his own ever since. 

He spends quite a bit of time on the computer now. He called me the other day to say he installed Feisty! *On his own*, just popped in the cd he ordered from ShipIt and did a clean install. This is actually a big achievement for him and I could tell he was proud of himself. And so was I.

A lot of people just accept Windows because that's what they think a computer is. If they started with an alternative OS, they may find it more to their liking. My father-in-law is proof of that.
