### Post by luckylucky on 2010-06-05
Well, I've been using Ubuntu pretty much exclusively since nearly the beginning, back in 4.x days.  Of course ubuntu has always had its' issues, but with each new release it kept on getting better and better over all as a serious desktop OS.

I have multiple computers which I've dropped windoze on for ubuntu.  Like I said, I use ubuntu pretty much exclusively for 99% of my computer usage.

Ubuntu 8.04 was and still is my favorite release.  It works.  Period.  Sure, of course I had to configure stuff to get it going, but it works.

Since 8.04 each new release kept on having various issues, things breaking that used to work in earlier releases, and on and on.  I kept on running the treadmill of hoping that the next release in 6 months will fix the issues, and will be better.

Sigh.  I am now hoping that 10.10 will satisfy the above wish.

In earlier days I enjoyed the 6 month release cycles, sort of like a kid waiting for christmas to enjoy the new toys.  Now I feel that I have matured and am happy to wait for longer releases when things work.  Spending a day to backup, install new OS, and fix things is no longer the joy it used to be.

I've read on several sites that people think ubuntu should give up the 6 month release cycle, and go to a "it'll be released once it is done" cycle.  Obviously the push to meet a deadline means that known bugs are released before fixed.

My suggestions

1) @ Ubuntu - Switch to a yearly release, and spend the time to make a good OS, rather than making many.  Once released spend the 6 months to fix problems rather than working on the next release.

2) @ Users - Wait at least 3 months after an LTS release to download it.  Then install it and do all the updates.  The play around to get your system running like you would like.  Later only update the security updates, and forget about the other things, unless you really want it - if it ain't broke don't fix it, as often they end up braking.  When you are happy with your system just use it and enjoy a good OS.  Forget about the little releases that happen every 6 months.  Eye candy is fun, but expensive for your time in all the work involved to upgrade your OS every 6 months.  BTW, who even bothers playing around with the cool eyecandy cube anymore?  Just make a happy computer and get on with your life/work.
