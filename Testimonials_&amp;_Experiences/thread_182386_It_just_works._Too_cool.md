### Post by jlhughes on 2006-05-26
About two months ago I bought a US$250 computer and installed Ubuntu as a dual boot.  I wanted to see what all the Ubuntu talk was all about.

The system had CD-RW/DVD-ROM drive, and I decided I needed to write DVD disks. I  yanked a Creative DVD+/- drive out of an old XP machine and installed it into the Ubuntu box.  After messing with jumpers so that both the old CD-RW and the new DVD+/- were using cable select, I rebooted.

When I stuck a blank DVD disk in the drive, the software launched and I was ready to copy the data over.

Everything just worked. I don't have to know why. I'm happy. :)
