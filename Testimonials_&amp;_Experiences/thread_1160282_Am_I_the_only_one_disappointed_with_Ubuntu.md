### Post by sub2007 on 2009-05-15
All I can say is that a lot of people who try Arch from Ubuntu don't seem to want to go back. I love Arch in that it's rolling release but you do have to be prepared to fix things breaking when it happens. It doesn't happen that often but you do have to be aware that it can. The good thing though is that you tend to have a pretty good idea when it does happen and there are often fixes posted on the forum the same day. And not having to wait between release cycles for backports (if you're lucky) to get the latest versions of stuff is very awesome. It really is rock solid stability wise too, I have no complaints from that point of view and you can do the rolling upgrades as often as you want (you're encouraged to check the forum for issues before you do the upgrade). Also requires a bit of work getting it going initially (it took me a few hours but it's pretty low maintenance thereafter). You definitely do get complete control of your system and that's one aspect that convinced me to do the switch from Ubuntu to Arch and I've never regretted it since, as grateful as I am to the distro that introduced me to Linux and still my second favourite.

### Post by sub2007 on 2009-05-17
The things I like about Arch are:

- rolling release
- fantastic package management (IMO pacman is far superior to apt, it's faster and more powerful). Also most packages come as just one package rather than being split into multiple data and dev packages.
- AUR (the community repository) that has nearly anything and that anyone can contribute to
- the focus of Arch is on simplicity and therefore Arch devs apply very little patching to the packages - most of the packages are exactly as the developer intended them
- excellent documentation and very helpful forum

The main difference between Arch and Gentoo is that Arch is a binary based distro whereas Gentoo is source based - with Gentoo (whilst there are some binary packages) most of your packages are made by compiling which has a theoretical speed advantage (using the right compilation flags) but takes far longer. But from the point of view that you have to build up from CLI then yes they are pretty similar.

This page gives you a comparison between Arch and other distros: [http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Arch_vs_Others](http://wiki.archlinux.org/index.php/Arch_vs_Others)
