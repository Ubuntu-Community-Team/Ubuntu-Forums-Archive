### Post by animaniac on 2009-02-21
**a recent comment on gizmodo:**

> used XP,

had to reinstall way to often, had to call MS every time to get a new key because my LEGAL key didn't work after a couple of reinstalls (due to broken H/W drivers), didn't like that. Even without the phone calls, way too much nagging on install.

no easy way to install all the software/drivers you need with a couple of clicks, and missing .dll files at times without any easy fix due to the lack of dependency handling. no system wide update (including all the apps), and annoying system notifications. and need to restart after every other application installed. Didn't like any of these things.

Had to constantly have anti-virus and ad-aware and have it constantly updated, and have it constantly use system resources. And even that wasn't bullet proof, and constantly nagged about one thing or another. Also unenvironmental, think about the number of watts consumed by AV software globally and annually. Did not like the viri.

When the system stopped working a couple of times, there was no easy way to recover the files, everything is on the same damn C: drive, DID NOT LIKE THAT.

Then i got Ubuntu, booted live cd, recovered my files to an external drive and installed Ubuntu, the drivers all worked nicely, any applications could easily be installed from Add/Remove programs with a few clicks, the whole system including all applications updates simultaneously. I don't get nagged for any licensing apart from the rare paid app, all apps in the repos are free and superior to many payed ones, saving me $100s without compromise. Any support i have ever needed is supplied by the community forums, and they answer quickly, there has never been a problem that couldn't be easily solved.

Out of curiosity i tried the much hyped Vista too, more eye candy, but same problems as XP plus a couple more. Tried Win 7 too, pretty, but it shares the same problems as its predecessors, DRM, no centralized package handling, single partition system, and prone to infection. So NO.

I'm a PC. And i use Ubuntu.
