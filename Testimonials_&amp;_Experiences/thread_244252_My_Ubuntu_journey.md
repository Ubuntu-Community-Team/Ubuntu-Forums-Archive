### Post by daou on 2006-08-26
My Ubuntu experience begun late June 2006. And what a ride it has been so far.

A little history first. I got my first Windows at the age of 9. After 13 years of use, Windows had become an extension of my body and mind. The choice to leap over to Linux doesn't come lightly. My first attempt was in 2001 I believe. I downloaded some popular Linux distro at the time and had them ready but never burned it or attempted to install it. After a while I had forgotten about the whole attempt. Then after starting university a couple years ago I had my first, forced, encounter with Linux. I had to learn a couple of shell commands and left it at that. I was horrified at the thought of Linux and connected the idea of a Linux user with the stereotypical long bearded, fat guru.

Fast forward to summer of 2006. I read a tech article about an alternative to Windows. I was bored so I took some time to read it. There talk about something called Ubuntu. I had some time to spare during the summer, so I thought maybe I should give it a shot. Once again I downloaded a Linux distro, Ubuntu, but this time I burned it and stuck it in. Let's see what happens.

My mind gets blown to pieces. I am actually running Ubuntu while installing. Look, I can surf for help. Hey, a calculator! Swap size = 1024 *1.5. Nifty.

Disaster. I'm unable to boot into Windows. A part of me has been maimed. Something with the RAID array perhaps. A week goes by reading forums and howto's. Reinstallation after reinstallation. Finally, I'm dual booting and everything seems to work. Feels good to succeed.
Let's see, I need to get my dual monitor working. More reading. Xorg.conf after xorg.conf, 4 days worth infact. But hey, finally it's working! Again, the sweet taste of hard earned success.

Two months have gone by. Windows still dual boots but it's mostly dusting in the corner. I feel Ubuntu fire at my fingertips. The transition from Photoshop to Gimp was easy after taking up a little personal art project. Inkscape, very cool. Gedit, gcc, valgrind, etc. Life couldn't be sweeter. Or could it? XGL/Compiz. It still amuses me, not matter how many times I've rotated the cube and wobbled my apps. A little incident with the xserver update. I get excited at the prospect of learning more as I get an xserver error and get booted into command line. The fix is too easy, only a couple simple commands. Maybe I should corrupt something else so I can fiddle some more. I just can't stop loving this.
