### Post by guest100 on 2010-11-21
Hello Newbies,

I'm new to Linux (Ubuntu), for about a week now, and just wanted to share my thoughts. Perhaps my comments can help other new users out. 


Here's my PRO list so far:

1. Ubuntu is absolutely Free :). This is by far was the most appealing aspect to me at first and still is.

2. Workspaces - The coolest thing EVER!  Right away I was blown away with the OS structure incorporating workspaces. What a fantastic tool to keep projects separated and the desktop uncluttered!  For you new guys like me, the workspaces are located on the bottom right. There are four of them. It's 4 separate desktops you can easily switch between.  You can also move any application to whatever workspace you like.  As I said... the coolest thing EVER! 

3. Applications are so easy to find and install. Naturally, there are many applications that are just a "must have" for every individual. For me, a good console program, snipping tool, and RAR extractor come to mind right away.  After trying a few console programs out, Yakuake fit the bill just right. Shutter is a great snipping tool and even expands over the Windows snipping tool as it incorporates sessions and I found it  super easy to work with.  I was a little disappointed when I tried to unarchive a RAR file and Ubuntu wouldn't do that with what's initially installed with the OS, but none the less, in about a minute I had an app installed that did the trick!

4.  Modifying network settings are very simple and plenty of apps to support.  This was very welcoming indeed!

5. Secure, Secure, Secure!  :-))

6. Built in torrent down loader. Living outside my home country, I frequently download TV shows in English. It was nice to find the "Transmission" downloader built into the OS.  No more BitTorrent or what not installing all kinds of spyware and such.

7. Absolutely NO video/audio codec issues.  From the get go, ALL video and audio worked absolutely perfect!!!

8.  Speed.  None of the overhead stuff that bogs down other OS's here! (Antivirus, etc..). Ubuntu is fast once I got all my hardware up and supported.


Here's my CON list so far:

1. Having to constantly hear about Windows vs. Linux on every thread.  Naturally to NON LINUX users, running a different OS will have it's difficulties. I can't help but be put off every time I searched a forum for help and had to read someone inject "the greatness of Linux" and how "Windows sucks", or how they've NEVER had any issues with Linux. This does absolutely nothing to help new users!!!  The truth is Microsoft is the worlds OS market leader, which means more people in the world use Microsoft OS products over other OS products (including Ubuntu). Please don't be surprised when users, who are used to Windows, compare what they are looking for to a Windows tool or application that they are familiar with.   
 
2.  The Ubuntu File Manager.  To new users:  there's an icon that says "Places", please click this and there will be a menu with other options available.  Picking "Tree" will make it more like Windows Explorer (the file manager). This aided me in moving around until I got use to Linux things a bit more.  I don't like that I can't right click and drag/drop to another folder with options.  You can left click and it will copy by doing a drag and drop, but you can't "move" something by drag and drop method.
 
3. "Ping" in the network area tools.  A great set of networking tools are included with Ubuntu in easily arranged tabs :).  I work with a lot of Cisco networking equipment and only wanted to do a ping.  No response from the Ping tab in the Networking tools, but I can ping the devices on my network from the command prompt?  Just an annoyance, but none the less I'd like the Ping to work in the network tools area too.

4. No Blackberry desktop application support. I know this has absolutely nothing to do with Linux, but this brings me to the "WINE" application.  Novel idea, but it is not that great. I have yet to run a windows app that works correctly in WINE. So a dual boot system is the best way to work if you absolutely need to use some Windows apps in my opinion.  Other than Blackberry desktop, I also use some trading platforms that are sadly only Windows based. 

5. MS office vs. Open Office.  No comparison here, MS Office has all the bells and whistles. Actually it was a nice fresh breath of air to have a really simple Office app at first, but sometimes those bells and whistles are nice to have on hand.  For example I wanted to highlight some text in another color other than the initial yellow and I couldn't find a way to do this? After about 30 seconds, I just gave up and had to go with yellow... but my point is I couldn't easily change something like a highlighting color (or don't even know if this is possible).

6. Hardware issues. For Windows users: don't get rid of Windows completely!  Ubuntu would not read one of my hard drives. I had to boot back into widows a few times and run disk utils until Ubuntu would even read the hard drive.  No faults were ever found on the drive but Windows read it just fine and Ubuntu couldn't at first. Eventually Ubuntu was able to read the drive, but the OS would hang if I clicked on the "place" of my hard drive which just drove me up the wall.  The only way I could close out Ubuntu was to power off my PC hard. Point being: Install a dual Boot system!


My experience so far with Ubuntu has been very positive, I really like it.  It does mostly what I need. The only thing from really keeping me 100% from Ubuntu are apps that I have to run under Windows. For those of you new, like me, I hope my rantings can help you out and I highly recommend Ubuntu!

Best,
Eric
