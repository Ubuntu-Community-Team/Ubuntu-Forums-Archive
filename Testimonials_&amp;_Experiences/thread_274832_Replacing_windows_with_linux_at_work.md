### Post by MadeR on 2006-10-10
Greetings! I apologize for my english, but it's not my native language (i'm italian and i speak esperanto better than english, no joke).
Let me introduce the scenario:
I work as a resolve-all-informatic-problems man in a national public office.
Recently, using Ubuntu and FreeBSD I have showed to my bosses and coworkers that Unix world, or at least a part of it, can be user friendly and can help reusing old PCs and exploiting more efficently the HW and infrastructures.

They were simply marvelled by the fact that while I am using a PC, 3 other users can use a program created for windows with wine using a remote X session...
(not to mention the Windows licence costs and irregularities :) )

So finally today some of the coworkers and bosses of other section of the office asked to me: «We are interested in using Linux, but what "professional programs" exists?».

So I'm asking your help to create a list of "professional" programs.
What's a professional program by their words? a program who tops the bill in his category and can read/save (when applyable) windows formats, if possible with Drag and Drop support.

We already identified Openoffice, firefox, blender, gimp, audacity and scribus as champions of their categories.

But what about scilab? or multimedia format player? image slider (like xnview/acdsee) and so on?

thank you!


Hot parts are multimedia (they need to play also encrypted DVD), plugin integration in firefox, pdf handling (they have acrobat to modify pdf), copying easily from/to floppies, copying from/to shared folders.
