### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-11-14
To answer your question:

XGL is not currently included in Ubuntu. What is included is AIGLX, which is a competing technology.

In order to get special effects, you need a window manager to work on top of AIGLX. The most common special effects window manager is Beryl. Have a look around the forums for instructions on how to install Beryl, and then your windows will be able to wobble with the rest of 'em! (note: You might want to wait until you're more familiar with Ubuntu before trying Beryl, it's reportedly a bit tricky to get going since it's in heavy development).
