### Post by Sturmeh on 2007-10-06
I'm done looking through windows, and i've moved onto walking through doors.

Sure there's alternatives to "getting through the wall" ( Metaphorically the barrier between you and a computer that does nothing. )

I've been staring into it, never really being able to control it, hardly anything new or innovative except for the occasional update this, update that.

Moving to ubuntu is the best thing I've done in years, make away with having to put CD's in, the last CD/DVD I put in this computer was the Ubuntu installation disc.

( Games are an exception, I dual-boot windows, and store cd images on a network. )

Too many people take Piracy for granted, they use Windows, not aware that they are probably stealing dollars per second. This is not always the case, but a lot of people tend to pirate music, games. Sure linux doesn't help with the music or the games, but it does provide a plethora of Free and Open software.

At first, ubuntu was a little daunting, this doesn't work, that doesn't work, but with a little guidance, and the massive support found here on this forum, everything ends up working better than ever.

"I can only show you the door, it is you that has to walk through it."
