### Post by s0563764 on 2010-09-18
First off I have been a Windows user all my life basically, apart from using Macs in school. I had never considered Linux until I was going to build a pc mum, which I bailed from due to the amount of warnings from tutorials to check compatibility etc.

But I ended up converting after I gave up salvaging the XP of my old PC. In all honesty I am impressed by the performance compared to PRE Windows 7 PCs.]

What has thrown me is:

how to installs for linux on websites

Arranging folders on the desktop

Various Wine and windows issues

Use of commands e.g. joining files

But what is impressive, is even WITHOUT installing other players etc, many types of media can be played,

It can read cache files from chrome it seems

It invites personalization without installing much more stuff

speed at booting, and mounting drives. In fact it has allowed me to retrieve data from a broke HDD much more easily and faster than on Vista

In fact I have been so impressed by Ubuntu I have installed wubi on all the computers I have, apart from the one I installed cleanly with ubuntu. However I am still very reluctant to wipe windows, but ubuntu (bar a few programs I've downloaded) has been incredible to use, and interesting to learn.

### Post by juil on 2010-09-18
Wubi is an awesome way to try ubuntu and just delete it if you don't want it anymore. But Wubi actually slows down the performance of ubuntu from what I heard. The difference may not be that noticeable with recent PC's but on older PC's with 1GB or less RAM, the performance will lag a bit. I prefer the traditional way of dual-booting.

Don't hesitate to post in a support forum for these. People are very helpful. A word of warning about Linux howtos on the wider internet. Many of these are produced for the best of intentions but are, too often, a - how shall I put this diplomatically? - triumph of enthusiasm over ability. Be very wary. You need to spot the ones that are giving you poor advice. Trouble is, you need to be experienced to recognise these and the inexperienced are the ones who need  them.

If you are using Ubuntu, look at the community documentation and wiki before relying on some howto you picked up from a website somewhere:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/)

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Home?action=show&redirect=FrontPage](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Home?action=show&redirect=FrontPage)

Use the search box in the top right. There are hundreds of excellent howtos and guides there, particularly the community site.
