May I make a suggestion?

In the future when testing any alpha or beta software, do so on a separate drive. Maintain your functional version on your primary and use a secondary drive for testing. This assures that any new software will be compatible with your system or can easily be removed should it result in failure.

### Post by manzdagratiano on 2010-04-03
> **VinceG3 said:**
> I've been using and supporting Linux at home for a few years and at work only recently, so I thought I'd give back and beta test 10.04. Ran "update-manager -d" let it run all night.

The most important lesson that all Free Software users should learn, and that I learned right away after I upgraded my Jaunty to Karmic one day before the final release - do not screw with your existing system merely to test a new one. Fire up virtualbox, and test the new stuff there until you are sure it will go fine with your system. If an upgrade does not work right in spite of that, it just means you had better do a clean install. The reason is that since the system is still at testing stage and beta testing is but a voluntary process, there are inevitably going to be unforeseen errors, and it does take some time to remove them. The blame rests with no one, just that a small measure of caution must be exercised before one relinquishes the firm ground they are on. Remember - if you take the risk, know that there is a finite probability that there could be consequences, and prepare for it beforehand.

I have been spurned by installing Karmic when it was still in the process of maturing, but I eventually did switch over from Jaunty to Karmic and I am glad I did - one compelling reason being that I can play 1080p files right out of the box, unlike in Jaunty. Newer is always going to be better in the sum total, even if there are a few initial hiccups. After all it IS the 'latest and the greatest'. And I am not going to stop beta testing either.
