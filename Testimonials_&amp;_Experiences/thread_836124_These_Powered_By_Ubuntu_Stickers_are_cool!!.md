### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-06-21
> **nickdbliss said:**

I don't know if you were joking or not, but by doing this you are supporting "free as in beer", not "free as in freedom". Both stickers (System76's and Linuxmall's) are not "free as in freedom" or "open source", because the image file (pdf, svg or whatever) used for the printing is not available to the public for modifications and sharing.

The concept of freedom has nothing to do with money. The GPL itself imposes no restrictions on charging money for your product, as long as you supply the source code and allow modifications and sharing when you sell it.

System76 initiative of giving free stickers is great and I applaud them for that, but not charging for the stickers does not make them more "free" or "open source" than Linuxmall. Linuxmall is a very good store that supports Linux since before Ubuntu even existed. They make beautiful products which I'm more than happy to pay for.

Even if you were just joking and know all that I said above, I felt it was important to mention for anyone who doesn't know.
