### Post by Rodemire on 2010-07-08
I have to say the Forums have become this place where people with problems come and look for help, (which is good, don't get me wrong), help which they usually get.Unfortunately not all people get the help they need, which is to be expected, but from what I have seen from the threads they post, it still does't disheart the thread-starters, it only fuels them to find a solution and when they do, they don't hesitate to post it on the internet to help others who might be in similar situations. That is Ubuntu in its true sense.  
 

 There are of course, some, who use the forums to vent their frustrations at the lack of assistance they receive, it too is expected.
 

 Anyway, I just thought I could air my "not so negative" opinions on how Linux and Ubuntu, to be specific, made my computing life as great as it is now.  
 

 In my line of work I deal with a lot media, purchased, received by way of gifts and a lot of self produced videos, songs and pictures such that over the years I had amassed vast amounts of media. And before i started using Ubuntu i had great difficulties in managing it all as I always had issues.  
 

 Firstly and more obviously I had a bad virus problem, this is almost non-existant for me now. And ever since i started with Ubuntu a lot of my issues were solved or relieved, for example, the problem of renaming my thousands of pictures and songs was made easy by Purrr and Metamophose, my privacy issues (some of the content i have is for my eyes only, lol) were easilly dealt with by ecrypt-utils and Cryptkeeper, I can now easily allow access to my partitions and make sure that none of my media is renamed or deleted through "chmod", my desktop looks superb with the Compiz cube and Conky letting me know what's under my hood. I mean, Ubuntu just seems to have an answer for almost all of my otherwise unique concerns.
 

 Ubuntu almost has an application for everyone and what strikes me the most about it is, the OS itself is for free, (the App as as well) and the people who create and maintain some of the apps are other people just like me out there who saw a niche they could help in and they just went and did it, for the Ubuntu community's benefit. I admit I have occasional problems here and there that need me to lose sleep one or two nights but after "Googling" the solution or creating a thread here, I always appreciate the solution and the help of this forum and those who participate in it.
 

 I'd like to give the Ubuntu distro all my thumbs up, the Ubuntu community, the maintainers, other Ubuntu related online resources, Full circle magazine, Ubuntu-Geek and basically everyone who makes the Ubuntu user's experience more enjoyable. Oh, and Mark Shuttleworth. :p
