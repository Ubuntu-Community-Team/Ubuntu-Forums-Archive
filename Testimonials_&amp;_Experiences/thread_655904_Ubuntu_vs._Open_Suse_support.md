### Post by wulfhound on 2008-01-01
I wasn't sure where to put this so please, feel free to move it to wherever it needs to go. When I started using linux, I started with XFCE and Xubuntu. I soon outgrew that, and switched to Ubuntu. Unfortunately, Ubuntu was also too buggy for me (perhaps due to my reliance on 3rd party applications, rather than the set standard). I tried Kubuntu but it did not like my monitor or something LOL. So, I ended up running Ubuntu in the background, keeping it installed on the computer, but use Suse 10.2 for my every day needs. 

Here is the thing I have noticed, and is what makes Ubuntu the real winner: support. This forum, the people who use Ubuntu, genuinely want to help others, and yes, a lot of posts do go unanswered - but that is on all forums. When people do get answers, they get REAL answers. Spend any amount of time on a SUSE help forum and you'll get answers to common, everyday problems that go like this: X happened, how can I fix it? Answer: reinstall Suse. Now, yes, some problems will require a reinstall, but it seems to be bandied about frequently at the SUSE forums.....and so, I tip my hat to you, Ubuntu people......you are what linux needs, to be pushed forth into the world.....and used by more people, whether it be a learning point, a stepping stone, or their system for life.....

Thank you Ubuntu.

Sandaili
