### Post by BoyOfDestiny on 2005-11-09
All I can say is I am impressed. A little tweaking here and there for extra mouse buttons, 3d accel [radeon], and monitor refresh rate. Disabling ESD, let the audigy2 handle everything alsa (and for oss the wrapper), 5.1 worked out of the box (just a matter of raising the volume of the front/rear/center etc)

I decided to go 64-bit and no more dual boot. So I got another drive, formatted ntfs, copied my data...

When in breezy using the awesome disk managment tool, mounted everything, started moving data around (tower of hannoi style) then formatting one drive, copying the data around, and again for the 2nd...

Not a single crash, breezy took it in stride!

Pretty much everything works great, firefox with auto scroll, back/forward buttons. The usual (for me) privoxy, thunderbird, dosbox, gtetrinet, sopwith, etc work as well if not better than their 32-bit counterparts...

Able to compile uade for beep...

Few things left to do, make zsnes compile one day (on 64-bit), get mednafen working (using fceu from repos in the meantime). Advmame .99 built nicely.

Do not fear trying 64-bit!

The next things on the list are java and azureus (use bittornado in the mean time)

Overall very very impressed!
Thank you for letting me dump windows completely
