### Post by darkghost on 2005-10-23
Ok, I installed some days ago kubuntu on my laptop.
It's a Sony Vaio PCG-GRX416G coupled with a D-Link DWL-650+ Airplus wifi PCMCIA card (ok I did it :-)

I'm experiencing on laptop because I plan to totally switch to Linux (from Win, I tried Mac but apart from eyecandy I couldn't find much in it for my use).
Experience : easy installation, it recognized my Dlink on the fly and configured just right. Suse 10 (the open versione)couldn't do it. Sound / 3D even the memory stick reader...all good.
I tried Fedora Core 2 one year ago or so, and I'm impressed by the progress of linux. At the time I had the same idea....switch, but couldn't really do it 'cause I couldn't find all I needed.

What do I use the PC for ? I work on my own, and my job is a mix of management and reaserch. I do manage the (micro) family business, and some reaserch in the financial field. Plus all the usual personal stuff : email, browsing, digital camera pictures collection, watching DVD, divx , chatting, VOIP etc.
I don't do games. I used to but no more time. Thought I plan to buy the next playstation :-)

So where is my point....? 
I find Kubuntu very good BUT: normal average users need flashplayer, java, mplayer for reading DVDs, etc.
It's all the little extras that are not installed because of licence problems.
No more packages in the multiverse, you are on your own. I know this community exists...but the average user (the one who want to turn it on and simply insert the dvd and watch) could easily get frustrated.

I was thinking about something people I know usually do...home banking. Many of them couldn'd check their accounts with firefox + java. Some banks still code ONLY for IE. So you still have little glitches here and there. little buttons, big buttons, bad layout, etc. Especially for trading on line platforms. Why go to the bank for investing little money in a stock/bond when you can do it from home ?

Many platforms simply aren't supposed to work in linux. I'm lucky and the platform of my bank is in java. It's the only one in Italy....

Mplayer....libdvdcss.....not for the average guy who just see the pc as an evolution of the tv set.

Why not to move in the direction of more integration a little further ?
For instance making a browser who could not be recognized as non IE by servers ? SO you can just fool them to think you are a ms user ?
I know mozilla could do it, and even konqueror, but not 100% bulletproof on every possible situation, website.
Why not to make an easy to install (basically push a button and you get all the multimedia stuff in one click perfectly running ?)

Sometimes ago I was thinking to make a proposal to the bank...as many users had bad problems with phishing, viruses etc. . Let's see if a linux distro could be done so that it may cost a little money and be used without security problems by the customers....maybe offered when you open an account. Bank could personalize it and in change devolve some money to open source community for each copy given to customers...

Do you think this would be possible ?

Buy, I get back to try to install mplayer and all the codecs :-D
