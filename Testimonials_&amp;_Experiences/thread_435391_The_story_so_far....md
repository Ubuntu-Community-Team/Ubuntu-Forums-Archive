### Post by ephonk on 2007-05-06
Applause, applause...

Big kudos to any and everyone involved with Fiesty.  It's the nicest distro I've seen yet.  I'm spending WAY more time in it that in any previous distro and am seriously thinking about how I could ditch XP entirely.  I'd tried a few before, SuSE, Mandriva & Fedora, and was impressed in various ways by all.  Always went back to XP tho, mostly on usability issues.  This time, maybe not.  Got my nVidia driver working well and am actually viewing this on a 50" tv at 1280x720!  Rythmbox recognized the iPod easily enough.  I even have something running reasonably well in Wine.  I think I've done pretty well...

Still have some glitches to work out:
- My Diamond Xtreme Sound 7.1DDL card doesn't deliver the surround channels to the SPDIF out under Ubuntu.  I have a hunch this will never work.
- I can't locate a checkbook manager I like - Gnucash and Moneydance were both very frustrating.  MS Money 2001 is annoying too, but it's an annoyance I am used to.  I'm trying to get Money to work under Wine right now, but it's slow going.  The Wine App DB is helping so I'll crack it eventually.  Either that'll start working or I'll stumble across another package I like. 
- The other thing I have running (sort of) under Wine is Home Architect - it's usable but has a couple of rendering issues.  Wish I could find a nice Linux home design package that did 3D walkthrus and doesn't require me to define individual 2x4's.  There is a lot of nice CAD software, but I haven't found any tailored to home remodellers.
- Still haven't gotten the fstab to auto-mount my ntfs partitions as rw for all users.  I'll probably stumble across the answer to that one pretty soon too.

So yeah, Feisty is sweet!  I am impressed...
