### Post by blockcipher on 2007-02-28
Thought I would post my experience moving from Windows XP to using Ubuntu primarily. For my home machine we have very little demands when it comes what we use. At home we use the following:

1. Internet: Mostly just searching and the kids play online games. So we needed Flash.
2. E-mail: Using email to send to friends and family is a must for anyone really :)
3. Work E-mail: At work we use Exchange server. I would have like to use an "outlook" client on Ubuntu if I could.
4. Pictures: We take a lot of pictures. Being able to plug the cam in and grab the photos is a must.
5. iPod/Music: My wife has an iPod nano I bought her for her B-Day one year. Being able to use the iPod and transfer songs to it.
6. DVD Playback: Sometimes the boys want to watch a DVD on the laptop.
7. Word Processing: We need to create and open documents from family members, and sometimes docs people send me for my side business.

So as you can see its not like I need to use Windows to do the daily tasks we do at home. So now that you have a background of what we need I will let you know how the install and everything went.

I backed up all our music, docs, and pictures to DVD. Once I knew I had all of our data I inserted the fresh Ubuntu burn into my XP machine. The install was very easy to use and go through, nothing a person couldn't really understand. I chose to wipe my hard disk and install primarily linux. Wasn't long when I was at the login screen for Ubuntu. First thing I saw was my resolution was kinda of whacked out. I have a DELL 700m, with a 12inch wide screen. So I had a feeling it wasn't going to work out perfectly at the beginning.

For the screen resolution all I had to do is download and configure 915resolution, I was able to then configure my specs for the resolution I wanted. Now to the tasks we do at home.

1. Internet: Even on XP we used FireFox, so everything we used on the web that worked in XP w/ FF works fine in Linux so no problems there. I let FF install flash player for me.

2. Email: We use gmail, easy enough.

3. Now for work email that uses Exchange. It took me about 2 minutes to setup Evolution to sync my Exchange email via OWA. This was a plus for me and I am glad I was able to utilize this via linux.

4. I was able to put in my DVD and copy over all our photos to my /home/mikee/photos folder with no problems. I was able to pull them up and view them without an issue. I used Picasa heavily with our photos. And I was glad to see I was still able to use it: [http://picasa.google.com/linux/thanks-deb.html](http://picasa.google.com/linux/thanks-deb.html). The only feature that is missing which they are going to add is the quick Web upload to post pictures to your "Picasa Web Account". I can live without that and upload manually for now. The only thing I haven't tested as of yet, which I will tonight is hook up my cam /w USB and see if I can easily pull those off without hacking.

5. iPod/Music: I plugged in my iPod and showed up instantly on my desktop. I was able to open it and browse the contents. Now for a application to move files between the computer and the iPod I am using Amarok [http://amarok.kde.org/](http://amarok.kde.org/). In the application you can easliy hook up and browse your iPod. So this worked great! As for my old music in iTunes I will have to burn them to CD then rip them into mp3s. DRM should die.

6. DVD Playback was a bit tricky. I was able to use it my default I had to do the following in order to get it working properly.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/6.10/ubuntu/...dplayback.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/6.10/ubuntu/...dplayback.html)

I primarily use VLC that worked better. I tried a couple other apps and the video/audio was out of sync, VLC is the way to go.

7. Word Processing: Open Office is awesome, it took care of everything I needed/wanted to do with word processing and opening up MS word documents. I was able to even open Word Perfect Doc with no extra configuration.


So far I am extremely happy with Ubuntu and recommend it to anyone who uses it for the same reasons I do.

PS: I wanted to go Mac. But I wanted to not use Windows but also keep my existing hardware. That was the reason for my decision.
