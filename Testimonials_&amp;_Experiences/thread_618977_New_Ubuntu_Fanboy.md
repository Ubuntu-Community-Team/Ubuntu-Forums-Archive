### Post by slappstah on 2007-11-21
Well, it finally happened.

My wife left me alone for the weekend as she went out of town to visit family.  One night that weekend I begrudgingly turned on her Athlon desktop and ... waited ... and waited... for WinXP to start up.  Then, I logged in, and the virus scanner turned on, and demanded a full system scan.  I started firefox, and waited a good 30 seconds before it opened up.

When Windows shut down after installing updates without warning me, that was the last straw.  Wifey was out of town, and I snapped.  I wiped the box (after making some backups) and shazam, I installed Ubuntu 7.10.

After the adrenaline wore off, I realized that I might be in a world of hurt when wifey got home and realized that she couldn't use her favorite apps, or print to our GDI printer.  I was pleasantly surprised to realize that the following items worked, after some tinkering:

* - Rhapsody (through FF plugin)
* - Okidata B4100 Printer (via community-provided PPD)

I also really like how installing restricted drivers is now streamlined, that really helps the simple-minded hacks like me get things working for Broadcom wireless cards and such.

Overall, it is great to have a responsive computer again.  FF now starts up in about 5 seconds as opposed to 30 sec under XP.

The wifey gets in tomorrow, let's see if she likes the Gutsy Gibbon as much as I do.  Much love to the Ubuntu organization and the community that helps make it available to the little people.

### Post by erlyrisa on 2007-11-21
Some tips:

I have to do the same thing --try and convince others about how much better this is.

So what do I do...

First: 
Have saved sesions enabled -> people are so suprised when they boot thier computer agian to see what they did last time. --> trying to teach them to NOT close all the applications is the hard part.

Get the Exteneded UI for tweaking Compix XGL (CompizConfig)... and...

Setup it up so that when you move the mouse to the bottom of the screen the shift switcher or other 'alt-tab' functionality comes up --you should see my mum on ubuntu.

And setup the top of the screen for something else - I have it set to Expo, so that I can organise all the desktops for the family. -thye don't really use it, because I have setup the sides of the screen for rolling onto the next desktop. -oh and I switched off the mouse wheel for dektop switches --it not only confused them, but once they got it, all they did was play with it.


The only option I couldn't find in CopizConfig was a way to chagne the defualt button for the show desktop feature. - it would be great to assign it to a mouse roll on the desktop, with each increment/decrement "pushing" the windows off the desktop.
