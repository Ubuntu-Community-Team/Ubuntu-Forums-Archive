### Post by Monstroxus on 2009-04-29
Hi all,

Sinds 2006 I was running Ubuntu (gnome), and the last couple of months I was running openSUSE 11 (gnome). Now that xubuntu 9.04 came out, I thought "lets give that a try". So I made a backup of my files on a thumbdrive. Burned the iso on cd, boot from the cd, and continued the very easy and fast install... during the main install I took a shower, and after the shower the install was done perfectly. I just completely replaced openSUSE 11 which is a good looking distro, but has annoying issues, such as a very slow add/remove program... and it needed some fiddling to get wireless working etc. Also updates and servers are very slow with openSUSE. (here in Asia) A pity because it looks really good though.

Anyway, so the install was done, (I installed xubuntu on my HP2133 mininote)... wireless working, check, sound working check... ok... add/remove... install xubuntu restricted codecs... done. Test youtube, check... everything checked out perfectly right away. As the HP2133 has a very shitty processor and even shittier graphics... compiz fusion doesn't work. Actually I don't mind because the XFCE compositor works flawlessly. Looks very good, classy. Anyway, needless to say I am very happy... and going to stick with xubuntu from now on.
