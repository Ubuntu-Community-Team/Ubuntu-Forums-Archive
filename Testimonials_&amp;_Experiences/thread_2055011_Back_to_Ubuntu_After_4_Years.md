### Post by djross95 on 2012-09-08
Just a quick post to say "well done" to the Ubuntu developers for their work on 12.04.  I'm generally a Mac user and had played around with 2 or 3 versions of Ubuntu several years ago.  I hadn't used it much since, but I was thinking of giving it a go once again.  I had heard such negative feedback on Unity, though, that I was almost afraid to give Precise a try.  Fortunately a Lifehacker article on how to dual boot Ubuntu on a Mac got me over the hump.

And you know what?  I love it!  The interface, while it has a few quirks, is beautifully designed (dare I say it, almost "Mac-like"!).  Finally running Ubuntu natively (as opposed to in a VM) is a much better experience.  I really like what been done to Ubuntu in the years since I've tried it, and it wouldn't be hard to use Ubuntu as one's primary OS..

Thanks for all of your hard work, and I look forward to posting from time to time!     DR

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-09-11
With the variations of Ubuntu, I believe Canonical tries to support the needs of it's users. For example, those who don't care for Unity or the new releases of Ubuntu, there's alternatives such as [Xubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/") or [Lubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/"). Both of which perform admirably our systems, Xubuntu is extremely stable and has become our OS of choice when 10.04 reaches it's EOL. IMHO this is one of the true benefits of Ubuntu/Linux - CHOICE.

All one can hope for, is that Canonical continues to support these choices long into the future.
