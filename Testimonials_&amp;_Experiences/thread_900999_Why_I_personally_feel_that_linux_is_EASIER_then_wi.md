### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2008-08-26
Semi testimonial I guess.
In my recent days I have been getting more people interested in using linux as opposed to windows and I have seen a surprising trend.
Giving out copies of Linux Mint and Ubuntu to my friends has always been an interesting experience but now I am noticing I am getting less and less calls about support and more compliments then complaints.
The reason?
Linux

Take my husband for example, he has used windows for years and day after day one isssue or another popped up for him under windows.
Antivirus, anti spyware, anti malware, defragging, the constant checkups he made to his system was beginning to way on him.
But after linux mint, his concerns are not nearly as high as are his problems, sure small hiccups here and there but overall he has enjoyed Mint a million times more then Windows XP.
The only trouble zones I see with him is wireless (though he understands why) and the occasional disk check.

Then there is my friend Rob, he too long time windows user... so many issues with XP and vista, no issues with linux.
He has Hardy and he says its the best system he has ever used for his computer.

Jenny my other friend also a windows user for a long time, but now is in love with Mint.

David another friend is obsessed with Mepis 7, he has been using it for some time now with very little problems.

I have converted at least 3 others to linux but they are newer, but so far no complaints! :D

### Post by bmac on 2008-08-26
Most individuals I've converted to Ubuntu (>15) lacked awareness. They honestly didn't know that a free user friendly OS was even available. After providing them with an opportunity to explore Ubuntu on my system - the majority reacted very positively to the experience. I truly believe that lack of awareness limits Ubuntu's ability to expand it's market share. Many Windows users I converted implied that they would have investigated Ubuntu, had they know it was a free user friendly OS option. Especially, the users that had no experience outside of Windows.
One might question why Ubuntu doesn't actively market to these individuals? Target audiences such as uninformed Windows users and baby boomers don't appear to be a priority. I believe this is a mistake if Ubuntu really wants to become a mainstream competitor and increase it's market share.

Just a thought......

### Post by michaelbogardus on 2008-08-26
I find that people who have very little experience with computers, or technology in general, are suited to an OS like Linux, in particular Ubuntu, because of its simplicity.
