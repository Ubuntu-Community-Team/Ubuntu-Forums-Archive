### Post by Free Me on 2009-08-04
Hi people. I do have a couple of issues with Ubuntu but before I start moaning I'd better say that I like it and I run it on both my tower and my laptop. 

I've been messing around with _complicators _(Not a type'o) since 1982 but have finally decided to save myself all the hard work of programing and leave it to people who are better at it than me.

I don't hanker after glitzy finishes but use the software as it comes, so I can't comment on any of the bells and whistles.

With Ubuntu, I can surf the net without having to run anti virus and registry repair software after each session, a point which most people overlook when they have MS XP or whatever and depending on how much you have on the hard drive(s) this can take hours. I use Buzzsaw to ovecome defag problems on XP! I keep XP on a separate HD solely to use my HP 3770 flat bed scanner which is notoriously bad tempered in the software department.

After having read numerous posts on here I have to agree that the hardest part is getting to grips with downloading, installing packages and then getting them to work: However with a little help from our friends... 

The last word has to be that when grumbling about something as good as _free software_, don't forget that MS Windows, which ever varient you use, is also subject to upgrades, patches and fixes galore and after years of meddling is still not FREE of PAYMENT, virises, hackers and adverts, time checks, nags, registration, and ultimately having to pay again if you reinstall too many times or upgrade your hardware!!! 

Keep up the good work and kick Tyrants like Microsoft into touch.
