### Post by Istrebitel on 2010-06-10
After having my first try at migrating and picking kubuntu for the eyecandyish look, i had numerous problems and were unable to go on.

After writing a detailed "testemonial and experience here" [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1503686](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1503686) i was offered to try ubuntu or linux mint, which (former) i did. So here it goes.

My experiences of Ubuntu are mixed. They are much better than those of Kubuntu but still, i am not statisfied with what i get, cant tell that, unfortunately... Before people start thinking bad of me, i dont expect anything of this being fixed asap nor i assume consumer-seller model. I just hope that maybe if some of my "rants" are listened to and probably problems are fixed, ubuntu will become more user friendly, more popular, which is good for all of us. Also engish isnt my first language nor did i study it professionally, so my choice of words might be bad or harsh. I apologise.

Ubuntu vs Kubuntu:
+ Much simpier menus. You just find what you need by looking at the menu. You can set up most of your system gui-shly.
+ Much better icons. They are more intuitive and easier to recognise.
+ I can mount ntfs drives at least through Disk Manager without passowrd prompt
+ Network manager actually remembers network settings! Yay!
+ I got much more options at tweaking my audio card, which finally made its surround kinda work (kinda, but WORK!)
. The initial setup of one panel on top, one on bottom wasnt very good. I do like having panel with everything at top, and panel with links to programs at bottom... anyway, easilly set up
- Ubuntu is ugly.
The abilities to tweak inteface (window decorations and color schemes) suck compare to Kubuntu. They really do. 
In Kubuntu you download and install themes on the fly right from the theming manager. You get much more freedom in the themes choice, and much more effects at your disposal to make a unique look. You can change interface completely and beyond that. And you get it all without visiting any sites, just scroll, install and apply. 
Every Ubuntu theme is the SAME! Just plain old SAME. And very confusing ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1505911](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1505911)) and buggy (same link). You cant make Ubuntu look nice without switching to Emerald, and that doesnt work since its no longer supported (or so i read in the webs...)
- There is still no built in way to have my ntfs disks automounted and running. Trying to tweak /etc/fstab rises all those nightmare errors again (external fuse, no root access...) and breaks Disk Manager. 
Without touching /etc/fstab i can use Disk Manager which is confusing (how come can it do w/o passowrd what i cannot, but then it spits errors "only root can do this" which means it isnt having root access rights, so how did it access mount and mkdir in the first place?). And Disk Manager does not offer automount.

Ubuntu vs Windows 7:
+ Its free. 
+ Its faster. Definetly. Boot time, working time, lagging time
+ Package manager way of acquiring software is superior to visiting 1000 sites watching 10000 pr0n ads just to dl a trial with a virus
+ Linux, after you learn it, becomes easier to understand than windows, its built around simple ideology (everything is a file) that really helps against windows registry,logs,preformance counters, policies, scattered all around
+ You have more control if your pc is being unstable or just more control over your pc. Being able to ctrl+alt+f1 and kill the process that hanged your gui is nice. Being able to delete something you want deleted is nice ("NO PERMISSION! SYSTEM FILE! ACCESS DENIED! BAM!..." ffs THIS IS MY COMPUTER NOT YOURS, bill gates...). Being able to destroy your system in one command is nice (practical joke? ;)
+ The filesystem with its mounting, linking is very nice. I never imagined that what i do with paid software on windows is done as simple as dd if=/dev/cdrom of=image.iso - a BUILT IN linux command. Daemon tools? Buy full version? mount lol! Windows is so owned in this case
+ The "Gui is a frontend to console" is very good approach and it pays off. It is easier to copypaste a text into console and run it than follow gui instructions.
+ The desktop, the cube, the option to have multiple desktops, this is all very cool and is very usefull. Works out of the box!
+ Preinstalled software rocks.
. Installation is as slow as it is buggy. 
Windows installation gives you choices you cannot do ("Wanna upgrade, keeping your settings? remove drive and reboot, you cant do it from here! You just did and wanna upgrade keeping your settings? You cant, because ultimate cannot be upgraded to corporate!..." Now why did it show me the option in the first place if i cant?)
Ubuntu Linux installation for an odd reason spits errors (it says there was error during installation, so try to install from livecd mode... what does it mean? I dunno, but it works afterwards, and i didnt get that error in kubuntu). And its not offering enough choices except "advanced (manual partitioning)" - i would say that an option to partition your drive home-root-swap should be added, and an option to choose which drive to use when doing the "install side by side option" would be welcome, right now its choosing for you...
. Hardware support. Really, having hardware developers that dont support linux at all isnt enough? Do we have to read agitation texts about "this driver is prorpietary! Bad bad nvidia wont let us good good africans from ubuntu tweak it and bugfix it and make a torch out of it to hunt lamas! Africa is starving, support us, dont install the driver!" really? Until there is an alternative to nvidia proprietray driver (alternative that lets you use your gaming video card for what you use your gaming video card for, not for simpe graphics that a 10 year old one would do), i just dont have a clue why ever prompt one to install them. 
Well then, in Win7 you still have to manually dl drivers and install them, this means visit 1000 sites, watch 10000 pr0n ads, dl a virus and reboot your pc 4 times so... Cant say Windows beats Ubuntu here.
- Interface. Ubuntu is ugly. You can tweak it with compiz, but every theme is the same old GTK (i presume) just another color. Not much transparency (wtb this gui [http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VFqj2hS_0iQ](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VFqj2hS_0iQ)). Themes are installed by hand and are bugged (dont appear in list, have to 'customize' another theme). Even win7 has a built in theme downloader now. I mean, they install from website seamlessly. And theyre easy to switch. Yet i must say, i cant make things beam up and burn with Win 7. Still, so far i'd say that Win 7 interface is not worse than that i have with Ubuntu, probably better. I suppose this can be fixed but... have no clue how! Especially with all that confusion with interface switching.
- Drives. Automount. You dont have to mount anything, or enable automount by googling, posting on forums and still not having an answer. Really, 99.99% desktop users expect their flash to be accessable as soon as they plug it in. AS well as they expect every hard drive be available for access as soon as its discovered by the system. Manual mounting is cool, but automount option out of the box should exist as it will ease the work for most of users, really!
- Wont' mention that windows has no problem with hardware drivers. Not ubuntu fault. I hope that's coming to an end.

And things about Ubuntu i just dont like in general:
- Ubuntu in general defies linux standarts by introducing, what i see, unneeded changes in ideology. This makes earning Ubuntu harder tenfold!
- Why on earth wont they make their setup applications follow "gui is a frontend to command line" philosophy? 
Why do we have a "Disk Manager" that works in a miraculously hidden magical way, and starts working when i try to edit /etc/fstab, a half a century proven way to make linux automatically mount something? Why do we have utilities that we are not told where their config files are stored (if you read man mount you learn of fstab. if you read man ifconfig you learn of network file. if you read about disk manager you just learn when it was done and thats ALL!!??!)
- Why do we have same with networking manager that doesnt use networking file but its own, hidden somewhere?
- Why do we have init scripts (autorun files) sticked everytwhere and wherenot, instead a common place for them? I read outdated guides about linux and it was so simple there with init and init.d, now you have to just look all over the hard drive and ask,ask,ask forums....There isnt even a full list of whats running when you boot ubuntu on the ubuntu website! Its "work in progress"... Even ubuntu developers have no clue what their system is running when it boots?
- Why do we have the system running at runlevel 2 isntead of common 3/5? Whats the point of this?
- Why do we have ntfs-3g driver with external fuse instead of default one, with internal fuse? WHY? This makes /etc/fstab useless as if i do a user entry, user still cannot mount this entry!
- I heard arguments for disabling root account. I still disagree.  This is unneeded, confusing and serves absolutely no purpose. Sudo still gives you same privilleges to destroy your system. Why break something that works for ages? It worked for ages, it would work further! Really... People working from root? Well, just dont let them? never show root at login unless user reads how to enable. But user still should be able to login as root in terminal and work as root if he su's himself.

So far, Ubuntu experience was much better than that of Kubuntu. And much less sacrefcies to be done (so far i'm only missing automount and eyecandy, everything else can be beared with...) to use it. Ubuntu definety looks like something you can work, relax, watch movies, listen to music and game on. And live with. 

But Ubuntu experience is very confusing. There is just a horde of guides, hints and howtos online that DO NOT work. And no explanation why. Maybe marking version of ubuntu the guide is for would help?

By the way, did someone ever make a poll what people mostly do after they install ubuntu? I mean, alot of my discomfort with Ubuntu comes from that i have to do something that should have aready been done. Or something i cant use my pc without (setting up an eyecandy theme, installing graphics drivers, turning on 5.1, installing codecs...) Maybe that would mutiply system user-friendliness tenfold? I mean, if guides would be written about "first steps after install"? I suppose that would do. Because so far it looks like:

I installed Ubuntu.
1) Okay, i dont like panel layout. I want windows-like.
I spend 30 minutes finding out WHERE should i click to move panel, to merge something, to remove something, to resize something in panels
2) Okay, i wanna play dvds and listen to mp3s. I cant. I start googling. Finding "Get this" or "get that package" as results. Trying. Still not working. Trying more. Find outdated guide, try more. etc etc. Spend 3 hours instead of using one "ubuntu-restricted-extras" package... because i didnt knew and couldnt know it was there
3) Okay, i wanna tweak my interface. I've seen those neat gui's on the webs. Nobody expains how they get it. 
I start googling. Googling more. Find out confusing articles and statements about something like compiz, beryl, emerald, metacity... Start installing themes - they stop appearing in the ist. I enable compiz - but i cant install a compiz theme, wtf? I enable emerald - it isnt supported/doesnt work? No comprehensive guide how to switch around gnome, compiz, emerald and mecatcity. Only one seriously good guide is that girl on youtube, doesnt cover installing emerald though...
4) Okay, now i want my ntfs drives mount automatically, i dont want to open settings-drive manager and do it manually every boot and every time i plug it in. 
I cant. Google, forums, different options only suggest guis to fstab. Tweaking fstab breaks disk manager. More google, waiting for forum reply....
5) Maybe i want to share files with windows users? how do i do it? Google again for outdated guides?

And so on. I dont thinkg that i am a minority who does those. Yet you dont get guided through those possibilities. And while you start to google/search, you stumble upon OUTDATED gudies that either dont work, or confuse you, or break your system totally! 

A getting started guide with those listed would be very appreciated i think. I think that if people would write what they do and how they do it exacty when they install their new Ubuntu, this would provide enough material to create such a guide. Guide centered not on "after you read 100 pages you will know how to setup basics of the system". This is not a user approach, but an administrator approach. The "ubuntu-manual.org" only covers very basics. It lets you use what you have, it doesnt fit those that want Ubuntu to preform something more than it has by default (i.e. eyecandy, automount...) 

I greatly appreciate work done on Ubuntu and Ubuntu-manual.org. But still, something is lacking... I dunno maybe i'm to picky... But i cant live without a good eyecandy and drives mounting on full auto. I cant live with interface glitches and lags. 

Frustration comes from not having a way to achive what you want. Even putting effort into it. You keep on reading, but you are reading material that is confusing, giving different statements that controdict each other. Nobody seems to have a clue either. And then migrating starts looking like a scarifice. I hope i could avoid that...
