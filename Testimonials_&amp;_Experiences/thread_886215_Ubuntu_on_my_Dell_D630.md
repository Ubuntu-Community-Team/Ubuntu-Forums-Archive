### Post by kbrill on 2008-08-10
I have been working with Ubuntu since about December 2007 or so and I am almost ready to delete the windows partition completely.  Now I did choose this laptop because I knew everything in it was supported. So my install took about 45 minutes with absolutely no searching for anything, it all loaded for me.  The only thing keeping me in windows is GoToMeeting and thats not Ubuntu's fault.  

**_Software_**
With Wine allowing me to run Safari and IE for testing, WinMerge and WinSCP my transition was painless.  My programming environment (Zend Studio) run 10x faster in linux than it ever did in Windows and I don't have to reboot every 5 hours or so because I am out of memory.  I found the available WYSIWYG HTML editors to be OK, I chose Quanta and have not been back to Dreamweaver in at least 3 months.  Open Office is more than I need and runs 100X faster than office did.  Firefox and Thunderbird with just as they always did.  Apache and MySQL run much better than they did under Windows and I don't even want to talk about IIS.  And then there is Compiz and Emerald which makes my screen prettier than Vista I think, and they were both 10 minute installs including the time it took me to learn what I needed to.  A couple of clicks in the package manager and everything was installed and has been automatically updated a few times.  Windows never updated my non-OS software like this.  Like I said, the only thing I really go back to Windows for is GoToMeeting and I hope to end that soon.  

**_Hardware_**
Like I said, I bought this laptop knowing it was supported so my hardware install was also painless.  My two monitors were a breeze to set up using the NVidia tools and drivers that installed correctly the first time around and Compiz/Emerald look awesome on them.  My Wireless took about a day to configure right (because I was not sure how to do it), Video, sound, LAN and everything else loaded and configured automatically.  My machine boots faster,runs faster and uses memory better than XP or Vista ever let it.

**_Final Thoughts_**
I have been trying to move to a Linux desktop since my RedHat days maybe 6 years ago.  I've tried every year and failed for many reasons.  This year Linux has come far enough that I have been successful.  I don't need my computer to play games anymore, and the software that I do need has matured enough that it's as good as the Windows stuff.  I am not looking back at this point, and I won't have to spend several hundred dollars to upgrade to Vista even if I wanted to.  I might have succeeded in another flavor of Linux but Ubuntu had it all and has performed flawlessly these last 8 -9 months.
