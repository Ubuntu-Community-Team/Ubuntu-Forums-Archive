### Post by chrislangton on 2010-03-10
As the subject implies, I have been a Jaunty user, Since approx 1 week  after its release.
Jaunty was my first Ubuntu experience, but not my  first Linux installation though by far Ubuntu was my first satisfiying  Linux installation.
The desktop is fully customized from hardware to  firmware, and all my skills were gained from a Windows environment  having never accepted any Linux distro as a base to work from, UNTIL  UBUNTU JAUNTY.

In addition i must mention i own a laptop running  Windows XP (Professional) SP3, a Dell Inspiron 1720. Though this systems  use is Gaming, and the occasional work use while on the go.

Last  week i purchased a ASUS EEE PC 701 with a 8Gb SSD :-D 
It came with  WinXP pre-installed and the licensing sticker on the reverse side.  *turns over and see's the pompus shine still taunting the hardware*
Now  i know that didn't come free, at least some of my hard earned money  went towards that WinXP license, and in Australia the PC stores are  either always sold out of the one Linux system they stock and waiting  for another one to be shipped (with someone already on back order  waiting for it) or you mention Linux to the salesman and he replies with  a ludicrous ignorant comment like "Yeah, that works with XP just  download it or something when you get home" and no matter how many times  you explain that you want a system WITHOUT windows they still think  your crazy and the system wont function without a WINDOWS on it and say  would you like the new one (refering to Win7). So I cave after nine  stores and buy the system in stock with WinXP and go home with no  intention to even boot WinXP once.

So the USB was previously made  ready, to install Ubuntu Netbook remix, having known the futility of  looking around for a linux system, knowing the outcome before i left,  and knew i'd want the system running the minute i arrived home.

So  after approx 15min (or less) after plugging in the USB i have a logon  screen. The quickest O/S install i have ever known (well done Dev team),  the USB preparation tutorial and installation were flawless.

Ubuntu  Netbook remix, thus far (10 days later) has proven to work on my ASUS  EEE PC 701 straight out of the box (so to speak). Hardware such as  webcam, USB, memory expansion slot, ethernet NET/WAN/LAN, wireless  NET/WAN/LAN, bluetooth, speakers, and even the keyboard functioned 100%  as intended without any update or installation of drivers.

Transferring  my personal settings and bookmarks form the desktop also worked  flawlessly. I logged onto all my favorite websites, including secure  logins and sites such as net banking which require java and flash  without any need to look for missing plugins.
I added some music  files like .mp3 and .flac and though the codecs were not present, a few  clicks later (and active internet access) i had installed a package of  codecs without any effort to locate them on my own, Ubuntu supplied a  list of packages and i chose with one i wanted to install. Later while  browsing the folder "music" which hosted my music files I was amazed to  see that a mouse-over plays a preview of the track NO SOFTWARE NEEDED!  Although the Ubuntu remix includes a media player program (Rhythmbox) i  installed my preferred software, VLC Player, again there was no issues  and functionality was as expected.

Next i opened the  pre-installed email client Evolution, it was my first time using the  software, thunderbird being my previous preference. The setup was quick  and i was sending/receiving emails on my three account within minutes.  Now after 10 days of use i am considering Evolution to be my new  preferred email client. 

A quick mention about video files,  though i'm not positive about their playability from a fresh install, i  do know they worked first time i tried to play them. Files such as .mpeg  .mpg .mp4 .avi all worked flawlessly in the pre-installed movie player  program. It may have been the codec package i installed that allowed  them to work first time, but i am too lazy to check right now if that is  the case.

Needless to say all my images opened, F-Spot is a good  photo manage and doesn't cause me any discomfort as a first time user.  File types i tried were .png .bmp .jpg .jpeg and .gif but thats all i  tried at the time. I install GIMP from the Software Center (the remix's  program manager) and then checked my files .tga .dds and .psd.

Empathy  as a instant messenger worked as expected, had a few unexpected  conveniences so overall i was happy to keep the application. Though i  have been hearing a chime sound without any message appearing on screen  when logged into Empathy. The message that appears on screen with a new  message is placed badly, would be better placed against the side and  tucked up higher but not too high to cover the window tools.

Openoffice.org  is and has been my preferred office suite since the release of  openoffice 3.0. Needless to say its a lot quicker on the Ubuntu system  than on a windows system, MS Office cannot compare to OO.org with speed  and value for money, even plugin features far exceed what i know to be  available for MS Office.

I had a few more things to check before  going to bed, like getting my favorite editor working for my web  designing, and was pleased to see that the pre-installed program worked  for html, php, vb, java, and flash, without any plugins. And after a  quick search on the forums here i learned that the plugins can offer me  all the functionality of Notebook++ Plus more!

Since checking out  the functionality of Ubuntu Netbook Remix Karmic Koala, i have located  all the system utilities i sought, and very happy with my experience.
I've  showed a die-hard Mac OSX fan how well the Netbook runs on UNR, and  knowing his personality is usually critical and he spares any  pleasantries, his responses we good and all his questions and concerns  quickly remedied. I may have just converted him to Linux Finally.

My  next goal is to bring the Netbook to a teenage female cousin who has no  computer skills past Facebook and get her to test run it and hopefully  all her constant windows headaches will be no more and she'll move to  Linux too. Then before i convert the MS world of users I'll test my  conversion skills on a co-worker who thinks he knows programming because  he recently stumbled upon torrents, and is a die-hard MS fan. If he can  be converted i'll start the MS user world domination project (aka  ubuntu-marketing).

Regards,
