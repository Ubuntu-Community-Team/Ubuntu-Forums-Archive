### Post by AICollector on 2009-12-24
Hey all, lovely experience I just had...a friend of a friend was having a lot of trouble with his Vista box.

So, being in a helpful mood, I went around his to sort out his problem;

He had a Fujitsu laptop with Vista installed, but that wasn't his complaint; his complaint was that he'd taken it to a repair shop due to a large amount of malware on it (he had anti-virus installed, so I figured the shop would of really followed standard practice; format the hard-drive and reinstall the OS) but the shop didn't do that, they locked the current partition, and created a new one for a Vista home basic install...the end of this practice turned his 120gb HD into something with 13gb remaining, and a boot-up time of one minute and 37 seconds. The machine was about 5 years old.

I had told him about Linux; and indeed, Ubuntu, he was of course, interested. Particularly in the no-needing-drivers and no-viruses part.

So, after backing up his data, I installed Ubuntu, when it was finished I began sorting out the network...turns out he hadnt been able to get the network password, and his neighbors (they were house-sharing) didn't know it either.

Only two people knew it; one had left for a new place, the other had the router in his room...but was in Austria...*sigh*

So, I decided to make the best of it, and I showed him GNOME; it bore some similarity to Mac's menu. I showed him the packages Synaptic had got in it's memory, I explained how most software was installed from Synaptic, which contacted a secure server to download those applications.

I showed him Rythembox, and Totem.

But of course, without an Internet connection, there was very little I could do;


The next day, I get a phone call, my friend, and the recent Linux-convertee, come around to use my wireless. So I continue where I left it the other night; I imbued his machine with media playing powers, I installed VLC, totem-extra-plugins, aMSN, the restricted extras package and Moovida, along with a few other tools. and of course, I performed the all-important update. Most annoyingly however, there was no driver able to give him desktop special effects...

He was happy and was on his way home.


Later that day, I got a text from him telling me he'd got internet access at his place again.

The next day, I get a phone call from him; his machine is slow, and he wants to move to Windows 7. I tell him I don't deal with Windows anymore, but seeing as this man needs my help, I tell him I'll help him with Windows if I can't solve the problems he's having with Ubuntu.


He comes round, we boot up, log onto the network....it was freezing that day, and he had brought his computer and it's powersupply up underarm, so the unit was covered in condensation. I booted it up, and lo and behold, it took a while...

As soon as we got in, I checked a few things...and bloody hell, he'd installed Emerald theme decorator...now, to my mind, that's old, obsolete tech. But it requires POWER to work. Without the driver, he did not have power; checking the model number against Google; I could see a lot of bug reports relating to the inbuilt graphics card in Ubuntu; the driver 'SHOULD' be included...but it wasn't being applied by the system, or the hardware just wasn't liking the driver...

I had run him through the standard Linux-inductee cycle; that Linux was not Windows, and that any expectation to think Linux was Windows would be met with disappointment; Linux is a workhorse, not a games console. I also mentioned it takes some adjustment on the user's side, you are, after all, dealing with an OS, and moments of, dare I say, revulsion, would be expected.

Still, I had to keep my word; he got his Windows 7 disk out (yes, I know, this wasn't going to work) and we tried. Nope. I had suspicions that his CD drive was faulty...and we didn't have the tools to turn a CD into a LiveUSB stick. So, I copied the disk into an ISO and fed it into VirtualBox...now, here's another problem; his machine is old, and suffered from the whole Vista requirements problem. He had 750mb of RAM.

and I was trying to run Windows 7 in VirtualBox with 256mb.
(hopefully you can see why I titled this thread as such)
After five minutes, we actually got to an install screen.

But, after hearing horror stories of how long Windows 7 takes to install on a properly equipped computer...I canceled our install of it and left him to deal with that part.

He said his goodbyes and I haven't heard of any problems since.

The real annoying thing is that my friend had told him that Linux was cr**. His entire experience of Linux was Ubuntu 6.10 for all of five minutes. Considering I've spent the last year dealing with Linux, with it's bugs, spoken to developers, made suggestions, filed bug reports, and even started coding for a Linux app...I found that quite offensive.

It's this event that makes me wonder if Ubuntu is ready for the average user; if your willing to become part of a community then Linux is ready. If you want a box you can't screw up, then it isn't.

BUT, if you vote on this bug:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/500148](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/500148)

You could help me, and hundreds of others, solve a very difficult problem.

Thanks for reading, and Merry Christmas! (and happy holiday to everyone else) 8-)
