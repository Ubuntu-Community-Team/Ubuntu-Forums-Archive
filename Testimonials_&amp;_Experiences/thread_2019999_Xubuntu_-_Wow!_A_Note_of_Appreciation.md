### Post by doctorbighands on 2012-07-07
I wanted to take just a quick moment to express my gratitude for the time and effort of the Xubuntu devs. My long and stressful search for a replacement for desktop Unity is finally - thankfully! - over, and I have you to thank for it. So, without further ado...

...thank you. :)

You have built an elegant, attractive, fast, and functional alternative to Unity and KDE, and you've made a permanent convert out of me. I feel at home and productive again (it's like having Gnome 2 back, only even faster, prettier, and more solid!), and I have never met an Ubuntu release that felt more stable and trouble-free. I recognize that such stability comes from the entire Ubuntu team, and I deeply appreciate them as well; but this time, I felt the need to pay particular recognition to the Xubuntu folks. Xubuntu used to be the weird, ugly Ubuntu stepchild, and not only has it totally come into its own now, but I'd make a strong case for it being the best of the Ubuntu breed!

Many moons of frustration, head-scratching, face-palming, and nearly abandoning Ubuntu entirely can finally come to a close. Since Feisty Fawn, I've been a massive Ubuntu fanboy and evangelist, and despite my very-nearly-hatred for Unity, I can continue on with my evangelism -- only now, I'll be extolling the virtues of Xubuntu.

Truly an excellent job, and once again, THANK YOU.

### Post by Peripheral Visionary on 2012-07-08
I discovered Xubuntu after trying a few distros out including Ubuntu, Mint, Crunchbang, Debian, PCLinuxOS, Mepis, Phoenix, and Xubuntu's closest rival in my limited experience for simplicity, versatility, and beauty - a little Slackware-based derivative called SalixOS.

**Only Xubuntu** worked effortlessly out of the box and was almost already configured just the way I want it. It is well balanced for usability but still lightweight enough to be as fast as minimal Ubuntu+LXDE on this 8-year-old Dell. That is no small achievement!
