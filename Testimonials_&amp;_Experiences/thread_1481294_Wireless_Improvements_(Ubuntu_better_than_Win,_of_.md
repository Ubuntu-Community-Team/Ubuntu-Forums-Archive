### Post by holocene on 2010-05-12
I have a wireless broadband card from Sprint (Compass 597U) that has caused me constant problems on Windows 7 and Vista. 

The problems revolve around starting the network connection. I would prefer to use the icons in the tray to activate the connection but many times this would NOT work. But often I would have to use the bloated Sprint supplied GUI to do it. When these two things failed, I would have to reboot and try again. Another problem I had with the GUI was it  took a long time to (re) install and was quirky to get going. 

With Ubuntu 10.04 (on a notebook), I just plugged in the wireless card, and after a non-technical dialog or two, it just worked! And now, it starts up and stops predictably. 

Kudos to Ubuntu and the developers!
