### Post by Anduu on 2008-04-26
I totally see nicedude's point.

I have been running Ubuntu since Dapper testing.I have installed,reinstalled,updated,upgraded and dist-upgraded on 2 completely different desktops and one laptop many many times.I have never had any issues with anything...period.

The only problems I have ever encountered were way back when I was a newbie and messed with stuff I shouldn't have.

There needs to be readily available information to new users so they understand the consequences of messing with things willy-nilly when they don't know what they are doing.They should make a "Golden Rules" sticky for newbies.Some of the rules should be:

- Don't mess around with permissions.They are there for a reason.
- Don't mess with repositories.You will regret it!
- Don't login as root.See above.
- Basically if you don't understand what a command does or a file is for leave it be.
- The list goes on...

If I had a nickel for every time I saw someone in trouble because of these I would be a rich man.

I believe if everyone used a little patience and common sense there wouldn't be near the amount of problems we see today and the preconception that "Linux is hard" would virtually disappear.
