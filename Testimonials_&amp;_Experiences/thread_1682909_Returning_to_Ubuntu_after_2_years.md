### Post by Luinar on 2011-02-06
Just wanted to share my experiences with Ubuntu over the past couple of days.

Now, I'm no stranger to Ubuntu or Linux. My first distro was Mandrake back in 2004 which I installed on an old PC just to tinker about with. That lasted for about a fortnight before I nearly gave up in desperation, until someone pointed me in the direction of Ubuntu. I've used it on and off ever since then. In the Fiesty Fawn days I even ran it as my sole OS for a year. The laptop I was running it on eventually died and I decided to stick with Windows on the new PC I got, mainly due to gaming and a few other gripes I'd been having with Linux at the time.

Yesterday I decided that my computer was long overdue for a re-install. Windows Vista was starting to get a bit tired, and I was getting annoyed at being a version behind. There was also no way I could afford the frankly obscene price tag of Windows 7. So I thought I'd see what was new in the world of Ubuntu.

And I must say that I'm very impressed with the progress that has been made in the past 2 years. The installation process was a dream. The boot time on Ubuntu is insane... in the good way. My system goes from GRUB to login prompt in 10 seconds. I didn't think such boot-up times were even possible on a non solid-state drive! I have had virtually no hardware problems whatsoever. A bit of configuration was needed to get my wireless card (Broadcom BCM-4306) working, but this was just a case of enabling its firmware. This was a breath of fresh air since my previous experiences of wireless in Ubuntu involved a lot of time setting up drivers with ndiswrapper. I got my webcam (Microsoft Lifecam VX-1000... so I hadn't been holding out too much hope) working after a very brief google search. Even the scanner on my aging Lexmark X1180 combi worked straight out of the box, which was something I had NEVER managed to get working before. WINE appears to have come a long way also, I've got Spotify up and working perfectly which was the one bit of software which could have made coming back to Ubuntu tricky for me. I also really dig the new UI, it makes everything feel much cleaner than it did before. I've kept a small Windows partition on my HDD but apart from gaming I can't see me needing to boot into it any time soon.

It's made me happy enough that I felt the urge to share my good experiences. It's good to be back. :)
