### Post by janberk on 2008-06-30
Oh well, yet another complain thread about Hardy, but I'm REALLY disappointed.

I'm not a geek for computers or software and my job has nothing to do with computers, but one year ago I installed Feisty to my work laptop just to give a try to Linux. Actually I was very impressed by it, and then came Gutsy even better. I stucked to Ubuntu and didn't even touch Windows since then. I gave considerable amount of time for learning Linux and trying new things on it.  

And Hardy. LTS, a major expectation, a major excitement, but what a disappointment! Here is a list of issues that didn't appeare in other distros.

1. Wireless card intel 3954agb doesn't work (I tried some suggested workarounds in forums but it still doesn't work)

2. System freeezes weekly (at times of high CPU or memory usege I suppose.)

3. OpenOffice freezes sometimes when Save As is hit (because of malfunctioning of a process called vpview or sth like that). The problem isn't resolved unless the system is restarted.

4. No sound after restart when a device is present at a USB slot, even mouse. This is I guess because of Linuxant modem driver, but didn't used to happen in other distros.

5. Mplayer and Rythimbox doesn't work simultaneously, Mplayer gives error.

6. Firefox 3: flash issues! Some flash add-ons freezes FF, and some don't have compability with all web sites.

7. Nautilus freezes when a wierd file is present at the selected directory. For solving this, I usually remove files one-by-one in the directory until nautilus stops freezing when that directory is opened. (!?!?)

These are over the top of my head.

I really like Linux and I hate Windows/Microsoft, because I really like the idea of open source software, colaborative software production. Using windows would make me a better coleague to people I work with but I insist on Linux and it's not about money, since my company would pay for the software. I just want to support or want to be a part of this beautiful movement.

But I really don't have time anymore for finding workarounds for all of these issues, really don't. I keep asking myself, WHY HARDY TOOK SUCH A MAJOR BACKWARD STEP??? It just seems to me Hardy is a suicide for Ubuntu.

I checked KDE4, but it seems it has its issues also. I honestly ask, do you have any suggestions? Any other recommended Linux distro?

Thanks,
