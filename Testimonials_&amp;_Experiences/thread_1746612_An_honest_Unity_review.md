### Post by werewolves on 2011-05-02
I thought I'd take a few minutes to relate my experiences with Unity.  Fear not, this isn't going to be another "Unity = Satan!" review.  Nor will it be an "I want to take it home and marry it" review.

When I first started reading about Unity, I thought "this sounds like a mashup of OSX and Win7, WTF?".  I didn't want that.  But I have to say, actually using it is pretty nice.  There are some "Mac-like" things that are actually pretty nice once you get used to them, and likewise the "Win7-like" things.


Let me start out with the things I like about Unity.

What I like most about it, despite it's Mac heritage, is that I can just "shove" the mouse around to the edges of the screen to get things done.  I thought I would hate the menu-bar alway being on top for every window.  Then I realized that I could alway just toss my mouse up, no precision required, and be at the menu.  Every time.  Nice.  I can grab a window and just toss it to the top or sides to expand it.  I can shove my mouse to the top-left, and I'm at my main menu.

I love the "dodge windows" behavior of the launcher.  When I go to my browser desktop, the launcher says "oops, I'm in your way there, sorry about that" and does a slick little slide to stage left.  When I go to my main desktop, it says "hey bud, you need somethin'?" and comes right back.  Nice.

Program search: I hit the "Super" key and start typing a program name, and there it is.  No searching menus.  I like this but it can also be a negative (see below).


Now on to what I have issues with.

Not configurable.  There you go.  A bit more detail?  OK.

The launcher.  Canonical, are you listening?  You need to add a simple config program when I right-click an empty area of the launcher.  This is intuitive, it is what I expect, what I have been trained to expect for many years.  I should not have to install CCSM, or hunt through gconf.

In that menu I need an option to make the launcher smaller.  It is just too big by default.  I'm **not** saying you should change the default, it's for touch-screens, I get it, but many desktop users will want it smaller.

There also needs to be an option to move it.  I understand why that is the default, and I agree.  I personally will not move it.  I love it there.  But this is **not** the only valid place for it.  Many of your users will want it elsewhere, and they are not idiots.  They are not wrong, and should be allowed to make their own choice.

You should allow the launcher to be themed (better yet it should take cues from the Gnome theme).  Some may find it petty, but being able to make your desktop look cool is one of the reasons I love Linux.

When I right click a launcher icon, and click the name of the program, it should open a new instance, not focus the open one.  The way it is now makes no sense.

If I don't know what the program is named, the program search isn't much help, and programs can be a pain to find.  Adding a category dropdown on the initial search screen would fix this.

Finally, it is buggy.  Especially if I do go to CCSM and change something, it blows up.  In fact while I was typing this post, it just died and bumped me out to the login screen.  I'm not exaggerating.


Now, if it sounds like I'm down on Ubuntu or Canonical, I'm not.  I love Ubuntu.  I've been a user since Warty Warthog.  I respect what they are trying to do, and I am interested to see where they go.  Even the "warts" on Unity are understandable.  It was developed **very** quickly, amazingly so, and this is the first release.

I have faith in you Canonical.  I have faith that you will prove the nay-sayers wrong.  I have faith that you will not become the next Microsapple and limit user choice.  I have faith that you will address the configurability issues in 11.10, and impress the crap out of me in 12.04.

Let's rock!
