### Post by viperdvman on 2011-10-06
I too am really loving running Ubuntu. I dual-boot it alongside Windows on both my computers. And on both computers, I use Ubuntu a **lot** more than I use Windows. I love how it looks, I love how it acts, and I love going against the norm and running something other than Windows.

However, I prefer running GNOME 2.x over Unity. Don't get me wrong, I'm liking Unity more and more and I have absolutely no hatred towards it, other than a couple minor gripes. It's just gonna be hard as hell to drag me away from GNOME 2.x. I love the fact that GNOME 2.x can be made to look and act almost any way you want it to. You can make it look and act like Windows like what is done in Linux Mint, or you can make it look and act like Mac OS X (which is how I have mine set up on both computers).

I too think Ubuntu did a wonderful job with this OS. Even with GNOME 2.x going away, I don't see myself making a permanent move from Ubuntu anytime soon. I like running Unity, and I can give GNOME 3.x and GNOME Shell a much better and more thorough chance. If what all I hear on these forums about GNOME Fallback is true, then I can really play around with that. Unless another great Linux distro on the same level as Ubuntu and Linux Mint comes out, I don't see myself moving away. :)
