### Post by UniverseA7X on 2008-02-12
I am running Ubuntu 7.10 on my Macbook. I had it in and extra laptop HDD in an external enclosure with the internal HDD running Leopard with Vista under boot camp. 

Within these past two weeks though, Linux has seen more use then either Leopard or Vista. Tonight, I decided to swap hard drives, and make Ubuntu my primary OS. Why? 

One it just works. One thing that OS X promised, but for some reason, airport is wacko, yet under Ubuntu, it's all good. And no lost connections either. 

I like the ability to customize any aspect of the OS to your needs, or just to make eye candy. I have a very simple layout right now, and it tickles my tummy every time I look at the screen. 

As for future things, I am looking to take a Windows box I built not too long ago, and install Ubuntu Studio on it. I'm looking to record guitar and midi through it, to see how far up to programs like Pro Tools and Reason I can match up to. To perhaps reveal the true power of open source. I plan to get a firewire I/O more than likely and test things out. I want to be able to pretty much make the music, design the cover, burn the discs, print the artwork, and design a website perhaps all on one machine, with open source. It's a big prospect, but I think it would be an outstanding project and might encourage people to look into open source more, and more specifically Ubuntu. 

Thoughts or comments?

Thank you Ubuntu Community, it's people like you we need more of in the world. :)

Rock on.:guitar:

Brandon
