### Post by sparklet on 2010-06-30
I have used Debian for approx 10 years and a few weeks ago decided to try Ubuntu 10.04.
I obtained a CD and used it in trial mode to see what it was like. Well... it looked great and worked a treat. My home network was found, the laser printer attached to the network was very easy to get going, that was a big treat.

Most importantly it recognised and connected with the variety of digital cameras I have, including some very small "keyring" camcorders. This was great !

I installed it and was so impressed that I also installed it on a second hdd as a complete dual boot backup system.

Everything worked but NOT any of the cameras.... Oh dear ! I searched around but didn't come up with any useful information. I remembered that the kernel was upgraded after installing Ubuntu.      That made me think   :confused:

I put the CD back in and ran it in trial mode - sure enough all the cameras connected perfectly

There were some updates to download tonight and amongst them was a new kernel and kernel headers.... maybe.... just a hope.

Downloads were installed and the computer was rebooted so I could start up on the new kernel. Will it.... won't it......  ?

Whoopee  it did !  All my digital cameras are now recognised with no problem .
Ubuntu --- I love you             :lolflag:
