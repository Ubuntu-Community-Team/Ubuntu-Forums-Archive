### Post by Sematary on 2012-06-19
I am both a Windows and a Linux user and there are alot of things I appreciate about both. One of the things that I always struggled with in Linux, however, was connecting to my Windows network and keeping it connected. It seems that those problems are now part of the past. I also installed Cairo dock which is a sweet applet, I must say. Over all, I am extremely impressed with the newest version of Ubuntu and am quite happy with the speed the OS works at on what is admittedly an outdated system - a pentium dual core with 2 gigs of ram. It picked up all the hardware nicely though and as with previous versions, the Nvidia control panel works nicely. I am REALLY glad THAT problem was solved several updates ago. So Great job to the community and developers. I already recommend Linux to people as an alternative to Windows anyway (especially those who have issues getting viruses on a regular basis). Now I feel that Ubuntu has really gotten there.
