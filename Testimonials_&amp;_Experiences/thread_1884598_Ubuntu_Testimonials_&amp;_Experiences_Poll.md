> **effenberg0x0 said:**

Most online communities die or become uninteresting when moderation politics get to tight or loose purpose. We can all think of some good forum that died because of that.

I voted the same as you and appreciate your comment.  

I don't think this forum is going to die any time soon, just because of the sheer number of Ubuntu users if nothing else.

But I must say that I sometimes find myself wishing there was an alternative watering hole for those of us who prefer a more laissez faire approach to keeping conversations productive.

I've been participating in online discussions since literally before the Web was invented, and I've never seen anything close to the level of moderation we see in the Ubuntu forums.

CompuServe was fairly tightly regulated, with an insistence on using only real names and maintaining a professional tone in conversation.  I don't know that I would advocate requiring real names, but I did appreciate the quality of the discussion there.   Still, compared to this forum it was the wild west.

I understand the rationale behind the Code of Conduct, but in all fairness it seems to be applied somewhat selectively.  

For example, with the divisiveness Unity has brought to the community we can see may examples of critics of the new design being very thoroughly moderated, but proponents of the design are even allowed to use thread titles that describe Unity critics as "haters" - definitely not in keeping with the spirit of the CoC.

And I don't think it's unfair to note that the CoC seems to have a more liberal interpretation for the moderators themselves than for us regular folk.  Please don't make me cite examples; I'm trying to be respectful here and have no interest in pointing fingers, just hoping to raise awareness of how such things can play out when moderation happens on the nearly unprecedented level it does here in these forums.

Maybe I'm naive, but I think most people are fundamentally earnest and want to participate in productive ways.  Communities can be largely self-regulating if given a chance.
