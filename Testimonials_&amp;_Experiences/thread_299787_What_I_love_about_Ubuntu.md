### Post by mo_roodi on 2006-11-14
I've just downloaded and installed Edgy on my laptop. Now my laptop is by no means a new box. In fact it's about a year old now but does what it's supposed to well enough for me not to even consider upgrading it - That plus I have a pretty beefy desktop which I use for gaming and some development work when I need the power. But for a lot of mundane day to day tasks like checking my emails, writing the odd Java or PHP script I'll use my laptop (Wireless lets me sit in front of the telly - always a bonus). Anyway I'm getting off the point. What I want to say is that I've used ubuntu on an off (admittedly more off recently) for the past couple of years and I was a little dubious about getting rid of Windows off my laptop in favor of Linux. Needless to say my fears were unfounded. Just about everything worked out of the box. As soon as the installation was complete I had wireless up and running and I was online. I had Evolution configured and I was checking my emails. I had synaptic configured with some additional repositories and I was installing new programs and updating my OS. Even little things like my bluetooth and the integrated SD card reader worked out of the box. The only thing I know won't work out of the box is the modem (it's one of those winmodems so it's going to need a little work - if I ever need it). But what's more is that hardware I didn't expect to work even worked. I have a PCMCIA USB hub which I use to add 4 more USB ports. Under windows it took me 25 mins to install all the necessary drivers (partially my own fault because I installed the wrong drivers the first time round!). Under Ubuntu I plug it in and it works... Beautiful! There are a few things I still need to get working - like figuring out how to configure an external display running off the VGA port and TV out. But on the whole I would like to thank the Ubuntu team for making my switch away from Windows so painless...

Thank you from Mo.
