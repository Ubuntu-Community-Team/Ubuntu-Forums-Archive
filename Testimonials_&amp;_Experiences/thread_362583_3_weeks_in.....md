### Post by Tuna-Fish on 2007-02-16
xara xtreme is vector based, while photoshop is a raster program. "Common knowledge" says that a vector program is better for reproducing artificial shapes such as illustrations of man-made objects and all kinds of computer graphics, also they are (very) good for photo compositing, where you don't actually draw anything yourself but for example merge two photos. In contrast, a raster program is better for reproducing natural objects such as landscapes and human faces, and retouching photos by editing the photos themselves.

However, these days they have both neared each other in function considerably, and you can do pretty much anything with either. I pick xara because while gimp isn't really all that good compared to photoshop, xara is really among the very best in vector graphics.

### Post by potentia on 2007-10-22
Nothing available for Linux compares to Photoshop. 

The commercial program mentioned is Pixel. It is what I would call a Photoshop wanna-be. If you need Photoshop then you need Windows or Max OS X. For professionals, GIMP simply cannot do what Photoshop does. Thats why they pay a huuuuge amount of money for Photoshop, not because of ignorance, but of the possibilities.

If you recommend people Xara then you really don't know what you are talking about, and they you shouldn't give advice, honestly. Xara is a vector editor!

Lightzone (and Rawtherapee and Bibble Pro) are interesting products for processing of RAW files from digital cameras only although they are able to process TIFF and JPEG files as well. That is for photographers.

Personally I need adjustment layers, 16 and 32 bit processing of images, LAB color mode, advanced RAW file processing (and NOT UFRaw), ALL they nice features from Photoshop. GIMP is hopelessly behind.

Let your needs control your choice. I like Ubuntu, but the software is incomparable to the professional software available for Windows and Mac OS X.
