### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-08-12
Tip: If the memory of your wireless device gets corrupted (sometimes this is caused by oddities in reverse-engineered Linux drivers), you will need to turn the computer off completely in order to reset the wireless card's memory.

Simply rebooting will not work because it doesn't shut off power to the card, thus the corruption stays in the card's memory.

That's probably the explanation for why the wireless options disappeared and wouldn't appear even after you rebooted into Windows.
