### Post by ranch hand on 2008-11-08
I have been reading different threads here and there seem to be a lot of complaints so I thought I would try to follow that lead.

For the first time in 3 weeks I switched HDDs and fired up Vista.  Boy, what a difference!  Needed to get some data to transfer.  Only took 11 CDs to get 5 CDs worth of data.  Must be the crappy Maxell CDs.  Kept getting pop up that the CD was no good.

Switched back to Ubuntu HDD and transfered data.

I have dual booted Hardy so I can mess up one and use the other.  Most of this new transfer went on the Play install.  Used one of the "bad" CDs to transfer choice bits of data to Work install.  This is using the same hardware as under Vista except for the HDD.

Under Vista it is a Samsung 320gig and under Hardy it is a WD 320gig.

Now see what this means, Hardy can't even tell when a CD is bad, uses it anyway!

I just burned 3 CDs for my son.  Used 3 more "bad" CDs.

When inserted in CDrom none of them showed up on the desk top.  Brasero erased them anyway.  On eject/insert they showed up fine.  This is bad.  All parts of the system should reject anything one part does.  All 3 burned great and played back great.

Another problem is the ETAs of work being done.  On windows I could easily se that a job would take a half hour because it said 12 minutes.  I erased and burned the first CD tonight and the combined ETAs was 22 minutes.  It only took 17.  How in Flindration am I supposed to get any thing done at this rate?

So there you have my whine.  My blood pressure is not as high as when I used Windows.  This is dangerous, I am sure.  I hope I can live with it.  If it gets too low, I can always switch back.
