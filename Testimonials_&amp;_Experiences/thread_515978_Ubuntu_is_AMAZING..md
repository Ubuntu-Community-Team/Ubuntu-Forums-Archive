### Post by NinjaTails on 2007-08-02
I have to say, I've been with Linux for about two years now (that DOES say something, considering I'm only fifteen) and Ubuntu is by far the BEST distro of Linux I have ever seen. I was part of the Fedora Art Team for a while, creating icons for their Echo theme, but the support for Fedora and the drivers weren't NEARLY as great as they are for Ubuntu. I'm not too fond of the orangey African theme, but that was changable pretty easily :P Another thing, I don't think Fedora had too much going for them in the area of computers they had support for. Such as, my computer is a Compaq Presario, and Fedora wouldn't even INSTALL on here. My friend, who I had recruited from Windows to Linux, had already hated Windows enough (his family had been Mac users until about a year ago.) but Fedora didn't support his graphics card (which was relatively new and top of the line.) but he installed Ubuntu, and it ASKED him if he wanted to install the restricted driver, rather than having to learn how to use the terminal and many other things about Linux, or more primarily, Fedora, just to get his graphics card to work. I was still shady of switching from Fedora to Linux at the time, but then he sent me a screenshot of his computer running on Ubuntu, running FIVE operating systems under it using VMWare, and he had Compiz-Fusion installed and running, and he showed that off too, so I just had to switch, and this is only my first day of using Ubuntu, but I have to say, the community here is bigger, more helpful, and the operating system is just SO much more advanced than I have EVER seen.
