### Post by alanmoore78 on 2010-09-19
It took me an HOUR to install Ubuntu on my little Acer laptop last night...

Let me correct myself.  It took A MERE FEW MINUTES to install Ubuntu on my laptop last night and be running and usable and all that.  It took me the rest of the hour to figure out why I had no menubars.  I run my laptop closed in the keyboard tray and have an LCD on my desk.  It took me that long to think about opening the laptop and changing the settings so the laptop screen was off and only the desktop LCD was active.

I added Java.  Easy.

I added Flash.  Easy.

I added Chrome.  Easy.  Java and Flash just suddenly worked, too.

My Kodak C643 camera and its dock are plug and play.

Wireless took ten seconds to set up and connect (WRT54G, easy).

/host lets me access all the stuff in Windows folders on this hard drive.  See Gran Torino avatar with fatty me in front of it to your left.

My external bits: keyboard, mouse, speakers, 23.6" display, and headset...are plug and play.

My internal webcam is plug and play but my external webcam does not wish to work at this time.  It's supported in spca5xx so it should work, and it's plugged into a USB port and not into the hub I have, but I'll mess with this later.

I am more than impressed with everything.  I'm really enjoying this new OS.  It makes me happy to know that when I build a fast desktop (Phenom II X6 1055T at least) here in a few months, that I don't have to shell out $99 for another copy of Windows to run it and then spend a couple of hours getting everything to work.  I can burn a LiveCD and be up and running in minutes.

I started a new topic in networking and I can see my wife and kid on their computers but they can't see me.  All stealth-like, even.  And how.

I gave 10.10 a couple weeks to get the "bugs" out.  I messed up and didn't bother checking into if Wubi would cause a problem.  Didn't think it would be a problem.  It is.  So I lost everything I had.  Good news is all I had was some bookmarks and cookies and such in Chrome.  I had made a habit of saving my pictures, videos, documents, and other such "important" bits on the Windows partition.

So I putted around on Distrowatch for awhile comparing different flavors of Linux and decided Ubuntu 10.10 is still my favorite.  Thought about going straight up Debian and poked into the Ultimate Edition and Mandrake but decided to stick with what I was used to.  I don't think I'm alone in this decision either.

Dual-boot is flawless.  No more wubi.  Startup screen is now the right size.  My desktop doesn't "tile" on startup and fix itself after a few seconds, it just shows up right the first time.  I like Shotwell.  I like Pitivi.  I poked around the Software Center and discovered one of my old favorites is available on Linux and now I have Sweet Home 3D again!  Don't have to make a new .sh3d floorplan of my house.  Don't have to start over on my garage design.  I can use the ones I already have.  AWESOME!

My next trial is finding the Flash plugin.  It won't load from the Software Center.  Neither will Reader or AIR.  So I may have to do those command line or something.  Don't mind at all.  Made a point to do NOTHING on command line unless I HAVE TO this time.  And so far, so good.  We'll see.

I burned a CD this time for easy reinstallation in case I mess something up.  So I now have 10.10 on disc and plan to use that to set up my kid's eMachines box, my daughter's laptop, and probably this little Dell here I never got around to playing with.  That will be fun, I wonder how tough it'll be to get Ubuntu to recognize a USB wireless internet thingie (Linksys WUSB11) on this older P4 1.8 box.  Maybe it'll just work out of the box.  That's what I can hope for, at least.  Then getting additional packages will be easy.
