### Post by NathanLT on 2009-08-22
> I do believe though that maybe Canonical should reevaluate the 6 month policy. I would prefer a new version once a year if it is less ridden with bugs and overall more stable.

I think the commitment to a 6 month release cycle was really key in the early days of Ubuntu, when it went from unusable to functional in a very short space of time. That energy and sense of urgency really drove things forward. But now we seem to be reaching a kind of plateau where the improvements in overall user experience between releases aren't so great, and the race to offer the latest features often results in buggy, unstable releases.

I think Ubuntu can afford to slow down somewhat. It's not fair to expect users to have to suffer half-baked, or just plain broken releases - poor quality is the main thing driving people away from Vista, so if the open-source alternatives are even worse they will be driven right back! If Ubuntu wants to position itself as the perfect introduction to Linux for newcomers, then it can't at the same time be chasing after the newest bleeding-edge technology, unless it splits Debian-style into 'stable' and 'unstable' branches...

But this is an issue right across the Linux community: the kernel developers, the Xorg team, as well as the leading distributions.
