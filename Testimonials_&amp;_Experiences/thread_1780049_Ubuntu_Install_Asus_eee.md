### Post by f37u5g0d on 2011-06-11
I've been using linux since 2007.  I have seen my fair share of Ubuntu and linux installs over the years.  Typically there has been a 50 / 50 success rate.  I count a success as 'an install in which all the hardware works out of the box.'  I realize that this is not always possible due to the high standards Ubuntu holds software to (freedom and all that).  However I think when installing linux for a new linux user this trait is the most impressive.  Everything simply works.

Yesterday I helped two people install linux on their personal machines.  One was an windows XP era Dell laptop, the second was a brand new Asus eee.  In both cases ALL of the hardware worked out of the box.  This includes the dell's card reader, the wifi (and wifi lights and switches!) on both machines, and compiz (which means compositing and unity!).  I must say I am impressed.  As I have mentioned before I've seen my fair share of installs and installs where things do not work perfectly.  You can now welcome two new memebers to the linux community due to your hard work ubuntu developers!
