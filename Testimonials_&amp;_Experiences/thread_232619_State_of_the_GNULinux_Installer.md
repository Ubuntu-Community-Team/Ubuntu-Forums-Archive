### Post by valnour on 2006-08-09
Quick Intro: Hey everyone! I'm new to the community as you can see from my post count. Quick background information is in order, however. I'm an 18 year-old male college student, I've been using Linux in one form or another since I was 12, and I like long walks on the beach, yadda-yadda. If I posted this thread in the wrong section of the forums, please excuse the mistake, I'm new here guys, and still kinda lurking... Anyways, on with the rant.

Once upon a time, about 6 years ago to be exact, I was playing with an old Sony VAIO laptop that had a busted up screen. So I plugged in an external CRT and keyboard and mouse and shoved it into a desk and wow... now it's a glorified desktop. Well, then I heard about this thing call Leenux, or some shit like that, so I thought I'd give it a try. So I read about it online and stuff, and got my Dad to buy me a copy of Mandrake at the local Best Buy. So then I tried to install it all on my lonesome. Now, remember, I was like 12, and my dad didn't know anything about Linux (still doesn't) and I didn't know anyone that had ever installed it. Nevertheless, I felt adventerous. So I tackled the project after messing up once or twice and actually got the job done relatively easily. I mean shit, it was a graphical installer, it was real easy (even then, and looking back on it now, it was really damn easy), and it looked better than the Windows installers of the time.

Fast forward 6 years. Since those early days of my Linux infancy, I've used Redhat, Fedora, Slackware, Zenwalk, Debian (my favorite until about 3 months ago), a couple other distros, and of course Ubuntu.

Of all the different installers I've used (anaconda, and all that), and all the different Distributions I've used, I still don't understand the argument that Windows is easier to install than Linux. I mean seriously, if a 12-year-old dumbass like I was (and probably still am) could have installed Mandrake on a busted up Sony laptop, I think a Middle-aged Joe Anybody can install Ubuntu.

So please, someone, please, explain to me why everyone says Linux is so hard to install! It's not! Now, granted, I will say that Slackware and Debian were a little tough to wrap my head around the first time I installed them, but after I realized that the lack of an eye-popping GUI actually gave you greater control, I was all for the concept. And geez, I plopped the Ubuntu disc in my laptop (IBM Thinkpad A31), rebooted, clicked install, and half an hour later I was good to go. No fighting with any configuration files like everyone's always crying about (which I'll admit, I do normally have to mess with in some other distro's, but it's not as hard as everyone makes it out to be), no configuring hardware, nothing like that. It all just worked... So then I decided to install it on my Desktop... What do ya know? That worked too. Even the soundcard that doesn't work in WinXP SP2 worked right out of the chute on my Ubuntu install.

Ya know, maybe I'm missing something, or maybe I just got really lucky through all of those installs, or maybe I'm some mad genius and I'm so smart that I don't realize, but GNU/Linux is not a hard OS to install. And I'd like to say that the installer in Ubuntu especially is extraordinarily easy to use.

So yea, sorry about the rant, but I would just like to see how other people felt about this topic, because I think most of it is just FUD.

Thanks...

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-08-10
Some people use those "recovery CDs" that they get with their computers, thinking that it's the Windows installer.

It's not. The Recovery CD just wipes the hard drive and copies a preinstalled Windows instance onto it. The Windows instance is already set up with all the drivers.

So that's why they think that Linux is more difficult to install than Windows.
