### Post by PrinceArithon on 2006-11-19
Alrighty I should state that compared to most people, I'm not a long time computer user.  I only started back in 98, with Windows 98.  Got really interested in the computer in 99.  OK so as I started studying in college and figuring out what computer field I enjoyed best...which took 2 years...I had started hearing about this Linux.  Had no idea what it was.  Just knew it was another OS.  OK well in 2001 I finally used Linux.  Thought it was interesting, but only tried it once in class.  2002 came and I had to take a class just for Linux.  I loved the class.  It was Redhat 7.3 or something like that, which I used lol.

Ok so this class hooked me on Linux, well being poor I didn't have money to go and get another computer to dump Linux on, so that way it wouldn't confuse my parents lol.

Well a few years later I have now tried Fedora Core, Suse, Debian, and even Gentoo.  I became a Suse fan.  Then someone last year showed me Ubuntu Breezy.  I tried it and loved it more than any other ones.

Ok well I had a problem with a driver and couldn't get any Linux on my laptop.  IT was the ATA PIIX....I needed to recompile the whole kernel to get it to work and I didn't have the time.

Well, back in October I was at Borders seen a really interesting Linux magazine...yes I buy one from time to time to see what is going on, or if there is something really interesting to me.  I noticed in the magazine that there was Dapper Drake in it, so that was cool, but I Had no money.  So my girlfriend bought it for me, oh I Love her so much.  Anyway it installed, it worked, and the only time I have used Windows for my own use has been this weekend because I was playing Grand Theft Auto, San Andreas all weekend.  Anyway I will hold onto my windows partition of course, I can't convert all the way yet.  As soon as I can get my webcam working in Linux then I will convert completely.

Anyway all I want to say to the developers is thank you.  Thank you so much for the most amazing distro I have ever used in Linux since Redhat 7.3 and Suse 9.3
