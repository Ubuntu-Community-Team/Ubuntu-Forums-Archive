### Post by Mazza558 on 2007-08-13
I recently went round to a friend's house to give him some addons to UT2004 on his XP install. Here's my experience summed up:

-He turns on the PC. 3 minutes later: ... ... . . .. . . :?
-4 minutes later: We're that the login screen. He logs in on his user (family PC) and we wait....

...and wait... :(

Okay, now we're at the desktop. Finally, I click the start button. Nothing happens. I click again... nothing happens. He says "Oh, you have to wait a bit, Norton hasn't loaded yet." :-s

I flinch slightly as I hear the word "Norton". Looks like it's now Norton Internet and Antivirus Full Suite Premium Platinum Edition 2007 Edition 2007. :| 

A few minutes of silence (marred by the furious whirring and straining of the PC), and we have a Start menu. Excellent, the PC is pulling its weight at last. 

I click "My Computer". Yes, Windows, that means you have to open the "My Computer window. Not in 5 minutes, now. NOW! :-x

I decide to install UT2004's latest patch for him, as it fixes loads of issues in thegame (he only had vanilla UT). I was beginning to realise how much a simple task had turned into a monolithic operation, as the patch executable is compressed and took ages to automatically unzip (heaven forbid me needing to use the trial version of WinZip :shock: )

We leave the patch to install for 30 mins, and luckily it's done. Time for some music to calm me down (he's obviously used to this waiting).

I open iTunes up, and it opened after 20 seconds (What kind of program needs 20 seconds to load all of its data?!? #-o - I understand if it's some kind of university-grade simulation involving several GBs of data, but not this!). My next horror was just around the corner. Waiting 5 seconds for the scrollbar to move up! What is this Madness?!? It's certainly not Sparta, that's for sure. 

By this time I was fed up. I think you can guess why. As a last resort, I tried to update iTunes and saw that the download was 47 MB. 47 MB?!?!?!?! That's insane! I know it comes with Quicktime, but if that bloat was converted to real-life bloat affecting everyone on earth, we'd have an obesity pandemic on our hands!

They say that the only cost of Linux is your free time. I say that the cost of Windows is money AND free time. :|

Oh, and by the way, this is on a newly installed system running SP2 and specs are: AMD Athlon 64 3000+, 512 MB RAM. Not really a slouch of a computer, to be honest.
