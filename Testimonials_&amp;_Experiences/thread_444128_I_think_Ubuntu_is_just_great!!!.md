### Post by eddieb on 2007-05-15
Like a lot of users I have looked at Linux from its conception but found it just too hard. I've used just about every O/S including Mac ( Oh! how I yearn for Beos). About six weeks ago I decided to look again and downloaded Mepis 6.5 64 bit. Faultless install, but gave up after my ADSL kept disconnecting every 2 1/2 minutes only to reconnect immediately. Next tried Suse 10.2 64 bit, slick professional looking install, again faultless, ( I have been in Computing for 25 yrs plus and this was the ONLY O/S to pick up the Monitor model and install the driver) but after using it for a while decided it wasn't for me.
I then turned to Ubuntu, Oh! why wasn't Linux like this years ago. Fantastic faultless install, even asked me would I like to have my Nvidia drivers installed. The only thing I had to do was install the printer driver. On that point under Windows the printer is noisy,(Epson c70) printing and sheet feed is jerky , under Ubuntu very quiet and faultless printing.
I like not having to worry about Viruses and such!
I would like to say one thing,(not a critism) I wonder about all the probs that people have installing software, I have installed Ubuntu on my Desktop and on my Laptop without one hiccup, maybe people just don't pay enough attention to detail when installing!!
One point, again not a critism, Linux 64 bit isn't as fast as Windows 32 bit, although I know how to tune Windows I have yet to learn how to tune Ubuntu.
