### Post by jfbaro on 2007-12-03
Hi Guys!

Although this community is really great, I think I had enough with Kubuntu 7.10 on my laptop, I'll go back to vista (the one which came with the new laptop). Also 2 of my friends were counting on me to replace vista on their laptops. No way jose!

I bought a new laptop (Vostro 1700) and then installed Kubuntu 7.10. I made linux take the whole HD as I was really confident everything would just work (or at maximum need some simple tweaks).

What I wanted on top of that was:

- Kde 4;
- Firefox 3 beta;
- Some fancy themes;
- Skype Beta (the one with video);
- Flash Player;
- All sort of video and audio codecs (like those pack codecs for windows);
- Eclipse;
- Java stuffs for development purpose;
- and then some other softwares which I have seen have Linux versions.

Before installing Kubuntu, all this seemed quite feasible, but then for my surprise I had loads of problems:

- The audio worked only once (after a change I did by following some instructions in the forum - Item G) and then after the next reboot it didn't work; Tried more than 10 different approaches, none have worked for me. (Jezz!! NO sound is a really problem in a laptop which will be used at home more as a multimedia unit....)

- Tried to uncompress some RAR files, it was a pain to install and then didn't work either;

- Copied those RAR files into a blank DVD to try to open and use on another laptop (Windows), the two tries weren't read by the window laptop.

- Try to play some videos, WMV (I know they are proprietary, but I though it would be straight forward to install the codecs)... Wrong again. Tried 5 different ways of installing (again, by following the instructions I found in the forum) all failed!!!

- The internet was really, really slow. That was because the IpV6 which was easy to remove (again, by following the instructions in the forum);

- The default fonts are just awful, I would have to install others (I haven't tried that yet as I am giving up now).

It was a shame, I know I am really new on Linux, but I thought I would spend some days and would get the basic working!!!

I was wrong though!

Next weekend I'll put Vista back (which I don't like, but give less pain then Kubuntu). It's a shame, because I was really excited about having Linux at home.

I knew things would be harder than on Windows, but it's just that everything is such a PAIN!!!!

I am ok using the command line, reading instructions, but there are just so many different approaches for such simple tasks, and even worse, most of them don't work  (some times none of them).

Maybe in the future I will try again.

One thing I must say: The community around Linux is amazing!!!

Cheers
