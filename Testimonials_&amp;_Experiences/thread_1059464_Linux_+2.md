### Post by HittingSmoke on 2009-02-03
So, I was kind of proud of myself last week at work. I work at (not for) the local Best Buy store. I'm a product rep for Sony. The BB employees hate it cause I make three times their pay and my only real responsibility is to know what the hell I'm talking about.

A guy came in looking for a very cheap notebook for keeping in touch online and web browsing. He was looking at a $500 HP. I showed him an Eee PC I had on the shelf and asked him if he had ever heard of Linux. I preached like a legal disclaimer about how this was going to be a totally different experience than he was used to and this would never behave like Windows but if he was willing to get past the big hurdle than it would pay off in the end.

After explaining to him that he would very likely never have to purchase another piece of software again, how easy it is to install software after you break yourself from the .exe mentality and how malware would be a thing of the past he decided it was worth it.

I told him to come back by if he had any problems in the next few days. I haven't seen him since.

The other +1 is my friend who got fed up with his dumb girlfriend (and I'm not just insulting her computer skills, this girl is a damn idiot from every angle) screwing up his laptop and finally said, "I want you to install Linux for me".

My first two converts and not once did I have to bash or even bring up Windows to make my point.

I had one other guy who I just got into a random conversation with at work and we got into a discussion about computers and our OS's came up. I told him I used Ubuntu and he said he had tried it a while back but it wasn't up to the point he needed it to be yet. I told him about how much it has come just since I started using it and he went home with an extra Live CD I keep around for diagnostics on unbootable computers people bring in.
