### Post by sryth on 2008-02-18
Yes, I really do.  That bloated, proprietary, blue-screen giving, piece of commercial spyware that it is....I love it.  It was the final nudge that pushed me over the edge to Ubuntu.  

I've been dabbling in CLI linux off and on for a few years since I do a lot of internet development, and host on a remote VPS to test, compile natively, etc.  

A couple weeks ago everything was normal in Vista (annoying but working)  And I went to bed one night, the next morning when I got up and sat down to get to work, my screensaver froze and wouldn't give me my desktop back, so I had to do a hard reset.  

Note above that I didn't do anything unusual to cause this, which means I'm absolutely, positively furious.  I can't stand software doing things that I don't like when I didn't specifically tell it to do so.  But anyway, I reset the machine, it starts to boot up, and I get the "sytem files corrupted, would you like to attempt to repair?"  So I fume and push Y.   Forty-five minutes later the devil-code kicks out, "Unable to repair files, would you like to boot from a previous safe configuration?"
  Now I'm livid, but I don't want it to find out yet, some important data is still on there.  So I calmly press Y.   A few minutes later I'm back in windows and I've "only" lost 3 days work.  I immediately copy everything important off the drive, download and burn a liveCD of Ubuntu 7.10 (It is what I use on my VPS, so I felt familiar with it somewhat, though at that point I had NO idea of the full potential.)  Then I cackled with glee as I booted the CD and repartitioned the hard drive and wiped it clean.  Installed Gutsy and now, a couple weeks later, I've replaced every tool I used on windows with FOSS alternatives that work just as well, and in many cases better.  I'll never go back.


And that my friends...Is why I love Vista.

In addition, I now make bootable DVDS with my entire system, OS, work, everything, restorable to the point at which the backup was made, on ONE dvd.    Now THAT'S what I'm talking about.

### Post by zami on 2008-02-18
sryth, you have both my condolences and my congratulations.

I've just gone 100% Ubuntu myself, just night before last.

I've been a happy dual-booter for two years now.  Xp for games, Ubuntu for everything else.  But I received Vista as a gift for Christmas..... I think you can guess where this is going.

I can not love Vista.  I *despise* Vista.  And not for being a hunk of crap, but for totally destroying my neutrality in the Windows/Linux argument.  I was *very happy* dual-booting with XP and Ubuntu!  I liked being someone who could appreciate both Windows and Linux for what they are!  I never needed to scoff, sneer, or make snide remarks about Windows.  It was what it was and it had it's job and it's place.  But Vista has completely sullied my opinion of Microsoft and Windows!  I feel... downright offended by how brutally Vista treated my computer and my gaming.

The wipe was only a day ago, so hopefully some of this anger will burn itself out, or just limit itself to Vista.  I don't want to become a Linux zealot just because I hate Windows.  Hate is a poor motivator for anything really and zealotry is a waste of energy.  (Promotion good, zealotry bad.)

Hmmmm... I guess I *can* thank Vista for the money it will save me down the road, because I know I wont be shelling out the money for another Microsoft OS!
