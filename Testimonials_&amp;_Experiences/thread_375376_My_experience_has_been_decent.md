### Post by Tahir on 2007-03-06
> **DonoFL said:**
> I too am new to Linux/Ubuntu and am also curious as to what book to buy?  Should I buy the Official Ubuntu Book or???

Thanks

Regarding the above post.. WELCOME!

It depends which version of ubuntu you installed.  if you installed edgy then I would either wait for another official book for ubuntu or just buy a standard book on linux.  There are plenty of good books published by oreilly.  Most "linux" books will have information on the most popular distros and information on Linux which applies to to any unix-type system.  I would suggest buy one with good reviews and see how it goes.

If you do by a book pm me to tell me how good it is.  Trust me, buying a good book is the way to go because a lot of time is wasted following instructions blindly.  Let me give you an example.  I have read on some websites, the advice they give to noobs about how to change the boot order.  They tell them to open a terminal and type

```
sudo vim /boot/grub/menu.lst
```

but what is sudo? what is vim? why are there forward slashes instead of backslashes in the path?  How come the /boot directory starts from nowhere?

If you dont buy a book and try to learn this all on the fly then you most likely wont succeed. Trust me buy a book. 

I already knew a few linux commands from university so it was not that hard for me to pick up (that, and the fact that I have a degree in computer science). 

If you want a recommendation,  buy Beginning Ubuntu Linux: From Novice to Professional by Apress.  I reckon that this is the best one and they will publish a second edition in april 2007 if I am not wrong.  This is your best bet.  

-Tahir
