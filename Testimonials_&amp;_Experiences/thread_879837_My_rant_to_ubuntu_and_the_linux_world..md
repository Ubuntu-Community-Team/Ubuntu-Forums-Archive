NTFS ([windows] NT File System) is a whole different world in and of itself.

In the NTFS file system there is a certain byte that labels the volume as "dirty" when a NTFS partition has been tampered with (ether by incorrectly removing a drive, or resizing the NTFS partition).

This "dirty" label serves as a safeguard for your data just in case it's corrupted or moved in any way.

sure, you could make a GUI for forcing an NTFS partition to work, but you would be risking overwriting your data from some kind of error.

So boot up an XP machine with the drive attached and "presto-chango" the drive will be read as "dirty" and will be checked for errors and be as good as new!
