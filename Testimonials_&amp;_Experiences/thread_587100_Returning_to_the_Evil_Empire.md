### Post by darclaird on 2007-10-22
Sadly later on today (once a few file transfers finish) I will be removing Ubuntu the primary operating system on most of my machines (I currently run a laptop, 2 desktops, and a server)  and reinstalling Windows XP as my primary desktop, I may keep some hd space aside to tool; about with various Linux distro's we'll see. The primary reason why I feel compelled to do this.

Ubuntu is a waste of too much of my time.

I say this with a fair amount of regret, I first installed ubuntu 4.10 and have off-again, on-again been using it for every release until Feisty (I haven;t installed 7.10 and am going to look at OpenSUSE before I do) It has been my primary desktop (and server base) since early 2005. As each release has come out I have installed and patiently gone through all the steps to get something close to a workable system, generally just in time for the next release, some problems I have never been able to fix. I run my own business, I don;t have the time to spend getting my systems to work, its now losing me money so, Im prepared to spend the money to have the hasle taken away. Things that have really bugged me though

1) despite following numerous howtos over and over again, I have never ever managed to sync my smartphone with my Ubuntu install properly (even scheduleworld has its bugs)
2) No decent CAD app for theatre lighting
3) My wifi has been patchy at best
4) the lack of MP3 and other codec support
5) Poor choices for default install software
6) Open Office is slow as a wet weak
7) Single sign on across a network is nigh on impossible to implement
8) No decent business accounting app
9) Suspend/hibernate never worked properly

I've tried free software, I thin I will from now on try as much as I can to use open source, but I want my free time back

### Post by wirelessmonkey on 2007-10-24
1) Sadly, every smartphone is different, and they all have their problems.
2) Very specialized I think... would something like wine or crossover work for your windows versions of this software?
3) Yay drivers.... I highly recommend the Netgear W111v2 usb adapter.
4) MP3 and the vast majority of codecs are quickly and easily available, but due to license restrictions and Ubuntu's philosophy, aren't included by default in any *buntu distro before gutsy.
5) For what?  I'm very happy with the default software.  If you used the dvd installer, most packages are available via the dvd, or if not, from the repos. 
6) Amen. Installing the openoffice packages directly from openoffice.org seems to work significantly better!
7) Samba?  I use single sign on for much of my work network, and though it did take a little while to configure (reading docs, I know...) it works quite well.
8) GnuCash or KMyMoney
9) Doesn't work on my laptop either, but does on my desktop.

But, in the end, use what's best for you.  If M$ is what you're most comfortable using, please remember to come back and try the next Ubuntu release.  

Best of Luck
