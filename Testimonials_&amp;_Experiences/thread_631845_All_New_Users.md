### Post by Kunks on 2007-12-04
Hello all.......
  My introduction to Linux was with a  Dell Pentium 3 that I re-conditioned(new hard drive-extra memory-from 256 to 768) to accept any operating system & found that Ubuntu was an viable option instead of Windoze.....I installed Ubuntu from an ISO disk I burned on Nero & I'm glad I did... no problems whatsoever(except my graphics card isn't good enough to support Compiz Fusion) even without that it's a fine working machine.... I might give it to my Mother.....The excellent support that the Linux community(Forums) gave me helped me with the installation & it resurrected an otherwise worthless computer into a machine that could have easily ended up in a landfill in China.....
So I took it a step further & re-partitioned the hard drive on my laptop & added a dual boot option(also with the help of the Ubuntu Forums)so now I'm enjoying Compiz Fusion(better graphics card) & the effort that so many people put to make this operating system usable to anyone, no matter what their hardware configurations or what level of computer knowledge they have, is greatly appreciated by me, the end user....
 I ran in to some issues with my sound on my laptop(no sound at all) & again with the help of the forums, I was able to totally correct my sound issues..... Don't ever give up if you have problems because of the generosity of all the people out there who are trying to help you with your specific problems ... They need to be honored... This is an effort by a lot of selfless people who may be much more computer savy  than you but still can take a lot of time out to help people out they've never even met......
All I can say is thanks.............
