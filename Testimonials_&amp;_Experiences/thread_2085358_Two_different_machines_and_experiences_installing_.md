### Post by ramblinche81 on 2012-11-17
Overall, I love the entire system and community of support. I like to solve puzzles and fix things. This was my second install on a machine over past three years and I am glad I made the move overall. That said, my patience wore thin on installing 12.04 LTS to my Toshiba laptop. This weekend I almost came to believe MS Windows products were worth the frustrations. I enjoy technology and problem solving, but I am not a code writing personality. I want technology to work for me, not so much the other way around.

My prior experience was loading Karmic to a home built desktop (asus32mvp with amd) was almost three years ago and it went really well except for sound which was solved in a day or say. I had a clean boot and install within an hour, with full functionality. While I struggled with sound for three or four days looking for permanent fix, it was a minor inconvenience and I had confidence the overall OS was going to work. I also spent some time learning the nuances of command line instructions. 

For three years, no crashes, dumps, bumps, or critical failures or system crashes. Not one. That is motivation to replace Windows on the family laptop with the latest version from ubuntu world when Windows updates started routinely failing and MS support says, reload and start over.

This weekend I really was expecting to install 12.04 LTS with the same fluidity and ease as my Karmic install. Oh, was I in for a surprise. At one point I told myself I regretted my modest contribution to the community as a poor investment of $30.

I was NOT prepared for the idea the kernel would never load and the system would lock up.  It was Windows all over again with a Black Screen of Death with no message or clue to the cause (which makes it tough to find a cure). Despite all the best efforts of other user's previous failures, there was nothing I could find which related to or solved my problem. 

At one point, I convinced myself Windows knows how to get a fresh system started (whether you like the feel or content of the installed product as a different topic) and well, maybe this product isn't for all users, or at least people trying to revive an older laptop.

The really good news is the community came through with some ideas and with a little blind luck I tried a solution of my own creation based on the intuitive nature of the command structure....and it worked. Hey, if x is giving you problems, turn it off and see what happens.

A tweak here, a tweak there. I doubt I will touch the command system again, same as the Karmic which has not been upgraded and won't be until it crashes and can't get up on its own. I am really glad the auto updates have worked flawlessly.

So, long winded testimonial overall. I love the system, I love the community of support, I love the ability to fix things or try things transparently without relying on a remote support group with a plan B is always, reformat and reload your OS.

Now, if I can just figure out how to hack my TiVo boxes and share files within the house network it will really be worth it.
