### Post by proberts on 2007-01-12
I have been a user of the *nix world since the first release of Slackware. However, have always used Windows for my professional life. 

Recently I found myself in a position where I was tired of fighting Windows over and over again, and well, quite frankly found myself extremely sick of spending money on software upgrades and constant hardware upgrades to deal with those software upgrades. 

In order to save some money, or recoup some money, I sold my laptop (brand new Dell) and took an old computer off of a friend. It is a Dell L566cx. (566Mhz) I had no network card, and only 64MB of Memory. 

I usually would install Fedora Core, CentOS or Debian, however, I recently had heard a lot about Ubuntu as a Distro. So I figured, what the heck, give it a try. I was really just looking for an inexpensive alternative to my expensive Windows machines. I have no real pieces of proprietary format software that can't be replaced with SOMETHING open source. 

After about an hour I had Ubuntu installed (that hour is completely my fault too, I have to tear things apart to see what makes them tick, as a programmer / networking / disaster recovery expert, this is just my natural way of doing things). Everything installed great. Dell Monitor, Video Card, everything was detected. When I decided I wanted to add an application, and saw that I could add a Ubuntu package with the click of a mouse, I was even MORE impressed. 

Ubuntu ran impressive on a 566Mhz with 64MB of RAM, but still a little laggy for me. So I ran out to the local geek store and bought myself a used peice of 256m PC100 SDRAM for my little project. (The first and only $25.00 I have invested into my new found system)

Over the past two weeks I have put Ubuntu and this old computer through its paces. Running Eclipse with Every plugin known to man. It runs beautifully. I have had no problems at all, and still can do other things in the background like surf the web, keep my GAIM Instant Messenger open with EVERY ONE of my Instant Messaging accounts. My email stays open in the background with evolution, and from time to time, I have a movie playing as well. 

See, most experienced linux users enjoy the power they have over their system with out all the frill of ease. Personally, in this project I was looking for something that made me just as, or more, efficient as my Windows systems did, however, for a cost that is much less. Ubuntu has given me the best of both worlds, if I want to tinker it, I can tinker it and control it to the tiniest detail, because, it is linux, however, I dont HAVE to operate that way, and can focus on my work. 

I have to give the entire Ubuntu Development Team they have done a great job. 

I will be posting a copy of this on my blog as well, [www.phillipbroberts.com](www.phillipbroberts.com)

Thanks!
