### Post by tjwoosta on 2008-10-22
So about 6 months ago, my parents computer (running windows) got infected with some horrible viruses.

They took it to the computer shop! (i guess they didn't trust me, lol)

The computer guy told them 
"It has too many problems, and its hardware is too old, its not worth fixing."
"Your better off just buying a new machine"

He then proceeded to convince them that they should just throw away the old computer and buy a new one (from him obviously) and then he told them "I can dispose of the old one for you for no charge if you want"
(lol)

So my parents probably would have listened to the salesman and bought the new computer, but they were down for money at the time.

Fortunately they never bought the new computer.



Later that week I asked about the computer and they told me  
"we are probably just going to throw it away, because the computer guy said there are too many problems with it."

So I said "hey if your just going to throw it away anyway, then you can just give it to me."

I took the computer home and checked it out.

It has a 120GB hard drive and 512mb RAM!

Instantly i thought WOW, this computer is definatly not "throw away material"

So i tried to boot it up.

There was actually something wrong with it, because the computer just kept rebooting over and over on its own.

I thought "well lets see if its a hardware or a software issue"

So I booted the Ubuntu live cd.

It worked first try!

I was even able to mount the Windows drive and salvage all of my parents files!

I then formated the drive, installed Ubuntu Gutsy, and gave the computer back to my parents.

They couldnt believe it.

They asked me how much did it cost to fix.

I said NOTHING!!  It was FREE!! HAHAHAHA

And it took me less then 2 hours!

The computer has been running perfectly ever since!

Now if they have a problem with their computer, they know exactly who to call.

### Post by estyles on 2008-10-24
> **tjwoosta said:**
> So i tried to boot it up.

There was actually something wrong with it, because the computer just kept rebooting over and over on its own.


Every time I've had this problem, it was due to corrupted files in \Windows\System32\, or similar system-critical location.  At least twice, this was because the drive was failing.  There might be other reasons for the problem, but it's definitely possible that the hard drive is slowly failing.  Make sure your parents back up their data.
