### Post by chimprich on 2011-11-01
I wanted to like the new Unity. It looked shiny. I'd used it for a few weeks on 11.04 and thought it was promising but unusable. I was interested to see what a revised 11.10 release would look like.

I've used it for a few weeks to give it a chance to "bed in" and have also spent a bit of time with Gnome Shell for comparison.

OK, good things first. It looks very pretty. It's a bit slower than Gnome 3 and Windows 7 on similar hardware, but it "feels" quite nice. The dash auto colour is pretty cool.

The integration of maximised windows with the top bar is nice. The few extra pixels aren't going to change my life, but they're nice to have. The difference was noticeable when playing with Gnome Shell.

The vertical bar makes sense for widescreen monitors (but why can't I move it if I haven't got one or I'm using two monitors?) I like the way it autohides (I'd prefer if it didn't autohide at all, but it's kind of cool the way it works). I like the notifications about how many active windows I have (but I'd rather have an icon for each).

Things I'm ambivalent about include the window controls on the left (I'd prefer it if they just stayed put, but I can live with it; just please don't move them again!) At least Canonical let me keep the controls though. Gnome Shell's decision to steal my minimise/maximise buttons was just evil.

I'm not convinced by the hiding scrollbars. The extra pixels are nice but they just don't feel quite right; still, not a major problem. 

The unified menubar is something I don't really like. It makes no UI sense to divorce the menus from the window. The autohide also looks pretty ugly when it only hides part of the window title. Again, I could live it with it if this was the only issue. I suppose the extra pixels you gain are a tiny advantage.

Now the stuff I don't like, and I'm afraid this is a long list.

First, the lack of a taskbar. I find that switching programs is very distracting and severely interrupts my workflow, making me less productive. Before, I just clicked on the program I wanted to activate from the task bar and it had minimalimpact on my consciousness. Now, I have to activate the sidebar (if it's autohidden), then make at least two clicks (one on the program icon, then on the window). The windows all fly around, which is visually very distracting. I have a very poor concentration span (almost to the level of a disability) and this makes me much less productive. It works fine if I'm just messing around but it's horrible for serious work.

The dash is also bad. The old menus were fine, but the dash now takes me longer to find anything. OK, being able to search is nice, but this could have been integrated into a menu system. 

"Suggestions" for apps to download is annoying. Having a bunch of random icons for programs I can download pop up (and a lot of them are not very good; even the icons are extra-ugly) every time I'm looking for the program I want is pretty distracting. This seems contradictory to the release's stated goal of reducing visual clutter and distractions. Please let me turn this off!

(An aside: if I do see an interesting program, and click on it, the dash disappears and I'm hijacked from whatever task I was trying to achieve. Suggestion: middle-click on an icon in the dash activates the icon but leaves the dash open. Come to think of it, this technique should work for all menus in all programs!)

The virtual desktop switcher. OK, looks nice in operation... but it requires more clicks than in Gnome 2. If I wanted to jump to workspace 4, I'd just click on workspace 4. I now have to click on the switcher, move my mouse to the other side of the screen and double click. OK, it makes moving windows between workspaces easier, and easier to get an overview of all workspaces (and it looks nice) so there's a bit of a tradeoff but it requires more effort to move around. And why (why, why, why) is there no visual cue on the icon to show which workspace you're currently in? This feature seems like it should be simplicity itself to add and I'd assumed it would have been fixed in 11.10. 

No screensaver. I appreciate this is a Gnome 3 decision but that doesn't mean Unity has to follow. I *like* my ElectricSheep! Fine, it's completely pointless in work terms, but it makes my life better. (Yes I'm sure I could fix this, but it takes up my valuable time. It used to be a pleasure to use Ubuntu because I didn't have to tinker with things unless I wanted to).

My view of Unity is that it's pretty, some things are well thought-out, and it has potential, but it's close to unusable for serious work at present. It's maybe marginally better than Gnome Shell. Overall I'd say it was two steps forward, one step back for the casual user, and three steps back for the serious user/developer. Overall: C-

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-11-02
I wouldn't go so far as to say "unusable" for anything. You bring up some good points - the workspace switcher should be more illustrative (maybe with a pop-up switcher too, that appears near the icon and shows while the mouse is held down?). The "Installable applications" in the Dash should be hidden unless you're in a search.

You might prefer to keep the Launcher showing, but with the smallest possible icon size. I do prefer the icons instead of the taskbar; with a taskbar it's too easy to get the situation where there's so many windows that you can't read what each window is, and you have to keep clicking until you find the right one.
