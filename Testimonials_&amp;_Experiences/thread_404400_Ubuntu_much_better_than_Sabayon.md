### Post by izanbardprince on 2007-08-01
I tried Sabayon 3.4 over the last day, and already managed to send the entire OS up in flames, yes, I broke it, and I got to keep all the pieces.

The official update method is "emerge -uavDN world", and it totally hosed all the packages it brought in and compiled.

Portage also runs at an absolutely glacial speed compared with APT, and if there is any performance improvement by compiling packages locally, I didn't see it.

The NVIDIA kernel modules that ship with it work alright, until you change your resolution and save to xorg.conf, then reboot, and you're back at 1024 x 768, I ended up editing xorg.conf by hand.

Sabayon ships with every package you can think of, which means everything works from the get go, but it also loads a lot of stuff most people don't need, which bloats the system and eats RAM.

Sabayon is mostly good, but needs polishing.
