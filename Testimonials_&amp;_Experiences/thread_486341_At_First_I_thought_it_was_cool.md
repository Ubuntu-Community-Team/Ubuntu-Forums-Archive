### Post by nyvalbanat on 2007-06-28
I understand your pains. I've been there. My conclusion is that the only way to protect from software/hardware failures is to keep backups (this is particularly recommended when doing major upgrades). At any given moment hard drives can fail or a pipe could burst and soak the machine or it could catch fire...

While your experience is no doubt frustrating, look at it on the bright side - next time, potentially working on a much more visible system, you'll remember to take precautions. I learned my lesson when I wiped out the whole hard drive of a linux build lab machine (while working at a major software company - no, I didn't get fired or anything like that) due to a faulty script that I wrote and ran as root like a complete amateur.

I moved on after that experience and the lesson paid off. At my current job I kind of bullied my way into using ubuntu as my primary desktop. After only a month or so I ran an upgrade and X wouldn't start any more. This could have turned into a major embarassment had I not made a complete backup of my /etc, /usr, /var, /sbin, /boot and ~ so restoring was a piece of cake. No one even noticed.
