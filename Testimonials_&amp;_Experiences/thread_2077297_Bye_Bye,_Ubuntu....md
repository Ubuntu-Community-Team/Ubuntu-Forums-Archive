### Post by COMECON on 2012-10-28
Hi all!
If you read my introduction, you'll see I used to be an Ubuntu user, but I'm not now. I tried to live with Unity's slowlyness and crashes, because I liked it, but no more. I think Unity is the worst thing Ubuntu never did.
I really liked Ubuntu when I discovered it, at version 10.04 LTS. It wasn't the usual desktop, it wasn't a pure GNOME 2 desktop. I loved its theme, Ambiance, the Mac-like details and all but... Unity is too Mac for me. Well, but I don't mind about it. I don't want a beautiful desktop, I want a **productive** desktop. I have work, I don't have time to waste by reseting the computer or waiting for freezes.
I use Kubuntu sometimes, and I can say beautiful things about it. It's cute, KDE 4.9 is solid and it didn't freeze to me. Xubuntu also was my prefered OS from May to July, I should give 12.10 a try, I hope they don't **destroy** it as they did with main release.
But all my love is focused on **Linux Mint** now. I love their philosophy. They're themselves, they don't want to copy Apple, Microsoft or -even worster- Canonical. It's weird that the companie that does a linux distribution has to be included to the "evil companies" list! 
They have **Cinnamon**, which I use because it's GTK3 and it's more like GNOME Shell (which I like also) and **MATE**, which is rock solid, a GNOME 2 fork, but of course it hasn't the things I like of Cinnamon like its "style" and, in example, the integrated music controls on the sound menu. Maybe **Cinnamon** is more unstable, yes. It crashed one time. But... it's not Unity, I still like it and I can switch to MATE if the things go worster.
You'll say, *you're still using Ubuntu*. It may be true, but no worry. If Canonical destroys also Ubuntu's *back end* and it can't be used neither with Cinnamon/MATE, there's another option. **LMDE**, which is semi-rolling release. 
**LMDE** has the ***Latest*** repo, which have Debian's **Testing** (wheezy) packages that have been tested by the developers, and there is (or was) **Incoming**, which is basically Debian's **Testing** repo. Of course, you can also add Debian's repos, but you can break the system.
Personally I don't like LMDE, because it's not really a Rolling Release, you have to wait to the Update Packs or something like that. But I have it on a VM with MATE, just for testing. If you want a real semi-rolling release, then you can try **Debian** Testing (Wheezy) or Unstable (Sid). I'm on it, virtualizing also.
And about Kubuntu, I used Mint with KDE some time, and I liked it. Maybe it's even better than Kubuntu, but as Kubuntu has KDE 4.9, I'll stay with it.

*So it was just my opinion. Why don't you tell me your thoughts about Mint and other Unity-free alternatives? ~comecon*
