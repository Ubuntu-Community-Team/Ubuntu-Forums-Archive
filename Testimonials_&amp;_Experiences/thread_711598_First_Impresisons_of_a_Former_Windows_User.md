### Post by jjk7288 on 2008-02-29
I consider myself a fairly technologically savvy person. I've dabbled in programming, I have my own website, I can diagnose and fix computer problems, replace hardware, and assemble a machine from scratch. But I was always a Windows user.

I tried giving Linux a serious go at one point back when Ubuntu was a little younger, but I had a hard time setting up hardware and all that good stuff. Eventually it came down to the nvidia driver breaking X Windows, and wouldn't you know it, a Linux newbie got discouraged and ran back to Windows.

I recently wanted to dual boot Ubuntu with Windows XP to use Scheme - that's what were using in my compute science course. The Windows implementations sucked, so I figured what better way than to go with the same OS we use in our labs?

Since installing Ubuntu, I don't think I've booted into my Windows partition not one single time. Not **once**. With 7.10, my hardware just works. I didn't have to set anything up. I turned on the restricted driver for my graphics card and 3D acceleration was enabled.

My laptop was a different story - the Ubuntu 7.10 CD causes it to hang, but I downloaded 8.04 alpha 5 and it works great!

I really wanted to congratulate the Ubuntu team on a job well done. The way Ubuntu is now, any user that needs basic productivity software has everything they need in Linux with your distribution. There were some things to get used to, but I think I much prefer the Linux environment now.

As a side note, I tried Fedora 8 on my laptop (which worked for a while) but I really didn't like it - it had nothing on Ubuntu!

So it looks like I'm a convert. I look forward to being a (hopefully) active community member and helping out where I can, but I'm still learning a lot myself!

Way to go Ubuntu. =)

### Post by Sammi on 2008-02-29
Reminds me of myself three years ago :)

I was lucky when I installed Ubuntu Breezy Badger 5.10. For the most part it just worked for me on my main computer, and over time Ubuntu has grown to be perfect for my machine.

I was feeling a little sick of Win when I decided to try this thing called Linux, that by so many accounts was supposed to be so great, and felt like trying something new. I was adventurous and open minded to something new and different, and I was determined to struggle through the diffuculities of learning a new OS.

There were some difficult times, when it seemed like everything was going wrong, but somehow I got through them all with flare (mostly by the help of forum members), and after a year or so I started to get really comfortable, but I'm still learning - there's so much to learn. And I feel free now. Feels like I'm in control of my computer again, like in the good old days before Windows.
