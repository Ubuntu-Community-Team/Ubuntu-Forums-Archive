### Post by rpared01 on 2011-06-12
A couple of years ago a came across an issue of PC Magazine and it had a free copy of Mandrake linux 8.0. i didn’t know what this linux was, so i ask my brother to help me install it. After several tries he couldn’t figure it out and reinstalled win2000 for me. So about 2 years later i see a review for mandrake 9.0 and think “hey maybe it will work now”,at this point i have better trouble shooting skills than last time. i back up all my stuff and gave it another shoot. This time around, i got it to work on my system. At this point i was still new to the world of linux. i tried to use it for my everyday stuff and it would meet all my needs but one. i just loved to play video games and linux was not able to play my windows games. At this point i needed my games, so i removed mandrake and went back to XP. A few months later i see an article about the end of free red hat and the birth of a new distro called fedora. This article really caught my eye, so i started to keep tabs on anything fedora.And sure enough i see a release of fedora core 1. i downloaded all 3 cd’s and dual booted my system. Upon logging, i could see that most of my hardware was detected. i then started looking for linux forums and other fedora forums. i could feel myself being pulled into this world. This was all new and amazing, i had to learn this new environment called Gnome. Before i new it, i had phased out xp and full custom setup in gnome. i had custom backgrounds, icons, gdm themes. i was very please with the speed and the fact i didn't have to worry about viruses. As time when by core 2 then 3 and finally i was on core 4. With each new release i notice that i had to do less thing custom mods to the system. it made me wonder if linux was heading the same direct as windows by making the system more user friendly. As i saw the Core 5’s release i had lost interest in fedora and just didn't have time for it anymore. i started working for major companies that required me to learn more and more about xp, so for the time being i reloaded xp went back to my normal everyday stuff. Some 2 years had gone by and i had realize that almost every 4-6 months i found myself reloading xp. With vista released to everyone i had made the switch to it and found it work perfect for me but again i would run into the same problem as before, every 6-8 months i had to reload my system. At this point i thought i would revisit linux again, i went back to fedora to see how it has come along from core 4, i had be become use to things just working for me in windows and wanted the same out of fedora. i couldn’t get the same out of fedora without going through 3rd parties for apps, plugins and extra stuff that could break the system. Then while browsing a linux web site i see news of a another distro that was causing all these big waves. Ubuntu was it’s name, what a weird name i thought to myself. I downloaded version 9.04 and again dual booted my system. i was really impress with how everything just works and how little i had to do to configure the system to just work. This time around i notice i spend more time on ubuntu then vista. Ubuntu became my primary system while vista was just a back up. By now i had lost interest playing video games on the computer due to the fact that newer video cards were popping up to fast for me to keep up and i just enjoyed playing games on my playstation even more then my PC. As newer version of ubuntu were release i just couldn’t wait to get my hands on the newer versions like 9.10, 10.04, 10.10 and now 11.04. Each new release a new enjoyment, each new version had become so much easier to user that now i look back at the times when i was using fedora and just say wow we have come along way. Now i find the ubuntu does everything i need and more, when ever i need to edit a photo, surf the internet, rip a dvd or download my mp3s from amazon. Ubuntu is it, i don’t see the need to have windows at home anymore. At home all i run is snow leopard and ubuntu, there isn’t windows os in my house hold anymore. although i’m typing this from my new 11’ macbook air, i can already tell you that i’m doing my research on how to load ubuntu on it. For the longest time i’ve want to write this post and help inspire those who are unsure weather to make the move over to a unix/linux OS. If and when you are ready, i can tell you that you not find a better community of people so willing to help you with your system issues. Thank you Fedora and Ubuntu communities for teaching and converting me to what i call the best operating system i have ever come by.
