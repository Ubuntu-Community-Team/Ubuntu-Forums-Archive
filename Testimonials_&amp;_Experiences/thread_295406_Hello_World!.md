### Post by N/A on 2006-11-08
Hello World!

I finally find a meaning to the above, more than just testing that a program is running.
For some reason I feel i should share my experience with the rest of the world. I have the feeling that no Windows fan is actually reading this but if by some miracle there is then maybe it will try something better after reading this.

So how did i ended up here:
I was googleing for something (other than 'adult material') and found something about 'Trusted Computing'.. very interesting .. so i actually spent about 1 hour reading a paper of a guy explaining the possibilities of this 'Trusted Computing' and Fritz chip and Microsoft and all who involved in promoting it.

As i was reading i turned from intrigued to red and eventually start feeling  like i was some sort of digital junk and even more important i felt like there is no way out of it.

I should mention that i have never ever paid 1 cent for software that can be found on the Internet  Windows included... is just against my better judgement.

I started to use a computer when i was about 11 years old.. maybe 12, i had a HC (Home Computer) with 64kb volatile memory (the RAM of today), it had a Z80 processor (i'll always remember that because I actually did some machine coding for it and i had to learn its mnemonics) and it's OS was Basic-S, i knew computers which held their software on cassette tapes, sure there was something called PC which had a floppy and a hard disk but who could afford one not to mention that there was no Internet, anyway to crush the  fuzzy white cloud of some: there was something else before Windows and the Internet!
Many things i've learned with that first computer proved very useful, starting with English, not to mention programing.

Eventually at 15 i got my firs relatively "real" PC (it had no HDD) it booted form a Diskette the really big ones you can't find anywhere except a museum. It had ms-dos ... wow! it tooked me exactly 2 days to figure out what command.com was doing, why Io.sys and ms-dos.sys wore crucial for booting, and how to make, delete, rename files and directories and that a .BAT file can actually be executed an that AUTOEXEC.BAT was the first thing to load after boot ... ancient days! Oh and if you are wondering, NO i did not spent a penny on the software.. only on the machine.

Great memories with that PC too... made my first clone of Norton Commander (which i didn't have) in Turbo Pascal III, my fist experience with a programing language where the lines wore not numbered (structured programming) i also ported a few games from my old HC witten in Basic-S to the 'new' PC and it's Pascal ... you have no idea (or maybe you do) what fun it was to be able to make a .exe file that didn't needed anything extra to run .. wow! i had lots of fun with the Turbo Pascal compiler shortly after Windows 95 popped up and i got a real PC... so i jumped from the ms-dos command prompt right into point and click technology... big leap!

I had dreams... i dreamt that with the windows I'll be able to program so many things... almost everything was possible to me the only thing i was certain i wouldn't be able to do was to teach the PC how to cook. 
It was a short dream ...
My firs shock: where is a programming language so i can port my games which i was so very fond of!? Nowhere to be found.. there wore games already why would i need a program to make programs ?
Second shock: why do my .exe games made in pascal and compiled and that wore running on my earlier PC don't work?
Lol ... unknown file format
Third shock: what the heck is a device driver !? and why does my new sound card doesn't make any sound, you said is Plug and PLAY!

Of course more shocks hit me one by one systematically screwing my dream and killing any form of creativity a 14 years old kid has.
Why make something when you can buy it!? well i never bought it, i love warez!! although i think right now i no longer need it so it's probably correct to say "i loved warez".

Should i mention how disappointed i was when i realized that the free Internet: the world entire knowledge database, the place for free knowledge and stuff turned into a sales network!? Jesus...
And now... trusted computing!? copyright infringement!? wt* ? if you want to sell stuff put it on CD and send the CD's to the shop, because if its on the Internet i and the rest of 70% of Internet users consider it free.

So in regard to the above i certainly had to do something... and obviously i herd of Linux before, and i also herd that you need to be a programing guru just to install it.
Well i didn't wanted Linux because it was complicated, even tho in my first experience with Linux (RedHat i don't know what version) it seemed logic to me to say 'eject cdrom' instead of saying unmount cdrom and then push a needle in the Emergency Eject Hole  (you do know that the small hole in the CD/DVD readers is for emergency eject, right? ) yeah obviously it was the box of a friend and i was just visiting, and he was demonstrating his Linux skill to me. :lol: oh yeah he was definitely skilled .. no wonder you need to be a guru to install Linux.

After deciding to actually give a try to a Linux i decided to pick one, let me inform you that until 2 days ago i knew of 1 Linux... the one I've meet before: RedHat, but before getting into it i did some more unrelated browsing and stumbled upon a guys blog that was saing how he used Debian for a long time but it got screwed after an update and he went for a Linux that seems to be the latest fashion: Ubuntu ... Wow! what luck ... matching perfectly with my newly imposed need to get rid of my snailish (i mean it, 2 minutes to boot up!?) Windooze eXtra Problems... so i instantly went to Google and to my joy the first link is :"Linux for Human beings" ... Wow .. so finally you don't need to be a guru to get a Linux working? maybe Linux got 'dumb free' just like windoze... obviously there must be some sort of evolution.
So i went to the homepage of Ubuntu and saw that there are not one.. but 4 versions of Ubuntu, Ubuntu,Kubuntu,Edubuntu and Xubuntu ... now that's something ... certainly not like Windows CEMeNT

I've read a little and the manifesto really gave me a sense of 'the world is not going to end because of Trusted Computing and because your Windos will delete itself from your machine because is cracked, the world is a nice place if you dig a little, is definitely free and you can help improve it.'

So i had to chose which Ubuntu i should install ind after excluding Edubuntu, i decided i like the Rat better than the rest... and is light.. simple etc (read the presentation page) and the thing that actually hammered my decision was that you can upgrade to Ubuntu just by installing some extra stuff.

I spent 1 hour to download the .iso, got the last empty CD in the house and at 3:AM i was burning on it Xubuntu.
After that God knows i tried hard to mentain my WinXP on the 80 GB HDD, but it wasn't ment to be... due to some formatting problems the /root didn't wanted to share the same HDD with WinXP, the swap was ok, on its own partition but / just didn't wanted to be in the one i made for it ... so i lost about 65 GB of copyright infringements... big deal :P

And after putting the CD in the drive and rebootig i was impressed ... first acquaintance with 'Live CD' definitely cool!
I saw the Firefox hanged on top of the screen and i thought.. yeah.. right like is going to work... i clicked it and my jaw felt down when i saw Google.
Damn .. i didn't even set the IP and I got Internet ... well for someone who needs to wait 52 to 90 minutes for a windoze installation and then spend 20 minutes to install the networking and only after that to dare setup the protocols for proper Internet connection and after that run a wizard to actually make the device and settings usable and then reboot the system and hope he got it right, that is SOMETHING

Finally something the actually works as i have always dreamt, plug and use... very nifty!
Oh well that was something that really made my jaw fell and i still can't get over it, anyway i instantly pressed Install and the install wizard started and BANG! Chose language .. and i thought .. yeah .. right ... like they're gonna have 'Romanian' in the list sure ... well i actually found Romanian there, and my eyes popped after i saw that the entire wizard actually changed language to Romanian ... so with my eyes popped and my jaw almost broken i pressed buttons to go on to install ... i had to wait about 3 minutes for the partition thingie to start ... it didn't ... it hanged the machine.. oh well you can't have it all, restarted the installation and after abut 3 trial i figured that i should leave English as default language... and it worked... it was after that when i had to let go Wingoose fly away from my HDD because for some reason / would not be happy if installed on the partition that i had specially made for it.

And like any installation a long waiting was due to happen... Yeah sure... you can actually browse the Internet while your OSless box installs Ubuntu... that broke my jaw for good! I'm still recovering by the way.

After about 30 minutes i had a functional OS, further futile attempts to break my jaw wore made by Xubuntu, but i was already ecstatic, Sound works, on board RAM eating graphic card works, Internet works, keyboard and mouse, cd works.. darn! nothing seems to require my extra worked-out nerves and patience... WOW i can actually be care free about my devices ... that's a huge thing.

The Synaptic Package thingie .. i love it!
The window management .. WOW do you have any idea how much RAM window blinds eats!?
Countless workspaces, office stuff... just GREAT! and yeah, no more CD's!

All in all Xubuntu is closer to what i expect form an OS than the previous born failures i had... and i had them all. I can finally enjoy the 1.6 Ghz of my processor and my 196 MB RAM.

Right now I'm a 'Noobuntu' (credits to saalota) proof of that is that it tooked me a day to setup the proper Iptable rules to route my Internet to my laptop... it wasnt because of Xubuntu, not even the rules ... it was me the one to setup the laptop DNS to be the same with the Gateway ... a mistake I'll not make again, and right now I could say that I know a thing or two about iptables :)

Ubuntu feels good and i feel like I can take the pieces of my widoze shattered dream and glue them back to live it one again.

So dear devs, you may disappoint people, and you may disappoint your parents, and your friends and even the Pope, but don't disappoint the dreams of someone because i (and everyone else) rely on you to keep doing a great job... until we become 'Gurubuntus' and take your place.

Oh and one more thing...
[This]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=280193") post made me cry, not of pain but of joy and hope, i don't remember that happening with microsoft, do you!?
