### Post by deinonychus75 on 2007-06-25
I've been an Ubuntu user for, I guess, all of a couple of weeks now at the most, and I think I'm hooked.  My main PC still has Windows XP Home on it, however, Ubuntu 7.04 Feisty is now the primary OS of my laptop, which is an IBM T23 Thinkpad for anyone curious.  It took quite a bit of reading, trial and error, and playing around, yes, but now it is a fully functional (at least for my needs) Ubuntu machine complete with wireless internet access, home networking, firewall, etc.

When I first did the install, I left half of the hard drive open in case I felt like installing Windows in a dual-boot configuration.  Now that I have Ubuntu up and running, I'm thinking more like forget Windows and I'll just leave it a 100% Ubuntu machine.  I'd open up the other half of my hard drive and go ahead and do that, except I'd be afraid of undoing what I've done and losing my current configuration.  Unfortunately, I do not have a CD/DVD burner on this machine.  Only CD-ROM.  Oh well.

Anyway, hats off to the designers and all who work hard to make Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Xubuntu and their lineage before and after possible! :D

### Post by rillip on 2007-06-25
You can, with reasonable safety, boot the LiveCD again and go through the install process, but stop when you get to the partitioning.  Then you can manually edit the partition tables and grow your partitions to use the whole drive.

There is always the danger of data loss, but I have resized my Linux partitions with the LiveCD software at least a half-dozen times and have never had a problem.

Welcome to the community, glad you enjoy it!
