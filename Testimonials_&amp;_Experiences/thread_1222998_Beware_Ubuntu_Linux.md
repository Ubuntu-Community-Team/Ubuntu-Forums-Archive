### Post by frkingz on 2009-07-25
Just a friendly warning to anyone thinking about migrating to Ubuntu.

Ubuntu Linux is a great idea that is poorly executed. It is non-intuitive, overly complicated and too difficult for the average computer to use as an operating system on their primary computer.

People love getting something for free. And many don't like MS and would like an alternative. Thats why Firefox has more than 20% of the browser market now despite IE being pre-installed on every Windows computer. So why has Ubuntu's market share only increased by 1.6% in the United States from 2003 to 2009? It is now up to a whopping 4.2% and the increase is primarily from its use in netbooks, which are mostly just used for web browsing and email. 

If it was a free viable alternative to Windows for most people, most people would be using it. What it is is a boutique operating system created by geeks for geeks. And the geeks just don't understand why everybody else doesn't see the wonderfulness of their creation.

By the way I don't think being a geek is a negative. Geeks are who put us on the moon.

It is fine to use if you install it and then never do anything else but use it as is. Start changing things and watch out. Welcome to entering your password over and over. Welcome to "dependencies not met." Welcome to the terminal. 

I am not a computer tech or an IT professional. I am just the guy my family members, friends,  co-workers and employees come to when they have a computer problem.

After weeks of trying to use Ubuntu as my primary OS this post was prompted by a recent experience I had. On the advice of a friend I attempted to install Wicd in place of network-manager. When I originally went to install it I got an error message saying it conflicted with network-manager. So uninstalled network-manager and then went back to installing wicd. But guess what! A new error message popped up saying dependencies were not met and wicd could not be installed. Super. So I went to Synaptic and tried to reinstall network-manager. Couldn't be installed since files needed to be downloaded and guess what,,,, there was no way for files to be downloaded because.... there is no network manager.

I then spent two days on the internet, on one of my other computers, searching for a network-manager or wicd download to install. Found plenty. Each time they would start installing and then give me an error message about this or that missing. Many of the posts I found related to the subject were really helpful. They basically all said to use Synaptic to install it. Of course most of those posts ignored the fact that THERE WAS NO WAY TO USE SYNAPTIC TO INSTALL IT SINCE THERE WAS NO INTERNET CONNECTION!

Tried a few of those wonderful .tar.gz files too. Guess what, no simple directions anywhere for how to install them. They have readme files in them, they have install files in them but nowhere do they tell a newbie how to actually install them in simple easy to understand language. Neither do the web pages they are downloaded from.

I accept the fact I probably missed some simple way of reinstalling either of those programs. But the fact is if it was Windows I would have just searched briefly on Google found the program I needed, copied it to my flash drive and installed it. Period, end of story, done. 

As it was I wiped out the computer and installed Windows 7. Windows 7 works very well. Almost makes me forget about Vista.

Sorry if I offended anyone but too many people are out there promoting Ubuntu as an easy to use alternative to Windows. It's not. It could be. But it isn't. Maybe someday. The people who are working on it are more than smart enough.
