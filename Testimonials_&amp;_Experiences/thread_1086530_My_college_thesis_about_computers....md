### Post by cwortman on 2009-03-04
I am a psychologist major. I run OSX for most of what I do and will probably do so for a good decade. I do play with Ubuntu quite a bit and like it a lot, but if Ubuntu can surpass Mac then we might have some odd rift. I don't hate Windows but time has outgrown it. 

My experiences with computers
Thesis

	I am writing this in Pages from iWork &#8217;09, because in my opinion there is not a better word processor in existence. If Open Office were to have the full screen option like that of iWork '09 then I would dump pretty much all other office applications. I propose that humans will never get along. We need drama and we thrive on it as a whole. Most just pick their fights and run away from ones they don&#8217;t want to deal with. I like computers they are simple and do what you tell them to do. The outcome is expected. If only people were more like the machine, instead of trying to make the machine mimic human behavior.

	It is in my experience that we must include humans when dealing with computers. Humans in my experiences come out to be in one of two categories. The Einstein group, and the numpties. I can&#8217;t think of a better word than this slang I learned while in the United Kingdom. These groups of people have sub-classes, however they will fall on either side. With people there is no middle of the road. Those who think that there is a middle or a grey area, are numpties.

	With people we must also include religion. A blind faith that overwhelms a group of people who choose to follow the path trodden down by those who came before hand. If it weren&#8217;t for these brave individuals before them, the path would not exist and people would be forced to follow a different path. Most people who start these trends, are  Einstein. Those who knew what they were doing, did it with ease, grace, intelligence, and vigilance. Those who were too short sited to see what they were doing had a bigger (in their mindset) goal and were too busy elsewhere. Those who knew from the beginning, want to protect their investments and lock you into their vision. Forcibly conform you to their goal. Those who didn&#8217;t had no care about investments, and what happens, happens.

	This is true in every walk of life, however it seems to be magnified in computers ten fold, as nothing in life is as simple as the computer; a machine that does what you tell it to do. No matter what though there is, inevitably, the human interaction. And with humans comes their baggage, emotional and otherwise. You have two groups of trend setters: the Mac and Windows crowd, and the F/OSS crowd. Both have good and bad, because sadly, humans created them.

	I want to talk of the latter first. From floss comes great things. Firefox, Open Office, and Pidgin are some great examples. You use the software and enjoy it immensely for free. You almost always feel something is missing though. The creators, however, are another story altogether. When I spoke with a few creators, both male and female, they were completely burnt out, and in some cases, complete *. I am sure it is because they put in a great deal of effort, and see little to no return. I remember one incident quite vividly; I went to congratulate them on the amazing amount of work and they didn&#8217;t want it. I then went on to tell them what I felt was wrong and then I got snapped at and almost punched out. This left me wondering quite a bit about what they could want.

	On the closed side of computers, it is almost an exact opposite. You use the software with some buyer&#8217;s remorse but still enjoy it anyway. You never feel anything is missing, and when you do, it is seldom, and in the next release, fixed. When you want to go in and play with it and change it to your will you can&#8217;t. However, when talking with the creators, they always seemed in great spirits, almost excited. Both male and female were always nice and courteous to me. I was able to speak freely about what I felt was wrong, and while I might not have gotten the answer I set out to look for, I never felt alienated. I had the fortune of meeting some of the programmers for Windows 7 recently  and they were all very nice people, with homes and families. Their ideas were set in stone and had a common goal. If they had a difference of opinion, they came to a resolution they all could agree with. I then went to a Mac conference later that month, and the results were the same. The creators were all there with a common goal and were happy.

	My experiences with Mac are mixed. I have some buyers remorse, but I work through it. My experiences with Windows are surprisingly good. I have a few gripes, but not as many as with Linux. In Ubuntu and many other distributions I went through, I always had issues and headaches. I love Ubuntu please don&#8217;t get me wrong. I own a Dell Ubuntu with paid for codecs and paid for Nero. The speed is another issue. Ubuntu seems quite slow and keeps getting slower over age. I recently tried 9.04 alpha 5 and I enjoy what they are doing and are taking the qualms I have right away.

With Windows, my issues are as follows:
I have to defrag my hard drive. Even if I, personally, don&#8217;t have to do it, it still gets done. Instead of fixing the problem they patch it over.
Bit rot. After a while I have to re-install Windows just to keep it working smoothly.
Letter labeling the drives. It is confusing and I wish they would change it.
They could easily take some BSD code and a BSD file system, and implement it legally and free. The work is already done. I asked a few developers about it and I didn&#8217;t get anywhere, but I was never physically threatened.

With Mac, my issues are as follows:
I dislike the top menu bar and not having the menus in the applications. This is especially annoying while working with multiple applications at a time.
The dock(which will annoy me greatly in Windows 7) has no text near the name to tell me what things are. Again when working in multiple applications this gets confusing.
The icons are all right aligned. I can&#8217;t change it, thats how it is. Even in Windows I can change the icon layout.
I can&#8217;t change the color scheme.
I can&#8217;t link to applications on my desktop. I am forced to use the dock.
The users can be quite elitist for no reason at all. I hate it when I am in a place and someone either snickers or applauds me for having a Mac. I wish people would leave me alone about it I am agnostic about computers as I am just a user. 
I asked a few developers why they chose what they did, and I was told to deal with it(nicely) and that they were going to improve the dock in coming releases and updates.

With Ubuntu, my issues are as follows:
Some people, but not everyone, are quite elitist and rude. Anyone who has ever tried Linux will know you can&#8217;t run Linux without running into these types of people. However this number is dwindling fast
I have to jump through hoops to get work done, which on commercial software I do not.
General slowness. I don&#8217;t get why it is slow. Even with &#8220;lightweight&#8221; WMs, Ubuntu is slow. In 9.04 this is beginning to be addressed, however, I still need to compile my kernel to get the speed I want.
When I asked a few developers about coming things, I was rudely told, when its done it will be done. I went on to ask about the slowness, and they just denied the fact that it exists. I was treated like a nobody, and heavily ignored most of the time I was there. There was one very nice person there to help me out. His name was Edwin and didn&#8217;t even work there. He told me that he went to these things a lot and it is normal. You have to keep coming and donate code to be respected. Either that or donate an insane amount of money(jokingly). To me this struck me as very rude and not something I want to be a part of.

	I have come up with a synopsis of the religion surrounding computers. Whether open or closed source, developers all seem to have some sort of thing. It is more than just a label, or mere fanboyism. It is more than obsession with these people. They love it like a physician loves being a doctor. Honestly nobody would do it except for the love they pour into their work. One could see why some people act like 5 year olds crying about either fair rights or their own right to pick and choose how the end user uses their product. There are no papers to sign but you must pick a side. There is no club to join but to get in you must have intelligence beyond that of even the great Einstein to even be accepted amongst them. Most laugh at him claiming to be smarter, sure he was good for his time, but his knowledge is high school material. The ideas thrown around are that to throw us forward so far into the future, flying cars will be an idea of the past. Not that far off and can be easily achieved and if anyone I think these will. The holy war between Catholicism and Muslim couldn&#8217;t hold a candle to these individuals. They could only dream to have the passion shared that developers and programmers have. 

	A place where race, traditional religious beliefs, sex, or creed are absolutely meaningless and only intelligence, wisdom, passion, and your individuality define who you are. The computer has accomplished in twenty years what took centuries, murder, bloodshed, pain and anguish. It has broken down the walls of hate, it is changing laws, and the way we live life. Some see it as a way of world domination, while others see it as a way toward freedom and world peace. It is no wonder such passion exists and when you look it through the larger scope, it is quite simple to see. 

	I think the computer has, however, replaced many things in which we need. Exercise being one of them, and real human interaction is another. There is a darker side to computers, one of which we live with every day. We are married to them for better or for worse. This could turn on us as we weaken as a species, and some catastrophe happens. We rely on electricity and if the power were to go out we would have no clue as how to handle survival. Have we softened beyond repair? I think not I think those of us who just use them, should get out and run. We should pray to the God we believe in. We should make friends and have real human interaction and rely a little less on them. 

	In conclusion, I think that the floss community needs to adapt some close sourced attitudes and the closed source to adapt some open source ones. I think, those in floss, should learn some kindness and how to deal with people. I think those in closed source, should be a little more lenient on what people do when they buy their software. It shouldn&#8217;t matter once you sold it. Though not everyone in the floss were horrid and were extremely loving and nice, and some in closed source were very rude and needed an attitude check. Over all though this is my experience. The attitudes of FOSS are learning kindness faster than in the closed source bubble learning openness, and this could change the way we use computers. Everything software wise might be free and open rather than closed and arbitrary.  I touched on the intelligent and ignorant theories from the DSMIV. I, with a 3.7 GPA and consider myself intelligent, am very happy. Ignorance is not bliss, it is hell to those informed, and dangerous to those ignorant. I had a very pleasing time with F/OSS software once I found the right people to hang out with. With closed source, however, I was always treated like a consumer and nothing more. When I went to the developer conferences, however, I was treated like an equal and welcomed with open arms. There is no happy medium with computers and goes to prove my theory on people correct. Even I sort of enjoy the irony of my drama. I hate people, of all sorts. I love the person, however, but prefer to be alone. People as a whole make me sick to my stomach, but the person, alone, never ceases to amaze me. My drama is a love/hate relationship with human kind.
