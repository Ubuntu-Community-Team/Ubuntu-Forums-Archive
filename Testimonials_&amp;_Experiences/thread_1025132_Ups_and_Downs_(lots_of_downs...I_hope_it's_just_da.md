### Post by the mage on 2008-12-29
Ok, I have been using almost full time ubuntu since summmer. (with some attempts before).
I have got to praise the open source community for all these really remarkable achievements...
So , I can't say I haven't had good experience on some part of it, but I think it's pointless to zoom on that, since we get better from correcting what's wrong. Unfortunately I notice more and more lately the funny yet ridiculous way of thinking that leads to what we have today and even more what we will pretty soon get.

Let's make it more specific , ubuntu , linux or open source in general tends to see stuff in a really unproductive way, that of "productivity", meaning we should get stuff as fast as we can, with most staff packed inside and as possible new stuff it can fit. 

I have been recently thinking in a way that surprises me now, I wanted as a user more features, neglecting of stability. But I realised that the PC and OS in general serve the user , not vice versa, this means the main goal for a developer should be usability. Some might say "yes, but we work on making our program/OS/whatever better" Yes , but better considering the goals of a program/OS should be usability. Ok, I might get that reply again "But doesn't adding all those new features add usability?" Yes AND No... In theory, of a perfect word they do. There is just something they are missing, or actually neglecting.

Usability gets better as features and new fuctions and such stuff are added and get better, but under one single term...It has to WORK.
Therefore although new features are important for usability, usability can't exist without STABILITY.
Think of it like an equation: > Usability = (Core + NewFeatures) * Stability

with C >= 1 , NF >= 0 , S = {0,1}
From that you can see that if newfeatures take the value of zero it does have an effect on usability decreasing it BUT the effect of that is completely uncomparable to the one you would get if Stability becomes 0 then usability gets 0. 
Now some might ask why I limit stability between these two values, 0 and 1. That's because a system can be stable or unstable, just like the pair works-doesn't work. Of course one could debate over that , but I would suggest making your own equation, I release it under the GPL after all hehe (lil joke, I was kidding).

So anyway let's go back to my point... An OS goal want it or not is to OPERATE (hence the name Operating System), When something is full of of glitches just because almost everyone works on getting new stuff done and added instead on working out to solve most of the issues already on the core of stuff... That's what makes quality OS and software.
Choosing quantity over quality usually tends to have good results especially with some marketing of any kind, but that won't be for long as many it has been proven many times. As fast as they go up , they go down afterwards and by down , I mean to sub zero. (hint hint...Ubuntu went upwards pretty fast, but with the wrong choices, that can change faster)

Ubuntu , developers and the community have tried so hard to make it a mainstream alternative OS, but the root seems to change, since people sometimes want good results fast neglecting the viability of those.
We must put quality FIRST! :)
Of course it almost impossible to deliver a system free of bugs I know that but devoting more time and efford on finding and solving bugs before going on new features is nesessary. 
And remember Linus's Law, both of its notions being applicable, 
[LIST=1]
[*]"given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow", yes but when our eyes are not on the bugs, but instead our heads are turned faaaaaar ahead, bugs are all but shallow.................
[*]Linus Torvalds himself also describes a notion as Linus's Law : "*Linus's Law says that all of our motivations fall into three basic categories. More important, progress is about going through those very same things as 'phases' in a process of evolution, a matter of passing from one category to the next. The categories, in order, are 'survival', 'social life', and 'entertainment'.*" This idea is similar to that of [Maslow's hierarchy of needs]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maslow%27s_hierarchy_of_needs").
[/LIST]
Hierarchy, that's what we need, and please make it right.

(also tell me if it doesn't fit in this forum or if u are a mod, move it plz )

Thanks for reading :)
Manos
