I don't think there is such a thing as "Flawless Full-Screen Flash Performance."  Standard Definition stuff looks bad full screen, and HD requires a lot of bandwidth.

As a matter of fact, for HD video, you have a lot of Hardware requirements in order to shoot it over Flash.  Take Youtube HD for example.  You need a lot of bandwidth, otherwise you get buffer problems.  You need a HD decoder built on your video card, like Nvidia's CUDA (8000 series and up) enabled chips (VDPAU for us Linux users, Pure Video for Windows Users).  And then you need a processor capable of piping such video through fast enough.  Take a seat Intel Atom.

So, in a nutshell, I do have the HW requirements, and I still get choppy HD video every once and a while, mainly because of buffering.  Apparently internet connections are never consistent.
