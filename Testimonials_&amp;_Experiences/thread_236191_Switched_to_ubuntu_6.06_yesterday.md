### Post by smiff on 2006-08-14
After months of pondering, I finally upgraded my PC and embraced the 64 bit age.  I decided upon a clean install of my old favourite, Suse, going for 10.1 in 64 bit guise.  All was fine for a while, but I was itching for a new challenge.  Numerous friends and colleagues, and articles in various mags/sites persuaded me to give ubuntu a go.  The final push was this months Linux Format that had a rather clear message plastered all over it.

So, I downloaded the 64 bit (AMD of course) version and set off installing it.  After the usual headache of getting my wireless card working (the good old Broadcom 4306 chipped Linksys WMP54G which causes so many people problems) everything seemed fine.  I was amazed at how quickly I managed to get a fully working system up and running.  In less than 2 hours I had installed an OS, got a badly supported wireless NIC working (in 64 bit!), upgraded all my favourite apps (Firefox, aMule, etc) to latest versions and had very few issues at all.

Overall, initial impressions are good.

In the last year I've tried a number of distro's and here's a quick run-down of what I think of them (in chronological order):

FC3 - slow and very unstable.
Suse 10 - A huge leap forward.
FC5 - Superb, I even loved the default theme, very professional looking.
Suse 10.1 - Bit better than 10 but still some niggles with some bits.
Ubuntu - Too early to tell, but early signs are very promising.
