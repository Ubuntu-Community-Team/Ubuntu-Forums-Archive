### Post by jarland on 2006-07-02
Wow is pretty much the best word I can use to describe my experiences already with Ubuntu. I've experimented with linux for a good 8 years now (yeah since I was 13) and it only confused me. I've had good experiences with other distrobutions, but there were always things that despite hours of work, I simply could not get working. These things were always enough to drive me back to Windows. I mean, why settle for half my hardware working when it all works with Windows?
When I discovered Ubuntu, my first thought was just "woohoo another one I can try, wish I could get it to work, and then replace with XP again." But that wasn't the case this time. Finally I have a 100% working Linux operating system. I am absolutely in love with Ubuntu. But here's the catch...it's not just the OS. I mean, what is a Linux distrobution anyway? Sure there's more to it than just this, but the majority of it is simply a proper packaging of linux software that already exists. It's a kernel choice, a desktop environment choice, a choice of libraries & packages. But there's way more to Ubuntu than just that. Not only do the developers have insanely good taste on which packages go best together, but the community that surrounds this distrobution is challenged by no other. It's not just the Ubuntu name that will carry it to the top, but it's the large community behind it that helps even the least computer literate people to use what was once considered such a complicated operating system.
For the first time I can honestly say I feel as comfortable using Linux as I once did with Windows.
