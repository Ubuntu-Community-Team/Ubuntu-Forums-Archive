### Post by falsedust on 2007-01-31
It has been a few years but I am finally here, I now have a computer that is purely a Linux machine.
After getting frustrated with my old slow previous computer, which was a  300MHz CPU with 128Mb RAM and a 4Gb HD. Having been at university for a few years studying photography and using the latest Macs and Adobe software, making animations and scanning negatives all that productivity stopped once I left because my home PC just couldn't cut it. 
So in mid 2004 I was at a loss to find something I could actually do with my PC  and then I found a copy of XandrOS on a magazine.  Which worked out well because at that point my Windows 98 spat out a big FAT error, blue screen of death my file allocation table had become corrupted - time for a re-install.  
I think it was about this time in mid 2004 that it seemed like every computer magazine I saw had a copy of a Linux distro on the front I was familiar with what Linux was and it did appeal to me.   So I ended up trying a few distros - Redhat, XandrOs, Knoppix and Mandrake.  In the end Mandrake 9 went on full time, some how their image of a cute penguin impressed me most.  It was alot of fun having to learn new things for my computer, I felt more involved in what my computer was actually doing and how it was running.  The more I read about Linux the more I liked it's ethos. There was a few things that I never got to grips with but I knew it would only be a matter of time before these things were resolved.
Things were looking good and in mid 2005 I bit the bullet and bought a new PC and was going to do everything proper this time, finally get myself a good scanner and printer, big LCD monitor, 2Gb RAM, the latest AMD CPU (thanks to my sister she was working for them at the time), RAID my two HDs and be able to burn DVD's.  But most importantly get myself the latest 64bit OS. So no Windows XP for me!
Now this is where things went pear shaped, I had trouble setting up my RAID under Mandrake 10.1 as I don't think mdadm was included in the install and plus I was just a beginner with Linux. So things didn't start well and resolving the RAID issue by not using it was fine enough but then it came to my printer and scanner.  Now this is one thing I really didn't research enough as I found out my Canon printer and scanner were not supported, still today I haven't been able to set them up even using Ubuntu, but that is down to the manufacturer not being Linux friendly. 
So what was I meant to do if I wanted to continue making my short films and produce artworks? Thats right use the only OS that supported my hardware, or is that the only OS that my hardware supported?  This is funny as I realised later that my first real attempt to get into Linux was also my first time using Xp. 
So basically I was using an illegal copy of Xp and dual booting with Mandrake, it was funny because I had just as much trouble setting up the RAID under Xp as I did Mandrake I even had to go and buy a 3.5' floppy drive because Xp would only accept my RAID drivers on a disk not a CD!!!!  My use of Linux dwindled as I did more work and played games using Xp, but now and then I went into Mandrake and messed about but not like when I first put it on my old computer.
I never really liked Xp that much and all the issues of 3rd party protection from virus and malware and other threats did annoy me, but I could use my scanner and Photoshop. 
The catalyst for me making the move back to Linux was after I quit my job and went traveling around the world in mid 2006.  I came back and after a week my RAID had a problem and I decided to re-install everything. This always gets me thinking about Linux when I have to re-install Xp, so I thought I would setup my computer properly and get back into Linux. But it was after getting out my Mandrake DVD (now Mandriva) I realised that they were quite old and maybe should look at getting a new version, but I thought I better check out what other Linux distros are out there and that is when I came across Ubuntu. It's philosophy and ease of use is what makes it fantastic for me. I could not believe how easy and simple and quick it runs one test using the live DVD and I was hooked, I knew this is what I wanted.
After dual booting Ubuntu 6.06 with Xp it became clear after a few months that I was just not using Xp, albeit for scanning some stuff for other people but this was rare. I put on 6.10 and from then on I knew it was a matter of time before I would chuck Xp in the its own recycling bin.  
For me using Linux is an ethical issue, everyone is familiar with what Microsoft does in it's business practise and this is not something I support, also for the fact that I can do everything I want under Linux so I don't need to use Xp or Vista or waste money on poor quality products that you effectively 'rent' from Microsoft. 
Linux is also something that is growing and developing in a positive way.

I am all for an alternative and a freedom of choice. Everyone should get that choice.

I know this is not the most exciting of tales but this is just my journey.

Recently I send away for some Ubuntu stickers to put on my case and after reading the sticker 'powered by Ubuntu' I thought to myself "that should really be Empowered by Ubuntu".
