### Post by jms1989 on 2009-04-24
After 3 years of experience in linux, I'm going to tell you mine. I promise to keep it short. ;)

It all started around August 06, I was starting to get fed up with all the trouble windows was causing me so I started to look for alternatives. I thought, "Theres gotta be something better then this". Sure enough there was, I came across Debian. So I downloaded it and installed it but it wouldn't boot. Now at this point in time, I was familiar with hard drive partitioning and after installing windows for the upteenth time, how can you not be?

The debian install didn't work for be, I couldn't get to a gui or at least some sign that it was working. All I had was a bunch of, what it looked like to me, random text for things I had no clue existed. So back to the web, I come across Ubuntu. I downloaded it, burnt it, and booted it. Low and behold it work, how 'bout that. I even had a gui. I booted up with Ubuntu 6.04 Dapper Drake. Now I don't remember the exact challenges I had but at least it worked except my broadcom wireless pci card.

After going to Google for help, I found the knowledge to get the job done using &#65279;bcm43xx-fwcutter. After extracting the firmware from my wireless card and rebooting, I got it working. The road was a bit rocky but I made it to linux with a few bumps and cuts. The only disappointment is I couldn't play my favorite games, still can't. :( So after a while I decide to kick windows out and boot only to Ubuntu. Ran it for a year and a half 'till I got an iPod in Christmas of  '07 but I had opted to try to see if it will work in Ubuntu but sadly I could get them to play so I just used my moms' pc, with windows, to upload my music to my iPod for about 6 months. At that point I decided to just welcome windows back but with a catch, I would only use it for iTunes and games and Ubuntu for everything else.

So I've been dual-booting it ever since and having a nice experience overall. I'd hit a few snags every now and then but I'd have a second pc to research on if I mess up my internet connection.

So now I'm quad-booting with Ubuntu  8.10 64bit, Windows 7 Beta 64bit, Ubuntu 8.10 32bit, and Windows XP Home SP3 32bit and the experience is great. I have to say, it is odd that I have 4 operating systems installed but can be convenient. If I have a critical app I need that Win7 and wine won't run, I'd have xp to run it or if I need a 32bit app on linux, I can boot my 32bit version. However, it will soon be replaced with the newest version, Jaunty Jackalope 64bit edition.

I do have a copy of Ubuntu 8.10 32bit and Windows XP Home SP3 installed in a couple Virtual Boxes, just in case. :)

I just recently upgraded ktorrent 2.1.3 to 2.2.1 by temporarily adding the 9.04 repos to upgrade it and it solved my problem with it crashing randomly and I would only be running 4 torrents. Usually I like to run additional smaller torrents that are under a GB but it kept crashing so I upgraded it and it runs great with just 5 torrents running.

I understand that nothing is perfect but Ubuntu is pretty darn close if you ask me. Its a lot better then Windows will ever be. Granted I may like Windows 7 but I start missing my favorite apps after while, not to mention, it will occasionally slow down on you if you use it for too long like say 12 hours. Ubuntu, performance remains the same even if I am using 3GB of ram through vms and Ubuntu itself.

So I'm satisfied with Ubuntu for the most part so I can't complain too much. To the developer behind this fantastic product, thank you. A job well done.
