### Post by caro on 2007-06-09
I received my Dell Inspiron e1505 a week ago.  It shipped a week earlier than promised.  I had no problems except one -- the kernel loaded by Dell and an update to the kernel differed in where they expected to see the boot record, but that was easily fixed.  Otherwise, my experience has been great.  I had it up and running within minutes, including connecting to my wireless network and using my shared printer on my old Win2K machine.  Still playing with all the software and learning how to navigate Ubuntu and linux in general from some of the online resources.  

If your experience is like mine, you'll be glad you made the change.

### Post by brownknight on 2007-06-16
I got my laptop from Dell last tuesday 6/12 via DHL earlier by a week than what their email says. The box came with nothing different about buying other laptops from Dell except for th Ubuntu 7.04 install CD.

I have been playing around with it in the following days and here's what some of my experiences.
1. the media buttons on the front works fine. I have used it to lower/increase system sound, used it in playing totem and xmms. All keys work fine. There is also a media direct button at the top of the keyboard. pressing it automatically opens up rythmbox.

2. I tried closing the lid and after opening it - the screen came up and ask for password back to my session. I tried this on several occassions and it is a hit and miss because sometimes the LCD remains blank but you know processes are working at the back as my HD led keeps on blinking.

3. Resolution is not set correctly. I had to install  915resolution but no additional thinkering is necessary. After logging out and in the resolution is now properly setup.

So far so good. Could have been nice if a "powered by ubuntu" sticker came with the box. I hope to hear from others who bought their pre-installed ubuntu systems from Dell.
