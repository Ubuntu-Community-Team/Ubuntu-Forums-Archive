### Post by tilii on 2011-08-08
[SIZE=2]An Ubuntu experience.

     [/SIZE][SIZE=2] Sunday August 7th 2011... the day that I finally got Vista off my laptop forever.[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]
[/SIZE] 
[SIZE=2]  [/SIZE][SIZE=2] I have been using Ubuntu since Intrepid and not having much in depth knowledge of computers I opted for the relatively easy and safe route of a Wubi install. This served me well and despite my laptop having very humble specifications I managed over the years to upgrade all the way to Natty. This came as a bit of a surprise because I was half expecting my hardware to start complaining or rather the OS requiring better hardware. But no... it battles on regardless.[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]
[/SIZE] 
[SIZE=2]  [/SIZE][SIZE=2] So yesterday, after years of frustration with Windows Vista... not being able to fully uninstall elements I didn't use... all the time wasted on defragging... all the time and bandwidth updating anti virus packages, all the time wasted wondering just why it took sooooooo much memory to do sooooooo very little... annoying pop ups confirming what I'd just asked it to do... you get the picture... I decided do a clean install of Natty and dedicate all the disk to Ubuntu.[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]
[/SIZE] 
[SIZE=2]  [/SIZE][SIZE=2] With Wubi I had used Natty in classic mode as I hadn't liked Unity at first mainly because I have used Gnome-do at various times and came to the conclusion that typing names was slower than a launcher on a panel. However, as it seems there will be no classic mode in Oneiric, I thought I'd better give it another go and it doesn't really take too long to get used to.[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]
[/SIZE] 
[SIZE=2]  [/SIZE][SIZE=2] Result? Well the boot time is down to 30 seconds or so. And Linux is just so much lighter on the system. That was immediately obvious even with the Wubi install. The fan hardly kicks in at all. All in all I am now getting a performance which almost belies the nature of what was, after all, a cheap entry level laptop and would hope that it's lifespan has been extended somewhat. I hadn't expected such a performance boost compared with Wubi.
[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]
[/SIZE] 
[SIZE=2]  [/SIZE][SIZE=2] Don't ask me why I waited so long to do a "proper" install. I think it just came to a point where I didn't need Windows for any particular reason that Linux couldn't support. I wish I'd done it years ago. I think my learning curve peaked at the right time. :D
[/SIZE]
[SIZE=2]  
[/SIZE]
