### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-12-01
If that's a Toshiba computer where the install 'is not working', usually you can hit Escape right when you start it up, and it will give you a choice of devices to boot from. Lots of computers' BIOSs (especially laptops) just boot straight from their main hard drives instead of checking other options, so there is a key you can press to manually choose booting from CD or USB. Check the line of text at the bottom of the screen for information relevant to that, or mash keys randomly - some common ones are Escape, F12 and F11.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-04
If you want Windows XP back, just call the Microsoft activation hotline. You'll be transferred to an operator and they'll ask you some basic questions that are easy to answer, and they'll give you a code to put into the Windows Activation program that will activate Windows again. It takes 10 minutes and is cost-free.

Until hardware vendors start to standardise on drivers, any Free implementations of drivers are going to be a little slow coming, or are going to be patchy. Free Software developers are doing the best they can, as quickly as they can, and hardware manufacturers who co-operate and provide assistance to developers get precedence with hardware support over those who don't want to know.

If you're frustrated that your hardware doesn't work out-of-the-box with Ubuntu, you should get in touch with the makers of the hardware inside your computer.

When Ubuntu is on well-supported hardware, it works like a dream.

Well said.

I'd add that reporting bugs to Ubuntu is vital as well. If you find show-stopping hardware problems, report them to both Ubuntu (via Launchpad) and the hardware manufacturer. Then either dual-boot with Windows or switch back completely to Windows until the issues are resolved.

The more immediate alternative is to exchange your unsupported hardware for models by vendors that do support Linux (e.g. Edimax wireless cards, HP printers... basically products that are marked as supported by the manufacturer). However, I admit that this is not always a viable option for some people.
