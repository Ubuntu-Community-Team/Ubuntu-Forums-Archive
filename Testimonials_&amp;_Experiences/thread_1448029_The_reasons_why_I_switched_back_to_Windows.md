### Post by MindFusion on 2010-04-06
Hi everyone, first off, I want to thank you for the support you gave me with my many topics throughout the year. The responses were mostly quick and accurate, and I would like to thank you for that. But... I have switched back from Ubuntu 9.10 to Windows 7... and I wanted to give some feedback on why I did so, so you guys could maybe discuss with me about the shortcomings of Ubuntu. Here are the main reasons why I quit Ubuntu after using it from september to februari: 

**1. My audio driver.**
I have spent numerous hours trying to find the driver for my connexant Hi-Def built-in audio, but I have still not found it. I tried bringing up a topic here, but it didn't get an answer, and after a week, I finally got a sophisticated reply that me, as a newbie to programming, did not understand one bit. The lack of audio driver made my laptop incompatible with games: I couldn't start up a decent game or my laptop crashed or the game went on soundless, while I could play sound with rythmbox, although mono, and not stereo.

**2. Games**
Yeah, games. I'm a 17 year old guy, so for me, games are still quite important. I have a 4 GIG ram laptop with a Nvidia 8200 M and a dualcore AMD processor of 2.1 mghz, but apparantly, that's not quite enough to run counter-strike:source smoothly through Wine. Due to the lack of audio driver, i couldn't play sound, but also, trying to get cs:s in wine into the right resolution (that's 1366 x 768 for me) was nearly impossible, and installing updates on steam was a tough one as well. A lot of other games crashed because i didn't have an audio driver.

**3. Video editting suite**
I searched long and hard for something resembling Windows Movie Maker, but I never found anything quite like it. Windows Movie Maker may be a simple piece of software, but it excels in its simplicity, while most other video editting suites on ubuntu were either user unfriendly due to their bland looks and interface or overcomplicated for doing thesame simple things as WMM could do. I've tried running Adobe premiere on it, but that didn't work out great either.

**4.Virtualization**
Apparantly, I have some serious issues with my laptop (of which i was unaware), because after trying it on several occasions, with several OS', I still can't get Windows to run on my linux through virtualization.

**5.OpenOffice is not Office**
The title pretty much says all; if ms office and openoffice were twins, openoffice would be the evil one. Trying to do the simplest things with openoffice, like inserting an image in the correct way, just didn't work out, or it glitched. Also, the interface was again not as easy to use as MS Office, and for someone like me who'se on it for 2 hours a day, this can seriously get on my nerves.

Now these were the things I could come up with for now, but I do want to add that although these might be some negative aspects, I'm still a Ubuntu fan, because it is one hell of a fast operating system.
