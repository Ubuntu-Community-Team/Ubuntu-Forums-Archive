### Post by imbjr on 2008-10-22
I'm planning a little trip for next week. All the regular details are done and dusted. I just needed to get details printed ...

First, I copy and pasted what Google Maps gave me for directions and pasted them into an OpenOffice document - crashing the application. I installed AbiWord, but it didn't understand a pasted HTML doc.

So I printed off those directions and the travel email, no problem.

But I wanted to print off screen shots of the map Google presented me. Screen-shotted what I needed and cropped it in the GIMP. I thought of printing the maps in the GIMP, but despite attempts at getting each image to fill the page, it did not seem to update the preview when I changed the scale, nor was there a fill-the-page option.

No mind, I shall insert them into OpenOffice. Inserted the first one. Hmm, needs rotating so it will come out landscape - no rotate option that I could find and one dialog led to a re-occurring "You need Java" error message that forced me to kill the application (despite having Java installed). So I used the image viewer to rotate the images.

Back to Office. Inserted the first image OK. Attempted to insert the second one and instead of the File dialog coming up a properties dialog for the first image came up - hey, I did say insert new image, not work on the first one! I had to make sure I did not have the cursor near the first image/the first image selected before I could insert the rest.

Well I got it done, but that seemed a little less than polished. I normally don't do much office-related work on this machine, just a spreadsheet to keep track of blood sugar readings, so I was a little disappointed that the office experience was so lumpy.

I hope you hardcore office workers are doing better than this.
