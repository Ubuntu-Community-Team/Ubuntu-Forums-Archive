### Post by petersjm on 2006-07-20
Hi guys.

I don't know what to say here. Just thought I'd give my experiences since installing Ubuntu two weeks ago.

I started with the LiveCD (I ordered the CD online first, then two days later I saw it on the cover disk of a magazine so bought it) and it recognised all my hardware and peripherals without a glitch. So I installed it and everything was going pretty smooth until I noticed I had partitioned my 40Gb drive the wrong way around - I wanted to give Ubuntu approx 30Gb and leave 10 for WinXP but I did it the wrong way around. So I messed with GParted but couldn't correct it. I had to reinstall, but knowing nothing about partitions and whatnot, I wiped the entire drive and reinstalled WinXP, too.

Bad move. Now my WinXP partition doesn't recognise my graphics card (highest res is now 640x480 and the option to change it is grayed out!). It also won't recognise my cable modem to let me get online! Ubuntu, on the other hand, still recognises everything and is working as good as ever.

The only reason I still haven't deleted my WinXP partition is that I need QuarkXpress for my freelance book-cover designs and I can't get it to work under Wine. Fortunately, I've got InDesign and work and I've managed to downgrade my much needed Quark files to v4.0 and burn them to CD so that hopefully tomorrow in the office I can get InDesign to recognise the files and work on them from there. Assuming that all goes according to plan, I intend to delete my Windows partition as soon as possible and go exclusively Linux.

I don't game much (if at all) so have no need to keep WinXP. Ubuntu does all I need - Internet (Firefox - my fav browser, even on XP!), email (Thunderbird), Office (OO.o2 and AbiWord), graphics (the GIMP, which I'm desperately trying to understand, but I'm getting there! And Inkscape), DTP (Scribus, as soon as I get to grips with it). So like I say, as soon as I get my Quark files converted, I'm a 100%-Linux man! :D And I don't think I'll ever look back!

I've got no gripes against Windows. Never have, never will. It's just that Ubuntu works better on my PC now. Even if I can fix Windows to support my graphics card and modem, I don't think I want to. Simple as that! :D
