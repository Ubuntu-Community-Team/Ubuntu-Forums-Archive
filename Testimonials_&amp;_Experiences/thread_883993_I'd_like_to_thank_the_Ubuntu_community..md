### Post by Electric Bill on 2008-08-08
for the wonderful work you guys are doing here.

A large percentage of us new users are either current or former Windows users and without the support of you folks here in the Ubuntu community a successful and painless migration to Linux would be difficult if not impossible.
This is my third try at Linux and this latest Ubuntu release (Hardy) is remarkable.
I probably would have succeeded sooner if I had found these forums but instead I got discouraged at the other places I went to for advice.

I have to wonder why so many people get satisfaction out of belittling new users.
Just yesterday for example I went to such a place and mentioned that I had successfully cloned my new installation onto a spare hard drive with Seagate DisKWizard, something I'd never been able to do with Ghost.
DiskWizard is a free version of Acronis and you need at least one Maxtor or Seagate drive installed for it to work.
To make things more difficult I wanted to be able to have the option to clone directly to another drive or to be able to make an image file. 
My source drive was a 36GB WD Raptor (SATA) and my destination drive was an 80GB Maxtor (IDE).
I knew about <dd if=/dev/hda of=/dev/sda bs=32256> but I've screwed up before doing that and wound up erasing the source drive.

Instead of either ignoring my post or thanking me for the information I got the usual response - 10 people throwing command lines around and trying to outdo each other.
They weren't interested in the fact that the app was free but instead they seemed horrified that it used a GUI. [-(
It's just this type of behavior that stops most Windows users from even attempting to try Linux.

[/RANT] So in closing let me thank once again and keep up the good work.
Maybe some day soon we'll see the downfall of the evil M$ empire.

Bill

People giving advice and instructions to use the Terminal can be both a good and bad thing. While the terminal isn't [new] user friendly, and can be daunting to some, its often possible to perform a task faster in the Terminal, than when navigating through menu XYZ. I agree that the Terminal and the commands people give can be confusing/ off-putting, but I believe it is more likely a Terminal command will work for user X, than a GUI option will- this is mainly because, the system structure/ commands generally remain the same throughout the Ubuntu distributions, but the GUI's can be very different. However, on the other hand, for a new user, I agree with you that, if available, a GUI based option should be provided first, as it is less daunting, and more user-friendly.

Let's face it - the GUI is the only way to get people to cross over from Windows.
The message that is being broadcast from most Linux forums (not this one thank goodness) is that they aren't interested in converting the uneducated massses.
While that may satisfy the purists' interests it doesn't help Linux to grow.
After all software venders aren't interested in investing time and money in small elitist communities.
 
Linspire (Lindows) made inroads and I actually bought the 5.0 version when it was first released.
It had the look and feel of Windows and I was amazed that I could transfer files from my network with Samba.
I eventually gave up when I got tired of the lame apps in their warehouse and couldn't figure out how to apt-get from the other Debian repositories.
The search engine on their website was pretty much usless and I finally got frustrated and gave up.
 
Now with Ubuntu getting better with each new release and the help from this community I see that all changing.
People want an alternative to Windows and are even hacking the Mac OS X Leopard to work on to their PCs.
Btw I should mention that the vBulletin Forums search engines all suck and I soon figured out that if I used Google or Google/Linux then put ubuntu in the search box followed by my query I would usually wind up here with the answer I needed. :)
 
Yes the GUI doesn't always work and there are times when you have to use the CLI so most of us **crossovers** willl eventually have to figure out the commands but until then it's nice to have a functional OS. =D>
