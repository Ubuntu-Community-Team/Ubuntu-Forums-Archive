### Post by Gordy on 2007-01-14
I have installed many Linux Distro's and find that Ubuntu is far easier to manage and as for the security of the files that I have created and saved,Ubuntu has never failed me yet. I have always been able to save the files and know where they are at all times. I used  Freespire awhile back and lost hundreds of files because Freespire somehow corrupted my hdb1 drive. 
Yes Freespire has all the toys installed by default however, If I wan to play, I now use Puppy Linux that is on my Thumb Drive. I just boot off the Thumb Drive and watch the movies, play the mp3's, and whatever else I want to play around with. This keeps me out of trouble and is a safe way for entertainment without screwing something up. Beleive me, Linpire or Freespire are not for those that depend on keeping files organized and safe from loss. Ubuntu on the otherhand makes it far easier to manage files and directories. Also, Linspire and Freespire has a problem with remembering where Icons should be placed. Everytime you boot Linspire, the icons are all over the place. Very hard to manage...

So I am happy with Ubuntu.  :mrgreen:
