### Post by trxDraxon on 2012-05-08
Just wanted to give a quick shoutout/thanks to the Ubuntu team and the community.  I have been a long time Slackware user since about 99.  Never really used Linux much on my desktop as I have always been into gaming but I use it in my line of work and on all the servers I use.

I played with Ubuntu a bit back around the 6.10 days.  I really enjoyed the vision of what Canonical was trying to do.  Though I still stuck with Slackware as it was what I knew and what worked best for me at the time.

I recently bought a new laptop for my birthday.  A really nice HP dv6-6145dx.  For the price I just couldn't pass it up even though I was kind of weary about the ATI graphics, having had bad experiences in the past.  Specially since I was being it specifically to put Linux on it.

Basically could not get Slackware running the way I wanted it.  ATI drivers don't work with the latest xorg in -current, though I think there is a patch for that now.  And I couldn't get my wireless working, a ralink card.

So I decided to give trusty ol just released 12.04 a chance and I have to say I am extremely impressed with it.  Everything but my fingerprint scanned worked right out of the box, including both of the headphone jacks.  ATI drivers are working flawlessly with compiz running.  Fullscreen HD youtube videos, no problem.  And I am actually kind of liking Unity.

All in all I am just really impressed with this release and how well it runs on my laptop.
