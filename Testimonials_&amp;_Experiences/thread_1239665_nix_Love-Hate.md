### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-08-14
It sounds to me like some of your unfamiliarity with Linux has caused you to be a bit slower in getting things working. Networked printers should Just Work these days, and your NAS should be mountable out-of-the-box in the Gnome desktop.

You mentioned removing Firestarter... it's not very well known, but running a firewall on the client will stop the client from being able to automatically discover Samba shares or connect to computers by name.

Ubuntu by default doesn't listen to incoming ports, so a firewall is not necessary. Besides, I imagine your network gateway or router already has a firewall built-in which will protect your whole network.
