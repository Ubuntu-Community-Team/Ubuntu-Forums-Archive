### Post by ImaMadGoat on 2007-09-29
My excitement got the best of me and I felt it absolutely necessary to post my latest Ubuntu experience. 

If you go back and read all of my posts (all 18 of them!), you'll find that I first delved into Linux back in 2005. I've always been a computer geek, or so I thought until I met some of the people around here :D I loved getting into trouble on my computer only to find my way out. I used to break my fathers computers all the time and had to learn how to fix them before he got home and realized what had taken place. 

Linux, in the beginning, really felt like a second language to me. Ubuntu has the nice GUI to make me feel safe and warm, but the terminal felt cold, clammy, and...wrong. Or at least it used to. While I am still very much a nublet of the Linux community, I really do enjoy my experience every time I sit down in front of my computer. Current windows users: There's another world out here, just for you (maybe) and ripe for the taking. 

The one program that kept me tied to windows was World of Warcraft. I have played the game for about 2 years and have devoted about 6 months of actual play time to it. Scary. As I'm getting older and moving on from college into the working world. I realize I really don't have the time for it anymore. The game doesn't have the same mystery that it used to. I don't get that warm feeling anymore when I sit down to play it. I get that warm feeling now, from Ubuntu.

Amongst the community of newbs, and basic users, to the experts, coders, and even MS haters.... there's room for me here. The vast majority of the world operates their computers using Windows, and yet they are all strangers to one another. With Linux, and Ubuntu specifically, I feel the presence of a community.  

That presence gives me the comfort and confidence to log on each morning and select Ubuntu instead of windows. I appreciate the power of individuals and the collective power that we can channel to create something as awesome as this. Thank you to everyone that has and will help me over the coming months and years. Here's to Ubuntu!=D>

Cheers,

Chad

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-09-29
Since a lot of servers run Linux, many of the web pages you visit are served up by Linux.  Many folks use Firefox on Windows.  As more open source programs become available to Windows users, they will feel at home in Ubuntu.

It's interesting:  As Windows has become more bloated and as energy prices have climbed, there is a move to more energy-efficient computing--smaller form factors and less overhead.  Linux runs just fine on these new, energy-efficient platforms.

Also as the price of hardware declines, the cost of the operating system is now significant.  Linux looks more attractive for low-cost machines.

Linux is all around, and growing.
