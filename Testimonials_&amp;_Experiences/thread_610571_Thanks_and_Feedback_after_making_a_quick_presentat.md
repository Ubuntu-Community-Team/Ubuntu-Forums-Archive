### Post by wattazoum on 2007-11-12
Greetings,

First I would like to thanks Canonical again and again for Shipit. I wanted to make a presentation of Ubuntu to my company ( +2500 Employees ) and ask 30 CDs for Gutsy in Shipit in case some people would like to test. My request has been accepted and I recieve them. Thanks again and keep up this wonderful service. 

After my presentation, all CDs were gone. I was really relieve of so much success and happy that so much people wanted to give it a shot. That was thanks to the look those CDs have. The feeling is really different to having just a burn cd. Feels more professional thus more serious. It was also thanks to the fact that there is no need to install it for testing purpose. The live functionnality of this CD is very useful and even more, mandatory now.

One question risen was : "You gave us those CD, then what ? what do you gain in counterpart ? will we have to pay you for support or something else ? Cause what is sure is, those CDs have a cost so how do you make profit ?"
What a pleasure to say, I am giving you these for free, and wanted no fees counterpart in return ! First I didn't have to pay for those CDs, Canonical sent me them for free. 
As for the support, I informed them that Canonical do provide paying support but IMO this is mostly for companies, and users support are mostly a helpful community.
However I couldn't answer to the question "How does Canonical afford sending numbers of CDs for free ?"

Well, I didn't have time to make a functionality presentation of Ubuntu and instead did an explaination of what is Ubuntu and what is GNU/Linux as of today ( people was till thinking of Linux with the "every thing on the Shell" feeling ) so I had to explain that now a lot of things runs out of the box. 

So given the fact that I didn't make an overview of functionalities and instead gave a Live CD for testing, people after testing gave me some very light feedbacks : " Well, it is just an OS with a Desktop :-( " . Let me explain what happened in fact :
They take the CD, thinking of what is different in this OS from Windows or Mac. They boot on the CD and then ... well, they are facing a desktop with a menu bar with some applications in it and an install button. The more curious would launch one or two applications. Then,it is all. 

My point ? One of the goal of the LiveCD is demo but there is nothing in it that tells the users what to see and what to look for. 
One thing I propose is to make a demo application that will tell : 

* to a new user : 
** what he can do that he were doing in the windows world ( browsing, OpenOffice, mails ... ) 
** what he can do that was not possible in the windows world ( compiz, Software Management ... )
** what he cannot do compare to what was possible in a windows world ( Games, Hardware Support (advising them to request drivers or specs from the manufacturers ) ... )

* to an already Ubuntu user :
** what is new in this version 

Since the CD doesn't have much place, this application should just be a launcher that autolaunch a browser with a 'Welcome to ubuntu' page that can just present the above information along with some mini HowTo (so that the user knows how to do things ). Those HowTos could be some links to the documentation or/and links to an online video demo storage. But the text offline version is mandatory (for those that are not connected to the Internet).
The application would be launched automatically when the LiveCD User logs in.

I think this will make the LiveCD even more useful.

Others feed back are from some people having a black screen (no X ?) with the LiveCD, thus no test or overview possible. That is really sad. If the problem was that there were no good drivers available for their card, you should maybe fallback on VGA or VESA. I know that there is an entry (safe graphic mode) using VGA but I mean dynamically load the VGA driver cause after this black screen, people won't try again.

I would like again to thank Canonical and the Ubuntu Community for all the good work they do and Keep Up the the Good Work :-)

Best Regards
Oumar Aziz OUATTARA
