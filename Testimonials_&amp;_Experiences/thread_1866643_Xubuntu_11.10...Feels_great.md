### Post by lin73 on 2011-10-21
I had Ubuntu 11.04 installed, and it was working relatively fine with my Macbook 2,1, but then I decided to upgrade to 11.10 to see if there are any improvements in performance and usability. To be fair, I didn't notice much change usability wise, as for performance, it declined annoyingly: My Macbook was just too slow. I'm not sure if that is due to an unsuccessful upgrade process, or to Ubuntu 11.10 being more resources hungry than its predecessor, but in all cases, I decided to give the Xubuntu 11.10 a try, instead of a fresh Ubuntu 11.10 install. And to my surprise, Xubuntu works amazingly well: It is still Ubuntu with all its great benefits, but it feels lighter, makes more sense, and looks just neat and consistent. To be fair, it's been one day with Xubuntu, but so far I absolutely love it. Xfce team, great job.

It's not so bad to want to switch Thunar but it would be great if it either caught up to PCmanFS or the Xubuntu team switched to it in future versions.

I will still give Canonical the benefit of the doubt and try Unity in future releases hoping they get things right
