### Post by AnonymousJustified on 2012-06-07
I'm not sure what it is, but after installing Ubuntu, I feel a lot more comfortable with obvious things like browsing the web, but also using command prompts(eg, the GNOME terminal) registry editing(gconf), and a whole bundle of other things that used to make me feel like I was wandering on an alien planet.

Over the course of one week, I've turned my standard ol' Ubuntu install into an install running LXDE, where I completely replaced LXpanel with Docky, completely customized my programs all the way down to my audio player(DeaDBeeF is amazing), have done many a trick to add to the speed of my computer, and most things like this, I'd be scared to do on my Windows XP boot.


I just want to say that something about Ubuntu, or maybe just Linux in general has made me feel infinitely more comfortable with my computer, and, over this past month, I think I've gotten better with Linux than I have the past fifteen years of my life spent using Windows.


I'm really pleased with Linux, and I don't think I'll ever revert back to Windows.

I couldn't agree more. It's been my experience that many Linux apps are more intuitive and simpler to use. Which IMHO helps users feel more confident. They often don't have all the bloat or unnecessary functionality.

I prefer the straight forward, no frills, get done type of apps. Personally, I don't need pretty GUIs or memory intensive apps - just ones that do the job. ### Post by Max Blyss on 2012-06-07
I've found that since I switched over to the Linux team, that not only do I feel more comfortable with my system and apps, I also find it all more interesting as well.

The whole Free / Open Source scheme of creating and distributing software makes the process quite a bit more immersive and inclusive, with users contributing reviews, feedback, bug reports, etc...  I think Ubuntu (as well as Linux in general) allows the user to develop a much greater awareness of the workings of his / her OS and software than Windows. (Never used a Mac, can't comment on them.)
