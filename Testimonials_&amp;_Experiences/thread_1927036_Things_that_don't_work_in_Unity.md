### Post by trilsys on 2012-02-17
By "don't work" I don't mean broken. As far as I can tell, v11 with the Unity desktop is as stable as any other release of Ubuntu. What I mean is things that are counter-intuitive and obstructive.

Menu bar at the top of the screen:
Yes, it may always be in the same place, but it is way up there, off the window that is active. I have to shift my eyes to find it. When the menu bar is with the window, it is just up there. This menu bar is for this window. I don't have to READ the text of the menu bar to make sure the menu bar is active for the window at which I am looking. 

If the window is a small window, the mouse movement to the menu bar can be a large one.

Consistency is a problem. Where are my functions? For example, with gedit, "go to line" is in the menu bar at the screen top only. So, I go to the toolbar at the top of the gedit screen to save, but go to the menu bar at the top of the screen to check spelling.

Unity Launcher:
omg, so many things to hate.

To launch a program that is not on the launcher, I must switch modes from mouse mode to keyboard mode. People hate to switch modes. First, I click on Dashhome. The quickest way is to remember the name of the program I want. Is it pydsm or pysdm? Nope, neither of those. It is "Storage Device Manager". Or I can type a topic word. "Finance" gives an icon for KMyMoney, but "budget" does not.

I am an orderly person. I like to group things together. For example, for my work I have the VPN client, service ticket client, browser company phone book, PHP & HTML editor, and a Citrix client. They have all disappeared into the great sea of "More Apps".

How do I remove items from the Launcher? I rarely use Workspace Switcher, but there is no way to get rid of it.

How do I move things on the Launcher? I use the Trash a lot, but it gets squished down at the bottom and when I want to delete a file it is difficult to do by drag & drop.

Then there is the question of what am I running. With Windows I just look down at the System Bar and I see not only what applications are in use, but the documents using them. If I mouse over them, I even see a postage stamp image of the app. If I start an app that is not one of the defaults, it adds that app's icon squished at the botton. Impossible to see or understand.

As I mouse over the Launcher, the icons stretch and roll, but the only clue as to which are active or not is this little teensy white triangle. wtf? I defy you to quickly scan the Launcher and tell which apps are running and which are not. 

Sometimes there is one triangle and sometimes there are two. Oh, one triangle means the application is launched, 2 triangles means it is launched and currently in focus. So, sometimes the 2nd white triangle is there, and sometime it isn't. But no hint as to the document or task it is working on. This is a huge omission.

At the moment I write this, on the dashboard, there are full size icons for the Ubuntu Software Center, Desktop Sharing, and Skype. I never use these applications. And they are not in use. I _am_ currently using 3 applications. They are squished icons at the bottom of the dash. Looking there, I cannot tell what they are.

Unity seems oriented towards people who only want to use their computer for entertainment. The 4 primary defaults on the Dashhome are "Browse the Internet", "Check Email", "Listen to Music", and "View Photos". The only things missing are "Buy Stuff" and "Internet TV". I start my browser and email client once per work session and then I'm done.

The 1st choice for the program groups is "Media Apps". omg.

Then, a full third of the popout sorta window is "Programs Available to Download". I don't want to know this. Period. I find it offensive. It takes up critically valuable real estate.

I've tried to keep this from being a personal rant. I apologize if things "leaked". What I am seeing is computers moving from devices to enhance humans and create things, to consumer devices. I have always considered Linux to be my tool for getting things done. Unity changes that. It  moves Linux perhaps more into the general user domain and away from being useful for me, and certainly away from being useful for commercial work (I cannot imagine showing my boss a database server whose first program choice is "Media Apps"). Whether that is good or not depends on your general view or personal view. But even for the general view, I think that Unity is a poor and ill-thought-out interface.

Let's see what v12 brings.

### Post by Version Dependency on 2012-02-17
> **trilsys said:**
> Small correction. It is easy to get rid of items on the Launcher. Just right click the item. Still, not easy to add items.


Very easy to add items.  Open the dash, find the app you want to add to the launcher and drag it to the launcher.  Or open an app and it appears on the launcher...right-click on the icon on the launcher and select "keep in launcher".  

Or, if you need to make a custom launcher...say for something that doesn't appear in the dash normally (say launching "conky" for example).  Install alacarte if it's not already installed (sudo apt-get install alacarte) and add your custom item.  Now that custom item should appear in the dash.  Drag it to the launcher.
