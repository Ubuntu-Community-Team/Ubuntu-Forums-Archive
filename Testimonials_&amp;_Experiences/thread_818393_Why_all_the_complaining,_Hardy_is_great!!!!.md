### Post by stchman on 2008-06-04
Hello all.

I recently upgraded (clean install) all my home PCs to Ubuntu Hardy and I am very impressed.  

I had an ATI 200M equipped laptop that I tried to get Compiz workeg and I could not.  I installed Hardy and enabled the restricted driver and BOOM, Compiz worked.

I was running Feisty and Evolution would never print right.  Hardy cured that.

For my newer 8800GT video card the restricted driver worked for that.

My new laptop with X3100 and 4965AGN everything worked OOB including Compiz.

Firefox 3 Beta 5 rocks.  Very smooth and the add-ons are catching up now.

Hardy boot faster than Feisty or Edgy.

Is Hardy perfect?  No.  Is it the best Linux distro out there?  IMO Yes.

I give 2 thumbs up to the Ubuntu developers, they are doing fantastic work.

I sold the 200M equipped laptop to someone off Craigslist and he is so impressed with Hardy he has decided to use it over Vista.

Another friend of mine I installed Hardy on his laptop and he is very pleased with it.  Does everything he wants.

I have read about the lockup problems on here and I have not experienced them on any of the machines I own.  I even run the 64 bit version on my Athlon 64 and it runs great.  I am considering using 64 bit on the rest of my machines (Core 2 equipped).

I am what you consider an average user.  I surf net, check email, use office apps, chat using Pidgin, rip/play MP3s, rip/play DVDs (I own of course), do some HTML, C/C++/Java programming.  Ubuntu has fulfilled everything very well and Hardy is the best to date.
