The added bonus is no need for anti virus either and a community out there who provide better 'customer care' than any corporate body. Also considering the lack of required hard media needed and all the associated packaging therein the carbon foot print is massively less with Linux. So currently the only disks i have and really need now are my HH 8.04 iso, FF7.04, 2 wireless driver disks, and as a weapon of choice gparted for messing with the Drives. its a bit different to all i needed for windows and a welcome breath of fresh air. Lets not also forget that it seems to take up far less HDD space, i know with the cheapness of large drives its not so much of an issue but everything also seems to run that bit quicker as well.

### Post by Fenris_rising on 2009-03-06
Hi all

Well it's only 2 months off a year since I changed to Ubuntu. I have taken the step of cleaning my whole system out and re-installing from the ground up. No, there was nothing broken, wrong, or failing.

My PC has been down for 2 months with a busted PSU. Having just got and installed the new one I am back up and running. In the meantime I had been using my xmas EEEPC with Easy peasy (ubuntu derivative) and have used it to really try and learn better 'management' of what I do with
the os.

Hence my reasons for a clean install. I had experimented and played around so much with my PC's install that it was frankly a mess :D All critical data is on a 2nd drive so nothing lost.

In fact I have gained a lot! Mentioned elsewhere on the forum a small change in one bios setting has, it seems speeded up my PC, also I don't have to edit my menu.lst any more to ensure the PC boots up to the desktop.

One sticking point was that the program 'diskmounter' seems to be unavailable so my 2 backup HDD's were not on my fstab. This is due to them having to be unplugged so that the sata drive that is my primary OS disk appears as a candidate for the OS to be installed to.

Any way it seems that something has 'clicked' because once I accepted the fact that diskmounter wasn't available it only took me 4 minutes to actually manually add them to the fstab........I'm in shock :D

The secondary intention of this clean install is that once everything is setup to my liking I will be using 'remastersys' to create an backup ISO of my setup. I have already done this to my EEEPC and it's a great program. I understand it is only a GUI front for the hard of understanding as the same group of programs can be accessed via terminal. I have no problem with terminal I use it as and when for basic tasks but there is a limit to my capabilities :D . All in all it's a good day to rediscover Ubuntu. Just in case you didn't notice......... I think Linux rocks and long may it continue.

regards

Fenris (Linux junkie)
