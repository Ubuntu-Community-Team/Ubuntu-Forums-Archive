### Post by CJ56 on 2007-11-22
Just to add my voice to the praises in this section - I've been using Ubuntu since the start of this year, beginning with 6.06 - and I'm now onto 7.10 and am really delighted with it. Easy to set up, a few compiz tweaks and I have an elegant and functional dektop, and so far (!) everything is fast and smooth. A great experience all round.

And to those having trouble getting it to work - just keep trying! I know that different versions suit different people and, for all sorts of reasons, sometimes don't work at all (I couldn't get on with 7.04); and it's deeply annoying when smartasses write in to say how great everything is when you're stuck with an unworkable machine. But the way Ubuntu has grown and matured in the space from 6.06 to 7.10 is so impressive, I can't believe that eventually it won't come right for most would-be users. As a refugee from M$ I was originally just pleased to find in Ubuntu an OS that did everything that Xp did, without fuss - and I've always found Xp to be a pretty good OS. But now Ubuntu is a generation ahead of Xp and still moving forward. It just has to be worth sticking with. 

Sorry if this sounds over-zealous, but there it is. Congratulations to the Ubuntu team and all the people involved with it. A great effort.
