### Post by loclarkey on 2007-01-18
Hello everybody-

I thought I'd just put this out there.

I started messing around with Ubuntu back in May, and had a dual-boot system up until yesterday.  I was hesitant to switch over from Windows completely mostly because I wasn't sure if musical notation software was good enough.  Admittedly, Rosegarden/Lilypad is not Sibelius, but back in May I messed around with it enough to realize that it was perfectly reasonable to fit my needs.

Anyway, months go by and my XP installation starts degrading.  I wish I had kept up with it, but the number of times I had to reboot escalated at an alarming rate, culminating in an almost unresponsive system.  I spent almost an entire day reinstalling it and ended up with a still-slow OS that wouldn't recognize a network, or install upgrades.  The funny thing is, I couldn't install the upgrades because of my lack of connectivity and I couldn't connect because I didn't have the right update.  Typical.

The next day my computer died.  It wouldn't boot into ANYTHING.  Since I had lost my Ubuntu LiveCD, I drove to the nearest borders and picked up a random Linux magazine which came with a Mandriva install.  Within an hour I had a Mandriva partition.  You can read off of the Windows partition in Mandriva, so I copied all of my files from Windows and burned them onto a DVD.  I remember going through all of my programs to make sure I wasn't forgetting any important config files, and seeing an entire menu structure which I had called "Tweaks", and realizing that I would never have to do that crap again.  I would never see a bizarre registry entry, pore over a HijackThis log, run some Spyware detector or defragmenter ever, ever again.  It felt good.

Well, long story short, while in Mandriva, I realized that I like the KDE environment, so I downloaded Kubuntu and was off to the races.  That was last night, and today I am fully configured, connected, organized, and just ecstatic to be rid of that monstrosity.  My hard drive seems like a vast open prairie with rolling hills now, rather than a landfill.  It's also nice to work on a computer where the hard drive doesn't spin when you are not doing anything.

Thanks for the support, everyone.  I hope I can contribute a little at some point.

Sincerely,
loclarkey
