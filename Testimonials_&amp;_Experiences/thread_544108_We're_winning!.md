### Post by whitecrow on 2007-09-05
Just a note to say that I have never before posted successes, only problems.  Despite this, I have a thriving little computer business in rural Oklahoma that has crept into nearby towns.  I have been running Ubuntu since it began and I love it!  Just enough geek to make me smile and just enough ease to make it simple for over ten of my customers to take the complete plunge.  No more windows!  Of course, this happened because they could not afford a Windows license, but I am heartened that they all believe the only difference is that the "start" button is in a different place.  LOL.  No matter their misunderstanding, every single one of my customers is happy.  Their music plays, their cameras work, their printers hum and their Linux perks!  Of course, in the spirit of things, I offer an unlimited period of no cost tech support for my Ubuntu installations.  Interestingly enough, I have more long, drawn out phone consults with my Windows customers, who pay through their collective noses to even dial my number.  

I think the final test occurred this weekend when I was trying to watch Oklahoma's football debut against North Texas.  My daughter's laptop croaked just as she was preparing for her first college tests.  In a pinch, I cobbled together a desktop running Ubuntu, hoping that she could make the stretch to Open Office.  When she talked to her professor about her computer crash and presented him with an Open Office "Word" formatted document, he noticed and asked her if she was running Open Source.  My kid stammered but told her prof she thought she was.  LOL.  He raised her grade even though she told him her Mom had built her "new" computer over the weekend. A couple of her classmates even came over to look at the "new look" my kid's computer had.  Even if I manage to fix the laptop, I'm not sure she wants to go back now.  

My point? Open Source is HERE and working in the most important venue of all...the college campus.  Yes, you gotta come up with the hardware.  But I have sent 12 college students off to study in far-away realms running Linux.  We are the future.  

Whitecrow

### Post by BrendanM on 2007-09-07
I'm a college student, but I also work for the IT department at my school. For some reason, all of a sudden this year I've noticed quite a few people around campus seem to be running Linux. Even though we don't officially support it, I've personally helped three people install Ubuntu so far this semester, and I helped another guy set up an XP-Vista-Fedora triple boot.
