### Post by charonred on 2009-08-28
Ditto what others have said; Ubuntu and other Linux distros are not clones of Windows, nor is Mac; if they were, there would be no point in having them - Linux is different, & viva la difference.

Like lots of others here, I've been a Windows user since 3.11, through '95, 98, 2000, and XP, but not Vista (it sucked).

I was getting ever sick n tired of all the crap you have to run to secure Windows; of the constant BSOD and the inevitable & gradually slow down of the OS over time requiring yet another re-install; and all the time MS and other vendors wanting ever increasing amounts of money to use their stuff.

Over the past 7 years or so, I also tried various Linux distros, but nothing was ever easy or appealing enough to take the leap; until I came across Ubuntu 8.04 July last year.

It's not been an easy road, but has been worth the move, learning as I go. I think I've learned and achieved more in the past 12 months than I did the whole time using Windows; and I have a much faster, friendlier, stable & reliable system than I ever had with any Windows version. Ubuntu is not perfect, but nor is Windows (and you have to buy that).

There's a few applications I use that don't have exact Linux equivalents, or won't install via Wine; so I run VirtualBox with XP-SP3 as guest to run those few apps.

And ditto with IE ... why? Having said that, I build websites and test in Firefox (which I preferred in Windows anyhow), Epiphany & Opera in Ubuntu; as well I have IE4linux (IE5.5 & 6) installed, and also test with Firefox & IE7 in VirtualBox XP - there are ways around all obstacles in Linux, just some take longer than others to figure out.

Persevere with Ubuntu for a while; install VirtualBox and drop XP or whatever Windows you prefer to use inside it. If you have problems with Ubuntu, ask for help, but be specific with your questions and provide as many details as possible.

ps - I have installed Ubuntu to 5 other PCs of friends & relatives; none of them are particularly computer literate as such (I was their Windows IT guy for every little problem), but all have embraced Ubuntu (Jaunty), and are making their own progress in using, understanding and fixing it; and they love it.
