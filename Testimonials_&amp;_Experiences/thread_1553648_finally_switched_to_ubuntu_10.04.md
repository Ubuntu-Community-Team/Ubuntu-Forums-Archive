### Post by LoneWolfJack on 2010-08-15
Well, I finally had some time to do the switch from 8.10 to 10.04

What I like
- ubuntu now correctly recognizes my adaptec raid by name (not that I was having problems with gutsy or intrepid... but it was never shown by name)
- the improved startup time
- I now have 4 "desks". yay!
- slight performance improvement
- flash can now be installed through software center even for 64bit. cool.

What I don't like
MAJOR
- the theme. jesus.
- the min/max/close buttons of the windows. I don't care what Marc wants to put in the upper right corner (fixed that by using "clearlooks" theme)
- the partition settings tool of the installer was MUCH more usable in 8.10
- ctrl+alt+backspace doesn't work any more

MINOR
- "show desktop button" still not working properly when turning on desktop effects (probably gnome issue)
- gnome toolbars still don't stick to their position when rebooting (probably gnome issue)
- samba not installed by default


so, my first impression is more positive than it is negative, but with the theme and the window icons we have a potential show stopper in the future. there's no way I'm gonna follow Marc in a disneybuntu adventure.
