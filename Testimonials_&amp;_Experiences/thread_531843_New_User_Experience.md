### Post by robbbzilla on 2007-08-22
I have used Linux now for about three years. My first Distro was Suse Linux 9.something. It was a horrible experience because nothing worked and I had formatted C!! 

Since then I have used just about every distro on Distrowatch website. I have seen a steady progression in Linux that has been really great to be a part of. Windows ME was the experience that pushed me into Linux. 

Mepis Linux was the first easy distro I used and was a great learning experience. Since then I have done almost everything I can do in Windows in Linux on distros from Slackware to Debian. I could never do Gentoo, my brain just did not work that well!

I have tried Ubuntu before Kubuntu and Kubuntu since. I always liked it but it just did not really work for me. I tryed Kubuntu 7.04 recently and I could never get my sound card to work. I was using Open Suse and Mandriva recently. I liked the pre-configured approach in Mandriva and all the proprietary stuff pre-loaded. A really easy install and the Control Center in Mandriva is very nice. However I felt the distro was slow and at times buggy. Recently I installed Debian Etch and really liked it but the wireless internet conection was problematic but I liked Gnome. I was a user of KDE for as long as I can remember. So I thought I would install Ubuntu.

Sound card gave me issues again. I decided to use the Ubuntu forums, what a thought, and after reading a few posts and trying a few things to no avail I found a post that was very easy and solved my problem. I then tryed to view proprietary stuff o the net and Ubuntu prompted me to install certain programs. I did and it worked. I have done a few different things and everything works very well. The system is fast and responsive. 

I am very impressed with the polish of the distro. I think that Debian has done a fabulous job with Etch but there are annoyances here and there that I do not have the time or expertise to fix. Using Ubuntu those things are fixed. The repositories are up to date and almost any package I want is available. Synaptic is probably the best package manager out there. I have heard rumors about Linspires Click n go or whatever it is called being used in Ubuntu. I hope they never use it. Having the multiverse repositories and synaptic has been the absolute best software management tool I have ever used on any OS!! I know it takes a little getting used to from Windows but once you use it once it is pretty easy. I think maybe some tips on using synaptic and a few reccomendations of popular packages for newbs would be a good idea. I am no open source finatic but I think the direction some Linux distros have gone like Linspire are just wrong o so many levels. Mandriva club also comes to mind and Xandros. I do not mind paying a nominal fee for a preconfigured system but the cost these guys are charging is a little much for outdated buggy software. Some of the best distros I have used have been completely free. I will say that Mandriva has worked with all the hardware I gave it and just worked. 

Back to Ubuntu, I can now see why it is so popular. I really like Mark Shuttleworth and his open source stance and his dedication to helping spread free software and making it really great!! 

I think this will be a long term relationship. I have to test my printer and camera and usb ports but so far so good!!

Now if iTunes would work in Linux and a couple of other programs I would never go back to Windows malware platform. 

Robzilla

### Post by robbbzilla on 2007-08-22
Another person said it. I have an iPod and want to use and buy music on iTunes. I also have a Zune and the same thing goes. 

Sure I could use Amorok and I like it as an application. Although even when I have used it it has been buggy. 

I am not saying that it is the fault of Ubuntu or Linux that Apple would not make an iTunes version for Linux as supposedly open source friendly as they are but it sure makes life difficult when you have a piece of hardware like a iPod. I think Apple should give back to open source because most of there OS and not there new file system is based on open source. Of course Apple and Microsoft need each other. They act like adversaries but they need each other to survive. If they both made their applications work in Linux no one would need or use their proprietary OS's. 

I would also like to know if there is a movie maker in linux. I am sure there is but I have not found one that is as intuitive as iDVD or Windows Movie maker. I would like to make a dvd with music and a slide show of my pictures. Any help and suggestions would be great!!

As time goes on Linux only gets better and when you have people like Mark Shuttleworth, IBM, SUN, etc, backing it the time of the big proprietary OS's is limited. 

I can't say how thankful I am for the GNU-Linux community and the companies and backers who support the people behind the scenes. Whether or not Linux becomes mainstream is un-important to me. I think the real goal is simply to make Linux the best it can be. Make it simple with advanced configuration. Make the best software apps out there. Refine and simplify. It is not dumbing down it is refining and improving technology. Ubuntu is going in this direction and that is one of the reasons it is so popular. Eventually when the apps are as easy to use and refined as apple apps and the OS is as easy to use as Ubuntu is then I think more people will migrate to it. 

It takes big numbers to make a change. The only way people will change is to make a better, easier product. Even then you will have to fight inertia. So I think the goal of making Linux compete with Windows and Apple is short sited. Just make it better. Continually improve it and simplify it. Not just the OS but the apps. Then the people will come. 

I am amazed at the progress of Linux. I am amazed at a free OS with the amount of useful software that is free. I am amazed with distros like Ubuntu that are such a pleasure to use. 

I love it. I tell others about Linux. I give them cds/dvds. 

Robzilla
