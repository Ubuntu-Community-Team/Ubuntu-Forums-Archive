### Post by HappyFeet on 2009-12-30
I heard that at gnome-look.org, that some malware was found. 

I also had an experience that I'm not 100% sure it was malware, but it sure seemed like it. I had installed a skin for vlc, didn't like it, and simply removed the file. Since you don't install skins in the traditional sense, you would figure that simply removing it would be the end of it. Not. The skin would not go away, and rendered vlc useless. To make a long story short, I did everything known to man to remove every possible instance of vlc, (did most of it by hand) until nothing was left. Then cleaned out /var/apt/cache, and any unused dependencies. Reinstalled a newer version of vlc, and lo and behold, I still had a useless vlc player. I *need* vlc to watch and record tv.

Fearing some form of malware, I rolled up my sleaves and did a full reinstall of ubuntu. The lesson I learned? **Neve**r install anything that is not in the official repos. End of story.
