### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-05-14
I went to the shop to buy a motherboard that I'd had my eye on. Not in stock. They did however have a premium model with wifi; I hadn't researched this different motherboard for Linux compatibility, but I took a chance.

The wifi worked out-of-the-box. No Restricted Drivers, no Ndiswrapper; just web-surfing straight from the live CD.

Bought a wireless card for a friend. It also works out-of-the-box.

Why not just research a wifi adapter that plugs into USB and is supported by Ubuntu out-of-the-box (or with a small amount of effort)? It might take 20 minutes to find something that a local store has that works with Ubuntu. That effort allows you to run Ubuntu, and these devices are usually pretty cheap.

Or is there another reason why you're not using Ubuntu?
