### Post by Mr. Picklesworth on 2008-08-30
Just today I accepted someone's wish for me to help fix her computer. Basically, Windows was in such a sorry state that I decided to run a system restore from scratch.

This involved popping in my Ubuntu Hardy Heron live CD to back up user documents to an external hard drive. Obviously the reason I accepted this job (given that I generally say "I don't know Windows") was because I felt it necessary to tout Ubuntu.

Anyway, she was watching this process and said suddenly "that's a great picture". I was gobsmacked! Dumbfounded! Amazed! Shocked!

Yes, this was *Ubuntu* -- on a very basic computer with xorg doing its low bit depth thing (thus the image being very oddly banded) -- being complemented for good looks.

My answer was "yeah, we were really happy when the art people came up with that". I didn't realize until after I said it how unique that line was. It got her attention and I managed to explain all about the open source methodology, free software and Ubuntu. In the end we decided not to install Ubuntu, instead to just restore Windows. (Two reasons: She owns lots of Windows software such as Office 2007, and she is currently a substitute teacher who may need to use different Windows-only software for the job).

This was a nice thing. I 'fixed' her computer (for a good price while pointing out the way that big box retailers gouge their customers for tech work!) so this ended on a good note. I pointed her to some free software such as Firefox (bless them) and GIMP. I talked about how the desktop computer is basically a warzone of corporations developing competing standards, where the person who suffers most is the end user who just wants a working product and has to keep paying more and more to get one. I gave her many examples of the Ubuntu Linux operating system, so she is now *well aware* of alternatives. She may not be using it right now, but being seen is key, here, to being noticed.

Err, back to my point: Kudos to everyone involved in creating the heron wallpaper. I think the end product, while debated, is ideal. Ken's (sorry, it was Ken's, right?) more vibrant version was cool, but it would not be coherent with the banding effect here. The more subdued one, however, still looks good even in ugly mode.

Here's hoping for another miracle image in Intrepid and beyond!
