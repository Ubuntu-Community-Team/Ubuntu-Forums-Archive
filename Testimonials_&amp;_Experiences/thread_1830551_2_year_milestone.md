### Post by yankeeboy254 on 2011-08-21
I had heard of it before. It was for the hardcore, the real geeks. It was a scary concept. I had spent all of my life on Windows. I followed the instructions to a letter. I burned the .iso to a CD, and put it in the drive. When I rebooted, I was greeted to a black screen, followed by a cursor, two white bars at the top and bottom... And slowly, everything loaded... The wallpaper, icons, everything. It was amazing. It had a weird name though. 

Ubuntu. How the heck do you pronounce this thing? uh-bun-too? oo-boon-too? oh-boon-too? I later found out that it is, technically, oo-boon-too. And then there was another amazing prospect: 9.04. That was the version number. And, it released every six months. Windows comes out every 4-5 years. And, nonetheless-was free! I ran the installer. It was quite easy and simple. But it stopped half way through, and was stuck. I tried twice more. 

Didn't work. I ended up with three Ubuntu partitions, no GRUB, and stuck on Windows. I deleted the partitions and stayed on Windows. 

Then, for my birthday, I got my laptop, that came with Vista. At this time I was so excited for Windows 7, which I would receive for free in a little over a month. But, I managed to successfully install Ubuntu 9.04 next to Vista. It was amazing. So much better than Vista. 

The rest is really history. After doing a little bit of distro hopping, through Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Mint, I'm now settled on elementary. Very sleek and fast. 

Two years of Ubuntu, Linux and Free Software, and no sign of turning back. I can't see myself using anything else. So here's to two more great years with Ubuntu, Linux and Free Software. :D
