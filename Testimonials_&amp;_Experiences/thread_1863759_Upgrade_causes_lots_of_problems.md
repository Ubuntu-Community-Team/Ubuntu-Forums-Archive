### Post by cswert on 2011-10-18
Hi!

This is just kind off a general complain - I will take a look for specific problems in other forums. But it's quite frustrating that upgrading to 11.10 left me with an unusable system:

First the upgrade caused errors that had to be solved manually (recovering console etc). OK - since I'm on Linux for 15 years know I'm used to that.

Than the wireless connection was lost (a rt3090 - yes I know the issues, but it's quite frustrating that I already did solve the problems - and now they are here again and no solution available so far).

And THAN the evolution upgrade killed a 10 year supply of mails - incoming, outgoing, folders - just everything. The "conversion" was a joke. Everything is gone! And I can't remember a set of about 60000 mails. 

And there a some some further annoying things: these fine toolbars on the desktop do not hide any more (I checked all the settings even in the config files - no result), all my icons in the toolbars are gone (yes - I'm on classic gnome), I cant' set the external screen resolution manually any more and thus some beamers do not work, all my mailfilters are gone.

This is so frustrating! 

So if you think about upgrading: Wait a least foru weeks for the major issues to be sorted out.

### Post by buddhuu on 2011-10-18
The experience varies greatly depending upon the machine that is running the OS and the method of upgrade.

I always do a fresh install rather than an upgrade per se. I've found the upgrade process less reliable than full install from scratch.

One of the reasons I use webmail rather than downloaded email is to ease OS upgrades and reinstalls.

For me, the installation of 11.10 went like clockwork and all is working very well. 11.04 gave me the opportunity to work through my initial dislike of Unity. I'm a happy Oneiric user.

Not gloating, just illustrating a contrasting experience.
