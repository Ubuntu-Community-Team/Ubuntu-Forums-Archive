### Post by jage on 2010-01-27
So I've been using Live CD of Ubuntu 9.10 (Karmic?) for a little over a week now.  I originally started just trying it out, but soon discovered some of my XP problems are probably from a failing harddrive.

I've been running from the Live CD for a few days as I formatted the HD to see if I could salvage it before ordering a new one.  First off kudos that I feel comfortable enough to skate by on the Live CD.  The only issue is I have to plug a network cable in as the wireless doesn't work.

I'm planning to dual boot, but most days I'm convinced that I'll be using Ubuntu for everyday things replacing Photoshop, Outlook and Quicken with GIMP, Thunderbird and ... well I can't remember the financial program.

Anyway, while I'm doing everything on Live CD, I'm using Firefox (Yes, I'm still on IE under Windows...) and one of the things that reverted to web only is my email (no point in setting it up until installation IMHO).

So... I'm typing a letter to an old friend in SquirrelMail and of course it's just a textarea for the subject.  I HATE HATE HATE losing things I've typed, and I've often lost things under Windows, no doubt.  I can't ever recapture the tone when rewriting, and often will abandon whatever the task was, even a heartfelt letter to an old friend.  I'm sure you see where this is going, but for me this was an epiphany.

I got distracted from my letter and opened some YouTube videos.  After a few the responses were strange and the inline ads seemed to hang the video, the whole thing started to lock up.  Clicks were so laggy I'd stopped and started, muted, unmuted and muted again as it choppily lagged so bad I couldn't tell how each would stop and the ad wouldn't close, nor the screen resize.

"Ah, this is familiar," was the unbidden thought.

Then it CRASHED.  Firefox went down in flames.

*THEN* I remembered my half-finished letter in the other tab.  I hadn't copied to the clipboard as was my normal modus operandi while using webmail.  While Ubuntu and Firefox were doing their things, asking for a password for the webmail, I was facing the reality that I would abandon the letter and the sentiment instead of trying to recreate what I'd written.  Looking back the whole death and rebirth of Firefox was a lot less painful than those in Windows, where IE might bring down the whole OS.

But that's not the point, the point is that when Firefox came back my letter was still there, complete, sitting in the text area waiting for me to continue.

I'm agast.  Somewhere deep inside I knew this was possible, had glossed over Linux not needing reboots and whatnot, but I'd never in a million years expected that it would keep entries in form fields... Not, I'd guess, because they were saved or stored elsewhere, but simply because that part of the process wasn't brought to it's knees by whatever failed in the YouTube tab of Firefox.

It's like, instead of dying a horrible death and taking everyone with it, it just fell and scraped it's knee but then got up and continued the game as if nothing had happened.

I'm sold.  I'm off the fence.  This one thing alone was worth it to me to do all the learning and use Ubuntu as my primary OS.   Awesome.  Awesome that it *just worked.*
