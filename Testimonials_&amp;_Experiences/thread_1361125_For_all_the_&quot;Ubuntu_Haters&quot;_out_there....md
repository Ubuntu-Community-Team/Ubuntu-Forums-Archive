### Post by eveningsky339 on 2009-12-21
Hi everyone.  I am a new Ubuntu user starting with Karmic, and [SIZE=5][FONT=Arial]I freaking love this OS!  

[SIZE=2]I have been rescued from Vista by a better, more user-friendly OS, and it didn't cost me a dime!  Whenever I have any sort of "issue", I just come here to get fast, free, and friendly tech support which inevitably solves the issue, no questions asked.

My only reservation was my status as a Flight Sim geek.  I installed FlightGear, but it just never jived with me.  I was thinking that I would have to work out some sort of dual-boot with my dreaded Vista, but no-- I have my old XP bootable disc, and I found VirtualBox.  Piece of cake!

Now, about the title of the thread...  I've noticed many Linux users who aren't happy about what Canonical is doing.  They say that Ubuntu is not following in the spirit of GNU/Linux-- too many GUI's, too much like Windows.  I would just like to know... are these guys functioning at full capacity?  Did they even read Ubuntu's mission statement?  The point of the whole thing is to make a **user-friendly, desktop-based **Linux distro to combat Window's monopoly.  Just because it is possible for the average, dumbed-down-by-Microsoft Joe to operate a Linux distro without taking a class on using the command line doesn't mean that Ubuntu is a bad thing!

Indeed, Ubuntu offers the best of both worlds: a sleek, efficient set of GUI's as well as the ruthlessly powerful terminal.  I could use one or the other however I choose.

So, in closing, if I am approached by a Linux elitist who has a lot to say about the heresy of Ubuntu, I will chuckle to myself and kindly direct them to Ubuntu's home page, where they can read the mission statement.  They will find that Ubuntu is doing exactly what it was meant to do.
[/SIZE][/FONT][/SIZE]

While reading I felt a sense of peace that I never felt before with an operating system and after a while I truly felt like I was home.

I really admire all the work going into the forums here and have exponentially expanded my horizons by just shutting up and reading.
