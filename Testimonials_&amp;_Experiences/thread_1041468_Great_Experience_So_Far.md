### Post by belmie on 2009-01-16
I tried Ubuntu last year because  my hard drive failed and I had papers due lol. It was the worst time for the failure, but I'm glad that it happened because I would've never burned that iso and ran Ubuntu from the live disc. I got all my papers done on time, and walked away with a great experience. 

My first impression was that Ubuntu was very dull visually. I soon bought a new hard drive and went back to XP. My main problem with Ubuntu was that my computer's wireless card wasn't recognized by Ubuntu. Linux became an afterthought to me since I usually run XP. I never have any problems with viruses or spyware or anything. I like to think I run a tight ship with XP lol. 

My computer has recently started to show its age. It's a Dell 9100 with a Pentium 4 and 1.5 GB Ram. It's been serving me well these last four years, but programs like Itunes, and even Firefox of late have been chewing through my ram and processor. It takes about a minute for Itunes to launch after I click the launcher. Don't even get me started on Microsoft Office.

I finally remembered Ubuntu, and installed it through Wubi. I installed it, and updated. My wireless card was detected and the drivers were installed which is a big deal for me. I was so happy. Everything is running so smooth. It's almost like I upgraded my memory it runs so smooth. When I boot up it's ready to go in no time. OpenOffice is great. Songbird is pretty cool. I got a couple books and have been having a great time learning about Ubuntu and Linux. It's been two weeks and I haven't even booted to XP. Using Ubuntu is making me second guess my decision about buying a Macbook. I don't know what to do now lol. I'm thinking about buying a cheaper, but same spec laptop and just running Ubuntu on it. I'm still not sure.

It has been great learning a new system. The only thing that I can maybe see as a potential problem is the terminal. I'm not a programmer, and have no programing experience at all. I'm just scared that I'll mess something up in there. But, like I said I'm still learning. Everything has been great so far and it keeps getting better. Thanks to everyone who donates their time in the forums to help. You guys are really appreciated! Sorry for the long boring post.
