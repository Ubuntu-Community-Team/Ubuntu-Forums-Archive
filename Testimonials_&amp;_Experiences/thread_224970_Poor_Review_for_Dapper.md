### Post by AdrianVeidt on 2006-07-28
Jem Matzan at softwareinreview.com posts a brief [hit piece]("http://www.softwareinreview.com/cms/content/view/44/1/") on Ubuntu 6.06. His final comments:

> After spending three days with Ubuntu Linux 6.06, and recognizing that a large percentage of first-time GNU/Linux users are trying out Ubuntu before other distros, I understand why many people think that GNU/Linux can be difficult to install, configure, use, and in general be "not ready for the desktop." Ubuntu Linux 6.06 is a step below Fedora Core in terms of usability, quality, and ease of use, but it's far below any modern commercial desktop GNU/Linux distribution like Xandros Desktop 4.0, SUSE Linux 10.1, or even Mandriva 2006 PowerPack Edition.
What I can't understand is why I seem to be alone in my assessment of Ubuntu 6.06. Everywhere I look, I see glowing reviews and "best of" awards and such. I wonder what computers those people used for testing? What other GNU/Linux distributions are they comparing Ubuntu to? Obviously they aren't Java programmers. As far as I'm concerned, Ubuntu Linux 6.06 doesn't live up to the hype. There's a lot to like about Ubuntu in general -- I mean, as far as distribution design is concerned -- but this release was poorly tested.

### Post by 23meg on 2006-07-28
To address the main complaints:

Java: Sun Java is in the repos, just install it.

Better testing: Won't happen in Ubuntu. The cycle is fixed at months, FIXED AT SIX MONTHS, I'm amazed at how many people fail to comprehend this simple fact. Limited time, limited quality. There will always be bugs. Only Microsoft and Debian have unlimited time; everyone else is mortal and has limited time and MUST resort to compromises.

ndiswrapper on default install: ..which means moving ndiswrapper to main, which is impractical if not impossible, and software that MUST work with non-free components won't fit well into the big picture.

Plugins: Why, when they're an apt command away? And honestly, what good is the mplayer plugin without non-free codecs? Heard of Automatix and BUMPS?

Flash and Adobe Reader? Non-free software on main? Again impossible. Asking for this is asking for Ubuntu not to be Ubuntu any more. Anyone who needs non-critical non-free components ON THE CD should move to Linspire or something similar, or make an Ubuntu derivative with all the non-free stuff they want and pay the f'n redistribution fees and be happy, but not ask Ubuntu to change its stance regarding free software.


It's so easy to write a "review" nowadays without considering or asking around to learn why certain choices and compromises are made. 

Bottom line: yet another underinformed "review".
