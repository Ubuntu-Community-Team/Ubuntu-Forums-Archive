### Post by Her Ghost on 2007-09-24
This isn't a request for help, more of a summary of my experiences and (what I hope may become) a general discussion about the 64bit versions of Ubuntu and Kubuntu (the two *nix distros I've tried).

My move to Ubuntu was inspired by the Vista backlash, coupled with the genuine belief that open-source is the big up-and-coming tech fad and I wanted to seize the opportunity to learn about it before it became de rigeur and I had to panic-learn just to keep up.

My experience so far has been mixed, but I have to say mostly positive; I have certainly learned a lot (how to RTFM for one thing :)) and I would say that I am better for it.  But...is this a new path for me in computing?

The answer would have to be a qualified no, at this stage.

See, the problem I have continued to run into is that I have an AMD64 CPU, so I've been using the 64bit versions of Ubuntu (orginally) and now Kubuntu.

Out of the box, both work pretty much fine.  Ubuntu didn't recognise my sound setup, but Kubuntu did, Kubuntu struggled with dual-screen for a while, whereas Ubuntu seemed to just get it done.  But the problem isn't with these "vanilla" builds, so much as when it comes to wanting to do something more with it.

Now, I'm not talking cutting edge in any way, and the fact that I have a 64bit processor is an accident of economics more than anything else, but it just seems that whilst Linux and Ubuntu is still the fringe of desktop computing, 64bit Ubuntu is the fringe of the fringe, and for a user can be mightily frustrating.

For instance:
Flash - there are loads of simple tutorials to get it working on the 32bit version, but the 64bit was a lot of messing about (thank you though, to the people who took the time to write the 64 bit tutorials that did eventually get it working on my rig)
Java - I can't upgrade beyond 1.4.2, which cuts me off from a whole raft of apps that I could use if I had 1.5 or greater - FreeCol...:guitar:  Again this, from reading, appears to be a tribulation for 64bit users more than for 32bit users
Wine - Won't just install, there are workarounds and tutorials available (again, thanks to the authors) but it's like trying to build a house on the sand.  Out of salt.  It's a development project anyway, so having to "botch it" to get it "working" doesn't necessarily get you running your Windows apps...
Beryl - Again, a bunch of workarounds and tutorials to get it working, but it was always temperamental with me, so I had to abandon it

Whereas the majority of people are sitting in the blissful ignorance of Windows-land, the fringes are embracing an exciting new world and community of Ubuntu and *nix in general.  It's a tougher existence to start with, but the awesome support usually means that this majority crowd on the fringe are making quick progress.  Unfortunately, the 64bit fringe of this community is still a bit "out there" for a novice user (like me), so I find myself in a tough situation:

Abandon Kubuntu and go back to Windows or try and stick it out a bit longer..

At this stage, I reckon I'm going to go with "a little from column A and a little from column B" - I miss playing games (occasional though it is, it's nice to be able to do it) and I miss trying new things on the net on-the-fly without having to spend an hour familiarising myself with the ins and outs of what's going on.  I also like to be able to go to websites and have their "features", however annoying, just work, again without the requirement of familiarising myself with another development lifecycle.  BUT, and this is the positive I have taken from my experience so far:

*nix is the future, as it being demonstrated mightily with 32bit.  It's just that for a 64bit guy, it's still a bit too difficult for me to sort out yet.


Sorry for going on a bit, and thanks to everyone that has helped me over the last few months.

Her Ghost
