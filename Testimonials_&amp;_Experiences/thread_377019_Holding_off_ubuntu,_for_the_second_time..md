### Post by Zsev on 2007-03-05
Ok, i'm switching back to XP after trying out ubuntu and kubuntu. My first install was 2 years ago where there were no support for NTFS drives. I immediately deleted my linux partition upon discovering that, and fast foward 2 years, itching hands got me installing kubuntu again. 

I like how everything works straight from the LiveCD and the thrill of using a new interface. Using the console and executing commands isnt all that bad once i got used to it, and the add/remove programs feature was just brilliant. 

I must admit i'm a little bit surprised how kubuntu automatically managed to get my internet working through the university network which required some dirty configuration using windows XP. 

So after all that pros, why did i return to my trusty XP ? One sole reason really, i cant for the love of god get my radeon x1950pro drivers to work properly. Surfing the net and watching movies are choppy as hell, and using latest drivers freezes kubuntu on boot screen. I must have rebooted a million times editing the xorg.conf trying to get it work, but to no avail. I have tried every single guide online, and read every single topic in the forums and still no go. 

Not pointing fingers at ubuntu/kubuntu though, i'm pretty clear about how shitty ATI driver support is for linux. My bad luck for buying a new video card too. 

Oh well, hopefully things are better in next release of feisty fawn. Till then i'm a happy XP camper.
