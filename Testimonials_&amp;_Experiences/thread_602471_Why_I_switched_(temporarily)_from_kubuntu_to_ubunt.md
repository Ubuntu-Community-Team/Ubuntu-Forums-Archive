### Post by Rumo on 2007-11-04
I'm a big fan of kde. The applications are top-notch and it's possible to configure almost everything. However with the switch to gutsy I also switched to gnome.

First why I like ubuntu:
ubuntu 7.10 really gives a polished impression. Nice default theme, good selection of 3D effects (no wobbly windows) and tracker desktop search is actually quite good.

Now to kubuntu:
kubuntu 7.10 has also made some progress: the kubuntu-restricted-extras metapackage is great as it simplifies the installation of codecs significantly (like ubuntu, kubuntu will propose to install this package if you need it). Dolphin (file-manager) is also very nice. And I LOVE yakuake (although it's not in the default installation).

However strigi desktop search sucks at the moment. I can't possibly understand why it was chosen to be installed by default. It uses a lot of cpu-power so the fans in my laptop are spinning constantly. And the strigi frontend is a joke. Clicking on it will open konqueror where you can enter a search word - the html page is so ugly it hurts. At best it looks like a pre-beta version. Strigi is totally useless right now. Even beagle was better (kerry, the kde beagle frontend, was actually quite nice). And the theme! Ok, not really an important point but I hate it.

I am aware of the fact that the kde developers are working on kde 4 right now so I did not expect anything fancy from kubuntu 7.10. What I did expect was a rock-solid kde-version of ubuntu. Not a testing field for the upcoming kde 4 search engine.

In conclusion, I will use gnome for the time being and see what hardy heron has to offer. I'm also very curious about kde 4.

### Post by linuxlizard on 2007-11-06
I switched from kubuntu to ubuntu for gutsy as well- have been using kubuntu the last 3 releases. 

I switched because I was tired of setting stuff up. Ubuntu seems way ahead of kubuntu for ease of installing 3d drivers, compiz, codecs, etc, and has for the past couple of releases, and I just don't see a good reason why that is. Kubuntu should be able to have the same features- they aren't kde or gnome specific. 
I like the apps and panels in kde more- the dividers and ends in gnome panels make them look semi-retarded when you make them transparent. But it's been quite a while since I last used gnome, and I say it's improved a lot the past couple of years- used to hate it, now I've no real problem with it, so I may stay here unless kubuntu decides to catch up to ubuntu when it comes to easy setup of the stuff I mentioned after installation.
