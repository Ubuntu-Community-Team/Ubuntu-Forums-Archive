### Post by armandh on 2008-04-29
clear to me anyway, that as Ubuntu grows, there will always be undiscovered, untested, and unfixed situations pre release. [can you say vista SP1] the corollary is if you have a mission critical machine wait for the patches then test in parallel with your working OS. 

I am an Ubuntu Noob user. It just works for me. but I have been lucky too. My first Ubuntu was a little less than a year ago and 7.04 was fairly polished by then. winning the "equipment lottery" where everything worked helped to win my support.

yes there are frustrations [mostly unsupported printers for me] but lets be reasonable, If you are willing to jump in to the deep end of the pool you better know how to swim. [I am hanging around the float rope.] I have found drivers for printers so Ubuntu beats Vista on that one. 

the wife's lap top now dual boots vista/ubuntu 8.04RC and she uses ubuntu [she hates vista's constant updating interfering with her mailing/surfing.] 

my son the gamer is stuck with xp-sp2 as is my work box. 

my fun machine is Ubuntu-7.10/xp and many of the work bench castoffs [from years of replacing/collecting] are ubuntu only, after discarding the genuinely disadvantaged XP1.

so how do I rate 8.04 so far....
as Bobby Bare sang "there ain't no ten" But I give it a 9.8 !!!!
