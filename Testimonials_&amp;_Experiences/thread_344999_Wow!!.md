### Post by some_random_noob on 2007-01-23
I know... I explained the installation process as I installed it for someone and I just ended up saying stuff like "then you choose your country... then you type your name and user+pass..."

Its just as easy as the Windows installation. I find the partition manager the most technical - even then its still really basic. The thing is that with Windows you need to install all of your drivers, most people lose their driver disks thinking they'll never reformat so Linux is the REAL user friendly installation. I laugh at the people who recommend XP for home users. Stupid people sometimes say Linux is for geeks... ya de dah... but honestly, try explaining internet security on XP to someone who knows nothing about computers. "Ok, you need a really fast internet connect to get your patches. Heres five AV programs I've installed for you and heres the update button for all of them... make sure you don't open any pages with java, activex, javascript or font types. Do scans every ten seconds and reformat every month..."

... Linux is so much easier to install and maintain. Unfortunately Windows has the high ground because its rammed down everyones throats. I have yet to hear someone say that Linux is hard to install or hard to maintain. 

Just remember the important rule that most people forget when using Linux: have fun! :D
