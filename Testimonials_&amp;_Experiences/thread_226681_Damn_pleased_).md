### Post by x64Jimbo on 2006-07-31
You will soon find that the permissions rubbish is what makes Linux so much more secure than Windows. In Linux you can be a lot more careless about what you run as a user account, because the worst damage it could do is to your personal files and settings. You need root access to foul anything up on a systemwide level, and you have to get root access on purpose, as opposed to Windows. 
I've only been using Kubuntu as my primary OS for a few months, but I already feel very at home. Just a little getting used to, and you'll be way more secure than you'd ever be in Windows.

### Post by aysiu on 2006-07-31
For single-user computers, damage to your personal files and settings *is* the worst damage you can do. Make regular backups and encrypt (or don't store on your computer) any extremely sensitive personal data.

And... keep having fun! Now you and your boyfriend can have Linux fun *together*.
