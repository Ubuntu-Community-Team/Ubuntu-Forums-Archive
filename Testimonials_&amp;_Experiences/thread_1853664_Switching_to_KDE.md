### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2011-10-02
I recently started a thread about my disdain for both Unity and Gnome Shell, which as of this month will be the only desktop options available in Ubuntu.  I'm pretty disappointed with this move, but hey... I guess that's progress in some people's eyes.  My thread ended up causing some flames and ruffled a few feathers but as a Linux user I feel that it's important to speak up about the direction that the desktop takes.  After all, this isn't Microsoft.

That being said, I've been an avid GNOME user since I switched to Linux with Ubuntu 7.10.  I've tried KDE several times, first with KDE 3.5 which I just didn't care for and then with KDE4 which in my experience was an absolute disaster, the closest thing to Vista that Linux ever had.  However, being fed up with the so called "improvements" in GNOME I decided I had to do something.  I did a little distro hopping, but ultimately ended up giving Kubuntu 11.04 a shot and I must say that I was absolutely amazed at the speed, flexibility and appearance of the desktop.

Dolphin can be configured to act like Nautilus Elementary.  I can still keep my global menu.  I can put my dock on the bottom where it belongs, not on the left.  After compiling "I Hate The Cashew" from source, I was able to get rid of the last eyesore on the desktop keeping me from having the computing experience I am used to.  I can say with 100% honesty that I am surprised and very happy with KDE.  I will probably be staying here for a long time (maybe another 5 years if Gnome 3 takes that long to lure me back)

### Post by Juan Largo on 2011-10-04
The Kubuntu desktop can be customized to look and do almost anything you want.  You have a choice between the Classic Menu and the Kickoff Menu, and between Desktop View and Folder View.  You can even make Kubuntu look a lot like Windows, Gnome, or OS X.

In addition to that, you can locate the Panel on the bottom of the screen, the top, the left, the right, or on all four edges at the same time.  You can hide the panel(s) or have no panels at all.  Plus there are widgets galore.  

I like how the 3D desktop effects are integrated right into Kubuntu without having to deal with Compiz, how smooth and seamless the effects work, and how easy they are to configure.  

Of course a lot of it depends on your hardware, so not everyone will get the same good results that I did.
