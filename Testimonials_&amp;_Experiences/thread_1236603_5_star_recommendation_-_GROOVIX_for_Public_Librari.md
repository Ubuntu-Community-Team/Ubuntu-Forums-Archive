### Post by marianlibrarian on 2009-08-10
I am the library director of a very small public library in southeast Alabama population less than 5000. About two years ago, I upgraded our public access computers and our staff computers from windows 98 to Ubuntu. Things were great for about a year. From 2005 - 2008 we had a 406% increase in public access computer usage. We averaged about 5 people per day in 2005 to 35 to 45 people per day in 2008.

Then we began struggling with patron sabotage of the desktop. Some intentional but most just bored people piddling with options and walking away when the computer freezes.

Previously, I had Ubuntu Dapper Drake, Hardy Heron, and Kubuntu. The Kubuntu was my attempt to create a Kiosk computer. This failed in an abysmal way. 

I desperately needed a solution. I searched through these forums and received advice and admonishements, but not a solution that worked.

I googled an application called Webconverger. It is an operating / kiosk program that works from the CDROM. The computer is booted from the CD and it opens up a web page. This was closer but I needed access to office applications also. While there are Google Documents and Zoho, they were not what we needed.

In one of my posts "Call for Help" someone provided a link to a webzine published by Ubuntu. It was about a library in 2007 that used Ubuntu on all of their PACs - 300 of them!! They also had staff devoted to only taking care of these machines and two of them were former Unix administrators. So I thought, "Kudos for them, but I am all alone." The only IT person I have available is ... me. I went back to Webconverger and in my conversations with the developers, one of them suggested Groovix. That was familiar because it was the same solution that the Howard County Public Library used in 2007. It is Ubuntu with a Kiosk twist. I reread the article. I called the Groovix company and the nicest guy named Mike sent me a link to download the iso. 

The iso is HUGE. I had to put the file on a flash drive and make bootable DVDs from home. I brought the DVDs that I made and booted up 3 of my machines that had been trashed by patrons. I let the patrons use them for a day and had no problems. YIPPEE!

There are a couple of settings that I have to reset every morning. 
[LIST]
[*]One is the timer for each session. It is set for 30 minutes and I have to change this to 60. 
[*]Then I have to "reinstall" our network printer.
[/LIST]
However, I installed Groovix to one of the PACs on the harddrive so these settings are now permanent. 

So we now have 5 WORKING PACs. Not one complaint!!! "Be still my heart." I am SO glad I did not give up. I have cried and said bad words but did not give up. There have been so many people on this forum who went the extra mile for me. You were kind and patient with me. Thank you all so very much. I have much to learn. It will be very helpful for me time wise not to have to constantly watch over my public access computers. Now I can put up those shelves in my children's room, install the storage shelves in my new (1 year old) storage room, finish an inventory that I started a year ago, clean my bathrooms, vacuum and dust and... go home at the end of the day feeling like I accomplished something positive!! :)
