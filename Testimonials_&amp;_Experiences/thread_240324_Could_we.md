### Post by joshier on 2006-08-20
My experience with ubuntu so far has been very good, I see massive amounts of user contribution, problems being fixed, ideas being discussed and a whole wealth of user interaction. 
Something that we really don't see in the windows department.

Open source is really coming together now, and the mass population of online users left in the dark are slowly being shown the light of the benefits of this way, and at the quickest rate there has ever been.

But I have been wondering...

What if developers could devote a little more?... and actually get some money from it?

Yes, of course, we have to bear in mind the whole main purpose of ubuntu – for it is free and I am very happy with that, but what if we could have a system, set up for developers which would give them the money that they have earned?

In my personal experience, there are many developers who would be more than willing to work on ubuntu almost full time, and I see that it could work very well, if setup correctly.

So, here's my idea...

The current developers receive money from the work that they have done, this could be bug fixing, feature implementation and anything else that the user base in the forums want – if it is agreed on by the majority of users.

The sole basis of money income they receive would be dependant on a donation box. If the donation box should ever go empty, then so be it, but the developers must bear in mind that their work they have done is for ubuntu, a non-profit organisation – and any money they get – is a bonus.

In my eyes, this would be a huge encouragement to the developers, and we would need to think of a quota which works with the level of donation income per X amount of months.

I do see some issues with this idea at this very early stage, but I honestly feel we could iron them out if we all worked together, much like the ubuntu project itself.

I'd love to discuss this futher, thank you for your time.
