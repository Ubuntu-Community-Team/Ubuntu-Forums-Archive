### Post by an.erni on 2011-02-16
First of all, sorry for the title but I want to gain some audience for this issue - especially since it's a serious one!

Sad to tell you guys:
- One week ago, I bought a MacBook Air
- And (even worse) after using Ubuntu since Dapper Drake (back in 2006!), I am not willing consider it as my weapon of choice for the next future


Of course, this is not the place to discuss the performance of an Apple product. However, I am willing to name the main reasons (which should only outline my personal point of view) for me to switch:

1. My hardware was old and I had to buy a new one anyhow (unfrequent crashed, probably caused by cracks on the mainboard). So I had to spend some money, so I was looking into an ultra portable laptop without any performance flaws.

2. As I will go back to uni in a couple of months, I was looking for a solution where I can open even the newest MS-Office documents. I wouldn't use MS on my own, but I expect lots of fellow student to neglect using OpenOffice - and I have to collaborate efficiently. As well, I won't be able to fiddle around at my system, just because I won't have the time for that.

3. I really loved the idea of an opensource community (that was the main reason why I originally chose Ubuntu). Unfortunately, I got really disappointed recently and did not really feel that kind of support or enthusiasm I expected / required (please refer to my older posts).

=> In particular the third point prevented me from opting for a linux-solution.
The following considerations (i.e. "Why Mac, why Air, ...") should not be part of this thread.


In conclusion, I won't change my mind just because twenty or twohundred Ubuntu-Geeks think I should... I just wanted to give something back to the community - and what's worthier than a healthy and honest feedback?
I hope that my experiences can help the community to evolve from the current state and finally reach a higher level.

I wish you all the best!
Cheers, Andy
