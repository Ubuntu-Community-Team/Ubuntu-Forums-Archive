### Post by kainalu on 2011-06-17
Aloha,

I am Kainalu. I was at one time a loyal and supportive Ubuntu user. I remember a time, around 7.04, when I first started using the Ubuntu OS. At the time, I was running Windows XP, and although I had experimented with Linux, it wasn't right for me. When I first discovered Ubuntu, I found that, compared to Windows, it was more secure, easier to use, loads more customizable, and easy to tinker with safely. It also ran fast, smooth, and could do things with great ease and fluency. If you had any trouble, the Forums and community was able to help, and cared.

Now, with 11.04, I face a dilemma. My favorite OS, customizable no more. Speedy? Only if my Core i7 is supposed to act like my Core2 Solo. Reliable? No, I can't even copy files to a flash drive any more. It takes too long, and too many resources. At one time, I could show off my computer, and get a ready convert, just install and smile. Now, everyone I have shown in the past year has had far more problems than the new Windows OS (7), me included. They think the Desktop is outdated, and if I show them unity, they wonder why their computer looks like a cell phone. Gnome 3? Cant even change the appearance settings. They all left in disgust, shaking their heads in bafflement. What do I see in this restrictive, clumsy OS, they wonder? I cant even change the screen layout or hide the panels. It crashes with basic programs. It doesn't work with a lot of the things called normal now, like video chat with multiple chat protocols. Skype? Joke. Multiple wallpapers that change regularly? Learn XML. Want to eliminate common problems? Learn Bash. Real well. Want Remote Desktop? Good luck. Hope you like VNC. Have a nice video card? Have fun installing and setting up right the first time.Want help? HAH, good luck. Try finding any answers. The Forums? Most of my complaints (and my customers') go unanswered, or someone copies and pastes silly nonsense that can be found on Google, 4 years out of date. File a bug. Go ahead, Ill wait. It will either go unanswered, Marked “out of date” “not our problem” or some other silly nonsense, and get voided out. I use an X52 Joystick. It stopped working several years ago, shortly after I bought it, because of 4 lines of kernel code that got changed. I had to compile a whole kernel, because someone decided to break support for it. The next time the kernel updated? Same thing. I still can't use it, to this day. It worked when I bought it. Why is this? What about that flash drive issue? It affects lots of Linux distros. Lots of people. When I built the computer, flash drives worked great. Then the kernel updated. Now I don't think flash drives are useful on this machine at all. The sad thing is it is still new. Only two months old.

Forcing changes on users, and alienating long standing members. Too much change, too fast. The Linux OS has been known for its legendary performance, Rock solid stability, and customizability. Not ooo's and aaaah's. Shiny toys that break easy are MS's and Apple's game. My Linux Desktop shouldn't crash like this. I feel like a test dummy. See how this one will crash, my friend!

After shaking my head for the past year and a half, my decision is final. I am leaving Ubuntu, and most likely Linux. If I can't have my computer running it's best, why own it? I will remember a time when I could have my old laptop, behind current times, blow away modern machines, and not this current mess, that doesn't run well on my top-notch (and fully Linux compatible) computer. After a time, maybe I will see that it is once again :

Customizable, Fast, Secure, Easy, and MY OWN.

Until then, I hope you all the best. I'll come for a visit some day soon, maybe it will look better.

--Kainalu
