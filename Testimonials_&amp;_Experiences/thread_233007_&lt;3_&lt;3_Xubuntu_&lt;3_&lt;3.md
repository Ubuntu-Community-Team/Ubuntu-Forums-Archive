### Post by Modki on 2006-08-09
Great OS! Easy to follow detected 99% of my hardware and supported my 802.11g PCMCIA card right off the bat! I don't know if that's a credit to Belkin or Ubuntu. I'm an advanced user so partitions, network settings, etc were a breeze.

XFCE makes me feel at home. In the first 2 days I had Xubuntu on my Laptop I tweaked the settings quite a bit. My Applications menu reads "Start" and my window theme is set to RedmondXP. I've made the "My Computer" and "My Documents" shortcuts on the desktop and even got my "Bliss-like" wallpaper up. I let my friends and family get on it and so far none have noticed that it's not windows!!

The support is so great here with tons of helpful advice and people I've even gotten (i hope, i'll try tonight) the last 1% of my hardware setup. Ubuntu just works most of the time though. Everyday I learn something new, dunno if thats a good thing to say about it but I like to learn new things!

This is running on a 333Mhz / 96MB laptop and it performs so much better than the 98SE that came with it. I got it for free from my dad because it had a broken LCD Display. 25 minutes with some solder and electrical tape and performs like new (New was 1998 teehee) I got the Laptop for free so I figured Free OS!

### Post by mostwanted on 2006-08-14
> **bodycoach2 said:**
> How did you create "My Documents" and "My Computer" shortcuts on the desktop. I've looked all around the menu, and obviously have missed the trick.

Right-click on the folder, select "create shortcut" or "create reference" (I'm using Danish Ubuntu so I'm not sure), drag and drop to your desktop.

OR

Right-click on your desktop, select "Create launcher" and you get some more customisability options. The command to be supplied is ```
nautilus /path/to/folder
```.
