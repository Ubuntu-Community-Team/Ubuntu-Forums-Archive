### Post by gerowen on 2006-10-26
Yeah Ubuntu has really impressed me in the 24 hours I've been using it.  I had a horrible time at first, turns out I just had the PCI address of my video card wrong.  After I solved that issue, I had no problems whatsoever.  It picked up my app shortcuts and volume control buttons on my keyboard, which Fedora Core 5 didn't do.  The fonts are much smoother, and dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg is the pwnzorz.  It makes it "much" easier to reconfigure xorg without opening it in a text editor.  I know I know, there are probably ways to make Fedora Core 5 operate as smoothly as Ubuntu but in Ubuntu I didn't have to spend hours figuring it out, it "just works".  The only area I can find it lacking in, is where a "lot" of Linux distros are lacking, multimedia codecs, but we've got proprietary software to blame for that.  Depending on how UT2k4 and WoW run on this after I get them running, my 80 gig Windows HDD might become one big ext3 storage drive.
