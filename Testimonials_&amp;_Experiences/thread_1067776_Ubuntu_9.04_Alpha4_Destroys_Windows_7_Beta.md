### Post by HavocXphere on 2009-02-13
> **moster said:**
> Numbers do not lie
Thats the point. They do in a VM.

e.g. program measures how many floating point operations can be performed in 20 seconds. The problem is, that under one OS, those program-preceived-20 seconds are actually say 35 in realtime (i.e. physical stopwatch). So it can put a lot more calculations into those "20" seconds. And worse, that clock drift differs across the different O/Ss (both guest and host), so they aren't even handicapped equally.

The X operations ***per second*** part is what makes it all useless....because time, as some Physicist was fond of claiming iss relative.

Some VM configurations try to minimize clock-drift by updating the clock every few seconds. Not helping either. But this would again depend on whether or not the guest OS add-on VM helpers were installed on each OS.

Nonetheless, chances are that the outcome is similar if the test is performed under less ludicrous circumstances....because we all know that the one OS is superior.:twisted:
