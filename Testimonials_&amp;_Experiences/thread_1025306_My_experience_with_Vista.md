### Post by kjb34 on 2008-12-29
I ordered an Everex computer with Vista pre-installed from Newegg for my mother. It came in the mail today so I took by her house to set up a dual boot system. I swapped out the PSU for one that my mom had previously purchased and installed a second hard dirve from her old computer. Anyway I start up the computer the first I notice how it takes to boot. After a few minutes Vista finally starts so I set up the user accounts and what not. So then I go try the internet, it doesn't work so then I go into the start menu and go the internet connection thingie and start that up. Everything goes okay it says that it can't connect to the internet. I try it a few more times same thing. I called up the ISP and the username and password because it kept asking for that. That didn't work so I cut off the modem and the computer and restart, nothing still. I call the ISP back and finally they take me through a few steps and voila the internet is working after two hours. Now when I installed my Ubuntu it immediately recognized my internet connection and connected without me doing anything. I expected the same thing of Vista but it wasn't so. I was very disappointed with Vista.

### Post by Pompeius on 2008-12-30
Your mileage may vary but...

I've really had no problems with Vista at all. Whenever I have, it's _always_ been my fault. I've had a very positive experience overall and would recommend it to anyone in the market for a Windows PC. However, I realize it may not be for everyone; some have issues with the proprietary nature of it, others have extremely frustrating problems that are never resolved, others just have so-so or poor experience with it. It's hard to get everything to work right with the large diversity of hardware it must support, and in a different way from Linux.

I still think that Windows is the best choice for your average user with a few caveats: they must practice safe browsing and downloading. Run a virus scan on any files downloaded, esp. executables! It's not that difficult. I think the main problem most Windows users have is with the depredations introduced by the OEM installations, viz crapware to no end. If every new PC came with a clean installation of Windows I believe that many of the issues would be solved.

I myself installed Ubuntu 8.04 onto a small partition on my laptop just a few days ago, so I am by no means an experiences user. I have experienced frustrating problems in some respects but I think in time I'll get used to it and be quite comfortable. One thing to note is that for every problem I've experienced with it (albeit minor ones), the recommended actions involve use of the terminal. I'm fairly comfortable using the command line in Windows, so it's a not a big deal to me, but for an inexperienced or new user it can be intimidating. I think Linux has a way to go (a short way though!) before it will be ready for common usage.

At any rate, if I had to choose an alternative to Windows, it would definitely be Linux. The closed environment of the Mac is completely unacceptable.

Just my two cents.

### Post by Pompeius on 2008-12-30
You do have a point there, but- people also run other software, and not just word processors. In my experience- this is just on the Windows platform- many of the FOSS implementations simply don't stack up with commercial and proprietary counterparts. They lack features, or are simply poorly coded and/or difficult to use. This is not to say I don't use open source- I use GIMP,7-zip, Sumatra reader, Notepad ++, GraphCalc, among others, and have tried many other fine ones which I ultimately don't really have a use for.

Mind you I think that the proprietary model is dying and the OS will reign soon enough, and it's a good thing in my opinion. 
If your 'average user' has no other demands on their PC than just an internet portal, then yes, it will work fine for any such user. That is not to denigrate the work that the open source community, they do things excellently and in many cases better than commercial developers. But in critical areas there needs work. For power users, perhaps nothing is better than Linux.
