### Post by lordofkhemenu on 2006-03-07
Man, it's good to be back...
I first installed Ubuntu on my home computer back when Warty first came out and was pleasantly surprised (I've used Linux 100% for a long time..RedHat 4.something, OpenLinux, Suse 7...) at how well it worked and was put together.
Long story longer - circumstances intervened, computer died, only had my work laptop and had to work with Windbloze XP. A Gateway 450ROG was taken out of circulation and was given to me last week (working in the IT department does have its advantages).
XP? Outa there! Linux? Yeah, baby. Yeah!
I tried installing Mepis but the wireless configuration was a b!tch. I had to use a PCMCIA wifi card.
Downloaded and installedBreezy. **EVERYTHING** worked "out of the box", including the *integrated* WiFi.
The sound works, but is extremely 'staticy'...it was this way under windbloze, from other search results, I found this is apparently the built in speakers themselves and the problem goes away if connected to a dock or maybe external speakers.
Funny thing ("funny peculiar", not "funny haha") was that the PCCard only got 86% signal being only 10 feet from the wireless router :???:. The integrated has been slamming at 100% without tweaking.

It's good to be back home :mrgreen:.
