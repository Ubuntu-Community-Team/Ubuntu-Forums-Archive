### Post by empty_spaces on 2010-08-24
I've gone through a whole bunch of distros for my Acer Aspire 532h-2588 netbook (10.04 Desktop and Netbook, Maverick Alpha, Mint 9, EasyPeasy 1.6, Moblin, Meego, WattOS, Jolicloud 1.0, PCLinuxOS, Puppy) and every one of them had issues. 

Either the internal mic would not work right or needed a workaround, or the touchpad scroll had to be enabled through config files, etc, etc.Some like 10.04 and EasyPeasy would get to a desktop, but the mouse pointer would go totally haywire making them unusable.
To top it all off, none of the above distros had anywhere near the battery life of Win7. Where Win7 displayed 8 hrs, I was getting an average of 5.5 hrs with the above distros.

Then I tried Peppermint. Everything worked out of the box, including touchpad scrolling, internal mic (skype), etc and I was getting 6-6.5 hrs of battery life. I installed laptop-mode-tools and Powertop to further help battery life, and now I'm able to get 7.5-8 hrs with Peppermint.

Of course, there are still a few things that do not work with any distro period, such as the card reader and the wireless Fn switch, but I can live with those. 

I'll probably test drive maverick and the new EasyPeasy when they come out, but for now, I'm more than happy with Peppermint.

Edit: The 532h-2588 is a pretty good netbook, that I initially got for $279 from Office Depot, but was later able to pricematch to $199 when Target had it for sale a month ago.
