### Post by shadowmane on 2011-10-14
I like Unity.  However, I'm not sold on it thoroughly.  Let me explain.  I began using Linux back in the mid-90's.  I started on Red Hat with KDE.  For years, I loathed the Gnome environment.  For years I have wondered what the attraction to it was.  You have this bar across the top that does the exact same thing as the bar across the bottom in Windows.  In fact, in Unity, you still have that bar across the top.

I used KDE to begin with because it looked so close to Windows, that I could just migrate and not look back.  Unfortunately, that computer died, and I DID have to look back.  I had to use my dad's computer for about 3 years.

On my return to Linux, I used SuSe with KDE.  I still couldn't get my head around Gnome.  It just looked too ugly to me.  I used SuSe up until about 3 years ago, when my mother board died, and I had to start replacing stuff on my homebuilt computer.  Luckily, I only had to replace the motherboard and the DVD drive.  But in the interim, I had an old Acer laptop with Windows XP on it.  I used it until I could get off my lazy butt and set up my desktop again.  Only this time, I had a built in wireless card.  I couldn't get SuSe to recognize the card no matter how much I tried.  So I decided to give Ubuntu a try.  I hated Gnome, but I didn't like Kubuntu (I don't like the name... its as simple as that).

So, now I have this system (Ubuntu 10.10), and I'm having to learn this new user interface (Gnome).  The wireless works with one minor tweak.  Of course, it wasn't long before I got an external wireless card which solved everything along that front.  Not long after that, Canonical starts playing with the user interface.  All of the sudden, now ugly old Gnome has given way to Unity.  Okay, so I tested Unity, and went back and forth between it and Gnome.  Then, I started playing around with Gnome 3 and KDE4 as well.

Out of them all, I like Unity the best.  Of course, I like Enlightenment much better.  But that's another story.  This is about Gnome.  Anyway, I have read over the last year all of the crying and whining about how Gnome 3 and Unity don't do what the old Gnome could do.  Who cares.  I hate Gnome anyway.  But with Unity, I can at least half-way like it.

Which brings me to Ubuntu 11.10.  I like it.  For what I do on the computer, so far, it handles itself well.  It just works.  So... please forgive me if I'm not someone who thinks its the end of the world if Ubuntu 11.10 with Unity looks like a tablet computer.  I fully intend to build a multi-touch table some time in the near future anyway, and lining up user interfaces that can be used on it will be much easier if Gnome and Canonical continue at the pace they are going.  After all, I'm looking for my dream system.  So far, it hasn't materialized with the status quo.  Ubuntu and Gnome are breaking the status quo.  I applaud them.
