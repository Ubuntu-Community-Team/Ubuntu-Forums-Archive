### Post by carusoswi on 2009-02-07
I go back and forth between loving and hating Ubuntu.  For my multimedia work, it can be, at times, most frustrating.  I have never been able to get audio features to work on my machine in Linux.  My machine has two sound cards, but I cannot record via line in on either of them.  There is no comprehensive video editing package for Ubuntu/Linux.

OTOH, I cannot complain about a lack of photo editing software anymore, because, between my getting more familiar with Gimp and it getting a significant upgrade, my needs are adequately met with respect to photos.

The reason for this thread is to express appreciation for a feature that I don't often use, but, whenever the task arises, Ubuntu is the answer for me.  That feature:  File Handling (as Ubuntu calls it).

I pulled out a half dozen old external drive enclosures (with drives) to browse their contents and back up whatever I found of importance.

A couple of the discs were packed full enough of good stuff that I just decided to copy the entire disc to one of my 750 GB external drives.

In XP, when I try to copy the contents of a drive, the process invariably stumbles over one or two files that appear corrupted.  When that happens in XP, of course, your choice is to try again or cancel.  Cancelling ends the file transfer process, so you either have to determine where to resume or try copying the entire disc again.  If the file is corrupt (or reports itself so) again, Windows stops the process again.

In Ubuntu, this sort of error is handled with a screen that allows you better options, one of which is to skip over the bad file and just keep on copying . . . much better than the Windows setup.

I also appreciate the way in which Ubuntu handles mounting and unmounting of drives.  You right-click on the drive icon, select "Mount" or "Unmount" as appropriate, click, that's it.  No hunting around for a little green icon in the XP task bar, and no sitting there waiting for XP to release the drive only to spit out a message that it cannot be topped because it is in use (even when it isn't in use).

That's it.  Nothing earth-shattering.

Just my two cents.

Caruso
