### Post by LeDechaine on 2011-11-25
Ubuntu, or "Why linux is NOT READY for human beings".

After years of using Ubuntu, I finally became sick and tired of being sick and tired of fixing things that should never have broke in the first place. You don't know how many times i've dreamed about a read-only linux system, only to protect itself from its own stupidity. Ubuntu Linux is undoubtely the most irrational thing I came across in my entire life. Ubuntu is irrationality with eye candy.
In our world, getting rid of weed in our garden is a simple task. But in the Ubuntu world, you can remove weed and feces will start coming out of the ground meters away.

Yeah, I know, "linux is free, so I should not complain". Guess what, when I get an operating system, I want an operating system with, at least, the guarantee that it's an operating system. In french, operating system is "système d'exploitation". You want the system to exploit the computer, not to exploit the user. Ubuntu has the slogan "linux for human beings". This is nothing more than a blatant lie. Linux for human beings, yeah, except those with an ATI sound card, and those with a high-end creative sound card, and those with a firewire sound card... and it's just what i'm using myself. Oh yeah I know, there's a hardware compatibility list for Ubuntu. There should also be a user compatibility list. Because Linux, even Ubuntu, dare I say, Ubuntu even more than Debian, is for unix programmers. Not for human beings who aren't. Because by hiding every bug behind new and shiny eye candy, you make it even harder to fix, and easier to break things for no apparent reason. Ubuntu is at the middle between "linux geeks" and "human beings not knowing anything about linux". You might say this is the perfect mix, I say this makes it unuseable for both. Linux power users scratch their heads wondering what the heck happened to the system, and teens scratch their heads wondering why their graphics card stopped working.

I used Ubuntu since 7.10 and liked it, because it seemed to me like, "Windows 98, but better". Simple interface (Gnome 2), correct system. Looking back, i've wasted so much time I should have square installed Windows 98 instead, even if we were in 2007. Dependency hell, audio hell, and every freaking bug I came across made me search google and these forums and enter command lines, and then copy the error lines, and then command lines, and then copy the error lines, for hours, sometimes days.

There's a reason why people tell you "good luck" when you have problems with ubuntu, it's because you actually got to be damn lucky to get anything to work well in Ubuntu, and if your entire system is working FINE, you're so damn PROUD of the miracle you ACCOMPLISHED. The problem is this: **Proudness through accomplishment in making an operating system OPERATE is for CODERS, not for USERS.**

Bottom line: Would I recommend ubuntu to anyone? NEVER. Life is too short to waste your time doing some others job.
