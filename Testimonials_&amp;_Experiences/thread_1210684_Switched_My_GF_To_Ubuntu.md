### Post by 1roxtar on 2009-07-11
This story begins with my girlfriend's computer.  It is an older model Gateway desktop.  Some home-based computer tech sold her this old pc about a year ago.  It only has a 40 gig HD and three 128MB memory sticks.  Her Windows XP finally crashed (I say "finally" because I have been dying to find a reason to switch her to Ubuntu).  She had called the same tech and he told her that it would cost her almost $200 to install a new XP os.  Then he suggested that it would be "better" for her to just buy a newer computer....from HIM, no doubt.  

I asked the guy if he just had a copy of Ubuntu with him.  He said that he didn't know too much about Linux, but knew that you could download Ubuntu from the internet.  Well, being a tech myself, I told him thanks for his help but I would take it from here.  Using her brother's laptop, I downloaded a copy of Ubuntu 9.04 and proceeded to install it on her computer.  I didn't upgrade the memory or HD yet, because I wanted to see how well Ubuntu would run on such an old system. 

I popped in the cd, installed it, and took about 30 minutes to customize it to her needs.  This family mainly uses their pc to surf the web, download music and burn cd's.  I changed the wallpaper and gave it a familiar look and feel so they don't get intimidated that it isn't a Windows OS.  I didn't want to change it too much because I wanted them to know that it ISN'T Windows and that Ubuntu can have them do everything they did before but faster and more securely.

After booting it up for the first time, I was surprised at how fast it still worked.  I browsed through her folders, tried out the music and video player (which played a dvd, videos and mp3's with no lag at all, despite such low memory).  Even the boot up was fast (25 seconds).  That amazed her the most.  With her Windows XP, it took about 10 minutes before she clicked her power button and got a working desktop.  With Ubuntu, the desktop was up in seconds and she got online immediately.

Her teenage daughter was impressed with the fact that she could listen to a sample of her music files by merely waving the mouse cursor over each music file.  I love that feature myself.  She has her Limewire and so she is happy.  After letting them get familiar with it for a bit, they said that although the desktop interface is a bit different, it wasn't hard to get used to it.  All they wanted was for it "to just work" and with UBUNTU, it did!!!
