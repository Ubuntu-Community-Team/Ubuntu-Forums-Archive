### Post by dv-design on 2008-11-24
I have been a long time Windows user, like so many other people. Not knowing what other options where available. I had a stint of using and working on Mac's and loved them back around 2000.

However as much as i liked Mac and its software, it was feasible for me (and many others) because of the cost...not only of the system but of the software.

So i kept the struggle with windows...then i heard of Ubuntu. I had heard of Linux before and had a very poor misconception of what exactly Linux was.

I was under the impression that Linux was some archaic, terminal/comand line based, only for super dorks kind of OS.

What i found in Ubuntu amazed me!

Most notably it was easier to install then Windows. Very quick to install too. First time landing on the desktop and i was amazed...it connected to my netwrok without any setting up, automatically went and got the video card drivers, and started updating the rest of my software.

I also figured that software would be an issue...and in +90% of situations its really not. I was using Openoffice on my windows box anyway, so i knew that wouldnt be a problem.

The best feature of Ubuntu and software are the respoitories which make fidning and installing software a sinch. Need a program to view a video or listen to music or open some document...goto add/remove (repository) and more often then not you will find multiple options available to you.

Some of the Ubuntu/Linux programs actually are better in my opinion then the windows counterparts...Totem and VLC both play videos that my windows computer could not, they would even go as far to grab the needed codec's if i didnt have them already. HD video from my camera that is unplayable on my windows XP computer with 1.5gb ram and a better video card (all other hardware the same), plays on my Ubuntu setup with a worse video card and 512mb ram!

The user interface for Ubuntu is much more friendly too, getting to programs, places, and admin/settings is intuitive and easy to understand.

What grabs my attention the most about Ubuntu is the ability to use several "workspaces" which essentially are different desktops without logging in and out...and being able to seemlessly cycle through them with ease...helps keep clutter down. For instance if i want a email client, a mp3 player, and my im program open i can have them on 1 desktop while i borswe the internet on the active desktop! Then seemlessly switch between them.

Add compiz or other addons and you can get 3d and special effects into play, makes Windows Aero seem like a joke.

Everything runs smooth and fast, im in a pretty unique position of being able to compare very similar computers (only ram and video card really differ) one that runs Ubuntu and one running Windows XP pro.

I had a relative comment on how fast the browser was (FF and Opera), and asked why his wasnt that fast...i had to explain he had a virus, called Windows XP, that was keeping him from reaching his computers full potential.

So far i could not be happier that i switched. While there are still things i havent figured out in Linux and thus need my Windows PC, the future may hold truely converting to Linux for me.

Great things about Ubuntu:
-Fast
-Easy to use for 90% of things (the other 10% is stuff most people wouldnt need to do)
-Software is easier to get then expected, and free!
-Has better 3d desktop effects then Windows
-Requires lower specs, runs better, does as much if not more then Windows
-Multiple workplaces reduces clutter, so useful!
-many things just work!
-No more blue screen of death!

Things that hold Ubuntu/Linux back:
-Drivers could be beefed up...still dont have my sound card right, got sound, cant get mic to work. Printers a pain to setup too.
-Some software requires use of terminal or steps that are not automated or intuitive still. Could be an issue for someone who doesnt "understand" computers or the average user.
-Few big programs that i will miss, Photoshop, PrimoPDF, Expressions Web, Live Mail...there are alternatives, but they are exactly that, not on par.

So far, Ubuntu has surpassed my expectations in many ways, however the few short commings make me see why Linux is not as mainstream as it could and should be. Right now im having trouble printing to PDF (from internet/FF), cant get my mic to work, and could not get my @hotmail account setup in thunderbird (@live works with an addon). Other then that im so happy its like having a new computer...and im sure the issues im having now will be resolved in the future when i learn a bit more.
