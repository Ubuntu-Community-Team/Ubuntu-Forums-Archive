### Post by Muty-bg on 2006-11-20
Hello everyone, 

 First I would like to introduce myself. I'm 22 years old student currently living in Vienna, Austria. I'm studying technical informatics and I just discovered the magic of ubuntu :). 
 I have experience with Linux for more than 6 or maybe 7 years now. At first it was very hard for me with Linux, because I was very young and there was no one around me to give a helping hand. 3 years ago I moved to Austria to study  informatics and I found a great Linux community in the University. I started with slackware as recommendation from a friend and learned the basics on it. Then an year ago a friend of mine introduced me to gentoo. Considering that slackware had no packet management system at the time I was using it, I was greatly impressed by gentoo and I'm still using it on 2 of my machines at home. 
 But there was something wrong. As I understand it was good for me to start with them because I learned lots of the stuff the hard  way. But I've spend weeks just to get a simple thing working properly( because of my inadequate knowledge). And as I learned it the hard way, it was next to impossible to recommend them to a friend who never used Linux. My first try to introduce someone was a complete failure. 3-4 months ago A very good friend of mine asked me if I can help him install Linux on his notebook. He doesn't have good knowledge about pcs so I told him to install the basic gentoo cd and from then on I was doing everything from ssh. 4 days later more than half of the functions on his notebook weren't working and it was still unusable. He was very frustrated and finally gave up. I felt bad about it and realized I made a mistake. 
 This friday I had some friends hanging at my place and they were very very impressed seeing gnome with beryl running. One of them approached me and told me that he really wanted to install linux for quite some time and asked me if I will help him. Then I started considering what should I do. Gentoo was out of the question and I wanted to try out Ubuntu for some time but my colleagues told me it's for pussies(no offense meant I'm just quoting) but anyway I decided to give it a try. I got to his place. We downloaded the image burned the cd and booted from it. I asked him if he likes what he sees on the live cd, he said yes and I started installing it. 
  In the meantime he was reinstalling his windows xp on his other machine and we started at the same time. 1 hour later I was stunned as much as he was. I had just setup a fully operational desktop with absolutely everything working as it should and even beryl running. And on the other side he was still installing the armada of drivers on his windows box. I played around with it the whole night and I wasn't able to belive it. It's working and fully functional. So easy, yet so beautifull. This changed my whole viewpoint regarding Linux. At 6 am in the morning I was running back home to try it on my notebook. I would like say that  I have 3 pcs at home, 1 server running gentoo, 1 desktop running gentoo and my notebook running windows xp! After spending 2 sleepless weeks trying to install gentoo and get it to work properrly with the mobility radeon 9700 on my nb I gave up.
So I was very curious to see what will happen when I install ubuntu on it. 1  hour afterwards it was up and running. Everything :) from the touchpad to the radeon with the screen with weird 1680x1050 resolution. It was gorgeous I couldn't believe my eyes. So I got to sleep and after I woke up I've spend the whole weekend on it. I was so frustrated and disappointed when I bought my notebook and I was not able to get it run properly on linux. And now I'm truly happy :). Still there are some issues with the stupid radeon. I'm using it with the proprietary drivers( I'm sorry but I'm a hardcore gamer and it was hard enough for me to drop windows, I really need my fix otherwise I would've installed the os drivers) and I get the weird white screen and lockups when I switch VTs or when I'm shutting off X and I can't use the standby because my screen is whit and irresposnive when I resume it but with the rumors I hear that ATI will release full specifications of the radeons to open source programmers after amd bought them. There is hope and I'll wait. 
 At last yesterday I removed my ntfs partition from my notebook and I got to bed. Today I woke up made a coffee, sat on the kitchen table with my notebook and realized a stange thing. That's the first day of my life without any windows installed on any machine at my home. Just the fought of it made me very happy :D. I was reading things about the ubuntu philosophy the whole morning and I enjoyed it. I would like ask if you can point me to some more resources that I can read.

 At last I want to say sorry for my long rant and thank you for reading it. I really wanted to share my joy with the rest of the community. This is my first post on the forums but not the last one. You'll see me around :D. 

Kind Regards,
Yassen

PS: Again big big thank you to the ubuntu community for the great OS that you made
