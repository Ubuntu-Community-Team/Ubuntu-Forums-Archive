### Post by zeroseven0183 on 2011-11-01
That is probably not the best title but I have to confess *this little thing* saved me from an embarrassment.

I was about to do a presentation regarding Ubuntu during [our very first (Oneiric Ocelot) release party here in the City]("https://plus.google.com/photos/104168843479135841309/albums/5663877292408463473") when the most unfortunate occurred. I don't remember what caused the system crash but sure it would be embarrassing if my small audience, majority of them are Ubuntu beginners, would know what happened. And so I had to keep my cool down. Honestly speaking, I almost panicked. 

Then the light bulb over my head turned on.

It was not actually part of the back up plan. Everything was ready the night before -- Ubuntu CDs, complete ISO images of Ubuntu and its official derivatives, the slideshow, a complete copy of Full Circle Magazines, a few raffle prizes and an Ubuntu Live USB. :KS I was supposed to do a demo after the slideshow but since my system is not working properly, I had to do both at the same time.

The Live USB, as almost always, worked perfectly. My laptop booted smoothly. The graphics and the sounds were perfect. All the hardware drivers were immediately recognized including my remote control which I used for the presentation. And LibreOffice performance was Impress-ive. The presentation went very good. Without my friends knowing the issue, I was able to present to them the beauty of Linux, the freedom in using open source software and the wide and active community of Ubuntu -- all because a Live USB was available.

The party continued with a few discussions, sharing of experiences, food and drinks. I'm sure everyone were convinced to try Ubuntu and get involved. I hope to meet with them more often and continue to build this excited community of beginners.

How about you? What was your most memorable experience?
