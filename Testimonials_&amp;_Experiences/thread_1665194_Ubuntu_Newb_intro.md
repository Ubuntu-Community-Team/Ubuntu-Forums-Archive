### Post by Hunter Tech on 2011-01-12
Over the past couple days i have used this forum extensively to overcome some issues with getting setup, so now that i have most everything figured out i decided to join.  

I did not plan to use linux, i bought a new desktop that came with no os.  I had just bought Win 7 for my laptop and had intentions of going to xp pro on laptop and 7 on the desktop.  That whole plan went down the tube when i realized that during the christmas holiday and my careless throwing away my 7 disc got tossed.  So i had a new toy with no OS on it, im not a very patient person so instead of waiting to go buy a new disc i called up one of my engineers and asked what he would do to get both machines functional.  he told me that he uses linux and spent a while going over it with me.  The list of problems that i encountered were extensive to start with the cd-rs i had were 650 MB and the OS is 693 so i had to figure out how to boot from USB.  Then after up and running i was lucky enough to gain much experience installing Ubuntu due to my invidia graphics card driver which caused everything to freeze after the restart on the checking battery state part (still have not found a solution to this).  Then the executable bit was kicking my a$$ for a few hours, but long story short so far so good and all is well except for my graphics card issue which really is not effecting much that i can tell, my dual monitor setup is working fine and i dont play games so im not sure that i even would notice a difference if the card was working.  Anyways im liking the new OS enough in fact i did a dual boot on my laptop today, it takes take a while to get used to using the terminal, feel like the old days of dos command prompts.

Thanks for all the information that is in these forums it has led me through a few problems very successfully.

### Post by JC Cheloven on 2011-01-12
Hi, and welcome. 

Coming from the windows xp world, I also thought in the terminal as an outdated and cumbersome thing at the beginning, back in late 2007. But take my word: it isn't at all.
- On one hand, it's way more polished and powerful than that "other thing" in that "other OS". You just need to get used to it if you want to use it (no real need for a dayly use).
- On the other hand, it's a great tool to give help. It's much easier to drop a line with a command, than saying: "well open that menu, in the popup window, somehow at the middle and to the right you will see a click-box ... blah blah..." , and probably attaching some screenshots and such.

I can think of more benefits, as lower-level control on your system, but the above should be enough ;)
