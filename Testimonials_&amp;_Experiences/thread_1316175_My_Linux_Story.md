### Post by Zoot7 on 2009-11-05
Figured I'd post my linux story since day 1 here, thought I'd done it already, but it seems I haven't..

Anyway,
I started with openSUSE for C programming at the start of my degree about 4 years ago, it was Windows only up until then (how blind I was). 
So I thought I'd try it out for myself, so I installed it and fiddled with it for about a month or so, then I realized that openSUSE wasn't Linux, that there were countless other distros out there, so I decided to play around with a few of them. Tried out Knoppix, Gentoo, Slackware, I never really took to Slackware or Knoppix. 
I heard great things about Gentoo, and the fact that it was very much a power users distro. So I thought "hey I'll throw myself in the deep end", good way to do it; I learned a lot about the workings of Linux, but then I got sick of compiling stuff here and there for a more or less miniscule gain. Then I found Debian and gave it a shot, worked very well out of the box and was really stable (a little behind I found, but the most stable so far). I settled on Debian for about a year. 
I then got wind of Ubuntu from a friend who recommended it to me, back in the days of Feitsy. So I gave it a shot and was pleasantly surprised. It was the only distro where all my laptops hardware worked flawlessly, and I also found that the nifty apps it included over the other distros made things so easy, but it still catered to the power user I found. 
So I've been using it ever since, the only release I've skipped since Feitsy was Intrepid after a botched upgrade from Hardy (only time I ever tried an upgrade).

I'm still dabbling with other distros at the moment but I've yet to find one I like better than Ubuntu.
