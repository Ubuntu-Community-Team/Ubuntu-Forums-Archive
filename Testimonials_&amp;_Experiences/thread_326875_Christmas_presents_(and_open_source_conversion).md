### Post by mostwanted on 2006-12-28
My mum isn't really a hardcore computer user. She mostly uses it for playing small games and using the Internet. Making Firefox the default browser and Thunderbird the default email client in Windows was tolerable for her, she doesn't really mind what program she uses. I even removed Windows at one point to see if she would accept using Linux (well, Ubuntu) as a replacement and she seemed to cope with it (instead of playing Solitaire she played the Gnome games).

But something was missing! Somehow, her just using free software wasn't good enough for me. If she is completely indifferent to what software she uses then we, free software proponents, haven't really won anything. The second I stop managing her computer for her she's going to drift back to the proprietary Windows world because that's where most of her other influences want her to go.

I had to get her interested in software, the Internet and make her usage of free software something she will consciously chose. The means for this, I decided, was to her educate herself! So I got her two books for Christmas. One was about how to get the most out of Firefox and Thunderbird, the other was how to get the most out of Google and the Google services. She seems very interested so far, so I think it was the right choice and money well spent.

Hopefully, in the future, we can get to recommend each other our favourite Firefox extensions. That is my long-term goal for this :p

BTW, during the unwrapping of the presents my aunt who teaches Word and Excel at a night-school (not sure if that's a word in English) pointed at the *Thunderbird* logo on the book and said "That's what I use. It's really good!". So with my help or not, the silent revolution is definitely taking place ;)
