### Post by jnewl on 2008-08-06
I just want to add my voice to the chorus of Ubuntu converts. I first tried Linux like 12 years ago or more, when a "package" was little more than some files and a set of instructions on how to read man pages so you could, using many arcane spells and incantations, try to configure everything from the command line. Needless to say, that install didn't last very long. I like messing with computers, but not that much. :)

Then, maybe four years ago, I tried again with a Suse install. Much, much better and friendlier, but still not quite up to snuff in terms of ease of use.

Now, a month ago, in a fit of rage at Vista's crappy performance and a constant need to reboot caused by God only knows what, I decided to check out Ubuntu. Omigod. I've been totally blown away. Everything about it is a dream come true, from the quick and trouble-free installation to the stable operation and wide range of quality software available. It's been so easy and glitchless, I really feel like with Ubuntu Linux is finally ready for the masses. Sure, there are still some rough spots (until you never need the Terminal again, there always will be), but overall I think the average clueless Joe could probably install the default package and accomplish 99% of his computing goals with no major pains.

In fact, I feel so free and so happy now that I've become something of an Ubuntu apostle, trying to convince my friends to "just say no" to Microsoft and the whole money-sucking industry that's grown up around them in favor of what is plainly vastly superior software (Linux) in a vastly superior package (Ubuntu). 

Vista? What's that? Thanks, Ubuntu guys!
