### Post by 1cewolf on 2008-07-03
After I fixed my computer a couple of weeks ago, I thought I'd treat myself to an interesting new distribution to try out since I had been forced back onto my Windows XP laptop. I decided to give the latest version of Linux Mint a shake since it was what I used before my computer broke.

I could tell that a lot of improvements had been made, but it just didn't give me the same positive experience that I remembered. I became acutely aware of several small things that bothered me...

1) The Mint software portal, while a good idea in concept, was something that I simply didn't use and didn't find particularly helpful. Judging by the lack of comments and ratings for many items in the portal, I suspect that I'm not alone in my opinion.

2) The Mint update utility provided more information than was necessary. Is an update available? That's excellent - install it. That's all.

3) The Mint menu was good but somewhat inflexible. If I wanted to rearrange the menu so that the applications were on the left and the places/commands were on the right, I couldn't do that.

4) Although this isn't directly applicable to Linux Mint itself, the format of the Linux Mint forums is awkward. Why is the user information on the right instead of the left?

5) The default layout wasn't ideal. It seemed too much like Windows.

In addition to those issues, I also ran into a bunch of strange glitches and lock-ups such as logging in and finding the desktop completely black and unusable. After some unsuccessful trial and error, I ditched it for OpenSUSE 11.0 because I heard it was an important release.

I tried out OpenSUSE's KDE 4 version first since I was eager to see if the developers had taken any of the "bleeding edge" off of it since the horrible KDE 4.0 RC I had used before. 

OpenSUSE 11.0's KDE variant seems like the first of many necessary steps in the right direction. All in all, KDE 4 seems to be a compromise between GNOME's feature restrictions and KDE 3's feature abundance, which puts it squarely in the middle of the pack and (probably) in the crosshairs of devotees from both camps. At least, that was the impression I got from what I could see during the brief moments when something wasn't crashing...

GNOME and KDE 3 in OpenSUSE 11.0 weren't much better. I've never found KDE 3 to be particularly useful, so I'm honestly not sure why I installed it. The GNOME version seemed nice at a glance, but I tried to run an update and it threw up a huge window filled with something about manually resolving dependency conflicts. I'm not an expert at Linux yet so that was enough to scare me away from OpenSUSE.

Having found two popular distros to be miserable, I thought I'd go back to my roots and try Ubuntu just to see if it was really as bad as some people said it was. From the moment I stuck the LiveCD in and chose to "Try out Ubuntu without changing the computer" (an excellent choice of words) I knew that I was in for something special from Ubuntu all over again.

What can I say? It's good to be back. I missed Ubuntu's nifty default layout with the dual panels. I missed Ubuntu's 3 menus and being able to squeeze a bunch of shortcuts in right beside the main menus. I missed having the Add/Remove program around since it's so helpful when you're looking for something new. Most of all, I think I missed Ubuntu's way of making things just easy enough so that it's convenient to use but you still have to apply yourself to learn and become better at Linux. 

The best part is that on my 64-bit Hardy setup, my system hasn't missed a beat! The only issue I've come across so far is that Enemy Territory: Quake Wars doesn't seem to be launching maximized and I'm not sure to get it how to do that. I'm not too concerned, though...with all the Ubuntu support here and elsewhere, I'm sure I'll find an answer sooner or later. These forums are an incredible resource.

My hat is off to the Ubuntu community for putting out an excellent release which has once again given me a superb OS to work with. If I have time in the future, I plan to volunteer in some respect to return the favor :)
