### Post by halcyon_skys on 2011-12-12
Just thought id register and stop by add my thoughts and experience about Ubuntu.
I should have probably stopped in a few years ago as ive been using Ubuntu for quite some time. You know how it is, when things are going well, i just didnt see a need , as i loved it. 
I tried to always get my friends and family to use it, people i worked with (tried to get the whole office to use it) , and even talked about it to strangers sometimes. 
Now however, due to Unity, ive completely lost faith. Its almost like noone even cared when they dropped this on top of us. Also from what ive read, despite all the negative reviews and 'unity sucks' comments across the web,  they still cling to it as some sort of grail with blind ambition.

I tried just removing it, and trying to ignore its existance. Ive found i cant just keep doing that however. I think the only way we have left is to voice our opinions and move on. I just cant support this anymore or the carelessness they have about what the users think.

Hopefully im allowed to post my experience. I know some places unless you worship them or throw flowers and praise they dont let you say anything. This is of course, all just my opinion, and my personal experience. Word will continue to get out regardless i think.

Heres to a good few years i had with you Ubuntu. Cheers. If things somehow miraculously change ill be back.

Since it's not a scientific forum, we definitely shouldn't be sharing  biased information. In a scientific forum, I suspect such bias would  immediately come under scrutiny. Unfortunately, it's difficult to  collect unbiased information, especially when humans are involve. We all  tend to have opinions, even researchers [struggle]("http://wikieducator.org/Research")  acquiring quantifiable unbiased data. It's one of the reasons controls  are implemented and the data must be repeatable and supportable.

Regardless, how illuminating it may be we should always remain skeptical  - until it's verified and not just by the biased group(s) acquiring  said data. In a poll, how the questions are asked (and to whom) is  critical. Other variables that may influence poll results should be  addressed and defendable. Or, the poll lacks the necessary integrity to  be viewed as factual.

I believe it's in the best interests of all forum members to state  opinion and not share biased information. As I think it's one of the  reasons FUD exists - fear/doubt based on unsubstantiated or erroneous  data. But that's just my opinion. :smile:

I admire the fact that you pointed this out and noted said bias. *Excellent post 3rdalbum*
