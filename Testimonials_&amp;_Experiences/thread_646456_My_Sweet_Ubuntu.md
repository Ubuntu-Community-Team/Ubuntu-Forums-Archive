### Post by wilerk on 2007-12-21
First of all thank you to everyone on this forum for being the best support team one can ask for. Replies are quick and come in great numbers, it really feels like a community.

I'm a .NET developer (with an own company), I've always been used to Windows. But I have a big interest for Opensource Software and buy the Linux Format so every now and then to see how green the grass is on the other side. I've been living in South Africa for the last four years and Mark Shuttleworth is South African, that's how I actually got my interest in Ubuntu. I was a big Suse fan before this.

I bought a new laptop this year and Acer Aspire 5610Z with Vista Preinstalled. My laptop had 1 Gig of RAM when I bought it and Vista gave me headaches with Vista and SQL, I had to install patches and everything, plus it was really slow. So I decided to dualboot with Ubuntu 7.04, it ran beautifully until work pushed me to use windows more and more. After a while Vista became even more slow and I got annoyed with the security permission dialog everytime I want to do something. And then I got some adware on my machine. I couldn't update my avast anymore I couldn't go to websites to get rid of it, so I got rid of Vista. Installed Fiesty and upgraded to Gutsy.

Now I also upgraded to 2 gigs of RAM because I had a plan, which worked out prefectly for me. I have Ubuntu for admin and office use and run Windows XP with my dev tools in VirtualBox. I did have some problems with wireless on my machine but with teh help of the forum it's sorted. Ok the little camera doesn't work, so what.

Thanks again guys for making this a great experience!
