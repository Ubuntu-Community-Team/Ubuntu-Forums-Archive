### Post by StephenWoods69 on 2010-01-07
I thought after having used Ubuntu for a short while now I could safely say where I am coming from and what I think of Linux as a whole.
I began computing in the old days of a 486 and DOS 6.22 with Windows 3.11 tacked on top moving as the years went by through Windows 95, 98, 98SE, Millennium, then to Windows 2000 and finally XP.
As the years have progressed so too has the need for better hardware and the constant need to pay through the nose just for a program that you might only use occasionally, Office of course being one and Visual Studio the other. Of course there were many other programs too games etc, but anyway finally I decided to try Ubuntu 9.10  64 bit and see what happened.
The Live DVD lasted only once before I dual booted just to see what it was like and shortly thereafter I completely fresh installed and am now running just Ubuntu. 
Having used Windows for all those years I have to say hand on heart that Ubuntu is giving me now all the things that Microsoft promised but never seemed to deliver, faster boot times , stability and most importantly security straight out of the box. I might also add that finally I have to read up and learn before using programs which makes me smarter (hopefully) and if I can't find the answer in man pages there is a great community to help out. 
I just wish I had decided to move over sooner :redface:
Anyways to all those who tirelessly give your time on the forums, bug testing and just generally being there thank you.
