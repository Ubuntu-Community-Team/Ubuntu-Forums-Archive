### Post by mlejayne on 2009-04-06
I'm an inexperienced ubuntu user, and my DOS experience is limited to opening Jazz Jackrabbit when I was seven. I've installed it on one other computer, but it puttered out soon after (I'm blaming that on windows ME that shared it's brain). So, I discover my brother's abandoned tower (emachines, ran the startup screen of XP) in the attic, and I decide to ubuntu it. 

Okay, so two weeks have gone by. Frustration galore, with small glimmers of hope. Getting DVDs to play, transfering my music, SNES emulators, good. Tiny screen resolution (still need to post about that), crashing (my bad. Should not have jumped on the Jaunty beta), bad. 

My main frustration has been getting the stupid wifi to work. In addition to getting either the PCI or USB dongle up and running, I had the trouble with WPA encryption that keeps turning my password into hex. As a result, I've gotten pretty good at transfering my computer from my room to the dining room table in three trips (stairs are not fun with a CRT monitor) but really, these days, a girl needs her wi-fi.

After so many attempted fixes and having to start from the very beginning again, I was perturbed. I really like Ubuntu, the philosophy and all, but it wasn't working out. Granted, when you don't have the money, linux is pretty much the only option. However, after spending (don't laugh) most of spring break trying to get stuff to work, I was not a happy camper.

So today I come home and, out of habit, try to get the wi-fi to work. I use, for the most part, the regular search terms. I've tried everything. Configuration editer, switching the router to WEP, using WICD, doing who knows what in terminal. Nothing worked. I prepare to bite the bullet and post in the forums. I'm trying to find other posts reporting the problem and I find [this forum page]("http://forums.msiwind.net/debian/wpa2-ubuntu-msi-wind-t7338.html"). Pissed of user, someone trying to help. I decide to give it a try.

I type "sudo ifconfig wlan1 down" into terminal. I'm used to typing things into terminal when I'm not sure what it'll do.

And bam. The PCI card (which I harvested off a windows 95 PC) started working. The essid popped up. I typed the password in. It connects. I remove the ethernet cable and type "facebook" into google. It loads. 

I'm pretty sure a choir started the chorus to Handel's Messiah. Thank you, salamanderpants, whoever you are. May your live CDs be without blemish.

-----

To get to the point, I would not suggest ubuntu to people who are more computer inept then I am. This computer has been a project. However, I did learn things. And it was free. And I'm going to feel pretty good about myself for sticking it out and finally getting it to work. Ubuntu, and the helpful forum-dwellers, are pretty cool.

And tetravex is the shiznit.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-04-07
Technically, your passphrase for wireless encryption IS hex. The software converts the passphrase into a hex "key" for transmission to the router. I thought Vista required you to type in the hex key rather than the passphrase? I couldn't get my passphrase working on Vista, but my key worked fine.

Connecting to WEP on the Wii requires the key rather than the passphrase, too.

Sounds to me like you've hit a bug in Jaunty that has been mostly affecting Acer Aspire One users; hopefully they'll fix that one before release.
