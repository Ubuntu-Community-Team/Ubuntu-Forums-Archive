### Post by jso2897 on 2007-08-28
I had never used anything but Windows until about a year ago. I was having trouble with an old PC, and a friend gave me a Knoppix disk to use as a repair utility. I was intrigued, and when I asked him about a noob-friendly distro, he recommended Ubuntu.
 Since then, my interest in computers has become a real hobby, and I'm having a blast. Now I am running four machines, and three are Ubuntu. I do keep XP on my gaming rig - but that's the only reason I even need a Windows box.
Just yesterday, I took an old Imac somebody gave me (which I couldn't use, due to OS 8.5 being such a P.O.S.), and loaded Xubuntu on it, and of course it screams now, and will do ten times the things it would before.
This has been my experience with all my installations. I have had to learn a few things, and take the time to figure things out (I am a technological moron), but I've enjoyed it immensely.
 Ubuntu is a real pleasure to work with, and the support system, here at the forums is just stellar. I cannot overstate the pleasure, ease, and satisfaction I've obtained with Ubuntu. I normally don't get excited about tech stuff - but the whole concept of open source holds real promise for the future of technology and it's implications to society as a whole.
 My only dissatisfaction is that my own technical ignorance prohibits me from giving back to the community, and I want to strive to be a contributing member of the community, and not just a passive consumer.
Ubuntu is the best thing since sliced bread in my book, and the people here and elsewhere in the Ubuntu community are, collectively, the most helpful and constructive folks it's been my pleasure to deal with in quite a while.
:KS:KS:KS:KS:KS:KS:KS:KS
