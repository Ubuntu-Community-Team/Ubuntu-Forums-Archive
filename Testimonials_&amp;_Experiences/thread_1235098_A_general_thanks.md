### Post by TheNessus on 2009-08-08
To the entire Linux community.

So I had a laptop, a good ol' IBM thinkpad T42 with XP on it. I got interested in linux because of a hardcore friend, and I installed it through Wubi, on the main XP partition. I was impressed, it all flowed well. 

Then my laptop died on me, of old age. So I bought me a Fujitsu Siemens. A horrible mistake since it arrived with an Nvidia 8400. I got Vista with it, and installed Ubuntu alongside it. Exploring and experimenting with it, I became hooked even further. I tweaked Gnome to exactly what I want it to be, and it serves all my needs pretty flawlessly. Except that Nvidia card. Using that hardcore friend to install a newer driver which is not in the repositories, and unsuccesfuly, I gave up on it for a while. I'm not a command-line freak, I hate using it, it's why I use Ubuntu and not Archlinux. But when the time come it has to be confronted, so I googled up on that driver and saw that it was pretty easy installing that driver actually. Took about 3 minutes for the whole thing to work properly, and it's done. Now I have no complaints, and the last time I used Vista, I cannot recall. Now everything not only works fast and without glitches, it's also pretty, thanks to compiz + a proper driver. Plus, it's always awesome to solve a puzzle (however noobish it may be) and make things work 100%. 

I have no idea where you people get the time and will and strength to actually support such a community and consolidate it. Kudos, to all the volunteers.
