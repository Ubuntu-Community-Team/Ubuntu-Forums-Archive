### Post by ackley on 2009-12-05
I installed Jaunty on a total of 4 of my computers & one friend's Eee pc. The 4 that I have are as follows.

eMachines T5246, Compaq SR1945 something (I can't remember. It's a server machine.), MSI Wind U100 & a Dell Studio 1535 (My wife's).

All installations of Jaunty went flawlessly & the installed OS was perfect. Everything worked right out of the box with each computer minus some proprietary drivers. No big deal. Easy to download.

Now, I'm not usually one to download an alpha/beta release of an OS for fear of breaking my system. I usually wait until it's nice & polished by the company that's releasing it. I Actually waited until mid-November to download upgrade my machines. 

I upgraded the MSI Wind first. Once it finished, I was greeted with a flickering display. I figured it was just an issue of upgrading 9.04 to 9.10. I made a live USB of 9.10 & re-installed Karmic on the Wind. Still the same thing. Googled it & there isn't a real fix for it yet. Awesome. Went back to 9.04.

Upgraded my wife's Dell Studio 1535. Upon completion of the upgrade, I restarted. I was greeted by an infinite loop of restarts on this machine with no fix available. Awesome. Glad I made that USB stick for my Wind.

Upgraded the eMachines. No idea why the ethernet port decided to stop working after I upgraded. But a downgrade to 9.04 fixed it.

With 3 tries & 3 fails of upgrading to Karmic. I wouldn't dare try it on the server. And my friend seems happy with 9.04 on his Eee pc.

I love Ubuntu. Don't get me wrong. But Karmic has been the absolute worst out of any release that I've used. There was a lapse for a couple years when I was using Fedora, but I started using Ubuntu at 4.10 (Warty Warthog) & thought this was the OS to end all others. But 9.10? Should have waited for 10.4. I just see so many people having so many problems with 9.10 & wonder if they've tried just downgrading to 9.04. It seems to have fixed all of my problems.
