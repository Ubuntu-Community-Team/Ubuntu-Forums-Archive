### Post by cirabisi2009 on 2010-05-28
Man. Couldnt send enough thank you's to the people that have made Ubuntu possible. I have been hooked since 8.04 was released. I must say from my first experiences as a green horn to now where I can understand C++ and Python. Ubuntu IS easy to pick up and use, if your a new person to anything technical with computers Ubuntu is a much better starting companion apposed to Windows. But I'm not here to bash any OS. 

If you enjoy a simple yet HIGHLY CUSTOMIZABLE UI then this is definitely  where you ought to be. Then again if you like the technical approached by using command lines, there is no stopping you. You get the best of both worlds, I comfortably say that you would never need to look at a terminal as long as you choose. But I will admit, no matter how much you try to stay away... its going to happen.

"Customer Service" more known by the fellow Open source community goers as FORUMS, I must say impeccable. I can ask any question and with in 10 minutes I got atleast 3 solutions. 

Over the years Ubuntu has certainly improved with the friendliness to new users, and even old experienced users, adding more support for drivers and etc.

ThE SWITCH FROM WINdOWS GRASP TO OPEN HANDS OF LINUX

I was for sometime a huge WINDOWS ONLY man. I tried to keep people away from this community not even knowing what it had to offer. If i could go back I'd slap myself. All I had to do to try this OS out was find a thing called wubi, which was not a hard task at all. Waited 2 1/2 hours for the download/install to finish.. never experienced such anticipation from a piece of computer software. I clicked restart and clicked UBUNTU to boot. It came to the login and I fell in love... Love at first site? CERTAINLY POSSIBLE.

NOWA DAYS

I can smugly say that I have gotten my college to accept open office org as a default word program to replace EXPENSIVE microsoft word which nowa days EVERYONE is looking for a way to save a few $$. They are still a litte iffy on the ubuntu switch but im working on it. Ubuntu was what inspired me to become a computer technician. Im like the geek squad only cheaper and friendlier :D. but that is my experience, if I repeated myself which I'm sure I have, I am sorry. 

Again, thank you Ubuntu and team to making such a revolutionary OS. Alot of mac users cannot deny the potential this OS has. Thanks again.

Sincerely,
Alex Cirabisi
