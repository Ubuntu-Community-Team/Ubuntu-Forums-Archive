### Post by smartfeller on 2008-09-22
Just wanted to post my high level of satisfaction with Ubuntu Hardy. I use PCs at work, but have 12 Macs of varying vintages at home running various versions of the Classic Mac OS up through Leopard OS X. I installed Hardy on a G3 B&W, 350Mhz, 768MB ram tower via a Live CD install. It's in a dual boot configuration with OS X Tiger. I also have Hardy running on a 333Mhz G3 Lombard Powerbook with 320MB ram. I must say that I am very pleased with the performance of both machines. I don't need Flash on these PPC machines, and the ability to run Firefox 3 on the Lombard has me laughing with joy. Had Tiger on the Lombard previously via XPost facto and it was sluggish, to say the least. OS X Panther was not a solution as Firefox 3 requires at least OS 10.4 Tiger. I don't have wireless working yet on the Powerbook, but I haven't really tried. I understand there are some difficulties with the Broadcom chipset and I've got a Buffalo card in the Lombard. Overall, though, I am just thrilled at how spry and quick everything else is--and it all fits on the original 4GB drive--amazing! The developers, programmers and folks on this forum have done a superb job "unofficially" supporting Hardy on these old PPC machines. Although I just use these machines for email, word processing and light internet use, and I haven't had any issues, I know the people that frequent these forums will provide any assistance I might need in the future. Too often, you only hear about criticism and disappointment when it comes to operating systems of all kinds. For Ubuntu to work as well as it does, especially on these older boxes, is a huge testament to all the great support you can find here. Thank you one and all for a great Ubuntu experience.
