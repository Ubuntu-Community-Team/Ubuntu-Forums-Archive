### Post by TheThinker on 2007-06-14
It's been about a month since I've gotten Ubuntu Feisty 7.04 and I can remember the day when I got the boot-cd in the mail. The package came all the way across the Atlantic to my home here in El Paso, absolutely free. Free! Yes, I felt FREE when I decided to back up my data before diving into a different O.S. No more having to install updates for anti-virus, anti-malware, and anti-hacking software. No more griping on how I'm not allowed to install certain programs on Windows XP, even though they worked fine on Windows 98. It was an open-source operating system, a system that put me in the Captain's chair, so-to-speak. I was ready to say, "Warpspeed" and "Give me more power!" while knowing that there are measures in place to stop me from sinking the ship. Mutiny? I think not!

As I formatted my hard-drive, I began to worry. Looking at the progress-bar was like casting off the sail for the first time into a vast and uncharted waters. The first waves of anxiety hit, but the ship went forth defiantly. Today, this ship is sailing smoothly and we have gotten along fine. Its members salute me, and I salute them back. I say "North!" and that is where we go. No particular place in mind; just a direction.

As the saying goes, "In order to discover new lands, you  must lose sight of the shore." Well, you don't need land to discover new things.

Many thanks to the Ubuntu teams for designing and supporting such a great product. Here's hoping your philosophy and business-model continue to dance across the sea to people like me.
