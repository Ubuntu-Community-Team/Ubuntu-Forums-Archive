### Post by sixtyten on 2012-09-17
I've been a Linux user off and on for a while now. For the past year I've used Ubuntu 10.04 as my primary desktop at work. Prior to this I used a Mac and PC at work. My work duties involve web design/development and I got tired of feeling dumbed down on the Mac. 

Now.. back to my point of this post. I ran into a problem today running an Elluminate session for an online class. The problem involved OpenJDK and I eventually got it fixed thanks to the wonderful Ubuntu community. While I was searching for a solution, I ran across a post from someone who was complaining about Linux. You know.. the usual post.. "I'm giving up on Linux and going back to Windows". They gave up.. They were sooo close to solving the problem whether it be a driver issue or a software problem.. 

I get the most satisfaction from solving a Linux issue. The satisfaction of knowing you learned a little bit more of what lies under the hood of Linux. The satisfaction of knowing I didn't give up and learned something new today.. And you know what? Once I got it working under Linux, it ran better than it did under Windows. 

Please... if you're thinking about giving up on Linux, give it a little more time. Yes it may take some time to figure it out and it may not be easy.. BUT.. You will thank yourself in the long run. Plus, the Ubuntu community is outstanding so you don't have an excuse! :wink:

### Post by sixtyten on 2012-09-17
> **monkeycrayons said:**
> Great post, thanks! As a new user, I'm curious: which web development tools are you using?

I use Aptana and Filezilla. Filezilla handles the ftp side of things and Aptana the coding side. I use the Site Manager portion of Filezilla to remember the connection setting on all of my sites. I can open a site and easily transfer any recent changes to my local machine (Sometimes I work from two different locations). During the download process it asks if you want to overwrite if source newer. Once you're in Aptana, it also has remote abilities in case you need to easily upload a file you've edited or created. 

I came from the Dreamweaver world on the Mac and at first it bothered me to use different apps for coding and ftp. Now I like how the pieces are separate. Dreamweaver was a bear and I got sick of all the bloat. Aptana is nice and streamlined. 

I still have to use Photoshop under Oracle VirtualBox but that doesn't really bother me too much. One day I need to learn Gimp but for now, VirtualBox has worked for me. I also like to have access to an Windows image in case I need to test a site in IE :-& or something.

### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-09-17
> sixtyten
Please... if you're thinking about giving up on Linux, give it a little more time.

I  totally agree with your over all premise. Unfortunately, the "give it  more time concept" may be somewhat flawed. Experience and knowledge base  may also play an important role in a new user's decision. Some users  may not have time to invest in the learning cure or have the knowledge  base to understand specific issues. They may rely on this forum's help  sections for assistance, but should (for any reason) a new user struggle  - time and knowledge base may play an important role in the decision to  give up.

Asking a user to invest more time, must be weighed  against the users available time. In today's busy world, time may be an  extremely valuable commodity. IMHO it might make more sense to provide  one on one assistance (by request) through a simple mentoring program.  These forums have a plethora of experienced and knowledgeable members. I  believe these members could easily assist new users (one on one) and  reduce the time factor frustration by communicating at their level of  understanding.

Instead of a response to a single issue. A  mentoring program would provide new users with the opportunity to  quickly enhance their level of understanding and issue resolution  capabilities. Thus, helping new users by offering an assisted learning  opportunity. I also believe it would open doors to community friendships  and improve the perception of what some believe is an elitist  environment.

Just my $0.02
