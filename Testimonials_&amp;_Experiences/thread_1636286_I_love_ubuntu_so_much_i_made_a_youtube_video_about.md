### Post by 3Miro on 2010-12-04
Nice indeed.

People already pointed out the 4 desktops and you can also check the cube deformations from CCSM (Compiz Config Settings Manager), you can make things like Cylinders or Spheres (note that those do stress the GPU). CCSM is a good place to show on a video what customizations mean, considering how many screens of options you have.

It would have been nice to show more of the apps in the sense of what they look like. OO is nice, but Chromium looks exactly like Google-Chrome and I am sure most people have already seen that anyway.

A note on Transmission and Bittorrent. Bittorrent is a technology for fast download of large files, there is absolutely nothing illegal in it. Transmission is a client that uses Buttorrent protocol, it is no more illegal then FireFox or Chromium. You can use FF to download legal and illegal stuff and you can use Transmission to download legal and illegal stuff, in and of themselves the client programs are not illegal. People form Linux distros prefer to download .iso files from their distributions using bittorrent, that is why you will find torrent clients often to be installed by default on Linux.

Again, nice video.
