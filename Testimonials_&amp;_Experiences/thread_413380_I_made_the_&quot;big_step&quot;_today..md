### Post by janga on 2007-04-19
Here is my story.

I had my first linux experience in 2001 with SUSE 7.1. In those days, the range of applications was very limited. It was usable as a server or to play around, but definitely no desktop OS IMHO.

I dropped linux for several years and came back with Knoppix 3.2. This was amazing. Linux was getting there. I decided to set up a dual boot with Widows XP and SUSE 9.2, but got fed up with the infamous "RPM Hell" very soon. Also, SUSE was very slow on my machine, it booted like 5 minutes or so...

Then I read about a forthcoming Debian based Linux distro called Ubuntu "Warty Warthog"  and gave it a try. Yes! I loved it from the very first moment.
So I spent the following years from 2004-2007 dualbooting XP and Ubuntu. More and more I used XP only for games, Photoshop and my favourite tag-editor "mp3tag".

So yesterday I wanted to reinstall my box because of the forthcoming release of Feisty.
My Windows Installation was getting slow as hell anyway, so i decided to reinstall Windows, too.

Popped in the Windows CD, and a floppy disk with the SATA driver. I thought: Boy, its the year 2007 now!:rolleyes: 
Anyway, during installation, the XP installer loaded the drivers from the floppy, mounted my SATA drive, let me configure my partitions. But then something strange happend: When the drive was formatted and the copying of the files began, the installer wanted to copy the drivers from floppy AGAIN, but it couldnt access the floppy drive anymore! What the..????
So I spent yesterday about 4 hours of changing floppy drives, floppy cables, floppy disks, setting bios to default, etc. Man, I felt like 10 years ago. Anway I had no success.

So, about 3 o clock in the morning i was fed up and made the decision: NO MORE WINDOWS FOR ME! NOT EVER!

Writing this from a fresh Feisty install.:guitar:
