### Post by DoctorMO on 2006-10-13
If all the time of all the people were to be paid they'd have to be paid more than that. but the fact is most people do what they love and those that earn a living from it are willing to accept a pay cut in order to work for the likes of IBM on open source projects.

Open source is like a culture of software; every artist adds his creative touch and those that are good are used by many, those that are bad are reused and improved until they are good. the culture becomes richer the more people you have in it.

Closed source is like a single entity making a single product or set of products. the only people feeding those products are those in R&D and that team is very small. this both explains the out of touch nature of these products and the feeling that it's not been through the riggors of a dawinian culture like open source.
