### Post by dejavu_01 on 2010-05-28
For the last couple of years I had been having compatibility issues with Linux since I got my current mother board (Asus M2N MX Plus). Any linux distros including Ubuntu would either crash during installation or refuse to load up GDM, that I have to resort to windows XP. When 10.04 LTS was released, I grab the ISO and give it a try (dedicated the whole harddisk for it) and find that the 10.04 runs flawlessly. Everything works. I find that Ubuntu has become mature enough and is extremely simple, user friendly and intuitive, and I really mean that. It can easily fulfills my personal day to day computing needs. The simplicity and Unix folder structure, /home and user specific configuration files is infinitely preferable and logical than Windows.
