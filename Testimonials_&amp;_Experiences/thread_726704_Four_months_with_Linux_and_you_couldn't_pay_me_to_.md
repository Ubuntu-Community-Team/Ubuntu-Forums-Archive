### Post by fullmetalgerbil on 2008-03-17
I installed Ubuntu in December with next to no prior knowledge of Linux. I had only owned a computer for about a year, had learned about Open Source via the web, and supporting it ideologically I figured I'd install a Linux OS. My friends tried to warn me off by telling me I'd never be able to handle the learning curve, but I decided to try anyways and Ubuntu seemed the right distribution for me.
I was amazed from the start at just how easy the learning curve really was, and getting everything dialed in "just so" made it more like a fun do-it-yourself project than a pain in the neck like it was using Windows. The amount of choices as far as customization made me a big fan from the beginning, plus the speed and stability of Ubuntu very much impressed me. There were things I had to learn, and yes, I had to make friends with the terminal, but I learned more about computers in general in the first month than I had using Windows for a year.
Eventually I began to try out other distributions-and I went through a bunch of them, Debian, PCLinuxOS, Linux Mint, Open SUSE...and really, none of them I liked as much as Ubuntu. It was easy to see why the folx at Linux Questions.org voted Ubuntu Distribution of the Year.
I cant say at any point that I've missed Windows a bit, and really I can't see ever going back to it.
