### Post by Dynaflow on 2009-05-05
We lost an Ubuntu convert today.  My housemate, who asked me a few months ago if there was anything I could do to make her borked Windows XP laptop work better, and who has been happily running Ubuntu ever since, has hit an insurrmountable roadblock to her continued use of Linux: the Apple iPod Touch (FW 2.2.1).

Due to Apple's [ongoing war with its customers]("http://marcansoft.com/blog/2009/01/using-amarok-and-other-itunesdb-compatible-software-with-the-iphone-2x/"), the newer iTouch firmware doesn't want to play nice with anything but iTunes, on any platform, and since Apple doesn't deign to release a Linux version of Steve Jobs' answer to Windows Media Player and Wine can't run it, an un-jailbroken iPod Touch is semi-useless to a Linux user.

My housemate paid quite a bit of money to Apple to get this device, and she is adamant that she does not want it jailbroken because of the risk of voiding the warranty if it craps out.  That is her right; Apple's warranty terms *are* somewhat paranoia-inducing, and I am loath to push the point too hard.  Since the iPod Touch is worth more than her laptop, she has asked me to strip out Ubuntu and re-expand the rump Windows partition I left on the drive when I did the original installation.  

Even though my housemate dislikes Windows and was happy using Ubuntu, she wants the full functionality of her iPod Touch badly enough to tolerate Windows until she can afford to buy ...

...a Mac.

### Post by aysiu on 2009-05-06
This is why Linux migration has to happen slowly (it also helps if the person migrating actually *wants* to migrate and isn't being coerced by a Linux-using family member or friend).

If I knew someone interested in giving Linux a go, I would first assess her software needs: does she use Windows-only or Windows/Mac-only software that does not have a proper Linux equivalent? If she could get by on open source software, I'd start her with open source Windows software in Windows first. Then I'd set her up with a dual-boot after a time. But even before the whole process started, I would tell her that moving to Linux means you have to be careful about the hardware you buy (no Lexmark printers, no Broadcom wireless cards, no iPod Touches or iPhones).

Someone who's down with OpenOffice, Firefox, Thunderbird, Pidgin, Songbird, Skype, GIMP, and VLC; and who also likes Sandisk instead of Apple is a perfect candidate for Linux.

Someone who's down with AutoCAD, Adobe CS3, Microsoft OneNote, and Outlook Exchange; and who also likes the latest iPhone... not a perfect candidate for Linux.
