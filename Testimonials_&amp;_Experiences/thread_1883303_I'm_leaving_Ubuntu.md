### Post by towheedm on 2011-11-18
You may well say 'Another one bites the dust'.

You may say that I started my Linux experience with Ubuntu Karmic, although I did dabble in it from way back as 2000 (I think).  In Dec 2009, while going through some old CD's, I stumbled upon a Ubuntu LiveCD (I think it was a 5.x version).  Rather frustrated with XP at the time, I decided to give it a try.

Started it up, saw a link to the Ubuntu website, followed the link, saw a new version 9.10 was out, D/l'ed it, burnt a CD and the rest is history.

I think all that I've learned about Linux on the whole all started out because of this.  In the two years of using Ubuntu, I done two main tutorials and was presently on a third.

Unfortunately, my last update of Maverick caused constant random crashes on my system.  A rather frustrating experience I must say.

This together with the fact that my last few questions both here and on Launchpad kept going unanswered, either because no one is either willing and/or able to answer them.  Let's not even talk about filing bugs that the devs think is neither critical nor important enough to answer.

Well, I've been backing up my important files while I write this short goodbye, and it just finished.

I'm off to Debian stable for now, it's been working flawlessly for the last two weeks and I've successfully built the latest versions of my most used apps from source without any problems.  My testing is done and I am now going to clean and reconfigure my drives and start afresh.

Unlike others who have migrated, I'll still be monitoring the forums, if only to continue to provide support for those who are using my 'Definitive Guide to Theming GRUB 2'.

It's been a mind-opening two years.

### Post by recluce on 2011-11-20
Actually, I am now sure that I am leaving as well. I came on board with Ubuntu 8.10, upgraded to 9.04 and loved it. Started with 10.04 alpha1 (aside the main install of Jaunty), was more than impressed and moved to 10.04 as my main OS a few weeks after release date - I am still running 10.04 and I still find it to be the optimal OS for me. I also played with 10.10 from alpha1 to final release and found it excellent (but no incentive to move from 10.04). Things went downhill with 11.04 and haven't gotten better for me with 11.10. 

Just two days ago, I installed Mint 12 RC (to play around) and I am really impressed. They have managed to tweak Gnome-Shell to look and work just about the same as the classic Gnome, just faster and more snappy. As further alternatives, you have Gnome 3 Classic mode and MATE available.

Sure, all three alternatives under Mint still need a bit of polishing. For example, under Gnome-Shell with "Mint Gnome Shell Extensions" panel applets are almost non-existent at this point - and I really don't like missing my Sensors Applet and CPU Frequency Monitor. Also, while one click selection of workspaces is possible, you don't get the mini-icons showing what windows are open in each of the workspaces (lower right panel on Gnome 2). An no wobbly windows or other special effects yet. :rolleyes:

I didn't spend much time in Gnome 3 Classic, it just feels like a less polished version of Gnome-Shell with MGSE. MATE works, but is more like an early alpha, missing quite a bit of Gnome 2 functionality.

However, just as over here at Ubuntu, the Mint guys have another six months to polish everything until the next LTS version. Baring a miraculous improvement with Ubuntu 12.04, I will move my work environment to Mint 13 and retire Ubuntu from all machines here.

Don't get me wrong, I am still thankful to Ubuntu, no Mint without it, just as I am thankful to Debian. However, I could never live with with Debian's idiosyncracies - and now I feel less than comfortable with Ubuntu's new particularities. 

oh btw: Launchpad seems to be the new /dev/null of Ubuntu developers, judging by the attention that most bugs get, even the serious ones.
