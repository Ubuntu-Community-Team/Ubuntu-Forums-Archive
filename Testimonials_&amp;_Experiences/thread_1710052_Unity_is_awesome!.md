### Post by 3Miro on 2011-03-20
Gnome2 officially came with Metacity as Windows Manager. Ubuntu and some other distributions replaced Metacity with Compiz, thus we got two "flavors" Gnome2 + Metacity and Gnome2 + Compiz.

In the very same manner, Gnome3 would come in two "flavors" Gnome3 + Gnome-shell and Gnome3 + Unity. Unity IS Gnome!

Gnome-shell is a huge change compared to how things worked before. Unity is hardly a bug change at all. I just installed 11.04 Alpha 3 this morning, and Unity is simply a dock plus different menu, minus the deprecated gnome-panel. Everything else is Gnome + Compiz exactly as it was in 10.10 and 10.04 (and 9.10 and 9.04 and 8.10 ... )

I wouldn't call Unity awesome and I probably wouldn't use it as my main environment, but I also don't see why people are complaining about it.
