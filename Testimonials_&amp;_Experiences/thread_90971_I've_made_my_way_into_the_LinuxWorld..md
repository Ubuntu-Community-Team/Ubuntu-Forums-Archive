### Post by bulldogzerofive on 2005-11-16
I have to agree with Jan.  I tried a couple of linuxes (linucii?) before settling with Ubuntu.  None of these really were "better" or "worse" than Ubuntu; each simply had its own strengths and weaknesses.  The difference, however, is in the forums and the wiki.  The unofficial ubuntuguide was both helpful and educational.  Beyond that, I never had a problem that i could not solve by looking for it on the forums.  I find the wiki less helpful, but maybe at some point in the future i can help write some good how-tos.

In comparison to the forums, google is useless.  It really is.

Furthermore, the Ubuntu community just does things that make sense.  When i want to do a clean install, i can just run the new automatix script and that takes care of probably 2/3 of the customizing i wanted to do.  It is not that i do not know how to get, for example, JRE, but that gets boring after a while and this just takes care of it.

So, kudos to the community and the forum staff.  Thanks!
