### Post by faustism on 2010-08-14
Hello all,
    I've just come back from my distro hopping adventure, and only now can I appreciate how things run so smoothly in Ubuntu.  The recent rants by various sources about how Canonical hasn't contributed much upstream (to GNOME specifically) made me want to support those guys that supposedly are contributing a lot.  I tried Fedora 13, and to my delight, it worked very well, very smooth. That is, until I went a on software trial spree giving yum a workout.  When I went to uninstall all the software that just wasn't for me, it left a lot of no-longer-needed dependencies on my machine. (which just annoys me, I love Computer Janitor) Then, I decided to try out the mother distro, debian.  Aside from chromium wanting to print 100 copies of about 10% of web pages, everything worked fine, until i did an install on another computer only to get a boot error upon reboot.  So, I'm back on Ubuntu, and everything just works.  I really appreciate what the development team is doing for users, like myself, and for the Linux community by showing people that Linux is a viable option, and that it can "just work."

That said, if you think that I missed a great contender in the field of usability, I would love to hear your input.
Long Live Ubuntu! (or at least until someone makes something better.)

btw, I'm not a complete n00b, Im not afraid of the command line, I just dont like having to fix problems in one distro when those problems dont arise in another.
