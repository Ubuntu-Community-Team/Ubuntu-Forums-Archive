### Post by darth_indy on 2008-05-07
When I browse this thread, I see so many threads with titles like "Hardy Sucks!" or "Hardy Rocks!" I have a different point to make: Hardy works perfectly for me and my computer. Your mileage may - and probably will - vary, but I must say that Hardy has made Ubuntu perfect for me.

when I bought it, I went full-out with everything except the processor (which I upgraded but not all the way), and the RAM (which was far too expensive, and I upgraded to 2 GB shortly after purchase; I plan on maxing it out to 4 GB soon).

I bought my Dell Inspiron 1420n way back when they were offering it with Feisty. I loved it from the moment I got it, and was willing to live with the little quirks that it had. No desktop effects, no suspend/hibernate, things like that. All the normal issues like wireless were no problem since that was Dell's job to make sure components worked.

I later upgraded to Gutsy, and I noticed barely any difference except a couple extra configuration buttons here and there. I was able to enable Compiz with a few bugs, but I like my Ubuntu pretty so I bit the bullet and gave up my multiple desktops and some performance for wobbly windows (I know, I know; my priorities were out of whack).

When Hardy came out, I was going to wait for Dell to come out with their own ISO so I'd make sure everything worked. But when I had to reinstall XP for a friend that b0rked it with a registry "cleaner" and convinced her to dual-boot Hardy, I decided to give the LiveCD a go to see if I would have any of the reported issues.

IT WORKED!

PERFECTLY!

I was so excited. Wireless, sound, Compiz, video, anything that I'd had problems with before, were fixed. I backed up my home directory and did a fresh install (to make sure it didn't inherit any Gutsy glitches) and I have had no problems, and it has fixed any others I have had:

Compiz: Works flawlessly with one exception... the rain-on-the-screen effect. Which I can definitely live without. I played with the desktop cube for at least five minutes straight, and I still do just for the heck of it.

Suspend/Hibernate: I've done a test and it appears to work. The only program I was running while I tried it was Pidgin, besides those I have at startup (screenlets mostly), so it may not work under a heavy load, but I had no problem bringing it out of suspend or hibernate. I hadn't really missed those features before because I often keep my laptop at home, but this came at the perfect time as I've started traveling a lot more.

Scrolling: My trackpad hadn't been able to scroll when I recieved it, then I was able to fix it, then some update somewhere disabled it again. And now it's back. It's not a huge thing to most people, but when you're traveling and in cramped quarters, it's far better than the up/down arrows or page up/down. I had no idea how much I missed it until I had it back.

Sound: This was only an issue I was afraid of having. I'd read many threads on sound issues with Hardy, and since I'm a pretty big audiophile I would have really missed it. But no problems at all. In fact, I think th speakers work even better now (they were staticy sometimes under Gutsy).

Video: I had problems with this mostly because of enabling Compiz in Gutsy with my blacklisted Intel video card, but all that is now resolved. I can now watch Spaceballs and The Matrix with impunity!

Little things that bothered me: I am glad for the small improvements given by OpenOffice 2.4 being installed, over 2.3. Also, the default BitTorrent app is much better (though I still prefer Deluge... but I don't want to start a torrent war here).

Well, that's about all I can think of right now. But I'm incredibly happy with Hardy.

Hope everyone else has the same success. Cheers!
