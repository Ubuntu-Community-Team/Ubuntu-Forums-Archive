### Post by thorbs on 2010-06-17
Having taken my first steps in this forum, something really comes to my mind. Its about the ability to respect and value other peoples opinion and experience of Ubuntu and life.

It is for sure that people will have different experience no matter what subject one is talking about. And allowing other people to have other experiences than oneself is a sign of strength. 

A key to the amazing progress that we have been able to do as humanity (no matter how flawed we still are), is our ability to keep a little self doubt. This allow us to consider other peoples input and receive even there critique. This is the seed of our growth both individually and as a group. 

I have noticed that sometimes when it come to Ubuntu, the ability to accept the differences in other peoples point of view and different experiences is lacking some what. The small bit of doubt that opens up for a discussion is not there. This can feel a bit cultish when people who a critical is insulted and personally devalued, just because they have a different opinion about Ubuntu. 

Now unless one thinks Ubuntu is perfect, and there is no need for further growth, this is not so good, because it closes us from the audibility to learn. What is more it closes the Ubuntu community to only have space for the true believers. This I believe is not in the spirt of neither Ubuntu, Linux nor Open Source. 

I think it is important that we who participate in the Ubuntu community makes an effort to make space for those who think different and have different experiences. There by the community becomes stronger and Ubuntu becomes stronger.


t.
