### Post by TBABill on 2011-04-04
I don't feel the intent was necessarily gnome vs xfce, but rather unity on top of gnome being less friendly than xfce. I have installed and kept the beta of 11.04 on my machine for a whole day now and I have forced myself to just use it. And I have. Hour after hour and I just kept feeling like the OS was in my way. Just try to open 3 sessions of Firefox, for instance. Click the left panel to get the first open, but click it again while it's already open and what happens? Nothing. Click again....nothing. You have to open your applications menu or go into it another way and click search...then you can open it again. Why on earth would something so simple as multiple sessions of the same app be a big deal to do? Imagine multiple spreadsheets. You open one with Calc, then have to hunt for the app via a menu system by searching or clicking to open all your apps, then scroll down to it if you chose the open all apps method.
 
The whole usability of Unity as a desktop shell is wasteful of time and not very intuitive or productive. Since we're in beta it is what it is. They'll fix bugs (crashes!), but the functionality is already complete or mostly complete. Not a good "out of the box" experience. Yes, I know I can fall back to Gnome, but will a new user? Will they know how? Will they even want to after experiencing Unity? I'm not sure what those answers are and each of us will have our own feeling after using it, but I try to put myself back into the shoes I wore as a newcomer to Ubuntu and wonder if I'd even give Edu/Myth/X/Kubuntu a shot after this.
 
For me I'll seek what else may work better. Unity will probably be off my machine by late tonight, but I am determined to give it another evening of use. My mind is not closed, but the flaws jump out quickly. The more I have to click to get to a destination, the less I will want to use something. And I refuse to type in a search block to find an already installed app. That's just senseless unless I didn't already know where it was. But I did type "syn" a few times just to open synaptic and "term" to get to the terminal launcher....crazy!
 
My unity skills are weak and I acknowledge that. There are tricks I have not learned and shortcuts I do not know, but just having to find workarounds or tricks because the OS requires more work of a user is a sign that things aren't right. Nobody wants to click more, search for apps, scroll through every app on the system or have a semi functional launch panel to use. They would like something that cuts out extra work, streamlines functionality and enhances the user experience. Unfortunately, this isn't it, or at least not yet, which is a shame because the desktop feels fairly snappy to use. Xfce is possibly next to try for me :)
