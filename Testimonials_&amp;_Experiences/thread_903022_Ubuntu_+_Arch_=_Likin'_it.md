### Post by jayson.rowe on 2008-08-27
Good going. I love Ubuntu's simplicity - I've been using Linux long enough I don't have the desire to be fiddling w/ the underpinnings of my OS all the time, but running a Distro like Arch will teach you a heck of a lot about what makes a Linux box tick, and you can apply those skills to other distros down the road. Plus, you get a great since of accomplishment when you get one of the more "advanced" distro's up and running - you made decisions about what you wanted, and you created it. As great as the "out of the box" distro's like Ubuntu, Mandriva, etc. have become, nothing can replace the power of choice you get w/ a distro like Arch.

My "teacher" was Slackware, yours can be Arch!
