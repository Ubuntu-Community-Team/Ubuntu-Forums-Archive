### Post by N00b-un-2 on 2009-03-30
Don't take this the wrong way.  I appreciate Ubuntu for what it's done for me.  I've seamlessly transitioned from being another Microsoft whore to switching entirely to Linx and free open source goodness... just not with Ubuntu.
I've been using Ubuntu since 8.04, I've rolled back and forth back to Gutsy, then to Hardy, then Intrepid, and back to Hardy.  I can't quite put my finger on it, but something was just not right.  I've experienced a lot of glitches and hangs with trying to get my computer to do what I want it to do.  I've spent hours upon hours researching, rewriting things and geeking out on one problem after another.
Don't get me wrong, I'll take an operating system with "quirks" (Ubuntu) over an OS with Viruses (Windows) anyday.
I just transitioned from Ubuntu 8.04.2 to openSUSE 11.1 and I couldn't be happier.  Everything just seems to work better out of the box.  There was no craziness with my wifi drivers or my graphics card like I've always had to deal with in Ubuntu.  I'm very happy with openSUSE.  I had to do a LOT less tinkering with it before I got it configured how I like it.  Not only that, but it runs a bit faster and is less glitchy.  having MP3s and AVI files work out of the box was a nice improvement as well.  One of my biggest issues with Gnome has always been the lack of support for the Global Menu (Mac Style Menu Bar, goes very nicely with AWN or Cairo).  I've had the global menu configured on every ubuntu install I've done and it ALWAYS was a pain in the rear to get it to work, and even when it did work it would always be a little buggy, and would crash fairly often.  Lo and behold, the Gobal Menu applet was available in the repos on openSUSE and up and working within 5 minutes of a fresh install.  Go figure.

Perhaps I'll give Jaunty a whirl when it reaches maturity in a few months, but my motto has always been if it aint broke don't fix it.  I'm happy where I'm at with openSUSE, so I doubt I'll ever go back to Ubuntu.
