### Post by DrMega on 2008-11-12
Ubuntu is great. I've been using it for about 2 years now.

Dapper was rock solid, and fast. There were times I had to follow tutorials and seek advice to get stuff done, but it was great. The only trouble is I had to do a bit of work to use it.

I skipped Edgy, giving it the briefest of look, but it looked OK.

Skipped Feisty.

Gutsy, now that was great. A real contender for the mainstream I thought. Nicely polished, rock solid (in my experience), a good range of apps and very easy to configure.

Then came Hardy, the LTS version. The version that was to be pushed out for serious use. The version that was going to take over the desktop in industry as well as on home machines. Hardy was great. I had to do so little work on that one. Why? Because I couldn't. I was one of the many users who found it unusable due to the frequent random freeze issue (which despite being well covered on this forum and on LaunchPad, is, according to many on this very forum, an issue unique to me).

So, I give Intrepid a try. Perhaps this is even better still. I have to say I am impressed by its minimalist approach. If ever I need an OS that will force me to relax, Intrepid will be my top choice. It is great. After waiting nearly 20 minutes, plenty of time to make a cup of tea, for it to boot, I get to look at a plain brown screen for as long as I like. Fantastic. I guess I am lucky that I have the necessary fairly standard hardware to support this impressive feature.

I wonder what Jaunty will bring. I look forward in anticipation of more relaxing time, with even less to do. Perhaps I might not even have to choose an option from the boot menu, perhaps it will just give me a very zen like blank screen.
