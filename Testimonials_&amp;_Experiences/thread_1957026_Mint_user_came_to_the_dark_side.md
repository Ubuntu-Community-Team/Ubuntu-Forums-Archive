### Post by anejo on 2012-04-12
Umm... the purple side!?
I've been a staunch Mint user ever since a Mint rel3 CD arrived on my desk as a freebie.

When Mint 12 arrived with the new gnome 3 shell and new methods of working... it caused some frustrations to say the least. After some months of using G3  in default and classic modes--and on occasion defaulting to Cinnamon--I  struggled to transition to the new look-n-feel. Classic G and  Cinnamon at the end of the day, are about getting into a G2 way of  working albeit with G3.

Recently I had the opportunity to install both Ubuntu and Xubuntu 11.10.
Being an ex-XFCE user, it was easy to  be impressed with Xubuntu but I digress... back to Unity. After playing  on the live CD, I made a partition and for awhile dual booted. After the G3 debacle I couldn't get what the fuss with Unity was about. Ok. So the 'docky' is a  'dash' and instead of sitting horizontally it sits at the left vertical.  It didn't look cool at first... but it works. The dash  button--imho--works better than the one in G3, its fast enough and has  more features and options than the G3 dash... imho.

So. After a few weeks here in purple land, I find Unity to be full  of interesting surprises... that just work for me. Sure... you don't have  to use the dash and can revert to G3 methods but I find that 'classicmenu-indicator' satisfies enough when I don't want to use the dash and best of all--again imho--I get to use my fav compiz features again... that are not available in gnome3.
