### Post by seanc7 on 2009-01-04
I just want to share my experiences with getting a DLink wireless network card working on an older Toshiba P4 laptop I have.

That laptop started with WinXP SP3 but after spending a day on it, that wireless card didn't work. The drivers on the CD didn't work, the updated drivers from the web didn't work, I installed Windows updates that the DLink support site suggested, all to no avail.

So after getting fed up, I threw on Ubuntu 8.10 (which I run on my desktop already) and after messing around with madwifi for a bit, I realized I didn't need that, the Network Manager in Gnome automatically worked with it.

So after 1 day and about 45 minutes (and that 1 day being wasted on trying to get it work on Windows), I have a laptop with a working wireless G network card. All thanks to Ubuntu.

I've been a Linux user for about 3 years now, all of that time with either Ubuntu or Debian.

It's still amazing how things "just work" the way they should. Like Windows was supposed to be.

Sean
