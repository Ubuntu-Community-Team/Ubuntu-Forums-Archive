### Post by vexingmodstwo on 2008-06-24
I've read a bunch of negative testimonials complaining that 1) Linux isn't ready for primetime and 2) Hardy Heron "sucks" and I felt compelled to relay my experience with Linux and Hardy in particular.

I am what is probably the "target audience" when it comes to Linux going up against Windows in the desktop OS arena.  A linux noob, if you will.

I have to say that I am now a huge fan of Linux.  I've always thought it was an OS for "hackers" and that it was all command line stuff until I saw a few screenshots and figured I'd give it a shot.  I had the room on my hard drive to peel off 10 Gigs and give it a whirl.

The only real problem I had was getting my wireless internet to work.  After a few days of reading and getting help from the most helpful group of people I've ever come across on the net (yes, that's you guys) I not only got the wireless running, I learned a lot of command line stuff along the way.  

Since then I've done some customization (i.e. found a dock I liked and installed it and configured it to my liking, and installed conky and tweaked the settings) and I have to say that I'm a full blown convert to Linux and would completely wipe out my windows partition if there wasn't some pretty expensive software on it that I don't want to get rid of.

I'm on a Dell Inspiron 1501, btw, and like I mentioned above, the only component that didn't work out of the box is the wireless card (Broadcom/Dell).

So in summary:  I'd say Linux and Ubuntu, in particular, is about 90% ready for primetime and will be there once there's more out of the box compatibility with a wider (not that it isn't wide now) range of hardware configurations.  And, to be honest, there might just be a section of the market that will never be ready for Linux, so in that regard, it might actually be more like 98% ready for primetime.
