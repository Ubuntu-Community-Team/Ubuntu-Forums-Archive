### Post by zambizzi on 2006-01-01
I've been a staunch Gentoo zealot for a couple of years now...and I would say that 1-2 yrs. ago it was still the finest Linux "distro" available...especially for those who really want to learn how things work under-the-covers.

About 2 mo. ago I recommended Ubuntu to my boss who was testing out linux distros as a true beginner (wouldn't want to cause permanent psychological damage by exposing him to Gentoo right off-the-bat.)  He tried it, loved it, said that "everything just works"...I said puah!  I use Gentoo and ONLY Gentoo!  After all, it is superior in every way...I have total control over my system!

That *is* true...Gentoo gives you bottom-up control of your hardware *and* software...while educating you in a boot-camp sort of method of Linux use.

However, the QA process (such as it is) with Gentoo seems to have slipped in the last year dramatically.  To make a long story short; I had so many problems w/ both the Gnome and KDE desktops once they were finally up and running that my system was unusable as a desktop system.  Most of my problems went unanswered in forums where I used to get near-immeidate answers to problems...I was pretty frustrated.

So, I burned a copy of Ubuntu and now I have a fully-functional Gnome 2.12 desktop that I honestly couldn't be happier with!  There simply cannot be a better linux distro available today for the desktop.  I'll stick w/ Gentoo on the server only because it doesn't require x + DE and is more powerful on the manual-configuration front, IMO.

Great work guys!

No, it's not, but that is the spirit of Gentoo...it's not a beginner's distro...in fact it isn't even a distribution, per se.

That's the beauty of it, you build it yourself and make it your own...and it's tailored exactly to your hardware (if you do it right.)

I've actually completed dozens of successful Gentoo installations as desktops and servers and have loved it up until the last 12 mo. or so.  What used to be the absolute best online community has slipped a little, I think, and a lot of critical issues were going unanswered for me in the forums.  I had to assume that they were critical bugs and there just were no solutions to what I was asking.

It sorta breaks my heart as I had really grown comfortable w/ it...but maintaining a gentoo box just finally became unmanageable.

Like I said, as a server, it can't be beat and I'll continue to use it there...no doubt!
