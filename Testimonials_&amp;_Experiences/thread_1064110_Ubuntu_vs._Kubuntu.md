### Post by jbysmith on 2009-02-08
I'd have to agree with the above sentiments.  For Gnome, Ubuntu does as stellar job.  On the KDE end, not so much.  I've had nothing but problems with it. A couple other distros just do it better, at least in my opinion.  

OpenSUSE does KDE absurdly well, Arch's KDE-Mod is pretty good too; stupidly fast but takes a while to configure as you're doing it from the ground up, but that's just a one time thing as it's a rolling release. Disaster aside you never need to reinstall or risk the six month dist-upgrades; you're always current. PCLinuxOS wasn't half bad. Sabayon was another one that was pretty snazzy. Even comes with proprietary drivers and codecs by default.  Just keep in mind that one's based on Gentoo, and you may need to spend a lot of time with Google if you want more than what's included.

Haven't tried Debian's lately, may be worth looking in to if you prefer the Debian way of doing things, which is what Ubuntu is derived from.  Lenny is heading towards release status and is very up to date, and if Etch is any indication it'll be supported for a looooong time.  Doesn't get much more bleeding edge current than a Sid installtion, but that can work against you as there's the odd breakage from time to time.
