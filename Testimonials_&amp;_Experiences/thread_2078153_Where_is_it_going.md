### Post by zebutron on 2012-10-30
I have only posted once before here. I am not a member of this community in the communicative sense but rather of the fraternal sense. I've been using Ubuntu in tandem with other OSes for around 5 years. Originally it was only curiosity and excitement that led me to Ubuntu and Linux in general. Very quickly I came to appreciate so many of the strengths that Ubuntu represents. There has been constant issues as well. These problems are usually a result of the proprietary enterprises and that of the open-source world. I understand this well and even support it. It felt almost as a battle against those trying to keep us away from our dreams. "Those fiends!" I would say to myself, "How dare they put a chastity belt on this USB WiFi dongle?! Why should they be able to control our hearts?" Perhaps that is a little overly dramatic but the sentiment is true. A struggle for freedom. I accept that struggle as my own. 

Things feel different now. It isn't strictly a change in the desktop GUI, or dropping support for a well loved application. These just seem like a regular changing of the time. Ubuntu has always been flexible, resilient and customizeable. It is here that I feel a loss. What I held as a standard, that I might have to suffer a few bumps but would always come out feeling like I was using the better system, is disappearing. If I can quickly and easily do more with Windows7 or OSX, then why would I use Ubuntu? Every place I turn while trying to customize my expeience seems to be a deadend. These are not complex, advanced user issues. A prime example would be system sounds. I can only select what the alert sound, and only the alert sound, will be out of 5 choices and no options of simply selecting something from a personal file. There are work arounds of course, but why should there be? What was once simple and standard has become hidden and frustrating. 

I am not posting this, as per the rules, to ask for help. Simply, I am sharing my reflections and lamentations over my lost love.

### Post by Chdslv on 2012-10-31
> **zebutron said:**
> Now that I have looked into it a little bit, what is the differnce between GnomeRemix and gnome-shell? Thanks for any reply.

Nothing, except a bit of eye-candy.

If you have 12.10, you can install Gnome-shell 3.6.1 and even try to change the shell scheme. If you install mini.iso and only Gnome-shell, you get your minimalist UbuntuGnome Remix. Then, you can add any apps you like.

If you move the repos to Raring, you'd still get Gnome-shell 3.6.1, but you can get Gnome 3.7.1, only you don't have many extensions yet. By the time, Gnome 3.8 comes out, those extensions would be old too. Gnome is growing very fast, too fast for my liking as I just can't have the extensions, which are from the community, not from Gnome devs.

The screenshot is of Raring Ringtail with kernel 3.7.1-rc2 and Gnome 3.7.1, built from mini.iso No Gnome-core or Unity. Two more apps, Shotwell and Tweak-tool other than what you see in the screenshot yet. 

Unity will be installed later. Only needed apps would be installed as the need arises.
