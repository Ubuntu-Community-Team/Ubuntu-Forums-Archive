### Post by nonly1n on 2008-03-17
Dear Reader

Does anyone happen to Google news Linux or Ubuntu. Just see what great new thing is happening in the Linux world. Honestly i feel like am getting addicted to it. My homepage is google and when i look it i feel a need to just google linux. Addiction, no, not really wouldn't think i got to that level, yet. But i prefer to refer to it as just a normal satisfaction of what am getting and looking forward to something new. I've been preaching Linux for 4 yrs, when i was 15/16, my eyes where open as wide as a boy who just a got a dog he always saw in the window, when i was handed my first Linux distro to go home and remove boring Windows XP and try out Slackware Linux(i loved it what can i say). I got used to all the power i was handed and just fiddling with stuff and being able to compile programs to my systems specifics so they would run faster, honestly i think that's when i really lost my :)(figure it out). I fell in love with something so free and worked so great and if i run into problems i had an experienced person to help me out, plus the internet ready almost instantaneous help. I jumped from distro to distro, tried Mandrake which i thought was to windowish, and i was kind of the KDE fan, tried SuSE, which i thought was a devine gift sent from heaven(alot has changed), bumped into Ubuntu(just when it came out) and honestly when i didn't figure it out i moved on. I honestly wouldn't have thought then that Ubuntu would be my OS of choice now. first off i hated Gnome thought it was unuser(?) friendly and also didn't know how to get KDE on it and didn't understand why i could not SU(super user any more). Provided i moved from South Africa back to my home country Zambia(where i'd be lucky if i got a chance on the internet) so i settled back to using Windows XP, which felt cold because i didn't get the love and care and i got from the Linux Community from Microsoft. Well i went 2 3 yrs through hell and happened to almost forget about Linux. Last year i started College in Canada and up until April dared not breathe a word about Linux, till a friend of mine told me about Ubuntu 7.04. Well with my previous experience with it, all i had to say was tried it and denied it so I don't want to know. But later on i decided, lyk wtf, its not like am goin to die, and anyway my sisters laptop fell sick and wasn't willing to install windows on it so i put Ubuntu 7.04 on it and let her use it, provided she left my PC alone. At some point I even changed Desktop Environments to KDE being well familiar with it  and thought Kubuntu may just be what i was looking for.Don't know when and don't know how but @ some point in my life i decided to use Ubuntu as is on my PC and what do u know, I'm a die hard Gnome and Ubuntu fan, i preach the word again and I'm proud to actually be free of windows(not as much i produce music and Linux equivalents don't cut it:)) I just wrote this so i can thank Anna and Eugene ( [http://www.opencafe.org.za](http://www.opencafe.org.za) )for introducing me to Linux 4 yrs ago. I owe every bit of my knowledge and love for it to them. And also thank them for giving me this hardknocked addiction to just want to google linux or ubuntu whenever I get on-line. wierd but wierd is normal to ppl who understand. Linux:guitar:my life.

Thank You Mark Shuttleworth(sp) for giving the world something un-replaceable think of a world without Ubuntu and you'll feel me. 

ps. i'm a computing science major(undeclared but am sure when i get to it it'll be my choice) who can't wait till he lend his hand.

:popcorn:
