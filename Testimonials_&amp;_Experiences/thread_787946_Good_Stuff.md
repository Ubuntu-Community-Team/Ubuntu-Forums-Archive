### Post by TurboRush on 2008-05-09
So I'll start off with my story, which is probably going to sound like everybody else&#8217;s. Please note, these are my observations and opinions, get ready for a damn book.

I had dabbled with a bunch of other distro's through out the years, Suse, RedHat, yada yada. Getting them all setup to at least a workable state, although I was somewhat skeptical of my configurations and whether they were viable long term or if I just got lucky. The most reoccurring problems being with getting my wireless to work and the appropriate screen resolution. So in a nutshell things were working, but never to the point where I could consider it more than something to just play around with.

Enter 8 or 9 months ago... I buy a new dual core AMD with 3gig of RAM yada yada, ships with Vista, which I was actually pretty excited about. Expecting the thing to fly with all this new "horsepower" I light her up and am just disappointed. The harddrive is constantly going, takes too long (in my opinion) to fire up multiple instances of Firefox, and or other items. 

In the mean time I decide to dig out an super old P3 800 with 200meg of RAM to act as a little web server running XP. Works fine, but you can definitely tell that XP was weighing this thing down. You knew when someone would hit my website just because of the HDD noise. I would reboot it every 10 days or so when my site just seemed to be slow.

Back to the main thread... I decide to try out a few distro's in a VM in Vista when someone mentions Ubuntu to me. Download it (7.10), install and set it up in a VM with 1g of RAM allocated and WOW, Ubuntu is running faster inside the VM with only a gig of RAM than Vista does as the resident system. Well that does it, not wanting to risk losing data I go down to the store and buy a second harddrive and install Ubuntu on the second harddrive. The thing CRANKS, I finally feel like I am getting the performance out of the machine that I should and this time, instead of spending days getting wireless and screen resolution in place... I am up and going in a few hours.

Few weeks go by and I decide it's time to gut the little p3 "server" and roll over to Ubuntu Server Edition... I get everything setup and configure it to host 3 separate websites, and how does it respond... 

```
10:20:20 up 32 days, 21:40,  1 user,  load average: 0.35, 0.13, 0.10
```

It would be at 60+ days right now if I hadn't pushed the thing to its absolute limit. This computer does not sit idle either.. I am constantly in it on a daily basis messing around with new software, b2Evolution, drupal, gallery2, yada yada.

So to close the story out... I just recently reformatted the Vista partition and am running free and clear.

Feel free to slug on one of my websites, its not much, but I want to see if it croaks if 500+ people it over a short period... [www.cadzima.com](www.cadzima.com)
