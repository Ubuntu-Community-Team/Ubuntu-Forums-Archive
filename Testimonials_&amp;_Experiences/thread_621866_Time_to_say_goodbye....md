### Post by bgast1 on 2007-11-25
I probably fit into the category of asking first, and researching later. Although I have found myself checking out some of the threads that seem to talk about the same subject that I am ready to ask about. I spend most of my time in the absolute beginner threads. 

One thing, though that many of you advance users need to realize is that many people wanting to switch from Windows to Linux have absolutely no idea what Linux is. Linux looks cool, face it. Many of these people are not old enough to know what DOS is. They have no idea what it means to enter a command into a terminal. GUI is all they know. Asking them to research something is sometimes like asking them to search for a needle in a haystack. They have absolutely no clue. They are quite frankly intimidated by Linux. This group of people just like one poster alluded to above just wants the desktop effects, and their machine to work just like it did in windows, and right now. In most cases these people will waste your time, and be gone when things don't work out right. But having said that I think Vista will bring a lot of users over to LInux, if Microsoft doesn't get busy and fix it's problems.

However, with me, I would rather read a book on the subject than try to research it on a computer screen. Being old enough to know something about DOS, I happen to appreciate that you can probably get more done, and faster from a command line. I am neither a programmer or anything else, but I do think that if I am going to use an operating system on my computer I should know how it works. So I went out today and spent $50.00 on a book which is probably already out of date, even though the copyright is 2007.

What I find irritating is when someone insults someone who doesn't know what they are doing, and has forgotten that once they were newbies too. I luckily have not had that experience yet, but I have seen it. Another thing that newbies like myself need to remember is that when they post in the Absolute Beginner Threads, that probably a lot of us there are absolute beginners too, and couldn't give you answer no matter how much they would like to. 

Anyway, I completely agree that family comes first AWAYS. Good luck, maybe we will see you around.
