### Post by getmeoutofhere on 2008-01-16
Well, the problem didn't go away, and I reinstalled.

So, to me, the golden rule of Ubuntu is... make sure your files are in a convenient place, where they can be easily backed up.  Indeed, have as few essential files on your Ubuntu computer as possible - use another computer, or external storage.  Because, in my experience, you will have to do a complete re-install every month or so.  The install won't take long, and it's just part of the territory.  Also, take a note of the many ubuntu forum posts you read, to get your system working.  Then you won't have to look them up all over again when you reinstall.

It's interesting that there are a fair amount of posts saying how well people's grandmothers are getting on with Ubuntu.  Well, I suspect that the people who have most problems with Ubuntu are the people in the middle.  If you know very little about computers, then you might be OK.  You treat the computer and the OS with respect, you don't mess around, and nothing goes wrong.  If you're a computer expert, you're also fine.  If things go wrong, you've studied UNIX etc at school, you know what kind of problem you're dealing with, and you can fix it.  However the guys in the middle run into trouble.  They like playing, they're interested in the OS, but they don't have the knowledge or experience.  So when something goes wrong (that was probably caused by their ham-fisted attempts to adjust the system) they've had it, and they need to reinstall.  There's no system restore in Ubuntu!
