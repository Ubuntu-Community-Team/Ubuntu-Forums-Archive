### Post by santanu14 on 2008-11-23
Hello,

I had been a Windows user for more than 15 years and was really bored and irritated with its various problems. Couple of weeks back I decided to move ON to Ubuntu for my official work.

I installed Ubuntu on my IBM laptop and got MS-Office (on crossover linux), Evolution & Thunderbird email client, KVpnc, SoftokenII on Wine, Pigdin messenger etc application running. However after using the system for more than 10 days, here are my feedback -

- No support in Ubuntu for EAP-FAST, not even in roadmap it seems. Due to this I am not able to connect to my office WLAN.
- MS-Office on Crossover Linux is bad! Simple operations on Word, Excel, PowerPoint and Outlook causes crash in the application.
- PowerPoint can't copy drawing object from slide to slide of same/different file. Searching the support database in Crossover suggest that they do not have a solution to this at this time.
- Our office email server is Exchange. Unfortunately I couldn't figure out a single email client on Ubuntu that works even OK with MS-Exchange server. Tried Evolution, Thunderbird and Outlook on Crossover - all of them failed to produce an usable solution!

Though as an OS it is great and far more superior to MS-Windows, but due to these application issues the overall experience is not too good as you can see.

Until and unless the application experience is decent on this platform, the general adoption would be a issue in my view. I hope the developers would address these issues in near term so that more people can start using this OS.

Regards,
Santanu

### Post by Frogbarf on 2008-11-23
My cut on Ubuntu is that it's like the little girl of fable: when she's good, she's very good, but when she's bad she's awful. No, not awful; "downright horrible" is more like it.

Two things in particular get my knickers in a twist about Ubuntu:

1. Shortcut key assignments vary from application to application. Sometimes to copy it's ctrl+C, other times it's shift+ctrl+C. Sometimes to invoke menus you do the Windows-like "press alt, release it, press menu shortcut key" (I.e. F for the file menu). Other times it's "press alt, hold it down, press meu key". The latter sucks as it means you are chording (as it's called), whereas the other method means you only press one key at a time. But it's the inconsistency from one app to the next that drives one mad!

2. The other knicker twisting issue is the Ubuntu forum system. With something over 6 million threads (or is that messages?), trying to find what you need is like trying to find a needle in a haystack. Moreover, in far too many threads the replies are merely guesses: "do this, and show us the output", "try this", "try that", etc.

I've had more success on the Craigslist Linux forum than here in the Ubuntu Forums, I'm sorry to say.
