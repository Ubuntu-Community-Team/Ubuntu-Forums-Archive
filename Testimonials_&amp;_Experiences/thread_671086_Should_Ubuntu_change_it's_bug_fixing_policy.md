While this too is my knee-jerk reaction when I sit back and think about it a little I realize that it's not that simple. Canonical is selling their brand implicitly by getting the name out there and getting people committed to it and (hopefully) buying the LTS contracts for large offices. I would say there is a responsibility on some level to offer support that is credible and consistent.

Having said that, I've submitted bugs in many different technologies (both open source as well as proprietary) and I don't think I've ever come across a support group that consistently offers effective and well-thought out S/W support. All in all, I think we should look at this as a quick blip and assume that things will get better.
