### Post by gigaSproule on 2008-12-27
I've done a few searches on what people thought on this netbook, and they say they had a few problems. So I though I would write what my experience was.

My first experiences of Ubuntu were on my Dell Inspiron 1501. Now this was just before dell started selling Ubuntu on their products and before other companies did similar things, so there isn't much in the way of driver support for it. I still loved ubuntu, but it was always a pain to set it up. But the Lenovo S10e is available with Linux, so I thought I would install Ubuntu on it and see whether the driver issue has changed from my 1501. A lot of people said their wireless didn't work first time, but I found that even without the restricted drivers installed, it worked straight away. This is what always annoyed me with the 1501, because I couldn't get all the drivers without the internet and no wireless meant getting under a desk to steal a cable. I've set it all up to my usual setup and it's working great. So far, I've tested everything I can think of apart from the built-in microphone, but I'm sure if everything else works straight away, so will this.

I have to say, ignore what other people say about it, it's deffinatly a worth while buy at £279 (probably going to get quite a bit cheaper now it's after christmas).

Installing Ubuntu won't effect whether the computer can POST. The installer only puts things on the hard drive and the hard drive is not needed to POST.
