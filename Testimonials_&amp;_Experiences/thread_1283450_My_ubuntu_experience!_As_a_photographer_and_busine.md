### Post by ElSlunko on 2009-10-05
Hello Everyone!

Just thought it was about time to give my testimonial of my experience with Ubuntu.

About 2 years ago was the first time I even noticed linux (ubuntu). My local Circuit City (R.I.P.) had it running on a laptop near the entrance to the store. No one was around so I started playing around on it for a few minutes. Completely intrigued by this new operating system I went home and scrounged the internet for more information on this mysterious OS.

Now 2 years later I've been running Ubuntu on 4 different PCs and have gone through various stages of uses. I learned how to install and run my own web/media server. All kinds of fancy schmancy commands so I can easily access my computer remotely. Just a plethora of neat things that I felt more secure executing in Linux rather than Windows.

Now that's not to say there weren't any bumps in the road! My first Ubuntu installation was on a PC with an ATI GPU. This was prior to the more recent efforts from ATI to have some sort of Linux support. Needless to say I ended up buying a cheap Nvidia GPU and never looked back. Aside from this purchase, I never found a hardware problem I couldn't solve with the help of these forums.

More recently (in the past year) I've taken the free time being unemployment has blessed me with (ha ha) to develop my photography business. Initially I went back to windows XP to learn Lightroom as it was slowly becoming the industry standard. I learned quite a bit from my trial period with LR, however I left unsatisfied with the performance so I began to look for alternatives. I found Bibble and wouldn't you know they have a native linux version! SO back to Ubuntu I went and have been happily using Bibble for my photography needs.

Besides an MMORPG that I participate in (Aion) I don't use windows for much else. I feel confident that I can continue to satisfy client needs with what Ubuntu has to offer and I am very glad for it.

Thank you all for your support in these forums. They are undoubtedly unmatched with useful information when compared to other support forums.

### Post by ElSlunko on 2009-10-06
I completely agree with the "getting the right components" comments. I mean it's like getting Chevy parts for a Honda and expecting it to work. Sure they might have some interchangable parts, but for some critical stuff you can't interchange. So if someone is serious about Linux they should know to take the time and research their needs.

As far as my photography workflow goes it's pretty simple. I do mostly events or portrait shoots so I just tag them with apporpriate labels upon import. I don't bother with renaming folders since the tags suffice. 

I do my color adjustments and create virtual copies of photos that I'll be doing special changes to (IE black & white or sepia or something else). Once I'm done I export my photos and do post sharpening in GIMP or if it's less critical, photo phatch. 

Upload to my zenfolio account, password protect, and share with my client! I don't charge for prints as I let them have their full size images. I just factor in the price to the engagement session.

Again I must say that I am very happy with the Ubuntu environment. Though compatibility in Windows is strong (once you get 3rd party drivers) I often got bored of using it and really preferred working within Ubuntu. Once Aion gets better frame rate in WINE, my xp partition will go into hibernation once more :).

Nothing sharpens RAW files...  RAW files are just that, raw data.  When you apply a sharpening value to a raw image in a workflow, nothing has been done to  the original file.  You are just telling it what action to perform on the image when you export it to whatever format, jpg, tiff, png, etc...
