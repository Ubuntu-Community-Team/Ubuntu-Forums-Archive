### Post by threepot on 2010-11-14
Well the title says it all really.

I've been threatening to ditch the Microshod Windows platform for about ten or fifteen years or so now but never really managed it.

But looking to get a PC working at a mates house I downloaded the Ubuntu USB flash drive installer stuff and got it booting. A few stalls later due to bad bios settings and the AMD64 box was running better than ever, and the web browsing was sweet so it was left like that and I ran off with the USB stick but with x86 version armed. And the best bit, I didn't have to do the whole windows drivers crap.

A week later I deploy it on my mums net book and another friends dual core laptop. Loads of happy users, I felt like the Ubuntu Fairy, waving the Ubuntu wand over unsuspecting victims.

So last week I thought sod it, although I have this internal need to hang onto the comfort of Windows like a baby on a nipple, I know inside really I've grown far too big for bittie.

Since the change i've fallen in love with computing again. I love a bit of command bashing too, got my display resolutions right with a script on startup. Linux life is good.

So really this is not just a rant of joy, this is a rant of gratitude, a great wail of thanks to all those people who have contributed to this platform in front of me. I like it a lot!

LMMS
GIMP
Openoffice
The whole ubuntu distro affair
And all the tiny little bits in between

I THANK YOU VERY MUCH :)

To anyone scared of the jump, just get another hard disk, they are cheap as **** nowadays, and get rocking. You'll never boot that sad sorry state of an OS again... (well maybe once or twice)

It also doesn't support itunes, or should I say iTunes doesn't support it, which is ****ing fantastic because its the worst piece of crap ever, totally resolving the girlfriend/itunes argument. Her ipod has now sold on ebay. VICTORY!!

LMMS is fantastic, it works so well. It's not crashed on me once yet, it handles its workload well. A fantastic usable vent for music creativity. And for free? WTF!! Okay, it doesn't really do the vst stuff perfectly but just with the included instrument plugins you can do so so so much. A+ 9.5/10 "****ing Mint Mate" This was my main motivation, I was after some sort of music suite/DAW, and after getting cheesed with Reason and Reaper crashing on my PC all the time I thought there must be something on the linux platform, and I found Ardour first. But we never really hit it off, i'm not sure its geared for software instruments or whatever, either way its in the past and now LMMS keeps me awake. tut tut! To think I was going to build a Hackintosh just to run Logic Studio Pro. Glad I took this pill first!

I hate the corporate emmulsion we call todays marketplace, and my computer infront of me right now proves to me that there is another way. This was built not for profit, but it bloody works, and its better than the alternative that also costs the earth.

So I salute all you developers and user, and I bow down to this fantastic creation. 

Goodnight

### Post by threepot on 2010-11-19
I'm going to keep updating this because its turned out that if you type "Ubuntu Linux Logic Studio" into google this page is really well ranked... well done admins.

So i've now been bashing on LMMS daily for a while now. Really happy with it, it hasn't crashed once, I had loads of software synths running the other night, more than would run in any windows enviroment I've used yet on this actual PC.

I'm running some proper top notch gear pal! NOT!

Socket 478 P4 3.2ghz running 800mhz FSB
2gb = 4x512mb OCZ XMS PC3200 Ram
Creative Labs old school PCI 128 Soundblaster type thing.
Rubbish MSI motherboard
65gb IDE HDD + 80GB IDE still with my old Windows XP setup
ATi Radeon 9500 128mb 8xAGP Dual head Graphics Card
1 x 17" Monitor and 1 x 15" Monitor

I feel that I can multi task more software synths on the above peice of crap than I can on my mates Dual core Imac on logic studio. I will say its not as feature packed as Logic, nor does it have the same availibilty of synths etc. Also LMMS's interface does have a few quirks but you can accept them and work around, although I've found logics interface doesn't always do what you want.


So if you want a go at making the latest Dubstep banger or a beautiful ambient washover and your not sure where to start I'd really recomend LMMS on Ubuntu. Its free, its stable, its feature packed.... give it a blast, whats the worst that can happen. It comes with plenty of instruments, drum packs and samples to keep you busy.

If your a bit scared and need moral support, or even just pointing in the right direction to start please reply to this topic...

But heres your best bet:-

Get a 2gb USB dongle out, download [http://www.pendrivelinux.com/downloads/Universal-USB-Installer/Universal-USB-Installer.exe](http://www.pendrivelinux.com/downloads/Universal-USB-Installer/Universal-USB-Installer.exe)

Run it, it'll ask you which version of Ubuntu you want (probally the x86 10.10 version) and send you to the right download page.

Then it'll format the USB stick and make it into an ubuntu bootable installation USB Stick.

Boot your machine from it, choose to install.

If it stalls press escape to see its text progress debug stuff, bang the error into google and god will answer all your problems.

Good luck,
