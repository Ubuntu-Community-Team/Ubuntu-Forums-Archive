### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-05-01
On my old Dell Dimension it was all good except for Thunderbird and sound not working. I'm disappointed to see PulseAudio in Lucid Xubuntu now, too. And the new Thunderbird is completely different from the previous version - almost  like a complete do-over from scratch. The first thing it did after I entered my gmail information was to create a dozen new folders and downloading my entire account history, including the trash (c'mon, the TRASH?!?). I closed it immediately (very limited bandwidth allowance here). No legacy version in the repos, so I found Seamonkey in the Ubuntuzilla repos and installed - ah, just like my old familiar and trusty Thunderbird 2.0. It downloaded only my new mail. I'll go to the web site and delete everything else before I try T-Bird 3 again. But not having sound? Show stopper. A dancer's gotta have music.

Lucid Xubuntu feels like "two steps back" after Karmic's forward step.

Perhaps in another month or two I'll give it another shot. Or just stick with Karmic until the end of it's life, and look for a new distro.
