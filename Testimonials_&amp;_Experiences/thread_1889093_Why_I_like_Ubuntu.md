### Post by ajgreeny on 2011-11-30
The problem is that the gnome desktop you speak of may not remain available in the future.  The classic gnome 2 desktop is dying, I'm afraid, and gnome 3 with its various shells on top is now the basis of the gnome DEs, gnome-shell, unity, gnome-fallback, and maybe the Linux Mint MGSE.

For Mint there is also MATE, if it keeps going.  This is a fork of gnome 2, but many feel that it will be far too much for a small distro like Mint to keep it running and that it is likely to eventually fold;  only time will tell!
