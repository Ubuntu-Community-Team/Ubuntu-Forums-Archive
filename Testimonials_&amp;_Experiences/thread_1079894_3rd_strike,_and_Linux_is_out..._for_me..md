### Post by Grant A. on 2009-02-24
Well, I have officially moved back to Windows. Why? Because I'm tired of the Open Source attitude that patents are "unavoidable", and that Windows has features that we "need". TBH, those are the most ******** comments that I have ever heard. Patents exist for two reasons, for protection of Intellectual Property to cover R&D costs, and for the freezing of ideas to force people into innovation. Well, lately it seems that the innovation has died in Linux, and Linux is going from UNIX-clone to Windows-clone, due to all of the stolen features like Compiz (Aero) and Mono (.Net).

Honestly people! We need to make things that MICROSOFT wants to copy, not the other way around! The key to success is through innovation that catches the eyes of the consumer, not through stolen innovation that catches the eyes of patent litigators and trolls. Why use what is essentially a buggier version of Windows when I have a less buggy version that I bought?

Farewell Linux, I hope I may comeback one day, when the innovation returns.

Consider this a boycott.
