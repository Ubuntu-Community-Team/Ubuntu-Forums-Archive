### Post by utmost on 2008-10-10
Hi! I am a computer manufacturer and retailer who for many years has stuck to the Windows path - with only a vague awareness of Linux and its many distros. After recently becoming very disillusioned with the corporate character and nature of Microsoft, I decided to investigate Linux by downloading Ubuntu 8.04 and Fedora. I installed Ubuntu first (it came down 1st) and I am so impressed with it!

The culture behind Ubuntu is so radically different to Microsoft and capitalism in general! It's great. Ubuntu is so fully featured out-of-the-box and runs beautifully on my machine (which is admittedly state of the art) but I'll be testing it on an old Pentium 4 2.66GHz box to confirm its prowess in coping with old hardware.

I just wish I'd known earlier - I feel like I've ripped off my business customers by selling them XP and Vista when Ubuntu could have done a great job with so much less cost. I am trying to get some Windows games up and running on Ubuntu and as yet this appears to be one of the only areas where Windows is superior, although I've not heard much feedback on multimedia production on Linux systems.

I totally commend the entire Ubuntu (and Linux) community for demonstrating the potential of humanity when its efforts are not hindered by greed and secrecy - we can all learn from this example! For myself, I'll be advocating Ubuntu and Linux to all my clients and hopefully one day it can displace Microsoft from the top OS installations on PCs worldwide.

Full gratitude!

Bless Ubuntu and Linux!
