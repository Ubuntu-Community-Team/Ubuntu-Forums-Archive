### Post by gunashekar on 2008-11-12
Honestly, I felt the same several times in the past and even switched back to windows a few times on some of my machines. 

My advice to new users:

Stick to LTS versions if you use only one machine and resist that urge to upgrade to the 'latest' . Ubuntu is constantly developing with a release every 6 months. Issues with display seem to have disappeared in recent releases, but issues with sound and wireless creep into every new release for a month or so and they get resolved ultimately on six different machines i have. 

There are automatic updates almost every day even on stable releases. Decide for yourself, how important are each type of update before you click. You can also select/de-select types of updates that may not be critical and choose to install on a day when you have time to take a close look at each update. 

Sure, enjoy the thrill of watching the operating system evolve through different development stages alpha, beta ..and be proud about having the latest but only on a spare computer and not on your work machine till you are sure that a release is stable and needed by you really. Heck, even a 3 month old release becomes outdated and I can understand the urge to try the newest, hot download, nevermind if it is alpha, beta.

As for ktubu the starter of this thread, I am sure he will come back wiser .. maybe after a few exciting adventures.
