### Post by retrogeek on 2007-02-16
I am pleased to report success with my installation of Ubuntu 6.06. I installed a new Belkin NIC today and was pleased to see the driver disk contained Linux drivers. I had a couple bumps in the road, namely audio codecs (which were solved with the assistance of easyubuntu...a utility I highly recommend for those who want ease of installation rather than customizing everything themselves), and the NIC, but again, the solution was very easy once I dug into the driver disk.

All in all, I would say that as far as user friendliness, Ubuntu actually BEATS the boys in Redmond hands down on this. I've used their newest O/S, and while there are features I like, it is anything BUT user friendly. And don't get me started on MAC (my common response to Mac fanbois is to ask if I can come over to their house and play WoW...wanna see a Mac fanboi cry?). Ubuntu was simple to install, not too difficult to bring to full functionality, and runs nicely on the old PIII machine. I think I've finally found the "alternative" O/S that I can bring home to momma!
