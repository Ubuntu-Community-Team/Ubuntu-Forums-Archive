### Post by SonicSteve on 2008-01-10
Greetings and happy new year,

I have a customer that repeatedly needs help with his Dell running Windows XP. Most recently he has somehow been corrupting his TCP/IP, it's happened twice in the last 2 months. Nothing I tried could restore his networking and I tried many things. So I proposed an Ubuntu Trial to him. I sold it as something that could meet his needs (in fact surpass them) and that it wasn't vulnerable to TCP corruptions like Windows is. 

My customer isn't computer savvy, he uses his computer to:
- e-mail
- surf the web and related web activity
- type documents (he was using Office 2000)

I set him up with 7.10, and a windows xp virtual machine as a security blanket. I told him to use the VM as little as possible perhaps using it for a website that won't quite display correctly (unfortunately that happens). I even installed wine and MS word/excel 2000 as further comforts.

I updated it,  and installed as many helpful apps as I could think of. I then delivered it last Monday evening. I promised to make myself as available as possible to him. So far only one phone call, I don't know if that's good or bad:-s

Anyway I'll keep you up to date on how it goes.

Having said that last night I saw my client. I revisited the idea of Windows Vs. Ubuntu/Linux. After a very short discussion of pros and cons he said "Well lets just assume that I'll be staying with Ubuntu, I really appreciate it's stability"

We are talking about a man in his late 60's who routinely had problems with Windows, some of his own doing, some not.
