### Post by the8thstar on 2008-03-30
@ original poster (**xxxamazexxx**): 

Should you choose to calm down, regroup and reconsider your choice, here is my advice to you: when you set your partitions (ext3 I know) with any LiveCD, make sure to create one partition (5-10Gb) for the system and another partition (10-30Gb) for your Home partition. You can select /Home under 'flags' when using the Gparted tool.

This way, if your Ubuntu install crashes and you need to reinstall it again, your /Home partition will stay intact with your documents and your settings.

### Post by mick8985 on 2008-03-31
Linux doesn't have to be installed on an ext partition, it just can't be installed on partitions that don't have user priveleges and can't do symlinks (ie FAT and NTFS). GRUB needs an ext partition to boot, thats why people make boot partitions. But the root partition can be on any filesystem supported by the kernel.
