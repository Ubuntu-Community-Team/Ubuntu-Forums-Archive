Somewhat of a long story

I have been trying to install Ubuntu 8.04 since its release, a little over a month now I guess?.
Trying to install on 3 older AMD sempron 1.5 ghz based machines with Nvidia geforce mx 4000 pci cards.

No luck I just cant get the video drivers to cooperate, after a month of reading google searches and Ubuntu forums,installing drivers, uninstalling drivers, installing envyng, uninstalling envyng,apt-get this, apt-get that.
sudo reconfigure this, sudo reconfigure that. still no love.

Well somewhere in the past month my wife has become somewhat irrate with me for always having her computer down, so she went to wall mart and bought her a $500 Acer Aspire which comes with Windows Vista.
It works great no problems what soever Vista recognized my new memory and nvidia card and HP Office Jet printer no problems.

Well being the knuckle headed fool that I am, I decided to try to install Ubuntu  on her new computer today while she is out.:confused:

Success, flawless install, recognized all the hardware, incuding the extra 2gig 800mhz memory, and nvidia geforce 8500gt pcix16 card.

After it installed I enabled the proprietary driver, installed the compiz setings manager, and every thing works perfect, even after 123 updates installed and reboot!!:)

I am HAPPY!!:)

I acidentially didnt pay attention to what version of Ubuntu I downloaded, and got the 32bit version.

Should I try to install the 64 bit version? Or should I leave well enough alone, the 32 bit is working good.
