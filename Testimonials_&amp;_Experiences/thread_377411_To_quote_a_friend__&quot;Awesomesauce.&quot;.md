### Post by XeroZohar on 2007-03-06
So one day, my original desktop, going on five years old, start showing some inexplicable problems.  This lead to building a newer, quieter, sleeker machine, and though I've been a slackware user for a long time, I decided to try one of the newer, hipper distributions of linux, especially since I wanted to try one of these newfangled package management systems.  Kids these days.  Anyway, Ubuntu has been making waves as the 'easy as pie' distro of choice, so I decided to try that.  Upon visiting the main Ubuntu site, I noticed the links to other variants.  I knew of Kubuntu, and peeked at edubuntu's page, but Xubuntu caught my eye especially.  It uses XFCE, which I've used in the past and adored for it's minimalist yet still "environment" style window management.  Thus, I downloaded it and installed it on my newly built computer.

Sure, there were hiccups along the way.  I had to compile a newer Linux kernel to support my motherboard's onboad LAN and sound, as well as figure out how to do that and still use the package management system.  Later, I had issues getting Nvidia's graphical driver to work.  And then there's my PVR-250 video capture card.  But long story short, Xubuntu supports everything I use and more, and I have to wonder how I existed without a package management system for so long.

In summary, Xubuntu is a wonderful distro, and I'm proud to be using it now for all my day-to-day operations.  I may have cut my teeth on Slackware, but Ubuntu (and family) is definitely my new distro of choice.  Thanks for making it such a great one.

Regards,
Lynn
