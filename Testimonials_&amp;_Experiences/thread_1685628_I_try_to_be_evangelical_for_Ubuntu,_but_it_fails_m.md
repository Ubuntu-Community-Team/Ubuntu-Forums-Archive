### Post by jatoo on 2011-02-11
I am very much into Linux (specifically Ubuntu), I use it at work and at home, and only use Windows when I absolutely have to. I'm so into it, i basically tell everyone that it's way better than windows and everyone should use it. I say "go on, just install it now, you can dual boot, it's as easy as anything." 

But the only times when people have actually listened to me and given it a go, it has failed me. 

I got a colleague to try installing it on his machine at work.  Couldn't repartition the drives.  That was that, he never looked at it again.

I've been telling my girlfriend for ages she should use it because it's easy and i can help her remotely.  She bought a new computer, and finally let me install Ubuntu, and the wireless card didn't work! (obviously a deal-breaker). She's running Windows 7 now.

What more can i do?  I give it nothing but glowing reviews, but when people try it, it fails me (them).

It's great that Ubuntu is getting all these cool new features and they're developing Unity, adding new pop-ups and social networking etc., but none of that matters if there isn't hardware support, or the system won't install. Someone has a bad experience like that, then installs Windows and everything works, can you really expect them to give it another try? 

I'll just have to stop trying to spread it.  Sorry to use the 'f' word, but maybe i'll give Fedora a try and see what it's hardware support is like.

It's a shame, really :(

I really watch how I praise. Every OS has it's issues. I now know I tried many of them. 
But don't lose faith this OS is always getting better and more support.
