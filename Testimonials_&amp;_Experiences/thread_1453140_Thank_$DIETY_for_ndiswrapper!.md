### Post by Paul Miller on 2010-04-12
This isn't the usual kind of post for this forum, I know, so, if it violates any rules, I apologize in advance.

I'd like to just take a minute to thank both the developers of ndiswrapper and the folks who packaged it for Ubuntu.  Here's my story:

I have a RealTek rtl8185 card in my laptop, for which the installer selected the rtl8180 driver.  As long as signal strength was high, this driver worked, but if the signal was less than perfect, let's just say "flaky" wouldn't begin to describe my experience.

At first, I struggled along, thinking that was as good as it would get.  I had some workarounds for when the connection would cut out on me (for instance, reloading the driver frequently helped temporarily), but the overall experience was still pretty crappy.  It was time to install ndiswrapper!

My first try installing ndiswrapper didn't go so well, because I downloaded the wrong driver for my card.  Oops.  But, after I got the right drivers going, my experience has been nearly as smooth as if I were running Windows.

I hate to just be a cheerleader here, but the moral of the story is definitely "ndiswrapper FTW!"

Once again, thanks to the ndiswrapper and Ubuntu devs that made this all possible for me.
