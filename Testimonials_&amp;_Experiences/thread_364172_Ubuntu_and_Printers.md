### Post by Falcorian on 2007-02-17
I haven't owned a printer in years, and when I have, I've hated them. They break. They stop working in the middle of jobs. They print in funny colors, or spit out archaic symbols. So when I switched to Ubuntu about a year ago, I though "Well, at least I don't have to worry about printers!" because I'd always heard that printers don't work on Linux, or that you'd have to sell your first born to get it to work, and then only halfway (which would have been a problem, since I don't have a first born to trade in!).

But this year I found that I had need for a printer. So I found [http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting](http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting), printed out (on the library printers, of course) a copy of their list, and headed to find me one. I got an HP Deskjet that was on the list, but I still didn't believe that it was going to work, I'd been let down by printers far too often.

I got home, opened it (carefully! What if it didn't work!), plugged it in, and turned it on. My heart skipped a beat as nothing happened. No pop up, no "Hey, you plugged in a printer!". So I headed to the System > Administration menu, and clicked add new printer.

A few seconds later a box popped up saying "Hey, I found your HP D2360", so I clicked next, it asked me to confirm the printer type, and then give it a name. I did so, loaded up a PDF, and hit print. It spat out a beautiful JFET Characteristic Curve plot.


So bravo! Installing was a snap! And it works perfectly!
