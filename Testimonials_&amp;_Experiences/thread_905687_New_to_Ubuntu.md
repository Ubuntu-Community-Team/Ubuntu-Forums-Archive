### Post by burpootus on 2008-08-30
Hello, I'm a Windows user and I've been "trying out" Ubuntu for about a week now. I'm pretty much a Linux newbie. I tried Linux Mandrake 6.5 back around 9 years ago and by the time I got my hardware configured and working ( I don't think I ever got CD burning to work) I was ready to throw in the towel and go back to Windows. It was just waay too much work. Then XP came along and I forgot about Linux. I work with a lot of XP machines at work and it pretty much does what you want it to fast and easy. Anyways, where I work at has resisted Vista and I hear a lot of negativity about it. So I've been wondering how Linux has advanced and I had a spare drive to try it on and here I am. I must say that I am impressed. The Mandrake experience was NOT what I was looking for to say the least, so I was hesitant, but Ubuntu has worked really well from the get go. I haven't been able to find anything so far that I need to do on XP that I can't do on Ubuntu. The troubleshooting hasn't been that bad. As a side note, I am moving my gaming to a PS3 (which has Yellow Dog 6 on it, another Linux success story), obviously the whole gaming thing isn't the same on Linux as XP so it's not really fair to compare. This is getting long winded so I'll just leave it at "pleasantly surprised" by my Ubuntu experience and I think that when the XP train pulls into the station for the last time, I will not make the switch to Vista.
