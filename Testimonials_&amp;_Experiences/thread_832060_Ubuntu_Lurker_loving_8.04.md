### Post by toppknott on 2008-06-17
I'd had Ubuntu on my system any number of times over the years, starting with Ubuntu 6.10 (I think that's the version) but never felt truly satisfied with the entire experience as compared to other mainstream operating systems. Sometimes I would use Ubuntu heavily, then reformat my computer, and not use it for another six months. You could say I have been flirting with Ubuntu Linux for a very long time.

After reading some recent reviews about the "Hardy Heron" release (I JUST figured out they are doing this kind of "alphabetical" thing) I thought I would give it a try. I had always loved the experience of Linux but was never enchanted with its speed or compatibility.

After installing the recent LTS version of Ubuntu, it is staying on my computer for good now. I am extremely impressed with some of the new improvements:

o The first good sign was that Ubuntu booted relatively quickly, in about 30 seconds or less. For me, that's a very competitive boot speed, and a big change from past distributions. 
o The next good sign was that fast user switching worked properly. 
o Then, I noticed packages and applications were being installed at blazing speed. I had only seen that kind of speed on FreeBSD 7. 
o Firefox consistently loads itself in just about six seconds. Other applications started very quickly as well.
o With some difficulty, I was able to activate my Genius Mousepen drawing tablet. This is a *huge* plus because I love to make cartoons & comics and will need it for art-related projects. 
o With a little bit of tweaking, I got "Firestarter" to properly share my wireless internet connection to my LAN. 

Thanks Ubuntu community & team for covering my basic functional needs such as painless boot time, fast application install & startup times, system stability, and beautiful interface. I am looking forward to such improvements as faster USB external drive access speeds, plug-n-play tablet support (so I don't have to use the terminal for half an hour if I have to reinstall :)). Great job, this recent version really gives me a lot of satisfaction using Linux on my computer and I'm a lot more confident in recommending it to people.
