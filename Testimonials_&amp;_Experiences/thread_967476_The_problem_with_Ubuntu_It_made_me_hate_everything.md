### Post by MrSnazzy on 2008-11-02
My history with computers started with Windows- first 95, then 98, then XP. A friend of mine introduced me to Linux, and I installed Ubuntu 5.10 (Breezy Badger). It took some getting used to, but I soon realized that Ubuntu was a good thing, though there were problems. My friend assured me that they would soon all be worked out. That in a year or so, Ubuntu would work flawlessly.

I have been using Ubuntu for three years now. It is all I have on my laptop, though my rarely used desktop does still have an XP installation. All I can say is that Ubuntu is as unstable as it ever was.

I am no computer expert. I am the average joe computer user that has adapted to using Linux. When people ask me how I like Linux, I tell them the truth: "It is great- when it works."

The big problem with Ubuntu is inconsistency. Sometimes I'll boot up and find that my widgets didn't load correctly. Or that my touchpad settings didn't work. I reboot my computer a couple times until everything loads right. I do not know why this happens. To me it is completely random. I will sometimes find myself praying to the almighty father to please let Ubuntu start right. (Lucky enough, if flash isn't working, it's only a restart of Firefox that fixes it.)

Is this why I hate Ubuntu? No. I hate Ubuntu because although it is unstable, it is still much better than using Windows. And believe me, I have tried going back. It always leaves me tired and frustrated. I truly believe that if Microsoft came after Ubuntu, it would be no more than a joke. (Though I suppose I could be wrong. Apparently even Mac is getting popularity now. What is that about?)
