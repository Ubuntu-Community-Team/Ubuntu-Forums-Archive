### Post by Tony Sivori on 2008-06-06
My trusty Mandrake 10.1 (a four year old install) finally bit the dust. I decided to replace, rather than repair.

Long story short, I settled on Kubuntu. I see a few users here saying that 8.04 is a troublesome version. All I can say is that if 8.04 is bad, I probably won't be able to contain myself when confronted with a good release.   :-)

I've had a few small problems. And I like to customize my OS quite a lot, somewhat for appearance, but mostly for functionality and preference. I like everything to be just so. In less than a week, I am down to the most trivial of details (anyone know how to make the Xine 0.99.6 control box slide under the KDE Panel like it used to?). 

I've had to ask very few questions in the forums and on Usenet. Thanks to the popularity of (K)Ubuntu, Google already had all the answers.

I'm really quite delighted with Kubuntu 8.04. Stable, secure, attractive, easy to install, all of my hardware works, easy to configure, easy to install new programs, and supported by an active and friendly community.

If I had to pick the biggest disappointment, it would be that after I installed it I found out that unlike Ubuntu 8.04, Kubuntu 8.04 is not a Long Term Support release.
