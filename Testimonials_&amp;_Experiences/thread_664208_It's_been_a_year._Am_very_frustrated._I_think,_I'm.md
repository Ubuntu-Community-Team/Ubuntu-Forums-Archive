### Post by wersdaluv on 2008-01-10
I have been a big fan of Ubuntu. I have faced so many problems. I studied on my own. I reformatted my laptop for, I guess, more than 30 times already. 

I did not know anything about open source, then. I am a psychology student so I wasn't really tech savvy. I just found out about Ubuntu through StumbleUpon. I wanted to maximize computing so I tried it out.

When I first installed Ubuntu, I deleted all the data in my hard drive. All my important archives were gone. My daily routine changed since I lost my important personal files. I didn't give up. 

I had problems with my video card, wireless card, etc.; but I didn't give up. For about nine months, I tried to make my Pocket PC work with Ubuntu, but I failed. I managed to sync with Evolution (but I was a fan of Kontact). At some point in time, for some reason, my Pocket PC syncing stopped working. I just gave up. I  don't want to waste time trying to sync. I decided to spend my time writing my appointments manually. 

My latest problem is my webcam. My Logitech Quickcam Orbit/Sphere worked well in Feisty. On Gutsy, it didn't. There are things that I didn't get in Feisty so I didn't want to go back. I bought a Genius Look 316 webcam which claims to have Linux support. It worked for other Gutsy users but not for me. I decided to reformat. Now, I'm on a fresh install and it still isn't working.

It's been a year.  Whenever I am at home, I spend much of my time trying to make Ubuntu work for me. I didn't personally know any Ubuntu user since I met one just a month or two ago. That time, I already learned so much, so I guess, I didn't need his help anymore. I suffered enough already. My friends and family are having a hard time because of this. They want to see me through my webcam but I can't make it work. They want to do stuff with me on Y! Messenger but I can't. I spend so much time on my computer to the point that I do not have enough time for my loved ones and my responsibilities anymore.

I love Open Source. I love Ubuntu. I was addicted to it. Now, I'm thinking of living my life instead of trying to make Ubuntu work for me. I don't want to waste time anymore. It's frustrating me. I hope, Ubuntu will be for me one day.

### Post by AusIV4 on 2008-01-11
Good luck with whatever you decide to do.

I get irritated with Ubuntu from time to time and think of switching back to Windows, but then I end up thinking about why I switched to Ubuntu, and I stick around.

With Windows, I'd hit the power button in the morning, go to the bathroom, brush my teeth, come back, type in my password, hop in the shower, and when I got back I'd have a usable desktop. I'd leave my computer on all day, because it took so long to boot it up. With Ubuntu, I hibernate my system if I'm going to be gone more than 20 minutes, and it takes ~1 minute to get back to a working desktop.

My computer was so bogged down with a bluetooth manager (just so I could use my mouse), anti-virus, firewall, and a dozen different auto-update programs that it was barely usable. With Ubuntu, the anti-virus and firewall are unnecessary, and one program takes care of all of my updates.

Then I consider that to switch to Windows, I'd have to pay $200 for a license, and $50 a year to keep my computer secure, and that's a cost I can't afford right now.

I do spend some time tinkering with my system to get things working, but when I think about the amount of time I spent hunting down viruses and spyware on Windows, or the time I spent booting to a usable desktop, or just waiting for programs to load, I figure Ubuntu saves me a lot more time than it costs me.

If Windows works out better for you, by all means use it. I think most everyone here uses Ubuntu because we feel more productive or less irritated using it (this is the Ubuntu forum, after all), but if you're happier with something else, we won't hold you back.
