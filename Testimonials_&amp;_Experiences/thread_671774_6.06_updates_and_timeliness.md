### Post by mister_lister on 2008-01-18
So far, over the past few months since I went fully ubuntu 6.06 there have been regular updates. Everyweek there is something new or a security update. I am comfortable with this pace at this point. Although it is more frequent than windows, it seems each patch, feature, and security fix is timely and works.

With windows xp, the second service pack screwed my usb devices. At work it is a constant issue because our devices, those that we sell are USB and that service pack royally screwed (using a light term) USB .

I always grit my teeth with a new update, waiting for disaster of some sort, but so far with the 6.06 updates, nothing bad (for me) has happened.

Keep up the analytical and good work Ubuntu team!

So far so good.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2008-01-18
I've applied 150 or so patches to Dapper at one time without ill effect.  217 days and 165 days of uptime.  The downtime was due a beeping UPS battery (which I couldn't stand anymore) and a windstorm which caused a several-hour power outage.  The UPS actually worked this time.

With several months of uptime, the patches add up.  I'm looking at 49 overdue right now.  I think I'll wait a month or 6.

I used to be paranoid about patches breaking things, but the LTS seems to have things under control.
