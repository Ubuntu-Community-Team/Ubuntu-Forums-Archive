### Post by 1roxtar on 2011-01-06
Welcome Community.  I'm here to share my monthly report as an Ubuntu user.  I've been running full installations of my favorite OS since Jaunty (9.04) and cannot get enough of my Ubuntu.  There are 6 machines running Maverick in my house, so far.  As a tech, running a small pc repair business out of my home, I have begun a personal mission to educate my customers about Linux and Ubuntu.  I want them  know there are real options and great alternatives to Microsoft Windows.  

I don't preach. I DEMONSTRATE Ubuntu and let them handle it themselves.  I have mostly been targeting XP users who are no longer getting mainstream updates from MS anymore.  I teach about XP's end-of-life and still being able to have a modern, elegant, secure and FREE OS through Ubuntu.  I have since made about 20 such conversions and continue to get awesome feedback and great results about stability and usability.  I still haven't had a single customer ask for me to switch them back.  I am even getting customers bringing me their friends and family saying, "I want you to show them about the Ubuntu you put on my computer".  Tech support for Ubuntu installs have mainly been about them wanting to know about program alternatives to Windows programs and customization and a couple of printer installation helps.   

Mind you that these are mostly people who are not very tech-savvy and just want things to work with as little interruption to their daily lives as possible.  They want to surf their internet, download music, burn cd/dvd's, print homework for school, play basic games and not having to worry about their kids crippling the family computer with tons of viruses.  Ubuntu for these families that blast my phone incessantly with computer &#8220;emergencies&#8221; has been a breath of fresh air and stress reliever.  My &#8220;TRADE XP FOR UBUNTU&#8221; campaign is moving forward with positive results.

### Post by Zoomy on 2011-01-06
Much as I'm enjoying my Ubuntu experiences and experiments, I am not ready to let go of Windows.  I recently bought a new laptop and loaded Ubuntu as a secondary operating system, and I really have no complaints about how it performs, however, I cannot get my hosted exchange account to work properly with Evolution Mail, nor can I program my Harmony Remote properly with it, even though the program will install thru wine, it will not properly communicate with the USB port.

My feeling is that if you just want to browse the internet, view your gmail, yahoo mail, or hotmail, do a little facebooking etc., then Ubuntu will nicely fit the bill.  But the next time you go to Futur***** and buy some gadget with a CD bundled with it, you are going to have some problems.  For that reason, I cannot fully leave Windows (unfortunately).  However, I have noticed many wonderful changes in the OS since I first experimented with it a couple years ago, and no doubt it will one day be a real contender.  

My hope for Ubuntu is to sell cheap $50 - $150 computers from the give-aways that I've been inheriting.  As long as people realize the limitations, I think it's a wonderful way to put old hardware back into service.
