### Post by scotte on 2006-09-21
OK, It's probably really silly to post this, but here I go anyway...

I've been a hardcore Gentoo user and supporter for 3 or so years. I've installed it well over a dozen times on various machines (x86, amd64), and overall been really happy with it. That is, until I had to recompile a box due to a GCC 4.1.1 upgrade which really hosed things (lots of broken ebuilds, and so on). Not going to mince my words here - I'm really pissed about this.

Now, my move to Gentoo (and Linux in general) as an even longer time FreeBSD user (9 years) was with some serious foot-dragging. Basically, I needed to do Java development, and at the time FreeBSD was way behind with SDK availability unless I wanted to build it myself.

At any rate, I decided to see what else was out there (rather than go through the same pain on my other Gentoo boxes) and got a good reference for ubuntu from a buddy. Honestly, I sort of never played around with it since I had always seen it discussed as a "beginners linux" or "linux for windoze folks", etc. Well that's all a bunch of BS.

I installed kubuntu on a test box, and was pleasantly surprised at getting a running box going within under 30 minutes. Then, I decided it was time to toss it down on my main box after shrinking my seldom-used NTFS partition (hey, there are a few games that don't work under cedega).

Thanks to gparted destroying my partition table while moving my Gentoo reiserfs partitions to make room at the end of my extended partition (whoops), I decided to just reinstall with kubuntu and see how it goes. I'm really impressed. I can't tell any speed difference between my highly customized Gentoo (with specific USE and CCFLAGS and all that) and kubuntu. Everything seems to work really well! Amazing how quickly apt-get installs things, too!

To be fair, it's only been a day, so I guess I'll see how much I can screw things up in the near future, but so far, so good! Unless anything changes, I can easily see my other running Gentoo installs eventually becoming ubuntu boxes, because I'm pretty sure it's less effort to reinstall with ubuntu than it is to upgrade to Gentoo with GCC4 and the 2006.1 profile...

This is my opinion as of right now, your milage may vary, no warranty is expressed or implied, blah blah blah.
