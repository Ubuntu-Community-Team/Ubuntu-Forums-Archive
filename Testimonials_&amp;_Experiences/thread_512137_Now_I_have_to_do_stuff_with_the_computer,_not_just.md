### Post by flick on 2007-07-28
I ran Ubuntu from the Hoary Hedgehog till the Edgy Eft, then decided I wanted to experiment with source-based distros. Tried Sabayon, versions 3.26 through 3.4 loop 3.

What I learned : Gentoo QA is spotty. Basing a distro on Gentoo's arch branch seems questionable. There are always updates that need to be applied, and doing a world update is a delightful process if one really wants to spend hours and hours resolving dependency conflicts. Sabayon's stable releases tend to work well without hassles right up until you recompile, or add software, or remove software, or upgrade software. There are apparently only two devs for Sabayon. Smart, passionate guys, but only two of 'em. And I began to wonder if all the compiling, optimizing, the computer always being on because there's always compiling going on, was worth it.

So I backed up my data, bought a copy of Linux Format with an install of Ubuntu/Kubuntu/Xubuntu/dev tools on it.

Twenty minutes later I had an updated Feisty Fawn. Five minutes later my gosh-blessed Broadcom wireless was working. Five minutes after that nvidia driver was installed along with my beloved Beryl. Twenty minutes and a visit to the "Ubuntu Starter Guide" later, every app I use was installed. No conflicts. No compiling. Ready to roll. And, most amusingly, everything runs faster than they did on my Sabayon install. 

This isn't "Sabayon sucks." Those guys are working hard at something they love and believe in, and they're imaginative devs. But days and days of trying to get stuff installed and working versus an hour, and instability versus stability -- a lot of nixers seem to put down Ubuntu as not being 1337 enough or something. I say, sometimes you really can't argue with success, or stability, or speed, or convenience, or...

So now I'm back to learning Python and bash, along with the usual surfing and IMing and music and movies. Actually using the computer, using apps, instead of finessing the OS for the thousandth time.
