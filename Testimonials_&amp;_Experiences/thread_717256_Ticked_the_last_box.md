### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-03-06
I switched to Ubuntu dual-boot with Mac OS and then Windows over 2 years ago, and slowly migrated everything I do to Ubuntu. Initial problems were video encoding, casual gaming, video editing, DVD authoring, and loading my MP3 player with music.

[LIST]
[*]Video encoders on Linux got better, solving my AV sync issues
[*]I ran native games, including Return To Castle Wolfenstein and Unreal Tournament 2004, and forgot about the non-Wine-compatible Windows games I had bought.
[*]Video editing finally became usable for me with Kdenlive.
[*]And I bought a Sony Mp3 player, which works perfectly with Linux.
[/LIST]

At the start of the year I learnt how to build computers, and built my own Ubuntu-only computer. The only thing I hadn't managed to do yet was reliable DVD authoring - the DVDs worked sorta okay on some DVD players but not on others.

Last night I discovered Tovid and gave it a good working out. It made DVDs that worked beautifully on even my most finiky DVD players! I've ticked the last box! Now Ubuntu can do absolutely everything that I want of it, and the best bit is that things will get even better.
