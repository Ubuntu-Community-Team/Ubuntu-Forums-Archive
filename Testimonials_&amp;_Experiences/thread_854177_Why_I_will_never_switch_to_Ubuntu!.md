### Post by gavnone on 2008-07-09
To whom it may concern:

Last night, my Windows computer crashed, so I considered switching to Ubuntu since I had heard good reviews of it - suffice it to say, however, that after my 7-hour "nightmare" experience with getting Ubuntu to recognize my music library by using three separate iTunes clones (Rythym Box, Banshee, and Amarok) and having all three programs fail utterly and completely, I will continue to be a Windows user. Why, you ask? The answer is simple: Windows is an extremely intuitive and easy-to-use operating system that, unlike Ubuntu and it's associated programs, does not require me to spend hours compiling code snippets as well as scouring the Internet in order to possibly find an answer to the specific issue I am facing. Windows, in my opinion, is a much easier to use Operating System whereas Ubuntu is very klunky and quite unwieldy. 

To summarize, I will continue to use Windows and will no longer consider switching to Ubuntu. The Linux community has lost me as a new user.
