### Post by Ralob on 2007-07-14
So, I was a Windows XP user until 3 weeks ago. I had been hearing about Ubuntu all over the internet, and XP was starting to work my last nerve. I could not stand the long load times, the longer shutdown times, and the constant XP notifications were really frustrating me. So, I backed everything up on my external, installed Ubuntu and spent a week trying to understand what the heck I stepped into.

The installation went well enough, unfortunately i had some issues with my nVidia card, Then I had to reinstall XP, then format it to reinstall Ubuntu due to some NTFS error that would not allow NTSF 3g to properly mount it. Anyways, after a week of poking and prodding I knew I was not going back. I was (and still am) a newb in terms of Linux, but everyday I learn something new that makes me bounce up and down with glee. Whether it is something simple like learning how to copy and link folders, or finding a new piece of software gold, I adore Linux. I constantly check sites and read reviews to get as much information about everything as I can.

Some things I love about Ubuntu? Stability. Easy on system resources. Little software bloat. I also love that everything on my system totals about 10GB. And I can back it all up onto a single DVD with Mondo Rescue, *and* keep my home directory update with rsync (or in my case, grsync). I love that everything starts up quickly, and shuts down even faster. Also? I love that Linux has such a wonderful community. I think the best thing is that the developers care about your input, and are actively try to make everything better. The consistent release cycles for Ubuntu, and constant software updates keep things up to date with the always changing internet. 

So, long story short? Me likey Linux. And me likey Ubuntu even more. I hope progress continues as more people switch to Ubuntu and Linux in general. The future looks bright and i am glad to be a part of it. :)

### Post by Ralob on 2007-07-14
The thing is, when I first installed Ubuntu I was completely lost. I had my graphics card acting funky, my HD was not writable due to an improper Windows shutdown error, and my sound was flaky. It took me about a week to get everything set up, find my perfect collection of base software I needed, learn what was what, figure out how to do things that I took for granted in Windows (such as copying folders with the middle click wheel button). And the thing is, once i solved my problems I could slap myself for not recognizing the solution sooner. But I also felt proud that I fixed the damn thing myself. All I had to do was research which I did not mind at all. 

While researching, you pick up so much that seems like useless information which later becomes incredibly useful. For example, I was reading about people running DVD Shrink on Linux with something called WINE. I looked it up, read about it, learned what it's goals were, figured out what it ran well and what it is trying to run well. And that opened up a whole new avenue for me to explore. I then followed WINE to Cedega and Crossover, and then to VMware and VirtualBox and so forth. The thing I love about this community is that it is constantly moving towards progress- new things are ALWAYS happening and it is great to be apart of something that gets better with every passing day. 

I apologize is this is long winded, but I get really hyper and excited when it comes to topics I am interested in. :)
