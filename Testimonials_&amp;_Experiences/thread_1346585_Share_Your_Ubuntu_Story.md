### Post by MooPi on 2009-12-05
Many years ago a friend from high school became involved in the early adoption of Linux. He was aware of the potential of Linux and influenced me to tinker with the OS. He later started a business that has flourished and made him an influential person in the Linux community. His success only encouraged me to delve further into this arcane OS. 
   My first distribution was Turbo Linux and I failed miserably to get it to function. There was no sound and I seem to remember having trouble with the browser. Needless to say my time using Turbo was short lived. Besides they were sold to a Japanese venture and became oriented towards the Asian market. 
   So I was searching for an alternative and moved from Mandrake and then to Red Hat Linux, that was before they became Fedora. When Fedora was first introduced it became my distro of choice and my first really successful Linux installation. I believe that was sometime around 2003 or 2004 if memory serves me correct. I tinkered and learned mostly by trial and error because I didn't have any local support and I didn't want to bother my high school chum. He was busy building his business and I was just tinkering.
  I was still using Microsoft products and still am but I only use Windows for gaming and nothing else. I became increasingly disillusioned by Windows and the bugginess of that OS until I decided that enough is enough and migrated full time to Linux for my daily computing needs. I moved from distro to distro , learning more with every new experience. The breakdown of the distributions is Fedora, Debian, PCLinuxOS, Puppy,and now Ubuntu. I've used others but thats the core of what has graced my hard drives. Ubuntu caught my eye intially because they were based on Debian and that was what I was using.
  Linux is now a passion of mine and I encourage all my friends and family to move to Linux. I have personally converted several people to Linux and am proud to call myself a Linux Pusher ( you know the first one is free). :twisted:
 So that is my Ubuntu story.

### Post by Zoot7 on 2009-12-06
About 4 years ago when I started my degree, I had to use openSUSE for C programming.
The fact that it wasn't Windows was interesting to me, so I went away, downloaded it and installed it on a spare hard drive just to fiddle around with it.
I got to like it and found most of the stuff I could do in Windows I could do in openSUSE aswell.
Then I learned openSUSE wasn't Linux alone and that there were countless other distros available. Then I gave Knoppix a shot, played fairly nice with my desktop at the time used it for a few months again.
Then on to Slackware and Gentoo to "throw myself in the deep end" just to learn the inner workings of Linux, good way to do it - I learned a lot having to compile my own binaries most of the time.
Heard good things about Debian, settled on that for a year.

Then a friend of mine recommended Ubuntu to me, then I gave it a shot on both my Desktop and laptop and both worked perfectly for me (including wireless, which until then was an issue for me).
That was back in the days of Feitsy, and I've used every release since Intrepid (which i skipped because of a botched upgrade).

So that's how I got to Ubuntu. I'm still "Distro Hopping" at the moment, but I'm yet to find a distro I like better than Ubuntu (openSUSE comes second for me).

### Post by Priswell on 2009-12-07
I'd been thinking about an alternative to Windows for a long time. I had started going to a computer users group that was less than inspiring, and one of the members mentioned that he knew of a [linux computer group]("http://fosug.org") that met monthly. He gave me the contact information, and I went to see what I could find. 

The group was exactly what I was looking for in a computer group, lots to learn and the energy to inspire. Everyone in the group used different distros, and I was looking for something easy to use. One of the guys suggested Ubuntu, and I gave it a shot. 

I loaded it on and older computer to play with while I kept my primary running Windows. In the meantime, I installed Ubuntu on every junker I could. 

I intended to make Ubuntu my primary operating system, but to do that, I needed to find replacements for all the software I used, and I had to be competent at it before I made the final switch. 

I've been running Ubuntu only for more than a year now. Since I do some tech support, I expect that I'll be forced to run Windows again someday, but only as a Virtual Machine. In the meantime, I'm a happy little penguin running Ubuntu.
