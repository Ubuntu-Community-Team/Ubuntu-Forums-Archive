### Post by Shibblet on 2011-08-13
You know, it all starts with Ubuntu.  What a great transition from Windows to Linux.  There is a bit of a learning curve.  Take the time to learn how to operate it.  Kind of like going from driving a car to a motorcycle.

Then I started to wonder if Ubuntu is a good distro, and started trying other distros to learn their strengths and weaknesses.

I started with Ubuntu Hardy (8.04) then changed it up a bit.
Mint was great, but the repos aren't the most reliable.
Mandriva was great too, but seemed slow in comparison.
A friend suggested Sabayon for speed, but I don't have the patience for all of that compiling.  That leaves Gentoo out of the mix too.
OpenSuSE comes loaded with the kitchen sink.
Arch is great for people who want to build their own linux.
Puppy is perfect for really old machines...

Finally after all of that distro-hopping, and learning about the different distros, I found that the best distro is the distro that works best for you.  So, I was back to Ubuntu, and I stuck with it from 8.10 to 11.04.

But just recently, I was at a friends house, and he was running Fedora.  This is a distro that I haven't really messed with because I thought it was mostly command line.  After toying around with it at his house for a while, I figured I'd give it a spin on my home PC.

Wow.  The Gnome 3 interface is amazing.  This is definitely the direction that interfaces need to go.  Being a minimalist, I have to admit that I love that Fedora comes with next to nothing installed.  I also don't have the Nvidia tearing issue with video playback that I tried desperately to get rid of in Ubuntu (8.04 - 11.04)

MY friend tells me that it's usually what people do, is get Ubuntu, then distro-hop a bit, go back to Ubuntu, then work their way into a different distro.

So, unlike a lot of Windows users complaining that Ubuntu sucks, I am happy to have met Ubuntu, and thank it for leading me in the direction of Linux.  Who knows, maybe it'll be back on my PC in the future.

I'm not leaving the forums though, there is just too many good people here, and too much information that can be used and given.

### Post by cbowman57 on 2011-08-13
Ok, upper left, Eureka extension by FP Murphy, provides app & places menu, workspace switcher & panel favorites (any app in the Favorites dash show up on the panel)

Center right, weather applet, a couple variations are out there.  This one does current & 2 day forecast, there are others that do 5-day (FP Murphy's).

Right side, media player extension & basic status icons. (gnote extension not shown, moves notification icons from bottom panel to traditional location in top panel)

Upper right corner, right hot corner extension, does the same thing as hitting the upper left but you don't have to move the mouse clear across the screen to do it.

Anyway, just showing that you don't have to give up everything you liked in Gnome 2. :)

Also, once you start getting familiar with the .css most of the extensions can be modified for function or aesthetics.  I'm sure there will be some nice gui apps for manipulating the scripts eventually.
