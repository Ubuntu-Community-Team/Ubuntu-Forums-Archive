### Post by Snowmayne on 2006-08-10
Last month one of my managers "suggested" that maybe it was time I learned a bit of linux since half the servers I'm helping to monitor are on some flavor of unix (sco, aix, linux). So a couple of weeks ago I took one of our spare PCs and worked on installing red hat linux on it. 2 days later it was finally up..... sorta.... no GUI, network card not recognized. But it had the basics that I would need. Being the curious sorta bloke that I am, I started looking at maybe installing linux on my home PC. So after some searching around I found out about LiveCDs. Great, just what I needed. Get to try out some linux without making a comittment (sorta like casual dating, I guess lol) Anyway.... I ended up trying SLAX, Gentoo, Santa Fe and almost every other LiveCD I could get my paws on, trying to find the one that would cause me the least grief in migrating my microsoft mentality over. when I fell onto Ubuntu's site something just sorta 'clicked'. Maybe it's just the meaning behind the word Ubuntu, or the fact that the community seemed so bloody friendly.
To make an epic story just into a novel (couldn't keep this short), I took the plunge last night and installed Ubuntu onto my second hard drive. I thought it might give me a complaint or two about not being on the primary drive with WinXP, but nada. Now, thanks to all of you reading this (and the umpteen many more behind the scenes hard at work making this), I'm anxious to get home to play with my 'new' toy.
You know, on a lot of forum threads I've looked over the usual arguments of "Well, I'm going back to Windows 'cuz linux doesn't do what I want" sorta fall short here. From everything I've read, Ubuntu's made my transition from windows to linux as painless as could be expected.
(I'm just gonna have to start a petition for MythicEntertainment to come out with a linux client so I can play my one MMORPG and not have to go back to windows at all...... {after i convert everything out of outlook that is})
ANYHOOOO!  Yes, thanks and congradulations to all those who've made this such a great linux distro :D
