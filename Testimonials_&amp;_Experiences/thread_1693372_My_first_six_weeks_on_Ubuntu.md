### Post by SEisch on 2011-02-22
Hello everybody.
    I started using Ubuntu in early January, and am new to GNU/Linux in general.  Up until a few months ago I had little knowledge of open source software.  I first learned about it from reading  a book on the history of computers and electronics.  There was a chapter focused on Linux and all the different groups that started using it to create their own G.U.I.s.  I found it interesting so I decided to check out another book specifically about Ubuntu Linux.  It had a live CD in the back of the book.  So I put it in my computer and tried it out.  Even though it was an old version (Breezy Badger), I liked the way it looked and worked.  At Ubuntu's website I downloaded the 10.10 version and decided to give it a try.   It is now the operating system on my only computer.
    This is a second-hand computer, I purchased for fifty U.S. dollars.  So I figured why not try it.  It's not like I'm taking a huge financial risk if it don't work.  I wanted to get rid of the Windows XP O.S.   It still worked, but I had some issues with it.  It had no backup discs, and I would get malware  warnings every other week from the anti-virus program.  
   I have had a mostly positive experience with Ubuntu.  I use my computer for web browsing, email, and a few other simple tasks.  The programs the Live CD came with have mostly worked great for what I do.  With the exception of Totem movie player.  I was surprised that it would not play DVDs right away.  Firefox and Evolution work fine for me.  I use them every day.   As far as speed goes, I think it is a little faster than the original O.S.  It is an older computer so I can't expect it to buzz along at the fastest speeds that new computers do.  I have learned that the suspend option doesn't work well with my hardware, so I either leave it on or shut it off.
    The forums have been very helpful.  Every day I am on the internet, I read a few threads, and try to learn something new every week.  I would like to say thanks to the people who offer help on the forums,and to the developers.  Volunteer help is invaluable to the open source community (or any community for that matter).  

I will continue trying Ubuntu and see how it works for me long term.

Glad to hear that Ubuntu is working for you and has given new life to your older computer. :)

 I hope you have tried VLC for playing DVDs. Also, make sure you have Ubuntu-Restricted-Extras installed. It's in the Software Center. This will install all your plugins and you be able to play DVDs and other media formats. It should make DVDs work in Totem as well. 

I do hope in the long term your experience with Ubuntu will turn you into a lifelong user.
