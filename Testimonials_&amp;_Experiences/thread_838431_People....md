### Post by AndrewCooper on 2008-06-23
To a certain extent I agree with the original poster.  My personal difference is that I recognize that there are a good number of Ubuntu/Linux evangelists out there preaching the good word of Linux to the masses.  They are much like Mac Evangelists.  They use phrases like... 

"Ubuntu/Linux is better than Windows!" 
"It's more stable!"
"It never crashes!"
"Windoze sucks!  Use Ubuntu/Linux!"

And so on and so forth.  Guess what happens.  Some of the "sheep" actually pay attention and decide to check it out.  They check it out expecting it to live up to the rather unrealistic claims of the evangelists.  They expect it to do everything Windows does... only better.  

Guess what.  Ubuntu/Linux doesn't do everything Windows does only better.  It does some things better, some things worse and almost everything different.  So the so-called sheep try it out, get frustrated and feel like they've been lied to.  Is it fair or just for them to blame the distro or the entire Linux platform for their feelings? No, but I understand their point of view.

I *like* Ubuntu.  I use it.  It's the only OS installed on my laptop.  I don't go around pushing my choice of OS at everyone else and listing all the ways it is better than theirs though.  Sometimes someone sees me using it and asks a question.  I answer it to the best of my ability, pointing out strengths and weaknesses of the software and then hand them (or point them to) a Live CD so they can try it out.

If you want the majority of the folks who get frustrated and quit to stop having unrealistic expectations, get the fanatical evangelists to stop preaching the second coming of our Lord Ubuntu who will save us all from the evil schemes of the arch-devil Gates.  (Good luck with that, btw.  Let me know how it turns out.)  Even then, there are going to be some who complain anyway.  There always are.  Until then, there are some folks with a legitimate gripe that the OS didn't perform as promised, even if it wasn't promised to work that way by any reasonable individual.  Oh well...

Andrew Cooper
