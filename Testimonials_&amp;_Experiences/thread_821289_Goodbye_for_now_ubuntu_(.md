### Post by strabes on 2008-06-07
This post is in no way a troll or a complaint. Let me first say that I love linux and ubuntu and dislike Microsoft as much as the next linux user. As you can see from my post count, I have been very active on these forums helping people and asking questions. However, after two years of using ubuntu full time on my laptop, last week I purchased a 17&#8243; macbook pro. I intend to keep OS X Leopard on it.

Here&#8217;s why I did this: I&#8217;m getting ready to graduate from Wheaton College (Chicago) in December and simply do not have time to battle ubuntu for hardware support and stability anymore. My time and nerves are limited.

My parents are still using ubuntu on their computer but I intend to have them purchase an iMac for their next computer (since I am the computer person in our family). I will most likely continue to use ubuntu on hobby computers in the future. Eventually I wish to set up a MythTV box, but that is a project for the future.

I hope that linux will continue to grow in popularity and that more hardware vendors will accordingly begin to cooperate and provide proper drivers. I am sad that I will not be able to continue to help make this happen. For now I want to wish the ubuntu community the best of luck at fixing bug #1. I hope my contributions here have been helpful in achieving this goal, but now it is time to say goodbye to the ubuntu community. It was fun while it lasted. I learned a lot but it is time to move on. Good luck and goodbye.

### Post by mivo on 2008-06-08
Well, going from Windows to Linux means you break free from vendor dependencies. Going to Mac means that you tie yourself to one vendor that provides both the hardware and the OS, and both are closed. Apple's reputation is better than Microsoft's, but they are not a monopolist (certainly would like to be the alpha dog). But Apple is sneaking in stuff as well and engages in some obscure activities that you'd normally only expect from Microsoft. [Here is an example]("http://blogs.sun.com/ahl/entry/mac_os_x_and_the").

Yes, Macs are nice, but the wide-spread belief that they "just work" and that there are no dubious decisions on Apple's end are just the fortunate result of good PR and marketing. Note that I believe that computers and operating systems are only tools, not religions, so everyone should use what they like best and what works best for them. There are certainly issues with Linux, the amount of time to work around problems being one of them. But I don't see much of a difference between Windows and MacOS from a "political" and ideological perspective. If anything, closed hardware (at a premium price) paired with a closed OS is arguably worse than "flexible" hardware paired with a closed OS. :)

Usability is a different issue, though. No disagreement here.
