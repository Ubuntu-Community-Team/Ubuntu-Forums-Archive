### Post by Thanh-BKK on 2008-05-05
Hi :)

I'm a complete newbie as well so to speak but the first thing, before even making Ubuntu my only OS, was to tweak the hell out of it in terms of "looks". To me as a gay guy this is more important than the engine that hums under the hood, altough i sure appreciate when that Linux engine doesn't stutter like Windows does sometimes :)

So i used Vista for over a year, which is about as "beautiful" as Windows can be. But it took me a few hours (!) to have Ubuntu blow it out of the water and shoot it to the moon. Ever tried changing your icon theme on Windows? In XP that's a lengthy procedure that can break your system (and did break mine a few times when toying around with it!) and in Vista it is, as far as i know, not [possible at all.

Ubuntu? Download an icon theme (and those are free, non-hacked, no activation needed and there's guaranteed no virus hidden in the package!), drop it (!!) into the theme manager and voila - done. Changing icons (all of them! At once!!) is now a matter of a couple of clicks and it's INSTANT, not even a reboot required!

And if you don't like some of the icons, you can even mix-and-match from different icon themes (altough that requires a few more click, still all clicks, no command line!) and even use specific, single, icons. That works ANYWHERE - in the menus, on the desktop, even in the AWN-dock. 

Just as easy you can change the appearance of your window borders (try that on Windows!) and the colour of just about every single item in the whole Linux system. Transparency a la Vista? A click away if you have Compiz installed (requires 3D and comes stock with Ubuntu). 

Ubuntu is WAY more colourful than ANY Windows version, and YOU are the one who choses what colour and what effect goes where :) 

I guarantee you - you will have tons of fun with Ubuntu once you are getting used to it :)

Best regards......

Thanh
