### Post by nlvivar on 2010-11-27
I just made the plunge installing Lubuntu 10.10 on my Eee PC. I have been using Ubuntu Netbook on it for almost 3 years now, running a stripped-down Openbox session as my window manager. So I am fairly experienced with installing Ubuntu on this machine, and I was very excited to see an LXDE packaging for Ubuntu.

So I just want to recount one snag that I hit in installing Lubuntu. I installed from scratch, but specified to encrypt my home directory partition. I did not realize that this would cause a problem wherein my home directory would not be mounted and "start" menu would not work.

Fortunately, I had a fair amount of experience troubleshooting Ubuntu installations and had another computer to search on. I was able to get everything working once I had mounted the home partition manually, and updated to the newest packages.

If I could be so bold to make a recommendation to the Lubuntu maintainters, here is my recommendation. I would either remove the option to encrypt the home partition or update the ISO image to include the up-to-date packages.

Lubuntu looks like a great addition to the Ubuntu family. It fills a void where netbooks and low-powered machines reside. I would hate to see newcomers to Lubuntu being turned off by this.

Even though I hit this little snag, I have to say, "Great job, Lubuntu team!"

-Noel
