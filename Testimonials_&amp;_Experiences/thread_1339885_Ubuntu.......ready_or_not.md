### Post by 23dornot23d on 2009-11-29
There is a time frame ........ where at the time you buy your computer the Linux Distros
are being made for the hardware available at that particular time ....

What happens as time passes .... new hardware and new ideas and features come out .......

I have always found that the Distros produced closer to the time my computer was produced are often better than the newer ones ........ 

( its not always best to upgrade . as upgrades themselves can be troublesome )

But I often burn new Distros and run them from live CD's/DVD's just to see how well they do work first ...... 

Mandriva 2010 and Karmic seem pretty good at picking up lots of new hardware ...... as a fresh install ......

But why anybody would want all the additional clutter on a older computer that works
perfectly well as it is ....... is another question ........

If you have an issue with a particular item that does not work ..... keep trying the Distros on CD's/Dvd's till you find one that better suits you .......

But upgrade or fresh install only if there are really clear benifits from doing so ........... 

I still run something called KANOTIX on a old laptop ....... because most of the other newer distros cannot pick up the Old Graphics set up on it ......

Also some of the good and useful older software still runs on the old Distros ....... 

I installed a DVD the other day KNOPPIX ...... 11 gigs worth of packages on it ..... all tested and all work well together for that particular time frame ......

If you start upgrading ..... it gradually knocks the packages off one by one .......
till there is very little left ....... yet the ones supplied with it at the time work well
and very very quickly ........

Sort of saying that would go with this ...... 

"There are horses for courses ......"

Ok thats my 2 cents ..... for what its worth ...... but interesting  ... as some
automatically think because its the latest ..... its going to be better .....

It does not always work out like this ,,,,, in my experience and I have had a few
bad ones in the past but learn from them.

PS ..... for people that love to mess about ....... and try new things out .......

Have at least one USB Hard Drive as a backup facility (I have 2) ...... love these even if your computer dies ...... they just easily disconnect and can be taken straight to another computer ..... and plug in ...... you quickly get what you need off the drive .....

.......... and always back your valuable data up ...... on a regular basis

Music Photos Designs Documents whatever ........... or don't ..... your choice  ......

I'm confused.

What new LTS are talking about - 8.04 or 10.04? If it's 10.04 Hmmm. General release (I believe) is scheduled for April 2010.

The option to upgrade is just that "An Option". If your looking for stability, it's been my experience that the LTS releases tend to be extremely stable.

No one forces us to upgrade or even update. Believing that the newest version of any software must be the best, is questionable. Many new versions of software are not compatible with specific hardware and may not cooperate with other software packages.

Perhaps, by reading [this]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/philosophy"), you may better understand the philosophy of Ubuntu. Nowhere, is it stated that your required to upgrade or even use the OS. Only that your given a choice. I struggle with those individuals that somehow believe their entitled. That no matter what hardware or software package they have - it's Ubuntu's (Canonical's) responsibility to assure that it works.

IMHO, comparing it to any other OS is foolish and a waste of time. If it doesn't work for you, use a different OS - at least you had a choice. If it were up to some - there would be no choice.

Just my $0.02
