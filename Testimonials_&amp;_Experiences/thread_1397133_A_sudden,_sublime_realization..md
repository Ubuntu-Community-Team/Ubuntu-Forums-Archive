### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-02-02
So, I got up this morning, ate some toast and chocolate milk, and sat down to my Dell Mini 10v. I'm greeted by a Gnome desktop with an autohiding panel at the top, peacefully out of the way, with the Mira v2 theme, and compiz. Very light, fast, and easy to work with.

I proceed to open "Movie Player" (Totem) to watch some Youtube videos as I reply to some emails very quickly in Google Chrome, open OpenOffice to add an entry to my preformatted journal, then start Pidgin to talk to some friends for about 10 minutes before starting up some games in Wine to stimulate my brain a little before going on my errands. Wiggling some windows out of boredom along the way. XD

This all happened so gracefully, and I realized something. I may have liked Linux at first because it was fast, because it was open source and preserving my freedom in software,  because compiz was fun.

But now I realize that Linux has created such a comfortable, integrated, and care free environment for me to work in, that it feels like the OS isn't even there half of the time. It's just so natural. So easy.

I have a deeper appreciation for Linux, now. It's finally what a computer should be. It lets me have the power of modern technology, unhampered by any perceptible issues. It's just so GOOD.

I used to think people were correct, but somewhat biased and overexaggerating when they said, "Linux is the best OS ever."

But really, for me, today, this software is perfect. I can only imagine how much better things will be in the future, and how much greater my satisfaction will be.
