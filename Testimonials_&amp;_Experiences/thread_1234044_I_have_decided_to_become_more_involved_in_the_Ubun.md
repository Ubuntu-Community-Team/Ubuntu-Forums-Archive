### Post by hoppipolla on 2009-08-07
Hey all!

I've been a linux fan for years, using it in place of MS Windows up until only recently when I switched back to XP (mainly just for ease of use and because Windows XP happened to be there at the time!). I've checked out quite a few distros like Suse (before it was named OpenSUSE), Slackware, Ubuntu, and a lot of live CDs and smaller distributions..

Now, I wanna switch back! I've been impressed by the new KDE (4.3), new versions of Compiz, work on apps like Amarok 2... plus I love the Open Source community in general!

However, I'd just like to share my views on a few things in the hope of just getting some discussions going and putting some thoughts out there :)

Ok, well the first thing is, I think the Ubuntu approach is amazing. I love the philanthropic and benevolent nature of the company and Mark Shuttleworth, I love the financial support of KDE he offers, and most of all I love the focus on friendliness and usability :)

But, I'm afraid I shall not be using Ubuntu when I re-install Linux. I will be using Fedora, but still favouring Ubuntu as my favourite overall community/distro.

The problem I find currently with Ubuntu is it just appears to move too slow. The desktop has not changed in so many releases, and even though I know the big change may come about when the distros start making the switch to KDE 4 (when it becomes stable), I can't help but wonder why they don't just make little changes to enhance it's aesthetics for new users.

I mean just check out this mock-up created TWO YEARS ago: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=437694](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=437694)

This is all comfortably possible in Ubuntu now, using Gnome.

The thing is, as much as I love the distro, I need a default desktop that I can show off to new users and convince them that Linux really is a good OS and can compete with Windows and OSX! Does anyone else agree?

I guess for the most part that's my only gripe. It would also be nicer to see a slightly more attractive boot-up like on Fedora, and continues to embrace the latest in Linux technologies :)


Anyway, take care all and that's my 2 cents... can't wait to get stuck in again using Linux!

Mike, UK ^_^
