### Post by Yoknapatawpha on 2008-05-08
I just upgraded while working on grades for a recent class. I was asked four questions, the first one was quite simple, "do you want to continue?" with a recommendation to close any open programs. I closed everything (had backups anyway) but Gnumeric and kept working.

The second question was "Gnumeric is open, do you want to skip the Gnumeric upgrade or close it?" I paused my work, saved, and closed Gnumeric.

The third was regarding SAMBA, and whether or not I wanted to keep my etc/profile regarding a share folder I have or use the developer's script instead. I was presented a side-by-side comparison of the two files to decide upon. I chose the new file, since I don't share anything anymore anyway.

Lastly, it asked me if I wanted to remove all the 'obsolete' software. NOW THAT may have been confusing to a standard non-tekkie-windows or newbie Linux user. 
I said yes, because I noticed they had been 'replaced' with newer versions. However that was not clearly presented as such; it took me looking at the logs to discover it and some people might have been afraid to say yes. Maybe I should have said no, but that remains to be seen. I'm not completely sure why I needed to respond to that question at all, so this bears looking into.

after the reboot, I'm still working where I was; so far not a hitch. 8 hours later; nothing hasn't worked and no speed problems whatsoever.  As people posted in the alpha and beta releases, Firefox Beta  3 is faster.  Doesn't matter to me, I use Galeon anyway.

I'm not a guru; I can generally fix what is broken but I've still never been able to get cmpfz working properly. I was always able to go back to the previous configuration, even though I relied on some Web forums/tutorials to do that at times.  I use a sony viao with 1G RAM and of course, the "stubborn" video card.

I've been *trying* to be MS free since '98.  I always kept win4lin 'just in case' and generally there were 'cases'.  Ubuntu 7.1 was the first distro that I used that allowed me to wander away completely.  This PDF Editor software finally allowed me to ditch Acrobat. :-({|= 

I work for a U.S. college in China, so being able to use QQ would be nice, but the Tencent people keep thwarting Pidgin's efforts there somehow.  Anyhow, THAT certainly isn't worth a VMWare system.  I DID run VMWare in 7.1 for a while, just until I figured out how to use the Python add-on for using SMS in Skype for Linux.  VMWare is here now just so I can show people the different 'flavors' of Ubuntu (K, X, etc) :guitar:

Oh, there is one other 'benefit' after doing the upgrade. I went from 25.2 G free space to 26 G of free space. Let's see an MS upgrade match that! *oh yeah, there WAS doublespace*
#smack#:lolflag:

Thanks to all of you :KSdevelopers:KS that have made this a truly remarkable product!

peace y'all,
vincent
