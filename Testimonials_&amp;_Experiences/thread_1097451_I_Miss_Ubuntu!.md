### Post by weweboom on 2009-03-16
I was going on vacation for spring break, and decided, before i left, I'd try out Mandriva while on spring break. I had just tried to upgrade using a faulty cd, so I had no working os at the time. Mandriva is painful. For me it is anyway. With all the ease of use and availibility of ubuntu, switching to Mandriva was like swimming while covered in shrink wrap, it's just not do-able. I have been taking ubuntu for granted for a while, and just wanted to share this experience with other ubuntu users. A warning not to be taken lightly: DON'T try a unfamiliar linux distribution if your going somewhere where you won't be able to reinstall ubuntu. I greatly look forward to the end of spring break, and getting back to my cdr's where I will promptly download and install ubuntu again, until then :'( If you have an experience that relates to this one, please share it!
