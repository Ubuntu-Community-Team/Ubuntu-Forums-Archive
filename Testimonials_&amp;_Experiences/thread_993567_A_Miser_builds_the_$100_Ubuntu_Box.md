### Post by w4ett on 2008-11-25
A Miser Builds the $100 Ubuntu Box.

Being the frugal old geezer that I am, I decided to try and build a full featured Ubuntu Box for $100 (or as close as I could get it).  I might add that while discussing this project, I am not endorsing any  online or downtown (highstreet)  suppliers, but I will give a nudge or two to head you in the right direction if any of you decide to duplicate this project.

I have a pretty well stocked junkbox in the closet and decided to include a few parts from it to help keep the cost down.  This fact and the current prices of hardware on [www.pricewatch.com](www.pricewatch.com) made me feel like I could succeed in this project.

The first thing I did was drag out a case and power supply, dusted them off and then looked through the assorted cards I had collected over the past couple of years.  What I came up with was:

1.A generic mid tower atx case.
2.An Ultra-Finity XF-500 Power Supply
3.An IX-TV FM TV Tuner Card
4.A Jetway FX5500 128MB Video Card
5.A CoolerMaster HeatSink/Fan
6.Various IDE and Audio Cords

The Case and Power Supply were hand-me-downs from a friend....the TV Card I purchased from Amazon.com, way back in 2001, and the video card was a gift from a friend's basement collection.

I started searching for a CPU and MOBO at this point.  A quick trip to the local Mom and Pop computer shop gave me an Athlon XP 2500+ CPU for $5.00.  Looking on the web turned up a New FIC K7MNF-64 Mobo (an E-Machines N.O.S. Castoff) with an Nforce2 chipset for $22.00 shipped and a stick of DDR PC3200 Ram (1 GB for $18 shipped----I splurged and bought 2 :) ) Now for a Hard Drive....a quick search gave me a 200GB White Label WD 8MB Buffer drive for $38.  I then waited a few days for the parts to arrive.  While waiting, I had a quick look at the local Goodwill thrift Store.......UREKA......what did I Find????? A  Sony DVD-CD/RW drive for $5.00 (in a sealed box---NEW)!!!!

Well, after a week, all the parts were in a pile in front of me......and after an hour of fiddling I got all the various bits together, connected a mouse, keyboard and monitor and proceeded with a smoke (post) test......

All went well.....I booted into the Bios setup and made sure that the setup was correct and I also set the FSB to 400 Mhz in the hope of overclocking the CPU for a little extra performance.  Upon reboot, the boot splash screen indicated I was running an Athlon XP 3200+ @ 2.10 Ghz....I suppose that can be called a coup.......a 2500+ Athlon XP normally runs at 1833 Mhz at a FSB of 333, but this one seems quite stable at 400 FSB....This places it faster than the  Sempron 2800  in socket A/462, especially since the Athlon Barton Core has 512Kb  L2 Cache as opposed to 256Kb for the Sempron.

The install of 8.04 went smoothly, including the restricted drivers for the video card...I've been running it now for two weeks continuously with no crashes.

I haven't had the time to fiddle with the MythTV install, but the TV card is working, and I am going to tackle it in the next week or two.
The moral to the story is, I suppose, that one does not have to spend a fortune to get a good computer running......I could have even used the GeForce 2/4 video on the motherboard if I didn't already have the 5500 card lying around and only used 1 Gb of memory (or even 512MB!) which would have even driven the price lower.....

This machine is certainly not a “gamer's delight”, but I do run Warcraft and have full use of 3D Effects...glxgears runs about 2000FPS...I also came in a little over budget, but it was worth it for a full 2 GB of Ram!

If anyone wants to know more complete details, drop me a PM and I'll reply!

Total Costs:

Mobo: 22.00
CPU:     5.00
Ram    36.00
HDD   38.00
DVD     5.00
Total:  106.00

Good Luck !!!

### Post by w4ett on 2008-11-26
For sure there is lots of hardware available out there to run Ubuntu quite handily.  As far as my build goes, I was looking at the EeePC 701 4G that I use on a daily basis....it only runs @ 600 Mhz and has enjoyed great success in the marketplace....Millions have been sold worldwide....Mine runs perfectly with Eeebuntu 8.04, and prior to that Eeexubuntu 7.10.  Older, little used parts can be placed in service instead of collecting dust or filling up a landfill polluting the environment.

As far as daily usage goes, i can find little difference between this build and my Athlon 64 X2...Of course I don't do video editing or other cpu intensive things, but it just goes to show that one does not need to have the fastest processor on the block to have a decent, trouble free computer system.
