### Post by harbdog on 2010-07-23
I know its a common theme to see people new (or old) to Ubuntu get frustrated when things don't work out of the box (just like my eee1000 wifi issue with 10.04).  But I thought I'd share the story of my newest laptop, a Dell Studio laptop I got just a few months ago, because very often installing Windows yourself can be a much more frustrating experience when it comes to drivers. Probably good for most people that their computers comes pre-installed or that they pay someone else to reinstall for them.


First I'll begin with the experience of what the laptop came with: Windows 7 x64. Like any sane person I immediately wiped the factory install due to all the bloatware and installed over it.  After an hour of installing, I then had to pop in the driver CD and started loading the drivers, not in any particular order really just things I knew needed to be there. 

Sure enough I get all the drivers installed... only the wireless doesn't work. I look around for at least an hour trying to get it work, but in the end it comes down to something I read on the Dell site, that there was a specific order in which I had to install every driver.  So, I wiped it, installed windows again, and installed each driver in the order specified to finally get everything going right.  It probably took at least 5 hours from when I started installing the first time to when I finally had a usable installation.


Some time later, around the week that Ubuntu 10.04 was released I downloaded it to see if it all would work and if there might be extra work I might have to do for the wireless or whatever to function.  I loaded up the live version first and the wireless worked right out of the box, as did the video and sound!  Probably no more than 30 minutes later I had a fully installed Ubuntu with no driver problems whatsoever.


**Summary:**
[LIST]
[*]5 hours = Time to install Windows 7 AND all necessary drivers
[*]30 minutes = Time to install Ubuntu 10.04
[/LIST]


So to finish up I hope people can forgive Ubuntu and other Linux distros when they can't get everything to work this easily, perhaps give it some time and try again as its getting better all the time. Realize its still an uphill battle since these hardware manufacturers aren't always kind enough to provide the drivers in an open enough fashion as to be included for all of our convenience.  Cheers!
