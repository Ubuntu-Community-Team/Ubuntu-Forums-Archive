I can honestly say my hardware is almost perfect, everything works being to video card, all the way to wireless.

Another point being, It also makes me frustrated when people say "My hardware was working on Windows but not Linux, so Linux is a terrible OS". Well as the old saying goes "Linux is not Windows". Most of the time you just have to do a little configuring, then the hardware will most likely work with no issues.

I'd also like to ask a question to the people that complain about hardware and installation problems with Linux. Have you ever setup a Windows box? The installation process is way worse than one of Ubuntu in my honest opinion. Most computers come pre-installed with Windows, so the end user would never now how it was to actually install Windows from scratch, and believe me I've done it, it's a pain and I'm not taking anything away from Windows, I think Windows is a great OS and when it works right, it works great!

The bottom line is as stated above you need to do research and think of logical reasons why to switch to Linux and why Linux may have advantages for you. I know a lot of people that post saying "Ubuntu is terrible" are just venting, but logically, you have to stop and think and ask yourself is it my problem, or Ubuntu's problem? That brings me to my next and final point, no OS is going to be perfect, every OS has it's flaws, and if you think an OS is going to be perfect you're in for a surprise.

In conclusion, just please do research and don't just blame Ubuntu for all the problems you may be having. If you do enough research I can almost assure you that the solution is out there somewhere and you can get everything up and running, and the main thing is patience. Thanks for reading.
