### Post by Groucho Marxist on 2010-01-08
"I may not have gone where I intended to go, but I think I ended up where I needed to be."

As far back as I can remember, I have been a Windows user. I can clearly recall using Windows 3.0 on one of my mother's engineering computers in the early nineteen-nineties, or browsing the Internet for the first time in Internet Explorer. This pattern of Microsoft loyalty continued unabated with only minor dabbles into the world of Macintosh in secondary school into my current college career.  
 
 Microsoft Vista changed everything for me. A sub-par operating system from a hardware and software standpoint, Vista was worthy more for a laugh than a laptop. On the other hand, I frankly could not afford to purchase any of Apple's computers, nor was I pleased when reviewing the technical specifications of said computers in relation to what I would be paying for.  
 
The choices before me were as follows; purchase broken Windows or an over-priced Apple. Never settling for the "A or B" illusion of freedom, I chose a third option and I've never been happier using a computer; I chose Ubuntu Linux. :)

### Post by staf0048 on 2010-01-08
> **Groucho Marxist said:**
> Succinctly speaking, no worries! :)

Actually, if you'd like me to elaborate, I did try Linux some time ago.... 

I purchased either a Redhat or Mandrake version at Best Buy around '99-'00.  Had it set up as a dual boot with Win '98, but could never get my 56k modem to connect.  I had no clue about Open Source or Free Software, at the time I'd just heard chattering that Linux was better so I wanted to see what the fuss was about.I liked the interface and everything, but then Win XP came around.  I ended up buying a new computer and gave up trying to get whatever distro I had working with my modem.

It wasn't until around 2007 that I got back into Linux.  I purchased a new laptop with Vista on it and checked out a book at the library written by a few fellows at cnet about all the cool things you could do with a laptop.  Low and behold one of the things they talked about was Ubuntu.  So I figured I'd check it out again as I was none too thrilled with Vista.  I was very happy with how far it had come in those 7 years.  Everything worked out of the box - except suspend/resume - and since then I have been learning and slowly converting completely over to Linux.  I can honestly say I'm much happier running Ubuntu than I was with Vista or XP for that matter.
