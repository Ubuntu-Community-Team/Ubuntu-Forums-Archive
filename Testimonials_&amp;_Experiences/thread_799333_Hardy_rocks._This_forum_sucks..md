### Post by JC Cheloven on 2008-05-18
Hi, I've spent some time reading this forum over the last weeks. I've seen far more good experiences that bad ones about 8.04, both in my neighbourhood and in this forum. 

But when a negative experience is posted (such as "leaving ubuntu", "hardy is the worst ubuntu yet", "back to xxyyzz"...), an endless suite of posts follow. And they're usually from satisfied/enthusiastic  people!! From some point on (after answer N 100 or so), the posts have nothing to do with the original subject. A nonsense.

This has the effect of keeping the negative titles on top, while the (many) positive ones drop very soon (a positive thread usually has no more than 3 or 4 answers, if any). When a newcomer visits the forum to decide whether to give ubuntu a go, he/she's presented with a screen plenty of negative thread titles. It's a biased, non desirable result.

So, please, avoid be engaged in endless discussions under negative thread titles, as it is of no help at all. If someone had a bad experience, just try to help with his particular problem. And if he likes M$, that's ok, no need to post about that. After all, the whole thing is about freedom, isn't it?

BTW, My own experience with hardy is fairly positive. I'm being asked to install linux in a growing number of pc's, as people has more and more bad experiences with vista (the only release which seems to work is the most expensive one, "ultimate"). I've installed hardy in some 10 desktops and laptops so far, most of the times with no or little problems. In average, I had more problems with gutsy.

Greetings

### Post by OmniCloud on 2008-05-19
I think the problem with Ubuntu is 

1) it's own popularity, there's just too many headless chickens running around here...

2) the release schedule is more about getting bleeding edge out rather than stability. 

I have buntu and Lenny running on my system and that's probably how it will always be. I know, that my Debian will back me up if something goes wrong with Ubuntu. Being that this is a LTS release, that might be different now. But before Hardy, Ubuntu sometimes just seem to "not like" my hardware lol. 

I think this is due to the updates. I truly believe this place would be much more appealing if the Ubuntu community encouraged strongly for users to stay with the major releases. 

For Debian you have 
Stable
Testing
Unstable 

Now, there's plenty people who would say testing is just as stable as anything, and there's plenty who have no problems running sid. But it's a matter of perception. 

People who try out Debian for the first time and a fairly new, tend to flock to stable. Because there scared of testing/unstable. It's not a bad thing. It's a fear out of respect. It's like "Hey, I'm a new, let me stick with the basics."

having said that, I'm surprised just how stable Hardy is on my Dell. But I don't like the fact that so many other people are having problems. Maybe there running compiz with too many effects enabled? Maybe compiz shouldn't be enabled by default? Maybe there's just a bunch of idiots who don't know how to handle a Linux OS. 

Whatever the case, Ubuntu is all right by me. It's hopefully here to stay. And I hope Hardy becomes more stable for everyone! 

Stick to the main releases! Only upgrade if your not a newb anymore, until then, run bleeding edge at your own risk, and be prepared to tweak/fix/customize/learn. Because that's the only way your gonna learn linux the right way.
