### Post by cstudent on 2005-11-02
Last night I decided to install Breezy onto my Acer Aspire 3500 laptop. I had it setup as a dual boot system with XP and Linspire. I installed Breezy over the Linspire partition and left the system dual boot. Making sure I had Breezy installing on and formatting the correct partition, the installation went without a flaw. After installation I updated the software and then installed and ran Arnieboy's Automatix script. Again things went smoothly. But the real kicker that made me smile a great big grin was when all I had to do was go into the network settings and set my wireless adapter to active and my laptop connected to my network via my wireless access point. I messed with that for 3 days when I installed Linspire. And then I would have to screw with it for five or ten minutes everytime I booted up Linspire. Breezy just worked out of the box! I stayed up until 1:00am installing software and playing around with my new Ubuntu laptop with a big 'ol smile on my face. Linspire claims to be the easiest desktop Linux, but my vote goes to Ubuntu. Great job Ubuntu team!

Bill Kirby
