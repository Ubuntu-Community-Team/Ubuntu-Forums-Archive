### Post by generalpurpose on 2008-11-23
the only option was Ubuntu...

     I had accidentally downloaded Ubuntu Studio, call it fate. I burned the DVD and have found it quite likable. Sure it takes a second to get used to and the only things I can't use are Photoshop CS3 and Sony Vegas Pro tools. The video and photo editing tools just don't cut the cheddar in Ubuntu, but I found all the rest of the tools to work perfectly for me.

     I went to put in the XP disk and I couldn't get it to boot, albeit the disk being fine and booting in other machines, just didn't want to boot in this one. I went to a friend's house and googled "download Ubuntu" and whilst not paying attention clicked on Ubuntu Studio download page and thought nothing of it. Downloaded the DVD image and burned it said thank you and left. Booted it up and was like "hey this isn't what I remembered..." but I gave it a chance.

     Not having my programs on my work machine makes me a sad ducky but I really like Ubuntu Studio. I am going to just buy another hard drive for XP and put this one in another machine. Thanks for such a great distribution everything I had qualms with are gone. The theme is much better, there is only one task bar which is much better imho, everything just seems to have all the little nicessities that regular Ubuntu seemed to lack. Everything looks and feels much more professionally done. I am much happier and better for the were. Ubuntu Studio seriously rocks and I love it. Everything seems faster too. I think I finally found a home Linux Distribution.

     Thanks for the free beer! Everything will go to really good use! I must say the work done on this one makes me smile and gives me the "wow" experience Vista promised!
