### Post by MycroftMkIV on 2008-02-13
I have a friend that I build computers for, and I talked her into an AMD system and Linux, since that is what I mostly use. At first she had openSUSE, but when I switched all my computers to Ubuntu, she had me switch her too, so she could get instant tech support.

When she started having trouble with her AMD-64 motherboard, she told me to get her an Intel motherboard. So I got her a new case, MB, memory and NVidia 8500 video card (the old machine had a 6800 card).

Once I got the new machine assembled and running (using a live Ubuntu CD booting from a USB drive to verify), I unplugged the SATA hard drives and the DVD drives from her old machine and plugged them into the new one. I powered up the new Intel Core 2 Duo machine and sat back. It just ran! No tweaks, no reinstalls, no nothing. Having not done something like that before, I was impressed.

If the machine had been running any flavor of Windows, I would have had to either uninstall all the drivers and install new ones, or re-install the entire O/S and all the applications.

Kudos to Ubuntu...

Mike
Hayden Lake, ID
