### Post by bibble235 on 2011-10-18
Hi All,

I love ubuntu and move from Fedora around v8 (I could be wrong). Fedora was Redhat and that is where I started.

I moved because installing on Redhat was so hard. I worship the command line but rpm was so difficult compared to apt-get.

So from 7 to 8 to 9 to 10 (lucid) I admired the improvements and the additional functionality. The numbers ubuntu had meant most problems could be solved. And there were problems

- Editing cups to work
- ndiswrapper
- SATA and 1.5 setting
- Ralink 2070 wifi

to name a few. 

This forum solved them for me and I am grateful 

So it is ashame to see me remove 11.10 off my two machines

This is because

- The Unity interface is rubbish for me
  Alt tab is crap
  multiple instances of applications hard to navigate. Previously clicked on taskbar
  No menus is time consuming
  Many application produce white screen for window
  low resolution for nvidia does not work for DVI
  mouse locks up
  DVD playback is useless for nv44a
  refusal to move taskbar for Unity is plain stupid
  menus at top of screen is crap

If you want people to like your new distro then make

- install easy (OK)
- network works (improved)
- browser works (OK)
- mail works (FAILED often screen is completely white)
- dvd playback works (FAILED slow and unusable)
- easier to use (FAILED style issue maybe)
- prettier (FAILED style issue maybe)

Hopefully peeps at ubuntu will listen. The fact I am willing to go back to 10.04 is a plus.

Sorry to be mean but it is hard to persuade people to not use windows and this release did not help

Iain
