### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-06-01
> **za.zen said:**
> I wonder, are you using Xubuntu on older hardware?  Is Xubuntu good for fast, multi-core CPUs, too?

Yessir. My 'puter is an old hand-me-down Dell Dimension B110 with a paltry 512 of RAM. Xubuntu runs sweetly on it.

But speed and lightness aren't the only reasons that people use [Xfce]("http://xfce.org"). It's an awesome DE in it's own right. It's ease and simplicity without any loss of features and integration is what won me over from [LXDE]("http://lxde.org"). I like the little bit of eye candy, the applets (weather in the panel, analog clock, opacity, Orage calendar, etc.), and it's superbly elegant integration with the Xfce apps: Midori, which I added in place of Firefox, Ristretto, Xfburn (even easier than K3B, but much lighter), and more. For the simple ordinary stuff I use a 'puter for, Xubuntu is more than sufficient, and it's positively gorgeous as well.

Xfce-4 on top of minimal Ubuntu is pro'lly faster, but to get an idea of what I'm ranting about, read the [Xubuntu Strategy Document]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Xubuntu/StrategyDocument"). It makes things alot clearer about why Xubuntu is built the way it is.

I loved LXDE too, by the way - very light and fast, but my experience with it was buggy, and I have not seen any noticeable difference in performance on my 'puter. I like to revisit LXDE from time to time just to see how it's doing. But my most recent experiment with it was buggy as before, and kinda unpolished. My older brother calls it "Windows98ish."

Loving Xubuntu,
Robin
