### Post by Woolio1 on 2010-02-06
I began using Ubuntu Linux a few months ago. I heard about it on Lifehacker and decided to see what all the hype was about. So I found the site, downloaded the ISO, and fired up Virtual Box. After it booted, I was amazed. 

The interfaces were well-polished, it was solid, and it seemed to work well. So I burned the ISO, and used it as a liveCD. Rebooted, booted from disk... Everything worked! I was shocked at that point, because half the stuff connected to my PC used proprietary drivers. For something to just work... It's amazing. 

Recently, I purchased a Dell Mini 10 with one intention - To have a primary Ubuntu machine. Setup was a breeze, as always. Take the 9.10 ISO, burn it to a thumb drive, and plug it into the side of the PC. In fifteen minutes, everything was up and running. I plugged in the ethernet cable, and installed the BCMWL-Kernel-Source to use the Broadcom Wifi chip. Five minutes, and the download was finished and working. No reboot! 

I have had a few minor problems, but most were brought on by my poking around in places I shouldn't have. I learned to not take Sudo lightly (Sorry Sudo.), and I learned to not type that RM-Rf type code. But, I believe, without my poking around, none of those things would have happened, and it would have "Just worked". 

I give the Operating System an A+ for User Friendliness, and I thank everyone who worked to make this possible.

-Ericson
Split between Windows and Ubuntu, with more than half siding with Linux.

### Post by sh4rkbyt3 on 2010-02-07
I hate to be the naysayer but unfortunately my "go's" with Ubuntu have not been as nice. It seems with each release new issues and problems arise that make users have to do so much work to get the Linux OS to just work properly on their systems it barely seems worthwhile. Personally I would love nothing more than to finally be rid of Windows once and for all. 
For those who have it hook up and work right off the bat I'm sure it's a pleasurable experience but for me and many others it's been anything but pleasant. From wireless issues that seem to have no resolve to conflicting hardware issues, it never seems to end.
In comparison for example wireless, Windows has had this problem solved for almost 8 years now! But in Linux you have to try this, download that, turn off this, attach that, I mean who wants to go through all of that? I'm sorry but it's not a user-friendly system yet! And then to find out their is no resolution in the end?
Also on some of these forums is the smug and arrogant attitude of quite a few of the replies. Part of the mantra is Open Source Community, whereby others with knowledge help those without. Instead I see responses like "aww someone said something mean to you on the internet"?

A) People are often taking on a heck of a commitment here and trying to learn something which in turn might also benefit the community later.
B) It's frustrating enough trying to convert the thought process from normal everyday thinking to apply to technology.
C) If I came to you for help in public after you offered it and gave me a response like that (aww someone said something mean on the internet) or several others I've seen, you'd be getting punched in the face, literally. 
D) Believe me, there are a lot of people who like me, want to free themselves from the chains of Bill Gates or Steve B. (now) but we want an OS that works, is at least semi-user friendly and you don't get hassles for trying to learn something new (which the community talks about) but then hassles you about.

Ubuntu is getting better but it's still a huge learning curve and in some cases there are NO resolutions for some problems still. 
Windows isn't better but it sure as hell is a lot user-friendly and works more often than not which I can't say about Ubuntu yet.
D)
