### Post by macobas on 2008-04-26
I am now convinced that Ubuntu and AMD 64 are not a good combination.  I run gutsy on my wife's Intel box and have experienced one tenth of the problems I have had to address in my AMD 64 laptop,

Yesterday I upgraded my laptop from 7.10 to 8.04 and the first thing I noticed was that sounds were all screwed up, upon login in, it sounded like the speakers were broken.  After trying for a couple of hours I decided to do a fresh install from an 8.04 live CD.  I lost all my settings and customizations but I was willing to redo everything.  After the fresh install the sound problem disappeared, but it went downhill from there.  I got my nvidia drivers loaded and working as well as Compiz but Emerald themer would not do anything.  When the display was turned off by power management I could not get it back on -- it would not wake up with key strokes nor mouse moves so I had to do a hard shutdown and restart.  I lost my volume names and could not get them back.  Actions that required admin password would not do anything the first time and I had to try a second time for them to work.  Whenever I tried SUDO, it failed with a message saying "cannot resolve <my computer name>"...

Today I spent 5 hours trying to get things into some sort of reasonable working status but I gave up.

All I can say is thank God for remastersys.  It was a snap to restore everything back to where it was before 8.10.

I guess I'll wait 2 or 3 months and try again.:(
