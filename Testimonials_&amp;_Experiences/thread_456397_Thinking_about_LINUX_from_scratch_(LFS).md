### Post by 13thMonkey on 2007-05-27
I'm a 'born again' Linux n00b (used Unix and Linux way back when but forgot everything I knew, even after I installed Ubuntu). I certainly never had a clue about compiling things or how Linux really worked.

I installed LFS a few months ago and got it (almost) working. I went back and fixed a few things but I couldn't get my network card to work or get bash to work in the native LFS after boot,  but I had a few things to try before I had to get rid of the partition so I think it was doable if I'd had more time/hd space.

It took me a day and a half on and off and I learnt a lot from it. If you're just going to c+p then you may as well save yourself the trouble, but if you're going to c+p and then go and research why you've just c+p what you did as it compiles then it's definitely worth spending a weekend on - even if it doesn't work. *Especially* if it doesn't work as expected!
