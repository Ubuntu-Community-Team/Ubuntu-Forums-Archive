### Post by OldGamer on 2008-07-12
Since I first heard of linux many years ago, I have been interested in learning the 'new' operating system.  I was raised with DOS and never wanted to make the switch to windows, but had to because of obvious reasons.  I know, same company, but I hated leaving my command line and was never a big fan of a GUI and there 'start' button.  But at the time it's what I had to do.
Anyway, I bought Corel's version of Linux (10 years ago???) but didn't get far.  Then went to Red Hat, but I could never get my wireless to work (probably because I bought cheap).  I still used it, only with wired internet.  I eventually moved to Fedora when it came out, but could never find out how to get the wireless to work there either.  Also, I could never get video(mpg) to work.  I dual booted all that time, or set up another machine just for linux.
Last October, I was talking to a friend who mentioned that he dual booted ubuntu.  Since I was still extremely interested in running Linux instead of XP, I decided to look it up.  I actually downloaded many different distros with live CD's, but ultimately decided on ubuntu because it was the only one that actually recognized my wireless card.  (It still didn't work, but at least it saw it...)  
So along with the wireless issue and many video issues and some other problems, I went ahead and installed ubuntu on an old Dell laptop.  From coming to this site, I got everything working perfectly.  I never even had to post a question. I have found this forum to help me with every problem I have had in my time using ubuntu without ever having to ask a question.   I now run Ubuntu on my desktop, and two laptops (one older running openbox, which I found from this site also).  
I guess I just want to say Thank you to everyone who has had a problem and everyone else who has told them how to fix that problem.  Ubuntu is not only a wonderful operating system, but also a wonderful community.  I hope I can someday help someone as every one on here has helped me.  With the help of the people on this site, it has been a fun, pleasurable experience setting up linux(ubuntu) on my computers, and I now have the confidence to be able to get any distro I want to working.  But I will be staying with ubuntu becuase it has been the best I have found, with the best commununity.  Thank you all for being patient (sp?) and helpful.  Keep up the good work!

OldGamer
