### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-08-29
Allow me to explain the problem:

Linux distributions thrive, and depend, on the community that forms around it. That is the reason for the Ubuntu forums' existence - to accomodate users. Windows does not have such a community. If people have problems with Windows (and believe me, they do), there is nowhere 'official' for them to turn. Microsoft offers support, but it is generic help only - and people tend to get better results if they talk to a real person (hence, support forums like we have here). Due to this, **many more people** using Ubuntu, who happen to have problems, will show up at the forums and ask for help. Fewer people do so with systems like Windows. This does not mean that Ubuntu has more issues, it just means that Ubuntu offers an obvious way for Ubuntu users to express their complaints.

You have problems with Ubuntu - but you are not the only one, and while the problems you experience seem silly problems which shouldn't be present anyway, the reality is that for **most users**, these problems never happen. The developers can't test every single combination of hardware and software, and they don't try to. It works on a call and response basis - you file a bug report, your problem gets fixed (hopefully anyway). Windows has just as many stupid issues - maybe even more, but because there's nowhere obvious for people to complain about such issues (and even those areas that ARE available, most people aren't aware of them - you know about the Ubuntu forums because you had to look for Ubuntu on the internet before you installed it) - very few people complain about such problems.

The short and shrift of it is that if you ask for help in a civil manner - most times you will receive it. If you just whine that you have problems, then that doesn't give us any information, and many of the volunteers here will not want to try and help you anyway.

I will repeat - just because lots of people complain about problems, they are still a minority, compared to the millions of people who have no problems and hence don't have the need to complain.

In any case - the developers aren't obligated to read these forums. The only way you can be sure a developer will read your complaint is to file a bug report for whichever program is giving you trouble. The devs will generally send the bug report upstream, if it is an Ubuntu specific problem, or will advise you to do so.

These forums are for user-based help - if you don't know how to do something, don't know what to install, etc etc etc. There is little we can do about problems inherent to the software, such as bugs, aside from advise you of alternative software, or point you in the direction of the project's bug-tracking system.
