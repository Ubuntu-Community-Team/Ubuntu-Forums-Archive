### Post by timzak on 2008-08-19
It's been nearly 4 months since I switched my email to Evolution.  I wanted to give it a fair shot.  I didn't need the MS Exchange support, but I do need a calendar and event reminder.  I loved the Gnome integration, and as I used it, I found it to be a pretty nice PIM.  My favorite feature was the birthday calendar.  Basically, for every contact on your list, if you put their date of birth in the alloted place in the personal info tab, Evolution places not only their birthday on the calendar, but tells you how old they will be.  You don't have to search through 12 months to find someone's birthday - just go into their contact sheet and there it is.  Very cool.

Unfortunately, it was all downhill from there.  First, the debacle with the gnome keyring manager suddenly asking for a password everytime Evolution was launched.  That was a feature that was added via Update Manager with no prior warning.  Next, calendar reminders would not pop up on my screen.  Instead, a subtle icon would pop into the notification area, which would then pop up a reminder IF you noticed and clicked it.  Evolution developers basically told me "it works on distro X, so it's an Ubuntu problem, go to them".  Ubuntu developers (from the mailing list) gave me no help.  A post in Ubuntu forums produced no help.  So I found the next best solution:  I changed the launcher to open up the calendar instead of the email, so I could see my upcoming events.  Not the best workaround, but better than it was.  Finally, the last straw:  I recently noticed that if I leave Evolution running throughout the day, it will ALWAYS lock up, causing me to force quit and restart.  If I leave it closed and rely on mail-notify, then all is well.  I asked for help and the only response I got was "sorry I can't help, but I'm having the same problem."

I REALLY wanted Evolution to work.  I loved the gnome integration, the GTK-ness which made it look consistent with my desktop, the calendar integration into the panel clock, etc, etc.  Awesome PIM!  Except for a few deal-breaking issues that no one cared enough about to help me or anyone else with.  Granted, there were a few other issues that Evolution developers DID help me with, and I am grateful for that.  But wouldn't you want to fix any problem related to your app, even if it might be specific to one distro?  Arguably the most popular distro?

With some sadness, I reinstalled Thunderbird, then the Lightning calendar extension, and remembered that Evolution will not export your emails, addresses or calendar to Thunderbird.  Nice.  (Apparently it is possible, though only through some hacks that were too confusing to follow) So I manually recreated my address book, emailed myself a few key emails I didn't want to lose, manually input my future calendar events, and have had a much better experience with Thunderbird.  The Lightning calendar pops up reminders like clockwork, and will even keep reminding you AFTER the event is over - something Evolution wouldn't do.  Once your event is past, Evolution will never try to remind you again (not that it ever did a good job of reminding, regardless of whether it was Ubuntu's problem or Evolution's).  Lightning will keep reminding you until you specifically tell it to stop reminding you.

So the look of Thunderbird isn't consistent with the rest of my desktop.  I will always take function over form.  I think I will keep Evolution installed so I can refer back to the old emails I have in there if I ever need to.  I will also keep things like holidays and birthdays on the Evolution calendar so I can see them in the pop-up calendar that's integrated into the panel clock.  So in a sense, I will keep using Evolution for a couple of key features that it does well, and use Thunderbird + Lightning for everything else.

Hopefully Evolution developers will care enough to fix these problems in future releases and not just throw the ball into Ubuntu's court.
