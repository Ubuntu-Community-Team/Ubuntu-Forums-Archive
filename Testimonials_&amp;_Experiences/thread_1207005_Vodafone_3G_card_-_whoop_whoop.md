### Post by fatski on 2009-07-07
Okay, just got a Vodafone 3G card from my work. Wanted it to work with my own laptop so I could get some online chat out in my lonely hotel room.

Windows: Download 80 meg driver onto work laptop, wish I'd brought ethernet cable so I could transfer driver to my laptop. Get home, transfer drivers, install slooooooowly, reboot, piss about with settings finally connect.

Ubuntu: Plug in 3G card. Read helpful popup notification. Click networkmanager icon, connected in less than 30 seconds from plugging in card.

I love the way ubuntu surprises me like this 9 times out of 10. If only the 1 time it doesn't wasn't so painful to fix it would be absolutely ideal.
