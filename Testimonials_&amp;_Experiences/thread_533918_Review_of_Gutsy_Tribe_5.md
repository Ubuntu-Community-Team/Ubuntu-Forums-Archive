### Post by wolfen69 on 2007-08-24
i am reposting this here for those that don't frequent the community cafe.

all i can say is WOW! (stands up and applauds developers)

ok, first off i was expecting maybe a few under the hood changes and not much more, but was i ever wrong.

upon booting to desktop for first time, my native resolution was being used. (1440 x 900, which never happened before without tweaking) next, i was told there was a video driver available. click, done. it also gave the option to install video drivers for the new nvidia cards. sweet. i went to places>computer and lo and behold, all my drives (4) were mounted and had read/write capability out of the box. very nice.(no more ntfs-config BS) next, i went to software sources. all repos were checked off. went to add/remove apps chose all available apps from the pull down menu, searched ubuntu restricted extras. 1 click and had all codecs, flash, and java. (stands up and applauds again) add/remove apps seemed to have alot more packages available too.

automatix is now officially dead.

the screen and graphics preferences was a nice touch. after video driver install, the default desktop effects are nice without being overkill. you also have the choice of no effects or enhanced.
the windows have a more 3D look to them with the shadowing effect. (a kind of aero-ish look) the shuffle effect when switching windows was kind of cool also.

i am sure that there are other goodies i havnt discovered , or havnt been implimented yet. and can't wait to see.

i have a 100% running ubuntu install without having to go to the command line once. this should make newbs even happier and draw greater interest from non-linux/ubuntu users.

great job development team! keep up the good work.

I would agree.

So I restarted, removed the CD and was greeted with a Grub promt. For those who do not know what this is this means unless you know grub commands, where your image is and booting options you will remain on a black screen with the work grub and a blinking prompt reminding you with every blink that you cannot access any part of you system.

After this I selected desktop effects. This is where it's gone silly. Now everything still works, but the border are all gone. No buttons to close, expand, or send to task bar. No Cube, no wobbly windows nothing. You have to click file>exit to close anything. But first you have to move the menu bar from the top to the bottom because the file menu is now under the bar.
 I am really excited about Gutsy and will continue to play with it. I just want some of the newer people to understand that this CAN break your system and it IS still beta.
 If you do decide to mess with it and have problems please be sure to file bug reports so the devs no what needs to be addressed. (this is the reason for releasing Betas!
