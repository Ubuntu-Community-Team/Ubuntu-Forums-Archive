### Post by hizaguchi on 2006-04-30
Ubuntu was the first Linux distro to draw me away from Windows.  Not because I liked Windows, but not because I hated Windows either.  Really, I've never had any problems with Microsoft products.  But I had enjoyed a very positive experience with a Debian system set up by an IT administrator (and Linux zealot) at my school and when I came across and easy to install Debian for my home computer I couldn't resist giving it a try.  It turned out be be better than I expected, and before long I got rid of my Window's partition completely to have space to try other distros.  Some were more newbie friendly (Suse) and some were leaner and faster (Arch) but I just have some kind of draw toward Ubuntu.  The community is great, the auto detection handles all of my hardware, and I've got a great platform to explore more open source software and the inner workings of my computer with the knowledge that I can put it all back together easily if I break something.  It's great.
