### Post by ticklu2deth on 2005-07-08
Well, finally I've found a distro I can love. I have vacillated between Ubuntu and Mepis for the last year or so, but the latest release really works well for me. I'm not a total noob, but still enough so that I like an easy install (yes, I'm a bit lazy). By install, I mean the whole process, not just the presence (or lack thereof) of an install GUI. Really, a GUI is somewhat irrelevant so long as the process is guided. After all Windoze, at least not at the beginning, is not entirely graphic either; I say this because I have heard some say that the Ubuntu/Kubuntu install lacked a GUI. Everything was detected and setup flawlessly including my wireless network which surprised me a bit.

I really like Kubuntu as I prefer KDE to Gnome (don't hate me...it's just personal preference...Gnome is a very nice interface as well). While I know KDE could be installed from the Ubuntu system, it's nice to have it integrated and optimized to the distribution (it's the laziness coming out).

Well, I'm sure this is nothing most of you don't already know, but just wanted to introduce myself. I'm not a total noob (know enough to be dangerous), but I will try to help out on the forum if I can.

Peace, Jeff

### Post by crashtest on 2005-07-08
Hi, and welcome!  I was a distro-hopper until approx 4 weeks ago, when I first installed Ubuntu.  Four weeks is a LONG time for me to stick with any distro.  I've been known to try out two or three on a weekend.  I seem to get restless almost immediately, and want to try something else.

Last weekend I actually removed Ubuntu and installed Mandriva 2005 LE - not for any particular reason.  I just wanted to see what it was like.  Two hours later I had Ubuntu installed again, so it looks like I'll be sticking with Ubuntu for a while.

The other odd thing for me is I've come to love Gnome.  I have always been a KDE fan, but after using Ubuntu for a couple of days I became hooked on the clean and simple look of Gnome.  You might want to try it out just to see something different.  Who knows?  You might end up liking it as well.  Either way, it's nice to see another happy user.
