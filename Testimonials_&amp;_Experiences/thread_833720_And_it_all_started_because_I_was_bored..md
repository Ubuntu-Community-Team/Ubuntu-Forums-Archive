### Post by LMD1990 on 2008-06-18
So, my girlfriend has this friend at college- smart guy, big into computers, even bigger into Linux. A couple of months a go I visited him, and was impressed as I saw him running Ubuntu on his PC- in particular I was left drooling over his swanky Compiz Fusion cube, and I decided then and there "I wanna do that." He said "Oh, you can- because in Linux, you can do anything."

As a born and bred Microsoft Windows guy, using Windows XP Home Edition as his primary OS, I was going into this blind, but nonetheless I installed Ubuntu when I got home- and I was instantly annoyed that my wireless USB adapter didn't work, and therefore decided that, coming up to my exams, I had not the time nor the patience to waste on figuring this problem out. I decided that, if I couldn't use internet, then it wasn't worth using. I found myself thoroughly annoyed that, even though I was fluent in DOS prompt at the age of five, using the Terminal not only started off as an experience which reduced me to wanting to deliver a stiff uppercut to Tux the Penguin in a fit of rage, but it was also rather intimidating, and I began praying for an all-purpose GUI that came with built-in telepathy, so it could read my mind and install everything for me. However, once my exams were over, I had two weeks of study leave free- and I got bored. Not one to be defeated by obstacles (or lack of telepathy), I decided I'd have another go at Ubuntu.

I chose Xubuntu as my version- I decided that I prefered the lightweight XFCE desktop over GNOME and especially over Kubuntu :p And once I'd installed it this time, I decided I wasn't going to give up. After three painstaking weeks of all attempts to make my piece-of-crap BT Voyager 1055 USB Wireless Adapter work with Linux, I decided that it was time to take drastic measures, and I proceeded to unplug my physical box, drag it downstairs, and plug it into the LAN.

After some more epic fail and some even more epic swearing, I finally found the God-send that was a combination of Ubuntu help documents and ndiswrapper, and FINALLY, I got my internet working. It's been all uphill from there really, because despite how much I've wanted to nail Tux the Penguin to a giant wooden Ubuntu logo and burn him alive for Ubuntu not being exactly useable out-of-the-box for me, I decided "screw that, because unlike Windows- once you've got it working, it *WORKS*", and it works wonderfully. Once I'd put in my research and did my reading, I found that I could handle it, and that it is actually an awesome OS.

All the effort I've put into installing Xubuntu has been most worth it- I've come across a wonderful, simple and free operating system with a huge library of free software (and for someone who's kinda struggling for money, that is such a blessing), and although initially I may have been peeved that I couldn't run Microsoft Word, Windows Media Player or Windows Live Messenger in Xubuntu, I decided that I simply did not need to- because "in Linux, you can do anything".

You really can, if you're determined to put in the work. I don't need Microsoft Office when I have the equally competent and not-as-full-of-useless-crap OpenOffice. I may like Windows Media Player, but I can just as easily use the wonderfully lightweight, no-nonsense Rhythmbox. Using Windows Live Messenger feels like a sordid and shameful one night stand with a rather cruel, fat, loud and obnoxious prostitute with heavy make-up and a penchant for bondage- sure, it works, until you find yourself at it's overwhelming mercy, and you really wished that you'd not even bothered as you are left feeling dirty and violated.

While for convenience sake I am not switching 100% over to Xubuntu (since I feel that in terms of multimedia- and also gaming when I get a new graphics card- that XP handles it ultimately better), Xubuntu definitely has earned it's place on my boot up menu. When I'm feeling tired of Windows XP and it's dripping gooey interface and random error messages, I log into Xubuntu, and find it quite relaxing to be in a desktop environment that is lightweight, simplistic and not choc full of rubbish. In particular, doing word-processing while chilling out to music and talking to friends on Pidgin is wonderful in Xubuntu.

And yet, at the end of it all- I can't actually use Compiz Fusion. :p
