### Post by Puptentacle on 2006-01-24
Back in the middle of December, after a couple of years of trying various live CD distros, settled on Ubuntu. After installing it I absolutely loved it. Everything about it is quite well thought out, the repostitory method of doing installs is wonderful and I was quite happy and going on my way learning all I can about Linux in general and Ubuntu in particular. 

Well, this past weekend, while walking through Barnes and Noble, I spyed on the magazine rack a nice little package with a full five cd distro of OpenSuse 10 and a magazine of pointers and tutorials. It was definitely a learning experience, and I'm not saying I won't install Suse on another machine to play with at some point but as far as a "daily use" distro, Ubuntu seems to win HANDS DOWN!

I'll never stray again. Please for give me! [-o<

### Post by Lord Illidan on 2006-01-25
My experience with SUSE is similar. I downloaded the OSS DVD..

However, the installation was quite good, until it came to the packages. Too many packages in one subsection and very complicated. No metapackages to speed things up.

In the end, I installed it. Booting took a real long time, and it does look too windows like.

The interface is however, excellent. Superb. Real well polished, something which I have to strive to achieve in Kubuntu. Speed of KDE was also good. However, everything began falling down.

1. Lack of support. SUSE has a forum dedicated to it, but it is nowhere as good as this one. And many topics go unresponded.

2. Crippled multimedia. I can understand mp3 codecs not being included but, do you have to cripple Amarok, Xine, etc too? I had to reinstall all my multimedia players, what a waste of time.

3. YAST. YAST is damn slow, and imho, offers no real improvement over kcontrol. It takes roughly 1-3 minutes for something to start.

4. Dependency hell. It seems that many packages have conflicts, and while the availability of rpms online is greater than that of .debs, I had trouble installing them, with obscure dependencies. Even KDE 3.5 wouldn't install, and then, I just quit... fed up of the whole thing, and re-installed Kubuntu.
