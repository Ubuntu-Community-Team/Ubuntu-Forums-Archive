### Post by benign on 2008-07-08
I tried out Redhat/Mandrake about 10 years ago, fooled around with it a little, and found it to be incompatible with everything... ever. I suppose it didn't help that lightning fast internet connections were not exactly the norm either, a situation that perhaps hindered the open-source community and the development/distribution of the great linux software that is available today. It goes without saying that I took a very long break from the Linux world.

Fast forward to around 8 months ago I had to recover some data from a corrupted hard drive, and I stumbled across a marvelous little 80mb OS called Puppy Linux that did everything it said it would, and more - much more than the byzantine and precarious WindowsPE/BartPE. I fooled around with it for fun, customizing it to my system through mostly command prompts and editing config files - much like the old days. The OS, being so sparse, soon lost my interest. I was also on a very slow dial-up connection in a third-world country, so I was limited to mostly software that I already had... which happened to be XP.

2 weeks ago, tiring of the windows experience, and the literal vomit that is Vista, I decided that linux would be a proper replacement. I had heard much of Ubuntu and Compiz in my quest of troubleshooting and commands while utilizing puppy, so I decided I'd give it a try. I've been happily using Ubuntu Hardy 64 bit, and it is a wonderful OS. How ironic was it that the only caveat was the inability of the system to play the linux+ and CCNA video courses that I found myself with? Faced with the torment of having to boot up into Vista again, I scoured the internet for a remedy - one that I did not find (perhaps do to DRM); however, I did find a glorious workaround with VirtualBox that has stopped the inception of a potential nightmare of instability and incompatibility.

To sum it up, I haven't had this much fun on a computer since DOS, BBSes, and multi-tasking in Desqview.
