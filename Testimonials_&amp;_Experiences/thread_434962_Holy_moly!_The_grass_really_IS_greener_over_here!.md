### Post by Nobel on 2007-05-06
Hello Fellow Ubuntu users. 

I've been having a very good Ubuntu experience so far, and i had a need to tell someone my story. To share my experience and to show my support.

I started using Ubuntu because i always had an interest for computers. I don't code programs or anything, but i have a huge interest in my software, and knowing how to use it efficiently.

When i started the next step of my education, studying Communication/IT - English, a classmate of mine was talking about Linux, and how he was hoping to check it out. We decided to hang out one day after class, and began searching for a system that would offer us an easy beginning. My friend has some programming skills (mostly web-related stuff), but was in need of a friendly enviroment to learn as much as me.

Ubuntu jumped into our lap, radiating useabilty, stability, and most importantly of all: Potential.

We downloaded Dapper Drake, burned it to a CD, and booted. Everything went smooth and about a good hour later we were using our very first Linux platform.

Since then, i even installed Kubuntu on my mothers laptop. Her old computer died, and she was in need of a new system, and i suggested Linux. She was skeptical at first, but now she's very happy about it. She is happy with KDE, since it resembles her old system (Windows XP) in terms of graphical design. There is the odd complaint every now and then (minor things like the placement of original text when answering an e-mail in thunderbird), but nothing you would not expect in Windows as well.

I am proud to be a Linux, and especially Ubuntu user, and i'm happy to sail along with all of you on the proud ship "Open-Source" into the future.

Ubuntu has also awoken a desire to help and contribute. I want to learn to programm, and i want to help to make this OS even better then it is.

So, that's my testimonial. A bit corny, i agree. But it's how i fell :KS 

Now, i would appriciate any help as too where i can start contributing. I'm learning web coding at school, but might you suggest a "real" language to learn, so i can start contributing in a few years? :)

### Post by spin2cool on 2007-05-06
There are many ways that you can contribute to Ubuntu at the present, even with no coding knowledge.

1) Be a Ubuntu user.  Use it all the time, and report bugs when you find them.  

2) Spread the word - get your friends and family to make the switch

3) Use your knowledge to help others here on the forums 

4) Triage bugs for the Ubuntu developers.  They get hundreds of bug reports a day, many of which are repeats or are not filed in the appropriate places.
