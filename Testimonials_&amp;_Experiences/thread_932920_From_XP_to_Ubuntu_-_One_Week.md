### Post by nbakewell on 2008-09-28
I've been an XP user for most of my computer life, and although I've dabbled with Redhat in the computer labs I never wanted to make the switch on my laptop.  Now let me say first off that I am not a Windows hater - I think XP is a good OS.  Not great, but good.  But I have been getting very frustrated with the classic Windows antics - the no-reason slowdowns, the random restarts and freezes, and the clutter that just starts building up.  That, combined with my current CS college course which is all about networking in Linux, has prompted me to make the switch.

So a couple of days ago I reformatted my laptop and installed Ubuntu 8.04 LTS on it - not my first time playing with Ubuntu, as I had briefly installed it back home on my desktop's second HD, but wasn't really serious about it.

The installation almost went well, I came across that 94% hangup error - not sure why, but as soon as I switched to ext2 format it worked.  Got logged in and was pleased to see that all of the software that came with Ubuntu (like Firefox) were pretty much their latest versions.

Also, I was very happy to have my internet immediately working after plugging in my ethernet cord, no fiddling needed.  Same with wireless - more than I can say about XP.

Then I came across a concern - how is my printer going to work, when I had to install the CD that came with it on my XP machine, and as far as I knew the CD did not come with a Linux equivalent.  Well I decided to just plug it in and turn it on and see what happens - low and behold, Ubuntu immediately recognized it, installed the appropriate HP driver in a second or two, and I was good to go.  Sweet!

Now I know I will come across my fair share of problems as I delve deeper in Linux, but I must say, I am very impressed so far.  The only thing that is making me keep my XP partition is Adobe Photoshop - I just don't really like GIMP.  Maybe that will change with time as I learn it better.
