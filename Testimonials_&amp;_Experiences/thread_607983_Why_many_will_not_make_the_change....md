### Post by Hagar55 on 2007-11-09
Don't get me wrong I like Ubuntu...
But the fact that I still need to use the command line and help from the net to get things like extra buttons on the mouse to work is a pain.
I tend to look on the net and try these things but most people will pack up and leave.

When I changed a file (usplash.conf) for a possible solutions to slow bootup it completely crashed. And despite going into recovery mode and changing the file back with MC it still was broken beyond repair.

Even then reinstalling from the live CD on the same partitions proved to be complicated and no real help files exist for these kinds of problems.

I use windows combined with Ubuntu and the fact is that it is very hard to break XP beyond repair. and when you do break it the CD offers you possibilities to repair and undo your damage.

Ubuntu has no "return to last known configuration" and that is a major shortcoming.

Also the fact that there are virtually no help files that help know users is a big shortcoming.

So I'll stay and try Ubuntu even more in dual boot with my Xp, but new users wich aren't techminded and ready to actively search solutions on the net and are determined enough to start from scratch when things break will not stay with Ubuntu

### Post by ticopelp on 2007-11-09
Messing with your usplash as a beginning user is hardly something you can point at as a fault of the OS. I'm just saying. You can break Windows just as easily by going into msconfig and disabling stuff because you want to "fix a slow boot." 

Personally, I've found an active community of forum members to be far, far more helpful than any help files. I also know of almost no one who actually reads help files. There are a lot of very easy to use resources out there like the Ubuntu Wiki and the Ubuntuguide -- frankly I often think they should be included bundled with the distro itself and the UbuntuWiki made Firefox's home page. 

I do agree that a simpler, GUI-based solution to hardware configuration would be nice, though, especially where mice are concerned. I'd love to see a development like that.
