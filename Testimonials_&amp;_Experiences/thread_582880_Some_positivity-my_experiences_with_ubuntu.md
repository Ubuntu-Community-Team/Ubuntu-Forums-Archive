### Post by Exonimus on 2007-10-20
After reading quite a lot of negative posts,(I feel sorry for you guys btw, hope the problems get fixed real soon)
 I decided I'd post my current experiences with ubuntu, gutsy in particular. I've been running ubuntu feisty for a few weeks on my 2nd deskto, on which I always do all my school work. Basically, everything went flawlessly. Only thing that was bugging me was that my screen didn't manage to run at 1280x1024 resolution somehow.. I really like how quick ubuntu is. I hear people say it's slow, takes 5 minutes to boot up, etc. But on my p4 2ghz (not the best of the best anymore...) it easily boots up in about 1 minute. all the apps I'd ever need work perfectly, I even got office 2000 to work without a sweat(I need powerpoint for school). I recently upgraded to 7.10, and what did I spot there? the screens and graphics option. Now, changing the resolution to the desired 1280x1024 was done with a click. and it worked! :D I can also run quite some games without a problem. There's one kind of funny thing though. There's 512MB of RAM in my pc. I've recently purchased another motherboard because the old one was broken. Today, I found out it actually only recognizes 256mb of RAM (that's a problem with the motherboard, not ubuntu) so it's been running ever so smooth on only half the memory! and I never even noticed. thumbs up though, fix those bugs, but keep up the good work!
