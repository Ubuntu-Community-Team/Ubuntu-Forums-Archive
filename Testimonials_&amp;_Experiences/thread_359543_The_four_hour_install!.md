### Post by ctt1wbw on 2007-02-12
I am currently going to ITT Tech in Norfolk and have been preaching the GNU/Linux and opensource movement.  I have told numerous people about OpenOffice since they couldn't afford MS Office and they were shocked to hear about something like that.

Anyway, my next quarter I am taking some Linux classes and pretty much have a woody about it all.  I have had numerous people ask me about Linux since they all know I run Ubuntu.  The school teaches on Fedora Core 6, so I have helped out some people getting that up and running.

Come Saturday, I helped someone try to get it on their laptop.  His hard drive was a mess so I spent almost 45 minutes defragging it before making new partitions, just to be on the safe side.  He already had Partition Magic, so I created a root and a swap partition for him.  All this took over an hour and a half.  So then I started the install process, and halfway through, the school kicked us out to lock up.  So we put the laptop in the back of my Durango to try to finish.

About 10 minutes later his battery started to die on us, so we hiked over to another building and asked if we could use one of their outlets to finish up.  They were more than happy to do so, they even gave us a private office!  By then, his laptop had died, and I had to start over.  So his battery is charging, I'm installing, and it's getting close to 4 pm.  Now we all know what happens at 4 pm on a Saturday, don't we?  Hmmmm....  :)

So we hike back to my truck to finish off.  His battery indicator starting flashing orange, which is NOT a sign I needed right then.  But lo and behold, we finished and rebooted and came to the login panel.  And right then, his laptop died for the second time that day!  What an install!  That was about 4 hours worth!

And at about that time, I was late getting to Portsmouth Naval Hospital for an MRI.

But there's another wayward sheep back to the GNU Flock!
