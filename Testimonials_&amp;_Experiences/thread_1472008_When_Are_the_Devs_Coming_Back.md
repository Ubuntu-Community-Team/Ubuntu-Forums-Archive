### Post by uRock on 2010-05-04
I have had a total of 2 little software updates and there seems to be a lot of work to do to get people's systems fully functional. I for one plan to turn both of my Lucid systems back into Karmic systems. 

There have been multiple threads knocking the look of Lucid, but I have no problem with the new layout and branding. I do however have a problem with not being able to run Compiz because the driver doesn't allow it. I never had problems with Ubuntu and nVidia before this.

1 out of 3 start ups on either machines invokes a disk scan which mostly always locks up between 70 and 80 percent. 

My netbook has to stay within 50 feet of my wireless router or it will drop its connection. With Karmic or Windows running on it I could go out by the pool at night and enjoy the fresh air while getting stuff done and still have a strong connection. I have tried backports in Lucid and though the worked 3 or 4 kernel releases ago, they don't help now.

As I stated in Kiwi's thread earlier, I have tried every DE of ubuntu's LiveCD to test on my daughter's system and the mouse and wireless card do not work with Lucid, yet they worked effortlessly with Karmic.

If I were new to this OS I would have left by now. I love ubuntu and have even tried to become a team member, but I can not help people if I can't run the OS on my own systems without problems.

I know the devs have worked hard and I am grateful for that, but I really hope they get knee deep in fixing bugs soon.
