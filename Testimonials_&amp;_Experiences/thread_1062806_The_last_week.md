### Post by stalkingwolf on 2009-02-07
In the last week I have sold 4 computers, 3 laptops and 1 desktop (for a 6yrold) and am building a 5th.  The laptops were a Toshiba Tecra 8200,
an Hp Compaq 8000, and an EEEPC 701 4G. The desktop is just a mid to low end
that I built for my Landlords daughter.
The 5th is also a desktop for someone that does work for the landlord, a
beginners box.

The thing that all of them have in common is Ubuntu.  All are running a version of 8.04.  Be it with the edubuntu packages or the EEE version.

I also upgraded the landlords business computer from 7.04 to 8.04.

The point of all this is actually a neat story.  I took the tower over
to the house. when we got it all set up we discovered that the only keyboards they had available were verstunken. Dead keys. One of them, one that was needed for the password.  So they went to Officemax looking for a keyboard.  Alijah ( the daughter) had a great time telling all who would listen " I have a computer."  " we are looking for a keyboard for MY
computer".  Actually she has 2.  She also has one in her Dads office, also
running Edubuntu.

She and a friend of ours daughter ( both home schooled) will at least
reach their teen years knowing Linux with little exposure to windows.

The next generation.  Both are in homes that are if not completely Ubuntu
95% Ubuntu.  And Im working on another household.

The HP is going to a customer who's desktop I upgraded a few months ago.
When I did the upgrade I also set it up as a dual boot.  In the last few
weeks they have been having serious virus issue with the windows side.
She has been using the Ubuntu side more and more and has noticed the lack of virus problems.  I think they are about ready to make the switch completely.

The last one I think is going to be a learning experience for me as well.
It is for a Mexican family. They only speak limited english , I speak
limited spanish ( i can order beer and food and of course cuss), The landlord speaks pretty good spanish but not Ubuntu.  Ought to be interesting.
