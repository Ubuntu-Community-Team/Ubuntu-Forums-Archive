### Post by bprins on 2010-08-31
Hi,

90% of the world still doesn't know that Windows is **not** the best a man can get. 

I (re)joined Ubuntu not too long ago (I am back on Ubuntu since Karmic). I know most of the people still think that Ubuntu is just a fun OS for some nerds that can't stand Microsoft products (which is also true, fair enough :)). 

But GOD if they only knew how much Ubuntu improved, I've seen a bit of Debian, and a very little bit of Ubuntu (4 if I recall correctly). 

I'm getting more impressed every day, of the convenience, flexibility, stability, and speed improvements. I've had my share of problems getting stuff running on TV via HDMI, but that stuff works out of the box in the yet to be released Ubuntu Maverick 10.10. And today I had another joy-full moment adding a printer.

I've installed a HP all in one printer a while back on the misses laptop, and to be honest, I didn't take the effort of installing it on my laptop, because I didn't expect it to work (without a lot of effort at least). Funny side note: installing the printer on Win7 took me 45 minutes, god knows why it had to take that long, but it did. Funny meaningless progress bars gave me the impression that it was still doing something.

So, today, I had to print something and I thought; let's "ask" google if he knows anything about the HP all in one, and ubuntu. 

Google knows. Google said: should work out of the box with some HP package. 

I was like "sure". I'll have a look if I can find the package => already installed. "Hmm, interesting". Let's give this a shot. 

I plugged the all in one in my USB slot, and guess what: **in 10 seconds, the HP all in one was recognized, installed, and ready to use. Now I say, right back at ya Microsoft.**

Yours truly,

A convinced and proud Ubuntu user!
