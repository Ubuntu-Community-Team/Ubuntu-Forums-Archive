### Post by Jay Car on 2012-04-07
Wow! :)

My new Wacom Bamboo Create Pen & touch (CTH670) arrived on Thursday, a gift from my sister.  I am grateful to have such a gift (and for such a nice sister!), but when she first talked about it I was unsure of my ability to use it with Ubuntu, especially since I've been using Precise, which is still in beta (though Precise is absolutely beautiful already!).  

I read everything I could find about Wacoms with Ubuntu, subscribed to threads that contained how-to instructions (they all seemed terribly complex to me), kept notes and tried to keep a positive attitude.  But honestly, it worried me a lot. I was sure it would be beyond my ability to accomplish. I was dreading it. 

So when the tablet arrived I just set it aside for a day (and then two days), stressing over facing a task that seemed beyond my skills.  My sister will be visiting this week...I wanted to be able to show her the software I would be using with it...if only I could get it to work.

This morning I decided to give it a try.  Time's running out, my sister will be here soon.  I had to at least make sure the tablet was in working condition, so it could be returned if defective.  

Oh, the pressure...

Here's what I did:

I added this ppa to my software sources, choosing 12.04 as the Ubuntu version:  [https://launchpad.net/~lekensteyn/+archive/wacom-tablet](https://launchpad.net/~lekensteyn/+archive/wacom-tablet)

Opened the software center, found the wacom listing and installed it.

Opened MyPaint and used the Wacom to draw a few curly-Qs with different brushes and colors...it worked!!!

Opened inkscape and curly-qued some more...it worked too!

Opened Gimp and got a little braver, made some squigglies and stick people this time.  :)  It worked!

I still need to figure out some settings and learn more about it all.  But it's working, and it was easy.

It only took two and a half days to worry about it, and about ten minutes to actually get it working. :lolflag:

Also, posts by Favux, tokenrove, and others about getting Wacom products to work with Ubuntu were incredibly helpful!  I'm adding them to my list of UF heros, along with the awesome folks on the Ubuntu+ forums.

What an amazing place this is!  UF is pretty damned awesome.
