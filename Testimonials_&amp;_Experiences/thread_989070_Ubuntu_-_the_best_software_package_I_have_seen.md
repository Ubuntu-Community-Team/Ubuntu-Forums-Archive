### Post by Idefix82 on 2008-11-21
I have been working with Ubuntu for two months now but I now feel like sharing my experience.
Two months ago I got a new Dell Vostro 1710 with Vista pre-installed. The first thing I did was to wipe Vista (unfortunately, at that time I didn't realise that I could get the money back if I hadn't accepted the license) and installed Ubuntu. This was my first real contact with Linux.

The first couple of weeks were kind of bumpy. Ethernet sometimes worked, sometimes didn't, wireless didn't work at all. But once I had found my way round the Ubuntu - Google - Launchpad world I managed to fix both problems without much hassle.

Despite the initial problems, I was extremely impressed at the ease of use. Synaptic and the package system were a revelation! Compiz made me feel like I was already in the next century, compared with Win XP. But at that time I hadn't quite appreciated yet, what a precious thing I had there on my computer.

So now, two months later, my work flow has completely transformed:
[LIST]
[*]I can connect to the server which hosts my website and see my webspace in GEdit as if it was a folder in the file system. I can seemingly edit files directly on the server.
[*]I will never have trouble with too many windows on my Desktop. Different workspaces and tabbing give my a feeling as if I was walking around my house rather than confined to a prison cell.
[*]My wife is still trying to get used to the fact that she can install anything without the fear of viruses and that a notification in the notification area does not come from a Microsoft spy.
[*]If I program, then with the external tools plugin in GEdit it's one key click to run a test or to invoke a different program in a terminal which will load my file or to backup the entire current working folder or...
[*]To manage files with the terminal is not a tedious clicking and dragging any more, but hardly noticeable in terms of time and effort. My wife is still amazed, how quickly I move, rename and copy files. And I am still amazed how I can send an email with several attachments with one line in the terminal, instead of opening firefox, loging into my account, browsing to find the right attachment and so on. Or how I resize 20 pictures to get thumbnails for my website with one line in the terminal.
[/LIST]
The list goes on and on. I log into my account and simply feel good. Everything works, I am extremely efficient with routine tasks, the desktop looks pretty (with cairo-dock, screenlets, compiz, completely customised theme,...) and I am free to do anything I want on this system. The fact that it's all free is not even such an important factor for me. I would happily pay money for such a system and probably will in the shape of a donation.

And of course, Ubuntu even takes care of what to do with the extra time that one suddenly gains: roam on these forums and help others become as excited as I am.

Thank you to the developers and to the community for all of this!
