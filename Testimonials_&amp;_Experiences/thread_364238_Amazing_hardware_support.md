### Post by bmk789 on 2007-02-18
I began my linux adventures August of 2005, dual booting Ubuntu with XP.  I transitioned to ubuntu for my main needs and tried gentoo after a while.  After not being able to get a GUI running with Gentoo, I switched back to ubuntu again and tried some Xgl/Compiz action.  After tweaking the crap out of that install for several months, I wiped with an install of Gentoo via Kororaa.  I got everything working and learned a lot about the gentoo system and linux.  I used it for a few months with Beryl/AiGLX.  After updating my kernel to 2.6.20 one day, I could not get my wireless driver to install.  So after being tired of the DIYness of gentoo and some things not working I decided to come back to Ubuntu, and now I'm wishing I had switched sooner.  Im amazed by how all my hardware just works.  USB scanner, card reader, and all internal hardware.  Everything is mounted when connected and everything just works.  It's all held together with a polished interface.  Ubuntu has been so easy and convenient.  I highly recommend it to anyone and everyone!
