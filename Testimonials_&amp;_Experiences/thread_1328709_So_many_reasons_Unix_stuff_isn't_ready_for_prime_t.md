### Post by SteveHillier on 2009-11-27
> **urdrwho said:**
> The fact remains, it is an MS world and unless someone is ready to spend a lot of time, getting a Ubuntu up and running in an office is a headache.

The headache for me is not that Linux would be difficult to set up or maintain in a business situation.

Most businesses I support only run programs that have no special needs.  They want internet access, they want email, they want an office suite (and that is usually only WP and SS, very few use graphics or DTP or sound), they need printing.  All of these have better offerings in Open Source.

One problem in the UK is the reliance of businesses on the Sage Accounts & Payroll suites and these do not have Linux versions.  There are however other products about that can be used (eg online accounts systems).

The problem is not the technology but the unwillingness of businesses owners to take the risk of going away from Windows and the associated retraining costs.

Talk to any business running Apple Macs and they abhor Windows with a vengeance but they are happy with their systems and probably would not move to Linux anyway.

I still promote Linux product to customers where I can but it tends to be in back office functions rather then front office uses.
