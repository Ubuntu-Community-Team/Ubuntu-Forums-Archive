### Post by Down8ve on 2009-11-13
I've got to share my experience, not only with Kubuntu, but with many distros in general.

Yes, openSuse 11.2 is good, but I ran into dependency errors the first time I tried installing multimedia codecs from Packman.  Also there was no Kdenlive, no Nvidia (I was on x64), and certain other "holes" in the softwares I wanted.  

After spending an hour hoping for another way to install the video driver (I know, the old-fashioned way... but I wanted to keep things as by-the-book as possible due to upgrades and such) I began to reflect on my experiences with various distros.

Sabayon worked pretty well, but I had a couple of updated apps I wanted via Entropy that had yet to appear for some time.  Still, it's a great distro.

PCLOS hates my hardware, has ever since the updated ISO some months ago.

Mandriva One (x86)... for some reason, on two different installs, Amarok could not play my Apple Lossless files.  Other apps had codec issues, too.  No amount of searching and following guides seemed to help.  Again, the amount of time spent fiddling with it began to anger me.

Sidux... nice, but I'd like a GUI to fix things.  Elive, etc... the list goes on.

**Remember, I'm an average fella who simply wants things to work, even though I'm willing to accept a fair amount of learning and work to get what I want.**

Kubuntu x64 has surprised me.  Updates terrific, fonts look far better than the others.  Asks if I want a Nvidia driver, installs it easily.  When I start to play my music in Amarok... the files I ripped via iTunes with Apple Lossless... a dialog pops up asking if I want the multimedia codecs needed to play them.  Now that's what I needed!  Within a couple of minutes I'm happily listening to Tony Bennett.

I am really, really surprised to be using Kubuntu, but it really did everything I wanted.  Most "experts" have put down Kubuntu, and I was silly enough to believe them.  Thanks for bringing freedom and joy to my humble desktop!
