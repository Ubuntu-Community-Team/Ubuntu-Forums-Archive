### Post by exmatelot853 on 2006-08-16
I bought an AMD K6-500 running Win95 back in 1998 because my eldest daughter needed a PC for her education, it was promptly updated to Win98 and then WinME because of stability issues. During this quest I tried Corel Linux but gave up when I couldn't get dialup to work correctly and via system upgrades got to the heady heights of WinXP, at last my stability issues were essentially gone but still there was something missing for me.

I have since tried Fedora (FC1 thru 5), Suse, Mepis, Xandros and more but always back to Windows because Linux never quite did what I needed, until I chanced upon Ubuntu which was a revelation:o  here at last something that did not only what I needed but so much more.

So at this point in time I am on the fourth install of Ubuntu Dapper and seen the last install of Windows go from "My" PC's, my youngest daughter has a laptop running WinXP for school which is beyond my control.:) .

Not all of the installs have been straight forward but that has been part of the fun for me along with customizing the installs to suit.

So do I recommend Ubuntu? Yes it works for me, I need little more than Email, Internet, Ipod access and DVD ripping, it isn't for everyone perhaps especially if your needs are greater.

Finally, my thanks to the developers and the members of this forum for their invaluable knowledge and help, long may you all continue.

exmatelot853
