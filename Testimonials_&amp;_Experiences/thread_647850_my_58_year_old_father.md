### Post by bufsabre666 on 2007-12-22
well last january after a new computer was built for myself, me and my mother made the first computer my father has ever used.  we both were gun shy about him going on the computer for him being the least technologically inclined person ever ((it took him almost half an hour to figure out how to set reverse on a new power drill i bought for a job)). initially we had put windows on his computer for reasons that would seem sort of obvious, its always said to be user friendly ((jokes)). this being my dads first experience with a computer it seemed okay to give him the most mainstreamed os there was. after roughly 4 months he was complaining on how his computer was so slow. running a quick viral and spyware scan, i discovered well more then 200 different offending intruders and eventually he couldnt even log in. well at this point i had had my ears worth of having to fix his computer problems, and i backed up his application settings ((firefox bookmarks and email settings, not exactly a lot)) and i installed ubuntu.

despite his original reluctance to change over to the new os ((mainly because his work had recently installed a new computer system that used windows)) and his initial complaints of stuff not working ((it was my fault, i didnt install the video codecs and flash)) and other things like he didnt want to move away from m$ office and settle for my beloved OOo ((which ive pushed to him for a while at this point))

but at this point, for his total lack to computer knowledge and reluctance of change hes doing very well. he hasnt complained of a slow down, he loves the eye candy and majong ((hours of majongg)) he goes to his website, and even the ones he shouldnt cause he doesnt know anybetter but to.

honestly of all people complaining that linux isnt ready for the main stream need to take this into account. windows is so prone to turning your computer into a brick that using linux is prolly the way better choice for people. now all i need is MFT's to start making better drivers, or at least drivers period

but now that dad uses it and even tells his friends about it i could be spreading it, now all i need to do is get my mother to switch, shes the original computer nerd and loves her windows, and knows how to take care of it, but maybe she'll realize that linux can take care of itself and may change

1 down 1 to go, another member to the linux family
