### Post by Alekth on 2008-03-28
Two days ago I decided to give Linux a try again. I'd dabbled with Debian on and off before but never quite got anywhere that was enough to replace Windows. For the most part I still have very little experience with anything Linux.

Lately though I got a second internet connection (one with semi-decent ping times, good upload/download; one with great ping, great dl, amazing local dl - but poor upload) - and Windows made a mess of it.

So I went back to trying Debian, and started off with my (oldish Compaq evo610c laptop). Halfway through it I knew this would be quite complicated even on Linux...and one of the problems was that my laptop mostly just serves as a TV-out, and I couldn't get any player consistently manage to output various video files without pixelating in fullscreen...so I got Ubuntu (per pure luck), and while i'm still reading on the load balancing and advanced routing...for the rest of it Ubuntu worked perfectly. Enough to make me install it on my desktop as well, and I love it so far.

... while I was planning for a dual boot on the desktop, I managed to kill off the XP boot, but so far I haven't been missing it :KS

Getting SoundBlaster to function was some work, I still have to fix some refresh rates to stop casual screen flickering but that's pretty minor, as long as I have a working computer I can get small things right one at a time ^^

Rest assured, I'll manage to totally mess up this installation in 2-3 weeks of exploring :) I'm giving myself 27 days of tinkering and trying out, and then a fresh install of 8.04. Truly hope this will be the OS for me!
