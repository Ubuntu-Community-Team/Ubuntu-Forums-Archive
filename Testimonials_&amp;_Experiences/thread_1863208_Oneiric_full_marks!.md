> BigJules
What I can't understand is all the noise about Unity - its a very usable  and fast improving interface and while it does things in a different  way it can't reasonably be called hateful.

I think the difference is one of personal preference. Some individuals  (such as myself) who have been around Ubuntu for a period of time,  actually enjoyed the configuration aspects of it. In essence, one could  fully customize their system to meet their own expectations. I also see  the benefit to some, of set and forget or limited configuration. Instead, I will switch all six of our systems over to Xubuntu in 2013.  At present only 2 are running that version, the rest remain in the 10.04  world. I think  it's nice to believe that just because one's using the newest release  of Ubuntu, that somehow it equates to cutting edge. But, I also know  that it's strictly self fulfilling. ### Post by BigJules on 2011-10-18
For me, no special magic. Sys: vanilla No Name Core Duo 2.5MHz, 4G ram, integrated Intel graphics, samsung 1920x1200 monitor. Clean install of alternate version (for the RAID).

From DVD boot to first new system about 2-3 hrs, pleasantly passed with magazines and decent music. Having previously done a "#dpkg --get-selections > InstalledPackages.txt" to win myself a complete list of my installed progams, its then a trivial "#dpkg --set-selections < InstalledPackages.txt" and "#apt-get -u dselect-upgrade" to get them all back in one hit and I'm back in business. /home/xxx easily restored from an external hard disk and you're nearly there in only another half hour.

Its what comes next that counts. Bringing up a system to satisfaction only requires a bit of patience and calm. You've gconf, dconf and Compiz settings manager as great tools. The secret is to WRITE THINGS DOWN that you have altered from the original so at any time you can wind back. Compiz is a wonderful bit of kit and can achieve impressive effects but you can get in a bletcherous tangle if you forget that it's easy to make settings that fight each other. So keep the brain in gear, alter one variable at a time and +write it all down!+ This has got many pluses: if you have another machine to set up (wife's?) or you want another clean install then you just follow exactly what you did. And you've also got a record for the future of what happens when etc. Not the least is that when reporting a bug you can lay out for the devs just what steps from a known good situation are needed to replicate it. They'll bless you for it!
