### Post by gogogo111 on 2007-04-22
Ok, after 6.06 Dapper failing to partition my hard drive correctly (I asked it to free up space for Ubuntu, and it failed and messed up my Windows XP partition.), I was scared of trying to do the same thing with 7.04 Feisty Fawn. Well, I tried it.

I burnt the CD on Windows XP, made a backup of all my stuff, and booted into Linux. I get to the partition part, and instantly the first choice is to ask how much space I wanted to resize. I gave Ubuntu about 40GB.

I asked to transfer over my wallpaper and bookmarks from firefox. It transfered my wallpaper, but not my bookmarks. But they weren't very important either, just stuff like Imageshack, Gamefaqs, and my own website, stuff I can just readd.

Hoping it worked, I restart. Grub detects Linux AND Windows XP!!! It worked! This is awesome. I boot into Linux and it finds 2 updates. I update, and then install nvidia-glx through the restricted drivers. It didn't detect my 1440x900 resolution when I restarted though, so I had to edit xorg.conf.

My music was all FLAC format, so it worked without a hitch.

Overall, this version of Ubuntu is VERY usable, though the Desktop Effects are a little buggy, I'll have to report them later, this is EASILY the best release of Ubuntu yet. I only boot into Windows for games now. :) 

Total score: 9/10
+ Easy driver access
+ EXTREMELY easy installation, partitioning went perfect.
+ Very stable

- Didn't get all of my settings I wanted to transfer over.
- Desktop effects are a bit buggy, however they sort of are beta software.
