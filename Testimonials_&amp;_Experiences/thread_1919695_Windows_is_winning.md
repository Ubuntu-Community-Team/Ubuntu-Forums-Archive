### Post by zaentzpantz on 2012-02-03
At least in the audio and video side of things.
I converted to Ubuntu 8 months ago, and was won over by it. I love it, and welcomed the command line I have missed since the days of DOS. 
I do alot of video copying/editing/dvd authoring and audio copying/restoration/transcoding. I used to be the DTS support engineer for Europe and have spent a career in pro-audio, film and broadcast.
However over the past couple of months I've had to revert back to Windows XP for more and more of my audio video applications (computer is dual boot as the SO is not won over yet). 
It started after the printer went dead when Ubuntu 11.10 came in, and after two months of trying every fix in the forums and failing I just went back to XP for printing and that's the way it still is now.
Then video copying and editing was the next reverse migration. Every application I tried in Ubuntu worked sort of ok but failed in at least one area. Gopchop closes down every few minutes and is unusable, editing apps seem to need tinkering to make work properly and documentation is either weak or often missing. I give each app a week of hard work then give up, after all I'm interested more in the end product than a lifetime of software fixing. 
I now have TMPGEnc in Windows and it's heaven after the flaky Ubuntu apps I've been trudging through. I use MP3tag in Windows which is much better and quicker than anything I can find in Ubuntu.
The only thing that works better in Ubuntu is WinFF. 
I'm keeping on with Ubuntu apps but finding I'm now going back to Windows XP more and more for audio visual stuff, even though I have the Medibuntu options installed.
As long as I do a clean install of Windows every year or so it's been remarkably stable and quick.
