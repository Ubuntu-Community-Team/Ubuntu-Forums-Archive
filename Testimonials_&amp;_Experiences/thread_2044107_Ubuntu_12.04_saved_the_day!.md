### Post by exploder on 2012-08-18
I picked up a free dresser someone had set out this morning and me and the gentleman got to talking when we were loading it in my SUV. The man and his wife had picked up a Toshiba laptop for $60.00, it was real nice with a nice 17" display, 2 GB of RAM, etc. The laptop had Windows Vista 32 bit and would not connect to the Internet.

I told the guy I fixed computers in my spare time and would be happy to take a look at it. I booted the laptop from the 12.04 x64 Live CD and it connected to my network just fine and all of the hardware worked perfectly. I called the guy's wife and asked her if it was alright to install Linux on her laptop and she said that was fine as long as she could connect to the Internet.

I wanted to go with Linux because she was concerned about viruses and I knew that I could have everything working and up to date in a very short period of time, installing Windows meant hours of work and I will set someone up with Linux for free.

I went with Ubuntu because many people that are new to Linux really like Unity because it has the same kind of elegance as the MacOS. I personally like the length of support the LTS release has. I have 12.04 installed on my own laptop and Unity has worked out very well for me and my daughter was impressed with the interface.

I got the laptop loaded and configured in less than 2 hours and it ran beautifully. I took the laptop back to the owners and they could not believe I had it completed in such a short amount of time. I showed the woman all of the basics and she was really happy. She was shocked when I told her that I was not charging her for fixing the laptop.

I told the woman that it was free because it was so easy to install and I gave her a 12.04 CD in case she ever needed to install Ubuntu on another computer. The woman said that she wanted the laptop because she was wanting to use it for college and in my opinion 12.04 was perfect for what she needed to do.

Things worked out really well because she now had a 64 bit operating system with a complete set of applications, no worries about viruses and a very easy secure system that was very easy to maintain. It was rewarding for me to be able to help someone out and see that she was happy with the end result.

I wish more people were open minded about running Linux. I have a few different distributions on my machines but Ubuntu 12.04 really seems to appeal to new users and they seem to enjoy the fact that it is nothing like Windows. My step-dad has even decided to duel boot 12.04 on his laptop after using mine.
