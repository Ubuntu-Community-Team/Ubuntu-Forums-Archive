### Post by zero244 on 2011-06-23
Now I am not bashing Ubuntu as you can see Ive been using it for a while.

One thing I have learned that I forgot I had learned is if you keep doing updates to your current installation you will most likely do one too many updates and break something.

For the last couple of years my policy has been to install Ubuntu and do the first initial update and then turn off the update notifier and not do anymore, till I upgrade the version to a newer version.

This has worked very well.......without any broken packages etc.

Well when I updated Ubuntu Lucid on my latest install, Ive updated on three occasions.

And you guessed it some things got broken like VM Player, Gparted started acting up and actually stopped working.
With my initial install and update of Lucid my machine was real snappy and rock solid.

I know a lot of you update constantly and don't have problems, I am just saying this is what works for me.
If your  a noobie linux user you might want to cool it on the updates till you get your feet wet.

Just my two cents

I love Ubuntu, its on all my machines.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2011-06-24
Keep the regular security and bugfix updates turned on, and do them whenever you can. If you're REALLY squeamish, at least keep the security ones on - they barely change anything.

Lucid got a bit stuffed recently for people who had the "Proposed" repository enabled. The latest Linux kernel was accidentally uploaded to Proposed in Lucid. It was withdrawn, but it did cause breakage for people who, for whatever reason, wanted to stay at the bleeding edge of updates for an old stable distro :-)
