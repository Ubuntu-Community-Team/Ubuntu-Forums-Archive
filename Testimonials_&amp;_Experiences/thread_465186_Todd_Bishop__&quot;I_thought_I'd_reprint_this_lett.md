### Post by newbie2 on 2007-06-05
> For those who might have missed it, I thought I'd reprint this letter, below, knowing that many people will appreciate it.

    Todd Bishop's article of Tuesday concerning Microsoft's new venture in computing is irrelevant. Microsoft is about to join the history books as one of the companies that brought about the personal computer, along with WordStar, Lotus 123, hardware produced by Syquest and Iomega, and DOS.

    For 15 years since Microsoft Windows 3.1 hit the shelves, experienced users like me have alternately cursed at and praised the operating program designed to make the computer more user friendly. With Vista, they have continued to use a deeply flawed program, saddle it with new bells and whistles, and continue to proclaim their supremacy over the computer world.

    This notion of a table computer is nothing more than a toy. I got into this racket two decades ago to do something I couldn't do with a typewriter, make good documents. In these 20 years I have seen an incredible increase in power and capability to the point that we have boxes that can do things unimagined at the beginning of this project.

    That includes bringing the computer's promise to the people. A few months after Vista hit the streets with its $300 price tag and 40 million lines of code, another program hit the computing world. It is free, legal, just as powerful as Vista, and can utilize the capabilities of the modern computer in a way Windows never could.

    I type this letter from the alternative, codenamed Feisty Fawn. It is known to the world as Ubuntu 7.04 and it can do almost everything Vista can for none of the cost. With Linux as its core, Ubuntu has made Microsoft irrelevant. It has applications every bit as powerful as MS Office, or almost anything Microsoft can produce.

    Ubuntu in tandem with Portableapps,com, which produces an office suite that can run off a jump drive, delivers free alternatives to the Microsoft world. Installed side-by-side with Vista, it is head and shoulders better. And did I say, it is free and legal? Firefox, Thunderbird, Open Office and Linux will be tomorrow's killer applications. We will finally get the computer we have bought and paid for with our sweat, imagination, and time.

    Gary D. Brune
    Paradise, Calif.

The back story: Gary is a journalism student at CSU Chico, my alma mater, and I've met him when I've been back for some alumni-related events. It's fun to talk with him.
