Fellow students of Hale School, with the advent of Microsoft's new version of Windows and it's launch of it's new MP# player, I'd like to bring to your attention that you have a choice not to waste your money.
Linux is an alternative operating system to Microsoft Windows and Apple Mac OS that has clear and distinct advantages over its competition.
Microsoft and Apple have a ?????? history for thievery. The concept  of windowed programs was developed in the mid-1980's by a company named Xerox. This software was stolen, (annexed, acquired, assimilated, however you like it) by Apple Corp and was patented and copyrighted trademarked until it was Xerox's no more.
Microsoft had been seeking a tangible way for users to utilize computers and this window system was perfect. So they took it from Apple. Apple, as any corporate entity with only profit in mind would do, went ballistic. They attempted to sue Microsoft before the two struck an armistice over the matter.
 Bill Gates opinion on this matter at the time was: "It was like we'd broken into Xerox's house to steal it's TV, and found Apple had beat us to it."
The point I am attempting to make here is that closed-sourced software, like Windows and Mac OS is wrapped up in so many legal deals and licenses that it's restrictive and expensive to use. You're limited and constrained with proprietary software that doesn't even exist for the purpose of aiding you.
Ubuntu Linux is a completely free Operating System for your computers that is designed to be easy to use and powerful. Ubuntu Linux can do anything your school laptop's Windows can do. It can browse the web, play your copy of Starcraft or Counter-Strike, write your essays electronically and play DVD and all your music off your (regrettably)Apple ipod.

Ubuntu software is freely downloadable from anywhere in the world , or freely available on disks that can be mailed to you.
Your laptop won't crash when you attempt to edit the enormous poster you've designed for art, it's core-coding has been meticulously corrected and and continues to be so that processing demands are met properly, unlike in Windows. Linux is impervious to viruses! Computer "script-boys" write windows viruses IN Linux, otherwise they'd be susceptible themselves!
The enormous advantage Linux has over Windows is it's open-source base. Anyone and everyone can contribute as much and as little as they need, and the results are obvious. Linux runs on almost any device that can be defined as a computer. Your gameboy, your playstation, your iPod, you mobile phone or your Apple iMac.
You won't have to pay to download games onto your iPod with Linux, the open-source database of freegames will by your portal to videogames
All this has been possible due to Linux's tremendous open-source advantage. Communities online are places where you actually receive help from people who code the operating system, and not incomprehensible faceless mammals online, or impersonal phone help-lines that require you to "PRESS 3 FOR HELP". Just recently, software giant Java released it's platform to the open-source community because it recognized its advantage as a freely changeable, usable and redistributable 'product'.
So before you consider having to buy a completely new computer just to install the newest version of windows and its 'Aero' theme, make sure you consider ordering a free version of any Linux distrobution you like, knowing that it will meet all of you needs without any hiccups whatsoever.

Need I point out that almost all of the internet is run off Linux? It never -over- crashes unlike the Microsoft attempt at webserving which runs as stable as a house of cards.
I began to use my family's 8 year old Pentium 2 computer again because it ran so well with Linux. It processes instruction 3 times slower than a commissioned Hale laptop, but never ever does it lock up when booting or spit a blue screen rudely professing "MEMORY DUMP" when I take it out of hibernation.
In fact, I'm fairly sure my linux computer takes less time to boot up to the desktop than my laptop does. Security updates and other miscellaneous patches for my ancient computer arrive daily - the community finishes projects rapidly and sends them to every user - and I constantly feel like my needs are being cared for. When I first installed Ubuntu Linux onto my old computer  the soundcard did not work. The solution to this problem, I quickly discovered was on the Ubuntu Forum. I typed in my problem into the search bar, and lo -and- behold, a very kind developer had written instructions on how to make any soundcard work. Within  minutes I had any CD in my collection playing on my computer.
 Ubuntu Linux is sponsored by the South African millionaire Mark Shuttleworth, who sees enormous potential in Ubuntu and open-source software alike, and I urge everyone of you to consider Linux with your very next computer upgrade or sooner.
 The problem with buying a new computer these days is that Microsoft Windows is effectively shoved down consumer's throats  . Microsoft has made business deal with computer manufacturers to make it profitable to them to install windows onto their machines. This is where vendor-lock in originates from. Users like you and me have probably only ever used Microsoft products on our computers because of these Original Equipment Manufacturer deals. Linux compared to Mac OS and Windows is very different, and like anything (riding a bicycle for example) takes time to master before you realise just how much Linux outshines its competitors!

Say no to closed source. Demand your computer be Windows free- and install Linux today!
