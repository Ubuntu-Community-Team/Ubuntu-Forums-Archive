### Post by ander111 on 2011-05-12
> **Habeouscorpus said:**
> Toshiba netbook (NB 255)... My 10.10 install is beautiful, the only problem is suspend/hibernation (I installed acpitool and used the hiberation on that) and screen brightness (adjust by booting into the bios menu, then adjusting with the hotkey).
Reboot to BIOS every time you want to change your display's brightness? Er... 

I'd love to use Linux on my NB 255. Its speed over Windoze would be quite welcome.

Unfortunately, I couldn't get any of several distros, including so-called "netbook" versions, to recognize the NB 255's hardware brightness controls, or change brightness through any other method (including suggested terminal commands). Linux didn't seem to have a clue about any of the power-management features, which significantly shortened battery life.

So regretfully it was back to Windows Starter Edition. (Which really isn't that bad on the NB, BTW, and doesn't require an upgrade "to do anything", as you rather dramatically put it... If you just want to bypass Mr. Gates' limits on desktop personalization, you can download the free Starter Background Changer app. And remember, it's a *netbook*, designed for humbler tasks.)

If anyone figures out how to get Linux to do better on this machine, power-wise, I'll be happy to go back.
