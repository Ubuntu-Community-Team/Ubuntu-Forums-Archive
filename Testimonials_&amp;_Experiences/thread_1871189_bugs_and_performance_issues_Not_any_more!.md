### Post by julianb on 2011-10-28
I have been trying Ubuntu 11.10 in various versions through the pre-release cycle. I've given every major desktop environment a go, and I am pretty impressed with what I have seen! 

I tried KDE as Chakra and as Ubuntu and it runs pretty slowly on my old EEE-PC. The same was true when I tried Unity(3d) many months ago and when I tried Gnome Shell this month.

Today I installed Unity-2d and I'm delighted that performance seems good AND I am not experiencing a bug that was a huge inconvenience:

wireless networking didn't work (and maybe wired too but I can't test for that). I had figured out that logging out (to lightdm) and logging back in would usually, not always, fix the problem... and that setting my computer NOT to auto-login also worked most of the time.

But for now the whole problem seems to have gone away.

To the makers of every single desktop I tried -- good job!

KDE, Unity, and GNOME Shell: Good job on features, convenient to use, and good looking!

Unity-2d, Xfce,LXDE, and Enlightenment: yes, these desktop environments will do pretty much everything I want. There's the occasional problem but all in all i am impressed with the balance of features, looks, and "it just lets me get to the apps I want, when I want them".

Thank you, Ubuntu!
