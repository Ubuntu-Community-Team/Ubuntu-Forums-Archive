### Post by yester64 on 2009-11-03
Hi, hope you are ok because i am.

I mad the switch from Vista to Ubuntu couple of weeks ago and it went wonderful.
Then i made the upgrade which was a disaster for me or my system. Not sure.
But besides all that, i think linux will be my system since it does everything i ask it to do.
Currently i have the 9.04 running which runs on my comp pretty well.
The only concern i have is perhaps gaming. So i will keep my partition with windows, just in case.
But everything else is now in linux hand.
I knew linux from the old days in 1996 when i had Suse running and everthing looks a little more steril. But at that time games were my drive and so i stayed with windows.
Even with some troubles in linux, i think we really got accostumed to all the windows error we see.
Ok, Vista did not have a lo of Blue Deaths anymore, but there are still programs that just crash. Altough i think thats the applications fault.
But one thing i really hated was the (in my opinion) hype of 64bit. I have not seen any realy benefit for normal user at all.
Gaming 64 = almost non existant
Web = most apps are still 32bit
Graphics = only high end
And i think its at linux the same (web related). Thats why i wait on 64 bit since its not pressing to me really. Even though i have 64bit hardware i run 32bit software.

Ubuntu is one of the really nice ditstros i have tried. I had Suse (early) and Fedora. But Ubuntu is much easier to use and to install. And even if 9.10 was a complete failure for me, i stick with Ubuntu.

My advise is, don't be tempted to upgrade. Its like they say. 
'Never change a running system' and true that is.
