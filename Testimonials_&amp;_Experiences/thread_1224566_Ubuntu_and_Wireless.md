### Post by canam101 on 2009-07-27
I used mepis for a couple of years before switching to Ubuntu. They were about equal in what they could do with minimal tweaking, but ubuntu seemed a little more polished, and I stuck with it.

One thing I missed in using mepis was that my wireless card would not work. It was fine in windows, but I try to avoid using windows. I just assumed this was a limitation of linux and never bothered trying the card in my ubuntu pc.

I have read that there is a relatively simple way to get it to work in mepis - copy a few files, set a few parameters somewhere, but I was too lazy to make the effort.

A couple of days ago, just for the fun of it, I booted our laptop, which has wireless built in and runs on windows vista, with the ubuntu iso cd in place. Lo and behold, the laptop's wireless connection was running!

So I pulled the wireless card out of the windows/mepis pc and put it into the ubuntu pc. Lo and behold, it worked there without me having to do anything except boot up the pc!

This is a big plus for us, since it opens the possibility of setting up our pcs in a more convenient spot, or even moving the dsl modem + router to the demarcation point in the basement, bypassing our ancient, noisy, telephone wiring, which might get us a better profile, meaning faster speeds.

Good for ubuntu!
