### Post by azimuth on 2008-05-12
My son's wife was in the process of buying her brother's old laptop. He is in IT and had a lot of proprietary software for his job on it. He was having trouble finding the original XP disks that went with the computer. After him stalling for a week, she told him to just wipe the hard drive and her father-inlaw would put Ubuntu on it for her. His typically sibling response was the title of this thread. A couple of days later her mother dropped off the laptop to her at work. It had Hardy Heron OEM installed on it. She just answered the questions when it booted and was up and running. 
She brought it by to show it off and get a little tutelage on using the new to her system. Running it through it's paces, we found the sound was not working. It took three trys to get the right string in Google to find the answer. The fix was as simple as they get, two clicks in the Alsa Mixer GUI and that was it.
She was amazed that her camera and her printer were plug-n-play. Her camera phone was a simple plug the sd minicard into her printer's card reader and that was working too.
I guess the question is, how "cool" do you have to be to use something that just works?
