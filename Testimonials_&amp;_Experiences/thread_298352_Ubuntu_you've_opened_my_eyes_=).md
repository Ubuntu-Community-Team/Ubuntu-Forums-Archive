### Post by coastdweller on 2006-11-12
Well it's a heck of a ride but I'm making it.

Since I downloaded Dapper (kubuntu) I've managed to convert the entire house and office from all windows to entirely ubuntu.

My wife has two new machines, a desktop and a laptop. **She has since been re-invigorated** into computing and has actually started to look over in the office and troubleshoot (ahead) when I am stuck on something (Amazing!)

She runs **Ubuntu Edgy** on her desktop (After several installs she's stopped mucking around a bit where she shouldnt have lol), and after getting stuck trying to get her wireless radio working on her laptop she is slowly getting her laptop into order (She doesnt ask for help, simply trudges through install after install, falling asleep at her chair trying to figure it out lol).

I've managed to get my scsis, satas, and ide drives all running with ext3 to completely **de-microsoft** what we do here.

I have **ubuntu edgy** running on my desktop and have managed to get Photoshop and Dreamweaver running in wine well enough and bug free enough to finally get passed the "Man, I think I'm still going to have to have a damned microsoft product on this machine".

My laptop still runs kubuntu dapper which is fine, it does just what I need and also runs photoshop and dreamweaver just fine.

We have two local servers, one ubuntu dapper and the new one (Built last night) ubuntu edgy.

The dapper server runs a **local torrent server** that we both can access from anywhere (Uploading torrents, managing downloads etc) from anywhere in the world.

We have our billing system running on the same machine (We're moving the torrent server today some time) which is our accounting system for our online customers.

The dapper server is a dedicated rack mounted server with a single 80 gb drive (**Will be installing a second 80 gb for mirroring redundacy only**).

The new server we built last night is a monster (When considering its just for the house).

Its a p4 3.06 with 2gb ddr and 6 drives.

pata cdrom
scsi 36gb 10k lvd (boot,root)
pata 320gb (hda)
pata 100gb (hdc)
pata 100gb (hdd)
sata 160gb (sda)

We call it **badmuthafucka** (root@badmuthafucka) cracks me up (From pulp fiction) and man... it is (She came up with the name btw lol).

So I'm posting into testimonials? Why yes, and I have to say its been a hell of a month or so converting, reinstalling, moving tons of data.

To the develpers, to the community here, DEFINITELY the irc channel #ubuntu (That has helped a great deal) I just wanted to send a big thanks. Ubuntu was a great way to finally get me to fully convert to linux (Have ran remote servers since 2001, always lacking that "little bit".

**Ubuntu, you've opened my eyes =)**
