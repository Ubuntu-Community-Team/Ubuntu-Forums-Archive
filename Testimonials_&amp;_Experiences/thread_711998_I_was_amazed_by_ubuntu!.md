### Post by patrickaupperle on 2008-03-01
One day, about 1 year ago, I was surfing wikipedia.org and somehow found Linux. I started looking carefully into it and found this amazing community and  the fact that I could try this distro with a live CD. This seemed like it would be a unique feature.  I continued looking around on these forums, Ubuntu.com, and Kubuntu.com. I eventually decided that I would like KDE over Gnome. I downloaded kubuntu 6.06Lts and burned it to CD. I was thinking, "This is probably going to be a  terrible experience." My thinking came from the general oppinion that free stuff is never as good as the stuff you pay for. I rebooted and popped the cd into my drive. After watching KDE load, I was unpleasantly surprised that my wireless did not work. I also noticed that it didn't look very nice. Too windows like. I looked around on these forums. They were incredibly helpful and pointed to a how to for the wireless. I looked at this how-to and decided that it looked complicated. I decided that I would try to follow it if/when I installed (K)ubuntu. The wireless was a pain, that I was unsure whether I could fix. I was going to leave the Linux world, forever.

         I could not stop thinking about Linux.  I knew the community would help me fix everything that did not work. I decided to give it one more chance. I downloaded Ubuntu 7.10 and burned it to a CD. Maybe KDE was the problem. Maybe Gnome would pleasantly surprise me. I also noticed that It was 7.10 now. My expectations were, once again, low. I put the CD in the drive and booted from CD. I watched the orange bar fly from left to right. Then when it was halfway full, MY CD DRIVE OPENED. Why would something like that happen? It, of course, showed a whole lot of errors. I thought, "No big problem I'll just reboot." I rebooted and it got all the way to the desktop. It actually looked like somebody made it to be user friendly and not at all windows like. At first this scared me. I did not recognize how to do anything. Then I realized the applications menu. Duh, thats how to run applications. I opened up the menu and found a wealth of fun games. This beat a base install of windows, bad. I played with the games and decided that this made Linux pretty good. I was in the middle of playing a game of connect four when my CD drive opened again. It shut off again. Lots of errors.

         I decided I had to try this out more. I did not know about wubi, so I had to install on a hard drive or dual boot. I read up on dual booting. I downloaded the GParted live cd. Then I decided that I didn't want to dual boot. There were too many problems. Too many people were having there windows partitions disappearing. I even read of some one who accidentally deleted there windows. In the end I  decided it would just be easier to buy I knew hard drive. I wanted my full 80 GB anyway. I bought a 60 GB hard drive for my laptop. My laptop, being a dell Inspiron e1505, has a hard drive that easily slides out and a new one in. I found this out when my windows crashed and  I had to spend 2 hours with Dell customer support. After they tried "trouble shooting", they sent me a new hard drive. It took me somewhere between 3 and 5 hours to install Windows. I installed Ubuntu, on this new hard drive, in about half an hour. Wow, doom 3 took longer to install.
       
  I decided that I needed wireless, so I opened the how-to. I followed the instructions. They were mostly cut and paste. I had 3 or 4 problems. There is no way this will work with these problems, or so I thought.  I rebooted and it connected to my wireless network. Wow. I looked around the repos, which are my favorite part of Linux. I found some great games, Armagetron advanced being one. I put the little weather thing up in the top bar next to the evolution mail launcher. I also  tried out some of the themes and stuff. I prefer the human theme. 

         I  booted into windows for the sake of comparison. To my surprise it seemed very sluggish. It also seemed very unorganized. I decided that Ubuntu was better then windows. I then went to shut down windows. I had gotten used to Ubuntu so I at first went towards the top right corner. Oh this is windows. The shutdown is on the start menu. I thought, "no problem, I'll just add one in the top left." :lolflag: After a few seconds of trying, I remembered that this is windows, I can't just add stuff wherever I want. I was really disappointed with windows. I booted back to Ubuntu and continued enjoying my computing experience. Since that time, about 3 months ago, I have booted windows about 5 times. I now have no idea why anyone would pay for that piece of junk OS. I'm sure not everyone has the same experiences, but from what I have seen, Linux beats windows. I don't play any games on my computer; so my opinion may change if I ever get a good computer with a good graphics card.  This is my experience with Linux and will be dual booting soon. Not with windows, but with another Linux distro.
