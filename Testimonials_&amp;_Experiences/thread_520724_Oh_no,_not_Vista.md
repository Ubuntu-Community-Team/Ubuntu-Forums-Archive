### Post by inyoka on 2007-08-12
My brother got my parents a new Vista box, its GREAT!!  It can't run the web-cam I gave them, so they sent it back to me (yes it works perfectly under Ubuntu), and my brother doesn't want to install any new software on it incase it goes belly up, so they can't Skype me.

I however have Ubuntu playing DVD's, running Google Earth, Skype is setup and working, GAIM (or Pidgin) is setup for my google, hotmail and yahoo accounts, and I have Warcraft III running like a charm under wine to de-stress.  Its just a pitty I can't Skype my folks.  

About two years ago when they had XP the situation was almost totally reversed.  Now though I run Ubuntu on over 50 machines at the school I work at, and soon our Fedora terminal server will switch and that will go up to over 70.  I also have another 12 Edubuntu PC's in the Infants school.  

Four years ago switching to Linux freaked me out, a world without Windows was very scary, for those of you in the same situation its using Windows that now makes me feel scared.  Apt and synaptic make linux a great operating system, but Ubuntu's philosophy and community make it the best distribution.  

Thank you to everyone who works or helps with Ubuntu, because its the community which makes Ubuntu the knockout distribution for me.  Lets just hope that someday the people in redmond understand the difference between communism and community!
