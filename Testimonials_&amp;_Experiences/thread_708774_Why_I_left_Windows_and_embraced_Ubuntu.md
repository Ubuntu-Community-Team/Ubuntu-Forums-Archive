### Post by jorgecarrillo150990 on 2008-02-26
In two months is my Ubuntu anniversary!
Should I throw a party or something? :P
Well anyways, I thought that this could be a good time to post why and how I left Windows.
So, first of all, I am mexican, and we take the 15 year birthday very seriously (in young girls, in guys not so much).
Well, I was making a photo video for my sister since she was celebrating her 15th birthday, I was a Windows slave at the time (hated Apple and never heard of Ubuntu). I used Pinnacle Studio to make the video, I was almost done, when my laptop BSOD'ed. I was quite angry, but nothing that could be fixed just by restarting the PC. So I restarted and everything was back to normal, everything but the video I was making!
I was really pissed, I had done nothing wrong to deserve that fate!
So, in my desperation I borrowed a Mac from a friend to edit the video while some PC technicians were repairing my HDD.
I found Mac very nice looking and decided to buy one.
But then at my sister's party, a cousin who studies PC engineering told me about this thing called Ubuntu. I was intrigued, so as soon as I got my laptop back, I quickly downloaded Ubuntu.
First I was all freaked out because of the interface, but the more I digged in it, the more I started to comprehend the OS. So, here I am, 10 months later. Recently I wiped out my Windows partition and installed windows using VirtualBox (which I only use for school purposes). And I couldn't be happier.

### Post by bill.blankenship on 2008-02-26
Congrats!  I think part of the reason why Ubuntu is catching on so fast is due to the "word of mouth" advertising from people that actually use it. But that's just a part of the reason. Another significant factor is that Ubuntu just works! :-)  

I'm using a Dell Latitude D620 laptop, and one of the first things I did was wipe it and install Ubuntu. Feisty was the current version at the time, and everything on the system was automatically recognized, from the gigabit NIC all the way to the built-in smart-card reader. And for those that are mobile, Gutsy will recognize your AT&T AirCard 850 right out of the box!

Unfortunately, as you mentioned, there are some reasons to keep Window$ around in some form or another (school, the few Window$ applications that are amazingly difficult to run on Linux...). Fortunately, some places are making smart decisions. For example, University of Phoenix used to rely on Outlook Express for nearly ALL of the online classes, but recently switched to a standards-compliant browser-based learning environment that doesn't CARE what operating system you're using. 

You mentioned VirtualBox. This month's "Linux Format" magazine has an excellent article on converting the existing Window$ partition to a VMDK file before wiping the disk. (Pretty cool stuff, but I haven't had a chance to play around with it yet...)

Again, congrats on your up-coming Ubuntu anniversary! :-)
