### Post by armandh on 2008-10-25
I remember our family's first desktop a 486-33 with win 3.1 over dos 5.x installed, and installed once again when I mucked up some system files. over the years I got proficient at reinstalling windows for self and others plus the occasional upgrade or new computer assembly. Over those years I would try a boxed set of Linux disks. Just no luck getting anything to work. 

this changed when I tried Knoppix. here was an example of linux/GUI that worked. Ubuntu 7.04 was next. It worked and installed. A stack of coasters has convinced me there is no easier/better Linux distro.

Installing Ubuntu takes the minimum of skill with good and compatible hardware, but there after it gets real tricky. I've learned a lot this past year and a half on those trickier issues. mostly the problems have been over hardware drivers or favorite software not available in native linux.

Medibuntu.org makes the whole DVD/codex issues go away and the restricted drivers for the laptop deal breakers are growing in number. In the beginning it all depended on that great first experience, for me it was the best linux OS yet. 

then there is the pre installed OS. mac and win are ahead in that race, but mac lost it's cachet adopting x86, and vista crawls at the speed of molasses in january. 

so in my old age I am comming full circle ordering a notebook with Ubuntu on board. no need to reinvent the wheel.
