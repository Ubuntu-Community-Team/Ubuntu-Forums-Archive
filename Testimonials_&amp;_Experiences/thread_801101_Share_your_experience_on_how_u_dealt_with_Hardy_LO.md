### Post by rgb1701 on 2008-05-20
Stating "My OS locks up" without stating the complete hardware config is meaningless.

Please state motherboard make & model number, CPU make and model, RAM type and brand, video card, audio card, any additional cards in PCI/PCIe/AGP slots, Ethernet card used (motherboard or addin), wireless chipset if used, and any other device plugged into any port (USB, Firewire, etc).

Lock ups could be the result of bad motherboard, bad RAM, bad video card, bad CPU cache, and lots of other stuff.

It could be driver issues- like Win people, don't blame the OS- "It's the Drivers!" ;)

### Post by immerohnegott on 2008-06-10
UNGH! Ok. So, I updated all of my machines from Gutsy to Hardy the day it came out (two desktops and a laptop). Both desktops were fine (32-Bit Ubuntu/32-Bit Kubuntu), but the laptop was hard-locking left and right. My main desktop I switched to 64-bit UbuntuStudio, still fine. Fed up with trying to fix the lockups on my laptop, I installed Fedora 9, only to be confronted with massive piles of video glitches. OK, so I went back to Hardy (clean install) and it was *better*, but it still locked up. Turned Compiz off, and it seemed to work fine.

Now here's where the fun begins. The laptop in question is an aging PIII-M system with an Intel 830M CGC. The new "experimental" intel driver for xorg won't properly display 24-bit color on this chip (it bands gradients, looks like 8 bit). So, I switched to the old i810 driver, which provides a better display, but the lockups came back. Just switched it back over to the "intel" driver, and it *seems* to be ok. Relatively ugly, but it's at least usable.

I'm not sure what's causing this, but it's driving me (and a lot of people, evidently) mad. I don't think it's directly kernel related (I installed the -rt kernel after I went to the i810 driver, and the lockups persisted). Perhaps a conflict between certain combinations of network/video modules?

I fell in love with Ubuntu when I first installed it a few years ago, but this is starting to test my patience, especially on an LTS.

At least there's always gutsy...

for the interested, specs on the affected laptop:
Dell Latitude C400
Pentium III Mobile @1.2 gHz
512mb PC133 RAM
Fujitsu 80gb ATA
Intel 830M chipset/graphics
Intel ICH3? southbridge (i think)
Intel IPW2200 b/g wireless NIC
