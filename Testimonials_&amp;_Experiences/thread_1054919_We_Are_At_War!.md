### Post by markbuntu on 2009-04-13
Rather than petition for linux support like a bunch of marginal cranks, complain that you cannot watch your favorite show on your new netbook but you can watch this other show on a competing network so that is what you are doing but could they please fix their problem.

We are, after all, consumers so we need to take a consumer centric approach.

More people use linux than macs now and critical mass will be reached sooner than later. Be patient, complain individually and often as an innocent consumer and ask politely, and with regret, that they fix this problem that is preventing your consumption of their product.

A petition with 10,000 names does not have the impact of 10,000 individual complaints. Statistically, in marketing, one person who takes the time to complain represents between 50 and 100 others who are equally upset. A petition represents a political agenda whose multiplier is generally less than 3 and at the most 10. This is how marketers see these things. it is what they are taught.

### Post by chrisinspace on 2009-04-15
Whether you happen to like the programming or not (I'm a Lost fan, but I could care less about the rest of the content pushed by the Move Media Player) there is a principal involved.  Adoption of Linux is being undermined even as the market is driving more and more people to our OS, especially through netbooks.  If Joe Schmoe netbook owner comes home, fires up his new machine, and goes to ABC or Fox to watch one of his favorite shows, but sees that Linux is explicitly not supported, what do you think his reaction will be?  Many consumers will return their Linux netbook, exchange it for a machine running XP, and write off Linux as an inferior product.  These are the kinds of first impressions that we want avoid.  I know you can get episodes of your shows through other means (mostly illegal, but whatever) and I know some people have had success getting the Move Media Player to work using Wine, but most Linux newcomers are not going to make that level of effort.  If you think the petition is a waste of time, but you agree that this is a threat to the Linux community as a whole, then send a message to ABC, Fox, etc.  Here's a link to the ABC feedback form:

[http://ll.static.abc.com/s/fep/feedback/index?origin=install]("http://ll.static.abc.com/s/fep/feedback/index?origin=install")

If you do send a message, please make it constructive and not inflammatory.  That kind of stuff doesn't do us any good.  I don't agree with the sentiment that this is a war; in my eyes it's a movement.
