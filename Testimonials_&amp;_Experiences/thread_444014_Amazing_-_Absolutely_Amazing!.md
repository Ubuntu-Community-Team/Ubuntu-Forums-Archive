### Post by wbsorsby on 2007-05-14
I've been waiting on Linux for years.  I played around a little with Red Hat 5.2 years ago, and it kinda worked. Then the 6.0 upgrade broke my sound and printing badly, so I just went back to Windows. I couldn't let go of my fondness for Linux though and I came back with Fedora Core 2, or more correctly, the Planet CCRMA incarnation of Fedora. I learned about the awesome Audacity back then - there wasn't a Windows based program that compared to it.  I employed Planet CCRMA/Core 2 as my main OS for about six months, but finally went back to Windows when my photo-processing backlog piled up high enough. Never really got the scanner working right either that time. USB was essentially worthless then, too.

So, six weeks ago my motherboard gives up the smoke. Rather than paying another M$ toll, I decided to just resurrect a 2.4 GHz box which had gone unused for a year or so. What really motivated me to dump Windows was the fact that all my data and programs on my hard drive would all have to be reloaded. Nothing wrong with my hard drive of course, just the OS. The Windows OS license was literally tied to the hardware. It died with the motherboard. (Like I said, the M$ toll just frosted me . . . )

So I came back to Linux expecting, maybe not the worst, but to find that Linux still wasn't ready for prime time. Don't get me wrong, it's taken a lot of my time to get up to speed with Ubuntu, and it's easy enough to think the problems I encountered could have been easily avoided in the first place. Overall, though, I'm really pleased with Ubuntu Feisty. Sure, I use crutches, like Virtual Box - a big surprise, that program. Virtual Box is my way to run programs which simply can't run under Linux otherwise. And, I use Codeweavers Cross-Over Office for some stuff, like paying bills through Quicken, and I also use it to run IE6 - for those websites which give Firefox grief. It's even nice to know I can resort to Word and Powerpoint if necessary.

Still, Ubuntu Linux just seemed kinda drab - that is, until I installed Beryl. What a difference that makes - no more dull windows. Even those drab Cross-Over Office screens now have color and pizazz. I even converted both of my daughters machines to Ubuntu a few weeks ago, and I've not gotten a single complaint yet. One daughter is just glad to have a machine unencumbered by spyware which had slowed her machine to a crawl. She also loves the Beryl effects. My other teenage daughter likes the games - go figure. Anyway, so far so good. Beryl puts such a shine on Linux that one easily begins to overlook the numerous flaws of the OS - or perhaps they are just marks of character.

Anyway, I'm still amazed. I realized within a week or so of trying Ubuntu, I could live with the OS. With Beryl added into the mix, going back to Windows would seem like a real step backward. It's just one of those intangible things which seems subtle, yet engenders endearment. Linux is clearly on a roll - not just with Beryl, either, although Beryl does make it shine.

Linux - yeah, still painful. If you can persevere through the pain though, it's a great OS.


Regards,
Bill
