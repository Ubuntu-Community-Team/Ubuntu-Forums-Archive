### Post by SirBismuth on 2009-11-12
Hello all,

**My Karmic experience was as follows:**

I downloaded and installed the .iso the day after it was officially released.  I usually run the vanilla Ubuntu at work, and then just use Synaptic to get any extra apps I need, or download (preferably) .deb files.

I don't have sound at work, so that isn't an issue for me.  Reinstalled all the apps I was using in 9.04, that went off without a hitch.  Except for Skype, the version I had was complaining about what seemed to be unresolvable dependencies, so I just downloaded Skype again from their site, and it installed without a hassle.

All my data/mail/bookmarks restored flawlessly.  I was having an issue with Evolution NOT remembering words saved to the dictionary, but before I had to chance to look at it, an update fixed that problem.  I also had issues with FF crashing randomly, but not anymore since some updates have come through.

So far, still smooth sailing...

**Alright, now for the Ultimate Edition 2.4 experience:**

My download from unixheads was corrupted, as per the MD5 checksums, so I ended up downloaded 4.8Gb instead of 2.4Gb.  Oh well, at least the second download from the main server was successful.  I did not have the bandwidth to torrent like I did with the vanilla 9.10.

When I did load the LiveCD, it gave me a "frequency out of range" message.  A bit of research here told me that this has something to do with the graphics card output, and seems specific to the 7600 series.  Anyway, the answer was to "Ctrl-Alt-(-) or (+)" until I at least had an image to install Ultimate Edition.  The error persists after an install, but downloading and installing the latest drivers from Nvidia fixed this.

What I see so far, I like.  Will carry on with it later when I get home, only restored my mail and bookmarks, then it was 00:00 :eek:, and I had to work today!  :D

I removed XP entirely when upgrading to 2.4 at home, but unfortunately still dual-boot at work, for now.
