### Post by InsertNameHere on 2007-10-10
OK, i hate long posts so I'll make this short and organized

I simply love this system, I'm in highschool, and it almost suit my every needs.

1. The hardware detection is great : I used to be wary about installing and all that stuff, but after I hit the "install" button it worked just like magic (the automatic partitioner wont work but i had experience with partitioning so no problems). Picked up EVERY piece of hardware Im having.

2. Everything I need is here : Openoffice is great, OK, I admit it's not as advanced as MS word but it doesn't cost me a crazy amount of money. Another note on hardware, my printer totally works with Openoffice and the surprising thing is that the LCD at my school works with this system.

3. The eyecandy is awesome : I personally is a fan of eye candy. I love beryl, it's a simple install and i got a cubic desktop and everything, (I made my friends jealous :)) The multiple workspaces really kept everything in order, and it just gives a relaxing feeling to the eye.

4. Customization : I am able to customize gnome to my wantings without ANY knowledge to linux what-so-ever. Everything is built in, great info provided on the forums and 3rd party, Everything (except the boot screen) is customizable and I really like that.

5. APT is the greatest way to install everything : APT and synaptic, are the greatest ever, before on windows I probably had to search the web for 5-10 hours just to find a FREE SCREEN RECORDER!! That is really ridiculous, but on Ubuntu the add/remove is in 1 window, just type a search term and Voila everything you need is there.

6. Probably the most impotent one Virus Free : On windows due to my intensive web useage, I *MUST* reinstall windows every 1-3 month, but with Ubuntu that doesn't seem to be a problem at all. I even tried ON PURPOSE to get in to the sites that i got hacked from before and nop, it's so great that it doesnt ANY sort of viruses.

7. And most importantly, IT'S FREE. It's just as/better then windows and costs about 500 bucks less. not to mention (almost) all the software is absolutely free. In windows the ways that you can get software is really to pay big bucks or to download illegal once (hey everyone does it *at my school at least, I got a teacher to admit it once*), stuff like photoshop and MS Word can be replaced by GIMP and openoffice.

Overall I think this is a really great system, I recommended it to my friends and stuff. Im dual booting from windows (its the last on my grub list xD) for the pure propose that a game called Maplestory (please don't criticis this) wont run natively on linux (too bad) and I can't get it working in wine (stupid gameguard, no ones going to hack -sigh-). But everything else in windows is pretty much useless.

As a side note Microsoft should really lighten up..... I get bogged down by a error message about user32.dll everytime i log on (I don't really want to know how to fix these anymore since more just come up) At least they should give you some tips on fixing the problem instead of a BOING then complete foreign computer language. And I feel im treated like a pirate with MS's Validation and all that stuff........ So theres my little rant about that.. now back on topic.

Anyways I think that Linux will eventually get better (it takes 6 months for a ubuntu release I think and 3 years to go from XP to Vista.........) And I'm really looking forward to Gusty
