### Post by SnTholiday on 2007-10-08
When people post of their difficulties with Ubuntu in this "Testimonials and Experiences" forum, you will usually see the following types of responses:

"We have seen this exact argument a million times over. I have officially stopped caring, if Windows is for you then go back to it, don't hop onto the forums and decide you want to rub your problems in our faces."

"Maybe I'm missing something but I really do not understand this type of post."

"Actually as venting seems to be in vogue - the more of these posts I see the more I feel like saying "Cheerio - don't let the door hit you in the *** on the way out."

The OP of the thread the above quotes were taken from expressed his dissapointment with having to use Windows:

"As much as I hate having to be stuck using Windows, and how much I wish I could dump all the M$ stuff, I just don’t see it happening anytime soon with Linux. I’ll keep Ubuntu on my system, maintain it, and continue learning how to use it, but it won’t be used for anything of great importance. That, sadly, is very disappointing to say the least."

This is very much a testimonial for those of us who would love to use Ubuntu as our sole OS, but cannot for whatever reason. I posted two weeks ago in this very forum about how impressed I was with the 7.04 release and got one response. After using Ubuntu for a couple of weeks I have decided to go back to Windows, mainly due to audio/video problems. I do not like having a dual boot system and just don't have the time to spend researching these forums and asking questions to get something to work. I like what Ubuntu stands for and have a lot of respect for it's developers, but it seems to be an OS for people who love and have the time to fix problems. 

I do want to say that threads such as "Bye Bye Ubuntu" are uncalled for, but when a poster expresses his/her experiences with Ubuntu and states they wish they could dump M$, isn't that what this forum is for?

that said I will say again HARDWARE PROBLEMS are often mistaken for Ubuntu problems. Not just drivers but actual hardware. Ubuntu is not tolerant of hardware not up to the proper spec.

the old, the odd, and the newest are going to be driver problems.
there seems to be a growing enlightenment at the hardware level that Linux is a  segment worth writing drivers for or providing the necessary hooks for open driver development.
