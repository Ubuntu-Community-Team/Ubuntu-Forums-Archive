### Post by KeyWork on 2010-05-30
I've been a windows kid ever since I got my first PC, which ran Windows XP Professional. I'd never really heard of any other operating system for computers except from Mac computers. I'd had my PC for a good 2 years before I could no longer take the slow and sluggishness XP had become over that time, which lead me to searching for a new PC but at the time I had no money. It had got to the time in my computers life where Windows just constantly crashed at the slightest thing I had done, and eventually it lead to a constant loop of BSoD's. 

At this moment in time I was so annoyed at how something which I had paid for, initial costs and subscription fees to Anti-virus software, had lead me to loosing my entire media collection ( I know I should have backed it up, but I was 15 at the time and had little computing knowledge ), all my school work, and other data in the blink of an eye. I went on my parents computer and browsed the net looking for a solution to the problem, but much to my dismay at the time, nothing came up except a link to the Ubuntu website from a reply to my question in a forum. 

I was intreged at how an OS could be free, needless to say I downloaded and burned the ISO file to a disc with the help of a YouTube video. I put the disc in my laptop and BOOM, it was as if new life had been put in m computer. I let the installer install Ubuntu, and from then on in I've been hooked on the OS. Upon the purchase of an iPhone, I was stumpt as to what to do. I needed iTunes to put songs on my iPhone and Ubuntu had no support for the device at that moment in time. I ended up installing Windows 7 on my PC and stuck with Win7 for a long time. Like all Windows products in failed after 5 months and like XP suffered from countless errors and constant BSoD's. 

Now Ubuntu 10.04 is out with the iPhone support, it looks like I will be back with Ubuntu forever now. I can't express my gratitude to everyone at Conical, and everyone in the Ubuntu and Linux community for making Ubuntu such a wonderful OS. 

James.

Me too!  According to what I have been able to find, 10.04 does something different with data alignment, possibly in the boot sector, than previous versions. With my latest installs, I partitioned the drive first with gparted live and used ext3, not ext4.  That's all I know and probably way more than I was supposed to say in this forum.
