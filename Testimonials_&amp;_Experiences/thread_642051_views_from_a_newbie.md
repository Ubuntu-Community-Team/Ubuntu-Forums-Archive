### Post by Gazz777 on 2007-12-16
Hi Guy's, I thought I would give you my views based on my experience so far, after all it's close to 2 weeks using Ubuntu.

Installation/ partitioning/dual booting
Installation I found a little difficult I formatted the drive added windows xp, and created partition for Ubuntu, clear sailing and no hic-ups.
Installation of Ubuntu however was stressful and can I use the word buggie, it ( Ubuntu ) didn't seem to see my partitions I had created using the Windows disc, I cant speak for others, but when your at this stage sort of DOS world, your anxiety is high, and the last thing you want is error, no root or please create swap, I was making it up as I went along, but eventually got through it, ( relieved ), the actual loading of Ubuntu was great, it found everything updated all, add to the fact I was on-line without effort and I was coasting.

There was/is a few problems I must resolve, for instance I can play music, but the actual peeps for opening programmes etc is silent, this leads to a very silent Ubuntu Experience.
Didn't see my spare h/drive, but there is recognised issue's there between sata & pata, "yip  I know it is way above me as well"
As far as I have went with Ubuntu all is looking well, I am finding it ok to get around, and the learning curve isn't ( so-far ) severe, Office will be a challenge, but I'm a believer in one step at a time, the mail is great, web ( is it faster ) is great, the feeling I get from Ubuntu is solid reliable and most important secure, although as yet I still haven't the confidence in my own ability to use Ubuntu thus all important stuff is still handled with Windows, but I know this will change given time, I'm at the stage of “ok Ubuntu cant do this or how can I get newsleecher to work”, and I have still to try and load pictures direct from my Camera onto Ubuntu, I have a games keyboard and mouse, but it only sees it as a normal keyboard, the Idea of just adding drivers is out the window sorry for the pun, so lot's to do.

I have used Windows for years as far back as win 95 and a little before, I see myself however as a driver of windows not a mechanic, thus if anything happens that is beyond the odd con fig click this click that I step back.
The reason I am moving is,” if I say what I feel, I sound like a conspiracy nut”, so I will say I lost trust in Microsoft and the OS, Vista I have used a couple of times and find it slow, with thousands of programmes working in the back ground doing something I have " as a user " no control over, I realised then there is no way to go forward any longer with Microsoft, for some reason it struck me that Microsoft priority isn't the actual running the program but the collection of data from the user, and the sinister thing about this is the user isn't aware most of the time this is going on, now I have began to rant " sorry"

Ubuntu is today the answer, certainly for me, forums like this are the safety net we newbies need, the attitude of the people who take time out to answer our errors, and there patience for they must answer the same things many times, I apologise on behalf of all us newbies, we thing our problems are original we forget millions have went first, but to give up your time for others, unseen hero's.

Kind Regards
Have a great Christmas

Gary

Linux doesn't use letters to say what drive it is.  They show in /dev (short for devies) as /dev/sda1 (a=first physical disk, 1=first partition) and /dev/sda2 (first disk, second partition), and /dev/sdb1 (second disk, first partition), etc.

>  I have still to try and load pictures direct from my Camera onto Ubuntu
Your camera may have a mode setting for "bulk storage" (I think that's what mine calls it).  It should just be a matter of plugging it into USB and copying the pictures you want as if it was a thumb drive.

### Post by sgtbob on 2007-12-16
Gazz777- I am relatively new to the Linux OS world - a year or so of dabbling - and have tried other flavors of Linux - Fedora, Red Hat, SuSE, Mandrake, etc., and find that Ubuntu is by far the least disruptive to my system.  I was able to install Ubuntu with only minor obstacles, although I did install on a second HDD and run it in dual boot with Win XP.  The logic behind Ubuntu and use of Synaptic makes it the best for me.  The single disc to get started is a real neat issue. I have not tried Kubuntu nor Xubuntu, so have no input on those.

The are several things I hae found that are hard for me - a home network, using WINE to enable me to work with my Windows based programs, and a few others.  

The greatest thing I find is the folks who are willing to go out of their way to help when a problem occurs without the flaming that occurs on some flavors. I can't believe some of the kindnesses shown by people all over the world when I have had a problem. Having lived in a number of places in the world, I like the international cooperation of users that I have encountered on the various forums. Maybe a side benefit of Ubuntu is that one finds that people are people no matter where they live.  I hope I can contribute a bit as I continue to use Ubuntu.

Good luck
