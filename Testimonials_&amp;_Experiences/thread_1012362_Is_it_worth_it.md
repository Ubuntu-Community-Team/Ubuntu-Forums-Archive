### Post by aysiu on 2008-12-15
Well, it's really up to you.

If you want to invest the time and energy, keep looking for and asking for help, and things may work out.

If you don't want to invest the time and energy, give up.

I don't know if this is the case for you or not, but I think most people who try to migrate to Linux dive in too quickly and then get frustrated.

If you have trouble connecting to the internet, in about 99% of cases, you have Linux-unfriendly hardware (a winmodem, a Broadcom wireless card, etc.).

Here are some steps (in this order) you can take to make your migration less painful:

1. Go back to Windows and start switching to [Windows open source applications.](http://www.opensourcewindows.org)

2. Once you're used to those, [install Ubuntu as a virtual machine inside Windows](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/virtualbox) and use that for a while so you get comfortable with Ubuntu.

3. Keep doing that for months or even years until you're in the position to buy new hardware. Do your research and buy Linux-friendly hardware or, better yet, buy Linux preinstalled.

If you want to keep driving ahead, your best bet is to start a new thread in the support area of the forum saying what model of network card or modem you have and outlining what you've already tried to do to get it to work.
