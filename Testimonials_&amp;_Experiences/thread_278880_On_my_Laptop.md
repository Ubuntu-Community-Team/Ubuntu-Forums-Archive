### Post by mlat on 2006-10-17
Well, I have a laptop which I use for college, internet, and so on. I've been a Windows user since the Windows 3.1 days... the only linux builds I played around with were Mandrake Linux (way back when, and I didn't even get it installed properly which pretty turned me off to linux back then), and Mandriva (about a year or two I had it installed, I removed it because it's not one of the greatest flavours out there). Generally, I was not very good at the whole linux thing. I even had a lot of trouble compiling programs and so on.

Well, a few weeks ago my friend said I should try installing Ubuntu. I did, however linux doesn't work too well with the recent dell computers, and I found my wireless to be completely disabled. I didn't know much about these things so I just left Ubuntu on my computer and had grub boot into windows in 3 seconds and forgot about it.

My "change of heart" as you may call it, happened when I read an interesting piece of news for Windows Vista licencing. Apparently they're making it so whenever you upgrade your motherboard, you need to get an entire new licence for like 200 bucks. Right there, I knew I had to remove my dependancy on windows. I turned right to my copy of Ubuntu I previously abandoned and got to learning commands and such for the terminal. I found enough to get me compiling programs and file muniputation through terminal, so I checked out some of the topics here for help setting up wireless, and eventually got it working due to someone's detailed step by step tutorial.

So, here I am. I've been using it for more than half a week now and I love it. The feel, customizability, and appearance all look amazing.
