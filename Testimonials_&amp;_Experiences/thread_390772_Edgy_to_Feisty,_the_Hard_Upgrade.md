### Post by drf_av on 2007-03-22
This story is so sick that I could not avoid to tell it.
After 3-4 months of happy Edgy using, I decided that it was time to take the great step and try the upgrade to the new shiny Feisty.

The pain starts when i hit "sudo "update-manager -c -d"" and I realize that I had to download 1,3GB of stuff (yes, my previous install was pretty a mess and I think what happened was the right punishment for putting all that s**t in Ubuntu), anyway, after 3-4 hours, the packages were ready.

Upgrade starts. 2 minutes later I see that almost each package has trouble with dependencies and upgrade is destined to fail. I decided to risk and hit CTRL-ALT-BKSPC hoping it was not too late.
Well, it was.

After 1-2 hours trying to solve dependencies. knowing that X wouldn't have started if I had to reboot, I decided to give up and take some sleep.

The next day

Luckily I have 64studio installed on a spare HD, and i was able to chroot into Ubuntu from there. I had a half-edgy/half-feisty distro, that actually was unable to solve dependencies for gnome-session.
I realized that I couldn't do anything.
So I decided to fresh-install feisty.
Everything worked, but, on startup, Metacity seemed not to load for my home user. I spent another 3 hours playing with init scripts, till I've done what changed suddenly my attitude. I simply said "Ok, metacity doesn't work? Then f**k metacity".
And I discovered Enlightenment.
God, since I installed linux the first time, this was the best choice I've ever done.
Now I don't even remember how Gnome looks like.
Now I got feisty, a shiny and fast desktop (with flames too :/ ), and I learned a lot of new things starting from a mistake and a failed upgrade.

Well, I think this is what Open Source is all about: learn, learn, learn.
And what's better than a mistake to learn new things?

Cheers guys  ;) Sorry for taking 2 mins in your life :|

### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-03-22
I have a similar story from about 3 years back when I started playing with Linux.  The year was 2004 and I was getting fed up with Windows XP.  So I burned a Knoppix cd and loved it.  So I searched for a good easy to use distro to start with for installation, and decided on Mandrake 9.2. Mandrake 9.2 tought me the tough lesson of rpm dependency hell.  So I thought to myself, "Hey, I have 2 hard drives installed in my computer.  Why not resize the partition that Mandrake is on and install another Linux distrobution next to it."  

Now this is were my story gets funny.  Stupid is, stupid does, I tried to resize the partition my Mandrake installation was on, *why I was using it*.  Doooo.  Shortly after I hit the button to apply the changes to my partition, my pc locked up and Mandrake 9.2 was no more.   The not so sad idiotic destruction of Mandrake 9.2 led to wonderfull experience really getting dirty learning linux with Slackware.  Slackware was the best distrobution choice I have ever made for really learning how Linux works.  Editing config files from the command line,  compiling packages from source.  Pure bliss, with the exception of all the time spend hunting down dependencies when installing packages from source.  

Sometimes the best learning experiences come from your biggest mistakes.
