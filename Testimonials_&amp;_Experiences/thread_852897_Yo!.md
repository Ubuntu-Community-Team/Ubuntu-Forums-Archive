### Post by xx.canes.rites on 2008-07-08
I have been having the hardest time with Linux, mainly because I have always used Windows OS on all my rigs and I have just recently switched over, so now I have to actually use my brain to do things (thanks for stimulating my mind!).

Freedom is just one being self responsible in his or her everyday life and that's exactly what Linux gives me, absolute freedom.Yo I have been having the most complicated time with Ubuntu but that's what life is, complicated, but with the freedom attached I have had the most exhilarating learning experience.

That alone has made Ubuntu a Googol(yes googol, it's a very large number!) times better than any other Windows operating system I have ever been on.I just wanted to say that even though I'm a computer newbie I will persistently try to learn and pass on everything I can to others within our Open Source communities. Freedom is a very old yet very new Idea and I'm glad that Microsoft's late Bill Gates has moved on and maybe now we will get a chance to expand more.

I never really understood how much of a monopoly other companies had until I switched over to a Linux Kernel OS. I'm no longer going to buy any of these ridiculous peripherals that require you to have Windows. Free Markets have always been an American tradition yet during the latter years our government has allowed so many companies to totally break those very sacred traditions of ours.

I only wish more gaming companies would build their games to allow the Linux communities to participate so we wouldn't have to go through the hassle of having to make programs so that we can play their games. I'm thinking about not even playing or purchasing their games anymore, I just downloaded some of the games that Add/Remove provided me through the Ubuntu OS so I can fool around and have some fun.

Anyway, heres my experience, it isn't very detailed only because I just switched to Ubuntu like 2 days ago but, it's still been a good one.Yo thanks for sharing such cool stuff, if I could say anything to all the people who put in work to make Open Source what it is today I would just say, "Thank You and I will use it for as long as I can to show you that it was not wasted efforts". If I knew how to program or develop some sort of source coding then I would, but since I have no idea what that is or even how to operate my OS right now I think I'll just stick to the basics and take it one step at a time.

Yo, my name is canes.rites, that stands for resistance if you change the letters up. I have been a freedom advocate for many years and now I'm applying that very idea in my everyday computing experience to further my cause. I have never even known about Linux, I always thought it was for servers only but thanks to YouTube I saw a video of Ubuntu vs Windows Vista and obviously Ubuntu blows it away. I no longer have to spend 3k US Dollars just so my computer won't explode on me when I boot up my OS. Not only that but it's also a Googol times better than Windows OS.
