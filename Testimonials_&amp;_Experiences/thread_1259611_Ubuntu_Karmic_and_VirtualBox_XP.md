### Post by AlanR8 on 2009-09-06
Just thought I'd drop a quick post about the above.

I train people, mainly in the Financial Services sector, and have to write training material from time to time.

No problems so far, but most of my clients have their own templates for composing training notes and guess what, the format's mostly Word....normally Office 2003.

I migrated to Linux about three years ago and the first time I had to write material was a NIGHTMARE! The client used a propriety font and Open Office couldn't handle it because the font wasn't installed. I started with a populated document that "captured" the font then just edited it carefully. However, the formatting often went wrong when I sent the documents to the client, mainly line spacing.

Today, I've written a session using VirtualBox XP running Office 2003.

Smooth! 

I had VB/XP open to do the writing, Songbird (Linux) open to play some tunes, Firefox (Linux) open for research, and Basket (Linux) open for notes I'd already made for insertion into the text! And it all worked seamlessly. I could copy and paste between XP and my Linux apps.

I was impressed so I thought I'd just share.......
