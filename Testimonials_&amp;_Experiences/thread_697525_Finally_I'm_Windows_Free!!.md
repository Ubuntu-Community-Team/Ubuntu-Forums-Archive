### Post by dj_padawan on 2008-02-15
I've been a Windows user for about as long as I can remember.  Recently I decided to buy a new laptop because my Dell Inspiron 700m (which I love) was getting painfully slow.  Years of patches and updates were starting to bog it down.  Booting took what seemed like an eternity.  I thought the time had come to retire it and send it off into the dark unknown.  So when shopping for a new system I couldn't bring myself to buy a Vista machine.  After about 5 minutes of playing with it at Best Buy I knew it would be nothing but a headache.  So instead I decided on a Macbook.  I point this out because it was the Macbook and Leopard that changed my perspective on what you should expect from an OS.  Which is what led me to Linux and Ubuntu.

Since I now had a Mac I couldn't bear the thought of throwing away my Dell that had been so good to me over the years.  Anyway, my sister-in-law asked if she could have it because her laptop was stolen.  I agreed and started cleaning it up of all my files.  I figured I would do a clean install of Windows so she would have everything as if it was fresh out the box.  However, I came across Ubuntu one day as I was doing research on Linux for work.  I needed to learn some basics about Linux so I decided to make my old laptop my test project.

AMAZING!  After doing the first install I was shocked at how fast the system booted and ran programs.  Pages loaded faster then they ever did even when the system was new.  The only problem was now I found myself heartbroken that I had to give up on my faithful laptop because I had promised it to my sister-in-law.  
What followed was a series of trials and tribulation that I'm sure every new user goes through when making the transition.  The following month was without a doubt the most challenging and most rewarding computer experience I've ever had.  I learned about how to tweak your desktop environment to get it to look the way that I want, not just provide me with a set of lame themes like Windows does.

I feel as if I've had Stockholm syndrome for years.  I was so stuck on Windows because I thought that because it was everywhere then they were the ones doing things right.  In some sick way we start to loved the our captors and begin to think that they are looking out for our needs.  I guess it is kind of like trusting that your government is looking out for your well being then finding out that really they only care about the people giving them money.  (But that's a rant for another time.)  

Since my Ubuntu project I've gone through my house and made the transfer from Windows to Linux and I can say that I couldn't be happier.  Never again will I have to deal with the McDonalds of the computer world.  Now I only eat organic.  Oh and anyone that says they love their Big Mac and fries needs to read "Fast Food Nation" because I don't know about you but I don't want fecal matter in my meat or my operating system.
