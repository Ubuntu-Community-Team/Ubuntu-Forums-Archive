### Post by armandh on 2008-07-06
my use of computers has changed dramatically in the last year.
friends, family members, and my son's friends who brought their computers to be fixed got the original OS back on and if they did not have the disk I had an student xp1 that finally ran out of licenses. now those without the original OS get Ubuntu.
or they can go buy whatever. It saves me time on the install as they have usually lost the driver disk as well. 

the open software movement owes two thank-yous to bill gates

first is the advance of the internet popularity [mid 90s on]
I down loaded my first Knoppix and Ubuntu with IE. compared with the first boxed $25 linux I tried and discarded this was truly free save one blank CD.

second is the poor start of vista. while it may improve the one box in our family that has it dual boots 8.04 [vista is seldom used and next I will cancel the auto renewal of the AV fee.]

Ubuntu needs just a few things easier 
those things Ubuntu cannot have and be open must install with a prompt to a click or at worst an easy copy and paste. drivers, codex, et-al.
I edit to add that medibuntu works well but takes about 6 copy/paste operations.

Ubuntu has lists of the hardware that are compatable [ot not]. sales numbers are the only thing that will force lap top makers to forgo the win-modem of wireless, Broadcom and others who make installing Ubuntu a greater chore than it needs to be. these lists exist but it needs to be featured at the top of the forum page.

ubuntu works to my needs I'm happy to recommend it, install it, and use it.
