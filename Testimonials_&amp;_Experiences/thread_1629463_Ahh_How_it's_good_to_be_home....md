### Post by ajmctaggart on 2010-11-23
I don't have a ton of time but thought this important to express.

This community, this OS, this whole thing- rocks!

I can't get over how Ubuntu in this case has completely won me over yet again.

Basically, I've been a user for nearly 5 years - in some capacity.  I've maintained XP boxes at work, used Mac OS in my personal life, etc.  About 2 years ago - I took the plunge, got a Sony Vaio Laptop- completely ditching Vista, and utilized the dual video cards switching through Ubuntu (Launchpad Team still exists ~200+ strong).  I loved to hack, but sometimes felt a need for more from my OS.  That has always been lurking in the back of my mind.

Recently, between school and work, I found an increasing need to run a bigger laptop - bought myself a Dell Latitude E6510.

I wasn't satisfied with the performance due to poor hardware support in Linux - battery life, touchpad, you name it.

Then, I got document corruption via OpenOffice that frankly scared the hell out of me.  If it weren't for Dropbox's revision history, I would have been really screwed.

So, I started trying to use the Windows 7 Install that came by default.  I got alot of my favorites back pretty quickly - VLC, Handbrake, Picasa, Skype -even though it didn't feel great, it was working.

I felt lost...not knowing where things were, not having the detailed access of how things worked. It drove me nuts. The whole idea of not really having any understanding of how things work was weird.  Just trusting them too work seemed a little too "Kool Aid-ish" for me.

Then all of a sudden - **** started breaking!
Touchpad wouldn't come back after sleep - computer blue screened after updates.  Sometimes, it was just unresponsive.  Dell setting would override Windows settings - causing things that although reboots fixed, wasn't acceptable to me on the daily.

Not to mention - when we need something here in Linux Land - we patch it.  We script it. In one way or another we gain some intimate knowledge of how our system worked.  I was loosing that and it sucked.

So, I upgraded to Maverick, I'm dealing with my hardware and battery issues, and I couldn't be happier.  I feel like I'm a part of this - I've turned a half dozen people onto Ubuntu - by no means many. But, they all use it on a weekly basis- so they are users for life.

For no other reason that when **** breaks, I know what's broken.  I thank you- I have been empowered beyond what most Windows and Mac users will ever experience.

Ahh, How it's good to be home.
Thanks to those who make this possible.
