### Post by lazymangaka on 2010-04-18
I have to say, in this the most public of public Ubuntu forums, that I am switching my laptop to Ubuntu full time--and it feels fantastic.

After rebuilding my Asus V83X notebook, I instantly embarked on trying to use a retail copy of OS X and a spare HDD I had lying around to boot a Hackintosh. 2 days and many failed attempts later, I quit, disheartened, and did a fresh install of Vista. While idly researching where my Hackintosh went wrong, a came upon a comment suggesting that one simply install Ubuntu rather than goof around any longer trying to install OS X. I shrugged my shoulders and figured I'd give exactly that a try. I hadn't really messed around with Linux since '04 or '05 and now that I had some spare hardware it was a perfect time to try again.

And quite frankly, I love it. It's perfect, and it just works in ways that Windows never has for me. The installation went off without a hitch and when the OS booted it seemed simply made for the hardware. Even the video card--normally a tricky part of any install--was supported and simply worked. 

Everything about it has simply worked. I'm leaving it on this laptop, and I'm recommending it to everyone I know. Ubuntu is simply the best operating system I've ever used.

Hey, you seem to be having the same experience I had back when I started using Ubuntu!  Man, wait until you find out what's below the surface, you are in for a real treat, I assure you that!

I have to say, though, that your experience should be put into perspective, just like others from users having a really bad time with Ubuntu.  In your case, as you rightly put it, all the hardware was working perfectly from the get go, which may not always be the case.

In other words, please tone down your excitement a bit before you pass on the message, just enough to create the right level of expectation.  Someone else may just assume their experience should go as smooth as yours, and unless they have the exact same hardware, that's assuming too much.  Don't get me wrong, I am always trying to let people know about the wonders of Ubuntu myself, but I have realized that 1 person with a frustrating experience can create lots of bad press!!
