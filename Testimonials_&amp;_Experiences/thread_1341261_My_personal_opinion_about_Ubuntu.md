### Post by Lanelor on 2009-11-29
Laptop ASUS F5R:
CPU - Intel Dual-Core T2080
RAM - 2GB
VGA - Ati Radeon Xpress 1100 (Mobile Radeon M200)
LAN - Attansic L2 Ethernet Fast-T adapter
WLAN - ASUS Integrated 802.11b/g
Camera - D-max 6a31

First, let me state that my knowledge about Linux is very limited, I have always been a Windows user, but after several stupid errors/bugs of XP ( after near 10 year of development ) I decided to give a try to the new W7, which turned out to be Vista v2. Because of this I started to look for a Linux distro that is good choice for beginners.

My first try was a Mandriva 2010, but I had missing drivers for my WLAN car and the camera. Also, the suspend option was a fiasco. After the PC sleept, I couldn't turn it back. the screen was blank, the HDD - out. No response at all. I thought there is something was burned. After several tries to start it up, suddenly, for no known reason, at least by me, it started normally. This and the hardware issues were the main reasons for letting go of Mandriva.

My second distro was Ubuntu 9.10.

Install - easy
Interface - pretty, intuitive, no remarks. 

fonts - I am not a native English speaker, and most of the sites I browse are Cyrillic. This was a problem, because the letters were merged, I needed to zoom wit Firefox to be able to read easily. Generally the fonts are great, if the text is English that is.

Programs - Ubuntu has everything, just add the codecs and it is finished ( and the True Type fonts - Cyrillic looks better with them)

Hardware support - same as Mandriva.

My WLAN is enabled, but the laptop keys for turning it ON/OFF do not work. So I have to use the manual switch. Lame

The camera is without drivers ( at least I could not find ).

The SUSPEND option is not working properly. I have to shut down the PC with a clamp!

Sorry, but such flaws are intolerable.

---

What I would like to see in future is better hardware support. Whatever the machine is, the should be drivers for it's components.

Why there are so many distros, when they are so much alike and the same problems occur in most of them (may be)?

More beta testing which can produce better and more stable releases.

---

I read an article that the idea of Linux is to provide "the best" OS. So far, it is quite far from the goal.

Free software is almost infinitely configurable..that is a key difference with proprietary operating system like windows.

What the other posters are saying is that your problems might not be insurmountable.
Granted, it would be better to have no problems at all but your issues might very well be due to auto-configration that did not happen.
Such issues are fixable with a little tweaking; you do not have to be a software developer but will probably need some guidance.

For example, there might be drivers available for your laptop camera that are not being loaded automatically as is usual and preferable.
In this case someone can walk you through determining the type of camera and loading the appropriate driver.

So I second the suggestion that you choose the issue that annoys you the most and ask for help in the support section.
Otherwise, it might be the case that this release is not the best for you; I have come to that conclusion myself.
