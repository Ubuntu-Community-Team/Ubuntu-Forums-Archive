### Post by k-bl on 2011-04-11
Ok, I have a hp p6130y, a quad core amd, with 8gig of ram.  When I was running 10.04, the thing was pretty fast, but not breaking any speed limits.  After I upgraded to 10.10, my heart fell out of my chest.  The thing was a paper weight.  SOOOO SLOW!  So slow I almost, (gulp), reinstalled 10.04.

But you know, Ubuntu has always hung in there, always pulled through, and always gotten better.  And that is what I wanted to mention.  As a community, we try new things, we install new software, we delv into new hardware... Pushing ubuntu & linux to do what it can to empower us, the very ones developing and using it.

So rather than shrink back, I decided to leap forward, and do something I had never done, push a boundary that had never been crossed!  I opened my cheapskate wallet, and purchased a well rated video card (NVidia GTX 260) and power supply in hopes that perhaps the integrated graphics card may be the culprit.

The result?













Wireless internet... Rocket speed, way faster
Video... Supreme clarity and dances with no lag to my every whim using Compiz, games, whatever
USB... For some odd reason... no problems, where before I had constant lockups.
Wallet... A little lighter, but well worth the added benefits of growing ever bit closer to my beloved Ubuntu.

It seems now no matter what I through at this system, it can take the heat, and keep on trucking along without any noticeable slowdown.


Lesson?
New video card $150
New power supply $102

Hours of relentless effort of the Ubuntu & Linux team to ensure a great user experience on experimental drivers, that are under-paid, over-worked, but know that every little bit counts?

Priceless


Ubuntu 10.10 = Awesome, great work guys!
