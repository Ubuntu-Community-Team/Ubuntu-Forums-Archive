### Post by christianxxx on 2009-11-24
After running Ubuntu for 3 years, and several years of Windowsing before that, I'm no stranger to this game. But at this point, I just want something that works. Plain and simple. I don't have time to finetune and fix. I need an operating system with that little extra to make it a success for both me and the developers.

I was very happy when the Karmic Koala finally arrived and I could install it. After running the beta on two computers at home, the time was here to upgrade.
I had experienced a few problems with flash and sound (as always), and decided to upgrade the desktop computer, and do a fresh install on the laptop.
And guess what, flash and sound issues continued after the installation. Specifically the sound crashes. I can't pinpoint a scenario, but I usually only use youtube and Spotify, and a game every now and then. And after a while it just crashes and it's usually just as well to reboot to fix.
Actually, it got to the point where I looked at other alternatives. So here it goes:

I tried Windows 7. Yes I know, it's a bit drastic, but why not?

I had heard so much great about the latest version of Windows, but was it so great I could pay money for it? The first few glimpses of it was promising and really lived up the reviews. There was always a few annoyances, like things you can do in Ubuntu which you can't do in Windows.
First challenge was to populate the start menu with useful programs, and to fill the desktop/taskbar with the information and fun I'm already used to. It proved to be almost impossible to replicate the good feeling from Ubuntu, but at least I had no woes with flash and sound. For now.

I thought that maybe a different distro might ease my pains, and I started looking at KDE desktop environment, thinking that some of the problems could be caused by the Gnome environment. First Kubuntu, then Mandriva and Fedora (both with KDE).

Kubuntu was fun. But it lacks the excellent and functional software center to make installation of new applications a walk in the park. At the time of writing, I cannot say for sure that the issues with flash and sound was as annoying as with Ubuntu, but I have this gnawing feeling that I had some of the problems. Mandriva would not run in my virtualbox, and I ended up rather bald trying to get Fedora to run the same way. But it was also crippled with a similarly useless Software Installer. Overall, it looked really great, but that's not enough.

I actually looked at OSX, but Apples are way to expensive even to consider buying. There are ways to run it on the laptop I have, but there could be all sorts of drivers missing or not functioning properly, so I never even tried.

Windows 7 was hell. It sucked the life out of my laptop, leaving it an empty useless shell not even able to log out. The memory leak might only be in Firefox or some other application, but running nothing but Firefox with a mere 30 tabs (might be a bit extravagant, but I “need” it) ate almost 2 Gb of memory, and it was actually not possible to log out unless waiting for a very long time. 
The memory leak also made flash videos lag, and sound acted very strange. This happened several times, so I know it's not a one timer.
Also, the biggest annoyance was hibernation. Hibernation settings was hidden from the standard power settings, so it took several days with irritating hibernation before I found it. Waking up from hibernation was usually smooth, but I noticed that memory usage increased greatly.

Now what? 
I back at running Ubuntu. I missed the features of my favorite GNU/Linux distro, and the memory issues of Windows was superseding my woes with flash/sound. I haven't done much differently while installing, and I suspect I will soon experience some sound issues. 
The point of telling this story is to show that Windows is not at all a walk in the park. It can be quite challenging for even the experienced users. Yes, it's pretty and polished. But is that enough? 
Maybe the reviewers should try it for more than a little half-hour, just to look beyond the polished outside and deep inside to the flaws and mistakes. 
Ubuntu isn't perfect either, and I feel pretty confident there would be something wrong with OSX as well, but at least Ubuntu feels good to me. And I know others that have converted successfully so I know I'm not off.
I hope to solve the issues with flash/sound, but I'll stick with Ubuntu anyways. :D
