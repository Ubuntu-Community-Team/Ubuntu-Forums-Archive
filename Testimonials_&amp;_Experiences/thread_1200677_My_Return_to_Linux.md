### Post by zoomy942 on 2009-06-30
First off, I will preface this with - I have used Windows for as long as I can remember.  I have seen everything from BSOD's to rootkits to even profiles being corrupt for no reason.  I had seen Linux a few times here and there but noone had taken the time to explain the advantages or power of a linux system.  Enter my older brother. 

He has used Linux longer than anyone I know and has been Windows free for probably longer than that.  He has used several different distros and when he opened my eyes to Linux, he was/is on Ubuntu 8.04.  He is easily the smartest Linux person I know, and patient I might add, as I was REALLY frustrated at first with Ubuntu and I will explain why later.  

I dont remember how the conversation started, but once I downloaded Ubuntu 8.04, let's just say I dove in head first and eyes closed.  On my HP Tablet PC, it's no small feat to get all the devices to function correctly, and with my crippled abilites due to Windows use, I was obviously frustrated.  I couldnt get the Tablet screen to work, couldnt get stand by to function, I couldnt even get more than 2 hours battery life - all stemming from the entirely new world Linux happens to be. 

So I try 8.04, then 8.10, and after 10000 reinstalls, I seem like everything is going well.  However, all the tinkering and errors has left me burnt out, so when I see Windows 7 as a free download, I jump on it.  Now let me explain something to anyone who wonders - Windows 7 feels faster, but give is 30 days.  You will be right back to where Vista was in performance.  For example, a resume from hibernate takes a whooping 2 minutes.  Yeah, you read that right and a boot up take right at 90 seconds.  Now, my machine isn't some older slower machine, it's a very up to date Tablet PC, so these numbers are worrisome.  Moreover, a Windows 7 install takes nearly 40GB of space once applications are added and updates are applied.  Wow.

Fast forward to 2 weeks ago.  I had simply forgotten about the release cycle of ubuntu, so I had forgotten about 9.04 being available.  I had used Windows 7 for several months and after a while I grew tired of long boot times and constant hard drive action.  So, I DL Ubuntu 9.04 and see if things have changed.  Well, not only is my Tablet PC screen enabled by default, but so is my CDMA data card to boot!  I must mention that before I reinstalled Ubuntu 9.04, I researched all the things I would need before hand - so I was prepared - and refused to give up this time.  Windows 7 had shown me the direction MS plans on going, and to be honest, it's just a lateral shift from Vista, no improvement or degrading at all.  

So, long story short, I installed Ubuntu 9.04 and had forgotten the speed at which it not only installs, but simply functions.  I have a boot time of just shy of 30 seconds and even with Crossover Linux (for work apps I need) everything functions flawlessly.  Even more, when I did have errors, I was able to find the answers either from the launchpad (ext4 and intel controllers) or here at the forums (updating to a newer kernel).  On top of that, I am in complete control of my computing experience.  With Linux of any flavor, I have nothing stopping me from changing anything, from the kernel to themes to compiling my own drivers for a device if i so chose, and all I need to do is learn the steps and do it; simple as that.  

Lastly, my 6GB install of ubuntu (take that Windowss, 6GB vs 40GB) provides everything I need in the package manager and then some.  I never would have learned about dozens of applications and ways to integrate them into my daily functions, without Synaptic.

So, to everyone who floats around hating Linux or being afraid of it.  Trust me, there is no need to be.  My brother was infintely patient with me and DID NOT hand hold me.  Instead he helped just enough to get me moving then watched me fight it out on my own.  There is no better way to learn Linux than to be unafraid simply do it.

I'm glad I returned to the Linux arena and don't plan on leaving anytime soon.
