### Post by Gotti on 2012-12-17
Don't get me wrong, I love linux. I've been singing it's praises to everyone since I was a kid. But at this point in my life, it's not a viable OS.

By profession I'm a graphic designer. Inkscape and Gimp are great for making icons or playing around with photo effects, but they don't come close to the versatility of the Adobe suite. (I blame Adobe for this problem...if they'd only support linux! rahhh!) I've made an honest attempt to do my work using open source alternatives, and yes it's possible, but it takes more then twice the time, and time is money, as we all know.

Secondly, I love games. Currently I'm addicted to EVE online like crack. From what I've read you can get it running on linux. But after error upon error upon error I've given up. Again, I love linux (and the community) to death, but theres something to be said for clicking an executable and it just working. I'm not saying every single game should work, but the big hitters should be supported (WoW, Diablo, CS, EVE, TL, this list goes on.) Gaming is a serious flaw of linux, although it's no fault of the open source community. Developers need to start supporting it, and until then we're all stuck with TuxRacer and Solitaire. Works for some, but not for me.

I'm fairly literate when it comes to the command line. I write shell scripts when I'm bored. When that get's old I look for new things to install, tweak, compile, and sometimes, break. I genuinely enjoy the *nix experience, and I truly wish it would work for all my needs.

I'm gonnah miss the customization and the feeling of freedom that linux provides, but I simply can't deal with the shortcomings anymore.

I'ma keep tabs on things, and I hope I'll be back! I'll miss it! Happy nixin, dudes, and merry christmas!

[venting over]
