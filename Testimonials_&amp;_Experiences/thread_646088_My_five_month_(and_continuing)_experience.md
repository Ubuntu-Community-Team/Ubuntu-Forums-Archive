### Post by Tuxoid on 2007-12-20
I have been using Ubuntu for five months now. I have had ups (choice of interfaces, zero viruses, everything free) and some downs (lots of forum members telling me to do things on the command line when not necessary). Generally, it has been great, but I feel the need to make some points about the negatives while praising the positives.

First, I'll get the negatives out of the way:

-People telling to do things in the terminal while they are easily accomplished in the GUI:

 The average user is frightened of the command line. Ubuntu developers are trying their darnedest to reduce the need to use the command line. At the same time, forum members rarely advise others to use the GUI (and even more advise to try that first). I myself am capable in the command line, being fairly technical, but with Ubuntu being targeted to average users, the command line is daunting.

Now the praise:

Choice of Interface:

Different users have their needs fulfilled by people dedicated to their specific needs. Whether you're technical, or just need a GUI, you won't be stuck without a GUI right for. I currently have both KDE and GNOME.

Zero Viruses:

Part of the reason I switched to Ubuntu, was a kernel virus in Windows. I had to run Spybot, and AVG almost every night. My terrible Windows Firewall was broken for 4 months. I have only ran anti-virus once on Ubuntu. Obviously, it didn't find anything.

Everything Free of Charge:

If you think of it, software is just information. Linux Developers are actually right in their head distributing software free of charge along with source. In my opinion, Linux is the best deal I have ever come across.
