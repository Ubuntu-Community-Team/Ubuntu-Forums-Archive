### Post by Fenris_rising on 2010-02-14
Hi chaps

Well today was bite the bullet day. Having backed up my 'important' files, and spending a couple of days running a live CD of 9.10 to check it would work with my hardware, I installed it.

All went well and with only a little research to fix minor post install issues, play DVD's and MP3's, and auto mounting my second HDD it was tinkering time. The 'little research' was required because with Linux being so stable by the time you need to do those little tweaks to get a few things working at a fresh install you have forgotten how =D

I seem to have been very lucky since my first go with 8.04 and other than 9.04 which I just didn't get on with so stayed with 8.10 and everything has worked great. Now I have dual screens, a recent change, and with compiz running to boot I have a hell of a setup, in my eyes anyway. Productivity wise it is terrific. Entertainment wise, just as good. I used to have to turn compiz off to watch DVD's but not anymore.

The ati card is the same I had when I first made the move to linux and currently it is not using any proprietary drivers but the graphics look and work even better than they did before. Fast smooth and responsive as is the whole shebang. My xorg file comprises only a dozen lines at the most =D

9.10 is just fantastic and ups the ante yet again IMHO. I do find myself wishing something would go wrong on occasion because playing with linux is nearly as fun as using it =D Great stuff! How will they top this? I also now have a 30 second boot time!

regards

Fenris
