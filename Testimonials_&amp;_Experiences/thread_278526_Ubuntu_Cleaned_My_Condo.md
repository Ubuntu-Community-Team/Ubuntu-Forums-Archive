### Post by Opsidian on 2006-10-16
I've been dabbling in various Linux distros for years now but never managed to make the big switch from Windows. 

About 5 months ago I really started playing with Ubuntu more. 3 months ago I created a 10 computer network running Edubuntu at the local museum I volunteer at. It only made sense, it would have cost around $2200 to install XP and MS Office on them and after showing some figures to the board of directors they agreed that Ubuntu had everything we needed and the cost was $2200 cheaper then installing MS software.

During the install phase at the museum I pulled out my old Thinkpad T23 that I haven't used in awhile, grabbed a few beer and proceded with a fresh install. After getting the wireless card to work I noticed that I stopped using my desktop as much. Since I do volunteer work doing web design as well I was able to get everything I needed working flawlessly on the laptop, NVU, Fillezilla, GIMP, just to name a few. I soon found myself pluggin away while lounging in the recliner whatching tv. 
So this is how Ubuntu cleaned my condo, the laptop is rather fast now and I use it 90% of the time so there was no need for the computer desk in the living room. The desktops are now hidden away in the bedroom giving the place a less then geeky appearance.  

That's what Ubuntu has done for me, given me more freedom then I had before. As a result Ubuntu has been installed on no less then 30 systems in my area, 15 of those I played a hand in installing and the rest are from people I have converted. Thank you Ubuntu!
