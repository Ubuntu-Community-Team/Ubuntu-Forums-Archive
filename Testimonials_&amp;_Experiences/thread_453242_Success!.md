### Post by sprinkles on 2007-05-24
I've just come from a friend's house after installing Ubuntu for him on his laptop. Me and a few other of his friends use it, so he wanted to see what the fuss was about.

Everything went fine, I downloaded the ISO (Although had to change mirror to the Virgin media one, as the Canonical one was very slow) and burnt it to a disk.

I had to change his BIOS settings to boot from disk, but he was amazed when it booted straight into a usable operating system. He was even more amazed when the restricted drivers dialog popped up informing him that his wireless card was working OK.

I then installed it for him (which went very quickly, about 15 minutes from starting the installer to booting into the new desktop).

He loves the look of it - even more so when I turned on desktop effects (that one click thign is fantastic - when I tried to do it it was pages of commands!).

He's by no means a computer novice - he can use Windows very well, and knows some programming stuff, but anything outside Windows was alien to him (even re-installing Windows). 

He's settled into Ubuntu right away, though.
