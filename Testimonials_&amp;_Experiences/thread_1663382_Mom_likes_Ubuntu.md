### Post by travissparks1307 on 2011-01-09
My Mom wanted a computer for awhile. She had not used one in probably 6 years or better. Well, I had an Acer laptop that was new-ish, (mfg 12/2009) and had Ubuntu 10.10 installed. Then I got a NEW notebook that smoked it. So I gave the Acer to my Mom with a fresh install as an X-Mas gift. 

She loves it. 

The fact she hasn't used a computer for so long has actually helped her learn Ubuntu. She isn't used to doing things the "Windows" way, so she was a blank slate. In her own words "This is a breeze to use" she loves the fact she doesn't have to worry about things like viruses, or malware, and once I walked her through Gwibber and the Me Menu, it was over. Of course now she's on Facebook and I have to deal with *that* but... 

Just goes to show the power of Ubuntu. Someone who was completely computer illiterate is now enjoying the benefits of a computer, safely, and easily. She mostly uses it for the web, but also some music and keeping track of things like finances. Of course on the *rare* occasion she has a problem, she has her Ubuntu geek son on speed dial, and it usually takes less than 5 minutes to clear it up.

Ubuntu was a perfect fit. I cannot praise it enough. Neither can she.

### Post by chriswyatt on 2011-01-09
I do believe Ubuntu's best for either absolute beginners or computer geeks, people in between who are used to using Windows and software that only works on Windows may find it difficult to switch, and also people who use lots of production software like the Adobe suite etc., geekier more advanced users have more patience and are better at finding alternative ways to get the job done.  People like my dad (basically a beginner) can browse the internet just as they do on Windows and they're less likely to download a virus in the process.  My Dad was always installing dodgy software and going on untrustworthy websites, and numerous times I've had to try and remove the dodgy software that he's managed to install on there.  In the end I thought I'd stick Ubuntu on there for him alongside Windows and as I was around if he ran into any problems he could ask me, and he's been happy with it ever since.  He loved how much faster it was compared to XP which was starting to run like a slug, also it doesn't take much to p*ss off my Dad so he's also happy that there aren't loads of annoying notifications popping up all over the place (update this, update that etc.).  Also if he didn't like it I could just set Windows back to the default and the Ubuntu partition could be there as a backup in case he buggered up the Windows partition again.

I never even bothered recommending Ubuntu for my brother as I doubt he'd have much patience for it, and also he'd be annoyed that iTunes doesn't work.
