### Post by exploder on 2009-10-29
Congratulations to the Ubuntu Developers and everyone that contributed to the 9.10 release cycle. Karmic Ubuntu, Kubuntu and Xubuntu are fine releases that turned out very well. 

I never would have thought things would turn out so well after the flurry of bug reports that were submitted in the last few weeks of development! The Developers stayed focused and pulled off the impossible by integrating so much new technology and making it work! 

The introduction of XSplash really turned out nice and it does enhance the boot process, there is also the cool factor because Ubuntu was the first distro to use this approach. The Ubuntu family of releases also are leading the pack in boot times, this is a nice accomplishment and makes Linux more competitive in the world of operating systems.

I appreciate all of the hard work that went into fixing the Intel graphics issues that plauged the last release. My low end on-board Intel graphics preforms just as good as my Wife's high end NVidea graphics on her Windows gaming machine. Multimedia playback is smooth, even with compositing enabled. I know it took a great deal of time and effort to get Intel graphics working without causing other graphics card users any problems.

There are way too many improvements to mention in this thread. I just wanted to express my thanks and congratulate everyone on a fine job. Karmic has met the goals that were set for it! :D
