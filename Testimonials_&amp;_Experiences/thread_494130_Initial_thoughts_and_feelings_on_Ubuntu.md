### Post by Apnu on 2007-07-06
I have a interesting past with Linux.  I was dragged, kicking and screaming, into the fold way back in the heady days of Red Hat 4.2, that is about 1998 folks. How I got into Linux is a long, but funny story that I'll save for a different post.

Since then I've been a pretty loyal Red Hat user.  I used every version of Red Hat from 4.2 to 9.0.  In 2000 I flirted with FreeBSD for a bit, but my experience with RH brought me back. Then RHEL came along and I didn't like the idea much, it reminded me of Win2k enough that I cast about for another distro.  I tried SUSE for a while, then the Fedora Project came along and restored my faith in Red Hat.    Still looking for a distro to run my servers on, I found CentOS 3 and have used that ever since (now deploying CentOS 5 servers).

And, of course, my career in tech support / sys administration / network administration has kept me current with Microsoft 'products'.

Over the years I've thought about trying Debian releases (and Slackware too) but could never stick a toe out of my cozy Red Hat blanket -- I was a happy camper and wanted to stay that way.  Also I mainly used Linux in a server capacity with a lot of success.  I had, unfortunately, never been to comfortable in Linux as a workstation.  Between my  tech support duties, a bad gaming habit, and some laziness, I kept coming back to Windows.   I find its easier to support my lusers if I can mimic their environment, as a result, I stayed in whatever Windows OS was tops at the time.

So that was my world: Linux servers and Windows workstations. Microsoft has given me a sense of loathing for almost a decade now, but with that much negativity, you get numb to it after a while. I had resigned myself to a life with Windows. However, virtualization projects like VMWare and VirtualBox started to change my mind.

I've been aware of the Ubuntu project for a while, and when Feisty came out, along with a soon to be released Fedora 7, I thought I'd give them a test drive. I decided that with VirtualBox I can have a windows support environment, and I had the itch to switch anyway.   The idea of Ubuntu's live CD and install media was very interesting to me, plus it came out before Fedora so I dropped the disk in my Thinkpad T43 and let it rip.

Wow, was I impressed.  Ubuntu's environment was warm and inviting from the start.  Everything was easy to find and fresh looking.  The system worked out of the box, and I didn't have a single weird issue on my ThinkPad (unlike Fedora Core 6 which took a bit of banging around to get going) I felt like I had come out of a fog.  The next thing I knew, I was installing it (after backing up all my crap in XP) and not looking back.

When Fedora 7 came out, that old Red Hat blanket called me, old habits are hard to break.  I played around in their live CD, but it felt, I dunno, stale to me.  Plus, having to download a different ISO just to install once I decided I liked it seemed clunky.  So, I've put the blanket way in the closet and I am feeling good about it.

Ubuntu is, easily, the best personal/workstation Linux distro I've ever encountered.  It blows the doors off everything else I've seen and Windows Vista doesn't even compare.  The only other thing I've ever seen with such a little learning curve is Mac OS 10, which does impress me greatly.  Feisty stuck me as quickly as my wife got stuck on Tiger.  I've never had that feeling with a Linux distro, it was like I was coming home.  Its good to be here, I hope Ubuntu hangs around for a very long time.
