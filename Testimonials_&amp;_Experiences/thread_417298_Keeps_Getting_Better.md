### Post by seeklinux on 2007-04-21
I installed Feisty on a new Thinkpad T60. I have an older laptop with Edgy.  So the good:
[LIST]
[*]Set the resolution correctly
[*]Hibernation seems  to work well
[*]Standby seems to work well
[*]Sound card works
[*]It detected my network printer
[/LIST]

I got wireless working (intel wireless 3945abg) but surprisingly it didn't happen automatically. I had to follow directions posted for previous versions and edit the /etc/network/interfaces, commenting out all but the l0 entry. Upon reboot, Network Manager detected wireless networks and allowed me to enter the WPA passphrase and get connected.

On my previous laptop I had to use ndiswrapper with the card I had. From reading various posts it appears this is still a problem for many users. Mandriva seemed to do a better job of helping you set up ndiswrapper when I last installed it a couple of years ago. 

I was able to get my new QuickCam for Notebooks Deluxe web camera working with the help of the Michael Xhaard driver.

I have been using Linux for years and am willing to jump through quite a few hoops to get things working, but I have to say each release seems to require less and less of that. Also, my boot-up time is significantly less than a minute now.  

I think a non-technical user should be able to learn to use Ubuntu pretty easily. Depending on the hardware, it may or may not be realistic for them to install it, especially if they have cards requiring the use of ndiswrapper.  

I got another Thinkpad because I like the trackpoint; otherwise I might have gone for a System76 machine with Linux pre-installed.  It would be interesting to see some bigger players offering Ubuntu pre-installed.

Good work by the Ubuntu team!
