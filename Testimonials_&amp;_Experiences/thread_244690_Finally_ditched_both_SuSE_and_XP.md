### Post by rabidwalrus on 2006-08-26
Hey, I just ditched both SuSE and XP a week ago to install Ubuntu, and let me tell you that's one decision I'll never regret. Windows XP has viruses and spyware galore, not to mention having to be in a perpetual state of being sent binary globs in the form of "updates" every few days. SuSE was alright, except that the Gnome desktop is not nearly as smooth as ubuntu's, and it's bloated and I couldn't for the life of me get DVD's to work. I followed countless instructions all over the web... No luck. SuSE seemed to lock up a lot for no apparent reason, and I was always having to switch out CDs whenever I wanted to install something. With ubuntu, I get a nice slick debian based distro that comes on a single disc that's a combined livecd/installcd, and doing any sort of administration work is easy. The community is great and friendly to newcomers. I've been using Linux for two years now and Ubuntu (combined with the automatix installer) is the only distro that comes close to a replacement for Windows. Sure, Linspire and xandros can offer a debian based distro, but $50? And they're ugly, too. Just take a look at the screenshots. Why pay $50 for something that you can get for free and is better?

I use ubuntu for gaming, word-processing, web-browsing, anything that I would do on windows only without viruses!

To sum it up: Ubuntu is free (as in freedom and beer, and canonical will even ship you cds for the low price of $0), has a great community, is easy to use and is fast. So the question is, why aren't you using ubuntu? You have no reason not to try it, as the livecd won't destroy your current os and it's free (I mean, free cds)
