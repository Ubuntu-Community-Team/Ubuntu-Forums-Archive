### Post by canoemoose on 2008-10-22
This may well be the wrong place for this thread, but if so someone will move it.  Long story, no short version, here goes:

One of my friends who is a Ubuntu supporter like me made an interesting point in conversation recently.  He said something along the lines of
"I think Ubuntu is trying to be not Linuxy (!).  I mean, there's Windows, Mac, Linux and there's Ubuntu.  Sort of like there's Linux, over there; we're Ubuntu, over here.  We're not Linux.  Everyone knows that Linux is complicated and hard and wild and stuff.  We're not.  We're tamed.  We're Ubuntu."

I think his point is an interesting one.  Taken as he said it a positive thing, slightly tongue-in-cheek as an experienced Linux user, it was an interesting view as to how he saw Ubuntu's image.  He reckoned the whole "Linux for human beings" thing was what gave him that image.  Comments??

This leads nicely on to my second point.

In my A level Computing class is a Fedora fan(boy) who may or may not be called Lee.  He reckons that Ubuntu is Linux's version of Vista and doesn't teach any skills transferrable to what he calls "real" Linux distributions.  Being a fanboy, he has a loud and often-stated opinion on everything ("I mean, KDE is so obviously better than Gnome.  I can't believe you use it", "p/q/r is more important than x/y/z) and anyone talking to him feels they have to justify their own opinion.  When I finally get sick of him and give the good old "Fine.  You use what you want to, I'll use what I want to" he goes and picks on someone else, someone I helped get Ubuntu up and running about a month ago who we'll call Garric as that is his handle on these forums.  Whereas I remain unaffected by Lee, Garric feels uncomfortable by Lee's onslaught and almost loses confidence in himself, simply because Lee calls him a noob if he doesn't know the difference between sigkill and sigterm for example.
This I feel upset? annoyed? by as Lee's idiotic elitism is driving away other potential users.  Comment??

</rant>

That is more a stream of conciousness than a post, but hell, it does me good!!

### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-10-23
> **canoemoose said:**
> And comments on the first point?

It's not a wrong point of view. Ubuntu does try to move away from the "Linux is hard" stigma. However, this stigma itself is becoming deprecated. For most types of hardware, installing and using Mandriva, Fedora, Sabayon and even Debian is a breeze nowadays.

It's also worth noticing that Ubuntu could be *even easier* for the Windows user and Canonical is aware of that. They could install proprietary drivers and software by default, make more Windows-like configuration wizards, etc. However, the "Linux for human beings" motto is much more than a matter of user-friendliness. Ubuntu tries very hard to keep a balance between satsifying non-savvy users and sticking to the principles of open source and free software. Including mp3 codecs by default, for example, would involve both problems with freedom (as they are proprietary) and licensing (the only way to solve it would be charging for Ubuntu in the countries where the patents hold, which would be against Ubuntu's philosophy of being free of charge).

Granted, this is a very risky route and the side effect is that it displeases people on both sides. Stallman hates that Ubuntu includes firmware binary blobs. New users tend to complain about the lack of a Windows-like this and that.
