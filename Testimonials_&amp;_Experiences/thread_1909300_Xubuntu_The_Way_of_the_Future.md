### Post by Maximus559 on 2012-01-15
After months of deliberation (read: indecision), I've finally made the move from Ubuntu to Xubuntu, and so far I am very, very pleased. 

I'd been using Ubuntu 10.04 on my primary desktop since it came out, and I would have been happy to stick with that version had it not been for the frozen software repositories. However, my upgrade path became unclear after 11.04 was released, as I am a stubborn resident of the anti-Unity camp and no longer see vanilla Ubuntu as a viable option

Yes, I could have simply upgraded to Ubuntu 11.10 and then installed a different desktop environment, but I'm really not in the mood for that level of tweaking. I did mess around with openSuse 11.4 briefly, but KDE 4.6 just never felt right. Ultimately, I settled on Xubuntu 11.10, and as I've said, I think I made the right decision.

So, for all the Ubuntu users out there who want to move away from 10.04 or 10.10 but aren't thrilled with the prospect of switching to Unity, I strongly encourage you to give the latest Xubuntu a try. The interface is almost identical to the old Gnome and is easy to customize, and all the standard Ubuntu applications are available in the good old Software Center.

I might even go so far as to suggest that once the touchscreen fad is over, the Ubuntu developers might think about making Xfce the primary desktop environment and either abandon Unity or relegate it to the mobile sphere where it belongs. I'm not interested in starting yet another Unity debate - no point in beating what must by now be a very, very dead horse. I just think that Xfce might be the best way forward for Ubuntu as a distro. I, for one, will certainly be sticking with Xubuntu for the foreseeable future.
