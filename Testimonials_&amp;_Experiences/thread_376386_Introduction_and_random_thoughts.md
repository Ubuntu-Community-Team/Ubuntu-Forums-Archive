### Post by TheGameAh on 2007-03-04
First off, I’m neither anti-MS or pro-Linux.  Just wanted to get that out of the way.

I’m an IT admin who knows a lot about MS software.  That’s all we basically use.  But I’ve always been interested in Linux.  Heard a lot of good and bad about it, but basically for time reasons, just never got the chance to play with it.

The app that really pushed me to try it was Beryl.  Not sure if that’s good or bad, but there it is.  Seeing videos of it impressed me, and I just wanted to see what it was all about.

My journey began with FC6.  I was a little familiar with Fedora, didn’t hear too much bad about it, so I downloaded the DVD iso and got moving.

The kernel bug stopped me in my tracks.  Couldn’t load 3rd party drivers, ick.  This turned me off right away.  That, and Evolution didn’t have native Exchange support.  After tinkering just a little, decided that FC wasn’t for me.

So downloaded the Live CD of Edgy.  Very impressed right away with the simple fact you could play with Ubuntu without installing right off the CD.

So installed Ubuntu, then Beryl, and started playing.  Solid, all around.  OpenOffice, Evolution, etc., just all very impressive.  I’ve been running Ubuntu with Beryl for my office machine for about a month now.  It’s amazing software.  Now when I work on a friend’s computer, I’m trying to scale windows or rotate a cube.  I see all kinds of arguments about how useful is Beryl.  Well one simple thing, Beryl makes things fun again.  And after the fun wears off, it's still productive.

That’s about it, just wanted to kinda introduce myself.  There are downsides.  I think Office 2003 has a slight edge on OpenOffice (but not Office 2007.  What a nightmare.  Took me 10 minutes just to figure out how to File – Open).  I think, for the average user, that Linux as a hole is just a little behind.  It took some terminal tweaks to get things the way I wanted, which I just don’t think the average user could do, or would want to.

However, Vista I think is doomed to fail.  It just doesn’t offer that much over XP, which is another tiny reason I wanted to try Linux, see what I was missing.

On a final note (just kinda rambling at this point), I must say how impressed I am with the community, these forums in particular.
