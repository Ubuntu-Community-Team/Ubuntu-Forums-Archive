### Post by stalkier on 2009-04-02
Ok so I am not a hardcore Linux user. I like things to be easy, look good, and just work for me with little trouble. Linux did NOT start out like this. I have been using Ubuntu since version 5.10. I have really enjoyed the experience for the most part. Yes I have run into a glitch or two from time to time. However, I have used the Forums to my advantage. After all this time I have had NO problems that could not have been fixed. Can I say this about Windows? Not really. I believe that each OS has its strengths. I am currently using Vista Ultimate 64bit (runs perfectly), and Windows 7 beta (also runs perfect). Both run awesome on 2 different PCs. I believe that Win7 will be an truly awesome OS that will blow Linux out of the water. Does this mean that Linux is doomed? Not a chance. How much is Win7 going to run us? 2, 3, 6 hundred??? Who really knows? My point is Linux is free and you get what you pay for. Yes it can't run Adobe CS4 apps, it can't run this and can't run that. Who cares? I am a very resourcefuly person. I use what I have to for the work I do. Sometimes cost causes us to compromise. I can't use Adobe CS3 but I can run Macromedia Dreamwever 8 perfectly in WINE. Does that make me mad? Nope. It gets the job done. I believe in the open source community. I have even stopped using Office 2007 and switched to OO.o 3. Guess we all have our own tastes. I like the taste of Ubuntu. :D

### Post by goettlek on 2009-04-02
You've got a good point.

Too many times I hear people complain about how "Oh well Linux can't run __________" or "I'd use Linux but I can't play __________." They instantly think it's not worth it.

To be honest, I feel like using Linux is safer. My computer feels like it runs better and applications seem to load faster. For example, I'd been using Microsoft Office 2007 in Windows Vista Home Premium 32-bit. I switched over to Kubuntu and started using Open Office. I find it runs better and quicker and still accomplishes all the tasks I need it to. I'm not working in an office or anything, just writing the occasional research paper or other document for school. I find that if it runs better, saves me money, and still does all I need it to, why not use Linux.

However, I disagree with you on the Windows 7. I have somewhat lower expectations than you do for it. The betas that have been coming out look exactly like Vista, just fancier and with more visually appealing eye candy. Granted, I've never tried one of the betas, but I've still done research on it and don't feel as optimistic for it's release. Do I think it will be a big improvement on Vista? Yeah, you could say that. Will it completely give Windows its reputation back it had in the days of XP? No. The betas are still 15GB, much more than (k)(x)Ubuntu at 2-4GB. My Kubuntu 8.10 installation with 1 extra gig of audio production software as well as maybe half a gig of Java updates only took up 4GB of my 20GB. 

However, my computer is just an Intel Core 2 Duo 1.5GHz with 2GB of RAM and integrated graphics, so it may be blinding my views of Vista. I guess if it runs great on your system, more power to you, man. I have a friend with a 2.5GHz Intel Core 2 Duo with 4GB of RAM and a nVidia 9800. Vista runs like a dream and can run high demanding games and applications like Crysis. If I had a computer like that, yeah I probably wouldn't care to try Linux except for the fact you don't have to shell out for virus protection.

Also, I think you hit the nail on the head with "We all have our tastes." It's absolutely true! Linux isn't for everyone. Whether it be a piece of software (Like Photoshop CS4 that you mentioned), a game (Crysis, CoD4, etc.) or a piece of hardware (most likely brand new hardware), there are certain circumstances where Linux isn't for you. It might have nothing to do with software, maybe you get all bothered at the sight of a terminal prompt or don't like Gnome/KDE/Xfce or whatever, if Windows suits you better then by all means use it. Like I said, more power to you.

I don't think that the release of Windows 7 will boost it's market share over Linux by too much, but you may see a bit of an increase. Two different beasts will remain two different beasts.
