### Post by Ric95 on 2007-05-11
My best experience with Linux has been with Ubuntu 6.10 ! [edit- I meant 6.06 LTS]
It didn't look as good, it didn't have the nice driver manager, but it was the most stable. In fact the only thing that could break it was updating!
I would go back except that I need apps from the newer repositories.... and that brings me to the point.
Ubuntu is the only Linux in a position to break the big bad monopoly, but it never will because of dynamic libraries.
   For an OS to 'just work' it should run apps that are compiled from static libraries. It it frustrating when I update a lib for one driver or app, and that lib doesn't work for another driver or app!. Also its frustrating looking at newer apps in the newer repositories and knowing that installing it wouldn't work unless I ugrade the libs and therefore break other stuff. :mad: 
Ubuntu is the only one that can do this:
Give preference the apps that are static compiled! 
 the reward will be that you may only need one repository, the OS will be smaller and more stable. 
And that will beat M$.

I second the preference towards static-libraries. They work. Who cares if a program which was 300k is now 2mb because it has built-in libraries?
