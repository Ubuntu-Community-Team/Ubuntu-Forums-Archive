### Post by Macchi on 2009-01-24
I work presently as IT-manager for four different business enterprises having from three to fifty people. Our path towards modernization and efficient development of these companies goes through stepwise growth in the adoption of open source and free software. 

This is a forum for Ubuntu, but firstly I would like to mention that success in that area is due to the joint achievements of GNU, Linux, Debian, Ubuntu (Canonical and the community), OpenOffice.org, and many people and organizations that see free open source software as an exciting and productive paradigm. 

There is a lot to tell about this, I probably could write a book about it, but I am very busy actually managing the practical aspects of this transition. Thus here are som fragments and I may add more information later on, based on the type of response we see around here.

The overall trend is to start with the hidden parts without so much fuzz about it. Thus FOSS first enters the server room and later eventually reaches the desktops. This is the safe way, since GNU-Linux/BSD servers simply work, and offer high flexibility, increased reliability and lower costs. To mention a few services it is easier to start with webservers, fileserver, printerservers, primary domain controllers, firewalls, filters, proxies and backup solutions. I Forgot CMS and CRM solutions as part of the arsenal.

A complete transitions to a new desktop environment is another complex story, but it has already started in many places. Start with Firefox, OpenOffice.org and Thunderbird. After some time it may be possible to switch to a new desktop environment and Ubuntu is an excellent candidate.
But the psychological resistance to change from most human users is the most important barrier to increased adoption of GNU-Linux among business enterprises. (could be continued)




Well, this was a start. Please tell us more about you project and its goals so that we can feel more confident on releasing more information to the public.
