### Post by 3Miro on 2010-04-22
I used Gnome (Ubuntu) and KDE (Kubuntu) in 8.10 and 9.04. Then in 9.10 I included XFCE (xubuntu) to the list. Ubuntu in general is geared towards Gnome and that is why kubuntu often has trouble. XFCE, however, uses the same basic tool kit (GTK) as Gnome, except it is made to be smaller and faster. In many ways XFCE is just faster Gnome. There are a few minor differences like Gnome has drag and drop feature from the menu to the panel or desktop to make a shortcut and in general Gnome seems to have more eye candy (even though compiz runs with XFCE), however, I think for 10.04, I will be using XFCE exclusively.

KDE is more different from the other two. It looks more like windows vista/7, but it requires more resources. There seems to be something like a minimum hardware requirements for KDE to run, but once you satisfy those, it runs great. Some would argue that KDE is the best looking, others would say it is the worst. Some find it very functional, others hate it. For me, I like KDE a lot, but I like XFCE's speed better.

As for the apps, you can run KDE, Gnome and XFCE apps on any of the other two.

### Post by XubuRoxMySox on 2010-04-24
I'm on Xubuntu Lucid RC now. It's as good as Karmic! It now includes PulseAudio (and it was smart of them to withhold it from Xubuntu karmic until it was ready), so the newest version of Skype will work nicely with it. It also ships with Abiword and Gnumeric instead of the huge, complicated, and somewhat bloated OpenOffice suite (and those two are *more* than adequate for all my schoolwork and dance cue sheets and stuff). 

It seems to me that independent developers at Xubuntu have consistently managed to avoid the persistent bugs and pitfalls of the standard Gnome and KDE 'buntus. 

Xubuntu is a "step child" that doesn't enjoy the same status as its siblings. Canonical's ShipIt service, for example, offers free Ubuntu and Kubuntu CDs, but *not Xubuntu* - they had to make their own arrangements with another vendor, but you can get free Xubuntu CDs from On-Disk.com. It is developed independently and rarely ever the includes buggy, Beta-quality crud that plagues its more privileged counterparts, Ubuntu and Kubuntu. That alone is one of the best reasons to use it. Plus, I always root for the "underdog."

Loving Lucid,
