### Post by dvn3ch on 2009-09-29
Years ago, I bought a PC with the intention of downloading my music collection, write some emails, make a family budget, and probably look at some porn ;).  I assumed in the beginning that I would find myself after a while becoming very efficient with the PC and probably go on to bigger and better things as time marched on.  Besides, I cut my teeth on a Commodore 64, I was a geek down in my heart just chose fast cars and fast women over the keyboard as the years pressed on. 

So I bought a HP desktop with Windows 95 (I think).  I had my share of completely disappointing times, i.e. HDD blow-outs, OS crashes due to crap programs, viruses, etc, etc. :(  This went on for several years until one day I took a look at myself and said, "I am much bigger and better than I was when I started this whole thing."  However, the bigger was my waist line and better was in the form of virus killing and "preventitive medicine" programming.  What I have become is a Windows doctor.  Why am I constantly looking after this sick patient (MS Windows) that continues to lure me into its next disease (XP, Vista, Win7,etc).  Why can't I just enjoy my time in front of the computer without worrying about my files being corrupted because I DL a cool bitmap of my favorite MMA fighter?  Why this cat-n-mouse game all the damn time?  :(

That is when I decided to look for something else.  I was surfing the net one day and found this forum.  I peered into almost every thread and read almost every word (almost).  It was at that moment that I took the plunge and broke away from microsoft.  As many of you may have done, too, I started with an old LT that had outdated everything (Gateway Solo 9300...) which I tried earlier to put XP on but crashed repeatedly on anything MS Office (imagine that).  After many reloads of the OS and MS Office, I reverted back to the WinNT that came on it.  I figured that this was a perfect test bed to see what Ubuntu was all about. 

My first try was installing Hardy Desktop Edition.  Eventhough I checked the Live CD on my main PC and even booted from the disk to check it out several times, the LT froze trying to load it up.  I returned to my favorite forum and found that someone had installed the Server Edition and then installed the GUI afterwards.  That worked perfectly.  Once gnome was installed the LT was purring like a kitten.  I made a few tweaks here and there, added a little more RAM just to speed it up (it only had 64mb, Ubuntu site states 128mb minimum).  I was impressed that the OS even ran when there was not even the minimum required RAM installed.   I eventually gave this LT to my daughter (she's  who has had no problems adjusting to the new OS (she has not been indoctornated into the microsoft school of crash-happy OS).  

I then set my sights on an older (not junk) Dell with the latest XP and all the goodies.  I wiped the HDD and installed Hardy.  It loaded in 9 minutes.  In 10 minutes I was surfing the web.  IMPRESSIVE!!!  I have loaded many microsoft OSes in my day and I can honestly say that I have never went from start to finish in 10 minutes.  After using this rig for a month or so, I decided that I was going to turn my main rig into a Ubuntu machine and make this Dell a web host server.  So I dual booted the big rig and installed Server Edition into the Dell, which I added the gnome desktop just to have an extra rig on the table (simultaneous web surfing and email while I work on the big rig).  The dual boot was okay and I had some issues with programs that used all the time that would not work in WINE.  So, again, I looked to my favorite forum and found a fellow that stated he had the same problems as me and went to XP on virtual machines.  I decided that was my answer.  I did it and I cannot be happier.  It does everything I need and then some.  If there is an application that I need that is strictly MS and there is no way around it or WINE just refuses to interact with it, I just install it into the VM XP Pro and away I go.:)

To date:  I have only one XP-only rig (my wife's Sony LT) left in my house.  It is for this reason that I still have a subscription to Norton's.  The funny thing about it is, that she had a Trojan Horse come in through a Script from a shopping site (so she says).  Even with Norton's, Window Washer, and MalwareBytes running constantly, she still got infected.  I have been running three rigs with no "active" virus scanning programs for over 6 months and have yet to get any infection, crashes, or simple brain-dead lock-ups for a given period of time.  I cannot say enough about Ubuntu.  Even when there seems to be little support for something that is hooked to it or running on it, there is someone that has a good idea and a little code to get me started.  This forum is great and this OS is great and I really couldn't care if I was the only one who says so.

'Till the next time...Rock on with Ubuntu...or suffer with the MS headache in the morning.

Nice to read. Welcome to the free kingdom ;-)

Just for your records: you used a server install in your LT as the base system, which isn't recommended for a later build of a graphic desktop environement. The reason is that server edition has a slightly different kernel, lacks some modules, acpi, and some more things. The recommended path is to install a base c.l.i. system from the standard live cd. [See here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Installation/LowMemorySystems") for details.
