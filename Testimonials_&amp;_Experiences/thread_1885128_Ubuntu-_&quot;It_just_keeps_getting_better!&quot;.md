### Post by jimmydean886-2 on 2011-11-22
When I was first browsing for a laptop to replace an old 2002 Toshiba Portege with 64 mb ram, I stumbled upon a deal. A brand new Toshiba (I like them) for $375. The only drawback was that this specific model was said not to work too great with Ubuntu. Of course, I went with it, originally starting with Fedora, because I tried Ubuntu and it didn't work. I just got so fed up with it, that I inserted the same live cd for 10.10 (which was the latest at that time) and it worked. The next day, I came into school with a laptop that ran linux with flash, MP3 Support, and all sorts of other great Ubuntu features that just don't exist in Fedora. All of a sudden, working with my computer went from being a pain, to simply amazing and fast. Originally I had to edit a file to convince the sound in Ubuntu to work through headphones, but with Natty, that problem went away, then with Oneiric, all of the display problems I was having went away as well.

I'd just like to congratulate all of the developers working on Ubuntu, and Linux, and all of the components within. You've all done an excellent job!
