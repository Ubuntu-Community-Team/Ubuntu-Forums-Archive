Not sure what your response expectations are. But I would suggest "You Use What Works For You". Regardless of what may or may not be the problem. It's been my experience, that in my professional life when I'm using a product that has historically proven itself - a replacement must out perform said product. Not meet or duplicate, but actually perform significantly better.

I'm a big fan of not making comparisons between OS's. I would hate to think that Canonical sees Windows as a standard or that a user would feel the need to defend a product, by pointing out the deficiencies in another product. I find it sad when a user feels the need to defend a product by comparing "faults", instead of simply pointing out the benefits of said product.

If your truly concerned and know that a certain product will meet your expectations, why discontinue using it? When using what works, it often reduces the potential for failure and gives one confidence. Like many products, Ubuntu may not work for everyone or on all hardware. Should you change to Windows, it will be a personal choice based on your experience. Hopefully, one that will meet your needs and expectations.

Good Luck with whatever you decide on.

Just my $0.02
