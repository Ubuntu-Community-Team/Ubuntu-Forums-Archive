### Post by Knuckleball on 2010-08-03
I have to say to the community... 

I've spent countless years on Windows. I even remember it back when it was 3.1. I've even migrated as far as Windows 7. Until one day i finally got fed up with it. I decided to try Linux. Based on my knowledge of its users SWEARING by it & my exposure to it on hackaday.com. (not trying to spam you)

Truth be told, i thought it was going to be pretty bad. Not because it was a bad product. But because its an entirely new system which i wasn't familiar with. I expected a big learning curve. But despite that, i jumped in anyway. I wasn't going back to windows. 

And i have to say, this is literally a work of art. Not only aesthetically, but even down to its deepest corners. Its a work of **genius**. Sheer _***brilliance***_. I have absolutely no idea why 1. Its even bashed in the first place. and 2. Why MORE people aren't using this? I can only speak for myself in saying the transition was simple to me. There was NO learning curve. Some may disagree? But thats how it was for me. 

I really do strongly believe, this absolutely has the potential to leave Microsoft in the dust. This is a system with limitless potential. It's clearly the future. I've successfully convinced 17 people to switch over in the past few months alone. My own experience has been the same for them. They can't get enough of it... and there are so many different flavors i/we have yet to try... 

I find it pretty.. neat.. that even I can make a difference. That this is a project which everybody can contribute to. (i absolutely plan too) So i don't have just one person to thank for this, but rather all of you. so.

Thanks, all your work will not go unforgotten. :)
