### Post by tpho2500 on 2010-06-04
Hi,

I am a former Windows user. I have been on XP for about 3 years. Vista 1 year and 7 1 year. XP has served me well and had everything I could ever ask for. Times changed and I moved to Vista which IMO was not as strong as XP. 

Windows 7 came out months later which I quickly grabbed. I thought to myself that it was everything I ever dreamed of. As you all know MS still has quirks to solve in Windows 7. I remember one time my boot file got messed up (deleted) and I was not able to access my hard drive/files. It was the most terrifying moment of my life. I called up support and the guy on the phone was like my only option is to reformat and reinstall everything. I felt like was going to throw up. 

A day later my free Ubuntu CD came in the mail. I had totally forgot I had ordered it. I popped it in and booted into LiveCD. I noticed that I could access my HD and all of my important files. I felt so relieved. 

After a couple months my hard drive began failing. It has been 2 years since I got it. My computer was not able to detect it anymore. I didn't know what to do, so I said, why not try that LiveCD? It worked, I backed up all my files onto an external HDD and got a new one. 

Not long ago some of my programs I installed began giving me error messages every time I started Windows. I got sick of seeing the same pop ups so a couple days I ago I realized that Ubuntu is the only OS that I can rely on. I have been using Ubuntu since the last week of May until now. 

All I can say is that Ubuntu is blazing fast. Boot up is like 30 seconds, shutdown is like 5, and applications open up immediately. I have no regrets for permanently switching to Linux. It's hard at first letting go of an interface you recognize so well but it is well worth it.
