### Post by naelurec on 2007-06-07
I helped convert over a school to Linux last year ... they have a Windows terminal server for legacy Windows apps but overall its been amazing. They are using thin clients for the desktops and have 4 dual-processor Athlon servers in a closet. Everything is centralized, everything is very fast and all administrative tasks are able to be done remotely.

They have drasticly reduced their support costs, teachers are able to pull up their desktop and items from any computer across the entire campus, its possible to share a desktop and have adhoc meetings (no need to schedule times to meet in person, yada yada..) and the software selection is much richer on the system ... programming tools, CAD tools, desktop publishing, office suites, graphic editing, access to lots of flash/web based educational titles, the KDE Edutainment apps, etc..etc..

Best part? The fear of "screwing everything up" has subsided. Historically there has been spyware, viruses and other issues on the network .. no longer. yippie! :)

Congrats on migrating! :)
