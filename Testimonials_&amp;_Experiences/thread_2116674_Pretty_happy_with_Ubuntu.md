### Post by exploder on 2013-02-16
I am running Ubuntu 12.04 x64 on my HP laptop and have to say that I am pretty happy with how it runs and the way it is being updated. At the time my laptop was purchased my ralink WiFi was not supported by any distribution. I was not happy with Windows 7 that came pre-installed so I started out running a development build of 12.04 because it supported the WiFi.

Unity works well on the 15.6" screen. The dash and the default theme make the laptop a real pleasure to use! The laptop also has hybrid graphics and works just fine under 12.04. The use of mutter and muffin on the laptop causes some graphics issues but 12.04 using compiz works with no issues.

I run Linux Mint 14 x64 Cinnamon edition on my desktop computer. Even though it is not Ubuntu the core is still Ubuntu 12.10 based and it also works very well for me. I know I could install Cinnamon on Ubuntu 12.10 but Clem did such a good job with the appearance of Mint 14 I really enjoy it on the desktop computer.

Ubuntu has really done a fine job of handling updates with 12.04 and 12.10 that I have had no problems with either system. Quality has greatly improved with Ubuntu over the last couple of releases! Now that gaming has finally made it's way to Linux, graphics drivers that do a good job are starting to appear and things will only get better. Ubuntu is the distribution that has made all of this happen and I am grateful for that.

It's nice to see manufacturers working with Ubuntu and software developers. These really are the best of times for Linux!

### Post by exploder on 2013-02-24
I was messing around with my laptop yesterday. I tried running the latest Mint Debian edition on it but I ran into graphics problems. Just for the heck of it I tried installing Mint 14.1 x64 Cinnamon again on the laptop before putting Ubuntu 12.04 back on it.

Much to my surprise after applying all of the available updates on, including kernel updates, the laptop is working perfectly! Originally I could not run Mint 14.1 because I had way too many graphics issues, 6 blurry images of the greeter, black screen, artifacts, black around any apps I would open, this happened with the open source drivers as well as the proprietary drivers.

Once all of the updates were installed everything was working just fine! I am using the open source drivers with the ATI hybrid graphics and everything looks great now!

There is nothing wrong with Unity at all in my opinion. Cinnamon just happens to be my personal favorite and with my eyesight problems Cinnamon gives me the least amount of eye strain. If it were not for the Ubuntu developers updating packages it would not have been possible for me to run Mint 14.1.

I would never have believed my laptop could run this good using the open source drivers! I sure am thankful for the Ubuntu developers constantly fixing bugs. This is the most usable my laptop has been to me since my last eye operation. I do change the default theme to Gnome, the glossy black with the white letters is the easiest for me to see and I can sit back and use the laptop pretty comfortably now.
