### Post by AdamPieniazek on 2007-06-01
Short version of the story:


A few days ago I dropped my WD Passport 120 GB (formatted as a FAT 32) drive on the ground (d'oh) and after re-plugging it into my Macbook Pro:

Windows XP would find the drive and mount it but would do nothing else. Didn't even mount it with the correct name...

Mac OS X would find the drive, mount it but would not read it, but would produce a somewhat working copy via dd (yay Unix).

Ubuntu on the other hand, found my drive, mounted it and let me access everything on it, including my 50 GB music collection! 

I'd score this as a victory for Ubuntu...for the (very) long version of the story with pictures, [check out this blog post]("http://www.adampieniazek.com/technology/clickclickclick-how-a-virtual-kubuntu-penguin-saved-my-50gb-music-collection/").
