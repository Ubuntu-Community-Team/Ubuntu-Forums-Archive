### Post by simplemind on 2007-08-05
I hope some may find this helpful, especially for those thinking of trying out kubuntu.

My PC experience is set firmly in windows, from win 3.1 to win xp, my expertise is fairly average, I can install new graphics cards and memory as well as install new drivers etc., I have learnt to fix problems, even the "*OMG!!!! did I do that*" problems :)

I use the pc for all kinds of reasons, photo printing, word processing, games, media, and all things webby and glorious :)

My home has 4 computers networked, 2 on cable and 2 with wireless connection to a netgear DG834N router. Please don't think I am an expert, I am not. I can't even set up my games for network play as yet. so I learn through trial and lots of errors, my poor brain even gets the odd blue screen of death now and then, but I still muddle through.

The one thing that has really miffed me off with windows is the expense and the way they try to rail road me into upgrading to vista, 4 computers that in the end will probably have to be upgraded at some cost. And not forgetting the constant problems and frustrations that come with windows.](*,)

So with that in mind I decided to install Kabuntu on my kids pc first, :twisted:just to see if the network tec guy (me) could hand the stress from two irate teenagers who can't use there msn and music.
 All went really well, no shouts of fix this or it wont do this or that, It worked, OK they don't have msn but they use the other Linux program that does the same thing, as well as other teenage stuff with Linux.
I even used a dual boot install for my own pc with no problems, I still need xp for my website stuff and games, but I am amazed at the speed of Kubuntu and the security of the os compared to windows.:)
I don't expect the ghost of windows past to make things easy for me, I have found it hard to get my head around the new os in some respects, and yet I also have found it surprisingly more logical in others, take for instance the system settings, easy to use and easy to understand, I think that some people who find them hard are just stuck in window mode, it takes time but I have found that it is surprisingly very customisable and user friendly.

I still  have problems such as my Epsom R340 is not supported and I can't play my games, I know that programs like wine can help with games, but that's way over my head at this moment, and as for the terminal window aarrrrggh, but in the future who knows.

In my opinion this Linux distro is  a brave attempt at providing an OS that rivals windows, and it is not far off doing that as it stands.
All it needs is a bit more support from the hardware manufacturers and the games industry, and a bit more built in user friendliness.

I would love to know how many people now use Kubuntu, I think you will find thousands more have taken the step into the unknown as I have, in time the games industry will in the end have to pay more attention to the prospect of the possible sales of Linux games. (even valve).
The main problem with this software is for people like me, who have been brought up on windows.
We judge every thing against what we know, and like it or not Kubuntu will be judged....but that is not to say that the judgement will be unfavourable, on the contrary as in my experience there is much to complement Kubuntu for, and the future is looking very bright in my inexperienced opinion.=D>

I can honestly say that if any one is thinking of trying it, go for it, its a good experience, a brave new world and fun at that.:)
ps
I can highly recommend the netgear rangemax router I use, its easy to set up and its fast.
