### Post by Armor Nick on 2009-01-11
Yeah, you hear right! The reason I'm staying with Ubuntu is the same reason some people stay away from linux. Allow me to explain.

I've done a reinstall of Windows XP a year ago because viruses attacked my system. The first (and most annoying thing) I had to do after the initial install was finding and installing drivers. This was very hard considering XP didn't detect my ethernet modem. I almost wrecked my pc out of stress.

At that time, I was already interested in Linux, but I didn't really like it. Primarily because of KDE (I tried OpenSUSE 10.1). A few months later, I decided to use Wubi to install Linux alongside Windows. I was blown away when I restarted my pc.

Ubuntu not only figured out my entire system and installed all the drivers automatically, it also (after logging onto the internet) automagically informed me that I wasn't using my ATI video card because of the restrictions. It was super-easy to activate it, and this was something I had a lot of trouble with on windows; no need to know the version and brand, just click 'yes'. True, there might be some trouble with 3D acceleration every now and then, but IT JUST WORKS (tm) !!!

Thank you so much, Canonical and the community, for being so supportive of every new user. And thank you for taking away the pain!

On a side-note, something I read worries me a bit; will Linux be GUI-ified? Because I really like to use the terminal. (The real terminal, not just a terminal emulator. Y'know, Ctrl+Alt+F2)
