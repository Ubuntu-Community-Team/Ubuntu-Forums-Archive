### Post by u-noob-tu on 2011-05-11
i couldnt restrain myself from trying out GNOME 3 any longer, and took the plunge today. install went perfect, except that not a single guide online told me to run update manager immediately after getting into GNOME 3 (fixed all my issues). i was immediately blown away by how slick it is, i mean, this is futuristic looking. the lack of minimize and maximize buttons doesnt bother me, i just make a new workspace. sure, there may not be a compiz plugin (yet), but it looks so different from anything else that people still ask what the heck it is. it feels very intuitive and fast (to me, at least), and i love the minimalist approach. the less stuff on your desktop, the better. im not sure why mark shuttleworth went with unity instead of GNOME 3 (which was ready before natty. having the option would have been the least he could do), but this is an incredible desktop environment. i cant wait to see where it goes from here. i dont think ill be using anything else for awhile.
