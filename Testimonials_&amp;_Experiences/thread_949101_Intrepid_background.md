### Post by decoherence on 2008-10-15
Just rebooted after an update and was greeted with a desktop picture I hadn't seen before. My first impression was "cool!" I think it's a very beautiful background. It seems like a redux of that other leather background, which had potential but looked far too... fleshy imho.

That got me thinking about the various default backgrounds people use. OS X and it's spacey abstract style gives an impression of newness and "cutting edge-ness" which is certainly what Apple banks on in their marketing. 

The most popular version of Windows uses a nice, oversaturated landscape shot which gives an impression of calm and peace, which I'm sure is much appreciated by those users...

This latest Intrepid background? What does it say? What is its impression? Well like I said, it reminds me of a more refined version of that old leather background.   Much more refined. And with a specific point of interest -- a darker, 'distressed' looking part.

The impression it gives me is one of decay or erosion. It's definitely something I'd personally use as a desktop. It looks retro, it looks natural, it looks hi-fi... i think its great! I wonder if it will actually be the default? An impression of beautifuul decay or erosion is something MS or Apple would never dare. They have people paid of have a heart attack if any product leaving the company at all implies anything less than complete perfection (not the parts that actually matter, of course. just the parts visible to joe consumer like desktop backgrounds, widgets and genie effects.)

So anyway, I guess this is an open-ended post. What do you think of the new intrepid background? Does it 'speak' to you? ;)~

### Post by Keyper7 on 2008-10-18
> **thrasher6900 said:**
> Ooh that sounds nifty.  How can I get it? :D

If you're asking how to do it, it's quite simple:

1 - open the wallpaper in gimp
2 - go to colors > hue-saturation
3 - put the hue slider all the way to the left or all the way to the right
4 - save it and done! :)

The Hue is a circular parameter, measured in degrees. So shifting the slider all the way to the left (-180) is the same as shifting it all the way to the right (+180).
