### Post by YoYoSan on 2007-06-30
In the past month I've thought more about the tools I use to earn a living than I have in the past 5 years. I rely on my laptop and mobile phone and use them daily for research, to create work, and to communicate; so it was a gentle risk switching to Ubuntu at the beginning of June.

Since then I've struggled with screen resolution, wireless networking, WAP security and still can't get my HP printer to print crisp 10pt font.

I replaced my mobile phone after getting hopelessly tangled trying to follow instructions for syncing calendars with a Windows Smartphone.

I'm nervous about getting the projector to work the next time I do a group presentation, and I'm looking for fonts I know in the font directory (Arial, Helvetica).

But it has been:

**amazing** to see what has been achieved by this community, and for free!

**refreshing **to use applications that load quickly and do exactly what they say they will, no more, no less;

**stimulating **to think about what I need to use, and what effort I'm prepared to put in to use it.

So, thanks everyone, I've really enjoyed my first month amongst you and look forward to many more.

YOYO
