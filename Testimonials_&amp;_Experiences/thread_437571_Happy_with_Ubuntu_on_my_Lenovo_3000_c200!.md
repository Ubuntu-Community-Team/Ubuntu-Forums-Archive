Anyway, at that time I installed Edgy, but had some major issues with the on board wireless card (broadcom)... so no wireless internet :( bummer... and no sound! another bummer. (but I always knew that a few months later some fixes would come out, so I didn't worry) Then came Feisty... made a clean install, searched this forum and found a fix for the sound and wireless... basically copy paste some commands in the terminal, hit enter several times and things work perfectly now! I mean I know very little about commands, but there is no excuse for people who say they cannot get things working with linux or they don't "like" linux, because of these commands, come on... you just follow the instructions step by step... basically copy paste the commands in the terminal and hit enter, easy as that, even though I've little knowledge about what exactly I'm doing, you know.

Also installed Kiba dock, really fun and nice eye candy... just searched for a deb of kiba dock, found this one...

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=268645&page=15](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=268645&page=15)

and this one...

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=312789](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=312789)

now honestly I forgot which one of these two is the correct one... :) just try it, see if it works for you.

Also installed Beryl, easy, for it is now available in synaptic... so all I can say now is wow... now I am a proud owner of a relatively simple laptop that has all the bells and whistles, runs smooth as silk, is VERY responsive... fast and is build like a brick! All the keys do work, volume up and down, touchpad...everything! This is a good lesson that you don't have to spend large amounts of money to own a good laptop. To some extend I am sometimes content that most people don't know this (linux and such) and want to fork out lots of money to buy a fancy laptop so they can run the resource hungry Vista. I bet in most cases they will be dissappointed because Vista is such a resource draining vampire, perhaps it still won't run on their machines. (hopefully they will find out about linux, Ubuntu in particular). Simple but extremely good (and durable, it is build like a Soviet tank) new laptops such as the Lenovo 3000 c200 are therefore much more affordable, because it is seen as a machine that cannot run the latest full blown MS Windows version, Vista. - hence the low price.

Just wanted to share this with all of you, and recommend this laptop, in case if you are looking for a simple and durable machine. (but don't expect to play demanding games on it, since the internal graphic card is too simple for that) It runs Beryl/kiba dock beautifully smooth though, hats of to these developers!) Thank you, thank you!
