### Post by lemmy15 on 2010-11-27
This partition business is quite maddening for the novice, and particularly annoying as I ordered a Breeze Pro 4220 computer from zareason with Ubuntu 10.04 and had to ship it back as it failed.  They said they fixed it and shipped it back with Ubuntu 10.10 loaded with no further instructions (but with their admin password still on the computer, and a follow-up e-mail has so far gone unanswered).  When I had problems originally with the Breeze Pro I was able to EASILY re-load Ubuntu 10.04.  I was never prompted to partition the drive.

Since there is nothing on the disk really except the work zareason performed, I guess I could select erase and use the entire disk.  But I'm not sure.  "Install alongside other operating systems" give me to sections to allocate drive space by dragging a divider, one labeled Ubuntu 10.10 (251.1GB) and the other just Ubuntu (242.7GB), /dev/sda1 (ext4) and /dev/sda2 (ext4), respectively.

Sorry for the rant, but I sure as Hades didn't expect any of this to install Ubuntu 10.10.

### Post by lemmy15 on 2010-11-27
Well, I figured it out...'erase and use the entire disk' if you don't care about partitioning (it will do it for you) and are basically starting from scratch and don't have (or care about) another OS such as Windows 7.

If only the process had stated that this 'erase/use entire...' selection would partition what it needed to partition automatically, things might have been easier and with much less consternation.
