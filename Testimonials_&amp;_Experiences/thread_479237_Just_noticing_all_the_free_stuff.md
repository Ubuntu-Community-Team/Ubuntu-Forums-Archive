### Post by weblordpepe on 2007-06-20
You know, I was just browsing the applications menu and thinking - there is so much free stuff you get with Ubuntu and with Linux in general.
A few notible things:
SSH
OpenOffice, Evolution
GIMP
A client that does MSN/AOL/ICQ/IRC etc

And those are just the things Unique to Ubuntu/Linux. It seems like all the stuff you get for free with other unnamed commercial OSes you get with Ubuntu, but you get added things too.

I've come to realise that the difference is - the dudes and dudettes who make Ubuntu aren't trying to convince you to use the software that came with the OS.  You're free to do what you like. There's no dirty little tricks to make stuff only compatible with what came with Ubuntu. (I beleive the default format for burned DVDs in Vista is a Vista-only format).

I was nosing around Vista and its still pretty much the same thing as XP. Except there's no built in SSH, and all the stuff has licence restrictions that stop you from doing stuff.

Linux & Ubuntu are quite refreshing in that they don't focus on what you can't do ('dont download attachments', 'dont copy this', 'dont try & figure out how this works')

It seems to focus on what you can do. Encouraging you to do stuff. Its the kind of computer system you'd expect in 2007.
