### Post by murderslastcrow on 2010-03-11
Every model of iPod, including the Touch, iPhone, Nano 5g with camera, etc. etc. works with Ubuntu 10.04! T^T No more worrying about noobs' media player choices anymore :D. It's a glorious day.

But that's not all, the desktop is becoming more usable, prettier, and the kernel is just getting amazing. Nouveau supports experimental 3d, meaning that soon I can put Ubuntu even on old PowerPC Macs with NVidia cards, and keep compiz!(lol). Like, Ubuntu was always this, "so amazing I can't believe it works in so many places" kind of thing. Like Linux was this miracle OS that did almost everything with its own tools, and all this manpower put into reverse engineering hardware and file formats and everything, and the Wine project is just outstanding.

But now it's just wrapping up loose ends, and soon all the original and brilliant parts of open source will become all the more evident, robust, and feature-filled.

Just think of how far we've come in five years! O_O It's frightening to think how much will happen in the next five.

I just really hope Canonical or Gnome doesn't screw things up, that's all, since Ubuntu's at the top right now. Don't wanna' see it fall because of silly, arbitrary design choices that don't benefit users at all.

Thanks to the guys behind all the iPod libraries for opening up Linux use to a new market, thanks to the Wine developers for bringing us our professional software and a good amount of games, and thanks to Gnome, KDE, and Canonical for all pushing the limits of usability. You've all created an even better world for computers everywhere, and the benefits are starting to overwhelm me. XD

It's almost like a "we're almost there," feeling. Oh, I hope they port Steam to Linux, too, not just OS X. K, now I'm rambling. XD

Linux is like the perfect OS for so many different systems, it boggles the mind what it works on.
