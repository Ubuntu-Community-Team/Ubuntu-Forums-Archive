### Post by wandalalakers on 2009-01-23
I recently installed Xubuntu 8.04 on a cousin's Dell optiplex GX1 with 384mb.  My cousin forgot her password and that's why she needed me to work on her PC.  It p*ssed me off because I hate when people forget their passwords and expect you to do something about it.  Well anyway, I reset her password but when trying to access control panel to add another user account, the computer crashed.  I assumed it must have been spyware and/or viruses causing the crash.  I refushed to put windows back on.  (Since I'm fixing her computer for free no one can really say anthing)  This is because it takes too long to load, plus I would have to locate all her drivers.  A ghost image wouldn't take long but I've gotten to the point that after discovering Ubuntu, I can't stand to install windows.  I'm not going to go around creating ghost images for all my relatives.  Anyway, I installed Xubuntu on this Dell and it was much responsive (of course).  I think it took my cousin's sons four weeks to finally connect the Dell.  I guess they didn't want to deal with slow windows.  One of my cousin's sons said, "What is this?" when the desktop came up.  I would have loved to see the look on his face.  He is pleasantly pleased from what I understand.  He ran GIMP, games and some other stuff.  I think he is either a junior or senior.  So him telling his Mom, "She really knows her stuff" (he's referring to me making the computer much faster and having good software installed).  My mission has been accomplished.  Another windows user has been converted, at least on this desktop.
