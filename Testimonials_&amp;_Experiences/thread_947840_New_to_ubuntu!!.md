Well, that may have been a little bad choice of words.  I should have said "challenged" instead of frustrated.  Most of it comes down to the fact that, as yet, hardware manufacturers do no support Linux to the extent that they do Windows.  This is changing, albeit a little more slowly than most would like.  But, that being said, imagine trying to use a piece of hardware in Windows if the manufacturer did not provide a driver disk....probably almost impossible because you can't get "under the hood" in Windows...You Can in Linux!  Because there has historically been insufficient hardware support for Linux by manufactuers, the Linux kernel developers have had to build most of the support right into the kernel, something you will not find in Windows because the manufacturers DO provide drivers for Windows.

In any case, my original statement holds true....stick with Linux, persevere, and like many of us who abandoned Windows long ago, you may never find a reason to back.
