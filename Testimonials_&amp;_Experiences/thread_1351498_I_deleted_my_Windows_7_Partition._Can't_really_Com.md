### Post by ScrewdriverClock on 2009-12-10
So yeah, sounds like a great title, waiting for people to insta-post how I'm such an idiot... however the title isn't really too straightforward. I made a live USB stick of 9.10, placed it into my system, ran it, started the partitioner, which would hang trying to edit my 7 partition. Up to this point I've loved 7, and had no reason to switch other than just having Ubuntu to play with. Anyway one phone call and pet rats to distract me through a failed install, I decided "hell i have the 7 CD for later, I'll delete the partition. Anyway it was nice to see my hardware all working for the most part, at least being detected. I need to figure out how to configure my WINTv card at one point, but its okay. I'm not having these stability issues people seem to have with 9.10, and Empathy is my favorite over Pidgin (I HATE the name and logo). 

However.

To any current Windows user, I wouldn't tell them to switch. If someones buying a new computer and wants to game, I wouldn't tell them to switch. Windows as a whole has been a pleasant experience as well, and I'll even go to say Vista was one of my favorite operating systems to run on my little box, though 7 really raised the bar. If someone (like my current situation) wants a cheap, strong little box to go on the internet and watch flash videos or movies, you can bet I throw out the idea of Ubuntu. I don't, however, spout evangelical blather about how amazing it is. Take it as you want, but Ubutnu is just an OS. OS X is just an OS. Windows is just an OS. And if one suits your needs, its good. Ubuntu appears to be doing that so I'm going to keep playing with it until it doesn't.


Hahah okay so I doubt you all wanted that little rant, so here is the final verdict.

It works great out of the box on my system, the applications show much more polish now (hell I am glad Totem doesn't look like something from the 90s computing era) and overall I feel like the computer works for me, not the other way around. It's a nice alternative to Windows 7 and Mac OS X and stands alongside them as an equal. 

[COLOR="Red"]Would use again.[/COLOR]
