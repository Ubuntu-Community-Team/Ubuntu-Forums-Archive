### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-05-27
I decided to just go for it, since I have nothing to loose, and installed beryl on my Apple ibook G4.  I first checked to see if the video driver I was using (the open source ati driver) supported 3d direct rendering by running this command.  

```
glxinfo | grep direct
```

I recieved this as an answer

```
direct rendering: Yes
```

So I installed beryl and fired it up.  And to my surprise, beryl works. The cube and some window minimizing and maximizing animations are a little poky, but it mostly just works.  I didn't even have to edit my xorg.conf file.  

Special thanks to all of the developers working hard on Ubuntu and those working on the open ati driver.  This is the most fun I have ever had with my Apple ibook G4, and I have owned my ibook since January of 2006.  :D
