### Post by opticpursuit on 2010-01-19
I just switched from an optimized XP pro os to Xubuntu on my EEE netbook - and I'm amazed at the speed of everything.

A few days ago I was playing around with Puppeee (I got to run off sd card) - and I was pleasantly surprised. A couple days ago I committed - formatted and installed full Xubuntu and I'm looking forward to staying open source. The install was seamless. All hardware just "works" - no flogging around installing drivers.

It's free - and kinda fun.

Google Chrome is grease lightning lol - everything else is just snappy as Bruce Lee. Boot and shut down is so fast it's like a game.  So much so I made a shootout vid comparing 2 machines.

I just bought another netbook as a gift with windows 7 on it - and I feel like I'm back in Vista Hell(BLEH). It does run pretty well for a machine with only 1gb mem - and benchmarks look promising too. Time will tell. I have been assured linux won't get bloaty mcnasty after continued use.


I know they are different machines - with similiar specs - the only major advantage going to the EEE with 2gb memory vs 1gb on the Acer e-machine...

### Post by GodLikeCreature on 2010-02-17
Welcome to the Linux brotherhood.  Glad you are enjoying your time, I can only tell you this is just the beginning!

As for the test you posted in YouTube, I believe it is a bit unfair.  Xubuntu is intentionally a light desktop environment, perfect for machines with limited resources.  I think a comparison against Gnome or KDE would be more like it.

Having said so, I am pretty confident you would find both of them, specially Gnome, to still be faster than Win7 anyways.  Shutdown times in particular are quite different.  Boot times are also better, and it seems they will improve them yet again with Lucid (not something I am particularly crazy about, but it doesn't harm either).

In any case, the initial performance differences, and the fact that Linux can much better stand the test of time, are just things that come as a sweet surprise.  However, it is when you start diving deep into the Linux world that you really find out what a great OS it really is.  

I stopped using Windows a couple years ago and have never looked back!
