### Post by youknowme on 2012-06-26
You know, I have been a GNU Linux user since 1996. I started out with a  Redhat distribution. Over the years I have at various times switched to try SUSE, Mandrake (later known as Mandriva), Slackware, Debian, DSL,FreeBSD (UNIX) and others..

Now I can say that I have really appreciated all of the distributions / alternative operating systems that I have used in the past. A lot of talented dedicated people put a lot of effort into them - and continue to do so to this day.

I had been a FreeBSD user for a very very long time. Then I tested Debian and realised that simply using a PC did not have to be a difficult task. I can't recall the first time i tested Ubuntu, but I can remember that apart from Mandrake, it was the easiest OS to install and everything seemed to work out of the box - not fighting to get the network up or the strange USB printer to work.

I reached the stage where I no longer felt it productive to be in a constant battle to master my computer. I simply wanted to get things done. For example, I wanted to print off a document from a USB stick. I could have done it in FreeBSD and indeed did do it. 

I switched to using Debian on all my machines and the transition from FreeBSD was really noticeable to me. Even so, some of my hardware still did not function out of the box and there was a need to configure the system. So last month I attempted to download a 'fix' to make my wireless network card work with Debian. When I rebooted the system it was completely broken. I then decided to try ubuntu and sure enough it installed without a hitch. All of my hardware worked. That was all I ever wanted.

So to me, the real importance of Ubuntu is to enable regular people - who are not necessarily computer 'geeks' to have access to the wonderful world of GNU Linux. Today I believe that Ubuntu has evolved to the stage where it is equally as easy to use as Windows XP / Vista etc. I believe that progress in development will continue and eventually, due to the superior open source development model, Linux will one day overtake proprietary systems such as Windows in general use. Ubuntu has been a leader in this innovation and I hope it will continue in this vein):P
