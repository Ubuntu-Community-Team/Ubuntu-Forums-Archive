### Post by Upochapo on 2008-08-29
Sorry to hear about ur troubles.  I have an acer aspire laptop.  similar specs.  When u get the full screen code stuff is it actual code or command line stuff?  Sounds like the xserver is crashing on u.  Something might be slightly boogered up with your xorg.conf.

I'm not a huge linux geek or anything but have switched over recently because I just am not impressed with Vista and have learned quite a bit.  And, about on the same time frame as you.  Reinstalling ubuntu is quite easily a snap...at least i've found it to be.

But, hey, when I had my first bout with linux it was a nightmare.  It's just a matter of figuring out all the pieces of the puzzle, how they fit together and how much patience you got!  Hehe...cuz it can be quite trying at times...especially when u just want it to work.

I went through a bunch of crashes myself LOL.  I'm good at breaking OS's.  It's a gift.  Hope u find the courage to give it another go sometime in the future.  If you're ever interested in trying to figure out what's goin' on...drop me a pm and try and set something up...but if you've moved on that's cool too.

Edit:

Couple of things.  You have an ATI video card.  From my understanding, linux and ATI don't like to play nice all the time.  My card is also ATI.  I noticed that you fiddled with emerald and mucking about with the command line stuff.

My suggestion.  If it has gotten to the point of being unstable and hair pulling...

Reinstall Ubuntu.
Turn your advanced graphics on.
As far as switching different window compositors theres a handy little program u can use called Compiz Fusion Icon.  This has proven quite valuable to me when i want to change things safely.  As I have to because I get the ATI Flicker with opengl stuff.

you may be able to reset your xorg file
i believe at a command prompt you type sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg

you can also reset it i believe through recovery mode by selecting it in grub. before booting.  But i think it just takes ur last backup.

another thing you could do is to locate the oldest xorg backup file u have.  when it creates a backup i believe it appends dates and times.  Anyways...that's all i could come up with at the moment
