### Post by solwic on 2009-05-20
There seems to be an ebb and flow to the content of Testimonials and Experiences; that is, either there are posts singing the praises of Ubuntu, or there are posts flaming Ubuntu, Ubuntu's mother, and anybody who looks like Ubuntu.  Plus the horse it rode in on.  

The negative posts are necessary; they provide balance.  If the testimonials section was only rainbows and flowers, I, at least, would think it was a bunch of crap.  But people, from new users to veteran users (and I include myself in the list, too) tend to get caught up in these angry threads that are totally counterproductive to both the point of the forum (which is to help people) and the very definition of the word Ubuntu and the philosophy behind it (which is community).  People get angry, and they want to be right, and damn the consequences.  

The thing is, though, that we all need to keep things in perspective.  For instance, I am recently divorced.  Before my divorce, I was on the forums quite a bit.  I stuck to the Testimonials section because I don't know a lot about the inner workings of Linux.  I'm not an expert.  But I wanted to help, so I figured encouraging others here, who were frustrated and having a hard time, would be my way of contributing.  The problem, however, is that anger begets more anger, and before long I found myself pounding angrily away at the keyboard, arguing with people I don't know and likely will never know about topics that, ultimately, don't matter.  That wasn't what I had in mind when I decided to give back.

And when my divorce happened, that cosmic slap across the face gave me some perspective.  Before that, I would spend hours posting angry posts here.  But when the proverbial crap hit the proverbial fan, what OS I used (or my neighbor used, or some total flipping stranger used) didn't even make my list of Stuff That Needs Taken Care Of.

My point is this:  it's fine to like Ubuntu.  It's fine to post your experience.  It's even fine if that experience is negative.  But let's all try to have a little perspective here.  In the end it's just an OS, just a bunch of 1's and 0's.  Is it really worth being angry and snarky and snotty, posting message after message about the same topics from the same people, arguing the same points and ultimately coming to the same end, which is everybody is going to think what they want?  

Have some perspective, calm down, and post rationally.  And for God's sake, remember, it's just software.  If it's broken, well...it's FREE!  What do you expect?  Perfection?
