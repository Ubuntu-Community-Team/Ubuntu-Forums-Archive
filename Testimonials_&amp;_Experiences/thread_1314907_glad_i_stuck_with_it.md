### Post by $teve on 2009-11-04
I've been using Ubuntu since its first release; it was purely chance that 4.10 became available at the exact time I gave up with Windows.

I previously had _absolutely no_ experience with any linux distro & even found it hard to understand the concept of a computer without Windows :P However, I wanted a change and saw a lot of hype surrounding Ubuntu, so I downloaded an .iso image, burnt it and I was away.

Initially I couldnt believe that all the basics were installed and ready to use out of the box & it cost me nothing! 

Things havent always been straight forward, especially with some of the earlier versions, but with each release I'm more pleased I stuck with it. Actually thinking back, the amount of times I've been through the installation process is mad. Ultimately though I learned from every mistake and admittedly most of the problems I encountered could be attributed to my curiosity ](*,) I still have a small partition reserved for Windows just in case, but I very rarely use it and with each update from ubuntu there's less reason to do so.

I dont want to bang on about it, but just wanted to say how much of a great OS I think Ubuntu is, and how impressed I am with 9.10. Aside from an issue with hibernate/suspend, everything is working perfect and looks beautiful.

Thanks Ubuntu ;)
