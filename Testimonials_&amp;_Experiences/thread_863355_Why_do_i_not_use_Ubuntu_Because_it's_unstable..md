### Post by andrewusesvista on 2008-07-18
I like to start things positively and give things a good go and I think with Ubuntu i gave it far more chances than most people would to impress me and it didn't on all levels. I tried it on 6 seperate occasions where i installed it using wubi on my laptop running vista and then uninstalled it when it broke completely. Each time I had it installed for time scales ranging from several days to several weeks. We'll start off with the things i liked about it. I loved the customisation, the way the folders are laid out, the switching between desktops, the compiz animations/other effects and the running/shutdown speed and i also liked the way to install things on Ubuntu.

Now onto the negatives. Quite a lot more often than Vista does, the system completely freezes up. This means that ctrl+alt+F1 does nothing and the only way for me to resolve this issue is to take the laptop battery out and put it back in again. This happens maybe once every 2/3 days.

I have a logitech quickcam sphere mp. Ubuntu doesn't work with quite a lot of logitech webcams. I have another account on here where i have topics reaching as many as 5 or 6 pages long trying to fix my webcam with no real solution in the end. I spent days and days trying to get it to work before i found a solution which meant that none of the effects would work, it would only work with flash and i obviously therefore couldn't do anything else with it.

Flash support is awful. The 5th time i used it, when I went on certain sites flash showed up with a grey box. I spent days trying to fix this and since i use flash quite a lot, this was ridiculous. People said to just uninstall the flash player and then reinstall it but it was always the same sites that stopped it working so am i going to purposefully avoid certain websites when i use those websites quite regularly anyway just so i can use ubuntu? No. The flash problem occured on my 5th use and no timee before that showing that this was a random problem. On my 6th go with Ubuntu, when i used that method to install my webcam, flash went back to showing up grey on certain websites and that was breaking point for me and i stopped using it.

I have an iPod Touch. Ubuntu doesn't have iTunes. I need iTunes to sync my iPod Touch. There's a program which I have forgotten the name of which you can sort of trick into doing it but that doesn't have the App store and won't sync things such as applications, pictures and videos.

I make videos for youtube and therefore use editing software all of the time. I use adobe premiere for that and Ubuntu doesn't have that. It does have some video alternatives but none which come anywhere close to being as good as adobe premiere. I also require cyberlink powerdirector to turn my camera's .MOD files into the .avi format.

One day when i switched on ubuntu on my 5th try of it, it said it was in safe mode and no matter how many instructions i followed from people on ubuntu forums, there was no way to get it out of safe mode and get it working normally. It wasn't the problem they suspected and nobody really knew what to do so it was "oh well, things like this happen, reinstall time." No, things like that don't just happen. Not if you're using vista anyway.

Whenever i'm using programs on ubuntu they are usually alternatives to windows programs but they never have any real advantages but always seem to have drawbacks. For example, word processors are missing many features from microsoft word, pidgin is missing many of the MSN features and obviously the video editors are missing a lot of the adobe premiere or even just movie maker features.

I understand that ubuntu is free and don't want to hear the "OMG you should demand a refund!" sarcastic comments but it just doesn't work and is unstable. Also, if you have a system you've paid for, why would you want to go on ubuntu when you have so many workarounds and allowances you have to make for it.

I also don't want to hear the rubbish about "oh, well it's not for everybody" because when things like flash and hardware don't work and then it crashes with no proper way to turn it off and when it stops working meaning you have to restart your system, there's no way you should just avoid all those things just so you can use ubuntu because doing that is just pathetic.

Ubuntu has far too many problems which users somehow fail to mention. They may take pride in saying that their programs are open source but those programs don't do a lot of the things programs of the same kind for other operating systems do.

I'm sure there may have been other points i've missed which i can't think of right now but intil all of the problems which i'm having (webcam not working, no itunes, flash not working correctly) have been solved, there is no point in me using the operating system because huge flaws like that can not be ignored and make it unusable.

There will probably be several Ubuntu releases over Vista's lifetime.

Maybe your views will change.

Remember though, that many of the problems you raised are related to proprietary software not open source. Flash is from Adobe and iTunes is from Apple and Logitech provides no linux webcam drivers. There are already technological answers to some of these problems but the companies won't improve their linux support unless enough users are there to agitate for it.

If there are things you like about Ubuntu, you'll probably try it again in a few months and one of these times you might not want to switch back!
