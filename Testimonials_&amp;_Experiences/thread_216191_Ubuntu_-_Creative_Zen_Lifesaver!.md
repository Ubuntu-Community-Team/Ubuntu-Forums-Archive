### Post by krazyd on 2006-07-15
Funny story...

A friend of mine has a Creative Zen Touch which she got not long after it was released. Back in those days, the only way to load music onto the player was to use Creative's proprietry software (from what I can remember - I helped at the time).

The other day, having lost the drivers/software CD, she asked me to help her get the music from the Zen onto her new XP notebook. 'No probs', I thought, 'I'll just head over to creatives website, get the old software again and robert's your father's brother.' 
But.. twas not to be. The cryptically labelled downloads page didn't appear to offer the original software. Instead, there was a windows driver available for the Zens with the original firmware, and a firmware upgrade which enabled the device with <super-duper>Plays for Sure©®™</super-duper> functionality but which unfortunately would wipe everything already on the device.

'Okay I'll just install the driver and copy the music off first,' I thought... but that would have been way too easy. Having installed the driver, the player showed up in 'My Computer', but any attempt at opening it and selecting the files for copy froze explorer. Completely. ](*,) After the third round of waiting for half an hour for it to un-freeze and having to kill explorer, I had an idea - I have only started dual booting XP / Ubuntu in the last week and I wondered how Ubuntu would handle a Zen..

A quick search in synaptic turned up KZenExplorer ([http://kzenexplorer.sourceforge.net/)](http://kzenexplorer.sourceforge.net/)). Five minutes later it was scanning for devices.. found the Zen.. displayed the music.. I set a folder to sync with, clicked the sync button and away it went! Being an older box with only USB 1.1, this took a while but a couple of hours later all the music was transferred!

Needless to say, I was very impressed. As was my friend when she got to keep all her music :). The end.

Ubuntu / KZenExplorer / Free Software - 1
Windows / Creative / Proprietry Crap - 0
