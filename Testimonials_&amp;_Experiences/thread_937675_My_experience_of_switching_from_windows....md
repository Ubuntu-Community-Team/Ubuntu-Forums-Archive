### Post by EddieBarzoon on 2008-10-04
Hi there,

By accident I ended up installing Ubuntu yesterday and I think I will go on using it. 

I killed one of my machines and rather than reinstall from CD I had a USB boot drive to hand with an imaging tool and Ubuntu on it for something I had been doing with work.

Didn't actually think it would install as I had captured the image on VMware and it wouldn't have the correct drivers for my Dell optiplex GX280.

Well I popped the USB stick in and all of 6 minutes later I had a working Ubuntu install! No problems at all.The distribution must have contained all the necessary drivers.

This is my play machine and I use it for 90% internet and 10% games and not much else.

Internet browsing speed is vastly superior so far with firefox on Ubuntu. Light years ahead of what I am used to compared to ie or firefox on windows. Sites just load quicker! Why is that?

Very impressed with the PDF handling as well. I don't need to download something like adobe or foxit there is a pdf viewer pre-installed and pdfs seem to open much, much faster than windows.

It was a slight pain installing flash player for sites like youtube though. Eventually it worked the install wasn't as intuitive (for me) as double clicking in windows. Tar.gz, yum, rpm? Um, what? Ok well I picked tar.gz before lets go for that. Hmmm, nothing much is happening when I click. Obviously this is a user training problem on my part which a bit of learning will soon clean up.

So those are my thoughts so far. This has made me think about how I use my computer... it is mostly an internet machine. Pretty much everything I do is online and if Ubuntu is better at that it makes my world better.
