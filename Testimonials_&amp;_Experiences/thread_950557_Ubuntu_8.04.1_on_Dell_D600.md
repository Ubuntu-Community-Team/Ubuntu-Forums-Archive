### Post by MrWES on 2008-10-17
I've done several desktop installs and have always been very pleased with the results. I just completed my first install on a Dell D600 laptop and as many probably already know, the Broadcom 4309 wireless miniPCI card was a nightmare. 

Upside:
Ubuntu runs very well on this laptop; everything other than the wireless ran out of the box. I was very happy.

Downside:
As I already mentioned the Broadcom issue can be a real deal killer. I worked very hard with the many many howto's out there to get this card running, but with no luck. However, I'm glad to say I do have wireless working, as I was able to get my hands on a dead Dell Inspirion 6000 -- which had an Intel Pro 2200 card inside. Once I made the swap my wireless was up and running with in minutes, and with good connection speeds/percentage. 

I know this is not an Ubuntu issue directly, but from the reading I've done of the past several day in Network and Wireless forum -- Ubuntu seriously needs to develop stronger relationships with manufacturers of wireless cards to get the open source driver issues solved.

Bottom line: 
I'm happy with my laptop and I very happy it doesn't have a Mickey Soft product install on it.
