### Post by tribaal on 2006-05-01
Actually the software Ubuntu distributes is free, as in free speech (meaning you can distribute and modify it), and as in free beer (no charge for it). So Canonical *cannot* make you pay for it. Linux is covered by the [GPL]("http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/gpl.html"), and so is Ubuntu.

The folks at Canonical (the company "making" ubuntu) provide the Web sites and forum for free to the community, too. 

So how does the company manke money? 
Well they sell support and professional help for their operating system. 'cause of course, you might be fine to install your own Ubuntu on your system, but Canonical provides paid help for anything related to Ubuntu.

Of course, that is as far as I understand it, since I am by no means related to or employed by Canonical.

Hope this helps... =)

- trib'
