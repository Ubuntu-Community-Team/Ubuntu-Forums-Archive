### Post by ffluff on 2007-12-23
For example I need to adjust the refresh rate for my monitor. The first thing I found was the display settings, great! I'll choose the right setting and all will go well. I just kept lowering the resolution ratio till I could make out at least what I was seeing. 400 by 640 works. Oh but no such luck cause now the resolution is so small that I can't see the buttons at the bottom the dialog box to install Ubuntu onto my hard drive. If only I could change the refresh rate. Oh here it is! Right on the screen resolution dialog box I opened before, fantastic! It has a refresh rate drop down menu... let me see it's at 85Hz, that's too much I'll go all the way down the list to 60Hz, Oh but no such luck the only other setting is 80Hz hmmm. Guess what the solution was? After searching this website I found the solution... That's right goto the Terminal or Konsole. I shouldn't have needed to go there if the proper option were available to me in the first place! 
As I went on I notice most things I need to do just to finish installing Linux will require many visits to the Terminal or Konsole. I've come upon so many obstacles I still have to resolve before I can use Ubuntu. 
I didn't mention that changing the values for the refresh rate did solve the problem... somewhat. Ubuntu once loaded runs at the correct refresh rate however I have yet to see what a login or logout screen looks like. 
I wrote this not to vent but rather to wonder if this information will help the next version?
