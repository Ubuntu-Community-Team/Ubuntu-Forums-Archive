### Post by LandyMan on 2006-02-08
Hi everyone,

My 2 cents worth on Ubuntu:

Getting any linux distro to run semi-okish on a notebook used to be a daunting task, and henceforth I never really entertained the idea to replace XP with linux. But wait, things have changed!

I am running a dual boot Win2000/Redhat config on a PC at home, but haven't touched Redhat on it in about 3 years (not alot of time these days to play around) which means my linux skills have deteriorated a bit in the last 3 years. After reading a news article relating to Ubuntu a couple of weeks ago, I thought to myself, hey, let's give it a try, and WOW, am I impressed!

After installing Ubuntu, basically everything worked "out-the-box" or rather off the CD so to speak :). I had to tweak the Wireless config a bit, change the touchpad drivers, and finetune my MS mouse. This morning I got my iPAQ to sync to Evolution (which was a definite have-to-have before killing XP).

So after all of that, I would like to congratulate everyone involved in getting Ubuntu in the state that it is now, and also thank everyone contributing on this forum. With the posts/information I found on this forum, my HP NX9110 notebook can officially run Ubuntu, without XP lurking in the dark somewhere! \\:D/

Thanks again to all!
