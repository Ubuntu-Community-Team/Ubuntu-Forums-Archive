### Post by fisheater on 2010-02-04
One year opensource – A retrospective

Hi all,

Thanks for a good year. I took a challenge to switch all my computers to open source for a year and see how it went. What sent me down the path was the incomparability between a propitiatory word processor 2003 version and 2007 version. To that point I would update my computer and software to stay relevant with my work. Now that an alternative was available, I switched for a year and have been very happy.

This post is a thanks to all of the people on this forum that have taken time to help along the way, and to the other posters for sharing their (mis)adventures with me so I may learn from their experience. Perhaps the best strength of opensource is the people that believe in it.

So in summary:
2 laptops with 9.04
1 desktop with 9.04
router with DD-WRT
D-link NAS with FFP (linux upgrades to a simple NAS)
No Machine + dyndns + portable NxNoMachine client = highly portable computing.

Laments (and these are largely based around my lack of understanding of permissions and setup in linus – garbage in, garbage out):
Difficulty with setting up my NAS as a mounted HD. Managed in the end with extensive forums support.
Poor success with grsync across to my NAS. For some reason, it fails. Workaround has been to 'backintime' to my 2nd HD in this desktop.
Not getting photoshop and indesign to work in wine. I have kept this machine dual boot, but have found that my virtual copy of Wxp works heaps better even though it runs inside of my linux... Dont understand it, but keep using it!
Despite these bumps, I will chip away at them one by one and get them sorted.

It has been a great year of learning about my computer and what it can do. Your help has been instrumental in this success, thanks and see you for another year of opensource. I hope that my post on my (mis)adventures have helped a few people also.

Thanks.

FE
