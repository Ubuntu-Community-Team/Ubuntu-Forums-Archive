### Post by photodan on 2011-05-21
I'm an old guy (72 years!) and I've used Ubuntu for four years, loving every minute of it. I'm sitting here on a dual-boot machine with Maverick on one side and Natty on the other (this one). And, by the way,I consider Maverick (64 bit) to be the best operating system I've ever used (I've tried KDE a couple times and just don't get it.)

I'm looking at the Unity desktop right now and, you know, I like it! It's sleek, clean and easy to get around. Nothing to dislike there, right?

But, What I don't like is the lack of customization.

I click on the icon at the left of the top panel and I get "Shortcuts," to "View Photos", Media Apps", etc., none of which are arranged or appropriate for my use. And, there's no way to customize anything!

I click on the "Applications" button on the launcher and I get "Apps available for download." ?????? Can't change it - can't remove it. I'm stuck with suggestions for apps I don't want to see, much less to use. And, I thought that was what the Ubuntu Software Center was for. At least there I can find apps I might be interested in, not apps suggested by???????who know?

None of these menus are customizable through the "Main Menu," like previous versions of Gnome. It seems ridiculous that a user can't turn off applications in the main menu editor, without deleting them from the computer, but you can't. The Main Menu Editor is obviously for Classic Ubuntu only,

I suppose the bottom line is; Ubuntu has a winner here and they appear to be firm in their resolve to "dumb it down," much like Windows older operating systems. In fact, my old Windows XP, running on Virtualbox, has more options for customization.

Again, this desktop feels right, with the above exceptions, I will, of course, stay with it and hope the developers address my "issues." 

arkiedan
