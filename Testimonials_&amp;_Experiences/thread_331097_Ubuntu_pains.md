### Post by papajoe on 2007-01-04
Sorry to interrupt on the feel-good ... feel of this forum! I wrote a short testimonial about installing Ubuntu 6.10 on my laptop: [http://www.abo.fi/~jstaffan/ubuntu/](http://www.abo.fi/~jstaffan/ubuntu/)

I won't dwell on the details, just on the problem that makes me consider going back to Windows. As I write in the testimonial, I suffer from the constant-HD-activity problem described for example  [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=36976"). Enabling laptop-mode took care of the constant HD activity when the laptop stood idle, but the HD still produces a lot of noise when in use. Imagine clicking a link in Firefox and recieving a grinding noise, four seconds of HD wheezing and then a grinding noise again! (and no, it's not a swap issue)

When running XP on the same computer the hard drive isn't even noticeable, Ubuntu on the other hand makes me notice the hard drive all the time. Call me picky, but this is one of those details that makes all the difference to me. It's a pity, because I enjoy using Ubuntu very much.

Anyway, for now it seems I'm going back to XP - I guess I could try some other distribution and see if the same problems exist outside Ubuntu, but I really don't want to waste any more time installing operating systems and then trying to get all the bits and pieces to work. That's not what computing is about for me.
