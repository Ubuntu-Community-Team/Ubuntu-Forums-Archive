### Post by dookiebowser on 2010-06-11
Everything with 10.4 worked out of the box for me spare Skype so far. That was actually a problem with Skype though and not Ubuntu. Updating to the newest proprietary nVidia drivers gave me a screen flicker which was fixed easily by setting the resolution manually rather than it's default of automatic. Those are the only problems I've had with 10.4

I have an HP pavilion laptop. Nothing fancy, nothing spanking new and nothing rare and old. 

Here's a great rule of thumb for distro releases: if it already does what you need it to do...don't upgrade. This is especially true for production machines. Or...look at the changelog and see if it is relative. If it isn't relative to your situation, don't upgrade.

I just reformatted and set up Win7/Ubuntu 10.4 dualboot and it was much quicker to get Ubuntu to where I wanted it than it was to get Win7 to that spot. I have to literally scour google to find the programs that I use regularly, and even if I was organized and had them bookmarked it would still be a much bigger pain than using a package manager. 

To be fair though, there is a program called [Ketarin]("http://fff") for Windows that keeps setup packages up to date. You have to give it URLs of the programs though, and know some regex if the setup URL is one that changes (like by date/version/release).

### Post by QIII on 2010-06-11
Bear in mind that Mint is a derivative of Ubuntu (itself a derivative of Debian).

Mint adds some things that Ubuntu does not because that is what the developers of Mint have decided to do.

The developers of Ubuntu do no include proprietary applications by default.  Flash is one of them.

Ubuntu makes fewer assumptions about what you want by default and allows you to add what you want as you choose.

"Why can't they...?"  They could.  They don't.  They leave it up to you to decide what you want.

I, for one, like it like that.

Oh... By the way.  Fedora does not do everything you might want out of the box.  Everything some might want, but not everything others might want.
