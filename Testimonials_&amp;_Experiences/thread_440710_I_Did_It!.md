### Post by muddler on 2007-05-11
It started several months ago when I learned about Live CD. I had been interested in open source software for some time. I used Firefox. I used Thunderbird. The only Microsoft program I used with any frequency was MS Office. My computer requirements are quite modest. 

I downloaded Ubuntu 6.06. At first I just “played” with the Live CD. It was not long before I realized that I liked it. Still reluctant to give up the security of familiar Windows XP, I installed Ubuntu as a dual-boot with Windows. As I learned more about Linux and Gnome/Ubuntu, the more it appealed to me. I noticed that despite little glitches form time to time, I was having fewer problems than with Windows. One of my daughters, a granddaughter and I are doing some family research. We frequently exchange documents. Open Office was out there waiting. There has been absolutely no problem with compatibility as they continue to use Windows while I switched to Open Office Writer. Later it dawned on me that I had not booted Windows in a long time. What was I missing? Why do I have Windows on my hard drive taking up space that is not being used? Admittedly with some reluctance, I did a clean boot.

Now my computer looks the way I want it to look, not the way Bill Gates wants it to look. I have downloaded the programs that I want. I feel like I am in charge. It is liberating. It also is an ego booster.  Linux is something that only young kids and geeks understand, isn't it? But this eighty-four year old nubie did it. so I guess anyone can.
If you are undecided about taking the plunge, I encourage you to “check it out.” You can do it a step at a time and decide for yourself. In my case there is no question or doubt. Ubuntu is the way to go. Even Michael has Ubuntu on his new laptop.
