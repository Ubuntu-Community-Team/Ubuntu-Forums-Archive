### Post by azimuth on 2008-02-18
It is really strange that the first post some make here is a goodbye. On the one hand it really chaps my a.... donkey. On the other hand I realize that after 15 or 20 years of Windows, most computer users have been trained to work on their own. They have no place to get the technical and emotional support that they need. It's find the fix yourself or you're out of luck. That's not the way it works in Ubuntu. Ubuntu is not just an OS, it is a community of dedicated volunteers willing to code the OS and help make it usable for the individual. Many in the forums do not have the technical skills to write the code, but they do have the experience to identify the bugs and find ways to circumvent them until they are repaired. If you have problems, you are not alone. We have all had our share of problems, we just asked for help when we couldn't find the results on our own. If you are still too proud of your Windows skills, then you had best use an OS where they have some meaning. 
The ability to keep a Win system running is one of the biggest detriments to a new Ubuntu user. 90% of what he knows is not about computers, but rather about Windows. I hate the "Linux isn't Windows" line, but thankfully it is absolutely true. This is not so much about the OS as it is about the community that comes with Ubuntu. If you are intent on leaving, Goodbye and Good Luck. If you would rather not let your problems get the best of you, make your second post a request for help in the Network or General help forums. The people there will help you fix your smb issue, and they won't quit unless you do.

edit: I see you posted while I was writing this, so make the help request your 3rd post.
