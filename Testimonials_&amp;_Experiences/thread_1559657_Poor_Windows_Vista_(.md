### Post by yankeeboy254 on 2010-08-23
So, back in 2007, my mom got a laptop at Wal-Mart. Some Dell Inspiron POS that my mom probably paid $200. She got it for my dad as a present, as back then, I did not have my laptop. It had Windows Vista on it. Here are the specs:
896MB RAM
2GHz AMD Sempron
80GB 5400RPM HDD

Yes, I say again, running Windows Vista. So, I set it up, and it was extremely slow of course. My dad never really used it, only occasionally. It was barely usable, I mean, it took around five minutes for firefox to open. Eventually, it was just put in our dining room (we eat in our family room in front of the TV :D), and wasn't used for many months. Well, back in September I discovered Ubuntu, and put it on my new laptop, which I got for my birthday. The rest is history. So, several months ago, I decided I would install Xubuntu on it. So, I installed it as a dual boot with Vista. Set it up, got open source wireless drivers up, and it worked great. One day I tried booting into Vista just to see if it worked.

When I hit enter in the grub, a few seconds later a black screen came up, and then it rebooted the laptop. I then booted into GPartED and deleted Windows. Now, my brother generally uses the laptop, sitting on our couch with Xubuntu on it. Plays online games, talks to friends, and loves it. I'm thinking about turning it into an HTPC for our TV, but we'll see. So, that is the story of my mom's formerly useless laptop, and Ubuntu.
Thanks yet again Ubuntu. 
Kind of sounds like a super hero...
