### Post by 3rdalbum on 2013-01-08
> **doxramos said:**
> I've always loved Ubuntu since I first was introduced to it by a friend. However. Nowdays I feel like it's trying to compete with Windows. I recently upgraded to 12.10 from 10.04 on my Laptop and with it came more issues. Kind of surprised to be honest. As well as the interface on 12.10 opposed to 10.04. Before 10.04 we were also able to customize our desktop far more in my opinion. Does anybody else feel like Ubuntu is taking away the customization aspect of the OS or is it just me?

How so?

Before, you could customise Gnome, or choose KDE or XFCE or LXDE and customise those.

Now, you can customise Gnome, or choose KDE or XFCE or LXDE and customise those, or use Unity and have a limited amount of customisation power over that.

Technically, you've got one additional option rather than any fewer options.

There's currently a shift in computing towards less customization ability, for the sake of user-friendliness; this is not limited to Ubuntu nor even to Gnome. To use a perfect Ubuntu example, one of the most common questions on Ubuntu Forums back in the Gnome 2 days was "I tried to change my panel and now I've lost my network and sound icons and I can't get them back!". As it's not trivial to customise your panel on Unity, we rarely get this question, and when we do it's because the question asker is using 10.04.

The average user, even the average Ubuntu user, does not go for far-out customisation. For several releases of Ubuntu before the switch to Unity, my desktop was pretty well stock.

At some point, computing had to grow up and become more of a "product" than "something to mess around with for the sake of it". It's happening now, and what Ubuntu and Gnome is doing is simply a reflection of this maturing process.
