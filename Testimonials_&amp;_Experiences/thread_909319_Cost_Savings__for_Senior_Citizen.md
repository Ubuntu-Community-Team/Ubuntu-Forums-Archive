### Post by Sharka on 2008-09-03
I managed to pick up a low cost PC ($199 - 512MB, Celeron, 160G)with Ubuntu (Hardy Heron) preinstalled from Microcenter for my father whose former computer died suddenly.

I have very little experience with Ubuntu but did dabble with it last year on my XP machine. 

My initial experience was frustrating as the Codecs were not installed by default and my father is a avid fan of BBC News which broadcasts in Real Audio or Windows Media Player. I spent some time researching how to install the Codecs but gave up. I took the computer to my friend who introduced me to Linux and he managed to install all the necessary codecs to play BBC news programs.

Two weeks into the process, I have found significant cost savings:

Choosing Ubuntu OS CPU instead of VISTA:              $100
                              
Antivirus @ ($40/yr for expected life of 5 years)     $200

A total savings of $300 or in other words not spending 1.5 times more on acquiring a VISTA machine.

The cost savings would be more if I did have to install additional memory as the cheapest VISTA CPU retails for $299 and comes with 1GB of memory.

My father so far has gotten used to using Ubuntu for his internet surfing, emailing and finds it quite easy to use Open Office (I shudder to think how expensive Microsoft Office 2007 is and how different it is for a previous user of Office 2003 to use).

Then there are the non monetary savings of which is peace of mind attained from no requests to help deal with no machine slow downs, viruses & malware issues. 

My only gripe with Ubuntu is the codecs issue. I believe every retailer who does sell Ubuntu machines should either put them on or provide resources for a user to do it themselves.

Overall, I am quite impressed with Hardy Heron. It is much faster than my Vista laptop and quite easy to use.

Easy way to install the codecs:

Applications > Add/Remove > Show: change to 'All Avalaible Applications' > Search: restricted > click on the top box next to Ubuntu Restricted Extras > Apply changes > Apply > Close
