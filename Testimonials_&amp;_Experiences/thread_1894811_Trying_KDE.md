### Post by realitykid on 2011-12-13
Hello all. Just letting you all know that I am trying out KDE for a little while to see if I like it compared to Unity. I already know that I don't like gnome-shell. But I really enjoy Unity and, so far, KDE.

I had to go through and configure it the way that I like it though. The default install left me very unsatisfied as I hated the way it looked (actually gave me a nausiating feeling). So I changed the theme, including the icons.

Now, I still have Unity, as this I installed KDE on vanilla Ubuntu 11.10. So I'll be able to drop back into that when I'd like to. I may keep it this way, or just move to Kubuntu in 12.04. However, I doubt I'll move to Kubuntu. As I said, I still enjoy Unity.

One pet peave I have  with KDE already: I can't go into the settings for my laptop touchpad and make it so that the touchpad turns off during typing. It gets really annoying when I am typing, and my palm hits the touchpad by accident, causing my entire windows to scroll down, confusing me, and almost making me lose track of what I was doing.

Edit 12/15/11: Problem with the touchpad solved. I found the option in synaptiks. Thanks [McNils]("http://ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=213884").

Anyway, I'll attach a screenshot of what I have so far. Yes, I am using rekonq. I also decided to test that and see if I like it compared to FireFox.

I'll keep you all updated as my little experiment presses on. If anything changes, I'll update this thread with my new experiences.

Mods/Admins: Feel free to move this thread if it'd be more suited in another location.

UPDATE 12/15/11: So far, everything is working perfectly. I honestly can't complain about much right now. I honestly can't see why people didn't like KDE 4 when it first released. Must have had some weird bugs or something. Oh well. It's working great now. Still not sure if I'm going to go straight Kubuntu and ditch Unity in 12.04. If anything changes, I'll let you all know.
