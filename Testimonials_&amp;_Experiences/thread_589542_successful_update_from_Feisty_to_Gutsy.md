### Post by Denis on 2007-10-24
Hello everyone

Yesterday I have successfully updated Feisty to Gutsy!

The update process went absolutely smooth, without any problems. The update manager, which I used, worked very well. The only thing I had to do was to answer whether I wanted to keep some config files or replace them. I was also impressed by the download speed of the packages (about 1000 KB/s). After the update, the computer rebooted into my new Gutsy system. 

I like the updated packages in Gutsy Gibbon: Firefox, The Gimp, Thunderbird etc. Also the new features are very nice. I think Compiz is nicely integrated in the system: present, but not dominant or distracting. The "Compiz configuration settings manager" which can easily be installed, provides a convenient way to play with all the options that Compiz has. And believe me, there is a lot you can change. Also the active theme was still in use after the update. What I think is very handy is that the various settings concerning the appearance of your system are now grouped in the "appearence" window. 

Some things that bothered me in the past were also fixed: 

[LIST]
[*]My scanner (CanoScan LiDE 25) works properly again
[*]The text during a non-quiet boot is now orange instead of blue
[*]Some bugs in the previous Nvidia drivers are fixed
[/LIST]

The only problem I had was fullscreen task switching (with compiz enabled). But I'm sure there is a solution for that, now or in the future. 

My general impression is that things just got better. So I congratulate all the developers of Ubuntu and the various programs. This really is a great release :-D

I posted my experiences to show that there are a lot of people who are very satisfied with Gutsy Gibbon. Even though you mostly read about the problems people have on these forums. I can advice everyone to update, it's worth it.

### Post by Denis on 2007-11-02
> **RawMustard said:**
> Just out of curiousity, you guys prasing the smooth upgrade, do you's guys tweak your system at all or use packages other than Ubuntu provided ones?

I usually change some system settings in config files. As mentioned in my post, I was asked to replace them or keep the modified files. Which worked pretty well for me. 

Though I don't use a lot of non-ubuntu packages. And I never tweak my kernel or any other important piece of software, because that could make updating a bit more awkward. 

I do use some third party software, like google earth and frostwire. But I always try to install this from other repositories or DEB packages (afterwards I also remove those repositories from my list). 

As you see, I somehow restrict myself in using other packages or tweaking my system. Most of the time, when I would like a never version of a package I just wait for the next release. But I'm pretty happy with the freedom I have now. 

Making the update work is also part of your own responsibility. If you manually installed lots of programs, compiled yourself a new kernel and managed to install all the latest drivers, you should be aware that this may cause trouble updating.
