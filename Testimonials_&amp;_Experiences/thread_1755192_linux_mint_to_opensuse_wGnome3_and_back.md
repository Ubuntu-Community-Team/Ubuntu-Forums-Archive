### Post by DarkSideKlyde on 2011-05-10
I have a netbook, an old Acer Aspire One ZG5. After dual-booting Ubuntu and XP for quite a long time, I gave up my MMO (Guild Wars) and my pirated Windows software in order to have a 'FREE' operating system and did a fresh install of Ubuntu 10.10. Shortly thereafter I switched to Linux Mint 10 and was very comfortable there. After about 6 months on LM10 I got the "Gnome 3 Fever" and began searching for a distro that offered Gnome 3 with Gnome Shell. I hated Unity, and for some reason thought Gnome Shell would be better. I had tried Ubuntu Netbook Remix before with Unity, but was disillusioned by the fancy videos on Gnome3's website. Anyway, I tried compiling Gnome 3/Gnome Shell in Linux Mint 10 and was unsuccessful. I backed up all my data on my roommates hard drive (mine is broken at the moment and my roommate was gracious enough to lend me his) and downloaded openSUSE11.4 with a somewhat vague promise of GNOME3. After some crafty command line work (dd_rescue) I got openSUSE 11.4 installed on a flash drive. Keep in mind that I don't have an optical drive. I installed openSUSE 11.4 only to find out it dosen't come with GNOME3. I know, I didn't do enough research and GNOME3 w/GnomeShell, which is still not quite ready. Then, after some more research, I downloaded the Gnome3 LiveCD (Try It).  After a bit of boot finagling ('liveinstall' needs to be entered at the boot screen) I finally had and openSUSE system with Gnome3. 

I HATED IT.

Geared toward tablet users or just plain old "simpletons", with a terrible package management system (compared to the Ubuntu/Debian "apt") I was ready to switch back. 

a note : I am getting a new laptop this summer thanks to student loans and will be using this netbook as a secondary device

Wanting to turn my netbook into the simple machine that it is, instead of trying to make it my "all-in-one" PC. I decide to just wipe openSUSE and GNOME3.

             A bit of back story - I decided to install Gnome3 to get the latest greatest thing, since gnome is trying to get away from 2.3.*  Now I am trying to get back to a "desktop" UI.

So to speed up my netbook a bit, and turn it into the netbook that it is, I decided to download and install Lubuntu. Turns out I could not find any program in openSUSE to make a live USB of an Ubuntu distro. 
Unetbootin would not recognize my flash drives (openSUSE has no option to format to FAT32) and after using my SystemRescueCD thumbdrive to format the flash drives, Unetbootin will still not make a live CD out of my .iso downloads of Lubuntu, at least not in openSUSE.

So now I am anxiously awaiting my roommate's return from school tonight so I can use his Windows 7 laptop to run Unetbootin and install a debian based OS on my netbook. 

What I took away from this experience is that once you go away from Ubuntu/Debian based OS's, it is almost impossible to go back, that is, if you don't have a CD/DVD drive. Netbook users BEWARE. Switching from Ubuntu to openSUSE and back is not easy!!!!!!!

I could have easily burned an Ubuntu based/Debian based distro to a cd or dvd if i had an optical drive and been rid of openSUSE. My problem was that openSuse wouldn't let me go back to Ubunut/Debian easily (or at all!!). I couldn't even format my flash drive to FAT. I have been distro hopping for a while, and have finally decided to stick to Ubuntu-based Linux. As much as I wanted to like the openSUSE platform, there were just too many differences. Maybe it was just Gnome3. I don't know. I installed Lubuntu after getting my roommate to use Unetbootin on his Windows machine. After a test run I decided I hated LXDE (I'm fickle, I know). I'm now running Linux Mint 11 RC, with the old Gnome 2.3 **. I'll probably just stick here for a while. I love Mint. I was just trying to get the newest, latest thing. I love Gnome 2.3.**. In closing, my netbook will be great/safe for testing new distros once I get a new laptop with an optical drive (i could have easily gone from openSUSE to Ubuntu if I could have burned a liveCD).

@MaxCarnage - I tried to install Ubuntu 11.04 in VirtualBox. It took a little over an hour to install. I only have 1GB of RAM on my netbook and I dedicated half of it to my 11.04 install. NOT ENOUGH. My roommate has 8 GB of RAM and can easily run 4 different OS's (Win 7 and 3 in VB). I used VirtualBox to install XP in order to use Internet Explorer to fill out my FAFSA for school (before I figured out you could install IE7 with PlayonLinux). I love VB, and it is my fave opensource software, just doesn't run that well with only 1 GB of RAM, lol.

@mastablasta - I installed Unetbootin in openSUSE. It wouldn't recognize my flash drives unless I formated them to FAT32. I could not format to FAT using openSUSE. ImageWriter wouldn't recognize Ubuntu as a valid image to write. I tried compiling Gnome3 from source using jhbuild before and after about 8 hours and a few too many errors it failed. Just as well, I hated Gnome3 w/ Gnome Shell once I finally got it installed. 

In the end, I got my roommate to make a live Lubuntu USB using his Windows machine and Unetbootin. I installed Lubuntu and messed around with it for a while and after some bad file transfer experiences (13 hours for 46GB and 20 min for 800MB) I decided to just install Linux Mint again. I am currently running Linux Mint 11 RC. 20 minutes for 46GB file transfer with USB 2.0. I will probably stick with Mint 11 RC until the official release comes out, then reinstall/upgrade (upgrade if I can). My point was that going from Ubuntu to openSUSE and then back was a nightmare without an optical drive. Thanks for listening to my rant!!!
