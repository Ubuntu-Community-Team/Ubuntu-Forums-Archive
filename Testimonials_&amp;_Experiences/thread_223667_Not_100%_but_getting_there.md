### Post by navymom on 2006-07-26
I've just installed it on my computer to dual boot with Windows XP Professional and I love it.  I am hoping to eventually do away with Windows completely.

I've been wanting to get away from Microsoft ever since they came out with XP.  I don't mind buying a software package/operating system or anything like that.  I know a lot of hard work goes into the development.  That's not a problem.  What is a problem is that I have two computers in my home (well actually there are about a dozen but they're mainly to tinker with and use for spare parts).  I feel like if I buy a program and it's something I have to pay $200.00 for, I should be allowed to put it on any computer in my house.  I don't feel like buying it gives me the right to give it to every friend I have that wants to put it on their systems, but still, for my private use, I should be allowed to do it.  XP put an end to that with it's authorization codes and activation.

I won't say that it's easier than Windows because for the average Windows user, if they have little experience with the command line they're going to have a lot of questions.  But for me, I like playing around with things.  If you're going to give it to your grandmother so she can use email to get pics of the grandkids, set it up for her.  Once it's set up and everything is working fine, it's a great system.

So far I love Ubuntu.  I wasn't sure if I would or not until I actually got my livecd.  The thing that sold me on it was probably the dumbest reason in the world...but it's what made me love it instantly...It comes with Mah Jong. lol  For me it works great.  As for my husband and my kids, they loved it after everything was set up for them.  

It's still not our only operating system.  We still have to use XP to connect to the internet.  But I've been busy this week and haven't had a chance to go modem hunting yet.  Hopefully, this weekend will put Windows behind me forever.

I just feel like Microsoft is too greedy.  The cost of their programs (Microsoft Office for me was 700.00) are outrageous and always needing patches and fixes for bugs.  I love Open Office!  We run a business and it does everything we need.  I also feel like people should have a choice and with the way most computers come with Windows preinstalled, I feel like most of the time we don't have that choice.  It's shoved down our throats and it's always more more more on the money issue.  If you want it to do this, you're going to have to buy that.

As for some of the things I've heard about it not being recommended for dialup users...Well, that depends on how bad you want it.  I wanted Macromedia's Studio 8.  It took me 4 days to d/l it but I wanted it and I did it.  I could have ordered it on a cd, but the way I see that is, it's going to take a few weeks to get here anyway, so what's the big deal with just d/ling it and getting it quicker?  Four days is still better than 2-4 weeks.  Btw, that cost me almost 1,000.00 lol.  Gosh I'm cheap!  So, me personally, I don't mind the large d/ls to update.  I do it anyway with XP.  I may as well be doing it with an OS that gives me what I want without bankrupting me in the process.

So if you're a tinkerer (I just created a new word), you like playing around with things, and you're stubborn like me, it's the system for you.

### Post by navymom on 2006-08-01
I know people will get tired of seeing this thread pop up from time to time, but the more I work around in Ubuntu, the better I like it and just feel the need to express my awe over this operating system.

At first I said that it wasn't as easy as Windows...Well initially it wasn't...But the more I work around in it, the easier I've found it's become and it just blows me away.  Here's an example of what I'm talking about.

A lot of people, well everyone probably, when first starting out with this system want to know which programs are good for this.  At first I was searching through the thread in the Absolute Beginners section for programs that did what I needed.  But, geez, there are almost 700 replies in there to look through!  And a lot of times I didn't have the time to just sit there and browse before I could locate what I wanted.  Then one glorious day I noticed the "Search Icon" in Synaptic....I clicked on it...Typed in the word "virus" and bam!  There it was!  I huge list of virus software I could use.  All I had to do was browse through the list, pick out the one I wanted, click apply and it told me the other files I would need and I clicked apply again and it installed it for me!  Even better than if I had put in a cd to do it in Windows!

I did the same thing by putting in "3D" in the add/remove programs...It showed me Blender 3D and I love that program!  I had d/led it in the Linux version from the Blender site but hadn't tried installing it yet. But there it was already in my list.

*Everything* that I've wanted so far has been so easy to obtain just by doing a simple search in the programs already provided by Ubuntu.  So in case you've noticed since I've installed this I haven't been back around a lot, it's because of the ease I'm having thus far to the transition from Windows to Ubuntu!

Since I got my modem to working I haven't been back into Windows a single time.  Everything I normally do in Windows I can do in Ubuntu now.  I can't say the same for my family, though, unfortunately.  It's not that Ubuntu doesn't do what they need because it does...all of it...They are just to used to Windows.  So far only one besides me uses Ubuntu for everything.
