### Post by RickoDean on 2011-04-26
After being a longtime fan of Windows os, I learned that there is light at the end of the tunnel and it comes in the form of the Ubuntu OS. Not only is it free, attractive and easy to use; but I can learn to love & develop with this platform. With me being a freelance web developer, usability, ease of use and speed is a must have. The thing that won me over is the fact that the linux like interface (kinda) reminds me of a web server that is on my desktop. I am able to access my hosting server directly from connect to server feature. windows may have this feature but at least the Ubuntu version puts it right in your face & will have you there in a matter of a few clicks without even using a web or ftp browser to access cpanel.

The next thing that got my attention is the system is app driven. There are no executional files, thus closing the window (a bit) to viruses & most importantly, you can easily customize your desktop to cater to your own look and feel. The only thing I dislike is I can't use pro tools in the Ubuntu environment. If I could, all three of my machines would be powered by Ubuntu. But until then, I enjoy using my Ubuntu desktop for business & education purposes, my notebook for web development. My windows (pro tools) workstation is on reserve. hopefully oneday Ubuntu will be respected enough by pro tools to make a version specifically for this unique system.

But in the meantime, I am here to support this community because I found something that I can actually work with. I look forward to seeing what version 11.04 brings to the table & I will continue to tell my friends about the wonderful benefits of Ubuntu. Regardless of the neigh sayers have to say; I think Ubuntu is a wonderful platform to work with, all the best  

Cheers,
:popcorn:
