### Post by Old Jimma on 2012-08-24
Hi Ubuntu Community:

I waited for August 24, 2012 to install 12.04.01 one day after it became available.

Being advised on a previous thread to upgrade from 10.04 to 12.04.1 by using the "upgrade" feature of the 10.04 update manager, I gave it a shot.

**[COLOR="DarkGreen"]IN SHORT, I WAS VERY SATISFIED BY THE AUTOMATIC INSTALLATION AND WOULD RECOMMEND IT TO A FRIEND![/COLOR]** :D

Here is what worked: **[COLOR="DarkGreen"]ESSENTIALLY EVERYTHING[/COLOR]**.

With one click and an authentication, 12.04.1 installed seamlessly and quickly (within 1 hour), preserving the moderately complicated set up I used with 10.04. Preserved set up after the installation included:
[LIST]
[*]use of an ssd for my grub boot and the opperating system;
[*]use of a home directory on another hd;
[*]use of a second hard drive to backup the /home directory, as scheduleded by crontab;
[*]almost all of the programs I had installed;
[*]use of superuser crontab to schedule events, like backups, and recording streaming audio, etc.
[/LIST]

As an FYI for Canonical, I noticed during the upgrade that intervention was occasionally required, with a box opening, asking a question, and then giving me the option to go forward with the installation... once or twice the words in the box were not readable... just "boxes" not letter characters. I think this would be a good thing to fix to give users even greater confidence and satisfaction.

After long consideration and searching for alternatives to Ubuntu and Unity, I stuck with Unity. The support is superb and unparralled by any other distro.

:-D

Now that I am using Unity, I can see that it will take a little learning... however, I can see that the adjustment will be a nice journey that will lead to a pleasant destination.

I'm glad I stuck with Ubuntu!

Old Jimma
