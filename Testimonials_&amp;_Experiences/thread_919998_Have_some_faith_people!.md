### Post by Yellow Stevens on 2008-09-14
I just want to get something off my chest. Something that has been bothering me for a while.

I hope you'll forgive my naive enthusiasm.  

I just wanted to express my surprise. Astonishment even.

Ever since I, as a non computer literate individual, started using ubuntu, I have been amazed at the disbelief this has been greeted with from people who are part of the open source community. The first guy who told me about Ubuntu made it sound interesting. So I said to him 'Sounds interesting! I'd like to have something other than windows on my computer.' The open source thing really appealed to my inner commie.

"Oh no you don't want to do that." he said "I only have Ubuntu on my system because I wanted to learn how to code."

He told me about the terminal window and made out like ubuntu users had to spend hours there programming. Effectively he left me with the impression that if you wanted to use ubuntu you were going to have to actually *write* half the OS.

At the University where I am a Christian Chaplain the president of the Alternative Computing Society gave me a copy of Ubuntu and a couple of months later he came round to see how I was doing. I told him I had not only installed it but was using it as my main OS. 

"Whoa!" he said "I'm not sure I'd be that brave!"

Another person who works in the computer science department told me that linux users have to manually configure the sinc rate on their monitors, and some one expressed dismay that I had put ubunut on my main desktop, like why would anyone do that unless they had at least a bachelors in computer science. 

The thing that really amazes me though is the fact that when I tell people I am just Joe Public not having any problems with Ubuntu they tell me I can't possibly be. I must have some professional knowledge of computer from somewhere.

HOW? I'M A PRIEST!

I have had to spend a lot of time finding out how to do stuff some of which 'just works' in vista, and I did find that 8.04 didn't work initially with my NVidia card. Some friends who understood helped me out and it worked. Oh yes and I have had to learn some other stuff like what an NVidia card and an 'OS' actually is. A lot of people who use linux know about computers, which is why they use linux. It also might be why they don't think just anyone can use it. Maybe one day, but not now.

What I am trying to say is simple:

Guys, guys, guys....

HAVE SOME FAITH!

Have some faith in your OS, it is brilliant. It is stable yet flexible, powerful yet uses a fraction of the resources vista requires. It is open yet secure. It is easy to use, maybe not as easy as windows but not far off....and anyone can use it successfully.  

AND it's just fantastic to look at!    

I want to find Mark Shuttleworth and buy him a drink, I want to tell the world about the best computer product since the VIC 20. 

Start believing!


Pleeeeeeeeeeease!!!!!!!

### Post by Abras on 2008-09-16
Yeah, I had a similar experience of expecting Ubuntu to be a lot harder to use than it actually turned out to be. No one really pushed that idea on me, though. I guess I was influenced by the general opinion that Linux is a techy operating system. And the prospect of installing my own OS seemed a bit daunting and possibly reckless at the time. Obviously, Ubuntu proved me wrong and I have been using it for close to a year now. And I've learned a lot, which is always a good thing.
