### Post by Jacobonbuntu on 2011-05-15
....well, I am *starting* to understand; the desktop as it was (and I also think it was good)  was going to end anyway, by the future release of Gnome 3. I took a look at Gnome 3 and I can understand why Ubuntu didn't want to go in that direction. not that I want to criticize Gnome on this forum, but I get the feeling those interfaces are nice to provide visitors in a museum some information on the painting they're looking at. If your computer is not only to look fancy, but needs to be productive in a wide range of tasks, it's horrible to my opinion, simply a suit that's too tight, making you walk in a stiff way.

I guess Ubuntu didn't simply want to reconstruct the Gnome interface as it was, and that's logical. I see Unity as the "birth-pain" of a new interface, no more, no less. Apart from the sometimes excessive reactions (both outrageous critics and aggressive "defenders"), I think it is a good thing to share experiences, positive or negative. 

that's my opinion
