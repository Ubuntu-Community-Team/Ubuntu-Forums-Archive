### Post by moore.bryan on 2008-09-19
so, much of the criticism ubuntu, specifically, and linux, generally, garners has to do with lack of hardware support and/or installation woes. just thought i'd share this with you fine folks:

my father's computer, an "older" dell running xp, died. last thing he saw was a warning from his, very expensive, antivirus program telling him trojan was detected. he has a lot of stuff saved on that hard drive which he needs, so i went over to his house, popped-in the ubuntu 8.04.1 livecd and force-mounted the hard drive to make sure the information was still on the drive. good news: it was there. so i shrunk the ntfs partition down to half the disk, created a new partition in the left-over, and formatted it fat32. scanned his "my documents" and when i found no viruses/questionable files, copied it over to the new fat32 partition.

then came the complete wiping of the ntfs partition and the reinstallation of xp; although ubuntu had apparently "saved his day," he wasn't quite ready to jump onto the linux side. so, i popped-in the reinstallation windows disk, let it do its thing and was greeted with an xp desktop with the wrong resolution (640x480), no internet connectivity, and no sound. now, the ubuntu livecd had the 1024x768 resolution automatically set, the network adapter working fine, and beautiful sound; huh?

turns-out, ubuntu worked **out of the box** on this machine and **windows** needed me to go onto each piece of hardware's specific website, hunting for the correct driver to use. i was spun around so backasswards, i even tried to "sudo aptitude install" at the dos prompt a couple of times!

just thought i'd share...
