### Post by redandgreen on 2008-12-14
So, about a week or so ago, I installed 8.10. I had tried 8.04 for a while, and though I liked it, nothing I could do would get the wireless to work, so it just wasn't feasible as a primary OS. I'm delighted to say that 8.10 has fixed that, the wireless only took a little tweaking and has given me no bother since, and ubuntu is now everything I need in a system.

(including, and most importantly, Tetravex! how did I live without you?!)

Having said that - I had to work through a multitude of little hitches and annoyances to get here (what drugs were they on, thinking that system beep was a good idea?). It was enormous fun doing it, and I learned a lot. But most of the people I know, even the computer-competent ones, just aren't comfortable with anything different or new. When you can't convince people to switch from IE to firefox, how can you hope to get them to give linux a try?

At the moment - I'm a complete novice, and that's a strange feeling, after being so windows-savvy. I have so much still to learn, and I want to know it all *now*! :P I'm not convinced I'll stay with ubuntu forever, either - there are so many linuxes out there to try. And unixes. And others. 

Still, hopefully in a year or two, I'll be answering questions on the forums instead of asking them :P I honestly can't ever see myself needing to go back to windows, and I hope I never have to.

### Post by Haruhara on 2009-02-21
My experiences have been similar. I was using Ubuntu as a primary desktop for about 4 months straight, and I still use it frequently (the linux machine is now my media centre/projector machine). The reason I installed it in the first place was because I wanted to learn more about Linux in general. Unfortunately for me, I can count on one hand the number of times I've actually needed to use the terminal. I really haven't learned a lot. 

I guess it just proves how easy to use the system is. I really like how it just plain works with things. It's always hit or miss, fingers crossed, pray-that-the-last-patch-fixed-the-driver-issue with Vista. Neither my cellphone or printer worked with Vista initially, and I've had to go reinstall sound and chipset drivers more than once. No problems with Ubuntu. The thing just works.
