### Post by jso2897 on 2009-11-17
I've had a mixed experience with it. On my more or less contemporary box, 64-bit is running great - they apparently fixed a lot of broken stuff, at the last RC. On a kind of mid-range machine (P4, 512mb ram)it ran OK, but I decided to go back to *.04 - it runs a little faster, it can still use the old HP printer drivers that I like - so I'll worry about that box come Spring of '11.

My legacy PIII boxes, however, are dead in the water with karmic. Won't boot from GRUB - says error, object kernel not found. This is on two different machines, both Dells. But thye still run XP fine, and Hardy, of course.
I'm a little saddened, but an O/S has to move forward. No O/S supplies legacy support that goes on forever, and sooner or later you have to retire your old stuff.
Some time in the mid nineties, they learned to make computers and computer components well enough that they often last well past the point of obsolescence, and one has to part with reliable old equipment that has stood the test of time. It's sad - especially with Ubuntu, since legacy support has been such a tradition - but I guess there are practical limits to it. I know that the BIOS on both those old machines have been flashed with the latest updates - but even those are very old.
Cest la vie.:(
