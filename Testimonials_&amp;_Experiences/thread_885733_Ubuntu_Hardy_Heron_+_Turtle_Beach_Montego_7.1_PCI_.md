### Post by Roasted on 2008-08-10
I have used the Turtle Beach Montego 7.1 PCI Sound Card with both 32 bit and 64 bit Ubuntu Hardy Heron 8.04 with ALSA.

32 bit - Stable as ever. Works perfectly out of box. With all settings maxed out, it still leaves my speakers reaching volume levels with such clarity I didn't even know was possible out of a walmart desktop stereo system. I am running something almost identical to this through auxillary on my sound card.

[http://www.110220volts.com/Merchant2/graphics/00000001/nsxs777a.jpg](http://www.110220volts.com/Merchant2/graphics/00000001/nsxs777a.jpg)

With that being said, I haven't experienced true 5.1 or 7.1 surround sound with this card. I heard rumors on reviews and such it works fine, but I personally have not tinkered with it. I simply use my desktop stereo system which serves me absolutely fine.

64 bit - ehh, not as stable as 32 bit. It's evident in a lot of situations that 64 bit is not ready to fill the stable shoes of 32 bit and take off riding into the sunset. I had a few qwirky things happen with 64 bit in general, let alone relating to the sound card in particular. The particular problems I had with 64 bit and the Montego was with Amarok freezing. Anything music related would often crash on me. Oddly enough, even after ending/killing the process and it being removed from the process list, the application would still be on my screen and fully functional. However, I do get sound out of the sound card... so it's evident that the developers are working on it, but it's "just not there quite yet." Kudos to them for their work, but for the time being, 32 bit is just the more logical way to go.

Other thoughts - I purchased the SIIG SoundWave 7.1 PCI Sound Card before trying out this card. It gave me the exact same reaction the Turtle Beach Montego did when using 64 bit Ubuntu. I didn't even realize I was running 64 bit at the time. I installed 64 bit out of curiosity reasons and left it there. I ended up returning the SIIG SoundWave before realizing I didn't test it in 32 bit. I'm telling you this because I'm pretty sure the SIIG and Montego use the same C-Media chipset, which is supported by ALSA. It's very possible that my SIIG SoundWave 7.1 was compatible with Ubuntu, however, I only ran into issues with it in 64 bit environment. I am not sure if this is the case, however, it's just a thought I want to throw out to people.

Overall - the Turtle Beach brand is a pretty damn good one, and I am very happy to know that I was able to spend 60 dollars (NewEgg) to have the item shipped to me in such a fast rate, pop it in, and bam... it just works.

And now, I am off to spend my Sunday afternoon listening to my live concert DVD of Pearl Jam... Live in Madison Square Garden.
