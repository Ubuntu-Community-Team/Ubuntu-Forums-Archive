### Post by Xploit on 2007-01-03
I started using kubuntu back with breezy.  From there on I upgraded to dapper.  Since I only partitioned 5 gigs I figured I would do a nice fresh install of edgy.  Still all on kubuntu.  I was upset for about whole 10 seconds because I had customized the hell out of my kde desktop before.  But that all changed very fast.

First was to get wireless working.  Using a broadcom chip I have ndiswrapper down pretty good by now.  But with edgy all i had to do was strip the firmware and wireless was up and working.  All in about 2 minutes.  That blew my socks away.

Next was Ati drivers.  I have always used official drives in the past but this time I kept hearing bad things about 8.32.5 drivers and on top of that there was no good tutorial for official Ati drivers on edgy.  So I apt-get the ati drivers in the repos.  This blew my socks off again.  Not only was I getting good frames but direct rendering was also working and i only had to make one line change to my xorg.config file.

Next was to get WPA2 support.  In the past I always had just MAC address filter on my router cause I couldn't get WPA or WEP to work.  I downloaded KNetworkManager and WPA was working instantly.  No files to edit, simple straight up GUI just like in the WINBLOZE world.

Last but not the least installed XGL, ran it as a new session and fired up beryl.  All worked without flaw. 

On dapper and breezy this would have all taken me about 1-2 weeks.

On edgy I had everything up in 3 days.....actual working time was only about 6 hours.  The rest was finding good write-ups online.

Hats off to the ubuntu team for one kick *** distro.
