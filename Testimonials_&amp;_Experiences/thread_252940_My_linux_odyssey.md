### Post by ago on 2006-09-07
My first encounter with Linux was with Mandrake (now Mandriva), I do not even rememeber the year (1999?). I just did not like it too much, I could see that Linux was promising on the desktop, but initially I was dual-booting a lot, then I gave up and kept using windows at home.  In the office I started experimenting with RH, not much better than Mandrake, but I was using the CLI a lot more and did not mind, the machines were working really well, and NT was out. 

The thing I liked best were the linux people. With windows, when I had a problem, I often received the 4R-solution, I am sure you are familiar with it... 

[list=1][*]Restart the app
[*]Restart windows 
[*]Reinstall the app 
[*]Reinstall windows[/list] 

Thank you! 

I normally can google out of problems by myself, so when I get stucked it is not generally something too obvious, and when that happened in windows I often got the 4Rs, even by supposedly knowledgeable people. I knew that the 4Rs were an admission of ignorance. 

I still remember the first time I tried to get help on a Linux chat (with RH), it went like this: 

[list][*]ago: what the hell is happening here?
[*]rhx: execute this
[*]ago: done, so?
[*]rhx: what is the output
[*]ago: this
[*]rhx: ok then run this
[*]ago: one moment
[*]ago: I don't believe that!
[*]rhx: What?
[*]ago: it works!!!
[*]ago: I mean, it really works!!![/list]

I could not believe my luck, I had managed to meet the master of the universe on my first try! But then it happened again, and again, and again. Granted, it wasn't always that easy, but nobody, ever, suggested to restart the system, let alone reinstall it... I was impressed. 

At the time I had used windows for 10 years, I mean fairly heavy use (I started programming since C64, and used windows since its very first releases on a 386, I must have installed it about 50 times on different occasions), and still I never even got close to that level of proficiency, in fact I could not think of anyone I knew that was that knowledgeable in Windows. With Windows it was more monkey-see-monkey-do type of knowledge: you want to do this? Press that... And at every new windows version, it was the same story: "where did the *** button go???". And when something went wrong... ...the 4Rs. But those Linux guys seemd to really understand their OS! I certainly did not understand mine, and I was considered a sort of "guru" by all my friends and colleagues. I knew I had to dig deeper.

So, I tried Gentoo, at home. The installation literally took days (I started straight form stage1) but boy was that a REALLY great experience! Gentoo is one of the most instructive distro you can try, and its docs are second to none. Anyone who is in linux-learning-mood, should give Gentoo a go. Finally I felt like I discovered Linux, I could start understanding how a computer worked... Plus portage was just great, one command and KDE was up with everything else it needed (after a bit of compiling that is)! It made me feel the pain every time I had to install an rpm, and plain guilty whenever I doubleclicked on a setup.exe file... In short I liked it, a system I could tweak to my heart content, a system where to discover all sort of OSS jewels (including lots of desktop environments far beyond kde/gnome). I moved all PCs in the house to it, windows was out. Mind you, I am not a gamer, I was having much more fun playing with software. Of my 3 machines at home, only one remained with windows, and in dual boot. 

But, like others, I soon got bored of all the compiling, honestly, I do not care if my system is X% faster, I do not even want to think about the appropriate flags anymore. Yes, I had cron jobs for it, and yes, I knew I could use binaries in Gentoo too, but it just did not feel right. If I had to use binaries I thought I would rather use a distro similar to Gentoo, but designed for binaries. I also wanted to replace RH in the office but never really felt confortable with the idea of having Gentoo in there. So I started looking for something else. The number 1 requirement was a package manager as close as possible to portage, I just could not see myself using a machine without something as powerful as that. But I also wanted a system designed for pre-built packages, with a strong reputation on the server market, and possibly free as in freedom (and as in beer). I steered well away of RH/mandrake & co and I met Debian. It seemd to fit my bill quite well, and the whole philosophy behind it was intriguing. I quickly moved up from stable to unstable (the office set-ups remained on stable). It was love. 

Then Warty came out... I was pretty happy with my set-up and VERY scheptical about "easy to use" distros... But my friends always asked for suggestions and I could not point them straight to Debian/Gentoo. So I figured that if I had to try an "easy" distro, a debian based one was my best shot. At the time Ubuntu was moving its first steps, but it was moving quite quickly for a newborn, and it was soon climbing DistroWatch, just behind Mepis. Choosing between Ubuntu and Mepis was easy: Ubuntu was less compromising on freedom, I liked that. But I had little hope. 

Imagine the surprise. The setup was close to my Debian install, just without the hours it took me to get to (almost) the same point! Almost all of my hardware was working out of the box, even screen resolution was correct! And I could do everything I could do before in Debian and even in Gentoo (ok... almost everything)... Since then I have never moved back, I have never even tried another distro. I guess I am just becoming lazier...
