### Post by elvinatom on 2009-07-12
Don't ask me for specifics, but in the latest couple of versions, Ubuntu came up with some bug - right out of the box.  Now I'm not the bickering type - especially since it's all free software - but I must say the quality went down since feisty (when I switched to Ubuntu as main OS):
* Laptops that run on a wireless network don't shut down properly,
* X hangs at least 2-3 times a week. (with Intel or Nvidia video)
* Network manager seems to disconnect sometimes for no apparent reason
* Compiz not working right with all applications (I know it's more of a compiz issue, but since Ubuntu installs it by default ... you get my point)
* so many other quirks that get annoying.

I stuck with Ubuntu as default distro for over two years and loved all of its innovation and "user friendliness", but now I consider moving to debian.  A little behind all the time, but no functionality problems whatsoever.  And by the way: the wireless/shutdown issue is embarrassing, since it's a known bug since when ... half a year or more?

Now you can go ahead and tell me how unreasonable I am or how it's basically my fault.

### Post by calrogman on 2009-07-13
> **elvinatom said:**
> But now I consider moving to debian.  A little behind all the time, but no functionality problems whatsoever.

Replace every occurrence of "lenny" with "testing" in /etc/apt/source.list (make sure you don't have the volatile repo enabled, it's only available for stable and old-stable), then run:```
apt-get update; apt-get dist-upgrade
```BAM!  Reasonably up to date installation of Debian with very few bugs.

You just need to remember to upgrade everything every two or three days.
