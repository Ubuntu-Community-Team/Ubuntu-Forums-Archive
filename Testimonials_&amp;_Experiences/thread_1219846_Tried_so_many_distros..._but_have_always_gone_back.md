### Post by manilaph on 2009-07-22
I've been distro-hopping and fully-installing various distros. After a week or two with a particular distro, i try another one to see what best suits my needs.

After all this time.. I have not found any distro that can beat Ubuntu in terms of ease of installation, friendly and helpful community, the much-needed proprietary hardware drivers and non-free software, the ease of adding new software .... etc etc etc.

Beginners will have difficulty in those distros like Fedora, Opensuse, Sidux, Debian etc... those that only have free open-source software and drivers. They may try those distros... but will land in Ubuntu.

For me, the only other easy-to-install distro that is also newbie-friendly with all those proprietary stuff is Mandriva.

I've tried to stay away from Ubuntu (just recently) by installing Kubuntu... but encountered a very big road-block... NVIDIA screen resolution problems which seems to be a very popular topic on the threads nowadays. Kubuntu developers (or the KDE) guys should fix this for the next release. I havent tried Xubuntu 9.04 but I am currently downloading it. 

Lastly, I did not include Linux-Mint since it is an Ubuntu clone with more spice added and I consider them to be one and the same. (not the same "one and the sameness" as Ubuntu with Debian).

Distro-hopping is fun... it makes you realize that Ubuntu is the best and its popularity speaks for itself.
