### Post by jojopumpkin on 2008-04-03
My wife and I are expecting our first child in a few weeks. We are very excited but also sad at the fact we live in Tucson, AZ and our family lives in New England so sharing this event with them will have to happen online.

My wife purchased a Digital Video Camera for me as a Daddy gift and I was thrilled thinking I could take video at the hospital then post them on our blog so our family can see them almost immediately. I figured I would use my laptop {Dell Inspron 2600 with XPsp2) at the hospital to do this. I bought a firewire cardbus and proceeded to check things out and see how it all works. I connected it up and tried to capture the video and the computer froze instantly. I tried again with limited processes running and still no capture and a frozen system. It seemed to work fine on my Desktop XP machine and I thought I was out of luck and would have to wait until we got home from the hospital because my laptop is just inadequate for the task.

I then thought "I bet I could get a better performance out of my laptop and this camera if I tried Ubuntu" (having about 6 months of limited Ubuntu experience).

I did some research into whether or not Ubuntu would work on my laptop and it appeared to be a bit tricky because I needed to roll back the BIOS and change some info in xorg.conf. Turns out it's actually quite easy and I managed to get 8.04 Hardy Heron running with the default compiz type effets. It runs great . Did some more research on DV Cameras and Ubuntu and found with the help of the XP drivers for my cam and ndiswrapper everything worked and there is no noticeable lag in performance while capturing. It just works.

The default video editing software leaves a bit to be deisred but I'm greatful it works. **(Any suggestions to alternatives?**)

Now I will be able to quickly share and introduce our daughter the same day she arrives thanks to Ubuntu and the many others that put it all together and make it work or post tips and tutorial on how to make it work.! :)
