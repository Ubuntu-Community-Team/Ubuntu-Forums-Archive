### Post by jharadie on 2008-08-25
I started with 7.04 and, having been around Linux for 10+ years, knew there'd be some faults, but nothing that wasn't workaround-able.  Upgraded to 7.10 and my mood upgraded to the stratosphere.  I run 2 machines and after a while with 7.10 chose to go 99% with it (I have a VM XP for those two programs that have no equivalent at all in Linux).  Bragged to my friends about how good Linux is, how great Ubuntu is.

My only complaint with 7.10 was that gparted seemed to have disappeared from the partitioner.  One of my machines has 2 HDs, but for reasons known only to the electronic powers that be, while 7.04 saw both drives (during install), 7.10 didn't (neither did 8.04).

Then along came Hardy.  Because of the rules of politeness, all I will say is that I've encountered situations that were equal to (if not worse than) what my poor M$ friends went through when switching from XP to Vista.  I realize that not all of it is Ubuntu's fault (I understand part of the problem is with Gnome, and part with the kernel), but that doesn't change the fact that I've spent days getting nowhere.  I was really looking forward to Hardy since it's an LTS (the 18-month cycle never really made sense to me), but not anymore.

I'm going back to 7.10 and staying there for the forseeable future.  When 8.10 is released, I'll watch the forums for a couple of months before deciding whether to go with it, or go over to Fedora (or something else).  Maybe Ubuntu will have mercy, get enough fixed by 8.10, and make it an LTS, too.

Meanwhile, I no longer tell people I'm an Ubuntu user, just that I'm a Linux user.  I've quit recommending Ubuntu.  When people ask, I tell them it's a wide-open field, search around and find what works best for them.
