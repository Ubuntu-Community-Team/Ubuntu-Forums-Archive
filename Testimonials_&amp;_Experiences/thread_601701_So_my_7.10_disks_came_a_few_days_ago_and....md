### Post by klange on 2007-11-03
Finally decided to use them.
My desktop has been giving me (or more specifically, my mother, and I can't even really call it *my* desktop anyway...) some problems. It has a virus, of course, having run Windows XP as everything from a rough gaming machine to an Apache server. The virus is one that even the newest definitions from AdAware on their most expensive installation (multiple-use business license with no limits) couldn't even find. And it's a real pain too: I can't even find a single thing in the process list that looks suspicious, and even ending everything including "vital system processes" doesn't help. The main symptom is annoying pop ups all the time in Internet Explorer, which itself is a pain to deal with.
Anyway, I though, "hey, didn't they fix things up in the 7.10 live cd so that it supported more hardware?", and decided to plop it in.
Things didn't go well at first, as the disk refused to boot multiple times in my CD drive. Ran disk check which complained of 126 erroneous files. Decided I'd give it one more go and slapped it into my 'supposedly superior' DVD drive after giving it a quick clean. Sure enough, it booted!
My first problem after getting it running was that my Linksys wireless card doesn't work normally (it's Broadcam-based). I noticed that the restricted drivers manager noticed it but - oh snap! - the machine has no other source of internet. I ran over to my laptop, slapped in my 2gb USB stick, and searched the forums for the proper drivers. Slapped on the stick, ran back over to my desktop, and installed them. After fiddling with NetworkManager (which froze when I had first booted, as it notoriously does), the two green dots were flashing green and I was welcomed by a set of blue bars. Only two, but good enough for me (the network card *is* underneath a desk in a basement, so what do you expect?)
The next thing I noticed was that RDM also reported the ATI drivers for my x800, but I ignored them because the default Compiz effects seemed to be running fine (and off of a *CD!*), also they require a restart, and if that happened, I'd be back to square one.
So I started up Firefox, which loaded quite quickly, and jumped onto my family's webmail. Then I felt something I hadn't felt since I first installed Ubuntu on my laptop --- speed.
I added the System Monitor panel applet to my top panel and perused the graphs. Using all of my 512mb of ram (of course), but what's this? Only 20-30% of my 1.66ghz AMD junker? And when starting up Firefox? Blasphemy! Ubuntu was running faster off of a CD than Windows XP when I had first installed it! The next thought that entered my mind was "why are you amazed by this?"
Anyway, case in point, everything worked fine and I have a feeling my DVD drives going to get a massive workout over the next few weeks. It's a good thing I ordered more disks, because by the time I'm done with this one there won't be a disk left.
My desktop won't be suffering from any virii any time soon, and my mother should be happy.

EDIT: I just enabled "Extra" effects (would have set up a cube and everything if I could off of the live CD), everything is blazingly fast with the standard drivers for my card. (Lot better than on my low-end i945 on my laptop)
