### Post by Catraphact on 2011-05-19
Hello,

I made my first thread here to post my experiences with 9.04- Jaunty. That was the first time I tried Ubuntu (and Linux). Prior to that, I had been a (relatively happy) Windows user.

I have stuck with Linux & Ubuntu since then. Now, the OS has not really changed THAT much since 9.04, until now. I have upgraded to every new release since 9.04 and 11.04 is by far the most different from its predecessor. 

Let me start by saying 11.04 has been very stable for me. It recognised all my hardware, and after I installed the NVIDIA drivers, I was all set. In other words, it installed just the way I expected it to.

This is about Unity. I do not understand the angst that this has brought on. Perhaps I've been lucky not to experience any bugs. It's...alright. It generally stays out of my way and I can do everything I want to quite easily.

What I do not understand is why Ubuntu moved away from GNOME to its own thing...I tried GNOME 3 (on 11.04), and preferred that to Unity..though I can't get it to quite work right (perhaps someone can help me on this).

Unity is fine, however, GNOME 3 is better. I guess as OK as I am with Unity, this is going to make me try something different for my DE (perhaps KDE...though, I am not too hopeful). I really like GNOME, and I am very disappointed that Ubuntu chose this route.

As I said, its not bad, but its not great either. Unity will probably get better and more customizable, however, GNOME 3 is the way of the future.

My only hope is that Ubuntu includes vanilla GNOME 3 as an option in its next release. Certainly using GNOME 3 would be easier than putting all this work into creating something that most users seem hostile to.
