### Post by cmeyer13 on 2010-12-20
I'm a 30 year car detailer who is getting off his dead *** and going back to school shooting for a BA in Software Engineering. 
So the first class I take is "Intro. to Micro Computers." About the third week in, the chapter we're studying is on Operating Systems. And low and behold There is this 3 paragraph blurb about Linux and Operating Systems such as Red Hat, Ubuntu, etc. etc. and about how they're free. Me, being curious went straight to the Ubuntu Homepage and downloaded the 10.04 ISO. I did a test run off the CD and was really intrigued by the Software Center, so I installed with the Dual Boot option. Slowly but surely I was using Ubuntu more and more every day for the sake of speed. I was learning more and more about how the community works and the theory behind open source software, which I believe is what makes Computer Science a Science. 

In the process of going to school and really learning and realizing the dominance Microsoft holds on the computer world, I became disappointed in this whole Cyber world I've been apart of since I was 13 (I was really into BBSing when I was younger, before the internet boom.). I decided to delete every bit of pirated material on my computer and install 10.10 on a clean Microsoft free slate. 

Having a Ubuntu only machine is easily the funnest and educational way to go with a home PC. The web based community support is far more faster and more convenient and reliable than any tech support line or "that buddy who knows what to do." Ubuntu has allowed me to learn my system and take car of my own system better than any version of Windows ever had. While I was a kid raised on DOS and Windows 3.1 was for the folks, I am really liking the necessary integration of "Terminal." 

Anyways, I just wanted to express my gratitude to anyone and everyone who participated in this community to make it what it is today. I advise everyone I know to at the very least try this OS if anything just to try something new, and to have a way to save files for when Windows crashes ("Try it without installing" is a great feature on the install CD) 

At this point in my schooling and interest in Linux. I am trying to introduce myself to programming via Python, and looking forward to being a contributer someday. Now if I can only get this damn thing to stop asking for a WPA key every five seconds... :)  Congratulations on making to the end of my gushing session...

-Chris
