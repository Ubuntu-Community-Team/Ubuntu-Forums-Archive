### Post by Mugzilla on 2009-02-16
I haven't been a Microsoft user my entire life.  I started out with a Texas Instruments TI99 4a.  I love that machine (Yes, it still works.  I break it out once per year just for nostalgia purposes.)

My first PC was an IBM PS2 286sx Model 30-286.  What a deal my Mom got on that baby.  I think with her teacher discount it was $4000! 2 mb of RAM, 40 mb internal HD, and IBM dos 3.3! It was so cutting edge, it didn't have a 5.25 floppy drive, just the newest 3.5"!!!

Eventually, I bought my first computer.  It came with windows 3.1.  The next computer purchase came with Windows 95, then 98, then XP, then Vista Home premium.  

After I realized my new computer was not really "vista ready" (Vista ran buggy and SLOW), I gave Ubuntu 8.04 a shot.  I was impressed, but had issues with the SATA HD.

I went back to my copy of XP for awhile, until 8.10 64 bit.  WOW.  It just works.  If it doesn't work, or I have a question, REAL ANSWERS are available online!  I have not stumped anyone yet with a question on 8.10 yet.  Usually, I do not have to ask the question; A well-written google search usually brings up the answer I am looking for.

My point in all of this is, how did us 33 year old gen X-ers get assimilated into a Windows world? All of us learned TI-basic or some form of programming on a commodore 64/128.  All of us trudged through learning DOS commands on old PS2 dos systems.  All of us saw windows 3.1 for what it really was (An ugly GUI that was really just typing in a command to a command prompt in the background.  After 2 months of trying to trick my vista machine into running faster, I fell back, regrouped, and learned linux.

Will I stick with Ubuntu? Absolutely.  Will I totally get rid of my XP partition?  The day STEAM comes with an ubuntu client so I can play a few games, yes.

Am I a linux terminal power user? Nope.  Do I understand the shell and kernel and that there is a GUI plastered over top of all of it? Yes.  

I am happy.  The computer is mine again; Working for me, instead of me working for the next flavor of windows.
