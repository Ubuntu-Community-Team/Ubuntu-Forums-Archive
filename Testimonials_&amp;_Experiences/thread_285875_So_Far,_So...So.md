### Post by DeadEyes on 2006-10-27
Having used Ubuntu for over a year now, I was really looking forward to this release, I had a new PC and a new version of Ubuntu would go great with it.
After creating a CD I clicked on it to bring up the browser, oh NO the title bar said "Ubuntu 6.06 Disc tree". Don't tell me I downloaded the wrong one? Checked the ISO and it *was* 6.10 phew!
Rebooted my PC and sat staring at the progress bar... finally the desktop appeared but there was an Error some Gnome daemon failed to  start. That's not good.
Placed the CD in a different drive and tried again this time I just got Buffer I/0 errors over and over again, oh well lets try the first drive again.
Boot took ages got the same Gnome error, this time I ignored it. Went through the setup only for GParted to crash in the middle of organising my partitions. I feared the worst but there was no damage done and rerunning GParted worked the second time.
The first proper boot wow that was quick. Everything else seems pretty zippy too.
Now I just have to sort out my age old wireless problems, but I'm feeling confident I can.
I have to say that if I wasn't such a Ubuntu fan I'd have given up trying to install it. But the few weeks on spent on my new PC having only XP installed made me realise just how much I like Ubuntu.
