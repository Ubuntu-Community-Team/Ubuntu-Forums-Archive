### Post by devking1 on 2011-02-26
First off my name is Kyle! I've been a member of the forum for quite some time, however this my first post. (got to love a good cherry popping eh?)

Anywho, I thought i would introduce myself and tell you all a quick story for a laugh. So for the past couple weeks I've had a medical problem that has put me out of commission for work. With my laptop in hand and wicked narcotic painkillers I went to work on building my own hobby studio for music recording/creation. I installed various windows programs (cubase, reason, fl studio, etc.) but i wanted to dual boot a linux system (ubuntu studio). So I partitioned my drive and booted up ubuntu, installed it, now time to admire my work? 

oh no, no no no. In my infinite wisdom all i did was erase windows entirely....oh joy. so whatever. id used ubuntu before i knew my around (just the basics) so i began looking for drivers for my wireless...only to find guides that no matter what i did, no matter how much i screamed, cryed, or barfed i could not get my wireless to love me.

so i finally got my hands on ubuntu 10.10 which for some reason recognised everything in my laptop. (before ubuntu 10.10 i tryed old copies i had of Mandriva, Fedora, Musix and PClinux so when ubuntu started up perfectly i wept with joy)

so for the past week my tasks have been:

-learning to use the terminal
-learning difference between install from software center, source
-learning linux etiquette 
-learning (quick glances to understand) ruby, python and c++


and basically unlearning everything from windows. everything i do in linux i'm searching the web to do research. my first experience with linux was not a very pleasing one. alot of head banging. but now im having more fun stumbling around the net. 

i gotta say ubuntu rocks. only thing im not happy about is the abundance of info on the net. Im slowly figuring out were to start and what resources are beneficial to me. linux is just like jazz music theory. you could spend your life learning it and still not know squat.

cheers,
kyle
