### Post by magmon on 2008-12-02
Short version:

Pros: I can still boot into vista if needed. Great color scheme. Good features right out of the box. Add/remove programs is AMAZING. Its stable. Installations are easy.

Cons: Can't hard shutdown or it wont work. Installation through wubi is quirky. Parents wouldn't permit installing over vista xD.


Long version:

I am personally a windows user since 2001. Please keep in mind that I am a teen, so I didn't see windows evolve. When I got my first computer, my dad just let me role with it, and I learned and used windows until about two months ago. I finally got bored of windows, and stumbled upon this. To be honest, I couldn't be happier with ubuntu! At first, some thing are quite hard to learn and configure, such as wireless internet, but once I got those, I absolutely fell in love with ubuntu. The best feature is the add/remove programs menu, I get all of my games/work programs/plugins etc from there! Its really just endless, and the installs are absolutely effortless! I also love the look of ubuntu. The mac-like top bar and windows-like bottom bar makes this OS like a dream. The orange color scheme is also a nice change from the bland blue Im used to. Ubuntu is fast, averaging about twice as fast as vista. It is easy to locate the files I need, and there are multiple easy to find search tools to make it even easier. The built in virtual desktop feature is also nice if I am doing multiple things. For example, playing mahjong and typing a document, or playing frets on fire while browsing the forums while doing an update! The bring desktop to front button is also useful for digging you out of multiple programs quickly.
