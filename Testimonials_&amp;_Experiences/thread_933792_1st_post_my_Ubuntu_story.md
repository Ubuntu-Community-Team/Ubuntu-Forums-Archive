### Post by surelock22 on 2008-09-29
LOL, 1st post.

Wanted to just say hi and to share my Ubuntu and Kubuntu story.

Basically, my brother has been touting the joys of Ubuntu (or anything Linux) for a few years now, but I've always been too, i dunno, scared (?) to run a version, let alone install a version as my main OS. Why was I scared? Because I've been a Windows user since Windows users used to be called IBM users. I'm a child of the 80's, and if Leisure Suit Larry says that an OS and it's parent company are OK, then by golly, you've sold me.  That's right, SOLD ME.

Well, a year ago, I upgraded my Windows machine with some needed upgrades: beefed my hard-drive from an 80 gigger to a 300 gigger, sent back my busted NVidea GeForce 6800 GT video card and got a free upgrade to a 7200 GS (sweet), upped my DDR2 from 512 to 1024, brand new power supply, and of course, to top it off, a fresh install of Windows XP (a Vista user I am not, nor would I be for at least another year). A buddy of mine helped me with the install and a boatload of programs, got my computer back, and MAN! My computer F-ing sung!!!

Things are a little different now than they were 2 years ago though: I have a fiance', and we have a 2 year old together, for one, and she has a 13 year old teenage daughter that runs my computer like a silly, typical teenage girl. Even though I have told her (and her mother for that matter) that my computer is not a "download-this-cool-free-game" machine, countless times I have come home from a hard day of work and I have found a silly new icon on my desktop with a puppy or a troll or some spooky murder mystery game demo. Even after I have told them a big fat N-O to direct P2P software like LimeWire and Kazaa, I found Kazaa and a couple versions of the same Hanna Montana songs in their respectable download file.

I've even gone so far as to write on a piece of paper specific instructions as to what downloads are ok and what arn't. I did at least get her to only use FireFox, and did my damned-est to eliminate Internet Explorer to no avail. She eventually stopped some of the bad habits, but looking at the 100's of emails she gets daily, I know she's bound to open up something stupid (same goes for my fiance'. It don't matter how many times I tell her that she'll never get that XBox or Ipod for free, I know she's going to open up at least one of those damned emails once or twice a year out of shear stupidity. If they said that they were giving away free rainbows and unicorns to the first 50 responses, she'd probably go for it.

Well, about 8 months ago, my nice Windows machine turned to a big ol' turd of a paperweight/ paper shelf / dust collector. My buddy that normally helps me with my 'puter kinda gave me the cold shoulder about trying to get it up and running again, so my brother took it off my floor's hands a month and a half ago. I asked him to just work on it in his spare time, no rush, it would just sit there otherwise, and he was fine with working on it. He's the IT guy for one of his town's big employers, and he knows his stuff.

He said that among some of the crazy virus's I've collected, my new Hitachi hard-drive was going up. So we got an RGA on it, and it's getting replaced as we speak. Thank god for warranties.

I went to a friend's house over the weekend. He was showing off some of his newest gear (its been quite a while since I've seen him), and he showed me his shiny new lap-top running, of all things: Kubuntu. I asked him why he chose Linux, and he bluntly said "why should I pay for something I know is shitty". "Why should I download a pirated version of something and expose myself to virus's and getting arrested for pirated software when there's freeware that's open sourced and just as good, if not better than, anything anybody would/should have to pay for on Windows" His desktop was freakin' awesome looking. My brother's Ubuntu set-up was considerably more spartan, my friend's set up looked like some C.S.I. Miami set-up (if it their I.T. guy was a graphic designer raver maybe).

He showed off some great apps. He ran Fruity Loops through what I'm guessing is VitualBox, after that demo, what the else do I need, right?

So, after doing some investigating of my own, I told my brother to go ahead and do a full install of Kubuntu. No dual-boot crap, just give me Kubuntu, and give it to me hard. I'm sick of the failures of Microsoft, I'm not going to buy an all new machine, so I can have an overpriced Mac, so what else should I do? Kubuntu seems like it has the best custom features, and I didn't get anything negative from my bro about Kubuntu (he prefers GNOME). Kubuntu is Ubuntu with a K, and my name is Kenny. It just fits.

So there you have it, my Kubuntu story. I'm still waiting for my machine, but me being the poor ******* that I am, I'm not going to complain about things taking a while with getting my desktop PC up and running.

The End. *bang*

(the above was written on a Windows XP laptop that I haven't converted yet! Waiting on that CD Burner and Hard Drive to store my files from this PC).
