### Post by rmfought on 2007-10-25
Wow.  Just wow.  I've been so happy with Ubuntu, and then something like this happens and just makes me sigh.

I decide to try out the nifty new monitor manager tool, so I can hook my laptop up to my widescreen desktop monitor and easily move between the two screens.  So I enable the monitor as default, select the right model, disable my laptop screen and - have to reboot.  OK, fine.

Reboot.

Now my once perfectly functioning Gutsy laptop comes up in "reduced graphics mode" or some such nonsense.  OK fine, I'll just fix it when I get logged in.  Log on, pull up the screen manager.  Hmmm, I was using the flgrx driver, but somehow I get kicked back down to VESA driver.  OK, let's just go back to where we started, flgrx driver, laptop display as primary, monitor disabled, generic LCD 1680x1050 as laptop display with widescreen.  OK, gotta reboot.

Reboot (visions of MS Windows popping in my head now)

Crud, now I am *really* in reduced graphics mode.  I get no warning, but my resolution is somehere around 640x480.  I'd love to be able to pull up the system menu, but even though it *appears* to be in the upper left corner of my screen, clicking on it actually opens up evolution.  Somehow the image shows one thing, but the mouse clicks behave like I really was at higher resolution.  Or something like that.

Long story short, me trying out new Ubuntu feature on a lark = half an hour of my life wasted with at least 1 more hour of serious *** pain left I'm sure.

I'm so disappointed.
