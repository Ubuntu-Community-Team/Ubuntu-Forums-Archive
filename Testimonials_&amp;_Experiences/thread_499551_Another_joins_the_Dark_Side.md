### Post by weblordpepe on 2007-07-12
After a bit of commotion at work regarding Linux and Windows and a huge heated argument between me and a Windows troll resulted in some good news:
One of my work colleagues was nosy. So I gave him an Ubuntu 7.04 disc after work. 

This guy's never really used Linux before so I said that he could just pop in the disc and he'd be at the desktop and there'd be nothing to install. We were in a bit of a hurry so he asked if I would be on Msn that night. I said yeah, the program to use to get on Msn is 'Gaim'.

So what do ya know - later that night he came online on my Msn and said 'hey guess what, im in linux!'. This is the guys first time with linux and he's already online & using Msn etc. Without me being there at all.

After a bit of a walk in the park, explaining what repositories were, & how linux avoids the spyware problem & a few other things about how drivers work being in the kernel, he was like 'Im sold!'

I said the price is the icing on the cake: $0

Now he wants to install it on his next machine & try out some games.
