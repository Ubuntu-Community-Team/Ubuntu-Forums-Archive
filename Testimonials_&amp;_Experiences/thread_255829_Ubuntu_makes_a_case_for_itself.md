### Post by majesticturkey on 2006-09-12
I've been using Ubuntu for a while now. After using several other distros, I found Ubuntu to be the easiest to get up and running, user-friendly, and still maintains customizability.

I've been living at home these summer months until autumn quarter starts back up at school. I'm not taking summer classes, and my dorm closes over the summer. Linux is nice at school, it helps me get my work done, and The Ohio State University caters to all operating systems. While everything is Windowscentric, they do feature Firefox browsers in all the labs, have Mac labs around, and the IT department helps with Linux problems. Also, professors accept open-source document formats.

So I bring my computer home, with Ubuntu installed. My sister sees me playing around with Amarok while I'm working, and exclaims, "What a weird music player. You don't use iTunes?" So I explained to her (she's 17) about Linux and how Amarok is my favorite, even over iTunes. I showed her the Wikipedia and lyrics functions, the last.fm integration, and such, and she fell in love with it. Then I showed her my compiz toys (I don't use them normally, but hey, I wanted to show off.) That spinning cube made her so jealous. She wouldn't do well with Linux though, so I didn't try to sell it to her :P

But what takes the cake is the past month. I had a huge client. I do consultation - making and designing powerpoint presentations, graphics, websites, and on a side I also help with server administration. I go to school for biomedical research, but I do that as a hobby. Yet I don't have any other nerd stereotypes. Go figure. This client was a managing partner at my father's law firm, and this case was big. The firm he went to for the presentation first wanted $20,000. My dad said "My son is good with that stuff, he'd do it cheap." And so, they turned to me, mostly because they didn't want something as "professional and polished"...boy were they shocked!

They offered to pay for any proprietary licensing fees. I said I wouldn't have any because I use OpenOffice. Over several weeks I created this thing. I present it in front of the litigators and boy did I wow them. They were so amazed at some of the things I did that the professional firm couldn't do. I even exported the openoffice presentation to powerpoint and, with a little tweaking, got it working perfectly on a Windows laptop they were going to use at the trial.

They finally asked why I was able to do this so fast and so well. I said "Because, I'm not tied up in Microsoft red-tape. I use software that's powerful, efficient, and extensible. I can edit photos, videos, animation, sound clips, and everything." They then worried that I had used things like pirated Photoshop because - let's face it - a 20 year old kid can't afford an Adobe suite. I said "No. I use free, open-source software. And it doesn't cost me a dime. It's called Ubuntu Linux, and OpenOffice.org."

And, to top it all off, after several computers in my mom's classroom got fried, I did a little rescue work with edubuntu - you know, just until they could get a copy of Windows XP - and my mom, a teacher and librarian, fell in love with it. Loved being able to design her own quizzes and tests. And the kids loved being able to mess around on a computer for an hour.

Ubuntu isn't for everyone, but for those who fit in its niches, it's amazing.
