### Post by iceportal on 2008-01-27
I completely understand the desire to post a note as to why a new user has been deterred from using Ubuntu, or any other OS. It can help us understand what problems were found, and how we can possibly improve.

However... I agree with aisyu. Linux is about choice. You have the choice whether to use Windows, Mac, Linux, BSD... And within the choice of Linux you have the choice of using Debian, Slackware, Ubuntu, OpenSUSE, etc.

Every user has their needs, their expectations of their OS. While Duane might desire a completely bloated system with every possible package preinstalled, and with bloated packages which include all possible options. Meanwhile, newer users, or older users with specific tastes, might feel more at home with a computer that ONLY installs the base system and a few extras, then allows you to add additional packages if you wish. This is easier for new users as they don't have to worry about wading through gigabytes of documentation, thousands of options and various programs... They are given a good and stable starting point with which they can learn about the system, from which they can customize, to their own tastes, their distribution.

So, Duane, I'm happy that you've found distributions in the past that fulfill your desires. And despite the fact that Ubuntu didn't meet your requirements, I know that you'll return to your comfortable distributions of choice, so I'm not too worried.

My advice, however, is to keep in mind that you're not the only user in the world, and that we don't all see things the same way you do. Therefore, in the future, try not to rant, but rather to suggest. This is, after all, Ubuntu -- humanity towards others.
