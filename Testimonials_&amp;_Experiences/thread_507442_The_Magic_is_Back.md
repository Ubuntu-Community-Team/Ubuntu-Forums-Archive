### Post by yeastpuppet on 2007-07-23
I hate to sound like an old timer, but I distinctly remember my first interaction with a pc was with an Apple II running Colossal Cave (interactive fiction).  I was amazed and started a long term affair with computers.  

The Sinclair Timex, Texas Instruments TI-99/4A, Commodore 64, and my first "real" computer a IBM Clone 286 then a 486, my first Pentium class, and now exclusively AMD.  

DOS 2.0, 3.0, 3.1, 4.0, 5.0, Windows 3.0, 3.1 95, 98, 98SE, 2000, and now XP.  

After decades with Windows, I installed Ubuntu last month and for the first time in years I was lost.  Really lost.  Package managers, tar and deb files, whacky file systems, no drivers, no "my documents", no "so many things".  

So for the first time in years, I bought a couple of books and thankfully found the Ubuntu Forums.  And for the first time in years, the magic was back.  Ubuntu is fresh, vibrant, so capable of great things.  

Ubuntu got me thinking again, got me poking around in the system, got me making mistakes and re-installing Ubuntu night after night for a fresh start.  Working through problems with the great people here on the forums, and finally I solved a problem on my own, and I was amazed!

Now I'm surfing the web, streaming music from Pandora in the background, reading my Excel files with Open Office, running Photoshop CS under WINE and I'm even more amazed.  This is all being done without Windows software, this is being done with a free operating system that works.  

I saw a quote here in the forums that I wish I would have thought of... With Microsoft you get windows and gates, with Linux you get the whole house.

Join the community and let's help one another. 

YP
