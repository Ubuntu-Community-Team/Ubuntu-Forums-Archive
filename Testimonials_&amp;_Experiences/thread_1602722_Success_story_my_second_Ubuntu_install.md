### Post by Rocket J Squirrel on 2010-10-21
One of the XP machines in my wife's office was collapsing under the burden of too many Windows patches, too many years of OS crap accumulation, too much, too much. 

All the user needs is email, web browsing, and word processing. My first Ubuntu machine is a little netbook I installed Lucid Lynx onto in spring. There were issues -- wireless issues, mainly. And I had to learn how to get it to talk to the network's windows shares and networked printers. So some learning curve there. But just email, web browsing, and word processing, on an older Dell desktop machine? Piece of pie, I thought, and it worked out exactly that way. 10.10 installed cheerfully from the CD, an hour or two of tinkering with settings and getting it to talk to the other bits on the network, and hey presto! they have a new machine. 

Cost of OS: $0
Cost of MS Office workalike (OpenOffice): $0
Appreciation from wife: Priceless. 

Thanks, Canonical and everyone else who tinkers together great free software.
