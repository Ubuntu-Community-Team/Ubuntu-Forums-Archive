### Post by jfl on 2009-07-04
[FONT="Comic Sans MS"]As I had nothing in the works for this week-end, I decided to upgrade one of the office machine from Hardy to Ibex; it is a dual-boot system with W2K.
I believe in partitions, so I had a separate /home partition on the second HD. I still backed up all the data and more, just in case.
The machine is a "home-brew" Intel Celoron 1.8 GHz.
It had to be 100% operational Monday morning !!!
So Friday evening, after I made sure all the updates were "up to date", I fired Synaptic and  clicked "upgrade"  :???: :???: :???:
It started well, announced 2 hours and some to go, so I went for dinner.
A couple hours later, it had stopped, waiting for my input; did that 4-5 more times and by midnight Intrepid was running as well as all my apps, including "Autokey" !!!
So this week-end I can celebrate Independence Day and ride my Harley ..      ):P .. :biggrin:

**Kudos to the Ubuntu team !!!!!!**  

My only complaint (got to have one), is that you have to "baby sit" the box as it asks for input several times at long intervals.
[/FONT]
