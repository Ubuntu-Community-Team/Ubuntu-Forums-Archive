### Post by Ultra Magnus on 2007-06-17
As every one who's used Linux knows, Linux is the webs best kept secret - Allot of people are using it but allot of people have still never heard of it. One such person is my technophobic girl friend who only recently got a cd player because when her old one broke she couldn't find anywhere that sold walkmans anymore.

Well I installed Ubuntu on my computer recently and I've been really pleased, its clean and simple to use and lacks allot of the major head aches you get with windows such as virus' and spyware etc. I told my girlfriend and she wasn't really interested so I havn't mentioned it since. She came round the other day and wanted to check her emails but she was scared because she thought she'd have to "programme it" before she could do anything so I showed her the ropes and let her have a look, and she liked it - her first question was "are you sure it is legal to download it for free?" - followed by "Can you get me one?". Of course I obliged - I'll give her a hand to install etc but I'm actually really pleased that someone with no computer knowledge or views either way on the "Open source is good, microsoft is bad" thing actually perfers the look and feel of linux over windows. 

Stuff she likes about it are:
The two panels (It makes the screen look nicer (??))
The menus (Its not as confusing as windows)
The add/remove programs package manager (Its well easy)
Firefox (I had colourful tabs on - but she likes it except it won't load up her work website)

Incidently - I decided to turn beryl on and show her all the nifty effects and she laughed at me and told me I was a geek but atleast you get to choose and I suspect the effects in Vista would probably prompt the same kind of response.

Anyway - I'll have to see if she likes using it in the long run - she only ever uses her computer to access emails and write documents so hopefully she won't have problems finding programmes she likes etc, so there you have it, an unlikely win for Linux!

So how come Linux remains so much in the shadows when it comes to everyday desktop computing?

### Post by OzzyFrank on 2007-06-18
I've been using Ubuntu and Linux since 4 days after Edgy came out. I thought I'd finally allow myself some fun geeking time and try a version of Linux as a pet OS to play with. Never dreamed I'd quickly end up with a system I could do pretty much everything on, and while I still have a couple of Windows programs I use, I have great, fast-loading Ubuntu apps for all my tasks. Some things even ended up being much easier to do in Ubuntu, so next thing I was only going into Windows every 3 or 4 days. I've showed my rather customised Ubuntu desktop to a few, and I often get "Is that really Linux??". When I loaded Beryl and showed them my windows burning up when i closed, that totally floored them, hehe! But as for apps, I'll load them up casually and they'll be like "So you can get apps for that?" and I'll be like "You can get apps for anything, for free, and as simply as doing a search in a package manager and choosing your apps from the resulting list!". I may yet get some converts!
