### Post by SCHolland on 2009-04-24
Haha,  I just had an interesting experience with my girlfriend last night.

I was helping her with a lab report for one of her classes.  I was using Gnumeric spreadsheet with R stats package installed as well. She was using Excel.  

It would seem that Excel does not do a box and whiskers chart (can't remember the 'real' name for it now), however Gnu can. Excel doesn't have all the stats formulas that R + Gnu does. So I build her spreadsheet, and save it to a flash drive.  She loads it into Excel, and of course all graphs and formulas don't work.  I try to save the graphs as JPEGs, but it won't save to the flash drive (it turns out if I don't 'eject drive'/unmount it loses the files). I've only used Ubuntu for a couple weeks, and this is the first 'project' I've tried to do with it. So while I'm trying to figure this out she says to me, "Why would anyone use that (Ubuntu)?  It doesn't work?!?"  I calmly reply back, "Ummmm, let's see... I'm the one with the graphs and the formulas on my computer... And what do you have?  I think your the one with the program that doesn't work..."

Thanks Ubuntu, Gnumeric Spreadsheet, R, and all OSS!
