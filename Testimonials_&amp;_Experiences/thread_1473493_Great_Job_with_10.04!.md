### Post by Lee05162004 on 2010-05-05
I just wanted to say that I think the developers did a great job with 10.04 and Ubuntu in general. I've been using Ubuntu for a few years now and with each new release it gets better and better, always moving in a positive direction. 

   I consider myself an average user. I don't really adjust my system much and I basically use my computer for web browsing, email, chat, music and some graphics editing.

   I have not had any problems with 10.04 and I like it a lot. I really like the new darker theme and the integration with social networks and IM clients is great too. After the initial install I setup the chat programs, my social networking sites and my email and it's already saving me a lot of time.

   Even thought I've gotten used to installing all my commonly used applications via the command line I've browsed through the Ubuntu Software Center and I've come across very useful applications that I didn't know existed. 

   I've got an older laptop with the Broadcom wireless chipset and an integrated ATI graphics card and I was anticipating problems with them with the new release. The wireless adapter didn't work out of the box but after I activated the driver it's worked flawlessly. I didn't have any problems with my graphics card out of the box. 

   I can't wait to see what new features and improvements they implement in the next release, looking forward to it.
