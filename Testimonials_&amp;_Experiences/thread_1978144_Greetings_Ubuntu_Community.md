### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-05-15
> c0nstant1n

If the staff would permit, I'd give you some of my beans. I'm more than glad to share all my beans, if it helps  those who can't live without them to get involved in the community. This  community recognizes the need for Ubuntu/Linux support and it's  mission is to provide that support. I believe the goal of the forums is  to be a  one stop shop for all Ubuntu/Linux users who need assistance or wish to  share information to improve the Ubuntu/Linux experience.

IMHO the value of this community shouldn't be measured by one's CP, but  rather by being a member of a support community that "delivers". A site whose  members find satisfaction in helping other users to enjoy the  experience and volunteer their time in doing so. For nothing more than a  simple "thank you". Hence, there's no need to place emphasis on one's  virtual personality or bean count. It's not a competition, it's a concerted effort to provide support for a project that many believe in.[SIZE=1][FONT=Papyrus][COLOR=#000000][FONT=Arial]
[/FONT][/COLOR][/FONT][/SIZE]
One misconception is the fact that members with large bean counts are  somehow more enlightened than new members or those with lower bean  counts. This false assumption can lead to a misinterpretation of  knowledge base. I've seen many a new member here that have far more  knowledge regarding certain aspects of Linux than myself. Just because  one might have limited experience, doesn't mean they can't provide  assistance to another user by helping answer basic questions. It's  amazing how many users just need a little fundamental support with  installation. Obviously, you've done the installation and might be  surprised at how many support requests you can contribute to.

It might help to stop thinking about social networking or one's virtual  personality and think along the lines of contributing. Try to keep in  mind what the goals here are and "how" you can contribute. Hopefully,  inspiring others to see the value of this community. I seriously doubt a  user cares how many beans you have or what your virtual personality is -  if you help them solve their problem. :-k

Good Luck

Just my $0.02
