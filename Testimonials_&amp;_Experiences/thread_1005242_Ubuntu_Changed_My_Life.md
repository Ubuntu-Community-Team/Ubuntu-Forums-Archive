### Post by HungryMan on 2008-12-08
This week I have been reflecting on some things. Two things that I couldn't get out of my mind were UNIX's "Do one thing, but do it well," and Ubuntu's "Humanity towards others". Based on the former's philosophy, I'd keep this short and concise.

The UNIX philosophy (also YAGNI and KISS) promotes usefulness without bloating. It makes me want to say and do as little as possible, but do it well.

Ubuntu's philosophy, on the other hand, is truly admirable. I can't argue with the fact that the FSF has a noble goal, but it has sort of become an organization out to boycott those who do not want to share (i.e. FSF's campaigns to boycott proprietary products). Ubuntu means "The belief in a universal bond of sharing that connects all humanity." (wiktionary.org). It promotes sharing unconditionally, with absolutely no strings attached. Humans are what we are, and humans are what we should be toward ourselves. I read in one of the Welcome to Ubuntu pages that "A person with Ubuntu feels down when others are down," and "A person with Ubuntu is open to other's ideas." I dream of a world when we all have Ubuntu in our hearts. Yes, some people may not want to share and maybe never will, but it's okay with me. We have our reasons why we do things, and I want to make mine "for the benefit of myself and my fellow humans."

Ubuntu is the greatest OS to me, mainly because I salute to it's philosophy. It has truly changed my life. :D
