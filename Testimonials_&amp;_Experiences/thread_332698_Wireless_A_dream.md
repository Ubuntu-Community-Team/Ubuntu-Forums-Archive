### Post by qalimas on 2007-01-06
...come true =D

After fighting all day yesterday with my girlfriend's wireless in Kubuntu, I decided to try one final step.  Compiling ndiswrapper from source, then putting in the driver.

It was as easy as that, after I added in the .inf file, one "sudo depmod -a && sudo modprobe ndiswrapper" later I was connected to the wireless access point.  Since I have the wireless working, tonight she'll bring her computer over to install Kubuntu on, and I'll have her wireless working quicker than she can in Windows =D

She shall be another happy convert =)


Thank you, *Ubuntu, for being so easy to make work!
