### Post by NoSmokingBandit on 2007-11-08
Let me preface by saying that i know there is a sepcific "bye ubuntu" thread, but i want to give my whole experience at once in the proper place.
I love linux. I love the idea behind it, how it works, the general feel of things in the way i can customize it any way i want. I love ubuntu. I think its the most advanced linux distro available right now and its making a ton of progress with each release.
This is my story:
I installed Gusty Gibbon the day after it was released, i would have sooner but the servers were clogged and i was downloading really slow, but no big deal, i expected that. It took me a while to get my wireless card to work at full speed, but then someone sent me a link to a guide that fixed me up just fine. All was well and then my sound just up and quit on me. Im not sure what i did, but i couldnt figure it out and i got frustrated and re-installed gusty.
Next i got everything all seated in again and began going about my usual business. I wanted to try out kde because i heard that linus likes it more. I installed kde just fine and uninstalled gnome easily. I found i didnt like kde and wanted gnome back. I did the whole process in reverse and found myself with a dumbed-down version of gnome that didnt function very well at all. I spent a whole day trying to get it back to normal and it refused to function correctly. Once again i got angered and reinstalled Gusty.
Next time around i tried to be very ginger and not install too many different programs in case i broke it again. I installed audacity so i could record some music from myspace. This killed my sound again. I posted the problem and the best response i got was "make sure the volume is turned up." I went to synaptic and installed every alsa package i could find and eventually found a program that sets the default sound card. Apparently it was set to not use any sound card at all :confused: so i set it to my SB Live card and all was well again. I downloaded and .ace file and installed the proper package so the Archive Manager could open it, but every time i tried it gave me an error. I looked at the terminal output and it just gave me a blank line. I rebooted into xp to find that the archive was password protected. I guess the Archive Manager doesnt like that.
I went about writing some cd's then i noticed that gnomebaker would go through the transcoding process but would never actually write to the cd. I had that problem in Feisty which i remedied by running the program "sudo gnomebaker" or "sudo serpentine" since it affected every cd writer on the  computer. Even then, half the cd's i wrote came out as duds. I posted the problem when i had feisty and did again a few days ago but never really got it solved.
Throughout all of these installations, firefox would randomly turn gray and stop functioning. If i wa luck i could still 'force quit' the program, but sometimes it locked up the entire system requiring a reboot. Also on a reboot sometimes the resolution would randomly kick back to 640x480/50hz and would not change until i rebooted again at which point it would be fine.
After all this frustration i have to go back to windows. I cant spend hours troubleshooting everything i do. I like ubuntu, and i realize that most people dont have the problems i do, but i cant spend more time fixing ubuntu than i am actually getting work done.
Ill install Hardy Heron when its out. Hopefully that will offer more stability for people like me. Until then, i must go back to my xp install where everything works. I will miss ubuntu, but its just too inefficient for me right now.
