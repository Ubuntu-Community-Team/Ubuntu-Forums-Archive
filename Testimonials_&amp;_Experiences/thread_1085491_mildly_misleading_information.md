### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-03-04
It doesn't say that Ubuntu will "run as a Windows application", it says that the installer will run within Windows. Big difference.

Also, the information from the hardware manager does not show what we really want; we want the information about your wireless card. What the vendor is and what the chipset is. For instance, if I was being asked this information, I'd say that mine is a Realtek RTL8187. Yours might be Atheros, Broadcom, Prism, Intel, Realtek, or possibly something different.

Some wireless cards will work out-of-the-box with Ubuntu. Some require an additional driver to be installed. We're just trying to determine what driver your card needs. Ideally, either you should buy a card that is known to work "out-of-the-box", or the wireless card manufacturers should settle on a unified standard driver.
