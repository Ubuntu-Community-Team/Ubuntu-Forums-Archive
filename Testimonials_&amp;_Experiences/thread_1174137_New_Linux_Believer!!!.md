### Post by snakeman21 on 2009-05-30
Just thought I'd share this!

     My wife always thought my computer was sub-par because I use Ubuntu.  She swore by Windows XP, and wouldn't even try Linux.  Then, about five months ago, Windows crapped out on her and refused to boot.  We didn't have the CD to do a recovery.  So, I plugged my 2 GB flash drive with Ubuntu 8.10 persistent install and was able to recover most of her files.  Being poor and not wanting to spend money on a new Windows CD, and with her not okay with the morals of downloading a pirated XP, We had no choice but for me to install Ubuntu, so I did.  She grumbled about it for a couple weeks, then quieted down.  
     Then, a couple of months ago, we moved to Pennsylvania and stayed with her parents while we found new jobs and a place.  Her parents had her old recovery CD.  Grudgingly, I backed up her files and installed it.  After using it for about a half hour, she gave it back and said those three wonderful words:

"I hate Windows."

A little surprised, I asked why.  She said that she never noticed all the problems with Windows, because she was used to it.  But after three months of using an OS that gave her NO problems, NO viruses, and NO freezes, she just couldn't stand Windows.  So I backed up her files again, and installed Jaunty.  That was two days ago.  Since then, she's been going on and on about how easy it is, and how she doesn't know why she didn't realize sooner.  She LOVES how easy it is to find programs in the repos, and how you don't have to scour the internet for a good download;  just check the box, click "Apply" and be done with it.  I'm happy to say, there's one more Linux believer in the house!  

Now, if I could just get her parents to switch...
