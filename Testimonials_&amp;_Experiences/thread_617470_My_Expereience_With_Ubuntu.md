### Post by Lster on 2007-11-19
I have been using Ubuntu for quite a while now. I used it on my laptop (the Acer Aspire something...) in which mostly it worked well. Before that I used it on an old computer which also worked (although not as well as Windows Xp in my opinion).

However I have recently bought a new PC which I have installed Ubuntu dual booting with Windows Xp. Just quickly, here are my specs:

Ubuntu 7.1 64Bit, Windows Xp SP2 32Bit
AMD 64 X2 6000+
4GB DDR2 RAM
Nvidia 8600GT 512MB
19" 1440x900 Monitor
DVD-ROM Drive
DVD/ CD RW Drive

So I downloaded Gutsy 64Bit and burned it to a CD. Setup was smooth and very fast as it had been on my laptop, but I had been expecting this. I went to reboot after the install - and I was amazed... It booted in 10 seconds (not counting the GRUB menu). And for those of you who are interested in Windows Xp, it booted in just under 20. (These are just approximations though.)

I next found that my screen resolution was supported "out of the box" (although I installed the Nvidia drivers). Even with the special effects enabled the system is exceptionally fast. Everything is instant!

I know the feature of Gutsy as I've been using it for a while now - and it had been performing very well even on my laptop, but this is what I feel Vista should have like which really is funny as my friend reinstalled Vista a while ago (about 3 months after getting a new computer), as it was becoming slow.

So, I am definitely very happy with Ubuntu's performance and responsiveness. And was very happy with how easy everything is just working - this is the third computer Ubuntu has worked well on... out of 3 computers.
