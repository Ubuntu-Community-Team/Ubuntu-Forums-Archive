### Post by x__dark on 2005-12-06
I've been a Windows user since I first laid eyes on a computer when I was 7. Windows is a great operating system, although it does have it's downsides and shady areas, it's good for home use and that's what I used it for. Up until a year ago, I hadn't even thought of the word "Linux". Things changed in early 2005, when a friend from a forum was boasting his Kubuntu Desktop in the monthly "desktop thread". 

I was curious, because the picture I had of Linux and it's users wasn't anything he was portraiting to me. I always thought of Linux as a command line operating system that some dark and shady hackers used. After talking to my friend a little more, he suggested that I put linux on the new PC i was nearly finished building. I thought he was crazy, How could I possibly live without Windows?

Well, Time went by, LiveCDs where used, I played with dual booting Kubuntu on my new PC with Windows. After about 4 months of just using Linux on the side, I realized I was spending more and more time learning about Linux and using it than I was Windows. I found myself drawn to it in such a way that I couldn't deny it, Windows had to go. 

One night I decided it, right before laying down to sleep, and I downloaded the latest Ubuntu 5.10 ISO and went to work. I've now had Ubuntu 5.10 as my main and only OS for 2 months and **I LOVE IT!**
