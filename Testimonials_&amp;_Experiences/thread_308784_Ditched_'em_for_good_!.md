### Post by LLRNR on 2006-11-28
**[COLOR="SeaGreen"][WARNING][/COLOR] This is a really really really long post... I guess I just needed to "Stand up and shout!" like RJD said... so don't mind me, I'm just "doin' my number here" :D [COLOR="SeaGreen"][/WARNING][/COLOR]**



Hello everybody!

I've been avoiding this for too long... My first real contact with Linux in general and Ubuntu in particular dates back to October this year :D 

I was absolutely stunned from the very beginning... no, I *really* mean it - let me provide a little "background": I loved computers throughout my entire life (20 years until now... and still counting). The first computer I "used" was an OmniBook Mac, a small and lovely B&W laptop. That was my only contact with a Mac, in fact. Then came old MS-DOS. I was quite acquainted with it when Windows 3.1 got out. 

My first PC was a 80386... sheesh!! such good days!! In the past few years I was thinking more and more often about those days, when I used to turn on the PC, "inspect" a little, then **cd nc** and **nc**... Play around there, too, and finally type in **win**... Alright, call me stupid if you wish, I don't mind, but back then I *really* had a very good grasp on things, I knew everything that was to know about my computer, I actually controlled it, I was actually *the owner* of my PC. (Still, I regret a single thing: I'm sorry I wasn't "out there" to use a 80286 or even an 8086...)

Then, starting with Windows95, came the era of abrupt changes a.k.a. *improvements*, when everything should have been (r)evolutionary... strange though, I only saw crashes, "Invalid access operation"s, an UGLY default theme... oh and... needless to say, lots of BSsOD. No, don't count me as a zealot and/or MS hater, but those things occurred to me just because I was *too curious*, I guess; I'm sorry, I can't help myself, this is the way I am, if I don't understand the very basis of my PC then I might as well quit.

My nostalgia towards the days when I had the sense of being - as I said - the actual *owner* of my PC increased dramatically as time went by... Windows98, Windows2000 and lately WinXP (no I don't even want to try Vista - I read a lot about it and... it's just not for me). ( As a side note: I didn't really have big issues against XP *itself*, it runs perfectly well and smooth and stable **IF** a) no Internet access; b) no apps installed except the ones that come by default + some Office stuff... otherwise things might not run as good as expected. )

Lately I was literally demoralized - I was getting to think that there's no reliable things out there, that I'll never be able to control a computer the way I like it and - yes - that my old P3 (1 GHz CPU + 384 MB RAM + 64 MB nVidia) was indeed outdated, obsolete, crappy and that it would soon be suitable for a tech-museum or something...

I feel a little sad now, since I realize I wasted such a long time before giving Linux a shot... Well there are some personal problems here too: I didn't have Internet access until this summer or so and I felt literally lost - you can't just walk right into an Internet Cafe (here) and ask the admins to allow you to download stuff... stubborn useless people!! 

Oh well - that passed and I began to open my eyes, I began to "dig", to read lots of articles... yeah I even found out there's a Google for Linux :D and I finally decided to try it out. Did some background research (if I can call it like this) and finally decided to settle with Ubuntu. 

Main reasons here (as they chronologically came up): 

1. all my "sources" stated that Ubuntu is a very good distro and I began to see lots and lots of stuff on the Net regarding Ubuntu; this made me very curious so...

2. I discovered (wow) the Ubuntu website. LOL. Really. I felt... behind, way behind what's actually happening in the IT world. I mean... think about it!! I can't tell you how strong *the Ubuntu philosophy* struck me. Of course, I knew about FOSS in general, but the Ubuntu philosophy was way, way "advanced" so to call it. Afterwards,

3. I of course began to "dig" the website... and yeah, I finally discovered these forums. I "stood in the shadows" for a few days and I got a good grasp on the atmosphere around here. Wow!! I tell you... now that REALLY struck me right in the head! I never imagined that this "humanity towards others" concept, combined with the idea that everybody should be able to have access to information and different tools not just for free, but *as a natural thing*, could ever lead to such a caring, wonderful, huge community !!

I was literally dazed. I said to myself "I surely must try Ubuntu. It seems... so different, so *human*!" (Since I got to this point, I really don't think that "we should just ditch the brown", it gave me a really earthy feel, since I first installed Ubuntu 6.06 and eventually tried KDE...)

...And here I am, after a month (yeah I guess it's been a month since I started actually digging Ubuntu) - first installed Ubuntu 6.06, then switched to KDE (Kubuntu 6.06)... and the best part is yet to come :D

Check this out - yesterday I just wiped off my entire HDD and installed Kubuntu Edgy - yes, that's right, I don't have Windows anymore on my computer !!! November 2006 were MS's last days in my life !!! I'm so happy, I finally feel fulfilled in my... computing life :mrgreen:

Sad, though, I set up an XP virtual machine through VMware Server since I need 2 things: school: BC++ 3.1 and work: Macromedia Flash 8 - Wine is "close" but... not perfect.

Well the reason I did all that is just because I felt REALLY UNCONFORTABLE with the mere idea of dual-booting (what I did for a whole month) - I didn't want any more dual-booting in my life, so here I am, my entire HDD now praises the EXT3 filesystem... (Yes, I'm a "wrecker", I'll try out ReiserFS, I'm really curious about filesystems and stuff... I'm just starting to learn :D)

[ This is just dumb, you can skip this one... Today's "triumph": Hooraaaay! I got my Edgy boot (u?)splash screen a *verbose* one, just like in Dapper!! ]

Future projects: I really want to "mess" with XFCE, Fluxbox, IceWM (well at least these are the ones I heard about...) and... ummm... yeah, I wanna try Feisty too !!! I guess I'm nuts, but I'm loving it ! I guess this is the true way, I'd really enjoy "productive crashes"... since I'm not a developer (hey, I've got some programming skills though and I surely *will* dive into Linux programming, but it will take some time), at least I have a strong "civil sense" so to say and I think that one pebble-tester more can't hurt, right ?



I really hope you didn't get through all this, it's just my midnight enthusiasm and joy, anyway,

Best regards,

LLRNR
