### Post by TBABill on 2010-03-22
For anyone looking for a "light" distro, Lubuntu in it's current beta is working great on my machine. No glitches found yet and several from alpha 2 have been corrected (like when it wouldn't retain video configuration or wireless security settings on reboot). The system is really fast and everything installed cleanly. All I had to do was setup my wireless Broadcom proprietary driver and install flash, java and OpenOffice post-install. 
 
If you want fast, give it a shot. Definitely feels faster than Ubuntu or Mint, particularly online. Video playback is great and my ATI video card was setup perfectly by default. So far I am loving it.

### Post by TBABill on 2010-03-22
It's a laptop...Acer Extensa with an AMD x2 Tk-58 (1.8MHz??), 2GB RAM, ATI junk video card, Broadcom wireless (requires proprietary driver after install), 200GB HDD. 
 
It's not great but it does the job really well with Linux. With Lubuntu even better. It may not be as full-blown as the others, but I pretty much use my browser, office suite (OpenOffice for now...may install Wine/Office 2007) and a few cheesy games. You'll be able to tell a difference if you already have a distro installed on your older machine. If not, it may surprise you. A co-worker just tried Linux Mint 8 Fluxbox on a really old machine (8+ yrs old) and said it is really fast for him. I would imagine Mint 8 Flux is on par with Lubuntu for speed, but I can't say because I only use Mint 8 Gnome. All I can say is comparing Mint 8 and Ubuntu to Lubuntu....clear winner is Lubuntu for speed. It's not a huge difference, but you can sense the difference switching between them on a reboot.
