### Post by throese on 2010-11-07
Well folks, it all started in...July. My dad had recently bought me a copy of Windows 7. I had originally been running Vista on it, but was having too many of the following problems:

1) Extremely slow
2) Constantly got "Program Not Responding" messages or freezing/crashing programs
3) Took what felt like forever to do one task
4) Just time consuming with AV software and what not
5) Too many viruses, trojans, worms, malware, spyware, and other crap that made it slower than it already was on 1 GB of RAM (Random Access Memory for those who DON'T know)

So, when my dad got back from Taiwan, a short time later (shortly after my 19th or maybe before birthday) I had Windows 7. Great improvement over Vista. Used it for a long time. Still slow and what not. Same problems with viruses.

My girlfriend's sister's boyfriend uses a Mac (don't ask me what brand, all I know is that it's a laptop) suggested that I try Ubuntu out and see how it works for me. So, I was like "Alright, I'm willing to try anything as long as it works." Well, I didn't get the disc from him, but that's fine. I downloaded Bit Torrent for Windows and downloaded Ubuntu 10.04 LTS Lucid Lynx. Took TWO nights (Hughes Satellite Internet SUCKS), but it was well worth it.

Of course, prior to installation, I made a lot of mistakes. First mistake was the whole Wubi process. Gave up on it and went to dual booting, was an awesome experience with all my apps, minus the occasional error checking from both OSes due to Windows never gets along with other operating systems.

Anyway, recently (a few weeks ago), I realized this: The ONLY reason I kept Windows was because of the following:

1) I could chat with my gf through AIM (except it kept logging me out all the time when we tried video chatting), so we switched to Skype which was more reliable and stable, but video quality SUCKED.

2) Well, actually I think the first reason was the only reason since I pretty much NEVER played games on my computer anyway, or rather haven't since I was 18 (Age of Empires = Most amazing game for computer ever. RUN LITTLE PEOPLE AND DO MY BITTING AND EVIL DEEDS! Muahahahah)

Sorry bout that. Anyway, I completely wiped my hard drive entirely. I installed Ubuntu entirely and and LOVING the experience. I've used a Mac here and there since 3rd grade, but that was through school.

My Ubuntu experience, I give it an overall 9.9/10

Reasons are as follows:

Trying to get Remastersys to make a distributable copy that WORKS so I can give it to a friend in my Networking class. He wants to give Linux a shot, so I'm gonna help him.

Trying to do the ffmpeg and x286 thing or w/e for an easier time with recordMyDesktop, because it takes FOREVER to encode or w/e a 3 minute video.

Samba is a huge necessity, since EVERY desktop & laptop in the house is Windows based (except me).

Here's the specs on that:

Dad's laptop: Win XP Home Edition
Mom's laptop: Win 7 Home Premium
Mom's work laptop: Win Vista Home Premium
Sister's (my mom's OLD laptop): Win XP Home Edition
Dell Desktop in Den: Win XP Home Edition (primary computer where our Belkin router and all that jazz is set up)
Sister's Desktop: Win XP Home Edition
Brother's busted laptop: Win Vista
My laptop: Ubuntu 10.04 LTS Lucid Lynx (Virtual Box = Win XP Home Edition and Ubuntu Server)

So, overall, I LOVE Linux and am glad I switched to it. Thank you to the Ubuntu Community and your volunteers, you've made me a very happy and dedicated person
