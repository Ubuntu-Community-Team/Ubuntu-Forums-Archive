### Post by akand074 on 2011-08-05
I've been using Ubuntu since Intrepid was just released and have always sticked with gnome 2 until 11.04 where I used both gnome-shell and Unity. I just installed KDE 4.7 on my oneiric alpha 3 install and I was almost surprised about how gorgeous and polished it is. It has a lot of great and useful features such as the activities, and even had a nice search function using Super+Tab. But overall it was very nice to look at at very nice to use. I also think, as of now, a big chunk of it's software is quite a bit better than GNOME's applications. That said, I'm not sure KDE is my type of DE (not really the way I've liked to work), I may give it a chance sometime, but it is really nice.

Back to the main topic. Gnome-shell is very nice putting aside the real work it still needs, but looking at Gnome 3.2 release features, it looks like they are already starting to really improve it. Unity as of alpha 3 is also getting much better. It's smoother, less bugs and a bit easier to move around applications. Right now, I'm thinking I'll be going to go back to gnome-shell when 11.10 is released (using Unity now) but I just wanted to say how great it is to have an OS with such different yet high quality complete desktop environments. I haven't tried XFCE but it looks good from what I've seen and I've heard lots of good things about it. So there is basically a whole different user experience for everyone, all of which are great. All you have to do is pick the one that's best for you. I think this is the one place where Windows and Mac have always lacked in comparison to Ubuntu. Your Ubuntu box is so much about you, it's personal.

That said, although it's really great to have the choices, in terms of the shear shell, there is select few things from each one that I really like. If I could take a few things from KDE, and also a number of things from Unity and improve the search functionality and put them all into gnome-shell, I'd have by far the most perfect desktop ever. At least for me. Perhaps I'll have to write extensions for gnome-shell in the future to give me the functionality.
