I understand that ubuntu is trying to COMPETE with windows, but honestly, Ubuntu, is making a mistake in trying and compete in the same ways as windows. This is a community of cooperation.

I'm not a computer hardware/software professional. I am an enthusiast who is tired of greed, in every way. This is the reason I USE ubuntu. I'm not going to install Ubuntu and expect something better than Windows; guess what? It's the best paid for Operating System.

I understand that I won't have the BEST performance in everything; what I don't understand is that the games or programs that are known to be NATIVE linux programs, won't work with the "NEWEST and BEST" ubuntu OS.
GUESS WHAT?! If the programs that should work, don't work..... stop updating ubuntu! Start doing longer release cycles so that ATI and other companies for example Nvidia, have a chance to create compatible drivers... Or at least, you give the open source community the chance at developing and filling in the "greed gap".

We are at a point in computer evolution, that for some reason, ATI thinks the R300/x1400 Graphics card, is outdated. What I'm seeing, is that we are at a point where there are a lot of excellent computers, that can handle almost anything. I think it is clear to everyone, that Computer Graphics aren't as realistic as possible and that isn't going to change in a cheap/affordable way anytime soon.... we are a long way away from computer graphics looking real. 

I'm sure that having 1000 wrinkles on someones cheek is an amazing increase compared to the 500 an r300 can display... but  most people aren't computer enthusiasts... most people will prefer to buy an xbox 360 than upgrade their graphics. Most people like me(these days), have laptops. I have a 2 and half year old laptop... It's a good computer, but it's just not supported anymore. As far as I'm concerned, either ATI and nvidia have a monopoly, or ati is just terrible.... Im not sure, so Im going to go Nvidia in my next comp just because I don't understand why ATI refused to support a 3 year old computer and I have no current Nvidia experience.

Anyways.... I know I've ranted..... but I'm super frustrated..... I want to use Ubuntu... and Linux..... but if you cant support the older hardware(and most likely people who are trying to save money), why are you even trying to compete. You can't compete against a privately funded Operating System if you aren't going to think outside of their line of thought. Honestly, I might as well pay for windows, since it works with everything, rather then suffer and struggle figuring out that the  year old ubuntu is better than the newest.
