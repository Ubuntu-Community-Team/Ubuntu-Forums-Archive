### Post by mrpeachy on 2009-11-04
Windows XP decided to run CHKDSK /F at bootup, without asking me and before i knew it my screen was filled with:

replacing invalid security id with default security id for file X

I assumed it was doing something important and necessary, so i let it do its thing...
but when it rebooted my system was completely messed up and vitually unusable.  

I could have tried to use my windows cd to try and fix my installation but instead I downloaded a copy of Ubuntu live cd, burnt it to a disk (luckily these things were still operational on my computer) and decided to ditch windows.

Since then I havn't looked back!

I installed Ubuntu 9.04 for the first time but I couldn't get my sound working.  The problem was that I had an unused audio input card installed in my computer which was confusing the sound system. 

I also got a bit lost in the changes from Windows to Ubuntu.  In particular, one of my earliest feeling was that I was loosing track of what I was installing, as when I installed a program it seemed that lots of other stuff got installed alongside it.

Anyway, I pulled out the unused audio card and tried again, and this time it worked fine.  After reading some stuff I soon got everything up and running (flash etc) and had my desktop re-themed and set up to my liking.  I really liked the customization aspect of the system.  I also really liked the desktop effects.  9.04 was much prettier than XP.  It was also faster in terms of boot, shutdown and use.

The only thing I couldn't get to work right away was my Creative Zen Mozaic music/picture player.  I followed some guides and got it synching with Gnomad2 but no matter what I did I couldnt get pictures to transfer without causing the player errors and to freeze (Im still having problems with this now under Karmic and this is the only reason why i have to boot into XP on occasion.)

Then Karmic was released!  I though "why not just upgrade?".  Turned out to be a bad idea.  The upgrade from 9.04 to 9.10 went badly and I was left with no sound (again) but also no ability to use desktop effects.  So I backed up the little data I had generated and did a fresh install of 9.10.

The fresh install went much better.  Although I was quite alarmed at first when i was informed that my hard disk was about to fail!  But it didnt take long to find out that this was happening to lots of people and was just a bug.

I had written myself a checklist of things to do after installing, so I had everything back to how I wanted it in no time.  I am also much more comfortable with how the system works (breaking out of my windows way of thinking i guess).  Since installing I have had a few Karmic gripes... things have been happening that I would rather not be happening.  A couple of bad boot-ups where the system has been inoperable.  I thought on a couple of occasions that I should just reinstall 9.04 out of annoyance.

But unlike MS Windows I feel much more willing to try and work through the problems.  I'm just waiting for the updates to come and the 9.10 release to stabilize fully.  I think when I decided to go with Linux I sorta knew it wasn't going to be as "easy" as Windows.  After all I havn't ever installed a version of windows where my sound has been non functional.  But the freedom (both monetary and otherwise) is worth it.

I'm thankful to the developers of Ubuntu for giving people like me an alternative!

sorry for the extra long post
