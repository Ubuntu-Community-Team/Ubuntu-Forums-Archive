### Post by rybu on 2007-11-10
Having a bit of trouble with allergies this morning.  I was logged onto a website, trying to buy a christmas vacation ticket, when I sneezed.  It seems something happened on my laptop during the sneeze and I have no good explanation for it. 

A terminal window opened.  I went to shut it down, but by the time I got the cursor over the "close" icon, 10 other terminal windows had opened.  The terminal windows kept opening up so fast no inputs were working, even <CTRL>+<ALT>+<BACKSP> wouldn't do anything.  My taskbar had (as far as I could tell) about 400-600 terminal apps on it by the time I powered my laptop down. 

Running Ubuntu 7.10 on a Thinkpad t61p.  This isn't the only problem I've had with Ubuntu 7.10 -- but strangely, all my problems began on the day the Release Candidate was made available.  All the beta and alpha versions ran near flawlessly on my laptop.  Since 7.10RC, in the boot process between Grub and the log-in screen my screen has been blank, too.  And I get the occasional random system power-down although those aren't as bad as they used to me.  Just once a week now, it seems. Overall I'd say Ubuntu 7.10 is one of the worst Linux distros I've ever run.  7.04 was fine.  I've also run Redhat, Mandriva and Suse without many problems.
