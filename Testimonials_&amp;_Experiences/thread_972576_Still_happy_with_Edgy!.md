### Post by Darrell Lawrence on 2008-11-05
Edgy 6.10. I think the official support ran out in April 2008. That said, support only ran out 6 months ago (end of 18 months), you have it configured the way you want, it works, and you like it. Nothing wrong with that.

IMHO, upgrading is an option. I've bought a Dell XPS M1530 preloaded with 8.04 a month ago. Well Ibex 8.10 is out, so I tried an upgrade. my web cam no longer worked, and pmount no longer worked until I made an entry in /etc/pmount.allow. 

Long story short, Dell furnished the laptop with a complete factory reinstall built into a partition on the hard drive as well as the ability to burn a factory reinstall DVD. Did the factory reinstall and all is well again. Did I need to upgrade? Not really. 8.04 is a long term support release (3 years). Nothing wrong with sticking with what works. That's the great thing about Linux - you can stay as stable as boredom will allow or as bleeding edge as your heart can tolerate :).

Darrell
