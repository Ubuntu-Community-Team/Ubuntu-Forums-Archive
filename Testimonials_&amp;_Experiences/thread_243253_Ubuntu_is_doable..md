### Post by Guns90 on 2006-08-24
I've been reading some of the testimonials in here and thought 'another viewpoint couldn't hurt', so here goes.

I abandoned Windows, reformatted, and installed Breezy a few months ago.  I did a clean install of Dapper the day it was released on a new hard drive.  I moved the Breezy drive over to be hdb1 (my safety net if I had trouble with Dapper).  

So far, we have been able to do everything desired using only the Linux programs.  We find that OpenOffice is as simple to use as Office Suite, as is gnome, firefox, gimp, rhythumbox, and soundjuicer are compared to their counterparts.  We're using everything on ubuntu that we did on Window's to include CD/DVD's, digital cameras, and MP3 players.

I am not very knowledgeable of computers.  Ubuntu is not an easy switch for someone of my computer experience, but it can be done if you have the desire.  I'm sticking with Linux because this computer is also used by my children and they have managed to get 9 viruses when using Windows, even though I bought every 'anti-whatever' on the market.  I've been virus free since the switch.  And best of all....it's free!!

### Post by SDREnate on 2006-08-24
Good to hear your Linux experience is working out for you and your family.  I've noticed that most of the time with Ubuntu, or really any Linux distro is that it doesn't matter how much you know about computers, but your attitude.

A person that begins using Linux with an attitude like "I know Linux is different that Windows, but I'm willing to spend time with it, and I'm willing to learn" often sticks with Linux.

I do agree- not having to worry about antivirus software is a huge plus.

### Post by CydoniaRaven on 2006-08-24
I found that it's easy to learn how to do things "the Linux way" if you can get a comfortable base set up.  By that, I mean having your media files arranged in a familiar manner in your Home directory, easy access to programs that do what you're used to doing in Windows, and so on.  If you can get a nice comfortable base, you will probably feel more confident in trying "new" things.

Not needing to rely on all those partially-effective virus defenses is like waking up and having a huge headache be gone.  I'm trying to convince my virus-prone roommate to at least give Ubuntu a try, now that I have a basic understanding of how to use it and can help her.
