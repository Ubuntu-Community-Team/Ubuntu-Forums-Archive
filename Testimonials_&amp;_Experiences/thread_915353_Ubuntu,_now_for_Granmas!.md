### Post by curvedinfinity on 2008-09-09
Hey all,
Just recently, my grand mother's 10 year old computer broke. Being the techy of the family, I was tasked with replacing it. She basically exclusively used her computer to play solitaire (which makes me crack up every time I think of it). I've been a long-time Ubuntu user, and since it is cheaper than alternatives and includes solitaire, it made sense to try to work in that direction.

I ended up choosing a base Inspiron N system from Dell with very decent specs for $440, including 19" monitor. I had success with two Ubuntu laptops I've purchased from Dell, and because their desktops are an especially good value without any options, that's the way I went (I considered making it myself, but the cost advantage was only about $100).

Anyway, I worried that some of the minor differences might turn her off, since she was used to Windows. When the computer arrived, I set it up for auto-login and auto-shutdown-on-case-button-press. Then I put solitaire's icon on the desktop, along with Mahjong for good measure.

Anyway, it worked great, and she got the hang of it right away. Props to Ubuntu for being an OS simple enough for granmas. :)
