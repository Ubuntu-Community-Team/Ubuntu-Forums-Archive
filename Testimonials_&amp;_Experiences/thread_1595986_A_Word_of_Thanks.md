### Post by State of Blind on 2010-10-13
So, I know many of the first posts by people new to Ubuntu are pleas for help setting up their new install, or questions about how something is done or configured different from Windows.  It's not a bad thing, just sort of the nature of the beast.

I was lucky.  All of the problems I had when getting started were already answered, mostly by the folks at this forum and over at the Kubuntu forums (I'm running Kubuntu 10.04).

So, my first post gets to be a simple, earnest 'thank you'.

Thank you to everyone who puts time and effort into answering the questions we newcomers to the world of Linux throw at you.  Thank you for dealing with our problems, lack of understanding, and I'm sure, sometimes maddening requests for hand-holding.

Because of help and guides on these two forums, I've managed to work around an ACPI issue on my laptop by rebuilding the kernel, fixed a minor but alarming network issue, troubleshooting sound problems and managed to customize my mouse, keyboard, and desktop theme.

I feel a thank you is also warranted to those newbies who did come forward to post their questions and their problems.  Without you, folks like me wouldn't have gotten so lucky as to have an answer to their problems at their fingertips.

So, thanks to all the folks here and at the Kubuntu forums.  Your work is very much appreciated.
