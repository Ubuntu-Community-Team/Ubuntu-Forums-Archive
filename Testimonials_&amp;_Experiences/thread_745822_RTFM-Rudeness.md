### Post by Jouke74 on 2008-04-06
Well I think there is  a huge difference between having a serious problem and not getting through it, and something that could **very** easily be found here already.

In another forum where I post frequently the policy is that you should first state the things you tried and the results of that, and what you actually found in Google which did (not) work. If the posts do not meet these criteria, so if you have done nothing yourself, the threads are closed by the moderators. Now these are all computer Tweakers so they are supposed to know what they are doing with it :-) here there are many newbies. 

All of us are spending time on finding the answers, it's also a part of learning Ubuntu. Answering posts also takes time. As a replier you can choose to answer a post or not. Use this. Then, if you answer make it count and don't post RTFM or at least refer to the thread with the M or link. 

As a thread starter make sure your post also has some "work behind it". Some prior investigation into the problem would be useful. The first questions that are going to be asked are Google hits anyway. (Many of the newbie questions I had lead to this forum in the end btw :-) )

An example: A black screen on boot with a Nvidia 8*00 card I am not answering anymore. After about at least 20 posts on this forum people should be able to find it. Still if you have only Ubuntu installed and this problem occurs, it is a nasty thing, since you cannot fire up Google to find it....

### Post by chromedome on 2008-04-07
In a prior existence, I did sales and service for a major electronics retailer here in Canada.  I came on board when DOS 2.X was just disappearing from the landscape, and people still needed to be pushed into buying a hard drive for their system (!!?).  After a dozen years of working with newbies, I certainly had days when the temptation to swat people with the manual was almost overwhelming.

"It said what?  And...you clicked it anyway?" <sigh>

Last summer, I decided to commit to Ubuntu.  I'd played with the live CD's of Knoppix and Ubuntu previously, and when I got my current "new to me" machine I downloaded Edgy and installed it.  Getting my POS Belkin wireless card to work was quite an odyssey, but with the help of various posters here on the forum (and, yes, my own Google chops) I eventually got it to work.    

There's a great deal of merit in both methods: self-directed learning; and community help.  I learn more when I have time to plug away at things diligently, but hey...I'm self-employed, and I often don't have that kind of time.  A quick question/browse here will often solve my problem, without needing to get into endless research.  In short, I guess, the community gives me quick answers while the docs give me more detailed knowledge.  Both things are good.

One thing I would very much like to see (are you listening, mods?).  There is a tremendous body of information here in these forums, and in the doco.  Much of it, unfortunately, is scattered across the various Ubuntu sites (wiki, official docs, forum) and much of it is dubious, inadequately explained/documented, or just plain out of date.  Last week, for example, I looked up any number of performance tweaks, most of which turned out to be already implemented by default in my up-to-date releases (then Gutsy, now Hardy).  Not that this is a bad thing, of course.  

What are the odds that, to coincide with Hardy and its LTS status, we could build an up-to-date repository of current advice?  I mean, the mods must by now have a pretty good idea of the - shall we say - 40 most-repeated questions on the site?  Let's compile 'em, make 'em sticky, and update as necessary.  Life would be so much simpler if the standard handful of fixes to the standard handful of questions could all be found easily.

Loving life in the Ubuntu world, folks.  My Gutsy and Hardy installs combined took a fraction of the time my fiancee's Vista install needed.
