### Post by MichiganBill on 2007-09-09
I went to a meeting with friends, all of whom use various flavours of Windows. We each brought our laptop, because our meeting consisted of downloading pdf files from a  server and discussing them.

Well my one friend has a very high-end T*shiba laptop, very fancy with 3 gigs of ram. When it booted up, a widget (gadget? whatever they call it?) started up and displayed a beautiful graphical interface. It was like a radar screen and showed summaries of each WiFi node that the card could see. Then the summaries started revolving around a graphical representation of his laptop, which made some pretty "ripples" in the interface. At last a tiny window popped up, so small I could hardly read it: "Wifi card cannot be initialized because it is in use by another program."

So my friend fooled around with various wizards and dialog boxes for almost 45 minutes (after the meeting started) trying to get it to work. Finally somebody came up with a 6 meter ethernet cable and plugged him in.

Of course my trusty Ubuntu Feisty simply connected when I booted it! I didn't even need to start WiFi Radar, it just worked!

Ubuntu is GREAT!

:):):):):)

You should have seen his face when I offered to lend him my laptop!!
