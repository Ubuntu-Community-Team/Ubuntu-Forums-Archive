### Post by redsmoke on 2007-05-21
(let me preface by saying my Father is by no means a computer expert. He barely even knows how to check his e-mail and browse the web)

So, I get a call from my father telling me he can't install his new printer or anything on his old laptop it just refuses to operate giving some sort of windows installer error for everything and runs extremely slow. I tell him he should just reformat and install Ubuntu it would be faster and his printer and everything would just work out of the box.  Being surprising receptive I point him to ubuntu.com and tell him to download the alternative install cd(his computer only has 192MB of ram) on his new vista laptop and burn it to a CD. 

While he was doing that I told him to just copy all his files he might need off the old laptop to the new one which took him a few hours due to large number of pictures etc. I left him with kind of a quick start instructions on how to install ubuntu and get a phone call back a few hours later when he is done backing up and beginning install. 

He is sitting at the screen asking if he should do a guided install with resize or guided install use entire disk. I tell him to use entire disk and he is off. it takes a few hours and I get a phone call as he has booted up and about to login. I walk him through setting it up to automatically login(he doesn't care about typing a username and password everytime),wireless network, and  have him grab automatix and install some added software that I knew he was going to need. 

Finally I walked him through setting up his e-mail and printer(a networked HP photosmart printer) which he seems surprised as to how easy it all was compared to setting it up on his vista laptop. 

Long story long....He is absolutely loving ubuntu and how easy it is to install software(using add/remove...) and how much software just comes free with the computer. He was very frustrated when he found out his copy of office and other software on his new vista laptop were not apart of the deal and only trial edition. 

He is so happy with ubuntu he is talking about putting it on his new laptop in place of vista due to its user unfriendliness. Imagine that Linux more user friendly than windows. I see a change in the winds soon. Thanks Vista! :-)
