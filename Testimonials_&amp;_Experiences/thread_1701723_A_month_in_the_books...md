### Post by comagnum on 2011-03-06
And I am pleasantly surprised. I've been a PC user for the last 15 years, since the ripe ol' age of 8. At 23 now, I feel that I have an above average knowledge on a large range of pc based topics, and thanks to Linux (ubuntu namely) it is ever growing. I had been a windows fan-boy of sorts I guess, always hearing about Linux, it's pros and upsides, yet ignored them. Most of my love for windows came from my gaming scene and as a competitive gamer, it wasn't even an after thought to ever try linux. Being I'm at a different juncture in my life, ample free time, thirst for knowledge, around a month ago I had the curiosity to see what the buzz was all about. I researched different distros from Mint, PCos, Pardus, xubuntu, and ubuntu and decided on the latter. 10.10 seemed like it had everything I would look for in an alternate operating system, tons of apps, ability to customize, compatibility, slight learning curve, ease of use and decided I'd take the plunge. 

As with all new things, it wasn't with out a few hitches here and there, a learning experience at times with drivers, utilizing Wine and the headaches it can cause, Amarok making me want to pull my hairs out trying to get it to read my media drive, but after the trials and tribulations I can honestly say I haven't booted in Windows in over 3 weeks. I have no need to, no desire to, no want to scan for virus's every week to ensure I haven't accidentally been infected, no desire to wait for programs to lag and stutter on opening, no desire to wait for programs to load on start up (I know there's ways around this but I'll admit.. I'm lazy), no desire to look at the same interface I've gazed at for 15 years. Ubuntu is fast, fresh, easy to use (a lot easier than I thought), has an expansive collection of applications, ability to utilize the very few windows apps I need, clean, streamline, and widely supported. The developers have a community connection, they listen, they support, they help, they are users themselves. That's what I find beautiful. The developer to community connection. There's love in each line of code. Love for users, love for developers, love for the curious, love for the laymen. It's a beautiful thing to me.


As I'm sure you've all seen and heard, I'm sold. I will never (unless it's needed) use windows for a personal machine again. If Ubuntu 10.10 is a sign of things to come, I strongly feel that you may see many others, companies, hardware and software developers, consumers, etc coming on board. I hope that the user/developer connection never changes.

Keep it up guys, you have gained a life long user. Everything you do, I'm sure, is appreciated greatly. I'm thoroughly impressed.
