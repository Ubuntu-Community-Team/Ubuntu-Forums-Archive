### Post by cprofitt on 2007-06-07
Last night I worked with my six year old daughter to migrate her machine from Windows XP to Ubuntu. Her machine has some issues (due to a dirty, dirty cdrom reader) that forced me to have her go to bed before the machine was finished.

When she woke up she was very excited and asked if I had completed putting Ubuntu on her machine. When I told her I had she ran to her computer and instantly went to [www.webkinz.com](www.webkinz.com) (yes I pre-installed for her the necessary plug-ins) and was excited that her computer seemed to work better.

She then wanted to get rid of the ugly brown desktop background (girls, even young ones apparently are not fond of the color brown). I anticipated this and had downloaded five or six desktop images for her and added them to her desktop choices. She right clicked on the desktop and knew right where to go to change the setting. She chose a nice tropical scene with white beaches and a over-hanging palm tree.

As she was getting ready to head out to the bus she was telling her mom that she had "Ubuntu" and it was cool. She told me that she was probably the only kid at school with Ubuntu; so I expect her to brag about it all day.

We will see what after school brings...

The relevant PC specs are:

PIII 500
512 MB PC 100 ram
4x CD-ROM
Nvidia MX 4000 video card

I will post more about after school and day two later... 

If anyone has some suggestions on educational packages beyond what was included in the add/remove options that would be most appreciated.
