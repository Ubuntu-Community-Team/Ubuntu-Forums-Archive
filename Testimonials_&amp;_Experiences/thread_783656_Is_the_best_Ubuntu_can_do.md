However, as with any popular piece of software, there have been numerous complaints about fundamental issues. Despite this, I do not think that the problem lies in the release cycle. If you feel that the release cycle is too rushed, then you are more than welcome to not upgrade to the latest version for a few months, until you feel that the newest release is ready, or you can skip releases entirely. I think that the 6-month cycle is due to a desire to be able to updgrade to the latest and greatest improvements (new version of x-server, gnome, kde, etc.) and give official support to new updates, including making sure that new versions of software are compatible with other software that comes included in the OS. Of course, I may be wrong in my reasoning, and if someone knows more about Canonical's decision for the 6-month cycle, please feel free to correct me.
