### Post by mr-bean on 2007-08-05
I've been using Ubuntu for around 5 days now, and I have to say I really like it.
It's nice and fast, the 3D destop effects from Compiz-fusion are cool and one or two of them will even be quite useful for me, but it's mostly just eye candy.

Linux also has a lot of apps than can replace the ones I'd use in windows (mostly).  I have a few apps that I would still need to keep WinXP for.

General day to day running would be ok using Linux, the thing I'd really miss is chatting to buddies (Voice) via MSN and Skype.  I know Skype is available on Linux, but I've found it not to be all that stable on 64bit Ubuntu. aMSN and Pidgin will do text IM, but not voice.  :-(

One thing that bugs me is how difficult it is to setup your displays if you're using the accelerated ATI binary drivers.
I thought the XP drivers were sucky, but these are something else....
I was looking for a dual DVI card to replace my ATI X550.
I shall definately be buying Nvidia hardware this time.

Linux has certainly changed a lot since I last looked about 10 years ago.

:guitar:

Regards,
Mr-Bean.

### Post by zero244 on 2007-08-05
Welcome to Ubuntu: Hope you hang around a while longer.
Im a new user too.....8 months and my machine is working just fine.
Glad you have Compiz working good. Im still running beryl with most all the features turned on. Its very stable and very fun to use.
It uses some resources but I have plenty so I might as well enjoy the effects.
Try and do your research and learn the ins and outs of Linux, it will pay off in spades up the road.
On my final install I set up a partition for my home folder and that works out very nice for doing backups or if you should have to do a reinstall most of your stuff comes right back if you don't format your home partition.
I dont even think about viruses or spyware anymore (unless Im in Windows) which is a big relief.
And Im not in Windows very much.
Good luck with Linux and keep us posted on your opinions and progress.
