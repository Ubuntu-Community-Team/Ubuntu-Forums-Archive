### Post by ericmc783 on 2009-10-27
I've been using Ubuntu 9.04 everyday since July. Before this, I had tinkered slightly with other distros before. My experience has been very positive. 

I have an HP DV7t notebook. 
Specs: 

-2.26 GHZ Intel Core 2 Duo.
-4 GB RAM. (3GB available, I think I made the mistake of installing 32bit instead of 64bit. Nonetheless, 3GB is enough).
-nVidia 9600 512MB Dedicated.

Ubuntu runs very smoothly and stable, consuming far less resources than Vista (obviously). The only minor annoyance is that sound only comes out of the left speaker (this may be more of an HP drawback, than a linux issue, since the exact same issue occurs when installing XP). For great sound, I can use headphones. DVD and music playback all work great. Everything works smoothly, with virtually no issues. Anymore, it seems like the only time I boot into Windows is when I want to do some video editing, or I get a hankering to play some audiosurf.

Not only is Ubuntu and Linux great, but the community surrounding it is as well. My thanks go to the countless # of people whom have contributed to the GNU project, and the Linux kernel, over the years. Not to mention those whom have helped with Ubuntu and Debian. I can only hope to pay forward in the future by somehow contributing myself.

### Post by Marlonsm on 2009-10-27
Glad to know you liked it. It's always nice to see new people around here. I'm also quite new to Ubuntu, about one year, but I've really liked what I've seen so far.

BTW, to edit videos, there are some good editors for Ubuntu, the one I like the most is Kdenlive (you can find it in Applications > Add/remove).
It's designed for KDE, so it won't integrate very well with your desktop depending on your theme, but it's easy to use and has a great functionality.
