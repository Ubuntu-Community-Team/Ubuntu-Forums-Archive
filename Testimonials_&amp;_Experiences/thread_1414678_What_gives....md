I agree in many ways. I too, along with many techie friends, all adopted Ubuntu because we all hoped we "saw the light" as you say.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2010-02-24
Pulseaudio does work very well for a lot of people. And I know that there are some people who criticise Pulseaudio because they don't actually know how to use it; to be fair, until 9.10 Ubuntu didn't ship by default any programs to actually set up PA.

Some people criticise Pulseaudio for conflicts between ALSA and OSS that always occurred (when using older OSS programs).

And some people criticise Pulseaudio for problems with other sound libraries such as OpenAL, which have been problematic since before Pulseaudio.

I guess some people must have actual trouble with Pulseaudio that are not caused by user error and are not actually caused by other libraries, but it's difficult to tell who's complaining about what problem. When a hundred people post to a forum complaining about it, how many have actually found a bug in Pulseaudio? Ten? The other ninety are giving the software an undeserved bad name.

I still see people refusing to use Network Manager, and trying to advise other people not to use it, because of problems they had when the software was in its infancy.

If it's not perfect by the time it is first deployed, some people will never give it a second chance.

EDIT: I'm not suggesting that you don't have a legitimate problem with Pulseaudio. Just thought I'd clear that up. But make sure that your problems are not from the above things before attributing blame. I have had an excellent Pulseaudio experience on all my machines over the last year.
