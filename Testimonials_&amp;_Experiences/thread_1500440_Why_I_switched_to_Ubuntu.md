### Post by Pangoria on 2010-06-03
For 15 years I've been using a Windows PC.  Every 2-4 years my PC would either catch fire, become completely corrupted and die, or get a horrible virus.  My last PC was no exception, and somehow Vista became corrupted when it decided to auto restart to install an upgrade while it was doing a defrag.

I was at work when it happened, but needless to say I came home to a computer where nothing worked, and I couldn't even reinstall it, since even the restore was corrupted.

I started using my wife's PC, and started reading about Linux.  I remembered hearing horror stories of everything being command line, and it being really buggy etc.  But what pushed me over the edge, was Leo Laporte talking about Kubuntu to replace windows on Netbooks.  From Kubuntu I found Ubuntu, and finally decided, why not?  I'll just completely format my hard drive with it and just start fresh, besides, it was free.  At least I'll have my PC back until I can afford a newer gaming PC.

So I installed, and it was like finding a sort of heaven.  It was an easy installation, it was fast, it had access to stuff I could use.

Never before have I felt more nerdy than using Ubuntu.  Something about having to do things via terminal instead of pop up permissions.  It was much like the "horror" stories people told me, but I found that I was okay with it, and that sometimes just being able to quickly type up sudo get-  can be really helpful, especially when I see a website talking about a program or what not.

I then spent 2 days setting the desktop the way I like it, and getting  used to the interface. On the third day, I came home to see my computer not starting up.  I spent 3 hours troubleshooting, until I found out that one of my RAM cards went bad.  Took it out, and I'm now back.

It sucks that I can't play my Steam games on Linux, like Batman, or my MMO of choice Champions Online.

That is my Ubuntu experience, the first week.

Much love,
Pangoria Fallstar
