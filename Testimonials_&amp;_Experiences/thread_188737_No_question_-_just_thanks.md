### Post by Csabo on 2006-06-04
**This forum rocks!**

In the past week(s), I had so many questions, as I'm new to Ubuntu and Linux. Every time I came here, searched, and found an answer. It saved me time, I didn't have to post a question and then wait... So I'd like to say **thanks** to all the people who spent time answering all these questions so far, it's really appreciated!

So now, since I'm so happy, I'll give you guys a quick list of what I did on my mahcine with Ubuntu on it.

- It updated all by itself without a hitch to Dapper. Amazing. Love the new icons and the new Human theme. (I know there must be a million things improved under the hood, but that's what most obvious.)
- I had a tiny 6GB drive in the machine, and now added a 250GB one as a secondary drive. I was worried a bit, the packaging was full or warnings like you need SP*this* for Windows *that* etc. Ubuntu hasn't failed me though, it saw the drive right away.
- Didn't know how to partition the thing. Found it on the forums: Gnome Partition Editor. Easy.
- Didn't know how to format it. Found it on the forums, and used the built-in Disks Manager. Easy!
- Didn't know how to access/mount it. Found it on the forums, but I screwed up here first... I edited /etc/fstab and mounted the new HDD as /home. This totally screwed up the system, I count no longer log in. Now I understand that /home is special. I figured out by myself how to login to a failsafe terminal, commented out my newly added line from /etc/fstab and restarted, everything was fine. Then once again I searched the forums and found the perfect tutorial here: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=46866](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=46866), and it's now done.

Right now I'm copying stuff over from my main DELL machine to my Ubuntu machine (how did I set up the networking? searched the forum). That'll give me lots of free space, so I can follow another tutorial I found here, to make my main machine dual boot between XP and Ubuntu :)

That's all I wanted to say. Ubuntu rocks! :-D
