### Post by 1roxtar on 2011-06-04
Whether it be with Ubuntu's Unity, Gnome 3 Shell, Mac OSX Lion, or Windows 8...everything on the desktop is changing and evolving. Users have to adapt to this change and when we do, we will find our computing to be richer and richer if we just give it half a chance. Wasn't it just last year that people where whining that the standard Gnome DE was growing stale and full of bloated old code???

Personally, I figure, I get my Linux for free and it's miles ahead of it's proprietary counterparts, in my opinion. I'm not a programmer, but I give back by testing dev versions and submitting my bug reports and offer some suggestions along the way. If Canonical or the Gnome Team decide they want to try something new, I try it out, adapt or simply find another alternative.

I'm not going to join the disgruntled mud-slingers. I'm a two-year Linux user and I use my Linux for everything! My thanks and gratitude goes to the thousands of people who work hard to give us the freedom to choose how we use our computers.

Other Things I'm Looking Out For:

1.  Android's Ice Cream Sandwich
2.  WebOS on HP desktops
3.  Kubuntu on mobile devices
