### Post by Se[BBB]e on 2006-06-16
I've now been using Ubuntu Dapper for a while and I plan on keeping it my default OS, though I'll also XP as a backup for those things I just cant do in Ubuntu, like playing Half-Life 2 or getting TV-out in color (I know it can be done, I just dont know how, and I spent loads of time getting it to work in XP).

Anyway, I have a large passion for computers and tech in general. Ive used computers since I was 5. And Im 15 now. So I know a lot about them. I do programming. And now I do Linux too.

But the way to get here has been long. I tried out Linux first in 2000. It was Dragon Linux 0.8 ;) and I dont know why but it was the slowest thing ever... It took 10 minutes to start up on my 2 year old PC (from the HDD). And I couldnt figure out how to do anything at all on it. I wanted to try and install some things to it but all I got was C++ source code and instructions to "unpack the file and write ---" and a whole page of commands. I didnt even know where to write it...

A LOT has happened since then. Linux IS superior to Windows now. There's no arguing about it. It has better hardware support, better customisability (obviously...), is more stable, faster and surprisingly, is more user friendly once set up.

Over the years Ive tried quite a few Linux distros but never been able to do much and gotten very poor hardware support. When I found Ubuntu I was surprised. It was the most user friendly distro I had used. My hardware worked. MP3s didnt :-x 

But then I found out about Automatix and it installed the appropriate codecs and a lot of other goodies :p But I still had pains configuring some things. But while Linux is superior to Windows, it is NOT the same thing. It's a lot closer to the hardware and a lot more powerful, potentially meaning harder to use for the beginner. But I know feel pretty familiar with the UNIX-like structure. I know that C: is /dev/hda0 and I know how to mount it.

But the most amazing is, I feel happy when I press the POWER button on my ugly black Dell box. Im know I wont be greeted by a nasty ad popup and I know my PC wont slow to a crawl while loading antivirus, 2 firewalls and 3 zillion other applications. And I wont have to watch the awful flashlight rolling back and forth while the computer supposedly mounts the harddrives, load the icons, or whatever it's doing when I click "My Computer"... Because I have no idea why that takes such long time...

So overall, Im very, very happy with my Ubuntu setup. It works, it's fast, it's stable, it's beautiful, it's fun to use. I know most probably dont think it's fun to mount a harddrive, but I somehow get satisfaction out of it. Im closer than ever to my hardware, I decide. If an application crashes, I have the power to kill it instantly. Mastering Linux is a lot easier that I, and probably most, thought. I know feel like a pretty experience Linux user, and I feel proud demonstrating the file permissions and mount commands to my friends, though it isnt as advanced as they believe...

As a hardcore computer user and programmer, Linux is the operating system Ive always wanted. It has gotten incredibly mature since I first met it. I dont fear the penguin anymore, and I have more control than ever. Linux is the closeness to hardware of the C64 and Amiga 500, combined with a modern operating system and a wonderful GUI (actually loads of wonderful GUIs) and awesome free applications. This is not a good thing though... Since it means I use my computer even more :p

To the total beginner, I believe Linux is not harder for them to learn than Windows is. The setup on Ubuntu is much, much, MUCH easier and simpler than the Windows XP setup. And when the working system is jolted with some Automatix, it's ready to go and do whatever the newcomer wants to do.

I believe Linux is close to mass adoption and if it continues to evolve like this it will even be superior to products costing hundreds of dollars... Wait... It already is... But if it gets any better, even more people will switch. And once people realize Linux is the link that makes you and your computer the best friends on earth, they will never go back to Windows.

This is my experience.
Sebastian Fabian, 15
Sweden

PS. Im sorry I wrote an entire essay, but Im just so happy using my computer :p

Positive! - If it's a "black and white"-issue, it's PAL/NTSC issues i 99% of the cases!

### Post by stortford on 2012-03-12
Over the last five years or so I experimented with various versions of Linux, but it was not until I tried Ubuntu 10.4 LTS in September 2011 that I felt that this was just what I needed. From then I've used it exclusively.

The installation was flawless and the changeover from Windows XP Home straightforward. I've since installed it on several other family computers including a laptop and all now work perfectly.

It is superb. It is totally stable. It boots up in less than half a  minute and shuts down in even less time. Updates are rare and fast, and  after install the computer always still works perfectly. 

The range of software available is huge and I've found that whatever I've needed, even highly specific widgets, has been written by someone cleverer than me who has had the kindness to share the solution freely with other users. The Ubuntu Community spirit is amazing and a joy to experience.

My message to anyone thinking of a change to Ubuntu is  - don't hesitate, do it now!!
