### Post by 1roxtar on 2011-09-27
I am a computer tech in a town of 10,000 people.  I do a lot of fixing and upgrading computers, virus cleaning, etc.  I have some customers who are always getting the same viruses over and over again because they continue visiting the same unsafe websites and downloading the same infected freeware.  

I get to the point that when I get tired of the same constant "emergency" phone calls, I suggest they try out Ubuntu.  I have been doing this for the past couple of years with great results.  Ubuntu haters keep saying that it isn't ready for the mainstream or has too steep of a learning curve (which I find laughable), but my own personal experience has proven otherwise.  Ubuntu is serving these families well and they have been happy with it's performance.  

My customers who have never heard of Ubuntu or Unity before, have found it easy to learn 11.04 and navigate through it with only a brief tutorial on my part.  In fact, the younger crowd see the Unity interface as a cool factor because it looks so much different than the common Windows OS.  All in all I find myself proud to support and promote Ubuntu because of the freedom it gives me and because it hasn't failed me since I began using it (9.04).
