### Post by DforVendetta on 2009-01-03
I had been a Windows user for nearly twenty yeas. Being only twenty-six, you can say that I have become quite familiar with it's workings. If I tell you that I am a windows power user you likely response may be "Son, is that possible?" I can prove that I am a Windows 95, 98, xp, and Vista power user because it takes me nearly a week to install it, assume proper configuration, utilize speed and efficiency, and even make it internet safe (Vista netbios anyone?). Furthermore, I was even able to update my Windows. It was only a task I would confront about once a month and 40 mg ritalin because I would have to edit registry services configuration, edit router ActiveX configuration, edit firewall rules, edit PC side web filters, and last but not least, analize and track each update to ensure all hardware and software performed correctly and up to snuff.

As a personal note, I am a member of the Steelworkers Union here in Ohio and have been recently laid off. It was then that I realized just how few hobbies I have to challenge my ability to learn and intiution. I said to myself "Self, wouldn't this be a great time to pick up a hobby that can help you forever?" The answer was yes. All my adult life there has been a monthly "IT menopausal ritalin abuse" rolling around in my brain. Don't get me wrong, when I was about fifteen I managed to intstall a copy of Redhat. I couldn't get anything to work and it didn't seem as if anyone was willing to help me. Then I tried Debian and Linspire with the same results. So for years I kept pushing Windows and like a high school bully it would always push back.

Ubuntu 8.10. Holy sh*t, I can control the speed at which my panels hide themselves! I have desktop temperature without crActiveX! I can control the behavior of EACH one of my four core processors! I don't even need those memory hogs like Norton, which in my opinion is practically VMWare with A.I.

Sure Ubuntu has taken my 2.4 GHz quad, 6 gigs RAM from a Windows horse to a circuit powerhouse. But I think that the one reason why I LOVE it so much is because of people like you. No matter what problems I had a month ago when I installed my new baby I was always able to find help. Not a worthless online help application, but real people with a passion to make my life just a little bit easier. No one, wherever I was looking for help asked for a damn thing in return. Everyone who helped me had one thing in common, they all took pride in what they were doing and had the common sense to have faith in the better product. Over time I will grow old with Unix, this I am sure, as your pride has become mine.  For this I say Thank You.

Dan
