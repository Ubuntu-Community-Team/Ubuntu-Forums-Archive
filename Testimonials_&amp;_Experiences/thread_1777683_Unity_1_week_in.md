### Post by FormatSeize on 2011-06-10
> **usagiakumu said:**
> I must say this is a sane choice for Gnome, but wouldn't it have been easier to just use KDE?
Probably, but the setup of KDE and Gnome are quite different in that you go to the environment depending on what you want. If you want over-the-top graphical effects, bouncing cursors, a desktop widget labeled "Desktop" on your desktop containing more widgets that are on your desktop (it's a desktop inside of your desktop), along with three services running in the background, then you go to KDE. Or, if you want to spend several hours going through far too many menus to make your desktop environment suitable to your needs, then you go to KDE. If you want something sleek, elegant, yet highly configurable and easy to use, then you go to Gnome. Basically, you go to KDE if you want to work backwards to what you need, and you go to Gnome if you want to add things until you get what you want.
