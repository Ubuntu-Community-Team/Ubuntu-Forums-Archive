### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-12-14
> miguelpires
This is my humble opinion, and I think, that Linux companyes, or Linux Foundation, need to start talking to software-houses about this. 

In [2001]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License") MS CEO Steve Ballmer referred to Linux as:
> a cancer that attaches itself in an intellectual property sense to everything it touches

I don't believe that opinion has changed one bit and I seriously doubt Windows app developers would agree to license their software under a GPL license.

Your observations regarding the merging of proprietary software and FOSS/OSS have been documented here many times before. Yet, IMHO the gap seems to be widening. I can't speak for others, but personally I hope the Linux community (as an entity) never permits MS or any of it's affiliates to engage in proprietary/predatory practices under the guise of porting.

Much like Windows, Linux is a commitment. Some may wish to enjoy "all" the benefits of both without dual booting. But, I doubt that's going to occur anytime soon. Ask yourself, why MS would use the WGA system to check for the existence of a wine registry key with the intent of disallowing genuine Windows downloads for that system? Doesn't sound to me like MS (or it's affiliates) have any desire what so ever to engage in fair practices with Linux. Big surprise!

As for what people purchase for a home system. Seems logical that they'd purchase that which they're familiar with. Especially, if they have used Windows "only" prior to being indoctrinated to Linux at work. Much may depend on how they view their work system and some with families may simply not wish to invest in the learning curve. There's just to many variables for why a user may purchase one system over another, including availability.

This is actually a recurring discussion, which IMO belongs in the forums RD.

Just my $0.02
