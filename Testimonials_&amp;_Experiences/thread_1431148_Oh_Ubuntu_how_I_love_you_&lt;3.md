### Post by _h_ on 2010-03-16
Growing up with Windows starting with Windows 3.1 and eventually ending with Windows 7, Linux was something I've wanted to try for the past few years but never got really got the time to try it out until recently in the past month or so.

Linux was a major change from Windows, and I started into the linux world with Linux Mint 8 "Helena" edition. I used Mint for about a week, and knowing Mint was derived from Ubuntu, I decided to give Ubuntu a go.

I've only been using Ubuntu 9.10 64bit for a little over a week now, and I must say I am glad it is now my one and only OS on my laptop.

I had pretty much fallen in love with it within the first few hours of installing it and running it.

And knowing that Linux has very little amount of viruses, I was finally free from the security tasks in Windows that I was performing everyday for hours on end.

I was spending so much of my day trying to keep my Windows secure, spending nearly half the day every single damn day defragging, running norton, spybot, malwarebytes, tuneup utilities...only to turn around and still have people breaking into my computer and still getting viruses, malware, spyware and other junk.

On top of that, I dreaded formatting with nearly 300gb of data I had collected throughout the internet, including every little software that I had going on. Formatting meant I was spending another 4 days after the format getting every bit of software and data I could get back.

But not with Ubuntu. Pretty much all the software I use is right in the Software Center or Synaptic. This saves me time, and helps me get back to working online alot faster.

Now about performance, Ubuntu tops it all. I beat the living daylights out of my laptop sometimes doing heavy hard drive intensive tasks, and intense CPU tasks requires tons of cycles.

This was making Windows constantly non-responsive, forcing me to have to reboot and either pick up where I left off or start over again.

But not with Ubuntu. I've been beating the crap out of it for a week now, and the only thing that happens is the screen greys out for a few seconds as everything catches up. No blue screens, no non-responsiveness, no crashes, no lockups...nothing.

All in all, I just want to say how much I appreciate Ubuntu and I want to thank Canonical and all the developers for producing such excellent OS.

I love you Ubuntu. :D
