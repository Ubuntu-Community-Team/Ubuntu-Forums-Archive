### Post by calraith on 2008-06-10
> **deadtom said:**
> Could be Bad memory, mother board issues, bios problem, CPU problem... any number of things.

Could even be a nut loose on the keyboard.  :)

Anyway, I agree with deadtom.  Quit using that crappy Kingston RAM and get yourself some Mushkin or Corsair.  Run a memtest86+ overnight and see whether you have any errors when you get up the next morning.  Or if your motherboard has an SIS chipset, an upgrade to a motherboard with an Intel, VIA, ALi, or even an nVidia chipset (even in spite of the sometimes buggy APIC) would be an improvement.  You aren't running a laptop as a server, are you?

Incidentally, I've also seen significant stability improvements in areas prone to brownouts and other power anomalies, by simply adding a UPS.  Has to be one of the brick-style UPSs with the outlets on the back; not the strip-style with outlets on the top.  Those strip-style UPSs aren't much more than glorified extension cords, in my experience.
