### Post by wazupwiop on 2011-08-09
Hello Fellow Forumers,
     
I just joined the Ubuntu forums today because I have been impressed by Ubuntu 11.04.  I have used Ubuntu off and on since 8.04.  I started using it as an experiment.  I installed it on a bootable USB stick just to mess around and play in a Linux OS.
     
As I entered highschool, I realized that Ubuntu would be the perfect OS to use for all of the reports and things I have to make for school.  Open office was great, and it suited my needs well.  My mom (my homeschool teacher) was impressed with how my reports turned out.  I also downloaded Open Office for the family computer so that she could open my files that were created in Ubuntu.  It was amazing how easily things slid back and forth between XP Pro and Ubuntu.
     
I was a steady user for about a year or two, then 10.04 came out.  I found 10.04 to be extremely glitchy.  I had a glitch where the mouse wouldn't move and my bootloader was completely messed up.  After fixing the mouse glitch and the bootloader, I experienced more issues; I switched back to 9.10 and XP.
     I tried Linux Mint and Fedora, but I wasn't impressed.  My dad works for Microsoft currently, and he worked for Toyota in the IT department before that.  Fedora and Ubuntu were the favorites of his coworkers.  For this laptop, I think Ubuntu is the best.  Linux Mint totally screwed up my bootloader, and Fedora was lacking something.
     
I recently decided to try 11.04 because I am now trying to start a little home studio so I can record songs and guitar parts to share with friends over facebook and youtube.  I must say, recording with Ardour in 11.04 is so painless.  I am experiencing about 20ms of latency, which isn't bad at all considering that I am using a relatively older Dell 600m.
     
I was extremely impressed as soon as I booted up for the first time after updates.  To record, it was simply plug and play.  It was super easy and everything worked from the beginning.  There have been no glitches to speak of, and I have been really impressed with the ease of use.
     
I downloaded the Ubuntu Studio packages.  They are awesome.  Ardour seems to be performing well, and I cannot wait to try the video editing software.  I contemplated buying a mac, but Ubuntu seems to fit the bill pretty well.
     
The LibreOffice applications work great for my school requirements.  Spreadsheets and text files are easily saved into Windows formats, and Windows formats are easily opened by LibreOffice.  It is perfect for me, because the rest of my family are Ubuntu users.
     
While Ubuntu isn't totally perfect..... yet, I am glad that I am using it for my main OS.  My printer doesn't have a driver that works with Ubuntu, so I am dual-booting with Windows XP (shiver).  The latest release has been mostly plug-in a play.  I am extremely happy.

I have been extremely impressed by this version of Ubuntu.  I hope that in the future all updates and upgrades will perform like this.
