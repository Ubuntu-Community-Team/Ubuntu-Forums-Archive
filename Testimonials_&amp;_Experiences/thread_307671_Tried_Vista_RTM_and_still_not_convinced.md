### Post by rev_b on 2006-11-26
Ok, so the thing should only hit the shops next january, but it's hard to resist after seeing all those ISO's floating around in the Internet. :rolleyes: Hard to believe, but for those who like conspiration theories, this actually serves M$ interests in making "Vista" the new standard, as too many people may fail to see any advantage over the now "old" XP.

It has improved over the RC1, but I'm very far for being convinced. It still takes forever to install. It still has to reboot 2 times to install. It takes  a long time to boot. After that, it still feels bloated and unfinished. They care to add a driver for my printer and video card, but they couldn't be bothered shipping a WDM driver for my very rare and unhead sound card, a sound blaster audigy :rolleyes: Network still needed a few tweaks to start. It was properly detected and configured, but by default, it blocks all network access, and when one removes it, it complains about lowering the security, blah, blah. Fine, but I do need the OS to see my router in order to connect to the internet...:rolleyes: The control panel is complicated and unintuitive. All those nice "aero" effects feel a bit pointeless and limited. It's fun to see how a alpha project like Beryl can already provide much neater effects than Aero...:-D

But what really pisses me off is, 7 years after the first dual-head card being available, windows still doesn't allow to stretch the taskbar into the second monitor, or to offer 2 taskbars...  

After installing the OS, and using Ubuntu and apt-get for some weeks, one gets the feeling - "what now?", and suddenly the idea of open-source, repositories download software makes a lot of sense...    

So I rebooted, started Ubuntu from Grub (I have my windoes installs in another HDD) and after getting (much faster) into my customized gnome desktop, I found it to be much better looking, sharp and functional than windows vista.

So this is the testimonial of someone who has used windows back since MS-DOS ;), and I really don't like the way it's taking. What's even funnier is there's free software out there that's actually better - not just doing the same thing - it's better.

So I'll stick with Ubuntu Edgy, just make that Feisty as nice and make sure the upgrade is painless...;)
