### Post by 3Miro on 2011-05-13
Gnome 2 - good, but deprecated and dead.

KDE - well rounded, fully features, the most customizable DE out there. Somewhat heavy on the hardware. I have experienced some sound conflicts between KDE and Ubuntu, also if you come form Gnome, then you will have to change many of the apps that you are using. This is due to the different toolkit (QT vs GTK).

XFCE - there are a few differences between XFCE and Gnome 2, and in many of those, XFCE wins. It is small and fast, yet it supports a lot of customizations. It is very easy to install and test under Ubuntu (sudo apt-get install xfce4). XFCE is my DE on 4 computers, both slow and fast.

Both Unity and GS are very new and a lot more would be done on both of those projects. Currently I prefer Unity as it is build on familiar and very customizable compiz. In the future only time will tell.
