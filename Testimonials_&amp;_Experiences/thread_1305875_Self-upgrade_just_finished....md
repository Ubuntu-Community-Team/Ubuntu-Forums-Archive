I hope not.

I must be one of the few that prefers the Ubuntu brown and with the simplicity of modification, why change the original? It's unique and IMHO differentiates Ubuntu from other OS's. However, I'm certain a change is probably necessary to attract more MS users who like the glitz and glitter.

As minimalist user, I also believe in KISS. It's been my experience that KISS works and increases stability. Since first loading Ubuntu  three years ago, all our Pc's have been rock stable and we've never struggle with functionality. It works every time we push the start button and has never failed. Wish I could say that about our previous OS.

My suspicion is by increasing the bloat and number of shining objects, Ubuntu will become significantly more Windows user friendly. For example the Control Center. None of the users I know have ever used the control center. They prefer the terminal or the package manager. I remove the control center every time I upgrade to a new version and have never found a use for it. But it does remind one of.....

Just my $0.02
