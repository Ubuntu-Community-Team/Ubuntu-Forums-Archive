### Post by redsmoke on 2007-06-09
So,

My brother in-law just graduated from high school and is headed off to college and wanted a laptop for college. My wife had an old laptop that she doesn't use anymore that wasn't too bad on specs but not exactly the fastest thing around anymore ( 1.3ghz celeron M with 768MB of Ram and 40gb hard drive).  The laptop hadn't been touched in a while and had windows xp on it and was running real sluggish so I decided to load Ubuntu on it. I figured with Openoffice.org and the plethora of other free software available on Ubuntu it would be the best option for a college student. 

I installed it and configured it with the help of getautomatix (for codecs, google earth, picasa, swiftfox, beagle, etc) and then added a healthy selection of desktop themes for him to choose from as well. I then though laptops wifi was detected and setup with the native Linux drivers they seemed rather buggy (bcm4308 ) so I installed ndiswrapper and used the windows driver. Finally I changed the login window to the list users section.

After having configured the laptop fully I decided it was time to hand it over to him and he couldn't be happier with it. At first he was a little weary of the fact it wasn't windows xp(he has never used anything else) but in his own words after he got used to how things work he loves it over windows. Now, he came and asked me for a Ubuntu CD for his friend because his friend wants Ubuntu too now because he thinks its cool.

So yeah thats another person for me I have helped move to Linux and couldn't be happier with it. Before Ubuntu Feisty I was personally very skeptical of getting anyone to use Linux but after Feisty has made everything so easy its hard not to recommend it for its price, performance, and usability. Everything just works and one big selling point for me is the fact with Debian package system ALL of your software is ALWAYS up to date and you never will have to worry about upgrading again.

### Post by MattDTownsend on 2007-06-11
You make some good points, but I don't think they make my argument moot.  I don't think *that* many people are really used to MacOS -- until a few years ago, only people in fields like education and design really knew that much about them.  Even now, I would suspect your average Windows user knows little about OSX, except that they'd rather buy that next than "the Vista."  (I've heard that a lot.  There's a great deal of animosity toward Windows Vista, in people who never really knew there were options before.)

Wine is fantastic, and it's amazing what the guys have done.  However, compatibility can still be a bit spotty, and it takes time to work out those problems. I don't think it's a weakness to have many options -- this is one of the greatest advantages of operating in Linux.  Rather, it's a weakness that there is no *clear* option.  One could use konqueror, galeon, mozilla, opera, IE-in-wine, lynx, and God knows how many other browsers, in Linux.  However, Firefox is the clear choice.  Not everyone uses it, but it's now a baseline from where all of the other browsers can be measured.  The same cannot be said of any of the various Wine projects.  This could change any time, of course, as VirtualBox magically dropped out of the woodwork only months ago and is now the standard choice for many Linux users.

Finally, I think it's a mistake to think that anything -must- be done to defeat M$.  First off, that assumes that Linux has to beat M$.  I agree, that'd be nice, but I've been using Linux on the desktop for nine years, only one of those in Ubuntu, and Redmond's existence hasn't been particularly noticeable to me.  Since I don't care.  And I can use Linux for another nine years, and I don't think it'll really matter if Gates is still around.

Keep in mind that Microsoft loves "software wars."  There were the "browser wars" between Netscape and IE for many years, and by insisting it was a "war," Microsoft implied that they could, and probably would win.  And they love making that implication.  But observe what happened when Firefox came along.  It absorbed a large piece of the market share almost overnight, I believe, and anyone who knows an ounce about the internet is now using Firefox and telling others to.  The war was over before it began.

Any "war" between Microsoft and FOSS will have nothing to do with quality of software or image or even point-of-sale access.  It's going to have to do with litigation, smearing, and buy-outs.  This is how these people operate.  Ultimately, though, FOSS wins every war before it begins, since it's impossible to kill and the  mantra of open source technology is that it MUST and WILL improve.  Linux (as a kernel and as the body of software that runs on top of it) has done a hell of a lot of improving as a tiny, tiny percent of the market.

So, I think your hurdles are misplaced.  In the end, the only hurdle Ubuntu must overcome is the one that would allow it to be killed by some dirty trick out of Redmond.  Novell/SuSE has already taken the bait, and they're goners.  Either way, though, Linux will soldier on.
