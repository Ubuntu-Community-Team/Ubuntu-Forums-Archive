### Post by sdegrace on 2008-04-27
I have used Ubuntu exclusively at home for over a year now and have never been tempted to go back to Windows. The thing I like about it is that unlike many Linux distributions, which I feel are aimed at enthusiasts who love computers for themselves and *want* them to be complicated, Ubuntu is aimed at ordinary people who just want to use the damn thing, and it does this extremely well. It is simple and elegant. Even my very computer-non-savvy boyfriend can use it just as easy as Windows, and I would say that this is the first Linux distribution that approaches this ease of use and hence potential to seriously rival Windows.

I would say definitely go with Gutsy, though. I just upgraded to Hardy and I'm not entirely pleased. For example, downloading pictures from digital cameras was "improved" to a state of uselessness, I just discovered. Previous releases had a vary natural and easy interface when you plugged in a digital camera that let you easily download all your pictures to an automatically-named directory, must as the software for Windows that comes with your camera probably would. It was a poster-child for "just works" computing and a prime example of what makes Ubuntu special. The new "improved" version is more complicated yet somehow does not do what you expect or want and has less useful functionality. It's awful, a huge step backwards. There's lots of other nasty little surprises, although some good points as well, Firefox 3 Beta 5 being a huge good point.

The scary thing is that this is a long term support release. I don't like that my desktops effects are no longer supported on my laptop and I really don't like that my digital camera has ceased to "just work". What a disappointment.
