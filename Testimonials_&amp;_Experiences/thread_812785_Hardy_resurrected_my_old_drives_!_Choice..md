### Post by robert shearer on 2008-05-30
I have an odd collection of equipment salvaged from charity shops and landfill recyclers.

Half a dozen computers 4-12 years old and plenty of old graphics cards,cd/r/rw drives,modems etc etc  Just plain old junk.

My expectation is that as o/s's advance their support for older hardware declines. stands to reason eh. Keeps TinyFloppy in business too.

I was about to recycle a couple of cd-rw drives that worked ok in Windows(which I don't use) but not in Feisty or Gutsy.

In fact I have had big problems burning disks with those releases.It is a hardware problem coupled with MoBo chipsets and the scsi emulation.Not an Ubuntu problem as such.

So imagine my surprise to find that these problem drives work with Hardy.
They just work. No fancy configuration needed, they just go!.

And so far that is how Hardy has been for me, everything just works. Even the things that haven't worked before.Even my ATI graphics cards.

To have a new o/s that improves on the old version AND extends the life of even older hardware is a dream come true.

Now I can have a better computing experience and feel good about recycling parts that would have been biffed for lack of support.

I have never bought a new off the shelf computer and with Linux and Ubuntu may never have too.

Getting all this old and disparate hardware working has not been easy but the helpful and abundant forum information,a little patience and the willingness to experiment has paid off.

As the equipment hasn't cost much and the investment is in time rather than dollars I am happy to try any and all advice as the worst that can happen is that I have to reinstall on a box that I don't rely on for everyday use.
Once a fix works I then apply it to the Everyday box and slowly improve my setup and computing experience.

I am still a noob but I am learning and having fun.

Thanks everyone for all your helpful posts and thanks too to the Ubuntu team.
