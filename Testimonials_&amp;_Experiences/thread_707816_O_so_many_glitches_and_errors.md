Then i tried installing cs:s it installed fine. When i tried to run it i kept getting fatal errors, and i had to continuously restart my computer. Its kinda cool i must admit, but i wanted to know how to change it back, because it was kinda bugging me. I closed terminal and reopened it, didnt work, restarted my computer, didnt work. Also when i restarted my computer, kiba-dock erased the bottom quarter of my screen, the only way to get that bottom half back is to close kiba dock. Finally, a previous problem came back, ubuntu keeps switching randomly from emerald themes to gtk2 themes on startup. Sometimes it will boot up in emerald, somethings gtk. If you have solutions to any of these problems please help, im contemplating going back to windows,

### Post by Rucas on 2008-02-27
Im trying to get a mate over from MS to at least try Ubuntu, hes a real MS addict, mostly because, it works for him!!! Seriously, he's happy doing the general antivirus scan, and the lots, but i must admit, he runs XP and ever since he got the machine its been working like new. 
Anyhowz, he finally looked at my Ubuntu on the laptop and was overall impressed with the Graphics and such, but still to try and explain to someone whom knows no more than Win is like trying to win the lottery.
I personally use Ubuntu now on 2 machines, and have a MacBook as well.
I think the thing with Ubuntu and other Linux Distro's is simply that it's not Windows, it takes time to get to that level of confort one had with Win, even if that was minimal.
I cannot say that i have not experienced some annoyances with Ubuntu, but overall the plusses well outweigh the minusess, and usually most annoyances are due to lack of knowledge anyhowz. 
So im with Ubuntu all the way, i love the ideas, the community(makes up for a huge part of the experience!!!) and simply put Linux is a beast of a OS.
What i do think is that there are a lot of people whom are simply not prepared to take the learning curve with Ubuntu and thus go back to XP, but im almost 100% shure that come April when Hardy is out, all the nay sayers will be right back...
