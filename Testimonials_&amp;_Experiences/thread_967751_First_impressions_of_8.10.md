### Post by paul101 on 2008-11-02
ok. the computer restarts. badum. the login screen is ready. nothing new there except the "login ready" sound is more soft, less harsh than previous, and to my surprise the speakers worked first time without twiddling with settings!


i enter my username and password and everything starts up - a wee bit slower before the desktop shows up, nothing much.


once i get in, i begin playing about, seeing whats new. it then told me that it had found a wireless network. which is the one sitting beside me, i entered the pass for the network

> you are now connected to buffalo

a nice touch, eliminates doubt when connecting

i then open up firefox and am confronted with a simple, minimalist page. i then went on-line and got the latest plash player. with the minimum of fuss, the .deb was installed. i then tried it out on youtube. but first thing i noticed was that the scroll thing on the touch pad worked out the box! and so did the scroll buttons, which previously i gave up on trying to get to work

i then connect all my other stuff: my printer/scanner, digital camera, drawing tablet and mouse. it just works; with the minimum of fuss - and that's what's great about Linux
