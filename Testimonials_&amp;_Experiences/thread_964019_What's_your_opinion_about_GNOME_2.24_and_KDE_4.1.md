### Post by LexRoss on 2009-03-25
At home with KDE, I eventually found myself spending way too much time playing around with it, customizing every single piece of it to whatever I wanted my desktop to look like on a given day.

And when I tried Ubuntu for the first time, what a relief it was! It wouldn't matter whether I liked it or found it to be plain ugly, The real beauty of Ubuntu GNOME desktop to me came from the fact that there wasn't much "interesting" stuff to do with it.

To me, it was like coming from my home to the library or office - Ubuntu GNOME desktop forces me to concentrate on my job, otherwise making me boring. And if there is some KDE functionality that is missing in GNOME, I can always adapt to it. The real productivity gain comes from the fact that it is really not much to do with GNOME but the real job. I do not play around with my computer any more.

So I would say that in my experience GNOME is the perfect tool for older and mature people looking for the tools to help them to do their day time job. While KDE is modern, fancy and somewhat revolutionary computer experience suited for younger people who have time and energy to learn and to experiment.

To me, I really appreciate the job done by Ubuntu team to make GNOME menus look clean and tidy. And I do not care if my laptop looks like Mac or Coffee or whatever as long as it does the job. And it performs well with default GNOME install. I use it as my universal communication tool. Well, I continued to use KMail and Korganizer on Ubuntu for about a year but finally made myself comfortable with Evolution for the sake of smaller footprint and better font rendering. So what I do on my laptop is Office apps, e-mail, VPN and wireless networking, checking time and weather conditions in several locations, chat, skype, sending and receiving faxes, watching online videos and playing DVDs, making Ekiga VoIP phone calls. Even for those simple tasks GNOME feels much less integrated than KDE is, but overall I am more productive with it. Just lacking self discipline to be productive on KDE you know. 

Those are my two cents. Other people's needs may vary.
