### Post by GenePayne on 2009-12-08
I have been creating family home movie DVDs for 6 years on windows. I just completed a DVD home movie using 100% free software on Ubuntu. I know this is no breakthrough by any means, but it is exciting to me because I never thought there would be free software options that would be able to deliver the quality video editing that I was used to with Adobe Premiere.

Sure, Adobe Premiere (Elements) has a lot of advanced features that probably no linux video editor can match. But what I had to work with in Ubuntu was more than enough to enable me to produce a DVD with great picture & sound, nice transitions, fancy slideshows, subtitles, chapters, menus, etc...

I used Kino to capture & convert video from a few different sources, and kdenlive to do the main editing. I used Videoporama to create slideshows with kenburns effects (slow panning/zooming). The only thing I couldn't do was add a video stabilization filter (anyone know of one in linux?).

Gnome CD Master will create an iso image file from an existing disc. Gmount-iso will let you mount an image file on your drive as if it's a physical disc.

### Post by Irihapeti on 2009-12-09
About a year ago I made a couple of DVDs completely in Ubuntu.

Some of the raw material came off a cellphone, so I needed to do some conversion using ffmpeg and MP4Box. Other parts of the raw material came from a video camera that uses mini-DVDs. I used Vobcopy (repo version) to get the files onto my hard drive.

I did the actual video editing in OpenMovieEditor (the most recent compiled version, not the one in the Hardy repository) and then used DVDStyler (again a compiled version) to create the ISO file.


I really enjoy reading this of post, where someone describes what they've done. Makes a nice change from generalised "you can/can't do X in Ubuntu."
