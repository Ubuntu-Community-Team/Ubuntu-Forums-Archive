### Post by Yatta99 on 2008-04-24
Last week as I was using my computer I heard a rather loud *SNAP* followed by a blank screen. I tried to reboot but the system wouldn't even POST or give a beep code. It seems that several caps on my NVIDIA FX5200 (256MB) video board blew. I was, and still am, running Ubuntu 7.10.

So I dig out an old spare MX400, and pop it in. I boot up, get a warning message that my card is older than dirt and need to settle for some lesser graphics mode, and spend a week in 800x600 with no SFX while waiting for my new card.

Today I got my 'new' card; an NVIDIA NX6200 (256MB) card. I pulled out the temp, put in the new, and booted up to hi-res full SFX in all it's glory.

AND NOT ONCE DID I HAVE TO INSTALL OR CONFIGURE ANYTHING.

IT. JUST. WORKED.

Thanks Guys 

Tom
