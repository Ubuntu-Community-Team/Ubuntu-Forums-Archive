### Post by Portending Ennui on 2008-06-10
I started tinkering with linux before I took any coursework in it at college, and Immediately liked it.  I played with Damn Small Linux and a USB key to learn some basics, and have ruined several installations tinkering with it. It has become my primary OS. My first main distro was 4.10, and I've tinkered with others, too, but mostly ubuntu, up to 6.10.  Then I tried OpenSUSE 10.3 and I was hooked. It had the best hardware support for my computers, and I used it a long time.  It was easy to configure, clean looking (well, GNOME was), and generally hassle free. And everything worked pretty smoothly. 

Then along came this "Hardy Heron," and once again I was blown away. *It just worked! Everything!* It upstaged SUSE completely, and kicked its *** in some categories, especially with my main SUSE complaint, speed, and also, though I am not an active member, *community*. I'll probably go a month or so giving OpenSUSE 11 a whirl, as thats what I do with different distros, but I am damned glad to be back with ubuntu, because I have always admired the spirit of this distribution.

/end rambling, disjointed post about being happy to use ubuntu. :)
