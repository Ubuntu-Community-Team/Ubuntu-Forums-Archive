### Post by Minyaliel on 2005-12-02
I ordered a free Ubuntu disc last June. I didn't get it before fall sometime, and I'd totally forgotten about the whole thing. I was running Windows XP, and by the time the discs arrived, I was so fed up with re- installing my OS (I'd had to do so at least twice a week the last few months) that I didn't hesitate to run the live cd. It convinced me, and I installed Ubuntu. Of course, a Linux newbie like myself installing such a thing... of course I managed to delete Windows and everything on it. That hurt for a while. But now I don't regret it at all. Especially after finding out how to update to Breezy. When running Windows, I always had trouble getting my WLAN card to work. Guess what? Today, I plugged it in again. It started flawlessly, without even having to install the drivers. I **can't believe it**. What had been a nightmare in Windows XP was a dream in Ubuntu. My relatives have asked me when I'm going to install Windows again. Never, I tell them. And I mean it. No more paying for software, no more 30- days trials, no more high fees at the computer store because I can't get other tech support. I have total control of the look of my desktop, every shortcut, everything on my harddisc. Where Windows is "userfriendly" (ha! Don't make me laugh....), Ubuntu is freedom. It's as simple, and as complex, as that.
