### Post by Pirate Zoro on 2012-10-23
I started my Linux journey nearly two years ago with Ubuntu 10.10, and I loved it to bits. But upon learning of the many wonderful distros out there, I began, as many do, distro-hopping. In my two years I have used all of the major Ubuntu flavors, the major Ubuntu respins (Mint, Pinguy, Zorin), Debian, Fedora, SuSE, and Arch and a few Arch respins. But I would always come back to Ubuntu proper. It was my first distro, so there was always that draw to it, but more so that it was and still is extremely stable, at least for my hardware. But in August, I decided to go back to Arch from Ubuntu 12.04. I used it for a while, and decided I would go to a distro that I had used briefly, but couldn't optimize it 100% at the time, Gentoo. Having learned a lot in the few short months since my first attempt at running Gentoo (which I dropped after, let's say 2 weeks to return to Arch and Ubuntu) I decided to give it another shot. And it is, with no disrespect meant in the slightest towards Ubuntu, the greatest distro I have tried. The stability, the speed, nothing about Gentoo's usability have I been able to replicate on other systems. I believe I have found a keeper.

I have a deep amount of respect and admiration for Ubuntu, it was my first distro, the distro I used to learn about Linux. But I feel that I need to move on into the more advanced, being the tinkerer I am. I will be sticking with Gentoo from now on, at least on my personal computer. If I ever need a work computer, a workhorse that I don't need to mess with and it will just work no matter what, of course I'll be using Ubuntu on that hypothetical machine. But for personal use to continue tinkering, messing around, and generally learning more about Linux while I go about my activities, I'll be using Gentoo.

So, as with the title, I'll leave you with parting words from the late great Douglas Adams, "So long, and thanks for all the fish."
