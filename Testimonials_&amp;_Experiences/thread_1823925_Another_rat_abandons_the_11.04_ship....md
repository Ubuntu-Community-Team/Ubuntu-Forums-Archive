### Post by omelette on 2011-08-12
I thought I could stay the course but I've just reformatted my drive and gone back to Ubuntu 10.04 LTS.  11.04 is by far the biggest Ubuntu disappointment I have had the misfortune to download.  A brief summary of what I would term the "un-endurables";

1. Although my 2 year old hardware is reported as Unity-incompatible at first-boot - maybe most see this - I nevertheless am prompted & install Nvidia GeForce drivers.  However after rebooting, these are listed as "installed but not activated", and I have no hardware acceleration whatsoever - not even the screensaver works!

2. Despite the above, Unity does boot up.  I didn't like it so switched back to Ubuntu Classic.  However I find that if I switch back to Unity again, it no longer works, ie. I'm stuck with Classic.

3. The keyring manager seems to be completely screwed up.  I have reinstalled 11.04 at least 3 times I seem to encounter a different variation each time.  Last time I was being prompted twice for the same 'login' password on each restart.  It was also not storing the network password.  And that option to unlock the keyring automatically seems to be completely useless on 11.04 - it just does not work, either reliably, or as in my case, not at all!  Ubuntu developers seem completely incapable of fixing it either, as it has been an issue for the past few years at least.  The only other OS I had keyring dealings with was Debian Lenny (about 1 year of full-time use) and that was hassle-free, it worked perfectly.

4.  And finally, the thing that has really driven me nuts, there is a bug in the kernel which prevents the computer from sharing its resources over a ad-hoc network.  Or more correctly, it fails to complete the network connection automatically.  I have downloaded 4 different versions, all Natty but the last, from 2.6.36.1 (the first one available) to the brand-spanking new 3.0.1 (oneiric) and each of them suffer from the same problem - this bugs seems to have been missed completely - but only by the Ubuntu developers!  Fedora 15 has the very same bug when first installed (with kernel 2.6.38.11, Natty comes with 2.6.38.8 I think) but has a version 2.6.40 waiting as an update which works perfectly.  And having just installed Ubuntu 10.04 again, I can also state that the 'old' 2.6.32.x kernel also works flawlessly.
