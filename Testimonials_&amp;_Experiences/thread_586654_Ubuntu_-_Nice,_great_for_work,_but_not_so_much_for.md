### Post by arjones85 on 2007-10-22
Well, after a few years I finally am running linux full time on my school laptop... but alas, I still can't use it as a full-time machine for home use, or for multimedia use, and that saddens me.

- The graphics card drivers are still not as flexible as its Windows counterparts are, which are to be expected. Nvidia and ATI have just recently began investing time and effort into these drivers, and I hope in the next few years they will catch up. Along with that problem comes the dual-monitor support issue. I have a TV hooked up to my home computer through an svideo cable, and gutsy just can't seem to figure that out and set it correctly, as neither can the nvidia control panel. Windows didn't have a problem, 3 seconds and it was working well.

- Multimedia support, such as Divx movies, are still far behind its Windows counterpart as well. No matter how much I try, and no matter what graphics card it is on (nvidia or ati), the movies just don't look as clean and polished while playing as it does on Windows.  That is a big thing for me. I am a big divx movie guy, and I need it to play well! Every codec I have used (divx and xvid) and about 5 different multimedia players, and the video still either looks grainy or has sharp edges and lines that appear when fast-moving scenes are playing.

- Font issues. I shouldn't have to set the font not only in the OS, but also nearly every single application I run. I know that is not so much a fault of the Ubuntu developers as it is the application creators considering the nature of how Linux is put together, but it is still irritating none the less. When I set a system font, I want that font to be used everywhere unless I explicitly tell it otherwise, as is the case in Windows.

- Copy Mandriva's network control panel please. I really, really liked the ability to have Mandriva install ndiswrapper for my broadcom card instead of having to hunt down how to do it within the ubuntu forums. That is a very nice feature, add it!

- Hibernate and suspend needs a lot of work. "It just works" in Windows for both of my computers, but doesn't in Ubuntu.


Other than that, Linux in general, and Ubuntu, has come a very, very long way. I look forward to being able to use it full time on my school computer as well as home computer in a few years once it's more polished. Until then, unfortunately it has a home only on my school/work computer.

### Post by kidders on 2007-10-23
Hi there,

I thought I'd add a contrasting experience ...

I've always found Linux's graphics drivers to be tremendously flexible, certainly as far as X is concerned. The degree of direct, low-level control X provides over graphics card & monitor configuration has never failed me, in over a decade of using Linux and Linux-like systems. Although I find the platform comparatively weak in the area of multimedia *authoring*, it's impossible to beat when it comes to more basic tasks, like playing or converting media.

In general hardware terms, my experience of Linux has probably been coloured by my tendency to avoid things that seem somehow unrealistic to me. For instance, the idea that Linux can use software written for a completely unrelated platform to drive networking hardware is "interesting" to me, rather than being something I would actually want to *try*. I've used ndiswrapper on no more than a handful of occasions, and only then on a temporary basis ... easy setup & smooth, quirk-free functionality simply isn't something I would expect to be able to get out of it. I suppose good hardware choices have helped me avoid many of the nightmarish issues posted in Linux support forums.

For me, one of Linux's greatest strengths lies in the fact that arjones85's concept of a "system font" simply doesn't exist. Issues like these are an obvious side-effect of having chosen to use a platform with no universally imposed UI development framework.
