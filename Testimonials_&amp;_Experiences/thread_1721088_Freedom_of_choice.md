### Post by Sylvestra on 2011-04-04
OK, first of all I'd like to say, that I've been using Unity since 10.10 on my Laptop and I love love love the port to compiz in 11.04! It's faster, cleaner and I'm simply amazed by the improvement that happened in such a short period of time.
Nevertheless I was kind of shocked when I found this post on OMG this morning [http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/04/ubuntu-11-10-will-not-ship-with-classic-gnome-desktop/](http://www.omgubuntu.co.uk/2011/04/ubuntu-11-10-will-not-ship-with-classic-gnome-desktop/)
Since Unity came up, I discuss a lot with my husband about usability, intuitive handling of an OS, etc. Whether you like panels or docks, are you using the mouse or rather keyboard shortcuts, and so on and so forth. And we always came to the point that we like Ubuntu and Linux in general so much, because it's highly configurable. Everyone is getting just the OS he needs with little effort :)
But now for Ubuntu there will be just Unity in 11.10., a very good implemetation of a dock based strategy, perfect for people like me. But what alternative is given for people like my parents, who already had a hard time with the transition from one (Redmond) to two panels (Ubuntu 10.04) last year? There's really no need for them to buy Windows 7, they got all the software they need, running out of the box on their computer, but I simply don't know, if they would like to be forced to another transition in 2 years, when Lucid is not supported anymore.
Another example is my husband, who is just faster with the panel-taskbar-menu based approach. If that's working best for him, why not?
I don't want to blame this all on Canonical. I know that Gnome3 was meant to be a step to the future and away from the 20 year old Redmond-like panel-based desktop, which is fine. But now we got the Gnome-Shell (not usable from my point of view) and Unity (=D>), two dock-button-touch based UIs. If you're a panel person, please leave Gnome and try xfce or lxde and then try to use the gnome apps you got used to on those desktops. At least that's how it feels sometimes :(

So in short, the point I want to make is: 
Please, Mark and Canonical, make a reasonable decision for a **smooth transition**! Keep a classical Gnome2 UI until the next LTS and drop it afterwards, so people have the freedom of choice. Maybe you can ease the transition to the future by putting some more lens and zeitgeist stuff into the classic interface.

### Post by 3Miro on 2011-04-04
I would also recommend that you take a look at XFCE. I like Unity, but I am just so much faster with the Rodent panels. The new XFCe has completely rewritten panels with more options then before, in many ways XFCE is the "spiritual" successor of Gnome2.

A note on Xubuntu, 11.04 is scheduled to come with XFCE 4.8, but last time I checked with them, they were running a bit behind on the alpha. Their alpha release essentially installed 10.10 and then gave you the option to upgrade to 11.04. I would wait a bit longer fro Xubuntu to mature before I give it a consideration. You can also consider Mint XFCE, which is now Debian based and even more stable and suitable for low end machines (the default setup is also painfully similar to XP).

I am using XFCE on Gentoo and I know that Arch has it too (although it seems a bit less stable). I am not sure how many other distros have released a stable XFCE 4.8.
