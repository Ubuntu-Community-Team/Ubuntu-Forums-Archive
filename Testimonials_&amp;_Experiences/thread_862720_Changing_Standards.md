### Post by solwic on 2008-07-17
A couple days ago I bought a new 500Gb SATA hard drive.  The plan was to duplicate the setup I had on my 80Gb IDE drive; dual-boot Windows XP and Ubuntu, using Grub to handle the booting.  It had worked before, no problem, so I was confident going into the whole thing.

What I hadn't counted on was the SATA drive; I thought it would be the same as IDE, in that when you set the SATA drive up as the primary master, it acted that way.  With Ubuntu (and Grub, in particular), that is not the case.

To cut this whole thing short, when Ubuntu installed but Grub wouldn't load anything but Windows, I decided to say to hell with Linux and just go back to XP full time.  It had no trouble with the SATA drive, after all, and why keep fighting with Linux when it refuses to work properly?

The problem with that, though, was that my standards had changed.  I felt naked, vulnerable using Windows.  Yes, I know Linux isn't perfect, but after weeks and months of surfing the 'net with impunity (pretty much, anyway), Windows felt like putting aluminum foil over your windows when a hurricane is coming; not only will it not protect you, it'll actually *draw* the lightening to you.

In the end, I missed my Ubuntu too much to go back to Windows.  I got on the forums here (which I had written off for the most part when my posts got no replies and what replies I could get were criticism - i.e. "don't pinch people you're asking for help") and was totally surprised that people were willing to help in the small hours of the morning!  

I guess my points are this:

1.  I had written off the community based on the immaturity of the few, rather than the desire of the many to be helpful and understanding.

2.  I missed Linux, even with it's problems!  I have gotten so used to things here that I can't stand to run Windows for anything but games.  I even uninstalled everything but the games I play and Firefox (I left AV, Firewall, and Spyware Protection, too).  

3.  Windows has just as many problems (if not more) than Linux.  True, Linux might be a little tough to install and set up (impossible for some, effortless for others), but Windows isn't perfect.  I dealt with a video driver crash and three freezes just installing the updates from Microsoft.  

That last one got to me, because I remember reading a post by somebody here that said Windows has just as many problems as Linux, but since a) we'd all paid for Windows and b) we're all used to it, nobody complains about it, they just look up the fix or download the drivers/programs/scanners they need to keep the machine running.  And that's exactly what I was doing; downloading all kinds of updates and new drivers and malware scanners when instead I could be searching for the fix to my Grub problem (which was retardedly easy to fix, really).  

In the end, I'm writing this from Ubuntu Hardy Heron.  I love Ubuntu, I love Linux, and I even love Microsoft, if only for the fact that it rests upon 40Gb of my shiny new 500Gb SATA drive, reminding me to be fair to Linux when things don't work right away (they don't work right away in Windows, either) and to rely on, and support, the community here (because Windows doesn't have one at all).  

I've read that the developers don't read these forums, but if they do, thanks for the hard work.  And thanks especially to the people here that help without whining or sniveling or complaining when a newbie gets frustrated.  You guys make this community, and your efforts are heard more clearly than the sarcasm and jealousy of the ones who ridicule and belittle new users.

:D
