### Post by 73ckn797 on 2008-09-01
It has been almost 1 month since I first loaded Ubuntu. There is a learning curve but not that steep for the average user, IMHO.

Me, I like to tinker and look under the surface to see what is going on. That has caused multiple issues but that is how I figure out what happened and am able to fix things.

In my brief time with Ubuntu I have found that it meets all of my basic computer needs. I am self-employed and use a laptop on the job. I have been able to perform all of my tasks with the out of the box installation. 

I have added several tools to smooth my usage. One was installing Thunar File Manager since it can rename multiple files very simply and create archives to store the photos that I take on each job. All of this from within the file manager. No need to switch over to another app to complete the tasks.

I have a 2nd HD on my desktop system and have been loading Studio and other variants of Ubuntu. Studio is a great system on my computer with the nVidia graphics card. I cannot say so much for Studio on my son's computer running an ATI card. That has been addressed in other forums.

All in all my experience has been good. There have been some frustrations but not enough to make me load Windows again. The more time I spend using Ubuntu the more I learn. I believe that Ubuntu will remain my primary system. I will figure out what I will need as I continue looking into the features and apps at my disposal.

### Post by calzakk on 2008-09-01
I too am new to both Ubuntu and Linux, I'm a Windows user who decided to make the switch a couple of months ago.

But while 73ckn797 seems happy, I must admit to being a little disappointed with my Ubuntu experience so far.

Ubuntu is a good OS... when it works! But I've encountered a number of problems that have *really* irked me...

Printers: It took me literally hours to get my HP LaserJet 1000 working. A lot of help from the 'net, in particular the Ubuntu forums, got me there in the end. Then, recently, I bought a cheap HP Photosmart C3180, and can I get it working? No chance! Cannot print, cannot scan, cannot even detect it. Whatever I try, nothing works. Fine in Windows, works faultlessly, supposedly works in Linux... but nothing.

Sound: Took a while to sort out, no sound in Firefox or Unreal Tournament 2004 were big problems for me. Sometimes the sound still goes and I need to reboot (there's probably an easier way but I'm not quite there yet!). Tonight there's no sound at all in UT2004! I've rebooted a few times but still no sound. But it'll probably be ok in a few days' time, as past experience has shown!

Games: Linux is not a gaming platform, and IMHO it won't be for a *long* time. Ok, some games work - I discovered OpenTTD :) - and I've had some success with Wine, but it's just not straightforward. And the performance sucks, badly. Half-Life 2 gives me about 25% the performance of running on Windows!

On the plus side, I do like many things. Synaptic and the software repositories. Security, no anti-virus. Performance, faster than Windows. Stability, *generally* better than Windows. Software development is much easier than I thought. (BTW, I can't recommend Code::Blocks enough. It's great, far better than Anjuta!)

Ok, so my problems are mostly hardware-oriented. But I had high hopes for Ubuntu and so far I'm not entirely happy :(

I was hoping to ditch Windows for good, but it looks like I'll be dual-booting for some time...
