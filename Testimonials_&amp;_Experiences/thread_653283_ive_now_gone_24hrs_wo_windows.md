### Post by uwishurockedthishxc on 2007-12-29
well being a hardcore gamer i love my awesome computer but I was getting sick of microsofts crap so for christmas i built myself a respectable little linux box.  x2 4000, biostar tforce 520 mobo, 7300gt, 1 gig of ram.  I just got the box up and running yesterday.  the first thing that struck me was how much better the ive cd works than that bullcrap stuff m$ puts you through to install.  being a gamer im upgrading my other box alot so i reinstall alot.  then when i went through the install it amazed me how fast it was.  once i was booting off my hard drive what struck me most was the speed.  this box pales in comparison to my c2d@3ghz, 2 gigs of ram, hd3870 gamer rig but it was moving as fast if not faster than that box in does in windows xp.  i must say that that impressed me quite a bit.  
now the next thing was how it had all the updates in one place ready to install.  that was great.  the other thing was all of the included software, man i cant even get a decent word processor with windows, here i get it all.  i think the thing that impressed me most was that almost everything worked out of the box.  my internet was running, my sound was working and my graphics driver was right on the userbar waiting for me.  that has to be the most impressive thing.  there was no going to my other computer and downloading drivers to a flash drive just to get the internet running.  this was out of the box working.  thats not to say its the same for everyone since i researched compatible parts and made sure all of it was but thats still mighty imprssive.  Thats about where i quit last night.  
then today i found the thing i love most.  the repositories.  finding and adding programs is easy.  not much else to say other than i love that system. 
 it was simple to get et:quake wars running using the tutorial reccomended on these forums also which was a big plus for a gamer and im gonna try to get tf2 and cs:s working in wine soon.  there were a couple bad things though.  i had refresh rate problems but they were solved with some fidgeting.  the other problem is that i couldn't and still cant get my second monitor working right.  if anyone can give some help with that, that would be appreciated.  im also having problems getting codecs??other than that im mighty impressed.  so far my experience is great and if i ever lose interest in gaming im going linux all the time.  but as it is i had a few issues so i give it a 9/10. 
 ill update with later impressions after i use it more.  but i gotta say i cant imagine it will be negative.:guitar:

edit**
i successfully got all my codecs installed and compiz fusion along with it to boot.  plus i went alittle over board on emulating the mac style.  i got a dock, the mac backround, i made my top bar transparent and deleted the bottom bar entirely.  and i used an emerald mac theme.  it looks pretty nice.  but what im most impressed with is the cube.  i love it. i just sit and spin it sometimes for the fun of it lol  its really useful having virtual desktops.  its made the absence of my second monitor much easier to swallow.  other than that i can't say that ive done very much.  aMSn sucks in case you didnt know that.  at this point i would say this comp looks 10x better than vista and much more functional as well.  quite an accomplishment.
