### Post by DIZZAY on 2012-03-07
Has ubuntu helped anyone else in college? It's helped me a ton and I love it. I've been running 10.10 for a while now and I must say it has kept me organized and hasn't cost me a penny (which is a good thing in college). I have to frequently take notes and I'd never heard of tomboy notes before ubuntu. I used it and it helps me stay so organized it's crazy. It does crash sometimes but that's a problem I can easily fix. All I gotta do is type in a term and then BAM!, all my notes are linked together that have the same content. Also, I take Elementary Japanese and I downloaded this free app called Kana Test; it helped me memorize all my Hiragana in like a day or 2 before the test. Before I used the app, I only knew the 1st five letters. I also run rosetta stone in wine (I can't get the mic to work for anything in the world though).I haven't really used the Libre office suite because I don't have to write any papers or do presentations but I still have them handy (it beats paying for Microsoft Office). Not to mention, since everything works out of the box, it's a bonus. If I ever need a page printed, it works without having to install massive drivers.:D. I just wish there were more college math apps available to help with solving trig and calc problems.I've used inkscape and Gimp to help do designs. I designed a few obituaries for family and did a few pictures for a friend of mine who is in a fraternity. I didn't think it was all that great because the pictures he gave me to work with were all LQ from facebook but everyone loved it. I've even gotten a few clients lined up from using these apps and a possible summer job at a print shop. If all goes well, I may look into making tshirt designs to make some cash on the side and buy a printing machine to have a side hustle (and make a profit $$). I really want to get together with a few friends and test Ubuntu's animation software and make a cartoon. I had a 2 laptops die on me and neither was running linux. I also have a few friends who were too lazy to learn how to do video editing and they will pay me just to edit some of their clips and add music and subtitles to it (which is free with openshot and kdenlive). The only thing I had to pay for this semester besides books was a $100 replacement laptop battery (I had no clue they were so high). 

So if you are in college, learn how to make Ubuntu work for you like I'm doing because you can stay productive with it and bring in money without even having a job. If you know how to do things like cut your own/other peoples' hair, do your own maintenance on your car/bike or whatever, graphic design, etc you could really be bringing in the the dough and barely spend a penny.
