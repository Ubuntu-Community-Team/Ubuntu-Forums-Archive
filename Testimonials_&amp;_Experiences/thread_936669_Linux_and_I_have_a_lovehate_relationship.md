### Post by MyNameIsDerek85 on 2008-10-03
You know that relationship that most users have with their Microsoft operating system? That 90% hate, 10% love? Well, that's not the relationship that I have with Linux. You know that relationship Mac lovers have with their computer? That 90% want to rape their computer, 10%... I don't even want to know what they do with the other 10%. Well, that's still not the relationship that I have with Ubuntu. 

As a former Microsoft user with a deep, naughty love for technology (why do I keep making all these sexual references when I'm talking about computers? It's kinda scary isn't it?), I considered myself a power user. I liked to take advantage of all aspects of my machine (and by taking advantage of my machine, I mean "taking advantage of my"... okay, I'll stop). I love to be able to customize every aspect of my computer and I have a desire to build a PC from the ground up. Most of all, I like to be able to have complete control over my operating system.

Then, one day while planning my custom built PC, I started wishing there was another operating system besides Macintosh and Windows that I could choose from (a guy like me loves choice) and since the only way to get the Leopard OSX is to buy a Mac, that narrowed my choice down even more. Most of my friends knew that I was gaining a deep hatred for Microsoft products. So much that everything about my computer supported an Apple logo or some other kind of anti-windows logo. That's when I decided to look into Linux a little more and came across Ubuntu. I was surprised how much Linux had grown since the last time I looked into it and I decided to download the Live CD for my laptop. 

To make a long story short, Linux isn't as complicated as I thought it would be. Honestly, the only thing that I have had troubles with so far was getting my wireless internet up and running, which was pretty simple after I went to the Ubuntu Forums as a last resort. Then, everything sort of clicked into place with LIMITED command lines. I did have a few minor problems with my audio (turned out it was only muted) and getting flash to work on Opera (easy fix, I just had to make sure that it was being installed to the Opera browser and not Firefox). Other then that, everything just worked. I thought the codecs would be a pain in the anal region, but whenever I needed a codec, Ubuntu lead me to the right place. Which makes me laugh since I still can't even play DVD's on Windows since I downgraded to XP (they require you to BUY the codec to play DVD's, which is something that should just work if you ask me). 

None the less, I don't think Ubuntu gets enough credit for the things that you CAN do out of the box. The add/remove programs feature is revolutionary. I never have to worry about finding the software that I want (or buying it!) because it's already right there and ready to be installed. and there are litterally TONS of things that you can do out of the box that most people don't even know about, because they haven't taken the time to browse through the operating system. 

As far as my "love/hate" relationship with Linux Ubuntu? Well, there's still a few things that I think Windows does better (like games!) that I think Linux needs to catch up on. However, truth-be-told, I've used Windows a total of two times since I installed Ubuntu on my system and that one time was to get my wireless internet going (which I wouldn't have had to do if I had access to a direct internet connection).
