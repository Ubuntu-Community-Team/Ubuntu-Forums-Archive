### Post by b_k_chu on 2010-11-13
this may seem like a little bit of a rant.  but i just want to let off some frustration.

i've used intrepid ibex for at least 2 years now, and although my experience with it has often been less than happy, at least it *worked*.  and at least it kept me free from viruses, since nobody makes viruses for linux (well....generally speaking).

though in the past few months i've gotten the sense that my intrepid was getting old and i've had a comment or two about "well, why don'tcha just upgrade?" and ubuntu/canonical even pulled the repositories a few months back so that i could no longer get any of the xorg-devel packages at all, which i was looking for a few days ago so i could compile a newer mplayer from source.  google didn't know where to find any xorg-devel packages either for my version.

so i thought, "ok, i'll just choke it an attempt an upgrade.  intrepid to jaunty.  let's do this."

it barfed cheetos.

so i thought, "meh, no sweat, i have a karmic livecd here that i can pop in and do a fresh install....once i backup all my files."  and there was a whoooole other adventure behind that.  but anyway, i got my backup done and then did my fresh karmic install.

it barfed doritos.

it crashed within 60 seconds of the first-time bootup.  and now it won't boot up at all....just hangs at the shiny new startup screen.  which looks dadgum spiffy by the way, but i'd kinda like to do more with my OS besides look at it.

i mean, come on, seriously?  a fresh install doesn't work?  granted, i saw the message plain as day during its bootup sequence.....something about unable to find the UUID and something wrong with the /etc/fstab file, and i generally know what that means....something in the device configuration is flippin' hosed.  and with google by my side (and perhaps a few nice people from the ubuntu community) i could probably figure out what's wrong and fix it.  but i'm thinking....why should i?  it's a fresh install, and i chose the option to blow away my partition and let karmic do whatever it wanted to the hard drive.....and it can't get the /etc/fstab right?  come on.

i'm thinking i'm just about done with ubuntu here.  like i said, intrepid was not always that much of a joy for me.  and now this.  meanwhile, Puppy Linux, which i'm currently using off a livecd to get by, is pulling through quite nicely.  it doesn't have much, but it *works.*

anyway, if you read all this, thanks for listening.
