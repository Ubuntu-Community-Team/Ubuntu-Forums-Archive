### Post by cricketshadowolf on 2007-05-08
Howdy there from a transplanted Okie. I am in Eastern Tennessee nowadays. Its good to see I am not the only older person who remembers the old mainframes. I remember as a kid my stepdad asking me a question about how to connect multiple computers in various locations and have them communicate to each other. He worked for HUD and was involved in the political arena. I guess its the old "ask a kid" if you want answers about new technology or need ideas that no adult would think of. I said why not use the telephone lines. They were just developing the PC. He said how would we do that. I said well the computer uses binary code doesn't it and the old morse code was a binary type system and so why not send pulses or tones over the phone lines. He said "The phone lines?" Yeah, everybody has one. " Well, not everybody!" I said anybody who could afford one of those new PC's would have a phone line Dad! Duh!!! He asked how that could happen I said the hardware is not my problem don't you have people who can figure that part out. He said he guessed he could go tell his bosses what I had said and see if it worked. I don't know if that conversation ended up being what started this whole shebang of the internet but it is possible. I do know that shortly thereafter computers were talking to each other over the phone lines. I just got my hands on a keyboard in 2000. My own PC in 2002. My first computer I built from scratch and I installed Debian on it by the trial and error method and then a laptop with a whole whopping 8M of Ram on it. I found Ubuntu a little over 2 years ago. It is great. Welcome and I hope you have many hours of happy computing. Can't program yet but I am working on it. It just goes to show You can teach an old dog new tricks! LOL! Glad I'm not the only "Old dog" here.

Have fun!!! cricketshadowolf
