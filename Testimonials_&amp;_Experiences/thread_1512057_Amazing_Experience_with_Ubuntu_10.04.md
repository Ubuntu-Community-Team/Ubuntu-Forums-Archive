### Post by theosam on 2010-06-17
I used Windows XP and Vista for about 12 years with an okay experience. I installed a fresh copy of Ubuntu 10.04 few weeks ago and had nothing, but good things to say.

1. Every piece of hardware I have worked smoothly including my DVD drive, webcam, game controller, and microphone.

2. I don't have to take a lot of time to do complicated google searches to find the program I want. I found every piece of software in the package manager and the software center.  

3. I discovered tons of useful software that saved me a lot of money like SciLab and KdenLive. I can't wait to put SciLab in use next semester.

4. My Windows XP system took about 15 minutes to boot and initiate system programs. Ubuntu only took me 3-4 minutes.

5. All of my favorite Windows program works like a charm under WINE. I am a huge game emulator fan and all of the emulator programs (including Project64) worked without any problems. 

6. I love the multiple desktops and compiz. I put my work stuff on desktop and my fun stuff on another. The animations, cube, and wobbly windows was pretty exciting.

7. As a student (major Math minor CS), programming was less of a hassle. Building and compiling large libraries for my programs were tenfold easier.

8. I connect my computer to a window's network at school. Oddly enough, connecting and transferring file was much faster on Ubuntu than Windows.

9. I cannot stand Window's default styles (especially on XP) and the very few options they provided. Outside skin changer programs crashed way too often. Ubuntu is very pretty to look at and I can try thousands of different skins without any crashes.

10. Searching files amazes me. Within thousands of files on my computer, it was able to locate the file I was looking for in a matter of a few seconds.

11. My battery life increased for 2 hours 30 minutes to 3 hours.

I don't see myself using windows again.


A big thank you and a round of applause to the developers.=D>
