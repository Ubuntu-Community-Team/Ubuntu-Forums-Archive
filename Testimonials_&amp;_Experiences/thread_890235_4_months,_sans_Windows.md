### Post by Kotjze on 2008-08-14
*Warning: Somewhat long post! :)*

It's almost been 4 months since Hardy's release, and I've seen other people make threads like this so I wanted to do one :p

I've been on and off with Ubuntu since Edgy was in beta, and I've always tried each release when it came out. My laptop's hardware was pretty annoying back then, but with help from my IRC friends, and these forums, I eventually got the wireless and graphics card to work. However, after a week or two I started missing Windows.

Anyway, Hardy has changed that for good ;) Even though I only have experience with my current hardware and none else, I think Hardy has the best hardware support yet. The first time I booted Hardy, there was this Hardware Drivers thing in the menu to install my graphics and wireless, and it actually worked!

Blah blah blah... Skip to my next point... :p I pretty much installed Ubuntu because I was bored with Windows. 4 months later (now), I'm getting bored of Ubuntu and have thought about installing Windows again. But why? The stuff I'd miss on Ubuntu outweighs the stuff I miss on Windows. I love the programs, and even though they could run on Windows, it's not the same. I love how the fonts are rendered (lol). I love how tasks that required Googling for programs on Windows just require a simple command in Terminal, like converting audio formats for example. And I love apt-get; no more visiting websites upon websites, download all the .exes, run the setups.

I also think the linux equivalents to Windows programs are much better. For WLM I use emesene (it's so awesome that, if I went back to Windows, I would use their Windows port of emesene :P). I can't find a decent audio player on Windows, but Banshee on linux is the best audio player I've used ever. I somewhat miss mIRC, but I like XChat. I also prefer GIMP over Photoshop now.

One of my favourite things that I would miss when going back to Windows is Compiz Fusion. I only use the small stuff, like the scale plugin, because I liked the exposé thing on a mac. And the animations for opening/closing/minimizing etc... :p

There's too much else to talk about, and I'm pretty sure nobody would bother to read it all. You guys are just happy to know that another person actually thinks Ubuntu is worth using, and that I'm not yelling "THIS SUCKS, LOL" haha.

Woot, Ubuntu! :D
