### Post by Don1500 on 2008-02-06
About two months ago I decided to try Ubuntu. Never used linux before and didn't know what I was really doing. I just knew that Windows was P*ssing me off more and more. 
I loaded up and started trying to get it to work. Came here often with questions and rants ("Why did they do this that way?", "That's not the way you do it in Windows." (Duh!)) Had problems with my audio, video, learning how to get around in the terminal, the file system. Learned I shouldn't boot to root, but do it anyway. 

I also know windows was getting pretty boring. I grew up in DOS, learned Fortran on a key punch machine where the output was printed.....on paper! Was getting pretty good a basic. Then POW out came windows and everything I knew was no longer relevant. Now I had to learn VB if I wanted to program, just didn't have the time. I'm not an IT guy, I am mostly hardware. My first computer was a Varian 100 with 1K of Iron core memory and you booted it up by hand (65 commands in 16 character hex).

This has been fun but most of all (and the reason for this rant) I want to thank everyone here for helping me the conversion to Ubuntu. I have had it up and running correctly for about a month and it is about as stable as windows. I haven't has a crash for over a month and I am ready to leave "Absolute Beginner Talk". I will be back to read the comments and help, if my little knowledge can help. Again thanks to all the guys and dolls here that helped get me to use linux and Ubuntu.
