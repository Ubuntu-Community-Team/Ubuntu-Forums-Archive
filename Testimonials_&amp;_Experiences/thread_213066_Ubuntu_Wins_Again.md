### Post by Third Thoughts on 2006-07-10
Every time I try out another Linux distro I always end up coming back to Ubuntu within a couple days. Why? Because Ubuntu just works, basically right out of the box. Now let me be clear, I don't shy away from tinkering. I broke my iPod trying to install Linux on it. I've wiped various parts of my harddrive (accidentally, and on purpose) on more than one occasion. I'm currently running E17 from the CVS. I like digging around in my system. But whenever I've tried other distros, they required so much digging it was impossible to get anything done. Vector Linux's package manager seemed to start off with a broken setup up, Fedora Core 4 was impossible to get multimedia support for, and Elive managed to wipe out my entire /home partition (not that it was entirely Elive's fault, a combination of poor instructions/warnings, and my own stupidity). I'm not trying to distro bash here, I guess what I'm getting at is that I'm really impressed with the Ubuntu devs. All the other distros out there are evidence to the fact that its not easy to make an operating system, but you guys have really done it.

~Andrew S.
