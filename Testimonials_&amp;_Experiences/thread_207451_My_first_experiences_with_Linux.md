### Post by Miki01 on 2006-07-01
Well, yes, Ubuntu is my first exposure to Linux. I was originally going to download and burn .ISOs for Slackware or Gentoo, but then I read about a linux thread from another forum about Ubuntu being so awesome and that they even give you free installation CDs. Well heck, as far as I know, just as long as its free, its good. But I was skeptical on it. I received my discs and plugged in my LiveCD into my old HP desktop. Wow! Linux has such better program management than Microsoft Windows. 

From then and there, I was sold. I knew that I probably wouldn't be able to play some games that I used to in Microsoft, so I just partitioned my HDD 50/50 for Ubuntu and Windows. Haven't installed Windows yet, so I'm on Ubuntu. Well, like some, I was confused, that it wouldn't play some of the Mp3s that I had. I read that I could get over that later so I kept it as-is and just played around. I am a total n00b at Linux so I will be on these forums a lot. Yes, to me, Ubuntu is still quite complicated, I'm still fazzled by some things. But practise and experience make perfect. So the longer I am using Ubuntu, the more I'll progress into it. But what I truly love about Ubuntu is that no one else I know uses it! Ahahah, so that makes Linux users unique. They know how to tango with the system! Unlike the users on Windows, they just point and click. We use Terminal! Ahehe.

Well, I'm not really sure that was all on-topic, but, its got my opinion somewhere in there, I hope! :P

Ubuntu FTW
