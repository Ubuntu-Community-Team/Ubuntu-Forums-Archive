### Post by Jh00 on 2007-05-14
RobotAlligator, I can understand you perfectly, but although tgm4883's answer sounded a little rude to me, he has a point too.

I've been following Linux progress for almost 10 years, hoping that one day I would make the switch. However, I will only make it when I know that I will feel totally comfortable with it, without ever needing windows again. Unfortunatelly, that's not yet the case. Like you, having a dual-boot windows machine isn't the best solution for me.

I have Ubuntu installed on another HD and from time to time, I switch HD's just to play with it. The problem is: my games don't work like they should under Linux, and lots of applications that I need (as a lawyer) don't work either (even under wine). Besides, I don't feel that Ubuntu is polished enough (although I'm very excited about this distro). When I be able to setup my system without EVER touching the command line, I will be very satisfied.

So, to answer your question: Ubuntu (and probably, Linux as a whole) isn't yet ready to fulfil your needs, just erase it. If, however, you (like me) are eager to follow Linux progress, just keep a look at it from time to time... I've been very pleased to see how far Linux went after ignoring it for just 2 years.

I'm certain that in no time, Linux will be able to run things that I want/need, so I want to be ready. 

Cheers.
