### Post by madjr on 2008-06-14
i used to be a **mediocre and conformist** person just a year ago.


i didn't finish college

i was living in my parents basement

i didn't know how to advance in life or why i should advance.

i had a vague idea of how the world worked.

i wanted things without working for it.

i was just another windows/computer geek, playing games and with low self esteem :( 




But.... Thanks to everyone in the Linux community and the Ubuntu forums **something changed inside me**.

I've seen how everyone struggles **All Over The World** to fight for Open source and **Linux adoption**.

I've learn that in order to archive that, **Linux needs to get better** in every aspect of people's needs or else windows will stay on top forever.

Linux is getting ready and so should I. It's a mirror image of my life.

The Linux community has learned to fight back if we want to stop being an "unsupported" **niche**.

**FOSS** is competing now head to head with **Billion dollar companies**.

I've now learn that i can improve and become a better person if **i work hard**. I can be a competitive person if i propose myself.

Thanks everyone for letting me be part of this community. I appreciate it and learn something every day.

so, am i the only one who feels like this?

### Post by oleksus on 2008-06-15
Hear hear!

I've long felt like this!

Linux is complex and alive, and it allows you to study its inner workings, and learn how to use them. 

Using Linux is a journey of sorts. It makes you a different person.

Widows is like some guys built you an unfinished house and left, leaving none of their tools, saying that '*not only that, but you also are not entitled to enter certain rooms, for security reasons. Why? Because you're not supposed to know what goes on in there, okay?*'.

While on the other hand, Linux is like a house which you can refurbish or build to your own taste and liking, because all the tools have been provided.

It's just the matter of you own zeal to Learn. I think Linux will never be for the lazy-minded. Even with all the visual tools and eye-candy (which I find WAY more top-notch and varied than ANY version of widows) Linux will always challenge people's ability to learn, investigate. Challenge their brainpower and curiosity. You will be able to use it if you're lazy, but the question will inevitably  be lurking in the back of your mind: "I wonder if I can learn more..."

I guess, yes, tinkering with *nix systems has taught me a lot about how the world works. It's a lot like we're using nature.
When you're just a dumb consumer, you know only how to eat and never bother to learn how this or that works (and that's what software industry and globalism are trying to make people like: **dumb consumers**, clickers of 'buy' buttons, unwrappers of glossy packages).

But if we dare to take that fog of consumerism oof our clouded minds, and become co-creators of the world instead, our attitude changes. We feel invited to experience our world from a totally different perspective: the one of creation. THEN and only then, do we truly become Human.

Linux is totally on par with other systems in terms of accessibility. But only **Linux invites you to become a co-creator of itself**. It tnink it's the main feature granting it the way into our future.

The world is changing, and we can see that. Chasing profits will no longer be the task. The Nature will simply not allow that anymore.
Learning and Co-Creating will be our privilege instead.
