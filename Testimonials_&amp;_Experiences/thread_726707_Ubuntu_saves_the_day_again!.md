### Post by Anduu on 2008-03-17
First off don't shoot me...I still keep Windows around for certain things :p

One of those things is the nifty one touch DVD burning software for my HDD camcorder.All I have to do is push a button on the dock and it will burn 30 gigabytes of movies spanning as many DVDs as needed.No fuss.No muss....About a month ago,however,things got ugly.

The software started failing at the DVD finalization stage with no errors of any kind.It would just say "Finalizing disk failed".I burned many coasters trying different options etc to try and fix it.I Googled for hours on end to no avail and finally gave up.

Here is where Ubuntu comes in.

I had finally resigned myself to the fact that I was going to have to burn 300+ home movies/clips to DVD by hand.I figured I would start off by going through all the movies on the camera to try and trim the fat a bit and save whatever time I could off of the hours long task I had in front of me.

That's when I saw it.In the Nautilus window before me were the thumbnails of all my movies.As I started scanning them I noticed 3 or 4 mpegs with no thumbnail.I tried to play them to see what they were but they would not play.The thumbnailed mpegs played fine.Then the lightbulb came on :idea: I deleted those files(they were all less than 1 megabyte...) and couldn't boot into Windows fast enough.I hit the one touch DVD burn button and held my breath(well tried to anyways...20 miutes is a long time....:p )

As you can probably guess it worked!I am assuming those 3 or 4 mpegs were corrupted and messed everything up.If it weren't for Ubuntu,even though I discovered it completely by accident,I'd still be completely clueless as to why things were so screwy.

Anyways that's my story.Ubuntu has yet to let me down :)

Now if only we could get some automagical DVD burning software for Linux I could probably kill Windows forever!
