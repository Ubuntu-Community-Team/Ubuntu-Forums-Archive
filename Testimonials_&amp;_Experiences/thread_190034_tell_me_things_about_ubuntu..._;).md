### Post by 23meg on 2006-06-05
If you're happy with Fedora right now, why exactly do you want to go back to Ubuntu? Don't get me wrong, I'm not saying you aren't welcome to do so, but..

Fedora Core 5 is a very competent distro and I don't find anything that's so important as to be universally *better* on either side. For what I know I don't think ATI support is particularly bad with Ubuntu, but you may have been an unlucky odd, it's perfectly possible.

I endorse healthy competition between free distros, on a development basis, but I have yet to encounter a reason that justifies distro evangelism. If Fedora works for you, why not stay with it?

### Post by jdong on 2006-06-06
I choose Ubuntu over Fedora because more hardware works out of the box -- in fact, the most "extra effort" I've had to put in post-install is editing xorg.conf to use an accelerated binary driver (ATI/NVidia). My systems use atheros and ipw3945 chipset wifi cards, so with Fedora I'd be compiling kernel modules on every kernel update and such.

Speaking of updates, don't you feel that there are too many with Fedora at times? I sure did... 25 updates a week is simply not acceptable as a "stable" workstation, not to mention that some of the bugs that they've fixed via these updates are ... well... *concerning* to say the least!

Also, Ubuntu's package collection (universe/multiverse) is phenomenal, and Fedora even with the popular 3rd party repositories does not compare to the 18,000 packages you can install in Ubuntu. I rarely find myself reaching out of the Ubuntu package collection anymore.


But credit does go to Fedora -- it's a wonderful distribution. I have a copy just to explore with RedHat's most recent technology. Its end user experience is almost as smooth as Ubuntu, except in the areas I just mentioned. Whether or not that affects your decision is your call :)
