### Post by lmkwak on 2008-09-05
Hi everyone,

I wasn't sure if I should post this here or in the 'Absolute Beginner Talk forum', but since I don't need help (yet), here it is.

When I first heard of Linux it seemed to be more for programmer types than for me. However, I have recently become extremely impressed by open source software and the vast amounts of help available for "established" programmes!

After much surfing and reading I decided to give it a try. My original idea was to install Ubuntu on my Fujitsu LifeBook u1010. I bought this beautiful little machine in on a vacation in China, but only received the Chinese Windows for it, not much help to me as a non-Chinese... But after searching information on how the u1010 works with Ubuntu (not yet completely...) I held off on that idea.

In the meantime, my desktop was becoming more and more frustrating, viruses and Trojans were having a great time at my expense, and I could never quite manage to make them disappear. So two days ago, I transferred all the files I wanted to keep from my c-drive to my external hard drive, unplugged two other hard drives from the machine, and installed Ubuntu (wiping out Windows!)

I was ready to go hunting for solutions for problems that were bound to pop up. Much to my surprise, there weren't any! Everything just worked! I have now played around with Ubuntu for a few hours, installing and uninstalling programmes, clicking on things, and copy-pasting commands from forum answers into the console to see what would happen, and am I just about ready to see if my new OS can read the files on my other (still unplugged) hard drives. 

Hopefully with as much luck as I've had up to now! If not, I know where to ask for help :-) Thanks to all of you providing it, and even though I haven't actually asked for help yet, for making me feel secure enough to give Ubuntu a try!

Laura
