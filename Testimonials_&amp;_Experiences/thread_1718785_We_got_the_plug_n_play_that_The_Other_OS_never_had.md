### Post by varelov on 2011-03-31
I've been exploring Ubuntu 10.10 on my newly revived Dell desktop and it knocked my socks off in terms of hardware recognition!
I am not new to Linux and have been playing with other distros before, having to install drivers for slew of USB devices that we all get to attach to our PC boxes. Some worked, some didn't and in all cases it involved a decent amount of research and forum postings to make it work.
MS Windows' Plug N Play still involved installing drivers for USB devices. Yes, you'll plug it in and it will play, but only after you install the driver. Where's the Play part in "Plug N Play" then?
I attached my USB printer the other day on my Ubuntu box and was up and printing in less time than it takes to say Meercat! It was instantly recognized, configured and ready to print...
Same for my USB speakers. Instantly recognized and all it took from my part was choosing those speakers as audio out. Never was this easy on any other distro, neither on the Redmond OS!
Awesome job Ubuntu team! My hat is off to you!
