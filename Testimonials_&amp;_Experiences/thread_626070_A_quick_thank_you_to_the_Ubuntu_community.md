### Post by laptoplinux on 2007-11-28
I just want to post this as a big thank you to everyone involved in these forums and the community represented by them.  Every now and then I have to deal with other distros, and I keep forgetting that not all forums are like Ubuntu's.  

I think I'm probably already dangerously close to being banned or permanently ignored in another high profile distro's forum simply for repeatedly pointing out the last 4 releases' inability to work with a common and years-old piece of hardware (despite several years' worth of promises) and asking for help and/or any word on when support will actually be added.  (A necessary chore because workarounds for one release are usually broken on the next release or kernel upgrade.)  On that note I'd like to tip my hat to Ubuntu specifically for both excellent hardware support and a friendly community willing to help whenever a rough spot appears.

My own experience with Ubuntu is that, after a short initial rough patch that was soon cleared up by updates and a few trips to the forums, I've not had any real problems.  And whenever I come here, I usually get timely, helpful, and friendly advice on nearly any topic.  Even a post as obscure as getting Maple and Matlab to work correctly under Linux received an answer within an hour of posting.  So thank you all again!

Keep doing what you are doing!  The more I deal with the rest of the Linux world, the more I see that the level of professionalism, polish, and dedication both to quality and community found in Ubuntu is rare even in the bazaar.
