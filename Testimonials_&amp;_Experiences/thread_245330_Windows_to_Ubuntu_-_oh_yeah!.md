### Post by JReagan1990 on 2006-08-27
I started working with computers in shcool, mac os 7 (then 8), to be exact.  Then, in 1998, I was first introduced to windows 98, and my life changed.  First I had to learn the new operating system, but the system seemed stable and remided me somewhat of mac os 7.  I worked with windows 98 all the way until the release of windows xp.  At first, I thought it was cool, then, I started to discover the microsoft "lost touch" with the user, and there were many security holes.  I worked with the mess i like to call, Xtremely Pathetic for a long time up until 2006.  My first experience with linux was in school, and the computer instructor was using it without a desktop environment - how oldschool is this!?, I thought to myself...

I the heard of this great thing called open source software, which I originally got into with palm os handhelp apps.  Then I tried open office, and several other apps like firefox, that worked great, a most importantly to me at that time was that it was FREE!  This amazed me greatly, that good apps could be free.:D   

One day in early 2006, I was having a little bit of trouble with my pc's.  My dad was very upset that I had installed certain apps on his computer.  He said that everything was working fine until I installed all this "crap" on his computer.  He also claimed that firefox was'nt more secure and was asking him to pay for it.  This was, after some research, a hoax, and firefox was fine.  Within a matter of a few minutes, he deleted all of the open source software from his computer. This fueled my search for an alternative... something better.  Something that actually reguards its customers better than just a number, and wouldn't delay its newest operating system by three years.](*,) 

I had thought about getting a mac, only they were (and still are!) too expensive. I thought I had lost any chance at getting something other than windows, until one day a friend of mine told me about ubuntu linux linux, and how it had a great looking desktop, and worked great.  I first saw ubuntu on his computer, and was surprised at what I saw.  I thought, wow... this is linux? A desktop cleaner than a mac, an definately better than windows, was one of the best I had ever seen.  

I then got a copy of dapper, and finally got the guts to install it on my laptop, after having it on my desktop computer for over six months.  It was beautiful, but I could not get my wi-fi working.  I tried to reinstall windows, only to fail time after time.  With a quick look at my settings, i saw my mistake, and I had wireless internet streaming to my computer.  All my printers, external sd card drives, floppy drives - everything worked with linux, and my printer never even worked with windows!  

Now I have made the complete switch to ubuntu linux, and have been informing everyone I know about open source software - and ubuntu - linu for human beings!

I cannot put into words the appreciation of the ubuntu, canonical, all others involved and mucho thanks to Mark Shuttleworth for coming up with the idea in the first place!:KS
