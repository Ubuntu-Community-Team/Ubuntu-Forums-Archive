### Post by optique on 2008-05-08
Just wanted to recap my experience installing 8.04 on two machines.

1. A few weeks ago I installed on a 3 year old AMD desktop, with onboard sound and network, cheap video card. All was detected and ran fine. No config required to get anything to run. 

2. Just now, I installed it on my HP Pavilion DV2315nr laptop with Nvidia 6150 graphics. Internal wireless, video, etc was detected and has run fine. Even the notorious broadcom wireless was detected and installed. Sound works and battery status seems to work but I have not tested other laptop specific features yet.

All in all, I am very happy so far.

### Post by lorsban on 2012-09-06
Hey there!

I know this is an old thread but since I'm using the same hardware. I thought I'd chime in on my experience with this model as well. 

Unlike the original poster, I had a couple of hiccups with the installation. It wasn't a full install but a dual boot using Ubuntu's windows installer. The installation itself was extremely easy! Got the GUI up and running in no time and connected via LAN immediately. 

First issue was when an "install additional proprietary drivers" notification popped up, telling me I had a Broadcom driver and 2 nvidia drivers. I installed the Broadcom driver but there was no internet, no connection. I also installed the 2 nvidia drivers, but I lost Compiz.

After uninstalling the nvidia drivers, everything went back to normal. 

Bigger problem was the Broadcom. After surfing for a couple of hours and trying a lot of stuff (including that messy ndiswrapper thing) to no avail, I chanced upon this thread on this site:[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1763621&highlight=hp+pavilion+wifi](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1763621&highlight=hp+pavilion+wifi)

We have the same Broadcom version 4311 so everything that came up on the OP's terminal came up in mine. Solution on the second page worked as well! Was so shocked and happy to finally see that blue LED light up in front! And it detected my wifi! haha! Awesome

Anyway, I'll be sure to add more as we go along. This is my wife's laptop and I'm trying to get her to migrate to Ubuntu for good. Only thing now is to get her ipod sync'ed with it (hopefully with banshee). 

regards,

lorsban
