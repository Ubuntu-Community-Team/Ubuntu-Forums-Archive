### Post by wazupwiop on 2012-05-04
I am very disappointed in the latest release of Ubuntu 12.04 64-bit.  The changes in Unity along with the problems with printing in 64 bit are extremely disappointing.  I have used Ubuntu for 5 years now, and I remember 8.04 being a great OS with amazing ease of use.  I was looking forward to 12.04, but after using it, I have to say that Ubuntu has lost its touch.  I tried everything in the book to get my laptop to print with my Brother printer.  It worked fine in 11.04 and 11.10.  The lack of usability is extremely frustrating, especially when I depend on basic functions, such as printing, to complete school work on a daily basis.

I am afraid that Ubuntu has been growing away from what I like in a linux distro since 10.04.  I have tried to learn the new desktop interface changes, but they are highly glitchy, especially the Unity bar in particular.  Over the last 5 years, I have used Ubuntu Studio, Ubuntu, Xubuntu, Linux Mint, CentOS, and Puppy Linux.  I almost feel like puppy linux, one of the most basic OS's, is more usable than Ubuntu at this point in time.  Even downloading Oracle Java is a royal pain now.  The repositories should be entirely redone, and the OS should be made more user friendly.

Ubuntu has been my main OS for 5 years.  I have tried other OS's for various reasons, and I feel like Ubuntu, if it continues to be developed in this way, is fighting a losing battle.  In my opinion, I feel like CentOS makes a better server than Ubuntu server.  It is lighter, easier-to-use, and tends to perform better.  I feel like Ubuntu in general should be re-evaluated to decide the future of the OS.

That said, I am a huge fan of the individual applications that are used in Ubuntu.  LibreOffice and the default programs that are included in Ubuntu are great.  I think the applications are the strong point, but the OS as a whole is really lacking at this point in time.

That said, I am moving to Fedora 16.  I know redhat distros have their issues, but I think that the RedHat OS's are better at this point in time than Ubuntu.  I will use whatever distro fits my needs the best, and Ubuntu has failed in that respect.
