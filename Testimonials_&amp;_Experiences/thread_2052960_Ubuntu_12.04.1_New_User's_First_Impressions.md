### Post by stevem531 on 2012-09-04
Hello to everyone. I downloaded the .iso image just over a week ago, my original intention having been to try Ubuntu from the CD, mainly out of curiousity. In fact I liked it very much, and am now in the process of gradually migrating as much as possible of my Windows supported hardware and software to Ubuntu.
 
Ubuntu runs very well on my most recent (2 year old) desktop PC. In addition, experience of a live CD session on my 5 year old laptop suggests that its useful could be extended by switching to Ubuntu.
 
I expect that in a few cases, there will be problems during migration that cannot easily be resolved, but there have been some pleasant surprises, in particular ALSA driver support for my sound card (DMX 6Fire USB), and the availabilty of drivers specific to my Canon printer.
 
As far as the installation was concerned, I chose to have my desktop PC dual-boot Ubuntu with Windows, but from a separate internal hard drive. Advice on the forums and elsewhere suggested that this would be the safest approach, but it does require modest expenditure on the additional hardware.
 
For most of the technical questions so far, answers seem to have been readily available by a search online, but it is good to know that help will be at hand should anything unusual crop up.
 
So, thank you to everyone who has contributed so far and keep up the good work !
 
Stephen
