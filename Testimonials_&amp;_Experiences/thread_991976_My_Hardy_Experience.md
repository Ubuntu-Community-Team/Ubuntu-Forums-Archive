### Post by DrDevice on 2008-11-24
Greetings Ubuntu community!

This is my first post here, and I thought I'd share my thoughts and comments on Hardy Heron, which I've been using for about a month and a half on a dual-boot with XP 32-bit SP2.

I chose Hardy 64-bit as at the time I installed it was the most recent version and an LTS one as well, which I like because I'm pretty much a noob at Linux.  I wanted 64-bit for encoding purposes (more on that later).  Previous Linux experience has been Slackware, great for learning the ins and outs but to be honest I like the simplicity that Ubuntu provides, and the Debian base it runs off of without being as intimidating as Debian seems to be (at least to me).

And for the most part, I'm in love with it.  Slick interface, easy install of Hardy as well as whatever program I need using apt-get (I'm a terminal whore :)), and the forums here are a veritable cornucopia of help and information.  And the kicker is that my file-sharing with the other computers of the household works better in Hardy than it does in XP.  XP gives the other computers major problems in copying or streaming videos, Hardy just works perfect.   Freaky but true. LOL.

However, there are some areas that I find XP does do better, although I can't for the life of me figure out why.

1)Firefox
I use Firefox in both XP & Hardy, and both work great.  XP however, seems to have the edge when it comes to scaling web pages.  I read a lot of web comics, which I like to enlarge for easier reading.  XP scales those images beautifully, with some sort of anti-aliasing or something that displays the images without massive 'jaggies' on the lines in the comics.  Hardy does not.  Same versions (3.0.4), same plugins.  Anybody know why?

2)Video
Here again, I use the same program in both OS's: VLC.  It's been my "goto" video player in XP for years; simple, easy, self-contained.  However, in Hardy, the videos tend to be choppier, and slightly more pixelated, than in XP.  I've tried different players and video output settings to varying degrees of success, but none match the default VLC settings in XP.  I feel this may be in the differences in the codecs they use, is this correct?

3)DVD burning
Now this one's weird, but it might just be that I need to find a better burning app.  I use DeVeDe to make DVDs of lectures and conferences I record for my job, and that works perfect.  Output to .iso, open up Brasero and burn, and the DVD is often out of sync when I play it.  I take that same .iso, and use Nero in XP to burn it, and voila, perfect.  Again, I think I need to find a better burning app, but the weird part is the first DVD I made using Brasero...was perfect.  Just none of them after that.  :-s

4)Gaming
Developers need to put out Linux versions.  'Nuff said. 

To wrap up, I love Hardy.  The only real use I have for XP right now is for my DVD burning and games, and I'm working on paring that down to just games ASAP.  Other than the minor quibbles mentioned above it's a beautiful experience, one that I'm trying to get my company and my friends to try.

P.S.
Discovered cool new thing that makes Ubuntu better:  Amarok control using the Windows key on my keyboard.  I'm actually using the Windows key.  *Never* used it in Windows, kept interrupting what I was doing with that annoying menu when I accidentally hit it, so I disabled it!  Woot! ^_^
