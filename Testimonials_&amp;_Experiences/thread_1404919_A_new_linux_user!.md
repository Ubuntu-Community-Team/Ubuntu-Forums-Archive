### Post by snakeman21 on 2010-02-12
I've converted a few people since I was introduced to Linux, but this worked out so well I simply had to share.  

I went to visit an old friend a few days ago, and while I was there, he was having troubles with his computer.  He was running Vista, and had 1.5 GB of RAM... Not a good combo!  Anyways, he was complaining about how slow his computer was, and how often it crashed and froze, and how nothing would work the way he wanted, etc., etc.  At one point, he told me:  "I'd just buy a Mac if I could afford it!"  

This is when I decided to show him Linux.  I started off with the usual question:  Have you ever heard of Linux?  He'd heard of it, but he'd also heard that it was complicated, and required extensive use of a CLI.  I told him that while that may be partly true for certain distros, it was certainly not the norm.  I got my laptop from my car (I always have it with me;  I know, I'm a nerd.) and booted it up.  

First, a little info on my laptop.  I am an absolute eye-candy addict.  I love it.  I have the cube desktop with a different background on each face, no panels, Cairo-Dock with OpenGL, every compiz animation imaginable, transparent menus... The list goes on and on.  But I use my laptop to demonstrate Ubuntu, so I also keep a separate "bare bones" account on it to show what the system looks like before any customising.  

Anyways, I logged into the bare bones user account to show him the basics.  I told him I could easily install it on his computer, if he was willing.  He played around with it for a little bit, and told me that he was planning on getting a new computer soon anyways, so what the hell?  Why not?  

I keep a live USB on my keychain with the latest version of Ubuntu (I know, another symptom of being a nerd!) so I was ready to install.  I booted his computer to the USB drive, and accessed his hard drive to do a data backup to his external HD.  While the backup was in progress, I opened Firefox and pulled up some reading material about Ubuntu and Linux in general for him to look at while we waited.  He read the entire time, and said he was feeling good about trying Ubuntu. 

When the data backup was finished, we removed the external HD and began the install process.  After he entered his info, I logged into my personal account on my laptop to show him the full potential of Linux.  I showed him all of my animations and modifications.  He was shocked that it was the same system.  I explained that **everything**, right on down to the smallest detail, could be changed and customised to suit your preference.  I showed him gnome-look.org, kde-look.org, and the plethora of information that is ubuntuforums.com.  The more he saw and read, the more excited he became.  I explained the concept of open-source software, how it works, and why it's generally more stable.  

The installation finished, and I left him to play with it.  I went to bed (I was staying the night--it was a long drive to his house).  It was about 10:30pm.  I got up around 8:00am to find him on his computer, drinking coffee.  I asked when he got up, and he answered:  "I never went to bed!"  He had been up all night, drinking coffee and playing with his new system.  He couldn't wait to show it off.  I was amazed--it was cooler than mine!  In one night, he had gone from a crappy, freezing, slow  Vista system to a sweet, tricked-out, fast and efficient Ubuntu Desktop!  He said he'd spent a lot of time on youtube looking at other people's desktops, and googling how to get the programs to duplicate them.  He had deleted the bottom panel entirely, and replaced it with Cairo-dock.  He had set up compiz with all of its coolest features, and had installed emerald and had a sweet theme for it.  He'd downloaded new icon sets, login screens, themes, and cursors.  He'd totally tricked out Firefox with a ton of add-ons, and overall he was very proud of how everything looked.  But his real appreciation was in the performance.  He was shocked at how fast and efficient it was, and loved how Ubuntu just did what he told it to without a fuss.  The speed was what surprised him the most, as he never thought his computer was capable of it.  I explained that 1.5GB really wasn't all that bad, but Windows tends to gobble your resources like a stoner gobbles potato chips.  

I was just so happy that I was able to help him so much, and he was really stoked about having a system that works--He told me it was like getting a new computer!  He's not buying a new computer anymore, as he's satisfied with the one he has, now that it works properly.  I'm sure he'll be setting up an account here on Ubuntuforums pretty soon, too!

I have tried both Windows and Linux in day to day demanding activities and there is no real comparison, really.  After a year of heavy use, installs and uninstalls a plenty, Windows wears out and its performance is heavily compromised.  Linux behaves almost like the first day.

To share a real example, many sales representatives in my company need to be able to start their machines quickly before they visit a customer.  Due to the painfully slow boot times (only partially to blame on Windows, I must admit, as we have all kinds of nonsense privacy software and policies on the corporate build), they simply never shutdown their machines.  They are always on, then go to standby mode.  After a month of doing that, performance once again becomes an issue, and most of the times users are enforce to reboot in order to go back to acceptable performance levels.  

I also love the fact that Linux does not force me into the "latest-hardware-and-strongest-resources-required" race.  Come on, even Windows 7 requirements are ridiculous.  A bare installation (almost no applications available) is recommended to have a minimum of 8GB of disk space (WTF?), which by the way is an improvement over Vista.  Now, try and install the available Service Packs (no choice if you want a minimum of security) for the OS, MS Office, Internet Explorer, Outlook, etc.  You will end up taking more than 16GB of space before you know it, and that's before you start installing heavy applications!!

Just a quick comparison.  WinXP SP3 weights 1,5GB+.  All linux kernel upgrades are within the 70-80MB range, and you can easily remove older versions if you so desire!

In my case, I need to have the ability to boot Linux from many different machines without changing anything to their setup.  I installed Ubuntu Karmic on an 8GB USB drive, which still left 3.6GB of free space available.  I have booted from that USB drive on at least 7 different PCs, always flawlessly.  You obviously cannot even dream of doing that with Windows.

The list goes on and on, and I haven't even started to talk about security, multitasking and multiuser architecture, and the kind of things that REALLY make a difference...  

Ultimately, it really is down to what's important for you, and whether Linux can cover it.  Having said so, under comparable scenarios, I am convinced there is no contest: Linux is leaps and bounds better.
