### Post by compres on 2006-09-08
I love Ubuntu and I will take GNU/Linux over windows any time of the day.  However a couple of things are keeping me from going away from linux:

1) Hardware compatibility:  This is not the comunity's fault and I know I should not be buying propietary stuff, however every once in a while one needs a piece of equipment that is GNU/Linux incompatible.

2) Games:  Sad but true.  Developers always code with windows in mind.  OpenGL has gotten less and less support over the years in favor of directX.  This is one of those thing that really pisses me off.

3) (This one is very personal LOL) I can't seem to find a better bittorrent client than uTorrent wich currently is windows only.  There is always some piece of software that is windows incompatible.  And emulation does not work all the time and when it does it's not entirelly perfect.

4) I am getting really choppy performance on my laptop, and as much as I hate to say it, it's close to 3 times as fast under windows.  I understand it might not have much to do with the OS, but I could not resolve this.  And it is not the processes since I disabled all the non critical ones and booted with about 50MiB of used ram(it has 256MiB).

All in all I prefer Ubuntu since most of the things I do in school are better done under Gnu/Linux, however I can't just let go of the crap Windows as much as I hate it.  Damn monopoly, man...](*,) :confused: :evil:
