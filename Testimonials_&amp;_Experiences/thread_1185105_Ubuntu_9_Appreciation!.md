### Post by Ben Logan on 2009-06-11
Forgive me for the fanboy post, but I'm so happy with Ubuntu 9, I'm inspired to communicate some praise.  I can't believe a product this polished, useful, easy to ease, and FUN is FREE!  :D

I've been stuck on a Windows machine at work for the past 10 years, because the grading and attendance programs we use (I'm a high school teacher) were proprietary.  However, next year we're moving to all ONLINE record keeping (via webrowser).  Enter Ubuntu!   

I just installed Jaunty Jackalope (Ubuntu 9) at work.  I've been away from Linux since Dapper Drake (Ubuntu 6).  I could not believe my eyes!  What a beautiful user experience.  What a transformation!  Such speed and elegance!  

I know this is "old hat" for you longtime users, but: 

* I love the way the windows fade, zoom, etc. 
* I like the Mac-Style icon zoom after you click on an icon in the top bar
* I love the Mac OS-9 style candy-striping 
* I'm amazed at the speed of workflow on a relatively "old" Pentium 4 2.6 GHZ machine with 512 mb of ram
* I'm astounded by Open Office's ability to open ALL of the documents I've created via Microsoft Office over the last ten years
* I'm thrilled that there is a Linux analog to each of the Microsoft programs I wrestled with / labored to use over the past 10 years: the Gimp, etc.

THANK YOU so much to all you developers.  What a positive movement Ubuntu is.  I can't wait to put some Ubuntu posters up in my room to inspire my high school kids' curiosity.  The product truly speaks for itself.  Can't wait to get some low-income kids excited about Linux.  

Looking forward to learning more about filing bug-reports, etc.  Anything I can do to help as an "end-user."  Just ordered the Ubuntu Book from my local library.  

Woot!  
Ben

I later realized that the windows drivers had not been updated since 2005 and were not fully compatible with the XP SP3 update. Meanwhile, the PCI card was running flawlessly in Ubuntu. ### Post by mdsmedia on 2009-06-12
As an Ubuntu user of nearly 4 years, I'm rapt to see the enthusiasm of new users. I'm equally disappointed at the negative comments.

I'm one of the "lucky" ones who's had few problems, in 4 years, with sound, WiFi, printers, graphics.

Linux aint perfect, but neither is Windows. Consider the fact that Linux developers have to work against the tide to make your hardware work. Consider the fact that hardware manufacturers make their hardware work with Windows and don't consider Linux, except, possibly, as an afterthought. Consider the fact that, for all its imperfections and those of the software you'll find in the repositories, you're free to try them, use them, install them, uninstall them, distribute them to friends, to your heart's content.
