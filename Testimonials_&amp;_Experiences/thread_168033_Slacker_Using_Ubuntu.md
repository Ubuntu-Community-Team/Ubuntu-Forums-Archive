### Post by MasonM on 2006-04-29
Ok, I'll get this part out of the way first. Slackware has always been my favorite distro. My home PC runs on Slack. Now that's out of the way :rolleyes: 

I'm a truck driver and I carry a laptop around with me when I'm on the road, which is about 3 weeks out of each month. I've used various Linux distros since 1995. I recently purchased a new laptop and decided to install Ubuntu on it. I wanted a stable distro that was quick and easy to set up and maintain for my road machine.

I help a lot of drivers fix problems on their machines, almost always Windows related stuff and I have passed out a lot of Ubuntu CDs and helped a number of fellow truckers set it up on their machines and give them a quick lesson on how to use and maintain it.

Ubuntu gets the job done for me and I have been quite happy with it. I can get online from any WiFi hotspot I come across (most truckstops have WiFi now), I can watch my DVD collection with no troubles and everything on the laptop works including the the function keys and hibernation (very impressive there, I could never get hibernation working on Slack).

Once Ubuntu comes out with a good 64 bit mix release that incorporates the 32 bit stuff that's needed for a complete system, I think I'll be installing Ubunbtu on my home box as well.

It looks to me like Ubuntu is heading in the right direction as far as Desktop Linux is concerned and while I still get an occassional pang for Slack (I guess I miss all the configuring and tweaking :shock: ), I'll be sticking with Ubuntu.

I'm looking forward to the next release (soon I understand). I don't install testing releases because I need my box working when I'm on the road.

Good job Ubuntu! Keep up the great work.
