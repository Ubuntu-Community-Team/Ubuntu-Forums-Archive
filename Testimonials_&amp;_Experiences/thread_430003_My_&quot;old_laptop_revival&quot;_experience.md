### Post by raul_ on 2007-05-01
My dad told me he bought a new Vaio (one of the little ones), and before that, he bought a Toshiba for my sisters. So I asked him "Hey Dad, what happened to that old Toshiba you had?". He told me "I don't know...but it's still here...do you want it? What do you want that for?". Well, i spared him the details and told him "Don't worry, i'll make something out of it".

My dad lives in Angola, and I'm studying in Portugal, so it took about a month before the laptop came to my hands. It seems geeky, but I spent hours looking for pictures in the internet, since I didn't quite remember how the laptop was. Turns out it was an 8 year old Toshiba Satellite. 333Mhz and 64MB of RAM. Exciting huh? One more detail: No ethernet. 

Finally I got the laptop. Not quite as I expected, since it survived my younger sisters. And the battery was dead. Nevertheless, it was time for installation.

From the start I wanted to install Ubuntu server, and then build my system on top of it. So that's what I did. I installed Feisty Server, and all went well. So I plugged my usb modem in and....nothing. Google told me that usb modems in Linux were a no-go. So i had to wait for Monday to get a brand new PCMCIA card. No store had it. I had to look in the Internet and I found a little IT shop, in the middle of nowhere, that sold them. At least it worked.

I installed E17, but for my disappoitment, it struggled with 64MB of RAM. I tried Fluxbox too, but if I tried to install anything, the pc just crawled. So I invested in a brand new battery and 64 MB module. I won't mention numbers, but let's say that they were worthy of a museum laptop. 

Moving along, things were nicer now. I tried to install Fluxbuntu, but it wasn't quite what I was looking for, so back to the E17/Fluxbox install. It turns out that E17 still crawled, and my compiled Fluxbox had some trouble with the fonts (they were pretty crappy). But I remembered that Fluxbox from the repos had no trouble with fonts. Time for another format =)

This time I got it. Feisty Server + Fluxbox, and  everything is running smooth. My quest for a Web Browser started. Kazehakase is my favorite, but the Feisty version has some issues (that have nothing to do with the application, it's just Feisty). I couldn't build Skipstone, and Dillo is awful. So...I don't know why...I tried Opera, even though is not free. And I'm loving it! It's extremely fast and renders all the pages correctly. 

Conclusion, I'm just proud of this baby. To all of you who have old computers, don't throw them away. Right now I'm using this one for my college works (mainly programming). It's really handy.  I just need to hit the jackpot with the D-Link DWL-G122 usb wireless adapter drivers, to use internet in my college.

Thank you for reading this to the end =)
