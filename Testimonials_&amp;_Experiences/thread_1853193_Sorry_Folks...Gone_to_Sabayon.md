### Post by 8_Bit on 2011-10-02
I tried Sabayon and it is a good distro in theory. I love the idea behind it (the power and freedom of Gentoo, but ease of use of binary packages)

What made me trash it was the dreadfully slow downloads from the package repository. Seriously, it was dreadful. 4 KB to 20 KB per second, per package, no matter what mirror I tried. (The few mirrors that worked, anyway. Most gave "not found" errors)!

It's such a shame because this isn't even a problem with the functionality of the distro itself, it's just a problem with hosting. They seem to have trouble getting substantial funding and donations for it. I wish more people supported it so package downloads were faster, as fast as Ubuntu. (Really, going back to Ubuntu made me appreciate so much more what Canonical has done, the download speeds are terrific here. Commercial backing really makes a difference.)
