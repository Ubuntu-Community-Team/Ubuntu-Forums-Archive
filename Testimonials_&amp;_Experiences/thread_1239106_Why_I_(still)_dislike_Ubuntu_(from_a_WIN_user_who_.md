### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-08-13
Most people quit Linux because their hardware isn't compatible, or they realise that they need to play Windows games... it sounds like you're quitting for very tiny reasons.

Any theme can be installed by dragging it to the Appearance window when you have the Theme tab open. You might need to click the Customise button and choose the window theme and the controls theme.

If you choose them and they look really archaic and chunky, then you don't have the correct theme engine installed. Check the documentation for the theme, which is usually at the place you downloaded it from. Ubuntu has many theme engines available from the repositories. Unless you are comfortable with compiling software, try and stay away from ones that require "Murrine SVN" or "Aurora", as they aren't available for download from the repositories.

I don't have any problems with Flash in full screen, so I can't comment on that. Flash Player is made by Adobe anyway, not by the Linux community, so you need to get on Adobe's back about it, not on ours.

And I have no idea how anyone could think it's difficult or unintuitive to install proprietary support for RAR archives - do a search in Synaptic Package Manager for "rar" and the "unrar" package will come up. If you want to un-rar a file, then you need to install unrar. Easy.

You only have to provide your password to unlock your keyring when you have automatic login enabled. You shouldn't have automatic login enabled on a laptop. I hardly think that having to provide a password to use your computer is really a "Linux desktop blocker" because all other operating systems default to showing a login screen anyway.

I think the main problem is that you've realised that you're a newbie in this world, rather than the expert user you were in the Windows world. You'd rather be one of the bigger fishes than one of the smaller fishes.
