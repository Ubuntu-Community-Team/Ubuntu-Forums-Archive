### Post by mdsmedia on 2009-01-15
April 2005 I got my new laptop (courtesy of a HP warranty which had about a month left after 3 years). Within 6 months XP was running like a dog.

I'd installed Red Hat in 1998/9 from the APC Mag pocketbook previously mentioned, and managed to get dial-up internet access, but it was just too foreign, and I'm no IT professional.

So I remained interested in Linux, subscribed to the local LUG mailing lists, and when my magazine cover DVD came with Ubuntu Hoary (5.04) I burned and booted up the LiveCD and played with it for a few hours. I remember mentioning to a friend on IRC how it was quicker than XP (yep the LiveCD), and I actually liked the fresh look (you know the "ugly" brown). So I found some instructions on dual-booting and installed Hoary. It became my main OS right from the start and the rest is history.

Everything on my laptop just worked, as I recall. The only thing I remember having to fix was switching off the tap to click on the touchpad.
