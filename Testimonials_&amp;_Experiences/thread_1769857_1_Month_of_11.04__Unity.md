### Post by Frogs Hair on 2011-05-28
I will start by stating that when it was announced that Unity was going to be the default desktop in 11.04 I was very negative about that decision.
I remained positive overall  and decided not to spend the next few months building up a dislike for an application I had not used.

Installation day arrived and as with 10.10 , Natty installed without any problems . I'm happy to report no freezes , crashes , or (buggy ?) behavior of any kind . As usual for my computer, flash is working fine both browsers .  

With the the help of Web upd8 and OMG Ubuntu I was able to get my cube running and add Launcher short cuts that improve usability . Having used a dock for a year I found little difference except the location of the panel. 

I just wanted to report that this Ubuntu user is not having any problem with 11.04 / Unity.

### Post by candtalan on 2011-05-31
I quite like Unity, and after a little effort to get to know it, I keep coming back to it (from 10.04.2 LTS) and I think I will be using it a lot more in future.

I do have a problem though. I really like to sit/lean back away from the display and keyboard and very much want to be able to do most things from mouse action. The one thing which after some time I seriously miss is a mini thumb view of workspaces with similar facility as in classic etc. This is unexpected for me because I had not been aware of how much I used the mini thumb view of workspaces to visually prompt what I do. It turns out to be central to my use.

I have used CCSM to rearrange the 2 x 2 workspaces to the inline 6 x my own mind can cope with, and I have arranged a couple of adjacent keys (shift bar) to show the workspaces, which is sometimes faster to use than trekking my cursor across 400mm of (wide) screen to the switcher icon in the launcher. The workspace switcher in the launcher seems to be non movable, and gets squashed hidden in my full launcher. My problem is that I use multi workspaces so much in recent years that anything other than the (original) type of mini thumb view of workspaces - on display real estate constantly - will probably become the reason why I end up not using Unity long term, however much I like it.

1) Do you think there is any chance that somebody would or could create a mini thumb view of workspaces, maybe floating, in future?

2) is there a useful place for me to go to help steer a future app, or (unity) feature? 

tia

### Post by cbowman57 on 2011-06-02
> **candtalan said:**
> Looks pretty good from the workspace switcher point of view, yes!!
(my taste in skulls is not quite what it used to be though....) :-)

how to, pretty please?

It's just gnome shell with a few added extensions, I find a lot of information on [fpmurphy's blog]("http://blog.fpmurphy.com/") & his [extensions repository]("http://www.fpmurphy.com/gnome-shell-extensions/").  The key one that give a menu showing places & applications, displays favorite apps in the panel & workspace switcher is a bit of an all in one extension called eureka (don't know what the differences are but I use v2.1). The weather extension is one I found on the net & modified the location to prevent a location conflict with a couple other extensions.  The running application indicator that moves a couple things from the message notification tray to a more traditional gnome panel looking location & functionality I call the Application@Indicator.  Murphy also just came up with an extension that adds an additional activities hotspot to the upper right corner.

The link in my signature leads you to a tread with a download link to what I call the gNatty Pack, that contains the weather & app indicator, as well as a few other extensions that cooperate nicely on ubuntu.  As someone already posted, you can add xfce4-panel to it if you want a nice panel or dock that you can position where you'd like, or AWN, cairo-dock, just about any DE agnostic panel works.

For my personal preference it's like old gnome on steroids, it's fanatastic.  I also use Synapse, which rounds it out nicely.

As for the wallpaper, I go through a variety of them, obviously you saw through the window dressing though, that's a good thing.  Down here on the Texas coast we get in a bit of a piratey mood from time to time.  Argg!  :)
