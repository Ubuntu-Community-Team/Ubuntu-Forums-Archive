### Post by Inygknok on 2011-03-25
I Couldn't find an introduction section for the life of me, so I thought I'd just post here instead.

I've been using Ubuntu 10.10 for about 24 or more hours now.  My only experience with Linux was many years ago while in high school (early 2000's).  I knew my way around with a PC but wasn't too interested in diving into Linux back then as it wasn't that user friendly yet, so the experience was very short lived.

I don't know what triggered the sudden wish to try it out again, but I was browsing makeuseof.com and ran into a page mentioning the latest Ubuntu releases and how it was supposed to be very user friendly.  I gave it a shot.

The first install went without a single hitch (aside from my girlfriend's laptop crashing once Ubuntu started downloading updates).  Problem was, I couldn't figure out how to get my integrated video card to output sound over HDMI, which was a similar problem with WIn7.  I searched and searched and searched.  Quite a few hours later, after TRYING to learn how to install programs and properly use the Terminal, I accidentally ran across the volume control and dived into it.  Well what do ya know, Ubuntu installed the latest Alsa drivers all on its own and I just had to enable the sounds.  My gf was complaining (jokingly) about how the OS is stupid while I stared at her and told her, "Win7 didn't do that for me.  I had to personally call ASUS and get them to talk to me about the Catalyst thing after a week."

Thing is, I was ill that night and really couldn't figure much of anything else out and I needed to burn a disc at 5am, so I had to go ahead and re-install Win7 cuz I had made the mistake of completely deleting it.

I got WIn7, remembered what a total hassle it was to get the HDMI sound to work again, and said, "Screw it, let's give Ubuntu another shot now that I'm rested."  And so I did, but with a dual boot.  Second time I installed Ubuntu, the internet wouldn't automatically work (it did the first time).  Deleted it, had to reinstall Win7 AGAIN, and then reinstalled Ubuntu for a third time.  Everything was fine.  Since that moment, I've only used Win7 ONCE to make sure it would boot up fine (it did).

I'm an industrial engineer graduate, with some minor experience years ago doing some TCL (eggdrops) and some website work using Dreamweaver and a tiny bit of Flash, but all that was for the heck of it.  My real hobbies have always been doing extensive work on cars and sports, not computers.  I have to say that once I got the hang of Ubuntu (which took just a few hours of reading the Wiki guide), I seriously like it a bit more than Win7.  There's a few things I like more in Win7, but many that I prefer here.  FIrst and foremost, is the Ubuntu community that I've been checking for a while.  Second is how much easier it is to actually get things in Linux (install-wise) with the Synaptic thing.  It really is a nice experience in itself.  Not as entertaining as finishing an automotive project, but amusing in its own way.  I'm keeping Win7 because I'm not planning on running a virtual desktop for games  :p, but that's the only reason.

Thanks to everyone that has made this OS as user-friendly as it can get and for those that have given it its deserved support.  Hopefully, I'll be able to sort out the very minor jinks I still have (which I also had in Win7, so we're even).

Have a nice day!
