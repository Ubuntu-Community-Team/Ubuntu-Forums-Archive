This refers to what the user is allowed to do with the system. Ubuntu is both "free as in freedom" and "free as in cheap", however, companies like Mandriva, Redhat, Novel and IBM make good money out of Linux.

If you want to learn more about "free as in freedom" you can Google around, but here is a quick example:

If you are buying a car, would you ever consider getting a car from a dealership that tells you not to look at the engine? Windows and MacOS are closed source, which means that they don't tell you how they work and any attempt to reverse engineer (i.e. figure out the inside mechanics) is punishable by law. In Ubuntu and FOSS in general everything is 100% transparent, you can see everything and change anything that you don't like.
