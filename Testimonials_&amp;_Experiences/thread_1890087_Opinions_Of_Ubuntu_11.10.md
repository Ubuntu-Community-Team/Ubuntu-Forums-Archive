### Post by KirkS on 2011-12-02
I did a search, and could find no thread that dealt with this.  I have loved Ubuntu, as if delivered me from the crushing trap that Windows had me in.  I have Ubuntu 10.10 on my main laptop, and it is absolutely fantastic.

That said, I have to say a few words about Ubuntu 11.10.  I loaded this platform on my Dell Latitude laptop, and it did load up all right.  After spending the next two days looking for the correct xorg.config file to correct the double screen problem, it was starting to looks pretty good.  That's where the positives end, in my opinion.  I really like the look, and setup of Ubuntu 10.10, and under.  I really hate the same attributes of 11.10.  I feel lost half the time, and now I'm running into one issue after another with this platform.

One of the first problems was usage of my NetGear network card.  I had to finally accept the fact that I couldn't use it with Ubuntu 11.10.  Very frustrating.  I broke down and bought a supposedly proven Ubuntu friendly USB netword card.  Now the fun really begins.  This card won't work with 11.10 either.  I find this unacceptable, as I really need to have that working.

I have tried techniques of all kinds to install kernals, and files that will enable this platform to use either of the cards I tried.  Nothing works.

I've had enough.  This is the kind of struggle I was attempting to escape from in Windows.  I tried reinstalling 10.10, and guess what?  11.10 seems to be using certain kernals that 10.10 needs to install the software, and 11.10 won't stop using them, even when it isn't being used.  Bogus.  It's an obvious way to keep people from using the platform most preferred.

I have spoken to other Ubuntu, and former Ubuntu users, and I get the same feedback from them.  Ubuntu 11.10 is no where near as good as 10.10.  The former users have switched to Fedora, and I'm looking into that myself.  Why it was necessary to change the setup of Ubuntu so drastically is beyond my understanding.  The way you had it first was what drew people like me into loving it.

That's all I have to say, and I'm afraid that Ubuntu will soon be something left behind in my computer use.  :(

### Post by Doc on 2011-12-02
I'm on the same page. After staying on 10.04 since it was released (and initially jumping on the Ubuntu boat with 6.06) I decided to upgrade to 11.10 two weeks ago. My experience in a nutshell:

**THE GOOD**: I had no hardware trouble whatsoever. Major score!

**THE BAD**: Unity and all the eye candy slowed down the system to a crawl. My hardware specs aren't that out of date that this should be an issue.

**THE UGLY**: Banshee is a disaster, it would brown-out a minute or more every time it launched. To be fair I have a music library of several thousand songs of several different formats, but frankly it oughta *just work*. 

THE VERDICT: I can understand that progress demands more features and better visuals, but the drag on my system is the main reason I quit Microsoft products years ago. In the end, I decided to try Lubuntu. A few little quirks here and there, but it hums along nicely and doesn't get in the way of me getting my work done. Which is about all I can ask of an OS.
