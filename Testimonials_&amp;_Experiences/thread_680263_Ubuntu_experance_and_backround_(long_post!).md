### Post by Raths on 2008-01-27
Let me start by saying thank you to all of the great posters, how to writers, advice givers and general commenters that make this the most informative forum on the net.  I'd really be lost without all of you!

My background in computers goes back to my very first VIC-20 back in about '82.  It was just enough to make me want to find out more.  The games were lame, I don't even remember if it had graphics, and everything was sooo slow!  Moving up to the C=64 was the best thing ever!  Two disk drives, a tape backup and a 14.4 k modem.  Wow, those were the days! 

Fast forwarding to '98, I was married, had some kids and wanted to get back into computers.  Found a used M$ 3.x machine and quickly learned how to fix the darn thing.  Lots of late nights and frustrating fixes and OS's that didn't seem to work any better than the last...well, we have all been there.

Four weeks ago I finally figured out that M$ seems to make a computer die every two years.  The viruses get so embedded that you just can't get rid of them!  Being the “computer expert” in the family, I couldn't go to the wife and tell her we needed another computer (I'm saving that argument for my first build).  So I decided to try a Linux OS.  I have experimented with Red Hat and a couple of others but never really got too far.  I just couldn't get them to install.  This time I was more determined.  After much research I found Ubuntu.  Seemed like a good OS and these forums were full of advice.  

So I downloaded the .ISO image, burned it, and couldn't get it installed.  Tried a different image, burned at a slower speed, tried the graphical install mode, tried to wipe my hard drive, tried everything I could think of.  Suddenly, it looked like it was working!  It really was!  A few glitches getting it all installed, but over all fairly painless, again thanks to these boards.

So to finish a long story, I'm now running Gutsy on my laptop and upgrading to it on the desktop.  And really liking the system.  Still having some problems with a couple of the boy's favorite games, but the games available for Linux are keeping them content for the time being.  Still researching on how to get the computers to share files, but I was never able to do this with the M$ systems.  My wife say says that she hates the new system, but she doesn't use the computers for more than surfing the net and playing card games.  I'm sure she will come around!

Again, thanks for all of the great information that is contained here and keep up the good work!

L8R

Raths
