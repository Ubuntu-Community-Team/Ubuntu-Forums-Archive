### Post by kforum on 2008-11-16
*this is a big post, so be prepared to read a bit.*

this time around testing ubuntu(i use gentoo as my main), i was installing it in my mom's old laptop(which ill make a guide for later) that only has 256mb. ;) and on my main machine.


first off, -installation.
the alternate installation method REALLY needs a change, it could be seriously simplified for newbie users, i'd be frightened of telling a ms.mom of sorts to do that alone. o.o
and, beyond being doable, its not that absurd...
anyhow, also, on the same lappy, with the regular installer i had this weird bug(maybe its a general ubu 8.10 bug -not sure, since i had very little mb to work with, that might have been the cause) every time the installer reached its end, it would roll back to the partition editor, and behave strangely... basically, it would get stuck there, no matter where i clicked... o.O

the kubuntu installer on my main machine however was quite ok.(i still don't like it, but it does its job... sort of anyways).
-one of the main things i dont like in it is the part. man. :)

Also, in an honest opinion the normal and alternate install should be in the same cd, how many more mbs would the crappy alternate installer take?
most probably not enough to justify its non-existence. it was a real hassle to dl the regular cd, just to realize i needed to download -a whole nother cd- just so i could switch installers... o.O 
not... very... logical...

but beyond that, everything went fine with installing. and aside from a few bugs, which i reported and are being given their proper attention, kde booted fine.

-from here on ill talk about kubuntu, since:
1. ubuntu has always been the beloved and taken-care-of child of canonical, therefore it always works quite flawlessly.(and personally i love the new features in 8.10)
2.the ubu machine was my mom's lappy so i just set it up and she's using it, not me, so i dont have that much data on it. 
-not like there have been any issues thus far anyways.
3.kubuntu is the ugly duckling of the *buntu family, so its always mother-bugland and whatnot. :p but i like kde, so... :(


anyways, on to kubuntu:

kmix is clearly bugged right off the bat, it seems it is that way because of the fact that 'front' is chosen as the master channel, instead of pcm. upon choosing pcm it gets fixed.

still, when i select mute, it works fine, but doesnt light up my led of 'mute' when its muted(it could, since most of my leds work on my compal fl-90).

also, why the hell do i need to install a TON of ubuntu stuff, when i want to install firefox? i mean i know that the FF in ubu has its own custom fixes, but... why cant they use kde stuff inside kubuntu? i know its not the end of the world, so... may i call it laziness?

i think this new 'auto-detect' feature in kde4 is rather strange, i mean 'new divice notifier' has done tis job, full time, flawlessly... but what about autorun? where art thee! another + to ubu, and - to kubu...

Adept has been dumber then ever in this version, im seriously considering using synaptics, this is not fun.
it has 'bugged' on me all the time(for instance, i might do a search of something that has just been added, and it wont show up on adept... but if i use apt-get in the command line... its there you bugger!
also, sometimes search just plain dont work...)

:mad: very bad move.

a few multimedia problems seem to have been fixed in the latest updates, so i wont mention them.

on a general rant:
one thing that is quite sad is that the powermanagement feature in kde cant help you determine how much is the battery charged, this basically makes my 'charge' button on the left be ignored.
the only way to get this workign way to boot into XP, where it would be set to only charge till 50%, then back into kubu, and ti would be working fine. but since i dont have windows anymore...

back to kubuntu:
my kde network manager sometimes takes for ever to start/simply doesnt. so i have to logout/login to get it to work, quite often <- major issue.
this is a pain if you're using wifi all the time.
also, amarok bugs on me all the time i try to listen to streaming, it seems like a buffering problem since, it simply stops 'playing any sound' after a while, even though technically the radio station is still connected...
-anyone know how to fix this?

i dont really like the way konqueror is atm, as a internet browser, but ill just write about this in the kde forum.(i'd love to use it though)

-another minor, when kde starts the starting sound is cut after a little, i read about this somewhere, ti was being addressed i think. but its worth mentioning.

I also havent seen anywhere where i can edit the touchpad config, unlike ubuntu that has a lovely new gui/feature for that. why not make one for kde?! that would be greatly appreciated for newbies...
and having to edit xorg.conf is not my idea of working-out-of-the-box. ;)


Overall kubuntu is buggier then ever, we all know its kde4's fault, but regardless... the least canonical could do is tell users that the kubuntu 8.10 version, is a very buggy/glitchy/more of a testing verions, that would certainly hold new users from getting frightened by the nonworking 'linux' experience.


maybe there are more issues, ubuntu has been shaping up with time, and is quite a nice distro now-a-days, its not neglected brother aren't quite as reliable however.
and kubuntu 8.10 is ok as a distro, its nice to see kde4.1 around, but we have to admit its not heaven thus far... lets hope for 9.04. 


canonical can do better in many things, this is my view of the lates. hope this sheds a light on some things/issues.

any comments are welcomed.


cheers
