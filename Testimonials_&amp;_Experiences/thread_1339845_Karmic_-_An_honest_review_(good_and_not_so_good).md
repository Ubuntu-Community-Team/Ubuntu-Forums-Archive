### Post by Orographic on 2009-11-28
I love Ubuntu. Its the best Linux distro, IMHO. 

I'm still fairly much a beginner (been using it for a year after 15 with Windows) but Karmic has been a bit of a disappointment in stability for me on the same machine that ran Jaunty, Ibex and Heron. It ran perfectly for three weeks but then crashed when the monitor went to sleep (could not recover) and also crashed again when blanking a CD - could not recover from that either. Thankfully I had some good Clonezilla images to restore. Actually, in the end I did a new install with EXT3 instead of EXT4.

I just love the new innovations in Karmic but without stability, its not really workable as a productivity machine. I will have to wait for the next LTS for that. I'm hoping the bugs get fixed by then.

I can't wait for the next edition of Ubuntu and thank the team for all of the innovative work they are doing but for now I will have to remain with PCLOS 2009.2 for reliable productivity.

Blizz
PS: I've had to make regular moves up from Heron to Karmic as there have been annoying crash bugs in each version although Jaunty was rock solid except for the intel graphics issue. PCLOS has not crashed yet and the remaster facility is superb.
