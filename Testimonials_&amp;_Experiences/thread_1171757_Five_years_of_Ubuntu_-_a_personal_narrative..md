### Post by thinktyler on 2009-05-27
I first installed Ubuntu when I was fourteen. ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=589313](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=589313)) Like most people, I spent the first year switching between XP and Ubuntu. The first thing I remember about Ubuntu was the 'Ubuntu Philosophy'. A philosophy I believed to be unselfish, charitable and worldly. This philosophy seemed to connect myself not to just to my neighbor, but to the entire world. The ability for a person to help out no matter the political, social or economic status before seemed unimaginable. This philosophy enabled a person from the United Kingdom to help me whenever I had troubles with my BIOS. It is is the same philosophy that enabled me to return the favor and help a person who didn't understand what command line was. The largest of deeds to the smallest of needs never goes unthanked, or unnoticed. Oddly, strangely or as crazy as it may seem my political views have been greatly impacted by this community. Gender, race, religion, or region of the world has no barring to ones ability to help, succeed and to give back.

After mastering an operating system like Windows, it was hard for me to give up everything I had learned and start from nothing. Application installs seemed to be complex, security measures seemed to be advanced and exactly what Ubuntu was doing or trying to do was somewhat of a mystery. I had installed php scripts on a webserver before, but editing variables, conf files seemed to be difficult to grasp. Why do I need to do things, why can't somebody just tell me how? My first problem with Ubuntu derived from the flgrx driver, in which I wanted to install Beryl, so I could install some awesome visual effects. The purpose was at this time, to show it off to all of my friends. It was, however, a fairly daunting task that led me to re-installing ubuntu about four times. Year one I learned that ATI graphic cards were not good and I should think about investing in Nvidia. 

Year two, I wanted a better graphics card because almost every update from Beryl seemed to break my O.S. Although I could eventually figure out a solution it was becoming a problem and a hassle. So I purchased my first Nvidia graphics card to replace my Radeon 9800. It proved to be a good investment because to my surprise everything worked out the box. At this time, I was still playing counter-strike and starcraft in xp, which forced me to keep the XP parition around. I remember starting to read forum posts and articles of PRO LINUX ONLY USERS! Users that refused to accept that somethings simply could not run in ubuntu. They refused to dual boot, setup virtualbox and swore that any software that wouldn't install on ubuntu they wouldn't use. I'm not exactly sure what made me decide to accept this idea, but nonetheless I deleted my XP partition ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=508037](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=508037)) (Where I flat out deleted XP, finding out I should have been more careful, haha) On this day, I stop playing PC games that I couldn't install on Ubuntu and refused to accept windows xp as a good operating system. 

Year three, My friends and I decided to go to Cancun, so I bought a HD camera to take along with me. I captured about 40 hours of dumb drunk high school students binge drinking until we passed out. I then returned home wanting to edit the video into a montage to give to my friends before we all split ways to different colleges. Video editing on ubuntu was miserable. I couldn't find software to edit my videos and export it to a DVD, I kept on getting Segment faults. This project was scratched and my thoughts of returning to windows once again surfaced. I did gain some valuable insights on audio and video bitrates however. Keeping to my original idea, refusing to install xp once again proved difficult because of the video that could have been made.

Year four was year one of college. Openoffice was a good tool for the mediocre standards of high school English class and besides it was high school. I wrote my first college paper in English 1000 at the University of Missouri. I got an C-. Apparently, grammar and spelling check had not been enabled, so like this narrative it had tons of errors. I was spoiled by Word 2003, which made me rethink how smart I really was. I chose to keep writing with openoffice instead of on one of the schools computers, because I felt that I could benefit from it more than I could have benifited from a bunch of green and red lines that word had. My paper grades increased and somewhat my grammatical and spelling errors. I'm not blaming openoffice for my grades, but I'm a product of spell check, so like most my grammar is terrible. I was convinced by year four that I would never go back to XP.

Until year five, I enrolled in my first computer science course which made windows XP a requirement, lol. Until I found code::blocks. To be continued...
