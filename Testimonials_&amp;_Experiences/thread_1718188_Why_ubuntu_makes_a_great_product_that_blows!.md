I have been using Ubuntu for a little over two years now, and I think it has great things going for it and love most of the things. However, I think I have just grown tired of so many incompatible hardware issues I am seriously contemplating returning to Winblows.

You are absolutely barking up the wrong tree. It is not Ubuntu's fault.  The Dell xps 15 is Optimus enabled and this spells doom for Linux.

Optimus is a Nvidia technology that allows the laptop to switch between a high end Nvidia card and a low end intel card when performance is not needed to prolong battery life. Optimus doesn't support Linux (actually it only works for Win 7, not even Vista).  But  Optimus doesn't just switch between two cards, the two cards actually become a hybrid in a sense, with optimus enabled Linux can't use the nvidia card **at all** even though the card has nvidia driver support, even worse the Nvidia card would continue to drain your battery even though you can only access the cheap Intel card. So you are paying for a Nvidia card which in effect is worse than a paper weight.

 And, if you have installed the Nvidia closed driver it would behave exactly like you described and probably worse. There are tutorials on UF to teach people how to disable/supress the Nvidia card just to get a functional computer back, but that means you are paying a lot more to use a crappy Intel graphic card so that is no solution really.

When more and more Nvidia laptops come with Optimus there could come a time that Linux laptops are completely shut out from Nvidia (unless you get a quadro or something which would cost a lot more)

Some laptops have an option to disable Optimus and set the graphic card to Nvidia in the BIOS, but most don't (The thinkpads apparently support this)

Read this thread for more info and write a letter to Nvidia.
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1712278](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1712278)


**EDITED:** you may want to write to Dell as well, since it could add a BIOS switch to disable Optimus and set graphic card options.
