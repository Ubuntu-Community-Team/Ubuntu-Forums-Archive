### Post by 1roxtar on 2011-06-01
I have to say, first of all, is that I am an Ubuntu man through and through.  Since 9.04 to 11.04, I have been for the most part a Gnome DE Ubuntu user.  I've installed Lubuntu on some low spec'd computers but haven't really ever given KDE much of an honest use or test.  

That all changed this week.  I've been reading some good reviews on Kubuntu 11.04 and after seeing someone eye-candy his Kubuntu box so beautifully (I lost where I found the screenshot), I had to give it an honest to goodness try.  I started with installing Kubuntu on a USB thumb drive set as persistent.  As I customized it and tried the various defaults programs, I found myself really diggin' it.

The only programs, I am not too crazy about are the browser Rekonq and the music player Amarok.  I installed Firefox & Chrome for my internet and Banshee as my media player (that's just my personal preference, although I may try Clementine too).  Since this Sunday, I have fully installed Kubuntu 11.04 on my personal laptop and on one of my home desktops.  

I have to add that I love the Unity desktop (yes, you read that right), but I have another favorite *buntu.

### Post by christopher.wortman on 2011-06-18
Words of warning about KDE lol

Those of you who think KDE is an ideal world, I agree, but its not always ponies and icecream. KDE likes to be bold and they will often do things that breaks other things that you are used to just working.

With that being said, welcome to the wonderful world of KDE where things are done right, for the sake of doing them right. It doesn't always agree with the world, or even with me at times. If you stick it out, you will realise why things are done in the manner they are.
