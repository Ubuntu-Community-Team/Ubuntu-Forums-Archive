### Post by vcrpex on 2010-10-30
Thank You Ubuntu and all the ubuntu-related forums!
It is the support of the community that make Ubuntu so popular. 

My journey with Ubuntu started one and half year ago. Due to a virus attack on my wife's PC, I have to re-format her computer even though I have anti-virus in there. I have re-format computer multiple times in my life, but I am pretty sick of the Windoze crap and the slow start-up and all the licensing issues. Thus upon hearing a friend of mine recommend me to give Ubuntu a try, knowing that I have fiddle with Redhat 5.0 and 8.0 many years back. I decided to give Ubuntu a shot in dual-boot mode just in case I am not able to solve certain issues and might need to do some simple flash if need be. I must say I was pretty lucky to install Ubuntu pretty easily for my wife. I was impressed, thus I decided to do that on an old Sony Vaio laptop that is running Windows XP then with no USB boot support and no CD-ROM drive in that machine to begin with. 

That was really a challenge to me as I have ask around all my IT friends and none could provide me with a solution, other than asking me to buy a fire-wire CD-ROM drive. After a few days of googling, I finally found a way to do USB-boot through software on that machine as I did not want to have windows in it anymore. After that other things simply went pretty smoothly. From running MT4 in wine to using GIMP instead of photoshop, it is perfect. Only thing is that I still cant figure a way to develop flash in ubuntu. Within 3 mths, I took all the old working parts of 2 of my desktops and put it into one and install Ubuntu as well. Getting my old Geforce and my PCI wireless card to work was quite a learning process. Really almost to the extent of throwing in the towel, but the 2 other computers are running perfectly well after the installation hassle. I got most of my solutions from this forum. Big Thank you to all of you who contribute your knowledge to this community!

After using 9.04(laptop & wife) and 9.10(old P4 desktop), I was keen to see what 10.10 can do. I bought a long delayed new CPU AMD athon II x3, 4gig RAM and 1 TB of hard disk and a asus usb N10 wireless card. With my experience of installing ubuntu last year, it certainly help. I was quite shocked to find my left mouse cant click at the beginning of the installation, did it through my keyboard and then eventually found the fix for it. I was dual-booting in my previous desktop, this time round I decided to install Virtualbox and try Windows 7 Ultimate on a 20 gig image. It work flawlessly now. But honestly I mainly use windows 7 for Ppstream and maybe abit of flash work. I have tried Sopcast in ubuntu, it worked but for the 64bit Maverick, it caused some dependencies issues for wine when I got Sopcast to work. In the end, wine is more important to me as MT4 is more important to me than Sopcast in Ubuntu. Just do all the video streaming in the virtualbox. 

This virtualbox thingy have gotten me to try a few other flavours of linux in time to come. Only slight problem til now is I can't sync my iphone directly in Ubuntu as yet. Will probably jump to Android Phone next year when my phone contract ends. All in all, Ubuntu rocks! 
sorry for the super long post.
