### Post by adamitj on 2012-09-30
Hi folks!

After a long time without posting, here I am again :)

Well, in the last 3 years I changed my job four times. And now I moved to a bigger - and better - city, to work for a State government company, called CELEPAR.

As a governamental institution, almost every software is opensource. And this includes the OS. For the last decade, the company created a fork of Debian, called "Desktop Parana". This OS was running very well, but with new machines coming it became obsolete and without significant updates. So the company choosed to move on to Ubuntu Linux 12.04.

A very few employees are testing this new OS, including me. And everyone is frustrated, hating, blaming, and getting a little annoyed with Unity. Even "Gnome Classic" on 12.04 is not working properly, there are a lot of known bugs as the one regarding ALT+TAB.

So, I'm asking myself for days, and trying to find something good on this new Unity, but every mention I found in Internet just tells me the Unity really sucks, and Canonical still want to go on with it. Why they just rip off this bad part of this wonderfull OS and bring back Gnome, or just Mate?

I'm testing Xubuntu Desktop Manager this weekend, at home. Tomorrow I'll install it on my workstation. I have tried Gnome-Classic, MATE, and XFCE. The only one which works well on GTK applications was XFCE.

Does anyone likes Unity in this world? Will Canonical spent time and money on this "rotten apple"?
