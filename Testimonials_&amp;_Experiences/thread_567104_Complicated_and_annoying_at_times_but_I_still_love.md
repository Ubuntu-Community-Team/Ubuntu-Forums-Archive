### Post by BOZG on 2007-10-04
I've had a number of painstakingly annoying experiences with Ubuntu but I'm definitely warming to it.  Open-sources is definitely the way forward though I do think it will take a lot of effort and a lot of time for people to move away from Windows.  There are obviously a number of problems in which Linux lags behind Windows which I think has stunted Linux's popularity so far.  If you just want to do basic task like use the internet, use word and play games then Windows is the simplest way forward. At this stage unfortunately, unless you've got a lot of patience or interest in computers, it's easy to give up on Linux but I'm quite confident that as people become more and more technologically able, Linux will become a strong challenger to likes of Windows and Mac. 

One of my main dislikes about Linux is the small number of users who have become the new Mac users, the type of self-righteous and pious people that the solution to any problem in Windows is "Linux is better" or "if you had Linux, you wouldn't have that problem".  That type of attitude just puts people off wanting to try different OSs or open-source software.  I'm sure if I talked to some of these people in real-life, rather than on the Internet, I'd find myself beating them up out of frustration. :)

On the other hand, something that I really like about Linux, more than the OS itself is the enormously helpful and friendly users (the majority of users), who seem to donate a huge amount of time to helping out new users, even when it's a simple question that's been asked hundreds of times before.  You only have to look at the number of users online, on these forums who are scouring through the threads trying to help people and trying to promote Linux.  It's absolutely fantastic and  I think that anyone who's involved in this type of work deserves a huge pat on the back.
