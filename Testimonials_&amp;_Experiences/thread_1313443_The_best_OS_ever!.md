### Post by ~Lisa~ on 2009-11-03
Hi everyone,

Two days ago I decided to play with Ubuntu after a friend told me about it, so I downloaded the live cd and checked it out. I thought it was really neat that you could run an OS from a cd without anything changing on your computer, so I played with it for a few hours and sort of got comfortable with it.

 I was amazed with the how quickly it responded to me, even from the live cd, which I had read would be slower than if it were installed. from the live cd it was faster than Windows, and that really surprised me. So I thought I would dive in and ditch Windows completely. 

I was surprised again at it's speed and responsiveness, it installed much much faster than I was anticipating, and installed all of the drivers and everything all by itself, it made me happy that I didn't need a half a million driver cds and didn't have to spend hours installing AV and firewall software and clean up tools and all of the crap you need to run Windows safely. 

When I rebooted after install it loaded so fast that I just like OMFG! Now it's responsiveness was even better, applications opened almost instantly, and everything worked perfectly. I had some trouble with the Terminal, figuring that out took some time and adjustment, but now I love it. Everything feels so much better than Windows. 

I also get lots of stuff to start me out, and it's all free. Free stuff rocks lol.  Even an entire office suite, and media players, and all sorts of fun games to keep me entertained lol. And if I want even more stuff, it's free too!! 

The only negative is lack of sleep because I can't make myself stop playing with Ubuntu and go to bed, lol. 

Thank you the people who make Ubuntu, and the support people. I am so impressed with Ubuntu, and it's just such a fun and rewarding OS to use.

I also want to thank Aysiu for all of the time put into the psychocats tutorial, and blogs, I've read most of the articles, and find them very well written and insightful. The Ubuntu tutorial also was extremely helpful, I don't think I would have done as well as I did with Ubuntu without your help...thank you, so much!!

Also, thank you to the people who run the forums, and the people who give advice and support, thank you so much for your time, and your concern. You give of yourselves with no thoughts of compensation, I think that is so awesome. Again, thank you, so much.

I hope to be a regular poster, as time permits, and once I learn enough about Ubuntu, I'd love to give back, and help others.

~Lisa
