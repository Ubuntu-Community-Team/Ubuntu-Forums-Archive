### Post by Barrucadu on 2008-04-02
I'm sorry Ubuntu, but I'm leaving you.
We had a whirlwind romance, you introduced me to many new and exciting things that I didn't even know my computer could do. Every night I would go to sleep comfortable in the knowledge that you were nearby, watching over me whilst ticking over your nightly tasks. Every six months or so you would go out for a few hours and come back having done a complete makeover. I liked the change, you were always adding new things to yourself, making sure that our experiences were always new and exciting.
But then Arch showed up on my desktop. Arch enticed me with her rolling-release model, simplicity, speed, and low RAM usage. I didn't stand a chance. Arch called, and I answered. You just weren't fast enough to catch me. Arch and I easily sprinted ahead of you, you were being weighed down by all your extra features.
It pains me to say this, but I'm leaving you. We had something wonderful, but Arch took it away. I know this will break your heart, but try and cope. I may return, and I'll always keep your LiveCD in my drawer so I can return to you if I want.

### Post by angryfirelord on 2008-04-02
C'mon guys, let's not be hasty. Not every user who leaves Ubuntu is a Gates or Jobs fanboy. In fact, I sometimes question Ubuntu's QA team, such as the inclusion of Bulletproof X when it really wasn't bulletpoof yet. I'd wish they would extend the release cycle for another month to just work out the bugs.

If you looking for rolling release distro, you may want to consider Ubuntu's older relative, Debian. You can dist-upgrade to Debian's testing branch by changing anything that says "stable" or "etch" to "testing" in your sources.list. By that method, you will always have a rolling release. Debian also doesn't explode into a million fluffy kittens or pink ponies when something goes wrong. ;)

The problem is that all these distros are as capricious as the women folk that entice with promises of everlasting excitement and ecstacy, until you get them home when the nags are the same, the efforts to change you to their way of thinking - to make you become what they want you to be rather than leave you as you were to begin with.  It reminds me of the "trouble upgrading from Girlfriend V7 to Wife V1" skit that went around a few years ago.
The problem comes with the splitting up, the tears and heartbreaks and the division of all your worldly goods, the disappointments for the things left behind that were worth keeping but don't exist in the newer model.
I don't know if I can cope with the continual upheaval of looking at the greener grass over the fence.  I will settle down in my armchair, with my slippers on and put up with the one I have got because for the most part I understand most of her foibles, can tolerate the little niggles, and the effort of change is just far too much.
