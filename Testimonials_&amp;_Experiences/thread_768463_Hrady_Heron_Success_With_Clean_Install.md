### Post by CosmicFlux on 2008-04-26
Hi,

Just though I'd share this with you, might help out some people who are having issues with Heron. 

I initially selected the 'Upgrade' option from within Update Manager, but obviously there was high load on the servers cause it just kept hanging. I eventually just decided to leave it and see what happened. It finally let me upgrade to Heron, which I did and all seemed fine...

At first it seemed to have been successful, then I began to receive error messages with certain apps and utilities. I couldn't use Synaptic, packages were being listed as broken and my sources were all to hell. After backing up my files, I decided to try a clean install instead, choosing Xubuntu as I had it on Gutsy but after the upgrade it was hanging too. 

In typical Ubuntu fashion, the installation was a breeze and my system is now fully upgraded to Heron. 

If any of you are having similar issues I would seriously suggest going for a clean install. I don't think upgrading is as efficient, even on other systems it's usually better to do a clean install. I know it's a pain having to go through the process of burning/backing up files but it's worth it to have a working system free of corrupted files and the like.

See Ya!

CosmicFlux
