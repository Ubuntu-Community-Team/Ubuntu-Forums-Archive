### Post by anewguy on 2008-06-23
I feel your pain, but try to also remember that if everything on your Windows PC "just works" it is because someone installed drivers - be they in the Windows database or provided by a manufacturer on disk.  It's very true you don't find this with Linux, and the simple answer is money.  Vendors don't like giving away something for free, and they also don't see a need to develop things like drivers for an OS that isn't anywhere near the installed base of Windows desktops.

That being said, I remember the same frustrations - and there is a vast majority out there who do.  But for the most part things can be made to work - not always as easy as in Windows, but they can be made to work.  You are also dealing with a very different animal in Linux - the complete way the OS is designed, etc., is very, very different from Windows.

I did what most have done - searched these forums, searched the net, cussed, searched some more, asked a lot of questions (I still do).

I won't tell you flat out to go back to Windows.  I would instead say try to live with the up-front headaches until everything is working (and it will) then just drive Linux for a while.  If you don't like it then, go ahead and go back to Windows - I won't criticize you for doing so.  People have to go with what ever they feel the most comfortable with.

I will say, however, that I don't think Ubuntu or any other variant of Linux has ever said it's easy, and it particular easier than Windows.  Up front there are some headaches - mostly due to the differences in the animals.  You may find that once you get used to Linux you may actually like it.

Best of luck in what ever direction you choose.

For potential users reading this thread, I would tell you to do some research first.  Know what hardware you have - in particular the video card and wireless if you have it.  Research the net and these forums for information on those before you just jump over to Linux.  You'll be far better prepared and it will make your installation far less painful.

### Post by mnmus on 2008-06-24
OK, there's a middle ground here. I have more than a few years' experience with DOS/Windows (and before DOS with CP/M and... older stuff, water under the bridge *heh*), and Windows installations can be as big a nightmare as any I've read about in the forums over the past three days. 

In the past, I've just fiddled with various Linux distros, not enough to really learn the OS but enough to find it intriguing. With Windows, however--largely because of work-related experience--I can do just about anything a middle-to-large Windows-based network needs done. (Heck, and most of what a Novell network needs as well, but I'd rather not *heh*).

Now, I've taken the plunge and decided to eliminate three or four different computers I have cluttering my home office with one Ubuntu-based box (and a couple of virtualized OSes "inside" it). It's been a bit of a trip over the last three days' time setting this thing up, so I can relate to what the original poster expressed. 

But.

I've had quirky Windows installs, as well, one recently in fact when building a system for my 85-year-old dad who wanted a Windows XP system. Took me two days just to get the OEM restore CDs to install correctly on an "old" HP (and in fact, they never did; I had to repair the OEM installation with a different XP CD). And yes, after restoring the thing to pristine condition, I then upgraded it to a more contemporary hardware configuration, which necessitated another round of hair-pulling (and "arguing" with Windows/M$ about "permissible" upgrades w/in licensing requirements: the thing was only barely "HP-ish" by the time I finished).

Has my 3-day, still _almost_ finished, installation of Hardy onto a bare metal AMD 64 X 2 box been as arduous as the lil box I worked on to give my dad? 

No. But at times, hassling with nVidia drivers, wondering why the heck there was no sound from a plain vanilla Creative Live! 5+1 sound card, and more, I could see the original poster's point.

But, harder than a bare metal installation (or even an OEM restore, at times) of Windows? Depends on too many factors to say. Why, even simple Windows images propogated across an install of "identical" boxes in a volume licensing situation can go widly wrong. The devil's in the details.

I'm happy with my Ubuntu-based box, so far, even with the few remaining glitches (why does hibernation work sometimes but not others, for example--still chasing that one down). Sure, I'd not hand the proverbial "Aunt Tilly" a bare metal box and an Ubuntu CD and have her go at it, but my wife was able to do an unguided wubi install on her Toshiba notebook with no problems. (And yes, I think I know the limitations of a wubi installation.)

Does Ubuntu suck? No. No more or less than Windows or OSX do. *heh* Do I think Ubuntu has advantages for my situation that putting Vista or even XP on this box does not? Absolutely. Is any single OS the Swiss Army Knife of Life, one size fits all, etc.? I don't think so. Why, I think there's even a place for Apple to continue to write code! *LOL*

Oh, and I have done other Ubuntu installs as test runs on other boxes--some dual boots with another OS (Windows--two versions, PCBSD 1.5) and others onto bare metal. All of them were easier than this one *LOL* It figures. Murphy strikes again, eh?
