### Post by Bruce M. on 2008-07-31
Hi Folks,

Today, 31 July 2008, is my **One Year Anniversary** using Linux, and it's been Ubuntu all the way.

I started with Ubuntu 6.06.1 as I couldn't download 7.04 for some reason but ordered the CD Version that arrived in just under two weeks.

Put Ubuntu v6.06.1 on my P-III and was instantly gratified, 7.04 was even better. Ubuntu 7.10 was slow though, so I downloaded Xubuntu 7.10 and installed that. Now that was more like it.

Then came Xubuntu 8.04, and the old P-III complained a bit but still better than Ubuntu 7.10. OK, so I was looking at using Xubuntu 8.04 for the full term or until my P-III died.

However I came into a stroke of luck and a friend decided to send me some computer parts he wasn't using anymore. The results of which you can see in my signature below. Some parts turned out to be a complete computer minus the case. It was Christmas in July.

For this grandpa it's a dream machine. I finally managed to get it up and running after a few weeks of trials and tribulations as the last machine I built from scratch, so to speak, was a 486 in the mid 90's. Things had changed, so this was a real challenge. With the help of a couple of people
here though I managed to get it together and running about 1/2 an hour before midnight last night and only three days after the P-III actually died, or at least the built in LAN card died.

So here I am, celebrating one year with *buntu and I've just downloaded the two 64bit versions of Xubuntu 8.04 that will get burned to CD's tonight and installed tomorrow.

I switched to Xfce out of necessity, but I'm staying with it because it has grown on me. I'm retired, I don't need Office, I didn't like Evolution, but then I didn't like Outlook either. I found Nautilus to slow and much preferred Thunar in Gnome anyway.

So, here's to you Ubuntu. I'm now 100% Windows free, my wife finally gave in and I will not be installing it on this machine despite the fact our ISP does not support Linux.

To each and every one of you who have helped me go from a green behind the ears Linux user to someone that can actually use this OS with a degree of confidence, I thank you. My way of repaying you is to help others with what little knowledge I have picked up along the way that you have shared with me. To you and everyone else here in the forums, congratulations for creating a great place to spend time to share, to learn and to meet other Ubuntu users.

I wish you all a GREAT day.
Bruce
