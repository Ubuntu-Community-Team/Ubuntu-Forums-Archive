### Post by goettlek on 2009-04-26
I had been running Kubuntu 8.10 for a few days, but I really was not fond of KDE 4. It was sluggish, choppy and I didn't care for it at all. I fell back down to 8.04 with KDE 3.5 until the release of 9.04. I've always loved new Ubuntu releases, its like Christmas in April, so naturally I downloaded the 9.04 Live CD and tried it out.

On the CD alone I was liking what I was seeing. Programs ran smoother, it wasn't bloated with a bunch of extra features or visual effects and the interface was flawless. I figured why not install it, so I did.

Now I've had it since it's release and so far I'm liking it a lot and really enjoying it. Those who were like me and were frowning at KDE 4 frown no more. KDE 4.2 is really nice, and it's starting to grow more and more on me everyday.

The only beef I have is the lack of Adept. I really liked having the Adept package manager. The Add and Remove programs menu in KDE 4.2 only lists individual packages and not the program names like Adept did in KDE 3.5. I miss that:(. Overall though I think KDE 4.2 is starting to make it's place in the world.
