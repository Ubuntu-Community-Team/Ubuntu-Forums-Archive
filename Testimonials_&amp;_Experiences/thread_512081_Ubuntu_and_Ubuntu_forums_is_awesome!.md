### Post by john8791 on 2007-07-28
I have messed around with different Linux distros for about 3 years now such as Slackware which we use at my place of business for in house web and mail hosting.  For the "desktop" though I was always a little disappointed because of the endless hours it takes to get drivers and multimedia stuff functional.  I thought Xandros was the answer but they seemed to abandon the "free software" thing. 

About 2 months ago I installed Feisty Fawn on a second hard drive as a dual boot with XP and I've been blown away.  My Netgear wireless pci card was recognized right away, sound worked without messing with "alsaconfig".  Not everything was rosy but it has been very painless.  I think the first thing anyone should do is install "Envy" from Alberto Milone.  It flawlessly downloaded, compiled, and installed all the correct drivers for my Nvidia GeForce FX5200 video card.  Secondly, install Automatix2 to take care of getting the multimedia Codecs, etc.  Of course I hammered and chisled through it before I discovered Automatix, but it was a learning experience.  I'm also impressed by how far Codeweaver's Crossover Office has come since I messed with it last.  I know this isn't part of Ubuntu but it makes Ubuntu even better.  

Although I can't completely abandon Microsoft, I would say I am using Ubuntu for about 80% of everything I need to do.  Thanks Ubuntu!
