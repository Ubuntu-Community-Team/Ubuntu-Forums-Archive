### Post by Joshuwa on 2008-07-18
Naturally, I don't think it would be necessary to point out what a testimonial is, but I think it has come time for a reminder that testimonials may not always be positive.

It has become a growing trend here to welcome positive feedbacks, but shun - and rudely - dismiss anything else.

Generally, the forum is polite, and attempts to help those who are having less-than-ideal experiences; more and more however, those users are insulted, joked at, and told to "just leave."

-- Here are some real examples --
> **saratchandra said:**
> Shut the f**k up and stop trolling. Get a life ffs.

> **wolfen69 said:**
> how come it works good for me and everyone i know? when you say it's not quite there yet, you mean for you? it is obvious it will never work for you until you decide to get a computer that will work good with linux. i know 3 people that can't run vista properly without serious issues. i suppose windows isnt ready either. if you were that serious about running linux, you would get a computer with linux pre-installed, or pick up a cheap used computer. please, spare me the "linux is not ready" whine. *you* are not ready. goodbye.

> **bmac said:**
> Excuse me while I wipe this tear...

I don't want to do anything, it should work perfectly out of the box just like windows (OMG). I shouldn't have to learn or understand anything different, much less make changes.... Somethings wrong with Ubuntu - it just doesn't work like windows....

> **kevdog said:**
> Good riddance.  Say hi to Steve Balmer for me while you are at it!

> **stchman said:**
> Our great loss.  The community is less than what it was.

....

Goodbye.

----------------------------

At what point does obnoxiousness become necessary? If you cannot help someone, or simply do not wish to, just let it go and move on. What good comes from snide comments and arrogant undertones?

Further, not only are these types of comments being made at an increasingly popular rate, they are being welcomed and tolerated more and more. 

In most cases, the replies are helpful, and at times, forum staff even respond to alert others that the thread is fair and legitimate. But should that be required?

I think we as a community of linux users can do better.

Sure, some new users who had poor experiences may not fill their first post with happiness and sunshine - but why should they? They either wiped out a ton of their old data or spent time backing it up, only to spend even more time configuring something that in the end, didn't even work for them. 

One should understand that someone in the above scenario would be frustrated; they'd have every right to be. Keeping this in mind would be very helpful, I think.

Not everyone who tried Ubuntu (or any distro) will have a good experience. Not all those who have a good experience will like it. 

And that is just fine. The whole philosophy behind open source projects is to give the user freedom and choice. Let's not forget that the choice may be to not use it.

Those who do not use Linux or even dislike Linux are not our enemies; they are simply other users with different preferences.

Linux is not for everyone, and it is a flaw to assume that it should be.

Can't we be a little more polite and helpful to those who didn't have such good experiences?

Just my $0.02

Many times, I've suggested that we ignore these posts and not respond to individual bad behavior. However, like many other community members I find it difficult to accept the concept of providing them with a platform to spew their hostile comments. This could potentially impact new users and question their confidence in Ubuntu. Many of these OP's have no interest in assistance, their only objective is to have a negative impact on the forum. This is apparent, as they often post in sections other than Testimonials. Their actions are intentional and often times does target new users, which in my opinion is detrimental.

Keep in mind that all individuals on the forum have an opinion. Denying or restricting (even suggesting) their opinions or responses be suppressed reduces the objectivity of the forum. I for one, totally disagree with that philosophy. I do support individuals that are frustrated with Ubuntu and wish to express their opinion in a reasonable post that reflects their experience and respects all readers.Condoning anything less opens the forum to increased negative verbiage that often includes terminology that many may find unacceptable. Serious testimonials that define specific issues and express the OP's efforts (successful or unsuccessful) usually receive positive feedback. Many community members detect the lack of effort by negative testimonial posts and react accordingly. For example, if an OP post a testimonial that reflect a negative experience and investigation shows that the individual has never posted in the forum for assistance - the responder will probably point that out. One of the many ways of potentially identifying a troll.

Opinions are like bellybuttons, most everyone has one. Providing a platform that only permits negative comments from frustrated individuals or trolls is ridiculous. Logic suggest that everyone in community should be permitted to express their opinion. Not just individuals that are willing to tolerate trolls or who will only respond in a pleasant manner. The ability to express one's opinion in conflict with a stated opinion should not be denied, assuming they stay within the forum rules and guide lines.
