### Post by Guitar John on 2010-01-03
My sister-in-law has a [Dell 1525]("http://www.dell.com/us/en/dfh/notebooks/inspnnb_1525/pd.aspx?refid=inspnnb_1525&cs=22&s=dfh") that came with factory installed Vista.  It is not that old, but it is just recently out of warranty.  It also recently crashed.  I live about 3 hours away, so she took it to someone local, who told her it had a virus.   

While she was visiting for the holidays, she brought it out here and asked me to reinstall.  I backed up her files, wiped the HDD with [DBAN]("http://www.dban.org/") and reinstalled Vista, which went pretty well.   


Since we are completely wireless here, that was an early order of business.  I installed the supplied "Drivers & Utilities."  The wireless device was still not showing up.  I noticed that all of the wireless drivers installed by the cd were Intel.  I had also noticed when I ran Xubuntu from a Live CD on that machine that the wireless device was a Broadcom 4300 chipset.  

My wife told her that she really had nothing to worry about running Ubuntu.  We've been using it for 3 years.  It would handle everything that she uses the computer for.  Etc, etc.  

Long story short (TOO LATE!!), she is happily running Ubuntu.
