### Post by chris_x1 on 2008-04-16
i started dual booting with linux about 18months ago. I tryed several different distros
including ubuntu (preferred kubuntu at the time) and generally wasn't too pleased about stuff like
the root PW, non free codecs hunting, ntfs r/w..and others, you know, like most XP users are!
but i learned quite a bit about the whole process in the meantime.

all that kind of fizzled out after a while and i went back to a single boot XP for
one reason or another.

a couple of weeks ago i decided to try linux again, and to my delight the newer releases
have even more ability out of the box and are very simple to configure, i ended up preferring
ubuntu (like the majority of linuxers it seems). i think the final straw was when i managed
to get the 3D desktop working, i thought "this is awesome!!!" it even has more "WOW" than
XP now, not that i really need 3D but it just demonstrated how developed linux desktop is
today even compared to ,say, vista.

a couple of days ago i just replaced the XP on my main drive with ubuntu and
I'm pretty chuffed that i am finally able to do so, thanks ubuntu guys! (and everyone else)

just wanted to share this..

PS. the fear of incompatibility with other windows users has become insignificant but i
keep my storage partitions in fat32 for remote access, not sure i need to though.
