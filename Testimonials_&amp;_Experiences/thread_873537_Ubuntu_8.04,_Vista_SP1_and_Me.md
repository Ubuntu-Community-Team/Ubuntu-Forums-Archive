### Post by dnzz on 2008-07-29
I have been using Ubuntu since Hoary (5.04). In this period I have changed 3 laptops and used XP, Vista, Linux.

1-Fujitsu-Siemens P4 2ghz 256mb ram 40gb hdd (XP, Ubuntu)
2-Asus Centrino 1.83Ghz 2gb ram, 80gb hdd (XP, Ubuntu, Pardus, Slax)
3-Toshiba Core 2 duo 2ghz, 2gb ram, 160gb hdd and Ati HD2600 graphics card (XP, Vista, Ubuntu)

I did not read ubuntuforums like 2 or 3 months. Today I saw that everybody wrote that Vista is too slow, unsecure etc. Now I want to tell my experience with Vista and Ubuntu.

People are right to say Vista is slow but I think Vista gets some points against Ubuntu in some way also Vista SP1 has great improvements. 

I use ReadyBoost technology (4gb Sandisk Cruzer Contour) in Vista. I googled this tech. and everybody agrees that it does not make any difference with 2gb ram. I do not agree with them. It really makes difference if you wait 10 min without activity or 30min with some disk activity. I am sorry but sometimes Vista becomes more responsive than Ubuntu 8.04.

Vista Desktop Search is very effective. When you search a text in a folder, Vista searches not only file names but also inside of the documents (I think there is still no way to search inside the documents in Ubuntu)

Management of the system is easier in Vista. I still have some problems with wireless connection in Ubuntu 8.04 (Everytime I try to change my IP in Ubuntu, connection goes for at least 5 min and I still do not know why)

I still use Ubuntu for Java development, secure internet, online shopping etc. Ubuntu is great with security, and really fast. I love tinkering Ubuntu and I will use Linux forever.

What I am tring to say is Linux is great but Vista has great feats and really not that slow. Please do not write topics with prejudice.

Thanks for reading

DT

P.S. English is my second language and I am sorry for mistakes =)
