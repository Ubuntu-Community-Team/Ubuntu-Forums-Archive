### Post by m4tic on 2010-03-09
I am leaving ubuntu after 2 years of good experiences. I use a fairly old computer, about six years now. I am leaving on because i descovered Mandriva 2010 two months back and noticed i haven't had to spend money on my broadband charges and also the fact that i forgot about ubuntu for two months. It's only now that i logged in to send this post on ubuntu as a farewell thing. I have converted four of my friends to ubuntu and they are happy, who wouldn't be? I would like Ubuntu to feel as solid as Mandriva and Windows XP. It really makes a difference and font rendering. It's not a KDE thing but just how everything is implemented, I tried Kubuntu and i really see no difference and can't see where the creativity is. Ubuntu is great but it feels like paper and not as rock solid as Mandriva and XP feels like. Some suggestions are when clicking on a button, it should behave like a button and look like it's pressed, it really would feel like the OS is alive and responsive. Ubuntu One has made me lose all data i backed up, i tried Mandriva's click'n Backup and Windows Live mesh, they all work great. Maybe if Ubuntu didn't trick everyone into trusting a service which is laughable and just stupid to be on a diefault install, stability is the key? nah, i just opened on another tab right now and keep getting ```
                                                                                                                            **Oops!**

                                 Sorry, something just went wrong in Ubuntu Single Sign On.           
                        We’ve recorded what happened,             and we’ll fix it as soon as possible.             Apologies for the inconvenience.           
                        (Error ID: 1529carambola6)           
                              
     
                      

```

Well i am not leaving because i am angry or what, just that i found something productive. looking back i have been held back because i was feeling locked in, i couldn't do certain tasks quickly and been craving to mess things up. Mandriva 2010 is a good example of how desktop linux should be, i can edit grub with a GUI tool that's already included and easy to find from the system settings, well this might be possible in Ubuntu but i haven't tried looking for a program in that 2 years, i thought menu.lst /grub.cfg was the only way. Feeling locked in is how i can't login as root on my own computer, THAT IS BULLSH*T WHOEVER CAME WITH IT. sorry but i had to say that. I will continue to spread linux but Mandriva One will be my 1st advice
