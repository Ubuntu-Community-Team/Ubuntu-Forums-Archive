I use KDE on my Gentoo computer as Gentoo is pretty KDE centric. I know they have other desktops, but most of the devs use KDE. I am so excited for 4.7! 

I just follow Ubuntu religiously, but Unity may as well do it in for me. Like it's been a great run, but with Unity I see Ubuntu sinking into obscurity, rather than being the demanding desktop it once was.

> Congrats on managing to stick with it for that long.  After upgrading  and using Unity for less than a day I ended up wiping my install and  trying Fedora with Gnome 3 and wasn't impressed with it either.   Currently running a Debian install with Gnome 2. At least Ubuntu still  offers Kubuntu and Xubuntu both of which I will be trying soon.     I wanted to really give it a fair go (currently typing this from Ubuntu +1 10.11) I really don't like where Unity is going. While a bit more refined, it's OSX-like tendancies really turn me off. It is like using fake product, or having a fake Prada purse or, like the store brand of Coco Puffs, rather than being "Ubuntu". I feel that, rather than standing out like it used to, it will be "that OSX clone". And while this may attract some new users, you will see users who were there from the beginning sort of walk off and find a new distro to lurk on. 

I really think Ubuntu needs to kill off Unity and pick up either Gnome 3 or KDE 4.7, lest it fall into obscurity. People will scoff at it, laugh at it, whatever. The problem with Unity is that it doesn't use it's own DE base, it is a derivative of GTK and it is too much of an OSX spin-off rather than being it's own thing. I really think Ubuntu should focus less on the desktop and more on the applications. I really hope the developers grow some humility, drop their pride, and realize this before it is too late for this distro, lest it be the next Linspire. KDE is really really stable if you use the proper kernel configs and such, it gives the users eye candy, and you can mold it into whatever you want it to look like, while not hand-cuffing me down and not allowing me prowess.

I think Ubuntu should use the community it has, rather than being white noise. Create some killer applications. Get off the "easy to use" kick because believe it or not, the fact that Linux is kind of hard to use is not what is keeping people from using it, its the applications!!!
