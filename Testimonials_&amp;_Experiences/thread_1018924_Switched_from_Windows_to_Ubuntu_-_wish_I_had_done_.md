### Post by Coriiander on 2008-12-22
Hey all,

This is my first post on these forums. It's going to be a happy one. For happy is the state I ended up in tonight. Because, you see, I made "The Switch". Bye Windows, hello Linux-world. Ubuntu. I am amazed. I am astonished. I feel like a total idiot for not having done this transfer before. 

I am 31 years old now; I first started programming at the age of 8. Been at it constantly since then. For some reason, once I switched to IBM-PC systems, I mainly sticked to Microsoft technologies. I even used to have 2 software development companies - also based on Microsoft technologies. Should go without saying I have respect for their products; its quality at least. Don't like to go into discussion about this, as its an everlasting flame ;).

Anyway... although I'm sure there's many immature things about Ubuntu yet, I was amazed to see its maturity once I had it installed. The installation process was SUPER easy and SUPER quick. I was afraid my NIC wouldn't work and that I'd have no access to the Internet (my provider doesn't seem to support it). But.. wow.. I didn't have to do ANYTHING - it just worked right out of the box. Same for my (NVIDIA) graphics card. All in all I was rolling in about 2 hours. Had everything updated, working, adjusted my settings, learned how to install a *.sh file lol :). Set up my new development environment - Java 6 with NetBeans IDE. Can't wait to see how it is - coming from MSVS 6.0 to 9.0. 

Anyway, so I was rolling in no time. It rocks! It's awesome! It's quick! I have it installed for about 2 hours now. The software packaging & updating systems are great. Such convenience to obtain libraries, development tools, and what not more...! Everything works, but my audio systems / studio gear. But that was to be expected. It's  quality gear (e.g. a 24-channel digital Audio I/O system from MOTU, and a MOTU XT Express MIDI-bay). It seems that for my audio productions and sound engineering I'll be stuck to Windows after all. But it's definately going onto another PC ;). Ubuntu won't be leaving for now. Especially not for development. After 23 years of MS-crap, while striving more and more away from it the past few years, its just final now. No more. I'd rather start from scratch hehe.

To cut a long story short: good job "World". I'm impressed. I'll be using some time now to make myself familiar with things (architecture etc.) and then I hope to be able to join your ranks as a developer. I'm sure a extra hand on development wouldn't hurt :).

Cheers,


Coriiander,
Ectoplasm Studios

### Post by Coriiander on 2008-12-23
> **smartboyathome said:**
> Just remember to read the bolded link in my sig, as it will help you keep from "preaching" like most new Linux converts do.

Haha. You're right on that. I'm wondering where this came from though.

I woke up this morning and launched my PC. I was about to make some coffee, but Ubuntu was already booted before I even got out of my chair :). To learn my way around I decided to look into playing around with the appearances, desktop manager, etc. Learned about the Compiz plugins - which are great. In fact all went so smooth and easily I got so sucked in to the fine-tuning I nearly forgot to feed my cats! /brb ;)

/back

One thing I like very much is the packaging system. It's so amazingly easy to obtain updates, libraries and applications. When working on Windows with MSVS it usually took alot of time to obtain and install correct 'platform-independant' libraries (QT, SDL, Boost, etc.). CVS was a horrible thing there, but here it's all nicely integrated. Packaging utilities, CVS within NetBeans, global version checking.

I am a bit confused on several things though. I see tons of C/C++ compilers. GCC, GCC debian, GCC Ubuntu, GCC for Sun Workshop, and more. Which GCC am I supposed to be taking? Currently I'm running NetBeans with GCC & G++ Ubuntu. Another thing I'm confused about is about all the GUI development libraries. I see GTK, QT, wxWidgets, and many many more. Not sure which to get. Probably doesn't matter much on the compiler-side.

For the more casual users: yes, even Office, email, mediaplayers etc. all work great. Right out of the box! In fact - email was even easier to set up than on Windows. Servers offer many choices as how to connect and login to them. Ubuntu, at least the email program "Evolution" coming with it, let's you automatically detect what the server uses (supports). Really nifty :).

I found one TERRIBLE DOWNSIDE though! I was, just to get a feel of things, playing Tetris within the CodeBlocks IDE and it told me to get back to my development work ;) I had to wait 1 hour before I could resume my play. Very funny hehe.:)


Cheers,

Coriiander,
Ectoplasm Studios
