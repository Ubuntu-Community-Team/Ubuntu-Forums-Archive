### Post by jaimeaux on 2011-12-03
Hey all,
 
   I just wanna throw my praise out there, as Ubuntu has made it easier for me to evangelize open-source software for the common user.
 
I started out with 8.10 (I think, I'm not sure, but I hope so), as my Vista box was failing, miserably. It couldn't even boot to a full gui, and it had viruses (including Vista itself!). Fed up with the situation, and with no viable alternative, I asked my roommate what to do. He installed Ubuntu for me, and it was smooth sailing from then on. 
 
At this point, I've tried other distros, and enjoyed almost all of them. I've used Fedora, Gentoo, Arch-linux(most recent), OpenSuse, VMWare, RedHat, BackTrack, and Slackware... but I've decided to come back to ubuntu after I noticed things like Ubuntu One, and Ubuntu's MASSIVE amounts of available software. I've learned that it's not the distro you use that makes you a great linux admin, it's the way you use it.
 
Thanks, Linux, and thank you specifically, Ubuntu.
 
 
Jaimeaux"
