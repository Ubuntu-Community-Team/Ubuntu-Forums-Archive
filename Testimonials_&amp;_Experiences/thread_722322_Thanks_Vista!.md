### Post by vikigal on 2008-03-12
I love Ubuntu!  Wanda and the geyes keep me company and I adore them. They may make me comfortable with the terminal yet...

I received a new computer for Christmas loaded with Vista Premium since my old one was on life-support.  It sure was pretty, however it never worked correctly (whatever correct is for vista..) and, thank heavens, died at the age of 20 days. I absolutely hated it. I returned it for a refund, and went searching for something better. And I found it.

I started at the Linux home page, clicked through to Amazon and bought a couple of books with live CD&#347;. The books were The Official Damn Small Linux Book and Ubuntu for Non-Geeks 2nd ed.  Within a week I started my search for a Linux only PC.

I felt overwhelmed by hardware issues so I cheated in a manner of speaking. I found my local computer shop and told him what I was looking for, a new computer with a Linux only os., no dual boot. He found ant IBM ThinkCentre with Ubuntu only.   We had a bit of a problem getting one that wasn´t dual boot but he kept working on it. I did not want a dual boot; I´m finished doing windows. 

Anyway, I finally got it home, plugged in my Westinghouse monitor and Logitech trackball mouse and everything was beautiful. I love a clean desktop.

I have added some programs using ¨add/remove programs¨ and had no issues, problems,  or glitches. I have been continually amazed at the number of programs available. I even found a genealogy program that I like better than the one I paid a minor fortune for. I have shared documents created with abi, saved as .doc and opened in xp with no problems.

I find everything works like I want it to work. Maybe I should say I started out my computer life using windows 98 and haven´t liked anything since, till now.

THANK YOU to all who have made it possible to enjoy my computer again.

### Post by vikigal on 2008-03-20
Hi, I´m the original poster and I´m back. 

There are a few reasons for not having dual booting: I am lazy. Which meant that if I had run into problems with Ubuntu I might have been tempted to go to the other system. I did not want windows on my computer at all. I see no need for it for me. I also believe we will continue to have windows shoved down our throats as long as people will accept/buy machines that have it pre-loaded. Microsoft doesn´t count whether you use it or not, but if is on your computer.  It would make a much better marketplace if people refused to pay for something they don´t want.

And I just didn´t want to get into what might become a hassle. I have a gift. If it can be uniquely screwed up, I can do it. Dual booting terrifies me, to be honest.

The genealogy program I found was GRAMPS genealogy system. I found it by going to ¨add/remove programs¨ in the drop down menu. Then type in ¨genealogy¨ in the Add/Remove Applications search box. I found a surprising number of programs. I am using 7.10 and it installed straight in, no problems.
