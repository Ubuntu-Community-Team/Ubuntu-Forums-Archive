### Post by LostArt on 2007-06-20
I've had linux for a while now. and after countless headaches, I've finally perfected how to handle the whole opensource experience.

Firstly I want to say the I LOVE opensource, I don't care what it is, if it's free it's good. but at first I was hesitant about linux, and it wasn't until my two friends, one obsesed with windows, the other with apple, took a breather from there long and violent war that I decided to join in on said battle for the side of Linux.

Now I'm no computer guru but I do some basic hacking, try to learn sophisticated programs, volunteer in graphic art centers, (city hall), will be the designer for my schools yearbook, and record for my band as well as sing and do an asortment of audio edditing. Now this scared me seeing that I don't see Adobe, talking about their amazing products for linux but I said ehh what the hell II'll give it a go and dove in...

After installed Linux I tried getting Beryl to work, but seeing that I have an ATI card, I just gave up after two weeks, it wasn't worth the hassel, but I kept using Linux.

After about a month Ubuntu studio came out. I was in heaven and although some of those programs confuse me, I love tinkering with them, and I'm hoping to do some drum lines and synth stuff with the audio tools.

Soon I started thinking maybe I could run ubuntu studio along side Windows and they could be friends. Bu this would not be...

First of all I hate product validation. After trying for two days to install XP, calling microsoft, calling toshiba, answering dumb questions, and dealing with people in India, (no offense to any Indians, but everyone in this forum delt with that at some time or another). I gave up, and I think that it shows how bomb linux is cause I gave Ubuntu two two three weeks of frustration, and only gave windows two days.


Lastly this forum is amazing, if it weren't for all of you guys, i don't know what I would do you all rock!!!

oh yeah, when I get a mack I'll be running linux in parrallels and windows will never touch my machine.

Finally, I even got responses to my post where I asked about Algebra Two Trig, now that is amazing
