### Post by jasonrandolph on 2007-06-28
Why not dual-boot?  Like you, I have Windows software that I want to continue to run, specifically Photoshop CS2 and iTunes.  But that doesn't mean that I need to have my entire hard drive dedicated to Vista.  I am very happy dual-booting Ubuntu and Vista.  In fact, the only time I boot up Vista is to use CS2 or iTunes.  And by dual-booting, you don't have to insert a CD every time.  Not a big deal, but I like being able to hit the power button and automatically get to the Ubuntu desktop.

There's certainly nothing wrong with running Ubuntu from the CD, but I get quite a bit of satisfaction from the fact that 99 percent of the time I'm using the 10 percent of my hard drive that Bill Gates does NOT control!

### Post by scourge on 2007-06-28
