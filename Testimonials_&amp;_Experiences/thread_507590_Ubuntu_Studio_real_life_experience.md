### Post by theorganloft on 2007-07-23
I wanted to share my real life experience of Ubuntu Studio (Feisty). 

I use the software with Audacity and K3b to record live. Here are some tips for the builders out there.

** Do not depend on the Gui to get you through configuration problems, learn text editing and from where the configuration files are for your programs and system configuration.Since I use two sound cards, one for record and one for playback, I needed to configure the system to prioritize the internal card for playback. I had to edit modules manually to do this.

** Use an approved ALSA card and driver. I had great success with Creative SB Live 5.1 and the M-Audio Delta 7.1. These are PCI cards that install automatically and sound fantastic!

** During live recording, the systems run with no latency and really show off when we have long music programs.  A CD recorder can not hold a candle to this type of power.  I can create as many tracks as I have hard disk space.  Since EXT3 does not fragment, we can delete the files and start a new session. For live recording, we use a 250Gb Sata.  This is much larger than we actually need but it leaves plenty of room for recording.

** Build your machine cool and quiet.  No off-the-shelf Dell systems here. All my machines are custom built. I need to know what is in there. I use very quiet fans and/or liquid cooling to keep the machine quiet and cool.  It will heat up during hard and long recording, especially the hard drive.  Use one drive for the OS and one for recording. Get fans for both. Do not forget to keep cable away from the Mother board. The mobo needs to be kept cool. 

** Turn of Screen savers and power management for studio work.  

** Aside from custom configurations, the package is easy to install. It is even easier if you know basic Linux administrative procedures. 

** The quality of my recordings are astounding! Add in special effects and you have nothing less than a professional recording. Many people ask how much we pay to get the quality and this is from a SB Live 5.1 card! or the M-audio Delta! :guitar:

** USE GOOD PRO-LEVEL AUDIO CABLES!! I USE MONSTER CABLES.  THE CABLES THAT THEY MAKE FOR IPODS ARE EXCELLENT!!!

** Use a compressor limiter from your mixing board. Send a +6db signal to your soundcard. Terk makes an automatic limiter used on TV sets, that is good for this too. I also use a distribution amp to share the signal with my cameras, CD recorder, cassette recorder, and TV modulator.

I just wanted to share some of my experience with you.  Good Luck!
