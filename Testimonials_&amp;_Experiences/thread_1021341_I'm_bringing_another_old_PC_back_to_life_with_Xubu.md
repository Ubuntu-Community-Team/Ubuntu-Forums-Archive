### Post by wandalalakers on 2008-12-25
A friend recently donated some old computers to me so that I can make them useful for kids.
I'm working on one that I think is a Pentium III and 600mhz or so.
(X86 family 6 model 9 stepping 3)
I'm going to install Xubuntu on this desktop.
Long story short, I was able to get Humongous Entertainment's "Spy Fox" to work with linux.  This is great!  This desktop will be for a first grader.  Instead of giving her the desktop with win98 on it, I'll be installing linux on it.
Thanks to your great software, she can play some windows educational games in addition to the linux educational games!  She will also have a pc with an updated operating system.  The desktop doesn't come with a network card, so I'll be installing one from my spare parts.
For those of you who don't know who "Spy Fox" is here you go:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spy_Fox](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spy_Fox)

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freddi_Fish](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freddi_Fish)

Atari Games bought the company a few years back and now Backyard Sports owns the company.  These old games were really fun to play, even as an adult.  These games are not made anymore but I highly recommend them for ages 3 - 10.  I also purchased Putt Putt, Pajama Sam and Freddi Fish a few years back.
