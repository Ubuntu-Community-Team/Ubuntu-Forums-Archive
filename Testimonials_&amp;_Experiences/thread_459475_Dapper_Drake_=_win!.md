### Post by No Whammies on 2007-05-30
A while back, I switched from Windows XP to Ubuntu for both philosophical and practical reasons.  I grew tired of unnamed processes using my modem to send undisclosed information to "home base".  I was sick of having programs that were visually attractive, but in reality offered either tons of lame options or a ton of general uselessness that I had to sift through to get my work done.  I was also sick to death of supporting a company I felt was getting away with criminal activity (their theft of "intellectual property" is much more grievous than  some kid downloading an album...  or five)  So I left.  I took my files and walked over to Ubuntu.  After a five hour session (which would have been two without all the beer...  darn college buddies!) my 256 MB RAM AMD computer-equivalent of a Honda Civic was up and running better than ever!  I was so HAPPY.

Until about a month ago.  It was coming up on the end of a rough few months (including a home fire) and my parents, in their ultimate awesomeness spring for a computer.  Of course, this was done as a surprise so i could replace my old, scorched box.  As a result, I couldn't help them make a good choice and they went to QVC and got a new Dimension C521...  With Vista.

Needless to say, my second act (after kissing mom on the cheek, hugging dad and enjoying the family unity) was to fire it up with Feisty Fawn.  MISTAKE.  Nothing worked.  i hooked everything up, it ran, then when I had to reboot...  HARD crash with no hope of recovery.  After 12 reinstalls and trying everything I could read about (ATI apparently hates Linux), I finally hung my head in defeat, got the blue boot disc from Redmond and booted.

It all worked fine, but it was like a stretching out a relationship with a  one night stand a few weeks after breaking up with the girl you thought you were going to marry.  Pleasant, a decent diversion...  but, dammit, it just ain't RIGHT.   I ran Vista for a full month.  I had a few minor issues, which were fixed by a Dell tech with that service where they control your computer from another country (No, I did NOT like that at all!) and only 2 blue screens of death (no one heard my screams).  A brief vacation period started up for me this past week.  I sat down, looked at Windows and finally just said ENOUGH.  I resolved to make this work, or trade this box in for a new one.

I worked with Feisty for a FULL day, 24 hours with no joy.  Then it hit me.  Actually, I hit it!  As I kicked random objects around my room, I kicked a 35 pound weight, screamed , fell on my bookcase and The ubuntu Book fell off the shelf, landed in my lap and opened to the Dapper DVD!   I installed Dapper, hooked up fglrx and waited.  I rebooted,  no problem.  I made updates and rebooted...  No problem!  I installed my usual programs and rebooted just to see what would happen.  NO PROBLEM.  IT WORKS!!  :guitar::guitar::guitar:

I've rebooted about 12 times since just to be sure, but everything looks great!  I'm going to stick with Dapper for now, until the next LTS.  I'll leave the pointy end to the more adventurous among you ;)  .   Now, I'm happily back with Ubuntu...  And everything, from my MFC 7820 printer to my digital camera to my video card  just works.  :KS
