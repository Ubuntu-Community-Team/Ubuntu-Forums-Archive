### Post by jbysmith on 2008-10-29
Welcome aboard --

Yea there's tons of help available for Ubuntu (or Linux in general). When I first started I had tons of questions, but nothing that a quick Google search didn't answer. Just keep an open mind; pretty much on a daily basis I'm still learning something new, especially if its console related.  (I just *love* the Linux console)  Just a suggestion, if you get stuck, make sure you've researched your problem first.  Chances are the answer is a quick search away; some people get annoyed with questions that the user obviously hasn't even bothered trying to find an answer for first.

Also keep in mind Ubuntu is just one distro.  Need to find the one that works best for you.  My main "personal desktop" system is running Ubuntu 8.10.  My laptop is running Arch Linux in a pure console environment.  (I *hate* touchpads) My servers are mostly running the server version of 8.04, and I have a test system running Debian Sid. And that's just tip of the iceberg. 

If anything, install a copy of VirtualBox or VMWare, and head over to Distrowatch.com.  Browse through the various distros and give them a spin, find what works good for you. I'm partial to the Debian/Ubuntu flavor of things, but Fedora, OpenSUSE, Mandriva etc are pretty strong too.  Also a huge fan of Arch if you like to tinker; stupidly fast but not for the first timer though.  But if you want to learn, its an excellent way to go as you pick up how things work under the hood rather quickly. 

Also, there's different window managers to choose from too.  Ubuntu runs Gnome by default.  There's also KDE, XFCE, Enlightenment, etc etc.  Take a look at xwinman.org for some ideas.  

Just keep in mind though that just because it works in a VM, may not get the expected results live. I'm currently experimenting with FreeBSD (which is not Linux).  Installed great in a VM, but on my one junker test machine, it absolutely refuses to install. Something about drive parameter translations keeps messing up the boot partition. Still tinkering.

Personally though, I can strongly recommend Ubuntu 8.10 RC.  Fast, stable, and pretty easy to get started with.  At least on my hardware, a huge improvement over 8.04.
