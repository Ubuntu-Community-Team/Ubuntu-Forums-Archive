### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-04-25
There are many games available for Linux, although you'll probably have to take a drop in graphics capabilities. I suppose the biggest advantage is that most are free!

However, did you know you can get many Windows games and programs to run on Linux? Take a look at [Wine](http://winehq.org), or [Cedega](http://www.transgaming.com/index.php?module=ContentExpress&func=display&ceid=2&meid=-1),  which is a version of Wine which is more focused for games. You have to pay a small subscription fee for Cedega though, but you can get a free version by downloading the source and compiling it yourself through CVS. A guide to doing this is [here](http://www.linux-gamers.net/modules/wiwimod/index.php?page=HOWTO%20Cedega%20CVS).
