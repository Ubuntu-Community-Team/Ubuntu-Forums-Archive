### Post by MrMessiah on 2007-03-06
Hi

I have decided to make the switch to Linux and that Ubuntu would be the vehicle for me to do this. I'm doing this now, because of the way Windows is going, but I'm also doing it slowly, and dual booting, because I still need to use Windows for at least 50% of what I do, which is gaming. 

My experience of my first 24 hours of Ubuntu is how much smoother the process is than the last time I looked at doing this, not so many years ago. For a beginner, the prospect of changing OS is daunting, but the ability to "try out" on the Live CD and effortlessly install from there took a lot of that pressure away, and I have to commend the experience from that point of view, it's a great aid to migration.

The process wasn't entirely without it's niggles; the graphical performance on my machine - reasonably new PC but with an ATI graphics card - was poor "out of the box" and indeed it made some of the installation a little slow. As I'm in the UK, I was faced with slowly scrolling down a huge list of timezones or clicking on a map which then took about 2 minutes to zoom into. Also the partitioning tool took a long time to do its thing, as did the first boot: because I have 9 FAT32 partitions I wanted to be able to use, (the curse of a machine that has grown over time) it was doing a full fsck on every boot. As I have a little Linux (non-desktop) knowledge I was able to hit up Google and sort that out in short order.

The desktop experience is great, and from someone who remembers having to sift through .RPMs with a huge ream of printed out instructions from someone who knew what they were doing, the ability to Automatically update painlessly was great. I actually loved the way it gave you the least threatening amount of info as it updated, but you could view the individual updates if you so wanted to - that's the right way of doing things IMHO.

Bundled apps, very slick compared to what I've been used to. As a desktop to go in an office and make up a work machine I'd have no issues with rolling that out to non-technical people, and you just know I hate putting myself in the firing line when it comes to IT support. :)

My only lasting gripe with it, so far, is that graphics issue. Like I said, though I'm familiar with certain aspects of Linux I'm still a newbie when it comes to desktops, and it's here I felt the difficulty curve ramp right up. My aim is to run Windows games through Wine and get myself involved with that project, as I have a certain amount of coding experience in Windows, and that's what we all have to do, right? Help out in what way we can? To do THAT I need to be able to get 3d acceleration on my dual-screen setup, and I'm not in a position to be able to upgrade my ATI X1600 at the moment. This I'm having a real problem with on so many levels. From what I've read it's a hot potato at the moment and I don't want to get bogged down in politics (on my first post!) but I really felt like how to go about doing this - if it's at all possible - was hard to find.

I'm going to stick with it for now - I'm sure the answers are out there.
