Hello all. I tried a Linux distro once before, and wasn't very satisfied. Now that I believe one of my drives is going out (as I have a 200gb main & 75gb slave (this is one going out), I decided to use my slave as the "Linux test-experiment". I was originally thinking about kororaa (based on gentoo, and hopefully I don't get in trouble for mentioning this - even though it is public knowledge), but after installation problems involving grub, etc. - I decided to give something else a try. I just happened to burn an ISO of Ubuntu on the side "just in case." :) 

My first problem was (and still is to an extent) video graphics driver support. I've gotten the glx thingee installed and it's all supported now, and haven't had any probs with 3d-apps so far (as far as I can tell). I'm still desiring to do tv-out, but am giving up on that right now because the research I have to do for everything mounds up on me (*sigh*). But, after the work is done - it's worth it.

Coming from Windows, and aspiring to be more free, yet also to have some nice little apps (specifically widget-like) this was a good choice. I'll rather shoot myself than move to Mac (seriously), but for now I like running dual-boot for Ubuntu 64x and Windows Xp Prof (32-bit ...the 64-bit is too "quirky" still IMO).

I'll include some screenies at the end of this post, but - I've finally configured WINE decently, and found out exactly how to keep my E-Sword along with the many Bibles, commentaries, and books I've downloaded for it. I've probably got 20-50 extra books in topical format, so I wasn't looking to switching to another software, though I have downloaded BibleTime to check it out. That reminds me, I put a link for E-Sword, but forgot about IE link for my desktop (and I'm wondering if I have an IE7 dl I could sneak on somehow?).

At any rate - I like the free alternatives (most definitely), especially gimp (and I must admit - I was starting to use Gimpshop on Windows), but it's too bad Gimp nor Gimpshop still have many of the options that Photoshop CS2 does (Gimp still seems a little "rough" around the edges). I still have some questions, and I'm trying to make sure to visit as many forum posts and places as I can before asking (I know everyone hates redundant questions, especially without any research).
