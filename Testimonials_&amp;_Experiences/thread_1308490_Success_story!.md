### Post by aa4bb on 2009-10-31
I just want to share how ecstatic I am that Ubuntu is giving me such great satisfaction that I never achieved in Windows or on the Mac, both of which I have used quite a bit.

Specifically, I have made great leaps in organizing my photos and music. I have thousands of digital images and music tracks that I've accumulated over the years. I *kinda* knew where things are, in multiple folders over several computers. Each time I've started to get organized, I ran into software like iTunes and iPhoto that seemed opaque to me, though I'm pretty computer literate.

Ubuntu for me started as an experiment to repurpose an old computer for my step-daughter, who can't afford a new computer. But I'm hooked, and have installed Ubuntu on every computer in the household, including my wife's laptop. The results are phenomenal!

Using F-spot (great triple entendre! - the sense of humor is marvelous), I've organized my photo collection so I can now find photos I want quickly, rather than spend hours searching manually. Also, my wife can easily crop and print her photos herself, instead of waiting for me to get around to it.

I'm a musician. I have ripped many a CD and transferred many a cassette to digital, mostly with Audiograbber. Unfortunately, I did this over a period of time and wasn't as systematic in editing the tags as I should have been.

EasyTag has been a true blessing. In just a few hours, I have cleaned up the tags on my entire collection. Bringing the tracks into Rhythmbox, they now show up with a coherence that I previously only dreamed of.

I'm dual booting Vista and Ubuntu on two computers and XP/Ubuntu on a third. I don't think I'll be "upgrading" to Windows 7. Ubuntu gurus, I can't thank you enough. I'll be passing the virtues of Ubuntu along to everyone who will listen.
