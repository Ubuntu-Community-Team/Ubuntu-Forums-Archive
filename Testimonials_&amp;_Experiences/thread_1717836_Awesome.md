### Post by Arleas on 2011-03-30
Ubuntu:

Control. Freedom. Community.

For me, it was an absolute disaster. I've tried to install Ubuntu on various computers since version 8.10, but I'd been thwarted every time. If I could get it to install, which was rare, I would then be presented with some seemingly incurable illness which would in turn reduce my enthusiasm to a bed-ridden, feverish patient, who, in between sweats, would mutter something about 'command line' and 'repositories'.

With the updated look in 10.04 and 10.10, I decided, once again, to give it 'one more go'.

*It wouldn't install.*

I'd resurrected an old Dell with a battered version of Windows Home on it. Without wanting to remove my partner's previous OS and all her files (although long discarded), I burned a bootable partitioning CD to try and resize the partitions. This was another epic adventure. The boot disk was a little worse for wear, and the stress involved with curing bad sectors started to mirror the damage caused to my mind.

*Just use the Wubi installer*. Of course! It worked.

I finally had a working version of Ubuntu. But I didn't know how to use it.

Once again, I retreated to the familiarity of Mac OS and the machine sat, dormant, under my desk. Until last week.

I wrestled with updating Firefox. Using the terminal. By following guides. And won.

I installed Spotify under Wine. Dropbox. Blender.

Added repositories.

I even *compiled a driver* for a Wacom tablet *which actually works*.

**And it's all thanks to you guys.** Thanks for being helpful, writing guides and supporting the community so buffoons like me can have a crack at a great operating system.
