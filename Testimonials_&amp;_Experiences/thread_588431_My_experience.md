### Post by Dreamy1 on 2007-10-23
I was tempted to try Linux back in January, however I was rather intimidated by Linux and how tough it usually is for newbies, I visited the Ubuntu website several times and often thought about getting the OS.

But being rather busy I had held it off for a couple of months but decided to further research into this distribution once again, after reading several articles online and on magazines I was impressed with what I read, so I went to my ISP to download to desktop version of Ubuntu 6.10, since my ISP offers un-metered downloads to its members it was too good to resist.

I waited for about an hour to download one early morning, followed the directions to burn the ISO image onto a CD and it was seamless, I had no problems whatsoever!

I checked the disk for errors then I rebooted, and decided to give a live CD a try, when I saw the interface for the first time, it was love at first sight! honest. I had played around, tested different themes and even went to see what was installed, It was truly a breath of fresh air for me. What blew me over was a semi-functional version of the OS on a CD!!!

I decided to install it the next day, went online for some help for installation advice and in half an hour tops my installation was working! even the install process only took ten minutes, I managed to correctly partition the hard drive and successfully created a dual boot between Windows XP and Ubuntu, After this I customized my theme and the panels, but it got better from there. But I was very scared before this because I thought that I might do something silly and wipe my whole hard drive. But doing it slowly and thoroughly it worked like a charm.

I soon found out that Ubuntu has a download service to install extra software, I found over a dozen stuff and I was on my way to downloading, this was very impressive for me, as OSes like Windows don't offer that so it was really cool. I am also impressed that Ubuntu already has Firefox, OpenOffice and Evolution already installed, these three crucial things I use everyday and they are already there in Ubuntu!

To sum it up, I am amazed things went so well despite the fact I am a newbie to Linux, this experience was truly something special, and even to this day I still use my Linux daily, Also what astounds me is that Ubuntu is free of charge!

Yesterday I deleted my whole Linux partition and started again, it took only two hours to re-figure out (since I had forgotten) how to partition and install, everything went smoothly. I really like the Improvements with Gutsy, now I don't have to reset the resolution everytime I logged in and, my refresh rate stays the same, these things really bugged me in the last release and I am glad they are fixed, Compiz Fusion is nice too!

Thumbs up for me! :mrgreen:
