### Post by veroslav on 2011-02-01
I have read and posted in these forums for quite a while now and wanted to write something about my experience with Ubuntu during the last few months.

It all begun by "accident" when one of my collegues at work brought his Ubuntu-laptop with him to work in order to run "alien" (we needed to convert between rpm and deb). Prior to this I've never heard of Ubuntu and only a little about Gnu/Linux. For me, it was just a command line OS. It is painful to admit it even though I work as an IT-consultant.

Immediately, I was struck with the great looks of this Ubuntu-thing, I think it was Ubuntu 9.04. I got curious and started searching the web and found this forum and started reading. A lot. After two weeks of reading and analysing what was posted here by other people, I was ready to make a switch.

I've never had any problems with XP I was running on my laptop but I am a curious person so I guess that was the reason I decided to give it a try. One night, out of nowhere, I just put the Live CD and begun installing. I burned the Live CD a while before that and played alot with it. I thought I would go with a dual boot, but I had some difficulties with this option so I went for a clean Ubuntu install. Boy was I right!

I've never been happier with a computer during my whole life! No problems whatsoever, everything was smooth, the boot time took seconds, it was pretty, a bunch of free applications... I was so happy that I didn't choose to dual-boot, as I dont play games and do not really need Windows for anything.

After a few months, my father, who is not really into computers and stuff, started to pay attention and be interested in this Ubuntu thing. He liked the look of my "new" computer. It was the usual thing: my parents were always complaining about the slowness of their computer (running Windows XP for the last 8 years, withouth ever reinstalling it, it was REALLY slow). I told him that I would gladly help him install Ubuntu if he ever wanted me to do it, but also mentioned that it might not work as Windows and that there could be some (compatibility) issues. My father was tired of slowliness and all the security warnings provided by antivirus/firewall so that was not an issue for him.

On 24 dec, suddently he said to me "Lets have a go!". So I inserted the Live CD and begun the installation. He did not wanted to dual boot. The installation took a while (2 hours) and I was a little terrified I admit, because I thought something was not right. It was! Everything worked as it should I now my parents were running Ubuntu! I was told that it was a best Christmas present my parents ever received! :)

My father had thanked me so many times since then, that I helped him install this Ubuntu thing and he actually said that it was easier to use then Windows. He is not planning to go back to Windows, and neither am I.

I am happier for each day that goes by, for my newly discovered freedom and also kind of angry/sad that I didn't knew about Ubuntu/GNU/Linux before. I would also like to thank this beautiful community for their outstanding support whenever I had any issues (those were very few by the way).

The next step for me is to buy a new, more powerful computer, so currently I am looking around at what is available. Guess which OS I am installing after I bought it?

Kind Regards,
Veroslav
