### Post by indigoshift on 2006-08-14
I figured I'd might as well stick my testimonial in here, as well, seeing as how all the hip kids are doin' it.

I started using computers when I'd come up with any excuse I could to go over to my friend's house and mess around with his Commodore 64.  I'd played with hex programming on the Odyssey 2 when I was a kid (which really gives away what an old man I really am), but it wasn't until the C64 that I really gained an interest in computers.

The C64 gave way to the Amiga, with its Unix-like CLI and mind-blowing audio and graphic capabilities.  I stuck with the Amiga until about 1998, when I went back to college and needed a Windows box.

(I don't own my first Windows machine any more, but still have a pair of Amiga 500+ computers safely tucked away in my garage)

Soon, I was using Windows exclusively, and not really enjoying myself (well, until I bought Half-Life).  I still missed the hell out of my Amigas.

Now, I'm working in IT, and constantly answering questions about XP, Outlook, Excel...I thank Microsoft every single day for helping me put food on the table, but still can't get comfortable with the OS itself.  I don't like an OS that tries to act like it's smarter than I am.  I may not be the brightest bulb on the marquee, but I'm tired of clicking the "Yes, I really want to do this!" buttons that seem to pop up everywhere in Windows.

A friend of mine who's a long-time Linux user kept trying to get me to switch, but why would I want to figure out yet another OS?  I really didn't feel the need, until Ubuntu.

I like how it's as point-and-click or as CLI as you want it to be--just like my old Amiga.  I also love how some kind soul over at gnome-look.org created an Amiga OS4 theme, which I'm using now.

It's nice to be comfortable with a computer again.  I mean, sure, there are some things that need extra attention (like the antiquated external wireless card on the old Evo N600c I'm typing on now), but I always enjoy learning new things.

I don't know what Ubuntu is to the rest of you, but for me it's like having The Amiga That Never Was, But Should Have Been.  I go back to my Windows machine to play some video games (Half-Life 2, Battlefield 2, that sort of thing), but this has become my primary OS.  And in such a short time, too!  I was amazed that it was so easy to take to.

Blah blah blah.  That's my testimonial.  Any other old Amiga fans lurking about?  ;)
