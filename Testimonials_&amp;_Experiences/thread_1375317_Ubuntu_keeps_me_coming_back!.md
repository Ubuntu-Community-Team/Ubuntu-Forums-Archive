### Post by wideyes on 2010-01-07
I'll start this by saying that Ubuntu is not my favorite distro. It was the first linux I ever installed (Gutsy Gibbon, seems so long ago), and it is what got me started on the road to linuxdom. But I have had more banging my head against the wall dealing with Ubuntu and Grub than I care to explore here...

... But I can't seem to stay away from this OS! It really has been the most usable distro, overall, that I've encountered so far. I use Debian Lenny at work, which is currently my favorite distro, but Debian installation is still pretty poorly implemented imho, considering what a giant it is (but I'm no expert, certainly!). I've had repeated annoyances with its installer that other OSes (like the the 9 year old Windows XP) seem to have conquered long ago. Xandros came installed on my eeepc, and after *lots* of tweaking I still didn't dig it, so it's toast. Puppy is cute, but very proprietary and a bit limited (although I really *wanted* to like it). I also took Easy Peasy and Mint for a whirl, but neither of them were as functional as good old plain jane Ubuntu. There is a simple unity to Ubuntu that I like, and I just keep coming back to it. Karmic Koala has solved just about all my complaints, and runs superbly on my netbook. Ubuntu's installer is kickass, and Grub2 has treated me very much better than Grub Classic (which I have often fantasized about nailing to the wall). I am developing the opinion that a strong installer is key to bringing new users into the fold. An end user is less likely to invest their time in a piece of software if they have to troubleshoot it just to reach the desktop, especially with so much competition in the Linux world.

For once, I might just stick to Ubuntu and stop the search altogether.

...For now.
