### Post by Tamlynmac on 2009-03-26
I often get a kick out of posts like this. "I'm leaving and want everyone to know", "I don't want to share why, just want everyone to know". :)

The selection and use of any software is a personal choice and often hardware dependent. Why should a community member care which OS an individual uses, if said OS provides them with the best computing experience and meets all their requirements? _I for one, am pleased when any user finds a solution to their computing problem(s)_. Even if said solution includes the use of an alternative OS. It's been my experience, that the majority of OS problems are directly related to hardware compatibility.

I can't count how many times I've read postings in this forum, advising individuals to "Use What Works For them". Yet some still feel the need to share their decision with regards to using a different OS, as if seeking permission. So insecure, that they must announce their decision in hopes that someone will provide them with justification to either continue using Ubuntu or switching to an alternative. As witnessed in this section, some individuals attempt to circumventing the assistance sections in an effort to acquire instant gratification by announcing their intent to uninstall Ubuntu. I find this type of behavior disgusting and a belief in entitlement (not suggesting that's the intent of this OP).

The primary objective of any OS is to provide the user with a platform from which to run applications. Hence, the selection of an OS should be fundamentally based on user requirements and functionality. Of course, system hardware compatibility would be mandatory in assuring functionality.

Many will probably regard this post as a flame, when in actually I'm simply pointing out the obvious (from my perspective).
