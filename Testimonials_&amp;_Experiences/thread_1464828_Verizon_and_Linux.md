### Post by blackbelt_jones on 2010-04-28
Uh... bump.

I've been using Verizon for about six years, and I've been using Linux all that time.  Verizon has always been incredibly unhelpful, and sometimes even rude, but my experience has always been that when you activate the DSL with Windows, Linux works on the network.  It's a simple act of enabling of the modem, a turning on of the switch, and then the DHCP works fine.  The last time I signed up with Verizon, I simply installed Windows XP from an old disk I had, ran their software, activated the modem, erased the Windows system, and proceeded to run just about every Linux distro I could think of with for the next four years with no trouble.  You boot Linux, you're online.  It runs trouble free, and it's a good thing because you might as well not calling Verizon with a problem with your Linux machine.   


Around January, my modem stopped working. I had another modem (the same model) that my son had given me when he switched to Road Runner, and ever since then, I haven't been able to enable the modem for DHCP.  I've been able to bypass the problem with a text based program called pppoeconf, or a GUI version called pppoe-config that runs on Sidux.  Stable Debian based-distros seem to require that I install a few more packages, which I get off the Debian DVD.  Not knowing much about networking, I haven't yet been able to get pppoe working with any non-debian based distro.  Now, if you were to be limited to any family of distros, you'd certainly want it to be the deb-untus, but I've always liked checking out other distros, especially Slackware, and had even released my own Slax based live CD which I have been unable to get online.  I tried installing a pirated XP on my computer, but I seem to be missing some video card driver, and there are resolution problems when I try to run the software.  I can only see the upper left hand corner of the window, and that's not where the buttons are located.  

And now I see that Verizon is exclusively handling Microsoft's new social networking phone, the KIN.

[http://www.facebook.com/KIN?v=app_111573805528394&CID=OFFNET_PreLaunch_SEMGoogle](http://www.facebook.com/KIN?v=app_111573805528394&CID=OFFNET_PreLaunch_SEMGoogle)
  

I think that Verizon's boycott of Linux is willful and anti-competitive, possibly illegal, and needs to end.  This is absolutely a battle worth fighting, but that battle may not be necessary when Chrome OS, which u8ses a Linux kernel, is released.  If Verizon chooses to boycott Google, THEN you're going to see a battle.  

Either way, I'm leaving Verizon for my internet access, and also my home phone, and my cell phone.

### Post by 3Miro on 2010-04-29
When I first set up my Verizon DSL modem, I was dual booting Mandrake and Windows 2000. I had to do it over the phone, however, since their software did not agree with my ZoneAlarm firewall or something.

When I set up Comcast cable modem, it could not be done on-line from Linux and it did not work from within VirtualBox. I called them, the tech guy said that they don't support Linux, but he can activate the modem for me. Other than that, I was on my own. Once activated, the modem worked instantly.

I guess you can just call Verizon and say that their software doesn't work w/o giving details. They will probably just activate it for you. Other than that, I guess you can tell them that you will take your business somewhere else (that is assuming you have another broadband provider in your area).

I don't know about ppope, I have only used DHCP. I think I did it once, but it was long time ago.
