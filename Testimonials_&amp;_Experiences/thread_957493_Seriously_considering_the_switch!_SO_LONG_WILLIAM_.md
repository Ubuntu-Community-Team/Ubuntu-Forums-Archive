### Post by Tu1J4kXk8NUhMz on 2008-10-24
Then again, Bill Gates only works at Microsoft as talent now. Smart? You be the judge.

Anyhow, I've noticed the couple of times I've looked at this board, the Titles all read "Hate Ubuntu" or "See ya Ubuntu!" or whatever...a lot of negativity. I thought I'd interject a little positive nature! After all, it's easy to criticize that which we don't understand. And if you've been a Windows user all your life (like me) it's easy not to understand. Damn Bill Gates for brainwashing us!

Don't get me wrong...Ubuntu has not been easy to "get." Then again, I didn't expect it to be easy. But the difference between Ubuntu and Windows (at least in my opinion) is that Ubuntu is difficult in a "how can I get this to work?" kind of way. Windows is difficult in a "WHY THE #&$@ WON'T YOU WORK YOU PIECE OF !#@$@$" kind of way. At least with Ubuntu, I can't imagine anyone sitting in an executive chair chuckling, saying over and over "Suckers!" (I'm looking at you, Bill)

I made the move to Mac OS X two years ago. Smartest move I ever made. However, I have necessities in Windows (3ds Max, Torque, etc.) and so I kept it in waiting in Boot Camp. I found Ubuntu about 6 months ago, but knew that I had neither the time nor the energy to learn a new OS. Last month, I finally took the plunge and downloaded Ubuntu Studio. After learning that, even though this is geared directly for "my kind of people," it wasn't my flavor, I downloaded Ubuntu "vanilla" (as I like to call it).

History aside, I'm happy I did it. For the first time EVER, I find myself questioning the need for Windows. In fact, if it weren't for Wine's inability to handle 3ds Max (soon?), I would be Windows-free right now! In fact, shortly, I plan on reducing my Windows partition on my MBP to bare minimum!

I've never been happier with my computer! Mac OS X and Ubuntu are, by far, the most intuitive OS's on the market today, Mac edging out. However, what Ubuntu lacks in out-of-the-box functionality, it easily makes up for in customization and support! If something don't work with Mac, you need to Google it or find a Mac Genius. As far as free support goes, if you can't find it on Google, you're SOL. Meanwhile, if I can't figure something out on Ubuntu, all I need to do is turn to UbuntuForums. If I can't find the answer, then I can ask someone else. Many times, within the hour, I have responses! Usually, ones that actually work!

So anyway, I think I'm done here. I thought that I'd let you folks at Ubuntu central (wherever that may be) you're doing a great job! And to all you folks who keep coming up with open source alternatives to Windows and Mac OS software, keep up the great work!

Intrepid Ibex comes out in less than a week! :D
