### Post by sheol on 2008-02-28
My girldfiend has never owned a computer of her own before,  She knows almost nothing about computers.  She recently got a Thinkpad T30, and she decided she would install Ubuntu on it.  

I told her I wasn't going to do anything except when she was really lost.  I had a Kubuntu CD and so she put it in and booted it up.

She got through fine until she got to the part about partitioning the hard drive.  I explained partitioning to her and helped her decide on a size.

When it booted in there was an error in the menu's showing up something like "KDE menu item:" before each menu item.

We went ahead and did an upgrade and I had to explain about repositories and upgrades.

The upgrade broke and the computer did a kernel panic upon boot.

We started over and did it all again, this time it was the recent KDE language pack bug that wouldn't start the KSM server

So I installed Xubuntu-desktop.

Then she  had no problems until trying to connect to an encryupted wireless connection, and I had to explaine choosing the correct encryption key.

Otherwise so far she is happy with the OS.

So in summary the following were the only issues with an install from a completely computer illiterate person:

1. bugs
2. Understanding packages
3. Understanding repositories
4. Understanding keys when connecting.
