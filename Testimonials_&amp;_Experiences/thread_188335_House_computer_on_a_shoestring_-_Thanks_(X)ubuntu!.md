### Post by smittyinside on 2006-06-03
I just moved into a cooperative house and I saw that we had no public computer for everyone to use (some people don't have a computer for financial reasons). I had an older P4 1.4 GHz machine I had gotten for free from a client that I wasn't using at home, as it only had 128 MB of RAM (RDRAM is expensive, so I couldn't swing the upgrade). I saw the potential to showcase Ubuntu Dapper Drake (the Xubuntu variety) and I took it - turned out wonderfully. We now have web browsing, IM, office suite (OpenOffice runs decent on it), etc., for a total cost of $0. No one has complained about the machine and based on the Firefox history, it is being used.

The GNU/Linux philosophy is very much in line with our cooperative philosophy, so it was a win-win situation. Cheers to (X)ubuntu! =D>

### Post by smittyinside on 2006-06-03
It runs suprisingly well. I enabled prelinking on it and that seems to have added a little pep as well. I even have it do Folding@Home 24/7 and that doesn't bother usage at all. I would not have put GNOME on here - 128 MB of RAM is not enough for speedy usage of Metacity.

With regards to the porno question - it is in the main living room, with the monitor facing the hallway, so it would take some major chutzpah to start surfing for porn - but that's not my problem I suppose. We're in college (meaning all residents are 18+), so they can make their own big kid decisions.
