### Post by ScottinSoCal on 2010-02-05
I switched to Ubuntu at home several months ago. I'm by no stretch of the imagination a *nix whiz, but Ubuntu just does what it's supposed to, so I don't have to be.

My sister just bought a new notebook, and the first thing she did was bring it to me to install Ubuntu on it. She hated Windows, and had seen my laptop and wanted it. She's no whiz at anything computer, but she loves it and I haven't had to support a thing.

On Monday my work notebook took 90 minutes to boot. Updates, patches, rebooting, more updates and patches, rebooting, updated virus definition files, rebooting... I got fed up with it and talked to IT - told them I wanted Ubuntu on my work machine. They gave me a long list of "Yeah, but...", but finally agreed, with one last parting shot. "Yeah, but you know you're on your own for support." No I'm not - I've got this whole forum. Ubuntu installed flawlessly, I had a little trouble getting Active Directory/Likewise-open/Samba all coordinated and working together, but since then, I *love* my work machine. And so do a lot of my coworkers, enough of them that I got a terse phone call from IT, telling me if anyone asks about having it installed on their machine, I'm supposed to discourage them and tell them it can't be done. Instead I'm telling them to try it at home.

Ubuntu rocks.
