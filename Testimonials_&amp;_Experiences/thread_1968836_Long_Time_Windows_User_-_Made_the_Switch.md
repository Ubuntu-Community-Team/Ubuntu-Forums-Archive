### Post by cloyd800 on 2012-04-29
I figured I'd give my own testimonial for anyone sitting on the fence based on a switch to a linux distro.  

**_My Story_**

Up until about a month ago I considered myself a person that was pretty good with computers.  Family, friends and co-workers would come to me with various issues regarding their home or work PC's and I could pretty much fix anything. 

I took a new position at work a month ago, and was shocked to find out that about 80% of the work was directly editing databases and working in RedHat environments, never touching linux before in my life(I work in the Healthcare IT field). 

With the weight of the new position on my shoulders, and the pressure to be up to speed within a few months from now I decided to start digging as fast as I could to learn this new land.  I had always heard horror stories -- about the linux community and how unforgiving they were -- and it was a bit overwhelming to say the least.  

A couple of night's later while playing around on my desktop testing out Windows 8, it decided to crash on me.  It wouldn't allow me to reinstall 8, so I went with my $300 bought copy of Windows 7 Ultimate - Which wouldn't install either.  After going back and forth with Microsoft Customer Support for the rest of the evening and them telling me that I had already installed all the copies that they would allow with a single purchased distro(Which was bull, because this was my second reinstall).  I said FU, and went browsing.  

I came upon Ubuntu, and decided to give it a shot based on me needing to learn the linux environment anyway for work.  I was shocked, it was rather easy to use and the community was nothing like the horror stories I had heard from others.  

Now, after a month of using Ubuntu(Just upgrading to 12.04) and working in the environment at work, I have to say I'm quite pleased with the results.  For once, I've felt like I actually have the power and tools to fix my own issues instead of hunting around in various GUI based filesystems trying to figure out what files coincided with what Windows applications...and even better...that !$#!%% registry system that's on Windows OS is a thing of the past :)

I now only use a Windows OS for one of my laptops at work due to varying application support, but don't think I'll be leaving the Linux community for a very long time :)

Thanks to all of you for making all the distros what they are today!

### Post by thekat on 2012-04-30
All my servers run either CentOS or Scientific Linux 6.x, however; I run Ubuntu on both my work and home Desktop.  

CentOS / RHEL are what I call 'conservative' long term stable desktops that 'servers' really need.  

Some folks do run CentOS /RHEL as their desktops but since I do a wide range of tasks (sounds like you will also) I prefer a more flexible Distro.  
Also, adding new tools / widgets on Ubuntu is more readily available (read: in most cases easier) than in CentOS / RHEL...

It is all about choice and what your needs are...
just my .02

I will tell you that in my short 10 years of Linux's and BSD's

- Ubuntu (and variants like Kubuntu, Mint) is the simpliest 
to update and add new packages (hence my decision for both home and 
work) 
I spend much less time 'working on my box' and more time 'doing work that I need to get done'...
- OpenBSD is the best firewall (but not the friendliest to get help on)
and there is a lot of 'working on the firewall' (but you learn a lot)
- Pfsense is the best purpose built GUI firewall (based on Freebsd using OpenBSD's stellar packet filter)
- PCBSD (last tried a few years ago , I love zfs) did not support my
hardware and did not have all the packages I needed.
- CentOS / Scientific Linux are the best free 'servers' in the Linux arena (hence why all my servers at work are running) RHEL did not support DNSSEC (which I had to have) , most all of my servers have a very minimal install and are all command line.  
Less is more in my case.
