### Post by wj32 on 2007-04-08
I've used Windows from as far back (which isn't very far back) as I can remember, when I was using Windows 98 to play Red Alert 2. After that, I got a new computer. With Windows XP. I still had little computing experience and I watched as my dad installed drivers for the sound (*gasp*, it was only integrated sound), video, and other crap.

I saw this thing called "VB 6" somewhere. I installed it, and it turned out to be programming. I thought, OK, let's learn this. I remember getting stuck when using an "End If" when I didn't even have an "If". Gradually I became a big Microsoft fan: I went to their website every day, downloaded C#, blabla. Back then I thought Mac sucked and "Linux" was some random experiment by some random guys.

One day, I went to the library to look for some books about computing to borrow. I saw this strange book called "Free as in Freedom: Richard Stallman's crusade for free software" or something with a picture of a guy on the front. I read it, and I started not-hating-Mac-and-GNU/Linux and I started hating Windows. I wanted to use GNU/Linux, and a friend introduced me to Ubuntu. I installed it on a separate hard drive, and... it worked great! Sound and video worked out of the box.

Of course, I wanted to have Ubuntu as my main OS, so I got a new hard drive and installed it. Everything worked. Since I couldn't use C# (well, I could, but I didn't know about Mono), I learned C. And I like it. Now, I use Ubuntu 99% of the time, and for games, I play Nexuiz. For the 1%, that's for Windows games.

So... thank you Ubuntu team!
