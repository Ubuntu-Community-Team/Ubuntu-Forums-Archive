### Post by umattu on 2007-10-17
I have been Windows free, at home that is, for almost 18 months now. I have tons of ubuntu at home, 1 feisty desktop, 2 feisty edubuntu desktops for the kids, 2 fesity lappies, and a dapper server. I absolutely love ubuntu. 

Now to get back to the thread at hand, I work for a Corp. that is, like many, a complete Windows environment. I support XP all day everyday, and just can't wait to get home to the REAL computers!!!!

Recently some of the officers of the company, bought themselves some new rigs with Vista pre installed. Can you imagine the look on my face when I first sat behind one of these Vista machine, in front of the COO mind you, and was just lost........

Luckily it was only a simple printer install and it went off without a hitch. This made me decide that, unfortunately, I would have to dedicate one of my home machines to Vista temporarily, to learn my 
way around that nightmare, Microsoft calls an OS. I got a copy of 
Vista, Oh Yeah it was legit :lolflag:, and installed it on one of my laptops.

Seriously, I started the install on a Sat around noon, and did not have a fully functional machine until Sun in the evening. (had to install the OS, drivers, antivirus, open office, anti spyware, java, flash blah blah blah) This really almost had me wanting to drop the whole "learn Vista" and continue on in my world of ubuntu, "but this is for my job" I reluctantly convinced myself.

I ran Vista on my lappie, and used it most of the time, (needed my daily fill of ubuntu) for the last 6 weeks:( . Last night I decided that, enough was enough, and joyfully formatted over that wanna be OS, I mean I didn't even take the time to get some of the files off of the machine that I wanted, I just wiped it ALL:D

I threw a feisty disc in and was back up and running in just under an hour. Far less time then that piece of MS crap. Not to mention running smoother, and faster.

Up until now I kept the Vista bashing to a minimum, only because I had not experianced it first hand. I hate when folks that have never tried ANY GNU/Linux distro, and feel that they have enough insight to bash it, so I try not to be one of them peeps. Well now I'm versed in Vista and it quite frankly SUX!!!!!!!

I am so happy I got to kiss Microsoft and Windows good bye for a second time.

Sorry for rambling on, but I'm sure someone else out there in the same boat I'm in.

Thanks ubuntu,
Thanks ubuntu community, 
Thanks Mark Shuttleworth,
Thanks Canonical,
THANK YOU THANK YOU THANK YOU to  everyone and everything that has helped create this wonderful OS known as ubuntu. 

There is no better!!! 

Well that is until the next release LOL!!

umattu.
