### Post by shawnerz on 2006-09-28
I have an old SunBlade 100 that was in a corner not doing anything.  About a year ago, I pulled it out and wanted to get it up and running.
It had Solaris 4 on it, so i went to update it.  I started with Gentoo but I got lost very quickly because I had to compile the kernel.
Then I tried Aurora (Red Hat).  That didn't work.  I would get a seg fault everytime I tried to su to root.  Then, the box would not boot up properly after the seg fault.

I tried different things off and on.  Yesterday, I tried Debian and that ended in another failure.  Debian could not detect configure the keyboard map properly and the installer could not detect the CDROM (even though the kernel had just booted from the CD).

I run Ubuntu on my home Linux box and say there was port for the SPARC.  I downloaded it today and it installed without any problems!  I am very impressed.

Thanks to all who worked on this project.
-Shawn
