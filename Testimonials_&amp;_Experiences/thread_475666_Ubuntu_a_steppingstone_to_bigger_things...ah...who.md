> **wolfen69 said:**

why wouldnt ubuntu suit your needs? what does gentoo do that ubuntu doesnt? ubuntu is for wimps? if it is, call me a wimp. just because i dont have to use the command line for every little thing doesnt mean its less powerful. any distro can be made to do whatever you want. if you're a command line junkie, what's stopping you from doing that in ubuntu?

people need to stop this whole macho thing. if you want a "manly" distro, try arch linux. chances are you'll come running back here like a frightened schoolgirl.
I have just switched to Arch and I have to say it's not that difficult, and at times I have found it even easier than Ubuntu. I don't feel more manly than before :P

I think the point is what you want to achieve. Ubuntu is very good if you want everything up and running in an instant, manually configured distros are better for those who need their system fine-tuned to their priorities, or who enjoy learning Linux (like me).

I compare "ready-made" distros to complex Desktop Environments, like Gnome, where all configuration is made with a couple of clicks, whereas the "raw" distros are more like essentialist Windows Managers such as IceWm, Fluxbox, Openbox and the like, where all configuration is mostly made through text files. 
I personally don't fancy spending hours setting up a Window Manager and I am content with ready-made stuff such as Gnome, and I don't think there is anything manly about text-configuration. 

Finally, GUI sometimes is more user friendly than CLI (i.e. for graphical stuff, using drag & drop, etc), but at times CLI is more user friendly than a GUI (adding modules to be loaded at boot time, irssi, editing text files, etc).
