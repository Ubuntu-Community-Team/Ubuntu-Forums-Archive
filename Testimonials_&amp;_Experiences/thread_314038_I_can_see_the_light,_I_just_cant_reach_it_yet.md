### Post by sean150 on 2006-12-06
First off let me state that I'm little more than an average windows user. Also if this does not belong in testimonials then please have it moved. 

I want to switch to Ubuntu, I really do! I know it can do everything I want it to do except its being too difficult for me to get setup. My feeling right now is that certain processes could be simplified or explained better (or maybe I'm just not looking hard enough). I'm starting to figure out how to use terminal but as soon as I run into a problem I have to browse the net for an hour looking for the solution. 

A month ago I installed Dapper on my laptop without a hitch, I feel in love with it and saw its potential, except my laptop doesn't have the power to do what I want. This means I had to install on my desktop, this is where the horror begins. 

basic system summary: 
AMD64 
2 74Gig Raptor SATA drives in raid0
1 250Gig SATA

1: Two hours of frustration I finally get Edgy 64 installed on the 250gig.
2: Will not allow me to dual boot back into windows (I suspect because Ubuntu doesn't automatically recognize existing raids, thus it couldn't see windows)
3: An hour and a half later I can't get it to recognize my raid, along the way it would take multiple reinstalls and reboots of Edgy simply because it didn't like my machine. corrupted ubuntu splash and freezing all along the way. 
4: Break my raid0 and format drives. everything is lost (my fault that I didn't backup)
5: Install windows to one Raptor, Dapper on the other. everything works fine. 
6: Time to get to work, Attempt to install Wine! Hurray no 64bit version of Wine exists. I then read I have to create a 32bit root.
7: I give up and go back to windows in the hope that some day I may be able to use Ubuntu.
