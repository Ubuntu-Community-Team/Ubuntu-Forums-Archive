### Post by Sesshomurai on 2013-03-19
Because of the way menus now work in gnome/ubuntu (only at the top of the screen), it is hard to know what window you are accessing.

Case in point. GIMP

Gimp opens a bunch of floating windows. All of which can appear on top of all other windows. Yet there is no menu at the top?
Why is that?

Well, because the gimp main toolbox window doesn't have any menus. So where is the menu?!?!
Is it in the layers floating window? Click window.....move mouse back to top of screen.....nope...
Is it in the empty document window? Click window......move mouse back to top of screen.....yes!!!
Only 3 or 4 clicks to find the right window....

Take a break....come back.....go to menu "File"....wait, what app am I in again? Who belongs to this "File" menu that is not attached to a window?
I forget. So let's start clicking on windows again....click....move......click.....move.....click...click...click.

Horrible...waste of time...

Yes, it's a shortcoming in Unity that's never been fixed, that GIMP's menus only appear when you have the correct window in the foreground. Should have been worked around a year ago, but has never been addressed. Sorry.

Global menus are a good idea as they clean up the interface and save on screen space, but the implementation with GIMP needs some work. I don't believe they'll bother actually fixing their multiple window implementation because they've now just decided to copy Windows Photoshop's horrible single window UI.
