### Post by mattias.jn on 2008-11-13
yeah you read it correctly.

I'm kind of a n00b or whatever experienced people call us new-comers. In the windows-world I'm all but a n00b and I've been using computers a lot since I like them. They are the most awesome tools invented by man since we found or created fire! If you disagree I suggest you spent a couple of nights out in the swedish arctic bush or in any favorite desert. It's really cold without a fire... and what would modern society be without computers? Really boring! Just ask your grand parents ;)

Anyway. I find it a bit disturbing that people have no faith or even worse, no patience with the excellence of Ubuntu. So what if it takes a while to adapt... I mean really, it's like getting a new partner... you need a little dating before things run smoothly ;)

I recently invested in a new computer and since I've been running windows (XP/2003server/2008server, I hate Vista) since ever, I thought it was my duty to do an informed buy, so that I get the most for my hard earned cash.

I wanted a desktop machine that also could hold all my movies and music as well as my large collection of loved photos... so I figured I should look into raid6. After a lot of reading software raid with Linux was the undisputed winner in terms of speed, portability, cost and security. I also realized that I use my computer for surfing, listening to music, writing emails, IM-chat, downloading, storing and editing photos and last but not least watching movies. All this is easily done with Ubuntu. Since it was free and I liked the idea of people helping each other out I thought "why not try it?". So I waited for the latest release, since I thought this would be better than the current. In the mean time I bought my dream machine: black, stylish, completely silent and with a bit of ghetto-modding it holds 13 hard drives =) Top it with a 25" flat screen of the upper range and you've got yourself a date that lasts ;)

Ubuntu got released and I installed. Went like a charm. Easier and quicker than any windows system I've ever installed! Everything just worked! ******* beautiful. The first couple of hours I was really annoyed though cause I had some difficulties finding menus and stuff.. Then I remembered the revered saying "Things going shitty? Ask google!" and found this guys blog on ubuntu which showed me with screenshots how to configure Ubuntu to beat Vista's hairy *** to the moon and send Mac OS X to the dungeons (where dictators should be!). I was amazed by the time this guy had spent on helping others get an excellent desktop that not only looks good, but also sports productivity. 

I found another guide and set up my raid6 and was amazed by the power and genius of mdadm.

Then the hell started. Stuff I put on my raid got corrupted.. I noticed it by accident while helping a friend installing some stuff I'd downloaded from uni, and it didn't work! We checked and yes it was my raid the corrupted stuff. I then spent 2 weeks (2-5hours/day after uni) trying to get to the bottom of my problem. It was very frustrating. In the end it had nothing to do with Ubuntu. It was a faulty drive (one of the 7% that break in the first year according to google)!! So in my anger regarding why my computer (ie Ubuntu or mdadm) hadn't "told me" that it was a faulty drive I read up on file systems and disk failures. Which led me to ZFS, raidz2 and ZFS-fuse. Awesome ****! Self healing filesystems! Ever heard about that in windows? I wouldn't think so and if we had it, it would cost a fortune and be M$ only. Now it's owned by Sun and by some weird (as with all *nix licensing?) way you can use it on linux thanks to this great guy who as a summer project merged it in to FUSE (more linux magic). So now my awesome new computer run ZFS-fuse with raidz2 and it has already healed som files for me =) Me so happy =)

Ubuntu is just awesome! I'm sure I'll get to use the forum to get help and maybe I can help someone too in the future... Sure, it's a bit of a hassle to get new relationships to work, but in the end who doesn't prefer a long lasting, free and mutual give/take relationship than the quick and costly relationship of whoring where you only get what you pay for?

Ubuntu is the way to go and I think it's great that all you old-school-no-n00bs help us new-comers =) I know it's a pain in the *** sometimes (I do win support for my parents) but seriously, if I'm part of the future ubuntu crowd YOU are the present that makes that future possible. Newer forget that and keep the faith in an awesome product (the ubuntu community)!

Hahahaha =) I previewed my post and saw that all my emotional extravagances was censored!! Hilarious. This has to be a USA-hosted site?:lolflag:
