### Post by Novega on 2008-05-08
This is just a little testimonial from a new linux user.

I've been a windows user all my life but I built a new computer last month b/c I thought my motherboard was fried (random lock-ups and restarts) Anyhow I got myself a new AM2+ motherboard, A64x2 4800 cpu and 2Gb's of DDR800 ram.

I come home, put it all together, boot up XP and ... "Your hardware has changed since you first installed windows" warning appears. I thought "wow, they noticed my hardware changed and now they want to help me load the drivers...pretty nice!"  No, they don't really care about that, the only thing they cared about was verifying my license. :(  I jump through the hoops, call them and we exchange a series of codes that are like 15 digits long. Finally done, Yay!

Computer still randomly restarts (to make a long story short, it was my video card) I buy a nvidia 9600GT and install it only to get that stupid verification warning **again** By this point I'm so tired of playing these stupid games of exchanging super long codes with the company I jump on my brothers computer and decide to look online and see if I can find another way around the "Genuine Advantage"  *wink *wink

It's at that point I hear about Ubuntu 8.04LTS and how it's got some good reviews. I had an old 30GB hard drive so why not give it a try? 

Installed fine, looked a little plain to me, but hey it's still nice. I want 3d effects but the nvidia 9600GT driver is a *beta driver* after asking in these forums and doing my own homework I figured it out, got compiz working, changed my theme, icons, background, installed all my codecs, installed AWN, learned how to add to source lists, use synaptic, update my system, install pretty much all the programs I needed...**ALL WITHIN A COUPLE DAYS!** 

At first I was scared of messing it all up *(Linux is made by geeks, for geeks...it isn't for average everyday users you know :p)* but as I went along, I became more and more confident in my abilities.

Now 2 weeks later, I've converted two more users in my house to Ubuntu and set up their systems for them. No real problems except for my moms computer which has a MSI television card connected to HD cable/ Satellite.  I'm not really scared of messing anything up, even if I do, the forums help is AMAZING!

My step father has been distro hopping and seems to try a new distro every couple days (all non- *buntu ones) and can't get them working. I think the main difference between Ubuntu and the ones he tries (suse, linspire,mandriva,ect..) has been the forum help and the popularity of *buntu. It seems no matter what I want to do, theres a guide for it. If not, I can ask on the forums and get an answer.

Everyone in my house still dual boots with XP, but our time is increasingly spent in Ubuntu.

### Post by wdaniels on 2008-05-08
Hi Novega, it's really nice of you to take the time to post a success story!  I see too many people turn up here just to say that something or other doesn't work quite right and they're going back to Windows, then they don't even bother to reply to people trying to help with whatever the problem is.

I tried a number of Linux distributions on-and-off for years, but there always seemed to be some hole (usually in the hardware support) that made life difficult, and it's a steep learning curve when you're pushed for time, regardless of technical skill.  Ubuntu was the distro that "just worked" for me and gave me a complete full-time usable system, which is what I needed to be be able to start getting into Linux properly... after that it only took me a few weeks to get everything set up and all the pieces in place to confidently ditch Windows for good.  That was nearly 2 years ago now, and I've stayed with Ubuntu ever since.

I think this is what some people in the Linux community forget when they complain about Ubuntu - sometimes you just need an easy way to get started and before long you're in a position to help others yourself.
