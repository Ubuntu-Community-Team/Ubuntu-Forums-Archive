### Post by mscman on 2006-06-06
Hey everybody, This is my 100th Post!!!  (A screenshot will be included once I'm done writing this, as that number will soon change...)  I thought I would take it as an opportunity to recap my journey through Linuxland.

First, a little background:

I started college last fall at Purdue University only having used Windows machines, with the exception of using a Mac on occasion (the daycare I went to as a child had all Macs.)  I really had no clue about the existence of Linux.  Like most people, I kind of just thought of it as a geeky system, something only the really smart computer guys used (which is saying a lot, sinceI currently maintain all of my family's and some of my friends' computers).  That was until I got a job working in a lab at Purdue last fall.  The class (ECET325) teaches operating system performance and benchmarking.  The lab contains Dell Windows machines, as well as Solaris Sparc workstations (accessed through ssh), and the "test" machines that dual boot Debian and Windows.  We were getting rid of a bunch of old Kayak workstations, and since I had just been introduced to Linux, I figured it would be a good machine for me to start on.

Thus, the journey begins...

I started downloading distros like CRAZY!!!  We had just gotten broadband internet, so I was still amazed at how fast these came to me, it was like a gift.  I did so many Linux installs that I could do them in my sleep (and do on occasion in the lab... :p )  I tried everything from Debian to RedHat to SuSE and finally ended on XandrOS.  My computer sits in the basement and I have yet to run an ethernet cable, so I needed something that supported the D-link wireless card I have in the desktop.  At the time, Xandros was the only one that would support the card out of the box, and since I didn't have any Linux experience, it was my best choice.

It wasn't until around November that I tried other distros.  I still wasn't very accustomed to Linux, as Xandros is very point and click.  The driving force behind my switch was my laptop's wireless card.  It's a Toshiba Satellite M45-S331 and uses the horrible combination of Intel Wireless 2200 and a Marvell Yukon card.  After reformatting my laptop, I started installing distros on it.  It was very hard for me to find a distro that would support the graphics card, sound card, and all of the extras.  I can't remember the exact process, but I ended up on Ubuntu.  I managed to get the wireless networking going on Breezy, yet it would keep freezing.  It was around this time (Dec. 2005) that I joined Ubuntu Forums.

After struggling with crashes on both my laptop and desktop, I decided Breezy wasn't very good for my hardware.  I heard about Dapper, and despite all of the warnings, decided to upgrade.  I was amazed!  It fixed everything, even though it was still in its alpha stage!  I was stuck to it at once.  To make an already long story short, I'm still a faithful user today, sitting and typing this on my dual display setup using XGL and Compiz.  I never would have believed a year ago that I would be a Linux user.  I owe most of this credit to the Ubuntu Forums, as well as my Professor who helped me with my laptop networking ordeal.  

To all those who helped along the way, thanks!  I know this has been long and I doubt people will read it, but oh well.  Thanks anyways.  I plan to order a ton of Dapper discs and hand them to anyone who mentions the word "Linux".  I've already converted a Debian user...

The End!
