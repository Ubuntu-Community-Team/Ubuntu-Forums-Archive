### Post by studiesinsound on 2008-07-20
I just have to pipe in here and say, I am loving my Linux Experience.
Here are the quick bulleted reasons:

1: First and foremost the community is great!  Very supportive and welcoming.  Whenever I have a question, folks always share their knowledge.  And why?  No one is paying them?  They just do it for the enjoyment of helping someone else enjoy Linux.  Rock!

2: The OS's and applications are free of charge.  Wonderful.  I have been able to try many distributions and applications before settling on my current setup: Ubuntu Studio. 

3: Some folks mark this next one down as a negative, but for me this is a huge positive: Linux does require a bit of a learning curve.  Depending on ones familiarity with computers and OS architecture, this may be a steep curve or an easy transition.  I am fairly savvy with computers and am learning so much under Linux.  And good stuff too, specific to hardware and software architecture and how they all talk and what not.  Good stuff.  Whereas with windows and MAC one can work with a system for years and have no real clue as to how things work under the hood.  With Linux, yes a relatively inexperienced user can install the OS and do basic tasks, but to really get the benefit of the OS, the user must learn at first basic, and then more advanced concepts of how exactly the system works.  The good news is number 1 from above. 

Maybe the reason folks say widows crashes so much and Linux is more stable is just that by necessity folks who use Linux must be more familiar with the system and what it is and is not intended to do, and how best to achieve their goals.  The right tool for the right job so to speak, and more to the point how to use that tool. And actually knowing you are using some sort of tool to do a job.  And if you use the wrong tool you can easily troubleshoot your own mistakes and correct them.

With Windows or Mac you could be using a hammer to tighten screws and wouldn't even know it until the system abends with some obscure error.

anyway just my 2 cents.
I hope some of that made sense.
