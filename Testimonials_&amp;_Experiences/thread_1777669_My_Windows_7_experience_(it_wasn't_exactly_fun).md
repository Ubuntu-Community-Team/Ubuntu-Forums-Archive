### Post by usagiakumu on 2011-06-08
30 Days with Windows 7
 

 I had recently bought a new computer for around $580, came pre-installed with  
 Windows 7 Home Premium  
 1TB Western Digital Blue
 Dual Core AMD Athalon X2 220
 2Gig Ram DDR3 1666
 24X DVD Burner
 21&#8221; LED display
 GeForce 8400 1G nonshared DDR3
 Speakers, keyboard, and mouse
 

 Not a bad little system, my original plans were to format and use Gentoo as I haven't used Windows since Windows 2000. I skipped XP and Vista and am an avid Linux user.
 

 Upon boot I was greeted by a Windows 7 default desktop, and to me this felt a little close to home almost every aspect of it seems to be almost a direct rip off of KDE 4. At least a mosh-up of OSX and KDE 4. The task-bar reminds of me of the docky, and the kick-off style menu. Getting used to it took some time as I was unsure as what I could do with Windows, and how far I could push it. I hear for Windows 7 that this is a bare minimum starter system, but for the likes of Gentoo, it is a beast. It came with Windows Live Essentials, and Windows Security Essentials, Netflix, and an MS-Office demo pre-installed.
 

 After removing the Office demo and getting LibreOffice installed I began to write this document. I downloaded X-Chat which I found out the Windows version they want you to pay, which is complete garbage, shame on you for charging for an OSS application! So off to get X-Ychat a fork which is basically X-Chat built on GTK for Windows. VLC Player, and Firefox were also added to the list.
 

 One very large annoyance was the wording they choose to use for their naming scheme. Gadgets instead of widgets, everyone else calls them widgets, but in light of &#8220;hey we aren't copying anyone&#8221; they HAVE to be fidderant (I spelled it wrong on purpose to exaggerate the ignorance behind this). Homegroups for networking? The language they use in naming things reminds me of pawn shop language or gangster movie language calling things &#8220;the daddy of computing&#8221;, in other words very demeaning to the intellect of the user, as if he can't understand terms and you are the mob leader &#8220;using lamens terms to explain it to us idiots&#8221; Also it has to call everything Windows this and Windows that as if I completely forgot I am using Windows, for example Windows DVD Maker, or Windows Media Player, or Windows Media Center. Everything seems to be &#8220;bigger&#8221; from the window edges to the taskbar right down to the icons. It is kind of like the giant pencil they made you use in kindergarten.  
 

 There used to be a saying in computing, keep it simple, but don't treat your users like idiots or only idiots will use it, and even then if you over-simplify it, even the idiots will catch on and quit using it. It really teeters on the edge of this.
 

 All in all, it seems pretty stable but it seems to be missing that &#8220;professional&#8221; feel that Windows 98 and 2000 had. Like it lost it's magic and this is it's last great trick and everyone can smell their fear.  Everything exudes this demi-masculine homo-erotic feel right from the start-up sound that you cant change down to the wallpaper decisions. The C: drive now has a phallic bar that extends just a bit too far, while the Start Orb is reminisce of a buttox. To get to where you want to go, just &#8220;start me up by clicking here ;)&#8221; Oh and by the way, not all of the United States is from Texas and Maine! (theme reference)

 After getting past all of this, lets boil this down to a technical standpoint. I found myself disabling &#8220;Windows Search&#8221; for speed; another thing that just has to be the surname to Windows ugh. I went through using it and found that it worked, at least at first. After installing a lot of applications and uninstalling some it began to just slow down and no amount of disk cleanup and defrag could speed it up. It also didn't just sync with my Linux network. My Popcorn Hour A-110 just would not network with Windows 7, no matter what I tried. Forget making it a Media Center Extender. My Android device seemed to work OK after installing drivers, but again it wouldn't play nice over the network with it. When contacting Microsoft they insisted that I use their Cloud Service, and I told them where they can take their arcane 1970s technology.  
 

 If and that is a big if, I were to suddenly migrate my three computers to Windows 7, I would need to buy new devices, but here is the thing, Microsoft killed the Zune, way to go! I would like to see a Windows Mobile device that didn't require me to have a cell phone contract. I would need to buy a Microsoft Certified device for it to be a Media Center Extender. So far, this is looking pretty dim for Microsoft. I am a pretty full time worker then when I come home I am a dad, so for me, gaming is not even on the table for me. At some point people grow up, and I don't see any real need for me to have Windows.  
 

 My wife uses Linux with ease, she knows nothing of computers at all. She will get on, play for a little while on Facebook, set the Popcorn Hour from the computer in the bedroom to record a TV show, maybe play solitaire. I let her try the Windows 7 machine and her initial reactions were pretty much my own. &#8220;You know if they spent half the time making it work, instead of making it pretty they might have something worth selling.&#8221; &#8220;You still have to defrag it?&#8221; &#8220;Why does everything I want to accomplish have to be buried so I have to click through a hundred things?&#8221;
 

 Back to Gentoo and KDE 4.6! Microsoft you need to try harder at not sucking so bad. This might become my Ubuntu machine for a little while.
