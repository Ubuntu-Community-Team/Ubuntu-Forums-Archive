### Post by keylime on 2007-09-26
[FONT="Arial"][SIZE="3"]

My experiences with Ubuntu have been a rapid rise from uncertainty to confidence in both the O/S and the software that powers it.

I am a client desktop analyst for a trade association that advocates on behalf of credit unions in Washington D.C. Due to my post and our non-profit status, we are constantly struggling to manage costs and to gain ease-of-use in our numerous applications. That, and we are constantly trying to get Windows to behave itself...

About a month ago, I decided to try a Linux distro for the first time - being predominantly a Windows guy with some Apple experience, this was a big change for me. A peer of mine handed me the 6.06 LTS disc and I plopped it into my laptop (which is now my test bench for all things Ubuntu - have used a HP NC4200 and NC8230) just to see if it would load. 

The first thing that was self-evident to me was the speed difference. Both laptops aforementioned are not the swiftest of machines, but they were noticeably quicker than the XP Pro images that we use for our users, and this was from the LiveCD. So, away I went - installation which took about 30 minutes from start to finish on the 4200, and after 2 O/S upgrades arrived at the latest build.

I decided to perform a battery test just to see what Linux could do - I was floored when the battery laster 50 minutes longer than the Windows O/S (for the record, I had configured the ACPI items to try to have some parity). That alone was a big positive. 

The day-to-day use of the O/S is a joy. I can do everything as before, except it's done better. The level of intuitiveness and ease exceeds that of Windows, so much so that I am strongly considering installing it on my mom's brand new Vista desktop because of the struggles she is having currently. 

I am strongly recommending some of our servers to run Linux-based apps in the near future (good bye Oracle), and Ubuntu was a prime reason for this. Not only will costs dramatically reduce itself, but it will simply just work. It's nice that with heavy use, the O/S has yet to fail or error trap - not one single oops. 

I am happy that I took the plunge. I went out and purchased Ubuntu Unleashed which has been a treasure trove of information.

It will be quite hard to leave the ease that Ubuntu offers, plus the superb support.

Dwayne Hoffman
Washington, D.C.
