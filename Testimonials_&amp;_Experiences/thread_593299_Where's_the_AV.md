### Post by 13thMonkey on 2007-10-27
I got a phone call the other week from my soon-to-be-ex-new-brother-in-law-twice-removed  (Bob for short). (The following conversation is highly paraphrased ;))

Bob: Your sister's computer needs more memory.  Where's the cheapest place to buy it?
Me: My sister's computer is a lot shinier and newer than mine. What do you use the computer for?
Bob: Google, bbc.co.uk.... [long list of URLs] and email.
Me: OK, next time you visit, bring the computer with you and I'll upgrade it.
Bob: How much will it cost for us to upgrade to the spinny cube and wobbly windows like you have? 
Me: *Sigh*  £9.77
Bob: That's cheap!
Me: That's the price of a litre of vodka.

[Some weeks of Gutsy RC/Final use later]

Bob: My friend's computer needs more memory.
Me: You know that CD I gave you? Give it to him.
Bob: And then what?
Me: You're now a Linux sysadmin, you know about computers.
Bob: Yes! I've even used the command line. I can install Ubuntu on his PC.
Me: Don't forget to ask him to buy a bottle of vodka. You can bring it with you on your next visit.

[Today]

Bob: My friend wants to know where the anti-virus program is.
*Me opens vodka and waits for phone call. 

Some people 'get it' more than others....
