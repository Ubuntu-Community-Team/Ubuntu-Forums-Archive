### Post by rahilm on 2009-08-12
This is an account of a user (which is me) who has been using ubuntu (gnome) for the past half a year(and really loves it) but then decides to install what is known as kde (kubuntu-desktop) to give it a try for the first time.
[SIZE=2][B]
Day 1:[/B][/SIZE]
With many difficulties (they are common if you don't have a good internet connection) i finally managed to download packages for kubuntu desktop and install them. A dialogue box asked whether to choose gdm or kdm as default and i chose kdm (random).Then I did a reboot to see a new astonishing sight..the fist thing I thought was **WOW**...

There it was... a nice blue desktop greeting me with cool menu's, applets and so-called widgets. I was too excited.. then i started using it..

I have always read on forum posts or blogs about gnome vs kde.. One particular point was that gnome concentrated on simplicity while kde concentrated on giving full control..Well, they were right...5 minutes into kubuntu and i was struggling for space.

Everything seemed complicated..even running an app from the K menu..not to mention that kde was much slower than gnome..I was almost lost in it. You see I was seeing a KDE desktop for the first time in my life. I decided to do a quick search through the net to learn this new desktop (something I did after weeks when using gnome) and stumbled upon a new browser konqueror.

The first page that popped up was kde.org. I tried to look for help but then decided to do a google search. I should say Firefox is far more better than konqueror. After searching I learned that kde used kwin windows manager... I quickly checked if any of my compiz effects work here but they didn't. I learned how to enable kwin effects (again clumsy)

One thing with kde is that except the awesome file browser everything in it is clumsy, everything from menus to lists appears to be stuck together. Even I feel being crowded when I search through them.

I have to say that the cube effects of kwin was a little better than compiz's (atleast on my pc). The cube was transparent without being dark. Other effects are cool too but compiz is better at those.

I hate the K menu and the bottom panel is cluttered and what the heck is the show plasma desktop. I found it ultra useless.

More than half an hour in kde, i decided to see what the default applications where. So here is a comparison by a gnome user.
-> Konsole was good almost like gnome-terminal, i cannot say anyone was better.
-> As sad before Firefox is better than Konqueror (but Firefox is only a browser though)
-> I was disappointed to see that no new games had been installed. I was expecting something like kde-games or such
-> Speed crunch was good. Considering that typing calc is enough to bring it up. Plus a more detailed UI
-> Ark was good too. In fact now i realize that kde was made to 'feel' good
-> In many ways dolphin was better than nautilus and in many ways nautilus is better.. a 50-50 here
-> Configuring Kopete was a headache given that I didn't succeed in creating an account. Advantage Pidgen.
-> Gwenview is cooool.
-> Even I liked Okular. Very impressive
-> Gedi package manager is far better than kde's default (atleast it asks you before it does anything)
-> Amarok was one of my favorite players back in gnome days but this amarok was different plus it doesn't work and it doesn't tell why
-> Dragon player was something better than Totem. but it doesn't matter I use vlc
-> Since I am learning programming i decided to try out Kate which I had so much about and it is here that things turn out to become a little messy

My initial reaction to Kate was .... nothing.. it was like gedit but with a different feeling. Two minutes into it and i began to realize the difference. Kate was a far more advanced editor than gedit. It's really sad that gedit doesn't have code folding else I would have had a hard time deciding between the two. I changed some settings. Customized plugins and the display to suit my needs and was typing like never before. But when I did File->Quit, Kate closed down as expected but with an error message relating to some backslashes. I tried searching for a problem, found one or two sites but they were acknowledging the problem but never solving it.

It is getting late so I am deciding to stop, post this and close the (Firefox) browser. Tomorrow I'll try to solve this problem (look for a solution that is) and explore a little more of this environment.
***Good night***

### Post by rahilm on 2009-08-14
[B]DAY 2:
[/B]Demotivated by last times use, I decided not to login as a kde session again. But then I did just to write my day 2 results.

I don't know why it happens every time with me. whenever I decide to hate something with a passion, I suddenly start to like it. Same thing happened today. Surprisingly, kde didn't take much time to load. And everything was running smoothly. I still use firefox though and KaTe problem is still unsolved.

I found that i can still use synaptic (relief!!) instead of Kpackagekit. I installed Kwrite which is good as a simple text editor. Programming is best left to gedit and specifically code::blocks

I played with the settings and got rid of the pesky desktop folder that appears on my desktop. and the show plasma desktop. Once you know the pros and cons you can get around the cons using the pros.

Using it for an hour, I said to myself "It isn't that bad, with a little work it can get better than windows in terms of usability.

Ofcourse gnome rocks, but do watch out for kde.
My advice -> If you want to use kde, i recommend install kubuntu-desktop on ubuntu or if you want to use kubuntu, be sure to install gnome desktop. KDE alone is very irritating, and i can't imagine wat would i do without those gnome apps.
