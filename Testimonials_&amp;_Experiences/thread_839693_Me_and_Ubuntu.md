### Post by S1xp4ck on 2008-06-24
Computers have fascinated me all my life.  I can remember in grade school when we all sold countless chocolate bars to help fund 7 Commodore Pet Computers the school ended up purchasing.  The simplicity of tossing a cassette into a tape drive and waiting half your recess to play Lemonade Stand always to me seemed worth it.  I remember working hard all summer pulling tassels from corn in a hot field to save enough money to buy a shiny new Tandy 1000-EX system from Radio Shack.  The C-64 I got a yard sale ... the Amiga I got for Christmas, that top of the line 386 I got for another Xmas.  I remember in 1998 I decided to build my first computer myself... it was a PII 266 with 64 megs of ram, an ATI Xpert98 vid card with 2 3DFX voodoo2 cards SLI (Quake 2 here I come lol) and that top of the line 56k Modem.  Even today I sit at another custom built computer admiring the world of technology and the joy of the fascination it brings me.  To my own surprise in all these years, I hardly thought about my OS up until a few months ago.

It started with my upgrade to Vista from XP.  I like many was afflicted by the chronic driver issues between Nvidia and Vista. For me this was the breaking point on a many issues that started my journey here. Now I am not going to smash Microsoft for the problems or the finger pointing between Nvidia and MS for the 20th BSOD in a day and who was to blame for it.  In a way I need to thank them for allowing a door to open... after all if it wasn't for finally being fed up with the problems with my computer and my drivers I wouldn't have fired up google and headed to the Ubuntu site to read up on the latest version.

As I researched, I got thinking.  I started to realize that the world of Linux and open source has fingers everywhere.  More than Ubuntu or my own personal problems.... I started to ask myself:

"I wonder why so many web hosts run Linux servers"

" You know come to think of it, a lot of those gaming servers I play on are Linux too wonder why?"

" Now that I think of it, I use a lot of applications now to replace those MS ones I hate anyways"


Well might as well check out this new 8.04 version Ubuntu has in beta and see what it is all about.... if that isn't good I can always go back to XP.  Little did I know just how much that decision would impact things to come.  

For me Hardy was the version that has me running Linux 98% of the time at home. My wife and 8 year old some have had no issue in moving over from an MS based OS.  Really at this house now the only time Vista is booted up is because my All in One printer from Lexmark is still awaiting a decent driver for Ubuntu. Kinda funny I paid $120 for an OS that is now only used to print the odd thing out.  But what has me sold on Hardy is that the "out of the box" software it comes with or easily obtainable in the repositories meets or exceeds for the most part every need a home user would expect out of an OS.

Sure one may argue there are some things Ubuntu lacks behind other OS.  But I think you can say that for any OS.  All I can say is that for the majority of things I do on a computer, Ubuntu can handle 98% of it.  My wife enjoys it, my 8 year old son can use with ease. As for the few edges needed to be polished and smoothed, I think Ubuntu is poised to become a major player in the mainstream in home computing, and I am glad I am here to be a part of it.
