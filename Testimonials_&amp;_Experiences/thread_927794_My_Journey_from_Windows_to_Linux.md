### Post by SarahMarieNC2 on 2008-09-23
SO, now that I have an up and running computer again, outfitted with Linux Ubuntu 8.04 I thought I would share my journey.

I bought my first computer in high school (2001) and it came with Windows ME. Oh man I cannot remember how many times I had issues with it nor how many times I had to reinstall the OS. It was a royal mess.

That computer, as bad as it ran with Windows ME still lives. It's outdated but it works. In fact, a friend is doing some upgrading on it and making it into a linux desktop for his daughter :) I had that computer until February this year (2008)

Finally I decided I should  get something a little more updated. SO I shopped around and found a refurbished HP Media Center M8100n on newegg.com. I bought it and it arrived at my house on Valentines Day, just three days after my purchase! 

It ran great right out of the box and even with that ugh version of 32 bit *ist@ on my 64 bit processor. But then service pack 1 came out. I downloaded it and on the restart I no longer had a pretty computer. It was badly broken. After 5 hours on the phone with HP that day and many hours on the phone with them over the next few weeks I decided to try something else. I knew my harddrive wasn't trashed and I knew that there had to be something else I could do.

SO I searched "free operating systems" and found Linux Ubuntu. It took a while to download and burn the ISO simply because my mother in laws laptop runs Vista Basic (and really shouldn't given it's specs) and had all sorts of processes going. BUt fastforward, right out of the install my computer was working again, and fast too! My theory proved right. I was happy.

Next came the figuring out how to configure my wireless internet. Oh joy! NOw this was work but it shouldn't have been. After getting frustrated I decided maybe I had just done things that didn't need doing. I reinstalled a fresh version from my live cd and wouldn't you know that my wireless internet works now. Nothing needed doing and here I was trying to complicate things with drivers and such. Ha! 

Now I look forward to learning new things and a new way of doing them. I cannot say how happy I am today but I hope it shows through here because after 4 months of  being computer less, I finally have a working computer again... and perchance maybe even a better one than I had before the crash.

SO I want to say "Thank you Ubuntu" for breathing life back into my PC. Thank you for having a great cause and a wonderful forum. And I hope that in the future more people will see that Linux has much to offer the world. 

Sarah in NC
Former Windows User
