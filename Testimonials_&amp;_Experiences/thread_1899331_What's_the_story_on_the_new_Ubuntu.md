### Post by Tamlynmac on 2011-12-24
> armandh
just look at the distrowatch rankings

What is Distrowatch's acceptable margin of error? If one of the primary data collection criteria is based on the number of downloads, numerous question arise. Such as the number of users that actually installed and are using the distro vs how many downloaded it for trial. I think many of us can envision downloading a distro for the purpose of trial, but that doesn't necessarily equate to one being a committed user of said distro.

As with all data collection, I think one should always consider not only how data is collected, but what the data actually represents. IMHO any poll or ranking system based on inadequate data collection methodologies, accomplishes little beyond that of an estimate. I certainly don't believe it should be used as a definitive reference in any debate regarding actual usage numbers.

Consider the questions of who could identify exactly how many users exist (of any distro) at any given point in time and how one would acquire such data? Not to mention how many users may have multiple OS's on their system(s).

Just my $0.02 and in no way is it meant to bash Distrowatch.
