### Post by tehbeermang on 2007-04-26
I had apprehensions about removing NTLDR from the MBR and replacing with something called GRUB. Lilo is a happier name, but GRUB is what comes with ubuntu and what all the tutorials have. GRUB makes me think of what my hand felt like after shaking hands with a mechanic.

"Herman's" tutorial on dual-booting took away much of my worry about being GRUBby.

A few questions answered here, and I'm on my way.

First: cool Beryl 3d effects that I saw on Youtube, just to torture the laptop.

Second: Talk to my existing windows workgroup. WHat's this? It sees the windows workgroup? I can play/copy my music from my desktop out of the box? My work can travel from one point to another? No additional software? YES! One worry gone.

Third: Java. Thanks to synaptic package manager, I was coding within a half hour of the thought entering my head.

This weekend: configure the "AMP" portion of LAMP. I have PHP and JSP classes in the near future.

This is beautiful. 

One gripe: that pesky keyring password matching user password bug. There's a fix, I'll implement it later.

Thanks to everyone for a great OS
