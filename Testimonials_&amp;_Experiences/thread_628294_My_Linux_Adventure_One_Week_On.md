### Post by SteveHoffmanUK on 2007-12-01
Hey Cajun Techie: Welcome to Linux and Ubuntu! Glad you had such a great out-of-the-box first experience. Lucky fellah.

Just one comment and caution, though:

If your wireless got installed and up and running without your having to do anything at all (which it sounds like), that must mean that you're running an unencrypted wireless network, otherwise it would need you to at least key in your WEP/WPA key.

While I agree that it's nice just to hit Firefox and have the net appear like magic, it's not really a good idea to run an unencrypted network, unless you like being generous to your neighbors! :)

An interesting experiment I tried at my work.  I had a wireless router and fired it up, with an "out of the box" configuration and no LAN connection, which means default SSID and no encryption.  The router was not up 30 seconds and there were 10 computers connected to it.  Since I was in charge of the network and the computers connected to it, I knew the names of the systems.  The computers that connected were not familiar, which means there were many roaming wireless computers within range in my office building.

Interestingly enough, I set the DHCP to expire max (which is about 6 days).....at the end of the week, there were over 30 unrecognized systems that had attached to the open wireless router.........

Depending on your location, you may need to take extra steps to ensure that no  one connects or attempts to hack your connection. (turn off the beacon).  If they are connected to your router, they have unfettered access to all computers connected..........
