### Post by sparkyjoe34 on 2008-06-09
I recently purchased a Belkin G Wireless USB Adapter so that I could move my computer upstairs in my house and not have to worry about running cables to it. 
I have a dual-boot system (Ubuntu 8.04 “Hardy Heron"/XP)and I tried and tried to get it up and running with the Belkin software that came with it and it would not see my signal from my wireless Linksys router. So then I figured I would let XP handle my wireless connection and although it saw the signal from my router, the connection was sporadic. 
Finally after about a day I thought DUH... I do have Ubuntu and even though I might have to do a little research on how to get it up and running, I might have some luck with it.
Needless to say as soon as Ubuntu booted up to the GUI I looked up in the right corner for my connection and it showed full strength. I clicked on it to connect, my router asked for my WEP key and after about 10 seconds I was connected. Sweet!  
This is yet another reason as to why I love Linux.

It's nice to hear the occasional success story with wireless, considering the number of problems. It's easy to forget that Windows has it's fair share of problems with wireless too - I found that out when I was having trouble getting a certain wireless PCI card I'd bought to work in Ubuntu and eventually, exasperated, I gave up and decided to swap it with the one in my girlfriend's computer. Unfortunately, XP didn't like the card either and I spent nearly a whole day trying to make it work in Windows before I had to just swap it back. A couple more hours later I'd finally managed to get it going in Ubuntu, which overall had taken less time than I spent trying to make it work in XP.

what I like especially is the re-setup of a system
