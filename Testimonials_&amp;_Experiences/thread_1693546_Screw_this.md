### Post by 3Miro on 2011-02-23
Ubuntu is Gnome-centric distribution, which means that non KDE variations will have some issues, Kubuntu in particular has somewhat of a bad reputation. One example is the Software Center, sound was rather problematic in the past (haven't used Kubuntu in some time), GTK based apps have to be tweaked to look normal ...

For KDE in particular, yes KDE is not Gnome and KDE stays away from anything Gnome or GTK. KDE is more customizable then Gnome, with widgets and overall better panels (Compiz is not an official part of Gnome so Kwin vs Compiz is not KDE vs Gnome). As for sluggishness, it depends on your hardware. KDE is more demanding, but once you cover the "minimum requirements", then you are just as fast.

If you don't like KDE, don't use it. However, if you want to learn more about it, try Mint, Sabayon, Susa or Slackware.
