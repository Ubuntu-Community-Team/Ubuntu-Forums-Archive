### Post by despidey on 2008-01-07
If you are a gamer, disregard this post. LOL

I've been using Gutsy for a month now.  I've installed Ubuntu, Edubuntu, Xubuntu on different "legacy" computers for different uses.  Each install had gone reasonably well with minimal intervention on my part. I am not a gamer so virtually everything I want to do can be gotten from repositories and installed flawlessly.

What I can't understand are people who are trying to move to a new O/S from Windows and complain when life isn't perfect, as if it had been before.  I read posts written by people who will mess around with code, registries and such to get a video card working, are willing to pay the never ending costs of upgrading their computers, O/S and all the apps within, but overlook a simple solution like spending 40 bucks for a card that just works - a one time investment.

Here's what you get from Ubuntu: 
A free operating system with good software that will run on almost any computer you can lay your hands on (although you may have to spend a few dollars to get it to work).
Free updates forever
A huge collection of cost free applications to do almost anything reasonable.
The world's fastest tech support for almost any problem
A huge worldwide community of people who spend untold days and weeks doing for free what MS/Apple pay millions to accomplish, often poorly.

Oh yeah...if you are insincere about wanting to get Ubuntu to work and post poorly thought out criticisms, you may get flamed. I personally don't agree with flaming, but I sure do understand it. This is the work of a community of people without a profit motive not some mega corporation looking for the next best way to repackage the same old stuff.  So give everyone here a break.

Whoops!! I guess this is a rant...sorry.
