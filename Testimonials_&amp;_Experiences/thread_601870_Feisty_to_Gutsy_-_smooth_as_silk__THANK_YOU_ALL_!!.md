### Post by Tony3286 on 2007-11-03
I just had to post and thank EVERYONE who posts and most of all
answers the posts. For two weeks, I've tried to read as much as I
could about **upgrading** from Feisty (which I loved) to Gutsy.
I've read all the nightmares of upgrading Gutsy and about clean installs. Well I bought a external USB hard drive, used Acronis 11 to backup my Feisty partition and even backed up my /home as a separate backup - just in case. After two weeks, I decided to make the plunge and I'm happy to report ALL IS WELL !!

Once again, thank you all for your time and most of all your patience!
I was new to Ubuntu 4 months ago when I installed Feisty and now
with Gutsy installed and EVERYTHING working I can finally say
a farewell to Windows ( at least for 99% of my computer work).

For those interested - I have a Sony Desktop, about 4 years old,
running Windows XP. I had upgraded my memory to 1000mb. I Dual
boot ,thanks to Ubuntu's Gpart  and Grub and all the posts on partitioning hard drives. If 'I", as a newbie could give any advice,
I would say:
                          1. Defrag your hard drive and clean it up
                             as much as possible before the install,
                             if this is your first attempt at Ubuntu.

                          2. BACKUP, BACKUP,BACKUP

                          3. The more memory the better
r
                          4. READ READ READ - the Ubuntu
                              forum. Have patience and search for
                              an answer to your question BEFORE
                              you post a question. More than not, you'll
                              find the answer to your question and
                              save these WONDER people, who respond,
                              the aggravation of answering the same 
                              question OVER and OVER ( I admit, I have
                              been guilty)

                          5. When a new upgrade is available,
                             ** WAIT** for the bugs to be ironed out.
                              A month is a good period of time.
            
                          6. And ***most of all*** give a
                             big thank you to everyone who gives a lending
                             hand - read the forums and try and help
                             others with your experiences. 

   Tony
   A very happy Gutsy user  :)
