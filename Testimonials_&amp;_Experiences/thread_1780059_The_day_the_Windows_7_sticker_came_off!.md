### Post by arukaddp on 2011-06-11
Hi all,

As an introduction of myself to this forum I wanted to share my migration experience from Windows to Ubuntu. 
First of all I have to say that I was/am not a Windows user by choice only, but my profession which at times involves graphical and CAD work makes it almost impossible for me to migrate to a unix distro 100%, as I use specialised software that is not always ported or able to run in wine with satisfying results. 

I have tried several times to migrate towards a unix based OS, the first time was somewhere around 2000 but the unfriendly face of Red Hat made me change my mind. Periodically I have installed or ran Live CDs just to see where the development was, and finally I thought I would make a bold move. 
At the present I am working towards my MA dissertation, and I have, for the most part, migrated from graphic work to the policy side of things so my work mostly consists of large amounts of text which people usually find extremely boring :D (probably as boring as this post :) ).

I tried migrating towards Ubuntu at the advice of a very good friend who was using Mint for quite some time, and was actually able to offer me support during this transition. I installed Ubuntu 10.04 and I was both amazed and frustrated at the same time, but I kept telling myself I was not going to quit this time. I had some issues with a couple of drivers which I managed to solve (I am kind of a get you hands dirty type, as I do this in Windows as well) by my self or with support from my friend, and I was kind of happy. My only problem was my webcam image was upside down (I have a X52F (K52F) laptop), and none of the solutions worked. I have also suffered through the hate relationship between Ubuntu and MSI routers which brought me to the edge, but I kept going. 
Two and a half weeks ago I installed Natty. Everything worked out of the box. I was amazed, the only remaining issue of the upside down webcam I managed to solve through a revisited solution which this time worked. Deep down I am kind of a eye-candy ***** :D but that is only because I spend a lot of time in front of the monitor and I like to see things that I enjoy. However, Unity managed to **** me off at maximum levels :D. It might be my Win7 reminiscences talking but I could not get it set up as I wanted, so I opted for the Classic look, but tweaked it with AWN, Conky, Compiz/Ubuntu Tweak and I finally made it look sexy as hell. 

Since I have to manage an enormous amounts of journals and books in Win7 I was using Zotero Standalone (I stopped using FF some time ago) which fortunately runs on Java so the port to unix works like a charm, after installing Sun Java 6. My problem was mainly the links to the files, that in the database where in Windows style, but that is nothing that a SQL editor could not fix. My only disappointment is the still existing incompatibility between MS Office / Libre Office / GDocs which kicks in at various points in time. Also because of my video card driver (apparently) MS Office has some artifacts and black spots in the menus while running in Wine, which should be resolved if I downgrade my video card driver, but which I do not feel confident about just yet, so it will wait. 
Overall, I have two weeks since I've seen Win7, except the occasional login in order to print, as my University does not support printing from unix systems, and I have to tell you I don't really miss it.  I am seriously thinking about migrating my wife to Ubuntu as well, but I have to put together a strategy so that everything works by point-and-click as she does not like to fiddle around with software. 

I might go back and try Unity at some point, and Gnome 3 looks really nice, but for the time being, and with my current knowledge my existing setup is more then enough.

Relating to the title of the post, today while cleaning my laptop the Win7 sticker was peeled forcefully off the laptop. ;)

It is true that maybe Ubuntu, or any other unix distro is not for everyone, but as long as you keep an open mind and have a little persistence you can make it through. 
I have found the UbuntuForums extremely useful, especially regarding the X/K52F laptops issues, and I am looking forward to further development from the Ubuntu community.

Alex 

P.S. Please excuse my occasional English slip-ups as it is not my first language.
