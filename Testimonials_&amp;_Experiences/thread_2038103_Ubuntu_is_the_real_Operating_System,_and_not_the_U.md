### Post by Ariya243 on 2012-08-05
**Ubuntu is the Operating System, and not the Ubuntu clones around in the Internet.** Here is my experience for last few days.

For few days I've been playing around with Zorin OS 6 Core and Ubuntu 12.04. I was thinking what's so big in Zorin OS 6 Core, just like I think of other Ubuntu remixed distros. What is this "Core?" I found the core is inside Ubuntu, and that Ubuntu12.04 is the core of every Ubuntu remixed distro we have in the net. Every user, who has a Ubuntu installed has his own remixed distro.

I am not a geek, but a user. So, I think like a user. 

First the Look-changer piqued my curiosity. I found out that it is GPL licensed, so I installed it in Ubuntu12.04. It was pretty easy--just copying and pasting files. I also gave back Software Center its Ubuntu name in Zorin 6. I found that it is easy to delete names or add them. I also found how to make/delete the slide show, which comes when installing.

After having spending some time on that Look Changer, I found that it is nothing other than 2 different AWN panels and with GnoMenu and Lancelot separately. Win 7 and XP eh? And that under the Zorin desktop, you have only Gnome Classic of new Gnome 3.

Earlier I thought it might be pure Gnome 3 without the Gnome-shell in Zorin OS 6 Core, but that is not to be. I like Cairo Dock--not that I don't like AWN--so, when I installed it and logged out, I found that the Unity Greeter has 3 additional DEs; Gnome, Ubuntu and Ubuntu fallback desktop environments (DE). Interestingly Unity DE had come back. It also had 3 Cairo Dock DEs, altogether 6 DEs + Zorin Desktop and Zorin DE fallback!!! Now, where are the 2 DEs, Gnome Classic and Gnome Classic with no effects?

To Ubuntu12.04, I added the proprietary drivers, Flash plugin, CCSM and few add-ons, and then installed Cairo Dock. Well, here comes the missing 2 DEs. So, Zorin desktop is Gnome Classic and Zorin fallback is Gnome Classic without effects. I checked few files in Gedit to be sure. Hmmm... Gedit had also got a name change to Text Editor.

So, we have a simple situation. Win7-look is a different AWN panel with Gnomenu and XP-look is another AWN panel with Lancelot. These are AWN looks, not otherwise. The Gnome2-look, is not a look, but the Gnome Classic without AWN panels, Gnomenu and Lancelot. So, I am having a distro with eye candy, and blings from Compiz, but with the Gnome Classic environment, not exactly modern at all. Not even modern from Ubuntu 12.04 without proprietary drivers, codecs and plugins.

Even the AWN panel is blocked from changing themes, which any user of AWN would like to do. AWN can be a very beautiful 3D dock. Even relieving information that there are updates from Cannonical and the Community is also blocked. Where is that 5-year LTS maintenance? Zorin OS 6 users are blocked from that LTS, even though it is assumed. If you were an absolute newbie from Windows, you wouldn't even know how to update.

Ubuntu 12.04 is more modern than Zorin OS 6 Core or Ultimate, as we can add all that eye candy with Compiz and all other applications. You can have the AWN dock working even exactly like Zorin's Win 7-look. Or you can have a beautiful 3D AWN panel.

Here goes.

You don't like Unity and the Gnome shell? OK! You want to install Cinnamon too? OK!
In your Ubuntu 12.04 installation, you install your proprietary drivers, flash plugin, etc and then you install Cairo Dock to pull out the Gnome, Gnome Classic etc. You don't like Cairo Dock? That is OK too. You can uninstall it later.

You install Cairo Dock through Synaptic or Ubuntu Software Center.

You log out and boot into Ubuntu DE. Then you install AWN dock. It is in the repos.

You go to Synaptic and install whatever that's not installed in Compiz including CCSM- all the addons, and whatever you install extra won't trouble you.

Now you click on the Avant Window Manager in Accessories and choose themes, applets, and also mark "Start Awn automatically." (If you need the Win7-look, you go to Zorin 6 /usr/share/avant-window-navigator/themes and copy ZorinOs.awn-theme and zorin-os-thumb.png inside thumbnails folder to your Ubuntu 12.04 /usr/share/avant-window-navigator/themes and to the thumbnails.) But, you might like 3D look, though...

Now you take out Unity, that is, if you don't like it. I like it! You can find how to uninstall Unity in the Internet.

You don't like Gnome-shell? You take it off too. But, you keep some Gnome stuff in, as you need to install Cinnamon someday...

And logout and boot back in. If you have not played with AWN panel, before you took Unity and Gnome-shell out, you can do that now. Actually, there is no need to take Unity and Gnome-shell out, as you won't be booting into them. It would be much better keep Gnome-shell and boot into Gnome and then autohide the top panel and completely block the Gnome-panel opening up. You can find out that from [https://extensions.gnome.org/](https://extensions.gnome.org/)

If you don't like Cairo Dock, you can now uninstall it.

Actually after fiddling with it for sometime, I find Zorin OS 6 a pretty old fashioned Ubuntu remix, the only thing that holds it with Windows refugees is Zorin Look Changer. My interest in Zorin OS 6 is over. There is nothing to learn from it. 

I am uninstalling Zorin OS 6 and going to work with Ubuntu 12.04. Every user, who has an Ubuntu installed, has his own remixed distro.

I take this opportunity to thank Cannonical and the Community!:D:D

PS: Why can't I or one of you make a Look-changer with Docky instead of Awn, or even with Awn, but who wants a Win 7 look or even the archaic XP look, when we have an excellent operating system and a distribution Ubuntu?! And you don't need to be a geek to do that, a bit of thinking...

By the way, the Win 7 and XP looks are from the creator(s) of Awn than from Zorin os...
