### Post by Gingalone on 2011-11-03
In my experience when updating Ubuntu there were always something to fix or to re-tune in the new Ubuntu, but generally there were only some minor troubles.
Upgrading (online) from 11.04 (Gnome2) to 11.10 was (and still is) a nightmare.
Online upgrade left my laptop almost dead. So I restored my old file system image (with Partimage) then reinstalled Oneiric from live CD (this time it was OK) then installed Gnome3. Then I tried to go back to Gnome2: as a result I got a dead system (no boot), again. Reinstalled Oneiric (from CD) on a new partition, copied old home folder to the fresh Ubuntu, installed Xfce4 and
here I am now (after a week of tragedy) still with problems (e.g. Xscreensaver doesn't accept characters that are not letters or numbers in pw, my Libreoffice macros keyboard shortcuts appear lost, Samba Connection to NAS shares impossible (continuous asking for share pw)....)

The lesson I learned: in the future I will stick to LTS releases of Ubuntu and, as a general comment, I think that the continuous hard search of new look, of novelties and the such is not always for the better from end-user point of view...

Ciao to all
