### Post by weblordpepe on 2008-07-17
Hi there boys & girls.

I once owned a laptop with Ubuntu 7,10, and a desktop with XP for gaming. My laptop died a while back due to severe hardware & hdd damage.

Since that time I had been using an Asus EEE with Xandros so I have been away from Ubuntu for a long time.

But I have suffered not 1, but 3 NTFS filesystem corruptions in a row on XP and I am sick of it. Every time I would recover my files to a new disk - BAM - the new disk would poop its pants and be unbootable.

Hardware problem? Nah...

Because I thought I'd try booting from Linux to check. And this is the part of the story where I bump into the brand spanky new Ubuntu 8.04. Holy cow dude Canonical & the FOSS community just won't stop eh? This desktop experience is damn polished and sexy.

I had a love affair with compiz but we decided to split up due to personal differences (ATI drivers on laptop).

But the good news is that my desktop has a modern nVidia card, and Compiz and I are dating again!!

Watch out Mark Shuttleworth, you're going to be a granddad. There's going to be little Weblordpepe/Compiz hybrids running around here shortly. I'm pregnant.

Ok that last paragraph was a bit of an exaduration. Although I really am impressed. And don't get me started on this Ubuntu MID or Ubuntu EEE :)

I have got a thread going for my NTFS recovery help if anyone's interested:  [NTFS file recovery for Linux]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=5403771#post5403771")
