### Post by nightfire117 on 2007-06-19
I have Ubuntu Feisty on my PC, dual-booting with XP Pro SP2, and Ubuntu Feisty on my notebook. The reason I installed it on my notebook is so I will start to switch over from Windows. Basically all I need to do is word processing, Internet (for e-mail, news, IM and communications, etc.), and maybe a *few* games - most of which (I checked) are supported by WINE.

The change to Ubuntu is pretty much just wanting "something different," and the feeling of better control over your PC. (As well as less overhead, hehe.) And the abundance of free and open programs you can download easily and for $0.00 is very nice.

Pretty satisfied with Feisty on both my desktop and notebook, but what keeps me from switching full-time on my PC is simply Foobar2000 and the abundance of programs on the PC such as WLM that make communication simpler. 

As for my notebook, everything worked out-of-the-box. Feisty + Gnome upon startup uses about 130 MB of RAM, so I installed Fluxbox and thus uses about 90. (Even better than Windows, I gotta say.) Not using Fluxbox since there are still some issues to be resolved but I might tackle them today.

I love Ubuntu's support for hardware, ease of use with Synaptic Package Manager, and the language support. I use OpenOffice (even on my PC), Quod Libet (and am going to try Listen and Exaile), Firefox with my favorite plugins, and the assurance of better security in that I am not as vulnerable to viruses and malicious software.

But Foobar2000, erm, deserves an honorable mention and is unfortunately a definite +1 point for Windows. Hehe. And thus Foobar2000 and Windows Live Messenger, and perhaps a lack of Photoshop CS2 support in Wine (though I have yet to test out some of the methods that supposedly "work") are keeping me back. Otherwise I've been a user for over a year now, on-and-off, my first distro being Debian.

The community here's been awesome, they've always helped me and provided great information, and they're responsive and fast. I'm very pleased with that.

Thanks to Ubuntu and the community. Keep it up!

~Nightfire
