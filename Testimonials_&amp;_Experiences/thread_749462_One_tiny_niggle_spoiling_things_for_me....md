### Post by Bluecube on 2008-04-08
I've used Ubuntu for nearly a year now without any major issues - or so I thought. Firstly I should say that I really like Ubuntu. It works and I've never had any problems that weren't my fault! But I've just come across an issue that's now driving me nuts now that I know about it - hard drive performance.

I was copying a 20Gb of MP3s from one internal drive to another (a SATA to IDE if it makes any difference) and the performance was beyond dreadful. 3.1Mb a second... On investigating it further I found out it was because I didn't have HDPARM working so I looked into it and have had a hell of a time trying to get it to work. My issues with HDPARM aren't what I'm complaining about though. What I want to know is why HDPARM isn't installed and automatically setup as a matter of course by Ubuntu. I can already hear folks typing in "compatibility issues" but if Windows can squeeze max performance out of my hard drives with no tweaking whatsoever then why can't Ubuntu? I would expect at least 15Mb/s from my hard drives so this is quite a major issue. 

And yes, I know this may be seen as a very minor niggle on what's been a relatively long and fruitful relationship with Ubuntu but it has soured the experience slightly :)
