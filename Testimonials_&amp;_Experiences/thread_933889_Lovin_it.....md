### Post by cha0sthe0ry on 2008-09-29
I have two laptops and 2 desktops in my house.  As of now, all but one is on linux, the 4th is my zune computer (hopefully soon that will change).  I have learned so much in the time that I have had Linux, I gave it a couple months and now there is nothing intimidating about it.  I have tried many distros, but this is the one for me, at least for now.  There are so many places to go for help, I have never not found what I am looking for, even if it is obscure.  I write this hoping that someone who is having trouble or expecting that Ubuntu is a piece of cake will see that it only takes a little getting used to.  Those new shoes aren't always comfortable the first time you put them on, are they?

My girlfriend, the owner of one of the two laptops in the house, knows little about computers at all, let alone Linux.  Yet she is on her Ubuntu now (she just learned how to change the color scheme to pink *barf*).  While I have had to do some major things for her, like set up Office 2007, which is needed for college, she has done most everything else.  The point is that with even little computer knowledge it is possible to use Linux, especially Ubuntu.

These forums (first choice) and google (second) will yield virtually any answer you need.  If you can't find it, don't give up, just log in here and ask the question.  I know I have asked questions, and gotten answers within minutes, literally.  Just give this a real shot before you take the easy way and go back to Windows (or Mac).  I assure you, it isn't the easy way.  This isn't any harder, just different, and definitely doable.
