### Post by Ateo on 2007-04-26
For those who say Ubuntu isn't ready for the masses, please take this advice. With a  **LITTLE** effort on **YOUR** behalf, research before you buy your hardware. Verify that the hardware you plan to buy is supported by Linux. Not only will this make things easier for **YOU** but you'll also send the message to hardware manufacturers that you are ditching them for their Linux friendly competitor. They will notice as their sales decrease.

Here's a tip. Buy NVIDIA. For laptops, make sure you get the Intel wireless card (IE: IPW 2100/2200BG/2915ABG/3945ABG)... With this combo, you're certain to have a great experience....

----------- And now, for the testimonial(s)...

My mother, a 67 year old woman is using Feisty Fawn. I installed it on her box shortly after Ubuntu gave me so much love and just worked (last day of march 2007). She uses it for everything except Quickbooks so I did leave it as a dual boot. However, with time, hopefully QB will have Wine support which will complete her transition.... (maybe it already does, I just haven't researched it yet)...

I think the best part of this experience is that she has both Gnome and KDE to play with AND both are in her native language, Spanish. Putting Ubuntu into one's native language is easy. The same cannot be said for Windows.

As for me, personally... I've been using various Linuxes since 1997. I survived Redhat's RPM hell with little scars. I've compiled 4 years worth of packages with Gentoo. I know Linux and I know it well enough for it to be my primary work environment, both server and desktop. I've seen it evolve and never has anything hit the market that is this easy and this pleasant that just works.

The thought of bloat originally repelled me from Ubuntu. I tried it a couple of years ago but did not have the same experience I did this time. Installation failed and I lost interest as I did not want to troubleshoot a distribution I was not familiar with. Installing every driver known to man isn't what I am accustomed to. I like tight kernels. I like installing only what I need. But after a week with Ubuntu Feisty Fawn (beta) and a little thought, I learned to care less about bloat and focus more on whether I can work or not. In order for Linux to ever have an impact on a world dominated by one OS, bloat must happen. If bloat bothers you, consider this. At least the bloat that comes with Ubuntu isn't crap like AOL, MSN Explorer and other crap that comes pre-installed even with a retail version of Windows.

If Feisty Fawn is this good, image what Gusty Gibbon will be like in 6 months.

Oh, btw, the server edition has no bloat. It is a thin, nicely installed base system with BIND and/or LAMP installed at your request.
