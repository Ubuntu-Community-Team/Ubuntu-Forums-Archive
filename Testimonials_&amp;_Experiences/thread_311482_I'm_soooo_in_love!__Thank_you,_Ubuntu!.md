### Post by Chatbox on 2006-12-02
Being a laptop (IBM T41P, 2373-GGU) user most of the time for work (software integration, mostly IBM related stuff) and personal purposes, I've been somewhat reluctent to switch to a Linux-based OS because of the possibility of the lack of hardware support.  However, I really want to get away from the Windows environment, not sure why exactly, but I just want to get the hell away from Windows as my main OS.

Fedora Core 6 was my first pick at the time when I switch over.  Being an RHCE, I thought it would have made the transition somewhat easy on myself.  Man, I was soooo wrong about that and spent 3 weeks in hell with FC6.  First off, FC6 installed (default installation) a i586 kernel on my Thinkpad instead of a i686 kernel and that caused the CPU to run at full speed all the time (unable to throttle).  Wifi wasn't working, bluetooth was flaky, no onscreen display for the Thinkpad function keys (volume, screen brightness...etc), suspend to memory or to harddrive were having issues.  OMFG, FC6 was just not polished enough for my Thinkpad (not sure about other laptop users).

Then the time came again to reconsider my choice of OS for my laptop.  Heading back to the dark-side?  No freaking way, not without a fight anyway.  So let's try another distro.  Not certain as to whether another distro was going to help, but I was sure about that putting a distro together is no easy task and does require a great deal of understanding of the open-source software, hardware on the market and what users want to do with a computer...and possibly more.  I wasn't sure about Ubuntu at all, but that's the name I keep on seeing a lot of when I google Linux related stuff.  Hell, I don't even know how to say the word right (I still don't).  Downloaded the CD (only one CD?  Wow...sweet, better than downloading 5 CDs when I was using FC6, or a DVD).  I was totally surprised when I first booted up my Thinkpad with it, for a few reasons.  One, it's a live & installation CD??  Cool!  Two, is that my Wifi icon in the corner?  Sweet lord!  It connected to my neighbore's AP (insecure, un-WEP/WPA) without any configuration.  Three, Thinkpad's function keys were working WITH on screen display.  That's just freaky!!  Everything that should be working were working just by boothing from the CD...without an installation.  Man, I was sold!

Jumped right into the installation, totally smooth!!  No problem there at all.  Boot time was exceptionally fast (compare to Windows XP and FC6).  My experience with Ubuntu is just like falling in love for the first time, everything is how it should be, and more!!

To the Ubuntu Team:  Thank You for putting this distro together, it's a job well done, to perfection!!

Software update is easy and FAST (damn Yum takes forever!), choosing software sources was so easy!  The lack of AIGLX or XGL doesn't bother me at all because as far as work goes, having an OS that works well, smooth and stable out-of-the-box is so much more important than eye-candy.
