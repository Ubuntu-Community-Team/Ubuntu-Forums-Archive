### Post by Algus on 2011-05-24
I'm not much of a tech-head (which leads to a lot of "Why do you use Linux?" questions from my techie friends) so usually when I have problems with my build, it takes me a few days of asking questions on these forums to get things fixed.

Among other things, I was a bit timid of installing new software on my machine from the net an mostly stuck to either terminal commands people told me when they were helping me or what I could fetch from Synaptic.  

I had a lot of headaches with 11.04 - webcam didn't work, laptop wouldn't shut down, etc.  However, being busy I just didn't have time to look into a lot of these problems and to my very pleasant surprise I found that almost all of my problems have been patched over the last week or so. 

To top it off, I just tried grabbing the new version of Skype and with how the package opens right into the Ubuntu Software Center, it is easy as heck to set up now.  

I really think Ubuntu is starting to get there for people who aren't super savvy with using the terminal and knowing all the mechanics of how a computer operates.  In just a matter of weeks, 11.04 has gone (at least for me) from being a bit to buggy to being a dream to use.  

To the Ubuntu team - thanks a lot!
