### Post by jingo811 on 2008-05-31
On Windows you change OS maybe once every 5 or 10 years.  
On most Linux distros you change OS nearly every year.  That means there's a higher error frequency for Linux users compared to Windows users over a certain amount of years.  I'd like to illustrate this to Windows defectors so they don't come on board this ship thinking: 

open source **=>** Free **=>** Don't have to spend time administering it. **=>** Angry posts on forums.

Personally I think changing OS every 2-3 years is annoying, no matter the reasons.  But that's because I'm a lazy person :)
There should be a 5-10 years **VLTS** (Very Long Term Support) release then I would be happy!
