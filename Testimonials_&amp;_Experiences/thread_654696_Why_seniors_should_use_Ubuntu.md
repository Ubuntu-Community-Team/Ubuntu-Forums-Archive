### Post by despidey on 2007-12-31
As a senior I love Ubuntu because:
1.  It challenges me to do new things. I don't think of myself as a geek, but In the process of resurrecting old hardware, mostly with spare parts, I've learned how to install hard drives, memory and cards.
2.  In the process of rebuilding 3 machines so far, I've started to understand the software installation process and how to search these forums for solutions to problems.
3.  I'm not a gamer or media nerd so almost any hardware will do what I want in Ubuntu: Internet, e-mail, office apps, etc. If you have older machines and follow install instructions explicitly, it basically works out of the box, at least it has (mostly) for me.  
4. I am awed at the ability of a group of volunteers to create and regularly update an incredibly elegant operating system twice a year and then support it thru this community.  It would probably cost Microsloth 10s of millions of dollars to reproduce the support system commercially what is available to anyone here for free.
5. I'm concerned about the environment.  Ubuntu allows people to reuse computers that typically would be destined for landfill.
6.  There is a lot of talk about how seniors stay in good shape mentally by doing things that challenge them intellectually, and socializing with younger people. This certainly fills that bill.
7.  As a child of the 1960's I love the whole open source concept.  It reminds me that ordinary people working in unison can accomplish monumental tasks for altruistic reasons.

I'd be interested in finding/creating a group of seniors who are Ubuntu users to hang out with on-line.  Sorry for the long post
