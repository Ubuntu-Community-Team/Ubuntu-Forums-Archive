### Post by dkaddict on 2007-10-17
Is this as good as Gutsy gets? A day before release and loads of problems. This ain't gonna look good for Ubuntu. If someone trying Ubuntu for the first time has to wait for as long as I am for their comp to boot up will they persist and stay with this distro? Will they stay with Linux? If they choose to try and sort their problem(s) out by way of coming here and looking for answers but only get a rude response or ticking off for attempting to use a development version of Ubuntu (regardless of how close the cycle is to a full release) will they still want to try Ubuntu? I know I wouldn't. The adage 'don't fix what ain't broke' keeps ringing in my ears.
 
The above gives me reason to ask if the present six month release cycle a good idea? It seemed to be when I did my first upgrade from Edgy to Feisty but now I have my doubts. I know that some may, in response to the above, say that a new user should install the long term support 6.04 release and they may well have a point. In the real world, however, new users, and old for that matter, will want the latest release because it will  supposedly have all of the new functionality, fonts, etc. Perhaps a yearly release cycle would give the developers time enough to provide end users with a fully functional, less hurried, upgrade when the time of release comes? Just a thought, albeit a pertinent thought given the current state of affairs.

 Also, when I come to the forums to seek help to whatever problem I may have, or to offer help to others if I am able to, yet find myself confronted with a moderators post which is SHOUTING at me with huge fonts in garish colours, I really lose respect for whoever feels it is necessary to treat Joe Bloggs end users such as myself as children and/or lesser mortals. 

No. No. No.

 The shouting at us with huge fonts is both rude and demeaning. Is it, therefore, absolutely necessary? If someone wants to try a beta release of Ubuntu, the very fact that they want to try it means they most likely realise that their comp could suffer some bugs and breakage. If they don't know the score, they most definitely will when they run into a problem. They do not need an angry moderator or anyone else shouting at them to realise they may be in over their heads. My technical knowledge comes nowhere near the level of expertise which one post deems (by way of obnoxious, huge text and garish colours) one should possess before even thinking about trying out a development release of Gutsy. If attempting to upgrade was as dangerous and stupid as the screaming warnings in these forums would have one believe, why on earth had my update manager been giving me the opportunity to upgrade to Gutsy for the last month? Surely this is contradictory? Anyway, I don't like being shouted at so went ahead and upgraded to Gutsy. My first upgrade attempt rendered my xserver useless. I had a pretty good idea about how to solve my problem but would have liked a little advice. For the first time since switching to Ubuntu, however, I hesitated before leaving a post here that explained my dilemma and asked for advice. I hesitated because I didn't want to get shouted at or told off for trying to upgrade my comp (despite the fact that the option had been available in update-manager for some time). I hesitated because I am obviously deemed by some to be so stupid that I need to be shouted at.

I did leave a post in the end but I am obviously nowhere near guru enough to warrant the attention or advice of the real Ubuntu gurus so my post drew a blank. I had a good idea of how to fix Gutsy, however, and managed to get my Ubuntu upgraded and functional. There are glitches though and the fact that they remain at so late a stage in the upgrade cycle is cause for concern (I know I am not alone in thinking thus). 

I am going to stick with Gutsy for the time being because I can live with the 5minute boot up and know what is causing my notebook (which ran flawlessly and fast with Feisty) to behave so sluggishly in some areas. The slow boot up etc is a result of the following. In order to be able to connect to my wireless router and have USB working at the same time I need to append the 'kernel' line in '/boot/grub/menu.lst with the 'irqpoll' option. Using 'noapic' or 'apci=off' will enable USB but kills my built in wireless (Fujitsu Siemens Amilo L7310GW  nb-I know this is not the best piece of hardware for running Ubuntu and that there are hardware specific problems surrounding the combination of the notebook+Ubuntu. It is all I have for the time being.). 

I am hoping the coming updates will correct this behaviour. If, in a months time, the situation remains with no fix on the horizon I may go back to Feisty and wait for 8.04 or try another distro with perhaps KDE4 or something. I do like Ubuntu and like Gnome because it is easy to create my own gtk-2.0 themes and metacity and just seems to be more intuitive and, when working ok, more stable than KDE.

That's me really. I am not trying to have a dig at anyone in particular. Honestly. 
Remember this--when someone starts shouting at another person, at that point in time, the time of the first raised voice, communication between the two parties has broken down. There's no need for it.

peace

dk
