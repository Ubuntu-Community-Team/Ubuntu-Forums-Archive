### Post by ByKeLaO on 2008-03-31
I finally got around to installing Ubuntu Hardy, and... just wow.
Finally Ubuntu has a user interface that isn't just functional, but it looks nice too. And the new wallpaper is awesome.
But that's not all. The developers have finally put the "working" into "wireless networking". My rt73-based card was recognized straight away, without the use of ndiswrapper! Ubuntu Hardy is truly a quantum leap towards a true works-out-of-the-box operating system.
What's more, the installer actually detected my operating systems and configured GRUB properly. I was expecting to have to do some editing to menu.lst to get it working, but no!
I still find it hard to believe that this operating system is free. It's just friggin incredible.

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-04-08
I've installed Hardy beta on a computer that I've built for a friend. It's been rock-solid so far - the only indications that it's a development release are the flickering before the login screen and the fact that there's 300 odd updates in the Update Manager.

This machine is the tail edge of the computer industry - An Athlon X2 4200 CPU, 1 gig of RAM, integrated ATI card that steals 128 megs of RAM from the system. Yet Hardy runs very nicely on it.
