### Post by SC_Frank on 2006-10-28
Greetings all - 

After shunning Ubuntu's ratings at Distrowatch, I decided to give it a shot and try it for at least a month. Well, I've been using Dapper for about 2 months now and I'm totally content with it. Worth noting is that I've dabbled in Linux since Mandrake 7.2, (I was not too keen on using Gnome, preferring to use KDE) and I've used almost all versions of Windows. After two months, I can confidently say I will not change my OS any longer...I'm that happy with Dapper. Bare with me and I'll give you some of the reasons I love Ubuntu --
1. In 20 years of working with PCs, it's the easiest OS to install...period. A few clicks and less than 15 minutes later - I have a functional, loaded PC, ready to use. 
2. The application selection is awesome and all this on a single CD. Plus, the repositories extend Ubuntu's capabilities far beyond what I expected. 
3. There were always a handful of things I could not do in Linux (listen to my music subscription, print directly on CDs, etc). I can do all of these easily in Ubuntu, so I truly have no need for another OS.
4. Forums are friendly and helpful. I've needed some advise (not much though, as Ubuntu works so well) and was able to get it quickly.
5. It's stable and virus free ----:-D 
6. I actually like the "non-blue" Human color scheme and icons. And the art manager makes adding wallpaper, controls, borders and the like, a piece of cake.
7. It's free and I don't need to register, activate, validate .... well, you all know what I mean. Just install it, and use it. 
8. It supports all of my hardware after the initial install, with the exception being use of the proprietary nVIDIA drivers, but once again, thanks to the forum, that too is easy.
9. I love eyecandy and with XGL enabled, it's slick, slick slick. 
10. I've learned more in these last two months than the prior 10 years of using PCs. 
11. (This is more towards Linux in general) My PC is fun to use again. I enjoy using it and playing with settings, schemes and other things. 
12. It's easier to network with my family's windows PCs than it was with XP.

There's probably more reasons, but the post would be too long. Suffice to say, I'm happy and I want to thank all the developers, admins and users of Ubuntu who have worked so hard to make this distro what it is.
