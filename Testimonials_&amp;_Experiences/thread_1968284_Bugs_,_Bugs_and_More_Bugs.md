### Post by Tamlynmac on 2012-04-29
> cdo

Historically, I've always waited for > than a month to install a new  release. It's been my experience that updates often resolve many of the  release bugs. By simply delaying the install process one can effectively  skip the initial bugs. Some will argue that it's part of the experience  and by providing feedback they help to improve the release. Which is  fine, but for those users who rely on their system(s) and require  dependability, it might make more sense to be patient.

Or, one could use a separate HDD or partition to test the new release  and identify that bugs could be an issue. This step is one I recommend  to all Ubuntu users. Test before committing, unless it's a non-essential  PC. :-k

Just m $0.02
