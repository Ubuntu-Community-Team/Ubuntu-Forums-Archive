Anyone looking for a compatible all-in-one printer - I can recommend the Epson Stylus DX7400 - it prints, copies, and scans easily in Ubuntu linux.

Hi,

[This post]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=4498083&postcount=5") gives easy instructions for escputil, a utility to clean and align the heads of Epson Stylus printers.  It can also check the current ink levels in the printer (it gives nice little percentages).

It's terminal based (that is, no pretty pictures), but it does a great job for me. :-)

### Post by ChrisWebb on 2009-07-06
For anyone still wondering, you can use the scanner driver provided by Epson (via Avasys), just go to [http://www.avasys.jp/lx-bin2/linux_e/spc/DL1.do](http://www.avasys.jp/lx-bin2/linux_e/spc/DL1.do) and select your printer from the list.

In the case of the DX7400 the printer is already supported in Ubuntu (I'm using Jaunty) so you only need the scanner driver. There's even a 64 bit DEB.

Ubuntu stole me from OpenSuSE, and I don't think I'm going back.
