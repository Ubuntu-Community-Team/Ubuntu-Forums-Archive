### Post by TeraDyne on 2007-06-13
I've been using Kubuntu since shortly after the Breezy release, and now I can't live without many of the simple features Linux has. I'm currently running Fiesty and I love it.

I had used nothing but Windows since Windows 3.11 for Workgroups. After finding out about Ubuntu, I decided to throw it onto my laptop just to try it out. I really didn't like the GNOME interface. When I tried Kubuntu, however, I fell in love with the KDE interface. 

Being someone who loves to customize his desktop, I can honestly say that the tools KDE has makes it MUCH easier than anything Windows comes with. With Windows, I didn't have much choice in terms of themes without buying some expensive program and going out to find and download themes for it. With KDE, it's built right in. If there's something that can't be customized, chances are that it can be via some sort of configuration file. If I can't find a theme that I need, I can just make it, and it's much simpler to do so in KDE than in Windows.

Another thing I like is the amount of programs it comes with by default. OpenOffice, Amarok, Kopete, Kontact, Konqueror, digiKam, KTorrent, and so on. And getting any application I need is a snap with Adept, or even opening Konsole or switching to another terminal and using aptitude.

I also love the fact that it's easy to install and upgrade Kubuntu. I have to admit that, as someone who has installed Windows 98 and Windows XP, it's MUCH faster and much simpler that trying to get windows installed and running. Upgrading to the next version is even faster, since you just run a simple command and start the update, and everything is taken care of. No disks or anything.

Finally, I love the fact that it's fast. It's much faster than Windows on this laptop. It's an eMachines M2352 with 512 MB of RAM, 64 MB shared Video RAM, and a 2.16GHz AMD Athlon XP 3000+ Mobile. Even KDE runs faster than Windows XP using the classic interface. Very nice "feature", if you will.

Kubuntu, and more importantly Linux in general, has truly gained my support. Awesome OS, and awesome people behind it. Two thumbs up!
