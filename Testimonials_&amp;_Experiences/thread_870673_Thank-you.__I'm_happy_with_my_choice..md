### Post by Janeleaper on 2008-07-26
A few months after getting a new Dell computer loaded with Windows XP, Service Pack 2 installed badly and had to be sorted by a Microsoft technician. She got my machine working again, but with the idiosyncrasy that all folders opened in a new window regardless of what option I chose. In the real world if you buy faulty goods you can expect to get your money back -- but that does not apply in the world of Microsoft.

A while later a friend who'd never used a computer before, was given one loaded with a Linux based system, and I helped him learn how to use it.  I was surprised and impressed by how user friendly it was.  

By this year my XP computer had developed more tics, including a 10 minute boot up time and a virus that caused jpegs to duplicate when opened that was not recognised by my Symantic virus scan and had somehow got through my firewall. So, I decided it was time for a new computer and this time it was not going to be Windows. 

After a bit of research I decided that Ubuntu seemed the most non-geek friendly of the Linux systems, and also had the advantage I could get it pre-installed on a Dell machine.  The only real alternative was buying a Mac, but I was deterred by the cost (and also their ads which get on my nerves - it is possible to be too cool). Even so, I had to take a deep breath before taking the plunge.  

I've had my Ubuntu Dell Inspiron for a couple of weeks now.  It probably did take longer to set up to my satisfaction than a new Windows loaded machine would have done, but it was more fun and I haven't had to spend hours on the phone to technicians.  I've got all the advice I needed on these forums. My new computer now does everything I want it to, more elegantly than Windows, and isn't loaded with dozens of programmes I don't need and will never use.

Ubuntu/linux features that I like most: 
[LIST]
[*]Tomboy notes - more user friendly than notes in Microsoft Office Outlook
[*]The way Ubuntu found my internet connection and network without my having to do anything.
[*]Firefox - much better than IE

I've downloaded JUploader for my Flickr account and GRAMPS for my gedcon files and I'm very happy with both programmes.

So I'm very happy with the choice I made and a full convert to the way of Ubuntu!


[/LIST]
