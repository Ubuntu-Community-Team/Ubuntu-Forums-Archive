### Post by jlspawnas on 2008-02-11
I just wanted to thank all the users who have come before me.  Thank to their hard work and the posts on these forums I had the simplest and easiest install of a Linux distro ever.  My goal was to achieve as much functionality in Ubuntu as I have in Windows XP or on my MBP.  For my uses, I would say I am at about 90%.  

Hardware wise almost everything was detected at install and with the exception of updating to the restricted Nvidia drivers, is working perfectly.  The only two pieces of hardware attached that currently are not working, are my Canon S520 printer and Epson V100 Photo scanner.  The printer is receiving print jobs, just not printing.  I assume it does not like any of the existing drivers.   Epson does offer Linux drivers for this scanner, but I have only found them in .rpm and I am currently too much of a newbie to attempt converting them.

Software wise I have found more than enough to accomplish anything I would need to do in Windows.  It has not always been easy to install, but so far I have always been able to find a thread in the forums that stepped me thru the process.  I am not much of a command line guy, but I do have a new found appreciation for those who are.

Once again, Thanks to everyone.
