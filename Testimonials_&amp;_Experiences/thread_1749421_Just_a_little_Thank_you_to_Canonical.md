### Post by Leed on 2011-05-04
Right now there's still a lot of critisism and some talk on bugs that still need fixing.... and yes I was also one of those who didn't smile upon the first look on Unity. 

But the more I study it, the more I begin to like it. I still have all my Compiz Effects, I found out where to make the launcher Icons smaller (even thou the 3d Effect when having to many icons is cool), I freed the Indicator Icons... After learning the Shortcuts from the from the Power user guide things got really cool. 

Therefore I can only say:

[SIZE="4"]**Thank you Canonical.. the world needs more of your kind!!**[/SIZE]

Just a few things many don't notice at first

[LIST]
[*]Unity is made by Canonical... so their investing money to make a cool Ubuntu optimized Desktop Environment
[*]Unity does/will offer multitouch support, so netbooks, tablets or whatever will run well with it
[*]You can change the Icon size in the launcher
[*]You can set the icons to glow when a program is opened and to pulse while a program is being opened
[*]The icons group instances of the same program
[*]If you get used to using a screen edge for "desktop switcher" and one for "scale" you will get much more productive
[*]You can still move windows when in the "desktop switcher"
[*]A right click in the Desktop switcher immediately goes to that desktop (no need for doulbe clicks)
[*]If you want to see something else that's cool, and we may soon have... check out what a Unity "Lens" is
[*]Right clicking Launcher Icons gives you more options
[*]Instead of searching for something, just press alt+F2 and start typing the name, you'll find what you want much faster
[*]Just a personal favorite, activate the animations plugin in ccsm and set "window focus" to dodge, Close/open to magic lamp... I just luv it
[/LIST]

The one thing I believe Canonical missed out, is to tell people what's cool about Unity... So I believe the best thing to do is spread the word :)
