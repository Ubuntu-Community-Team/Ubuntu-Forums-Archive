### Post by thetimekeeper on 2007-11-02
I have been using Windows most of my life, from NT, 95, 98, ME, XP and even Vista.

It's what I am used to, and I humbly consider myself somewhat of an expert in its workings and troubleshooting.

Then Ubuntu 7.04 came out and I decided to install it, dually with my XP installation as I was doing a fresh install of my hard disk. I got as far as installing Compiz Fusion, not without some stutters, and sort of left it alone as there were strange glitches when I used it.

Cut to when Ubuntu 7.10 came out. I grabbed a CD from the university media center, did a complete format of my ext3 partition, and reinstalled it. I'm glad I did.

Here is my current, customized desktop with Compiz Fusion on full blown:
[http://img136.imageshack.us/img136/3094/screenshotvq3.png](http://img136.imageshack.us/img136/3094/screenshotvq3.png)

I like it. I am also the most surprised at this, and I can't explain it precisely. Maybe I'm just a sucker for a cool interface (Super + Tab!). I have my usual ftp, music, files, browser, everything customized to my tastes, and MSN is serviceable if a bit clunky using Pidgin. I even have WINE so I can amuse myself with some small windows apps. It's come to the point where I prefer working on my Ubuntu desktop to working in XP.

 Some of my minor irritants are the not very elegant file browse/toolbar interfaces, there seems to be an awful lot of wasted space, especially in Firefox. 

The file browser also takes a bit of time to get used to, as well as the file system naming conventions. Generally, it's a pretty positive experience, but I do miss the more 'slick' feel of the toolbar interfaces, especially the professional look of Outlook 2007. I know there are themes for thunderbird, but they don't seem to do that much, and the amount of wasted space in the toolbar remains.

However, one major tweak is that, in my opinion (I may be wrong), Linux pretty much sucks compared to Windows in the field of graphics and web design. My FTP client (tried both gFTP and Filezilla) is clunky, GIMP may have a lot of hidden power but I sure can't find it! 

Nothing really beats Dreamweaver and Photoshop CS3, especially the auto-complete and other features. I guess for those tasks, I will still be booting to my XP partition.

Not a bad price to pay though, for an awesome looking and interesting OS. It's certainly different, I can say that.

Just my two cents. :)

### Post by carlosjuero on 2007-11-02
I actually prefer Bluefish to Dreamweaver.. sure, it doesn't have the code complete I have grown used to on Windows side with Dreamweaver.. but it gets me back into the Raw code which I have missed. Plus, running a web server & mysql seamlessly in the background (much more seamless than in Windows) makes it all that much easier for me to develop my web sites.

Of course I am a coder first and foremost, and a designer second - so the design aspects of Dreamweaver were never fully utilized by me.

That said, supposedly Komposer (used to be NVU) is a dreamweaver 'replacement' equiv here in Linux; and GIMP, while not as 'sexy' as Photoshop, can do just about everything you can think of in Photoshop - it just takes more getting used to.

Like my sig says though, I am in sort of the same boat; 90% of my time I am here in Ubuntu, but the other 10% I am back in Windows to work on some .NET programming, voice chat with a friend on Vent, or play a game that doesn't run right in WINE.

Once I figure out ways to bypass those few things, I will become 100% Ubuntu :)
