### Post by some_random_noob on 2007-01-23
The best way you can spread Linux is by talking to someone in real life. I set up Ubuntu for my dads-friend (on an old computer) and showed him around just as an example, because Vista is coming out and I wanted to show this guy an alternative just for his interests. 

Then he asked if his printer would work. I had no idea what I was doing but I went into the obvious place: system->administration and then found the printers management section. We configured it to go through the USB port and then clicked "install driver" for his HP printer. A box popped up asking where the drivers were, so I knew we had to download them :mad: 

Failing that, I clicked forward and continued without drivers, so the only change is telling Ubuntu to use the USB port. I went into openoffice and typed "linux rocks" and clicked print. Then it printed with no problem (actually the paper got totally munched on the way out, because there was stuff in the way of where the paper comes out and it got a bit jammed... But thats not Linux's problem :lolflag: )
---------------------------------------------------------------

So anyway, Ubuntu ran although it was a little bit slow. And the printer seemed to work fine. So............... that my story I guess? I knew he had an old computer and I was very tempted to find a use for it. I buggered off, caught 2 buses and went to his house – well worth my time. I might go there again and try to get the internet working because he seemed interested in getting that done.

This guy doesn't know heaps about computers, hes also not stupid. He doesn't like the idea of Vista and we laughed at a shopping catalogue that was promoting it along with other trash :D 

I just thought I'd post this because someone else might be inspired/motivated. I found it pretty damn easy... and..... um....... thats about it.
