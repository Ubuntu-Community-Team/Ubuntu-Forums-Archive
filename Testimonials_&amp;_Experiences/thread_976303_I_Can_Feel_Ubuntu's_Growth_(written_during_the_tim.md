### Post by wersdaluv on 2008-11-09
I've been using Ubuntu since Edgy and I would say that a lot has changed. Before, after a fresh install, I had to fix a lot of issues and configuring my hardware is the biggest among them. Looking back, I am happy because a lot of improvements come release after release. A version might not be so different from its predecessor but versions that are a year away from each other feel so different.

I remember the days when my main issue was making USB support work on Ubuntu. I spent six months just for that. Syncing my Pocket PC also took me a lot of time but it's still problematic now but the difference is that, I don't bother trying to make it work anymore. My simple issues before were about look and feel. I hated so much how Rhythmbox looked. The icons just sucked big time. hehe. Also, I spent so much time changing my GNOME's theme. I always hated ubuntulooks. hehe. I envied OS X and Vista users for their very polished nice desktop look and feel. I used to think that I'm in a less aesthetically pleasing desktop. Now, it's nice that it's gone and people are utilizing murrine better. I'm still not contented with Ubuntu's default theme though but I managed to fall in love with the Human feel. Now, I'm using the unofficial, Shiki mod, the Human-Dust theme plus the Human icons. Ubuntu's feel has always been warm but it had never been this polished and modern for me. I don't envy OS X and Vista users anymore because of look and feel. I feel better of now because if I was using those other OSs, my eyes would have hurt after an hour of using my computer because of the lack of the dark themes that I am enjoying now, though I still feel that OS X is more aesthetically pleasing. I can also feel so much improvement when it comes to technical aspects. Maybe, the biggest leap for me was Edgy to Feisty. I really thought that the difference between those two versions is significant. Now, as new versions come out, it seems to me that Ubuntu is getting closer and closer to perfection. My issues with it aren't basic anymore. I'm not anymore trying to fix hardware issues or compiling applications all day. I'm already trying to optimize my Ubuntu desktop for my computer to better help me. haha. It sounds so profound.

I hope, you can also feel the improvements. Let's kill bug #1. :D :guitar:
