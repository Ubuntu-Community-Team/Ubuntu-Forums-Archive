### Post by k4sgt on 2008-11-14
About a year ago I was wondering if Linux had truly become easy enough to be "Joe know nothing's" desktop OS.  I decided to perform an experiment to test this.  My first victim was my mother.  She has little to no computer user experience.  I set her up with Gutsy and turned her loose.  I made sure that the computer was set up with everything from the libdvd to flash and so on.  She loved it.  When Hardy came out I upgraded her to the new LTS.  She has been using her computer without any major issues for almost a year now.  Since then, she has taken a job in which she has to use a windows XP computer.  After starting with Linux, she is easily aggrivated with the quirks of XP.  She hates the anti virus program always asking her to update.  She hates when her computer locks up for no reason.  I just remind her that at least her computer does not have annoyances like that.  
     
     After a few months of my mother running Linux with no major complaints, I decided to expand my experiment.  I have to date converted 10 windows users to full fledge 100% Linux users.  All of these people had no Linux exposure prior to me.  I get about one phone call per month between the ten of them.  It is usually an easy fix, or I just need to show them how to do something easy.  I might add that three of these people actually paid me to install and support Linux for them.  How does $115 a pop sound?  I have set aside a demo computer that I loan out to prospective converts.  If they like using Linux then I give them the option to load it on their own machine with a disk that I give them for free, or they can pay me the $115 and I make sure that everything works (printers, DVD playback, webcam, etc.).  They can also call me anytime with questions.  So far everything has been working out great.

     So, what I ask of you is how many people that are Linux users can you attribute to your efforts?  As stated earlier I have 10 in about one years time.


Barry Odum

### Post by waspbr on 2008-11-14
