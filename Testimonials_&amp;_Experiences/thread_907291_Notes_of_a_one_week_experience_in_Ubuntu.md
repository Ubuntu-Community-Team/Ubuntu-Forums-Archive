### Post by AlesUbu123 on 2008-09-01
After two years of using linux, I have realised that I have become so much accustomed and happy with it, that going back to Windows stopped being an option. Still, I can't get rid of a funny mixed feeling about Ubuntu (and probably linux in general) and I have found out that maintaining a functional system in Ubuntu takes more effort from me than it did in Windows. 
I have been trying to think out why my impressions were like this, and I have decided to make a little experiment by noting outstanding occurences or experiences (in a positive or negative way) during 1 week (22th August to 29th august 2008 ) regular use of Ubuntu. 

I have been using Ubuntu, with KDE 3 environment installed and all updates installed. I was not in any way specifically reporting events related to exclusively Ubuntu or Linux (kernel) or some other project, but just experiences in general. 

**[SIZE="2"]=> Negative experiences[/SIZE]**
**22. 08 11:27 **
Firefox hang up, while browsing.  Only borders of firefox browser window were seen and one smaller window appeared, again with only borders drawn around it. After about 60 seconds smaller window was finally completely drawn and it offered to Kill Firefox.  I think that viewing page [http://popcon.ubuntu.com/by_inst](http://popcon.ubuntu.com/by_inst) killed my Firefox. 
**22. 08 12:24**
aMule died without any warnings. The cause was insufficient space on device. 
**22. 08 12:50**
My removable external disk won't stop becoming read-only. :(
I have fixed this issue by doing a fsck on the drive. But sadly this did not work, giving me output that no changes have been written to the disk. So I had to format this drive (not losing any data though) 
**24.08 09:51**
I was working in R statistics package while it suddenly crashed with output:  *** caught segfault *** address 0xc021ecd4, cause 'memory not mapped'. Terrible! I hope my stuff was saved.
**24.08 13:13**
Adept_manager crashed on exiting the program. I don't know the reason.
**24.08 16:54**
Whenever my screen is locked and my laptop is plugged into main power, the brightness goes to 100%. This is probably due to some bug in kpowersave? Quite annoying.
**24.08 17:58**
Basket notpads program crashed. I don't see why.
**24.08 20:42**
I would like to listen to Amarok music with my bluetooth headphones, but I think the configuration process would take cca. 30 steps of configuration, and still I am not sure whether it would work. I don't have the energy to go through all the steps w/o being sure it would work. This should be simpler (and before PulseAudio it was!)
**25.08 15:57**
After I have finished surfing on the net I closed my laptop lid and plugged in the power cable. Linux completely froze (with Caps Lock light on) and I had to reboot. My laptop is configured to turn off the display when closing the lid, so this isn not a suspend or hibernate problem.
**26.08 11:20**
Adept_updater crashed again. :(


[SIZE="2"]**=> Positive experiences**[/SIZE]
**24.08, 09.34**
I needed the countdown timer for some purpose and I simply went to [www.linuxappfinder.com](www.linuxappfinder.com), searched for "timer" and by sudo apt-get install ktimer I got what I wanted. No download.com, no pop-ups no trialware - great, this is the way computing should be.
**24.08, 09.43**
I was able to listen to shoutcast streams easily in Amarok. The function is fully integrated and it works. 
**24.08, 14:07**
I have 15 programs opened and playing youtube videos and my system is still perfectly responsive. Very nice.
**24.08 16:57**
I am writing this in Basket. This is probably one of the ten programs that i am dependant on and couldn't live without in Windowns or other systems :)
**24.08 16:58**
I can run my medical dictionary in Linux using Wine. Great.
**24.08 20:41**
Amarok automatically added music from my portable drive to its collection. No questions, no problems, it worked seamlessly.
**24.08 21:22**
A guy at the winehq.org helped me to solve the thesaurus bug in MS Office 2003 running over wine. [http://bugs.winehq.org/show_bug.cgi?id=14041](http://bugs.winehq.org/show_bug.cgi?id=14041) 
**26.08 19:50**
I needed multiple files on my disk to be renamed. I used KRename, which was very easy to use, perfectly integrated into KDE (drag-and-drop files worked!) and it did what I wanted! Great!

Taking all this in consideration, I could say that in Ubuntu certain things weren't running perfectly smoothly for me and I got relatively more crashes in Ubuntu than I had in Windows, and this week is not in any way an exceptional - there is rarely a time that I don't have to fix and tinker with my computer in order to be able to do my work. I know this is not the way to help open-source in its deveopment, but I wanted to share my opinion here. (I'll post the necessary bug reports - I promise!!)
On the other hand several outstanding applications and especially the kernel project make me feel that linux has a bright future. For a regular home user Amarok, Digikam, Basket, OpenOffice, Gimp, Inkscape and others represent exceptional tools, playing an important role in preventing me from using other OSes.

Have a nice day!

Mine is stable too. 
Just wondering why you are using Ubuntu with KDE3 and not full Kubuntu?
Might be part of the display crashing problem.

If you prefer the KDE desktop then you are better to run Kubuntu and install a few Gnome applications than to run Ubuntu and use the KDE desktop.

Kubuntu is configure to run KDE whilst Ubuntu is configured for Gnome so there may be conflicting calls on application libraries when using KDE on Ubuntu and that could be one cause of the behaviour you are experiencing.
