### Post by albannach1 on 2010-02-24
I got a new computer from Dell with the following specs
AMD Athlon II X2 240 (2 cores at 2.8GHz)
2 GB RAM (256MB dedicated to onboard video)
ATI Radeon 3200HD onboard video
320GB 7200rpm SATA HD

After re-partitioning the drive (to get rid of those stupid recovery and dell utilities partitions)...

I have had to re-install Vista **5** times and it now finally works. It took forever to get drivers configured and updates installed (SP2 broke one of the installations as did the AMD Catalyst Control Center).

Ubuntu worked right the first time, had it installed updated, and ready-to-use within 2.5 hours. NO hiccups, no fooling with hardware drivers.

The best part though:

Vista boot-up time (after extensive tweaking to get this number down): **3 minutes**

Ubuntu boot-up time (out of the box): **16-20 seconds!**
