### Post by dat311 on 2008-05-22
Hi guys,

I'm the kind of guy who's happy being different.  I drive a 40 year old sportscar, which is inevitably awkward at times - new components don't always suit, parts are hard to source etc.  but there's a great community of car clubs and online forums to help me out.  A bit like being here.

Here's my experience with Ubuntu.  I had an old Pentium II 1.1ghz pc with (initially) 128mb of ram.  It was running windows 98, and some old versions of Office, and some assorted other apps for this and that.  A real clunker, but good enough for writing documents, a bit of scanning and so on. 
 
I'd been interested in mucking about with Linux for some time, got hold of a copy of "Ubuntu Linux for Non-Geeks", which had had some good reviews, and tried the live CD.  Which of course didn't work - not enough RAM.  So we scrounged around some other old pc's in the spare room, found some compatible old RAM and managed to double it (!!) to 256mb.  WOW!  Tried the Live CD again.  It worked!  But it was very very slow.  And Open Office was very very *very* slow.  A bit more research on which distro might be better and I decided on Xubuntu.  Downloaded an ISO (not as easy as you think - no advice on the Xubuntu site as to which one would suit me) and got it up and going.  Nice.  So we bit the bullet and installed...

Dual boot setup worked without a hitch.  My wife screwed up Windows by deleting some stuff to make some room (which wasn't subsequently needed :) ).  That's okay, because the Xubuntu side does everything we need, and faster.

However, we've run foul of a couple of things.  Well, no, one thing, actually, over and over again.  I love the feel of the operating system.  I love that I've got a plethora of software to choose from.   I love that I can get help so easily online.  

I *hate* the lack of documentation.  I hate that I had to really nut out which ISO was the right one.  I hate that clicking on the "help" button either directs me online to a forum or (often the case) does *nothing at all*.  I hate that very very frequently asked questions aren't addressed at a program level: Press a button to rip to Mp3 and you get a weird error message, instead of a message saying "this function not enabled, you need to install blah blah..." or at least grey out the function.  Or have it listed in a help file.  But at least it'll do it now.  And it recognises my DVD burner (which windows had trouble with) and it runs broadband easier.

Why my title?  When you deal with a 40 year old sportscar, you expect problems to have to be worked around.  You expect that nobody will have a listed fix and you'll need to find out which bit of some other vehicle will fit the problem and solve it.  You expect that some problems nobody has had before, so you might have to nut it out yourself.  I didn't expect it with a strongly followed *brand new* operating system.  The system (like my car, mostly) runs really well, but the lack of initial info., for me, is frustrating and annoying.  Xubuntu isn't a 40 year old sportscar.  

So, before you ask, I'll be joining the documentation team - because I believe that *that* is where Ubuntu needs the most work.

Cheers,
Steve
