### Post by luckylucky on 2010-06-08
I've been using ubuntu virtually exclusively for years... except for the occassional time needing to go back into Winblows for printing / scanning, and other rarer reasons.

A while ago my printer broke, so I just didn't use it for months.  Then I upgraded to Ubuntu 10.04, and then bought a new HP All-In-One Deskjet F4480 this past weekend.  I was mentally prepared to attempt another battle to get linux drivers working somehow to attempt to use it natively in Ubuntu, but also prepared to have to resort to windoze to make use of my new printer/scanner.

Unpacked it, set it on shelf, plugged in power, plugged in computer, then pushed the power button.

WoW!  When I turned it on it returned the favor!!!!

Ubuntu 10.04 automatically recognized it!  Too good I thought... hmmm... let's try to print something.  I printed a pdf that was on my desktop.  Awesome!!!

How about scanning?

SimpleScan, Xsane, gscan2pdf... they all worked BEAUTIFULLY!!!!!

I was sooo thrilled and overwhelmed by how awesome it worked, just by plug & play, and without ANY tinkering around by me, that I literally stood up and gave my computer a standing ovation!  I was elated with joy seeing this!

This was by biggest thing still holding me back to needing windows from time to time, so now I'm officially 99.995% ubuntu!
