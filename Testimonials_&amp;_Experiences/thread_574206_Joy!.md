### Post by Zipster90 on 2007-10-12
I've posted a few times here in the past few days, but I still kinda want to introduce myself and express my elation at getting Linux up and running successfully.

For the past few years, I've dabbled in Linux. Not a whole lot, but I knew what it was and how to do the basics (badly). I installed, and ultimately uninstalled, distro after distro after distro, including Ubuntu, looking for the right one for me. I finally knew that Ubuntu was number one in my book

These past couple of weeks I've found myself looking up more info on Ubuntu, and testing out Feisty on my old HP Pavilion 750n. I wanted to make sure all of the things I needed to work worked. 

First off, I needed Microsoft Office for a school competition. In UIL (a Texas academic organization) Computer Applications, it's either MS Office or nothing. Well, I downloaded VMWare, installed XP and Office '07 and was up in a jiffy. What an easy process, albeit tedious, it was to install VMware! Sure, I stumbled a few times at the command line; being the cutesy fisher-pricey XP and Vista UI addict I am, but I soon got used to it.

Next, my iPod. I can't live without my iPod. I'm NOTHING without it. So I had to find out if there was any possible way to make mine work with Linux. Once again, a piece of cake. I installed gtkpod and I was through in a snap.

After testing a few more things, and backing my school papers and such, I finally decided to make the leap, and switch my main computer, my Dell Inspiron E1505, to Ubuntu. I popped in the CD, and rebooted, closing Windows Vista for the last time.

Once again, it went flawlessly, and I was able to complete the install in my third period banking class. I need to set up my video card and wireless, but I'm not worried, since I've found tutorials for both on this forum.

I just wanted everyone to know how happy I've been so far, and I hope to contribute much more to the forums as soon as I learn more about Linux. And I want to tank everyone on here who has helped me so far!:)
