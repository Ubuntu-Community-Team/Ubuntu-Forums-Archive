### Post by wilem on 2006-09-16
And I must say that it has been the best experience I have ever had installing Linux and getting everything working. I have been using Linux off and on for 6 years. I purchased a laptop in January of this year for the purpose of getting back into Linux.

After some time I gave up and put Windows back on it. I used it for awhile and decided to try Linux again. I installed Dapper without any issues, got my resolution going and wireless going in about 10 minutes. Ran all of the updates, followed a howto guide to Compiz and got it going as well. Not only is it the best looking OS I have ever used, it is the most functional.

Some history about the different distros I have used in the past:

I started with Slackware then went to RedHat, Mandrake(now Mandriva), Suse and then FreeBSD. After FreeBSD I used Gentoo for awhile. I put my Linux fetish on hold for a bit then tried Ubuntu. Ubuntu is what will make Linux an OS that will move more into the mainstream I believe. 

On windows I have to install 8 different drivers and then reboot between each one AND then install over 60 updates which overall consumes about 5 hours. On top of this, I have to install the software I want. ubuntu had me up and going in no time.

I now recommend Ubuntu to my friends and family looking for something besides Windows for their needs.
