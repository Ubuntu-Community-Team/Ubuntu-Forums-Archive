### Post by daggerx on 2008-06-23
So, in an attempt to stay humble about this whole new experience I will say that, aside from some hardware not being supported here and there, I see no reason to stay with windows. I respect Microsoft for everything that they have done for the software world. Microsoft has really made me appreciate why I enjoy Ubuntu so much. I've been reading the forum documents, watching the screen casts, reading the documentation and even googling if I need it. One thing is certain: I know more about linux in the past week and a half than I ever did all my life. 

There is no competition when you are dealing with a "free" OS this powerful. Over 16,000 apps in repositories waiting for you, 30 known virus compared to 140000 in windows, there is really no reason to not use it unless 99.9 % of your hardware isn't supported; highly unlikely. Ubuntu is cooler, cleaner, _faster_, stable (er), and you get what you need if you type the right commands. My father in law has been using windows ever since he can remember and after 2 days of showing him around my ubuntu, im still trying to get his jaw of the floor. The only reason I'm dual booting is because of my wife - even though she has her own PC, and the church since they bought me a laptop as the admin and I know they have no clue of linux anything. Once I found the terminal  - it was over simply because most of the cool apps can be installed via the terminal very quickly. I like the fact that the user is a "limited" user until he or she has to do a major change on the machine - so if they walk away from the PC and an ignorant (no offense) person sits down and starts fiddling, they wont mess up the system. I'm having a lot of fun and my productivity and speed on doing things on the laptop has increased. If you are still considering this os, check out the [screencasts]("http://screencasts.ubuntu.com/") and give it a chance. heck, you've put up with all the nonsense windows threw at you for the last few years haven't you? You've complained and complained and yet you're still using windows? That's not logical at all!

From what I've noticed while I am still on my first sip of ubuntu, is the "I don't like ubuntu" statement is usually said because of familiarity (lack of) - There is a learning curve folks. Most people don't want to read the stuff. **I don't need to know what sudo means** I just need to know what it does  - and I found that information by reading. Some have left ubuntu due to lack of discipline (reading) and others have left for other reasons and _I hope they come back_. So this is called Linux for human beings - I like that - one thing that we can do that others groups may not be able to do is "read" - the more I read, the faster I customized my ubuntu. I realized that there is too much data to be ignorant about this Linux distro (ubuntu). I would encourage anyone to become a student, humble themselves and sit and the imaginary classroom as you read the docs via the [forums]("http://ubuntuforums.org") and [community]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community").

Hats off to all the contributors in the open source world. God bless you.

Bravo!!!
