### Post by Draw2much on 2008-08-16
Hello Ubuntu Forum People! Here is my story, sorry if it's long. I've always been a "chatter box". :)

I'm probably one of those few females who loves to tweak her computer, to the point where she'll open up the case and start messing around with things. I love to upgrade, to modify, to stream-line... I hate a "messy" feeling computer. (In case you're wondering, Windows feels messy the moment it's installed. *sighs* )

It's just the type of person I am. I'm also pretty cheap, which is why I never got into Macs. (But I will some day! OH YES! I will!)

So you can then imagine how attractive Linux has always been to me. I've been dabbling in it for years. The problem is that I'm a hardware person, not a program person. So when it comes to an OS I'm pretty dumb and need visual cues to get to the settings I want. This is where Linux always seemed to fail: to get to the "good stuff" always required the Terminal. And since I knew nothing about "DOS commands" I never even knew where to begin. It was frustrating.

But I always understood that Linux was good for a lot of basic computer type tasks without messing with settings. Which is why when my friend had me build her a new computer, I suggested salvaging her old Dell computer by giving it to her son for educational purposes. Her son is 5, just starting school. I'd never give a 5 yr old a computer with Windows on it. The amount of damage a kid that age could do to a Windows machine terrifies me. So I went searching and found Edubuntu.

I was really surprised at how easy it was to install and setup everything in Ubuntu. There was, of course, a learning curve (always is with a different OS) but it wasn't near as sharp as I was expecting. I didn't have to hunt for drivers to make things work, they worked "out of box". Getting programs was simple with Add/Remove. And GNOME was the most streamlined "actually makes sense" desktop configuration I'd ever encountered.

I was.. impressed. So I tried it on my Asus z70va (or Chembook 2370Va) laptop. It worked. The x700 driver, card reader, wireless, USB ports, [bluetooth]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=5380575&postcount=3").. it all worked. And anything that didn't work out of the box (like encrypted DVD play back) I could search extensive Ubuntu communities to find out the solution. In fact, the large community helped me realize something else: the terminal really isn't that scary once you know what to type into it. :rolleyes:

Utlimately I know I won't be rid of Windows. There are just some games that won't play in Wine. (Sims2, stupid EA!) But I can drastically reduce Windows usage. I'm looking forward to it.

And really, who knows? Someday I really might not log into Windows at all. With the strides Linux is taking every year, it doesn't seem too far fetched...

15.4" WSXGA (1680x1050) Matte Wide Screen 
1.86GHz Intel Pentium M 750 Sonoma/2 MB L2 Cache/533 FSB
100 GB Hard drive (5400RPM)
2 GB RAM
Ati Radeon x700 Dedicated 128DDR2
DVD player/CD-RW
Internal 56 modem,Internal IntelPRO 2915ABG Wireless Ethernet/ Lan (802.11a+b+g),10/100/1000 Base T Ethernet LAN port
Bluetooth Enabled, Integrated Microphone
Internal 4-in-1 card read (SD/MMC/MS/MS Pro)
S-video out, 4 USB (2.0), 1 PCMCIA, IR, IEEE Firewire

I also like having "virtual monitors" to flip between. This is really one of those things I'm beginning to wonder how I lived without. It's just really convenient for separating tasks.
