### Post by craig10x on 2009-12-04
I'm sure you will get lots of feedback and suggestions here...but if i may make a suggestion that may work out for you....and you might want to try...

if you would be able to manage to live without windows on your computer ( i do and love being "windowless") then download 9.04 (Jaunty) instead of the current 9.10...9.10 still has some problems for some....9.04 is pretty rock stable (in my experience) which is why i am sticking with it...

I installed and had Ubuntu do a full hard drive install, no partition...thus taking Windows out altogether....and everything works great...my laptop is newer then your computer (bought this year) but yours doesn't sound that old and should work pretty well...

Of course, before you install it like this....make windows recovery discs for your computer if you didn't get them when you bought it, then usually there is a program on the computer that lets you do that....(i did that with my toshiba laptop)..just to play it "safe".....
And download from the Ubuntu website and burn an "iso image" disc to a cd and install from that....that works best....
