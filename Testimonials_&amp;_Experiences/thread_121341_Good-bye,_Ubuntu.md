### Post by aragorn2909 on 2006-01-24
It is not without regret that I say goodbye today to Ubuntu, and hello to another distribution that more suits my needs.  Ten months ago, if someone had said to me that I would have ditched Windows for Linux, I would have told them they were crazy.  I have been using Linux as my primary OS for the last nine months.  That was when I discovered Hoary.  What a gem!  This was *exactly* what I needed to make the jump.  At first it was a little here, a little there.  Fight with my Wife because she doesn't like the look of it, doesn't know how to use it, whatever.  Soon, it was just there, always there, no Windows (I maintain my XP partition for certain things), and I couldn't have been happier.  It always was, and still remains today (and I'm hoping for a long time from now) to be a learning experience.  And what an experience!  
     So what happened?  Why am I leaving Ubuntu?  One week ago today, certain things just happened.  My sound card stopped working, my floppy drive wasn't being recognized, printing stopped.  All at the same time.  No problem in Windows for any of these things.  I began to think, what are the reasons that I use Linux, when I could use no brainer Windows for everything?  Nine months ago I would have told you that it was to escape virus' and to learn something new.  Today, I'll tell you one more reason.  Stability.  And if Ubuntu can't provide me with the stability I want/need, where do I go?  Whats more stable than Linux?  I'm not even going to attemp BSD, let me try and figure out Linux first!  So where does that leave me?  Slackware.  But Slack didn't work out for me, so how about slack based?  Vector?  Feels too busy to me.  Zenwalk.  Freakin perfect!  After a week of configuring, compiling, reinstalling and *learning*, I'm ready to let go, with almost no regrets.
     Which leads me to this.  The one thing that I regret is leaving this Community and these forums.  The best in the world, in my humble opinion.  Helpful to a fault, and always willing to answer a question.  I will never forget the lessons I've learned here.  Thank you, Ubuntu, for everything.

A
