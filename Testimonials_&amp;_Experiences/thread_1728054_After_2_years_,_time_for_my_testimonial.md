### Post by Ghost_Mazal on 2011-04-13
Lo All ,

After 2 years with Ubuntu it's time for my testimonial.

I live in South-Africa and I am a IT profesional by trade.
I loved computers ever since I was 13 and have always had it in my life.
I was always a Windows user. But I always had a hunger to learn new things.

In 2002 I decided to try out Linux. Red Hat 9 it was. I immediately liked
the server side of things in Linux. The stability especially pulled me in.
I switched over all my servers to Linux. I always used the Fedora distro's
after that for my servers and never even concidered Windows for my servers
again.

As much as I loved Linux , I was one of those people who had the misconception
that Linux is a good server but a bad desktop. "It won't be able to do this and it
won't be able to do that" Was always the thoughts. And also thought that it is not
user friendly enough and doesn't have enough software support to be a proper desktop OS.

Being South-African I have always had my eye on Ubuntu. Then two years back I stumbled
upon Ubuntu Ultimate Edition site. I read through all the features and apps and thought
"hang on" looks like everything I need is there.
I installed it on a secondary pc and started playing with. I quickly saw , that Ubuntu
can be a serious contender to be my main desktop OS. Then last year with the release of 10.04
I decided to go away from Ultimate Edition and install and build up Ubuntu 10.04 and take it from
there.

I dual booted as so many do and piece by piece added the software I need to do my tasks on my
main pc (This is the best way to learn Ubuntu as you can take your time with it)
I would boot my Windows , do my important things , and then boot into Ubuntu to "play" further.
As time went on I found myself needing to boot into Windows less and less as I could do more
and more of my tasks with Ubuntu.

Today , I don't need Windows at all anymore. Ubuntu have become my fully primary OS.
Not only can it do everything I did with Windows , but Ubuntu does it better and easier if
used the right way.

At work I am still forced to use Windows , and it irritates me the whole day.
I am a fully converted user and I love my Ubuntu. Like I read somewhere "Windows is like a shiny
Nintendo game , Linux is a Mecano set" Your mind is the limit.

I am still learning , have much to learn , but the great people on this site have gotten me so far
and I will iron out the few problems I have left with all of your support.

Ubuntu is a great OS. This cummunity is the best I have ever used with the greatest people.
And that is the thing of Ubuntu , it's not just an OS , it's a way of life and you become
part of a great "family".

It wasn't easy. I spent countless hours of sifting through manuals , how-to's and forum posts. I got frustrated many many times. But it was worth it.

Well done on a great job by everyone involved with Ubuntu. Keep up the great work.

To everyone who ever helped me here , thank you very much. You helped to convert me and I am
sure I will need your help a lot more in the future with questions and problems.

- Maz
