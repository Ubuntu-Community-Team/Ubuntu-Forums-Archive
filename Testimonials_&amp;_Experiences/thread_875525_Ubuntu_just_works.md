### Post by kazzmir on 2008-07-30
I recently installed Ubuntu 8.04 on my laptop and it has been working wonderfully. Initially I had Gentoo on it but after playing around with power management, my system became quite unstable and averaged an uptime of about 4 hours. I use my laptop for work so this was obviously unacceptable. I tried out some other OS's such as Fedora 9, but kde 4.0 is terrible so I tried Ubuntu next and so far everything has been great.

Actually when I got the laptop I installed Ubuntu on it but skype wouldn't install( the laptop is an amd64 ) so I quickly gave up on ubuntu and installed Gentoo which a) I like better, really and b) gentoo would install skype without any problems. Well, after Gentoo ceased to work I decided I would figure out how to get skype working on Ubuntu and with help from the forums I managed to do just that.

Now I have a completely stable laptop where *everything* just works( wireless*, sound, power management, even my ati card ), and I think that is quite an achievement for the Ubuntu team. Maybe that sounds like small potatoes to some people, but trying to build your own linux system( which gentoo basically is ) can be extremely difficult.

I am eternally grateful to the Ubuntu team. I suppose I should buy something from them, huh?

*Ok, thats not entirely true.. I had to compile my own wireless drivers but I knew that would be an issue anyway.
