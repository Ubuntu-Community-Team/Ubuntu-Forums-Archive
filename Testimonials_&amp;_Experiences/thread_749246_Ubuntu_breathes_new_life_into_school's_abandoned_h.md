### Post by newbie2 on 2008-04-08
> In explaining why the school went for Kubuntu, Stefyn said the students responded well to CDs put out by the Ubuntu project. Many had tried Ubuntu at home, which led to a decision to provide a familiar working environment at the school as well.

"During our last hardware cleanout, we challenged the students to create the best Linux install and customization, and the winners would get to keep the hardware once it was decommissioned.

"As our school is one of the poorest in the state, and many students have no access to computers outside of the classroom, this happened to be a popular challenge. An added benefit is the hardware being put to a new use instead of being dumped in landfill" Stefyn said.

### Post by linuxlizard on 2008-04-14
> in today's world many older computers are not going to do for today's software. 

That's bull.

Sorry but I've got a p3 700 mhz laptop with 192 mb ram that I use on a daily basis.

It can't run ubuntu with any speed. 

BUT- it runs pcfluxboxos very fast. Boots in less than 30 secs. And it runs all the same software versions as my ubuntu box. Same version of firefox, same version of open office, same version of gimp, same version of wesnoth, same version of everything. Only thing it can't do is play most 3d games because the 3d card is only 4 mb. In spite of that, it runs loads of 2d games.

In fact, it opens apps as fast as, sometimes faster than ubuntu on my amd 2800+ with 256mb 3d card and 756 mb ram machine. The repositories are updated regularly, and it runs everything fast and well.
