### Post by TechHut on 2006-06-20
I loved it. I started out on October of 2005. Here is how it started:

I chucked money out for Windows Pro x64, but one day in October, it crashed! So I resorted on my other computer, and found theopencd.org, I found a link to Canonical, and from there, found Ubuntu. That is when I switched completely to Linux. Now lets move back. To 2001. I was on TechTV forums, they were great (until G4 screwed them up). I loved the place, the people were nice, and I had fun, I found a Linux forum, and I thought "OH yay!" and went there. I found Mandrake (now Mandriva) Linux and fired it up in my newly acquired test machine. Mandrake's install was just beautiful, and I liked it. It ran KDE, and the look just felt... right. The UI was easy to use, and I liked Linux. It was a tad slow though, and using a few CD's as coasters was interesting. I then ordered stuff off some linux cd site, which I believe I got Red Hat Linux 9, and tried that. Gnome it was, and sure did it look nice. I started to like Linux. Then I tried out FreeBSD, after looking on LinuxISO's site, they had a FreeBSD section. I tried it out, but it wasn't for me. It compiled things a bit slow, and just felt wierd. I went back into Linux. Now I didn';t like it much anymore, and went back into Windows. Fast Forward to 2004. I joined a forum about computers, that place was great! I learned about Firefox there, and  what opensource software was. OpenOffice saved me many dollars (which are now in my college fund). That place was great. Now lets go to 2005. I started getting slowly back into linux when my computers fritzzed up. The live CD saved me many-a-time. D*mn Small Linux was also pretty cool, because it was an entire distrubution all in 50MB. Then go back to my story above.

After I started using Ubuntu:

I loved Ubuntu, I kept my Windows partition to get some data from it (Family pictures) and I just kept it. Windows still wouldnt boot. I still used Linux. But yes, I love Ubuntu. It's installer gives me a "DIY" feel, while the usuable part just makes you get the feeling of "I did it". I converted over, and am never looking back.

Good job Ubuntu Devs, as this has been an excellent distrobution for me and my family to use.
