### Post by AntiEmoSquad on 2005-11-15
Hey, I'm new and I figured why shouldn't my first post praise this absolutely terrific distro of Linux?

The short story: Ubuntu is a godsend when compared to installing Windows

The long story: Well, I had just gotten my new laptop (That I still love. That's right. I'm a Linux freak for general computing. Windows freak for gaming.) and decided that I should probably try reinstalling Windows on my old HP so my mom had something to use. Now, I mainly decided to do this due to the *massive* amount of spyware that was on the machine. So, I formatted the drive and popped in my Windows XP CD.

Well. My CD was corrupt somehow. What the hell.

Now, I couldn't exactly call up Microsoft and go "HEY BILL, CAN YOU SEND ME A NEW WINDOWS DISC? YEAH, CAUSE MY COPY THAT I'M USING FOR REASONS THAT COMPLETELY DENY THE LICENSE IT'S UNDER IS CORRUPT. NO? YOU'RE CALLING THE FBI FOR WHAT THEY SOMEHOW CONSIDER PIRACY? Whoops." The CD was from RIT, and it was only supposed to be installed on things that related to the school (such as a personal computer for schoolwork, software/hardware project, etc.) but I figured "it's a home PC, I know what I'm doing." But no. My luck was that the disc was somehow corrupt, even though it worked just 3 months previous.

Ah well. I decided to give it a random shot and try out some form of Linux. So Linux-Distro-hunting season it is. I ran across Ubuntu and saw that they sent out CDs for free. Now, if that didn't make my eyes light up and make me let out a giddy little squeal, then nothing will ever make me do that.

And I didn't. But you get the idea. I have a dialup connection (I know. It's teh suck.) and the idea of downloading an entire ISO wasn't very appealing to me. So I ordered a few Ubuntu CDs, considering I know others who might want to give Linux a try.

Well, I waited a really long time (ie. 3 months) for the CDs. Then I saw the sticker that said it came out of the Netherlands. So I kind of understood what took so long.

I popped the live CD into my laptop to give the OS a shot, see what it could do. I was nothing less than impressed. It worked with everything, perfectly. My trackpad worked, my video card was fully supported, my PCMCIA slot read just fine, all my hardware that I normally carry around with me worked. I was the happiest 15-year-old geek in the world at the moment.

So, I threw the live-CD into the old HP and it seemed to work good with just about everything. My hard drives showed up, the nVidia card worked fine, networking was good, and hell, the motherboard was more supported than it was on Windows. So I could take out that second network card that I needed to use with Windows. I decided to wipe the crap that was Windows 98 (yeah. I was stuck with 98, and no modem support working, for about a month. Ew.) and install Ubuntu. It all went smooth. After installation, I was pleasantly surprised at the full installation of Ubuntu.

Now, it's working great. I have everything working on it except for a sound card and a modem. I'd have a modem already since I have two of them, but one of them is a Winmodem and the other one isn't detected when I try to install the drivers for it. *shrug* I presume I'll fix that soon. As for a sound card. Well, I just need to get a Linux-compatible soundcard. Simple as that.

I never thought I would point to the case of an old Hewlett-Packard Pavilion 520c and say "I love that computer" but thanks to Ubuntu, I can.

Now to just get that ugly "Designed for Windows XP" sticker off of there.

- Case Boyle
  Pleased Ubuntu n00b.
