### Post by Crakie on 2007-12-27
I thought it might be a nice idea to have a topic that sums up the reasons why people stick with Ubuntu rather than another OS. To put things into perspective for the folks that consider giving Ubuntu a try, let's add some info on what your level of expertise is.

I'll kick off. 

I have been running Ubuntu for a good year and a half, after running XP for years. I am not profesionally involved with computers, but I have been using them since the MS-DOS era. I like tinkering with them. Currently I run Kubuntu Feisty (main OS), Arch Linux (for horsing around) and XP (games).

The reasons I like Ubuntu more than XP are:

- Stability. People generally refer to Linux as being very stable. While this is slightly inaccurate because it refers to the kernel and the overall stability still depends on the programs you run, I still consider Ubuntu to be very stable. I do manage to crash an application on occasion, but simply killing the process from the terminal takes care of that quick and easy. In XP it could take ages before the task manager poppep up. To be fair, though, XP is pretty stable too. I never saw a single blue screen of death.

- Responsiveness. A clean XP installation runs quite smoothly on my current computer, but annoying little hickups interfere with that. For example: unexplained harddisk activity. In XP I regularly had the harddisk rattle for no apparent reason, slowing down my system. When I went into process management to see what program was causing it, it would be a process such as 'svhost.exe', giving no further insight into what was going on.

- Software is easily installed. The install wizards on XP are not complicated, but you generally need to buy the software that they install. How great is it to find out you need something, do a quick search and install it with a few clicks or a simple command from the terminal? Simple, free, fast and legal.

I'm sure I could come up with lots more, but now it's your turn ;)
