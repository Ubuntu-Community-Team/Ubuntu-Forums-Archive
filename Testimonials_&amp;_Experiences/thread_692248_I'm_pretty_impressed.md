### Post by agtownz on 2008-02-09
Well, somehow I managed to screw something up and Update Manager wouldn't start.  I though nothing of it, and just went to Synaptic to reinstall the packages, but for some reason I got an error.  After that, almost anything I opened only had the window outlines but nothing inside of it, and I was like umm, ok.  From my experiences of Windows XP, stuff was like this usually a very bad sign and would mean complete re-installation.  I decided log out and see what would happen, and something popped up and told me my X was messed up (great).  So I just shutdown my computer and turned it back on.  As soon as Ubuntu loaded, it started doing the system scan because it found errors.  Then after a short time, I logged on and turned on Synaptic again.  It told me some error occurred and gave me instructions on what to type in the terminal (something like dpkg -configure --a), which I did.  After doing that, I turned on Synaptic and it worked, along with Update Manager!  I mean, how cool is that, an OS that actually knows how to take care of itself!!!  Guys, seriously, major props to those who work on this project, words just can't describe how impressed and happy I am.
