### Post by Zsev on 2008-07-16
I've been an avid follower of ubuntu since 2005 and have faithfully tested each yearly release of ubuntu up until today. Unfortunately, ubuntu still isn't quite there yet. 

2006
Tried ubuntu for the first time, had troubles adjusting so i went back to XP. The installer also bugged out and wiped out one of my partitions which contained all my office documents. 

2007
Tried ubuntu on my desktop rig, with at that time had an ATI radeon x1950 graphics card. ATI graphics driver refuses install properly no matter how many different methods I tried. Couldn't live with the broken resolution so I removed ubuntu. 

2008
One evening, the sluggishness of vista finally got to me. I simply cannot understand why a Vista laptop with a 2.4ghz core2duo and 3gigs system RAM runs slower than my 4 year old computer. On impulse, I whipped out my kubuntu 8.04 disc and installed it on the spot. 

Mistake 1
I downloaded the kubuntu with kde4. At present state, KDE4 is a way too undeveloped. The start menu itself is horrendously incomplete. The desktop freezes up randomly way too much for my liking. 

Mistake 2 
I forgotten all about the load_cycle_count bug with notebook harddisks and linux. While it isnt entirely ubuntu's fault that its killing hard disks, I simply cannot accept the fact that running ubuntu is equivalent to placing a short fuse bomb on my stored data. When running ubuntu, my disk would click once every 4-10 seconds, increasing the load_cycle_count at a extremely rapid rate. 

Mistake 3
After the terrible experience with KDE4, i reverted back to kde3.5 which was a much much better desktop environment IMHO. All hell broke lose when I decided to tinker with display settings. The default installation detected my monitor resolution perfectly, but the moment I enter display properties and restarted X(without any changes), the desktop will somehow downscale to 640x480 and everything becomes massively big. At this point the OS becomes unusable because I could hardly remedy anything since one icon covers one quarter of the screen. Sigh, I need better luck with linux. 


Goodbye for now, perhaps I should wait for 8.10 to try ubuntu again.

### Post by Geekkit on 2008-07-17
Since we're doing the memory lane thing ...

I've been an avid follower of Linux since 1995 when you had to download Linux from Gopher from the college mainframe. I've played with many different Linux distros since then. I've also been a Mac user since 1991 (bought me an LC II), and I've used many Mac models since then. I've also been a dispassionate and disgruntled Microsoft user since 1993. Actually, my first version of DOS was PC-DOS by IBM. I actually bought it even when friends offered me disks of MS-DOS because I was disgusted with what Microsoft called a so called 'ready' OS.

Yesterday I had to come to my wife's rescue because her brand spanking new laptop running Vista started opening new windows of Internet Exploder on its own. By the time I stopped it (via task manager), it had consumed over 650 MB of memory with about 50 or so windows. No explanation, no idea why. Today, I tried to help her copy over some backup files from a DVD. After 15 minutes, Vista reported a copy speed of 20 KB per second. I stopped the copy, copied them to Ubuntu and was finished in just under five minutes.

Ubuntu has its share of bugs and quirks. But so does Windows, so does Mac. I've heard horror stories from guys that use to work on mainframes back in the 1970s. They all crash, loose data, and on occasion eat small pieces of our sanity.

You're looking for the Holy Grail Zsev ... it doesn't exist. And if you want my advice, yes, I think you should wait and then give 8.10 a go. Do it when Vista pisses you off. It shouldn't take too long ... at least it doesn't for me.
