### Post by MeisterLampe1 on 2008-07-04
I did a lot of research into this problem and it seems that pulse somehow triggers a bug in flash 9. Adobe is already aware of that. I found a lot of different solutions none of which worked. Here's my solution:

1. Uninstall libflashsupport:

sudo apt-get remove libflashsupport

2. Uninstall flash:

sudo apt-get remove flashplugin-nonfree

3. Install the Flash Beta 10 from 

32Bit:

[http://ppa.launchpad.net/punischdude/ubuntu/pool/main/f/flashplugin-nonfree/flashplugin-nonfree_10.0.b218-0ubuntu3_i386.deb]("http://ppa.launchpad.net/punischdude/ubuntu/pool/main/f/flashplugin-nonfree/flashplugin-nonfree_10.0.b218-0ubuntu3_i386.deb")

64Bit:

[http://ppa.launchpad.net/punischdude/ubuntu/pool/main/f/flashplugin-nonfree/flashplugin-nonfree_10.0.b218-0ubuntu3_amd64.deb]("http://ppa.launchpad.net/punischdude/ubuntu/pool/main/f/flashplugin-nonfree/flashplugin-nonfree_10.0.b218-0ubuntu3_amd64.deb")

This helped for me...
