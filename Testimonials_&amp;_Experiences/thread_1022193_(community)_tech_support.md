However, since the switch, I'm finding that I might have been a little too optimistic. Since the end of September, I've posted a half dozen posts regarding some rather basic technical difficulties with the switch to Ubuntu, and have received almost no help (though a lot of post views).

[post 1]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=928119")

[post 6]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1021799")

Frankly, I think six tech issues over 5 months is actually rather meager for a new user on a new OS. Additionally, the questions I had were pretty basic stuff (webcam and sound, DVD playback, IM software). On top of this, before posting, I've read the docs and searched the forums (and other sites) for similar problems and attempted other previously suggested fixes before posting. All in all, I don't think I'm being a burden, nor have I been too lazy to try to figure out these problems on my own.

Some of the problems in those posts I was *eventually* able to fix (on my own). My mic/webcam is working, I got sound for zsnes, and my music playback seems to be going smooth. However, I'm still having problems with pidgin, the internet radio still isn't working for some stations (even when their servers are actually online), and I can't get DVD playback. The last is probably the most annoying because I have to reboot and load Vista (which is on another partition) to watch DVDs on WMP (and I really dislike WMP). I had recently considered getting rid of that partition all together (I could use the extra 80GB of HD), but apparently I can't do that because I still need Vista for DVDs.

Anyway, the point of this post is to ask the community what more I can reasonably do to get Ubuntu running smoothly (or at least smoother) for me. When I registered here, it was the folks at Absolute Beginner Talk that convinced me to make the switch. For the most part, I like Ubuntu much more than Windows. I don't do so much stuff with a computer that I would need more than one OS (mostly music, movies, internet, IM, and office) and I'd like to have only one for everything. With that in mind, I find myself actually tempted to go back to Vista. Sure, it's sluggish, there's loads of spyware and security problems, it's ugly, and has a few other problems, but at least I can get everything to work.

I'm not raising the white flag just yet, though. Before I do, I want to know a few things. Is there something wrong with how I post? Is there any other resource out there that can help me get Ubuntu working properly? Is there another distro with better support? Or, is this just as good as it gets?

Thanks.

### Post by w4ett on 2008-12-26
One of the best howtos [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntucat/getting-the-best-help-on-linux-forums/](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntucat/getting-the-best-help-on-linux-forums/).

The key points:

 >    * Give a brief overview of the problem you’re experiencing.
    * Recount a couple of steps you’ve tried in order to research/fix the problem.
    * Post any error messages you get.
    * Ask for help. 



And always remember:  The "helpers" here are ALL volunteers.  We may or may not have a solution for your particular problem, but we ARE here to help.  After 24 hours or so a "bump' is perfectly acceptable for you to use as well, because sometimes posts DO slip through the cracks--sometimes for no other reason except timezone differences of the global Ubuntu Forum Posters.
