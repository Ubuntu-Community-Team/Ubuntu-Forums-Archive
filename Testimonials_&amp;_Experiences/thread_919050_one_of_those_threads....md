### Post by jetblack on 2008-09-13
You know the type - "Hey, look at me, I'm using ubuntu!"

Well hey, look at me.

Just wanted to say hi. I think I've said hi before, but I've now finally decided to give ubuntu a fair shake after a number of years of dithering. I haven't been using it for very long, but I have to say that so far I'm very impressed with the desktop experience.

I come from a background of debian and gentoo, and while I really like both of those distributions, suffice it to say that I've done my fair share of kernel recompiling and config file tweaking to get things to work. To be fair, this is largely because I got started around 2001, but still, I'm very impressed with how far linux has come in the intervening near-decade.

Within about one day's worth of actual time spent on the system, I have a functioning desktop that recognized my video card (granted, an old matrox g400, but I love the little bugger so back off), picked up the widescreen resolution on my tv, recognizes every usb device I've thrown at it so far, plays all my videos and lets me do basically everything I've tried without having to do a lot of work first. I've had to do next to nothing aside from installing the system and installing the extra apps I want. Bravo, ubuntu!

Anyhow, since the distro allows me to do the basic things with such ease, I'm getting the itch to start digging into the non-basic things, so you'll likely see me poking around in the future. I just wanted to say hello and congratulate the ubuntu devs and community for putting together what is, at first glance, a superior desktop linux experience. Thanks!
