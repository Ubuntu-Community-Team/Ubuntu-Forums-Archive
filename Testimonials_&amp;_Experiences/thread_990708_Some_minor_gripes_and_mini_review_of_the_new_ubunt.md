### Post by Castlef on 2008-11-23
Just thought I would say first of all I am impressed with how much has improved. It used to be my wireless card would not automatically work.. Now from a fresh install of ubuntu all I need to do is double click the wireless indicator and type in my key.. that is it. Also my mouse seems to be smoother when scrolling around... and my sound issues are solved. I can record.. playback and even change the volume in applications separately. But there are still several annoyances I have that I hope get fixed one day.

Ubuntu does not automatically mount my windows partition. I know this may be greedy? to want this.. it actually mounted my partitions in one distro but I forget which.. but it does not automatically mount my windows vista partition.

When watching a dvd.. there are graphical errors... there is a tracking line in the middle line like I am watching a video cassette tape or something on an old VCR.

I find the compiz effects nice but buggy for me... for instance if they are going on while i try to watch a dvd... the screen flashes... so I have them turned off. I have used vista over a year and never encountered such issues with it's 3d effects like I have with ubuntu after just using it a couple of days.

Installing from usb key did not work.. I followed the guides I read to the letter and it did not work. The error I had is also reported by several others when I did the google search.

This one bothers me the most... I have tried this on SEVERAL computers... using several different versions of ubuntu... on several different hardware configurations using different brands of graphics cards... open sourced and closed source drivers.. etc. When I simply hold a window at the bar at the top to move and drag it.. and start moving it around it's redraw effects are glitched... and distort all over and it spikes the CPU on the computer and basically grinds whatever is going on to a halt. I cannot believe more people don't report this... because it is not my imagination. It seems to me of sloppy coding somewhere... and it has been this way for several years now. The  computer I am on currently has an ATI 4850 graphics card with an athlon 6400 and 4 gigs of ram.. not quite top of the line but pretty dam close and it still does it.

And lastly this one is not a fault of ubuntu at all but gaming on Ubuntu/linux sucks.
