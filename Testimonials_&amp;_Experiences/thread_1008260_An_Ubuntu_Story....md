### Post by docdraino on 2008-12-11
so I've been messing around with linux for a few years now, never really understanding it, always testing out the latest live cds and basically feeling unhappy with them, and lately, using my laptop as a linux experiment machine.

that was until, of course, my ubuntu installer wiped out my partitions on my main computer's hard drive. the same partitions that contained a back up version of XP (yes, trust me, i tried burning the back up to a dvd, it didn't work out so well. corrupted cds and what not). Needless to say, my legal version of XP is pretty much gone forever and ubuntu, while attempting to make a new partition for itself, slain it into oblivion.

i was pissed. i was infuriated. i tried ubuntu out on my laptop, but it's wifi support was minimum at best and i rarely used it at all. i wasn't all too excited to embrace the world of open source software, especially when I use various audio programs on windows for recording my band.

after a day or two of using ubuntu, it's safe to say i'm never using windows or paying for/using their bloated products ever again.

the first thing i did was check to see if i could upload my tape recordings, the only thing that's truly important to me. I quickly found out that ubuntu did not support my soundcard's front mic, but supported the rear end mic. after some tinkering, i got everything to work just fine. this is a good sign.

also, the packages system is simply amazing and easy to use. this isn't the linux i remember, seeing how nothing ever worked and finding the things i needed was even more a pain. i love the large amount of open sourced, free software available with just a few clicks. this is real good.

however the only problems i stumble on are when some packages cannot be easily removed. I.E. when i wanted to remove OpenOffice Draw, it told me i must use Synaptic Package Manager. so I fumble around in there and somehow i was given the option to remove "ubuntu" and "ubuntu desktop". HOW? thank god i was paying attention. :p

anyway, this is by far one of the best distros i've used in a long time and as long as i don't go out of my way to break the OS, i think it'll work out for me for a long time.

### Post by a0u on 2008-12-12
Glad to see you liking Ubuntu. :)

> **docdraino said:**
> so I fumble around in there and somehow i was given the option to remove "ubuntu" and "ubuntu desktop". HOW? thank god i was paying attention. :p


Just some technical information here: "ubuntu-desktop", as meta-package, is merely a collection of all packages that constitute the default installation.  If you are removing something that is installed by default in Ubuntu (i.e., OOo Draw), then "ubuntu-desktop" must also be removed, in order to resolve dependencies.  However, removing "ubuntu-desktop" does nothing to disturb anything else on your system.
