### Post by agtownz on 2008-03-15
The story began when I read about a boot optimization which involved changing the concurrency option to shell to allow threaded booting (hopefully faster).  I tried that out, and when my computer started up again, I got a HAL error, and internet wasn't working.  So I got on my XP installation and read how this tweak had serious problems with Gutsy, and proceeded to try out about 6 solutions that had apparently worked for the other people.  Sadly, I had no luck.  I changed the settings back to pre-optimization and fixing attempts state, but the system still refused to load HAL correctly.  As a bonus, I was unable to use any administrative programs (such as Synaptic or Services manager).  Finally, I decided that it was probably time to reinstall.  I turned on the PC again with the CD in my hand and the USB in case I could find something that I would want to scavenge.  However, something was missing.  The HAL error didn't appear.  Now, I've had experience with Ubuntu fixing itself before, but the previous problem wasn't as serious.  I checked internet - it worked.  I opened up Synaptic and Services and other things in the System tab - they all worked.  All I gotta say is, great job guys, I failed to fix the problem, but several power-ons later, it got fixed by Ubuntu.  Ubuntu seems indestructible to me.
