### Post by jso2897 on 2009-11-27
I've had a mixed, but ultimately staisfactory experience moving to Karmic. on my better couple of PCs I installed it on, the results were stellar, after I got past an annoying library omission for JRE that got fixed in the final release.
On a couple of old PIII Dells i keep around (because they refuse to die), I hit a snag. Even though the Live Disk ran fine, when I installed and went to reboot, it couldn't recognize the kernel, and aborted the boot.
One of them I just reverted to XP (I have  need for a Windows box in my study anyway - Kodak camera, Photoshop, office), but I kept fooling around with one of them, until I hit on a solution: I installed Hardy and then ran through the upgrades one by one, until I had upgraded to Karmic.
Amazingly, this sorry little machine (500mhz P3, 448mb ram) boots now, and runs great. After a day of poking around, I can find nothing broken, and it actually runs smooth and (sorta)fast, even with Compiz effects enabled.

This is an amazing new release - it's a pretty bold move, and a lot of people are going to have problems, and they will b**ch and moan - and that's a good thing. Conflict and negativity, in reasonable doses, are good for a community.
We all know about that family at the end of the block, who all smile all the time, and never fight, or quarrel. You know, the one where little Bobby turned out to be a serial killer.
We don't want to be them.
Viva la Controversy!:D
