### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-12-05
> **cb951303 said:**
> Well if it works in gutsy, it will work on newer systems. there may be regressions and you may not get them work out of the box but with a little tweaking it's almost always possible.

I thought I was buying compatible hardware to negate the need to tweak everything to get it working; and I thought I was using Ubuntu to negate the need to recompile the kernel. If I wanted broken wireless, I would have bought something from Broadcom. Now I've either got flaky slow short-range wireless with WPA support, or reliable fast long-range wireless with insecure WEP encryption. That's *with* tweaking.

> I always buy my computers linux compatible so far they all did work like a charm and I built 4 computers since I started using linux. So I don't think it's correct to say that there is almost no compatible hardware...

I said there was almost no *certified* hardware - that is, hardware that is tested with every new version of Ubuntu and is guaranteed by Canonical and the manufacturer to work. That's what you need, otherwise your hardware could stop working in the time it takes you to dist-upgrade.

I have the Proposed repository enabled in the hope that a fix will come down the line. It's disturbing to note that small, less significant problems such as "xyz package doesn't have internationalisation support enabled" get fixed and uploaded, but bug reports of critical regressions don't even get looked at. It took a while for my bug report about the wireless to be triaged, and I have not even been asked for any further information. Other users have confirmed the bug too.

If I was a PC builder, I'd be flat-out scared to ship an Ubuntu system unless all the hardware I used was certified; too much risk of your customers updating their Ubuntu system and finding bits of their computer don't work anymore. Imagine the complaints and warranty claims! Of course, this isn't the reason why PC builders don't ship Ubuntu, but it would definitely be the best reason not to.
