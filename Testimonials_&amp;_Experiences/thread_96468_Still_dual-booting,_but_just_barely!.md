### Post by akniss on 2005-11-28
My first computer was a Magnavox with GeoWorks Ensemble GUI running on DOS.  It worked well for typing papers for high school and playing Tetris.  When I went to University, I 'upgraded' to a 486 running Windows 95 with 8MB of memory and a 1 GB hard drive.  My computer savvy friend told me that I'd NEVER need more than 1 GB of storage.  I've been hooked on MS WIndows and Office ever since.  About a year ago my wife's laptop running XP refused to boot.  We tried a system restore, which in hindsight wasn't an entirely good idea.  We ended up losing ALL of her files, including a Master's thesis proposal, and her fourth grade student's grades for the year.  (She never heeded my warnings about backing up her data...)  We used a Knoppix LiveCD to attempt to recover some files, but the system 'restore' actually wrote all over her data and made about 70% of her files unrecoverable.  It was at that point that I made the decision that I would put a dual boot system on all future computers that we owned, so that even if windows fails (as it often does) we should be able to have an operating system to salvage important files.  I looked around and found that Mandrake wasn't too bad, but I couldn't get my wireless working.  Not a huge problem, as I was only going to use it in an emergency anyway.  

I also attempted to take some other precautions to keep our computers safe; such as using a separate partition to store files away from Windows, and not running as an administrator for normal tasks.  However this became rather tedius, because many programs won't run in Windows without admin priveledges, and others don't install properly when using 'Run as...'.  

I purchased a laptop for myself for work a couple months ago, and was looking to see if there was a distro other than Mandrake to use as my backup OS.  I tried the Ubuntu LiveCD and really liked it.  The live CD even saw my wireless card, though I couldn't get it configured properly.  I decided to try Ubuntu as my dual boot, and went through the install process.  I fell in love!  Everything worked out of the box!  I was ecstatic!  I loved Gnome! I hated the color scheme, but that was easily changed!!!    It was tiime to cut out the middle man and not use Linux as my 'backup' but use Windows only when I couldn't do something in Linux.

I'm happy to say that Ubuntu is now my default OS in GRUB, and I only boot into Windows once per day to sync my PocketPC handheld.  Once I figure out how to sync my PocketPC with Evolution, I will be Windows free!  I have really appreciated all the help I've gotten on the forum and from the wiki.  Much more helpful than any attempt I've made at calling MS tech support.  I have given out two of the five ShipIt CD sets that I ordered to family (one of whom is an 18 year old brother-in-law interested in software programming). As soon as I finish graduate school, I will be installing Ubuntu on all of my work machines as well.  Congratulations to the Ubuntu team!!!  You have a convert for life!
