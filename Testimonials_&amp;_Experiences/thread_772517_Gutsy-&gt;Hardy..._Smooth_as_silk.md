### Post by Naan Yaar on 2008-04-28
I upgraded from Gutsy to Hardy last week. It was an unbelievably smooth process. Everything worked "out of the box" when I rebooted after the upgrade. The following were especially gratifying:
[LIST]
[*]Fonts look better in Hardy than I have ever seen them in a Linux distro. All apps looked really sharp.
[*]I bought a new APC Back-UPS, plugged it in and connected the USB cable to the computer. When I turned on the display of the gnome-power-manager applet, I was able to see the status of the UPS. Also, the applet picked up the line down status when I unplugged the power cord to the UPS. I had to do absolutely no configuration to make this happen.
[*]Connecting to a PPTP server was nearly trivial. I used to use pptpconfig, but it did not upgrade to Hardy properly. Instead, I used the network manager applet to connect. It was a completely GUI-ified configuration that worked flawlessly.
[/LIST]

The last two items may have been available in Gutsy already. In any case, Hardy sets the bar to a very high level for ease of use and automagic configuration of myriad things.

Congrats and thanks to all the developers that made this possible. Simply amazing!
