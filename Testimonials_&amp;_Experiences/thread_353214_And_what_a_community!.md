### Post by whitefort on 2007-02-04
I'm one of those people who have been finding the switch from XP to Ubuntu to be not so smooth and easy.  Ubuntu is, without any doubt, the friendliest and best Linux I've tried.

But what has impressed me even more is this community.  There's a patience and helpfulness towards newbies like myself that's all too rare on the net.

I had a problem setting up a network - still not resolved - but I was shocked at how willingly people gave up their time to help try to solve the problem.

Yesterday I was trying to install the Nvidia drivers.  I made a stupid mistake and messed up my xorg.conf file.  After a few hours of futile mucking about, I reconciled myself to a complete new install, but first I posted a 'help?' message - and a few minutes later got a reply that fixed the file by typing a single line into the terminal.

I'm honestly starting to wonder if there's something about the Ubuntu philosophy that rubs off on its users.  And hoping that I'll soon know enough to be able to return the favours by helping other newbies.

Thanks everyone.
