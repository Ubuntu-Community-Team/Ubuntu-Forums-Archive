### Post by perpetualcacophany on 2009-04-01
Hello,

So, I was just thinking today, and I realized something. Back in my days of Windows use, I would pirate almost every program that I had. It wasn't due to me not wanting to do it the legal way; I just simply didn't have the money to pay for all of the programs I needed (I'm a 19 year old college student). I always felt bad using these pirated programs, and even when I started using OS X I continued having to use pirated programs.

What I realized though, is that since I started using Ubuntu about a month ago, there hasn't been a single piece of pirated software on my computer. So, I would like to thank all of the developers out there for helping me find a way to use my computer the way I want to without all of the associated guilt from piracy.

Thank you.

I never felt guilty either. When I used Windows, I endeavored to use as much freeware as possible. However, when an opportunity to acquire an MS "pays as you go software package" arose I always took advantage of it. As for feeling guilty, I believe the individuals who sold the software (at an unbelievable profit) should have felt guilty. Now as the FOSS footprint increases, perhaps many will realize to what extend they have been exploited and seek a FOSS alternative. As the economy dictates spending, many may seek free alternatives that will still permit them to utilize their PC (to perform everyday activities) without the excessive out of pocket costs.

IMHO, awareness is the key and eventually knowledge of alternatives will spread.

### Post by pbpersson on 2009-04-01
Hello, my name is Phil and I spent years living in guilt.  It used to stress me out thinking about all the license agreements I was breaking and all these software packages would display messages, "This is the 5,284,942th time you are using this software for free, don't you think you should pay for it?"

Also, every so often a Microsoft product would say, "We think that you have stolen this software, please insert the first CD from your install package to prove you are not a crook.  This software will not function until you comply with this request, regardless of the deadlines you are facing or whether or not you know where the CD is."  :o

However, now I have escaped that horrible life and have entered the world of FOSS, I am never looking back.  :)
