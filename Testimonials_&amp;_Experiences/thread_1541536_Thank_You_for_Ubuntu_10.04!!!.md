### Post by jromer on 2010-07-29
I've been dual-booting Ubuntu on my desktop for several years now. (Used Hardy from inception and had a clean upgrade experience to Lucid a couple of months ago...too cool!)It has slowly edged out Windows. I now do everything on Ubuntu (Office, Internet, Personal Finance, Audio/video encoding, DVD production and burning, etc.)I just keep Windows around to run certain proprietary software for work.)

I recently bought a Dell 15R laptop (i3 processor, Intel HD graphics)...my first laptop. I was a little concerned how difficult the installation process might be...Here's my experience intalling Lucid 10.04 32-bit...

Camera                          Worked no adjustment
Wireless                        Worked no adjustment
Logitech m305 (Wireless Mouse)  Worked no adjustment
Touchpad                        Worked no adjustment
Compiz(rotating cube and all)   Worked no adjustment

Plus the Grub dual-boot menu worked flawlessly. (As I set-up a dual-boot system with Win7)
