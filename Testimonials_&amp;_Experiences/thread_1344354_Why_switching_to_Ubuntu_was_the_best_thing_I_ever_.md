### Post by Justinlinux on 2009-12-02
I am a recently changed user when it comes to computing. I know a great deal about Windows, but even still I suffered through many terrible problems and I finally had enough. So, I switched to Ubuntu and I have not looked back. First off, Ubuntu is MUCH FASTER than Windows, and because of my old computer, the faster, the better. Second, and some will argue this fact, I believe Linux has more capability and potential when it comes to having a good user experience. I never had any real problems.... I simply installed Ubuntu, and that was it! It simply worked from there on in! My wireless driver worked, my apps worked in Wine, and that was it! Third, Linux is more secure. I don't have to worry about getting viruses all the time like I do with Windows. Also, Unix is built differently than Windows, and obviously for the better. It was built secure from the ground up, which gives me more peace of mind. 
    And so, these are just a few of the reasons why I love Linux. Way to go Ubuntu, way to go.

### Post by kingbilly on 2009-12-02
Thats great to hear you are having such a good experience.  Sheesh i started in the days of hoary hedgehog man thinking about wireless was like a literal dream lol.  

Just wanted to comment on the speed increase you can find in Linux.  Remember that your dealing with more than just the Linux kernel.  You are installing a whole operating system with many more services and processes to make it functional.  

Now the fun part,
Not everything that comes with Ubuntu or Kubuntu is necessarily functional to you, or perhaps that important to the experience your looking for on a computer.  There is another distribution called Xubuntu, as well as other similar operating systems that strip away some of the ***flair/shortcuts (e.g. In older Xubuntu you couldn't use the mouse to select more than one file at a time on the desktop).  This, i'm not sure what to call it.. trimming of the fat?.. allows and old machine like a Pentium 3 at 1.2 GHZ to feel like your brand new dual core in terms of responsiveness and speed.

The main point you should know is that Linux is very SCALABLE! 
From cluster computing down to embedded devices, you can scale it to your needs.
In our case, imagine you wanted to scale windows down to run on old hardware, but to be modern enough to run today's apps (sorry, not gonna happen.  have fun having people tell you app X won't run on windows 98).  You can scale a linux operating system to get more life out of your old hardware.  Have fun!!!:popcorn:
