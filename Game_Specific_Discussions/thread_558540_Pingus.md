### Post by 5hundy on 2010-05-02
one jump to catch them all: have the first pingu jump to the left (he does all the work in this level), have the next pingu just block the rest on the stating platform. once the first pingu turns around, have him build a 1x-long bridge starting at the corner of the platform such that when all the pingus are released, they will fall onto your bridge. Once he's done with that, just have him build the necessary bridges to the exit and blow up the first blocker.

full circle: turn the first pingu into a floater. when he touches down, have him jump twice to the left so he's on the stairs. once he turns around, have him build a bridge 2x long between the stairs and the rock (starting from midway down the stairs) - this will contain any pingus which end up over there. repeat the process with 4+ more pingu (float -> 2x jump) until you have several collected on the left. it's difficult to save many on this level because of the limited number of floaters. once you have enough, build a 1x bridge starting at the top of the stairs. as each pingu reaches the end of this birdge, have him jump to freedom (2x towards the exit).

### Post by def_mornahan on 2011-01-29
"Locked up by the witch"...I solved this by having one pingus bash out, leaving the rest behind a blocker very near the entrance.  The pingus that bashes out can jump from island to island...it's a little tricky but I made it on the first try, so it can't be that hard.  Eventually he'll walk up the stairs to a spot where he can bash/mine his way back into the prison chamber.  I had him mine until he hit steel and turned around, then waited for him to get back to the far wall of the stairway chamber, where you can mine/bash your way to the exit.

At that point you could bomb the blocker to let the other pingus out.  I had a miner left over so I let the others out that way and jumped the blocker the way the first pingus had gone, just for the heck of it.
