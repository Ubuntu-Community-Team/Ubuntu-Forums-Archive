I may get this comment deleted--like the last time I dared suggest someone try another distro--but hopefully you'll read my comment before that happens. Mepis is a wonderful distro. I currently triple-boot Mepis, Windows XP, and Ubuntu. Having tried well over ten distros, I have to say Mepis and Ubuntu are the best for new users--each with its own strengths.

Ubuntu's strengths lie in its stability; its positive, responsive, and helpful community; its substantial financial backing (it will always be free); and its thorough documentation.

Mepis's strengths lie in its user-friendliness, Windows-like interface, pre-configured partition mounting, and its live-installer CD combo.

Even though you can use the command-line in Mepis, you don't have to use it to mount partitions, to browse as root, to enable extra repositories, etc.

One caution, though: Mepis is a bit "bloated." If your computer hardware is modest, you'd be better off with Ubuntu. Mepis needs a fast computer to run properly (I'd say 256 MB of RAM at least).
