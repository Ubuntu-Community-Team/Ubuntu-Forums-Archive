### Post by Raineer on 2007-07-02
If you are in an area with a lot of wireless networks, this is almost to be expected.  I had to go away from wireless completely because of it, there are too many networks around my apartment complex and they interfere with each other.

For me, this was not at all limited to Linux, it happened just as much in Windows. Some of the new routers have settings to reduce this (and it does help) but it's not perfect. My Belkin N1 has this setting and it's not half-bad, still get knocked off and back on maybe once or twice a day.

### Post by TrueJk7 on 2007-07-02
Would need a little more information.

Are the other networks on the same channel? Have you checked your Router to see if it's dieing off @ all? 

A good way to check is find out it's IP address (usually close to 192.168.1.1) and ping it constantly and see if it drops at all

# ping 192.168.1.1

Let it run for a half hour or so and see if there is much packet loss (greater than 1 or 2 %)
