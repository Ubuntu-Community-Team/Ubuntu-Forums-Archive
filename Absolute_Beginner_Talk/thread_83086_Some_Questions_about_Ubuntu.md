### Post by Mustard on 2005-10-27
Ubuntu sets up *most* of the stuff you are likely to use in a Desktop environment by default, so I guess you can say it 'holds your hand'.  If you are the type of person who wants complete control of everything I believe Gentoo is very much along those lines, I have never used it myself.  The other day I was reading a post by someone who was talking about setting up a Gentoo install that took 16 hours to completly compile his entire environment, I believe it was.  I much prefer my hand being held personally. :)

I want stuff to 'just work' when I install, and barring any annoying hardware issues Ubuntu does a good job of 'just working'.

I notice you have amd64 avatar.  You might find that amd64 features are not all supported in ubuntu too, if that is the type of thing you are trying to achieve.  It certainly has **some support** for amd64 though
