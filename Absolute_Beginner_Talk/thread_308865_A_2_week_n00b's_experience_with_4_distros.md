### Post by blackangst on 2006-11-28
Hey everyone I just wanted to share my experiences (so far) being an absolute complete n00b to Linux. I have extensive Windows experience, but never really messed with Unix or Linux. Why am I looking now? Its not because of security, as my box is pretty secure. Its not really for speed, as Im happy with mine. Its because Im getting nervous about Windows apps intrusiveness. Every major app now calls home, and it really makes me uneasy. So, heres my review so far:

First my system. AMD64 3200+, MSI mobo, SCSI drive for Win OS, WD 300gig for storage, with 50 gigs set aside for Linux, Nvidia 6800 Ultra Vid card, on board LAN, Logitech 500 series USB mouse and keyboard. Now on with the distros in the order I tried them:

1. Ubuntu Edgy/LinuxMint
Install very easy. Very nice partitioner. Saw ALL my hardware immediately. Had a little trouble dual booting back into Windows, but easily fixed with a grub tweak. All in all, pretty uneventful. It was very nice on Mint to have 3rd party apps preinstalled. Started getting buggy after playing with desktop widget package, had to reboot a few times. Was nice having my SCSI and storage drive icon placed on desktop right away. Overall pretty happy. I thought it was a little bland, but mind you I hadnt used KDE yet...

2. DreamLinux
Nice interface, although icons are a little big for my taste. Easy enough to fix tho. Saw all my hardware first boot. Not quite as user friendly as Ubuntu, but not bad overall. Seemed a little bland to me. Downside: completely fvcked my MBR. Formatting was a little confusing. Ended up reformatting Windows (no big deal anymore as I use Ghost). Wasnt smiling happily much, so away that disk went. FDISK my partition and on to the next...

3. MEPIS
Again, easy install, not as much so as Ubuntu, but manageable. Dual booted into Windows right away. Saw all my hardware right away. The difference between Gnome and KDE was a little overwhelming for me, as KDE has sooo many options. I liked it tho. So far I like this one best. No problems.

4. ELive Revolution
Wow...very pretty initial desktop! However, felt a little clunky to customize. Ended up in terminal window alot to fix/download/move stuff. Seemed....buggy to me. Although I dont have enough experience with Linux to say, but not as easy as MEPIS or Ubuntu. Grew tired of little annoyances and tossed it. FDISK and on to the next one...

5. Fedora Core 6
OMG WTF did I get myself into...I dunno...I borked the install 3 or 4 times and gave up LOL didnt see my SCSI drive right away, and had a hell of a time getting my vid card to work properly. Too much work for me...

Conclusion so far:
Ubuntu and MEPIS. I cant decide yet. Let me also say that NONE of the distros got my webcam (Labtech Pro)running. I made a post asking for help last week, and I literally tried every single piece of advice I could find in here, starting from fresh installs. Kopete gave me a blue screen and Camorama kept telling me /dev/videox had nothing plugged in, even tho lsusb said otherwise. I spent more time on this than anything.

Also, couldnt get either Wine nor Cedega (paid of course) to run Everquest correctly. I got fairly close on a Mint install, but got frustrated. As with my cam, I scrounged 3 or 4 forums looking for answers, and couldnt find a fix. Will need to work more on this, as I am a gamer.

Over the next few days I'll try Linspire and PCLinuxOS. I also will check out Kubuntu as I think I like KDE just for the eye candy and options. 

I will say right now I work on a Win2000 system at work, various router and switch OS's (Im a network guy), and I have no desire to learn how to line code linux. I want it to be as close to Windows as possible. I realize its its not going to be the same, and I dont mind different. I just dont have the time or patience to learn a new line item OS from scratch. Call me lazy, but thats how it is.

So thats it for now =) Not really an informative thread, other than a complete n00b's experiences. I'll update when Im done with Linspire and PCLinuxOS. Any comments/flames/suggestions welcomed =)
