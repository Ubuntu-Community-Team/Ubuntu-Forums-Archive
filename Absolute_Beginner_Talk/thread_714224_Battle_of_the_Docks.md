### Post by ironflippy on 2008-03-04
I personally love Cairo dock. I used AWN for a while, but found it a bit slower than I would have liked. Plus, there were some odd graphical things going on. Cairo dock is far more customizable and just feels a lot smoother than AWN did. However, there are some bugs that need fixing. Both are being actively developed, although Cairo dock releases updates more often. Both are very excellent docks with huge potential in the future, although I feel Cairo dock is a bit more "modern" than AWN, but we'll see what happens after the rewrite AWN is going to have soon.

Basically, if AWN didn't feel right to you, try Cairo dock. I haven't used Kiba dock much, and it doesn't seem to have much of an active development, but it may be the dock for you.

AWN seems to have the best stability, although I have had very few problems with Cairo dock. Cairo dock has a TON of customizable features, but some are half-implemented and help is half in French, so be a little cautious. I've had the least luck with Kiba, although a lot of people use and enjoy it. It does have a cute physics engine, which I think is broken at the moment, but should be fixed in the near future. Gnome dock is just a different name for Cairo dock. It's had a couple forks, and developers have traded hands with it over the years.

Hope that helps!
