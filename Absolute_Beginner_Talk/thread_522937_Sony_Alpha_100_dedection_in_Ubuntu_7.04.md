### Post by julian67 on 2007-09-08
Camera recognition in unix type systems is usually handled by [gphoto]("http://www.gphoto.org/"). There is a list of supported cameras [http://www.gphoto.org/proj/libgphoto2/support.php]("http://www.gphoto.org/proj/libgphoto2/support.php"). Others may work, for example I have a Konica Minolta 7D, quite similar to the Sony SLR and it works even though unlisted. Unfortunately if the Sony SLR is not UMS (USB Mass Storage) compliant and Sony do not offer a driver for systems other than Mac or Windows then you only really have 2 choices. 1st  wait for it to be reverse engineered or 2nd use a card reader. A card reader can be very convenient anyway and they are extremely cheap.

A card reader is often better anyway, you can get a very tiny one and keep it in your camera bag and you'll be able to use any recent PC/Mac with a USB port to upload/copy/burn to CD or DVD your pictures.  My one cost less than $10, works fine,  and can read every type of card I ever heard of.  I can even use it to copy to/from my iRiver H340 music player (which is USM compliant and can act as a USB host). It's a real shame that so many camera/music player/phone manufacturers choose not to comply to standards.
