### Post by Zelut on 2006-03-19
You should be able to do a 'dist-upgrade', which will upgrade you to the next version.  This can be done fairly easily & shouldn't affect your /multimedia partition.  Of course it is always a good idea to back that up (I've got all of my multimedia backup on DVDR), but technically it shouldn't affect that at all.

So technically you can go either way.  You can install fresh to hard drive 1, which should recognize hard drive 2 and you're set... or upgrade from where you are.

### Post by markmark on 2006-03-19
You will be able to do either. It's your choice.

Incidentally, you might want to think about using your second drive as your /home directory (and then sticking your multimedia stuff in your home directory). That way when you do a new install you just tell the installer to use that as your /home and you get to keep all your application preferences and things that are stored there. It makes doing fresh installs much more seamless.
