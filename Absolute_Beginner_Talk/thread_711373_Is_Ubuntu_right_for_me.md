There are plenty of various apps for different parts of audio work, but you will most likely not be able to use the Windows software you are used to.  You just have to keep an open mind about things and be willing to try out different software.  I think Rosegarden is good for midi stuff.  Ardour is great for analog recording.  Audacity is good for sample editing.  Hydrogen is good for programming drum patterns.  There are lots of LADSPA effect plugins.

Check out Ubuntu Studio.
