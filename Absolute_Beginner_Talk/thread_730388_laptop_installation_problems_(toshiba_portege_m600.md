### Post by Hospadar on 2008-03-20
If you do the "use largest coninuous free space" it should work just fine, the manual setting is good for setting up seperate / and /home partitions, but that's really not a biggie, I think you'd be much better off just going with the standard install, and maybe later if you have the reason to you can do a more advanced setup.

Also at the end of the installer dialog right before it actually installs the system, it will give you a run-down of how the drive is going to look after installation, make sure it's what you want.

Do you know what video and sound hardware your machine has?  it will probably be easier to resolve these issues after an installation (not to say it will be easy or just work right off the bat, but just easier and quicker)

Another thing too, if you see hardware problems like this now, I would spare yourself the trouble of configuring the system just the way you like until after you get all your hardware working satisfactorily, in case you have to do a re-install or something.
