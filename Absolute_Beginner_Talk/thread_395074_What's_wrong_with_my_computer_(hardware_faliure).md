### Post by Patrick K on 2007-03-27
My guess is the CPU fan is dirty. I've had this happen a number of times. Depending on the type of fan they can sometimes be a pain to clean. If you have a temperature reading app and the mobo has sensors you can see if the CPU temp is elevated (i.e. over 60C or so). Also, many BIOS have a temp and fan speed readout. Enter the BIOS and check that. Many BIOSs will shutdown or throttle back an overheated CPU.

If the CPU fan is dirty, try to just remove the fan without pulling the heat sink. Heat sinks have a heat conductive paste between the CPU and the HS. If that is smeared or damaged, it is somewhat difficult to replace. And it has to be there to keep the CPU cool. Use compressed air to blow the dirt out of the HS. 

If you are physically unable to tackle this, you may need to call in some help. If you are in So Cal I might be able to assist you, depending on your location.
