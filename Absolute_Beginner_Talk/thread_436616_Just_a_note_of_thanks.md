### Post by dimbulb1024 on 2007-05-08
If you notice my bean count, I haven't posted much. I haven't had to.

To make a long story long ...

I have been using Ubuntu since January of this year. It was a snowy winter here in Denver, cabin fever was setting in hard and I needed something to do. I decided to check out Linux. I hit the WWW and did some research. I had always thought I was a "Windows Power User", I wasn't. I found, much to my dismay (tongue in cheek), I was actually something of a geek and (along with those with little computer knowledge) Linux could be for me.

Picking out of the hat, I chose Ubuntu. I downloaded an ISO and played around the LiveCD. I decided to take the plunge and create a dual boot on my laptop, a Dell Inspiron 6000. Not really knowing what I was doing, I created a computer that wouldn't load to anything. Having lost data early in life with computers, I have always backed up everything. As such, I was shocked, but not to worried. I decided to just jump in, load Ubuntu and see what happened.

To an extend I was lucky. I was already using Firefox and Thunderbird, my bookmarks and email I had no problem importing. I have used MS Office most of my computer life, but had been playing with OpenOffice recently. By some twist of fate, when I decided to start a blog I fell upon Thingamablog. All cross platform programs that did most of everything I needed. There were a couple programs I had to adjust for, MS Money being the main one. I tried a few financial programs and settled on Moneydance. It wasn't a perfect replacement, but I liked it better than the other available software. And then there is iTunes. I haven't had to update my iPod or Nano yet, so I really haven't done anything about it.

But, I digress. I loaded 6.10 and was amazed to find how most things just worked. Not everything did, I had problems and went looking for answers. Thus, we come to the point of this post, this forum and the people who take the time to answer all the questions here.

It seems that every question I have come up with, someone already had it, posted it and someone else had taken the time to answer it. I have also just cruised around this forum looking for knowledge and insight of Linux and found plenty of it.

So, to all of you that have taken the time to watch this forum, answer the questions and pass along general info, I just wanted to say thanks. The time you spend here is much appreciated.

And when the day comes that I take the big leap and change over my HTPC from XP/BeyondTV I'll be sure to visit here all the time and you all shall get another note of thanks.

Peter Larson
