### Post by Kyral on 2005-05-29
[QUOTE=HenryVI]well how do you boot a live cd from a cd onto your comp?[/QUOTE]

If you are asking how to load a LiveCD, you gotta stick it in your CD drive and then boot your computer. Most computers (unless you have messed with your BIOS, and I don't think you have) will detect it and boot from it. It will be slow, because its loading all of the stuff offa the CD instead of the HD, but its still nice :D


And the UbuntuForums will always be around to help you out.

As to what makes it so special. Well, it just seems to run "cleaner". I mean back when I ran XP, it would take like 4-5 minutes to boot up, the AV to start, ZoneAlarm to start, etc. On Linux it just boots within' 1-2 minutes. Also you don't really have to worry about virii either. And did I mention that everyhting is free (well, Cedega is the exception). Also the endless customization. I could never pull off the look I have in GNOME in XP without a bunch of thirdparty programs that eat up CPU cycles and memory.

Well, I hope I helped you. And here is hoping to a future in Linux, no?
