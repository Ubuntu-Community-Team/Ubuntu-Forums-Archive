### Post by Bachstelze on 2007-01-05
The difference is the desktop environment installed by default (GNOME vs. XFCE) and there is no better, only different. The best choice for you is the one that suits your tastes best, just test them both, that's what Live CDs are for :) Remember you can also install XFCE on an installed UBuntu system and vice versa.
