### Post by JohnCub on 2007-10-16
I just wanted to drop back in on this thread and let everyone know that I received a call yesterday from Blue Cross Blue Shield of Tennessee.  They were the other website that I was unable to access using anything but ie.  Their regular public side works fine with firefox but the employer's area as well as other non-public areas have been identified as ie only.

It took some talking.

I started with customer support whose official response was "we are only able to support internet explorer."  Then I asked to speak with a supervisor whose position was the same. I then asked to speak to someone who could, at the very least, give me a technical reason why this was so.  None were available so I left a callback number.

Later a gentleman called me back and I disagreed with his logic and told him as much.  I thought he was brushing me off when he asked if he could look into it further but I knew I was sort of up against a wall.

Finally, yesterday, a gentleman called me back and said they had decided to work towards supporting firefox by the end of the month and would call me back when they were complete.

I consider it a victory for those of us that have had to resort to workarounds, and I consider it an act of good faith by the website owner, Blue Cross Blue Shield of TN.

My method wasn't fun by any stretch.  I'd really rather not tell someone their logic is flawed or they are supporting a monopoly but in this case it got their attention and showed them another path.  I'm sure it won't work in all situations but it might work in some, so don't give up the fight, so to speak.  Everyone's first line of defense is going to be a canned answer that indicates there is nothing they can do.  I guess probing to find the actual reason let the people who could change it know that someone was probing.

My method for getting companies, my own included, to even consider anything goes something like this:

...some conversation with someone that can empower change...
So as you see, open source software is a viable alternative in many facets of our business.  We all know there will always be a need for our ...say, autocad in your example... but if we can come up with a migration plan, even a small one, perhaps eventually we can make small strides and start saving the company a little bit of money here and there.  We both know it can't happen overnight, or for that matter anytime by the end of the fiscal year, but that doesn't mean we can't think about ways we could help the company, right?


I find when talking business money is the driving factor.  Bring up the free like thought subject and and you'll get a deer in the headlight look.  Save that for after there's a plan, as a reinforcer.


Like:

So now that we've migrated everyone to open office and we're saving about $300 a seat on office licensing I feel really good about it.  Not only are we saving truckloads of money we are able to examine the source code and modify it to our own needs if we so desire.  We could brand the application with our own logo or even contribute back to the community by fixing that bug that Mary Jane found in the "word" part of it.
