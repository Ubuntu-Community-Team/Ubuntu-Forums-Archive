### Post by andb on 2006-08-14
Remember old office structures. Imagine a report. It must be sent to the department managers. they are concidered critical recipients. Their assistants got copies (cc). There may be some other people, for example outside contractors who needed the info, and they got BCC'ed. Therefore, the people in the know all know who is in the know (I know, its confusing) and those who you want to keep out of the know, dont know.

examples today:
Special price offers. Salesman sends to his boss the special deal he's doing, then BCCs his clients. If the clients were CC'ed, they could give the full client list to a competitor.

One language school I know sent messages to all their teachers via CC. One teacher was angry, left to another school and gave the email with the list to the new school. They promptly recruited a large numebr of teachers at the cost of sending an email.
  
Request for a tender. Send it out bcc to everyone, so they dont know who they are competing against.

So, a traveling student may send an email to his parents, CC his whole family, and BCC his freinds (if he's really lazy)
