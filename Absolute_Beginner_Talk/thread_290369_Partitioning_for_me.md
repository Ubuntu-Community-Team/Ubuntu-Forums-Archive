### Post by Stex on 2006-11-01
Partitioning was a scary prospect for me when I first looked at ubuntu. Just sit back, relax, and let the ubuntu live CD/installer do it all for you :) You don't need to "prep" anything. There are no known problems with dapper's partitioning so it's all safe.

I'm not sure what you're wanting to do, whether you want to wipe your computer and replace it with ubuntu or whether you want to do something called a dual boot (Keep the operating system you have already and choose which to boot each time you turn on your computer). You'll have to decide this before you install.

If you wipe your computer and replace it with ubuntu you will lose all of your files and your original operating system. If you dual boot you will have less disk space for each operating system which you set with a simple slider during the installation.
