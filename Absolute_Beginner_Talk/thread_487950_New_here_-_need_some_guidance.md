### Post by punx45 on 2007-06-29
ubuntu.com has some wonderful documentation for the desktop and server systems.   [Take a look here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/7.04/advanced-topics/C/index.html") for info on many of the popular server services, as well as an introduction to the command line.   Its a good place to get started.

you can browse around the rest of the ubuntu.com docs and wiki for even more.  

there are also a couple of ubuntu books available in print if you want to kick it oldschool.   I have seen some in the various major book retailers.   as far as which one is best, I can't help you there, as I have read none of them myself.   Also, there are plenty of resources and books for linux in general.   Check out all of the O"Reilly press books.   They have a book for every major service, language, shell, etc that you could possibly think of.

Also, I have some links to a couple of the Ubuntu books that are available to read free online.  The bookmarks are on my Mac though, which i am sadly away from at the moment.   I will revisit this thread later on and add those.

Oh, PS.   while you are getting into the server, force yourself to do as much by command line as you can.   You will be glad you did!
And learn vi!
