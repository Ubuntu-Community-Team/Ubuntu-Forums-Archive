### Post by e_james on 2007-05-03
Do you mean that you can't even get to the first menu (install, memtest, verify cd etc)? If you can get this far, check out the options at the bottom of the screen. There are some for unusual video requirements.

Over the past year or so I have tried out about 2 dozen live cds, mostly magazine cover discs. I found some would work and some not. Some versions would work and others not. Sometimes I could get them working with the suggested startup options and sometimes not. The ideal solution is to pick your hardware to suit linux, but most of us don't have that choice.

You should also check out the hardware forum. You can search (as I did) for references to your hardware. Generally the more often it is mentioned, the more problems users have found. What I found (just glancing at the titles), is that there are issues with your graphics card but nothing as fundamental as you describe. It makes me wonder if your cd is good. Try running the test if you can.
