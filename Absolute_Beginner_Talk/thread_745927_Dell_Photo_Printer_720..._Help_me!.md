First off, good luck with this.  I have the same printer and I've only ever got basic functionality out of it.  The Dell 720 is actually a Lexmark Z600 (or maybe Z615, I forget) with a Dell logo slapped on it.  Luckily, Lexmark *does* make drivers for it.

I haven't personally tried this, but [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=340749&postcount=4") somebody has packed up the lexmark drivers and you should be able to get things working.

I HAVE, however installed them myself (very tedious, and not fun)
You can get the driver here:
[http://downloads.lexmark.com/cgi-perl/downloads.cgi?ccs=229:1:0:390:0:0&os_group=&file_attachment_name=CJLZ600LE-CUPS-1.0-1.TAR.gz&dldiv=downloads/cpd&fileID=1151&searchLang=en&download=yes&fsize=4301167](http://downloads.lexmark.com/cgi-perl/downloads.cgi?ccs=229:1:0:390:0:0&os_group=&file_attachment_name=CJLZ600LE-CUPS-1.0-1.TAR.gz&dldiv=downloads/cpd&fileID=1151&searchLang=en&download=yes&fsize=4301167)

Now, once you extract this you'll have an rpm (for redhat systems, not debian systems)
Last time after scouring the internet for hours and hours I ended up finding a gentoo site that mentioned using rpm2cpio to get the files out and put them where they belong, but I'm afraid I can't remember the specifics so you'll need to use google if the debs from the forum post I linked earlier don't work.
