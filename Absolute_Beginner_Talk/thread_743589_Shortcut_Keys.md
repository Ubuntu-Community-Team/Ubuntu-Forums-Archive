### Post by Jhevon on 2008-04-02
Hello All,

I saw other posts about setting up shortcut keys in general, and I actually stumbled on how to do that beforehand. But how do I set up shortcut keys within applications?

In particular, it is a habit of mine, from Windows, to hit the Backspace key to go back in web pages (as opposed to clicking the back button). I find this a lot easier. But Ubuntu does not have this feature for browsers. How do I set it up?

Thanks guys,
Jhevon

---

### Post by cardinals_fan on 2008-04-02
Open Firefox (the browser).  Type 'about:config' in the address bar (the one where you have a url).  Search for 'browser.backspace_action' in the filter area.  Click on the part under 'Value' and change it to 0.  You might have to restart Firefox (not the computer) for this to take affect.
