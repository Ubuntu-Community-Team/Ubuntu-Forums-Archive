### Post by davmac on 2006-09-27
I've found the easiest way (certainly for a first timer) is just to let the installer sort it out, after you've told it how much space to allow for Ubuntu.

Boot from the live CD and you'll see the install icon on the desktop. Start it up and you're away. Personally I've found this 100% reliable and have installed on loads of different hardware (Asus s6f laptop, Thinkpad T23, Dell Inspiron 510m, Dell Dimension 5000, etc). Other people have had problems though - but you've got your backup so that's OK!

On my Dells I have always deleted the backup partition, but I do this as soon as I get the machine - nuke the whole disk, partition it as I require and then do a clean install of everything. In your case, at the moment, I'd suggest you just leave it be. Try out Ubuntu and see if it fits first.

If you're still a bit wary, why not get in touch with your local Linux User Group ([http://www.lug.org.uk/lugs/london.php)](http://www.lug.org.uk/lugs/london.php)). When I was starting out I found it particularly helpful and I know the London LUG has install days every now and then, where you can take your kit along and they'll walk through an install with you.

BTW: If you're interested in music composition also have a look at dyne:bolic and Agnula live CDs. Not up to Cubase SX3, Reason et al, but they provide some great tools for the musician.

Hope this helps,

Dave Mac
