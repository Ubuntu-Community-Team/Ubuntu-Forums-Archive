third, in synaptic, search for "mplayer plugin" for mozilla/firefox.

### Post by tw.bodean on 2008-02-05
This is very interesting...

I dual boot both WinXP as well as Ubuntu.  One of the main reasons for keeping WinXP on my machine is because my other half loves to listen to Napster (which I actually pay for...).  This site provides the exact same service, as far as I can tell, which keeps me as a paying customer in addition to keeping WinXP on my machine.  Having found this site now, I'm at a loss as to why I should continue being a paying customer.

After further investigation, it appears that free Napster only allows a user to listen to each track up to a maximum of three times after which we need a subscription in order to play the same tracks again.

The ability listen to every track more than three free times is the only reason I will keep my subscription.  However, if I were ever yearning to hear a particular song and were not at home, I know where I can now go, in order to find great music.

The only thing that could be better than free Napster, would be the ability to sign into my account and access the music through my web browser.  Until this option is available or an Ubuntu compatible client is released, I will continue to be a loyal subscriber.
