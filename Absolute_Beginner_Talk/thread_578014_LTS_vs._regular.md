### Post by kyphi on 2007-10-16
An LTS version is a very stable and reliable version of Ubuntu that is supported for three years on your home computer.

The 'regular' releases are supported for 18 months and contain functional improvements.  For example, they have at their core a kernel that will accommodate the necessary drivers for the more recent changes in technology, such as motherboards with various controllers for your hard and optical drives, multiple-core processors, sound cards, graphics accelerators and other peripherals.

### Post by kyphi on 2007-10-16

Correct.

> do you guys prefer LTS or regular releases?

For my mission-critical system I prefer LTS, although I do try out any of the newer versions as well as any new Linux distro on another computer.

Generally, it depends on what you expect to achieve with your computer.  To write a letter is the same in Dapper Drake (6.06 LTS) as it is in Feisty Fawn (7.04) and the same goes for web browsing, email, web page creation, playing music, listening to radio streams, playing a video or any other activity.
