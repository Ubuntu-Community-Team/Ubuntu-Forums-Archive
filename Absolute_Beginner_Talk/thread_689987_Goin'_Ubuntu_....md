### Post by sloggerkhan on 2008-02-07
gparted is my prefered partitioner. It comes on a live CD version. google it.

However, if you only have 4-5gb of space on your drive, I do not recommend repartitioning or changing things around. When a drive is getting to be full, you should really back up most of the files and free up some space. Another few gigabytes and windows will start to really crawl if it hasn't already, not mention the fragmentation problems with their filesystems. Plus, the less space when you try to resize, the more likely a problem will show up.

Your computer sounds fine spec wise, except for the HD space. I'd say try to make a about 20 gb free space, then use 10 for ubuntu, 1 for swap.
