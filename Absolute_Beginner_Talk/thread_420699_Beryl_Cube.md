### Post by Dave54 on 2007-04-23
I have beryl working so I'll answer any questions, but I need some more information.

I assume you have beryl installed and running? I'm pretty sure "Desktop Effects" have to be turned off in order for beryl to work...

So for me, once beryl was installed, the cube was on by default. Press ctrl+alt+right to spin around the cube. If it isn't working for you, what happens? Does ctrl+alt+right not do anything at all?

edit
If you really think it's just the settings, then here's what to do. Open Beryl Settings Manager, and on the buttons at the top, click "Desktop". Then, on the left side, make sure "desktop cube" is checked. As long as that is, it should work.
