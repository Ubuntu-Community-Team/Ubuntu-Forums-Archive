### Post by AndyCooll on 2006-02-02
Fedora Core is probably what you want. As mentioned above, this is the free version. Red Hat use this as a "tester", of which the best bits go in to their Red Hat Enterprise Linux which is a commercial version primarily aimed at businesses.

Fedora Core is one of the mot popular flavours of Linux (along with Ubuntu, Suse and Mandriva). By default it also uses the Gnome desktop. 

Fedora Core was the first version of Linux I tried and I quite liked it. I tend to think of it is a bit of a "jack-of-all-trades". By that I mean that it tries to appeal to a wide variety of users. 

Ubuntu appealed to me even more however because it kept things simple, didn't present me with a bewildering number of choices (though I can still have access to just as many applications as is available with Fedora if I choose), and just worked (apart from my wireless stuff!). And though Fedora was quite good, for me Ubuntu was even better.
