We just bought a pc in the office and decided to use Ubuntu AMD desktop.  We booted with the Live CD and had it installed from there. While installing, it displayed some I/O error. Our Linux expert looked at it and found that the SATA hard disk was not being read. 

He fixed it by adding an IRQPOLL line somewhere in the bootup. The ubuntu desktop came up at first. BUt after installing automatix and downloaded some stuff (like nvidia drivers) we restarted the PC and we couldn't get to the ubuntu desktop. We did this almost 10 times and he kept trying to add irqpoll again. It would save properly. Eventually he changed the cables that were connecting to the SATA harddisk. 

Suddenly, it worked.

I've totally no idea what he did but decided that it may be worth posting. So today, I'll be working on a linux desktop. :-D
