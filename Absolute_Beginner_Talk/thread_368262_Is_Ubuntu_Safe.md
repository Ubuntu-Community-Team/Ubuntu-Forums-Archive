### Post by nickoli_02 on 2007-02-23
I've installed and reinstalled Ubuntu many times, and I can say that not once have I not been able to boot into my Windows partition (though, I never do that nowadays anyway :) ). That said, there is a possibility that grub doesn't immediately recognize your windows partition. I'd recommend searching the forums or look at the wiki for the most common problems with dual booting XP and Ubuntu. Just be sure you back up any important files that you don't want to lose before installing Ubuntu and you should be set.

It's up to you whether you want 6.06 or 6.10. There isn't much difference between the two; the main difference is 6.06 will be supported for 3 years where 6.10 is supported for 6 months.

### Post by rajan on 2007-02-23
There's absolutely no guarantee that ubuntu is safe or that it will ever work correctly. There is a great likelihood that ubuntu will work correctly and not ruin your XP, but absolutely no guarantee. The best thing you can do is to back up ALL your data and then try to dual boot. Otherwise you could simply buy a new hard drive and install linux on that. for about 70 bucks you can have a really nice hard drive that runs the best OS (ubuntu) and you can really learn to use it well. That's probably your best option if you're concerned about overwriting your XP data/OS. Anything while booted from the one HD will not affect the other one. good luck!

### Post by steven8 on 2007-02-23
Try liveCD's of both, and see which one works with your hardware best.  Then use that one.  I just this past week worked up the courage to do a dual boot on my main HDD.  I didn't manually do anything during the install.  I allowed gparted to halve my HDD and install in the free space.  It works great.

I will warn you.  When I first booted into XP, it made me run a scandisk to check for errors, and I had to reboot.  On second boot, it claimed to have found new hardware, but didn't say what, installed the software for said unknown hardware, and made me reboot again.  It has worked perfectly ever since.

Just don't be frightened if that happens.
