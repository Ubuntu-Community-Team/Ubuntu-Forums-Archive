### Post by schurtek on 2006-08-11
linux will run on almost every i386 compatible system. I have yet to find a pc it won't run on. First thing you need to do though, is make a list of all the hardware on the chosen pc (laptop). Then by searching google, windows device manager and using some other nifty tools you'll find lying around tucows and c-net... start collecting the make and model of each device as well as chip numbers that drive the device. Then you will find that driverguide.com is your friend.

I wanted to load Redhat Enterprise Linux 3.4 on my Dell PII-300 Laptop. Phoned Dell up to get all the chip numbers... and they told me it wouldn't work. So I installed it, got everything running except sound, so I called them back... again they told me it won't work... I argued and stated that it was running, on the Dell PII-300 on my lap at that exact moment. Again, he protested. So I took a photo with my phone... got his email and sent the photo on... I kept the call going for a few seconds, and then he suddenly gave me the chip number... 45 minutes later I had sound...
