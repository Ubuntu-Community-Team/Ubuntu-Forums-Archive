### Post by old_geekster on 2007-04-22
You can use "Synaptic Package Manager" for both of your questions.

Open "Synaptic" and search for nvidia.  Then, uninstall the drivers.  Once this is done, reinstall them and reboot (Ctrl + Alt + Backspace).  Hopefully, your problem will be corrected.  

Sometimes reinstalling the drivers is the only way to eliminate glitches.  IMHO, you made the correct choice by going back to a nVidia card.

In "Synaptic" again, search for KDE.  Install all of the components.  Then, reboot (Ctrl + Alt + Backspace).  When it asks for your USERID:, look down at the lower left corner.  You will see "Options".  Click on "Otions" and "Select Session".  You can choose to boot into KDE.  Also, you will have the choice to make it for one-time or permanent.  I would choose one-time until you see how it works.

Hopefully, this will help you.
