### Post by pappapump on 2007-06-03
Thats usually caused by a faulty bios battery on the motherboard.
Ubuntu like most other linux installs will try to auto correct the time but if the bios clock is running awry then you either need to clean the battery contacts or replace the battery.
Since All os's refer the the bios clock at some point, most clock errors are the result of an improper battery voltage.
This happens a lot with dusty PC's or PC's located near a smoke filled environment.
