### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-15
[QUOTE=dermotti]KDE will be easier if you expect your system to act similar to windows.[/QUOTE] Slight correction--KDE is similar to Windows if you expect the *default* to have one taskbar on the bottom, as opposed to two taskbars--one on the top, one on the bottom.

You can easily delete the top taskbar in Gnome and move a few of those items to the bottom bar, and it'll look just like Windows, too.

The default mouse behavior in KDE will not act at all like Windows--it uses a single-click instead of a double-click. That can be changed as well.

Neither desktop environment is really more like Windows, and both are extremely customizable.

Read more here:
[http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome](http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome)
