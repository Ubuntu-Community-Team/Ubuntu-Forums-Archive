### Post by CaptainLinux94 on 2008-04-10
Have you tried setting up your GMail account with thunderbird countless times and are looking for a solution?

Look no further...

[B]
[LIST=1]
[*]Log onto gmail
[*]In the upper right hand corner, click "Settings."
[*]Click the Forwarding IMAP/POP tab
[*]Enable POP
[*](Assuming you've already created your unsuccessful Thunderbird account)Edit>Preferences>Click your account>Click the edit button on the right side of the preferences window
[*]Click the "Receiving Email," tab.
[*]On the dropdown menu at the top of the preferences/receiving email tab select POP.
[*]In the "Server," text input area, type (without the quotes) "pop.gmail.com".
[*][u]This step is key to using gmail in Thunderbird and is not outlined in any gmail tutorial, so read carefully:  In the same window there should be another drop down menu under "Security," select SSL Encryption.
[/LIST]
[/B]


Thanks for reading and I hope I helped,
