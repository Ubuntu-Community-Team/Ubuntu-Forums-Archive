### Post by Linux_Man on 2007-11-23
For number 1, I would imagine that it is a graphics card issue, not a monitor one. Post your graphic card specs so we can better help you (like vendor and which drivers you are using). For number 2, you can install "restricted" codecs that can let you play other forms of media files, **These may be illegal if you are in a patent-restricted state such as the USA where you do not have the freedom to use these free codecs** its more or less a legal issue and isn't because Ubuntu can't give you those, but legaly you can't. On 7.10 and 7.04 there is an easier way of installing codecs via Codec Buddy, for 6.06, try searching for "Codec" in synaptic when you have Mulitverse, restricted and universe repositories enabled.
