### Post by Ambimom on 2006-08-13
Use the Synaptic Software Manager to load Azureus.  It is the most popular client and there's a lot of online advice on how to use it. In Azureus, you see immediately if you're seeding or not and even with whom you are sharing. 

As long as you are connectable, you will be able to receive files and seed them efficiently.  Assuming that you are connected to a router, you must open a port to both TCP and UDP traffic to become connectable.  Portforward.com provides excellent instructions on how to do this.  Search for your particular router. Then follow the step-by-step instructions on how to open ports. 

Just remember, it is common courtesy to seed a file until you have uploaded as many bytes as you have downloaded. 

FYI, even if your ports are closed, you can still download and upload, but not as efficiently.

### Post by Ambimom on 2006-08-13
> does it automatically share or do you have to have it open to seed

You have to be online to seed; but if you are, it is automatic.

> What if you're unsure if you are connecting to your full potential? How can you tell?

Azureus will show you.  Green is good; yellow is off.  Do the Nat Test in the configuration menu and it'll become obvious.  If you're ports are opened properly, you're gonna be green!
