### Post by benplaut on 2005-07-02
I've noticed alot of people lately who seem to be afraid to ask questions, as if they're going to be beaten down for being new to Ubuntu and/or Linux.

Don't Be Afraid!

You don't have to ask  to ask a question, just boldly step forward and ask it, you won't be shunned. I've seen alot of people apoligizing for using Windows (or other derrogatory names for it) and, practically begging for forgiveness. No need for that, almost everyone here uses/used it as well.

Also, all questions made by begginers don't need to be in this forum. If you're having problems with, say, Gnome, or a package not installing correctly, just put it in the forum made for it. You aren't restricted to this forum.

When you make a new post, you don't have to put "newbie with question" in the title, just go ahead and put a *real* title (like, "firefox randomly crashing"). A huge number of the people here are newbies, so there's no need to warn us of it. All of us were begginers at some point, and none of us have mastered everything.

Enjoy!
-Ben (still a newbie at many things)
