### Post by Contrast on 2007-02-05
I've installed Ubuntu on four different computers (two laptops, roughly two years old, and two desktops, one about two years old and the other about six), and the only hardware issue I've run into was getting Wi-Fi to work on one of the laptops. Aside from that, everything else worked flawlessly, from soundcards to basic networking.

As for Dreamweaver, you might check here:
[http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=3482](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iVersionId=3482)
and here:
[http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=183](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=183)

There are several other solutions for getting Windows applications running in Linux, VMWare being one I've heard good things about. You might also look into those if Wine doesn't offer a viable Dreamweaver solution for you. Alternately, as raul_ stated, there are plenty of great web design applications for Linux. You should give those a try as well.

As for the digital camera issue, when I plugged mine (a Sony CyberShot) in for the first time, it was automatically recognized as a USB device and I was able to move the images from its memory stick to my hard drive without any problems.

So to sum up, your worries will most likely be unfounded, and your hopes for Ubuntu almost definitely fulfilled. To paraphrase raul_ again, the best way to find out is to pop in the live CD, keeping in mind, of course, that it will be considerably slower than if you actually install the OS.
