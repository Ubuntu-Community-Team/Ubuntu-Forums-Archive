### Post by Herman on 2006-10-15
Well I'm very happy with Telstra Bigpond.

I'm on ADSL with an unlimited downloads account through hibis billing, you need to be in a remote area to qualify for that, it gives us a discount.

Wiith Telstra Bigpond we can access their[ files library]("http://files.bigpond.com/library/index.php") and download any Linux distribution or other software we want free of internet useage charges.
My wife buys all her music from the internet from Bigpond. We are too far away from the city to be able to go to a shop and buy our CD's. Our local newsagent has a few CDs, but not a big range. There are some at the truckstops. So we are grateful for the ability to be able to access the range of music we want.

Out here in the bush , we see Telstra vehicles and staff servicing remote transmitter sites and things like that. I think we would be waiting a while before we see any service vehicles or technicians from any other ISPs or telephone companies.

As far as actual Linux and Ubuntu support goes, well, they don't officially admit it, but I kind of suspect that some of the tech support people are closet Ubuntu users, and, well, lets put it this way, I have not been disappionted with their assistance when it was needed. (Even though it isn't official). 
I have never had any trouble connecting to the internet with Ubuntu, I just plug it in and it works.

My website that you can see by clicking on my sig links under this post is hosted by Bigpond too.

I agree with Kevin Funnell, I recommend Bigpond. :D

Regards, Herman
