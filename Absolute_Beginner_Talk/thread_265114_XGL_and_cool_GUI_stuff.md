### Post by pbaehr on 2006-09-25
I think XGL by default got scrapped from Edgy, but I may be mistaken.

I partially agree with the above notion, but I would add this.

** Do not try to install XGL if you are a novice AND you (a) don't have any interest in being frustrated for a bit, messing around, trying a lot of different options, and learning some new stuff OR (b) absolutely cannot afford downtime on the computer if something goes wrong (ie: you're using the computer at work)

If you're the tinkering sort and are looking for a bit of a project, though, there's no better way to learn than by jumping right in, in my opinion.

EDIT: Oh, and to answer the second part of your question, the hurdle in making XGL work right out of the box is that the user may not have hardware which is compatible. Nothing is more frustrating for a new user than being greeted by a blue and red screen with an x-windows error.
