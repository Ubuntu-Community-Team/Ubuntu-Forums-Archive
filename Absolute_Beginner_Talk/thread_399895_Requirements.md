### Post by jem7v on 2007-04-02
Usually they say things like "Windows or Mac" because they don't have any support staff who know anything about Linux, and if you need help logging into the network and stuff like that they won't know how to help you.  (For example: I had to talk to a technician with our cable/internet provider, and when I told him I was running Linux his reaction was "Um, okay. Uh, Linux? Hmm. Uh... so... do you have some sort of network menu or configuration or anything?" "Yes." "Really? Er, that's good, that's good..." (In the end, the problem was with the modem they sold us and nothing was wrong with Ubuntu at all.))  (Another example: the many computer repair shops in our city that Only service Windows PCs.)

Forunately for me, though, the CompSci department my university (University of Saskatchewan) has an entire open-source computer lab of 66 PCs running Mandrake, and another computer lab dual-booting Mandrake/Win2000, and a number of Slackware 11 computers scattered around the Chemistry department to process data about crystals (or something weird like that). We also have a "Linux User Support Group," which is more like a club...
