### Post by Dr Small on 2007-11-03

### Post by erginemr on 2007-11-03
You can right click the menu and enter into the menu editor. From there, you can find and erase the obsolete item for this program.

But you should first check the menu item's properties, to check where the real executable program is, and make sure that the executable file (kiba-settings or similar) does not exist in your system any more.
