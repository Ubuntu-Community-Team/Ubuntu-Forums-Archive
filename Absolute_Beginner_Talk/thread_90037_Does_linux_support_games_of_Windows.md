### Post by welsh_spud on 2005-11-26
For most mainstream games out there, your best bet is to buy a licence for Cedega (WineX). Cedega creates a kind of 'fake' Windows layer on your Ubuntu installation, and allows your to play certain Windows games.

I think games like Half-life, Counter Strike, and Star Wars Battlefront are officaly supported.

Personally, the $5 odd per 3 months is pretty good value for money, and helps fund the Wine project.

However, if you don't mind not getting the real-deal with all the propreity drivers and such, there is a tutorial to install Cedega from its CVS tree [here]("http://www.linux-gamers.net/modules/wfsection/article.php?articleid=45")
