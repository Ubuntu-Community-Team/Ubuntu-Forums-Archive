### Post by scramasax on 2008-03-20
> **BL00dFox said:**
> When I installed ubuntu, I noticed that the Firefox that comes with it looks choppy and dry... it doesnt look as smooth as it does in windows.

Yes, it's always looked really poor on the Mac, too.  It's hardly surprising that Windows is Mozilla's main focus, because that's where most of their customers are.  However, as has been said, Firefox 3 is a big improvement.  I haven't used the Linux version yet -- I'll wait till Canonical roll it out -- but I've used the Mac version and it finally looks at home on the OS.

In time Epiphany, which is the native GNOME browser:

[http://www.gnome.org/projects/epiphany/](http://www.gnome.org/projects/epiphany/)

will be going over to the WebKit rendering engine (the one Safari uses and which was based on KHTML from KDE) and then you'll have a choice that is fast, renders well, and is native to the environment.  But, in the meantime, Firefox 3 is going to *look* much more like a GNOME application, besides simply looking better -- thanks to Cairo:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo_(graphics)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo_(graphics))
