### Post by benerivo on 2008-01-26
Compiz is the bigger of the two. It manages the whole of the desktop visuals, such as minimize effects, wobbly windows, and the cube. Emerald acts on top of this, but only as a window decorator (ie. the border of the windows). Ubuntu will probably not default to compiz-fusion, but if your graphics card driver is properly enabled, then ubuntu will use compiz-fusion as the window manager.

gnome-look.org has themes for many aspects of the linux desktop. If you are interested in transparent window decoration, you should look to emerald themes (make sure you have emerald installed first), which are all listed under the 'beryl' link in the left hand panel of gnome-look.
