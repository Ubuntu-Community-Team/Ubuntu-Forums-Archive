### Post by brennydoogles on 2008-03-12
Let me see if I understand your problem correctly... You have a laptop that easily lets you remove the hard drive and add another. You want to install Vista on one HD, and Ubuntu on the other. If this is the case, as long as you only have one of them in the computer at a time (especially during installation) I see no reason for this not to work. I would caution you however, to be careful. It may not be a great idea to be continually switching the drives out, unless you have a really good way to protect them (from static and whatnot) when they are not in use. Hope this helps!!
