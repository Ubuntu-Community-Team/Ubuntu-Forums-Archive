### Post by russell.h on 2007-04-18
If you don't have any problem with 6.06 stick with it.

From a practical standpoint the main thing I have heard about Feisty is that it has more hardware support, but I can't really remark on that because Dapper, Edgy, and Feisty have all worked fine on my hardware.

From a less practical standpoint, if you are just getting bored of Dapper and want to try something new (but maybe a little risky, I have had no problems with Feisty in the last month and a half, but thats not to say its impossible) then by all means try Feisty. It has the "desktop effects" (compiz) installed by default, and beryl is absolutely *simple* to install (check the feisty forum, I think the thread is pinned). OpenOffice is updated and at least looks way better than before. And it seems as though Edgy and Feisty are both a little quicker than Dapper was.

But like I say, if you just want stability and you have stability then there is no reason to go messing around.


Edit: I just noticed your dual monitor, I haven't tried messing around with dual monitors in Feisty, but it has a new version of Xorg, so that could be one potential problem, or it could be way better, not really sure.
