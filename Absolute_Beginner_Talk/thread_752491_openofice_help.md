### Post by c_07 on 2008-04-11
I looked into this a bit ago and came up with a few solutions, but none of them really worked well, so I have been doing the best I can to get accustomed to OpenOffice.

For converting documents, you might look into [this method]("http://www.oooninja.com/2008/01/convert-openxml-docx-etc-in-linux-using.html") of converting them from the OpenXML to the ODF format. I haven't actually gotten this to work yet.

Installing Office 2007 on Linux using WINE is *possible* using[ this guide]("http://wine-review.blogspot.com/2008/03/office-2007-on-linux-with-wine-install.html"), but very buggy. I personally haven't had time to track down which DLL's need to be emulated to fix the annoying little errors (like getting bullets to display properly), so even though I have 2007 installed on Ubuntu at the moment, I would still recommend that you give OpenOffice a fair try.

I found that by[ installing the 2007 fonts]("http://www.oooninja.com/2008/01/calibri-linux-vista-fonts-download.html") and setting the page margins to 1", setting a keyboard shortcut for the math formula editor, etc., it's not too bad at all and I can get most of what I need done for school. ;)
