### Post by ubnewbie2 on 2007-04-25
I placed a previous query here about the lack of crispness to the fonts I was seeing in my new feisty Ubuntu install (I haven't had any other version before this).  Particularly when browsing the web in Firefox, my LCD panel was displaying the fonts rather badly.  I selected the best smoothing that gnome could do, but still it was not good.

Well, finally I bit the bullet, and installed kubuntu-desktop.  I just booted into KDE a few minutes ago, and turned on anti-aliasing with full hinting, and the fonts are back the way they should be.

So, is gnome really inferior to KDE with respect to anti-aliasing, or is there a setting/tweak that nobody's mentioned for gnome to fix it?
