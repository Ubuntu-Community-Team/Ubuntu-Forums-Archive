[COLOR="#ff0000"]Q: So how do I go about disassembling the computer?[/COLOR]
[/QUOTE]

Take a good look at the case.  If something can easily be screwed or unscrewed, it probably has a purpose which makes your life easy.  Look for things that swing out instead of pulling them out.  Simple things like a power supply fitting which makes removing the power supply easy may give youa clue as to what is the best way to fiddle with the insides.  

Usually, the motherboard is on a tray which swings out.  You need to remove all the pci cards which are additionally attached to the case frame first, though.  Don't be shy to remove all the connections and ribbons.  It's fun to put them back.

EDIT:  Added image to show you where to push to release the cartridge.  Push both while you pull up.  Pull on the fan wires (pull it out -disconnect it) where it connects to the motherboard first.

### Post by ske1fr on 2006-08-03
Hanzj, your third picture shows rivets, not screws.  Leave them alone.  Can you see the screw ends of the standoffs (the little bolt things that hold the motherboard above the base plate) sticking out from the other side of your box to picture one?  You'll have to hold them still to unscrew the motherboard screws.  Look very carefully at that side of the case and see if there are screws along the edges there that allow you to either swing the motherboard plate out, or even remove it altogether.

Sometimes the black retaining clips for the processor require you to squeeze tabs in, sometimes you have to pull tabs out.  Have a look online for information about SECC and SECC2, these are two slightly different Slot 1 package types that were available.  Yours are probably SECC2.  Try carefully pulling the clips away from each other and see if one or other side of the processor comes loose.  If alll else fails for machine 2, and you can get to the back of the motherboard as well as the front, then it's possible to detach the retaining clips from the motherboard altogether.  Mine are fixed on by a kind of two piece plastic popstud that can carefully be unpopped.

All the other advice is sound.  I use an Abit BH6 with a Rage Fury 128 and a P3-650.  You might be better with Xubuntu with that amount of RAM though.  512 MB is fine for Kubuntu.

One more thing.  Sometimes the BIOS on a motherboard had code for just the Pentium 2 or just the Pentium 3 so you couldn't just switch from P2 to P3.  You might have to reflash the BIOS to support the Pentium 3 instead.
