### Post by Imsati on 2007-10-14
Well in that case, do your research and compare features/prices. As I mentioned before, I'd opt for a graphics card (either pre-installed or purchase at the same time) over on-board graphics. You don't even need a great graphics card, 256 MB should be fine. Don't let a sales rep tell you that you'll absolutely need the top of the line model. If you purchase the card separately, expect to pay around $60-$80 for a 256 MB model.

A normal dual-core CPU will suffice as well. Don't bother spending extra money for a Quad-core or extreme edition.

Other than that, it's pretty much open as far as other options go. Nothing will make or break the rest of the setup.
