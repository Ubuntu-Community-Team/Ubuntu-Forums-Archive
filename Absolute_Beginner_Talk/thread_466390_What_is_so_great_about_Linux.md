### Post by steve.horsley on 2007-06-06
It may be worth starting  another thread with a  better title if you want help with the sound issues. Out-of-sync sound is sonething I have never experienced, so it's not normal, that's for sure. It may help tro say which viewer you are using, what file type, and where you got the codecs.

As for what's so great - it's different for different people. Personaly, I like the configurability, tweakability, the open-ness in that I can get in and rummage around under the hood, finding out how bits work. There is no real limit to how deep I can go other than my own capacity to keep digging. This is the computer enthusiast in me of course. I also dislike MS's habit of breaking standards and interoperability and generally wasting their customers time on purpose, and I find Linux to be like a breath of fresh air.

If you don't have the time or inclination to learn something new, maybe you should stick with windows until you have more time. But bear in mind that a lot of any problems seem a thousand times worse at the moment purely because you are not familiar with Linux, and similar problems on Windows would not really phase you. It's awkward that you have a problem right at the start of your learning, but that happens sometimes. Persistence and patience will pay off.
