### Post by David Marrs on 2005-09-10
If you're used to UNIX then you're most of the way there anyway. Aside from reading the usual literature, I'd spend some time at the terminal getting to know the system. Most, if not all, relevant files are easy-to-read text files so you can get a feel for how everything is stuck together quite quickly.

I'd also recommend the debian documentation and the books at tldp.org. The progeny manual is particularly good and very relevant to Ubuntu and GNU/Linux in general.
