### Post by Kvark on 2006-05-13
There are 3 different ways to do this:
* Linspire pays licence and royalty fees to include these things without risk of getting sued.
* Ubuntu does not include these things at all to avoid legal risks.
* Mepis includes them without paying which could get them sued. But the risk is very small that the patent holders will bother to go through a legal battle and negative publicity over such a small issue. Mepis will almost certainly get away with it.

Ubuntu could probably get away with it too but the small theoretical risk may make business corporations (the big buyers of the official support Canonical wants to make money on) uneasy about using a distro.
