### Post by Therion on 2008-02-08
Okay, a certain amount of poor information pooling in this thread.  First of all, there is no reason you can't install whatever OS you would like on your HP laptop.  If you're unsure about which Linux distro is right for you, it's hard to go wrong with Ubuntu, but there are several distro choices, and many have very good, very supportive forums to help the new user.  

My suggestion would be to cruise on over to the [Linux Screenshots]("http://thecodingstudio.com/opensource/linux/screenshots/index.php") page, poke around for a distro that looks promising, and then downloading some LiveCD's.  

You can boot from a LiveCD and play with the OS without modifying a single thing on your hard drive: the Linux LiveCD will use only system RAM and the optical drive.  You can "test drive" as many distros as you want this way then remove the CD, reboot and go right back into your Windows setup until you decide you've found the right distro for you.  Some distros I might suggest (personal opinion) would be [Linux Mint]("http://www.linuxmint.com/screenshots.php"), [PCLinuxOS]("http://www.linuxgator.org/Gnome/gnome_page/gnome.html"), or my personal choice [Ubuntu Ultimate]("http://ultimateedition.info/Ultimate_Edition_1.7/#screenshots").

As for what you want to DO with you Linux install (pictures, music, videos and internet surfing), no problem.  As I said, I personally use Ubuntu Ultimate, and I do all of those things and more with ease.  You'll find Ubuntu Ultimate has plenty of software pre-installed that allows you to do all of that right out of the box.  As for making the OS look pretty, any distro of Linux is going to be fully configurable; some just look pretty right from the get-go.  Even the ugly ones though, are just a few clicks away from total make over.  Making a Linux distro look just the way you want it is a cakewalk.
