### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-02-21
You are not required to wipe your Windows installation at any point of dual boot setup. Depending on your partitions table, you may not even need to touch the Windows partition, least of all, wiping the drive. Basically, you are required to resize one of the existing partition to free some space for Ubuntu, then create Ubuntu partitions (root,swap)and format them only, and then install.
You can also install Ubuntu on another HDD.

### Post by taurus on 2007-02-21

### Post by Bartender on 2007-02-21
You should not go into this assuming everything's going to be OK.

You need to think about the worst case scenario because it's happened too frequently.  Could you rebuild your Windows data if it all disappeared?

At the bare minimum you should:

Back up your important personal data (pictures, documents, email addresses, etc.) to an external HDD or CD's or even a USB thumb drive if you don't have a lot of personal data.

Find the CD's for your applications (programs like Adobe Photo Elements, Microsoft Office, software for your digital camera, etc.)

Make sure you can re-install Windows and find the drivers for your hardware.

If you have the tools to do an entire image that would be great but lots of us don't.
