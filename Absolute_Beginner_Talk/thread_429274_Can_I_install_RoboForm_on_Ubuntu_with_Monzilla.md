### Post by jiminycricket on 2007-05-01
Hmm if it's an XPI it's generally cross-platform.  However certain extensions don't work on Linux-- hopefully others with experience can chime in, here.

For a similar tool there's something called [Google Browser Sync ]("http://www.google.com/tools/firefox/browsersync/")that, well, syncs your browser history, bookmarks, open tabs, passwords, etc. across a Google account so it's accessible from anywhere (with a password).

There also seem to be several other form fillers here: [https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/search?q=form&status=4](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/search?q=form&status=4) I think the Google toolbar for Firefox is another that can fill out forms, also FireForm.  In other browsers, Opera or Mozilla Seamonkey (the old Mozilla suite browser) also have formfillers built in.

For system-wide passwords, you can use native browsers like Konqueror or Epiphany which take advantage of KDE wallet or GNOME wallet.
