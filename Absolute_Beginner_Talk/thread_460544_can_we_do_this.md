### Post by GrueTamer on 2007-05-31
Yes, you can do that.  In fact, I do that myself, having ubuntu installed to my second hard drive, at first to protect the other OS on there that I didn't need, but now, I keep it there just in case my main HD goes bad (it's a laptop, and it's happened).  I use the main HD for OS testing now.  But back to the question.  GRUB can go pretty much anywhere, from a main HD to a second HD to a floppy, and you can even have it on a CD.  And this does protect the main OS, having ubuntu on a second hard drive, because you don't have to worry about partitioning over your main OS.
