### Post by ayenack on 2007-06-12
Just reread your post try this.

**cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup /etc/X11/xorg.conf** If this does not work follow instructions in section 2.

To recover your xorg.conf follow the instructions in section 2.

1. To get your resolution Working. This is a bit of a punt in the dark but try this. From recovery mode in boot options type this.

**sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf**

This will bring up your xorg.conf in nano. Scroll down to Section "Monitor" and add Option "DPMS" as shown bellow. To save the file you need to press "Ctrl & o" to exit press "Ctrl & x". Then Reboot to see if your res is correct.

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "FPD2185W"
HorizSync 31-81
VertRefresh 56-76
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

2. If this does not work do this in recovery mode from boot options.

**sudo nano /etc/X11/xorg.conf**

This will bring up your xorg.conf in nano again. Scroll down to the bit where it says Section "Monitors" and delete the added resolutions and edit it to look like the example bellow. To save the file you need to press "Ctrl & o" to exit press "Ctrl & x" this should put your xorg.conf back as it was.

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "FPD2185W"
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

Then reboot.

This should do it. But I'm out of ideas on how to fix your resolution issue. Sorry.

ayenack.
