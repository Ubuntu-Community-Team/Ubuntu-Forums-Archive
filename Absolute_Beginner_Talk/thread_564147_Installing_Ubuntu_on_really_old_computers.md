### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-10-01
I believe that DSL has a netboot floppy.  You boot it and (hopefully) it will detect your ethernet card and grab the install files from the net.  What is your ethernet card?

You may need to get a different one if it's not a common card since the Linux drivers are pretty generic for the old stuff.  If you have a dual pcmcia slot, I've had luck with a 1 GB Compact Flash card in a pcmcia adapter (~$10) as a good place to put DSL.  I've also pulled the drive on an old HP Omnibook and used an IDE-to-notebook-drive adapter (~$6) and put the drive in an old P1 machine.  Installed DSL on the drive and put it back in the laptop, and it booted!

There are lots of ways to do it.  You just need to be creative.
