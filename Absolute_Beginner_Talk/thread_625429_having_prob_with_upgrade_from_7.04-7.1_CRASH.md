### Post by Grey Box on 2007-11-28
From the information you gave it's hard to tell, but you'd be wise to backup your stuff before you do anything else (in case you didn't do that before the upgrade). 

If you **do** have to reinstall your Ubuntu, it's a very good idea to have a separate /home partition, because this is usually the one that is unique about your pc - everything else can be quickly and easily reinstalled, compared to losing your saved files and settings. Ubuntu (and most other linux distros) doesn't like being installed in a place that already has files in it.

If you don't already have a separate home partition, then you could try to resize your existing one to make room for a new partition (your home partition). Then copy your home folder across to it and then install your new Ubuntu over the top of the old partition.
