### Post by Torajima on 2007-01-22
I really think someone should create an open source store similar to iTunes. It wouldn't sell music by artists who've signed with the labels... it would sell music by indie bands just starting out, or any musician who didn't want to "make a deal with the devil" just to have their music heard.

All the money would go the artists and to maintain the site hosting the service... no RIAA,  no record labels, no middleman.

Of course, there would have to be a rating system, as such as site would no doubt play host to a lot of crappy music... but there'd probably be some gems as well.

And there would have to be a choice in formats... mp3 for compatibility with iPods and other music players, ogg for folks who'd prefer a free and open format. Neither would have DRM.

Basically, what I had hoped Apple would do when they created GarageBand...
