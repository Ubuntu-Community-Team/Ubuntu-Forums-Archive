### Post by Coucouf on 2007-05-02
I've made the move at the beginning of the year after eons of slavery. ;)

I started using M$ things back in the same MS-DOS/Win3.1 days as you.
I've been using WXP for a long time and had no special complaint about it. When you don't know what exists other than this, how could you anyway !

But the more things I read on Vista (which I had been using since early betas), the more I got afraid at all the unproductive technologies -- all the DRM-like things -- integrated deep into the system.
So I  decided to make the move to be sure to keep my freedom in the future, and to avoid paying anything for those technologies which are just harmful for the end-user.

As I had experiences of multi-boot in which I ended using only one of the systems, I just put Ubuntu in place of WXP on my 2 computers, to make a radical transition.
With this I was forced to find solutions for all my everyday tasks with linux.
I'm really happy with it now and don't even think I'll ever be going back.
I must say I was particularly impressed with the multi-user design in linux which we can easily see was conceived from the beginning. Having all user-data sorted in /home and being able to do all your usual tasks as a simple user was unprecedented in my Windoze experience !

I'm now becoming just linux-fluent enough to start converting my family members and friends.
People don't like change, there's still big work ahead ! :)
