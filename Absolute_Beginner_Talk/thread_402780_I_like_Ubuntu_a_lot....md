### Post by jahwire on 2007-04-06
I've been using computers since 1985, Macs and Windows, and have seen the evolution of the hardware/software industry very closely. I've always been interested by the Linux project, but never got time or real need to check it out.

I recently decided to get into Linux and read quite a lot about it until I decided to choose Ubuntu. I got serious problems finding the best solution for my wireless connection (3 days of tries) but got it to work very easily when I found the right answer at this page:

Using Broadcom Wireless in Ubuntu Edgy 6.10
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/bcm43xx/Edgy?highlight=%28cutter%29](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Driver/bcm43xx/Edgy?highlight=%28cutter%29)

There as still issues with the 64 bits version but it worked great for me with the 32 bits version. I also love the fact that it takes about 15 minutes to install the OS on my portable. I had to do it quite a few times before finding the right one (I tried 6.10 x386, 6.10 AMD64, and 7.04 beta AMD64 and settled for the first one). It would have taken me days with MS and MacOS... I now feel that I have a brand new laptop with run faster that before!

I found a lot of information on the Community Forums and really appreciate deeply the commitment of all the participants. Questions are answered very quickly and always with kindness.

I am so fed up with the $$ software industry. 20 years dealing with it!! When you see the latest CS3 announcement from ADOBE and the price tag, it's just sickening!!! Not to say that the release will be full of bugs – as always - and require hours of downloads for updates for the next upcoming months... and then, a new ver$$ion, and another one...

I truly believe in Free Software as a humble developer that I am myself too.

I want to congratulate all the programmers who are making this project a reality. I see that there is still a lot of work to do but you all have moved larges steps forward and soon Linux will be very easy to setup on all computers, which will make it finally the only acceptable OS for all. **The list of software is truly amazing** and more is to come.

**Congratulations to all and thank you for being here**.:lolflag:
