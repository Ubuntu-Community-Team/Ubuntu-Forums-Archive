### Post by NightwishFan on 2008-02-11
Hibernate to disk allows you to power off, then you will restore session when you boot back on. Suspend means you will be able to quickly restore to desktop but your computer is on.

### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-02-11
Suspend to memory is also known as Standby - the computer turns off the monitor, spins down the hard drive, and disables some peripherals and enters a low power state.
Suspend to disk is also known as Hibernate - the computer writes the entire contents of RAM to the hard drive and powers everything off, then when you turn the computer on, it loads everything back so your desktop is exactly how you left it.  

Standby is nice to just save power but get the computer up and running again in just a few seconds.  Some power is still being used.
Hibernate is nice for when your computer is not needed for a longer period of time (like toting a laptop back and forth from school/work).  It takes a little longer to load, but not as long as booting the computer fresh.  It does not use any power when in hibernate.
