### Post by n8bounds on 2008-01-03
Good for you. I don't think the gnome update-manager has a way to hide unwanted updates yet, but you can certainly mark certain packages as "Lock Version" with the Synaptic package manager. Alternatively, if you don't like Pidgin, you can just remove it; apt won't try to upgrade things you don't have.

The size difference you may notice might be differences in GB vs. GiB or byte rounding in Windows or a million other things. Remember also, that you have a swap PARTITION not counted there, whereas Windows has a swap FILE.
