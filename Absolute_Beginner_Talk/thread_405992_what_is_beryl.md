### Post by daynah on 2007-04-10
Beryl is very unstable. Make backups of your computer. The worst with me is it will either (on laptop with intel video) just randomly switch back to Metacity or (on desktop with nvidia) just not load any window manager. Oops. If you have... half a gui, remember Ctrl+Alt+Backspace. It'll reload xorg and have you login again.

Don't even try Beryl on an ATI card. It's a waste of your life. Learn to play the piano, start a garden. There are much better things to do with your life.

Beryl's beautiful... but beautiful like a newborn. And like a newborn, it will puke on you every once in a while (verson 0.2) and boys just can't have babies (and neither can ATI).

The Beryl website has the best guides. Only project where you should follow the guides on its own website. Just follow that and, really, you're all set.

### Post by garrido on 2007-04-10
I've been using Beryl since around last september, on an Intel-based notebook, and it has been working fine.  At the very beginning, it crashed randomly a couple of times, restarting the window manager by itself.  Nowadays, however, it is very stable (to me, anyway), and I use it regularly - it is a joy to use, and once you get used to it it can actually make you more productive, plus it makes your desktop look absolutely awesome.  Install it, configure it, then invite some windows users to check it out ;)
