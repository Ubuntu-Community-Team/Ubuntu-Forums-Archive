### Post by asmiller-ke6seh on 2008-02-11
One of the many problems with so called "Digital Rights Management" is that all rights are retained to the producer/distributor and none of the rights remain with the user who paid for the access.

For example, it is difficult (if not impossible) to assure yourself that you can make a backup to whatever media you desire; also, it is difficult to be sure that you can move the performance (music/video/whathaveyou) to whatever system you find convenient for your own listening/viewing pleasure.

Even APPLE has gotten onto the NO DRM bandwagon as of late ... and yet iTunes and iPod are among the worst of the worst in DRM "enforcement". Microlimp is THE worst. What they want to do is disable your PC if you are not using VISTA with the correct DRM disabling hardware.
