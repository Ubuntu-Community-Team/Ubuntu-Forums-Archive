### Post by tommcd on 2006-11-28
I don't know what is wrong with your xine, but if it freezes your system just type 'killall xine' in terminal to shutdown xine. You should not need to reboot.
EDIT: you can launch xine from terminal by typing 'xine'. Then if it crashes or locks up you will get an error message that may tell you what the problem was.

### Post by tommcd on 2006-11-29
Typing 'killall xine' and then restarting xine should restart it. AFAIK, as long as you can type in terminal, you should never need to reboot to restart anything, except for kernel upgrades. To launch xine from terminal, type xine (lowercase x, not capital X). 
I don't really like xine for playing music. I do use it for video though. Personally, I just use rhythmbox or sound juicer or xmms for music. Lots of people like amarok (KDE) or banshee (gnome) for music players. Perhaps if xine is giving you problems you could try one of those. I don't know what else to suggest.
