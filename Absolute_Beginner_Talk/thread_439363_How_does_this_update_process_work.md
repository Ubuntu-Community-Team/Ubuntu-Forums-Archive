### Post by bedohave on 2007-05-10
Hey Eric,

First, these fora (<--how erudite, eh?) are not the "official" channel for reporting bugs.  Check out [http://www.ubuntu.com/community/reportproblem]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community/reportproblem").

Second, I'm not an expert on sound drivers (or any drivers, for that matter).  So, I'm perfectly willing to be corrected here. 

Nonetheless, my understanding is that some drivers are not available in the basic installation/distribution because they are proprietary/not-open-source.  

Try looking at System->Administration->Software Sources.  Is the option called "Proprietary drivers for devices (restricted)" marked/checked?

Normally, if an update is made available, the Update Manager will notify you that updates are available and let you choose to install them.

Last, try searching here and more broadly on the 'net for "ubuntu" and the specific name/number of the sound module on your Toshiba laptop.
