### Post by bobbob94 on 2007-02-02
[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=263851&highlight=beryl]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=263851&highlight=beryl")
 has guides to installing beryl. Installing most programs is really not harder than in windows, as the synaptic package manager has thousands of programs that you can install with a couple of clicks. Installing stuff other than that is a bit more involved but really still pretty staightforward too, see [http://http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware]("http://http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installingsoftware")
for a good step by step guide. As with many things in Linux, it's just different from Windows so it can seem frustrating at first as you go from knowing how to do things to suddenly being a beginner again, but you'll get used to it pretty quick if you stick with it for a couple of weeks, and you can always come to these forums with any problems you can't work out.   As for Gnome or KDE, that's really just a matter of preference. You can install both and when you start Ubuntu you can log into either- so you can try them out and see what you prefer, or try the Ubuntu (Gnome) and Kubuntu (KDE) live cd's. Both are very customisable though, so don't be too swayed by what they look like on default settings...there are tons of options for eye candy desktops on Linux if that's your thing :)
