### Post by msak007 on 2006-12-03
> **aysiu said:**
> That works if you're using KDE.

By the way, this problem doesn't exist in Edgy Eft's Nautilus. You actually get a list of available programs (see attached screenshot).
Kubuntu Dapper had this problem, and Edgy, although somewhat easier, is still a little broken. I can see in your screenshot that clicking on "Add" in Ubuntu opens a list of all the apps installed, with an option to browse manually. Kubuntu also does this, but instead offers up the entire contents of the KMenu, menu folders and all. You still have to wade through the menus and submenus to find the app you want, but it's easier than getting a file browser and having to look through /usr/bin/ to find the exact name of the executable. You could argue that it's not intuitive as a list, but it may help some people if you sort apps by category rather than throwing everything out there at once.
