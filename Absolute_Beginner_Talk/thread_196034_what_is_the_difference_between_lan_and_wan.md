### Post by u.b.u.n.t.u on 2006-06-13
As elemental666 said, also:

LAN is a "Local Area Network", which means two or more computers networked so they can communicate with each other. For example, a "home network" is typically a "LAN". Offices usually have a LAN too. Some places have more than one LAN.

WAN is just another term for the internet. WAN, "Wide Area Network" can consist of any number of LANs including none at all, as in the case of a sole computer that is only connected to the internet.

LANs typically have a single access point to the WAN for security reasons. This is in offices. My LAN has every computer on the LAN and WAN.

It all depends on what one wants to achieve.

WAN is not another name for the internet.  The internet is an example of a WAN.  WAN's can also be, for example, the network of an international company (wide-area).  And typically they have to consist of two or more LAN's.
