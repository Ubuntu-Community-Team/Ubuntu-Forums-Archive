### Post by nenyalorien on 2007-04-18
[COLOR="Purple"]good to know that there are more pinoys exploring Linux![/COLOR]

a. what is the minimum specification requirements in ubuntu..?[COLOR="Purple"]
--From the Ubuntu Website: Ubuntu is available for PC, 64-Bit and Mac architectures. CDs require at least 256 MB of RAM. Install requires at least 2 GB of disk space.
--http://www.ubuntu.com/products/WhatIsUbuntu/desktopedition[/COLOR]

b. is it actually advisable to use ubuntu for an internet shop..?

[COLOR="Purple"]--pros and cons of using Ubuntu in an internet shop

pros:

1) better spyware and malware protection. as long as you don't run the OS (esp Ubuntu) as root, then you'll have considerable protection against bugs.

2) it's free

3) sometimes it's easier than Windows

4) it comes pre-loaded with applications and there are hundreds to choose from in the application repository.

5) it's faster and less prone to crashes


cons:

1) users who would do office applications on open office may later complain that their work is "messed up" when opened on windows. the solution is to get Wine or Ubuntu Feisty Fawn with Wine and run MS Office from there.

2) as far as my experience with a commercial Wine distro (crossover) goes, crossover was heavy, and a huge RAM hog. your users may complain of slowness when using MS apps on Wine. 


c. would it let lan and online games (War Craft, RAN, Gunbound, Ragnarok, etc) run..?

--yes. try to get Wine from [http://www.winehq.org](http://www.winehq.org), or cedega from [http://www.transgaming.com](http://www.transgaming.com)
--for gaming, i think cedega looks better. but i really don't know. get feisty fawn the official release, and try out wine for yourself. you should test cedega, too, to compare.  if you're not satisfied, type "windows emulator" or "run windows applications on linux"  or something to that effect on google..
[/COLOR]
d. does it come with an office..?
[COLOR="Purple"]--yes it does. open office. you don't even need to install it. but you would want to install abiword, too. it's in the add/remove programs on the applications menu, under "office". or you can search for it on the search bar. :) it's much faster a word processor than open office, and your customers might like them better. 
[/COLOR]
e. it true that Fedora 6's backbone is MS..? if so, will Ubuntu's backbone be MS too..?
[COLOR="Purple"]--eep. Linux is a Unix erm, sorry, spinoff, created by Linus Torvalds. the core is so much different from windows'. it's "family" with OS X (apple/macintosh) though. i think it is pure sacrilege to the Linux fan to say that any Linux distro has an MS core. it is just so much different, and i could go on and on. please go to wikipedia to learn more. :)
[/COLOR]

Hi there, you've had a fair few answers thus far, so I will avoid repeating everything previous people have said. I will reiterate the point already made that it is EXTREMELY important that you look into this carefully before a switch. Although it isnt always hard, it is different from windows and as such you have to look into it carefully and begin to learn how to get your way around things in Ubuntu.

a/ Min specs are pretty low, at least 256Mb RAM for Ubuntu to be honest, but you can get away with less if you install Xubuntu.

b/depends what you want to do, how competent you need to be with the OS to run an internet shop, whether your customers will mind running Ubuntu rather than Windows - if theyre just going on the internet then its not a problem as they can just use firefox which is the same as firefox on windows. It might be worth having a test or two to see what people think - after all every business is (or should be) consumer driven, and thus its important to find out what your customers think. Linux OSs will offer you more security (as long as you look into what to do to make them secure) much much fewer virus threats and no spyware/adware. (Please note before someone jumps down my throat, I have avoided with the 'no viruses' tagline, mainly because although I dont use virus scanners on the whole myself, I think it is naive of the Linux community to run in the total absence of thinking about the threat, as there are linux viruses (although very very few) but with a growing userbase the platform may become more attractive to attack, and rootkits definitely do exist for linux.)

c/Some possibly via Wine, I won't jump in with the boundless enthusiasm of saying yes, as its far from the case, you'll have to look into wine and cedega for information on what will run and the likely issues you may have.

d/ Yes - open office. Different from MS Office, though you could install it on your Windows boxes if you want to have a look at it, and also using it on your windows boxes may allow your customers to get a feel of some of the apps that you would use on Ubuntu if they come time and time again as regulars.

e/ Goodness me no! You have a little bit of reading/research to do, but NO linux is MS based. Linux is not based on the NT kernel it is based on the Linux one, for more information look around wiki or other such information sites to explain the differences. Ubuntu is debian based, which is another distribution of Linux, Fedora is red-hat based I believe.

f/ Not familiar with these I'm afraid.
