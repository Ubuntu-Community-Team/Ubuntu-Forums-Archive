### Post by RaiSuli on 2006-01-09
Am also a newbie and have made the switch after more than a decade on Windows machines. My recommendations based on my own (limited) experiences would be:

1. Get a live cd, see how you like the new look and feel of a different operating system.

2. Don't switch completely at first. Make a dual boot system that allows you to switch between Windows and Ubuntu. There are excellent guides on how to do this. You should try to mount your Windows partitions, so that you can access your files from Linux. Constantly logging in and out just to look at a file kept on the wrong partition can be quite annoying. Remember that Linux can only read NTFS formatted partitions, but can read/write partitions formatted with FAT32. 

3. Use Automatix when first installing software and codecs. I know that more experienced linux users frown upon automatic installers, but for a newbie like me it sure made things a lot easier.

4. Unless there's some specialised Windows software that you just can't do without, don't worry about installing wine. Try instead to find similar linux programs. It's fun trying out something new, and even if the graphics aren't as slick as you're used to, most of the time linux programs will do more or less the same things as their Windows cousins.

5. Visit the forum. It's a great place full of nice people who know their way around linux.

And most importantly:
6. Don't panic. :)
