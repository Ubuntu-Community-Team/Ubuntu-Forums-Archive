The other factor is cheap pc's tend to be lacking in exapndibility/upgrade capability.  They may not have PCI-E slots or are limited in the numbers of expansion slots-Ilooked at cheap ones on walmart.com once and they had like one pci slot, no agp, no pci-e, just one lowly pci. In the long run it is best to pay for quality.  When I shop now I check Linux compatibility before I buy.  I also like supporting companies that try and support well the open source community - like I will buy nVidia and never ATI as an example.

I have bought enough PC's to find that cheap is definitely not a good choice a lot of times.
