### Post by carlosqueso on 2006-08-02
1 and 2:  As long as your are careful about picking supported wireless adapters and graphics cards, it's very easy.  Just plug 'em in and Ubuntu should be able to auto-detect

3. I used OpenOffice back on the PC for my entire senior year of university and it worked great (I actually prefer OpenOffice's handling of charts).  If you need excel-style statistics handling, or just find OpenOffice too slow, try Abiword for word processing and Gnumeric for spreadsheet.  I like both sets.
4. I don't do much photo-editing, but the GIMP is pretty powerful.
5. I once ran my compter for a month without shutting down/logging off.  Why did I finally do it? I lost power.

Also gaim supports most major IM programs, and I even prefer it on windows (no ads).  There is very little if any viruses/spyware/adware in the linux universe.  As almost all software is free (free speech sense) and open-source, it's really hard to sneak that stuff in.  

One final note, no operating system or hard disk drive is 100% reliable.  BACK UP YOUR DATA!!!!

Hope this helps.
