### Post by mac.ryan on 2007-03-10
Unless you have a very particular system, then you have to burn the CD of ubuntu, and boot your computer from the CD. The installer is very user friendly and you will simply have to answer a few questions to get your system going.

If you are a windows user, then the steps are:
[LIST=1]
[*]Brun the CD image on a blank CD (please make sure you don't simply drag and drop from windows explorer, but you really burn the image (if you don't know how to do that: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto))
[*]Reboot your machine leaving the CD in the CD bay.[/LIST]If nothing happens after point 2 (i.e. you get windows again) you have probably to change your bios settings in order to tell the computer booting from the CD. This can be done VERY easily: usually you can enter bios, pressing "del" or "F2" or "F8" or "..." in the very first moments after you switch on your computer.

You might not need to do that, however.
