### Post by mcduck on 2007-06-12
> **EdThaSlayer said:**
> Why don't you try shutting it down, letting it cool, and then starting it up and measuring the temperature after 10 minutes of surfing the web? Then you should be able to find the temperature it is supposed to be at. Also, didn't you get a manual or something with your laptop? They should tell you the average temp. it should have.

Actually the way would be to stress the CPU to get max heat, and after that let it idle until the temperature doesn't drop any more.

Anyway, the max temp depends on the CPU model. Some run around 40°C, others closer to 60°C. Typically max temp is somewhere between 60°C and 100°C.

Also with laptops, the manufacturers need to make compromises between keeping the CPU really cool and keeping the laptop silent. Often the fan doesn't really kick in until the CPU is very hot.

On my laptop, with Core Duo CPU, normal idle temp is bit over 60°C, fan starts working around 65°C and the max temp I get (after 3 days of 3D rendering, running constantly at 100% CPU use, was 74°C.

In the end there's really no reason to worry about your CPU temps. All modern CPU's and motherboards have systems for either cutting the power or throttling the speed if the CPU gets too hot. You'd surely notice if that happened. :)
