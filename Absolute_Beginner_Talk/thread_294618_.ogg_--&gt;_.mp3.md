### Post by JayTee on 2006-11-07
I use Sound Converter for Gnome. It's in the Multiverse repositories. It works on an entire folder of ogg files at a time if you want it too. I use Sound Juicer to rip songs from CDs and there are preference settings for ripping so you can do lossless to FLAC or WAV and then use Sound Converter to convert those to MP3. I rip at 256kb instead of 128kb for better quality (but larger files). As for the OGG to MP3 being doubly lossy, I've ripped to OGG (lossy) and then converted to MP3 using Sound Converter  and I can't hear any difference between the OGG version or the MP3 version of the same song.

### Post by woedend on 2006-11-07
in many cases, at higher bitrates and on shoddy speakers(or even car speakers in most cases), you won't be able to hear it.  The best test is with good set of headphones or a nice surround system.  The difference between ogg and WAV is obvious enough if listening for it, much less the difference between the mp3ified ogg vs original wav.
but if you have the originals, why double encode.  its doubly stupid(twice the time twice the loss).
