### Post by HermanAB on 2007-09-16
Linux plays nice in groups and if Windoze is already installed, then it will make your machine dual boot with no sweat.  Just let the installer guide you.  I've never had trouble with this.

However, there is nothing wrong with a little paranoia, especially if they really are out to get you.  If you have mission critical data on your machine, then by all means, remove the present disk drive and put it next to you on the desk while installing Linux for the first time on the other disk.  That way, you know that nothing can possibly happen to it - just don't drop it on the floor...

After that, you can re-install the disk drive and switch using the BIOS as you suggested.  I have to admit that the first time I installed Linux (about 10 years ago when things were really very flaky) that is exactly what I did!

Cheers,

H.
