### Post by Aranel on 2006-11-13
> **gnama said:**
> No, I don't know how though. Help would be appreciated

When your computer boots up, before anything else happens, you should briefly see either some text or company's logo (depends on what kind of computer you have). While this is happening, it *should* say what key to press to access the BIOS Setup - again, it varies from computer to computer, although common ones are F1, F2, and Del. It may be a good idea to hold down the key at startup, just to make sure you can press it in time.

Once you're there, you should see "Boot Order" or something to that effect somewhere on the menu. When you nagivate to that option, there should be a list of devices. Normally, you can move one up or down with a keystroke (again, it depends), but your main objective is to make sure the CD drive is listed before the hard drive.

Once you've done that, save and exit, and it should boot from the CD. Sorry I can't give you more detailed instructions than that; it really varies between the manufacturer and model of your BIOS.
