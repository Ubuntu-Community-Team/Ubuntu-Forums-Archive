### Post by Jimmey on 2006-01-03
It's a desktop manager - Like Gnome, which is the default that Ubuntu is shipped with. It basically organises your desktop...With menus and themes. Gnome has, by default, the menus
'Applications' , 'Places', and 'System' 
At the top of the screen. KDE, the desktop environment that Kubuntu has, has a more rectangular menu going on, down at the bottom, and Fluxbox, the most lightweight, has no menu at all - To get one, you right click.

Fluxbox is probably the best for running on older PCs.

Hope this helps.
