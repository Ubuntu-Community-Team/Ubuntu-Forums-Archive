### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-02-12
**Reminders for Absolute Beginners**

Welcome! We welcome you with open arms and open sources! But you might want to check out this list of reminders, it could make your stay much more enjoyable.

***** The Live CD will run slower. It just will, because you're running off of a CD instead of the much faster read/write speed of your hard drive.

***** If you're going to dual-boot (run more than one operating system, like Windows) please defrag first. This will almost eliminate any overwriting issues.

***** Try Google with your exact question/problem/device/error output. You might even put "site:ubuntuforums.org" before the search string.

***** Linux is different. For instance CD drives aren't their own drive, but are mounted onto the root file system. So things will look, act, and be different. But most people find that it's actually easier and more intuitive once they get into it.

***** Everyone has problems. Even mods and developers. So no need to say you're dumb or anything like that. It's just a computer problem. That's what happens.

***** Be patient with posts. There are a lot of people looking, so you will probably get an answer. At most bump every 24 hours, and no more than three days in a row. And, of course, the weekends and evenings may be a little lighter traffic. Remember, there's nothing like this forum in Windows and no one is paid to do this.

***** Check out the stickies to see if they answer your question or have some good suggestions. It might take a little bit, but they're there for a reason.  They're REALLY good information.

***** Everyone really appreciates proper spelling and grammar. Many people here don't speak English as a first language so it makes it easier if they don't have to decypher 1337 5|*34|<, IMtalk, or slang. It may take longer, but it also makes searching more effective.

***** It's nice to be nice. Whether it's true or not in science, you will catch more flies with honey in the forums than with vinegar. If you're respectful, than nine times out of ten people will be respectful to you. That's just how life works.

***** When you post a thread, try putting your problem in the title line instead of something like, "Linux noob just doesn't get it." More people will be willing to help if they can tell at a glance if they have the required knowledge of the subject.

***** Get ready to learn. Although the motto is "Linux for humans" that doesn't mean you don't have to learn anything. That's part of being human!

***** The terminal may be scary at first, but it is so so powerful. That's why many post fixes by using the terminal; it's just faster and easier. That said, don't just blindly trust everything posted for the terminal. Investigate things you aren't familiar with. [www.linuxcommand.org](www.linuxcommand.org) is a great resource.

***** For almost every major WIndows or Mac application there is a Linux equivalent. And many are better than the ones you're familiar with. So give 'em a try: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Wi...onsEquivalents](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Wi...onsEquivalents)

***** It's usually very useful to post the specifications of your computer. Processor, RAM, video card, hard drive space. All those go into making the decision about what will be the best fix for you.

That being said, welcome to Ubuntu!
