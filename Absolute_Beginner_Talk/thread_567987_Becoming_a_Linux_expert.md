### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-10-05
When your bean count gets to over 2K, then you will be an expert.

Seriously, there are Linux/Unix fundamentals to learn; things that are common to most distros.  Then there is distro-specific knowledge; the tweaks that are needed to get things to work.

One effective way to learn is to take an old machine, with a large hard disk and partition it to accept 4 different distros.  As you load each one and use it you will quickly discover the similarities and differences between the distros.  The similarities reinforce Linux fundamentals, the differences expand  your knowledge of desktop environments and tweaks needed to get hardware to work under different distros.

To become profficient in one distro (say Dapper), stick with it for a while and help others on the forums.  Solving other people's problems will quickly get you up to speed.
