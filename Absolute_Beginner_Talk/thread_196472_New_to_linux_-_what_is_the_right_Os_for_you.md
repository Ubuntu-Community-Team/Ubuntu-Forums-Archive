### Post by newbymick on 2006-06-14
As the handle suggests I am new to linux after 20 years of using the Mighty BG OS - windows 3.1, 95 (missed out the rip off 98) and XP.  I have been using Linux for about 5 weeks now and won't be going back to MS on my home desktop.  

I would like to post some advice for those who like me, are about to or have moved over to linux.  This is based upon my experiances with Linux

1. Make sure that your hardware is compatible with the Linux distro that you choose to use - this is the be all and end all of a succesful life with Linux.  Try out a live CD before you do a full install.  

2. Perserver with your choice of distro.   

3. Get involved and become active with all the Linux forums - there is so much help out there.

4. Don't become a Linux zealot - just because you have found something that works for you there will 100 others that will think and say that what you are saying is total BS.  And they are afraid to tell you so.

5. Remember that Linux is not a "slam in the CD, click and it works".  There is the command line terminal that needs to be used.  Again, don't fear the command line - that's half the fun.  

6. Learn the real difference between MS OS and Linux OS and why MS has so many viruses and why Linux doesn't.  Viruses for linux are out there but there is a real reason why gets so few of them.  Seek and you shall find.

7. Linux is a learning experiance and if you are prepared to put in the work it will reward you.  Nothing tastes better than the pie you made yourself.
