### Post by zisme on 2006-08-03
You probably have to change your BIOS settings.
Step 1: While your computer is on, insert the CD into the CD-ROM drive.
Step 2: Restart (I'm guessing here) Windows.
Step 3: Just after it starts loading (not Windows, like just after everything starts up again) check to see if it says "Press F11 For setup" or something of the sort. If it doesn't, restart and press and run your finger down the keyboard from the ESC key to the F12 key. You should get a BIOS settings screen. 
Step 4: Somewhere in there, you should see "Primary Load Device" or something of that sort. Change it to CD-ROM.
Step 5: Save the changes and reboot (should say how to do that on screen)
It SHOULD boot onto the Ubuntu Live CD. Please note you can mess stuff up in the BIOS settings, and if you have NO clue what I said, don't try it.
