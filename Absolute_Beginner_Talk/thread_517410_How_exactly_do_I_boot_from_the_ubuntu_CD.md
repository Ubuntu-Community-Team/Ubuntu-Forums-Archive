### Post by aysiu on 2007-08-04
You have to burn it as a disk image, not as data. You also have to make sure your BIOS is set to boot.

Read more about both of these details here:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso)

And, no, the Desktop CD won't harm your system unless you click the *Install* icon on the desktop.

### Post by Cobbs on 2007-08-04
If you change your boot order to boot from the CD (go into setup when your system is starting) then you will be able to use Ubuntu as if it was your OS.  Just make sure you don't run the "INSTALL" icon that will be on your desktop.  To revert to your old OS, just reboot and remove the CD.

You should be pressing F8 or F12 depending on your OS as the system starts to get into the BIOS
