### Post by az on 2006-05-29
[QUOTE=smile-its-smiley]seeing that ubuntu is free and everything should be free
[/QUOTE]

Everything?  No.

Software freedom refers to the software not being someone's property.  Free-libre software is everyone's property.  You have the rights to use it, obtain and study the source code, modify that code and redistribute the changes.  That way, the software can never be taken away from you, nor is your computer a "black box" doing untold things behind your back.

But that does not mean that people should not make money out of the software industry.  The model for making money from free-libre software is through services.

You pay someone to write software.  You pay them to maintain your network.  You pay them for their time and effort, but the software that is used goes out to everyone.


 [QUOTE=smile-its-smiley]does this indicate that there will be free broadband?[/QUOTE]

There is no cost to download any stuff from the repositories.  That means it costs nothing to install and keep up-to-date.  You need to have internet access, though.  And that has nothing to do with the services that Ubuntu can provide to you.
