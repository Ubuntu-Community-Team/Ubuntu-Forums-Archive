### Post by Eyeball Tickler on 2006-12-27
Hi everyone, I've just joined the forum here. I've been using a Mac running OSX for the last four years - I love the OS and think it's great. Recently, I considered switching to Ubuntu. I really appreciated the philosophy of the software, and was (and still am) quite keen to ditch Apple, who I disliked for a number of reasons, including their poor environtmental record, allegations of the use of sweatshops and the fact that I wanted more flexibility and didn't want to be so tied to one company. 

However, after researching things for a little while, I decided to stick with OS X on my Powerbook. Considering I haven't directly paid Apple for my Powerbook or the software on it, I can perhaps say that I'm not 'supporting' them. Mainly though, I was turned off by a number of things I heard about Ubuntu.

Firstly, I should make it clear that, as a journalist, I use my computer or word processing, the internet, music, photos and videos. I have no inclination for tinkering around with things - I just want it to work with minimal need to configure things or set them up myself. This is one thing that turned me off Linux.

Secondly, I read about needing to find different codecs for playing different types of media files, and this seriously put me off.

I was also put off by the fact that certain files that I will be working with, such as Adobe and Quark, will require OS X, requiring me to use two different OSs and have different files in different desktops / OSs.

Also, for the type of computing I do iLife is a great package and I'm seriously concerned I won't be able to get the same level of usability and integration with Linux software.

Now, the reason I'm mentioning this hear is because, if I though Ubuntu was a viable option, I would happily switch in a second. So I'm hoping to hear some honest and straightforward advice here if any of you folks have the time. I'd really appreciate it.

Are my feas and concerns valid?
Will I be able to just get on with what I need to do without too much tinkering?

Any advice or help would be hugely appreciated.
