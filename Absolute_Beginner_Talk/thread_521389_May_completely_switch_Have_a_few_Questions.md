### Post by Inxsible on 2007-08-09
2) RhythmBox : It comes installed with Ubuntu and syncs with iPods without issues. Its interface is also inspired from iTunes. Other popular music players are Amarok(KDE default), Listen, Banshee, Exaile, Songbird

4) RhythmBox also supports PodCasts. Amarok, i know supports PodCasts. I am not sure about the others.

6) If your computer supports bluetooth, you can use that to transfer ringtones, which is what I do. Unfortunately, most cell phone manufacturers do not make Linux based software. My Sony W810i however mounts like a hard drive when I connect it. So I can simply drag and drop the ringtones/photos/mp3s.

7) [GPSDrive]("http://gpsdrive.de/"): I am not sure if it allows data transfer, but you can surely connect a NMEA compatible receiver and use it as a GPS. Explore it. Maybe you will find what you need.
