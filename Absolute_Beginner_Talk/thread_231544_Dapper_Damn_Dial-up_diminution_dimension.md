[I][COLOR="Blue"]I had the same problem.  The important thing is that now you know for certain that the modem is on ttyS4.  In "Newwork Settings", do not worry about "autodetect".  Under the modem ports, first choose ttyS1, then change the 1 to a 4, and click "OK".  

Open a terminal.  Type:  sudo pppconfig  and choose "Change a Connection".
Manually edit whatever port is shown to be ttyS4.

Now type:  sudo pon

The modem should dial and connect.  

Also, you can right click in the upper desktop panel field and then left click on:  "Add to Panel".  Under "System and Hardware", select the applet icon called "Modem Monitor", a red telephone, and then click "Add".  Once the icon is in the panel, right click and then choose "Activate", which should cause your modem to dial and hopefully connect.

Addendum:  You must also manually edit your  wvdial.conf   file to reflect 
phone number to dial, username, and password.  Do this by typing:

gedit /etc/wvdial.conf

in a terminal window, and then manually add the required information between the < and > brackets.  Also make sure the modem is shown on ttyS4.
Good Luck[/COLOR][/I]
