### Post by mcduck on 2007-12-11
I recommend just making a backup of your files and then doing a fresh install of 7.10. To do the upgrade you'd need to first upgrade to 7.04 before you can upgrade into 7.10, and that would probably take more time&work than the fresh install would.

But if you wish to do the upgrade anyway, the command would be 'sudo update-manager -d'. Make sure you have the 'ubuntu-desktop'-package installed before doing this.

### Post by Anicka on 2007-12-11
To upgrade, you need to go one distribution at the time (6.06->6.10, 6.10->7.04, 7.04->7.10). This would be a long process and a lot of things can go wrong. (I personally have upgraded from 6.06 to 7.10 during the last couple of years without any major problems, but doing it all on the same day might be stressful...) However, before the support of 6.06 is terminated there will be an other "long term support" distro (8.10 I think) with supported upgrade directly from 6.06. You can read some more here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UpgradeNotes) [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GutsyUpgrades](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/GutsyUpgrades)
