### Post by dbott67 on 2006-11-01
There are a couple of anti-virus packages available --- ClamAV and Grisoft's AVG ([http://free.grisoft.com](http://free.grisoft.com)).  As the popularity of Linux grows, there may be a concerted effort to target Linux, but overall you're relatively safe.  Keep in mind that security is not just the OS; it's services and applications and an unpatched system  running unneeded services and weak passwords can be hacked very quickly.

If you like to surf on the "wild side" (pick your own definition --- warez, XXX, microsoft.com), then AV protection is recommended.

As for the NV drivers, I would use the Automatix NV drivers if you want best performance.

Just my 2 cents (Canadian).
