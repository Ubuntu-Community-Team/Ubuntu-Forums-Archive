The best known is Azureus as this is also available on Windows. Deluge (GNOME) and Ktorrent (KDE) are popular too. Transmission is a good lightweight Torrent client.

### Post by kpkeerthi on 2008-02-01
rtorrent, deluge, azureus are all good. Which one to use depends on your needs. 

I have a 2 GB/month limit for downloads during normal hours and unlimited for anything I download between 2 am and 8 am. Given that, my downloads (regular http/ftp and torrents) are scheduled to run **without manual intervention**. So there is no need for me for a fancier torrent client or download manager. 

I have setup crons to start rtorrent at 2:05am and stop at 7:55am. I just download and drop the .torrent files to a folder and rtorrent picks it up automatically when it starts. It also supports DHT & encryption. It is so slim that you hardly notice it running even at peak speed. Deluge & azureus do use significant cpu & memory at max speed.
