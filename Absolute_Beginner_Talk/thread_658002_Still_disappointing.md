### Post by eolson on 2008-01-04
I guess it all depends on what your expectations are.   I really haven't noticed much difference on my two desktops .... one put together from spare parts and a Dell 530.  They both work fine.   I have noticed a little improvement in driver support for my laptop,  a Wal-Mart specail eMachines (hey you need a laptop at 3 in the morning, you take what you can get).  Wireless is weak on it, but I got a Belkin Wireless G USB for it and works fine with that.  Audio is a sometimes thing.  Sometiems it recognizes the card on boot up, other times it doesn't.   Hoping Hardy will help some more.  Laptops are a problem,  got to get the manufacturers on board.

I mean I thought  Redhat 7 was bloated, then  8 came out.   DOS,  with every release, became more bloated, the same with CPM.   Look at Windows.  I think the only solution an individual has is just delete all the stuff that you don't want.
