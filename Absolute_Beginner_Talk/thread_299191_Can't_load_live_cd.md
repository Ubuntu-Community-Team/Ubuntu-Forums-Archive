### Post by d3v1ant_0n3 on 2006-11-13
It's recommended to have at least 256Mb of Ram for the live cd, otherwise you may have problems (which apparently you are having!)- To work, the live cd needs to load Ubuntu to RAM, since it doesn't write anything to the HDD.

You may be better off with the Alternate install cd (which only has the 'install' option, rather than the nifty live desktop).

Judging from your specs, you might be better with Xubuntu rather than Ubuntu also- It utilizes the XCFE desktop rather than GNOME. Without going into too much detail, it works better on lower spec machines.

Downloading a new iso on dial up may take a while, however....
