### Post by Hopworks on 2007-10-30
I too am real curious as to the cause of your issue. I'm not qualified to offer any advice, except, if that happened to me, I'd hook up another monitor and see if the problem is duplicated.

And of course, check all your cable connections to make sure something didn't wiggle out a little, maybe because a cat got curious or something. That actually happened to me.

I suggest this because I had a weird issue with drives on my raid controller vanishing, and weird hangs after I installed a lot of stuff. I suspected the stuff I installed (I know you didn't install anything, just saying), and researched it for a couple of days. The problem turned out to be a failing PSU, and nothing with the OS at all.

Just food for thought.

Hop

EDIT: I looked at your screen shot and didn't see anything out of the ordinary. Maybe I'm blind, but if the monitor is the cause, and not the video card or a driver issue, a screen shot won't catch what you are seeing. I would suggest grabbing a camera and taking a photo, and posting it. Is your monitor an LCD? A few more specifics on that might help people to diagnose your issue. ;)
