### Post by daveshields on 2007-09-01
Most modern machines are so fast that it's not worth the bother with overclocking. It's of most interest to "gamers," but they are all Windows users as that's where the gaming action is.

Also, Ubuntu is much more efficient in using hardware resources. Windows may profit from overclocking since there is so much cruft. But Ubuntu doesn't have that cruft.

If you want a new challenge, look into virtualization. That's the "next big thing" coming down the pike...
