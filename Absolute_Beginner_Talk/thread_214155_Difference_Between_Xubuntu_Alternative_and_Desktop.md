### Post by MetalMusicAddict on 2006-07-12
The "Alt" cd is a text based installer. Its how older versions of Ubuntu were done. The "Desktop" cd is a live cd as well as a graffic installer. I myself prefere the "Alt" cd as Im used to it and I have heard of people having problems with the "Desktop" installer.

### Post by lordlollo on 2006-07-12
Your're going to receive a Desktop/Live cd.

In order to install, you just have to insert the cd into your drive, reboot and double-click the icon that will be displayed on your desktop. You will be asked for some questions, and then voilà, your brand new system will be ok. The alternate cd is for you if you have particular issues (low memory system, raid devices and so on). 

Welcome to Ubuntu...
