### Post by therunnyman on 2006-04-11
Your CD-R drive has firmware as well, if you'd want to give that a whirl first.  In all my days flashing optical drives, I've only fried one, and that was clear back in 1990.  Flash upgrades for optical drives have come a long way since then.

Just out of curiosity, what are you using for CD-R software?  Remember: you're burning an image, not a data disc.  An image is a kind of utopian data layout on your hard disk, and must be burned as such.  So, find the option to "Burn image" in whatever you're using for burning software.

Maybe reverse the above two paragraphs, in terms of steps.  A low speed is good too - I see you did that - and BurnPROOF-like technologies (i.e., buffer-underrun prevention) is no darn good.  My suggestion to everyone: burn at 12x max, BurnPROOF disabled.

I may or may not be an authority on optical drive issues.

### Post by therunnyman on 2006-04-12
Still no dice?

Here's what's what, to the best of my recollection: in Nero, under "File" or, um, scour the "File," "Edit" and so on for the option "Burn Image..."  Clicking on that should bring up a dialog, asking where your image file is located (it's always "*.iso).  Tell Nero where it is, and burn.

*It's imperative to burn an image as an image.*  What happens otherwise is you'll burn a disc that has a different file layout altogether.  Think of it this way: the image is a stamp, placed directly on a piece of CD-R media, where plain old data gets rearranged by your software, collated, whatnot.  Image=stamp, Data=Charlie in marketing who can work the fax sometimes and has his own way of keeping records.

If you're nervous, use CD-RW media, as mentioned above.  Simply erase it and try again if something goes wrong.  The other benefit of CD-RW is that it forces you to burn slowly.
