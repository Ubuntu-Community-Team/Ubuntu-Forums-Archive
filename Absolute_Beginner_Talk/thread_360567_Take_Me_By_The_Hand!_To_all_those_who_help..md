### Post by lhtown on 2007-02-13
I'm glad you stuck with Ubuntu. You are right on with your constructive critism. After all, this is the Ubuntu forum and not the Gentoo forum. We do want to keep it newbie friendly.

However, as someone who has tried to help some newbies, you have to understand the other side. First of all, it takes several times as long to give really detailed answers. Instead of 5 minutes, it might take 15 minutes. Normally, I try to give some detail and trust that the person will either post again or search for what I left out.

Also, in most cases, there is no way of knowing just how clueless a user is unless they give us some indication. There is no need to bore more experienced users.

In other cases, I wonder if a lot of newbies ever check back on the forum after they ask a question. In a LOT of cases, there is no follow up by the original poster to say if he/she is still having difficulties, has given up or resolved the problem. Personally, I consider that to be very rude. Why should we waste a lot of time trying to help a moron who is never going to even check back. Usually, it seems to me that if they post back once, they will see the issue through which in turn can encourage people to try to help them.

### Post by coolen on 2007-02-13
The thing with new Ubuntu users is that a lot of them are actually very good with computers. They may have years of experience with Windows, but they find that things just work differently in Linux...they may know what the different X server options do, but have no idea where to impliment them.
Even if they're not experts, they may still be proficient. It's generally assumed that a poster knows how to work a terminal, for example. Going through the level of detail you suggest with them, the user may come accross as insulting, which is definitely not the impression you want to give anyone, especially if they're new to Linux. The whole point of Ubuntu is to shed the idea that Linux is newb-exclusive.
If you really just have no clue where to find a terminal or work it, mention it in your post, or ask back. We've all likely been there at some point, and it's only the very occassional user who'll judge.
