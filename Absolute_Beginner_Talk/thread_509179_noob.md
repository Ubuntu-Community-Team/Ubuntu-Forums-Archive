Oh and pistyll, you will need to keep an open mind while learning to use Linux, things are done differently, some better some worse, some things are done for reasons which may become clearer to you later.

Be prepared to learn and fail, but have another go and ask questions.  No Question is too stupid to ask, and you won't be made to feel like an idiot for asking it on these forums (well, you shouldn't be!).

Don't be surprised when you find that your flashy late model printer/scanner/webcam etc' don't work under Ubuntu.  It's not the fault of Ubuntu, but of your hardware manufacturers as they won't release drivers or information for people to write drivers, and sometimes the easiest thing is to simply sell your unsupported hardware and buy supported stuff!

Linux is NOT a direct replacement for Microsoft products, and doesn't pretend to be.  Linux is Linux :).

Enjoy :)
