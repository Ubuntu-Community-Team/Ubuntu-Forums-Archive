### Post by Herman on 2005-10-22
Hello Jason,
               You use GIMP image editor to make animations with. 
The quickest and easiest example if you have never tried GIMP before, is to click on a bitmap that's already made. It doesn't have to be a bitmap, any kind of image file will do.  
1.Right-click on it and click 'open with GIMP image editor'.
2. You'll see a panel called 'channels, layers and paths', in the middle of it there should be a window with a small icon of your bitmap in  a brown (highlighted) rectangle. Look for a six buttons under that window, hoover your mouse pointer over them and find the one that says 'duplicate layer', and click that one.
3. Change something about the image. For example, I used a picture of Tux, and simply closed one eye with a black brush, to make him 'wink' at us.
4.Click 'File' (This time on the main picture itself), (a) click 'save as',(b) on the line titled 'name', change the file type from .bmp to .gif, (by deleting the letters 'bmp' and typing in 'gif' instead), (c) if you want, click the double arrows on the other end of the 'save in folder' box, and change it to 'desktop', (not important, but just so you will be able to find it easily). (d) click 'save'.
5. (a)On the 'export file' panel, click the radio button for 'save as Animation'.
    (b) Click 'export'.
6. On 'Save as GIF' panel, scroll the amount of milliseconds delay between frames to something like say 2000 or so, then click 'OK'.
7. Close GIMP by closing the panel headed 'The GIMP', (that will automatically close the other GIMP panels too).
8. On your desktop (if you saved it there), find your new image, and right-click on it and open it with gThumb image viewer, (not just image viewer, the gThumb image viewer is third down, on the 'open with' menu, (second under 'GIMP').

You should see your new animation! In my case I have Tux winking at us. (I'm not sure if I know how to include my example image in this post, but I will try, and if we're lucky he'll come through.)

### Post by Herman on 2005-10-24
Second lesson:
If you want to be able to do a more complex one, try using more layers. Then click 'filters', then 'animation' and you can either try out the 'playback' function, to see how you are doing, and/or/also use the 'optimize for Gif' function. With that you can have access to a lot more settings, for example set different numbers of milliseconds pause for each frame, and all kinds of other tricks. You'll discover more the more you experiment! Have fun! (Herman):)
