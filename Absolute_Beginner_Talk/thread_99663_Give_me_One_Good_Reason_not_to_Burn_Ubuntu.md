### Post by cilynx on 2005-12-06
I hate to put it this way, but "Welcome to Linux".  With every piece of hardware designed to work in Windows, specific componants are harder to make work on Linux.  The likelyhood of finding a "How to make [some piece of hardware] work on [my flavor of linux]" isn't nearly as likely as finding "How [this family of hardware] works with linux in general".  It simply isn't feasable to document the process of getting every specific piece of hardware working on every specific flavor and update level of linux.

If nothing else, Linux will teach you patients and/or gritty determination.  You might even learn a bit about your hardware and how it works.  If this isn't a world that you're interested in, maybe you should look at alternative operating systems.

And by the way...if Ubuntu is the worst OS you've seen, you should really check out FreeDOS and/or Zenith DOS 0.8.  They are both much worse =).

[edit]I forgot the "One good reason":  Because the smoke given off by a burning CD platter is pretty nasty stuff.  If you're going to do it, at least make sure you're outside[/edit]
