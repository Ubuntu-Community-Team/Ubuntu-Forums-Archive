### Post by ninotob on 2007-05-25
> **raffytaffy said:**
> from new egg tottal price around 700

case - IN WIN IW-Z589T.J350BFU2AD Black Best Performed SECC Japanese Steel metal ATX Mini Tower Computer Case 350W Power Supply - Retail  54.99 ....

I wouldn't skimp on the case.  Think about it -- your case is likely to be the longest lasting component you buy and can easily live through several versions of innards.  By the same token, it isn't necessary to go spend $300 on a case either.

I've been using Antec cases for a while.  They're typically fairly reasonable, $100 - $120, and they have some great features.  The best of all (depending on model of course):  side HD slots.  When I want to add or change the HD in my cases, all I do is remove the side panel, the HDs are oriented so the connectors are facing the side panel, and the rails slide right out.  I don't have futz around with getting the front panel off, breaking one of the six little tabs that holds it on, poping out metal or plastic spacers in the face plate.  Nothing -- just open the side and put them in/take them out/rearrange them from the side.

And if you don't think you'll ever mess with the HDs again after installation -- I find that isn't true.  For example, the brain dead safe way to instal a new OS is to disconnect your current live drive, and use a new or unimportant drive for the install, then reconnect the old one, mount it up, and txfr data.  Disconnecting or changing the drive order is a snap when the cords are exposed to the open air of the side panel rather than to the spaghetti innards of the computer you are subjected to when the drives aren't sideways oriented.

Anyway, my 2 cents, double the case investment and you'll be glad you did.
