### Post by paradoc on 2008-01-12
First things first, and although this is simple to do, it will give you some insight into how things work:

Go to Applications>>Add/remove>>Sound & Video and scroll down to VLC media player. Highlight it, check it, view its properties to see if that's what you require, and install it. (click OK, click 'Apply' on next screen, put in your password, and watch it all happen!)

Without rebooting you will now find the VLC media player in Applications>> Sound&Video.

Experiment a little, and do the same for as many apps you fancy, e.g.Movie Player Totem (xine backend) is particularly good.

To d/l Flash player is a little more complicated, but similar to what you've just done...In the Add/Remove applications dialogue, Highlight 'All', tick Ubuntu restricted extras, and follow the prompts again.

When all is safely installed, you will be able to play your youTube videos...Good Ubunting!
