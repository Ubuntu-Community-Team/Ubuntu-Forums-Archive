### Post by fastpakr on 2007-05-18
For what (little) it's worth, I'm also very much a new Linux user.  I installed the 64 bit version of Feisty on my pc at home - at the time I hadn't read everything and just assumed that I had to since I had a 64 bit processor.  Although that was obviously a misunderstanding on my part, I've had no reason to consider switching.  I've got Flash and the JRE working, every other app I've put time into installing is now going.  I still need to fiddle with getting Lotus Notes and Citrix running, but there are threads here where people have figured them out, so it's definitely possible.

Don't throw in the towel on 64 bit before you've given it a try - it's not nearly as bad as this thread has made it sound so far.
