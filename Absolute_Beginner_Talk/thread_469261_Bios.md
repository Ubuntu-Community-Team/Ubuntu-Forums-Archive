### Post by Jimmyfj on 2007-06-09
Unless you have a MSI motherboard and a Windows XP partition, or similar I wouldn't attempt to update the BIOS myself. MSI, and a few other board-producers has a nice BIOS-update-tool that does the update via the Internet. If you have not done a BIOS update before turn to someone that has. You CAN crash your BIOS beyond recovery resulting in you having to purches a new motherboard for your computer. 

But, again - Have you NOT done a BIOS update before turn to someone who has. Why, btw, would you want to update your BIOS?

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-09
BIOS updates are a risky business.

If something goes wrong and your motherboard doesn't have a dual-bios setup, your motherboard is completely useless.

If this happens, it requires a factory fix to re-flash it.

I had a similar problem when updating the ROM on my Sharp Zaurus PDA.
The update froze half way through and the whole circuit board had to be replaced.

To cut to the chase, decide whether the upgrade is really required, as it can be risky.
