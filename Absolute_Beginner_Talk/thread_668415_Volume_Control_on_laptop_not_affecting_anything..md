### Post by lian1238 on 2008-01-15
Does it show the window with the volume going up/down at all? If not, I guess the system does not recognize the keys on your laptop.

But if so, then maybe it is affecting a different "track" from the one actually playing the sound. Try going to preferences->sound. Under the "Default Mixer Tracks" section, make sure you have selected the correct device and the channel you want to control with your laptop keys. (On my laptop, the internal speakers is Surround and headphones is Front. Edit: PCM controls both.)

If you don't know which channel is for you speakers..
To check the channels:
You have add a volume control on your panel if you haven't already. Double click that to open Volume Control. Edit->Prefrences. Tick all of them and try all of them. You'll eventually find the correct one. Now, choose that channel in your Default Mixer Tracks.
