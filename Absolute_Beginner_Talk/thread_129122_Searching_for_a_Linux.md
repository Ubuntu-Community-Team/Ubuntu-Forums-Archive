### Post by darkbullet87 on 2006-02-13
Well, speaking from the prespective (sp?) of being new to Linux as a former hardcore windows user as well, I can tell you this:

I'm not going to go into the pros and cons of linux vs. Windows, bceause you said you already read the sticky threads, so you have proabaly read that suff already. So let me just say that Linux is different. You have to be willing to put the time and effort into learning the system and how to do things. When I first installed Fedora 4, about a month ago, it took me 2 days just to get a stable internet connection. Now, I personally feel Ubuntu is easier to learn that Fedora. Perhaps it's because I'm comming into it with a lil bit of previous knowedge from FC4, but I'm learning Ubuntu quite easily. It's not impossible, you just have to have the time and patience to sit in front of a terminal for a good half an hour trying to figure out how to do something. But, with anything, the more you learn the faster you learn (if that makes sence). 

And as far as the command line goes. If you despise the command line with a fiery passion and refuse to use it, you will have a very hard time with not only Ubuntu but Linux in general. I've found the command line invaluable to installing and removing packages, updating files, managing directories, etc. There are ways to do many of these things without the terminal, but it is much harder and more time consuming than just learning the commands and typing them in. (specifically the apt-get command) 

Speaking of apt-get, another thing to take into consideration is the instilation of software. You don't get nice .exe programs in Linux. You have to use a program like apt-get, or Automatix to get the programs you want, and download them from online repositories. This can be time consuming, especially when your learning. 

So in short. I guess what i'm trying to say is that you really have to try it out for yourself. If you don't like it and find it to tme consuming and hastling, Linux isn't for you, and I would recomend you go back to Windows. On the other hand, if you can get through the learning process, I'm sure you'll find it to be a wonderful option other than Windows. Heck, you may even be like me and find yourself completely replacing Windows with Linux. In either case...good luck and I hope this was helpful!
