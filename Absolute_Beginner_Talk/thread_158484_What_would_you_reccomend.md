### Post by niviche on 2006-04-11
You could start with the regular (gnome) Ubuntu, and see from then on if you like it, and if it fits your needs. Both Gnome and KDE are nice, it's a matter of taste and use.

Moreover, you can also use Gnome applications in KDE, and vice-versa
I like both versions, started with Gnome, and switched to KDE because I found myself using more and more KDE applications.

PS:I however don't agree with the "KDE is closer to Windows" comment. Besides the blue-ish theme, I didn't find KDE any closer to Windows, and it appears to be a lot easier to Mac-ify a KDE desktop than a Gnome one (if that what's you're after).
