### Post by mcduck on 2008-02-06
GTK2-themes change the way hoe your programs look, changing buttons, colors and so on. Some themes may require you to install some gtk2-engine package, for example all Murrina-themes require you to have gtk2-engines-murrina installed for themes to work correctly.

Metacity themes are for Gnome's default window manager and change your window frames and title bars.

Compiz/Beryl themes (they are actually the same thing as both projects merged back together into Compiz Fusion) are for Emerald, window manager used by Compiz. Use these instead of Metacity themes if you are using desktop effects (and have Emerald installed).

Icon themes are exactly what the name suggests, they change your icons.

GDM themes are for your login screen.

You can also theme your panels, just drag&drop any image into your panel to use it as panel background image.

In addition there are different types of splash screens, Grub splash is for the Grub menu, usplash themes are for Ubuntu's loading screen, and Gnome splash images can be used after you have logged in while your desktop is loading (although they are disabled by default in Gutsy).

For desktop widgets I recommend Screenlets (if you are using desktop effects) or gDesklets.
