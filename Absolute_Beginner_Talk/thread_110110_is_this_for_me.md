### Post by Rxke on 2005-12-30
Hi, Shawn,

viruses and spyware are all but nonexistant on *nix stuff. Most people don't run a virusscanner, and they do fine :D
You'll get a mediaplayer, but because Ubuntu is 100% free, (as in free beer,) meaning you don't have to pay for it, and free (as in free speech) it does not come as standard with some widely used codecs (COder Decoders) for some mediafiles, especially music and video, because these are licenced. The good news is you can still use those, it's just impossible to put them on the disk (a lot of legalese stuff) 
There are some scripts in the forums thay you can download and simply clickety-click install all these things for you. You could see it as a kind of upgrade.
It is fairly simple to navigate basic *and* specialist stuff, but you probably come from a Windows environment, it may take a while to get used to, but that's so with everything new. If you buy a new TV it takes a while too to figure out how it works etc...
Games... There's a forum dedicated to games. Short answer: some games work, some not, but don't expect to find everything you're used to on a Windowsmachine. There are 'virtual' windowsemulators that can run games, on top of Linux, but then again... Head over to the section on the board to get an idea.

So: music, chat, surfing, storeing pics: absolutely. Games: I don't know (but I don't know much about gaming to begin with.)

Just visit here often, read some stuff, ask questions, and if you learn something new, share it, that's the way this whole free stuff works: learning from eachother, and in return help out others etc.
