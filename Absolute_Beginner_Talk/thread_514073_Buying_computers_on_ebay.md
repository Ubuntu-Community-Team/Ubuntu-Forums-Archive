### Post by thefoolisme on 2007-07-31
I've had good experiences buying parts and old computers on eBay, but I have noticed that a lot of PC stuff on there now is way overpriced, not the individuals per say, but the vendors.  They seem to be going after the crowd who assumes that "if it's on eBay, it must be a deal".  My point is, know your purchase - if you buy from someone on eBay, buy from a reputable person, and compare prices before hitting the commit to buy button.  You will probably find a better PC deal elsewhere.

Of course, if you already have a PC, then you could use the $400 to buy new components, and mix the new with the old.  That would be feasible too.

### Post by Gobbie on 2007-07-31
Ebay can be a great place to buy a pc.....my new one just arrived today and it's great.  It's important to remember that ebay isn't the only place to buy a pc!  Like other have said, check out the seller well.....a good seller will be able to answer your specific questions.  Obviously, check the conditions, shipping etc.

i'm in the UK and my pc was less than $400 equivalent.  The only place any $400 pc is likely to let you down is with your need to run the latest games.  That's where high spec pc's come in.

What about building your own?  Don't go for top spec on any of the components i.e. latest processor, biggest fastest hard drive, latest graphics card etc.  Take a small step down in these areas and you'll save a lot while still getting a very fast pc.  Still not sure if $400 will do it though!  Good luck.
