### Post by OffAxis on 2008-02-20
> there shouldn't be a need to buy more RAM as a P2 motherboard probarly wont support much more than 256mib which is in it.
A P2 board should be able to handle a fair amount of memory; it probably tops off at 1 or 2 GB. 

Not that I'm advocating the use of a P2 system; that whole thing should just be tossed or shelved (for later use as a router or something). It won't be worth your time to use it nor worth your money to retrofit.

But, if you don't have money for a new system, and you don't know what your system currently is, crack open the case and look or download cpu-z and find out.
[http://www.cpuid.com/cpuz.php]("http://www.cpuid.com/cpuz.php")

PCxxx SDRAM is backwards compatible, so you could stick some PC166 in there and have them function even if it's an antiquated P2 system.
