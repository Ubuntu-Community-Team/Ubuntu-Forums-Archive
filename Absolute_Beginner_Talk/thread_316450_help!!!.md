First of all:  Welcome!  You chose a great distro to start (and stick) with!

The procedure for installing stuff varies depending on where and what format you got them in.  The easiest way for you to start is the "Applications" button on your task bar.  If you'll notice, at the very bottom there is an item that says "Add/Remove...".  Click that and your're on your way to the easiest way to install stuff.  However, may or may not install the program of your choice (one you may have seen or read about).

There is also Synaptic.  It can be started from the "System/Adminstration" menu in the taskbar.  I *HOPE* you remember your root password... you'll need it to install ANYTHING... no matter what method you choose.

These are the two easiest ways.  There is also installation through the console, as you noted.  My advice is to see how you fare with these two methods first.  You're very new and we don't want to be taking too big of a bite right now.  

I'm sure others will be along to offer their advice.

### Post by RRS on 2006-12-10
Whenever prompted for a password use the one you created with your username during the installation. It's not actually a "Root" password but rather a "sudo" (superuser), same principle for our purposes though.

To access the command line environment select "Terminal" from the "Accessories" section of the "Applications" dropdown menue. IE; Accessories>Applications>Terminal
[Here's]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BasicCommands") the Wiki page on using the terminal with some basic commands.

[This]("http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/") is a good guide to installing aditional programs(applications) using the most common methods. One of the great things about Ubuntu is the ability to download and install multiple applications with only one or two steps and without having to reboot.

[Aysiu's site]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php") is full of good info and guides on these and many other subjects.

Hope this gives you enough to get you started and if you have further questions or problems don't hesitate to ask.

Welcome aboard :)
