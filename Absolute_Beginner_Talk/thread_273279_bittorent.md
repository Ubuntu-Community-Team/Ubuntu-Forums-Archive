### Post by jaboua on 2006-10-07
I used to hate bittorrent (ftp ftw ;)), but now I love it...

A couple of tips: in order to be "connectable", you either need to enable UPnP and make sure it's working, or configure portforwarding. If you're not connectable, only you can initiate the connections to the connectable -> less connections. If you're connectable, you can communicate with everyone.

Also limit the upload speed - if not, the uploading will hose the outgoing packets dealing with the download... and you won't be able to download as fast as these outgoing packets are delayed. But don't overdo it as bittorrent clients seem to prioritize to upload to those they download from.
