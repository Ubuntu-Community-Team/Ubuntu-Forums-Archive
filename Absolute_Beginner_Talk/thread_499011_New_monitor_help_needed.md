### Post by DSn0wMan on 2007-07-12
In my experience as long as the video card can handle it you are good to go. For instance I have a 19" widescreen Acer with a max resolution of 1440 X 990 or something like that. My graphics card is a NVIDIA FX5500 and with the NVIDIA binary driver I can only get 1280 X 800. With the vesa driver I can get the full 1440 X 990 but no 3D acelleration. 

I am guessing that with your graphics card you should be good, but it wouldn't hurt to look up the maximum resolution supportted by your driver/gpu combination.
