### Post by lila on 2008-01-23
Do you mean the window of whatever program you are running or everything, i.e. when you turn the computer on the picture is too small?

If it's the whole thing, you have to go into system - preferences - screen resolution and adjust to your screen.  If you don't know, experiment a bit, but not wildly, go one or two above or below what there is at the moment and look if it's going in the right direction.  You can also try the dials and buttons on you physical screen.

If it's a particular program, well the obvious thing (I hope I'm not insulting you here) is to click on the "maximise" icon, which is the little square between  the X and the _ at the top right hand corner of the window.  It will maximise as much as possible.  If you click on it again, it will make it smaller.  If you find it's too low, and the top bar of Ubuntu annoys you where it is (like it did me), you can right click on the bar somewhere where there is no icon, choose "properties" and choose to put it, for example, on the left hand side.  You loose a bit of width, but that tends to be less annoying than height.

Hope this helps.
Lila
