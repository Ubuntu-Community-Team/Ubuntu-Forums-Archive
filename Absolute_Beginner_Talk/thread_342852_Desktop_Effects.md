### Post by Shatrat on 2007-01-21
Despite all the posts on Beryl and Compiz in the Absolute beginner forum... I really don't think this is a topic for someone who just installed Ubuntu.
I'd recommend you get familiar with configuring your graphics drivers and settings at least, especially if you have a non-nvidia card because then you will need to run XGL as well and mistakes could leave you without a graphical environment so you will have to know how to undo changes to /etc/X11/xorg.conf

That said, beryl is making really fast progress, the version just jumped from 0.1.x to 0.2.x and it is getting more and more usable on my nvidia card at least.  Its a lot slower and uglier on my ATI based laptop.

### Post by jd65pl on 2007-02-02
Beryl **is not **for beginners you will more than likely end up in trouble if you try to run it, it is beta software and can prove fairly buggy. If beryl goes wrong for a beginner its is quite likely to look impossible for them to fix.

### Post by richaoj on 2007-04-22

however, in fiesty, compiz has been quite easy to get working.
