### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-11-08
If you are using the fglrx driver, as it says in the Xorg.conf; and you can get the desktop effects, then you do have 3D acceleration. It's just that XGL causes the "glxinfo" program to think that you don't have acceleration.

When you want to run a program that requires 3D acceleration (like Google Earth or a 3D game) you will need to log out of the desktop, click the Options button (bottom-left corner of the login screen) and click "Sessions...". Select the ordinary Gnome session and log in. Then Google Earth and your games will run perfectly, but not Compiz.
