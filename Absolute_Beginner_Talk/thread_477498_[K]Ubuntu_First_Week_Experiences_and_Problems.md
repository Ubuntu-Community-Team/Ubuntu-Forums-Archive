### Post by notbitmonk on 2007-06-18
I was reborn to Ubuntu (and Linux in general) on Friday, June 8, 2007.  I have to say that it has been good for me.  I have been a long time Windows user (over ten years) and as every addict can tell you, it's not easy breaking up.  I will provide the reasons for the transformation, the joys of a new beginning, the gripes of living a new life and falling in old sins...

**Reasons**

I was tired.  I wanted A New Hope.  I had started working with the innards of my PC a year ago.  I have never been a (PC) gamer before and when a Soundblaster Platinum Gamer card with five games was presented on the shelves of CompUSA at a very good price, I jumped in the wagon that took me in a downward spiral from which I have not been able to climb out.  From there, I changed the Graphics card (twice), the motherboard and the processor. I gave it a new body and the fountain of its power had a new heart.  I lost myself in the genesis of the Creature but I was pleased with myself and a new dawn shone before mine eyes.  The creature was made from pieces collected everywhere and now it needed a conscience, something that will guide it as it made progress in this world of collective minds.  I decided to go [Micro]soft on it and saw That It Was All Good and decided to rest.  I let my creation loose on a world that I soon realized was full of festering worms and that war presented itself in hollow, wooden envelopes that contained microorganisms that injected their virulent rRNA in their mindless need to reproduce, propagate and infest.  The Creature was vaccinated to prevent many illness, including some fatal ones, as it got older it wasn't as agile at handling my requests.  It seemed that the conscience was having trouble maintaining itself and several retirements were needed to keep it fresh an nimble.  Looking upon the vile world upon which I had loosened my Creature, I saw a flightless bird.  This bird lived in frigid conditions, it battled some of the fiercest predator of the World, and it survived.  My curiosity was picked and I decided to investigate.
--Basically, my computer was bloating and taking a long time to turn on and off.  The cost of software and its' maintenance was kind of high for the performance obtained.  Norton was really a resources hog and the main reason the computer was so slow to start up.  I have looked at building several computers from scratch (and with used parts) for basic needs like web surfing, document writing, spreadsheet creation and presentations.  Buying Microsoft software for these refurbished computers wasn't feasible and Linux [K/X/Ed]Ubuntu looked like a good alternative.  During the previous year I learned a lot of how (Microsoft) computers work and I had been designing Microsoft Access databases since 2001 so I wasn't afraid of using the Terminal to enter commands.  Another thing attached to cost was the use of computers in a business environment.  It looked like most business needs like production software (word processing, spreadsheets, presentations, e-mailing, diagramming), security, networking and others were addressed by Linux, not to mention product support.  For the cost of 10 USD I would obtain an OS and most of the capabilities businesses need in a computer that might have cost less than 500 USD.  Now that's what I call business sense.  Also, users new to computers would go through the same learning curve as if they were experimenting with MS Windows for the first time.--

**The Joys of a New Conscience**

The flightless bird was unassuming and young but it was also smarter than what appeared at first glance.  After observing and studying it for a while, I placed its' conscience in my Creature.  And the Creature was Whole.  It was a new joy, like observing the world clearly after getting glasses for the first time after ten years of seeing through a foggy mist.  Yes, it was good; the Creature and the Maker were smiling.
--After installing Ubuntu, navigating through it took almost no time to getting used to.  The interface is very clean, aesthetically pleasing and responds like the Windows environment.  Improvements were evident in the use of streamlined applications to handle several tasks at which they excel instead of trying to handle everything mediocrely.  Applications ran without trouble the first time around and the OS detected my hardware not to mention that boot times were greatly improved.--

**Gripes of a New Life**

The new Conscience moved the Creature to new heights but occasionally fumbled it.  At those times the Maker cringed observing how the Creature stumbled upon some rudimentary actions.  It appeared as if the parts from which the creature was assembled from were polluted from their exposure to the world and the Conscience, coming from such a pristine environment, was not able to handle them.
--I did several re-installations of Ubuntu over the week and settled for Kubuntu.  I liked it multimedia applications better than the ones provided with Ubuntu.  Load times for Kubuntu are longer than Ubuntu and are actually on par with my freshly re-installed Windows OS (sans Norton Antivirus -this alone made the boot time incredibly fast- since I don't have an internet connection anymore). Both [K]Ubuntu had problems detecting the correct sound configuration of my Soundblaster Audigy Gamer card.  Ubuntu's sound manager was easier to navigate and enable sound through all my speakers (although I wasn't able to set it to 5.1 sound, if that mattered at all) than the one from Kubuntu which I actually dislike.  Kubuntu's sound (or speaker) interface is cumbersome and actually identifying what I needed to do took some time.  However, by reading what was presented on screen I was able to find out, which is something that was not always possible to do in a Windows software.  Another thing is the Nvidia card drivers installation.  Right now I haven't been able to use the drivers following Nvidia instructions because the Xserver is running even when Nvidia says in the download page that it can be done.  And that takes me directly to next gripe which is internet connection.  I have a dial-up modem that haven't been able to try since a I don't have a dial-up account anymore.  However, the broadband connection works all right when I have access to broadband (which is not very often).  Most of the options offered in the system require internet access equivalent to broadband and I understand that the need is legitimate but at least the restricted drivers should come in the installation package to be available to the user when required by him/her (I know that mp3 and dvd-playback may not be legitimate in some countries but at least Nvidia is offering them with no problem).  Also, the detection of the drives that I have installed wasn't the best (in Kubuntu).  My main hard drive is SATA.  I have a 90 Gig partition for Windows and had separated 20 Gigs for [K]Ubuntu.  I installed Windows first and then installed Kubuntu.  Although the installation was complete, I was never able to boot the Kubuntu OS as I was not presented with the option of which OS to run.  I had previously installed Ubuntu in a 2.5 Gig partition of an ATA hard drive and everything was good.  At load time I was asked what OS to load and my data drive (separate from my OS drive) was recognized by Ubuntu and I was able to navigate through it.  After moving the OS's to the new SATA drive Kubuntu was not bootable from it and had to install it in a third ATA drive.  Kubuntu does run from the ATA drive (which it has for itself) but does not recognizes the DataDrive.  I moved the data and deleted the partitions from the DataDrive trying to get Kubuntu to recognize it to no avail.  I think that the problem has to do with the way the SATA and ATA drives are recognized by the motherboard).  Also, the location of user documents isn't very clear but that may just be the overexposure of a My Documents folder in the Windows environment.

**Falling in Old Sins**

Sensing that the Conscience was not able to handle the Creature, I decided to return it to where it came from.  The journey was a long one.  The Creature really liked the Conscience and the parting was not going to be easy.  My own feelings were divided.  i knew that the Conscience and the Creature were making great efforts to comprehend each other, to fuse together for their own betterment; and they were succeeding.  As we were nearing the Conscience's land, the Creature became restless.  I was the first one out.  I stood there feeling the cold against my skin.  I turned to watch the Creature disembark.  It did so, slowly.  Tears were frozen in its face, its skin was purple but its eyes were on fire.  It could not understand how I could have shown it such a blissful existence and now wanted to end it.  To end it for what?  Wasn't the process of learning a never ending one?  Wasn't knowledge, when used and given freely, the foundation of civilization in its purest, most benign and human form?  It was then that I, The Maker, received my most important lesson from the Creature.  There was no more fire in the Creature's eyes.  They were now the Conscience's eyes looking at me knowingly, dearly.  I moved aside as the Creature left me towards its new road.  And a road it was, the road that went back to my land, a road that was shorter that the one I would take, a road that would teach the Creature more than I have learned in a lifetime...
--[K/X/Ed]Ubuntu is a great environment and has a greater (I believe) ROI in business environments and casual/home users.  I will still use Windows for my gaming and certain applications (Geographic Information System (GIS), MS Access design and development) but I will take my time learning [K/X/Ed]Ubuntu.  I'm recommending it to some people as an alternate to MS and installing it to those that are new to computers.  [K/X/Ed]Ubuntu is not for everyone.  The use of a Terminal/Konsole is not for everyone.  Windows has made it real easy to just double click on a piece of software and install it.  [K/X/Ed]Ubuntu (Linux) still needs to work on it.  By not giving users administrative rights as a default, the environment is safer but it will only be so long before viruses, worms and malware are targeted to the OS.  Linux is still safer because the 85-90% of the population uses MS.  When those numbers begin to shift appreciably, viruses, worms and malware will begin targeting Linux and developers will be under fire to fix the problem.  Good thing about it, we can all help to prevent/fix it.  That's the beauty of it.

But if you edit your menu.lst file to include this text at the bottom, Your grub boot menu will include Winows as an option to load, hence you will not have to change the boot order any more.  system:/media/hdXX/boot/grub/menu.lst

title Windows
root (hd1,0)
map (hd0) (hd1)
map (hd1) (hd0)
makeactive
chainloader +1

OR

title Windows
root (hd2,0)
map (hd0) (hd2)
map (hd2) (hd0)
makeactive
chainloader +1
