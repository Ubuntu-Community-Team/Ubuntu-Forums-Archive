My current wireless setup is a wireless router that sends its signal to a desktop. It's a far way to go, relatively speaking. By the time the signal reaches the desktop it's weak. Is there a device that can receive the signal halfway and send it anew, so that when the signal is received by the desktop, it's not as weak as it would've been had it been received directly from the router.

### Post by chili555 on 2007-06-28
You want a repeater. I bought a Linksys WAP54G off Ebay because it has a repeater mode in its newest firmware. If you google up 'wireless repeater' I am sure you will find other solutions, some better, some worse.

I got quite a bit of information here: [http://www.dslreports.com/forums/18](http://www.dslreports.com/forums/18) You can search for 'repeater' there, too.

### Post by lintoon on 2007-06-28
You can also buy an external antenna for your desktop.  It all depends on your environment.  Eg, metal objects between the desktop and the wireless router, types of wall - plasterboard false walls/solid concrete walls.  Some routers have an option to extend the wireless range (Eg Netgear - Rangemax). Also, cordless phones and microwaves can interfere with your signal.
