### Post by geokok1981 on 2006-08-30
Basically, all distros are based on one common thing. That's the kernel, linux. From there on it really depends on each distro to give a certain flavor, a look and feel lets say. 
So there are others which are more like windows, there are others that "everything works out of the box" and so on.

However, even after installing and setting up your machine, you ll 
appreciate different tools for different tasks that each distro provides. Of course there are similaritites in some cases, but nothing is quite the same.

And of course there is the community behind each distro, that gives to it its chararcter :) .

### Post by edrodgers731 on 2006-08-30
If I have it right, Ubuntu comes from Debian, the first real corperate free distribution.  Debian, however is a bit too slow with updates.  The debian crowd seems to value stability rather highly.  For that reason, updates are few and far between.  Ubuntu is updated every 6 months, and backports updates(at least with version 6.06) for five years.  They slap on some spiffy easy going graphics and a comfortable installer.  They put together the official Ubuntu tested packages, and voila-  A less uptight version of Debian.  (Which, to be honest, is not really designed for the masses.)  Ubuntu works well, is comfortable, and has a nice big group of easy-going types to help out if you have problems.
