### Post by steve.horsley on 2006-11-06
Dual boot is where you get given a choice of two operating systems to run when you turn the computer on. You choose one OS, and the computer goes on to load and run that OS. The other OS is completely ignored.

In these forums, we usually mean a choice between Ubuntu and Windows, but dual boot could mean a choice between any two OSs. Right now, I guess a lot of techies are dual-booting Windows XP and Windows Vista for instance.
