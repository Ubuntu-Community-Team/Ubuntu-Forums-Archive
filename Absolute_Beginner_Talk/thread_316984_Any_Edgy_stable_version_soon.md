### Post by Hex_Mandos on 2006-12-12
Edgy IS a stable version. But stable is an ambiguous term. A "stable" version is the opposite of a "development" version. "Development" versions are not meant to be used by most people, and are just for testing purposes. They are also released much more often, with the latest advances.

Now, is Edgy stable enough for most users (as in "not crashing")? YES. I haven't experienced crashes or bugs, and I'm a Linux newbie.

LTS doesn't mean "Long term Stable", it means "Long Term SUPPORT". It means Dapper security patches will keep being added for 3 years after release (for desktop users). That's why it's the recommended version for beginners and people needing stability. In a way, we could say that Edgy is stable, and Dapper is ultra stable.

On the other hand, as most of the community uses Edgy by now, you might get better community support if you install Edgy. Trust me, it won't kill your computer.
