### Post by lila on 2008-01-18
Hello,
This is NOT a question, but advice I just figured out for myself and have not found anywhere else.  Other people might find it obvious, but we didn't.
We bought a house in the summer and since then live on a building site with no real heating (a couple of petroleum stoves).
We installed Gutsy about three weeks  before Christmas.  A cold wave started at about the same time.  
The computer would quite often not start up properly, stalling somewhere  along the process. There were a few other start up problems too, so  It took us a good while to make the connection:  The computer won't fire up properly under about 10 to 12 ° Celsius (no idea what that is in Farenheit, but it is not very warm).   It needs about three to four atempts (during which, presumably, it physically warms up a bit).  
So, if your computer stalls and it lives in a cold place, put a heater next to it a quarter to half an hour before you turn it on ( keep to safety distances, obviously).  Works wonders.  Is also appreciated by the human users.

### Post by DrMega on 2008-01-19
> **kvonb said:**
> Also, it might be a capacitor inside your power supply, the same problem, but be careful as there are dangerous voltages inside there, unplug from the mains power and wait a few minutes before putting your fingers in there :)

Take care. Please remember that capacitors (especially the larger electrolytic capacitors found in power supplies) will hold a charge even when disconnected from the power source. In most cases there is little risk of electric shock from these as the voltages are usually (but certainly not always) fairly low. However, occasionally, depending on the design of the PSU, they could be high enough voltage to shock you, and even if they are not, they often hold enough charge to give you a bit of a burn if you short them out.

Also remember that with polarised electrolytic capacitors (as very commonly found in power supplies) will explode if you put them in the wrong way round, so make sure you keep your face away from them when you first try them after replacing them.

If in doubt, pay somebody else to fix it.
