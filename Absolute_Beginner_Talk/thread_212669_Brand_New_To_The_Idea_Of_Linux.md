### Post by gr0kzer0 on 2006-07-10
Gnome vs KDE...

Gnome and KDE are the 2 most popular window managers for Linux.  They control how Linux looks and acts when you're using the graphical interface.  Some users get all agitated when discussing their favourite window manager, swearing that their favourite manager is the best, and the other one's a load of crap.

It's a real personal thing.  Out of the 2, I prefer Gnome.  But many people prefer KDE, and I'm not going to say they're wrong.  It's a matter of taste.   And anyway, Gnome isn't my absolute favourite WM.  I think xfce is the best.  No doubt most people reading this now think I'm crazy.

When you do a standard Ubuntu install, you get Gnome.  You can get KDE by installing Kubuntu instead.  But if you don't know them, it's best to install Ubuntu, then download kubuntu-desktop.  Then you can run both desktops side by side.  That's probably the best way to choose between them.

You can also get xubuntu-desktop to try xfce.  It's a bit minimal compared to Gnome and KDE, but to my "less is more" philosophy, its lightness is an advantage.  Try it!
