### Post by Third Thoughts on 2007-06-29
Fiesty is the newest version of Ubuntu. As a result, its got more programs available through synaptic, and more recent versions of many of the programs that both fiesty and dapper share. It also has a more userfriendly method for dealing with certain restricted drivers and I think better hardware support. Also if you have a powerful enough graphics card, it allows you to use compiz, the more powerful equivalent of Aero for Vista, by just changing a menu preference. 

Dapper is considered to be the more stable version of the two because its had more time to get tweaked to perfection. However, Edgy is stable enough for a normal home user. Unless you're talking about a critical development machine that simply cannot fail ever, then Edgy is probably a better bet. I've been running edgy since it came out and haven't had a serious crash. I really haven't even had a non serious crash except for those brought on by my tweaking with my xserver in attempts to get beryl to run on my crappy graphics card.
