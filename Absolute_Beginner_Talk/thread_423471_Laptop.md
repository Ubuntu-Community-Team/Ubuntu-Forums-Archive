### Post by kolinab on 2007-04-26
What I would do if I was in your situation is check your local trusted computer store for a used laptop. We bought one recently as a gift for exactly the type of scenario you are describing. Usually you can get a really good deal and sometimes a basic warranty. Don't be afraid to dicker with these guys, these older laptops aren't appreciating in value! Find one you like (check the battery to make sure it's not totally gonzo, or at least that you can get new ones on ebay) and then . . . I would check it against the information at:

[http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/](http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/)

These pages can give you some basic clues on how Ubuntu should work 'out of the box' on these laptops - there may be certain features of the systems that don't work without tweaking or not at all, but they might not be things you really care about.

I think that should help you - good luck!

Kolin
