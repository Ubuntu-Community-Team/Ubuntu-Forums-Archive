### Post by dstew on 2007-03-11
Your Live CD may be bad, or your computer doesn't have enough memory to run it. When you burn the .iso, it is usually a good idea to do all the checks, like the hash sum before burning, and verify after. In addition, the startup screen has an option to check the disk -- do this too. If the CD tests out bad, burn it at a slow speed, like 4x.

If your computer has less than 256Mb memory, the live CD will not work. See

[http://www.ubuntu.com/download/releasenotes/606](http://www.ubuntu.com/download/releasenotes/606)

Rarely, some piece of hardware in your computer is not compatible with linux, but this is usually not the case.
