### Post by kinematic on 2006-08-29
i would go with ubuntu.
the obvious reason being the huge forum loaded with info and knowledge and the second reason being that kanotix and knoppix both use the debian sid repo(kanotix is actually pure debian sid,also known as unstable)and the sid repo is in a constant state of flux.

ubuntu is a six monthly snapshot of debian sid after wich packages are stabelized guaranteing a certain degree of stability.

### Post by ecspike on 2006-08-29
I would agree that Ubuntu is the one to choose. For the record, I have used all three: 2 months Kanotix, 1.5 Knoppix and Ubuntu since Hoary.

Firstly, neither Knoppix no Kanotix were designed to be installed. Knoppix does have an installer script but Kanotix, the last time I checked does not. All three are based on Debian so you will get a similar experience as far as installing packages are concerned. KDE is the default for Knoppix and Kanotix , though you can install other window engines.

What really tipped the scale for me is that Ubuntu has a helpful community, auto-detected most of my peripherals[which out of all the distros I tried, Kanotix was the only other], and allows me to shield myself from details when I want and get down and dirty in the commandline when I want.
