### Post by jamesjtucker on 2007-05-29
Whether or not you should open the case depends on the make and model of your laptop. Some will be really easy to dissect, others will be impossible to reassemble once you have done it. Typically, you can pop open the keyboard fairly easily, and gain some access under there with a few screws.
   Here is what i have done in the past after returning from iraq with a dusty dusty laptop: 
1) pop out hard drive, battery and keyboard
2) blow compressed air into the fans while using the hose from my upright vacuum to suck in the dust from the keyboard tray hole, then the battery compartment, then the HDD. 
3) reverse the above, use the vacuum to suck from the fan opening, and blow the compressed air into the other openings. 
   This may not be the best method to do this, but it has worked for me in the past, and i use the vac to clean out my desktops and servers as well. Just watch out if you do the keyboard, as i have spent far too much time fishing out loose keys from the inside of the bags.... eew. 
   If the compressed air seems sketchy to you, then perhaps just a good vacuuming would suit your needs?
