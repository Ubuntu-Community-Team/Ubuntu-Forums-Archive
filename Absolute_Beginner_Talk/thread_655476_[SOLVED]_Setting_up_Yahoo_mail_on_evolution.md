### Post by rat_poison on 2008-01-17
You could check out a program called yahoopops and/or switch to thunderbird and use the webmail extensions I don't have time to post the urls right now, but I'm sure you'll find them after a bit of googling.
Also, I think that the thing about POP support to yahoo mail being a paid service is valid only for those yahoo mail addresses that end in yahoo.com and some others.
Some localised yahoo mail servers do offer free mail POPing. You could always create a "buffer" account on one of these servers. You can then have your original mail forward all mail to that account automatically and then you can check that account through evolution
