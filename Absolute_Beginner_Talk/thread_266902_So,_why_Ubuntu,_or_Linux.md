### Post by b_martinez on 2006-09-27
#1 PRO: You get to learn.
#2 PRO: Once you learn , you can make an informed choice.

#1CON: Linux requires learning, it is not as easy as falling out of bed
#2CON: After you learn, you will be faced with making a choice.

There is also the vaunted security. And the amount of control. The staggering amount of choices in software. The amazing ways to totally customize your computer "for each user". The endless (almost) choice of forums to get info. In many languages,and all around the world. The satisfaction of knowing you did this for no one but yourself, and you did it right. How many other reasons do you need? 
 It doesn't hurt, BUT, before you jump in, read the posts about installing. Even with the most knowledgeable users, accidents happen. Back up your system, if you are going to dual boot. Get an old hard drive, disconnect the Windows hard drive, and d/l and burn your distro of choice. Ubuntu and derivatives have good rescue functions, but they will not rescue/restore Windows partitions. D/l and use Vector linux. It has an option to boot (most) installed Linuxes. then come back to Ubuntu.
 Does it seem as if I'm trying to scare you off? I'm not, but you need to understand that there are risks.And allowing you to hose your Windows system, it's not the way to convince you to try Linux. The less you know about total system install, the greater the risks.

Heres my two cents:
