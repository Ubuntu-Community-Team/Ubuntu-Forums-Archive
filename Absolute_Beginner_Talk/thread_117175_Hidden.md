### Post by amasidlover on 2006-01-14
This depends a little on what you mean by hidden - Nautilus will hide a variety of things by default, such as files beginning with . or ending with ~. The shell (i.e. what you see in a terminal) will hide files beginning . by default. However there is no equivalent to the MS-DOS 'hidden' attribute - at least not in most distributions of Linux.

Alex

### Post by AAUU on 2006-01-14
.filename

The . will make it hidden to a normal "ls" command (from a shell prompt) and the default view with the Nautilus application.
