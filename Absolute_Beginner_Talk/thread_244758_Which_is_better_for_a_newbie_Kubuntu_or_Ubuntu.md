### Post by Buzzygirl on 2006-08-27
I'll echo most of the other posts here, in that the KDE and GNOME interfaces are pretty much a matter of personal preference. 

However, I will share a personal observation on the difference between the two desktops.

I recently introduced my boyfriend to Ubuntu. I installed it on one of his laptop computers last week, and he immediately took to it like he'd been working with it for a long time. I was very pleased he liked it so much, but I was curious as to WHY he took to it so quickly... after all, he was barely familiar with Linux, let alone having any actual user experience.

He has been a fan of Macs since they came out in the mid-1980's and, up until about 2 years ago, he only owned Macs. He is an illustrator by trade, and Macs always fit the bill for his purposes.

He has another laptop 'puter that's running WinXP. He likes it and it's his first foray into Windows. He has had no problems with that computer and it runs great. But he prefers to use his other laptop, the one that runs Ubuntu 6.06. When I asked him why he prefers that one over the other one, he said it's because the GNOME interface reminds him of his erstwhile Mac computers. 

To me, KDE seems more like a Windows interface, whereas GNOME looks more like a Mac interface. I've used both operating systems extensively, and this is my basic take on the difference between the two.

However, what counts is what's underneath. Ubuntu is Linux through-and-through, and it's flexible enough to offer several choices of graphical user interface, enough to please most people who're migrating over from another operating system. 

Good luck and have fun!

Jackie
