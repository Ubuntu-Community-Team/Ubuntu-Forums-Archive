### Post by heimo on 2007-08-23
> **pcexpert2 said:**
> i'm planning on geting the ubuntu 7.04. and what else should i know is it hard to learn linux. i'm an exert at windows xp but its just so limited. will ubuntu give me more bang for my time :) (because i'm not wasting any buck get it)  thanks for answering my noobish question

You really need to find out yourself. Evaluate it. Use as much time as you think it's worth. My personal opinion is, it's definitely worth trying. But you need to be patient and to understand that Ubuntu is not Windows, it's different and thus you need to learn some new things, which will take time. But - again, in my opinion - Ubuntu is much easier to keep going, there's not much maintenance. Easiest way to solve hardware incompatibilities is to plan ahead and buy stuff that works out of the box with Ubuntu / Linux, and even replace pieces that are hard to get working otherwise. So, there is no such thing as free lunch. But freedom is worth a lot of money and time. It's also a learning process that will fascinate some of us, as you can really actually go deep into the operating system if you so wish, unlike in any proprietary system. And if you're not interested in messing up with internals of your system, and just want your system to work, make your system work with Ubuntu, learn couple alternative Free programs, and you can pretty much forget operating system or applications and concentrate on getting job done.
