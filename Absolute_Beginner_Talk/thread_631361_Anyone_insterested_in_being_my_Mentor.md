### Post by linuxlizard on 2007-12-04
Hey, 
I don't know if I'm enough to be your mentor, but I can give you some quick free advice before you bungle things further.

When I was new to linux several years ago, I made a similar mistake to yours and thought I'd lost everything- my photos and stuff on my hard drive.

So, I gave up and did clean installs- big mistake it slowly dawned on me a couple of years afterwards. Get a live cd- if the ubuntu one won't do the trick, try puppy linux and go in and mount your windows drive. If it's just the bootloader that's messed up, all your old photos and pics and stuff are still on there and can be copied safely to an external drive.

Then stop trying to do things the hard way- re-install ubuntu and do manual partitions and let grub put the bootloader on the mbr. Odds are pretty high if you do this, you will have your current windows install still safely there and bootable after you install ubuntu. messing up the mbr doesn't mess up windows - just booting into it. If you use install ubuntu and let grub do it's thing, windows should still be there.
