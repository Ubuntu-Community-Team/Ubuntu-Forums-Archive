### Post by nalmeth on 2007-03-12
What is your primary use for your laptop?

Are you absolutely tied to any particular programs?

If you have relatively simple needs, a lot of the programs you'll want are easily installed with Add/Remove programs (point and click). 

If you have very specific and specialized needs, it may require some work on your part, but the community will help.

I suggest you download the Ubuntu disc and run it to test how well your hardware works. You can find a good starting point for that here:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCD](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/LiveCD)
(Choose 6.06)

This CD will load up WITHOUT touching your harddrive. It is safe to use with Windows already installed. Just don't install it until you're ready :)

### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-03-12
Not sure what you mean. But I would go dual boot tell you understand the OS better. That means when you boot your system you will be given the option of what OS to use: WinXP or Ubuntu. 

As far as going back. You can always delete the ubuntu partition and then fix the master boot record (deleteing GRUB). 

Not to add insult but do you think Ubuntu installs on top of windows as an extension?

If unsure of all that and you don't want to dual boot or run from CD, you can always try the VMWare image.
