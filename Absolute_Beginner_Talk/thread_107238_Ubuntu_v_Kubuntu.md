### Post by estel on 2005-12-22
KDE is slightly more resources intensive, but the difference is negligible on a modern machine. KDE is also more customisable (a lot more) and can look like Windows (does this by default); but it can also look like nothing else in the known Universe. 

KDE also has a good suite of programs that even GNOME people use, such as k3b. Conversely, GNOME is a lot easier to pick up and use to its full(ish) potential quite quickly. All of the options to do stuff are much more evident. There just aren't quite so many options.

In the end, it is all really down to personal choice. I used to only use GNOME, but I installed KDE to try it out and am now using KDE much more than GNOME, even if I stilluse various GNOME apps.

### Post by bscbrit on 2005-12-22
