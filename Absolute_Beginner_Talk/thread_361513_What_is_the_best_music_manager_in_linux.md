### Post by DigitalAxis on 2007-05-31
Why isn't OGG popular?

* There are no major companies behind OGG (Vorbis is the name of the actual codec, Ogg is the container)

* Vorbis doesn't support DRM

* "Not Invented Here" syndrome

* Not popular (a vicious cycle)

* PlaysForSure at one time required players to NOT support other codecs not directly in the specs.  This has changed, though.



That said, there are many hardware DAPs and PMPs that play Vorbis...   Nearly anything from Cowon, Miezu, iRiver, the Rio Karma, and the new Trekstor Vibez will play Vorbis.  And those are just the ones I know about.  Creative, Sandisk and Apple players don't.

Sigmatel makes a line of sound chips for digital audio players that can handle Vorbis and FLAC (the Ogg Free Lossless Audio Codec) among many others, so anything that uses their chips should be capable of it.  I don't know how you'd track things down that way, though.

As for the best music MANAGER, I don't really know.  I would have said Amarok, but you already said you tried it and it wasn't sufficient.
