### Post by Lord Illidan on 2007-10-09
They're pretty much two different Desktop Environments.

KDE : [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE)
GNOME : [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME)

Both are very good in their own right, but there are some notable differences.

KDE applications use the QT widget set, while GNOME uses the GTK widget set. This means that KDE applications, like Amarok will look out of place in GNOME, and vice-versa.

Secondly, the developers have different philosophies, so to speak. KDE gives you plenty of configuration options, GNOME aims to provide sensible defaults, and hides many options away. Some like it, some don't.

Graphically, they look quite different. KDE looks more like Windows, while GNOME looks more like a MAC. But, of course, that's just skin-deep, and you can radically change their appearance with themes and such.

Ubuntu comes pre-installed with GNOME, whereas Kubuntu comes with KDE.

You can have both installed on your computer, and you can chose at login which to run.
