### Post by aysiu on 2005-08-20
[QUOTE=Maupertus]I'm thinking of dual-booting my computer Ubuntu-XP, but although I have a couple of years Linux experience, I'm a bit anxious with this idea, as I've no experience what so ever with running a Dual-Boot.

Is there a good Dual-Boot guide out there? Especially as I don't want to lose the data on my PC.[/QUOTE] Don't be anxious. If you have a couple of years Linux experience, it should be a breeze. I set up a dual-boot within a couple of days of using Linux. Now I'm in a quadruple-boot after a few months. If you don't want to lose data, does that mean you're not going to back up your data? Because you really should back up your data. Not backing up your data before repartitioning is playing "Russian Roulette." Sure, the majority of the six chambers (five out of six) may be empty, but there's still that one chamber that isn't. Likewise, for repartitioning, in the vast majority of cases (99%), data will remain untouched, but do you really want to take that chance? Back up your work! That said, just read this thread:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=16287](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=16287)
