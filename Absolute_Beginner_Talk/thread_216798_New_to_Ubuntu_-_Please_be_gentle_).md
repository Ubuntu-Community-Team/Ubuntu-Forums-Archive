### Post by wombat20 on 2006-07-16
Welcome!  Hope you enjoy Ubuntu.  The forums are part of what makes it great, so don't be afraid to use them to help you learn.  For simple questions you'll get answers quite quickly if someone knows the solution.  Remember to search for answers to your question first.

Also, when you do work something out, or come across an unusual solution, post here to let others know how you did it.  When you get a bit more experienced, you'll find it very satisfying to answer a few easy questions.  Ubuntu is free, but it's good to give as well as take.

If you want things to run faster, the thing which makes the biggest difference is hard disk.  Get a 7200rpm one or better next time you upgrade.  I'm using 2x7200 SATA 3 RAID0.  The RAID0 makes a small difference (about 5-10 seconds faster bootup), but the 7200rpm change (was 5400rpm) seemed to decrease my application load times by 25-30%.  In my experience a 7200rpm HD isn't much more expensive.  To get the same out of your processor or RAM, you have to spend twice as much!
