### Post by Sef on 2008-03-19
[OpenPrinting]("http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Dell-AIO_Printer_966") also says it is a paperweight.  Dells are rebranded Lexmarks, and they don't provide drivers for GNU/Linux, expect for their Optra line, which is for businesses.  For you, either you have  to install Windows to get your printer to work or [get a printer]("http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting/Database/SuggestedPrinters") that will work with GNU/Linux.  Best printer company for GNU/Linux is HP, followed closely by Epson.
