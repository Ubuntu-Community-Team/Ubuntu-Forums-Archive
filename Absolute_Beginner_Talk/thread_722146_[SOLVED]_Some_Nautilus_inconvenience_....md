### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-03-12
If you have the status pane open, you can click on the folder name then hit Ctrl+V to paste it in there. Or, if you use button location bar you can click on the folder name you want to paste stuff into then hit Ctrl+V

Even if neither of those conditions are true, once you click into your folder you can immediately click Ctrl+V to paste stuff into the window.

Lastly, you can go into >preferences >windows and check the box that says make window active when mouse is over it. Then you just point at the folder, and hit ctrl+v

In other words, you don't need to click on a free space to make it active.

Well, you do have rather particular needs and wants don't you? Luckily, this is Linux, where everyone and anyone is free to hack away at and change things to suit their own needs. And, given the large size of the Linux community chances are there is someone out there who has wanted something Linux couldn't do, so set about trying to make it happen.

Take this for example, [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=455656](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=455656)

It's a little daemon you can install that will let you configure your mouse. So, if you've got a wheel that clicks down, you can use that app in the link to tell ubuntu that everytime I click the mouse wheel button do Ctrl+V.

Thus, with that app and mouse over window activation you can point at the window, wheel click, and bam. Even easier than left-click to activate, right-click for options, and left-click to paste.

If you're really keen, you can read this over,[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=316441&highlight=edit+mouse+events](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=316441&highlight=edit+mouse+events) which explains how to do all that without running a daemon in the background.

Point being, there are many ways to do something, and almost anything is possible. Nimble hands or no.
