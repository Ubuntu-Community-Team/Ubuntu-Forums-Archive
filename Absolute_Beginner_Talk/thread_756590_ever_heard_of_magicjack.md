### Post by anewguy on 2008-04-16
I've seen the ads, and was curious if it worked in Linux.  Apparently no drivers yet.  However, there are many devices that do work in Linux to allow you to hook your existing phone up and make calls.  I use a generic USB "Telbox" - you can find them on Ebay.  There is an open source driver for it.  I hooked my phone to it, plugged in a phone line, plugged a USB cable into my PC, loaded the drivers, and it works.  I use it with Skype.  In Windows you would get more functionality due to enhanced drivers, etc., but at least I use my single cordless phone.  When I have disconnected from a call on the PC, it falls back to using the phone line.  So, I use the single phone for both regular phone and on my PC.  Not to advertise here, because I know there are other devices and programs to allow you to do the same thing, but it has worked great for me.  My family all live anywhere from 650 to 2100 miles from me, and being disabled it is rare for me to see them (I haven't seen my Dad in 10 years unfortunately), but $30 a year to Skype and I can call anyone anytime anywhere in the US and Canada - no extra fees.  Plus I used the Skype service "Skype-In" and set up a local number my folks could call and it rings via Skype on my PC.  Saves me LOTS of money, and saves my folks a LOT of money too - no more long distance charges!!

Hope that helps in some way!  :)
