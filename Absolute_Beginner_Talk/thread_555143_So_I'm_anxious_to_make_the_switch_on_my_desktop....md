### Post by Ragnar Bocephus on 2007-09-19
Well, it seems to be an either/or situation with ATI cards.  In a normal Gnome session using the proprietary FGLRX driver, direct rendering is enabled and higher end games and applications work very smoothly.  However, if I want to use Beryl I have to log in under an XGL session, and direct rendering is not enabled there.  The Beryl effects work flawlessly, by the way, but graphics intensive programs are either choppy or won't load at all.

I haven't bothered updating Beryl to Compiz Fusion yet, as I'm waiting around for the Gutsy release to muck about with it further.  

There are several how-to documents on this forum that will show you step-by-step how to install Beryl or CF with an ATI card.  It's not really that difficult to accomplish and may be worth messing around with if you're really interested.  Don't expect it to work flawlessly just yet.  :)
