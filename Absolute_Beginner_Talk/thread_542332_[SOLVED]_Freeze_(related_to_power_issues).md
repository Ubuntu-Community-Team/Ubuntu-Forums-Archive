### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-09-04
Before running off and buying a new cord, I suggest running your laptop on battery, without the cord plugged into the PC and see if the problem still occurs.

Certainly, if there is a problem on the DC side of your power supply cord and that cord carries multiple voltages to your laptop, I would be wary, as you could end up with say a 12V rail being introduced onto a 5V rail, which could be catastrophic.

Also have a look at the cord and plug and see if there is any obvious damage.

Again, have a close look at the socket on your laptop, where the power cord plugs in. Is there any obvious damage? Is it loose?
