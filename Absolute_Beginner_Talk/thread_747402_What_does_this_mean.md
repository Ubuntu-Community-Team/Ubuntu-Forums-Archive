### Post by mister_pink on 2008-04-07
Downloading the codecs is not stealing. The library you need to download to get dvd playback is free open source software, but the US media lobby claims it's illegal because it breaks the content scrambling system on the dvds in order to play them back. The truth is that there's never been a test case against it, so its not illegal, but it is a bit of a grey area. Hence its not included by default to avoid distributions getting involved in any legal issues, although I really doubt there ever will be any now.

Edit: Also, I see people claiming things about having to pay for the software being the problem. It is really nothing to do with that, it's about whether the software itself is legal because it breaks the encryption. Check out the wikipedia article on it: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libdvdcss](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libdvdcss) See also [http://ubuntucat.wordpress.com/2007/05/28/the-legality-or-illegality-of-w32codecs-and-libdvdcss2/](http://ubuntucat.wordpress.com/2007/05/28/the-legality-or-illegality-of-w32codecs-and-libdvdcss2/)
