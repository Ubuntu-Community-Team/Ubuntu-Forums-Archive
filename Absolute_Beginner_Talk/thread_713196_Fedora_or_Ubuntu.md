### Post by scottro on 2008-03-02
Fedora people will tell you that it does aim to be cutting edge, and the key word is cutting.  :)  Things break, frequently. 

Ubuntu--apt is faster, there are more packages.   My impression is that it tends to work better out of the box.  I'm not sure of its mission statement, but the stated number one bug is that Windows is more popular, so I think that indicates it does aim to be a more stable desktop O/S.  

They both have excellent communities.   Hardware support is usually going to be about the same--I've had Ubuntu work out of the box on something Fedora couldn't do, but I've also had the opposite experience.  

However, regardless of all the advice you'll read, eventually, you'll probably decide for yourself that you like one better than the other---and though you'll have lots of reasons to give people, it's probably, at bottom, an emotional decision--one will just feel better to you, for whatever reason.  <shrug>
