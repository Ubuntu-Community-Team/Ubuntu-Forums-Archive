### Post by millol on 2006-04-04
The Live CD should be run from boot. So you can't run it while logged to windows. You might do the following to boot from the Live CD.
1-Burn the image to a CD.
2-Insert the CD.
3-Restart your system
4-Make sure the system is set to boot from CD first. If it's not the case, change the boot order from BIOS

And it should work

### Post by dvarsam on 2006-04-04
Do what "millol" says.

You do NOT need a winemulator or anything...

1. Just write the Ubuntu ".iso" to a CD.

2. Shutdown your PC.

3. On your BIOS set your PC to boot from the CD first & then the Hard Drives.

4. Put your Ubuntu CD your created on your CD-Rom drive...

5. Save Changes to your BIOS.

6. Restart your Computer.

Your computer should Boot from the CD - and your Windows or Whatever OS you have on your Hard Disk is not going to be harmed or anything...

It is as if your Had Unplugged the Power & Data Cables from your Hard Drive...

As if a Hard Drive never existed in your PC...

Good Luck!
