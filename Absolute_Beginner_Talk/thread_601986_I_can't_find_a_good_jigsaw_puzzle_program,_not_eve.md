### Post by Chris S. on 2007-11-04
For xjig, take a look at both

```

xjig --help
man xjig

```

You can set the number of pieces in x and y directions and the image used from the command line, as well as quite a few other things.  In the program, you can grab a piece with right mouse button, grab it with left button to do a funky rotate thing, and zoom in and out with PageUp and PageDn, respectively, and more.  Give that a try and see if it's too your liking.  It apparently allows double-sided puzzles too, but I didn't see it in the manual (or I wasn't looking hard enough).

In the future, try those two commands to learn more about any program.  Most packages include manuals (man <program name>), and most programs have a --help or -h switch.  Oh, and thanks for bringing up jigsaw puzzle programs, I've found a new thing to waste time with. :D
