### Post by Diabolis on 2008-03-19
You have to change the place from where you want to boot in your bios. Just after you turn on our pc, press f2 and you'll see your bios. You'll have a menu, move to the one that says boot. There you'll have many options from where you want to boot. Choose your cd-rom as your first boot option and your hard drive as your second option.

Do not  hesitate on asking more questions.

### Post by The Cog on 2008-03-19
Also, the CD must have been burned the right way (in case you don't already know this):
An .ISO file is a special file that you don't burn inthe normal way. You must not unpack it with winzip or anything like that and then just write the contents to a CD. Nor must you just write the file to CD as though it were a data file. You need to burn it **as an image** file. Most CD burning programs have an option for handling image files. If you do it right, you will never have opened the file to see the contents, but when you look on teh burned CD you will see lots of files and folders, and the CD will be bootable.
