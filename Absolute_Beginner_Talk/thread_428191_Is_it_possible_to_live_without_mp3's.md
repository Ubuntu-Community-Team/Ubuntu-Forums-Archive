It is specified that the mp3 codec is free to the user if it is not used for comercial purposes.  The royalty is charged on the distributor, which is why the mp3 codec does not ship with Ubuntu or any other distro that does not pay the royalty.  It is perfectly legal to use mp3 unless you are doing it for business purposes.

That being said, I buy my music online in ogg format and I can fit more songs on my iRiver that way.  Yes, you can be mp3-free.

### Post by mozetti on 2007-04-30
> **nirpius said:**
> I'm new to Linux and I've been looking at trying to do everything *properly*. I've been told to steer clear from mp3s because they're proprietary and apparently this is bad.

I think the most important thing you should take away from this thread is, _You don't have to drink the Kool-Aid!_.

Linux doesn't have to be an "all or none" endeavor. It's a spectrum - on one side is 100% completely closed-source programs, and the other side is 100% completely open-source programs. Find your nitch somewhere along the spectrum.

When I used Windows, I didn't restrict myself to only using 100% completely closed-source, pay-to-play programs. There was freeware, shareware, open source and closed source programs all existing peacefully on my system. It's very similar now that I'm using Ubuntu. Take the time to learn about why other people think you should/shouldn't install certain software and then decide for yourself.
