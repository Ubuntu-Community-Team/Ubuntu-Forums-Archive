### Post by evilgold on 2006-04-09
If im understanding you correctly, your asking if compressing your mp3s and oggs into a tar will affect quality. Well no, it wont affect anything really, Tar is a container, it doest compress files it just puts them all in one file. Gzip, Bz2, rar, zip, ect are compression formats.

In anycase compressing mp3s or oggs is pointless, because they are already compress, you'll end up with a file the same size as your collection was before. May as well just backup the directory itself, because trying to compress them will only waste time.

And to your other question regarding riping/burning/reripping. I'm not sure on this one, but I think the quality would degrade, though not in a noticable manner. Perhaps if you stuck to the same version of the same codec everything would be the same, but i doubt it. Just use the higest quality (bitrate) you think you need, and you should be okay, as long as you dont convert from one lossy to another lossy (like mp3 to ogg). Although even that doesnt hurt too much.

### Post by eriefisher on 2006-04-09
For your first question, I don't know if you can compress a mp3 or ogg any more than they already are. They are already small files. 

As for ripping, only the original format conversion will cause loss of quality. Other than that it's just making an exact copy, there should not be anymore loss of quality other than the quality or condition of the actual disc.
