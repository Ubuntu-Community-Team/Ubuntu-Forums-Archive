### Post by jordanmthomas on 2007-12-03
I've been using Linux for about two and a half years now, and I can say that the best way to learn how everything works is to just use it.  Any time you want to do something, look up how to do it.  If you don't understand something in the instructions, look it up.  You'll never know everything, so don't worry about it.  Just learn what you need to learn as you go along.

Any time something breaks, look up how to fix it on your own before coming to a forum and just asking.  After enough things screw up, you'll notice you might be able to figure out how to fix it without even being able to look it up.  You'll know, for example, that errors a lot of times show up in /var/log so you'll look there.  You'll know that configuration files often reside in /etc.  You'll eventually understand what error messages mean, giving you an idea of how to fix the problem, and so on.  

Basically, as long as you continue to use Linux, its "ways" will make themselves present to you.
