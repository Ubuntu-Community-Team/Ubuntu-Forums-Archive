Thanks for posting those lists - they should be compulsory reading for anyone who thinks that Wine will be the perfect solution for their essential Windows software that has no direct Linux replacement.

I would just add the warning that sometimes when a program is listed as 'working', more careful reading may show that it ***kinda*** works - lacking functionality that may be essential to some users.   I think sometimes there's too much hype that* 'Oh, you can run your Windows software using Wine',* and it's ultimately disillusioning for new Linux users to discover that it just ain't so.  (and, uh, I might just have ***been*** one of those new Linux users!)

But I have to admit that I'm not a Wine fan.  I slowly worked through my whole shelf of Windows software - games, text-based apps, planning apps, graphic-handling apps, etc.  ***All ***installed very happily in Wine - ***Not one*** worked.  Either they refused to run, or ran in such a crippled fashion that they were unusable.
