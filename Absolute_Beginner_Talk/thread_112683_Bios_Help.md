### Post by kenweill on 2006-01-04
There are shortcut keys that you can press when you turn on your computer for you to go to Setup or to activate the options of selecting the device in which you want to boot.

With my ASROCK motherboard:
F2 = Go to Setup
F10 = Select boot device

Or something like that.

With my IBM ThinkCentre computer:
Enter/Return = Enter Setup
F12 = Select boot device

I'm sure your machine have that. Newer machines/motherboards have shortcuts. From the shortcut, you can select from different devices for you to boot without entering to the BIOS setup.

It pops up/display during POST(Power On Self-Test).

Hope this helps.

### Post by Bartender on 2006-01-05
MC1234 -
Have you made any progress on the BIOS issue?  I read thru your previous post and this one and was just wondering.  
BIOS is a little scary because when you get there most of your keys don't do anything, and you've gotta find the ones that do, and then it's frightening to make any changes because you don't want to screw everything up, then you're never quite sure if you actually made any changes...
But it can be done.  If I figured it out so can you.  Once you get over the initial fear, you'll see the BIOS is fairly simple.  After all, there's only so many things you can change, depending on who put the BIOS together.  
Having said that, I noticed in your previous post you asked about getting a BIOS upgrade.  Flashing the BIOS is a project you might want to put off for now.  I've done it a couple of times but it's a procedure that can result in complete and utter disaster.  "If it ain't broke don't fix it" definitely applies to your situation.
I've found the guys at Techsupportforum  [http://www.techsupportforum.com/](http://www.techsupportforum.com/)  to be super helpful with this kind of stuff.  If you're still struggling with getting your arms around BIOS you might want to try them.
Just to reiterate what some of the other guys have said, your BIOS has nothing to do with Windows 98.  The BIOS instructions are stored on a  memory chip that's installed right on your motherboard.  Soldered in place on older boards, removable in lots of newer boards.  That BIOS will be the same as the day you bought the machine unless someone went into it and changed settings.  My experience with BIOS is limited but I can't imagine that your BIOS doesn't have a boot order (or sequence or whatever COMPAQ calls it) menu.  Can you grab someone who's dinked around with BIOS and have them help you find it?  BIOS is frightening at first, but setting boot order is a really simple thing to do once you're familiar with which keys do what in the BIOS.
