### Post by turbojugend_gr on 2006-11-24
In Linux you have control over everything as you see. As it's already covered, I will just give a hint. The gnome panel is very powerful and customizable, I would suggest you adding a third panel, play with it as much as possible, add thing remove things, get yourself familiar with it in any possible aspect, then delete it, and use the knowledge you gathered while playing, to set up your existing panels to your needs.

When I was a newcomer, I was advised to create a new user account, log into it and play as much as possible with everything in it (panels, nautilus prefs, desklets, icons, script I made to get familiar with scripting, EVERYTHING that _doesn't need root access_ (**no sudo involved** in other words)) and when I manage to destroy this account, to get back to my regular account and use the gained knowledge to customize my system to my needs. It was harmless, I made 3 accounts unsuable, in  two days, but when I was back, I set up my main account perfectly matching my needs. I found that very handy, and knowing you will only crash an account made for this, is so great! _(DONT FORGET, nothing that involves root priviledges, other words no sudo, no password asked -exept of course for when you enter the account)_

I came to learn a lot more, by trying to make them work again too.

Linux is about choice, and to prove that, it gives you this ability. Use it responsibly (do NOT include ROOT privileges while you play, do not do things that require your main account password).

Cheers, TJ.
