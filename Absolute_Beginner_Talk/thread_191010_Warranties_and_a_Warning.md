### Post by rcarring on 2006-06-06
Please read your pc warranty and make sure that if you do any of the following you do not invalidate your warranty:

a) Installing a dual boot with ubuntu and Windows XP

b) fitting more memory yourself

c) changing or adding a hard drive yourself

d) wiping the pcs diagnostic partition by accident or by design

e) using a partititon manager tool

f) running your monitor at too high a resolution so it goes bang and dies

g) doing something to your pc that implicitly breaks the warranty such as trying to get it fixed at comp usa first (nb, insurance replacements usually require an engineers report)

When returning a machine for warranty repair, back up your data (if you can) and re-image using the restore to factory fresh condition option

### Post by Jason_25 on 2006-06-07
There's an act in the US called the magnuson-moss warranty act.  I have seen it referenced to cars but I think it applies to computers as well.  You should read up on it.  Basically, your allowed to modify whatever you buy without voiding the warranty unless your modification directly contributed to the failure that requires the warranty work.  a, b, c, d, and e should definitely be covered.
