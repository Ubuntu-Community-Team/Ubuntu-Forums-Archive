### Post by kevinlyfellow on 2007-06-27
Welcome to Ubuntu.  I'm glad to hear that your enjoying your experience!  Some advice that I have is if you have any questions, search the forums.  If you can't find the answer, you can also use google.  Try before you post, chances are someone has asked the question before.  But also, if you can't find the answer, don't be afraid to ask.  Linux forums have had a reputation of being exclusionary but things have changed and these forums are perhaps some of the most newbie friendly.  Also, there are some great resources floating around that you may or may not know about.  Aysiu on these forums has a great website to help you get started [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php) and maniacmusician is working on a beginners book [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=448312](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=448312).  

The best advice I can give is to have fun with linux.  Many people come in hoping that its a better windows or that people in the forum are here to serve they're will when something goes wrong.  There's no such thing as a free lunch and sometimes linux will take some work and you'll see why you like linux (or you don't) if you give it shot.  Enjoy the learning process and your linux experience will be all the better.

Best of luck
Kevinlyfellow

### Post by KIAaze on 2007-06-27
Never tried Wubi (will really have to one day :) ), so I can't help directly with that.

Other than that, here some hints for apps you might need:
_web browsing:_
-Firefox is installed by default with Ubuntu, but if you want others:
-Opera (proprietary)
-Konqueror: kind of Windows explorer+Web Browser all in one (even has integrated terminal)
-Lynx: Terminal-based browser->only text, no pics&videos. Use only if you have extremely low system resources or just want to be geeky. ^^

_burning dvds:_
-GnomeBaker
-K3B

_playing music:_
-AmaroK: The best music app ever, better than anything on Windows even :)
-XMMS: player similar to Winamp

And here some usful links:
[Psychocats Ubuntu guides]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php")
[Ubuntu video tutorials]("http://ubuntuclips.org/")
