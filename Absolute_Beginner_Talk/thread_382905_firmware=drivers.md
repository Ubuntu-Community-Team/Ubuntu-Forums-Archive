### Post by penp on 2007-03-12
> **the_nomad said:**
> firmware=drivers?

> In computing, firmware is software that is embedded in a hardware device. It is often provided on flash ROMs or as a binary image file that can be uploaded onto existing hardware by a user. ([http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware") )

Usually, it's written on a flash ROM that allows you to update the firmware, which is something I wouldn't recommend tackling unless you understand what you'll be doing to your device. Also, in a PC the only thing you'd usually flash is either a motherboard BIOS or maybe an optical drive - usually firmware updates are for standalone devices like an MP3 player, a digital camera or a dvd player.

**Device Drivers** is software that tells the operating system how to communicate with a particular device. These need to be installed before your device will work properly.

Does that answer your question?
