### Post by LKRaider on 2006-08-17
I agree that getting the original resized is a better idea if you want quality output, but also notice that Gimp has an image resize quality setting: 

-> when you go into Image->Resize, notice the _Interpolation_ setting there. The **Cubic** option should give the best output most of the time, but some images may look better without interpolation (the **None** option) too (especially the ones where clear edges or small dots are displayed).

So, you can experiment with that first and try all options to see if you get a good output ;)
