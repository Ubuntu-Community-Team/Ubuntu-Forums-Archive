### Post by flatwombat on 2007-07-29
Well, for those linux users who are concerned about forwarding infected emails to their friends and families, it's not a bad idea.  Kind of an interesting situation happened to me recently.  My son, who is a Windows XP user, had a particularly nasty trojan which kept him from booting XP as it kept reloading when his email automatically opened.  It had also blocked Safe Mode (neat one!).  The solution was for me to access his email and scan it with clamscan (ClamAV) and then remove the nasty.  He now dual-boots and only opens email under Ubuntu.
