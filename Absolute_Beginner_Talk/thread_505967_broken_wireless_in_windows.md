### Post by citadelgrad on 2007-07-21
Do the easiest thing first, reboot the router as suggested previously. Then I'd update the drivers. I'm recommending these be your next troubleshooting steps. Then I'd try connecting to an unsecured access point. If it works in Ubuntu but not Windows there is little chance it's a hardware problem. Upgrade your router firmware. Reset your Router to its defaults and reconfigure it. 

Often installing new drivers or uninstalling devices from within the Device Manager will remove any corrupt configurations lingering in your OS. Updated drivers also fix bugs and are typical going to be *better* then previous versions.
