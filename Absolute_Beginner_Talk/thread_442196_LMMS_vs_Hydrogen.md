### Post by srhlefty on 2007-05-14
I've used LMMS before, and can say that it aims to be a Fruity Loops clone.  It's sample-based, with both simple sequencing (like creating beats) and piano-roll (for creating complex melodies) capabilities.

Hydrogen appears to be very similar, though I believe it is geared specifically to MIDI capabilities.  For example, it can record events from a real MIDI instrument as you play something with it, and quantize them.  

Bottom line: Hydrogen bills itself as a drum machine, not a song writer.  LMMS is more structured towards creating songs with melodies by instruments other than drums.

If you're interested in something even more advanced, you might check out Rosegarden.  Not only can you record from MIDI, but you can have it show you the musical score that it corresponds to.  From the other end, you can create a score and then have it play it for you.
