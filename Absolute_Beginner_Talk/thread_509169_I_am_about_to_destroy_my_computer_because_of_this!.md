Holy smokes! An Ubuntu brawl ...hold up.....:popcorn:  <  I always wanted to do that....

Seriously, it's not Ubuntu, it's the ISO, for one, NEVER use rewritable media for installing an OS, they are for the  most part, unreliable. 

Second, the more you erase it, the worse it gets, wears out. 

Third, spring for 20 cents or 3.99 for some CDs, usually 50 for a decent price. 

If you use the MD5 checksum it tells you to, you may save some hardship by checking to verify the download ahead of time. 

I know this because I had a bad download from one of the links and went through a few CDs. 
Check the MBs as well, if it adds up to the download size it says it's supposed to be, most likely a good ISO, however, mine was off by 20kb or something, it would not install. 

Just a reminder, any time you download , especially a large download, errors can occur and information can be damaged\missing. This can happen with any download , even a small one , just more likely with larger ones. A few misplaced\lost packets, and bye bye ISO. 

 If knoppix didn't work, you may try using "DEFINITELY" non-RW CDs and something like IMG burn, that works very well.
