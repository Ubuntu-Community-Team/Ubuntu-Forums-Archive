### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-21
> **ginestre said:**
> This may well be a silly question but I'm going to ask it anyway. If a program is given as installable on (let's say) Edgy, can I expect to install it hassle free in Feisty? In other words, do we generally have backwards compatibility?  Or is life more complicated than that?

All programs you can find in the repos and install in Edgy, will have a functional version for Feisty and its repos (unless the project has been discontinued, like nvu, kompozer is the new product). If the program you had was a specialist one, and not in repos, you may have to compile it I suppose again, I assume you would have done that the first time around though too.

Other than that, yes, everything _should_ work but may not always, there were kernel changes in Feisty and I have seen a few reports of broken apps... most will soon or already have a new version thats functional.
