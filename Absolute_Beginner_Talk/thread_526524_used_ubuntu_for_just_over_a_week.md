### Post by newlinux on 2007-08-15
> **terry_gardener said:**
> 
Thinking in the future of getting a tv card, is there a good media centre software that can handle tv and record tv and maybe even pause tv. Play audio and video files. from nas and local disks.


Welcome!  As for media center software, I use and recommend mythtv, which can do all you need and a lot more. It has a lot of different options, so some consider it hard to configure, but the basic install is fairly straight forward using the ubuntu community help pages. With the current version to play video files and music from many different locations you'll need to do some mounting and linking, but nothing too complicated.

I use it for all my videos, all my recorded viewing content, and home movies. Unfortunately, I don't think its interface is  the best for music but it does have it. I run it on multiple computers throughout the house and stream live and recorded content to multiple PCs and network devices connected to tvs and monitors.

Make sure to get a tv ard well supported by Ubuntu and mythtv. Also consider what type of signal you want to capture (digital/analog, Hi def or standard definition, etc.) and what signals you can capture in your area from your provider(s).

Good luck. There is a lot of support here for mythtv.
