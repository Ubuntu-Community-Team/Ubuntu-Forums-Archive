### Post by ofb on 2007-10-21
[http://www.ubuntu.com/community/processes/newdev](http://www.ubuntu.com/community/processes/newdev)

Y'know what? Start /anywhere/.

It really doesn't matter. Once you get a few months of poking around in any corner you pick, you'll have a much better idea of the landscape.

It's kinda like how so many people change their major in the first year of university. They had no way of making an informed decision from the perspective of high school.

So just dig in. There must be something you like -- like are you into music? Then check out Ubuntu Studio and PureDyne and play with them. You'll naturally find bugs and things you want to add, then just offer to help out. Anything like that. Just pick something that you find fun and then go help out with the others who find it fun. Those developers will be the ones to ask about what more you should do to learn.
