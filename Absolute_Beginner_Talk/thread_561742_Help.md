### Post by jppaynesr on 2007-09-27
An electric screwdriver will not help, it will probably cause damage especially if you cannot get them out by hand. Chances are the electric bit would just spin and ruin the screw.

First, look closely at the head of the screw. Is it really a phillips head (looks like a + ), or does it look like a hex (5 flats) or a star ( * )?   If it is hex, then get a set of hex keys from your hardware store. If it is a star shape then get a set of TORX or STAR bits from your hardware store, or an automotive parts store. (Stars are used on cars)

If it is a phillips, be sure you have the right size phillips bit. It is very important to have the right size phillips bit to properly remove the screw, especially if it is very tight. The wrong size will just slip out and damge the slots so badly that even when you get the correct size it will not work.

Post a picture (macro mode or high res, make sure you focus) if you need more help.

### Post by podunk on 2007-10-03
That looks like a #1 square drive bit. For sure a square drive bit. Square drive bits are usually available at hardware and home center stores (Home Depot, Lowes). I've never seen a square drive "screwdriver" before (never looked for one), what you do is by a tool holder that looks like a screw driver that holds the bit.

I have a little kit that I got for around 10 bucks at Home Depot. It has the holder and a large assortment of bits, and comes with a little rinky dink plastic case to keep everything organized. It has come in very handy over the years.

To loosen a screw turn it counter clockwise as you look down on it.

Just my experience - but putting a laptop back together is much harder than taking it apart. :) The cover you are trying to take off doesn't look like it would be anywhere near big enough to cover the memory my laptop uses.
