### Post by jingo811 on 2006-03-14
As a newbie going from Windows to Linux always gets Windows more + points than Linux somehow.  No matter how much you hate the M$ monopoly Linux somehow manages to put Windows in a better light when going trough the process as a beginner, no matter how much you prepare yourself beforehand with reading material etc.
Unless that is, you have a contract with God and never ever encounter any technical difficulties in your life :rolleyes: then Linux would be like a cakewalk.
Which I've come to the conclusion I am not.  I think God has put a Voodoo Hex on me when it comes to stuff like this with computer hardwares :evil: [-( 
My stories about all my failures could fill a whole bible :mrgreen: but then who doesn't.  So instead I'm gonna share my first and hopefully not last happy Linux story with the world.
OK, here it goes....once upon a time in 1994 my family bought our very first PC an IBM Aptiva with a stupid Recovery CD, god I hate those things why do  you have to punish me like that? :) 
Then in 1997 we bought our second PC that was built for Internet and faster Multimedia, another barebone PC from Compaq I think the expression goes.
Then in 2004 I personally got myself a custom built monster machine built for CAD and 3D-gaming.

Now the '94 IBM has long been retired sitting in the basement since '97.  But now my newest monster CAD machine or it's **TFT 17"** screen to be more precise started to fail on me after only 1,5 years.
So I had to replace the monitor quick, I couldn't use my '97 PC monitor then the other PC would be without monitor.
So I went back to the basement and revived my old **'94 IBM CRT-monitor** from the dead.  Knowing that it wasn't certified by TCO and such which pretty much all modern monitors are today.  I was a little afraid that using the wrong screen resolution and frequency rate would make my '94 monitor burst into fire.  I've heard about stories like that before on the net.

What happened was when using the '94 IBM monitor with Windows XP the screen got all scrambled and duplicated, I could see 8 simultaneous mouse pointers moving on the screen.  I felt like I travelled back in time, back to the 70's or something , I was born '81 :mrgreen: 
According to my calculations replacing the malfunctioned TFT monitor with a brand new monitor will take me around 5 weeks so I still need to have monitor access under this whole period.  So if Windows XP wasn't reverse compatible with my IBM '94 CRT-monitor.  Then lets see if the same goes for Ubuntu: Breezy.  But my hopes wasn't too high having singe my wings before with Linux.  But when it booted Ubuntu handled the retired '94 monitor like a charm \\:D/ I didn't even have to change any settings.  
HuRaaaaah for Linux!
The only quirk is that I'm running on a monster CAD, 3D-gaming graphics card and the '94 IBM-monitor wont go to higher resolutions beyond 640x480 :-#
