### Post by kalwisti on 2008-02-23
Hi,

As a new Ubuntu user, I thought I'd mention another couple of books which have been very helpful to me:

Grant, Rickford. *Ubuntu for Non-Geeks*. 2nd ed. No Starch Press, 2007. ISBN 1593271522. $23.07 (Amazon.com)
Covers Feisty Fawn and includes a companion CD-ROM. Clearly written, geared towards beginners without insulting their intelligence or the sometimes lame humor of the "Dummies" series.

Sobell, Mark G. *A Practical Guide to Ubuntu Linux*. Prentice Hall, 2007. ISBN 013236039X. $31.49 (Amazon.com)
Haven't used this yet but am planning to order it; it got favorable reviews on Amazon. Includes companion DVD-ROM (not sure which version of Ubuntu it has).

Gagne, Marcel. *Moving to Ubuntu Linux*. Addison-Wesley, 2006. ISBN 032142722X. $23.09 (Amazon.com)
Includes companion DVD with Dapper Drake. Slightly older information, but Gagne writes well and covers what "real-life" beginners need to know to get up and running.

Try checking at your local public library; with some luck, it will own these titles and you can "try before you buy."
