### Post by tszanon on 2007-12-21
I'd say don't do it. Both MP3 and OGG are lossy audio formats, which means they discard some audio information during compression. In the end, no matter how good the OGG compression goes, it will not produce a better audio than your current MP3 file.

But, if you converting from a lossless audio format, like WAV or FLAC, then it's ok to do whatever you want. :)
