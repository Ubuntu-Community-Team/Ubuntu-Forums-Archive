Awesome.

My family is somewhat computer illiterate (but very intuitive) so I managed to get them used to ubuntu. All I did was show them Firefox, GAIM, Frostwire, and K3B and I have never had to help them after that. My sister even knows how to open up GIMP and use the scanner; and also if we are sharing the computer in one sitting, I can have all of my work open on one desktop and she could have hers open on another desktop, this, I think is one good sell for linux once they learn of that advantage.

### Post by Zeroangel on 2006-03-04
Oh wow, only 400mhz? Ok here goes anyways:

Ok, right now you have firefox open (and probably a few other apps), try clicking on the second square and opening up say... evolution (the email program). Now after you're done checking your mail, leave evolution open and go back to the first square and continue surfing.

Whenever you want to check your mail then your mail program will be open, but it will be on the second 'desktop' and you won't have to wait for it to load up again.

You can do this with all sorts of apps, like set one of the desktops for your music folder and your music player. That way your taskbar isnt all cluttered up. It's really good for multitasking.
