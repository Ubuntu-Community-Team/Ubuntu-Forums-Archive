### Post by shuttleworthwannabe on 2007-10-20
I will seconda that. Even though upgrading is supposed to be painless, it could go wrong, sometimes even horribly as stated above. The only advantage is that you retain your user preferences in your home directory, and documents. Otherwise, you will still have to download a huge MB (almost he size of a fresh install CD). 
 
My suggestion would be to:
 
1. Back-up you data (documents only)
2. If you have good internet connection, download the Live CD, and keep it (this will come handy when system crashes for some other reason just in case, even with a failsafe upgrade)
3. Try the upgrade option, and test it out
4. If it screws your system, use the live CD to install Gutsy
5. If it upgrade goes well, you are king
6. If you are having problems with dual-booting, seek help here. There are people here just waiting to help.
 
Good luck,
 
S
