### Post by moshuptrail on 2006-07-19
Okay this is a Ubuntu forum, and I love it, but in your case I can't recommend Ubuntu.  I've loaded it onto several PC's and the slowest I'd consider for Ubuntu or Xubuntu would be a 750Mhz Pentium.  I tried Xubuntu and was dissapointed.  It's basically Ubuntu with a lighter desktop, but the underlying OS is basically the same.  Based on Debian, which is a pretty comprehensive OS, any flavor of Ubuntu is going to have it's limitations on smaller PCs.
Having said that, I've been lurking around here for a few weeks and "lightweight linux" is a pretty common topic.  One that I've seen recommended several times is called "damn small linux", or just DSL.  But I've never tried it.  I tried something called Vector-Linux and it was pretty good, speedwise.  I think it's based on something called "slackware".  Check the Wiki to learn more about that.  Good Luck!
