### Post by DoricMan on 2007-05-12
I was in the middle of installing a Dual Boot Ubuntu 7.04 when I had to phone the Fire Service to extinguish a rapidly advancing scrub fire on our home. The point I had got to was fairly near the end when passwords were being installed. Some hours later I got back to the install and Ubuntu presented me with its password entry screen. I tried what I thought was the password and was locked out of the install. OK XP was available but it too was locked up in this password conundrum, and unreachable. 
My first thought was to search in Ubuntu Forums and began to understand what a problem I had. Knoppix Live CD gave me information about the affected MBR and GRUB but decided to chicken out with these data.
Ubuntu 7.04 was installed on a clean NTFS Hard Disk in its own partition.
I then turned to Paragon's  Partition Manager's "Recovery Media Manager" that lived on XP, and was in my emergency XP Repair CD. I found that I could repair the corrupted MBR, delete the Ubuntu Partition. After a reboot and some XP verification everything was back to normal.
There are some important lessons here for this Geriatric Newbie, avoid fires and have access to a solutions that  can recover from Password lockouts.
Hope this helps for those faced with similar disasters.:confused:
