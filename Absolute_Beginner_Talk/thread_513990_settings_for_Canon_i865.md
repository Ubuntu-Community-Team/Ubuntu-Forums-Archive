### Post by Benbow on 2007-07-31
During many posts I have questioned the ability of my canon printer, MP170, to print at all under Ubuntu. I eventually bought Turboprint which enables it to print basic type and spot colours but for photos or anything slightly intricate it is useless. A really bad software investment!!
I now use ubuntu for simple web printouts or text but _ALWAYS_ have to return to xp for photos etc. which is unfortunate but necessary.
The simple fact is that most printers offer many options to produce a finished product of your choice, but this invariably applies to windows only. The one option I think may be Hewlett Packard which everyone says support linux but whether this is a full support with print options like the windows version I haven't a clue!
This is a major issue for many people and an issue which keeps windows on machines purely to print successfully.
I have seriously considered buying an HP but which model to get and the risk of it being a "white elephant" puts me off. Plus the fact that replacing a perfectly good printer is wasting money.
I wish you luck with your i865 but don't hold your breath!
