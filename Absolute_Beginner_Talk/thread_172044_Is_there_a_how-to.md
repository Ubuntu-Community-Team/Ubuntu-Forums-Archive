### Post by Monster_user on 2006-05-07
Which version of Ubuntu you want to dual boot with? This is in the "Beginner" forum, and does not indicate which. *For the most part, the instructions will be the same, but there may be some differences to look out for.*

Also, what are your system specifications? Or the Brand, and Model #?

Ubuntu 5.10 Breezy Badger (Intel x86) is the Ubuntu by which all other sare judged. It uses the very different Gnome DE (Desktop Enviroment). A clean, and professional enviroment, designed to be functional, and easy to use. As well as NOT be a clone of something else.

Xubuntu uses XFCE. It seems to be "Ubuntu lite" as far as the resources it uses. XFCE is lighter, and often faster than Gnome. It lacks a lot of the extra features of Gnome though. It seems it would be great for older systems. I have not tried Xubuntu, so I do not know anything else about it.

Kubuntu uses KDE. It is the biggest, most tweakable desktop of the three. It can also be the slowest of the three, depending on various things. It is the closest to the "Windows" enviroment, and as such, it is the easiest for new users to learn.

KDE is the most popular DE in Linux.
