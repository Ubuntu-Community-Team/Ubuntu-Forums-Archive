### Post by Paqman on 2008-02-07
What exactly are you trying to do with Bluetooth?

Basically Bluetooth is designed to replace cables. If you have two Bluetooth-capable devices, they can communicate over short distances wirelessly. A good example would be transfering data from a Bluetooth mobile phone onto a computer. Typically that requires a Bluetooth adapter for the PC (either a card or a USB device), although some laptops come with Bluetooth built-in.

### Post by fiddledd on 2008-02-07
i'm also not sure exactly what you want to know, but here's a start at least :). 
I have a Mobile Phone that has Blutooth connectivity and that accepts standard .mp3 as Ringtones. Let's say i find/compose an mp3 and i want to use it as a Ringtone, I do the following:

Plug in a USB Bluetooth Dongle into my PC (pretty cheap about £10?)
Set my phone to connect to my PC via Bluetooth (settings vary by phone)
Transfer the .mp3(s) to my phone
Use the Ringtone.

There are other uses of course, but that's a start for you :)
