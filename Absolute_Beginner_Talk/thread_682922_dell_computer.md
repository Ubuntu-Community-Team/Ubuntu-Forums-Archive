### Post by blippy on 2008-01-30
Just received my Inspiron 530 with Ubuntu pre-installed.

Not sure if it's a one-off, but the system took 18 days to arrive, and the carriers delivered it to the wrong address. That's strike one.

The system itself performs admirably. Not as quiet as I'd like, though. I changed the BIOS settings to get it to run headless.

I booted the Dell up to see what it looked like - well, seen one Gutsy, seen them all ;) What I liked was that it had LinDVD - so you could play DVDs with it. After quarter of an hour I decided it was time to wipe the hard drive and install another Linux. I tried Zenwalk and Fedora. They seemed to freeze part-way through installation. Maybe they didn't freeze, but were "just resting". Oh well, I quickly tired of them and installed Gutsy server.

Upon reboot, it struggled through the boot process, and gave me something about
usb 3-1 device not blah blah error
and
at1.00: failed to set xfermode blah blah
Then it tried to do some other stuff, and just rebooted by itself.
I managed to change the boot paramater to include irqpoll, which seemed to fix it.
Still, room for improvement, methinks.

The machine was cheap,which is nice. Can't say I'm impressed with the boot weirdness, though. Dell seem to have this knack of introducing weirdness into their hardware.I would have liked to have seen more Just Workiness. I'm no expert at Linux by a long shot, but I can imagine someone fresh to Linux getting all excited at his new experimentation in Linuxland, only to feel a sudden pang of regret at having shelled out cashola on something that doesn't work. 

So, hmmm, Linux still not ready for the desktop. At least not the way Dell serves it up.
