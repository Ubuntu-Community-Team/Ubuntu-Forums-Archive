### Post by mivo on 2007-10-01
> **artir said:**
> Yeah. Wine is fine for anything with the exception of very advanced games. It runs WoW very well. Look here [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WorldofWarcraft]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WorldofWarcraft")

Many games that were released two, three or even four years ago do not work properly, and the same applies to current releases. World of Warcraft is an exception, but it is not really a "very advanced" game -- it has low hardware requirements, comparatively. For someone who plays a lot of games Linux is still not a very viable option. It prevented me from switching for quite some time, but nowadays I just play on consoles -- and for everything else I do, including work, Linux offers me much more comfort and performance.

There are some native Linux games, too. Unreal Tournament 2008 (release is in November) will come with a Linux version in the Windows retail box. Also in November there will be a Linux version of X3 (an Elite-style game). Both require good hardware, but don't need an emulator. Dominions 3 also has a Linux version. There are games for Linux,just fewer than for Windows. :) (Saves a lot of money.)
