### Post by maaronB on 2006-10-08
I would say it is more a comment on the development cycle.

I tend to agree that the release cycle needs to be changed.

The way it is now with the constant throwing of new features in while still pushing such a tight schedule, they will always have many minor problems.

What I wish the cycle was 
6 months new feature development; release
6 months pure refinement; rerelease
6 months new feature development; release
6 months refinement; rerelease
...

That they could spend those 6 refinement months placing all their resources into making sure printer and modem drivers work, finding and fixing bugs, and incompatibilites.
That way we would be guaranteed one truly stable release every year.
You might have to wait a year before you can get the newest and greatest features, but since this is supposed to be "Linux for human beings" the emphasis should probably not be on latest and greatest.

Plus if they followed that schedule they would never have to postpone a release.  People would expect the feature release to have bugs, so they could release it "as is" whenever they reach their release date.
Then they would have 6 months of bug hunting time to get the refinement release ready.
