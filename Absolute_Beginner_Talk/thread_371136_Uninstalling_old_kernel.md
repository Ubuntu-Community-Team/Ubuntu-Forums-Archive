### Post by go2dell on 2007-02-26
Unless you're really pressed for drive space, there's no real reason to remove the old kernel.  It will sit there ignored until/unless you decide to use it.  The reason it wasn't removed automatically was Just In Case.  If the new one doesn't work, you can always boot fine with the old one.

That said, it's sitting there ignored, just taking up drive space, and you can safely remove it via Synaptic or whatever method you prefer.
