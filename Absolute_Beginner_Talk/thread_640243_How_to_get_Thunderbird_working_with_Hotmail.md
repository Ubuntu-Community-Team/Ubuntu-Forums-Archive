### Post by SaddleTramp on 2008-04-05
The attached is a screenshot which I hope helps a little.

When adding the SMTP acct, once you click (highlight) Outgoing Server, then look up in right hand corner of box where your SMTP accts are listed.  It's here you add the SMTP acct.

Also, keep in mind that some systems block any port BELOW 1024.  If you're sure all settings (i.e. server, host, username, etc..) are correct by the instructions, try (in the web mail options first) setting the ports higher ( for mine I just made 110 to 1100 and 25 to 2500).  This may get you the green lites in web mail.  IMPORTANT:  Once you got green lights, you HAVE to go back under your port settings under your Thunderbird account for webmail and change those to correspond with webmail.  

And last but not least, when at first setup of Thunderbird acct where you get the mail handler window (think it lists something like email, gmail, etc...), select "email" as the instructions point out and NOT webmail.

Hope this helps.  I've decided to work on a complete pictorial guide for setting WebMail up with Thunderbird and will start tonite.
