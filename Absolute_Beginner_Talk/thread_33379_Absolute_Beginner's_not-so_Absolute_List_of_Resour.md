### Post by thegreedyturtle on 2005-05-10
Allright, since I don't see it in here yet, I guess it's up to me. I'll try and keep this list at least decently updated, and I give anyone with the ability, (read: moderators) permission to update and change this as they see fit, so don't be suprised if this has been edited by someone other than me.

Another page similar to this post is at [http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/HowToGetHelp]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/HowToGetHelp"), you can look there too.

One thing to keep in mind is that Ubuntu is a distribution of Linux, so many guides and howtos on the internet that are about Linux, or another distro of linux such as Fedora will also apply to Ubuntu. But you'll have to figure out for yourself what applies and what doesn't.

Since you are an Absolute Beginner, you might have absolutely no idea how forums work! :-P Well, I'm not going to get really deep into specifics here, but you should get a username so that you can post threads, which are these lists and their content, or reply to a thread. The next thing you should know about is the Search function, located at the top, but beneath the tabs. I strongly reccommend using this before you start posting threads, because people like to help, but people do get tired of answering the same question over and over! The next important detail about posting on forums is to try and be as specific as possible for your problem or question. If you aren't, the only answer you'll get is "Can you be more specific?" You also get questions on what they want to know, so don't be discuraged if you aren't sure. Also, make sure you are posting on the correct forum subsection! When there's so many people accessing the forums, it's really nice to keep things clean. Finally, be sure to abide by the [Ubuntu Code of Conduct]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/community/conduct") or in other words, respect the other people here!


Allright, getting to the gritty:
If you are totally out there, and have absolutely no idea what is going on with your computer and/or life, you can start with [http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/UbuntuHowCome]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/UbuntuHowCome") which is a creation by yours truly. It goes thru Linux basics without being too in depth. It's mostly a holdover until some real work gets done at [http://people.ubuntulinux.org/~mako/docteam/quickguide/]("http://people.ubuntulinux.org/%7Emako/docteam/quickguide/")

An excellent place to get information is at the Ubuntu wiki: [http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/FrontPage]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/FrontPage")
The front page is a little disconcerting right now, but that's being worked on as we speak.
The places you'll want to look into are going to be:
[http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/UserDocumentation]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/UserDocumentation") - Lots of application howtos
[http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/HardwareSupport]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/HardwareSupport") - See if your hardware works with Ubuntu
[http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/RestrictedFormats]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/RestrictedFormats") - see why your mp3's aren't playing, why, and how to get started on fixin' 'em.
[http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/EverydayUsage]("http://www.ubuntulinux.org/wiki/EverydayUsage") - This would be an awesome place for the Absolute Beginner... but there's really not much content right now. You can look, but don't get your hopes up for now.

You can connect to irc.freenode.net and join #ubuntu on irc. If you have Ubuntu installed, you can go to Applications -> Internet -> XChat, and it should spit you out in #ubuntu with the default settings. It'll even use your username as default.

Next, some really useful locations for you to look at:
[http://ubuntuguide.org]("http://ubuntuguide.org") is a cookbook of doing things in ubuntu. It won't really explain what's going on, but it'll certainly get your mp3s working! Take a look at the Add-On CD for those of you who don't have a decent internet connection.

[http://www.mrbass.org/linux/ubuntu/]("http://www.mrbass.org/linux/ubuntu/") is being worked on all the time, there's some really handy stuff in there.

I think as a final word, I should mention that you've taken a big step when you installed Ubuntu for the first time, and sometimes that can be scary. There isn't quite as big of a 'saftey net' as there is in Windows or Mac, because there is so much power given to Linux users. You've got every right and ability to fine tune your machine into just about anything, which also gives you the right to snafu the whole deal!

But what Ubuntu lacks in a safety net, it more than makes up in a community Net. Between the forums, the wiki, and just sites on the internet like ubuntuguide.org, chances are you'll find someone who can help you out, or at least point you in the right direction.

Good Luck, Humanity to Others, and Always Remember to Enjoy Life.
