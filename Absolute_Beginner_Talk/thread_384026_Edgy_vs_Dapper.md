### Post by jem7v on 2007-03-14
Advantages of Dapper:
- It's a long-term supported release

Advantages of Edgy:
- Software has more recent versions available in Edgy repositories vs Dapper
- Supposed speed increases
- Definite Gnome stability improvements
- Newer kernel and Xorg server
- Easier to set up pretty visual effects

Disadvantages of Edgy:
- If you upgrade to a new release (any new release) you'll find that a lot of your programs get uninstalled in the progress, so you'll want to make a list of everything you had installed before you upgraded so you can re-install them later.


However, if you wait about 5 weeks for Feisty 7.04 and then another week or two for them to iron out the initial bugs, you could probably list the same improvements of Feisty over Edgy.  To make the move from Dapper to Feisty you would either have to Upgrade to Edgy and then also upgrade to Feisty, or just do a clean install of Feisty over Dapper.  (When UPGRADING you can't skip releases, but if you're just doing a clean install over top it doesn't much matter; note that upgrades usually work less well than clean installs.)  Feisty should supposedly have much improved hardware support out of the box and improved stability in the Xorg system.  We'll see.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-03-14
I like the LTS feature of Dapper.  You can always try the Edgy Live CD and see what works and what doesn't.  You must realize that some architectural changes have taken place so you may experience some hardware problems.  Most of these are fixable.

I have a music server that has been running for 140 days straight on LTS.  I have several overdue upgrades, but since they don't impact serving media files, I'm not worried about it.

Older hardware tends to work OK with older kernels.  Brand new hardware will generally need the latest uncompiled kernel to function properly.  So the Dapper--Edgy--Feisty decision depends on your hardware level and your patience to fix things.

Buy a cheap USB drive to put Edgy  and Feisty on it and triple boot.  Dapper when it absolutely has to work.  Edgy when you want to check out newer stuff and Feisty if you want to help the community and post what works and what's broken.

Either way, having a triple boot machine will help out the community because you can share with us your hardware configuration and what problems you experience with all three distributions.

Welcome to the community and good luck.
