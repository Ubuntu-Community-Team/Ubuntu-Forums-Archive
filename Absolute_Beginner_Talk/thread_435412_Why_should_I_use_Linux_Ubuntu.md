### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-06
Why should you switch? If Windows works for you, keep using it.

But you can try out Ubuntu without *switching* to Ubuntu. The Desktop CD you download of Ubuntu is a "live CD" and runs an entirely fully functioning Ubuntu session from the CD itself and your computer's RAM *without affecting your hard drive*. Just try it out and see for yourself. No commitments. If you like it, you can always install it. And if you don't like it, just reboot your computer with the CD out of the drive.

### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-06
Well, the bottom line is that if XP or any other windows install works for you keep it (I still have my XP install though I only boot into it once in a while for a really old game wine hates). Some people just aren't ready to use Ubuntu, its a culture shock. I wouldn't say things are harder, things are different. Just like if you try and learn hebrew after speaking english for 40 years. However, if you talk to a person who has spoken and written hebrew his whole life, he'll likely say its like riding a bike and its easy. So, just like that Ubuntu is only different, some people just don't have the will or desire to relearn an OS.

As for why do people switch? There are quite a few reasons for that. Linux is very easy to maintain, requires no defrags, no spyware scans, no av scans, or really any other maintenance and is very stable (in direct comparison I can definitely say I've crashed more in xp than in linux for same time period). Some people choose Ubuntu because they believe open source to be the future or their preference (for instance, I was using Firefox and Openoffice and a whole host of OSS on windows before I switched). Some people find that they've been treated badly by microsoft and other close source developpers (WGA for instance has invalidated Millions, yes thats a fact, of legitimate copies of windows around the world, I personally had two friends invalidated for no reason). Or maybe they just want to have the ability to do whatever they want without feeling like they are breaking rules, to hack and customize their PC at will.

There are lots of reasons, there isn't a single one that will be like "OMG you MUST get Ubuntu", equally though I would say there isn't any super compelling reason to use windows XP or even more so, upgrade to Vista. So, make your decision, its up to you. We are here to answer any questions you have though.

### Post by gantww on 2007-05-07
I was very much on the fence for a while about Linux (of any kind, not just the Ubuntu variety). I had installed Edgy on an old Dell laptop that I had. When I upgraded laptops, I gave the old one to my wife (to replace the ancient Mac that she had). 6 months later, I was screwing around with Fiesty in a VM, when it occurred to me that we even had that laptop at all. From this, the following points are very important.

1) If she had a windows laptop, it would have been totally owned from missing updates for that long.
2) No flakiness happened at all in that time, else she would have asked me to fix it.
3) Everything she needed was on the machine and was easily accessible. 
4) I'm not going to be forced to upgrade to Fiesty just because it is out and some company wants revenue. True, support will eventually end for any particular version, but when the upgrade is free, it's not such a big deal.
5) Switching from XP to Linux improved the battery life on that laptop by around 40%. I still have no idea why. The machine also seems to run cooler, although I don't have actual temperature readings to back me up. Regardless, it is a very good improvement.

I switched from Windows 95 to 98 because 98 was cooler. I switched from 98 to 2000 because 2000 was more stable. I switched from 2000 to XP because support ended. I'm switching from XP to Linux because as an IT person, I love the fact that a computer can be so low maintenance that I forget I own it. Granted, it wasn't the easiest thing in the world to set up, but it is SOLID once you have it configured. From what I've seen of Vista, it takes at least as many hacks and workarounds to get running as Ubuntu does , plus it is bloated, loaded with DRM garbage, and is not a cheap upgrade.

Just my 2 cents,
Will
