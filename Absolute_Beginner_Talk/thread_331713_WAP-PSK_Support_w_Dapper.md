### Post by imonlyhuman on 2007-01-05
Install network-manager-gnome through synaptic, or through terminal, (possibly after reboot) you will see a new icon on the top taskbar, left (not right) click on it, choose your network, type in your PW, and your all set.

I had a lot of trouble with wpa and linux, until I read on a forum to do the above.  :-D 
Ever since then life has been good.

EDIT: That is assuming your wifi card is working. (All drivers are already set up)
