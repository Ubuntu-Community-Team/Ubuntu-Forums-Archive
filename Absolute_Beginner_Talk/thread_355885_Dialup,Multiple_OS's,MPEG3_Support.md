### Post by Bartender on 2007-02-07
I did a quick google of "canada isp".  Here's a place to start -
 
[http://www.freedomlist.com/find.php3?country=148](http://www.freedomlist.com/find.php3?country=148)

As mentioned, you're looking for one that doesn't use any proprietary software.  You didn't say anything about your modem, but it's highly unlikely that your modem's gonna work out of the box in Linux anyway.

Linux is much more compatible with DSL services than it is with dial-up.  Lots of times you'll talk to someone at the ISP who doesn't know anything beyond Windows and you'll get the wrong info.  Also, 99% of them will say they don't support Linux.  That doesn't mean it won't work.  Got a friend on DSL?  Check to see that your network card is enabled, lug your PC over to their house, plug the ethernet cable in before starting the PC, and see if you can go online.  If you can't, or the connection is very flaky, don't give up.  Might be as simple as [disabling]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=87798") IPV6.

If you can go online, and can do that for a few hours, check into Automatix for getting the multimedia stuff you need for mp3's.

Partitioning is a complex subject.  You should do some research, decide how you want to partition, how many partitions you want to make, etc.  Tough to give you one quick answer on that.
