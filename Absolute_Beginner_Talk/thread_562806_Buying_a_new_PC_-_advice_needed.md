### Post by mivo on 2007-09-29
ATI just released the specs of their cards to the public, so it seems likely that we will soon see better ATI drivers. If you do go with an Nvidia card, the 8300GS is a sub-optimal choice. See if they have a 7900GT or a 7950GT, or at least a 8600GTS, though I'd stick to the 7er series for a Linux computer. The 8er series are DX10 cards. Higher numbers don't mean better performance with Nvidia cards (i.e., the 7950GT is significantly more powerful than the 8600GTS. You might also want to check on the type of cooling the card has ... passive cooling is best for your purpose (noiseless).

See here for Nvidia card comparisons -- look at memory bandwidth, tact, etc.:

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_NVIDIA_Graphics_Processing_Units](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_NVIDIA_Graphics_Processing_Units)
