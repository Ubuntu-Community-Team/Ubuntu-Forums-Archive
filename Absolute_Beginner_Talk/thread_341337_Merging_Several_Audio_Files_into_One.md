### Post by zerhacke on 2007-01-21
> **tonyr1988 said:**
> I tried playing the CD in a regular CD player and it plays the same (tons of tracks). I then looked and noticed that on the back, there are small numbers that correspond to when that song starts. They split the songs up on purpose. I'm assuming there's a reason, but for what I need to do in the class it's not necessary.

There is a reason.  Most classical music is done in movements instead as just a simple track here and a track there.  Each movement is further separated into sections of the movement.  What your CD has done is turn each section of a movement into a track.  Each section and movement has a proper name.

I don't know why they would have done that other than to give a better distinction between sections and movements.  It's not like you listen to just one section of a movement without listening to others - you're not supposed to anyways.
