### Post by Goober on 2005-10-04
The process is the same for Laptops as it is for Desktops. Burn the ISO to a CD, set your Lappie to boot from CD in the BIOS (if not so already), put in  the CD, reload 'er, then follow the steps on the CD.

### Post by brentoboy on 2005-10-04
If your laptop uses wifi dont expect thatto be functional right from the get go.  It might, in fact, it probably will, but if you *need* it to in order to have an internet connection, you might be in a bad spot.

Other than that, I havent seen many laptop isses, Ive put it on 4 different models.

one word of advice: use the info on ndiswrapper website to find your wifi chipset (if it doesnt work right after bootup) dont use the driver that came on the driver disk that came with the wireless card. (unless it has a linux driver)

the ndiswrapper website on sourceforge can help you find your chipset, and also help you get the most stable driver for your chipset.

power managment doesnt always do what you might hope without tweekage.
