I found one free converter that does the job right every time, WinAVI Video Converter version 8.  Here's the download link:

[http://www.winavi.com/WinAVI_Video_Converter.exe](http://www.winavi.com/WinAVI_Video_Converter.exe) 

To make it work right you have to have these encoding settings under the Advanced options tab: 

Video: ZJMedia Uncompress RGB 565  
Audio: IMA ADPCM  
Video Frame Rate: 15  
Picture Size: width: (128 hight: 96)

Using the lowest settings for audio works fine and reduces the file size somewhat. I've been leaving AC3 unchecked but it may not matter. A typical 700 MB movie file converts to a Zen V Plus playable file of around 2 GB.

With the unregistered WinAVI version you get a perfectly working video of any length you want with no watermarks.  The only other software I could find that worked at all, Daniube?, charges $25 for the program and the video always cuts out after 41 minutes but leaves the audio still working.  You don't get any problems like that with WinAVI at the settings I gave.
