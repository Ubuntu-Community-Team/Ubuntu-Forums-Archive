### Post by WedgeWouldUseLinux on 2007-08-31
I'm not exactly here because of Vista problems, but I'm also not exactly Vista's biggest fan.

If your computer is older than about 4 years, then you will probably have an easier time getting everything running under Ubuntu than Vista. With Linux there is always hope for getting something working. With Vista there is none.

If you have a brand new computer running a high-end (ATI especially) video card, then Vista will be easier to set up.

And if you can get good Vista drivers for your hardware, its going to beat the pants off most Linux setups as far as top-of-the-line hardcore gaming goes.

So basically, if you have an older computer and some time to mess around with settings, Linux could be a better option. And no matter what system you have, its more fun than Vista.


Hope this helps!

### Post by anewguy on 2007-08-31
Download the LIveCD and try it out - it won't change anything on your disk - you'll just be running from the CD.:)

Try your sound, your internet, etc., while on the LiveCD and note anything that doesn't work, then search this forum for help on those things so you are prepared if you do make the switch to Linux.  Having a little bit of idea of what you will need to fix before you install can make the process a lot less aggravating!:)

### Post by anewguy on 2007-09-01
This will sound pretty basic, but here goes:

1.  Make sure you burned the CD as an image, not a file, and don't worry about making it bootable.  The ISO image for the LIveCD will make it bootable when you've burned the CD.

2.  Check your BIOS and be sure it is set to try booting from the CD before moving on the the hard disk.

If these don't work, let me know, but I have to forewarn you that I know nothing about Vista so you'll probably need someone else's help beyond those points.:)
