### Post by drummer on 2005-09-28
1) Add/remove programs shows just a small selection of common apps that might need installing.. for the full shebang, open up Synaptic (System > Administration > Synaptic Package Manager) and in there you can search for any package you like. also make sure that you've enabled the extra repositoies per ubuntuguide.org

2) open up a terminal and type in "killall gnome-panel" (without quotes) and press enter. The K3B icon should appear in the Applications menu (probably under the accessories submenu) once gnome-panel is reloaded.

3) updating to breezy will keep all your personal files (ie. /home) and should keep all the settings. I'll be backing up anyway just in case, better safe than sorry.

Hope that helps, and have fun with Ubuntu ;)
