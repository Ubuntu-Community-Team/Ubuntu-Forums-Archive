### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-03
The quick answer to this question, if you want to be able to play games with 0 trouble and without any extra effort, don't delete your windows partition. Not trying to stop you from switching, but DON'T expect Wine/Cedega to be a perfect solution for playing games, a lot of people expect things to work exactly like when they were on windows and thats just an unrealistic expectation. 

Now that thats done, I will direct you to [WINE]("http://appdb.winehq.org/") thats the database and you can find any game you want and how well its supported. [Cedega]("http://cedegawiki.sweetleafstudios.com/wiki/Main_Page") is also an option, you do have to pay for it though (*cough* torrent *cough*) it however is a bit better at supporting games.

As for installing guide. Read through this, and think about it. Ask questions about anything you don't get :).
