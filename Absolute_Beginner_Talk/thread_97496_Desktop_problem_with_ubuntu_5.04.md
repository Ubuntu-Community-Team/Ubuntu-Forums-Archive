### Post by ChrisTheGeek on 2005-12-01
Hi,

I've had similar problems and I know it's possible to get the taskbar back so no need to worry.

I think it may just be a case of restarting the gnome display manager - maybe somebody else who knows more than me will comment about this.

Can you still see the top panel? (the thing along the top with the Applications menu etc)

If so, we can just re-create the bottom panel for you.  To do this, right click anywhere there is an empty space on the top panel, and choose "New Panel".  You will then have a new panel appear which you can move to the bottom of the screen.

To make this new panel exactly like the vanished taskbar, simply add the applets you want to it.  Applets are little programs that sit in panels and do a particular job.  So, the menus you see at the top are just an applet, the icon to show the desktop is an applet, the list of open applications is also just an applet.

You need to add the following applets by right clicking and choosing "Add to Panel"

Show desktop
Task Switcher
Workspace Switcher
Trash can

I'm sorry if the names aren't quite right, I'm not at an Ubuntu box right now.  Hopefully this will fix you up at least temporarily!

Good luck :)
