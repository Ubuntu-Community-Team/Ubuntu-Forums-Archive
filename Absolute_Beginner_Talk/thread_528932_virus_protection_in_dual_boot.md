Thanks - YP

There is at this time no need for AV to protect Ubuntu. So I would say don't worry about AV on your Ubuntu install. Yes, you will need to install AV in windows (while in windows) as that is probably the best way to protect that OS.

If you still want AV for Linux, look at ClamAV. It is great.

Virus protection packages may or may not conflict. The usual rule is to use only one in each installed OS.

For a great intro to security, [see here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=510812").
