### Post by andrew.46 on 2007-08-24
Hi,

 Read your comments about ogg:

> **YoungCthulhu said:**
>  [...] For Linux rippers try GRIP and GOOBOX.  SOUND JUICER is OK but I can't figure out how to control the bit-rate when you are ripping OGG files.

I have spent some time recently with ogg and usually encode at -q 6. The manual states:

```
       -q n, --quality=n
              Sets encoding quality to n, between -1 (very low) and  10  (very
              high).  This  is  the  default mode of operation, with a default
              quality level of 3. Fractional quality levels such  as  2.5  are
              permitted.  Using  this  option  allows the encoder to select an
              appropriate bitrate based on your desired quality level.

```

 Quality 6 encodes at about 190 kbps.

                      Andrew
