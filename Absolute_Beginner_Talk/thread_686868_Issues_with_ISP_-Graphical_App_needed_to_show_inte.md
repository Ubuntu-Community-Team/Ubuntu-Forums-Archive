As i said previously he best way to check your true download speeds (without the graphs) is to download some large files at different times of the day.
Make sure you use servers you know capable of 8Mb...such as MS:)
I use any of [these](http://mirrors.virginmedia.com/) but then they are *on* Virgins own servers so i dont know how well they would work for you.
Fully capable of 20Mb though,even with just the one DL.
Of course if your DL from newsgroups then *that* will surely give a proper indication:-\"

Use the "Down Them All" FF extension or any other good multi threaded download manager for the multiple connections as you`ll notice the difference between FF`s own DL manager and the likes of Down Them All.
Even wget from the terminal is a better option.

I`m not sure if you have any monthly usage allowance with Tiscali but if you can leave a few ISO`s downloading till they finish then you will get an average speed.
OR.....just take note of the speeds once their off & running then cancel the particular download/s  so as not to reach any potential Bandwidth allowances you may have.

Do that in the morning,noon(if possible) and night,as well as weekends,while taking Screenshots of each terrible result.IF thats what your looking for anyway....Proof:wink:

You have to remember that you signed up to a relatively crappy(imo) BB provider with an "UP TO 8Mb`s."
I know friends,family & neighbors with Tiscali and none of them get anywhere near their "UP TO 8Mb`s".......certainly not during the hours that matter  anyway.

Once/If you start getting toooo concerned about seeing your full 8Mb`s it can become a bit of an obsession and you will find yourself spending more time doing speed checks than actually *using* your connection.
I went and upgraded from a perfectly adequate 4Mb to 10Mb,thinking that me & the kids,with 4 or 5 pc`s online at the same time would *need* that much.
Then Virgin upgraded that 10Mb tier to a 20Mb tier and the reality is that i never come close to *needing* it all.
I will not be upgrading to their upcoming 50Mb.....unless i get a similar type price:-\"

Our 1Mb Set Top Box there is just as good if truth be told although the upgrade to the original 4Mb Stand Alone Cable Modem was more than adequate for us all......even *if* every pc in the house was in use,which is very rare(unless i`m tinkering).
