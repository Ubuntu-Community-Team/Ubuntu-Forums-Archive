### Post by TreverT on 2007-10-28
I am new to Ubuntu - just set up a dual-boot with my XP install, but ideally I'd like to do virtually all of my work in Ubuntu.  I'm still working my way through the various "XP alternatives" programs (I already used OpenOffice, FF, Tbird, etc) but there's one wonderful item that seems unavailable in Linux - Mailwasher.

I have a commercial email account and get a pile of spam every day as a result.  In XP, Mailwasher would connect to my email accounts, apply some excellent filters, and present me with a list of the email on the server.  Then I could mark friends, blacklist if needed, and click 'Process mail' and Mailwasher would delete all the junk off the server without my ever having to bother downloading it to Thunderbird.  Excellent way to avoid viruses and such, too.  

By comparison, Thunderbird's junk mail filters are woefully inadequate and only work on email that's already been downloaded, anyway.  I saw there was an extension called Spamato which is supposed to improve this, but after installing it, it doesn't seem to do anything and I can't even tell if it's working.  

Is there any good, SIMPLE solution to heavy spam filtering under Linux?  I've seen a few posts that talk in great detail about setting up and running server scripts and proxies and all that, but that is WAY over my head.

Thanks in advance for any help you can provide!

### Post by kc8hr on 2007-10-28
Hi,

There are a lot of  anti-spam solutions that work well.

Like you, I didn't care for Spamato, but there is another Tbird extension that I have used to good effect called Thunderbayes

[https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/thunderbird/search?q=thunderbayes&status=4]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/thunderbird/search?q=thunderbayes&status=4").

There are also several e-mail filters available in the repositories:

Bogofilter
Bsfilter
Mailfilter
Pop3browser
Popfile
Spamassassin

These can be tricky to set up, but work well. According the the description in Synaptic, Pop3browser seems to be closest to mailwasher.

Good Luck!
Tim
