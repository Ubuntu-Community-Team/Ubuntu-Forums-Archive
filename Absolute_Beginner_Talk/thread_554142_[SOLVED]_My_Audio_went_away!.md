### Post by jamvegan on 2007-09-18
I don't really have an answer for you (I've been searching for one, myself, for years), but I may have a fix.  One of the computers that I have Ubuntu installed on has the same issue that you describe.  When I first installed Ubuntu (and, actually, this happened with other Linux distributions that I used previously on the machine, too) sound worked, but after rebooting it didn't.  What I have found that I have to do is adjust the sound level--up or down, it doesn't matter--in TWO places.  For instance, I can adjust the volume in the top menu bar *and* in xmms (the music player that I use), and then I have sound.  If I just adjust one or the other (or neither), no sound.  It's irritating, but as I said, I can't find a better solution.  I'll be avidly watching this thread hoping that someone volunteers one. :-)
