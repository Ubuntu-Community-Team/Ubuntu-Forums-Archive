### Post by Moonbuzz on 2005-11-03
Hardly the most popular, besides, I'm not really sure what you mean by "most popular".

Most known? I doubt any survey you'd conduct will come up with Ubuntu at the top. My guess will be Red Hat. Even if you limit your survey to people who are familiar with Linux, you'd get names like SUSE and Red Hat first, followed by Debian and one of the other commercial distros (Mandrake, Xandros, Linspire). 

Most loved? Probably by its users, but I think the communitee will probably hail Debian first. Another side of this is that the more "complicated" distros (in the vain of Gentoo) are usually those with the better "hard-core" buzz, and will be more popular in any such survey.

Most used? How do you even measure that? Unlike proprietary software, you can't get "real numbers" here. Distro X can publish Y downloads from its site. How many of those were later installed, and used? on how many computers (you don't need a license per machine, remember, download once, install as many times as you like)? How about people that upgraded their distro, but did'nt download any cd. You can't really tell. Ubuntu sends you a pack of 5 CDs. That doesn't mean they sold 5 licenses, or that they have 5 new users. It means that Ubuntu is now installed on between 0 and several dozens of computers. You can't measure free software use.

To sum it, Red Hat and SUSE have a better brand recognition, and outsell every other distro (mostly because others are free, but still). Debian and Gentoo have better "creds". Most businesses will probably go Mandrake, Linspire, SUSE and (most likely) Red Hat. And I haven't even started mentioning stuff like FreeBSD.
The Distro Watch numbers are fine, but not really reflecting. Red Hat is number 26, but Fedora is 4, makes sense for a site that mostly concerns people who are interested in downloading distros rather than seeking which gives them the best, overall business solution. Damn Small Linux is 8, which is a bit high for a distro mostly used as a backup/rescue disk, but then again, it's one that every one probably downloaded, for that exact reason.

I can go on and analyse the distrowatch list, but bottom line: it doesn't indicate anything. So, which is the most popular? Dunno, really.
