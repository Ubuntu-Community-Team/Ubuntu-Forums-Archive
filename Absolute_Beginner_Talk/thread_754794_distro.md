### Post by northern lights on 2008-04-14
In the Ubuntu family, there's [Fluxbuntu]("http://fluxbuntu.org/"), using Fluxbox as its desktop manager, which is way more simplistic than GNOME or KDE.
There's also [Xubuntu]("http://www.xubuntu.org"), running Xfce. It will run with 64 megs of RAM, but recommended are 128.

You also could consider [Puppy Linux]("http://www.puppylinux.org") or [DSL]("damnsmalllinux.org").

What are your systems specs?
