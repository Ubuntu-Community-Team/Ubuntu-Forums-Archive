### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-11
I used a Pharos 360 (SirFstar II chip) from MS Streets and Trips with Dapper on an old Dell Inspiron with GPSdrive.  If buying new, I would look for a waterproof SirFstar III chip for better in-car sensitivity.

If your car has a coated window (like BMW, Mercedes) then you may need an external mount antenna or stick it through the moonroof.  Then you'll want a magnetic mount as well.

As in-car NAV units are coming down in price, you're better off just buying a Tom, Tom, or MiO or any of the dozen in-car units available.  GPSdrive is a great framework and I have used it for playing around, but you will find better navigation integration with the hand-held, in-car units.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-11
You can find SirfIII, waterproof, USB, with magnetic base for around $80 US.  I don't have the link handy it's on another machine, but you can find several sources with a simple web search.

If you want to save money, check craigslist.org for old copies of MS Streets and Trips with the SirfStar II USB antenna.  They usually go for $15 to $45.  The maps go out of date quickly so people dump the software--or they find it's too clumsy to navigate with (it really is).  Mapquest or google maps is so much easier--and current.
