### Post by happysmileman on 2007-12-09
> **hiloguy said:**
> Several times a week, I get a notice that there are new updates to install.  Somehow this brings to mind some unpleasant memories of Windows and I'm having visions of my once sweet little Ubuntu 7.04 O/S becoming bigger and BIGGER . . . and why? These updates are updates to anything you installed through Add/Remove Programs, or anything that came with Ubuntu. Ubuntu (and most Linux distributions) will keep track of ALL your programs and offer to update them, unlike Windows, which only updates Windows itself, and leaves it up to you to update everything else

Most likely these are mainly updates for your programs such as Firefox and all, most will not actually be for Ubuntu itself. (Though the update to 7.10 is)

Generally you should get the updates, they will help to secure your system, but none of the updates are ever mandatory, and you're free to update some programs and leave others, or just not update anything (but I can't see any reason to do that)

### Post by CaptainInsaneO on 2007-12-09
Updates in Ubuntu don't work like they do in Windows.

The windows updates are normally whole files that are used to secure your machine from certain holes they've figured out in the main system.

In Ubuntu, the updates you get are usually there to update your individual programs. Updating your Linux system is like updating a program you're using from version 1 to version 2. It definitely doesn't take up as much space as WU.

### Post by meborc on 2007-12-09
most of the upgrades are upgrades of programs in your computer... very small part are security updates... the reason why you get a lot of updates is that you have a lot of programs installed on your computer... and since open source environment is constantly improving itself, it constantly updates the programs available through synaptic (apt)... as soon as new versions are in the repositories, you will get notified, as we all like to stay on the edge of new soft :)

the size of the programs usually does not change... for example when upgrading from terminal you could see something like "need to download 1432 kB data... extra storage needed 0 kB" meaning that you will download an update, that REPLACES the old program/library :)

if i were you, i would not upgrade to 7.10 unless you really need some new features included in 7.10... i would wait for the 8.04 LTS release, which will probably be as stable as a rock... "don't fix what is not broken" :D
