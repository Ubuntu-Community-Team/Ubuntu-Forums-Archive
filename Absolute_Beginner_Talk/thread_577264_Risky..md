### Post by mbro96 on 2008-01-09
Do you have to install a program/(the correct term may be "driver")?
     -Sometimes. I didn't when I used my NETGEAR wg111v2 wireless adapter, but be warned... Tux(the Linux penguin) usually prefers to use Ethernet and not wireless, so I suggest trying the LiveCD(normal CD) to see if it detects your wireless adapter/network modem. Also, if it doesn't work, I recommend to go buy a LinkSys router. Something that you /know/ works with Linux (there are Linux-certified products; such as LinkSys).

Is UPDATING hard?
    The answer to that is a definite "No! Hurah! Hurah!" :) 
          You will get a message on the top bar of your screen that says updates are     available, and then it will know what all programs you have installed, then update EVERYTHING (unless you uh-check something you /don't/ want updated, of course).
