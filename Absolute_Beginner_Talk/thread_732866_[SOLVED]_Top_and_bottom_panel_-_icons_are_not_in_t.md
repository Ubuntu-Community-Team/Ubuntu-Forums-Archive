### Post by zoopzoop on 2008-03-23
After setting the correct resolution for my monitor (1680 x 1050), the stuff on the right in the top panel and the bottom panel is not really in the right corner but a bit away from that (see screenshot).
In the bottom panel, the icons to change desktops are in the right corner, but the trash icon is somewhere near the middle. In the top panel, the panel with the clock, logged in user and the "system tray" icons (how is this called in Ubuntu?) are to far towards the middle as well.
I saw on several screenshots from other Ubuntians that it should be in the corner... I also tried to find a way to move the panels but couldn't find any...
Anyone know how to fix this?

---

### Post by jken146 on 2008-03-23
Right click on the items on the panel and use the 'lock' and 'move' options to unlock and move them.  "System tray" is called "notification area".

You can move the panels with right click -> properties I believe.

---

### Post by zoopzoop on 2008-03-23
Yay! Thanks, that worked!
The notification area icons cannot be moved separately, though, but you can click on the left border of it (two dashed lines) and move the whole panel.
