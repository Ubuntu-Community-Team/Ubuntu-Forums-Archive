### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-10-10
Install Conky: [http://conky.sourceforge.net/](http://conky.sourceforge.net/)
(it's also in synaptic)


then customize it with these pages:
[http://conky.sourceforge.net/config_settings.html](http://conky.sourceforge.net/config_settings.html)
[http://conky.sourceforge.net/variables.html](http://conky.sourceforge.net/variables.html)

This is also a good guide for installing it, although it's pretty old and out of date: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=205865](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=205865)


You can also learn how to make your own gtkrc themes. Just study your /usr/share/themes/gtk-2.0/gtkrc files that you already have, like human or clearlooks, then make your own with new colors that you want. You can also download other theme engines like murrine and aurora, and add the theme packs and/or get your own themes from Gnome-Looks.org or Xfce-Looks.org:

[http://murrine.netsons.org/](http://murrine.netsons.org/)
[http://gnome-look.org/](http://gnome-look.org/)
[http://www.xfce-look.org/](http://www.xfce-look.org/)
[http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=100](http://gnome-look.org/index.php?xsortmode=high&page=0&xcontentmode=100)
[http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Aurora+Gtk+Engine?content=56438](http://gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Aurora+Gtk+Engine?content=56438)


You can also try to learn how to compile from source, and install something that you want that's not in the repository.


And then there are always the speed hacks to make everything faster:

This is a list of different links:
[http://nighthacker.blogsome.com/2007/08/05/how-to-speed-up-ubuntu/](http://nighthacker.blogsome.com/2007/08/05/how-to-speed-up-ubuntu/)


I found this one for sidux, but it's basically what all the other lists have, just in one straight through list:
[http://sidux.com/PNphpBB2-viewtopic-t-3484-start-0.html](http://sidux.com/PNphpBB2-viewtopic-t-3484-start-0.html)
