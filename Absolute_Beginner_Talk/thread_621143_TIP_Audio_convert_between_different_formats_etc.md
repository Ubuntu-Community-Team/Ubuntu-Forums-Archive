### Post by hans796 on 2007-11-23
Hi just want to share my experience with different audio formats and converting between them and archiving or backing up CD's for future use.

Here are some experience:

Never vonvert between lossy formats (mp3 -> ogg or vice versa), you loose kvalite every time.

If you like to backup CD's: use lossfree formats: APE, FLAC or whatever suits you.

If you convert a FLAC filer to mp3, keep the flac file, burn it to CD, or else you will regret it sooner or later. I have.

If you rip to mp3, rip in the highest bitrate. EG 320 or --preset insane. Otherwise you also be sorry sooner or later.

My music-collection is huge. But almost all of them i have in FLAC. In insures me of the highest possible sound kavalite if i prefer that. Now my diskspace isn't to big so i converts or rip my music into mp3, always keeping a backup in Flac or Ape, burning theme on DVD.

Happy ubuntuing from sweden.
