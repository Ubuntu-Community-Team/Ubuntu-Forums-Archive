### Post by Zimmer on 2006-03-01
There are ALWAYS problems, but...
if you are saving files created by the OpenOffice suite in the relevant MS Office formats then the answer is 'mostly' yes.
However, when you open these files in the relevant MS Office app. they may not  quite match what you had in OpenOffice for a variety of reasons.
eg. fonts: you may have used a font in OO that is not present on the windows PC and not embedded it in the document (something that can also happen from Windows PC to Windows PC).


macros in Calc: macros written in OO calc will not work in excel format on MS Office, and vice versa.

If you have to move a lot of spreadsheet data about then try using the .csv (comma separated values) format which takes up less disk space and is easily imported into many cross platform applications.

It all depends on your needs and expectations as to whether you find OO 'great to use' . Personally, it works for me...I got ripped off when I bought a new computer with XP some time ago, and discovered my W95 MS Office would not work withXP.. expensive upgrade... nope, installed OO. Hence my dalliance with LINUX, as I don't want to get ripped off by MS again....

### Post by secular on 2006-03-01
"Saving As" an MS Office file works pretty well in OpenOffice most of the time. And opening most MS Office files in OpenOffice work well, too. The compatibility is strong, but not everything  works exactly in one as it does in the other. 

The weaknesses of OpenOffice are its slow start times and inability to translate some of MS Office's advanced features--which most people don't use, anyway. One of its strengths, though, is its Draw program. As a student, I'm sure you would find great uses for it in many of your courses.

I use OpenOffice exclusively at home and at work  and have had only one instance of something I created in either not working in the other. 

I suspect  that differences in the two office suites don't matter much to most of us but matter quite a lot to a few. In my case, the differences are minimal, and I'm quite happy using OpenOffice for every task I used to do in MS Office.

I hope my anecdotal words here help you in some way.
