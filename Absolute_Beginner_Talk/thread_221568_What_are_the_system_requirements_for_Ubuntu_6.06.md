### Post by catlett on 2006-07-23
I've never seen an Ubuntu document that states the required resources but I find Dapper is about equal to XP when it comes to required resources. Ram is the biggest thing. Gnome is pretty ram intensive so if you were to go with Ubuntu or Kbuntu I would say at least a PIII 500mhz with 256mb of ram.
I have run Dapper on a computer with that exact specifications and it did fine. Obviously the more ram the better and the faster your processor the better but 500mhz chip with 256mb ram can get it done.
Anything less and it will seem sluggish. If you had a P2 and 128mb then Xubuntu would be a better choice. Xubuntu has XFCE as the window manager  which is less ram intensive.

### Post by Bartender on 2006-07-23
My sig PC runs a standard Ubuntu 6.06 install fine.  I can play ripped tunes from the HDD and do other stuff simultaneously.  Music hiccups every once in a while if I do too many things at once, but pleasantly surprised for the most part.
CPU usage shoots to 100% (momentarily) at the drop of a hat.  The little clock spins for a sec or two when I open programs, but the dropdowns (System, Places) respond very quickly so it doesn't "feel" unresponsive.  Gdesklets (a utility that places widgets on your desktop) killed me, so had to turn that off.  
If you just want to see Ubuntu run, you could get by with lesser hardware than mine.  If you want a PC that you'll use regularly, I think you'd be happier with at least a 1GHz proc and 1GB RAM.
