### Post by skymt on 2006-09-13
Audio Players:
* Features/power: Amarok
* Usability: Impossible to tell, different people find different things usable. Rhythmbox and Banshee are very iTunes-like, Winamp fans will like XMMS or Beep.
* CPU/memory: XMMS. Not the lightest out there, but IMO the lightest player really usable as a jukebox.
* My choice: MPD/GMPC. I like being able to hack my desktop, including my jukebox. You've got to love a music player with at least a half-dozen interfaces available.

Web Browsers:
* Usability: Firefox or Epiphany. It's a tie in my opinion. People are used to Firefox, but Epiphany is simple and has some nice usability features.
* Features/power: Opera. If anybody tells you that every Opera feature is available as a Firefox extension, they're dead wrong.
* CPU/memory: Probably Opera.
* My choice: Opera.

Email clients:
* Usability: Evolution or Thunderbird
* Features: Evolution
* CPU/memory: Sylpheed or (for the adventurous) Mutt
* My choice: Gmail! \\:D/ 

Word processors:
* Usability: Another toss-up, between OpenOffice and AbiWord. They're both MS Word clones, so they're nearly identical.
* Features: OpenOffice
* CPU/memory: AbiWord
* My choice: OpenOffice
