### Post by pappapump on 2007-06-03
Well if the FPS games are any indication, it won't take long for mmorpgs to catch up.
Matter of fact there are several linux only mmorpg's, and if I knew exactly how to install em I'd be messing with em right now.
The new nexius is slick as silicone on a foosball rail.
I liked the first one, but this new one with the added maps is at about Q3 or UT level.
So for my FPS needs I'm fully covered.
Only thing I miss is the dodge feature that comes with Unreal.

Now, it's no great leap to imagine THOSE graphics on a RPG or MMORPG.
All thats left to do is expand the engine a bit.
Making maps is a breeze, I made about 12 new ones for Unreal2k3, UT and Unreal Gold, so all I can see that may need to be changed is the gameplay engine.
MMORPG play is basically co-op mode with an infrastructure.

I use Kaneva for my game design now and plan to explore the Linux end of that proggy.
The only thing I'm lacking now is more time to go along with my patience, well that and my ability to make player models.

Skinning a pre-made character is easy, but making a character from scratch isn't as ez.
If you have the time go to the Ubuntu gamers section and get a nice surprise.
They been busy.

As far as profit margins go, again you missed the mark.
MMORPG'S=Constant Income, or P2P (pay to play).
No matter what sells over the counter, it's the P2P crowd who actually make the difference in profit, so any projections based on over the counter sales at EB-Games just don't measure up.

FPS may be slowly fading in sales but MMORPG's are being released faster than a lot of new linux distros, and a lot of them are free downloads. The smart guys like G-Potatoe are not charging a penny to host and run the server OR to download the game.
They make their money from the online items shops which you use paypal to purchase with or a credit card.

I know players who over a period of 3 months plopped down a net total of $1000 for extra power up items and Armor enchantments.
Compare that to the $13.00 a month blizzard charges for WOW and you can see a trend forming.
So you can imagine that anything that increases that income for them will be worth doing.

Now lets take a look at the statistics concerning Linux users who want online games and are willing to pay if needed.
In brief look see around the many linux forums with fps, rpg and mmorpg gamers, I have counted memberships as low as 15,000 and as high as 150,000, and thats just 5 of the forums I checked, the lowest being 15k..

Sourceforge is hosting 8 new rpg, mmorpg and fps games designed either exclusively for linux or for both Win and Linux OS.
Kaneva is funding some too, and though it's a relatively new program and website, interest is growing.
