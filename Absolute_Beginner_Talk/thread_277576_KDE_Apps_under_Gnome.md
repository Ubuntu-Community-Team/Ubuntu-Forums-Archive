### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-10-14
It could be that you got a customized CD, or you may have installed the programs without noticing it. As far as performance goes, running KDE apps in GNOME will, in theory, be slower than running all GNOME apps. This is because KDE apps depend on certain libraries, such as QT and other KDE-specific libraries, whereas GNOME uses GTK+ and some GNOME-specific libraries. Running a mixed set of programs requires both sets of libraries to be loaded and running, as opposed to just one in a pure desktop. However, if your computer is fairly modern, you probably wont notice any speed differences. Of course, it will still take up more disk space with a mixed desktop, but that's hardly an issue unless you have a **really** small hard drive.
