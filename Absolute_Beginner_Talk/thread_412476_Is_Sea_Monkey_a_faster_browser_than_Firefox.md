### Post by rillip on 2007-04-18
Quick in what way?

The most interesting article I've ever seen about browser speed brought up a lot of good points.  Quicker on a cold run?  Quicker after it'd been started once?  Quicker at loading pages? What if they use progressive display instead of waiting for the file to finish, does that make them quicker, since you can see it sooner?

And really, I think it's a lot of perception.  I hate IE because it feels so slow to me.  When I did actual time trials on Windows 2000 using Firefox 1.04 (current at the time) Opera 8.... something and IE 6, FF and IE were within a tenth of a second at loading and loading pages, but IE just FELT slow.  Opera was not statistically signifigantly faster or slower (got kind of quirky range, no particularly large variations though).

My anectdotal experience is that Opera on Ubuntu 6.06 is comparable most of the time, but occasionally has dog-slow moments.  FF is more consistently fast, and Konquerer is consistent, but pokey.
