### Post by Andavane on 2008-03-24
I have very recently installed Ubuntu on my Sonay Vaio VGN-S3 laptop. The machine is approaching the end of its 3rd year now, and arrived here pre-installed with Windows XP Pro, and still with its original battery.
I was beginning to think that it was approaching the end of itslife as it was very sluggish and took a very long time to boot up and settle down.
After a few teething troubles with installing Ubuntu (where forum members kindly helped out with difficulties) it feels as though I have a brand new machine. The responses are lightning fast --almost too fast in some respects, ad the touch pad has become ultra senstivie so I need to be very careful in my typing. In fact I was never aware that it could move so fast! :guitar:
All this has made me really think:
When running Windows, an awful lot of things must have been running away in the background: In comparison, working with Ubuntu feels peaceful and serene, like being in a room where I can be alone and quiet. In comparison, with Windows it felt as if whlst I was working, little sprites were sneaking in and planting adverts around the place, nicking the odd possession and setting off little mischief bombs which would go off unpredictably.
Well that may be a bit fanciful, but now I think I can see why the computers need faster and faster processors... partly to make sure they can cope with all the background stuff.

Ubuntu, in comparison, seems to have a much smaller carbon footprint... all-in-all cleaner, greener and sane.
Oh, and it's vastly increased battery life as well.

Regards

John
