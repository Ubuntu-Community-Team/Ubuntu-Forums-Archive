### Post by gunksta on 2008-03-27
Lightning is evolving very quickly. For example, 0.8 will be released within a month (give or take). The Ubuntu repos can not keep up with this kind of a rapidly changing technology. Fortunately, the product that it is building upon (Thunderbird) is very mature and stable, which the Ubuntu Repos do a good job of keeping up with.

I use the repos for programs such as FireFox, T-Bird, OOo, etc. But, for extensions I prefer to download them myself because the application will auto-update them when a new version comes out and I get to take advantage of the new toys sooner.

It's just a preference though.
