### Post by Jive Turkey on 2007-10-20
You can search for stuff online, look for pages specifically related to *buntu and debian distros over others, much of it is universal though.

conventions "$" indicates the command prompt, "#" usually indicates root command prompt.  Go read up on sudo

The best help IMO are from these commands in the terminal itself:

$ apropos [some keyword]
-will list lots of other commands and brief descriptions of what they are, it will all be somehow related to the keyword, not always "apropos" to what you are searching for but usually pretty close.

$ [some command]  followed by "-h" or "--help" will list the options and their purpose in the context of that command "-h" itself is an option.

$ man [some command] 
-will list the "man page" or manual of the command if available press "q" to escape the scrollable man page.

enjoy!
