### Post by Skidpad on 2007-09-18
> **skymera said:**
> people say you should burn slow, althoough i burned at 52x and worked fine
I won't dispute that; I've done faster burns as well and gotten way with it, but I also have a coaster or two - it all depends on your burner, media, etc.  To alleviate this potential conflict for those just beginning to dabble in Linux, it is recommended to do a slow burn.

> **gshockxc said:**
> Thanks for the tips.  I've download a .ISO image before when playing with Red Hat.  Never got very far into it.  What is an image?  How is that different than downloading the actual files?

An .iso image is a collection of all the files needed for a particular installation - boot files, etc.  When you download Ubuntu, you will be downloading an .iso image, and for it to function correctly as a Live Cd, you must keep it in that format and burn it as an image.  See this page [HERE]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso") to see the whole process (and more).

> **oilchangeguy said:**
> if you are talking about dual booting, no, you won't lose all of your files. if you do a clean install of ubuntu, then yes. always, always back up your data, BEFORE you do anything else.
Heed this advice - *lots* of stories on here regarding people who didn't back up their data first, had  unexpected trouble during installation (perhaps of their own doing) and were left at the mercy of their spouse after having lost wedding pictures, music, etc.  Backup your data prior to attempting an install, and you'll think more clearly when it counts most.  :)
