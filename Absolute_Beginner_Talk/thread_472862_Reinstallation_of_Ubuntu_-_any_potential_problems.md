### Post by sacherjj on 2007-07-20
If you are installing Linux, make sure you have a separate partition for /home.  If you have a system pre-installed, convert your system to a seperate /home partition.  There are tutorials available for the searching.  After you do this, reinstalling is a breeze.  Gone are the hours of reinstalling Windows software for the Registry is setup correctly.

I recently hosed my Ubuntu install by doing something stupid.  I installed a second drive for my /home mount point.  Had I have put my tarball backup of the main drive on my /home drive, the restore would have been a few minutes.  As it were, it took about 15 minutes of my time to setup the Ubuntu reinstall.  Then it took 5 minutes to select the apps I had installed and let the install.  I came back 30 minutes later AND EVERYTHING WORKED.  Have /home on a different partition meant that my email was still in Thunderbird, along with account settings.  No trying to find a PST file and get it loaded, then configuring all of my 12 email accounts.  It just worked.  After installing Wine, all my Wine programs were already installed, because they are under /home/.wine.   This made me love Linux even more.
