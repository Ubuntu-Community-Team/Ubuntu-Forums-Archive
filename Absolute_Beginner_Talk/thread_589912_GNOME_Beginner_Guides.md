### Post by Steve1961 on 2007-10-24
You might find this link interesting to get you started:

[http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome](http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome)

Just remember that Gnome is a desktop environment that runs on Linux. There are many other environments, most are customizable, and to a large extent which one you use depends on personal preference (Gnome, KDE and XFCE are probably the most popular).  You can even install multiple environments and decide which one you want to use from the login screen.

1) To switch themes go to System>Preferences>Themes : click on a theme and you will see the effects right away
2)A good source to find more themes is [Gnome Look]("http://www.gnome-look.org/").You can see ratings (popularity) on the different themes there
3) Not sure what 'task' it is you are referring too. As mentioned above by Steve1961, GNOME is a DE
4) Your alternatives to list some are KDE,XFCE,FluxBox...
As far as 3D themes go, you might be referring to Desktop Effects ([Compiz Fusion]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compiz_fusion"))
