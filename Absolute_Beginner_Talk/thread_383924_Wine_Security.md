### Post by brettg on 2007-03-14
Firstly, congrats on making a move away from windows, it's a difficult step to take but it is one that you wont regret in the long run.

Secondly, you may want to consider dual booting windows because a lot of stuff you want might take a while to get working under linux. Best to get it all working before you cut the cord permanently.

As for your question, it relates somewhat to point 2. WINE is by no means perfect at running Windows stuff, and fortunately that also includes the various malware items that afflict windows users. Even if you were to manage to execute a piece of windows malware from within wine it would not affect the underling system whatsoever and any effects it would have would only last for the duration of your wine session.

Good luck
