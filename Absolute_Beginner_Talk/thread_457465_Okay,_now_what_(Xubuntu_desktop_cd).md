### Post by SoulinEther on 2007-05-28
What you don't want to do is drag the iso file onto your CD and burn it as a data file... That won't work at all.

Here's a GREAT video that will explain this all the way. It's an ubuntu screenscast, too.

[http://screencasts.ubuntu.com/Downloading_and_Burning_an_Ubuntu_ISO](http://screencasts.ubuntu.com/Downloading_and_Burning_an_Ubuntu_ISO)

Basically, you want to use a program called imgburn to burn the image of the disk (which is like "zipped" in the .iso) onto a CD. No, don't worry about checking its hash or whatever. If its the same size it's probably the same. And a little faith now and then doesn't hurt in your endeavours :)

Good luck. And xubuntu is awesome, trust me. :D

the 'hash' check is testing the integrity of the image you downloaded. try [this page]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso"), which explains it. Look for where it mentions 'checksum'. 
This will then tell you how to test your download, and then to ensure it was burned as an image (the iso is burned as a full disk, rather than as a file on a disk
