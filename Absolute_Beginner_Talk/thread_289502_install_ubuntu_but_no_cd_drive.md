### Post by carla on 2006-11-02
> **joy83 said:**
> can u explain me a bit more what to do

Okay, the BIOS will tell you exactly what's physically inside your laptop. Once you know what cards, drivers, ports (like USB) that you have, the better we'll be able to help you with your purchase and then your install. The other route is to examine the outside carefully. Look at the ports (places to plug in external devices, as you mentioned).

So, step 1: 
Do not be intimidated or frustrated. Most important step. Every single person using a computer today has felt as you do--staring at the infernal machine before them, wondering, "What do I do, and why does everyone understand this but me?" ;)

Step 2: Check the outside of the laptop carefully, with the screen closed. See any grooved rectangles? Those might be expansion slots, and might allow an internal CD/DVD player to be installed. What you're seeing is the protective cover for an unused expansion slot of some type.
Also, look at all available plugs. If you have USB ports, you can easily use an external CD/DVD drive. USB ports are distinguishable by their shape (usually rectangular, and halfway filled with a white object--which you can see if you peek inside the USB outlet) and by their trident symbol. If this computer has "helpful" symbols over ports/outlets. you'll see the USB trident symbol.

If you look at this website, on the right-hand side, you'll see both the shape of the USB port, and the USB trident symbol: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USB_port](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USB_port)

Or...you can examine the BIOS report, which will tell you more about your laptop, but is the more (frankly) intimidating route to take for step 2. Start up the computer, and pay close attention. Soon after activity begins, you should receive a prompt to push a certain key (it depends on the computer which specific key) in order to run BIOS/change and/or view the computer's settings. As soon as you see that prompt, do what it says, or you'll miss the opportunity.

Here's more information: [http://www.answers.com/topic/bios](http://www.answers.com/topic/bios)

Now remember, the goal is simply to see what your computer will or won't accept as a CD drive, so don't get bogged down or overwhelmed in all this. Frankly, if all else fails, take pictures of the two sides and rear of the laptop, and share them here. One of us can at least tell you what we see.

Don't worry, we'll get you up and running. :KS
