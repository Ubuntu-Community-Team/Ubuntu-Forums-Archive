### Post by dmizer on 2006-06-29
i have a 500mhz pII desktop with 64meg of ram that runs decent with [ubuntu lite]("http://http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=98233&highlight=howto+ubuntu+lite") ... no frills, but it works very will for surfing, email, and im.

i have two 600mhz pIII laptops (toshiba tecra 8100).  one has 128m of ram, and runs ubuntu lite fast enough to play dvd's.  the other one is a 700mhz pIII box with 256m of ram, and i have xubuntu set up on it.  my server box is an old 700mhz pIII box with 128m of ram, and i have no problems with it at all either.

you might want to take a look at what kind of hardware is on that laptop.  you'll probably need acpi enabled, and pay attention to your cpu fan, because old toshibas are notorious for having acpi issues.

don't expect to get gnome on it, and you won't be able to totally trick it out, but there's no reason why you couldn't make it into a very nice browser/email/im machine.  and if you have a dvd player, you should be able to watch dvd's as well.  but again, do not use gnome.  it's too heavy for your system.  lucky for you, linux has many alternatives.
