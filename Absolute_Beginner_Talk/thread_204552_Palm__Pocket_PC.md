### Post by Lonestar on 2006-06-27
Flavain...

Personally, I have used a Palm device for many years (so I am biased) but I can tell you that I am able to sync my Palm with Ubuntu.  Additionally, there is a Palm desktop like application for use on Ubuntu called JPilot that works very well. 

I did read that several users experience problems getting their Palm to sync (outside of using JPilot, which always seems to work), look at this link for details on that issue [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=78918]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=78918")

There have been rumors for several months that Palm will come out with a new Operating System in early 2007 and that the new OS will be Linux based.  PalmSource, the company that makes the OS was bought by Access who is a Linux software company (making Linux OS's for cell phones, etc), plus, Palm itself has hired a bunch of Linux programmers, but so far there is not any concrete information about a Linux version launch date...but I am eagerly watching.  I am going to wait for the word on whether the Linux OS on Palm is going to happen or not before I upgrade, you may want to take this news into consideration.

Someone else will have to address sync issues with PDA's running Pocket PC.
