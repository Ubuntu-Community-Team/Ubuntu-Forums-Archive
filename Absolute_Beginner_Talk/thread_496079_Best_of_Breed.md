### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-07-08
The easiest suggestion. Try them all. Every app does something slightly differently, whether its layout, features, theme. Each has its niche and no one suggestion applies to everyone. That's the beauty of Linux, we can have all those applications and freely choose which one is best :). I certainly don't recommend like you said installing 10 apps that do the same thing, rather try all ten and then remove the ones you dislike.

Best site I can recommend is [linux app finder.]("http://linuxappfinder.com/") It indexes most of the major applications by task, so you can find all the options, look at them, read the main pages and have a look. Then try the best. It even has a [Windows alternatives section.]("http://linuxappfinder.com/windows").

As for recommendations, I will tell you what I use.

What app do you all prefer for each of these tasks:
-word processing [COLOR="Blue"]- OpenOffice Writer[/COLOR]
-editing home pics (not fancy graphics) [COLOR="Blue"]-GIMP[/COLOR]
-music [COLOR="Blue"]-Banshee [/COLOR]
-movies [COLOR="Blue"]- Totem/VLC
[/COLOR]

[Here is the entry for graphics editors.]("http://linuxappfinder.com/graphics/imageeditors") My recommendation would be to try Krita.
