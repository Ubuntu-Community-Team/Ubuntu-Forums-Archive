### Post by welshboy on 2006-09-16
Hi there,

When you say "what to google" I assume you mean what to type in to the search engine.

Well you have a couple of options, 
1.  You could check out somewhere like Amazon, who usually have a load of books on programming for linux

2.  Just type in C++ programming in linux or words to that effect and you should find what you need.

3.  Another possible place is the Ubuntu Document Storage Facility, I've not gone through the whole lot of it yet, but you may find some useful tips there as well.  The link is [http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Main_Page](http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Main_Page)

### Post by Adramelech on 2006-09-16
Nice Im in the same situation, just porting my thesis over linux.

Get an IDE, anjuta is fine, as you say you already know "console" programming I guess you are looking for graphical interfaces, so look for GTK, GTKmm, glade, libgalde, libglademm, all in repositories. 

Theres plenty of information over internet, references, tutorials, etc, etc. thats the advantage of being an open source OS.
