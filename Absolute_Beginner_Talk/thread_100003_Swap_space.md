### Post by dalee on 2005-12-06
Hi,

If you are talking about during the install, that will take care of itself. But, you will need a swap afterwards. You can set the size of the swap (and any other partion), when you get to that part in the install.

I'm new to Ubuntu myself, so I'm not real up on the partioner. But from what I remember, it was fairly simple to follow and use. Hopefully someone more expirenced than me will come along with better instructions.

Since you pretty challenged for ram, I'd set the swap at least at 128megs, but probably not more than 250megs, (won't do much good).

One last thing, Gnome might not even install with so little ram. Leaving you with no GUI, just the commandline. A best it might squawk and complain mightily!
