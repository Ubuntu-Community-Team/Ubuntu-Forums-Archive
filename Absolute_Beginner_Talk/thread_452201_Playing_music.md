If you try to open an unsupported file (MP3, DivX, Xvid etc.) in Ubuntu's default media player you will be asked whether you want to install the required codecs automatically. This is really not much different from the way things work in Windows. After installing the codecs your files should play just fine. Standard audio CDs should play out of the box without additional downloads.

If you don't like Ubuntu's default player, I'd recommend VLC instead. VLC on Linux works just the way it does on Windows, i.e. it has all necessary codecs built in from the beginning. It can play standard audio CDs and video DVDs as well.
