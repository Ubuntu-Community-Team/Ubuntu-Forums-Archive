### Post by arjun.shankar on 2006-08-09
philetus, I think stubblepoo is talking about 'upgrading' from Breezy or Hoary.

stubblepoo, Dapper is an LTS (Long Term Support) release, which means it is supposed to be way more stable and bug free than other releases around it. Unlike Breezy or Hoary (which have an 18 month lifetime, because that is the promised period for which updates will be availabe) it has 3 years of support on the desktop. If you upgrade, you will probably get better look and feel, newer programs and better hardware support (Breezy couldn't detect my battery, for example).

Also, more people will help you out with any problems you face, because it is the current stable release, and has the maximum number of users.

Hope that clears it up.

And like philetus says, if you are still stuck with windows, get rid of it. Free Software is way cooler!

### Post by robins_web on 2006-08-09
I'm probably going to get jumped on for this, but if your current version does what you want it to, why upgrade? The First Law of Engineering says, "If it ain't broke, don't fix it!"

Of course, the First Law of Software Engineering says, "If it ain't broke, upgrade it."

But the other points are valid: latest & greatest technology, long-term support, etc. When I upgraded, I found that my Toshiba laptop just seemed to work a little bit better. But others have found the opposite: things stopped working after the upgrade.

But I'm glad I did it.
