### Post by mattisking on 2006-07-19
You might be better served to look at some of the many HOWTo's available and looking at Ubuntu's Wiki. In the meantime, did you install Ubuntu Breezy or Dapper (the latest release)? To find and install packages not already installed by default, use Synaptic, available under System->Administration->Synaptic Package Manager. Is that wireless USB (Linksys I believe) device your only access to the internet? If so, Synaptic won't work for you until you have a working internet connection. Unfortunately, I can't help much with setting up your wireless device. If Dapper doesn't handle it automatically for you (and it does with alot of wireless devices/cards) then your best bet is a tool called ndiswrapper and your installation CD that came with the device. ndiswrapper can be setup to use your actual Windows driver to use your WUSB11 device. Just do a search in the forums or the wiki. I'll check back with you later.

By the way, here's a good reference for using Synaptic: [http://www.monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/]("http://www.monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/")
