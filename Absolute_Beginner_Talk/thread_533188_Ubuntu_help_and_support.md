### Post by djroze on 2007-08-23
Hey caddy, I'm sorry that you've had so much trouble with Ubuntu and getting assistance with your concerns.  It seems like using Linux tends to be hit-or-miss for a lot of folks; the last few times I've installed Linux I have had really annoying little problems that send me right back to XP (example: can't get console beeps to stop happening when I hold down backspace in the terminal, and stuff like that); I usually have a dual-boot setup, but rarely use the Linux OS.  I just reinstalled Ubuntu (7.04) recently and I really like the new version, so I think things have definitely improved, but I understand that sometimes there are frustrating roadblocks to making it a part of your daily computer usage.
   I think something that can add to this frustration is the fact (in my view) that many *nix users are eager to suggest that Linux is *the* way to go for basically everyone, and that you can overcome mostly any incompatibilities or bugs that you encounter if you put in the time to do so.  However, a lot of people have trouble taking the time to do this when they have lots of non-computer-y stuff to take care of, and I think there are unequivocally some places where Linux falls short of being an easy desktop solution for non-technical people (though Windows also falls short in many ways of its own!  ;)  What's also a bit frustrating is that Linux enthusiasts are eager to advocate the usage of Linux, but at the same time they can't be held responsible for the OS not meeting a user's needs in the same way that Microsoft is responsible if Windows doesn't meet your needs; when  you ask for help with Ubuntu, you're (in a way) treated as a fellow developer or contributor more than a paying customer.  This "makes sense" from an economic/literal perspective, but it's not what many people will expect when they are convinced to throw out their Windows XP in favor of Linux.
   Just my two cents there...not trying to start any flaming or whatever-you-call-it.  I hope that you can find the time to continue to tinker with Ubuntu and I hope you find Linux to be a suitable OS for your daily stuff, as I think Linux and open source software are really great and a necessary competitor to folks like Microsoft.  And unfortunately, you can't strictly depend on anyone else to really guarantee that things in Linux will work for you, but hopefully you can become knowledgeable to the point where you can feel confident in predicting the results of your computing choices and feel that you have the resources available to make Linux work for you.  Best of luck!  :)

- Daniel
