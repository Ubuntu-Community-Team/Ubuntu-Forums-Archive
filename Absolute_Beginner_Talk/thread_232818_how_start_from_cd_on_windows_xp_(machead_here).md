### Post by eXisor on 2006-08-09
You have to go into the Bios setup (usually one hits DEL) when you reboot or switch the machine on. It's on the bios features screen. Scroll through the 'boot sequence' options entry until you see 'CDROM, C', or something similar. CDROM must be first.

### Post by robins_web on 2006-08-09
Unlike your Mac, PCs have many different schemas for getting into your BIOS setup. Sometimes you have to press "S", sometimes one of the function keys (such as F1, F2, etc.)

Your manual for the PC (yeah, I know: whoever even keeps those, much less reads them?) should tell you how to do it. Other than that, an email to the manufacturer should get the answer.

### Post by Tomosaur on 2006-08-09
If you have a BBS Popup menu, you can temporarily alter which device you want to start from. I think on most PCs, the BBS menu is accessed by pressing 'Esc' during boot up. If you don't have one, you should be able to alter your boot sequence by entering the BIOS setup screen. You can normally do this by pressing one of Function keys (f1, f2 etc) during boot up, but you'll have to read the information carefully for a line which says something like 'Press F1 to enter BIOS Setup' or something similar.
