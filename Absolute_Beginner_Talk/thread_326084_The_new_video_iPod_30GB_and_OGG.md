### Post by WiseElben on 2006-12-27
You won't be able to play your ogg formatted files on an iPod, even though they are set as .mp3s, as the file extension name does not make the file that certain type. Sadly, iPod does not support the OGG format. I would look at Creative's line of music players, especially the Zen Vision, which is similar to the iPod Video. The Zen Vision:M plays OGG, DivX, and others. If I had enough money to buy a player, I would probably go with the Zen Vision:M.

### Post by MrHorus on 2006-12-27
> **WiseElben said:**
> Sadly, iPod does not support the OGG format.

That's slightly incorrect.

Apple does not support Ogg Vorbis - the iPod however is just a computer and I can assure you that it will quite happily play back Vorbis and FLAC if you flash the firmware with Rockbox :D

If you Google for it you will find where to download and install it and it's quite a simple process and if you follow the instructions you will be able to make a backup of the original bootloader and firmware before reflashing it with Rockbox.

Pretty much the only thing that Rockbox will not play is WMA as it's a proprietry, closed source format and natrually goes against the principles of open source software.
