### Post by gwoodard on 2007-11-06
you don't need "imgburn" just keep the default

Directions: Download Ubuntu ISO (version you want) from Ubuntu.com, Save to desktop, insert cd or dvd, click data disk, click desktop in the sidebar, double click the iso, then (if possible) choose your speed, or just click write...after its burned test to see if cd works (usually you have to restart pc with cd in drive) and choose "start or install ubuntu" once it gets to the desktop just restart the pc and your cd should work and your ubuntu install will be what you have already
