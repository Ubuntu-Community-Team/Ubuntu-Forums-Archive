### Post by philinux on 2007-09-06
Linux Format Magazine Oct 2007.

> Surely linux's permissions will stop a virus doing any real damage?

This is a common misconception. Sure unless a virus runs as root , it will not be able to delete or rewrite system files. But ask yourself, which is more valuable , the OS which can be reinstalled, or you personal data.... A virus or trojan , if one were to be created in the future, or by the time you're reading this, could also be used as a spam-bot without root permissions. Something that could cause your ISP  to suspend your internet access.

This question keeps coming up on here and I tend to err on the side of be prepared! :lolflag:
