### Post by Tsen on 2007-04-23
Sure, so long as your BIOS supports booting from USB.  Instead of burning, you'll just extract the contents of the .iso to the flash drive (it would probably be best to do so after formatting the USB drive, just to be safe, so back up anything on there you need).  WinRAR can extract .iso's in Windows, I think.  Then just copy all of the files over to your flash drive's root.  Make sure you get all of the files, including hidden ones, though.
