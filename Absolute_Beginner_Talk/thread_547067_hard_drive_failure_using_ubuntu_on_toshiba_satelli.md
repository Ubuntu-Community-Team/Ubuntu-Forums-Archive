### Post by halfmanhalfbug on 2007-09-10
Hi msmithy12.
That's right almost all laptops have 2.5" drives. When I was shopping around to replace mine (drive melted down, no recovery) I found many variations of interface (e.g. ATA-3, ATA-5, etc.). Two things you can do:
1. Go to Toshiba's website and download the detailed specs for your computer. They should also have instructions there for replacing your drive, which brings me to...
2. Pull out your old drive and take it to a parts store with knowledgeable staff
I did both so I could be sure all the pins on my new drive matched up. On my drive (Hitachi Travelstar) there was also a jumper on two of the pins. This determines the drive's device number. Make sure you note how that jumper is placed.
