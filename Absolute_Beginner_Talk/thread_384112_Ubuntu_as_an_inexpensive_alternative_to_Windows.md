### Post by Stochastic on 2007-03-14
Also just a note, if you're planning on giving these computers to families you may want to look at doing a few different setups.

1) Standard Ubuntu single partition install.  - extremely easy to install, works well for any teenager/adult

2) Single [Edubuntu]("http://www.edubuntu.org") install - less user-friendly install process, great for any family with young kids

3) Dual Boot, Multiple Partition install with both Ubuntu for the adults/teens and Edubuntu for the kids - this setup may be a little more difficult to maintain/understand for the end user if they're non-techy but it can give great parental control over kids without giving up too much for the adults.  To do this, modify Grub or Lilo depending on which versions you're installing.

You'll find one of Linux's main strengths is choice and customization.  Linux can do many things very well if it's setup right.  Also you may want to stare at Beryl/Compiz desktop effects for a while (6 months at least) on either your own computer or on video screenshots from youtube before thinking about sending them out to people as it's still BETA software.  It's sweet though.

### Post by shultzjr on 2007-03-14
First let me say that I do what you are planning.  I install Ubuntu on older computers and sell/give them away.  Here are several points you should consider:

1. you can buy brand spanking new computers with XP Home on them for $279 or maybe even less now, that was six months ago and they come with warrantees.  Microsoft "screws" the little guys by selling Windows to big suppliers like Dell and HP for next to nothing a machine but turns around and sells OEM's for big bucks to people like you and me so we CAN'T compete with the big boys.

2. even if you get the hardware for free, your time is worth something and at 3 to 5 hours per machine how much are you worth?  You may get that figure down per machine but it will take you a while in experience.  Not all the hardware you get will be the same.  The learning curve on it will be a killer.  I know but by the time you work out the bugs and troubleshoot the different problems it WILL take longer than you think.

3. to make any kind of real profit you'll have to sell at least 100 machines a week not just the onesies and twosies many people do now.  Can you handle all that support after the sale?

4. Ubuntu 6.10 DT i386 requires 192M of ram just to install.  If you have to just upgrade the ram in older computers, that will kill your profit.

5. new hardware is getting cheaper and cheaper by the minute, even as you read this.
