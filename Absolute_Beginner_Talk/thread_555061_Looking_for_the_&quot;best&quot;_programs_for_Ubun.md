### Post by sunexplodes on 2007-09-19
Here's what I've got for you:

**Video Player**:
VLC, hands down. It's fast, highly configurable, and plays EVERYTHING. You might be familiar with its Windows version. It's the best video player on ANY operating system.

**Music Player**: 
I'm a little torn on this one. Personally, my favorite is a command-line player called Music Player Daemon, with a frontend called Sonata. It's VERY fast and easy on memory, looks good, and well organized. BUT, it does require some configuration file-editing, and can be a pain for a new user. With that said, Exaile and Banshee are both very nice players.

**Web Browsing**: Most people really love Firefox, and with good reason, but I found the 2.x versions have been very bloated and slow.  The progress being made on 3.0 looks very good, but in the meantime, the default GNOME browser, Epiphany is speedy, modern, and extensible. I'd recommend giving it a try. 

**File Management**:
Try Thunar. It's fast and versatile. Does amost anything Nautilus can do, but it eats up a lot less memory. 

**CD/DVD Burning**:
If you don't mind the fact that it's a KDE app, and thus doesn't fit too well with the look of a GNOME desktop, k3b is hands-down the most well-rounded disc writing application linux has to offer. Really, really solid. Brasero is a pretty good GNOME alternative.

**Office Applications**:
OpenOffice is a rock-solid alternative to MS Office, but it's slow as all hell. If you just need a basic wordprocessor and spreadsheet application, I'd recommend giving AbiWord and GNUMeric a try. They'll do most things an average user would need, and they're much faster than OO.
