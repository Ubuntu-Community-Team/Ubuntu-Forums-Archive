Currently I using the ATi card at 1280x1024 ..... and I'm sure the Nvidia card does the same.

The Geforce 4 is a considerably newer card, and better prefoming card. But Nvidia cards don't seem to last as long on average as ATI (between heat and bad caps). If the Geforce card starts to crap out, the Rage 128 is a very reliable card. Nothing special, but solid, and it runs considerably cooler. Handles Google Earth just fine (though the GeForce would do it better).  Linux support is questionable, though. 

The rage 128 has 8, 16, or 32MB ram, and the Geforce 4 is offered in 64mb or 128mb. 

Here's the rub: if you don't use 3d applications and don't care about Desktop effects there is NO difference. 

If you DO, then the Geforce wins. But the Geforce card is often undercooled, so it may be worthwhile to use supplemental cooling such as a squirrel-cage fan that screws into the a pci slot.
