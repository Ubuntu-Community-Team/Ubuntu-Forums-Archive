Yes, OS X and vista co-exist quite happily, as long as you format the mac partition without journaling. Also, are you on a PC? because, yes, OS X on a PC is possible, if you're on a PC, you may wish to see:
[http://maconpc.org/](http://maconpc.org/)

>  2. I have downloaded the new Mac OS Leopard but the DVD's i have only can hold 4.7gigs of space and the Mac OS is 7.5gigs, which would be the best way to burn the ISO on a DVD? Do they even sell larger storage DVDs?
Yes, to burn this sized DVD, you must get a dual layer DVD, make sure your burner is dual layer also ;)

> **tylerdurden15 said:**
>  3. Assuming i accomplish the two prior tasks is it as easy as placing the Mac OS in the drive and it giving me an option to partion the hard drive and be able to dual boot, or is it more complicated?  If so what would be the proceedure?
Well, it can be a little more complicated, especially if you're on a PC. If you're familiar with partitioning Hard Disk, you should fine it more or less straight-forward. Although, don't get your hopes up too quickly, there is no guarantee that OS X will work on **your** PC. Certain hardware is difficult, e.g AMD processors and ATI graphics cards.
