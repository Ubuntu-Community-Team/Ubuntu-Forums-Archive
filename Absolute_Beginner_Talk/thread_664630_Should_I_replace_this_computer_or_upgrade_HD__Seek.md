### Post by m9ke on 2008-01-11
Regarding two hds vs one:  I would consider buying one big drive.  Puttting in two drives means making a mountpoint for it, perhaps changing the ownership, etc.  All this is doable, but keeping it simple is a fine design principle.  I have two drives in my system, but it's because some one gave me a free 20GB drive.  If I were going from scratch, I would definitely stay with one big internal drive.  You can always add an external USB drive later.

Also I totally agree with the poster who said put your home dir in a separate partition.  I did not know the value of this and how to do it on my first install, to my regret later.  For me, a separate partition for home is so valuable that I almost think it should be a default in the install.   How to do this is well covered in this forum.  It's worth checking out.

Also agree with the folks who said keep your existing system.  When I first got interested in Ubuntu I asked the exact same question on this forum, and got the same answer as you are getting.  I went with the advice and never regretted it even a little.

Good luck,
m9ke
