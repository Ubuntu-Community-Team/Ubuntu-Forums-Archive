### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-12-10
There are a number of signigicant differences including changes to X, compiz, fstab, security (apparmour), and booting.

This is a nice overview :

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu_(Linux_distribution)#Releases](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu_(Linux_distribution)#Releases)

The advantage of LTS is the duration of support, 3 years, as opposed to I believe 18 months with non-LTS.

Some desktop users prefer to run "cutting edge", always installing the newest packages and are willing to accept glitches or occasional breakage.

Others prefer stability. Newer is not always better don't you know ? This is especially true of servers.

### Post by SteveHoffmanUK on 2007-12-11
jaybombalous wrote: 
*"stop spreading dishonest statements"*

Feisty or Gutsy may work better for you, but not for everyone, so it's not "dishonest" at all to say that the newer versions are not always better.

My case: a D-Link DWL-520+ wireless PCI adapter worked "out of the box" in Dapper, but when I upgraded  to Edgy and Feisty it wouldn't work until I got an ndiswrapper to use with it. That's disappointing, but it does happen; I prefer the newer versions, but I have to put up with fiddling about with ndiswrapper in order to use them.

Businesses in particular my not want the hassle and risk from newer versions and prefer the stability implied by the LTS system.
