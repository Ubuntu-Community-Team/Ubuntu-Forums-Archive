### Post by cnelson26 on 2007-10-31
First, I can't be certain but I'd bet that your sound card is supported in Ubuntu.  I have the Xi-Fi Elite Pro, and it works as well as it did in Windows.  I also know many ATI drivers are supported right after installation, and I know it's possible to get drivers for many ATI cards  even if they don't work totally right out of the box.  There are a couple of options to play games , but the best solution I found is to simple get Parallels virtualization which allows you to run Windows directly inside Ubuntu with full graphics, sound and every other component support, so gaming would be no problem.  It's full Windows, meaning you can download and install any program right inside, and do anything you're used to doing in Windows, but all directly on top of your running Ubuntu OS.  This would undoubtedly also work for your wife's needs.  (There's also a virtualization program called VMware but I'm not sure if they support full 3d graphics).

You could use your iPod no problem in Ubuntu.  In fact, I was pleased to find many additional features on the media players in Ubuntu that go beyond what iTunes offers.  For example, Ryhthmbox the default media player in Ubuntu offers a much more comprehensive search function for gathering all album art work, lyrics search, and can even consult an online database to correct any errors in your MP3 metadata you may have.  I was also pleasantly surprised when I first plugged in my iPod and the first thing it asks you is "Would you like me to build your music collection from your iPod?"  So it grabs the music currently on your iPod and puts it on your computer as a start to your library and to simplify future iPod syncing.

Let me know if you have any other questtions, I'll be happy to help the best I can.

### Post by strabes on 2007-11-02
Welcome to ubuntu! I hope you like it. About medibuntu, I got the following commands from [this page](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Medibuntu).```
sudo wget http://www.medibuntu.org/sources.list.d/gutsy.list -O /etc/apt/sources.list.d/medibuntu.list
wget -q http://packages.medibuntu.org/medibuntu-key.gpg -O- | sudo apt-key add - && sudo apt-get update
```Just run those commands in a terminal, which you can access by going to Applications > Accessories > Terminal. Just copy and paste them. To select entire lines easily you can triple click on them. In linux you can paste things that are highlighted by middle clicking. So to easily paste those commands into the terminal simply triple click on them one line at a time, then middle click in the terminal, one at a time.
