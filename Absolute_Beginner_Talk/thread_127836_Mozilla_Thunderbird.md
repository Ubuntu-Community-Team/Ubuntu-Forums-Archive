### Post by SectionThree on 2006-02-09
First off, how did you install Thunderbird? Did you download it and use dpkg? Did you use Synaptic? Did you use the apt-get command?

Also, please tell us about the setup you have, i.e. your machine, other operating systems (if any) you are running, and any other information you might think to be pertinent.

**Anyway,** I have some advice for you if you're running a dual-boot scheme on your machine between Ubuntu and another OS:  Don't use an email client like Thunderbird, access your email through your provider's web site instead. I speak through experience because Thunderbird will download your emails from the server then erase them from the server so your emails are stored locally.  So, should you choose to boot up in your other OS and decide to check your email, it won't be there. Since it seems that most Ubuntu users have some sort dual-boot scheme going on (and thus I'm assuming you do too), I advise you stick to the web-mail.

To conclude, you should do two things.  First, ask yourself if an email client is better than webmail for the position that you are in. Then, if the answer is yes, let the rest of us know about your setup, because it's easier for the rest of us to help you that way.
