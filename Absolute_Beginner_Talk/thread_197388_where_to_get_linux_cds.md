### Post by catlett on 2006-06-15
[http://www.linuxiso.org/](http://www.linuxiso.org/)  This sight has a large selection of linux distros available for download. It also has a brief description of each and links to their home pages.
There isn't as simple answer to your question. Linux distro's get very specialised. So the best one depends on what you want linux for. 
Ubuntu is quickly becoming the "new user" distro. Fedora Core 5 is very popular because it is the free version of the very popular Red Hat. Red Hat is big in Europe especially in Germany and is sold not given away.
Gentoo is for the power user who wants to customise everything. In Gentoo they compile and customise their kernels and then compile the window manager etc.
Other people want linux for a server. 
Debian is known to be rock solid stable. That they only put an application in their repository that will absolutely work and not cause errors. The stability comes at the price of being behind other distros in "new" applications.
Puppy and DSL specialise in being as small as possible, Their distros are less than 100mb and can be kept on usb flash drives.

So, you see, there isn't one best distro. It all depends on what you want linux for.
