### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-12-04
Another way to test distros:

Buy a cheap hard drive.  Partition it 4 ways.  Put a different distro onto each.  Edit your GRUB entries then you can have your original distro or 4 new ones to play with.  Overwrite the one you are least impressed with when a new version of SUSE/Fedora/Ubuntu comes out.

You will always have access to your data from any Linux distro on the machine.  This has the advantage of testing hardware compatibility (including sleep, suspend, and hibernate) for a distro that you may be considering keeping as your main distro.
