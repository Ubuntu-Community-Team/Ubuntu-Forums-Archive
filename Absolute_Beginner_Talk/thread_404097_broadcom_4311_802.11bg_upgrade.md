### Post by moffa on 2007-04-08
You won't get a 802.11g card running as a 802.11n card.  Just to let you know even though your broadband is 100 Mbps, it never goes anywhere near that.  If you're only using the wireless network for internet, 34Mbps is far faster than any home internet service can provide.

I agree with Moffa. Also you might not reach 54 Mb/s is your wireless connection quality is not 100%. I am working with an connection quality of about 65% since I'm downstairs and the wireless accesspoint is upstairs. Hence my speed is 36 Mb/s.
