### Post by CatKiller on 2007-12-14
Realistically, you're probably going to find it easier to install Ubuntu onto your external drive, and then copy your Home folder - with settings and the like - over to the external drive later.

You'll need to establish whether your computer is able to boot from an external drive, and it would probably be sensible to disconnect your internal drive during the install process to help prevent anything going wrong.
