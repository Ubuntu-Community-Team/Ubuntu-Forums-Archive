### Post by Bartender on 2006-02-28
Good morning -
I did the Automatix thing a few weeks ago.  After typing in a few commands, which downloads the basic Automatix program, you start Automatix.  I sat & watched Automatix download and install TONS of data off the internet.  This went on for about an hour on a fast connection.
So, I'm not sure what you were thinking when you asked about being  permanently connected to the internet, but yes, you need a broadband connection for the hour or more that you're running Automatix.  
This doesn't necessarily mean you're dead in the water if you don't have that at home.  Try bringing your PC to school, work, or a friend's house and see if it will go online.  Just make sure to enable your network card or onboard networking, then plug in the borrowed ethernet cable before turning your PC on.

### Post by AndyCooll on 2006-02-28
[QUOTE=m0o0oeh]So, do I need to be permanently connected to the Net to use it? 

J[/QUOTE]

You need to be connected to the Internet while using Automatix. It works by installing a range of programs for you (you choose which ones you want, e.g. codecs, latest version of Firefox, Acrobat reader, Beep media player etc etc). To install the programs you choose Automatixc downloads them off the Internet. Once Automatix has done its stuff you can disconnect your pc from the Internet again if you wish.
