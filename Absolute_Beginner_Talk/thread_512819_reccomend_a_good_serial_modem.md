### Post by Bartender on 2007-07-30
The cheapest new serial external I've found which certainly appears to work with Linux (take a look at the owner reviews) is the Amigo found at [Newegg]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16825137104") and who knows where else.
Just about any US Robotics serial modem (garage sales, craigslist, ebay) should work.  You've gotta watch out with these used ones...the 56K models are visually indistinct from older 33.6K versions.  Make sure you're buying a 56K.  Also, I see a lot of them up for bid without the serial cable and/or the wall plug.  The absence of either or both parts will mean extra money and hassle for you. 
The correct wall wart for the USR's is a 9V 1000mA output.  I've got an old Radio Shack 9V 800mA that appears to run them OK but wouldn't go any lower than that.
The correct serial cable for the USR's looks like [this]("http://catalog.belkin.com/IWCatProductPage.process?Product_Id=14303"), with a male DB25 at one end and female DB9 at the other end.
