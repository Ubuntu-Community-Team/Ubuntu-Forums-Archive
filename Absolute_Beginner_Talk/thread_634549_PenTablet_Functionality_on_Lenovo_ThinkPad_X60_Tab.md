### Post by Dr. Dumb on 2007-12-07
I installed Ubuntu 7.10 (Gutsy) on my ThinkPad X60 Tablet PC just a few days ago, and have been very happy with it except for one thing: the tablet functionality is completely nonexistent.

I've looked at the Gutsy Wiki _([http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Gutsy#Wacom_tablet](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Gutsy#Wacom_tablet)_), this forum (_[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=323580](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=323580), [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=460203](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=460203)_), and elsewhere _([http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/#input](http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/#input)_), and nothing I've tried has helped. 

It seems like some people have issues with getting the stylus to function properly, but I can't find anyone else with the problem of the stylus not working at all.

I've downloaded all of the Wacom packages available through Synaptic, and I've changed the tablet-related section of my xorg.conf to match the one here (_[http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/examples/xorg.conf](http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/examples/xorg.conf)_) as recommended by this (_[http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/#input](http://luke.no-ip.org/x60tablet/#input)_), but my stylus still does absolutely nothing.

Is there anyone who knows what might be the problem, or knows another source I could check for a fix?
I would greatly appreciate any input you've got.

### Post by freund on 2007-12-12
Try inserting 	Load	"wacom" in the xorg.conf under Section "Module".

Also could you show us your Xorg.0.log file in the /var/log directory to see what Xorg is doing.
