### Post by jgoguen on 2006-05-29
Just because there's 70 seeds, doesn't mean you're actually connected to them all.  They might not have a slot open to give you parts of the file, so you've probably got all of the file parts from the seeds/peers you've actually connected to.

If you double-click the torrent, a new tab will open giving more details of the torrent.  At the top is two bars, the top one is how much of the file you have and the second is how much is available.  If the number beside that bar is less than one (1) you won't be able to get all of the file.  If it's greater than one, you may just have to be patient and wait for slots to open on seeders or for other peers to get the missing parts.  The "Peers" tab will show you what peers/seeds you're connected to and how much of the file they each have.  If you don't see anyone with a completely full bar, you're not actually connected to any seeds.
