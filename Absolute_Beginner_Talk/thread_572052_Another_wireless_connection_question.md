### Post by SteveHoffmanUK on 2007-10-10
I also have a Dell Latitude. It came with a cheap dialup Winmodem and Windoze of course. When I changed to wireless I installed Dapper, checked the forums here to find a PCMCIA wireless card that would be sure to work in Linux. Chose a Belkin Wireless G Notebook network card, and it's always worked "out of the box", in Ubuntu Dapper, Edgy and Feisty.

But phidia is right: if you've already got a wireless card, search the forums for its name and search Google, and you may find an easy solution and not have to buy a new card. There is a program called Ndiswrapper that enables you to use a Windows driver in Linux for many wireless cards. Again, do some searches and you may find an answer.
