### Post by kebes on 2006-12-11
It depends on exactly what you are trying to do. Some GUI programs that work with video files:

avidemux2
[http://avidemux.org/](http://avidemux.org/)

cinelerra
[http://cvs.cinelerra.org/](http://cvs.cinelerra.org/)

kino
[http://www.kinodv.org/](http://www.kinodv.org/)


As usual, one of the greatest strengths of Linux is the command line. There are many command-line programs for working with video files:
mplayer     -- Command-line video player.
mencoder    -- Command-line video encoder.
transcode   -- Video stream processor.
mplex       -- Command-line utility that combines multiple audio and video streams into an mpeg file.
dvdauthor   -- Command-line utility that makes a DVD filesystem from mpeg files.
growisofs   -- Command-line CD/DVD burning utility.
tovid       -- A collection of command-line scripts that automate detection of video type and transcoding to mpeg format.


I think all of the above programs are in the Ubuntu repositories (can be installed with "sudo aptitude install <package>"). Which one of the above to use depends on exactly what conversion you want to make. For instance "tovid" is a really great way to quickly convert just about anything into an mpeg video (the correct format if you want to then burn to DVD).

Hope that helps.
