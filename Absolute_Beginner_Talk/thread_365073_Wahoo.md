### Post by Resurrection on 2007-02-19
The above is good advice, but I think you will find that you don't really need an antivirus or firewall on Linux.

IMO:  
1)  You are not likely to have to worry about getting hacked with linux unless you drew someone's ire and challenged them to try and get into your specific machine.

2)  Viruses are really a non-issue on linux.  On Ubuntu, since you should not be logged in as "root" (think "administrator" from Windows) it is difficult for any kind of virus to install itself without your permission.  I recommend reading up on "sudo" to understand this concept a little better.  

Now I am sure that there are ways around these things, however I have been using Ubuntu for over a year and haven't even really heard of any one in the forums ever mentioning having a linux virus.
