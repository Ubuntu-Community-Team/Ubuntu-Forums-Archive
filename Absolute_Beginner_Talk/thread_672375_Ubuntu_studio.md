### Post by Presto123 on 2008-01-19
When I did it, I installed directly to the current Ubuntu I had on my laptop. I loved it, but I ran into a problem:

It only has 13 gigs left being a dual-boot with Vista and adding Studio took over every bit of free space I had. Doing it separately is a bit of a pain IMO. If you have plenty of HD space, I would suggest doing it directly to your current Ubuntu. It has loads of cool things, like a weather station (basically) that you can load on and loops with real time weather and radar. :)

If you do back out on it though, the only method I found was to reinstall. SO, as with anything...backup necessary items!

Edit**Like Smartboy says, it won't break a setup. It just adds to what you already have; and he's probably right about getting the ISO.**
