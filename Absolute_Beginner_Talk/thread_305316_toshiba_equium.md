### Post by PartisanEntity on 2006-11-23
I am a complete newb to Linux. 2-3 weeks ago I installed Dapper on my laptop, an Asus A6K. The installation took about 15 mins. Dapper recognised all my hardware, I had no issues.

Wireless will require some time to get to work. The one lesson I learned is that you should first connect using ethernet, this way you can download updates and the necessary packages to get your wireless working. (I tried it the other way round and had a major headache that nearly made me ditch the idea of testing Linux).

Now I use Dapper as my main OS, I have rarely logged into Win XP. And I am using Dapper with my home wireless network using wpa encryption.

I have not tested CD burning yet, but multimedia works if you install the codecs. I listen to mp3's, and watch movies (divx, mp4, avi's etc..)

I would also recommend using Automatix2, it automates the download and install process for a wide range of common applications ranging from multimedia and office to surfing and p2p file sharing.
