### Post by Hendrixski on 2007-04-01
You're going to love college! I was a CS major just a few years ago, here's some of my musings.

This may not be true for other universities but IT in the college I went to consisted of teaching people how to configure windows servers, how to use MS Office, and how to program webpages with ASP, and game development using Microsofts Active X.  While I do hope that your schools IT program will be different I recommend you keep vista for dual boot or on a virtual machine (like VMWare orQemu), and use Ubuntu for when you want your computer to work ... like for taking notes, and stuff.   

Computer Science in my school was programming on Solaris UNIX, although I assume by now they probably have a few windows machines.  It was a lot of theoretical math, and very little actual hands on dealing with systems.  It was hard when I graduated because I could write programs to crack cryptographic cyphers or solve tough math problems, but I couldn't do Windows programming and I was hopeless.

Software engineering was the business end of programming, those guys could program business systems, deal with documentation and all that important stuff. If I had to do college all over again, I would do SE.

Anyway.  Don't kid yourself.  You will use your computer for taking notes exactly ONCE in college... after that you will use it for downloading music, looking at porn, and playing video games (don't do too much of that though or you WILL flunk out, it's worse than having a drinking problem).  If you want to avoid that, and instead do something useful like join an open source project (which I WISH I had done in College instead of going to all those fraternity parties) then I recommend you spend most of your time on Ubuntu.

While I did not go through an IT course in uni, I used Ubuntu for an engineering course to say 99.99%. I use Fortran/Python/Octave to program some of my codes to run. Note taking and many other general stuff can be easily done via Ubuntu.
