### Post by regeya on 2006-11-03
A lot of KDE and GNOME apps are similar with different interfaces; many of them you could just go with what your desk env has and you'll be happy.

Having said that:

[LIST]
[*]If you're looking for a decent GUI code editor, I prefer Kate to GEdit.
[*]For burning any sort of conceivable CDs and DVDs from a GUI, k3b is hard to beat on ANY platform.
[*]While I don't currently use it, AmaroK will whip Banshee and Rhythmbox's rear ends any day. (Personally, I use a GTK+ app under KDE named Quod Libet.  So very flexible.)
[*]A nice alternative to toDVD is ManDVD, not available via official Ubuntu repos, but available as a .deb on kde-apps.org.
[*]DigiKam FTW!  Sure, you get folder browsing with gThumbs and you get photo collection management with F-Spot, but digiKam does it all, and with kipi-plugins adds all sorts of things like DVD slideshows, photo editing, calendars, and more.
[*]Konqueror.  Home to kHTML, the engine that became Apple WebKit.  'Nuff said.
[*]Though it's a purely Qt app, I'll add Scribus to the list.  In the open-source world, nothing comes closer, and its xPDF export functionality is excellent.
[/LIST]

Aside from that, a lot of apps tend to tie in to the KDE desktop, and others won't be noticeably different in their feature list to make it worth it.  Personally, no matter which desktop I use, I use a mix of KDE and GNOME apps anyway (QtCurve is a lovely theme engine, and gtk-qt is a neat idea that needs some work) and tend to pick what works best for me.  And yes, I tend to curse the fact that we have what is essentially a redundant desktop running around ;)

### Post by Arisna on 2006-11-03
A nice little Qt app that ships with kubuntu-desktop is a calculator called SpeedCrunch.  It supports functions, variables, etc., similar to what you'd expect from a good scientific/graphing calculator.

For games, I like Kolf, a miniature golf game in which you can cheat by editing levels on-the-fly. :P  I also like KSokoban, a cool little puzzle game.

KMail and especially KOrganizer beat Evolution any day, in my opinion.  I've found Evolution's calendar feature to be a little flaky sometimes, but even my busy schedule can't break KOrganizer. <_<

KOffice is an office suite that is somewhat tougher to figure out than other office suites I've used, but light, fast, and with lots of components you don't get in OpenOffice/GNOME Office.

To name some little utilities, I love Keep, a KDE interface to rdiff-backup, and Kron, an interface for job scheduling and environment variables.  Finally, Katapult is pretty neat.  It's a quick application launcher you activate with a keyboard shortcut, and has no GTK equivalent to my knowledge.
