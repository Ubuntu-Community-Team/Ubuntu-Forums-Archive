Choose the ISO option, wait for K3b to check the file (scroll down the file information in the K3b window and you will see a progress bar creeping accross the screen. When the check is done a green check will appear in front of a series of numbers and letters.) Once the file check is done, choose your burn speed and burn away.

Once burned, the disc will eject. Just reboot the PC if you wish to install from that disc and it should boot, assuming you have the PC set to boot first from the CD/DVD player. You can change the settings via the BIOS.

### Post by Kobalt on 2007-04-03
There are no .exe files in Ubuntu/Linux.
Is your .iso files the size of a CD (~700Mo) or the size of a DVD ? When you know this, you've got the answer to your first question. 
You can simply burn an image making a right click on it and selecting "burn cd/dvd image...". One advise though, burn it at 4x speed max, it's safer to get a working iso disk.
After that you should get a disk that's bootable, meaning you can reboot your computer with the disk in the drive and it will boot on automatically.
