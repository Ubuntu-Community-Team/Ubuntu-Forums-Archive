### Post by az on 2007-06-29
> **sublimation said:**
> Why did the original Ubuntu project decide to use Gnome? I assume that Kubuntu and Xubuntu came after Ubuntu to extend to other desktops, but I'm just curious why Gnome was chosen first.

Reason 1 - Canonical's third employee (the third person they hired) was Jeff Waugh.  At the time, he was the gnome project's release manager.
Reason 2-  Ubuntu's aim was to deliver the best of the fast-paced Ubuntu Sid (unstable) branch could deliver.  Gnome had decided to go to a time-based (six-month) release schedule at the time.  That suited Ubuntu's goal to deliver a  six month release schedule so they are synchronised.
Reason 3-  At the time, Gnome offered a more structured and adaptable platform.  For example, Gnome moved to HAL and Udev before KDE.  Gnome had established human interface guidelines and were just, in general, better organised at the time.  That may be different now in that KDE have cleaned up things.
