### Post by CurlyChris on 2005-06-16
As a humble health professional, not an IT specialist, and coming from WinXP, I really enjoyed "Linux for Non-Geeks" by Rickford Grant (pubs No Starch Press) as my first Linux book.  Although based on Fedora Core 1 it helped me to understand the basics of the Linux system, even though I was using Mepis at the time.  Since switching to Ubuntu only a month ago, it's helped me find my way around the Gnome desktop pretty quickly, too, and I still dip into it now and again.

Even though the author started writing it as a guide for his mum  :)  I found it a great preparation before progressing on to more technical info such as "The Linux Pocket Guide" by Daniel Barrett (still Fedora based, though) and finally "Linux in a Nutshell" by Siever, Figgins & Weber (both from O'Reilly).

My only other learning resources since I first started looking at linux just over a year ago have been magazines and the web.

But I must also say that since I switched to Ubuntu I haven't yet had to post any questions here, as a combination of the unofficial ubuntu guide ([http://www.ubuntuguide.org](http://www.ubuntuguide.org)) and searching on this forum has always provided the solution to any problem I've had!  :grin:
