### Post by randy78 on 2008-02-13
You may also want to run a port scan against your own computer to see which ports are open and then take the appropriate steps to lock them down.

You can go to the ShieldsUp section of [www.grc.com](www.grc.com) and once there, you can perform an online port scan.
Just remember that if you get a 100% green result, but still fail, your HARDWARE firewall is probably responding, if you are using one.

Beefing up your HARDWARE firewall is also suggested, and will prevent your router (again, if you are using a router) from replying the ping, or any ping for that matter.

I use a Westell model, and have everything set to medium, which allows me to allow services by hand, if I need to.

You can also look up the IP address, just go to Google and search for WHOIS

Good luck
