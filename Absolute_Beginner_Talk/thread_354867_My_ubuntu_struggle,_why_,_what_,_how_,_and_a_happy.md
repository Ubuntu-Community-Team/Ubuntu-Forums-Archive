### Post by alexjamestodd on 2007-02-06
I really wanted to write this two months ago when I first discovered ubuntu. I forget how I made the discovery , but was fed up with windows xp, which I had modified completely. I was using a dell latitude 64 ram pentium 2. I was also disturbed by the new windows vista , codename longhorn, Longhorn for those that dont know is the name of the hand sign that rockers , satanists and even worse , George bushvlike to use. Anyway im no christian and am not easily offended, I just thought that now microsoft were taking the PISS!. 

so it was a breath of fresh air when the first alternate os I stumbled across (ubuntu), was aimed at human beings , had earthly tones, and the best forum for support that i have ever seen.
It was my thinking that even though my laptop was v.v.v.v.v.v old , there must be billions of others in the world that cant afford the latest technology and software . Ubuntu appeared to be the answer.

Anyway, my first set back was when i discovered that ubuntu s system requirements were going to deny me the chance to use it, ok no biggie , xubuntu, and fluxbuntu are available. Though both were nice neither were as quick as my highly modified , highly illegal "tinyxp beast edition" Also being completely new to any linux I was getting slightly frustrated.
Stioll i decided to keep working at it. By now i had discovered that linux was not just 1 os but hundreds of different variations, each aimed towards different users with different needs, this i liked. So first off was an old version of vector linux, too slow. Next was puppy , quick but ugly and with too few extra apps( another thing that immediately appealed to me about ubuntu was the huge amounts of free, open source software) , next was dsl, quick , fairly pretty, too few apps.

So my struggle to find the perfect os continued, i looked at bsd, free bsd etc, but considering the reasons that i dropped windows, those little devils put me off.


Then the breakthrough I was looking for, My laptop was dying, 1 boot in 6 and screen freezes galore.

The answer , a second hand toshiba targa s1 , 512 ram(to me this is loads) and a good processor.

Did I go straight out and download ubuntu, no . On my distro travels i discovered many distros that i liked but decided were to big for my old laptop. 

dream linux- beautiful, not quite stable

and the best yet - elive!   outstanding all round and based on debian- could this be everything i was looking for, no                   the cursor took ages to move across the screen , a tiny problem you may think, but trust me it can get annoying very quickly.


By now i had fallen in love with enlightenment, so i did some research on google and found "installing enlightenment the easy way- ubuntu forum"
Now, ubuntu with enlightenment , this could be it i thought, so i quickly installed the latest ubuntu, followed the instructions for installin g enlightenment, I NOW HAVE THE PERFECT OS THAT I WAS LOOKING FOR, THE BEAUTEY OF ENLIGHTENMENT ,PLUS ALL THE JOY OF UBUNTU 
(NOTE TO DEVELEPORS, THIS HAS TO BE THE WAY FORWARD FOR UBUNTU, YOU HAVE TO LOOK DIFFERENT TO OTHER DISTROS, AND TOO MANY USE GNOME)



Thanks for letting me get that off my chest :)
