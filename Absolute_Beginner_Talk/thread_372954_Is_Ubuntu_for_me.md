### Post by userundefine on 2007-03-02
> If you ever get to witness it deflecting a trident mediashare attack, you might change your mind! I wouldn't ever want to do without that sort of program.
While you think you're beating back bad IPs, what you're actually doing is thwarting KNOWN IP ranges of anti-piracy hunters.  You'd have to be kidding yourself if you think that all the IP ranges are known of every company that the MPAA/RIAA contracts.  That would entirely defeat their purpose for existence.  And, in addition to that, you get a ton of false positives.  The programs are useless and a false sense of security.  But if that's what you're into...

> What is the difference between Gnome and KDE? I read something yesterday about the Linux founder criticising Gnome for being too limited and preferring KDE. It gave me the idea that Gnome is for Linux beginners and KDE for experts. Is that a fair way of summarising it? Would I find more flexibility for doing the things I listed in my first post in Kubuntu?
KDE and Gnome are two different desktop environments.  Linus Torvalds was just expressing his own opinion, one which is controversial and not shared by everyone.  Try both and decide for yourself which one you like better and which one suits your needs.

> Will my ATi graphics card be able to display anything while I'm trying to set up the acceleration?
Oh yes, of course.  The acceleration is only based on the ATI driver.  There are several drivers that will be installed by default and will give you a full, 24 million-color GUI.  But you wouldn't be able to play games or have great video playback with those drivers.  You need the ATI drivers for that.
