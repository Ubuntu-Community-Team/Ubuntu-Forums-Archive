### Post by kidders on 2006-09-02
I once got ActiveX to work by installing MSIE on my Linux box. I'm not *entirely* sure it's legal to do that though, so I won't go into it. Suffice it to say that Google is your friend :P

The other thing you may be interested in is Crossover Office. Wine has a number of spin-offs, tweaked to help you get through things that are typically kinda tricky. I imagine getting ActiveX to cooperate qualifies as "tricky"! Crossover Office will help you put MS Office, MSIE, etc. on your Linux box (god knows why you'd want to lol!). Another example is Cedega, which has ready-made DirectX support for playing games n such.

I guess my point is that if generic Wine is giving you grief, you can always have a look for one of its cousins that might be tuned to do a better job.
