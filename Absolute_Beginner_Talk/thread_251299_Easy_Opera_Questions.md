### Post by kidders on 2006-09-05
Hi there,

1. All good mail clients block images that aren't part of the email message itself by default, to prevent their users falling victim to a common spamming technique that relies upon the retrieval of "images" to verify the existence of an email address. Once you've opened an email, and can see that it's genuine, you can then choose to load the images.

2. I'm not aware of a calendar/todo extension for Opera. Oddly enough, Opera seems keen on ignoring everyone else's apps and redesigning things that have already been written, for themselves. (Opera's widgets are a good example.) So, even if there *were* a calendar app for Opera, it would most likely not work with any of the others on your system.

3. Displaying an entire web page in a feed would defeat the purpose of RSS.

### Post by gkiller on 2006-09-05
1) If you really want images to show in the mail, despite the warnings above: In the mail view toolbar, navigate to View -> Display -> Block external contents and disable it.

This setting is global, so it affects all mails. You should switch it off again after viewing a particular mail.
