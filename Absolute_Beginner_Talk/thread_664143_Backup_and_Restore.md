### Post by EXCiD3 on 2008-01-11
If you are having problems with the laptop overheating you should buy a cooling pad or at least tip the laptop up to allow more room for air to blow through. I recently ran into the problem of playing Battlefield 2 for more than 10 mins and my laptop would overheat and shutoff. Put it on a cooling pad and it worked perfect for hours. You also might want to open up your laptop and clean it out with some compressed air. Dust clogs on the heatsink and can cause your laptop to overheat especially if its an older laptop.
