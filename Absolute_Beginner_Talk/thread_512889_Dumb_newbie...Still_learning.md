### Post by raja on 2007-07-29
Why are there so many distros ?
Because Linux is finally a collection of programs and all of them are opensource. So I may decide to put them together in a way that i like and release a new distro. It helps that Linux is so modular. The difference between the distros finally boils down to ease of installation, level of control and package management. Ubuntu has established itself as one of the best distros to start with. But my belief is that Once you get used to it, you should try out some other ones - I have found Arch very impressive.

About Beryl and others..
I dont think it is just eyecandy. I have been using Beryl regularly now. I have learnt to disable fancy effects, but depend a bit on the fast desktop switching, the scale feature, annotate feature, zoom and so on. And you get all this with very little additional processing power.

A "distro" is simply a collection of Linux programs put together. Therefore, I could take a kernel, put a few programs on it, put it all on a CD, and say that it's a distro. Other people have done just that, and one of those collections is the Ubuntu system you are working from right now. 

Beryl and Compiz are eyecandy at their core, but some things, like Annotate and the cube are useful. Experiment and see what works.
