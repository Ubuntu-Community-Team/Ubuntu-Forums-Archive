Edgy have many advantages like faster boot times and better hardware support. The upgrade takes a while, when I upgraded from dapper to Edgy there was almost 650 mb to download, and yesterday I upgraded to Feisty (still on beta) and the upgrade was something in the 850 mb( depending on your internet connection you can make a guess of how much the upgrade process will take)

### Post by jem7v on 2007-04-13
The software packages (both frontends and backends) in Edgy are more up to date than Dapper.  As such, some things have new features, some things run better, etc, etc.  I definitely saw some stability improvements in the Gnome applications from 6.06 to 6.10.  If you're happy with Dapper it might be worth staying with that...

...but if you Really want an up to date, cutting edge OS, why not wait a couple weeks and install 7.04 Feisty Fawn instead?


Edit:
Oh, right.  You can't upgrade straight from 6.06 to 7.04 because you can't skip Upgrades.  However, if you moved your /home folder onto a separate partition you can just install 7.04 straight over 6.06 without losing the stuff in your /home directory.  [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/separatehome) for how to do that.

P.S. I wouldn't recommend using the system While packages are being upgraded because it seemed (to me) to increase the chance of breakage when I did it last time.
