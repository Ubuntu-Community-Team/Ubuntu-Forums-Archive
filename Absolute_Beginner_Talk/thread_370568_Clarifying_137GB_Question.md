### Post by yabbadabbadont on 2007-02-25
Common forums courtesy is to wait a couple of hours before bumping a thread.  Next time you do it, we'll have to give you a "time out".  :lol:

To answer your question, the BIOS is where the limitation exists.  However, the BIOS is only used long enough to load the kernel and transfer control to it.  Once the kernel is loaded, Linux is in control and it won't have any trouble seeing the full drive size.  I hope that clears things up.
