### Post by Borniet on 2006-05-05
First of all, what do you WANT to do with your computer? In windows indeed, you have to start installing drivers and software and the whole bunch. None of this in Linux. Alot of it will already work out of the box, and alot (a whole lot) of 'standard' and 'not so standard' software is already installed too, depending on the options you choose while installing.
As for the problems you mentioned above:
1. this is rather normal, has alot to do with what you are all starting up of services and servers (no worries, they are harmless). I've also noticed for my own pc that the booting goes quite fast, but then looses alot of time with the network configuration (which is due to the DHCP process), and then goes fast again.
2. No worries here either.

So.. what do you want to do with your fresh PC?

### Post by skippy81 on 2006-05-05
If you click on the help icon (lifebelt logo) on the top menu, you will be able to find your way to the starter's guide.  I actually found a lot of interesting stuff to do in here , and it will help you get all the file repositories set up easily - you will then have tons of software you can install instantly.   It also tells you how to get setup for playing MP3s and movies.  

Once you are able to download packages using synaptic, you can start installing stuff :) There are literally thousands of packages to choose from, just grab stuff that takes your interest. You can easily remove it again.

When I first started using linux, my favourite activities were exploring the filesystem and config files, learning how to use the terminal and playing with nmap.  One of my fondest expieriences with linux was when I was packet sniffing my home network for the first time - it's really surreal being able to see your network data moving around :)  Its fun stuff like that that linux really excels at.  

Setting up your desktop to look pretty is also a good way to waste some time.  Go through all your programs and try them out, I was loving the Gaim instant messenger when I started using Ubuntu.  IRC is another fun thing that people for some reason only get into when they start using linux.   

As for your slow loadup times, that is something you can optimise once you have learnt the ropes a bit :)
