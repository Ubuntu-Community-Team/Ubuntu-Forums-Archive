### Post by turbojugend_gr on 2006-11-30
My answer sortly ; GNOME.

KDE is actually equially customizable, the thing is it is not as stable after heavy customizing imho, and in my little experience with KDE (I actually installd kde for that reason, havily customize it, it rather failed my standarts, in stability, but I was amazed looking at my Baghire os x emulation). Generally if you wish to have your PC customized for show- off, then KDE will suit you, but if just tweaking your environment to match your everyday need in look 'n' fell then Gnome is more stable, and very easy to customize. PLZ also take note, that beryl, worlds best composite manager (Vista's aero, is like a mozquito thing to us.. hehe), still depends on some Gnome Libs (this is on it's way to be solved) so it will run better and more stable in Gnome, for the time being at least.

A nice way to experiment with you Gnome (or KDE), is to create a new user account, and completely mess it to the ground (just make sure you will not use sudo nowhere there), trying to see what is possible with your Gnome, delete/remove/completely change anything in your panels, add anything you see fit on those, add a whole bunch of desklets (adesklets, or gdesklets), add a wonderfull conky setup, change wallpapers/themes/icons, try out experimental stuff from gnome-look.org and I don't waht else it takes to make your wonderfull dekstop (or ruin the new users environmet), anyway when you are done learning, go back to your main login and wisely make your cahnges there... beware Ubuntu can be made to look (and fell) so different you won't be able to live without your unique box after creating it.. I can't at least... Linux is all about Freedom, get hold of it!
