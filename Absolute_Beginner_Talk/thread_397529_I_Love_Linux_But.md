### Post by Yoooder on 2007-03-30
Firstly, are you running the 64 bit version of Ubuntu or the 32?  The 32 is much more stable (but less suitable for some Open Source video apps like Cinelerra).

As for your media playback, Ubuntu Edgy does have some shortcomings--however Ubuntu Feisty (currently in beta) improves on multimedia integration and playback quite a bit.  Feisty is set to released in the 2nd half of April (but you can get the beta now if you like).

As for your video editing needs, Linux may not be your best solution.  Linux has some decent video editors, but they aren't anything like a professional package--and you will invest a good amount more time editing if you use the OS apps.

As for your specific applications, you may be able to run them in Wine or crossover.  Perhaps even in a VM--but you will suffer a performance hit.

If you're serious about video editing, you should probably get a Mac.  Linux is improviing on it, but it's not on par with many of the major applications by a long shot.
