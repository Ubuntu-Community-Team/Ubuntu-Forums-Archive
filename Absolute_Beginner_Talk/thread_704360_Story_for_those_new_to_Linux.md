### Post by Hendrixski on 2008-02-22
I would like to share this story with all the beginners on the forums.  It's kind of funny, and will kind of give a picture of what it's like to use Ubuntu in the business environment.


I'm in a startup, and we're all Linux based, and we are getting help from a business incubator.  So we wanted to make use of their printers and they sent us a file on how to connect.. it was of course how to get Windows computers to connect, and they told us that nobody seems to be able to get it to work, so they also gave us the number for tech support.

We looked at the WIndows-specific manual and just called tech support they're like "*I swear to God, nobody at that place uses Windows, we get a hundred calls a day on how to get that printer to work with  Mac and Linux.  Just go to the Xerox website and download the drivers*".

So we got that all set up, and talked to the incubator staff, and sure enough, they say that all the other startups are using Ubuntu, Mac, Fedora, or whatnot.  So I just typed up quick version of the instruction manual for the setting up the printer and sent it to the.... as an ODF of course :-D

At first they couldn't open it in Word.  So I sent them a link to the upgrade at [http://www.sun.com/software/star/odf_plugin/get.jsp](http://www.sun.com/software/star/odf_plugin/get.jsp) so that they can fix their broken word-processing software and have it read ODF documents.

:-) So, now all the other startups there don't have to bother the tech support guys, they're going to get an ODF that tells them how to set up the network printer.... and not some Windows-only bullcrap.


Doesn't that just make you feel warm inside!!
