Well it's time for me to pony up with something to help others, as you all have helped me out allot. I was inquiring about e-book the other day on another thread so I could learn how this stuff works and I came up with a couple of good ones for me that are easy to understand for the biggest Linux newbie: ME!

I recommend these FREE PDF downloads, just stick them on your desktop for easy access.

1). Introduction to Linux - A Hands on Guide 
[http://www.tldp.org/LDP/intro-linux/intro-linux.pdf]("http://www.tldp.org/LDP/intro-linux/intro-linux.pdf")

2). Linux Dictionary  [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Linux-Dictionary/Linux-Dictionary.pdf]("http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Linux-Dictionary/Linux-Dictionary.pdf")

3). Bash Guide for Beginners  [http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/Bash-Beginners-Guide.pdf]("http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/Bash-Beginners-Guide.pdf")

There is more stuff at; [http://www.gayanb.com/free_linux_books.php]("http://www.gayanb.com/free_linux_books.php")

In just 2 hours I have learned soooooo much that I am now quite confident that I will finally have a strong grasp at Linux.

I hope this helps at least one person as much as it has helped me.
