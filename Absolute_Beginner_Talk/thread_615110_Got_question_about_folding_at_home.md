### Post by carlosjuero on 2007-11-16
> **fedex1993 said:**
> what exactly does folding@home do what does it o and is it working and why is it needed?
Folding@Home is a distributed computing application that is helping the sponsoring university fold genes for determining gene therapy and other things. It takes and combines the processing power of thousands of client computers through the internet, and uses that power to fold the genes - something that usually requires a Super Computer, the whole process turns every helping computer into a node of one of the largest super computers imaginable.

When you run it, it downloads data regarding certain parts of the gene and starts crunching the numbers; the data is then sent back to the main host and added to the extrapolation.

It isn't **needed**, but it is a helpful thing to run if you are into that sort of thing.
