Linux is remarkably stable; most users speak of their uptime it terms of months.
If you are experiencing crashes, then you could have a bad install.  That is on the software side...
 
On the hardware side, only you can tell or suspect that you may have mainboard problems, etc.
 
I suggest you download the Alternate Install CD (.iso) for Ubuntu 7.04 Feisty Fawn,
and check the md5 hash for the file you downloaded, immediately after downloading (and before burning).
 
Then burn the .iso to CD, and before installing, go through the "Verify CD integrity" routine.  This will, in turn, verify the contents as 100% correct, before installing those contents from CD.
