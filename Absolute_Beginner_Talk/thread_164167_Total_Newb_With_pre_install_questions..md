### Post by louis_nichols on 2006-04-22
it is possible to run windows apps on Linux, using a package called [wine]("http://www.winehq.org"). It won't always be easy, though, and with some apps it will be darn right impossible. Still, help on this matter is better found on their site, I think. Threads and posts on this exist here on Ubuntu forums aswell, though. Just perform a search with keyword "wine".

Furthermore, it is possible, to my knowledge, to get, for a monthly fee or smth, professional support for wine. I think it's codeweavers that do this. So you just pay them and they have a list of software that they guarantee they will get working on your machine.

My opinion is that switching from Windows to Ubuntu in particular is not hard at all. You just have to want it and the rest is simple.

Not to put a dampen on your enthusiasm too much, but I think your expectations may be a little high with regards to what you will be able to succesfully achieve with linux.  On the games side of things, you are probably going to find it easier to run them on windows.  On the office side of things if you have any specialised software that is native to windows, there are no guarantees that you will get it working well in linux, nor will you necessarily be able to find a linux equivalent.  I encourage you to give it a try, but be prepared for some disappointment and frustration. :)
