### Post by leifharmsen on 2007-03-16
I'm a new linux user too.  I too switched to get out from being led around by the nose by MS.  Keep in mind that the fact that we both wound up with a Canoscan scanner is part of the annoyance cost of Windows - nobody told us it was operating system specific when we bought it, I guess that just wasn't a selling point.    It is cheaper probably to buy a new scanner than to take the time to write a driver, for any one person.  Having said that, if you do write a driver let the world know, so we can keep more MS-rubbish out of landfill and help save our environment.   I'll let you know if I get the stupid piece of Canon junk working.  Canon is infamous for sucky proprietary nonsense, a good reason to avoid that particular problem brand.

As for your SLR, regardless of your operating system it is best practice to not connect with a wire but to use a proper card reader - for a number of practical and technical reasons.  USB card readers are supported extremely well in Ubuntu.  Another problem is various proprietary image formats that some cameras generate (including Canon), but fortunately someone wrote a free utility that converts these to a useful format (like tif) that actually works much better (ie much better quality image) than the Windows specific crapware that comes with the camera.
