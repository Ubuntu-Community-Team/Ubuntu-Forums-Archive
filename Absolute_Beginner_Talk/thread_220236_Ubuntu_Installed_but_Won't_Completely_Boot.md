### Post by Paradoxical on 2006-07-21
I managed to install Ubuntu on my 60GB external drive (USB2.0) with the alternate CD, and everything was peachy until I booted for the first time. It went through the entire sequence, up to the point at which you put in your username/password. It froze at the blinking cursor just before that login. It wouldn't respond to anything other than pressing the power button, and that resulted in seeing the login entry but not being able to use it because Ubuntu was shutting down.

What can I do?

---

### Post by ChrisDerson on 2006-07-21
When you get the login screen, try pressing ALT-CTRL-F2 at the same time - you should get a text only terminal screen.  Login, and then type **df** and hit enter, you'll get a summary of used disk space.  When I have had a similar problem in the past I've run out of disk-space.  Either way, post the results.
