### Post by pizzutz on 2007-10-09
Congratulations on talking the plunge!  If you are indeed ready to format you drive and start from scratch, there s nothing special you need to do.  You will need at least 3 partitions.  I leave about 10GB for the / partition, 2-3GB for swap, and allocate the rest for your home directory.  Do manual partitioning when you install, so you have control over the size of the partitions.  depending on the size of you drive, and how much space you need for your home dir,  you may also consider leaving another 10GB partition open to install other OS's like the Hardy beta, or (god forbid) other Linux distros.  That way, you can boot into other systems to try them out while keeping your home directory, without messing with your main operating system.
