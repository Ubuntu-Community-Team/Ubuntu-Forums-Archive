### Post by HereInOz on 2007-09-15
I have found that WPA support in Ubuntu is a little patchy, with some cards refusing to connect to a WPA-PSK secured network.  If you have control of the network, then try taking off the security, and seeing if you get a connection, then enable WEP, and see how you go there.

This won't help in your quest to connect your card to the WPA-PSK network, I agree, but it will let you know whether a connection is possible at all and how far you can go with the security.

Clearly if you can connect using WEP, but not WPA=PSK, it is a problem with the driver for the card and you might want to look at Gutsy which will have much better wireless support.
