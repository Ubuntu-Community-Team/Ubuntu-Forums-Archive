### Post by CSMatt on 2007-09-29
That depends on what you men by "compatible."  From what I understand most players are USB mass storage devices, and the kernel will mount them automatically as USB drives.  However I would imagine that most player firmware has some unique way of recognizing the media that isn't as simple as copying it to the mounted player.  I do know that the iPod can be used with Rhythmbox and possibly Amarok too (except for the new ones, which use a new - albeit already reverse-engineered - database that probably isn't in either Rhythmbox or Amarok right now).  You can also buy a player that runs [Rockbox](http://www.rockbox.org) and install Rockbox on it.  Rockbox actually searches the player for any media no matter where it is on the device, so you can just copy it with Nautilus or Konqurer onto the mounted player.  Rockbox also supports Vorbis and FLAC, and there's an MPEG plug-in provided that the videos are already at the correct resolution.
