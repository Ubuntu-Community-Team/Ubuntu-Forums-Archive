### Post by Starks on 2007-04-21
Lets say I have an ipw3945 card in my laptop. Is there any significant difference between the free driver built in, an ndiswrapper'd driver, and the restricted drivers?

---

### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-04-21
Yes. Ndisrapper is a project to allow you to use Windows drivers on Linux. The card you've mentioned has a driver made for it. It should be loaded, so there is no need for ndisrapper. Restricted is usually the stuff that does not comply with Ubuntu philosophy of openness, or having legal restrictions.
