### Post by jem7v on 2007-01-09
GNOME is very straightforward.  You can access MOST programs, settings, parts of your system, etc with 2-3 clicks of the mouse.  Things are layed out relatively logically, and very few things need a lot of setting up.  (The down side to this would be that many things that might normally have user-editable settings do Not... but the default settings are usually pretty good anyway.)  It's easy to adjust your menu bars and where things are, because almost every part of your GNOME toolbars and the likes can be dragged and dropped into a new location with the Move function in the right-click context menu.  KDE can probably do this too, I would assume... I just prefer GNOME.

KDE is probably the more "powerful" of the two, but GNOME is definitely the simpler, more intuitive desktop to use... and considering that you can use KDE programs in GNOME and vice versa, I'd probably just go with the easier desktop environment.
