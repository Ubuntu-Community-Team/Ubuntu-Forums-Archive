### Post by nocturn on 2005-07-07
[QUOTE=snoopgst]what are the differences? and is ubuntu - gnome & kubuntu - KDE?

thanks[/QUOTE]

Gnome is build on another graphical toolkit (Gtk) then KDE (Qt), this brings about some of the differences.
But in addition, the focus of the projects is very different, resulting in different design decisions and different functionality.

Ubuntu has Gnome, while Kubuntu has KDE.
You can cross install them by installing Kubuntu-desktop or Ubuntu-desktop.

### Post by manicka on 2005-07-07
I ran kde for many years and was quite zealous about it and as a newbie it probably suited me in my transition from windows. Since switching to Ubuntu about a month ago and using gnome full time I can now see why people like it so much. 

I still run the odd kde app like amaroK and klipper, but on the whole I'm pretty much a convert to Gnome. I find that it is far more responsive and is all round a better work environment. 

As code junkie said, KDE probably has a bit more eye candy but it comes at a performance price, although gnome also has some neet tricks when you start to look under the hood. Gnome to me just feels more professional and what Linux is all about. 

Gnome also feels similar to OSX which I use at work everyday, so this has also influenced my decision to stick with it. I guess you could say that I'm competely hooked and won't be changing back ;)
