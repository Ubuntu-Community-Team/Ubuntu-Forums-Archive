### Post by MoebusNet on 2008-02-05
Before you buy a UPS (a good idea in any case) or rewire your house you can attempt this for free:

If all of your computer components (CPU, monitor, printer, external hard drive, room lamp, shredder, etc.) are all plugged into the same wall outlet, why not try to distribute the electrical load to more than one wall outlet? Preferably a wall outlet that is on a different circuit breaker than you are presently using.

For example, place your CPU, monitor, printer and external drive on one circuit and your room lamp, shredder and other appliances on a different circuit. This will distribute the voltage load more evenly across your electrical supply panel and reduce the demand on any one leg of your electrical supply. Note that to do this, you may need to run an extension cord into a different room to get your power from a different circuit protected by a different circuit breaker. Use a good quality extension cord (12 gauge wire would be best). If money is tight you can probably just borrow one for the day to try it out.

Again, the object of the game is to have your computer components on one circuit breaker and your appliances on a different circuit breaker. Best of luck!
