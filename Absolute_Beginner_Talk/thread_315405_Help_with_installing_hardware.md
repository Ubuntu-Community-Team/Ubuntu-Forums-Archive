### Post by lyceum on 2006-12-10
When it comes to hardware, I have learned to live and learn. I search the net, find what I need, make sure it works with Linux, buy it, take it home, then find out it is model "A" and I needed "B7". I have found the easiest way around that is to, 

A. get used parts, and return them if they don't work. This works for me on two levels, the people in the stores now know who I am, and they are into Ubuntu too, so they let me know what doesn't work when they can. And I find that really new parts are less likely to work with Ubuntu if the box doesn't say they will.

B. Look for the Penguin.

These are my two easy ways of doing things. I hope I am not coming off like a jerk, I guess I am just trying to say, lug 'em in and see what happens!
