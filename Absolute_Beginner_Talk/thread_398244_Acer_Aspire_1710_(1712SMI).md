### Post by Kobalt on 2007-04-04
The best would be to check it for yourself : you can download and burn a LiveCD of Ubuntu that you will place in your CD/DVD drive, then reboot. An Ubuntu session will run off the CD, affecting none of your data. You will run Ubuntu just as if it was installed (though it's much slower from the LiveCD) and check for yourself what's working and what's not, according to your needs. 
Once you have that little list come back here and ask your questions precisely... 

Here are a couple of useful links you might want to check out in order to have a good start : 
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso)
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing)
