### Post by Trail on 2008-02-03
From a programmer's point of view...

After a period of getting used to it, Linux now seems to make much more sense than Windows ever did. I know or can learn how everything actually works, as opposed to a closed-source software.

Another major issue is that GNU/Linux applications are developped by people that are users themselves; in that, they know what they need from that application. It might not sometimes have the polish or documentation that a commercial product might have, but its functionality is guided by users needs and NOT by deadlines and business decisions (think IE integration in windows to battle netscape etc). And, usually, support is better than windows. Someone has a problem with some application? He will rarely get a "it cannot be done" from a company that is not interested in solving such a problem, but he has a bunch of geeks with access to the source code to help him. Not to mention, older versions are still supported.

Anyway, I think the open source world is very much different than Windows. It does get some time to get used to it, but after that you'll be thinking "oh my god, why did i ever spend time with closed-source products?"
