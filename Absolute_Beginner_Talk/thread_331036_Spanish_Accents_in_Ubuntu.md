### Post by ubuntu27 on 2007-01-04
Right click on a panel and choose "Add to panel"

in the new windows, search for "Character Palette" (it's in the utilities section), select it, and then click on "Add"

There, now you have a palette to let you insert symbols and all those stuff.

ñÑ á é

1) Choose the character that you want to insert by clicking on it
2a) Right click and paste in the OO.o or any app
2b) Ctrl+v  (shortcut for Paste in a american keyboard)

### Post by dr_d12 on 2007-01-30
:::I find that this is the quickest way if you want to actually type the characters (short of learning a spanish keyboard layout):::

Rightclick on the panel and add the Keyboard Indicator applet. Mine says USA.

Right-click it and open up keyboard preferences. 

Click add and select "US English International (with dead keys)".

Toggle between the layouts by clicking once on the applet. USA* is the international layout.

With USA* activated, you type ' then the vowel:
' + a = á
' + e = é
' + i = í
' + o = ó
' + u = ú

~ + n = ñ
" + u = ü

To get a ` or ~ you have to type them twice:
' + ' = '

For the punctuation, hold down the Alt key (only the right one works on my keyboard):
Alt+1 = ¡
Alt+/ = ¿

### Post by Golem XIV on 2008-03-31
Use a US International keyboard layout (with dead keys).  Typing ' followed by a vowel will give you the accented letter - á, é, ó, etc. while typing the tilde (~) followed by n or N will give you ñ or Ñ.
