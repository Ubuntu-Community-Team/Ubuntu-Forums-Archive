### Post by drewster1829 on 2007-11-16
Okay, I FINALLY figured out the problem.
> 
:-[ PERFORM OPC failed with SK=3h/ASC=73h/ACQ=03h]: Input/output error

Which is the power calibration area error. ([List of possible error codes and descriptions]("http://pcburn.com/article.php?sid=2127"))

OPC stands for "Optimal Power Calibration".  Essentially, the drive will write a test sequence onto a special calibration section of the disc, in order to set the laser strength and verify the ability to write.  If there are errors, it will not burn anything else, and will return an error.

Looking this up on Google, I am informed that this is usually caused by bad media, a bad drive (or bad firmware), or dust/dirt on the lens of the laser.  I cleaned my lens (BTW, use compressed air or an alcohol wipe, or maybe one of those drive cleaners....do NOT use tap water, or physically wiping it, because if you scratch it or get deposits on it, you'll be buying a new drive.  Also, be careful about static electricity in your body...ground yourself to the computer chassis before getting your hands anywhere near the inside of the drive), and solved the problem.  Do this AT YOUR OWN RISK.  I am not liable for any damage you do to your drive by taking (or not taking) my advice. **Disclaimer over**: :lolflag:

This is one of the things that drives me crazy about Linux.  Instead of saying "Power Calibration Area Error", I get SK=3h/ASC=73h/ACQ=03h...it took me a month to figure out this was an error code (Googling the whole line would usually get me about two results, both unresolved problems).  A better way would be:
> 
:-[ PERFORM OPC failed with Power Calibration Area Error
(SK=3h/ASC=73h/ACQ=03h): Input/output error
This could be caused by bad media, a bad drive, or a dirty lens.

Or something like that written in English.  There are so many people who have posted about growisofs errors who don't have a clue (like I didn't for two months) why they can't burn their CDs/DVDs.

Anyway, I hope this helps someone out there.  And PLEASE PLEASE PLEASE be careful if you try to clean your drive.  They are rather delicate.
