### Post by Raavea on 2006-08-11
My GNOME install came with several 'high-contrast' themes.

 I think he meant that, if you've installed gnome ubuntu, you should be able to enter the 'gnome-theme-manager' command in the KDE terminal and the gnome manager will open. Then you can modify gnome from KDE, before you even enter GNOME.

 Btw, I think the gnome high-contrast themes are fugly...
Here's two themes I found that are bright coloured, with good contrasting text colours on windows etc.
 [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=39949](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=39949)  (turquoise one, nice and bright)
 [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=34546](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php?content=34546)  (yellow, big text. I could read it without my glasses with much less trouble than with most themes..)

 You can also change font and font size in ubuntu.. The command is;
> gnome-font-properties
