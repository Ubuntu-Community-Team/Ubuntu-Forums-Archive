Hey, all.

I've been using ubuntu for a few months now, and I'm loving it. I'll not go back to Windows....BUT....

In all honesty, I've not found a mail-merge system for ubuntu that matches the interfacing capabilities of MS Word and Outlook. Let me explain. 

With Word and Outlook, you can create a document in Word, merge it with a database, send it to Outlook, and email it with the document in the body of the text (as opposed to an attachment). Before you say you can do that with OO, I don't think so.

With OO, you can merge a document with a database, turn it into an HTML email and send it via  OO's built in email sender. That's fine, except for two things that are critical to me: 1) It does not merge with Evolution, so there is no record of what emails you sent. 2)It works like crap. It's difficult to tell if your messages were actually sent. Overall, it is just an inferior email merge setup. (Other than that, I love OO.)

Here is what I'd like to know:
Is there a dependable system for Ubuntu (either stand alone, or one that will interface with evolution) that can create, send, and keep record of email merge operations?
