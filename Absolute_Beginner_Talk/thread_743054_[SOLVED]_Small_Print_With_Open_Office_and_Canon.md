### Post by m60dude5 on 2008-04-02
Ok. I am writing this because my problem has been solved and it was actually stupidity on my part. It was no one's fault but mine. (Imagine that!) Anyway, the problem was that the canon printer I have is actually an MP530 and not an MP500. I looked through the drivers list again and realized that when I had first selected my driver, there are several models that use the MP name, and I thought MP stood for Multi Pass. However, in some odd way, it actually stands for something else that starts with an "I" (don't ask). Anyway, the point is, the problem is solved, and for anyone looking for help with a similar problem in the future, the best advice I can give is to check your work. Also, if your driver number isn't listed, use the closest one number wise. 
To philinux, I'm sorry I wasted your time. Thanks again for your help though, I appreciate it.
