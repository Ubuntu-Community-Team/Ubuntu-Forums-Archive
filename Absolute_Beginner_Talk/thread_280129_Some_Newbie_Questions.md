### Post by CatKiller on 2006-10-19
The programs you've cited are pretty hardcore, and are unlikely to run satisfactorily for you in Wine. You can check the AppDB at winehq.com to see the hoops people sometimes jump through to get things working under Wine. There aren't, at the moment, suitable drop-in replacements for the video editing things you use at the moment, and given that you say that that's pretty much all you use the computer for, that's pretty much a show-stopper for you, in my opinion.

My suggestion would be to go dual-boot with XP. Backup any data you want to keep. Defrag your hard drive as much as you can (you'll need to resize your partitions, and defragging will reduce the amount of file-moving that you need to do). Boot from the cd and when it asks you where you want to install Ubuntu, tell it to resize your existing partition and install itself in the free space. You'll have a GRUB boot menu to pick which OS you want to boot into, and you can do all your fun stuff in Linux and just go into XP when you want to edit a video. :)

Use the live cd before you install. It will give you an idea of the environment without affecting your XP install in any way until you choose to install.
