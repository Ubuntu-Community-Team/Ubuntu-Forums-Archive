### Post by rkd on 2008-03-27
Have you tried removing the battery, then putting it in again, being careful that it goes in exactly right?  It might be that the battery just isn't completely connected.

I don't have any experience with that notebook computer to say that this is the problem -- I'm just speculating on what might have happened.

A quick check on the web seems to indicate that's a pretty old computer.  The batteries in notebook computers wear out in just a few years, even when they are not being used, so if the battery is old, it could be that it is simply worn out and can't hold a charge any more.  Was it holding a charge okay just before you installed Ubuntu?  If it was holding a charge for a decent period right before you installed Ubuntu, then I think the problem is not likely to be that the battery is worn out.  But I'm not an expert on that, and I can imagine it could be that the battery can rapidly deteriorate if it had been sitting unused for a while before you decided to put Ubuntu on the computer.

If you determine that the battery really is dead, you probably could replace it for around $80.  Be careful that you deal with a reputable store, though.  Some places will sell you a "new" battery that has been sitting on the shelf in a warehouse for several years, and it has almost no life left.

### Post by rkd on 2008-03-27
You said the battery is supposed to be new.  Did you buy it recently?  Do you believe the place you bought it from is trustworthy and would not have sold you a battery that had been sitting on the shelf for a few years?  They DO go bad just sitting -- it is characteristic of the Lithium-ion chemistry; it deteriorates even if sitting unused.

I don't know of a good way to check the battery except put it into another computer that you know is charging its battery properly, and I imagine you don't have such a computer handy.  You might be able to find a computer repair shop near you that has a universal charger that they could try on your battery.
