### Post by drs305 on 2007-06-15
My favorite if you have access to a terminal is xkill. Type 'xkill' in a terminal and the mouse prompt turns into a skull & crossbones. Whatever window (anywhere on or in it) you click on next is instantly closed. Of course, you have to have access to a terminal and unfrozen cursor for it to work...  If there is a way to invoke it with key combinations like the poster above mentioned that would be even cooler.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-15
If you are using GNOME, just right-click on a blank portion of either the top, or bottom panel.

Then click on [COLOR="Blue"]Add to Pane[/COLOR]l

A window will then open.

Scroll down to the[COLOR="Blue"] Desktop and Windows[/COLOR] section and click on [COLOR="Blue"]Force Quit[/COLOR].

Then click on the [COLOR="Blue"]Close button[/COLOR].

The [COLOR="Blue"]Force Quit applet[/COLOR] should now be visible on the panel that you chose.

If an application freezes, just click on the applet and then click on the offending application and it will close.
