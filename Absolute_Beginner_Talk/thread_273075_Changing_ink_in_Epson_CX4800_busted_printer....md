### Post by Phosphoric on 2006-10-09
You might find that this is a "design" feature with Epson.  On my (ex) Epson, it counts the number of times that the head is cleaned.  The waste ink from the cleaning is absorbed in pads in the bowells of the printer.  When the counter reaches a predetermined figure, the printer stops and tells you to take it to your nearest service centre where they will change the pads and reset the counter for £50!

I found some service software to reset the counters and found out how to change the pads.....but in the end I went out and bought an HP.  Works fine in Ububtu!
