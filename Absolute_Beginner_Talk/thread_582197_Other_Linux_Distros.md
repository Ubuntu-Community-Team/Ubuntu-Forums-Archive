### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-10-19
> **xsabrewulf said:**
> so if I get very familiar with Ubuntu its gonna be a whole new learning experience when using another distro in the future?

I believe so, but in a good way. I used xubuntu for a year (6.06, 6.10, and 7.04...and a few days on tribe5/beta 7.10), but then I tried Archlinux 2007.08-2, and it's been a brand new learning experience for me. The basics of course are still the same... but there are some things you have to do different. I spend a lot more time in su, and learning pacman is pretty easy, so is the AUR makepkg -s...


In xubuntu, I had basically learned my way around the terminal and the file system, and had spent a lot of time theming my computer and adding some nice speed hacks to make it faster... I was comfortable and happy with both my eye candy factor, and how fast and stable it was, and I usually knew how to do whatever I needed to do in xubuntu, and if I didn't... there is always some one who does know in the forums.


But after being on Archlinux for a couple of weeks, I have learned so much about xorg.conf and rc.conf, as well as ndiswrapper, network-profiles, and how to configure the synaptics touchpad... plus, all of the other things I had learned in xubuntu... makes me feel like I'm finally really learning Linux.


Plus, installing Arch got me using nano and tty much more.


Don't get me wrong, I love xubuntu/ubuntu, but I probably will wait until 8.04 for my next install... and I think the Ubuntu forums is the most helpful Linux forum I've seen, with so much info to learn about Linux. I still use Ubuntu forums as a reference to anything I do on Arch... since there are so many threads on this forum for almost any question.
