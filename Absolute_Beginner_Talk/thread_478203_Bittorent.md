### Post by Golyadkin on 2007-06-19
> **Codename said:**
> 
And to remove the hard disk icon on your desktop you might want to try, "Right Click, Remove from Panel"

I think what the poster means is the mounted disks on the desktop, not in the panel. Those are there automatically when they get mounted. You can make them go away by unmounting the drives, or hide them by going: Alt-F2 and typing "gconf-editor", press enter and go to "Apps > Nautilus > Desktop > Volumes_Visible" and uncheck the box.

### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-06-19
Hi. Bittorrent doesn't show up on the menus by default: right-click on the menu, select "Edit Menus", on the left pane select "Internet", and on the right one, check the box next to "BitTorrent" (should be the first one). Now it will show up on the menu under "Applications-->Internet-->BitTorrent". However, you really don't need it to show up: just double-click on a **.torrent** file and the application will come right up asking you where to save the file.

### Post by fakie_flip on 2007-06-19
Use alacarte to unhide it. The bittorrent client is installed by default with Ubuntu. You can type alacarte from the command line and then look in your applications menu for it. You will find it without a checkmark in the box. Check it, and it will show in the menu.
