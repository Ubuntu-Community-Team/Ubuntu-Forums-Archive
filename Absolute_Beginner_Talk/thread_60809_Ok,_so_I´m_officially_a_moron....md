### Post by AgenT on 2005-08-29
Sounds like you need to burn your image properly. You have to burn it as a CD image (iso). The best way to check to see if you did it correctly is to just put the CD in a computer and open it up. What do you see? Do you see multiple files and folders? Or do you just see one file? If one file, then you did not burn it correctly. If you see multiple files and folders that means you did burn it correctly. Although it sometimes also happens that while you did everything right your burner did not so it could still fail even though it looks good.

But if you did everything correctly, now you have to have your computer boot the CD. What motherboard do you have? This may help as someone here may have the same hardware as you. Different motherboards do things differently. Sometimes you have to manually set your motherboard to check the device in addition to having the CD as the first boot device (as noted above). The older your motherboard the more pain it usually is. Also, if you did not build your own computer and have a name-brand one, it may use a super-cheap motherboard making this even more annoying. Or it could be as easy as pressing the right button during the bios screen. Check your computer manual on how to boot a CD.

Do note that all this has nothing to do with Linux but with your hardware. This is required for any CD you wish to boot.
