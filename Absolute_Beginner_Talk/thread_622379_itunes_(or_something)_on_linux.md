Dude, you're screwed. When you plug an iPod into an iTunes running computer, the iPod gets "stuck" with that computer and won't work on any others. ### Post by rune0077 on 2007-11-25
There is, but not without iTunes. I'm fairly sure of that. And, as I tried to say before, it won't help that you take it to your friends computer, 'cause then the iPod will simply associated itself with that computer - you want it to associate itself with your computer instead (it will only work on one computer at a time, I know its stupid, but, like so many stupid things, its also true). It needs to read a serial number, or some kind of similar information off of your hardware, so that it will know that your computer is now its new best friend and it can't go out an play with any of the other computers. You can still charge its batteries, but it will not let you transfer anything to or from it. Needles to say, the only way you will get the iPod to associate itself with your computer, is by plugging it in while your running iTunes (then a message will pop up, asking you if this is what you want to do). 

There may be ways around this (there ought to be ways around it), but its none that I have heard of.  

Best of luck :)
