### Post by candtalan on 2007-07-24
Windows experience is a bit slippery when it comes to using linux, yes. The way I started was to rely fairly heavily on the GUI and menus and also the lists and forums.

I use the KDE version (Kubuntu) which has a more rich menu content I suppose, and is  little slower also, but that is ok for me.

I very rarely need to use the terminal, and even then I use google for the content and then google also for basic commands. With ubuntu you will use command line (cl) as user not root, unless you precede the commands with sudo. This elevates you to root for your immediate action. This is a fairly secure way of using root prividges. When you use them you can easliy screw up.... even in ubuntu.

After about three years I would say I avoid cl if I can. I do not aim to become a power user anyway. You can probably do everything you want without it, in K/Ubuntu anyway. However, cl is very powerful and fast, and I do use it when I need to. I have to be careful with my key entry though, cl can be unforgiving.

Please dont feel bad about asking on things you think are 'petty'! If you need an answer and cannot see one or find one easily, then just ask. You will get good at using google - once you get to know what question to ask anyway :-)

Start with things you will be most interested in using, and just find out about them initially.

Good luck, and Welcome!
