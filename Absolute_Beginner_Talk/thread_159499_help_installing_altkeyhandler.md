About the Pali diacritical shortcut keys, in Ubuntu I found the best solution was to set up a mini-key-toolbar thingee (sorry, can't remember the proper name, and I'm not using Ubuntu now so I can't check) that worked across all programs. It wasn't great, but a lot better than manually inserting special characters every time. So from memory, after more than a year, I think you right click on the taskbar at the bottom in Ubuntu's desktop, then you choose 'add applet' or something like that. Then choose one sounding something like 'key toolbar', and then go to 'options'> 'add new key set', and then copy and paste the string of diacriticals you need with a space between each one, and click ok. Then you get a little mini toolbar at the bottom of the screen available at all times which works in all programs with each diacritical as a button. To insert the character then, you click on it, and that is like copying. Then to insert you paste. I told you it's not great.
