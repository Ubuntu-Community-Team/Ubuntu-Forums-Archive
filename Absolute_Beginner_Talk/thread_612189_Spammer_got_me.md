### Post by eldepeche on 2007-11-13
> **carlosjuero said:**
> rm stands for remove, the -rf tells it to remove recursively (meaning every folder and file down the specified tree). Running it as sudo means your system goes kaboom.

The "r" stands for recursive, and the "f" stands for force, as in don't comfirm.

If you indeed ran "sudo rm -rf /", then you need to reinstall. If you just ran "sudo rm -rf ~/", as I saw in some other threads, then you only deleted stuff from your personal directory, and you don't need to reinstall. You will just have to reset any application settings.
