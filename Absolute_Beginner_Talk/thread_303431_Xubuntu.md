### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-11-20
From my experience, I've found Xubuntu to be very good. For being so lightweight, it has almost every program you need right after installation. I say almost because it doesn't have a complete office suite (no presentation tool :() or any games, although you can easily install those programs and others. Overall however, I recommend Ubuntu or Kubuntu unless you're on an older computer. Both come with a more complete set of programs, especially Ubuntu. Anything with less than 128 MB of RAM or a Pentium IV processor (or equivalent) probably belongs with Xubuntu. Just my two cents...

**EDIT:** I should have mentioned that with any Linux distribution (including Ubuntu, Kubuntu, and Xubuntu), you could run into problems with hardware and software support. New hardware may not always be supported (especially video and wireless cards), although I've had no trouble getting drivers when I needed to. You might find that for many of your old Windows programs, there are just as good programs, or even the same ones, available in Linux. You can even run some Windows programs in Linux with a program called Wine. However, not everything can be handled. You could also have trouble with some audio and video formats, but those are usually easily solved.
