### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2007-07-03
The classic way to reconfigure your video drivers is to run the command
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
This takes you through a dozen or so screens asking you about your hardware.  I think you would have tried this already if you've been troubleshooting the problem for awhile, but here it is in case you haven't.  If you run this, be sure to actaully read, don't just ENTER your way through.  When you get to the page on resolutions, use TAB to move through the options and SPACEBAR to enable/select.  Choose your monitor's highest resolution capacity and everything under it.
