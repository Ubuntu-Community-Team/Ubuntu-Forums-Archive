No twice of RAM is good if you have 512MB of RAM or less. Beyond that,i think its a waste of hard disk space. Its good to have your RAM completely filled and swap used less.

### Post by iris-n on 2008-03-31
That's just a rule of thumb, for when someone is installing and needs a quick way to tell him how much swap to reserve.

Actually, the amount is based in how many memory you requested that your RAM couldn't handle, like the windows pagination file.

Setting more than that won't have any ill effect, is just an waste of disk space.

And people with 4 GiB RAM seldom ever uses the swap file, so, personally, i like best the function Swap(RAM)=2/sqrt(0.5 +RAM)
