### Post by BarfBag on 2007-05-29
Welcome to the world of free software!

The differences between Mepis, SUSE and Damn Small are mainly GUI related.  Mepis and SUSE are KDE-based distros, Damn Small is something lightweight (forget the name), and Ubuntu is Gnome-based.  Both Mepis and Ubuntu are Debian-based at the core, so package management is the same.  SUSE is an .RPM distro.  I don't know about Damn Small (as you can probably tell, I'm not familiar with it).

The thing that makes Ubuntu stand out is how simple the system is.  It doesn't come with anything extra - just the common things people need (web browser, word processor, etc.).  I find it more mature then the other easy distros as well.

### Post by mkoehler on 2007-05-29
Joho5,

     As everyone said, the difference between ubuntu and other operating systems are suttle, but I feel that they make ubuntu superior to all other distros (and I have used many).  To answer your question about ubuntu studio, it is not technically a new operating system.  It runs using Feisty, just as if you downloaded ubuntu fiesty.  The DVD iso's that you see on the Ubuntu Studio's download page ([http://ubuntustudio.org/downloads](http://ubuntustudio.org/downloads)) are simply versions of Fiesty that have all of the additional applications built in.  If you wish to simply download the normal version of Fiesty without *all* of the additional applications, you can add the repos and download only the additional applications that you need.  I hope this helps.
