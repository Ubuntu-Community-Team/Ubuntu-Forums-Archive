### Post by thephotoman on 2006-10-17
Most of the software available for GNU-based Linux systems like Ubuntu that you may want to install is available from the repositories.  Simply open Synaptic (it's under System > Administration) and look for the programs you need.  It's a rare occasion that you've got to download something from a third party.

However, as everyone here has already said, you need Gaim to satisfy your need for those functions, and it's already installed on your system by default.  Yahoo! Messenger for Linux hasn't been updated in quite some time, and I simply have to laugh at the thought of Microsoft ever publishing software that runs on Linux--which isn't to say that their protocols aren't supported.
