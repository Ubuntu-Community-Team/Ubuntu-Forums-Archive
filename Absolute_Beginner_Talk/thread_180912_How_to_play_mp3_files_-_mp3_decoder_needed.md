Read the WIKI! and then for the rest of your ubuntu life, whenever you need any codec, plugin or program, First: 
=>System => Administration => Synaptic Package manager

and then google it! ;) 

When ever you need anything, chances are synaptic has it in it's repositories!

For myself, coming from windows, the SPM is the one of the most amazing tools in ubuntu!

For further detail checkout this how to page:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/](http://ubuntuguide.org/)

or this one

[http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/](http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/)

Especially look at the page for expanding you repositories

A good mp3 player that I can recommend is Amarok and then also, when searching for a mp3 decoder,
libmad works for me. I used XMMS but when switching Amarok i was told it also could not play mp3's
so if Synaptic doesn't download it download MAD MPEG audio decoder plugin for GStreamer as well.
