### Post by gjtoth on 2006-12-22
I think there are a LOT of folks here that are recovering M$ users... me being one them.  All the hype about Vista doesn't even appeal to me.  

I was weaned off Windoze about 2 years ago and never looked back.  One of the things I find useful to bear in mind is: don't try to do Windoze things in Ubuntu.  By and large, the operation of the applications is FAIRLY similar.  BUT, getting things "installed" is way different and you'll pick up on this stuff if you keep an open mind and HAVE PATIENCE.  You didn't learn M$ stuff overnight.  Don't expect to learn Linux stuff overnight.  Take it one small bite at a time.  You'll get it.  Don't be afraid to ask questions here and don't be afraid to read and try stuff out.  But, above all... BACK UP YOUR DATA!  While you're learning, stuff can and will happen.  Who knows, you might just have to wipe out your installation and start from scratch.  I've had to do that a couple of times but I learned from the experience.  Ubuntu is far and away more rock solid than Windoze could every hope to be.  I haven't had to reboot my system from a locked-up application in a couple of months.  I don't sweat viruses, trojans, etc.  And, I'm not afraid to ask questions here.  A little advice before you do.... use the search function!  There's enough experience on these forums to choke a horse and chances are pretty good your problem has been experienced by someone else or there is a "sticky" on it.

Hope this helps.  Welcome to Ubuntu... fasten your seatbelt! :)
