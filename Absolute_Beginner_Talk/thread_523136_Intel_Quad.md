I've heard of only a few problems with the new core 2 duo architecture and Linux. I'm pretty sure it should work fine, what you should be more careful about is motherboards and ensuring all the hardware is compatible that goes into/on it. Oh and there are 4 cores on the Q6600, not 3. It is really 2 duo chips on the same die. As for how well things take advantage of the extra cores, that depends on the applications in question and how it's coded. I know there is an SMP version of the kernel that you will use since you got a quad. Applications you'll have to look at them individually.

Oh and the Q6600 is a great cpu. Overclocks really well if you have a good cooler/mobo and do that. It's been clocked in at over 3.2 Ghz from it's standard 2.4 without much effort at all (don't really advise going higher than that...). Have fun with your new rig.

Oh and small note is that the new PCIe 2.0 standard, new mobo chipsets and new cards (GeForce 9 series) are all coming out betweens September and November of this year, I'd hold off but if your not a gamer/performance nut you've no need. You will probably need a new mobo if you upgrade card/cpu and want to take full advantage though.

### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-08-11
> **conphara said:**
> How strong should the cooler be? And whats motherboards are concerned, what motherboard would you recommend?

Sunbeam Tuniq 120 is a great cooler, it is MASSIVE though, it is my favourite though. Ensure that both the case and the board have room. The thermalright Ultra 120 is also a great but massive cooler. More moderate one would be the Zalman 9700, it won't give the most cooling but will offer quiet and decent performance.

As for boards, the one I was looking at just a short while ago was this [one by abit.]("http://www.ncix.com/products/index.php?sku=24630&vpn=IP35-PRO&manufacture=ABit") Decent board, mostly intel based, ddr2 only, supports the new 1333 chips, has built in RAID controller, lots of expansion, and room for a large cooler like the tuniq I believe (heard a few people put it on this. Draw back is that it won't support the new DDR3 which is still expensive, but will be standard inside a year or two, doesn't support NVidia SLI if your into that. That's about it. Oh and it won't support the new PCIe 2.0 standard for the next generation of NVidia cards. That support will only come with the next gen of boards later in the fall/winter like I said.

Oh and if your aiming at these large coolers/graphics cards make you get a large case (might even want a Full Tower rather than a mid range one). The abit is a good all around board, and the tuniq will do a good job cooling the quad under maximum load. Make sure you have a decent 650-750 watt PSU for the rig, quads and these heavy cards draw lots of juice.

Hope that helps. I'm waiting to buy as I said till the new tech comes out, I'll be building around January/February. Best luck with the machine.
