### Post by 3rdalbum on 2008-01-21
Sorry to bump a thread, but what about Marathon Aleph One?

It's by Bungie, the guys who did Halo. It's a port of a classic Macintosh game. The game engine is quite old, but in terms of technology it's somewhere between Doom and Quake, and the gameplay is good. It's got a single-player storyline, and the engine itself is still under active development.

There are a couple of gotchas though - you'll have to compile from source, I believe you might have to compile a new version of Lua as well, and although the engine is open-source and there are open-source maps around, the original mapsets are free-as-in-beer only.

I used to play Marathon 2 and Marathon Infinity on the Mac quite a bit, and they were lots of fun. I should try Aleph One for Linux some time though and see what advances they've made.

[http://source.bungie.org/]("http://source.bungie.org/")

### Post by smurphy_it on 2008-01-21
Openarena is kinda neat.  I've just started to play it.  I grabbed the .deb files from the website as the one in the repositories are older.  It has a few game modes, like "deathmatch, Capture the flag, etc" and has I believe 32 maps in it.  It's basically a quake 3 engine.  Nice taking on some bots when no network connection exists.

Alot of games and utilities are accessible through this website (like Ubuntu Tweak for giving you the right Power profiles when AC power is connected).  The website is [http://www.getdeb.net]("http://www.getdeb.net")

You'll find Openarena and a few others under the games category.  Make sure you choose your architecture like 32-bit or 64-bit before snaking it down.
