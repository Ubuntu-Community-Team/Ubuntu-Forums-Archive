no, your not being attacked, all those things are blocked transmissions that probably werent even an attempt at a hack, but probably just confused services looking for something that used to exist at your IP address, mabey someone that used that IP before you was running gnutella or something on that port number..  Hackers usually look at ports that are normally running services,  for example SSH (port 22) or a web server (80) or some other service that they can try to break into..  If you aren't running any services then you have nothing to worry about.  As far as i know ubuntu doesn't come preinstalled with services like that. The ports that people are trying to connect to on your computer pose no threat.  if  you weren't running a firewall and those connections did make it to your computer they would be dropped.
