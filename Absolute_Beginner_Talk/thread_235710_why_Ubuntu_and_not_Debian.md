### Post by az on 2006-08-13
> **cccc said:**
> hi

why should I install Ubuntu instead of Debian ?
what has Ubuntu what Debian doesn't have ?
is it, because Debian Stable uses an old software ?

greetings
cccc

Debian is a general-purpose distro.  Debian has both a slow-and-reliable stable release and a fast-paced cutting-edge Unstable release.

Ubuntu is a desktop distro as well as a server distro.  Ubuntu takes a small subset of packages from Debian Unstable and supports them (makes them stable).  You get the benefit of the cutting-edge free-libre software community and the stability of a professionaly supported platform.

Depending on your needs Debian or Ubuntu are excellent choices.  What are your needs?
