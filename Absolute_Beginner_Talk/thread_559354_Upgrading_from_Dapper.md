### Post by Mud.Knee.Havoc on 2008-01-02
Andy, you'd notice a tremendous increase in speed and responsiveness if you used something a bit lighter than KDE or Gnome.  You could play around with Openbox, IceWM, XFCE, and so on.  I've got Openbox running with pypanel as a taskbar.  It's got everything I need and it's so much faster than running gnome.  It takes a few days to get used to (since you'd probably want to manually modify your right click menu, make some programs autorun, etc - it's easy, there are only two files to modify manually to do anything you'd want).

Also, if adding another stick of RAM is a possibility, go for it!  I remember when I went from 256 to 768... hitting the swap was slowing my computer down like crazy.  RAM is the best upgrade in the world. :D
