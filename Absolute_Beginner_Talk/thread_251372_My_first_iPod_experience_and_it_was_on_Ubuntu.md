### Post by dynacrylic on 2006-09-05
My first iPod experience and it was on Ubuntu :)

As goofy as it might sound, I just got my first iPod- a nice refurbished white iPod Shuffle. It's difficult to believe that because I'm a 30 year young ;) female who is the support / [code monkey]("http://www.jonathancoulton.com/lyrics/code-monkey") for an academic institution and until Friday I had never touched an iPod. I'm your typical shut-in techie- i.e. single, likes [music]("http://pics.greatestjournal.com/userpic/981778/28723"), [tv]("http://static.flickr.com/33/65389567_9737b2a6b0_o.jpg"), [movies]("http://www.mandypatinkin.net/PB/inigo4.jpeg"), technology and plays games to pass the time and save money. Despite my 5 PCs- main running Dapper, 2 older machines running windows for gaming/[botting](”http://www.mmbot.net/") only and 2 very old 233mhz computers/server which I was using a server  until they recently quit on me)- and an electronic drum set, owning an iPod was never on my top list to get.

My main motivation to purchase an iPod was work. The boss has been talking about incorporating pod-casting for a number of months.  I had really no idea what or how an iPod worked. All I knew up until today was what I had read about or [heard from others](”http://dictionary.reference.com/search?q=hearsay&x=0&y=0"). I knew the day was coming that I would have to start doing pod-casting and incorporating iPods into the curiculum. Rather than look like a fool at work trying to it out, I braved the unknown and purchased an iPod shuffle for my personal home use.

Anyway, what I wanted to do was share with everyone my first encounter with an iPod shuffle and Ubuntu- so here goes.

**Friday 5:30pm**- I received my newly refurbished iPod shuffle from [FedEx]("http://www.clear-lake-reflections.com/fedex-hphoto.jpg").

**5:31pm**- I bust open the package.

**5:35pm**- I look at this small white piece of plastic (which is the iPod shuffle) and find a cap at the end which slides off. Wow- a USB connection!

**5:36pm**- I sprint to my PC (running Dapper) and plug it in. A pretty iPod icon appears on my desktop and Music Player automatically starts.  I notice “IPOD" in the left nav portion of the program and click on it. 

I'm thinking how do I move a music file into the iPod now... I open my home folder in a new window and navigate to my collection of music files. I click and hold on a music file in my collection, drag it to the Music Player window. [Nothing]("http://www.meshuggah.net/p/cover-nothing-big.jpg").

**5:40pm**- With the iPod in hand now, I retreat and open the the instructions that came with the iPod- “iPod shuffle: User's Guide". I get nice and comfy on my couch and prepare to read over the entire manual. 

**5:40.15pm**- After 15 seconds my short attention span kicks in and I stop reading word for word. I start flipping through the manual. [Click click click]("http://urubu.wordpress.com/files/2006/04/KingCrimson_Essential_Box.jpg"). [Touch touch touch]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DJ_Shadow"). I just play with the iPod while attempting to skim over pages.

I get a page entitled “What You Need to Get Started". Well that's great it has software for a Macintosh and a windows machine. What about Linux? What about Dapper?  I immediately start to think “Hey silly use wine and install the software". I reply to myself “You can't even get Diablo 2, Warcraft 3 or [F.E.A.R.]("http://www.sierra.com/en/home/games/game_results.html?keywords=&title=F.E.A.R.&genre=&platform=pc&submit=Search") to run in it- what makes you think you'll be able to install this software and get it to work in wine?"

“No."

I'm think I could actually install the software on the XP machine I use only for gaming- Nope.

**5:42pm**- I jump back on my main box and pull up my friend- Google. I do a search using “ipod mp3 ubuntu".  The first hit back is [“Help converting mp3 to audiobook (m4b) for ipod- Ubuntu Forums"](”http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=127266")- let's see what this has to offer. 

**5:43pm**- I quickly scan over the thread in the Ubuntu forum and catch talk of an application called gtkpod. I continue to [scan]("http://www.filmtotaal.nl/images/wallpapers/full/scanners/scanners1_1024.jpg") the page for the anything else that might be of use.

**5:44pm**- I use the Synaptic Package Manager to install gtkpod.

**5:45pm**- I plug the iPod back into the UBS port.  I think to myself “did SPM put a shortcut to it in the Application menu." Yes it did! 

Click and we have gtkpod up and running.  I click on the “iPod" nav. I test to see if I can click and drag a music file from my collection into gtkpod.  I think it worked. 

I pause and think to myself “You do I eject/unmount this [puppy]("http://imagecache2.allposters.com/images/aqua/9096.jpg")?". Out of the corner of my eye I catch the button on gtkpod called “Sync". Hmmm, when hovering over it it read “Write changes to disc/iPod". That has to be it so I click.

It comes back with an error “Failed to save /media/ipod/iPod_Control/Artwork/ArtworkDB". I don't know what it means so I click “Ok".

**5:50pm**- I right click on the iPod icon on the desktop and click eject. Ubuntu does it's thing and the icon disappears.

“Dare I? [I dare]("http://t3.preservice.org/T0110280/Anthem.jpg")!"

I pull the iPod out of the PC and plug in my head phones.

**5:51pm** – We have music! It worked!

**5:52pm**- Dancing in my hallway with my iPod in hand.  :)


I know I'm not the [greatest writer]("http://philosophy2.ucsd.edu/~rutherford/phil32/spinover2.html") in the world, but I just wanted to share with everyone the enthusiasm that I feel. 

Now I may be slow at times, but I'm pretty proud of myself that I get my iPod working on Dapper in about 20 minutes. I'm also pretty psyched that I didn't have to resort to using windows. Now if only I could get some of my games to work in wine I'll do another little [dance]("http://www.isc.tamu.edu/~lewing/gallery/penguin-dance.3.jpg") :)

-- -- -- 

**Monday 7:00pm**

It's now Monday night and I'm still rock-n-rolling with my shuffle.  I'm a bit pleased with the purchase itself.  I just keep thinking how much money I saved by buying a refurbished one rather than buying a brand [spanking]("http://images.google.com/images?q=tbn:hMxHkhhF0a4JtM:http://www.vitusproductions.com/family%2520guy%2520lois%2520spank.gif") new one. If I had the money, I probably would buy an actual iPod (a refurbished one just to save the money).

I tried sound-juicer; I searched the Ubuntu forums and Google and tried a couple of “suggestions" I found but still couldn't get any to work. I also tried ripperx but have had no luck with it so far (the interface is a bit cryptic and bland).

**Question now**- what are people using to [rip]("http://www.punkasspunk.com/videolog/20050308/Family_Guy_%5BFamily_Guy_Viewer_Mail_1%5D_(2002)_5.jpg") MP3s on Ubuntu? 

I tried sound-juicer; I searched the Ubuntu forums and Google and tried a couple of “suggestions" I found but still couldn't get any to work. I also tried ripperx but have had no luck with it so far (the interface is a bit cryptic and bland).

From the sound juicer manual:
> If you need to store tracks in the MP3 format (for example, if your portable music player only supports MP3 and not Ogg Vorbis), you will need to create a new profile.  To do this, click on Edit Profiles, click New, and name the profile MP3.

Select the MP3 profile and click the Edit button.  Set GStreamer Pipeline to audio/x-raw-int,rate=44100,channels=2 ! lame name=enc vbr=0 bitrate=196 ! id3v2mux

Set the File Extension to mp3, and select the Active check box.  You will have to restart Sound Juicer to see the new audio profile.

This profile uses the LAME MP3 encoder, so you will need to have the GStreamer LAME plugin installed.

This may help if you don't have LAME installed - [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats#head-1340337f2ca1d0c54900935468515ba7630fcc59](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats#head-1340337f2ca1d0c54900935468515ba7630fcc59)

### Post by Marsolin on 2006-09-05
I personally use [Konqueror]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/konqueror") to rip CDs, but I assume you are using Ubuntu and not Kubuntu. You could try [Banshee]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/banshee"). It's a complete music manager that can rip CD's, play music, and sync with your iPod.

[Goobox]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/goobox"), [Grip]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/grip"), and [Listen]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/listen") are other Gnome based options.
