### Post by az on 2006-04-13
Linux is a kernel for an operasting system.  A kernel is the first program that the computer runs when it boots the operating system  It is the program that takes care of the space between the hardware and the programs that the computer runs.


Linux is a great example of free-libre software.  Free-libre software does not take away any of your rights when you run it.  Your computer is a pile of plastic and metal which is useless without software.  If the software is not transparent, and you do not have the right to know what it is doing, you have given the right for someone to violate your privacy.  As computers become more and more indispensable, it is important to have the choice to use free-libre software.
