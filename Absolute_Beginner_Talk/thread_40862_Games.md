### Post by Kyral on 2005-06-10
by "win compat" I assume you mean Windows games, right?

Well, for the most part, no. Not unless you get Cedega/WINE. Now, already a few games have Linux versions (Unreal Touranament for one). 

WINE is free, Cedega you have to pay at least 15 bucks for. I say at least because its a subscription service. You have to subscribe for 3 months at 5 bucks a month to get Cedega (I mean sign up/commit to 3 months). However, if you keep on subscribing, you keep getting updates. I consider the Cedega Project to be a very good idea, and a worthwhile cause.

WINE is in the Repos. Cedega is at [www.transgaming.com](www.transgaming.com)
