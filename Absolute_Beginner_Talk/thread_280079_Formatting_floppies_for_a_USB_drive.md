### Post by rufousfelix on 2006-10-18
To reformat PC-formatted diskettes to Linux Native (ext2), I find myself getting results as follows:

First have Floppy Formatter window open (Applications>System Tools) and check "Quick" as formatting mode.  Two other modes don't work for me.

Next, insert diskette into USB floppy drive. Let the drive make a few happy noises for a second or so.

The "window of opportunity" for a successful reformat appears to be but seconds in duration.  Click "Format" too soon or too late and you might as well go on to the next diskette.  

So assuming you click at the right time, you should soon see the message "Floppy successfully formatted."  Just wait.

Let the floppy drive do its thing, until the floppy icon for the diskette file shows up on the desktop.  Then click on "Eject."  Not straightforward, but probably the expected price for using trailing-edge technology like floppies!
