### Post by Mr. Svinlesha on 2007-03-07
Otherwise, you can try this walkthrough, which did the trick for me (I notice you don't have HorizSync and VertiRefresh under your monitor section, for example):

Okay, then, a step by step walkthrough:

1)  Make a backup of your xorg.conf file.  VERY IMPORTANT!

> sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf_backup

2)  Edit the file: I prefer the following command to the &#8220;nano&#8221; one:

> gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf

3)  You should navigate down to the &#8220;Monitor Section&#8221; and add the following lines:

> HorizSync	xx-xx
VertRefresh	xx-xx
where xx &#8211; xx are the values for your specific monitor.  These values can be easily found by Googling your monitor's name. 

When you're done, at this point, xorg.conf should look something like this:

> 
Section "Monitor"
Identifier "Generic Monitor"
Option "DPMS"
HorizSync 28-64
VertRefresh 43-60
EndSection

 
4)  Now, the next bit is a little tricky.  You should have xorg.conf open in gedit.  Open a new terminal and type in:
> gtf horizontalresolution verticalresolution refreshrate

Where &#8220;horizontalresolution&#8221; is, in this case, 1440, &#8220;verticalresolution&#8221; is 900, and, for my monitor at any rate, &#8220;refreshrate&#8221; is 60.  In other words, the command here would be &#8220;gtf 1440 900 60.&#8221;  When you hit enter Ubuntu will generate a &#8220;modeline&#8221; for you.  Copy and paste it into the xorg.conf Section "Monitor" under &#8220;Vertrefresh&#8221; and above &#8220;EndSection.&#8221;

5)  Navigate down to &#8220;Section Screen.&#8221;  You may have several such sections &#8211; I did.  (This is the step I missed, by the way, that was so simple.)  It should look something like this:

> 
Section "Screen"
    Identifier    "Default Screen"
    Device        "NVIDIA Corporation NV34 [GeForce FX 5200]"
    Monitor        "CM752ET"
    DefaultDepth    16
    SubSection "Display":
        Depth        16
        Modes      "1280x1024" "1024x768"
    EndSubSection
EndSection


On the &#8220;modes&#8221; line, add your desired resolution in quotations (&#8220;1440x900&#8221;).  Just to make sure I added that line to every &#8220;Section Screen&#8221; in my xorg.conf file, in front of the "1280x1024" entry.

6)  Save gedit.  Close all open programs.  Hit Ctrl-Alt-Backspace.  Ubuntu restarts.

That *should* do the trick.  If it doesn't, let me know and I'll add a couple more steps.

:)

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=129379&postcount=21](http://ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=129379&postcount=21)
