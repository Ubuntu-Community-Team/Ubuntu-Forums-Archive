### Post by Jose Catre-Vandis on 2007-09-16
If you have just installed Fiesty, then you already have a version of desktop effects on your PC. You may need to invoke the restricted drivers for your graphics card, and then enable "Desktop Effects" in your System/Preferences menu, and also start Beryl Manager. You should then get an icon in your system tray to adjust settings.

Search the forum for HOWTOs, using terms suchs Beryl and 3DCube.

IMHO, you would be better off to not bother with all of this Beryl and 3DCube stuff and desktop effects.

From my experience, you will have enough problems just getting a driver for your particular video card working properly.

My experience has been that if you try to use a video driver that is built into the O/S then one thing does not work and then if you try another method for the video driver such as directly from either ATI or Nvidia, then something else does not work properly and if you try say ENVY then something else does not work properly.

This is sort of a ridiculous situation.  It seems to me that as long as they have been working of these O/Ss that they should concentrating on getting something like this fixed instead of going of on a tangent and trying to write yet another and another version of these O/Ss.

And please don't give me that old line that this is not their fault and that the card manufacturers are to blame for not providing proper Linux drivers for their video cards.  If Ubuntu is going to write an O/S, then they should bear some responsibility for putting pressure on or providing some incentive for the card manufacturers to work on these Linux drivers.

And also referring someone to these stickys and other forum sources for getting answers to these video card driver problem is not a solution because when you go to these threads what you wind up with is reading thru a bunch of other people's experiences which in a lot of cases will NOT fix your problem.

I certainly don't want to be negative about this.  I certainly would like to see this O/S have great success but I don't think it is going to have a overwhelming user base until things like this are fixed.

Thanks.
