### Post by alphaniner on 2008-03-10
So you are getting an invalid kernel error, too?  Does that occur before or after you get to the main menu screen?  Either way, I would suggest burning the cd again if you can, and if your burning software supports it, have it verify after burning (for example, in Nero, there is a checkbox that says 'Verify the compilation after burning' or something very similar).  Also, either with the cd you already have or if you burn a new one, check the md5sum:[How to MD5SUM]("<https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM>")

edit: and close as many processes/programs as you can while burning, including stuff that shows up in your system tray (the little box on the bottom right of the screen in windows, where the clock is).
