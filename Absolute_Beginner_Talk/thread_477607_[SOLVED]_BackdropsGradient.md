I have noticed something about the wallpaper/backdrops that come installed with Xubuntu.  Some of the backdrops are made so that they work with color gradients and some of them do not. How does that work, since they are all .png files?

### Post by mcduck on 2007-06-18
PNG images can have transparency. Background color or gradient is shown trough transparent parts of any PNG image if you use it as background image. This also works in Gnome, and probably in KDE too.
