### Post by GSF1200S on 2007-09-22
The reason everyone says Ubuntu is due to the FACT of its easiness. My 49 year old Mom who couldnt keep a Windows box running uses Ubuntu just fine.

Arch would be at the bottom of that list. It is DEFINITLY fast, and its a very nice distro with good wikis and a fairly good forum, but it involves hand configuration of files, and the install process will take at least an hour or so. The first time I installed Arch on my virtual machine I was reading a HOW-TO and the very first boot I received Kernel Panic. :(

Fedora is pretty good as well, although I like debian based distros better. Its an offshoot of RedHat (kinda) and it has a decent community. Worth trying out.

PCLinuxOS looks VERY nice as well. It didnt work with my laptop hardware very well, so I never really got to get a good run. It is fast though, and it places focus on ease of use. Its also a little conservative for my taste, but thats just me.

Ubuntu helps you out by starting you with Gnome, which is known for its ease of use. It is still very powerful, but its clearer than other DE's. BUT, Ubuntu doesnt limit you. One command in the terminal and you can get all the DE's you want. Ubuntu is also pretty cutting, maybe even bleeding edge, which allows you to try many new and exciting programs.

I might also sugges Debian. It has the largest repos, its debian based (of course :)), its easy to use, and also has an excellent community. You can also choose between 3 types of Debian: Old Stable, Stable, and Unstable. Old Stable gives you VERY old packages and programs, but its VERY stable because theyve been so thoroughly tested. Stable gives reasonably new stuff, and still remains stable. Dont let unstable fool you though- Ive heard plenty of people who run it as there primary OS and have YET to have a problem of any kind.. the name just implies that programs tested very little are included in its use (kind of the same place as Ubuntu)

Personally, I think youll be fine with any of the above except Arch. Arch is not that hard and its great distro, but I think you might want to learn a little about Linux before understanding it. good luck whatever your choice!! :)

### Post by jordanmthomas on 2007-09-22
I absolutely LOVE Arch (simple, but still on the bleeding edge), but if you're a beginner I wouldn't suggest it unless you want to spend your time reading how to configure things.  Once you have it set up, everything works great, but it does come at a price.  It's a great distro for a beginner who is interested in learning about his system, so don't be scared off by the fact they claim to cater to "competent" users.  This doesn't mean you have to know much ahead of time, but that you have to be smart enough to look things up if needed and to be able to figure things out (read:  not be an idiot)

If you're using Linux just for the heck of it or to learn, then I would whole-heartedly suggest it.

If you just want it to work and you don't want to deal with many problems, I'd suggest Ubuntu.

I can't really suggest Fedora for anyone, unless they've sped up their package management.
