### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-06-02
Here's a link to get you started: [http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Networkwifi](http://doc.gwos.org/index.php/Networkwifi)

Availability of different wireless PCI cards varies from country to country and some manufacturers change the chip from version to version.  This makes Linux compatibility a bit hit and miss, I'm afraid.](*,)

There will be cards that work natively - no drivers needed. Intel cards do this, but I've never found a PCMCIA card.  They are most likely to be the ones built-in to high-end laptops.

Some may have "closed source" drivers available from the manufacturer

Others (it seems the majority that are available where I live, including Netgear and D-Link) can be forced to work using some free tools (ndiswrapper or madwifi).  

Depending what card you get, there will be "how-tos" on these forums to walk you through the installation.
