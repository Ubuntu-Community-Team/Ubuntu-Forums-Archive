### Post by shadow-of-sin on 2007-12-06
> **Cammy said:**
> Ahh, a theme thread for me to hijack. :D

Since we're talking about themes, anyone know how to change the trash can icon on the bottom panel?
First you'll have to check the size of your panel, to do this right-click the Panel and click on "Properties" and take note of the size.

To replace the icon, open up nautilus and navigate to your home folder, if you can't see hidden files (the ones that start with a ".") then press Ctrl+H. Go to .icons/THEME_NAME/PANEL_SIZE (where THEME_NAME is the name of your theme and PANEL_SIZE is the size of your panel). Now just replace the "user-trash.png" image with the image you want for your trash can (make sure it is the right size).

### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-12-07
> **Cammy said:**
> Ahh, a theme thread for me to hijack. :D

Since we're talking about themes, anyone know how to change the trash can icon on the bottom panel?

In usr/share/icons there are your default (and installed) icon themes. In each folder there are other folders called "scalable" "24x24" "22x22" and "16x16" as well as other size folders... In those there are folders called "status", "places", and "emblems"... they all have trash can icons in them for you file system, panel, desktop...


When in root, you can substitute your full trash can and empty trash can icons with your new icons as long as they are using the exact same names, and the same size images, as well as the same file types like .svg and .png  


Before you mess with your icon packs, you should make a back up copy and keep it somewhere safe.
