### Post by meatpan on 2007-07-22
Making the best hardware decision on linux requires understanding what type of applications you want to run.  Also, consider your expectations for third-party licensing.

Are you going to try to run modern games built for windows, via wine, while preserving a high fps? 

If so, nvidia works well for many people.   Keep in mind that the nvidia software is invasive to your machine (by requiring kernel mods), so the performance and reliability of your ubuntu installation will be subject to a closed-source product from a corporation that does not have a formal relationship with the ubuntu community.  I'm not saying this is a problem, but simply a matter to consider when you make your decision.
