### Post by deanjm1963 on 2006-06-30
It's not so much the issue of 32-bit versus 64-bit, it's what you want to do with your linux installation. If you're deep into graphics/multimedia/music/etc.., 32-bit is the way to go for the moment, if you're after a server environment, the 64-bit version might be better suited.

There's been so many posts/threads about the 32/64-bit issue on many different boards, not just here on the Ubuntu Forums. 64-bit isn't necessarily faster than 32-bit when doing every day tasks. Until there's a complete mirror of 32-bit to 64-bit linux software, for the everyday user, 32-bit might be more useful.
