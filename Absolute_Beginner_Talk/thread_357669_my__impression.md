### Post by MSlaveubu on 2007-02-09
I have been ubuntu 6.10 for 1 week now.  Its my first time ever using linux, so it has all been totally new.  A very interesting experience, and 100% different from windows.  I havnt gotten any viruses, I havnt had to reboot because I installed a program, No security warnings either.  Its amazing, how much better ubuntu is.  

Cosmetically, well it looks OK out of the Drive (cd drive).  But one thing I have noticed, is that themes are much easier to install, and they do not appear to use up more memory than the default themes.  I have an OS X theme right now, no problems, feels just like "human".  When I had the OS X theme on XP, slow as a turtle.  I like how it is so easily customizable.  Icons are easier to change, and make (in gimp).  You can create panels for whatever you want.  I have a small panel in the corner of my screen for quick access to websites I visit often, or may need quick access to.  Which is very easy to make via the custom application launcher.  I click them, they open Firefox, and go straight to the page I want.  I even made my icon for "wine" a bottle of Asti (martini and rossi) .Its awesome.  

[view my desktop here]("http://i13.photobucket.com/albums/a263/cyoda/Screenshot-2.png")

Force quit, why doesn't windows have this?  Oh, it has task manager.  And end process, which then gives you the "end program now".  well thats a nice thought, but it never works.  Force quit is a godsend.  

Which leads me to memory managment.  XP does an offul job.  I can run GIMP and firefox with ubuntu.  (I have only 248mb ram on my PC).  With XP, I could hardly run gimp, it used 130 mb of ram, which dont exist.  Plus I dont have to reboot from it slowing to a crawl from the OS eating the memory.

My one problem, support.  Its not there.  The good news, I like open office better anyway.  The bad, other programs I like have equivalents, but I have to get use to them (I will).  I just wish more people used linux, its better, then it would get more support (like from my printer which will not work with linux).

I love it, I am hooked.  Even when I get my macbook in june.  I will dual boot with ubuntu (it there is a version that will work on x86/x64 macbook pro)

thanks for listening, and getting me throught the first week without my PC blowing up.:KS
