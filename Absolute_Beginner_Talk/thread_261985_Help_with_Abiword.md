### Post by Brunellus on 2006-09-21
I use abiword, and actually like it quite a bit.  

For accents:  GNOME has a nifty little applet that lets you change your keymaps.  right click on the gnome-panel, click "add to panel" and you'll see one for changing your keyboard layout.  I have mine set to switch between US English, UK English, and Spanish (which is what I use when I need accents).  

For fonts: see directions at 

[http://wiki.ubuntu.com/FontInstallHowto](http://wiki.ubuntu.com/FontInstallHowto)

Frankly, I'm not that big a fan of Times New Roman any more, though.  Not all that great from a typographical perspective.

And as for other programs for preparing documents:  the KOffice word processor (Kword?) is quite nice.  For a different perspective on how these things are done, there's always LyX, the 'document processor' which is not WYSYWIG, but outputs beautifully to paper or pdf or postscript or dvi.  It's a pretty steep learning curve for LyX--I'm still on it--but the printed output is GORGEOUS.
