### Post by Madpilot on 2006-10-08
There are several packages worth looking at.

gLabels is a nice, basic business card & label maker; it has pre-made templates for many of the common Avery/Avery-clone sheets of cards & labels. 

Inkscape is a full-blown SVG graphics editor, able to do anything, including nice posters & such. It's got really, really nice & powerful font/text features.

The GIMP is free software's answer to Photoshop - powerful, hard to learn, but able to do almost everything.

Scribus is a desktop publishing (DTP) app that I've used in the past for posters - it's good for PDF production, and PDF is the best way to put your posters on the web, or send them to a professional printer. I'll often start a project in Inkscape or the GIMP, then import the graphics to Scribus to produce the PDF version.

Whichever app you choose, get the Open Clipart package installed as well. It gives you a very wide selection of Public Domain (ie, totally free) graphics to use or modify as you need to.

All of these are in Ubuntu's repos, of course.
