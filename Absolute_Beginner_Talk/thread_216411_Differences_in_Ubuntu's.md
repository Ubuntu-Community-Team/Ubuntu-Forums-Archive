### Post by andb on 2006-07-15
> **IrishGangsta said:**
> I heard KDE was more graphical, and i am using Gnome but i also just installed XGL/Compiz but is kind of laggy.  Is KDE laggy with all of it's gaphical-ness?

XGL/Compiz is doing heavy graphical processing. I wouldnt even play with it if you have an onboard shared memory graphics chip. KDE/GNOME will run on the typical intel 8mb shared video chips just fine. 

Gnome is simpler, it tries to give you only what you need. KDE is more configurable, tries to give you everything you may need. I prefer gnome and then use the terminal if there is something I cant do in the interface, or maybe just because I like the terminal.

Install both. When you log in you can choose the session type. Try them, and find out for yourself what you like. There are other windows maagers too that you may want to look into...
