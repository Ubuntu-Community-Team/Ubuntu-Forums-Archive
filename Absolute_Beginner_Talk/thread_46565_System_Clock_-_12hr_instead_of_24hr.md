Your system clock?  Do you mean the one in the Gnome panel, or in a terminal?

You can change the panel clock by right clicking and selecting preferences (I think).  It has a 24 hour option.

The others can be changed by setting your locale to one that has the 24h format.

### Post by knewbix on 2005-07-05
Right click the clock, choose Date & Time Format > Times & Dates tab > Choose pH:MM:SS AMPM

I am using kde so hopefully it's the same for Gnome. I had the exact same problem a few hours ago and I don't blame you for not being able to find it because pH:MM:SS AMPM means nothing really. Call it "12 hour time" or something similar and there would be no problem. Hope that helps.
