### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-07
[QUOTE=pbaehr]I don't need a huge banner to appear everytime my mouse rolls over the application menu that tells me if I click here I can find the programs on my computer.[/QUOTE] Not only is this behavior modifiable--it being the default setting is unique to Kubuntu. I've seen no other KDE desktop behave that way (not SuSE, not Mepis, not Xandros, etc.).

I can give you specific examples of what makes KDE "so great." I can also give you some examples of what makes Gnome "so great." I don't think either is "dumbed down," to tell you the truth.

For further exposition on the pros and cons of each:
[http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome](http://www.psychocats.net/essays/kdevsgnome)

A few things, though...

**Better in KDE**: Random little things that you may want to configure tend to be more GUI and readily available. For example, having rotating wallpaper (a la a slideshow), toggling numlock's behavior at login, setting up a kiosk mode, editing the KMenu (sorry, SMEG just does not compare). If you right-click on a window, the first option is to close it, not to move it to another desktop.

**Better in Gnome**: Ease of installation for themes, particularly for GDM themes. Volume management (gnome-volume-manager beats ivman any day, in my book). Better-looking aqua themes--Baghira can be visually awkward at times. The Gnome archive application actually remembers that I like to extract to the desktop instead of always defaulting to my home folder.

Just a sample. Again, you can visit the above link for more details.

Having spent quite a bit of time with each desktop environment, I've generally found the conventional wisdom about both to not be true (i.e., I don't agree at all with the following statements):

1. Gnome is simplistic and thus better
2. Gnome is for idiots
3. KDE's options are too confusing for regular users
4. KDE is "more Windows-like" (whatever that means)
