### Post by aiwarrior on 2007-05-06
You can use twinview to run an app in each screen, for instance i use mplayer in fullscreen in my secondary display and keep the normal desktop in the primary. Also the bars etc dont strech to the secondary screen, but metacity has a bug/feature(? :D) in that when you already have a maximized app in the primary screen it will automatically open the new window in the secondary screen that is empty. Thats annoying and there isnt a fix for it in feisty that i know of.

About the performance i got no issues under twinview, as it is integrated with the nvidia drivers, and i doubt xinerema is faster, but i recommend a little research on that. 
Even in windows when you open a browser or even see a internet page the game/video on the secondary screen will have som momentanious chopping. If you increase the the process priority with the nice command chopping is going to be more unlikely on the game/video but will make other programs less responsive

By the way you use linux to play games? :D 

All that i said is about twinview as i never used xinerama, perhaps some research will yield better info about the two, than mine.
