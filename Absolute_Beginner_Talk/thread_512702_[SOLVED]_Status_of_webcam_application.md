### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-07-30
We are up against hardware manufacturers here with webcams, just the same as printer manufacturers.

I find it interesting that some Linux users seem to have the attitude that it is the fault of Linux if a particular piece of hardware can't be coaxed into working.

If a manufacturer doesn't release an open source driver, or at least firmware and hardware details, so that a driver can be written, where is Linux at fault?

The golden rule here is to do a bit of research on the internet first, before buying a peripheral.

I have seen it so many times, where someone comes onto these forums, stating that they have just bought a new Lexmark printer and they are complaining that it won't work.

Admittedly, if someone has just loaded-up Linux and a piece of hardware that they already have doesn't work may cause some consternation. 

However, direct your ire at the manufacturer, not at Linux.

If enough people did this, perhaps hardware manufacturers would release open source drivers.

I wouldn't hold my breath, though.

Regards,
Roger.:cool:
