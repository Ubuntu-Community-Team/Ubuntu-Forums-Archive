### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-05-07
I put Freespire 1.0 on a spare machine to compare it to Dapper.  It has a lot of polish and it's more stable than the Kubuntu Breezy that I installed a little over a year ago.  I like the simplicty of LSongs, it rips CD's at a default 192 kbps VBR.  It works well with a Samba share where I have put all of my music.  What I don't like about LSongs is that it is written in Python and the python interpreter uses up 50% of the CPU while the other 50% is used for encoding.  So your load factors are always 1.0 when LSongs is doing nothing, and 2.0 or more when ripping or listening to music.

I'm not sure if it is the Samba share that is causing this or if this is normal.  The music file library can be read and processed by Rhythmbox and Banshee (of two that I have tried) so that's good.

It's nice to sudo apt-get install justaboutanyprogramyoucanthinkof in Freespire.  That's the only reason that I keep it.  Not sure how it compares to Kubuntu Dapper in terms of stability.  I got tired of Amarok, Konquerer, and Kbittorent crashing.  You know, stuff that you use every day.

I haven't thoroughly tested Freespire, but from what I've seen, it's got some polish and it's certainly workable.  The Linspire forums are not as extensive as the Ubuntu forums, but then any problem that you can't solve in the Linspire forum usually has several Ubuntu approaches that will solve your problem.

Click and Run seems to bother some folks, but I see it as an outlet for commercial Linux software.  Open source is free, but there are valid commercial solutions and CNR makes it easy to try them and purchase them.

The updates work similar to Ubuntu's update manager.  

The best part is mp3's and videos work out of the box.  No Automatix, no LAME drivers to load.  Of course when you install,  you have to click on 4 or 5 EULA agreements, and that gives some folks the willies.

Bottom line if Ubuntu (Dapper, Edgy, or Feisty) doesn't work out of the box on your machine, try Freespire.  Less headache, more myspace.
