### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-01-24
Ubuntu has no mp3 support by default, due to legal issues. It is very easy to enable mp3 support after installation, however.

Your iPod also may not work immediately, but there are a few guides to getting it all working (I think). A quick search should give you some good guides. I also have an mp3 player (Creative Zen Vision: M), and it works fine with gnomad2 (not the one in the repositories though).

Yes, your internet and email stuff will work fine, as before. You just need to configure your email client (Evolution is installed by default, but you can install a different one if you want to). If you are using wireless internet, you might have some problems getting it set up, but again, there are thousands of guides on how to go about doing this.
