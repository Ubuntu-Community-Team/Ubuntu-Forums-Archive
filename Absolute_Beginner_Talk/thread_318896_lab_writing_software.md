### Post by kebes on 2006-12-14
Well many people love LaTeX, which is a somewhat programmatic document preparattion system. You write your document using a special syntax (with tags like "\figure{blah.eps}" or whatever), and then you basically "compile" it into a PDF document that you can email or print.

It's a very powerful system, although quite daunting at first. It is well-suited to inclusion of graphics (typically in EPS format) that you export from the graphing program of your choice (GNUPlot, OpenOffice, etc.). It is also excellent for including math (it has a very efficient built-in "language" for writing math expressions). In fact LaTeX was originally created for laying-out mathematics and physics papers.

Like I said, it takes some practice but many people love it once they get the hang of it. There are graphic front-ends to make it easier to use (does syntax highlighting and so on). "Kile" is one I've used in the past. If you're interested in LaTeX, there are lots of tutorials all over the net.


If you want to stick to more standard GUI-like editors (i.e.: WYSIWYG), in addition to OpenOffice, there is also AbiWord and KWord (part of KOffice).
