### Post by MrFSL on 2007-12-01
```
the "return" key was originally implimented on typewriters.(remember those?) 
```

Consequently we hang onto a lot of the language and mechanics from those days. When typing text and pressing "ENTER" or "RETURN" some hidden characters are typed. These characters can be <CR> or <LF> or a combination of the two. These characters literally stand for "Carriage Return" and "Line Feed." Which were the two functions a typewriter or old style printer would have to accomplish to go to the next line. -- The carriage would have to return to the left, and the roller would have to feed the paper upward one line! =D>

I expect that on newer computers the Return key (which was replaced by the Enter key) will again be replaced by the LineFeed or FEED key! :D ;)
