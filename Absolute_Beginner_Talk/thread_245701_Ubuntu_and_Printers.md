### Post by steve.horsley on 2006-08-28
Epson printers are generally best suported, although I don't know about the combo scannet/printer/copier ones. But I have a personal dislike for Epson - they always seem to dry up on me if I don't print something every few days. And even buying new ink cartridges doesn't help because it's the nozzle beneath that's clogged.

HP are fairly well supported. After I smashed up my last Epson in frustration, I got an HP, and I couldn't be happier. It always "just works", even after a month of non-use. And I gather the replacement cartridges also have built-in nozzles so even if it did clog, a new cartridge would fix it.

This is a good place to look for details:
[http://www.linuxprinting.org/](http://www.linuxprinting.org/)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters)
