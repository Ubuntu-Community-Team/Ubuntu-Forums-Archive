### Post by Orochi on 2007-06-22
First of all, I assume you already checked this, but you *do* have the Intel graphics chip that 915 resolution is mean to fix right? If you're having resolution issues with a different graphics card, 915 resolution won't fix anything.

If you do have the correct chipset and you're using 915resolution, make sure your video driver is listed as i810 in xorg.conf (should be under the "Device" section). 

It also might be helpful if you posted your xorg.conf here so we could take a look at it.

### Post by Orochi on 2007-06-22
Hmm... everything looks ok to me (although I don't actually have my own Intel graphics card'd Ubuntu machine with me right now, so I can't compare to my own xorg.conf). 

I also assume you've restarted your comptuer since installing 915res yes? If not you'll need to before it works.

My next suggestion would be to try the new intel driver that is supposed to have widescreen support built in without need for 915resolution. I've updated to this driver on my computer and it works perfectly (although 915res *also* worked for me, so I don't know what will happen for you).
To install the new driver, go into Synaptic and uninstall 915resolution (choose "completely remove"). 
Then open terminal and type 'sudo apt-get install xserver-xorg-video-intel'
Then open up your xorg.conf and under the "Device" section change Driver from i810 to intel.
Then restart your computer and hope this driver works better than the last one :)

I still have a lot of problems with my resolution with Intel chipsets, the biggest problem being that it seems to ignore the resolutions listed in xorg.conf and supplies its own list if you go to System->Preferences->Screen Resolution. However the new intel driver correctly determines my native screen resolution, so hopefully it will for you too.

### Post by phr0ze on 2007-06-22
This setting here is the biggest cause of incorrect resolutions:

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "Generic Monitor"
Option "DPMS"
HorizSync 28-80
VertRefresh 43-60
EndSection


You need to ensure the Horiz and Vert lines are identical to what is specified by your monitor. If you tell us the laptop brand and model we may be able to look it up for you, but your best bet may be some specification sheet. DO NOT take a guess.
