### Post by LaRoza on 2007-09-05
> **Officer Dibble said:**
> Why is it advisable to write Ubuntu at speeds such as 4X to CD-R?

Faster speeds mean errors.

For many things, little errors are no big deal, for an OS, it should be exact.

---

### Post by jordanmthomas on 2007-09-05
Some writers are prone to make errors when writing at high speeds, so if you have problems with your install, people suggest you burn it slower to avoid these errors.

Personally, I am always able to burn Linux isos at the highest speed and I've never had any problems  (well, once, but there was another reason).

### Post by deadgobby on 2007-09-05
The ISO hold lots of data. Burning it on high speeds such as 52X. May cause the data not to burn on the CD properly or burning on the foil on the CD. Burning at a nice speed of 10x or lower. The ISO data can be etch on the foil with out loosing important information.
 It can be the same thing. Like burning lots of MP3 files to a CD and play them on your MP3 car stereo for example.
