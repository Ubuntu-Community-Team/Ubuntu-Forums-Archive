### Post by Kulgan on 2006-10-22
I wouldn't recommend getting a book. I have Linux Complete lying around, and it's already out of date, just because linux updates so quickly. You can get windows books because nothing really changes - you have XP for six years and get two service packs, neither of them changing any of the core functions. 

With Linux and most open source projects, things change faster because a) the developers listen and b) there are more people working on it. So your book will be fine untill the next release, if you are lucky, but no further. 

Just my advice...
