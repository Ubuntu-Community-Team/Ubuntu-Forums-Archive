*Andrew K.*:  I presume you got a screen similar to this:  [http://img255.imageshack.us/my.php?image=feistysingle01fb0.png](http://img255.imageshack.us/my.php?image=feistysingle01fb0.png)

If so, and nothing is working (no error messages to diagnose the problem), I would suggest downloading the Alternate CD and booting into that.  It's a text based installer that will rid of most video card issues that tend to hinder the installation process.  A lot of video cards aren't support by the manufacturers in Linux yet so it can cause problems, especially during the installation process.

[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download)

Below the green 'Start Download' button there is a check box to get the Alternate CD.  It's a very easy installer and it will walk you through the process step by step.

Hi. Did you download it from [here]("http://www.ubuntu.com/")? What are your system specifications (processor, memory)?

> How do I boot from CD?
Seems like you already are. In any case, to change which device to boot from you need to get into the BIOS of your machine by pressing DEL, ESC, or one of the function buttons after powering on the computer; usually a screen with the brand of motherboard shows up, and then yo press the button. As for actually setting the option to boot from CD, it all really depends on the brand of motherboard you have. Most are failry intuitive to navigate.
