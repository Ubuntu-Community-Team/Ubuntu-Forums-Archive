### Post by dusan.saiko on 2007-04-26
Hello, 

I have recently decided to migrate my two OpenSuse 10.2 computers to kUbuntu,
so let me share my opinion and reasons for this action.

I have come to following conclusion:

OpenSuse:
- out of box OpenSuse looks nicer and works better than out of box installation of ubuntu
- loads quite slower (may be is slower in general)
- has quite bad package updater, very slow and not always working repositories + ZMD took 100% CPU for several hours before I have killed it (zmd/kerry/beagle uninstallation would be my first task to do after new install)

Ubuntu:
- needs more utilization at beginning
- has more and newer software packages
- has bigger community 
- has better 'license policy' :-)
- after connecting Novel to Microsoft, Ubuntu has probably better perspective than Suse :haha:
[http://www.google.com/trends?q=ubuntu%2C+kubuntu%2C+suse%2C+opensuse](http://www.google.com/trends?q=ubuntu%2C+kubuntu%2C+suse%2C+opensuse)


in overall, after some time spent with Ubuntu, it seems to me that it works nicer, faster and I am feeling (quite subjective :-)) somehow what I would call "better Linux experience" :-)

just what I do not understand too much is why ubuntu is acting like kUbuntu is different product with different homepage/downloads/forums etc. that seems quite strange and unlogic to me ;-)

so thanks for this software and keep rolling :-)
