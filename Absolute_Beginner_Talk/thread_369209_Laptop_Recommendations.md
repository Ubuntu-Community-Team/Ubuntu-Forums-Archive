### Post by vayde on 2007-02-24
Nvidia graphics.  Others can be made to work certainly, and many work very well, but   if linux can be said to be designed with any specific hardware in mind, it was and is Nvidia.

You are going to have to do a little bit of investigating.  Certain devices that computer manufacturers use interchangeably are just not interchangeable under linux.  Dell for example used to ship Intell Pro Wireless cards with their laptops, which work right out of the box with at least Ubuntu Dapper, and now they're selling rebranded broadcom cards that can be tricky- not impossible, but tricky.  Linksys just changed a chipset in their 200M usb ethernet adapters without changing the model numbers, and guess what?  They don't work with less than a 2.6.18 kernel.  This sort of stuff happens to us on the Linux side, and you should be prepared.  Not discouraged, but prepared.

Here's what I use, and I have had nothing but success so far:
Dell Inspiron 9300
2.0G Pentium M
1G Ram
Intell Pro Wireless 2200
Nvidia Go 6800
Ubuntu 6.06 Dapper

Good Luck!
