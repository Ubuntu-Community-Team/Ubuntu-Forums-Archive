### Post by Linux_Man on 2007-10-20
OK First you will see all your drives/partitions you have You might have a floppy drive, CD-Rom Drive and if your dual-booting Windows you will see a X.X Gigabyte volume, thats your Windows partition, you might even see a smaller volume, that is most likely your "Recovery" partition, some computers have them others don't, the "Filesystem" is your main Linux partition with all your data, If you click on it you will see a large amount of files with names such as "dev" "etc" and "var" don't worry about these, unless you wish to be come either a Linux sysadmin, or a total power user, you don't need to worry about these files, up in the Nautilus (Your filebrowser) click the Home Icon, this is your Home directory, these are all your files such as your pictures, music, and documents, its equivalent to the Windows "My Documents" folder, there you can click on "Desktop" and that is everything that is shown on your desktop, think of it much like a folder rather then a different thing. Hope this helps you better understand how Linux works.
