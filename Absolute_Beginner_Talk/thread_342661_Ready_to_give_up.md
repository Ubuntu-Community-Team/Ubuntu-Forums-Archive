### Post by an.echte.trilingue on 2007-01-20
It should not make any difference if you burned it on the mac or on a windows box.  What does make a difference is if you burned the iso as a bootable disk image or as file that is on a non-bootable disk. [Read this, if you haven't already.]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BurningIsoHowto")

Now, when you say it freezes at the welcome menu, just what exactly do you mean?   Are there any words on the screen?  If so, what are they?  Have you let it run for a while after it freezes, maybe a half hour?  There might be something going on in the background that is slowing things down.

 (in my experience, freezes are usually related to a hardware issue and not a problem on the disk, and this problem is usually the video card or in some rare cases the CD drive)

I hope that I can help you!

### Post by mysticmarks on 2007-01-20
burning disk images must be done slower on most older burners. High speed burning isnt appropriate for disk images as the can become corrupt very easily during the burn. Burn at a slower rate (1x-4x). High speed is fine for lots of little files and for music. If your burning software allows for a verification of the burn, do so.
