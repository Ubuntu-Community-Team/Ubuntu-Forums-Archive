### Post by kplaxmaster on 2008-01-25
Is your graphics card installed correctly on your computer?  Do you have an ATI or NVIDIA graphics card?

Go to System > Administration > Restricted Drivers Manager.  See if there is anything you can install from there.

I only ask because when you don't utilize your graphics card, graphical interfaces will load up slower, and when there is movement on your screen (in turn which could be used by your GPU instead of CPU), this can slow your computer down.

Since you said you have a clean install, I can't see it being anything else since you aren't loading up any new irregular services.
