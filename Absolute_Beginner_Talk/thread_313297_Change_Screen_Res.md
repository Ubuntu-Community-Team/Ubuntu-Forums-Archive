### Post by kakalaky on 2006-12-05
You need to edit the xorg.conf file:

```
sudo nano -w /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

change the screen section to look something like this (used your example resolution of 1280x720):

```
Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "NVIDIA Corporation NVIDIA Default Card"
Monitor "DELL D1025HE"
DefaultDepth 24
SubSection "Display"
Depth 1
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 4
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 8
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 15
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 16
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 24
Modes "1280x720" "1024x768" "800x600" "720x350" "640x480"
EndSubSection
EndSection
```

### Post by floke on 2006-12-06
It's a good idea to make a backup of your xorg.conf file in case anything goes wrong in the future - that way you can simply rewind to a workable setting.
Navigate to the /etc/X11/xorg.conf file in either Konqueror or Nautilus open it up (double click in GUI) - it should crack open in Kate or something. Then click 'save as' - and rename the file xorg.conf(backup) or something. Save it in an easily accessible place (desktop or documents or something). 

This just saved my xorg life today - am very glad I did it!

Good luck
