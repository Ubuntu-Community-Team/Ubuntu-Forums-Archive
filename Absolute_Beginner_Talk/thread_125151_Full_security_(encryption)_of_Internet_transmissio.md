### Post by rmjokers on 2006-02-03
I dont know anything about the Total Net Shield product, but it sounds a bit fishy to me.  The only way you can ensure that your communications are protected by encryption is if BOTH ends of the communication agree to do encryption.  Thus, you might have encrypted communication to Anonymizer, but once it arrives there, they most likely decrypt it and send it in the clear to the intended recipients.  I suppose you could argue that this is more secure if you trust Anonymizer, but that is a matter of opinion.  If you want real security, you are probably looking for VPN software, which linux does support.
