### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-08-23
Well a distro is a collection of apps on top of the linux kernel and GNU ...

Differences in distros :

1. How large and "up to date" are the repos ?

2. Package management (.deb , .rpm , emerge, pacman, slapt-get)

3. Frequency of kernel and security updates.

4. Some distros allow more control/configuration then others.

5. Desktop environments. Some kistros are KDE, others gnome, some xfce, occasional icewm or fluxbox.

My take :

Redhat/Centos/Novel = business oriented 

Fedora =  Development arm of Redhat

Debian and Ubuntu are very similar.

Slackware based distros are very slick. The seem to run faster and are configured via text files. You will learn a lot about how Linux works with one of these distors. Consider Zenwalk or Wolvix.

Then there is Gentoo/Arch/Sourcemage : These types of distros cater more to those who want to more highly configure their OS, including if one goes to gentoo, installing from source.

Of course there are a ton of distros I left out ...

HTH
