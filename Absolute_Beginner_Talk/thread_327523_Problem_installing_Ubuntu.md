### Post by jml on 2006-12-29
I agree with meng.  Your system will have problems running Ubuntu.  The Gnome desktop environment, ( a part of the GUI,) is very resource intensive.  If you have access to another computer with an internet connection, you might try downloading and trying Xubuntu.  Its specifically designed for older computers with more modest specifications.

Damn Small Linux is also a good lightweight distro, however, the most recent version has trouble installing on a system such as yours, the graphical intstaller needs more resources than older versions od DSL did.  Good luck and enjoy the ride!

amber, you didn't mention the capacity of the new harddrive. your computers bios probably won't support anything above 80gb. this won't stop you from installing an operating system, just spending extra money needlessly. as far as your 96mb of ram your computer either supports a max of 256 or 384mb depending on how many slots your motherboard has. 128x2, or 128x3. upgrading is cheap especially if you have a computer store in your area the sells used parts. go to the ubuntu home page and you can order a copy of the current version (version 6.06 on cd, or download the iso files for version 6.10) i'd order the cd as 6.06 has a life span of 3 years, and i think 6.10's is 3 months.
