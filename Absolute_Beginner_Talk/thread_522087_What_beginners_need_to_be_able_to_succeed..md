Hi. I just got Ubuntu Edgy Eft installed last week, and I'm in devouring reading mode. In my attempt to get the modem working, I have discovered that there are MANY helpful people - however, I still haven't succeeded. 

You see, absolute beginners need to be talked to as if they know *nothing*. People have said, "make this file, install that,"  etc. but, it needs to be spelled out - click to open the Terminal, type this *exact* set of words, hit <enter>, then type this other exact set of words, and so on.

People assume a level of knowledge that seems to you experts to be absolutely obvious, and even a cretin would know it. Unfortunately, this is not the case (or maybe there are a lot of cretins out here!;o)).

I think it's really *cool* that the Ubuntu Community wants to be inclusive, and get everybody onto Ubuntu and Linux. The more people that embrace Linux, the more drivers will be supported for everything, and the more we can thumb our noses at old Bill!

### Post by eentonig on 2007-08-12
Yes, it 's hard and confusing in the beginning. The best advice I can give you?

1. If you have a question. Search the forum/internet. 
2. If you dont find the answer, post a question. 
3. Try to not show your frustration in your post. difficult sometimes. But people here are all volunteers and human. They tend to be more helpfull for those who are in an 'open' state of mind.
3. Be precise in your question. 
- 1.1.  What is the problem? (What do you want to achive)
- 1.2. What did you already try?
- 1.3. In case of errors, ask which logs could be usefull.

If someone gives you an advice you don't know what to do with. Say so. Tell them to give very basic tasks.

Learning will come along the way. Soon you'll learn the corrcet 'terms' and your google searches will become more and more successfull.
