### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-02-23
Download the ordinary Ubuntu CD and burn it as an ISO. You can then boot from it to see if your hardware works (it doesn't look like you'll have any problems). You may want to try the 32bit version instead of the 64bit one, which still has issues apparantly. The LiveCD will run very slowly, and is no indication of how fast Ubuntu will run once installed. If your hardware works in the LiveCD, it shoulw work once you install Ubuntu with no additional tweaking.

### Post by houstonbofh on 2007-02-23
The WiFi card could be a problem.  I would order a well supported one.  Trust me...  I have been doing this a while.  How much is your blood pressure worth?  The big consumer WiFi card makers change chips without changing part numbers.  The use the driver to load the BIOS. And they care less about Linux...  Many of the smaller makers just work right out of the box.  Often they are better cards for the money as well.  Spend some time and find a good and well supported card, and then install.  Play with the Live CD and read more in the mean time.  Also, consider that Feisty Faun comes out if 2 months.  I am running it now, and it has some big improvements.  Once it is stable, it will be amazing.
