### Post by daynah on 2007-04-10
I remember that one of the good things about linux is that when you delete something, it's actually deleted, unlike in windows when it's only halfway deleted, clogging up your system.

Unfortunately, this is one of those cases where it's not a good thing.

I don't think you can undelete that.

This thread is referring to ext2/3: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=403396&highlight=undelete](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=403396&highlight=undelete)

This thread says you CAN'T recover from ext3 (nullifying the thread above... doh!) It has a bunch of helpful links: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=367510&highlight=undelete](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=367510&highlight=undelete)

All of the ways are very, very difficult. I'm not sure if they can be done on vfat (I've never head of vfat actually, you're stumping me). Hopefully someone else will reply with something more helpful.

But for now, I think the most helpful thing I can give you is this link: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=35087](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=35087)

Change the instructions to suit your needs, double check them. Backup and save to a disk, and write the date on the disk. I like putting it on a re-writeable disk, so I don't have a bunch of backups floating around and I'm constantly thinking "Don't I have something more recent than that?!"

Chances are 10 gigs didn't appear just yesterday, and you had a chance to back most of it up. So learn from mistakes and don't let it happen again, ya? :) Also, backing up now will be quite helpful if you hurt your computer while trying these crazy ways to recover those 10 gigs.

Good luck, and I'm sorry about your day. :(
