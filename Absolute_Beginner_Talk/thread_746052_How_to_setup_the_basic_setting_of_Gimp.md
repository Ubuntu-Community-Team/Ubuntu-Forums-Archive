### Post by a.v.l on 2008-04-05
When I open Gimp I only have the block of tools open on the left hand side, those containing the selection tools, text tools and zoom tool etc. How do I arrange the tools on the right hand side of the  display, such as layers, histogram etc. so they stay there when the image in in the centre of the screen, roughly as you find in Photoshop?  

I would also like to know how do I revert back to the arrow pointer after selecting one of the tools on the left.

---

### Post by BobCFC on 2008-04-05
If you mean that you closed the toolboxes on the right and you want to get them back you can reset the layout by going to **File->Preferences->Window Management** and click the button at the bottom called **Reset Saved Window Positions to Default Values**.

You will have to restart GIMP to take effect.

You could also use the Dialogues menu that is at the top of an image when you edit it.  (yes multiple menus is confusing)



If you mean that you want the toolboxes to stay on top permanently like in Photoshop, so that they don't disappear underneath when you click on the main image, you can also do this from the preferences:
**File->Preferences->Window Management** and change the **Hint for the toolbox** from **Normal** to **Keep Above**.   Do the same for **Hint for other Docks** underneath.

You will also have to restart for these to take effect.



To revert back to the default Move button from the toolbox I think you can do this by pressing the **M** key.   You can see the shortcuts in the menus... when you have an image open look at the Tools menu... you can see items for the toolbox buttons such as Move, Rectangle and Pencil,  next to them are the Shortcuts such as M, R, N etc.

Hope this helps
