### Post by ubuntu27 on 2007-04-20
is there a specific feature in NJSTAR that you need?

If all you need is to be able to type in Asian language (Japanese, Chinese...) then you won't need a special application like NJSTAR (I used to use that in WIndows :) )

Click on the link in my signature that says "How to imput Japanese, Korean or Chinese"

Or just click on this one:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SCIM](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SCIM)


After you are done following that guide, you will be able to type in any of those languages on any applications.

SO After you enable Chinese input, you can just launch OpenOffice.org (from Applications Menu/Office/OpenOffice.org) and right click and choose SCIM_(language)  [I am typing this on top of my head, I can't remember that exact process] 
you should be fine with it. 


Good luck,
