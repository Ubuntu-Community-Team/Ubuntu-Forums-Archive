### Post by Yookji on 2008-01-13
> **kanati said:**
> -Vista boots faster then Ubuntu in spite of loading nearly twice as much data into the memory.  (minor complaint, Ubuntu still boots quite fast)

Here's one trick you can use to boot faster:
Restart your system, and once you get to grub press 'e' on the line you boot from. Then go to the second line down, press 'e' again and add 'profile' to the end of the line and press enter to save and 'b' to boot. This time, it will take a while to boot, but subsequent times it will be a lot faster. My understanding of this is that it's making a list of the locations of the files it uses to boot so that it can go faster later boots.
