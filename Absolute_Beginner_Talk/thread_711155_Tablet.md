### Post by sillyxone on 2008-02-29
I have the same concern. My cousin just bought an IBM tablet, I'm still hesitating as tablet is not very well supported under Linux yet.

His job mainly is on research and teaching, so he take notes a lot on PDF files. The problem is with handwriting, you need something that both fast and indexable. Usually your handwriting is stored as digital ink, and at the same time the application try to recognize and convert to text in the background for indexing. The process of recognizing sometimes can stagger your computer to a crawling slow, but without it, you won't be able to search for text later on, especially when you have to deal with 100+ notes eventually.

For his specific purpose (PDF), he looked at jarnal (free) and PDFAnnotator (commerical). I don't know what the difference between them but he picked PDFAnnotator (his tablet runs Vista Business).

I'm looking for a tablet too, but there's no way I'm going back to Windows, just stick with my laptop for a while more then.
