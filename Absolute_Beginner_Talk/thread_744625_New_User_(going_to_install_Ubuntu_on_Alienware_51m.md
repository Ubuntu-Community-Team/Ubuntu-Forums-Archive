### Post by privaterolf on 2008-04-03
Yes, Ubuntu has a GUI.

I would suggest getting a live CD of Feisty, available at:

[http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu)

Burn the image (don't just burn the ISO), and you should be able to boot from it.
This will in no way alter your system settings.

You can try most of your hardware settings in there.

Just be aware that Broadcom wireless chipsets aren't really supported out-of-the-box yet to my knowledge.

The live CD should automatically configure display settings based on your monitor and graphics card.

### Post by slackmaster on 2008-04-03
Patronsaint,

Ubuntu 7.10 is easy to install, and with you background in computers, it will be even easier.  

As privaterolg said, just get a livecd of the latest distro to test it out on your hardware.  With the livecd you will be able to tell if there are any issues.  

If it works, just click on the install icon right on the desktop and then answer the questions.  They are all really straightforward.  

Once it is installed, that is when it can be a really simple and easy system to use or a very complicated one, depending on what you know and what you want to do with it.

Keep in mind that if you are a gamer (alienware) you should take a close look at what is available on Ubuntu.  sure you can run alot of windows games on Wine, but it might not hurt to do a dual boot just in case.  
On the other hand, windows is just too annyoing because of malware issues, so personally I just gave up on it completely and switched to Linux.  Still, I keep a copy of windows running on another computer since I am sometimes on a deadline and Linux sometimes takes more time than windows depending on what you know and what you are trying to do.
