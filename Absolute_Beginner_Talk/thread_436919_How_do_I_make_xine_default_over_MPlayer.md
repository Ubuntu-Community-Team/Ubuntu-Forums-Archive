### Post by BobLand on 2007-05-08
Initially, I played an instructional DVD (Woodturning) that I view fairly often.  My TV is "HD Ready" whatever that means, but the picture is very sharp.  As it is very small (17") and my eye sight is ancient I didn't notice how bad the picture quality of the DVD was until I viewed it in Mplayer, VCL and xine.

At first I thought it was the players.  Then I popped a very new DVD in and it was sharp as a tack.  Overall, I found xine to be much sharper, crisper and had better color rendition MPlayer, which is barely acceptable and VCL which, for me, is a very close second.

I prefer xine's controls over both.  My only wish would be single-step frames so I can freeze a frame to analyze the techniques in the DVD.  The slomo is sufficient for now.

I've made xine the default player for all my video files.
