### Post by JAwuku on 2007-03-14
I recently came across a page where you can encrypt directories in Ubuntu.

Details are given on this page:

[http://www.debuntu.org/2006/06/04/62-how-to-use-encrypted-directories-with-encfs-and-fuse](http://www.debuntu.org/2006/06/04/62-how-to-use-encrypted-directories-with-encfs-and-fuse)

you can even encrypt whole partitons:

[http://old.pupeno.com/blog/encrypted-home-in-ubuntu-or-kubuntu-or-debian/](http://old.pupeno.com/blog/encrypted-home-in-ubuntu-or-kubuntu-or-debian/)

A much more detailed Ubuntu page:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EncryptedFilesystem](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/EncryptedFilesystem)

I would recommend backing up your hard disk before attempting anything.

You'll have to remember your pass-key or write it down and store it securely. Due to the strength of encryption, if you forget it, you will not be able to retrieve your data.
