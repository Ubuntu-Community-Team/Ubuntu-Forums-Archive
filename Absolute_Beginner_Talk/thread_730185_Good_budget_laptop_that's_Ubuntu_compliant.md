I am using a Dell Inspiron 6000 with Ubuntu 7.10 running Mac4Lin with Compiz desktop.  About 3 years old, 512m RAM, nothing spectacular. Works flawlessly with a clean install - I removed Windows completely about 14 months ago - haven't used it since. These are available second hand fairly cheaply (ebay etc)

Regarding Dell, in Australia I tried to order a new Vostro notebook with Linux preinstalled and was informed by Dell they will not do it - will only provide XP or Vista preinstalled - because of a licensing agreement with MS. Not only that, they will not ship me a notebook with no OS - so I have to pay for a copy of XP or Vista which I have no intention of using, and I have to do my own install of Ubuntu. Not a big deal but the principle involved annoyed me to the extent where I decided not to do business with them at this time.  It is interesting to note that the Dell US website allows you to order laptops with Linux preinstalled - the Aussie site does not - and I don't think that Dell US will deliver to Australia.  So much for Dell support for Ubuntu.
