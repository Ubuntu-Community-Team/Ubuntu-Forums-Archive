### Post by netwerx on 2007-05-16
Due to your need for these commercial applications, i would recommend a dual boot setup for you.

First of, download the iso and get it burnt.

second, pop in your new best friend (your ubuntu/kbuntu cd) and give it a whirl. You can run the live cd on most systems. This will not edit your files or existing windows programs.

This will give you a taste of ubuntu and a quick view on if your hardware is working well.

When your ready to take the plunge, backup anything important, and get going on the install.

If your use requires Adobe products specifically or development using this special programs, you won't be doing a full switch.

There are many great alternatives to the programs listed, however for your easy of use, i would recommend doing the above.

> how does the OS know what drivers to put in?
AFAIK hardware detection loads the drivers. If for any reason the hardware isn't detected, you can manually load drivers using modprobe. Failing this, if you have really exotic hardware, you may have to compile a custom kernel with the correct drivers.
