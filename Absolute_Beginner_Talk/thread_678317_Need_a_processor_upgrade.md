### Post by dcstar on 2008-01-25
> **waltervalderrama said:**
> Hello, i have an AMD 64 AM2 3200+, 1 GB RAM and K9N Neo2 and want to upgrade to an X2 processor. i don't want to loss my system already installed. Do I have to reinstall everything after upgrading? or can i continue with my actual system after upgrading.


The Ubuntu kernel already has multi-processor support built into it, you will just find that you now have "two" CPUs available with the dual core X2 and things should be  a bit quicker.

The biggest issue may be that your motherboard BIOS supports whatever new CPU you are purchasing - I recently upgraded from a Sempron to an X2 but I purchased a slow(ish) version that I knew my (old) BIOS supported.

Also be aware that the CPU temperature diodes on the Brisbane core X2s read incorrectly, I have to add a 20C offset to get a reasonable approximation of the core temp.
