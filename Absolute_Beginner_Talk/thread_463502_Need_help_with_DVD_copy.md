### Post by yabbadabbadont on 2007-06-03
Unless your recorder is dual-layer, you will have to settle for either a partial backup, or reduced quality backup, any time the original disc is dual-layer.  There are several differnent programs that can do this, just use synaptic to search for DVD and read the descriptions.  Just be aware that some of the newest DVDs have a copy protection scheme that the OSS tools don't yet seem to be able to handle.  (this is different from the CSS encoding)

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-03
I have been very happy with k9copy.

It produces a good quality copy of a DVD9 onto a DVD5, without noticible degradation.

I have tried a couple of the other programs and there is noticible pixelisation with them:

It runs fine under both KDE and GNOME.

ps I have found it best not to use the auto-burn option, but get it to create the iso. Then use k3b to burn the iso.
