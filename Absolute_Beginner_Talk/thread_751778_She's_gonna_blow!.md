### Post by krinderlin on 2008-04-10
I had the same issue with my Gateway C140-X.  However, Gateway can't build a laptop to save their lives apparently.

I'm not sure if your laptop is under warranty and how these actions may void said warranty, but here's what I did to alleviate the issues:

First, I grabbed a tube of Arctic Silver 5 and put that between all the contacts the head sink/pipe/fan combo contacted my CPU, GPU, and one other chip I've yet to recall what it is.

Second, I dusted out all the fan blades.

Finally, and probably the most horrible thing you could ever do to a computer under warranty, I tore out all the heat shielding on the compartment's cover plate.  I don't use my laptop on my lap much, so I really didn't need it.

Apparently, all of the above finally solved my over heating problems.
