### Post by browndog on 2006-03-22
For some years now I've used the Windows OS and related software at an expert / administrator level...what an interesting experience to take up Linux to satisfy my curiosity, become seriously interested in the Ubuntu disto, and then have to swallow my pride like a kid on the first day of school and start all over again.  No complaining here...I'm thrilled with what I've learned over the past few months using Ubuntu; I've learned much about Ubuntu but even more than that I've learned about Linux (the best and future de facto OS) and free software in general.  While I certainly consider myself a novice now and for some time to come, I would offer this one piece of advice to any Linux/Ubuntu newcomer...USE THE UBUNTU LIVE CD UNTIL YOU FEEL COMFORTABLE WITH UBUNTU.  Honestly, Ubuntu really is one of the best (if not the best) distribution of an operating system that is still in a developmental/grass roots stage when it comes to user friendliness, but is on it's way to surpassing MS Windows.  I say use the live CD first because if you do you won't experience the frustration of making major / sweeping changes to a computer that is probably very important to you...I know mine is to me.  Even small mistakes can cripple your system, and they're all too easy to make when you're an enthusiastic newbie wanting to make everything "just work" all at once.  With the  Ubuntu live CD, you get to pack up your things and quit playing when you become frustrated, and your pc is no worse for the wear...some humble advice from a fellow newcomer.

### Post by aysiu on 2006-03-22
That's the best piece of advice I've seen, and I wholeheartedly agree with it.

Too often I see new users diving straight into a clean install or dual boot without getting used to the Gnome/KDE interface, familiarizing themselves with Linux commands, or even seeing if Ubuntu will work with their hardware.

Hardware detection is the most important part about choosing a Linux distro or deciding whether or not it's worth an installation, and live CDs are wonderful for testing the waters on that.

My advice would be to use the live CD for at least two weeks before even considering a dual boot.
