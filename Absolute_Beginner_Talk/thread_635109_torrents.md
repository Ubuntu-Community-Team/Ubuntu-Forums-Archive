### Post by Ub1476 on 2007-12-08
I would suggest [Deluge]("http://deluge-torrent.org/") rather than Azureus. It's built for GTK and and also have plugin support. To say it simple: From a website you can download a .torrent file. Open the .torrent file with Deluge/Azureus (or any other bittorrentclient) and you will join a "swarm", where you download the contest of the file (say a movie) and also upload (share) what you have downloaded to others in the swarm. After you have downloaded the movie (example) you will become a "seeder". A seeder only upload to others, and it's sort of a rule to at least upload as much as you have downloaded (most bittorrent clients show something like "ratio: 1.0)".

Good luck:)

### Post by nsilva on 2007-12-08
The cool idea behind Torrent Vs P2P is that in p2p when you connect to a slow peer you are stuck with a slow transfer rate and in case they disconect you are out of luck. Instead with Torrent you download small packages from multiple sources at the same time, improving drastically your transfer rate.

I like DEluge or Qbittorrent, both are lightweight and have a bunch of features. Qbittorrent is not in the Ubuntu repositories but you can go to their website for downloading instructions
[http://qbittorrent.sourceforge.net/](http://qbittorrent.sourceforge.net/)
