### Post by NonPapa on 2007-02-07
This will close your PC **without** shutting it down. This means that your machine is not using the normal amount of electricity when in 'standby' mode, but when you press the "on button" of
your machine, you should get back to where you left your work (programmes open etc.). I would, however, always save all my work before doing this. Just in case. :)

NonPapa

### Post by mcduck on 2007-02-07
It stores all data from RAM to hard disk and then shuts the power down. Next time you boot it just reloads the data back to RAM, allowing you to continue from the point when you activated Hibernate.

Hibernate is not as fast as Suspend, but it doesn't use any battery.
