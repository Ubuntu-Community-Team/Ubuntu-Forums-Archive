### Post by adelahunty on 2008-03-28
Gnome or KDE... That, to some people I know, is a whole can of worms best not opened... ;)

They are both very large installs, use a lot of memory, and can both look very, very good indeed (so make sure you have the resources to run them!). People coming from Windows seem to grasp KDE quicker in my experience, but with a little time and sitting down at a keyboard, the user experience is rarely that different.  Political opinions aside, people generally settle on one or other for reasons they can't *entirely* explain, but at the end of the day, doesn't really make that much difference.

Since both can sit quite nicely together on the installation you choose, the easy answer is to install both, and after playing with them, decide which you like.  Or choose neither - I still prefer xfce, even though both KDE and Gnome more than fly on my desktop (hence, I use Xubuntu).  And remember, you can use KDE applications in Gnome, and vice versa - k3b is still a much better cd-burning program than the Gnome offers, so I stick with that.

Just to add to the mix, Hardy has a KDE4 option too - look for the KDE4 Kubuntu.  Many people have commented on it being too much like Vista, but again, that's a choice thing.  If you have the bandwidth and time to grab that, have a look and see what you think.  I am told that the KDE developers wanted something with a lighter memory footprint, but still looked good.  Whether this memory issue is true or not I don't know, but it certainly does look quite polished...
