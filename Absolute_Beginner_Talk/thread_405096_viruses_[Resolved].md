### Post by 3rdalbum on 2007-04-09
Less prone than *what* to viruses?

If you mean less prone than Windows, then definately, no question about it.

If you mean less prone than Mac OS X, then definately too.

If you mean less prone than other distributions, then it's debatable.

If you mean less prone than OpenBSD, then probably not.

There are almost *zero* viruses written for Linux. None of them have been as successful as even the most modest Windows virus.

Wikipedia's list of Linux viruses:

    * Adm
    * Adore
    * Bliss
    * Cheese
    * Devnull
    * Kork
    * Lapper
    * Linux/Lion
    * Mighty
    * OSF.8759
    * Ramen
    * RST [1]
    * Slapper
    * Staog

Fourteen. None of them are in the wild.

You're safe, dude. Keep your system up-to-date, and you'll probably never even get new viruses if any are written.

If you must, you can run a virus scanner to stop your machine from carrying Windows viruses, but I personally don't see the value in it unless your Linux machine is running a network of Windows machines.
