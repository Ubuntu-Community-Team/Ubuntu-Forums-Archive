### Post by jhenager on 2007-05-09
Right. The option you selected runs the Live CD. That's the first step. Then, as the poster above mentions, you will have an install icon. Double click that to put it on your hard disk.
Just be aware that the default settings will erase anything currently on your disk.
If you don't need anything, then you are good to go. Otherwise, boot into your old operating system and do a backup.
You can never have too many backups!
