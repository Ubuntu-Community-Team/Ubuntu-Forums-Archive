I'll go in reverse order

5. You should beable to scroll normally without pressing the middle mouse button, instead just roll it up and down.

4. It's probably because you have Opera set to open in a new window. If you have tabs enabled it will open a new tab. Do you see the "new tab" icon when you open another instance? I guess you could probably add a link to your homepage next to the "new tab" icon or in place of it, then that should appear in the new window at least. Clicking either should bring back up the address bar for you. Or maybe you could add a shortcut to your home and put that on the desktop (if Opera is your default browser it should open Opera with your homepage in it). ### Post by WHICKS on 2007-05-29
Don't forget you can have your tabs open in a cascading manner or in a tile manner, or not at all.  I prefer to use their new speed dial option , which loads 9 of my favourite websites in the speed dial and you choose your favourite.  I also have a large monitor, and sometimes I will be 15-20 windows open, and yes, they are all stable.  Just click on whichever one you want to maximize and away you go.  

Click on your toolbar at the top and choose customize, then buttons, then left click on "tile "and drag it to the taskbar, and do the same with the" cascade" option.  I personally like to tile them, but some prefer the cascade method.

I've been using Opera for at least 3 years now, it is very stable now, and I rarely if ever have problems.  Note the bit-torrent is loaded and you don't need a plug in for it.
