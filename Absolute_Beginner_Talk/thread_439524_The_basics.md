### Post by canadianwriterman on 2007-05-10
Well, to answer your question about Flash Player, I assume you mean the Flash plugin for Firefox? If so, Just click on Applications>Add/Remove. When the window is loaded, change the "Show" setting at the top to "all available applications." This will list everything that's available, not just programs supported by Ubuntu. Type "Flash" in the search window and you'll get the Flash plugin to install.

### Post by Madpilot on 2007-05-10
I haven't messed with Compiz/Beryl myself - owning an ATI video card will do that - but for Flash & other multimedia stuff, have a look at the [RestrictedFormats]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/RestrictedFormats") page.

RPMs are install files for the other major 'type' of Linux - they're Redhat Package Management files. The equivilent in Ubuntu would be DEB files (.deb), but using individual RPM or DEB files is not really encouraged - use the built-in package management for as much installation as possible, it keeps your system cleaner.

### Post by srt5 on 2007-05-10
First of all, welcome to linux :) if ever you need help with anything, the forum is an excellent source for help, don't be afraid to ask questions, there's always someone willing to help. I think that the best way to get to grips with Linux when your starting off is to dive straight in, you will come across barriers along the way, but thats all part of the learning process. Try not to get put off by all of the lingo that's used and complex looking videos on YouTube, it's really not that bad when you understand the basics.

If your just starting out, then make use of the Add/Remove program which is located under the Applications menu. Simply typing in "flash" in the search box should bring up the Mozilla flash plug-in. Simply tick the box and install :) It is worth noting though that as you broaden your knowledge, you will probably need to start exploring Synaptic package manager since Add/Remove does not show you all of the available packages, but it's very useful for finding all the essential packages for getting you started.

Good luck
