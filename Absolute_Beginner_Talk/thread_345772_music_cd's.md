### Post by Buck2348 on 2007-01-24
In Windows I always used MusicMatch Jukebox for ripping and burning.  I made .wav (very large--40 to 50 megabytes) files for burning to an audio CD.  However, MusicMatch was capable of using mp3 files and transparently changing them back to the proper audio format during the burn process.  You need mp3 or wma (Windows Media) or similar files for loading into a portable music player because those files are much smaller (3-4 megabytes) and enable the player to hold a lot of tunes.

But:  some cd players are now designed also to play the compressed formats like mp3.  I have 3 disks that contain about half my music collection in that form.  My stereo player (a few years old) won't even think of playing them, but I have a portable cd player--the kind about the shape and size of a pancake--which will play them.  But I believe the software that burned them had to be told that I was burning mp3 disks for playing, and not just archiving them as data files.  

So--you have to know what you want to do before you burn, and then have to tell the software correctly what that is.

I'm no expert, but I'll watch the thread for questions.  Good luck.

Buck
