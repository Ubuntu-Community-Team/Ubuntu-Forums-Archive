### Post by Dies on 2006-12-27
> **DSn0wMan said:**
> Correct me if I am wrong, but I believe the more ram you have, the more swap space you are going to need to a certain extent.

I think it should be double your amount of RAM up to 2Gigs

Yes, you're wrong. 
Typically on machines with that much RAM swap simply doesn't get used, unless you're talking about servers which I don't have much experience with so can't really comment.

To answer your question Sweetpea, yeah you're machine will run without swap, that said I still give mine a small swap space.

As far as processors a dual core is not twice as fast as a single core, and depending on the models you're comparing may be slower. The true benefit of dual core comes when you do a lot of multitasking it just handles it a lot better, in other words you can run a lot more apps at the same time with less stress on your processor but it doesn't necessarily mean they're going to run faster.
As far as 64 bit, at the moment there's not many benefits for typical users and not many apps that take advantage of it, so right now, no its not a lot faster, just more future proof.
