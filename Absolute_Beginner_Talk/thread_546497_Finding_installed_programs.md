### Post by alan_daniel on 2007-09-08
Likely what you installed was not one of the fully integrated "Ubuntu programs." There are thousands of programs available to you through the repositories, and when they install, they will not necessarily automatically add themselves to the applications menu. What you can do to run it, if you remember the name, is open up a terminal and start typing in the first few letters of the program, press TAB, and it should fill in the rest of it (or show you the possibilities left with those few keys).

To answer your question directly, yes, there is a directory that, by convention, the binaries (executable files) of programs are placed or linked to. That directory is /usr/bin or /usr/local/bin (more often the former).

### Post by tak1150 on 2007-09-08
most command line-based programs do not appear in the menu. One thing you can try is to go to the main menu editor (right click on the menu bar and go to "edit menu"), and enable (or simply check) the thing that says "debian menu."

Debian menu usually has more programs listed than the normal one.
