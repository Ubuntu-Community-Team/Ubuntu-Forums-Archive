### Post by bward1 on 2007-01-28
I take it you are planning on building this system yourself.

I have had good experiences with asus boards, and that is why I have three systems running on asus boards.

Is there any specific reason why you are looking for an amd cpu?  The new intel core 2 duos are extraordinarily fast and priced very well for their performance.  The E6300 is about $190 and I am fairly certain outperforms any amd chip in that price range.  If you can afford it, the E6600 has 4M of cache and is really a great chip (it is what I am running) but it is a little of $300.  Either of these processors CAN run the 64 bit version of ubuntu despite the fact that it is called amd64.

If you want an amd processor go for an amd x2 processor of whatever price range/performance you want.  If  you are looking for something cheap, than I think amd has more amd x2 processors in the lower price range and the boards to go with these chips are a little bit cheaper as well.

Your decision on your processor will dictate which motherboard you buy.  Avoid buying a board with a JMicron raid controller on board, that can give you headaches too.

For video cards, again it depends what price range you are looking in and what type of applications you intend to run, but I would say go with an nvidia card.  Nvidia is just so much easier when it comes to linux than ati is.

If you give a price range you are looking for and what types of applications you will be running the community may be able to give you better guidance in this issue.
