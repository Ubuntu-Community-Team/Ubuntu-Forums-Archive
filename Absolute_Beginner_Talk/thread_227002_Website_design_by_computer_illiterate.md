### Post by _simon_ on 2006-08-01
Personally I think that if you have never made a site before then you need to start with the basics i.e. pure html coding then move on to CSS.

There are many guides online and many, many books you can buy or borrow from the library.

It will take time and effort to have a decent looking site. If you plunge in head first without knowing anything then you will end up with a site that looks like it was made by an 8 year old....

For ideas on design, broswe the net, download free templates and pick them apart... ask opinions before going live etc. 

Research so that it's designed for your target market, you don't need or want fancy bells and whistles if your target audience can't use them / isn't interested.

I use Nvu for site design / coding, gedit for CSS and Gimp for graphics.
