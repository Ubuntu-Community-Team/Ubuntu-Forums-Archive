### Post by tom56 on 2007-04-24
7.04 is definitely more user-friendly than 6.06. However, it's a question of how often you want to upgrade. 6.06 will be supported with bugfixes and security fixes until June 2009 (possibly April 2009, it hasn't been announced yet), whereas 7.04 is only supported until October 2008 (so about 6-8 months less). Once support stops  you can continue to use it but you will not receive any updates. At that point it is recommended you upgrade to a supported release.

### Post by tom56 on 2007-04-24
6.06 was a LTS (long-term support release). These are supported for 3 years on the desktop (5 years on servers).
7.04 is the latest release. It was a regular release (i.e. not LTS), these are supported for 18 months.

I'm not sure where you got the 6 months from, that's just wrong.

EDIT: In terms of upgrading you'd probably be best to backup all your files to an external drive, install 7.04 (i.e. a complete reinstall), then drag your files back over. If you don't have an external drive I'd recommend you buy one - they're very useful for backups (which you ought to be doing regularly!) and are very cheap these days (£40 for 200GB at PC World a couple of weeks ago).
