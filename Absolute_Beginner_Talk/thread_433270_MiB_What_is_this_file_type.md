### Post by jfinkels on 2007-05-04
What K. Mandla said.

Traditionally, kilo- means 1,000, mega- means 1,000,000, etc. But in the computer world, things are in powers of 2, and when we say kilobytes, we might actually mean 1,024 (2 raised to the 10th power) bytes. So somebody came up with KiB and MiB, etc., for clarity.
