### Post by bulldog on 2007-02-17
The best way is to open your case and take your RAM out of the computer.
Take it with you to a local hardware shop and let them see it.
They should now what kind of RAM it is and what you need to buy.

Be very carefull wen you take out the memory,and use a little anti-static bag to put it in so the change of damaging it is reduced.

### Post by Daveth on 2007-02-17
you may find this

[http://www.crucial.com/uk/index.asp?gclid=CLH968aitYoCFR5UEAodfElaPQ](http://www.crucial.com/uk/index.asp?gclid=CLH968aitYoCFR5UEAodfElaPQ)

useful. Just enter your pc details and it should go off and find the appropriate memory. Crucual's support team are helpful. Of course, you can buy it anywhere, not just from them.

This

[http://www.helpwithpcs.com/upgrading/installing_dimm_memory_ram.htm](http://www.helpwithpcs.com/upgrading/installing_dimm_memory_ram.htm)

is one of the many how-tos online. In addition to Bulldogs correct comments about static, remember DIMMS are keyed, so only go in 1 way, and have retaining clips at the end. Push the unit in straight otherwise you will find 1 end in and the other not. There are rules about matching memory types and which dimm socket gets what but, from memory, this is quite motherboard dependent.
That aside, buy as much memory as you can afford as you can rarely have too much.
