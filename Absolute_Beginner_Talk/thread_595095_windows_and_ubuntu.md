### Post by Mondoman on 2007-10-28
No, Ubuntu will need its own partition, but you need to be very careful when installing a 2nd OS that you don't accidentally change/destroy partitions used by the first OS.
No, none of your Windows utilities will likely work in Ubuntu.  However, your graphics card, like your other hardware, should work with Ubuntu.  Right now, nVidia-based graphics cards have better driver support in Ubuntu than do ATI-based graphics cards, so there may be more issues with the latter.

### Post by dagvei on 2007-10-28
Hi prapanj,

I am running both XP and Ubuntu on the same laptop with no problems at all, BUT: first time I tried to install Ubuntu 7.04 I let Ubuntu do the partitioning, and it turned out that I had lost some files in the XP partition later, so I had to totally re-install XP again to make everything work, and thus, goodbye Ubuntu partition.
 I have a partitioning programme for Windows on my other two pc's, from German company 7Tools, that is really excellent, "Partition Magic" and I forked out $20,- or so, and downloasded it to my laptop. With this programme I split my harddisc in two, let Partition Magic re-partition my Windows, and then installed Ubuntu 7.10 on the free part of the disc.
Ubuntu worked straight-out-of-the-box, and the GRUBE start-menu lets you decide at start-up which OS to start.
