### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-21
Where is your wireless antenna located?  An ideal location is on a high shelf away from other computer equipment with the antenna straight up.  Adding a coffee can antenna can improve reception.  Poor antenna placement will cause problems.  Once set up, WiFi works well on Ubuntu.  Antenna strength is another issue.  There are several strength meters and radars available that you can install to get a better picture of what is happening.

If you have other wireless computers (windows or mac) they can also be used as signal meters to determine weak spots in the house.

The networked nature of Linux requires a reliable LAN connection including wireless.  Some cards are better than others, some wireless routers are better than others.  Pairing a cheap wireless card with a cheap router with poor antenna location will result in poor wireless performance and poor linux performance.  Using a good card with a good router and good antenna placement means you can watch steaming high-definition movie trailers in the backyard 80 feet away from the transmitter.

There is not enough information in your post to make these assessments.
