### Post by mbsullivan on 2008-02-22
Although e-mail does not have an "anonymous" mode, you can "spoof" another e-mail address, which can be real or fake. Yes, this may be possible, depending on the mail server you want to use, and yes, it has almost no valid application. The most convenient and ethical way to e-mail "anonymously" is to get a junk webmail account at gmail, hotmail, or yahoo, and just use that.

Although gmail refuses to do it for me, setting the "Reply-To" field in Thunderbird (under Edit-Account Settings) may work with some mail servers.

If you know how to code in Perl or PHP and have access to a free webpage service with a mail server, you can definitely make it happen. It basically boils down to just putting a different or bogus address in the "Reply-To" field of the outgoing e-mail. The recipient can tell what e-mail servers the message came from, but not the true e-mail address. However, they will respond to whatever e-mail address you send, so getting e-mail back from them will not be possible.
