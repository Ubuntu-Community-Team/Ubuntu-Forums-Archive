### Post by mcduck on 2007-03-13
Short answer: Just plug them in :D

For monitors and mouses you don't usually need any drivers. Monitors don't generally speaking need any drivers with computers, they aren't actually communicating with your computer any more than, for example, your speakers are. If the resolution of your new monitor is different than old one's, you need to change that of course.

For the mouse, basic things will always work without any extra work, but if the mouse has more than standard buttons you might need to do some work to get those going. But for this you shouldn't need to install any drivers either.
