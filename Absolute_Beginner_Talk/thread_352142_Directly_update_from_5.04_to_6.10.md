### Post by K.Mandla on 2007-02-02
Technically, in theory, it's supposed to work. But I've tried this and it doesn't work *very well*. 

If you would want to try it, I would **strongly** suggest *backing everything up*, and then going from 5.04 to 5.10 to 6.06 to 6.10. Things change between distros (most notably between 6.06 and 6.10), and when I did it, my installation just started to break down.

And remember, going from 5.04 to 6.10 is going to require downloading *at least *an entire new distro. So why not download the ISO, burn it, back up your data, blank the drive and start from scratch? It would save you the bandwidth over jumping from 5.04 to 5.10 to ...
