### Post by Madpilot on 2006-02-26
For WMV, you're going to need to install some codecs - follow this guide: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/RestrictedFormats)

Rhythymbox is included by default in Ubuntu, it has a bunch of ways of organizing & playing back your music - **Applications menu -> Sound & Video -> Rhythymbox Music Player.**

For mounting your Windows partition, have a look here: [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AutomaticallyMountMSWindowsPartitions](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AutomaticallyMountMSWindowsPartitions)

### Post by TeeAhr1 on 2006-02-26
Yeah, there's nothing wrong with Rhythmbox.  It's a very no-b.s. music player and that's it, but very functional.  I used it for six months and was very happy with its capabilities (search, indexing, whatnot).  Alternately, if you're looking for a Winamp feel, try XMMS.
