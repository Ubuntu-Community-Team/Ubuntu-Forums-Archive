### Post by cknight on 2007-11-01
Here's a way handy trick I picked up today, though maybe most of you know this already.  If you want to resize or move a window, you would usually drag a corner of the window or the title bar to resize/move respectively.  Try this:

Press the Alt key and left click in the middle of a window.  Hold both and move the mouse which will move the window too.

Or to resize...

Press the Alt key and right click in the window.  Hold both and move the mouse to resize the window.

I use fluxbox and resizing was a pain since the window border was so small you had to be careful and percise with your aim to grab the resize handle.  Now its so easy since you don't have to aim at all.

I've also tested this with KDE, which will give you a different resize arrow depending on where your mouse is in the window when you right-click.

Can anyone confirm if this feature is present in Gnome/Metacity or other window managers?

Hehe, I love finding features like this.

### Post by paul.matthijsse on 2007-11-01
That is indeed present in Gnome. For those who lack a mouse: move a window with Alt-F7, resize it with Alt-F8.

### Post by RedSquirrel on 2007-11-01
I use keyboard shortcuts to move windows in Fluxbox:

```
Control Mod1 KP_4       :MoveLeft 10
Control Mod1 KP_6       :MoveRight 10
Control Mod1 KP_8       :MoveUp 10
Control Mod1 KP_2       :MoveDown 10

```There are commands to resize as well. See:

[http://fluxbox-wiki.org/index.php/Keyboard_shortcuts](http://fluxbox-wiki.org/index.php/Keyboard_shortcuts)
