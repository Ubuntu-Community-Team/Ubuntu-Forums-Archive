### Post by Taboo Mirage on 2007-11-25
Frankly, there is *so* much online documentation in relation to Linux issues and Ubuntu out there.  I'd really recommend checking out online resources first and foremost before deciding to buy anything.  There are links to collections of [articles](http://scc.pinehurst.net/linux.html#linuxhowtos) in some places, as well as [sites](http://www.linuxnewbieguide.org/pdfs.php) and [pages](http://www.ctssn.com/) dedicated to educating newer users about Linux generally.

All you need to learn is an internet connection, frankly.  [Google](http://www.google.com) can provide an article or HOWTO on pretty much any topic you desire to know about also.

Of course buying a book won't do any harm, but I'm highlighting that the information you desire is fairly easily accessible purely through web searches already.  Using Linux consistently and trying out some things will help you achieve your goal.

### Post by scorp123 on 2007-11-25
> **fmbugdadi said:**
>  Anyhow, can anyone recommend a "no nonsense" book for absolute beginners??  To my apprentices I always recommend "UNIX for Dummies" ... or "Linux for Dummies". No kidding. Those books are excellent. Written in a very very funny way and they teach you some basics such as understanding some of the "UNIX lingo" us folks use (e.g. what's a shell? What's a pipe? What's "X11"? What in the world is a "block device"? ...), they teach you some of the most basic commands (e.g. chown, chmod, grep, who, finger, cat ....) and they even teach you how to use the feared-and-hated-amongst-beginners "vi" editor. Trust me. Those books are good. And as I said: funny as hell. I think having something to laugh about helps a great deal to gain confidence with this new stuff. I also think once you're finished with one of those ".... for Dummies" books (you don't need both the "UNIX for Dummies" and "Linux for Dummies" ... they are very similar, one will do) it will easier for you to understand and appreciate that other book you have now.
