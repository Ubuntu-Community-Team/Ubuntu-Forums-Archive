### Post by Cypher on 2007-04-23
Ahh..Enlightenment. It's a window manager from the gold old days of Linux. Back when window managers such as FVWM, TWM, and others were just down right fugly. Enlightenment looked brilliant, but was an absolutely CPU hog. Unfortuantely for it, it never really got mainstream attention, a lot of geeks who had the drive to configure/use it, did so, but the rest got into the GNOME/KDE bandwagon and never looked back.

I'm fairly certain that Enligthenment NEVER got out of 0.xx release number, and frankly speaking I wouldn't really bother it. KDE and GNOME are just so much better, if you want some of the funky desktop-effcts, then Compiz and Beryl will do that for you.
