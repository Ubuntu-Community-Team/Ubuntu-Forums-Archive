### Post by rsambuca on 2007-12-02
When the kernel is updated, then both the old and the new kernels will show up in the menu.  If you want you can delete the entries from your menu.lst, or remove the old kernels once you are sure the new ones are trouble free on your system.

---

### Post by Trebaruna on 2007-12-02
When there is a new kernel version it gets added to the list, but the old one does not get removed.
It is nothing to worry about, and you can remove the old kernel without problems (when you've verified the new one isn't giving you any trouble)
