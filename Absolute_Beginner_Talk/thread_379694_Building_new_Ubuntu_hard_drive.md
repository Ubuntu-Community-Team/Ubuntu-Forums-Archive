### Post by schwascore on 2007-03-08
Just from personal experience, I recommend using Nvidia based graphic cards because they have better support under Linux.

You can go with either Intel or AMD, that choice is up to you.  For the most part, they both offer high end dual core processors that are about equal in power (***Depending on what you want to do with them***) see [www.tomshardware.com](www.tomshardware.com) for benchmarks.  Select your CPU first, then you motherboard.  Your RAM type depends on what motherboard you purchase, but typically you want DDR2 800 as a base.

I have found that most on-board sound cards (built into the motherboard) are acceptable, but some have bad interference from the motherboard.  I would suggest using a simple PCI sound card instead.  I don't have any personal experience with the high end sound cards out right now but the good old $15-30 Creative cards work just fine.

Now for wireless... well, good luck.  This is the hardest component to match up with Linux because some vendors have a nasty habit of putting different chipsets in cards that share the exact same designation.  Try searching the forums for cards that are compatible.  This one will take a lot of research, but you should be able to find something.

Good luck!
