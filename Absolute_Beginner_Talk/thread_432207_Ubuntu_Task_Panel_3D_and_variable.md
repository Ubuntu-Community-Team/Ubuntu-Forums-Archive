Ok, since you are new, shall crush this dream swiftly and coldly right now, mwahahahahahhaha.

Ok, just kidding, but... In all seriousness, I wouldn't recommend installing any of the dock programs out there, none of them are bug free and many are unstable and will in many cases crash ubuntu. They all need more time to well debug and improve, maybe in a few months we will find a stable perfect version :) (Maybe Beryl will develop its own perfect compatible one , we can hope :D)

If you absolutely, MUST try to do this... here are two.

[URL="http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=385981"]http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=385981
[/URL]

EDIT: [Kiba]("http://www.kiba-dock.org/components/com_mambowiki/index.php?title=Installing_Kiba-Dock#Ubuntu")

Note: If you do that, read UbuntuGuide.org and make sure you fully understand what your doing, and have previously installed your graphics drivers and likely you will also need beryl or compiz for compositing, so read up to get to there. Have to do your homework to get to the cake :) I don't take responsibility for you crashing your computer with these.

In the end though, even if the cool look takes you in, it really just eats up CPU cycles. For now, right click on your top panel and add to panel, then add any launcher you want.
