### Post by jordilin on 2006-10-28
You have several options. If you have your /home/username in its own partition, you can do a clean install without formatting your home partition. That's what I always do. It will do a clean install without touching your files. I've heard that many people are having problems with apt-get dist-upgrade, so you have to choose. In my case, I've always done the first option pointed out. Clean install, but without touching my home directory. Another possibility is having your /home in another external hard drive, installing the whole new distro and then transferring your personal files again to your /home directory. It's all about decissions. If you don't want problems though, go with a clean install.
