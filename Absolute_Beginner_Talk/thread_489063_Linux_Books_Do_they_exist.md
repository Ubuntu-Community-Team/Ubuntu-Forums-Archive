### Post by chrome saint on 2007-06-30
I started by reading "Moving to Ubuntu Linux" by Marcel Gagne and found his style suited me well.  He covers the basics and has a conversational writing style so the book doesn't read like a tech manual.  He eases you into basic shell commands and provides a lot of examples, with screenshots, that you walk through as you read.

The book is around 450 pages long and I read every page and followed along with the examples up to around page 220.  At that point he goes into detail sections about using open office apps, gimp, irc, etc so I only skimmed what was of interest of me from there on.

### Post by Alterax on 2007-07-01
Try the Linux+ study guides (certification but good broad cover of topics), the Ubuntu book (yes.  That is what it is called), and Unix Power Tools by O'Reilly press were what I used to get started.  The first and last of those can get pretty in-depth, but that info has come in handy.  The ubuntu book is newbie-ish, but we all have to start somewhere and that might just be what you are looking for.

Are there plenty of books avaiable?  Yes.  Doing a query on my home library has come up with 52 titles in-stock, so yeah, there are plenty.  Look for stuff on Unix too, since Linux is based on it.

Happy reading!
