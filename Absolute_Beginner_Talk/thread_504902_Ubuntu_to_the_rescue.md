### Post by jerryduk on 2007-07-19
Last week my windoze system broke (again).
Blue screen, no boot, couldn't even see the hard drive.

I ran chkdsk /r but it still didn't boot.
Rather than spend a week searching the net, restoring files then eventually giving up and clean installing windoze (again), I decided to cut my losses, attempt to recover my data and re-install windoze (as I'm not quite ready to make that leap (forward) to Linux.

In went the Ubunto Feisty Fawn (nice names) CD and up came the desktop. I use an Acer laptop and it found the inbuilt wireless, external keyboard, external mouse etc.. I could see the hard drive and my external backup drive. Firstly I tried to copy Settings and Documents to the external drive. This failed because I had previously formatted the drive to NTFS to overcome the 4 GIG limit in FAT32 and I don't think Ubuntu likes NTFS without an additional application. I then went and stole my wife's 40GIG external drive which is formatted FAT32. That worked like a dream and I copied all the Settings and Docs to the small external drive.

I then re-installed windoze (from my HardDrive image files) using the Acer restore disks. Then did all the updates including SP2 (what a bore it took hours). Then I booted Ubuntu once more off the CD and copied all the Settings and Docs back onto the Hard Drive. Hey Presto when I booted to windoze my Outlook Express, Outlook, Desktop etc. files were all there.

It's just brill.

Can't wait to wean myself of windoze altogether. Shame wine isn't a little better as I have three apps I can't get away from yet.

Hope this helps someone else with windoze blues.
