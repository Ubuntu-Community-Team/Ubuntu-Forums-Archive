### Post by irish_flu on 2007-02-12
How does your Camera hook up to the computer?  Is there a USB or Firewire connection on the camera, or do you plan to use the "TV-in" feature on the machine?

The computer you're looking at looks pretty heavy-duty in every way except one: the video card RAM.  64 MB isn't much, these days.

If I were looking for something like that, I'd look for something with 128MB or 256MB of RAM on the video card, and I'd probably look for a dual-core processor as well.

To be honest, in my opinion that computer seems a little expensive (mostly because of the video card and the processor).  Otherwise, it seems like a nice computer.

I'm certainly no expert (or even a novice) at DVD building in Linux, so I won't speculate on that; however, I would think your first problem might be whether or not the NVidia driver for Linux supports everything you'll need in order to capture the video.

If it were me (and this is ONLY an opinion) if you really want to buy that computer, you're paying for Windows Media Center anyway.  You might as well use it, even if you only boot into that when you want to make your DVDs and use Ubuntu the rest of the time (it looks like that computer has 2 250GB drives; you could leave Windows on the first one and put Ubuntu on the other).
