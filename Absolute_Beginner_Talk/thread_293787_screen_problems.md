### Post by LLRNR on 2006-11-05
Hi wolfie-uk and a warm welcome ! I understand you perfectly, it must be really annoying to have 0 Hz refresh rate... 

Ok, the first thing to do would be to go in the System menu -> Preferences -> Screen Resolution and see if you can fix it from there.

The second thing you can try if this fails is to go to Applications -> Add/Remove... (you will probably be prompted for the root password), then you might have to click on the "Reload" button. From here you can easily add and remove applications and for your screen resolution issue you should check the "Show unsupported applications" box, then click (on the left) on "Accessories", and then, in the list in the right, you should scroll down to the "Resolution Switcher" utility, check its box and then click Apply.

Next, you will find your newly installed utility under the Applications menu -> Applications -> Resolution Switcher. When you click on it, you'll notice a small box in the upper-right corner of your screen, near the red Power button. That's the resolution switcher, click on it and then choose "Configure Display Settings". 

Hope that was helpful !

LLRNR
