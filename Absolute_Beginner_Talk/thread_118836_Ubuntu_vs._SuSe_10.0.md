### Post by matthew on 2006-01-17
There are more similarities than there are differences between the two. What makes Ubuntu stand out is that it

- tends to have slightly newer packages for software (but not TONS newer, just a version or two generally)

- it is committed to being free, like in liberty as well as no-cost. Suse is a for-profit enterprise. Neither is necessarily better--Suse is great for those who want to have the audiovisual codecs installed automatically, for example to play mp3's and dvd's out of the box

- the kicker for me is that these forums are unsurpassed in helpfulness, friendliness and general, all-around niceness. The Ubuntu philosophy involves more than technology, but also treating people well and valuing everyone just because they are human. That's enough for me. Even if Ubuntu were a lot more difficult or didn't have the other advantages I see I would likely stay with it just because of this commitment.

### Post by tufkakf on 2006-01-18
As not surprisingly people here seem to prefer ubuntu, let me add some advantages of Suse (all imho of course):

1. Yast. Not perfect, but let's you configure about everything without ever dropping down to a terminal, which is great for new users.
2. Newer packages. Though backports is great, you'll definately have more luck finding up to date packages for a stable Suse release.
3. For all the people complaining they didn't find enough packages, check out:
[http://www.opensuse.org/Package_Repositories](http://www.opensuse.org/Package_Repositories)
4. Yast does dependencies resolution quite well, though people don't seem to be aware of it. But if you want to, you can also use apt and synaptic with Suse, which works great btw.
5. Suse offers things that are clearly missing from ubuntu. For example, an easy graphical way to set up your dsl connection and let it start on demand.
