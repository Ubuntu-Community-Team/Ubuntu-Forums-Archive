### Post by dancallo on 2006-12-28
Sage,

I would go back and run the LiveCD to test out the video compatibility in the HP.  If that tests okay, then you shouldn't have problems with the install.  If it fails, then it is either the fact that the video card in your HP Pavilion model is incompatible with Ubuntu 6.10 or the CD is corrupt.

Did you obtain the CD from the downloadable ISO file?  Did you run an MD5 Checksum against the CD after burning it to determine if it is a good image?  I always do that before installing software, especialy an OS, from an Internet download.  I use UltraISO to accomplish this.  Great software product.

I have recently installed Ubuntu 6.0.06.1 LTS on my HP Pavilion 8485Z, 450 MHz, P3, and didn't have any video issues.  Although, I do have sound card issues since I have a very unique Rockwell Riptide Audio/Comm combo card that isn't supported.  HP is terrible for supplying drivers for their products, especially for the Linux OSes.
