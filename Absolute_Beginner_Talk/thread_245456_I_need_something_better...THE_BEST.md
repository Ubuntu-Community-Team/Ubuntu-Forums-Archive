### Post by The Noble on 2006-08-27
It all depends really. You need to look up what will work best for YOU. Many that are good don't work with linux, some have a high chance of crapping out, some are draft N and may not be the best in the long run. Look on newegg for good priced cards (whether they are pcmcia or usb) and if they are rated well, see if they work in linux easily. There is not just THE BEST, but more like a bunch that may or may not work. It all depends on what you are trying to do with them, and if they work on your computer.

Many of us will recommend good cards, but you need to be more specific in preferences. Do you like Linksys? Netgear? Buffalo? Do you want range or speed or both? Do you have a laptop with pcmcia slots or a desktop with pci slots? Clear it up and we will help.
