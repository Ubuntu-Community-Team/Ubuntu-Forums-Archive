### Post by priegog on 2008-02-21
Oh, ok, I know exactly what you mean now. 
The frist thing you do is remove that pidgin icon you created, as that is not what you want. What you created is called an application launcher, and it works only to launch applications. What you need to do is right click on the upper panel, close to the date and time where it originally was and click on "Add to panel". Then on the window that appears scroll down to the last section called "Utilities" and from there selec "Notification area", and click on the Add button. Nothing will show up, but when you start pidgin the pidgin icon should show up there and behave like you want it to. That is also commomly known as "system tray" under windows, and is a place not only for pidgin, but for other applications as well to place icons and allow them to be running while not being shown on the Desktop. 
hope that helps.
