### Post by qpieus on 2006-09-24
I've read a few. My library purchased several on my suggestion :) 

Beginning Ubuntu Linux: From Novice to Professional: OK for total beginners. For people that know a little about ubuntu, too much time is wasted on installing walkthrough and explaining several apps like OO, gimp, email. I didn't learn a lot from this book, you can easily learn more by reading these forums.

The Official Ubuntu Book. Browsed through it at Barnes and Noble. Looks OK. Had lots of beginner stuff, but also some more meat than the first book I mentioned. I didn't like it enought to buy it though.

Moving to Ubuntu Linux: Reading this now. Similar to Beginning Ubuntu Linux. A lot of beginner info and explanations of apps. Would not buy.

Ubuntu Hacks: I read this book via the library. I liked it a lot. It's basically 100 "tips and tricks". Go to Amazon and one of the reader reviews listed out all the tips, I'm not gonna repeat them here. I've installed several of the tricks they described. I liked this enough that I bought it. Honestly, there's nothing in the book you can't find here on the forums or elsewhere on the web, but I like having this book around.

The other posters here are right though, you can find any info that these books have right here on the web.

Do what I did and make suggestions to your library. Mine has never turned me down, I get to read these books before buying, and the books will be there to lure potential ubuntu users!
