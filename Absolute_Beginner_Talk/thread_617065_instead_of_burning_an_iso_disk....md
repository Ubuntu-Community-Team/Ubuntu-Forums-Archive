### Post by shadowbw on 2007-11-19
Being able to boot from a jump drive is dependent on whether your motherboard supports it.  Older machines don't have this.......even some newer machines (Dell proprietary types and etc.) still don't include this feature.  You'll just have to research the board.  Also....to find out more about your computer (Dell).....go to dell.com and type in your service number or the model number of your computer (service number will give you exact details about your computer configuration, while model number will only list available options with your computer configuration.) and you will be presented with all the information about your computer hardware setup.
