### Post by mivo on 2007-09-30
Well, the 7950GT is still more powerful than the 8600GTS (see [here]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_NVIDIA_Graphics_Processing_Units")), for basically the same money. If I were to buy a card with DX10 or OpenGL 3.0 in mind, I would probably get an 8800 now, but they do cost more, and it seems that many Windows action gamers are not very happy with the DX10 support of the 8er series (many recommendations to wait for the next generation). It'll be some time before DX10 games for Windows will run acceptably in Wine (most DX9 games don't work properly).

In the end it is a matter of how much someone wants to spend on the card, and what they intend to use it for, though. Someone who just does typical desktop stuff, minus gaming, doesn't need a 8800 or a 7950.:) He mentioned the 8600GTS, so he seems to want a high-end card -- I feel  the 7950 would fit the bill better than the 8600GTS right now. If money isn't a concern, the 8800 Ultra is the card to get.
