### Post by bluepowder7 on 2007-11-20
i was just about to mention the psychocats page.  i found it a week ago, well after installing ubuntu on my machines, and there's definitely some good points to honestly consider there.

for the most part, it seems that 7.10 is fairly straightforward to set up if you don't have obscure or exotic or hand-made hardware.

one thing about the Live CD - yes it will give you a taste of ubuntu, but be sure to ignore some of its limitations.  it'll be slow since it's running from a CD, it might not be able to set up all the drivers for your monitor and printer and whatnot (a full install solves that 95% of the time), and it might not let you play with stuff that's already installed on your hard drives.  all of that disappears once you actually do a regular install, though.

what you might consider doing is getting your hands on a small used IDE hard drive, a 10Gig or 20Gig, and using that to install Ubuntu on.  obviously, remove or at least unplug your existing hard drive that has Windows on it, so that your Win is 1000% safe from whatever you do on this "new" hard drive.  this will make switching between Win and Ubu a bit of a pain since you'd swap hard drives, but it's very very safe from an "i don't wanna lose any of my files and applications" point of view.  if you don't wanna use windows anymore, then just don't plug in that other drive and keep on living Linux.  if you decide Linux isn't for you, put your original drive back in and you're back to where you were originally.
