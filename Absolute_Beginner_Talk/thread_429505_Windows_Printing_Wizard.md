You can certainly do what I think you are saying in Inkscape Vector Illustrator.  Open new document - import image, resize using mouse on corner grab arrows (holding down the control key will keep the proportions constant).  Once you have the right size, pressing control+d will duplicate the image (won't be obvious until you try to move it) drag the copy next to original and so on until you have say 4 or 5 across the page. Marquee all your images.  Then go to Object > Align and distribute.  Align the tops and space them equidistant.  While still aligned if you control+d you will now copy all 4 or 5 objects. If you then move these five below the original five you will have ten copies then 15 (or you can marquee the 10 and get 20 with one ctrl+d, and so on until you have as many as you need.  

It's not quite as quick as MS but a lot more configurable - also the program can do tons more if you care to learn it.  I haven't looked into a neat way to get the images aligned properly down the page (very easy to do in Corel Draw - which is the inkscape equivalent in MS) but no doubt it is there (I just don't have time to look for it).  

It took a lot longer to type this than to do what was required.  Lol.
