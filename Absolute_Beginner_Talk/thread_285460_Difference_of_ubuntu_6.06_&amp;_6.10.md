### Post by blendmaster on 2006-10-26
The difference is that Edgy has a bunch of updated software, while Dapper remains with software that has been thoroughly tested for a while.

Like Firefox, it is updated to 2.0 in Edgy, while we still have 1.5 in Dapper.

The point is, is that Edgy will have programs that are stable, yet not completely tested for bugs, while Dapper will only ("sort of...") have versions of software that have been tested for a while.

That, and new x-server stuff in Edgy, adding coolness. :)

### Post by crimesaucer on 2006-10-26
For me, the difference is, that as a beginner and a "newb" to xubuntu/ubuntu/linux, I had only messed around for about a weeks on Dapper and then decided to go ahead and start installing the Edgy betas to learn, and now, todays version. And it has been a learning process.

By jumping in to the "beta" version and now the newer, edgier, "Edgy", I have speed up the process of needing to learn xubuntu/linux because of the need to fix the errors by reading the forums. So instead of simply installing automatix2 or easy ubuntu and treating xubuntu like my Xp, I have learned a few things really quick by having to trouble shoot some things and spend a little more effort in fixing things. Sometimes I had to do terminal procedures in ctrl/alt/F1 with no gui just to get pass an error with the upgrades.

Now, I don't know what the ubuntu 6.10 edgy looks like and feels like, but a good running version of the xubuntu 6.10 is really fast and has a smoother graphic look to it. The Firefox 2.0 is nice, (but the tabs aren't as good as tab mix plus was in 1.5) and the icon themes are really nice for the installed xubuntu themes. 

To me it seems faster than 6.06, which was already faster than any other OS I've tried, and I'm on a notebook using a celeron M chip.

My media player xine-ui played better on 6.10, than on 6.06 and until I tried to install Beryl, my system was running perfectly.

But I still have a dual boot of Windows Xp as my main OS with all of my data on it, so I don't mind messing around with the forums on xubuntu, asking question, and trying to learn terminal and proper xubuntu procedure, and if things get to ugly, I'll just reinstall and try again.

That's why I'm on edgy, and I'm a total "newb" to ubuntu/xubuntu, so don't listen to me, listen to the forum posts with the most latte cups brewing.

Plus xfce 4.4 is really fast.

Also, I don't know if it's like this for other packages/programs, but my virtual planetarium "Stellarium" got way better in Edgy in comparison to Dapper. Like now it has world wide city locations plotted on the map.
