Actually, that's not so bad. It may be bits/bytes confusion. Browsers measure download speeds in terms of bytes, where service providers use bits. Which is slightly annoying.

So a 512 megaBIT dsl connection will push out 64begaBYTES per second. A 56k dialup connection pushes a little under 7kilobytes per second. (is your download speed in THAT range??)  So that 68k is par for DSL.



It may also be worthwhile to chat with your ISP. Just like a phone connection, dsl lines can be affected by whether. So you may get poor connections on days when the soil is wet (and there's a problem with the line).  Call them up, next time you have bandwidth issues.
