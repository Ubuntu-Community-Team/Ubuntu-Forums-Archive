### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-06-20
i don't understand what you're saying, just open mplayer on the screen you want it to open on? if you're using 2 seperate screens configured thru nvidia-settings, then you should have a panel at the top of both of your screens, then simply use the Programs pull down, chose mplayer and it'll open in the screen you want it to.

As far envoking a command and having it open on a certain screen, sure that's this:

DISPLAY=:0.1 mplayer bla.avi

that will start mplayer on your 2nd display or whatever that is mapped to in your xorg.conf and it will play the .avi file called bla. Here's the link I found it on:
[http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Separate_x-screens_on_Monitor_and_TV](http://gentoo-wiki.com/HOWTO_Separate_x-screens_on_Monitor_and_TV)
