### Post by eXisor on 2006-08-04
Do you have an onboard graphics card? If so you need to go into your bios and switch it off. Or, if your system supports an agp card your bios may be set to boot from agp. Change this to boot video from pci.
If it is none of the above, check the card is firmly slotted, that the cables are securely plugged and try to switch off and on a few times. NB: Wait at least 12 seconds between switching off and then on again.. The chips need to dissipate their electrical charge.
I have a system which an old box which sometimes stubbornly refuse to activate the monitor. Letting it almost boot, and then resetting, gets it going after a few tries. I fear something is dying with this machine, maybe monitor, maybe video card, but at the moment this does the trick.
