### Post by Kosimo on 2007-07-29
Is a matter of choice.. 

KDE and Gnome are both great Desktop UI.  

Gnome is more simple clear and customizable  (in my opinion).
KDE is a bit more familiar for Windows Users and some of the better programs in Linux use KDE libraries (K3B, Amarok, ecc...) But You can use KDE programs in Gnome and vice versa. 

You can even have both in the same distribution. You can install KDE on your Ubuntu and then choose witch one you want to use from the login screen.

So, if you really don't know yet witch one you want to use I recommend to install both, have a look and use it. Then you'll decide witch one is the right one depending in your preferences.

By the way I'm using Gnome with a vertical bar located on the left side. (I have a wide screen).
