It takes me back to my DOS days.  I'm pretty rubbish at it as yet but I'm trying!  Windows conditions you to work a certain way, it's difficult to break out of that. It may appear lie that, but there are plenty of windows geeks out there that can't do anything with Linux, despite in depth knowledge and years of professional experience.  It simply does things differently.  Look at it this way.  I often post on techspot and have to tell people to go to start -> settings -> control panel -> etc -> etc -> ad nauseum.  With the command line you can just give someone a command such as sudo apt-get install k3b or the like, and it works 99% of the time.  It's a lot better.  For some things the GUI is the way to go, for others it's not.  I still think you need both.  Also if your xorg or your graphics card driver breaks in Linux, though it may be frustrating, you can still get to the command Line and edit configuration files and have a go at fixing it, with Windows you'll probably end up repair installing, messing about with the *extremely* limited recovery console or reformatting and reinstalling the whole thing.

Also the moaners that you get on here and on other Linux boards, posting stuff such as "why can't Linux be like windows" or "with windows I double click and the driver installs".  They may have a point sometimes, but generally they tend to be windows professionals that simply *hate* becoming newbies again.  so what do they do?  They try out Linux, realise that they can't do it, and that they've been using a very restricted and basic OS then head for the nearest board to rubbish it.  We all get annoyed from time to time, I've lost count of how many times I've "given up", but if you stick with it, it's much more rewarding than going back to windows.  It puts you in control of the OS and you learn something worth learning.
