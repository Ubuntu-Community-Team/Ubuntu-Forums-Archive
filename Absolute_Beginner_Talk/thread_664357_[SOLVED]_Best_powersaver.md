### Post by Niniel on 2008-01-11
I wouldn't be too concerned about the power management shredding your hd, especially since you got a new unit. Just set it up so that it won't shut off the hd every 5 minutes. 
Powersaving may be a problem though with regards to graphics cards. I've read about problems with computers waking up from Suspend mode, or rather, not waking up, and from Hibernation. 
I've personally experienced a situation with one of my computers (desktop) where one of the screensavers wouldn't let me back in and I had to do a hard reset.
My laptop though regularly goes to sleep and I haven't had any problem at all yet. :)

### Post by Niniel on 2008-01-12
Yes, I do. :)

I rank this... myth right up there with the one that made the rounds in Windoze 3.x times about how it would be better to never switch off the PC so that the drives wouldn't spin down/up because that might damage them (sound familiar?). Or how you shouldn't switch off fluorescent light tubes because starting them drains soooo much power (as it turns out, if they are off more than 20 seconds you start saving power).
Please keep in mind that the biggest power drain in laptop remains the LCD screen. So you could set that a bit more aggressively (don't use a screensaver either, just have it switch to "blank" after a while), and keep the drives powered for longer so that they'll only go into save mode when you really are away for a while, and not just gone to grab a drink or something.
