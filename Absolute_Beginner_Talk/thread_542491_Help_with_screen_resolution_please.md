### Post by VraiChevalier on 2007-09-04
[SIZE="5"][COLOR="Blue"]Solved[/COLOR][/SIZE]

Ok! I finally solved this little conundrum! You won't believe how simple and obvious it was (well, yeah, dealing with gnewbs you probably will)

I examined nowshining's screen shot of the configuration editor and compared it to mine.  I had previously forgotten to click the "apply" button after selecting the "make default....." check box in the Screen Resolution Preferences dialog box which is why I had no entry for "screen". Fixed that flub up. I changed the setting there to 1024x768 but to no avail. 

Scratch head.

I tried deleting the higher resolution settings in xorg.conf. No go.

So I went back to the posts in this thread and reexamined them all. Upon closer scrutiny I noticed something in the "Section Monitor" post by mikewhatever:
Section "Monitor"
Identifier "Generic Monitor"
Option "DPMS"
[COLOR="Red"]Horizsync [/COLOR]28-64
[COLOR="Red"]Vertrefresh[/COLOR] 43-60
EndSection

I had been entering this by typing HorizSync, VertRefresh. Capitalizing sync & refresh. So I typed the refresh rates in correctly and restarted and [COLOR="Green"]Presto Whammo[/COLOR] it works! 

Such a simple and obvious error.

### Post by skyjacker on 2007-09-05
Here are instructions i received from this forum. Don't remember who from, but it worked for me.

"If you want to fix the res, open a terminal window (applications -> accessories -> terminal) and type "gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf" (note, this is case sensitive). Scroll down to a section called "screen" and there should be a place to add your resolutions there. Save when you are done, then hit ctrl + alt + backspc to restart the graphics server and when it comes back your res should be fixed."

Good Luck
