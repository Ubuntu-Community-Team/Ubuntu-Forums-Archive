### Post by kyphi on 2007-10-01
Every time that you upgrade to a new kernel it is added to the list in your opening grub menu because, by default 'all' kernels are displayed.  The reason for that is that should one or more of your attached pieces of hardware malfunction with the new kernel, you can reboot and choose another kernel (the one that worked previously).

There is a way to alter that but at the moment, until you are more familiar with Ubuntu, I would recommend that you leave it as it is.  Kernels do not come out all that often.

I hope that you enjoy using Ubuntu.  I think it is great.
