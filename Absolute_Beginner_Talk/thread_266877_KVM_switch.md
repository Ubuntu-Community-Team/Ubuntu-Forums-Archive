### Post by pseudonym on 2006-09-29
For what it's worth, the model KVM I've used is a D-Link DKVM-2K . I'ts a 2-port model equipped for PS/2 and VGA .  It's worked perfectly for me, between machines running linux and windows, or both linux. Shielded cables make sure there is no loss of video quality. You can use the keyboard or a button on the switch itself to change computers. Makes a slightly annoying 'beep beep' when you switch screens, but if you keep the switch-box near the floor, it's not really a problem.

### Post by senorian on 2006-10-01
One of the things that I learnt reading the Amazon reviews is that unplugging a cable and then plugging it back in can make the KVM work; sometimes.
It worked for my scrolling problem; I just unplugged the mouse from the KVM and then replugged it. BINGO, I have scrolling.
Thanks to all who have supplied info about particular KVMS that work.

 I am keeping this Cables Unlimited unit even though the audio does not work because it would seem from the reviews at Amazon that any Kvm under about 100 dollars involves the luck of the draw.
One reviewer suggested that the more expensive KVMs use a seperate power supply and do not rely on the usb or ps/2 to supply enough power.
