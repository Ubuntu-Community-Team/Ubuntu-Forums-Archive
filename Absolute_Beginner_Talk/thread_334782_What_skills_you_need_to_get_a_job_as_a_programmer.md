I think the original question specified what skills **beside** java. The sheer number of times the 'Java is the best post' has come up here is tiresome! Its been said once, thats enough! Yes, its a good language, but not everyone programs in Java, not everyone uses Java for everything, and its important to know that. If you go to do a job and the only tool you know how to use is the fretsaw then yeah, you have a good tool, and yeah its a finesse tool - but you cant use that for everything, and sure as anything there will be people out there who could use a different tool for the job - and if the job is dictated by your employer, then the odds are you need to be using the tools you're asked/handed to use. Repetition doesnt help anyone, theres a lot of useful things in here, but I think a few important ones stand out:

1/ Get a degree and some education (vital for just about any job now)
2/ Get good at working to deadlines, and being able to think on your feet (vital skills for everyone)
3/ Make sure you're flexible. You need to be able to solve problems from any number of angles, one approach is not always the best, or not necessarily the easiest. (Like using a fretsaw to cut a log)
4/ Get experience - always important, second maybe only to (and on a par with) education. Without experience your likelihood of getting jobs ahead of people with years of experience is less likely.
5/ Set yourself challenges - work hard, as that means you'll get more experience faster, challenge yourself to learn, adapt to change and work with it - things always change, languages come and go - but without adaption you end up as useless as anything which is superseded and consequently unused.
6/ To be a good laugh - noone likes to work with someone who is entirely locked up in their work - have a life beyond just work and that way you'll continue to enjoy it :)

Have fun!

1. Degree. I know it's not really a skill and may not even be needed for the actual work, but nowadays they do like hiring people with some sort of post-secondary education.
