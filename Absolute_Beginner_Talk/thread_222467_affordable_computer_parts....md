### Post by Dr. Nick on 2006-07-25
If you have a bit of money that you are able to tie up in rebates then you could just go get the 300-400$ computers you see every week at places like Best Buy, Office Dpeot etc. the only problem with that is you have to be persistent with the rebates on occasion, and it can be a "large" intital investemnet up front, even though you will get alot of it back.

I built the computer I am on for <500 or so over the course of a few years, had another computer that I had upgraded, then bought a few parts and combined then. Here is a little price breakdown

70$ for case and power supply (2-3 yrs ago) from best buy
150$ for a ECS socket 754 mobo and a AMD 64 3000 from fry's
70$ for a Western Digital HD after 30-50$ in rebates about 5 yrs back from Best Buy

1 Gig of pc3200 ram for 100$ got 2 (512) at diferent times, both 30-50$ after 30-50$ in rebates

for a video card check newegg.com or zipzoomfly.com as they usually have some decent nvidias for 30-70$ without a rebate


If you have a little time their is usually a good deal after rebates at most local stores for items like cd/hd/ram etc.. But usually they are bad deals if their are no rebates involved, in that case check newegg or zipzoomfly for the parts instead
