### Post by psyopper on 2007-05-28
I'm not sure but I don't think the 3/4/4500 cards had per pixel shading engines. I'm pretty sure (about 99%) that Compiz (Ubuntu's "desktop effects") and Beryl require per-pixel shading.

And now I must reminisce about the good old days of 3dfx. I once had a pair of Diamond Multimedia 32 meg VooDoo-II's in SLI back in the Quake II days. I remember paying something like $70 each for them and that was cheap because they were refurbished.

I also remember 3dfx creating the Voodoo-3 4/4500 that ran on AGP 4x and required direct power from the PS. I also remember the whole world laughing at them because they couldn't manage power consumption off the 1.5v AGP line. It's what eventually led them into bankruptcy due to really poor sales. 

You know what else I remember? nVidia's next card after they purchased 3dfx required direct power because they couldn't manage power consumption from the 1.5v AGP line... I believe that was the 5500 but I may be mistaken. 

Even funnier is nVidia's current line of "SLI" cards based loosely on the technology they purchased from 3dfx. They predicted 3-5 years before they could incorporate the technology into their systems and they were right on 5. What's really funny is that SLI'd nVidia cards don't actually double throughput to the monitor. 3dfx's SLI cards did.

And here I sit typing this on a machine with a GeForce 6200 to support my lame Athlon 3200 w/ AGP 4x and 512 megs of PC2700.
