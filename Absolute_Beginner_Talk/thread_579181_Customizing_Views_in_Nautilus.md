### Post by clive5 on 2007-10-23
I dont think you can as i have searched far and wide for the same thing. Sadly gnome and its apps are not very customizable. What you can do is download an alternate window manager called thunar. You can find it under synaptic. Just search the forum on how to make it your default window manager. You could also try thunar in its native environment on xubuntu which uses the xfce environment or you could try kubuntu which has the kde enviroment. Once again just go to synaptic and search for the xubuntu or kubuntu desktops. Great thing is you can have multiple environments at once and just chose which one you want to boot into by chosing your session on the logon screen.
