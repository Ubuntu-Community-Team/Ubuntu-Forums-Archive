### Post by citizenofnowhere on 2007-02-12
There's no reason someone can't write an extension to say beryl to trigger a sound on particular events, but its generally up to the desktop manager. KDE has a very extensive sound management system - in fact if you're looking to customize the heck out of your machine KDE is a good option. But by default it's not very snazzy at all so you have your work cut out for you. It's also a little on the heavier side, especially Kubuntu - not very sure why.
