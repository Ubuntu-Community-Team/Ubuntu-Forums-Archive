### Post by Alter Ego on 2007-08-24
I have never been a fan of dual boot in all it forms for various reasons. What I do is use removable drives. The removable IDE drive bays with trays can be had for less than $10 US dollars and with the cheap prices of hard drives these days it is easily affordable. Of course I haven’t seen anything removable for SATA drives so IDE is the only option as far as I know. I have 3 different drives for the 3 different O/Ss that I use (Linux, XP and Vista). In around five seconds I can be booting to a different o/s on its own physical drive. I have a permanently mounted sata drive in my system that is used for data and can be accessed by all 3 operating systems. Dual boot and virtualization has its merits but I wanted to present in my opinion a simpler “0 configure” alternative.
