### Post by Robynsveil on 2007-04-01
I think a lot depends on how long you've used Windows and how entrenched you are - Ubuntu has made me realize just how Windows-thinking I'd become. I cut my teeth on DOS back in '86, and my first GUI was Windows 3.1 on a 385DX with 8meg of RAM, and 128 meg hard drive... so I've been doing Windows for awhile. The Linux command-line is not a problem - kinda reminds me of the DOS days and batch files, and I do admit I think it's nice to have that control back.

If you feel, though, that the GUI *is* the computer, you might find Linux (even Ubuntu) frustrating. The best analogy I can come up with is: since you know and feel comfortable with 3DSMax 8 (so do I), you are most productive doing modeling (and rendering?) in that program. If you've tried Blender, you might have found Blender's *interface* thoroughly irritating. At first, it is, I agree... but it grows on you. I am quite comfortable with Blender, and I find myself infinitely *more* productive in it than I ever was in 3DSMax. Blender was one of the main reasons I decided to move to Linux... it runs *SO* much faster! - and the fact that I don't have to worry about shutting down my system every day, or that it might freeze if I do a long render, or viruses... the list goes on. I feel a lot more comfortable in Ubuntu now than when I first started, so I guess it's a matter of what you get used to.

However, there *is* a learning curve. You might not be productive straight-away - I wasn't. The hard-drive space requirements aren't too great - so why don't you make your system dual-boot Win/Linux - there are some great threads on here to tell you how - and have a play. You might find you like it for certain things, say, final rendering, whilst you could do your modeling (in 3DSMax) in Windows. I reckon that - with a bit of patience - you will find your productivity steadily increase in Linux and frustration with Windows increase at about the same rate.
