### Post by teet on 2006-08-16
A lot of the new machines I have seen do not come with a "recovery cd" or for that matter a windows xp install disk.  The "recovery cd" would allow you to format your harddrive and put windows on fresh just like when you bought it.

Instead of the actual, physical cd, a lot of manufacturers are creating a 5 or 10 gb recovery partition.  This partition serves the same purpose as a recovery cd, but it can make it hard to repartion things to install linux or another OS AND it's wasting space on your hard drive.  If you have the option, choose to pay a little more (usually $10 or something) and get a recovery cd instead of the recovery partition.

Also, as others have stated, STEER CLEAR OF ATI VIDEO CARDS.  Nvidia video cards "just work" with linux...I'm not saying ATI cards CAN'T work, they just generally require a bit more effort.

-teet
