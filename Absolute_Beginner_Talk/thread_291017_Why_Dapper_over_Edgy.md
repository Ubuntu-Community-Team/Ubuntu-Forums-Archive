### Post by UltraMathMan on 2006-11-01
Dapper is an LTS release and is generally considered to be more stable than Edgy. A search of the forums will reveal many users who have had or are having problems with Edgy (and Dapper); As I recall, Edgy is a "testing version" of Ubuntu, that is, it contains some beta and alpha software. 

However, the single overriding reason to stay with Dapper, at least for the time being, is that Edgy is a newly released version and as such it is bound to have some level of bugs and problems, and as a new version it *may* be more difficult to get help on more specialized problems as those around who fool around and find fixes for these problems simply have not had as much time to see what works and what doesn't. 

Commonly dispersed wisdom for those considering upgrading from Dapper, "do you have time to mess with the system if something goes wrong or doesn't work and are you willing to do so? Are you willing to risk having to reinstall your working Dapper system from scratch in the worst case scenario?" If not then you may want to reconsider making the move now.
