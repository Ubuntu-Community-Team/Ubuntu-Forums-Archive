### Post by Officer Dibble on 2007-09-13

Okay, so you've had some problems with your financing software, which seems to have sorted themselves out - that's good.

A NIC is a Network Interface Card... your network card... now then... are you connected by a network cable running between your computer and the router/modem or are you using wireless? (no wires in your network)

You seem to have been installing a few applications that affect your network, possibly applications that do a similar job - I wonder if you have conflicts there.

As you have been making so many random changes, it might make it difficult to back-track or undo them. How do you feel about re-installing Ubuntu and then ask for advice before making any big changes or when you come across things you don't understand? Only until you gain more experience?

There might be a very simple fix to your problem, but the way you are explaining the matter is too convoluted. Try to be a specific, to the point as possible - try to avoid talking about things that don't have anything to do with the problem you would like to have solved.

Before we all start, have you checked all of your connections? Have you contacted your Internet Service Provider (ISP) to find out if they are having problems with their servers?

The guys and gals here are willing and able, just try to be concise. :)
