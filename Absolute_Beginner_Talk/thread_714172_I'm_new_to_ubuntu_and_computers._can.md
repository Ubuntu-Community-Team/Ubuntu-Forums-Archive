### Post by Het Irv on 2008-03-03
"Save" as in the website's address (Bookmark, the website's content could change)
or "Save" as in what you are looking at on the screen.

For option 1
Goto the Bookmarks Menu and Click the first option "Bookmark this Page"

For Option 2
File -> in the dropdown box that lists your printer select Print to File.

This will save the current webpage as a file on your computer.

### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2008-03-03
no problem, i assume you want to do this just in case your net goes down huh?
when you have the web page you want you can either:
A: right click on the page you desire and select "save page as"
or B:
go to "file" and then select "save page as"
or just press control+S at the same time

to view the pages, just as easy, you can just click on the file you desire (web pages usually end with .html) and it will open with firefox.
there might be an issue viewing images though as some pages wont load their files but normally this method works.
