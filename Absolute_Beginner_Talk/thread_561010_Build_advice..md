### Post by voided3 on 2007-09-28
About the onboard sound, just boot up the live CD when you get it all together and try it out. Worst case scenario, you give the computer a performance enhancement by finding a compatible soundcard (I recall reading that integrated sound can be slightly detrimental to game performance since it uses some CPU resources whereas a expansion card has all of its resources independently; I got a VIA Envy24 sound card even though my SiS based integrated is compatible because I wanted more outputs since I use multiple speakers).

### Post by Daveth on 2007-09-28
you might want to check out Seasonic PSU's which have a high efficiency rating (well up into the 80%'s), deliver a reliable load on the rails and are deathly quiet. 
You should also budget for a gpu cooler- the stock Nvidia one is akin to a small cyclone in your box and your friend will not thank you. Ditto the CPU cooler. For the latter check out Zalman coolers- easy to fit and v quiet. For your Nvidia gpu cooler look at Gigabyte's coolers. If you really want to impress her, silent eagle case fans are good, if your cooler master can take them.
My previous box - an off the shelf- could be heard downstairs, whilst the one I built based on the above hums quietly away to itself. It is suprising how wearing PC sounds can be.
