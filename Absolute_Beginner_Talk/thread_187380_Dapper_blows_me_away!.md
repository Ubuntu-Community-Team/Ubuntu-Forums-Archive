### Post by sfpeter on 2006-06-02
Hello:

Just a quick fan/rant on 6.06.

My history with Linux has been long, shallow, and ugly.  I've played with everything from Suse to Mandrake to Redhat to PinkTie.  (2 points if you know of Pinktie.)  My problems have usually come down to something like this:

Can't get sound to work, twiddle endlessly with ALSA/OSS drivers.

DVD's?  fuggedaboutit!

3D hardware support. Usually only got Voodoo cards to work reliably. Nvidia recently would work, but something else would break.  ATI was an unholy nightmare.  One time after 20+ retries I got a Rage 128 working in 3D on a PowerPC, and had no idea why it finally started working.

Updates.  Download this patch, dependency here, update also there.  Update the kernel....oops, non-standard kernel or something else not right.  Download kernel sources.   Oops, version numbers not matching, or sources not in expected directory.  

All this followed by format/re-install hello Windows! again.  

So my hopes weren't high, I mean Fedora comes on a DVD and this is a single CD.  Plus, my obsolete Pentium 4 not only had a Windows XP install I wanted to keep, but an ATI Fire GL video card.   Double the whammy on getting it running.

So I install, and to make it short(er):

Automatic repartitoning worked, and took an incredibly short time; my copy of Windows liveth.

Install, and hey, we got sound!

Check it out--a device manager!  How intriguing!  And wait a minute, it knows what video card I have?  No, let's run Glxgears.....my GOD, I HAVE 3D!  PAINLESS, SIMPLE 3D!

Totem...doesn't play DVD's just yet, but a quick totem-xine/dss and we're in business.  Even looks better than my Windows boxen.

Java....well, that one took a bit.  Found out I needed to not only check off the additional repositories, but add them in preferences.  The instructions I found were for 5.10 and a little different.  Once I fixed that, we're in business.  

So what do I think?  Well, I was considering plunging for an Intel Mac, but now I wonder why bother?  Aside from being prettier, I can do all the same functions from word processing to playing videos.  I've got the Gimp for Photoshop and  Blender for 3D.  Even some games to play.  

It's simple, it's easy to figureout, and the BLOODY THING CAN UPDATE WITHOUT A FREAKIN' KERNEL REBUILD! 

Okay, enough of that.  Tomorrow I figure out how to get Cinelerra and Povray working.
