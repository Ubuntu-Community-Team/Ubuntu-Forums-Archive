### Post by bwhite82 on 2008-02-09
Open up your webbrowser, and navigate to 192.168.1.1, this will take you to your routers settings page. Navigate to the correct tab to set a password for your wireless network.

Edit: Oh, and if you've changed no default options, the username should be: admin and password either blank or admin (can't remember). If those don't work, its likely someone has hijacked your router, in which case, simply taking a toothpick and pushing in and holding the tiny reset button (on the back of the router) will reset the passwords/default settings. After doing this, its a good idea to change the routers default password as well.
