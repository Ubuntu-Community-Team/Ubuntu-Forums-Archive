### Post by igknighted on 2007-02-01
Suse is not a distro you purchase, however SLED is.  In reality, the only difference between OpenSuse (the free version) and SLED is commercial support.  Both download the same beryl, but with SLED (in theory, I've never tried it) you could call up Novell and get support instead of comming online to forums.  The same is true of almost all linux distros, the exceptions being truely beginner distro's like Linspire and Xandros.  In these distro's you pay for some proprietary stuff (like crossover I believe?).

### Post by compiledkernel on 2007-02-01
Paid distros give only one real advantage that I feel or am aware of -  (these are mainly paid for support, like redhat offers with its boxed product), while free alternatives you must rely on other forms of support. 

In Ubuntu's case you rely on the community , the forums, the wiki, mailing lists, or such. (if your dire enough you can actually offer to  pay for support - [http://www.ubuntu.com/support/paid)](http://www.ubuntu.com/support/paid)). 

Most times the actual community and forums and so forth give you enough help not to resort tot he last possibility (most of which is considered for the enterprise/businesses). 

I wouldnt nessecarily consider it more stable. Buying a packaged distribution gives you proprietary drivers, proprietary codecs (or access to them), and whatever else the Distro owner is willing to penny up for really.
