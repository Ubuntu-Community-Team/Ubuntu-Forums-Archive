### Post by Tilex on 2006-12-05
It should not be too complicated to connect to their network but it depends if they are using some sort of encryption which Ubuntu does not support out-of-the-box (like WPA).

If the wireless network in the coffee shop is open to everyone, then you should see it in your Network Manager (do you have Wireless Network Assistant installed?  with this little piece of program, you can connect on a wifi network as easily as it is under Windows).

The Ubuntu community knows very well the improvements needed in terms of wireless networking.  At least that's what I heard of...

### Post by caricc on 2006-12-05
Your wifi card should show an icon at the top right of the main windows. souble click on this. This will bring up the connection properties window. select configure. enter your password. You can either select your wifi card and choose properties or double click it. under wireless settings: netowrk name: you will have to select the drop down arrow and select your wifi network. then ok. You should only have to do this once for each new network. 

Enjoy.
