### Post by catawba on 2007-12-19
Us absolute beginners are going to have a way of seeing things alike.  I am on the Ubuntu train and it is good to finally settle on a distribution and get that thinking behind me.

Ubuntu is going to suffer because it does not install hardware like Windows.  It would greatly help if there were a real concentration to pick up the video cards so that at least you could read and work with Ubuntu instead of having Ubuntu installed with a resolution that cannot be read.  The reason I mentioned my problem is because, I am using an old machine and hope to transform some old Windows computers to Ubuntu, in the spirit of Ubuntu.  It seems my first purchase should be a USB video card and I have looked all along for a couple of weeks for a segment at some website for Linux friendly hardware. I would like to purchase Ubuntu/Linux friendly hardware because it works and because it will develop the market place for Ubuntu.

I have not read any commentary of Dell as a supplier of Ubuntu components, but eventually it will surface, hopefully below.  I would like to be able to help you with your specific question on a Skype headset and really the answer above that any headset will do if the audio drivers are working seems fair enough.  What I offer is talk of Fatwallet.com and slickdeals.com although I am now done with talk of slickdeals.com.  Fatwallet.com is website for bargain hunters and is populated enough to get the good deals up. It also has people that build computers or resale on Ebay. Anyway, there is a wealth of knowledge in the threads on all the stuff that is too technical for me.
