### Post by ByteJuggler on 2008-01-09
> **supertails said:**
> It shows me the name of the company but the screen isn't like one color. Like I see most the screen being dotted. Like blue or gray dots over a black background with just the name of the company showing. Nothing else really happen until you press Control Alt Delete multiple times then you see this [] on the upper right hand screen with nothing else.

OK so it sounds like your machine just about starts getting into the BIOS and then crashes somewhere in there, and sometimes you're lucky enough for it to get through that stage and get to the point of booting. Anyway, there can be several reasons for that, including video card problems (most obviously, as your video is affected/distorted) or system RAM problems or even CPU problems.  The beep you hear *may* be a thermal warning, for example.  Check the CPU Heatsink hasn't become dislodged for example.  

If you're allowed to open the PC without voiding your warranty, I'd open it, reseat the RAM DIMM's, check the CPU's heatsink hasn't become loose, check the other heatsinks on the motherboard hasn't become loose, check the video card hasn't become unseated (possibly reseat it too) and verify the power cable is properly connected, and then try again.

If it then still doesn't work, and/or if you cannot open your PC without voiding your warranty, then I'd suggest you take it back under warranty as the machine clearly has some hardware issue.

What are the specs on the machine? Ram, motherboard speed,hard drive. Original OS, make and model # . Were you doing anything else while installing the CD like looking inside the computer...something might have moved or got disconnected. Did you move the computer while trying the install?


Could be a ram problem, ram problems can show up as errors to even making the machine non responsive. I know, I tried putting two PC 100 512 MB sticks into a machine that could only handle two PC 100 128 MB sticks. 


How old is the machine? Meaning that it could be a bad hard drive, though that should not account for a non-responsive machine.

Could the Power Supply be defective? Older E-machines were famous for this, can you swap out another power supply and see if that is the problem.

Are you able to pop out the CD from the machine? That could possibly, rarely,  cause it to hang.

Try the alternate installer CD. I encountered this early on with trying to install Ubuntu on a laptop using a live CD, it went to install it and for hours the screen went blank with the CD still spinning. The problem was that 192 MB was not enough Ram to run the Live CD for a proper install. I went with the alternate text installer and had no problems installing it, from then on I always used the alternate installer for any Ubuntu installation.
