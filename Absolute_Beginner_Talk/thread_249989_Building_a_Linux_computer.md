### Post by muep on 2006-09-03
Not all ATI cards are bad. Also, most motherboards work right out of the box.

Have you tried your current setup with Linux, for example a livecd?

My Radeon X300 worked without installing anything, the open source reverse-engineered driver works great. The performance isn't as good as it would be with the official drivers, but it is good enough for me. I also like that I don't need to install closed source drivers to my kernel. Currently you can't have that luxury with any Nvidia card, if you still want some acceleration. 

Also, all recent ATI cards I have used with the official driver, have worked very well.

You should at least try Ubuntu with your old hardware, there's nothing to lose.
