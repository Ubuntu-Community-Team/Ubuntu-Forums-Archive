### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-10-27
The reason why most games don't run natively on Linux is because they are written for Windows, not for Linux. All programs use specific functions of the operating system they are running on, and all operating systems have different functions. (i.e. they are named differently, and they accept different input and give different output).

WINE takes the functions that are available on Windows, and maps them to their Linux equivilants. The reason why many games still don't work on Wine is because DirectX is completely closed-source, and it's very complex for the WINE developers to figure out exactly what the DirectX functions do, and how they do it. Also, the WINE developers have only recently begun focussing on DirectX support, so it's early days there.
