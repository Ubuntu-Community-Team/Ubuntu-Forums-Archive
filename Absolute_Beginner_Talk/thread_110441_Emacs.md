### Post by darth_vector on 2005-12-30
emacs is a (highly customisable) text editor with a whole bunch of features. at its side rally (slightly less than) half of those involved in the text editor war (see this: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Editor_war)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Editor_war)). this is a war in which i am completely impartial.

now, if you want a **real** text editor, i would recommend vi or vim ;-) vim is smaller, more efficient and can be found in just about any *nix system, whereas emacs can not.

such text editors are often used for programming, changing system settings, writting emails, etc...
