### Post by matthewv on 2005-11-21
The disc cover and cds will have the release written on it: e.g 4.10 for warty, 5.04 for hoary, and 5.10 for breezy
Although they are usually referred to by their codenames, eg. hoary and breezy and now dapper, the official release name is the number, which takes the naming from its release date: eg. 5.10 was release in october 2005

### Post by Wallakoala on 2005-11-21
Hi,

To find what version you have...first go to you filesystem. Click on the folder called "etc" and then scroll down until you see a bunch of text files. One of them should be called "issue" or something like that. If you open it, it will say what version of ubuntu you have. There are some other text files in the same folder that have the information also.
