### Post by DutyDuty on 2007-11-15
I'll do my best:
Companies like Windows or Macintosh are closed source, which is to say that the coding for their OS is not something you and me can see. A problem I see with this is that this makes black hat hackers. When someone finds a hole in Vista, for example, they send everyone they can find a virus that hits them in this hole. Linux is open source, though. You and me can see the codes that run our computers. We can find holes, and rather than exploit, we can fix them. Linux hackers are more white hats in this way. Related but not the same is the GPL, which is that I can edit your code and do what I want with the code I made, as long as I credit you too. Someone can then take my code and do the same. I may have that concept confused with copyleft, but that's a quick summary.
