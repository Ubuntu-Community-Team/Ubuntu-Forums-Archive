### Post by imdidactic on 2007-05-03
Linux has a variety of basic programs for recording music.  You'll probably have the most success with live music recording, because the sequencing programs aren't up to par as of yet, and Ableton live won't work in wine or Crossover Office (at least not in my fifty attempts). I'll just list the popular programs for you.

Multitrack recording: most people use Ardour because of it's ease of use with Jack, but I prefer Audacity because it's a less buggy overall better program, in my opinion.

Sequencing: there's a whole mess of mod trackers and drum machines out there (best drum machine = hydrogen), but the only decent sequencer is LMMS.  It's basically a stripped down version of Fruity Loops, which also won't work in wine.

Honorable mention: ZynAddSubFX, a killer virtual synth you can find in synaptic or Add/Remove...

Hope this is a good start for you.

### Post by imdidactic on 2007-05-03
Ubuntu studio is a new distro coming out in a week or two geared for recording.  It's probably going to have some good built in support as far as drivers go, but if you already have an ADC installed and working, you can get all of the programs it offers with apt-get or synaptic.  Get the MBox2 from M-Audio, as it is currently the most supported input device in linux.  Don't try to run anything through your sound card if you don't want to sound like a ten year old pretending to make a radio show.
