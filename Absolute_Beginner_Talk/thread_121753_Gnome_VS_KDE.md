### Post by mrgnash on 2006-03-02
They're both great, but I prefer Fluxbox. It's **much** faster, and if you start up with KDE and Gnome support you get the best of both worlds in terms of apps. In fact, I find that a lot of applications run better under Fluxbox than they do under their 'native' window manager. Under Fluxbox I haven't had nearly the same amount of trouble with multimedia applications, especially video players, crashing and taking out the sound, and all the applications tend to load faster.

All the same, if I want to experience the ultimate in eyecandy, I have to go with KDE. Superkaramba and ksmoothdock make KDE the prettiest thing this side of Enlightenment DR17. :mrgreen:
