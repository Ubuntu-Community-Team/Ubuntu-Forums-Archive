### Post by OffAxis on 2008-02-13
You're right; the intel doesn't hold up for gaming at all. nvidia's pretty good about supplying their drivers and generally supporting linux, so their restricted and open-source drivers are going to give you much better performance. Additionally, the intel chips use the system RAM, so it'll be slower than an add-on card and cost you system performance overall.
And don't forget to ask Dell about putting Linux on that laptop instead of the ubiquitous Windows install. By not having Windows on there they should knock ~$50 off the price.
