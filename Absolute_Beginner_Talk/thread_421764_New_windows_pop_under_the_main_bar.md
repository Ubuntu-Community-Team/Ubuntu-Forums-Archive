### Post by pkarjala on 2007-04-24
Took a look at the screenshot, and unfortunately I'm not as familiar with Gnome.  You might try doing a search in the search function for "Window Pop-Under" or "window positioning on open."

Don't quote me on this, but I'd imagine that there may be an option for the menu bar that allows you to select whether the bar is on top of windows or not.

As a temporary solution, you may wish to enable Hide Buttons for the panel.  Simply right click on a blank space on the panel, select Properties, and on the General tab, check the "Show hide buttons" box.  You can then use these buttons to hide the panel temporarily until you're able to find a resolution for this issue.
