### Post by Amplidude on 2007-07-14
> it's really inappropriate for people to come to these forums and "lecture" this community on Ubuntu's supposed shortcomings. 

Thing is, it's really destructive. I see a lot of posts from people who are contemplating shifting to Ubuntu whose first step (wisely) is to take a look at the forums and see if they have the appropriate kit etc. Then they see a post from someone complaining about how bad Ubuntu is because of a problem they're having and decide "oh no, not for me!" 

I loaded Office 2007 over XP a couple of months ago and hardly a day went by without the new equivalent of the BSOD ("application" has encountered a problem and has to close, sorry for the inconvenience. Click here to tell us about the problem.) What the complainers seem to forget is that the problem isn't solved, it's just sent to someone else. At least with Ubuntu you get to fix the problem so that it doesn't happen again. And you don't have to wait for the next SP. 

So, for those of you who are thinking about changing to Ubuntu, go for it. Yes, you will encounter problems, but they can be solved (mostly). The more esoteric your problem the more likely you are  to encounter problems. I read yesterday about someone having trouble with a dongle in his machine. Personally, I wouldn't put my dongle anywhere near a machine.:grin:

Seriously though, give it a whirl. There are no sharks in these waters.
