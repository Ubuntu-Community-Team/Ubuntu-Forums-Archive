### Post by bmt on 2007-06-16
You might find setting a 'Compose' key is more convenient for typing text.  In System>Preferences>Keyboard, go to the 'Layout Options' tab and select 'Compose key position'.  There are several alternative keys available, but I find the Right Win-key is the most useful (as it does nothing else for me).

Once this is set, accented letters can be 'composed' with three keystrokes.  Press the nominated Compose key, then the letter required, followed by its modifying accent key.  The accent key is ` ' ^ or , to give grave, acute, circumflex or cedilla, respectively.  (In fact, the two keystrokes after the Compose key can be made in either order.)  Thus, é can be produced by Compose-e-'.  The same principle works for all International characters, so ß can be written by Compose-s-s, for example.
