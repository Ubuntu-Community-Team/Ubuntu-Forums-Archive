### Post by Ampersand on 2005-10-04
if your computer isn't booting to the cd, you'll have to change the boot order in the bios.

To do this, reboot, and press Delete when 'press Del to enter setup' (or something similar) appears. Look through the menus, and you'll find the settings for the boot order. Make sure that your CD drive is first. If you have the manuals for your computer, it should tell you how to do this, since it varies according to your hardware.

Once you've saved and exited, your computer should then boot into the Ubuntu installer. 

Are you planning on replacing Windows completely, or installing Ubuntu alongside?
