### Post by Alterax on 2007-06-29
As for the formatting above not matching, try pasting formatted text from ANYTHING, OO to AbiWord to MS Office, et cetera into an all-text box.  You'll be lucky if it does match if you use any kind of formatting.

Now if you mean formatting for documents, as opposed to the cut and paste job I did to start this post, I do admit that Word is a bit more sophisticated--for doing Word documents.  It's native.  That doesn't mean that this is the only format available.  PDFs are better--as another poster noted--but not just for formatting purposes, but because it's much more difficult to alter the contents of a PDF, and the recipient doesn't get the luxury of tracking changes.  PDFs are better because they are more secure, and OpenOffice has that capacity by default.

Now, regarding the implication that OpenOffice doesn't do quality formatting, I have never had documents get kicked back for looking sloppy.  I have had others--who use Word and Excel--send their documents over to me to get the finishing touches.  Word may be more sophisticated than Writer, but the real difference is in the level of skill that a person has in document design and the use of their proficiency with the program.

Finally, I would never walk into a client's office telling them they need to drop one product and to use another instead.  You do that, you risk bruising the ego of the person that may have had a hand in it to begin with and lose the sale.  Instead, you do a lot better by giving them options--I have had the best luck with letting them work with the product for a bit and then letting them make the decision on their own.  And so far, the only complaint I have gotten from OpenOffice was that they had problems making envelopes.

--Alterax
