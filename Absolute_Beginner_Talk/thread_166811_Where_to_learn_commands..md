### Post by jrib on 2006-04-27
Here are some good ones imo:

beginner:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BasicCommands](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BasicCommands)
[http://www.linuxcommand.org/](http://www.linuxcommand.org/)
[http://www.tuxfiles.org](http://www.tuxfiles.org)
[http://wiki.ubuntu.com/AptGetHowTo/](http://wiki.ubuntu.com/AptGetHowTo/)

intermediate/advanced:
[http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/html/index.html](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/Bash-Beginners-Guide/html/index.html)
[http://www.tldp.org/HOWTO/Bash-Prog-Intro-HOWTO.html](http://www.tldp.org/HOWTO/Bash-Prog-Intro-HOWTO.html)
[http://www.tldp.org/LDP/abs/html/](http://www.tldp.org/LDP/abs/html/)
[http://rute.2038bug.com/](http://rute.2038bug.com/)

The tldp.org ones are really good for learning bash's more advanced stuff and rute is just really good for general linux!

nice tricks:
[http://www.ukuug.org/events/linux2003/papers/bash_tips/](http://www.ukuug.org/events/linux2003/papers/bash_tips/)


That's mostly bash and some apt stuff... if you are looking for something else in particular just ask :)
