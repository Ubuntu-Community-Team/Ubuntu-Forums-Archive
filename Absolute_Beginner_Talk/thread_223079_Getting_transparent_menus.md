### Post by Jucato on 2006-07-25
It's easier in KDE, without using any compositing/XGL.
Launch System Settings > Appearance > Style > Effects tab. Then from the drop down list beside "Menu effect" choose "Make Translucent". Then you can adjust the translucency settings with the controls near the bottom. Hope that was what you were looking for.
