### Post by philipMac on 2005-11-30
Basically the answer is no. 
When you boot an OS, you only boot one. It doesnt matter if you have two or more OSs on your hardrive, your memory only loads one. So, there is no problem there. 
If you are worried about your hard drive being slow, you are going to be splitting your hard drive into two "sections". The Windows section, and the linux one. (Realistically, there will be more than one partition per section.) Again when you are searching and loading about on your Win section, it makes no differance that you are looking in slightly smaller partitions (unless it is very full). Intuitively it would seem like this would actually be faster but dont quote me on that. Same goes for the Linux partitions. So, there is no slow up. 
I think if you use a windows machine, and you fill up the HD the system will slow up. This is due to the way that win organizes itself. I do not know if the same goes for Linux, but think it doesnt. 
Good luck with things.
