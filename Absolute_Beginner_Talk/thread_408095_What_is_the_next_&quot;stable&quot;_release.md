### Post by 23meg on 2007-04-12
[QUOTE=Sef]The the next LTS desktop will be April 2009, and the next server LTS will be April 2011.[/QUOTE]

Those are support expiry dates for the current LTS release, Dapper, not the dates when the next LTS release will arrive. There's no exact schedule for LTS releases, but it's safe to expect one in every 4-5 releases. The next one will most likely be Gusty+1, which is due in April 2008 if no extra bug fixing period is added, and around June 2008 if one is added, like in Dapper.

### Post by 23meg on 2007-04-12
[QUOTE=ricardisimo]I was of the impression that Feisty was dev, not stable. Am I wrong about that?[/QUOTE]

Every development release becomes a stable release once work on it is finished, at the end of the six month development period. The term "stable release" doesn't have to do with how stable a release is supposed to be in comparison to other releases. Long term support releases are expected to be more stable than regular stable releases, however.

[QUOTE=ricardisimo]I know about the 3- and 5-year paid support for Dapper, but I didn't know that this also reflected release schedules. Does it?[/QUOTE]

That's not just paid support, it's general update support by the Ubuntu developers; in other words the period in which security fixes will be provided for the "main" component of Ubuntu (more about components [here]("http://www.ubuntu.com/community/ubuntustory/components")). And no, it doesn't reflect release schedules.
