### Post by dbott67 on 2005-11-03
After installing Ubuntu, go to Synaptic and download KDE and dependencies (they will be selected automatically).  Next time you login, select SESSIONS and choose KDE from the options.  When you login, it will ask if you want to make it the default.

Keep in mind that the only issue with using alternate DEs & WMs is that the menus get clogged up with apps from the other DEs/WMs.  There are specific projects for KDE (Kubuntu), Gnome (Ubuntu) and XFCE (Xubuntu) that have their own ISOs and apps.

-Dave
