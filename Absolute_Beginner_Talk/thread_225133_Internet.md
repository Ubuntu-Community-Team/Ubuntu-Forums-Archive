### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-07-29
You can dual-boot Windows and Ubuntu on your laptop if you don't want to fully take the plunge.

It doesn't surprise me that your driver CD for your wireless card doesn't talk about Linux compatibility. To my knowledge, no wireless card manufacturer actually makes Linux drivers. However, due to the efforts of many open-source developers, a number of cards "just work" without installing drivers, and many more can be made to work using ndiswrapper.

Try searching for "Linux compatibility" and your card's manufacturer on Google or these forums. Ndiswrapper works by emulating the Windows wireless APIs, so you would need to install the Windows drivers into Ndiswrapper.
