### Post by catlett on 2006-04-23
Go to your Gmail account. In the top right there is a "settings" link. Click the link. It takes you to a menu. Click on POP and forwarding.
In option 2 "When messages are accessed with POP" choose 'keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox' from the choices in the dropdown box. Click save changes and your messages will stay on Gmail's server. When thuderbird in XP or Ubuntu accesses the inbox it will make a copy not move the original message.

### Post by MHlavsa on 2006-04-24
Actually I think I came up with something that'll work the way I want it to:

I thought about the "leave in inbox" option, but I didn't want to deal with the mess of archiving e-mails myself.  Instead, I started a new e-mail (name doesn't matter, I just added Ub to the end of my old one) and had my e-mail forward all messages to it.  Then if you go into Accounts in Gmail, you can tie accounts together, which I did, so that even if I send from Ubuntu through the [email]xxxxxxUb@gmail.com[/email] account, it shows that it is from [email]xxxxx@gmail.com[/email] (my real e-mail).

That way [email]xxxxx@gmail.com[/email] still gets auto-archived, Ubuntu Thunderbird still downloads all e-mails, and I can use both to send out of the same e-mail account.
