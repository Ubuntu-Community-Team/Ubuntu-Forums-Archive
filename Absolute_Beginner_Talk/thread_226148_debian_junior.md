### Post by Indras on 2006-07-30
Yeah, unfortunately, many packages in Synaptic don't install menu items.  You often have to make your own shortcuts.  Once you know how to run it from terminal or the Alt-F2 (run) menu, just pop that command into a shortcut.

Often, I'll find out what the command is by going into Synaptic, right-click the package, click "properties" and go to the "installed files" tab.  Scroll down to get a list of files in the package.  The program name is often in /usr/bin or something similar.
