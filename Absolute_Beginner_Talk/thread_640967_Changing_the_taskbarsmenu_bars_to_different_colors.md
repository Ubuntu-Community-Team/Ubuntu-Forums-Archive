### Post by SunnyRabbiera on 2007-12-14
well you can download a panel background from either here:
[gnome look]("http://www.gnome-look.org/")
or even
[kde look]("http://www.kde-look.org/")

KDE look might be a better bet as they have a section for panel backgrounds called "kicker background", even though these are for the panels for KDE they can be resized and redone for gnomes panels (that is what ubuntu uses by default by the way, KDE and Gnome are two different desktop environments but do about the same thing in the end)
for gnome look you might want to search for panel backgrounds but KDElook is a little better for options.
if you need to know how to use one of these panel backgrounds do the following:
download a kicker panel theme you like, rather it be a zip file or a straightforward PNG file its easy to use either way.
now if its zipped, well you can unzip them using the built in unzip tool in gnome.
now once you download and or unzip them you might want to open the images in the gimp, the default image editor in ubuntu and resize the hight of the images to say 23 pixels in height (remember to leave width alone though)
once you get the image resized you can now save it and apply it to the panel.
now to make it the background of your panel right click on either the top or the bottom one and select "properties"
now in the background tab you can now click next to the circle/ box or whatever next to "background image" and then select the image you just downloaded and edited.
just remember to put whatever you downloaded in a decent place like in your home folder or something, dont want to clutter your desktop.
