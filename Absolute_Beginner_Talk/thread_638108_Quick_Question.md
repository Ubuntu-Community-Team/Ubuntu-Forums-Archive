### Post by ardchoille42 on 2007-12-11
In almost any distro, you can have gnome and/or kde.. thus the "look" will be quite similar across distros. However, Fedora and Ubuntu use different package managers and many of the other things "under the hood" are quite different. The best person to decide what will work best for you is **you**. I would recommend using them both and then choose the one which works best for your needs.

I switched to Ubuntu from Fedora for the following reasons:
1) APT and dpkg work better for me than yum and rpm
2) Due to the size of the Ubuntu repos, I've not had to compile anything in the 3 years I have used Ubuntu
3) I have not had a single problem in Ubuntu since 2003
4) apt seems to be much faster than yum from what I have seen
