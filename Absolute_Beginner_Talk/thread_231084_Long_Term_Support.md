### Post by Kurt` on 2006-08-06
Even if there is a commercial version released, all of the software used in it is still available because of the GPL.

Ubuntu (as a company) can make money by selling enterprise/business support to cover costs, while still providing home users with a totally free OS.

Mr. Shuttleworth is passionate about this project, and I doubt that he would ever do anything to harm this community.

"Long-term support" is something that businesses will be more comfortable seeing, as having to upgrade all of your machines every 6 months is a bit of a daunting task. Having guaranteed support (enterprise or not) for your software makes it a little more reassuring.

### Post by popkid on 2006-08-07
As I understand it "supported" means that the release along with enhancements and security updates will continue to be developed and hosted in the repositories. I guess as Edgy and later releases come on stream the emphasis will move to them and not so much will change in the dapper branch of the repositories, but there will still be someone making sure that there are bugfixes and updates available.

I think its a good idea, I use linux on an older laptop (my second computer) and for me using the OS is part of the fun, so I tend to jump at each new release, but if Ubuntu and Linux in general are going to start tipping the balance of users toward them they need to accomodate first the people who only want to install an OS once every 3 years... and then the people who don't want to install one ever (IMHO probably the majority of computer users out there). 

For me Dapper is easily the biggest step in the right direction so far, it is the first Linux distro that I have used that I can honestly say I spend more time using my computer to do "normal" stuff I want to do, than I do simply playing with the OS itself. It still took me a week or two to configure my machine as I wanted it but since its been done, the stability has been great and aside from a few remaining niggles here and there I don't have to worry about it.
