### Post by Redcard on 2006-08-31
The answer to your question is that ALL modern OS's (yes, even your blessed XP) require passwords.  Some of them leave the passwords blank, but when Vista comes around, count on having to have a password for your computer.  If your XP has a blank password, you are running a nice little security risk.

Now, over to Ubuntu.  GOOD SECURITY CONSCIOUS softare requires a password.  Ubuntu is that.  You typed your selected password in TWICE.  If you're unable to remember that, reboot the machine into "rescue" mode, and type "sudo passwd <username>" to reset your password.

If that's too much, bye.  Linux is just as easy as windows, though we DO require you to read the instructions instead of blindly doing things.
