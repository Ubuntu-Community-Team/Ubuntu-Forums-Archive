### Post by Sprogg2001 on 2007-08-07
What your asking is not as simple as it sounds. 

This is a predominately windows area I'm afraid 

Converting VHS tapes to DVDs requires

• An analogue-to-digital video converter -( Video Capture card with good linux support) 
• Editing software - (some good linux programs out there)
• AV cables 
• DVD-RW 
• At least 250GB of free space 

then you need to play VHS tapes and at the same time capture the analogue signal with the analogue-to-digital video converter hardware 

then you need to edit the video and sound converting to a more dvd friendly format I.E. mpeg2 
And finaly burn it on to dvd.

In my opinion its far easier to get a professional studio to do the conversion for you and its not as expensive as it sounds as all this can very difficult for an amateur to get into.

good luck whatever you decide.
