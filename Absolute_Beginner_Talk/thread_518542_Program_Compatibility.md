### Post by Max Luebbe on 2007-08-06
None of those programs will run natively in Linux.
You'll need to run them in some kind of emulation layer (Wine or Cedega) and performance won't be as good as in windows.

Also, I wouldn't be surprised if one or more of those won't fully supported by emulation and experienced glitchy behavior.

Apps make the platform - if you need all of those apps, there's no justifying switching over to Linux. That is however, not saying that theyre aren't a lot of compelling alternatives that are freely distributed for use with Linux.

Why not try a dual-boot and get your feet wet in Linux while maintaining a usable environment for all the apps you currently depend on?
