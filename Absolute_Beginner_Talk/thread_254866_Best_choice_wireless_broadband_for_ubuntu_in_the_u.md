### Post by furiousV on 2006-09-10
I'm with [Seriously Internet](http://www.seriouslyinternet.com)

They start at £15 if you use very little in a month, but the more you use, the fee for that month will rise. 

When you use 30GB, it will be £27.99, and then the price will *not* go any higher :)

Since I'm an internet power user, I usually use about 35 to 40GB, and have hit 60GB. I look at the "pay-as-you-go" as a bonus for using less bandwidth :P

Also, you get a static IP address.

I have had a very good experience with them, for about a year and a half. The only problems I have had were when BT were messing about with the exchanges when they introduced 8Meg broadband.

I would strongly recommend you get a router, that way you just need to get your network card to work (out the box 99% of the time) and not have to worry about fancy USB Windows-only modems and messing about with drivers.

### Post by Article22 on 2006-09-15
I personally wouldn't recommend AOL, NTL or Tiscali unless you are looking at a complete package.

My parents have used AOL and NTL and their customer support was terrible. My sister uses Tiscali and they also seem to have poor support.

I went with Eclipse ([www.eclipse.co.uk](www.eclipse.co.uk)) a few years ago with their ADSL service and have been delighted with their service.

Currently only paying £17 a month for a 8 Mb ADSL line with no real cap (I DL around 40 Gb a month), the only thing that they ask is for you to DL mainly offpeak which suits me fine as you get a higher DL rate, and it is easy to schedule it with uTorrent.

Wireless is always going to be more tricky to set up in Ubuntu than wired, and setting up a Wireless Router without a wired connection is a little dodgy.

I use a wireless ADSL modem/router which has a 4 port wired switch seemed pretty easy to set up.
