### Post by thinktank on 2006-08-16
What's the difference between packages that have the ubuntu icon next to  their checkbox and those that doesn't have the ubuntu icon?

---

### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-08-16
Hi Thinktank!

The programs in Synaptic marked with the Ubuntu logo are official Ubuntu packages, created and/or maintained by the Ubuntu development team.

Packages without the Ubuntu logo are community-maintained or non-free packages.  That is to say, they are generally compatible with Ubuntu (or made specifically for Ubuntu) but they are not part of the official distribution.  This doesn't mean those packages are unsafe, just that they aren't official.  If you have questions about any particular package, do a search on the forums to see if anyone else has asked about it.  If not, go ahead and post.  We'll be happy to share our experiences.

(Packages with a yellow star are newly-available packages.)

Some of the packages have a suffix like ubuntu6, ubuntu8, ubuntu12 etc. in their version (for example in the Dapper release, OpenOffice 2.0 has a version 2.0.2-2ubuntu12). What are those 6, 8 and 12? and do they have any significance for other distros?

### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-08-16
Some packages are tweaked a bit to optimize them to work with Ubuntu specifically.  The -ubuntu6, etc indicates the revision number of the Ubuntu-optimized package.
