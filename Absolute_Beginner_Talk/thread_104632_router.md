### Post by dbott67 on 2005-12-16
A router (in it's simplist form) takes packets of data from one network and puts them on another network.

In the case of home cable/DSL routers (such as the D-Link DI-604 and others), they also perform a few other tasks:
- act as a firewall by using NAT (network address translation) & stateful packet inspection
- has built-in PPPoE software for DSL (saves you from having to install it on your PC)
- allows multiple computers/devices to share a single connection
- certain brands offer additional features such as content filtering, blacklists, time-limits, etc.

All in all, I consider these little routers an essential part of any home network.  They are very inexpensive and provide an extra layer of security against unsolicited inbound requests.

### Post by bscbrit on 2005-12-16
Hi Richard.
First of all, your new purchase is a modem.  It will allow you to connect to your ADSL line using the ethernet port on your computer.  You have a PCI network interface card (NIC) already.
Secondly, it will allow you to filter the data passing through the DI604 to enable you to make you computer more secure, and to reduce the possibility of anyone from the internet getting access to your computer data.  You do not have to set this up immediately but can do so later as you learn more about how it works.
Thirdly, it will allow you to share your single DSL line between 4 computers at the same time while putting all those computers on a network.  You may not have more than one computer at the moment, but this will give you the ability to add computers to a network later on, or perhaps allow you to connect a friends computer to your own to play games or exchange data.  Once again, you do not have to use this facility straight away.
To set it up, you will need some of the information that was provided with your BT Voyager modem.  No problems, just come back here if you need help.
