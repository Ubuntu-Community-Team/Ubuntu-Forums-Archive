### Post by RKCole on 2007-03-24
Hello, nitez.

I'd like to put in my two cents on this thread, so please bear with me.

I"m a legally blind (partially) computer user. A long ways back (well...ti feels like it...) in 2004 I tried to use Linux (Red Hat v7.0 I believe).  The installation scared me to death, so I jumped out of it.

A year down the road (in Q2 of 2005) I was required to take a Linux class at my college.  They used Fedora Core 2 for the class  Now it's just my opinion here, but I didn't much care for FC2.  The professor himself preferred Debian-based distros (i.e. Debian, Ubuntu, etc)

During that time even the professor himself did not know much about Linux accessibility, but that soon changed.  I learned some things and had things up and running (in FC2) in no time.  At home I decided to use Ubuntu 5.10...It was quite frustrating.  I could not get the Linux screen magnifier (gnome-mag) to work properly, so I stopped using v5.10 and waited for v6.06.  after that things worked very well, and I kept upgrading to the newest system.  I did not do dist-upgrade, however, as I was kind of afraid as I had heard some horror stories...I would download the newest ISO, burn it to a CD, and go from there.

I'm now using Ubuntu 7.04 and I am amazed at how far things have come along.  The magnifier is something which I never imagined ti to be (it is pretty much the same quality of a screen magnifier I had to pay $600 for for Windows)...I also found that there is a built-in codec wizard which will try to install codecs (automatically) when they are needed.  It's still in beta, but I have found ti to work very well with both hardware support and functionality.

You asked in one of your previous posts (on page 1) about whether people were in the IT field.  I'm an IT student, but I have had little experience with Linux until 2005.  I'm still no guru, but I'm working my way to that point.  I also know others who use Ubuntu who are not in the IT/Computer field at all.  As a matter of fact, my older brother (who knows nothing about Linux) intends to switch to Ubuntu soon as he is not liking what came on his new Dell laptop...(if you can't guess it...it's MS Windows Vista).

Now, I apologize for my rambling, but I just want to try to make a point...

There is some learning to be done, but I have found (out of the other distros I've tried) that Ubuntu is the most user-friendly, accessible system I have ever seen.  I can't get my wife to see that as she's used to Windows, but when I get another computer, I will solely be using Ubuntu (I dual boot right now).  There is some learning to do, that is true, but unlike the other OS..I've said the bad "W" word too much already...there is a vast community here who will do their best to help with any problematic situations that may arise.  Honestly, without them, I don't think I'd be as far into Linux as I am now.

Well...I guess I'll wrap this up...sorry for the long post...I hope that this has helped you at least a small bit.

Take care...and if you have any questions (if I can help you) feel free to message me on the forums.
