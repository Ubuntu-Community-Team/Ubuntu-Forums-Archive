### Post by quietglow on 2005-12-06
There are issues with certain laptops not having their battery detected. If you run the Live cd and the battery applet doesn't show a battery (I think you'll probably have to add it manually--> right click on the title bar and "add applet" choose the battery monitor) there may be a problem.

One very good thing to do is google: <the model # of your laptop> ubuntu and/or linux

This will give you a good idea what to expect. If there are lots of pages detailing problems, you may want to steer clear. If there is no info out there, it is likely a newer machine. You're more or less on your own in that case--make sure you take a look at your store's return policy. 

Also check out the ubuntu laptop wiki for some listed laptops:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam)

Also, an excellent source of laptop info is here:

[http://www.linux-laptop.net/](http://www.linux-laptop.net/)
