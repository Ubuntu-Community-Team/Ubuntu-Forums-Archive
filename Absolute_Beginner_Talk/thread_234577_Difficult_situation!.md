### Post by msak007 on 2006-08-12
> **Bad Hair said:**
> why frostwire instead of limewire??

I don't want to turn this into a FrostWire vs. LimeWire thread, but I thought I'd answer the question since you had trouble installing LimeWire. Among other things, FrostWire is open-source (based on LimeWire), there's no commercial nags or filters, and last but not least if LimeWire is ever forced to implement DRM in order to avoid being shut down or to avoid lawsuits, the FrostWire team will strip their software of DRM. It's free with no restrictions, there will never be a paid version, and there's no adware / spyware. You can read about it [here]("http://www.frostwire.com/?module=about"). If you're having trouble installing FrostWire, try using Automatix to install it.

To answer your main question though as far as games go, I've never had much luck trying to get games running and still have an XP partition for that. I've tried Wine, and even paid for Cedega but could never get good performance out of it and the support wasn't that great. You can try a Cedega demo if you want to see if it'll work for you.
