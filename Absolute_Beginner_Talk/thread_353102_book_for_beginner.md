### Post by whitefort on 2007-02-04
I have three Ubuntu books so far.  I found the 'Official' book a bit too basic, even speaking as a total beginner.  It gave me lots of history and philosophy (which were interesting and important, and I'm not knocking it for that), but when it came to actual nuts and bolts I found it a bit lacking.

To be honest, it struck me as the kind of Linux intro that's going to leave hopeful migrants from windows feeling frustrated - A little too much of 'it's free, and you can do anything you can do in windows, and it works out of the box...'  Only the first of these is strictly true.  Reading the book, there's no indication that some things just don't work 'out of the box', and no advice on how to proceed when they don't.

I also have ***Ubuntu Hacks***, and recommend it ***VERY ***highly.  Using it, I've installed Jave, various other bits of useful software, I've added the stuff I need to play CDs and DVDs, added the Nvidia drivers for my graphics card, and all sorts of other cool stuff (as my wall paper, I have a picture of the planet, showing the areas currently in light and shade, and updated very twenty minutes).  I think it's a great book.

I've also bought the ***Ubuntu Linux Bible. *** I'm only a few chapters into it, but I get the feeling it has everything you need to get from beginner to quite advanced.

FWIW, the book I read before downloading my first linux distro (Mepis), was [this one]("http://www.amazon.com/Moving-Linux-Screen-Death-Goodbye/dp/0321159985/sr=1-2/qid=1170602568/ref=sr_1_2/002-5939808-3167215?ie=UTF8&s=books"). The best teacher I ever had, though, was direct experience. Just downloading and burning a .iso and booting it up, then relying on the internet for any questions you may have (and there'll be plenty), taught me more than any book could.

I went and bought a cheap 10GB hard drive to do my first install, too. This way you don't have to mess with your Windows installation. In fact, if you're dual-booting, 2 disks is the way to go IMHO - one OS per disk. I recently posted about it [here]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showpost.php?p=2103831&postcount=16").
