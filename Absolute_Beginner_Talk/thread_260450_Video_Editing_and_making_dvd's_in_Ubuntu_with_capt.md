### Post by Marsolin on 2006-10-26
There are quite a few TV apps for Linux. See [here]("http://linuxappfinder.com/multimedia/television") for a list of some. [TVtime]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/tvtime") and [KDE TV]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/kdetv") are a couple to check out.

If you want a TiVo or Media Center style interface then try [MythTV]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/mythtv") or [Freevo]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/freevo").

As for video editors, [Kino]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/kino") is a good option if you are importing from a DV video camera. [Avidemux]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/avidemux"), [Cinelerra]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/cinelerra"), [LiVES]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/lives"), [PiTiVi]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/pitivi"), [KDEnlive]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/kdenlive"), and [OpenMovieEditor]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/openmovieeditor") are other options.

For creating DVD's you should check out [DeVeDe]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/devede"), [DVDStyler]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/dvdstyler"), [QDVDAuthor]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/qdvdauthor"), [KMediaFactory]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/kmediafactory"), [tovid]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/tovid"), or [ManDVD]("http://linuxappfinder.com/package/mandvd").
