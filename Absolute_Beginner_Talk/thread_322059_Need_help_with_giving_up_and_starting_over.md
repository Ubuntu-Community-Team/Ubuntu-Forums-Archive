### Post by gn2 on 2006-12-20
By far the easiest thing to do if you need to completely re-install XP is to borrow a CD from someone else.

Just use their CD and your product key.

Make sure if your key is XP Home the CD has to be XP Home. Pro/Pro MediaCentre/MediaCentre etc.

If your XP installation is still present on the hard drive and you haven't deleted it you can re-install the bootloader by running "fixmbr" from any Windows boot floppy.

Super Grub Disc can also get you going again.
[http://supergrub.forjamari.linex.org/](http://supergrub.forjamari.linex.org/)

There's a user on here- Xpod -who may know how to help with your specific request.
