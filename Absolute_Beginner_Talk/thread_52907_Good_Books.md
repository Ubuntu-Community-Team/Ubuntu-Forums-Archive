Like an Ubuntu for dummies? No...not really.


Honestly Gnome is pretty intuitive. Its pretty easy to see and use all of its features I think. Anything that is not obvious in Gnome (saying using ndiswrapper, or messing with system files) is usually stuff that you need some terminal time to do (so your other users shouldn't do it anyway).

Basically Linux runs at two levels: The high end- the terminal hacking, the permission fine tuning, the compiling of non synaptic software. And the low end: installing stuff with synaptic, clicking on the Firefox icon to get the internet, going through the Gnome menu to chane basic settings. For advanced stuff there is tons of Linux documentation on the internet. For low end stuff Gnome is designed to make those things stand out, to not need documentation.

There is no middle. Everything is either really easy or really hard. 

What you want is a mix: "gui functions" and "command line." They are two different worlds with big differences in needed skills. If the people you are trying to convert are middle of the road computer users, maybe Ubuntu is not the best choice for them.

I like to give straight answers.
