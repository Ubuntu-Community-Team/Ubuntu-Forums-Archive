I know this is probably more in hope than in expectation, but is it possible, once you have installed ubuntu (or any Linux) and set it up as you like (with other programs, fonts, etc.), to save the whole lot onto an external drive and, if a reinstall is ever necessary, simply copy it all back onto the computer?

### Post by T700 on 2006-07-21
You would normally save your home directory.  By default, that contains all your files and many/most of your customized settings.  You can of course use a cloning software (Ghost, in the Windows world, for example) to copy the whole thing and just use that copy to reinstall.

Just FYI:  Giving a specific title to your question will generally get much better results.
