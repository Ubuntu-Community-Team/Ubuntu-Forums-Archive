### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-02
I don't know, only you can decide if its really beneficial. I can tell you I like Ubuntu a lot. It has lots of benefits like :
- Little need to worry about virusses or spyware.
- Free open source programs that do nearly everything one could want on windows.
- A great open community that is helpful and does not consider you a pirate for tweaking your OS (bad microsoft WGA and DRM)
- No need to ever defrag or run any scans again, saving you hours.
- A much more stable platform as a whole, once set up.
- Decent integrated support for hardware that will only improve.

I think thats enough positives. If you are a gamer though, a complete ubuntu switch may not be for you as unless your game is supported by Wine / Cedega you won't be able to play it.

I think it would be safe for you to try a dual boot, then if you don't like it you can wipe it. Easy enough, just pop in the disk and follow the instructions. When you get to the partitioner leave your NTFS partition alone and just make 2 new ones. First should be / (root folder), Primary partition formatted with Ext3, 6 GB is enough. Then the second partition should be a swap file system, and 2 GB.

Just one additional note, if you plan on using ubuntu for a long time, make a /home folder, logical drive with ext3. Since your just testing it don't worry about that.

Extra Note: Do back up any critical software, things can always go wrong that is the nature of a PC.
