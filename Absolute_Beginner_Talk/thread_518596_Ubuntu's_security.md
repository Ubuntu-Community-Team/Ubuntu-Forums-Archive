If you want to take a bath in a bath top dressed in a life west, just to ensure you don't drown - those measures should be nice and provide you some extra security. 

Ubuntu comes as secure as you could want it, for home users. I have never done anything to increase on security. I just use my common sense when surfing the Internet. ### Post by alienexplorers on 2007-08-06
Have been running version 6.06.1 of Ubuntu since it came out in 2006.  I have found iptables the built in firewall works great in keeping me protected. I also have a DSL Modem with built in firewall.  The combination of the two catch everything trying to get in.  I also have never used a anti-virus program since there are only about 10 or so Linux viruses and I figure what are the odds of getting one of them.  The security holes are usually patched before I have to worry about them.
