### Post by azteech on 2008-01-06
Your system can certainly handle Xubuntu, and possibly Ubuntu, or other flavors of the Ubuntu family. Whichever direction you chose to go, recommend you upgrade your memory to a minimum of 256Mb. Without the added memory, system will be limited and slow to respond to certain processor intensive applications, such as OpenOffice or GIMP. But the good news is, you will have faster responsiveness than when it it running Win98. 

Case in point is my weekend project which i just posted the results for [here]("http://georgia.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?p=4083567"). Hopefully the project I posted will help you, and others during your endeavors.
