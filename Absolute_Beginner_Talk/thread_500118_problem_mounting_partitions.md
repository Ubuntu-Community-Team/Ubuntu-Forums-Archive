### Post by Inxsible on 2007-07-13
The first thing I notice in your fstab is that you have the same mount point for sdc1 and sdb2. Meaning you want to access both drives in the same place. No can do !!!

Change them to /media/sdc1 and /media/sdb2

As a side note: You should use pmount to mount your external drives. You dont need fstab entries if you use pmount and therefore it keeps fstab cleaner.

### Post by Inxsible on 2007-07-13
I am trying to answer your questions one by one as I understand them.

No you cannot use the name of the 'Extended Partition' to mount another partition. If sdc1 is your extended partition and sdc5 is the one you want to mount. Then use sdc5. 
Mounting sdc1 doesn't amount to anything as you wont be able to access the data in the logical partitions within the extended partition.

Read more about partitions on [this]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_%28computing%29") wikipedia page
