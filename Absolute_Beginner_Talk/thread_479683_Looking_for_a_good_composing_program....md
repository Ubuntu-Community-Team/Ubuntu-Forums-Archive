### Post by dancavs on 2007-07-08
you might find this link useful

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio/Applications]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio/Applications")

i think the term used to describe audio production in linux is "modular". meaning the use of different programs to perform different tasks. what holds all these different programs together is jackd, or qjackctl. via jack you can use for example hydrogen to produce drums, and perhaps qsynth or something for melodic stuff. 

apparently rosegarden is good for midi music production, it may be your best bet if your system can handle it. you may not need a low latency kernel. currently i am using straight ubuntu kernel and am having no problems with ardour, and my guess is it would place greater demands on system resources than rosegarden.

good luck
