### Post by NeoLithium on 2006-10-29
Well, actually, the command line is far more controlling of the system than a lot of aspects of the GUI interface; pretty much anything you want to imagine to do to your computer; can be done with the command line.  Most of the time, from what I've noticed, with people who are having problems and such; the people who reply to the forum generally give both command line and GUI instructions when possible; just to help the newer people get used to it.

It's pretty much a practice and learn as you go thing; really; moving files between your personal /home directory and / directories that require root permissions are really easy to do when using the command line, I find; and just general little things like that.

One important thing to keep in mind; which I learned the hard way; when doing thigs with superuser permissions, always make sure you double check your commands :)  I've deleted and moved config files and weird things by accident; but then again, nearly everything is also avoidable with a simple doublecheck of your command string, etc. When in doubt, just search the forum, google or post here as well; usually searching is a bit better cause not everyone can reply ASAP; and sometimes you can get the answer faster by hunting around...
