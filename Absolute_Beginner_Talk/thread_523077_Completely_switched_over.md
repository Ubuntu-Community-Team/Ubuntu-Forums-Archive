### Post by Baby Boy on 2007-08-11
This poster is proud to announce that after 2 days of struggling and rethinking the whole thing, he decided to wipe clean the Windows XP partition and become full Ubuntu :D. One reason I've been wanting to keep WinXP for is like in 99% of the cases - games; not those new games, but those Black Isle gems like Baldur's Gate 2 and Icewind Dale. But, I guess a man gets fed up eventually, no matter how good the game is (although I must admit I regret that I haven't finished Planescape: Torment and Baldur's Gate 1, heard they were great games; who knows, maybe one day you'll be able to play them properly in Linux). So now there was nothing holding me back from switching to Ubuntu 100%, and frankly, ever since I got Ubuntu those pop-up windows, spyware, laginess and whatnot that Windows offers made me sick and all I could think about when I was on Windows partition was Ubuntu ;). Besides, even though I want to *and* currently have loads of time to, I can't do active gaming anymore, it just doesn't hold my attention (reading e-books, learning to work in some programs and other more 'serious' stuff takes the majority of my spare time in the past couple of months). Don't get me wrong, I will still play games in DOSBox, SNES emulators and those that can run in Linux from time to time - Battle for Wesnoth has already caught my attention. 
Don't despair, this thread does have a purpose other than to share my joy of becoming full-blooded Ubuntu user. I have some quick questions about the programs I'll be needing:
1.) Can you read word, powerpoint, excel files etc. in open office alternatives and are the files you make in readable by Windows users?
2.) Are there any pdf to html or pdf to rtf converters?
3.) Is there a way to read .rmvb files in Linux? I used Media Player Classic in WInXP.
4.) And finally, what are the must-have programs for new Ubuntu users, say a top10 list. Besides the updates, drivers, and programs needed to make everything run of course.

### Post by Lucifiel on 2007-08-11
Hi, welcome! :)

Cdr/dvd burning: kb3. 
A great Pdf reader: kpdf. ;)
Also a great music player: Amarok(great sound quality). 
Also, if I were you, go get Swiftfox and install these extensions: NoScript and Session Manager . Firefox on Linux can be kinda painful for some users.
For an office suite, try OpenOffice or KOffice. 
For email client, Thunderbird will do just fine if you don't have a lot of emails. Otherwise, you might want to try Claws-mail or some other email client.

### Post by BobCFC on 2007-08-11
Welcome to the Ubuntu Community! 

You can surf in complete safety now.  Makes me chuckle when some nefarious website tries to download an .exe file on to my hard drive lol.


Applications are very subjective and everyone will tell you their favourite media player (xine) or cd burner (k3b). I would look through the categories in Applications-> Add/Remove programs and pay attention to the ones with four or five stars.  (This is only the basics there is more in System->Admin->Synaptic Package Manager,  plus you can download .deb files manually from web sites)

For a guide to installing popular software check out [The perfect Ubuntu desktop tutorial]("http://www.howtoforge.com/the_perfect_desktop_ubuntu7.04")
