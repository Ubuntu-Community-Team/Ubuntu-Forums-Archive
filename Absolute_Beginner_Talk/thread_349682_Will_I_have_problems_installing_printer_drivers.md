### Post by kebes on 2007-01-30
Typically printer manufacters do not bundle Linux drivers on the CD they give with the printer. However many of them do have Linux drivers on their webpages. Also, Ubuntu includes hundreds of drivers for the most common printers, so many printers are supported "out of the box" without installing anything.

If you have a particular model of printer in mind (already own it or are thinking of buying it), you should check the hardware compatibility lists to see if it works with Linux:
[http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi](http://openprinting.org/printer_list.cgi)
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportComponentsPrinters)

And like I said, checking the manufacturer's webpage never hurts.

Hope that helps.
