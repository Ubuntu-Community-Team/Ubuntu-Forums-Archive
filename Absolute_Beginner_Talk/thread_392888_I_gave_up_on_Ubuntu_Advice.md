### Post by brandoncolorado on 2007-03-25
Hi everyone,   

I will keep this short.  This is more of an advice post that anything else for anyone who may read this.  I just gave up on Ubuntu and reformatted after 6 months of battling with my Gateway MX3414.  The sound card is not supported and the wireless is not truly supported.  The video card has problems.  While I was battling this problem (I say battling because I spent a LOT of time dealing with the problems) I became frustrated.  I found myself being more mad at unknown people who were not helping me (probably because the problem was hard to fix).  I realize now that my biggest mistake was not buying a laptop that was actively supported by Linux to begin with.  When I bought a laptop, I looked for the best processor, memory etc., but I did not look online to see what was supported by Ubuntu.  I can say now that this was a huge mistake.  I am a law student and I sit in a room with hundreds of other students, and I wish I could demonstrate Ubuntu.  However, because manufacturers have not openly embraced Linux, I was left with a 1/2 functional laptop, so I have reverted back to Windows.  Rest assured, I will be buying a laptop that is Linux ready in a couple of years.  Until then, for anyone who reads this, do your homework.  Support the open source movement and everything it stands for.  Support the people who put so much work into Ubuntu.  Understand that every problem is not their responsibility, and that they are donating their time.  Also, understand that it is your responsibility to understand the pros/cons of Ubuntu prior to installation.

Thanks Ubuntu!!!

I will be back

Brandon:guitar:
