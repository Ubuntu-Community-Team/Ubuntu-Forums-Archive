### Post by Ireclan on 2006-12-17
Which drivers you will need depends largely on what hardware you have. If you have the right kind, you may not need any at all. I would do an install first and see what happens. Drivers are not flavor-specific, however the way in which they are packaged WILL make a difference. Always try to shoot for ones with a .deb or .gz extension; avoid .rpm, as these have to be converted first before they may be utilized. The only situatuion in which I forsee you having to mess with drivers (though keep in mind, I'm a Linux noob myself) is if you have dial-up.
