### Post by davidsrsb on 2007-07-19
Run the memory test from the install cd for a few loops. Memory manufacturers do NOT do exhaustive tests on their modules - especially the burst modes. It just takes too long to be economic for them.

Linux is very sensitive to bad memory, more than Windows, which seems to muddle on while silently corrupting disk files

That's a second from me, but use *more power!* If it's overheating, there's probably an automatic shutdown at a certain temperature. Don't be afraid to open that baby up. Just don't break anything while doing it. *Take your time!* Duct tape looks bad on a laptop. And use a good vacuum cleaner. Get one of those long skinny nozzles on the end of the hose. ### Post by djchandler on 2007-07-19
> **pyros said:**
> while excessive heat is certainly death to computer hardware, this is something that just started, don't you think that advising him to crack open his cased and vacuum it out might not be the most useful first step?

You are right about that, and right to caution the OP. It does depend on your level of technical expertise. I thought my mentioning duct tape would caution the inexperienced. It may be okay for me to do, but certainly somebody not used to dealing with the insides of a computer, especially a laptop, should probably have a professional do it.

Perhaps I was a little hasty in suggesting such an extensive cleaning. But I've seen the insides of lots of computers. Dust is the very worst enemy of hardware when it comes to keeping it cool and at optimal operational temperature. Before today's advanced BIOS protection, I saw a few dead computers with very dirty insides. It was quite obvious what the cause was. I haven't cracked a case yet that couldn't use a good cleaning, mine included.

There's no reason given by the OP to indicate he installed something that suddenly caused the computer to crash. He has plenty of power, although I am a little suspicious, as is pyros, of his using Beryl without his giving more specs on the graphics hardware. That is possibly overloading the on-board graphics subsystem, even if he does have 1.5 GB of ram.
