### Post by Threbus5 on 2007-02-03
Hi,

I believe that 49985 means that you have 50 Megabytes of RAM.

Ubuntu needs 192 Megabytes for an install from the live CD. 

If your computer actually has 50 Megabytes, then it needs more RAM.

If your computer has two open RAM expansion slots, for about $30 you could add two 128 Megabyte RAM cards (call a reputable online retailer and they can tell you the exact module that you need based on your computer and then ship them to you.)

If your computer has only one open RAM slot, you could remove the one that is installed and then install the two new 128 sized cards.

If you have no idea how to add or replace RAM modules, try the manual that accompanied your computer. Otherwise look for some how to's and illustrated procedures online or carry your computer down to a local store that installs RAM. (I am not plugging any particular store but some people take theirs down to a place such as Best Buy.)

Good luck and welcome to Ubuntu.

### Post by jml on 2007-02-03
A the risk of sounding dense, 425,984 megabytes of RAM is 425 gigabytes!  No consumer computer has that much RAM.  I agree with what another poster said.  For a computer with a 500mhz processor and a 20 meg hard drive, 50 megabytes of RAM seems more likely to be the amount you have.  And if that is the case, you do need more RAM.  Now its possible that you may be reading 425,984 bytes of RAM and that would represent about 512 megs of RAM, but its would be unusual for a computer of your vintage to come with that much RAM stock.  Although it might have been added afterward.
