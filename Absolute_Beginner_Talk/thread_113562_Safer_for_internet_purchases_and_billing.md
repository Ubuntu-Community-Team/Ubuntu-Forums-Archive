### Post by aysiu on 2006-01-06
[QUOTE=Cesium]Am I correct in assuming that Ubuntu is safter for online purchases and billing than if I use Windows XP (due to a lack of spyware/virus issues)?[/quote] You will not get spyware or viruses on Ubuntu by doing online purchases.  

> I'm just wondering if I should do my online purchases with Ubuntu instead of my XP OS. If the site you're visiting is untrustworthy, or if you open yourself (via your browser) to an insecure network, you may have your passwords or personal information (submitted via forms online) stolen--regardless of what OS you use. It's a browser/common sense issue, not an operating system one.

> 
Also, will "clearing my private data" in Firefox be sufficient enough for clearing out billing information and such? Private data means cookies, cache, passwords, and form information. All "clearing" it does is delete the copy kept on your hard drive. I would advise not saving passwords, not saving information entered, deleting cookies upon exit from Firefox, and using the Firefox extensions NoScript and Adblock. There are also [a number of anti-phishing Firefox extensions](https://addons.mozilla.org/quicksearch.php?q=phishing&section=E&application=firefox).

Use https whenever an online transaction involves credit cards or important passwords.
