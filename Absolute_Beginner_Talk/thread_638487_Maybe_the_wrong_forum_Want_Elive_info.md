### Post by jimmj43 on 2007-12-12
I've test-driven several Linux-based OSs lately, including Feisty, Gutsy (present), Sabayon, Mint, Puppy, DSL and MniMe,. In my[sharply]  limited experience, Elive stands out.  Nothing else Linux holds a candle to Elive. And that's with all due respect to to the super-sophisticated Sabayon. 

Browse through these help threads and you can't help but stumble over the myriad "video" problems/issues. Been there, done that with Feisty & Gutsy.. 

I dunno if I'm in the right forum, I took a flyer and burned an unstable copy of Elive.Then I ran it..... just from the CD. WTF??  A single click to connect to the internet was pretty much a given. But then, a Google search for Youtube found that Flash was already INSTALLED! Then off to various sites to explore other video formats. Click 'n play.. Done deal!

It gets better! I have an old, practically steam-powered computer: 566MHz CPU, 256 Ram and a dinky 6.5G HD. Yaknow those sexy 3-d animated desktops that suck RAM like there's no tomorrow? Eliive seems to have tamed that propensity. Go figure.

All of which, I suppose, brings us back to the original question: What's the best Linux-based (free) OS for a noob?

I happen to be an old fart noob, well into my sixth decade. I want something that will work reliably - that I don't have to fuss with. So how come Ubuntu distros seem to top the list for rookies? MINT has Skype pre installed for businessmen, And it's based on an Ubuntu distro. And it's stable. Puppy is based on an Ubuntu distro. It's stable and what kid wouldn't love it? 

Sabayon, slick and sophisticated as it is, fails in the area of support (where Ubuntu SHINES!!), MINT, though it might be stable, is as exciting as a rain-drenched newspaper.
On the other hand, Elive ignites a spark of excitement! along the same path! Howcome?

More to the point, why are noobs generally urged to suffer the travails of Ubuntu. when less-painful alternatives, Like Elive are around?
