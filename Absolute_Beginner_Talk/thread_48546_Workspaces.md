### Post by cwaldbieser on 2005-07-13
Yes.  Some people who have lots of applications open at the same time find this very useful.  I sometimes remote into work, and I like to put my remote sessions on the even workspaces and my local sessions on the odd workspaces so I don't get them mixed up.

When I'm not remoting, I like to keep my Web browser maximized in one workspace, my email in another, and an open console in another.

### Post by crashtest on 2005-07-13
Actually you can have more than 4.  I know a lot of people who use 6 or 8.  Use them to organize the windows you have open in any way that suits you.  You might have email in one workspace, web browsing in another, music player in another and so on.  It starts to get really useful if you have a lot of things open at the same time.

### Post by JoeUbuntu on 2005-07-17
I find workspaces incredibly useful as well, however is there any way to automatically (i.e. when the icon is clicked) move a program to another workspace?

---

### Post by varunus on 2005-07-17
You can set up keyboard shortcuts to move windows to other desktops from "system->preferences->keyboard shortcuts."  You can also drag the little thumbnail icon of the window from one workspace to another in the workspace switcher.
