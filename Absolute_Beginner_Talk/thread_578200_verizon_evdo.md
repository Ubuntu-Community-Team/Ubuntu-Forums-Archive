### Post by KCPokes on 2007-10-22
Speeds are really going to be dependent on your coverage in your area.  If you are in a EVDO area, shouldn't be bad; if you are in a Rev-A area, even better.  I would caution about going in expecting near broadband speeds though, as it really is going to be based on your coverage area.  I have a Rev-A card, not Verizon though, and it is decent speeds here, but when I traveled to San Fran on business, which does have Rev-A rolled out, it would fly.  Also depends on what kind of speeds you get today from your cable connection; we have a 7MB connection now, thus it would be hard to match that with EVDO.  

One other note of caution, in case you hadn't read the TOS for Verizon, is that their unlimited data plans are not unlimited at all.  If you plan on downloading large amounts of data you may run into issues as they cap their service around the 5GB per month range.  Do some searching around and you'll find others that have ran into this issue.  They also frown on using that connection for more then a single machine at a time, which means the routers you can buy for your connection cards are "illegal" according to their TOS.  My intention is not to steer you away from Verizon, rather to make sure you are aware of some of the limitations when you replace your high speed with a Verizon connection card.
