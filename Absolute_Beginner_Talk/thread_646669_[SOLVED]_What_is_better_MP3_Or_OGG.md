### Post by t0p on 2007-12-21
This is a contentious issue... among people who really care about audio quality and who have bionic ears.  Apparently, ogg vorbis gives better audio quality than mp3.  But the difference is very slight.  As you said, you can't hear any difference between them - not many people can.

In my opinion, mp3 is better, and I have reached this conclusion because of mp3's popularity.  Us Linux users can listen to oggs, but not everyone is so lucky.  There are a lot of people out there who simply can't listen to oggs, their software doesn't support it. And those people would be very surprised to learn that ogg vorbis is better - all they can hear, when they try to play an ogg, is silence.  So mp3 wins by default.

### Post by quandary on 2007-12-21
The question is: How do you define better?

Technically speaking, ogg is simply better, because:
- smaller file size (=better compression)
- good quality (=no difference to mp3)

Economically speaking, it depends:
ogg is a license-free format. You don't have to pay to use it.
MP3 was developed by the Frauenhofer institute in Germany, therefore requiring license fees for anyone who uses it (media player manufacturers..., game manufacturers, etc.)

Now if you're the Frauenhofer Institute (or a media player manufacturer receiving money or patent protection) or anybody else making money from it, using MP3 is of course better, but for anyone else, MP3 is bad...

From the user perspective:
If you use Linux, ogg is better, because it lowers download time (and storage space), and because you can play it by default.

If you use windows, ogg is very bad, because windows media player can't play ogg files by default... (it wouldn't be hard for Micro$oft to do so...)
(yes, you can install a codec-pack, because you anyway have to do that for all the video formats, too)
But really, that's allready too much for all the average-garden-variety-multimedia-computer-users with iQ < [xx]

So it's that: Windows has 90% market share in the desktop market.
So mp3 is the prevailing format.

That's the whole point. 
The reason for mp3 is, that people are stu***... - err mean "unintelligent"  and don't care if they have to pay $$$$$$$$, even it it wouldn't be necessary...
