### Post by parvinder on 2007-09-06
you might want to download a fresh ISO for ubuntu and burn it. There is no reason as to why it shouldn't work. 

Try booting first with any other known good CD and see if it boots from that. If not then probably problem is either with bios setting or your drive. 

If you are able to boot from any other disk (known good bootable disk) then probably your ubuntu disk is not burned properly. And burn the disk at the normal speed that should be okay. In case if you are burning at max speed read the max speed supported by the media it is printed on the disk packs mostly.
