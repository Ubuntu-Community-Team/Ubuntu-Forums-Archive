### Post by ajgreeny on 2006-09-06
I think there are quite a few isp's who suggest they don't support linux, but I don't think it will mean you can't connect, it just means that if you have problems with setup, they may not be able to help you at all.

Bear in mind that some of the isp's in UK supply usb modems which can be difficult or impossible to get to work in linux.  An ethernet modem/router is probably easiest to get working, especially if it's wired; wireless can be more difficult.
