Have you installed the Chinese language pack? One place to look is 
[http://zh.openoffice.org/](http://zh.openoffice.org/)
and another
[http://oootranslation.services.openoffice.org/pub/OpenOffice.org/2.0.1rc5/](http://oootranslation.services.openoffice.org/pub/OpenOffice.org/2.0.1rc5/)

> If you have not installed an Asian build of StarOffice/OpenOffice you 
probably need to open the dialog "Tools / Options - Language Settings - 
Languages" and check the box for Asian in the bottom section called 
"Enhanced language support".
(This e.g. will show additional entries in the "Format/Character - Font" 
tab-page and makes the dialog "Format/Asian phonetic guide available")
You may change your default document language in this dialog as well.

Aside from this you only have to check if you have the apropriate fonts 
installed.
(I don't know about the current arrangement but in the past fonts with 
Asian characters were only included in the Chinese/Japanese/Korean 
builds and not in Western ones).
