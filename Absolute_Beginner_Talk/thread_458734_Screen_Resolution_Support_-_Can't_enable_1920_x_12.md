### Post by Brightbelt on 2007-05-30
I have an ATI X1650, but have been through the same issue of getting my screen resolutions as high as they can be.  I didn't get the highest, because I still can't get my accelerated driver installed without booting to a blank screen, but I got the next to highest, which was good for me.

Instead of telling you what to do, I'd rather tell you that this is what I did and what worked for me. 

-I rebooted and went into the recovery mode...
- at the prompt I entered 'dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg' 

Then the text-based graphics reconfiguration wizard came up...Here it gets trickier, because you have a different brand card, but here's what I did:

-I was (correctly) instructed to choose 'Vesa' for the driver (this is the first menu you encounter)
-From there I chose all of the defaults, or as many as I could
- find out from your screen display in XP which resolutions are really available and that they work, and then choose these (with your space bar) when you get to that part of the wizard;
-For the X.org Server Modules, I enabled all of them except 'glx'
- You'll want to enter your specific Horizontal Freq. rate and your Vertical refresh rate towards the end. You can find these in your monitor's manual. 
- Then when you're finished with the wizard, do 'sudo reboot' 

Hopefully you'll have the higher resolutions you're looking for when you reboot. Good Luck,
Frank Bright

### Post by Brightbelt on 2007-05-30
There is possibly a quicker route which may work, since you have an Nvidia card; they supposedly "play better" with Linux. 

Go to your System Menu and choose 'Administration' and go to the 'Restricted Drivers Manager'. You may find a more advanced Nvidia Driver there that has not been enabled. Check the box to enable it and reboot. 

IF you get a blank screen, then the driver may have some compatibility problems. THEN, it may be the best plan to reboot and go into the recovery mode and do the steps I suggested in my former comment.

Good Luck!

Ok, I am going to say this right up front:  Don't use Automatix.

It causes too many problems.  You are relying on the creators of it to always keep you up to date, which isn't always the case.  Anything you want installed, you can do through Ubuntu, and if you can't, you can compile or even go down the easy road and download a .deb file yourself.  There are so many posts on this forum with people having trouble with Automatix.  The very first time I installed Ubuntu (nearly 1 year ago), I installed Automatix.  I had a lot of troubles with Ubuntu, and went back to Windows XP for a few months.  Now, maybe 6 months ago, I installed Ubuntu again, and just decided I needed to start learning things for real.  I compiled things myself and just spent a lot of time searching and reading solving each new "project" I wanted to complete one step at a time.  I suggest you do the same.  :)  Automatix really doesn't make things much easier, Ubuntu is already easy enough.
