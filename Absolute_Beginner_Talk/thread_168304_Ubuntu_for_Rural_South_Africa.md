### Post by Biltong (Dee) on 2006-04-30
We have a small electronics shop in Parow near Cape Town. 

Being part of Africa we don't have "throw away computers" nor do we have easy and cheap access to computers courtesy of the EU. In fact our bread and butter lines are fixing TV's from about 20 years ago (that are still in daily use) and fixing and selling 486 and P1 computers.

Not that the latest bells-and-whistles computers aren't available - at a price. Unfortunately our customers can't afford a new mouse, let alone the computer! (Nor can I for that matter - this is written on a resurrected Celeron)

So we buy PC boards, 3 gig hard drives, and any motherboards we can lay our hands on and we create what we can! 

The problem we have then is putting an operating system onto the computer for our customers. 

Think Africa. Our customers live in two roomed houses (if they're lucky) with one electricity point. Phones are non-existant therefore the internet is non-existant.

Up until recently we have been putting on Windows 95 but this is getting a dangerous thing to do, because as everyone knows we need to actually BUY Microsoft products, and we can't add the cost of that onto the price!

So we appeal for help.

Is Ubuntu the way to go? Is Edubuntu perhaps better? 

Most of our customers require three things - games (for the parents), a simple word processing package for their childrens projects and a box of floppy (stiffy) disks so that the childs project can be copied and printed out on the school printer.

We have 24/7 broadband access to the Internet at the shop and can set up the Open Office suite and games, but what OS do we use? Ubuntu or Edubuntu?

Any help from the international community would be welcome.

### Post by Sef on 2006-04-30
> ...but what OS do we use? Ubuntu or Edubuntu?

Since you have such a small hard drive, I would do a server install and then install fluxbox.  It's the lightest of the lightweight window managers.  You could try others (icewm, XFCE {Xubuntu}, etc.  and see if they would work.

If that doesn't work, I would look towards either DSL, the barebones of lightweight distros.  Others I would look at would be Puppy Linux and Feather Linux.

How much ram do the computers have?
