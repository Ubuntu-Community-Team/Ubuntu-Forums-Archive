### Post by Redenbacher on 2008-02-19
I would go with the windows and then dual boot. Just make sure it uses an nvidia card, and you should be good. Double check on the hardware in the inspiron for compatibility with ubuntu, but it shouldn't really be necessary, ubuntu has come a long way.

### Post by notwen on 2008-02-19
According to [Dell's Linux wiki]("http://linux.dell.com/wiki/index.php/Ubuntu_7.10") the Inspiron 530n does not have any hardware issues.  If you do choose to go w/ the Inspiron 530 w/ Windows installed, be sure to compare the hardware used in the 530n to make sure when you install Ubuntu you will have no issues.  Some of the XXXn models have had slightly different hardware than their Windows model.  I also have noticed since purchasing my Inspiron 1420n laptop that not all of the promotions going  on w/ the standard Dell PCs/laptops are being offered w/ the Ubuntu-installed models.  So you may wish to check for certain promotions and see if you're able to save money by purchasing a 530 w/ Windows over a 530n w/ Ubuntu.  

I have had very good luck w/ my Inspiron 1420n, Be sure to read over the wiki page I linked to earlier as Dell is doing a pretty good job on recognizing user's issues and trying to resolve them.  Good luck w/ your future purchase. =]
