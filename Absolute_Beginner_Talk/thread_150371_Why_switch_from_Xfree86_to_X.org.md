### Post by Pragmatist on 2006-03-26
The main reason for the switch, which occured in 2004, due to a split regarding licensing issues. According to XFree86:
[http://xfree86.org/legal/licenses.html](http://xfree86.org/legal/licenses.html)
> ...the main reason was to ensure that the Project and its developers receive their full due for what they have given freely away these past 12 years.

According to the X.org Foundation (the group that was formed in 2004 by many developers who left XFree86 due to a disagreement with it's new licensing ideas.  They are now the defacto X server for Linux distributions):
[http://wiki.x.org/wiki/XorgFoundation](http://wiki.x.org/wiki/XorgFoundation)
> [In a period between close to the end of 2003 and beginning of 2004, there were attempts from leading XFree86.org project members to apply a few restrictions on the existing 1.0 license of the upcoming XFree86 X4.4.0 release. Since not all (developers, distributors, hardware vendors) were able or willing to agree with and implement that new licensing policy in their code, their development process and their products, they consequently split up and rejoined in the form of the X.org Foundation.

As to why there is still the XFree86-4.config file?  I've no idea :)
