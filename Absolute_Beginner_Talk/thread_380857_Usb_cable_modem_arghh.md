### Post by HereInOz on 2007-03-10
If the modem supports Ethernet, then the best thing to do is to get an PCI Ethernet card.  They are not expensive, and for Internet, all you need is one of the old 10Mbps cards, and that will do fine.  

If you are short of cash, then go to the local computer shop, if you have one, and particularly if they do repairs or upgrades, and ask if they have an old 10Mbps card.  If they have one, they will probably give it to you.  If not, you may have to buy one.

Much easier than bashing your head against the wall trying to get a USB modem, for which you may not have the drivers, working.
