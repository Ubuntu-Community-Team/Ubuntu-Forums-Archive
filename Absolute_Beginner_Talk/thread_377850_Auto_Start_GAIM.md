### Post by TimJBart on 2007-03-06
Yes.  I have asked this question in the past.  The 'Startup' replacement is in   SYSTEM--> PREFERENCES --> SESSIONS.   Then click on the STARTUP PROGRAMS  tab.  then hit the ADD button, and type in "Gaim" to get it to boot on startup.

This thread discusses it further and tells you how to get it to start minimized.  You need to enable the "Extended Preferences"

[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=314141](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=314141)
