### Post by coffeecat on 2006-08-05
An alternative to Automatix is EasyUbuntu. That will install the mutlimedia stuff for you as well. There are sections in the forum to read about both - together with links. Your choice, although others may express a view.

If you don't want to use either of those, you need to enable the Universe and Multiverse repositories. (I can't remember which Mplayer is in.) Easily done. In Synaptic go to Settings > Repositories. Once you've enabled these repositories, you might want to investigate Add/Remove in the Applications menu. Somewhat unimaginatively named, it's a simpler program than Synaptic but for installing a couple of named applications it's a little easier than Synaptic for someone new to Linux.

One important comment. There are legal issues with multimedia apps, multimedia codecs and certain proprietary software. That's why they are not installed by default and why you have to jump through a few hoops to get them. It's not a matter of the devs being ornery. :wink:
