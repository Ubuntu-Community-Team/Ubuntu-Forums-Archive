### Post by rillip on 2007-05-14
The only hardware manufcturers I've heard bad things about is Broadcom and ATI.  And frankly, the ATI hateration that goes around is largely overstated; I've successfully run installs on three different machines with Radeons.  Now three is hardly an exhaustive test bed, but it seems most people can get ATI hardware working with a minimum of trouble.  On all of mine, regular graphics worked out of the box, but 3d support wasn't available and I had to install the drivers, which I followed a guide for and had no issue.

I haven't had first hand experience with Broadcom, but I hear there drivers aren't ported at all, or are done very badly, so you end up with a kind of hacked together generic version rather than something specific to their architecture, and you get the expected performance (i.e. often none, particularly on wireless).

I've installed all of mine on AMD Athalons without issue, across three different generic board manufacturers, so I really don't think those stats matter either.

Edit: As for 64/32 bit, I'd frankly stay with 32 right now.  The software support, i.e. apps, isn't really there to make much use of 64 bits, and I've heard uncomplimentary things about 64 bit OS's, both *Nix and Windows.  Simple fact is that it's new, and new techs have bugs.  Give it a little while to become mainstream, IMO.
