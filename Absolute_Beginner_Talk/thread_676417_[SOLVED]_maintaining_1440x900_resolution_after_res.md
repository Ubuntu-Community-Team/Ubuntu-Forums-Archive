### Post by TeaSwigger on 2008-01-23
Hello, 

You may need more expert help than I, but one thing I can suggest that you can do now.

First, that's the right file, congrats! Now, be sure to make a backup of this file before changing it.

Open this file with admin. priviliges, such as

```
gksu gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

Find this section:

```
Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"Generic Monitor"
	Vendorname	"Generic LCD Display"
	Modelname	"LCD Panel 1440x900"
	Horizsync	31.5-56.0
	Vertrefresh	56.0 - 65.0
  modeline  "800x600@56" 36.0 800 824 896 1024 600 601 603 625 +hsync +vsync
```

I dunno, I call 'em taskbars lol ... don't worry about the shifting of the stuff in 'em. What happened was the resolution changed, with it the relative positions, so it stretched out the bars and for whatever reason it doesn't move them to the same relative position... basically, just right click the items, unlock them if they're locked (that lets you move or remove them) and the next right click on them should give you an option to move; hit that and you can slide 'em around to your heart's content. 

A tip, right clicking on an empty area of those task bars gives you an option to add more stuff to 'em, from weather reports to sticky notes (post-its for the desktop :p ) to various geekly goodies. Just play around with it :)

Glad it's working at the size you want. That xorg.conf looks ok to me, tho' I'm no expert. If you can play videos ok, if windows aren't sticky, and you don't get any other hints the graphics rendering isn't what it oughtta be for that computer, then best to leave well enough alone I suppose. Oh here's a quick test; in a terminal, just enter this:

```
glxgears
```

What that will do is pop up a simple graphic of two moving gears. What they do is tend to show up problems with graphics. You just want to see them spinning smoothly, not skipping, hanging etc. If they are happily turnin', cool! Close it and if everything else seems ok, forget the graphics stuff and enjoy. :KS
