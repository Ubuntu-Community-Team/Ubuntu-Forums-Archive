This anti-eye candy thing perplexes me.
Windows has bloat, Linux just can't compete with that type of BLOAT.
I kind of take offense when I hear comments like that since I LIKE eye candy.
Why in the world would I want my Linux to look plain drab and dreary?
You can be upset about eye candy all you want, but a lot of us like a slick looking 
desktop, screensaver and cool icons.

I suppose that there are still a lot of purists still hanging on from the days of a black screen and, the 
joy of typing 20,000 lines of code, but make no mistake, in any OS you're the exception not the rule.
Call it bloat, eyecandy or screen dirareah if thats what tweaks your banana, a lot of us love it.

I for one don't love doing everything by hand, I think thats why they made mouses?

I was reading a post earlier where some guy claimed that you have to type a lot in windows too, to change this and that. No you don't, In almost all of the repairs, installs and msconfigs, spyware removal etc, typing has never been a common thing.

I never had to type to change net settings, screen resolution, desktop settings, icon arrangement or much of anything else except email and word procesor.

So, where I can ease my transition from Windows to Linux with a simple point and click GUI, I will, and where I can't I'll type. I suppose it's according to your likes and dislikes.
