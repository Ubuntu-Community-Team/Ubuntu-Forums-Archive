### Post by Frak on 2007-01-14
Umm... That would be very difficult as XboX's only have 64MB of RAM, Ubuntu requires at least 256MB for LiveCD, and 192 for AlternateCD, Xubuntu requires less, but not that less, the processor is more than able, and you have ~10GB of HD space.

But if you really want to, some major Modding of either the Xbox or Ubuntu, where the XboX would be easier, and I've done it, as I have a newer Xbox and Microsoft put Anti-Linux protection on it, I installed a Mod-Chip on mine to be able to boot between Xbox-Linux and Xbox, but I can only run maybe 2 apps at a time, before swapping occurs.

So either use [Damn Small Linux]("http://www.damnsmalllinux.org/"), or [Xbox-Linux, Xbox-Linux]("http://www.xbox-linux.org/wiki/Main_Page") being my reccomendation.
