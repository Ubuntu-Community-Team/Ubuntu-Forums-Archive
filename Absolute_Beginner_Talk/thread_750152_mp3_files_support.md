### Post by ibutho on 2008-04-09
Many distros (especially those that do business in the US) don't include packages that could result in lawsuits due to patents and other restrictions. If the distro maintainer has to pay for licences for mp3 and other formats, then it means that they can't provide the distro to you free of charge. Many enable you to install the required codecs from online repositories that are not located in countries where there could be issues with patents and restricted licensing.
