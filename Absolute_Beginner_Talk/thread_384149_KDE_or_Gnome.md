### Post by wxnker on 2007-03-14
I didn't like KDE much at first when I added kubuntu-desktop. I found it a bit messy but that was actually mainly because of Gnome. That is because the menu's in Kubuntu were full of Gnome programs and I found it hard to tell what a "clean" Kubuntu menu would be like.

So I cleaned the menus from most Gnome programs, which helped a lot. 
Later I did a complete clean install of Kubuntu Feisty and I absolutely love it.

In general I think that many KDE programs are better than their Gnome counterparts. Which is why I decided to try Kubuntu. I do think that "synaptic" is better than "Adept" though, so I use that in Kubuntu, 

BUT, then again, this is merely a matter of taste and temperament. 

One thing though. By adding kubuntu-desktop to Ubuntu you do not get to start your KDE experience with a "clean" Kubuntu. At least not before cleaning it up a bit. I hate messy menus so personally I prefer a clean install of Kubuntu and actually, there isn't that much more work involved. (I downloaded it while ZZZZZ....) \\:D/ 

I'd say,  Try kubuntu-desktop on your Ubuntu and if you like it but you find it messy, then try a clean install on a seperate partition some rainy day when you don't have anything else to do. :D
