### Post by rillip on 2007-06-01
If your CD rom is broken, how do you propose you install Windows?  It's easy enough to create a partition for it from gparted, but you probably would need to do so from a liveCD so the filesystem isn't mounted... and Windows only comes on CD... so....

And why do they need Windows to sort it out?  That's just plain stupid.,  What's wrong with the drive?  Does it not open?  Does it not light up?  Maybe you're having some issue with Ubuntu rather than the drive itself.  If it's legitimately a CD issue, then tech support doesn't really need Windows, they need to fix the hardware/replace, and that has nothing to do with OS.  They just want it because they don't know how to do anything to test when done most likely.

If it's not a real CD issue, they'll just tell you it's not broken and you'll have wasted you time and will come back here asking about the CD.

So, more information please!

### Post by gn2 on 2007-06-01
> **Simran said:**
> Hi,

I managed to break my CD-Drive on my laptop and the tech support people who are going to take it away need windows on it to sort it out, 

No they don't, all they need is the laptop so they can physically remove the optical drive and replace it.

You might want to back up all your data or remove your hard drive before you send it in, or the chances are it'll come back wiped.

If the laptop is no longer under warranty, you can either replace the optical drive yourself (easier than you might think) or get a USB one.
