### Post by NESFreak on 2007-07-25
yes , you can. From within the graphical installer you can choose to shrink your windows partition. This makes room free on you hard disk to install ubuntu to. (note that in order to do this, you do need to have enough free space left on your harddisk. You can then choose to fill in the free space you just made available to ubuntu automatically (root, swap, ed). After installing you get the choice to install Grub, grub will autodetect your windows installation and add it as a startup option. Now everytime you boot you'll get the choice to either boot win or lin. (pay attention that you resize your windows patition, not delete it, we wouldn't want to do that yet, do we?)
