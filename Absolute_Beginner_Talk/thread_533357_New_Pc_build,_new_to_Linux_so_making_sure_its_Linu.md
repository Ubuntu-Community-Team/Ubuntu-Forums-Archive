### Post by paper-sith2 on 2007-08-23
Here is my 2 cents on the hardware that you have selected.

Motherboard:  I would select a uatx MB, preferably running the 690 chipset.  The board you have selected is overkill and you decided to go with NVIDIA for video with a crossfire chipset.  The Biostar  T-force boards are very stable and the newer ones even include HDMI out.

Case:  That case is a mediocre case at best; I previously owned one.  It does come with a power supply, but you would be better off with a Cooler Master Centurion case.  A PS is not included but the case is tool-less and it will give you the option to select a better PS.

Processor:  Save yourself $80 and pick an X2 4000 (2.1 Ghz).  There is always the option to overclock if you want greater speed.  The thing to remember is the X2's are in the same boat as the P4's.  AMD is in a rut and they are just releasing processors with increased speed but no new horsepower.  If you are set on spending a little extra,  the X2-BE line is a solid choice and only consumes about 45W of power.  Selecting this processor will always give you the option to build a silent pc later down the road.

Hope this helps and enjoy the world of Linux.
