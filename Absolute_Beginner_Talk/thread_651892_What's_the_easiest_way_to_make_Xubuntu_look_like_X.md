### Post by yaknowwat on 2007-12-30
Yes unfortunately some people freak out about a change in Design Interface even if it is MUCH easier.  Gnome / KDE Basic design is much easier and nicer than window XP / Vista (Vista's start menu is Horrid)

Most of the Time you can get people to understand by simple Making large Icon's on the desktop and setting font size larger and bold.

Then placing their most Used Application On the desktop.

Another good thing to do is setting up a Shortcut Folder tree.

In the Home folder make a folder called
'.shortcuts'
This can be different make sure to put a " . " (period) before it so that its hidden.
From there use it for Short-Cut branch folders.

I've covered up names for reason's. though it still should be a good enough example.
You may want to label them according to what they are as some people get weirded out.

[http://img247.imageshack.us/my.php?image=screenshotsm9.jpg](http://img247.imageshack.us/my.php?image=screenshotsm9.jpg)

Anyways Those shortcuts to folders except the Downloads one lead to folders filled with shortcut in the ".shortcuts" directory.

This allows the supposed folder on the desktop to give the user access to all their documents etc organized and fast.
This is also much more secure as if the folder is deleted its really just a shortcut and nothing is lost other than a shortcut.
