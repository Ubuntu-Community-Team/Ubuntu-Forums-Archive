### Post by risbac on 2007-02-10
I tried my old 100Mo zip drive on Ubuntu last week. Worked without anything to do. Just plug it, insert a disk, and blink, icon on the desktop. To eject the disk, right click on it and there is an "eject" or "remove" option.

I guess it should be fine for a 250Mo as well. usually for this kind of very usual hardware, Linux has no problem, no matter how old it is. I won't bet my salary on it, but I'm quite confident...

To eject with a powered off system, depends of the zip drive I guess... No hole, no solution :)
