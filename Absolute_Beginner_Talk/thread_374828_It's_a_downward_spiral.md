### Post by Amenemhet on 2007-03-03
Well, it is called Edgy for a reason, and as a systems tech, from personal experience, I would never 'ugrade', always a fresh install. One other thing I always recommend is to partition your drive so that when things get screwed up, you have only a small (reletively speaking) partition that has the os on it, and all your programs and data on another. I have been running edgy for a wee while now and apart from some wireless issues, stemming from a private network I need to connect to occasionally, I have had no problems at all. At home i use wireless exclusively.
