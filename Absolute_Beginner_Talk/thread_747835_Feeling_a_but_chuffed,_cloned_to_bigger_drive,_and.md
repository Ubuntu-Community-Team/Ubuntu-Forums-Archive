### Post by SlappyPappy on 2008-04-06
My big problem was that I had a bad IDE cable. Are you kidding me? It's a freaking cable! How can they go bad?

Well after I got that settled I got my DVD player running now in Totem, and gxine so maybe it isn't gjunk afte rall? Still won't play in Xine. I can't get that thing to find my DVD drive. Also put in VLC for fun and it plays really well the old DVDs. So far the big strikeout is Xine.

Then I thought I might as well go for the bigger drive with the better ATA and faster RPM drive. This turned out to be easier to clone than I thought! Only thing I think went bad was a UUID for the swap but that's an easy fix.

I used this forum post and followed pretty much what he said to the letter. 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=599599](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=599599)

Made a matching size partition on my second hard drive with gparted and a swap. dd the stuff over which didn't take too long. Then I installed grub. Booted up and it worked great. Then I rebooted with the LiveCD and made the partition the remainder of the drive. Booted again and noticed the UUID was different for the swap, fixed that.

The cloned drive is way faster so windows snap open now. Really glad I made the effort. I'm watching Heat as I type this so the DVD is working great, as is music. Got my printer a cheap brother laser up with no hassles. 

I now know way more, I mean a ton more than I ever wanted to know about OSes but hopefully the info will come in handy if I do have a problem. Right now though I just want to use the OS and do some work on it. I don't want any problems.

Thanks for all the help here. I hope my experience helps a few of you out as well in helping others. A freaking IDE cable?  :lolflag:
