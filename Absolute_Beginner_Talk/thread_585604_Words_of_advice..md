### Post by ArcadeHooligan on 2007-10-21
For every new or potentially new user out there who is coming from Windows, I'd like to give you my two cents:

When you switch to Ubuntu, realize that you don't know squat.  The only knowledge that you should carry over to your new environment is your understanding of how microcomputers operate and the basic GUI concepts that you know.  Have that mentality, and learn how to do things just like you had to do when you first started using Windows; you'll have a really easy experience.  You'll be productive and have all of your compatible hardware working in no time... Ubuntu has a great Help file built in and IMO the Ubuntu Community is second to none.  (look at my post count, :)  I used the search function)

If you start to think that* this* or* that* is so easy to do in Windows & why isn't it the same, you're just going to get upset and frustrated.  If you look at everything with an open mind and the realization that you're simply a beginning user and you really don't know anything, you'll have a much more desirable experience.

:lolflag:  The only reason I'm posting this is because I found this out the hard way.  Now, I'm a very happy Ubuntu user.

### Post by HermanAB on 2007-10-21
Yup, I studied Unix post graduate and I too found Linux confusing at first.

The trouble with Windoze is that you only learn how to use a GUI - click here, click there, say a prayer to Bill Almighty and click OK.  That doesn't tell you how anything really works!

On Linux, you can also use GUI Wizards, but frequently you need to look at error messages to get an idea of why or where the problem lies, which may be a connectivity problem outside the Linux box - the router, the Windows firewall and so on.  For immediate error feedback, nothing beats knowledge of some basic console utilities: ping, traceroute, nmap, tcpdump, telnet...

Cheers,

H.
