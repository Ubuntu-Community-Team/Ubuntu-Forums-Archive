### Post by eternicode on 2008-01-04
I don't use gutsy (yet), but here's my best guess:

Pre-release repos will give you access to the latest builds of bleeding-edge apps, such as FireFox 3 beta.  These are probably not garanteed to work flawlessly, well, or even at all.

Unsupported, I'm not sure about.  It could be proprietary, third-party software (such as non-open-source drivers).

EDIT:

From [WikiPedia]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backport") :
> Backporting is the action of taking a certain software modification (patch) and applying it to an older version of the software than it was initially created for. It is part of the maintenance step in a software development process.

The simplest and probably most common situation of backporting is a fixed security hole in a younger version of a software.
