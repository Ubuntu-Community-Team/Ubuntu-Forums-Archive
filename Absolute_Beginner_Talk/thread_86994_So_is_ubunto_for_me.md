### Post by Nightwind on 2006-02-05
> I know this isn't the best medium to be posting this on but there are thousands of PC forums that I do not have time to register for only to get sub-par answers and at least this forum has a great reputation for 1 on aspect of computing.

I wouldn't say Ubuntu is always easy as there are some issues that require some work, research, and extensive reading on your part. Some things to consider are: If you are going to have wireless, USB, need the i686 kernel, the configurations of any other machines, exactly what you want from Ubuntu and how much effort you want to put into it. Your own confidence in your skill level and patience is a must. Some hardware works some doesn't.
If you don't have time to register on other forums, I'd say you don't have the time to work out any bugs. This is a good forum but not the only one. All forums are about the same. Don't expect anyone here to have all the answers to all your questions and problems and certainly don't expect any answers to come in any hurry and don't expect them to resolve all issues all the time. There are good people here that try to help, they do their best to help and solve problems, but it is a forum.
You could use paid tech support and probably get any issues ironed out faster but that's a call that you'll have to make.
So it depends on how much time, effort and yes in some cases money you want to expend into your project. Just because an O.S is "free" doesn't mean it's free, some people have no problems and some have all the problems. 
So your best bet is to look in to the supported hardware 1st, make a list of it, make a list of what you expect from the O.S. and how much you want to invest into it. Then do some research, I think one of the biggest problems I see on all the forums is that folks hear about this great _FREE O.S _and how easy it is with out reading the facts and making sure it will work for them.
