### Post by Udaho on 2008-01-13
If you put a Ubuntu disc in your drive and restart your computer it will run a session that you can try out with installing anything to your hard drive. Browse the web for a little bit, watch youtube, etc. You'll get a good idea of the issues that you might see if you were to install it permanently.

One thing to consider is that if you are willing to work through any initial kinks Ubuntu is a better system for a non-technical user, especially in terms of avoiding problems with viruses and security. I think it's worth the trade off.

There is a big community of people out there willing to help you out if you decide to give it a try.

### Post by bumanie on 2008-01-13
This may not be a popular reply, but as I have had major hassles with ubuntu 7.10, but none with the previous 7.04 version, on an older machine, I would suggest using ubuntu 7.04.
Despite my own prejudice, I am aware that there are many who have got 7.10 going well on older machines.
