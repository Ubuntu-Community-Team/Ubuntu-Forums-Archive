Net neutrality is going to have to strive and fight these things politically.  I am no expert and I feel i should become better informed on the subject.   Laws attacking net neutrality in the US are often under the guise of "to protect the children". Kind of like the boiling frog story, which states that a frog tossed into hot water will immediately jump out, but if he's placed in normal water that is slowly heated, he'll stay until he's boiled to death.

### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-04-19
> **Craftycorner said:**
> ...Then there is the splitting up of the Internet as we know it into private PayNets or Channels like cable TV got cut up into where you gotta buy packages to get content...or is the Net already split up?

Here's a link to a site called...

[http://www.savetheinternet.com/](http://www.savetheinternet.com/)

...which is an easy to use place to post letters supporting Net Neutrality to your Senator and Congressmen/women.  It also explains more about why Net Neutrality is important, in order to keep the playing field even, and not squelch communication or innovation by turning the internet into something akin to cellphones, where you get socked roaming charges if you view content on another "network".

Users already pay for web-hosting, web-surfing, and for pay-to-view sites.  What the cable/fiber companies want to do is split up the internet into separate networks that each owns ("my pipes"), and you'd have to pay to join certain ones.  They'd control the speed and content at a more fundamental level then ISP's already do.

Now, if you look at what they've done with cable TV, from when Cable TV first came out, you got some channels, and they were ~$30/mo.  The cable/dish companies figured if they "provide more content/channels" they can charge more.  In theory, this makes sense, but the content/channels they provide are fluffy junk you could care less about.  100 channels of public access garbage, 15 shopping channels, etc.  For this "basic" package, you pay $50...  Why?  Because you're getting "200 channels of entertainment goodness!"  But, you want Skintimax, movie channels and stuff you REALLY want to watch.  So, that's another $25+ depending on how many additional "premium" packages you add on.

In watching all these channels, whether on a dish or cable network, you'll notice a pattern, which falls along these lines.  They play about 4 - 8 hours of real content during the day, then rerun it the rest of the day until midnight, when they switch to Paid Infomercials until 6am, to which they switch back to real content again, which is usually reruns anyways.  And while you have 20 different movie channels, they usually rotate the same movies between them, so there isn't much selection.  About the only good thing is that most of what you want to watch can be selected "On Demand" and viewed whenever you want, sometimes having to pay additional fees to do so.

So, you're paying tons of money for what looks like tons of content, but isn't really (proportionally) as much as you used to get, and you're paying for them to play Infomercials 6 hours (or more) out of the day (!)  

Now, imagine if the web turned into that.  Or if the web turned into a huge cellphone-like network with all kinds of loopholes and fees to charge you extra for surfing.  Yeah, sounds like a great idea.

The government is in a bit of a catch-22, because in the past when they haven't regulated something, it was bad.  But also, when they over-regulate, it's bad.  What they want to do, with lots of lobbying from the cable/fiber companies (ATT, Verizon, etc) is to basically hand it over to them and let them decide how it would best work out "in our best interests".  Essentially, they're handing over our freedoms (the freedoms the net started with, which made it the precedence going forward) to companies to dictate for us.  The cable/fiber companies will dictate this all-right...they'll dictate it in such a way that they squeeze more money out of you then they already get from contracts with ISP's to service you.

Personally, I want Net Neutrality, and I'll vote with my dollars.  I already pay enough to my ISP and pay-to-view sites as it is.  Why would I want to hand out more $ for it?  The cable/fiber companies argue that it's "their pipes" we're using, so they should do with it as they please.  However, the US government dumped tons of money in subsidies to fund the fiber build-outs of all the cable/fiber companies, providing free access to public land for private / business use, etc.  But, the cable/fiber companies want to act like they did it all themselves, so you they should have full say.  Annoying...
