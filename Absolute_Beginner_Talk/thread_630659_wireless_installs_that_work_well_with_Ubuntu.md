While you can run ubuntu on 256MB of memory, I would advise bumping it up to atleast 512MB (more if you can).

I have 2 desktop systems using wireless at home.  They both have a PCI Linksys g card and are both supported by Gutsy out of the box.  Look for a card with a Ralink 25xx chipset and you should be good.

### Post by Sarteck on 2007-12-03
Before I bought my video card, I had the very same question, and I was told to get an EDIMAX EW-7128G.  It has a RaLink RT2500/RT61 chipset, and from what I was reading at the time, it was supposed to be fully supported under Linux, and that was the main reason I bought it.

However, I have had a lot of trouble getting it installed and working correctly.  It would intermittently just totally crap out, and I couldn't access anything (Internet, local network, nothing).  However, I did find a lot of great resources (thanks in large part to the help I received here at the Ubuntu forums) that helped me getting it working properly, and I have not had any problems whatsoever since.

If you don't mind an initial hassle (to get it working properly), I would recommend it.  However, it might not work (at least not all the time) "out-of-the-box".
