### Post by seanUSXIII on 2007-06-26
You can do it, but certain things like flash are a little tough. Just forum search "64 bit flash"... You'll see. If you want to spend an hour or more configuring your new system, feel free. I however would suggest 32 bit for the time being until 64bit codecs are easy to get. I use 32 bit on my AMD 64 X2 and I don't notice *too* big a difference when I boot 64 bit. I do notice that CPU intensive stuff like transcoding video goes faster, but for average computing, its not very noticeable.

Just my $0.02

Sean

### Post by Wynne G Oldman on 2007-06-26
I agree with the other replys. Go for the 32 bit version for now. I'm a newbie, and have tried both versions. They're both as easy to install and use as each other, but you will run into problems with not being able to use quite a few things that you may want to with the 64 bit version (it's the same with Windows). You probably won't notice any difference in everyday use, but the 64 bit version will be quicker on processor hungry apps.

I'll throw in my $0.02 with everyone else.  I have 8 machines in my house; all are 64bit machines except 1. I use both the 64bit version and the 32bit version.  I too am a newbie and need handholding on many things.  I found it easier to install and configure the 64bit than the 32bit.  The flash and codec issue is a non-issue really and takes less than 10 minutes to work out by simple downloading and clicking on a script.  Everything installs and you're done.  At least that's how it all went for me.

Issues I personally have with Ubuntu of ANY flavor aren't related to the "bitness" of the OS.  Things like video card drivers, wireless drivers, printer drivers, scanner drivers, default font settings, etc... have nothing to do with 64bit or not.

I say if you have a 64bit machine then install the 64bit OS and learn how to use it.  You won't regret it at all.  You will have some stumbling blocks regardless of 32bit or 64bit so you may as well go for the 64bit.

You're going to get an unbelievable amount of help here to solve almost any issue under the sun, so take the plunge.

Just my $0.02
