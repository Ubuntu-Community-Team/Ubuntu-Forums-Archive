### Post by linuxlizard on 2007-10-22
That would be helpful for beginners. 

Most things are pretty easy to do though.

To get you started with your list-

to go windows free and install ubuntu- pop in the cd, turn on your computer and it will load to ubuntu desktop with an icon that is labelled install. Double click it, and away you go. Select "use entire drive" and your done with the hard choices. 20 minutes later it will tell you it's done, you turn off the computer, remove the cd and boot to your new ubuntu.

to go online, if you have cable or a dsl connection, simply locate firefox in the applications menu under the internet submenu and click on it.

to download files off of the web, it's just like windows, nothing new there. In firefox, you simply click on the file you want.

to view your drives- when you boot to your new ubuntu there will be icons on your desktop for your drives you can click. You can also find them under the places menu in the top left side of the screen on the panel.

moving and copying files can be done just like in windows in the file manager (known as nautilus which will appear when you click on the drive icons).

to play a cd, you pop it in the cd drive and it will autoplay

open office writer, located in the application menu in the upper right hand corner of the screen, under the office submenu, is an excellent word processor that comes standard with ubuntu, so click on it and you are good to go.

 If you want to install another (If the menus in open office are a little confusing, you may find abiword's menus more familiar) you again click on the applications menu in the upper left corner of the screen and at the bottom of the list you will see add/removes. Click on that and 
add/remove applications window will open. On the left are categories like games, education, office, internet, programming, sound and video, etc. Click on the category you want (in this case office) and a list of available software will appear in the right hand window. Select what you want from the hundreds of free programs, click apply changes, and they will be downloaded and automatically installed to your computer free of charge and added to your appropriate applications submenus. Doesn't get any easier than that- way easier than earning money, driving  to the software store to spend it, ripping open the packaging, popping in the cd, clicking yes yes yes to half a dozen questions and then clicking ok ok reboot, like in windows.

Many printers will auto-install in the background. My HP printer did. Didn't have to click a thing, just turn the printer on and there it was.

If not, the first place to set it up is under the system menu in the upper left hand corner of the screen, in the administration submenu, click on printing. A window will pop up and the first choice is add printer. Do that and it will give you a list of manufacturers and printers to choose from.

Really, windows people IMO make too big a deal out of it. Most stuff is simpler than windows, but it's a little different so you have to keep an open mind about it. 

The command line can be a little scary for beginners, but most things it's used for is stuff beginners aren't doing anyway. 

Another great place to look for info is in the tutorials/how to sections of this forum. And the search function answers almost everything. If not there are a lot of happy users who will assist you.

Hope this helps and have a lot of fun!
