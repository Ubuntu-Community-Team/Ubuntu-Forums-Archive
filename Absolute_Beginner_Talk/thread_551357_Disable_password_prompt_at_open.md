### Post by Spr0k3t on 2007-09-15
In Power Management, check "When laptop lid is closed".  I've got mine set to "Blank screen" but ymmv.  In the screensaver dialog, check to make sure you have unchecked "Lock screen when screensaver is active".  This should fix your problem.

### Post by MyR on 2007-09-19
i own a dell inspiron 6000. i had these settings as described above and unfortunately whenever i closed the lid the screen frequently refused to turn back on and when it did i was prompted for my password, which i did not want.

now i have the "lock screen when screensaver is active" in screensaver prefs unchecked and "do nothing" selected in power mgmt prefs. when i close the lid the screen turns off and when i open it again the screen works and doesn't prompt for password.
makes me happy :D
