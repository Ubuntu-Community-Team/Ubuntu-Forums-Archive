### Post by Ek0nomik on 2007-04-11
You should present your ideas as if you were submitting them to your English teacher.  Makes it a bit easier to read, easier to help you, and I think you'll find people would be more willing to help you.

**Remote Desktop**:  You can use Vino (It comes with Ubuntu via System/Preferences/Remote Desktop).  I personally dislike Vino.  Linux has a bit of work to do when it comes to Remote Desktop.  Many people here will argue that Remote Desktop is a Windows thing, but I find it very useful.  Here is a link to what I currently use:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=363236](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=363236).  You can also try SSH.  I find SSH quite useful, and I hope to just abort Remote Desktop and use SSH at some point.  Here is a link:  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SSHHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SSHHowto)

Do you mean **KDE vs. Gnome**?  I know this is a lame move, but:  [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNOME) & [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE).  They are two different desktop environments.  I don't really know the pros & cons of each, it just seems to be a preference really between users.  Many people like KDE, while others prefer Gnome.

**Clock Screensaver**:  I don't personally use screensavers, but I am assuming you checked the default Ubuntu screensavers.  If there isn't one there, the best you can do is search the Ubuntu forums or Google:  [http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&q=linux+clock+screensaver&btnG=Search](http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&q=linux+clock+screensaver&btnG=Search).

**Login**:  System/Administration/Login Window & Security Tab.  Something like that?  Unless your usename is insanely long, it shouldn't be to big of a hassle to type it, but to each his own I suppose.  :)

**Networking**:  I haven't messed with any networking on Linux yet, I just use FTP to transfer things for now.  I think an application named Samba should be able to help you though, but I can't be certain.

**Terminal**:  I just did a Google search for "guide to linux terminal".  This may help:  [http://linux.org.mt/article/terminal](http://linux.org.mt/article/terminal)

I hope I helped a bit.  Good luck, and if you have some other questions I will try to help.  Just remember:  Searching the forums is your best friend, even if it doesn't huggle or give you kisses, embrace it.  :)
