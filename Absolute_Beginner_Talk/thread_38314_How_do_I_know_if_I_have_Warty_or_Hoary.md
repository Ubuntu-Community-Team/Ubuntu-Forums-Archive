Uhhh... you installed an OS with no idea which version you installed?

*ahem* sorry. okay.  Well, when you boot the computer, it should say Ubuntu 4.10 (Warty) or Ubuntu 5.04 (Hoary). It would also be on the disk you received, or the name of the .iso you DLed and burned.

You can also check your etc/apt/sources.list file, that'll list your version at the top of the list.

Plenty of ways, pick your favorite.

### Post by KLineD on 2005-05-31
Press CTRL+ALT+F2 and that takes you to a console screen. There you can read "Warty" or "Hoary"

To return to graphical mode press CTRL+ALT+F7

Hope it helps ;)
