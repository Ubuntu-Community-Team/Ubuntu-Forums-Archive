### Post by flowingfire on 2007-06-11
You might try [http://www.sourceforge.net](http://www.sourceforge.net).  It has all sorts of open source software and a good deal of it is for Linux.  Of course, lots of it is in development and simply not ready for the big-time yet.

I think repositories kinda take the place of download sites in the world of Linux, though.  Like, if you download Synaptic Package Manager through Adept, you'll have more stuff available.

And if you download Automatix2 from the Internet and use it as a package manager, you'll have even more stuff.  Be careful w/ Automatix2, though.  People have horror stories about the wrong packages breaking their installation of Fesity.

But be sure you have all the up-to-date repositories installed.

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-11
If you haven't enabled the extra repositories in [COLOR="Blue"]Synaptic[/COLOR], do so.

There are a huge number of packages there.

To enable the extra repositories just open Synaptic, click on[COLOR="Blue"] Settings>Repositories[/COLOR] and click on all of the boxes, except sourcecode.

While your there, click on the [COLOR="Blue"]Download From[/COLOR] drop-down menu and see if there is a server closer to your location.

Of course, you can dowload software from places, like Sourceforge, but remember that it is usually sourcecode and you will need to compile it and sort out dependency issues yourself. This is something I would leave alone for a while, until you get a bit more comfortable with te OS.
