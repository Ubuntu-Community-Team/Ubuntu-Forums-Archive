### Post by K.Mandla on 2007-03-29
+1 for the click. 

From my perspective, it follows the Xhosa and Zulu language groups where the X is pronounced by placing the tongue against the ridge behind your teeth, inhaling slightly to create a vacuum at the rear of your mouth, then forcing your tongue away, creating a clicking noise. 

This is different from the Q consonant, where the tongue is pressed against the rear of the hard palate, and emits a more hollow, deeper click (a sound commonly used to urge horses). Or the C consonant, where the tongue is pressed against the teeth (the "tsk, tsk" noise common in some Western cultures).

[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_alveolar_click](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateral_alveolar_click)

Of course, all that being said, it's probably just an ordinary X sound. :roll:
