### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-03-12
The bottom line with this issue is that you get to choose which suits you best.

I suggest that you load both, play with them and choose the one which you feel more comfortable with.

Yes, if you have the minimal components installed, the vast majority of apps will work on either platform.

There are also several other window managers, like Enlightenment, Fluxbox, etc, but if I were you, I'd stick with GNOME and KDE, until you get your head around things.
