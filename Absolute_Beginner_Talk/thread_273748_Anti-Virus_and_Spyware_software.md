It's generally accepted that such nasties only afflict Windows, and no A-V or Anti-Spyware is required.

Ubuntu comes with a built-inFirewall, called IPTables, and can be controlled with Firestarter, which is a graphical front end.

Anti-Virus for Linux is available from AVG (Free Edition) and also Avast has a Linux vesion. Also Clam AV.
Only required to prevent passing on viruses to windows, Ubuntu is virtually immune (so far)

For general home use, it just isn't needed.

### Post by catlett on 2006-10-08
It is possible to have a linux virus but I do not know of any. The bulk of viruses are targeted at Microsoft systems and just like your windows driver will not work on your linux computer neither will your windows virus work on your linux computer.
The only real reason to have an anti virus on linux is to not be a "carrier". You can infect windows' computers if you have a virus on an email etc.
clamav [http://www.clamav.net/](http://www.clamav.net/) is a good one, it should be in synaptic package manager
