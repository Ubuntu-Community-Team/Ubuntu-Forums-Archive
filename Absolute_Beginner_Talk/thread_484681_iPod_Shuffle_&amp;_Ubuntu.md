### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-27
The standard white ipod shuffle works well with several Linux music players.  I've never encountered errors with the current library that is used to address the ipod.  You can drag and drop files, you can play files on the shuffle as you see them in one of several music players.  It normally works plug-and-play.

Shuffles do have a problem with the firmware getting borked.  The green-yellow flashing light is usually an indication of a borked state.  You used to have to send it in to Apple to get it unborked.  I had one that borked and they sent me a new one.

Apple released an updater program that wipes the shuffle and reinstalls the firmware.  This should unbork your player.  I have not used it, and I know it is released on the Mac platform.  I can't speak to how well it works under Windows.

I have a theory that the plastic casing of the shuffle creates static electricity that puts it into a borked stated.  I use a waterproof case for mine, and it keeps the moisture out--the most common mode of failure--sweat.

The Shuffle is worth keeping because it has balanced output circuitry and it can drive reasonable volume levels--making it good for boomboxes and other amplified applications.

Good luck.
