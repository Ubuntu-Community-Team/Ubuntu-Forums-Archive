### Post by skwishybug on 2007-05-12
It depends really on what he is looking to do. A quick search revealed a few landscape design programs available for windows in the $30 - $50 range. If he is not planning on changing his landscape each year, it might be a bit of a lot to invest.
 
From personal experience, the best way to design a landscape is good old paper and pencil. Take some measurements (foot steps work, if you know how big your foot is) and plot out what you'd like to see.
 
Programs that model what your garden will look like are usually way off since stuff never grows the way the picture shows it.
 
If you are going to have an on-going need to have a specialized program (such as a landscape design package) you might want to look into creating a partition and dual booting.
 
And since you mentioned, what type of wireless card do you have? Some can be tricky, but usually can be figured out.
