### Post by qamelian on 2007-09-25
Cinelerra is way over the top for the average home users needs. It's meant for pro-level video compositing. My favourites at the lower end of the spectrum are Kino and LiVES (Linux Video Editing System). Kino is in the repos and an Ubuntu deb of LiVES can be found at [http://www.getdeb.net/](http://www.getdeb.net/).

The closest you're likely to find to Quicken is GnuCash, also available in the Ubuntu repos.

### Post by rac56 on 2007-09-25
There is not a native version of Quicken for Linux.  I have run Quicken 2006 under Crossover Office (commercial Wine implementation), but I have not tried it in Wine.  It worked pretty decently in Crossover.

As for open source software, GNUCash was the one I liked the best, but it was very unstable for me, aborting constantly for no apparent reason.  I wound up buying Moneydance and have been very happy with the choice.  It's not quite up to Quicken in features and functionality, but it's fast, stable and very usable.  For around $30, I regard it as money well spent.
