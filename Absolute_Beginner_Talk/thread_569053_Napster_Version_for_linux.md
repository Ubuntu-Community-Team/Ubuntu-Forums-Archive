### Post by Puppy fam on 2007-10-19
RyanZec I got some good news and some bad news. The bad news is there is no version of Napster for Linux :( . The good news is mjitkop is correct! Let me tell you my set up.

I have a computer that has Ubuntu and Windows on it. I go into Windows just for software like Napster(software only for Windows). But now instead of not being able to listen to my Napster songs in Ubuntu, I can just go to napster.com, click on sign in on the top right, and after signing in I get my whole library just like that. The really cool thing is that I have a Napster to-go subscription. So I can listen to just about any song Napster has!

So for you. I do not know if you dual boot with Windows or not. If you do then you could have a set up like me (I also have an mp3 player and I have to pay like $15 a month for this service). Or you can buy per song. Or you could even have the same set up that I do but not be able to put songs on a mp3 player and just be able to listen to the songs on the computer.

However you want to use Napster is up to you. Just a few things you may want to remember are: If you don't have Windows to be able to download the Napster software then you will not be able to burn C.D.s, watch videos, or put songs onto your mp3 player. You will just be able to listen to the songs with the Napster on-line version.

NOTE: This is just how I understand Napster. I could be wrong about a few things. I also am not completely sure that Napster has unlimited listening and unlimited to-go. They may just have to-go.
