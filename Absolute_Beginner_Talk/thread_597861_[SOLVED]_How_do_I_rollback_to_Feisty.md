### Post by overdrank on 2007-10-31

Hi if you use this command it will let you modify your xorg
```
gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

Edit but you may want to back up your xorg before you modify
To copy Xorg
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf-backup
To restore backup
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup /etc/X11/xorg.conf

7GB Gutsy
7GB Feisty
1 GB SWAP
REST DATA

So, when I upgrade from Gutsy to Heron I'll do a clean install of the OS by overwriting the Feisty partition. Make sure it all works. Then change my grub default. Data always stays the same. You can either mount it as a volume on your desktop, or else create simlinks to it in your home folder. Anything ever goes wrong, I just edit grub and boot to my "old" stable ubuntu.
