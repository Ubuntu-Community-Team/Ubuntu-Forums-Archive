### Post by jarocooke on 2007-05-24
> **JC_510 said:**
> Wait for the deals that Dell do every month or so, then you might be lucky and get money off your model, and possibly free shipping (I've seen this before).

Anyone know any places to get Ubuntu laptops to be delivered in the UK?

Yep, I bought a laptop with Ubuntu installed from [www.EfficientPC.co.uk](www.EfficientPC.co.uk) about two weeks ago.  To be honest I wanted to get a Dell, but living in the UK it became obvious that they weren't going to be available for a while and without even knowing how long I would have to wait, I just got impatient.  Linux Emporium also sell them, but they have a rather limited consumer laptop range and the webcam doesn't work (at all).

The laptop took about a week to arrive after first order, which is pretty good for a small company, and I thought that the customer service was excellent (an advantage of small companies).

The models offered are fairly good value for money I think and they do have one extremely handy bonus.  Getting a webcam working in Linux can be very difficult, these laptops have a built in 1.3MP webcam, which is supported by the standard Ubuntu kernel.  No tinkering necessary.

You don't get tier one manufacturer's quality, but after two weeks of using it for hours and hours every day, it is working perfectly (touch wood - its never a good idea to push your luck).

Another benefit of this laptop is that it comes with a Nvidia graphics card WITHOUT TurboCrash, sorry I mean TurboCache.  This means that you'll be able to use Beryl or Compiz without the maddeningly annoying Black Windows Bug.
