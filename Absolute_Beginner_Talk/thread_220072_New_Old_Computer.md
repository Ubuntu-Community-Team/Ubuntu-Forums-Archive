### Post by ferebee on 2006-07-21
>   I live wayyyy out in the country and our only choice in ISPs is dial up. 

I'm out in the sticks too, and high speed is only just coming our way, at twice the price of dial-up unfortunately, so no high speed for us.

Check that your modem isn't a winmodem, linux as a rule doesn't
like them. If you can, get hold of a hardware modem. I have a second hand external serial modem and it's never met a variety of linux that it didn't like.

[This page]("http://start.at/modem") has some info on which modems are generally known to be supported.(It's a bit out of date,but a good place to start.) You could have a search through the forums for specific models, see
if ubuntu users had any troubles with them.

Also see [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/DialupModemHowto") for the Ubuntu Community documents page on modems.
