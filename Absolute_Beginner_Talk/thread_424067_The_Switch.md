### Post by mrfr0g on 2007-04-26
Preface of my computer::

Intel P4 2.4Ghz Processor,
1.5 Gb Ram
Nvidia Gfx 5500 256 Mb
80Gb HD 7200 RPM, not sata unfortunately :(

So it is a fairly older system, I purchased it around 2 years ago and I do need to upgrade.

So last night I was trying to create a dual boot with Ubuntu and Windows Xp, which shouldnt have been a big deal. Until I messed up the master boot record on my hard drive that is. For those of you that don't know, the MBR is the part of the hard drive that tells the computer where the Operating System is (Windows, Linux... etc.) I tried to recover it, to no avail.

Formatting was my only option. At that point I decided that I was indeed tired of XP, and wanted something... better. Vista (Also windows) was, and still is a memory hog. The only reason that I would want it would be for the Aris(Aeris?) 3d desktop feature. Luckily Ubuntu had a solution, Beryl. That was what my last post was about. What you saw was a 3D Cube, literally showing my desktops in real time. I can spin the cube on all axis', without any issue, while installing WoW and other packages on several different desktops. It also has a really cool mouse over feature, in which I can mouse over my minimized running programs, and see them in a small thumbnail window in real time. If I decide to roll my mouse over to the upper right corner, it pops up all my open windows in a small thumb nail view, so I can easily switch between them.

The biggest issue is of course my addiction, World of Warcraft. It is true that it did not play as well as in Windows, but I must say it's made a HUGE improvement from the previous version of Ubuntu. It is to the point of being playable. Slight lag in the cities, but in the open area its beautiful. What I really liked was the ease of the installation. From everything that I had read online, I was worried that it would become this huge issue. It installed without any issues what-so-ever. Even the expansion, I had zero issues with it. I literally popped the disk in, and it worked. Then I worried about the bug updates, like everyone does. Again, zero issues. They downloaded, installed, and were ready to go.

What I'm really excited about with Ubuntu is the ease of installing packages. Beryl was incredibly easy to install. Ubuntu detected that my card was a Nvidia GFX 5500, and told me that I could use restricted drivers for it, if I wanted. Overall it's just the ease of use, the ease of transition that has really done it for me with Ubuntu.

I have this list of 10+ sites, that offer several performance tweaks for Linux + WoW. I want to play with OpenGL drivers to, to see what happens with that. I really want to "show up" windows as it were, by making my WoW play better on Linux. We'l see what happens, but needless to say, I'm excited. I feel like I'm finally done with Windows, and it feels good.
