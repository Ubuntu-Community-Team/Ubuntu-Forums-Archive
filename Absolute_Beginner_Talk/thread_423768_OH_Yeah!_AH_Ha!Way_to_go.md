### Post by tact on 2007-04-26
Hey there big kiwi...

When you installed ubuntu the Open Office suite would have been installed at the same time.  (Applications>Office menu)

In my work i have to work heavily with spreadsheets, documents, powerpoint presentations etc.  All the people I have to exchange documents with use MS-Office (as I did also a few months ago).

I find that the Open Office that comes with Ubuntu has had ZERO difficulty in handling any of the thousands of documents I have created/used over the past 7yrs, and has ZERO difficulty in opening any of the documents I have to work with every day from MS-Office users.

I am sure that there must be some weird incompatibility somewhere but I have not found it yet in my very heavy day to day use of Open Office to work with MS-Office users.

I would also let you know that you dont even need to do any "conversion" step.  Open office just opens any kind of MS-Office document itself.  You can even set Open Office to save any files in MS-Office format by default.

Another cool thing is that Open Office can save your documents directly in PDF format!   Unlike MS-Office.  Windows users who have to find some clunky freeware PDF converter, or pay $$ for the real thing.   

Oh ya..  printer support - you will likely find that your printer driver is already available in Ubuntu.   Click on System>Administration>Printing and "Add new printer" then select the right options and  select the right model.  I just peeped and there are a dozen Canon Pixma printers supported by default.
