### Post by d1337 on 2006-03-17
I have installed Ubuntu on quite a few machines, both desktops and laptops, using intel or AMD.  While there will likely be a few bumps in the path, I don't think that Ubuntu should cause any major issues as long as you don't mind taking your time and reading.  To begin with, is MS Win already installed?  Do you by chance have a live CD version of Ubuntu?  I've always just jumped in knowing that the end result, regardless of the means, was going to be having Ubuntu (or another distro) installed.  Take your time...

A couple of pointers, if MS is already installed, make certain that you defrag before partitioning.  If MS has been used much (is the machine new or used) and you have defrag issues, there are several free defrag programs available that work much better (in my opinion) than the MS preinstalled defrag tool.  DirMS comes to mind and runs in windows...registration required for full version but it gives you a 30 day trial (or $10 for 5 licences I think).

Think out your partitioning scheme before you start the Ubuntu install.  Do you want to share files between the two OS's?  If so, you'll probably be better off to use a more advanced partioning tool from a lived CD like Knoppix or SystemRescueCD (based on Gentoo I think).  QtParted is an awesome tool for this.  If you are not worried about sharing, then determine how much of your 80G you want to leave for MS and how much you want to have for Ubuntu.

These are just a couple of pointers.  This forum has some incredible resources as far as the users.  A bit of searching and quite a bit of reading is you best bet.  If this is your first dual boot and you want to get it up and going in an hour, I think you'll be dissappointed.  If you take your time, make wise decisions and spend time researching, you can make a solid dual boot machine.
