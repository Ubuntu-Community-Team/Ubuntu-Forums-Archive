### Post by rplantz on 2006-01-03
I had been using Quicken on my Mac for several years.

When I exported everything for import to gnucash, I found several errors. It took me a while to locate the problems. Fortunately, everything was in text format, so I was able to fix things with a text editor.

Quicken never caught my errors. The double entry system of gnucash caught them.

So far, I prefer gnucash. There was a moderate learning curve, and I reorganized lots of theings. And I continue to work on it. But now it simply makes more sense to me than Quicken.

The next challenge is to see how gnucash works when trying to transfer things to a tax preparation program. I suspect that I will have to create a report and enter the numbers by hand. Not a big deal. The upside is that it will force me to check everything.
