### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-10
> **Solarise said:**
> Whats the compatibility like with games etc?

Just to be clear, Ubuntu is not a cheap windows shell that will run your latest games. Its an entirely different platform/OS for you to use all the time. So it isn't a windows replacement and don't come into it expecting it to work like it.

That said, it does plenty of things. Why should you want to use it?

Never pay for a program again, instead of thousands of dollars for photoshop and illustrator you get gimp and inkscape for free :). You'll never be treated like a pirate again, in fact, we WILL do everything in our power to encourage you to hack Ubuntu to death :p You can customize your own Ubuntu look, you can get free support here on the forums (never be on hold again for 30 minutes just to end up in India).

There are lots of reasons, but the most important thing I have to say is if your happy with XP and have no patience, don't switch. Ubuntu will likely give you a big culture shock once ya get it installed and try to use it constantly, not to mention that you'll most likely have at least 1 or 2 issues with getting it fully functional.

### Post by MarkX on 2007-05-10
Get yourself a second hard drive (old cheapy used would do), unplug your main hard drive. Install Ubuntu on it. That way your xp remains untouched and you don't have to worry about uninstalling dual boot thingies.
You can use your spare cheapo drive for testing a variety of distros fearlessly.

Oh, make sure the plug thingies on the back of the drive are set to master, not slave (that's only for using both simultaneously).

### Post by kragen on 2007-05-10
If you are into gaming then you won't want to scrap your windows installation just yet. There are a number of good (if simple) linux games, and many windows games run fairly successfully under wine, but in general gaming under linux is not as good as it is under windows.

If you want to give linux a try, then I strongly recommend Ubuntu. There is a great community here, with plenty of people willing to answer questions, and Ubuntu focuses on working "out of the box" a lot more than many other distro's, so the chances are you will have fewer problems.

### Post by The Minder on 2007-05-10
Here's my two penneth worth.

I totally agree with previous comments about the difficulty of playing games (especially the high end ones) on Ubuntu vs Windows.   However, there is another scenario I think you should consider and that is the multitude of 'business' applications that just don't sit well with Ubuntu.   I'm not necessarily talking about custom build applications say for running a drug store, or a real estate office or the likes, but mainstream 'business' applications like Quickbooks for financial management.   I damn near drove myself insane trying to get Quickbooks to run under Ubuntu and ended up conceding defeat.

My recommendation (for what it's worth) is to use Ubuntu where ever possible (I love the distro) but if there is ever the case where an M$ application just HAS to be used, then stick with windows.
