### Post by gate on 2007-10-30
My experiences:
   Intel 950 on board (Edgy and Feisty) worked well enough but 3d acceleration and the wide screen resolution took some work. (Fixed in Gutsy, works out of the box, THANKS GUYS). 

   Nvidia 7300 (Feisty): Worked out of the box with restricted drivers manager, don't remember ever having a problem. 


   ATI 850XT (Gutsy): Works out of the box with restricted driver manager. Haven't done alot 3d acceleration yet, but Compiz works beautifully.


   Nvidia has better support now, but ATI is making waves that could mean they take over in that area (releasing significant amounts of documentation for free drivers) so who will have superior support in a year is up in the air in my mind (ATI hasn't finished the job, yet). Best course of action, find a card that fits your fancy in power and money, and google it to see how well supported it is.

 Good Luck!

### Post by daengbo on 2007-10-31
Right now, older radeon cards are well supported for Compiz and the like, working out of the box with the open driver. All Nvidia cards are as easy as choosing to use the restricted driver. Intel 950 and before (to the 810) are supported out of the box with the open driver.

Newer ATI cards should be well supported soon enough -- about a year from now, but that doesn't help the present.

My preference is to go with Intel first, because the drivers are complete and open, then for NVidia, because getting them to work is dead easy, then for ATI. ATI has had a lot of bad history for a lot of people, so it may take time for the order of preference to change.

Hope that helps.
