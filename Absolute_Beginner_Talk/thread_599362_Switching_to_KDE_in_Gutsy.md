### Post by mummra1 on 2007-11-01
I don't know about you guys, but the GNOME desktop in Gutsy for me was slow, unresponsive, and took forever to come up from the login screen (almost 2 minutes).  I switched over to KDE last night.  There is a nice tutorial here:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/purekde")

This shows you how to install KDE, and completely remove GNOME.  It is important to completely remove GNOME, otherwise you'll end up with a hybrid GNOME/KDE bizarro environment that will still be slow and clunky.  In my opinion, KDE is more responsive and intuitive, and runs better, at least on Gutsy.   Took about one hour to complete the whole transition (KDE install + GNOME removal).   Of course, you will lose your bootsplash screen (that is, if you ever recovered it in the usplash.conf to begin with:)), but you can install splashy in kubuntu just as easy to have your own customizable bootsplash...:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=41709]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=41709")

Best of luck!
