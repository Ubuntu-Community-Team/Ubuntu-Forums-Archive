### Post by ftmichael on 2006-12-21
I bought _Linux for Dummies_ ages ago, and have since sold it, but it does give you some basic insight into how to use the terminal and how the file system is structured, if nothing else.  (Bear in mind that it's based on Red Hat, though, not Ubuntu, and I'm not sure that Red Hat and Ubuntu have much of anything in common.)  I wouldn't advise buying it, but it might be worth finding at your local library and flipping through.

A much better resource is the wiki at ubuntu.com and these forums!  And the assorted IRC channels on freednode, of course.

### Post by r76 on 2006-12-21
I too was looking for books, not all of us work well exclusively from online guides!  I found the following books really good (both are on the amazon page linked above, and were in my local bookshop):
The Official Ubuntu Book by Benjamin Mako Hill et al.
Beginning Ubuntu Linux: From Novice to Professional by Keir Thomas

The second one helped me get my broadcom wireless going when I was ready to give up.  :-D 
Although they will always be more dated and much narrower in scope compared with the online help, they really can help some people get going - but look for them in the shop/ library before deciding if you want to buy them.
