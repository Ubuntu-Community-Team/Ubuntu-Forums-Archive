### Post by atraeyu on 2007-03-16
Gaim supports ICQ and is preinstalled on Ubuntu 6.10 - and it's great.  Give it a try.

Rhythmbox seems to be the default mp3 player.  I've been using it and I like it (though I've recently been listening to pandora.com pretty much exclusively).  MP3's are a restricted format and therefore you'll need to install additional codecs.  I believe it they were in one of the gstreamer packages.

I've been using gxine (was not preinstalled) to play dvd's - I believe I had to install an additional gstreamer codec as well to play dvd's.

Most of your personal data is stored in the /home/yourusername/ directory.  I'm not sure about application data.

### Post by mahy on 2007-03-16
Icq => Gaim (I use it on windows too)

Disk encryption : dunno, definitely supported, but no point-and-click utils AFAIK

Dictionary: Lingea Lexicon (me)

Disc burning: not so great stuff. however, k3b and gnomebaker are satisfactory

Picasa works on Linux. Period.

Winamp => Xmms, Amarok, gXine, VLC, whatever

Clearing internet traces? Are you serious? Purging the browser cache is not enough for you? Look, there's no registry here and no MRU lists.

Sorry, don't use VPN

VMWare works FLAWLESSLY. Not opensource, but free as beer/parking. (Same as on windows)

InterVideo WinDVD => VLC Player

RDC => ssh, vnc

USB modems are very tricky, it's best to steer clear of such stuff. (not a Linux's fault!)

Your personal settings (not the apps themselves) are stored in /home

For partitioning there's GParted (Linux) or Acronis (Windows - my favorite).

Please note that some of the above mentioned apps are not as nicely looking as their windows counterparts, but their functionality is on par.
