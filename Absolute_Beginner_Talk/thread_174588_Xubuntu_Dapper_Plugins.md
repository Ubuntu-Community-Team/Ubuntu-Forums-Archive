### Post by nalmeth on 2006-05-12
Do you mean a desktop 'plug-in' display like gdesklets?
The only xfce specific CPU and data display (that I know of) build into xfce is in the panels.
Right-click, then select -add to panel- and add what you need
Check screenshot, I've got CPU and RAM display, I've disabled the other stuff.

Edit:
To change the icon, just right-click the launcher, go to properties, and you can change the icon from there.
When you click on it there will be a little bar with 10 different icons, but pick ... to choose your own.
All the system icon's are kept in /usr/share/pixmaps
