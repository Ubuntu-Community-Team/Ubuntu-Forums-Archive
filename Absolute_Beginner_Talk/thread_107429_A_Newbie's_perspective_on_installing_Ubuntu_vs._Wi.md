### Post by Dr. E on 2005-12-23
I just finished installing Windows on one hard drive and Ubuntu on another.  My experience?  It took me hours to install Windows during which time I had to reboot at least 8 times, after each time a new batch of updates had to install.  I also include the time needed to download and install antivirus and antispyware programs, printer definitions etc.  I have a Compaq version that came with my machine that is loaded with junk (trial versions of programs, internet provider ads etc. that I had to manually uninstall and get off the desktop.  There are actually 8 disks to load!

How did Unbuntu compare?  I only booted once during the process.  It recognized all of my hardware (except the Lexmark X125 printer.)  I was immediately able to get on the internet, and check my mail after configuring Thunderbird.  With some help I got my Windows disk mounted so I could read from it.  In fact, that was the only thing that took some line commands to accomplish.   ( I actually feel that so much of the online information discusses this sort of command that it gives people the impression that everything is done that way.  Most of what I did was done simple with the GUI.) I set up accounts for my family members and they immediately started using it.  The only problems they encountered were online games that require Shockwave.

So, who wins?


**WIndows drawbacks:**
1-Slow and painful install.
2-Had to use hardware's CDs and online support to install some things.
3-Annoying to have to reboot so many times.  (Why can't all the upgrades be found and installed at the same &*%$$ time?)
4-The antivirus programs are constantly asking whether to allow certain programs I have never heard of to access one thing or the other.  How am I supposed to know?
5-Windows froze twice while getting everything working.

**Windows advantages:**
1-All software, harware,  and games work.

**Ubuntu advantages:**
1-Quick install.  I was instantly able to use many programs including the Terminal Server Client (Remote Desktop).
2-It is easy to navigate the graphical interface to find what I need without much hassle.
3-Upgrades were made with ONE keystroke!
4-Sometimes the answer is easily found on the forum and most people are extremely willing to help.
5-No rebooting.
6-Ubuntu never froze up.

**Ubuntu disadvantages**:
1-I found the drivers for my printer but it requires a tweak which is explained in detail for Red Hat, but is of no help for Ubuntu.  Pleas for help on this forum have produced nothing so far.
2-Lack of Shockwave-type program
3-Lack of a complete manual.  The online manuals were somewhat helpful.  Searching through pages and pages of the forum is sometimes extremely time consuming when a quick look up from an indexed manual would yield the results immediately.
