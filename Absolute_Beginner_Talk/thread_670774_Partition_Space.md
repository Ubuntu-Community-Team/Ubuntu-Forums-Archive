### Post by PeterJS on 2008-01-17
Assuming that you're dual booting you can get away with a pretty small parition for ubuntu and put all you big media files on the windows parition. 10Gigs is a pretty reasonable number for a small install. I'm working with 20 gigs and never had any space concerns. If you've got the space you can certainly go higher.

### Post by PeterJS on 2008-01-17
> **firsttimeubuntero said:**
> how higher can you go? someone told me you can't have Ubuntu in a really big partition because it won't recognize it, is this true? or is there no limit for how big your Ubuntu partition may be?

I've never heard that. Generally the accepted upper limit for a partition size is the size of your disk, unless you want to deal with RAID or lvm, and then it's a question of how much total disk space you can get in to a box.
