### Post by jonny on 2007-01-24
I wouldn't recommend that guide for beginners - it's intended to facilitate installation of the very latest bleeding edge nVidia drivers and there's no reason to believe that the current Ubuntu drivers won't work.

You need to do two things:

1. Follow the instructions in the Ubuntu wiki: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/BinaryDriverHowto/Nvidia).  That will give you the official nVidia drivers rather than the slower open source nv drivers.

2. If you still don't get the right screen resolution presented as an option, type this in a terminal:
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
You won't understand all the questions but don't worry - accept the default and move on.  When you've finished, restart your computer and all will be well.

### Post by jonny on 2007-01-25
I absolutely stand by my advice that David should first attempt the official drivers.  Beginners shouldn't use unofficial repositories unless there is absolutely no alternative or they're prepared to have to do a full reinstallation when things go wrong that they lack the skills to fix. 

The bleeding edge driver might work well now, but will it survive a kernel update?  What will happen when it's time to upgrade to Feisty and beyond?  What happens if the unofficial repositories are no longer available?  Any of these scenarios could leave a broken system with no GUI interface.  I could fix that easily, but I'm willing to bet that few beginners would know where to start.

My advice remains to try the official route first.
