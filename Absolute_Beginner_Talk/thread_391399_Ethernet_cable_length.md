### Post by Icidic on 2007-03-23
Yep.  A 30m ethernet cable will work fine.

The theoretical limit for Ethernet Cabling (Cat 5\Cat 5e\Cat 6) is exactly 100m.  However, I have personally seen people exceed this limit, with a ensuing stable connection as well.  However, 100m is the limit, so after that - you're on your own :) .

### Post by mcduck on 2007-03-23
CAT5 100m, CAT5E 350m, CAT6  550m, CAT6E 1000m. These lengths are for UTP cables, with STP cables you get a bit more.

When buying new cables it's better to get at least CAT5E, as CAT5, by standard, only supports max speed of 100Mbps and gigabit ehernet is getting more and more common..

No - The TS standards are 100 meters (95 horizontal and 5 for patch leads). There is no way ethernet will run over any copper cable at 350m . For distance you require radio, glass or dsl type extenders.
