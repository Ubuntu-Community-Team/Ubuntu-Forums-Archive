There is [wine]("http://www.winehq.com/"), which you're probably heard of, but if that utilities CD has anything to do with drivers for your camera, it won't work in wine. In any case, special software exists in Ubuntu to work with digital cameras.

If your model uses USB storage, you should be able to mount your camera/memory card reader like a USB flash drive. Word of advice - just copy pictures to your computer, don't delete them. Use your camera's formatting function for that.

If it's the other sort of camera, the program you want is [gtkam]("http://www.gphoto.org/"), which you probably already have installed if you're running Ubuntu (as opposed to Kubuntu or Xubuntu). Try plugging in your camera to see if it comes up automatically.

Once you have access to your pictures, you can browse them in gthumb (Ubuntu) and edit them in GIMP.

HTH
