### Post by scruff on 2007-06-27
I just want to say I am very impressed with Ubuntu. A little background on me... I have used Linux exclusively at home for the last 6 years or so. I started with Redhat 7.x and didn't really like it. Eventually I found Slackware and really dug that distro. After awhile though I wanted more control over my OS and newer, bleeding edge packages so I went to Gentoo around the 1.4 days (back when stage1 installs were still the norm... 2003?). I completely fell in love and used Gentoo ever since. My kids and girlfriend use it as well.

Recently, I have been reading all this hype around Ubuntu. My good friend whom I turned on to Linux 2 or 3 years ago left Gentoo for Ubuntu. I ragged on him a bit when he did, but whatever . At work I administer a few *nix machines, and write code on said boxes so I am around Linux most of my waking hours. Reading all this hype prompted me to install Ubuntu on my work laptop (Levino X60) as an experiment. 

Now I know I said I am around Linux all day (even our product runs embedded *nix), but its still primarily a Windows shop. Well I used a Gparted live CD to resize my NTFS partition (amazed it worked flawlessly and didn't bork my windows install), booted the Ubuntu CD and installed and I am completely amazed! Everything works right out of the box! Sleep, hibernate, all keyboard hotkeys, video, sound, etc etc etc. I am not used to this... 

My general experience with Slack and Gentoo were me making everything work from the go. Not that I mind this or am uncomfortable with it. I enjoy building a custom kernel and compiling/configuring drivers. Its how I learned enough to get the job I have. However, THIS is Linux for Jane and Joe next door. It is also freedom for me to not *have* to do all this manual work anymore. I install, and everyone has what they want after 10 minutes in the package manager GUI.

I was even more surprised at stuff I didn't even know Linux did. Like it knows the battery charge in my wireless keyboard. The calendar in the menubar integrates with my Outlook appointments. Speaking of, Evolution seems to have matured into a true drop-in replacement for Outlook. Now my experiment has changed. I am going to run Ubuntu at work for 2 weeks straight and see how well I get along in a 99.9% windows environment. I even have my C# code running under Mono!!! 

I used to rip on my buddy for switching, but now I think I am off Gentoo (a terrific distro btw) and on to Ubuntu :)

-Scruff
