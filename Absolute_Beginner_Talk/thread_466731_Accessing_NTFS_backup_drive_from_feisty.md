### Post by ecs_pf5 on 2007-06-07
Resolved. Unfortunately I don't know how.

I logged out from Ubuntu, rebooted into XP. Fdisked and reformatted the 'new' drive as NTFS, using a proprietary Windows program, to see what would happen.

Rebooted back into Ubuntu as plain user, ran 'Applications' > 'System Tools' > 'NTFS configuration tool' again on a whim, and it reported that it had found a new partition to configure. Went to check the box, assuming it to be the disk I'd just reformatted... but it wasn't, it was my main backup disk, the one I've been having all the trouble with.

All is hunky dory now. Both of the IDE NTFS disks are showing up just fine & are fully read/writeable,

Thanks for helping me through this, I'd have given up halfway through otherwise.
