### Post by Kobalt on 2007-06-25
About your optimization concerns : it isn't harder to tweak Ubuntu, but it's just a bit less required than using other operating systems. Nonetheless, if you still want to improve a little bit the speed of your system here is [a very good guide]("http://kmandla.wordpress.com/2007/04/22/howto-set-up-feisty-for-speed/"). I actually consider this one being the best one out there. First because it lists tips that actually work, and second because these are tips that won't completely break off your system without warnings or explanations before.
A couple of advices of mine to finish the optimization : installing/uninstalling softwares can create a messy system, hence a slow system. If you want to clean a few things, [check this out]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=140920"). 
Then, don't do anything you don't understand or anything you feel you are not able of recovering. You may read many tips'n'tricks around here, and some may be unsafe... Getting a fast system is one thing, keeping a stable system is another.
