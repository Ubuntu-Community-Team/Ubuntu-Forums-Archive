### Post by russell.h on 2007-03-04
In ubuntu you don't "log in" as root. You can modify all the settings and whatnot from your own account. When you open something that requires root priveleges it will prompt you for a password. Just enter your own password, and you will basically have root permissions for... 15 minutes I think?

You can also preface commands in the command line with "sudo" for non graphical apps, or "gksudo" for graphical apps.

I highly recommend [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php) - it explains just about everything.

Good luck!
