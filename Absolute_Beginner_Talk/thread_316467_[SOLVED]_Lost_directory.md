### Post by lhtown on 2006-12-11
Are you trying to install a proprietary driver for an otherwise unsupported printer?

The normal way of installing a printer is to connect your printer, turn it on, boot up the computer and go to System>Administration>Printing and then double click on the new printer icon and then follow the directions.

You can go to linuxprinting.org to see if your printer is supported and the best driver to use.
