I have been a PC guy my entire life. I flirted with switching to Mac, but i like my hardware cheap and custom. But i still might switch in the future. I have also been testing differnt distros on and off for the past 2 years. And i never found one that could work with my wireless card. Then i installed Dapper Drake on my laptop, and it worked out of the box, i was jumping with joy. I think this is the first distro worth looking at (for the desktop). I am currently installing differnt apps that i need, configuring Automatix, and installing XGL. Then i will transition all my files over to this computer. And i will setup a windows VMware machine, CodeWeaver, and Cedega for Gaming, Certain Applications, and Windows Programming.

I have just a few quirks. How do i turn off the tapping funtion on my mousepad. Also if i want to take an image of my drive, and install it on a another computer, how do i do that.

I think this is an amazing leap forward for the linux desktop. I tryed to play around with openSuse 10.1, which has XGL and Xen. But it didnt work correctly. Ubuntu is like the Mac OS X of the Linux world. I think if they can implement a stable version of XGL/XEN/Mono/Beagle in their next release, with Automatix already installed, and just an upgraded UI. That will be amazing. You will have more consumer adoption, and small business adoption. And smaller PC vendors will start offering it. But what Ubuntu really needs is for Cannonical Ltd. to make a really advanced support system, and partner network. And they really need to make a deal with HP, Dell, and Lenovo to include it at least on the Workstation, if not some low end desktops.

Linux has a long way to go, but compare it to Microsoft, who started in 1975 and took until 2007 to release Vista, a advanced desktop OS with amazing GUI features and to develop a Stable server OS and apps. Now look at Linux, it started in the 90's and is already catching up with Microsoft. Thats the power of open source. In the next 10 years open source is going to take the lead in innovation. But strong business models, and corporate backers are needed for it to succeed (sorry hippies). I see the 3 dominant Linux companies to be the following, in order of power, RedHat, Novell, Cannonical, Xandros, and Mandriva. RedHat and Novell are going to be aquired by IBM and Oracle. Xandros and Mandriva arent that powerful, but they do have a presence. That leaves Cannonical to be alone with Ubuntu. They need to fully develop their Desktop and Server lines. And grow. The next 10 years are going to be excitin, i cant wait!
