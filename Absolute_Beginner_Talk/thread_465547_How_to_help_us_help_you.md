### Post by lazyart on 2007-06-05
We were all first timers once.  We all asked questions and have built up a ton of answers.  And tomorrow more first timers will show up and ask questions.  Hopefully they search the forums first but they don't always do.  But we still hope.

As the legions of us who choose to help scour the boards for questions that we might have answers to, we undoubtedly come across some thread titles that really give us no indication of the content.  Not only is it a bit frustrating when they come up, they don't do much to help the next person who comes along, searching the forums for the bit of information they need to fix a white screen, X server crash or file sharing fix.  YOUR question and the resulting discussion may solve an issue for hundreds of people, but instead the answer was buried in a thread with a title that gave them no clue.

Examples of good thread titles-

X server won't start after upgrading video card
No window borders when starting beryl
Help! Black screen after kernel update

If I'm having a problem and I see one of these thread titles that happens to relate to my problem, I know to look in there for a possible answer.

Examples of not-so-good thread titles-

Newbie here...
just a question
need help
installed ubuntu but

So many of us put hours in every day trying to help folks who have questions.  If I have no experience with a LAMP configuration I'm probably not going to jump into the thread asking for assistance.  I can help with simple file sharing, general networking, setting ownership and some other stuff specific to my set up.  We really want to help.  Just put a bit more thought into your thread title and everyone wins.

If you find the solution to your problem, go back and mark the thread as resolved.  That REALLY helps out everyone.

Thanks!
