### Post by Jasper Houtman on 2006-07-11
What fun would that be???

Why do you think people (or groups of) start their own distro?

Mainly for two reasons:

1/ They enjoy building and tinkering with an OS (i.o.w. for fun)
2/ They don't find the functionality they need in the available distro's

With MEPIS I'm guessing the second. Why add to a distro and wait for ages (in Ubuntu's case 6 months). For your work to be included in a distro if you can build your work in and can launch it as your own based-on distro?

Only seems logical to do so and share it with the community. And the beauty of open source is that the Ubuntu developers can check out the MEPIS source and freely add some of those functionalities to their own distro if they wish.

### Post by Jucato on 2006-07-11
You also have to understand that MEPIS and Ubuntu have different goals and different methods, not to mention a different default desktop environment. Also, I'm not absolutely sure, but AFAIK, MEPIS already existed before Ubuntu's first stable release. MEPIS isn't really writing a new distro. It's merely switching from being based on Debian itself, to being based on Ubuntu.

This is just how the open source world works. There's nothing to stop you if you want/need to make a program, even if something slightly similar already exists. You might see it as duplication, but it's really just diversity. And there's beauty in diversity. :D

### Post by Jucato on 2006-07-11
I forgot to mention some things.
Ubuntu's desktop environment of choice is GNOME. Although there is Kubuntu, which uses KDE, it's just a derivative project, and in some ways, not as well developed if you compare it with the attention that GNOME gets in Ubuntu. MEPIS, on the other hand, has purely been a KDE distro from the very start. This gives it the advantage of focusing and polishing on making KDE work well.

Another point of difference between the two is "restricted" media formats/codecs. As we all know by now, Ubuntu doesn't ship with support for propreitary formats like MP3, AVI, etc. MEPIS, on the other hand, does.

Those are the two big differences between the two.
