### Post by HermanAB on 2007-10-19
Too many problems!

When you have this many problems, then it is time to try a different distribution.  Each distro has a lab full of computers that they test with, but nobody has every device ever produced on the planet.  That is inevitable.  I use various distributions, depending on what works on my hardware: Mandriva, Fedora and Ubuntu are the most common.  For client servers I am frequently forced to use OpenBSD, RedHat or even OpenSolaris.  So don't feel shy - shop around.

I suggest that you head over to the Mandriva web site and download the brand new Mandriva 2008 version: [http://torrent.mandriva.com/public/](http://torrent.mandriva.com/public/)

Mandriva has better wizards than Ubuntu and is actually the most new-user friendly Linux available.  Ubuntu is the light-brown minivan of Linux - not the easiest and not the most difficult, but sometimes not any good for what you want to do either...

Cheers,

Herman
