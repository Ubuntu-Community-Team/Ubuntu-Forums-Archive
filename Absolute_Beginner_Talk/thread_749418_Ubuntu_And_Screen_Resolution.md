### Post by Therion on 2008-04-08
> **jtrink said:**
> What screen resolution is everyone using?

I have a 19'' monitor and can't decide which res to use....
If your monitor is LCD, then it will have a "native" resolution which is supposed to give the best display result for most applications (meaning other than gaming for example) and refresh rate is immaterial, since LCD's don't refesh (they have response times).

If you're using CRT use whatever resolution looks best to you and the highest refresh rate supported (85 is good for me, nothing less than 75).  I wouldn't want to use anything lower than something around 1024x768 on 19" CRT and I'd probably go a notch higher; but that's me.

### Post by Wim Sturkenboom on 2008-04-09
> **AndrewEsther said:**
> technically speaking, the 75hz is useless without a transformer connected to your monitor, isn't it? since wall ac power is 60 Hz here in the US, **then theoretically isn't that the locked-in refresh rate**?

PS if 60Hz is risky don't ever go to france... power can dip as low as 50Hz in some areas... it drove me effing crazy I was seeing stuff and people thought I was nuts lolFor VGA monitors, there is no relation between the wall outlet frequency and refresh rates. Further transformers don't change frequencies but voltages.

In most European countries (if not all), standard for wall outlet is 50 Hz. I think that it's even safe to say the 60Hz is an exception in the world.

Televisions (CRT types) is another story. As it was easy (and probably cheap) to derive a 50/60Hz refresh rate from the 50/60Hz wall outlet, there is (maybe 'was' is the better word) a relation. As one has to change the whole system (source as well as TV), we're still stuck with that.
