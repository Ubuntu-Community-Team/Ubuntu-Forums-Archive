### Post by go2dell on 2007-02-23
It does sound like the hard drive might be having some trouble, but not all hard drive trouble is caused by the hard drive itself.

If the machine has a floppy drive, you could try running the Dell Diagnostics diskette (should have shipped with the system) on it to rule out any hardware problems.  Even if those diagnostics pass, it doesn't mean you don't have trouble, but it will test a variety of systems in one place.

If you don't have an original Dell Diags disk, and they do appear to have stopped offering the older ones for download -- at least no so you can easily find them -- then you could also pull the drive from the computer to find out what brand drive it has.  Once you know that, you can download the drive manufacturer's diagnostics and run them.  Worth a shot.

Before you do anything else, though, **backup any important data** in case your drive gives out completely in the near future.
