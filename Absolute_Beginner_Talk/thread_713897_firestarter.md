### Post by OrangeCrate on 2008-03-03
I'm having a bit of a problem following your question, but let me make a stab at what I think your asking...

Firestarter, which is the GUI interface for iptables, your firewall, must be installed from the Synaptic Package Manager under System > Administration > Synaptic...

When you get there, search for "firestarter" and mark for installation. Firestarter will then show up as an option under Administration. Click on it, and walk your way through the installation wizard, and you're all set.

Read the Firestarter documentation before you start. You can find it here:

[http://www.fs-security.com/docs.php](http://www.fs-security.com/docs.php)

All that being said, Ubuntu is quite secure on it's own, and you really only need to configure a firewall if your running a server.

You can read more information on Ubuntu Security here:

[http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/security](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/security)
