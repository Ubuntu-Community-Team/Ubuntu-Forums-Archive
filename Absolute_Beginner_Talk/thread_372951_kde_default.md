### Post by Arisna on 2007-02-28
To summarize this greatly and objectively, GNOME and KDE, and their graphical toolkits GTK and QT, both have a number of "killer-apps" that are specific to them.  However, as long as you have the correct libraries installed, you can have the best of both worlds in terms of applications no matter which environment you primarily use.  Unless you are extremely picky about interface consistency, it's almost purely an issue of what feels more comfortable to you and/or which environment's applications are more important to you.
