### Post by Shatrat on 2007-03-11
This probably isn't the answer you want to hear, but I recommend buying a real microphone and real headphones (every 'headset' ive ever had has broken in a few months) that plug into your sound card, and probably get a good printer as well.
I have an HP printer, a Logitech desk mic, and Sennheiser headphones and they all work great.
[http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting/Database/SuggestedPrinters](http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting/Database/SuggestedPrinters)

I'm sure it's possible to get some (possibly limited) functionality out of the printer and headphones you have, but it is probably more trouble than it's worth, especially with more and more hardware manufacturers like HP and Epson actually supporting linux well.
Whichever way you go, good luck.
