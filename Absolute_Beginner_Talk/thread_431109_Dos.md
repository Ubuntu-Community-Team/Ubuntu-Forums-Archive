Hi, Things have moved on a considerable amount since DOS was first released, I would say that it is not the best operating system to learn to hack / crack (call it what you will) on. A great deal of what you are talking about is more down to the applications that you run, and understanding what they are doing, than the host operating system. Additional "Dos" commands were added to each release of Windows, and the list of "dos" commands, or command line controls that can be used under XP rivals linux and unix (duck's and covers :) )

You may hate microsoft, but it's (currently) the most prolific operating system out there that you will come across, so I would suggest using XP to start with. And looking at applications that can help you identify potential security vulnerabilities.  MSBA(Microsoft baseline security Analyser) should show you how vulnerable your own system is. Things like wireshark will allow you to scan networks, sniff packets to identify machines, then you need to tie up that info with known vulnerabilities.

(BTW if you have a games console hooked up to broadband, it could include that as well!)

Rather than doing this, why not put your thirst for knowledge to a better use? Think about really understanding what an O/S is for, and does, and how it operates, then help others.
This is a good place to start, as Ubutnu seems as gooder (sorry for spelling) place as any and you aren't breaking any laws but looking and understanding.
You will find much more satisfaction in helping people, and maybe stopping hackers / crackers getting into your friends systems than becoming one of them (well, I do anyway)  Also you may fund a bit of profit in it for yourself at the end of the day.
