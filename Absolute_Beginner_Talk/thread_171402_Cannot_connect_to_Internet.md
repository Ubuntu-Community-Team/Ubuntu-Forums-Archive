### Post by richbarna on 2006-05-06
Hi Diver9, sorry PriceChild, but if diver9 is a new newbie, when he sees this :- ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=128875)](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=128875)), he is going to scream, (I know I did).
I was using linux off and on for years dualbooting with xp just for the internet. I won't tell you how long i spent or how many times i tried to configure a usb modem "especially" the speedtouch 330 with eagle drivers and so on.
My best advice Diver9, is to get yourself a cheap ethernet router, and a network card if you haven't already got one.
I would even go as far as to find out what your ISP uses as standard (apart from USB), that way you can download their router config program from their web site and set it up from windows.
Once the router is configured most Linux distro's will automatically find the connection with little or no need for any tweaking.
Hey presto you are on the net !
I got a second hand 4 port router for 30 bucks.
IMHO it is the best way for a new user to get on the net.

I hope you will be surfing soon ; )
