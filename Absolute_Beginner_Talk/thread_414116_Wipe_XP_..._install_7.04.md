### Post by brion@cbkidder.com on 2007-04-19
Like you, I am very impressed with the elegance and swift performance of Ubuntu. I have it running dual-boot with XP on my desktop, and I completely wiped out XP in favor of 6.10 Edgy on my laptop.

A few lessons learned:
[LIST=1]
[*]Being a newbie, I screwed up the laptop and have reinstalled Ubuntu thrice because I didn't know how to fix my mistakes. Don't wipe out your XP if you depend on it; instead let Ubuntu create a new partition for you (its automated partition manager has always worked well for me).
[*]Accept that you will have a learning curve. You may find that you don't know how to do things you take for granted in XP. I for one am still having a heck of a time with wireless networking.
[*]Based on the above, consider keeping XP on one partition and Ubuntu on another for a few weeks or even months until you're certain you can do everything you need to in Ubuntu. It's been about a month for me now, and I just last night found a work applet I need to run on Windows and since I've never used Wine before I'm glad I still have the XP partition on my desktop so I could get the job done now and learn how to do it in Ubuntu when I have time later.
[/LIST]

All that said, you will find a wealth of support information and expertise in this forum. Hopefully there will come a day soon when you rediscover your forgotten and unused XP partition and ask yourself "why do I still have this dinosaur on here?" I'm getting there.
