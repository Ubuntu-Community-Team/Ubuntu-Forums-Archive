### Post by pshnfry on 2006-04-29
Explore the wireless how to's.  Watch for chipset issues though, I wasted a lot of time with a PCI card identified on varios sites as using the RT2500 chipset, after much angst I find it has RT61 chipset and is not supported AT ALL in Linux unless ndiswrapper is used on windows drivers.  Given some research on ndiswrapper my preference is to try another wireless pci nic I have with an Atheros chip.

In short, don't waste too much time, if the guide selected for your product doesn't work there give it away.
