### Post by cliff0108 on 2006-04-04
Thiss not a huge problem - but I notice in order to copy and paste - the source that you must paste from must be kept open for the operation to work.
In other words - in windows you can copy some text from an application - close that application - open a new application and paste into it - the data remaining on the 'clipboard.
Unless I am mistaken (very possible) Linux doesn't work this way and requires the source to remain open before the paste.

### Post by johnnymac on 2006-04-04
Correct....a work around would be to use a clipboard applet to your tool-bar.  In XFCE (that's what I run) you can use clipman.  Clipman will allow you to put stuff in your clipboard and keep it....so even if you close an app or terminal it will still be there for however long you set your preferences.
