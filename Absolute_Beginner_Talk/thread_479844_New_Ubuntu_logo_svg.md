You needn't be confused - I reckon that some of the income from the Ubuntu Store is used for paying some of the programmers at canonical, or where ever they are sited. Anyway - Ubuntu IS a trademark and therefore protected by the trademark laws nationally as well as world wide. If not registered then ALWAYS protected by laws on copyright, world wide too. If he want to make a shirt of his own the right thing to do is to ask for permission at Canonical and not try to obtain permission trough this forum. Ubuntu.com/contact us will be an even better place to ask this question as they are able to provide the right answer.

In order for the Ubuntu logos to be free in the same way as the software the logos need to be released under either GPL or any of those licenses. And the page shown earlier informs that the logos are trademarks owned entirely by Canonical and therefor are NOT free to use by other than Canonical.
