### Post by MasterJS on 2008-03-16
Well, the 3D desktop that your talking about is done through a programs like Beryl or Compiz Fusion. Now, if your using Xubuntu out of necessity because of your hardware, then programs like that are most likely to much for your computer. Xubuntu in itself is already pretty cool. You could try enabling compositing in the Settings Manager (I don't remember where to find but if you look around, you should be able to find). Then if you can get compositing, you could get a Dock (like the Macs have) and that could be programs like AWN, Cairo Dock, etc. If you want to customize your desktop, you could always go to [http://www.gnome-look.org]("http://www.gnome-look.org") as the things they have their can also work for Xubuntu.
