### Post by Mr Frosti on 2006-09-25
Many of the different distributions have different approaches and philosophies. Without getting into a forum war, let me outline some of the approches for a few of the distributions below:

Debian - the Debian distribution has a very firm "FOSS" policy. It concentrates on a minimalistic enviroment. After an installation of Debian, you will find yourself sitting at the terminal prompt without a GUI. This is seen as too much overhead, as it seems to be geared towards servers.

Ubuntu - the Ubuntu distribution builds on the Debian base, but auto detects and configures a lot of low level hardware.

OpenSuSE - the OpenSuSE distribution focuses on being a complete desktop solution. It comes with YaST, and many management tools that the normal user may never use. An installation of this distribution will come many things working out of the box that you would be suprised to see coming from a Ubuntu / Debian background.

Yes, with hundreds of distributions, some of the differences are small between them. Most of the differences lie in the community, and the tools bundled with the distribution. I recommend trying one out and becoming proffecient at it, then moving on to another.
