### Post by rivalarrival on 2008-01-01
I run into this problem a lot. I'm a property inspector covering a 100-mile circle. I put 1000 miles on my car in an average week, almost all of it under TomTom direction. I absolutely love tomtom, but on occasion, it can be a real creationist. 

What usually happens is that tomtom uses a different name for the road. For instance, in my area, the popular designation for a particular road is "State route 91" - the mail service will deliver to addresses on state route 91, and most of the residents use the number rather than the name. TomTom even recognizes state route 91, but it has no addresses on this road.  I have to enter the actual name of the road. SR91 is named Cleveland Avenue, Darrow Road, South Avenue, North Avenue, Munroe Falls Avenue, and then Darrow road again, depending on the town and the part of the road you're referring to. 

In other regions, especially rural areas, it is the exact opposite - TomTom uses the route number rather than the name. These can be even harder to figure out, especially if the route number is an obscure county road. 

In some cases, the city is the problem: the mailing address for many rural areas is the nearest town with a post office. Most of the time, tomtom uses this city, but occasionally, it uses the name of the township instead, and when all I have is the mailing address, that does me no good.

The best workaround I've found is to choose to navigate to a point on the map, rather than an address, and manually scroll the map to the desired destination. i've also used google's text-message feature to give me directions, followed them on the tomtom map, and selected the destination road at the end. You might also see if newer or different maps are available. If it can find the name of the rural road, navigate to a cross street on that road, and just look at the addresses as you drive down it. 

I use the Pocket PC version of tomtom, so your situation might be a bit different. I figure it has saved me a couple hundred hours of time planning and following a route, and at least $250 in gas in the past year.
