Main Differances between Fedora and Ubuntu

1)Ubuntu has a "sudo" command. Fedora you login as "root" (even if you just do it in the terminal)
2)Fedora is based on Redhat (.rpm). Ubuntu is based on Debian(.deb)
3)Synaptic Is replaced with PUP inFedora I believe.

### Post by laptoplinux on 2007-06-02
Allow me to share my experiences with Ubuntu and Fedora.

My computer is a Dell D820 Latitude.
Centrino Duo processor
Nvidia graphics card
Intel Pro Wireless 3945

And while I'm at it, let me refute a few statements:

*Wireless is YEARS ahead of Ubuntu*

Fedora core 5 --> No wireless out of box.  I followed about 5 different guides involving compiling wireless drivers from source, installing wireless driver rpms, and recompiling the kernel, and never got wireless working.

Fedora core 6 --> No wireless out of box.  I followed many of the same guides updated for core 6, and one of them got wireless working, which was soon broken again due to a kernel update.  Repeating the successful procedure of recompiling the wireless drivers against the new kernel failed to get things working again

Fedora core 7 -->  Very very very spotty and unstable wireless out of the box.  Network manager has to be manually told to associate with my wireless network on each boot up.  It is unable to remember either the network or the password.  Reminds me of the state of wireless in Linux about 3 years ago when I was using Mandrake 10.0.

Ubuntu 7.04 -->  Flawless wireless out of box.  Network manager automatically associates with my network.  With the keyring hack in place, Ubuntu always remembers my password and auto-connects on login.  KDE's knetworkmanager is capable of auto-connecting without a "keyring" hack.

*the speed is incomparable (Fedora is much quicker than Ubuntu, in my experience)*
I wonder if this poster has ever actually used yum/pirut and apt-get/Synaptic?

Yum is on par with apt-get, I suppose, but Pirut (the graphical "Synaptic" of Fedora) is pitiful, even after all the supposed improvements in Fedora 7.  Pirut does not display any information about how large the package download will be or how long it will take, and at times it is so slow that I seriously think it might have hung up.  Add to that that I can go from power button to login screen in about 30 seconds in Ubuntu, and I could never get that under a full minute in any Fedora release.


*the overall packaging is superior (in terms of branding/default themes etc)*

You got me there.  At least Fedora's default theme doesn't look like Naruto having diarrhea. (Sorry, I'm not a big fan Ubuntu's default look), but a user can re-theme Ubuntu to look like anything they want, including a Fedora mock up (minus the branding) in only a few minutes.  My own ubuntu theme uses Clearlooks and Echo with some Beryl coolness added. 

*The only place Ubuntu wins is in forum assistance. I've found the Fedora forum experience to be incredibly fragmented. Things that work in FC4 don't work in F7, yet they are still around on the forums.*

In the 3 years that I've used Linux and distro-hopped all around, I've never seen any forums as friendly or responsive as the Ubuntu forum.  I've already asked questions about everything from simple Linux howtos to questions about Ubuntu-isms that are different from other Linuxes and even questions about advanced scientific software, and I've always received helpful answers in a fair amount of time, often within an hour or two of posting.

Oh, and things that worked in F6 don't work in F7 either.  There's currently a bug in F7's kernel that causes it to hang at boot for many dual-core PCs.  The only fix is to disable either one of the cpu cores or disable the power/battery management or both.  I didn't have any problems like this with Ubuntu.

If Fedora does it for you, then great.  And I'd probably recommend Fedora over Ubuntu for a server.  But my own experience has shown Ubuntu to be miles ahead of Fedora on the desktop.
