### Post by nvteighen on 2007-05-17
SweetKiss, I'm still (sighs...) a Windows XP Home SP2 user. The best you can do is to download the Live CD at: [http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download](http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download)

To burn the CD, please use this fantastic guide: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso)

The Live CD doesn't touch your hard drive, it runs a **full** Ubuntu (no, not a crippled demo) from your RAM. The only requisite is to have a RAM big enough (aprox. 512 MB, but the bigger the better!).

As it is a full OS driven from RAM, you'll be able to detect any hardware issue without having to install.

Then, read regularly things on this forum, starting from the most simple (setting up a new desktop background, for example) to the most difficult, and then try to do it by yourself on the Live CD. The good thing is that, when you restart the computer from Windows, you'll lose any change you've done and you'll be forced to learn what you did before.

That's what I do to learn how Ubuntu works!
