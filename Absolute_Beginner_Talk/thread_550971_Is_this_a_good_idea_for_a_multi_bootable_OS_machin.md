### Post by lentomies on 2007-09-14
Hello People!

I'm in the process of designing, collecting, buying all the bits and pieces I need in order to build a Windows 2000, Windows XP and Ubuntu 7.04 system.

Having reviewed all the material there is available through this forum and the Internet, I have concluded that I'm only asking for trouble if one of the O/S's were to fail or become corrupted. I don't even want to think of the hours that would be involved in getting everything back up and running. What a nightmare! NO!!!!!!

How could I minimize my overall risk while at the same time enjoying 3 operating systems? Finally after weeks and months the obvious struck me. I could buy 2 or 3 Vantec Serial ATA EZ-Swap Mobile Rack's. When I want to use one operating system, I simply turn the key for that drive to the "on" position and when I want the other I turn one "off" and the other to "on"

Surely at first glance this seems like the most reliable, perhaps practible approach while reducing my over risk BUT have I out foxed myself.....? Is there something I need to consider still to have the operating systems I want.

Is my approach a viable alternative is the question.

### Post by ske1fr on 2007-09-14
Perfectly good idea to physically isolate the OS's with hardware-switched hard drives, I use a similar system myself, albeit PATA rather than SATA, on one machine.  Much safer than trying to get several OS's to play nicely on the one hard drive.  You might want to have another internal hard drive  - non-switchable - that's a data store accessable no matter which OS you're using.
