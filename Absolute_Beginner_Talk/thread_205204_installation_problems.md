### Post by tommcd on 2006-06-28
1) Did you verify MD5sums of the downloaded ISO? Your ISO could be bad:
[http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso.html](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso.html)
(It's about halfway down the page.)
2) Burn the CD at a very slow speed, like 2x or 4x so the ISO don't get corrupted.
Try the alternate CD if you can, you may have better luck with that:
[http://mirror.cs.umn.edu/ubuntu-releases/6.06/](http://mirror.cs.umn.edu/ubuntu-releases/6.06/)
(Again, it's about halfway down the page, but be sure to choose a mirror close to you, if your outside the USA.
Hope this helps.

### Post by SkyNet2029 on 2006-06-28
There are a few installation specifics that deal solely with Dell laptops when using Debian-installer. Use F1 to get to the menu when you first boot FROM live-cd and go to where it says Alternative methods of booting/using this cd. Something to do with the way the installer probes the mobo, I beleive, although its been a while since I put u/kubuntu on a Dell, I did see the string still listed in boot options for the Dapper live/install cd.
