### Post by Jerome36 on 2006-08-26
That's not a lot of hard drive space, that's for sure.  Is that how much space you have left, or the total space of the drive?  Ubuntu needs over 2 gigs to install, so you need to make sure you have that much.  Before you even get started you should also defrag your drive in Windows ME.

I recently setup my sister's computer to dual-boot (she too has Windows ME, on an old computer).  It was quite easy with the graphical installer.  After putting in the Live CD, and booting into Linux I double clicked the Install Icon on the desktop.  After a few steps it gets to the part about setting up a partition, or completely formatting the harddrive.

I selected the option to have Ubuntu find the largest free space on the hard drive, and then a slider appeared, which let me raise and lower the amount to dedicate.  Her hard drive was 12 gigs and there was over 8 gigs free.  I lowered it to 6 though.  After that it automatically setup the partitions, including a small swap partition, and installed the GRUB boot loader.

Your computer sounds a bit old, and perhaps might not be good enough to do a graphical install with Ubuntu, as I've heard you need at LEAST 192mb of ram.  But you might be okay with Xubuntu, since it's lighter then Ubuntu.

### Post by catlett on 2006-08-26
You need to find out how much space has been taken up ny windows already, unless you are going to re-install windows.
Both OSs need 2gb to install with 3 being a safer amount. That will be without third party software or youre personal documents etc. 
You will be cutting it extremely close. The only way to make this plausible is to have an external hard drive where you keep personal data.

Back to my first point. You most likely have 4gb of space used on the drive. If this is your family computer, I would be surprised if you had room for xubuntu, but you never know. Go to My Computer and right click on your C drive icon. Select properties and you will see a pie graph. That will tell you the size of your drive and how much space is free.
