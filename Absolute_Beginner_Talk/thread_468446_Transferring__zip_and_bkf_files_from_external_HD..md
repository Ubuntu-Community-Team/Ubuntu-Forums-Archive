### Post by taurus on 2007-06-08
Just copy them over to your $HOME directory with nautilus.

<Alt>F2 -> nautilus

### Post by ryanVickers on 2007-06-08
Yes, copy them to your home directory and then extract them.  This also has the benifit of not having a bunch of unneeded files left behind afterward on the external drive, and negates the risk of using up all your disk space doing the sxtract, although being as smart as ubuntu is, if it thinks it wouldn't fit, it won't start! :)

Anyway, yes, just get the files off the external drive first.
