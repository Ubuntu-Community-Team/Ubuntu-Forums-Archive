### Post by kebes on 2007-09-23
> **chadrlz said:**
> I want to write in 1.3.16 and I just can't find anything on the net that tells me how to do this.

Instead of typing "1.3.16" (which autoformat interprets as a date), type:
'1.3.16

Just put a single quote before whatever you want to type, and it will take it as a "literal" and not try to convert it to a number or a date. This helps also to enter things that start with a dash, which again are converted into numbers or forumlae.

You asked about tutorials and help files, so I thought I'd just mention where you can find some.

**Tutorials for OpenOffice.org can be found at:**  [http://www.tutorialsforopenoffice.org/](http://www.tutorialsforopenoffice.org/)

**The pdf version of a basic users manual can be found at:**  [http://documentation.openoffice.org/manuals/index.html](http://documentation.openoffice.org/manuals/index.html)
 
**For writers and Authors:** [http://oooauthors.org/en/authors/userguide2/published/](http://oooauthors.org/en/authors/userguide2/published/) 

Streaming video tutorials, and online classes are also available through openoffice.org
If your current install of open office didn't include the help files, you really should download them, they are invaluable. Native language versions of OpenOffice.org pages are available through links on the home page. Maestro23 posted a link for the support page in an earlier reply, so I didn't repeat it.
