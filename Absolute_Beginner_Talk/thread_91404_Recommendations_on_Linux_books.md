I am new to Linux too and have been looking through  a number  of books. I  have yet to find one  that leaves me feeling  satisfied  with  the content. One  that  I bought, and have  spent a lot of time reading, is Debian Gnu/Linux 3.1  Bible__. I have also ordered the new edition of Running Linux.__ It  isn't out yet, but should be  out the first of December. ### Post by zerhacke on 2005-11-17
Oh, I'm all over seconding the Bible reference.  If you can find a copy of "The Linux Bible" get it, it's not distro specific and spends a very good amount of time on the CLI (mostly BASH, but you find some ZSH and others).  If you want to know your Linux, know your CLI.

If you're new enough to not know what CLI means, it's Command Line Interface.  The "dos prompt" of Linux.

### Post by matthew on 2005-11-17
O'Reilly publishes the best computer books, IMO. For Linux here are some that I found the most helpful. Take a trip to the bookstore to look through them before you buy. I generally buy used through Amazon and can get them for about 30% of the cover price in new or like new condition.

_Running Linux_ (Best to start out)
_Linux Cookbook_ (Great second book)
_Linux in a Nutshell_ (More of a reference to all commands available)
_Learning the Bash Shell_

Also helpful are:
_Unix Power Tools_ (best of breed...really!)
_Linux Power Tools_ (this one is published by Sybex)

### Post by bored2k on 2005-11-17
Although I have a ton of begginer-to-not.so.begginer eBooks at my disposal, I have the best learning tool I have is the UbuntuForums. Some people hate it, some people we the Staff stink at our job, but the meat of it, the friendly  and always willing to help userbase is just superb. I joined the forums almost 10 months ago (February 20th to be exact) and I am sure ANYONE who remembers me from back then knows how much I've grown here. 

You got a problem? First step: try your hardest at solving it yourself in every way you can imagine. If you fail to succeed, ask here where you'll get people willing to help and explain what you're about to do :).
