### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-07-25
Hi. It was probably [Wine]("http://www.winehq.org/"), [CrossOver Office]("http://www.codeweavers.com/") or [Cedega]("http://www.transgaming.com/"); Lindows (now [Linspire]("http://www.linspire.com/")) is indeed a linux distro. It could also have been windows running inside a [virtual machine]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_machine") (such as [QEMU]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QEMU"), [VirtualBox]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VirtualBox"), [VMware]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VMware"), or [Xen]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xen")).

### Post by Mornedhel on 2007-07-25
> **mlentink said:**
> They had to change the name because e certain software company felt the name could mislead people....

Actually, not exactly. MS went to court on the basis that "Windows" was theirs, and lost (because they had used the term "windows" to describe GUIs before they even released an OS called Windows, and even then, Xerox and Apple had windows years before). But then, MS paid Lindows $20 M to change its name, which became Linspire.
