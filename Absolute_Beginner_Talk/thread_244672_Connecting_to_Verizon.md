I assume a) you have a notebook and b) you are trying to connect using a Verizon aircard.  If so, you should first try Verizon's tech support, but be prepared for them to tell you they do not support LINUX.  What you describe in Windows sounds like a program Verizon designed specifically for Windows to connect their aircard, in which case you might be out of luck.

Unfortunately, when it comes to internet service providers, and particularly aircards, to get things working properly you will need their tech support (unless of course you understand all the fundamentals of setting up the connection, which is unlikely without inside knowledge).  If Verizon does not support LINUX, you may have to find an alternative solution.
