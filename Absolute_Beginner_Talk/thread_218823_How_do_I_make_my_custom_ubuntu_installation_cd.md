### Post by OpsVentus on 2006-07-19
I think a backup would be a better way to go. Making your own installation cd will take way to much time.
Or when you reinstall your system don't format your /home. Then you will keep all your settings. And save all the programs intalled with Synaptic. This way you got your system up after a reinstall in no time.

Cheers,
Bart.

### Post by mhancoc7 on 2006-07-19
You should take a look at [UCK (Ubuntu Customization Kit)]("http://lichota.net/~krzysiek/projects/ubuntu-livecd-customization/"). It has a nice little gui for creating localized Ubuntu LiveCDs. It also comes wiht some example scripts that allow you to add and remove applications to create your own custom LiveCD. It is really awesome. It took me a little while to get it working, but now I am working on ["Ubuntu Christian Edition"]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=218724").
Good Luck, Jereme
