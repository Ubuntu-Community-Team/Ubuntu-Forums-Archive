### Post by tubasoldier on 2007-04-19
I have been browsing these forums for some time now. And never, until I was in this forum, did I see someone refer to having two operating systems as a "duel boot".  

I think it is time to correct our grammar a bit here. 
The definition for DUEL: A duel is a formalized type of combat. 
The definition for DUAL: A dual is a pair or a grouping of two.

Now I personally have never been able to get two opposite operating systems to have a duel. It would be pretty fun and may destroy some hardware along the way, however, they just can't do it. Dueling has nothing to do with booting or with the number of operating systems your computer has on it's hard drive. 

So for my sanity and for the sanity of others, could we please start "dual booting" our computers instead of "duel booting" our computers?
