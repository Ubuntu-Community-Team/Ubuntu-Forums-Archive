### Post by gpilkay on 2007-07-30
I second the VLC/Mplayer combo, as between the two I can play anythign I've tried.

One warning, though, on Mplayer, a lot of people, including myself, have had to change the default setting for video or it can't 'open the device'.  To do this, right-click the 'screen' where the movie is supposed to play, then go to 'preferences', under the 'video' tab, I picked the xV x11/x/v option.  You may need a different result./

No problems after that.

It's also useful to have mplayer even if you're like me and prefer VLC's playback, as mplayer is used by many other applications, like AcidRip and such.
