### Post by Medieval_Creations on 2006-10-03
I have a small herd of PCs and I've run the gambit of most of the Linux Distros out there.  Debian is still my favorite, because of it customization, but it does get tiring having to figure out a lot of the hardware driver and configuration.

So Ubuntu is the best of both worlds.  It has a very good hardware detection and drivers for most things and since it's based on Debian, you still have the flexibility of customizing the heck out of it.

If your a casual user definitely go with Ubuntu it will save you a lot of headaches and as previously stated try a Dual-Boot until you make sure everything that you do in Windows is working in Linux.
