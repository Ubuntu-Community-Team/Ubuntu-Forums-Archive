### Post by i-am-me on 2007-03-16
> **Skia_42 said:**
> Can you do a search for the .iso image? Otherwise you could check your download menu and find out where it was downloaded.

Like he says, you could search, but if you don't want to or you can't and you have Firefox, you can type in about:config into the browser and type in download into the box at the top. It should be easy to find (in bold and says something about the last download dir) and you can use that to find the image. Of course, if you downloaded anything after the iso to a different place, then this won't work.

### Post by Sef on 2007-03-16
> Hi to all, very new to Ubuntu, I downloaded the lastest version, but when I try to burn to CD, I can't find the ISO image.


0) Did you burn it from [Ubuntu's]("http://www.ubuntu.com/getubuntu/download") site?

1) Download it to the Desktop.  Easy place to find it.

2) [md5sum]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToMD5SUM") the download.  

3) Make sure you burn the iso as an iso and not a data file.

4) If you have any more questionsl, please post them.
