### Post by maddog39 on 2007-04-19
I wouldnt recommend using K3b to try and do that because its going to look for the right kind of disk. If you are in Ubuntu 6.06 or 6.10 with GNOME then just insert your blank DVD and it will ask you what you want to do with it. Then click "Make Data Disk". The file manager will open the DVD's directory in a new window, just drag and drop the .iso file into the DVD window and hit "Write Disk". At this point it will ask you weather you want to create the disk from the disk image (the .iso) or just burn it as is. What you want to do is click "Create from Image" and then choose your DVD drive and hit burn. It will burn and your disk is ready to use as normal. Hope this helps.
