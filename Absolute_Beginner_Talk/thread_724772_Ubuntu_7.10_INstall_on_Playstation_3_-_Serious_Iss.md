### Post by igknighted on 2008-03-14
1) Linux on the PS3 is not a simple install.  It involves countless tweaks and hacks to get it working "properly", and even then you can never use the system to its full potential.  Don't try it unless you really are willing to get down and dirty with it.

2) Yellow Dog Linux is the best distribution for PS3 installs.  They have done a lot of the above work for you.  However, YDL is not free, I think it costs between 30 and 50 dollars (US).  You can get older versions for free, but I don't think they are as PS3 ready.

3) Look for a how-to that people recommend.  You seem to be going at this without a complete plan.  I would make sure you have a good guide that has worked for others, and stick to it start to finish.  I have never put linux on a PS3 (or ever touched a PS3 for that matter), so I cannot recommend a good one... but many here have links to them.  Do a forum search, as there are long threads discussing this that will help you,
