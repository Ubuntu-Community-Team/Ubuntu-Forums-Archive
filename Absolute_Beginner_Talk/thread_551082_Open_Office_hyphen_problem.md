### Post by getmeoutofhere on 2007-09-14
I've got a problem with Open Office.

I tap in a word.  Put in a space.  Put in a hyphen.  Put in another space.  Then, as I start tapping in the next word, the hyphen lengthens.  

For example, I want to write 'you - me'.  I start writing this, the hyphen remains short, but when I write the me, it becomes 'you – me'.  This might seem a trivial observation, but as far I can see the two hyphens are separate characters, and under certain circumstances the long hyphen will be encoded as '=96'.  

Does anyone know what I'm talking about, and if so, can you think of a solution, aside from a find and replace, finding the long hyphen and replacing it with a short one?

---

### Post by klytu on 2007-09-14
I know exactly what you are talking about! What is happening is that OpenOffice autocorrect is replacing minus signs with dashes. Try this in OpenOffice Word Processor: Go to Tools>Autocorrect>Options and clear the check marks next to "replace dashes".

### Post by lisati on 2007-09-14
It sounds almost as if Open Office's "auto correct" option is doing things to your work. Tinkering with its settings might be of some help.
