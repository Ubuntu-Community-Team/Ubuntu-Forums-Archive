### Post by skymt on 2006-06-04
I did some quick Googling, and it looks like MusicMatch for Linux is a no-go. I found an article on it with a link to a download, but it redirected me to the Windows download page.

Googling the RCA Lyra only produced more bad news. The Lyra, as you said, only accepts .MPY files, which are encrypted. The encryption hasn't been broken, and isn't likely to be, since most crackers are working on more recent technologies like iTunes Fairplay and whatever Blu-Ray uses.

Your best chance is [CrossOver Office](http://www.codeweavers.com/). It's a commercial version of Wine (a Windows compatibility layer for Linux) that officially supports MusicMatch 8.2, 9, and 10 (along with over a thousand other programs). It won't be native, but it should work. CodeWeavers offers a free trial, so download it and try it out.
