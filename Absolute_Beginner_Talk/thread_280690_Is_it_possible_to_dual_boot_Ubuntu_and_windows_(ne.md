### Post by BaffledMollusc on 2006-10-29
> 
Thanks. I already have the disc, I am just trying to figure out the partition part of the install. XP Media Cneter Edition on a 300 gig hard drive. Just one big partition. How do I partition to give XP half the space?

Maybe I'm misunderstanding here -  is this your situation? You have two 300GB hard drives. The first has XP on it, taking up 15GB, and about 285GB of free space. The second hard drive has your media collection on it. You want to put Ubuntu on the first hard drive with Windows.

If that's the case, just go ahead and choose what you describe as "the first option", i.e. let Ubuntu partition for you. It should then ask you how much of the first hard drive you want to give over to Ubuntu, and then it will resize it for you. For example, if you say you want to give 50GB to Ubuntu, Windows will then be taking up 15GB of a 250GB partition, and Ubuntu will be installed on the new 50GB partition.
