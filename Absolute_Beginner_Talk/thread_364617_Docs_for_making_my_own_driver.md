### Post by Old Pink on 2007-02-18
Writing your own driver is going to require a large amount of knowledge on writing and coding in Linux, and you're still posting in "Absolute Beginner"? 

This isn't a job for beginners, you'd be better off selling this and buying a similar product that'll work on Linux. However, you could always try a few MSI drivers, I believe the device you're talking about actually has an MSI chipset.

It wouldn't hurt to ask ATI if they have any plans for Linux support, that'd be a good first step. If the answer is no, I'd highly reccomend getting yourself a device that works natively in Liunux, and selling this one on. :)
