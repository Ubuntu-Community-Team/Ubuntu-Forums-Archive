### Post by barley342 on 2007-03-05
I have found that this error usually means that there is a hardware problem of some sort. The error usually tells me that there is a non compliant cdrom or such. Even though the hardware may be in good working order. It may be that some of the hardware you are using may not be supported by the operating system. It is not uncommon to have to replace a components in order to have a fully clean working install with no hitches. I have also seen people compile homespun drivers to aleviate problems....

Usually, if the hardware is compatible with Linux, it is compatible with Ubuntu....:KS

I went back to the Mac System Preferences - Sharing and enabled File, Windows and Web Sharing and STOPPED the firewall.  Rebooted Ubuntu (still have no RDSP) and clicked on the exclamation point in the top right corner and it found the network!  I suspect the Firewall.  BTW, color, resolution, sound, etc.  are all great even with no RDSP.
