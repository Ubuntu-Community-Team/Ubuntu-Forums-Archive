### Post by ferebee on 2007-08-11
You can make your top and bottom bars transparent so the desktop wallpaper shows through by right clicking on a blank area on the bar and selecting 'properties' and then going to the 'background' tab. Select the 'solid colour' radio button and adjust the slider to the transparency level you like - then repeat it on the other bar.

Yup, cool feature. There are a few other nifty things you can do with the menu bars. On the Background tab under solid colour you can import your own custom background (say you want something custom that compliments the wallpaper. Best to make the image the exact height width of the bar (easily done with GIMP or such).

On the first tab General, you have an option called "Show hide buttons". This puts little arrows on the right and left side of each menu. If you push one whole menu disappears in the direction you clicked. Good when you want to reclaim the extra 40-50 pixels the two bars take up by default. The rest is self evident I think, tweak the size if you want things to appear just a bit bigger.

Just thought I'd add. Have fun messing around :).
