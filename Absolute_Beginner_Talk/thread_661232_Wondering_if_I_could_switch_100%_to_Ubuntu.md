### Post by rs3 on 2008-01-07
I found [Oblivion to be a bit rocky in Wine]("http://appdb.winehq.org/objectManager.php?sClass=application&iId=3150").  I wasn't able to escape the sewer and keep my uptime. :(  Additionally, some of the graphical effects had rather anomalous results (such as when the assassins would fall and lose their gear; the screen would fill with mustard yellow and similarly warm colors).

That said, WoW runs fantastically (just ensure to specify: gxApi "opengl" in your CONFIG.WTF), StarCraft generally runs well (though battle.net is currently broken), and your mileage may vary with most games.  The newer the game, the more difficult it will be to get running in Wine.

If you wish to convert to Ubuntu completely, you'll end up leaving some games behind.  You might be happier with a Windows partition devoted to gaming.  As far as your creative suite goes, most of the aforementioned analogues should prove sufficient, given a bit of learning curve--Inkscape and Scribus are particularly excellent applications.
