### Post by GranpaDan on 2007-03-12
Just a note to anyone else who is interested in using the Gramps geneology program. I loaded it via the synaptic downloads, and even though it said version 2.2.6 on the synaptic title, it was actually an earlier version (I don't remember which number). By going to the Gramps link: [http://gramps-project.org/index.php?module=pagemaster&PAGE_user_op=view_page&PAGE_id=4&MMN_position=27:27](http://gramps-project.org/index.php?module=pagemaster&PAGE_user_op=view_page&PAGE_id=4&MMN_position=27:27)

which is under the "Download Now!" link on the Gramps home page, under the "Upgrading to latest version" heading, item 1 for Ubuntu, there is a link to download gramps_zzz.deb and gramps-help_zzz.deb. Downloading these and opening using the automatic installer updated Gramps on my system to the latest 2.2.6 version.

I just thought I'd post this in case it helps anyone else.

Sorry I took so long to respond. I have been on vacation and away from my desktop.

1. From the GRAMPS' main page, click on the "Installation' link.
2. Click on the link on item #3 in the content's box 'Upgrading to the latest version'.
3. Click the 'download' link on the Ubuntu sentence.
4. Click on the 2.2.6 stable release (or the unstable release if you prefer).
5. Click to download both the gramps_2.2.6-1ubuntu1_all.deb and the gramps-help_2.2.6-1ubuntu1_all.deb. I downloaded them with Firefox.
6. When you click to open these files, the package installer should automatically start.

Good luck!:)
