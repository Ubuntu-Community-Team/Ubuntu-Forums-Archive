### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-03-25
As far as a motherboard look into VIA. They have really small motherboards known as nano-, micro-, etc. Some as small as 12cmx12cm. As a bonus they have almost all parts built in (eg video, sound, etc.). Thay also have small power requirements and that leads to low heat, a major issue for inside a car. Almost forgot they have built in MPEG decoders.

Hard drive, I would go laptop. Again, they are small and require very little power.

Touch screen won't be cheap. They do make some specific fold out units for cars, but I'm not sure of linux combatiblity.

Optical drive, again laptop for the same reasons mentioned before.

You do know that there are sites setup just for this purpose. Just do a google search for car PCs. They carry many custom parts to suit your needs.

### Post by Spenser_Gilliland on 2007-03-26
are you wondering how the headphone jack turns into speaker wire?  
the way you do this is by installing an amp.  then you buy an 1/8th inch stereo headphone jack to rca's.  Plug the 1/8th inch headphone jack into your computer, and the rca's into the amps input.  Then you connect your speakers to the amp.  Any cheapo amp will do unless you want to get a nice amp.  You can then control volume on the computer as if it were a deck. 

so in conclusion computer-->amp-->speakers
