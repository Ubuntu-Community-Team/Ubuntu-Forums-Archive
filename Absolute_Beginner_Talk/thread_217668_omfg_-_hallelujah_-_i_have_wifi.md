### Post by timofthec on 2006-07-17
FINALLY - after nearly four of the most frustrating days of my life (no exaggeration) and nearly pulling my hair out I have gotten my wifi up and running.

hey ma - i'm posting this from inside ubuntu :D 

I have a Belkin F5D700uk 54g wifi card connecting to a lynksys broadband router.

for all those still struggling with this issue - keep going.  it is possible for us less than intelligent computer users to crack the problem.

I used ndiswrapper, incorporating ndisgtk to make it work and am using the Broadcom Driver bcmwl5a.

I have tried umpteen posts and guides in these forums to get it to work and unfortunately, I cannot recall them all.  However, if you are looking for some help and guidance on this check out a post by ubunturules in a thread called "Broadcom 4306 with ndiswrapper 54mps", or a post by jagot in thread called “ndiswrapper help”.

I think the key for me was to "blacklist" the firmware drivers.

To all those that helped - thanx a bundle and to all those whose posts i read and cant remember - keep the info coming, there are plenty of us out there desperate for the help.
