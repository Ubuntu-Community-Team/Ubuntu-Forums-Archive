### Post by Bartender on 2006-06-18
Check out SPCR.  You'll have to browse around a bit, but they've done some work on how much power a PC really consumes.  For most of us, it's nowhere near what you might think unless you're running a hungry vid card.  
And you can consume more power than your PSU's rating - the most efficient is only about 80%, so they consume more in AC than they give you in DC.  Rest of it's turned to heat.

### Post by omion on 2006-06-19
My Ubuntu box uses 43W at idle, measured from the wall. I've got an Athlon64 3500+ with Cool-n-Quiet on, and 1GB of fairly power-hungry RAM.
Stand-by uses about 4W.

I'd guess most retail Athlon-based computers are about the same. P4s and dual-cores will be higher. Current high-end video cards will add about 30W at idle and over 100W when in use.


On another note, I was quite surprised by how little power my gaming box takes. My other computer has a dual-core processor, 3 hard drives, a decent video card, and fans all over the place. At full tilt it uses 230W. That's less than half of what my power supply is rated for!
