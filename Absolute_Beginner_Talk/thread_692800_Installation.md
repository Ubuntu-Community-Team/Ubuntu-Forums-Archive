### Post by Jon Monreal on 2008-02-10
> I want to try Ubuntu without installing the program on the hard drive.

You can do that by burning the image to the CD as already explained and then simply running off of the CD. Running Ubuntu does not install it on your hard drive unless you use the installer, so none of your data from whatever OS you are using will be erased.

Once you have the image burned on the CD, leave it in the drive. Turn everything off. Start the computer. You'll need to make the computer boot from the CD drive. Some computers, for a few seconds, present a screen where you press a special key, Esc, F1, delete, to allow you to boot from different devices. CD or DVD drive should be obvious. If you don't get this possibility, you'll have to press the key to get into your BIOS and change the boot sequence. Pay close attention to the beginning of the process when you start. Once at the BIOS screen, look into the first couple of categories for something like "boot sequence" or "boot order" or something of this nature. Don't be afraid to snoop in there, you can always exit without saving and once you've changed the boot sequence for the CD or DVD drive to be first in line, go to "Exit and save". Follow the instructions at the bottom of the screen. Once out of the BIOS, your computer should boot from the live CD. Good luck.
