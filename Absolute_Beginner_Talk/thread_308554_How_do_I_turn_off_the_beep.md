### Post by Cod on 2006-11-28
Hi folks,
When I'm using the terminal and press a wrong button, the computer makes a loud beep noise.  This is annoying (and embarassing as everyone around knows I've done something wrong...).  I've turned the sound off the computer by pressing the speaker buttons on the keyboard.  This stops all sounds (like the opening and shutting down shimmery sounds and MP3 player) except the beep.  It reminds me of the beep in the old MS DOS windows.

How can I turn it down / off?

Thanks

### Post by djf_jeff on 2006-11-28
Hi,

First, you can disable it in gnome-terminal. Right click on the terminal window and click on "Edit current profile". You can uncheck "Terminal bell"

Second, you can go to the System menu in the panel, Preferences and Sound. You can disable the System Beep here too.

Hope it help

---

### Post by autocrosser on 2006-11-28
You can modify your sounds with the panel--Menu>System>Preferences>Sound 

Goto the third tab & disable "system beep"
