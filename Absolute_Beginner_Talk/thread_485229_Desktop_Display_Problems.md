### Post by freebird54 on 2007-06-26
On my monitors, there is a control panel that contains some buttons.  One of them starts a menu, and within that are adjustments for brightness, contrast, vertical position, horizontal position etc etc.  That is one easy way to set things back to normal.  I often had to do that when switching between PC and Amiga :)

Also - the other settings are related to the capability of the monitor in terms of adjusting horizontal sync and vertical refresh rates (how the picture is drawn on the screen).  If those are set to a different value than usual, you can get displacement of that kind. 

What is the model number of your monitor?  You should be able to find the exact numbers there, and then we could describe it properly to Linux.  BTW - this issue is not related to the OS directly - I have had this in Windows (and even BEFORE Windows) and on the Amiga etc etc - ever since so-called Multi-Sync monitors came on the market...

EDIT:  BTW - the horizontal position is the cure!  Forgot to mention that.... :)
