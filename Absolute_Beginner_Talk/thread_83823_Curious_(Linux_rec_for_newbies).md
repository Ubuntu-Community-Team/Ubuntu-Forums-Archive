### Post by aysiu on 2005-10-29
First of all, I have to say this isn't the best place to get an objective opinion. It's a bit like shopping for a cell phone at a Sprint store--"Uh, do you think maybe I should go with Verizon?" Of course the vast majority of folks here will say "Ubuntu." If they didn't think Ubuntu was a great OS, they wouldn't be here. You're probably better off asking at LinuxQuestions.org or LinuxForums.org.

That said, you may find a bit of objectivity in here, believe it or not.

I have to disagree and say that for the absolute Linux newbie, Mepis or Blag is a better choice. Ubuntu is an excellent second distro, but Mepis and Blag are excellent first distros.

A lot of new Linux users are afraid of the command-line and don't understand what the deal is with proprietary codecs and open source/free software. All they care about is "does it work?" and "can it do play x,y, and z?" Mepis and Blag have proprietary codecs and a lot of programs you can try out (some would call it *bloat*), but that's a good thing for first-comers. Mepis has the added bonus of being a live CD that can also install.

You pop the live CD in. If you like it enough (and it likes your hardware), you can click the "install me" icon on the desktop, and it'll install. Best of all, most of the configuring you do in Mepis and Blag is point-and-click. Very rarely do you need to use the command-line.

After a while, though, like me, you might start thinking Mepis and Blag don't have good support in their communities and seem a bit bloated/clunky. That's when you'll realize that Ubuntu is a good place to move. You may need to use the command-line for a few things here and there, but you may actually start to like the command-line. You may not have proprietary codecs right away, but you may realize soon that they're easy to install or that you can use an open source codec instead.

Unless you're comfortable with the command-line or you have someone else installing Ubuntu for you, I would say it's a bad first distro.
