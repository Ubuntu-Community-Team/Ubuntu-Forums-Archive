I'm a graphic designer as well and I have been so impressed with the results of my experimentation with the Ubuntu Live CD that I installed it yesterday and dual-booted my machine (which is now making my life miserable!--xp won't boot!)..

Yeah, I support the whole "open-source-free-no-strings-attached" idea when it comes to software, unfortunately, it's not easy as it sounds.

**While most people see free alternatives, some users will see as another roadblock to productivity. Yes, they are free to use--but we don't see the hidden price.**

What's this *hidden price*? Well, it's many things slumped into one. **Yes, you'll need not spend a dime on these alternative programs,but you'll spend a hell lot of time trying to change your workflow.** This means studying these new applications--which spells out non-productivity! I mean, the time I'll spend learning the GIMP can be translated to a dozen thumbnails, studies and proposals I can send to clients.

Then, there's the hidden cost of quality. Yeah, **I respect the GIMP and I think that it's a nice application, but at the end of the day, I'll still use to Photoshop.** Why? Because it just works with my work. I'm not saying that all free and open-source applications suck, it's just that for me, who is so used to the applications I work with, making the change just isn't work it--yet.

### Post by deanjm1963 on 2007-05-16
If you're doing "high end fashion magazine artwork" stick with Adobe. I'm a graphic designer and I work from home, I have done for 8 years. I don't do a lot of high-end stuff, but the open source software out there is quite capable of doing it. Sometimes it takes a little "work-a-round" to get things just right, but it is do-able.

Inkscape is an excellent replacement for Illustrator, Gimp is also a great replacement for Photoshop, it's only real drawback is that it doesn't support CMYK yet, tho its supposedly on its way. This is where Scribus comes in, an excellent replacement for InDesign or Quark, it converts RGB images into CMYK on the fly when creating seps. The latest version of Scribus 1.3.4 (which is a developmental version but HIGHLY stable) has everything you'd need in a page layout program, great PDF creation too.

I took the plunge and when cold turkey when changing from MS to open source. I had a 3 month break and just "dove in the deep end". At times is was frustrating, but now I look back and think I could never go back to "the other", I like the way I can tailor my OS for my needs
