### Post by Joe_CoT on 2006-11-29
Firstly, you'll find that a large amount of the power required for a pc is the monitor; if you have a low power lcd for your server, or no monitor at all, you're much better off. Either way, I don't picture you needing a powered-on monitor on the server very often, so continuing...

If you're really concerned about power, you'll probably want to look at [Intel Pentium M and AMD Turion Desktop Motherboards](http://www.silentpcreview.com/article300-page1.html). Both chips are designed for low power consumption. After that, you can look at low power hard drives (which might not be necessary). There isn't really much to do with the RAM. For the video card, I suggest sticking with integrated graphics. After that, get a low power power supply that has maybe 40 or so more watts of output than what you need for the system, and not much more. You might be able to get away with a passive cooling solution, or maybe just the fan in the power supply.

All that said, there's always an efficiency to cost ratio; the most specialized for low power consumption, but more expensive it'll be. Keep this in mind. You may just want to go for the bottom-of-the-line dell or emachine for a home server. I really can't picture anything faster than 1 ghz being an issue, when you're using a server in the way you describe.

You might consider getting a small flash drive (ie usb stick) instead of the floppy. They're fairly cheap, last longer, and you'll need to change it much, much less often ;)
