### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-27
Ok, I can understand your frustration, but I suggest that you take a deep breath and try to understand that Linux isn't Windows and doesn't try to be.

There are major differences and you are on a steep learning curve here.

What I suggest is that you learn to crawl, before you walk.

There are a huge number of programs in the repositories and if you haven't already done so, I suggest that you have a look at this document:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty)

It is a great reference and will be of use to you in almost every area that you need help.

I suggest that at this stage, you stay well away from packages that are not in the repositories, as many of them are in sourcecode and require you to compile them and quite often satisfy what are called dependancies manually. This is not for the beginner and you need to take things a step at a time.

If you open the [COLOR="Blue"]Applications>Add/Remove[/COLOR] window and in the right-hand side click on the drop-down list and choose [COLOR="Blue"]All available Applications[/COLOR], this will give you a very large selection of applications to install painlessly. When it has finished, it also tells you the menu section where to find the application.

Stay with this for a while and then have a play with it's big brother, [COLOR="Blue"]Synaptic[/COLOR], which is availabel in  [COLOR="Blue"]System>Administration>Synaptic Package Manager[/COLOR]

If you stick with it and don't try to be too ambitious initially, it will all come together for you.
Don't expect miracles overnight, it has taken you years to learn to drive Windows.

Regards,
Roger :cool:
