### Post by EReckase on 2006-08-01
Avoid ATI graphics chipsets in the notebooks.  I have an HP Pavilion dv8000 (AMD64) with the ridiculous Radeon Xpress 200m graphics, and I've had nothing but trouble with the proprietary ATI drivers ( other than 8.24.8 ).  Go for nVidia and you'll be OK.

Also, check to see what the manufacturer of the wireless chipset is.  Avoid Broadcom if you can (although they're in a lot of them) since there's some issues with Broadcom drivers (I'm using ndiswrapper for mine, can't get the native Dapper ones to work).
