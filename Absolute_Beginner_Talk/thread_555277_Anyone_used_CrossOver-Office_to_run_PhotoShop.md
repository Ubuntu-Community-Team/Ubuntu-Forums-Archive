### Post by FM1 on 2007-09-20
"Just as they would on XP or Mac," no. Photoshop 7.0 and earlier are usable with a few bugs, but CS and later are listed as "known not to work" and "untested", apparently because the product activation fails under Crossover (and presumably, Wine). I've read about ways to get CS and later working, but I'm not sure that is legal even if it's only separate partitions on the same drive, which are obviously on the same computer and cannot be used at the same time. Anyway, legally "grey" or not, I tried installing CS2 under Crossover Linux 6.0 (install failed) and then tried a workaround but the program quit with a message about failed authorisation. I ended up removing it and just using The GIMP unless I need to do something that *requires* Photoshop, for which I have to boot to Windows. For most of the work I do, GIMP is quite adequate, and GIMPShop moves the menu items around to make it easier for those of us accustomed to Photoshop.

### Post by hiloguy on 2007-10-17
Finally, a success story!  After several failed attempts to get PhotoShop to install with CrossOver (Ubuntu 7.04), I uninstalled the version of C/O I had (6.0) and installed 6.2.0.  After that, I tried once more to install PhotoShop and this time I got the same "installation failed" report, but it installed anyway.  I've worked with it for several hours and have not found a single glitch.

I was so stoked that I then installed Adobe PageMill (I know, it's old, but I've used it on hundreds of Web pages and it works fine for what I do, so why "upgrade?")  Anyway, it works fine, too.

Those two apps were what kept me in the air about dropping Windows altogether, prostituting myself  :)  by running a dual-boot machine just so I could use PhotoShop or what.  

Now I am FREE!  And to those who are confused about the posts recommending GIMP as a PhotoShop substitute, I can just offer this: For a new user who wants a program that will in fact do a lot of what P/S will, go for it.  But for anyone who has been using P/S for many years, especially professionally, I'd suggest forget GIMP.

And if there is ever an open source package that will replace CrossOver, I'd give that a try, as I fully support open source at every level.  I just needed to get this done and working, and for now, it does and I'm officially Linux-only!

Thanks so much to all of you who have helped me get up and running with your patient and ongoing support.  I will check the newbie questions and if I can give any tried-and-proven help, I will.  It's only fair!

Aloha from Hawaii!
