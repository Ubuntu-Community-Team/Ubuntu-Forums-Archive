### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-02-20
There are a number of possibilities.

One is that your ISP may be running into problems with respect to adequacy of bandwidth in relation to the number of users of its service.  This can mean degraded quality of internet connection, or even the attempt at connection timing out.  Where in the world are you?  In Australia, for example the ADSL broadband is known in some quarters as 'fraudband' for like 'service' as you describe.

The problem could, however, be related to a wireless router periodically needing to be reset, if you are using such equipment.  Turning it off, waiting a minute or two, then powering up again can reset it.
