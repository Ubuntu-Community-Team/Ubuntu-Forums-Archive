### Post by spiderbatdad on 2008-04-19
I believe you are talking about a Dynamic Naming Service. Names, as opposed to ip addresses, must be registered by a DNS. DYNDNS provides such a service. To have a free service through them, you have to append the name with something like "whatever.end-of-internet.com," or "whatever.podzone.com."  You can get a paid custom name for around 10.00 a year.
