### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-09
Ummmm, its not a good idea to have no swap file. Swap is the virtual memory that your computer uses when it runs out or just doesn't use your ram. Most computers need at least twice their ram in swap. I assume the spontaneous crashing is likely to be tied to you not having any swap at all... an easy recommendation is to use [gparted]("http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php") live disk to resize 2 GB (or more) off of your install and make it swap.

Of course the other option is to reinstall. What kind of trouble did you have with installing? I mean I don't recall ever hearing anyone having trouble making a swap... if the live cd didn't work for ya you may wanna try the [alternate]("http://releases.ubuntu.com/") CD, it works more often.

On a final note, you really don't need 40 GB for an Ubuntu install, the main root files after a clean install only really add up to 3 GB 4 at the most. I usually recommend 6-8 for a buffer of enough to install as many programs you like and kubuntu/xubuntu desktop even. You may want to consider partitioning a /home directory with the rest of your space.

To read up more on installs, look [here.]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php")
