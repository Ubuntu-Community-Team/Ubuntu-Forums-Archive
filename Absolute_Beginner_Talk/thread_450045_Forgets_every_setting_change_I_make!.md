### Post by esoterica on 2007-05-20

Well, there's probably the answer to the problem. One thing I've noticed in using Windows and Linux. When something is released to the public by Microsoft as "beta" it's usually just about ready for the general public to run and use it so the masses tend to not find any real problems when using it. However it's been my experience that when something is released as "beta" in Linux they mean it rather literally and you can just about guarantee if your a new or slightly less experienced Linux user your going to have problems with it.

If your a new user to Linux and it sounds like you are, I'd advise staying away from beta and experimental software if you want to stable environment to learn Linux in. Save that stuff for after you've spent more time getting to know the OS.

Personally I like Windows and Linux both a lot, I have the luxury though of owning multiple systems so I can risk losing one. I'm also by no means what so ever a Linux expert, but I have used it enough to be completely gun shy now of Linux beta software. If your wanting something you can just jump into and go and only have your single PC you should probably stick to the Operating System for now your the most comfortable in or you are likely going to have a bad experience like this.

When I first started playing with Linux I decided it best to just get a cheap second hand computer to do so on, then no harm done when you screw it up. There are posts in here that explain how to reinstall X, I don't know how to tell you how to undo everything you may have done though that messed things up for you.
