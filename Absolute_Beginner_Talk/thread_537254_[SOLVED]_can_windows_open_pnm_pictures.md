### Post by Paulmd on 2007-08-28
> **Steveway said:**
> Gif for a lossless compression? You'r kidding! Ok if that picture only has 256 colors then I would believe you.

For scans of text, and line art, GIF compression can beat Jpeg, if one takes care to reduce to the colors actually present. PNG generally achieves higher compression, but not always. GIFs are also much faster to load.

Jpeg can be real horrid for scans of text documents, as sharp edges and lines become unclear. You can improve the quality of JPEGS, at a cost of MUCH larger images. PNG provides better quality, but you really don't need 16 million colors to display a b/w image. You can reduce the color depth on a png, but not it doesn't make a huge difference in image size. For sending scans of b/w documents, an 8-color gif is actually provides better compression than PNG (which is atypical in general).
