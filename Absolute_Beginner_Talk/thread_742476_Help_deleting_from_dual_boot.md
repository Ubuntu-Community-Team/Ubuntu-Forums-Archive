### Post by wannadumpwindows on 2008-04-01
The last time I needed to do it, all I did was boot into Vista, right click Computer, click Manage, and under the Storage tab, click Disk Management. Then delete your Ubuntu partition. Extend your Vista partition to use the space again. And then download EasyBCD to fix the boot loader. Although I'm sure there are better ways to do it like fixmbr or sumthin like that from the Vista CD. It was pretty simple. Hope I helped.
