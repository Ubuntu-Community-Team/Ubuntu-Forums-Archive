Where are you located?  Unless you're pretty far out there, the year that you refer to would have given you plenty of time to find a couple of used serial external modems.  I've got 4 of them now, three USR's and a weird Boca I got for free.  I used ebay and craigslist.  The total cash outlay has been around $40 or $50.  I couldn't get the Boca to work in Windows but it does work with Feisty!  
To me, the winmodem directions are confusing and of limited value.  Even if you were to get one working, lots of them break again after you update to a new kernel.
It's far easier to find a full-hardware, external modem.  If serial is a pain, there are adapters that will allow you to go to USB.

I'm very curious to hear from anyone about the new Zoom 3095.  It's a tiny USB modem that Zoom claims to be Linux compatible.
