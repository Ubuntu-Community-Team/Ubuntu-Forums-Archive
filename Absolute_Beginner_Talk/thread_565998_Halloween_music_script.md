### Post by ryanVickers on 2007-10-02
Well, it's October now, and that means that Halloween is not too far off now!  I decided I was going to work on a little project to play scary music in the background and that switch to a "sudden shock" theme when the door opens or something :), and I started early because I thought it would be hard, but I guess I don't know my own strength! :p  I finished it in about 8 minutes, so, here it is! :KS

To use it, simply extract the folder, and run the script (in the terminal!) by clicking it (you may have to set it as executable first though).  It's quite simple - it makes sure that zenity is installed, and if not will prompt you non-stop until you obey :), and then if it is installed (or once your done) it will begin - simply click the button to start the music, and click it again to switch!  You can go back and forth quite fast...

You can run it out of where ever, and even change the folder name, just be sure that you don't rename the music, and make sure the music and script are in the same folder! :p

so, without any further ado, [halloProject.tar.bz2]("http://www.hotlinkfiles.com/files/442822_cz35g/halloProject.tar.bz2")
And if you want to swap the included music out for any one of these: [replacementMusic.tar.bz2](http://www.hotlinkfiles.com/files/442832_gt2rz/replacementMusic.tar.bz2) (just put them in the folder and be sure to call them "amb" or "cut" (for ambient or cutscene), or just change the script if your confident enough :))
Also, this is just thunder [Thunder.ogg]("http://www.hotlinkfiles.com/files/442818_ku23z/Thunder.ogg").  I don't think you could really use it as a main sound, but if you play it constantly along side the script in something other than "totem" :)

Theres' not much to it, but still, please don't just steal the code! (not that I'm really expecting that of anyone here :))
