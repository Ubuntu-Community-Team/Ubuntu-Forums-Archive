### Post by richardgundersen on 2007-09-19
You can try your luck with ndiswrapper like I did - it might work, but not for me because it turned out to be a really dodgy chipset I had. 

But, if you have USB in your laptop, I would recommend just buying a newish USB wireless adaptor. I have both LM Technologies and a Belkin one and both work with Feisty perfectly - no configuration at all and they support WPA2. You can pick them up for about £10 ($20).

That's what I'd do - rather than spend hours trying to get an old wifi card working. Depends how much time you have to spare ;-)
