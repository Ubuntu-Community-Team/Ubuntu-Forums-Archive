### Post by MasterOfDisaster on 2006-11-09
Beryl is basically alpha software, and while offering more effects is more unstable.  Note that I used it in XGL with no problems, and in AIGLX (seemed very slow but no crashes).

Compiz is the original, and is more focused on stability then more and more cool effects.  Pretty sure that it doesn't have a GUI for configuration in Dapper, and many people prefer, or at least use Beryl instead: 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=278664&highlight=beryl%2C+compiz%2C+3d)](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=278664&highlight=beryl%2C+compiz%2C+3d)).

Here's a thread on Compiz vs. Beryl:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=268548&highlight=beryl%2C+compiz%2C+3d](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=268548&highlight=beryl%2C+compiz%2C+3d)

Anyways, I have a 6600GT and both worked great under XGL.  There was a thread somewhere with a bunch of howtos for beryl/compiz and XGL/AIGLX, but I can't seem to find it.
