### Post by pappapump on 2007-05-31
You know that you should only use a bios update in dos mode right?
If you are booting into xbuntu and trying a bios update there then you will experience severe problems.
Install kfloppy and kwikdisk if you haven't already and see if that helps with your problem.
Make sure you have at least some type of dos based bootup disk or CD such as your hard drive utility CD handy and choose to simply boot to dos, then try using your floppy.
IF the floppy fails at that point then its your hardware not the os.
Remember that all floppies are prone to fail due to dust generated from wind turbulence exhausting from the rear of your PC.
Unfortunately, most floppies are located in-line to the air flow of your power supplies exhaust fan and due to the nature of the floppy's open to the air case structure, dust often renders them useless within the first year of use.
