### Post by nickpaton on 2007-04-27
Hi and welcome to Ubuntu and the forums!

You don't actually need any antivirus software since there are no 'real' Linux viruses , and in any case the software structure is such that a virus would not be able to proliferate throughout the distribution.

Have a read of [this article]("http://librenix.com/?inode=21").

Firewalls - Ubuntu comes with a firewall installed as standard - called iptables, there is a GUI which you can download via Synaptic (System > Administration > Synaptic Package Manager) called Firestarter.

In order to connect to a printer on another PC or to 'talk' via a network to other PC's, at first it's easier to simply disable Firestarter.

A good Firestarter tutorial can be found [here]("http://www.fs-security.com/docs.php")

I also have done a little HOWTO within a post [here]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=202605&page=30#298") 

Both will help you to configure Firestarter to be enabled and to connect to other PC's on a network.

Good luck!
