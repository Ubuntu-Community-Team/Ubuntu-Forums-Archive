### Post by Iowa Dave on 2007-04-09
Open Office gets most jobs done. I'd recommend it whenever you need to integrate content from multiple applications, i.e. a spreadsheet chart inserted into a word processing document.

The learning curve is definitely a factor with OpenOffice, but the help files really do help if you take time to read them.

MS Office 2007 has gotten a lot of press. They did change the interface. Newcomers to MS Office will find the new version easier to use than the old one. Experienced users of all prior versions back to Office 97 will feel like fish out of water. Over time, though, MS/OS users will migrate to Office 2007 and its children.

In other words, people are going to be learning new software eventually, one way or another. OpenOffice has a lot going for it, and I expect it will help win converts to Linux from MS/OS. The price is right. Unless you need VBA, and few people do, OpenOffice could be a good choice.
