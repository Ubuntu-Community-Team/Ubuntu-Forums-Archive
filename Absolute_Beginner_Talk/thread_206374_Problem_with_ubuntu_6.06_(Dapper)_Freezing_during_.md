### Post by bobpur on 2006-06-30
I skimmed over your other thread and I think you got some good advice there that may apply here. Check it out if you haven't already. 
I'd remove the PCI video card and install using the onboard graphics until things got running then download and install drivers for your video card and install it and deactivate onboard video in the BIOS.
I wouldn't rule out a bad install CD either. If you can't download and burn a new one, have a friend or computer store do it for you. Burn it at a slow speed to insure a good burn.
I had a similar problem and never could get it resolved to a point where my computer would run without locking up at some point.
BTW, I don't remember you saying where you got your video drivers. Some people prefer Automatix. I had better luck with Easy Ubuntu in subsequent installs without problems.
