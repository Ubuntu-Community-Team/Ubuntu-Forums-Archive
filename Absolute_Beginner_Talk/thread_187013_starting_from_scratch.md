### Post by catlett on 2006-06-02
Do you have a cd from ShipIt or a linux magazine with the installation on it? Or did you download the iso? Did you already burn the iso to disk?
If you have a cd with ubuntu on it you will start your computer with the cd in the drive.
First start your computer and see if it is already set up to boot to the cd drive. If not you need to enter into bios. Bios is the programming that runs your motherboard. It deals with starting your computer and accessing your hard drive where your operating system is installed. Once your hard drive starts the OS takes over.
So even if you don't have an os the computer can start and access the cd drive.
When your computer starts there is a splash page with your computer company's logo on it. This is the screen you acces the bios from. Look for "press F10 for setup" "enter F8 for menu" something like that. All manufacturwers are different. You may have to google your computer makers bios features to find out how to access. If your lucky it will have text on the splash image with instructions.
Once you get in bios you wil be at a text based menu. Use the arrow keys to navigate to boot or boot sequence. Again they are all a little different. What you want is to establish the boot order. You want the cd drive to be ahead of the hard drive in the boot order. Once you enter the correct settings hit F10 to save and exit.
Restart your computer with the disk in it. It will boot the installation process. If you have the Dapper live cd install, this is the best guide [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/windowstoubuntu.html](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/windowstoubuntu.html)
