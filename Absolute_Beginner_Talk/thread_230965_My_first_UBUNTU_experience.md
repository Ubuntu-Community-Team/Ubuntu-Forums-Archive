### Post by geokok1981 on 2006-08-06
My friend do not be dissapointed....
On second thought you should be!!!
Yes, you should be very dissapointed that a company such as ATI chooses to make life hard for its customers by providing low quality drivers that are hard to install.
I, myself have an ATI card and feel like a second class citizen when compared to the lucky owners of an Nvidia.

Anyway, the solution to your problem is here:
[http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide")

You can choose to install the drivers from the repositories (method 1) or the newest ones provided from ATI (method 2).

I myself have found method 1 to be the safest bet for a new user so I would recommend to follow that one.

Before closing, I would like to tell you that I am an ubuntu user for the past two months.Like yourself I could do "magic" in windows but had serious problems with Linux at first. I soon realised it was not the OS that was difficult to operate but myself, being used to working in a specific way for everything, the windows way.

Just to make things easier for you at the beginning, you should consider using Easy Ubuntu or Automatix to get some things working (mp3, dvd, truetype fonts, etc.)

Don't give up! The best thing you can do is keep an open mind and try to learn Ubuntu as something new, not something that should be like windows. I am really a long way from claiming that Linux is a second nature to me, but I also can't understand how could have been using windows for so long, when something so fascinating like Ubuntu, was there, just waiting for me to open my eyes..........and trully see....
