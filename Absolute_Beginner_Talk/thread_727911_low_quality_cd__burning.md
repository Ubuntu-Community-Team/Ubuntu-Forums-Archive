### Post by russo.mic on 2008-04-13
Okay guys, time for an audiophile to clarify all of this.

RIPPING is the process of converting the CD to files you can then play on your computer, place on a portable media player, or reburn as a CD.  WMP rips all of it's audio to it's own LOSSY format.

Lossy - compressed and now unable to be restored to original fidelity
Lossless - compressed yet still able to be restored to original fidelity

to obtain lossless quality, rip with anything that can rip to WAV files, (VERY BIG), or FLAC (not as big), or Apple Lossless (only ITunes, not a very compatible format)

May I recommend Sound Juicer.  Go into the preferences, and adjust sound quality to taste/preference.  if you rip as a Lossless file (FLAC) there will be absloutely NO quality loss no matter what app you use to burn the CD.  Keep in mind however, that once you've ripped the music in a LOSSY format, nothing can be done to restore the audio to it's original quality.

Thanks for still paying attention to audio quality!  As a recording engineer, this subject touches very close to my profession and my passion, and it's nice to still find some people who aren't content with the loss of quality involved with these lossy formats.

Russo
