### Post by Brunellus on 2007-02-02
Recommendation:  stay with the mac.  If your outside vendors require Quarx or Photoshop output, you really have no choice.

The GIMP is an excellent program, and I am usually the first to praise it, but it is really not up to the task of real pre-press work, as it lacks proper CMYK support.  

Remember that you would be shifting OPERATING SYSTEMS--Linux is NOT Windows.  It is a very safe bet that software meant for one OS will NOT work on another OS without considerable work.  The WINE project (and its commercial branch, Crossover Office) tries hard to provide some level of compatibility, but it's not something I like to "sell" to new users a lot because compatibility is (still) hit-and-miss.

### Post by Unterseeboot_234 on 2007-02-02
Let me add input as a "typesetter" for ... well longer than 20 years. I was the guy that had to fix the artistic files for the printing press. Ad agencies could certainly produce better design, but I could rebuild their art for the production. I'd have to tell you that ubuntu or any linux will help you in publishing, but it is definitely not there yet in art production. If I was still freelance Yellow Pages ads, I would earnestly try to see what ubuntu art programs could prep and export to get ready for Windows. The Mac is SLOWWWW and usually has someone in charge of the dept that doesn't even know what bit-size their hardware is. I will never work again for an outfit using Macs. XP was a clunker compared to Win98. I suspect Vista will have bugs plus other surprises.

If you will set aside time to learn what art programs are and are not on linux, then you will find a valuable tool for prepping and a workhorse for internet work. You "roll-your-own" on the linux software and that is especially true of 64-bit. I run AMD64 and I crave the speed it gives with video editing. Photoshop is the one software that won't configure at all to a linux machine and that is probably true of the rest of the StudioCV series. Give it a year, and I think Scribus will outperform Quark.

If you do try Linux, try Xara. It's available commercially for Win. Xara just needs to export SVG and I'll never touch Illustrator ever again.
