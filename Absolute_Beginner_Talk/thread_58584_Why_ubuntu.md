### Post by kvidell on 2005-08-20
If you have no other experience with linux, then you should better be able to pick up on concepts quickly and have a lot of patience...

Gentoo provides you with very little in the way of automation for the most part.
For instance, a rough example of it's install procedure is that you load their LiveCD, make a chroot'd loopback filesystem in a series of directories within the RamFS then drop it on to your harddrive.

It's tricksy but very worthwhile if you like to learn lots and quickly.

Other distrobutions are meant for different purposes. Some are meant to be workstations, others are more atuned for the desktop (this distrobution), some are meant to be highly customisable (Gentoo, Slackware), some are better for small servers (RedHat/Fedora) some are better for large servers (SuSE/Novell SBS).

I personally am a Debian/Gentoo boy. I use Hoary and Breezy on my headless 1Us, I'm personally not a fan of Ubuntu on desktops because it's either too stable or not stable enough (Hoary/Breezy).

I'd say fiddle around and find one that suits your tastes.
Gentoo and Ubuntu have the two best communities I've seen for linux distrobutions, and you'll never find an easier package management system than the Aptitude Deb Package system found in Debian based distrobutions (Knoppix, Ubuntu, Mepis...)

Have fun with it and good luck :)
- Kev
