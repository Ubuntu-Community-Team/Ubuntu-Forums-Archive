### Post by haldean on 2007-11-04
You can, but you risk destroying your distribution. Some people have upgraded that way and completely messed up their computer, because you get new versions of system libraries, but some of the programs you still have on your computer require older versions, or vice versa (new programs that require new libraries, but you've only got the old ones). So yeah - you can do it - but it's way better to upgrade instead.
