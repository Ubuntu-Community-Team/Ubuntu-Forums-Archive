### Post by mlentink on 2007-08-28
Wow. 
I don't  know where to start.
Basically it's dividing a physical harddisk into two or more 'logical' pieces, called 'partitions'. In Windows, you would have a C:, D:, and perhaps also a E:...., but they would all be parts of just one physical harddisk. Partitioning allows you to treat all those pieces as separate disks, so you could put one operating system on one piece, and OS #2 on another. Or you could use one piece for your OS, a second to install programs and a third to store your data.
