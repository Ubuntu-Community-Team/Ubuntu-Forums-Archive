### Post by MenZa on 2007-08-12
> **DSdragondude said:**
> Will do but how does burning it at a lower speed help?

Your burner will be more "careful" when placing the data on the disk; ensuring nothing is fragmented, etc.

When burning important data to a disk, it's always recommended to burn at as low speeds as possible. It's a very common to have people burn Ubuntu at 24x, only to find out they can't boot the disk, or the installation fails.
