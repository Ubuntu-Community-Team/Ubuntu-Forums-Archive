### Post by Naralas on 2006-11-14
Mail clients that are on your computer operate with mail servers. They send and receive (typically) through a POP and SMTP server which you have access to. Hotmail stores your emails and lets you look at them through a web application, it won't give you access to "higher up" functions like downloading e-mails to your computer because it takes away from the amounts of Ad's they can force feed you (at the cost of much lag)

GMail (far superior to Hotmail) offers POP service and although I do not like there "relevant ads" (not because im a security freak just because I hate people who make too much damn money) they are a good option for what you are looking for. They also don't add a little advertisement to everything you send.

Google also offers the "how to" you are looking for in there Help. (Great How-To's with pictures and support for many applications, its pretty damn good)
