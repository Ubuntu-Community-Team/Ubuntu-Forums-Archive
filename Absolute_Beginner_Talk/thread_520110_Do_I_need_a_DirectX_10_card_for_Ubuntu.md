### Post by botulismo on 2007-08-07
Well, the answer to the question is that yes, when Wine includes DirectX 10 support, you will need a DirectX 10 compatible card in order to play DirectX 10 games (it's just the way it works, for example, you can't run an unhacked version of Oblivion on Wine without a DirectX 9 compatible card) - but if you are going to be playing Linux native games, the specification that matters is OpenGL - and most DirectX 9 and 10 cards use OpenGL 2.0, as 3.0 hasn't been released yet. So if you were sticking to only Linux native games, either card would be fine. But if you are going to be using a Wine game to play a DirectX 10 game, then you will need a DirectX 10 compatible card.

To be honest with you, though, much like Oblivion was hacked to be able to played on DirectX 8 cards, I'm sure many DirectX 10 games will be hacked to be playable on DX9 cards... Even more so because of the number of XP users unwilling to convert to Vista which is the only OS with DX10 support (Microsoft is ridiculous, forcing people to upgrade their OS just to play a game.)
