### Post by Lary Grant on 2006-03-21
I have used CD ripping software and Acoustica on Windows to rip voice (lecture) CDs to RealAudio.  I would get about an hour in about a 7MB file.

I am now trying to do the same process in Ubuntu.  I rip the CDs to ogg, then use Sound Converter to create a WAV, then use RealProducer to get the RealAudio.  However, I am getting RealAudio files in excess of 100 MB!!  What can I do to reduce the size?

---

### Post by Lary Grant on 2006-03-21
I figured it out... I needed the -ad (audience) option of RealProducer.  I used -ad '56k Dial-up' to get it much smaller.
