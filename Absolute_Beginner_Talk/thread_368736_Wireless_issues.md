Yeah, this is where I'm kind of lost at.  What are you comparing your 41% to the 100%?  Is the 100% previously in Windows using the wireless zero config.  Also did the distance from your wireless card to the wireless access point change?

Couple things I can gaurantee here... wireless in windows does not always report the correct signal ratings.  When I had Windows running it said my connectivity was 95%, but when I looked on the router settings the signal was really only 63%.  The distance never changed... the only change I made on my system was Ubuntu, and when I did the iwlist scan, my signal reported a much more accurate 65%.  So perhaps you are not seeing the 100% signal due to seeing what the numbers really should be for once.  One thing you can do to help your internet speed is to disable ipv6.
