### Post by Nequeo on 2005-09-13
[QUOTE=Villenya]no one can help?[/QUOTE]
 If you have a fast connection, you can type 'sudo apt-get install kubuntu-desktop' from the command line to install KDE. Then when you log on to your computer, you can pick between Gnome and KDE desktops, and decide which one you like better.

However, that being said, if you look in the lower corner of your screen you should see a 'workspace switcher'. (Unless you've moved or removed it, of course!) It'll look like a bunch of grey boxes. Each of those boxes represents a different 'workspace', that you can switch between. That way you can have web browsing on one workspace, e-mail on another, graphics programs on a third, and so on... And your window selector won't get so cluttered. 

You can move windows back and forth across the desktops by right clicking on the title bar, and you can also switch quickly between the workspaces by spinning the mouse-wheel when your mouse is over the switcher.
