### Post by ubuntu27 on 2008-02-16
There is NASA's [World Wind]("http://worldwind.arc.nasa.gov/") which is Open Source.

[http://worldwind.arc.nasa.gov/](http://worldwind.arc.nasa.gov/)

NOTE: World Wind unlike Google Earth has lots of scientific information such as temperature, altitudes, distance, etc. BUT, unlike Google Earth, it does not show towns and buildings. In conclusion, WOrld Wind is nice if you are studying geography or other fields related to earth.

Read [a review about World Wind]("http://www.linux.com/feature/47650")

KDE's [Marble project.]("http://edu.kde.org/marble/")- Still under development.
