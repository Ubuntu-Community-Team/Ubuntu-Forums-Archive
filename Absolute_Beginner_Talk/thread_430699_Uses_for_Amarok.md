### Post by dfreer on 2007-05-02
I really like the Wikipedia/Lyrics scripts: During any song I can click on a tab and poof: there's the lyrics for the song, and the wikipedia entry for the band. It's kinda of a old feature now, I think alot of other media players have copied it.
CD Cover manager is *pretty* good, the only thing there is that you can't copy + paste the covers on to your HD from amarok (I like to backup the covers).
Another thing is the GUI is designed really well, It looks a lot nicer than any other *nix player (Although Songbird is pretty sweet too), my problem with xmms was that it always looked SO small, and I couldn't figure out (1-2 years ago) how to change it's size.

Last.fm is a free service, they make money through advertising on their website, and 'possibly' to advertise in between songs, although I've never heard any adverts in between songs. You can either use their website to listen to music, or you can download the last.fm player, or indeed any player which supports last.fm. The last.fm software is also open-source, so it's all good ;)

[http://www.lastfm.com](http://www.lastfm.com)

You may also want to take a look at Jamendo, which is a website for unsigned artists to upload their albums for people to listen to, although even signed artists can upload, as long as they agree to release their music under a creative commons license.

[http://www.jamendo.com](http://www.jamendo.com)
