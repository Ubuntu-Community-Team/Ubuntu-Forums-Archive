### Post by st33med on 2007-05-16
If you want iTunes, there is very little you can do. You can try virtualization software, or use WINE to run iTunes 4.0. However, there is other programs that are like iTunes. Banshee is a good one (even though it is beta) and it plays unprotected AAC tracks, but not protected AACs bought from the iTunes store. The Banshee developers, I hear, are working on a solution. No program that I know of besides iTunes can play protected AAC.

   Any music player can play protected AACs bought from iTunes.  Here is how:
1) buy & download music from iTunes (at this point, you are in windows or your Mac of course)
2) inside iTunes, burn a CD with your music
3) exit iTunes, and Windows, then either load up Ubuntu or go to your second computer, however you do it, load up you music CD, and rip the tracks.  You might have to manually type in the names of your songs, but so what?
4) now you can play your MP3, ogg, flac, or whatever files on whatever you want

   Now, was that hard to do?  *sigh*  :)

  There are other alternatives however:

1) Don't buy an iPod, buy an ARCHOS music player.  My Archos 504 not only plays a variety of music & videos, and it is 100% compatible  with Linux, including Ubuntu.  Amarok and Banshee are just a joy to use  with my Archos 504.   Maybe it's all because the  internal O/S on the Archos  linux.  :)

E-classical is cheaper than iTunes, just as good if not better quality than iTunes,   and the music you download is totally free of any DRM - you can play or copy it as much as you want.  Look around, there are others as well.
