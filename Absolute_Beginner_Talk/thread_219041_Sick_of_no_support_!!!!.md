### Post by Brunellus on 2006-07-19
I'm going to stand up and defend the corporate types here.  

Individual users and enthusiasts make for a nice community, but they won't drive mass adoption.  All great mass conversions in history have had an element of coercion, and computer software is no different.

Microsoft came to power not because of Windows, but MS-DOS.  IBM PCs running MS-DOS were by far not the best technical solution for many problems.  There were better solutions out there at the time.  

But in the end, they gained not because they won over individual enthusias users, but because purchasing departments in large corporations and Federal Government agencies bought them by the ton.  The userbase simply adopted what the bureaucrats decided to buy in bulk, and that priming of the pump set the stage for the computing world as we see it today.

Ubuntu (and, more likely, its commercial competitors SLED and RHEL) will compete for the same large organizations.  Over time, their economic model will win.  Then users will be forced to come around.  The fact that they will then all be running Free Software will be incidental to them.
