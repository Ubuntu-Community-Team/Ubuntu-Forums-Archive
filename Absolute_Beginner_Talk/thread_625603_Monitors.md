### Post by Grey Box on 2007-11-28
People will no doubt correct me if I'm wrong, but I don't think the type of flatpanel display you have makes any difference to Linux. What is important is how your graphics card will handle the display it and whether the graphics card is supported (almost all of them are).

Again people will differ, but honestly I don't think you need to worry about getting a display that has 'too high' resolution. A 1920x1200 display (full high definition) is wonderful to look at, regardless if it's a 17 inch (like on my old Dell) or projected on a wall at 5 metres wide. 

For readability and so forth, you can set up Gnome to adjust font sizes and so on for the DPI of your display. A 17 inch display running at 1920x1200 has a DPI of roughly 108, I seem to remember.

So, go forth and buy what you want. Spend to your budget and get the best you can for the money. 

Ps: A 2GHz P4 is fine and should work well with most resolutions. It will always feel a little sluggish, compared to a new box, but if your gfx card is accelerated (nvidia geforce or something similar) you are well equipped to get the full 3d bling that linux offers nowadays, at whatever resolution, even on quite old accelerated graphics cards. If your gfx card is not accelerated, then you should consider buying a cheap old one or something, just to kick things along a bit. But Ubuntu won't let you run in bling-mode if your gfx card doesn't support it anyway.

### Post by Blojic on 2007-11-28
LCD panels will just work as your CRT, no probs.

The only thing that needs consideration is that LCD moniters only look sharp at their native resolution. eg. a normal aspect 19" LCD usually needs to be set at 1280x1024. I've seen people come unstuck by buying a 19" wide screen (1440x900) and then find their graphics driver can't display that resolution. - It needs bearing in mind.
