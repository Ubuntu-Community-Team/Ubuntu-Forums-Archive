### Post by climatewarrior on 2008-04-21
> A second question: will Hardy as run through Windows enable me to connect to the Internet? I've played with the Gutsy LiveCD environment, but the inability to connect to the Internet significantly limits the experience.

If by this you mean installing it trough wubi then yes you can use the internet but you have to configure it first. The reason you cant use the internet on the live cd right now is because it is not configured. Configuring wireless cards in linux can sometimes be hard. It depend on what card you have. Some support linux really well like the intel cards but some do not support linux and the only way to use them is trough programs like ndiswrapper. Check in system/administration/hardware drivers to see if ubuntu knows of a driver for your card. If it does then you are in luck and configuring it will be extremely easy( even easier than on windows). In the case that ubuntu cant automatically do it for you yet we are here to help you.
