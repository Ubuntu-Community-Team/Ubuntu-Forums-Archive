### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-02-11
Unfortuntely, that was a fullish thing to do. The good news is that your Windows partition should still bi intact, and there are several ways to fix it.
1. Boot from Windows CD, get into recovery console and type fdisk /mbr.
2. Use s friend's PC to download a bootdisk from bootdisk.com, and use one of the ways outlined on [this page](http://www.users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p18.htm).
