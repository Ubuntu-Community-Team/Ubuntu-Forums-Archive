### Post by aysiu on 2007-02-20
What exactly is the problem? You want it cooler-looking?

You can go to [http://www.gnome-look.org](http://www.gnome-look.org) and download some themes and icons to change the look of your interface. Look particularly for GTK themes (**not** GDM themes--those are for the login screen), Metacity themes, and icon themes. Download the theme to your desktop, then drag and drop the .tar.gz to the theme manager window (System > Preferences > Themes).

If you prefer one menu, as opposed to three, right-click the panel (toolbar, whatever you want to call it) and select **Add to Panel**. Then add **Main Menu**.

Once you've done that, you can right-click the main menu (it'll be a yellow and orange circle) and **Edit Menus** to rearrange the menu layout to your own preference.

### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-02-21
Linux requires a learning curve.  No question about it.  Once you lift the hood, you find out you have a V12 under the hood, not that crappy 4-banger that Windows is built on.  The beauty of Linux is not how cool it looks, it's what it can do.  Linux works best when it's not loaded with eye-candy, but you can play with it and make it look cool.  It really depends on how you use the computer.

Notice how often you don't have to reboot under Linux.  I reboot about once every 3 months--and only because of system updates.  If I didn't care about the updates, then I would probably reboot once a year.  Get yourself a good UPS because power will be your real issue with uptime.
