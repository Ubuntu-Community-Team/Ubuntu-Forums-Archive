### Post by H.E. Pennypacker on 2006-07-25
I believe, although I am not sure, the answer is in disabling the current file manager from startup, and adding Thunar instead.  I am pretty sure this is how it is done.  Go to Sessions, switch to the Startup tab, and select what you want to add to the startup, and what to remove.  You could also simply disable something from startup, instead of removing it.  That leaves the item on the list, but does not load it while starting up.  It's useful.
