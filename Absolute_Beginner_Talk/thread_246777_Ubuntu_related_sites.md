### Post by LotsOfPhil on 2006-08-29
I think you learn best by doing. Pick a manageable project that is interesting and new to you. Set up a webserver, write a script to do something near, understand what all the things in /etc/fstab mean, etc. The just search with MSN or Clusty (one could say "google") how to do it. Not only will you learn how to do a specific task, but you'll get the hang of using a search engine as your first resource (which is how I survive).

I'm saying all of this because you mentioned "lessons." If you are higher-level than this, try volunteering your time for some open source project (check out sourceforge). You could be a kernel janitor too.

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/)

As far as Ubuntu-specific news, I'm not aware of any news sites specific to Ubuntu and your best bet would probably be RSS feeds. I use Akregator in Kubuntu (not sure what the Gnome equivalent RSS reader is), and it has a couple RSS feeds already in there by default. The "Planet Ubuntu" RSS feed's address is [http://planet.ubuntu.com/rss20.xml](http://planet.ubuntu.com/rss20.xml). There's also the "Ubuntu Fridge" [http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/feed](http://fridge.ubuntu.com/node/feed). The others are KDE specific so probably wouldn't interest you (I assume you're using Ubuntu / Gnome). 

On a side note, I hope you don't think that Ubuntu users are all "pompous". I've often heard that's the case in the Fedora where every answer is RTFM (I can't vouch for that), but users in this forum are often very helpful and eager to get your problem resolved if they know the answer (even if they don't, they'll suggest something just to keep the thread moving along). Hopefully you decide to stay with this great distro and won't let one negative experience deter you.
