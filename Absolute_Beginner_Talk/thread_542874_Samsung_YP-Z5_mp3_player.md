### Post by NET WT on 2007-09-04
I have a Samsung YP-Z5 also. Mine works well with Ubuntu. Do you have the UMS option enabled? Just in case you don't, it can be enabled under "Settings>System Info"  on the device. Make sure "UMS Only" is on. If the option isn't available, update to the latest firmware version. Which is available for download on the Samsung website. Hope that helps.

### Post by NET WT on 2007-09-09
There is a user manual available for download on the Samsung website. You should download it. There are a few pages in it on how to upgrade the firmware. I have not yet tried to upgrade mine with Ubuntu though. So I'm not sure if it will upgrade properly. I upgraded mine a while back with Windows XP. I do think it will work with Ubuntu. You just move the DAT file into the "data" folder on your YP-Z5. Then disconnect the player, select upgrade and connect it again, will take a minute or so. The manual has better instructions than mine so you should check it out. When UMS is enabled on the device, it should work better with Ubuntu. When I transfer music and photos onto mine, I just drag-and-drop, and It works fine. I am also able to delete music I no longer want on it. Hope it works for you.
