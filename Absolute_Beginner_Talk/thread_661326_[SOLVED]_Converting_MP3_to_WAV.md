### Post by Giradman on 2008-01-07
> **suomalainen said:**
> But will these burn them as wav files or not?

Can't add much regarding a program to 'up convert' from MP3 to WAV, but keep in mind that your MP3 files are 'lossy', and when upgrading to WAV files the size will be expanded (like from 1 MB to 10 MB per minute, depending on the compression ratio of your MP3 files) - the point is that you will not have the same quality as from a WAV file 'ripped' from a CD - my point is that if your MP3 files were 'ripped' originally from CDs that you own, you may want to make new CDs in that fashion; OTOH, if these are 'downloaded' MP3s, then you'll have no choice - good luck in whatever your selection - :)

### Post by RomeReactor on 2008-01-07
> **suomalainen said:**
> But will these burn them as wav files or not?

It will create an audio CD playable in any standalone CD player. If what you want is to specifically transform MP3 files to WAV, then take a look at [SoundConverter]("http://soundconverter.berlios.de/") (for Gnome) or [SoundKonverter]("http://kde-apps.org/content/show.php?content=29024") (for KDE). Both are available in the repositories, so look for them in Synaptic or Add/Remove.
