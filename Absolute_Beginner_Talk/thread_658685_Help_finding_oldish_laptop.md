### Post by SidewinderPro2 on 2008-01-04
Older laptops are probably going to use IDE drives, which you'll have to configure as a slave for one if you want to dual boot. Its a pain in the neck and I can't remember how to do it. Most new computers use SATA II drives, which don't really need special setup outside of RAID.


I'd go with the EEE if its gonna be more of a project than a serious component of your computer life. Newegg seems to be the cheapest/normal price, but their listings are all over the place with the lack of supply of the little midget laptops.
