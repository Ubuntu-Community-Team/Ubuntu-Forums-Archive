### Post by bpollen on 2008-03-30
EVERY operating system has cr*p that most of us aren't going to use. In that respect, Ubuntu Ultimate Edition just joins every other operating system.

While UUE may be bloated (opinions obviously differ - I'm more anti-bloat...) it installs a large portion of the most popular programs in the repository for a wide variety of tasks. Since this IS "Absolute Beginner Talk" it would seem to be a positive thing for the novice to not have to learn much to try a variety of applications.  Novices HATE to RTFM. Hell, most of us hate to RTFM. 

From my perspective, it was the first linux distro (or version, or "tweak" or whatever term you choose) to recognize all my hardware out of the box, and the first in which I felt at all comfortable. 

I personally believe you should build your own computer, but Grandma probably ain't gonna. She probably ain't gonna want to spend a lot of time installing additional software either. So when you go over to set up her linux box, I would suggest Ultimate Edition. You'll be gone before the lutefisk is done........:lolflag:
