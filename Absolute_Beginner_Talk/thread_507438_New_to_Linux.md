Usually in Linux, the two biggest obstacles concerning hardware compatibility is the video card and wireless. The former is usually only a problem if you have an ATI card; Intel and Nvidia linux drivers are readily available. for wireless, it's usually only a problem with the cheaper broadcom/linksys cards. Your sound card and other basic hardware will almost certainly be compatible. 

Abiword, Kword (idk if that's the name, it's part of Koffice suite), and Openoffice writer are all good solutions. Abi and K are very lightweight.

### Post by Frak on 2007-07-23
I want to start off with, 95% of the software you bought, will probably not work, unless they came with Linux versions with it. ### Post by anewguy on 2007-07-23
As a student taking Visual C++ in school and working with .NET, you DEFINITELY want to keep that in Windows.  It will eliminate the extra work for now of getting that all working (IF it's possible) in Linux and keep you on the same page with the course and the other students.  Don't underestimate that!  As already mentioned, there is the ability to dual-boot, so you could leave Windows on your machine and also install Linux.  I happen to like Linux, and Ubuntu in particular, but I'm also not one of those people who is going to trash Windows and say you HAVE to go to Linux.  Keep Windows for your school work.  If you eventually take some operating systems classes, you might want to consider dual-booting with Linux at that time, depending on the class.  There are enough headaches in school with out adding the "first-time-frustrations" that normally occur for people trying Linux for the first time.  Your games, etc., will be better off just leaving them in Windows as well until you have the time and ambition to try them in Linux.  My limited experience has been that it's not just a "drop-in and go" thing - there is some work involved, and probably some frustrations along the way.  Enjoy school - play with and migrate to Linux when either your coursework dictates or when you have the time later.  Linux, and Ubuntu, will still be waiting for you!:)

Good luck with school from an ex information systems techie.:)
