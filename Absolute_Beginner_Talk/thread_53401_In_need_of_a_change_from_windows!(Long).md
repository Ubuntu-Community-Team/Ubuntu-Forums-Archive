### Post by GavinX on 2005-07-31
It's good that you're thinking of moving to Linux and that you've tried Ubuntu and also did some research.

As for some of your concerns:

1. In terms of hardware support, you will find that Ubuntu will auto-detect your hardware and automagically configure them. I don't use SATA drive, but I know that it is supported under Linux. This will be no major problem. All of the other hardware are well supported and for your Nvidia card, there are Linux drivers available which are even better than those used by Windows.

2. For games, there are lots of great Linux games. To name a few: TuxRacer, PPracer (a more developed form of TuxRacer), Return to Castle Wolfenstein, Lots of card games like Pysol etc., and if you're not satisfied with all of these, yo can always use Cedega which allows you to run many Windows games under emulation. All in all, you wouldn't have much of a problem as far as games are concerned.

3. For file-sharing there is Limewire. Anyone who knows about file-sharing will let you know that Kaaza is no match for Limewire. Limewire will connect to many different file-sharing programs, including Shareazaa and Emule. And all of this comes without the annoying ads. You don't have a problem here at all.

4. DVD support is not a problem. There is Mplayer and Xine which can be installed easily. Along with these, you can install all the video codecs including DVD support. On my IBM T20 I have a DVD player and I've had no problems at all. MP3 support is also not a problem.

All in all, in my opinion Linux is just about on par if not way ahead of XP. Give it a try and you won't be disappointed. There is also another plus, if you ever run into difficulty the forums here are always available for extensive and comprehensive guidance. Have no fear, you won't be left behind.

Peace out,
GavinX

### Post by Kvark on 2005-08-01
[QUOTE=Mr_J_]Been looking threw Cedega and the transgaming site, but...
How do I get it?
Is it a purchased good? I read something about WineX becoming Cedega, so I believe WineX was a bought item...

It costs $15 and then you also get access to updates for 3 months. After that you can either pay $5 a month to keep your access to updates or stay with the version you have. You can always sign up for another 3 month period a year or two from now if you get too much out of date.

The source is freely available but rumours say it is very hard (or impossible) to compile from source and make it work decently. Also some parts are licenced to transgaming from patent holders and thus not included in the source version, such as ability to play copy protected games with original CD but without crack.


To mention a 'nasty' thing. Most programs are not automatically added to the menu when you install them. I put links to all the programs I use in the panel anyway so it doesn't bother me. But for those who perfer the menu it's a bit annoying.
