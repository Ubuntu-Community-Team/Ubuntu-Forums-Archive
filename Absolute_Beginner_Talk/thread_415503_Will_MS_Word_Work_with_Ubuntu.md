However, my advice would be: just wipe your Windows: you&#314;l go through one difficult week, but after that, you'll get used to your new "world" and never look back.

You seem quite attached to Word. Four years ago, I installed OpenOffice.org four times and wiped it three times. I persuaded. Now I never will goback, since I am on Ubuntu since Eastern (yes, only two weeks on Ubuntu Edgy, and now over 30 minutes on Feisty ;)

To tell you my whole history: I tried already a switch two years ago with Mandrake in a dual boot. Eventually, I stayed with Windows. Didn't see Linux for two years. I heard a presentation of Richard Stallman, I realised that the Badvista campain is a campaign from the Free Software Foundation, and two days later, I installed the Edgy that I burned six months before.

My transition this time went extremely smooth. As I worked already exclusively with Free Software under windows, the OS is the only new aspect for me. After barely two weeks, I can again do all what I want, albeit it takes a little bit searching by times. I am sure now: I'll stay with GNU/Linux .
