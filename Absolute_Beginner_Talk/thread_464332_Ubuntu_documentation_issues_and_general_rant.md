### Post by samuriCat on 2007-06-04
Hi, everyone! I have a couple questions, and I'd love a bit of feedback.

To give a bit of background, I'm ditching Microsloth forever and putting some version of Linux on my soon-to-be-built system as soon as it's ready. Since I have been trembling with anticipation for a while now, I ran Ubuntu from a CD this weekend just to get a feel for it. I did it the American way, too: I did not have any instructions printed or manual in front of me, despite the fact that Ubuntu Unleashed and Ubuntu Linux for Non-Geeks (Fiesty Fawn Update) were a few feet away. Directions? Manual? Ha! I laugh in the face of fear...and competent planning.

When I loaded up the Ubuntu 7.04 CD, I immediately tried to do something that people on message boards have been complaining about quite expansively: I tried to print from my Lexmark Z517. I have read all over the place that Lexamrks weren't supported, no drivers for that printer, etc.To my surprise, Ubuntu recognized it right off the bat. I could print. No problem.

This leads me to my first question: Is anyone making sure the documentation in the wiki and on the boards is accurate and reliabe? If not, why not? Am I unique? Do I have a magic distro?

My next issue: Because Fedora 7 gets a tepid review and a few hundred people downloading it instead of something else, suddenly Ubuntu is getting slammed. Pardon my skepticism, but isn't the whole purpose of having so many distros that you find the one that works best for your purposes? But it has new features! Gimme! Bah, I say! Why would anyone pay attention to those rankings? Okay, rant off.

 Whattya think?

### Post by lazyart on 2007-06-04
I mentioned this in another thread but I think it's safe to repeat it here.

In the early 80's when alternative music was cool for a few, a group called U2 put out some music and people loved them.

But something happened.  They released the Joshua Tree.  They got popular real fast.  And the whole Ratte & Hum movie and double album.  And a lot of people who liked alternative music started to hate them.  They weren't cool anymore because they had received mass appeal.
