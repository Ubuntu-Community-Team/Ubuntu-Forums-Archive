### Post by _simon_ on 2006-07-29
After trying many ISP's over the years, using many types of modem and having worked for an ISP, my advice is buy a router with modem BUILT IN.

They are extremely easy to setup and you shouldn't have a problem.

Currently I use a Linkysys ADSL Router and a seperate Linksys Modem which has been a bit of a bugger when I wanted to change various settings.

I used to have an ADSL Netgear Router/Modem which was very simple to setup and configure when needed. I only sold it when I moved house to a cable area and then regretted selling it when I moved out of a cable area lol.

You should be able to connect to any ISP with Linux. They very rarely mention Linux support because they can't be bothered to take the time to train the staff in supporting linux. The ISP I worked for DID officially support linux and you know what, in the 4 years I worked there I only remember receieving 1 call from a linux user... probably because linux users are more techy ;)
