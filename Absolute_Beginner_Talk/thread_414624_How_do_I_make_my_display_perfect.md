### Post by z1p101 on 2007-04-20
I'm not using Ubuntu now but I'm thinking of switching

Find your xorg.conf file, on my system it is in the  /etc/X11/ directory.

In the file, you may have to have root permissions, you will see blocks that look like this

Section "Monitor"
	Identifier   "Monitor0"
	VendorName   "GWY"
	ModelName    "FPD1730"
	HorizSync    30.0 - 83.0
	VertRefresh  50.0 - 60.0 [COLOR="Red"]<-- if your refresh isn't correct change it to manufactures spects[/COLOR]
	Option            "DPMS"
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier "Screen0"
	Device     "Card0"
	Monitor    "Monitor0"
	DefaultDepth 24 
	SubSection "Display"
		Viewport   0 0
		Depth     16
		Modes "1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480" [COLOR="Red"]<-add what you want to these[/COLOR]
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Viewport   0 0
		Depth     24
		Modes "1280x1024" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"[COLOR="Red"]<- and these[/COLOR]
	EndSubSection
EndSection

reboot X or your system and see if those options are available on your display monitor 

and always save a copy of what you changed JIC

Hope this helps

oh yeah your video drivers are defined
Section "Device"
	Identifier  "Card0"
	Driver      "nvidia"[COLOR="Red"] <-here[/COLOR]
	VendorName  "nVidia Corporation"
	BoardName   "NV17 [GeForce4 MX 440]"
	BusID       "PCI:1:0:0"
	VideoRam    131072
	Option      "UseEdidFreqs" "false"
EndSection

make sure they are correct
