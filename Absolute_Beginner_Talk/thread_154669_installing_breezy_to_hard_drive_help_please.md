### Post by catlett on 2006-04-03
Just choose guided partitioning. It will give you options. Take the first one It will ask something like"Erase entire drive /hda and install lvm". Sopmething like that, you'll know what I mean when you get there. Just hit enter when it boots up and asks you if you want the default install. When it gets done detecting your setup it will come to partitioning. That's where you will choose to let it use the whole drive. If you don't have any partitions you won't have many choices to choose from and it will be simple. But it should be the first choice. If you don't care about windows just let the install do its thing. nHere's the Ubuntu guide just in case. This one is for Intel chips. [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Installation/I386](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Installation/I386)
