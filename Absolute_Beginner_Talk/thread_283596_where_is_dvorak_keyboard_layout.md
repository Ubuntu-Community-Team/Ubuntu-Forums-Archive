### Post by x64Jimbo on 2006-10-24
For me, i've only been on dvorak for about 2-3 months and i'm already at nearly 75% of my old qwerty speed which took me 5 years to get to. It's not that it's cognitively easier to type, it's that it's easier on your hands. My mother has Carpal Tunnel Syndrome, and I didn't want to get it. There is a sweet webpage that teaches dvorak in any platform.
[http://dvorak.nl/learn.plp](http://dvorak.nl/learn.plp)

### Post by skymt on 2006-10-24
> **bodycoach2 said:**
> 1. How long did it take for you to learn Dvorak layout?
2. Did you learn Dvorak first, or Querty?
3. Is it really that much easier to type Dvorak layout?
4. Are there any Linux typing tutor packages that teach Dvorak?

1. I learned by total immersion, and it took around two weeks to get as fast in Dvorak as I was in Qwerty. I'm currently around twice as fast in Dvorak as I ever was in Qwerty, and I was using Qwerty for a lot longer.

2. Qwerty first.

3. Yes, once you learn it. The biggest advantage is that the most commonly used letters are on the home row, so you don't have to move your fingers at all to type some common words ("the", "an", "and", etc).

4. Yes. One I know of is called dvorak7min. It's text-based, but it's in Universe, so it's easy to install. I didn't use a tutor. I just switched to Dvorak, and kept a layout diagram close at hand until I didn't need it anymore.
