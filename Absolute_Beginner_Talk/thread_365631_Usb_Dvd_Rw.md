### Post by lhtown on 2007-02-20
I believe it should automount and show an icon on your desktop much like your camera. Of course, this is assuming that it has a disk that is properly mounted in the tray or seated on the spindle (if it is the click kind, the disk has to be pushed down over the spindle). If there is no disk, I doubt that it will do anything.


You can try googling or looking on the manufacturer's site to see if your peripheral is linux-compatible. I think there are some external drives that aren't.

Also, if you have a choice of how to connect it to your computer, you could try the alternate method.

If you can run a live CD on your computer, you might give it a try with Edgy or even Fiesty (still in beta) just to see if it works. Other than that, I don't really have any ideas without getting into some heavy-duty troubleshooting.
