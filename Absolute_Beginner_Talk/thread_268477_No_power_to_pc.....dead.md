### Post by Scorpuk on 2006-09-30
Quickest way to see if the power supply is dead is to plug it into a working computer to see if it will power that.


Make sure that the power switch is connected to your motherboard. If it is then to check if the switch is faulty note down which 2 pins the on/off switch is connected to and short those two with a small screwdriver. **NB** be very carefull when you are doing this not to touch any other pins! - Ofcourse use a power supply you know works!!!!


If that doesnt work then your motherboard is more than likely fried and needs replacing. Probably :) 


Hope this helps.

### Post by Scorpuk on 2006-09-30
lol :-k 

Well the idea behind the screwdriver short is to see if your on/off switch at the front of the computer is working or not.


All the switch at the front of the computer does is short two connections on the motherboard. Makes then breaks!


If you are not happy doing this yourself then THE most basic thing that happens when you press this button would be the fan on the power supply coming on. Even if your motherboard is fried as those two pins are directly linked to the power supply cable from your power supply box. 


Of course if the link is broken due to a scratch on the circuitry then nothing will happen, but you would be able to test this theory with the unknown power supply box (current one) and a fully functional one from one of your other computers.
