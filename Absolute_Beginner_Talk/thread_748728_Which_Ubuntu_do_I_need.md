### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-04-07
> **keypox said:**
> Standard is fine... while your cpu does support 64 bit you do not need it.

I agree, standard will be just fine with his hardware.

And while 64bit is not necessary, it is not needlessly problematic.  A long time ago 64bit had a lot of problems, but that is not true anymore.  In fact, there are some times where you will see a big improvement (~30%) by running 64bit.  I personally use it and the only problems I've had is a little messing around with gDesklets and Java.

At the most 5 years from now we'll all be running 64bit OSs, so why not have one now?

### Post by lackofcreativity on 2008-04-07
The 30% increase is normally only when you're using a high percentage of CPU power for things like compiling programs and FPSes. You might not see the increase when doing things that involve downloading or frequent disk writes. Most programs nowadays have amd64 versions anyway, so there's really no downside to it.

---

### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-04-07
Yeah, the standard answer is that you'll see the most improvement in the following:  rendering (especially 3D modeling), compiling, and encoding.  Anything that is super heavy on processor and can be scaled up to 64bit well will be improved to some degree.
