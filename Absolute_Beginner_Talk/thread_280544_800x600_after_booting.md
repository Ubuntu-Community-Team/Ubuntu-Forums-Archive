### Post by steve.horsley on 2006-10-19
You can probably fix it by specifying the monitor's scan frequency ranges in the file /etc/X11/xorg.conf. You can edit manually with **sudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf**, or perhaps **sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg** will ask the right questions.
 If editing manually, add these two lines to section "Monitor" (substitute your real monitor frequency capabilities though):
```
    HorizSync       24.0 - 80.0
    VertRefresh     40.0 - 75.0

```
