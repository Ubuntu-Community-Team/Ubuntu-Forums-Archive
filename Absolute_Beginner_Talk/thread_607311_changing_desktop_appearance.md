### Post by Therion on 2007-11-08
> **littleasian said:**
> 

... I just want to switch a theme since the default orange is kind of...ugly.

anyways, which one should i choose on gnome-look.org and how do i apply it?
Well which one you choose is up to your personal preference.  But I suggest you go to [GNOME Art]("http://art.gnome.org/themes/gtk2/") and click on "Application Themes" on the left.

On the drop-down menu that says "Date", click on "Popularity" and then change.

Now, suppose you wanted to install, say "Shiny - Black" for instance.  Click on the icon for it.  Find the download icon for the "Shiny - Black" theme, but don't save it or open it or anything else yet.

Now click on System, Preferences, Appearance on your Ubuntu tool-bar.  Make sure you're on the "Themes" tab at the top.

Find the icon on GNOME Art for the download file for "Shiny - Black" and now DRAG and DROP that icon onto the Appearance Preferences button.  The window will open allowing you to drop the file right into the Theme Manager and apply the new theme immediately.

You can change your Icons and Window borders the same way.  Just go to those sections on GNOME Art and do the same drag and drop routine right into the Appearance Preferences button like you did the theme.
