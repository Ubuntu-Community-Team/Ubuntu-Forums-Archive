### Post by crimesaucer on 2007-01-07
I had no problems when I used these wiki instructions for 6.10 edgy: [http://wiki.beryl-project.org/index.php/Install/Ubuntu/Edgy/AiGLX](http://wiki.beryl-project.org/index.php/Install/Ubuntu/Edgy/AiGLX)

Really easy directions.

It was difficult when I first tried to install Beryl because they hadn't written any guides for the xfce4 window manager (xfwm4), and the configuration of AiGLX. But then they re-wrote that wiki that I linked you to and it now fully covers everything you need for xubuntu, and it took like 10 minutes to get a fully working Beryl.

I have it running on a hp pavilion dv4230us that uses an Intel celeron m 910GML so I can't use all of the effects (like water) without it slowing my computer, but besides that, Beryl is really cool. My xubuntu xfce4 is still really fast, and I have no complaints.

You can update to the 1.5-svn by adding the "Treviño's Beryl-SVN Ubuntu Repository" which is under the Experimental SVN repositories. They are working on some cool things like having 4 different desktop wallpapers for your 4 different workspaces.
