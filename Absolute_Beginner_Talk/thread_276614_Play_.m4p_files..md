### Post by igknighted on 2006-10-13
Depending on how many purchased songs you have there is a way around this DRM (at least a year or so ago when I moved away from iTunes this worked, who knows what they've changed).  If you burn the DRMed files as a music CD in iTunes, you can then re-import them using any mp3/ogg encoder as any other cd and the files are now unDRMed.  If you have less than 4 or 5 cd's worth of music this might be your better choice, because as far as I know wine, vmware, etc. are the only other ways to play these.
