### Post by newtanker on 2006-09-01
your computer has a bios, which is like a program to tell it what to do....before it even has anything to work with....

So, your bios is told to look to the hard drive in your computer for an operating system, which it finds, and hands control of the computer over to windows....

You need to tell the bios where you want it to look for further instructions, this is usually done with either the del key or f2 or other f keys, youll have to look at your documentation for the computer or motherboard.  youll wnat to change the boot order for cdrom then to hdd or hard drive

Good luck and have fun

### Post by reacocard on 2006-09-01
You probably just need to change your BIOS settings. Watch carefully just after you turn on the computer, it'll tell you what button to press (usually F1 or Del). press that button (quickly!) and in the resulting setup program, find the section for booting. put the cd drive before the hard drive. save your changes, and reboot. it should now boot from the cd.

Good luck, and welcome to Ubuntu!
