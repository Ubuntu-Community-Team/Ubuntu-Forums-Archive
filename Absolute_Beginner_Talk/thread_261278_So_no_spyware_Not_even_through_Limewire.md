### Post by Sukarn on 2006-09-20
Fist of all i dont know how you can get spyware through music, except those wma files in windows media player that require a licence.

Secondly I use Limewire (well... Frostwire actually) quite a lot on Ubuntu, and I haven't acquired a single spyware yet. Correct me if i am wrong) and exe files cannot be executed by default. You need wine or cross over office to use those.

### Post by merlyn on 2006-09-20
Most if not all spyware, malware and the like is written to target Windows systems and as such is not binary compatable, ie will not run on a Linux system.

Viruses have been written to target Linux systems, though they are few and far between, check  [this ]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Linux_computer_viruses") out, though I'm not aware of anything recent.

Also security vulnerablities do occur,  patches usually follow very hotly on their heels, and so it would be prudent to keep your system up to date.
