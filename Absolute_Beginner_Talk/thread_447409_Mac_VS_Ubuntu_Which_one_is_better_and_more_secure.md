### Post by geekphreak on 2007-05-18
OK, so I may use a stereotype here, but Macs are extremely good for anything that has to do with graphics/graphic design. So, if you are specifically needing that capability, go OSX. For general use, I think Ubuntu, Fedora or SuSE would be more versatile and usable. There's tons of absolutely free applications, community support is awesome, and you can express who you are through your OS, as you can customize it all you want. Ubuntu also runs on older hardware, so it is great if you want to revive your older computers and save up some money to get a higher-end machine. You can also suggest new features and help make it better that way. 

There's never a good clear answer to 'which is better' type of a question. It all depends on your needs. I personally always try to use the best tools for the given situation, which means a dual boot setup Ubuntu/XP. 
I would like to have a Mac, just so I can have all major computing environments. However, the more time passes, the more I am personally convinced that Apple is no better than Microsoft. (No intention of flaming) They're both big companies that need to come up with effective ways to increase revenues, and that does affect their products and strategies. For Microsoft, it may mean that they will make you buy a $2000 or more machine because all new hardware is needed to run Vista, for Apple, it may mean sleek stylish design that will make their hardware so popular they know people will spit out a lot of money to get it. Whatever it is, I like the peace of mind of using Ubuntu or any Linux distro, because I know developers and community are driven by completely different things than Apple and Microsoft. 

I guess the point is: use whatever will be most productive and enjoyable for you. There's really no wight or wrong answer. And there may not be only one answer either. I use XP for work, and Ubuntu for anything else other than Visual Studio.
Whatever it is, you are the one making the final choice.

### Post by maxamillion on 2007-05-18
statistically OS X has less open security holes than linux does as a whole, but take that with a grain of salt.....

I think the problem with this question is that you are asking simply "which is better" instead of "which is better at <insert something specific here>?" because each OS is better than the other in certain areas, OS X is fully POSIX compliant, Ubuntu GNU/Linux is fully open source (with exception of the multiverse and canonical repositories), OS X is very elegantly designed and easy to use (if you argue this, you are entitled to your opinion but the rest of the world constantly tries to copy the OS X user space interface so they must be doing something right), Ubuntu gives you freedom in the selection of desktop environment along with many other aspects of the system (and of course applications), etc ....

There is far too many things to compare to give a solid answer to "which is better?" ... it would simply come down to which you enjoy using better, because then that system would be better suited for you.
