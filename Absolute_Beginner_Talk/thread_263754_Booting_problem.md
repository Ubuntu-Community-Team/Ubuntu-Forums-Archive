### Post by tommyx53 on 2006-09-23
My previous Linux distribution, SimplyMEPIS Linux, crashed, but I tried installing Ubuntu but it wouldn't boot. I tried: upgrading the hard drive, vacuuming inside the computer case, changing the boot sequence (so the BIOS polls the CD-ROM drive first). But nothing worked. Finally, I tried unplugging the mouse, and it booted! But when the mouse was plugged in, after Ubuntu was installed, the on-screen pointer would erratically jump about even without moving the mouse. What I finally figured out: different image patterns on the MOUSEPAD were screwing up the optical mouse (you know, the kind which doesn't have a trackball but a light facing the desktop). That is, the image of the globe on the mousepad, with patterns of alternating white and blue and black, confused the optical sensor on the mouse and made it jerk erratically. Merely doing without the mousepad solved the problem. I don't know if this will solve your problem but perhaps you could give it a try.
