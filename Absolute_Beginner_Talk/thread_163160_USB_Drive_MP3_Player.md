### Post by richardward101 on 2006-04-20
Basically when you normally delete something on your hard disk it puts it in the wastebasket, which is actually just a folder in your Home directory called ".Trash" (the "." makes it hidden, but you can see it by plessing ctrl+h in the file browser). If however the file is on a different volume (i.e. your mp3) it would be silly to copy it to your home directory, so instead it makes a folder called .Trash-Richard (in my cae) and puts it there. (i think this folder is always on the root of the drive but i'm not certain). Either empty your wastebasket or open up your mp3 players folder, press ctrl+H and delete the .Trash-(username) folder.
Windows only uses the recycle-bin on local hard disks, stuff on removable drive just get deleted.
Hope this helps.
