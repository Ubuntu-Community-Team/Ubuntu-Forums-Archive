### Post by mattkirwan on 2006-08-24
Two weeks ago, while mooching around the supermarket, I stumbled across a copy of Personal Computer World. There upon on the cover was a drawing of a very happy and content penguin.

After hearing dribs and drabs about the wonders of Linux, this happy and content penguin persuaded me to shell out the disgraceful £5.00 for the magazine and investigate further.......

Getting the DVD version with the magazine, I made my .iso image of Ubunutu 6.06 and proceeded to install.
The installation went smoothly on my Samsung X10 Laptop, with an excited but anxious click, I logged in.

Dual-booting with XP - was a wise decision for a complete newbie, like myself.

Firstly, The modem was not working, I was constantly rebooting and connecting to the internet via XP, visiting forums, narrowing searches - google was my best friend! But I perservered, reading article after article, whether modem related or not - the sources of information on the internet are truly incredible, and I already owe so much to so many people.Thankyou.

I was getting frustrated, no, I was getting very frustrated, and to be honest nearly gave up on several occasions and contemplated wiping it all and surrendering my hard drive to C:\WINDOWS\ again.

Realising one day, I was going about the whole thing the wrong way, getting frustrated only made things worse, I mentally slapped myself.

It was like a light came on in my darkened introduction to Linux, I started to enjoy the learning, making simple mistakes but enjoying fixing them afterwards.
Using Windows is only natural because we get it spoon fed from day one, people do not know any different, I know I certainly didn't.
I am only a couple of weeks into my Linux exploits, I could not feel anymore positive about the whole thing. The communities are warm and friendly, the articles in-depth and to top it off - Ubuntu is fantastic.

Keep going with your problems, you will get there in the end - and most importantly, make sure you enjoy it, every step of the way.

This is my first thread on the Ubuntu Forums. 
I figured, the high majority of first posts are frustrating toned questions - I wanted to make my impression different.
