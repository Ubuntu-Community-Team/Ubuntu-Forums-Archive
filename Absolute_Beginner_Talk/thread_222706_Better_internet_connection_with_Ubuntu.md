### Post by Ann667 on 2006-07-25
I am wondering if anyone else has had a similar experience.  

I switched to Ubuntu 6.06 recently from Windows XP Prof.  When I was running XP I was having problems with my internet access.  This was an almost daily, and often several times a day, occurrence.  I was unable to access the internet.  No pages, regardless of the site, were loading.  (I would try several sites to see if it was just one particular site that was having the problem.)  All the lights on my cable modem would be lit indicating that there was an active connection, but yet I couldn’t access anything.  (I would have to reset the modem, sometimes several times, before I could load any pages, and it didn’t always last very long.)  Of course this wasn’t all the time.  I’d be able to access the internet fine sometimes, and then I would have this problem other times.  As far as I knew my computer was not infected with any viruses or the like.  I ran and updated a virus scanner daily, had a firewall up, and even scanned for malware.  (I also don’t open attachments I’m not sure of or click on anything like animated pop ups, not that I got many pop ups as I had them blocked.)

Now that I’ve switched to Ubuntu I do not have this problem at all.  I have the firewall up, probably even more restrictive than when I ran XP.  I’m using Firefox, the same as I did with Windows.  So far I have had absolutely no problem accessing the internet.  I even called my internet provider to see if they had made any upgrades or fixed any problems recently, and they haven’t changed anything.  

I was curious if anyone else had a similar experience or had any ideas about why this problem went away.  (I’m really just curious.....  and very happy! – much less frustrating!)
