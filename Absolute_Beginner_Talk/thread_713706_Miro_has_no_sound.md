### Post by jeffus_il on 2008-03-03
You are downloading videos for which you don't have a audio decoder. You can open the .miro/Miro folder to see the formats of the videos you have downloaded (e.g. mpeg-4, flash video, ...) Miro uses Xine to play videos, so I would install the libxine1-all-plugins package. Alternatively you could play the files from Nautilus using the VLC player. You could also try the starter channels they provide and see if they have sound. The Miro site has a faq section, here is the section about which formats are supported:
[http://www.getmiro.com/help/faq/#02-04](http://www.getmiro.com/help/faq/#02-04)
