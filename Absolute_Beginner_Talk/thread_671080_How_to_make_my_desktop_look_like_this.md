### Post by Nythain on 2008-01-18
ok, the OSX like panel on the top is easy
```

alt+f2
kcontrol

```expand desktop on the left side list of options and click on Behavior...
The middle section "Menu Bar at Top of Screen" has an option for that particular bar, its called "Current Applications Menubar"
now for the themes... lets see what i can see see see...
definately running a compiz/beryl/fusion theme of some sorts, cant tell you exactly wich one... find a dark one you like that looks similar, there's a lot of dark aero glass themes out there.
color scheme is hard to pinpoint exactly... [http://www.kde-look.org](http://www.kde-look.org) should have plenty of options for you to choose from.
the real slick part about the entire thing, is what he has done with his kicker panel??? my guess would be a custom panel (or just the default one) with specific size options set to be centered in teh middle of the screen
```

alt+f2
kcontrol

```Desktop---->Panels (its a few options under the above mentioned "behavior"
change length and height accordingly (shrink length and increase "size")
on the panels it looks like:
Taskbar
Clock
Whatever Media Controls those are
and i have no idea what the Bars/Graph/Thingy is
You can add remove panel applets by right clickin on your kicker panel and unlocking panel...
right click anywhere empty to "add applet"
right click on a specific applets little bar thing to get options to move, remove, or edit said applet

After you have added the current application menubar, you can add applets to it too, by rightclicking on it... it appears thats where they have their "system tray" applet at

So after all of that, all you would have to do is ask the artist what Beryl/Compiz/Fusion theme they use, and what color scheme they are using, and you should be close if not exactly like that
