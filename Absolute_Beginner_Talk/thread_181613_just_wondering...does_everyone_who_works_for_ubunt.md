### Post by az on 2006-05-24
[QUOTE=youthforlinux]if ubuntu is free who pays them[/QUOTE]
The software comes from many thousands of developers all over the world.  They do it for a variety of reasons.  It is free-libre so that they do not have to start over every time they want to do something.

With free-libre software, you can take someone else's work and improve upon it.  So, for example, if someone is emplyed to keep their schools computer network running, and they need a certain utility to work better, they can add that functionality and then offer that improvement upstream so that every other person who uses that tool can get to use that improvement along with it.

So can people make money from free software?  Sure.  Even though they don't sell it as property?  Absolutely.

The model for making money from FLOSS is services and support.

That network guy who works for the school is making money.

Your ISP is probably using FLOSS since it works as well if not better than proprietairy equivalents, and they are changing you.  There are lots of other examples.  Cisco, IBM, Novel all make millions from free-libre software and the services they provide in relation to them.

### Post by jimcooncat on 2006-05-24
I love free-libre FOSS FLOSS opensource community-driven software. I wish we could make up our minds what to call it though.

Some developers are truly generous to give their work to the community under an unrestricted license. Some do it in the hopes that others will improve upon their work, so you do see a lot of unfinished or unpolished projects. And there are some who publish under one license for non-commercial use, but if you want to use the product commercially (read the fine print to see how they define that), you have to pay for a commercial license.

There are a lot of different licenses out there for a developer to choose from. Some of the more popular ones are GPL, LGPL, and BSD-style. For more on this, see the [Wikipedia List of Software Licenses]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_software_licenses").
