### Post by bulldog on 2007-11-14
Well,you have to burn the ISO as an Image to a cd-r,do this on a low speed [4x] on a good quality medium.
If done,you have to set your cd-rom as first boot device in your BIOS,put in the created cd and boot from it.
You'll get a menu and you should use the first option normally.
This will bring you to the live desktop environment,a little slow,but it gives you a chance to have a loook if all your hardware is working as it should be.[internet,sound,mouse etc.]
In this stage there's nothing happened to your hdd,it run simple from the cd.
