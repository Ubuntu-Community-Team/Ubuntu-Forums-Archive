### Post by podunk on 2006-10-09
The program Firestarter isn't really your firewall - it's the program that modifies your firewall settings. Once you turn the firewall on it stays on and loads every bootup. You can take Firestarter out of the start up items.

---

### Post by dmizer on 2006-10-09
if you configured it to start without a password, it would negate it's security.

once you have firstarter configured, it runs automatically in the background on boot.  so you don't actually have to open the gui to have it be enabled.

remember ... firestarter is not a firewall.  iptables is your firewall.  firestarter is just a gui you can use to configure iptables.
