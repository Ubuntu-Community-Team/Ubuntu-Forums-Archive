### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-06-15
> **alex-desktop79 said:**
> I got this eerie feeling the drive is damaged. If it is, I just lost 4000 songs.

Do you not have any other copy of these songs at all? I mean its never a good idea to rely on an iPod for storage of anything, their drives are known to fail. Also, since the iPods are not open (not easily moded/replaced hardrive) they are designed to be thrown away and a new one bought. That of course is Apples philosophy with hardware.

If the actual hard drive itself has been damaged, your hosed. If it is a software only problem (bad sectors), I know of people who have recovered their songs by cracking open the iPod, finding an adaptor  to make it another drive on their computer (iPod drives that are large are just optical ones) and then running [spinrite]("http://www.grc.com/sr/spinrite.htm") on it. If its software related, spinrite is the solution (there are ways to get a free copy to try to test) I don't know how to mod it for your computer though, google that bit. If the medium has been damaged, the songs are gone for good methinks.
