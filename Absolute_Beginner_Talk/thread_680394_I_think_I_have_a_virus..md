### Post by Algus on 2008-02-06
Just before I installed Ubuntu on my notebook I nuked my expired Norton security software (god that stuff is like a virus in and of itself, I hate trying to get Norton off a computer) and installed AVG.  Just doing sweeps with AVG and Ad-Aware I got like 8 hits for malware.  Just junk I'd picked up over the years by surfing the net.  Nothing to serious, but would cause pop ups in Internet Explorer and eat up my RAM.  

So switching to Ubuntu and then being nervous about viruses...I can definitely understand it :P Windows is always going to have its place on my systems, if for no other reason then because I do like video games, but it really is remarkable just how much better everything works in Linux.  I can browse without worrying about finding new little friends and when I get lame chain emails warning about viruses I can just chuckle it away, realizing that some little virus in my email isn't going to do a thing to me :P 

Oh, did I mention that I like how in Linux  you authenticate things? Sure it was set up like that in XP but it ended up being a pain because any time you had to authenticate you had to log out of your session and log back on as an administrator...and then you just stop using regular accounts because it gets to be a pain.  

Anyway, there's so much that can be said complimentary about Linux.  Now you don't have to worry about viruses, so your system doesn't need an anti virus with constant updating running so your RAM is free which, in turn, means that everything works great on older hardware.  Running Ubuntu on my P4 with 512 MB of RAM feels more efficient at times then my Vista rig with a C2D and 2 GB of RAM.
