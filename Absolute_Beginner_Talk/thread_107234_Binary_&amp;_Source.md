### Post by bscbrit on 2005-12-22
No, you will never _have_ to compile from source if you are happy with the many thousands of programs that the ubuntu and debian communities have available to them.  Indeed, the vast majority of computer users will never compile a program.
But there are some who want to tune a specific program to maximise its capabilities with the specific architecture of their computer, or to remove unwanted code that has no function on their box.  Others do it simply to learn how to.  And some because there is not a specific package tailored for Ubuntu i.e. they have no other choice.
I would suggest, and I'm not trying to be insulting here, that anyone who has to use this forum is perhaps not ready for compiling their own programs from source.  Some may not agree with me, others will tell you how easy it can be.  I know, I write programs.  But, if I were you, I would wait until you can read these threads with a degree of confidence that you understand the issues being discussed before trying to compile your programs from source.

EDIT:  While you do not need the sources you will not gain anything by removing them from your repos.  They will only be downloaded if they are needed.
