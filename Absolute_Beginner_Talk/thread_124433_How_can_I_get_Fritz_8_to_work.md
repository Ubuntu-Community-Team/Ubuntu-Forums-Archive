### Post by Pragmatist on 2006-02-05
I'm also a chess enthusiast.  I haven't tried running my chessbase or fritz via wine.  However, FYI, there is a database program called "scid" and many phenomenal chess playing programs that will be equivalent to all but strong GMs.  Try "xboard" and look through its menus for the playing engines.  All of these can be found using apt-get or synaptic.  For playing online, checkout "jin" just go to [www.google.com/linux](www.google.com/linux)  and use keyword "jin" and you'll find a place to download the tarball.  You don't need to compile anything, just unpack the tarball, go into the new jin directory, and type: ./jin

Thats it!
