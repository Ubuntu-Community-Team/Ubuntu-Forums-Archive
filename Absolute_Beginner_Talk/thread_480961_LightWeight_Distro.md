### Post by mengfei on 2007-06-22
Well, most importantly, how much RAM do you have?

Also, just how much are you willing to sacrifice for "lightweightedness"?

Because we can go pretty far, although I doubt it'll be such a friendly distro anymore (for beginners, that is).

If you have no fear of the command line, and are quite comfortable with linux, there are plenty of zippy dstros.

Hmm... I'd say that comfortable to use, yet not quite so difficult distros would include
Xubuntu (closest you'll get to Ubuntu)
Zenwalk Linux
There's always Fluxbuntu as well, although not so Ubuntu-ey
Vector Linux (avoid the recent KDE release)

And then there's:
Puppy Linux and DSL as you mentioned, but they're not so easy as one click to install.

I've also heard of quite a few other distros....

It really depends on what you're looking for.

What I would say for you to do is to try Xubuntu. You don't even have to download a new ISO. run in Terminal

sudo apt-get install xfce-desktop

After it's done, log out, change your session to XFCE, and log back in.

Xubuntu isn't really that lightweight. It's lightweight compared to Ubuntu, but compared to the other lightweight distros, it's a heavyweight.

But hey, give it a shot, and if it works good, it might be just the thing for you. If not, try other distros, but keep an open mind and be willing to learn lots of new things! (fun fun!)

Hope that helps!
