### Post by blueridgedog on 2008-01-20
Your hardware seems sufficient to share a disk on the wire and a NAS is nothing more.  Your real challenge will be to list all of your clients and document how they connect to network data (protocol) and then configuring your NAS to implement that protocol.

### Post by Thund3rstruck on 2008-01-20
Take my advise on this one; don't bother standing up a linux box for this task. I have done this and it was an extremely tedious task finding RAID cards to work in linux and configuring  mdadm. 

Do yourself a favor and purchase a pre-built appliance NAS such as the Netgear ReadyNAS or the Buffalo Terastation Live. 

I did get a Linux NAS up and running but after spending some time with both the Buffalo unit and the netgear offering, I have to say I recommed these. I'm using a Buffalo Terastation Live (4TB) unit now that I store all my DVDs and Divx movies on which I stream to a MythTV box and an old XBOX running Xbox Media Center (XBMC). The NAS appliances are small, tight, and consume minimal power.
