### Post by bluepowder7 on 2007-11-24
buy a router.  seriously, it's an easy solution that removes the need to set up log-in crap on any other computer or operating system you have.  program the log-in info (user id, password, etc) into the router itself, and then get the router to assign addresses to the computers that are connected to it (DHCP).  that way, you connect your PC to the router, the router says hi and tells the PC what address it's going to have, and says "ok, there's the internet.  have fun!"  and if you or your buddy brings over their laptop, they can hook up to that same router (they usually have 4 or more ports), the router says "cool, another computer.  here's YOUR address.  feel free to start your browser and let's go!"

so, why the router approach?  you set the router ONCE, and you're done.  you can do whatever you want with the PC and your log-in info isn't affected.  you can install another OS or two (or seven), and NEVER have to set up the PPPoE stuff again.  offload the responsibility to the router.  i'm sure you can score one for $30 that'll work just fine.

### Post by bluepowder7 on 2007-11-24
the router would go between the modem and your computer.  if you're in japan, they (routers) should be VERY easy to find.  if you draw a picture of 3 computers connected to one box which is then connected to a modem box which has a line going to a wall and show it to the sales dude at the store, they'll probably know what you need.  you might not actually USE three computers, but that's the box that lets you do that (and stores the log-in info automatically)

it's possible that you could get ubuntu to set up the various log-in things, but having a router is SO much easier and more friendly when it comes to adding another OS to play around with, that it's probably $30 well spent.  if you know how to set up the network profile in Windows, then you'll know how to set it up in the router too (it's very similar).  depending on who makes the router, you can likely download an english version of the manual from their web page (for a US-equivalent model)

at home, i have a Linksys router (model WRT54G), and i just plug my PC's network cable into it and things work.  same with the laptop - i plug in the cable to another free port, and it just works.
