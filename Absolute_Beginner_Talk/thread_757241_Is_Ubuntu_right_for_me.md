### Post by NightwishFan on 2008-04-16
I always recommend dual boot to new users. How that goes is to create free space with any partition editor and use the "use largest continuous free space" option in the install. Also you can try out live cds of course but they are far slower than the installed system. I recommend Ubuntu even over other Linux distros simply because it is so easy to get simple things such as installing codecs or drivers. Mint may perhaps be good for beginners but I have never tried it so I cannot say. How Ubuntu compares with Windows is that its faster and more stable, and only lacking in some odd hardware support and games, although many can be run in wine perfectly. (Starcraft, Morrowind) The customization is great, even though it looks good by default. Overall it is easy, and of course these forums as free sometimes professional support. It has almost every feature of every modern os, including some that were said to be exclusive and innovative. Things such as superfetch which is called preload on linux and is easily installable from the repositories. It will also update the system and all the applications installed on it for free, and fast. Give it a try you will not be disappointed, also if you have a 64bit processor try out the AMD64 version for peak performance.
