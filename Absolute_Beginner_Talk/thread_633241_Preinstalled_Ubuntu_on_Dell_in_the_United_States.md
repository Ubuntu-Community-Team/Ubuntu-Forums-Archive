I have no idea where to put this so I'm dropping it here for the time being.
Dell is now offering Ubuntu on it's the Inspiron 530 N desktop and 1420 N notebook.  This is all well and good and an admirable move on their part, but the Unbuntu system is very scaled down form the windows system to start, but when I "upgraded" the Ubuntu system to be about equal to the windows system the total savings was well under $100 US, but they offer $100 US "instant savings" on top of it.  Then to see if they had real information at the sales service line, I called.  The information they gave me was slightly unintelligible.  They said that the PC was basically for people that didn't like windows, accurate to an extent.  Also they told me that the PC had to be configured by the user, so I guess they don't install the OS at the factory, or they don't take the time to configure it.  But here's the real kicker that through me off,  they seem to be under the impression that Ubuntu is solely terminal based operating system, with no GUI, "it's like Microsoft DOS.  You know about DOS?"  I think that if they wish to offer a Linux based operating system, such as Ubuntu, they should at least train the sales reps on the features and try and make some selling points.
Just a thought from John
