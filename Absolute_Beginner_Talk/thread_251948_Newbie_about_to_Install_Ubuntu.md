### Post by Rhubarb on 2006-09-06
If everything works from the live cd, it'll all work from an ubuntu install too.

As for the suggestion of the file and settings transfer wizard, it's not a bad idea.
It is however always a good practice to backup all your documents before messing around with your computer.
Actually, you should always have a backup of all your documents anyhow, unless they have no importance.

There wouldn't be that many settings for the transfer wizard to do, maybe the wallpaper (if it can find what the current one is), and your firefox / ie bookmarks.  Possibly your email.

You can mount your win xp partition in Ubuntu, if it's formatted as NTFS (it usually is), look up in the forums here how to do it.
That way you can read or even write files from your XP partition.

Sounds like a very nice setup you have there Half Baked!
