### Post by PurplePenguin on 2006-11-23
It's true that a lot of stuff can be done so much more quickly on the command line.  For example, I've been talking a lot on the forum about Automatix.  It looks like a nightmare to install, since the instructions are a full page long.  But you just open up a terminal, go step by step pasting whatever they tell you, hit enter after each command, and you're good to go.  You don't even have to read or understand the commands if you don't want to (although I suggest you do)!  

Even though it's work with the command line, all it really is is cutting from your web browser and pasting into the command line... no actual typing or thinking necessary. :)

You only really have to use the command line interface when you're setting some programs up (others can be installed very easily through the package manager).  In fact, sometimes I don't even touch it for days on end.

### Post by DirtDawg on 2006-11-24
I like to think of Synaptic as a giant software store where everything is free. There's three stores really, but only one is available by default. To add the other two, follow the steps outlined in [this]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy#How_to_apt-get_the_easy_way_.28Synaptic.29") guide under "How to apt-get the easy way (Synaptic)". 

After you do that, all you need to do is open Synaptic, find something you like, click the little box("Mark for installation"), hit the "Apply" button at the top, give it a few minutes, and Synaptic will download and install the program *for* you. Yay!

In addition, you may be interested to browse the [How to Install Anything]("http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/") guide. It will give you a great idea about all the different ways to install software on your new Ubuntu Box(some more complex than others).

Have fun!

### Post by DirtDawg on 2006-11-24
> **cs_baliw_cs said:**
> yo... i'm also new... you guys suggesting synaptic right... and i guess it really needs your own internet connection but waht if i don't have... actually i just rent on a computer shop just to surf the net and download things... thanks..

Go to the [Ubuntu Packages]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/") site. Choose the distribution you have (i.e:Edgy), choose a category (i.e.:Games), and choose something you would like(i.e.:Nethack-console). On the bottom are download links for your archetecture (likely i386), just click and download.

Notice the links with the red dots next to them(i.e:nethack-common). They are dependancies. These are also needed, so you'll have to click those links and also download them. Where you will run into trouble is that the dependancies often need dependancies which may need other dependancies and so on until you enter "dependancy hell". Synaptic takes care of these for you, but you obviously need an internet connection. 

Anyways, after you get everything downloaded, copy them to your computer, then install them using dpkg, as outlined [here]("http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/#deb") under "installing Debian Packages(.deb)."

I did things this way for two years before I had an internet connection to my Linux box, but it was simply a matter of going downastairs for internet access. If you don't have easy internet access, you may want to seriously consider using a Linux with lots of pre-installed programs. Maybe [Knoppix]("http://www.knoppix.org/"), though there are several other distros available with lots and lots of software pre-installed.
 
Good luck
