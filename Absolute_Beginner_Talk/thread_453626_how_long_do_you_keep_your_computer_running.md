### Post by steveneddy on 2007-05-26
I believe that leaving a PC on is a good thing. The machine heats up and the MB and all the chips all stretch a little under the heat and every time you turn it off and back on again after everything has had time to cool, the heat cycle start all over again. Things like the solder joints and the mobo material will crack over time causing things like a failure on boot or strange operation when the board heats up and the connection moves far enough apart that it is no longer a connection.

It is mainly keeping the machine warm, close to operational temps so the hardware doesn't go through a cooling cycle and "stretch" the hard parts inside again. 

I leave all PC's on at home and work. My server is an old desktop, PIII, seven years old, that still runs. I believe it is because it has hardly ever been shut down on a regular basis. it is always on.

Same with my laptop. I really don't take it anywhere so I use it as my main PC while at home and just log out and leave it running. 

PC's don't really use that much electricity, so just leave them on. In the case of Windows, you may want to restart once a day for the OS, but you can leave it burning all the time. 

Before anyone starts flaming, I am NOT a Windows expert. I must use Windows at work and have found that I get more reliable performance on a day to day basis if I leave the machine on all the time, and restart when I come in to the office, go get some coffee and open up the office. It just makes a better experience for me, that's all.

### Post by freebird54 on 2007-05-27
In my experience with computers, it is marginally better to leave them on (with power saving features enabled - particularly monitors) as the heat cycle seems to affect the hard drives most. My current system is from 2000 (I think - came with ME :) ) and has had 2 fan replacements and a vid card upgrade in that time. BTW - those rebooting Win - not needed (even on ME) if you have RAM Idle installed - it forces the OS to clean up the memory situation when you require more free - and that has been the main reason for Win slowdowns for YEARS!
