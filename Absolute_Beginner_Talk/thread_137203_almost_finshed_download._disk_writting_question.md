### Post by Qrk on 2006-02-27
You will need to use a 3rd party cd burning program (not Windows media player or assorted burning tools) such as Nero. Nero offers a free trial edition, but there are other free programs available.

Use the program of choice to burn a bootable CD image with your .iso file. It should be fairly easy to do because it is one of the primary functions of the cd burning software.

Burn the CD at 4x. Once you have it, turn on your other computer with the cd in the drive. If you don't see the ubuntu menu, you will have to change your bios to boot to the cd first. On most computers this is Esc, F12 or Del.
