### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-09-10
Since Xubuntu CD's aren't shipped, you'll have to download and burn the disc image yourself. You only have 128 megs of RAM, so I suggest you use the alternate CD for installation (it uses a text-based installer rather than a graphical one, so it will run better on your computer). You'll be fine with the amount of RAM you have, as long as you have a decent processor. I installed Xubuntu on an older computer with a Pentium 3 processor (don't know the exact specs) and 128 megs of RAM, and I was truly amazed at how fast it ran.
