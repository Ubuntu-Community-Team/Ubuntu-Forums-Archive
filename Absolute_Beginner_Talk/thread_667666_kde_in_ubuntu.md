Ubuntu uses Gnome as a desktopmanager and Kubuntu uses KDE as a desktopmanager.. these are no more then default desktopmanagers, you can have all the window/desktopmanagers in the world installed at the same time and simply choose the one to play with from the login-screen.
Basically it's the same story with applications.. as long as you have enough hd-space you can have 'm all installed without performance-issues. You may only wonder why you're having applications installed you'll never use, seems a waste of hd-space.
KDE-apps are developed using libraries used by KDE, therefor they need these libraries loaded into memory in order to function.. you can use them from any other desktopmanager, so you don't need to install the hole KDE-monsterpackage, they just need a couple of libraries.
I use a couple of window/desktopmanager.. it all depends on what I'm doing and where I am and have a couple of KDE-apps I tend to use very often. For me it doesn't matter what libraries they use as long as they do there job. And in my top ten of gui-applications a hole bunch is made using these KDE-libraries, fine with me..
Like "dgray_from_dc" wrote.. play with the applications installed on your system and keep the ones you like, the ones you don't use you can dump if there presence bother you, or if you need the hd-space.

It's good practice though.. to clean-up your system, look at your installed stuff criticaly and dump the stuff you don't need.
By the way, take it slow, instead of uninstalling 100 packages at the same time you better take 'm down one by one, so when you get crap you know what package you need to install again in order to get things running again..
