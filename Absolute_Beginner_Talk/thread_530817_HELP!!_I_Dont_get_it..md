### Post by jfinkels on 2007-08-21
'Unstable' or 'Beta' means that the software is not ready for release to the general public, and that the developers cannot assure the consumer that the software will not crash/be buggy. Most of the time, this means that almost all of it works, but there are still a few bugs that the software developers are working on.

Compiz (/Beryl) has been beta for quite a while, because (1) it is somewhat sophisticated software and is fairly new and (2) the developers have to deal with graphics card vendors (i.e. nVidia, ATI, etc.) who do not support Linux by releasing open source drivers.

### Post by anewguy on 2007-08-21
Something that was mentioned above is worth repeating and clarifying on it's own, as it applies to many users:

Your video card may not be supported, or even more likely, particularly in the more inexpensive laptops and desktops with an integrated graphics processor, your video hardware may not even support Beryl and/or Compiz/Compiz Fusion.

It can be a real frustration trying to get this to work to begin with, then add a video processor that just can't run it to begin with, and you get a lot of complaints. 

Important thing to any newbies reading this thread:  it's eye candy, not a "have to have to run Ubuntu".:)
