### Post by lamalex on 2008-01-07

Automatix has been found guilty for breaking many people's Ubuntu installs due to the use of improperly maintained third party repos and some less than Universe quality packages. Dependency issues are a big issue, and it will almost certainly break your upgrade to the next version.

### Post by hyper_ch on 2008-01-07
Well, ubuntuforums.org has an official stand to not support Automatix in its current state. One of the Ubuntu Core Devs did look at the code for a short time and has very serious concerns:

[http://mjg59.livejournal.com/77440.html](http://mjg59.livejournal.com/77440.html)

While I agree that automatix is a great tool, especially when want to go up and running it leads to an unknown state of your installation. While a lot of people do not have problems at all, there are, not too few, who have problems.

IMHO recommend Automatix if you like it - but als tell tell that it can render your installation to a non-recoverable state - and that this DOES happen.
