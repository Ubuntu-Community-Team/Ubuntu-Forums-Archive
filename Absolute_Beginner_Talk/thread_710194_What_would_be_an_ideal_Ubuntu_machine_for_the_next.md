### Post by smurphy_it on 2008-02-28
Some manufacturers sell systems with different versions of Linux already on them.  I believe Dell & HP do that for some of their computers in the US.

Other than that, I'd probably go with any computer that has a half decent video card (nvidia preferably) so you won't run into too many driver issues.  I'd research the type of video card first though to make sure others aren't having any issues with that kind of video card first.

SD card reading, CD Burning, Wired network etc are pretty standard now so you shouldn't run into any problems on that front.

### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2008-02-28
And don't buy a new model. Try to get a laptop that's been out for at least a year. Intel hardware is generally better. Nvidia is better than ATI. Webcam support isn't that great at the moment.

If you are going to a store to shop, take a live cd with you of your favorite distro. See what works out of the box and what doesn't. Be sure to ask a salesperson first, they rarely say no but it's just good manners. 

If you're shopping used get the detailed specs (intel 945GM video card, intel ich7 hda audio rev03, etc..) and check out those pieces online to see what people are saying.

For added piece of mind, and to support the linux movement you can also buy Linux preloaded, Dell, System76, etc.
