### Post by stayingawhile on 2008-07-14
After wrestling with this question for about a week now, I come firmly down on the side of K3b.  Intially a pain to get KDE installed (justified for me by Amarok), but then has the clearest and most reliable track set up, a reliable burn and most importantly for me in-the-car CD-TEXT setup which just seems to work.  The other things I tried out extensively were: Brasera (pretty good but in later versions but no CD-Text), Serpentine (To simple and clunky) and Gnomebaker (quite good, but no CD-Text).
