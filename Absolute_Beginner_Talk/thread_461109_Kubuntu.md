### Post by doobit on 2007-06-01
There are no problems with installing the KDE desktop over Ubuntu. In fact, that will give you a choice at the log  in screen of either desktop. I have Gnome and Xfce on mine. The downside, if there is one, is that you get a lot of extra programs that you may not need. Teh repositories are the same, but the package choices are a little different since there are certain packages which are made to integrate better with one or the other.

### Post by Sparkster185 on 2007-06-01
If you already know you want to use KDE instead of GNOME, I would just install Kubuntu by itself.  Like the above poster said, you can use GNOME apps in KDE and vice-versa, but you'll have to install a bunch of libraries.  It's not really a big deal as your package manager will handle it for you.
