### Post by aysiu on 2006-03-29
If you [verify the integrity of the ISO](http://www.linuxiso.org/viewdoc.php/verifyiso.html), burn the ISO as a **disk image** (not as data), and burn it at a relatively slow speed (8x or slower), you should be good.

### Post by YuHoo on 2006-03-29
All cd burning software ought to have the option to burn from an *image* or ISO. If you are cheap like me CDBurnerXP worked perfectly fine for me [http://www.cdburnerxp.se/](http://www.cdburnerxp.se/) . If you use a CD-RW make sure that you do a full erase if data is already on it. The quick erase can be hit or miss depending on what is on it since it only removes the table of contents. I've had horrific experiances with quick erase so just take your time, rent a movie, clean the house, and come back to a fresh cd.
