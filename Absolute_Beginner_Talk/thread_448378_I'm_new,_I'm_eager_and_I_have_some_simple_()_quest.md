### Post by Simran on 2007-05-19
> 
What is the difference between Ubuntu, Kubuntu, edubuntu and xubuntu ?

Why have different OS's why not just install the basic, then offer the additional options ?


Hi :) welcome, 

Ubuntu / Kubuntu / Xubuntu are not really different OS's. they are just different desktop environments. Ubuntu runs GNOME 
Kubuntu runs KDE
Xubuntu runs XFCE 

I prefer GNOME but its worth trying each one to see which fits your needs or you are most comfortable with. Though XFCE is a lightweight interface for slower / older machines. 

> 

What is Dapper, Dapper drake and breezey and edgy ?



These are just the version names, like "xp" or "me" ubuntu has nicer names like "dapper drake" "Edgy Eft"

The latest release is Fiesty Fawn 7.04. I would reccomend using that one, though Dapper drake does offer Long term support.

> 


What is Beryl?



Beryl is a combined window manager and composite manager, using OpenGL. Basically its an alternative to metacity, which is the native one for gnome. It offers nice fancy graphics and looks smooth. bit of eyecandy really.

Hope this helped, have answered it to the best of my knowledge ( i'm a bit of a noob my self but trying to help out ) :)

Simran

### Post by TomFumb on 2007-05-19
If you're new I would recommend sticking with Ubuntu, I went with Xubuntu when I first started out with linux, as my laptop is quite old now, but having now switched to using Gnome instead of XFCE I wish I'd started with Gnome. XFCE on Xubuntu is really nice, and quite fast, but it's much harder to follow tutorials and how-tos when the authors are guiding you through the menus you get on Gnome. 

If I was you I would steer clear of Beryl for now, it does look very nice, but unless you can waxe lyrical about graphics chips and obscure drivers I wouldn't bother getting into it. If you're a newbie the headaches from configuring Beryl could put you off for life!

### Post by eentonig on 2007-05-19
If you want to try the different *buntu flavors, it's very easy to install them after your initial installation.

Just open a terminal and type in

> sudo apt-get kubuntu-desktop
or
> sudo apt-get xubuntu-desktop
or
> sudo apt-get edubuntu-desktop

Just make sure you keep using your original login manager when the question pops up. (Not strictly necessary, but keeps things easier).

 (or use the GUI synaptic for the task)

Afterwards, before login, you can choose which Desktop Environment you want to use.
