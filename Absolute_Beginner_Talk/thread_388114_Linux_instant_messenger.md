### Post by flossgeek on 2007-03-19
Hang on, there is confusion here. Gaim is fully open source, it is the standards that may not be open that you choose to use. Gaim can support many different standards wether open or closed. If you use Jabber in gaim you will be using an open standard. If you use MSN your using a closed standard, so just use gaim with jabber(which you can use GMAIL for also).

### Post by denver on 2007-03-19
There are a few IM clients out there out of wich Gaim and Kopete are the most complete. They each support a wide range of IM protocols. Basicaly you can connect simultanioucely to almost any well known IM network out there using these two clients. There are clients specific to one netw or another aMSN ( MSN messenger ), tapioca ( gtalk client ), Gyach and GyachI ( yahoo messenger ). There are few or poor proprietary clients out there. Yahoo! has one...but we have cars older then that and to tell you the truth...it suc*s. Skype has a decent client...but it laks functionality ( no video call ).

I strongly recoment you use eather Kopete or Gaim.
