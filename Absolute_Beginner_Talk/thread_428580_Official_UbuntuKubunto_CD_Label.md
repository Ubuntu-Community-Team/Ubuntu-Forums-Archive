### Post by ne1scott on 2007-04-30
Is it just me, or are others annoyed to no end that there aren't and downloadable CD labels for any of the *ubuntu series of releases.  Having to "create your own" label just seems stupid when the Free CD's already have a label designed.  I don't like downloading an OS, burning it to CD, and then having some cheapass looking homemade lable or handwritten label.  It degrades the whole feeling of the install media and the OS (a little) to non-professional. 
   I think the CD image artwork should be available from the download page as well as including it in the / directory of the CD/DVD images so those of us with CD printers (under $100 now) can have nice "Official" *ubuntu labels on our media so that people see it and ask "What's that disk with the cool label ?" and thus decide to try on of the various *ubuntu releases.
   I have been a Redhat Linux Administrator for years and am new to the *ubuntu series, but it seems that most Linux releases (including Debian, Gentoo, or Redhat based) don't have CD label artwork available unless you buy the media or wait for the free CD's.  Searching the web I can find homemade lables of old versions, but that's not the same as having an "Official" label printed.
   It seems a waste, but I may have to waste the money of the *ubuntu projects by requesting the free CD of each release just so I have a disc with nice official artwork on it.  I'd rather let the *ubuntu projects keep the money for servers or development costs and print my own, but it doesn't look like I'm being given a choice.
