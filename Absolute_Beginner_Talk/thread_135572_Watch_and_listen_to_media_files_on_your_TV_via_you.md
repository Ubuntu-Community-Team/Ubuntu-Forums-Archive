### Post by macerata on 2006-02-24
OK.  I wanted to access the content on my PC through my TV.  After doing some research into this, I finally found a solution.

You can use:
1. [TwonkyMedia (server software)]("http://www.twonkyvision.de/UPnP/index.html")
2. [Buffalo Linktheater (DVD player and media client [link between TV and PC])]("http://www.buffalotech.co.uk/products/product-detail.php?productid=96&categoryid=18")

I set this up yesterday, and it works.  A few files don't (very few), but most do:

*music, video, and photos.*

HDTV is also supported, though I'd opt for getting component leads to connect the LinkTheater to your TV.  I also opted to run an ethernet cable to my router, though the set up also supports wireless.

The quality's surprisingly good, though searching through folders takes a few seconds longer than you'd want.  Apart from that, though, I'm very satisfied.

You can get other hardware that works, though I can't vouch for that - check out the [TwonkyMedia Compatibility page]("http://www.twonkyvision.de/UPnP/devices.html") for details.  You also have to pay for TwonkyMedia, but it's not much - about 30 € for the full version with unlimited updates, and you can pay via PayPal.

I'm happy.  It works!  And it's another nail in the coffin for Windows.  Yeah!
