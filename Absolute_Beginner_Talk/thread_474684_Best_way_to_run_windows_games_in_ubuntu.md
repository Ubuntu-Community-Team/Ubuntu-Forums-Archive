### Post by Proteos on 2007-06-15
Personally, I think Wine is the way to go. It's free, the longest-supported, and seems to work. The others cost money and I know one of them is based on Wine anyway. 

I installed Wine easily. I looked at how to execute an MSI script (as the Steam client is), and it worked fine. I had to make sure my video driver was uptodate and I can play DOD:S quite well with it. I'll be looking at some tweaking, but overall very pleased. And, if it can hangle DOD:S well, it should do equally well with other games. Go to [http://www.winehq.org](http://www.winehq.org) and take a look at the list of supported games and applications. You'll be pleased.

They support 1933 games: [http://appdb.winehq.org/appbrowse.php?iCatId=2](http://appdb.winehq.org/appbrowse.php?iCatId=2)
