### Post by SkimWear on 2006-03-30
heh. sure.

depends on your computer manufacturer but whatever the case it is here are the steps.

1. Restart your computer
2. Here's where im not sure. but try the following:
Press "del" "f1" "f8" "f9" "f10" repeadetly. One of those will work. its usually DEL or F1.
3. You are now in BIOS!
4. Look for Boot-up options or Startup options. (if you cant find any just look for anything that might contain Boot-up sequence in it)
5. Look for something that says boot sequence, or startup sequence.
6. Highlight it and hit enter
7. Depends on computer, but its usually self-explanatory once you are there. Highlight Option 1, since you want CDROM to boot first (I believe SPACEBAR changes between your options) set your first option to CDROM or CD.
if there WAS something on that 1st option, make sure to add it on option 2 or 3, whichever is blank. Specially if its HD, because you want to make sure your hard drive boots up :)
8. Save that config
9. Save and exit BIOS.
10. QUICKLY insert the ubuntu CD :) because your computer is now rebooting with your new BIOS settings
11. BAM your done. get yourself a coke and pat yourself on the back.

hope this helps a bit ;)

### Post by catlett on 2006-03-31
Your BIOS options are configured during the boot process of your computer. When you start your computer there will be a screen that comes on quickly with a couple of options. Mine is a screen with IBM on it and down below a line that says press F1 for options. Press F12 for start up list. If I press F1 it takes me to my BIOS screen. This is a text screen. You want to scroll around and look for boot options. The options are different waysto start your computer. Mine is A,cdrom,hard drive. This setup makes the computer search my floppy drive A first for media, then the cd rom and finally my hard drive. If your bios isn't set up with your cd rom before your hard drive you won't boot to your cd rom because your computer will search your hard drive first. Before installing you should search the Ubunu wiki for a how to install. You should also make a backup of your current system in case something goes wrong.  By putting in the cd and booting from it you should be able to follow the instructions and get installed but it is safest to research a bit.
