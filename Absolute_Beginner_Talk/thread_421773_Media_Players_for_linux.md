### Post by stokedfish on 2007-04-25
For local music: Amarok

(by far! I tried pretty much all other players, Exaile, Yammi, Rhythmbox, Banshee, BMPx, Songbird, Listen, Quodlibet, etc - *all* of them are lacking compared to Amarok. it's the little things that makes Amarok so good...it's the best designed player on linux and the way it presents your music collection and its metadata is just superior to every other player I have tried, and those were many! well it's a matter of taste but I think Amarok is just ahead of the game)

[if you like it lightweight, xmms and adacious are still quite nice]

For music over a network:  MPD & NCMPC

(mpd as a backend, ncmpc as a *great* and very efficient console player)

For music streaming: Jinzora webinterface & dyndns

(it's fantastic...I can listen to all my library on my home-server everywhere in the world)

For wireless control of your music: Giantdisc & Palm TX

(friggin' awesome...[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=407786](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=407786) )

For video: VLC is my fav

(Kaffeine and MPlayer are very good as well though)

--> VLC is getting ported to QT now so it will fit nicely into a KDE environment...

As an alarm clock:  MPD/MPC & Cron

(very reliable, stable, easy to set up)

Mediacenter: Haven't tried any yet...but can recommend Amarok & Fullscreen script & bluetooth control over a Sony Ericsson cell phone

Sooo....it depends on what you want to do I guess!   ;)
