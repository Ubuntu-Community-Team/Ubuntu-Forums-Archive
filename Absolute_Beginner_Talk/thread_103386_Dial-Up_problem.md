### Post by Bentu on 2005-12-14
The tool for setting up your dial-up modem is under system, administration, I believe it's called network tools (there's only one in that category for networking). The first step in getting any modem to work in linux is getting the system to recognize your modem, which you would do by clicking detect modem. when you do that the modem info will fill in if it's recognized, and you've got it made. I'm not able to walk you through step by step, since that part of my Ubuntu won't come up when selected, haven't found a fix for it. If you happen to have winmodems, they'll work work with linux, you just have to have the drivers. Below are the two links which will guide you through that and there's links to the drivers that you'll need. Hope this helps you move forward.
