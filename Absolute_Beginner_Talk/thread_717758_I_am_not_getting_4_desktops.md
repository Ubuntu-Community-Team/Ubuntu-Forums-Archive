### Post by LuisGMarine on 2008-03-09
Well here is my post in case this issue hasn't been resolved, I think the post started to go off track.

To enable the desktop cube, just incase you don't have 4 desktops on your regular desktop.

Right Click on the workspace applet on your desktop.  You know the one that shows allt he available desktops , by default Ubuntu 7.10 only has 2 enabled.  After you right click it, click on preferences and its going to bring up a little window.

In the window set Columns to 4, this is how many desktops you are going to have, the rows is just if you want to have it display different.  Like 2 x 2 or 3 x 3, columns is what controls how many workspaces you have.

### Post by Sukarn on 2008-03-11
> **rajaram_s said:**
> Hey...

I am using ubuntu 7.10. I am not getting the 4 desktops as a cube as I was getting in my ubuntu 7.04. I have also installed compiz. When I ectend the no. of desktops till 2, it works. For any number beyond 2, none of the desktops work. When I switch from one desktop to another, I get a screen with the wallpaper alone and not even the taskbar. In such cases I will be forced to reboot my computer..... any remedies?

I had this problem once.

I suppose you have ccsm installed. Open "Advanced Desktop Effects Settings" and go to General Options -> Desktop Size

I suppose you have changed some settings here.

Change the settings so that the number of desktops you want is given only by "Horizontal Virtual Size". Change the other two settings to 1.

Your problem should now be fixed.
