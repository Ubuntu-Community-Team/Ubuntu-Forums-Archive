### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-28
There are some things to consider, if you would like a colour laser printer.

1. Do you only want it for text and graphics?
 If so, it will do the job nicely. However, if you also want to print photographs, you will be disappointed. Laser printers do not have the resolution capability if a good inkjet.

2.The cost of toner cartridges for a colour laser printer is quite considerable.

3. You can re-fill most inkjet cartridges quite economically. 

If you do decide to buy a colour laser printer, you get what you pay for. Generally HP printers are very friendly with Linux.

However, be careful of any laser printer that only has a small amount of on-board memory if you intend to print graphics, as they can be slow and even baulk at large graphic images.

A classic example of this is with two HP monochrome lasers that I have read reports on.

The HP laserjet 1020 only has 2 Mb of internal RAM and baulks at large graphics images, but the HP laserjet 1022, which has 8 Mb of RAM works perfectly.
