### Post by hartz on 2007-06-25
Hi,

I know neither Macs nor Wireless hardware very well, but I would like to help.

1) I gather that your problems are unusual - other people easily get the Wifi on their MacBooks to work.  This would seem to suggest that something went wrong with your setup.  Can I suggest that you start over?  It might seem unnecessary but I am also concerned that you may have made too many changes and did not undo each one carefully after you did them.

2) When you get errors, note them.  It will get you a lot of help in the forums if you can explain what you did and what the error message is.  Linux is extremely user friendly - it tells you what is wrong, but people are used to Microsoft's meaningless error messages so they tend to skim over Linux errors, assuming these are the same.  Pavlov had something to say about this, I think.

3) Do one thing at a time, make one change at a time, and test carefully.

You are going to find you either solve your problem yourself, or you easily get the right info to get someone else to help you.

P.S. I love your enthusiasm.
