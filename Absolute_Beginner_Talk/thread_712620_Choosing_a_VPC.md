### Post by owbizi on 2008-03-01
For games, I think a Virtual PC is not a recommended option. It will, on mostly of cases, suffer from a huge perfomance hit, highly noticiable - or it's what I hear about, at least.

By the way, you can try [wine (general, free), [url=http://www.transgaming.com/]Cedega](http://winehq.org/) (games, paid), or [CrossOve](http://www.codeweavers.com/)r (office/games, paid), with less perfomance hit and active forums to help ya - do a search here for wine, for example. :)

Also, wine has an [application database](http://appdb.winehq.org/) to inform you if the program that you want run on it or not, and how well it runs. Pretty nice, hm!
