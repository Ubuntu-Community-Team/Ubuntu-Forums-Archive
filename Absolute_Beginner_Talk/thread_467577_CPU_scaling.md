### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-08
By doing this, Ubuntu is just being efficient.

Most PC's when running software are just sitting there, waiting for you to enter something on the keyboard.

Frequency scaling will not affect performance, as the CPU is cranked-up when required and wound-down when little is going on.

In the long-run this will probably extend the life of your CPU as it is running much cooler and also the cooling fan will run less frequently.

To sum it up, you won't get any better performance by forcing you CPU to run flat-out and will probably reduce its' life if you do.
