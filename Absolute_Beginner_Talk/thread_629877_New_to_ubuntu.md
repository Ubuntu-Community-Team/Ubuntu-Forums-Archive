### Post by dyous87 on 2007-12-02
Ok if I am not mistaken the fglrx drivers may be the problem. ATI always has horrible linux support so when in doubt always stick to an Intel chipset or Nvidia. The drivers I believe are blacklisted by the compiz development team because they are not 100% compatible and certain things such as games and videos may not work well with compiz enebaled. 

If you want to try at your own risk, you can try to remove the drivers from the blacklist. To do this, open up a terminal and type in:

```
sudo gedit /usr/bin/compiz
```Gedit will open up the file. Hit 'Ctrl, F' and do a search for *# blacklist based on the pci ids. *Under that line you will see:

```
# blacklist based on the pci ids 
# See http://wiki.compiz-fusion.org/Hardware/Blacklist for details
T="   1002:5954 1002:5854 1002:5955" # ati rs480
T="$T 1002:4153" # ATI Rv350
T="$T 8086:2982 8086:2992 8086:29a2 8086:2a02 8086:2a12"  # intel 965
T="$T 8086:2972" # i965 (x3000)
T="$T 1002:3152 1002:3150 1002:5462 1002:5653 " # ati X300 X600,X600 X700 
BLACKLIST_PCIIDS="$T"
unset T
```replace it with:  

```
# blacklist based on the pci ids 
# See http://wiki.compiz-fusion.org/Hardware/Blacklist for details
# T="   1002:5954 1002:5854 1002:5955" # ati rs480
# T="$T 1002:4153" # ATI Rv350
T="$T 8086:2982 8086:2992 8086:29a2 8086:2a02 8086:2a12"  # intel 965
T="$T 8086:2972" # i965 (x3000)
# T="$T 1002:3152 1002:3150 1002:5462 1002:5653 " # ati X300 X600,X600 X700 
BLACKLIST_PCIIDS="$T"
unset T
```and save the file. Now you can restart X by pressing: 'Ctrl, Alt, Backspace'. After you log back in try enabling desktop effects.

Let me know what happens. :)
