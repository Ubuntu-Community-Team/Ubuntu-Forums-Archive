### Post by Nequeo on 2006-06-07
[QUOTE=Fornie]any one? please help :confused: :([/QUOTE]

If you can't get the drive to open even after turning the computer completely off then on again, then it's very likely the drive is physically broken.

If you have another PC, or a friend with a PC, just rip the drive out and plug it into their machine. If it still doesn't work there then the drive is fried. (Probably from sticking the manual release pin in a bit too hard or something). 

But if it does work, then you can probably stick it back into your PC and it will work again. (Just don't try to play that VCD again). I've had copy protected DVDs do that when I tried to play them. Both in Linux AND Windows. The solution was to rip the DVD to my hard-drive and play it.
