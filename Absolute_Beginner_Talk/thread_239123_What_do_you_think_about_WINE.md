### Post by drchocobo on 2006-08-18
It depends on what you whant to do really.  Ive run it for a little while now and I find it does well on common apps like word or office and such, but its a bit tricky to get it rolling with resource intensive stuff like games that make use of hardware acceleration.  Getting unreal or halfe-life to run in it can be a real challenge, but its great for the web page designer that I use and the other more work related things I couldnt QUITE find a perfect match for.

So in the world of applications, it is more stable than in the world of windows games or first person shooters.  THis much I have learned the hard way, but I'm just a newbie so I may have observed wrong.  A lot of problems or hangups are fixed if you have native DLL files.  Some programs simply MUST have them, but my experiance with WINE has been rather bittersweet, more so on the sweet side.  Its a masterfull undertaking if you think about what it must take to duplicate the ful functionality of windows in a non Microsoftian way :)

Anyway, there's my two cents :)
