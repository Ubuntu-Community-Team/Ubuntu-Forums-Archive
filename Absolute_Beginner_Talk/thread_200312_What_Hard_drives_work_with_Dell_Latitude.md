### Post by anaconda on 2006-06-20
The biggest limitation is probaply the physical dimensions of your hd. Some machines have only room for 9,5mm thick hd:s, but bigger laptops may have 12,5mm thick hd:s

Almost all new laptop hd:s are 9,5mm thick, but if you buy old used hd it could be thickker. (I am assuming 10GB hd is used, or are that small hd:s still sold new?)

The only other restriction may be, that your Bios may have a limit.. in how big hd:S it supports. (10GB isn't a problem) My old laptop (siemens manufactured 1998 ) can regognice 40GB hd without problems, so if yours is newer, you wont have problems with this

Otherwise ANY laptop (ide-)hd fits your laptop. Your existing hd may have some fitting around it to change the laptop IDE to different looking connector, but you can take it from your old hd and put it to your new one..
