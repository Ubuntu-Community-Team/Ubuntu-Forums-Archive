### Post by stinger30au on 2008-04-02
Many years ago when i was growing up and starting in computers my dad bought home a Tandy Color Computer. In fact in those days it was Called a Radio Shack TRS-80 Colour Computer 1. It was piainted in Mercedies benz silver and had 16k of RAM and we thought we were going places in 1982 when dad bought it home.

We learnt a lot from magazines in those days dedicated to the "CoCo" as it was affectionately known as to those of us who loved it.

One of the magazines was called "The Rainbow" magazine. It was an American magazine and came out of Fort worth Texas ,editied by a man by the name of Lonnie Falk if my memory serves me right.

One of the competitions they ran every month was the "one liner" competition. It was to see how much you could get the coco to do with a single line of program in basic. the input buffer for the single line was about 255 characters. there was all kinds of stuff like a skiing game, some random graphics displays, a disc editor and tons of stuff sent in by many users world wide every month for many years.

### Post by kpkeerthi on 2008-04-02
The .thumbnails folder in $HOME accumulates thumbnails nautilus creates for your files & folders that you view. Use this to clean up unused ones (15 days or older) while still keeping the recently used ones.
```

find ~/.thumbnails -name "*.png" -mtime +14 -exec rm '{}' ';'

```
