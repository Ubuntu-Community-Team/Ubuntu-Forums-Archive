### Post by ClarkePeters on 2006-09-28
My wife is Chinese, I am American. I need a dual language system. 
So first, CHEERS to Ubuntu for making this possible (and in a way that Windows doesn't support)

Here's what happened to me. I set my system up in English and then went to System-->Administration-->Language Support and checked off Chinese.  I also setup SCIM through System-->Preferences-->SCIM Input Method.

It worked fine for my wife in doing internet stuff, but then later we had  SCIM problems--never knew why--my wife couldn't us it anymore.  

SO... what did I do...

I didn't realize that you can boot into the language of your choice (because I simply never looked at the Options button down in the bottom right hand corner at login time)  So I wasted countless hours trying to get my SCIM to work for my wife so she could type chinese in my English system.

The solution is too simple. In short, you only need to make sure you do the System-->Administration-->Language Support and then check off your preferred language/s. That's it. Then you have a choice of language at login time. For example:

When I boot up, it's in English, when my wife comes along, she simply presses  Alt + Ctl + backspace  and then changes the language (temporary for this session only). When I come along, I do the same--only I don't have to make a language choice since mine is the default. (you can also just do the shut down button and then choose "log out" instead of doing alt + ctl + backspace)

But I almost forgot the most wonderful thing!!, which beats Windows to a pulp. When my wife logs in EVERYTHING is chinese, not just the input method! She was so thrilled!
