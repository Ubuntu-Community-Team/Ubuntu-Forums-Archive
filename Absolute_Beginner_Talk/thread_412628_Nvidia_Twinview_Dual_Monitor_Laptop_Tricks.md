### Post by vayde on 2007-04-18
I've been fighting with dual monitors on my Inspiron 9300 for a while now, and I finally got the baby working.  I found some interesting tricks/gotchas, and while I'm not really qualified to write up a full howto, I want to get the info out there.

Here's what I did in Ubuntu Feisty:  (I don't know how far back these tricks will work for, I suspect they'll be fine in Edgy and Dapper too)

Fresh install of Feisty.

Enable Restricted Drivers via System->Admin->Restricted Drivers Manager

Actually I installed nvidia-glx via apt-get, but I enabled it via above too, just to be safe.

The problem I was having was this:  When connected to an external monitor, my laptop wouldn't run the lcd screen.  If I hotplugged an external monitor, it wouldn't run the external monitor.

I could run xinerama and have all be well, but then I couldn't use beryl, and after all, what is life without beryl?

I could enable twinview from nvidia-settings, but that made my desktop all screwey, and for some reason disabled my sound.  Enabling it and logging out/back in fixed the desktop, but not the sound, and was too much of a pain.

What I wanted was a configuration that would run 1 or 2 screens depending on what I had plugged in, as I go from 2 screens (office) to 1 screen (home) every day, and was tired of messing with the settings.

Here's what I discovered:

First of all, there is a big difference between directives put in the ServerSettings and Device sections of xorg.conf

putting twinview stuff in ServerSettings wasn't working for me, as by the time that part of the file was processed, the second monitor was already turned off.

Twinview options needed to be in the Device section.  Putting them there gave me half a screen, as it was enabling twinview, but not firing up the second monitor.

The solution was in the following line:	
Option		"MetaModes"	" CRT: nvidia-autoselect 1440x900 +0+0, DFP: nvidia-autoselect 1440x900 +1440+0"

The nvidia-autoselect kicks it in the pants and tells it to go out and look for the monitor in question.

I found nvidia-autoselect by running nvidia-settings, enabling twinview and both monitors and watching carefully at what it was putting into xorg.conf.

What follows is my xorg.conf.  The commented out pieces in the ServerSettings section are just stuff I tried, I don't know whether it's better to have them there, or in the Device section.  I stopped tweaking when I got what I wanted.

I hope this helps someone.
