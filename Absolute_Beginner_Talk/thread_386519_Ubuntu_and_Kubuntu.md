### Post by tom56 on 2007-03-17
KDE has more tools for changing settings - if you want to tweak abasolutely everything then KDE is probably for you. There's nothing you can't do in KDE, it's quite liberarting. However in my opinion apps have a habit of all looking slightly different (like in Windows) which can be annoying to some people.

GNOME has a simpler interface. This can be good because it means you don't waste your time changing 50 settings because you don't know which one changes the thing you want. However if you know exactly what you want to do and you can't do it it's very frustrating. I find that GNOME apps "fit together" better than KDE ones do. Though I think that KDE is catching up in this regard.

Neither is better than the other, it's a question of personal preference - and most people when pressed will find it difficult to explain why they like one over the other (or will give reasons that aren't true/don't make sense). I suggest you use both for a week each and then decide which you prefer.

At the end of the day all KDE apps run on GNOME, and all GNOME apps run on KDE so it's not exactly a binding decision.

EDIT: Diskotek is right, GNOME uses less resources (mainly better on memory usage) - forgot to mention that
