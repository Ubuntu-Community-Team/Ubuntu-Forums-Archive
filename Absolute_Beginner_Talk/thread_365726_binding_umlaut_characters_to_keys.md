### Post by DerArzt on 2007-02-20
Is there any easy way to bind characters with umlauts to shortcuts? I'd like to make Ctrl+u a u with umlaut, Ctrl+o an o with umlaut, and Ctrl+a an a with an umlaut.

### Post by Strider42 on 2007-03-22
There is an easy way in Ubuntu to do this and somewhat more intuitive than M$ too.

Click: System - Preferences - Keyboard

Click: Layout Options tab

Click: Down arrow next to Compose Key Position

Choose your preference.  I use Right Alt is Compose

Restart X: Ctrl Alt Backspace

Now when you are in a document and you want ä, ë, ö, ü, Ä, Ë, Ö, Ü you hit the Right Alt key, the letter and then ", so for ä the key strokes are:
Right Alt
a
Shift 2

Hope this helps.
Paul
