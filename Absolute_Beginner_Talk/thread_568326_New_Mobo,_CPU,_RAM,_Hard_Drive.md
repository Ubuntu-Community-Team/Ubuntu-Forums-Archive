### Post by benerivo on 2007-10-05
I would probably ditch the 6GB drive because of it's age. It may be not be reliable, and as it offers you only a small storage size you would not lose much by just throwing it away. You may however find some use for it if you use it for backup of important files (the probability of both drives failing at the same time is astronomical).

So, I would go with 10GB for the Ubuntu OS space, 1 GB for the swap partition and have the rest as a partition for for personal storage. When installing, you can choose to have this partition as your /home folder, or you can just have it as a standalone (os agnostic) partition that contains your own data (as I do).
