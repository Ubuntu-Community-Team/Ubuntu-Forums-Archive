### Post by jflaker on 2007-12-16
I don't have links, but a good explanation of what programming is:

identifying a "problem" which occupies your time or other people's time which, with some level of automation (either complete or simplification from a screen entry) will apply efficiency to a process or a stream of processes.

As an example which presents a "problem", but not real world...or is it?....programming an onscreen soda machine.  You need to break the "Problem" into individual pieces as such:

*Receiving money into the machine
*Dispensing of product
*inventory control (how to know you are out of product) and not allowing for purchasing of out of stock items
*returning money if too much was put into machine
*Cash control.....you need to make change....can you?  NO?  Only accept exact change until enough change is accumulated into the "cash drawer"

The above can get more granular as you implement code to handle each piece of the "machine".......

The list can go on and on...depends on how granular you want to get.

Once your process is defined, you will need to decide on the interface and this will dictate what language would be best suited to the task.  If you want to make this a web applications...you have certain options.  If this is a gui application, you have certain options.  Your choice of what OS you will run on?  You want to run on any OS, your choice just became JAVA.....as it is portable across a multitude of OS's.

just a thought, more specifics will probably be suggested by others.
