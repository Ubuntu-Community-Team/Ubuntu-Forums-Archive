### Post by Jenda on 2006-08-12
First of all - you might notice that that comparison is written by the creators of Automatix, and can thus be hardly considered impartial.

Easyubuntu aims to create a secure, efficient way to get your computer ready to use after installing Ubuntu - through adding the features that were not included by default for legal or other reasons.
Unlike Automatix, Easyubuntu only works with tweaks that cannot be done through a simple sudo apt-get - Easyubuntu does not try to be a replacement for synaptic.
Easyubuntu now also offers an offline mode for computers with slow internet access or none at all. All you need is one other, internet-capable computer to install all you want.
Easyubuntu does not cause permanent damage to your system, as automatix seems to have in the past (haven't tested the newest releases, which seem to have overcome a lot of the problems of the past)
