### Post by Fass on 2006-08-14
It depends on your monitor and your graphics card. Some support higher resolutions and some don't.

To try out a higher resolution, you need to edit your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file and in the "Screen" section add the resolution you want to all the "Modes" lines. Or, at least to the "Modes" line corresponding to the colour depth you want to use it with. Then just restart your Xserver with Ctrl+Alt+Backspace.

### Post by PriceChild on 2006-08-14
You should use gksudo to launch a root gedit.

I think it would be much easier to reconfigure the xserver instead of manually changing the file:
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```
Leave all the options as default until you get to choose the maximum resolutions for your monitor.

Remember space will (un)check them whereas return will go to the next page.
