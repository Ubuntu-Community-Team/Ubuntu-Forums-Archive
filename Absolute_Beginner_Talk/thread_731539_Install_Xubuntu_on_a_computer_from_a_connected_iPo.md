### Post by Hospadar on 2008-03-22
The simple answer is no.

The problem is an ipod isn't like an external hard drive (in which case it would still take a lot more work than just putting the iso on it, look into booting off flash drives)  While the ipod can be mounted, I'm pretty sure that it can't be accessed by your bios, and as such can't be booted off of.

I might be wrong though, in which case you have to at least extract the files from the iso, and no doubt do some other work.

Long story short, it's probably way easier to drop the change on a blank cd.
