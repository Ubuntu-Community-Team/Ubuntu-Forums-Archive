### Post by Hendrixski on 2006-12-07
Check that you're not behind some weird firewall, that may interfere with Gaim.  There's manuals everywhere for that.

and most music players don't come with mp3 support installed because of legal concerns, you have to DL stuff (most often by clicking a button in the music player itself).  I use AmaroK, and the first time I tried to play an mp3 it asked if I wanted to upgrade it so that it will play mp3's.

### Post by Hendrixski on 2006-12-07
Also, make sure that it is MP3's that you're playing, not some proprietary format that only works on one "digital music player".

I bought a Sony "digital music player" a week or so ago, and it didn't play mp3's, it converted them to some format on the player.  I returned it.  I'm sure some engineer thought it was a brilliant idea but I wasn't able to play music from the player on Linux.

so yeah, make sure that it is MP3's, not something else.
