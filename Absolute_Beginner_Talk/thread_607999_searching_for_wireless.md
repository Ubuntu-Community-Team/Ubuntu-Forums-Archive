### Post by IamAcoconut on 2007-11-10
You can start network manager from the terminal OR alt+F2 prompt by typing nm-applet. You can also add it to your startup session (Preferences->Sessions).

Do you have a wifi router at home? Hardware or software (computer)? Or do you rely directly on an ISP to provide wifi connection, like in a hotspot? I mean if you don't know your IP address (wireless, right?), then you should probably enable the use of DHCP server running on your/ISP's router, that will assing you an IP.

BTW, you don't really need nm-applet. You may choose Wicd instead, which I can vehemently recommend. [http://wicd.sourceforge.net/](http://wicd.sourceforge.net/)
