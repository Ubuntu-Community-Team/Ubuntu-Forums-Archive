### Post by mike3k on 2006-10-16
USB adapters are evil. They're non-standard and each one needs a different driver. PC cards, on the other hand, use one of several chipsets and the drivers for most of them are built into the OS.

I use a Mac as my primary system and a box running Ubuntu as my server. If something doesn't work on a Mac, I wouldn't expect it to work with Linux. Some USB devices such as card readers & external drivers are standard and will work without installing any driver; they usually work with no problems on Linux. Devices that need a driver, such as webcams, wireless adapters, and USB modems, on the other hand, are trouble if you're using anything other than winblows.
