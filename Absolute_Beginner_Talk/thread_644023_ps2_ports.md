### Post by OffAxis on 2007-12-18
> but if you bought the rights and made a port, then modded the port to create diff games...
?

I think you're confusing porting with licensing.

The licensing of game engines is done all the time; the unreal or cry engine, for example, are used as the driving force for several games in their own right; gears of war or bioshock, for example.
The engine handles the heavy lifting- the processing, the physics, etc. and different 'mods' (game elements like gameplay and graphics) are applied to them.


There are more readily available engines (open source and closed source) on PCs than anything for the  ps2, so there's no need to try and port something over. Aside from the time commitment (1000's of man hours) and hundreds of thousands in licensing fees, the graphics of a game coming off the ps2 would be inferior to current standards.
