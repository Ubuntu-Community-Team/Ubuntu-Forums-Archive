### Post by steve.horsley on 2007-05-18
Three things you can do right now:

1) Donate money. 

2) Submit bugs. Yes it's been menitioned, but it is important. The more bugs that get submitted, the more problems can get fixed. And the more the developers can get a feel for which bugs are really causing the users grief - the same bug being reported many times is a good indicator.

3) Help out here. This forum has a well earned reputation for being friendly and helpful. And this is doing a lot to bring other people to try linux and to stick with it. However, there seems to be an endless supply of people asking the same questions over and over again. Now, in many forums they would be told firmly to RTFM. This forum seems to exel at answering the same questions over and over again without getting rude with the newbies, which is great, but people get bored and move on. We do need a constant supply of helpers to hand-hold the incoming newbies, with both help and encouragement. You can help out here that way even after only a couple of days on Linux. Answer the questions you can, and enjoy learning from the forums too.
