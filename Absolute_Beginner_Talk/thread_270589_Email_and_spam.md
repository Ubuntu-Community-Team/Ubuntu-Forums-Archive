### Post by seijuro on 2006-10-03
While it is true that if you have your email publicly posted ANYWHERE on the internet the spammers will find you. There are solutions that are simmilar to what you describe. I use [bluebottle]("http://www.bluebottle.com") email as my public email because they have a really great spam system that blocks ALL spam utilizing a block all less sender is in your allowed list or sender verifys they are not a spam bot by replying to the returned email asking to verify because they are not on your list. bluebottle also supports redirection so you can channel all of your pop3 accounts through it to weed out the spam for you. They provide both free and paid services I've been using the free service for a while now with no complaints.
