### Post by meng on 2006-10-17
When you boot up, there's some key you have to press quickly, it could be DEL, ESC, F1, F2, F12, it varies with different BIOSes. If you're lucky, it will flash up quickly on the screen. Anyway press this key to enter BIOS setup and look for Boot Sequence, and put the CD-ROM drive higher than the hard drive.

### Post by Tomosaur on 2006-10-17
Reboot your machine. Pay careful attention to the writing that comes up, it should tell you a key to press to access your BIOS (it may say something like 'Press F2 to enter setup'). Once you've found it, there will be an option to alter the boot order. Put your CD drive above your hard drive, save, and exit. The CD should now boot.

Failing that, it is likely you burned the cd wrong. Remember, you have to burn the CD as an image. The CD should have a whole load of files on it, not the one you downloaded i.e Say you downloaded ubuntu_desktop_dapper.iso. When you burn it as an image, the finished CD will NOT have ubuntu_desktop_dapper on it, but instead will have a whole bunch of files.
