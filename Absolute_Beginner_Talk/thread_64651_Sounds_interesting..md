### Post by Nequeo on 2005-09-11
Hi there,

If you're asking about the free shipping option - can I assume that you do not have broadband internet access? 

If so... then depending on your needs, Ubuntu may not be the right distribution. Certainly, there's no harm in trying it. Every 'cd' you order actually includes both a live CD and an Install CD, so you can always try it out without installing it.

As a side note, Canonical has stopped shipping Ubuntu 5.04 CDs pending the October release of Ubuntu 5.10 (which is looking *very* nice already). So even if you order now, you probably won't get anything until late November/December. 
 
Anyway, with no internet, or slow internet, your Ubuntu experience may be somewhat crippled, I am afraid. It is only a one CD distro, so a lot of the 'extra' software needs to be downloaded. One of the joys of Debian based Distro's like Ubuntu is 'apt-get', and its graphical front-end, synaptic. It lets you browse the thousands and thousands of programs available for Ubuntu, select the ones you want to install, and then the click of a button will download the programs and any dependancies, then install and configure everything for you.

Moreover, due to legal and philosphical reasons, Ubuntu does not have the ability to play proprietary formats such as DVDs, MP3s, Divx, etc. 'out of the box'. You will need to apt-get a few programs in order to play them. This is a very simple task, assuming, of course, you have access to the Internet. If you don't - and playing music/videos is a high priority, you might want to consider one of the other Debian distros that come with proprietary media support pre-installed. I hear good things about Mepis and PCLinuxOS, but you would probably need to buy CDs from Linuxcd.org

Anyway, hope this helps you make an informed decision. 

Cheers,
