### Post by nalmeth on 2006-05-25
You're not very likely to find anyone with that model camera who can tell you precisely the compatibility you'll get here in the forums. 

If there are difficulties (doesn't work), you might find that information on wiki.ubuntu.com or on a linux hardware compatibility site somewhere. There are many.

Commonly, the camera's memory are setup as mass storage devices, and will mount on your desktop automatically, but I doubt this is %100 probability. 

Take a good look around, I wouldn't want to see you go out and buy this, later realizing that it won't work.

At times, Sony has been found to be less than linux friendly...

In Gnome it's Nautilus
In KDE it's Konqueror

You can configure the default behavior when plugging in devices. I know that in Gnome you go to System --> Preferences --> Removable Drives and Media
