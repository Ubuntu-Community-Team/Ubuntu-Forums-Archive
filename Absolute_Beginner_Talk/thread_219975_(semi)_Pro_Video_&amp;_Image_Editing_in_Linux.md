### Post by Ben.McKenzie on 2006-07-21
For "semi" video editing, Diva, PiTiKi, and others would work.

For something more pro, Cinelerra is your best bet.  Also check out Jahshaka.

I get the feeling that people automatically say "Adobe" more as a reaction to them being around in the industry rather than for being the best option when it comes to Linux.

If you really want to go professional, there's Shake for Linux (not well advertised by Apple, but it's still around), Pirahna, and a slew of programs that have been ported over to Linux. A good place to start would be [http://linuxmovies.org/software.html](http://linuxmovies.org/software.html) which has a listing of both commercial and FOSS software available.
