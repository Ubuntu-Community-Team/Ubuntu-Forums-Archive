### Post by p_quarles on 2007-10-27
If you're talking about backing up DVDs to another disk, you'll want a shrinking utility. Both dvd95 and k9copy are very good, and will resize a commercial disk to fit on a normal single-layer DVD-/+R. These programs produce .iso files, which you can then burn to DVD using either Gnomebaker or K3b (two other very good applications). There's also Brasero for this, which some people prefer. 

For ripping DVDs to your hard drive in a readable format, there's stuff like DVD::rip. I don't do this, however, since I find that DVD-Rs are cheaper than hard drive space.
