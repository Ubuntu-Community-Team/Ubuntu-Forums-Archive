### Post by taurus on 2006-02-25
I guess it depends on what brand you like and how much you can afford.  Personally, I would go with AMD XP 3000+ or above (unless you want to go with AMD64).  You should try to get at least 512MB of DDR RAM but 1GB is even better.  For mobo, I always get ASUS (already built two computers with ASUS mobos and both are working great) and the new ones should have onboard NIC and audio.  I personally can't stand the onboard audio so I got myself a Creative Lab Soundblaster 24bit PCI card.  You can get a nice XFX nVidia video card for less than $50 and I like NEC DVD-RW/CD-RW and the last time I check, it's $38.95...  I believe Maxtor EIDE 160GB goes for less than $100 or you can go with SATA if you want.  Seagate gives you 5 years on their SATA harddrives so you can't go wrong with that.  Okay, you can get a Seagate SATA 250GB for less than $100.00!  \\:D/

### Post by procras on 2006-02-25
Mainboard:
Socket 939 unless you want to wait for AM2 
Nforce 4 chipset seems to work okay for most
See if you want SLI (support not there in Linux) or not 
?Asus A8N series, or Abit with uGURU clocks to see temps etc
SATA II on most modern boards
Do you need firewire connector?
Most on board Ethernet supported
If concerned re noise, look out for noisy northbridge fans. Consider Heatsink instead such as Zalmans

Processor:
Consider Dual Cores, although there may be problems with some software with the SMP kernels
Athlon x2 3700 rumour has it overclocks well.
If rich Athlon x2 4400
If really really rich FX-60

Case:
Noise an issue? Do you want an integrated PSU?
If you have a choice the 120 mm fans are quieter than smaller ones

HD:
Samsung spinpoint P120S

Noise reduction:
You might want to consider
Zalman Reserator plus 1 water cooling
