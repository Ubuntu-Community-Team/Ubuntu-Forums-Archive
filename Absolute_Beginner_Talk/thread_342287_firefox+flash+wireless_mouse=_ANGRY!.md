### Post by Spr0k3t on 2007-01-19
I've seen this before, but it did not have anything to do with Linux, instead it was tied to the wireless desktop components.  Amazingly enough, it was the Logitech DiNovo wireless set that would do such freakish things.  At first I thought the software was making it go mad since the key presses would be at random and the mouse would cease to function, that is until I tried the same components on a Mac PowerBook with OSX.  It turned out to the be the radio frequencies of the device interfering with other products around the same area.  Once the wireless devices were removed from the area, they worked like a charm.

Something you can try, get in touch with the manufacturer of the devices and see if you can find a way to swap out the components like for like.  That's what we did... took three sets of the keyboard and mouse before either of them worked flawlessly.  We have five of the DiNovo desktops in the area.  I love the style and feel they have, I just hate how poorly they were designed for the environment.

### Post by schwascore on 2007-01-19
I had a similar problem once and you may not believe what happened...

I also had a Logitech wireless mouse + keyboard and when using linux (at that time Fedora Core), the two devices would switch.  That is, when I moved the mouse, random things were typed and nothing happened when I typed from the keyboard.  The only thing I could figure out was that for some reason the wireless USB receiver thought that my keyboard was my mouse and  my mouse was my keyboard.  you can get the same results by swapping PS/2 ports with a wired mouse and keyboard.

It's just a theory for your situation, but that's all I can suggest from my experience.

EDIT:  The post above seems to suggest the same problem! I'm not alone!
