### Post by dokdoom on 2008-03-10
Which one is better depends on a lot of things (I know it's not a great answer) I use XFCE and like it very much. It's very lightweight when compared to Gnome (ubuntu) and KDE (Kubuntu) but your machine should handle any OS you throw at it. (Pardon my mac-fu as it is week, I'm assuming that's a decent machine you got there.)

Regular ubuntu comes with a lot of stuff out of the box and it also just works. Not everything but a lot of stuff that requires installation and setup comes with it already. I like XFCE because it's pretty bare and everything I want I can just apt-get install. You can do it with the GUI as well but I'm not to familiar with it. 

To get back to your main question, ubuntu: comes with a lot of stuff and to me is very clunky. Has a lot of support and documentation online.

xfce: lightweight, will bring life back to your older machines but if your have a decent machine you would be better off with...

kde (kubuntu) I would say this is the "sexy" version of ubuntu. Everything looks very nice out of the box and while it does come with a lot of junk like gnome it does look very nice and a couple visits to kdelook.org your desktop will look even nicer. 

There are other versions of ubuntu but these three I would consider the "main" versions. I could be sorely mistaken but my knowledge of ubuntu ends with gnome, kde and xfce.

Hope this helps some!

### Post by neurostu on 2008-03-10
Everything that was said above is true.

I would hesitate to say that KDE is the sexier then gnome. I really like gnome and feel like it can be made very "sexy"

If I were you I would install Xubuntu and then if you want more you can always run
```

sudo apt-get install gnome-desktop

```
which will essentially upgrade Xubuntu to Ubuntu and if you find you are not getting the performance you want you can switch back to XFCE with your session manager.
