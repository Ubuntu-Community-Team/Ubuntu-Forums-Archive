### Post by seventhc on 2008-02-24
A sticky is just that, it sticks, while a normal thread will move depending on replies.
So a sticky will alway be in the section you see it in, while the new threads are always changing. If a thread doesn't get a response right away then the newer threads will be put up over it. Once a thread gets a reply it goes back to the top of the list.
The stickies usually have information and it isn't where you would ask questions, but it is a place you will find helpful information and helpful links.

### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-02-24
At the top left of your post to any thread is your own user ID.  Click on it and you get a drop-down menu.  One of the options is 'Find all posts by <userID>'  (yourself).  Click that one.  It displays a list with the title of your opening post, and the opening words of that post.

Your most recent post will be at the top of this list.  The list is constantly updated with such info as how long ago the post was made, how many replies there have been, and how many views.

This list obtained from here, as opposed to the listing obtained from the Search menu, contains your **complete** posting history..  If you make numerous posts the list gets to consist of multiple pages, just like the Forum index pages.

I find it convenient to open a tab in Firefox where I keep one of my posts displayed all the time so that I can gain ready access to click on my userID to get to this list any time I want to check on replies or views.  Clicking on any title will take you direct to that thread.

Bookmarking the thread will also get you to it quickly, but you have to click and scroll to find out fresh replies, and it does not give you info on the number of views.  And bookmarking is not automatic.

You can also use this technique to view the posting history of any other user.
