are you dependant on the tools themselves or on their functionality?

you cannot expect to have the same program on different operating systems.
linux is not a better windows. linux is linux.

there are more or less good substitutes, sometimes better substitutes for (most of) the programs you mentioned.

irfanview was e.g. one of the programs i missed most when i switched.
try gqview for just viewing or f-spot for managing a collection.

you will surely find it awkward to the different gui and the different handling of each of the programs to the ones you are used to. but mostly there is a solution to everything unless you insist of a 100% copy of the program you are used to.

some other substitutes:
burning programs:
k3b (probably the most completle one)
bonfire
nautilus cd creator

mediaplayers:
totem 
xine
xmms
mplayer
amarok
rhythmbox
some are suited for audio only though.
