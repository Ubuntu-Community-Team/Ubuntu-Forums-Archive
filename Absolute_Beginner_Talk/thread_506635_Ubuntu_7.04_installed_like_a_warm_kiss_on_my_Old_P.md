I tend to agree.  So far I have installed Ubuntu on a 64 bit Athlon (64 bit version) a Centrino Dell laptop, an old Celeron system of dubious extraction and minimal hardware resources, and two fairly modern Pentium desktops.  Three installs were trouble free with zero issues.  Two involved some troubleshooting - mainly oddball hardware issues - all resolved in reasonable time by searching for solutions on this forum.  I am proficient in Windows (but never use it any more), and I have little or no experience with Linux, but suggest that if I can do it on such a diverse number and types of computer, it is certainly getting there in terms of "it just works". And even with the troubleshooting that 2 installs required, the overall time spent getting 5 systems up and running was about half of what it would have taken to do it in Windows.

Having said that, I also understand that it is not perfect for all users, because it depends on your needs (gamers for example may feel it unsuitable), your expectations (if you expect it to be Windows but better you are in for a disappointment - I think it is FAR better but definitely NOT windows), and the suitability of your hardware (most of which is fixable, but will cost you time, effort and patience).
  
But if you come in with your eyes open and your expectations realistic, I doubt you will be disappointed, and once it is fully set up it becomes almost boring in its stability, speed and reliability (and I find the need to try things that I know may cause problems just for the fun of learning something new).  I still have XP on my desktop, but only until I have transferred all my data.  After three months with Ubuntu I will NEVER go back.  Thank you all to those who contribute to this forum, because it is YOU that makes this more than just another OS.
