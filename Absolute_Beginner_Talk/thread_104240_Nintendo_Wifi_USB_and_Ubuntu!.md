Seems like quite a few steps, anyway this comment was interesting in regard to an alternative:
[I]
Configurable (part of the downfall of the Nintendo Wifi adapter is its lack of options for configuration)

-Don't have to replace/configure existing network.

-Better range than the Nintendo stick by approximately 10-15 feet

-Useful for more than just DS wifi games

-Fairly inexpensive (currently $30)
I used the Asus WL-167g, it's a USB 2.0 802.11g/b adapter with WEP/WPA compliance. It uses a Ralink chipset (for those that care, the same chipset used for the "Modified" drivers for Wifime). Generic drivers are available for Mac, all Windows versions, and Linux. It comes with the adapter, a universal extension (for pointing it any-which-way), a USB extension (in case you have to plug in the back of your machine), driver CD and nifty Asus lanyard (yay!).

[/I]
