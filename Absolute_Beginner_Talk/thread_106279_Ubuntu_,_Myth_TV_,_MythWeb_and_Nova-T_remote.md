### Post by mattybain on 2005-12-20
Having finally got sick of Windows Media Center, its tendency to lose TV guides and its insistence that it can't change from one computer to another without purchasing a new version (I updated my hardware) I attempted to install Ubuntu and MythTV.

This is not the first time that I have tried to use Linux (previously tried knoppix, Mandrake and SuSE). Although I am relatively experienced with computers and Windows (I build enough of them) I have never got very far before getting bored, mainly due to driver problems and the very steep learning curve. To be fair I never really gave them much of a chance before giving up.

However all credit to Ubuntu I have managed to get most of my peripherals working (including a Nova - T graphics card). I have also managed to get MythTV working to a point where I can watch TV , with proper channel icons and program guide. It did take about 12 hours of bashing and I have considerably less hair than I did but it works.

It has convinced me that this is the way forward as the quality is fantastic and runs super quick on my system. I also love the ability to rewind and pause live TV.

However it doesn’t connect, is there some trick or firewall setting that I need to change?

2) I have a problem that a few of the buttons on my Nova-T remote are not being recognised. The annoying thing is that this is the direction keys which makes navigating with the remote almost impossible. I found this site which claims to solve this [http://mythtv.info/moin.cgi/HardWare_2fRemoteControls_2fNovaT](http://mythtv.info/moin.cgi/HardWare_2fRemoteControls_2fNovaT) but it doesn’t make any sense to me. Could somebody translate it into simple steps that I can follow.

My main problem was that although I can find the drivers/media/common/ir-common file it is a .ko format and I have no way of changing it to what is being suggested.

3) Is there any way to get MythWeb to interact with MythTV i.e. to change channels on MythTV with MythWeb, this would be cool!!

Overall I am very impressed with Ubuntu, if I could get it to work with my Connexant Wifi card then I would think about installing it on all my computers and just have one dual booting to play games!!
