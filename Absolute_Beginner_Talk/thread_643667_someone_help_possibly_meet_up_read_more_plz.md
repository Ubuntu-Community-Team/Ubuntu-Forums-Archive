### Post by niteshifter on 2007-12-18
Start with Python and this book "How to Think Like a (Python) Programmer" which you can download the PDF from [http://www.greenteapress.com/thinkpython/]("http://www.greenteapress.com/thinkpython/")
 for free.

Set a study schedule and stick to it, of X hours per week. Work out the examples - each and every one - given in that book. When you get stuck with Python ask your question in the Development & Programming forum here (ubuntuforums.org) and-or in the Usenet group comp.lang.python - when you get stuck on terminology use Google (or the search engine of your choice).

There are two broad aspects of programming: learning the (a) language(s) is the minor part. The major part is achieving the mental discipline - learning to think like a programmer. That means learning to think analytically, to break a problem down into it's various parts and managing all those parts. The above book, Python and practice will get you a good start at that important part.
