### Post by Shinobi326 on 2007-06-28
I'm having flashbacks to a recent experience I had with this new house.  We're out in a more rural area but we are serviced by Comcast Cable Modem.  Over a few months after being in my house I kept having real difficulties in maintaining a connection to Comcast whenever I was doing any kind of uploads (torrents, pics, VPN to work, etc.).  I suspected that maybe Comcast was doing some sort of upload blocking so I kept calling to complain but they said they absolutely do not do any limiting and so my problem continued.

One day I was trying to work from home via VPN and it reached a point where I could not maintain a connection anymore.  I called Comcast and scheduled a tech to come out to the house.  He checked the lines in the house and to my router and there wasn't any problem.  He then checked outside from the mainbox to my house and he noticed a huge DB loss.  He had to run a new line from the mainbox to my house and ALL IS RIGHT WITH THE WORLD AGAIN!

In a nutshell, the service tech said if you start to get any interference in your cable modem or DSL line the frequency the data travels at is in an upper band which gets affected first by interference.  Namely, upload is really dependent upon good clean upper frequency while download frequency can tolerate a little more noise or DB loss.

SO I would recommend getting your lines checked by a service tech.  Especially get your outside lines to the mainbox checked to make sure you're not having too much DB signal loss which will affect your connectivity and upload first.
