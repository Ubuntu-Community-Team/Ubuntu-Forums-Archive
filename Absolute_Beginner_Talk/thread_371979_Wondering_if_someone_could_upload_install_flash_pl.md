Just a friendly word of caution:  The Ubuntu package regime exists (in part) to protect you from corrupted or maliciously security-compromised software, and in your shoes I'd want to be quite careful about going outside that system, and slow to abandon its protection.  (For example, I hope other people on this forum are benign, but you don't know that.  Installing a tarball that purports to be Adobe's Flash v. 9 just because someone you don't even know uploaded it here is inherently a bit risky.)

Fixes to the current md5sum-matching problem in Ubuntu package flashplugin-nonfree seem to be about to reach "main".  Maybe the better thing to do is to wait for them?

I have a few other thoughts about package policy and security on my local LUG mailing list:  [http://linuxmafia.com/pipermail/conspire/2007-December/003638.html](http://linuxmafia.com/pipermail/conspire/2007-December/003638.html)
