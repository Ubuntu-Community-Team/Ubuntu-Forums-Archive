### Post by markofealing on 2007-06-11
I believe it the card used the Broadcom 43xx chipset, if you want to then follow this guide [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=457371]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=457371") for cards with this chipset using ndiswrapper. 

Alternatively, for a few £s get a Intel Pro Wireless 2200BG card off eBay like [http://cgi.ebay.co.uk/INTEL-PRO-2200BG-Wireless-Card-For-use-with-Notebooks_W0QQitemZ250125246379QQihZ015QQcategoryZ74954QQssPageNameZWDVWQQrdZ1QQcmdZViewItem](http://cgi.ebay.co.uk/INTEL-PRO-2200BG-Wireless-Card-For-use-with-Notebooks_W0QQitemZ250125246379QQihZ015QQcategoryZ74954QQssPageNameZWDVWQQrdZ1QQcmdZViewItem). I assume the D600 has a mini-PCI slot, most laptops do for their wireless cards.

It works out of the box with no configuration. I used this on my Latitude C640, perfect connection every time. I use the ndiswrapper solution for the Broadcom 43xx chipset on a Dell desktop with a Belkin card, it also works well if you carefully follow the instructions.

Good luck!
