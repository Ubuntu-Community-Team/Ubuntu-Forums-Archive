### Post by rsambuca on 2007-08-31
If you research on the gentoo forums, most of the experts there do not recommend the gentoo live cd.  Most of them suggest doing a stage3 tarball installation, which is what I tried.  You can use your existing ubuntu to install gentoo into another partition so you don't have any computer down-time, and more importantly (at least for me), full internet access so you can follow the guide.  It was actually much easier than I expected, and everything is working well for me so far.

### Post by daveshields on 2007-09-01
Gentoo is not for the "faint of heart." You only want to go there to dig into the technical details of Linux.

Gentoo aims to deliver optimum performance by compiling everything on your particular computer. Sounds nice, but most modern machines are so fast that it's not worth the effort.

However, the Gentoo documentation is very good, and you can learn a lot, but be warned that the community there is not nearly as congenial as Ubuntu's. For example, Gentoo's founder, Daniel Robbins, tried to re-join the project earlier this year only to be thrown off mail lists and such within days, prompting him to resign from the project.

Stick with Ubuntu. You'll have  more fun, and more company, on your journey learning about Linux.
