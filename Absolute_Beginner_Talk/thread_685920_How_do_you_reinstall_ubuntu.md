### Post by DamagePlan on 2008-02-02
Pop the live cd in your cd drive. Reboot from the disk and go through the installation process again. When you get to the partition section, install over your current ubuntu partition.

### Post by randy78 on 2008-02-02
I'm not sure why you want to re-install, because most ANYTHING can be fixed with some work, but you can just pop in your live-cd, go to Menu>System>Administration>Partition Editor and right click on the appropriate partition and select delete, then press APPLY.

Then, just hit the install icon on your desktop again and you'll be set.

### Post by dondad on 2008-02-02
If you have not already done so and you are going to reinstall anyway, you might want to think about setting up a separate partition for your /home directory. That way, you can reinstall or upgrade without losing your data. If you have not done this already, make sure you backup anything you need because it will be lost when you do the install.
