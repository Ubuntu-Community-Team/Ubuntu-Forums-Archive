Actually this is a common misconception. KDE is less computer intensive than Gnome, despite Gnome being regarded as the "lightweight" desktop. Here is some detailed comparisons. 

[http://ktown.kde.org/~seli/memory/desktop_benchmark.html](http://ktown.kde.org/~seli/memory/desktop_benchmark.html)

But, either way, with 256mb or RAM it would be best to try out Xubuntu. Although XFCE actually doesn't fare much better than KDE... and it's actually worse when your having to call up the libraries from KDE/Gnome to run their native applications under the XFCE environment.
