### Post by p_quarles on 2007-12-11
I don't see why you'd want to uninstall Gnome if you're just trying out KDE. It's entirely possible you won't like it, after all. You can set KDE as your default session without uninstalling Gnome.

I have both Gnome and KDE installed, but my main desktop is Fluxbox. There's only one advantage to purging those other packages, and that's hard drive space -- and none of them take a big chunk out of a modern hard drive.
