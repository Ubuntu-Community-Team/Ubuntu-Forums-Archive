### Post by ugm6hr on 2007-11-19
> **Rmc22 said:**
> I'm interested in the many comments re old, low-spec PCs running happily with Linux.  I know it's fashionable to knock Windows, but is it the case that the rapid obsolescence of PC hardware is more a reflection of ever-growing Windows demands?  The specs you and I describe would hardly even load, never mind run, Vista etc.

Would you try sticking a spare HDD in it for the experimental loading of Ubuntu, or creating a dual boot HDD?  I'd just like to know I have something workable before erasing by existing OS/setup, bad as it is.

Increasing Hardware requirements as a result of consumers liking flashy graphics for their Desktop.  Have a look at the eye-candy in Windows / OSX and also Ubuntu (if hardware allows).  The important thing is that the **minimum** requirements for Ubuntu change very little (and for other linuxes - even less), while high-end machines get the extra features, whereas Vista has left a few generations of computer behind.  Remember that your setup could probably run XP without too much difficulty....

As for extra HD or not.... If you have a blank HD with about 10GB (I think minimum is around 4GB) - go with that.  If not - just use GParted LiveCD (google it) to resize your existing partitions to leave 10GB unused - and then use the "Use largest continuous free space" option when installing Ubuntu (it will make sense when you install).  Remember that resizing partitions is never 100% safe for data integrity (i.e it may mess up Windows anyway) - but I've never had a problem myself.

Have fun experimenting.
