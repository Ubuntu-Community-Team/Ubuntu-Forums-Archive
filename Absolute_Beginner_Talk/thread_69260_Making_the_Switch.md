### Post by jimcooncat on 2005-09-26
I know this might be a radical suggestion to some, but I'd seriously consider getting a second computer and load Ubuntu on it. You could start off with a KVM switch between them (keyboard, video, mouse), and then add remote-control software (like FreeNX and VNC) as you get more experienced.

If computing is your bread and butter, you should have more than one system in your shop as a matter of job security. And Ubuntu can provide you with some big perks, like an Apache (local-network-only) install for checking out drafts with a real web server, and monit to keep you informed of the status of client websites.

I think that the graphics programs available on Ubuntu that could probably give you all you need for web design, but you will need time to get used to them. Making a radical switch is fine and fun for home systems, but not when your next paycheck's riding on it.

With a little motivation, you'll be able to wean at least the work stuff off the Windows computer, anyway.
