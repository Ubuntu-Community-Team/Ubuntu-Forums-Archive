### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-09-22
The Add and Remove Programs tool allows for painless installation and uninstallation of commonly used software. Synaptic (and other tools like Adept, apt-get, and aptitude) is made for more advanced users, allowing for fine-grained control and selection of more software packages.

### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-09-22
> **comomolo said:**
> This is something that has been around in my mind almost since the beginning. Aren't these two ways of installing basically the same things? Why are both needed?

Add/Remove is basically a slimmed-down, simplified Synaptic.  It lets users install/remove software without giving them one-click access to things like repos that they could use to really, seriously break their systems.
