[/quote] As I mentioned above, the only downside is the integration with the rest of the desktop. They will look different, and may not be able to communicate with other KDE (in your case) apps as would native programs (e.g. konqueror). If you tend to use more GNOME apps under KDE, why not switch over to GNOME and use the fewer KDE apps there? It would make more sense.

They all do the job in a different way, or approach the task from a different angle. They may be just experience gaining practise for the programmer. It's up to you to choose the right tool for yourself, and if you don't find one exactly as you wished for, you have the freedom to alter/extent/customise it.

Talent and resources do not actually get divided. Design or implementation ideas in one free software program get re-used/affect another. Besides in free software world there is so much talent in need to get expressed that there is no problem in having similar apps for one task. Besides, pluralism and choise is a companion to freedom.
