### Post by Edward The Bonobo on 2006-03-31
I recently switched from '98 to Ubuntu on an even lower-spec machine...PII 333MHz, 64MB.

Gnome was unusable (surprise!).  Somthing like OO would take about 15 minutes to open.

I switched to Xubuntu.  Things started to become...tolerable.  At least I could now work my iPod, under gtkpod (iTunes incompatibility with '98 was a big reason for switching).  But I got hooked on Linux and wanted it to work better.

A couple of weeks ago, I bought 256MB RAM off eBay (£15UK pre-owned).  Now I'm a lot happier.  I can run Gnome no problem.  I'm still hampered by my processor speed, of course - especially for things like CD Ripping.  It spends most of its time running at full tilt.  Sometimes I switch back to xfce and it's marginally better.  One big inconvenience, though...gtkpod takes a loooong time to display my iPod database.

I'd seriously consider a memory upgrade.  It's not remotely tricky.  Even I can do it!  And there's lots of 'How To..' pages on the www.  It's a matter of working out what type will be compatible with your machine.  If you don't know what make your Motherboard is, go to [www.wimsbios.com](www.wimsbios.com) and it will tell you how to find out.  Then go to Crucial.com to find out what kind of RAM card you need.  Then - if short of cash - check out eBay. The only tricky part for me was applying enough pressure to click the card into place.

Oh...but I did have to remove my HDs because they were blocking access to the RAM slot.  And then the card was too big for them to slot back in.  Cut to the chase...I ended up securing them with a cunning arrangement of duck tape.
