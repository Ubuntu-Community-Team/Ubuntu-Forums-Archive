### Post by thelatinist on 2007-12-22
> **zmike1 said:**
> With beginner apologies, what do you mean by "blend nicely?"   Maybe I don't have any fashion sense and don't notice when things blend or not...Don't apps just open in a new window?

No, it's okay.  What I mean is that by default KDE apps will have completely different color schemes and icons than your native Gnome apps (gray and blue vs. brown and orange).  For many people, it is a rather jarring difference that keeps them from installing KDE apps on Gnome.  As I say, though, it needn't be since KDE apps can be themed to match Ubuntu well.

And no need to install KDE separately.  Just install the application you want and all the KDE libraries and dependencies you need will be installed.
