### Post by regomodo on 2007-06-14
ok, first backup all your important data. Make sure you have an XP install disc just in case everything goes tits up. 

Now, read on how to "Dual Boot Ubuntu and XP" and understand what it says properly. If you follow the steps here and in a dual boot guide you should not have any problems.

Also, whatever you do, do not resize your XP partition. Although you can i've just found it too unreliable with too many breakages. You are better off using a new separate hard disk.

BTW, what you are referring to is normal. It will not tell you it's XP or whatnot. Just know your partitions and leave XP well alone. The XP identification will be done with GRUB automatically.
