### Post by DarkN00b on 2006-11-13
Assuming you have written and legal (read:notarized) consent from the company that produced the DVD then you are alright.

The DMCA makes it illegal to decrypt a DVD without the owner's consent in the USA. That's the stupid thing about this law: you can make a copy of a DVD as long as you don't break the CSS encryption on the disc. :confused: 

Also, it is okay to use software like Cyberlink's PowerDVD which decrypts the disc so it can be played, but you can't use LibDVDCSS to do the same thing. What is the difference? One costs money and the other doesn't.

Some laws just suck.
