### Post by orkim on 2007-03-14
Is this something that just started happening?  It may be a problem with the driver, but more likely its a problem with the printer.

Can you reproduce the error in Windows?  Can you attempt to print the exact same document (Open Office perhaps) in Windows that you did in Linux?

Typically these older printers which are fed by rollers tend to wear out.  There is actually a clutch mechanism in the rollers that tell the printer how far it has rotated.  They tend to wear out overtime and cause "stretching" from side to side (exactly as you have described).

Now, this could be a problem with the driver (telling one roller to roll father than the other), but I don't even know that the printer will allow for that.  A simple command like "move up one line" is probably issued and the rollers work in conjunction.  There is typically no way to independently operate each roller (as that would be useless).  And, I would seriously doubt that a printer driver that has been stable for 7+  years would have a bug as serious as that in it.

Worse case, you might just have to reboot to windows to print.

I hope this might have informed you a bit and shed some light on the subject.  Let me know if there are more questions that I can answer.

-orkim
