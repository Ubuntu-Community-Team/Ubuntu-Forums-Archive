### Post by kwilliam on 2006-09-28
I use KDE for a few reasons. 
[LIST=1]
[*]I find KDE is more responsive: e.g. my mouse NEVER FREEZES! That's what had me hooked. It felt snappier running from the live CD than my hard disk installation of Ubuntu. 

[*] I like the default "looks" (e.g. shiny and blue) for Kubuntu much more than the default Ubuntu looks (e.g. flat and brown). Little things, like how the close button fades to red when you hover over it - the eye-candy just makes KDE a much nicer looking experience. (Although I've found customizing the theme and window decorations much easier in Gnome than in KDE.)

[*] I'm a customizer freak. I change *everything*. KDE has more options to change. A lot more options. I'm a very capable person, and Gnome sacrifices capability for as few options as possible.  For instance, compare Gnome and KDE's handeling of removable mass storage devices. On Gnome, your option is "Mount automatically" or not. On KDE, you get an XP style popup with customizable options based on the type of content (CD, flash drive), and you can define commands to run automatically when certain types of devices are inserted and what options to present when they're plugged in.

[*] Last and most importantly, I like KDE software better than it's Gnome counterparts. Konqueror is much easier to use than Nautilus, for instance, and I think Amarok is cooler than the Gnome music player (can't even remember what' it's called now). Also it has killer apps, like Yakuake, a console that drops down at the touch of F12, and Katapult, which lets you launch programs by typing Alt+Space and the first few letters of the program's name.
[/LIST]

These are just my opinions of course. Gnome and KDE both have advantages, and should both be developed further.
