### Post by Paulmd on 2007-11-23
> **badbadputer said:**
> i am trying to find info from someone who is or has used before, and despite the price, is it worth buying?


Economics:

At that low price: If it fails, you can part it out and recover most, if not all of your investment from the sale of good parts. Hard drive, ram and processor are very standard parts. Screens are less so, but having a cracked screen is a common enough thing, any number of people may want a compatible screen assembly. Plastics are another thing that people do want, from time to time.


You can't go all that wrong. 

Reliability:


Compaq in general: middle of the line quality. Don't expect it to live to be old (it might anyway, but don't expect it :) . Historically, on some models, (I'm thinking Armada e500)  dc jacks have been very fragile, and screens have had their backlights turn red, then die. 

Some of the ones that were populated with Durons (not the e500, which is an intel p3) , just up and die at some point. (dead MB/dead CPU) 

That model is too new for there to be a long term reliability baseline.
