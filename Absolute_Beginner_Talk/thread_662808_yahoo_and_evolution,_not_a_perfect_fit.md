### Post by Thelasko on 2008-01-09
It's been a while since I have setup my mail client but I know what you are talking about.  Yahoo! has all of the details about getting an email program working on their site.  If you log into your webmail account via your browser at [mail.yahoo.com]("https://login.yahoo.com/config/login_verify2?&.src=ym") and go to help at the right.  You will have to enter your screen name again and then search for "Email Settings  for POP and SMTP" it has all of the details on how to get evolution working.  The settings are the same for any client (Outlook/Evolution/Thunderbird).

I use AT&T for my internet so my settings are:
   1. Open your email client program.
   2. Locate the email account settings for your particular client.
   3. Change the POP server to pop.att.yahoo.com.
   4. Change the SMTP server to smtp.att.yahoo.com.
   5. Check the option labeled Use an encrypted connection (SSL) and change the SMTP port to 465.
   6. Check the option labeled Use an encrypted connection (SSL) and change the POP3 port to 995.
   7. Confirm the above settings before clicking OK.

However, keep in mind that yahoo may change their settings or have different settings for you if you are with a different ISP or have a free account so I recommend getting your information directly from Yahoo!

 If you have any further questions feel free to ask.
