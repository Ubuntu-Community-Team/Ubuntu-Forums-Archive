### Post by whitewizardcoder on 2007-05-11
I currently have an hp pavilion dv5000 laptop and I have to say I am pleased with Ubuntu's performance on it.  Almost all of the hardware works (except for some of the media card slots), and I was able to get it with an nVidia graphics card, which worked really well when I installed the proprietary driver.  Wireless and sound work out of the box, battery life is around 4-5 hours, and it runs really fast.

I'm sure either way you went (Alienware or HP), you would find Ubuntu to be compatable.  A word of advice though: avoid ati graphics cards as their driver does not yet support eye-candy like compiz or beryl.
