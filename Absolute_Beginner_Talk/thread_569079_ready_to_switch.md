### Post by mivo on 2007-10-06
I would do a 10 GB partition for /, 1 GB for swap and the rest for /home. Read up some on partitioning schemes/layouts. Getting this right will save you much more time later on if/when you reinstall Ubuntu. If you go with the guided installation, you will get one large partition for everything. This means loss of settings and personal files if you ever reinstall Linux. It is not harder or more difficult, just grasping the different concept may take a bit of reading. :) (But don't feel overwhelmed or discouraged, or start to worry about making mistakes -- we can walk you through this, you only have to ask here.)
