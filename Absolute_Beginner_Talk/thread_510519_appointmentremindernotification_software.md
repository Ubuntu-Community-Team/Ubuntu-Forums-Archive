### Post by Herman on 2007-07-29
Hello rhendrix9,
You should look into ways to use crontab. :)

I use crontab to make my computer into an alarm clock to wake me up each morning and even it turns the kettle on for me a couple of minutes beforehand too. Here's a how-to I wrote about it, it's written for beginners so it takes you through it step by step, [Alarm clock]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p8.html#alarm_clock") (set your computer to wake you up in the morning).

I also use crontab to automatically back up my email and other evolution files like addressbook and calendar and so on. I made another how to about that, and it shows the recommended way to use crontab. Once you've tried it the simple way, you should be able to learn the proper way, here are the links for that, [Make a backup script]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p13.htm#Make_a_backup_script") and [Configure 'crontab]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p13.htm#Use_crontab"). Actually, if you want you can start at the top of that page and work down, you might find t easier that way.

You can make your computer do just about anything with crontab, it's a very useful program. You can set it to do something just once a year if you want, or every minute. But for loner term reminders I normally just use Evolution. If you look in 'Evolution', you'll find a calendar, and you can set tasks and memos in it with reminders. They don't make any noise and they aren't too big, but they are good enough for some purposes. Those are good too, you should experiment with those too. I use them.

I hope I'm being helpful,
Regards, Herman :)
