### Post by Pikestaff on 2007-03-01
I would recommend getting the LiveCD for both (if you're new) and trying them both out and deciding which one you like.  You can always switch desktop environments pretty easily if you change your mind later.

You might also take a look at this page which I found to be quite handy: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/kdegnome)  It gives a general comparison of KDE and Gnome.  KDE is what Kubuntu uses, while Gnome is what Ubuntu uses.
