### Post by Stunt Double on 2006-12-11
When I purchased my linux CDs, I bought a Ubuntu and a Kubuntu, both 6.10. After installing and using the Ubuntu for a while, I loaded the Kubuntu CD onto the second 1/2 of my hard drive.
   The Ubuntu does everything I want it to and I prefer it to the Kubuntu. How do I remove Kubuntu short of doing a reformat and clean install of Ubuntu.
  Thanks

---

### Post by igknighted on 2006-12-11
You could simply reformat the kubuntu drive and use it as storage.  In fact, as long as there is no swap partition in between the two partitions, you could use he liveCD to remove the kubuntu partition and then expand the Ubuntu one into that space.
