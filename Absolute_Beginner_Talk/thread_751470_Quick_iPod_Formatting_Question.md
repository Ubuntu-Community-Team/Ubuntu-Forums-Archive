### Post by bradwilliamson on 2008-04-10
I am still one of the weird people who buy CD's, and I've honestly never even seen the iTunes Store. I had my 1st gen Nano for about a week and decided that iTunes was an enormous pile o' poo. It wouldn't sync specific songs to the nano, instead "Surprising" me with a smattering of random tracks that it thought I would like. Not fun with audiobooks. 

I wiped the nano, installed Rockbox on it and haven't had a lick of problems with it since. Note that I had NO iTunes tracks to preserve, so be careful if you try this!

You just plug it in, Ubuntu mounts it as a disk, Amarok sees it and off you go.

YMMV, and I admit to being old school, but Rockbox is so very cool and looks absolutely killer compared to the old nano software. The new nano's are cooler, I must say, but they are still tied to iTunes. Ick.
