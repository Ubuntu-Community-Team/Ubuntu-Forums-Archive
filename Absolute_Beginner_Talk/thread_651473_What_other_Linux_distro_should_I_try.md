### Post by Majorix on 2007-12-27
I would go with Slackware. With no default package managers, it is really a challenge to get things to "just work" under Slackware. Also, it is really stable.

You could also try Debian but it is too much like Ubuntu, so you won't be really learning much.

Finally I wouldn't suggest Gentoo, as it has hard time with hardware. Even if it is your 3rd OS on the PC, it will still be annoying when you have to edit your xorg.conf all the time just to make something work.
