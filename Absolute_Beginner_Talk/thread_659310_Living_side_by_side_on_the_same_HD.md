Hello everyone,

I recently installed Ubuntu 7.10 on a Compaq Laptop already running Xp Pro. Windows has a partition on 30gb (15gb free) with 2048mb RAM. The Ubuntu installation has a 10gb partition.

The installation was a joy and Ubuntu did a very good job of detecting all my hardware. I was able to access my local  network and browse the net via a wifi card and wireless broadband modem.

I did not have any problems at boot-up either and the GRUB boot loader did a very good job.

However, as the Romans would say " in cauda venenum " a sting in the tail in the WinXP now became an absolute slouch and I found it almost impossible to do anything on the laptop. I mostly use this machine for downloading image files from a Nikon D70 and use CS2 as the image editor. It was almost impossible to manipulate even the smallest files (3mb) in CS and eventually I sadly had to remove the Ubuntu installation.

After a slight cardiac event fixing the MBR, WinXp is the lone user of the HD and is performing very well, as before. It would seem that WinXp took umbrage at another OS on the HD and sulked until the invader was removed.

I was very impressed with Ubuntu and its installed software, especially OpenOffice, I don't have a spare PC or laptop to install it by itself. Has anybody ant thoughts on the matter or has anyone experienced similar problems with a dual installation.
