### Post by mcduck on 2007-05-08
No, there's no Shockwave for Linux. Sen e-mail to Adobe and complain about it.

To clear cache, history etc.in Firefox  just go to Tools/Clear Private Data ;)

For other cleaning, you may wish to run 'sudo apt-get clean' sometimes to remove apt's cached .deb packages. Other than that, there really isn't anything worth cleaning.

### Post by Ozeuss on 2007-05-08
regarding ccleaner- most of its necessary functions are useless in linux, since there's no registry. you can clean your cookies and other data straight out of your browser. the cache is stored in a hidden folder in your home directory.
if you want to clear residual packages from installing software, you can look [here]("http://www.ubuntugeek.com/cleaning-up-all-unnecessary-junk-files-in-ubuntu.html")
