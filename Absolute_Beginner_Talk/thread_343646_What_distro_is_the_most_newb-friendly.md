### Post by noalternative on 2007-01-23
Depends on a number of factors.

1)Your computer.  If it is a computer over ten years old which hasn't been upgraded you might be happier with Feather, Damn Small Linux or Ubuntu Light.  If it is older but you have kept the hardrive and memory upgraded, Puppy or Xubuntu would be great.  If it is brand spanking new, than Suse, Ubuntu, Kubuntu, Mandriva or Mepis.

2)How comfortable you are leaving the windows learning curve.  If you are not I would suggest Mepis.

3)A Helpful community. Ubuntu is tops IMHO.  Mepis is almost as good, it basically suffers for being a little smaller.  Mandriva and Suse have good reputations, but you will pay for it. You have to join expensive clubs or buy their off the shelf editions.

 Among the independent, light distros, DSL and Puppy probably have the most helpful communities, but Ubuntu light and Xubuntu take advantage of the friendly Ubuntu Community

4)Documentation: If you look at the bookshelves in most bookstores, you will see that Mandriva and Red Hat/Fedora are the most well documented though Ubuntu is catching up.
