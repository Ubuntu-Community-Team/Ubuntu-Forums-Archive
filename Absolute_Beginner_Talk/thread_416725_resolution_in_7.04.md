### Post by singedwings on 2007-04-21
I have been having a nightmare with the nvidia drivers and refresh/resolution problems.

To partly solve your problem you need to add the horizontal and vertical refresh rates to make the most of your monitor, eg:

> Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"CTX VL710ST"
	Option		"DPMS"
	VendorName 	"CTX"
	ModelName 	"VL710ST"
	HorizSync	30-95
	VertRefresh	50-160
EndSection

To find the valid entries for your monitor you will need to find the technical specifications, or if you are really lucky they may be printed on the back of the monitor.

Also try adding more valid modes in the screen section, eg:

> Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"nVidia Corporation NV25 [GeForce4 Ti 4600]"
	Monitor		"CTX VL710ST"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	1
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	4
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	8
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	15
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	16
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
	SubSection "Display"
		Depth	24
		Modes		"1600x1200"	"1200x1024"	"1024x768"	"800x600"	"640x480"
	EndSubSection
EndSection

This may not completely solve your problems, as I think there is another problem with the nvidia driver that I am banging my head against at the mo. However the steps above should open up resolutions/refresh rates when using over drivers, such as nv.
