### Post by Nythain on 2007-05-08
Ok, so i've ended up becoming rather addicted to the ubuntu forum community.
I am noticing however, a lot of really poor forum etiquette so i figured id throw out some pointers for anyone relatively new to the forum scene.

A. ask shorter questions or one question at a time if at all possible
B. when presenting your problem, try to be as exact as possible... things like "then its said it couldnt boot" arent very helpfull, and i've failed to have an error yet say "Sorry man, I just can't boot"
C. Try to give descriptive titles to your posts... things like "Help" or "in trouble" or "totally screwed" and my personal favorite "f-this" not only turn people away, but it also keeps people who might have a solution to you problem from reading your post because we dont know we might cover the area of expertise of your problem.
D. punctuation is good, but i suck at it so who am i to criticize.
E. CODE... the tag is CODE wrapped in square brackets followed by the text and closed with /CODE in square brackets, there's even a button to do it for you at the top right of where you type your post. Things put in CODE are given a nice pleasant scrollable box so we dont have to scroll past a 3000 line post of your conf file to see the next reply
F. Bumping is nice, but you really shouldnt do it every 15 minutes just to keep your post at the top of the first page... wait a few hours, maybe a day or two... a lot of forum readers have marked everything old as read already and check unread posts upon thier return, no matter what page its on.
G. Try searching the forums for similar posts before you post... im not one to get to upset over repeat posts, but in the last few days i think i've tried to help 10 different people all with similar "black screen after ubuntu boot" problems, and the sad part is, i pulled my advice from what seemed to work for someone else i found having the problem in a different thread.
H. wich leads me to another point... Thread Jacking... if you're problem is the same as someone elses, mention it in thier thread, that way we all now multiple people are having that problem and can possibly help you both with one go at it... BUT dont go posting irrelevant problems in someone elses thread... Just because they are having a problem with Firefox Fonts does NOT make it an appropriate place to pose your question on how to update firefox or install extensions... That just gets us sidetracked on trying to help the person who started the thread (although i have been guilty of sidetracking numerous posts with off topic random conversation and personall opinion points on prefered software, sorry guys)

and the last, off the top of my head, is this
I. Threatening to "Abandon linux and return to Windows" if someone doesnt help you get your problem fixed will usually just result in a bunch of replies about how you should probably just go back to windows then and how linux just "isnt for you"

if you remember and use most of these tips you will probably have a very quick and helpfull solution to most of your ubuntu woes in no time

### Post by Cypher on 2007-05-08
All useful advice and I have a few as well..:)

1. When responding to a post, adding "I agree" an hour or two later doesn't really yield any new info and is not really necessary.
2. Before giving out commands, please take a moment to make sure that it is correct and think about any un-intended consequence of said command when used by some new comers.
2a. The flip side, when given a particular command, question what it does and understand it's behavior before trying it. If the poster can't explain what the command does, perhaps you should wait for others to respond. Also, it aids in your learning.

The rest has been said ad nauseam, so I won't repeat..
