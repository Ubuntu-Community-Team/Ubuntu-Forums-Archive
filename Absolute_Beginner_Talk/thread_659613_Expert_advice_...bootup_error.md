### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2008-01-05
From the looks of it, either something is wrong with your hard drive, the BIOS is misconfigured for accessing the disk, or you have the drive(s) connected incorrectly or with the wrong jumper selection.  It seems like the system may have implemented a software fix, possibly at a performance loss.
If the disk drive is old, you may want to consider replacing it, or at least not putting enough faith in it by keeping important data on it.  If you messed around inside the computer, you may want to double check your connections.
