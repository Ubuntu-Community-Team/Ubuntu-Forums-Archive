### Post by kidders on 2007-04-02
Hi there,

Unless you're good with Linux, I would strongly recommend waiting until well *after* the official release before switching to Feisty ... particularly if you feel attached to your data. :-P Having said that, I've been using Feisty for some time without incident (at least nothing I couldn't handle cropped up).


Others may give you different advice, but I think your choice should reflect your level of familiarity with Linux ... If you're a recent convert, sticking with Edgy (or even Dapper) for a few months may be advisable. On the other hand, if you're a Linux veteran, you'll enjoy Feisty.

### Post by msak007 on 2007-04-02
> **Meir said:**

Thanks
That really depends. The beta is stable, but I wouldn't recommend it if you're just starting out as it may still have major bugs. If you're anxious to try Ubuntu out, start out with Edgy (the most recent stable release) and play around with it for a month to get the feel of it and learn some of the basics. You'll most definitely love Ubuntu, but just in case you don't there's no sense in waiting a month only to find out you that don't. The nice thing about it is if you do install it and then decide to keep using it, you can upgrade to Feisty without having to reformat.
