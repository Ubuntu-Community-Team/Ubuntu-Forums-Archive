### Post by Steve1961 on 2007-09-06
yes it can be done, but how easy it turns out to be will depend on the chipsets in the wireless adapters. Some chipsets are supported out of the box and will 'just work' whilst others might need to be configured with a utility called ndiswrapper that allows the use of windows drivers.  However, for the most part that's fairly straightforward and there's lots of how tos on this forum.
