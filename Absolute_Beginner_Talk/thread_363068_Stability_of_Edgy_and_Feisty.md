### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-02-16
Both Edgy and Dapper are stable. The difference between them is that Dapper is LTS (Long Term Support), while Edgy is a regular version. Feisty Fawn is still in development and should be released by the end of April. Both Dapper and Edgy should suit your needs. Edgy has its applications, such as Firefox, java, etc more uptodate, but Dapper's can be manually updated if needed.

### Post by spoot on 2007-02-16
> **rosieg said:**
> So, as Edgy has now been out for a while should it now be safe to upgrade to it?
As Mike said, there's no difference in stability between these two, they are both stable. The difference is in how long the two get supported, supported meaning getting security and major bug fixes for installed programs. Edgy is supported for 18 months after its release, Dapper for 3 years (desktop install) or 5 years (server install).

You can read more about the process here:
[http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/releases](http://www.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/releases)

and here:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Releases](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Releases)
