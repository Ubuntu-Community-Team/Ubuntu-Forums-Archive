### Post by blastus on 2005-11-12
I find that:

- KDE uses more resources than GNOME
- KDE is slower than GNOME
- KDE is more customizable than GNOME
- Certain KDE apps have more functionality than their GNOME counterparts (like Konqueror vs Nautilus...Konqueror supports bookmarks, tabs, and viewing locally saved web pages etc...)
- Certain GNOME apps are so unstable for me that they are useless...like Nautilus for example crashes every few minutes
- KDE has many bugs in Kubuntu (like administrator mode, erratic errors when trying to open text files, some programs that don't open up all the time like Adept, and just some general weirdness and bugs like font sizes that occasionally change between reboots and stuff like that...hopefully they'll work these issues out in the future)
