### Post by Sef on 2006-05-06
> Is there a necessary pretask and configuration that I need to do before entering bank account number and credit number into brwoser webpage?

No, you can go to the webpage and check your bank account or buy what you want.  The important thing is to make sure the URL (the web address) is correct and not mispelled.  Also make sure the lock in shown on (though it can be faked by a phishing page.) NEVER, NEVER click on a link to your bank or other site if you get an email from them.  It is almost always a phishing site.  If you do get an email from a site claiming to be them, call your bank or the other site, if possible.

Phishing: Crooks will send out a message claiming to be from a bank or other site, like Pay-Pal.  You will be asked to fill in lots of personal information, e.g., bank account numbers, credit cards, personal Identification numbers, birthdates, and the other personal information that can be used to steal money from you or be used to pretend that they are you.

### Post by catlett on 2006-05-06
I do a couple of simple things to be safe. The biggest issue is hitting on a false link. (a crook sets up a link that leads you to a page he designed and not your banks etc. It will have a similar URL but it won't be right) So I keep a text document with my bank's and credit cards' etc correct URLs on it. When I want to go to their sight I open up the text and copy and paste the URL I want into my web browser. This way I know I'm going to the correct web site.
I also requested a $500 dollar credit card. This is my internet credit card. This way even if something happens I only have $500 at risk and not all my credit  or bank accout. Plus credit card companies will credit back false charges if you can show you didn't make them.
Last I try to use Paypal [https://www.paypal.com/](https://www.paypal.com/) as much as possible. If you don't already know, Paypal is a middleman for internet purchases. You set up an account with Paypal. In my case I use my $500 credit card. When you want to buy something you look for a Paypal logo. If the seller has a Paypal account you can  use Paypal.  Paypal pays the merchant and then gets the money from your  credit card (or bank account, you can do it either way). If something goes wrong Paypal will get involved. The have an insurance policy and a complaint process. If you win the complaint, Paypal gives you the money back. It just gives you a layer of protection and somewhere to turn to if an internet seller is disreputable.
There is never an absolute guarantee about security but I try to do these few things to keep myself in a manageable position if something occurs.

### Post by confused57 on 2006-05-06
Just make sure when you enter sensitive data such as your passwords, account & credit card numbers, etc that the url listed in the address bar begins with "https" and should be a lock in the lower right side of your screen.

If you're worried about "phishing", install the Netcraft Toolbar extension in Firefox, it'll tell you a little about the site you're on(risk rating, etc.).

Your biggest risk is probably outside of your control.  Hackers gaining access to your sensitive info by hacking into the merchant or bank's site.

Security is definitely something you want to be aware of, but if you follow some of the excellent advice given by everyone in this thread, it shouldn't be a worry.
