### Post by cogadh on 2007-07-27
Wow, that is a great explanation, I'll have to bookmark that one.

However the answer to the question is simple: Linux of any variety is not Windows, and is not subject to the same security threats that plague Microsoft products. In the entire history of Linux there have been less than 30 viruses and worms written for the system and none of them have been able to propagate due to the inherent security of Linux. As for hacking, all ports on the system except for those you need to access your local network and the internet are closed. If you are running a NAT router on your network, you are, for all intents and purposes, completely invisible to anyone outside your local network. As long as you are reasonably security aware (i.e. don't open e-mail attachments from unknown sources, only download from trusted sources, etc.) you have little to nothing to worry about while using Linux.
