### Post by eentonig on 2007-08-04
They will get fixed. But when? That's another questions. And depends on the problems and related software themselves.

- Some problems get solved via 'dirty' workaround. And as such, the devs will not incorporate them in their apps. But will take some longer to find a decent solution.

- Some problems are because of closed source applications (Nvidia/ATI drivers, ...) and can not be solved. Or the Manufacturer should bring out a new version with the fix.

- Some are so rare, that they so rarely, that it would be waste of time to program the solution in the app.

- etc, etc

Don't forget. 98% (wild guess) of the programming in Ubuntu (and most linux distributions) is done in the spare time of enthousiasts and volunteers.

### Post by coolen on 2007-08-04
Many people submit bugs to various places (either the application developers or Ubuntu developers themselves). There's very little chance that a developer will see a problem on the forum, and even less chance that they will act upon it.
Submitting bugs is the best way to get something done. Some will be preferred, others will be considered a lost cause (the closed source drivers, for example), but posting to the forums is not enough.
