### Post by chewearn on 2008-04-17
This is possible "outside" the PC OS.  Since the PC is off, it's logical to assume the OS is not running; therefore how to tell the OS to switch on the PC?  Chicken and egg problem.

So, you have to go outside the OS, meaning a separate controller is needed.  There are industrial or specialized equipment for such usage, e.g.
[http://www.audon.co.uk/ip9258.html](http://www.audon.co.uk/ip9258.html)

Or, you can DIY from low cost, low power network device.  E.g. I believed there are hacks using Linksys NSLU2.
