Is it worth it ?

To me it has been worth it - As a long time DOS/Windows user I got fed up with M$ and the constantly calling up some M$ "hot line" every time some minor change in Windows made my Windows XP do a walkabout. 

Dual screen - Wouldn't know, never been there.

IE runs with ease in Wine - But hey - What's wrong with Firefox? In my opinion it is way better than IE.

For-the-good-looking-programs and to impress your friends Beryl and Compiz are where you should look. But remember Beryl is only beta-software and it might crash.

Work well on a laptop? Well Michael Dell think so - Even he run Ubuntu on his State- of the art laptop. So stick with a Dell laptop if you are to by one.

Good for web-programming? Bluefish and Quanta is what I use for my web-dev and it works great. Haven't had a web-page/Web-site not running on my Ubuntu 6.10 Web-server yet.

As for the GIMP check this upcoming web-blog: [http://87.51.255.157/wordpress](http://87.51.255.157/wordpress) - (Site still in construction phase)  Look at the banner-pic, it's made with the GIMP and I've been a CorelDRAW/Photopaint user since 1991 and are still learning the GIMP's way to do the things I want to do.

I too have a huge music and video collection and all my media-files have never looked that good on my old XP-box. Just install the codecs from add/remove program and you'll be home free.

Memory management in Linux is incomparable to Windows due to the endless amount of real-time-scanners you need in windows. The junkyard of antivirus-scanners, spyware-scanners and their total waste of system resources you do not have in Linux. You have no inexplainable hard-drive activity. The CPU doesn't constantly overheat and the Blue-Screen-of-death thing is non-existent. 

As for system speed - My AMD Athlon XP 2800+ with 1 gig presents me to the login-screen 30 seconds after turning my computer on. Vista didn't give me that kind of speed. My computer isn't exactly a small one regarding resources. 2800+ CPU, 1 GB RAM, 128 MB nVidia G-Force TI 4200 SE graphics card, 200 GB SATA hard drive 7200 rpm, on board  5.1 sound and on board 1 GBit Ethernet card - I've never thought that a config like that would make things I do go as fast as they're done in Ubuntu.

Is Ubuntu Linux for you? Try out the live-Cd, you're the only one that know what you expect from your computer.
