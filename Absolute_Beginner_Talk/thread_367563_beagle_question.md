### Post by jethro10 on 2007-02-22
In synaptic, there is an option to remove and one to COMPLETLEY remove. This takes away your settings.

Also look for hidden folders in your home directory (CTRL-H) in nautilus.

they are preceded with a fullstop eg .beagle might be there?

possibly it has an entry as a sub directory of .gnome or .gnome2

Deinstall beagle as above and chech for folders like these, then re-install.
