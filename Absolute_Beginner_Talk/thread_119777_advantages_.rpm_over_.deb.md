### Post by Vengeful Cynic on 2006-01-20
As a long-time RedHat user who has also meddled with Suse and is currently learning to taste the Ubuntu, I have to say that I really haven't detected a lot of differences between the two package systems on a practical level.

While there are some shortcomings in the rpm dependancy handling, they tend to be more than made up for in the fact that, at least for the time being, many things being released on the corporate side of the fence hit the Linux community in either rpm or source form.  Thus, the easier and cleaner a distro's rpm handling is, the better it will be for the Windows power-user turned Linux n00b... which, from my experience, encompasses a LOT of new Linux users.

So to answer the OP's question, the biggest difference is that rpm can really screw up its dependencies sometimes... but on the flip side, you can usually find a lot of stuff in rpm that you might otherwise have to compile from source.

-Vengeful Cynic
