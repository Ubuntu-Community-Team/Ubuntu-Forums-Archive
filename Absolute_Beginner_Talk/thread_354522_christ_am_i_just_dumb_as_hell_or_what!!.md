### Post by slimdog360 on 2007-02-06
dont worry, its not very hard once you figure out whats going on. You can use something called 'synaptic', which is in ubuntu, or 'adept' when using kubuntu. This is the main place you should go to when wanting to install things. If you are using Kubuntu just have a look around in the menus for something called adept and click it. It should ask for a password.

Then you can browse around for what you want. Its pretty straight forward to install things from there. This is how its usually done in linux, the ubuntu guys provide one big place with thousands of programs ready for you to install at your fingertips, no more wandering the internet trying to get cracks for programs.

A couple of links you may like to look at.
[http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/]("http://monkeyblog.org/ubuntu/installing/")
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy]("http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy")

edit: you can also search adept (or synaptic) for themes to install. Those themes are not very hard to install from gnomelook/kdelook or whatever but you may want to get some more experience under your belt first. So in the mean time just search adept for 'kde themes' or 'icons' or whatever you want.
