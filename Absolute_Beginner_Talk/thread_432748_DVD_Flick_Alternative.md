### Post by ron999 on 2007-05-05
Hi

There's a package called [SIZE="3"]**tovid**[/SIZE].
I used CLI, but there's a GUI option too.
Then use K3b to make an ISO.
Then test view the ISO with VLC Media Player.
Then burn it with K3b.

### Post by WillC36 on 2007-05-15
Hello there,

This is my first post and I'm a complete newbie to Ubuntu so forgive me  =)

I just recently moved to Ubuntu 7.04 FF

I too was using DVD Flick in Windows XP to convert my video files to dvd format.

What I ended up doing was downloading WINE, installed it and then ran at the terminal mode wine and the name of the DVDFLICK install file.  It installed it and ran without a hasstle.

I attempted to convert a file and found that it goes through the entire proccess of creating the files, or I may have checked off an option to create an ISO in DVD Flick.  

What it doesn't seem to do is recognize I have a DVD burner and burn the files back.  Which I had no problem fixing. I downloaded K3B and used it to burn my VOB files back to DVD.  It worked without a problem.  I know this is probably the long way of doing things.

I have used DeVeDe but when I have a file already in letter box format, wide screen format, it seems to put additional black bars thus messing up my video.  I've found DVD FLICK to convert my files very nicely.  If I could find a program simlar to DVD Flick in quality I would love it.  Yes I can get TOVID or DeVeDe to do the job but it just doesn't produce the same quality of file.  Of course being a newbie to Ubuntu, 1 week =) , I may just not have something set right.  Either way I just wanted to post that I got it work within WINE , ya well sort of.

WillC36
