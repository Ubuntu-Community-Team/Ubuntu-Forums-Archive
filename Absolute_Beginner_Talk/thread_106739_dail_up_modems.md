### Post by bscbrit on 2005-12-21
This is a little bit like 'how long is a piece of string' - but not quite.

I have had mixed results with USB modems.  Some have worked but most have not, at least not easily.

The same goes for PCI modems.  I have had great success with 3com but less success with other makes, including US Robotics.  Some work and some don't.  

I changed to broadband sometime ago and so I haven't had to install a dial-up modem for almost 2 years.  Perhaps things have improved.

However, I find that older modems which use a serial or parallel connection work every time and are reliable.  They are not, however, as common as the other varieties but if you can get your hands on one then, to me, they are worth their weight in gold.

I'm sure that every other user will have their own experiences and favourites.  This is just my view.

EDIT:  I have just seen localzuk's post - The type that I am recommending with serial connector is RS232, but I had forgotten that!
