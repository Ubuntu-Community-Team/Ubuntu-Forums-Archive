### Post by WhoSimplex on 2008-01-16
While I don't think gaming on Linux is necessarily a good idea for a reason to switch, I wouldn't say it's not possible, and I think most of those games work (I'm sure at least Portal does).

However, about the power supply: The minimum recommended for an 8600GT from the nVidia store says something about the 300's. The problem with cheaping out on a power supply is that if it goes out, it can take everything else out with it, so then you're not just out the $20 for your cheap power supply, you're out the entire price of the computer.

I'd be careful about doing something like that. If at all possible, order than and get a decent quality PSU off Newegg or Tiger Direct or something to put in it. OCZ, Corsair, PC Power & Cooling, Antec, and Thermaltake are all brands that you can (mostly) depend on. Go with at least a 400ish watt, and make sure you have around 18-20 amps on the 12v rail if you do upgrade. More is better.
