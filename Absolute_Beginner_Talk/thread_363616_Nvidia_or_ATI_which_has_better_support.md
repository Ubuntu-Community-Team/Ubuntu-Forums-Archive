### Post by xopher on 2007-02-17
> **duality said:**
> Nvidia, they have open-source drivers and everything works with them, with ATI its really hard to get XGL and Beryl working, and stuff like that because they have very little support for linux.

Please don't post things that do not check out.

NVIDIA's drivers are NOT open-source. ATI's drivers are NOT open-source.

Ok, now we're clear about that. If you play games, I'd go for an NVIDIA, since their proprietary drivers are still superior to ATI's in a linux environment. NVIDIA's drivers support Beryl 'out-of-the-box' on edgy too, so you just have to install a set of 9x.xx nvidia drivers, then run Beryl and it'll work. With ATI, you still have to use XGL, which is a pain in the ***.

But, if games aren't a top priority, Id consider INTEL. They do have open drivers, which work  reasonably well too.

Edit: And well, there are open-source drivers for both ATI and NVIDIA, but the ATI ones don't work with the latest cards, and the nvidia (nouveau) driver isn't ready, not even close to ready, yet. Oh, and these are UNOFFICIAL, so the support isn't that great either.
