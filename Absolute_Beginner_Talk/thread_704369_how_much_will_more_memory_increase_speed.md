### Post by SeanTater on 2008-02-22
Yes, as long as your motherboard can handle that much memory and has a slot for it, you can always add more. The question is: is it useful?

The answer he was trying to provide is that you can double your memory to increase the subjective speed if your computer. If you more than double it, you are largely wasting it.

The real answer is more complex, and it deals with what you do with your computer. For more information on what memory your computer is using, you can consult Gnome System Monitor (I think it is) or KSysGuard, and ```
vmstat
``` would give you something you can post in here that will give us information on what, if, and how much you need.
