### Post by Eggnog on 2007-07-04
You can download kubuntu-desktop from synaptic, and it will download KDE and all the k apps that goes with it.  Then, when you log out, you will have the choice of using Gnome or KDE when log back in.  Bear in mind, however, that your menus will have both Gnome and KDE apps.  There are a couple of apps you can install, though, that will put your KDE apps in their own menu when you run Gnome and Gnome apps in their own menu when you run KDE.  Helps keep things tidy.

The alternative is to run the kubuntu live CD to see how you like it.
