### Post by Thistlewait on 2007-06-08
Hi,

I'm Thistlewait. Brand new to Ubuntu and Linux. I just thought I'd offer a brief intro of myself.

I've been in the computer industry for over 30 years, starting in the with some truly ancient machines (like machines that used toroidal-core memory...a whopping 1K worth!) After the military I worked with UNIVACs, DEC PDP series, DEC VAX series, a few IBMs, and then I started working in R&D labs with microprocessors like the Zilog Z-80, Intel 808x, Motorola 6800 series, AMD bit-slice processors, and even some machines that we built from scratch using discreet TTL devices. I'm mainly a hardware geek, but I've also written software: diagnostics, device drivers, even micro-code for bit-slice machines. I've written a few applications, mostly under contract, for several small businesses. I've written software in most languages: assembler, Pascal, Basic, C, C++, Algol, Fortran, etc. But, I don't consider myself to be professional programmer. I think I'm more of a software "artist"...that is, when I finish a project I consider it to be a work of art more than a "tool" or "application". And that means that I'm not commercially viable as a programmer.

These days I'm retired, and get to simply play with my toys with few other demands on my time. In the past few years I've become obsessed with 3D art and animation, and use several programs to indulge my obsession: Modo, Poser, Carrara, Cinema 4D, TrueSpace, Blender, Adobe Photoshop, Paint Shop Pro, Corel Photopaint, just to name a few. I also dabble with video editing and have been using various versions of Pinnacle's "Studio" for video editing. I also use a little know program called "MainVision" from MainConcept for compositing animation, video, effects, and sound.

I also play guitar and make videos of myself playing and singing that I post on YouTube and LiveVideo.

Of course I do all those things within a Windows XP environment. But, I've been interested in Linux for quite some time...mostly because I don't like the extra overhead (in cost and system resources) to keep my systems protected with Firewalls and Anti-Virus programs. 

Lately, the amount of time I have to spend tweaking rules, blocking or allowing different programs network access, blocking or allowing cookies and pop-ups has become more of a chore than the benefits would seem to warrant. Thus I've been looking at Linux for some time. Ubuntu is the first release that had a "look and feel" that I liked, and, so far, a few problems notwithstanding, has been reasonably easy to get up and running. Of course, all my favorite programs don't run under Linux. Some of those programs have Linux versions available, but at added cost...and some of the 3D software I use is damn expensive.

But, looking through the repositories, there seems to be a fairly broad spectrum of open-source software for audio and video. The only 3D program I've seen that comes even remotely close to the functionality of my Windows-based 3D programs is Blender. But, I have to say, that Blender has become a rather interesting and mature 3D animation tool. I don't use it often...the UI is very, very strange and difficult for me to navigate, but I have played with it enough that I've been getting comfortable with it.

I don't know if I can ever completely leave Windows behind because of the amount of money I've invested in the software I use for my hobbies. But I'm gonna give Linux a fair shake, and the  Ubuntu release gets the credit for that.

Thanks for reading,
Thistlewait
