### Post by Mikjoba on 2007-04-24
I have recently installed Ubuntu from the live CD onto the hard disk of an old 800mhz Athlon PC and was pleasantly surprised by how everthing seemed to work perfectly without any configuration, Internet,no problem, sound ok, screen resolution ok, I then tried reading from a 4GB San Disk memory stick, again all went well, but when I dismounted the stick, whick I could see from the operative system was no longer accessible I could see that it was still active from from the activity LED on the stick, is it safe to remove it anyway? (I did not want to try incase I made it unreadable), the normal situation when I do this on a Windows PC is that the LED goes out once I have clicked on the safe removal of the USB device icon.
I have made a quick check about this "problem" but haven't really found anthing to help me understand this.
I left the stick in until I turned the PC off after about an hour but the activity LED was alight all this time.

### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-04-24
It's probably just that your USB key is receiving power from your computer motherboard, even though it is dismounted from the desktop.

### Post by tarpon_bill on 2007-04-24
When the OS takes the icon down it should be OK to unplug it. If you dismount the stick without explicitly doing an eject, it produces an error when I go back and read the stick. Always do the dismount command ... right click, select eject then unplug. 

The lights on my sticks stay on.
