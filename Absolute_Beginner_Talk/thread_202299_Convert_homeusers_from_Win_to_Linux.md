### Post by LPent on 2006-06-23
I have been a computer user since the time of the Commodore VIC20. I have worked with computers all my life, have been a (low level) programmer and a web designer. I can't imagine life without my computer (sad isn't it).
One of the up (or down) sides of this is that I am asked by almost everyone to help them with their computer related problems. As such I have helped literally hunderds of people. Next to this I have also worked for a company that provided educational software for people to learn how to use programs and OS's (well, Win mostly). At one time I headed a research into how people use and percieve computers.
Recently I finally made the move to Linux. Being somewhat familiar with Unix on servers I felt confident in doing so and am willing to learn along the way.

I would like to share some of my experience here in order to provide some background for anyone who want's to convert MS users to the world of Linux.

First of all, you must understand that you can place users somewhere in a spectrum that ranges from "those who use a computer as a tool" to "those who build and program computers". Or to put it differently: the higher one is on this spectrum, the more structural knowledge one will have of how a computer and an OS functions. I will refer to them as Low Level Users (LLU) and High Level Users (HLU).
LLU's can learn how to use their tool in two different way's (there are more, but basically it all comes down to two). 1) Simply learn to repeat the trick. and 2) understanding how it works.

The first group is actually the simplest group to convert. All they need to know is that all they have to do is learn new tricks to accomplish the same. And those tricks are very similar to the one's they allready know from Win. All they need to know is that once they learn these new trick, they will be able to enjoy a faster computer and free software. You must approach those people with caution though as humans have a tendency to "stick to what they know". Emphasize on speed and freedom versus a small learning curve. Install their computer so that is looks similar to Win.

The second group is a bit more difficult. They have aquired some knowledge of their system which they will want to transfer to their "new system" (after all, it's the same computer isn't it?) They are usually comfortable about installing new hardware and software as it is well documented for Win. It's is vital to understand that they will get frustrated over difficulties regarding hardwaredrivers and downloading software. Write a manual on these subject from their point of view and make the pluspoint in relation to windows stand out. But don't tell lies!

I myself had to recently reinstall Ubuntu clean as I had tried to install an Alsa driver since my headphones did not work. This driver was not in Synaptics. Well, I was very careful, but I messed up my system big time anyway! I am sorry to point out: this is something that cannot happen in Windows (well, I have never had that problem anyway in all those years).
It's not a big issue for me, but it will be for this group of people, so keep that in mind.

Another thing you should remember when trying to convert LLU: Make sure everything in their system is supported. Every peice of hardware must work. Otherwise they will start complaining. It's better to wait another year with the conversion than to try and reason with them that they don't need this hardware or that they should buy something else (Why must I buy that? I thought Linux was free?)

HLU's: Try to get them to install a dual boot and tell them to play with it. (HLU's love to play with computers). Help them with questions on their level, don't oversimplify or assume anything. Let them find out things for themselves, don't push; don't try to persuade them with arguments.

People that fall between LLU's and HLU's are the most difficult. They think they know enough and hate to be placed in a situation where they don't know anything anymore. I have yet to find an approach for this type of user. But I am convinced that for this group of users the Linux GUI has too many shortcomings.

Some extra general guidelines:
LLU's need not be bothered with the CLI, EVER!!!!!!!!
If you are going to use arguments, restrict yourself to the only 3 (IMHO) undisputed facts: Faster, Safer (virii, malware, etc.) and Cheaper.
Before converting LLU's make a list of hardware and software they use and be absolutely sure they work/have equivalents in Linux.

Hope this helps.
