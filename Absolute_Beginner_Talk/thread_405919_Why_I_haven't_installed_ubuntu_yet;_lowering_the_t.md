### Post by KIAaze on 2007-04-10
If there is no help during installation, then there should be one of course. ^^
I didn't need it, so I don't know.

I remember that Debian has a help for partitioning during installation.
However, it is quite technical and there is no real GUI, just a table of the partitions with what will happen to them.

The easiest way I found to understand the basics of partitioning was the graphical Gnome partition editor.
I just played around with it (because as long as you don't click on apply, nothing happens) and understood the basics of primary, extended and logical partitions. :)

Then there are all these advanced things like hidden partitions and so on, that I still have to understand.
