### Post by jslmg on 2008-01-01
I'm test-driving Galeon as well, at the moment.

Getting the Google Search window is a bit trickier than just checking View Bookmark Toolbars. 

You'll need to open the Bookmark Editor. If you're running Galeon brand-new, still with its defaults, there's a "Sample Smart Bookmarks" folder in Bookmarks. The Google search entry field is a "smart bookmark." 

In the bookmarks editor, find the Smart Bookmarks folder at the bottom of the left column. Click on it. You'll then see Google, first one in the folder, in the right column.

Highlight google, then click the properties button in the bookmark editor toolbar. Put a check next to the top level of your bookmarks. That will make Google appear in the very top level. 

Oh, by the way, be very careful here. DO NOT close the bookmark editor directly. If you do, Galeon will crash (a bug that needs reporting). To make sure all changes are saved, close the galeon browser, but not the bookmark editor. Then, restart Galeon.

If you've activated the Bookmark Toolbar, you'll now see Google at the end of it, with two arrows. Click the arrows, and you'll see the search entry field. 

You'll have to click those arrows after each launch of Galeon, but the search field will remain open as long as Galeon is.

Hope that helps.

Well, my Galeon is now broken, broken, broken. It is a buggy program, you know. Successive crashes finally crippled it, it seems.

Epipany is far more stable and looks nicer, so I'm sticking with that... typing this post in Epiphany, in fact. It's a fork from Galeon, and a far better one too.

But to get smart bookmarks in Galeon, there is documentation on it in the help documents.
However, it looks like even that is wrong. According to the help, all you have to do is go to "Bookmark" ( I presume that is in the menubar?), then select "New Smart Bookmark." I can't see any such entry. It might be in a contextual menu, but since my Galeon is now broken, I obviously can't look. I'll remove it and reinstall later, then have a look.

But here is a method that i know will work. It's the Epiphany method, applied to Galeon (they are of the same ilk, after all).

(1) In Galeon, open the Bookmarks Editor (from the Bookmarks menu). 

(2) In the editor's toolbar, click "New Bookmark"

(3) In the "Location" field of the new bookmark dialog, type "http://www.google.com")

(4) In the next field below the "Location" (sorry, don't remember the name of the field) paste the following: 

[http://www.google.com/search?q=%s](http://www.google.com/search?q=%s)

(5) In the right column of the BM editor, move the bookmark to the top level of the BMs. This will put it in the BM toolbar.

(6) DO NOT close the BM editor. Again, this will crash Galeon, and then it will die completely. Instead, close Galeon with the BM editor still open. This  will save changes. When you restart Galeon, select "Restore Previous Session". Hopefully, your Google smart bookmark will be there.

I have written all of this from memory, since my Galeon is broken. Try it, let me know if it works. Maybe you can tell me what I've missed. I'll test it out after removing and reinstalling Galeon, later.
