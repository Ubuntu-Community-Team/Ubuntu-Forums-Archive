### Post by kebes on 2007-11-06
It's not really free. As far as I know, only [VMWare Player]("http://www.vmware.com/products/player/") is a free download. This lets you run virtual machines, but to create and manage them you need one of the other products ([VMWare Workstation]("http://www.vmware.com/products/ws/") or one of the [VMWare Servers]("http://www.vmware.com/products/product_index.html")) which are not free downloads.

None of the products are "Free" in the sense of "Free Software" (open-source code, etc.). If you are looking for a free and open-source virtualization system that runs on Ubuntu, there are many other options (see [this list]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_virtual_machines") and look for GPL or BSD licensed projects). I've used [QEMU]("http://fabrice.bellard.free.fr/qemu/") and it works great. (It's in the repositories.)
