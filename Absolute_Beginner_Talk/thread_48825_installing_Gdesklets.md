### Post by C.B. on 2005-07-14
You should have an icon in Applications/Accessories sub-menu. Just click it.
Or use Run under Applications/System and enter "gdesklets"
Or from the command prompt: "gdesklets shell" or "gdesklets start." 
One of those should do the trick. I think that's right, but I'm not in Ubuntu at the moment to check.

To run it automatically at startup find Sessions under the System menu and enter "gdesklets shell".

Check out the forum at [http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org](http://gdesklets.gnomedesktop.org) for lots of info. to fine-tune and configure.
