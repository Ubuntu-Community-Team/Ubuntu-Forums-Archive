YES, Ubuntu will do it. Here are some links to the official web-sites

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InternetAndNetworking](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InternetAndNetworking)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NetworkDevices](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/NetworkDevices)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Servers](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Servers)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/SettingUpSamba)



I basically ignore the Internet-related stuff, since this is only acting as a storage device...
it's where my home computers (I have over six at any given moment) store EVERYthing. Most of my home machines have comparatively small HDD's... usually 10-40gb, just enough to boot. /home and settings are on the server., all firefoxes use the same profile, etc .


I would recommend starting with a single computer/user connected to your server, this will let you experiment and not overwhelm the task at hand.

Running headless is another task, do one or the other first, get it down pat then move on. Headless is probably better to get first.

Wireless is a function of the wireless router box, not the server box.

My .02.

Others may agree/disagree. Viewpoints/opinions welcomed.
