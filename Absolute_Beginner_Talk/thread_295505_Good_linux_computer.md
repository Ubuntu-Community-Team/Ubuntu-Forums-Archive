### Post by ExZen on 2006-11-09
"Upgrade path for AMD may well me AM3 socket, upgrade path from core 2 duo may be on the same socket."

You are incorrect on at least one account. Intel has announced that after this year, they'll be moving onto the Santa Rosa platform, which is not pin for pin compatible with LGA 775 so if you're ever planning to upgrade to the newest processors, a new mobo will have to go in their as well (of course, chances are they'll still support socket LGA 775 for awhile)

Yes, a conroe 6300 OC's like a champ.  However, the mATX boards available for it do not allow the user to take advantage of this.  I do not think you understand what I mean by mATX.  This stands for "Micro ATX" and they are basically smaller than normal motherboards which run quieter and take up much less space in a ATX case, or can fit into a mATX case.

AM3 is expected in late 2008 or early 2009:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AM3](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AM3)

Santa Rosa is expected April of next year:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrino#Santa_Rosa_platform](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centrino#Santa_Rosa_platform)

So, long term wise, LGA 775 is not the way to go.  However, in addition to this if I go with AM2, I can still OC, whereas if I get a LGA 775 mATX mobo i cannot, and will be stuck at almost stock speeds regardless of processor.

At the moment, it will cost be about an extra $40 CAD to switch to the energy efficient version of this processor, which is why I'm waiting.  chances are i'll find it on sale for the same price eventually.

### Post by Velotix on 2006-11-09
A small suggestion from someone who very recently got a new computer (the one I'm using ^^) - I went with the Core 2 Duo E6600 for four reasons:

1) Higher base clock speed
2) 4MB L2 Cache instead of 2MB for E6300 and E6400
3) As I was looking for info about the new processors, I stumbled upon the fact that E6300 and E6400 processors failed a number of quality control tests which is why they only have 2MB Cache (the other 2MB is disabled)
4) The price difference between the E6400 and E6600 is minimal

It's working like a dream so far: good luck with whatever you decide to buy. ^^
