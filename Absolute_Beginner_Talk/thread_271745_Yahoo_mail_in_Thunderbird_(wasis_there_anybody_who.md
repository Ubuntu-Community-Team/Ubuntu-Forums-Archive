### Post by HereInOz on 2006-10-05
You need to go to Options in Yahoo mail and set up POP mail.  Click on the Options link in Yahoo mail and then select POP access and Forwarding.

Select Web & POP access and follow the prompts and you should be able to set up Yahoo to act as a POP server.

You will then need to configure Thunderbird to read the POP server, in exactly the same way that you would configure it to read POP mail from any ISPs mail server.

You do not set up the mail account in TBird as an IMAP account - you set it up as a POP account.

Hope this helps.

Cheers,

### Post by gvgerman on 2006-10-05
The free Yahoo! Mail does not allow for this.

In the upper right-hand corner of the Yahoo! Mail page select Options > Mail Options > Pop & Forwarding.  
The "Pop & Forwarding" can be found on the left side of the screen.

Here you'll likely find:

> Upgrade to Mail Plus  so you can: 
[LIST]
[*]access your Yahoo! Mail through other email providers, such as Outlook 
[*]forward your Yahoo! Mail to a different address
[/LIST]
