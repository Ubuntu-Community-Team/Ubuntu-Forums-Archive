### Post by trakz on 2006-08-13
Some open comments to the Ubuntu community:

Ok so I've now been living with this distro for a couple of weeks and I thought I'd share my thoughts - for what it's worth.

First of: Ubuntu f%^*ing ROCKS. This is by far the best distro I've ever used.

The Synaptic package manager alone is worth the price of admission. The ability to reliably install the core suite of apps with a couple of clicks is genius.

Ok, ok, I know redhat has the whole yum thing, and it works well, but it's not integrated with the red-hat package manager, getting them working together is a nightmare (or it was for me). Then you've got RPM's, not to mention the compile and install approach. 

I consider myself ok with linux but I'd guesstimate I've become 50% more productive with this distro just because it is so easy to re-configure. Whoever is building this stuff deserves a medal.

Secondly: the people here rock. I've rarley seen a forum where the comments are not only helpful and constructive but the approach isn't laced with cynicism and elitism. Thank you for helping us newbies - it's not only appreciated but it's seriously helping promote your cause.

Lastly: Again for what it's worth - you're so close to getting this thing spot on, and being close to offer a viable replacement for the M$ OS's. Keep developing the wizards, keep doing the whole GUI based configuration thing, maintaining a stable OS, keep offering the stunning support this community has become known for.

The only thing I would ask (and this comes entirley from not being here that long) is that the kernel upgrade path is well documented and fairly easy for us newbies to follow. One of the things I hated about Fedora was the convoluted upgrade path between FC2, FC3, FC4, etc.

With huge thanks,

trakz
