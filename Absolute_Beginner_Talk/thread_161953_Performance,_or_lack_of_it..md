### Post by tribaal on 2006-04-18
You should consider upgrading the RAM a bit if you use GNOME.

And yes, RAM is "plug and play", it's being taken care of at BIOS level. 
Of course, be extra careful when handling RAM, as it's extremely sensitive to static electrecity. Appart from that, it's pretty fail-proof. 

You might want to force your BIOS to to a memory test the first time you boot your computer after the change.
