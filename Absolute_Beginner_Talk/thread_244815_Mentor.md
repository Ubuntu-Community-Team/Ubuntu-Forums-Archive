### Post by aysiu on 2006-08-27
I think it's a terrible idea. I've always been opposed to this sort of thing, and I turned off private messaging because of people trying to make me a mentor of this type.

Posting threads in a forum (as opposed to private messages to an individual) has several distinct advantages:

1. **The knowledge base expands.** If the mentor doesn't know the answer... well, tough luck. But if you post a thread, *anyone* can answer. Stump one person, another one comes along to help. That's what's called a *community*.

2. **The response time shortens**. Along with the community at large having a greater collective knowledge base than one individual, there's always *someone* logged in to the forums at any given time, so you could get an answer at any given time. If you have one mentor, you have to wait for that mentor to get around to answering your question. Depending on your mentor's schedule, you may have to wait days or weeks to get an answer. In a thread, you usually get an answer within minutes.

3. **You help others by being helped**. A lot of people have their problems solved by searching the forums. They find a thread where someone has the same problems they've been having, and they find a solution. You can't search others' private messages. The mentoring program you're suggesting promotes isolationism and doesn't consider posterity. The archives of publicly helped individuals help future individuals. The archives of privately helped inviduals just take up space.

4. **The focus stays on the community**. I love it when I see someone get helped within minutes by four or five people, all contributing a little bit more--in their own unique ways--to solving the problem or answering the question, and then the OP saying, "What a great community. You guys have really helped." I'd hate for that to turn into, "My mentor is so great." Once the mentor leaves, that person is lost and has no appreciation for how wonderful the Ubuntu community is as a whole.
