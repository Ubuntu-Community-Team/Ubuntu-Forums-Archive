Ipod has had some patchy support so I have seen.  I have a clone IPOD so I can't answer accurately,  but generally yes would be the answer.

Virtualization software generally works as well.  However I have no luck with Virtual Box.  I made a virtual XP on my wifes laptop and then transferred it over to my machine and it runs fine under VM ware Player.   The problem with this is you have to decide what your doing before you do it.  Not fun to plan on a machine that you probably use mostly for fun, but worth it in the long haul.

Also look into back ups.   There is an easy one called Mondo and one called Simple back up.   Mondo installs through Synaptic and Simple Back up installs through Automatrix -  very easy.    Worth it if you have any data that you want to keep.

Mondo makes rescue discs which for a laptop is pretty essential.  They are harder to get going,  and once you find the right combination of drivers for your hardware you probably want to keep an install as a back up.   Best to do this once its all working.  

With Mondo you can skip the /home directory where you probably keep your MP3's or other files that aren't essential to running your system.  This will keep your back up a lot lighter and save you DVDs.

Good Luck.
