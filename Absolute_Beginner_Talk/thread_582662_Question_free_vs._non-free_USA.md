### Post by Espreon on 2007-10-20
Basically, Free Software (as in freedom) is licensed so that one can modify it and redistribute it.

Non-free software (the payware and freeware) is licensed so you can't modify it w/o permission or at all, and then redistribute it.

So

Free Software = distributable and modifyable, w/o permission and free (as in cost)

Non-free = its free (as in cost) or is not free (as in cost) at all, but sometime is redistributable, modifying NO for payware, and MAYBE with freeware (you would need the developer's permission).

### Post by aysiu on 2007-10-20
Opera on Linux - non-free license, free cost.
Opera on Wii - non-free license, cost

Windows - non-free license, cost

Ubuntu - free license, free cost
Ubuntu DVD on Amazon - free license, cost

The license determines what you can do with the software, the code, the distribution and modification of that software.

The cost is what you pay for it.
