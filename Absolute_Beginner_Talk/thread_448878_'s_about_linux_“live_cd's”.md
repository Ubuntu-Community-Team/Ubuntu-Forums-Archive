### Post by Zuph on 2007-05-19
Viruses are interesting, because they generally don't behave in predictable manners.  Presuming you haven't mounted any of your hard disk partitions, then you shouldn't have a problem with malware staying resident after a reboot.  That said, there are no Linux viruses that behave like that, so you're safe.

As for browser history, no, it will not remain unless you specifically set up a persistent browser install.

### Post by aysiu on 2007-05-19
It could be conceivable to *create* a virus (or other kind of malware) targeted at live CDs that would mount partitions and do bad things to them. As far as I know, Knoppix and Ubuntu give you root privileges (more or less... passwordless sudo).

Since the session changes are in RAM, the history is lost when you reboot your computer.
