### Post by trak87 on 2007-06-16
Why would an operating system need to notify you of something? It should be the other way around: the user notifies the OS of something. Otherwise, the OS popping up messages is an annoyance. I don't want my email program doing anything until I tell it to do it. Otherwise being online becomes some compulsive thing where the computer is running my life rather than the other way around.

When it comes to being online, I am strongly opposed to blinking images and advertisements online vying for my attention. I've been using Privoxy as my http proxy for years and would hate to be without it. It squelches blinking gifs and annoying ads all day long. BTW, it's in the repositories.

### Post by testube_babies on 2007-06-16
You can also change the hide/unhide time of your panels:

In gconf-editor, go to /apps/panel/toplevels and change hide_delay and unhide_delay for the panels you use.

Also, for a good and usable dock, I use avant-window-navigator:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=385981](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=385981)
