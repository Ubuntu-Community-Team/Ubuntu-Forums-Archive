### Post by Sweet Spot on 2007-06-09
If you're dealing with an ISO only and also use the Gnome desktop, please don't waste your time opening any programs,  Just right click the ISO file and choose "burn to disc". That's IT. 

I do it constantly. Not one bad burn. 

If you have to make an ISO: I use DVD Shrink (with WINE)  to make the ISO, and have only had one or two instances when it didn't work because of a badly scratched disc. Every other DVD compressing program I've tried, has failed miserably, or just didn't work at all from the start. (includes those mentioned in this thread)
