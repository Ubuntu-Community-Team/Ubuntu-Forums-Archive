### Post by NeghVar on 2006-03-11
you need to go into your BIOS, I think it is the Delete key or one of the "F" keys to get there, it usually says. In there change your first boot device to the cd rom that you pu the disc into. Save the changes and exit. 

Your computer will restrart and eventually will either ask you if you want to run off of the cd rom, or it will just run off the cd rom. If it asks then just hit enter. 

From there it will load up and you can set up your language, how much you want installed and such, basically just following the on screen instructions.

### Post by Pragmatist on 2006-03-11
>  i decided to go ahead and try and set up Ubuntu on my spare computer.

So you've got another computer. Fantastic!  Put the CD in your other computer (again make sure the BIOS boots from the CD first) and reboot.  THIS WILL NOT HURT YOUR COMPUTER!!!  Your just booting to see if the CD works.  After you get to the ubuntu screen, and your CD light goes off, shut your machine off.

This will at least partially rule out whether or not the problem is the CD (which is a common problem).
