### Post by nealklomp on 2006-06-24
h2oclick--[this]("http://bdmorrison.com/ubuntu/kdegnome.html") should provide a bit more detail. But in brief* I* see KDE as more windows like, bottom toolbar default, one menu tab, etc, the look and feel of it is more XPish, and it is more work orientated, while Gnome feels more Macish, more recreation/art *to me*. Both are quite well developed and about equal performance wise, despite many varying opinions on this. They each have strengths and weaknesses and it is really what *you* are looking for and like that will inform your choice.
Xfce of Xubuntu is different, it is more minimalistic, the menu tab is availble from right click on the desktop and comes with two toolbars by default but only one is needed to hold open apps, and desktop changer. It is as robust, but much more performance orientated. Best used on old machines or for those who want something with bleeding edge speed who don't mind a little terminal time occassionally.

I personally tend towards Gnome, but when I am in an impatient mood or have a lot to get done, will use X, it is really much faster on my box.
KDE for me, just felt too much like a windows product, but that was just me.

edit: you can run all three with the same system, sudo apt-get install xubuntu-desktop or kubuntu-desktop from Ubuntu, then sudo apt-get remove when you decide what you want or keep all three. When you log in, you click on sessions and select the **D**esktop **E**nviornment that you want to use.
