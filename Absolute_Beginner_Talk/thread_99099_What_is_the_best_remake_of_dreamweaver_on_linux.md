### Post by Qrk on 2005-12-04
I don't believe there is a direct equivalent... but one of the best is Bluefish, which is available in the repositories. I don't do much design anymore, but bluefish isn't quite WYSIWYG. Instead it offers real time viewing of your page in progress. 

Otherwise there is always wine, or openoffice writer/web.

### Post by stuporglue on 2005-12-05
I'll second the bluefish and Nvu recomendations. Nvu if you're doing mostly straight HTML. It's very good at HTML. It strugles if you hand it too much PHP, in my experience. 

Bluefish requires a bit more knowledge, but if you've got the knowledge, it's very helpful, especially for php. 

I've also used Quanta (or maybe it was Quanta++, can't remember) which was pretty good. I didn't spend enough time on it to give a solid review, but my impression was that it was trying the hardest to be like Dreamweaver, but was still in development. Quanta uses the QT (KDE) libs, I think.
