### Post by c0met on 2008-01-15
I don't know Ubuntu 6.06 as I have only used the live CD for 7.10. But this might give you an idea...

Sometimes when I boot-up, Ubuntu does not always log-in to my router and I have to give it a bit of a "kick". What I do is to right click on the network icon (upper right near log-out icon). I then uncheck "Enable Networking" so that the network is essentially disconnected. Then I right click and connect the networking up again. This usually gets my system online. It's not often that I need to do this. Youc an always check if you have a connection to your modem by right clicking the network icon and then looking at the "Connection Information". If everything there is 0.0.0.0.0, then you do not have a connection.

As I said, the above information may or may not help.
