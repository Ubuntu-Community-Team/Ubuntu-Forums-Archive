### Post by Iandefor on 2006-10-11
A couple of things:

Both Microsoft and Apple have their media formats under lock and key. Ubuntu is committed to Free Software- in order for them to be able to use those, they would have to jeopardize the Freedom of their software, so instead of jumping through the hoops for those formats, they choose not to install them by default.

In regards to Flash, Adobe are working on a Flash 9 plugin for Linux that will have a variety of improvements over the current Flash plugin release. There is a beta planned, and release should be sometime in early 2007. However, because it's proprietary, Ubuntu chooses not to install Flash by default.
