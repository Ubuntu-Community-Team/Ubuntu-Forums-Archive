### Post by dvarsam on 2006-06-21
Good idea guys!

At the same time, let me add the following:

It is a _bad_ idea to put a subject/title in your Thread like the following:

1. Need Help (or "Please Help!" or "Help!!New Here")
2. Given up
3. I can't get this to work!!!!
4. New guy with simple questions! (or New guy wanting to get more involved!)
5. Help this total noob up and running!
6. Small question! Need answer badly!
7. Such a simple question, I'm embarresed to ask... (or "What a dumb question...but")

Your subject/title you choose for your Thread should _describe_ "roughly" what a reader is about to help you with!

The above Subjects/titles you give your Threads (& I read _all_ the time) are non-descriptive of your Thread's contents!!!

And if somebody wants to perform a search on this Forum about a specific subject (e.g. "partitions" or "partitioning software"), he will _never_ view your Thread on his Search results (even though your Thread could contain valuable info)!!!

At the same time, if I read a Thread with subject "configure X Server?", I have the option to choose whether I want to read the Thread or not...
I know what the Thread is about & can decide whether I want to "dive" in to help, or just read...
A subject "Please Help", makes me wonder whether it really worths to "dive" in the Thread to read or help with the problem...!
Not only that, but I might "dive" in a Thread that I can _NOT_ really provide any help, because I know nothing about that specific subject (& I might not really care to "dive" either!)...

Please, try to choose Subjects/Titles for your Threads that describe the Thread's contents!

Thanks!
