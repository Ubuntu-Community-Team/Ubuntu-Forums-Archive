### Post by Sef on 2006-04-24
It doesn't hurt to have an extra level of protection, and in fact, it is good to have it.  If you system is harder to crack, then it will more likely be left alone.  

Note: It is much harder to crack into a linux system.  Took about an hour or so on an unpatched windows system.  About 3 months on an unpatched linux system.

Get firestarter.  It's available in synaptic, Add Applications (Add/Remove in Dapper), or terminal.

### Post by Ningyo on 2006-04-24
Even on a 56k, a firewall is suggested.  You'll be surprised at how many 56kers get attacked, though the only people I know who have that problem are Windows users. (No offense to Windows users or 56 users, I've been both since 3.1 and 24kbs.)

While Ubuntu is a LOT more secure than Windows (mostly because there are more Windows users in the world, and people will attack the majority, unfortunatly), it wouldn't hurt to have a firewall.

Because, at worst, the firewall won't work.  At medium, you'll just have a small program running for no apparent reason.  At best, it actually prevents an attack of some sort, even if you never realize it.  Better safe than sorry, after all. :D
