### Post by Majorix on 2007-09-04
Do you have Turkish family members?

I am Turkish too, but I don't use the Turkish version of Ubuntu because of the following reasons:
-There is no good way of getting support if you have the menus in Turkish. For example, if you post in these forums, people will tell you to go to System > Administration > Software Sources etc, and I can't even guess what these could be called in the Turkish version, even though I speak close to perfect Turkish. The reason for this follows.
-The translations are incredibly awful. People here who know English too laugh pretty hard at the translations. Exceptionally funny translations can be found in Windows. Oh god, don't remind me of them :D
-You have to know the English words for computer terms if you want to become an expert with computers. And to do this, its best to use an English distro.
-Linux distros are only partly translated. Some apps still await translation. So its like being half-pregnant.

There are perhaps other reasons that don't pop up in my mind right now. But if I remember I will tell you.

What you can do is to find a local Linux distro that was aimed mostly at Turkish people. One such distro is Pardus. Even though I even hate its name, and everything else about it it might be your only choice. Some people like it though. So you have to try. I once tried Pardus before I started hating it, and all its menus and some of its apps were in a language of good quality. Some apps were still awaiting translation though.

Hope this helps you. Feel free to pm me for any matter regarding this. Good luck!

### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-09-04
To install Ubuntu follow the guide at [http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing)
The default language is English, but you can change that after the installation. Not sure you need this, but check the following link just in case [http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso](http://psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso)

Once Ubuntu is installed and working, go to System>Administration>Language Support and install Turkish. In the same window, you can also specify the default language. The menu layout may be different in other versions of Ubuntu, so these instructions apply to Feisty 7.04.

On the second thought, are you sure your family in Turkey is ready to use Ubuntu? Do they know anything about linux at all? You can't expect your ant or uncle to use it out of the box. Lastly, you may want to check out a turkish based linux [http://www.pardus.org.tr/eng/index.html](http://www.pardus.org.tr/eng/index.html)
I have not tried it, so it is entirely up to you.

### Post by Majorix on 2007-09-04
No problem. I see, they have to learn Turkish in the school.

Now for your problem. I believe the easiest way for you would be to install in English, and then change the language to Turkish once everything is set up.

I would suggest you install Xubuntu on these PCs since they are fairly old. Do they have at least 128mb ram? If check, go on.

Installing shouldn't be too hard for you since you will give all the partitions to Xubuntu. Make sure "Guided - use the whole drive" is selected when the partitioning screen comes up.

Once it is up and running wait for input from an Xubuntu user here. I don't use it myself so can't know where the language options lay. Someone will fill you in on that one I am sure.

And when everything is done, send the PCs over to Turkey. They need PCs. Just so you know, some people here have to work months (and don't spend money at all) to be able to buy a sucky PC. You can imagine the rest.

In your place I would learn basic Ubuntu and share my knowledge with them. You know, how to install new software, how to copy stuff, how to move them, how to make new files... I bet they don't know anything regarding that. Things are pretty much the same here like in Windows. You won't feel that bad.

Good luck. I am done for today :)
