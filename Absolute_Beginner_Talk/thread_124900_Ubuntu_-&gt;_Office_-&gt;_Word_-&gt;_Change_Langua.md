### Post by nursegirl on 2006-02-07
Hey Dimitris,

I'm going to answer your Question 2 first:
The first thing to realise is that the different programs installed on Ubuntu are not actually made by Ubuntu the same way that Microsoft Office is made by Microsoft.  So, a shortcut in OpenOffice.org's Writer might not be identical to one in Mozilla's Firefox (although more and more projects are following the interface guidelines).

That said, Ctrl+C works for me in OOo

Question 1:
You can change OOo Writer keybindings under Tools>Customize, so you can create keystrokes for anything you'd like.  That's the beauty of Open Source, things are often easy to customise, which is great, because I would never want to change languages with Ctrl+Shift, and you do.  We both get to be happy.
