### Post by Sutekh on 2006-08-07
It is unlikely as Ubuntu has a commitment to free software.  That is software that is both free of cost, and free to modify and redistribute.

The ATI drivers that PCLinuxOS comes with, while free of cost, are not free to do whatever you wish with.  Maybe one day, graphics card manufacturers will open their driver code, until then Ubuntu won't ship with them.

That's not to say that you can't install the drivers on Ubuntu, just that you have to make a conscious decision to install nonfree software on Ubuntu.
