### Post by mooscape on 2007-05-28
I have an ASUS USB 802.11bg WLAN connecting my Xubuntu Feisty (on an old  IBM laptop) to the internet.  This is a really nice solution as it is plug and play (Feisty detected, no problems). The USB format is handy as I can swap it out with my other computers.  The chip is by Ralink as far as I know. If you are concerned, try taking a laptop with Feisty on down to the shop and checking the specs. The only problem might be that manufacturers change chips from time to time.
Atheros chips are generally all good apparently. I have an HP laptop with built in Broadcom BCM4306 802.11b/g WLAN which works well on Dapper. (driver is bcm43xx). This should also work in Feisty.
