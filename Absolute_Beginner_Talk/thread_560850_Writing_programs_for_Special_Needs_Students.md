### Post by laidback on 2007-09-27
My own experience in this area taught me that Special Needs students need FAST computers, they have problems if any action isn't visible on the screen immediately. Unfortunately my software knowledge has become way out of date by many years. But I'm fairly confident that you will find that individual programs suit individual students. The ability to configure any software to meet the individual need is a high priority.

I also remember that any systems boot up reports that appear on the screen when you switch on were the source of much confusion. The student was unable to work out when he/she should be doing anything, some even found it freightening, assuming that they had made an error.

I wish you every success.
