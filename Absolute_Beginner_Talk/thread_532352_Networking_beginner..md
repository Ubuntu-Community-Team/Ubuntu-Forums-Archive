### Post by kidcharles on 2007-08-22
As a former employee of Best Buy (and for some reason a frequent customer) I can tell you that most of what those sales people tell you is often the child of their ignorance and their wild imaginations.

As far as routers go, most of today's routers are actually wonderfully designed to be configured using html so that any modern browser can be used to configure them using any operating system.

Adapters on the other hand, while pretty much any of them will work with linux (and Xubuntu in particular) some of them are really, really easy to set up and others are extraordinarily difficult. I would definitely do a little research before buying an adapter. Any decent router will work fine for you though.

### Post by kidcharles on 2007-08-22
There is a wiki for wireless cards in Ubuntu here:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported)

If you scroll down a bit on that page you can see a few entries for Linksys USB adapters with some mixed results on their functionality. The WUSB54GS is not listed specifically, but other similar models are. Do you have to have a USB adapter or can you get an add-on card? USB adapters I think tend to be a bit trickier to get setup.
