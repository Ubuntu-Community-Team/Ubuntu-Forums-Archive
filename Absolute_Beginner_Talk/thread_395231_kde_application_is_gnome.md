### Post by jem7v on 2007-03-27
KDE has lots of settings you can tinker with, usually looks shinier, and is reputedly more bloated.  In my opinion, it is a little more like Windows than Gnome is.

Gnome sticks to sensible defaults instead of overloading you with settings you can tweak, and thus has a reputation for being less powerful, but it doesn't need as heavy system resources.  It has a focus on being EASY to use instead of trying to do everything.


I prefer Gnome, myself.
