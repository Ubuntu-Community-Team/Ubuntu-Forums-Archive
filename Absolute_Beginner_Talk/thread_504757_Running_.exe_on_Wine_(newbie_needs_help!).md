### Post by Daveth on 2007-07-19
wine will set up a folder  in your /home directory called .wine. Because it has a "." in front of it it will be hidden until you do control+h to unhide it. In there is a drive_c folder, and from there on in it looks spookily like windows. Wine will also add itself to your Applications menu at the top of the screen.

The success of your venture depends on the Wine version, nature of the program you are trying to run, and what version of windows you are "pretending to use". As noted, the Winehq site has an excellent applications database which gives usability ratings to programs and is worth visiting.
