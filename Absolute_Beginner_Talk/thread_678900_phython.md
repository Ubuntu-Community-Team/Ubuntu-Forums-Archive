I myself am on a quest to learn python and I was pointed to a very nice guide by LaRoza, who has a very nice wiki with information for beginning programming- especially in python. 

LaRoza's Wiki: [http://laroza.pbwiki.com/](http://laroza.pbwiki.com/)
The Non-Programmers Guide To Python: [http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikibooks/en/8/88/Non-Programmer%27s_Tutorial_for_Python.pdf](http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikibooks/en/8/88/Non-Programmer%27s_Tutorial_for_Python.pdf)

I hope this helps. I have yet to come across a tutorial that is exclusively for designing games for beginners. However, I do know of a program (windows only unfortunately) that I started with and helped me a lot when I first started getting involved in programming. It is called Game Maker, and was originally designed by a programmer in the Netherlands, before he handed the reigns over to a third party.

Here is the link. There is a free version, which is great until you want to start doing the advance stuff, as you have to pay $20 to unlock that. (Worth every penny.)
