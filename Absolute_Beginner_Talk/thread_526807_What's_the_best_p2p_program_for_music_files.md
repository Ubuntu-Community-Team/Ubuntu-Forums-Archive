### Post by shaydee on 2007-08-16
Limewire is what i'd suggest, too. Its good for finding small files and does so reliably (if you ignore the spam).
The aMule/eMule/edonkey2000 network is great for very rare and/or huge files if you have the patience. Also, soulseek (the common linux client for that is called "nicotine" - you can find it in the repositories) is great for rare files, because you have the option to chat with people who have the same interests. But when it comes to anonymous and quick downloading of small files, then Limewire (i.e. the gnutella network) is the way to go.
Torrents are a wholly different issue, though. IF there is a torrent for what you are looking for, then this will most likely be the best way to go. The downside, however, is that torrents expire much more quickly than other p2p solutions. So its not that good for rare stuff. [Deluge]("http://deluge-torrent.org/downloads") is one of the good torrent clients.
