### Post by pieisgood4589 on 2007-10-27
If you didn't do anything to the system files, (which you probably did,) then all you have to do is go into the themes list and selecting the default Kubuntu desktop theme. If you did change the system files, then this will not work, and you will have to do a clean install, or just do the opposite of everything that you did.

PS
If you REALLY don't want a clean install, just reset the the KDE menu rename or delete the file
~/.config/menus/applications-kmenuedit.menu then restart KDE or run:

dcop kicker kicker restart 

This primarily works in MEPIS linux, which is why I am hesitant to post this up as a first go. Use at your own risk! PM me or comment with results! :popcorn::KS:guitar::popcorn::KS:guitar::popcorn::KS
