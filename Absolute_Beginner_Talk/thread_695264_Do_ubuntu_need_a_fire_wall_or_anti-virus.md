### Post by dcstar on 2008-02-12
> **articwolf939 said:**
> and if u do what are good ones?

Not really, but if you want to be warned about things like "Spoofed" web links in e-mails, then you can install ClamAV and have it scan your incoming mail.

I get regular warnings about scam e-mails that find their way past my junk filters, and I also (today) got a warning about some from eBay that had different URLs behind their links!

You will also be informed about any Virus-laden e-mails that won't affect your system (only those Windows users), so this may increase your "comfort factor" about using Linux.
