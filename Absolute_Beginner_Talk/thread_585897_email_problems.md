Hers a guide for setting up mail on evolution
[How to set up Evolution for email]("http://www.simplehelp.net/2007/08/11/how-to-set-up-evolution-for-email/")

For thunderbird you can try the method below.

Account Setup

open the Tools menu and choose Account Settings. Press Add Account. The Account Wizard should open.

Choose Email account and press Next.

Enter your name and e-mail address. Press Next.

Select the type of your incoming server. This is usually POP. Enter your server address (e.g. pop.xyze.com).

If your incoming server is POP, you may check Use Global Inbox. If you do not check this option, your mail account will have its own folder directory, including its own Inbox, Trash, Draft, etc.. Many people with multiple POP mail account prefer to use Global Inbox, where all POP mail is stored and managed in one folder directory.

You may also need to enter your outgoing server (e.g. smtp.xyze.com).

Press Next.

Enter your username for receiving mail. This is usually your mail account name (e.g. unknow) or your e-mail address (e.g. [email]unknown@xyze.com[/email]). You may also need to enter your username for sending mail (usually the same as the username for receiving mail).

Press Next.

Enter the account name (display name in Thunderbird). Press Next.

The Wizard should show you a summary of your account information. You may also have an option to immediately download mail.

Caution: if this is your first mail account and it is a POP, Thunderbird will immediately download messages whether you check this option or not. By default, Thunderbird will not leave POP mail on server after download. If you are only trying out Thunderbird, you many want to disconnect your Internet connection now to avoid having to re-import new messages back to your old mail client.

Review the summary and press Finish to complete the process.
Receiving Mail

To download mail, click the Get Mail button.

Thunderbird may ask you for password. Enter the password. Check Use Password Manager to remember this password if you don&#8217;t want to be asked again.
