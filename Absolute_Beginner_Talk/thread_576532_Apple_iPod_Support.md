### Post by Keith_Burton on 2007-10-15
There's an online petition urging Apple to support iTunes on Linux at: [http://www.petitiononline.com/itmslin/petition.html](http://www.petitiononline.com/itmslin/petition.html)

Don't hold your breath, though. Apple wouldn't even have put iTunes on Windows if Windows didn't have such a huge market share.

Here's a detailed report from someone who claims to have gotten iTunes to run under wine on Linux: [http://wine-review.blogspot.com/2007/10/itunes-73-on-linux-with-wine.html](http://wine-review.blogspot.com/2007/10/itunes-73-on-linux-with-wine.html)

I understand Banshee and AmaroK are able to sync with an iPod for managing music. 

There are other online music stores that will work with Linux. For example, Amazon promises Linux support for their store (and it works fine right now, from Firefox): [http://arstechnica.com/news.ars/post/20070925-amazon-launches-public-beta-of-mp3-music-store.html](http://arstechnica.com/news.ars/post/20070925-amazon-launches-public-beta-of-mp3-music-store.html)
