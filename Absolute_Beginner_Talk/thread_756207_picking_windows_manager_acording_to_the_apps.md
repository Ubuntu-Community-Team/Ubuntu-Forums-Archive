### Post by tvtech on 2008-04-15
I've been using ubuntu since 6.04 and had horrible and wonderful experiences with it.  I use a number of native windows applications for my work which has caused me to have to install parrallels and vmware software in order to get work done.   
     

    I also had the same quandry about gnome vs. KDE, as gnome really did the things I wanted it to do and supported me in the ways I needed to be supported, I also had some hardware incompatibilities with certain aspects of KDE.  I like you, liked the applications much better though.  So I just started installing the applications into gnome.  Amarok works fine under gnome.  I also use portions of the kolab suite and korganize because I don't care for Evolution although I'm sure it's a fine program, it's just not as intuitive, and it's too integrated I can't pick and choose easily the portions of it I want or don't want to use like I can with kolab.  it took some tweeking but I got everything to work fine under gnome and I've got no complaints at all.  KDE has by far in my opinion a more advanced application suite, but most of those applications will run just find under gnome.  Go gnome and use kde apps.  or go fluxbox and be confused for a while.  check out enlightenment [if you really want to confuse your senses]("http://www.enlightenment.org/")

it's a fluxbox/blackbox variant very different from gnome or kde it gave me a headache I uninstalled went back to gnome.

Based on your history I'd say check out all the alternatives you can always install KDE and Gnome in the same installation and pick and choose which one you want to use on any given login.  just change your session "the button at the bottom left of the screen"  when you log in. I
