### Post by Seisen on 2007-04-26
Once in while you have problems with Ubuntu, like recently their was a problem with the kernel and everybody had problems after installing it. The good thing was that the developers caught it before it became a bigger pain in everybodies side and froze the update so nobody could download it. Other problems that can happen are user oriented problems, like when people install beryl or compiz and then have problems with Ubuntu. Right now those really are not  supported by Ubuntu so you could have problems. If you don't want to have any problems with Ubuntu and lose all your business data. I suggest you stick with whats supported by Ubuntu and avoid the unsupported software. That way you have a less likely chance of breaking your system and losing your data.
