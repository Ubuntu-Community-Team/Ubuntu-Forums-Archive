### Post by Cypher on 2007-05-22
Debian and (K/X)Ubuntu are related in that Ubuntu is built on top of Debian. Ubuntu strives to have a faster release cycle (around 6 months) than Debian (around 2-3 years). Mandriva, if I'm not mistaken, is the old Mandrake which is based on the original Red Hat with a lot of fluff..

I'm not too familiar with Mandriva, so I won't speak much about that. Ubuntu is what I use and have enjoyed, I have always liked the stability of Debian and some of it's tools (apt/dpkg), but with their infrequent releases, you tend to behind the curve as far as Kernel and some of the newer features go. Ubuntu forgos some of that stability to bring you to as close to cutting edge as possible without burning up in flames. So it's a good combination. :)

KDE and Gnome are Window Managers that run on top of X-Windows (X.ORG) which runs on top of Linux. Layers and all. These two Window Managers (WM) do indeed shape the way the windows look and come with a set of applications tuned to take advantage of one WM or other. You could, however, be running Gnome and use an application meant for KDE, that means that it will bring in the KDE libraries and behave the same.

### Post by kevdog on 2007-05-23
Thought I would chime in here.  I recently made the switch from WinXP to Ubuntu after years of contemplating switch to some type of Linux OS.  Ubuntu is my first and only linux distro Ive tried to I cant speak to other distros.  As far as the original post, I would say the switch was downright easy (except for setting up the wireless capabilities and I had to tweak my xorg.conf file to make the video appear right -- the live CD was a strike out for me since at that time I didnt know anything about modifying the xorg.conf file!).  

As far as the KDE/Gnome thing -- I dont know why everyone makes such a big deal about it!  I installed both window managers with help of this guide ([http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index.php)). Although I probably run KDE more than Gnome, the great thing about having both installed is that you can make use of programs designed for both.  Ive found if you know what you are doing, the Gnome Programs are probably cleaner and do things faster, however they are not as intuitive as the KDE interfaces.  Anyway its not a big deal.  You dont have to specifically choose KDE or Gnome when you can have both.

Be ready however to spend some time tweaking in order to get wireless working for you. (Im not too sure if this is a problem with other distros).  The only other problem Ive found is that accessing compact flash card via CF adapter in a PCMCIA slot.  Although it works, its REALLY slow so its not functional.  CF cards accessed through card readers that plug into a USB slot are much better.
