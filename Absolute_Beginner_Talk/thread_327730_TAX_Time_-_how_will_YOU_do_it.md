### Post by moshuptrail on 2006-12-29
Taxes boils down to two major features: Tax Preparation and e-filing.
Tax prep is tough since laws change every year and each year's version is obsolete after April 15th.  (well, not exactly, but you get the point)  I'm not sure anyone wants to devote as much time as it would take to generate opensource tax prep software.

e-filing is something else.  It assumes the taxes have been prepared.  Only certain businesses that create tax prep software are authorized to e-file.  They charge a small fee just to let you use their software to e-file.  The IRS has a free e-file option for people earning less than $52,000 per year.

Question:  Does anyone know how much difficulty is involved in writing e-file software?  Does it change every year?  Would it make a good opensource project?  Would the IRS even allow it?
