### Post by Origin415 on 2008-03-05
Then it is definitely a DNS problem. You DNS configurations should be automatically configured by your router when you connect, so the first thing to try would simply to disconnect and reconnect to it, through netwokmanager, for instance.

If you look at the file /etc/resolv.conf, there should be the ip address of your router, which should act as your DNS server. Based on your ifconfig, that ip address is 192.168.0.255
