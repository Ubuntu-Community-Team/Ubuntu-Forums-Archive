### Post by d1337 on 2005-12-21
I've used Sound Juicer CD Ripper (Applications-->Sound&Video) to rip CD's to Ogg.  Your compression rate can make the file much smaller than 1/2 original as well.  While I am not an audiophile, I do like music and can't tell much difference in an Ogg file compared to uncompressed (maybe a little tinny).  To give you a bit of an idea of how compressed this is, I have 310 songs stored in 1.4GB on my  HD.  The format I use is CD Quality, Lossy (Ogg Vorbis audio).  Select a folder (ie. Music) that you want to rip to and let it do it's thing.  This method also maintains Artist, Album & Song Title if you are ripping straight from CD.

There are likely many apps to do the same thing...some may work better.  This has just been the simplest and most convenient way that I have found and it works for me.  YMMV.
