### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-18
1) If you really must have that suite from adobe, then get a VM solution (I recommend VMware, long time happy user) and get a copy of XP/2K (legit or not, >.>) and run a super small windows install for just those apps. Makes it easy to work on and then pull it back to Ubuntu when your done.

2) I guess if your having that much trouble with WINE, ya should look at Cegeda (again, legit or not is your choice how you get it >.>) its currently certified to run Battlefield 1942, 2142 and 2, all very well to my knowledge. Articles can be searched here at their [wiki.]("http://cedegawiki.sweetleafstudios.com/wiki/Main_Page") I don't really want to recommend a paid program, but if you really want to play in Ubuntu I guess thats the solution.

3) See 2.

4) I don't know, I don't use Audacity that often. Might want to check the dev forums/audacity site. Maybe compile a version yourself?

5) You may want [G-fire,]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=226456") it should let ya chat with Xfire folks... I'm surprised its not in GAIM by default, I guess next pidgin version maybe...

Thats it, I think I covered your issues. Have fun with Ubuntu, and spread the word we must recruit more members into our army if we are to win against MS :p.

Disclaimer: Not trying to endorse pirating anything, but it is laughably easy to get any of the above if thats your aim/you don't have money for them.
