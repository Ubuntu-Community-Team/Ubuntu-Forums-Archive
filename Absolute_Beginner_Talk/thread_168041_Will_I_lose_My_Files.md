### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-29
Will you be hit by a car when you cross the street? Well, if you look both ways, you probably won't, but you can't **guarantee** you won't be hit.

Same thing with your data. If you resize your Windows partition and install Ubuntu, in all likelihood (if you're doing everything correctly), your data will not be lost.

But do you want to be 100% sure or 99% sure? The only way to be 100% sure you don't lose your data is to **back it up**. Use 1000 floppy disks, 10 CDs, 2 DVDs, an iPod, a USB key, the internet--whatever you need to back stuff up.

I would highly advise against installing a new operating system if you have:

1. No way to back up your data
2. Really important files that cannot be lost
3. No experience installing a dual boot

That said, even if you do back up your data, I would advise using a Ubuntu live CD for at least a couple weeks before you actually install Ubuntu. The live CD will give you a chance to get used to the Gnome (or KDE) interface, and it will give you a chance to see how well Ubuntu detects your computer's hardware.

If you decide you want to do a dual boot, I highly recommend this guide:
[http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone](http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone)
