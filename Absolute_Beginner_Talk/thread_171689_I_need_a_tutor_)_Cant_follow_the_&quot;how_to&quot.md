### Post by gigi1234 on 2006-05-07
Don't give up!

Breezy Badger is the current version of Ubuntu that has been released for general public use. Dapper Drake is the next version (still in development) and will be released in June. Every six months or so Ubuntu releases a new version. Thus, Ubuntu is constantly being improved and made more user-friendly. One thing that is usually improved is device support. So, don't give up because Dapper Drake might include support for your device.

As a relative newbie I know that the initial conversion to Linux can be a struggle. It is fine if everything works. But in your case you have a device that doesn't seem to be well-supported. That is hard. When I first switched I had to learn to compile a driver from source to get my winmodem working. It took about a week to figure it out. You have to be willing to do some reading and searching, as well as asking questions when you are stuck.

Another problem is that your device may be a little obscure and therefore not many people on the forum will be familiar with it. 

Maybe have a look at this thread:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=171491](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=171491)

And also read the "sticky" posts on the front page of the absolute beginners forum. It is really isn't that difficult but like anything new you have to invest a bit of time to get going. 

Try posting your question again too. Sometimes if you try again the right person will happen to see the post. 

Once again, good luck.
