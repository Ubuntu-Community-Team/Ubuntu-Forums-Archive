First off, you can apparently get Photoshop all the way to CS2 working in Ubuntu with Wine.  So you might want to consider giving it a try.  See this:  
[http://blog.publicidadpixelada.com/how-to-adobe-photoshop-cs2-on-ubuntu-10-steps/](http://blog.publicidadpixelada.com/how-to-adobe-photoshop-cs2-on-ubuntu-10-steps/)


I have used both the Gimp and Photoshop pretty extensively.  Gimp is a pretty good program.  It does almost everything Photoshop does.  I noticed a few differences, but for most tasks the Gimp will meet your needs.  If you want to make Gimp feel more like Photoshop, there is an extension for it, I think it's called Gimp-shop.  You'd have to do a search on it.  I personally feel that Photoshop is still a superior program, although plenty in this forum are die-hard Gimp supporters.  I don't think you would feel disappointed by Gimp's limitations, because they are few.  As for the import/export factor, I haven't really tried to export to Mac or Windows, but have used the Gimp in Windows too.  I haven't had any issues with pictures edited from one, and then moved to another program.
