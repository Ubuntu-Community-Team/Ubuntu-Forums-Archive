I was wondering if it was possible to connect my Logitech x-5300e 5.1 speakers to two sound cards?

The reason for this is so that I can get audio output from my x-fi when in windoze, and audio from my built in ac'97 when in ubuntu without having to reconnect the wires every time I switch.

Any solutions to this matter are greatly appreciated.

### Post by kingcharles1666 on 2007-02-28
connect them both to an amplifier on different inputs and connect your speakers to the amplifier output.
then use the input select buttons of the amplifier to switch between computers

### Post by janz84 on 2007-02-28
I figured out a way... I got 3 reverse y-adapters from radioshack and surround sound works! The cables were originally designed to connect a modem and a sound card to one set of speakers. This seems to be an effective workaround until creative releases the closed-source x-fi drivers in Q2 2007.
