### Post by Bachstelze on 2007-05-13
There's much more that that. They're different in spirit and in objectives. Gnome aims to provide the user an interface that is as simple as possible, for example by hiding the uncommonly-used features, while KDE aims to be more powerful, and is also far more customizable.
