### Post by nalmeth on 2006-05-12
If your BIOS can't boot it, then ubuntu can't do much for you.
You can try flashing your BIOS, meaning getting the newest available one.
It's a little complicated at first, and risky if you don't follow the precautions.
You basically put the new BIOS on a floppy, and boot into it I believe.
Look up your Mother Board model and manufacturer and see if there is a newer BIOS available.
