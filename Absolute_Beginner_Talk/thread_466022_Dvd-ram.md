### Post by mcduck on 2007-06-06
Like said, DVD-RAM is just another rewritable DVD format. The interesting thing about it is that unlike CD's and other DVD formats, on DVD-RAM data is arranged much like it's on your harddisk. This allows reading and writing to the disk at the same time.. Also writing to DVD-RAM works just like writing to harddisks, so you can add and remove files as you like.

The bad side is that it has less storage space, and it's not as commonly supported as other DVD formats are.
