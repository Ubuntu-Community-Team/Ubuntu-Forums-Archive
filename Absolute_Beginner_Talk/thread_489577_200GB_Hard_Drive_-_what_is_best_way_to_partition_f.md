### Post by dfreer on 2007-07-01
(1) Depends on who you ask. In my opinion, if you are going to be reinstalling Ubuntu or upgrading, it is best to make a separate partition for your personal data, that way you do not have to back it up to DVD's or another hard drive when you need to wipe the OS.
(2) In terms of performance, keeping the swap on drive separate from the OS is best, although if you have plenty of RAM (more than 1 GB) you'll probably never notice the performance increase.
