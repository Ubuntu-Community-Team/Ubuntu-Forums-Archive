Aholiab, I'm not going to be of much help, but have a look at the link below.

[http://boingboing.net/2007/09/14/new-ipods-reengineer.html](http://boingboing.net/2007/09/14/new-ipods-reengineer.html)

It may be some time before you have support for the new ipod nano3g. In the meantime, you may be able to use [CrossOver Office]("http://www.codeweavers.com/products/cxoffice/") to run iTunes on your Ubuntu machine. See [here]("http://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/browse/name/?app_id=974"). This will at least allow you to use your own machine.

As for getting your ipod reformatted . . . . without a windows machine, I think it would be pretty ugly if even possible. However, if you are using iTunes through CrossOver, iTunes will take care of restoring your iPod.
