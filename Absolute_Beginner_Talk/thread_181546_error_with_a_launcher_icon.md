### Post by skippy81 on 2006-05-24
Im a little confused but I will try and help.  

First you need to know the command used to launch thunderbird.  You can get this quickly by right clicking on the thunderbird icon on the menus and selecting "add launcher to desktop".  Then just right click on the desktop icon, and look up its command in its preferences. 

Then just right click on your desktop and create a new launcher.  Use the command you found from the old launcher, and then click on the icon picture.  This will let you browse to the directory where the icon you want is kept. Select this as the icon and your done.

There is no need to copy any icons, just browse to the place where the icon is stored by default.
