### Post by tribaal on 2006-11-04
The software you're looking for are either Beryl or Compiz.
They replace the window management software of either Gnome or KDE, but need to run on a 3d-enabled graphical server (the very base of your desktop, what actually draws stuff on your screen).

Listing all the different HOWTOS would be quite time-demanding, so I suggest you search this very forum for either "Beryl" or "Compiz", and follow a Howto corresponding to your choice and hardware (you'll need to install 3d drivers for your graphics card, and ATI and Nvidia have differnt ways to get it working).

Beryl and Compiz are essentially the same thing (you'll get pretty much the same features), since Beryl is a Compiz fork, knowing that Beryl is the more "official" way on Ubuntu (Beryl is planned to be included in distant future releases).

Sorry I cannot be more precise than this, but I hope I "cleared the way" for your installation :) And it's probably one of the "toughest" things to do on your Ubuntu box, so you'll learn a great deal in the process, which is good ;)

And of course, should you encounter a more specific problem, don't hesitate to post a thread about it (provided someone didn't already ;) )!

Just don't give up if you run into issues, and welcome to the 3d goodness ;)

- trib'
