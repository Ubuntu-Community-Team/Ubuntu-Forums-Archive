### Post by stalker145 on 2008-01-29
> **lemon_duck said:**
> 1st Question:- are the dissimilarities between the two releases so significant that exercise instructions given 'Non-Geeks' that relate to FF will not work in GG? My experience is that most of the examples do not work the way they should and that some of the screenshots contained in the book are inaccurate.

Short answer: No. Long answer: Not really.

But seriously, if the exercises are having you use the command line, you can rest pretty well in the knowledge that *very* little has changed. The only thing I've really noticed is the deprecation of **aptitude upgrade** and its replacement with **aptitude safe-upgrade**. Of course, the latter is still allowed, but you are suggested by the OS to use the former.

Now, if you are being walked through with GUI applications, I'm sure there are a couple of things that were changed. The biggest one that comes to my mind is right-clicking on the desktop to get to themes, wallpapers, desktop effect, etc in Gutsy. In Feisty, you had to go to separate places to change each item.

All you will need is a little flexibility. Things aren't going to change so drastically that a fine book like the one you have is outdated in 6 months.

> **lemon_duck said:**
> 2nd Question:- is there a quick way of accessing threads I initiate? At present I search using my log in name.

Near the top of the page find "User CP" and "Edit Options". Scroll down to "Messaging and Notifications". There, you can decide if any further threads you create or reply to will be subscribed. Once that is done, you can click "Quick Links" and "Subscribed Threads" to find everywhere you've participated.

Check back if you need more.
