### Post by Yamataka-Eye on 2007-12-05
Hello Ubuntu Community,

This post does have a question, I'll get to it in a minute, but I'd like to praise the distro for a second first.

I'm a big newbie to Ubuntu (and Linux for that matter), I installed it on my Thinkpad Z60t about a year ago, as I was sick to death of windows and I have a few computers to play around with. My wireless never worked and I couldn't get Madwifi to work either, so I sort of abandoned it for a while (not to mention I was having a problem with the battery). 

Well, the other day I kicked it back up again and installed 7.10 gutsy and it worked right out of the box!  I had to get the restricted drivers for the atheros wifi card, but I'm even happy with that (it worked fine even with WPA2).

I'm extremely happy with the way it's working out thus far, it does exactly what I was hoping it would. 

Having said that, there are a few very minor problems that I've experienced.

1) When I close my laptop and attempt to re-open it the screen comes up scrambled. 

2) When I take it off of the dock, the screen scrambles, similar to when I close it the laptop.

I was wondering if anyone on the forum could pose a way to prevent this from happening.

### Post by victorgreen on 2007-12-07
When you are 'closing' your laptop, you are either suspending it to ram or hibernating it [suspending to disk]. The default is usually suspend to ram. Tons of ppl have issues with jacked up graphical displays after resuming from any sort of suspend. There are a myriad of fixes, search for the one specific to whatever graphics card/onboard chip is in your thinkpad.
