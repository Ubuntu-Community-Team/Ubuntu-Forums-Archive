I don't know where do you live, but in Japan the price of flash memory (and memory in general) is decreasing by time. I am a computer science graduate student and naturally I use flash memory a lot in my daily life. 6 years ago when I first bought a 64 MB of memory for something like 50$ I was very happy because I didn't need to carry many floppies with me every day. But now, having at least 256 sounds insufficient. However, I think 512 MB of hard disk sounds enough for your children. Unless your boys want to copy many MP3s and take them everywhere... For daily usage the maker is not very important, if your children try to use them carefully (specially removing them safely from the computer.). The only flash memory I had and it broke down was a Sony, which I used to think never breaks down and the warranty was not covering it too. So the chippest it is the better... Even in Japan, we son't usually use Japanese Famous Electrical Company's Memories alot, these  days.... Everywhere you go you see some Chinese makers you have never heard about them. So don't worry so much about selecting a maker. At last I think,  selecting a smaller one (size) is better... Some of them are  big and sometimes you cannot plug them to a computer.
