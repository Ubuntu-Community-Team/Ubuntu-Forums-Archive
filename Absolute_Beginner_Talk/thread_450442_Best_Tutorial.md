It is not bad at all.  I started working with Linux over a year ago.  I started with SuSE and then jumped right in to Slackware and Debian.  If you are willing to read a little and ask specific questions when you get stuck, you will get it in no time.

I strongly recommend you learn the command line, at least enough to fix things if your graphical stuff "breaks".  It is actually fun to use and still looks cool when you configure your terminal with transparency and such.  

There is free stuff all over the net too with respect to tutorials, install guides, how to's, and remember there is a wealth of info in the man pages.  Here is a fun way to research man pages from somewhere else (like work :) ) [Man Pages]("http://linux.ctyme.com/").

The other thing I did was make myself try things in Linux.  Make a few dummy files.  Move them, copy them, cat them, try commands on them.  Just remember Linux isn't really hard, it is just different especially if you have always used windows.  You had to learn windows once upon a time, so, you can learn Linux.
