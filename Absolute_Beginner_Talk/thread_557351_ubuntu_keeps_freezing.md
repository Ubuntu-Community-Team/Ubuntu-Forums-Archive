### Post by 22bsti on 2007-09-22
you may also want to check the fans are still spinning, and that there is no obstructions inhibiting air flow, or preventing the fans from spinning, depending on how much dust is in the house you may want to clean the processor heatsink, and possibly replace the thermal compound on the processor heatsink, if you didnt mess with the processor or heatsink, you can leave it, but every so often it is definately a good idea to crack the case open and clean out dust, just make sure that you are grounded when you are messing inside the case, you can buy a grounding stap for about 5 bucks, or if your cheap you can make sure to put your  keep your hand or forearm touching the metal of the chassis when you are working, just remember to connect everything back where it was, take pictures if that helps put everything back where it was.


you may also want to try memtest
