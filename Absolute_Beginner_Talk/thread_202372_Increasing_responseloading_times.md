### Post by Winblowz on 2006-06-23
Ubuntu wasn't meant to give blazing performance, it was meant to be easy to use and setup. The only real way of improving your performance is by using a Linux distro that you can configure exactly for your system.

I dual-boot with Gentoo Linux, and it is blazing fast. The Gentoo installation takes a bit, and is not for the faint of heart, but you get to tailor your system exactly to your hardware, and configure it to your liking. 

Another distro I have heard that is similar to Gentoo, and also lets you optimize your system to your hardware, is Arch Linux. I have never tried it personally, but I know that many people like it. 

Thats about the only way I can help you. I'd check them out if I were you;) 

[http://gentoo.org](http://gentoo.org)
[http://archlinux.org](http://archlinux.org)
