### Post by catlett on 2006-08-08
Someone in need of help is not a pest. This is a voluntary forum. Noone has to be here, If they do not like alot of "new user" questions then they do not need to be browsing in the "Absolute beginner talk" part of the forum. There are plenty of places to post and chat where the other members are experienced linux users.
My only suggestion to a new user is not to list 10 problems in one post. And be specific. You know your system and what is going on but noone else does. Try to give as much info about your hardware and the error that ocurred. Try to post the exact error output.
Posts like "I can't get this to work. Anyone have any ideas how I can install. It's a toshiba laptop by the way." ??? How can someone help with that information. (That was an actual post from the other day)
If you haven't received an answer, a "bump" every 6 hours or so is OK but don't post  "Anyone" "I can't believe noone can help" "I'm still waiting" every half hour. This is an interrnational forum. People are on different time schedules. The person that can help may be sleeping or at work. Even though the Ubuntu forum is very active, you cannot expect instant answers.
So be pertinent, polite, patient and  persevere,  chances are all your questions will be answered.
