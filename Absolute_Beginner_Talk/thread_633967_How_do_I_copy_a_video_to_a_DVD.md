### Post by leonidas666 on 2007-12-07
> **a.v.l said:**
> I have several videos which I want to write to a dvd. How do I connect a video player to a computer, what cables and connections do I use and finally which program should I use to do this please.
Do you mean to say that you want to use a stand-alone DVD-Recorder for recording your video? If you want to do it this way, you'll need a graphics card which can produce a suitable video signal (either s-video or F-BAS, aka Cinch). Your stand-alone dvd-recorder probably has this type of input, or a scart input. The scart input is basically just a different connector, there are adapters available where you can plug in a video and sound source to a scart connector. Then you somehow have to make your graphics card produce the proper video signal, but this can be a bit tricky on linux.
This procedure is quite complicated (and lossy, since you have the digital->analog->digital conversion), so i suggest that you get a dvd-writer for your computer (or locate a friend who has one), then you can write the video straight to dvd. Before you'll have to convert your videos to a suitable format, there are some tutorials available on how to do this. Try searching for "dvd authoring".
