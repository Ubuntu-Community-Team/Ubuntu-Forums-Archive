### Post by OffAxis on 2008-02-19
Before you buy a new motherboard...

Can you access the CMOS configuration utility?

If you fried your processor (which you probably didn't) your BIOS should be outputting a POST error code (a series of beeps) letting you know where the problem is.
Write down that code and hit up the site of your BIOS maker for the meaning of the code.

If there's no beeping, than everything is probably okay, you just need to reset the BIOS.

You may have a reset jumper on the motherboard. Just short that and your BIOS should come back to life.

If you don't have one, pull the battery and short across the contacts.
