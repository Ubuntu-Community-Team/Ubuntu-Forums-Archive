### Post by confused57 on 2005-12-31
Just wanted to say hello to the helpful and friendly community of posters in this forum.  I took the plunge and installed Ubuntu 2 days ago, I had researched Linux and had ordered Debian Sarge to install. After further research I realized Ubuntu was probably the best free distro to be had, I was pleasantly surprised at how easy it was to install on my old computer 500 MHz running win98, which crashed frequently and had to be rebooted frequently.  Have 2 other computers, one of which I built 2 weeks ago, running winXP that I use.  I don't want to start out trying everything that I read in this forum, I did install Universe and Multiverse, used the terminal and installed firestarter and flashplayer-mozilla; and I'm quite impressed with Ubuntu Linux, especially the free part and the willingness of experienced users to share their  knowledge.  Don't want to give away my age, but I'm a baby boomer  in early retirement with too much time on my hands.  By the way, I'm typing and sending this using the old computer running Ubuntu.  It's slow, probably because of the 500 MHz processor, but it doesn't crash and I have plenty of time to be patient.  One glitch I've noticed is after I logoff a session to the login screen and try to move the mouse pointer to click on "turn offf computer", the screen goes all haywire.  I can type in my username and password without actually  seeing the login screen and login into another session.  The Ubuntu experience has turned out to be a lot of fun and I'm looking forward to learning as much as my limited capabilities allow.
