### Post by RichNRockville on 2007-01-12
Just to quiet the fears of those who are trying Ubuntu.  I downloaded the .iso on my windows laptop and created the cd-rom. Went to my power computer with Pentium D 2 gig ram and a clean virgin 160Gig SATA drive and 256Meg video card.

It installed like a breeze. absolutely no problems and I figured out how to access my network printer by knowing the type and the IP for it. I was on the internet with email and browsing in about an hour.

I then went and installed Hamachi and figured out how to install ghamachi so that all I have to do when I login is to execute the ghamachi shortcut on my desktop and put in my su password.
I joined my existing windows networks and was able to browse all the shares on my computers in my hamachi network.  Put in a nickname and all is well.  I even copied my bookmarks for Firefox from my laptop in another room so that I could get to my bookmarks.  Importing them into firefox was easy.

And all this from someone who originally tried Ubuntu when it first came out and had a heck of a time getting my screen size set correctly. What a change. 

I even used VNC from my windows machines to access my main screen on ubuntu. I only had to make sure that the ubuntu question about authorizing the remote access was ok. I wish there was some way to allow it to do so without having to be at the console but at least I can access ubuntu from my windows xp computers.

I am able to access the shares on my windows 2003 domain server without problems.

Of course someone without a high speed internet connection will not be able to download the 150 Megabytes of upgrades. <G>

I do wish they made Eudora for Ubuntu..  but that's not a deal breaker.

Bottom line. Great product that works as it should.

Rich Schinnell
