### Post by A4006 on 2008-01-26
BIOS stands for "Basic Input/Output System".  It is stored on a chip or series for chips on your motherboard, and is used to configure startup passwords, hard drives, and the boot order of your system, etc. from outside of your operating system.  You can usually access it by pressing either DEL or F1 when your computer is initially starting, although your computer my use a different key/key combination to enter it.  From what it looks like in your linked article, you need to customize the BIOS settings so that anything that is distinctly made for windows is either disabled or set to work with OS X.
