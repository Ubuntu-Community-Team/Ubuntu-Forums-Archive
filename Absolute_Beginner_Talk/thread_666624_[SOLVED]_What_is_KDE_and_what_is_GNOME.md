### Post by steeleyuk on 2008-01-13
GNOME tends to be slightly more simplistic, less options but does the job. KDE tends to have more options available via the GUI to configure things. Thats a dumbed down, opinionated view from me anyway.

If you downloaded Ubuntu, you have GNOME.

When you install or KDE application on GNOME or vice versa, it requires extra libraries (software) to be installed and loaded. This increases the amount of disk space needed and CPU resources while the program is running. It is arguably better to have GNOME apps for GNOME and KDE apps for KDE but its not necessary.
