### Post by Forrest Gumpp on 2008-02-23
Have a look at this site:  [http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting](http://www.linux-foundation.org/en/OpenPrinting)

I use a Brother DCP 130C.  This runs under both Linux and Windows.

It is a printer, scanner, and copier, and has a built-in digital camera card reader.  It cost $140 AUD.

The main reason I bought this model was that it has individual cartridges for each of C,M,Y, and K inks.  Here in Australia I can buy genuine Brother formulated ink in bulk from a regular supplier trading at the local computer market, at prices way below those of cartridge replacements.  I simply refill the cartridges that came with the printer using a refill kit.  It costs about one twentieth the price of a new cartridge!

Should your particular colour printing use more of one colour than of others, with the individual cartridges you do not have to waste the unused ink when just one runs out.  Refill as required from your bulk stock.

If you decide on a Brother, it will not work on Linux out of the box.  You will have to download the Linux drivers from the Brother site, but they are available.  The printer comes with a drivers CD for Windows.
