### Post by disturbedite on 2008-01-28
kaffeine supports wma.
but its not recommended to convert from a lossy format to another lossy format.  (in this case, wma to mp3).  the reason is cuz it already threw away quality to compress your audio file and when you convert it to another lossy format that much more quality is thrown away.
if you don't know, a lossy codec is one that throws away quality in its compression.
lossless means no quality is thrown away & other methods are used compress the audio.
if you ripped from cds, it'd be a good idea to re-rip your audio to a lossy format like ogg (a free mp3 alternative that is better in vritually every way).  or a lossless format like flac.  (another free codec).

### Post by stchman on 2008-01-28
> **durdensbuddy said:**
> I am migrating my music to Ubuntu, is there a way to convert windows audio into something Kaffeine will play?

Ubuntu players like Audacious and VLC will read .wma formats.  There is no need to convert.

Make sure that in the future you rip your CDs to .mp3 or .ogg.  The .mp3 format is more widely accepted for portable music players.
