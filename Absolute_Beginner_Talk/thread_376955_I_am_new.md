### Post by annda on 2007-03-05
welcome tkafik!

some people still recommend dapper (6.06) and say that edgy (6.10) is too new, to unstable and so on. but i used both a lot and i can tell you that it is very good and nice. it has some newer stuff and i think is compatible with (a little bit) more hardware.

so get edgy (6.10) and remember, follow the instructions here:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BurningIsoHowto](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BurningIsoHowto)
some people forget that and can't boot their computers with the new ubuntu cd. and yes, burning at slow speeds is usually a good idea. but not always - some burners do better with higher speeds. if you have a cd-rw you can try any method you like and not waste a cd.
