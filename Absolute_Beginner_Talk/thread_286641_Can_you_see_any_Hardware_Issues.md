### Post by cborner on 2006-10-28
Okay, the only real end-user benefit of 64-bit systems right now (other than bragging rights) is the ability to access larger quantities of memory.

As most laptops max out around 2GB (with some really high-enders topping at 4GB), this isn't a huge issue.

Go with whatever'll fit your your budget better and will allow you to run the software you want to run.

Right now it looks like it all comes down to whether or not you want a 14" screen or a 17" screen.


As to the LightScribe thing.  Yes, you should still be able to burn disks even if you cannot get the LightScribe function to work.
