### Post by ZeroXR on 2007-03-08
Good to see another person with a Toshiba thinking of making the jump!

What model do you have? I know that with certain ones, they may work better than others.

Check the [Ubuntu Wiki Laptop Testing Team - Toshiba page]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/Toshiba") as some things may be supported and somethings may not.

Here's my [testing link]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LaptopTestingTeam/ToshibaSatelliteA65-S1762") if you want to see an example of what is working and what isn't working. Just know that you may lose some functionality...

I'll try to answer your questions below: 

1) As far as the wireless card, most of the Toshiba machines have Intel or Atheros chipset wi-fi cards and those work without a problem. If your machine has a Broadcom one, you might have to jury-rig it to work. Your mileage may vary...

2) iPod mini compatibility shouldn't be a problem... RhythmBox on Ubuntu by default will do the management. Though if you want to use something like YamiPod, you can get it at [Ubuntu Click & Run]("http://www.GetDeb.net/").

3) Edit: See r4ik's post above.

4) Webpages will work fine. Though there is a plug-in for Firefox to change what browser it is identified as, if you run into the case that a site requires Internet Explorer.

5) Gaim is the means to an end for AIM, MSN, Yahoo, and etc... It's quite friendly and much nicer than its Windows equivalent!

As for your security, check out the [Ubuntu Guide]("http://www.ubuntuguide.org") in the "Security" section for enabling things like firewall, anti-virus, and etc for network security.

As for your question about dual booting... The only way I know of is using the partitioner in the Ubuntu Live CD and having it make a partition with your Windows XP partition. Do make sure to back up XP in case things get erased.
