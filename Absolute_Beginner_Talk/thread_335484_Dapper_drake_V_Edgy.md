### Post by Scheater5 on 2007-01-10
Edgy is the newest version - it's been related to the "unstable" releases of Debian, or a "beta" release, but neither of these descriptions are entirely accurate.  However, in some ways it is less stable - that is, "edgier" - than Dapper.  Dapper is also LTS - "Long Term Support" - and is often referred to as the "enterprise" edition.  When it was developed there was an emphasis on "polish" and making it a stable and usable system.  I personally can't explain why you would have this particular error, but when you say "what does dapper have that edgy doesn't," that's the kinds of things dapper has over edgy.  
As for the fact that you have AMD64 - my advice would be to experiment with both the i386 and 64bit versions of both dapper and edgy.  If the 64 version works and suits your needs, I'd recommend that - after all, you've got two processors, might as well use them if you can.  But from the tales I've heard, the i386 version is more likely to be stable.

### Post by jvc26 on 2007-01-10
Dapper is the name for Ubuntu 6.06, Edgy is the name for Ubuntu 6.10 You have just updated all the packages within your Dapper install to the latest versions, you havent updated the total operating system. If you want to do that you can do that via the Update manager or via terminal, instructions are on the Ubuntu docs pages. If you want to get 6.10 you dont need to reinstall you can do it from within your dapper Ubuntu.

Some further Info: Dapper is an LTS release, in other words, long term support, which means they will be releasing packages for it for a few years to come. Edgy is the latest version, but it is not in beta - the testing/dev one is Fiesty, which will be released to the world in April next year I think. Edgy is less stable than dapper, or at least it has the reputation of being, but I havent had any problems with stability myself, others may well have done.
