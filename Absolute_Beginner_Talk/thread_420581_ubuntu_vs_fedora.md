### Post by smartalecks on 2007-04-23
I'm just going to say what my first impressions of Fedora and Ubuntu were when I first switched to Linux. I tried out both Fedora Core 5 (i think it was) and Ubuntu Breezy Badger (as well as SUSE) before I decided a distro to stick with. 

Community: 
I hung around each forum for about two weeks, and I think that the Ubuntu community is larger and friendlier :D. There are definetly more "Howtos" and people respond to your posts pretty much within 10 minutes.

Ease of the Distro:
Again, Ubuntu came out as winner. It installed without a problem on ONE CD, whereas Fedora Core 5 I believe had something like 5 CDs I had to burn, and then it didn't work until after some configuration.

I also had an easier time with Ubuntu. It felt friendlier, warmer, and ran a bit smoother. I liked the orange :D

Technical:
I don't really know the technical differences between the two, but I believe that Ubuntu supports more hardware, and handles video cards (drivers) and wireless internet better than other distros out there so far.


There ya have it. Hope it helps :D
Remember though, decide for yourself. Try, consider, research-- if you are going to switch completely to Linux this will affect alot in the future.

### Post by Tweedicus on 2007-04-24
Why not do what I've done put Ubuntu and FC6 on the same system and dual boot. I did this because I perceived both of them to be the main distros that were developing fast and were likely to succeed in the long run. This way you can keep up to date with both. I've been running both for about four months now with no problems. I do think 7.04 has a clear edge on FC6 now, but that's just my opinion. Maybe FC7 will even things out.

One word of warning though. Put FC6 on your system first then put Ubuntu on. I did it the other way round and it was a pain. This is because Ubuntu's Grub recognises FC6 and dual boots no problem. However, if you put FC6 on last after Ubuntu the FC6 boot loader doesn't recognise Ubuntu. It was a real pain to sort out.

Tweed
