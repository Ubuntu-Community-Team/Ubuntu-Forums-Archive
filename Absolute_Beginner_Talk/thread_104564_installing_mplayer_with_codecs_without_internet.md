### Post by bscbrit on 2005-12-16
I've just checked, and installing mplayer requires quite a few other dependencies to also be downloaded.  While it is possible, it might be quite tedious trying to work out the dependencies for your installation without using something like synaptic - which you cannot use because of the lack of internet connection.
Depending on how much time you have and your type of connection, you could (in theory!) always do a one-off copy of an online repository using windows, copy it to CD, and then install all the files on your linux box.  I'm not sure that I can even recommend this as a solution.....
