### Post by solasaurus on 2006-11-03
We could maybe help you out if you could provide a little more info. How do you connect your modem to your pc?

I connect to my modem (via a router, but that's not important) through an ethernet cable. No configuration is necessary on Ubuntu at all. However, I do need to configure my modem. I do this by opening a web browser and going to [http://192.168.1.254](http://192.168.1.254). Your modem (assuming you're connected to it via ethernet or wireless) may have a different IP address like 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1 (the modems documentation should tell you this). From there it just a case of logging in and putting in your ISP's settings and it should work.

As for speeds, there should be absolutely no difference between Windoze and Linux if you're connected to the modem via an ethernet cable as the modem and the ISP do not distinguish OS's. They just treat them all as network clients. Possibly your line test was being effected by other external factors, like amount of traffic going through the ISP at different times of day or something.
