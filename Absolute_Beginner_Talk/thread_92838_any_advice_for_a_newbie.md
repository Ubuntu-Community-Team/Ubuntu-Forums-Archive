### Post by Brunellus on 2005-11-24
Advice:

Linux is NOT Windows.  Repeat this to yourself over and over and over again.  Do NOT expect things to be the same as Windows.  Get this into your head now.

Use the Search functions in these forums--and the FindPage function on the wiki.  Everybody was new once.  Everybody has had questions.  Luckily for you, that means your question may have already been asked and answered.  Save yourself and us time and effort by searching first.  

Buy a good Linux book.  I recommend *How Linux Works:  What Every Superuser Should Know* by Brian Ward (No Starch Press).  It's quite comprehensive, fairly accessible, won't insult your intelligence, and, most importantly, is not distribution-specific.  

With a good Linux book in hand, my next piece of advice is:  Don't fear the command line.  Learn to use the command line;  it's quicker and more efficient for many tasks, and is one of the great advantages that Linux has over Windows.

One final piece of advice:  There is a "real cost of Linux" as poofyhairguy once said.  When buying new hardware, take the time to evaluate how well it is supported, if at all, by your new OS.  A bit of careful evaluation now saves a great deal of frustration later, and puts money in the pockets of those vendors who actually care about Linux support.
