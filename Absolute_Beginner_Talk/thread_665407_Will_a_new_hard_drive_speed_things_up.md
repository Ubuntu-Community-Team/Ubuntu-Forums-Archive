If the new harddrive is faster than the old, then you should notice a difference. You'd get the most noticable difference by adding some extra RAM though, but I can imagine getting RAM for such an old system might be hard.

Ubuntu uses gnome which takes up a lot of resources, you will probably get a further increase in speed if you switch to xubuntu. You can do that on your allready installed system by installing the xubuntu-desktop package.

### Post by gn2 on 2008-01-12
A hard drive that old will have a slower spindle speed and slower data transfer than a newer one.
You may not get the full benefit of a newer drive, depending on how fast your motherboard/BIOS can transfer the data.

Considering how cheap hard drives are currently, it won't hurt to try a new one.

Using a different distro may help.

Zenwalk is a good choice for such hardware, also Xubuntu 7.04.

### Post by Rewil on 2008-01-13
You should increase your ram if possible, I am writing this on a machine with a 400 MHz Celeron with 512 Meg ram. The system monitor shows about 10 to 20% cpu utilization and 165 Meg of ram in use as I write this in Firefox. I consider the performance quite good for an eight year old machine. 

My Linux partition is 25G, with 14G free. I am able to play DVD resolution videos (note: not DVDs, this machine only has CD drives) quite well. A larger hard drive would probably also help, depending on the type of use you plan to have. I wouldn't go any smaller than 20 G unless you are trying to reuse an old drive to save money. 10 G would be the lower limit for 7.10.
