### Post by AndyCooll on 2006-12-20
As far as I'm aware, if you're not using Beryl then transparency is only available in certain situations. 

For instance you can make the GNOME terminal transparent. You do that by editing the profile and under the Effects tab clicking on "Transparent Background".

You can also make the top and bottom panels transparent by editing the Properties > Background > Solid Colour > move slider to the left.
