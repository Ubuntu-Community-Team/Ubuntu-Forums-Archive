### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-11-30
Look for a tab that says PC Frequency or Multiplier.  Set it to a value higher than it is currently set to.

Also realize that your motherboard may be having problems and a simple fix is to lower the speed to get it to work probably.  More common than you think.  Perhaps they just declocked your processor to get it to work properly.  No hardware swap at all.  As computers age, the data bus will degrade (dirty connections, faulty capacitors, etc) such that a slower processor will work better.  Sort of keeping your speed below a 100 MPH for an old car.
