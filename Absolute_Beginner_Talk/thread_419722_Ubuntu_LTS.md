### Post by az on 2007-04-23
In a recent interview, Mark Shuttleworth hinted that while Gutsy will not be an LTS release, an LTS may come soon after that.  At any rate, the whole point of LTS is to have a supported platform.  There will be another LTS release before support for Dapper is finished.  And you will be able to upgrade from one LTS release to another.

For most common tasks, Dapper is fine.  I would only recommend Feisty to someone who has cutting-edge hardware which is not supported in Dapper.  Even the next LST release will have provisions for hardware driver updates.

### Post by Obor on 2007-04-23
Recent interview with Jane Silber, Canonical's Chief Operating Officer in Linux format magazine:
> LXF: Will 7.10 be one of the Long Term Support (LTS) releases?

JS: No, we do not plan that for Gutsy. We are looking at the following release, 8.04, to be a Long Term support release. We will probably make that final call in the Developer Summit in Seville in the first week of May. I think it's likely that 8.04 will be LTS.
