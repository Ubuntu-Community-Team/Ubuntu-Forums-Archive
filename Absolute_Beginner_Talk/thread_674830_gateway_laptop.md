### Post by Sef on 2008-01-22
1) Did you defrag Vista or XP before shrinking the partition?

2) If Vista, did you use its partitioner to shrink the partition?

3) Did you md5sum the download?

4) Did you burn the iso at 4x or less?

5) Instead of starting/installing, did you go down the list to 'Check CD for Errors'?

### Post by marquee moon on 2008-01-22
the sound problem is probably something you'll need to sort out after you've setup ubuntu- I had to on my Toshiba Satalite (its not that hard after doing a bit of reading around on the forums). Now the sound is working fine.  
When you install and get to the bit of the installation were your asked to choose which bit of the hard drive to install onto, chosse the button for 'largest available free space'. This'll be the partition you've just created. 

I would suggest (purely becuase this is what I did, and it worked for me...) that you use the vistas partition manager. Make your partition, then close windows and boot from the CD. 

My windows partitions also appear as 'Media..'
