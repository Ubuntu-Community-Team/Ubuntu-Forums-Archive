### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-06-03
This is not a definitive reply, but basically the packages with the Ubuntu symbol have been built or tested by the official team and are "guaranteed" to work.  Multiverse and Universe are a little more distant - ie. they are available and have been tested but don't expect the official team to take responsibility.  I also think that some of the packages in the Universe are not totally free (as in the licence).

The community exists in concentric rings around the distro - even beyond the outer reaches of the Universe there are developers and packages that will work (eg. Automatix) but don't have any official recognition.  If they live long and prosper, they may enter the gravitational field of Ubuntu and become satellites within the Universe or the Multiverse.
