### Post by Yoooder on 2007-10-01
Do you mean that you are streaming a local file to other machines, or are attempting to watch a stream?

If it's the first then your computer must re-encode the video first which can be very processor intensive--especially if you are trying to stream it at a high quality.

If it's the second, do you know the quality of the stream?  If it is comparable to HDTV then your machine will be about the bare minimum to playback the video.

If you are considering any upgrades, I would recommend upgrading RAM first as it will give you a greater performance boost than processor.  If you decide to upgrade your processor then get away from the Celeron line, as they have a fraction of the L2 cache (on-processor memory) of Pentium/Core/Core 2 processors.
