### Post by jordanmthomas on 2007-11-06
For starters, you can just help answer questions on the forums.  
Try to find solutions for problems you don't already know the answer to (it's more fun that way) and eventually you'll know all kinds of junk.

As far as programming goes, I've found that sometimes it's fun to rewrite things you know are already out there.  For example, I wrote a shell (like bash, only much much much simpler) a while back.  Also, you may find you need to do something but can't find a tool to do it.  In that case, you have the need, and need is the mother of invention.  You'll be forced into (re)learning a language and writing in it.

Yeah, I rambled...sue me.  :)
