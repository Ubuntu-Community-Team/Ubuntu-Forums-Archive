### Post by toidi on 2007-08-21
Personally I have been forced to use windows for a good few years due to some work contracts (programming security cards for hotels, large offices and the like on custom hardware that would only function in windows).


That out of the way these days I tried vista and actually enjoyed it for a few days until I realised it was pretty sluggish.  I also didn't like the way microsoft were going with their 'DX10 vista only' nonsense and their poor excuses (imho) with regards to letting xp/2k (2k I know, I know but it was a nice OS ) users have the joys of it.

I realized that windows had made me lazy.  I loved the challenges of getting games/apps to work in the old dos/win 3.1 environments.  Messing around with config files and the like, trying new and exciting software.

Ubuntu has brought some of that back to me.  Sure it worked straight away, detected all my hardware and installed a dream.  I could have left it at that, but I wanted to get under the hood and have a good tinker around with the engine (just like I did with win2k) and it hasn't disappointed in that regard.

There is much for me to 'relearn' using ubuntu and I am now relishing the challenge of making this my OS of choice, whilst being able to teach my two kids that pc's don't necessarily mean microsoft or windows.

Dual boot, give it a go and notice the positive difference that it will make to your computing life :;
