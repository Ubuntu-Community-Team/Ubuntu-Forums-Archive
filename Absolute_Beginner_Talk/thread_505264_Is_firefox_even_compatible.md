### Post by JesterDev on 2007-07-20
I honestly have not had any problems with firefox under Linux what so ever. However mileage varies. I´ve have never tried to copy my profile from windows to Linux, either but then again I don´t use windows all much anyway.

One thing to consider is that Firefox compiled under windows vs Linux is going to be different. I´m not sure how the profiles would differ but they obviously do. 

Have you tried removing firefox and re-installiing it? If your still trying to use it after your attempted changes then I would think that that is where your issues are coming from. colorzilla I imagine is a program to give your color codes? Windows and Linux tend to work differently here also. Most likely those codes are being called from a Windows specific location or most likely in a Windows related way which obviously does not exist/work under Linux.  

You should know that most of the sites you design might be run on Linux servers. So your text issues may just be related to your particular problem. 

As far as getting fireworks to run under windows try: [http://www.codeweavers.com/]("http://www.codeweavers.com/") and of course there is always Wine: [http://winehq.com]("http://winehq.com")

I used to run Dreamweaver under CrossOver Office with no major issues before I learned to code by hand. 

Other then that just try removing firefox and reinstalling it. Linux is not for everyone. It&#347; very advanced, and complicated for some people. However it is becoming more user friendly every day. I suggest doing what makes your life easier. Perhaps dual booting with windows/Linux for the time being until you get used to Linux. :) Just don´t give up entirely.
