### Post by Moop on 2007-12-21
Hi, 
The home partition is where your settings and personal data like music, videos, etc are normally stored. It's not where your applications are stored. An example would be your bookmarks and settings for Firefox are stored in /home. If you needed to reinstall Ubuntu, Firefox would automatically pick up your old settings and bookmarks. You would still need to install your applications again but most of your settings will be saved. 

I also have a 500GB hard drive and I have it partitioned 2.5GB for swap, 30GB for / and the rest for /home.
