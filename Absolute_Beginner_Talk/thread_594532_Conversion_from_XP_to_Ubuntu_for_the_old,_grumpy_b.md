### Post by Ubuntoob on 2007-10-28
I would do this for now:

Get a second hard drive or another harddrive.

Install Ubuntu 7.10 on it. Easily and painlessly, It will then dual boot with Windows that you have without messing with Windows. Then you and your people can mess with it and see how you all feel. I love it and I have been a windows guy for a long time. (I still dual boot)

I use Openoffice and did on windows too. I don't have gigs worth of docs to change or even keep though so if that is important...
One of my best mates is registered blind. He is a college teacher and his students send him their papers. (His speech synthesizer reads them to him) He uses Windows and Office 97 or something. Often he can't open a file attachment for some reason. (Many of his students use openoffice (odt) or the latest Windows files that his unupdated machine can't read).
He mails it to me and I use openoffice easily to convert it into any format he can use on windows. My openoffice can open the latest windows files that his windows can't open. So it can all work out quite conveniently. That is his job, yet I am in Japan and he is in the UK.

I guess you are limiting yourself if you think that it all revolves around your 1 gigs worth of documents and years of emails. If it really does - why change?
