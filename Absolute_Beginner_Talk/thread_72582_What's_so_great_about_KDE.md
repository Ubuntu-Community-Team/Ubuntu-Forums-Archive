### Post by aysiu on 2005-10-06
Because turning numlock on, getting truly transparent panels, and having some great native apps (KWord, Kolourpaint, KSnapshot, AmaroK) are a few reasons people might prefer KDE.

KDE is, however, slower and generally buggier (I see complaints about Konqueror crashes far more frequently than Nautilus crashes).

Please no one say that it's "because KDE is more Windows-like." I have yet to hear a convincing argument about how KDE is any more like Windows than Gnome is. Having one bar at the top in addition to the bottom bar does not make Gnome so unlike Windows.
