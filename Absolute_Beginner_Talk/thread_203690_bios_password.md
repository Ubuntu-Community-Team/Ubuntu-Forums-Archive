### Post by Solver on 2006-06-25
You open the computer case, and find the CMOS battery. It's on the motherboard and looks like an, uh, battery. A round one, somewhat large.

[http://www.mrao.cam.ac.uk/~jsy1001/dimmwit/finder/images/annot_cmos.jpg](http://www.mrao.cam.ac.uk/~jsy1001/dimmwit/finder/images/annot_cmos.jpg)

Something like that.

One way is to take it out, that will reset the password. Alternatively, near the battery, there might be a jumper labelled "CMOS reset" or something like that, you can use. Not all motherboards have one, though, so you can just remove the battery.
