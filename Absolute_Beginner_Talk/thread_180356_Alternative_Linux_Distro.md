### Post by dmizer on 2006-05-22
your answer is not so easy as just finding another easy linux distro.

linux is great because if someone comes along and finds that the system does not do what they want it to do, they change it so that it does.  that is why you have different distros.

there are so many different versions of linux because each one fills a void, or fulfills a purpose.  the question you should be asking your wife is ... what is she looking for in an operating system?  what does she need to do most often, what are her interests, what does she want to explore?  think outside the microsoft box and ask her what she wants that windows doesn't give her (other than the obvious virus/spyware issues).

if you explore your wifes needs, you will be able to provide a linux distro which can fulfill them.

edit to add:
along with the above advice, if you're searching for a distro which will make your wife happy, i see no reason to limit yourself to gnome and debian based.  there is so much more out there, and most have just as easy ways of adding software now as debian.

### Post by Blondie on 2006-05-22
I would give Mandriva (Free edition) a go, and select Gnome desktop from the installer. It has a great installer (v.g. partition manager) and very good hardware detection. It's focused mainly at newbies on home desktops, and has lots of bells and whistles to play with.

I prefer Ubuntu though. To me Mandriva is _too_ handholding, too cluttered with stuff I don't use and not as stable. In my mind it's kind of analagous to a comparison between Mozilla suite (or Seamonkey) and Firefox. I think I prefer Firefox for similar reasons to my preference for Ubuntu. Ubuntu is just somehow "smoother", and somehow makes me feel like my computer is under my control rather than the other way around.

Mandriva impressed me straight after I installed it, even automatically setting up my TV card for example, but after a while using both I returned to Ubuntu.
