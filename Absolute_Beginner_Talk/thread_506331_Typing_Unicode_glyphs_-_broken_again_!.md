### Post by mhenriday on 2007-07-21
This topic was addressed in, *e g*, a [*posting*]("http://tinyurl.com/2tbs4g") by **goat flyer** on 2 January and [*one*]("http://tinyurl.com/2qgxpl") by **John Jason Jordan**  on 21 January this year. It seems that there was a change over in the systems used to produce [***Unicode glyphs***]("http://unicode.coeurlumiere.com/") in **Ubuntu** from that used in **Dapper** to that used in **Edgy** and then in **Feisty**. At this point the method was changed to typing **Ctrl + Shift + u** (i e, depressing all three keys at the same time), then releasing them and typing the hexadecimal code for the desired character (e g, the glyph «&#367;», found in Czech, has the hexcode «0160f») and then hitting the space bar. I have used this system successfully up until a few days ago, when I suddenly noticed that the results produced were no longer those I expected, but either a little square &#5647; containing the last four ciphers (1-9, a-f) as in **Notepad** or here on the **Ubuntu** fora, or simply a blank space, as in **OOo**. I hadn't observed this phenomenon prior to installing **Flash Player** on my **x86_64** machine according to the excellent instructions provided by **pinguin** on [*this thread*]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=341727"), but the correspondence in time here may well be a simple coincidence and not have anything at all to do with the installation, as I have not had the occasion to use this particular feature recently. Now, however, when I need it, it is no longer there, which I find very frustrating indeed, as all other alternatives are far more time-consuming. Has anyone else noticed that the or she no longer can type in **Unicode** glyphs using the method outlined above, or is this problem unique to me ? More importantly, does anyone have any ideas which might lead to a solution ?...

Henri
