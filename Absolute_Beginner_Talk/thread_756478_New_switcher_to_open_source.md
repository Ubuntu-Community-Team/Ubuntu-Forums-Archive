### Post by spiderbatdad on 2008-04-15
The best choice in my opinion, is to try Ubuntu first, without making any changes to your system. This is why Ubuntu is available as a "Live CD." You can download the .iso, burn it to disk 'as an image,' and just pop it in for a test drive. [http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/hardy/beta](http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/hardy/beta)

The few pieces of hardware you mentioned sound like they will do fine out of the box, usually most laptop owners experience problems with the wifi card, but even that has gotten to be rare, I have 8 laptops that I maintain using 4 different wifi chipsets, they all worked out of the box for me with Ubuntu 7.10.
