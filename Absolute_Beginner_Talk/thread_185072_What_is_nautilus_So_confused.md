### Post by maskd on 2006-05-31
Well I guess it is confusing after looking at windows, in which the whole user experience is called one thing; "Explorer".

Nautilus is the file manager, which means it handles looking at the files in an easy and manageable way. (Eg. change view to list, change size of icons, etc).

Metacity is the window manager, basically all the programs are run inside windows", and metacity gives you the ability to manipulate these "windows", to see an example, kill the metacity process and you'll see that you lost the ability to move your programs around the screen, and lost all resizing functionality, aswell as the title and X, _ and square icons.
