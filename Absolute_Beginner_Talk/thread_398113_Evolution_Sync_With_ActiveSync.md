### Post by YolaGP on 2007-04-01
Hi Robin, I'm a complete newbie in Ubuntu, but I dare answer you because after much search and work, I managed to sync my old Ipaq with Evolution. First, I had to install Synce and Multisync, and Synce plugin for Multisync. You may find some info here:
[http://www.linux.com/article.pl?sid=05/08/17/1847227](http://www.linux.com/article.pl?sid=05/08/17/1847227) 
Here, too, but it's in Spanish: [http://www.servitux.org/view.php/page/ipaqusb](http://www.servitux.org/view.php/page/ipaqusb) 
[http://www.ubuntu-es.org/node/6567](http://www.ubuntu-es.org/node/6567)
Then you configure Multisync by adding a new syncronisation pair, in which the first plugin is Synce plugin and the second plugin is Ximian Evolution. Here, you hit options, and tell the device what you want to sync (calendars, etc). You have to configure Evolution too: (Edit - syncing options)
When you plug the PDA, Multisync will start, and you hit the Sync button.
(What really worked for me after all those installations and tests, was copying BY HAND all the info I wanted to keep, and doing a hard reset to the PDA. After that, the PDA deletes all its old associations and all the info - that's why I copied everything first - and it can start a new association with Evo)
Hope this helps.
