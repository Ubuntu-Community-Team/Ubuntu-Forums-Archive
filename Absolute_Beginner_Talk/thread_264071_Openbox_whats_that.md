### Post by Brunellus on 2006-09-24
> **h2gofast said:**
> how does it compare to fluxbox
In user interface:  very sparse by default.  Just the desktop, nothing more or less.  Fluxbox gives you a minimal taskbar, pager, and clock.  

In Configuration/backend:  Openbox stores its configuration in xml files.  Fluxbox's config files are also plain text, but not XML.  I personally find Fluxbox's config files easier to work with, but this will be a matter of choice.

As a replacement window manager for other desktop environments, particularyl GNOME:  Both will work, and I have experimented with either.  Either one is more lightweight than GNOME's default window manager (metacity).  However, fluxbox simply doesn't work as cleanly as openbox does in this role.  For users wishing to squeeze the most out of low-RAM machines, but not willing to move to XFCE or "pure" openbox or fluxbox, I would recommend the GNOME + Openbox combination.
