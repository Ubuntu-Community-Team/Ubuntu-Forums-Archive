### Post by scragar on 2008-03-23
moho is closed source and free, although I think they started charging after the name change... it's a flash program that works really well and is great fun to play around with.

GIMP has a plugin allowing you to turn multi layered GIFs into animated gifs, but that doesn't support sound.

You could always look at pencil, not as easy as moho, but open source and comes in repo's, so install is easy. [http://www.ubuntuessentials.net/?p=580](http://www.ubuntuessentials.net/?p=580)

### Post by muhkayoh on 2008-03-26
Well, strictly speaking, Moho was never free.  The demo version just never timed out, but it did insert a lot of watermarks on your finished product.  In its new incarnation as Anime Studio, the demo version times out after, I think, 15 days.

If you want to stay open source, a package similar to Moho is [Synfig](http://www.synfig.org), and though it's still early in its development cycle, you can make finished animations with it.  Here's a recent attempt at a walk cycle:

[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bpMHbRz9Ya8](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bpMHbRz9Ya8)

Whle Synfig is a vector/auto tween approach to animation, Pencil is more geared toward traditonal frame-by-frame animation.  Here's my first completed (and not terribly adult) animation with Pencil:


[http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9vz7nrFY-8](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W9vz7nrFY-8)

On a side note:  The current version of Pencil in the Gutsy repos is one update behind (0.4.3 as opposed to 0.4.4), so if there's anybody out there who likes to make .deb packages, I'd love to try the latest version on Ubuntu.  (I suck at trying to compile/make packages.)
