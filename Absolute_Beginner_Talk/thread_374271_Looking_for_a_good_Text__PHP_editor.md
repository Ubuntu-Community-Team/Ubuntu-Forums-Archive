### Post by go2dell on 2007-03-02
I don't think I really qualify as a "hard core" PHP developer, but there are a few good editors available in the Ubuntu repositories.

For quick edits, you'll note that gedit (Text Editor) does syntax highlighting for just about any language you want to use.  It's probably not what you're looking for, but it's often faster if you only need to make a simple change to one or two files.  It's definitely far superior to Notepad, but still maintains Notepad's simplicity.

Take a look at [Bluefish]("http://bluefish.openoffice.nl/") for something more full-featured.  Another popular choice is [Screem]("http://www.screem.org/").  For KDE there's also [Quanta]("http://quanta.kdewebdev.org/"), but there's no reason you can't use it under Gnome; Ubuntu will take care of the dependencies for you.

For my purposes, Bluefish has been my favorite, but I'm really more interested in speed and memory footprint than anything else.  For your purposes one of the others might be far better.  My vote would be to install two or three and compare them.  Removing the ones you don't want is pretty much quick and painless.
