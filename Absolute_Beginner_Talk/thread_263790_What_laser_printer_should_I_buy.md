### Post by houseisland on 2006-09-23
If you are looking for a good commercial grade monochrome laser printer, I would choose HP over anyother brand.  Lexmark too often = pain.

If you are looking for an inexpensive item, I would monitor the Brother web site for your country and watch the rebate section.

Currently in Canada the HL-2040 and the HL-2070 have $60 and $50 rebate promotions respectively:

[http://www.brother.ca/en/company/promotions.asp](http://www.brother.ca/en/company/promotions.asp)

These put the cost of the printers into the $90 CDN and $130 CDN price ranges -- if you shop smart.  Both are nice printers.  The HL-2070 has a print server.

TN-350 toner cartridges run about $61 to $68 CDN for about a 2.5 K yield at 5% page coverage -- not great economy by comparison to a business class printer using good quality 3rd party cartridges, but it is OK.

When the drum eventually and inevitably wears out on these grade of printers, whether Brother or another brand, it is a toss of the coin whether to replace the drum or replace the printer.  Often it is cheaper to replace the printer if there is a good promotion on.

Dapper currently has no drivers for these models (and I gave up on trying to install Brother's Linux drivers -- may revisit later); however, both work fine with generic PCL6 drivers.

### Post by suhasrao on 2008-05-26
Laser printers are PAGE PRINTERS, which means the entire page is  
processed and stored in printer memory before it is printed. The whole  
page or at least the printer stored image of the page or part page is  
printed at once without any more need for data flow from the computer. 
 
Inkjets and dot matrix printers are LINE PRINTERS and a line or part of  
a line is all that is stored in memory in the computer.... it prints  
either as fast as it can, or as fast as the data can be sent. You can  
get herky-jerky printer head movement.
