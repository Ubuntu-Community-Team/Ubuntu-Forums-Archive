### Post by nalmeth on 2006-02-19
> I think I will have to do this for some of the games I play anyway (despite not wanting to). But if I can't have an OS that does everythink I want then whats the point? I may as well stay with windows and get the functionality without the security.

The only functionality you give up is having your games working out of the box. Remember there are ways of getting your windows games working with ubuntu. Although I don't really support the project, Cedega does a fine job of getting all your games going, and only costs $5 a month, 3 month minimum. After the three months, you can stop paying for cedega, and keep playing. You just won't get the updates as new games are enabled.. Also, some of your games might work in wine, and [this site]("http://liflg.org/") has installers for games with native ports for linux. Not to mention open-source games. 

Don't let me discourage you from gimp because I found it difficult. I rarely ever edit images, and my difficulties come from laziness more than problems with the interface, ultimately. Why don't you try Cedega's free trial, and try gimpSHOP. I can't speak for you, but a learning curve won't hinder your work in the long term. You might find there are things you can do with gimp that you can't with photoshop. Not to mention, there is crossover office for running Office and Photoshop, etc

Also for your Tv Card, there are a number of apps that you can use: MythTV TVtime, and mplayer/xine/totem can watch your TV, assuming you have your video card setup.

You can have all the functionality with ubuntu, and $15 for gaming is miniscule compared to the cost of your windows license, albeit you have already paid for it. But will you buy the vista license too?
