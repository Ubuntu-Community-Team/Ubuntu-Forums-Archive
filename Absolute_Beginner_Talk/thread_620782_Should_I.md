### Post by quickshade on 2007-11-23
First off Wine isn't that great for gaming. It gets the job done but Linux support for gaming just isn't here yet. Developers aren't porting games over and the drivers for video cards are just now starting to be able to support linux way better. But it's not perfect. Keep XP for that as thats the one thing it does have.

Overall I love Linux. But I also love computers...so it may just be me. Everyone will have a different time with linux so really you can't expect someone here to tell you it's awesome, because what's awesome to them isn't to others.

I can tell you what I liked the most. First off you can change how everything looks, which is what I've been waiting for. Just want a "start" button. You can have it. Want only a clock on your desktop...good. Everything on your desktop is able to be customized. 

Another thing I like is most things are free software. Want a word program....free. Good music player....free. But even better is that you can choose for 10-15 different programs all of which have the same concept but achieve it via different ways. That coupled with a one stop shop to download tons of software means you don't have to search the internet for something thats right for you. Just search the software sources.

The last thing I love (I have a million reasons but just listing some) is that all your updates for every program is done though the OS. Meaning no more download new programs or switching to different versions. Update your computer once a day and all your software is up to date.

I'm not sure bout support but I'm sure someone else here knows more about the linksys stuff than I do. All I know is run the live CD. It's the best place to try everything. BTW you came to the right place. The community is huge and supportive so whatever problems you run into don't be afraid to search (or ask) on these forums.

### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-11-23
Hi. Ubuntu should run very well considering your your hardware, but as you said yourself, the Linksys wireless card could be tricky to set up; these [two]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs") [pages]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HardwareSupportComponentsWirelessNetworkCardsLinksys") (from the [Communtiy Documentation]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/") project) can provide you with more information regarding the wireless card. You could probably find more concrete answers in the [Networking & Wireless]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=136") section of these forums.

Regarding games under Wine, the [Gaming & Leisure]("http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=93") section contains loads of information and guides for setting up many games; just bear in mind that the Wine project, while a wonderful tool, doesn't advance exceptionally fast. There probably are ways to get your Evo joystick working, but I really can't say for sure, as I only play a couple of games, and use the keyboard and mouse for those.

Gimp is very powerful, but finding if it's the right tool for you depends on your level of expertise in Photoshop, if you are dependent on some of its plugins (which may not have native equivalents in Linux), how intricate the work you do is, and your willingness to learn the UI. I'm not a Gimp power user, so others will be better suited to give you a clearer answer there.

Regarding this:
> On to the last concern. Is linux better than windows? I don't hate windows, but there are many things about it I don't like. It's slow, it's virus-ridden, it takes to much CPU, it has design flaws, Windows Corp has their head right up their butts, but I've never HATED it. I've just had issues that instead of me working out, I worked around because that was the only way.
You'll find that the vast majority of Ubuntu users don't hate windows either: for us, Linux is just a better OS for our day to day use.

> I know linux says it's exactly what I want windows to be, but would it really be worth switching? Thats the one thing that REALLY never made me want to switch. I didn't think it would really be better. So here's my question: Do the success of Ubuntu outweigh it's failures in comparison to Windows XP?
My answer on both would be a solid "Yes". That, however, is something you have to decide for yourself. Give the Live CD a spin, get to know Ubuntu before installing it, and you'll have a very good idea of what the installed system will be like. Just keep in mind that some hardware may not work in Live CD mode (like some wireless cards), but you most likely can get it working once the OS is installed.

And welcome to the forums!
