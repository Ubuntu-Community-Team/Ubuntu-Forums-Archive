### Post by JBrehm on 2007-08-15
How do I make it so that when I scroll through something (like a webpage) using the scroll wheel on my mouse, it scrolls through everything faster with less actual scrolling (if that makes ANY sense).

### Post by nichipet on 2007-08-15
I don't know about the first question, but as to the second one, I assume you are in Gnome since KDE defaults to single-click. In Gnome, you can set it so everything, not just desktop launchers, will open with one click. Go into Nautilus, then Edit:Preferences, click on the Behavior tab and change it from "Double click to activate items" to "Single click to activate items." If you want only desktop launchers to do that, I'm not sure it's possible.

### Post by mgmiller on 2007-08-15
Instead of increasing the scroll speed, have you tried just clicking down on the scroll wheel (don't roll the scroll wheel, push it straight into the mouse)  and then sliding your mouse up/down/sideways?  The further you move the mouse, the faster the page will scroll.  I use this all the time in both Win XP and Ubuntu.

To turn on the feature in Firefox, click on Edit > Preferences and go to the Advanced tab on the top, then click on the General tab and make sure in the Browsing area that both "Use Auto Scrolling" and "Use Smooth Scrolling" are checked.
