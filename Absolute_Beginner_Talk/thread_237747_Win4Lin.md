### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-08-16
The closest thing you'll find, insofar as I know, is Wine (available through the repositories), which -theoretically- runs Windows apps in Linux.  It's not perfect and is under constant development, so you might want to go to [http://www.winehq.com](http://www.winehq.com) to make sure you're downloading the most up-to-date version.  I think they're up to 0.9.19 now.

If Wine isn't your cup of tea, you can try Win4Lin for free (gratis) by requesting a trial period.  They're pretty good about giving them out to anyone who asks.  But it isn't open-source.
