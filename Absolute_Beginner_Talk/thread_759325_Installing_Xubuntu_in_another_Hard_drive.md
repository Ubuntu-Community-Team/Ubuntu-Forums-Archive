### Post by DBrocks on 2008-04-19
I myself have succesfully dual-booted XP and ubuntu (XP had to stay. My bros hate ubuntu :(   ). So get yourself a hard drive. Either SATA or IDE. Hook it up as a slave. Boot the Ubuntu disk, and click install. Then choose to install to the new drive you just hooked up as a slave. (Remember to choose the right disk, should be the new one, NOT THE XP! You can also just remember the hard drive sizes. If XP is isntalled on an 80 GB, and you get a new 120 GB for ubuntu, install to the 120.) Ubuntu should do the rest. It worked great for me. Just DO NOT confuse the 2 hard drives, and wipe your XP. Once you install, it wil install a boot menu (GRUB), and it will take care of the rest. Good luck man!
