### Post by ts51 on 2007-06-24
First off, welcome to Ubuntu! 

As long as you are not an extreme gamer, Ubuntu should do what you want it to. It has a built in office suite, internet, and  image processing software. You may want to check to make sure all your hardware is compatible with Ubuntu, though. Wireless can be a hassle in Ubuntu if you have unsupported cards. If you post your hardware, someone on this forum should be able to tell you if it will work.

The answer to number 2: 

I believe that iTunes will not work on Ubuntu. Even with the compatibility layer of WINE, this is not possible. You could possibly transfer your music that wasn't bought in the iTunes store to Ubuntu and play it with another player. There are also programs that allow you to manager your iPod. I recommend  that you just use iTunes for your music/iPod needs.  

Just my thoughts, 

ts51

### Post by punx45 on 2007-06-24
after resolving any wireless issues you might have with the newly ubuntu machine, you should be able to share directories between the two machines using SAMBA.   long story short, make the itunes library directory available to the ubuntu machine, and play the music with the player of your choice.  assuming most of the music you have is not from the itunes store that is...

you should check out macosxhints.com and the apple support forums also.   you can find lots of info about itunes playing nice with different systems.
