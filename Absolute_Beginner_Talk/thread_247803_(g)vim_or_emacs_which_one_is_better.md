### Post by IYY on 2006-08-31
Advantages of vi: 

[LIST]
[*] You don't have to install it; it's always present in any *nix system, no matter how new or old. Even Macs have it. It functions more or less the same way on any machine you run it on.

[*] It loads slightly faster.

[*] It follows the old Unix philosophy: do one thing, and do it well. The old joke goes: "I love Emacs; it's a great operating system. Too bad it's missing a decent editor."
[/LIST]

Advantages of Emacs:

[LIST]
[*] It's a part of Free Software history; the first real program of the GNU.

[*] It's more customizable.
[/LIST]

Advantages of both, over standard editors:


[LIST]
[*] Great syntax highlighting features for coding.

[*] Incredible editing speed, once you learn all the shortcuts and position your fingers correctly.

[*] No need to use a mouse.
[/LIST]

Disadvantages of both:

[LIST]
[*] They take a fairly long time to learn.

[*] They are not what most people call "intuitive". You have to first get into the mindset (and it's not the same mindset for vi and Emacs) and only then it will make sense to you.
[/LIST]
