### Post by EagleRock on 2007-06-14
Hello all,

I installed Ubuntu 7.04 Feisty Fawn last night on my old Dell Inspiron 8600, and am quite impressed with the Beryl Window Manager so far.  However, I had one issue that I cannot seem to fix.

Whenever I open a pop-up window (such as the save window in GIMP) or I open a new program (such as from the Applications Menu), they never come up on top of the desktop.  If I have, say, Firefox selected in my window then I go to open another program, it always shows up behind this window.  I have Googled this all over and have also searched the forums (and all the Beryl settings for that matter), but I have not come across anything that would change this.

Can anyone direct me in the right direction?  Sorry for the n00b question, but I am new to Beryl.

Thanks!

---

### Post by BlahMan_of.Doom on 2007-06-14
On your taskbar, right click on all of the items that are there, then uncheck the "On Top" option.

---

### Post by EagleRock on 2007-06-14
I checked all the windows I currently have open, but nothing is selected as "on top."

I noticed that the new window is not behind all the windows I have open, just the one that is currently selected.  All the other windows do stay behind the new window that pops up.

### Post by Nekiruhs on 2007-06-14
Go into the main config menu for berly. Change the focus stealing prevention option to low or none.

### Post by EagleRock on 2007-06-14
