### Post by skippy81 on 2006-05-14
I speak not from expierience here, but I believe the difference is that Beagle is indexed.  

Beagle will work in a similar way to google desktop, it searches your disk regulary and keeps track of changes - it may even run a service which tells it exactly when files change.  

When you do a seach with Beagle it is pulling the results from its own database of the contents of your disk, meaning that it will return results instantly (almost).

Using the standard search in gnome is basically like grepping the entire contents of your disk, it goes through the stuff on the disk - it doesnt retain a list of where everything is.

In short - if you have tons of files and do loads of searching use beagle.  If you dont search much or keep an organised disk then use standard search - you wont have the overhead of a service running, and you wont have to sit there for an hour while beagle does the initial search of your disk.

MS windows has a built in indexing function - most advanced users prefer to turn it OFF.  So personally i wouldnt bother with beagle.
