### Post by anaconda on 2006-06-05
Well I would have to say, that it depends. How old is the IDE-drive? how much "internal cache" (sorry dont know the english word but usually it is between 2-16MB) does it have?

IF the IDE drive is old and slow or hasn't got much "internal cache" then newer SATA-drive would definetely be a lot faster.

If both driver are "new" then the actual difference is quite small..
