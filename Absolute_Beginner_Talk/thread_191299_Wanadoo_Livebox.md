### Post by keith whitehouse on 2006-06-07
Hi Joeone,

I originally had Wanadoo at £17-99 a month, and that was over a year ago. A friend said that the going rate now was only £14-99 a month, and he suggested that I upgrade to the Livebox for the same amount of money. They sent me the Livebox (giant thing), I followed the instructions in WindowsXP Professional and it installed without a problem. 

I had been playing about with Breezy Badger 5.10 for about 4 weeks and killed it a few times and had to install it again on no less than 7 occasions but I was not too worried as I knew Dapper Drake was out on the 1st of June. I did NOTHING at all. Dapper found and installed it.

As to the speeds I can't say that I have noticed any difference from this new wireless setup against when I was running the Alcatel Speedtouch 330. I do not download vast amounts of programs etc, I only download what I need and depending on how much you download you may get penalized. I have never had this problem.

best regards keith

### Post by stuh84 on 2006-10-06
Thought I'd update on all this. I work in a call centre that deals with Orange Broadband, and I'm doing both customer support (officially) and tech support (unofficially until I'm moved onto that department). 

If you are on the Wireless and Talk packages, you will have download limits unless you are on the Max package (although that may be changing soon, thats all I'll say on that), the d/l limits being 2 gb a month.

What I'm going to be trying to do is get the support lines to officially support Linux and OS X, because I'll be using them both myself (already using Ubuntu now, and OS X in the near future). Even if it means coding utilities myself to get this working, I'll do it, because I'm sick of telling people we can't officially supporting them. However, the Livebox's are just basically wireless routers so if you have a wireless card then you should be able to connect to it as normal. Also you should be able to do an ethernet connection to it too, just can't guarantee the USB will work with them.

What I will say is I'm going onto Orange as an ISP in January, and will spend my nights getting it working with as many distro's and different PC's as possible so I can set about writing the support articles for our support lines, and like I said, get them officially supported, because its just madness ignoring OS X and Linux.
