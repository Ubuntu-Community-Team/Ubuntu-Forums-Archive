### Post by Brightbelt on 2007-08-31
Hi,
I'm posting this in case anyone else has had difficulty (like me) finding the good Open Office icons in Gnome. They are easy enough to find in KDE - you right-click the default Open Office icon on your desktop and choose 'properties'. Then click on the present icon there and it takes you straight to an icon depository.

In Gnome they are harder to find. Here's how to get there: Click Places/Computer.
Double-click 'Filesystem' and go to this directory: user/share/icons/hicolor/48x48/apps

It is tempting to think you can find them in User/Share/Pixmaps, but they are not in the pixmaps folder.

I hope this helps someone. I mean the regular, default Open Office icons that come with Ubuntu are ugly with a capital 'U'.

Thanks, Frank B.
