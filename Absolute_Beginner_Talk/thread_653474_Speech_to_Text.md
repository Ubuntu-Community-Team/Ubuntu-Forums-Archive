### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-12-30
Short answer : No.

Long answer :

You can run Dragon Naturally Speaking on wine. It will do the basics (speech -> text) but not all of the (Dragon) options work.

[http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=2077](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=2077)

Version 7 is most viable at the moment ....

There is also Sphinx. 

[http://cmusphinx.sourceforge.net/sphinx4/](http://cmusphinx.sourceforge.net/sphinx4/)

I think sphinx has come a long way, but last I looked sphinx was not ready for prime time ...

You can look at this link, it is somewhat outdated :

[http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Speech-Recognition-HOWTO/software.html](http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Speech-Recognition-HOWTO/software.html)

I do not think IBM Viavoice is viable.

And here is the Ubuntu link : [http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=2077](http://appdb.winehq.org/appview.php?iAppId=2077)
