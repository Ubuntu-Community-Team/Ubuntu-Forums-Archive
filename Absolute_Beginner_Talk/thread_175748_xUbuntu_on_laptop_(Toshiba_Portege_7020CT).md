### Post by mybootorg on 2006-05-13
Getting Ubuntu (or Linux in general) installed on a laptop is very doable.  But getting all of your devices to work properly -- especially video, screen resolution and Wifi -- these are a fairly challenging puzzle that will require lots of research and reading.

Many of your problems can be resolved by Googling "ubuntu Toshiba 7020CT".  You will find quite a few webpages like "Linux on Laptops" where people post their experiences installing Ubuntu (or Debian) on your very model.  Sometimes it doesn't even matter if it's your exact model #.

For questions such as "getting to my Windows pc over the network" you're going to want to read up in the "How To" forum of ubuntuforums.org.  You will find several tutorials on accessing Windows shares.  And also, on what's achievable with regards to XP's NTFS etc.

Hope that helps.

Craig
