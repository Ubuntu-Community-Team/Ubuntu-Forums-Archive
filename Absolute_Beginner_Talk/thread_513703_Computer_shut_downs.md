### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2007-07-30
Ubuntu might simply be using more processing power than your windows setup did.  If your processor supports scaling (like intel's speedstep for mobile processors), you should enable that.  This helps to keep power usage and heat production to a minimum.  Also, it *is* summer which means because of the higher outside temperatures, computers can be more likely to overheat.  I happen to have a laptop that runs very hot, and it's even worse in the summer.
You can open your tower and clean out dust, and pulling it back from the wall might help a bit so you don't get a static pocket of hot air.
