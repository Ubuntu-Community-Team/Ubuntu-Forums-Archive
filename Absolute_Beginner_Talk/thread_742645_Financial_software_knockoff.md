KmyMoney is nice, I use if for my business and personal taxes.
can be found in the simple gui search Applications/Add Remove


I setup my chart of accounts very easily, I can add additional banks etc.

It is suppose to be geared toward personal banking etc. but I'm not an accountant and don't understand many of the terminology in most of the accounting packages.

KmyMoney was the simplest to use in my opinion.

tips for small business.
I setup a petti cash asset account just like another bank account so I can track money that I actually cash or withdraws from the bank that I plan to use for a specific purpose such as: purchase items from an auction etc.

But sometimes the actual withdrawl amount does not match the actual amount of the auction/item purchase which at times must be in cash. And sometimes I purchase nothing at all and now I either have to put the money back in the bank or hold it and perhaps spend it and keep receipts.

But how to account for all this because the bank only shows 1 transaction of a withdrawl.

Basically I'm not sure if this is the way to do it, but in order for my transactions to match my deposits in the bank accounts themselves basically I use a petti cash account as sort of a cash in limbo account. I setup a petti cash asset account.

And instead of trying to have multiple transactions of funds going out of the bank as a withdrawl, then try to match some receipts against this withdrawl which is an accounting problem for me.

What I do is create a bank transfer which will show transfer of funds as a withdrawl from the bank account, to the petti cash account, then you can spend the funds from the petti cash account and you will have the reciepts to show your spending of that cash.

And any funds left over that you may not have receipts for or if it just got spent on personal use. Then you can create a Partner Contributions expense which means the company spent the money, and gave it to you, or whoever for a partner contribution which is funds that you will have to account for on your personal accounting, and if it's considered an profit for you personally then you would pay your share of taxes.
But in any case at least you show your withdraw from the bank that matches your statement in your accounting as a withdraw of funds and transfer to petti cash, then you will show your petti cash getting spent with receipts to prove it, and also any left over funds that you took personally will show as partner contributions. in your accounting package.
This is also easy to figure out your personal income because you can just look up the report total partner contibutions and that is what you got.

But if you have more then one partner then you would need another line item such as partner conribution john, and/or partner contribution jane etc.

Anyhow thats how I do it with KmyMoney and I love it.

Also I store and open the saved KmyMoney file on my server so it's on any computer and KmyMoney will open the file without any problems with share

only other tip is don't forget to save your work periodically while working in KmyMoney, if you have a power surge or any techie problem all your entries will not be saved.

For example if you just manually entered a bunch of entries from your bank statement and you forgot to save, or simply had a power outage or something. Basically it will be like you never did all that work. KmyMoney will pickup from your last save ??

So I usually hit the save button every 10 entries or so.

also KmyMoney can import the data from your bank and paypal I use this for my whole business ebay business etc. ?

I'm sure gnucash and the others mentioned are better for business but I just couldn't understand them as well as I could KmyMoney.

Thats my opinion.

I hope this helps.

OOP Sorry it's called KmyMoney LOL

Go to the menu item at the top of the Ubuntu desktop = Applications/Add Remove

to the right select to show (All available applications) and in the search just type kmymoney

Then simply check the box and at the bottom Apply Changes.

I hope this helps.

You can continue adding data for years and print your yearly report.
or
What I did also was to create a template with a chart of accounts as a template,and use that for each year
Then I only have a file for each year instead of one big file that keeps getting bigger.
I don't know if that makes things harder or not, but when I import my data it seemed easier to work with one year and not worry about any data from the previous year getting over written.

You could also use your previous year as a template for the new year, and just delete all the data, and save as a new file so you don't overwrite your previous year but all your chart of accounts will stay the same and you'll have a fresh new year empty file to work with. It's a little bit of a pain to delete all those entries, but once you do you can save that empty file as a template also and just use it every year.

I guess there are many ways to do it, but it's all there.

Also if you plan to save the file to a server or something I would recommend LinNeigborhood for that, and you can open the file with KmyMoney instead of moving the file from the server to the desktop just to use it.

I do that because Gnome or KDE do not have very good network transparency and I don't know how to set it up to work exactly like my shortcut symlinks works etc.
I'm sure I can edit fstab and various things, to make shortcuts to my server samba shares, but I'm not fluent enough to get that done properly. 

Anyhow I hope this helps

If you need any help I'm happy to share what I know.
