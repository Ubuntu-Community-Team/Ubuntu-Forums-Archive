Depends on what you mean by "supported."  There are chat clients that work in Linux for those chat networks, as well as Yahoo, ICQ, and AIM.  But most of them don't have a version of the official client.  In GNOME I like [Pidgin]("http://www.pidgin.im/"), and in KDE I prefer [Kopete]("http://kopete.kde.org/").

Many of your other "important programs" will probably have a Linux equivalent, frequently fully compatible.  For schoolwork, for example, OpenOffice will probably do everything you need MS Office to do.

Games are mostly a Windows thing, so you'll have to run them in [Wine]("http://www.winehq.org"), or one of its branches, like the commercial product called [Cedega]("http://www.transgaming.com").

In my own experience, the best way to figure out if Linux is for you is to just take it for a test drive.
