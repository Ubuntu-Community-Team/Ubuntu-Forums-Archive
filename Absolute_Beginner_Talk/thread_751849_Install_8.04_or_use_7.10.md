### Post by Linux&amp;Gsus on 2008-04-10
I would either go with 7.10 or wait for another 6 weeks or so. Every new version can have issues that is sort of normal. No one has all possible hardware combinations to test every possible scenario.
So, if you want it know then stick with 7.10, well tested and problems are generally solved. Or, if you can / want to wait then get a hold of 8.04 after it had been in the wild for a while and the developers had a chance to iron out some issues. Of, course if you like to be an early adopter get it when it's out. But I wouldn't recommend that if you are new to Linux.

### Post by tact on 2008-04-11
if you install the beta PLEASE do not forget that there are several lots of updates every day and some of those updates break systems or applications.  What works today may not work tomorrow.  What does not work today may be fine tomorrow.

The team does really good work so the breakages are (for me at least) minor and infrequent.  But its foolish to ignore the warning posted here:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=305](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=305)

"HARDY HERON SHOULD NOT BE USED FOR YOUR DESKTOP UNTIL IT IS RELEASED IN APRIL 2008
If you decide to use Hardy Heron now, then you are doing so at your own risk, and with the risk of major breakage and possible data loss."
