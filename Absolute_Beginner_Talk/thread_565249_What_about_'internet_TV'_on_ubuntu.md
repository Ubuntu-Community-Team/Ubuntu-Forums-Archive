### Post by djchandler on 2007-10-02
> **bliffle said:**
> How does one connect to and view 'internetTV', i.e., TV channels served on the internet? Does VLC do it? Does one need to DL special software?

You can get Miro, a video aggregator. This will at least help you find what is available if nothing else. Got to [http://www.getmiro.com/](http://www.getmiro.com/) to see what it's all about. There is a download for Ubuntu.

Once I found the websites with video or podcasts I liked, I removed Miro, but kept the encoders and decoders. The main application had a tendency to hang on my system or network set-up. It also wants to load immediately at startup of your account, which I disallowed, which may account for its flaky performance on my system. Now I just go to the website, download the videos I want, and watch them later with VLC, Totem, or RealPlayer.

You're not going to get a whole lot without paying for it. The cable/satellite channels just promote what's current on their satellite feeds. But there's a lot of stuff for those of us who miss TechTV (which was swallowed and dismantled by G4.) Leo LaPorte, Patrick Norton, Kevin Rose, John Dvorak, and a bunch of others have tons of geeky stuff available. There's good stuff from NASA and JPL too.

DJ
