### Post by Teamgeist on 2007-08-11
Hi.
I may suggest not buying an ATI Card if you can avoid it somehow.
ATI drivers are bad and I mean really bad.
I don't know exactly about the x1900 in that laptop but as far as my friend's  1900 XT on his desktop i could not get Beryl (or any other compositing window manager) to work.
The drivers from ATI don't support AIGLX which is default in Ubuntu. You will have to use XGL and configure that first.

So if you can buy an Intel Chipset, do that. Those are all supported out-of-the-box.
I use Compiz fusion (the new Beryl and the default in the next Ubuntu in October) and have used Beryl with a 4-year-old Intel Centrino with Intel on-board graphics. Functions very well.

If you feel like you need more graphicspower then go for an NVIDIA Card.
Those drivers work, even though they're closed source, very well with Ubuntu, are easy to install and support AIGLX.

So my advice would be not to buy an ATI 'cause there will most likely be problems.
