### Post by emarkd on 2008-01-27
It's usually not painful at all unless you have some hardware that is not linux friendly.  You may want to search around and see if your video card and wireless (if you have it) are compatible.

I've heard that CS2 runs great in Wine, but I've never tried it.

Anything 3D that uses DirectX is a no-go in Linux.  You'll have to keep a Windows partition around and dual-boot for that one, unfortunately

### Post by mortsahl on 2008-01-27
Photoshop on Linux?  Qualified no.

You can run old versions of Photoshop (Photoshop 7) under CrossOver Office v6.x (commercial software).  Not sure about wine.

You can run PhotoShop CS3 under VMWare.

Games, I'm not a gamer, so I can't help there ... likely Cedega will run quite a few.

Cedega and CrossOver Office (now renamed to CrossOver Linux) are commercial variations of wine.
