### Post by erfahren on 2008-02-07
oh - a couple of other quick notes... just a couple things from my own experience.

open Applications > Settings > Settings Manager and then go to "Desktop Preferences" - see where it says "Allow Xfce to manage the desktop" - sometimes messing around with things will cause that to become unchecked. If you have any strange configuration problems you might look at that to ensure it's checked.

Xubuntu seems to me to be a little more picky when it comes to saving the session - when you get it set up the way you want make sure the option to "Save session for future logins" is checked in the shutdown dialog. If you happen to do something to mess everything up you can *uncheck* that when you log out - and when you log back in it should be back to normal.

I used Ubuntu for quite awhile before trying Xubuntu - after the first couple days of using Xubuntu I messed it up so bad (and wasn't familiar with it enough to fix it) I ended up reinstalling. - lol - It *is* a little trickier to configure than Ubuntu but you'll get used to it!
