### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-05-07
There are some "Golden Rules" to remember when re-filling inkjet cartridges.

1. Only use premium grade ink. There are some companies out there who sell cheap ink and that is exactly what it is.

2. Never let your cartridges run dry before re-filling. I always re-fill when around 50%.

3. Depening on your cartridge type, some need a slight negative pressure inside to hold the ink. This can usually be achieved by slightly squeezing the cartridge before re-sealing it.

4. Cartridges don't last forever. If you can get between 6-10 re-fills, your doing well.

I have a HP psc-1350 all-in-one and have only just replaced the ink cartridges after 2 years use and about 20 re-fills. You can imagine the savings I have made!

Most inkjet cartridges count the drops, to keep a track of ink levels. Some re-fill kits come with a chip re-setter. My particulur HP cartridges just require you to put bits of sticky tape over a couple of contact pins and plug the cartrige in and out 3 times to re-set the counter on- board the printer.

If you have a gentle touch and want to save some money, go ahead.
