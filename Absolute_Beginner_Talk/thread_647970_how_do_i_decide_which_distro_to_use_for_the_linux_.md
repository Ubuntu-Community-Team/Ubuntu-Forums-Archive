### Post by autocrosser on 2007-12-23
That's a very good question---Simple answer: It depends. There are at least 100 different Linux distros From Ubuntu to Linux-From-Scratch & everything in between.

I would recommend looking at  [http://distrowatch.com/](http://distrowatch.com/)  to start out.  Read the info about ones you might be interested in & look for 3 things:

1. Easy to install? Challenge to install?

2. Good user information available?

3. list of basic software installed meets your needs or is software easy to install?

The above 3 questions lead me to Ubuntu shortly after Warty was released.....

### Post by irish_flu on 2007-12-23
This page will tell you a little bit about the "major" distributions, their pros and cons, and what they're designed for:
[http://distrowatch.com/dwres.php?resource=major](http://distrowatch.com/dwres.php?resource=major)


Here's a "quiz" that you may find useful.  You answer a few questions, and it tries to steer you towards a distribution that might fit you:
[http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/index.php](http://www.zegeniestudios.net/ldc/index.php)


If you have the time and inclination, I'd pick two of three that seem promising, download the Live CD for each of them (remembering that Live CDs don't perform as well as an installed OS) and just kick the tires.  See which one fits *you* best.

Since this is the Ubuntu forum, may I suggest that the first one you download and try is Ubuntu?  You might as well try Kubuntu too while you're at it.  :)

Google around to learn the difference between the two, knowing that you can switch back and forth at will.
