### Post by MontanaMax on 2007-02-26
As a new user, I recommend taking a little time and installing Gnome, KDE, XFCE, and Fluxbox or Blackbox to get a flavor for how the different desktops operate. It's easy to remove them when you figure out which one you like best. It's a great learning experience and a real eye-opener about the power of Linux compared to the Windows environment. If you don't feel like doing a full install of each one, you can also get LiveCD's for each of them, but it doesn't have quite the same impact as seeing all your programs and files through the different lens.
