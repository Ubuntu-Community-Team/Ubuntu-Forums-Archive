### Post by ofb on 2007-10-20
Book & info thread
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=533639](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=533639)

A lot of us came into it with years of Windows experience. Some advice: slow down & just roll through the beginner documentation to get your bearings solid.
[https://help.ubuntu.com/7.04/](https://help.ubuntu.com/7.04/)
And read the documentation that came with your OS.

If anything the big problem is there's too much documentation online -- spread over different years and different distros. Ubuntu has done a great deal to move Linux into the General Public zone, so it's a little different. Stick with Ubuntu specific info at first, and don't look at older than 7.04.

Psychocats is a good read too.
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/)

### Post by kpkeerthi on 2007-10-20
As you are beginning to use linux, I suggest you read [A Practical Guide to Linux Commands, Editors and Shell by Mark Sobell]("http://www.amazon.com/Practical-Guide-Commands-Editors-Programming/dp/0131478230") before you jump into Ubuntu specific guides and how-tos.

All the links provided in the posts above would give you commands that you may have to just copy and paste into your shell. You may not understand why/what you are doing and may damage your system. Reading a good intro linux book like Sobel's help you bridge that gap.

(Most of the beginners make this mistake of heading to ubuntuguide.org without doing enough homework and then complain "this is not working")
