### Post by IYY on 2006-08-17
Free as in Freedom means "liberated". When talking about software. it basically means open source, under the GPL or a similar license:  the source code must be free to see, and everybody must always have the right to modify and redistribute it.

Free as in Beer means "no cost". 

Ubuntu is free in both senses: you don't have to pay for it, and it's open source.

RedHat or SUSE Enterprise are open source, but they cost money.

There are plenty of free programs that are not open source on the internet. You can download the exe file, but not the source code.

### Post by IYY on 2006-08-17
Yeah, it doesn't really make sense but the expression's been around for a while so the geeks are used to it. The idea is, that when somebody tells you "Look, free beer!" or, "Come to my party, there'll be free beer!", it usually means you won't have to pay for it. And when somebody says "I value my freedom" it doesn't mean "I don't cost anything".
