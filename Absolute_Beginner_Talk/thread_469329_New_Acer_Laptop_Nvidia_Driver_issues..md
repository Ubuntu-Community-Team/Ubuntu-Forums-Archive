Hi there. I like Ubuntu. I like the philosophy, and the innovations.

Unfortunately for me, I bought a new AMD Turion 64 laptop which is designed for Vista. If you are in a similar position, with the Nvidia drivers refusing to give you sound or graphics installers which stall half way, [even worse in windows XP by the way which also has the nasty habit of writing straight over existing operating systems, Vista included], (though XP can be the saviour when the grub error 19 paralises the motherboard to restore the mbr). If you have come to the end of your patience or available time are about to reluctantly give up with this truely wonderful alternative to MS windows, there is one more thing to try before you give in to the MS dominion. Xandros. Everything just worked straight away, which is much more than could be said for XP which is supported far worse than Linux for the new hardware. Ubuntu will be compatible instantly one day, I am sure, but Xandros might just be the stepping stone to keep you from leaving Linux until the laptop driver issues are sorted in Ubuntu, probably the best free Linux distro.

Thanks for your help guys on this forum the couple of weeks I have been on this journey. See you again maybe in a year or two. :)
