### Post by eumoria on 2008-04-20
There wasn't a General Discussion type of section to the forums I could see so I decided to post this nonsense here.

After seeing the wealth of support for TF2/WoWin Windows emulation (Wine) and other games that I enjoy on Windows I decided to try out a Debian based OS again. I started my experiences with Debian on a distribution called Xebian which was a dist. for the first XBOX console using a Mechassault loophole to install Linux on the XBOX.

From there I soldered off the write access to the firmware, flashed it with a custom BIOS designed for Linux, and from there fell in the chasm that is the Windows technician/user blindly poking around the *nix world.

Anyway long story short I sat on Windows XP with a big frown for the past 2 years and realized the only limitation to switching (I thought) was DirectX applications and windows emulation. However after seeing some tutorials/movies using Wine and Crossfire's manager addition to Wine I was convinced.

I installed 7.10 originally then upgraded to 8.04-rc. I am floored at the usability. My MTP MP3 device works amazingly (with a little digging/compiling of software), all my Windows games that I was worried about work BETTER (higher FPS in TF2 at least) although visual quality takes a hit but I'm not arguing.

:popcorn:

--> See forward 4 months when/for my post "Can't do <whatever> because of <whatever>, hate life switching to Windows like a coward because I refuse to learn new things."

I can only hope this will be the last switch.

Thanks for listening to my nonsense.
