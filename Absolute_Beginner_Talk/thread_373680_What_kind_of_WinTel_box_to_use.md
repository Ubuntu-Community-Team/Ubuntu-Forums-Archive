### Post by go2dell on 2007-03-01
There are many, many threads on this topic in the forums, but suffice it to say that practically anything you buy currently will run Ubuntu with rather snappy performance.  If you want to run desktop 3D effects for eye candy, then more machine is better.  If you're only going to be web surfing and typing an occasional letter, then less is better.

Don't forget that there are three primary versions of Ubuntu:  Ubuntu, Kubuntu, and Xubuntu.  Xubuntu is the most lightweight of the three.  There's also the even lighter Fluxbuntu, which isn't yet as complete as the others, but appears to be moving along nicely, and does have a CD available for download.

You'll want to note what kind of video hardware your intended system has and do a little research on how well that works in Ubuntu.  Just about any video chip will be supported, at least in a basic way, but for full 3D graphics and such there are some gotchas no matter who make the chip.  The [wiki]("http://wiki.ubuntu.com") is a good place to search for this, in addition to the forums.

The one area that you'll want to keep a watch on is RAM.  Anything less than about 128MB is going to cause you frustration, unless you just want to experience the triumph of running a fully modern OS on ancient equipment.  Stick with 192MB or more for decent performance.

All that said, go ahead and buy the fastest machine you can afford, put (U)(K)(X)ubuntu on it, and prepare to leave Windows behind. :)
