### Post by Irihapeti on 2007-09-10
My son, who lives in another town, has an Acer laptop with 256Mb RAM, currently running XP SP2. He uses an external wireless modem for internet access, which I suspect might be a USB modem.

Anyway, I'm thinking about making up a customised liveCD and sending it to him to try out. What can I put on the CD that would make it as likely as possible that he'll have all the stuff he needs to have an optimal first experience of Ubuntu?

A bit of background might be useful. He's already quite at home with open-source software in Windows, so is not quite your average hide-bound, can't-stand-anything-vaguely-different person. But he did say to me the other evening that Linux "has bits all over the place" (I think he's referring to dependencies and libraries) and it's like "a piece of equipment with wires hanging out the back". 

So I don't see him as an enthusiastic terminal hacker or someone who will put hours into getting something to work. Plus it's approaching the end of the academic year in this part of the world, and he's got a dissertation for his MA to hand in shortly. (Yes, he has got a brain!) Therefore, time's in short supply for him. Which is one reason why I've not hassled him for computer specs.

I'd be interested in any ideas about drivers etc that I could include. The basic software packages are fairly easy for me to sort out.
