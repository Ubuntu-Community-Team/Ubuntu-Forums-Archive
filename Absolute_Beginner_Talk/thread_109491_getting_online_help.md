### Post by d1337 on 2005-12-28
I haven't yet configured a dialup modem on a Linux machine, so I will likely not be much help.  But I do know that we will need more info to assist you, mostly regarding what kind of modem you have.  Output of "lspci" from your Linux side would be helpful, but since you may have to manually type it in, all that is important is the modem.  ```
0000:00:0a.0 Communication controller: Lucent Microelectronics LT WinModem (rev 02)
``` from my machine.  From what I've read, WinModems can be made to work with Linux, but it will take some work (if you also have a WinModem).  You can probably get enough info about your modem from the Windows side as well, which will be easier for you since you can copy and paste the info.    In a nutshell, knowing what kind of modem you have and getting the needed packages to install it (moved over to Linux) will most likely be the biggest obstacle.  Other than that info, you'll need the access phone number(s) that your aol account uses as well as your login and password.
