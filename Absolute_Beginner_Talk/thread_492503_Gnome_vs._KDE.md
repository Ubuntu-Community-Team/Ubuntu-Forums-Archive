### Post by Nythain on 2007-07-04
since no one actually explained what the difference is (probably because this question has been answered about 1 trillion times already on this forum) i guess ill give a quick shot

The core desktop environment libraries, and the software installed with it

gnome = primarily gtk apps, is used by the standard ubuntu, and, in my opinion is a little lacking in the customization department

kde = primarily qt apps, is used by Kubuntu, and, in my opinion is the easiest to configure and has the most options out of the box.

others will argue, some will say one is faster than the other (but on most newer pc's the difference is minimal if noticable at all), some will say gnome is great, has more options than kde, runs faster, etc... i say kde is great, better looking than gnome, has more options than gnome, some of the best linux software is qt/kde based, and a few people have reported it actually running faster than gnome on thier pc's.

if you want to compare the general look and feel of each one without installing both, head over to
[http://www.lynucs.org](http://www.lynucs.org)
click on applicatations, and select either one, to get a bunch of user submitted screenshots... there you can get a general feel for the look of gnome vs. kde... also other desktop environments and window managers like xfce and fluxbox

### Post by coolen on 2007-07-04
They each provide the components of a desktop environment. Window managers, file managers, panels, core applications, etc.
The advantages of one over the other are largely subjective. Gnome tends towards a simpler, although more static, interface, while KDE goes for one with more eye candy, and doesn't mind cluttering its menus.

I find Gnome to be more comfortable, personally, while KDE is a little more exciting. I tend to prefer Gnome for the look and feel, although the lack of configurability bugs pretty much every user.
Of course, KDE is just the tiniest bit narcissistic. Install the kubuntu-desktop package and take a look at the applications list to see what I mean :P
