### Post by toupeiro on 2007-11-21
First, Welcome and congratulations on making the decision to learn linux.  There is a lot of great software and learning experiences waiting for you, and we are all here to help!

I know a lot of people here are giving you instruction to repartition, but here is my suggestion, please feel free to do whichever you feel is best.

I suggest you download [VirtualBox]("http://www.virtualbox.org/wiki/Downloads") for Windows and install ubuntu there.  This will give you plenty of chances to learn how to properly partition linux, configure various settings and try various tools without the risk of compromising your primary operating system. It gives you a chance to run through procedures several times before you make changes to your production system.  I think you will find that becoming more familiar with linux before you put it on your system will make your overall experience that much more fun and enjoyable.
