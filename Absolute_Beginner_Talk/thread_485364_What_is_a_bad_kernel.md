### Post by Foxmike on 2007-06-26
Well the kernel is the lower layer between the hardware (CPU, etc..) and the softwares.  Kernel related problem are obviously problems with hardwares: your audio did work but is not working anymore, same for video or other things.  Generally, kernels are updated to fix security issues and  hardware problems could be caused by wrong settings as well, so it's wyse to check out what the problem really is before to revert to the ancient kernel.

It's always safe to keep a backup kernel just in case, and if you have room, why not?:)

Hope it helps!:)
