### Post by Harpoon on 2008-03-26
There is no reason why you cannot or should not do exactly what you described.  I have as many as five external hard drives connected to my laptop at any one time to reorder/cleanup/backup a rather large mass of data.  

As to the price, the full size externals will buy more storage for the money than a similarly sized lapt internal drive.  Check the prices and go with the largest drive that make sense (little diffference in price going from an 80 gb drive to a 300+ gb drive in my area).

Have at it.  It's worth the peace of mind.

### Post by indytim on 2008-03-26
As long as you can simultaneously mount both externals, you shouldn't have any problems copying from the parent to the child.  The obvious warning: stay alert to the parent and the child when you kick off the copy routine (I've messed this up  more more than once going from my second internal (320G) to my backup external (also 320G).  

My non-technical recommendation is that if the photos are important enough (like baby pictures), I'd recommend  investing in dvd's as hard medium backup also.  I do this on my "keeper" pics after the initial editing.
