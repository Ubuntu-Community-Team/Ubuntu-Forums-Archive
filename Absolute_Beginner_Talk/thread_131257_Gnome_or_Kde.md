### Post by AndyCooll on 2006-02-16
It truly is a matter of personal preference. Some say Gnome is more simplistic, with fewer options to view and therefore easier to use. Some say KDE is closer to Winblows, is better for the eye candy and has more configuration options.

There are plenty of threads on these boards advocating one or the other, however in the end all true Linux users point out that what I think is best isn't necessarily going to be the same as your choice.

The beauty of Linux is that you DO have choice, lots of it, right doen to the look and feel of your Windows management environment. Apart from Gnome and KDE there's also XFCE, Enlightenment, IceWM etc etc (and even just the command line. The choice is yours, pays your money and takes your pick (or rather in the case of Linux doesn't need to pay your money, but can still take your pick!)

### Post by frodon on 2006-02-16
I think that even if users tell you why they prefer one or another desktop you will really know which desktop you like once you tested them.
So my advice is to test GNOME, KDE and XFCE and see the one you prefer.

In my case i use KDE at my job and Gnome at home with the xfce window manger.
I like gnome because it's based on gtk and is a more supported flavor of ubuntu i think (for example try to compare adept and synaptic !), and i like to use my gnome with the xfce window manager because it is faster and has more features than the default gnome one (metacity) and it also has an embedded compositor.
Also i prefer the gnome spirit (less useless graphical apps) and i'm glad to see that many gnome based projects are in active development.
