### Post by Arisna on 2007-08-09
They just have different desktop environments.  The desktop environment is the graphical component of the system that you interact with most directly.  Ubuntu has GNOME, Kubuntu has KDE, and Xubuntu has XFCE.  Look up those different environments, check out screenshots, and see what you like best.  Once you have installed one, you can add the other environments to your installation fairly easily and try them out as well.
