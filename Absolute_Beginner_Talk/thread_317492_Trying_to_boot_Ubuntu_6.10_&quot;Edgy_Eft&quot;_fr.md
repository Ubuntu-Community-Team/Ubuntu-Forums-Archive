### Post by taurus on 2006-12-12
You need to get into your BIOS and set the booting to CD-ROM first and then your harddrive.  Looks like it is setting at harddrive first and then CD-ROM...

For Dell, F2 is what you want to press when it first boots up.

### Post by taurus on 2006-12-12
Should consult with the manual for your laptop on how and where you can change the boot order.  Basically, you want to tell the BIOS to boot from the CD-ROM first if the CD is a bootable.  If not, boot from the harddrive.  So, move the CD-ROM to the top of the list.
