### Post by candtalan on 2007-11-08
a) let the updates complete if you can.
b)consider using a live cd such as parted magic to examine (dont change, just look) your partitions.
c) you might have a few spare unwanted partitions, but if so, they may not be doing any harm, just taking space. If the unwanted attempts at install have resulted in grub boot entries, you may (?) be able to manage them in various ways, once you have decided what to do with any junk partitions - if they exist. be wary - do not take action which changes a partiton ID or your existing xubuntu will not run up properly (file is fstab).
