### Post by Nda on 2007-02-10
Suse,  I understand your difficulties with Tiscali - their reputation is diminishing daily.  I use them and am looking to change.

In answer to your main question, no you do not NEED a wireless router.  A router is a box that takes your ADSL (broadband) feed from the phone line and shares it with, usually, up to 4 computers.  What you DO need is a modem/router, i.e. a router with a built-in modem to replace your USB modem.  This can be a cable or a wireless router.

An ethernet router has 4 RJ45 sockets to connect to your computer(s) with a cable.  A wireless router will usually have the same 4 sockets but will also have a wireless transmitter so that you can connect without cables IF you have a wireless card in your computer.  If you don't have a wireless card in the PC, you can still connect your computer to the wireless router using a cable.

You say your PC is not near a phone line, so you have two options.  You either connect wirelessly - if you have a wireless router and a wireless card - or you connect with a cable.  The easier way is to connect with an ethernet cable.  

But, you cannot use a telephone extension lead to move the router nearer the PC because it won't work if you do.  Therefore you must place the router near the phone point and use a long ethernet cable to connect the router to the PC.  Routers usually come provided with an ethernet cable about 2m long.  If this is not long enough for your circumstances then you will also have to buy a longer ethernet cable.

Hope that helps.

Nda
