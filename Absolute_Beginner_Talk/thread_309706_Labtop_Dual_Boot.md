### Post by aysiu on 2006-11-30
Ubuntu on laptops can be tricky, depending on the laptop model. The fact that it has Vista installed on it means that it's a relatively new laptop with relatively new hardware... which means you might have a bit of difficulty getting some hardware recognized.

Wireless is notoriously difficult, but again it depends on what hardware you have. Some models get detected out of the box. Others don't.

To get an idea of how easy or difficult Ubuntu on laptops is, check out this page:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptops](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupportMachinesLaptops)

For general dual boot instructions, check out this:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing)
