Seriously, though, I think this forum right here is an amazing resource. Other resources I've found incredibly useful are the Ubuntu Wiki (i..e, the user-edited documentation), a book called "Ubuntu Hacks" (Oxer, Rankin & Childers), and Scott Granneman's "Linux Phrasebook" (a guide to bash). The best thing, though, has been the fact that since I switched away from Windows, I've been spending far more time learning how to do things and far less time defragging my drive and scanning the system for malware.
