### Post by Ek0nomik on 2007-06-01
sudo is merely a way of giving you temporary administrative problems.

In Linux, certain files are owned by different groups.  Files that are usually "dangerous" or core to the system, are owned by root.  In order to edit root files, you need administrator privileges.  That's where sudo comes into play.
