### Post by aysiu on 2005-12-14
Yes.

How *well* it'll run...? Well, that you should download and burn a live CD for.

The live CD doesn't affect your hard drive, but it gives you a sense of how Ubuntu will be on your Mac Mini. After you burn the ISO as a disk image (use the "Disk Utility" in Mac for this), reboot your computer with the disk inside and hold down the C key to get it to boot from the CD-ROM drive.

Then, you're ready.

### Post by aysiu on 2006-01-10
[QUOTE=jaywatkins]Why would you want to replace OS X?  Ubuntu is cool, but really?[/QUOTE] Maybe it's faster. Maybe people like OpenOffice (which *can* be installed and run in OS X, but it's a pain in the butt to do so, and it's extremely slow to load up in OS X). There can be any number of reasons one might prefer Ubuntu on a former Mac.

One thing new users should be cautioned on: the PowerPC version of Ubuntu has fewer available packages and minimal support for Flash (among other things). You may have to do a lot of compiling from source.
