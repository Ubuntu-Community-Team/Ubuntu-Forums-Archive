### Post by SteveHoffmanUK on 2007-10-03
Bean77:

I have an old Dell Latitude that I picked up used two years ago: Pentium III 250Mb memory, 8.9Gb disk, 256 processor (I think). I use it for exactly what you're talking about: as backup, especially when something gets borked on my desktop and I need online access. It came in handy last month when I installed Feisty on my desktop and my PCI wireless card was no longer supported as it was under Dapper :( I used it to download  Ndiswrapper for the card onto a memory stick to plug into my desktop and install. Sweet.

On this creaky device I run Feisty with no problems AND with wireless using a PCMCIA card. Not the swiftest horse in the stable, but it does the job. If you do go wireless, just be sure to buy a card that you know Linux will support. My Belkin Wireless G Network card has worked 'out of the box' with Dapper, Edgy and Feisty. 

But why dual boot? Can't you get the support you need just using Ubuntu?
