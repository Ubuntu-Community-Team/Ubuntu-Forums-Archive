### Post by matthew on 2006-05-17
If you want to make a change, it's possible. However, with 1Gb of RAM it really isn't even necessary to have a swap. It's unlikely your swap file will get much use, but it won't hurt anything to leave it as is. Unless you are really hard up for disk space I suggest you just not worry about it at all and use the time you save to do something else. :)

### Post by matthew on 2006-05-17
[quote=jethro10]I was under the understanding that it needs to be X times bigger than Ram for some reason[/quote]That was very true for RAM levels 2-3 years ago or before. Under 256MB te rule is 1.5 to 2 times the RAM installed. With 512MB a swap of 500MB or so is nice to have but not necessary. With a full gig it's unlikely a swap file/partition will be used unless you are doing lots of video stuff, like sef suggested so I just wouldn't bother worrying about. I've forgotten to make swap partitions on computers with 1GB of RAM before with no trouble whatsoever and I currently have 1GB RAM and my swap almost never gets used.

Congrats on the upgrade and on not having to do anything else to enjoy it!
