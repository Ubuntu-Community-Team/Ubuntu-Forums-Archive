### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-13
First of all, let me just say that WEP is completely insecure, today, right now, its the equivalent of leaving your access point wide open. I have SEEN right in front of my eyes, WEP cracked in under a minute (no exaggeration, try it urself). Google the cain and abel program for an example of how its so easy... So, I would strongly advise you to move to WPA when you fix your settings, it cannot be cracked as long as you use a secure password (more than 20 characters is advised, mine is 63). 

As for what happens if you don't have even WEP. Well, it means that anyone in your vicinity will be able to use your network. Worse though, is unless you run a Virtual Private Network (VPN) your data will be sent in the clear and anyone in your vicinity can monitor your traffic, thus they could steal passwords and other numbers. That is a problem I think, and I wouldn't assume security through the odds of a hacker being near you, the cain and abel program is free and will do all of this at a push of a button. What else, if your router has not been reset with a secure password (you should ALWAYS reset the password to something random and 12-15 characters long), it can easily be hacked, and if you have WAN management on, they can covert your Router... not good I think >.>

So, yes, there are security concerns to leaving a wifi point open... without a VPN and a reconfigured router, you are certainly not secure.

Easiest way to fix this, is to plug a line into your comp. Nobody can hack that. I'm not trying to scare ya, but wifi security is iffy, and recent statistics say that there is still considerable use of no security/WEP (I think it was hovering around 60% of wifi users the two of them combined, people really don't take security serious enough...)
