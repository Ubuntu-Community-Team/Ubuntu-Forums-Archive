### Post by LaurelLynn on 2007-04-10
Dear nutylinuxuser,

I to am forced to use lowly dial-up.

One posibility is that it might not be a modem problem at all. Some phone lines just have really bad transmision quality. Modems try to make a connection at the fastest speed, then progressively fall back to slower and slower speeds until they get a connection. My dad used to complain for years that I never fixed his modem because it keep loosing his internet connection every 15-20 minutes. I plugged in my laptop, connected to a different service provider, and same problem only much more frequent (worked fine at home).

Next I would look to the drivers, they may not be the best one for your model. Usually to get all  the features and performance of a piece of hardward takes drivers specifically designed for it. Since many manufacturers don´t make linux drivers, the operating system may default to a driver that is only compatible with some features.

All hope is not lost though, there are many drivers available on the internet that the operating system doesn´t know about. But, you do have to search for them.

I usually start by checking the documentation that came with it to get the model name or number.  Then I go to Google or Yahoo (or both for an obscure one). I then use a search string like this:

¨ubuntu   my-device  drivers¨

That will usually reveal several discussions of people with exactly the same device trying to do the same thing you are. Hopefully at least on of them resolved the issue, and left instructions. They may also have posted a link to a better set of drivers.

Getting some pieces of hardware to work under linux can be frustrating, but persistence usually does pay off.

I may not have all the features of my graphics card working, but I´m no longer stuck at 640x480, and I now have my webcam going.

If you get really stuck, it may just be easier to buy a new modem, they are not that expensive. A long time ago I bought a couple of external serial modem which do 56k. The protocols for serial modems are so old, that they are the closest you can get to univeral compatibility.

Laurel Lynn
