### Post by amadeus266 on 2007-07-17
This is a subject that will be debated until Christ's second return... I have been asked this question a thousand times and IMHO it all boils down to this... Case studies have shown that certain types of hardware are susceptible to the small power surges that occur every time you turn your computer on but under _normal_ circumstances the surge isn't enough to cause damage. Newer hardware is not much of a problem anymore but older hardware is definitely more sensitive to these surges. So, my advice to you would be this: If you have older hardware (read: more than 5 years old) leave it on if the noise from the fans doesn't bother you. Otherwise, you are totally safe turning your machine off when not in use just so long as you shut it down correctly. (I still know people who will just reach for the power button.) If anyone questions me on this, I have been the sole maintainer of a small business network for the last 10 years, I am still running some machines that were here for years before I started and I have only replaced 5 out of our 30 computers in 10 years. (now changing operating system is another matter. If I could just get our software company to switch platforms, we would totally Microsoft free in less than 3 months)

### Post by Haegin on 2007-07-17
To sum up what other people are saying:
~ There is a negligible effect on the hardware if you turn it on and off a lot.
~ Your computer uses up electricity when on.
~ A computer doing nothing (or very little) isn't worth leaving on.
~ Linux can run for days, weeks, months and even years without a reboot.

So now you can safely decide for yourself if you want to leave it on.

My recommendation is to leave your computer on at night if it will constantly (or near constantly) be doing something useful. Make it do updates, run any virus scans on Windows partitions, do any indexing of databases or music collections etc, seed and download torrents, run SETI@home or Folding@home or a similar project and anything else that you do regularly and takes time but doesn't need user input.

If it only needs to do that weekly, turn it off 6 nights a week. If it isn't doing anything then turn it off and save the planet. Use your common sense. Most computers don't need to be on 24/7 and you may as well give the environment a break.

If you are planning on keeping a computer on for a long time please consider offsetting the power with a renewable energy source such as a solar panel on the roof of your house or something similar. Currently I think you can get grants for "going green" in the UK and you may be able to in other countries as well.
