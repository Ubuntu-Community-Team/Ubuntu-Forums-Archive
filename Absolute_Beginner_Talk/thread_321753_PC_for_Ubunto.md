### Post by technodigifreak on 2006-12-19
The truth is, any modern desktop will suit your needs (assuming you're not doing high-end gaming).  Any 64 bit processor will perform adequately for the next several years.  I would also recommend at least 1 gig of RAM.  In fact, I'm going to recommend that you max out the amount of ram in the machine that you get (~2 gig or so).  As far as performance per dollar, memory is the best upgrade you can get.  And it's cheap now, so why not get it now and not have to think about it later.  In five years if you needed to buy more memory, you may not be able to readily find a type that is compatible with your motherboard (example, RDRAM :rolleyes: ).  Applications are certainly going to be more memory hungry in the future (example, MS Office 95 vs. MS Office 2003  ](*,)  ).  

I'll recommend also that for the next 18-36months you stick with a 32bit OS.  Simple reason, you have to wait for the industries to catch up with the generational change that's taking place right now.  No matter what anybody says, 64bit is still new technology to the PC world (forget the high-end server market).  The average service life of a business PC is 18-36months, by the time 3 years has gone by, most of the world will be forced into the 64bit upgrade cycle and 64bit apps will be readily available.  Then you can safely make the jump.  Until then you'll run into compatibility issues.  64bit OS's have a hard time right now with 32bit apps.  In the future you will see quite a bit more 64bit applications, but you will still see 32bit apps all over the place.

A look at the past 5 years will be a good indication of the next 5 years.  A word of warning though.  Multicore's are coming in fast, and we're starting to see 128bit PC chips on the near horizon (next 6 months), so we can certainly expect to see a chip with 8 32bit cores (~256bit) in the next 3-4 years (Moore's Law).  Also, let's not forget about the up and coming Cell processor...


Let me know if this was helpful, or simply more confusing.  Let us know what you decide.

P.S.  My 14 year old DOS box is still running just fine on it's original parts.
