### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-05-18
To answer your question, it needs to be in two parts.

If you either buy a system that has been pre-loaded with Linux, or if you have a friend who is literate with PC's and Linux, it is a definite YES!

However, if you intend to install and set-up Linux yourself, the results can be anywhere between dead simple, to a nightmare.

The reason for this is not Linux, itself, but whether the manufacturer of your hardware has released open source drivers. Things in particuar are video cards, printers, wireless network cards and modem cards.

Notorious offenders are:

Printers - Lexmark
Video cards and chipsets - ATI and SIS
Wireless - some Broadcom
Modem Cards - allmost all WinModems
ADSL Modems - many USB based ADSL modems

Friendly to Linux:

Printers: HP and many Brother 
Video Cards and Chipsets- Intel, NVidia
ADSL Routers - Allmost any 100/10 Base T ADSL Router

If your hardware is in the friendly region, your install in most cases is far easier than a Windows instal. 

However, if you have any of the unfriendly hardware, in most cases it is not impossible to get it going, but experienced help is often required.

These forums are an excellent source of information and help and almost any problem can be overcome here.
