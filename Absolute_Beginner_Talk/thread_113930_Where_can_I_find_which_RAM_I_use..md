### Post by Bartender on 2006-01-07
I'm running a PIII 450, too.  Specs in my sig.  OpenOffice does take 10-15 seconds (seems like more but it's probly not) to load, but is snappy enuf once it's done with that part.  I think that's pretty common experience.
Installing more RAM is just about the easiest thing you can do inside a computer.  It's a little confusing to find out which kind you need, but Corsair, Viking, Micron, etc. all have memory finders on their websites.  You plug in your motherboard or computer model and follow the prompts.  The worst part of it is that old PC100 or PC133 memory is more expensive than DDR or DDR2, the kinds that go into modern motherboards.
You don't mention how much mem you've got.  If 256 you'd see a nice improvement adding more.  If you've got 512 or more, you're not going to see as much boost.  The CPU speed probly becomes the limiting factor after that point.
