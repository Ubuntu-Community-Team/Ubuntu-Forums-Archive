### Post by Jake90 on 2008-03-10
A lot of people are still using feisty because gutsy for some reason doesn't work well with all hardware. Another reason could be that they got used to it and see no reason to switch. 
If you are new to Linux then you probably should wait a little before getting hardy. The people that work really hard to put it out there and fix all the bugs do a great job but it is nearly impossible put out a flawless OS on the first release.

### Post by Whiffle on 2008-03-10
For me feisty behaves much more predictably than Gutsy did.  I had gutsy on my laptop right after it came out and it worked great at first, but as updates started rolling out over the next couple weeks it went downhill fast.  It stopped suspending correctly, the wireless got dodgy ,etc.  I went back to feisty and its worked great.  

I'd say try gutsy.  If it works for you, great, if not, keep that option to go to feisty open.  I wouldn't try hardy though unless you've got another os to depend on.  I've got a friend who is trying out hardy and all kinds of things broke with the upgrade (broadcom wireless for example).

### Post by bumanie on 2008-03-10
