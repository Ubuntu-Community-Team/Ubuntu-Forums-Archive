### Post by jsandys on 2006-10-21
Here is the way I do it on a unix workstation, alias asdf switches to dvorak and alias aoeu switches to qwerty.
     [http://www.datawranglers.com/docs/dvorak.php](http://www.datawranglers.com/docs/dvorak.php)
You need to create an xmodmap for qwerty and dvorak, the qwerty xmodmap is trivial, here is a xmodmap for dvorak.
     [http://www.mit.edu/~jcb/Dvorak/dvorak-keyboard.txt](http://www.mit.edu/~jcb/Dvorak/dvorak-keyboard.txt)
My dvorak xmodmap is different, I left the brackets, braces, plus, minus, equal and underscore in the same place on both layouts.  I think this scheme will work on Ubuntu Linux.  I suppose you could make a gnome icon to change keyboards with the click of a button.

### Post by Sef on 2008-01-14
Easy way to be able to switch back and forth:

Put your cursor on the top panel > right click > Add to Panel > search: type in keyboard > click on 'keyboard indicator' > Add > Close.
