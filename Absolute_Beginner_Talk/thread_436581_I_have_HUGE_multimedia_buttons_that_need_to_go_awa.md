### Post by mcduck on 2007-05-08
I think the new style is used for on-screen displays as long as you have compositing enabled. So it's not actually Beryl/Compiz thing, but as you need to enable compositing to use those you'll also get the big on-screen display.

If you really hate it, you could possibly disable compositing in /etc/X11/xorg.conf.

I get the big on-screen display in XGL session, and the old, small one in normal Gnome session as compositing is onlyenabled with XGL on my laptop.
