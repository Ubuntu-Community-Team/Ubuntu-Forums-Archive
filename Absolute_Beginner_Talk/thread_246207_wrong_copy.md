### Post by aysiu on 2006-08-28
I admire your enthusiasm, but I'd advise against quitting cold turkey. The way to ensure success with Ubuntu is to take it slowly.

Start with using the live CD for a couple of weeks. Then do a dual-boot and slowly wean yourself off Windows. Once you're sure you can do without Windows, delete your Windows partition.

I tend to agree with aysiu and others re: Cold Turkey, however....

1. The Live CD is slower and slow computers are frustrating.

2. Dual booting can maintain dependency on Windows. Every time you re frustrated -> boot windows. To run Ubuntu, now I have to re-boot, easier to stay in Windows.

Thus it is possible to go to Ubutnu Cold turekey if:

1. All the applications you need have a Linux equivalent. I would not advise you rely on wine, especially as a new Ubutnu user. Wine can be difficult to configure and may not work.

2. You are willing to endure the steep learning curve.

3. You feel comfortable with the command line, or at least are willing to learn the command line.

4. Do you know where to go for help? Can you read the man pages for example?
