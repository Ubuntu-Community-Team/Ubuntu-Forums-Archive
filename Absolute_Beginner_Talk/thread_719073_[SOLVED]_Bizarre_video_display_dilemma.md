### Post by napzilla on 2008-03-08
Since I upgraded to Gutsy a while ago, I have faced a rather annoying dilemma on my Dell Inspiron 6400. I am using the default Window Manager (GNOME), and have been thus far trying to avoid any fancy desktop effects.

Originally, I was having trouble with Tellico and other KDE applications (mostly Tellico, because that's the only KDE application that I use regularly). There was a massive lag time in the display update, and simple operations such as opening a drop-down menu would take up to 2--3 minutes. I considered switching to the GNOME alternative (alexandria) and just not using any KDE applications, but I didn't really like that idea.

After looking around the Forums for a while, I found a suggestion to remove xserver-xgl. I tried that, and it fixed the KDE problem but created two more. A message began appearing every time I started my machine that said something to the effect of "Keyboard settings don't match. Expected pc101 layout us no options, found pc105, us, lv3:ralt_switch". It then gave me the option to use the X configuration or the GNOME settings. I did manage to resolve this issue by changing the settings in xorg.conf.

The second problem is quite a bit more annoying. The totem movie player with the xine backend crashes when xserver-xgl is removed, and I had to replace it with the other totem option. Miro also crashes whenever it tries to play something. Finally, all the people in my videos look like they're the asphyxiated walking dead (i.e. they are all a disturbing shade of blue). No matter how I adjust the colour balance, at least one of the three colours (rgb) is missing.

I tried reconfiguring my xserver with dpkg, but that just broke everything. Generally, I ignore the problem for a while, then I try to boot my computer or watch a video and I get annoyed. Then I think to myself, "Hey, this isn't Windows, this is Linux, darn it! The only reason it isn't working right is because I haven't taken the time to research the problem carefully enough." Then, after spending a few hours looking for help and trying different options, I start feeling guilty about the amount of time I'm wasting on the problem, and set it aside.

As it stands, I'm faced with the dilemma of either pulling my hair out waiting for KDE applications to display, or watching videos where everyone looks like smurfs. It only recently dawned on me that I could post it here and see if maybe someone else had some insight to offer.

I am using a Dell Inspiron 6400 with an ATI Raedon Mobility X1400. I have included a copy of my xorg.conf file below. Any insight that anyone could offer would be greatly appreciated.
