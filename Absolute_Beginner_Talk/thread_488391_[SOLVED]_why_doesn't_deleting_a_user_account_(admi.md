I have created a test account on my ubuntu system. Now I have decided to delete it. I go to system/administration/Users and Groups/.

I select the test account and click delete. Then I get this message:

> This will disable this user's access to the system without deleting the user's home directory.

In Windows (XP at least), the user is given the choice of deleting not just the account, but also the files of the account that is to be deleted. 
Why is it that in ubuntu we're not given the choice of deleting the user's home account? Why does Ubuntu make us have to delete the user account some other way? ### Post by Taliskerd on 2007-06-30
I'm a support specialist, not *nix but windows, and what usually happen when you delete the user's files along with the account is that they call back within a week because they forgot to copy something important..

Restoring that from a backup is not necessarily a problem, but just browsing their files and mailing them is so much quicker. So I prefer to add a reminder to my calendar to delete the files a week after the account is closed -it's simply that much easier all around.
But that's just how I do it ;)

On the other hand, I would like to have the choice to wipe it all in one go -add my vote if you're going to call Ubuntu about this.
