### Post by tonyr1988 on 2006-10-19
You're not going to get it to run natively. From the site:

> System Requirements:

Windows XP/2000, Microsoft Internet Explorer 6.0, Windows Media Player 7.1

But, some people have [reported](http://digg.com/linux_unix/AllTunes_software_(allofmp3.com)_works_on_Linux__2) success with it, but you'll need Wine (search the forums, use Automatix, etc) and [Internet Explorer](http://www.tatanka.com.br/ies4linux/page/Main_Page).

[Here](http://appdb.winehq.com/appview.php?iVersionId=4613&iTestingId=5584) and [here](http://appdb.winehq.com/appview.php?iVersionId=5710&iTestingId=5831) are the relevant Wine AppDB entries. It looks like it runs, but not perfectly.

The digg post I linked to earlier also says you'll need [CrossOver Linux](http://www.codeweavers.com/products/), but I wouldn't mess with it unless you're guaranteed that it'll work (from more than one source).

Good luck!

PS [appdb.winehq.com](appdb.winehq.com) is your friend. Bookmark it.

### Post by skymt on 2006-10-20
> **PriceChild said:**
> As far as i'm aware, allofmp3 is completely legal.

AllOfMP3 work through a loophole in Russian law. They pay rather small licensing fees (the same as needed to play a song on the radio, much less than needed to actually distribute copies) to a body (ROMS, the Russian Multimedia and Internet Society) that doesn't give any of the money to the artists or record labels. That makes buying from them unethical, even if the legality is debated.

See [AllOfMP3](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AllOfMP3.com) on Wikipedia for details of legality in different countries.
