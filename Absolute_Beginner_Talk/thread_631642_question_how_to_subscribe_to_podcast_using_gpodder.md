### Post by Old Jimma on 2007-12-05
Folks:

I discovered why people rave about Amarok: it seems to do everything you might want done.

For starters and for old guys like me here is what to do:

1. install amarok using synaptic.
2. install khelpcenter using synaptic (this will enable you to access amarok help and manuals).

3. Find your podcast at podcastalley.com or your favorite place. Search for your podcast there and get the URL for the rss feed. COPY THAT URL.

4. open amarok.

5. On the left tab, mash on playlists. Then click on podcasts. Then click on "Add."

6. copy your URL in there and start enjoying life again. You are going to really love amarok.

Stop feeling sorry for yourself for not having iTunes. Amarok REALLY ROCKS!

### Post by DishBreak on 2007-12-10
Easier than that, even, use the Rhythmbox music player that's already in your system.

Applications > Sound & Video > Rhythmbox music player. 

Now, click on Podcasts in the left hand column. There should be a button on top that says "Add New Podcast"

Now, find the URL where all the files in the podcast are kept. For example, a podcast from CNN has a URL that looks like this:
[http://rss.cnn.com/services/podcasting/lateedition/rss.xml](http://rss.cnn.com/services/podcasting/lateedition/rss.xml)

Copy and paste the URL into the dialog box and press enter.

The computer should automatically start downloading the latest episode of the podcast. You can double-click on older episodes to download them, too. 

As for your specific podcast, I'm not sure where it is in the link you gave. Try searching for it on google.com or something like that.
