### Post by DaBruGo on 2005-11-18
Hi Folks,

For those getting ready to test the waters with Ubuntu, I wanted to share a little book I found that may be of help. It was fairly informative without being too technical for a beginner.

Linux Desktop Pocket Guide
(ISBN#  0-596-10104-X)
(author: David Brickner)
(publisher: O'Reilly)
([www.oreilly.com](www.oreilly.com))
(price: $9.95)

I purchased it at a Barnes and Noble bookstore.

It introduces you to GNOME and KDE ... covers the most popular GUI (graphic user interface) programs ... and gives you some useful info on managing the five most popular distributions (Fedora, Gentoo, Mandriva, SUSE, and most importantly UBUNTU).

The author also touches on configuring video cards, screen resolution, sound, and wireless networking.

There is also a section for laptop users dealing with issues like battery life, sleep, and hibernation modes.

In some areas the author focuses quite a bit on UBUNTU because of its recent rise in popularity.
> "As this is a desktop pocket guide, I will focus upon Ubuntu because it has increasingy become the de facto Debian desktop distribution."

I thought it was a pretty good starter guide for me. I hope it helps others. Let me know if this was of benefit to you.



DAVE
