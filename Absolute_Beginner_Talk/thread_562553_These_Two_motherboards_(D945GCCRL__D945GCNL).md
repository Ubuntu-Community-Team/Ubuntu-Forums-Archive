### Post by Linux_Man on 2007-09-28
Well, im not sure, but for just about every computer I have used a Live-CD on or installed it, it worked fine in Ubuntu, really motherboards are so generic it shouldn't matter, about the only things that are hard to get is 

1. Embedded Wi-Fi (Some laptops Ubuntu doesn't detect them and USB wifi cards, Notebook PCI cards are trouble too, go for the cards you put in your desktop for best results)
2. Graphics Cards, Intels are fully compatable, but ATI and NVidia need propriatary drivers to work fully
3. Propriatary ports such as those on some Laptops


But Intel has been good at giving full hardware info to kernel hackers so most Intel things will work fine, try googleing it if you have your doubts but most motherboards will work
