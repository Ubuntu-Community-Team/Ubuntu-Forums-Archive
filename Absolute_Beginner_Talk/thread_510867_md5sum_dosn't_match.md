### Post by coffeecat on 2007-07-27
I very much doubt you're doing anything wrong, and the chance of getting a corrupted download seven times in succession must be vanishingly small.

I've downloaded the ISOs of many dozens of Linux distros over the last couple of years and I have had a corrupted download only once. And that was from a deliberately throttled server of a minor distro.

I have no experience of Vista (and long may that be true :D ) so I may not be able to help, but I suggest you post how you checked the md5sum, which app you used and what the output was. Someone with some Vista experience may see what is going on. Perhaps the downloads are perfectly OK and the Vista md5sum checker is giving misleading results. Perhaps it's a Vista quirk. Perhaps it's a Microsoft plot. :wink:

Do you know anyone with a Linux box who could check the ISOs for you in a Linux terminal? **Much** more reliable.

Lastly, double-check NESFreak's suggestion - have you got the right md5sum file? It's just a text file with the md5sum and the name of the ISO file. If you haven't done so already open it with a text editor and see what's inside.
