### Post by MariusSilverwolf on 2008-02-06
If I read the instructions here properly ([http://www.directron.com/switchat.html](http://www.directron.com/switchat.html)), if you are holding the switch with the button facing down and the top popped off so you can see the interior, connect the wires as follows:

Brown:  Top-Right (Pin 1)
Blue:  Bottom-Right (Pin P1)
Black:  Top-Left (Pin 2)
White: Bottom-Left (Pin P2)

### Post by louieb on 2008-02-06
> **Peter1234123 said:**
> An AT power supply..really old, for an AMD K6..I'm talking about hooking the supply up to the power button (AT power supplies, unlike ATX DO NOT connect to the motherboard,

Actually an AT does supply power to the mother board through  P8 and P9 connectors. 

Anyway I dug up my old Minasi book. Looks like your wondering about the 4 wires going to the switch on the front of the case.

The black wire is hot - goes to the hot side of the wall outlet.
The brown wire is the hot wire to the power supply.
The white wire  - goes to the return side of the wall outlet.
The blue wire - is the return wire for the power supply. 

What Minasi says is that the switch should have some sort of ridge on it. 
and that: 
black and blue go on one side of the ridge
white and brown go on the other.
     
Good luck and keep a flashlight handy - hook em wrong and you will blow the circuit breaker.
