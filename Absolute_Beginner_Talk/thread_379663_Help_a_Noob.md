### Post by Bachstelze on 2007-03-08
Another Linux golden rule :

**If it's not broken, don't fix it.**

I.e. : if it's working well enough for you, don't try to get it working even better or you could end up with it not working at all. As we say here "le mieux est l'ennemi du bien", "the better is the enemy of the good".

### Post by igknighted on 2007-03-08
Almost all device drivers you will need in linux are already included in the kernel.  To update the chipset or alsa (sound) drivers would likely require a recompile of your kernel... not something you want to do unless necessary.

> If it's not broken, don't fix it.
;-) I always that the line was, "If it's not broken, tweak it"
