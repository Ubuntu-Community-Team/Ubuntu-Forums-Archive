[QUOTE=liquid_surfer]shpppuld i be trying to find an alternative to FLASH, and what are the benefits to switching to this alternative, if any?[/QUOTE]

Please do. Flash locks you in with the player- so where the player can't go (powerpc linux, OSes that aren't Linux or Windows, etc.) your Flash can't be seen. Its ALWAYS (for something like this) better to go with the Libre alternative if only because then you are not tied to the fate of the owner of the closed format. Lotsof Visual Basic developers have learned that the hard way recently.

### Post by jobezone on 2005-05-22
There is a dstribution for music and multimedia creation called Agnula. Trying this distro, or checking the applications it contains will probably give you a good vision on linux's current state of audio production.

From the site [http://www.agnula.org/](http://www.agnula.org/) :
"AGNULA's main task is the development of two reference distributions for the GNU/Linux operating system completely based on Free Software (i.e. under a FSF approved Free Software license) and completely devoted to professional and consumer audio applications and multimedia development. One distribution will be Debian-based (DeMuDi) and the other will be Red Hat-based (ReHMuDi)."
