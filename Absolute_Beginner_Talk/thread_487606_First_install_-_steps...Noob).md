### Post by cogadh on 2007-06-29
You should also install the drivers specific to your video, especially if you want to do any gaming or if you want to use the 3D desktops. There is an excellent how-to for Nvidia and ATI products in the Multimedia & Video forum. If you onboard is not Nvidia or ATI, a search of the forums will provide you with plenty of advice on what to do for drivers.

KDE apps generally will work in Gnome, but even if they don't, you can just install KDE in Ubuntu. You can switch between the two different desktop managers at login.

### Post by neotasic1 on 2007-06-29
I presume you need to shrink dual layer DVD's to single layer and that's why you are using DVD Shrink. Personally, I have used K9copy in Gnome rather than DVD shrink.  I used to use DVD Decryptor and Nero recode in Windows but have never had to use them since.  I found K9Copy is quicker also.  Try it out.  If you find it serves the purpose, it may save you the time installing and getting Wine to work properly, assuming that's the only reason you are installing it.

### Post by aeiah on 2007-06-29
despite what a previous poster said, you wont need to install graphics card drivers. this is because your pc has onboard intel graphics. things like compiz, beryl, and 3D games should be supported out of the box.

but yea, you'll probably find it less hastle to use k9copy for shrinking/copying dvds and acidrip to convert dvd to avi than to mess around installing things with wine. you might as well give them a shot. they're available in the repositories, so you can easily download and install them with synaptic (you may need to enable the universe repository etc first, but its easy to do and a quick forum search should give you instructions).

you'll also probably want flash installed too
