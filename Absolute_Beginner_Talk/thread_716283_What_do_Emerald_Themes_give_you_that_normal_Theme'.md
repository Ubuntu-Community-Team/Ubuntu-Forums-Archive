### Post by rainwalker on 2008-03-05
Normal themes are Metacity themes, and they're not as fancy/customizable as Emerald themes.
Emerald gives you waaaayyyy more customization options, and it looks a little different (buttons can glow instead of being highlighted, for example)

### Post by Nythain on 2008-03-05
Yeah, basically Gnome uses Metacity and KDE uses Kwin as default window managers/decorators... Emerald is a different window decorator, giving you more composite options made available by using Compiz/Beryl as a Window Manager/Composite Manager.

Most noticable differences, true transparency and "glowing" effects... there are other customizations too, but they are more minor

No you cant really pick and mix a Metacity theme with an Emerald theme, but there are many Emerald themes modeled after popular gtk and metacity themes...

you can, on the other hand, still keep your cursor, icons, system colors, fonts, etc... as they arent really part of the window decoration process

### Post by Nythain on 2008-03-06
And now i will mention that im an artard and forgot to point out something.

While Emerald will take over the decoration of your windows, picking a decent GTK/Metacity theme is also important, because for the most part, emerald will only decorate your windows.

It doesnt decorate your panels, or change your system colors, or any of the other stuff i mentioned earlier... this is only important because most gtk themes will change your panel... so "combining" emerald themes with similar gtk/metacity themes is still sort of important

### Post by rainwalker on 2008-03-06
Emerald DOES handle the shadows on things, though, which is good and bad.
Good - You can adjust shadows to almost any position and any color you want
Bad - I've noticed they sometimes appear uneven or darker in some places (like around menus) than in others (like around my Firefox window)
