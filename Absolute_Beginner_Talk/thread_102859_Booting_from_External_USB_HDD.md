If your BIOS supports booting from USB, then it shouldn't be a problem. However, you will notice a major bottleneck of speed with the USB connection versus on the internal drive.  I have booted from an external drive using a USB connections, and it took some time to boot the OS.  A lot longer than when the drive was internal.  I have since partitioned my internal drive for both, and use the external drive to house my 4500+ mp3s.

That is my recommendation: partition your internal drive for dual-boot.  If you aren't comfortable with that, then use the Live CD until you feel comfortable.  However, Ubuntu makes install a snap, and you shouldn't have many problems.
