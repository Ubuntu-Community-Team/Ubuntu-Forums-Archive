### Post by JesterDev on 2007-07-15
1: I have an ATI X1300 Pro (AGP) installed and have gotten beryl running. However nvidia cards are the preferred cards for Linux users. I just bought myself a nvidia 6200 for an older AGP based setup and it looks promising. This link is what got desktop effects running on my "Buddha Box" with ATI: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=488385]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=488385")

2: I have ubuntu running on a 900mhz AMD athlon (the above mentioned older setup) and it runs at full speed. However I have not tested beryl on it just yet. I also have it running on a 700mhz P3 dell notebook (with desktop effects) and there is a bit of slowdown but not much. My HP laptop has a 3.2ghz celeron and while it's 'newish' is runs just fine also. 

Hope that helps!

### Post by ReaderRat on 2007-07-15
I was going to try out Compiz on my AMD Athalon (?Processor) and was informed (by my compute) that I had to use restricted drivers that would not be updated in the future.  I would suggest that you go ahead and update your old computer first (Processor + Motherboard are = around $125 for a decent speed), and try out the fancy stuff on it. In any case, [COLOR="Red"]**Back Up Anything That's Important**[/COLOR], if you are going to experiment with your current PC.
