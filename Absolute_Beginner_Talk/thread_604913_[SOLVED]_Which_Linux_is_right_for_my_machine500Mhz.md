### Post by rudeboyskunk on 2007-11-06
This is always the ultimate question:  Which Linux distro is right for me?

In your case, I would say regular Ubuntu would be great!  The gui isn't very Windows-esque, but it's easy and very usable.  The #1 thing to remember throughout though is that Linux is not Windows, so much stuff is different.  So be patient.

Fedora is good too, so if you want to try that out, wait two more days for Fedora 8 to come out.

I run OpenSuSE, which is recommended for beginners to Linux (Windows-like gui).

If you want to try an open-source (or at least free) UNIX, Solaris 10 is good (though not a lot of hardware support for wireless, etc), and PCBSD (which I also use).  Both of these have very Windows-like guis.

If you are curious about Linux, don't hesitate to try out some Live CDs. :) They don't cost you anything, and since you boot directly from the CD, nothing on your computer is changed. Totally risk-free. I'd recommend the Live CDs of [Ubuntu 7.10]("http://www.ubuntu.org"), [Fedora 8]("http://fedoraproject.org/") and [Sabayon]("http://www.sabayonlinux.org/"). By the way, don't give up on the old box! There is always use for older hardware (like my backup server), and you can learn a bit about the basics. Fluxbox should work well. Only thing to remember is that running a small Linux distro for old hardware will not show you how innovative and powerful Linux on desktops is today.

### Post by mivo on 2007-11-06
Any of them! :)  The ones I linked to offer different "manifestations" of Linux. It is all Linux, so under the hood it's the same thing and you can do whatever you see in any of the distros. This is really a lot like shopping for a car: you gotta test-drive different ones to see which vehicle you like best.

The Sabayon Live CD has the unique advantage that it uses the binary video drivers that you normally have to install manually, so it is likely to perform better (which is probably good for first impressions ;)). The others use the open source drivers on the Live CDs, so it is kind of like running XP with the stock video drivers (which as we all know suck badly) which do not support 3D effects on Nvidia cards. So in any event, what you are looking for is concepts and "how things are done", not so much "how fast it responds".

Unless you have limited bandwidth, I'd just try all the ones I mentioned, burn 'em and boot 'em. Fedora and Ubuntu use Gnome, so for the OpenSUSE Live CD I'd go with the KDE version -- just to have seen it all.

Oh, and do come back and tell us what your Linux impressions were! :)

### Post by HousieMousie2 on 2007-12-11
I promised to come back and give my opinion of the Linux I chose... so here it is.

Kubuntu - Gutsy Gibbon

(And just in case you have not read all of this thread and are wondering what Linux distribution you can put on an old machine: the specifications in the Topic Line are NOT for the machine I am using to run Gutsy.
2Ghz processor
1GB RAM
But I did slave the 17 GB HD to another drive that has XP on it.)

There are some issues, but they are minor annoyances and not major troubles.  For instance, having to open Amarok twice before I can play music (the first time it sits there and does nothing.)  I can not use the scanner on my Lexmark printer/scanner/copier, but keeping the dual-boot fixes that... again, annoying, but not unworkable.
I do need to find the thread I read during one of my random reading sessions, telling me how to allow XP to copy files from Gutsy.  It's nice that Gutsy has no problems sifting through XP, but I need it to flow the other way too... no fault there.

I was never one to heavily fiddle with the look of my machine... an imported wallpaper, and slightly altering an existing color scheme to match my wallpaper and I was a happy camper.  I have found tweaking the colors in K Gutsy to be something of a hassle.  Half the time you can not tell what the effects of your selections were until after you hit Apply, since the previewing area doesn't show examples of all of the tweakable 'fields.'
lol And I had to fight with it a while to make it stop showing my clock as a white font on a white background... Duh-me, I should have tried a reboot sooner.

Minor annoyances.

For the most part I am enjoying K Gutsy, loads fast, shuts down fast, great selection of programs (not always easy to figure out which one does what, but oh well, free is right within my budget.)
A firewall that just sits there silently minding it's business so I can mind mine... instead of asking me questions every few minutes.
My maintenance and good house keeping skills are developing atrophy. (Shall I cheer now or save it for when I am by myself?)
I know I could have had a program that would have fetched my e-mail for me while in XP, I guess I had a knee jerk reaction to on-board e-mail clients, but who can blame me?  It was a attitude set in stone by the horrid vulnerabilities of Outlook Express (Win95 vintage.)  Setting up Thunderbird was super easy and not having to do anything to get my mail delivered is a nice little luxury.

Initial set-up of Gutsy on a whole was frustrating and time consuming (more so than with Windows... but that was mostly in the way of research) but since then Gutsy has paid me back my lost time and then some!  The one click updating is hands down better than Windows ever thought of being.

Oh yeah... someone might want to warn people that after so many boot-ups it's going to take off and do it's own thing for few moments! LOL  The surprise is a good way to test a new user's heart function.

I am a task bar junkie, so I have yet to find a use for multiple desktops... I guess I keep the default spare on the off chance the primary desktop gets tired. :tongue:

I never realized that the habit of 'throwing' the pointer aside could be bad for my mental health, but I am getting used to not doing it (or more often, undoing it) since I do very much like the focus following the pointer.

... yes, just like it sounds, the majority of this is simply a matter of getting used to a new OS.

I do wish that I could be a little more hands on with this machine, not knowing how to go about various tweaks, and finding most pre-existing forum posts beyond my understanding is a little frustrating.  It is not likely that anyone will ever hear me say 'It's just a simple script' unless I am talking about a literary work.

While I am not afraid of the command line, I don't have the time or the facility to learn the commands, although there is 'some' interest.

In summary, I would have to say that it has been a largely positive experience and it seems highly unlikely that I will dump Gutsy... unless, that is, to give it a bigger home.  For the time being, dual-boot suits me just fine... XP for when I HAVE to have it and Gutsy for the rest of the time!

Long live Linux!

Oh yeah, one more thing... you forum folks just sweeten the deal.  I never have had technical support for my machines, at least not the paid variety, so I have prowled many a forum.  Take it from me, you all are some of the nicest around.
