Just FYI:  try not to say "shortcut" any more.  Microsoft introduced the word to computer users in several different contexts, and it has been a headache ever since.  

In Microsoftese, it can mean "launcher," (something which, when clicked, launches a program) "bookmark,"  "link", or "embedded object,"  each of which is a very different thing indeed.  

All your launchers and any files you place on your desktop are mirrored on all your workspaces.   

In fact, the "desktop" is nothing more than a respresentation of the contents of your ~/Desktop/ directory.  So it may even contain files and whole programs...(Windows does the same thing, but doesn't call it the same thing, obviously)
