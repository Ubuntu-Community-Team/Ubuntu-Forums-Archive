### Post by Omnios on 2008-02-27
Dells might not be the best but I bought XPS 1530 almost two months ago and totally love it. I bought mine as a replacement PC instead of a tower upgrade and it is working beyond my expectations.
 
 Since Dell started selling laptops with dell on them the honor the hardware service package even if you install Linux on it. There are some brands out there that will void you hardware agreement if you install Linux on them.
 
 Ubuntu installs on dell XPS computers seem bang on with a lot of users stating everything works.
 
 I also got the base 1280x res desplay and used the live disk for Ubuntu and Kubuntu. As for the desktop the Ubutu is similar to layout with vista and workable. Kubuntu is more tweekable allowing to shrink window frames etc to a smaller but still readable size allowing for better desktop usage and management.
 
 Also I purchased the extended harware service package that costs about an extra $200 with tax but gives you a total of three years service. I bough this on recomendations of a few shop keepers in the town I live in. If something is going to creap out iit usually happens about a year afterpurchase. The XPS is a new deveopment of dell and a sleeker and better so they should not crap out after a year but I felt better safe than sorry. There is also an accidental service which sounds good but I was already over my budget so did not get it.
 
 My experience with the xps 1530 so far is with its 15.4 wide screen it is just as usable as a tower with a 17" monitor and working well as a replacement pc. 
 
 Only problem I did not like is I was totally off on the hard drive. I nerver user Vista before and it uses huge amounts of storage. I have a 160 hard drive and its way to small for dual booting. I would recomend at least 150Gigs for a dual boot system.
