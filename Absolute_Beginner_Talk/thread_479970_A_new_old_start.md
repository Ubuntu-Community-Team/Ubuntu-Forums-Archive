### Post by p_quarles on 2007-06-24
I only know so much, but I think I can give you a couple ideas.

1) For speed, I think you'll need at least a Pentium 4 and 512 MB of RAM.

2) If you want wireless, go with a Netgear or Intel card. They have the best support in Linux. The others are spotty. For wired ethernet, support is much less of an issue. 

3) Best graphics controllers for Linux are Intel and nVidia. Avoid ATI, as that seems to cause a lot of problems for people. 

4) Read the Community Support hardware pages. They're kind of outdated in some cases, but it's a useful way of making sure that you're components will be supported:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport)

Hope this helps.

if a laptop is what you need, look for a ThinkPad. they are very well made, have top quality components, are very durable and are known to play well with linux. you can find plenty available on Ebay etc. look for one of the regular sellers who specialize in refurb lappy's that are back off enterprise leases. then look for the highest spec box ( i like the T series,myself) that fits within your budget. just check the vid card and the wifi card and search on these forums to see how they treat others.

i have a T42 - Pm 1.8Ghz / 1 GB ram / ibm on-board a/b/g wifi / bluetooth - which has been running flawlessly on ubuntu since Breezy. the wifi on mine has atheros 5212 chipset and works out of the box using the madwifi driver, but check the chipset used on what ever card you are thinking of getting. mine also has an ATI Radeon mobility 9600 vid card which works nicely enough (i do not use that box for gaming, anyway), including some hardware acceleration using the free ati driver although the fglrx driver does not support hardware acceleration for this particular card. if you can find one of these in your targeted price range i highly recommend it.
