### Post by Michl on 2007-04-24
I've been running a clean install Feisty on a computer I do not depend on and has very little installed.  I use it to check email and print files I bring to it from work.  Everything went smoothly,  so I considered upgrading to Feisty on my work computers where I run edgy.  I ran into trouble right away -- a 'failed to fetch error' note and so I began to  search here for upgrade trouble and discovered that there is a ton of it, a sh&*load of it.  It seems so bad that this is my conclusion:

Upgrade to Feisty only if you are adventurous and have plenty of time to learn about editing config files or are a very experienced ubuntu user.  If you don't have the time and you simply cannot risk days reconfiguring and reconstructing your computer because you have other work to do, do not upgrade to Feisty.  

That is so frustrating.  I know, I know, there are lots for whom it went smoothly, but there are also lots and lots for whom this was a trip into what I would call computer hell, whose way out from my experience is a clean install anyway, and I don;t have time to risk that.   It's the risk factor that is significant.  

Michl
