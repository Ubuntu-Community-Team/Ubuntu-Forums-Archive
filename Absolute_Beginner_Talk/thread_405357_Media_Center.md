### Post by zheepeez on 2007-06-14
It depends kinda on what hardware you're using... I'm in the (planning :D) process of setting one up, and I found that if you're wanting to connect your media PC to any form of home stereo system, you want a sound card with **at the very least** RCA outputs, maybe even FireWire/USB outs to a special receiver. There's very little point in putting audio out through a 9 mm jack (which is standard for PC's) and then splitting to dual RCA 
or using a data projector port for video - you want dedicated hardware.

Which means that if you don't already have the hardware, check the forums (and compatibility lists for video cards) and the pages of the software you're going to use (LinuxMCE is nice, also look up MythTV and/or Elisa) before you buy. This will make your life so much easier later on.
