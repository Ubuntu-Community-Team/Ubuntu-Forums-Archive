### Post by SkyNet2029 on 2006-07-11
That really depends on what type of wireless card you are using as to whether or not it will be a 'show-stopper' for your configuration. That being said, there are not that many wireless cards (or hardware devices, for that matter) that does not have an workaround available in Linux. Many times it can be a simple matter of getting to know the CLI and things like iwconfig/lspci/iwlist and such. I won't go into those as that could turn into a rather long post. The bottom line is that even though it might take some extra work on your part (extra like finding the windows drivers for your card and doing some magic with Ndiswrapper or Mad-Wifi), almost anything is possible with Linux. Especially with *buntu, since it's Debian-based and has a rock-solid hardware compatibilty history. Plus, there's this great Forum when your'e in a pickle. ;)
