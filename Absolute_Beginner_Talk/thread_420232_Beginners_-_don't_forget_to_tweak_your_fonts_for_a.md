Hi all,

Well I've been running Ubuntu for only a few months now, but when I upgraded to Feisty, something just didn't look right anymore about my fonts. They looked fat and bloated, fuzzy and generally just poor!

Then I remembered to go to System . . . Preferences . . . Font and select 'subpixel smoothing' for my LCD. I think I had to restart the system to see the change, but it is indeed drastic. So if you have an LCD - do yourself a favour and give it a try. I think there are more advanced tweaks to improve the fonts more, but this simple change made a world of difference for me.

If any of you experts know terminal command that enables the option I describe above, feel free to enlighten us . . .
