### Post by Dusty545 on 2006-12-28
Realistically, this is more of a task for something like your router.  It should be able to give you a report as to what MAC and/or IP addresses it's been feeding to,  I always advise that users lock down this aspect of their home networks first along with a good, solid password to go with their encryption.

On the software side, there's a veritable plethora of available tools for network sleuthing but, if you've got the IP and MAC addresses locked down and a encryption with a good password then this is likely enough to deter all but the most determined of bandwidth thieves.  I recommend getting these sorted first before you give yourself headaches with network analysis.
