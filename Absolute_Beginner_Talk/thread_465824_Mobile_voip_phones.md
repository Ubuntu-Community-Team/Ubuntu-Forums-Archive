That sounds like a great idea.

In the US Sprint has a broadband connection with unlimited usage available for laptops via a card.  I plugged in a headset and was able to use the free (but not open source) software from [Gizmo]("http://www.gizmoproject.com/") while on vacation.  It was nice when we went on vacation and I could just forward phone calls to the Gizmo # and receive incoming calls as if I was home.  When I wanted to anyway.  It would take messages for the calls I didn't take.

Some companies also carried ATA adapters that could you could hook up to your broadband and your phone system for VoIP service and you could insert one of your cell phone cards in it so you could make and receive free calls over your VoIP network with your cell phone.  That would be if you could make free "in network" calls or had a "family plan" etc.

The adapter would take the call via the cell phone card as if it were a regular cell phone and then forward the call to you.  Alternatively, you could call the card and the adapter would then forward it to whatever # you dialed over the VoIP lines.  Therefore you could end up using no minutes for incoming or outgoing calls that you routed through that other card & adapter.  Pretty slick.

A lot of those cell phone plans were about an extra $10 a month for an extra line so that and the adapter were your only expense if you already had VoIP.
