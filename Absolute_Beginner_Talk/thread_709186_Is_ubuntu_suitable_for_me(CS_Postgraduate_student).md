### Post by angrboda on 2008-02-27
I'd say the question is rather: do you really feel like you want to go to all the trouble of re-installing your system? If you _want_ to switch then you might want to take a look at texmaker, which is part of the texlive set of packages, a fork of the kile project (or rather, if I remember correctly, its ancestor), definitely faster and independent of a kde-environment. It is very much like kile but without the typical kde-sluggishness. I used to work with it a lot before I switched to vim (with vim-latex-suite), which has the advantage of running smoothly even on my carbon-dated notebook.
By the way has SuSE made the step from teTex to texlive yet? If not _that_ would be a good argument for switching back to Ubuntu as texlive is definitely better.
