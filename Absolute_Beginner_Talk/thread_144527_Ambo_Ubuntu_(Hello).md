### Post by Mr. Polite on 2006-03-14
Two days.
Not even two days really.  It was probably a matter of four hours sprinkled over the span of two days that it took me to install, configure and customize Ubuntu to the point that my wife can comfortably use it without asking questions. I haven't been able to get that to happen in XP for a number of years.

This is supposed to be hard for me. I've been using MS Windows since 3.11, and became a power user after the '95 patch, '98.   I've never stepped out of the Wincage (TM)  until I started looking for non-$300 alternatives for an operating system.

I installed Fedora Core 4 (GNOME) about a week ago. It worked with most of my hardware and I was able to get some functionality. What I wasn't happy with was the support. Everywhere I looked I saw people helping people with Ubuntu. One .ISO download later (only one, not four?), and I was Human again.

Mounting my Windows (NTFS) partitions was a snap after reading the Ubuntu Wiki,  as was installing the updated version of Firefox. Even the epic task of installing a printer sans bloat in Windows (2 hours), was handled in a total of 6 minutes thanks to all the built in HP support.

What I'm most happy about is Ubuntu passing my wifes test this morning. I left her a note listing her login and password and found my way out of the house for other diviations. When I returned, there she was, plugging away at a document that was previously prepared in MS Word 12. She looked up, asked "should I save in the OpenOffice format from now on?", and continued working.

Of course there is still much more to do. Next on my list is streaming content from the NTFS Media drive to XMBC machines on my network. I've installed SAMBA so I think I'm well on my way to getting that done.

A big thank you to the dev team, and members of the community who contributed to the Ubuntu Wiki. I've still got tons to learn, but hopefully I can one day become a contributing member of this community. Ambo Ubuntu.
