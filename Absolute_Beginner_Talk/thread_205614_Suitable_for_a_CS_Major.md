### Post by Max Luebbe on 2006-06-29
If you go the Java Route, you really can't go wrong with Ubuntu. 
Likewise for C.

However, my experiences at school were that sometimes the instructor you get may try and force you to use a particular tool to complete your assignments.

Where I was at, this was Eclipse and as part of our assignments occasionally we'd have to use various Eclipse features to get full credit.

If this happens, you'll probably still be fine no matter what - unless your teacher decides to become an advocate of MSFT VisStudio, or if you're going to a school from the dark ages, Borland Builder.

My experiences were that using linux helped me become a better Computer Scientist as doing all the experimentation that comes with one's first switch to linux got me more into my classes and computers in general. Also, having just about any development tool in any language at your fingertips, for free didn't hurt.

Sorry to hear that xtacocorex, I worked with a bunch of Mechanical and Aero guys and we were able to secure the university site license for Pro/E.

It really is too bad there isn't a legit GNU Cad suite thats worth a damn.

It's pretty slick. They also have scripts that will test students homework to see if they work properly.
