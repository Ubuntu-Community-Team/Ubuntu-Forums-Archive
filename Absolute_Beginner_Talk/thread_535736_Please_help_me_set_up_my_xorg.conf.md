### Post by bdoe on 2007-08-26
Ummm...  That's a flat-panel monitor you've got there (and a pretty darned nice one at that)!  Since LCD's don't refresh in the conventional sense of the term, there's really no reason to need anything higher than 60Hz.

Also, are you sure your video card can handle 75Hz refreshes at that high of a resolution?  The image artifacts you're seeing sound like the result of pushing the pixel clock too hard.

Lastly, most LCD displays have some sort of "auto" setting that will automatically adjust its display parameters to give the best display possible at a given resolution/refresh when you press the button for it or select it out of a menu on the monitor.  Have you tried this?
