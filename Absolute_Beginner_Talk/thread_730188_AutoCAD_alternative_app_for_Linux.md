### Post by prcm311 on 2008-03-25
I'm a Civil Engineer and I too am looking for a good alternative to AutoCAD.  I find QCad to be inadequate at first glance, it appears to lack 3D unless I'm missing something but I'll fool with it some more.  

As far as spending money I would have no intention of buying a CAD software for Linux as it would be for personal use.  However for a business go with Windows.  I know that's not what people want to hear but the reality is if you have to run a business you have to go that route.  With programs like Revit, Archicad and Civil 3D only available for Windows (Archicad may have a port to Linux as it is available for Mac) you will be left in the dust if you don't.  That's the catch 22 of open source, you can't use it because there aren't specific programs and there aren't specific programs because you don't use it.
