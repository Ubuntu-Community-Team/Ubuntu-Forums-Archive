### Post by kpkeerthi on 2007-06-28
You should be OK doing that. The only downside I have noticed is KDE apps start a bit slower when launched in GNOME, as the KDE specific libraries need to be loaded just-in-time. Thats only for the first time they are launched. But they 'run' as good as they do in KDE, in terms of speed. I use K3B in gnome and I have nothing to complain.

They will probably use a bit more memory, but if you have anything more than 512MB or 1GB then it wont be that bad.
 
Crashing depends more on the application. KoolDock crashed on me a bunch of times, so I got rid of it. K3B on the other hand has never crashed on me (yet).
 
Installing extra libraries has no downside except that a little more space is used up on your hard drive.
