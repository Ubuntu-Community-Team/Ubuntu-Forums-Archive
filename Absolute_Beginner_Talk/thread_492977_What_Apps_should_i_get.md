Everything depends about what you want to do with your computer...
Anyway I'm going to give you a list of my most typically used apps.

[LIST]
[*]**Pidgin 2.0.2**  (the new GAIM. Is almost the same but with lots of bugfixes) You can get a simple .deb installation file here [http://www.getdeb.net](http://www.getdeb.net)
[*]**Google Earth** (do you like our planet?)  download from here [http://earth.google.com/](http://earth.google.com/) then double clik to that file.
[*]**Firefox** (included by default)
[*]**OpenOffice** (Included By Default)
[*]**Zattoo TV**  (It works only in some areas) 
[*]**Google Desktop**  (A very useful tool to search and index your pc)
[*]**Skype 1.4** (a IPTelephone client)
[*]**Azureus** (Bittorrent client)
[*]**Wine 0.9.40** (A compatibility layer witch allows you to run some win apps in linux)
[*]**Cedega 6.0** (same than wine but specialized on games)
[*]**Audacious** ( do you use winamp? This is the most similar one you can find for linux)
[/LIST]



By the way, if you want to install flash player...
Just go to any webpage with flash (like this one [www.vilaweb.cat]("http://www.vilaweb.cat") ) Then just follow the instructions by hitting the button in your up right corner.

### Post by moredhel on 2007-07-05
dvdrip - dvd ripper (xD)
exaile - very good music player
music-applet - control various gnome music players from a gnome-panel applet
ntfs-3g - lets you write to NTFS formatted space on your harddrive
unrar - to extract .rar (never)
xchat - Best IRC client ever, the ubuntu irc is always busy will people needing help and people giving help ;)
vlc - imo best media/dvd player to walk the earth
gnome-art - convenient way to get gnome themes/wallpapers/icons. no more [www.gnome-look.org](www.gnome-look.org) :D
electricsheep - best screensaver ever. beware, it eats bandwidth. lots.
