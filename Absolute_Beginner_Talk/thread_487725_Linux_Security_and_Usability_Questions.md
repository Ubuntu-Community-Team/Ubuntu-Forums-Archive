### Post by cogadh on 2007-06-29
> **Nimaran said:**
> Hi,

I'm new to Linux and I was wondering about security. Yeah, I know everyone says Linux doesn't have any viruses and stuff, but if the source code is out there for everyone to see couldn't someone figure out how to hack someone's system?

On the rare occasions when some one finds an exploitable piece of source code, the exploit is usually fixed in a matter of days, sometimes in a matter of hours. Linux has literally hundreds of thousands of programmers worldwide looking at and improving the kernel and application source code on a regular basis. Additionally, if someone does find a flaw in Linux, the entire Linux community is notified immediately and advice is given on how to protect yourself from it. "Hackers" don't ever really have an opportunity to take advantage any flaws in Linux. 

Compare that to Microsoft, where there are just a few hundred programmers  who can take months to come up for a fix for a single exploit.  MS will hide exploit info from it's users until they have a patch ready, instead of warning them of the danger. In the meantime, innocent Windows users are left to their own devices, spreading viruses and allowing their systems to be hijacked.

Linux does have viruses, however... [14 of them since 1991]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Linux_computer_viruses"). Compare that to Windows where we had over 103,000 different viruses before 2005. That alone speaks volumes about the security of Linux.

### Post by lisati on 2007-06-30
Don't sweat the security stuff unnecessarily.......simple common sense is often sufficient, like not opening attachments from people you don't know (so you don't catch something nasty unintentionally), and don't leave your computer turned on if you don't have to (so people can't sneak in while you're not looking, and anything you might have picked up unawares doesn't have a chance to make a nuisance of itself....) Don't forget to keep your system up-to-date - check out System->Administration->Update manager

If you're still nervous, then there are tools available to check and/or protect your system, like firewalls and anti-virus and anti-malware scanners. The people reading your question will probably each have their favourites.....

And **don't be scared of keeping your system up-to-date. **
