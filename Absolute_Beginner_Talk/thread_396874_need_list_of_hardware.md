### Post by cowlip on 2007-03-30
**Graphics cards: ** Intel integrated chips are fully open source, 3d supported, and will be supported forever.  

ATI cards up to the X600 (and maybe further as they work on it) are supported by the 3d open source ati driver.  

Nvidia cards will probably be supported the open source 3d nouveau drivers in the future; as of now, they have pretty good closed driver support from Nvidia.  

Unless you're buying a card lower than hte X600, I would not buy ATI because the PROPRIETARY drivers do not support aiglx (beryl and compiz) or xvmc.

**Sound card:** anything integrated seems to be good.  If you're looking for high end stuff, anything NOT creative, recently, is good, and there's a thread in the Community forum about high end production cards on Linux.  The only good Creative cards are sound blaster live and audigy; the X-Fi does not work on Linux (and it's all Creative's fault)

Generally HP printers are supported.  I'm sure others will pitch in and post links :)
