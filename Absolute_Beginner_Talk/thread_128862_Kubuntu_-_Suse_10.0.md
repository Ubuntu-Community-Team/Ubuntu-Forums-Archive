### Post by garnertr on 2006-02-12
Greetings,

I've been away for a while and I thought I just give a quick update on what myself and my POS Laptop have been doing...

For those that have seen my previous postings, I have an Alienware Sentia 2-gig, 855 intel Chipset, wireless, 80-gig hd laptop.  I've been playing around w/ various distros for the past several months and well, I always return to Ubuntu.

Last week, when I was in Seal Beach, CA, I went to the local Barnes/Nobles and loaded up on Linux Magazines, there are only two/three that I follow (its the ones w/ distros enclosed), anyway, I started messing around w/ Suse 10.

SUSE 10, loaded up fine, looks great, acknowledged all my hardware (which various distros fail to do), but it would run slow as a freaking snail.  Install was outstanding and I'd suggest that Ubuntu team personnel look over SUSE's install/hardware detection and take a peak under the hood.

Last night fighting the 5-minute click and wait, I reloaded Unbuntu.  I then decided to try KDE, for some odd reason, this thought never occurred to me and I believe its b/c of my usual fear of trying new things...

I have to say that I'm VERY IMPRESSED w/ KDE, I can see why (now) there are numerous discussions, arguments throughout the web, regarding Gnome/KDE (which I chalk up to personal choice).  But for now, I'm using KDE and having a great experience.

My laptop has thus far been working fine, still have issues w/ my generic DVD-RW drive, but that is the least of my problems...which I'll work on sometime in the future.

But for now, I'm enjoying KDE, have my mail, IM and browser all cranking out great stuff and now I'm messing around w/ amaroK which has just finished my 2000+ collection of OGG files... so I'm off for some tunes...

Enjoy the day and thanks Ubuntu Team for creating a great distro!
