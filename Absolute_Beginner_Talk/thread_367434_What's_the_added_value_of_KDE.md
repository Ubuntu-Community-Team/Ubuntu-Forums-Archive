### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-02-22
Many people like KDEs 'configurability'. That is - virtually every aspect of KDEs appearance can be modified to suit you. KDEs core applications are also widely liked, whereas Gnome's are often a little underdeveloped. Gnome is also criticised because those tools which are available are often hidden, or not immediately obvious. I personally prefer Gnome - it doesn't get in the way, and it's not like I spend my whole time clicking through menus. KDE is far too flashy and 'look at me!' for my needs.

### Post by chavo on 2007-02-22
System wide mouse gestures and hotkeys.
Very configurable.
Speedier than Gnome, uses less ram. A lot of people think that because gnome has less featuers that it uses less resources, not true.
Kio slaves.
Dcop for interapp communication and scripting.
Some really amazing apps: Konqueror, Amarok, K3b, etc.
Tight integration between apps, this comes with the kio slaves and dcop.
fish:/, had to mention this kio slave in particular, it rocks. Just put fish://name@host into the address bar or open/save dialog to use your remote filesystems. You can bookmark it as well of course.

Just a couple of the cool technologies that KDE offers, off the top of my head.

### Post by nereid on 2007-02-22
KDE is a little bit better implemented. Under the hood everything just fits together (more or less). GNOME looks nice but it is a real pain in the behind under the hood.

You can skin them booth to look real good. On the downside, there are some things, that are possible in GNOME that you can't do easily in KDE and vice versa. But what really annoys the heck out of me is Metacity (I know you can change the wm but then you have to choose on of the *boxes to let it really fit in).

So everything is just your personal preference, which toolkit do you use more often? GTK or Qt? If you're on the GTK side GNOME would be the better choice and on the other hand with Qt goes KDE (you can use the qtengine for GTK but it ain't the same).

For ultimate L33TN3SS use only one of the window managers, for example Ion or WM2 maybe even RatPoison :D

**EDIT:**
XFCE uses the same toolkit as GNOME, GTK2, but is a little bit lighter. This comes at the cost of utils available directly for the desktop environment. But it is indeed a nice little desktop environment, the smallest available at the time.
