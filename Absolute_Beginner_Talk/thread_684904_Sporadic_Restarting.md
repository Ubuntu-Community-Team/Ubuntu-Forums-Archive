### Post by opscure on 2008-02-01
This sounds like more of a hardware problem, ie bad memory, poorly grounded motherboard, etc.  But to be sure you should check out your log files in /var/log/ (dmesg, messages, and any others pertaining to system functions) to see if any abnormalities have shown up.  Or post them to the board and maybe the community can help you a long.  

I would first ensure that your hardware is not bad by going through the motions of troubleshooting each device that would cause a full reboot.  so, do a memory test, ensure your motherboard is grounded properly, and check/replace your psu.
