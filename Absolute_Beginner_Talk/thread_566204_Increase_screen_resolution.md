### Post by johnbradbury on 2007-10-03
If that's as high as it will let you go then one of three things will be causing it:

1. The graphic drivers you have installed aren't the best suited for the features and performance of your card.

2. The graphics card doesn't support resolutions above this.

3. The installed monitor doesn't support resolutions above this.

I'm still new to this stuff myself so maybe someone more experienced could confirm?

### Post by Terl on 2007-10-03
Do you have the graphics driver installed?  If not, the easiest way to do this is to use the restricted drivers manager.  Under administration menu.

If you have the driver installed already, a quick edit of the xorg.conf will work and is quite easy to do.
