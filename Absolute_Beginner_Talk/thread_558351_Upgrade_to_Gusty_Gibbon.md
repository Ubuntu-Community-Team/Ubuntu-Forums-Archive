### Post by anewguy on 2007-09-24
As an old timer ex systems administrator and systems programmer, I'll drop my 2 cents in.  Mind you, I am relatively new to Linux myself and nothing more than a novice!:)

Old experience tells me 2 things in regards to operating systems upgrades:

1.  Never install a development version unless you either have a specific bug that requires you to try the upgrade  -or-  you are a developer intent on working on the new release.

2.  Never install a new release until at least a month or more goes by unless you absolutely have to.  Why?  The bigger bugs left after the OS goes into general release.  Let someone else find them and have a solution.  This is one point I think is really important for beginners.  You might be enjoying your current installation and thinking "why not?".  If you do, be prepared for the inevitable bugs that will appear.  Why not keep your experience enjoyable and let someone else find the bugs?

Just my 2 cents (if it's even worth that!!):)
