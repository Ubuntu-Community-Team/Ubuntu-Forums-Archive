### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-22
I have a laptop and I bought a second HDD and placed it in one of those external USB enclosures.

I downloaded Ghost for Linux (G4L) and burnt the iso to a CD.

[http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l](http://sourceforge.net/projects/g4l)

I just plug-in the external drive and then boot from the G4L disk. 
I then choose to do a local clone and it steps you through drive selecton to/from.

It makes an exact clone of the HDD, including GRUB.

The only downside is that to copy a 120 Gb HDD takes about 3.5 hours via USB. 
Of course, if you have an internal scsi, or IDE drive, it should be quicker.
