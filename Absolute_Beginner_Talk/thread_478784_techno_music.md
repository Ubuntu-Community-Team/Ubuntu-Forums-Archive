### Post by zettberlin on 2007-06-20
> **Codename said:**
> Fruity Loops was really good, I'm not sure if there is a Linux version of that.


No - there is none.

But to try a program styled like this, you should have a look at lmms:

[http://lmms.sourceforge.net/home.php](http://lmms.sourceforge.net/home.php)

At the other hand you want beats/loops right? So modular working with small specialized apps is quite OK...

If I want such loops I use Seq24 to trigger the Sampler Specimen (for percussion/noise etc) and/or ZynaddSubFX and Alsa Modular Synth for bass/synth-sounds. Very cool getting simple beats reall fast are also jackbeat and slag. 

Cut the loops with Ardour and/or Rezound.

In the end there is somewhat more work to do to get results in Linux but what you get is your very own stuff at pro-level - and that's what matters ;-)


All the apps are available via Synaptic, be sure to install linux-lowlatency, jackit and qjackctl. Easiest way ist to get the ubuntustudio-metapackages...
