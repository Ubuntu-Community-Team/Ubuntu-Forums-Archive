### Post by petervk on 2006-03-25
Sorry man, it won't as easy as you are hoping.

A dual CPU motherboard will be able to "combine the power" of two processors, but the processors must be designed to support multiple CPU's, and typically the desktop processors do not do this. (read up on the processors you have, but really, I highly doubt it.)

Another issue you have is the processors are different speeds. For this to work they will have to be running at exactly the same speed. Once again, if the processor supports it you may be able to underclock the faster processor to run at only 300mhz, but thats kind of stupid. (you would go from 500mhz to a grand total of 600mhz that may not work.)

And the final issue is that if the processors are of a different make (p3 and p2) it really won't work. You'll need two p3 or two p2 or 2 xeons, or whatever.

Sorry to so completely burst you bubble but there is a lot of big "ifs" that need to be addressed for this to work. But if you do get it working, tell us. Could be an interesting project.

[INDENT]
Oh, and on an interesting side note, I'm currently posting from a P3 550, running Ubuntu Linux. It runs great. You could probably get ubuntu running fine on the 500mhz cpu, you just need to make sure you have enough ram. I'd suggest 256 as the minimum recommend for running ubuntu properly. This computer has 384mb of pc100 sdram and it runs great.[/INDENT]
