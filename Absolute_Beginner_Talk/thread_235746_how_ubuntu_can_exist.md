### Post by kebes on 2006-08-13
> **kaffelars said:**
> Ubuntu was started by the very wealthy Mark Shuttleworth, who invested a lot in a company called Canonical. This company is developing Ubuntu (alongside all the volunteers), and is also selling support and customized solutions.
That's a *very* short version ;)

That's all absolutely true. However I think it's worth pointing out that Ubuntu exists because of an extensive amount of work that people have put into Linux over the years. Thousands of man-hours of time have been spent making all the sub-programs that make Ubuntu what it is. Although Canonical has done amazing things with helping create a very polished distribution, the core features in Linux were created (mostly) by people for free, and distributed for free. Basically alot of people independantly decided that they "wanted something better" and then worked to make it. They donate their time and we all enjoy the results.

Similarly, Ubuntu is doing very well mostly because of the time and effort of the people involved. There are lots of people who put in alot of their time, and do not receive money in return. Look at the amount of work that goes into these forums! It is really the community that drives these projects forward.
