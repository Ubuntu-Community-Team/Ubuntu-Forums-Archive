### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-12-14
Hi. Hardware manufacturers design and build their products mainly--and most often, exclusively--to be used by windows. As it is, very few create drivers for other operating systems, or release specifications so third parties can create the necessary drivers. You can then imagine the monumental task Linux developers have to face to create the drivers from scratch. Even so, the Linux community of programmers and contributors have made impressive progress in that area recently, and many hardware manufacturers are opening up their specifications and have even started to develop Linux drivers. The situation with nVidia motherboard sound chipsets is unfortunate at the moment, but given time we'll likely see drivers released. It is not for me to say when this will happen, as I am just a user, and I'm sorry I cannot offer you a clear solution to your problem, other than the all too common suggestion to get a new sound card, if possible.
