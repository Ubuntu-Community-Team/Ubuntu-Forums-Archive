### Post by InlawBiker on 2007-06-30
I've tried them all, and coming from the Windows world nothing on Linux is quite as good as bitlord - except for rtorrent.

I put up with Azureus for a while but I can't get over the amount of RAM and CPU it uses.  It's also prone to crashing and just doesn't work right.  The built-in gtorrent client is good but very simple.

So I installed rtorrent (required libtorrent) and it's awesome.  So it doesn't have a gui - so what.    It has almost no overhead - showing 0% cpu - and 11.7m RAM with a 4gb torrent open.  Take a look at what Azureus is using sometime, it's shocking.
