### Post by blueridgedog on 2007-12-30
First, boot off of the CD and run Ubuntu.  Are the results satisfactory?  Is your hardware recognized and functioning?
> 
1) When installing Ubuntu, will it automatically wipe Vista off my system?

If you are satisfied with Ubuntu and the hardware support for your laptop, and elect to install it, you will be given a chance to resize your Vista partition to make room for Ubuntu, resulting in a dual boot system.

> 2) Will I be able to use Wine to successfully run most of my old windows programs (and will Wine allow me to use games such as Call of Duty 4?)

The honest answer is probably no, but not unilaterally.  Check the wine database for each application:

[http://appdb.winehq.org/](http://appdb.winehq.org/)

Search on each application that interests you.  I use several apps in wine and find it meets my needs.

> 3) Does Ubuntu provide drivers for GeForce 8800 graphics cards and most new creative sound cards?

After installing Ubuntu, you will have the option of enabling restricted drivers and my experience is that support is pretty good for advanced graphics.
