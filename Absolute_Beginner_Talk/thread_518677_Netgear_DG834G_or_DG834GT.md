### Post by laidback on 2007-08-06
Funny thing, I have just borrowed a wireless DG834G v3 from a friend to test wireless on my daughter's laptop. Yes it does work, but I found setup annoying, not nearly as straight forward as on my eTEC (which doesn't have wireless). My ISP uses static IP addresses, after much effort I gave up with this method as it simply didn't work. Instead I did what the manual says not to do with non Windows OSs and static IPs, that's use the auto detect system. That worked!
I'm using a Netgear pcmcia card, WG511T. It works but the range appears poor. I'm only getting 70% readings when the laptop is right beside the arial!! doesn't seem very good. I can go downstairs and use it, about 25 feet. Beyond that it stops. I also have a D-Link Airplus DWL-650+ to try. That's todays project so I'll keep you posted. I'm using 7.04 which seems to detect the pcmcia card for a driver.

### Post by laidback on 2007-08-06
I've continued with my tests on the 2 pcmcia cards, Netgear WG511T and D-Link Airplus DWL-650+. Both work but the D-Link offers a greater range and apparent reception levels (as measured by Ubuntu's Network info). The Netgear gives levels between 69% and 23% for a working connection. The 69% is when the laptop is right beside the router/modem. The 23% levels are at a range of about 25-30 feet (upstairs to downstairs). The D-Link on the other hand starts at 57% when right beside the router/modem but doesn't fall off when I go downstairs, the levels are 52-57% there. In the garden, about 50+ feet away the Netgear gives up with a reception level of 2-16% whilst the D-Link works with a level of 34%. If I was to buy again it would be the D-Link for me. 

Perhaps more informed users can comment as this is the first time I've used wireless, so it's another learning curve.

In case it matters. In the above I rebooted the laptop at each new location, after each test I shutdown and moved to the next location.
