### Post by bigfox on 2007-07-21
Yes, It could have been somebody trying to get you to connect to it and then sniff your traffic to find information about you like passwords or CC numbers.

You should be careful on all public access points.  

Steve Gibson at Gibson Research does a podcast on network and internet security.

### Post by bigfox on 2007-07-21
If you just connected and didn't actually pass traffic (Like visit your banks website and login) then you should be fine.

However the same hacker may have also been connected to the official access point of the hotel and sniffed that as well.

The point is that no open wireless network or public accessible network (Such as the hotel lan) is perfectly safe.  There are many ways to sift through network traffic regardless of what operating system the attacked computer is using.

### Post by Cappy on 2007-07-21
One thing you can do is go onto a secure connection, say gmail, or anything else that has a https webpage. If you submit to it and it gives you a nearly normal looking error message that looks like "The host did not resolve to the same as the certificate, would you like to continue?" or something then someone is intercepting your Wifi. 

Basically, I'm not saying you can detect someone using this method but that if you get this message you should know that you are being sniffed. 

I recommend Hak.5 Episode 1×03 if you want to see it in action.
