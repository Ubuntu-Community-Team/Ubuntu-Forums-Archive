### Post by Crafty Kisses on 2008-04-10
If you have a powerful laptop why don't you use Ubuntu?

Video cards are really easy to setup, especially nVidia cards, all you have to do is go to Restricted Drivers Manager within Ubuntu and install them there. :)

Webcams are usually easy to install and setup, but you might have to get the drivers, which isn't all that hard. 

Personally you'd probably want to use Ubuntu or Kubuntu if you have a powerful laptop.

### Post by Melk79 on 2008-04-10
I am new to Linux as of this past weekend.  I also ordered a new PC and am excited to try Linux.  I actually used Xubuntu to replace Windows 2000 on an old Dell laptop to give it some more life.  It installed great, picked up my wireless card and even ran WPA encryption without a problem.

Since you did order a new PC, I believe you'd be better off trying Ubuntu.  Of course, you could try both out as a LiveCD first to see what you'd prefer.  There are some good screencasts you can watch on Ubuntu's site to walk you through install, etc.  If you run one of the Ubuntu's as a LiveCD you'll get a quick indication of what it's picked up on your PC and can decide if you want to do a full install from there.  Hope that helps.

BTW, I really enjoy Xubuntu and have been strangely using this old laptop more than my brand new one.  I plan to throw Ubuntu on it after 8.04 comes out.
