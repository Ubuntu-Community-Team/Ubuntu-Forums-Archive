### Post by mrphantastic on 2007-06-22
I'll reply real quick, cuz I re-read you're post and I payed really close attention, so I'm just gonna assume that you DO want to access ubuntu with vista....so, what you must do is download this program....([http://www.diskinternals.com/linux-reader/]("http://www.diskinternals.com/linux-reader/")) and then when you've installed that ON Vista !! you can quickly brows to you're Linux partition and grab files.  I am not sure, however, that it allows "write" access, though.  Sorry, it's just the best thing I could find.  I used it for a while when I had Ubuntu, and it worked wonders, but BE SURE TO SAVE IN THE SAME FORMAT.  When you save in the Open Office files save them all as the same types in Vista AND in Linux, for example in Writer...they should ALL be either  .doc or .odt  depending on whether you share them with folks that are purely Windows people or not, if you use the stuff with Windows only people, choose .doc...good luck !!!
