### Post by catlett on 2006-07-04
Make sure you have a good copy of the ISO. Burn at 4x speed and you should check to make sure the burned Cd's md5sum matches the one listed for the ISO.
If you think it is a hard disk issue. Download and burn to disk Gparted Live. It is the gpareted partitioner on a live cd. [http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php](http://gparted.sourceforge.net/livecd.php)
This way you can reformat your partitions or even change their size for the new install.
Since you are reinstalling, you may want to think about creating a home partition. This way if you ever reinstall you can do so without touching your personal data.

You should be able to just run the installer and use your current partition but if you are having issues it couldn't hurt to reformat first.
