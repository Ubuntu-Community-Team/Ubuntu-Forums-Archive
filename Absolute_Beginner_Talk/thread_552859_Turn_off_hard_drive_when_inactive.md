### Post by julian67 on 2007-09-17
This might not be such a good idea as it seems, disks have improved a lot and consume almost no power when idle. This might interest you:

[http://www.mail-archive.com/ubuntu-bugs@lists.ubuntu.com/msg428252.html]("http://www.mail-archive.com/ubuntu-bugs@lists.ubuntu.com/msg428252.html")

but essentially you need more power to (stop and) start the disk than if you just let the disk's own power management work without intervention.

### Post by gn2 on 2007-09-17
Additionally, the modern hard drive wears most during start-up and slow-down.

When it's up to speed there's no wear at all.
