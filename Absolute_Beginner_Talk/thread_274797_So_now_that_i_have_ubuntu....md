### Post by CarpKing on 2006-10-10
With Ubuntu, you often don't have to browse the Internet to find programs to install.  Just open up "Add/Remove Progams" and you'll find all sorts of useful applications.  For even more, go to the Synaptic Package Manager (under System-->Administration).  I'd definitely recommend checking out the guide mentioned earlier in this thread to install things such as codecs for restricted formats.  To do this you'll have to enable the Universe and Multiverse repositories (repositories are the places software is downloaded and installed from when you use Synaptic or apt-get or aptitude); I'm sure that this is covered in the guide.

### Post by Chemroydal Tissue on 2006-10-11
> **nick06 said:**
> Eh...sorry, but i must ask....  What exactly are repositories?  And how do you update?

A definition of the term [repository](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Repository). In Synaptic, go to Settings->Repositories and check all of the boxes. Then, after clicking 'Close,' click on the 'Reload' icon. You can also add repositories by going into a terminal and typing:> sudo gedit /etc/apt/sources.listthen adding whatever repository you'd like (for example, the repositories at: [http://ubuntu.systemadministrator.org/](http://ubuntu.systemadministrator.org/) - this isn't a plug for the repo, necessarily, just an example). As for 3D video, what kind of video card are you using? And for DVD playback, I like Xine or Mplayer. Search for them in Synaptic once you've added those extra repositories.
