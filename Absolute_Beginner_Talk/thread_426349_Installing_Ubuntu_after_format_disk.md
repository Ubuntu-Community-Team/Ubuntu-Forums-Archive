### Post by Nythain on 2007-04-28
yes... you can even do it without formatting first, as you will have the option to delete/create/format/mount any hard disk/partions you want during the install process... just make sure your bios boot order has cdrom drive before hard disk drive and have that ubuntu iso, either livecd or alternate install, in the drive, and voilla, you're on your way to a better (albeit sometimes more frustrating at first) computer experience :)
