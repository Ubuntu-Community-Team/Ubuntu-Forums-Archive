### Post by gn2 on 2006-10-23
> **mattgaunt said:**
> but is that goin to cause any problems as far as having an AMD64 processor??

or will it simply mean that i will not be using all 64 bits of the processor just 32?

The processor will be fine, they are built for either 32 or 64-bit operation.

The processor itsself doesn't have bits, the 32 or 64 just relates to how many bits it handles at a time.

Sadly almost all software is 32-bit, so having a 64-bit OS is utterly pointless in almost all cases. "Future-proofing" is marketing speak.
By the time 64-bit computing is mainstream (5-10 years) current day PC's will be semi-obsolete. (That includes Core 2 Duos)

You will experience no end of difficulties trying to get everything to work in 64-bit, and there's no guarantee it will.

Go with 32-bit, it's your best bet for quite some time to come.
