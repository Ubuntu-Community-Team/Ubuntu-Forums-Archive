### Post by dstew on 2007-03-17
If you want to remove the older kernel you may, but it doesn't take up much room, and give you a fall-back point in case your new kernel gives you problems.

To remove it, use the Add-Remove programs or Synaptic. You can also remove from the terminal (apt-get remove).
