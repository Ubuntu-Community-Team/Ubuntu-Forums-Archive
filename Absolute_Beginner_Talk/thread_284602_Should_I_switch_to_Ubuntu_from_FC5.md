### Post by brentoboy on 2006-10-25
more similar than different really.
The most significant difference for me is the repos and package manager.

Ubuntu has FREE repos that your system can automatically keep up with.  Adding new packages doesnt require finding your CDs, nor does it require any kind of subscription.

Apt / synaptic (the debian package manager) makes life significantly easier than FC.

Other than the package manager and the color scheme, I find the two distros pretty similar.  Both are up to date with the latest stuff.  Both have issues getting non-free stuff out of the box (as they should). 

Installation is similar, but ubuntu just loads you up with the default packages without asking what you want.  You can add more or clean them out after you finish installation rather than before.

the 1 CD install with online repos full of additional packages is way nicer than 5+ CDs for installation swappage nightmares.

But, in the end, Its linux.  There are more differences between Ubuntu and Kubuntu than there are between ubuntu and FC.

-just my 2 cents
