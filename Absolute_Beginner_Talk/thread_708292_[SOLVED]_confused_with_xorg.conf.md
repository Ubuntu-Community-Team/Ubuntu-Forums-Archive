### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-02-26
[This is Envy.](http://albertomilone.com/nvidia_scripts1.html)

And you would want to pick to install the ATI driver (not manually unless you knew which one you wanted for a specific reason).

The Open Source driver would probably be either "ati" or "radeon".  The ATI installer and Restricted Drivers uses the "fglrx" driver developed by ATI.  For newer cards they work better.  The low-graphics (and low resolution) driver "vesa" is what you're running.  Thats' most likely why you can't get your desired screen resolution.

If that doesn't fix your screen resolution problems, then we can do a couple small edits to xorg.conf and get what you want.  But the first step to try is to get the right driver installed.
