### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-08-17
Hi Justin,

Sorry your posts have gone unanswered.  Generally, if you have a question that does not receive a reply, or one that has been replied to but didn't answer your question, after a day you should give it a "bump" - which is just replying to your own post with the word Bump.  That'll put it back at the top of the forum where it will, hopefully, get more attention.

Reposting the same issue in a new thread just takes up more space on the forum.  And if you've had replies (as in the two posts you've linked), seeing what steps have already been taken will help new viewers to know how to help you.

Unfortunately, I'm not terribly knowlegeable about the two topics you're asking about.  I can help a little, though...

On the hard drive question: it looks like your Linux partition tested out okay.  And the "Linux swap" partition is virtual memory - that is, storage space the system uses to free up RAM from idle processes.  Windows also uses virtual memory/swap, but usually stores it on the same partition as the rest of your data, which can cause problems (like fragmentation).  That's why Linux keeps it separate.

As for your video question, I have only faint clues, such as the possibility that your video codecs and video player plugin for Firefox didn't install correctly.  Automatix isn't a perfect system, and sometimes there are errors.  You might try re-installing them through Automatix (check to make sure you have the most recent version) or [EasyUbuntu]("http://easyubuntu.freecontrib.org/").

Otherwise, just bump your original threads for additional assistance.

### Post by nalmeth on 2006-08-17
Bumping a thread typically doesn't work, because if people don't know the answer, they still won't know the second time.

Do some more research using:
Search function
Documentation (UDSF, or wiki.ubuntu.com)
Google 
Searching other linux forums

Come back with your new info, make your post as short, to the point, and easy to read as possible. In other words, make it only too easy for someone to respond, with something as little as a link, or an idea.

Read the zero post's thread written by aysiu. It still applies if you've had a couple responses, but aren't getting anywhere.
