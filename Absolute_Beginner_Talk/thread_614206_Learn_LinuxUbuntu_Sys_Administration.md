### Post by eDRoaCH on 2007-11-15
That was my biggest obstacle to learning linux. I had installed it on spare pcs many times in the past 5 years but always ended up going back to windows. The ultimate reason was I didnt have a REASON to use it.

Flash forward and I start a new job, The company has basically no training and every person is an island to themselves in terms of knowledge. So I set up a mediawiki server and started using it. As I needed to do things I googled for them and learned them, and gained a greater overall knowledge of how things work. (Not that I am that great yet!)

As far as systems administration in companies go, ms still rules the roost. I just cant find any really good replacement for Domains or Exchange (enterprise class messaging) in the free world. (I cant even find a gui to join a domain)

However, linux owns bastion hosts (web/ftp servers, proxies, etc) 

I prefer GUI's for things, but as I get more comfortable with linux in general I get more comfortable using the command line. 

I guess what I am trying to say is, a book cant give you a reason. That you have to find for yourself. Good luck!
