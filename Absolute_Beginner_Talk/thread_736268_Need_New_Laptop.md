### Post by northern lights on 2008-03-26
> **YotoshiWii said:**
> What can people recommend? As being a student I'm on a budget of around the £300 ($598.23) mark. (but I could stretch a bit if required)

I'd say don't stretch it. This is about perfect and certainly enough for work and non-3D-gaming entertainment. Further, the newer the hardware the more likely you are to run into problems.

I will abstain from googling for one or two laptops in that price range and giving a recommendation, as there are way too many and it's up to your preference in the end anyways.

Rather, it's much easier to figure out what **not** to get.

Intel ICH8 family sound devices are often problematic, so I'd get ICH7 or lower, for instance. Check what graphics cards are blacklisted - get one that at least Hardy will support 100%. nVidia is generally a safer bet than new ATI cards.

I'd say save your money when it comes to the size of the harddrive, as you most likely will end up getting another external sooner or later (music collection e.g., backups). Don't be cheap on the physical memory - RAM is usually the first thing you'll otherwise end up upgrading in a year's time. But then again, that's probably more important if you do run hardware accelerated 3D apps.

### Post by nickpaton on 2008-03-27
I buy cheap IBM Thinkpad T23's from Ebay and refurbish them and sell onto friends at cost (no this is NOT a advertisment for myself!) with Ubuntu loaded.  
Cost with an external wireless card and 40GB HDD and 512MB of memory comes out at less than £200 - usually much less!
T23 has a 1GHz processor and 14.3" screen - full information [here]("http://www-307.ibm.com/pc/support/site.wss/document.do?sitestyle=lenovo&lndocid=MIGR-4YTG43")

Just having a quick look round Ebay UK and there are a few currently for sale which look interesting.

Alternatively there is the even better T43 which has a Centrino 2GHz ish processor, 14.3" screen and built in wireless - more info [here ]("http://www-307.ibm.com/pc/support/site.wss/document.do?sitestyle=lenovo&lndocid=MIGR-58315")

For:

Why I like them is the bulid quality - extremely rugged; 
the way they are put together - simply and modular; 
availability of spares - every part can be easily got hold of and replaced if necessary; 
detailed service manuals are available for free; 
they are perfect for Ubuntu - I have never yet had to search around for extra drivers.  I tend to put on Xubuntu 7.10 which uses the leaner FGLX desktop and they fly with this.  Use the alternative version and at the first menu press F6 and add
```
noapic nolapic
```
to the end.

Against:

The T23 won't do Compiz due to having an onboard Savage graphics card.  Not sure about the T43. You probably will not be playing games on them.
You won't be buying a new laptop.
There maybe some signs of use by previous owners. 
 
If you need more info or advise please PM me, and I do emphasize that this is not a sales thing.

Re Dells, I recently set up a new Inspiron 1525  with 7.04 preloaded for someone, and it was a mess.  Feisty did not work properly (can't remember exactly the probs ) but in particularly the on board wireless card was not detected properly and hunting through these forums confirmed that it was a known issue which required some work to get it to go if you were lucky.
I did a reinstall with 7.10 and everything did then work out of the box including the wireless with Compiz etc so in the end it was a very nice machine.
At the time I couldn't help thinking that Dell were doing themselves no favours sending out Ubuntu laptops in this state.  Lucky for the owner I was there to give 'support'! 
I believe they are now shipping with 7.10 preinstalled so they may work OK out of the box - hope so!
