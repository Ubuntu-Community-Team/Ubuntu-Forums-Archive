### Post by professor_chaos on 2006-06-06
Dapper is faster in many regards.
Newer kernel is likely to better support newer hardware.

But, my recommendation is that since you are new to linux/ubuntu you should stick with a working OS (ie breezy) and learn for a while. Once you feeling comfortable, then make the switch. Your not going to see that much amazing things in dapper, compared to breezy. However, if your wireless issue is big enough then you should see if upgrading solves this. You could also do a search on this forum to see if dapper solved this for others.
But from a different point of view, perhaps because your new to ubuntu and you might not have a lot of time commited to you current install, perhaps its the best time to try the upgrade, because if something goes wrong you can easily reinstall. Good luck.
