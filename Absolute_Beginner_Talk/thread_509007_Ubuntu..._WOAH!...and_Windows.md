### Post by freebeer on 2007-07-24
Hello, and welcome!

No, dual-booting is not evil.  In fact, most folks will recommend that you do so and run both OSes as you need/want to.  You can decide later if you want to get rid of one or the other, but dual-boot gives you the best of both worlds and doesn't lock you in to any particular approach.

For details on how to achieve this, you might want to check out this site: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partitioning).  Apart from that direct link, there's other goodies there to look through to introduce you to the world of Ubuntu.

BTW, the Live CD comes with partitioning software - it's called gparted - and it'll be invoked automatically should you decide to install Ubuntu.  (It's part of the installation process.)

### Post by hyper_ch on 2007-07-25
And before you start messing around with an installation, just MAKE A BACKUP FIRST - it's worth it!

I've done partitioning and stuff for years (in windows) and even as experienced partitioner in windows there was this one time where I accidentally deleted the wrong partition... I just was too sure of what I was doing... it happens... we all make mistakes.

So, before you actually tinker around, make sure you have backups. If everything works smoothly, you "wasted" some time... but if something goes wrong, you still have your stuff ;)
