### Post by widowmaker on 2008-01-26
XP should automatically pick up your drivers providing there is nothing out of the ordinary or unusual about you computer. It is a very rare ocassion when you have to download a driver for your average ethernet, video, sound or modem hardware. If you have installed third party hardware you will more then likely have to use a driver provided with that particular piece of hardware. (example: dual monitor video card). Sometimes the computer may come with a driver disk with all the drivers on it especially if the ethernet, video, etc, etc...> are all incorporated as part of the motherboard. If you don't have a disk, figure out the make and model of your computer and go directly to the manufacturers website and you can download the drivers there. Most of the time you can find them under the help or support section, then look for drivers and the model of your computer and download the drivers to your hard drive and then install them. Of course if you don't have an internet connection you will have to do this on your friends computer. Make sure you have an Anit virus program installed. Then update it. Turn on windows firewall. Then do all your Windows XP updates. Anything less and you will have serious virus and spyware problems.

Hope this helps,
