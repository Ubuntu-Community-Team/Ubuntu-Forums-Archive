### Post by Fernanddo Saenz on 2008-01-25
Hi, I am new to Ubuntu and installed 7.10 on a low-end PC (Celeron 333, 15" CRT, 1MB Virge/DX PCi video, 20GB disk) to get to know it. I am really impressed... it has given new life and excitement to an old machine. 
I use an Epson LQ570+ dot-matrix printer  which at first printed absolutely horrible. I reinstalled it a number of times, played around with all the settings, tried installing it as a different but "related" model (LQ-1070, LQ2500,..) until I gave up.

I few days ago some system updates came in, something in the printing was corrected... now the **quality** is very acceptable (after changing the default resolution from 60x60 to 180x180), but an annoying behavior persists: When reacheing the end of the page (using continuous-form paper) instead of advancing to the beginning of the next page, it goes way down and continues at about 3 inches from the bottom of the page, and so on. The paper length shows correctly selected as Letter (8x11.5). It is like the driver were sending a **Form Feed **to the printer, instead of just "skipping over the perforation". I have checked the printer´s DIP switches (there is one for "skip over perf", another for paper length) but I finally left them at the defaults -where they work fine for Windows, btw.

Any ideas?
