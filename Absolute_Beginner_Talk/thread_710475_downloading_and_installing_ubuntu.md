### Post by drunkardivan on 2008-02-28
Put CD in drive, restart your computer. If it doesn't boot from the CD, go into your BIOS and change the boot order. It's self-explanatory from there... at least, as far as the install goes.  I'd suggest you run from the live CD for a few days though- it'll be slower, but it will also let you know whether your system's right for Ubuntu.

### Post by kellemes on 2008-02-28
Edit: Sorry, previous poster beat me to it. ;-)

Put cd in drive and reboot the computer.. The computer should boot from the cd and Ubuntu should start.

If not.. You need to enter your BIOS and set your cdrom-drive to be first bootdevice.
Probably you need to tap <DEL> or something to enter BIOS.
