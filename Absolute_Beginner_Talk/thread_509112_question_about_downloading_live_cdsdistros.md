### Post by Smu on 2007-07-25
If I got this right the differens (in short) is that when you download the iso (without torrent) you're downloading the file from one destination on one server. Via a torrent on the other hand you download different parts of the file from different locations... This means that torrent download is much likely to be faster, but it doesn't always have to be.

### Post by Raineer on 2007-07-25
Not really much of a preference.  Either way you can check the MD5 checksum of what you have downloaded to ensure it is the correct copy and not corrupted.

Theorectically I guess Bittorrent *could* be less likely to be a full, good copy...since you are downloading from 100's of people at once.  In practice I find that not to be the case, especially with files of this nature.  I would recommend the torrent as it should be much faster.

### Post by mcduck on 2007-07-25
Bittorrent is more reliable, since the protocol itself includes MD5 checks to make sure all downloaded parts are OK. So it's highly unlikely to get a cotrrupted download with BT as long as you use official torrents.
