### Post by SilverDragon on 2007-07-12
I was just wondering if there was a difference between the quality of an audio that is converted straight from a cd to FLAC and if it was first converted to mp3(or any other lossy codec) and then converted into a loseless codec such as FLAC.

Another question is if you convert the audio off a cd in mp3(or any other lossy codec) and then later convert it to a higher bit rate, would the quality increase? If it does would be very slight or would it sound the same as originally converting at the higher bit rate. For example, if you convert the audio into 320 kps VBR bite rate or if you convert it into 128kps and then convert it to 320 kps VBR bit rate.

### Post by hackle577 on 2007-07-12
1- IIRC, there will be a quality loss going from CD to mp3 to FLAC, but I'm not sure. I always go straight to FLAC just in case.

2- No, there is no increase in quality. Once the quality has been degraded then there is no program/conversion/magic wand that will raise it again. IMHO, any quality increases past about 128-192 are hard to notice and probably not worth the extra disk space unless you really need/want it.

### Post by Hallvor on 2007-07-12
No, once the quality is degraded you cannot make it better no matter how lossless the format you convert to. It is just a waste of diskspace. Better leave the bitrate on the same level (or even lower) if you have to convert, It is the same as with video; once you have made a low quality rip, it is not possible to make a high quality dvd from it with anything else than the original source.

---

### Post by FuturePilot on 2007-07-12
Converting from a lower bit rate to a higher bit rate won't increase the quality. It will only result in probably lower quality (from being encoded multiple times, the more times something is encoded the worse the quality gets no matter what) and a bigger file size.
