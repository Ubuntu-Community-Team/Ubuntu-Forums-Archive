### Post by laidback on 2007-09-27
You certainly can use Ubuntu without the internet. If you can get hold of a copy of the 7.04 release on the Live CD you can even try before you install. Obviously without the internet you wont have access to updates and fixes, but since you're not exposed to any nasties that doesn't really matter much. Where you do loose out is in the ability to download any software you might like from the repositories. However, something is better than nothing so go ahead. You can certainly learn a lot about how the desktop works, how the software on the standard CD/DVD works, and there is plenty of it. 

Then when you get an internet connection you will be able to update your system through the Synaptic auto updates system, which is wonderful. All you'll have to do is say yes please update, then sit back and watch it bring your installation bang up to date. 

Ubuntu is terrific, I strongly urge you to have a go at what ever level you can manage.

Welcome to a new world.
