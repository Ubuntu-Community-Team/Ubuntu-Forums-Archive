### Post by cilynx on 2006-04-30
I'm guessing that you have three partitions beyond the ones that you generally use for Ubuntu.  Ubuntu puts icons on the desktop for any partition that it can mount that isn't part of the basic system.  

Unmounting means that you "park" that partition.  It isn't deleted or gone or anything, it just isn't accessible until you mount it again.  This can be done graphically through  Places->Computer

### Post by akiro.yamamoto on 2006-04-30
The default Gnome setup in Ubuntu ( 5.10 at least ) is to display any mounted volumes (hard drives , USB drives as well as CDs and DVDs) on the desktop, this is just for your convenience. This action can be changed if you don't need it, however I would recommend that you leave it as is until your comfortable with the layout of your directory structure (including knowing where your drives are mounted) and with the linux system setup as a whole.
