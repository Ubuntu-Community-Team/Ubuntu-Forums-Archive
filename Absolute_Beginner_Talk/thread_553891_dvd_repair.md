> you know a software that repair dvd (close it if damaged etc....)

Any number of ways:

To fix scratches/dirt, the best method is to try cleaning it. Then there are various epoxies that will fill the scratches, and possibly make the cd readible again. The data is on the underside of the label, the clear plastic contains no data. Many video rental places will do this for you, for a small fee, but there are no guarentees.

If your burning program made a coaster, just reburn it! 

As another poster suggested, dd may work.
