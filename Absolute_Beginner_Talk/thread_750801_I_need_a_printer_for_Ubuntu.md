### Post by hurtstotalktoyou on 2008-04-09
A new printer for $30?  Whew, good luck, man!

The best I can suggest is the [HP Deskjet D2460 ($58 shipped from NewEgg)](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16828104655).  It's a solid printer from a reputable manufacturer, sold by a trustworthy retailer, and known to work with Ubuntu.  You can find cheaper printers (e.g. the Lexmark Z1300 for $41 shipped), but not only do they usually suck eggs, they cost more in the long run because they only come with a tiny bit of ink.  The Deskjet D2460, on the other hand, includes both color and black ink cartridges.  Whatever you choose, always remember to take into account the price of ink.

Good luck!

### Post by fjm03 on 2008-04-09
Here's a wild suggestion. Get a cheap, networked, monochrome, laser printer.
Toner cartridges don't dry out and *nix systems were born to network. Networking (CUPS) almost eliminates driver problems. Brother produces both wired and wireless, networked printers for under $150. They can be connected either through CUPS or a BRN node and work passably for home use. The big bitch is curling paper because their heater drum has a small diameter to reduce the footprint.
