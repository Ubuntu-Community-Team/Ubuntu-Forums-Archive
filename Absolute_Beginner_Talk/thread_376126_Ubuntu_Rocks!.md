### Post by rajncajn on 2007-03-04
I am new to the forum. I just wanted to relay my experiences. I started computing in the early 80s with an Atari computer. I went through a few different models and moved to the IBM compatible world. I started with DOS and moved all the way through XP. I had tinkered with Linux a couple of times in the past buy decided that it was not going to work as a replacement operating system for all of my needs. A couple of months ago I heard about UBUNTU and decided to give it a whirl since it offered the live CD. I have wiped out my Windows more than once before not knowing what I was doing, especially trying to remove Grub. I was amazed at how almost everything worked out of the box both on my desktop and laptop! I recently bought a Dell E1505 laptop mostly so that I could experiment on it with out causing major heartache to my wife.

My first challenge was the wide screen was not recognized. I finally ran across 915Resolution. It worked perfectly. Then a major hurdle was my proprietary Dell wireless card. I was able to make it work with Ndiswrapper and the windows 2000 Dell driver. I was one happy camper! Now the only things left that I had little hope for were my Dell  Axim with Windows Mobile 2003 and my Sony Ericsson W300i phone. I use both of these a lot and it is important to me to be able to synchronize them with my tasks, calendar, and contacts. First I have to say that when I put the phone into the file transfer mode Ubuntu recognized and mounted it (both phone and memory card) without me having to do anything! I found one program called Wammu that did some cute things but it would not access the Outlook stuff in my phone. Then I went through many gyrations with Multisync and yesterday finally managed to synchronize both my phone and Axim!!! It seems to work perfectly for both. I have to run a little script before the Axim will sync but I can live with that. 

I have been doing a lot of googling on both of them. I found a lot of information for syncing PPC and almost no information specific to the W300i phone. The information that I did find on the PPC was that it would work but it was buggy. Both seem to work as good as Acitvesync did for me.

Ok so now I have no more challenges other than maybe my antique USB1 Avermedia TV tuner capture card. I didn't have much luck in the past trying to get it to work on my Desktop with Linux. I rest easy knowing that there are many out there that I can purchase that are compatible with Linux and even include the drivers with them.

I learned that when I am shopping for hardware in the future, I will be checking for Linux compatibility before making purchases. I also learned that this is a lot easier in this day and age.

I am really thankful that Linux is developing fast enough to keep Bill Gates in check. It would probably be a really different world if this were not so. I don't hate Windows like a lot of Linux users seem to. I think that Mr. Gates has actually made a huge contribution to the world. Whether he is doing it as a service or to make money is irrelevant as long as he is not in complete control.

That's my story and I'm stickin' to it...
