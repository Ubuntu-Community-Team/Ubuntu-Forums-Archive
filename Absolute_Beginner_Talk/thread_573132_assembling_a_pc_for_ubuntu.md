### Post by skipbarker on 2007-10-11
For video pickup an Nvidia video card they seem to have the best driver support at the moment. (a cheap one will work fine)

Most onboard sound works just fine but you can check the [Hardware Comp. List]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/HardwareSupport")

I like the core2 duo processor from intel and prefer intel chipset on the motherboard.

For RAM don't settle for less than 1 gig. if you have the money go for 2 gigs.

Lite-On makes a good DVD-Burner for the money.

Most everything should be available in India, but i don't know of what shops to recommend since I'm in the US.

### Post by mivo on 2007-10-12
While ATI released the driver specs to the community, it will take some time before there will be solid open source drivers for the new cards. So, I would also go for an Nvidia card right now.

The 7er series gives you the best performance for the money right now (7950GT and GX2 being the best and only beaten by the 8800). If you want a card that will support OpenGL 3.0 (and DX10), the 8600GTS offers you the best compromise of price and performance. Either card will serve you for several years. If those are outside of your budget, I would look at the top-end cards of the 6er series. To compare different Nvidia cards, look at the [Comparison of Nvidia GPUs ]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_NVIDIA_Graphics_Processing_Units")at Wikipedia. Look at memory bandwidth and core tact.

I would get 2 GB of RAM, especially if you also plan to use Windows. 1 GB is the minimum, but memory has gotten cheap that 2 GB are a better bet. This should also be the standard for the next three or so years (most new notebooks come with 2 GB now).

### Post by PmDematagoda on 2007-10-13
Go for either Seagate or Western Digital for your HDD needs as they are the best brands for HDD's for now.

For RAM, I have always used Kingston and they have never failed me yet.
