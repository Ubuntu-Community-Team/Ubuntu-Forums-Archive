### Post by gtchamp7 on 2008-04-09
First off, make sure the LCD you are buying has a VGA connection because that is what is most likely supported by your video card (alot of LCD's have a DVI conncetion which won't work with old cards).

Both your monitor and your video caed have a maximum resolution, but you have to chose a resolution (from inside your OS) that works with both. The LCD will work, but you will probably need to back down to a 1024 x 768 res rather than the full 1440 x 900 since your video card (most likely) doesn't support that high of a res.

If you have Windows installed then just use the Display properties to find the highest resolution that works with your video card (if the res is too high, parts of the screen will be "cut off"). If you have Linux then just play around with the resolutions until you find one that does not cut pieces off the screen.
