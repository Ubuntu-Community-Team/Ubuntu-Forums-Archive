### Post by Malibu Illusion on 2007-07-03
You can't damage hardware from tweaking settings from within your operating system itself.  When you start playing around with the BIOS, RAM voltages, CPU clock speeds and whatnot then you can start frying things if you do not know what you're doing.  I strongly advise staying out of your BIOS unless you explicitly understand exactly what it is you're doing, otherwise then you have the potential to cause your hardware some unrecoverable damage.

Take care.

### Post by Trebaruna on 2007-07-03
Just to be complete: some tools allow you to change clock speeds etc. on the fly in the OS. Obviously, that can be as dangerous as poking around in the BIOS.
Basically, as long as you do not push any hardware element beyond its stock speed/voltage you can poke around pretty much wherever. Do not stay out of the BIOS just because it sounds scary: there are also very useful things there. Just make sure you know what setting you're playing with, and stay away from clocks and volts.

...and if you do feel a need to fiddle with those (you dont sound like you do, but just in case) there are 2 things to keep in mind:
1) READ! Make sure you know all about the settings, your hardware, cooling, basically everything.
2) YOU are responsible if you mess up :)
