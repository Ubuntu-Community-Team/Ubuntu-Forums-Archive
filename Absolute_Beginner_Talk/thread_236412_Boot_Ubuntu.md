### Post by nhandler on 2006-08-14
I am trying to install Ubuntu. I have a laptop with an external hard drive connect by USB.  I am trying to install Ubuntu onto that drive. On the internal hard drive, Windows XP is installed. I want to dualboot with this. I downloaded and burned the iso image. I then booted off that disk. I was able to launch the version of Ubuntu on the cd. I then ran the installer program. I told it to use the whole external drive to install. After the installation, I told it to reboot. Once it rebooted, it booted off the internal hard drive (default), and launched windows xp. I was unable to boot off the external drive. Booting off the cd still works. On the live cd, I am able to view the external drive, and it looks like linux was installed on it (all the files are there). Now, I just need to figure out how to boot off it (my bios supports booting off a usb device). Could someone help me out here?

### Post by SB42 on 2006-08-14
You will need to go into your BIOS and change the settings so that your computer boots from usb before booting from the hard-drive.  

If your BIOS lets you have 3 options, I would have CD drive first, then USB drive, then your internal hard-drive.
