### Post by Martin on 2007-05-08
If you created a separate session for beryl in gdm. Shutdown the pc. Restart and when you get to the login go 'options' and change your session back to gnome. Then login as normal. Beryl won't be running but at least you'll have your terminal back so you can do stuff while you work out what your beryl problem is.
