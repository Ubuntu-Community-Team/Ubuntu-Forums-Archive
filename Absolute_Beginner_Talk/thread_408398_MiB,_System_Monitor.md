### Post by insane_alien on 2007-04-13
its the true meaning of a megabyte in computer science. 

there is some history to this so bear with me. In the beginning when computers took up a large building and consumed the power output of several small countries, a prefixing method was born. it was based on binary numbers since thats what computers use. so 1 MB was 1024 kB and 1kB was 1024B. then marketing came along and decided to take 1MB as 1000kB and 1kB as 1000B.

there was no standardisation so people got confused. it came to pass that the binary meanings would be given a notation of its own. kiB, MiB etc.

1 MiB is a MebiByte which is 1024kiB, 1kiB is 1024 B
