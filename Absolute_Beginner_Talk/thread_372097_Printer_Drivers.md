### Post by Quillz on 2007-02-27
You should be able to get the necessary drivers directly through Ubuntu. Go into the Printer settings, and see if your printers is already recognized. If it's a USB printer, it most likely is. Then, you will be taken to a large list of printers. Select your model, and it should prompt you to download CUPS, which is a universal collection of printer drivers for Linux. Once that's installed, your printer should be functioning correctly.
