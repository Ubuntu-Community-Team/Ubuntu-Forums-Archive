### Post by james_san on 2006-09-21
Ideally, you may be able to find a "gstreamer plugin". gStreamer is used by most of the video/audio programs in Ubuntu to play sounds, so if you can find a gstreamer plugin that can play any of those formats, you will be able to play them in regular programs like Totem and Rhythmbox. Even if you have to find several plugins, at least they will all play in the same program :)
Not sure what your chances are though. I did some googling and couldn't come up with much.

### Post by bigfox on 2007-07-21
Audio Overload is designed to play many game console soundtracks.

[http://www.bannister.org/software/ao.htm](http://www.bannister.org/software/ao.htm)

It is a fairly stripped down program and the UI is quite limited, but it does work.

Available for Mac, Windows and Linux.

It isn't a .deb installer, you just uncompress it and run it.
