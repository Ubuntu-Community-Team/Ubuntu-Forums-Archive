### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-01-13
The thing to remember is that, besides the stuff under the hood, Linspire is based on Windows.  So menu stuff makes sense from a Windows background.  But that's just for if you want something that's linux but REALLY close to Windows.  Also, they charge you for the OS, and for new programs, and for upgrades, and for most everything else.  So in conclusion, if you want an OS that is secure but that just about everything else is the same as Windows, except for the amount of programs available, then go for Linspire.

Otherwise, go with Ubuntu and get comfortable with the forums.  I've heard many people say that this is the distro with the best community support.  And we're so nice!  We'd love to have you.
