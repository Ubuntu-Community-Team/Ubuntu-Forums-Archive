### Post by OffAxis on 2008-03-25
What you need to do is look up the stats on your monitor, presumably on phillips' website. What you're looking for is the horizontal and vertical refresh rates, resolutions, etc.

First, (from the command line) backup your current xorg.conf
```

sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup
```


Then, run this :

```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```

This is the setup utility for xserver; your mouse, keyboard, video card, monitor. If the option is acceptable just hit 'Enter' to move on to the next item. Once you get to the monitor section you can name the monitor appropriately and select the correct refresh rates and resolutions.
After you're done setting that up just restart the xserver (Ctrl+Alt+Backspace).

If anything goes sideways on you just get to a command prompt (safe mode or a liveCD or a terminal) and over-write your modified xorg.conf with
```
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf.backup /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```
and restart the xserver.

By the way, the threshold for 'flicker' for the human eye is typically 60 Hz.
