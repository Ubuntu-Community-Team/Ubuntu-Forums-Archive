### Post by trent dillman on 2006-03-21
OK, if your sound works in some programs, then that's not the problem. :-P

It's probably your sound daemon. I'm guessing xmms opens, and when you try t play, it crashes. Open XMMS, then press CTRL+P (or right click the titlebar > Options > Preferences). Under Audio I/O Plugins, switch the output plugin to something else (if it's on OSS, try ALSA, and vice-versa).

And try xine. It rocks.
