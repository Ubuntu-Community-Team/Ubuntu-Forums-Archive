### Post by catlett on 2006-04-02
Go to the top of the screen and click on System then on Preferences and finally on Themes. Next got to this website [http://art.gnome.org/](http://art.gnome.org/) Once your at the website  click on themes. From here you can add backgrounds. icons, splash screens and window borders. The process is easy. When there is something you want, click on it. It will show you a dialogue box to download a package. Right click and drag the package box icon. You drag it over to the themes window you opened up from systems<preferences<themes. You'll see an icon of a document being dragged and then a + sign when you're over the theme window. Release your right button and the package will drop in. A dialogue box will come up asking you if you want to install. Say yes. Drag as much stuff over that you want. They do not become active when you install them, they are just being installed in the themes app. You then go into your theme window and choose a theme to install. To customise further click on theme details. From there you can choose your type of window border, icons, and controls. All the things you dragged over will be here. If you don't get alot of theme choices after you drag and drop don't worry. Just choose any theme and you can customise it from the theme detail section. Most of the things you dragged and dropped will be under details and won't be listed as a theme.
