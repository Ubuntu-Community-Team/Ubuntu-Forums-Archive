### Post by Jimmyfj on 2007-07-05
Hi folks

I don't know if this is the right place to put this. I don't have a problem with my Ubuntu 7.10 Tribe 1 / 2 at all so take this for what it is.

Just today, after having installed a bunch of updates, 59 updates I think it was, my Ubuntu froze while surfing the Internet. Without warning, it just froze and the only way to recover was to hot reboot the computer. After that I had no Internet connection, but after fiddling around setting a manual static IP-address, which didn't work either I set it back to Automatic DHCP controlled connection which did the trick. Then again after a few hours my Internet stopped working again. I was tired of this and decided to go for a walk buying some dinner and when I got back I was prepared to downgrade my computer to Feisty for at least a few more weeks.

I'm no geek in the Linux world, and didn't know what to do about the Internet connection, other than wait for an update to correct the problem, which again required me to have access to the Internet, so... 7.04 it was to be from here on. BUT - To my great surprise when I first came home ready to start the downgrade Gutsy had worked the problem out all by itself. Now - This isn't the first time I've seen Gutsy Gibbon correct errors after a simple reboot of the computer. Last time it was some graphics stuff for the X-Server that didn't load and my desktop looked really Windows-like-ugly. But a reboot corrected the issue and not until today have I had a problem with Gutsy which I installed the day after Tribe 1 was released. I really look forward to the final, alpha release of Gutsy Gibbon - Think it'll be the best version of Ubuntu yet released.
