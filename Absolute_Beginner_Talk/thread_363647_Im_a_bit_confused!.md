### Post by jure1873 on 2007-02-17
kubunut and ubuntu are based on different desktops (gnome - kde) so choose which one you like more, you can always install kde or gnome later without a full reinstallation if you change your mind. 
If you have a 2gb free partition on your usb disk its probably enough, but it won't be as fast as from an internal hard disk. 
You'll get a bootable cd if you burn the iso image you downloaded. (In kubuntu you'd use k3b)
