### Post by dwhitney67 on 2007-08-30
Is it just me or are some of you also getting annoyed seeing silly posts here on the Ubuntu forum?  Any day now I expect some to post something along these lines:

*Help!  I cannot log in to my Ubuntu system.  The problem started last night when my house was burglarized and the thief stole my laptop.  Now when I put my hands down, I cannot find the keyboard.  Please help... I have an important thesis due tomorrow and without it I will never become an astronaut.  Can someone please help me login in?  Do I need to reinstall everything, or should I just call the police?*

Yep, I think we've all seen posts similar to this.  Is the forum too convenient or what?  For those of you who do not get (that is, understand) the satirical nature of this post, please move along.  For those who are still reading, please if you have a problem, try performing a little research on the forum (there is a search feature!) or try googling for something before submitting a post.

If you do not have a clue how to maintain your system, then please avoid doing it unless you have a backup plan.  I just read a post about somebody who "accidentally" deleted their /usr directory. What the h*ll?  Very few applications need to be installed in the root-controlled areas unless you want to share it with other users.  If you have an app or a game that you want to run, try installing it your home directory before ever attempting to install it in the root-controlled areas.

And lastly, please back off from using the "sudo" command.  It's not meant to be used for every command that is run.  It is for system maintenance.  Not for installing, deleting, or modifying files to see if your system would look good in "pink".

Ok, enough ranting.  Time to return to my lovely Fedora.
