### Post by Slipmyknot101 on 2007-11-11
> **OlyPerson said:**
> I've read all this stuff about Linux being so great and that everyone can contribute by fixing errors or making apps or such, but where do you start doing this?  What do you need to learn to start going into the process or developing and whatever else there is to do?

 You ask a great question. I can not, with any honesty, say that I am even NEAR a level where I am ready to develop applications or crank out brilliant code. Linux is a gigantic learning experience where there will always be an individual who knows more than you. Really, learning Linux is a a completely subjective process. There are multiple approaches and a wide variety of methods of going about attaining your Linux skills.

I have to agree with the previous post that time and effort are the two determining factors of how fast you catch on to Linux. 

But to answer your question of where to get started, I would start by reading these forum threads, particularly the beginner forum threads. Contributing and/or reading the beginner forum threads is a great way to learn many things about Linux. I've been with Ubuntu for just over a year now and I've had no problems with it what-so-ever, and I am still reading the beginner section and finding out new things...almost every day.

Good luck.
