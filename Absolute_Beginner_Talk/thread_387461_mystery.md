### Post by kvonb on 2007-03-18
It might be the mainboard, that's what I am thinking.

Do you know anything about electronics at all?

If you know what a capacitor should look like, I would suggest looking at your mainboard, and look at the tops of the capacitors, if they are rounded, and not flat on top (you will notice the difference), especially near the CPU, then that is the problem.

They can be replaced if you are handy with a soldering iron, if not then unfortunately you will be looking at a new mainboard :(.

There are pics available that show this condition, try a search on google for "popped caps".

Also you could be right, it could be the hard drive, I'm sure there are testing programs available, again search the net :).

Regards, Kev :)
