### Post by A4006 on 2008-01-20
Well, first of all, you should always back up your data before even thinking of installing an OS (tisk tisk).  I believe you can just plug your iPod in and Ubuntu will detect it as removable storage, allowing you to retrieve all of your songs.  As far as I know, iTunes does not exist as a Linux version, but you could use this alternative program <http://www.desktoplinux.com/articles/AT7150747782.html>.  You could also use Wine (a windows emulator) with the windows version of iTunes.  In order to play your music that you get from you iPod, you will need to download the audio codecs for it, also.  And finally... congratulations on switching over to Ubuntu!  It takes a little getting used to, but hey, it's free.
