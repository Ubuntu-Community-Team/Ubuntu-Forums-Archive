### Post by j002 on 2006-12-12
It could be the harddrive, motherboard, video card, processor,  power supply, CDROM, CDROM or HD cable...
What is the history of your system ?

Do you have embedded video, that is a monitor plug on the motherboard ?
If so, you could try taking out the video card and just using the embedded video.

Are you starting form a Live CD ?  Is it scratched ?

How long are you waiting ?  The Live CD can be very slow.

You could try with a different HD, cables, and so on.

If you don't have a system on your HD, which is likely, you could get a Windows boot disk (bootdisk.com, I think, Google it),  and run Fdisk to re-partition the drive and then format it.  Sometimes extra partitions can muck up Linux installation.
