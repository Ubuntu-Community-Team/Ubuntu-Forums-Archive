### Post by aysiu on 2006-04-29
[QUOTE=yozef]There is also some mis-leading and inaccurate information.[/QUOTE] Yeah, I just perused it at Barnes and Noble, and I was shocked that it spread FUD about Windows. I like Ubuntu a lot, but XP is not as buggy as the book makes it out to be, nor is Ubuntu as stable as the book makes it out to be. It claims you will **never** have your mouse cursor freeze on you in Ubuntu. Um... just not true. The Linux *kernel* may be stable, but the X environment (KDE or Gnome) is not necessarily so, and I *have* had my mouse cursor freeze on me (this was some bug in Breezy with JuK in a Gtk environment--XFCE or Gnome--if I used it too long).

Also, it asks you to use ```
sudo gedit
``` instead of ```
gksudo gedit
``` and insists that you put two spaces between elements in the /etc/fstab. I've had no problems putting only one space in between the elements.

Well... I could go on, but the point is really that the book is inaccurate and misleading. It also is not for professionals--it really is for novices. I consider myself a novice (I'm coming up on my 1-year anniversary with Ubuntu on May 24, 2006), and most of that stuff in the book I found very basic, except maybe the stuff about configuring wireless, as I've never had to do that.
