### Post by xpod on 2007-01-15
ext3 is ubuntu`s default filesystem..

I found the easiest way to start out with was to just create whatever space you were giving to ubuntu and leave it un-allocated....then pop that cd in and when the installer gets to the partitioning stage you just point it to the correct partition and let Ubuntu do the rest for you..

Including setting up your dualboot for you of course.
Of course many folks will tell you all sorts of different ways to pre-partition etc but i reckon the above is the most simple straightforward way to begin with.

You got all the time in the world to learn the many other ways of getting Ubuntu on to your pc and
like many you`ll probably end up trying all sorts of different methods once you get the hang of it.

You could also consider the 6.06(dapper) version if you are having issues with edgy(6.10)..
It`s the long term support version and sometimes works better for many people.
