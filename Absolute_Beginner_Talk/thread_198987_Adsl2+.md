### Post by viciouslime on 2006-06-18
Actually it has different up and downrates hence asynchronous. sDSL has the same uprate as down rate (synchronous). The 2 is just because it is the new version of an already existing aDSL (aDSL 1 if you like). 2 simply has the ability to support faster up/downrates. Upto 24mbps I believe. 

It is fairly new here in the UK, I don't know about elsewhere. Also, you have to live within about a mile of your exchange to actually get the 24mbps, but at greater distances you can still get better speeds than conventional aDSL.

If you want to know about the technical differences, i.e. how the extra bandwidth is achieved, I recommend searching google.
