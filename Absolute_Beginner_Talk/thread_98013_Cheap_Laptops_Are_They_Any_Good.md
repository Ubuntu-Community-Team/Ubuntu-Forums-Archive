### Post by public_void on 2005-12-02
I'd suggest looking at the specs closely, and see if they meet what you need and how it be in the future. IMO as computers get cheaper there is a catch somewhere. Example include intergrated graphics (although thats more desktop), smallish hard drive and okay RAM. There maybe a high end processor, but slow front side bus. Look at the software you get, is it just a laptop with no OS, or has XP home only. You may get dedicated modems or a anti-virus suite that lasts two months until you have to pay. OEMs will look to cut cost how ever they can and they will to get the computer cheaper. I suppose I'm scare-mongering a bit, but use your head when buying a computer and ask as many question as you can think of. If your not sure ask a friend or someone who know this stuff so your prepared. In the end its your money, you don't wnat to waste it.

### Post by Brunellus on 2005-12-02
To borrow a rule of thumb from buying cycling equipment:

Cheap.  Durable.  Light.  Fast.

Choose any three.

A machine may be cheap, fast, and light--but don't expect it to be particularly durable.  It may be cheap, fast, and durable--but don't expect it to weigh less than 3 kg.  It may be fast, light, and durable--but be prepared to pay for it.

Pricing for laptops can be broken down more or less by kilogram:

Heavier than 3 kg= 500-700 USD.
2-3 kg= 700-1,000 USD 
2kg and lighter= 1,100 USD and up.
