### Post by aysiu on 2007-03-29
Is it legal for you to be distributing Flash, MP3, etc.? You may want to look into that.

I think Feisty actually has "easy codec installation," so you may not even need it.

You may want to use [Reconstructor](http://reconstructor.aperantis.com/) to create a customized live/installer CD of Ubuntu (one with your wallpaper and maybe specially-selected programs). It'd be great for your customers to have as a "restore CD" of sorts.

### Post by AlanRogers on 2007-03-29
> **mgroat said:**
> Does anybody have any suggestions for other things I might include, or tweaks to make the OS more user-friendly?Have a search for the thread of favourite applications. That'll give a clue as to what else to include and ISTR that there were some really good ones listed. Try these for starters: [1]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=317480&highlight=favourite+applications") [2]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=382137&highlight=favourite+applications")

FWIW, I'd just stuff Automatix in to deal with your list above. Whilst Acrobat Reader is awful compared to Linux native PDF applications, I think there's some kudos to be gained in including the branded product rather than some no-name imitation, regardless of how much better it may be.

I'd ask [Ayisu]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/member.php?u=21941") for permission to include a copy of his HowTos from his site or implement them in your installation. Also, reclaim the Mozilla Firefox & Thunderbird icons, to restore brand image again, using the script in [this thread]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=199193").

Also, if they're students, perhaps include the Windows / Linux [application mapping list]("http://wiki.linuxquestions.org/wiki/Linux_software_equivalent_to_Windows_software") or prepopulate it in their Bookmarks. It's a while since I've had to think like a teenager or student ;)
