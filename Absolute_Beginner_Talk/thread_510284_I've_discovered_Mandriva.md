### Post by AdamWill on 2007-08-17
The Mandriva community is very active...take a look at:

[http://forum.mandriva.com/](http://forum.mandriva.com/) - official forum
[http://www.mandrivausers.org/](http://www.mandrivausers.org/) - un-official forum
[http://wiki.mandriva.com/](http://wiki.mandriva.com/) - the wiki, lots of useful info here

Mailing lists: see [http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Docs/Support/Mailinglists](http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Docs/Support/Mailinglists) for user lists and [http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Development/Mailinglists](http://wiki.mandriva.com/en/Development/Mailinglists) for developer lists.

IRC: #mandriva , #mandriva-cooker , #mandriva-kernel , and various native language channels (e.g. #mandriva-fr) on Freenode.

Newsgroups (yep, newsgroups): alt.os.linux.mandriva

Most translation of Mandriva (into several dozen languages) and much Mandriva development is done by community members (developers on Mandriva are around half @mandriva.com - staff - and half @mandriva.org - community - at present).

As far as free versus non-free goes: every free / open source package in Mandriva is available for free on the public mirrors. Most important non-free packages are also available for free, since 2007 Spring. The only packages still restricted to Club members and people who purchase boxes are a few commercial packages that we are not legally entitled to redistribute without restriction - most notably Acrobat Reader and Adobe Flash, which of course you can get from upstream.

What we really charge for these days is...boxes and manuals, paid tech support (community support is available in all the places listed above), online training, commercial products and support, and hardware stuff like Mandriva Flash. Which is really no different from any other vendor: after all, Canonical's business model is selling support. I don't think there's anything you can officially get for free from Ubuntu / Canonical that you can only get from Mandriva by paying.
