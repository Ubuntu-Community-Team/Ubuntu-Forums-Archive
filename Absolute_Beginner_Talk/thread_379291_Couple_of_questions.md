### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-03-08
Considering your fairly old PC specs, you may want to try Xubuntu rather than ordinary Ubuntu. Xubuntu uses a more lightweight GUI - but the software is exactly the same. It may give you better performance than ordinary Ubuntu does.

That being said - I installed ordinary Ubuntu on an old laptop (with lower specs than yours!) and it runs about a million times better than Windows did on that machine. It's not 'screamingly fast', but it's not so slow as it will irritate you. The main performance hit is in running lots of different applications at once, if you keep it to a minimum, you should be fine.

The advantage of Linux is that it's very modular. If ordinary Ubuntu is feeling sluggish, you can swap out virtually anything for something more lightweight. The GUI is only one example. There are lots of different GUIs available for you to try out. A big favourite for people who like lightweight software is Fluxbox. It's very quick, but (in my opinion) not quite as aesthetically pleasing as Gnome or XFCE.

Once you get to grips with Linux, you can even remove things from the kernel (the underlying core of your operating system) which you don't need, such as support for obscure filesystems, support for various types of networking which you know you don't need etc etc. Don't try this straight away - it's a pretty complicated thing to do, but there are tools available to make it all a bit easier and more straightforward.

Also, welcome to the community :)


EDIT: There are places on the internet where you can request a CD of Ubuntu to be shipped to you. Depending on the website, they MAY ask for a small sum of money to cover the cost of shipping - but most are very cheap, or even free. Alternatively, you can just try asking a friend to burn the image to a CD for you to ue.
