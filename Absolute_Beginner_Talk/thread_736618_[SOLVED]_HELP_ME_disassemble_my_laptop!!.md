### Post by Blacklife08 on 2008-03-26
I currently am using a IBM Thinkpad T43, and i dont know how similar they are, but im going to guess not very since i have a side opening to my harddrive.  I will offer the advice of not taking off the LCD screen.  They are very fragile and a bear to put back on.

When you flip it over to the back are there any openings or hidden covers that might pop off?  if so, take a look in those.  If that doesnt work, try taking off the front and rear bezel (thing with the touch pad, and the other plastic part between the keyboard and the screen)

After those are off, if u have taken out all the screws, the keyboard should simply pop off.  There is always a fine line between too much and not enough pressure.  U dont want to break the parts, but u also have to apply enough.  Try to get the keyboard off, after u can do that everything should be easy to get to!

Hope this helped
Good luck

To get the bottom cover off, you usually push the keyboard out first...there are some screws under the battery too. After the keyboard is pushed out, there will be more screws holding the cover down. You probably see the shiny metal under side of the keyboard under the battery though a small square space...push there.

### Post by doctorbighands on 2008-03-27
I finally figured it out. Good lord, I can breathe...

There's this stupid little plastic doohickey (about 3 inches wide by .25 inches tall) that has to be removed first. It looks for all the world like it's part of a much larger piece of plastic, but after coming to the end of my rope, I just stuck a little screwdriver under there, and it snapped right off! After surveying and finding no damage, I realized that popping this thing off revealed two small screws that were holding the keyboard in place. Unscrew those, un-jam the keys with a screwdriver, and voila! Puzzle solved.

What a PAIN.

Oh, and after more searching, I did finally find a manual posted on the IBM/Lenovo website - after I had already figured this out, of course. Thanks, Murphy.
