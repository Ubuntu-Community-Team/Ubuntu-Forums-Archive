### Post by timetunnel on 2006-01-28
I think Liferea is a very good (the best, in my book) RSS aggregator. It is able to subcribe to podcasts as well. You can then download the podcasted files to your harddisc manually or launch a play in Liferea. On my system, that's Firefox with the mplayer plugin. Podcast aggregators are amaroK or ipodder, for example. I found ipodder to be sufficient for my first steps in podcasting but then switched to Liferea. amaroK is a good choice if you want to have you music and podcast collection in one application (like iTunes). But amaroK doesn't play m4a / mp4 files so far. Rhythmbox does, but it is not able to download podcasts so far (though it will be able to do with the next ubuntu release, as far as I know). So, there's no equivalent to iTunes so far. But Rhythmbox could  be soon.
