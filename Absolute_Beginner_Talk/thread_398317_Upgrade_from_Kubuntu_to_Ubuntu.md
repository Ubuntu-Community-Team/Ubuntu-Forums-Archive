### Post by aysiu on 2007-03-31
Well if you installed the Gnome desktop, the you have both Gnome *and* KDE installed, not just KDE.

But if you're using *only* the Ubuntu Alternate CD for Feisty, you won't be able to do a proper upgrade, since you should be upgrading all your packages, and the Alternate CD you have will upgrade only your Ubuntu packages, not your Kubuntu ones.

What you should do is first [remove all the Kubuntu stuff](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/puregnome). **Then**, do the upgrade with the Alternate CD.

### Post by rillip on 2007-04-01
I think you're letting yourself get confused here over the idea that they're different.

Kubuntu is Ubuntu with a K.  The K is for KDE.  The rest of it IS Ubuntu.

Ubuntu 6.06 (Dapper Drake) is the exact same stuff as Kubuntu 6.06 (Dapper Drake), except KDE is installed instead of Gnome, and some of the KDE apps are installed instead of Gnome. The OS is exactly the same, the only difference is the by default installed software suite.  You can take Ubuntu and make Kubuntu.  You can take Kubuntu and make Xubuntu. They're all the same, just different guis and different program selection to match.

If you upgrade from Kubuntu 6.06 to 6.10 to 7.4, it'll work.  If you go from Kubuntu of those versions to Ubuntu of those versions, I'm sure all your Gnome programs will work, but some of your KDE ones might get left out of the upgrade, as Ubuntu doesn't care about them.  I believe that is why it was recommended you remove the "Kubuntu stuff" before doing this, so that when you reinstall, you'll have fresh, most up-to-date versions, rather than having to try and do a difficult upgrade on old packages.

If you want to have everything work, your best course of action is to upgrade to Kubuntu 7.4, then install Gnome again.  If you're not using KDE apps anymore, you can skip it and just go to Ubuntu.

Hope that helps.
