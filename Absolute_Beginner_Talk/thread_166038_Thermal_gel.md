### Post by echo $USER on 2006-04-25
What CPU are you getting, and it is coming with a heatsink?  If it is there should be thermal gel already on the heatsink that will melt into place once you start the PC and the CPU heats up.  

If not, grab some [arctic silver 5]("http://www.arcticsilver.com/").  If it is an Intel chip, place a small amount (about the size of a grain of rice) on the middle of the chip. Do not spread it out.  Then place the heatsink on top of the CPU, allowing the presure of the heatsink to spread out the paste for you.  If it's an AMD cpu put a little bit more on than you would it if it was an Intel chip.

### Post by arsenic23 on 2006-04-25
If you've purchased a Retail CPU and do not intend to overclock it, then I'd recomend using the provided heatsink, which should already have a strip of thermal compound on it.  This is almost always the best way to do it when you build your first PC.  

If your using an aftermarket heat sink, then I recomend you search AMD, or Intell's website, whichever your using.  I can't seem to find it now, but I'm almost certain both companies have needlessly over detailed instuctions for applying thermal compound.
