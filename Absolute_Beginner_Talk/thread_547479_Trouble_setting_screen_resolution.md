### Post by aquavitae on 2007-09-12
fluxbox is just another window manager (like gnome) but isn't really recommended for newbies. xorg.conf refers to the file /etc/X11/xorg.conf.  The "screen resolution" section in the settings is just really a nice editor for the xorg.conf file, and a lot of experienced linux users prefer editing it by hand because that way you can be certain of exactly whats going on in it.

Do you know what graphics card you have?

To fix your resolution, first try this: open a terminal and type the following
```
sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg
```This will start a little program to configure your display. Once its done, log out and log back in.  If this doesn't work, open the file /etc/X11/xorg.conf, and post its contents.  Its also often useful to have the last few lines of the log file: /var/log/Xorg.0.log

"After initial installation, widescreen resolutions are sometimes unavaible, even if you edit xorg.conf.

For models that have the intel onboard video chipset, installing the package "915resolution" from the repositories will solve this problem. Reboot the system, and your screen resolution should be correct. "
