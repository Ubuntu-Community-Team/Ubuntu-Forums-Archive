### Post by Paul820 on 2008-01-20
You can put the folder in your home folder. The reason they say download it to your desktop is so it is easy to find. I usually make a folder inside my home folder called **downloads** and download to that. Then i just extract it in that folder and do the configure etc from there. It's up to you anyway, put them where you can find them.

### Post by Paul820 on 2008-01-20
To save syntax errors when pointing the terminal to the folder download a program called **nautilus-open-terminal** through synaptic and then you can right click in the folder and choose to open the terminal from there. I use it all the time, saves the fingers and errors :) You need to logout and login again to get it to show in the right-click context menu though.
