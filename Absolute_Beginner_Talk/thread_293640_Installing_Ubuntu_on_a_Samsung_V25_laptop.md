### Post by Tarvok on 2006-11-05
I don't know if it does or not, but there is a safe and easy way to find out if it has your drivers by default. The install disk is also a live disk--it won't touch your hard drive until you specifically tell it to. Just pop that sucker in your CD drive, make sure your BIOS is set to look at the CD drive first during startup, and see what happens. It'll run a fully functional (though a bit slower than if you actually have it installed) session of Ubuntu, and you can try it out, see if your laptop works.

If there are things that don't, I am sure there are those here who can help you out. Either way, your laptop will remain unchanged until you start the installation process.
