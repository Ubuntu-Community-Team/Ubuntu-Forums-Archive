### Post by kewl1uk on 2006-01-28
Any Broadband provider which allows sharing of connections and the use of a router for a home network will work in Linux. I think the method of logging in with a router on BT/Yahoo is to make the router's Username [email]username@btopenworld.com[/email], [email]username@btyahoo.com[/email], [email]username@btinternet.com[/email], etc., i.e. the email address.

I'm on AOL Platinum and I don't have windows on this computer and connect on Ubuntu with no problems. I have a NetGear DG834B on a wired home network. The method to connect with a router on AOL is to make a new screenname and password for the router which is then permanently connected to the Internet with a static IP until you turn the router off. So you don't need the AOL software to connect. Full info is in the Home Networking FAQ on AOL.

btw I have AOL because it's one of the few UK ISPs which have unlimited downloads. With most of the other ISPs in the UK you're limited to something like 1GB per month.

A dedicated Linux ISP in the UK is [http://www.uklinux.net/](http://www.uklinux.net/) which provides free dial-up - phone charges apply but you need to provide your own modem. It's subsidiary [http://www.uklinuxadsl.net/](http://www.uklinuxadsl.net/) provides Linux compatible unlimited Broadband but you have to supply your own modem/router. Setup on UKLinux Broadband is expensive at over £100 but after that monthly charges are about average for UK unlimited Broadband.
