### Post by aysiu on 2008-02-22
While there might be some ways to meet your specific needs, you may decide those ways require too much work to get up and running or the applications are not familiar enough or "good" enough compared to the ones you're used to using.

Given your computing habits, I think answers to your questions won't really give you a sense of what to expect with Ubuntu. You should just try it out.

I would highly advise using the Ubuntu Desktop CD as a "live CD" (the default behavior, as long as you don't click the *Install* icon on the desktop), which will allow you to use the OS without affecting your hard drive at all.

If you have enough RAM (512 MB bare minimum, 1 GB or more recommended), you might also want to consider [trying Ubuntu as a virtual machine within Windows.](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/virtualbox)
