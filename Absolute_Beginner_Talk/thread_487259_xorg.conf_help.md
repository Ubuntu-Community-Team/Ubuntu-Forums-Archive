### Post by alienexplorers on 2007-06-28
To change your resolution you can try the old modeline trick.  You enter it in your xorg.conf.

>   # 1024x768 @ 70.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 56.00 kHz; pclk: 76.16 MHz
  Modeline "1024x768_70.00"  76.16  1024 1080 1192 1360  768 769 772 800  -HSync +Vsync

Your monitor section would then look like:
> Section "Monitor"
Identifier "Generic Monitor"
 Modeline "1024x768_70.00"  76.16  1024 1080 1192 1360  768 769 772 800  -HSync +Vsync
Option "DPMS"
EndSection

### Post by phr0ze on 2007-06-29
Dont listen to any of these guys. Yeah the Modeline might work but thats not the right way. You need to modify your monitor section to something like this.

```
Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"Generic Monitor"
	Option		"DPMS"
	Horizsync	30-92
	Vertrefresh	50-85
EndSection

```

But you see the horiz and Vert numbers above? Those are for my monitor. You have to look up your monitor and put in the correct number ranges. Your monitor can be googled to get these ranges or just put your monitor model number on here and we can google it for you.
