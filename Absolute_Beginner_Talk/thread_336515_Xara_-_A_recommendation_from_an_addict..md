### Post by thewump on 2007-01-11
I thought I'd take a moment to share my experiences of Xara.

I've been using Xara for years ( Xara Webster then Xara 1 the Xara Extreme ) and it's a STUNNINGLY capable design tool for the web.

ABOUT XARA IN GENERAL
While many people switching to linux may lament the loss of Photoshop and Illustrator, I on the other hand, shed a tear the day I booted off a Ubuntu disk and found that Xara have an opensource project.. 

So here is what Xara IS and ISN'T.
It's an illustrator like, vector graphic tool that also handles bitmaps.  What that means is, if you are designing a web page in its entirety, or some buttons and all that kind of stuff you can do it in Xara, and you can also bring bitmaps into Xara and encorporate them into your design.  Unlike Illustrator, you can then export individual "pieces" of your design.. a word, a button, a graphic out to an image file.. 

It ISN'T photoshop.. It's not something you use to manipulate images in terms of adding effects etc. It's not something you would use to create fake images etc.  It's bitmap functionality is more about adding borders, using an image as a fill for another shape, fading an image etc.

So... over all this time,  Xara has been my staple design tool, but I've also used Paint Shop Pro too.. and used them together some times.

ABOUT XARA FOR LINUX
You can install Xara straight out of your package manager, although as of today that installs the most "stable build" which I've learned on the Xara forum is really old, and the latest version you can get is also stable.

That's at [http://www.xaraxtreme.org](http://www.xaraxtreme.org)

I would very much to see, and assist in, the proliferation of this application.  In the PC world it has never gotten the acknowledgement it deserves - that's an Adobe dominated world.. but maybe over here in Linux it can get more attention.
