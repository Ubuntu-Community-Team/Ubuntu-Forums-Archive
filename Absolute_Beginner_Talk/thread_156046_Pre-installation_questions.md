### Post by jimbren on 2006-04-06
on most laptops, the wifi on/off is not OS-specific, so it should work.  people seem to have mixed results with the fuction buttons from laptop to laptop--i've got a dell latitude d400 that i haven't been able to get the function buttons to work with, but i know others have.  i think it just matters how much you care and how much energy you want to devote to it. 

You'll have a battery indicator on the toolbar with Ubuntu or Kubuntu.  I don't know about the photo editing questions.  

Gftp is an excellent ftp client that works well with Gnome and KDE.

### Post by Donshyoku on 2006-04-06
I have a CTO Compaq V2000, the Intel model.  

On mine, the function buttons work (save for the media controls... brightness and internet launch buttons are working).  The volume does work, but the orange light under the mute button does not light up.  You can, however, just glance at the notification area and see a red X next to the speaker to ensure you are muted.  

The wifi button does work, but again, the light does not.  It is a hardware switch, so it is not tied to the OS in any way.  I, for one, have just adopted the idea of leaving the wifi on constantly so I don't have to mess with it.

And yes, Gnome and KDE have power bars in the notification area.

Good luck!
