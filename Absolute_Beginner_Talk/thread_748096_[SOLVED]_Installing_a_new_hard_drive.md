### Post by backflip on 2008-04-07
I wanted something similar, but not for ipod reasons. I already had an old internal  hard drive which wasn't being used and bought a caddy for it from Ebay (about £5 for a 2.5 and £10 for 3.5 HD) and this allowed me to use it as a usb external.  I took the internal HD out of the laptop, just in case of a mess up, and changed the bios to boot from cd then usb HD. I installed the operating system on the external usb HD and then rebooted. I had to change the BIOS once again, this time to boot first from usb and then internal  hd. Once I'd put the internal back in its place I had a system which boots automatically into the external HD if that is switched on, and if it isn't switched on, it boots from the internal HD. There is no risk of any grub or loading problems, and at least one OS will always work. Actually I was doing the opposite of you, I have Vista on my internal and I boot Ubuntu from the external HD I made. Works fine for me, no problems at all.
