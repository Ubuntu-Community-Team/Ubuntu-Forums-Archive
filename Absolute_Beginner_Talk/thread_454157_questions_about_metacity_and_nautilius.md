### Post by mcduck on 2007-05-25
You are comparing apples and oranges here..

Metacity is the window manager, allowing you to move application windows around and providing frames for the windows.

Nautilus is the file manager (and also handles your desktop). If you want the address bar, just hit ctrl-L. I suppose it can also be configured to be shown by default through settings, but if not, the setting is in gconf-editor.

For default applications, the settings for browsers and e-mail and such are under System/Preferences, and for others just right-click a file, select Properties and set the app you want in there.
