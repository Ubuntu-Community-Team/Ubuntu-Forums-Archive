### Post by JawsThemeSwimming428 on 2008-04-02
It probably won't work 100% unless the new computer has the EXACT same hardware. The drive will be recognized and you will probably be able to boot into Ubuntu but some devices (video, sound, network, etc.) may not function properly.

---

### Post by Hospadar on 2008-04-02
It depends on the new machine's computer in comparison to the old one.  It may boot up just fine and get working right away, but there may be problems.

If you do encounter any serious problems, probably the easiest (if not only) way to resolve them is to just do a re-install.  but it certainly won't hurt to try and just plop it over.
