### Post by scorp123 on 2007-11-22
> **arashiko28 said:**
>   I already had the experience that the projectors do not support special effects as beryl  And why? What kind of projectors do you use? The projectors I have used so far are standard VGA projectors that will display whatever is on my screen .... Compiz / Beryl eye-candy or not. And in my experience having a bit of eye-candy during a presentation rather helps to catch your audience's attention ... Especially the "fire" effect (Shift + Win key + Left mouse) is *killer* if you want to highlight something on-screen :lolflag:

Of course the projector would need to support the resolution and the refresh rate you set your graphics card to. Having applets such as "resapplet" may come in handy. But the thing is not all projectors support all resolutions and not all refresh rates. Hence if you don't really know what brand / model and kind of projector you will encounter it's best to really test your setup way in advance. Most projectors I had to deal with so far support at least 1024 x 768 pixels, 16-bit color depth and a 60 Hz refresh rate. So I'd start there and try if it could support higher resolutions too. 

Testing this stuff only 1 hour before the actual presentation is a bad idea. Based on my experience I'd suggest you test that stuff at least half a day before, especially if it's an important presentation. Always have a "Plan B" ready if something fails. I always have 1-2 live CD's (usually: Ubuntu + the latest Knoppix ... just to be on the safe side) and a USB stick (with a copy of my documents on it) with me :) ... Just to make sure that even if my laptop gets stolen I could still do my presentation **somehow** .... (excuses such as "the dog ate my homework" or rather "my apologies, but my laptop just got stolen ... so I can't do the presentation now" won't fly too well with certain people I have to deal with. I guess you are in a similar situation ....)

One time the projector I was supposed to use "disappeared" (some moron had taken it away) and Ubuntu saved my life and my career: I quickly installed Apache via "apt", converted my presentation to PDF, created a wireless network and served my stuff that way to my audience. They liked the option of being able to download my presentation right away off my "web server" to their laptops or PDA's. In the end this "Plan B" turned out to be better than my original "Plan A". :)

Keep in mind that even if something silly like what happened to you kinda almost ruins your presentation, Ubuntu offers many possibilties, many potential "Plan B's" and "Plan C's" if your "Plan A" goes wrong (e.g. projector gets damaged and refuses to work).
