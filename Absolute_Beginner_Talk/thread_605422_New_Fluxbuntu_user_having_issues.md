### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-11-07
Okay, backgrounds:

You should have an icon on your desktop for the home folder? Open it. Drag the new window outta your way if you have to, to get to the icon again. Leave that window open. This time Right click the icon. I think in the popup you'll see an option for background, I think the bottom line? Click that. What you do is you just drag the pic you want from the window open to your home folder, into the little box that comes up, plus it'll offer to resize it for you, just click the option you want (you can try them first) and when you have it as you want, ok it. 

Like a lot of things in it, it seems so confusing at first but once you do it a few times and start finding your way around, it's real nifty. :)

Kazehekase (gotta love that name) is slow? Hm. You could replace Kazehekase with Firefox, but generally Kazehekase is at least as fast. Maybe there's something else going on.

Flash and PowerPoint. Not sure Kazehekase does Flash. You might have to replace it with Firefox then. 

Powerpoint. Actually I've never had to use it! But as far as I know, the free OpenOffice Suite can do much of the same thing. I'd suggest trying it.

OpenOffice, Firefox and Flash can all be installed free, safely and easily with the Synaptic Package Manager that should be included in your system. You may need to enable additional repositories; this page can help:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Repositories/Ubuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Repositories/Ubuntu)

Do feel free to post here, the Desktop Environments forum here, or
[http://community.fluxbuntu.org/](http://community.fluxbuntu.org/)

Good luck and always try to enjoy yourself :)
