### Post by majabl on 2007-04-08
Recently I added XFCE to my existing installation of Dapper Drake using **sudo aptitude install xubuntu-desktop**, as I have quite a low-end system and wanted to see if there was a noticeable change in performance.  There wasn't - Firefox seems to be the weak link in the change, and it's as slow for me under XFCE as it is under Gnome.  Although XFCE worked just fine, given that Gnome looks prettier, I decided to remove XFCE.

Somewhere along the way, XFCE appears to have overridden the default background colour with a sky blue.  This looks great for XFCE's splash boot windows, but AWFUL for the default Ubuntu theme under Gnome!

Please could someone running a standard installation of 6.06 go to **System >> Administration >> Login Window**, click on **Background Colour**, and give me the values (hue, saturation, value, and RGB) I need to restore the background colour to its default?

Thanks in advance!

---

### Post by zvacet on 2007-04-08
hue=31
saturation=40
value=85
R=218
G=176
B=130
coulor name=#DAB082
