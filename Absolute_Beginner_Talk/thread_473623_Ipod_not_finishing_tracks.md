### Post by tgalati4 on 2007-06-14
Cross-fade was added recently by Apple so it's installed in the firmware update when you initialized your iPod.  

We would need more specifics to help test out your scenario:

What model iPod?  What version of iTunes was used to initialize it?

xmms has a crossfade plug in.  You can hook your iPod to your computer and play your tracks through xmms to get crossfade.  I'm not sure about other music applications.

Sounds like your iPod needs resetting as tnseditor suggests or your iPod's database is messed up.  

If you have super-long tracks (half-hour or more) then sometimes you will get a buffering problem.  The iPod was optimized for short songs not long podcasts.  So does your iPod do this on all tracks or just long ones?
