### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-03-29
I did a little google search on defraging windows formats under linux and turned up this. NOTHING. It appears there is no native linux solution but there may be another solution. Maybe, this is just a maybe, wine may be able to do it. 

Never tried it but my guess is that you would have to associate the drive with wine as a drive via winecfg command. Then you may be able to download a third party program to do the defrag for you.

There may be another option too. If you have a legal full copy of XP (not an OEM install), there may be the option of [BartPE]("http://www.nu2.nu/pebuilder/") or my personal pick [Ultimate Boot CD for Windows]("http://www.ubcd4win.com/"). These are both like LiveCDs of windows but UBCD has more software/options. Assuming you have a valid copy of Windows this may be viable option.
