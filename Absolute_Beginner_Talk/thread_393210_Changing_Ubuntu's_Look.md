### Post by jem7v on 2007-03-25
GTK2 and Application themes are the same thing.  They affect colors, buttons, menus, scroll bars, and things like that.  They actually Don't come with icon sets most of the time.
Metacities and Window Borders are the same thing.  They only affect the window border.
Wallpapers and icon themes are what they are.

Sometimes the authors of a GTK theme will also have a Metacities theme by the same name.  If you install both and use them together you'll usually have a pretty unified theme.  On the other hand, you can mix and match a lot of GTK and Metacities themes and end up with a decent looking result.  I often do that.

Is there a specific look you're trying to duplicate that you could give us a link to, that we could be more specific helping you?


(Also, if you're running Edgy or higher, make sure you have the gtk2-engines-pixbuf package installed or else some themes won't display properly. I think it's in the Universe repository, so have that enabled before you look for it in Synaptic.)
