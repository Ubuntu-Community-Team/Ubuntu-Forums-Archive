### Post by shivam.seth on 2008-04-12
If you are not able to play various video formats then that could be because your system does not have the proper codecs for decoding the video formats. These formats are not open-source and thus as per Ubuntu's policies, they cannot be included in the Ubuntu installation pack. You would need to download these codecs from internet. You can use the synaptic manager and search for gstreamer. there would be three codec packages named ugly, good and bad. Download all the three ... they are enough to play a lot of formats.


 But I think if this would have been your problem then you would have already knew the reason as whichever player you would use to play your video files will show the error message along with the reason...missing codecs. and they give u an option to download the missing codec automatically from internet.

So did I interpret your problem wrong? Please clear by what you mean by 'flash drive' !
