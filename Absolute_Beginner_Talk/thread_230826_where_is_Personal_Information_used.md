### Post by kebes on 2006-08-06
As far as I know that information is only used internally. It may be available to your internal applications (like mail program and contact manager), but it isn't broadcast anywhere.

However your network identification (or "computer name" which you entered when installing), and your "hostname" are available to others who are on the network.

In a terminal you can type "hostname" to see what it currently is. For most home desktops it is just set as "localhost" and that's fine. Note that changing it may disrupt your network access, depending how all that is configured...

I think the short answer is: don't worry about it. You can change that data to be anonymous if you really want to, and it won't disrupt your system.
