### Post by msak007 on 2006-11-12
I'm in the market for a new phone and have done some research into this as well. First, it depends on how you want to connect to your computer. Personally, I prefer a USB cable over Bluetooth because of transfer rates (I assume you'll be transferring music to your phone - you'll want a good transfer rate), and there's no setup involved. A good place to start is [TuxMobil]("http://tuxmobil.org/phones_linux.html") - people try phones and then report which ones work.

From what I can gather, the best Linux-friendly phones are SonyEricsson and Motorola, but others such as Samsung or LG may also fare well. I have a Nokia 6682, and it will not mount as a removable storage device because Nokia uses a proprietary serial --> USB cable that does not have Linux drivers (nor will they provide any). If you're with Verizon, you're safe in that regards because they don't carry any Nokia phones. But, I can't tell you how well the phones they offer work. My brother's E815 from Verizon doesn't work well with Linux (over USB anyway), so I'm not sure how others fare. They mostly offer Motorola and LG so those are the 2 brands you may want to focus on researching. Another good place to check is [HowardForums]("http://www.howardforums.com/") - you can search specific vendor sub-forums (like Motorola or LG) and see if any users have reported success using Linux. Hope this helps.
