### Post by navneeth on 2006-09-06
I've been dealing with three differnt threads of mine in the last week, and although the topics were slightly different from each other, the subject eventually came down to the slowness of my internet connection. 

These are the ones...
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=250204](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=250204) (slowness of connection)

I was asked to make the 'value' true in about:config in Firefox, but that didn't work. I was also asked to disable ipv6 using the terminal if it worked in Firefox. 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=250416](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=250416) (radio on real player)
Well, I could listen to the wmv stream with mplayer and its plugin for Firefox, but it wouldn't last long thanks to - you guessed it - the connection.


[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=251479](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=251479) (installing opera)

Seeing that Firefox wasn't that all that good (I can't belive I said that, being an Fx fan in Windows), I wanted to try Opera. I eventually did install it, but again there were little problems with the internet speed being slow.

Now, as a last try, I disabled ipv6 as has been suggested in numerous threads here, and elsewhere in the www. I rebooted the computer, and lo and behold, a super-fast internet connection. I've been listening to the radio for the past 20 minutes or so (as of this post) without any interruption.

Now, what's the reason for this post, you may ask. Well, it's just to thank everyone who has helped me so far in all the threads mentioned above.
