### Post by Jucato on 2006-01-25
I'm also a new convert, just installed Kubuntu 3 weeks ago, but have been doing research on Linux since December.

Why Linux?
1. Open Source - got addicted to this concept. I mean, why dish out $$$$ for software, when there are alternatives (sometimes better) out there?
2. Among the other OS alternatives, it's was the one with the widest support I've seen, from books to sites to communities.
3. The power it gives me over my PC, especially the way it looks and feels. I've always wanted that.
4. I like the penguin :)

Why Ubuntu/Kubuntu:
1. Totally free! No strings attached. No money-driven, monopolizing tendencies.
2. Fits in 1 CD. Of course there are stuff to  download from the repos. But the installation "just works" out of the box (except for non-free stuff). Oh and the Live CD is also on 1 CD, that's just sooo great.
3. A great community. Have been looking here and there in some forums. So far this is one of those that don't have flame wars going on (..yet??)

I haven't tried out other distros out there because of reasons #1 and #2 above (free and 1 CD). I mean, do I really need to download 5 CDs for a Live CD, just to test it out?  So far, the only 1 Live CD Distros I've seen are Ubuntu cousins, Mandriva Move, SimplyMEPHIS, and SUSE Eval (excluding the *Noppix bro's). But I tried Ubuntu/Kubuntu out first and fell in love with it. :)
