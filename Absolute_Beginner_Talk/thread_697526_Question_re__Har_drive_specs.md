### Post by neurostu on 2008-02-15
Well it depends on what you need. You can pick up a 30-50gb HDD on ebay for really cheap. I bought a 250gb for $100 a year ago.  I imagine that you could get atleast 30 gigs for under 30 bucks.

If you are just using the computer for general use (Word Processing, Internet Browsing, some light music listening) then 30 gigs will be more then enough. If you want to build a nice media center then you should probably get more space.

### Post by neurostu on 2008-02-15
Again, if your computer is locking up it could be b/c the hard drive is actually failing. Most HDD's have a life cycle of about 3 years. Your computer sounds a bit older then that...  so a new HDD would most likely help out.
