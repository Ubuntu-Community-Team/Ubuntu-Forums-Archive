Did you update & remove items from the Startup tab from MSConfig, or just Add/Remove programs?  Startup tab items typically have a huge effect on boot times.  Also, you may want to download & install CCleaner to remove temp files, etc.  Don't forget to defrag when all is done.

### Post by Dave54 on 2007-04-23
I know this isn't the place for MS troubleshooting, but...

The msconfig tool messes with the options in an unclean way. If possible, use services (start > programs > administrative tools > services) and set them to disable. A;so, in MS config, I wouldn't recommend changing anything outside of the last "startup" tab.

I too have found that a (windows) computer after cleaning runs slower. On one of the wost (windows) computers I've worked on, before I fixed it, it would start up in literally about 7 seconds. It also wouldn't ever turn off (without the power being cut), but that's beside the point ;)
