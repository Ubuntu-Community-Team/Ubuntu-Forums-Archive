### Post by Terc on 2007-10-31
Welcome to the forums!

I'm also quite new to Linux, and although your learning style may vary, I think the best way to acclimate yourself to Linux is to just jump in.  Install Ubuntu at home, and try using it for a week or so.  You'll probably find yourself learning a lot, and you'll probably get a little bit better idea of just what it is you need to learn about Linux.  Some things just don't have parallels between Windows and Linux, others are quite obvious once you've used both.  I hope someone can recommend some reading for you, but I strongly advise you to start using Ubuntu on your own pc, or at very least, on a second pc or partition.

### Post by macogw on 2007-10-31
Check out The Official Ubuntu Book 2nd Edition (it's based on Feisty, but the first is for Dapper which is over a year old, so it's closer), for the beginner stuff of acclimation, then check out Hacking Ubuntu for the advanced stuff (including how to make heterogenous networks work).

Also, just diving in and not letting yourself switch back to Windows for a while works very very well.  I didn't dual boot at first (I do now, but it's a Linux/Linux dual-boot :p), so I was forced to learn, just like I was forced to learn on Windows.  Reading the questions and answers on here (and in the more advanced topics parts of the forum) will also teach you a lot.
