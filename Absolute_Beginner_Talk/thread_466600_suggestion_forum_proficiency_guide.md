### Post by DavidFourer on 2007-06-06
I suspect most of us use the forums to fix anything that's wrong.

Suggestion: review a lot of random forum posts and make a list of the the things to learn, based on the frequency of references in the forums.  This would include: 1) How to do things (find a system file, read it, get permission and edit it). 2) Most commonly fixed files and what they are about.  3) most used commands.

When looking for a fix (usually a hardware bug) I find that looking for exactly the same bug is fruitless.  Instead of looking for the exact bug or asking for help, I like to look at related problems and try to apply the methods for diagnosing and fixing that I find there.  Because fixing computers is not my job, I need to make a reference text file of commands and info that I used before.  Usually I'll need it again and can't remember where I found it before.  I also read a primer on the BASH shell, but forgot most of it long ago.

Weird things come up.  Once I miss-edited some seemingly un-critical file, but could not start my computer.  I had to find another computer, print a list of VI editor commands, work in non-graphics mode, and remove the typo.  This shows the value of some background reading when nothing is going wrong, even if you don't remember the details.
