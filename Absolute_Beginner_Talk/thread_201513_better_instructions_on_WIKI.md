### Post by Brunellus on 2006-06-21
Wiki and forum instructions use the command line because there is far far FAR less chance of confusion.  

How's that?  It is possible for us to do all the magic for you, typing all the commands that are **known to work**.  All you have to know how to do is copy and paste them into a terminal window, and things should work as we say they will.

The point-and-click way is fine if you can figure it out yourself, but it ends in terribly frustrating conversations.  In many cases, we don't know *exactly* what you're doing/seeing unless we see it ourselves.  

So don't be scared if some of the howtos jump straight into the command line. There's a reason.

### Post by aysiu on 2006-06-24
The best instructions I ever found--the ones that got me into Ubuntu and unafraid of the command-line for the first time--were at [http://www.ubuntuguide.org](http://www.ubuntuguide.org)

They were simple and cleanly laid out. All I had to do was copy and paste the commands, and everything worked. I didn't have to learn how the operating system was structured... but, you know what? I eventually did learn *something* about how it's structured.

Everyone has her own way of learning. Some learn by rote and eventually come to understand the concepts. Some need to understand the concepts before they even want to touch the basic functions and interface.

Something that I learned from five years of teaching high school: you have to address different kinds of learning styles.

It sounds cheesy, but it's true. At first, I just wanted to teach things the way I learned best--be the teacher I wish I'd had. Eventually, I branched out to reaching students who were different skill levels and who reached understanding in different ways with different approaches.

We're, in a way, in an electronic classroom with literally tens of thousands of students (if not millions). We need varied (but good) documentation. I like some of maspiotis' ideas, but in the Linux world things generally get done when you *do* them, not when you *demand* them.
