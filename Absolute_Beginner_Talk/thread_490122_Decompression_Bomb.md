### Post by Malibu Illusion on 2007-07-02
In short, and simplified, a compression bomb is an archive that has an insanely large compression ratio that appears to be very small but, if you try and extract it, could attempt to create files that are much larger than anticipated potentially causing your system to crash if you do not have the available memory to be able to deal with these files.

This could be intentionally caused by someone, or maybe accidentally triggered in your AV program if the compression ratio is detected as very high for whatever reason.
