### Post by T700 on 2006-07-29
> **kelo said:**
> I should probably read the forums.....;)

An excellent idea, but let's get you going first.

1.  You downloaded something called an ISO.  You must use your current CD burning software (Nero, CD Creator, etc) to **burn the ISO image.**  DO NOT copy it to a disk or make a bootable disk--BURN THE ISO IMAGE, or it will not work.

2.  Put that burned ISO image disk in your PC and restart.  If your PC is set to allow boots from CD, it will restart in Ubuntu.  (If not, go into the BIOS and change it to allow that.)  Play around.  Reboot and remove the disk.  You are back at Windows.

You do not need and can not use any of your Windows software.  Everything you need is include or available for installation.  The CD you downloaded will have Firefox on it, along with email and word processing software.

Down the road, you can install Ubuntu to your hard drive and get -much- better performance.  You can dual boot so you have Windows and Ubuntu or you can single boot to Ubuntu, but doing so will erase everything from your Windows setup.

Please note:  When you post a question, a descriptive title is much more likely to get you help than, "Panic!", "Help!", or the like.

Welcome to the forums!
