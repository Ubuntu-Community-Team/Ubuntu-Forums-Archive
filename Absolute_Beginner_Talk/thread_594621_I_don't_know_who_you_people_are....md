### Post by IWood on 2007-10-28
...but *thank you*.

By "people," I mean all you open source folks who build and compile kernels, maintain repositories, cobble drivers together, troubleshoot, kill bugs, write nifty apps, and show up in the noob forums to help folks like me out.

Obviously, I'm new to Ubuntu in particular and Linux derivatives in general. But I  just had to tell all of you how impressed I am by all of this. I found myself with an "extra" laptop (my Dell 700m wasn't actually dead...it got better), so I thought, what the hell, let's see what this is all about.

And it worked. Clean install, and it just *worked*. Every time I thought I had run into a problem, the OS ambled off into the net, grabbed something, and installed it. When I broke something and came to this forum with a question, the forum software looked for answers based on my post title...and found them. I mean, how wacky is that: free tech support that's so good I don't even need to post the question.

There's even a fix to get the WiFi LED working on my 700m, just because someone was annoyed by that, solved the problem, and posted the solution.

Yeah, I sound all effusive and earnest. But, understand: Vista was where I finally drew the line. I use a Mac for my music production. I need to use Windows professionally, because that's what business runs on. But after reading the horror stories about Microsoft's latest bloatware, and after seeing that there was one--count it, **one**--laptop at CompUSA that came with XP, thus forcing consumers to "upgrade" whether they liked it or not, I decided that I would never use Windows for my personal computing needs again.

And now, here I am. WIth a nifty free OS that does nifty things, an Office suite that doesn't have eight billion features I don't need, and a whole slew of folks who just hang around and give away knowledge if they've got it.

That's just cool.

And that's all I've got to say about that.
