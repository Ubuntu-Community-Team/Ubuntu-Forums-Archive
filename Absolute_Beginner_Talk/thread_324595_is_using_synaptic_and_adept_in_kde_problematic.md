### Post by kd7swh on 2006-12-24
The only real downside is that some GTK apps tend to be slower in KDE. (like synaptic) and some K apps are slower in GNOME. (like adept)

Both adept and synaptic work in either GUI. They are both front-ends for apt-get and they seem to handle dependencies equally well. 

Personally, I think aptitude does the best job at handling dependencies but when I am in KDE I also use synaptic.
