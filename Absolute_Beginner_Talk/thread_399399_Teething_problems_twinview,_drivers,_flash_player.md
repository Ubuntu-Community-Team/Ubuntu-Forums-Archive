### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-04-02
Well, I expect running a 32 bit browser just means it won't take advantage of both cores, since they can't make anything too different. Otherwise, they'd alienate their userbase. So, don't worry. 

Yeah, Beryl is complicated, but it is a hell of a lot of fun, once you spend a good hour or so just checking boxes and unchecking boxes to see what it does. Experiment! 

I think the reason your monitor's flickering is because it's trying to use your other monitor's refresh rate, is that it? I'm not sure how you set refresh rates manually, but if you search it on the forums, something useful may come up. And, as a last resort only, remember this line of code:

```

sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg

```

That resets your xorg.conf using what you tell it during the setup. It will completely erase your current xorg.conf, so be careful with it.
