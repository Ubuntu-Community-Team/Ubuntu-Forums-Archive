Firstly, anything in the xorg.conf file only applies once the X system starts, so mucking around with that will do absolutely nothing to the display before you see the Ubuntu login screen.

If you don't see anything when you boot your PC, then the issue is with your monitor and the signal from your system's motherboard (set by the BIOS) - it has absolutely nothing to do with Ubuntu (or any OS you have installed).

You can try going into the BIOS and resetting everything to default, but I suspect you have a monitor problem. You need to either put another monitor on the PC to test it, or take the monitor to another PC.
