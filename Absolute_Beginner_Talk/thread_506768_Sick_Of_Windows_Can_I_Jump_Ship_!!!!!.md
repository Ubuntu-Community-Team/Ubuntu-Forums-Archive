### Post by shkm on 2007-07-22
Indeed, always try to look for Linux alternatives before using wine.  XMMS, Audacious and Beep Media Player are all great alternatives to Winamp, and there are many managers/players like iTunes if you want to delve into that kind of thing.  As a gamer, though, you really can't expect every game you throw at Wine or Cedega to work.  I'm not sure if you can game on your other WinXP computer, but I'd still keep a small XP partition around, for the games that you can't play.  You'll have a massive advantage if your 3D card is made by nVidia.

### Post by TeaSwigger on 2007-07-22
Certainly you can. It's free as in your rights and with ubuntu and most if not all of the software you'll use, free as in cost too. Be wise though! Yes there's a lot to learn (re-learn really, you had to learn windows then so you'd have to learn a new system now). Yes it's true that it might not be the best idea for gaming - some won't fly and others are involved to get running I gather. 

Of course another thing you can do is to keep windows, install ubuntu and get the hang of ubuntu first while using windows for gaming. You can have both on your computer and a screen will let you pick which one when you boot. Enjoy yourself working with the new OS and use windows 'till you're ready, then maybe keep windows just for the problem games.

Winamp is no prob. There's even a program or two that will use winamp skins - Audacious is one - so you'll be right at home on that music front. The games are going to be the toughy. Even there it's not impossible.

Help is free too. I'd encourage you to try it, take your time and enjoy transitioning over from MicroSoft's World.
