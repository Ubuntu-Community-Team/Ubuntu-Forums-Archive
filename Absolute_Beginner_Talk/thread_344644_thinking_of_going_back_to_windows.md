1) ubuntu (linux in general) is much more stable.
 2) Linux gives you much more freedom to configure your system the way you want.
3) It is much more secure (no worries about viruses, spyware, etc).
4) Most distros, and most software is free.
5) no DRM, no "windows genuine advantage" no product registration, etc.
 How much is that worth to you?

Linux is not so much harder as it is just different. Many people (including myself at first, so I know the feeling) think that because they are pretty good with windows then they should be able to just install ubuntu and be off and running with it. You soon discover that all your windows knowledge don't mean squat with linux, and it is easy to get frustrated and discouraged.
Spend some time reading how-to's and tutorials. There are tons of linux sites that offer these. This forum is a great place to start.
There is a learning curve to using linux, no question about it. Ubuntu (and this community) is the best place to start IMO. Stick with it, read, and learn. You will be glad you did.
The only real problem with using linux is that if you must use bleeding edge hardware, you may find your hardware is not supported by the linux kernel yet. A little research about hardware support can prevent this, or if your up to it, you can compile the latest kernel once your knowledge level improves (remember what I said about freedom of choice). 
The only other problem is games. Your favorite FPS game will not run in linux, as most game developers do not offer linux ported games. So dual boot with windows for games as I do.

Don't give up just yet, learn linux in your spare time, you will be glad you did.

Trebuchet said:  > IBM-clones and DOS/Windows made computers affordable for the masses

IBM-PCs were niffty pieces of kit, but they were very expensive, and not many home users could afford them in the early 80s.  Then the prices crashed with the arrival of the clone makers (competition is good for consumers).
