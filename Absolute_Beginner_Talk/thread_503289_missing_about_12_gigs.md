### Post by Rocket2DMn on 2007-07-17
When hard drive manufacturers say 120 gigs them mean 120x10^9 bytes - a factor of 10.  However, in binary you get 2^some_power so a kilobyte = 2^10 bytes = 1024 bytes, and the difference cascades more and more through megabytes and gigabytes (1KB=1024bytes, 1MB=1024KB,1GB=1024MB).  So from the 120 you may only have 110 or so true GB.  Then you lose a little to swap and boot.  You're not really missing any space.
