### Post by buuu2 on 2007-08-19
I thought i would post this for any noobies who have been driven crazy with a blurry monitor on their laptop.  I searched and searched for how to change these settings. Lots of info on editing various ´files´ but at this early stage of learning i could not figure out how to actually get the process done. one posting suggested that we should use the command sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg    I did this and it worked.  go to Applications, choose accessories, then terminal.  when the screen comes up ready, type ¨sudo dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg¨ a little program opens up. it will walk you thru a number of steps. if the OK or YES NO words are not highlighted, you must hit Tab to get it highlighted, then hit enter.  anyway, by the time you get to the monitor settings, you can choose lots of different resolutions to be included in your monitor settings from now on (use the space bar to choose the ones you want after highlighting them with the up and down arrows- more will appear as you scroll down. i chose a bunch, including 1280x768. i also changed the color depth to 16, the horizontal sync to 30-64, and the vert ref to 50-100. <<these are little tidbits i picked up thru reading lots of posts.  anyway, i hope this helps someone.  the video  card? i have is a NeoMagic NM2200.
