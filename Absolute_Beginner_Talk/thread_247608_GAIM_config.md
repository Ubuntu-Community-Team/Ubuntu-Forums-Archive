### Post by telegramsam on 2006-08-31
If I'm not mistaken, the public IP is your WAN IP, or the IP address that the outside world sees.  You can't see your WAN IP on your machine by running ipconfig or whatever the command is in Linux (An experienced user could jump in here and inform us of how to see our TCP/IP stack in Linux)...

The local IP is the IP assigned to your machine and it's relative to a gateway, which controls your LAN.  Your outside IP is changed by your modem or router to keep you secure from hackers.  

You can find out what your outside or WAN IP is here:  [www.whatismyip.com](www.whatismyip.com)

Or, if you want more detailed information, like what ports are open on your machine, go to [www.canyouseeme.org](www.canyouseeme.org)

I think that's what you're after here.  I didn't need to put any of that into Gaim to configure it, though.  Do you already have an account with Yahoo or MSN or AIM?
