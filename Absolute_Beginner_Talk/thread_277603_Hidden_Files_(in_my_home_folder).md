### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-10-14
Those hidden files (denoted by a . at the beginning of the file name) are mostly just program setting. If you've removed a program, you can go ahead and remove the settings if you want. There's really no compelling reason to do so, unless you're crunched for disk space or just don't like things cluttered.

### Post by DirtDawg on 2006-10-14
Most of the files in your Home directory are likely configuration files. Usually, they are where options for individual programs are stored when you're logged in. You can edit them manually if you like (make backups first), though there's often a GUI within the program that will do it for you.
