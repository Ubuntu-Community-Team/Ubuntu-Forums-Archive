### Post by Brendantb on 2007-04-13
Hi, 

It is perfectly natural to have a certain amount of "culture shock" when switching to Linux. Experience in another operating system almost counts against you. Just don't expect that Linux is going to be a completely parallel experience to running windows; it is different. 

Here is a guide to all things Kubuntu: 

[http://kubuntu.org/documentation.php](http://kubuntu.org/documentation.php)

It is a good place to start, and will reward reading. However, don't be afraid to just jump in. You will be able to completely wreck your installation, that is the other side of the coin of having complete control of the operating system. But most problems can be solved by reference to the guide, or looking through the forums: yours is a popular distro, and if you have a problem, chances are someone else has had it too. 

You can set up what ever folders you like in your /home directory, linux doesn't baby feed you will preset up folders. 

This is a good thread too: 

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=359663](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=359663)

There is a lot of information out there, and the trick is not to be overwhelmed!
