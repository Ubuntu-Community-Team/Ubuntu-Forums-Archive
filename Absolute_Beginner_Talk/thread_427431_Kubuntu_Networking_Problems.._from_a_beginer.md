### Post by d3dtn01 on 2007-04-29
I didn't quite understand your reply, but it sounds like you went to the Wine web site and tried to download it from there. Why not use Synaptic Package Manager as described in the link that I posted? I assume that Kubuntu comes with Synaptic (I don't know since I use Ubuntu). Synaptic is really easy to use. Once you open it, you click on the search button and enter "wine". Then it will search and give you a list of packages that are related to "wine". You find the correct package (read the descriptions) and then right click on it. I can't remember what the choices are from there but choose the one that says something like "mark for installation". After that, you click on the Apply button and it will install.

Hope this helps.
