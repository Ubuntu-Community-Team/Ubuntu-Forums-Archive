### Post by neorou on 2007-04-06
Yeah, the fact that it is free is a big big plus. It took me a while to learn the interface, using GIMPSHOP makes it slightly easier. In the end though, the best part about it is the ability to create your own filters. I'm learning Python, and I'm nowhere near being able to write anything cool, but I am starting to realize the potential of being able to do that. In the long run, I think Gimp (or Gimpshop) will be a better tool for me. I look forward to being able to write filters for myself and other Gimp users.

One of the nice things about Adobe products, especially if you are a long time CS (1,2 or 3) user, is the seamless integration with Illustrator, InDesign, Acrobat, etc. Right now, for linux, I have 2 analogs of the CS suite. I use Inkscape as my vector drawing tool. It's nowhere near as good as illustrator, but it suffices, for now.

I keep hoping that a newer and more integrated KOffice (with its various suites) will complement GIMP in the end. I guess we will see. I haven't yet tried out version 1.6 of KOffice, but their ads certainly makes it hard to ignore the combination of (at least) Krita (bitmap editing tool) and Karbon16 (vector image editing tool).
