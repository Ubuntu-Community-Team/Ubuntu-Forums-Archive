### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-08-04
I think people are grappling with this because the traditional "Distrowatch" rankings have been hacked and no longer work as proxy for anything (current popularity, installed base, "mind share" or whatever).  The Google Trends data is interesting but Forum membership and activity is probably the best overall measure of "popularity" IMO - provided you cut the older distros some slack (deliberate pun) as their long-time users are more active on mailing lists.  

Ubuntu, Gentoo and Fedora have the most active web forums - Ubuntu by most registered users, Fedora by highest single-day visits and Gentoo by total posts.  Debian, Suse and Mandriva are also pretty active.  Some of the "rising stars" in the Distrowatch rankings have very little observable forum activity by comparison.

In a market with now over 300 active distros, I think it is important that people thinking about Linux get some indication of how good their support is likely to be and Forums are, IMO, the best overall measure of that support.  More experienced users will clearly choose a distro based on other criteria.

By the way - I actually really like some of the distros with low levels of forum activity - this is not a comment on their "worthiness" or even their intrinsic merits for new users.
