### Post by Jimmey on 2006-04-12
The only thing that will screw up when using a Gnome app on KDE ( or vice versa ) will be the GUI - And even then, not that badly. I use K3B ( a KDE app ) on Gnome without any problems at all.

### Post by helpme on 2006-04-12
First off, as Jimmey already said, Gnome apps will work on KDE and KDE apps on Gnome.

Second, a way that worked for me trying to get rid of KDE was to uninstall kdelibs-bin. This should get rid of KDE, but also of all KDE programs, so you will need to reinstall them afterwards.

### Post by Sef on 2006-04-12
> I am a bit confused. I was always under the impression that Linux was the operating system and the likes of Gnome, KDE etc were just GUI's.

Technically speaking Linux is the kernel. GNU is the shell.  Hence the name Linux/GNU is more accurate way of describing the os.   Gnome and KDE are gui window managers.  (GUI = Grapical user interface.)
