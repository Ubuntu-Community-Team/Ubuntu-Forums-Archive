### Post by Victormd on 2008-03-29
RAID stands for Redundant Arrays of Inexpensive Disks. Basically what it does is use multiple HD and combines them so the system "sees" them as one, i.e., (i) you can have 2x40GB HDs setup as RAID 0 and that means that the system will "see" one 80GB HD that has twice the access speed; (ii) you can have 2x40GB HDs set up as RAID 1 and you will double the speed but the system will "see" one 40GB HD. There are other RAID configurations aside from the ones I mentioned and each has its advantages, i.e., RAID 0 gives you higher READ/WRITE speeds and 1 HD with the size of the 2 combined (as stated earlier); RAID 1 gives you higher READ speeds but will backup all your data automatically so if something happens to one of the HDs in the RAID setup, you'll still have all your data stored on the other drive.

You can get some more info here:
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAID](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAID)
