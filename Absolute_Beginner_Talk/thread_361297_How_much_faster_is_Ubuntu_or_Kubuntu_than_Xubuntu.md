### Post by ygarl on 2007-02-14
Hello mate,

I use Xubuntu on my 1ghz Pentium 3 w/ 256mB of memory. Because I have a stupidly large harddrive for this antique, I also have KDE, Fluxbox, Gnome (obviously), Windomaker and Openstep installed - just for the heck of it.

Basically my experience is (subjectively unfortunately):
KDE is slow at times - sometimes taking frustratingly long amounts of time to do the simplest things like moving a window or starting up Synaptic

Gnome is perkier - perhaps 5% faster but perfectly acceptable for ordinary use. Not surprising as Ubuntu was originally designed to run Gnome...

Openstep is faster again, but unfortunately ugly and not particularly useful to be frank. Don't bother.

XFCE (Xubuntu, if you like) is plenty fast - somewhere between Openstep and Gnome, with a good amount of eye candy. More importantly it has improved since 2001 to the point where it's not just "lighter" than Gnome but still completely functional. By this I mean that it can be used to do everything Gnome can one way or the other without having to resort to Gnome programs or the shell all the time.

Windowmaker is very fast indeed, but fairly minimal. The windowing system takes some time to get used to (and the little icons at the bottom), but you keep resorting to KDE, Gnome and XFCE proggies as well as good old SH to get things done a lot of times. This obviously defeats the point of having it so light. 

This brings me to:
Fluxbox: Now flux is EXTREMELY light (not the lightest but close). It is extremely fast, but obviously most of the things you want to do with it will be KDE, Gnome etc. That said, it flies - I mean FLIES - through things. Synaptic is faster, websites render faster, etc. However, simple things like ALSA etc. take a good deal of fiddling and everything is a hodge-podge of gnome, kde, etc proggies which may look untidy. However, it has a pretty steep learning curve. I use it in my HD recording studio as it's so light but I would probably use Gnome or XFCE (Xubuntu) if I was doing general boring officer stuff or graphics/video stuff really.

Don't know if this helps any!

### Post by Richard Kut on 2007-02-15
Hello everyone. I have run several window managers on the following hardware:

Desktop PC, Pentium II 333 MHz with 768 MB RAM
Laptop, Pentium 4 3.2 GHz HT with 640 MB RAM

In my experience, here are the window managers in order going from fastest to slowest:

Openbox (with metacity disabled)
Xubuntu
Ubuntu
Kubuntu

I hope that this helps someone to make a choice. Note also that if you have 128 MB RAM or less, that you probably do not want to attempt running Ubuntu or Kubuntu, and in fact it may not even be possible. In that case you may also want to look at Fluxbuntu or Ubuntu Lite.
