### Post by filterpipe on 2008-03-12
**_Warning #1: Be Very, Very Careful With This Advice; You Could Damage Your Laptop!_**

Hi,

  Perhaps my situation was a tiny bit similar to yours. In my case, I have a Toshiba
Satellite A100-SK4 laptop with an LCD display of 1024x800 pixels, and I also have a
ViewSonic VA912b external monitor of 1280x1024 pixels. I couldn't get the external
monitor to show anything other than 1280x800 pixels, however. Here are the steps
that I took, but **_Warning #2: I caution you to be careful!!!!_**

[LIST=1]
[*]Download and compile gtf.c from [http://gtf.sourceforge.net/](http://gtf.sourceforge.net/)
[*]Run "gtf -h" for help in running the command.
[*]Run gtf with your particulars to get a modeline.
[*]Run "xrandr --output LVDS --newmode ..." supplying the information from the previous line to create a new mode for the physical display.
[*]Run "xrandr --addmode LVDS ..." with the name of the new mode just created.
[*]Run "xrandr --output LVDS --mode ..." with the name of the mode just created.
[/LIST]

  Instead of "LVDS", of course, I used "VGA" throughout, because I was trying to
adjust my external monitor.

  Good luck, and, **_Warning #3: again, be very careful!!!_**
Setting a mode incorrectly can damage a display. You have been warned for the third time.

Cheers...
