### Post by risbac on 2006-07-20
If you have a broadband connection, you can just change your repos source list to specify Dapper everywhere instead of Breezy; Then you update the system and woooooo, one hour later (or a bit more), you have a Dapper!

It can work with the CD of course, but if you installed more software from the internet repos, they won't be able to update themselves. Could be a problem. You can maybe try to put the CD, Ubuntu may recognize it's a Dapper CD and propose to update. I don't know if it's working, you can try.

Other solution, you backup your linux documents, and you format/install on the linux partition from the CD. This way you have a very "fresh" Ubuntu, but you need to reinstall and reconfigure the softs...
