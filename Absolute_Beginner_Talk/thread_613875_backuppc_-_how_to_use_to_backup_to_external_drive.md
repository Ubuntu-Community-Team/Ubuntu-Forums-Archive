### Post by Inxsible on 2007-11-15
I have never used backuppc. but sbackup is a pretty decent utility and you can also schedule backups on it.

[http://sourceforge.net/projects/sbackup/](http://sourceforge.net/projects/sbackup/)

You can also use rsync (which btw is installed by default)

Have a look [here]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/backup")

If you want a bit by bit image of your drive then you may want to use [partimage]("http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/partimage")
