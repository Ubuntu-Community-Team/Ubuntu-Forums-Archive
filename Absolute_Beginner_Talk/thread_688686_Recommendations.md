### Post by Nythain on 2008-02-05
by "special effects" im assuming you mean the "desktop effects" and other composite managers like compiz-fusion and xcompmgr...
thats really up to you...
if your machine can handle it without taking a performance hit, and you dont mind going through trying to get it goin, then sure, why not...
though i will point out at this time that most composite managers are still technically "beta" and though pretty much stable, can be buggy at times... so you cant get frustrated at random freezes or crashes (and by random it might never happen to you)

i personally dont run any, i find other ways to make my desktop pleasing, like themes, icons, fonts, wallpaper, exciting approaches to things like transparent terminals and the such...

but in the end, its all up to you
