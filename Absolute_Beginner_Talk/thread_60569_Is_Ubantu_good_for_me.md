### Post by evilghost on 2005-08-28
As FYI, it's "Ubuntu" not "Ubantu" :)

My wife has Ubuntu 5.04 installed on her Compaq M700 laptop.  It's a P3-700Mhz machine with a 10GB harddrive.  I installed 512MB of RAM in there and the laptop screams.

Your biggest issue is going to be the lack of RAM.  128MB really just doesn't cut it and you're going to be swapping alot, which will slow things down.

Pick up some SODIMMs for that laptop, they're real cheap, and up it to 512MB and it will scream.

### Post by aysiu on 2005-08-29
I definitely encourage you to explore Damn Small Linux. It's a fun little distro. But if Ubuntu's Gnome is working at a slow but reasonable speed, Ubuntu's XFCE will definitely fly. Seriously, just install XFCE through Synaptic Package Manager. Then, log out. Pick "Session" and "XFCE." When it asks if you want to make XFCE the default session, say "yes." You won't regret it.
