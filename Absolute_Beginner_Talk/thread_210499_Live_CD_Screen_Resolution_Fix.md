### Post by Jax Kovak on 2006-07-07
Hi there,

Iv been using the live CD of Dapper and have had some problems because Ubuntu does not recognise and/or setup my NVIDIA GFX card properly, leaving me with a screen resolution of 640x480 and no readily apparent way to change that. This even prevents the installation window being readable, effectively making the Live CD little more than a coaster.

Lots of people have suggested ways around this and Iv tried lots of methods to no avail, however, I stumbled across a way around it by sheer dumb luck.

[LIST]
[*]Start from Live CD
[*]Set Keymap AND VGA options from F-Key options
[*]Use 'Start in Safe Graphics mode' (See below)
[/LIST]

When booting the CD I used the F-Keys to set my keymap and the VGA option to set my screen resolution to something a little more respectable, just as I usually do, but instead of then selecting the top item in the boot menu (Run Live CD or Install) I accidentally selected to start Ubuntu in Safe Graphics mode.

Incredibly this worked fine, and although I didn't have the correct driver for my GFX card, I did have a usable screen resolution!

I hope this helps some people who may be suffering from the same problem as I was!

Jax
