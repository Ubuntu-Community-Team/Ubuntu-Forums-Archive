### Post by cotcot on 2006-02-11
Checksum the LiveCd ([http://www.irnis.net/gloss/md5sum-windows.shtml](http://www.irnis.net/gloss/md5sum-windows.shtml)) to see if this LiveCD does not have errors.  You can also check the CD drive with other bootable CD's. 

I suppose that your CD drive works fine for other CD's. If you have 2 CD/DVD drives try the other one.  BIOS uses 1 of them. If you pop in the CD in the one that BIOS does not look for to boot it goes to the next one which is obviously your hard drive with XP.

### Post by Bartender on 2006-02-11
slowpoke -
The download will be one monolithic file.  The LiveCD will have several files on it.  Well, this is interesting...I was gonna paste in some results but can't.  Tossed one of my LiveCD's in the tray, and could hear the drive looking at it.  The PC was stuck with the hourglass running for a minute or two, then became responsive again.  When I click on the drive, it just says, "Please insert disc".

My main W2K PC (the one in sig is my Linux experiment) was unable to run the LiveCD when I set the BIOS up to boot from the DVD drive.  Sounds just like your situation at first glance.  I'd never tried to explore the CD *after* the PC was already running.  If you can open/explore the CD, then your problem is different.

Dial-up...warning Will Robinson Danger Danger.  Have you been warned about dial-up hurdles?  If you are one of the few people with a full hardware-based modem you should be OK.  Most modems are going to argue with you.  In some cases you can work thru the problems.  I'm buying two or three hardware-based (Serial connection, not USB) externals and going from there.  Yes, they're ugly but the external gives me the freedom to experiment with other computers, bring one over to someone's house, etc.

Let's say you can get Ubuntu installed but Internet is unreachable.  Can you get your computer to a broadband connection at school or work or a friend's house?  I jacked my machine in to the network at my workplace and was online no problemo.  It was a gas to play with Synaptic, follow the steps to install Automatix, grab a few different wallpapers, etc.  If you can do this it will be a great morale-booster to keep you going while you work thru getting dial-up to work.  You wouldn't want to install Automatix via dial-up anyway.  Too much data.
