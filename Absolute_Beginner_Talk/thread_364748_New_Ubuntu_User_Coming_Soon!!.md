### Post by Tomosaur on 2007-02-18
There are hundreds of differences between Windows and any Linux OS. The great thing about Ubuntu (and many other Linux distrobutions) is that you can download a LiveCD and boot from it without changing anything installed on your hard drive. It should give you a taste of what Ubuntu is like, and will also give you a good indication of how well your hardware works under Ubuntu. Most people find everything works with no extra setup, although things like graphics cards may need to be tweaked after you install to get the maximum benefit.

Be warned though, that running from the LiveCD is very slow, and as such, is no indication of the speed of an installed Ubuntu, which is generally extremely quick, and in most cases, faster than any version of Windows.
