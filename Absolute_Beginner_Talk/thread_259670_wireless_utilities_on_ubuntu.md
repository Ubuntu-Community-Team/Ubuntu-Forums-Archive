### Post by gn2 on 2006-09-17
Top right of your screen there should be a Network Connection icon, click on it.

If it isn't there, right click on the top panel then click "addto panel" scroll down to "systen and hardware" then drag and drop "Network Monitor" into the top panel.

### Post by karamba_kid on 2006-09-17
I prefer using the command line tool iwconfig.  Which gives you more helpful information than a signal strength bar can.  Remember to enable wpa on your wireless network for security and configure your wifi card in linux with wpa_supplicant if you have a supported chipset.
