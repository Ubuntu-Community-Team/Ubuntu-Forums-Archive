### Post by mjwhitfield on 2007-06-16
If i can throw in my own perspective for you Kerry.

I've been using Windows since 3.1 and before that it was things like my C16 and C64.  I'm not really the prime type of person to swap to Linux, I know windows inside out and I've never had problems with it that I couldn't fix.  I started a new job 3 months ago which involves administrating Ubuntu desktops that our developers at work are using, as well as setting up servers with Gentoo. Before this job my experience of Linux was limited to a couple of installs on olds PCs before I binned them.

Linux is a big change from Windows, and when I first started using it I felt very lost.  I was lucky that the other guys I work with were willing to spend a lot of time helping me do things which under windows would have taken me 30 mins, but under Linux took me half a day.

After 3 months, looking back on the things I struggled with at the start now seem trivial.

Give Ubuntu a go, everyone here is willing to help.  You'll more than likely have problems even with simple stuff, but a few months down the line you'll know the basics, and even some of the complex stuff.  It doesn't sound like you want to do anything too tough.  After a quick 30 min install and a few questions posted on these forums you'll be racing a long with way fewer problems than you had with Windows.

I hope this helps, and I haven't rambled.
