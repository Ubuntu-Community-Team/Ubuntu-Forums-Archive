Ubuntu, Linux and most of the included packages are lisenced under the GPL v2 or similar lisences.  For more information, the wikipedia [article is very informative]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gpl").  This makes it pretty tricky to modify Ubuntu and sell it on, although it is theoretically possible (so long as you allow others to pass around, use and modify your distribution for free).  Essentially, you must give others the same privelages that allow you to modify Ubuntu.  Therefore you cannot copyright something derrived from Ubuntu but you can make a proffit from it.  You can even charge a **reasonable fee** (normally about 10 $US) for simply passing round copies of the Ubuntu CD.

If your just looking to play around with Ubuntu for fun, you'll find there's plenty you can do without actually making your own distributions.  For example, you can replace all images and text with those of your chosing and can completely change the appearance of pretty much every aspect - you can make it look like OS X, XP (if you so wish) or something else entirely.

You can also add and remove individual packages - the components that make up your operating system.  There are atleast five different packages providing similar functions that you can swap in and out, effectively creating your own distribution (or atleast your own branch of Ubuntu).  You could relatively easily create your own meta-package, which would allow someone else who had just installed Ubuntu to effectively convert it into your customised install, and you can use scripts to swap images and themes in and out.

However, actually making your own distribution with a Live CD and install CD is no easy feet, especially if you wish to do anything more than play around with it's surface appearance.
