### Post by puterfixer on 2005-09-17
Right-click in an empty space on one of the panels, for instance on the one showing the clock at the right. From the pop-up menu, select "Add to panel...". A list of items will show up; scroll down to "Keyboard Indicator", click on it, then click the "Add" button. The keyboard indicator should appear on the panel where you right-clicked.

To move the keyboard indicator around, right-click on it, select "Move" from the menu, move the mouse cursor until it reaches your favorite spot, click to make it stay there. To lock its position, right-click on it and click "Lock to Panel" from the pop-up menu.

Other items already on panels, like the volume control, could be already locked in position, so you can't move the keyboard indicator past locked items. You can right-click other items in the panel and click "Lock to Panel" to remove the checkmark and be able to move them around. This way you can change the order in which the items will show up on the panel.

I hope this explanation helps; I'm new to Ubuntu, but loving every minute of it :)
