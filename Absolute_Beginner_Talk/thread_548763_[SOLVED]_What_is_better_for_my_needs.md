### Post by w4ett on 2007-09-11
Why not (using your current system), install an additional HDD.......unplug your current drives and install Ubuntu as a stand alone O/S....then plug your original drives back in and when booting, select the Ubuntu drive to boot by using your boot device select toggle (F11 on my system) when booting computer.

It works like a dream, does not entail modifying your Windows MBR or partitions...and grub will only be controlling the boot for the Ubuntu kernels...not Windows
