### Post by lamego on 2006-07-05
The tip about using your country mirror is the most logical but not always the best. It is based on the assumption that your ISP has better connectivity with the mirrors geographically closer to you and assumes all those mirrors are reliable.
You can try any mirror and just select the one the seems faster to you. 
Anyway have you thought that the network problem maybe at your home/ISP connection ? I have downloaded a lot of .ISOs on the last years from several mirrors around the world. It was very rare to get corrupted images.
