### Post by p_quarles on 2007-07-07
Santa Rosa is the latest Core 2 Duo notebook chip, right? 

Power management is not great in Linux (yet), but definitely some features of the newer chips will work. My experience is limited to a Core (1) Duo 1.85MHz chip, but I think even that runs pretty nicely under Ubuntu. The resting temp is 22-27 centigrade, and the chip is automatically underclocked unless it's doing something really heavy. 

I get about two and a half hours out of a fully charged 6-cell battery. From what I hear, that's good for Linux, but this is my first laptop, so I don't know how it compares to others.

Finally, I'd recommend doing some research on the security vulnerabilities in the latest Intel chips before you decide. I've read some worrisome things on Slashdot.
