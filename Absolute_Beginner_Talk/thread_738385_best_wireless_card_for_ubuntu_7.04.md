### Post by coolbrook on 2008-03-28
Determine the location you'll buy from and the price you're willing to pay and then refer to the compatibilty resource [here]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported")

If you want inexpensive and functional then you'll consider installing ndiswrapper and using the card's Windows drivers.  I paid 14.99 after mail-in rebate and it was virtually effortless to get the TrendNET TEW-423PI working on my PC.  My Netgear WG311T works out of the box.
