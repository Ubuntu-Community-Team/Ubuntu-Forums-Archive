### Post by harveysburger on 2008-02-20
I just installed linux for the first time in 8 years... wow what a difference! back then when I was at university I played with few distributions of redhat, debian, slackware, and one i think that was made by corel... or some company like that... I hated it, well i liked the challenge of working getting everything working piece by piece.. spending hours to get my modem to work, etc... but really the overall experience was poor. 

however I see things have changed! I decided to give another try to linux after few weeks of dealing with Vista... Vista is unbelievably slow... I just spent big money on a new laptop and its running slower then my 3 years old dell laptop that has XP Pro on it... There are few neat things with Vista I must admit but overall its too slow and expensive... I miss XP... if you wonder why I went with Vista... well I am a developer and mostly work with .NET these days so I wanted to get used to Vista since the OS isn't going anywhere, Microsoft will shovel it down everybody's throat till they choke... 

Anyway all that said to get back to Ubuntu topic, as I am writing this post I am installing Ubuntu. This live CD thing is really cool :) internet works, my sound works, I can access the  files on my NTFS... all my favorite hotkeys that i use on windows are recognized, everything rocks so far!

since I am already an openoffice / firefox / thunderbird user the transition to make linux my OS of choice outside of work should go well :)

well I just felt like sharing my story!


have a good day everyone!
