### Post by steve.horsley on 2006-08-20
Don't take these forums as an indication that Ubuntu is an unreliable OS. It is one of those things that the people who post here are those who need help with problems, so if you look here, that's what you will see - people with problems. That's true of any forum for any product.

As for the AMD64 processor, the problem is that some proprietary software that you might want isn't available for 64 bit yet - e.g. some audio and video codecs, flash player etc. So you may prefer to go with the 32-bit Ubuntu for now, until you are ready to wrestle with getting 42-bit plugins to work on 64-bit applications. Actually, this is what I do - 32-bit Ubuntu on Athlon64.

A couple of references for you:
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Dapper](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Dapper)
[http://www.tldp.org/](http://www.tldp.org/)

Good luck. You can always ask here, or just lurk and pick up a lot.
