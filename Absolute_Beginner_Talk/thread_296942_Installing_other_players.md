### Post by CatKiller on 2006-11-10
Are you trying to install Windows software on something that isn't Windows? It's not particularly clear from a glance at that site what it is that you are trying to do.

There is something called [Wine]("http://www.winehq.com/") that can help to run Windows programs on Linux by emulating some of the Windows environment for the program. Your program may work in that, but it probably won't - most things don't, and the ones that do are the popular ones.

The best bet would be to either get a Linux client from the people that made the software - or enough specifications to let someone else do it for you - or to find a different solution.
