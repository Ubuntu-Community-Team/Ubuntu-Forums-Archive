### Post by nvteighen on 2007-05-21
1) Welcome!

2) Download the Live CD and give it a try. It won't touch your hard drive and will give you the chance to test your hardware in Ubuntu before installing it. Follow this guide: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/iso)

### Post by ThinkBuntu on 2007-05-21
If you trust Ubuntu's new utility, it should migrate all your old files (My Documents, etc.) to a new install. But you're taking a risk by relying on this, so I'd back them up on CDs, a DVD, or an iPod before installing anything. You'll be able to set up internet pretty easily, especially if you have a wireless driver handy ("Ndiswrapper" can make windows drivers work in Linux).

I recommend that you use Linux Mint. It doesn't have the account migration tool, but it's installed with an ndiswrapper utility and multimedia codecs so you'll be able to play all your music before ever connecting to the internet.

Either way, feel free to ask more detailed questions. We're here to help!
