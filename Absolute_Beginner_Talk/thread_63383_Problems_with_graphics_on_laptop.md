### Post by mlomker on 2005-09-07
You could temporarily change  Driver "ati" to  Driver "vesa", but it might reduce your resolution and be slower (useful as a test, though).  My previous laptop had the same video card, but I didn't run Ubuntu on it...also didn't have any video problems when running Linspire.  Linspire and Xandros have some downsides but *everything* was autodetected and worked out of the box.  

There are a lot of pages on Google that you could sort through...it's a very common card.  [Look here](http://rzr.online.fr/docs/comp/gfxcard.htm#3d) 

There are quite a few FAQ's on troubleshooting sound problems...do a search on here or Google.  Some folks are under the false impression that linux is easy to use, like Windows. :P
