### Post by Starks on 2007-03-06
Prior to Ubuntu, my experience with Linux was nearly non-existent. Other than a few 15 minute stints with Knoppix and Red Hat, I had never used Linux before. Now, as I been trying out Dapper for the past week or so, I've grown strangely attached to my new secondary OS (Windows XP is my primary and I intend it to stay that way for quite a while).

I've found Ubuntu to be very straight forward when it chooses to cooperate with me. It's a bit frustrating to get everything to work just right, but it's also a little fun. I'm learning a lot about the inner workings of operating systems and just how much control Linux gives you over it. The trial-and-error part of Linux is what keeps me attracted to it. The question running through mind is: "If I do X, can I elicit Y behavior?"

There's just so much to try, so many possibilities to test.
