### Post by yuku-aki on 2007-09-23
Yeah, as a newbie, this is one of the areas that's relatively painless in installation.  Nothing is written in stone until you fully accept, at the end of all selections, to partition, format, or install to the hard drive.  So go ahead and pop the drive and disc in, and once you get to the portion that asks you about partitions, select a Manual edit of the partition table (again, WAY easier than it sounds) and you'll see a neat little graphical representation of your hard drives, their types and contents.  You can always post the results or more q's here if you're not sure of the settings that are available.
