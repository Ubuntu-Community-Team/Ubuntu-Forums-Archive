### Post by batholith on 2006-07-06
Does the system have a new monitor or video card?  I had this problem come up on a new system where I got the "out of range" error.

I had bought a real cheap video card and basically the frequency range on the monitor was lower than the frequency range on the video card (it's an old monitor). They were out of sync.  I fixed it by spending a few more $$ on a better video card and the error went away...

One thing is for sure, it's a hardware issue.  If you have an extra PC laying around try switching out some of the hardware and see if it works...

Good luck.
