### Post by p_quarles on 2007-08-16
Music from the iTunes store can be burned to a CD, and it will lose its DRM. While this is not illegal (Apple gives detailed instructions on how to do it), it is of course illegal to distribute it to anyone.

After it's burned to a CD, you can re-rip it to whatever audio format you like. Not a very cost-effective solution if you have lots of iTunes-purchased songs, but it's the only way I know of.

"Like most (if not all) MP3 encoders, LAME may contain technology covered by patents owned by the Fraunhofer Society.[2] The developers of LAME do not currently license the technology described by these patents from Fraunhofer.
