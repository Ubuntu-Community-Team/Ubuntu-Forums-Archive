### Post by Sunforge on 2007-07-08
If you want to buy a couple of books, because like me, you like having a book or two around then there are two books specific to ubuntu that I've bought and rate:

Beginning Ubuntu - a readable beginners guide:

[http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Second-Novice-Professional/dp/1590598202/ref=pd_bbs_sr_5/105-1177286-2675628?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1183909612&sr=8-5](http://www.amazon.com/Beginning-Ubuntu-Second-Novice-Professional/dp/1590598202/ref=pd_bbs_sr_5/105-1177286-2675628?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1183909612&sr=8-5)
 
And Ubuntu Hacks:

[http://www.amazon.com/Ubuntu-Hacks-Tools-Exploring-Tuning/dp/0596527209/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/105-1177286-2675628?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1183909722&sr=1-1](http://www.amazon.com/Ubuntu-Hacks-Tools-Exploring-Tuning/dp/0596527209/ref=pd_bbs_sr_1/105-1177286-2675628?ie=UTF8&s=books&qid=1183909722&sr=1-1)
 
Apart from that there are the Ubuntu web guides which are pretty decent on specific issues:

The FAQ:
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu:Feisty)

and the other FAQ:
[https://help.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/desktopguide/C/index.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/desktopguide/C/index.html)
 
Of course there is also the Ubuntu forum when you really get stuck.
