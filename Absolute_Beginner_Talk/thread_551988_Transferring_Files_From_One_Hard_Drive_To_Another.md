### Post by KrisWillis on 2007-09-16
By default, you should be able to mount and read an NTFS formatted disk. In the GUI it would just be a case of **Places -> Computer** and double-clicking on the correct drive, it will be called something like *149.0 GB Volume* - The disk should then be mounted and browsable. Then it's a case of cut/copy and pasting from that point.

Are both hard drives inside your computer right now?? If so, you can actually mount the harddrive, and then do a simple drag and drop. Otherwise, it may be best to put your old hard drive in the computer and either burn a data cd/dvd or use a flash drive. The other option which could be more useful in the future, but more expensive now would be to buy an external hard drive enclosure, and then you could plug your hard drive in via USB. Hope this helps!!
