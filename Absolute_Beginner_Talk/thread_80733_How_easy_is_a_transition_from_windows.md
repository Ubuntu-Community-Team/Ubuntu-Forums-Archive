### Post by rsay on 2005-10-22
I installed hoary for a non-sophisticated neighbor in a similar situation 6 months ago. I pleaded with them to try it for a month and then I promised to re-install windows. They were willing because they didn't want to pay Best Buy $200 to fix the windows installation.  After the installation I braced myself for a series of phone calls the next day. They didn't come! About a week after the installation  they asked me to review the directory structure again. A week after that they asked me to help set up their lexmark printer, which I couldn't do. I convinced them to go and get an hp instead. They installed it on their own without help. During one upgrade, the nvidia drivers didn't get automatically updated with the kernel so X didn't start. I had just gone through the fix on all of my computers so I ran over, asked them to log in and re-installed linux-restricted modules and they have been good ever since. In a month or so I will upgrade them to breezy. They have been disappointed to find out that a few of their old games won't run, but their top priority was for consistency and stability. They have never owned a computer that ran so consistently for six months.

I have told my other friends that I won't help them with windows problems anymore. I explain to them that it is just a pointless stop-gap measure until their next catastrophic system failure. 

I would still be concerned about trying to convert my friends who are power windows users though. They have a lot more to unlearn.

No more blue screens of death in the middle of an almost finished, very long and important essay... 
If you like to fiddle around with the computer, go for Linux. If you want it "ready- to- go", for your own sake, stay away. This is what I've learned.
