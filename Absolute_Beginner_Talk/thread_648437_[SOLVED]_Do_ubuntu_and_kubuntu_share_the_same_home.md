### Post by seanc7 on 2007-12-23
Yes they do. A users /home folder is not related to the desktop they're using. Gnome has it's own hidden folders and files in your /home folder, same with KDE.

### Post by GGLucas on 2007-12-23
Firstly, you'd apt-get the package kubuntu-desktop, doing that enables you to choose to log in to KDE at the login screen by clicking "Session".
Secondly, assuming you log in as the same user, yes, KDE and Gnome will share the same home directory, and petty much everything else too, they're just different desktop environments.
