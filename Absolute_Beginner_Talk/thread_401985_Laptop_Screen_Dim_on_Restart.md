### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-04-05
well sometimes this happens with my really ancient Hitachi Laptop but all I have to do is hit the brightness key (normally laptops this is done by holding the Fn or function key and try either the pgup or pgdown and or the up or down arrow or a similar combination) so that it gets a little brighter. It's has only happened to me a few times out of a year that the brightness key (only the up one) wouldnt work, I could make it dimmer but I couldn't make it brighter, and only a restart would solve that. Not sure why it happens but take a look here ([http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work](http://www.thinkwiki.org/wiki/How_to_get_special_keys_to_work)) or here ([http://linux.spiney.org/debian_gnu_linux_on_an_ibm_thinkpad_t43p_special_keys](http://linux.spiney.org/debian_gnu_linux_on_an_ibm_thinkpad_t43p_special_keys))

if you don't have brightness keys on your laptop, check this out. [http://savannah.nongnu.org/projects/tpb/](http://savannah.nongnu.org/projects/tpb/)

or maybe check this out. [http://tpctl.sourceforge.net/tpctlhome.htm](http://tpctl.sourceforge.net/tpctlhome.htm)

here is a guy ([http://www.ferner-online.de/thinkpad_r32.html](http://www.ferner-online.de/thinkpad_r32.html)) who has a R32 and he says that him the Fn-key seems to work just fine, the thinklight can be switched on and off, brightness control, button for suspend, turning the display off and on are all working... he is using Debian/GNU Linux 3.0.

good luck
