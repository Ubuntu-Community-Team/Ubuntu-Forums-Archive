### Post by 23meg on 2006-08-17
Lossy compression means some data is thrown away in the compression process and quality of the content is somehow reduced while decreasing file size. Lossless means the opposite.> Should I be saving music files as Ogg or FLAC?Entirely up to your requirements. FLAC is lossless, OGG is lossy. Use FLAC if you have to preserve the data without compromise. Use OGG if you won't be able to practically tell the difference on your sound equipment (this is very often the case).

### Post by msak007 on 2006-08-17
I know the question was already answered, but unless you *really* want your MP3s in OGG format, I wouldn't suggest converting them. Going from one lossy format (MP3) to another (OGG) will degrade the audio quality even more. If they are MP3s you encoded yourself from an original CD, just re-encode them again using OGG. On the other hand, you're really not going to gain anything by converting MP3 (or OGG) to FLAC, as data was already discarded when the songs were encoded in the lossy format to begin with. Once again, the best thing to do is to re-encode the files from an original CD.
