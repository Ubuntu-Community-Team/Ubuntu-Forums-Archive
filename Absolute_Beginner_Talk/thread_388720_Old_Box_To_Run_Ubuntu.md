### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-03-20
Your system does have a sufficient amount of ram to run a full featured desktop.  For your particular system and to help keep your sanity since you are relatively new to Linux, I would recommend just installing Ubuntu on your machine.  You may also want to take a look at a window manager (desktop environment) called Enlightenment E17.  Enlightenment E17 is very easy to install and will run ***much faster*** than Gnome and even XFCE on Xubuntu.  You can find installation instructions for installing E17 on Ubuntu at this link.

[How To Install Enlightenment E17 Using The Elbuntu Repositories]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=319336&highlight=e17")

Also don't be afraid to explore other Linux distribution possibilities such as Vector Linux and Zenwalk.  Vector Linux and Zenwalk are both optimized for older pc's such as yours.  Back in October I installed Zenwalk on a very old pc that my sister has, a PII 500 that has 256mb of ram, and it ran very nice.  I also ran Vector Linux for a while 2 years ago and it also runs very fast.  Vector Linux also has the added plus of installing all proprietary media codecs for you, so that you can listen to mp3's and watch videos in Mozilla Firefox, right out of the box after installation.   

You can take a look at Vector Linux and Zenwalk at the following links.

[http://vectorlinux.com/](http://vectorlinux.com/)

[http://www.zenwalk.org/](http://www.zenwalk.org/)
