### Post by PurplePenguin on 2007-07-08
Hey, if you're building a machine for games, you've got to have a couple hundred gigs or more, right?  I'm sure you could spare 10 gig or so for Ubuntu.  Although if you're going to start playing with partitions, I'd suggest you do it before you get anything really valuable on your drive just in case you accidently choose the wrong method of partitioning (that is, wipe the whole drive)... come to think of it, now's the best time to add a little partition. :D

Actually, there's something else that's pretty cool that you might want to try.  There's a distro called cl33n that I recommend for Windows users.  It's a live cd that you pop into the drive of your Windows machine and reboot.  When it loads, it just comes up with a Firefox window.  It gives you save browsing - no viruses or anything to worry about. :D
