### Post by seijuro on 2006-12-16
If what you have has all the features you want and everything runs stable then I wouldn't see a point to upgrading. However, if some things you want are missing or certain things are still broken inspite of your best efforts then you sould probably upgrade. In general "if it ain't broke don't 'fix' it" applies to most people. Also taking into consideration the other side of the coin if you love to technology like to tweak things and prefer the newest versions of software then upgrading goes w/o question.
