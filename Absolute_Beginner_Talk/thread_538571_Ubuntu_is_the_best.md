### Post by Jaxilian on 2007-08-30
I just wanted to say what I think about Ubuntu. I been through a few distros now, testing them and see if they can fulfill my needs and replace Windows XP/Vista. I am a bit of a newbie still at linux after my 2 years of messing around with it so please be patient. This is my hardware. If youre curious on linux, do as i did and try different ones. I found that Ubuntu works best for my laptop.

My comp: Zepto Znote 2415W, 1.86Ghz Centrino, 2Gb DDR2 RAM, ATi x600 mobility

**Fedora Core 7**
+looks flashy
-Don't detect any critical hardware like network card, wireless or Graphic card.

Do I have to say more than I quickly went on to another distro...hehe


**Linspire 6** 
+ Good hardware detection
- Halfdone impression, They have troubles with their packet handler (CNR). Poor, messy support forum.

Installation went ok. It was easy install. I was kinda disappointed that I didn't get the CNR as they promised on their website. On my laptop, most hardware were found and it worked kinda ok. USB devices were recognized. I still got a distinct feeling a huge part was missing, namely CNR. The feeling I got from Linspire was sorta a halfdone OS, not really thought through. maybe this OS has more to give, I didn't stay long enough to wanna try my patience

I don't recommend this one.


**Suse Linux Enterprise Desktop 10.1 SP1**
+ Stability at it's best. Good hardware detection
- ACPI  (sleep, suspend) didn't work so good on my laptop. Didn't wanna wake up after sleep....lazy bugger :). Very slow packet handler.

Installation went smooth and it also detected all my hardware, even my TV-tuner. USB devices were recognized easy when put in. 3D acceleration was easy to get going. Installing programs was though a real pain. ZMD is a very slow installer. I uninstalled it and replaced it with SMART and then things were good. I got XGL desktop effects to work with a breeze. My SD card reader didn't work though, but I don't use it so much.

I can warmly recommend people to try SLED (Suse Linux Enterprise Desktop) It though it has some troubles, it's very stable. OpenSuse can be a bit more trickier though so make sure you pick right one.

**Ubuntu 7.04**
+Very flexible, Good hardware detection, ACPI (sleep, suspend) works. SD card reader works.
- Tricky to configure at some points.

Installation is easy, but getting the 3D acceleration to work isn't so easy, esspecially if you have an ATi card like me. After getting the right Wiki, I got that to work. The rest is a lot of apt-get to get what you want basically. Ubuntu is a fun OS to mess with. The possiblilities is endless. I decided to stay with Ubuntu

I recommend Ubuntu for sure. I can even get World of Warcraft going on this one with Wine. Úbuntu has THE best support forum I have seen.
