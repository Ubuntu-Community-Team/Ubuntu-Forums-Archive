### Post by yakkeh on 2007-07-13
Probably a new user and needs to know about synaptic package manager, apt and stuff like that. If you scroll through the menu in Gnome or KDE, you'll see stuff like Add/Remove programs and Synaptic Package Manager. Make sure you enable the universe and if you still don't see the software that you're looking for, digg on google to find some info about /etc/apt/sources.list!!

So you say you can't find anything "compatible with ubuntu" ... I assume you haven't tried these software management tools. It's as simply as a click or 2 and the programs are automatically downloaded, installed and configured for you. How much easier do you want it?


First of all, if you say you don't know what to do with it.... ask yourself, what do you expect from a computer? Different people do different things!

You want to browse the internet? Firefox comes with the standard installation of Ubuntu, Evolution (comparable with MS Outlook) or Thunderbird to read mail.

You want to use Word Processing or Spreadsheets? There's OpenOffice Document Writer comparable to MS Word and OpenOffice Calc which is comparable to MS Excel.

Do you want to create web sites? There are tools like Screem to edit your code.
Do you want to share files on your network, you need Samba installed or Apache2 for a web server.

See... there is SO MUCH you can do with Ubuntu (so much more than with Windows!), but you should spend some time familiarizing yourself with the Linux environment. There are plenty tutorials, howtos and other documentation that explains you step by step how to do things with ubuntu.

Like people here already said, be a bit specific and tell people what exactly your problem is and you'll get GOOD help (unlike MS again).
Don't ask people what you can do with Ubuntu, the sky is the limit... ask yourself what you want Ubuntu to do for you. Once you figured that out, look up or ask how exactly you do whatever it is you want.
