### Post by JeffAlbertson on 2007-03-10
Hey all.

No questions for you yet.  I just thought I'd drop a line to let you know what a full-fledged n00b thinks of the 6.10 distro.  I didn't see an "Introduce Yourself" thread, so here I am.  And here are my impressions:

First, I had trouble getting my ISO burn to boot.  So, I ordered the free disks.  All I can say is "wow".  A free OS.  Delivered right to my door. I'll be making a donation this pay period.

Second, I ran the 'live' disk just to check it out.  I swear, from reboot to surfing the 'net took less than one minute.  Wow again.  I now have my PC set up in dual boot mode, for now (XP & Ubuntu).

Third, maybe I got lucky, but everything works. Well, everything but my 'back' button on my MX1000 mouse.  My desktop resolution is also limited to 1024 X 768, but I've been lurking in the support forums, and I see fixes for that. Triple 'wow'.

Fourth, I'd like to remark on how clean and simple the GUI is.  Perfect.

So, all in all, I'd say you developers and all of you helping out here on the forums deserve a pat on the back, to say the least.

I've been building and supporting WinderBoxxen for about 12 years now, and this is like a breath of fresh air.

Many, many questions to follow.

### Post by tashmooclam on 2007-07-25
My first impressions: 
My DVD of 7.04 32-bit came very quickly via third party at amazon. Very nice.Well worth $1 plus shipping.
"Live" testing using the dvd was easy and network dsl connection was automatic, Nice.
Installing was easy. 
Now the impressions. The system identified my old Epson photo 960 printer, Nice.
The USB stick drive popped up on the desktop, a la Apple. Nice.
My "broken" sound in XP magically worked again. Nice.
Visting websites like google to download google earth was not nice. I didn't know what to do when the cardboard box icon appeared on my desktop.
SO, for downloading SOME applications, I use Automatix. Worked OK for Google Earth and Picasa and Skype.
Read here or someplace about using Swiftfox instead of the installed Firefox. Maybe Swiftfox is faster, but I could not stand to look at it too long. It really looks lousy.
Unfortunately, I don't like the looks of anything very much. My eyes are burning from trying to focus! :(
My rule now is I will obey Ubuntu and use the "Add Remove" under Applications.
My laptop trackpad seemed to be acting like it was on crack, so I went to System, Preferences, Mouse, Speed Tab. Under Acceleration, I slowed it down, and under Sensitivity, I made it lower. I am still adjusting.
I need to learn more, but the files I brought in via a USB drive were handled very strangely by F-Spot photo manager. Too bad. 
Starting up from Hibernation was unsuccessful the first time. 
The overall ugliness of the web and the fonts lack of sharpness in general are a disappointment. 
It's going to be OK, because this is some improvement over Windows in some respects.
The Apple experience is just superior, maybe not worth $1000 to some people.
BUT, I have no Windows on this machine, I am not going back!
