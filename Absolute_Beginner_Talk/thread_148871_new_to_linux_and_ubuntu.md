### Post by jimrz on 2006-03-22
Welcome,

1) using the "live" cd is an excellent idea and will give you an idea of what ubuntu is and how it will work with your hardware.

2) My laptop has Radeon 9600 which was recognized "out of the box" on install. Since I do not use the LT for gaming I have not taken the extra steps needed to get hardware acceleration working, but there are plenty of threads on this forum and elsewhere that can guide you through this successfully.

3) Should you like what you find with the "live" cd, [[COLOR="Sienna"]**_here_**[/COLOR]]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/") is an excellent guide to setting up an ubuntu / win dual boot. This includes explicit instruction for everything including resizing your win partition.

4) You really do not need to use the command line too often in ubuntu, but you may, like myself, find that as you go along you may grow to like it and the additional power / flexabilty it gives.
