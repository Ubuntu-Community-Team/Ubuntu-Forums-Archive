### Post by kragen on 2007-10-29
Sata is indeed faster than IDE, and yes your motherboard does need to have a SATA connection to work with SATA drives, which won't be found on older motherboards. There is also SATA 2 as well - so its worth checking to make sure that your motherboard and hard drive both support the same interface before you buy.

From [http://www.superwarehouse.com/NETVISTA_M41_DT_P4_1600_128MB_40GB_48X_ETH_W2K/679211U/ps/44108](http://www.superwarehouse.com/NETVISTA_M41_DT_P4_1600_128MB_40GB_48X_ETH_W2K/679211U/ps/44108), it looks like your motherboard doesn't support SATA or SATA 2 (that page doesnt contain the word "SATA")

As for whether or not its worth trying to get a SATA drive / motherboard, I cant really help. If you are buying a new motherboard, then I'm fairly certain that pretty much all new motherboards have SATA connections, but again its definitely worth checking first.

Drivers: I believe that Ubuntu has all the drivers you will need on the installation CD by default, however if you want to install windows xp you will need an additional SATA driver floppy disk (provided with the motherboard), and yes it must be a floppy disk, and hence your computer must have a floppy disk drive (I believe there is a workaround in which you create a modified windows xp cd with the drivers installed already, but its messy and I could never get it to work).
Windows vista on the other hand shouldnt need any additional drivers.

I hope this answers at least some of your questions! :)
