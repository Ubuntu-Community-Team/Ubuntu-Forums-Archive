### Post by Ian W on 2006-01-31
I work with Windows based P.Cs. I fix 'em, I improve them, I delete the sad stuff.
I teach people how to use them,  and I get well paid to help people understand Microsoft Office better.
For many years I have "tinkered" with Linux. Understood it's history, felt a need to switch from Windows. Looking for an alternative to suggest to clients and friends. 
And for years I have felt let down, upset that there really was no alternative to MS.
For years, these other Linux versions didn't install, didn't work, didn't find my hardware, my Internet connection, my graphics card, sound card etc, etc, etc. So I simply despaired and gave up. 
Today........I tried DistroWatch. Saw and read about Ubuntu. Downloaded it, burnt the iso, rebooted from the DVD drive where Ubuntu lurked and guess what guys......
IT WORKED. It just worked. I'm typing this whilst on the net with Ubuntu Linux. I've sent e mails telling people, I've played music, I've dabbled. But it all WORKS.
And I only clicked the download link 90 minutes ago.
Is Ubuntu impressive. Hell yes.
I have much new and powerfull equipment, great for me, not so great for most versions of Linux. But Ubuntu appears to have got it all correct so far. 
And even more impressive yet, it does actually dual boot with Windows XP, just like it says it will. 
Steep but hopefully fruiteful learning curve coming up now. If I can get to grips with Ubuntu, understand the terminology, talk to other "new boys", then maybe I can wean myself off other systems. I already suggest OpenOffice to Windows users. 
So thank you to everyone who has made Ubuntu what it is, your efforts are recognised, at least here. 
Good luck.:) 
Ian W
