### Post by laidback on 2007-02-05
Hi and welcome back.

In my view Ubuntu is the best distro to start with. I've tried several others and even spent a few months with Mandriva as my main system, but as soon as I got 6.06LTS Ubuntu running I haven't looked back. 
People seem to latch onto a distro because it suites them, which is what Linux is all about. I have had Fedora running but I didn't 'gel' with it. Others swear by it, I'm sure it's very good.
I prefer a distro that has easily obtainable books. This isn't true of Mandriva but is the case with Ubuntu and of course Fedora. 
The online support systems are important too. 
Since there are so many distros I was tempted to try lots. In retrospect this is a mistake for a newbie. I didn't get sufficiently involved with a distros that was installed just to give it a try. Once I become committed I made better progress. So the initial feel was very important otherwise I was likely to move on. I liked Ubuntu from the off, the screen, update system (synaptic), apps choice and of course the philosophy.
I have a copy of 6.10 running, but still use 6.06LTS as my main system, I like the idea of long term support and the reliability of a tested distro.
I went to the Linux World exhibition in London last November specifically to meet some of the Ubuntu people. Despite the fact that I didn't represent a company they gave me time and help. I was impressed and have continued to be happy with my choice.

### Post by jml on 2007-02-05
zetsumei, before installing Fedora, you may want to download the new Fedora Core Live CD.  (Ubuntu's cd's are both live and an install cd)  That way you can try Fedora without installing it first.  Just a suggestion.

Now for my opinion, I like Debian based distributions (of which Ubuntu is one,) better than RPM based distributions, (Fedora, openSUSE, Mandriva, etc.)  I found that Debian based distro's are easier to configure, give the user more control, and package management is much easier and quicker with either apt-get, or Synaptic.  Its also much easier adding 3rd party repositories, for things that are not installed by default, (proprietary codecs, video drivers, etc,) to Debian based distros.  Its my opinion that distros like Fedora, and openSUSE go out of their way to make that process more difficult than it needs to be.  Just my two cents worth.

Joe
