### Post by weel on 2007-06-17
You may have better luck going through an intermediate format. For instance, if Microsoft Outlook can export to Netscape mailbox format, and your Linux mail client of choice (Thunderbird, kmail, whatever) can read that format, then you're all set. I recall that at one point in the past I successfully got mail out of an old version of a Microsoft Exchange client this way, but I don't know which was the common lingua franca format I ended up using, so you may have to play around a little. Note that if your mail client is Thunderbird, you can use the windows version of Thunderbird to see if the transfer is working, so you won't have to boot into Linux.
