### Post by henriette_holm on 2007-08-29
The live cd is a perfect place to start although you have to expect quite a slow OS - because everything has to be loaded from the cd.

Dual boot is the smart thing to do when you're new to linux. This way you'll have a OS that you know and the OS that your learning side by side.

Nice places to start reading are:

[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Main_Page](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Main_Page)

[http://www.howtoforge.com/dual_boot_windows_xp_vista_ubuntu_feisty](http://www.howtoforge.com/dual_boot_windows_xp_vista_ubuntu_feisty) (notice there are three parts - read them all before you begin)

[https://help.ubuntu.com/](https://help.ubuntu.com/)

Feel free to ask more questions.

/Henriette

I hadn't really heard of Ubuntu until about 5 months ago. I was skeptical at first, but I like to keep an open mind. I always visualize linux in my head as a black screen and command-line, very dos-like...but much to my surprise, when I saw Ubuntu screenshots it blew me away! Now, I consider my self pretty tech savvy, but I'm not a programmer by any means. I know very basic C++. What I did intially, and it was pretty foolish on my part (even though I got lucky that my hardware worked perfectly), was I popped in the LiveCD and within 5 minutes I had wipe my harddrive clean and installed Ubuntu as my primary OS...no dual booting for me. My advice though, is to experiment heavily with the LiveCD. Test everything that you can...Printers, Wifi, USB devices...etc. If you find that something doesn't work correctly, do a little research...I'm almost 90% you'll find somebody who had the same problem and found a solution for that problem. These forums are essential to beginner usage of Ubuntu. Everything I know today about linux and Ubuntu comes from these forums. If you really don't have a need for Vista/Windows Operating Systems (gaming, itunes, photoshop)...and you feel that your computer is working well with Ubuntu on the LiveCD...then I wouldn't bother Dual-booting. Just a waste of space if you ask me.

In all honesty, you don't need to be a computer tech/junkie to really use Ubuntu. Just a little common sense, basic understanding of Linux in general, a little patience/time, and the willingness to let certain things you know about Windows go. Ubuntu won't ever be like Windows XP/Vista...it's better!

**Ubuntu=unlimted possibilities with customization, strong security, challenging/engaging, and great price ($0.00)!**
