### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-06-23
LOL

Turning off the ZIP drive in Windows will not help (as you see)

Boot the xubuntu CD (with the zip on)

Run gparted, it will give you a look at your hard drive and zip drive. You need to know the terminology for Linux partitioning.

See here : [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=282018](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=282018)

OK, now if you install from there you Zip drive should work as well.

If you get stuck, post a screen shot of gparted ;)

In terms of "off"

Look on your panel .... In the upper right hand corner is an icon ...

Or from the xfce menu choose "log off" (or log out ?) ... 

This will bring you to a second screen where you can log out, shutdown, or reboot.

See the box that says "save changes" ? If you leave this box checked off (on a hard drive install) next time you log in you will get you desktop as you left it, open apps and all :)

Welcome to Xubuntu :twisted:
