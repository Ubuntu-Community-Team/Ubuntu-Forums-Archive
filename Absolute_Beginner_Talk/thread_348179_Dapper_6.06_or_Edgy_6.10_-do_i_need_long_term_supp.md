### Post by mrdeeno on 2007-01-29
Personal testimonial...

I was on Dapper and had a hell of a time trying to get Beryl to work with both AIGLX or XGL on my Vaio laptop (with ATI Mobility 9000 r250).  I tried running both the open source graphics as well as the proprietary drivers (had to do this manually to get direct rendering), both worked fine except for implementing AIGLX or XGL.

I eventually got Beryl running ok on AIGLX with open source drivers (XGL just sucked), but woudl run into other problems such as crashes while logging out. and then read a post by someone saying that Dapper does not do beryl very well.

I installed Edgy and it detected my video card correctly with direct rendering set to on at installation, so all I had to do was install Beryl and get that running.  It's been working great ever since and I feel that Edgy is more stable while running beryl than Dapper would have been.

I guess the LTS is for people/companies that need the support and depend on it for day to day productiveness (people who probably wouldn't install Beryl).  I think the stability is a moot point between the 2 in my case, and it's more a question whether you need the long-term support or not.
