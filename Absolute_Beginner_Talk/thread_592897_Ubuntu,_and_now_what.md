You don't seem to understand. GNU/Linux is about freedom. Most of the software is released under the GPL license which says basically that the code is free and and must stay that way. ### Post by mivo on 2007-10-27
> **imperitus said:**
> Yar, I get that. But there's no reason there can't be a few really nice applications as well as a different mindset on how the os should work.

The OS itself works completely different, but you usually don't see this. The modular structure of the kernel, for example, is far more modern than monolithic systems like Windows. The way resources are used is also much better and more efficient, but a desktop user doesn't "see" that, yet they benefit from it. Windows users are usually used to bloat, so it is not impressive to them that Linux offers the same abilities while using half the resources for it. There are many aspects that "show how an OS should work", but that is not what you are looking for: you are looking for eye candy and killer applications, neither of which have really much to do with the OS itself. :)

I don't think that what you experience is "hostility". It's probably more an expression of slight annoyance with comparisons to Windows. Most people who use Linux do this because it works better for them, or as well, and/or because they agree with the philosophy. It's not about money; most here could easily afford Vista. There are many technical advantages, too, and you have full control over the OS -- in fact, as little or as much as you, the user, want to have. Windows gives you as much control as Microsoft wants you to have -- and their visions of how much control a user should have in the next few years clearly does not agree with my views.

But this is an OS, not a religion. Windows is the right OS for quite a few people. There are many who would really benefit from Linux, but they will have to find that out by themselves. So from this perspective I see no necessity in "showing" my Windows friends how great my preferred OS is, especially if all that would be taken into account is shallow stuff like eye candy. The Vista GUI cannot do anything that you cannot do with Compiz-Fusion and Beryl, and that is not true the other way around -- but that is GUI  stuff, it's not really the OS per se.

### Post by bonzini on 2007-10-28
Ladies and gentlemen;

The killer app in Linux is... everything.  C'mon, think about it.  Mr. Bill's legions from Redmond have all sorts of normal monetary incentives driving them to build killer apps for you - incredible salaries, stock options, condo time at Whistler, you name it.

Now think about Linux and the slaving horde that produces it.  What are their incentives?  Well, sometimes they work for an enlightened organization that sees value in supporting their work.  Sometimes they're just hacking away at stuff in the evenings, instead of helping their kids with algebra or knitting or whatever.

And despite the more-or-less complete lack of normal capitalist commercial "we'll pay you so much you can buy a new Cayenne every year" motivation, here we are using a stable, wonderful operating system with 21000+ packages that we can install and use FOR FREE right from the Synaptic manager and who knows how many other things out there.  Plus the stuff you can buy and run on Linux, like Oracle.

So you took some photos on your last trip to Cancun and you're running Ubuntu now and you want to use Gimp to get rid of your boy-friend's red-eye.

And some Windows fan-boy comes along and says "Gimp, aw come on it's not nearly as cool as Photoshop".

Are you kidding?  Photoshop costs more than a nice laptop?  Gimp does the same kind of thing, in a slightly different way, and you can run it for free, on Windoze or on Linux or on Solaris or on Mac, and you can upgrade it whenever you want and you can look at the source and you can write plug-ins without signing a developer's agreement where your first-born male child is held as collateral against you giving away any trade secrets, and you are tempted to think "Gimp isn't cool"?

It's all cool.  It's cool because you can be a Linux programmer, you can be a Linux beta tester, you can write documentation or wikis or whatever else you feel like doing, even just feeling groovy because the software you might need is out there, somewhere.

Linux, and everything that runs on it, and everything that was developed on it and runs on Windoze or OS/X, and everything that runs on Windoze or OS/X that was developed by a company that uses Linux somewhere in the organization - that's the killer app.

Got it?
