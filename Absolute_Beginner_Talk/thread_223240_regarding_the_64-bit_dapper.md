### Post by Kobalt on 2006-07-26
I installed a 64bit version of Ubuntu 6.06 to my father, total newbie in either Linux or computers. The main problem we had was the firefox plugins (such as java, flash) and video codecs. But all of these got solved because people on this forum worked and will work a lot on developping solutions for 64bit architectures. 
It depends a lot on what are you willing to do with your computer, to "measure" a potential level of issues... 
Though, I don't think Ubuntu 6.06 for 64bit will bring you so many troubles. On the other hand, the diffrence of speed with a 32bit version is noticiable when you browse your directories, when you load OpenOffice, or such things. That whould be sad not to enjoy the advantages of your CPU. 
Which is why I'd still recommend you to install a 64bit version of Ubuntu 6.06. :rolleyes: 

Cheers !
