Yes. If you can use the Gnome Ubuntu version, you should have little problem if any with Xubuntu. The main advantage of Xubuntu is that it works better on older hardware. [Free Geek of Chicago]("http://freegeekchicago.org/") uses it as their primary installation for their "[FreekBox]("http://freegeekchicago.org/about/freekbox")"es. We're starting a Free Geek of Central Florida, and have followed that example. Most of the donations we get are between 500 MHz and 1 GHz computers -celeron and PIII's. Xubuntu works just fine on those machines. These computers go to people who can't afford a computer, and sometimes have never had one. They have very little trouble getting use to Xubuntu.

### Post by pyros on 2007-07-19

ahem, nevermind. the 333mhz is pretty slow. but the ram may save you. I have two 450mhz computers that manage Ubuntu rather well, considering. I've even got compiz running well on one of them :)

Personally, I would recommend Ubuntu with the gnome interface simply because more work has been done on it as far as polish, integration, and documentation.
