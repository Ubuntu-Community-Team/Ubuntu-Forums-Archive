### Post by shamasis on 2007-12-05
I suggest:

1) As far as your laptop goes, pulling out a key would be enough to take it out. Then pushing back in will clip in the keys. As mentioned earlier, use a pen-knife or some other thing to pull out the keys. !st try doing it with some useless key that u rarely use (just in case.) Open one at a time, otherwise you may forget their original locations!

2) do NOT switch on ur lappy when its having any sort of foreign liquid on its circuitry.

3) After that keep ur keyboard top-face down for few hours.

4) keeping your laptop top-face down (i.e. keyboard facing the floor,) use hair-drier to blow out any further liquid within the keyboard channel. remember to blow it at an angle from normal, so that u dont force the liquid more inside.

5) HP 530 has spill-resistant keybaord... hence there is still a chance.

6) Drink less of fanta. If u do, dont spill them out!

Best of luck!

### Post by Dr Small on 2007-12-05
> **el escocés said:**
> I take that back...
Here is a quote from "Computer Service and Repair" by Richard M. Roberts:
> Most computer users understand the hazards of keeping liquids (coffee or soda) near the computer equipment. If a liquid is spilled on the keyboard, the keyboard should be flushed with distilled water as soon as possible. Residue from soda or any other liquid  containing sugar will leave a sticky film when dry. This film will quickly collect dirt and dust and the keys will begin to stick. Distilled water does not contain any minerals that will cause corrosion. Flush the keyboard liberally with distilled water.
Keys on the keyboard can be easily removed using a chip puller. A paper clip can also be bent to hook under the individual keys. Care should be used when attempting to remove the space bar. The typical space bar can be very difficult to reconnect to the keyboard after removal.
