### Post by bt224 on 2008-04-11
Here's what worked for me:

Install Amarok from the Synaptic Package Manager. After install you can plug in your iPod then transfer the songs onto your Ubuntu side directly from the iPod. stchman is right about AAC, specifically songs purchased from iTunes, you may have some issues. 

With that said, let me share me share my experience. I have the exact same situation. I tried to work mostly with Amarok (the other players weren't even close to Amarok as far as quality) for a few months. Don't get me wrong, Amarok is great, however sometimes my iPod didn't seem to function as well (artwork, shuffle, etc) It wasn't exactly broke, but it did some strange things every so often. This would also degrade over time. I would then boot back into Windows and use iTunes, and everything would be fine. It wasn't a Ubuntu or Amarok issue, the iPod just seems to need iTunes to function at it's best. So now, I do what you do, run Ubuntu and about once a week or so boot back into iTunes just for the iPod. Just my 2 cents.
