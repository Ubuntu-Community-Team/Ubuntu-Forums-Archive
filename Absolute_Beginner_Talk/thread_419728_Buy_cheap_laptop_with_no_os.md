### Post by Tundro Walker on 2007-04-23
You might want to call around to your local, small-business computer repair  / bulding shops.  They usually have 2nd hand computers...ones from people coming in and not wanting to fix it or not knowing what to do with their old comp after they get a new one.

Sometimes, it's just a card here or a HD there to replace and get something up and running, but since the computer's down, it's enough for folks to decide to upgrade.  A few computer shops may have a couple to sell off.

From experience, you probably want to avoid shops that acts as "consignment" stores.  IE: folks drop their goods off at the store and say how much they want for it, and the store sells it and gets a % of the cut.  I've found that (much like eBay) some folks have an over-inflated sense of worth for their items, plus since the store gets a % as a middleman, the sellers inflate their price even more to try to make up for it.  So, due to depreciation, a 1mhz laptop should cost ~$300 used, but folks selling one think they can get away with ~$500 or more.  

Because of a lot of "regular joe" folks not knowing the true value of their used hardware, but being greedy and trying to get an arm and leg for it, and the fact that when you buy used hardware, you never know what the person before you did to it (splilled coke on it, etc) or when the stuff will crap out, it's almost worth just buying a new computer that you know you can return if it craps out in the first 30 days.

For a really good deal, try asking around friends or other "regular joe" types and see if they're annoyed at having an old computer just sitting around.  If they're nice, you may get it really cheap, perhaps even free if they just want to get rid of it.  Even if they don't have something, they might be able to tell you how they got rid of it, which would point you in the direction to go look.
