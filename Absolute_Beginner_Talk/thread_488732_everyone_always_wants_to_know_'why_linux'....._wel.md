### Post by GMachine_24 on 2007-06-30
Hi:

I have used Linux in various forms for a decade or so. Having said that, I am surprisingly ignorant of much of the  system's capabilities - but I continue learning.

Now, on to 'why Linux'? I never read these kind of posts because I figured it would be a lot of "I HATE WINDOWS" vs. the nobility of open source, etc.

But today I am here to give you a concrete example of "Why Linux."

A couple years ago I bought a Compaq R3000 notebook. Came with XP Home; basically worked flawlessly until I decided to back up the system (yeah go figure, right?). I should note that I also removed a number of software packages I never used. After the back up everything was fine for about 24 hours. Then, all of a sudden, no sound from the speakers. Headset works fine.

To make the story as short as possible, I did all those things you are supposed to do to 'fix' such a problem (I was a MS systembuilder for five years so I know my way around Windows OK). *As we all know*, most computer problems are software-related. I contacted Compaq/HP - went through all the steps they advised (which I had already done 3 or 4 times on my own) and was then informed that my notebook needed new speaker.

I pleaded my case that, as I had previously mentioned, I loaded a 'live' copy of Knoppix onto the notebook and the sound worked fine from the speakers. This made no impression; I don't know if the person I was speaking with even knew what Knoppix is. 

Anyway, I gave up on Compaq/HP tech support; loaded the Kubuntu version of Feisty 7.04 and, voile, not only do the speakers work but so does everything else. I kept the Windows OS on the system because I am determined to figure out what the problem is. I even wondered if I could install the drive from Feisty onto Xp but something tells me that would not have a happy ending.

My point, of course, is that I have never had anyone in one of these forums, or any other Linux forum, tell me to go buy a piece of new hardware when it's obvious there's nothing wrong with the hardware. Computers minus the profit motive = Linux (ok I realize this might be over-the-top cheerleading but at the moment I am ((once again)) swearing I will never buy another Windows box/notebook.

Now... does anyone know how to get Bodog Poker's software to run on my Kubuntu notebox??;)

If you made it this far, thanks for your time.
