### Post by punx45 on 2007-01-09
Yes, Apple is *compelled* by the recording industries to wrap all iTunes Store sales with a DRM, and therein lies your problem.

They ship music in AAC format, which is the audio component of MP4 (so sometimes the extension still shows up .MP4 i think).  Most linux players i believe are able to play unprotected AAC.   The videos i belive are shipped in H.264. 

well for the music at least there is the clunky hack of burning all the DRM'd music to an audio CD and then re-ripping it.   iTunes currently does not support burning videos to disc, so in that method you are SOL.   

other than that, I haven't head of anything recently, and a quick 1-page google search was rather fruitless.
