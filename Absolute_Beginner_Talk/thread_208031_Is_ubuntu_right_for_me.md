### Post by aysiu on 2006-07-02
[QUOTE=.Michael]I would like A user friendly interface[/quote] Most of the popular Linux distros will provide this.
> Something that doesnt need me to use bash for things like installing flash and java... ugg quick time That can't be helped in Ubuntu. There are some graphical ways to install some things, but even some of the helper scripts will require you to at least paste a command or two into the terminal. If you want functionality for proprietary codecs "out of the box," go with Mepis or PCLinuxOS. > My system is 800 Mhz P3, 384 MB ram, 27 GBit HardDrive That should be fine. Always use a live CD to see how well the distro of your choice detects your hardware (video card, sound card, internet, mouse, etc.) **before** you commit to an actual installation. A live CD runs off your computer's RAM and does *not* affect your hard drive. But with only 384 MB of RAM, you should also keep in mind that the live CD will run quite a bit more slowly than a regular installation. > Support for my Ipod Any distro can do this--you just need the right application--Gtkpod, Banshee, and/or AmaroK. > The ability to play itunes music If you purchased these from the iTunes music store, the Apple-encoded AAC files will need to be converted somehow, but if they're regular AAC, MP3, or Ogg files, they will play just fine in any Linux distribution.
