### Post by kostkon on 2008-01-08
> **ryanVickers said:**
> This is what I saw somewhere - is this true?  How long would DVD's and CD's that you burn in your home computer last in a house at temperatures between 18 and 22 degrees Celsius and between 1 and 30% humidity? :(

The longevity of CDs and DVDs also depends on their quality. You can test the quality of the media you buy and use, and in case they are bad then you can try to buy better ones.

If you have a DVD+-RW (I don't know if it works with DVD-Rom drives) you can use the *dvd+rw-mediainfo* utility. Run the utility, having the media you want to test inside the drive, and from it's output read the *Media-ID* and check it against the list given [here]("http://www.digitalfaq.com/media/dvdmedia.htm"). The list categorizes media in classes of quality. Try to always use 1st class or at least 2nd class quality media.

You can run the utility by opening a terminal and giving
```
dvd+rw-mediainfo /dev/dvd
```
