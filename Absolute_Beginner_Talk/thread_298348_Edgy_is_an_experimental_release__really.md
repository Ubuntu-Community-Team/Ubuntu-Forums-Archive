### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-11-12
I think you have a valid point. I would agree that there is a discrepancy between the official line, which puts 6.10 on par with 6.06.1, and what you might see in the forums from front-line users.

Perhaps part of that comes from the usual hubbub that accompanies a new release. Inevitably people with problems are more likely to post their disgruntlement than people who had no issues, and as a result, word circulates that things are less than kosher. 

Be that as it may, Edgy implemented a lot of [new features]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/EdgyReleaseNotes") that were accompanied with an equally impressive [list of known issues]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/EdgyKnownIssues"). And since no one here wants a newcomer to suffer a bug that came about as a result of new additions or a too-fresh feature, the tendency is to point new people to 6.06.1 as a proven platform, and save 6.10 for more experienced, better-prepared enthusiasts.

I know I've done that in the past, and to be honest, I'm still inclined to do it in the future. My own experiences with Dapper were much more reliable than they have been with Edgy, though no one is to blame for that. Edgy is flashier and faster and prettier in my experience, and it has a lot of new features that I want (particularly within Xubuntu), but on one of my laptops, which I rely on in case of emergencies, I use Dapper.

So is Edgy unstable or unreliable? Not at all. Does it stand as an experimental or developmental release? Not necessarily. But which one would I recommend to a new user?

Dapper. :mrgreen:

### Post by JayTee on 2006-11-12
Hard one to call here. Edgy from my own experience seems stable enough. Only problem was with video resolution and I'm still trying to resolve that but I'm not in any big hurry because Edgy runs on my test box. My main system runs Dapper and that is rock solid. Seems most of the problem posts revolved around xserver breaks or freeze ups at boot time and the majority of those were from people doing a dist-upgrade from Dapper instead of clean install. I'm betting at least a few of those had kernel modules that had been compiled specifically for an earlier kernel and were causing issues, like truecrypt as an example or some other 3rd party software someone downloaded. A clean install of Edgy definitely boots faster than Dapper did on the same system and there are some other nice improvements. Should it be called unstable? I'm leaning toward no on that. Should it be called experimental? I have to go with yes. There is so much that is new in Edgy that it still has to considered experimental in regards to how it will interact with many different configurations and untested or lightly tested OSS software products. As it matures it'll only get better and for now it's a usable platform but I wouldn't run it in a mission critical production environment unless I'd tested it thoroughly with any of the applications that would be frequently used and any stress test scenarios I could bring to bear on it. As for any expectations people may have that a release version should be stable I could point out the number of Windows versions that have shipped without bugs on day one. The answer is 0 versions. Most versions weren't truly "stable" in a production sense until at least SP1 IMHO.
