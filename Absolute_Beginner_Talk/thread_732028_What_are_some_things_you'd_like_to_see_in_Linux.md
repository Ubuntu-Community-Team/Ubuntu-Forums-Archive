### Post by DayOldPorridge on 2008-03-22
Don't get me wrong; I love Ubuntu, but every now and then I wish it had a few more features/better hardware compatibility/things that I can do easily in Windows yet struggle to accomplish in Linux.  Now, these are just a few, yet minor and rather pointless things that have been bugging me, however I think it's important that even these things be taken care of, as it would definitely help Linux in seriously competing with Windows.  But what other things can you think of?

Here's my list:

1. Better webcam support.  I know that it's hard to even make just a few drivers for a small number of webcams, but this is something that Linux seriously needs to do some catching up on, since in pretty much all distros the support for webcams is substantially limited.  Is there a specific team working on better support for this?

2. An exact clone of MSN; aMSN doesn't have the handwriting feature that I need yet, while Mercury IM does, however it only works between Mercury users and the program is not Open Source, either.  I'm sure there are quite a few other features you could think of that cannot be found in any of the Linux MSN alternatives.

3. Faster boot-up times by default after the first install.  Yoper does this pretty fast on my laptop, and although I know it depends on the hardware, there must be a way to just quicken the boot-up process in general for most computers.

There's more, but those are just the things I can think of off the top of my head.

And something that worries me is how Hardy is just going to be more of a bug fixer; this is definitely an improvement,but in six months I think a little more progress for Ubuntu could be accomplished if a whole bunch of people got together to focus on some major features (not necessarily the ones on my list) that Linux is in dire need of.
