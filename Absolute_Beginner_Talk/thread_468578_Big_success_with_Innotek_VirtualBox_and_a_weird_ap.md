### Post by Jim March on 2007-06-09
I study electronic voting issues.  I'm a consultant for the Pima County Democratic Party (despite personally being Libertarian and one thing I do a lot of is take apart Diebold Election Systems databases, looking for improper procedures in the audit logs or forensic evidence of vote tampering.

Diebold's vote processing application is GEMS - Global Election Management Software.  Problem one: it's a Windows app.  Problem two, even worse, it's based on MS-Access and hence can't run as a Wine app.

I actually have a copy of the GEMS software, downloaded in January '03 by a lady name of Bev Harris in Seattle who was researching a book and found an unprotected Diebold FTP site (anonymous access allowed).  She grabbed 40,000 files over a three-day period :).

Anyways.  I managed to get Windows 2000 SP4, MS-Office 2003 and GEMS running on a 10gig virtual drive with 640megs assigned.  Performance is rock solid.  I'm only running a Centrino single-core 1.6gHz laptop, but I do have 2gigs RAM in it.

This is WAY better than dual boot.

Screenshot showing Win2k running 800x600 with both GEMS and MS-Access going - I was able to load the guest OS drivers giving me full screen control and I can run the Win2k guest full-screen at full res if I want to:

[http://www.equalccw.com/virtual2.jpg](http://www.equalccw.com/virtual2.jpg)

I started with VirtualBox 1.3.8 and upgraded to 1.4, haven't seen any real difference.  All this is on Feisty.
