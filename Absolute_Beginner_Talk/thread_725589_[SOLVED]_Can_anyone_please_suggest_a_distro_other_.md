### Post by R@inm@n on 2008-03-15
I started with Oubuntu, and stuffed it up a couple of times trying to get SPFX with video cards and 3D etc..

I gave up and went over to Linux Mint, which made everything waaay easier to get the special effects on the desktop.

Once I became more familiar with Linux Mint, I came back to 'buntu.

I realised that I didn't really need the SPFX and all the 'frills', just a reliable OS that works every time.


However .......

If there is one thing that Linux lacks, it is good video card drivers, but that's not the fault of open source, but the video card makers themselves.

Look at the wide selection Windows has for video card drivers, and then look at Linux...:(   No comparison.

Linux Mint can probably give you what you need, if desktop FX is what you are after, as Mint makes it all easier to get that stuff working, but in the end, you will probably find that even Mint can't make up for the lack of decent video  drivers.



   R.
