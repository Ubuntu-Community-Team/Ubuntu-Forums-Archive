### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-07-22
Well yes, I suppose you could mount a USB drive as a folder in home. I don't think that's very effective though. Most people install the whole OS on a portable pen drive, that's a real nifty solution (then they can boot and work on it on the fly). For public workstation areas, I'd just create public accounts and limit the home folder to say 50 MB (for each) or however much you want folks to have.
