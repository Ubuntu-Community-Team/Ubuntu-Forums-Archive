### Post by mikeyphi on 2007-08-24
1. You have the choice of upgrading or fresh install with each new release of ubuntu.
there are regular 6-monthly releases of new stable OS and every now and then there is a release of a 'long term support' OS this LTS release will have security, etc updates until the next LTS release. 6-monthly releases are supported for a shorter period.

2. Gnome/Kde - you can install either or both, and if required you can switch between desktops or uninstall either when you choose...you don't have to reinstall a full OS. Ubuntu is the OS, kubuntu is the same OS with some different but equivalent packages. Try both and see which you prefer!

3. You could choose to put your music or in fact all your data (normally in your Home folder) on a separate partition. That way changes in OS won't affect your data.

Look at this guide: [http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/index)
