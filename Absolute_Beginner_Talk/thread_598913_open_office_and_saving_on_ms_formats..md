### Post by Hagar Delest on 2007-11-01
Fonts have nothing to do with the content or formatting of the document, they're part of your OS in fact, they're not related to a document or even an application.

By converting in MS format, you'll have some formatting differences about spaces, tabs, page styles will be changed to section breaks (only if you've used manual page breaks), ...

There can be many aspects that won't be well exported so the only way to know is to try every feature you need and see if that works or not. Password protection is not supported for example. Basically, it's not advised to have OOo as a replacement of MS Office if you keep the MS Office file formats.

### Post by Bloch on 2007-11-01
When a document gets sufficiently complex: e.g. with many embedded images, text boxes, tables with very narrow columns etc then the difference begin to show up.

Usually it's a matter of a few minutes to adjust image positions, maybe change column width etc to get a document looking fine. (Whether it's opening a .doc in OOo or opening an OOo created .doc in MS Word) 

But if you are workijng with several documents a day then you cannot expect OOo to be a drop-in replacement for MS Word.

The warning is just a general warning that appears every time you save in .doc format.
