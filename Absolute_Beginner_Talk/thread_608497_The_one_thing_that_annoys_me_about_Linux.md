### Post by Gone fishing on 2007-11-10
The one thing that annoys me about Linux in general and very rarely about Ubuntu, are deliberately unhelpful posts on forums. The ones where someone asks for help and the reply is read the manual. It is so  irritating when you have a problem, have tried to understand the manual, have a few queries, Google for an answer and you find this type of reply, the subtext of which is yes I know how to solve your problem, I'm cleaver than you and no I won't help.

Why even make this type of reply, why not just sit smugly at home in the knowledge that you are superior, without inflicting your smugness on everyone else? 

The other is where the answer is a terminal command, without any explanation of how and where to use it. This answer is only of help to people who no what they are doing. The subtext here is, I'm only going to help clever people like me, insiders the rest of you can pee off.

It strikes me that one of the reasons for Ubuntu's success is the friendliness of this forum as well as the ease of use, stability etc of Ubuntu Linux.
