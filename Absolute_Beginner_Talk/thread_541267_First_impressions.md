### Post by macogw on 2007-09-02
What problems, specifically?  What hardware do you have?

I think with stability, part of the difference is that on here you see not-really-finished software sometimes.  It doesn't necessarily crash, but it might not behave in what seems to be the best way.  The nice thing, though, is that if one process runs astray, it's easy enough to kill that process and keep the rest of the system going.  For example, my wired internet driver is a bit sucky.  It dies at high throughput.  On Windows, a sucky driver dying would cause a BSOD.  On here, it means I can unload and reload the driver, and it'll continue running just fine.
