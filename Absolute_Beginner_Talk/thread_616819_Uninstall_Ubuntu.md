### Post by Arthur Archnix on 2007-11-18
If you're computer came with a rescue cd, or prompted you to make one, just put that it and it will take care of ubuntu for you. If not, hopefully you didn't install over either the rescue partition that is common on many new computers or installed a dual-boot scenario. If you have rescue partition you'll probably see a little thing come up at boot saying "press this button to rescue". If so just do that, and again, it will take care of ubuntu for you.

If you created a dual boot scenario then you probably get a screen letting you choose either ubuntu or windows, however, I don't get the sense that this is your situation.

If none of those apply then you may have erased Windows. You should contact your computer's manufacturer to find out what your options might be.
