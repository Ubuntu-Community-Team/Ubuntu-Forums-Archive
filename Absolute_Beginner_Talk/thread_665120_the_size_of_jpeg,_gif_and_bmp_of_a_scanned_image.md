### Post by macogw on 2008-01-11
bmp = HUGE, lossless
jpg & gif = small
jpg = lossy (it gets grainy/blurrier...fine with photos)
gif = lossless (image is exactly the same)

The exact size will depend on the compression v. quality settings you choose when saving.  You can scan them at 300dpi then use the GIMP to compress them & save at a lower quality level.

### Post by JAPrufrock on 2008-01-11
As a general rule of thumb, unless I intend to print an image I usually scan at around 150 dpi.  Since I scan in tiff format with scanimage, I will usually use Gimp to save the image in JPG format.  If I adjust the quality of the JPG image to somewhere between 30-50%, I can usually reduce the final image size to around 100 kb, an acceptable size for sending over the internet, and definitely good enough to see on a computer screen.
