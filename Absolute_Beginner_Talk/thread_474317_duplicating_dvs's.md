### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-06-15
If you want to copy a DVD9 and compress it to a DVD5, I would highly recommend [COLOR="Blue"]k9copy[/COLOR]. It is a KDE application, like k3b, but works fine under GNOME.

I have tried other compressors, but have not been pleased with the quality, as pixelisation has been evident. With k9copy the compressed DVD is virtually indistinguishable from the original.

I would only use k9copy to create the iso, though, as it is a bit quirky with te auto-burn function.

After creating the iso with k9copy, I just use [COLOR="Blue"]k3b[/COLOR] to burn it.

If the DVD is copy protected, you will need [COLOR="Blue"]libdvdcss[/COLOR] to decrypt it.

Roger :cool:
