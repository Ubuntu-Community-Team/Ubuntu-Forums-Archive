### Post by Bartender on 2006-10-16
How many of you are browsing the Ubuntu Forums with your Windows PC's, on the hunt for good threads but not sure how to save them to your hard drive or print them out without wasting reams of paper and losing the original formatting?  I've tried copy/pasting in Word or OpenOffice, but the formatting gets destroyed and the post is harder to follow.
I just tried PDFCreator, a free download that converts almost anything to .pdf.  It installs as a link in your drop-down list of printers.  Say you've found a great thread or guide you want to save and/or print.  You could use Thread Tools to create a printable version, or not.  Either way, when you click on File>Print, scroll thru your printer choices and PDFC should be there.  Click on it, click Print, and PDFC converts the webpage to .pdf.  The .pdf will be displayed in your default .pdf program.  I'm using Foxit, a great alternative to Adobe.  It'll ask if you want to Save, in which case you can save a .pdf version to a folder, then you can print too if you want.  Neat thing is you can print only what you want instead of the whole thread.  And you can print even first, odd second to save on paper.
