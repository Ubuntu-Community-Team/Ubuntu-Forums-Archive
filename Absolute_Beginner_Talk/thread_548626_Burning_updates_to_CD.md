### Post by maniac_X on 2007-09-11
AptonCD is a great solution for those "update blues" for non-high-speed internet users. It may not, however, be a solution unless one assumes that the OP is grabbing the updates on a system/*buntu version that is the same as the off-line system he wants to update. 

Irecently did a big update over dial-up and it wasn't fun. Thankfully Ubuntu resumes partial downloads from where it left off or I may never have gotten updated at all.

its_toby,  I would say just bite the bullet and do the updates over dial-up as you are able. The updates are cached on your system until you have them all before it performs the update. It took me a number of starts and stops over a 3 day period before I got all my updates mainly due to the fact I can't have the computer online all the time.

Once you are updated, make sure to connect once or wice a week so the system can check for further updates and keep you current. Ussually updates are fairly small unless a major packages gets updated..like OpenOffice.

Once you are updated, use AptonCD to backup those updates so you have a easier time of updating if something happens and you need to start from scratch.
