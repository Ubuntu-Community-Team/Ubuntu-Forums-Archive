### Post by jrz on 2006-10-14
Sorry, It turned out that everything I needed was installed, and all I had to do to be able to switch between Thai and English was to make something called a Keyboard Indicator visible. It didn't appear on any of the pull down menus, but I found that by right clicking on the top bar of the screen, then clicking on the Add to Panel..., and then scrolling down to Utilities in the window that appeared, there was a utility named Keyboard Indicator which when added displayed 'USA' on the middle of the bar at the top of the screen. When I clicked it, it toggled to Tha for Thai language. Initially it was still messed up as somewhere along the line we had a choice between 2 different Thai Keyboards so the characters displayed didn't match the keys struck, but we changed it to the other Thai keyboard and now it works as desired within OpenOffice.org Word processor. We can type in one language and just click the indicator at the top to quickly change to the other language and back as desired. I ran across the help needed to put me on to this by googling and finding someone in Europe who had mentioned using the Keyboard Indicator to swap between languages but couldn't find any such thing in the menus. Just luck, curiosity and the fact that it is similar to how we use it on a Windows system that caused us to right click on the bar at the top.
