### Post by elephant007 on 2007-03-14
Okay, I'm done with Vista (and any other Microsoft OS) and I've decided.

  Less than a month a go I purchased a Laptop from Dell with Vista.  The first time I fired up the laptop I received a "not compatible" error essentially telling me that the version of Roxio was not compatible with Vista... I have had some problems with it, I updated the O/S and Roxio and the BSOD!!!!

  Total reinstall of the O/S which then deleted my ability to use system restore and my media button (the one you can press with the laptop off and it will boot into a media o/s to play dvds etc..)  After struggling for a week to get all of that up and going again, I just decided that I didn't want Vista.  I have been dipping my toes in Linux since Mandrake 4  including dipping my toes into other flavors such as Gentoo, RedHat, Suse, Knoppix, Damn Small Linux, Yellow Dog and Ubuntu...

  So I check out Distro Watch to see the lastest most popular linux flavor is, usually that means there's more people using it (DUH!) which in turn means there are more people to turn to for support....  I download Ubuntu 6.10 blindly, wasn't sure if i could get all the hardware working at that time, after downloading I burn it to CD and install Ubuntu.

  I have configured my Wireless and everything else, I've decided to dump Vista and I emailed Dell for a refund for Vista.  I haven't received a response from them yet, I'll post updates... basically I told them that I'm tired of Vista not operating properly and always having problems with it, it's a bad product and as with any bad product you RETURN it and get a REFUND!
