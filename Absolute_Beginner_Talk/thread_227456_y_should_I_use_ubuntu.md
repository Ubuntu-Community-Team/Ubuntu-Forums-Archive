### Post by Uncle Spellbinder on 2006-08-01
I don't know about *"a List of Well Known or Growing Companies in the US (thats where im from New Jersey to be exact) that are currently migrating to Ubuntu, or currently using it."* But I thought the same thing before I tried Breezy back in January. Having used Breezy for a bit, I wasn't sure. As things didn't mesh well with my computer. Though I don't believe Breezy was "buggy", it sure had that feel for me. So I uninstalled and waited until the proper release of Dapper. 

I installed Ubuntu Dapper Drake 6.06 LTS around a month ago. The smoothest install of an OS I've experienced. The whole process took around 45 minutes (creating "unallocated space" for dapper and the actuall install). It's been a grand experience ever since. And aside from a program or two that have no Linux compatibility (Acid Music Studio in particular), I've not needed Windows at all. Anything I did on Windows, I can do with Dapper. Music, Video, and *Open Office* is *excellent* for your office needs. 

I'm inches away from dumping Windows for good.
