### Post by Hospadar on 2008-01-16
really you don't need to do anything other than plug it in and turn on the machine.  you won't really see any outward sign of it until you put a disc in it.

The trickiest part will probably be getting the master/slave settings correct if it's an IDE drive (big flat cable (probably grey) as opposed to skinny red cable, which is for SATA) If you don't know anything about setting the jumpers on an IDE drive, you may want to do a little googling, it's quite simple, but can cause a lot of trouble if you don't know about it.  Basically there are some pins on the back, and the right two pins need to be jumpered according to the drive's position on an IDE cable to get everything working hunky-dory.
