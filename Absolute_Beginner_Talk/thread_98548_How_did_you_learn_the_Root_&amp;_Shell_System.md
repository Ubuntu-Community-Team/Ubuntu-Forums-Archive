### Post by d1337 on 2005-12-03
I learned from a combination of books, online docs, some help from a mentor, but mostly from necessity.  My first install was [Libranet]("http://libranet.com/orig_index") in July of 2004.  I did this on a machine that I had in my garage as my office space was too limited at the time to handle another computer.  The first online documentation that I used was a course from [Linux.org]("http://www.linux.org/lessons/beginner/index.html"). I highly recommend a newbie taking some time to go through this as it has proved invaluable to me to learn navigation.  The [linuxcommand.org]("http://linuxcommand.org") link is still helpful to prod me in the proper architecture of commands.  Shortly after that, my wife bought me [The Linux Troubleshooting Bible]("http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/076456997X/qid=1133649463/sr=8-1/ref=pd_bbs_1/102-0344501-1995320?n=507846&s=books&v=glance") and [Linux In A Nutshell]("http://www.linux.org/perl-bin/search_db?q=linux+in+a+nutshell&qid=4&spp=20") for Christmas.  But necessity at work gave me the confidence to make it happen and tie everything together.  Now, I mostly rely on forums here on Ubuntu Forums and occasionally from [LinuxQuestions.org]("http://www.linuxquestions.org/") but also specific project forums.

### Post by Kvark on 2005-12-03
Both online info from sites that are already mentioned here and from the 3 built in help functions:

Large: **info** <command>
Complete info you can study to learn a command.

Medium: **man** <command>
A general reminder to refresh your memory of a command you know kinda how to use.

Small: <command> **--help**
An even shorter reminder for when you master the command but forgot which exact letters/words to use.
