### Post by ^rooker on 2007-02-22
For support of restricted formats, there "is" something bundled:
[easyubuntu]("http://easyubuntu.freecontrib.org/") or [automatix2]("http://www.getautomatix.com/")

They will provide you additional functionality by the click of the mouse, but please be aware of the fact that there **is** a reason why this functionality is not coming with a default Ubuntu installation... I know it's not easy to stay away from non-free software and formats, but at least one should be aware of the benefits of free software. 

Bedtime-story-like info about a "free vs non-free" world can be found here:
[Free software fables]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=354562")

<edit>Forgot to mention that Linux' way of handling file-extensions is even superior to e.g. Windows' way:
Instead of simply believing in the suffix of a file (e.g. mp3, ogg, txt, ...) linux looks inside the files to determine what they **really** are.
Thus, you can't be so easily fooled to open something evil, for instance - or: if someone sent you a file without suffix, linux would still be able to handle it properly.
</edit>
