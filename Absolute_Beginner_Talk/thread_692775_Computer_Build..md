### Post by treehuggerguy on 2008-02-11
Hi BluePlum,

I wasn't trying to say that there is something wrong with this board, only that you need to be careful when selecting products for your build.  You should google search your board and your video card to see if there are issues.  I made this mistake recently when I built my machine.  I have managed to get everything to work, but it wasn't as easy as I would have liked.

I had difficulty following your link the first time (it was a dead end for some reason) so this time I entered the product number for the board into Newegg.  I'm no pro - this was my first build - so I don't want to recommend one board over another, but [here's a link to some recommendations that I found by googling the board.]("http://www.linuxquestions.org/questions/linux-hardware-18/linux-motherboard-compatibility-558615/")  It implies that people have recommended your board for linux, but has other recommendations as well.

Also, I'm not sure if this will be important to you, but this is from one of the newegg reviews:

> Pros: Boots quick and stable, provided everything is compatable
Cons: Mislabled on the site. Board is not AM2+, AN2 only. Will not support Phenom even with BIOS update. So much for backward compatability.
Other Thoughts: Be careful when reading the specs for CPU compatibility. Neither newegg nor myself are infallable.

I wished that I had read some of these reviews more carefully.  There are lots of boards and cards that work well, however (I'm sure it's way more than those that don't work well).  You probably want to find a board that will run sound and network out of the box (and video if you're getting integrated).  You want to make sure that you don't need beta drivers for your video card.  This means most of the NVidia cards, but not all.  My integrated 7150 didn't work without beta drivers.  I think that on the higher end the 8800 cards don't work either.  I've heard good things about the 8600s - if I upgrade I'll probably get one of those although so far I'm quite happy with my video performance.

Didn't mean to scare you.  This will probably work very smoothly for you if you do a little homework.  Good luck.
