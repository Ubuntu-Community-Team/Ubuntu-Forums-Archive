### Post by aeb1 on 2007-04-07
You have Ubuntu Edgy or Fiesty, but cannot use Amarok or connect? Here is how I did it for several Armada 1750 laptops. If you do the hardware mods, you can even watch movies!

I did the hardware mods first. I now have a 500Mhz PIII ( yes, PENTIUM THREE ) cpu with 320Mb Ram

[http://www.wireball.com/armada_1750_review.htm](http://www.wireball.com/armada_1750_review.htm)

shows you how to do it. Note: A cpu faster than 500MHz killed the built in PCMCIA slots.

I still have no sound or connectivity. To fix that, I had a little USB help.

The solution to sound: A $5.00 USB sound card bought on eBay. Ubuntu sees it right on boot up. You still have to configure the programs to recognize the USB sound device in the ALSA settings/configuration. 

The solution to wired Ethernet access: You must purchase the Xircom Ethernet card with ONE port on the side. The standard 3 port card conflicts with the built in modem on the Armada and nothing will work. I got mine for $ 3.95 on eBay.

The solution to wireless Ethernet access: Purchase the GIGAFAST USB wireless USB card. This card was also seen by Ubuntu right away. You still have to configure your wireless access point access with the tools provided in Ubuntu.

I run Kubuntu and the latest release ( 7.04 ) on this Armada. I have full capability. I even watch movies using the VLC player. My mp3 files play in Amarok too! Just add a large hard drive & you have it all with you.
