### Post by toasterofirony on 2007-12-09
I guess it depends what you are looking to use it for. Some people have laptops as their only/ main computers (my girlfriend, for example) and some people have them 'cause it means they have a portable option (like me).

If you are looking to get a laptop as a desktop replacement, I would ask "why?" If you are going off to college and you need a computer, great idea. You'll want a decent sized screen and keyboard, as well as the kind of specs that a large footprint would allow you to get. Sure, it's a Dell, so you can expect a reasonable build quality and good price. It will not wow and certainly there are limitations to them, but I don't think it's a terrible idea.

If you are looking for a portability option, I would suggest the asus eeepc. But that's just me.
