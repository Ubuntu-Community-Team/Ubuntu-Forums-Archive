### Post by Brunellus on 2005-08-19
YOu can do a lot, including and not limited to:

1)  Desktop publishing and page design with Scribus.  If you're more hardcore and/or really interested in the guts of typesetting, there's LaTeX.

2) Photo editing and manipulation with the GIMP.  For most users, the GIMP is a more than adequate application.  I do a lot of photography, and haven't even touched on the limits of what the GIMP might permit me to do.  I can't name a better, more capable, or more flexible application for the price..(gratis!)

4) Website content creation.  More tools than you can shake a stick at!

5) P2P networking.  GTK-gnutella, if you're into that sort of thing.  Also Azureus, and of course your garden variety torrents.  Great for everything.

6) Customize, customize, customize.  Gnome + metacity themes + gdesklets= [My GNOME Desktop]("http://photos22.flickr.com/32026545_9a050a7d79_o.png").  Try that with XP.

Things you won't do with Linux:

1) Run anti-spyware, anti-adware, or anti-virus software.  No viruses or spyware for Linux have been observed or reported in the wild.  

2) Reboot your computer unless you absolutely have to.

3) Pay a dime for your OS, or many applications.

### Post by grim42 on 2005-08-19
da_buddha,

Similar posts to your original one from people experimenting with Linux (or intending to) pop up just about everyday.

See the following post for general thoughts on the matter:
[http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=58017](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=58017)

The important thing to remember is that GNU/Linux is not a Windows or Apple clone. It is an *alternative* operating environment.

With regards to your specific example of Photoshop. Adobe do not develop a Linux native version of Photoshop and you will probably not have success getting it to work properly with Wine (Windows emulation/compatibility).

The GIMP is the most powerful and popular *alternative* available on GNU/Linux systems. Unless you're a graphics professional, there's nothing Photoshop can do that GIMP can't. However many of these things are done in a different way to how it would be done in Photoshop.

The same is true for other open source apps you may try to use. Although the popular ones like Firefox are of course the same, and installed by default.

Another good link to look at is "Linux is Not Windows": [http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm](http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm)

Finally, don't be put off by the emotive responses -- understand that this is just a bit of a sensitive topic.

EDIT:

> it can't be denied that in all honesty, software writers make programs only for Windows (and Apple too) that just can't be matched (or haven't yet) on Linux. Nothing so far that I've found, or probably will find, comes close to Photoshops power (on either Windows or Apple).

The point is, that it **can** be denied. Just don't expect that you won't have to learn to do things in a different way. Commercial software companies do not generally write software for Linux, but the open source alternatives are more often than not** just as capable** and in some cases **better.**
