### Post by meng on 2006-12-10
Of all those tasks, I consider the video-editing the most intensive.
Purely my opinion, and I am not a tech-head:
1024 MB RAM
Athlon 2800+ (or similar Intel)
100+ GB HDD (more if you are thinking of having hours of raw video)
NVIDIA card with 128 MB VRAM

I have a system with similar (slightly higher) specs than this and I am happy with my video editing and 3D graphics performance. It won't knock your socks off though.

### Post by BarfBag on 2006-12-10
> **bradsubarcadia said:**

Edit video - a high end AMD or Core Due/2 Intel.  You'd also need a decent nVidia video card.  The drivers are much, much better than ATI.  1 GB of RAM wouldn't hurt, either.

Watch movies - a DVD player.  Not much needed here.

Image editing - you really don't need much power for this.  At work, I clean/edit images taller then me on a P3 with 256 MB of RAM, running Windows XP!  It's a pain, but it works.  If your PC does video editing, it will be more then capable of this.  If not, a decent processor and 1 GB of RAM would do the trick.

Rip CD's - Literally nothing here.

3D games - nVidia graphics card.

Audio mixing & editing - lower than video editing requirements, but if you get a PC that does all of this, it'll work fine.

Hope this helps.  :)

### Post by lyceum on 2006-12-10
Video ripping/editing is not cheap. You need a good processor if you really want to get things done. You don't really need a full gig of memory, but anything less that 512 will be painful. I would not say you have to get a due core processor, but you will need better than a P III, in my opinion.

I would say the stats you mention would be fine for now, but be ready to upgrade later if you need/want to. For example, get 1 stick for your 512 (memory), that way you can add another stick when you get the funds. I started with those states, and I was happy for a while. I was a lot happier with a 2nd fan and a second stick of 512.
