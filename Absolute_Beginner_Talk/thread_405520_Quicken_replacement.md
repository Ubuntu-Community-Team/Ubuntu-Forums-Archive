### Post by machoo02 on 2007-04-09
You could check out [GnuCash]("http://www.gnucash.org/") or [KMyMoney]("http://kmymoney2.sourceforge.net/index-home.html"), depending on your choice of desktop environment.  Both are available in the repos, and both can import and work with Quicken (.qif) and Quickbooks (.qfx) files

### Post by ramjet_1953 on 2007-04-10
If you follow this link:

[http://freshmeat.net/search/?q=personal+finance+manager&section=projects&Go.x=10&Go.y=10](http://freshmeat.net/search/?q=personal+finance+manager&section=projects&Go.x=10&Go.y=10)

Freshmeat lists a few personal finance managers. However, you will probably need to compile the source code yourself from any downloads from here.
If you like something from freshmeat, it is always worthwile to do a search for the package, placing a deb at the end. That way, if someone has already compiled a deb package, there is a good chance that it will work on your system. Of course, you may still need to satisfy dependancies.

You could also try GNUCash, which is available for dowload through Synaptic.

KMyMoney could also fill the bill. It is also available on Synaptic.
It should work under GNOME.
