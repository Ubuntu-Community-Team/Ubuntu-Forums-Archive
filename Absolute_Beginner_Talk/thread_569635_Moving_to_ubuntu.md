You can do [COLOR="Blue"]pretty much everything[/COLOR] with Ubuntu, but I would suggest trying the linux alternatives to the windows programs and running windows programs (through wine or crossover) ONLY if you really deem it necessary. Quicken 2007  requires probably quite some tweakings through crossover, but you could try the "on line" approach: [http://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/browse/name/?app_id=2634;forum=1;msg=5622]("http://www.codeweavers.com/compatibility/browse/name/?app_id=2634;forum=1;msg=5622"). 
Again, you could use instead GnuCash or KMyMoney,  both can import and work with Quicken  files. Less hassles and _a GNU/linux application_ that will keep getting better and better, instead of a proprietary dinosaur.

You'll find that very often the GNU/Linux alternatives are better, cleaner and quicker than the overbloated windows suites.

Once you try K3B insteas of nero, or Opera instead of microsoft explorer  you'll immediately see what I mean :-)
