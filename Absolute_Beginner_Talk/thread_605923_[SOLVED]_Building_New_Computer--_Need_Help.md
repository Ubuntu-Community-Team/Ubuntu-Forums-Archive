### Post by irish_flu on 2007-11-07
I like it, but I'd Google around and see what folks are saying about Linux with your motherboard (just in case!).

I share some of the other folks' concern with the case.  In my opinion, that case is probably gonna "feel" somewhat cheap and "plastic-ey".


I have this case, and I love it:
[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811119068]("http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16811119068")

It's metal, and the whole front is "mesh" with a sponge filter to allow for better airflow.  It's only a few bucks (like $20) more, in the grand scheme of things, although it's important to note that it doesn't come with a power supply.  :)

As for the PSU, I don't know anything about that brand but I'd wager that at that price (for both case and PSU) the power supply is pretty cheaply made.  Even if you do decide to go with that case, I'd order a name-brand power supply (thermaltake, cooler master, antec), even if it's a very inexpensive one from a better-known brand.

Nothing wrecks your expensive hardware faster than nasty power and heat, and a poorly-designed case and power supply can get you problems with both.  I'm not trying to get you to break your budget, but a good case (even at under fifty bucks) and PSU can make the *really* expensive parts last far longer.

Some general advice: Get yourself plenty of fans, my friend.  If whatever case you order has space for an extra fan, spend a few bucks and throw one in there.  Get yourself a nice CPU cooler, as well (if your mobo/processor doesn't come with one already).

If it's been awhile since you built (a situation I can understand) remember that heat is an even greater problem than it used to be.  Make sure you take care to provide your new box with good cooling capabilities.

Have fun building your new PC, I'm jealous!  :)
