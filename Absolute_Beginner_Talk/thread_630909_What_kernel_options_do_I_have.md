### Post by Severun on 2007-12-03
If you really want to supercharge it and have the time build your own, then you can get rid of any drivers you don't need and set any options specific to your processor/mobo/video setup and tune it just so.

I've found though that unless I need a specific feature or I'm tweaking a high performance server, I just can't be bothered to go through the hassle for a mere desktop, but if you've got time and want to build your own kernel, it is kind of cool and will certainly give you a smidgen of geek cred to be running your own custom kernel build from source.

As to the howto of build, a search in the forums for kernel source build howto will produce some nice results for you.
