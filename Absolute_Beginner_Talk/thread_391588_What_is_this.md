### Post by arbulus on 2007-03-23
Becareful, you might start a flamewar! lol

I use Gnome, and if you are interested in something that can give you widgets and an OS X like dock in Gnome, gDesklets is what you're looking for.

```
sudo apt-get install gdesklets
```

I like the widgets that come with this one.  I'm not a big fan of it's dock widget thing.  It's kinda ugly, and it always uses magnification.  I'm not a fan of that, even on my iMac, and you can't turn it off on the gDesklet.  So what I did was put program launchers in my panel on the bottom of the screen, remove the desktop switcher and move the window list to the top panel, and then right click the panel and say "Properties" and uncheck "Expand", so your panel shrinks to fit right around your launchers.  Then you can resize it to your preference and change the opacity and you have a fiarly accurate dock. I like this approach much better than gDesklet's dock.

However, I think it's great to experiment with different desktop environments. So, I don't mean to sound like I'm discouraging you from KDE or Kubuntu in general.  I like XFCE as well because it's kind of a nice blend between the two environments, but a little more minimalist.
