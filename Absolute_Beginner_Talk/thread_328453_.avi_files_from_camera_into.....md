### Post by SadaraX on 2007-01-02
Ezen, AVI is only a container format, not the codec of the video. So, while it is inside of an AVI container, it could be any type of video codec. You can save XviD, DivX or Windows Media Video into AVI's, so we will need something more specific than that. 

I suggest you go post your problem on the Avidemux forums. To get the best results quickly, explain your problem, and provide a small sample file. 5 to 10 seconds is normally enough. You can upload this sample file at megaupload.com and then provide a link for the Avidemux developers to look at. They may be able to tell what is going on with the video.
