### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-02-10
My PC with Ubuntu on it is setup with removable drives (ide). That way I can mess around with different OS' and not worry about messing one or another up.

So my basic setup (as far as hard drives) is as so:

Removable bay in 5.25" drive bay (under DVD drive):

Three seperate trays: 

Ubuntu dapper (200GB drive)
Windows XP Home & Ubuntu Edgy (100GB Drive)
Windows 2000, Suse 10.1 & LinuxMint (100GB Drive)

One I need to use a different OS, I simply turn off the PC. Then unlock the drive and pull it out. Place the new drive in and reboot. Sure I could get a massive drive and install all on one but why rick the chance of messing up grub. And if I plan to add a new OS and drive, I just buy a new tray. And those tray are cheap.

This way I can each OS seperate and setup for a different use.

And as a side note: 

For a case look at features and easy of use. If price is a major issue, I would go with Antec. They are solidly built and have lots of room too.
The new intel cpu's are cheaper and faster than AMD for the same price. They also run cool too, think overclocking.
Look into Plextor drives. Rock solid and you can get a good price after rebate. I even broke the tray off one of mine, slapped it back in and it worked.
I am a big fan of Logitech mice and keyboards. Plus if Ubuntu is your OS of choice, you can get "extra" keys to work. Think foward/reverse in browser and nautilus, it makes life easier. They also rule in windows too. Keyboards also have many hotkeys and volume control.
For good/cheap speakers, try to find some Logitech Z3 or what ever they updated to. Small 2.1 system with good bass and sound quality.
Don't get cheap on the power supply either. Unless you like an unstable system.
For the LCD, do you plan on gaming? Than look at a Samsung. No frills performance.
No mention of a video card? If you plan on daul boot, look at NVidia. I hear the EVGA 7600GS is pretty nice, even faster than some 7800's. Also less than $200.

What is your budget anyway?
