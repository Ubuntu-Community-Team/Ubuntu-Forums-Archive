### Post by bcrom on 2007-12-19
Dual booting shouldn't cause a problem.  If you partitioned your hard drive correctly, ubuntu should never see or know about Vista.  I highly doubt this is your problem.  Any incorrect partitioning would probably prevent Ubuntu from booting.  

There are no specific actions that I can tell you to do and not do.  What you should do when you encounter a problem is try to isolate the cause.  Eliminate variables until you're left with one cause and then come back and ask the forum how to fix it.  No one here has ESP, and we can't tell you what's wrong without more information.
