### Post by rmsoldato on 2006-08-04
This may be common knowledge for many, so please be patient.  

Like many, I've recently switched from Windows to Ubuntu, and have been loving it.  BUT, I still had to keep windows around for newsgroup binary downloading.  The newsreader 'Pan' in the repositories just didn't meet the same level of functunality that many windows apps do.  

However, I did some trial and error running windows newsreaders with wine, and they all failed . . . except one.  I found that [Usenet Explorer]("http://www.netwu.com/ue/") works without any problems in wine.  It's a subscription based application with an indexing function for searching all newsgroups.  I was happy with it and paid for the 2-year subscription.  I now have to visit windows even less (horay!) just to use one program.

I have seen a few posts in here regarding the same dilemma, that someone wants to switch completely to Ubuntu, but is forced to keep windows just for usenet downloading.  I thought my experience might help.

### Post by BIGtrouble77 on 2006-08-04
What don't you like about Pan?  I pretty much only download binaries, and I think Pan is perfectly suited for that.  I've used a ton of newsreaders over the years (not the one you mentio above) and think Pan is by far the best.  I'm currently using the new beta ([http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=223779&highlight=pan](http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=223779&highlight=pan)) and am very impressed.  

My favorite reader for ages was Xnews because you could create folders for binaries, but it was so unstable and had terrible memory leaks.  In fact, windows is completely unsuitable for downloading large amounts of binaries- downloading large amounts of headers in agent always crashed my machine.  Pan is the first reader that NEVER crashes on me.

I'd suggest getting to know Pan a little more and realize that you'll be doing some things differently than your used to. I did have to customize a few things before I got comfortable (the single click execution drove me crazy).
