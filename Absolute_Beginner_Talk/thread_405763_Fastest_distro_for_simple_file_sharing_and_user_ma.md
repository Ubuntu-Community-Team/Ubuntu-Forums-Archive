### Post by kazuya on 2007-04-10
You may have to be proficient in linux somewhat. The speed gains and functionality comes with knowledge in tweaking system and configuring. Do not go to linux for just speed or hearsay. Go with an open-mind that it may be challenging at first, but the end reward is far greater.

I would recommend Ubuntu, wolvix, vector linux, for beginners {desktop centric / easy}

Later, you may want to move on to Debian, slackware, Freebsd, arch, and gentoo --- -These are very challenging ones, but supposedly major fast and extremely reliable for server purpose.
