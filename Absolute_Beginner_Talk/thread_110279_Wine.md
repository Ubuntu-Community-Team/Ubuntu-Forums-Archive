### Post by DoorGunner on 2005-12-30
Wine is a program that tries to manipulate win32 files. It isnt an emulator. This means that it doesnt convert files to linux. It manages them.

You might try Crossover, It is a comercial version of Wine. It works with Macromedia. As for Sibelius I think that there are some music synthesisers around that may do what you want. If you have a good knowledge of ming c++ etc F4L is available (flash for linux) It is quite a bit more than the average flash user is used too. Hopefully it will be developed more for my flash level in the next year or two.

Your best bet might be just to dual boot and leave some of your programs on Windows. Wine isnt the greatest program. I have found very little to work on it. Just check the program listings and find out what the final status is. A lot will say garbage. Especially with popular games.

I am finding for myself that most windows applications that i used to use are available in most linux varients. In that i mean that linux comunities have put forth similar programs. These programs have been developed by a community that have put a lot of thought into their products and in most cases work better than their windows counterparts. 

I first tried linux in 1998 with Red Hat. It was far from being a keeper. Keep in mind it was grass roots back then and has a far more developed community now. In 2005 i tried linux again and I have 3 varients on my computer. Ubuntu, Gentoo and Fedora Core. Each has a different learning experience and are worthwhile. Linux has come a long way.
