### Post by scramasax on 2008-03-17
> **holyravioli said:**
> Hello, I am looking to buy a desktop pc that comes with ubuntu pre-installed.  Are there any companies besides dell that have these?  Thanks for the help.

If you look at the forum list:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=173](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=173)

... you'll see there are only two hardware vendors that Canonical has forums specifically for.  The two that've already been mentioned.  That suggests to me that the pickings elsewhere are likely to be slim.

Of course, others are offering pre-installed Linux but not always to home users and not always Ubuntu.  If you buy a machine with some distro other than Ubuntu pre-installed, that machine will likely run Ubuntu fairly well as well.  One columnist recently reports that on a new Lenovo, Ubuntu will run well, but there were niggles:

[http://www.cnet.com/8301-13505_1-9889681-16.html](http://www.cnet.com/8301-13505_1-9889681-16.html)

I expect it's SuSe that Lenovo offers pre-installed for those of its customers who want Linux.

So for the best out-of-the box experience, it probably does comes down to Dell or System76.  Which of those you can get depends on where you are.  AFAIK, System76 don't ship outside the U.S.  But I think Dell's Ubuntu machines are available in several European countries now, too.

### Post by bigfootnmd on 2008-03-18
Holyravioli!

About six weeks ago I was considering getting a new Desktop computer with Ubuntu on it.  Although Dell sells the Inspiron 530n with Ubuntu 7.04 preloaded I found their prices a little high.  So, on the dell main page I clicked on Home/office and then I click on Dell outlet.  I got a DELL Inspiron 531s with 2 gigs ram, 250 HD, windows vista basic, Nvidia Geoforce 63XX graphics.  I shrunk the Vista partition (in half) and I loaded Ubuntu 7.1 on the other partition. I got my 531s for about $300.  You can find similar offerings on the Dell outlet page.  I kept VISTA basic because there are times when I do need to run in Windoze (like running TURBO TAX to do my income taxes).

The only change I have made to 531s is to put in a XFX Geoforce 8400 low profile 
PCI-express 16 graphics card.  

Good luck.

Your most obvious choices then are Dell and System76.  Both have boards on this forum for support, which may be useful:

System76:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=158](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=158)

Dell:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=256](http://ubuntuforums.org/forumdisplay.php?f=256)

There are probably others outlets, but at any rate have a look at the offers on both those companies' websites and see what deals you like the look of.

As has been said, it may turn out that you can get  the same Dell models as are certified as able to run Ubuntu but coming with Windows on and no more expensive, or possibly cheaper.  So long as you know that it definitely is one of the models that they say definitely will run Ubuntu it then comes down to how "hardline" you are -- some people won't buy pre-installed Windows because, obviously, some of the money goes to Microsoft, and they don't want to put money in Microsoft's pocket. For others that's not a worry.
