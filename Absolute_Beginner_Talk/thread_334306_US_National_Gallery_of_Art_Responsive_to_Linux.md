### Post by Old Jimma on 2007-01-08
Hi Ubuntuers:

I was browsing thru the US National Gallery (NGA) of Art web pages and came across virtual tour pages (for example, try [[COLOR="Sienna"]http://www.nga.gov/exhibitions/vgwel.shtm[/COLOR]]("http://www.nga.gov/exhibitions/vgwel.shtm"). The NGA virtual tours require Quicktime... and this does not work on my machine (Dapper). When I clicked on the virtual tour links, gxine came up, but worked really badly! So, I COMPLAINED to NGA that they should use software that allows open source users like use to have a look, also. Here is their encouraging reply:

[B]Dear Mr. Smith,

Thank you so much for contacting us regarding our Virtual Tours. I am sorry that you are having difficulty viewing the VR tours. We created many of these tours prior to the popularity of open source software and as such, we have used Apple’s QuickTime software to view them. We are currently looking to reevaluate all of online media choices and either recreate the more popular features or do away with them all together.

I assure you that our mission is to present the collections of the National Gallery of Art to the nation and we take your criticism seriously. If you have the name of open source VR Tour solutions we should consider, we would be happy to hear them.

Once again, thank you for interest and your feedback.

Web Feedback at the National Gallery of Art[/B]

So, what should I tell these guys? What opensource solutions should they use?

Thanks!
Phil Smith
Duluth, GA
