### Post by mrgnash on 2006-04-05
The question is not 'Why Doesn't Ubuntu Just Work' but why device manufacturers don't provide adequate driver support for Linux. For that reason, as others have said, the onus is on you to select devices which are compatible with Linux/Ubuntu. 

Yes, it is a bit of a hassle. Apple get around this by producing most of the peripherals for the Mac essentially 'in-house,' Windows gets around it by having the largest market share, but with Linux it requires a bit of extra research on your part.

That said, sometimes devices that don't work under one kernel will work under the next because hardware support is constantly improving.
