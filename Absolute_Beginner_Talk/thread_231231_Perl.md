### Post by geek_Man on 2006-11-17
I would recommend Programming Perl by O'Reilly. It gives you an in-depth look on everything it covers. You might be a little nervous about the price, but I checked it out at my library, so maybe you'd be as lucky. Some of the stuff in it is a little scary, but if you did C++ then I guess you'll be fine. And some of the the stuff you'll be like "Why the heck would I need to do that?". It compromises simplicity for completeness (is that a word?), but overall it's a great book. You might also try some Perl forums.
