### Post by Sephiriz on 2005-07-17
Muine is great, but overly simplistic.  XMMS, to me, is too clumsy to use, and I never really liked the tiny layout of it.  amaroK is great, even when using Gnome :)

I started with XMMS, moved on to Muine, and was temporarily satisfied.  Its really easy and fun to use, but I got very irritated when I found out that it had difficulties playing songs with long titles, that or it just couldn't traverse directories well.  In any case, I moved on to amaroK which I just downloaded via Synaptic.  Works like a charm, beautiful layout, easy navigation.  Only thing I found slightly humerous is that it doesn't consider the output engine a necessary part of the download in Synaptic, so you got to make sure you download that as well.
