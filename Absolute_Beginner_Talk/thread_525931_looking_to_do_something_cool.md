### Post by some_random_noob on 2007-08-14
If its non-computer savvy people then go for Beryl - find out if your graphics card is supported and keep at it. In my opinion, I think that Beryl is the only worth while thing to show non-computer savvy people. Linux does heaps of cool stuff, but most of it is really "1337" behind the scenes kinda stuff.

Just try out Beryl. If that doesn't work then maybe try the Sabayon live cd ([www.sabayonlinux.org](www.sabayonlinux.org)). Apparently it comes with pretty visuals pre-installed, although I haven't tried it myself. Alternatively, Linux is free and OpenSource - what could be cooler than that? If your demonstration is about looks, then you're out of luck (unless you can show them videos). But if its meant to be informative then there's heaps of things you could tell people.

Random tip: If you plan on showing people Ubuntu, try out Kubuntu: its much prettier and looks more like Windows! (sky blue/white colours) The default "Earth" theme is terrible!
