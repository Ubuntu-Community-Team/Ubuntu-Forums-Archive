### Post by mikeincal on 2008-02-18
I have just loaded ubuntu and some of it's variants on a bunch of old computers, and I have some results that may help you make a decision. I have Xubuntu running on an old IBM 300Mhz laptop with 130 mb of RAM. It is a bit more than your listing, so I second the memory upgrade option if possible. I had originally tried Debian with the IceVM desktop, which worked, but was not user friendly enough for the recipient.  I had to load Fluxbuntu on an old HP laptop, which could not handle Xubuntu due to memory restrictions. If I recall, it had about 96 mb of ram. I had to load PuppyLinux on an old 486 with 64mb of ram, which worked quite well. For your requirements, it looks like Fluxbuntu or Puppy, both of which I have used quite a bit. Considering your requirement, Puppy is not to be completely discounted, it will do the job, and it's hardware support is actually somewhat surprising. Puppy actually picked up my wireless cards in other computers without incident. However, Fluxbuntu will easily get you into the ubuntu software repositories for convenience. Decisions, decisions! Good luck and happy Linuxing!
