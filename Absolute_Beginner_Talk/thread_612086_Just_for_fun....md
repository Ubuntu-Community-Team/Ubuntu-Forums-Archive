### Post by Shiva88 on 2007-11-13
Certainly!

DSL is the obvious choice.  I actually used DSL a few years ago on an old laptop as my introduction to linux.  It worked a charm.  [http://damnsmalllinux.org/](http://damnsmalllinux.org/)

Another option *might* be Vector linux.  It provides a more full-fledged "real distro" feel, at the cost of higher system requirements.  I forget the exact specs of the system I ran it on, but the machine had originally had Win 98 on it, so Vector might be a little beyond your machine.  If not, I'd give it a shot.  It's always easy to reformat and through DSL on there if it doesn't work out!  [http://www.vectorlinux.com/](http://www.vectorlinux.com/)


There are other distros made for this purpose as well, Puppy Linux comes to mind, but I haven't any experience with them and will therefore refrain from comment.  :)
