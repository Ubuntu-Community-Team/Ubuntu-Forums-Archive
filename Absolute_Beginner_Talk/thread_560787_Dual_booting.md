### Post by ddrichardson on 2007-09-26
You can do dual boot in 40 Gb, depending on your needs. I've an eMachines notebook with a dual boot in 40Gb:  using 8Gb for Ubuntu, 640Mb swap (i subscribe to the equals memory theory) and the rest for Windows.

Windows is surprisingly disk hungry, but I haven't had any problems and there's stil around 10 Gb free on the Windows drive. The system has been one of my main systems for over a year as well.

If it's just music, consider an external drive, they're pretty cheap.
