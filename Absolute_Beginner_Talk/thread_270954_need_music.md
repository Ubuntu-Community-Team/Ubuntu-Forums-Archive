### Post by skymt on 2006-10-05
> **macogw said:**
> Actually, they wanted iTunes.  I suggested paid LimeWire.  The paid one is legal, right?

Erm. No. You pay for the software, but the songs downloaded are free and illegal. You may be confusing it with Napster, which used to be free and illegal but is now paid, legal, and Windows-only.

There are a few good places to get legal music on Linux. I like [eMusic](http://www.emusic.com/) and [Magnatune](http://magnatune.com/).

One big problem you'll run into: there's no easy way to play iTunes-purchased music in Linux. You need to burn each track to a CD using iTunes, then rip the CD to get an un-copy-protected version.
