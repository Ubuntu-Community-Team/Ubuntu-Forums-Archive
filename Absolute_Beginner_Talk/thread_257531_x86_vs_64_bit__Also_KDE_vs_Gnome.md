### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2006-09-14
> **Fish Stalker said:**
> New to linux and ubuntu, but a seasoned windows user.
Welcome. As you are finding there is a steep learning curve with the transition to Linux.

> **Fish Stalker said:**
> I'd like to know the difference in running x86 vs 64 bit ubuntu?  Will I see a performance difference? x86 is 32-bit optimized. I suggest you install the i686. Once you learn the ropes you can give the 64 bit kernel a go. Performance difference will likely be minimal. 64 bit takes a little tweaking and you may not want to take on too much at once.

> **Fish Stalker said:**
> Also, what problems will I have running KDE with ubuntu instead of Gnome.  I've read you can't update Kubuntu as easily as ubuntu, thus the reason for trying to run KDE.
No serious problem, small problem with bloat. Lets say this is the first test of your Linux prowess.

KDE has a look and feel more like windows and is often easier for (some) noobies.

On the other hand I suggest you look at XFCE as well.

Try all three and you will find you settle into one or the other for personal reasons.

You can also try "light" window managers: IceWM or Fluxbox. You may want a little experience under you belt first.

Last start with your learning by becoming comfortable with the CLI. It will pay off in spades.
