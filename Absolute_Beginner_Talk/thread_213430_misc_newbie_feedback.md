### Post by jayk123 on 2006-07-11
Ubuntu setup bugs

There are many.
If you just have it take over your whole disk, ok.
But anything else and you hit bugs.
I have had it "crash" multiple times in something related to mount points.

I am trying to use reiserfs and I am trying to multiboot with Mac OS.

First something simple.
When one of those progress bars was up "starting partitioner"
I closed it to see what would happen.
Thereafter several parts of the ui come up in a very small unviewable window.

Ok. Setup is very keen on formating. Including reformating reiserfs
partitions as ext3. Including reformatting hfs+ partitions as ext3 and
defaulting them to mounted at / even when there are other Linux (reiserfs)
partitions.

Setup does not often synchronize with changes made outside setup
like with gparted or mac-fdisk.

I've only used Ubuntu a little bit and it does seem quite good, for Linux. Still not as good as Apple or Windows out of the box.
But then Linux in general seems to have progressed these past few years.
Suse Linux is also very very impressive. The only problem with it is that it
seemed a little sluggish on my 500MHz iBook.
I could say other good things about Suse. It's purely network based setup was nice.
Gentoo also had its good points but I don't want to manually setup so much.
Kubuntu seems sluggish.
I will try Xubuntu.
Ubuntu has problems with the time and the timezone.
It is easy to get /etc/passwd forward dated and su stops working.
Kubuntu's defaulting to Konqueror instead of Firefox seems like a big disadvantage.
Suse has a better key mapping -- the Apple key works.

Setup says "starting partitioner" like two or three times, wierd.
Hitting the next/forward button doesn't seem to work half the time, wierd.

It took me a long time to figure out that the eject button is the right mouse button and that was only because I was trying to eject the CD. (I'm on an iBook).

 - Jay
