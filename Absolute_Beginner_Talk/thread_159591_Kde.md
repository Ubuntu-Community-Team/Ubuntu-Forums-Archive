### Post by niviche on 2006-04-13
[QUOTE=Caligula]I want to try KDE, see if it's better than Gnome, I can download it through synaptics.
what happens after I install it? does it give me a choice between Gnome and KDE or is it automaticaly deleting Gnome?[/QUOTE]

To install it, get "kubuntu-desktop" through Synaptic. At one point, you will be asked if you want your default session to be Gnome (GDM) or KDE (KDM). You can choose whatever you want.
Then, when you'll log in to your system, you will be able to choose what session you want, and both systems cohabit nicely.

Have fun with KDE. And don't be put off by your first impression, it's an understatement to say that the default settings don't look so great.
