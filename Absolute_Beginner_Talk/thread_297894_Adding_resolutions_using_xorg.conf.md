### Post by confused57 on 2006-11-12
You can change your gdm login screen resolution by editing your /etc/X11/xorg.conf file.

First backup the file:
```
sudo cp /etc/X11/xorg.conf /etc/X11/xorg.conf_backup
```

open the file:
```
gksudo gedit /etc/X11/xorg.conf
```

Toward the bottom of the file, there is a line that says what your Default depth is(usually 24)...scroll down to the depth 24 resolution entries line and make 1024X768 the highest resolution in the line with the list of resolutions to be used.  Quit gedit and save the file.
You may be able to just logout, or Ctrl+Alt+Backspace, may have to reboot for the new settings to take effect.

I'm on Windows now, so I can't give you an example of my xorg.conf file...if you have problems, I can log into Ubuntu.

### Post by Velotix on 2006-11-12
Frankly, the fact you can get your monitor to work in higher resolutions than its meant to is simply a total fluke. You're just lucky. If you want your login manager to work properly, you'll have to drop down to 1024x768 or write the graphics card driver yourself.

Your monitor's from 1996, so back then 1024x768 would have been considered an extremely high resolution and would have been the largest resolution tested for absolute compatibility with your monitor. My experience with CRT monitors though is that the technology in them is robust enough to defy their time periods and become more useful and powerful as other technology catches up. You can thank your graphics card for the improved resolution (mine has a theoretical maximum resolution of 2048x1536 @ 60Hz, for example, but is forced lower than that by my monitor). :)

Why LCD monitors have no horizontal/vertical resizing is a mystery to me: it really hurts their versatility. :(

Oh, and LCD monitors have a native resolution of 1280x1024 because their height to width ratio is actually 5:4 instead of 4:3. Despite this, they display 4:3 images without any problems whatsoever. Mine does, anyway, though 1280x1024 definitely looks much better. ^^
