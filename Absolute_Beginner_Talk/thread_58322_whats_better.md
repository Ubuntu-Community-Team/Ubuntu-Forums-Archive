 I used SuSE 9.1

the .deb packaging system, with along with apt are the biggest reasons to switch.  I had tried to get apt4rpm working properly, and never really got the hang of it.  Ubuntu, being a debian derivative, had apt natively, and I jumped.

Without apt4rpm, SuSE was a pain to install new sofware on.  RPMs lead you down the path of dependency hell, leading you to download packages that need more packages that need....etc.  If you don't do it right, and force things, the system will be b0rk3n...

apt simplifies this.  that was a huge enticement.

### Post by aysiu on 2005-08-19
[QUOTE=sunrex]i love parts of gnome, but i also love parts of KDE, whats better for ubuntu?[/QUOTE] Either. That's why there's Kubuntu (defaults to KDE) and Ubuntu (defaults to Gnome). But nothing's to stop you from installing both KDE *and* Gnome on one distro (SuSE can have Gnome, too). I like Gnome better because it tends to run more quickly and can look just as good, given the right theme. I've also found themes easier to install in Gnome (usually it's just clicking "Install theme"). In KDE, I had to do a lot more ./configure and cutting and pasting to /usr/share/icons and such to get themes working.
