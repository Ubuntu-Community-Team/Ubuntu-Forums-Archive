### Post by gh0st on 2007-01-23
I think you're first step should be getting your dual-boot working happily then I reckon you'll find loads of great Linux alternatives to Windows programs. It all depends what you use your machine for, only you know that.

Here is a list of quick alternatives:

Internet Explorer --> Firefox
Outlook --> Evolution or Thunderbird
MS Office --> Open Office
Winamp --> Amarok, Rhythmbox, Songbird etc
MSN Messenger --> Gaim

Most of this stuff is pre-installed with Ubuntu. Personally I spend most of my time on the PC reading emails, surfing the web and instant messaging. Ubuntu can do all of them easily. Your first step should be getting Ubuntu installed and trying out some of the apps I reckon. Then maybe look at getting your bookmarks out of IE and into Firefox, emails into Evolution, all that stuff. Just go at your own pace and you'll be there in no time. I think you'll find it's easier than you think, go on jump in :-)

P.S - Incidentally WOW is supported by Wine and Cedega under Linux, I don't play it personally so I dunno if it's tricky to set up, just a suggestion.
