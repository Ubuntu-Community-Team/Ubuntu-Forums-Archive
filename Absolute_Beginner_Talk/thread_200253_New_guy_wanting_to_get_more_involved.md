### Post by IYY on 2006-06-20
I'd suggest finding a good book about Unix, GNU/Linux, or Debian (in a store or the library. I wouldn't suggest books that deal specifically with Ubuntu). You could also try some of these free online books I found very useful: [http://www.tldp.org/guides.html](http://www.tldp.org/guides.html)

Learning the basic and advanced commands, and shell scripting is very easy, but very important to reach the "next level".

### Post by n00b@linux on 2006-06-20
I'd recommend buying a book that deals specifically with Ubuntu such as "Beginning Ubuntu Linux: From Novice to Professional" by Kier Thomas.  I started with SuSE 9.3 before coming to Ubuntu and I found his other book - "Beginning SuSE Linux: From Novice to Professional" - invaluable.  I still refer to some of the text today when I get stuck - even though I'm working with Ubuntu.  I'm a case on point that linux is *basically* the same no matter what the distro!
