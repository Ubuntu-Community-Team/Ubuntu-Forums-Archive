### Post by starcannon on 2008-02-07
I bought a cloudbook, do NOT waste your money on a cloudbook, its a real heap. Warranty is worthless, already have had a HDD failure, via gpu and chipset, this thing was built for XP not gOS.
Get an Eee, these little guys rock once you set them up correctly.

I tried eeexubuntu on my Eee, i ended up ditching it and just installing a full Gutsy install, this seems to work better for me, your mileage may vary.

Really after you get everything up and running and do the essential hacks this thing works nicely with a full Gutsy install, the only major issue I have yet to resolve is how to get ACPI to resume correctly with the MMC Slot, my 16gb SDHC card never works right with suspend/resume, which is important to me as I put /swap and /home on that drive. If I can figure that out then this thing is nearly perfect.

I am considering buying an Everex Cloudbook, it comes with a 30gb HDD and would not require me to figure out this problem.

Asus Eee 4G-701, 2gb RAM, 16gb SDHC, Ubuntu Gutsy Gibbon 7.10
