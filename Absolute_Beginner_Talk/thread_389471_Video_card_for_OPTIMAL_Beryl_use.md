### Post by igknighted on 2007-03-22
I am using a 6600GT (pcie) and its great.  It's only got 128mb of vram, but the ram is so quick (gddr3) that it flies.  At this point, since no card in that range with dx10, anything is equally future-proof.  Therefor, go with you budget.  Note: get a card that is a "pro" or "gt" model.  The "le" models are stripped down.

As for the buggyness, I don't notice anything.  Install your video drivers (the hardest step, and its easy), add the beryl repo (see the wiki on beryl-project.org for instructions, its the best way to go), and then install through apt/synaptic.  You will need to add ARGBGLXVisuals manually to the screen section of xorg.conf (covered in the beryl wiki), but other than that its all just "apt-get install".  With beryl 0.2.0 I haven't found a single bug yet.
