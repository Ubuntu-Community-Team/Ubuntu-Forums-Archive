### Post by Sarteck on 2007-12-04
All around these forums, I ask for help and advice on my Ubuntu issues.  In turn, I help with what I know, as well.  When I have a problem that's either obviously very rare or obviously very common, I try to search (ironically, getting better results with Googling this forum than using this forum's own Search feature), and often I can find the solution.  (Sometimes, if there are multiple solutions, I'll ask here anyway, to find out what the differences are, and which ones will work for me, etc.)

Now, that's all good and well.  I get this nice warm fuzzy feeling knowing I've helped someone out with the same problems I've been having and reading about...  But I -really- want to help.  I want to do more than just sit as a lump behind my computer, watching porn (if the girlfriend is asleep, heh heh), playing video games, and putzing around with web/database design.

I want to develop applications, I want to write code, I want to get my work out there and in use by people from around the globe!  I want to stick my bare hands down into the gorey depths of ones and zeros and carve out my own little niche!  I don't care about the glory, and I obviously don't care about the pay.  I just want to learn, and to... well, to do!

### Post by macogw on 2007-12-04
[How to Think Like a Computer Scientist: Learning With Python](http://www.greenteapress.com/thinkpython/thinkCSpy/) by Jeff Elkner (an Ubuntu member) is a great resource to learn to code.  Python makes it easy to ignore crazy syntax rules and focus on learning what you're doing, how you're doing it, and why you're doing it that way.  That book really does teach you the right way to think.
