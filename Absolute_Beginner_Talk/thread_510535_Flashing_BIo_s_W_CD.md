### Post by kyphi on 2007-07-27
BIOS are best left alone unless you have a particular problem or are experiencing malfunctions.

BIOS information fits easily onto a floppy disk and that is simpler than trying to create a CD (it is also less wasteful).

I have never flashed my BIOS via Linux so cannot advise you on that.  I have done so on a number of computers running Windows.

Your mainboard manufacturer's  site will have all the information you need on how to do this as well as a downloadable new BIOS.

Before you jump off the deep end here, as your computer loads, access the BIOS and check to see what version you have.  It may already be the latest version.

You can also see the BIOS number on the first black bootup screen after identification of your graphics card.

Be aware, if there should be a power failure while you flash your BIOS, you will trash your computer and recovery procedures get to be very involved.

BTW nice choice of mainboard.
