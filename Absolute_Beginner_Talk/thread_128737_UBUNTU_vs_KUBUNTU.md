### Post by gingermark on 2006-02-12
I started with GNOME with Warty, but now KDE is my environment of choice. I think it is to with the default apps - compare the configuration options available from the GUI with programs like Kaffeine and KAudioCreator and compare them with Totem or Soundjuicer.

Now I use a lot of Gtk apps in KDE - Gaim, The GIMP, Grip, but they are all nicely configurable. I think Ubuntu and Gnome have taken simplicity in their default apps too far. KDE could sometimes be seen as bloated, but you can configure every element of it's behaviour from a GUI, whereas GNOME seems to only give the most basic options.

A big generalisation, obviously, but I never felt completely in control when using GNOME, and now I've grown to love KDE and am happy with it!

But choice is what Linux is all about, and that's what's so great about it...
