### Post by sKAApGIF on 2007-06-17
It is very possible and infact can be very easy.  I wouldn't recommend installing ubuntu however.  It is made for harddrive installation and installing it on a flashstick will most probaply give you a less than favouable performance.

Depending on your needs I would go for either [DSL]("http://www.damnsmalllinux.org/") (Damn Small Linux) or [Puppy Linux]("http://www.puppylinux.org/"). [More about puppy]("http://puppylinux.org/wikka/AboutPuppy").  

Unfortunately I couldn't really find a tutorial that covers it from tip to toe.  I'd be happy to help you further e.g. to burn the cd, boot from it, choose the correct menu option, and finally run linux from your flashstick.

I don't know how much experience you have, for me to write a whole document would be an over kill if you have the skills.  I will therefore just state the basic principle and you can ask me about anything that you get stuck with.

The procedure is relatively straight forward.
1. Choose which one of the two you want to use, or maybe decide to give both a try.
2. Download the cd image from their websites: [puppy]("http://distro.ibiblio.org/pub/linux/distributions/puppylinux/puppy-2.16.1-seamonkey-fulldrivers.iso") [DSL]("ftp://ftp.oss.cc.gatech.edu/pub/linux/distributions/damnsmall/current/dsl-3.3.iso")
3.  Write the iso to a cd (how to do this depends greatly on which operating system you're on so if you don't know you can just ask).
4.  Put the cd into your computer and restart, in most cases you'll see either DSL or puppy starting up
5. Choose the option from the menu to make a bootable flashstick and follow the prompts
6. Set your bios to boot from USB-HDD or else USB-ZIP and put your flashstick in the machine.
7. Boot up your machine and hopefully see DSL or puppy all from your flahstick! :D

If you need anything more just ask :)
Good luck
