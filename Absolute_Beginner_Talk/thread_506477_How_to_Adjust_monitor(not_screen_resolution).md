### Post by kyphi on 2007-07-21
That depends on how your graphics card is connected - Analog or Digital.
The Monitor connectors as well as the connection to your graphics card look different depending on which system you use.
To upgrade your graphics card would be the easiest solution - but before you do that, check to see if your monitor has a digital connection point.

If you have a CRT monitor I think you might be out of luck but most LCD monitors should be able to accommodate both connection types.  After that, all adjustments are automated and require no user input.
