### Post by Hallvor on 2007-05-02
It depends what you are looking for. If you want large and popular files, you will almost certainly find them online through torrents. A few clients have been mentioned in this thread. 

However, if you are looking for rare material that you can`t find as torrents (ebooks, rare movies etc.), I`d use aMule because of the large userbase and number of shared files. aMule can feel very slow, because the upload of most users is not concentrated on a single file, but shared between several. Most users have hundreds of shared files, and perhaps up to 30 large files on their download list. So if you download a single file, and not many at once, aMule will feel very slow. But if you are patient, you will be rewarded with rare files.

For all p2p applications, it is often a good idea to change the default ports. You should always open the right ports in your firewall (of you have one) and with Firestarter to download faster.
