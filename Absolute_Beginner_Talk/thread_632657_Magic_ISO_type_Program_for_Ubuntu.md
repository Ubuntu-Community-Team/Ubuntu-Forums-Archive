### Post by tistria on 2008-05-07
The above teqnique (burn:///) is to burn data CD's (CDs for the computer), not Audio CD's (to play on the stereo).

If you wish to burn a RedBook type audio CD (i.e. to play in your car), go to the Applications menu > Sound and Video > (something) CD Burner.  In 8.04 it's called Brasero CD Burning.  I don't remember what it was in 7.10.

To burn an iso from a nautilus window to cd, right click on the iso file and select burn to disk.

Hope this clears things up.


EDIT:
> If you want to save images of disks though, you'll need something else (other than just nautilus) I believe

You can create an iso from nautilus by mounting a CD (it should do this automatically when you put it in your drive) and then right-clicking on the desktop icon that appears.  Select "Copy Disk," and then change the Copy To location from your burner to "Disk Image."
