### Post by Hospadar on 2008-04-03
Those older cpu's should be work ok If they are the same socket.  Some of the newer CPU's aren't transferable because the pins get squished when you install them.

Most of the older CPU's are zero-insertion-force sockets or the kind that you just press in, and use a special little cpu comb to pop out.  If it's the funny comb kind, you just have to pull it out *very* carefully to make sure you don't bend the pins

if it's a ZIF socket, there will be a little metal lever one one side of the socket, when you lift it up the cpu will just lift right out.

Just be careful with your cpu pins, make sure you are grounded so you don't static it, and be sure to put the CPU back in the right way.

You may also need to check out the bus speeds on your zee pee you, but since you are installing a faster processor, I don't think that should be an issue (usually you only get problems when bus speeds are too fast)

All that said, there's no guarantee it will work, but I'd suspect it will.
