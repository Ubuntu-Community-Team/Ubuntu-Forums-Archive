### Post by CatKiller on 2006-10-12
There is a way - not quite "autodetect on the fly", but sufficient. Have a look through the other threads, and **man xorg.conf** to find out what each of the bits do.

Then you'll want to set up your xorg.conf with two Monitor sections - one for the laptop screen, and one for the external monitor.

Then, in the Screen section at the bottom of your xorg.conf, you'll put in **another** Monitor line, referring to the Monitor that you've defined above. Comment out one of these Monitor lines by putting the # character at the start of the line. Whichever line has this character will **not** be used.

Save the file and use **Ctrl-Alt-Backspace** to restart your X server. Whenever you'd like to use the other monitor, edit your xorg.conf to uncomment the monitor you want and comment the monitor you don't and restart X.
