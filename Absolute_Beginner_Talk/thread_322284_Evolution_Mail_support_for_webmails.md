### Post by patagonik on 2006-12-20
Yes, it does work! In my case i use yahoo and works great. 
In some countries Yahoo obliges their users to subscribe first to a special service (in Spain it's free!!!) (Access POP3) that allows you to receive emails with Thunderbird or Evolution.  
If that's ok, then in evolution you should configure your account with something like POP server -> pop.yahoo.mail.com and SMTP server -> smtp.yahoo.mail.com Anyway, in the OPTIONS link at yahoo they will explain you what to do, normally.
I use evolution and i'm pretty happy with it.
I had hotmail too and it worked the same way.

Good luck!

### Post by mhenriday on 2006-12-30
Just a post to let other thread participants who may be having similar difficulties know that I finally managed to get **Gmail** and **Evolution** to work together. The problems I was having were due to an ambiguity in the use of the term «user name» when configuring my set up - sometimes it refers to «user name» proper ; sometimes to '«user name»@gmail.com.' My solution was to play around with these two alternatives until I discovered which was desired in each particular instance....
