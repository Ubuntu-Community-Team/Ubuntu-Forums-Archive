### Post by jeffathehutt on 2006-10-05
Generally speaking, upgrading to a newer version of Ubuntu is painless.  You shouldn't lose your data, and you shouldn't have to reinstall many (if any) programs.  You can use the update-manager, or synaptic, or others to perform the upgrade.  Ideally it will download the new packages from the internet, and install them on your computer without any problems.  The only exception would be programs you installed manually by compiling.  Those programs could need to be recompiled to work with the newer kernel.
