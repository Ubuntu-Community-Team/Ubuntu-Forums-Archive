### Post by H.E. Pennypacker on 2006-06-16
[QUOTE=Drakkor]"Badges, We don't need no stinkin' badges";lol
firestarter,graphics front end ?? idiot's explaintion. Please[/QUOTE]

You see, everything that goes on as soon as you turn on your computer is all text based.  Notice when you turn on your computer, you see a lot of text?  The  computer is always up to something, doing all sorts of things most of us don't know about.

That's where graphical interfaces come in.  Instead of confusing you to death with all the text that is really going on behind the scenes, you are able to view everything visually.  You actually get images that represent all the text.  For example, suppose you open up a file that is on your desktop.  Lots of things are being done just to open the file, regardless of the fact that it opens up so quickly.  What you, the average user, won't notice know is what *what is being done*.  All you see is some file opening up that eventually shows up on your screen.

The same applies to a firewall called Firestarter.  As someone already explained, Ubuntu does provide protect from the onset, but you're not able to see what's going on.  Firestart not only allows you to view what's going on, you can also change settings and do what you want to do.

I hope this is an easy explanation of what a graphical front end (graphical user interface, graphical interface, etc.) is.  It's basically something that humanizes   everything going on that most people don't pick up on by allowing people to see images.  I believe it's considered a fact that we're better at remember images than text, as humans.  I don't know if this is a fact, but in the world of computers, it's certainly true.
