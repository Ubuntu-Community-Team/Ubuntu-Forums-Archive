### Post by LaRoza on 2007-07-19
If you want to boot from the cd when you first turn the computer on, you can delay the startup by entering the setup or options menu in CMOS, (In a Compaq, press esc). You can then take your time inserting the disk, and either selecting the cd drive, or by exited the menu.

If you are like me, you turn the computer on and put the cd in as fast as you can. As long as it is in before what ever is on the harddrive loads, it will boot from the cd. (The Bios will look in the CD drive before the harddrive usually, if it sees a disk it will wait until it can read it before continuing.)

You can also eject a disk when you first turn the computer on in case you leave it in. (I do that all the time)
