### Post by amingv on 2008-02-21
If it's a CRT I usually use a soft towel moistened with glass cleaner.

Then compressed air for the rest of the unit.

### Post by MobiusNZ on 2008-02-21
The best way is to use monitor cleaning fluid and lint free tissues/cloths, which you can get at your local electronics store. I think phillips also do tissues that are already pre-moistened.

For the el-cheapo way, use a very SLIGHTLY damp soft cloth.

### Post by some_random_noob on 2008-02-21
I wouldn't use cleaning products if I were you. It's kind of dodgy. I'd only ever use a slightly damp rag. Or better yet, a proper monitor cleaning solution from your local electronics store. Here in NZ we have dick smith electronics (Horrible store btw) and they sell a decent monitor cleaning product. Just be careful how hard you press - otherwise you'll leave a mark. Also remember that LCDs don't have solid glass (You can press the screen back... eeek! not cool). Then again, you may have a CRT?
