### Post by Tux Aubrey on 2007-05-03
Linux distros don't compete with each other like companies - it is not considered disrespectful to try, like or even prefer another flavor.  I have at least 12 live CDs of other distros and really quite like a number of them.  I would really suggest that you take a week or two to get familiar with Ubuntu first.  Nobody claims it is the best at everything but It is a very easy intro to a a very big and confusing market.

Several other distros are very similar (eg Mepis), but others take a different approach (eg. puppy linux - which I personally love).  The further you stray away from Debian-based distros and the big Window Managers (Gnome and KDE), the more different things become.  Package (software) management and installation is one key difference.  

Enjoy your experience!
