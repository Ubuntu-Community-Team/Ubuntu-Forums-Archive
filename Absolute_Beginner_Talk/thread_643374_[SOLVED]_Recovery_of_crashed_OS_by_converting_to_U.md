### Post by amaroKer on 2007-12-17
Depends on your idea of realistic.  If realistic means it can be easily done, then my answer is yes.  In fact, you don't even need to install Ubuntu in order to access the mentioned software.  Depending on the components in your particular laptop, it is possible to have full system functionality just by booting the Ubuntu CD. 
I switched to Ubuntu right before I started at my present school.  I fortunately had a well supported laptop and every component worked as expected out of the box.

For use in the particular situation you've described, you might be interested in a product called [Mandriva Flash]("http://www.mandriva.com/en/product/mandriva-flash-2008").

In response to your last statement, it's okay to use Ubuntu as a band aid.  Part of the philosophy of freedom.  I've used it to rescue plenty of effed up Windows boxes.
