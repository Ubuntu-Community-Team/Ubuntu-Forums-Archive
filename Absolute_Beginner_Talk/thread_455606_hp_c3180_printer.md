### Post by H.E. Pennypacker on 2007-05-26
It is funny you ask this question about a week or so after I have bought this exact model.

Well, the good news is that this all in one works perfectly with Ubuntu Feisty (printing and scanning - haven't tried copying, because you don't need a computer to copy anything, therefore, compatibility is a non-issue here).  The bad news is that you'll likely have to pay a lot more than I paid for it (I got it brand new for $45 - sweet, huh?).  

I haven't tried all the additional features of the model such as the card slots.  I have tried scan button on the printer, but it apparently does not work.  You can still scan, though.  Just go to Applications > Graphics > XSane.  XSane detects it right away, and you can start scanning right away.

Installing is super easy...probably a lot easier than on Windows as some Windows users have been saying (some people mentioned a long and hard drive eating installation on Windows).  Just go through the Printing wizard on Ubuntu, and select the second one (it detects the same model twice, for some reason - just select the second line).  

I did my homework, and it paid off.  This is for you so you don't have to spend hours comparing different models, and checking to see what others have said.  If you can get this printer at a low price, go for it.  Even if you get it at its usual price (about $99), it beats its competitors.  I would never, personally, spend that much money on a printer (regardless of how good it is).
