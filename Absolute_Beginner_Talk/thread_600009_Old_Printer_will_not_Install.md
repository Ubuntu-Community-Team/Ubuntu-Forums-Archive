### Post by go2dell on 2007-11-02
Canon's support for printing under Linux has been abysmal, in my experience (but only for North American users).  It appears as though they don't support this printer under Windows XP either, so you may be better off just dropping the thing off at Goodwill and buying a newer printer from a reputable manufacturer.  While you're there, you may be able to find a printer with equally good quality that will work under Linux for around ten bucks because some poor soul couldn't get it to work under Vista.  :-D

That said, the CP-300 appears to have quite similar specs, so it may be that it's like several other Canon models:  the same internals housed in a different body.  There's a report on the CP-300 on linuxprinting.org that you may wish to use:  [http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Canon-CP-300](http://openprinting.org/show_printer.cgi?recnum=Canon-CP-300)

There's also a product called TurboPrint, but I didn't see the CD-300 as being explicitly supported.

Good luck!
