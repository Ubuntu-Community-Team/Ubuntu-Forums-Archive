### Post by Dynaflow on 2008-01-18
Welcome aboard.

There are a few good books on Ubuntu specifically and a great many good books on Linux in general that are full of ideas on things to try.

I would personally recommend *Running Linux* by Dalheimer and Welsh and the *Linux Cookbook* by ... someone or other whose name escapes me ... if you really want to start diving into the meat of the system.

If you just want a general preview of all the things you can do with Linux, click the Add/Remove Programs item under Applications in the GNOME desktop, select for All Available Applications, and start browsing for fun stuff to do.

### Post by charleseddy on 2008-01-18
While the other commentors gave you a couple good options, I'll throw in my two cents.  First of all, I apologize for your extraordinarily difficult experience with installation:  that shouldn't happen, and I encourage you to work with the installation team to make it easier.
Second, did you know that most movies with CG (computer generated graphics) are created with the software Blender, almost always on Linux?  For security, you may consider looking into firestarter (a firewall that's just a front end (GUI) for iptables) and Clam AV (the best anti-virus for Linux, next to the very expensive AVG professional.

If you're looking to make simple scripts and programs, you might look here: [http://docs.python.org/tut/node5.html](http://docs.python.org/tut/node5.html).  Learning Python is usally the first step.  Otherwise, basic scripts (like windows batch files) are created by typing lines of Linux command, which can be learned with a google search.

Otherwise, if you have more individual questions, feel free to ask questions personally.  I usually answer within eight hours of any given question, but I can't be too detailed on the forums.
