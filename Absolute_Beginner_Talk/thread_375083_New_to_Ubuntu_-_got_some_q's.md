### Post by Amenemhet on 2007-03-03
LOL, yes lots of questions but here goes.....
No, Ubuntu will not overwrite your existing partitions unless you tell it to.

Yes, you can access your windows parts from Ubuntu, in fact when you install, your windows drive (s) should appear on your desktop. You can read many many types of file systems thru linux, though windows will not see you linux files/partitions. ( I use linux generically, for those hard noses out there reading this) So no you do not need to copy twice as it were...I have never ever heard of destroying your hd by accessing files from linux(your bro on pills??)

To sum up, your files will not be lost, you will be able to access them with Ubunto as well, and you will not lose your recovery partition. Just be cautious when installing, and do not format the windows ones.

Hope this eases your mind somewhat...
