### Post by dstew on 2007-03-17
What "boot menu" do you mean? The menu to change your boot order is in your BIOS setup. It should not change, even if you install a new operating system.

---

### Post by mikewhatever on 2007-03-17
You can choose the boot order in the BIOS, which is entered by holding DEL or F10 or some other key at boot. If you haven't changed the boot order, GRUB does not change it either, so you should still be able to boot from a cd.

---

### Post by kilps on 2007-03-17
You need to setup your BIOS to boot from CD first .... when you turn on your computer it should say something like press F2 to enter setup - sometimes it is other keys such as DEL ... AFAIK most systems will tell you on startup what the key is though

The Ubuntu menu you see is booted by the BIOS - so once you see that it's too late to boot from the CD and you will have to go back and look for the setting again

hope this helps :)
