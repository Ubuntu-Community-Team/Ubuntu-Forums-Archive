### Post by igknighted on 2007-03-02
You certainly could install it on an HP laptop, its just nice to have one where everything comes preconfigured, as laptops are hit and miss.  I would search for laptops you are interested in, and then get the stats on them and check here or other sites for compatability.  I would suggest looking for an intel wireless card.  Also, Acer laptops seem to be well supported, they might be worth a look.  HP and IBM generally do a good job supporting linux, so they might be a good place to look as well.  Dell as well.  In fact, you might be able to get linux preinstalled on a dell laptop too.

[http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16834115346](http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.asp?Item=N82E16834115346)  Just as an example, but this might work OK.  I can't see all the specs, so I would email them first, but newegg is where I get all my computer stuff (best online place to buy, period).  Its not that much cheaper than the system76 stuff, but its about as cheap as I can find in a new laptop.  While the celeron processor this has will work for most basic tasks, I would recommend if you plan on keeping this a long time to look at either an Athlon or a Core duo, as you will get a much longer useful life out of the processor.  In 4 or 5 years the newer Athlons and core duos will be usable, while celerons and semprons most likely will not.

### Post by muep on 2007-03-03
I think RAM is the most essential spec to watch for with virtualization. Of course you need also space for every distro you install, but you also need RAM for every operating system you are going to run at once. 1 GB of ram should suffice well for basic use but more wouldn't hurt.

Processor shouldn't matter much, they are all pretty powerful nowadays.

I've never successfully used any virtualization software, but this is what I think.
