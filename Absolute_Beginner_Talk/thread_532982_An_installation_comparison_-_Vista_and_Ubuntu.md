### Post by mikex on 2007-08-23
I recently built up a new machine last week (AMD Athlon 64 X2 6000) and had to re-install Vista (my cloned image refused to boot up on the new SATA drive). I attempted to re-install it, but when I got into the process a ways, it complained I didn't have an existing OS on the computer. I had forgot I bought the Upgrade, and it had no option to allow me to insert the previous OS CD (XP) to proceed. I had to exit out and re-install XP from my original XP CD. I then attempted installation of Vista again, but this time I was met again with a message that I had to have XP Service Pack 2 installed! Ugh. So exit out and get SP 2 installed on XP, then re-install Vista again. Finally that worked, but then I was greeted with the "Activation is required" message. It wouldn't allow me to activate over the internet again, so I had to place a phone call to a human being to get a new activation code.

Installing Ubuntu was one step.

Quite a big difference in that respect.
