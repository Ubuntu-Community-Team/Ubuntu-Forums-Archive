### Post by lisati on 2007-08-08
Installation is normally a breeze, if you take the time to follow the instructions - feel free to 'have a play' if you are using a "LiveCD" (also known as a "desktop" CD0 before committing yourself

You should keep two important things in mind:
[LIST]
[*]Make sure you make a backup of important data
[*]If you want to make a 'dual boot' system, **DON'T** use the 'guided - entire disk' option when asked how you want to organize your partitions.
[/LIST]

### Post by BaffledMollusc on 2007-08-08
You have an ideal set up for dual boot already. You just have to install onto the empty partition (the one you're calling d: ).

Put the CD in the drive and boot into Ubuntu. Play around a bit. If everything works, click on the "Install" icon on the desktop and follow the prompts.  As lisati says, choose the manual partitioning option, and make sure you install Ubuntu on your free partition.

It should be a snap, but BACK UP YOUR IMPORTANT DATA FIRST nonetheless!
