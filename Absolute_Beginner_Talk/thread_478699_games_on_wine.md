### Post by cogadh on 2007-06-19
> **themuddaload said:**
> ooh "frankscorner.org" has a bigger list
Um, Frank on has about 100 titles listed (at most) AppDB currently has 1943 games in its list with ratings on working status and in many cases details on how to make them work. In all it has listings for 4553 different applications that have been tested with Wine. It is the most comprehensive source for Wine application info.

You may have just looked at what is shown on the start page of AppDB, which are just the top 10 apps. You need to click on the "Browse Apps" link on the left to really see what AppDB offers.

> **Codename said:**
> I think it's a one time thing.
Cedega is 5$ US a month with three months due up front. It is not a one time charge. You can purchase a full year subscription ahead of time for a small discount, but if you want to continue getting patches and updates for it, you need to renew your subscription.

### Post by cogadh on 2007-06-19
There is also a search function, if you want to look at what a particular game is rated. Also, don't assume that because an application is rated low that it won't be worth it. Sometimes that low rating has to do with the Wine version being used, more than anything else. For example, Knights of the Old Republic was rated as "Garbage" on Ubuntu 7.04 using Wine 0.9.33 (the Wine that is available from the Ubuntu repositories), but I was able to install and run it nearly perfectly using the latest Wine 0.9.39, so it got a silver rating.
