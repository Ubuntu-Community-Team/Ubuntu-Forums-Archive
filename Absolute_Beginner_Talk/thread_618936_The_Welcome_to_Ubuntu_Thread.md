### Post by tyggna1 on 2007-11-20
On behalf of the Ubuntu community and myself:  welcome to Ubuntu!   If you're reading this, you're either curious about Ubuntu or have just installed it.

Whatever your reason, welcome to Ubuntu!   As part of my welcome, I invite other Ubuntu-ers to welcome the new users, and offer a little bit of advise to get you started.

You'll find this out quickly, but best to warn you before hand--you have to read a lot.  No matter what you want to do, you'll have to read up a bit before you do it.  After you've read a lot then things will go much quicker--until then, expect to spend a few minutes a day reading how-to's, threads, and articles.  

Next [Linux is NOT Windows. ]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm") 'nuff said.

And the last few quick tips:   read the stickies, post details about your system in your signature, and help others when you can.

Again, welcome to Ubuntu!


<If you are new, feel free to post, and if you also want to give a general welcome, feel free to>

### Post by siciliancasanova on 2007-11-21
In my opinion, new Linux users, the best way I went through my experience is to understand that it's not better or worse than Windows.  It's just different.  It's a different way of achieving your goals.

Coming from a essentially all GUI user here, there are things that can be done quicker in Windows.  There are things that can be done quicker in Ubuntu, but your results will be the same.

It's like driving a car, whichever one you choose will do the same job so now you are going to make your decision based off of how well it steers, its safety, it's mileage, its ability to be serviced.
