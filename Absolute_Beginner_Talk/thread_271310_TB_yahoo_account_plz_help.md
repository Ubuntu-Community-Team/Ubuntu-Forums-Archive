### Post by AmandaKerik on 2007-02-01
There are instructions on the webmail site:

save the webmail and yahoo add-ons to your desktop
open thunderbird and open up the extensions window
drag and drop the extensions into the window (it's the easiest way)
restart thunderbird
open the extensions window, click on webmail, click on preferences.
in the first catagory (servers) and under the third tab (ports).
Put in a number like 1111 in the pop3
put a number like 1112 in the smtp
click ok, and restart thunderbird
open up the extensions again, click on webmail and click on preferences
in the first catagory (servers) and under the first tab (status) you should have two green lights.

After this... set up your yahoo account using "yourname@yahoo.com" in most places, the pop3 server as localhost, the ports matching the settings you just set.
