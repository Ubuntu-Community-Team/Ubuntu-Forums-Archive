### Post by dwhitney67 on 2007-09-21
Usually applications, such as xmms, amarok, etc., will provide an interface where you can adjust a virtual equalizer so that you can control the bass/treble.  These same apps generally also provide presets.  Other than that, don't expect to find any built-in controls within Ubuntu.

### Post by dwhitney67 on 2007-09-21
Try to understand that Canonical (Ubuntu) does not develop music/video applications.  It merely builds and distributes a distro that contains lots and lots of packages developed by other parties.  These packages are the same available to Fedora, Red Hat, Suse, etc.  Canonical simply puts a different spin on their distro, but all in all, it is the same as the others.

So you can spend all day downloading and installing 3rd-party applications to control your speakers and/or sound card, however these apps will not be from Ubuntu.

Like I stated earlier, use you favorite MP3 and/or DVD playback application to control your system's audio output.
