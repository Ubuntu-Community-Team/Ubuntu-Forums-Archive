### Post by MS_Past_Away on 2007-07-07
Hi everyone, this may be good news for some, I have searched high and low for a good replacement for Streets and Trips by MS in Linux. Recently I was able to install DeLorme Street Atlas USA 2005 with a Win 98 crossover bottle. Works great! I suspect that it would be possible with wine too. 

My wife is a map addict and she would not let me rest until I had something other than internet maps for her new Dell & Ubuntu laptop. We take road trips often and maps to navigate by are indispensable. DeLorme has GPS and many other goodies, some that MS does not offer in their product.  Such as the ability to change the heading direction of maps from north, south, east and west. I think it is as good or better than the MS product. DeLorme covers USA and Canada for streets and the world map has no street maps only a reference map with countries and cities.

I did have to fork over some cash ($40.00) for crossover (tried install with 30 day demo first) and my wife had a copy of DeLorme 2005 that they upgraded at her job so that was free. I don't mind losing some cash to support Linux developers.

Here is a link for the software products I used.

DeLorme Street Atlas USA 2005 (you can still find this, not sure if other DeLorme Atlas years will work)
[http://search.yahoo.com/search;_ylt=A0geu9Wyr49GQyMBbL5XNyoA?p=DeLorme+Atlas+2005&y=Search&fr=yfp-t-501](http://search.yahoo.com/search;_ylt=A0geu9Wyr49GQyMBbL5XNyoA?p=DeLorme+Atlas+2005&y=Search&fr=yfp-t-501)

Crossover 
[http://www.codeweavers.com/site/products/cxoffice/](http://www.codeweavers.com/site/products/cxoffice/)

I hope this helps some others looking for great street maps in Linux!

MS_Past_Away =D>
