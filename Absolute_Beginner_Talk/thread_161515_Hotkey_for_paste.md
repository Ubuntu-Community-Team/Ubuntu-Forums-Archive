### Post by bscbrit on 2006-04-17
An even easier way to transfer text is to highlight the text you want to copy (you _don't_ actually have to copy it, just highlight it) and then move to the terminal and press the middle (wheel)mouse button.  Done!

I don't know why, but not many people seem to know this....

It also works in most windows (e.g. nautilus, firefox etc).

> This might seem like a dumb question to many of you, but what is the hotkey for pasting text in terminal? When I try to use Ctrl+V, nothing happens, and I have to right-click and press "Paste".

To paste in gnome-terminal, use ctrl+shift+v. To copy use ctrl+shift+c.
 
It has to be like this as ctrl+c (normal copy shortcut) is used to stop a program running in the foreground on the terminal.
