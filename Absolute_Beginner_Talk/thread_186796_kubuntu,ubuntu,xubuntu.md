### Post by Blondie on 2006-06-02
Gnome and KDE are the two biggies in terms of desktop environments for Linux - like Pepsi and Coke. Xfce is more for people with older systems and uses less resources, though some people prefer it irrespective of their resources.

It's very hard to tell someone which is better for them. It's largely a personal taste thing. My advice would be to do a trial using both / all three and see which one you like best. You can have them all installed on the same system and choose which one you want to use at the login screen. Once you've tried them all you can uninstall the other two later.

If you really, really don't care then use Gnome. It's what the majority of (X)(K)(U)buntu users use and hence it will be easier to follow instructions on this forum etc. that are specific to it.
