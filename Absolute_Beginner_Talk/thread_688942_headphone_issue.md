Desktop? Laptop?
The reason I ask is with a desktop you could insert some kind of volume control between your soundcard and speakers. Harder to do with a laptop. Since most soundcard mfg's don't support linux this is a major hurdle to overcome.

### Post by emarkd on 2008-02-05
Since he uses his computer in the library, I'm guessing laptop ;)  I don't know about that jack sense issue, but I have the same problem on my laptop and I get around it by right-clicking on the volume control, turning the master down and the headphone up.  It's basically like they've got independent volume controls.  You may have to click on Edit > Preferences and add headphone to your controls.
