### Post by ikonia on 2007-05-16
I think that depends on what your doing and what the drivers will support and your config.

For example, if you laptop has the ability to spin down disks and throttle back cpu (for an easy example) then a windows driver is neededto control this through the operating system. IF the ubuntu drivers for the same hardware supports this technology and is configured then you'll find it to be at least the same.

My personal experience on an older P4 based laptop system (Thinkpad) and a reasonably up to date Centrino solo laptop as that in a comparative configuration / usage I get slightly more battery life out linux distribution than in windows.

Things to keep in mind.

Compiz - this is a hungry little bugger in terms of resources so your cpu may not rest as idle as normal therefore not scale down.


My laptop running ubuntu 6.06 with clearly no desktop wizzardy is the best battery life I've ever had out of anything. Totally fantastic.
