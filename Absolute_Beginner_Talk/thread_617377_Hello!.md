### Post by tyggna1 on 2007-11-19
Your best bet is to understand the Linux culture.   Most people here are willing to help you.
Here's some good links for you to try.  Also, I write a [newbie zine]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=542431") on the forums.   Some people have found that useful--but the [community documentation]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/") is also very good.   Google is invaluable.

To know what to expect, if converting from windows, read [Linux is not Windows]("http://linux.oneandoneis2.org/LNW.htm") (would've saved me a lot of heartache).    To quote myself, "[by switching to Linux] you no longer watch the news at night, you read the paper."   Get used to learning and reading.

And lastly, but certainly not least, read the stickied posts at the top of each forum--the forum admin rocks here.

### Post by zabien1970 on 2007-11-19
Welcome  chakotay2.

Good place to start?

Top left   Applications>Accessories>Terminal

Once you're in the terminal type man man then hit enter

'man' means manual, it will introduce you to linux, then you can continue on with other commands like man whereis which will tell you how to search for files, learning a few basic commands will welcome and make your experience more than you can imagine

A very good intro can also be found here:  [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsingTheTerminal](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsingTheTerminal)
