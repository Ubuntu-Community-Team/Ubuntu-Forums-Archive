### Post by Hendrixski on 2007-06-07
The difference is that the open source drivers  can be seen and edited by anyone.  So if there's a bug in them, or a malicious program, then anyone can see it and fix it.  However they're reverse engineered and for some applications may not be as fast.

The closed source drivers are written by ATI inc. and are closed source so you have no idea what they're doing.  For example they could be sending a live stream of your screen to the CIA headquarters and you can't prove that they aren't.  However, they're faster.  If you want Beryl, or games or stuff like that then you'll probably need those.  :-(
