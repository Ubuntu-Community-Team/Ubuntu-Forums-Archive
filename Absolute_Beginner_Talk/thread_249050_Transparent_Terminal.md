### Post by Metacarpal on 2006-09-02
In the regular Gnome environment, open the Terminal, go to your Edit menu and select Profiles.

Select "Default" and click Edit.

In the Effects tab, click the radio button and slide the bar all the way to the left.

This will not give "true" transparency, but will show your desktop background image.  So if you don't have other windows open, it's like being transparent.

For true transparency, you'll want to use Compiz.  But I can't help with that, because my graphics card just isn't nice enough for all that.  :(  Do a search - there are plenty of HowTos

### Post by nrwilk on 2006-09-02
> **HdK said:**
> I want to do this under the  KDE environment


In Konsole, go to "schema" within the "Settings" menu, and select any of the pre-made schemes with the word "transparency" in them.  You can also make your own in Konsole's options.

Note that this does not give you true transparency.  It will just show you a virtual image of your desktop background behind the window.  So, any other windows behind it will not show through.

To get true transparency, you have to go through a much longer and more involved process.
