### Post by giallofever on 2007-05-18
Not sure about you graphic card problem, but as far as building a machine goes... Linux is very flexible with how you configure your drives. You can triple boot all of them. As far as which drives to put them on, how large are the hard drives? If they are large enough, say at least 100GB, you could put all three OS on one drive and use the other for your files, downloads and such, so in case of error you can just redo your first drive. Some would say put both Linux distros on one drive and Windoze on its own so when you don't need it you can throw it out the window. :) (That's how I would do it personally.) Also Ubuntu, Kubuntu etc. all have the ability to store their /home folder on a separate partition. No matter how you install the OS's, one drive or two, you can always resize and edit your partitions later to suit your needs. It would probably be easier to triple boot all from one drive though.

There are plenty of forums and howto's on what software to use to reformat or resize disks, so figure out which way you want to install them first.
