### Post by arif_moin on 2007-10-03
I'm also using an Acer aspire 5050 w./ the same config as u in Ubuntu. I installed the 32 bit version.

Here is a basic rundown.

Broadband Internet (Asl25020 driver) works fine. No problems using the ATI 1100. The restricted device manager thing handled everything in a finch. However, you will (or at least should) note that Ubuntu finds a bug pointing to the bios when you boot after installation. 

I'm really not too satisfied w/ sound tho. At first the sound simply didn't work. Then I went to the volume mixer, and maxed out all the settings. Esp. the "surround" "PC speak" settings. Then I did get sound but it's still strangely very low in total volume. I can't think of a way to get it out maxed. I can still hear stuff, but it's a bit on the lower volume side. 

And as far as gaming goes- hey- you can play neverwinter nights :biggrin: The music in the intro sounds a bit screwed up, but after that, the game runs fine. I also tried the famous Vega Strike. But no sound or music...

IF you ask me you should have no issues in Ubuntu  & Acer aspire 5050 except for sound. And be careful when you upgrade to the latest kernel in Ubuntu. ALSA suddenly dissapears eventhough synaptic shows its very much installed. However, luckily in Ubuntu you can still boot to the earlier kernel so it's all good. 

But other than sound Ubuntu's top notch w/ Acer Aspire.
