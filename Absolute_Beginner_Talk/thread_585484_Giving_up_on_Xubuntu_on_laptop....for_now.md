### Post by hmlessalky on 2007-10-21
Well, I have been trying to make the switch, but it is just too much work to set up too many things to give up MS for now. I can see it is so hard to make the switch. 

I am keeping Ubuntu on my main dual boot terminal, but as for my laptops it is back to W2K. Here are my overall views on using the (X)Ubuntu so far. 

I like the terminal feature and all of the intensive info I can get. If I was a Linux guru I'm sure I could rock it. 

There are bugs in my installations, but the forums here make it easy to solve most of them. 

Wireless support seems to be the biggest problem I, and a bunch of others, have. It just isn't intuitive enough. After spending 4+ hours just getting Xubuntu to initialize my wireless card, I still was unable to configure it to ever connect using WEP or otherwise. And yes, I read all of the posts and such, and tried a ton of different methods. 

It did take a bit of time on both installs to get my display options to work correctly. But at around an hour each, it wasn't that bad. 

Anyway, sorry if this post is a bit redundant, but I just had to air out my observations. I guess I will be here while working out my Ubuntu install, but hopefully not for hours on end. I have really been missing sleep th last few nights....
