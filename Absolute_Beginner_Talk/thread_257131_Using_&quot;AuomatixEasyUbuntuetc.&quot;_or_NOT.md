### Post by Neobuntu on 2006-09-14
[http://www.getautomatix.com/](http://www.getautomatix.com/)

Under the Install link, make sure you find the match for your system. You are basically just adding Automatix to your sources list. Follow the easy instructions. Tripple click the command strings, then right click and copy them. Bring up a terminal, paste a line in and hit the <Enter> key. Do this with the following command lines in the directions. Simple cut and paste to get in installed.

Now you may click to run Automatix, off your "System" menu and check off what you want to add. Watch it do all the work. 

It will not keep checked what you did (like the normal package managers) but most things can be quickly removed (should you desire) from the easy package manager "Synaptic" (or many others.) Plus if you Install something twice, it will just indicate that it's already current. No problem.

The reason for Automatix is mostly political and due to anti-competition. That's why it's seperate. Plus, it's just more custom enhancments. Everyone has thier preference.

You can do many selection at one time but you will also have to watch it more because your password will have to be re-entered regularly and some installs have to ask questions. Don't worry, Automatix leaves a little text file with what it has installed in your home directory.

I recomend picking one thing at a time, watching it (Broadband recomended) and then enjoying that benefit. This combined with Ubuntu stability, is what gives you the newest AND rare combonation of stability; amongst ANYTHING!

It's Automatic.
