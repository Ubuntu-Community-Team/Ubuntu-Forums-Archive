### Post by annda on 2007-12-05
another cowon d2 user here. older iaudio players used to record to mp3, i guess they just bought a cheaper licence from microsoft... not sure about iaudio 7, because cowon is very secretive about product specifications - a VERY BAD sign for a (linux) user in general.

i'd stay away from ipods, cowon and samsung for a while. a lot of people are happy with their iriver players, so check the forums for those. personally, i love my old archos (a French product, probably not available everywhere).

as to linux-friendliness, check amarok and rhythmbox compatibility if you want to sync the player with your computer (i never do that), and if you don't, make sure you can do what you like to do with your player without custom software. anyway, connecting the player to the computer as usb storage is a must.

### Post by little_penguin on 2007-12-12
I've got the Cowon iAudio 7 and it works fine with Linux (Ubuntu Feisty), just drag and drop. I've never owned an Ipod so can't comment on that. The iAudio 7 plays mp3, flac, ogg, etc. has a built-in FM radio, a tiny video screen which is more a gimmick than anything, too small to watch a proper film comfortably, but okay for music videos. I have an Archos 504 as well, which is ideal when I want to watch videos on the move, so the small iaudio 7 screen doesn't bother me, I got it for audio purposes anyway and because it's small and easy to carry.
On the recording side, I've never used it myself but just checked the settings - the maximum is 128k for voice and line-in. It records in WMA format, can't see any setting to record as MP3 unfortunately, so looks like no.
