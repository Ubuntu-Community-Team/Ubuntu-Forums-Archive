### Post by ThomasMarkas on 2008-02-14
I have a Toshiba Satellite with the Intel 82830 CGC graphics card. This portable at some point stopped working on the LCD and I could not find out why.

I have just installed 7.10 and from the start it would only work in the external monitor.

I changed the driver from "intel" to  "i810".

- Started the computer in recovery mode.
- went to  "cd /etc/X11"
- Edit: "nano xorg.conf"
- Changed the lines as below
- Saved rebooted and was happy.



Section "Device"
Identifier "Intel Corporation 82830 CGC [Chipset Graphics Controller]"
Driver "intel"
BusID "PCI:0:2:0"
EndSection

TO

Section "Device"
Identifier "Intel Corporation 82830 CGC [Chipset Graphics Controller]"
Driver "i810"
BusID "PCI:0:2:0"
EndSection

Running the command is a terminal window, once Ubuntu had restarted, shows me that direct £D rendering is working. Even happier.


glxinfo | grep rendering
