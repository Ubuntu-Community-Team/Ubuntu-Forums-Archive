### Post by Jimmey on 2006-01-03
After YONKS of hesitation, I finally bit the bullet, and slapped the install CD into my laptop disk tray.

As you can see from the number of posts I've already made, I've been interested in Ubuntu for more than a while. Just never had the confidence, nor the support from my Dad to do it (lool).

But, today, I thought - If the liveCD works, there'd be no problem with Ubuntu on my PC. Worst case scenario - I'd delete Windows, or some secret partition with a Windows back up. Let's be honest - That scenario, although being the worst, wouldn't be much to cry about.

So it started off, just like the liveCD. Which was neat - I recognized all the crazy writing. So far, so good.

Then I saw "Loading the partitioner". Now, it was go time.

Then, up popped the partitioning menu. I couldn't have been more pleased. It asked to split the right partition on my hard drive. How clever's that? It did the job pretty much all by itself. All I had to do, was press enter. But, damnit, let's not let the install take all the credit. If it wasn't for me pressing enter, It'd be no where!

So, I'd told it to write the changes to the disk. I was unsure enough from the moment I put the CD in. But, then, the screen went...Blank, blue. Oh, boy, was I bricking it. I really preferred it when it was spitting writing at me. But, for this first time in my life, I thought I'd be patient. There's no harm in being patient. So, what I did is, I walked away - I left it to itself. 

Sure enough, after a while, It was asking for my help again - Yehp, within another few minutes, I was mashing the enter button, and staring in amazement as it spat yet more writing at me.

Took the CD out, restarted the computer. This was my first real introduction to Grub. Damnit, when you've pressed enter at the grub menu, don't you just feel like the most intelligent person this side of the planet Mars?

Starting spitting writing at me. And random hex codes. That just added to the "I'm so clever!!" feeling that I was already learning to love.

Then Ubuntu started. And, as the name suggests, it nearly brought a tear to my eye - What a wonderful sight!! After months of liveCD, I can finally install programs, and expect them to stay there. Etc. 

And the rest, as they say, was configuration. The wireless LAN works like, better than it did in Windows. And so, I thought that I might share my happiness with the users of the "Absolute begginner talk" forum.

It's made my new year. I'm - Chuffed.

Yay!!
