### Post by Casual Fan on 2007-12-23
So, this was the beginning of an odyssey. Assuming that my lockups were the result of my ATI card not playing nice with Gutsy, I tried to install the new ATI Catalyst 7.12 drivers--and ended up only being able to boot to a black screen with a blinking cursor in the upper left corner.

:confused::mad:

SO, I picked up the Windows XP disk that came with my desktop, thinking that I'd had enough of Linux and it was time to go back to an OS that "just worked."

An hour and a half later, I had no widescreen, no sound, and no wireless.

:confused::mad:

At this point, I downloaded the Feisty ISO (from my desktop PC), and now am running Compiz AND suspending with no lockups.

:)

The moral of this story: If you have an ATI card, save yourself some time and trouble and run Feisty, not Gutsy. Hopefully, between now and Hardy coming out, ATI's driver and Hardy will have learned to play together, or there will be a better open-source driver that lets you utilize your video card more fully.
