### Post by crispy_420 on 2006-09-19
Do you have your ATI card setup with fglrx driver?

Could use post the part of your xorg.conf that has your graphics card?

If you don't know where it is: /etc/X11/xorg.conf

I had a similar problem and it just involved tweaking my xorg.conf but I have slightly different hardware. I have a ATI 9800 Pro and a leadtek TV card (bt878).

Here is the part of my xorg.conf:

> Section "Device"
	Identifier  "ATI Technologies, Inc. Radeon R350 NH [Radeon 9800 Pro]"
	Driver      "fglrx"
	Option 	    "UseFBDev" "true"
	Option	    "VideoOverlay" "on"
	Option	    "OpenGLOverlay" "off"
	BusID       "PCI:1:0:0"
EndSection

Try taht and tell me if works. 

On a side note, is your TV card setup? Do you have the drivers installed?
