### Post by gn2 on 2006-08-09
Perhaps controversially I'm of the opinion that sometimes it is easier to change hardware than spend hours fiddling away with drivers trying to get a working solution.

Take Wireless PCMCIA cards as a case in p[oint. 
Why spend hours struggling to get a difficult one working, when it may be simpler to buy one with a proven Ubuntu track record?

For example a D-Link DWL650+ can be bought for less than £10sterling on eBay.
You may well be able to sell on your existing card at a higher price and make a small profit?

I know that part of the enjoyment of Linux is the tweakery, but some times there's more than one way to skin a cat...

Another way to look at this is that as more people migrate to Linux and want Linux drivers the manufacturers will be under more pressure to provide them. 
Buying proven Linux-compatible hardware (instead of fiddling about for hours) will help re-inforce this.

This is just a personal view I'm sure there will be others?
