### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-07-14
[URL="http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/installing"]
If your using the Live CD (also called Desktop) use this guide.[/URL]

[If your using the Alternate CD, use this one.]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p2.htm")

I assume your meaning you want to install Ubuntu over Redhat9, these guides cover a generic installation. Follow them through and you'll understand how it generally works. When you get to the partition section of your (i.e. when your actually doing it) you have a few different options. If there is no data on the drive you want to keep you can just tell Ubuntu to wipe all the drives and give you the automatic partition. This option (Telling Ubuntu to automatically install over the whole drive) will wipe any data off of it, be sure you backed up anything you want.

The other option if you have a home partition or other place storing data is to manually repartition the drive. If the partitions (the old redhat ones) are how you like them, just reformat them to ext3 and remount them to their right places (i.e. old root to new root).

Shouldn't be too hard, read over the guides and if you got any questions feel free to post back :). If you previously installed the redhat it shouldn't be too dissimilar.

Oh and once your up and running, please feel free to consult blue link (in my signature) for installing things. Just in case you need it.
