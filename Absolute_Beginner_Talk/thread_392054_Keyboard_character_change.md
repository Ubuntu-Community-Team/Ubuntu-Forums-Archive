### Post by xopher on 2007-03-24
System -> Preferences -> Keyboard preferences

^ This is where you setup your layouts, make sure the list of layouts has both English and Thai.

Then, right click on your gnome-panel, add applet. Search for a language-bar or something like that, you can move it after you've added it. That works just as in windoze - clicking it will change the layout.
