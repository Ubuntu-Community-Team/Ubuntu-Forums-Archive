### Post by Inxsible on 2007-07-09
If the 120 GB (sdb) is a external drive, you can use pmount to mount it. pmount will mount your drive under /media and auto mount it every time you connect it.

If its an internal drive, then you can create an entry in the fstab to have it mount automatically.

I see that both of your drives (10 and 120) have a Linux boot on them. Do you have multiple Linux OS'es installed ?

Also as a side note: you can use the same swap for both installs, so you don't really need to have separate swaps

### Post by Inxsible on 2007-07-09
In that case, you can mount your sdb1 as follows. Open up your fstab and add this line to it.
```
/dev/sdb1 /mnt/sdb1 ext3 defaults 0 2
``` You can change the mount point (/mnt/sdb1) to anything you want. Generally internal drives are mounted under /mnt, you can use names like, /mnt/My120GBDrive or /mnt/Seagate etc...

Mounting your sdb2 and sdb5 wont do you any good, since they are swap partitions and do not have any file system on them.

### Post by ugm6hr on 2007-07-10
I used this tutorial:
[http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/mountlinux](http://www.psychocats.net/ubuntu/mountlinux)

Just substitute "/dev/hda5" for "dev/hdb1", "/storage" for "/*any_mount_point*" (e.g. /media/Drive2), and "marie:marie" for "micke:micke"

It certainly worked for me with no problems.
