### Post by Lakefall on 2007-01-01
> **Linux Lover said:**
> after placing of the processor, what to do with the plastic cover? should i replace the plastic protective cover and place the heat sink over that plastic cover? or throw away the plastic cover and place the heat sink directly over the cpu?
I'm not sure what kind of plastic cover it is, but the only thing you should have *between* the processor and the heat sink is some kind of highly thermally conductive material, such as [thermal paste](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_paste) or a [thermal pad](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermal_pad_%28computing%29), not plastic. As far as I know, even just leaving the paste or pad out will cause modern processors to overheat. Putting something like plastic there should be even worse and you don't want to mess this part up, unless you want to risk destroying your processor.

### Post by gn2 on 2007-01-01
The plastic cover is there to protect the socket during transit, as the pins can be damaged otherwise. 
Keep it with the packaging in case you ever need to return the board under warranty.
More info here: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socket_775](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socket_775)
