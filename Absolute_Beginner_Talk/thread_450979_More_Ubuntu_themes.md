### Post by Skidpad on 2007-05-21
Ok, here's a copy/paste from somewhere:

[b]GTK+ themes will change the appearance of windows, their colors etc, and the look of panels and buttons.
Metacity themes will change the look of your window borders.
GDM themes will change the look of your login screen.
Icons Themes will change the icon set (but not always ALL of them)[/b]

Depending on the theme format, you can download/save the theme(file), and then drag it into the Theme Manager accessed through System>Preferences>Theme.  (Edgy...)

Give it a try and post back with any questions.  Oh, and please don't bump your own threads - at least not for the easy stuff ;)

Here's a dark theme: [http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Darker+theme?content=32768](http://www.gnome-look.org/content/show.php/Darker+theme?content=32768)

### Post by Moop on 2007-05-21
You need to install Art manager. Go to Applications-Add/Remove Software. Make sure that you select Show "All Available Applications" in the upper right of the window. Then search for Art manager. Once installed you can easily add themes, wallpaper, etc. 
Hope that helps.
