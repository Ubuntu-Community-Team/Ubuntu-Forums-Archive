### Post by gruhland on 2008-01-20
1) I would say a "warm boot" is a restart instead of a "cold boot" which means to switch off the computer and switch on again.
2) If you do not need the computer why leave it on? It costs energy (and therefore money) and makes no sense.
3) if you have a modem connection to your bank then it's a distinct line which is relatively secure. If you have an internet connection used to connect to your bank then everyone has theoretically a chance to access your computer. A firewall could be helpful against hack attacks. If you use a DSL line from home often your router has an activated firewall installed.
Linux is much more secure than Windows but no system will be 100% safe.
I use a DSL line to access internet (p.e my bank or something else) and my router has a firewall. That should be fine.

### Post by Trebaruna on 2008-01-20
A warm boot is indeed starting the computer up while the hardware was already on (eg. a reboot, reset, such things). Cold boot is powering on the machine.

Like indicated, Ubuntu has a firewall installed. It is setup in such a way that by default no ports are exposed: there is nothing for a potential 'malicious user' to connect to and attack on your machine.
