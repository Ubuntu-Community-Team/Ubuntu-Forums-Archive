### Post by twin_57103 on 2008-04-21
Linux is definitely a better choice for limited system resources - you've got a good plan.  If there's enough hard drive space, you may want to dual-boot rather than completely remove Vista (that way, you'll have it if she needs a program that only runs under Windows).

8.04 (Hardy) is heavier than 7.10 (Gutsy).  I'd suggest trying the live CDs for a couple days on both to see if there's any performance difference.  Also, wireless cards can be a headache under Linux - if Gutsy doesn't pick up her wireless hardware, maybe Hardy would do better; there's no way to know except to try.

If Hardy is too heavy for her machine, you may want to consider Gutsy (but I have also installed Gutsy on a P3 500 MHz clunker and had it run perfectly, so 8.04 on a newer low-end box might do fine).  I wouldn't bother with 6.06 - the newer versions are much nicer.

There are also tweaks for speed - you can search for them on the forums (sorry, haven't tried them).  Stick with 32-bit; you'll get better compatibility and there won't be any performance difference on a low-end machine.
