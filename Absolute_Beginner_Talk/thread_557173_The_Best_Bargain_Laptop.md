### Post by soaklord on 2007-09-22
Best?  That's really a matter of taste.  I had "relatively" few problems getting a Dell 8000 Piii 700mhz (now up to) 512MB ram up and running on Ubuntu with virtually no money invested (I bought more RAM and 2 batteries which now give me about 9 hours of battery time).  If you are wanting to go mobile and cheap, just keep in mind that you need at least 256MB of RAM to install Ubuntu.  And that's RAM not shared with the video card.  For blogging, you just need a machine that can connect, obviously, it sounds like you get what you need out of your current machine for audio, so the above should do it.

### Post by Linux_Man on 2007-09-22
Go for Dell's preinstalled Ubuntu notebooks, those are your best bet. If you buy an old laptop a multitude of things could be wrong with it, power cords get bent and useless after a few years of use and battery life can be dwindled to nothing not to mention how some wireless cards don't have support for Linux. So get a cheap Dell notebook its by far your best bet, and you can save money by not paying the "Microsoft Tax"
