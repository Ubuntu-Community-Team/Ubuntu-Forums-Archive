Hi folks...

Let me introduce myself; My name is Edizkan and I'm a new Linux user. If it won't be too boring, I'd like to give some background on my computer experience (Smart *** way of promoting myself.LOL!)

I'm somewhat a dinosaur in computers. My very first computer was a Sinclair Spectrum that my aunt gave as a gift in 1984. That was the time when Commodore 64 computers were popular. My next experience and long time engagement with computers was in 1996 when I started to learn graphic design and it was a 8200/120 Mac PPC. Later I used 9600, G3, iMac and G4. I wasn't able to purchase one since they were extremely expensive at the time, so I had to purchase a custom Pc with windows95 (in 1997). Since then I've been juggling between windows Pc's and Mac PPC's. I've always heard Linux but I was kinda afraid to jump in, because even though I'm an experienced user, Linux sorta looked like a software developers platform. That idea changed 2 years ago when I saw Lindows (now Linspire) being promoted on deviantART (I'm neverness.deviantart.com) Still didn't have the courage to make the transfer. 

Last week, I decided to give it a chance. I have a Hp desktop and a Sony laptop pc. The laptop is the one I installed Ubuntu on. I've experienced some problems due to my lack of my knowledge but I've managed to resolve all. I hope this post will also help other newbies to resolve their problems. Here we go:

The Laptop: 
Make/model: Sony Vaio Pcg-Fxa36
Processor: AMD Athlon 1000 Mhz
Memory: 512 Mb
Hdd: 20Gib
Dvd/Cd-r Combo: Matshita Ujda710
Operating system: Windows Xp Home Edition

**Preparation and Installation:**
First I re-arranged the partitions. The partitions were pre-arranged by the Sony's system installation. So I used Partition Magic (](*,) to format Disc D as Linux ext3. Then I downloaded Ubuntu 7.04 and burned to a CD.  I installed the system, reboot and here I was, in Ubuntu 7.04. No problems what so ever with the installation. But I realized there was a boot problem with my windows Xp now. When I choose windows xp from the gnub boot menu, the windows start loading and then it gave an error: 
> autochk not found - skipping autochk
xmnt2002 not found - skipping xmnt2002
and right after that it reboot without starting windows. I went online, googled the errors and finally found this solution which worked like a charm: I edit the settings in the boot menu for windows Xp to this...
> unhide (hd0,0)
rootnoverify (hd0,0)
Now I had both Xp and Ubuntu working perfect. After the Ubuntu installation, I also tried Kubuntu, Freespire just to see how they work. Bad decision. Even Ubuntu is too much for my athlon laptop. I haven't tried it yet, but I may give a chance to Xubuntu later.

**Problems and Solutions**

**iPod** That made me nuts. I have a 30Gib iPod Video and I wasn't able to make it work on Ubuntu. When I first connected it, system automatically mounted it and opened Rhythmbox. Theplaylists were there too but when I clicked on a song to play, it gave an error saying something like (necessary plugin isn't found). I downloaded Amorak, gtkpod... none worked. Then I finally downloaded Xmms and voila my iPod works just fine. Ps: I think it wasn't necessarily xmms made my ipod work. It can be the suggested downloads I marked when installing xmms. Anyways. Do that, your ipod will play just fine.

**iceWM** I wanted to change the appearance of my desktop and windows. So I came to this forum and searched for a solution since I didn't see many options in the System/Preferences/Theme menu. I heard about this iceWM and also checked the opendesktop.org for themes, I decided to install it. I found a post here that describes how to change from metacity to iceWM. It worked somewhat but it changed only the windows not the whole menus and bars. I think there is a little issue there, or it's just me. Anyways. I found out that gnome-look.org had a lot of themes that I can install without changing my wm. Here is how my desktop looks now:
