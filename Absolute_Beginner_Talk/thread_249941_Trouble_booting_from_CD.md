### Post by Anonii on 2006-09-03
You have to get in your BIOS when your PC is booting.
You will most probably have to press the "Delete" button.
You will get in a screen with many options, and you will have to find the booting devices in there, and configure them in an other order. I'd recommend, your PC to boot firstly from a floppy, then from your CD, and then from your hard disk.
Also, please be careful and dont change things you dont know, you may screw up your PC :) Its not really tough, and most BIOS pages are really user-friendly nowdays.

### Post by Qwertyman66 on 2006-09-03
Your BIOS should support booting from CD. Have you checked under all of the available tabs? Sometimes there is a tab for boot order and sometimes it is under something different like advanced options. I haven't come across many that just have it on the first page and none that didn't support booting from floppy.
