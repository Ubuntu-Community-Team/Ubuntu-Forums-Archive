### Post by jml on 2007-02-18
As far as used laprops are concerned, I would recommend an IBM ThinkPad.  I have personally used an R40e and an R30.  Here is a link to a site that sells refurbished laptops.  I purchased my used R30 from them and I did not have any complaints.

[http://www.usanotebook.com/](http://www.usanotebook.com/)

As for what to avoid, do not buy a laptop that uses the Broadcom wireless chip set for their wifi card.  Broadcom does not release tech specs or drivers to the Linux community, so avoid that type of hardware.  I would recommend buying a laptop with at least a PCMCIA card slot.  Then you can buy a used laptop without built in wireless and look for a wireless card that is more Linux friendly.  The two I have had success with are the NetGear WG511T and the Cisco Aironet 802.11 a/b/g cards.  They both have the Atheros chip set and work well with Linux.

If you want to get great Ubuntu compatibility, I agree with what an earlier poster said and check out the company called System 76.  I have listed the link to their web site below.  They have new laptops that start at $699.  They only sell computers with Ubuntu installed and they make sure everything works out of the box.  I just recieved my new laptop (A Darter,) from them and am quite satisfied.  So, check it out.


[http://system76.com/index.php/cPath/1?osCsid=2dcb18aaf1e299c13bed110635f0f207](http://system76.com/index.php/cPath/1?osCsid=2dcb18aaf1e299c13bed110635f0f207)
