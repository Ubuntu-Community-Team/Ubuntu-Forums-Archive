### Post by beeldings on 2006-04-29
You'll have to access your motherboard's BIOS and manually allocate the memory. Turn on or reboot your computer, when the POST screen loads (it's the screen that usually displays basic system information: processor speed, amount of RAM, etc.) hit the Delete key. This should bring up your BIOS menu and from there you should look for an entry called "chipset configuration" or something similar. Then, from that menu you should be able to select how much memory is to be used for video.

This information may vary depending on what type of motherboard you own. When in doubt head over to Google and search for someting like "<brand of motherboard> BIOS setup" or anything similar. Or, if you have access to the motherboard user's manual, it will tell you exactly what steps are needed to configure the amount of memory you wish to dedicate for video. Perhaps the easiest thing to do would be to check the manufacturer's web site for details.
