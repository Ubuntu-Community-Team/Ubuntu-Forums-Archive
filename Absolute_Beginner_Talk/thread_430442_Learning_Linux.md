### Post by LuisGMarine on 2007-05-02
To learn more my advice it to get under the hood.  Go out and explore , do things with your programs out of the ordinary.

I know this might sound bad, but I tried to make Linux work as flawless as I could and mimick windows in a sort of way, just to easy my transaction.  For example, go through everyprogram that you use on a regular basis, and figure out if there is anything wrong, and if there is anything wrong, fix it!  Right now for example I'm trying to figure out why when watching a flash video the sound seems to flicker, then after that I'm going to move on getting software mixing working.

The other cool thing is when you fix something, keep a dairy, sort of like your own HOWTO on the problem that you can refer yourself to in the future.  For example I keep it in my blooger from google.  So this next time I encounter the problem again I know what to do, or if someone here on the forums is having the same problem I can refer them to that post on my blog and help them fix their problems.

Remember that you aren't goingt to learn everything over a week.  Just like anything it takes time and practice, like I said the best way is to 'explore' around.  Pick up learning how to bash script to make yourself some cool scripts.  I used to know a kid back in the day that was about 15 years old, picked up perl programming language, and now is writing the gui install for ArchLinux!

Anyhow, my best regards, and good luck :)
