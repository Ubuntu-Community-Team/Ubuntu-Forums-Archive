### Post by lazyart on 2007-06-25
This is the reason why I don't use Evolution (there is a Thunderbird vs. Evolution thread rolling currently where I bring this up).  It's something that so many mail readers have but the Evolution team just doesn't feel is a priority.

Thunderbird does this correctly and that is why it's my email client of choice.  It will do this with a simple POP3 account, which you will find is the standard basic email given by most ISPs.

Leaving mail on the server has a bunch of advantages-- for one, just as the OP mentioned if your hardware fails, there goes your email.   If your ISP gives you webmail then you have access to your old mails no matter what machine you are on.  It's a convenient way to move files around-- just mail it to yourself, then pick up the file at your destination.  I was once in Hawaii and needed a file from my machine in Florida.  I used LogMeIn to access my desktop, emailed myself a hard to find driver for my Motorola Razr.  Checked my mail, installed the driver which let me use my laptop as a charger for my phone since I had left the wall-plugged one back home 3000 miles away.

Now I use Ubuntu with automatically charges my Razr.
