### Post by keithpeter on 2006-12-29
> **kornelix said:**
> I just tried to download some music (via musicload.de) and it refuses to deal with anyone not running Microsoft IE and Windows Media Player. 


A common problem with many business sites.

I personally like some of the classical music on Magnatune ([http://magnatune.com)](http://magnatune.com)). You purchase a download key and your downloads can be in a range of formats including lossless so that you can burn CDs. You can download each album a number of times and can have downloads re-activated. There is no DRM in the files. The music is all fully licenced with the artists, and you are allowed to give download keys to up to three other people for each purchase. The classical music tends not to be 'famous' people, but is usually good quality nevertheless. One album I purchased is a recording on harpsichord of the Goldberg variations, the harpsichordist had built the instrument and released the recording as an advert for her instuments! Bit like Linux?

I'd also vote for emusic.com although I would say that the selection is definitely 'long tail' - back catalogue recordings. e-music require you to use a 'downloader', a small piece of software. There is a binary available for linux, which I will try to install later, I'm still using the iBook with Mac OS X on for music I have to admit. e-music works on a subscription basis and the MP3 files have no DRM, once you have bought them they remain yours even after you cancel the subscription.

I'm looking at classical.com - same subscription system and you can listen on a streaming basis to the whole catalogue and download a few each month. Anyone any experience there at all?
