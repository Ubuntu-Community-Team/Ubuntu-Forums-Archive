### Post by Papa-san on 2006-04-03
Where is 'Terminal'? I can't seem to locate it... 
I have read at least five posts asking this very thing. Well actually only four, the first one I posted myself...  Let me explain: (Please, grab a cup of coffee, and take the time to read this. I promise to make it at least a little bit entertaining...)

Moving from Windows to a Linux distribution is going to be quite a shock if you haven't used it before. Nothing is going to seem the same, because it isn't. These forums are going to be your best friend for a while, and if you find your experience to be like mine, that will continue... and soon, believe it or not, you will be giving back to the next person! Once this whole new way of thinking 'clicks', you will realize exactly why linux users have so much fun bagging on windows... Plus, the options are AMAZING. (Do you know there is a program that is just learning/ refreshing Latin? It doesn't cost anything either!) 

My computer guy, a friend of about ten years, had always told me I would be better off with linux.  He has been telling this to everyone he builds a computer for, but most of us say, &#8221;No thanks. I am familiar with Windows, so I ought to stick with it...&#8221; He just shakes his head and collects our money. Then he shakes his head as he collects our money over and over again as we bring our non-functional Windows boxes back for him to fix...

I finally got so tired of Windows crashing that I decided to make the leap, no matter the cost. I have seen several people doing this the smart way: Getting a live CD and 'playing' with it, Then setting up a dual boot system to get more functionality out of the Ubuntu. All the while asking questions. Then, they eventually find that the Windows portion seems to be more of a liability than an asset...

I however did it differently, and I have noticed some others have done the same. This was done in March of 2006. I popped the install disk in and did the unthinkable... Re-format that critter, NOW!  Once that process was finished, I was looking at a desktop that made absolutely no sense to me. (What was worse was the fact that I had some hardware/driver issues that didn't allow my wireless to work. I managed to muddle through it, somehow, but that is it's own story). I was so frustrated most of the time, I just wanted to pitch the whole thing in the dumpster.

It took me three days to find the terminal, or 'command line'. (Papa-san isn't the sharpest tool in the shed...) For the next three weeks, I fought with everything to try to get my wireless set up. I didn't know it, but the drivers I tried at first were corrupt. I could not get 'file-roller' to show up as an option to open the .driver.exe file. I dragged this into the forums. Some of my posts I don't even want to link to as they are so embarrassing. 

Even now, I know so little, I feel as though I shouldn't even be writing anything as a 'How-to&#8221; (I actually hope this serves more as a &#8220;How-NOT-to&#8221;.) Like: I really want to find one of those cute little cartoon looking penguins to choose one as an avatar. (They call the penguins a 'Tux'... I never knew that) but my searches have been futile. Or: I really need to figure out how to install and configure 'wine' (That is a program that allows Windows programs to be run in linux; a 'Windows Emulator') but I'm afraid I am going to screw it up and get frustrated again, so I will just deal with it until I read enough posts to gain confidence... And this is the biggest point I hope to make:

PLEASE, PLEASE, PLEASE: READ... As noobies, the Absolute Beginner Talk forum is a great place to start. Mostly because many of us are coming from Windows,and have no CLUE what things are called in 'Linux Lingo'. Do a search in this forum using whatever term(s) you are looking for, and read through the post results you get. (Even if they don't seem to apply!) Chances are someone has already asked your question. If no results are forthcoming, alter your search a little bit. Also, do not be afraid to search on Google. Also, The sticky thread on the top of this forum entitled "How to Help Yourself" ought to be required reading for all... once I figure out how to put links in my posts, that one will be first on my list! (Please read through it, also you will see how distracting it is to jump in on an unrelated thread... Don't worry, I am guilty of this myself, but don't do it anymore...)

Example: &#8220;I want to run E-bay's 'Turbo-lister' on my Ubuntu system, but E-bay doesn't offer it for anything but Windows, and I can't figure it out... Well, my searches contained 'Ebay+Ubuntu' and 'linux+turbo+lister'. This is where I learned what 'wine' actually does... 

There is a mind-boggling amount of information already there in the posts and wiki documentation of this site, as well as out 'there' on the internet. And don't worry, If you still cannot find your answers, you can ask here. 

One of the most AWESOME things I have learned about Ubuntu (and linux in general) is that it IS NOT an Operating System like Windows. It is a COMMUNITY. The programs are available in the 'repositories'  (That's like our own unique File Planet!) The files there are secure. no viruses or spyware that I have heard of. And if what we are looking for isnt there, chances are, someone else has it and wants you to use it. They want you to look for and report bugs so they can be fixed. Occaisionally someone brilliant comes up with something that just blows your mind. (arnieboy's Automatix is one of those things, in my opinion; I wouldn't have survived this transition without it. What does he want from it? I think all he needs is to know that we are seeing his name in our terminal, and once we figure out what went into it, we nOObs can just go 'WOW! He's SMART! And then we say Thanx!)

Please don't give up, and be ready, willing, and patient enough to learn what you need to!

### Post by az on 2006-04-04
Well, I'll use the attention to pimp this wiki page:

[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UsabilityCaseStudies](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UsabilityCaseStudies)


In short, if you come across a "feature" that is not a bug, but does not work as intended, or is not intuitive, point it out here.  Usability is not about bugs, but design flaws.

We have all sat someone down at our Gnome desktops and had then click around to do stuff.  It's all meant to be intuitive, but sometimes it's not.  

A lot of professionals do usability studies, so this is nothing new.  Most of the way that Gnome works is that way for a reason.  However, there are a lot of issues that are expressed on the forums (or irc, or mailing lists) and here is a place to put those.

It would be really great if someone picked this up and ran with it.  That means tending to the page so that it doesn't get repetitive or cluttered.  It also means getting someone who can use the information to know that it's there...  Once the page gets full enough to be useful, I will bring it to someone's attention.

Probably just after Dapper releases, there will be another Ubuntu round of meetings and sprints.  If someone creates a usability spec, this page will be another ressource for them to work with.  Let's fill it up.

Your mission:

Find someone who has no experience with Gnome (or KDE), and ask them to do something (like retreive photos from a digital camera or configure a printer)  Don't help them and make note of ther things that get in the way of them getting the job done.  If they are major, mention them on the page.

The same goes for a forum thread where something that is obvious to you is not obvious to the original poster.
