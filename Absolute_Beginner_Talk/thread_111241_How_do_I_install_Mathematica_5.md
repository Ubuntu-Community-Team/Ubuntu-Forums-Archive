### Post by ubuntuuser on 2006-01-02
I have Mathematica 5.1 installed on my system. It's fairly easy to install:

mount your CD
open a treminal and type```
cd /media/cdrom/Unix/Installer
```
inside that directory type```
sh ./MathInstaller
```
For some reason it doesn't work with ./MathInstaller, so don't forget the sh in front of it. Then just follow the instructions.

### Post by Hallavej on 2006-01-02

explaines how to install mathematica.
And by the way. Matlab is not an alternative to mathematica. The are very very different. That is what I think, anyway. Matlab does numerical computations, and it is very good at it. It does not do symbolic manipulations, indefinite integrations, equation solving etc. For that mathematica is very good, but mathematica is not strong at the numerical stuff. It is slow and not nearly as sofisticated as matlab.
Octave is a matlab alternative. It does not do all that matlab does but for most things is it a very good alternative. I dont no scilab. Open source alternatives to mathematica are eg. maxima and axiom, but they are not even close to be as cool as mathematica.
