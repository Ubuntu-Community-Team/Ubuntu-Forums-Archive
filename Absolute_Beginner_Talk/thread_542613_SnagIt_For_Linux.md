Yes, but that does not nullify the difference between a professional tool like SnagIt versus the utilities that the Linux side offers, even counting GIMP among them.

With SnagIt, I can capture 10 pages of a PDF by capturing just the window. SnagIt automatically scrolls the entire 10 pages worth of window. No Linux app does that. Linux apps that I know of only capture what is visible in the monitor, one screen deep, no scrolling.

With SnagIt, I can take that 10 screen deep capture, save it as a JPEG, BMP, or GIF and import into a drawing program, where I can add handy-dandy notes, callouts, or whatever my users need. 

This is just an example of what SnagIt can do. The newest versions go far beyond that.

So, in the time I spend futzing and fussing with some tool not made for the job, I can actually get some work done with no muss or fuss.

But do not confuse screenshot utilities (Windows and Mac have them, too, you know) with a professional-grade application that captures everything I want in the format I want. The scroll feature alone makes SnagIt worth the small price. Problem is, it is not ported to Linux. And I hate WINE with a passion.
