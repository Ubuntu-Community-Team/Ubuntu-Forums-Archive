### Post by xopher on 2007-04-11
I had to switch from checkgmail to gmail-notify because of a bug; when there was no internet connection, checkgmail sometimes started using 100% CPU and hogging my system to an unusable state.

When both work as they shold, the difference is marginal. But Id still say gmail-notify is easier on the resources, as it doesn't have to do that much. If both work correctly, you should just choose the one you like more..
