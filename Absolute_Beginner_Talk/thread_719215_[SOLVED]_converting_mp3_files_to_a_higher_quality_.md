### Post by freelinuxhelp on 2008-03-08
when you convert a wav to an mp3, data is lost. meaning, part of the reason that the file size is so much smaller is that the "extra" information is removed.

because of that, converting to mp3 is a one way street. you can convert it back to a wav or flac or something with higher quality, but it won't sound any better, and could actually end up sounding worse.
i recommend ripping your cd's to flac (lossless compression)

### Post by daengbo on 2008-03-08
As free mentioned, you can't get a high-quality sound file back from an MP3. That's like taking a picture of the Mona Lisa with a cheap digital camera and trying to get get all the original detail back from the crummy .jpg the camera gave you -- It's just not possible.

If you have a lossy format (like mp3 or ogg for music or jpeg for pictures), you shouldn't really do any manipulation of the file because you'll just lose MORE data in the process. Your best bet is to leave lossy formats alone. If you know that you'll want to do some conversion later, use a lossless format like wav or the FLAC format recommended by Free. Lossless formats generally  take up a LOT more space, though, so get ready for that. Audio programs generally use wav files for their internal work and professional photographers keep their pictures in raw format. They use these formats for a reason: no loss of quality.
