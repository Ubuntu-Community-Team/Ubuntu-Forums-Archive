### Post by buzzmandt on 2007-07-11
I recently gave my parents (55 and 60) a computer at my mothers request and like an idiot had windows xp on it.  They decided to get comcast highspeed internet, after all "We've got it, might as well have it right".    Had firewall, avg, adaware, spybot..etc on it thinking it be ok for the most part.  I'm a truck driver and only get home for 3 or 4 days for 2 to 3 weeks out.....(you kinda know where this is going).  

Well, after about 2 months of "how to get email" and "how to send email" everything was good, they learned how to search the web, print recipes, play games, etc...

Then "IT" happened, the first virus, got rid of it, then the second. then more, then more.  Got to the point of almost not wanting to go back to home town cause I'd be at moms working on their computer for hours either getting rid of crap, or just plain wiping the drive and reinstalling......

This is where me being an idiot comes in....I've been a linux user at some degree for about 10 years.  Mandrake (before mandriva) was my favorite.  Tried many others until I saw one I'd never heard of, "Ubuntu", I've now been a full time Kubuntu user for about 4 months.

My parents are now full time users of Kubuntu as well.  I stopped their windows addiction early enough that the change over was very painless.  They are absolute beginners to computing at all.  I just don't let them have the sudo password, but they really don't need it anyway.

Now I can truck to my hearts content and not wonder "oh man, what has windows let propagate this time??!!"  nothing against my parents of course, but they don't quite understand yet that that pretty waterfall screen saver click here link could be something nasty.

Thank you Ubuntu and the Ubuntu community
