### Post by jaclynL on 2007-06-15
Hmmm, that sounds to me like a bit of an urban legend.  What Microsoft did do was introduce a new open source format (Office Open XML) that was not immediately supported by OpenOffice.  I haven't heard anything about MS refusing to acknowledge any .doc files not created by Office, and I'm not sure that they even can do that, so I don't think you need to worry.

I still wouldn't receommend Office 2007 to anyone, though, just because it is really not backwards compatible w/ any other version of Office.  I worked the computer help desk when I was in college, and I've seen it at the center of some small tragedies.  If something happens to your computer, Windows self destructs, you spill orange soda on your keyboard, etc., just try taking that term paper you were writing in Office 2007 and opening it in a computer lab, which will have Office 2003.  Pretty ridiculous ploy on Microsoft's part to try to force people to buy the newest software.
