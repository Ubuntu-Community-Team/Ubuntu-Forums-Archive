### Post by henthorn on 2007-06-03
My main beef with migrating to Ubuntu has nothing to with personalities or the like.  It has to do with presentation.  There are scads and scads of absolutely brilliant how-to-do-its that are perfectly understandable to those who have prior knowledge of terms.  However for newbies they are unintelligible because the terms used are seldom defined in a way that a newbie can understand them. If you don't understand what the special meaning of "mount" is  as used in the instructions then the nstructions lose their meaning.  If the explanation includes a link to another section to define a certain word in the explanation and then that definition takes you to another link to explain another special word in the definition, which again contains another link to another word definition, the reader has forgotten what he/she  was reading about in the first place. The purpose has to be to instruct and instructions need to be clear to the uninitiated if it is to help.  What I am talking about is "technical writing" which I admit is a specialty in itself.  Computer experts such as those generously donating their time and efforts to this and other Linux projects are not technical writers.  They have not been taught to write as though they were writing for completely ignorant folks such as me, for instance.  I am not stupid but I am ignorant about computers.  All of us are ignorant about certain things.  I find it almost impossible to find specific answers to questions about the most basic manipulations after first loading Ubuntu.  I know they probably exist but there is no organized way to find them that I have found. Anyway, I love the program, I applaud those who are making it possible and I am looking forward to the day when there are enough volunteers to pull everything together so new people cam understand what to do. Again this is not intended as criticism but just the way it strikes a complete newbie.

Even Vista I've seen has lots of options only available via policy editor (kinda like registry editing) and other obscure means. Familiarity makes it appear easy.

IMO, the documentation is "out there" (on net) and the friendly help through them is "in here" (forums), I think the only barrier for a lot of people is time (some are too busy), indifference (some simply don't care and just want the OS that requires the least "apparent" effort) and will (if you don't go in it with a strong will to learn, you'll be discouraged and end up where you started before you know it).
