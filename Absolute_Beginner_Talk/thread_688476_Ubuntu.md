### Post by JoshuaRL on 2008-02-05
Couple things:

64 bit will have a couple problems specific to that version, but it's really good.  And higher-order computing will be noticibly faster.  Stuff like encoding, compiling, and 3D work.  And by using it you're helping along the furthering of the 64bit transfer.  Here are some resources:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=368607](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=368607)
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=661833](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=661833)

As far as hardware, go with nVidia.  Supposedly some of the newer ATI cards are pretty well-supported, but nVidia still beats them.  Also, I would warn against RAID 0.  From what I've read, it's fast but not secure.  If you have faults it can ruin all drives, whereas RAID 1 and especially RAID 2 and higher are safer with your data.  So just something to think about.
