### Post by luke411 on 2007-03-07
If you download and buy Crossover Office from Codeweavers, you can run an older version of Itunes on Linux. It's about 40.00. The fact is though, you should consider finding a cheaper place to get music and decide if that is really worth keeping you tied to windows. The thing to remember about Linux is it is NOT windows and it requires you to learn to do things differently. Sometimes you'll find little 'treasures' of things you didn't think any OS would do and sometimes you'll have to fight to do something that you used to take for granted in windows. It's not an easy decision to make but look at it this way. If you go buy a new pc with Vista on it, you can't use your IPOD anyway!!! At least Linux has numerous music player software to choose from for loading music on it. There are sacrifices and benefits and that would be the same even if you switched to a Mac or any other OS so it's a decision you have to make.

### Post by Strider42 on 2007-03-08
iTunes under VMWare definitely works.  As I type, I am syncing about 8000 tracks to my new Ipod.

If you're using Edgy there is a small bug in the way USB devices are regonised.  Dapper doesn't have this problem I believe, and I'm pretty sure it will be fixed in Feisty.

So, back to Edgy.  Add your USB controller in VMWare, and plug in your Ipod, and chances are the thing won't show up under VM | Removable Devices | USB Devices.  I spent bloody hours trying to get this to connect, then some smart wag posted somewhere in the these forums that when he plugged in a USB Pen Drive, like magic, the Ipod appeared and all was working fine.

So off I went, dug out my pen drive, plugged it in and, voila, Ipod up and running.

Give it a go.  VMWare was the only way I could make the jump to Linux as I need several XP only programs for my work.
