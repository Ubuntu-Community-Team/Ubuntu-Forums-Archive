### Post by tokyovigilante on 2005-11-20
I see what you mean, yeah it is a bit tricky reading the HTML when you're offline. 

Have you tried just running the installer and seeing how you go? As long as you don't interfere with any other partitions on your hard drive, there's no way you can break your system. Grub installs by default and allows dual-booting easily.

Other than that there are only simple questions to answer such as time-zone and user setup.

I haven't been able to find the Breezy version of install-en.txt online at archive.ubuntu.com. It looks like its been removed in favour of the HTML version.

Honestly I suggest just reading beforehand until you feel comfortable and then go for it. Provided you have adequate (>3-4gb) unpartitioned space on a hard drive you should have no problems assuming your machine is reasonably standard. If you do run into difficulties then just post up your questions here.
