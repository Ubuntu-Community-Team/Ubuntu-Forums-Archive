### Post by Leigh on 2006-02-11
I did exactly what you want to do. I built a PC from second hand parts that I bought from eBay. The whole thing cost $300 (Canadian) for the parts. Most of the items came from the States, so postage bumped that figure to $450 (CAD). The PC I built will outperform an Intel 3.x processor easily. Actually, I was quite surprised how high-spec a machine I managed to build on a budget. I was aiming for a mid-range PC just to learn Linux, but ended up with a power-monster! The only items I bought new on eBay, and that's because I got them at an excellent price, was the processor AMD 64 3400+ at $170CAD) and Nvidia FX5500 (w/256MB for $40CAD).

The only issue with second-hand parts from eBay is that they rarely come with a warranty. That was a risk I was willing to take.

By default, the installer will detect your current OS and offer to shrink that partition for you.  You just have to enter the size of the new partition, if that is what you want.
