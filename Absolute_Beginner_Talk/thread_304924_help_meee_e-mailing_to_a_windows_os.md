### Post by ewl1217 on 2006-11-22
The best option would be for her to install OpenOffice. Try to explain all the benefits to her (free, open source, OpenDocument format, she can still keep MS Office, etc.). However, if she refuses, just save your documents in MS Office format before you send them. Keep in mind that not all formatting may be preserved, although all the content should be there, and OpenOffice generally does well with basic formatting (bold, italics, underline, etc.).

File > Save As... > Filter > Microsoft Word/Excel/Powerpoint


**EDIT:** Make sure you just the format appropriate to the version of MS Office she is using.

### Post by jhenager on 2006-11-22
Email is email - there is no compatibility problem here. Did you change the format of the emailed document when you saved it? By default, Open Office saves it as a .swf file. You would need to change it to Word 97-XP format, and then attach that to an email. Then she can open it with M$ Word.
