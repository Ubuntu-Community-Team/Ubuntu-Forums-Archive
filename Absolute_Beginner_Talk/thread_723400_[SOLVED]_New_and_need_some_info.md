### Post by prshah on 2008-03-13
> **Sav98 said:**
> its the free cd im gettin not the paid one. and i just like havin a CD for this kinda thing over a download. and it gives me time to back all my files up properly and all that. also i got another question lol. looking through the document on partitioning my hdd. well ive got 2 in the computer im installing this to so can i just use one hdd for each? and if so how would i go about that?

Yes, you can use a separate hdd for each operating system; but for best performance, a setup like this works best:

"/" on your 1st HDD, 10Gb type linux/ext3, (lives along with Windows)
"swap" on your 1st HDD, 2Gb type swap
"/home" on your 2nd HDD, total space type linux/ext3

Breaking up your linux installation across 2 HDDs allows simultaneous access where otherwise you would have linear access, ie, rather than one man weight-lifting, it will be like two workmen collaborating to lift a heavy box.

If any of this sounds strange now, forget it, but keep it in mind when you actually decide to install; before you install, come back here and someone (maybe even me) will explain it in more detail.

And, rather than wait for the CD, it's better to just grab it off a torrent.
