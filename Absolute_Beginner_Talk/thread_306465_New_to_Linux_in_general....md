### Post by K.Mandla on 2006-11-25
> **BrokenPhantom said:**
> does Linux let me use all the cool bits on my new box? ... Should I just axe Windows on this thing straight out of the box and be done with it, or would I be losing the cool bits I shelled out my hard-earned cash for? ... I therefore ask you- what do I lose by axing Windows? What do I gain? Or should I just dual-boot and not worry so much?
I think the cool bits question is going to depend on the cool bit. I used to run Ubuntu 5.10 on a Dell XPS M170, and everything worked great. I got all the games I really wanted, I used Wine for the ones that weren't native, and anything else was just as usable in Ubuntu as in XP. So in my case (and granted, this was a year ago), there was nothing to be lost in switching.

Of course, things have changed and the benefit in using it is directly proportional to the amount of work you're willing to put into it, coupled with the coolness factor of your cool bits. ;) Extreme cutting edge stuff is going to be tougher to work with because it's new. Stuff that has been around a while has better support. It's just the nature of the beast.

I'd try a dual boot, if I were you. If you find you need to use a cool bit that doesn't work as well in Ubuntu, you can still use Windows for it. But if your experience is at all like mine was, you'll find that over time, you spend less and less time in Windows and more and more time in Ubuntu. And that's where the real benefits kick in. ;)

### Post by Voxxi on 2006-11-25
I say try out Ubuntu using a Live CD. That way you can check if all your hardware works, use some of the programs etc and if you really like it, you can configure a dual boot environment. If you're a media freak, there are heaps of great programs you can use on Linux:

Amarok - Music player, similar to iTunes, but better.
Audacity - Powerful audio editing software.
K3B - CD Burning application, similar to Nero.

But something you would really like is Beryl. Beryl is a piece of software that you install (No way! ;) ) which really spices up your desktop. I just installed it last night, and man am I loving it. Let me give you an example:

Normally if you drag a window, it just moves. No spectacular effects. But if you have Beryl running, when you move that window, it wobbles. Much like if you were holding a piece of paper by a corner and moved it.

Just search Youtube for "Beryl" and you'll see what I mean. Anyway, welcome to the world of Linux! :D

### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-11-25
Your graphics card, if it's ATI or Nvidia, should work. If not immediately, then when the next update of the proprietry drivers comes.

I don't know precisely about 7.1 audio. Is it being passed through to an external amplifier, or is that handled on your sound card? I know Totem has a setting for AC3 passthrough.

I assume you have a video capture card or a digital TV card. These should work without tweaking, but check Google just in case the manufacturer likes to reinvent the wheel.

My advice is always to begin by dual-booting. As a wise man (me) once said: "If for nothing else, keep your Windows partition as a ready source of DLLs for Wine!"
