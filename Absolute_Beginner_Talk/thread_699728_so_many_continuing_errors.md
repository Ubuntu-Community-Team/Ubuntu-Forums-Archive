I agree.  Maybe the Acer just isn't the best hardware fit for the default Ubuntu software setup choices.  I love Ubuntu, but only run it on desktops; not laptops only due to the priority of what I want my laptop to do.  Have you tried d'loading any live cd's to run different linux OS's?  Might be a little more time-and-effort efficient.  For example have you tried the Knoppix 5.1.1 live cd?  Maybe that would use different drivers, than your Ubuntu instl'n does.  For the cost of a cd each, you might find other OS's, which could give you a leg up on getting your system running.  D/l the iso's, copy to a cd and then run them from the cd-rom drive at bootup to see if they solve any of your problems.  Running from the cd can be a bit slower and also prevents you from using your burner (depending on your drives) from burning anything afterwards during the session.  But you can change this scenario, by entering the version and "toram" at the first bootup prompt to load the cd into the ramdisk and run it from there (in my somewhat limited experience).  Look at the cheatcode notes for each of these OS's to nail this down.    For example with Knoppix, at the first bootup prompt after turning on the machine with the cd in the drive, enter "knoppix toram" (without the quotes).   Then if you find the solutions you need, you could adapt to get Ubuntu up and running, if you want to run Ubuntu.  I think this might be somewhat quicker experiment-wise than doing full hard drive installs of a particular OS, if you really feel the problem is related to not having the ideal OS for your hardware.  Just a thought.  Others with more experience may have a better idea.  Cheers.

### Post by jordanmthomas on 2008-02-17
It seems like the majority of your problems are caused by power management issues.
> Lack of battery icon
Can't shut down
hibernate / suspend
These ARE problems with Ubuntu, but ones that can likely be fixed.  What kind of motherboard do you have?  

Power management is fine here, and my laptop gets better battery life and runs cooler in Linux than it ever did in Windows.  This is a hardware compatibility issue, Linux can manage power just fine for many computers.

As for Opera hanging up:  Opera is not open source and it's not part of Ubuntu.  You can't blame Ubuntu for this, you have to blame Opera devs.

www links in Evolution:  Do you have a default browser set in gnome?  Try looking through the options in Evolution, I'm sure there's one related to your issue somewhere.

missing files that were previously there:  This sounds like a hard drive issue.  I have never heard of files magically disappearing without either a) someone/something deleting them or b) the disk is dieing.  

Your internet connection I'm afraid I don't know much about.  Modern Linux systems are MUCH happier with broadband connections though.  *nix is built with networking in mind.

CD player not recognizing disk:  If the kernel recognizes a disk has been inserted and it gets mounted by some program, then the fault lies with the player you are using, and not Ubuntu.  

Wireless:  you are right, but it's not Linux's fault that hardware manufacturers keep the specs of the hardware secret and do not make drivers.  Cards with open specs work great.

Not sure what to think about your USB mouse issues, try this and look for errors:
```
dmesg | tail
``` after it happens

Don't think I'm defending Ubuntu.  In fact, I really dislike it and won't have it installed on my computer.  I am just suggesting that you need to look into finding *where* your errors are actually occuring instead of blaming Ubuntu as a whole.
