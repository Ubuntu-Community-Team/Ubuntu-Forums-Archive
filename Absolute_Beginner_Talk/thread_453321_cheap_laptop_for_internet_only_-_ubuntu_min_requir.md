### Post by lazyart on 2007-05-24
I have an Evo N620c, which is a Pentium M 1.4 with 256 megs running ubuntu.  Doesn't have wireless built in but I did add a Cisco PCMCIA card and it gets the job done.  I long for more than it's 1024x768 screen can give me, but I banged out my recent expense report on it while a kid was at my desk.  Then I VNCed to my desktop, turned on shared folders and transferred the finished product to my home folder.

With xubuntu you can run with less processing power than I had-- it only needs half the memory.

### Post by MinstrelBoy on 2007-05-24
I've loaded Xubuntu onto a Toshiba Satelite Pro 4600

Cost about £120 in the UK
