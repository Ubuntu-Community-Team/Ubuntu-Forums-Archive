### Post by doctorbighands on 2008-04-18
Ubuntu comes packaged with Pidgin (f.k.a. Gaim), Kubuntu comes packaged with Kopete, and Xubuntu...well, I'm not sure, because I haven't spent much time with it.

There are LOTS of choices for IM clients. If you liked Trillian, you'll do great with Pidgin or Kopete. I personally prefer Kopete (despite my being a Gnome user), but that's just me.

If you head for Applications > Add/Remove, you can see first-hand what IM clients are available to you. This is also how you install Wine: Search for it, check the box, and hit Apply. Couldn't be easier! :)

EDIT: I'm not sure if Trillian will work in Wine. My experience with Wine is that it's a total and complete crapshoot as to whether something works in it or not. I think that once you get a peek at Pidgin, Kopete, etc., you won't want to mess with getting Trillian working in Wine.
