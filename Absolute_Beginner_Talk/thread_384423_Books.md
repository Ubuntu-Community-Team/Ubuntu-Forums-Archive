I would recommend "Ubuntu Hacks", which has a lot of good information and techniques for more advanced use.  It also contains a lot of beginner's info that is readily found elsewhere, but no book can be all things to all people. I have skimmed through all the books on your list and all are useful to a degree, but I honestly found my best source of information on Ubuntu is right here in these forums. 

Two excellent books on Linux in general are "The Linux Cookbook" by Michael Stutz and "RUTE User's Tutorial and Exposition". RUTE is a bit dated, but the background information is first rate and I think it's an excellent tutorial suitable for beginners through advanced users.  There is a different "Linux Cookbook" published by O'Reilly, and it's good too; but I found the one by Michael Stutz (which is available for free online, I think) was more useful to me when I wanted to look at more depth and details about how to accomplish different types of tasks with Linux.
