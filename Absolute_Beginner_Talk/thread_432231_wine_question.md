### Post by starcraft.man on 2007-05-03
Short answer... no.

Cedega is really just a GUI facelift for Wine (Used to be called WineX) for gaming first, and CrosOver is the same with office apps in mind.

Essentially, most important things should run under wine from terminal if they can run under either of those (wow for instance, and photoshop 7). That being said, each of the two above products have made to a certain extent their own offshoots, and have made their own changes to wine to increase their own range of support without giving that back to the wine project(even this is kinda flawed, they give some code back just not everything) and support some products that wine doesn't. Its a complicated mesh and very difficult to fully understand how one takes a free product and then sells it back to people but anyway, try what you wanna run in wine first, and if your program really doesn't work but one of these supports it, the choice is yours...

[www.winehq.com]("www.winehq.com") for support.
