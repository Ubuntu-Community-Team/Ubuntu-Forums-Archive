### Post by mister_lister on 2007-12-19
I have a suggestion for those of you who constantly upgrade .... "If it works, don't fix it"

Unless you have a new computer don't try a new distro or upgrade, if it is working on your current computer, don't **** with it. HOW stupid do you have to be to fix a perfectly fine install with an upgrade or new distro. I can see if you buy a new computer and you need to upgrade and check distro compatibility out. But just because something NEWER comes out do you have to be a dolt and upgrade instantly? Talk about lemmings...constant complainers and circular logic.

There is nothing wrong with sticking with a distro or version that works for you. JUMP around to find a FIXED point but don't expect perfection JUST because you want it. 

I am still on version 6.06.... works like a charm, does everything I really need it to and more, plus I get get constant updates, almost one every other day. My system is radical. No worries. All of you constant upgraders and version chasers can throat  my crank. NO PROBLEM HERE.
