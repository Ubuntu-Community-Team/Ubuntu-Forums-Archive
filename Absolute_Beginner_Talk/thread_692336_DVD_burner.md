### Post by Sami_Sdata on 2008-02-09
Check your DVD player before you waste too much time on making it a video DVD.  Most of them will will DIVX videos with no problem.  The cheap $40 Walmart DVD I bought for my kids will play DIVX AVI files.  You can burn a lot more video in that format than standard DVD and with a lot less hassle.  I just burn them as a data DVD with K3B.  I do have to shorten the file names since the player truncates them down to 10 characters.

### Post by Aquaman420 on 2008-02-09
You first need to rip the dvd with a program such as k9copy, then use the k3b burning suite to burn the resulting iso image to a dvd.  You need to make sure you use the burn iso to dvd option and not the data dvd option.  Good luck

Edit: I do agree with Sami to just buy a dvd player with divx support if you can afford one.  They are like 40 bucks.  That's how I have been rocking my dvd collection for years now.

### Post by halitech on 2008-02-09
are the movies already in dvd format or are the avi/mpg/divx files? if DVD format, just use K3B to burn them using the DVD option. If they are in any other format, unless you want to buy a new player, you will need something like DeVeDee or manDVD to convert them to DVD format and then you can burn them using K3B.
