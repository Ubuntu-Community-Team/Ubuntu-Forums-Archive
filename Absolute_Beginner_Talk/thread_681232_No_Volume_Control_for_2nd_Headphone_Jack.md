### Post by Matt Nichols on 2008-01-28
I recently bought a new laptop, a Dell XPS M1530. I was happy to find that it works great with Ubuntu, but I have had one problem. It comes with two audio out (headphone) jacks on the front, so two people can listen at once. They both work, but one of them (the second, I think it would be safe to say; it's on the right) is always at full volume no matter what I have the system volume set to be. Is this a problem with my hardware? Or is it a (fixable?) bug in Ubuntu? I would greatly appreciate your input, thanks.

### Post by sciencectn on 2008-01-29
Go to a terminal, and run alsamixer. It shows all of the audio channels and their volumes that the gnome audio configuration programs don't always get.
