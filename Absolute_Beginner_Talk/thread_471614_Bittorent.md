### Post by rovernaut on 2007-06-12
Ok, briefly in laymans simple terms , bittorrents are  little pieces of files that other users of the bittorrent world share with each other.
 Programs like azureus, Utorrent, Korrent etc are used to collect these pieces form other users on the bittorent network.
Ie : say you are looking for a movie of say title "X" to download. Do a search for "X' bittorent, download this little file and save it in your bittorrent program like Azureus.  then start the program off and it will search for peers that have the files you are looking for. 
There are Seeders, these are people who have the full file you are after, and there are Leechers, like you, leechers are people that want the file.
The speed of download depends on how many seeders there are compared to the ratio of leechers.
As you download the 'bits, other torrent users will also upload bits from you, bits that they don't have, but you have and vice versa.
 So everyone shares. After you have successfully downloaded the complete file, ie the movie in this case, you are requested to leave your bittorrent program on to allow others to upload these files from you. So it keeps the bittorrent facility going for all.
Hope it explains it a little;)
