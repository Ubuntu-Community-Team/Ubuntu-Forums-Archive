### Post by Ragnar Bocephus on 2007-08-16
Two ways:

1)  Right-click on the bar and go to Properties-->Background.  Change to whatever color you'd like.

2)  Find a theme you like from [http://gnome-look.org](http://gnome-look.org) and install it.

### Post by kyphi on 2007-08-16
If you mean the top and bottom Panels, yes you can change the colours by a right click on the panel, go to Properties, then Background, put a tick next to Solid Colour and right click on the colour swatch.  Then make your selection..

### Post by Chaotic Thought on 2007-08-16
A program I used was **gnome-color-chooser** which easily allows you to change the panel color without changing your entire GTK theme. The advantage of doing it this way means that all GTK panels will change with it. For example, I run the xfce4-panel concurrently with the gnome-panel, so it means both panels have matching colors.
