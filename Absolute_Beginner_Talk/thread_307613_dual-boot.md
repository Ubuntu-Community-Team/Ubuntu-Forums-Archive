### Post by turbojugend_gr on 2006-11-26
You should boot into UBUNTU LiveCD (also known as Desktop CD), get familiar with it (note you haven't installer it yet, it running from the CD, so it is far slower than after installation, although you can do almost everything you would after an install, including browsing etc). When you feel like installing it, just double-click the desktop shortcut.

It's an easy installation, follow the steps, and when you reach the partitioning stage, be careful, select the empty drive and install it there. In order you feeling more secure follow [this guide]("https://help.ubuntu.com/6.10/ubuntu/installation-guide/i386/index.html"). Of course you can read all of it (using your Ubuntu LiveCD-before and while installing it!), or just the parts you are uncertain of.

WELCOME TO UBUNTU!

Best Regards, TJ.

EDIT: I don't believe that it is necessary to follow this guide, as it is verbose, and therefore can complicate things. You shouldn't have problems with their installer, whenever you fell uncertain about a choice open your firefox (yup while installing, just avoid it while the installer is copying files, since you won't need it that time anyway) and ask here!
