### Post by randcoop on 2007-09-29
Famous articles notwithstanding, it can also be said that there are many, many happy users of the Automatix program.  

The truth is that Ubuntu, like all Linux distros, simply can't be responsive to all users' needs.  That's why Kubuntu and Xubuntu and Edubuntu and Linux Mint and...get created.  And it's also why Automatix was created.

For those of us who have used (K)Ubuntu for a long time, we've found that the longer you use the distro, the less you need Automatix.  But for people starting out, it can be very useful.

The developers of Ubuntu would, I think, be the first to admit that it's extremely difficult to keep up with the demands of ever developing software and a schedule to produce new versions of their product.

Even in a forum of this enormous size, you can search in vain for solutions to hundreds of problems that routinely crop up in using Ubuntu, from making basic wireless connections to the Internet to viewing and listening to the multimedia that's available there.  If Automatix helps some people get through some of those problems, then it's useful, even if it's not supported by the developers of Ubuntu.
