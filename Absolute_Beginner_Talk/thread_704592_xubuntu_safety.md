### Post by monte48lowes on 2008-02-22
You should not need to install a firewall. Ubuntu uses iptables which prevent access to your computer unless a program from 'inside' requests access. grc has a website that will check for open ports. Ubuntu does a great job of passing their test.

### Post by macogw on 2008-02-22
And there are, in total, about 30 viruses for Linux in the wild.  The rest were all proof-of-concept (as in "hey guys, I can do this, this, and this, and then you're pwned.  We should fix that.").  Of the ones in the wild, I can't think of any that are capable of infecting a recent release...you'd have to be a few years out of date.
