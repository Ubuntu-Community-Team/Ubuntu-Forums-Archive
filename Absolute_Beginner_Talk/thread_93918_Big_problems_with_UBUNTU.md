### Post by matthewv on 2005-11-23
right click on the new panel, select Add to Panel... and add a Window List. To get it like the normal panel, you probably also want to add Show Desktop and Workspace Switcher.
For sound, go to a terminal, type in lspci, and try to find the model of your sound card in the output. Post the model here and we'll see what we can do

### Post by Iandefor on 2005-11-23
On the new panel, right click and click on "add to panel"
Select tasklist (Or is it Windowlist? If you can't find one, it's the other). You should be able to position it by dragging and dropping the little handle. You'll probably want to have the little handle at the far left of the screen.

### Post by ubuntuuser on 2005-11-23
To your first question:

Right-click on your new panel and click on something that must be called something like "Add to panel..." (I am running ubuntu in German, sorry).
In the window that appears you'll find an option called window list (again, might also be translated otherwise). Click on it, then click the add button. Et voila...

There will be just a tiny grey vertical bar, which is actually the left border of the window list. You may move it by right-clicking on the bar and selecting "move". Just minimize a few windows and see if it works (it will ;-) ). This way, you can add a lot of useful applets.
