### Post by PhinnFort on 2007-03-07
1. I have the same problem, but I've not cared enough to look into it yet;)
2. I don't know where it is available in System Settings, but if you go into the KDE Control System (probably under "System" in the K-Menu), or go to "Run command" and type in "kcontrol". Then go to "Security and Privacy" and to "Privacy" (or just search for privacy).
3. Run the program "KGPG" from under Utilites in the K-Menu. Now a tiny lock should be in your system tray (lower right of your screen). Right click and click on "Configure KGPG", and go to the "Misc" part. There, click on "Install Shredder", which should place a shredder on your desktop that you can drag files to instead of the trash.
I hope this helps. If anything's still unclear, please just ask.

-PhinnFort

### Post by ajgreeny on 2007-03-07
Also for cookies, you can use konqueror itself and just get rid of those cookies you want to.  I keep various cookies on the machine to allow quicker entry to banking etc, and if you wipe everything away, you need to get account numbers etc each time you go to their site; not very convenient.  Passwords etc are not contained in the cookies, of course, so security is still OK.

From konqueror go to settings>Configure Konqueror.  From here you can manage cookies much better than in kcontrol.
