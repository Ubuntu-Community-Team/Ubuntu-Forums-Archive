### Post by Nythain on 2007-04-21
installing will be a cinch, just format the partitions used for sabayon ( the /, /home, /boot etc) and violla

grub builds its menu list based on what it finds in the /boot so once you format and get the ubuntu stuff there, then it should only consist of ubuntu and vista

i would recomend the torrent download for speed.

The difference between ubuntu and kubuntu is basically the desktop environment and software associated with it... if you are a totall newb, possibly ubuntu since a lot of the forum posts are gonna be directed towards software installed with it (eg gedit, doesnt exist in kubuntu, we have kate and something else i never use) wich can be real frustrating to the complete newb trying to copy past from a forum post... once you get the hang of your system and how everything works, try installing the kubuntu-desktop via apt-get or synaptic and boot into it to see if you like kde more than gnome... personally i do, but others dont.

I dont know how you ran sabayon as long as you did, it took me a few hours to reaslize it installed way to much stuff, and didnt run anywhere near as well as it should have
