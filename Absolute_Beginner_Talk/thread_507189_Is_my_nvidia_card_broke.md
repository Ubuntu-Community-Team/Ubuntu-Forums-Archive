### Post by sad_iq on 2007-07-22
Well haven't read all your other thread...but assuming you tried every possible method to install a driver and failed...well the only advice is to take the card out of the Pc( phisicaly remove it) and take a good look at the aspect it has...make sure the little golden plates(whatever they're called) are perfectly clean and none of them are torn, broken or damaged in any sort(if they are broken your card is doomed).Make sure your card has no rust whatsoever in all it's surface. Also make sure the agp slot is clean, has no foreign object inside, dust, and is not damaged!!!
 When you plug it in make sure it's perfectly plugged(some times it sticks out from one side)! 
 Well that's about all the hardware tips I have right now. If possible try to see if a bios update can be done to your machine and double check your bios settings!!!
