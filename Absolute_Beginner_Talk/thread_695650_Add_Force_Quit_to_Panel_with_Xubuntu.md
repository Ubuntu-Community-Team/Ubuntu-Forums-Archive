### Post by stevejesus on 2008-02-13
Actually, that guide will work great for you.  This is what you do.  When you go to add an application launcher, use the command "xkill".
When you click on it, your cursor will turn into an X or sometimes a skull and crossbones, depending on your icons.
Then just click on the window of the app you want to "force-quit", and it will go away!
There are icons for xkill as well.
This will mimic the function you are used to in Gnome.
