apt stands for Advanced Packaging Tool
get was used in ftp in the days before http. in ubuntu you can use wget to download stuff directly from the net. 
su originally stands for switch user. it was very usefull in the days you needed mainframes to do your calculations. the default user you switch to is root. on *nix desktops (new phenomenon) people actually use su to become root. 
sudo means you want to perform a root task, but without really becomming root. for sudo you don't need to have the root password.
