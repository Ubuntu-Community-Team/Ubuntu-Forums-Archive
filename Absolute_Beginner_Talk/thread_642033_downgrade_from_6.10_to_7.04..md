### Post by macogw on 2007-12-16
Your personal settings should be fine.  That holds things like your background color, stored passwords, firefox bookmarks, etc.  Your wireless settings aren't in your home drive.

Just backup your home drive and reinstall.  Make a partition for /home so that in future, you can reinstall the OS and have your data and files in-tact.  Copy all your home stuff into that /home/youruser part, complete with the hidden dot-files (for your settings), and then you'll have a clean slate for the system part.

I don't know how downgrading will help.  If your wireless didn't work without fiddling on the older one and doesnt work now with the upgrade, when you go back, you're still gonna need to fiddle with wireless again, so what's the point in reinstalling the old one?  Your wireless isn't easy either way.  Why not just ask for help with figuring out the wireless?  Oh, and try wicd or wifi-radar instead of network-manager.  They work better on some wireless cards.
