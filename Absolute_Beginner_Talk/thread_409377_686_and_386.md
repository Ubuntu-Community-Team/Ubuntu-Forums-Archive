### Post by Bachstelze on 2007-04-14
All recent CPUs are 686. Fundamentally, they're the same thing as the old 386 but with lots of improvements. So the 386 kernel will work on every 386-compatible CPU, from the old 486-DX under your desk to your brand new Core 2 Duo. On the other hand, the 686 will work only on (relatively) recent CPUs but will perform better than the 386 one. If you try to boot a 686 kernel on a CPU that is not 686-capable, nothing wil happen, it just won't boot.

You can check [Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I686) for a list of 686-compatible CPUs.
