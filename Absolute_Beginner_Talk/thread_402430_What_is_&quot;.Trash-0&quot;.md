### Post by Happy_Man on 2007-04-05
The trash applet that Ubuntu uses isn't compatible with FAT or NTFS formats, so when you delete something from a storage of that format, it goes to a hidden folder-namely, .Trash-name_of_drive. The only real way to get rid of it is to go plug into a Windows or Mac box and delete it there. It's just one of those little quirks.... hope this helps!
