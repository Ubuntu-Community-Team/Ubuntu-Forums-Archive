I also recently switched to Ubuntu, but do also require the use of Photoshop.  

GIMP is very good, but if you absolutely must have Photoshop, I would suggest installing WINE and then use that to install Photoshop.  I needed the tools in CS2 and just didn't have the time on a particular project to pick it up in GIMP. 

Tho some of the familiar alt commands in PS work a little differently, so be aware of that.  You may need to change some of your keystrokes to get what you want done.

You will need a working (read:licensed version) of Photoshop, but I believe there is a way to move an existing install from a Windows machine to your Linux installation.

This Wiki was very helpful for me:

[http://wiki.winehq.org/AdobePhotoshop](http://wiki.winehq.org/AdobePhotoshop)
