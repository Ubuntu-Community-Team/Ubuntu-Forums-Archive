### Post by rabid9797 on 2007-04-14
After using linux for ~6 months, ive come to accept the fact that, even though linux is becoming much more popular by leaps and bounds(compared to the 90's), the linux userbase is still tiny compared to the amount of people who use windows.
so, for most companies, its not cost effecient to put alot of time, and therefore money, into makeing games/software cross-platform compatible(for linux) because the amount of people who would be buying the software for linux/gnu platforms would be so small it wouldn't make up for the money spent on it.

tl;dr version: time is money, not enough people who use linux to support the userbase.

ive come to accept this as a linux user, and try to get along with it as well as possible. ubuntu has pretty good hardware support, and its getting better all the time. so all we can really do is grin and bear it :)
