### Post by p_quarles on 2007-07-17
> what makes it different?
The major distiction is the organization that supports it. Canonical has its own update/upgrade schedules, which is not tied to anything else. Ubuntu is designed to follow these schedules.

> what is the difference between terminals like Gnome and KDE?
Gnome and KDE are *not* terminals: they are desktop environments. They are different frontends for the X11 windowing system. They both have pretty much the same level of functionality, they just look and feel different.

### Post by RomeReactor on 2007-07-17
Hi. For a basic overview of Ubuntu, you can see[ these]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/WhatIsUbuntu") [pages]("http://www.ubuntu.com/products/whatisubuntu/desktopedition") over at Ubuntu.com, and its [Wikipedia entry]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu_%28Linux_distribution%29") if you haven't already. A few of the things that set Ubuntu apart, for me, are the inclusion of default software like Network Manager and Restricted Drivers Manager (that make it easier to configure on a fresh installation), and Compiz (which admittedly is eye candy, but has a few useful elements); also, other invisible tweaks under the hood, and a 6 month release cycle.

As for the differences in terminals, I don't know much apart from them being specific to each desktop environment ([Gnome-Terminal]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gnome_Terminal") for Gnome, [Konsole]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konsole") for KDE).
