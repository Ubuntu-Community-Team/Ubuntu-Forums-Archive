### Post by crispy_420 on 2007-02-15
First of all what is your budget?

My comments:

Don't go cheap on the power supply, you'll regret it later. How do random reboots sound? Look at Antec models. They are stable and inexpensive (I've been using mine for 4 years now).

Only 160GB hard drive, would not cost much more to double up or add another.

Have you looked at Lite-On DVD burners? On even Plextor has good rebates almost all the time.

7600GT? You can get the next generation (7800's) for just a bit more, and they have 256MB. As a side note if you must get a 7600, look at the EVGA 7600GS. It is faster that many 7800's and has 256MB. As a even larger endorsement, it can be had for less than $200.

Any reason for Vista? For now XP would suit you fine. Look at Vista a year from now. The OS is just too resource hungry. Put those resources to something better, GAMING! Also Vista brags about being the only OS that has DirectX 10. Does that matter? Not now but a year from now yes. But the only DirectX 10 video cards are the NVidia 8800's. And they start around $500. But even when you drop the money on the cards, it won't matter. There are no DirectX 10 games out. They come out in late 2007. So as far as Vista, get XP for now. Let Vista mature and wait for some security fixes (it is Microsoft). Then "upgrade" or dual boot later on.

You could put a nice system together for around $1500. Seems like a lot? Just keep in mind with good choices in parts, you can upgrade latter on down the road (second video card and SLI). The PS3 will remain constant for the next 5 years, the system may look good now but time will tell. Also you can do more than game. This isn't a one trick pony like the PS3.

PS: Rumor has it there will a price cut on the PS3 in either late spring or early summer.

### Post by punx45 on 2007-02-15
PS3 vs. PC is the same as the PC vs. console debate has been for all time.   At the most basic level you are looking at cost vs. flexibility.   The PS3 is $600 (plus games and accessories)   Most gaming PC howtos online usually operate in the $1000 (budget) to $2000 range.   These too would not include games.  (accessories are open to interpretation :-/ )   The PC lets you do anything a PC can do.   The console lets you play games. (of course the PS3 specifically complicates this.)

You need to ask yourself a few questions such as (but not limited to):
How often will i game?
what games do I normally play?
are the games I want to play more readily available for a console or a PC?
do I have a PC that I can upgrade to play games at a reasonable quality, and thereby save money?

you get the idea.

The best part about the debate is there are no wrong answers, as long as your are satisfied with the decision you make.

Personally, I kinda want a PS3 to linuxify it.   I think it has great potential as an HD MythTV frontend, and its a game console/blueray player to boot!  But I also just built a Myth complete SD system for ~$450 so...

### Post by Velotix on 2007-02-17
Hm. A PS3 versus a gaming PC. You can argue back and forth about the hardware all you like, but the ** real** question is actually quite simple:

*Which games would you rather play: the games on the PC, or the games on the PS3?*

Assuming the answer isn't the PS3 (and honestly, I haven't heard of any truly standout game on the platform at this point - in fact, the PS2 is only just starting to pump out some of the greatest games it'll ever have, so right now you'd probably be better off with PS2), then first things first:

A gaming PC with Vista is a gaming PC crippled, crushed and wasted. Vista, due to 64-bit and DX10, will eventually be more useful, but right now gaming is horrifically crippled on Vista; you are much, MUCH better off with XP and DX9. When the time comes and DX10 games finally show their faces, you will do yourself a huge favour by dual-booting XP and Vista, or triple-booting XP, Vista and Linux if you're using that as well.

Similarly, your choice of the GeForce 7600 is a good idea in theory, but the 7900 and 7950 are definitely worth the extra $$. I'm using the Gainward BLISS GeForce 7900GS which has 512MB of RAM on its own, and I'm getting SO frustrated because without decent DirectX support that card is nowhere near running at its full potential. It's a crying shame. However, I bought it originally because it was only £160 (so it'll probably be about US$160 in the US even though £160 is almost twice that - we get ripped off on electronics here D: ).

Beyond that, you haven't told us what your budget is, so we can't really be more specific until you do. Gaming rigs tend to be very pricy, but well worth the money so long as you pick the affordable, quality parts.
