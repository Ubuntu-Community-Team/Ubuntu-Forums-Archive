### Post by earlra on 2008-04-16
Hi,

I am having the same experience.  I can tell you why this happens, but so far I have not found a way to fix it.  It happens because of the way GMail handles files by labels, and their preference for archiving over deleting, as explained on:

[http://mail.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?answer=78755&topic=12815](http://mail.google.com/support/bin/answer.py?answer=78755&topic=12815)

As a workaround, I drag messages to the Gmail/Trash folder.  They will then automatically be deleted in 30 days, or I just empty the trash whenever I access my Gmail account online.

That is not as convenient as hitting the Delete button, but it's the only answer I have found so far.  I am hoping to find a way to change the configuration in Evolution, because I know an email client can 'delete' Gmail IMAP properly - for example Kmail does.

Let me know if you find a solution before I do ;-)
