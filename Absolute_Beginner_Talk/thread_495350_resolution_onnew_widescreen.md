### Post by NT4usB on 2007-07-08
There appears to be a known issue with wide screens. Here's part of a post from a.o.l.u:

Okay, finally got it working, but it wasn't simple, so I'm going to 
detail what I finally did to get it working. The problem doesn't affect 
any particular video driver -- it affects them all! The problem is a 
known issue for Xfree86 and wide-screen monitors, which are mostly LCD 
of course.

First of all, don't bother to follow the common Ubuntu 
FixVideoResolutionHowto (found at [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FixVideoResolutionHowto](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/FixVideoResolutionHowto) ), it won't 
work for this problem. That howto basically tells you to rerun the autodetect script again, i.e. dpkg-reconfigure. No matter how many times you rerun that script, it will never be able to detect your widescreen monitor, so don't bother! The following Xorg bug report describes the problem:

[https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xorg/+bug/49827](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/xorg/+bug/49827)

There's some suggestions in the above bug report about how to work 
around it, but most of it is unnecessary. The only thing you need to do 
is manually edit the xorg.conf file and add "ModeLine" parameters under 
the "Monitor" section. Here's mine:

> Section "Monitor"
>         Identifier      "Generic Monitor"
>         Option          "DPMS"
>         #HorizSync      28-51
>         HorizSync       30-82
>         #VertRefresh    43-60
>         VertRefresh     56-60
>         # 1440x900 @ 60.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 55.92 kHz; pclk: 106.47 MHz
>         Modeline "1440x900_60.00"  106.47  1440 1520 1672 1904  900 901 904 932  -HSync +Vsync
> EndSection

The proper modeline parameters can be easily calculated online from the 
following website (just enter your monitor X & Y screen dimensions and 
your desired refresh rate, 60Hz, in most cases):

[http://www.sh.nu/nvidia/gtf.php](http://www.sh.nu/nvidia/gtf.php)

Then you copy and paste its output to your xorg.conf file under the 
Monitor section. Then you add the "1440x900_60.00" (or whatever your 
desired resolution was) as one of the "Modes" under the  "Screen" 
section, and the "Display" subsection. Here's mine:

> Section "Screen"
>         ...
>         SubSection "Display"
>                 Depth           24
>                 #Modes          "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
>                 Modes           "1440x900_60.00" "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
>         EndSubSection
> EndSection

So that's it. Hope it helps. I suspect with the preponderance of 
wide-screen monitors these days, a lot of people are going to be running 
into this problem, unless Xorg finally fixes this.
