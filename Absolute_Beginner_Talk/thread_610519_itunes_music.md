### Post by Rhubarb on 2007-11-12
One way (not perfect though) would be to burn your music to audio CD in itunes, then use Sound Juicer to rip the music into suitable mp3s / aacs / ogg vorbis files.
You will get a loss in quality of the music as a result of this, the only way to find out exactly how much loss in quality is to try it yourself.

Other ways of stripping the DRM out of your music would be to try and locate some software that does this for you.  Try performing a search on the internet for "itms drm strip" or something similar.

This one looks promising: [http://hymn-project.org/](http://hymn-project.org/)
Here is a guide to using hymn: [http://www.engadget.com/2004/05/18/pts-how-to-tuesday-how-to-play-purchased-music-on-other/](http://www.engadget.com/2004/05/18/pts-how-to-tuesday-how-to-play-purchased-music-on-other/)
