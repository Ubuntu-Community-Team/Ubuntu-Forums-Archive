### Post by someusernoob on 2006-07-15
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=169749&highlight=nzb](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=169749&highlight=nzb)

Here you'll find the HOWTO for Hellanzb. Great program.

Par2 and cksfv are 2 applications for checking the downloaded stuff. They are command-line only, but easy to use tho. Hellanzb uses par2 too, but you dont have to worry about that. After configuring Hellanzb you put a nzb in a folder, wait, and when its finished downloading, your files are extracted and ready to use/listen/view.

Pan is pretty good tho, you've got to install from the .deb file, not the one from the repo's, thats an old version. I only use Pan for some small groups when some files arent listed on the nzb search engines.

[Pan .deb file.]("http://darrenalbers.com/Pan/")
