### Post by AusIV4 on 2007-09-16
About a year back I ran into a similar problem - all of my 5 allotted machines were authorized and I wanted to authorize a VM.

I discovered that if you go into your account settings in iTunes, you can deauthorize all of your machines, then re-authorize a few or start re-authorizing. I'd give you exact details as to how to do this, but I don't have iTunes available on my current machine.

As far as removing DRM, you can either burn to CD's and rip (with quality loss, if you rip to a lossy codec), or search the web for legally dubious tools to remove the DRM. They are out there.
