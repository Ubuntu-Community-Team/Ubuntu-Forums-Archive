### Post by intangible on 2005-06-18
Unless you already have a proprietary case of some kind, most motherboards and cases are compatible.  In ATX cases there's a big square hole where the ports come out and the motherboard comes with a metal plate that fits in that hole and meshes with the mobo ports.

The only thing you need to worry about is make sure the case is big enough for the mobo you buy (if you get a Full ATX mobo, you need a case that can hold that, but a micro ATX mobo will go into a Full ATX case with no problem).

Also, make sure the case you buy comes with a powerful enough power supply, a name brand power supply would be an even better bet.  Antec makes good power-supplies.  You do not want to skimp on that oft-looked over part; power supply problems are hard to diagnose.
