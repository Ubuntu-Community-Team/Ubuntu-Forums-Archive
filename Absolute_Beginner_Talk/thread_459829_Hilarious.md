### Post by drmbogo on 2007-05-31
This Thread should probably be in the "Experiences" section, but I too find it funny that MS seems to be backing themselves into a corner.
I first started fooling around w/linux, maybe 8 yrs ago, at a point when I was much less computer-literate, and found it just too difficult to use. I understood what people liked about it: flexibility, scalability, security, et al, but I just didn't know how to use it!
I started messing w/ Ubuntu about 3 months ago, and have hardly looked back. A lot has changed in my life in 8 yrs, which has allowed me the time, as Simran said, to learn. I started out setting up my home computer dual-boot, w/ Ubuntu and XP, and despite reinstalling a few times, I haven't booted into windows in weeks! I kept the XP partition 'just in case', but I really haven't had to use it.
At work, I have 2 desktops running Ubuntu only, and a laptop running XP, just for Quickbooks. On the Ubuntu boxes, I've reinstalled at least a dozen times total, trying different things, and I agree: It's so fast and easy, that I find myself trying things that I know are risky, as far a destabilizing the os, things I would never try on the XP box, because I don't want the hassle of reinstalling XP.
I'm a tweaker by nature; I just can't leave things well enough alone, lol! I love having the the flexibility that linux offers in terms of 'environment' and that there are so many ways to skin a cat! The multitude if distros out there as certainly confusing at first, but after trying a few, and reading about others, things really began to make sense. I've also overcome my fear of the command line! One of the first things I do now, when reinstalling, is add a few aliases to .bashrc. I think what I'm saying is that linux offers a level of 'tweak-ability' that appeals to me.
