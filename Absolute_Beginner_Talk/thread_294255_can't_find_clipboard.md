### Post by ReaderRat on 2006-11-06
ReaderRat is my signature.
Go to the menu, System->Administration->Synaptic Package Manager. Enter your password. Click on 'Search'. Enter 'firefox'(without the quotes). Wait until it finds firefox. Right click on 'firefox' in the 'Packages' window. click on 'Mark for Installation'., then press Enter. Synaptic will gather any dependencies (other necessary files), then press Enter again. Firefox should be installed then (you may have to wait awhile while Synaptic downloads files.

### Post by ReaderRat on 2006-11-06
You have a Clipboard if you have Cut, Copy, & Paste. You cut or copy what you want, and the clipboard holds it for you until you paste it where you want. When you cut or copy anything the clipboard holds it until you cut or copy something else.
