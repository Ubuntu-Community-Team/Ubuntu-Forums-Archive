### Post by nickoli_02 on 2007-02-23
Welcome to linux! Embrace it, if you're really serious about giving Ubuntu a shot, have patience and be prepared to learn. You're going to break things, then you'll learn how to fix things, how to do things the hard way, and then later the easy way :)

I'd recommend sticking with Dapper Drake for the time being. Edgy installs xorg 7.1 which has a little issue with direct rendering with the ATI cards (at least, this is the issue I've had... my card is a Radeon Mobility X700).

Also, before trying to get all the pretty eyecandy working it's best if you stick to what works until you get your feet under you in Ubuntu. Beryl is still in the alpha stage of development, which means that bugs and crashes can be quite common. Don't get too far ahead of yourself :)

As for games... I don't really know how well games run in wine. If you have the space for it, why not keep your Windows partition around so you know you can still switch over and play a game if you can't get it working in wine?

Now, drivers. Since you have an ATI card, check out [this guide]("http://wiki.cchtml.com/index.php/Ubuntu_Dapper_Installation_Guide") to install the fglrx driver for your card. Be sure to write down the steps or print off the page before installing because it's very possible that you won't get a GUI when you first install Ubuntu (don't let this scare you :) ). This brings me to another point, download the alternate install CD, not the desktop install, as the video drivers the live CD will want to load most likely won't work with your card.

Well, that turned into quite a long reply... Anyway, to wrap things up, I'd seriously consider dual booting XP for the time being until you get used to Ubuntu. Just give it a shot and keep in mind the advice all these helpful people in the forums offer and you'll probably be Microsoft free in no time :D
