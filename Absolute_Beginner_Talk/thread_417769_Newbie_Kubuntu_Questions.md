### Post by teddybairs1 on 2007-04-21
If you really, really want to you can use Clamav from the repositories. It's a linux antivirus. Do you need to? No. But if it makes you feel better.

The truth is that no, you don't need an Anti-Virus in Ubuntu. For all intents and purposes it's bulletproof against them for the moment because they're almost all written for windows. The viruses that do exist for Linux aren't in circulation any more.

Panda software also offers an antivirus for linux, but it just slows down your computer and acts like a virus itself.

The truth about most antivirus software like Norton or McAffee, even for windows, is that they are basically malware themselves that don't really do a lot of good. The only thing which really works to protect you from viruses to begin with is common sense. Don't open emails from people you don't know. Delete spam when you see it in your inbox. Be careful as to which sites you visit, etc.

I know a guy who runs windows 2000, a tech no less, on his own systems. Doesn't use an anti-virus, and never gets infected. And the reason isn't the os or the anti-virus; it's just that he uses common sense. But I digress...

I tried running both clamav and panda "just to be on the safe side". After a while I realized that they were the only things slowing down my system, and I really didn't need them. It's like getting regular flu shots when you can't get the flu to begin with. There's no point.

### Post by z1p101 on 2007-04-21
Most of it has to do with the kernel
Windows uses a micro kernel which uses many outside programs to make the machine work. EX drivers.
UNIX and Linux systems use a monolithic Kernel which is much more self contained and safe but does not allow for on the fly updates.
MacOS is the same, it's just BSD that they have tweaked for there purposes.

Also, virus writers want to attack as many users as possible with the least effort and you won't find that here.
