### Post by Bartender on 2006-01-29
I'm stuck with dial-up at home and have been unable to go online.  
After installing Ubuntu I went into the networking utility (this was all mousing and simple clicking, no command line) and enabled the PCI network interface card.  Then I lugged the thing into work one nite, unplugged the Ethernet cable from one of the work computers, jacked it into my machine, and turned it on.  I had no idea what would happen.  Firefox went online like a horse headed for the barn.  Whoo-hoo  No dinking around with DHCP, DIN, or any of those other acronyms.  It was a gas playing around with Synaptic and apt-get.   
This is only one newb's uninformed guess so it's not worth much.  If you're not going to incorporate any exotic or bleeding-edge hardware AND you have broadband chances are good that you'll be one happy camper.  I like that you're starting with a blank drive rather than risking data.  That's what I did.  Us older guys aren't such risk-takers.  
The only hardware problem that I ran into, and you'll see lots of talk about this on the forums, is that Nvidia graphics products seem to work better than ATI.  My ATI 9200SE card had problems with the screen saver.  Bought an Nvidia card, problem went away.
I started out with just Ubuntu on this machine, then tried Kubuntu, then went back to Ubuntu.  During the last go-round I tried dual-booting W2K, following the directions for Ubuntu + NTFS at
 [http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/](http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/)
If you've got a legit copy of Windows you might want to think about trying this.  I mention this while you're in the planning stages because it's easier to do Windows first, then add Ubuntu rather than the other way around.
Don't forget, the LiveCD will be much slower on your machine than the installed OS!
