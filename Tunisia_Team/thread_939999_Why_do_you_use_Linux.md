### Post by hanen105 on 2008-10-06
*** **It's more suited for the job of a desktop.
* It gives me the freedom of controlling my laptop/desktop rather than being controlled!!
* I want to learn as much as I can in my life time. for that, I guess Linux is the best cos it is open source. I can learn its internals and how each and every process/program run in the machine
* I get all my programming tools free (as in freedom) and I have no restrictions to use them (In Linux, I have freedom to anything and everything. nothing is impossible in Linux)
* No viruses (more or less)
* Awesome networking tools, stealthy, uncomplicated, extensive assistance without having to scour Google search to get fixes and support. No adware/spamware browser slow-downs....granular security! 
* it's fast and doesn't frequently crash
* compiz fusion is giving gr8 graphics effects
* there are some many freewares available for it. 
* different, customizable and funny to use (outstanding from the  crowd) 

But I have prob. with it. it requires me to download large packages recurrently. 

The updates are tiny though [IMG]http://www.orkut.com/img/smiley/i_smile.gif[/IMG]
