### Post by amklinux on 2006-05-23
I have a G4, and the first distribution I used was Yellow Dog 3. Now I use Ubuntu Breezy, and it does have advantages and drawbacks. The advantages of Ubuntu to me are:
- many more people involved in the project and using the product. Very easier to get answers to your questions than with YDL.
- up-to-date software (Ok, I never used YDL 4).
The main drawback of Ubuntu is the video playing performance. I was able to get a by far better performance on YDL when compared to Ubuntu. I tried many hints posted in the forum to improve the video performance, but with too poor responses.  So, I'm really disappointed with this issue. amklinux

### Post by surroundu on 2006-05-23
Never tried YDL, but...

I also inherited a B&W G3 rev 1. Mine's the 300 Mhz/6 GB HDD model with 192 MB RAM. Dapper runs well, if a bit slow. However, it's an OLD piece of hardware, and I expected it. It's pretty amazing that something so old [yet gorgeous] can perform relatively well with a modern OS. I was running OS 9.22 with little complaint, except for the browser. I got tired of IE 5.5, and wanted a mainstream browser like FF, and didn't want to shell out the cash for OS X. Even older copies of Jaguar on ebay were $35-40. There were a couple other nitpicks, but nothing major. You should give Breezy a shot. It's new life injected into a classic machine.
