### Post by scourge on 2007-10-05
I just can't take this anymore. My mom always warned me about 64-bit operating systems. Somehow she knew that the good ol' x86 was a better architecture. I of course didn't believe her, 64-bit seemed so awesome. I mean, according to my calculator 64 is twice as much as 32. Twice! How's a young impressionable idiot like myself going to ignore that?

So anyway, there I was, installing this OS that was supposed to be twice as great as the old one. At first I thought I had accidentally popped in the 32-bit install disc because the 64-bit Ubuntu looked exactly the same as the 32-bit Ubuntu! I couldn't feel the awesomeness of the extra bits at all. Still, patience being my best virtue, I decided to give my new OS some time to prove itself. And that's when the problems started.

One sad Monday I came home from work, exhausted and tired. It was very late and I decided to cheer myself up by watching an episode of Penn & Teller ******** that aired that day... only to find out the stupid 64-bit SOB hadn't recorded the show for me. All of the 64 bits were just laying there, doing nothing. What kind of a retarded OS can't figure this stuff out? When I still used a 32-bit OS I never had to work late and I never missed an episode of ********. This alone would have made most people to wreck their computer with a sledgehammer and kick their dog, but I still gave the OS one more chance.

So last weekend it finally happened: Ubuntu x86-64 failed me for the last time. I was playing Neverputt, and missed 4 putts on the first hole! Guess how many times that has happened on 32-bit? That's right, IT NEVER HAPPENED, NOT EVEN ONCE! By all logic I should have been able to putt with twice the precision I had earlier, but instead I got betrayed and humiliated by my OS. I'd had enough, so I burned my 64-bit-infested hard drive, shredded the install cd and reminded myself to listen to my mom.

Parents, don't let your kids use a 64-bit OS.
