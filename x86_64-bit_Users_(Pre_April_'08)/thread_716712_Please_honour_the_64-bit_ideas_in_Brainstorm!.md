### Post by UBfusion on 2008-03-06
Please read, submit your thoughts and vote on [**idea #3336: Focus on 64-bit desktop system**]("http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/3336/") on Ubuntu Brainstorm!

Let's propose ways to foster the development and popularity of 64-bit Ubuntu.

Please post on Brainstorm (not here!) why you need better 64-bit, what are your bigger annoyances and what you suggest could be done to improve the situation.

There are very few (less than 10) 64-bit related Brainstorm ideas. This risks creating the illusion that 64-bit computing is dead and will stay dead.

### Post by felker2 on 2008-03-06
I use 64-bit Ubuntu both on my Athlon X2 with 4GB and on my 399 Euro laptop (Celeron 530), and I don't think this 64-bit Brainstrom idea needs my support: I see no reason **not** to use 64-bit. In other words: just use it. That's my advice to others too. Reason: Everything works. Flash just works. Binary GPU drivers just work. (I haven't tried Skype, because I don't like Skype anymore.)

And as soon as computers have 4GB RAM or more, people will need a 64-bit OS to fully utilize that amount of RAM. So that will happen automagically. ;-)
