### Post by cmost on 2007-02-22
I would start with searching the forums on keywords:  Beryl, Compiz, AIGLX.  Beryl blows the doors off both Mac OS-X and Vista in terms of desktop effects.  I suggest you visit their forums for further information  (see:  [http://forum.beryl-project.org/](http://forum.beryl-project.org/))  Beryl is a fork of Compiz and is renowned for its plethora of super fancy visual effects.  Beryl is in rapid development and sometimes resorts to less than standard programming techniques to achieve cutting edge results.  Compiz, on the other hand, is developed by a Novell developer  who prizes clean efficient code and rock solid stability over a multitude of eye-catching effects.  As I said before, both Compiz and Beryl offer much more than either Windows or OS-X in terms of visual eye candy.  As for Matisse, it's not really a 3D desktop per se.  It's more of a useability enhancement and a different way of doing things.  I'd recommend combining both for the ultimate Linux experience.  Good luck.
