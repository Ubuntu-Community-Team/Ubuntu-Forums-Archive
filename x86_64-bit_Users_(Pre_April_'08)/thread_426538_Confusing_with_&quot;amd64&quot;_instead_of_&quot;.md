### Post by jespdj on 2007-04-28
I agree, it should be called **x86-64** instead of AMD64.

AMD invented the 64-bit extensions to the x86 instruction set, and their implementation is called AMD64. However Intel has copied AMD's extensions and calls them EM64T. There are very small differences between AMD64 and EM64T, but these are so small that you normally don't notice them.

The 64-bit version of Ubuntu is probably made officially for AMD64 and that's why they call it AMD64 instead of x86-64.

See [Wikipedia for details on x86-64](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/x86-64) and the differences between AMD's and Intel's implementation.
