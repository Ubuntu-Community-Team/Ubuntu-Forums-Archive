### Post by zluka on 2006-06-03
i've installed ubuntu 64bit yesterday, and had little time to play with it.. today i began "the tuning" and needed the flash player. i had forgotten about it, and macromedia slapped me in the face "no 64bit version" :( 

then i remembered about swiftfox, and before tweaking firefox and cause possible problems during import, i decided to install it first. while browsing some news site, they had (of course :???: ) some flash ads (and i have no "noscript" or "adblock" yet), so swiftfox asked me about missing plugins, and when i clicked "install", worked like a charm... :mrgreen: 

just wanted to tell you people about it, don't know if it's a known issue, or is it just my luck, but we got it..

ps: i just unpack the swiftfox package, place it under the home directory, and point the "firefox" shortcut there (swiftfox.sh i guess) works without any problems, so there's no need to install, configure, copy, backup anything ;) great

love ubuntu..
