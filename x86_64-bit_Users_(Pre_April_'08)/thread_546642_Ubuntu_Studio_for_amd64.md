### Post by nowhere.elysium on 2007-09-09
Audacity (GPL 'version' of Cool Edit Pro) for recording, or possibly Ardour (GPL 'version' of Garageband) for recording and sequencing, and I believe that Hydrogen is the anme of the drum machine that I last used. It sounds a bit 'MIDI-ish', if you know what I mean, but it works well, and you can filter the sound a bit to make a touch more convincing.

The Ubuntu Studio metapackages are actualyl split into the relevant parts: Audio, Video, 2D/3D and Plugins: you can find them in the 64-bit repos, so I'm guessing that the software's either got a 64-bit version, or 32-bit with flawless integration. It's not going to take up all your 160GB by any stretch of the imagination. Even Final Cut Pro Studio, the hugest piece of bloatware EVER only (hah!) takes up 30-35GB, so don't worry too much about that. You're probably looking at about 3-4 GB for EVERYTHING.
