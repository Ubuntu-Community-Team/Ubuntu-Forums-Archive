You may wish to consider dual-booting if you can spare the hard-disk space. That way, if amd64 doesn't work out for you, you still have your 32-bit install.

### Post by BIGtrouble77 on 2007-01-25
Kilz is right, I'm getting peeved by the ignorant 'don't use an amd64 build because you can't use flash' comments.

You can run many 32bit apps under the 64bit kernel, some apps just need a few special 32bit libraries.  If you use automatix2 you will have great success running many 32bit apps like firefox, flash, google earth,  32bit codecs, skype, opera, etc.

The 64bit repos are phenominal.  Only thing you might have trouble with are some uncommon apps that aren't suuported well in either case.  Some of the howto's here many also require a little extra enginuity to get working. 

Many multimedia and graphics apps will offer up to 30% improvement in 64bit.  If you don't use number crunching apps then it may not be worth it to upgrade.
