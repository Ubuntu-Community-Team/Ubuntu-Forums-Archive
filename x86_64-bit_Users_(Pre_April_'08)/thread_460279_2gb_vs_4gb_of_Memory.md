### Post by shad0w_walker on 2007-05-31
I have 4gb in my system and to be honest, i dont really notice a diffrence in performance from when i had 1gb, but if you want to get it then it certainly doesnt hurt. It mostly depends on what your doing with it, normal desktop usage 1gb is plenty enough. Heaving media type stuff (Video editing, large picture files, etc) would obviously benefit from plenty of ram. The database stuff could also benefit as you could keep massive tables in RAM.

At the end of the day its mostly a preference, if you have the cash, dont have anything better to spend it on or simply want the 4gb, go for it, at the least the machine will make a decent server when its retired from desktop use.

Just an a side note, theres is also the possibility of running a couple of gig of unused ram as a ramdisk.
