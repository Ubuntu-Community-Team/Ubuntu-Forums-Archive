### Post by FrozenFOXX on 2007-03-20
The only real solution you've got is to deconstruct their argument.  Look at Slashdot, we get FUD in there all the time.  What do we do about it?  Ignore, laugh, and deconstruct their arguments.  Oh sure, it leads to a bit of division but it's not any worse for wear, really.


The other way is the MS way which is to beat your chest and proclaim the overwhelming awesomeness that is 64-bit.  Works for them most of the time if it's actually a solid product.  For example look at the Xbox 360...proclaimed to be the next big thing and they were a lot louder than the detractors.  What happened?  Well, the Xbox 360 is actually a solid, awesome product and made good on every chest-beating promise so now there's over 10+ million of them out there.


Now this kind of thing CAN backfire of course.  If you promise the world and beat your chest about it but it doesn't deliver you'll just end up crippling yourself (Sony's PS3 to continue the console analogy).

I think at the moment 64-bit Linux in general is something that's in a murky state where people know it's there, they've heard some benefits, they've heard some FUD, but nobody on the outside REALLY knows what it's like but not enough to scare off everyone.  All it needs is a little shaping in message.

I think the main issues with the 64-bit desktop linux world is simply that its ahead of its time and before you go "but AMD has been releasing 64-bit processors for years!" let me explain:

The software of yesterday is still what drives the world as it migrates into the software of tomorrow and thus in the current state of being we have media codecs, flash player, wine, etc. on 32-bit hardware and not on 64-bit (yes I know there are work arounds for these things but lets be honest with ourselves and admit that they are not actually supported on 64-bit), why? Because 32-bit been around longer and people have been developing upon it as a platform as opposed to 64-bit. I honestly think it will take 5-10 more years for 64-bit platforms to be as supported as 32-bit ones are now in respect to software for the desktop.
