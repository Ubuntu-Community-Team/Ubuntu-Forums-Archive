### Post by jdp on 2006-05-27
Lay the blame at [Adobe and their distinct lack of Linux PPC](http://www.adobe.com/shockwave/download/alternates/) offerings.  Flash is a closed format, and there are people trying to write players.  Of course, since they don't know how the format is supposed to work fully, the free implementations don't really work.  It's the same problem for many other technologies, but Flash happens to be one of the current most requested missing features.

EDIT:  I just sent their Customer Service an email with their online form:
> There is a distinct lack of media players for Flash and Shockwave under Linux on PowerPC hardware.  There are, however, players for Linux on Intel compatible hardware, and for Mac OS X on PowerPC hardware.  Is it really that difficult to combine the knowldge of the two and release a usable player for Linux PPC?  There are Flash 7 compatible players avaiable for multiple UNIX-like systems, including non-Intel systems such as Sun SPARC systems.  Surely the issue is not a technical one, but most likely a business decision to meet market demands.  With the further limitation of what hardware can run newer versions of OS X, and the further end of the PowerPC as the CPU in Macintosh machines, running Linux on PPC will only continue to rise in popularity and the support of projects like Debian and Ubuntu will grow.  Please help these projects make content in your formats available to users of PPC hardware, as you already do for people who runs the same operating system on a different CPU platform.
