### Post by Modred on 2007-11-05
WINE is free software and also does not cost anything to use (if you think I'm being redundant, please [check your definition of free](http://www.gnu.org/philosophy/free-sw.html)).  But considering that most Windows programs are non-free, how large of an etchical issue would it be rather you use a free or non-free (Cedega, CrossOver) program to run them?  Either way, you are still running non-free software.

From a practicality perspective, whichever can best run the games you want to play would probably be your best bet (although I must point out that CrossOver contributes to WINE, while, to my knowledge, Cedega does not, so at least buying CrossOver over Cedega indirectly contributes to the community).
