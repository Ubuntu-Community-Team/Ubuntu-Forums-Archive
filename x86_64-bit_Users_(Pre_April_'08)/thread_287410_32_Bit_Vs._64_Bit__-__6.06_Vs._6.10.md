### Post by qurly63 on 2006-10-29
From my newbie's perspective I would suggest 32 bit might be the way to go.  If Linux and Ubuntu are new to you you'll probably get a better "out of the box" experience with the 32 bit because of the occasional compatibility issues that seem to crop up with 64 bit.  Once you're comfortable with the environment its pretty easy to upgrade and by then you can more easily tackle the tweaking necessary to make 64 bit run smooth with all your favourite apps.
