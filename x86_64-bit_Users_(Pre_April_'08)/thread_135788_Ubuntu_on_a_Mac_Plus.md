### Post by jdp on 2006-02-24
Those wouldn't be called "Mac Plus" too.  The Mac Plus is an actual model, as is the Mac Classic.  They would be classic Macs, old AIO Macs (All-In-One), etc.  But, calling them SE/30 lets us all know exactly what they are.  The SE/30 is basically the best of the black & white display AIO Macs.  You can run NetBSD/OpenBSD, Debian and other 68K based BSD flavors and Linux distros.  Ubuntu is probably way too heavy, even if there was a 68k port.  Running Debian 68k will probably be the closest you can come to Ubuntu.
