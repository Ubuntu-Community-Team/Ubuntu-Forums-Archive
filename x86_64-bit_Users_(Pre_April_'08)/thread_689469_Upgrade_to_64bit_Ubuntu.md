### Post by MariusSilverwolf on 2008-02-06
Well, from my experience of running Ubuntu64 on my desktop and Ubuntu32 on two laptops, I'd suggest sticking with Ubuntu32 if you're using your system in a standard home environment,.

Ubuntu64 added some stability for me, but it also made getting programs like Flash and Gyachi to work fluidly an increased challenge, with a great deal of headaches and following How-To's for hours.

Unless you'll be using the system as a server, as long as you've got everything in your current setup working just fine, don't change it.

<end my two cents>

### Post by speedkreature on 2008-02-06
64-bit operating systems are the future.  This is exactly what the industry did with the switch from 16-bit to 32-bit.  Things were a little bumpy at first, but we managed to get along.  No major OS has gotten it just right as of right now.  I am currently testing Hardy, and for as long as a 64-bit arch has been available, I've been pushing it.
I will concede that I haven't used a 64-bit edition of Ubuntu until Gutsy, and I made that transition only three days ago.  What kept me away until now?  Most of my systems are production machines and the hurdles to achieve the same level of functionality as a 32-bit system weren't acceptable.

That is no longer the case (and honestly, hasn't been for a little while).

I do a fair amount of 3D animation and I can say with absolute confidence that making the switch from Gutsy 32-bit to Gutsy 64-bit was well worth it.  On every system I have upgraded, I'm seeing a 25-30% reduction in rendering time.  This has done wondrous things for reducing my operating costs and I can now shorten my deadlines allowing for more projects/stages to be crammed into a given time period.
