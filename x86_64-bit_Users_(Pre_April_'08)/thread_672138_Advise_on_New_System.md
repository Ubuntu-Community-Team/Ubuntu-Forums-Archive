### Post by steveneddy on 2008-01-20
Yeah - on the RAM issue, you really want to run equal amounts of RAM. So the 2 1 Gig sticks or maybe 4 1 Gig sticks, but don't use the 2 512 Gig sticks with the 2 1 Gig sticks. 

Motherboards and OS's like to see the same amounts of RAM is each slot and all in even numbers. 

If you have 4 slots, either fill 1 slot, 2 slots with equal amounts of RAM or 4 slots with equal amounts of RAM.

Opinion only, go Intel. Core 2 Duo or quad core Intel.

### Post by mperry on 2008-01-21
I just did a new AMD64 system myself. Got the whole shebang from Newegg.  It shook out like this:

Board:	 ECS GeForce6100SM-M2
Video Card: MSI NX8500GT-TD512E GeForce 8500GT 512MB
Memory: 4gb DDR2 800
CPU:  AMD Athlon 64 X2 6000+ Windsor

I'm very satisfied with the purchase and the stuff was a great deal at Newegg including a combo deal I got for the board and cpu.  The integrated sound and networking work okay.

I'm using a SATA 750g seagate drive for the main part of things and a 3ware two port sata raid card with two 320g sata drives in a RAID1.  This system does a few different things for me but the prices are so reasonable, I went after replacing all my older Pentium 4 systems with AMD64 stuff.  One cautionary note is that when I installed Ubuntu 7.10 AMD64, I had a rather interesting time with the screen just blanking out for minutes to the point where I thought something was wrong. I had another system which requires manual intervention because Ubuntu 7.10 wanted to use my on board video card even though its disabled in the bios.

If you want to do a comparison shop, Newegg and Ewiz are pretty nice.
