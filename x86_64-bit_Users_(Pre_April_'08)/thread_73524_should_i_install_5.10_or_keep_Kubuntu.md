### Post by Drain on 2005-10-09
Well, I'm running Hoary (5.4) on my AMD64 server, and it's been wonderfully stable. I've recently upgraded my "regular" x86 box to Breezy (Kubuntu 5.10), and aside from a little snafu on reboot, it's been great. 

However, here's my thought about upgrading my AMD64 box: I get a lot of large updates with my daily running of apt-get, for the x86 box. I'm gonna hold off for a bit on my upgrade of my AMD64 box (probably just a week or two) and see if any glaringly awful bugs pop up. Hopefully as Breezy becomes stable and a little more "set", the need for some massive changes will decrease. I'm really looking forward to several of the little things that are part of this release, but I figure by waiting a little bit, I'll avoid any major frustrations with the upgrade process.
