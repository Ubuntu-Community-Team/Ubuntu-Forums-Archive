### Post by gratefulfrog on 2005-12-07
well, the answer is it all depends: do you need anything in 64 bit? From what you say probably not. Is it worth the effort to configure? well there is a bit more than that. There's a good chance that stuff won't work, like some Firefox plugins realplayer, and other stuff. if you look through this forum and Hoary 64bit before it, you'll see that we've all had our share of problems.  

I run the 64bit platform because Ihave some IT savy and feel that if I can't make the effort, then no one can. If no one does, then there will never be a 64bit linux, and we'll all have to fall back to that other proprietary, low service, so called OS...

It's a lot of effort, if you want all your hardware to work at top performance - some things just will not work, like Kino. But then you can always make a 32bit chroot that will take care of most - but not all - problems.

Finally, you may be surprised to know that many packages available for 32bit are simply not available in 64bit, and cannot be built by hand either...

All that to say, without any reason to move, I wouldn't, unless you're an adventurer and ready to pay with your time...
