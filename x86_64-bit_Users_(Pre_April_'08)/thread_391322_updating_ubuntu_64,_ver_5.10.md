You have a few options. I also recommend upgrading as 5.10 will be at end of life next month. There will be no more updates for it, and Breezy has issues. There are 2 current releases, and one version is in the works. Yor options are.

Dapper Drake - Supported untill 2009
Edgy Eft - Supported untill 2008
Feisty Fawn - Not released yet, but the first beta should be released Friday. When released you will have 18 months of life.

All the releases are good.
Dapper, its very stable and has few if any issues. It was the first long term release, there will be more in the future. Long term releases are for those that dont want to have to upgrade every 18 months. The releases will get security and other updates for 3 years after release. They get extra work to make them polished.
Some people think Edgy is good, but some people have issues with it. Sadly if you chose it, next year you will have to upgrade as its end of life is not but 12 months away.
If you are thinking about Feisty wait at least a week before the upgrade. The first Beta should be out by then, right now its in herd 5 (alpha).

The good news is that all the versions can run 32bit code without a chroot. Its not automatic in any of them. But there are howto's. So you can install a 32bit firefox and plugins if you want. There is a link in my signature. 
Lastly, while its possible to upgrade your install, you would have to go in steps. First Dapper, then Edgy, Then Feisty. Depending on the version you choose. Since you are having issues, upgrading the install may not be a good idea. If you have the room , you may want to install to a new partition while keeping the old one. You could then mount the old one and copy over your files, then delete Breezy.
But regardless what version, or method you use. The #1 thing to do is backup anything you cant risk loosing.

Hope this helps. :D
