### Post by azcrumpty on 2005-12-13
A great way to check out Ubuntu is to goto [www.vmware.com/player]("http://www.vmware.com/player") and try Ubuntu under Win32.  VMWare makes a "browser" vm which is a customized version of ubuntu, and they also offer full ubuntu somewhere in the vm section.

Alas, ATI cards can be a pain under Ubuntu.  The fault isn't due to Linux.  You can get the proprietary driver from ATI.com.  Check out this [thread]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=60779&highlight=R4000") , post #6 to understand how to recover the console.
