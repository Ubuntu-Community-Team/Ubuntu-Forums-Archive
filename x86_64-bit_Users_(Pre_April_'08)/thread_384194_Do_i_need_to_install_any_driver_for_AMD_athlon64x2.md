### Post by mixium on 2007-03-14
Hi,

Gnu/Linux distributions use the Linux kernel which naturally comes with more built-in drivers than any MS product. You will soon realize this once you install Ubuntu. There is the rare occasion that you will want to get hardware drivers from the manufacturer, but that is very rarely required and most often not necessary. Personally in the past I have only found myself looking for drivers to obtain optimal performance for high-end video cards. Every other piece of hardware was supported without need of external drivers 99.9999% of the time.

Everyday you'll learn and grow to love the Gnu/Linux community more and more.

:)
