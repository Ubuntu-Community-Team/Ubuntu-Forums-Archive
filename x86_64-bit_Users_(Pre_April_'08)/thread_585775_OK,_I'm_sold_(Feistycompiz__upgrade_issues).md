### Post by praxis22 on 2007-10-21
I had "issues" upon upgrading to gutsy, but once I got it sorted it's a thing of beauty.

Mostly I had problems with compiz and the ati driver. (Not a problem with beryl.)  It was forever glitching in one way or another, once I got it to semi behave, it decided to offset the view pane. meaning I had to tweak the monitor when I switched between XP and gutsy. As a last resort I again tried the proprietary driver. which I fully expected  to screw up as it had done on my previous two attempts. This time however, it "just worked", (at least once I switched kernels) and in so doing, the view pane snapped back to the monitor edge as normal. I had to install the xorg Xgl driver, and then manually start compiz with **compiz --replace&** but after that, and following this page:

[http://forlong.blogage.de/article/2007/8/29/How-to-set-up-Compiz-Fusion](http://forlong.blogage.de/article/2007/8/29/How-to-set-up-Compiz-Fusion)

I now have a working desktop cube, and much other silliness.

For those experiencing issues with their audio codecs, etc, I'd recommend. Automatix, it's quick clean and painless. It's also unsupported, so I'll leave you to find it on your own. This will likely not be an issue if you do a clean install, but if you upgrade it can be a little bumpy if your Feisty install isn't strictly vanilla.

Persevere, it really is worth it.
