### Post by juicemansam on 2006-07-26
I don't really care for what brand is better, although AMD does usually get better results during benchmarks. I'm not so sure if AMD-based systems are that much lower in cost; competeing models are significantly lower in cost on the Intel side.

If you deside for an Intel-based route, don't get an Abit AW8-Max motherboard, especially if you're going with a Pentium D 805. They have little or no support for that particular processor (which I found out the hardway after two returns and forum posts at Abit's website), and all Intel-995x chipset-based Abit motherboards (or at least the AW8 series) are discontinued.

I got a pretty good "low-end" dual core 64-bit setup right now. It's composed of a Pentuim D 805 ($101), ECS C19-A SLI motherboard ($78 ), pqi TURBO 1GB (2 x 512MB) DDR2 533 ($85), and 2 SLI'd Geforce 6600 LE cards that I "inherited."

### Post by MS7 on 2006-07-29
It depends what you are doing with it. It's not as cut and dried as being able to make a comparison to a specific Intel processor across the board.

Look at this [http://www.tomshardware.co.uk/cpu/charts.html](http://www.tomshardware.co.uk/cpu/charts.html) You can choose  your processor and a benchmark, run the comparison and you'll see the scores from other AMD and Intel processors.

As a for instance, the 3800+ Newcastle scores 3184 in PCmark 05 CPU tests but the P4 3.0 Northwood scores 3899 so is quite a bit quicker in that test. If you look at DivX 6 encoding, the 3800+ is very slightly quicker than the P4.
