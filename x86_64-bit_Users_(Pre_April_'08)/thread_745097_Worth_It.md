First off i'll have to admit i'm still using 64 bit Dapper, and have been happy with it, other than the hoops i had to jump through to get plugins for Firefox32.  I'm still waiting to hear what the pros and cons are for Hardy vs. Dapper...

As far as your situation, i would say go for it as long as you get a good backup first.  The worst that could happen is you lose some time doing another clean install in 32 bit Hardy.  Then again, depending upon how you use the computer you may not notice significant improvements.  People who see the most gains are those who use memory intensive applications like big databases.  But you could feel a warm glow from being on the cutting edge.

I don't think you'll have a problem with KDE, either.  Gnome and KDE each have their good points, but AFAIK neither will be significantly affected  by the move to 64 bit.  The stuff that is affected is stuff that bangs the hardware directly [video and wireless], and stuff that isn't open source [plugins and video again]. Anyway, i have both Gnome and KDE on my PowerBook, and switch back and forth as needed.  KDE has gradually become my preferred session, however.  If you think you might be overwhelmed by too much newness, just get the regular [Gnome] Ubuntu and you can always install the KDE-base package on top of that when you are ready.
