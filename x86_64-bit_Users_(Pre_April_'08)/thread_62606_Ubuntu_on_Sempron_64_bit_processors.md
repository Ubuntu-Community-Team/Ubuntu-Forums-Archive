### Post by d1337 on 2005-09-08
> Hey, When You have the Processor which is 64 bit, why aren't you going for Ubuntu 64 bit version....?Some of us use our machines for work.  That may sum it up and you can skip the rest of my post.

The 64bit processors available now even claim that you "won't be left behind in the **future**".  All said and done, you can run a 64 bit as a 32 bit (backwards compatibility), but not everything is right in Ubuntu or any other distro for 64 bit yet (to my knowledge).  So...if you have a machine you can play with, go 64.  If you need a stable, fully functioning box, stick with the 32 bit install for now.  The time will come to upgrade which will probably involve changing your repositories (kernel will likely take care of itself then) and you'll be able to keep all that you have without having to reinstall.
I've been debating Breezy for weeks and it's a simple upgrade (after changing repos).  But I've held off until at least the pre release.  With time, I'll change...but I can't afford to have a crashing OS or certain applications on a box I use for work.  So, again, if you have a 64bit "playbox", knock your lights out.  Otherwise, I'd suggest running 32.  Just my two pennies, though...do what you want.
