### Post by incubus on 2007-06-21
jrme33,

I am, of course, biased in favor of x86_64.  But since you've asked that question here, in the 64 bit section, I will assume you won't mind it.  To put it shortly, I don't see where the 64 bit installation is any worse than the 32 bit one, with the caveat of a few programs.  But even with those, if you follow the recipes in this section (and I'm talking about copying and pasting or simply running a script), it's really hard to get it wrong.

I recommend reading this thread:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=368607](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=368607)

One mistake that, in my opinion, many people make when coming from Windows is that they want to solve all the problems in one day (or usually night).  That's asking for frustration.  It's like wanting to learn to play the violin in one lesson.  So, my humble suggestion is that you go ahead, install it, set up what you can, take note of what doesn't work.  Take a break.  Search the forums.  Google.  Think about it, don't just try to implement the first solution.  Ask here if things don't work.  Focus on having a system that works, rather than an exact replacement of what you had before.

Here I'm being pessimistic.  You'll hopefully find, as I have, that most things work out of the box or they're just a matter of clicking to install.

Do let us know if you have more specific questions.

incubus

### Post by Jouke74 on 2007-06-21
I am using both Windows 32 bit and Ubuntu FF 64 bit in dual boot now. This might be an option for you, as you can play around in Ubuntu and break it, and still have your Windows available as backup to do essentials if required. Later on a reinstall of onlu Ubuntu can be used, once you know the details. Another option is to mess around with the 64 bit live CD, this will give you a good indication of what is basically possible. You could even compare it to the 32 bit live CD if you feel like.

Actually there is very little difference in using Ubuntu 32 bits or 64 bits. Most programs do the same thing and are found for both "bits" in the Ubuntu repositories. The gain of a 64 bit system is mainly present in number crunching applications (audio & video editing, simulations, science etc.) The disadvantage depends on your hardware (there might be missing drivers) and on certain programs / codecs / macromedia flash which are not natively present for 64 bit. HOWEVER, this forum, it's posts and posters can help you with these problems. 

It all depends a lot on your knowledge and patience to read and learn about things to make everything work.
With 64 bits that sometimes might take a little "bit" longer than 32 bits.. :D
