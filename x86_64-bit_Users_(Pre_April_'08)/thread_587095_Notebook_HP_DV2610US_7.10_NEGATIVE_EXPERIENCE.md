Dell has already done this.  HP is probably not too far behind. (hopefully)

In general, I recommend that users buy systems with compatible hardware.  Too often I see people just going into Best Buy to get any system, the same way they might buy a toaster or microwave.  This is a recipe for disaster.  Plus it rewards hardware vendors who use components that are not compatible with linux.

The only good news is that over time almost all hardware becomes compatible with linux... and messing around with hardware that is marginally supported is a great way to spend time learning about the system.
