### Post by zuus on 2006-04-27
IMO that will be quite a good system to mess around on. It's a new world Mac so you shouldn't have many problems getting ubuntu installed and considering it has AGP it opens up a whole range of graphic options.

I only recently got into Macs when I bought this old-world Beige G3 266 with 192mb RAM, Voodoo3 and no AGP (first Mac I've ever used). Installing any OS other than OS9 was hell on it since the jumper I was using on the HDD was old and rusty/oxidised and was defaulting it to slave - and slave doesn't exist in old-world machines *sigh*. After finally discovering that, I tried sticking OSX onto it, but found out that it not only didn't support the Voodoo3, but it kept crashing and was way too slow. OS9 was well... OS9 - Kinda featureless, so I decided to put ubuntu on.

After about 10 attempts at installing it, and finally getting it on there, it runs quite decently. Can browse net, do email-y stuff and even run OpenOffice (after a nice 1 - 2 minute loading wait). After a bit of tweaking and installing Openbox to replace the gnome window manager it runs a fair bit smoother, and is actually quite useable.

I'm currently forced to use it semi-permanently nowadays as my main system carked it, so I'm having fun learning ubuntu and getting to know a bit about Macs - despite having to wait eons for things to load (in comparison to the 3200+ i'm used to) and having to use a chunky old-school keyboard and one button mouse. 

Anyway, good luck with the system :) It's no Athlon or P4, but should work well especially for messing around with!

### Post by seatea on 2006-04-27
I have a PM G4 400 Gigabit that I have been running Ubuntu on for about seven months. I think it runs  well for every day tasks with a couple of shortcomings. I can't play DVDs very well. My ATI 128 Rage Pro graphics card is not well supported for DVDs. I can't play them at all unless I disable use of Xv. Even then the playback is not smooth. I also haven't been able to get Realplayer to work for video. Additionally, I can't get video to play through Firefox with any of the plugins, so no movie trailers. I have not been able to figure out how to get Flash running either. These are ppc problems. The i386 version of Ubuntu is much better with these things.

My printer and CD burner,a Sony Spressa, work ok. They don't have as many features as were available under the Mac OS, but have only the basics. 

I haven't been able to get my Microtek scanner to work under Ubuntu.

I can't complain about  speed for the most part, but Firefox and the Gnome Help manual are terribly slow to load. That probably has alot to do with the programs themselves rather than Ubuntu in general.
