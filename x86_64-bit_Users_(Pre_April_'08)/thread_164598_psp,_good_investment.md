### Post by syntaxtothe on 2006-06-28
I own a psp, I run Ubuntu 6.06 and it gives me absolutely no problems. Runs a tad slower than windows but I'll live. There is however one problem. If you intend to watch videoclips or any other sort of video media..the market for coverters is quite shabby.  If you don't know already the Sony PSP plays only .mp4 files, and it's quite tricky finding a good converter. All that being said I still use windows to convert my .avi's but if you really want to get movies/clips on your psp and you only have ubuntu..it's a hassle. 

On a sidenote if you do intend to actually buy a psp, LOOK BEFORE YOU BUY, Get a version 1.5 psp. I repeat 1.5 anything higher...and well you won't be able to do much fun stuff on it.

I hope my comment helped in one way or another.

### Post by utari on 2006-06-28
It's the best investment so far, if you're a gamer.
As stated above, standard flash cards are supported, reading and writting are simple.  

You do want at the most version 2.0 (which you can downgrade to 1.5) 
I recommend looking either on EBAY (any PSP with 1.5 is going for a decent coin tho) or pawn shops.. if it's damaged on the outside you can replace most everything on the PSP.

I was in WALMART (and a few other major retailers) the other day and booted up a new PSP its running FW2.6 which is what you dont want :D)

The homebrew community is awsome for the PSP. You'll love it. 

Look around, take your time.. invest in the right one :D
