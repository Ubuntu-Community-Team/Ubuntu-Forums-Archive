### Post by Kilz on 2006-08-20
Welcome to 64bit. The first thing you will learn is there is a lot of FUD. You will hear a lot of misinformation about the 64bit OS from people running a 32bit OS and advice that the 32bit is just as good. Don't believe it for a second.
I suggest you look for yourself. I suggest you click on the persons name giving you the information and look at there posts. Look at the amount of posts in the 64bit section. Also don't go into other sections and expect anything but misinformation about an operating system they may not run. Be on the look out for people who ran 64bit Breezy, Dapper is not Breezy and has a lot of improvements.
What they wont tell you is that some people have problems running a 32bit os on there 64bit system. Crashes, applications running slow, and some that wont work correctly can happen.

Now back to the information. There are a few things that need to be setup differently than the 32bit. But that is the case in every different operating system. Some things need to be setup differently. There is a very good sticky in the 64bit section that tells what programs these may be.
Firefox with plugins. To use some plugins you have to use a 32bit firefox, look in my signature for a link to a howto/install script/.deb files for this. I also have one for Wine. Te scripts take a whole 2 commands to install the applications.
As for how much improvement you will see, it depends. Encoding, rendering , and a lot of number crunching programs can see a 30% increese. Some things that are older may only see modest gains.
In the end its up to you. Why not give the 64bit OS at least a try for a week or 2 and make up your own mind. But please come here to post questions about the 64bit OS if you run it, or run into any problems.
