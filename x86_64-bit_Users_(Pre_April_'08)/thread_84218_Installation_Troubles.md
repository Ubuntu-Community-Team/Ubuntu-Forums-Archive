### Post by jdp on 2005-10-30
Even if you try to put multpile Mac partitions and OSs on your machine, you'll have to deal with some of this.  Getting it to boot different systems takes work and some knowldge of what is going on.  The most simple way to install any OS is to let it blow away what is on your internal drive and do everything for you, be it Linux or OS X.  To get either going on an external drive will take some work and knowledge and some geeky work.  Linux just takes a bit more of it.  If you want to lay blame for the state of things, blame Apple for not being nice about how they set up how the systems operate to boot.  They have been nicer than many, but some things could use a bit better support, such as they having dropped USB booting ability on newer systems.

Anyway, I'm not trying to push you away, but when you try to setup dual boot on any platform (Mac or PC) you have to think about what is going on, like it or not.  :)
