### Post by ZootNerper on 2007-10-30
I am not one who can help you sort your problem, but our problem report is not detailed enough.

Your HW spec does not mention a sound card. Are you using one or the on-board sound? 

Have you tried plugging in headphones? Have you changed leads to your amp? Have you rebooted a second time and does the problem persist?

You say you don't think you tweaked anything in your previous session. This implies you may have installed or changed something. If so what did you do (even if it's not sound related it may ring a bell for someone to help you.) Or if you changed nothing what were you doing? This too may help someone. (what programs did you run?)

Have you tried different programs that use sound? Do any have sound? What errors if any do they report?

Right clicking on the speaker icon will reveal if the sound is muted. There two you can "Open Volume Control" and check what else may be muted or set too low.

Are you running 32Bit or 64? I don't know how you find this out but if you look in System->Administration->System Monitor, then the System tab it will tell you the kernel number and this must surely reflect the Bit number to some knowledgable person.

These are only simple ideas, but they may help someone better identify your problem.

Sorry I can't actually help.
