### Post by Robocoastie on 2006-03-19
Once you have a 32bit chroot enviornment going then the few 32bit apps you need operate fine.

The reason to use 64bit is greater memory access and multimedia "crunching" speed. I have noticed that operations such as cd ripping and mp3 encoding happen much faster in 64 bit (mine's a P4-HT-em64t). I haven't benchmarked dvd movie preparation performance yet because i've yet to find an application that does this as easily as Nero StartSmart can in my windows partition. I don't expect the burning would be any faster due to drive speed and cache size but the encoding part should be affected.

Speaking of this, why hasn't Real pushed their format for music to portable mp3 makers? If they would do that then we'd have a "legal" way to put our music onto our mp3 players because they'd likely make a linux compatible program to encode to their format. Heck I'd even support Real's drm music store if they did that cuz we'd have a fully legal solution on linux.
