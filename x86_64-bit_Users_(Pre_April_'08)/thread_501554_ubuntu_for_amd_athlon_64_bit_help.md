### Post by firmwire on 2007-07-16
I had to make those same choices when I was deciding on running Gentoo, Fedora, Mandrake, Ubuntu, etc. At first, when I was trying out the different 64 bit flavors of Linux, when they were first beng distributed a couple of years ago, I was displeased with some of the driver support, i.e. Linksys Wireless Ethernet. Since then the support has improved drastically. I am currently running Ubuntu's 64 bit 7.04 version, and I have to say it rocks! I have had little to no trouble, like I have had with other distros. My only regret right now, is that I am using an ATI card at the moment, as opposed to my usual nVidia cards, but I have gotten pretty much everything I have tried to get to work, to work with it.
Yes, the 32 bit version might give you less issues with certain things, but the 64 bit version does everything I need it to. Plus you can always install the 32 bit version of an app, if you need to.

Just my two cents.
