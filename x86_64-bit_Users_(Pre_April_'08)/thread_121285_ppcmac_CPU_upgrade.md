### Post by sulobanks on 2006-01-25
I always say that in the long run you will be much happier with a completely new machine rather than trying to upgrade just a processor.  All new components give you a chance for better longevity for your investment.  And seeing as how the promised focus in the processor market is efficiency rather than speed, you may be keeping whatever you decide on for a long time.  If you've gotten all the performance you can get out of upgrading memory, I'd upgrade to a new machine, especially when you're talking $300+ to find something that /might/ work in Linux.  But that's just my 2 1/2 cents.
