### Post by xopher on 2006-12-15
Even if you **can** get (nearly) everything working on an AMD64 system, you still have to fiddle around with something to get there. It shouldn't be this way. We are getting there, one step at a time, but for things to 'just work' [tm] -- go for i386. I'm sure the situation will change drastically when 64-bit gets more mainstream, with windoze etc moving to 64-bit.

But yeah, you got the point I guess.

Personally I use the 64-bit version, but I like fiddling around and braking my system every now and then.. :rolleyes:

PS. For what you described, the amd64 version will do everything just fine. Cedega hasn't caused any problems for me. But you need the ia32libs..
