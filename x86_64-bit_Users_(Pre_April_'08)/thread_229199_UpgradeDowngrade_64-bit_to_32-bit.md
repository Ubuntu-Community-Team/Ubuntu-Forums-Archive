Automatrix is a setup script, it has versions for i386, ppc, and AMD64. But most people I have talked to that installed the amd64 version had nothing but trouble.
What it dose is installs everything and the kitchen sink. Codecs, libdvdcss, peeer to peer programs. About 30-40 different things. The problems is that it flys by and if there are any errors they fly by. Leaving little info to fix them.
Add to it the fact that newbies that ask to install anything are pounced apon with "install automartix, it will solve all your problems" in the beginers section and you have a perscription for disaster.
Lastly it teaches nothing. The newbie will want something installed that isnt included eventualy. They will not know how since they didnt install anything, automatrix did.
Im not against setup scripts, I think they have good points and bad, but they are not a cure all. My idea is to have 1 or 2 for the really hard things. Then let people learn how to install most everything else.
