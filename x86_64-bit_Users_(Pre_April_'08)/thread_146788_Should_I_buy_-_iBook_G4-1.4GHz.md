### Post by engla on 2006-03-19
I have a similar iBook, 1.2GHz, bought 1 year ago. I didn't buy it to run Ubuntu, but it works very well for that. Ubuntu is great, but you won't have as much non-free things (ati drivers, skype, etc not available). The battery life is around 3-3.5hrs with pretty conservative use.. but I always run at 100% screen brightness so it might be able to push that up to 4hrs (yes it makes a great difference).

However, next time I'm buying a computer I'm buying it to run ubuntu on it. I won't buy a mac unless they do a considerable upgrade in speed, I think. I'm planning to buy a new one in perhaps a year, and I imagine I want an intel core processor in it, it seems to give lots of cpu for the battery. 
As noted, one thing I don't want to trade off when I get a new one is the size... 12" is portable, really portable; I like it!

### Post by slux on 2006-03-25
In addition to the casing, battery life might well be a point of difference, the G4 is less powerful but might also be less power-hungry. Also, Linux performs "variably" on different laptops and iBooks have been fairly well supported in recent times.

If you value top industrial design and quality and your uses are more battery than performance sensitive the iBook might be a good option. In any case I wouldn't recommend a Compaq laptop as I've got some bad experience of those. :P OTOH, the new intel iBooks (MacBooks) might be coming as soon as the beginning of next month (Apple's 30th birthday) so this might not be the best moment to purchase a G4 iBook.
