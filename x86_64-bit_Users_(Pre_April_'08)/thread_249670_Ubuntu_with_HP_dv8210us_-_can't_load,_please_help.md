Here is what I know so far. If you let the PC boot up and you hit the button to read the menu, then press the load Ubuntu option, it hangs with the INt1-6 error I reported before. BUT! if you just let it boot up and time out, then it works just fine. Wierd huh? Well, what ever works, works.

Connecting to the interent is a little challenging. You have to enable your Ethernet device. At first, I could not connect to anything, then I found the settings for the network. It defaulted to DHCP, but I couldn't connect to anything. I set it to static, and then I could not connect to the internet, but it could connect to my router, so I knew the hardware was working. I reset it back to DHCP, and then i could connect to my router, but not the internet. then all of the sudden it started working and here i am posting to you.
