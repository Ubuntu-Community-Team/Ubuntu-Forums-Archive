### Post by John Jason Jordan on 2007-05-13
> **Linoman said:**
> ... I do plan to use the modem for a while to come. Although I do plan to upgrade to DSL sometime (hopefully in the next 3 months or so). What worries me is that I see their tend to be probelms with the lucent fixes for the 64 bit kernal system.
Anyone gotten a lucent modem to work under the 64 bit kernal?
I don't know about the modem, but I can suggest you check with linuxant.com. They're experts at getting winmodems working with Linux.

As for the rest of your list, check on the onboard ethernet (lan), unless you don't plan to use it. Most ethernet is plug and play, but if it is a gigabit there are sometimes issues. Ditto for the sound.

But having said that, your questions would probably all be answered quickly by running the Kubuntu amd64 live/install CD. Just boot the CD and then poke at all the stuff to see if it works. If it doesn't, then you can do further research to see if you can get it to work by installing some driver or another. If it works with the live/install CD then you know it will work if you install Kubuntu. I don't know who originally came up with the idea of the live/install CD, but it was a good one.
