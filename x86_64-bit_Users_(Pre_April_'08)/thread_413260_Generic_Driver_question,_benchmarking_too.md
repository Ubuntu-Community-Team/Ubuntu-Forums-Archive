### Post by kvonb on 2007-04-19
Hardware manufacturers continually refuse to provide drivers for Linux, that is the biggest problem we face.

Every piece of hardware that Linux supports is thanks to a very small band of extremely intelligent and community minded individuals who give their time in order to write drivers from scratch, mostly without any help or input from the hardware manufacturers!

If a part of your computer is working, say the hard disk, or the mouse, it is because the driver is already incorporated into Linux itself, no need to mess about with extra CDs and searching the net for (sometimes virus ridden) drivers.

The only thing you may need an external driver for is your video card.  If you have an Nvidia card, you are lucky as Nvidia have excellent support for their products and provide a very well written, and functional driver.  ATI try, and sometimes get close, but the newer cards are still a problem.

Microsoft makes no drivers, and if you do a clean install of Windows XP, you will find that a few of your components won't work as they should.  This is because all the hard work is done by the hardware manufacturer in order for them to get their parts working on the Microsoft operating system.

This is what most users don't know as they buy a computer with Windows pre-installed, if they had to mess around with all the addon drivers each time they installed a copy, they would appreciate the simplicity of Linux more, well I doubt it, but we can hope.

Take for example a friend of mine, she bought an external keypad for her name brand laptop and it wouldn't work, she spent days messing around with drivers and called the manufacturer for help, and it still wouldn't work.  I plugged it into my Ubuntu desktop computer, and it worked instantly!

She gave up and threw it in a draw because it would not work.

Under Linux, if your hardware is working, then the driver has already been written and is doing it's job, you don't need to worry.  If something doesn't work then you have this wonderful resource to help you, the forum.

It's funny because I was just going through my massive CD collection the other day and was amazed at how many driver CDs I have!  Now that I have completely switched to Linux I don't need them anymore and am looking forward to some microwave madness :).

Regards, Kev :)
