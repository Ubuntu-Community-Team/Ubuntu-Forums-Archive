### Post by em3raldxiii on 2006-08-16
Wierd grinding noises?  Peculiar.  My instincts would say that you have some kind of foreign debris in the drive which is causing problems.

Burned CDs have "less visible" tracks than pressed CDs, so this may be why you can read pressed CDs.  Either way, I suspect a physical problem with your drive.  You MAY be able to solve the problem with a canister of compressed dusting air - make sure that it's approved for use on electrical equipment.

If you have to replace the drive, they're not terribly expensive any more.  You can get a decent DVD burner for under $50 CAD (check out [www.ncix.com](www.ncix.com) - they sell in US and Canada and possibly internationally).

I doubt very much that it's a software problem.  I mean, I suppose it's MAYBE possible, but I really don't know.  I've been building computers for many years now, and generally if an optical drive grinds ... I replace it. :D  Hope that helps.

By the way:  Hair is HORRIBLE for optical drives.  If you live near a busy road, you could also have road-dust in there which is one of the worst types of dust to get in an optical drive.
