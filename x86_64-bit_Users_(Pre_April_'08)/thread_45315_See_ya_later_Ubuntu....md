### Post by joekr on 2005-06-29
Hey all,

I've been using both amd64 and x86 versions of Ubuntu for my desktop and server respectivly.  Up until recently I was satisfied with Ubuntu, its features, and its community.

As my experience with Linux/Ubuntu began to increase, I found that my problems were becomming more specific and complex (my assumption would be the problem's difficulties are proportionally increasing with my evolving needs and tasks in Linux).

I have turned several times to the Ubuntu community to ask for help on issues ranging from chroots to usb mouse support.  I must say that (specifically) the amd64 forums here are pretty much dead and useless to someone looking for advice/help from experienced users.  A week ago I took it upon myself to do some fairly straightforward upgrading via synaptic to upgrade my kernel to a newer one.  Needless to say something didn't work when upgrading kernels, and I was stuck in a spot between a rock and a hard place.  So I turned to the forums here in search for some help.  I posted my problem on the same day that it happened.

Today (now one week later) my problem has only been addressed by one individual unsuccessfully.  This is when my faith in Ubuntu died.  No one out of the many people visiting the forums would offer me any advice on my problem.

I don't **expect** anyone to help me, but surely there are enough Ubuntu users on these forums that there are bound to be *some* helpful individuals with a high level of experience who are willing to set aside 2-3 mins to post their thoughts... Well apparently not.

Thus I've dropped Ubuntu as the OS for my desktop, and will do the same for the server when I have enough time to install it from the ground up.

The Ubuntu hype/energy/excitment that swallowed me whole a few months ago has chewed me up, spit me out, and forgotten about me.  Well nuts to Ubuntu.

Good luck to anyone who wishes to stay involved with Ubuntu, and thanks to the few people who did take the time to help me out when help was needed: apparently you're in ***very short supply***.

firefox-32
firefox-64
mplayer-32
mplayer-64
random-gnome-lib-32

etc
