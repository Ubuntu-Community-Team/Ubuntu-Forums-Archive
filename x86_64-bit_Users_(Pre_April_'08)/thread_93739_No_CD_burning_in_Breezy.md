### Post by etitor on 2005-11-22
1. I put a music CD in my LG DVD-CD combo.

2. I close the CD application that pops up automatically (Sound Juicer).

3. I right click on the CD icon and select "Copiar disco" ("Copy Disc"). For destination I select "Archivo de imagen" ("Image file").

4. Some minutes afterwards the ISO image sits on my desktop, around 600 MB in size. Let's call it queen.iso.

5. I eject the music CD and replace it with a blank CD.

6. I close the dialog that pops up asking what to do.

7. I right click on the ISO file (queen.iso) and select "Grabar en el disco" ("Save to disc" or "Burn to disc").

8. WTF! A warning window exclaims "No es una imagen v&#225;lida de disco" ("This is not a valid image file")! 

Has anyone the slightest idea about what is going on here with ISO / CD burning in Nautilus / Breezy?

9. So I go for k3b and download 113 MB of mostly KDE stuff.

10. I try to burn to disc the ISO file that Nautilus made for me in steps 3-4. "Seems not to be a usable image" k3b says.

11. I delete the ISO file. I put the music CD in the drive and ask k3b to "Copy Disc".

12. k3b launches a "copy-from-CDdrive-to-CDdrive" process, creating an ISO file in a temporary directory. Progress bar reaches 50% and k3b asks me to replace the source disc with a blank CD. k3b burns. Progress bar at 100%. CD tray autoejects.

13. I am listening to the music (by the way it was not really Queen -- it was Ami Tomake).

Keep it short: forget Nautilus, Gnomebaker, Serpentine, Graveman (all of these I had tried to no avail). For CD burning just concentrate in k3b!
