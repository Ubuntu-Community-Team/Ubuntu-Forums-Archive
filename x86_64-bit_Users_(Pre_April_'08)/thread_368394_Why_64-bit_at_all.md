I have more of a general linux line of conversation on the subject of 64-bit processors.  I have an amd64 processor that I've installed the 64-bit version of ubuntu on, and getting things to work in a 64-bit environment in linux is a total crapshoot.  I realize in the server world it's desirable to have all your processes running with your native architecture.  But for those of us running a mythtv machine and wanting multimedia accessibility, running things in 64-bit seems to be just a lot of trouble.  Is the only penalty for running the x86 version of Ubuntu just a compromise in performance?  Is it really severe?  With the rise in Core2 systems, what is this going to mean for the linux world, are we going to have a complete dichotomy of libraries?
