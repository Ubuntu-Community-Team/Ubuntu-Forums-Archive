### Post by Jouke74 on 2007-09-04
I have a 64 bit comp with AMD athlon processor without internet access.

- I seriously suggest that you get some internet access. It is possible to work without, but it will make your life in case of problems quite difficult.

- Can i get complete support for ubuntu?
Yes, but it will be quite expensive.

- Where do i download the complete repository and how many GB it would take? 
It takes about 15 GB (including the commercial & media repositories), since you can post I assume you can go through the tutorials and tips section to figure this out. It is also possible to buy all Ubuntu DVDs.

- What version of ubuntu should i use? 
Is a matter of personal taste and is between you and your computer hardware. Since you posted here I suggest the 64 bit version. However, since you won't have internet access the 32 bit version might be easier though...

is Fedora 7 a better choice?
This is also a matter of choice, "It's all Linux". In the end I liked Ubuntu best. It had all I needed without too much extra fuzz and moat hardware worked on my PC. Some people will probably say this about Fedora 7 as well (e.g. their RAID function integration is better). The biggest difference is the package management system: Fedora uses RPM, Ubuntu uses Debian. You can probably buy both distro's with some Linux magazine, try them out ans see for yourself.
