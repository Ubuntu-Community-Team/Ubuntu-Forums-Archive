### Post by markupstart on 2007-04-09
I recently rebuilt my computer and put together an Intel Core 2 Duo system.  I figured I might as well test the 64-bit version of Feisty.  I must say it runs quite nice.  It is faster in certain ways, I really haven't been using/testing long enough to truely know.  But it has been a real smooth transition.

### Post by dabl on 2007-04-11
Speaking for no one but myself, I just did the switch (actually not "switch", I added a 64-bit Ubuntu installation on a new partition).

I exported/saved my Firefox bookmarks, saved a copy of the little kubuki dance that gets my Nvidia card playing nice with Beryl, made my 7.04 Beta CD, and did a fresh installation.  It took a few hours on Sunday to set it up, but it wasn't a huge deal. Added Medibuntu to the repos, let it update and upgrade to current configuration (that took awhile), installed and configured nvidia-glx-new, installed and configured Beryl, got Amarok and K3B installed, got the Firefox package, imported my saved bookmarks. Put the calculator and the shutdown button on the panel, stuff like that.  Works great on my Core 2 Extreme system.
