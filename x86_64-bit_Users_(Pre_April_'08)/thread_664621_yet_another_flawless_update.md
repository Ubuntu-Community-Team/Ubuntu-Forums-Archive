The only problem you will run into will happen in July of 2009 if you use Dapper on the Desktop. Dapper at that point will be at end of life. 
But the m200 should be supported, at least by ATI. The 7 series drivers are not that stable yet. They actualy started in version 8.41.7  But the 8.40.4 drivers (I use them on Feisty) are very good. [The release notes]("https://a248.e.akamai.net/f/674/9206/0/www2.ati.com/drivers/linux/linux_8.40.4.html") say your mobility 200 is supported.
If and When you do consider upgrading, do it to a new partition if you have room on the hard drive. Then if there are any issues you can take your time and fix them while still having Dapper to boot into. Once everything is as you like it , you can delete the Dapper partition to free up space. Thats basicly what I did when I moved from Dapper to Feisty.
