### Post by Rinnan on 2006-05-27
My reasons for running Ubuntu on my G3 iBook:

#1:  Now both my laptop and my desktop (VIA C3-2 chipset) run Dapper.
#2:  Huge selection of free/Free software.
#3:  OpenOffice deserves it's own bullet point -- it runs better on Ubuntu than X.
#4:  I like Linux's window and desktop handling better in general, workspaces and so on.
#5:  There's a philisophical difference that I think is important.  Under OS/X for example, the Quicktime player wants you to pay extra so that you can play stuff fullscreen, which I consider an essential feature (don't include a movie player without it).  In other words, the full potential of the hardware and software is not being utilized, for reasons of commerce, AFTER I've already paid for the whole system.  I consider that manipulative.  You don't see crap like that in Linux, in general.  Also, when you load Quicktime, the first thing it does is play ads at you.  So Macintoshes are being sold to sell you Mac OS X, which in turn is used as a base to sell you more stuff.  When do I get the best that the programmers and artists have to offer?  With Linux, immediately upon installation.  This all goes back to Free vs. free vs. non-free and community projects vs. commercial projects.

Reasons why I don't run Mac OS X on the same machine:

#1:  See reason #5 from the last list.
#2:  No room.  This machine came with a 20GB harddrive, and I'm a starving student and can't afford to upgrade it.  That 3GB minimum is more useful for storing creative commons work or more Ubuntu apps.

### Post by EuroCity on 2006-05-27
Many interesting reasons...

Personally, I quite like to tinker and experiment with Linux: that's also why I have OS X 10.4 Tiger (also with Fink and DarwinPorts), Fedora Core 5, SUSE 10.1 and Ubuntu Dapper all installed in parallel on my "old" Power Mac G4 AGP; sofar, they all work quite well, and organising a quadruple booting with yaboot was much easier than one could imagine (even if it requires some manual adjustments in yaboot.conf); sadly, Mandriva Cooker doesn't work so well on the PPC and is often quite broken.

Of these distros, Ubuntu is certainly the most evolved on the PPC platform: in particular, it is the only one which has a completely graphical startup and shutdown (Fedora only has a partial graphical bootspash).

Certainly, the freedom associated with Linux is a great thing; and also the feature of having centralised software repositories is very, very good: for example, it's a real pain having to manually hunt down software updates on MacUpdate or VersionTracker (that's probably also why many programs now have individual auto-update features, like in Windows).

So, let's hope, among other things, that OS X 10.5 Leopard will have some kind of centralised, Linux-like software management preference panel... :) :cool:
