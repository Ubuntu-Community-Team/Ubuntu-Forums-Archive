### Post by HokeyFry on 2006-03-09
_Windows_
Statistically the most popular operating system out there. Because of its populatiry, it offers the best selection of programs for various things, such as games, media players, etc. The GUI is different than linux's, and instead of looking at devices as a device, you look at it as a drive letter. However, the Windows is also a very unstable operating system. For example, it crashes and hangs alot. And if the GUI goes down, it takes the whole system down with it. Another thing about Windows is that anti-Microsoft people program viruses and other harmful sotware in order to bring down Windows. This means more money out of yor wallet for virus scanners and firewalls. 

_Macintosh_
I don't know a whole lot about Macintosh as the only two Macs I have are the super super old mac classics. What I do know is that Apple computers are the best computers that money can buy. Apple boasts about how there are no known viruses for the Macintosh, and they are right. Unfortunately I do not know any more about Macs. Maybe I'll edit this later once I have enough money for an iBook or iMac.

_Linux (in general)_
Linux is a kernel that is based off of Unix. The actual operating system is built around it. That is why you see so many distributions of Linux, whereas Windows and Mac only have one version to choose from. Downsides to Linux are the lack of consisency between different OS's (e.g. Red Hat packages are not compaible with Debian packages), the (somewhat)lack of official hardware support from manufacturers, and copyrite laws keep Linux versions of software from being produced (e.g. dvd support is illegal in some places, mp3s are not allowed to be standard, protected aacs cannot be read easily). But there are more plus sides to linux than downsides. There is almost limitless customization, lots of software that is compatible with Windows files (OpenOffice is a big one), Windows libraries so you can run Windows programs in Linux, and the more popular distributions (like Ubuntu) have forums where you can discuss a problem and help others. There are no viruses (as far as I know) for Linux, there is decent support for almost any problem, and (here's the best part) ITS FREE. And so are most of its programs! That reason is one major reason why I like Linux the best. Actually, getting Linux is like buying a jeep. When you get it, you don't just get an OS, you become part of a brotherhood.
