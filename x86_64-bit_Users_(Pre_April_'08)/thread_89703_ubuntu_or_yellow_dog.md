### Post by ssam on 2005-11-13
i used yellowdog in the past and then switched to ubuntu.

i prefer ubuntu because
* the developement process is open
* there is a new release every 6 months (ydl still has kernel 2.6.8, gcc 3.3.3, gnome 2.6, the last major release was over a year ago)
* there are good support forums
* debian based package management is easier than yum/rpm
* far more precompile packages available (once you have enabled universe). on yellowdog you will end up having to compile stuff from source.
* yellowdog are more interested in their big customers (the us navy) than desktop users. even if you pay for ydl.net (or whatever its called) you dont get much respect from the devs if you have problems.
* the yellowdog mailing list is rude about ubuntu. go on, ask the same question on the yellowdog mailing list, you'll likely be flamed for using the 'U' word.
* security updates. sometimes months go by with no security updates for yellowdog [http://www.yellowdoglinux.com/resources/errata_ydl40.shtml](http://www.yellowdoglinux.com/resources/errata_ydl40.shtml) , maybe they are very good and fix everything be for releasing it, but it scares me.

yellowdog may have the advantage on
* mac specific support for fancy things in very new machines, like sleep. because they'll get mac specific patches before they get into the main kernel (but ubuntu take a hot new kernel every 6 months, so it does not make much difference)
* more help for odd mac related problems, this is less true these days because there is a lot of mac gurus in these forums now.
* yellowdog linux may be better on old-world machines, but i dont think they officialy support them.
* they sell macs with yellowdog pre installed.
* it was the best powerpc distribution a few years ago. without it i'd probably only just have discovered linux.

i recomend that you try both, then make your mind up. but remeber if something is broken in ubuntu then you can probably file some bugs, talk to the devs on irc, post here, and test out dapper to see if its fixed. if its broken in yellowdog then you can hunt through the mailing list archive, be told your not important by the devs, wait a year for them to start on the next version, pay for a subscription so you can test the beta version, wait for atleast a month after the next release for it to be freely available.

i am sorry if that sound like a big rant. if someone would address half of those points i'd give yellowdog another try (though i would not pay for it). i know some people are very happy and feel very loyal to yellowdog. maybe i just had bad luck with them last year. i have supported them in the past and paid for several versions, but things seem different now
