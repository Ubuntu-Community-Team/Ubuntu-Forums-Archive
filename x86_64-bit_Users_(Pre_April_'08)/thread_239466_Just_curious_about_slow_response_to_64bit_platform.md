### Post by Kilz on 2006-08-19
> **kuja said:**
> I'd suppose the main reasons would be that the processors clearly aren't the majority in processors, though, they are becoming more popular, slowly. 

I wish we had some facts available on chip population. Because it would be interesting to see just how close the gap is. I would also like to see data on processor populations of the already multiarch distro's like SuSE and Fedora. [I did a poll]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=203794") not long ago that showed that the gap in users isnt that big. It also showed that it will be getting less over time as more people plan on a 64bit replacement.
As it is its impossible to tell the populations. We cant really tell. The reason is so many people make the choice to install a 32bit OS. That becomes a catch 22 situation. They think they will change over when the 64bit OS gets better. But the 64bit OS wont get a lot of development without more population.
At some point even if its not a true majority the developers will have to look at what they are doing. Should they invest a lot of development in a platform that is getting replaced or in one that is growing?
