### Post by amaurynieto on 2007-07-27
Hello All.

Recently, Dell posted a DEB file with the HSFMODEM (Conexant modems typically installed in several modems and some PC's, mostly from Dell) drivers for Ubuntu.

These work nearly on every firmware there is for chipsets up to now, BUT THE RUB is that they only have i386 support.

I've tried the dpkg --force architecture, and every other thing I can think of to get them working under 64 bit, but no cigar.

Here's the link to the IdeaStorm site idea I promoted: [http://www.ideastorm.com/article/show/72265/CONEXANT_MODEM_DRIVERS_FOR_UBUNTU_64_BIT_FEISTY_FAWN](http://www.ideastorm.com/article/show/72265/CONEXANT_MODEM_DRIVERS_FOR_UBUNTU_64_BIT_FEISTY_FAWN)

I think we should get together as a 64 bit user force and vote it into existence! (if there is no pressure from customer demand, 64 bit will always be "that thing that somehow will come into effect sometime soon", with little to no REAL support).

The other option is to login to Linuxant.com and install these drivers with no fax/voice support, and go it at 14.4k  - or pay a nominal fee for full speed inside a software libre OS for something that SHOULD work libremente.

I know that many of us who own laptops don't really care for dialup, since there is a widespread availability of hotspots. but isn't the whole point of laptops to have mobility? and what if you went out to Sedona Arizona and were in a crummy hotel with no Wifi and desperately needed an internet connection?

Not the mention the HUGE benefit that other countries where pricing of HSI is prohibitive and an end-all in itself?

Please, pitch in.
