### Post by Maupertus on 2006-01-08
Myu question, which may be better suited in the community forums, but it applies rather specificly to AMD64 users. 

A lot of people I know are using 64bit computers, most of them are gamers that want the power or guys that like to use AMD instead of Intel. A couple of them, a relatively large number actualy, have now recently switched to dual-booting XP with a Linux distro because they want to get everything they want from their configuration. Most of them don't really mind the lack of OpenSource x64 programs and to be hones, I think all of us just take the bad with the good and look to the future for better days.

But I was thinking, is Longhorn going to be a threat for Linux64 distro's? Are a lot of people going to think: "Owh well, why bother" 
Is this a bad thing? Because is it good for the Linux community to have people that want a Windows-clone instead of a open community where you can exchange your ideas and experiences? (At least it's always been my view that to fully enjoy the switch to Linux you have to make the effort to delve a bit into it's inner workings, something thats completely contrary to the Windows Vision Statement)

Or is it a good thing, because it give an impulse to the development of programs that can be used to our advantage? (Note: How many *nix native programs do you know that have been ported to windows and vice versa)

I'm just interested in everybody's views.
