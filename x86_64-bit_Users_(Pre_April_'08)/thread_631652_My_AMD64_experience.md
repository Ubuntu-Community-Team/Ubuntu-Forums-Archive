### Post by kd5ob on 2007-12-04
I've been a big, big fan of Debian and ran i386 since 96.
Before that I ran Slackware.

And I've dabbled in RedHat and Mandrake.  The BSD's especially FreeBSD.
And Hurd.

Then came outsourcing and a divorce and deep depression for me for about 5 years.
Now that I drive a semi I have obtained a new laptop computer an AMD64 dual processor version and I have installed Ubuntu.  At first it was the i386 which ran well.

The AMD64 version runs equally well, just much much faster.   I have a passion for boinc and have subscribed into several projects.  When I converted my OS to the AMD64 version I noticed that everything ran well except for boinc.  It took boinc about 3 weeks to calm down here to where it's running well without bombing out all the time.

I would have assumed that the desktop for the most part would run exactly the same as it did under 9386, for why shouldn't it....  
I can understand why going from 32 to 64 bits might cause scientific projects to fail yet I've noticed they do have us bucketized by i386 and AMD64 catagories so this shouldn't happen at all.  I carried no shared drive space over from the i386 days at it was a CLEAN INSTALL.  Nothing ported over from my I386 partition.

Anyway, it won't be long before AMD64 will take over I386 I feel.  
And I feel that Ubuntu deserves a great pat on the back for a wonderful job.

And so, for grins, I see this thing called Fedora 8.  And yes folks.  In 10 years time,
RedHat is still the same as it was then.  Oh boy!!!   I tried that between my two Ubuntu installs and Fedora is WAY, WAY back there in userland freindlyness.  

They don't even have ndiswrapper on their install DVD.  How's a WIFI guy supposed to get 
things running without downloading this first???  

Things are far happier in Ubuntu world.   Thanks again,

Charlie
