### Post by Entity on 2005-11-17
I did buy my Powerbook to run linux on it. I now think buying this powerbook was not the best thing to do.

Why? Simply because you will probably get the best from your hardware and most important you won't get the best from linux.

For the 2500$ of a current 17" G4 powerbook, you will obtain less performances compared to the current generation of x86 based laptops. Airport Extreme not yet supported within linux even though you can use it through Mac On Linux. Direct rendering for radeon chips is not yet support but will be soon in next version of X.org (7.0), release candidates are already out.

On the software part, you'll miss flash and a java plugin for your web browsing, a bunch of cool applications won't be available like vmware, skype, etc. Also some multimedia codecs aren't available on PPC for linux.

So depending on your needs you might wan't to reconsider your options. I'm personnally thinking about selling mine, but I think I'll still wait for the Mactels to arrive... They might just be perfect :)

### Post by xerman on 2005-11-24
Well, this is a hard question, but for the record I'll state the following:

I have an iMac G3 DV/SE 15'' CRT (network and audio out burned). This machine now runs only and exclusively Ubuntu 5.10(breezy ppc), as repairment cost was 20 times the price of an usb-ethernet adaptor that works seamlesly in Ubuntu, no way to get it working under osx.

I have a powerbook g4, running osx I need certain app not available in ubuntu-gnome-gtk (CAD, autoCad compatible .dwg, .dxf)

I have an AMD64 PC running Ubuntu 5.10(breezy amd64).

All these machines are used for work tasks in my freelance job.

Buying a mac for using it with linux is nonsense. Using a mac with linux is the right switch: you come from osx, you get tired of propietary hardware, you get tired of apps not localized, you get tired of repairment costs, you get tired of customer support, you get tired of repairment times, you loose time and money. And most of all, you get to certain point where you realize there's a whole big world out there fully functional, that is the result of altruist cooperation of people from throughout the world, and you realize you can be as productive as with any platform. And most of all, if you wanna be a geek, you can be a geek, if you wanna be a power user, you can too, if you wanna be a desktop user, so you can, if you wanna be a linux dummie, go ahead, you decide what you wanna be and the universe doesn't care at all. The world won't disappear, and the sky won't fall down on your head (Asterix's fear), and most of all everyone will accept you as is.

On the tech side, intel-amd hardware is best supported in linux. PPC isn't. Not even with PPC only distros like YellowDog, which I tried. For the same money, you get better performance on the intel-amd portables world, even more functionalities. So spend the money on a mac to run linux? feel free to do so, noone will regret that. If so, hope you have an apple authorized technical service as apple hardware usually doesn't fail, but when it fails, it does fail (I had my powerbook travelling all across europe for 10 months back and forth until it got repaired due to apple's designers mistakes, policies, and so on).

My advise, get a cheaper intel-amd portable, install just Ubuntu on it and get ready to roll. But choose the machine you want and you can afford. As I said, I run breezy ppc on that iMac and it works quite well. I don't have flash but I don't care at all. The rest of the apps I use do their job, and I'm very happy with that.

Hope this philosophical thought can help you, and if not, throw it away. That will be just fine. :)

Take care
xermán
