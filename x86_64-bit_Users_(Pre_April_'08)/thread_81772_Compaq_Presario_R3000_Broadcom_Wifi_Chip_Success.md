### Post by splatteralus on 2005-10-25
I felt I should post this given the mass of people having problems.

I am writing this over my home wifi network after many failed attempts at getting my broadcom chip to work.  After following the instructions posted else where on the forums for installing the broadcom chipset drivers using ndiswrapper I hit a dead end.  I was able to get my wlan0 recognized but I could not connect and I was having no luck setting any settings.

The breakthrough came by using gtkwifi.  I installed it and ran it once, it detected my local network and I used it to configure the WEP.  It was THAT easy and I strongly recommend using gtkwifi if anyone is stuck in this position!

Enjoy! :p
