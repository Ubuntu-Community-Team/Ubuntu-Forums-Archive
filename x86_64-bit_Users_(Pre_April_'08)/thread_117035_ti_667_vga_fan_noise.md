### Post by ssam on 2006-01-14
i have a ti 1ghz (15inch), which i assume has basically the same energy saving systems.

the G4s heat output is very dependant on how much work they are doing, and whether they run at full or 2/3 speed. and the fan speed depends on the temerature (i can sometimes make the fans go off by fanning the powerbook with some paper).

if you add the system monitor and the cpu speed monitor to your gnome panel you can try an figure out whats going on. see if the cpu speed steps down if you are not doing anything taxing.

if the fan noise still bothers you then there are a few things you could try.
* make your room colder, if you are in a hot room the fans have to do lots more work. if you are in a heated room maybe turn the heating down and put on an extra shirt.
* find/make a stand that lifts the powerbook up slightly and lets more air circulate
* get a can of compressed air, open up the keyboard and make sure there is no dust in the fan and air vents.
* turn off your screen saver, set it to blank screen only. screen savers use so much cpu its silly (but sooo pretty)
* play with energy saving settings. make the cpu stay slow, spin down the hard drive. these settings are scattered all over, try powerprefs, laptop-mode, gnome-power-manager
