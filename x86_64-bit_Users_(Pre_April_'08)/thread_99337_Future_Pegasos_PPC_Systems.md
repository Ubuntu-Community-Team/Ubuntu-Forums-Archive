### Post by joshuapurcell on 2005-12-05
Just last week the Power.org site opened their new forum and allowed free membership to the community. Several people from the Pegasos community (including employees of the company) joined the forum and posted some information about upcoming systems they are planning to release next year. One is called the Open Server Workstation which is supposed to be a dual 970 system, and the other is the Open Desktop Workstation II. Here is the post that talks about these systems:
[quote=bbrv]Hi Joshua, we are developing a dual 970 that we will market as the Open Server Workstation. We hope to have these ready to ship in Q2 2006. We have also entered into discussions (that is all for now) with P.A. Semi about creating a reference design for them. We had an important first meeting with them in California this morning. Keep in mind that P.A. Semi won't have samples until Q3 2006. Finally, we will be introducing the ODW II shortly. It will be based on a dual 7448 board we designed for the blade server market. As such, and because we really are targeting "good-enough" performance in the right space and at a low-cost with the board, it does not have I/O and graphics support. For this we will use our EFIKA which is based on the Freescale 5200B. There will be two boards in one case. The I/O and graphics will be supported by the EFIKA and the computational power from the high density blade. It should make a nice machine for developers. That is what we are doing for now.

Hope that all sounds interesting to you!
