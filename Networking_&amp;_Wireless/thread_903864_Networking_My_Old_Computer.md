### Post by arch0njw on 2008-08-29
The routers advertise being compatible with windows, and only a few advertise being compatible with Linux.

I am a fan of Linksys, and I know they work.  I use the BEFSR41 -- it's pretty basic to setup.  There's a big ol' note that says "RUN CD FIRST", but you don't have to.  There are a couple of easy settings to make through the administrative interface that gets the job done.

How you would hook things up are as follows:

1. DSL modem -- network cable to -- router
2. router --- network cable to -- computer 1
3. router --- network cable to -- computer 2

You can share your printer a few different ways
1. if it is network capable, you can plug it into the router
2. you can connect it to whichever computer will be on the most and share it so the other computer can see it
3. get a network [print server gizmo]("http://www.linksys.com/servlet/Satellite?c=L_Product_C2&childpagename=US%2FLayout&cid=1115416837650&pagename=Linksys%2FCommon%2FVisitorWrapper&lid=3765045678B23") and share the printer over the network
