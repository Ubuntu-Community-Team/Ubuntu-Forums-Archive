### Post by Greg_Frederickson on 2014-04-29
I recently installed updates, and after restarting my computer, the wireless did not work. The icon in the upper-right corner of the screen that allows you to connect to networks did not list any wireless networks at all. I found a workaround, by selecting the most recent previous version of Ubuntu (3.2.0-60, instead of 3.2.0-61) from Grub. I ran the wireless script (after booting into the version that wasn't working), and put the results on pastebin:
[http://pastebin.ubuntu.com/7356972/](http://pastebin.ubuntu.com/7356972/)

Also, after looking briefly over the recent threads, I think that my issue is similar to the one in another thread: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2220282](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2220282)
