### Post by Wabash on 2013-04-12
I'm running Xubuntu 12.04 on an Acer Aspire One D270-1824 netbook. Wireless networking works flawlessly as long as there's only one defined autoconnect access point.

However, if I move the computer while it's running around the office (or from home to office) and thus out of range of one previously-defined autoconnect connection and into range of another previously-defined autoconnect connection, I am challenged for authentication for the new connection. Every ten minutes or so, in fact. Each in a separate pop-up "gimme your password again for the new autoconnect connection" notification. So if I move my netbook and get distracted, I may have sixty open authentication pop-ups by the time I return to it.

[IMG]http://www.troubling.info/wp/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/wireless-authentication.png[/IMG]

Why am I being challenged for authentication for the second wireless access point, which is already set to "connect automatically"? Is there a way to change that behavior? Other Linuces and Windowses don't seem to have a problem switching from one preapproved connection to another without reauthentication. I don't remember any trouble migrating from one autoconnect access point to the next with old Gnome 2 versions of plain Ubuntu.

If in Xubuntu 12.04 I must authenticate when moving from one autoconnect zone to another, how do I limit this to a single pop-up request, instead of zillions that accumulate if I'm not monitoring the machine?

Thanks for your help!
