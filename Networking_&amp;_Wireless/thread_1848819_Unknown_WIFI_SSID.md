### Post by OpSecShellshock on 2011-09-23
If you don't use wifi, you can always disable your wireless capability. You won't find anything out about the AP, but at least your computer won't see it.

There's not much risk of your communication being intercepted if your computer doesn't connect to it. If it's someone who wants to be left alone, then they probably have it secured anyway. It could be showing up for other reasons than being on your property, such as being on someone else's property who is nearby. As to why you are able to see it when away from home, I suppose it could be stored as part of a list of APs that your computer has encountered before, or you could have inadvertently joined an ad hoc network at some point (computers with wireless capability communicating with each other, but not necessarily with an access point connected to the Internet). That seems unlikely to me, though. It's also unlikely that there would be any risk as long as you don't connect to it.
