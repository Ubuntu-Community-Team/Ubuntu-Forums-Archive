### Post by BlueStreak on 2007-07-15
I finally caved, got tired of the nightmare of getting my Linksys PCI wifi card to work properly.  Everytime I install linux it's a major headache.  I always get it to work sooner or later but the method that was successful one time fails miserably the next time.  I'm sure many of you are familiar.

So I got the list of known good wireless cards, went to BestBuy and bought a DLink WDA-2320.  Popped it in, booted up and instantly found and connected to my wireless network.  No driver hassles, no ndis wrapper hell, no configuration, just worked out of the box.

This info probably doesn't help anyone, most wouldn't want to drop $50 bucks to replace a working card just to avoid a headache but thought I would share the experience.  

If you frequently play with different distros as I do it might be worth it to just get a supported card.
