### Post by chili555 on 2008-04-30
You are very welcome; glad it's working.> I did not know the command to find the info.There are probably more elegant ways to do this, but, for me, it's:```
sudo updatedb
locate tg3
```Updating the database will take a few seconds, so be patient. ```
chili@LAPTOP60:~$ locate tg3
/lib/modules/2.6.20-16-generic/kernel/drivers/net/tg3.ko
/lib/modules/2.6.22-14-generic/kernel/drivers/net/tg3.ko
/lib/modules/2.6.24-16-generic/kernel/drivers/net/tg3.ko
```Drivers are always, as far as I know, .ko in recent kernels, however, they are modprobed *without* the extension```
sudo modprobe tg3
```
