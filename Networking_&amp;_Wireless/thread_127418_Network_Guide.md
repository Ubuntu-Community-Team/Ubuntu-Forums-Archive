### Post by bscbrit on 2006-02-09
I'm not sure that your request is quite so easy to meet with the information that you have given.  How many computers do you wish to network together?  If it is only 2 then a very simple network can be knocked together using 2 NICs.  But, if you want more computers, you will need to include a hub.  How do you wish to set up the connection to your ISP?  Do you propose to use a modem, modem.router or a closed network? How do you want to connect the machines; by wireless network, wired, a combination of both?

All of these would affect any advice given.  The actual physical configuration is relatively simple - although this forum might suggest that the entire process is steeped in black magic and requires a significant degree of effort and luck.  Most networks don't!  Guides exist via the links on this page to the physical configuration of both wired and wireless networks.

If, on the other hand, you are still comparing and considering the merits of specific network topologies and physical components then a completely different guide is required.

Back to you - what is it that you would like people to tell you?
