### Post by MaindotC on 2009-05-14
It depends, but one benefit of GSM is from a hardware perspective.  Usually, an integral part of GSM is the SIM card which retains all important data as well as authentication information for operation on a network, and can be moved from one phone to another.  For example, if you break your phone and you have another as backup, you can take out the SIM and place it into the backup phone and continue making calls, texting, whatever.  With CDMA, you need to contact the provider and have them activate the other phone.

GSM is also more world-wide than CDMA.  If you buy a CDMA phone in the states, you may not be able to use it in other countries.  GSM is typically supported in all countries with cell service.
