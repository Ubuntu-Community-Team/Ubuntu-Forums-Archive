### Post by Fire_Chief on 2016-09-19
Hi Kris,

So really, the connection problem isn't from the Comcast modem but from the TP-Link router to your Ubuntu system.
Do you have any other devices connected to this TP-Link router? If so, do they have any issues losing their IP address? You may want to try setting the eno1 port to match the speed/duplex of the router port (I see it is currently set to auto-negotiate).

Would you mind posting the entries from /var/log/syslog and from dmesg right at the time of the failure? This may give some clues to what is happening on the NIC.

If you want to do more extensive testing, you could direct connect the Ubuntu system to the Comcast modem (instead of through the TP-Link router). But BE SURE you enable a IPv4/IPv6 firewall on your Ubuntu system FIRST and have no open ports inbound. Your network connection should acquire both an IPv4 and IPv6 address from the Comcast modem. 

Cheers!
