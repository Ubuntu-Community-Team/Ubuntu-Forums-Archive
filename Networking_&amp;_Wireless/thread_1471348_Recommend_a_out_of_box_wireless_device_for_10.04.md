### Post by dhoenisch on 2010-05-04
I've found that wireless adapters that didn't work well or with a lot of beating up in 9.? or 9.04 or 9.10 worked very well in 10.04.  In fact, I never could get the wireless adapter in my Dell Inspiron 8200 to work at all, but once I threw 10.04 on, it worked great.  

My mom's desktop PC has an external USB D-link adapter, the cheapest Office Depot had to offer, and it has worked with every version of Ubuntu, 9.04 and up, as well as with Xubuntu.  Very flawless.  In fact, that little adapter works better in Linux than it ever did in Windows.  It kept dropping in that OS, but it stays steady and strong in Ubuntu.

The only thing I am having some issues with is on an Acer Aspire One Netbook where the card works, but I have to activate it every time I boot up.  Not a huge deal, but just thought I'd mention it.

Sorry if adding my two cents was too much blubbering.
