### Post by sophicsage on 2009-12-13
OK, I finally got my wireless HP printer married up with my Sony Viao NS series running Ubuntu 9.10.  It was highly irritating for a few days, but it's been solved and it was actually rather simple.

I went into System, Admin, Printing, and entered in the IP address of the printer where it says "host".  Well, my advice to the Ubuntu programming community is this: ** MAKE IT OBVIOUS THAT HOST MEANS IP ADDRESS**.  Don't assume that everyone who is using the latest Ubuntu is a prior Linux user and is computer savvy, or even knows that much about their former Windows machine.  Think along the lines of the users being consumers of a product, instead of *fellow computer programmers* and Linux aficionados.  It's obvious to me now and for the future and I wouldn't make this same mistake again.....but through Windows it did everything automatically and didn't ask me to manually enter IP addresses.  Make it easy for new users to understand what's being asked.

Anyway, I added the drivers for my printer which were already there in the program, which was HP C4700.  I initially used the driver the system recommend which was *HP910*.  When I was finally able to print a test page, it spit out page after page after page of gibberish and it wouldn't stop until I manually canceled the printing.  But I then selected my own *correct driver* and it works just fine now.  I was surprised it was actually there and that's impressive and is a plus for Ubuntu.

I only have two more issues to resolve before my Ubuntu is totally up and running.  I have to figure out why my microphone doesn't work and why my laptop keeps dropping it's wireless signal on occasion and has a weak signal.  It didn't do that when I had Windows 7 on it, but I'm sure there is a fix for this, too.  I'll concentrate on that next.

Have a great day.
