### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-07-02
Ironically, replacing Broadcom wireless cards with Intel cards is usually the first thing I do in a new laptop I acquire.  I have had excellent out-of-the-box results with the Intel 5100 and 6235 and have installed them in numerous laptops.  The 6235 in my laptop (Ubuntu 12.04) stays connected for hours without dropping and gives me excellent 2.4GHZ and 5GHZ connection speeds, on numerous different access points.  I think some of the older Intel cards may have had issues, however, and I hear mixed things about the new 7260 AC cards.  Not sure which Intel card you replaced...

You can definitely get those Broadcom cards to work with some tweaking, but it seems to change from release to release, and to me, it's personally not worth the hassle.  Good luck!
