### Post by jonobr on 2011-11-10
ouff- Im not sure what you have setup on your system, but its not bridging between interfaces.

If brding was taking place and properly configured, you would have a br0 in your ifconfig and the two network interfaces would be the same subnet.

Subtle difference between the two which requires different packages and setups on ubuntu,

If your bridging then the connection on sidea a is 192.168.1.x and on side b, it 192.168.1.x
So, the network on both sides is seen as one complete contiguous network. 


Your ifconfig seems to show that your not doing this.
You have two different subnets on each interface.

That means that instead of bridging Ethernet frames, your routing (or should be routing) between two interfaces.

So, if a packet needs to go from 10 to 192 the ubuntu machine will be the gateway and pass packets accordingly.

Your routing table shows that it doesnt know about the 10.x network and so will not be able to route packets between them, this is why ping is failing.

I think you need to figure if your [Bridging which requires a package called bridge utils]("http://www.ehow.com/how_7313872_bridge-network-connections-ubuntu.html") or [Routing ]("https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Router")
Check the links to make sure they are right,.

When you have it figured and if your still having problems, I would recommend you open a new thread on it giving all the details of where you are
