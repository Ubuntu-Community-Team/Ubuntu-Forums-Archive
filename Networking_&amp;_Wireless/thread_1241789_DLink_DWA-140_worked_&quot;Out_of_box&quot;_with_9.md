### Post by dupaski on 2009-08-16
After searching for a wireless adaptor that would work with Ubuntu 9.04 64 bit I was more confused than when I started. There are lots of folks willing to help with great posts on how it could be done, but as different options and configurations were thrown around I was left more confused than when I started (I'm a very new Linux user). 
 
Considering that, I wanted to share my experience (and plesant suprise) as I installed Jaunty x64, figuring I would have to roll back to the 32 bit version if I couldn't get my wireless adaptor to work. 
 
I installed the Jaunty using the amd64 image from the Ubuntu site and **my DLINK DWA-140 wireless USB adaptor worked "out of box"** with no updates or modification. Jaunty saw the card and my wireless network. A quick WPA configuration and I was on my way. 
 
For further reference my system looks like this:
 
Dell Precision 490 with a 2 dual core 1.6Ghz Intel Xeon processors
8GB Memory
Nvidia Graphis card (8800 GTS)
DLink DWA-140 Wireless USB adaptor
 
Hope this helps
