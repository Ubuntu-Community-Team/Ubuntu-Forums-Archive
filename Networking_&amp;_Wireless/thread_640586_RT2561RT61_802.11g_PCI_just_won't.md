### Post by rustybronco on 2007-12-15
The mac (media access control) address is the hardware address that is burned in to the n.i.c (network interface card) the mac address is in the format of 00:14:a5:d4:94:18 it is the device identification just like your address, it is unique to each nic. if the router has mac address filtering on and it does not have the mac number of the device (your nic) in its lookup table of valid devices, the router will not give your device a i.p. address and subsequently no way of getting to the internet or other computers on your network.
clear as mud right?

### Post by almax00 on 2007-12-16
to add to what rustybronco said... try turning mac filtering off in your router using your windows connection. once you are connected you can turn mac filtering back on. my /etc/network/interfaces only contains:

auto lo
iface lo inet loopback
address 127.0.0.1
netmask 255.0.0.0

try commenting out everything except the above and then run nm-applet. right click on the icon in the menu bar and you should be able to select your network and enter password. 

good luck.
