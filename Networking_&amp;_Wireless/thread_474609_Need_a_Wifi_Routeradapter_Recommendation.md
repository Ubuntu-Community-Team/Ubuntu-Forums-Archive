### Post by spd106 on 2007-06-16
Most wireless routers should work without any problems (unless there is a bad firmware issue) as they use standard networking protocols to communicate with client PCs. It's the client network cards that cause problems because they require a Linux compatible driver.

Though it is not necessary, if you want to have greater control over your wifi router or extra features, then consider one that is compatible with the OpenWRT firmware such as the Linksys WRT54GL. (I've just ordered one myself :D)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenWrt](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenWrt)

Also, if you want to easily share your Internet connection with others locally (and have permission from your ISP) there is FON. I haven't tried it myself, but it's something to look in to.
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FON](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FON)
