### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-05-31
When you shut your computer down, even if it's only suspended, you lose your network connection and therefore will have to reconnect when you resume.  Windows has to do the same thing, although it may not be so explicit--I never had much experience with wireless in Windows.  If you really wanted to, you could write a script to automatically reconnect the wireless network upon resume without showing you anything, but unless this is a big deal to you, it's probably not worth the effort.

It should only be taking a few seconds to reconnect when you resume.  If it's taking longer, then there's a problem, so please let us know.
