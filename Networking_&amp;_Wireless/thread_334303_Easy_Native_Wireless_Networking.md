### Post by toddfinch on 2007-01-08
No need for help.  I just wanted to share something that worked very well for me.  I'm fairly new to Linux, and am also not very technical.  

My goal was to find a very easy native wireless networking solution to my desktop running Ubuntu 6.1.  I've used ndiswrapper in the past, but always thought it was such a backwards and poor long-term solution.

I bought two wireless networking products from CompUSA and almost gave up, but I tried one last time.  Third time was a charm!  

I bought a **[COLOR="Red"]D-Link Wireless G Desktop Adapter, Model WDA-1320[/COLOR]**.  It's a PCI card.  It was truly plug-and-play.  After I plugged it in and booted up, Ubuntu 6.1 detected it and I was able to configure it easily.  This worked with a fresh 6.1 installation with nothing foreign, i.e. ndiswrapper--all native.

Pass the word along!  :)
