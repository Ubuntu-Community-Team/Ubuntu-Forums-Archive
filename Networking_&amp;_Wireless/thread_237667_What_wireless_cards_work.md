### Post by jml on 2006-08-16
First, the two cards that have consistantly worked for me are the Cisco Aironet Wireless a/b/g card.  Its a little pricey, but cisco plays well with most linus distros.  The other card is the NetGear WG511T.

The big problem is WPA support.  Ubuntu does not yet support WPA encryption out of the box.  You need to download install and configure an application called wpasupplicant.  I have never been able to get it to work.  I sue Ubuntu for my "desktop" but for WPA encryption, I have only found three distros that support it (with the two cards I listed)  Kanotix (Debian SID based) and Mandriva 2006, and SUSE 10.1 (both RPM based)  Of the three, I've had the best luck with Kanotix, but its not really an easy to install distro.  Not as bad as Slackware, but not as easy as Ubuntu.  Just my two cents worth.
