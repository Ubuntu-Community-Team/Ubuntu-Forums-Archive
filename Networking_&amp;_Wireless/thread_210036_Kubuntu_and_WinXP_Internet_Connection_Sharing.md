### Post by nekr0z on 2006-07-06
It appears that not everywhere there is broadband internet, and now I'm stuck to dialup (that definitely sucks, but I have no choice). Thank Linux gods, my laptop's built-in winmodem works like a dream in Kubuntu, but having a laptop bound to the phone line with the cable doesn't make me happy...

So, here is what I do: plug the phone cable into my brother's WinXP desktop, then connect a Wi-Fi AP to it, and "Share the Internet connection" in Windows. Dial it up from WinXP box, then take the laptop on WiFi, and, surprisingly, everything works! I'm online on brother's modem via WiFi.

But one thing makes me really sad: here comes my dad with a WinXP laptop, connects to this little network and he is able to make brother's box dial up and hang up on demand, from his laptop! Extremely comfortable!

So I just wonder: are there any gurus who made the same thing in Linux?
