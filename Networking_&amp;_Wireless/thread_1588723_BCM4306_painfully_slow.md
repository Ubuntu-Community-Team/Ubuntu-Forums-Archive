### Post by immerohnegott on 2010-10-05
That's pretty much the gist of it. I've got an older machine set up in the living room as a media center/guest pc, with a Linksys wireless card based on the BCM4306 rev.03, running Karmic with b43. The router is at the other end of the apartment, with signal transmitting through approximately one closet. Security is WPA personal.

Signal strength is decent, at around 65 to 70%, and according to the connection information dialog in Network Manger, it's generally working at 48 mbps. 

Problem is, download speeds hover around 35 to 50 kbps, when I get sustained 1.5mbps downloads on the rest of the network. It's definitely a problem this side of the gateway, as even connecting to the router's admin page takes 5 to 10 seconds (as opposed to, you know, instant on the other machines).

I've tried several different versions of Ubuntu, with Karmic seeming to have the best performance with the card.
