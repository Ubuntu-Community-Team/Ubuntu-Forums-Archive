### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2015-08-13
Possibly you need a password. Most people set up wireless routers with encryption and your attachment seems to indicate that is the case with yours. Talk to whoever set up your router. While you're at, consider if you really need encryption. It's a PITA when a friend comes over and wants to use your internet connection, when you set up a new system and don't remember the password, etc., etc. It slows you down a bit and adds one more thing to go wrong. Whether or not there is any advantage depends on your physical location, etc. If you are in a giant apartment building surrounded by Mohammedan nutjobs, mafiosi, teen copyright pirates, and other suspects, by all means use the strongest level of encryption available, or maybe even forgoe wifi altogether. If you live in the middle of nowhere and the nearest road is a half mile away through the woods, there really isn't much point.
