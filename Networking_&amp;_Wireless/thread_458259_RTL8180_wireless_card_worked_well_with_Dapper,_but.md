With Dapper, that wifi card worked out of the box, you could see it straight in the network app. But with Feisty, the card was not there, non-existent... I know the card worked fine with Egy, as I tried the live-cd. So, I decided to re-install Dapper, that release being LTS, and me not being very worried about running any cutting edge apps...

Indeed, I thought than Ubuntu choose to include non-free firmware in all their releases, but when I tried to perform a clean install of Feisty in my Dapper laptop, my wireless card (RTL8180) wasn't seen in the network app...

So I re-installed Xubuntu Dapper, and it worked out of the box again...

I also tried Debian Etch, my first choice, but I had the same problem with my wireless card (RTL8180)...

But I am wondering what happened to the *Buntu Feisty, did they took some non-free firmware off of that new release ??? It is too bad, as I am only using *Buntu for that very reason, otherwise, I would use Debian...

Anyway, no hard feelings... Dapper is still a fine release...
