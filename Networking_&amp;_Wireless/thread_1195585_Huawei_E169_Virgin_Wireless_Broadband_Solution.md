### Post by YodaDaCoda on 2009-06-24
I recently installed Ubuntu on my second computer and found I couldn't get my wireless broadband to work.  Basically, the device was detected and appeared to be running fine (but ubuntu thinks it's a e620 or something).  I set up a connection to virgin internet using the wireless broadband wizard, but I couldn't get a connection going. Whenever I tried to connect, it kept asking for a password... which I knew I didn't need (yes, i tried entering my password & pin & whatnot). I searched the hell out of these forums and google before giving up and fiddling with a bunch of settings.

Turns out all I needed to do was change the APN to 'VirginBroadband' and disable all Authentication Methods, except PAP.

Once done, it worked like a charm.  Just thought I'd post this here for anyone else who might have had the same problem.
