### Post by Roasted on 2010-03-26
While I'm sure we can all agree that with each release of Ubuntu, the Network Manager applet gets better, but I'm still left with something to be desired. I know there are alternative's, but I'm concentrating this based on the fact it's Ubuntu's default.

At work we have several wireless networks spanning across several buildings. Each one with their own encryption, SSID, etc. Each one has a controller with a series of access points spanning off of it, all sharing the same SSID.

As I was waiting for a Windows system to bring down its bazillion updates, I decided to do a little 1 on 1 comparison, using two similar Dell Latitude laptops, mine being Ubuntu 9.10, and the other XP Pro SP3.

When I would slowly walk down the hall, the signal strength would change accordingly on both machines. But it seems like the network manager applet gets "jammed up" after a while, and will stay locked on to showing I have 20% signal, despite me sitting the laptop within 12 inches of an active access point. If I shut off my wireless card, then turn it back on, imagine that... 100% signal.

At one point I lost signal, okay fine whatever. The XP laptop was at 1 meg of connection speed anyway so I knew I was at the boundary of how far out the network could reach. I brought both laptops back into a room where there was an access point, and over and over and over my laptop would not reconnect. I looked at the preferences and here it was trying to re-connect to another SSID that was currently about 5 miles away. I kept selecting the SSID of the network I was at and finally it connected. About 10 seconds after connecting, it dropped me. After 30 seconds of trying to figure out what it was doing, it managed to bring me back just fine.

This doesn't go without saying I didn't have similar issues with my XP laptop, leaving me to believe a lot of it may be network related or just the way the controller hands off clients from 1 access point to another. This is also supported by the fact that ALL of the above is NOT what I see at home on my own private wireless network. It responds 100% fine in all areas on my home network.

But it still raises an eyebrow that my laptop was trying to connect to a network miles away. I'm anxious to see if any solid updates come out of the Network Manager in Ubuntu 10.04 that might aide in some of these weak areas I've seen.

However, I'm curious. What has your experience been like? Does anybody run an Ubuntu laptop on a wireless network similar to the setup I have at work? Just curious on some user feedback.
