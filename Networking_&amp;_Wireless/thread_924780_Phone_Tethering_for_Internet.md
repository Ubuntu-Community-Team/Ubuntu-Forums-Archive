### Post by lswb on 2008-09-20
It's fairly easy to get ubuntu or other linux versions to use a tethered cell phone, provided your carrier supports it and your phone is capable and enabled. Your data rate will vary with the plan you have and the coverage in your area. WIth the older 2G connections expect maybe 80-120 Kbs, if you get a current data plan maybe 500 Kbs-1Mbs. The problem is that most of the carriers don;t enable tethering your phone anymore, they want to sell you a PC card or USB adapter and another line, 5GB monthly cap, and the service is not cheap either. 

If you can get an older phone and somehow get your account enabled for it, it is still possible to tether without a separate data plan (I'm doing it now actually) but you may not be able to use the higher data speeds. 

You can find plenty of info about these issues via google.
