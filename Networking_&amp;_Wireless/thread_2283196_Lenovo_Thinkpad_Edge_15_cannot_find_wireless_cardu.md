### Post by chili555 on 2015-06-20
Let's take this a few steps at a time. First, here is your built-in wireless device:```
Network controller [0280]: Qualcomm Atheros Device [[COLOR="#FF0000"]168c:0041[/COLOR]] (rev 20)
	Subsystem: Lenovo Device [17aa:3545]

```If you search for the pci.id 168c:0041, you will find this: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2282387](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2282387)> At this time, we know of no way to get this device working. Sorry.Others are working on it with little success, but as of now, there isn't a confirmed method.

Next, we see this:> ##### lsmod #############################

mxm_wmi                13021  1 nouveau
wl                   6367819  0 
cfg80211              484040  1 wl
wmi                    19177  2 mxm_wmi,nouveauThe module wl is a Broadcom driver, I'm sure it isn't helping. Let's remove the probable interference:```
sudo apt-get purge bcmwl-kernel-source
```Now let's get the driver for the USB to load on boot:```
sudo -i
echo rtl8192cu  >>  /etc/modules
exit
```We also see that the known working driver is actually blacklisted. Please do:```
gksudo gedit /etc/modprobe.d/blacklist.conf
```Use nano or kate or leafpad if you don't have the text editor gedit.

Change the last line to remove the line:```
blacklist rtl8192cu
```Proofread carefully, save and close the text editor.

Detach the ethernet, reboot and let us hear your report.
