### Post by computer13137 on 2010-04-24
I believe you are not supplying the correct details for the members of the community to help you.  Whenever I stop by here, I make it my job to pick out the threads that go for a day or two with no response, and I try to help.  If I can't, I try to get the poster to supply the information necessary for those to help who can.  :)

The first thing I recommend is to completely delete the network connection from your wireless manager.  Since you're having issues, a good troubleshooting step might be to temporarily disable all encryption (WEP\WPA) on your router.  This will completely eliminate all possibilities that your card doesn't support the encryption type, or you're entering your code incorrectly.

If you still cannot connect, even with security disabled on your router, then there may be an issue that the folks here can assist you with.  However, if it works without encryption, then it's most likely a simple mistake.

Please let us know and we'll be able to assist you more!

Cheers,
