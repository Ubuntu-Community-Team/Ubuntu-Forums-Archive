### Post by Irv2 on 2010-04-02
A few questions.....
In the router do you have some form of security switched on, (eg WEP, WPA or even MAC address filtering)?  If not, could someone close by have connected to your router and changed the security settings then rebooted the router?  Since WEP/WPA only apply to wifi connections, that might explain why wired is OK but wifi is not?  Can you connect her PC wired, to check she can still get network connection?  Can you log on to the router to see what it's status is - particularly of the wifi connections?
On her PC do you have a network icon in the small panel on the top right of the screen and when you left click on it does it list at least one (or possibly more) wireless networks?  Can you see your router listed there?  Does it have a padlock symbol on the icon to indicate that WEP/WPA is switched on?  If you can see the router, you are obviously getting a signal and the padlock is there, the problem is more likely to be to do with connection authentication.  If there is no ico for your router the router is not providing a signal, or a strong enough signal.  Can you take her PC closer to see if you pick up a signal then?
