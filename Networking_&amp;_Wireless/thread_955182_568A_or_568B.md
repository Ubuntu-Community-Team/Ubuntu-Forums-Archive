Which one do you use? 

I noticed that some network cables you buy use 568A and others use 568B. Is there any real difference? Some people recommend 568A but the standard has been apparently superseded by 568B. 

I just made my first cable terminated with 568B and it seems to work. I had previously tried making a cable with 568A but could only get 100mbs not 1000mbs.

### Post by jmkelly on 2008-10-22
Functionally there's no difference (as long as the cable is the same on both ends!). The only difference between the two standards is that the orange and green pairs are transposed. 

T568B is much more commonly used, though various institutions have tried to push T568A, and EIA/TIA recommends T568A for residential construction. Unfortunately for them, cabling companies like to hire people who work cheap, which often means people who get confused if they have to learn more than way to do something.

If your T568A patch cable didn't work at 1000Mbps, you probably just didn't build it as well as your T568B. GigE over copper is pretty demanding.
