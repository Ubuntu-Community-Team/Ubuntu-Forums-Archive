### Post by sub2007 on 2008-06-24
Whilst it's not legal and certainly not ethical it is just part of the world these days, if someone can take advantage of free wireless then they're going to. It's like seeing a £50 note in the street with a note on it saying "take me" - of course you will! It feels like a victimless crime, of course it's not if you have a bandwidth allowance but it feels like it to them.

Forget revenge, just put a strong (minimum 20 character) WPA password on your wireless and that's all you need. Forget about the other "extra securities": not broadcasting SSID (hack tools can easily get round it) and MAC blocking just because it's so much harder to get through WPA then to get through MAC blocking seeing as you can easily spoof a MAC address and it's not difficult to get a list of MAC addresses already connected to a wireless network. It's like putting something valuable in an eggshell (MAC blocking) and then putting that eggshell in a strong safe (WPA) - if they can get through the safe then they'll have no problems in getting into the eggshell.

Also make sure you've changed your router password as well and don't forget to educate your girlfriend about wireless security.
