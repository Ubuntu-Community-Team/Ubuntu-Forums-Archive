### Post by lisati on 2009-09-17
A number of things can contribute to a slow speed - congestion either on your own network or at your ISP, throttling by your ISP, messed up settings, and even dodgy wiring. My own experience: I used to have two extesnsion leads from the phone socket in the front hall to the lounge where I have the base stations for our phones and an ADSL modem/router. Although somewhat better than dial-up, the ADSL speed wasn't great, and we were getting noise on phone calls. Moving things around so that one of the extension leads could be eliminated from the mix resulted in connection speeds that were way better (nearly double in some circumstances), a connection that seems to be more reliable, and phone calls that were much clearer.

Your router should be able to help you identify who has connected to your machine. The specifics seem to vary from router to router. One of mine shows devices that it has identified in the time since I last reset it. The other can show me currently attached devices - I recently checked it and spotted a MAC address that I didn't recognized, thankfully it wasn't assigned an IP address.
