### Post by fastpakr on 2007-08-01
That wasn't clear from your inital post - it sounded like you were referring to 'hacking' it from the WAN port rather than simply breaking the encryption.  Yes, any WEP based wireless encryption can be broken fairly quickly.  WPA takes a LOT longer.  This will NOT be solved by simply replacing your router if you're already running WPA2 (although even WPA takes tremendous effort to break - it's more than a 'bit longer').  You need to look at other methods of protecting your data if you're still concerned.
