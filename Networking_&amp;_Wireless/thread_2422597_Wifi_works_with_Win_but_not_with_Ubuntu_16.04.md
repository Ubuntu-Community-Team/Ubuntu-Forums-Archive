### Post by chili555 on 2019-07-10
The router password is the password that is used by the administrator of the router. It is used to change encryption, enable or disable MAC filtering, etc. It is likely different from the WPA2 encryption password. I think that the WPA2 encryption password is what you were referring to. 

The padlock next to the various networks simply indicate that the network is encrypted and that a password will be required to join.

Generally, you will be challenged for the WPA2 encryption password when you click on the network you want. What happens when you click on your network from the list?
