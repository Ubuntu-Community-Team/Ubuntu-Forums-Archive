### Post by hahahan on 2008-01-29
786souljah,

The thing that matters is the chipset on wich the usb-device is based on. What ever you buy, check the internet what chipset is used in it and verify if this chipset is supported by Linux.

Examples:

Netgear WG111 v2 uses chipset rtl8187
D-Link DWL-G122 C1 uses chipset rt73

There are many more so these are just examples.
[http://aircrack-ng.org/doku.php?id=compatibility_drivers](http://aircrack-ng.org/doku.php?id=compatibility_drivers) learns you more about this.

Good luck.
