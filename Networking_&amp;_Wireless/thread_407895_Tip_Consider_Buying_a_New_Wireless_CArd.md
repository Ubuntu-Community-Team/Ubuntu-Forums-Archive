### Post by odelay on 2007-04-12
I know tons of people have said this before...and it seems painfully obvious to most, but in all honesty the idea didn't occur to me until 4 days ago.  Like many people with unsupported wireless cards (namely, the BCM4311), I've spent dozens of hours first trying to get wireless to even work, and then trying to make it work practically.  I hold my breath with each distro upgrade waiting for better support of my card.  I'm completely sick of having to setup Ndiswrapper every single time I reinstall Ubuntu.

Then, a couple of days ago, someone mentioned that they bough an Intel 3945abg (which is supported) for their laptop.  I wasn't even aware you could replace the wireless card yourself.  And then, I found out it only costs $30 (free shipping) from amazon.

Now, for some people, ndiswrapper hasn't been that bad to them, and they don't lose any sleep over getting their wireless to work.  However, based on all the hours I've spent tweaking my wireless...I could have bought a new wireless card 10 times over.  I really think, given the option, many people would gladly shell out $30 on something that would make their linux on their laptop more stress-free.

In my case, I have a Dell e1505.  I just popped off the keyboard, took out the old wireless, put in the new one, and restarted.  In Kubuntu, I didn't even have to download anything...all I did was go to System Setup and enable wireless.  Boom...instant wireless with no questions asked.  Wireless was even easier in Kubuntu than Windows---in Windows I had to download and install a driver from Dell Support.

All I'm saying is if you feel your time is too valuable to spend getting wireless to work, search around and see if there's a replacement wireless card that works in your computer.  This was probably the best $30 I spent on this computer.  Oh...and I can't wait to try out wireless roaming in Feisty!

### Post by tirofiban on 2007-04-22
I'll reply to my own questions.

Installing the Intel 3945 card and removing the Dell 1390 for the Dell E1505 was easy. On the dell service manual, go to the mini card section and all the instructions are there. 

Basically, you remove the hinge cover, then the keyboard, and then the wireless card. I used a small screwdriver set that I bought from Target. They  were useful for removing the keyboard, I used 2 small screwdrivers to very gently release the corners of the keyboard. I also used a small screwdriver to lift off the antenna wires from the card. Then I used the bottom of the screwdriver to reconnect the antenna.

Feisty Fawn automatically recognized the Intel 3945.:grin:
