### Post by walt.smith1960 on 2011-07-27
Sorry, for some reason I thought you were trying to set up a wireless network.  So wired connections are okay? A router would probably still be my choice for its flexibility.  I think you'd need 2 ethernet NICs to be able to run one cable from the wall and a second cable from one machine to the second.  Ethernet cards only have one connection.  The other choice would be an inexpensive hub or switch but they're more $ than a wired router and don't include a fire wall.  I assume you're in the U.S.  Go to Newegg or Amazon and so a search for wired routers.  One cable from the wall to the router, up to 4 (or or more) cables from the router to PCs or other devices e.g. printers. TP Link 4 port router for $18.99 + $3.99 shipping at Newegg.  This seems like the least expensive and least complex method to share an internet connection.  If you think you might want a wireless network down the road, you could get a wireless router, most of which include 4 wired ports and disable the wireless portion until you need it.
