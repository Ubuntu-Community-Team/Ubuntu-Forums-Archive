### Post by LeoSolaris on 2008-04-18
Wired... any of them should work just fine. Just look for the fastest one you can afford. Even wireless it doesn't matter too much to Ubuntu. What matters is the card that receives the signal. Wireless is a little touchier, but it's getting better. Your Ethernet card for your wired network should be easily recognized and handled.

Just so you know Ubuntu has a powerful firewall built in. There is simply nothing listening at the ports by default, unlike Windows that has every port open and listening by default. Working behind multiple firewalls is somewhat redundant with Linux. Viruses exist but there are only about 100 or so, and malware/spyware would require you to install it, which is not something you have to worry about if you only use the repositories.

Congrats on taking the dive into Ubuntu and welcome to the community!
Leo
