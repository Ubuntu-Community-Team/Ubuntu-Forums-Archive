### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-04-21
Is ANY transfer on that computer slow?  Most residential broadband connections (in the US anyway) would have a hard time taxing anything but a truly ancient Ethernet card.  If you can go somewhere else (say, kernel.org and grab a kernel tarball) and get the same lousy speeds then it's possible it's your computer.

But unless that's confirmed I would be inclined to say it's the update servers.  With Feisty just out, there are a L-O-T of people upgrading / installing.  Even without that type of activity, I've found the US servers to often be slow.  Some people have reported that changing the servers their system uses gives them better speeds, but I've never done that so I don't know how right off...
