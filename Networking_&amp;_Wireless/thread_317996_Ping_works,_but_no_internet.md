### Post by stream303 on 2008-02-05
The absolute easiest way to get dhcp working without going through all the pain of editing files is to place your primary and backup dns servers in your router's configuration page.

The OpenDNS servers are good alternatives to your ISP's in case you don't know them.

[http://www.opendns.com](http://www.opendns.com) (208.67.222.222 and 208.67.220.220)  Check the site to verify.

I've been editing resolv.conf and dhcp3 files for years and this took all the pain away - no matter what machine/os is connected to the router.

While you are into the router's config page, be sure to change the admin user and password.
