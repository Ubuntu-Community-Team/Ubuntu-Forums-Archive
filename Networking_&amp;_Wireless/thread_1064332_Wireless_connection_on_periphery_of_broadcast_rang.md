### Post by CJ_Hudson on 2009-02-08
I have a wireless Netgear WG311 v3 network card and a wireless DG834g router. It is right on the periphery of my network because a friend took on the home broadband contract instead of me and moved the router closer to his station. I get a connection but it's just on one bar out of four (at about 7% strength.) The signal is fine (about 70%) on my Asus Eee mini-laptop in an even worse position (behind a granite wall.) Why is my Ubuntu/Wg311 desktop setup so insensitive and is there any way to improve the signal strength apart from moving the workstation physically closer to the router or vica-versa? I get all sorts of annoying drop-outs when using Skype and other slow-downs and glitches.
Thanks in advance. :-)

Well fixed that one! With the use of a 'Windsurfer' signal booster kindly donated to me by a friend plus an antennae extension cable on my desktop. I now get 'normal' signal strength.
