### Post by holiday2 on 2017-09-05
It sounds like you're expecting your wifi adapter to have an ethernet interface. Is this so?

Your lshw output shows your wifi adapter as 
 *-network UNCLAIMED     
      description: Network controller
      product: BCM4313 802.11bgn Wireless Network Adapter
      vendor: Broadcom Corporation
      physical id: 0
      bus info: pci@0000:02:00.0
      version: 01
      width: 64 bits
      clock: 33MHz
      capabilities: pm msi pciexpress bus_master cap_list
      configuration: latency=0
      resources: memory:f0500000-f0503fffThis adapter does not have a logical name. Look at the ethernet adapter listing. You will see that it has a logical name and you can use this logical name in your interfaces file. 

Your ifconfig output shows that the ethernet adapter is connected through the logical name eth0. 

So you need to get a logical name for your wifi adapter and put that name into your interfaces file. 

Sorry I don't know how to do that, but it gives you a path to follow in your research.
