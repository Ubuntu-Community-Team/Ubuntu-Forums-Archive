### Post by esr on 2008-11-10
I have a Lenovo Thinkpad X61 with an Intel 4965 wireless chip in it.  Under 8.04, wireless worked perfectly.  The 8.10 upgrade has broken it.

lspci and lshw confirm that Linux can see the 4965. I can do "iwlist wlan0 scan" and see the usual WAPs near me, including mine.  So this is not a low-level kernel or driver problem making the card inoperable, nor is it switched off.

What I no longer seem to have is any way to select or enable wireless connections.  The network applet that used to live in my 8.04 taskbar and allowed me to examine and enable connections to nearby WAPs has disappeared.  I cannot find any documentation on how to do the equivalent thing under 8.10.

Yes, I've read the WiFi troubleshooting guide.  What it has to say about low-level problems does not seem relevant, and the userland tools have changed.

### Post by garythornton1956 on 2008-11-16
This is what I found

1) lshw proved that the 4965AGN card is recognised by the OS
2) It turned out that nm-applet/netapplet had been removed during the install so I did a quick sudo apt-get install for the package and it cured that.
3) I added netapplet to System>Preferences>Sessions to put it back into the Panel

Hey Presto! it worked....at least on channels 1 to 11

If I try to use channels 12 or 13 for my access point, the PC does not detect them.  It looks like the channel plan for wifi defaults to the US standard.

This is not a problem for US users or most UK users as the default channel for most access points is usually 1, 6 or 7.  Any UK user using 12 or 13 will have a problem though.
