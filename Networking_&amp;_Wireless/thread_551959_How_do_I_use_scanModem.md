### Post by s3a on 2007-09-15
I downloaded scanModem and put it in my home directory (in its compressed state) then opened the terminal and typed:

"gunzip scanModem.gz"

then

"chmod +x scanModem"

then

"sudo ./scanModem"

and then I got the resulting "Modem" folder in my home directory.

*I include that Modem folder as an attachment to this thread. I am hoping you guys could help me use the results to get my modem working!*

Thanks in advance!

P.S.
I put the folder called "Modem" (which is what got created through the terminal commands) into another folder called "Results of scanModem" and then compressed it in the .zip format under the name "Results of scanModem."
