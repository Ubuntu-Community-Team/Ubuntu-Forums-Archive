### Post by mylesw on 2009-10-16
My 11 yr old daughter came to me the other day asking for a Netbook computer for Christmas.  I've been spending a bit of time researching options, etc. and there are certainly some nice machines out there.  Her main reason for the computer is that she needs it to do homework on from school (she already has a Mac at home, but she is an avid ballet dancer and spends about 6-8 hours per week at ballet practice and needs to be able to do homework between her dance classes).  On the surface, this all sounds like a good idea.

However my work here is as a software developer, and I work from home and here we have a solid IPCop based dedicated firewall system that she accesses the Internet through and using the proxy settings to IPCop, I can control and see what she is viewing.  I'm not trying to do the 'Big Brother' thing here, but as a parent I want to know what my daughter is doing online.  Also her computer is in a publicly visible place in the house so we can also see what she is doing, etc.  

As soon as I give her a mobile netbook, however, I lose that visibility.  As a parent its enough of a reason to not want to give her a computer.  Its a no-brainer to me that it will run Ubuntu and not Windoze since I don't want the computer pwned within a day with viruses, worms, etc. that is likely for a kid with a netbook.  But I do want some control on what she can and can't see online and certainly some way of tracking what websites she is going to, etc.

I know there are a bunch of companies in the Windows space selling 'NetNanny' type software products for this, but I don't particularly trust any of them to work correctly.  I haven't found anything like this for Ubuntu, but if there isn't anything like this out there, it is a golden opportunity for a developer.  However with that said, my thoughts go to how I could route her DNS to a DNS server I host on my public webserver here at the house, or somehow make my IPCop firewall publicly available as a proxy server.  The Wifi in the area where she would use the Netbook is provided by a local coffee shop that has little or no security on it.  

In light of all of this, does anyone have any suggestions on how I can gain better visibility on what she is doing on the Netbook, either by way of installing some local software to control access, or by forcing any Wifi connections to use my proxies or even if I install BIND or some DNS server here at the house that she has to go through?  Can I force a Wifi connection to use those settings, although the Wifi Hotspot might give her DNS servers automatically?  

If anyone has faced similar challenges with the onslaught of Netbooks and kids out there, I'm all ears as to your experiences.

Thanks
Myles
