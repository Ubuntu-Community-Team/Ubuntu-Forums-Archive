### Post by DFlame on 2009-09-01
I don't think many people here are going to help with that, myself included. Even though you state that it's your own network and your own systems, we can never be sure of it.

I can however tell you that what you want to get is going to be difficult against a Windows based computer and incredibly difficult against a well configured Linux box, at least with the lack of information you have.

If you're after protection, then it's time to use WPA2/AES encryption & MAC filtering on your router for starters. There's plenty of free security software out there for Win. based systems (AVG, Avira - COMODO, ZA to name 4), and Firestarter should be more than enough to add a little control over security on Ubuntu.
