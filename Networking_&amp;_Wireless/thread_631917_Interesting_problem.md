### Post by salefish on 2007-12-05
I think what people are trying to tell you, in their silence, is that we need more information.
What is your wireless configuration, are you connecting directly to a wireless router or are you using a Local Area Network? If you need to know how to find these things, just say so.
I had a similar problem, what happened was, the password that I had typed into the graphical network manager was not being recognized by my router. I was using a very strong ASCii password and the graphical network manager was not handling some of the characters in the same manner Windows does. Well, at least thats what I think was going on. I changed the whole network to a different password and the problem disappeared.
If you are connecting at say a college it would probably be easier for you to fix your computer than all the others.
