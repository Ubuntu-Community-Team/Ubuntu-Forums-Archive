### Post by geminias on 2007-07-14
Normally when you are "connected" but not able to transmit and receive data it is related to the MAC.  Clear the buffer in both your cable modem and your router.  I.E. unplug power for 20 seconds from each, then hook them back up starting with the modem.

### Post by Mr. C. on 2007-07-16
Exactly as suspected... your eth0 is not communicating with the other side, and it has been assigned an automatic IP address by the avahi daemon, but this isn't what you want.

In network manager, disable the eth1 network interface.  If this does not help, please post the output of :
```

lspci
lspci -n
```

and show any error messages or mention of eth0 or eth1 in your /var/log/messages file.  Include a few lines before and after such references.

Also check the "link" ligt's on the back of your router and ethernet connection on the back of the PC to see if they have established "link", which means that are communicated at least at the hardware layer.

MrC

ps. the command was **netstat**, not netstart as per my typo.

### Post by Mr. C. on 2007-07-16
It is not clear to me why your eth0 card comes up, and then immediately goes down again. This is happening repeatedly.

Can you try commenting out all lines in your /etc/network/interfaces file, so that it looks like this:

```
auto lo
iface lo inet loopback

auto eth0
iface eth0 inet dhcp
```

Add # to the beginning of all the other lines, and  then reboot.

MrC
