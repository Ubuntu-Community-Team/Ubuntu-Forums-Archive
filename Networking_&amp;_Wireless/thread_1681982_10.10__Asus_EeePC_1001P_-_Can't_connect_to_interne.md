### Post by sanderj on 2011-02-05
The good news:

You can indeed ping 192.168.0.1 and your default route is set up pointing to that IP address.

The strange news:

mtr shows no hops after the default gateway.

The bad news:

You have no DNS working.


Possible explanations:
1) 192.168.0.1 is NOT a gateway (but just a PC or so). That would explain no further and no DNS.
or
2) 192.168.0.1 is a default gateway, but not for you (ie refusing you)?

Are you sure you got your IP address via DHCP?

If you connect a cable to the same gateway (and disable wifi), what is then the situation and output of the above commands?
