### Post by JacquiOh on 2010-06-20
I got a phone call yesterday from my service provider telling me I'd exceeded my 2GB limit this month by 14.4 GB and my bill is well over €240 + tax. She asked do I use tethering, I said, "I tried it once to see if it worked."

I swore up and down all I use my phone for is Facebook, twitter, the occasional post to Wordpress and very occasionally, a YouTube video. Nowhere near 16.4 GB in two weeks. 

So I installed a data tracking program. Today, as of 7pm, I had used a grand total of 10 MB. Then on the train home, I plugged it into my laptop to charge. Within an hour, my data counter alarm went off (I'd set it to alarm me at 60MB). I'd suddenly used 133 MB.

It seems to me the only explanation is that my laptop is using my phone as a modem even when the phone is set to "charge only" AND the laptop has a functioning data connection (usually wifi, today, it was a USB HSDPA modem).

Is there anyone else who can duplicate my results? Or offer any assistance in teaching my laptop how to charge my phone without costing me a fortune in data charges?

### Post by oj111593 on 2010-06-20
When you plug in your phone, check to see if it's using it as a modem. If it is disconnect it from it and then re-connect with the internet connection you were using before you connected the phone.
