### Post by randomnumber on 2007-02-07
You need to break you thoughts into groups. There is lan and wan.

Lan is you local network at your house or buisness ( [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_area_network](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_area_network) ). There is a good picture of the network on this page. This is your local wireless 802.11a,b,g,n and your wired Ethernet cord. The Ethernet  cird looks like phone cord and connections but they are bigger. 

Wan is basically the internet ( [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wide_area_network](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wide_area_network) ). Wan is what you need the (ISP) Internet service provider for. They are usually cable, dsl, phone line. You wan connection does not affect you lan connection.

Reaches of each service vary. I believe that cat5 is 300ft / 100 meters. If i was going to put a cable that long in I would look at cat6 I think it is better at reducing noise so that it has a cleaner signal over that distance.
