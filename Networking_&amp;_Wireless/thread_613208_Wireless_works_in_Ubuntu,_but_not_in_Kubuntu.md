### Post by Trevor4706 on 2007-11-14
After much frustration trying to get wireless working in an upgraded Kubuntu Gutsy installation, I decided to do a little experimenting.  I have two identical HD for my Dell Inspiron laptop that I can swap.  On one I did a fresh install of Ubuntu 6.06 and progressively upgraded the installation to Ubuntu 7.10 through 6.10 and 7.04.  In every Ubuntu version my GigaFast WF728-AEX wireless PCMIA card worked flawlessly "out-of-the-box" and my wireless connection was/is fast and stable using network-manager-gnome.

On the second HD I installed progressive versions of Kubuntu from 6.06 to 7.10.  On Kubuntu 6.06 and 6.10 the GigaFast card worked "out-of-the-box" using KNetworkManager; not so with 7.04 and 7.10.  After much trial and error and implementation of many work-arounds/fixes published in this forum and its sister Kubuntu forum I still cannot get a consistent/reliable wireless connection in Kubuntu 7.10 using any of KNetworkManager, wicd, or wlanassistant.  Wired connection has never been a problem  (I'm wired now).  Too bad, because I really prefer KDE over Gnome.

It seems in 7.04 and 7.10 the Kubuntu developers managed to break something that worked quite well in previous versions. 

I've considered adding the KDE desktop to my Ubuntu installation, but am concerned that will somehow kill my functional wireless.  Does anyone have experience trying this?
