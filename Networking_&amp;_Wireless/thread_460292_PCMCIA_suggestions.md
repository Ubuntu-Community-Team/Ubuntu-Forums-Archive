### Post by jml on 2007-05-31
The answer unfortunately is not as simple as posting a link to an on-line store.  The critical factor in getting a compatible card is the wireless chipset that the card uses, not the brand of card.  Manufactures are notorious, (Linksys for example) for quietly changing the chipsets in their products without even changing the model number of the product.  You actually have to pay attention to the version number of the card. Did you check out the sticky at the top of this sub-forum?

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=370108](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=370108)

It might be of some help.  The cards that I have had the best luck with are the ones based on the Atheros Chipset.  (Cisco Aironet 802.11 a/b/g, Netgear WG511T.)  Cisco cards are a bit pricy, but they offer Linux drivers for the cards they sell and I like to support companies that support Linux.  The last bit of advice is that there is no way to absolutely gaurantee that any particular card will work until you put it into your computer.  So when you do decide to buy one, make sure the vendor has a reasonable return policy.  Good luck.

Joe
