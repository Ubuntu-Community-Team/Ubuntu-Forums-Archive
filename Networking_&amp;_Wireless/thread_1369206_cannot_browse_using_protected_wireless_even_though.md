### Post by gordintoronto on 2009-12-31
If you don't know, you are using DHCP.  However, it is supposed to give you some good DNS server addresses, and it is not.  This might be a problem at your router.

You can:
gksudo gedit /etc/resolv.conf

and put in the DNS server addresses your ISP gave you, but this is only a temporary solution.  I do not know the permanent solution.  Maybe editing the setup of your router.
