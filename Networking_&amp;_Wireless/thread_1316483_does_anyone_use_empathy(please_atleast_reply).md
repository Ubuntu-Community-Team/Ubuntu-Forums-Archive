### Post by michaelzap on 2009-11-06
I'm giving it a shot, yes. I've always used Pidgin before and I really need OTR support, but I decided to at least give Empathy a try. So far it works well enough (connects very quickly to all my accounts), although I wish things like the log files were configurable.

Anyway, as to your question: GTalk setup should be fairly straightforward. I have a Google Apps account (which is slightly different from Gmail), but my settings are:

Login ID: full email address
Encryption Required is checked
Resource: Home
Server: talk.google.com
Port: 5223
Use old SSL is checked

Hope that works for you also.

### Post by dry crust on 2009-12-06
My understanding is (rightly or wrongly) that Empathy is the official Instant Messenger for Ubuntu 9.10, so I removed Pidgin. I had tried Empathy prior to 9.10 and was never impressed with it and always found Pidgin was superior. Even so, Empathy is the official IM package and so that is the one I am currently using. 
I think I tried gtalk - voice on the latest version of Pidgin and it didn't work, but if it did I would have dumped Empathy without any hesitation.

As a guide, you may have noticed the port people to tell you to use (5223) isn't the default one that Empathy offers (5222). I don't know if that is important or not, but my uninitiated mind says it is. Also, don't expect email notification with Empathy. 
In regards to gtalk - voice, don't expect miracles. I will be interested to see how Google get themselves out of this dilemma because my experience is currently gtalk can't talk in Ubuntu type Linux, even with on-line Instant messengers such as IMO and Meebo.
