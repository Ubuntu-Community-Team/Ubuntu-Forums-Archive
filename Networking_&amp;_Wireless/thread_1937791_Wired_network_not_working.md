### Post by ytene on 2012-03-09
There are probably several different things that can cause this...

May I suggest that to help diagnostics, there are a couple of extra pieces of information that you could provide:-

1. Make and model of laptop

2. Make and model of network card [If you are not sure of this, can I suggest rebooting into Windows 7 and using the Device Manager in the Control Panel to check?]

3. Can you indicate if you are trying to connect via a wired or wireless connection please?

4. If wireless, have you tried wired?

5. Whichever method you're using, do you have another device that you can use to test your uplink to the internet and verify that the fault really does exist with this build? 

I'm not an expert by any means - I came to the forum with a request for help of my own - but based on experience I've learned that the more basic facts that "ring fence" your problem that you can provide, the greater the chance you will have that someone can help you. 

[ If you are able to easily provide all of the above, the next questions likely to come to you will be to establish whether or not your ubuntu build can 'see' your network device. If you are familiar with the system tools, or with command line utilities such as ifconfig, then it's probably worth pasting up some information. 

Also, you mention that you've had things work with an earlier version of ubuntu. Can you remember which version worked OK, and can you clarify which version is now giving you trouble? Although there may not be much difference between eg ubuntu and kubuntu, it might help to know if you're running 32-bit or 64-bit.

The best idea is to try and think about things you can do to narrow down the problem [eliminate things you know work] and/or look for more facts to share with the experts.

Hope that helps - and good luck!!!
