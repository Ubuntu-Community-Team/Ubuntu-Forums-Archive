### Post by chili555 on 2011-06-01
In my opinion, he can't be much of an admin. If one of his users embarked on a task he knew nothing about and, after thrashing about unsuccessfully for several days, failed, he surely would have said, "Why didn't you ask? I'd have been more than happy to help." However, he didn't take his own advice, he didn't ask the dozens of super-geeks on this forum that are quite accustomed to getting wireless cards going from afar in a few minutes.

Linux and particularly Ubuntu are not for everyone. Not everything works out of the box. Having said that, there are quite a few things that don't work in Windows out of the box. I'd be happy to give you links to quite a few Windows whiner forums.

I understand that a person experienced in Windows thinks they can Google up the answer to any question, but the answer that works in Ubuntu 11.04 probably won't work in Suse 9.0 and vice versa. What works in Mint probably won't work in Gentoo. Don't even get me started on Slackware!

All these distinctions are lost on newbies. They read a post on linuxquestions.org from 2007 and go to town on the terminal; they never noticed that it was for Gentoo! Some guy was asking for help with a HOWTO the other day and the instructions were written for kernels 2.4.x and 2.2.x. He asked for help. I about choked to death on my wine I was laughing so hard! I wasn't laughing at the newbie; I was laughing at the strange absurdities that pop up some days.

I realize that wireless and internet access are a deal-breaker for all newbies. If they can scrape and scratch over that hurdle, they will probably survive. If not, well...

I wish there was a perfect solution. There isn't. I also wish the admin asked me.
