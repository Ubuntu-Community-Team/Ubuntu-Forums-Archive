### Post by theDaveTheRave on 2010-01-08
Sometimes if you do an update config changes occur and create "conflicts", possibly stopping a functionality.

It could be that if you did updates, but didn't re-boot afterward conflicts where created, that the subsequent re-boot cleared up (ie temp files).

Is this possily your situation.

Technically you shouldn't need to (not with package management), but often it is the only way to clear out the temp junk that the system no longer needs.

With this in mind, did you need to do anything 'clever' with your wireless card to get it working in the first instance.
Are all the modules etc availabe in 'free' and 'open' format? It may be that the update changed to one that was?
