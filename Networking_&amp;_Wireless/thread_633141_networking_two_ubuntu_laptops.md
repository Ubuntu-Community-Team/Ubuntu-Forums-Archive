### Post by tact on 2007-12-06
hey there...  the possibilities are endless.  Need to narrow down what you have and what you need (how much data is it? etc).

You could "sneaker-net" it...  use a largish thumbdrive (assuming the old laptop has USB ports.

You could use use a crossover ethernet cable and direct connect the two machines (assuming they both have ethernet ports

You could do an adhoc wifi connection.  (assuming both have wifi)

You could have one laptop connected to the internet (assuming you have an internet connection and that laptop has 2 ethernet ports) and configure it for ip masquerading (internet connection sharing - assuming you'd want to to this) to get the second laptop on the internet at the same time.

You could do a lot of things.  But we all would be doing a lot of assuming.   Help us out a bit.  :)
