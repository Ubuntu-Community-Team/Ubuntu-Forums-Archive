### Post by tashmooclam on 2007-11-07
I have a Dell Latitude D600 laptop that I got used. The wireless was fine with XP. 
It had a Broadcom wireless card. I got the so-called "bcm43xx-fwcutter" and firmware via synaptic package manager. I got a little program called Wicd which replaced the default Network Manager.
I wasn't happy with the slow wireless.
I went on Ebay and bought an Intel Pro Wireless 2200 B wireless card. 
I took out the Broadcom card and stuck the Intel card in there.
I restarted the laptop. I pressed the Fn key and F2 key hard and turned on the card. 
Using Synaptic package manager, I removed the "bcm43xx-fwcutter". 
I went to System>Administration>Network> and selected wirless and entered the information in preferences.
Restart, and I have my wireless. But, this appears to be much faster than the Broadcom, so I am a happy camper.
I downloaded some "software updates" and the download never slowed down as before when using the Broadcom card.
My experiment cost me $24 for the Intel wireless card, but now this can actually be called a wireless laptop.
The firmware for the Intel card is in Ubuntu 7.1, evidently also the driver. 
If I had known that it was so simple to change my wireless card, I would have done it before installing Ubuntu.
