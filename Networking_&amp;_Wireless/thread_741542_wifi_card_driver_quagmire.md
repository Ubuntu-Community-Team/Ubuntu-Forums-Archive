### Post by chili555 on 2008-03-31
Have you checked here? [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported) It's not terribly easy because card manufacturers are busy making new cards and do not divulge what they consider proprietary information to Linux developers. They also change models and chipsets every week or so. Therefor, you cannot walk into BestBuy and get a perfectly working card that was manufactured 2-3 years ago. The stores all want to show you a box that says 'New and Improved.'

You will need a PCMCIA card that does 802.11g and WPA, I'd guess. There are some cards on that list that only do 802.11b and WEP.

Here is another resource: [http://linux-wless.passys.nl/query_alles.php?](http://linux-wless.passys.nl/query_alles.php?)

Also, in my opinion, USB devices are the trickiest of all. I strongly recommend you steer clear of USB.
