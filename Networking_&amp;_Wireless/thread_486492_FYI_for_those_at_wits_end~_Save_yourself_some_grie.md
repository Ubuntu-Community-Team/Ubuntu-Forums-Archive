### Post by ocdlampwork on 2007-06-28
I fought with my wireless connection for two months with no luck. I did everything I was told and had everything right and still no wireless connection. All my hardware was compatible and should have set-up easy. I finally reached a point where I had to ask myself "how much is my time and grief worth?"
I ditched my Linksys router ( I was never impressed anyway with it's wireless performance before I deleted Windows and loaded Ubuntu on my wireless desktop) I removed my "Linux compatible" Broadcom wireless card.
I purchased a Buffalo WHR-HP-G54 and their wireless ethernet converter. I plugged them in and used the nifty "AOSS" one touch feature and my Ubuntu system was wireless in just a few minutes. 
I have a wired XP desktop, wireless Ubuntu desktop and a wireless PS3 all running like a charm.
My stress and grief over failed connection attempts with my old wireless equipment was definitely worth $120 in my opinion. I spent over two months of my time with the belief I was doing something wrong. The only thing wrong I did was wait so long for my new set-up.:tongue: I know it may not be an option for everyone but it is definitely food for thought.;)
I read this suggestion when I was at my wits end and was about to go back to Windows so I could get my computer wireless again. (I'm sorry I don't remember who the person was so I could give him credit:))
This was a sanity saver for me and the perfect solution. Not to mention the Buffalo products are awesome!:D I have super fast wireless to boot!:D
