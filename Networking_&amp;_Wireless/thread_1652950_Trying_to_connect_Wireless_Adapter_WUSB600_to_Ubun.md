### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-12-26
First, we need more information. Is the wireless adapter built in to the motherboard? Is it a USB stick? Or a PCI card that is in a PCI slot.

Second, is your wireless adapter recognized by Ubuntu? Did you have the wireless adapter plugged in at the time of installation so that it could be detected by the installation process and the relevant kernel modules installed to work the adapter.

Third, do you need specific drivers to work this device? Go to System>Administration>Additional Drivers. That utility may tell you that you need additional drivers and install them for you. Do not forget to reboot.

Fourth, do you see a network manager icon? If so, what do you see when you right and left click the icon. In other words, how do you know that things are not working correctly. Like a doctor, we need to be told the signs and symptoms of what is wrong.

Fifth, please confirm that the router/modem is actually connected to the ISP. Are the LEDs on the modem activated as they should be?

May be I should assume that you have checked all these things. I am thinking of a recent post where someone was complaining that they could not make a wired connection to the router/modem. The person was given advice to do this and to do that. And it turned out that the problem was caused by the person not having plugged the ethernet cable into the modem. I think that it is good to confirm that the simple, obvious things are correct before we try the more difficult things.

Regards.
