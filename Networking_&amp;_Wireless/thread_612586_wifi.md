### Post by spd106 on 2007-11-15
The cards I know of that can support multiple connections simultaneously are ones with Atheros chipsets using the Madwifi driver. Even then it's tricky to do. See the [madwifi.org]("http://madwifi.org") website for instructions and card compatibility lists.

Some of the embeded chips used in AP can do this too (WDS mode), though I don't know much about that. Perhaps try the [openwrt.org]("http://openwrt.org/") website.
