### Post by kc1di on 2012-09-29
Well when you first got your internet connection your Internet Service Provider should have given you the ISP DNS Number. it's the routing number that connects you to their server. Normally it is just sent to your router/machine via the DHCP Automatically. But as I have had the problem before sometimes when a router goes bad DHCP stops working. But you can manually ask the machine to use your ISP's DNS # instead of seeking it via DCHP. Thus it will always connect using that DNS.  If you can find the DNS # for your Internet server and use it and it works then you know that something is amiss with the DHCP service. and most likely it may be  the router it's self. But that just a shot at it :) 

Hope you find the problem.  Does the router connect on other hard wire machines , or in windows?
