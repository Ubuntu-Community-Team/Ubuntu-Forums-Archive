Please run:```
route -n
```Verify that your gateway is actually 192.168.2.1. Then do:```
sudo gedit /etc/resolv.conf
```Amend it to look like this:```
# Generated by NetworkManager
#domain Belkin
#search Belkin
nameserver 192.168.2.1
```Proofread, save and close gedit. Now do:```
ping -c3 192.168.2.1

### Post by chili555 on 2010-02-12
Frankly, all your readings look just fine. I wonder if this is a signal strength issue, either at the router or at the card or both. Is there a provision in the router to change signal strength? Is the card at the maximum?```
sudo iwconfig wlan0 txpower 15
```Keep increasing the number until you get a complaint:> chili@LAPTOP60:~$ sudo iwconfig wlan0 txpower 20
[sudo] password for chili: 
Error for wireless request "Set Tx Power" (8B26) :
    SET failed on device wlan0 ; Invalid argument.
chili@LAPTOP60:~$ sudo iwconfig wlan0 txpower 14
chili@LAPTOP60:~$I also wonder if you have RF interference so that the router and card could better communicate on some channel other than 6, the usual default. My iwl3945 and router like channel 1.
