### Post by becominglumberg on 2007-07-19
I wanted to throw Homeplug out there with a strong recommendation for anyone looking to give your desktop a  cable free connection.

After 3 months of working to get a wireless card working on my desktop (since PCI wireless cards all seem to rely on chips that are just tough to work with, and I really don't like NDIS wrapper) I decided to give Homeplug a try to finally get rid of the annoying cord running through my bedroom. Long story short, when the two adapters came, I was extremely excited to see if they gave a good signal over the power line. Plugging the two adapters in allowed for an unencrypted signal immediately, and I didn't notice any lag in connection compared to the cable it replaced (the adapters are rated for 14mbps, which is much faster than either Cox or Verizon go for residential areas in my town). So far, there has been no resetting, no temporary outages. The only thing to consider is that you may not use any extension cords with these devices, so you really must have an accessible outlet. Encryption was a breeze, and took about 5 minutes and me to switch the adapters (since you have to have them attached to the computer when you set them up). Unfortunately, encryption did require a windows install to set the information, but once it is set, it is remembered, and the plugs may be moved - so all you really need is a friend with a windows box (or a work laptop).

As an FYI - the ones I am using are by NovaTech, and i bought a pair from newegg for about $70 shipped. This was the best deal I could find, and the units were highly reviewed. Linksys (my fav networking brand) also has them, but at a higher price point (of course). For some reason, they don't make a two pack, so you have to order two boxes, and remember to get the Ethernet Adapter one, since that will play nicely. The USB adapter requires a real driver, not just a configuration utility.
