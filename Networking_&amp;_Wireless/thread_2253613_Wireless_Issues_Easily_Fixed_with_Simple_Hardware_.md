There are tons of posts on all of the forums about wireless card drivers (mainly Broadcom 802.11). Most of the issues are being caused by generic cards being improperly identified as Broadcom Minicards. A simple swap has done the trick for me more than once. The most reliable cards are Atheros wifi cards and they are priced at around $5 on eBay. I'm greatful you guys keep working on fixes and work-arounds for older systems but for guys like me that just want things to work the way they were intended and don't have time to search endless threads and try out dozens of suggested scripts, this is the sure-fire answer.

### Post by weatherman2 on 2014-11-21
I have replaced many laptop internal wireless cards successfully.  (I prefer Intel cards.)  One caution: some brands of laptops - namely, HP and Lenovo - do not allow you to replace the wireless card - they "whitelist" the card to prevent you from installing a non-approved wireless card.  If you insert a wireless card that is not on the laptop's whitelist, it will not even POST until you remove the unapproved card.

And some internal wireless cards are easy to swap, but others are difficult - under the keyboard or somewhere not easy to access.  It varies by make/model of laptop.
