### Post by chili555 on 2007-04-12
There are really no known Ubuntu or Linux issues with consumer-grade wireless routers, that we know of. The protocol, 802.11a/b/g, is the protocol, whether you connect with  Linux, Mac or...whats their name. Once you connect, TCP/IP is the same for everyone.

Linux is a bit picky about connecting readily, but once you have the particulars set, it's no harder, or easier than that other operating system.

While the particulars can be set from the command line, they are easier to accomplish with some of the wireless managers, NetworkManager, wifi-radar, Wicd, wlassistant and there may be others. You may search this forum for dozens of posts reporting NM is great, NM is horrible, etc. for all the contenders.

My roaming is not from home to work to coffee shop, it's from family room to breakfast room to porch. I prefer hard-code.
