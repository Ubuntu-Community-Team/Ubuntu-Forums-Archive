### Post by mycatknowsbetter on 2011-01-28
I have beem having problems for the past month since setting up a home wireless network. First, I struggled to get my Linksys AE1000 adapter working. Finally managed it, but then it started to drop connections, (often) and run more and more slowly. I upgraded to 10.10 and it failed altogether. I bought a TPLINK adapter with an Atheros chip. Worked out of the box! A couple of weeks later, same problem. 

I have been a windows guy since 3.1 and am a tinkerer but not an expert. I was unable to repair the problem so started looking for a new distribution. I picked Fedora 14. Problem solved! Fast connection, full strength signal, good for about an hour. As soon as I began configuring my desktop, the problem returned. I will admit in hindsight this looked like a conflict the likes of which, looked like problems in the early days of PC's. 

To make an already too long story shorter, the problem surfaced as soon as I had installed Gnome-Do's Docky. It was resolved as soon as I disabled, and uninstalled Docky.  I don't have any explanations or repairs but as soon as I dropped the apparently offending software, the problem disappeared. I posted this in case someone wants to check this out, and come up with a more elegant solution than uninstalling. I am also running an old Lenovo laptop on OpenSuse 11.3, and two other PC's on Windows, with no problems. My o/s of choice is Linux and I am happy to have a resolution to this problem, no matter how crude.
