### Post by bra|10n on 2012-10-23
I recently purchased a pre-paid Telstra 4G USB dongle for use with a laptop from from a Telstra outlet. 
The device is specifically a ZTE MF821 modem with a 4G sim card which can be setup and used on a Linux O/S relatively easily. 
So far the unit is performing well with no performance complaints from me.

This being a pre-paid plan w/. a small 3 Gb usage allowance per 30 days I enquired with Telstra on the matter of recharge/usage notification, and how to setup my account so that I always maintain Internet access;

Telstra's suggestions;

1.  Login to telstra.com.au to check data usage.
2.  Activate a 'text alert'. This can be set at any minimum value you like.
3.  Setup a recharge date automatically debiting a credit card or bank account.

I asked what I should do though to recharge in the event I run out of credit...

Telstra: Oh, in that case you would need to ring Telstra.

I asked whether an email could be sent in place of a text message to notify of low credit warning. 

Telstra: No.

I asked whether the modem would redirect a browser to the Telstra site offering the choice to recharge in the event of having used all available credit.

Telstra:  No.

I enquired whether an automatic recharge date couldn't be linked to a selected recharge point. 

Telstra: No.

I asked whether it was possible to pre-pay the 'next block of credit' in advance to kick in once either the 30 days or credit cap had been reached.

Telstra: No.

Telstra, it was nice doing business with you however brief our (7 days) association was. Not once did I hear the word 'Yes' from you. Not once did you offer to put in a request or to review your current framework. Unfortunately your stupidity and blunt refusal to accommodate this customer has been at your expense. 
I'll now unlock my USB modem for the quoted AU$7 and switch across to your largest competitor Optus.

I want to bang my head continuously against a wall when I think about the dill who thought that sending a txt message to a USB sim was a good idea. 
IMO he should be beaten mercilessly "like an errant red-headed child".
