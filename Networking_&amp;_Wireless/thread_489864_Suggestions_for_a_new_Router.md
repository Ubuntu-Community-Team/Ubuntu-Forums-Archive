### Post by jml on 2007-07-01
Need to put my two cents in here.  Since the 802.11n standard has not yet been officially ratified, buying a device that supports the draft standard poses a bit of a risk.  I have heard anecdotal stories on various forums and in print about problems with interoperability.  Not all companies 802.11n hardware will play well together.  Problems listed ranged from slow connection speeds to an inability to connect at all.  You might be safe if you plan to stick with only one manufacturer for all of your 802.11n needs, but if you will be mixing hardware from different manufactures, you may run into problems.  Problems that firmware upgrades may not be able to fix.  This happened several years ago when the industry switched from 802.11b to 802.11g hardware.  Like Yogi Beara said,  "It's deja vue all over again."
