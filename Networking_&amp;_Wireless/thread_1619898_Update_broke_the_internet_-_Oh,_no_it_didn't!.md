### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-11-12
Last night I updated the system as advised by Update Manager. Today I could not access the Internet. But the update did not break my system. The fault was not with the modem, computer or operating system. I had the connected by ethernet icon and message. The listing from ifconfig said that both eth1 and wlan0 were UP and RUNNING. The listing from nm-tool said that eth1 was connected. The route command showed that I had the right IP address of the modem. The indicator lights on the modem showed that I had a connection to the ISP. The modem setup program showed that I had a DSL connection to the ISP. I switched the modem off and then on. I got a message from Discovery Services saying that my network connection was local and that was not good.

I could not download any web pages. The BBC headlines did not download. The Met Office weather gadget did not download the weather radar image for my area. What was I to do? I reverted to old technology. I looked out of the window to see what the weather was. 

I switched off the modem to save electricity. I stopped myself from trying to fix what was not broken. And so I did not mess things up further. I got on with my personal study project that I use the computer for.

That was about three hours ago. Just now I switched the modem back on. The network disconnected icon changed to the connected by ethernet icon and I am now connected to the Internet. So what caused the problem? Who knows? In recent months in this country we have had theft of copper cables from electricity power lines causing power failure for parts of the railways and into people homes. Just a few days ago a part of London lost the Internet due to the theft of copper cables. There have been severe gales over the country during the night. This has taken out the power supplies in some areas of the country. Perhaps my ISP had a power breakdown that has now been fixed.

Moral? Do not be quick to blame an update or an upgrade.
