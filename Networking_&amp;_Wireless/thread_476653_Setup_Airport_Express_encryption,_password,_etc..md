### Post by twocargar on 2007-09-03
Unfortunately, you need a Mac or PC to run the AirPort Assistant, or to administer a BaseStation.  Take it down to your local Apple Store (if you have one) and they'll help you I bet.  The settings will stay in its memory while you take it home.  The Apple Airport software is proprietary, so there isn't a Linux version that I'm aware of.

Once you get your Airport up and running, you can connect to it with no problems and can even print to a USB AirPort connected printer.

UPDATE:
I just ran across this [Java AirPort Admin Utility]("http://gicl.cs.drexel.edu/people/sevy/airport/#Configurator") (a little old, but may work).  You'll need the JRE for it.  It didn't see my network of Extreme base stations, but I have a closed network and didn't feel like undoing it to test it out.  Let us know if it works for you.
