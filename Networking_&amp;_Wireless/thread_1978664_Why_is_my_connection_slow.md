### Post by chili555 on 2012-05-12
The security setting has little to do with speed, generally. However, since you brought it up, it's fair game for discussion. WEP is quite insecure. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wired_Equivalent_Privacy](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wired_Equivalent_Privacy)> In 2005, a group from the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation gave a demonstration where they cracked a WEP-protected network in 3 minutes using publicly available tools.As long as you have a shiny new router that's capable of the latest encyption, I suggest you set the router to use WPA2-AES. [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Encryption_Standard](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Encryption_Standard)> Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) is a specification for the encryption of electronic data. It has been adopted by the U.S. government and is now used worldwide. Be sure to select a password that is hard to decipher; in other words, *_not_* your last name, house number, the word 'password' etc.

Once the encryption has been reset, let's see if you connect quickly.

If you need some assistance with the router, please post back and I'll be very happy to help.
