### Post by nickpaton on 2006-08-04
If your existing DSL Modem does not have a built-in wireless section, then you will not be able to use your wireless card with it.

Typically DSL Modems are referred as DSL Modem Routers, or Routers for short.  I'll continue with Router!

To tell if your router has a built-in wireless section, it usually has an external aerial to pick up the signals from your wireless card, which should also have an aerial.

So, to operate wirelessly:

1. Your PC is not connected to the router using a cable.

2. Both the router and the wireless card must have an aerial in order to transmit and receive the signals to and from each other - a bit like using a mobile phone.  With a mobile phone you do not have a long piece of cable attached to your provider, so as to use the phone. No, you connect to the nearest phone transmitter wirelessly, and your conversations are sent and received wirelessly.

A typical router which does not have a built-in wireless section is the Netgear DG834:

[http://www.netgear.com/Products/RoutersandGateways/WiredRouters/DG834.aspx]("http://www.netgear.com/Products/RoutersandGateways/WiredRouters/DG834.aspx")

There is a wireless version of this one the DG834G:

[http://www.netgear.com/Products/RoutersandGateways/GWirelessRouters/DG834G.aspx]("http://www.netgear.com/Products/RoutersandGateways/GWirelessRouters/DG834G.aspx")

Note the immediate difference - the first one has no aerial, the 2nd has!!

I hope this is of help, and does answer your questions.  I'm not trying to be patronising if you already know this, but if it does help, and you have more questions, please do ask - not a problem:p :p
