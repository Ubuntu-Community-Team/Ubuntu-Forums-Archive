Google is your friend! I just googled this: ubuntu track download usage

and found these links in first page of results:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=931854]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=931854")

So that should give you a few options!

Also, as one of the posters in the Ubuntu Forums thread points out, all ISPs who impose bandwidth limits also let you check your usage online, so that they don't have to get into arguments with their customers about going over bandwidth limits ("Well, you could have checked your usage at any time, don't blame us if we're shaping your traffic/charging you excess fees!")

Anyway, that's worth bearing in mind, to calibrate whatever software you choose with what the ISP says you're downloading. Because they'll go by what their tool says, not yours!
