### Post by balaknair on 2009-07-29
If you're looking for something like DUMeter in Windows(shows a graph on the desktop displaying download/upload speed), you can try either screelets(has a netmonitor app) or a conky script(you'll find loads of them at gnome-look.org).

If you just want a small graphical display, you can add one to your gnome panel(right click on the panel> 'add to panel'> sytem monitor ). This will put a small graph on the panel showing CPU usage, RAM usage and Network usage. You can right-click on the graph and select properties to choose which resources you want to monitor. If you want just network, uncheck the others(CPU, RAM, Swap etc.). It's what I use.


If you want to test your broadband speed, [www.speedtest.net](www.speedtest.net) gives you a pretty good picture
