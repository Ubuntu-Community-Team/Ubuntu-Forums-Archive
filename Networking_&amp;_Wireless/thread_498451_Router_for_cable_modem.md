### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-07-11
router's are OS independent, all they do is route tcp/udp packets to their correct places. You can even get one that has a built in SAP/NAT Firewall, DHCP, VPN, etc etc so you don't have to run a firewall on your internal machines. It's TCP/IP which has nothing to do with the OS.

That being said, go with whatever you want. I would suggest just buying a used one from Craigslist, there are normaly craigslist on the internet (gogle) that are classified sections for your local area where I'll bet you can find a super cheap router with built in firewall, DHCP, vpn, etc etc that will work. I can guarentee that it'll work whether it's kubuntu, ubuntu, suse, winbloz, or apple. Now wireless on the other hand, is a different story despite them supposedly being a part of the 802.11 standard or whatever it is. Good luck
