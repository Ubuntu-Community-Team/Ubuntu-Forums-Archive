### Post by SeijiSensei on 2014-03-28
Not for outbound traffic by those rules.  Your OUTPUT rule grants unrestricted access.  However you shouldn't be worrying about connections from the server to other places on the Internet.  What matters most from a security perspective are connections coming in to the server from outside.  You'll need to have access to a machine out on the Net to do proper testing. 

By the way, packet forwarding is disabled in Ubuntu unless you change the "net.ipv4.ip_forward" switch in /etc/sysctl.conf.  Your REJECT rule for the FORWARD chain is icing on the cake unless you have enabled packet forwarding.
