### Post by randcoop on 2008-01-20
Wondering why you're not letting network-manager/knetworkmanager do the work.  If you want to manually configure the wireless, then the link in the previous message may indeed help you with that.  But Kubuntu 7.10 (you don't mention which version you installed) defaults to knetworkmanager.  If you have an icon in your panel on the right, just right-click on it to see available wireless networks.  Select the one you want by clicking on it.  Knetworkmanager will try to connect to that wireless network.
