### Post by varunendra on 2014-07-21
Not sure about the IPv6 DNS, but you currently don't have an IPv4 DNS defined.

Are you getting the IP configuration via DHCP or manually? I suggest setting it to either "Manual" or "Automatic (DHCP) address only" in Network Manager, then define your preferred DNS server(s) manually in "DNS servers" field of Network Manager settings for your connections. Save the settings > disconnect > reconnect and see if you can browse again.
