### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-25
I can ping roseserver.mywire.org fine.  I suspect your WAN router is "protecting" your LAN from devious LAN-WAN-LAN attacks. There should be an option or you can run an internal DNS so roseserver.mywire.org is resolved to the LAN IP when you are not on the outside.

I did a quick nmap scan ( that was me, and if you didn't notice it already, you need to fix your security ASAP ) 
```
PORT      STATE  SERVICE
20/tcp    closed ftp-data
21/tcp    open   ftp
22/tcp    open   ssh
80/tcp    open   http
443/tcp   open   https

```

a) don't use plain FTP. Use ssh and sftp only.  Port 20 and 21 should never be open.

b) move ssh to a non-standard port or your logs will get thousands of attempts daily.  I have my router perform port translation for this, so on the LAN (inside) ssh is on default ports, but on the WAN, some very high port - say 54022/tcp is used.  This little change will remove 10,000+ daily attempts to just a few, less than 10, from your logs.  

You only need port 80 open during renewal of LE certs.  For some reason, they use port 80 for renewals too. IDK why.

I can reach 
>    Welcome to the Rose Server. This is the dummy index page. on port 80/tcp and 443/tcp.  So it seems you've solved the issue.

Stop your FTP server and move ssh to a non-standard port.  Also, I'd setup a ~/.ssh/config file on all ssh, scp, sftp, rsync, sshfs, x2go clients to make accessing the non-standard ssh port automatic.  Use a different name for internal use and use over the internet to keep the LAN vs WAN ssh-based access separate.

vsftpd is something I'd never use due to passed poor security that was not caught for a long time.  ssh has thousands of more eyes on it and has much better client support - sftp is a very well known protocol.  But it is your box. Do what you like.

### Post by TheFu on 2022-06-26
FTPS = "FTP over SSL" aren't firewall friendly like sftp is.  Dynamically assigned ports is the issue.  You can limit the port range, but do you really want to open multiple ports when sftp uses 1 and we know how to secure it (use fail2ban and ssh-keys) already?

Opening so much risk for 1 client seems flawed, especially since you already have ssh open.  I just hope that none of the WAN connections are using passwords. Always use key-based authentication over the internet. Always.

Be very careful choosing tp-link equipment.  They don't have a good history of patching.  For network devices that will never need patching, like a dumb switch, I don't have any issue with TP-link or other cheap vendors.  WAN routers are different and we need companies that will support their equipment for 3-10 yrs.  There are a few ways to get this relatively cheap, but you can spend more money to get supported routers that are just a bit easier.  Asus is one of those vendors.  That's because they were caught by the US-FTC and part of their settlement was to be watched by independent teams for 20 yrs to ensure they provide world-class support.  [https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2016/02/asus-settles-ftc-charges-insecure-home-routers-cloud-services-put-consumers-privacy-risk](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2016/02/asus-settles-ftc-charges-insecure-home-routers-cloud-services-put-consumers-privacy-risk) has the story and settlement conditions.

Of course, you can use an x86-64 computer (or tiny router) to provide a platform for well supported x86-64 router distros like openWRT, pfsense, opnsense and others. Those will be updated continuously thanks to the underlying platform.  I have an APU2 with an AMD 64-bit CPU. It uses 12W of power and was $144 new when I bought it around 2015. I patch it every Friday night.
