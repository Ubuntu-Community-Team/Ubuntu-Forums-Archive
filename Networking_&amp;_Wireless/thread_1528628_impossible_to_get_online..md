### Post by dmizer on 2010-07-11
> **hugin2 said:**
> usb-cable/ethernet-cable = same, same

No, they are not the same. USB requires proprietary drivers for your modem that may or may not exist in Ubuntu. With ethernet, you just need to plug it in and go, no special drivers.

Furthermore, if you have a modem with only one ethernet port and only one USB port, it's most likely that you cannot use both at the same time anyway. So, you'll either have to use ethernet, or you should invest in a router so you can connect multiple computers to your modem. You should also note, that a router purchase would be necessary if it was two Windows computers, two Ubuntu computers, two Macs or any combination of the three.

Wireless is an entirely different issue which we can probably help with once you have gotten online via ethernet.
