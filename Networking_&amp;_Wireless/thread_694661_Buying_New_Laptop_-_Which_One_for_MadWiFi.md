### Post by dca on 2008-02-12
Good luck w/ the Acers...  I have an Aspire 5610z.  Pentium dual-core, Intel graphics, w/ the Ambit (Atheros AR5007eg) WiFi card.  This card is a bear.  New technology using mini-PCIe versus mini-PCI causing a nightmare but it's do-able w/ either ndiswrapper or the madwifi unauthorized patch/fix...
 
The thing about Acers, I have the 5610z, the regular model came w/ ATI graphics.  I guess in order to compete on price Acer uses whatever hardware available to them at the time.  When one batch of mobos runs out, they switch to another and create a new model.  When the Ambit wirelesses cards run out they'll switch back to the Intel wireless cards...  I can't complain, I've had no problems hardware-wise but configging Linux distro(s) has been fun.

### Post by dca on 2008-02-12
On the side of the brown Acer box there's a small specifics listing that actually includes quite a bit of info (minus which wireless card, only lists it as wireless card).  For the price I paid I can't complain and have been happy for about a year.  Any laptop you buy you'll run into issues that will require attention from the hardware used to the particular distro you use.  openSuSE & Ubuntu have been the best.  The CPUfreq doesn't work in openSuSE for the Acer and Ubuntu indicates at boot that it can't allocate resources for two devices which I believe are Bluetooth module and possibly the built-in webcam.  I can't stress enough that on ANY distro chosen there will be items that need to be reconfigured.
 
On the  aside, it's not that Acer used cheaper components.  They just used too da**  many.  I've never seen such an expanse of hardware options between same and similar models.  IOW, go to Dell's website or Lenovo or HP.  The same model variation only consists of upgrade from CD-ROM to combo or DVD burner.  From 512MB RAM to 1Gig, a 120GB HDD to a 250...  With Acer, there's more model numbers than there are components in the darn thing.
