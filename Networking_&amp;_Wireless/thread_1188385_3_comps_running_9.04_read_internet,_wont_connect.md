### Post by scrooge_74 on 2009-06-15
One idea here, some providers tie down the MAC address from one PC to their router, so you need to clone that address into your own router and plug that one to the provider's router and then all the PCs to your own router.

All routers give you the explanation on how to clone the MAC (basically there is somewhere in the config where you type down the MAC of your ethernet card).

I had that experience with a cable modem provider which will assign one IP and if you want to conect more machines you have to pay extra
