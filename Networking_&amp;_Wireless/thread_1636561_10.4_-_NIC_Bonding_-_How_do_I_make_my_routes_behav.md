### Post by puppywhacker on 2010-12-03
if eth0 and eth1 get an ip-address and netmask assigned, it will automatically create a net-route based on the information. The question is, what sets the eth0 and eth1. if it is the network-manager you could disable it.

Based on your bond you don't need to have to configure an ip address for each interface, and if you do set them to something like 172.16.x.x that case you won't have conflicting routes.
