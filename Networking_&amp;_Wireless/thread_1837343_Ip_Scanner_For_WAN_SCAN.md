If you are trying to scan you LAN then there are tons of tools, NMAP is my preferred.

If you mean scan your WAN ip address (probably your router) then you can visit [https://www.grc.com/x/ne.dll?bh0bkyd2](https://www.grc.com/x/ne.dll?bh0bkyd2) to do a scan of your WAN address.

If you want to scan other WAN addresses then that i suspect suspicious activity in which case it is not supported here.

If you have the permission to scan other WAN addresses then you should already have the knowledge to carry out the task ;-)
