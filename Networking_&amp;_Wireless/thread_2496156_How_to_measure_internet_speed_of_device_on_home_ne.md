### Post by TheFu on 2024-03-16
320 Kbit/second is nothing.  If you don't have ISDN or dialup, then your internet is 10x, or more, fast enough.  Wifi from 2000 was fast enough to support that speed, so if you aren't getting it, your roku wouldn't be able to show any video streaming in hidef - even 720p is ~15000 Kbit/sec - much more than any audio.

Whether the Roku will play back audio at that quality is a different question completely.

Almost all internet streaming, video or audio, gets buffered. The stream isn't consistent. It is like turning on and off a faucet over and over and over to keep the buffer full on the downloading/playback device.

If you suspect issues with the roku, use a wired ethernet connection.  I always avoid wifi. It is nowhere near as stable as a wired ethernet connection.  The claimed connection speeds on any wifi device is one of the most common lies in networking.  It is up there with blaming the dog when someone farts. ;)

If your roku doesn't have a streaming status page, I really don't know what to tell you.  My roku has some codes for wifi details and network details.
Wireless Details
   Home (five times) > Up > Down > Up > Down > Up.

Network Information
   Home (five times) > Right > Left > Right > Left > Right.

The wired connection information is just the IP and gateway, so not exactly great. I did see aa "AP Speed Test Secret Screen" on the wifi screen.  Of course, YMMV since our devices are different.

There are a number of other roku codes. Google finds them easily.
