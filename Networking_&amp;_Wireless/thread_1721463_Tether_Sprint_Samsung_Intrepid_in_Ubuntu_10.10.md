### Post by emilward on 2011-04-04
In case anyone doesn't know, Sprint no longer supports Internet Sharing. You HAVE to purchase a special package that includes it (which is BS). So needless to say, I've been trying for awhile to get my Samsung Intrepid to tether with Ubuntu 10.10 and it turns out its ridiculously simpler than I thought. 

1. plug in your phone using SPECIFICALLY the USB Data cable. Do not confuse this with any cable that has a mini USB to standard USB connector. Your phone should of came with one but if not, buy one from Sprint as these cannot be purchased in a store (they can but they do not work!)

2. Next open up the 'Internet Sharing' and "PC Connection" should say 'USB' and "Network Connection" will say 'Phone As Modem'. Chage this to 'Sprint Data'.

3. Now press Connect and thats it. Ubuntu 10.10 will automatically detect the connection and will connect in a few seconds. 

I personally cannot believe its THAT simple. I have been trying WAY TOO HARD to get this to work using different programs, applications, hacks, etc.... Any ways, if anyone has been going through what I have been then I hope this works.
