### Post by bogan on 2011-11-19
Hi! **adwallum**, I do not know if this helps you, but on my set up, on booting and entering the GUI screen, there is no WiFI Icon; then I get a log-on window, followed by a `Default` Ring authorization password requester. Fill that in correctly, and the WiFi Icon appears showing scanning in progress.

Left Click on the Icon and the drop-down menu shows the recognized networks, but if you are not getting the Requester that implies your ESSID is not being recognized, hence no icon.

Good Luck, **bogan**

### Post by adwallum on 2011-11-21
Thanks bogan, that fixed it. I was set up to not auto log on. I didn't get the prompt for 'authorization' just asked for login password.

I went into System Settings>User Accounts, unlocked and then set my account to auto login. Upon reboot I got the 'Default' Ring authorization as you say. I entered my password and there was the wireless icon.

I then reset to not auto login and rebooted. My usual login window asked for password, I put it in and the wireless icon has been visible ever since.

It's an odd workaround, clearly a glitch somewhere.

Thanks again for the lead
