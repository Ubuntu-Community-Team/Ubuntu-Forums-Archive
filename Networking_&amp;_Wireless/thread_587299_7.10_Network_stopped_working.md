### Post by dr_agon on 2007-11-04
You have doubled interfaces: eth0 - eth0:avah and wlan0 - wlan0:ava.  It happened to me when I restarted the network with new configuration (probably without shutting down existing interfaces). You must get rid of this interface clones.
Try
ifconfig <interface> down
ifdown <interface>

Then tidy up your /etc/network/interfaces and restart networking.
Perhaps you should comment out "auto <interface>" lines and manually start the interfaces to see what happens.
