### Post by Dreamer Fithp Apprentice on 2013-04-23
I presume you're talking about using a laptop or similar thing with a wireless router with an encrypted connection and the password being asked for is the router password. 

If that is the case it could simply be a weak signal. Too far from the router maybe? To much metal in between you and it? Low battery on the laptop? Router is giving out? If steps to improve the signal enough to remedy the problem aren't immediately practical or signal strength isn't the problem you might remove the encryption so it can handle reconnection automatically. It will still slow you down some but maybe not enough to be bothersome and it certainly won't be as annoying. Whether or not this is desirable depends on your "threat environment". If you live in the boonies encrypting your router connection is just a pointless way to choke down your bandwidth and annoy you. If you live in an apartment next door to a guy that wears T shirts that make jokes you have to know Lisp to understand and has expensive tastes and NVMOS, encryption might be prudent.



Suggestion: Be a bit less economical in typing and use enough words to make questions immediately clear and less cryptic and you will probably get more responses.
