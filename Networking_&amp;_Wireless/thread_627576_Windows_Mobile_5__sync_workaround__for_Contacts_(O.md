### Post by xodus1914 on 2007-11-30
Hello, all. I am a Ubuntu/Linux newbie. First a little background:
1. I have a Moto Q with all my contacts/appointments on my phone (Freshly synced from Outlook) .
2.  I am running Thunderbird, not Evolution, and I want to transfer all of my stuff over. 
3.  *Key*  I just picked up a yahoo address as my primary email.  *Key*

Yahoo has a mobile app called Yahoo! Go. You need a yahoo email for this to work. 
1. Installed Go! on my phone and it asks me if I want to sync my phone's address book to my Yahoo! address book on the web, and I say 'yes' . The phone then uploads all of my contacts to yahoo's server. 
2. At my computer I log into my yahoo account and go to Contacts, then Import/Export
3. Surprise! There is an option to export your Contacts from the Yahoo! server to your PC in the Netscape/Thunderbird format. Download.
4. Inside Thunderbird, merely import your file and now you have successfully transferred all of your contacts from Outlook/phone to Thunderbird. 

Exporting from Thunderbird is slightly easier. 
1. Export your Thunderbird contacts into a .CSV file. Place it on your desktop. 
2. Login to your yahoo account and go to the Contacts...Import/Export screen. 
3. Import your .CSV file. There are two options here, though. One is an Outlook .CSV file and the other is a Yahoo! .CSV file. I think I used the Yahoo!  .CSV format. 
4. Bam. Your Contactss are synced. The Yahoo!Go program will automatically update the contacts on your phone with the ones on it's server. 

Somehow. yahoo! isn't quite as friendly with Calendar dates, but I'll figure something out. :)
