### Post by chili555 on 2015-10-11
> I have a fileserver running Ubuntu 10.4 LTSI'm probably a gluton for punishment for even answering! I am quite confident that 10-12 of my esteemed colleagues have read your thread and mumbled, "You need to upgrade, dude; we've made a lot of progress in drivers and the O/S in the last 5 1/2 years."

I agree.

If I were going to troubleshoot, I'd check the logs for clues as to why the driver isn't working as expected:```
cat /var/log/syslog | grep -e r8169 -e eth0
```I might assume it's a one-time error and unload and reload the driver:```
sudo modprobe -r r8169 && sudo modprobe r8169
```We have especially made a *LOT* of progress in Network Manager over the years. I suspect that might be part of the issue. I'd probably remove NM altogether and write my configuration in */etc/network/interfaces*; something like:```
auto lo
iface lo inet loopback

auto eth0
iface eth0 inet static
address 192.168.0.100
netmask 255.255.255.0
gateway 192.168.0.254
dns-nameservers 192.168.0.254 192.168.0.153
```Then, restart the interface:```
sudo ifconfig eth0 down && sudo ifup -v eth0
```Of course, substitute your details here.

But, most of all, I'd upgrade.

Hey guys,

one question @neoculture2:
Is your modem running a DHCP server? 

If yes, you could also try to set your eth0 to DHCP in /etc/network/interfaces
```

auto eth0
iface eth0 inet dhcp

```

and restart the interface with
```
sudo ifconfig eth0 down && sudo ifup -v eth0
```
just to see if your NIC will be assigned with an IP-address from your specified range.
