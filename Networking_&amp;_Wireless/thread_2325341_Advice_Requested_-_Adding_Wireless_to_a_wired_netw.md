### Post by ajgreeny on 2016-05-21
It sounds as if all you need is a wifi router, most of which these days have cable ethernet connections (4 on mine) and the ability to broadcast a wifi signal over both the 2.4 and 5 GHz bands.

Maybe you have a modem router without that wifi ability, in which case it is probably easiest to get a new wireless/ethernet router.

### Post by oldfred on 2016-05-21
I just replaced the Comcast wi-fi/router/modem device with my own modem & router, saves $10/month and will pay for modem & router in short order.  

My modem & router are at end of house and do not get good signal at other end. So with separate modem I can connect to Internet in one place and with some wiring, move wireless router to middle of house.

The faster speeds & more connections supported, in effect, cost more.  You want at least n with backward compatibility to g & b. But most new devices are now n with some now a newer still ac, but not a lot of devices support that yet. 
But then you have various speeds or even dual speeds like 450/1300 Mbps speeds. Faster speed is usually more expensive.

I have in closet an old Linksys that was only g. And a inexpensive ($20) Chinese brand n that was only 300Mbps, but it did work.
