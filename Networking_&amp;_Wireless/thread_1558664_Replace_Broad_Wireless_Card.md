So I said get an Intel card and toss your Broadcom card away and start demanding that your next computer have an Intel card as these cards have native support in the Linux kernel.

### Post by Roasted on 2010-08-22
> **ieee488 said:**
> Getting a Intel wireless card isn't expensive. I see a Intel Pro 2200BG for $10 including shipping on eBay right now. Of course, for N, you'll pay more.

Be forewarned about the 2200 BG. That seems to be the one problematic Intel card I've seen. I bought one for my mom's laptop since my options for G wireless cards in mini PCI format were limited from what I could see. The Intel 2200 BG card would connect to secured networks just fine, but after a while (20 minutes or so) it would magically disconnect - yet still say connected.

I let it online and continually pinged Google. After random intervals of times, it just became unresponsive, yet retained a connection to my network.

I found a bug report on it too around this time when I was troubleshooting it, but I never got it working.

Point is, while Intel is completely kick *** awesome with Linux, you should STILL do a little homework. I'm not saying the 2200 would be a guaranteed fail - I'm just trying to offer my experience to you just so it's aware that someone else out in the world had issues with that card. 

Oddly, I put a Broadcom BCM4312 into that laptop, and it's been working fine. I avoid Broadcom if at all possible - even in my Windows machines. I just think very poorly of them and I completely disagree with how they manage their Linux "drivers" as if they are doing us open source fanatics a favor by providing them and supporting us, but not realizing, what they're providing tends to suck. It's kind of like Dell. "We support Ubuntu! Choose from either of our... two... Ubuntu loaded machines at the same price of Windows machines we sell!"

Anyway, that's my 2 cents. Good luck.

### Post by ahallubuntu on 2012-12-19
Nowadays, I'd get an Intel WiFi Link 5100 instead of the older 4965AGN, if your laptop can accept it.  Should be about the same price on eBay.  Make sure you get the right version of it, though; this card comes in a "full height" size (same size as 4965AGN) or "half height" (smaller form factor).  And make sure you distinguish between the "Lenovo/HP" version of the card and the "Dell/Toshiba/everyone else" version.  They are not completely compatible!
