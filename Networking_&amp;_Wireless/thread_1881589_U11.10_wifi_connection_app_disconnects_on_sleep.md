### Post by northd_tech on 2011-11-16
I'm not sure what is involved in Oneiric 11.10's "Unity" environment (which I strongly dislike), but good ol' GNOME 2.x had the Gnome Keyring to store all your passwords on/in:

> **Why does Gnome Keyring ask for my password whenever I log in?**

                                                                     You may be asked for a password every time you log in so that you can connect    to a network. You can stop this from happening like so:   
                                
[LIST=1]
[*]                            Right click the Network Manager icon and choose        *Edit Connections*.
[*]                            Go to the *Wireless* tab, select the connection you        are using and click **Edit**.
[*]                            Check *Available to all users* and click        **Apply**. Enter your password when asked.
[/LIST]
               
                  The next time you log in, you should not need to enter your password in order    to connect to your network connection.   
[https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/internet/C/troubleshooting-keyring.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/internet/C/troubleshooting-keyring.html)

KDE has/had a thing called Kwallet that serves the same function.

[http://docs.kde.org/stable/en/kdeutils/kwallet/index.html](http://docs.kde.org/stable/en/kdeutils/kwallet/index.html)

I did discover that the newer Gnome 3.0 does have a "fallback mode" and Tweak Tools will bring it back to something that I might recongnize and use.
