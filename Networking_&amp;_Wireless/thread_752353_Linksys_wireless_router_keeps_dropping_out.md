### Post by dstew on 2008-04-11
Overall, it sounds like a problem with the router or DSL modem or wireless card hardware, since it is the same in XP or Ubuntu. It could also be your DSL provider is causing the dropped connections (same provider I have). I think sometimes they have issues, and try to shed users to save bandwidth. It might be some automatic process that they are not really conscious of, to be charitable. I have a problem like this, my connection speed drops from 856 Mb/sec to 32 Mb/sec. It doesn't matter if I am using XP, Ubuntu or both. If I turn off the DSL modem, and turn it on again, it is back up to speed. It might stay that way for a week, or an hour. Looking around on the web, I see that this happens a lot. The providers always blame the users, but I think it is really an issue on their side a lot of the time.

Sorry I can't really help you, but unless you can change hardware, I don't know how you can figure out what the problem is. Maybe try your laptop in an internet cafe with wireless, to see if the laptop card is at fault. Or, get someone else to bring their computer over to your house to see if it works better. At least that would convice you the problem was the router or DSL modem or the DSL provider, and not the laptop wireless card.
