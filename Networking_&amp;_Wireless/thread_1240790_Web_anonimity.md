No forgiveness needed.  I just want to impart some perspective.  Whatever you choose to do, please consider leaving your machine in as 'vanilla' a state as possible when actually crossing borders or customs stations.  

Anyhow, this is why what you are proposing should not be attempted by someone of your experience level:

The simplest manner of bypassing the great firewall is widely known and involves using caches in the cloud to move information around.  This does not solve the problem of 'last mile' monitoring.  So if you actually want privacy you are now reduced to leeching unsecured access points via a hardware device set to expose false identifying information.  If you want to post somewhere you are reduced to access via proxy, and your most secure proxy is likely the one you will be setting up at a friend's house outside of China.  

Now step back and ask how this looks from a security perspective.  You look like a terrorist.

You don't need to go all the way to China to find countries that consider aspects of this behavior illegal.  And at the end of the day what have you accomplished?  The Chinese government is not going to know that you visit UbuntuForums?  

Please do not set yourself up for trouble.  Configure your computer with security in mind (local firewall, manageable logging, possibly AppArmor to prevent priv escalations etc.). 

If you feel a burning need to go further than this it would be wise to review the potential consequences before they are crashing down on your head.
