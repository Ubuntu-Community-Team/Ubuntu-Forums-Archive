I would recommend something other than an Airport Extreme - the Airports require the use of a client application to configure (there is an old 3rd party Linux config app written in Java but it's flaky)

I'd go with a Linksys router (WRT54G or equivalent) I think you may have a tough time with storage/print drivers regardless of manufacturer (I've never used any of the consumer APs that share that kind of stuff out though it does seem from googling around that the Linksys printservers will work with Linux) If you don't have a printer yet, you may be well served to look at one with built-in network support, that usually works much better.
