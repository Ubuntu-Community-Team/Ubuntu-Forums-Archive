I installed Ubuntu 10 on my Acer Aspire 5100 AMD64 a week or two ago, but try as I did, I could not connect WIFI to the internet because of the infamous unsupported Broadcom drivers. :(  For those of you with Acers having the same problem, it can be easily fixed:).  Here is how to do it:

I purchased a **Linksys Compact Wirelless-G USB (Model #WUSB54GC)** from Circuit City for $49.00.  I booted up and lo and behold I had a signal strength of 73%.  But, it would not connect to the  internet.
This is the easy way to make it work ---

(1) With the USB Compact Wireless-G inserted, turn on your computer and allow Ubuntu to fully load.
(2) Click on **SYSTEM**
(3) Click on **ADMINISTRATION** After giving your password
(4) Click on **DNS**  Delete any information there
(5) DNS[/B]  Under DNS Servers inter your two DNS numbers
(6) Close that out and left click on the bars in the upper right hand corner of your desktop and then click on the Wireless network you are using.

This should connect you to the internet.  This WORKS OUT OF THE BOX.  You do not have to install anything!!!!!!:lolflag:
