### Post by gilf on 2007-12-15
When you click on the network manager icon at the top right of the screen you will see those bar graph network lines.

Click on one of the bar graph lines which has the name of the connection you want. This will connect you with that network. Note, it must be an unencrypted network, or you must have ready the password or passphrase needed to connect to that network. If either of these is required, network manager will ask you for the password after you click on the network line.

If you've already gone this far and Firefox does not automatically connect, open Firefox, click on Edit at the top of the screen, select Preferences, pick the Advanced tab at the top of the window, select the Network tab, click on Settings, try first "direct connection to the internet".  If that doesn't work, try "auto detect proxy settings".

If that doesn't work, you may want to try contacting your internet service provider by phone for support in connecting.

Hope this helps.
