### Post by bscbrit on 2006-02-11
I would say that most (all?) modern pci ethernet cards work very well nowadays.  You do not need to stick with the same manufacturer as your motherboard (e.g. Gigabit?) - the pci is a well established standard.  All of mine are recognised during installation and work immediately. I use a variety of NICs but, for your own peace of mind, I would recommend a well-known brand name such as Belkin, DLink or similar.  Don't go for anything esoteric or special - just a simply NIC will suffice.  However, there are cheap cards around which can work very well but, if they don't, there is little support or help available for them. As always, you get what you pay for to a certain extent.  :D
