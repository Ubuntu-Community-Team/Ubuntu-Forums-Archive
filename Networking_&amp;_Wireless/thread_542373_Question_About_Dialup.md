### Post by Benjamin G on 2007-09-03
When at home, I only have access to dial-up.  I never managed to get the internal winmodem in this laptop working with linux, although I once spent hours trying.  So this last summer I bought a serial modem, and used it with a desktop at home running a Knoppix LiveCD.

And to answer your question, the BT Talk21 (or something BT-ey) worked fine - you only have to supply a phone number, username and password.

Benjamin

EDIT: Forgot to mention - you need a PPP dialer like KPPP or WVDial.

### Post by Bartender on 2007-09-05
There are plenty of ISP's that don't require any proprietary software to work.  All you have to do is sign up with them, pay, and get a phone #, username, and password.
totalspeed, copper.net, basicISP, ISP.com, etc. etc.
Avoid the ones that want you to use their software.
