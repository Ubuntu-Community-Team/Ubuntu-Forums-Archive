### Post by gsparky2004 on 2009-09-17
I have two desktops, one running WinXP and the other running the latest, standard version of Ubuntu (9.04, I think.  It says "Jaunty Jackalope").  Yesterday, I had no problems networking between the two systems.  I had the smbfs running on the Ubuntu system, and sharing set-up on the Windows system.  All was well.

Last night, I loaded Maxtor Maxblast 5 onto my Windows system.  I then used it to clone the primary (C:) drive of my Windows system.  

This morning, when attempting to transfer some files between the two boxes, I was able to see the Windows system from Ubuntu, and I could even see the shared folders.  But when I clicked on the folders themselves, I got a "Unable to Mount Location" error.  Went through these forums, tried every trick in the book, all to no avail.  Nothing worked.  

Until I remembered that the only thing that had changed was loading Maxblast.  I just un-installed Maxblast, and VOILA!  All is well again.  Folders and files are flowing freely (pardon the alliteration!) between them.

Why is this?  I have no idea.  But if you are having problems getting Windows and Ubuntu to talk to each other *AND* you have Maxblast loaded on your Windows system, you might consider uninstalling it and seeing if that cures your problem.
