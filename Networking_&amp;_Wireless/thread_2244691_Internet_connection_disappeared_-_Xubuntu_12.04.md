### Post by varunendra on 2014-09-29
Since you mentioned the Ethernet connection doesn't work in Windows either, and we have no evidence of its 'present' existence in the system throughout this long thread (there are just traces of its existence "in the past"), I seriously suspect that it is either it is dead, or lose (connection or the connector, or the connectors pins soldered to the motherboard, since these are not 'plug-in' cards in laptops that may go loose).

Regarding the wireless card, it IS a plug-in card. But even with no driver, it should have appeared in the output of 'lspci'. Now that it is visible and usable again, I think it is indeed a 'loose card' issue - quite possible in older systems. If the wireless card is in a user-accessible area (usually located under a removable panel at the bottom), I suggest to -

[indent]- carefully remove antenna connectors (they are usually two pull/push type socket/plugs; remember which one connects to which side)
- unscrew the screw holding it
- pull the card out of its slot
- wipe clean its contacts with a soft and clean cloth (may apply mild cleaning agent like nail-polish-remover if really needed, but certainly not strong ones like petrol)
- blow away dust from the slot where it sits, as well as from the card circuitory (in fact it would be good to blow away dust from the entire system using a pressure blower (compressed-air-can)).
- re-seat the card
- carefully fix it in position again with the screw (tighten it only as much as can be done gently with your thumb and index finger)
- very carefully connect the antenna connectors again to their respective sides.[/indent]

Assuming the occasional absence of wifi is indeed due to a loose card, the above should solve it for another few years.

Can't say much about the Ethernet port except taking the machine to a qualified hardware service person.
