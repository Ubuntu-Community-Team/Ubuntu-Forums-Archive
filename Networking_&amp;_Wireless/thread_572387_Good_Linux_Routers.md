### Post by Lambert on 2007-10-10
> **xGutsAndGloryx said:**
> so my wireless card would have to support n range to get a n range router.  Basic most router support the linux OS.

No you're wireless card would not need to support N. The router would need to be compatabile with what ever your card is. Most routers with pre-n right now also support 802.11g, not sure about 802.11b but some will if not all/most.

I don't believe you really need to worry about router compatibility to linux. Just a router with good reviews and has solid/stable performance. I don't believe anything pre-n has that yet as they were rushed to market and the 802.11n spec is not finished yet. I have a pre-n router that runs stable in 802.11g mode only, running with 802.11n in mix mode or by itself shows problems.

Like said previously, find a good 802.11g router and you should be good. Netgear rangemax has good reviews, the linksys mentioned by kevdog is good. I've used d-link and never had problems but I have read bad reviews on some of their products.

And 802.11n just took its first step towards support in linux in the 2.6.22 kernel but it's not workable, just the framework being set up. Not sure if there are cards with specific drivers like intels cards that might work. Most won't but intel has been opensource friendly with their drivers.
