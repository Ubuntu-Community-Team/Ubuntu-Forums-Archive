### Post by sharpycore on 2008-02-19
So, like all the other Broadcom 43xx users on this forum, I've spent hours trying to negotiate with my wireless chipset. I got it to work, but suffered kernel panics all the time, and couldn't use wireless network auditing tools. I had enough, and the solution seemed to just be to chuck it away. So for £12 I found an Intel 2915 ABG on Ebay, took my Broadcom chip out, slotted my Intel one in, closed up the machine and turned it on.
And It Just Works. Think of all the collective hours we've spent trying to get these crappy chips to work and a solution is so cheap. I'm running 64 bit Gutsy on an AMD processor in case anyone is wondering about compatibility.
I mostly just wanted to post to confirm that the card works with my setup, for anyone else who might be interested in making the switch. Best 12 pounds I've spent in a long time.
