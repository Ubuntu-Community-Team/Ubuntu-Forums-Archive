### Post by jml on 2006-05-24
Search this forum for the hardware compatibility Wiki posted here.  It may be of help.  A generic answer is that Ubuntu is most compatible with cards that use the Atheros Chip set.  The challenge is finding out which card manufacturers use it.  As of now, there are two cards that I use.  One is the NetGear WG511T card and the other is the Aironet 802.11 a/b/g card.  These have worked well for me.  One suggestion, since manufacturers are notorious for changing the chipsets without notifying consumers, buy a card from a store that has a reasonable return policy.  If it does not work then you can return it and get your money back.  Although the Aironet card is more expensive, the manufacturer, (Cisco) is a very Linux friendly company offering native Linux drivers etc.  One other thing to consider.  Ubuntu 5.10 does not support WPA encryption out of the box, version 6.06 may.  So if you need WPA encryption your life will become a bit more complicated.  Hope this helps.
