### Post by carr011 on 2008-04-08
I finally solved the problem I had with Gutsy not "seeing" my Intel 3945ABG wireless card, even though the card recognized available networks and displayed signal strength. 

Here's a quick history ..... Started w/ Fiesty and a Broadcom card. Used an available script from the forum to allow Fiesty to use the card. Everything fine, although slow performance (56k dial up speeds). Upgraded to Gutsy. No wireless. Fought w/ Broadcom until I gave up. Purchased Intel 3945ABG as advised from many members because of plug and play capability. Installed and had excellent performance immediately. Months later, had to do fresh install of Gutsy (not wireless related) and afterwards Gutsy wouldn't "see" the Intel card. Had signal from available networks, but no connectivity. Tried many of the solutions proposed on forum - no help. So, I removed the card, re-installed Gutsy, shutdown, installed card, re-booted, and wireless works. Because I'm a noob and messed up the install, I had to re-install Gutsy again shortly thereafter and again Gutsy wouldn't see the card. Did the same process of removing card, re-installing Gutsy, installing card, and it worked again.

That was last week and I haven't had an issue with wireless since.I'm back to taking it for granted. I know it is not a technical fix, but it worked for me on a Gateway MX6214 laptop.

.....and, while I'm here, thanks to everyone out there who makes the time to help others remain microsoft-free. I certainly appreciate it!
