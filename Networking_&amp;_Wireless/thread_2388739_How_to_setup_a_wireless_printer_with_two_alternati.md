### Post by SeijiSensei on 2018-04-07
If your printer has an Ethernet port and is located near to your router, I recommend using a cable to connect them.  Connect the printer directly to one of the router's "LAN" ports with an Ethernet cable, then assign the printer a static IP address.  It should be visible from all your devices, wired and wireless.  My Brother supports wireless printing, but I much prefer this arrangement.
