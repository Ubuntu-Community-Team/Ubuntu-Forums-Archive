### Post by Eiríkr on 2008-03-22
From what I've read, adding a wireless NIC to your desktop should allow you to share your internet connection with your laptop, but I've never done it myself.  I *do* have a wireless / wired router here, sharing my one DSL connection with two wired boxes, a wired network printer, and two wireless laptops.  Wireless routers can be as cheap as ~$30 - ~$70, which might be on a par with any wireless NIC, and allow for more scalability -- since they're dedicated appliances, you can add more connections without having to worry too much about configuration or bogging things down.

### Post by ugm6hr on 2008-03-22
I agree - a wireless router is a better option, although marginally more expensive.

It will allow / provide the following:

1. Internet access on your laptop when the desktop is turned off.
2. Additional security (routers generally all have a built-in firewall).
