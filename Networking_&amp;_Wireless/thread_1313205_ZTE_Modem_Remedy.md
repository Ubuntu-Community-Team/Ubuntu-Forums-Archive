### Post by karlo on 2009-11-03
I haven't install anything on my Ubuntu 9.10 OS yet, like the wvdial or ZTE Modem M6x .deb files ..

Anyway, this usually works. I leave the usb plugged in to my computer whenever I boot up to Ubuntu. It also works after ubuntu finished booting up, then that's the time you'll insert the cd.

Now, usually after booting up you'll see the ZTE Modem Icon on your Desktop. What I did is that, I Eject it first, then go to the Networks Manager > Mobile Broadband, then I add a new connection, and wait for the modem to be detected.  If you have an existing connection, you may just click on the Cancel button.
