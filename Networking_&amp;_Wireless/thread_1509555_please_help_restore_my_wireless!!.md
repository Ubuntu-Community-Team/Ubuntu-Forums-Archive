### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-06-14
I am new to this myself. Ubuntu should automatically detect any nearby wireless transmitters or routers.  A left click on the wireless icon will bring up a list of any detected. Is your network listed? Click on it and enter the password as requested. I have found that Ubuntu is slow to reject the wrong password. It does not tell you that the password is wrong. It just asks for it again. Like me you may end up setting your log-in password when you need some other one. For my router it is the wireless key, also called WPA-PSK key.
