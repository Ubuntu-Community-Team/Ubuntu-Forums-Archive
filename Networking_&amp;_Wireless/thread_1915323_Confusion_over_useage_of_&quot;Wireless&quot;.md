### Post by jcobban on 2012-01-26
The term wireless seems to be used in the documentation to cover two very different technologies:
[LIST]
[*]802.11 wi-fi as used by home networking and wi-fi hotspots such as in many coffee shops.
[*]Cellular network connectivity, typically described as 3G or in high density locations 4G aka LTE.
[/LIST]
I am having difficulty determining exactly what I have to do to connect my lap-top to the latter form of networking, but most of the hits I get in the documentation refer to the former, which works fine right out of the box using the on-board support.

My cell-phone provider offers two USB sticks:  Sierra Wireless U313 and Novatel Wireless U679.  Neither of them appear in the Ubuntu Wi-Fi wiki, but then neither of them has anything to do with Wi-Fi.

### Post by lisati on 2012-01-26
You are right, cellular connectivity has [s]little[/s] nothing to do with WiFi. The USB dongles used for hooking up via a mobile phone service are, in effect, a mobile phone that has been configured as a modem and that is often missing the bits needed to make a regular phone call.
