### Post by UrBob on 2010-03-01
Yes.
 
I have seen these kind of figures with the router and the wireless adapter next to each other. It all depends on a load of stuff.
 
Some examples:
PC between wireless aerial on PC and router.
Someone standing between the PC/Laptop and the Router.
The humidity of the air. (Higher humidity attenuates the signal quicker).
The signal drops 80% when the door is closed. (It was a steel reinforced door and the walls were also metal reinforced. Not a common situation).
 
In short, wireless can be weird.
 
If I am setting up a wireless network for someone, I will not make any promises about the effectiveness of the network once the computer and the router are in separate rooms. I do a survey with a wireless laptop to see what the signals are like if it matters.
 
Having said all that, it will probably work if there is nothing strange about the structure of the house and the signal doesn't have to go through too many walls.
