### Post by Fransd on 2009-01-25
Hi, I have had the same problem with various computers.
First I had a Dell 1420 laptop that did not even connect after I had called Dell Help several times.
Then I found a Netgear WG111v3 USB dongle.
Plugged this in and was connected instantly.
A little later I converted the Laptop from Windows to Ubuntu and had the same problem, the built in Wi-Fi card did not work in spite of all suggestions from forums.
Stuck the Netgear dongle in a USB slot and problem solved.
Next I purchased a Aspire One from Acer, which came with Windows loaded.
I converted that to Ubuntu and could not get the built in Wi-Fi card to work.
But I used the Netgear dongle again and I was online before I could say try this baby.
I now have two USB dongles from Netgear and have no problems at all.
Try it, you will be surprised.
I use my Dell at home and since I spent a lot of time traveling, carry my little Aspire for roadside E-mail, Newspaper reading, , Hotel reservations and other tourist information.
I just love Ubuntu.
