### Post by Max Luebbe on 2006-07-09
For one, it helps to not run Windows.
Ubuntu is pretty secure by default in terms of somebody gaining access to your machine.

I'd install a firewall such as Shorewall to be extra safe if you're feeling paranoid.

The biggest risk in places with free wireless is probably people with packet sniffers, so as long you're doing casual surfing, and not things like online banking, you'll be fine.

### Post by tturrisi on 2006-07-09
There is no such thing as safe secure wireless computing.  ALL data packets can be captured by another using Kismet and you'll never ever know that your transmissions have been intercepted.  The only thing you can do is to use good common sense practices and use good judgement.  For example, ftp & pop mail send usernames & passwords in plain text, as do some online logins. So use your judgement when & where to use ftp & email. But then again, all wired traffic is just as vulnerable to interception, as are cell phones and phone lines.
