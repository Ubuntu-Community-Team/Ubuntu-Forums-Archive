### Post by TheFu on 2013-09-16
You didn't say what speeds you were planning to have ... that might change the answer.  

CAT5e handles GigE copper connections (1000base-tx) and lower (10/100base-tx) very well, even over distances that the specification says ... I think that is 100m, but don't quote me - look it up.  CAT6 is very much overkill for 1000base-tx networks.  If you are looking at [10G-base-tx networking,]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/10_Gigabit_Ethernet") then you need to read up on that spec and get the appropriate cables for it.  I think that is **Cat6a** (not plain Cat6).  I've not worked with anything faster than GigE on copper, so didn't needed to know.  I think [100G networking]("https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/100_Gigabit_Ethernet") is coming.  40G is already here.

I have both Cat5e and Cat6 here for my GigE networks.  Can't tell the difference in speed or reliability. The Cat6 is only used for short runs and the Cat5e is around 10m in length and for ad hoc long runs for LAN parties, etc..  If we look at the specifications for 1000base-tx, Cat5e exceeds them significantly.  Cat6 is so far beyond what is needed for that sort of network .... it just isn't worth it unless the thicker cable "helps" AND the price is the same, as @Keven_Arnold says.

### Post by Kevin_Arnold on 2013-09-16
All you need is 5e, it is just as reliable and will run just as fast as 6 on a gigabit network. On the other hand, if you can get Cat 6 for similar prices, you might as well use it.
