### Post by Bucky Ball on 2014-06-08
Welcome. Off-topic, but this is going to come up so may as well be now. 13.04 is no longer supported. No updates, including security updates. Probably best you're not online anyway! I advise a current, supported LTS release, 12.04 LTS or 14.04 LTS. 

I've never had a problem with Broadcom, especially since b43 took the hassle out of them. Realtek/Ralink are usually fine, too. Main thing is: if it came out yesterday, there is a good chance it won't be supported. Choose something with a chip that's been around awhile.

You will find that a lot of cards 'just work' as the drivers are part of the kernel, no need to fiddle about installing anything. If not in the kernel, update and check 'Additional Drivers' or 'Hardware Drivers', depending on the release.
