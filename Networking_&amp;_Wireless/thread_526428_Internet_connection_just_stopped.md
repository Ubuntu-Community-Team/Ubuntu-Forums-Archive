### Post by noob12 on 2007-08-15
Do you dual-boot Windows on this box?   Did you recently update the Windows side driver?  People have reported that rolling back the Windows driver or shutting down completely AND removing power (unplugging the computer) has resolved a similar issue.

I'm not sure this is the same problem though.

(EDIT: Why unplug? Because if plugged in, many ethernet cards remain in a low-power-state to support wake-on-lan).
