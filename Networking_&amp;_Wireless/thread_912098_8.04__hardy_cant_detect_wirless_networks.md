### Post by Bucky Ball on 2008-09-06
You could copy and paste this in a terminal and see if it does you any good:

**sudo iwlist wlan0 scan**

[you need to replace **wlan0** with** eth0** for wired connection or the name of your connection if it is something other than either of these]

If no good, try:[B] 

sudo dhclient -r[/B]

That releases your dhcp address. Then

[B]sudo dhclient

[/B]That renews it again from your router. If that didn't work try:[B][FONT=Verdana]

sudo /[/FONT]etc/init.d/network restart

[/B]Any luck?
