### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-07-30
I do not understand what you are trying to do. Why do you need to access the router? To put into it the Username and password or your Internet Service Provider. This is what I had to do when I obtained a broadband wireless router recently. The router then automatically connects to the ISP whenever it is switched on. To connect to the router using wireless I then needed to edit the wireless connections using network manager. I needed to enter the network security key, also called WPA-PSK key for that network. Your system may be detecting other wireless networks. It is 10 characters long . The letters in it may be capitalised. Without this the computer cannot connect to the router (network) and the computer cannot access the internet. Is this router connected to a telephone line? Are you allowed to plug your computer into it? Is it a modem? Or just a router?
