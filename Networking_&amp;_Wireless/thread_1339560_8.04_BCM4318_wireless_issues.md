Hi huviduc,

I'm not sure whether the 8.04 kernels are too old to use the "STA" proprietary Broadcom driver (which you can download directly from Broadcom), but it is supposed to work for the 4311, 4312, 4321 (or 4321AG/"4328" like mine), and 4322 Broadcom WiFi interfaces.

Take a look at this thread, and maybe give the "STA" driver a go- it was a really quick, easy fix on the Broadcom 4311's under the 9.04 kernels (and I seem to recall the ndiswrapper method working with either "bcmwl5.inf" or "bcmwl6.inf" with my 4321AG under the 8.x kernels, but it's been a while.  Be sure to get the correct 32- or 64-bit Windows drivers if you end up doing that):

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1336676](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1336676)

Edit:  Here are some older threads about the 4318 specifically (and the 8.x ubuntu kernels):

### Post by northd_tech on 2009-12-04
> ndiswrapper -l
bcmwl5 : driver installed
    device (14E4:4318) present (alternate driver: ssb)Have you tried blacklisting that "ssb" alternate driver- I remember the bcmwl[5,6] Windows driver having some issues with the older "b43" driver that required blacklisting.  My 4321AG will use either of the bcmwl5.inf or bcmwl6.inf successfully for over a year (with the "6.inf" being the 802.11n version as I understand it).  The ndiswrapper threads should talk about blacklisting (and there was a recent 4311 thread about it in the Networking & Wireless forum too).  The "b43" thread is here:

[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1266620](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=1266620)

You might also be having some WPA "access key" issues.  Are you sure that you've got wireless-tools installed?  There are links and info about WPA here:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WiFiHowTo](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WiFiHowTo)

If 8.04 is indeed Hardy Heron (from what I remember), here is the link to wireless-tools (but there are links to the other versions too at the upper right page):

[http://packages.ubuntu.com/hardy/wireless-tools](http://packages.ubuntu.com/hardy/wireless-tools)
