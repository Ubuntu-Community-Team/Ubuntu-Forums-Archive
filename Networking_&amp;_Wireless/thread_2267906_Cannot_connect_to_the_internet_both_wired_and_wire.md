### Post by TheFu on 2015-03-05
Seems your setup is odd - this stuff generally works. If the drivers are working, this is not a hard thing to fix, but you won't be point-n-clicking.  90% of the power of a Linux system is from the shell. If you want to avoid the shell completely, I can't help.

Linux is a different OS than Windows or OSX.  It behaves differently and solutions are very different for issues.  It is also extremely flexible - beyond what you've seen with other OSes before.  That is fantastic AND terrible.

For most people these days, they can wade into Linux by using point-n-click for the first 6 months before they decide to get more power and use the command line and automate 50 things. 

So - if you are willing to use the CLI to troubleshoot this: [http://blog.jdpfu.com/2013/03/01/linux-troubleshooting-101-networking](http://blog.jdpfu.com/2013/03/01/linux-troubleshooting-101-networking) Post the commands and results back here so we can help.
