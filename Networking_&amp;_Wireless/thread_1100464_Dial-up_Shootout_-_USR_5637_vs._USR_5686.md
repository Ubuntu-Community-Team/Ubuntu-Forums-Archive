### Post by Bartender on 2009-03-19
Has anyone done any comparisons between the new USR 5637 and the good old 5686's?  The 5637 seems like a wonderful little gadget, and I'm tempted to buy one.

I have some USR 5686's.  D and E models.  I can go direct to a serial port if available, or use a serial-to-USB adapter cable.  The modem shows up at /dev/tty0 on the first serial port, or /dev/ttyACM0 with the adapter cable.

I've been testing our dial-up connection on a Dell 470 workstation that's running dual-boot (XP & Ub 8.10) and a USR 5686D.  According to various online speed tests, the connection runs around 37 kbps in XP but only 28 to 30 kbps in Ubuntu.  That seems about right from seat-of-the pants observation.  Since all hardware is the same, it seems to me the difference is all in how Linux handles dial-up, or perhaps in the way the ISP (Fry's.com) responds to requests from a Linux PC?

If anybody has found the 5637 to be faster than the 5686 I might get one.
