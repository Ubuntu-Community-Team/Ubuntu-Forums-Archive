### Post by thefix0r on 2011-01-06
I am an experienced commercial 802.11 installer, and nefarious wifi pirate,   I havent paid for internet in 5 years.

First off, boosters are expensive and offer very little in return, a real high powered wireless card is a superior solution when faced with a decision like that.

In your case, I would simply purchase a ddwrt/openwrt capable router for under 70 bucks, load the firmware on it, and make a wireless client or repeater that connects to your existing ap. nice and easy.

### Post by perspectoff on 2011-01-06
I agree with the repeater suggestion, although I think the commercial variants of a repeater are now called range extenders or range expanders.

Cisco-Linksys has a range expander for about $90 (WRE54G) -- that's the kind of thing you want.

Sure, it's fun to tweak with custom firmware solutions like ddwrt/openwrt, but life is short and the router is the last thing I want to spend my time tinkering with. Their lifespan is usually too short.
