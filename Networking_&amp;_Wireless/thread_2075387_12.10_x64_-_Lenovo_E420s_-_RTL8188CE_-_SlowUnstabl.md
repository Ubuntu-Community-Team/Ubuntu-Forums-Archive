One thought is that if you get the driver from Realtek to install without error, you may need to blacklist the 'built-in' module in order for the driver from Realtek to drive the adapter.  I had to do this on Linux Mint 13.  In my case the 'built-in' driver was rtl8188cu and the driver from Realtek was 8192cu, no rtl at the beginning.
