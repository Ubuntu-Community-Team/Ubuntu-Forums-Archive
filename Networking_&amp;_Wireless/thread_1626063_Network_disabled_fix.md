### Post by Hwalker1 on 2010-11-19
I struggled for a week with no Internet connection on Ubuntu but with no problem connecting with windows on the same laptop.

Cure -** right click** on the icon at the top of the screen next to the battery icon which apparently is the network manager icon - something you will not find in the help files off line.There may not be a tick next to Enable Networking or Enable Wireless . Left click until one appears. Then wait for a while and your network will be found. I never think of right clicking on anything, which is why I only found out again by accident.

While you are at it, click on the connection information and copy and paste the information into a note and save it. Could be useful later. 

Download all information that you can find on networking from the net, as it will not be available if you get off line by accident.

Get a book on Ubuntu so that you know at least some of the unpronounceable words that you have to type into a terminal (whats that?) to get it to do things.

Have fun.
