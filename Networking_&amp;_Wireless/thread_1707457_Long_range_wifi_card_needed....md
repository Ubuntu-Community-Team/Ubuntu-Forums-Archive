### Post by crispy_420 on 2011-03-16
First option that I could think of is why replace the router when you can get an access point. Find one that supports replacement antennas or even take a Linksys 802.11n and flash with DD-WRT. Then get a nice directional antenna or build your own (wokfi) You could even have another DD-WRT on the other end acting as a wireless bridge. So two Linksys 802.11n routers, one configured as an access point and the other as a wireless bridge, and some decent directional antennas may do the job. Then the ISP doesn't have to see what has been done in your network.

This along the lines of what you were thinking? Not a very Linux specific answer either.
