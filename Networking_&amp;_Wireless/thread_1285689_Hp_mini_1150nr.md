### Post by t0mppa on 2009-10-08
As a rule of thumb, the easiest way to find drivers and/or software for certain hardware on Linux is first opening up a terminal and then running **lspci** or **lsusb**, depending on whether the device is internal (pci) or external (usb). Once you recognize the device from the listing, then just ask Google and you'll usually find plenty of tutorials/help for installing said software, since you usually aren't the first person asking the question.

This is not to say that you can't ask help on the forums, but we don't know any better than you what hardware you're talking about, when all you give is the model of your computer, so  at the very least, more information is required. And it's generally easier for you than someone who doesn't have said computer to find it, since the commands posted earlier tend to give a lot more specific information than say Verizons website.
