### Post by ajgreeny on 2012-09-01
Your nameserver 127.0.0.1 does not look normal to me.

As you are on a network including other computers, I presume you are connected by a router to the network.  Your nameserver would then normally be the IP of the router, most often 192.168.#.# (usually 0.1 or 1.1 at the end).

Unfortunately I have no idea what has gone wrong with your setup, but this may give you some idea of what to search for as a solution.

You could try simply changing to Google's public DNS, instructions at [https://developers.google.com/speed/public-dns/docs/using](https://developers.google.com/speed/public-dns/docs/using) to see if that helps.
