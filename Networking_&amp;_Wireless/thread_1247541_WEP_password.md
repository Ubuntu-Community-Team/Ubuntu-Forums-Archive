First, I don't use KDE but a WEP password is the type of encoding the router you are trying to connect to is expecting. There are various encoding schemes used on routers one of which is WEP. Sometime in the past you would have had to setup your other systems equivalently.  In Gome it will ask for that too unless you checked make available to all users. Don't know about KDE.

There is one other possibility, are you sure you are trying to connect with your router and not someone elses (another signal) in the area.  Perhaps you are running unencrypted in yours and are attempting to connect to a neighbors, accidentally.
