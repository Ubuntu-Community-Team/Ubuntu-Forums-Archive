### Post by jonobr on 2011-10-28
Ok Im just kidding.

Im thinking this would be a difficult one.

I used to support modems that had inactivity timers and disconnections timers and they were awful to support.
Mostly because it was an arugment between our support and the ISP of what should count as inactivity and what shouldnt

Also, your idea of connection time may differ from your isps.
They may want to start the timer on you you for one thing you may disagree and your stats may be differnet

I.e. if you have IM running - you fire up your computer and it opens automatically,
does your time start then even though your not using a browser and a couple of packets were sent by the computer?
If your idle but your email application sends a few packets to synch with an email server somewhere and finds nothing does that count?

When you do send a packet, how is that time counted?
Are there limits to when time is counted and when it isnt.

All in all I think your going to have a hard time getting an answer on this.
I think its really up to the carrier to provide you information of time usage and notification when your time is nearly up.

I did a few prepaid data applications where this was done, but as I understand it now, the carrier is not mandated to notify you which is crumby.
Sorry there is no real answer from me, but again I dont think there is an easy answer for this
