### Post by kamiller42 on 2012-11-19
My phone is rooted. BusyBox and Android Wifi Tether installed on my Samsung Note 2. But wifi tethering does not work in Ubuntu 12.04 64-bit and probably other versions. Works fine with Windows and other Android devices.

I am not alone with this problem. A Google search returns many people unable to make the two play well together. Even the project's FAQ says:
"**My computer is having trouble connecting! What can I do?** 
Check  whether your operating system and wireless card support ad-hoc  connections. Ubuntu is known to have poor support for these types of  connections, for instance."
[http://code.google.com/p/android-wifi-tether/wiki/FAQ](http://code.google.com/p/android-wifi-tether/wiki/FAQ)



My Dell XPS 17 connects to the phone, but nothing more. No domain name resolution. No browsing by IP.


Others have suggested USB tethering. The problem is either the solution doesn't work, too cumbersome to setup (EasyTether) or performs poorly (ClockwordMod Tether). And, it only works for the device connected to the USB, i.e. the PC.


So, has anyone been able to get any Android wifi tether app to work with Ubuntu using a rooted stock based ROM (No AOSP ROM)? If so, which and is it compatible with JellyBean?
