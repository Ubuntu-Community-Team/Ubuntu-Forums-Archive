### Post by davidm49 on 2012-05-01
> **imaginarythomas said:**
> I think this may have done it!

Not clear what other actions you had previously taken, such as perhaps editing /etc/NetworkManager/NetworkManager.conf .

If you have done nothing more than editing /etc/resolv.conf, you will probably find that your edit is overwritten when you reboot, once again setting 127.0.0.1 as the only nameserver. (That is what happens in Lubuntu 12.04. The comment in resolv.conf suggests that it will be overwritten in Ubuntu 12.04 as well, though I haven't installed that yet and could be wrong.)

To make a permanent change to /etc/resolv.conf, write the same nameserver lines in /etc/resolvconf/resolv.conf.d/base . (Dots are written and omitted correctly.) This file exists by default, but it is blank until you write something to it.

On reboot, the lines in /etc/resolvconf/resolv.conf.d/base will be written to /etc/resolv.conf .

CORRECTION: /etc/resolv.conf still reverts to 127.0.0.1 as the only name server after reboot, but DNS is working OK nevertheless. (Maybe 12.04 just needs a bit of internet use before the local resolver starts working properly? Any other suggestions?)
