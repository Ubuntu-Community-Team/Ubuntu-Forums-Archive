### Post by gentlerain on 2007-08-23
I use the basic Westell 327W DSL modem that is shipped with a Verizon DSL account.  One computer is connected to it.  I do not need wireless so I disabled it from within the modem's interface.  The red wireless light went off.

I have a Motorola Minitor II pager used in the fire service.  It receives radio signals at the frequency 46.50 MHz.  When I place the pager within approximately 5-10 feet of the modem, static is heard coming from the pager (the signal-static type, not quite the same as when you hold down the Squelch button).  I also noticed this on someone else's computer which uses the Westell 327W.  I know some type of signal is being emitted, but not sure what.  These types of pagers also have the same reaction when placed near fluorescent lighting (as fluorescent lights do emit signals).  Note that when I power-up the modem, the static will not come from the pager until the moment the Ethernet light comes on (the line-in from my computer).

I'm just curious if anyone knows what exactly this signal is that is emitted from the Westell 327W when the wireless is turned off?  Is the wireless hardware within the modem still powered-up even though the wireless was disabled on the software side?  Could this be a potential security risk?

Thanks, Mike.

### Post by str8line on 2007-08-24
It is the interference from the high frequency signal of your dsl service.  That is why installing the filters that came with the DSL kit on every analog device (phone, fax, satellite and alarm system as examples) is so important.  General rule for placing a high frequency device close to a modem is 6 feet.
