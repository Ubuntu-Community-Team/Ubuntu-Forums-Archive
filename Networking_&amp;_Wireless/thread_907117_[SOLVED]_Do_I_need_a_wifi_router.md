### Post by nicedude on 2008-09-01
Just buy a $40 Netgear or Linksys router from newegg.com and then you can easily hook that up to your DSL and then just plug desktops in via Ethernet cables or use portable devices etc via wifi connection. A router is the single best investment you could make to secure and add functions to a home network. Just be sure to use WPA encryption for your wifi as WEP can be cracked in very short ammount of time ( like 5 minutes ).


Hope that helps and if when you get your router you need any help or advise on how to configure it then PM me in these forums and I will help you out as I have setup a ton of home routers :-)

### Post by smurphy_it on 2008-09-01
Yes you can get a purchase an inexpensive router.  You can even get something like a linksys and then flash the router with a linux firmware (opens up tons of settings on the touer).  Just checkout the [dd-wrt]("http://www.dd-wrt.com") web page.

There are many different variants / projects on all of this.

As I'm getting a bit off topic here, yes get a wireless router.

If you were going to get a wifi card for you PC, I'd go Intel.  They seem to have the best support out of the box.  Which I can't say about broadcom chipsets.

### Post by AVT- on 2008-09-01
After setting one up myself, I don't recommend setting up a WAP in Linux.

Long story short, you can't do it with most chipsets, and with the ones you can, it's a PITA, doesn't always work, and requires you to spend a long time configuring it.
