### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-10-02
I think most Dell machines have Broadcom wireless cards, all of which will work on Ubuntu, but some of which may require some hacking to get working well.  Some high-end Dells may also have Intel cards, which should "just work."  Unless you can get Dell to tell you the exact chipset model of the wireless card beforehand, it's hard to say with certainty whether you'll have trouble getting your wireless working, but you can be confident that there will be a way to get it working regardless of which chip it has as long as you don't mind doing a little work.

Of course, if you buy a model with Ubuntu preloaded, the wireless should work great out-of-the-box.
