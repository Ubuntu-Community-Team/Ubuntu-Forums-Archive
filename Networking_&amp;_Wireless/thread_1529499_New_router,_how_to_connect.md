### Post by raymondvillain on 2010-07-12
Running 9.10 (64 bit) on a laptop.  Wireless worked flawlessly for some months, then my wireless router died.

Replaced the router, but now cannot access through wireless.  Ethernet connection (to the same router) works fine, and if I boot to Windows instead of Ubuntu, I can connect easily and the wireless works very well.

How to connect Ubuntu to the new wireless set up?  I tried administration>settings>network connections, chose wireless tab, and manually entered stuff (MAC address, IP address of router, WPA/WPA2 password, etc.) but still cannot get Ubuntu 9.10 to find the wireless.

Any suggestions will be greatly appreciated.

Solved!  Just add "notification area" to dashboard, and click on the signal strength icon.

### Post by raymondvillain on 2010-07-15
Well, LinuxHungry, I can't remember.

What I did was to connect to the router.  I did that by opening a browser and typing in the address of the router, which was 192.168.2.1 for me.

That gave me the router info, and I copied down the MAC address of the router and I think I used that.

But the main thing was to add a notification symbol to the "bar" across the top of the Ubuntu screen.  Then when the wireless was "visible", that icon allowed me to connect and all I had to provide was the password for the WPA/WPA2 network.

Hope this helps, sorry it took me so long to get back.  I marked the thread solved and I never thought anybody would ask another question.
