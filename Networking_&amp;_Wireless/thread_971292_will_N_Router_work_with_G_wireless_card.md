### Post by karlatsaic on 2008-11-05

I use a linksys wrt160N router with 2 laptops, one is G and the other is B. It works nicely, but of course runs at G and B speeds, respectively - neither of which is as fast as the router supports. I prefer to have a router I won't need to upgrade in the future, so I don't consider it extra bucks. Also figure in your time (which =$) to return the router. Main thing is to get a setup which works without sweat, a not-so-easy task as, when things go awry, it's not clear whether it's your wireless card, the router, your driver, or middleware such as Network Manager.
