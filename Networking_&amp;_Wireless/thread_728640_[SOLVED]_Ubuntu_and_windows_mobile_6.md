This was my original post:
> Is Ubuntu able to access a windows mobile 6 device?
By this I mean, as a removable drive, I don't give a stuff about activesync as I only use my phone as a phone/mp3 player and book reader. 
As long as I can access it like a removable drive that's fine as I can just copy the stuff over.
After a bit of research via a few google searches :D I came across the solution I needed!

If, like me, you don't really care for syncing your device, and just want to be able to copy files to and from it, here is the solution:
[http://www.modaco.com/content/Smartphone-Software-Freeware-Download-Discussion/238545/WM5torage/](http://www.modaco.com/content/Smartphone-Software-Freeware-Download-Discussion/238545/WM5torage/)

WMStorage will temporarily turn your device into a plain old USB removable drive. You can choose the SD card or even the device itself (use at own risk!) and copy/edit/create/delete files. This works with both Windows Mobile 5 and Windows Mobile 6 devices.

If you want to sync your device with contacts/mail/etc like you can on windows, well there are solutions out there, but I didn't bother looking as I don't need that :D

Remember, you can't sync contacts/mail/tasks  this way, but you can access the device like a removable drive, which, to be honest, is all I need.

Hope this is useful to someone else!
