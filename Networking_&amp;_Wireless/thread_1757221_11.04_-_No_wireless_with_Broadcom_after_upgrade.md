### Post by TheChaos0 on 2011-05-13
Ok I fixed it. Here's what I did. Since the popular solution didn't work I tried to install the other Broadcom drivers.

If you got 32bit then [this](http://ie.archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/restricted/b/bcmwl/bcmwl-kernel-source_5.60.48.36+bdcom-0ubuntu5_i386.deb)

Or if you got 64bit then [this](http://ie.archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/restricted/b/bcmwl/bcmwl-kernel-source_5.60.48.36+bdcom-0ubuntu5_amd64.deb)

Uninstall your previous driver with *sudo apt-get remove bcmwl-kernel-source* restart and you should be good to go.
