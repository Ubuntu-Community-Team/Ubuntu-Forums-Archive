### Post by TheFu on 2014-12-23
None, but I suppose it all depends on who you are trying to hide traffic from.

Many of these systems overstate the protection provided and the less expensive offerings are using VPN technology that is KNOWN to be non-secure. How much trust do companies like that deserve?

Even TOR is hard to get correct. A tiny mistake by the user and all the anonymity is lost.  Out of all the services, I'd trust TOR the most, but would only run it on a different PC with a spoofed MAC, on someone elses' network where my connection wasn't logged. All cookies (flash data/objects and other local storage objects) would be wiped before starting the connections too.

Security is hard. Deploying secure systems is VERY hard.
