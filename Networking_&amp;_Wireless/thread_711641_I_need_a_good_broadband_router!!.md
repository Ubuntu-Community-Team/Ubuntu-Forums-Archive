### Post by WiFi Ed on 2008-02-29
I've got a GigaFast EE400-RP "broadband" router (given to me by a friend for free) that ain't so "broadband-ish". It was adequate with my Roadrunner 6Mb/512kb cable internet. I got throughputs close to the advertised data rates (5.8Mb/485kb).

However, I just upgraded to Roadrunner's "Turbo" package (10Mb/1Mb) and the router chokes it to 5-6Mb download speeds. If I take the router out of the loop I get 9.8Mb download speeds, consistently.

The java-based speed test at [http://netspeed.stanford.edu/](http://netspeed.stanford.edu/) reports full duplex mode, with normal duplex operation and good cables, but lots of packet queueing.  Without the router packet queueing is minimal. These results are the same in Ubuntu or Vista, and the pc reports a 100Mb, full duplex connection to the router.

I'm pretty sure my router is the culprit so I'm looking for suggestions for a good broadband router with better throughput. What should I get? Wireless access is not necessary, but it might be nice to have in case **She Who Must Be Obeyed** lets me get a laptop in the near future.

Thanks in advance!

Ed
