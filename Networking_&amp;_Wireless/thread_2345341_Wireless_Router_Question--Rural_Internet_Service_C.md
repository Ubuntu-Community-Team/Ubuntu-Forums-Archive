### Post by geebeefan on 2016-12-03
We live in a rural area and have Internet service through Bertram Wireless. Essentially, they put a little dish on the roof and it points at a tower to bring the service into the home. The service we have has a maximum speed of 5 mbps. I just ran speed tests using Speakeasy.net and the download speed came in at 4.85 mbps and the upload speed was 1.66 mbps. We have a Belkin N600DB router in our basement where the service comes into the home and a wifi extender on the first floor. The house is roughly 2500 square feet with basement, 1st and 2nd floor. Like most families, we have oodles of electronics vying for bandwidth: several computers and tablets, 4 smart phones, wifi TV, etc. I realize the "bandwidth is the bandwidth", but I'm wondering if a newer router like the Netgear Nighthawk would provide better service and better coverage than the current Belkin product we have? There are several spots in the home that don't get great coverage and once you get outside the home (on our patio or porch, for example), it's rare you can pick up a wifi signal.

Thanks in advance for any assistance or recommendations you can offer.

### Post by Keith_Helms on 2016-12-03
It's certainly possible a newer better router would provide better range.  Why not start with adding a 2nd extender to your network and experiment with placing the 2 extenders?  You can download a free wifi signal strength app on one of your phones and roam around the house to see where the signal is best and worst.  That could help you figure out where to place the extenders.

Is is possible to run ethernet from the router in the basement to other places in the house?  If so, you could attach a wired access point to it.  That would give you increased range without the performance hit of using an extender.

### Post by ian-weisser on 2016-12-03
WiFi can be degraded by shielding and interference by building materials and electrical conduits and devices.

Ceramics, concrete, metal, and mirrors are most opaque to WiFi. Move the basement router to cast as few upstairs shadows as possible.

Lots of basement machinery may unintentionally create interference: Air conditioners, furnace fans, washer/dryers, anything with a big electric motor in it. You may notice a pattern - wifi suffers, for example, when the air conditioner is on. Move the router away from those...and away from your big metal breaker panel.

### Post by darren780 on 2016-12-06
If I understand you correctly, you pay for 5mbps, you get  4.85 sometimes, and you would like the additional 0.15mbps? I had subscribed to a wireless service while living in a rural area, I paid for 10mbps, while some of the time I achieved 0.03mbps due to interference, bad isp networking, etc.  I would not expect any better service by changing the router, at least in regards to the internet.

However, new routers use new technology to improve range while reducing interference between devices(mimo). I used an ac1200 router while on the farm and was able to surf my own internet while visiting my neighbour in his house, who was only separated by two large yards and a road.
