### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-07-22
My Asus P5B motherboard has wireless and wired LAN built in. Ubuntu recognises both of these. It detects nearby wireless networks including my new Thomson Gateway broadband router. And every thing works! From watching this forum I notice that so many other people have problems. I wonder why.

Two weeks ago I visited my sister at Eastbourne (town on England's south coast). I tried out a live CD on her Vista laptop. The wireless card was recognised. And all I needed was the WPA-PSK password, which I got off of a label stuck on the side of the router, and I was connected to the internet using my sister's Sky internet connection. 

Mind you, before switching my router on I read the motherboard manual, the Ubuntu help pages, & the Wiki pages. I also followed the instructions provided by my ISP and I made sure that I knew which username & password went where. Wrong passwords = no connection.

I think that some have problems because they buy a machine with everything pre-installed. They then install Ubuntu and wonder why there is no internet connection when they have not supplied the correct information to Network manager.

I have also noted that there are different names for things. For example, the WPA-PSK password is called the Wireless Key on a label provided by the ISP. This label lists a router password which is also called the router serial number and is in fact the password that gives access to the settings in the router using a web browser.

Yes, it can be confusing. I just thought that Ubuntu should get some credit among all the 'help me please' messages. :D
