### Post by Synflux on 2007-08-01
Do you mean a Linux router/pc or a stand-alone router such as Linksys etc? If you mean a standalone there are various ones which claim to work like that but all the cheap ones I've tried don't work. 

The mediocre priced draytek 3300 has two wan connections (both ethernet) which it can use for fail-over or combining the speeds, when I tried it I could only get it to work for fail-over. Other than that you'd be looking at a modular Cisco or similar.  I think the 1841 is the lowest cost option that let's you pick and choose which WAN cards you want (e.g. 1x cable and 1 x T1)
