### Post by joshua neff on 2009-10-04
I'm an idiot and have forgotten and lost the WPA password for my wireless router. My laptop has the password stored in Keyring, so accessing it is no problem. But connecting other devices (like gaming systems) to my wireless is a problem. I've checked the Keyring, but it has the password encrypted. Which is a good thing, but I need the actual password. Is there a way to recover the actual password?

---

### Post by stefangr1 on 2009-10-04
If you have secured your wireless network sufficiently, you can't just hack into it. Why don't you just login to your router to see what the password is? If administration from a wireless device has been disabled, you can always login with a regular ethernet cable.

---

### Post by Iowan on 2009-10-04
Most of the secure systems I've come across won't hand back an unencrypted password - even to the admin... although they will give the option to *change* the password.

---

### Post by aysiu on 2009-10-04
You need to log directly into your router with a wired ethernet cable.

If you have forgotten the login, you'll have to reset it (can usually be done by pressing the reset button on the router with a pen for about ten seconds).
