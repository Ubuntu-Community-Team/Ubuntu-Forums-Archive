Oh.  I suggest you try it.  The adapter is about $30 if you don't have it yet.  Just some tips:  make sure you only have that usb device plugged in when you first boot Kubuntu.  Then right-click the 'white' cord device logo which is 'KNetwork Manager.'  The scan for networks should be instantaneous and you should be able to see all possible connections (i.e. Wireless Networks).  Just click the one you are using (i.e. your ISP's and it will be the username ID you have entered for your router) and you will then be asked for your WEP/WPA info (whichever you use) and the passphrase (for e.g.).  

Anyway, there are probably several posts in the forums explaining this further.
