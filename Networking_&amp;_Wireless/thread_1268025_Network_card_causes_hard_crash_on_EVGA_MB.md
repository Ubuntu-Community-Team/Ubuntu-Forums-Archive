### Post by Ecclesia on 2009-09-16
Just wanted to post this in case others are having problems with this (or if anyone has a solution that would actually fix the issue with the motherboard)

I have this motherboard:

EVGA 112-CK-NF77-A1 LGA 775 NVIDIA nForce e-7150/630i HDMI Micro ATX

and have been having a hard time with hard crashes on and off (strangely) throughout the life of computer.  I seemed to have it narrowed down to flash and 3d effects combination (turning off compiz seemed to reduce the problem), and then recently it ramped up with crashes almost every time I watched an online flash video with high pixel density (higher than youtube - including Hulu, Revision3, and MLB broadcasts).  After playing with different live disks and seeing that the problem was no dependent on specific package versions, and seeing that rsync also had problems, I decided to install a dedicated network card to see if that might fix the problem.  Strangely, that fixed it.

Wanted to post this to let people know that if you have strange hard crashes with this motherboard it might be the integrated network card.

Also, has anyone else come across this and come up with a fix for the board?  It's likely mine is just defective (the first one of this I got needed to be RMAed, and this one has bad audio bleed through, which is unacceptable on a board designed to be used as a media center). Not likely I'll be buying from EVGA in the future.
