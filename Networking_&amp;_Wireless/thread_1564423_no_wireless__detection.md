### Post by grahammechanical on 2010-08-31
I have only just started using the computer today, so I have only just noticed your post. I will try to help but do not expect too much.

We need more information. In the top right corner of the screen you will see an icon for the Network manager. You can left click on it and right click on it. What information does it give you? When you left click do you see a list of available networks? Is your network among them?

If you left click you can edit connections. Select wireless. Do you see your network listed? Have you marked your network connection to connect automatically?

When you left click is there a tick alongside Enable networking and Enable wireless. If the tick is missing on either one then you will not be able to use a wireless connection. Click on them to enable them. Un-ticking and ticking either of these will cause network manager to search for any available networks. You may need to do this when making some adjustments. A re-boot may also be necessary to allow any changes to take effect.

I hope some of this helps. Also try using Ubuntu Help. By fixing problems you become a little bit of an expert and may be you can help others who have similar problems. This is what I am trying to do.
