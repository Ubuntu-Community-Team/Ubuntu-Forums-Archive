### Post by jimv on 2009-06-15
You don't have to do USB.  It's pretty simple to replace the internal mini-pci card.  I just did it with my laptop because I was tired of dealing with crummy drivers.  Luckily, these cards are cheap.  Here's the one I bought:

[http://cgi.ebay.com/ws/eBayISAPI.dll?ViewItem&ssPageName=STRK:MEWAX:IT&item=140324280919](http://cgi.ebay.com/ws/eBayISAPI.dll?ViewItem&ssPageName=STRK:MEWAX:IT&item=140324280919)

You should be able to find another one of the same model for the same price.

Here are the directions (specific to your system) for installing a new card:

[http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/ins6000/en/sm/minipci0.htm#wp1009645](http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/systems/ins6000/en/sm/minipci0.htm#wp1009645)
