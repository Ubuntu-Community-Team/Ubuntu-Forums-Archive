### Post by thedevnull on 2008-08-01
First things first.  Are your cable connected right?  It should go from the cable modem to the WAN port on the Linksys and then from ports 1-4 can be used for your wired computer.    

The details are all in the manual as well.  

[http://www.linksys.com/servlet/Satellite?c=L_Product_C2&childpagename=US%2FLayout&cid=1149562300349&pagename=Linksys%2FCommon%2FVisitorWrapper](http://www.linksys.com/servlet/Satellite?c=L_Product_C2&childpagename=US%2FLayout&cid=1149562300349&pagename=Linksys%2FCommon%2FVisitorWrapper)

After you verify that you are wired correctly you have to go to the Web Administration page as you logged into before and go to status.  It will tell you if you are actually getting a valid Internet IP address.  

Also have you checked if your ISP requires your MAC (nothing to do with Apple Inc) address, its a burned in network address?  You might be able to find these details and or help on your ISP's website.

Good luck

### Post by mb_webguy on 2008-08-01
Ditto thedevnull.  If after you've checked to make sure you're wired up correctly your router administration page shows you don't have an Internet connection, you'll might have to clone your computer's MAC address.  Fortunately, your router makes this extraordinarily easy, and it won't hurt anything to try it.

Just go to your router's administration page, and go to Setup->MAC Address Clone.  Click "Clone your PC's MAC", and restart your router.  If you still don't get an Internet connection, post back.
