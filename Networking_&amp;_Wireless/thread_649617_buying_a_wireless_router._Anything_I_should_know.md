### Post by odiseo77 on 2007-12-25
I have a wired/wireless router and haven't had any type of issues with drivers (I don't think drivers are needed at all). However, you probably want to check that the wireless encryption type(s) the router uses are supported by your laptop's wireless card, so you can connect to the router from your laptop via wireless (I guess you're going to connect your laptop to the router through WiFi?).

### Post by jrz on 2007-12-25
I've been quite happy with my Linksys WRT54GS V4 router which has 2 LAN wired connections permanently, and a friend often connects his Ubuntu notebook using the wireless connection.
If you go with a Linksys router, don't purchase a WRT54GS V5 (less memory), and also you might want to use some 3rd party firmware as the Linksys firmware leaves much to be desired. I'm using Tomato Firmware and quite pleased with it, and a simple install.
