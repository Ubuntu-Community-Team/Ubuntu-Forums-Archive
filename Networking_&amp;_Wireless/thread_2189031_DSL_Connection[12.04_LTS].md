### Post by varunendra on 2013-11-22
Perfect! It simply means either your router does not have DHCP enabled (to automatically assign an IP to your Ubuntu computer) or it is not functioning properly for some reason.

In either case, you can manually assign a permanent IP address to your Ubuntu computer via Network Manager.

To do that, click **Network Manager**'s icon to pull down its drop-down menu > click **Edit Connections..** > double-click your connection to **edit** it > under **IPv4 Settings**, set the **Method** to **Manual** > click **Add** > type in *IP address* **192.168.1.200** > press **Enter** twice *(the second one will accept the default subnet value "**24**")* > type in *Gateway* **192.168.1.1** > press **Enter** > fill in the *DNS* **8.8.8.8, 8.8.4.4** > click **Save**, then **Close**.

This will permanently assign the IP **192.168.1.[COLOR="#006400"]200[/COLOR]** to your Ubuntu computer with gateway **192.168.1.[COLOR="#006400"]1[/COLOR]** and Google's DNS servers. You may use a different IP address instead of 192.168.1.**200**, just make sure no other device on the network is using that IP.

However, if you have DHCP enabled on your router and you wish to get the IP via DHCP, let me know. We may try to dig deeper to find out why it is not getting the IP from it.
