### Post by alof8k on 2008-07-06
Yup, could very well be spyware/adware that you are not aware of or may be aware of.  For example, if you are running a anti-spyware or anti-virus program on the background (realtime scanning agent) then your windows OS is always being held down.  If you have spyware/adware that's not being detected, then perhaps something is making use of your bandwidth.  So if its not one thing, it another.

On linux you don't have that issue since spyware/virus is non-existent. Furthermore, the users of linux systems are much more aware of things bogging down your system (intentionally or not).  Think about it, on windows if something is slow, chances are you will fiddle with it a little bit until you get tired and then just decide to go buy an upgrade.  On the linux side of things, if something is bogging down your system, you'll spend hours trying to fix it and make sure the issue is gone.  Upgrading your hardware is the last option (regardless of the cost).
