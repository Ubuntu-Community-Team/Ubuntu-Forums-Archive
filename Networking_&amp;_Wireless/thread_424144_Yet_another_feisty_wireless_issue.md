### Post by sdide on 2007-04-27
it easy to see that your wlan0 is not getting an IP address assigned in your first listing, Now, that is the problem. Why is it not getting one...? 
Well ... 

To answer that - we need to know how you connect to your AP.

NetworkManager? wpa_supplicant? ...

How is your AP secured?  none, wep, wpa, wpa2 ... ?
This is up to you, and is defined on the AP. 

Is your AP acting as a DHCP server - that is are you dynamically assigned an IP address.
is your AP acting as your default gateway? 
If this is a normal default household network, we can safely assume yes on both these questions.

Lets say all is normal and and you have successfully associated with the AP. 

Check that with 

~# iwconfig wlan0
 
if the first line is;
unassociated  ESSID:"<something - maybe nothing>"

then you are not associated, and we need to solve this problem first.
