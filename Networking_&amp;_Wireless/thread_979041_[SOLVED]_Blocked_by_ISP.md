I remember 20-something years ago discovering a computer tape (yes, a tape!) in the tape library of the company I worked for. It was what might be called a "hacker's delight" - many programs and code snippets that illustrated how to do many different things. The trouble was that it was one of those collections of code that the average user wasn't supposed to know about - probably because of the potential for doing something "dangerous" to the system or figuring out a way round security; the company processed a lot of confidential data for other companies and was understandably nervous about staff making the "wrong" use of information entrusted to its care. Although the boss could be impressed with me figuring out how to do some seemingly cleve things after studying the code, use of the tape was frowned upon.

Good luck tracking down why there might be a block on your access and getting things put right.
