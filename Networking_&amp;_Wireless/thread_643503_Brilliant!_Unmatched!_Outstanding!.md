### Post by dmwit on 2007-12-17
So now that I'm on winter break, I decided to have a look at installing the great Gutsy Gibbon.  Of course my first test on the LiveCD was to get wireless working (as usual), and can I just say: "Wow!"  For the first time on any Ubuntu version, I was able to get wireless working completely from the GUI.  Great work!

A little more background: I have a Broadcom chipset which, for some reason, doesn't seem to play well with the usual bcm43xx module/firmware cutter deal.  I've fought and fought with it and never succeeded.  As Ubuntu's GUI always used to assume that bcm43xx would successfully drive any Broadcom wireless card, I never was able to get it to work.  *This* time, though, the GUI offered me a big fat "ndiswrapper" option, which I was thrilled to see.  The fact that it's smart enough to unload the bcm43xx kernel module just tickles me in all the right spots.  The moment it disappeared from my "lsmod" watch was the moment I started muttering the subject line out loud...

:KS

Great work, and thanks a lot!
