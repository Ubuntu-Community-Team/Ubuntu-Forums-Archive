### Post by AdHavoc on 2007-05-05
Okay, well, about a week ago after having used Feisty Fawn on two of my laptops, I decided to put it on my main desktop computer.  Its reliability and ease of use was extraordinary, and I wanted that experience on my home computer.  However, that was most certainly not the case.  

First, two of the .iso's I burnt and checked failed to initialize correctly, and only when I installed using the Alternate .iso did it install correctly.  However, it again froze before initializing, so I was forced to use an Official Dapper Drake CD I had and upgrade it to Edgy Eft and then to Feisty Fawn.  When that happened, three kernels appeared in GRUB: one was the *.*.20 kernel, one was the *.*.17 kernel, and one was the *.*.15 kernel.  As .20 was the most recent kernel, I decided to boot from that.  Of course, it froze again.  It worked fine when I booted from the .17 kernel, except my ATi video card barely pushed along.  

I thought I would settle with this, and I did for about a day or two, except then one day when I was rebooting, it would not boot at all.  The load bar would finish, but then the screen would go black and the cursor would stay on the "loading" sign.  I had to boot from the .15 kernel, and then when I did that, not only was my video card unstable, my computer would not connect to my home network!  I chose manual configuration on the icon, and put in my network name (linksys), along with no WEP key, as that is what I have it set as, and then chose DHCP configuration.  I clicked okay and yet I still could not connect!  

These issues have really made me loose faith in the quality of Feisty Fawn and Ubuntu releases in general.  Yes, I realize they have a tight schedule and that a lot of bugs make it through, but this is just unbelievable.  Unless someone can offer me advice or I figure out how to fix this soon, I'm sorry to say I'm just going to go back to using Windows XP.
