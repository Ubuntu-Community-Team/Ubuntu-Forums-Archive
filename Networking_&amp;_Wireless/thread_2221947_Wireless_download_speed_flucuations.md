### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-05-04
It could be narrow-band versus wide-band setting.  Look for a switch in BIOS or a parameter setting for the rtl driver to enable or disable interference robustness.

Wireless transmitter/receivers are ultimately analog devices.  By setting a narrow band for frequency, you can get less interference, but shorter range.  This may improve speed because there is less overlapping frequencies.  When you bring up network manager, how many wireless points do you see?

I don't see any switches in the driver itself:

Try this:

Shut down the machine.  Pull the battery for an hour.  Put the battery back in an reboot then test the network speeds.  Sometimes the wireless firmware needs to be reloaded and one way to do that is to pull the power.  Also, check your antenna connections.  Remove the underside plate and make sure the two connections are secured.  Sometimes the antennae wear out as they are thin wires that run through the hinge.  If they are shorted or one is broken, then you only get 1/2 the signal strength and slow speeds.
