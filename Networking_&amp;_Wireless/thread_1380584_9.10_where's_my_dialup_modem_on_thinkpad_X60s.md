### Post by adam814 on 2010-01-13
Can you post the output from "lspci"?  Hopefully your modem shows up in it.

I think it should be one of the /dev/ttyS* entries instead of any of the /dev/tty0-7 entries, those are just your VTs.  I haven't used a modem in Linux since about 2003, so it's hard to remember, but I think it was /dev/ttyS0.  Of course if you figure out which one it is you can always symlink it to /dev/modem.

### Post by HangukMiguk on 2010-01-14
This is typically a sign of a winmodem.  I would run the scanModem utility:

```
wget http://132.68.73.235/linmodems/packages/scanModem.gz
gunzip scanModem.gz
chmod +x scanModem
./scanModem
```

this program SHOULD tell you what kind of modem it is, if there are Linux drivers available, and where to get them.
