### Post by bmullan on 2009-02-15
After a couple years of struggling to get various Wireless NIC cards running under Ubuntu.. from using NDISWrapper to beating my balding head against the wall I found myself struggling like a lot of others with a variety of issues.

Trying to get anything to work with stability in **64BIT mode** was even harder.

It finally occurred to me to *just go a simpler route.*

I bought a cheap "Access Point" (*note that's different from an wireless route*r) (Cisco WAP54g for $50) and just hooked the ethernet port of my Ubuntu PC to the ethernet port of the Access Point and let the Access Point become my wireless connection.   

Ubuntu/Linux understands Ethernet just fine and this removes any problems with 802.11x in Ubuntu.

Maybe someone else has posted this obvious solution before but in case not... I felt I should, so here goes.

You can buy a cheap 802.11g Access Point for ~$60  -or-  ~$120 for an 802.11n Access Point...  really not that much more than a NIC.

Configuring Access Points doesn't get much simpler... usually involves just 3-4 steps.

1) input of the gateway IP address of your actual Wireless Router
2) your wireless security method (WEP or whatever)
3) your SSID

Of course there might be 1 or 2 other changes required IF needed by the Access Point vendor's software for port forwarding etc.

***This WORKS GREAT and ALWAYS works ALL the time even for 64BIT Ubuntu/Linux !!***

I now have have 802.11N in my house (Linksys) and 64 BIT Ubuntu 8.10 working with total stability.   

***If I had a tail it would be waggin'***  :guitar:

Going the Access Point route eliminated ALL of the hassles.
