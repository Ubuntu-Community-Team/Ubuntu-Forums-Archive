Of course it looks good on their end. That's pretty standard.  Have they tested it from **your end** though?

When they test from the office, there's only so much they can test. i.e. resistance, stray voltage, shorts, grounds, loop length.  

What they can't see from their end is any interference from neighboring circuits within the cable.  This can be looked at from the customer end if they have the know-how.

They usually can see loose connections between themselves and you, but not always, and sometimes not if it's in your house wiring.

Are you able to see if you lose SYNC on your router?  Or does it stay up when you get logged off?
