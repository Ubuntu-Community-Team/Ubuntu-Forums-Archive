### Post by ChrisBieda on 2006-12-27
Yes, I have a plug and play solution, one that just lit up a half hour ago: Unfortunately, the item is no longer in stock where I bought it ([www.geeks.com)](www.geeks.com)), but what they described as "MSI MS-6834 802.11g Wireless-G PCI Adapter" ran perfectly out-of-the-box with 6.10 on an old HP Pavilion that otherwise had no useful life left in it.  (For those of you in the know, that's a Pav 8660.)  

Just on a lark, I threw one of these in and with my SSID/WEP credentials, I was cookin'.

I am amazed, since I too have had nothing but defeat in efforts to match distros and WLAN PCI cards.  I bought a half-dozen of these cards on a lark and now they are more precious to me than 802.11n gear.

I am a Linux noob, so if you have questions, I may offer ignorant replies, but I offer this as a proven (on one system) PnP solution.
