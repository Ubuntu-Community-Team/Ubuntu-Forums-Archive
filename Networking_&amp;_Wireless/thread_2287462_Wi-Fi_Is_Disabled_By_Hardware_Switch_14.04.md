### Post by coljohnhannibalsmith on 2015-07-20
Additional Info,

This is a dual boot system and Wi-Fi on the Windows 7 side had been working so I didn't  suspect the hardware.  My mistake!  I finnaly gave in and booted into Windows and indeed the Wi-Fi was not working; so I used the troubleshooting feature.  It told me to turn on the Wi-Fi switch on the front of the unit.  There was no switch in the front of the unit; but F2 had a radio-tower symbol on it.  I pressed this and the Wi-Fi came on and subsequently worked in Ubuntu.  F2 is also the key used to boot into the BIOS configuration menu.  I had recently changed the boot order to USB/CD-ROM/HDD.  I probably left my finger on the key a little too long, or this may be a hot key in Ubuntu for some other purpose.  Anyway, I fat-fingered the key.

Thanks, Hannibal
