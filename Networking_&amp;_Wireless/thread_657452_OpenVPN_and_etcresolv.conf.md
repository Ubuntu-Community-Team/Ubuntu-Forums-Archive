Hi all,

I have an Ubntu 7.10 box. I use openvpn to connect to my office network. When I connect I have an script that overwrites /etc/resolv.conf adding in the first line the nameserver of my office domain, because openvpn does not do it by default.

The problem is that some process from my machine is overwriting the /etc/resolv.conf periodically from the one I modify for OpenVPN to this file:

-----------------------------------------------------------
search mshome.net
nameserver 192.168.0.1
--------------------------------------------------------------

From what I have read this seems to be related to the dhclient process in my laptop making new requests to my local network. But this looks strange to me, is not OpenVPN tunneling all the traffic  generated by my laptop to the virtual interface and then to my office netowrk ? Why is then dhclient getting communicating with the DHCP server in my local network ?

Any help to stop dhclient from overwriting /etc/resolv.conf over and over again is appreciated.

This is my /etc/dhcp3/dhclient.conf
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
send host-name "<hostname>";
request subnet-mask, broadcast-address, time-offset, routers,
        domain-name, domain-name-servers, host-name,
        netbios-name-servers, netbios-scope;
