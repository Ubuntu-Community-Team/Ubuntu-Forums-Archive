### Post by technoboi on 2011-11-05
I have my desktop on Ubuntu 11.04 Classic and several other PCs on Xubuntu 11.04. They all use a fixed IP. Suddenly all stopped working. It was driving me crazy trying to find what was wrong. I could ping the router and modem and anything on my network but there was no Internet. I have replaced the cable modem with a cable router-modem. The same problem exists.
I found that if I connect via DCHP, from any computer, I can get the Internet. By fixed IP, even by the same IP as DHCP, there is no Internet. Let me reiterate: I can ping the modem perfectly OK but there is no Internet be it by IP or by name.
In summary: DHCP works fine. Fixed IP does not.
I see this as a bug. I can't find it on Launchpad or within this forum.

 I would post it on Launchpad but I also can't find where I can take a degree course in bug reporting which, IMHO, is what it needed for reporting non-automated bugs on Launchpad. So, I am posting here lest this post is useful for others. I have found a posting on another site where someone was having problems setting up his PC with a fixed IP. I could see nothing wrong with his settings so he could well have been getting the same problem.
