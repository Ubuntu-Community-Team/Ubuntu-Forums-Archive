### Post by aldo_m on 2007-11-06
step 1: install a fresh copy of ubuntu

step 2: your going to want to be hardwired in.

step 3: go to system/administration/synaptic package manager/search for ndis install your two options witch is ndiswrapper utily/ndiswrapper common.

step 4:go back to system/administration/software sources.you are going to check off all of them except source code.then click the close button you will get a new window with a reload button/click on reload.

step 5: go back to synaptic package manger  and search for the ndisgtk./install that.after that is installed close everything and look in system/admin the last program should be windows wireless drivers.

step 6:would be to find out what drivers you need i had a problem here on my xp os it was just bcml5 but when i installed it . you will see the name and under it it will say hardware present: no for me it was like that into i installed the bcml5a.inf file. i ended up getting my drivers from [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=405990](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=405990).

step 7: load your ndisgtk prog browse to your drivers. i used a pen drive and saved them to a home folder.  

if the hardware says yes at this point under ndisgtk and you wireless light isnt on then what i did next was go to system/admin/restricted drivers manager.and enable the firmware and them click on internet to get the firmware.
