### Post by jr.vizion on 2007-06-05
I spent two days on the weekend working on how to get Ubuntu to recognize my usb wireless adapter. So I decided to just buy another one. I went to microcenter which is on the web also and bought a Trendnet pc card model TEW-441PC 108Mbps (Version A2.0R) got home pulled it out the box and put it in my hp pavillion zv5000 laptop running Ubuntu 7.04, turned on the laptop from the grub of course I pick Ubuntu and logged in. When I logged in I saw something asking about restricted drivers the icon looked like a piece of hardware so I click on it and it disappeared and all of a sudden from the network manager a list of available networks shows up so I pick my network and choose to enter my hex code from the router and then clicked log in and I was logged in. I pulled my cat 5 cable out before I booted just to limit any confusion. It sounds kind of lame the way I detailed it but that's how easy it should be. The tech stuff is fun to learn but can be a pain when it still doesn't work after hours. Linux is awesome it allows you to do so much that would cost a fortune with windows. Plus you get smarter as you use Linux. I am happy I found Ubuntu because so many other Gnu/Linux version just don't return the same results believe me I tried.
