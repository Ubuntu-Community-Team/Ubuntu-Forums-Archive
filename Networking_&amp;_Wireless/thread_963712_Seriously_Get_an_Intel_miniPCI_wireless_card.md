### Post by TrondOscar on 2008-10-30
I have been having problems with my Packard Bell Easynote G1 (yeah, I know, stupid choice...) and the wireless in Ubuntu. Apart from the WiFi, *everything* else has been working perfectly.

NEC has been kind enough to supply these machines with the dreaded Agere wireless card that there just aren't any drivers for, and the ndiswrapper never worked on it either.

So I bought a Cnet USB-dongle, which didn't work for me in dapper(?), but somehow worked out of the box in hardy. Except that it just stops every no and again, several times per hour. And it didn't like WPA enterprise... I have probably spent an entire workday on this problem from the start. 

Today my new Intel 2915 MiniPCI-card arrived. It took me all of 30 minutes to tear the laptop apart, switch the cards, and put it together again. Boot up, et voilá! It just worked, and connected to my network right away. No hassle!

It set me back about USD 25 incl shipping to Norway, but hey... It'll keep the old horse alive for another year, and save me so much frustration! 

My advice to ANYONE struggling with a lousy USB network interface: Get an intel miniPCI card. Today.

-TrondOscar-
