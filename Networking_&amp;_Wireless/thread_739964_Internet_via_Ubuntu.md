### Post by Eiríkr on 2008-03-31
"NIC" stands for **_N_**etwork **_I_**nterface **_C_**ontroller, and is usually an internal card or part of your motherboard, but it could also be virtual, and exist purely in software.  Suffice to say that a NIC and an external modem are generally different things.  

That quibble aside, the parent poster had noted:
> I have a BSNL Bradband connection & use a ext. modem to connect

So there's an external modem, which is broadband, to which the poster's computer is connected (most likely via a CAT5 cable).

Eiríkr
