### Post by Mat10 on 2005-04-22
Try ebay or another used hardware source and look for a Cisco 350 series nic.
They were sold in large numbers and used mostly for some locales that only had a dish antenna access to the Internet.  Places like Nevada and wide open land, etc no trees !

Anyway this card is supported in most Linux distros with a driver and works out of the box.   It is 802.11b gear,  a g router can communicate with a b nic but not vice versa.  I happen to use the b gear, only cause I was stupid and bought it instead of the g stuff.  Live and learn

If you find a card grab it , they sold for 600 bucks with the outside dish and 40 feet of cable.  The antenna should be in the box with it.  You might even try Cisco and see if they have rebuilt or other stand alone stock.  I would think you could find a used one very cheap without the dish.  Lot of people in the midwest were upset when they found it could not send or recive through trees,   he he
