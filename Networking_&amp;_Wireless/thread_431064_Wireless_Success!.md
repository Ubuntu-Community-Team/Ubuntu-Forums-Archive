### Post by russw1 on 2007-05-02
After a week and a half, and trying out Feisty, Edgy, Linspire, two versions of Freespire, Simply Mepis, Xandros, Puppy, and Dream Linux for a distro that would connect wirelessly for me, I finally have success.  I had every kind of success and failure- no hardware recognition, positive connections that would not connect to the Internet, you name it.  Finally, I decided that the problem could be the wireless card in my Toshiba laptop.  I removed the Intel 2100 and replaced it with the Intel 2200 wireless b/g card. 

I reinstalled Feisty (prefer the look and feel to the others) and it worked immediately, with WPA!  This is what I had hoped for when I switched to Linux.  At least the 80 hours I have spent tinkering with the wireless  helped me become familiar with the OS.  I don't know if my problem was a bad card, driver issues or what...I'm not sure I care.  Thanks to those who have tried to help me.
