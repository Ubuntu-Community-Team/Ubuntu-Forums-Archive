### Post by Bucky Ball on 2011-06-05
Welcome.

I would perhaps talk to your computer admin people and find out if there are any security requirements to get on the network. Are you inserting a security key (WEP, WPA, etc.) along with your password??? Are you naming an ESSID (access point name) or is it automatically finding that? Are you using static IP addresses at your work or dynamic? You need to find out all this stuff.

When you have, right click the network icon and 'Edit Connections'. Click the wireless tab and set up the wireless connection for you work network. That will keep your details permanent and with luck you will have no more trouble.

Also, it would be a very good idea to try your card out on an 'open' network which should automatically connect you. If it doesn't you perhaps don't have the wireless card drivers/firmware installed correctly. 

Tell us how you go.

### Post by Bucky Ball on 2011-06-05
It's 'Available to all users' and you need to click on your ESSID name (Work or whatever) then 'Edit' to get there. Please read my last post (#2) also, please. ;)

Right click network icon,
Edit Connections
Click 'wireless' tab
Click name of your ESSID then 'Edit'
'Available to all users' is bottom left of that window
Insert the correct details for your network
Apply and restart your machine.
