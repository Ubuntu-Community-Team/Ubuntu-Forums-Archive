### Post by fragos on 2007-01-18
In the past when you tried to register a Comcast cable modem with a Linux system you were told to buy Windows.  I managed to register my Linux system using wine and IE6.  All worked well for almost three years until the supplied Motorola cable modem died.  Comcast will now register your MAC address for you.  I purchased a new modem and worked with their tech support to register the MAC address.  I could connect but was limited to a Comcast server that said I had an invalid system.  Pinging google.com and comcast.net all returned with the same IP address.  I also noticed that DCHP assigned me different DNS addresses and domain than I had before.  The new ones were in Silicon Valley a few hundred miles north of Fresno CA where I live.  They had no answer and no support escalation process to solve my problem.  I slept on it and the problem solved itself the next morning.  When I re-booted DCHP connected me to the original domain and DNS with a new IP address.  It would appear that they made the changes to a server that I wasn't connected to and over night that server synchronized with the Comcast server I was physically connected to.  All works again but Comcast tech support weren't aware this would happen.  When you call the 800 number you may be connected anywhere.  The last support center I connected with was in central Canada.  Comcast now recognizes procedurally that Linux exists and can connect to the Internet.  An improvement over telling me I had to buy Windows to use the Internet but still clueless about how the Internet works.
