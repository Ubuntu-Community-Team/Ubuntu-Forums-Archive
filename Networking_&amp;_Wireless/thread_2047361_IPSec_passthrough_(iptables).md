### Post by Doug S on 2012-08-26
I have trouble to follow your iptable rule set (myself, I don't use the mangle table).
However, since it has been over a day and nobody else replied...
 
I assume your "esp" protocol rules are for your IPsec stuff. They don't seem to go thru NAT. Shouldn't it be "j MASQUERADE" instead of "j ACCEPT" in the nat table, if the destination is via eth0?

### Post by oblivian on 2012-08-26
Hi Doug,

Thank you for taking the time to answer.

As it turned out the iptables was correct. The problem was due to two corrupted VPN client profiles (due to heavy testing I guess), both the Windows client and the Mac client. What confused me was that it worked on a box firewall/home router. The corruption was DNS related, and I think the reason it worked with the boxed firewall was because the VPN DNS settings was "frozen up" to that config... Just one of those days where everything goes wrong.

For the record, the ESP protocol must not be masqueraded. That will break IPSec. So you set ESP to Accept before masquerading in the NAT section.
