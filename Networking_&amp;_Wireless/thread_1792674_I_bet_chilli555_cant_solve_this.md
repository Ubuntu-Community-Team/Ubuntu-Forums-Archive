### Post by chili555 on 2011-06-28
You guys are too kind. I am truly touched by your very kind comments. 

I am very happy to have helped many people get going in Linux. I see many posts responding to threads started months ago where new posters report that my months old explanation (that I probably stole from one of you guys!) worked and they are answering from their perfectly working wireless. I assume there are even more that find and implement the fix and don't bother to post.

Along the way, I've met a lot of great people from all over the world that I continue to email and instant message. We have a little United Nations going here, only better. We are friendly, respectful and not only tolerant of our different cultures, but even celebrate the things that make life interesting in all the countries of the world represented here. Even Kansas. Just kidding, sis!

I have seen other forums and IRC chats that are very cruel to new members. I hate that. I am very proud that almost everyone I've ever encountered here is welcoming, warm and helpful. I am especially proud when a post is solved and the poster is complimentary of the Ubuntu community and the extraordinary help he received here, on General Help and Absolute Beginner. I am proud that there are people out there that recognize and appreciate what we all, not just me, do here.

Along the way, I have learned a lot. Not just what makes a Ralink tick, but what makes a scared newbie calm down; how to explain things so he'll take away a little new knowledge and so much more. I learn a new thing or two or three every day, lots of times from you guys above! 

This morning, Mrs. Chili asked, "So, what's next for you?" I told her 20,000 posts and I suspect it will be as fun as the first 10,000.

Most of all, I deeply appreciate your kind respect.
