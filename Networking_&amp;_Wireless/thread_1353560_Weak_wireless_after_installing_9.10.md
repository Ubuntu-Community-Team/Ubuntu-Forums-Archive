### Post by sophicsage on 2009-12-12
Right now, my wife is sitting right next to me on her laptop.  She has a 100% wireless signal.  Mine is at 40% but fluctuates betwen 26% and about 44%.  It shuffles constantly and sometimes totally drops and I have to shut down and restart. 

I totally uninstalled Windows 7 when I installed Ubuntu.  I had no issues with Windows 7's wireless capabilities and I had a 100% signal as well before this install.  When the connection is working, it works great.  When it's not, it's painfully slow.  I've had a few other issues as well since installing Ubuntu.  In general, this OS is workable, but there are some issues.

Here is another interesting problem with wireless:  It will show an 80% signal strength right after I boot up and that lasts about 30 seconds, then a notice pops up saying that I have a .local domain and that it's incompatible with Avahi.  Then when the popup goes away my signal instantly drops to the usual 26% to 44%. 

Any ideas as to why is happening?  I'm getting a little frustrated.  If these problem keeps continuing, I may have no choice but to do a fresh Windows 7 install.  I had a Vista, then upgraded to Windows 7.  Sony sent me a free Windows 7 upgrade disc but I wanted an OS with a clean install.  I don't want to mess with MS OSs any more.  But I HAVE to use this laptop for work and I need a reliable system, OS politics aside.  It MUST work.  This will mean I'll have to buy a full Windows 7 disc since stupid Sony only sent me an upgrade disc.

Anyone see a fix for what is apparently a very common problem with Ubuntu 9.10?  Thanks.
