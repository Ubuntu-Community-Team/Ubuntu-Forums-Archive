> ID [COLOR="Red"]148f:2573[/COLOR] Ralink Technology, Corp. RT2501/RT2573 Wireless AdapterYour device is claimed by two different drivers which probably are conflicting. Your lsmod shows they are both loaded:```

alias:          usb:v148Fp[COLOR="Red"]2573[/COLOR]d*dc*dsc*dp*ic*isc*ip*
```Let's blacklist one and see if the other takes over and performs as expected. Please do:```
sudo su
echo "blacklist rt2500usb" >> /etc/modprobe.d/blacklist.conf
exit
```Reboot and give us your report.

### Post by chili555 on 2011-06-11
I suggest changing it back to blacklist rt2500usb. Obviously rt73usb is driving the device, however poorly. At least it works. I know of two more things you might try. First, let's write a conf file:```
sudo gedit /etc/modprobe.d/rt73usb.conf
```Add one line:```
options rt73usb nohwcrypt=1
```Proofread carefully, save and close gedit. Reboot.

If it does little or no good, you can remove it:```
sudo rm /etc/modprobe.d/rt73usb.conf
```Then unload and reload rt73usb:```
sudo rmmod -f rt73usb
sudo modprobe rt73usb
```The other thing you might try is compat-wireless. Post back if we need to do that and I'll get my plasma cutter and welding gloves warmed up.
