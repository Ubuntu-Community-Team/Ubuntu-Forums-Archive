### Post by tgalati4 on 2015-11-13
Both, there should be a kernel configuration option when compiling to enable "n" wireless support and then /etc/modules [options]("http://http://askubuntu.com/questions/51226/how-to-add-kernel-module-parameters") to activate "n" wireless.

I do know that the Broadcom drivers have problems with Channels 12 and 13 used in Europe. So that may be related to switching between g and ac mode.

Use *Wifi Analyzer* on an Android Phone to see if your router is working on 5 GHz band.

When I said problematic:  [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2283001](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2283001)
