### Post by Twin Penguins on 2007-05-12
The only problem you have with a long antenna cable is line loss over the cable (dB signal strength loss over distance)  which depending on length can actually negate the entire purpose of the antenna.. how long are you talking? My wire is 8 feet long.. my router is almost 75 feet away and I drop signal occasionally but it works well enough to do what I need.

There are several solutions... you can get a laptop if you have one throw two wifi cards in it and use AD-hoc mode to connect the PC and the laptop together using the other wifi card to fetch the signal you are after and use it as the gateway.... in essence its basically a repeater or relay station. Thats what I just setup today and its working great.. put an old 333mhz pent 2 XFCE laptop to good use :))

You could also buy an actual repeater.... or you could try using an omni or a directional high gain antenna.. but personally I wouldnt run a cable for it over 10 feet long due to line loss over that distance.

Two things to remember with antennas. Omni will give you less boost to signal but is not as sensitive to direction.
Directional antennas will give you a higher boost... but are very sensitive to direction of signal and which way it is pointed.

Remember though.. this is mostly my opinion gained from actual commucations knowledge and a little practical expirience.. Im sure there are better ways of doing it....
