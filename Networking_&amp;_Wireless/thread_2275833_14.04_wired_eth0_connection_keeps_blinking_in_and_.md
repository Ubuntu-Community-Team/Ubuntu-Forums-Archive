### Post by chili555 on 2015-04-29
Is that you, Mrs. Chili? Did you register on the forum just so you could *finally* get me to fix your computer??

Just kidding. My wife has the same ethernet card and the same symptoms! I have been struggling for some time to find a solution. So far, it has evaded me. If you do, as I have many, many times, a Google serach for `e1000e drops,` you will see many threads, mostly with no solution. A few threads hint at a solution but the original poster never returns to report that it worked or didn't.

What is often recommended, compiling and installing Intel's latest driver from here didn't make the slightest improvement for me: [http://www.intel.com/support/network/sb/CS-032514.htm](http://www.intel.com/support/network/sb/CS-032514.htm) While I will be happy to assist you if you wish, I don't recommend wasting time and CPU cycles on an experiment that isn't shown to solve the problem.

A promising thread I read suggests that auto-negotiation may be the issue. I therefore suggest you try:```
sudo ethtool -s eth0 autoneg off speed 100
```Test for a day or so, as Mrs. Chili is doing now, and if it helps, we'll drop the setting into /etc/rc.local.

### Post by sir_christopher on 2015-05-12
I had foolishly ruled this out, but I think the problem was actually attributable to my cable. Turning off autoneg did reduce the number of connection drops, but didn't stop them entirely, even when set to a lowly 10Mbps (full duplex). Why did I rule out the cables? Because I had 3 brand new 50' Insten cables, and I was having the problem with all three of them. Surely all 3 can't be duds! 

Wrong. 

When I brought home one of the cables to test in a different environment on my Mac laptop, the OS X network tool detected that the cable was connected but that it was unable to get an IP from my router. Swapping that cable out for another and the wired network connection worked just fine. Got a 25' Belkin cable from Target, and my connection is working just fine (knock on wood). Stupid cables probably cost me a 40-hour work week. I should send Insten a bill for my time.

Thanks Mr. Chili. Regards to the missus.
