### Post by chili555 on 2010-04-12
Your symptoms sound like a DNS nameserver problem. If you do:```
cat /etc/resolv.conf
```There should be a line or lines in there like 'nameserver 203.405.whatever.' If the file lacks these lines, you can force them. Find out what your gateway address is with:```
route -n
```Then use that address here:```
sudo su
echo "nameserver 192.168.1.1" >> /etc/resolv.conf
exit
```Substitute your details, of course.
