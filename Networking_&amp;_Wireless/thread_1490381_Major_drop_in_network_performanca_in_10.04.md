Hi,

I've just upgraded from 9.10 to 10.04 a few days ago, and since then, several applications, most notably Firefox and Thunderbird appear to have drastically reduced network performance. Sometimes loading a website like start.ubuntu.com or youtube.com takes in the range of 10 seconds upwards, other times (usually after they had been loaded successfully recently), the pages come up instantly.

In Thunderbird, this issue makes itself felt by polls from my pop3 server taking significantly longer than they used to.

Interestingly, chromium (which I installed after the upgrade) does not seem to be affected at all. In chromium, all sites load lightning fast.

None of the apps on this computer use any sort of proxy.

Name resolution does not seem to be the issue, either. According to resolv.conf, the nameserver in use by this comp is the the little NAT router I'm using to access the internet. A few tests with dig did not show any problems or slowdowns with name resolution.

Does anyone have an idea what might be causing this and what I might do about it?
