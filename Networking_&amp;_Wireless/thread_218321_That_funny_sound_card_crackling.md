### Post by faddat on 2006-07-18
I just replaced a faulty Power Supply and everything is now fine with my computer *running*.  However, it now makes a strange crackling noise from the headphone jack.  I thought maybe it was a ground problem or something so I tried plugging the speakers into the headphone jack thinking if it was an electrical problem it would happen in all of the jacks.  The card is a motherboard-mounted CMI-8738-MC6.  It didn't do this with the old power supply (but that P/S could not reliably power the computer, so it was replaced).  

Think there's anything I can do without replacing the Power Supply *again*?

Thanks!

### Post by Indroth on 2006-07-18
Sounds like a grouding problem (as you said) probably a ground loop. Unfortunately, grouding problems are ridiculously hard to locate. You could try looking at the following:

1) What has changed when you changed the power supply? (i.e. what connections, and what was not working before)

2) How are things connected? If you have internal analog connections (like cd audio), that can create ground loops.

3) If all else fails, here is a dirty hack:
Tap the ground *directly* off your headphone jack and connect it to a very large ground (like earth ground or case).

Of course, all this only helps if the problem is actually a grounding problem.;)
