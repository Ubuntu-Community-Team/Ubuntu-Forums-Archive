### Post by varunendra on 2014-01-14
Hello Keshav,

Apart from what Iowan already mentioned, another reason for no replies may be that sometimes the wireless problems come in 'waves' (so many posts in a short amount of time), thus pushing older posts quickly beyond the first page where the potential helpers may not notice them. And the information you provided is too little for an average user to understand the problem and respond back.

That being said, may we ask for some more info to be able to help you? By looking at the NM icon in your screenshot, it seems you are using a wireless network. If that is correct, please follow the "Wireless Script" link in my signature, download and run the script as per instructions there, and post back the report it generates.

Also, from the contents of the 2nd terminal in the background in your screenshot, it seems you were trying something with the 'sysctl' command. So if you have tried some tweaks/workarounds to make the speeds better, please also mention them with details in your next post.

One more thing - If it is just the "apt-get..." commands, while the network speeds being good for other kind of activities (like browsing, direct downloads etc.), it may be just a slow server from which you are downloading. In that case, try changing the server (from country specific server to "Main Server" or vice-versa). It is not very uncommon for servers getting ridiculously slow at times (sometimes they may even go offline for a short time).
