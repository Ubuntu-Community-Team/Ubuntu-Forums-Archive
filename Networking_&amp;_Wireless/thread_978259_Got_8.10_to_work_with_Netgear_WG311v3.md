### Post by brokenjack on 2008-11-10
I just have to give a huge shout out, to David Tang Ye(sp?) for writing the tutorial on getting the Netgear WG311v3 to work. It's written for 7.04 and 8.04, but I couldn't get it to work with those releases.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Device/Netgear_WG311_v3](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/Device/Netgear_WG311_v3)

I literally installed 3 different versions of ubuntu(6.06)(7.04)(8.04) and tried to get this wireless card to work, with no success. I figured what the heck. I'm still a noob, but I installed 8.10 anyway.

Followed the wiki once again to the letter, and BAM...WWW greatness. I said to myself, in comfortable excitement...I can't f#$%ing believe it actually worked. I was so obsessed with getting this card to work. Anyway...enough of my celebrating, the ndiswrapper packages I used were:

ndiswrapper-common_1.52-1ubuntu1_all.deb
ndiswrapper-utils-1.9_1.52-1ubuntu1_i386.deb

and the Windows 2000 Driver

I guess I'll have to wait until after I've actually used 8.10 to crack open the champagne. This is my garage computer, so no real mission critical stuff going on in here....yet...
