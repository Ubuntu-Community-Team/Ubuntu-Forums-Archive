### Post by schmildo on 2008-03-06
You'll need to put it into a device capable of using it.

(mobile phone, or one of those PCMCIA thingies that you jam into the side of a laptop)

You'll need to check with your mobile carrier that the sim card has data enabled on it. (they often charge differently for data than they do for voice)

you'll need some way of connecting the pc/laptop to the phone, (cable, bluetooth, infra-red) bluetooth is probably the most convienient method, Infrared is fiddly, and the cables are expensive. These options will depend on the device you buy.

I'm not sure how to setup linux to use these connections yet, i know it's possible, but i'm still learning, and the exact details may depend on the mobile device you use.

Don't buy a mobile phone or device for the purpose of using it to surf the web without researching it first. Make sure it'll work before you get it, and it's always better to find a guide that you can follow when you finally get around to trying it out.

The PCMCIA card thingys you can buy in australia at the moment are pretty expensive, around $300, not to mention the bills you'll get for the data itself, and I dunnno about the 3G coverage here...
  you might find (with some investigation) that you can get a mobile phone that does the EXACT SAME THING for less, or if not, at least you have a phone you can use!

Some phones however might be 3G capable, but not capable of giving you alot of speed for data.... 

Anyway I'll stop rambling now, have you already got something to put the sim into?
