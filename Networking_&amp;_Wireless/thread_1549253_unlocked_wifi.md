### Post by dangerjunkie2002 on 2010-08-09
Hi,

The quick answer is "no" as you are using Linux. 

You are however exposing your computer to every computer on that network and potentially letting others monitor what you do because it's not encrypted. For example a malicious observer could capture your Ubuntuforum password when you logged in and then pretend to be you. This applies to any site that doesn't have a padlock in the address bar. If you use the same password for everything this is riskier as someone can get the password you typed in here then use it to log into Paypal, for example.

I don't know where you live so I can't advise you on the legality of using someone's network without their permission. In my country (the UK) this is considered to be stealing someone's Internet and is illegal. I would suggest that you find out what your local laws and penalties are before you decide. 

Your wireless card has a unique serial number (called a MAC address). When you join a wireless network the router will store the serial number of your card and will be able to show all the serial numbers it knows of so if someone found out what the number of your laptop was they could prove you had been on that network.

I am not advocating using others' WiFi without their permission. Please make your own informed decision.
