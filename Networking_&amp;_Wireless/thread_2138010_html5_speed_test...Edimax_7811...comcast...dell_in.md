### Post by silvertwilight on 2013-04-22
Just booted the daily build (4/22/13) of Ubuntu 13.04 from a usb on a Dell Inspiron 1545 with Edimax 7811 usb wireless n.
Ubuntu recognized the device and installed it without a problem.  Asked for the WPA authentication and connected.
The connection information said 18 Mbps so I wanted to speed test the connection.  Most on-line tests require a 
Flash player,  but I did find an HTML5 speed test at SpeedOf.Me   and it showed what I get on Windows from Comcast
(Same laptop) which is 18.18 Mbps DL and 4 something upload in this area.   Very happy that Ubuntu recognized the 
Edimax 7811  which is around $10 (materials that came with it said 2010)  and a good addition of wireless N for both Linux and Windows.  Also happy to see that HTML5 is getting more use by programmers.   Kudos to the Ubuntu team.  Even tho this is about a three year
old laptop,  some of the new laptops for around $300  make upgrading worthwhile.   I still own a Dell 4550 desktop
which I bought in 2003 and still use,  but I really like the newer laptop.
