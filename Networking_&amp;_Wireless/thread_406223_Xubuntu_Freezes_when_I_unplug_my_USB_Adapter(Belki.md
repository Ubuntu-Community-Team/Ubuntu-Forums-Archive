### Post by skysailor7 on 2008-02-05
No.1  Don't unplug a usb device like a printer or external CD/DVD/CD-RW while the computer is running.

No.2  On my windows computer I have a usb CD/RW that I have to disable while my computer is running for it to be safe to disconnect it.

No.3  There is a problem with usb devices and internet connections in Windows.  Might also be in Linux systems.  When you uninstall them they somehow mess up your internet connection.  In windows I had to Format and re-install to fix this problem.  I searched the internet and found that other people had the same problem with uninstalling them.  At first I thought that it got rid of some shared .dll's but that wasn't the case.  I just shut the computer down and unplugged the usb device and re-started.  Then went and deleted all of the folders relating to the usb device.  Problem solved.

Try logging off without shutting down and then unplug the usb device.
