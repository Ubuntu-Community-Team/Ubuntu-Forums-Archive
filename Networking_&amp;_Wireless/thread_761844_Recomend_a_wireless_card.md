### Post by sKAApGIF on 2008-04-21
I don't have experience with a particular pci card and brand (I mostly use mini-pci cards for embedded devices). But I do have experience with wifi chipsets.  

I would suggest you get a card with an atheros chipset.  Different atheros chipsets differ in price and performance (you will have to see what fits your specific situation best).  Everytime I've used an atheros card in an PC ubuntu picked it up right away and configured it.

There is a good list here: [Madwifi Compatibility]("http://madwifi.org/wiki/Compatibility")  I would suggest you see which cards are available in your area and go look them up on the list.  

It might not be the answer you were looking for, but hopefully it'll point you into the right direction.
