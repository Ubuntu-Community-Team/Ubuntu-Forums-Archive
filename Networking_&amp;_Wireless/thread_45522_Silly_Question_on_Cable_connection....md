### Post by jadugarr on 2005-07-01
Most of the time you hook your cable modem up to a simple $20 router (technically they're just NAT switches, but I won't bore your with specifics) and you hook your computer up to the router.  The router will more than likely already have DHCP enabled which ubuntu will dectect and setup your network automatically.  You might could do without a router and use a cable modems usb connection, but that could be a hassle depending on the modem and the fact that most ISPs won't support linux.  With a router though it doesn't matter what OS your running because the cable modem talks to the router instead of your computer directly. 

Hope that answers your question.
