### Post by justaname on 2011-01-22
Hey all,

  I have an old IBM T30 Laptop that I installed Ubuntu 10.04 on.  It has taken me about two weeks and three wireless cards before I finally found one that works.

  It's a D-Link DWL-G650 that I had lying around from the old days.  (Sometimes hoarding is good.)  Manufacture and support for this card ended in 2008, but I would be willing to bet that D-Link's other wireless adapters would do the trick also.  Strangely I cannot buy D-Link adapters at Bestbuy or hhGreg.  But Wal-Mart had the latest and greatest, via ship to store, for about $20.

  So if your having a problem getting an old/current system to connect to your router, I would give the D-Link adapters a try.

  Just thought I would pass this along so others will not pull out their hair like I did.

### Post by songspring on 2011-06-15
I have that card as well, and had some problems getting it to work on my old Toshiba Satellite 1805 in 8.04.  What finally worked perfectly, was to do a fresh install with the card plugged in, AND the ethernet cable also plugged in.  I had done an install of 8.04 on another Toshiba (Tecra 8200) with a built in wireless (orinoco_cs drivers worked on that one)--and the installation without the ethernet cable went smooth as silk and I had wireless without any fiddling around.  So I thought I would do the same on the Satellite with the G650, but the wifi did not work out of the box.  Soon as I caught myself in an endless loop of attempts and no joy, I thought to try the install again with the cable in, and to my amazement, it worked.  I used WEP and all I had to do was enter the info in the Network Manager, and reboot--bingo!  

So my advice to anyone trying and failing to get the card to work--do a fresh install, and have both the wifi and ethernet plugged in while installing.  I was so happy it worked.  

Wireless setup can be so frustrating in Linux.  Too many options to try and who knows what is happening to the system trying all that stuff.
