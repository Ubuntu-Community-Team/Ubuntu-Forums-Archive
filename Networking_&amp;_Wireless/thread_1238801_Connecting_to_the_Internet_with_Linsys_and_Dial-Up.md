### Post by Bartender on 2009-08-13
Hi, Jackalope -
A laptop is the BEST answer to dial-up.  Drive into town and find some broadband.

If my attachment loads correctly, you'll have proof positive that dial-up can be done with a laptop.  I'm using an old US Robotics serial modem and a Sabrent SBT-USC1M serial-to-USB cable.  Newegg has this cable.  I've scrounged up three or four old USR's on eBay and craigslist.

The new USR 5637 looks very cool but I don't know anything about it.  It's a little USB modem that's supposed to be full hardware and work with Linux.

Here's my old dial-up diary - wanders around but still useful I think.
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=480717)

One thing has changed from when I wrote that - Jaunty doesn't have wvdial in the default distro, or it does have wvdial but it's broke.  Not sure which at this point.  If you do a search in Ubuntu Forums for "wvdial" you'll see some recent posts.  wvdial is in the "gnome-ppp" package, which you could download with your Windows PC from the Ubuntu packages website, save to thumb drive, put on the Ubuntu desktop, and double-click on it.  It's not a big package so could be done with dial-up.

One of these days Synaptic will confront you with a 100MB update, and that's when you'll want to take the laptop into town and connect to a wifi network.  Our new library has been a God-send for me!

Do you have good cellular coverage?  We're inside of Verizon's 3G network.  If I found a sack of cash tomorrow I'd sign up for mobile broadband, then get an air card with external antenna and a Cradlepoint 3G router.
