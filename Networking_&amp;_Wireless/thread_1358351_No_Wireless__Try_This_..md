### Post by Avidanis on 2009-12-18
Like many other posters ,after upgrading the kernel or Ubuntu from 9.04 to 9.10 I had difficulties connecting wirelessly. I have seen all kinds of different suggestions for fixing this but not this one , and for me it was quite easy. It may work for you too. 

I have a Dell Inspiron 1521 laptop with a Windows install so your results may vary if you have a different computer/setup .

With your computer turned off, turn off the wireless switch. Mine is located on the left hand side of the laptop. Make sure your ethernet cable is not plugged in. The ethernet connection is the default connection and this is part of the problems I was having .

With the wireless switch turned off and the ethernet cable not connected go ahead and turn the pc on and boot into your Ubuntu installation. When the log-in screen comes up , go ahead and enter your password . Keep the wireless switch off up until you enter your password and hit the enter key.

Right after you enter your password and hit the enter key , turn on the wireless switch . Your wifi light should come on and stay on and when your desktop appears you should see the little icon at the top connecting to your wireless network.

This works everytime for me. If I have the ethernet cable plugged in it will not search for a wireless network and unplugging the ethernet after I have already booted up and logged in will not enable wireless. 


Hope this solves the wireless problem  for some of you . 

Cheers.
