### Post by Pow_2k on 2012-07-15
After using 10.04 on an Acer D250 netbook for a few years I'm upgrading to a Dell 1121 on which I've installed 12.04.  I'm looking for a way to save and migrate all the wireless connections configured on the 10.04 netbook to the 12.04 one.  In 10.04 I found (thanks to a Google search and likely a post on these forums) the settings are stored under ~/.gconf/system/networking/connections/.  Under 12.04 such a path doesn't exist, so I can't just copy the structure over.  So I'm wondering...

1. What is the path under 12.04 where the wireless network connections are stored?

and

2. Assuming there is a similar path, is there an easy way to take the connections from one laptop and import them to the other?

Note that while I am primarily doing this for the wireless connections, there are some wired connections that I wouldn't mind bringing across as well.  I assume they're in the same path since the name doesn't differentiate between wired vs. wireless, but I haven't dug through enough of the folders in there to confirm this.

Thanks for the help!

### Post by tpow19 on 2012-07-16
You'll want to copy over the files to your new laptop and run "nm-applet" to do the migration of your connections from the .gconf directory to the /etc/NetworkManager directory.

See this post for more information:

[http://askubuntu.com/questions/129547/gnome-network-manager-config-file-migration](http://askubuntu.com/questions/129547/gnome-network-manager-config-file-migration)
