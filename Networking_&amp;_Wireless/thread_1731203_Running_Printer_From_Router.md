### Post by espressobeanie on 2011-04-16
There are two ways to do this:

1) Your printer has an RJ45 (ethernet) port in it, and you can plug ethernet cables into it directly.
2) Your router supports USB connections and it says (print server)

Even if your router isn't a print server, and it does support USB connections, you could probably load the router with Open-WRT and work that way too. It'd be more complex though.

What's the make and model of your printer and router?
