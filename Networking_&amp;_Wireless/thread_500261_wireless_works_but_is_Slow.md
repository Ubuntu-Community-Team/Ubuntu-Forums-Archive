### Post by jml on 2007-07-13
That's an interesting problem.  I assume by the speeds you are listing that you are connecting to the net via cable modem and not DSL.  There are several causes for a slow wireless connection.  While a configuration problem may be the cause, the fact that it was initially a fast connection argues against that.

The quality of the signal makes a difference.  Anything that will degrade your signal strength will result in slower connection speeds.  the 2.x Ghrtz cordless phones can cause interference.  So, theoretically can microwave ovens.  The nature of intervening obsticals , (thick walls, metal partitions, book shelves, etc. ) can reduce signal strength and therefore connection speed.

On a local level, the more computers you have sharing the wireless network connection the slower each individual connection can become.  My download speeds plummet when both of my kids are home on line with their laptops downloading their music streams, and video clips.

At another level, for cable modems, many subscribers share one single connection, (as opposed to DSL connections which have only one subscriber per line.).  The actual number per connection varies from ISP to ISP, but if your ISP oversubscribed your line, you may notice decreased connection speeds when more people are on line at the same time, for example early evening and weekends when people are home from work and school.

To see if any of these things are operating, try using both your Windows machine and your Linux box at the same time and see if their connection speeds differ.  Hope this helps.

Joe
