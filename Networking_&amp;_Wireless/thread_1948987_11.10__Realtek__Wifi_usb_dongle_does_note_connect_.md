### Post by bogan on 2012-03-31
Hi!, **Chuskao,** 

I do not have any solutions to offer, but thought I might compare notes as I use a similar Realtek WLAN USB RTL8188SU Dongle with the same r8712u driver.
If nothing else, it will bump your Thread and may attract a more expert contributer. 
I am using 11.10 3.0.0-17 and 10.04 2.6.32-40. Have you updated to -17?

First an observation and query: I could see nothing in your Post or the dmesg attachment to explain why your Usb Dongle is given the Logical name 'wlan1' rather than the normal default of 'wlan0'.

Mine shows as 'wlan3', but that is because I have an installed Realtek RTL8191S card as 'wlan0', and have previously used different USB Dongles as well.

Do you have another WiFI adapter, or have you previously used a different one?.

Query, have you run or can you Post the output of```
 sudo hwinfo --netcard
```{ if you do not have 'hwinfo', you can install it with apt-get, it is quite small and very useful.}

The connect on boot problem I have, is quite different from yours.The RTL8188SU is very reliable and predictable, often connecting before the GUI screen is ready to use, if already plugged in, and connecting quickly when plugged in later.

The RTL8191S, on the other hand is totally unpredictable, sometimes  it is connected imediately on boot up, sometimes needing three sweep sequences of 50 sweeps before connecting, but sometimes - especially with 10.04 LTS - not even being detected. It is in the latter situation, or after recovering from 'Suspend', that plugging in the RTL8188SU Dongle is useful, as it provokes the Network Manager to reactivate and recommence scanning, usually connecting after less than ten sweeps.

Chao!, **bogan**.
