### Post by blastus on 2008-02-24
Well I bought the Belkin N Wireless Router F5D8233-4 today and it works great. It has a browser-based configuration interface so that you can use any operating system with a browser to configure it. While configuring the router and my laptop, I did have to restart the router once or twice and I did struggle with Network Manager on my laptop and had to reboot a few times (and one time I had to shut down the laptop as it no longer recognized the wireless card even after rebooting twice.) It seems to me that some NICs need to have the power physically cut off to the computer they are in to be reconfigured or reset or whatever.

I'm happy to report that everything is working; I didn't have to boot into Windows at all and the wireless card in my laptop works out of the box in Ubuntu :) My Internet connection is as fast as it should be and has been reliable so far. Network Manager needs some serious reworking though.
