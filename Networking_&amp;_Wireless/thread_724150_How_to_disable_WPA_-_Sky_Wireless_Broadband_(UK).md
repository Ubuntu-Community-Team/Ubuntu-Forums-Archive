### Post by NEUR0M4NCER on 2008-03-14
For anyone having the same drama as I had trying to get WPA working so I could use Sky Wireless Broadband:

You can turn off the WPA at the router. I know, I know - it's not a smart idea, but after you've spent a week following howtos and tutorials, none of them working (big thanks to all of the people who wrote them and offered help though), re-installing (with limited success), and a lot of headaches, I found out that if you hook up to the router via ethernet (my desktop's in the other room, so I had to dig out my old laptop) and go to **192.168.0.1** you can access the web-based setup for the router.

It will ask for user: **admin** and password: **Sky** , and from there you can turn off WPA, maybe turn on WEP or whatever, or set up MAC filtering. You should probably change the login password while you're there as well, 'cause anyone snooping 'round will likely know the default Sky login,

Not an ideal solution, but I tried everything I could find (luckily I hadn't cancelled my previous BB subscription yet, so I still had net access via my old router), and was close to just plopping XP onto my new 'puter bought especially to run Ubuntu only.

Hope this will be of some help :)
