### Post by David_Stolz on 2014-11-21
Dear all,

I have Ubuntu 14.04 LTS installed on a Lenovo L520 laptop and since some time I have a couple of issues with my Wifi connection (or possibly rather multiple symptoms of a single issue of a misconfiguration), for which I have found some workarounds that, however, aren't very satisfying in the long run (and the run has been far too long by now).

I will try to give examples of the issues in more detail:
- upon startup, even before logging in to my Ubuntu account, I get the Network-Manager interface asking me to enter my password repeatedly, and even so fast, that I cannot even log in. It does not seem to help entering the Wifi password, since the window keeps popping up. My workaround to this problem has been to switch of my wifi hardware with the little switch provided on the side of my laptop, to log in to Ubuntu, and then to switch the hardware back on. Quite annoying, but sometimes it works to establish the Wifi connection immediately after this process.
- At other times, however, this does not work either. I believe that when I switch between different Wifi networks (such as at home, university, some friend's house, etc.) and start my laptop, it might even connect to the given network, but websites will not load. This is not a problem of my browser, since other software, such as Mendeley also states that it cannot connect to the server. My workaround in this case has been to execute ```
**sudo dhclient wlan0**
```, which usually works.
- Third, there are times when even though network strength is at 50% or more (according to Wicd), the laptop loses its internet connection and asks me to enter the password by the abovementioned pop-up Wifi window. This is annoying, but not that bad. A strange feeling raises, however, because this seems to happen even more frequently when I download large amounts of data or stream news coverage or stuff like that (this might be, however, only some attentional effect on my side, since in these situations it is even more annoying;))
- Fourth, sometimes the wifi connection fails to establish altogether and in these cases either I restart the computer or execute ```
**sudo service network-manager restart**
``` with a following ```
**sudo dhclient wlan0**
```[B].

[/B]As you can see, all this is quite tedious (and I really wonder why I have not been asking for help earlier ;)). Below, I attached the output of wireless_script ([https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/57264241/wireless_script](https://dl.dropboxusercontent.com/u/57264241/wireless_script)), which I am unable to interpret. 

As you might have guessed, currently I do not have any issue with my Wifi, since I am able to post all this here. Maybe (?) this affects the output of wireless_script, and everything seems fine. In this case I will be happy to provide another output as soon as I have one of the issues above. That might be of additional diagnostic value.

Yet, if anyone has heard of comparable problems, I will deeply appreciate any help given.

Cheers and thanks,
D
