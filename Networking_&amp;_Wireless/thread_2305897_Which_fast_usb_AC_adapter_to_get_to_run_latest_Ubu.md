### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-12-10
It is more about the chip inside than the brand. Realtek, Broadcom and Atheros appear to be the friendliest. As far as speed goes, that is dependent on the speeds your router/access point is capable of giving you and what speed your ISP is giving you in the first place. I get about 25Mbps download. Most wireless dongles will do a/b/g speed or a/b/g/n. G is I think 150Mbps. That's what I use but am nowhere near that. You will probably find you are in the same position.

Again, check the device you will getting your internet from to determine the dongle speed you want. Wireless G is about as fast as most people can get at the moment anyway.
