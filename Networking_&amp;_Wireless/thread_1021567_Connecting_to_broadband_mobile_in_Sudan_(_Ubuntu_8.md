### Post by Nazaka on 2008-12-25
[SIZE="3"]After upgrading to 8.10 I tried to connect to broadband mobile in Sudan
 I am  happy that the  new network configuration is there but unfortunately I recognized that* Sudan* my country is not there in the list , although there are three broadband internet providers ,one of them is Zain company through its Huawei  E220 HSDPA USB modem which is already set in Ubuntu 8.10 

**_I solved this as follows:_**

[1]I just Choose  Internet configuration 
[2]I choose United Kingdom in Mobile Broadband
[3]I choose 3 for provider which after that I changed it to Zain by  selecting Edit 
[4]No need to change the number (*99#)  
[5]the APN is *internet* instead of *3internet* 
[6]last I check connect automatically and the Mobile broadband is working :).[/SIZE]

yes it recognize the modem in 9.04 and 9.10 but not conecting untill you do the following 

1]Choose Internet configuration 
[2]choose United Kingdom in Mobile Broadband tab (no matter where you are)
[3]scroll down provider  and click  3 for provider (3 is a UK company)

[4]No need to change the number (*99#) 
[5]change the APN to internet instead of 3internet 
[6]click connect automatically and the Mobile broadband is working .


Note: some time the connection is lost and you need to re-connect the modem or shut down or it may return when the signal is strong, I relized that sometimes when the signal is week it is not  going to work
to surf the internet change the firefox setting to no proxy or system setting 

you need also to configure the proxy from the preference to choose (No proxy)

### Post by elniemaa on 2010-05-22
[LEFT]Hi dear friends,I am glad to see that there are some UBUNTU fans in the Sudan. Although I am not residing currnetly in the Sudan despite being a Sudanese but the issue is of interest to me as I am a Sudanese and anytime I will be there. With regard to the issue of connecting the 3G Broadband internet, I think this issue is nicely catered for in the latest UBUNTU 10.04 recent release. Something very important to take care of after doing all the necessary settings is to click on (automatically connect) and the lower small window making the connection available for everyone. After that restart the Ubuntu and you will be connected automaticaly to your 3G broadband. I am using Huawei E1550 and E220 and both are working fine.One more issue to observe is that the speed limit of the modem whether it is upto 3.6(E1550) or 7.2(E220) does not make a big difference as the speed offered bt the ISP in most countries is well below 3.6 except in few countries in Europe and in China and Japan and a couple of coutries elsewhere.
 
After all if you have problems connecting, the easiest way is to use a 3G wirless router to which your UBUNTU as well as most of the other Linux distros connect automatically to the 3G broad band and you do not need to bother about settings. I should admit that I connected to the internet to the router in a WINDOWS environment.
 
Pleasure to share my experince and looking forward to hear about more fans of Linux in genral and Ubuntu in particular in SUDAN, specially those who are IT professionals and not only amateures like myself. [/LEFT]
