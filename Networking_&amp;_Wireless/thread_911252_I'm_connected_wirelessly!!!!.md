### Post by dlgoldie on 2008-09-05
I got Ubuntu last weekend and did a clean install on a HP nx9010.  I was so happy with everything until I found out my wireless wouldn't work.  And I was totally discouraged after reading all the posts here and other stuff on the internet that basically said wireless is impossible under Ubuntu.  Certainly impossible for me, a non-geek with no desire to tinker every time I wanted to get on line in a coffee shop.

I work in a Mac office, and we had an old wireless USB adapter laying around.  Just for the heck of it, I plugged it in.  It started working immediately!  I wish I could tell you what it is - the system doesn't identify it beyond "unknown USB vendor specific interface".  The adapter says Macwireless.com on the side.

So maybe I'll become a Linux convert after all...
