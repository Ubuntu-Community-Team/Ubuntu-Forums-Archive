### Post by spd106 on 2007-06-21
Yeah, probably not a good idea to go into the system76 forum and announce you're about to buy a PC from their new competitor (#-o).

Anyway, as regards to a wireless router or indeed an access point (AP), since they are mostly using open network protocols to communicate you shouldn't run into any problems. If you do they are most likely related to bad firmware. Despite what the manual says you don't need the CD, just connect up the Ethernet and drive your web browser to the router's address and configure it from there. Make sure that your ethernet interface is setup for DHCP, though that is the default behaviour. I don't really recommend that you configure it over the wireless initially even if you can.

It's unlikely that you will be able to take full advantage of so called turbo modes as they require same brand wifi cards. So I would recommend that you buy a standard 802.11b/g Wifi Alliance certified router. 

Since you're into gaming then you might want to check that it supports UPnP and virtual servers for port forwarding and maybe dynamic dns if you want to host a server. VoIP and VPN support are probably only added bonuses as you are unlikely to need them any time soon.
