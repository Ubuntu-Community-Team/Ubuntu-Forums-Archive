### Post by RICuser on 2014-12-15
I wanted to ask this in a similar post [titled "http://askubuntu.com/questions/502137/modem-appears-as-wired-connection"]. Unfortunately I could not log in there using this same account. There was no follow up to that question, and looked like it was not solved. I am therefore posting my question here again, in case anyone has come across this problem and found a fix. 

Soon after installation of Ubuntu 12.04 LTS in a HP Compaq laptop (the only OS in this), the Huawei modem (dongle) connected perfectly. I could see in the Network applet a new mobile broadband connection, upon clicking which and entering the country and network provider's name I could connect to Internet. It worked without any problems no matter how many times plugged out/plugged in.

*After updating the OS, and installing Google Chrome, and connecting a couple of times to a wireless network*, the mobile broadband connection started getting very moody. It was rarely detected by the computer (as seen in the Network applet) and decided to connect to internet. I saw in a couple of forums that installing the original driver from the manufacturer (of the dongle) might help. I did that, using the driver contained inside the Modem itself. It also had an instruction for installing it, and I followed that, as much as was applicable to Ubuntu, and as much was possible to follow. After that procedure, and while the Dongle still connected, I can now see that the Network applet shows a new connection called "Wired Network (Huawel Mobile)" and "Disconnected". Both these writings come in grey. The condition remains the same whether I disconnect/reconnect it, reboot the computer with it plugged in/plugged out and so on. 

Another thing is, before the installation of driver, when the Home folder was open, and the device had just been plugged in, it appeared momentarily (almost like a flash) in the Devices section as "Mobile Partner" and then would disappear. Sometimes it would come back after about 30 sec. and remain steady. That was the time when the connection would also appear in Network applet as broadband mobile connection, and one could connect to Internet using that. At other times it never reappeared, neither in the Devices section, nor in the Network applet.

After the installation of driver, the Device "Mobile Partner" has become steady (seen in the Home folder), but it is always listed as a wired connection in the Network applet.

Interestingly, I have used this dongle in a HP Compaq desktop where Windows and Ubuntu 12.04 LTS both are present with dual-boot option, and it never gave any problems, even after connecting to Wireless using the an external USB adapter (Netgear) which I used for the laptop also. I did not even have to install its driver for the dongle there, but the Windows of course had it installed.

Is there any wayout? Is there any 3G dongle that is more Ubuntu friendly? Or this itself is good enough for laptops? Its a 64 bit version.
