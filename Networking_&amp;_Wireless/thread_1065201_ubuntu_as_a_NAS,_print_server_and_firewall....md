### Post by dmizer on 2009-02-09
I highly suggest two things:

1) Use whatever operating system you are familiar enough with to administrate competently. I use Ubuntu becuase I know how to lock it down and it is easy for me to administrate. So for me, Ubuntu is the best choice. Being able to handle security is more important than the choice of operating system.

2) Stick with a hardware firewall like what comes with your router unless you are very comfortable with UFW or IPtables.
