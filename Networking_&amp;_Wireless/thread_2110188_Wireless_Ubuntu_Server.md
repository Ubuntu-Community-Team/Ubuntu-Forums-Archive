### Post by ahallubuntu on 2013-01-29
There is no "standard" USB wireless adapter - they all have different chipsets inside, from different manufacturers.  Some are better supported in Ubuntu than others.

You can get a cheap mini-USB dongle for about $5 USD on eBay with a Realtek 8192CU chipset.  If you don't mind downloading, building, and installing the latest driver from Realtek, it works quite well for a cheap wireless card in Ubuntu.  I have a couple of them.

I recommend Ubuntu 12.04 LTS which is the latest LTS version.  Don't use 10.04 as it won't be supported much longer.
