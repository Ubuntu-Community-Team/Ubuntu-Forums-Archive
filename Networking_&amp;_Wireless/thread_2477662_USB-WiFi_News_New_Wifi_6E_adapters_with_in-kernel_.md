Recently the first new adapters based on the Mediatek mt7921au chipset started shipping. I've been getting reports from users that have bought and are using the COMFAST CF-953AX and CF-951AX. Both are advertised as WiFi 6 AX1800 adapters but I now have several reports confirming that these adapter are WiFi 6E Tri Band adapters that are capable of 6 GHz, 5 GHz and 2.4 GHz channel operations. Of note: both adapter are reported to be single state which is good.

For what I think is the first time ever, the driver, mt7921u, made it into the mainline kernel before adapters were even available for purchase. Now the adapters are available for purchase and it is expected over the next few months that additional makers will supply additional adapters and many more retailers with carry the adapters. These adapters should be plug and play starting with the soon to be released Ubuntu 22.10 but you can upgrade kernel and firmware early if you want to do so.

Of note: The adapters are advertised as dual band wifi 6 capable adapters but numerous reports have confirmed that they are WiFi 6E tri band adapters, at least on Linux. Could it be that we have not only caught up with Windows as far as USB WiFi adapters are concerned but we have passed it?
