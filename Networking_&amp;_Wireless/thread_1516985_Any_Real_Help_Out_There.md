### Post by QIII on 2010-06-24
Microsoft hasn't fixed the problem.  That's a common misconception.

The OEMs have made sure that their hardware works seemlessly with Windows.

I see that this is your second post.

Would you care to post your exact problem, your hardware configuration, what you have done or tried to do?  A "step by step" for your particular issue may be of no use to another with a similar problem.  Although similar, it is probably not exactly the same.

With regard to "lingo", you get Windows "lingo" when you go to a Windows help forum.  It is just a "lingo" you are familiar with.

Many users, such as I, have no issues with wireless connections.  For me, it is not a problem.  We don't know how many users are having no problems.  We only see those who are.  People do not come here to complain when things go right.  So our perception of what is "wrong" is based on an unknown fraction of an unknown user base.

This is, nonetheless, a problem for each individual user experiencing the problem.  Some solutions work fine for some.  They may not for others.  The problem, again, is that some hardware is more easily addressed than other hardware.  But that is an over-simplification.  There is a complex interaction between the components of any given rig, and there are innumerable combinations of hardware used by a huge number of users.

So you see "partial" answers.

Is someone figuring it out?  One person - No.  The community as a whole is, however.  What you see going on in this forum is the community's effort to address issues.  Can we really expect some person, purely out of the goodness of his heart, to find a "once over the world" solution to a difficult problem while eating peanut butter and feeding his children cat food because nobody is paying him to spend eight hours a day solving the problem?

Please understand that I am not poo-pooing the issue.  It is real.  But we have to consider this rationally.

Something you can do to take part in the solution is to report bugs through launchpad.net.  Before you post a new bug, search through similar bugs to see if you are having exactly the same issue with exactly the same hardware.  If you are, just add a "This affects me too" so that the developers can get a handle on how many are affected.

If it is absolutely unique, start a new bug report.  But be very detailed in your description and give the developers as much information as possible to help them.

This is a community.  Take part.  You have the opportunity to help.
