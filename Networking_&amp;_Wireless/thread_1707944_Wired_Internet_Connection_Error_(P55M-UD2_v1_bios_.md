### Post by Henry Hollingworth on 2011-03-16
Greetings all,
Recently I installed ubuntu on my desktop computer (I am on my laptop now). The install was successful and detected the internet connection during setup, after install all was well. The next day I came home and turned on my computer I was extremely disturbed to find that Ubuntu was not detecting my wired ethernet connection. At first I blamed my mother vacuuming however upon further investigation I found the cable was indeed plugged in and the router was on. I then tested whether the cable was the problem by plugging it into this laptop. The connection worked. I re-installed Ubuntu and the connection wasn't found. Reformatted my HDD and installed XP, upon installing the driver for the integrated network card in my motherboard (P55M-UD2) the network icon spastically changed from "acquiring network address" to "no connection". I googled for hours and came across a script to change the r8169 driver in ubuntu but this resulted in me integrated network card not being detected. I require assistance!

### Post by Henry Hollingworth on 2011-03-16
Dw guys, fixed the problem. Unplugging the power for 20mins then rebooting fixed the problem. I suspect many other people are having this issue and a notice containing this fix should be stickied ;)
