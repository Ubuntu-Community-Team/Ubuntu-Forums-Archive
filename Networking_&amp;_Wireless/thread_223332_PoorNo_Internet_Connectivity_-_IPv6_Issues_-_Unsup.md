### Post by coffeecat on 2006-07-26
This thread is a good idea, but the information you are requesting is incomplete. What is needed is both the hardware **and** the date/version of the firmware. And please be more specific. What is the Linksys hardware you are describing and what is the **exact** model number?

Last year this issue very nearly made me give up on Linux and I am sure the Linux world has lost many new users because of it. I was using a D-Link G604T wireless router and (eventually) I found that disabling IPv6 or assigning IP addresses manually rather than using DHCP were effective workarounds. Then I replaced the June 2004 firmware (which the router had been shipped with) with a version from August 2005 and the problem went away.

And lest people think Linksys hardware will be problematic, I can confidently say, 'not necessarily so'. I obtained a Linksys WAG354G wireless router last April and now use it most of the time. I keep the D-Link as a back-up. The Linksys has worked faultlessly with Linux since then and clearly IPv6 is not a problem with it.
