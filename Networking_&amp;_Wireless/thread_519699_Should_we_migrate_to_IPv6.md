### Post by rockin_goliath on 2007-08-07
I read an interesting article in the Christian Science Monitor about how apparently we (as in all computer users everywhere) are running out of IP addresses. Since IPv4 only supports 4 digit addresses, it is possible that we could run out of combinations for addresses within 3 years. This might lead to people buying more addresses than they need and hording them, and  selling them for outrageous prices. Migrating all internet software to IPv6, which supports 6 digit addresses and thus trillions of combinations, would solve this problem. 

I'm pretty proficient at this point with Linux and Ubuntu, but I am a total NOOB when it comes to networking and this kind of stuff. Do you think that this article has any merit? If so, would the Ubuntu community be able to put the first foot forward to saving the world?

### Post by Synflux on 2007-08-07
I wouldn't worry about it too much at the moment. 

There's a few ways that the problem has been side-stepped:

1. Private addressing. 
2. NAT/PAT- network address translation/port address translation.
3. Subnetting.
etc.

And then there's IPv6 to migrate to in the future. You can run v6 alongside v4.
