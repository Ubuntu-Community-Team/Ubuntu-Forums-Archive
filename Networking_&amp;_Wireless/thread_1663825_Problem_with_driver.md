### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-01-10
Ubuntu Studio is not designed to have networking as it affects the working of some music programs that require real time processing. This is my understanding even though I have not explained it with the correct expressions. I do know that Network Manager is not installed by default. And also things have improved from version 10.4 to 10.10. I know this because I have installed both versions of Ubuntu Studio. I have only managed to get wireless working not ethernet. which is strange as wired networking is usually the easiest to get working. You will not see a network icon. There will not be any indication that you have a network connection.

I am not running Ubuntu Studio at the moment. I cannot talk you through the steps that I took to get wireless working. I will try to help you along the way.

1) Add to Panel. Look for Network Monitor. This will put an icon of 2 computer screens in the panel which flash or blink when there is network activity. That is useful.

2) After making changes a reboot is needed. Sometimes more than one reboot is needed. I have had Ubuntu Studio load without wireless activating and it took two reboots for wireless to activate. It sounds weird but I think that the system sometimes boots too quickly for wireless to activate. Just a guess.

3) Under System>Administration you will see something like Networking or Network tools. This utility works differently than the one in standard Ubuntu 10.10. So, I am trying to remember what I did and I may get things wrong.

You can either select your device from a drop down list and click configure. OR you will be given a dialog that lists possible connections which you can click to activate, select to configure. One dialog box  has a shield icon at the bottom that you need to click to allow you to make changes. Your will need to enter your login password. When you have made the changes, click the shield icon again to save the changes, to prevent further changes being made.

This is all that I can think of at the moment. Please remember that I am working from memory. If I was actually using Ubuntu Studio at this moment I could test out my advice to see if it is accurate. The best I can give your right now is pointers along the way.

Regards.
