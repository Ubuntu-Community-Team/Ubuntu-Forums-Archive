How to do wireless without wireless drivers...

or

How to save yourself some gray hair.

The internet is littered with messages from people seeking help with wireless problems.  One of the biggest areas that cause problems is driver support.  Wireless driver support is less than perfect for non-windows systems (and isn't perfect for some windows systems).  Here is how I handle the issue:

I hope this will be of use to some folks.  I have never used Windows as my primary operating system in my home or home office.  I have a lot of practice being careful about what hardware I buy.  My solution to wireless predates my moving to Ubuntu.  When I moved to Ubuntu last year I did not have a single wireless related problem and still haven't.  Here is a look at my solution:

For Desktop or Server systems (not needing mobility) I use hardware such as the following:

D-Link DWL-2100AP (in client mode)
[http://www.dlink.com/products/?pid=292](http://www.dlink.com/products/?pid=292)

For Notebook systems:

D-Link DWL-G730AP (in client mode)
[http://www.dlink.com/products/?pid=346](http://www.dlink.com/products/?pid=346)

Note: I'm not advocating specific hardware.  Many vendors sell hardware with similar capabilities.  It just happens that this hardware works very well for me.

Things to think about:

When I moved to Ubuntu last year I did not have to change a single wireless setting.  Not one.

With this type of setup Ubuntu does not even know that it is running with a wireless setup.  The above devices plug into the NIC and are controlled via a web interface.  I've been careful for years to select NIC's that are very very well supported by non-windows operating systems.

I'm not advocating anyone throw perfectly good hardware in the trash.  I'm simply pointing out that it is possible to do wireless networking without PCI cards, USB devices and PCMCIA cards (all of which require drivers which may or may not be available).

Something to think about.
