### Post by Topsiho on 2017-03-09
I assume your Probook is a laptop, and that it has wifi of course.
If it's a brand new model maybe there is not a driver for it's wifi yet, otherwise Ubuntu would have recognized it, and loaded the correct driver.
In that case you'll have to wait until a driver becomes available, in one of the near future updates.

You could also buy a wifi USB-stick, which will very probably work with Ubuntu, if the wifi chip in it is not the newest of the newest.

Maybe other people in the forum can tell you more on this.

Ubuntu is very good in recognizing older hardware, but it may not recognize the newest hardware 
Windows is very good in knowing the new hardware (the manufacturers eager to provide the drivers for it), but does not know legacy hardware (the manufacturers eager for you to buy the newer hardware),
That's what I think.
