### Post by kaginken on 2008-02-11
Which is it?  Do you want to connect the PC with a wireless card, or just replace your telephone (modem) connection?  I'll assume you meant both.  Do you currently have a non-wireless network card?  That is the recommended method for a stationary PC as it's a more reliable connection.

Why are you trying to connect the PC wirelessly?  It must be that you either have no way to run a cat5 ethernet cable to the PC, or possibly you don't have a ethernet network card and already have a wireless network card?  Or maybe even you have free wireless from an unsecured hot spot from a nearby neighbor?

If you want to go wireless, then you'll need a wireless NIC (network interface card).  It will have a little antenna sticking out of it and should work for you just fine.  Pick one up on Ebay for $20.  I'd recommend a slightly older model as they'll be better supported by linux than the latest (ie wireless n) cards, not to mention the extra help you'll find online if you're troubleshooting it.  If it's been around awhile, chances are there's more info online about it.  I seem to have the best luck with linksys wireless b cards - older, cheaper, and lots of good support.

Hope this helps!   rock on  :guitar:
