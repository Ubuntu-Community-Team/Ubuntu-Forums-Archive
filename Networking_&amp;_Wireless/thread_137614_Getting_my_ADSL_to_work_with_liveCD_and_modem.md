### Post by Herman on 2006-03-15
G'day, Skythe, I'm with Aussie Bigpond too and I have an alcatel speedtouch 530 adsl modem. 
We have five computers here now, and it doesn't matter if we go through the router or just plug directly into the adsl modem, they all just go on the internet automatically, no problem.  
We have never had to muck around answering any questions like phone number, username, password since the first time we set the connection up.
One thing we do here that's different from what you are doing though, we are using our ethernet cards for all networking and internet. Our USB ports are only used for printers and other periferals.
Would your sister be upset if you unplugged her for a few minutes and used your ethernet port to connect to the adsl modem? When we got our adsl modem, Bigpond included both a USB cable and an ethernet cable so we can use either one, whichever suits us.
I highly recommend a router if you can get one at all. I live in a remote area and I only go to town once or twice a year, but I wouldn't be without my router now that I finally have one. They are not that dear for how much good they do, if you can possibly afford one. I think mine was around $120 or so. It's great! Most routers have a 'hardware firewall' with them too which is very effective at keeping out unsolicited connection attempts from outside sources. :-D (Herman)

I think herman has hit it on the head.  It also seems to be more difficult to setup a USB DSL Modem from within linux, and the configurations can start getting kinda wierd.  I run ADSL here through SBC and i hooked up my router and once i got it setup the first time, i haven't had to configure since.  I just set some network settings on install and away i go.  If you can get ahold of a router, in the US they run less than 50 bucks in some cases, it will save a lot of headache and trouble.  They also take less than 10 minutes to setup and are extremely advantageous for multiple computer connections.  Wireless routers even run in the vicinity of 50-120 dollars and can allow for additional connections with computers more than a few feet away.
