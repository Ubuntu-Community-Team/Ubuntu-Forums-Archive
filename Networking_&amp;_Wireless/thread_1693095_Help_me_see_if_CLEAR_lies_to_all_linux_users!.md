### Post by Stray Wolf on 2011-02-22
The first time I heard of CLEAR wireless was last summer when a sales rep came by my office with a pitch and some literature.  We were discussing the "on the go" product CLEAR offers.  One of the first things I asked him (I thought it would stifle his sales attempt) was if CLEAR had 4G Linux drivers.  Instead, without skipping a beat, he said "Yes, we do work on linux".  Stifling my attempt to stifle him.  Over the next several months I called CLEAR four more times and each time got a different rep that answered my question about Linux drivers the same way.  Immediately, as if it was common knowledge.  "Yes, you just install the drivers from the USB.".  So, I ordered the "on the go" product and received it within 24 hours.  Guess what?  No Linux drivers.  Not a big deal if I had only spoken with one rep who made a mistake.  But I talked to multiple reps.  It's not a mistake.  CLEAR has their default setting on "LIE".  That can't just be the sales reps.  Management must have a hand in this.  So, do me a favor if you have some free time.  Call CLEAR.  Ask them if the "on the go" product has Linux drivers.  Record it if you want.  See for yourself and pass your story on to others and on here.  I have a feeling you'll all get lied to.  Seems to be what CLEAR does.  Lie for a sale.
