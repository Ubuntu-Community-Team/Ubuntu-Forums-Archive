### Post by ZeroError on 2009-04-30
I recently installed Ubuntu 8.10 on an old ACi 2700C Notebook laptop with a SiS Plug and Play BIOS from 1997.

The installation went well and several aspects of my laptop have actually improved; I also enjoy using it a lot more. But I have a problem. The wireless connection rarely connects, and when it does it doesn't last.

When I found out the drivers for my wireless card were already installed (Using lsmod) I immediately tried to connect - and it worked. It then worked again the next time I connected. But afterward the wireless wouldn't connect. Then it would ask me for the WEP key several times over before connecting. 

I then upgraded to 9.04 and I can now occasionally "connect" but the Network Manager icon shows 0% connection and nothing can actually use the internet.

I've tried several things:

[LIST]
[*]Using a guide to connect via Terminal (The interface is eth1)
[*]Changing the DHCP routes (Which worked once)
[*]Binding to the router's MAC address (Didn't work)
[/LIST]
I currently also have PCMCIAutils 0.15 and compat-wireless installed.
dmesg shows I have the Lucent/Agere 7.28 firmware.

I would appreciate any ideas or links you can give me to help. I really want to continue to use Ubuntu, but without internet access it seems very difficult.

Thank you in advance. If you need anything more, just say.
