### Post by mips on 2005-12-02
The way internet connectivity works in general for home users is in an asymetrical fashion. Most people download way more than they upload therefore you get these 3Mb/768Kb ratios. You can almost look at it as a client/server model where most of the traffic only goes in one direction.

If you switch to Speakeasy at "your" metioned rates of 1.1Mb/1.1Mb you are effectively reducing your download speed by 2/3rds or 63% and increasing the time to download a file by the same margin. Fair enough, ISP's providers usually only gaurantee those speeds in the last mile between subscriber & dslam. The internet is to unpredictable to gaurantee anything beyond that point unless you have $$$.

I dont see any packages listed as 1.1/1.1 in this page,  [http://www.speakeasy.net/home/compare/](http://www.speakeasy.net/home/compare/)

Unless you are a business customer wanting to sign up for their SDSL (Symetrical DSL, both directions the same speed) ???

In order to answer your questions better answer these:
What are you currently paying for the Verizon service ?
What do you use the internet for (Music downloads, Video downloads, Program  downloads, Voice over IP phonecalls & chats etc) ?


The Speakeasy network looks pretty good an well designed imho. 

Why is it that you say they support Linux & open source ?
