### Post by NutmegCT on 2010-05-07
Mark - wiser minds than I should answer your question.  But here's my take on it:  the kernel has a "built in" wireless driver.  For some reason the Broadcom driver (installed based on your hardware config) has a problem with waking up after entering sleep mode.  So if Ubuntu suspends that driver from being active, the Ubuntu kernel driver keeps the wireless from going permanently catatonic.

Or something like that.

Glad it's working for you.
Tom
