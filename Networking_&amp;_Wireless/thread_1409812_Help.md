### Post by w0296094 on 2010-02-18
you have to go to a wired connection first. go to apt-get update( once you are on the internet with a wired connection). then check and see if there is a popup notification on the desktop. Otherwise, look for the drivers tab in ubuntu(cant remember where it is). 
If the drivers tab doesnt get any new drivers, reboot your computer. Once rebooted check the drivers tab again, and this time yyou will see a wireless driver.
Click activate, the driver will install. Once its finished reboot the computer again, once in ubuntu login, and after logging in and having your programs load, remove the ethernet cord, and double click on the connections icon in the desktop, 
you will have the option to connect to wireless networks, and just select it. After that, you should get wireless.

I'm not sure why it happens with wubi, having all that stuff in windows, but for some reason ubuntu wubi doesnt have the wirelless drivers loaded to the wubi installation.
