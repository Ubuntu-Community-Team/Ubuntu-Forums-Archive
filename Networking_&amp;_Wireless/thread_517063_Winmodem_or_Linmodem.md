### Post by _duncan_ on 2007-08-05
A linmodem is a winmodem that can be made to work in linux, usually through the effort of an unpaid volunteer willing to reverse engineer the workings of the windows-based driver.

Regardless of what you call 'em, a winmodem/linmodem is an *incomplete* modem that requires the operating system to handle part of the work of a *complete* hardware modem. That's why it's very difficult to get them to work with linux. Most winmodem manufacturers only have drivers for windows.

Majority of USB-based modems are winmodems. It's possible to get them to work in linux, but it's not going to be easy. I'm not familiar with the AOpen modem though.
