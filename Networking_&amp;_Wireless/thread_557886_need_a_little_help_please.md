### Post by noob12 on 2007-09-23
No, but as long as you know the keys, you can delete the keyring or move it aside to a different name and start over.

The keyring is stored under your home directory in the file ~/.gnome2/keyrings/default.keyring

I suggest you use the same password as your login password.  You can then automatically open your keyring at login by installing the **libpam-keyring** package.  This also gives you a tool to change the keyring password, but you need to know the old one.
