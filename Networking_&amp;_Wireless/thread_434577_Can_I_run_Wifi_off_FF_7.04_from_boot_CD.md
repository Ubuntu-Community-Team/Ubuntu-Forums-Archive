### Post by chili555 on 2007-05-06
You can! The ease or difficulty depends on whether your card is natively supported in Ubuntu or whether you need to install the Windows drivers with ndiswrapper.

If your card is natively supported, it should show up in Network Manager and connect as soon as you input your ESSID and any encryption details. All of this, obviously, will disappear when you reboot, however, it will give you a sense of how easy or difficult the transition to Feisty (7.04) might be.

If you judge by these forums, you'd assume that upgrading to Feisty is the ruination of wireless. However, remember the people for whom wireless works perfectly usually don't post here: 'my wireless works, what can I do to wreck it?'

In my case, I have two laptops, one running Edgy and one Feisty. Wireless works perfectly on both.
