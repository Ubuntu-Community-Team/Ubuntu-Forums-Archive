### Post by coffeecat on 2009-05-13
AAaaaarrrgh! The dreaded Thomson Speedtouch. :evil:

:wink:

Seriously, you don't want to be bothered setting that ghastly thing up in Linux. Seriously. Especially over the phone. Need convincing? Read this:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsbAdslModem/SpeedTouch](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UsbAdslModem/SpeedTouch)

OK. Now that I've convinced you, :p why am I so against the Speedtouch?

- It's not a proper modem at all. It's a sort-of adsl equivalent of a Winmodem. The CPU has to do all the work.

- Poor security. There's no built-in firewall.

- It's only designed for use with Windows.

- It works poorly even in Windows.

- It makes a good doorstop, which is about all it's good for.

Right, now that I've got that off my chest, how about a constructive suggestion? Try to convince your father to get himself a proper adsl modem-router. A wired-only one would cost about £20. A wireless one more, but since he's got a laptop he might appreciate the freedom that would give him. He doesn't need the more expensive N-standard routers. Several places are discounting the older 802.11g devices now. I picked up a remaindered wireless adsl modem-router + USB dongle pack in Maplins the other day for less than £30. Bargain.

Good luck! :)
