### Post by mikeczap on 2008-02-07
I actually like the Gnome Network Manager Network applet for Xubuntu (though it took me
forever to figure out how to run it under xfce; I'd forgotten how much I hate X's labrinthine,
multi-layered configurations!).  I am a bit flustered that there is no xfce-native equivalent
that I can find (but I'm still new to Ubuntu/Xubuntu), but I've found it to be pretty good at
managing WiFi connections (including WPA connections), with one odd exception:  in two
places I go frequently, they have Verizon-provided WiFi routers (I don't know what brand;
they're all-in-one DSL modems & routers with Verizon's label on them), and the NM Applet
will pick up their SSID's, and give me the chance to enter their WEP keys, but never connect
unless I manually build the connection profile from scratch.

Weird, but only a minor annoyance.
