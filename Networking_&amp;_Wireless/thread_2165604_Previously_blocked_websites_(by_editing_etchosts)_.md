### Post by InspiredIndividual on 2013-08-05
I used to block time-wasting websites by editing the /etc/hosts file, for example by adding the line
```
0.0.0.0 foo.com
```.
At times it was necessary to access such a website anyway. In this case I simply temporarily commented out the particular line - to immediate effect. This may not be the most intelligent way of blocking websites, but it was very easy and worked well for me for a long time.

Recently, changes to the /etc/hosts file only took effect after a reboot of my system, which was quite inconvenient. Therefore I decided to do away with the hack altogether and find a better solution. Hence, I restored /etc/hosts to the system default. However, this did not work as intended: I still cannot access any of the previously blocked websites, even after rebooting the system. For instance:

```
user@user:~$ ping foo.com
PING foo.com (127.0.0.1) 56(84) bytes of data.
64 bytes from localhost (127.0.0.1): icmp_req=1 ttl=64 time=0.021 ms

``` 

I have no clue how to fix this... I understand now that I was foolish to use a hack without deeper knowledge of what I was doing and you should by all means laugh at me for my stupidity, but nevertheless I hope that someone will be able to help me...
