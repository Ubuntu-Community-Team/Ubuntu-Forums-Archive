### Post by arch0njw on 2008-09-16
Over that distance, I'd highly recommend getting a spool of CAT5e from Home Depot (or similar), a box of network cable heads, and a crimping tool.  I've assembled a number of my own network cables, and it is pretty easy.  You can run that wire from your wireless router to a simple workgroup switch.

You can find very direct instructions online about how to crimp the head onto the cable.  The only thing to pay attention to is the wire colors -- you want to get those in the right order.  I recommend starting with a short cable and test it by replacing an existing patch cable to make sure you get the wiring order right.

### Post by adamovera on 2008-11-08
I found a perfect solution, Linksys makes a wireless bridge (WET200), it works perfectly & is amazing. It picks up my internet access wirelessly from my Linksys 54g2 wireless router in the other side of the house & has 5 ethernet ports for me to use in the garage. It didn't work right away but I called Linksys at like 3:00AM (for free) & a tech talked me through setup, it took only like 20 minutes. They were awesome, most places won't deal with you if your not on M$ or Apple, so I usually lie & say I'm on XP. They didn't care, they said that as long as I could find the way to configure my IP in my OS they'd just skip over the part where they tell windows users where to go & just gave me the settings. Great experience, I've had many, many wireless networking devices over the years & Linksys is top-shelf. I highly recommend WET200 to anybody that has a similar Home Office, garage/workshop type of situation & doesn't want to run wires.
