### Post by noob12 on 2007-08-07
Loading of the ipw3945 module isn't the problem in this boot.  That happens quickly.  Also, I was looking for any PCI issues in your boot.  Didn't see any.

I think that **eth1** is probably your 3945 device.   (Verify via **lshw -C network**).  Bringing up eth1 does look like it may be contributing.  That is certainly near one big jump in the timestamps.   

If this is a laptop, I'd suggest you remove the stanza (all lines) regarding eth1 from /etc/network/interfaces; this will (a) tell it not to try to configure the interface automatically at boot and (b) will tell NetworkManager to treat the device in "roaming mode".  Typically the wireless interface on a portable is configured later by NetworkManager or the like.

The other jump in the timestamps is starting up the CDROM on which you apparently have a UDF format disk for Sabayon.  Try taking that out.

Try rebooting after this.  I don't see anything else.
