### Post by LubbersLine on 2008-11-14
I am a nuube when it comes to Linux although I have many yrs exp in the Windows networking world. I have a Netgear MA521 and I have read many articles related to the MA521 needing the NDISWrapper driver to be applied before the PCMCIA card will work. I was not looking forward to attempting to install any additional drivers in the Linux world. I even found a link that listed all the cards that did and did not work out of the box with Linux. The MA521 is not supposed to work out of the box.

Well, guess what? I fresh installed Ubuntu 8.10 on a relatively old laptop (Gateway 450sx4) with the Netgear MA521 PCMCIA card installed during the os installation. With a few mouse clicks and learning a bit of how Ubuntu networking works I was able to enable the MA521. I am wirelessly on the internet as I type this. Sweet!
