### Post by coffeecat on 2010-01-13
> **darrelljon said:**
> Can anyone recommend a mobile broadband dongle for the UK that's friendly to Ubuntu or Linux generally?

I tried someone else's T-Mobile Huaweii (E220 iirc) in Karmic and it "just worked". It was autodetected in network manager and it took seconds only to go through the wizard and get connected.

Ditto with a Huaweii (K3565) Vodafone Top Up and Go dongle that I bought recently. In both cases I took the precaution to eject the pseudo-CD drive that had automounted when I plugged the device in, although this didn't seem to be necessary with the Vodafone on at least one occasion.

Also - I first activated the Vodafone in Vista (:oops:). I'm not sure what would happen if you tried to register it in Ubuntu. And, of course, the T-Mobile would have already been registered. 

One tip if you're going for Pre-pay rather than a contract. With all the networks except Vodafone, the pre-pay credit expires after only 30 days. With Vodafone, the credit lasts 6 months (or so). Which is why I went with Vodafone.

If you search around on the [Betavine]("http://www.betavine.net/home/main/home.html") site, you'll find some [software packages]("http://www.betavine.net/bvportal/resources/datacards/os/ubuntu") specifically for the Vodafone dongle. Although it doesn't mention Karmic specifically on that page, the vodafone-mobile-connect app works fine for me in Karmic, although it doesn't have all the fuctionality of the Windows version. You can simply rely on Network Manager, but the betavine software adds a little more.
