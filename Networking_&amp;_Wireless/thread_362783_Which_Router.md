### Post by gradedcheese on 2007-02-16
Indeed, a router couldn't care less about the OS installed on your PC.  When you buy one, throw out the cute setup CDs and such that it comes with and just plug it in.  Find out what it's default IP address is (usually 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1 and I have also seen .50 versions of those two) and then go to that IP address in your web browser.
