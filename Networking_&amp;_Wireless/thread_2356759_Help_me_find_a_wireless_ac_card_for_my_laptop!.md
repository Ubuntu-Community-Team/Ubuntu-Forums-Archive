### Post by chili555 on 2017-03-26
Note to searchers: If you plan to change the wireless card in your laptop, be cautious to investigate whitelisting: [http://goughlui.com/2014/08/02/laptop-wireless-card-whitelists-an-upgrade-nightmare/](http://goughlui.com/2014/08/02/laptop-wireless-card-whitelists-an-upgrade-nightmare/)

> At this time, it is known that some recent Lenovo, Toshiba, Dell, HP and Compaq follow this whitelisting practice. From my experience, it appears that Asus, **Acer**, and MSI don’t.Having given the necessary disclaimer, I have good luck with my Intel 7260. There are at least two versions and one is not AC capable. Research carefully.

Here is a recent test result about 30 ft. (~9.75 meters) from the router: [http://www.dslreports.com/speedtest/7109859](http://www.dslreports.com/speedtest/7109859)
