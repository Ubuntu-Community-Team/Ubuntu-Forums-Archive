### Post by ixmatal on 2010-09-12
I have a fresh install of 10.04lts, on a separate drive from the original 8.10lts I had running.

The issue seems to be not only initial tcp connections, but also bandwidth. Regardless of http, ftp, smtp, dns, or icmp, from the time I hit enter or click a link, it takes anywhere from 10 to 30 seconds for an initial response, and then it takes several minutes for a webpage to load - even something as simple as google.

The funny part is that running ping or trace, all of the times are 50-100ms, but the overall time is still several minutes. 

The WAN link is a 25Mb/s Qwest fiber optic dsl. the router is a linksys wrtg. The 3 other pc's on the lan are Windows XP Pro with various ethernet chips, and the 3 laptops are WinXP Home on wireless. There is a Solaris10 workstation, a Windows 2000 Pro workstation, and few miscellaneous RedHat boxes on a downstream switch as well. None of them have this issue - webpages load within 1-2 seconds - even yahoo.

There are -many- posts on multiple forums regarding trouble with the built-in realtek chips and driver. I have rolled back and forward on the kernel rev and r8169 driver build with no change. I have disabled ipv6 and mdns, no change. I have also tested an Intel express100 and a Dlink 548 (via-rhine chipset) with the realtek disabled, no change. So I am not inclined to believe it is an issue that is specific to the realtek.

Not being terribly familiar with the linux tcp stack, I am not sure of what I can dig into to troubleshoot this any further.

### Post by ixmatal on 2010-09-15
Hello again,

-partial resolution-

I was browsing through the Realtek support website and found that they provide a different driver for the 8168b chip, and this driver was last updated just 2 weeks ago.

I downloaded the driver and ran through the compile/config/initramfs cycle.

The bandwidth issue seems to be completely resolved. DSLreports showed 5800kB/s on consecutive runs and webpages now load in 2-3 seconds.

Pings now get an immediate response too.

There is still the odd issue of the initial lag in making tcp connections, it affects http/ftp/dns/smtp equally. The delay is still 10-30 seconds.

I am working on tests now to determine if this affects both LAN and WAN traffic.
