Ah ... the dreaded  tplink TL-WR1043ND.  That explains a bunch.  The specifications for that router are awesome, but I've never had mine stay up more than 24 hrs and keep network packets flowing.  I'm serious - I have THE EXACT SAME ROUTER!!!!  What a piece of crap. I didn't realize how bad the router HW and firmware were until after the 30 day window. BTW, it has GigE ports, so why isn't your GigE card talking GigE to it?  CAT5 or less cable, not CAT5e?

So .... I'd get a different router. You'll need that anyway - I did.  Ended up with a Buffalo running dd-wrt, but I'm not good at suggesting routers besides something that runs pfsense. That's what I've deployed in businesses.

If the cable is only CAT5, you could disable autonegotiate and force 100base-tx. Ethtool can do that or there are driver settings. [http://docs.oracle.com/cd/E19248-01/819-7573-13/5_LP_driverparams.html](http://docs.oracle.com/cd/E19248-01/819-7573-13/5_LP_driverparams.html) says to use ForceSpeedDuplex=7 in the driver module settings.  might be easier to use ethtool - just put the command into the /etc/rc.local file.  This is a complete crap shoot. Don't know if it will help or not.

Did you try swapping the cable ports from each machine to the router? Ports are known to go bad on all devices - routers and switches.  A $20 GigE switch connected to the router can get around a lack of ports with negligible network latency. I have multiple cheap switches connected in this way.

Perhaps reinstalling the e1000 driver?  I'd also force an fsck on the / partition at the next reboot.

### Post by TheFu on 2014-07-20
Those details are vastly different from what you've shown before. You can ping the router! THAT is huge! **Can you ping internet IPs?** Does **dig google.com** return something?

We seem to be going around in circles.  It pings, it doesn't, it pings, it doesn't.  The questions to answer,** in orde**r, are:
* Can it ping the router by IP?  No? computer, cable or router issue.
* Can it ping someplace on the internet by IP? No?  Router or gateway setting or ISP issue.  Do other internet IPs ping? Try a few that you know work.
* Can it lookup domain names on the internet?  No? DNS.  Could be an issue with the computer setting, router setting or ISP.
* Can it reach popular websites?  No?  Proxy issue.

So ... if you work through these tests and questions, in order, the location of the error is known. If there are mutliple issues, start at the top and fix that until it is 100% solved, then move down 1 level and fix the next, and the next.
