### Post by Mattttttt on 2009-01-09
Hello everyone :-)  This is my first posting as I am a bit of a newbie to Ubuntu.  I have been using it successfully and relatively trouble-free for about 2 months now and I like it very much. :D  That was until yesterday, however...  :(

I was noticing that every so often the whole system would completely hang - no mouse, no keys, nothing.  It's never done this to me before and no updates had taken place just beforehand so I thought this was rather odd.  :confused:
It did it to me a few times (during live streaming of the world darts too - I was not pleased!) once with the wireless connection having a bit of a fit at the same time...  Ah-ha!!  I thought, and investigated the problem.

Upon dismantling my trusty friend, the Acer TM290, I found that my wireless network card had worked itself loose and was sitting at a jaunty angle in it's respective connector.  That'll be the badger! I thought, and reseated it snugly.  I then reassembled the laptop and fired it up again, quite confident that the system would just start up and function properly once again. :) But no. :(

Once Ubuntu had started up again, it was complaining of having no connection.  When I click on the icon my wireless network is in the list with an adequate signal strength but for some reason it will no longer connect to it.  Usually when I connected to it before, the icon showed one green light and the little blue thingy swirling round it, then a second green light only a matter of seconds later, then the signal strength icon a matter of seconds after that, showing that it had successfully connected.  Now I only get the first green light and the blue swirly thing then it times out saying the network was disconnected, upsetting me greatly.  :(

I have tried another hard disc in the machine which had a working installation of Ubuntu on it and that did the same.
The other PCs in the house connect to the router ok.
I've also tried connecting to the router directly with an ethernet cable - the wired network works fine but still the wireless one doesn't want to play.

So...  Now what do I do?  I'm stuck...  :confused:

I have an Acer TM290 with an Intel PRO/Wireless 2200BG network adaptor, connecting to a Belkin wireless router.
