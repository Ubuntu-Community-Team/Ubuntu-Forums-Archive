### Post by toastermm on 2010-06-16
Yesterday, I was trying to network a linux and windows computer together and played around with Samba.  I edited the /etc/samba/smb.conf and the /etc/nsswitch.conf files.  Soon I found my wireless went out.  Disconnected.  As soon as I click on the wireless network, it tries to connect and then says "Disconnected" again.

So I undid the changes to the two files (saved in a backup file) and still no wireless.  I know for a fact it's the computer because I'm typing this on a laptop that is connected to the wireless network.

There are several new processes running in the background now that I have attributed to networking.  They include gvfs and gvfsd processes, that I attribute to the networking.  I have a suspicion that the desktop now prefers the network cable (which is unplugged now) over the wireless.  How to get this back?  Not sure.

I tried to find similar problems out there, but most problems with wireless disconnecting seem to be gradual disintegration of signal, where this is an immediate disconnect and I can't get anywhere.
