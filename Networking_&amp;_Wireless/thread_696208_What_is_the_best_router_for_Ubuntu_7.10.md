### Post by Herman on 2008-02-13
I love my D-Link 524 router, it has a great user interface with all the options I need.
It's easy to access the user interface from Linux with a firefox web browser, just type the router's URL (192.168.0.1) into Firefox and type your username and password and you're in.
It's fully featured. I can control which computers in my LAN are allowed on the internet and at what time of day and which days of the week. 
I have my router mail logs to my email address showing any suspicious connection attempts, (it has a great firewall).
It's also a DHCP server too, so I can use [SSH Network]("http://users.bigpond.net.au/hermanzone/p11.htm") and still leave all my operating systems set on DHCP.
I can set up port forwarding easily and once it's set up I can turn it on or off easily for each individual computer.
They have a good support site, I was able to upgrade my firmware for it from their internet site without a hitch.

I vote for D-Link because I know it's good.

I would be interested to learn more about other brands to see what they can do, but I know D-Link works well for me. My D-Link is not even their newest or fanciest model either, but it's fine for what I want to do with it.

Regards, Herman :)

### Post by Melcar on 2008-02-14
Any router will do really.  They don't need any software to function, and most have web based configuration utilities (Firefox handles them just fine).  Linksys routers are crap, but that's from my experience with them.  I'm happy with my Airlink that I got from Fry's for $30.  D-Link and Buffalo are also good.  
The wireless adapters are the ones you have to watch.  I prefer Airlink and D-Link adapters, since they are very easy to set up with ndiswrapper, and most (if not all) of them are supported natively by Ubuntu.
