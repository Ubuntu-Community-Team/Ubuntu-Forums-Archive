### Post by earthpigg on 2009-12-27
i found this [here]("http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/how-to-easily-share-your-wireless-connection-in-ubuntu-9-10/").

a very simple and very handy little trick that i had not previously been aware of that lets you connect Computer B (which lacks wifi) to the internet by plugging it into Computer A (which has wifi) using an ethernet cable.

> On your top panel, beside your clock and your system icons, you’ll see your network indicator. When you’re connected to wireless, it features bars telling you the stength of your network connection. Right-click this, then click “Edit Connections.”

...

In the window you just opened, make sure you’re looking at the “Wired” tab. Then select the “Auto eth0” connection and click “Edit.”

...

Click the “IPv4 Settings” tab. Beside the word “Method:” there is a drop-down menu that allows you to pick the manner in which your Ethernet device connects to the network. Select the “Shared to other computers” setting.

Restart your computer. Assuming everything applied correctly, you should now be able to connect any computer to the Internet via your laptop’s [Computer A as i called it above] Ethernet port.

the website linked above has nifty pictures and whatnot.
