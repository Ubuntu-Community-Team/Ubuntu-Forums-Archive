### Post by RandomJoe on 2007-02-22
First, your NAT rules are in a different table from the default - which is what you are looking at.  To see the NAT rules, use:
```
iptables -t nat -L
```

Second, it appears you are using the wrong port in your rules.  You have 8080 in your NAT rules, but your proxy log says it's on 3128 (the default port for Squid).  Perhaps just changing  8080 to 3128 is all you need?

I would also advise you look into beefing up your iptables to be a more proper firewall (if it is in fact your firewall) as the rules you have currently are going to leave any services (such as Squid) running on that machine world-accessible.  Especially since it appears your iptables-save ruleset shows any packets coming in from the 'net on port 80 will be redirected and sent to Squid as well.
