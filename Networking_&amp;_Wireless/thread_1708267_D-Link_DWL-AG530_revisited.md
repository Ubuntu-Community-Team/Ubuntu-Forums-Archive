### Post by Objekt on 2011-03-16
I will soon receive a donated D-Link DWL-AG530 wireless NIC card.  Is there any way at all to use one of these under Ubuntu?

I searched these forums and found a couple of threads about this card, but they were all quite old, the most recent from 2006.  Has anything changed in the last few years with regard to this NIC?

Quick summary of the potential problem: D-Link doesn't offer any Linux drivers, and from reviews I've read, even the Windows drivers were flaky.  I don't know to what extent drivers are strictly necessary for the card to work.  Most of my PC's devices (disk controllers, and so on) worked from the beginning, without my having to install anything, so I'm hoping that will happen in this case as well.

What kind of shenanigans will I have to put up with to use this card with Ubuntu?  Or is it a doomed effort from the start?

### Post by Objekt on 2011-03-19
update: Got the DWL-AG530 working in Windows 7, despite MS saying it is "incompatible."

Wonder if it would be possible to capture the Windows 7 driver and use it with ndiswrapper or something similar?  I once had to do that with the wireless card on a family member's Toshiba laptop, to get wireless networking under Ubuntu 8.10.

update2:
The DWL-AG530 worked fine in a Live CD session of Ubuntu 10.04.  So I guess there are no more problems using older wireless NICs in Ubuntu.
