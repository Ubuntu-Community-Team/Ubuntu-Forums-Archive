### Post by chrisinspace on 2008-09-18
I'm so pleased with my new card, I just had to post some info in the forum.  I'm rebuilding an old HP laptop with Linux for my mom.  I actually used Mint, but it's the Ubuntu-based version, running XFCE.  I decided to go with Mint because I heard it came with all of the non-free software basically preconfigured.  That was important for me because I live a few hours away and I don't want to have to try to talk my mom through troubleshooting Linux over the phone.

Anyway, the laptop didn't have wireless, so I had to look for a PCMCIA card.  I didn't want to spend $50 - $60 for a brand name 802.11g card and I only had a couple of days to get everything together so I didn't have time for shipping.  I wanted a card in the $20 - $30 range that wouldn't take me hours to configure and flake out when my mom was trying to use it.  I went to my local MicroCenter and bought a LevelOne WPC-0300 (version 5.0).  I saw a couple of Level One cards on lists people had made of cards that worked with Linux, so I thought this was a good choice.  I completely expected, however, to have to fiddle with it for a while.  That wasn't the case.  I slid it into the slot, powered up the laptop, and when I got the desktop, received a message that Ubuntu was using a new restricted driver.  I picked my my ssid out of the list, entered my wireless key, and was online.

If you're looking for a good PCMCIA wireless G card, I had great luck with this one.  PS - I'm not working for or taking commission from LevelOne...I'm just posting this because when I searched the forums for success stories, all I could find was people having issues.  I hope this helps someone out.
