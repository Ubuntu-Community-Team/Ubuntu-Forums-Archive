Mba7ethThere are many places on the Web that offer practice quizes and materials for you to study from -- for free.  That said, and since this is the first exam you will taking from Cisco, it is not a bad idea to actually invest in a CCNA net simulator -- either virutal (as in, leased, hosted web space, where you can test your skills on actual equipment, done via the Net -- or physical (eBay is a great place to buy some used 2601 routers and a switch).  You should most definitely go buy yourself this book: [http://www.amazon.com/Cards-Practice-Self-Study-640-801-Second/dp/1587200791?tag2=gp04-20](http://www.amazon.com/Cards-Practice-Self-Study-640-801-Second/dp/1587200791?tag2=gp04-20)

Cisco equipment is expensive, yes.  But the payoff you will gain from hands-on experience (either in a lab or at your own terminal via leased access space) will more than pay for itself when you get your first network admin job.

If you still feel shaky a few weeks before you take the exam, my recommendation would be to hold off for now, study up, feel confident about your skills and then reschedule a exam time -- you will be glad you did. :-)  It's a much better feeling going into the exam feeling like you can "ace it" rather than feeilng like you're just going to "get by."

If you're going to take the 640-801 (1 test, as opposed to 640-821 / 640-811 dual test), then be sure you a comfortable and familiar with each of the following ([http://www.cisco.com/web/learning/le3/current_exams/640-801.html):]("http://www.cisco.com/web/learning/le3/current_exams/640-801.html%29:")

**Planning & Designing**[LIST]

[*]Select an appropriate routing protocol based on user requirements
[*]Design a simple internetwork using Cisco technology

[*]Choose WAN services to meet customer requirements[/LIST]**Implementation & Operation**[LIST]

[*]Utilize the OSI model as a guide for systematic network troubleshooting
[*]Perform LAN and VLAN troubleshooting

Mba7ethRead my "EDIT" above and at the very least, do some studying online, everyday for the next three weeks! (if you still have the intention to take the exam in 3 weeks).  Again, if you still feel shaky, my best recommendation is to put it off for now, continue to study, and then reschedule when you feel more confident.

As for "free simulators", you can probably find them on the Net -- don't know their names though...I've only used authorized, purchased material in the past...and I have my own practice lab at home when I need to "brush up" on materials and need a "hands-on" environment and setting.

The CCNA Flascards book I mentioned in my first post is an EXCELLENT tutor and won't set you back all that much.  You can buy used version on Amazon for something like $25 US

I don't recommend or frankly condone the downloading of p2p softwares...they are most of the time outdated....and....you haven't paid for them.  Someone went to the trouble to actually create them and they had the expertise to do so...so, when it comes to net sim's, it's best to cough up the bucks.  One good piece of software that I've used the past is called Cisco CCNA Network Simulator, put out by Boson.  Trust me man, getting the right study materials is KEY to passing Cisco exams.

If you still need totally "free", then I suggest you type in "free ccna " and you will get an enormous amount of information that pops up in the Google drop-down.

Again, good luck!! :cool:
