### Post by Insane_Homer on 2009-07-13
I've had my Magic for just over a week now, Android is one slick OS. My only gripe is the battery life at the moment. It's pretty poor, especially with wireless on. But that may be due to some of the apps I'm running too so not entirely nailed it down yet.

google apps sync is awesome. Cal, mail and Contacts all great. I've not needed to sync tasks and it's not part of the native 'sync'. however there are more than a few task apps from the Market place that are free. I've not come across any that sync with google tasks, but I've not really looked.

edit: just found gtasx which is a small wrapper for google tasks - seems to work rather well but looks like it will require a wireless or 3g connection to access the web page for the tasks.

edit 2: Astrid is the best of the task apps I've come across and sync with Remember the Milk, as do a number of other apps.

### Post by Insane_Homer on 2009-07-14
It's all setup to auto sync over 2G/3G or wireless.

Calendar, GMail and google contacts all sync seamlessly. When I first started it, it went through a wizard that asked me for my gmail details and it did the rest!

I wouldn't worry too much about the 3rd party applications, they are what make Android great. ### Post by CadetD on 2009-07-29
There is no manual step involved in synching. 
The simplest way is to create (if you don't already have one) a GMail account with the same name as your Google Apps e-mail address. 

So if you G-Apps e-mail is [email]foo@myDomain.com[/email], you should create a Google Account with the same name. Or if you have a Gmail account, you&#314;l have to reconfigure it to login with your G-Apps account.

Not sure about the tasks as I've never used them.
