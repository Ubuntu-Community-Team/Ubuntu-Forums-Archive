### Post by BastardNamban on 2009-05-06
I've researched some more, and again linux has ended up teaching me something new. I find things out now due to problems out of necessity in Linux where windows never made me think twice. It's kind of annoying, ok, very annoying, but I find I'm learning much more about computers now.

The difference in price is due to card type, the older card bus type having almost half the bandwidth capacity of the newer type, express card. Express card modems are more expensive due to this, and being faster- using an added USB 2.0 port connection internally in addition to the older card bus PCI connection too.

But as far as N Band itself, and it's marked "drafts", it's still not clear to me if they are indeed faster, more future proof, or what.
