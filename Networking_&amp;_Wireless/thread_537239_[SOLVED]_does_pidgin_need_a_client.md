### Post by Zorael on 2007-08-28
Well, Pidgin is a client that supports many protocols (ICQ, MSN, AIM, IRC, etc), so if you don't have any such accounts it can't connect to anything. :) It doesn't have a protocol of its own, per se.

Naturally, you don't *have* to have one account of each protocol, just the one(s) you want (to use).

### Post by spd106 on 2007-08-28
Pidgin (formerly Gaim) is the client, you need to register an account on a particular service to use that network. The wonderful thing about Pidgin is that it supports multiple netoworks, so you can talk to your AIM friends and your MSN friends from one client.

I recommend that you follow the links on this page in the help docs wiki [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InternetAndNetworking](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/InternetAndNetworking).
