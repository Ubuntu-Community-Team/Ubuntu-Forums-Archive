### Post by grahammechanical on 2011-02-18
The first thing that I would like to say is this: You seem to be stating two problems. Which one do you want help with? Connecting at n speeds and not just a g speeds. Or, just getting a connection?

If you are being asked for a password it is because you need a password and you are giving the wrong one. Is this your own wireless modem/router that you are trying to connect to? Do not use your login password. It is the wrong one. You need the pass phrase or wireless key that the modem/router has printed on a label stuck on the bottom.

You also need to have to the right kind of encryption set. If the modem needs WPA-PSK and not WEP then you must set the connection to the same encryption method.

You may indeed have a wireless adapter in your laptop/netbook that works at b/g/n speeds but what is the specification of the modem? b/g or b/g/n?
