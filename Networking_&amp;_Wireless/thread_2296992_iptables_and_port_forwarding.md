### Post by Doug S on 2015-09-30
Hi and welcome to Ubuntu forums,

With a quick review, I don't immediately see what is wrong, but I do have some suggestions that might help investigate / debug:

. List the packets counters to help with understanding the paths packets take. i.e. "sudo iptables -v -x -n -L" and "sudo iptables -t nat -v -x -n -L" or "sudo iptables-save -c"

.  Make use of logging to further help with understanding the paths packets take. (Just be careful not to flood the log file, although even then I will sometimes log everything for a few minutes.)

. Use tcpdump (or wireshark, if you prefer) to obtain information at the packet level. Useful to help determine if issues are in the sending path or the return path or whatever.

. Currently you have a default policy of ACCEPT on your FORWARD and OUTPUT chains, so you don't need any specific ACCEPT rules. However, work towards a default policy of DROP on those chains, by specifically dealing with every possible use case.

. You didn't mention eth0 in your description, so I don't understand the eth0 references in your rule set.

. Eventually, recommend you add some security minded rules to your rule set, to try to deter hackers.
