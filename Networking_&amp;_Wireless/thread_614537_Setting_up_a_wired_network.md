### Post by Giradman on 2007-11-16
Plenty available on the web, e.g. [HERE]("http://www.pcstats.com/articleview.cfm?articleID=1429"); also check the MS VISTA sites; basically, there are two options in connecting the computers together (not considering yet the different OS installed):  1) router; or 2) ICS (Internet Connection Sharing).  My personal choice (which I've done several times over the years) is to use a wireless router - currently I have a Dell DT running Windows XP - my cable modem is hooked to a Linksys 'wireless' router, which is attached to the computer - I have 2 laptops; a Dell Latitude running VISTA and an old IBM running Ubuntu - both connect to this router (wirelessly)  & the internet w/o a problem.

The other option (which I feel is what you may want saying just 'wired' together?) is ICS, which has been a part of many Windows OS over the years - I've never given this a try; but basically, one computer (your VISTA machine) would act as an internet gateway - you'll need 2 network cards - one wil be connected to your ISP internet, and the other supplies the 'sharing' computer(s) - the connections are handled by the ICS software w/i VISTA - never seemed like a good option to me; plus, the external router gives you a lot of extra protection.  Good luck - :)
