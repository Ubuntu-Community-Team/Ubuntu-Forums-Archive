### Post by hanzj on 2008-08-27
Dear friends,
We have a weird problem.


We are almost certain that the problem lies not with the ISP. We are almost certain that the internet service  from the internet company to our home is fine. We can say this because when we remove the wireless router from the equation, and just directly connect my computer directly to the Motorola Cable Modem via wired connection, we don't have any disconnection error / time-out error.

For about 3 months, we had the internet running without any problem. I was using a Ubuntu computer which was connected to the wireless router via ethernet cable. PersonB was using a Windows Vista computer and was connected to the wireless router via wireless connection. That was the setup for the first 3 months.

Then, PersonC enters the picture. PersonC has a Mac. Since early August, Person C joined us in our internet access via wireless connection (it's called AirPort on PersonC's Mac). I think 2 or 3 days after PersonC joined us, we've been having the internet access problem.

I'm on wired, but even I lose the internet connection.

We did several small experiments to try to isolate the problem. In the first experiment, for a major portion of a day, we had just me and PersonB sharing the internet and had PersonC without any internet connection. That is to say, PersonB knew the WPA key to access the wireless internet but PersonC did not know the WPA key. For those hours when PersonC was not connected at all, the internet connection did not drop.

Then we did a second small experiment. I reset the wireless router (by either pressing the reset button on the back of the router or by unplugging the router for about a minute) and I then changed the WPA key to access the internet. I then gave the WPA key to PersonC.  PersonB  at this time did not have knowledge of the WPA key and was left out of the internet access. I gave PersonC the access key on Tuesday, Aug 26, at about 10 PM. I powered on my computer on Wednesday, Aug 27, at 12 noon, and the internet was down for me. I went to PersonC and his was down too. I asked him when he first noticed the internet being down. He said that he was using the internet fine at 10 am or 11am. then he stepped away from his  Mac computer for a bit. When he returned to his Mac Computer, he then noticed that he was disconnected from the internet connection, even though I did not do anything.

Can someone please help us with this problem?

We have a Dell Wireless 2350 Broadband Router.

The user guide for the router is available online at [http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/network/2350/en/index.htm](http://support.dell.com/support/edocs/network/2350/en/index.htm) but it doesn't have all the links active.
