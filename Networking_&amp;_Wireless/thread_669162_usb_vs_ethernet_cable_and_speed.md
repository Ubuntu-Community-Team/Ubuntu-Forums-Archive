### Post by MariusSilverwolf on 2008-01-16
If you're just talking speed, check out this table here, that illustrates the data rate speed differences: [http://www.lyberty.com/tech/terms/usb.html](http://www.lyberty.com/tech/terms/usb.html)

Now, for networking, ethernet is always better.  Ethernet allows for the use of TCP/IP, IPv4, IPv6, UDP, and seamless integration into routers, switches, and existing backbones.

And for even more practical reasons, USB networking within Ubuntu isn't nearly as reliable or simple to establish as ethernet networking.

Always always always ethernet. ;-)
