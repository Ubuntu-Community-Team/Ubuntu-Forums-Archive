### Post by kurt18947 on 2017-02-04
I can't really speak to newish USB WiFi adapters - my wifi devices are Intel. I have a couple "plug it in and it works" devices but my favorite is difficult to find. TrendNet TEW-649UB hw V1. Another which may still be found at mainstream stores in the US like Staples is Netgear WNA1100. The WNA1100 is 2.4 ghz max speeed N150 but seemed pretty compatible the last time I used it.  Here's a resource worth knowing about when shopping for wifi gear for linux:

[https://wikidevi.com/wiki/Main_Page](https://wikidevi.com/wiki/Main_Page)

It's not perfect as most user maintained wikis are not but it should at least tell if there's a linux driver available for a given device. Do research if looking at ac capable devices, I'm not certain about linux support there. Remember that linux support is based more on chipsets than manufacturers. The same device may be sold by several vendors under different names.  Here's another resource:

[https://www.thinkpenguin.com/gnu-linux/catalog](https://www.thinkpenguin.com/gnu-linux/catalog)
