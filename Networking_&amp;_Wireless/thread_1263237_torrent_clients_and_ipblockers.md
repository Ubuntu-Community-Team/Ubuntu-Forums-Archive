Encryption prevents your ISP from seeing exactly what it is you're transferring (and possibly from seeing that you're using bittorrent in the first place), but it offers no protection whatsoever against groups like the RIAA and MPAA if they connect to the tracker and get a list of peers.  

IP blockers offer no protection against your ISP, but they block a list of known IP addresses used by organizations like the RIAA and MPAA.

The only truly safe way to avoid lawsuits/disconnects/etc is to not share copyrighted files.  That said - if you're using both encryption and a good IP blocker you have a decent chance of avoiding detection.  But IP blocking is not reliable - the RIAA/MPAA can easily get a new set of IP addresses every few days.

If you want to share such files, and are truly worried about getting caught, then use encryption, use a good IP blocker, and don't use public trackers.  But even with these precautions, there's no certainty...
