### Post by viper250 on 2010-03-12
capscrew is correct! your shorter cable is working ok but the longer ones are not
the longer the cable the more prone it is to noise unless you are running it in conduit
shielded cabling is more expensive but worth it for longer runs.
fiber optic is best suited in an electrically noisy ares such as factories but is too expensive for home usage.
you can get cable testers that will test both continuity and correct pin position relatively cheap but your more expensive ones will detect and quantify noise levels and measure cable length.
You can also have the cables tested for noise induction.
a quick thing to try is to is to run a couple loops through a ferrite ring (this is a method of dampening noise)

the fact that your pc is working with all three is telling me that continuity and pin position is  correct
this leaves noise as a possible culprit ( although some network cards may not be sensitive enough)
