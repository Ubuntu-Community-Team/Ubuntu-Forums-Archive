### Post by mindstalk on 2007-10-19
I have a Compaq Presario C500 laptop, with the usual broadcom drivers.  For Feisty, I'd followed the standard instructions, of blacklisting bcm in /etc/modprobe.d, and installing ndiswrapper, to get wireless working, but I tend to used wired at home anyway.  Last night I was prompted to upgrade to Gutsy.  On restart, I was prompted to turn on restricted drivers "else your wireless won't work".  I did so, tried wireless... and it didn't work.  And in the process of trying repeatedly and turning off encryption and trying to latch onto a nearby open network, the keyboard stopped working.  Mouse was still useful, I could restart that way, but keyboard didn't work anywhere.  It does after reboot, but...  bad sign.

I blacklisted bcm again, but haven't tried wireless again yet, especially after seeing the "hard lock need to reinstall" messages.
