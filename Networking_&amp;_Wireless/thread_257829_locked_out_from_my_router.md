### Post by Smandola on 2006-09-16
red_Marvin,

Do you still have access to the wireless router via a wireless connection ?
or is that baked too ?

What you may want to try is to just power up the router with out having it connected to your modem. And see if you can access the router's web page that way.  

I'm not sure what model of router, but normally there is a reset, where you can reset back to factory settings.  You would have to find this in your routers manual/documenation, or on the d-link website.  Try to reset it back to factory defaults, and then try to connect to it.  

There may also be a firmware update for it.  If and when you can access the router's web page. 

If you have a friend that has a wireless router, ask them if you can borrow it and try to make it work on your network.

If all else fails... head down to your local electronic/computer shop and get a new one (ask about return policy).  If you have the same issues with the new one, then return it, and say it doesn't work :wink: .  That would tell you if the problem is the router or the modem.  

I've had devices fail on me, no warning, just doesn't work anymore.  
It happens.  

Hopefully that helps, or gives you some ideas on how to pick apart the problem.  Post back with your results.
