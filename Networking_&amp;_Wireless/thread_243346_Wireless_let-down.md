### Post by x64Jimbo on 2006-08-25
You can only expect so much out of a Windows driver that's being forced to work through a compatibility layer. Your problem is not Linux's fault. It's Broadcom's fault for being so uptight about their chipset schematics. 
If you want really great wifi support in Linux, you should look at Atheros chipset cards. They are pretty reasonably priced on eBay and are supported right out of the box on almost all Linux distros.
