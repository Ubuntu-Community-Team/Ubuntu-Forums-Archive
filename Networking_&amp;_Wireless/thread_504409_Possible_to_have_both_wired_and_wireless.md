### Post by Phosphoric on 2007-07-19
You would need to buy a wireless network interface card (NIC) for the PC without.  You could then set up a network between the two and access the internet through the hard-wired PC.  Both PCs would need to be switched on for this to work.

A simpler solution would be to buy a wireless router which is not so expensive these days.

Another thought.  Can you not hard wire the other PC to another telephone socket?  You would only need another ADSL filter for that.

### Post by jml on 2007-07-19
There is no technical reason preventing two PC's from sharing an internet connection.  The only issue is the terms of use agreement that you may have signed with your ISP.  There may be some terms in that document that limits the number of PC's able to connect.  Doubt it but its a possibility.  

Also, if you are able to use a wired connection for both of your computers, ( both near each other, that sort of thing,) and you do not need wireless networking capability, then I would strongly recommend that you buy an inexpensive router.  Something like this, (I am not endorsing this company, just using it to illustrate the product.)  

[http://www.buy.com/prod/linksys-etherfast-cable-dsl-router-4-port-external-linksys-befsr41/q/loc/101/10235958.html](http://www.buy.com/prod/linksys-etherfast-cable-dsl-router-4-port-external-linksys-befsr41/q/loc/101/10235958.html)

I recommend using a router even for my friends who have only one computer.  While Linux is not as suseptible to attacks by virus' , worms and trojans, its not invincible.  The router adds another layer of protection by acting as a very simple hardware firewall, so the only IP address exposed to the internet is the router's, not your individual computers.  If you go that route, be sure to change the default administrative password as soon as you can.  Most people interested in networking know the default admin passwords for the more common routers.

### Post by Austin_KW on 2007-07-19
The cheapest way is to get an ethernet crossover cable. connect the two computers via ethernet and share the usb internet connection with firestarter.

The 'best' way is to get youself an adsl router and use it to share the connection and then the 2 PCs are independant but still networked together for filesharing but protected from the internet with a hardware NAT firewall. Nowadays, if you are getting a dsl router you may as well get a wireless one as the cost difference is small.
