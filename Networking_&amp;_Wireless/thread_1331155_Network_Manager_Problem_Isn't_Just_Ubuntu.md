### Post by gerowen on 2009-11-19
I just thought I'd announce this here in case you guys weren't aware, the problems present in 9.10 with wireless seem to stem from the new version of Gnome and not from changes made specifically to Ubuntu.  I just tried the LiveCD of Fedora 12 and used the Gnome Network Manager there with the same cruddy results.  Signal strength going from 98% to 50%, transfers stalling, random disconnects, etc.  Wired networks seem to work fine, and I've managed to use wicd to stay connected to my wireless and not have any issues, but I still notice that even wicd will show my signal strength jumping up and down, and I'm sitting in the same room with my router.  My wife's Vista machine has no issues, and I had no issues in earlier versions of Linux.

According to sysinfo I'm using an Atheros AR928X wireless network card on a Toshiba Satellite L355D-S7829.

I would submit a bug report but I see tons of them floating around, just thought this information might be useful to somebody.
