### Post by Col-1023 on 2009-02-10
I have a new pc with the Gigabyte EX58DS4 mobo, using Ubuntu 8.10.amd64, and when I start the pc or if I do a system restart, it will often fail to connect to the LAN.

LAN is enabled in the bios.  The light on the lan port of my pc is constantly green which the motherboards manual says indicates that it's connecting at 100mbs, and I watched as the pc starts and there is the occaisional flash indicating some sort of traffic between the pc and router.

I enabled the LAN Boot ROM is the bios, but this didn't seem to have any effect, is it a good to enable lan boot rom anyway, or should it be left disabled?

I have tried power cycling the router and the light for my pc does flash a little and then stays solid, indicating it is connected, however if I go into my routers administration section, it does not see the new pc as connected.

My router is a Netgear DG834G.

The only way I've found to get the pc connected, (assuming it doesn't connect first time when I turn on the pc), is to shut down the pc, turn it off at the psu so there is no power going to it at all, then turning everything  back on again.  I do this until Ubuntu registers that the network is connected.

This is quite an annoying problem, so any help in correcting this problem would be appreciated.

### Post by cariboo on 2009-02-10
Unless you plan on booting from the network, you don't need the boot rom enabled.

What chipset does your onboard network adapter use?

Open an Applications-->Accessoreis-->Terminal and type:

```
sudo lswh -C network
```

and paste the output in your next post.
