### Post by SeijiSensei on 2011-04-19
It looks like your firewall doesn't have rules to accept broadcast packets.  There are many addressed to your subnet's broadcast address, 192.168.1.255, and many others addressed to the "all-ones" address 255.255.255.255.  The latter address is used by machines trying to get their network configurations with DHCP, but in your case something else is sending these broadcasts.  They're using port 17500 rather than ports 67 and 68 that DHCP uses.  According to [this](http://forums.dropbox.com/topic.php?id=16253), it may have to do with using Dropbox.

The 192.168.1.255 broadcasts are on port 138 which is used by Windows machines and Samba servers to advertise themselves to Windows networks.

If you're sure you understand why these broadcasts are taking place, you can edit the firewall rules to turn off logging in these cases.  I don't use ufw, so I have no idea how you'd do that with it.
