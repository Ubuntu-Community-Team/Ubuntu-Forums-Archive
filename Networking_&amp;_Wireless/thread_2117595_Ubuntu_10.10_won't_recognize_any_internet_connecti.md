### Post by kurt18947 on 2013-02-19
You realize that official support for 10.10 ended on 10 April 2012, nearly a year ago?  One thing that IME has improved in recent Ubuntu releases is wireless networking support.  I have a couple WiFI devices that required additional software and tweaks to work with 10.04 and 10.10.  They're plug'n'play in 12.04.  Perhaps try a live CD/USB of a currently supported release?  A live install should tell you if you have network connectivity 'out of the box'.  Wired networking usually 'just works', wireless can be dependent on the chipset used in the wifi adapter.

I find 12.04 rock solid, 12.10 a little flaky but that is just my experience.  Others find 12.10 very good, especially on portables.   If you don't care for Unity or Gnome-shell, there are several other options.  Xfce/Xubuntu has many supporters, as do KDE/Kubuntu and LXDE/Lubuntu.  And of course don't forget Mint/Cinnamon/MATE.
