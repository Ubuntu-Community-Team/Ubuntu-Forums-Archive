### Post by celem on 2009-11-25
It seams to me that gnubiff should do what you want. In the preferences screen (see attachment) you could put the thunderbird command into the When New Command section. This would replace the sound played with new mail. Another option is to leave gnubiff's sound in place and replace the When Double Clicked command with the thunderbird command. Finally, you could simply download the source and modify it to do what you want.

### Post by celem on 2009-11-25
FYI - I just tested what I said above by putting thunderbird into the When Double Clicked field of the Preferences screen. When I doubl clicked the little gnubiff penguin on the toolbar, it opened thunderbird, as expected.
