### Post by jml on 2006-03-22
Getting USB wireless devices to run in Linux is very difficult.  I was never successful at it.  If your laptop has a PCMCIA slot, you are more likely to succeed getting one of them to work.  Ubuntu has good support for cards that use the Atheros Wireless chipset.  The card that I use that is recognized by Ubuntu out of the box is the NetGear WG511T card.  It works well with both unencrypted wireless networks and networks encrypted with WEP.  To get WPA encryption working is a bit more complicated, and to be honest, I have never been able to get it to work.  However, I have read posts here about people who were able to.  Hope this helps.

Joe
