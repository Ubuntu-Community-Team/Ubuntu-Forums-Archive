Long story short?

I had been using my phone's hotspot but my family decided to get a local cable provider and have wifi for everybody, unlimited data and all that. Since I still have data remaining on my hotspot, decided to find a way to keep both.

Basically, I use a Wifi extender to 'replicate' my hotspot and this extender has an ethernet port, which is then connected to my 5port gigabit switch and then to my Ubuntu PC. This setup has been working great, and its only limitation is the aforementioned.... limited data.

Enter the cable modem provider, and then we have WIFI in all our home. So, I decided to purchase the Netgear WNA3100 (after I had seen that people had succesfully installed in their systems). I followed that guide, asked to download a 64bit driver (since my system is 64bit ofc), followed the steps and in the end I used 'ndisgtk' to finally set it up. I was able to connect to my home's wifi and browse the internet, watch youtube and such.

I mostly play 2 games, an MMO and a FPS. Using the cable wifi... the FPS suffers a lot (even with noone else using the wifi), but I can do the MMO no problem and the other things.

So decided to keep both, and just select the apropiate connection for the moment needed.

At some point, I was getting horrid wifi performance, and I went to dab with connections settings (and worse, not taking notes on what I changed :( ).
