### Post by skymt on 2006-10-25
I used it for a while. I got tired of the ad-supported search they give you if you mistype a domain name. They offer a way to turn it off, but for some reason it wouldn't stick for me. Also, some domain names wouldn't resolve with OpenDNS, but would with my ISP's DNS. So, I gave up on it. The speed difference is real, but not large enough to make up for the inconvenience.

### Post by ciscosurfer on 2006-10-25
I've used it for a couple of months.  I think their idea is novel and I do get faster speeds.  If I accidentally enter the wrong TLD, it will resolve it for me (i.e., if I enter ubuntuforums.rog it will fix it to .org, etc.)

I don't mind the ad-supported search...you don't have to click their links if you don't want to, but they will offer up suggestions to any wacky addresses that you enter that can't be normally resolved.

So, I find the service useful.  It's fast, I've noticed a difference, and I'm stickin' with it.
