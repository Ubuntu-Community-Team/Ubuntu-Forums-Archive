### Post by bsky1 on 2006-08-17
Hello everyone,

I am a Linux newbie so I want to share my experience getting wireless working hopefully it helps members here and in turn I will receive help down the road.

I loaded 6.06 onto my IBM Thinkpad T21 without any problem, the installation was easy and the machine runs beautifully.  I have 2 wifi 802.11b pcmcia cards different brands but I know they both have Intersil chipset, they got 98% signal according to the singal meter but showed 'disconnected'.  After reading and changine the network setting for 4 hours I couldn't get them to work.  I didn't try to change the drivers as I just barely got to know Linux but from the reading I know Intersil chipset is well supported in Linux and if I change the driver (need to learn how to :) I could probably get them to work.  But then I remember I have a third card that came with the Thinkpad that never put to work because I didn't bother to look for its Windows driver.  I put the card in the T21 and it worked.  

The card is Netopia '3D Reach' model SWL-2300N in front it has 802.11b TER/WPC11N1 I don't know what chipset it has but it worked 'right out of the box' with 6.06.  If you want a picture of it go to eBay item # 290018433725 these cards are dirt cheap on eBay.  This card is straight 802.11b there is a later version of it which is 'g' I don't know if that works or not.

So that's how I got my wireless working :), pure luck.
