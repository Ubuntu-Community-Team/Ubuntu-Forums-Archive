### Post by J_we on 2012-10-25
It's actually a great card although not for your use. It's not recommended over the G USB adapter for most users. It is only really recommended for use in setting up wireless access points.

I'd get the Penguin USB G adapter as company suggests or as an alternative a PCI based card. G's capabilities will exceed your Internet connections capabilities so it's not much of a point in getting N anyway for typical use.

All other N PCI/PCIe cards work better with the newer chipsets from Atheros. The company is actually working on a new USB N adapter with Atheros. Hopefully it comes out soon. The problem isn't really with the adapter or chipset. It's crummy support from router manufacturers for the version of the standard. There are a lot of N USB devices out there that don't work as they should. I'd actually say not a single one works as it should yet. As soon Atheros releases the newer firmware and ThinkPenguin's got a new card based on it things should work much much much better as most routers support the newer revisions better.

 If you aren't on a recent version of Ubuntu I'd suggest upgrading. Also look at the companies support pages. They tell you how to upgrade the firmware on older versions of Ubuntu.
