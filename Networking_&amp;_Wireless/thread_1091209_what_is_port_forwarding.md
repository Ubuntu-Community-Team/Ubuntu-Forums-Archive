### Post by madverb on 2009-03-09
Port forwarding is a way to allow people on the internet access to only certain servers on your internal network without allowing access to the rest of your network.
If you have a webserver running on your internal network you can allow external access through port 80 to your webserver.
This means they can access your internal website but they can't access your internal FTP server.
There are a lot of standard ports for particular protocols/servers.
