### Post by dnvikram on 2007-12-26
Nmap will show you all the **open ports**,what services its offering on the target machine .I am sure you also know this.

Now,coming to how to open the ports which nmap showed you.Thats a redundant question as the answer lies within that only.Like I said above,nmap shows you the open ports or the available ports after the scan.

I think what you want to know is,how to use the output provided by nmap to break into that target machine via those open ports.If so,there is no step by step guide to do that nor a single failproof way to that.If only it was so easy,there would be tons of peeping toms cracking your box day in and day out.

A piece of advise,its kinda illegal to run port scanners.I hope you are not targetting that on some Government Agency machine or some Company.You may get into serious trouble if done repeatedly.
