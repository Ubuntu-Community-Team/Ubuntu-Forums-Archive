### Post by queenorych on 2006-07-04
FYI,
My sister in Chaska, MN had a chaska net.  The signal wasn't too strong, so it was better to use a bridge (supplied by chaska), then connect that to our own wireless gateway.  

What I'm trying to say is that you may be better off giong with a wifi-to-ethernet bridge to right by your window, then use ethernet or your own wifi router(different channel obviously) to propigate the internet through your house.

As far as wifi I have a Inspiron 5100.  I bought a nova mini-pci 54g card (rt2500) for around $20 shipped from newegg.  Doesn't work with network manager, but hibernates and all just great.  Wep and wpa are supported through the driver.  So your stuck with text configurations, but no issues.  Though intel gives you the best linux support. (newegg also had these).
