### Post by Jussi Kukkonen on 2006-10-19
Depends on the device (many chipsets have serious problems or will not work at all). If you buy blind, be prepared for problems... It would be best if you find something that's already tested to work.

The problem is that, as usb wifi devices are mostly the cheapest garbage around, the manufacturers tend to replace the chips without changing model numbers whenever it suits them...

It works with lots of them.

You can go to a store you know and ask if the device has native linux support (native means that you do not have to use ndiswrapper to get it to work).  You can bring the live cd with you, but I tend to put the responsability of figuring out whether a device works or not on the salespeople - that's their job.

If they don't know what you are talking about, tell them why you are walking away before you do.
