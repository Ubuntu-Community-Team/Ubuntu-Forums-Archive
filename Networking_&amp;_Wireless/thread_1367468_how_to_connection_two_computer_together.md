### Post by Project PWNED on 2009-12-29
Easiest way is to purchase a router or a wireless router. Wireless routers are good if you have laptops, PDAs, cell phones, etc. that you would like to get online with. A "wired" router (not a wireless one) is easier to setup, speaking from a Linux perspective, because you might have wireless cards that work with Windows but may not work at all with Linux or might need drivers installed.

I would go with a wireless router, because later on you may wish to go wireless but the wireless router has Ethernet jacks for your wired Ethernet devices to go into plus wireless connectivity. If you go wireless and do not use the wireless router, just the wired jacks, please turn off the wireless or encrypt it because if you do not, a neighbor could be using your wireless for free without your knowledge or permission.
