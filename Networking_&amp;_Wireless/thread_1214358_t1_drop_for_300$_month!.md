### Post by paulisdead on 2009-07-15
You know a T1 is only 1.5mbps up and down.  You'll likely have a solid and low latency connection, and supposedly your bandwidth is guaranteed, but it's still capped at 1.5mbps.  I hate how the ISPs really screw you just to get a bit of upload.

Without knowing where you're physically located at, it's impossible to know what options are available where you're at.  Availability can get very specific for some services, right down to the address, so a lot of options offered in your region might not have come to your neighborhood.

There are alternatives to T1s that are better priced, like FIOS.  I've seen a local ISP here offer what they call "ethernet" with high bandwidth rates for a bit over $300 starting, but I haven't looked at exactly what it is and how it works.  I have heard complaints about the latency being bad on FIOS, though.

You should probably check out dslreports.com, they have a wealth of information about ISPs, and are not just about DSL.
