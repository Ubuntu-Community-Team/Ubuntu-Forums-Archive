### Post by coffeeaddict22 on 2009-05-27
Hi,
Short answer is try it, unfortunately.  Having said that, you should be good with most reasonably new cards.  Intel are pretty good at open- source drivers, but most of the others improved substantially over the last year.  That's half your problem, actually- a lot of the info on the net is from prior to the kernel drivers getting sorted.  The other issue is it's next to impossible to figure out what chipset is running from the cardboard box; you'll have to plug it in to be certain.  You should be able to find someone who'll let you try it out; it's not like you're going to steal any proprietary drivers, after all.
