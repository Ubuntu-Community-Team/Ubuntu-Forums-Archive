### Post by 3rdalbum on 2009-07-14
Some 3G providers where I live will offer a router that their modem can plug into. Otherwise you would need to plug the 3G modem into one of your computers and do internet connection sharing - I tried it once but couldn't get it to work.

3G internet isn't a dream boat. Technically I'm inside my provider's "Broadband zone" but I get about 200kbps max, and it often drops to 30kbps. (that is, 3 kilobytes per second). Even inside an area with good coverage, you might find that your connection to a server will die and that you have to hit Reload; if you're downloading big files you want to either use Bittorrent or make sure the server supports continuing downloads.

And finally, you can forget online gaming on 3G.

Basically, my best advice would be to change from one ADSL service to another.
