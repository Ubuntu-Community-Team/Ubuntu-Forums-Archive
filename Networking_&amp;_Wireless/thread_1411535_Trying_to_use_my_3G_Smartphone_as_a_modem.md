### Post by efflandt on 2010-02-20
I am not familiar with mobile plans in the UK, but here in the states, just because you get mobile web or internet on your phone does not necessarily mean that you can tether internet to a 3rd party device.  You usually need an extra cost data plan for that.

In years past, you used to at least be able to use a mobile phone for dial up networking at a leisurely 14.4K to a dial up ISP.  But now you may not even be able to do that (or fax) without an extra cost data plan.

The monthly cost for a data plan that allows 3rd party hardware to connect is often the same whether you add it to your phone or use a separate data device (PC/PCExpress card or USB dongle).  My company got me a USB data dongle, and connecting that in Ubuntu Network Connections was as simple as picking United States, Verizon, Connect automatically, and I was on-line.
