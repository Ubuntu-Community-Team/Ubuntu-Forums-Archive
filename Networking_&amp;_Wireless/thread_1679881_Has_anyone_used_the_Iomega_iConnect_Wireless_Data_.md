### Post by Who needs windows anyway? on 2011-02-18
Well, see there have been a few people passing by but no one has replied, so I bit the bullet and bought one from a local shop.  Unfortunately the experience was not good. I inserted the CD into another laptop with Windows 7 installed and my problems started straight away.  The disk would not auto start, when I finally got the software installed it failed to run most of the time. Eventually got the device set up and connected my USB printer and external hard drive, the printer was recognised eventually put it completely failed to detect my hard drive.  Managed to print from the Windows laptop so I then decided to try the Ubuntu laptop.  It took a while and a bit of tinkering to get Ubuntu to detect the USB printer connect to the Iomega device but I eventually managed to get it to work and to print.  However I went to try it again the next day just to ensure it wasn't a fluke and nothing!  The Iomega device was now failing to detect my printer and no amount of fiddling was working.  The outcome is it's going back to the shop, I've read many a review saying that customer support at Iomega is terrible not to mention costly.Sso I've decided to cut my losses and stick to my faithful old Belkin unit, even though it only works in Windows, at least it works 100% of the time.
