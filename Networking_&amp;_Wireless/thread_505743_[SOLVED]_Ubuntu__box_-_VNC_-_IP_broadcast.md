### Post by KiloEleven on 2007-07-20
Probably the best thing to do is to look into Dynamic DNS. You can get this from various sources, I have used dyndns.org in the past (you get 4 or 5 host names free). Dynamic DNS works by updating DNS servers as your IP address changes. So you always connect to a name, instead of an IP address. I know there are clients for Linux, but I am not familiar w/ what they are named. I think there is more information on their website - clients can be found at [http://www.dyndns.com/support/clients/](http://www.dyndns.com/support/clients/)
