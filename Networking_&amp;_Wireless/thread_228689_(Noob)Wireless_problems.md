Hello,

I recently got my wireless card working after a lot of frustration and unsuccessful trial and errors.  I have a Zonet wireless card and since it is not as popular as some of the other ones (D-Link, Netgear, etc.) there were no instructions to get my wireless card to work in any of the ubuntu forums.

Recently, I stumbled upon a How-To website for Linux wireless networking and it worked wonders for me.  The link to the website is below.

([http://www.linuxhomenetworking.com/wiki/index.php/Quick_HOWTO_:_Ch13_:_Linux_Wireless_Networking](http://www.linuxhomenetworking.com/wiki/index.php/Quick_HOWTO_:_Ch13_:_Linux_Wireless_Networking))

It is important that (as mentioned in the website) you not use the windows driver that came with the Linksys WUSB11 v2.8 adapter.  There are Linux-tested drivers that you can dowload and use to make your card work perfectly.  The instructions on the website shows you how to get your adapter's PCI ID and look for a driver that is tested to work for your adapter.

Just make sure that you follow the instructions properly and your card will work (I hope) just like mine.  Hope this helps.
