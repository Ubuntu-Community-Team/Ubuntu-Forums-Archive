### Post by ttakun on 2007-05-03
I don't Know KisMAC. I suppose it is an application/program for MAC users, and I'm not one of those.
But looking for it in the Wikipedia I found that "KisMAC is a wireless network discovery tool for Mac OS X. It has a wide range of features, far exceeding that of NetStumbler, its closest equivalent on Windows, **and Kismet, its Linux/BSD namesake**."
I think the features of KisMAC are far beyond the Kismet one's. Kismet is only a wireless sniffer (wireless network detector). I think it doesn't support packet injection and WEP or WPA cracking. To do such things you should rely on other programs such as aircrack-ng, airodump-ng, aireplay-ng, ...
