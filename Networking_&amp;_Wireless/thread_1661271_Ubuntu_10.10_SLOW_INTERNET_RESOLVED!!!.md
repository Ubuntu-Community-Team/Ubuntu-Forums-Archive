### Post by StingzLD on 2011-01-06
So I am new to Linux recently installed Ubuntu 10.10 on my laptop, and like a whole bunch of other people out there have had ridiculously slow internet since the install. But for some reason this effect only happens in Ubuntu (not Windows) on the same system, am I right? Well after searching for what seems like forever and then some, I finally found a couple of threads that helped me resolve the issue! Since I know a lot of you are probably searching for the same thing, I figured I would make a post and put it all in one.

One.
IMHO, the major help came from this thread (I can't confirm that though because I did both of these before testing to see if it worked):
[http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=616744](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=616744)
Scroll down to the third (and last) entry. I updated every file just as is posted in the thread.

Two.
For this one, I am not going to post the thread since the link that I used was in one of the replies and this is a lot easier than telling you to find that reply to find the link. Here is that link:
[http://support.mozilla.com/en-US/kb/Firefox%20cannot%20load%20websites%20but%20other%20programs%20can](http://support.mozilla.com/en-US/kb/Firefox%20cannot%20load%20websites%20but%20other%20programs%20can)
The thread was talking about disabling IPv6, which seems to be talked about a lot with this issue. But I also found that in the link there was another section that seemed very fitting for my problem. Here is what I did:
1) Scroll down a little to the IPv6 section. Follow these instructions for disabling IPv6.
2) After you finish with that, go to the next section (DNS Prefetching) and create the preference to disable prefetching.

And there you have it. Now you can go back to blazing the internet at the Speed of Ligh..... well your fastest connection I guess haha.

(If this post does not work for you, then I am sorry that I cannot assist you anymore. I have literally been searching forums for days and finally ran across these, and this is all I know that works)
