Hi,

My wireless normally works fine, but has recently been acting up. I have an HP DV2000 with the Broadcom (aargh) wireless card, dual booting 9.10 and Win 7 (both 64bit).

Wireless was working fine, then last week I booted into Ubuntu, then rebooted back into Win7 & my Wireless was gone. I.e. the system did not recognize that the card was present. The only way I could figure to fix this was to reset my bios (unplug, remove battery, hold power button for 30sec, replace battery, plug in, reboot). After doing this wireless works fine - at least until I boot into Ubuntu, after which the problem recurs.

Any ideas how to fix/prevent this? 

Many thanks.

### Post by CanGooner on 2010-01-22
OK. Problem solved I think. It is a bios issue, and is recognized as such by HP. I updated the bios and it seems to be working fine.

So if you're having this problem, go to: 

hp.com/support

search for your model

Download the bios update, apply it, and hopefully it will fix your as well.
