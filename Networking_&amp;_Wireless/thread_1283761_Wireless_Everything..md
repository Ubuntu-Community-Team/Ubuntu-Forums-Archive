### Post by Roasted on 2009-10-05
Ha, sounds like you jumped right in!

.exe files are Windows only programs. Just like .dmg are Mac only programs.

With Linux, there's several types of package configurations. Ubuntu is debian-based, so it works off of .deb packages. Fortunately, Ubuntu has a truckload of software to install. I'm sure if you can't find the exact same program in Ubuntu that you used in Windows, that you can at least find something very comparable and in some cases, even better, and in almost all cases, for no cost at all.

About your wireless - check network manager, which is typically in the upper right hand corner, with either signal bars for wireless or two computers for wired. Just click that icon and see if what appears below it is your wireless network. If you see it, click on your wireless network and try to connect accordingly.

See how that works and let us know.

Oh, and by the way - Welcome to Ubuntu. You made a wise choice. :guitar: It'll be a curve ball until you get the basics down, but you might find that, well.. you like it here. ;)
