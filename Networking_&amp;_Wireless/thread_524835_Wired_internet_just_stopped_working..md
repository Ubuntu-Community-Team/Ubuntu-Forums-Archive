### Post by janipewter on 2007-08-13
Well if the other computers connected to your router work fine, but your main computer doesn't work with either OS, I would suspect it's either a dodgy cable between the router and your PC, or some sort of hardware firewall (such as the hardware firewall found on some nforce motherboards).

### Post by drex5483 on 2007-08-13
Well...I don't know what I did but it's working now.

I tried switching out the cable and that didn't work. Plugged directly into the modem and that didn't work either. Before plugging the router back into the modem I reset the router by unplugging, waiting a few seconds, and plugging in the power back in. Then used the first, original cable to plug back into the router, this time into slot 4 after already trying slots 1 and 2. And...presto, it works. Was feeling caviler so I tried slot 1 again and it still worked. :dunno:  I definitely tried reseting both the router and the modem several times when it initially stopped but to no avail.

So, if you have this problem I would suggest just resetting everything and if that doesn't work, wait a day and try again. Apparently that's the trick. Good luck, and thank you janipewter for the fast response.
