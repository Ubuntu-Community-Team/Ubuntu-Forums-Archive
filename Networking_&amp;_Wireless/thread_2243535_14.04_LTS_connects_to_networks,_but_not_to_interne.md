### Post by varunendra on 2014-09-11
Hello ryan109, Welcome to the forums!

As you can notice in the "nm-tool > Wireless Access Points" section of the report you attached, most of the avilable APs are using WPA/WPA2 mixed mode encryption, including the one you were connected to while running the script. That is usually a factory default encryption setting for most routers/access-points to ensure backward compatibility with ancient devices that don't support the modern WPA2-AES encryption.

The "WPA (1)" part of this combo uses outdated "TKIP" algorithm which can be a problem for certain drivers in certain situations. As such, it is highly recommended to keep the encryption to pure WPA2-PSK with AES (CCMP) in the router, if you have admin access to it.

But that doesn't mean you are out-of-luck on places where you can't do anything about it (not your router). There is a driver parameter for your driver (ath9k) which is often (but not always) helpful. To try it, please open a terminal (Ctrl-Alt-T), and run the following command in it (you may copy-paste to avoid typo) -
```
echo "options ath9k nohwcrypt=1" | sudo tee /etc/modprobe.d/ath9k.conf
```
Reboot, reconnect, and see if it makes the connection any better.
