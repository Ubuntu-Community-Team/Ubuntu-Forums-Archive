### Post by chili555 on 2008-04-29
> I'm hoping the answer is "nothing at all."The answer is, 'nothing at all.' I am assuming you will have a cable modem which you will connect to your computer with an ethernet cable. When they are done, boot your computer and you should be connected. Thank the guy and edge him to the door. If they want to say they don't support Linux, ask if they can supply a 6Mbs signal at the ethernet jack. If they can, we're all good.

I have run Linux since 2001 with both cable and DSL modems, with and without routers. I have never had the slightest trouble traceable to Linux. Traceable to the lawn guys? Yes.

The protocol is the protocol whether you run Windows, Linux, MacOS, BSD, Solaris or anything. Good luck.
