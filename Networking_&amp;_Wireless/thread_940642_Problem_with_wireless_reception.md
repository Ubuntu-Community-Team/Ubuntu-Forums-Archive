### Post by john183 on 2008-10-07
Your problem with the weak reception is not going to be fixed by upgrading your wireless adapter on the computer. No matter what adapter you use, if the signal from your router is weak where your computer is then you will get bad reception. To increase your coverage range, you will need to upgrade your router. If you just upgrade the router, you will get a stronger signal, but still be at the "g" speeds. If you upgrade the router and your adapter to "n" devices you will get both the better speeds and longer range. Let me know if you have any more questions regarding this, I spend much of my free time upgrading and setting up routers for friends, co-workers, etc.

### Post by john183 on 2008-10-08
The distance you can be from a router and still pick it up depends on the strength of the signal and the interference between you and the router. The new adapter may be able to pick up some weaker signals, but no guarantees. The approximate range for a "g" band network indoors is 38 meters, while a "n" network is 70. Outside those ranges can greatly increase, but that is all dependent on the router.
