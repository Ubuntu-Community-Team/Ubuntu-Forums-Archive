### Post by kevdog on 2008-12-01
Its not Ubuntu -- its your wireless card most likely.  You need a chipset that is reliably supported.  I really prefer Atheros chipsets that are suppoted by the madwifi set of drivers, but that is me.  Some broadcom chipsets work with the built-in drivers, however broadcom cards can be notoriously problematic.  I'm not sure about recent versions of Ubuntu with Ra cards b/c I haven't seen a lot of posts with people saying either way.
