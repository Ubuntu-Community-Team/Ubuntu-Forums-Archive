### Post by Katalog on 2009-09-12
Is your modem connected to a router? If so, have you checked the settings in the router to see if there hasn't been some restriction inadvertently set to limit internet access to certain times of the day? Reason I ask, is because my router does have such a setting in the parental controls to limit when users can and cannot access the internet.

As for your second problem, what do the memtest results show? Do the results show any errors (sometimes you have to run the test for several cycles for it to hit upon something)? If it does, you may consider trying a differnet brand of memory and see if Ubuntu still hangs on startup. You may also want to poke around in your BIOS and see if the settings are correct for your type of memory. Just a few shots in the dark, but hopefully one of them will help.
