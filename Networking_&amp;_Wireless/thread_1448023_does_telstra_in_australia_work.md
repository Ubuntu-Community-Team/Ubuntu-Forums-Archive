Also, with ADSL telstra tends to give you software setup discs, obviosuly there is no linux suport on them, so you have to do things manually. Telstra just assume that people cant do this because 'you need to be an IT professional to do that'- I was told that by an x-telstra tech. 
 
 I've setup a linux machine on telstra more than once. With cable, is just plug and off you go. With ADSL, well, if you know the right questions to ask can get the telstra techs to give you the answers you need to set it up, but heres the quick version- 
 
 Get the router model. Find the modem login page (eg 192.168.1.254). That will involve looking on the internet normally (annoying, eh?), or ringing up telstra and asking he 'right questions'. Put the username/password into the right spots (you shouldnt have to look to hard to find them, there is normally a 'quick setup' or something like that). Done. You will need to have PPPoE installed (comes with almost all distros) or else you will have to save teh settings with firefox, or whatever browser you are using.
