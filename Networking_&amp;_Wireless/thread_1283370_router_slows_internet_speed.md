### Post by Jefferythewind on 2009-10-05
Hi everyone, I know this is not exactly an ubuntu question, but i can't seem to find any good answers online.  SO i thought i would challenge the ubuntu community as they have been incredibly helpful in the past.

I have a fiber optic Internet connection that comes into my apartment as an ethernet cable.  When i connect my computer directly to the cable and run a speed test, i get at least 8 Mbs of speed.  I recently bought a small dlink wired/wireless router.  And after setting everything up and connecting the computer by wire to the LAN jacks, i only get about 1.5 Mbs on the speed test.  I found by searching google that this is a pretty common problem, but i haven't found a good solution.  I noticed that when i ping the router i get a much slower reaction than the dlink tutorial says i should get.

It says the router supports 54 Mbs of speed so i don't think the problem is that the router is too slow.  

I was wondering if anyone knows something that could help me.  Basically making the Internet flow a lot more smoothly through the router, especially when there is only one computer connected by wire.  

Thanks a lot!

### Post by Arup on 2009-10-05
Try upgrading the router firmware and turn of any logging in the router and see what happens. Also place the route in the highest point in your room for better reception.

### Post by Jefferythewind on 2010-02-09
It turns out this was a hardware problem, we returned the router and bought a "better" model, and the problem was gone.  Even thought the original router was rated at 50 Mb/s, it still slowed down the 8Mb/s fiber-optic line to about 2.
