This is really just checking to make sure I've got this right. We're currently experimenting with deploying 802.1x authentication on our wired networks. In Windows and OS X, we can successfully configure the systems to log into the network with the user's credentials before then continuing with the normal AD login. The machine is dropped into the correct VLAN for the user's role as part of this.

Ideally, we'd love to do it with Ubuntu systems as well. From what I can see, that's not possible and we get a choice of either having a single login for the whole machine (we tie it down to a single role through a wpa_supplicant config) or doing some real mucking about with login scripts. For example, editing /etc/gdm/PostLogin/Default to authenticate with a role matching their AD group.


Am I understanding correctly or is there a nice way of doing 802.1x on wired networks, with per-user logins as part of the Ubuntu login process?
