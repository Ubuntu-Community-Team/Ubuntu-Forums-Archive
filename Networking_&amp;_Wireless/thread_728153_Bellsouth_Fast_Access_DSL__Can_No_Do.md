### Post by dmizer on 2008-03-21
you do not need to configure your computer for the DSL connection.  it's that simple.  your computer never has, and it never will have anything to do with the speed of your DSL signal from bellsouth.  your computer has no controll over your DSL connection _at all_.  the upgrade is completely handled at the bellsouth side.  so, to answer your question: "just request the upgrade and go on with life just as you always have until now."

here is a typical DSL connection:

[COLOR="Red"]**Provider** -DSL-> **DSL modem**[/COLOR] [COLOR="Blue"]-ethernet-> **router** -ethernet-> **your computer**[/COLOR].

your DSL speed is ONLY controlled by equipment up to and including the modem (in red).  the modem handles the communication between your provider (bellsouth) and your local network.  your modem controls the speed and signal negotiation between your local network and your provider.

your DSL modem is the part of your network which will be configured for the higer speed DSL signal, and this will be handled (most likely from remote) by bellsouth.  Your DSL modem has no direct communication with your computer, and your modem not controlled in any way shape or form by your computer.

so, from the standpoint of your computer, you will need to do nothing at all because the signal coming from your modem into your router will be identical to the one you were getting before, except it will be faster.

i did not contradict myself at all because changing your DSL speed is not negotiated, controlled, or configured in any way by any equipment that comes after the DSL modem (in blue).

Or, in other words ... your computer does not need to be "upgraded" in order to handle a different DSL speed from bellsouth.
