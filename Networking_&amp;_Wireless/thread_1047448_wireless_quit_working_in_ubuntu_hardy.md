### Post by sabinedoll on 2009-01-22
I am the non tech-savvy owner of an Acer Aspire 5670 laptop and running Ubuntu. A few days ago I accidentally put my laptop into a "sleep-mode" , or suspend/hibernate. After that my wireless quit working. It took me 2 days of researching to find out the oft-mentioned problem with the new kernel and the kill-switch , which turns makes wireless unusable after going into suspend.

I got K-wifi manager to see my network again, (after I had it in suspend for a second time), after I rebooted and after ubuntu had shut itself down, before reloading, I held the wireless switch in front of my laptop to the right (=activated it) the whole time while the laptop started up, until the log-in screen appeared.

After this the red wireless LED was flashing again and my network ws seen again.
On my computer and with my network, I also need WICD (from the repositories in Synaptic) to make my wireless work.

I hope this helps someone in the same situation and saves them three days of agonizing work.

Happy networking to all!
