### Post by ddtmsa on 2006-01-30
If you have read the other threads on getting 'windows' modems to work, then you will be aware that there is no universal quick fix. You will almost certainly have to compile the driver for your modem, but there are faqs and readmes on how to get the necessary packages and files to do this. However you will need to have a basic understanding of how linux works to do this...  

First of all, you need to find out what chipset is in your modem e.g. is it Intel or Lucent. To do this you can either try to dig out the information from the Windows control panel or download and run the scanmodem utility, see 

[http://linmodems.org/](http://linmodems.org/) 

for more information.
