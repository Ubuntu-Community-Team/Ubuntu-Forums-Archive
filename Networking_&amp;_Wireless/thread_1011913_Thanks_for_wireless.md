I am still stumbling my way through this forum.  I am learning though.  I'm posting here because it seems to be the best place I can find.  If it is inappropriate here please tell me where to go.  (OK, I know what you are thinking)  
    I have been working with Ubuntu for almost two years and have had a great experience.  The fly in the ointment has been wireless.  I never could achieve a wireless connection - mostly due to my ignorance of wireless and wireless terminology coupled.  I read a post here that said the best way to make a wireless connection was to start with a clean install.
    I did that.  Installed Ubuntu 8:10 on my old Dell Inspiron 4150 laptop.  Everything worked out of the box.  The amazing thing was that after I installed the driver for my wireless card Ubuntu jumped in and did the rest.  I had wireless effortlessly.  
    That folks is the reason for this post.  Obviously connecting wirelessly has always been a problem for Ubuntu.  A lot of "somebodies" out there went to work on that and fixed it.  My hat is off to them and they have some of the most sincere thanks I have ever offered anyone.  Thank you all you unknown programmers and to this forum for bringing thins together.  KME
