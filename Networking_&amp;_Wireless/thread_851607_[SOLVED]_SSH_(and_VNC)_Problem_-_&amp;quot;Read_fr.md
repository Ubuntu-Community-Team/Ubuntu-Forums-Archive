no ... all you have to do is remake your ssh dsa keypair, as i said in my above post.

use the following code:
```
ssh-keygen -t dsa
```
to create a new keypair on your remote machines. then, carry the new id_dsa.pub (located in /home/USERNAME/.ssh/) to your currently unreachable server, replace your old key in ~/.ssh/authorized_keys2 and you'll be back in service.
