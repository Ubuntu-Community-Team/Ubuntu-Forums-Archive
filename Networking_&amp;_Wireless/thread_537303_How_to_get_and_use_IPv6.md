### Post by mpadams on 2007-08-28
1. If you don't have a static IP (most home DSL/cable users don't), you can sign up with a tunnel broker like SixxS or Hexago to get an IPv6 tunnel.

**[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_IPv6_tunnel_brokers]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_IPv6_tunnel_brokers")**

2. If you do have a static IP, and don't wish to use a tunnel broker, you can try using a "6to4" tunnel.

**[http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Linux+IPv6-HOWTO/configuring-ipv6to4-tunnels.html]("http://tldp.org/HOWTO/Linux+IPv6-HOWTO/configuring-ipv6to4-tunnels.html")**

3. If you're having trouble with IPv6, the SixxS forums contain a good deal of feedback between users of that service and others.

**[http://www.sixxs.net/forum/]("http://www.sixxs.net/forum/")**

4. If your IPv6 connection is working fine, check out some of these sites...

*** ["Cool IPv6 Stuff" linked-to on SixxS]("http://www.sixxs.net/misc/coolstuff/")**
*** [Comparison of IPv6 application support]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_IPv6_application_support")**
*** [How to setup an IPv6 VPN with tinc]("http://www.wolfsheep.com/technical/tinc_ipv6/")**
*** [APRS Florida (ham-radio & lightning-detection)]("http://www.aprsfl.net/")**
*** ["The Dancing Kame" (classic test of IPv6 support)]("http://www.kame.net/")**
