### Post by rplantz on 2005-10-07
I am using an ethernet hub to connect my Ubuntu box, Windows box, and Macintosh. My isp is kind enough to provide separate dynamic ip addresses for each, so everything works.

The problem is getting the three machines to talk to each other without involving the rest of the world. So I would like to get a router.

My connection is "wireless dsl" ([www.cds1.net](www.cds1.net)) since I cannot get cable or copper dsl where I live.

I've wired my house with ethernet and we don't have laptops. So I will be using wired connections. However, most of the sales I see are on wireless routers. I suspect that a wireless will actually cost me less than a wired.

My isp people say that they have seen good results with linksys. But when I read the specs, linksys lists Windows as the required os. Seems odd to me. Can I use a linksys with other os's?

Any thoughts on other inexpensive routers for the home?

Bob

### Post by matthew on 2005-10-08
I use a linksys router with linux with no problems whatsoever. It's a wrt54g and does wireless b/g as well as has 4 wired ports. All I did was plug it into the modem from my isp and plug the computers in and it worked. Of course, after that I did change all the security settings and stuff, but that was done easily through a direct interface using my web browser...oh, unlike a previous poster, I have had no problems with formatting and such when using firefox for router configuration.

### Post by rplantz on 2005-10-10
I installed a Netgear MR814. Compusa is selling them for $2.99, after a $30 rebate. It does not support 802.11g, but all my machines are hardwired. So it looked like a pretty good deal for a simple home hardwired router.

It seems to work fine out of the box. Haven't changed many settings yet, except to turn off wireless.

It's funny that wireless routers are often on sale, so are actually cheaper than wired. At the price I paid for this one I will simply throw it away if it stops working.
