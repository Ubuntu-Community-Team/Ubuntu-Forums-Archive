### Post by gradedcheese on 2007-03-12
Hi.  I don't use an ADSL modem directly but I do know that this might help you:
[http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy#How_to_install_Broadband_ADSL.2FPPPoE_Client_.28RP-PPPoE.29](http://ubuntuguide.org/wiki/Ubuntu_Edgy#How_to_install_Broadband_ADSL.2FPPPoE_Client_.28RP-PPPoE.29)

Personally (if you don't mind the slight expense), the nicest way to use these modems is to buy a cheap home networking router (with our without wireless too) and use that between your modem and computer(s).  The router will take care of the PPPoE stuff and your PC will just get an IP address without any hassle, just like on a 'real' LAN.  Otherwise, you need to install a PPPoE client (as explained in that link).
