### Post by OrangeCrate on 2008-11-29
As mentioned, there's some speed up if you have a broadband connection, but potential slow down with slower DSL or dial up modem speeds.

A piece of trivia...

On Windows, you can end up pre-fetching malware, and antivirus programs will respond when their cached. Many savy, security conscious Windows users avoid the extension.

And remember, extensions are not "built into the browser", their developed by independent programers. There are very good ones, such as NoScript, and Adblock Plus, but many marginal to poor ones. Some will conflict with others, which can be problematic in Firefox's overall performance.
