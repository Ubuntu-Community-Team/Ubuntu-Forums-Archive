### Post by chili555 on 2021-07-12
> [ 1539.989207] wlp37s0: send auth to 48:d3:43:79:58:[COLOR="#FF0000"]f1 [/COLOR]

And later:

> [ 1543.878966] wlp37s0: send auth to 48:d3:43:79:58:[COLOR="#FF0000"]f7[/COLOR] 

Notice that the MAC addresses of the two differ by a single character. Your wireless may be dropping because there are two wireless access points with the same name and password. This is typical when you have a 2.4 gHz segment and a 5 gHz segment of the same router. Your wireless may be roaming, looking for a better connection. If this is the case, I suggest that you rename the access points; something like VM4288165-2.4 and VM4288165-5.

The 5 gHz segment is preferred and I’d recommend that you connect to it and stop the roaming, looking for a better connection, just like my ex-girlfriend.

Your wireless may also be dropping because of power management; that is, the feature where the card partially powers down to save battery power during periods of inactivity and then, ideally, powers back up seamlessly when activity resumes. Let's disable power saving to see if it helps. From the terminal:

```
sudo sed -i 's/3/2/' /etc/NetworkManager/conf.d/*
```
    
Your wireless may be dropping because the channel to which it was connected has suddenly changed. Please set a fixed channel and not autoselect.

Please check the settings in the router. WPA2-AES is preferred; not any WPA and WPA2 mixed mode and certainly not TKIP. 

Next, reboot the router and let’s see if there is any improvement.

However, the OCD neatfreak in me wants to rule out as many side issues as possible so we can see what's really going on. Even more speculatively, I wonder if the iommu and dma issues are actually a symptom of the wireless card going/being bad; that is, electrically defective.

The rtl8821ae doesn't have a bad reputation here and at AskUbuntu. I myself prefer Intel simply because my first laptop had an Intel in it and worked perfectly.

I notice that there are a few GRUB boot parameters that deal with iommu and we may well try a few.

If there are no improvements after making the router changes, blacklist the wireless driver:

```
sudo -i
echo "blacklist rtl8821ae"  >  /etc/modprobe.d/rtl8821ae.conf
modprobe -r rtl8821ae
exit

```Next run:

```
sudo cat /var/log/syslog
```

Note the timestamp at the end; like this:

```
[COLOR="#FF0000"]Jul 12 17:33:14 [/COLOR]T440p systemd-timesyncd[887]: Initial synchronization to time
```Now run for a while and check syslog for entries after the time you noted. Are there now any related to dma or iommu?
