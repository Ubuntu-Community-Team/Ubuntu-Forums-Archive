Hi sulekha,

A patch panel is used to make wiring tidy in a large network.

Say you're in an office with 2 companies. Each company has 3 computers. Each company has their own Internet connection.

WITHOUT a patch panel, you'd have all the network cables from the office coming out the wall. Half would connect to one router, and half to the other one.

Imagine if one user wanted to connect to the other router - you have no way of knowing which cable is which!

So instead, you connect all 6 network cables to a patch panel. The ports on the panel are labelled 1,2,3,4,5,6.

Then you simply take a short cable from port 1, and connect it to whichever router you want. Same for all the rest.

If a user wants to change router, they just tell you the number which is written on their network socket on the wall. Say it's number 4. You just look in port 4 on your patch panel, unplug the cable from the first router, and change it to the second one.

I have made some drawings to help you.

While the concept is simple, it's proving hard to explain!
