### Post by praseodym on 2013-06-28
Hi folks,

obviously the user does not have any connection at all ;)

Please download this package and save it in "Downloads":

[http://de.archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/multiverse/l/linux-firmware-nonfree/linux-firmware-nonfree_1.14_all.deb](http://de.archive.ubuntu.com/ubuntu/pool/multiverse/l/linux-firmware-nonfree/linux-firmware-nonfree_1.14_all.deb)

Installation/Uninstallation of the Broadcom-STA driver:
```

sudo dpkg -i ~/Downloads/linux-*.deb
sudo apt-get remove --purge bcmwl-kernel-source
sudo rm /etc/modprobe.d/blacklist-bcm43.conf
sudo rm /etc/modprobe.d/broadcom-sta-common.conf
sudo rm /etc/modprobe.d/broadcom-sta-dkms.conf 
```
Reboot

### Post by Hadaka on 2013-06-28
@rrich1974...
the additional drivers..proprietary driver offer is
broadcom-kernel-source.
that driver Blacklists the ssb module and b43.
B44 and B43 share the use of the ssb module...so
if the wired requires that and the wireless.  you fail to connect,
in your case...most likely your wired connection does not use the b44 driver
but...your wireless..b43...bcm4311 does. Therefore loading the "additional driver"
loads the broadcom-kerne-source and kills your wireless. Apply my above guide
to remove the broadcom-kernel-source driver and then load the
linux-firmware-nonfree driver that your bcm4311 requires.
