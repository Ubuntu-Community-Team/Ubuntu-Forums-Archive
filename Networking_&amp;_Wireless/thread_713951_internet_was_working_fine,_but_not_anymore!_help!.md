### Post by Chappy7777 on 2008-03-03
I suggest you try what Eric said. Just unplug the power to the router, unless there is some reason you can't. I have done that with mine, with various success (when using XP, it works fine in Ubuntu).

What version are you using? I am using Feisty and there is an icon in the top panel on the right side that looks like a monitor. Hold the mouse over it and see what it says. Try using it to turn the connection off, then on again. Sometimes the strangest things work.
