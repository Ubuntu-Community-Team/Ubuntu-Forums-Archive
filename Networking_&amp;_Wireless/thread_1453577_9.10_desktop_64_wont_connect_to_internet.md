### Post by ubername on 2010-04-13
> **honey toast said:**
> ok, I tried : ping -v -c 3 [www.google.com](www.google.com)

and after about 15 seconds it returned:  ping:unknown host [www.google.com](www.google.com)


I think I know why - your DNS settings are wrong. Right click on the network icon and select 'edit connections'. Click on the 'Auto eth0 and select edit. Go to the ipv4 tab and set the method to  Automatic (DHCP) if it isn't already. Save the settings and then left click on the icon and select disconnect. Then left click on the icon again and selet Auto eth0.

That may do it.

If not, select the method Automatic (DHCP addresses only and put 208.67.222.222 in the DNS field (that's an openDNS server)
then disconnect and reconnect again.

### Post by dec3169 on 2010-04-13
Sounds like dns to me - try this:
ping -c 3 -v 74.125.47.99

If that works, post your /etc/resolv.conf file:
cat /etc/resolv.conf

Have you tried to ping your router's IP address?
ping -c 3 -v router.ip.address.here

If you can't ping that you may not have a good dhcp address.
If so, open up a terminal and do this:
sudo dhclient eth0

And then try the pings again.
