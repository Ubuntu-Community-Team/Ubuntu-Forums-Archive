### Post by Don DeGregori on 2008-07-27
Ubuntu 8.04 is on my Fujitsu P5010D laptop, and wireless works great, following this:
[http://linuxwireless.org/en/users/Drivers/b43](http://linuxwireless.org/en/users/Drivers/b43)

When out and about, sometimes the free wi-fi signal is too weak to connect to. I decided to try something.
I noticed the weekend Fry's ad had an AirLink101 AP431W on sale for $17.99, normally $44.99. It's a 4-in-1 Wireless Access Point. One of the modes is called AP Client. Reading up on it, this mode would be perfect to use with my laptop. The unit has a removable antenna showing a SMA jack to attach a high gain Yagi antenna. I see MFJ has just such an antenna, MFJ-1800 for $29.95. So, I bought the Access Point, set it up and got on the Internet. The best part, it plugs into the LAN jack on the laptop, so Ubuntu sees it as a wired connection. No hassle with wireless. I haven't ordered the Yagi yet, but probably will next week

If anyone is interested in the AP setup, let me know here, because the manual does not cover an important shortcut.
