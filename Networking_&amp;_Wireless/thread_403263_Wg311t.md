### Post by ariel on 2007-05-13
By now I hope you have already fixed your problem.

Just for the records, 127.0.0.1 is not your router; it is actually a loopback address in your own PC. It is supposed to always answer pings as long as the IP protocol stack in your system is running. This does not meaning that your actual ethernet interfaces are connected.
