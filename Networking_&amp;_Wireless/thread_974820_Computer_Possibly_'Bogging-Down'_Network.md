### Post by azurepancake on 2008-11-07
For awhile now, my wireless connection to my router has been very flaky. A few times a day, I will loose my connection and can take several minutes to reconnect. This is only effecting my personal computer in my bedroom (Ubuntu Hardy). There is only one other computer connecting wirelessly and three others connecting by category 5 Ethernet cabling. The other computer connecting wirelessly, *doesn't* exhibit any connection issues.

Also, maybe a couple times a week, my wireless router will ceases to function and no computers (even the ones wired in directly) cannot connect to the Internet, until the router is powered off and back on.

So, fed up with the issue - I thought I'd try a couple things. First thing I did, was disconnect the one other computer (Windows XP) that is connecting wirelessly from the network. To my amazement, this has appeared to resolve the issue. My personal computer in my room now doesn't have any connection issues. And once I reestablished the connection on the Windows XP machine, I began to have connection problems almost instantly on the Ubuntu machine.

Due to this, I have a question for you all. What would be the best way to approach this? Maybe there are wireless networking tools for Linux that I can use, to see how that computers NIC might be effecting the network this way? And once I have this software, what exactly should I look for?

Sorry, if I have been too vague. If you need anymore information, please don't hesitate to ask!

Thank you for reading :)
