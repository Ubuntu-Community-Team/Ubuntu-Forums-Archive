Ok: I am not condoning this particualr device, as it's just my system.:
Yes. Got it from Dick Smith - it's a Virgin one. I belive they can be unlocked easily, but you can also opt get it unlocked for "free" after $80 worth of top ups. Which is fine for me, as I can go a telstra sim for more remote places (v.expensive but at least there is more coverage) - anyway it's an option to have it.
It comes with a sim card, a bit like a phone, it sits behind the battery. 

I got a gig of data included. 
Because the data is transfered to wiFi, I have no problem with the network manager picking up the signal, it's just a wireless modem to it I guess. Just type in the password..
It can cope with 3 wifi devices at a time, printers etc - though I havent needed to do that.

Took it out to various parts of Sydney and got some bloody good speeds (for Mobile) so it does operate at 7.2, might do a speed test somewhere just for the sake of it - but I am just relieved.

OF COURSE, my house is in a null spot:p:p:P  - but I put the device up above the window and it's ok. 15 metre range, without having to trail a usb extension.

It's about $199, but for me that beats the price of an OS And I can use it in the future.
Have heard that Lucid has much better device management for usb devices, but quite honestly, this device is so idiot proof and usable  that I will continue with it. 
It comes with software for mac and Win, but you don't need it. Just turn it on access (tried it on pc and mac)

I'm sure it will soon become cheap and outdated. But then...so will I. And of course, no coverage...no connection, though it will drop down to gprs.
So I have NO excuse (apart from maybe cinepaint in .deb) to not use Linux pretty much all the time.
