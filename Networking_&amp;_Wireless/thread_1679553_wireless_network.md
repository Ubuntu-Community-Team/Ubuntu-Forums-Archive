Your router can be used to filter MAC addresses (most useful).  To look at it another way, you can block everything except those devices that you want to allow.  Read the manual for your router.  MAC addresses can be faked but it's usually enough to make it hard for the average bozo.

Personally, I'd just change the key and not give it out again.  Either way it's going to take a little effort on your part.

At the same time make sure you're running WPA encryption, with a complex key.  This will make it harder to crack.  A WEP key can be cracked fairly easily.  WPA isn't totally secure either.

Set the router to not broadcast the SSID.  A hidden network is that little bit harder to crack.

Make sure the firewall (in the router) is active, and that you are notified when attempts are made on your router (which they will be).

Unfortunately there's just no way to completely secure, especially if you are handing out the WPA key.
