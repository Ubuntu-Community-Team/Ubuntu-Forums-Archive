So, here goes...
I have been trying to connect my desktop to the internet, which isn't in the same room as my wireless router and modem.

You may say, why not use wireless, I could, but am too cheap to buy a card/usb thing for it. (especially when I know there is another way to do it)

So, I thought I would be able to do what I used to do with my laptop for Xbox Live, connect my desktop to my laptop's ethernet port, then use my laptop's wireless card to 'bridge' the connection between the two.

(This was fairly easy to do in Windows:

1. Go to "network connections"

2. Right click on "wireless connection"

3. then "properties"

4. Then choose the “advance tab”

5. Then in the "internet connection sharing" tick the box "allow other networks users to connect through this computers internet connection")


I am asking if there is anyway to do the same thing (easily) in Ubuntu?
