### Post by DoorGunner on 2006-09-20
Unfortunately i dissagree with you motin. (no offence intended) According to the router itself it appears that the extended range feature is a function of MIMO. If you check your router read the XR fucntion explanation. It States:

> Enable eXtended Range - eXtended Range (XR) Technology provides significantly longer range than basic 802.11 by maintaining connectivity when signals are made faint when passing through dense walls, floors, or other barriers. [COLOR="Magenta"]XR products require no additional configuration by end-users and are fully interoperable with standard 802.11 technologies[/COLOR]. 

Generally in any class of device range is a function of antenna gain and reciever sensitivity plus a few other environmental factors. Every time you increase the number of antennas for the same purpose you will have an inherent increase in gain.  It will also provide a certain amount of directivity. The netgear has 7 antennas providing over 100 different combinations. So in actuallity the reciever will hunt out the best signal combination to your device. So a weak signal may go through the thick door, but a better signal may have been reflected off two walls. The XR function more than likely turns MIMO on and off, allowing you to find the best possible signal. (MIMO = Multi inputs Multi outputs)

Here is how CNET discribes it. [http://reviews.cnet.com/Netgear_WPN824_router/4505-3319_7-31312124.html](http://reviews.cnet.com/Netgear_WPN824_router/4505-3319_7-31312124.html)

> Netgear unit includes seven internal antennas built right into the unit's circuit board. The unit's seven antennas help the router counteract the negative impact of interference by reconfiguring themselves on the fly to adjust to changing environmental conditions. If the signal of one of the transceiving antennas becomes weak, the unit automatically searches for a better signal from the other antennas and adjusts accordingly.

The fact that they sell routers and cards in pairs is more than likely a marketing ploy to get you to spend your millions with that company. As long as it has the same features like WEP WPA etc it shouldnt make any difference to you.
