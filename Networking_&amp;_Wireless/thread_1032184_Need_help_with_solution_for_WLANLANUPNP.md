### Post by bogoliubov on 2009-01-06
Hello. In my home I have one laptop, one stationary PC and one media extender (Pinnacle Showcenter 200), all connected to a Belkin F5D7230 wireless router (using WEP encryption). Both computers run Ubuntu 8.10 (32 bit), and the PC runs Mediatomb UPnP server.

Before the PC had a wired connection while the laptop and the Showcenter were using the wireless connection. For this setup everything worked OK; the Showcenter work without problems and LAN performance between the PC and the laptop was OK. 

Now I had to move the PC to another room. As I had a D-Link DWL-DWL-G122 USB dongle lying around I used this with the PC. The connection works, but the LAN performance is really bad. And watching stuff on the Showcenter is not working very well anymore; it lags and sometimes loses the connection.

So I need help with a solution for this. My ideas so far are these:
1. Get a router with better performance (perhaps pre-N or 108 MB/s stuff). Will this be of any advantage under Ubuntu?

2. Get another wireless card for the PC and use one for the LAN and one for the connection to the Showcenter 200. I'm not sure if this is possible software-wise (Mediatomb), or if there will be large problems with interference...?

Any other ideas and comments on the two above are greatly appreciated!

### Post by jimv on 2009-01-07
Not elegant, but effective:  consider running ethernet cable instead of using wireless.  If you want to do it easily, get white cable, and run it either along your baseboards or along the ceiling.  You can get special cable fasteners (they look like wide, rounded staples) for this at a hardware store.
