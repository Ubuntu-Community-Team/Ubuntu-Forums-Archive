### Post by Topsiho on 2005-11-21
If you have an ADSL-connection, or intend to have one, please don't go for a USB modem as these modems are very difficult to install, if they can be installed, which might not always be the case.

If you have a home network, or intend to have one, buy an (ethernet) modem router combination, with a builtin firewall. I myself consider mine as one of the best buys I ever did, makes me quite happy. Even if you have a single computer the firewall may  make it worth to buy the thing....

If you buy one you should be aware that there exist two varieties. As I bought mine two years ago, I forgot the exact details, but there is an A and a B variety, of which you need just one. So get yourself informed browsing the internet with Google :)

I have a cabled home network, most people nowadays go for a wireless one.
However I have serious trouble getting my wireless card in my Centrino laptop to work with WAP security together with Linux. With Windows it's just so so ...(which might be a Dutchism meaning that something is still left to be wished). With wireless security is much more a problem than in a cabled network. Then WAP security should be the minimum except when you **intend **to share your connection with other people.

Topsiho

### Post by landotter on 2005-11-21
If you have ISA ports, you can still get hardware based USR sportster internal modems for cheeeep on eBay. Otherwise serial external modems should work great.

I do enjoy Gnome as my desktop environment, but be aware that KDE's "kppp" is the absolute best way to painlessly dial a modem.

If you want to configure a modem in Gnome or other environ, "pppconfig" works great if you don't mind the command line.
