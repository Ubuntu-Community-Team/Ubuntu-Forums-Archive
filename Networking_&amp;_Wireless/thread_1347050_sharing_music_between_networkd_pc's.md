### Post by em3raldxiii on 2009-12-05
Hmm, first clarify for me ... do you mean you want your dads computer to tell your computer to play music through your speakers, or do you mean you just want his computer to be able to play the music FROM your computer on HIS speakers?

The second is simple - you want to share the directory that contains the music (right click on the folder, choose share, go from there), then you want to browse to that folder from your dad's computer, and maybe link that folder to a convenient place on his laptop.  Now, you can drag & drop the files from that folder to the music player playlist (such as VLC which is what I like to use).

If you want his computer to control yours, then you might want to use Remote Desktop.

You might also consider getting a router that supports an external mass storage device (like a USB hard drive).  This is a very nice and professional way to do things ;)

### Post by em3raldxiii on 2009-12-05
Like I said, it should be fairly painless.  If you can navigate to the folder on your computer that contains the audio files, then you should have no trouble getting a music player like VLC to play the files (stream) directly across the network with your dad's computer.  In this scenario, it has to be done from *his* computer's end (as long as the appropriate folder on your computer is set to be shared).

Now, if you configure your router right, you don't have to worry about other people accessing your NAS; if they don't have your password, then they don't have access - simple as that.  The benefit of going this route is that your computer does not have to be turned on for his computer to be able to access the music files.
