### Post by jarvoll on 2008-01-23
Fixed!

Since this was a fresh install, I didn't really think it could have been Firefox that was broken, so I focussed on your second point.  

I logged into the router to try to access port control (to open port 80 if I could), and as I was fooling around I noticed that the router had a LAN client at 10.1.1.3, which turned out to be the V300 VoiP adapter.

I logged in to the V300's admin page and looked for port control again, when it occurred to me that the physical architecture had the 'net connection running through the V300 when it didn't have to be.  I changed everything over so that the 'net line was just coming through the D-Link router, and Hey! Presto! Here I am typing in Ubuntu.

So thanks heaps for the step in the right direction!  I don't know if there's any way to get a connection through the VoiP adapter, but hopefully 1) anyone with the same problem as me will now know how to fix it, and 2) future releases of Ubuntu will be able to deal with VoiP adapters automatically.

Sounds like the dreaded "I can ping, but can't get any pages to resolve" problem.

The fastest cure for this is to put your ISP's primary and backup dns server addresses *inside the router setup* pages.  If you don't know them, you can take a look at [www.opendns.org](www.opendns.org) at the bottom right.

Our windows-centric soho routers sometimes don't play by the *nix rules. :)
