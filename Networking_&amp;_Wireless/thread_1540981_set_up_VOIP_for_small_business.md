### Post by quixote on 2010-07-30
Asterisk is *the* open source PBX.  You can even install it via synaptic.  I run voip at home, so I know enough about Asterisk to say that I found it very complicated.  But it's used by companies large and small for exactly what you want, so it may be worth the study.  Wikipedia has a good intro: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asterisk_%28PBX%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asterisk_%28PBX%29).  As for the actual voip provider, I use Callcentric, whose main business I gather is businesses.  I'd recommend them very highly.  No down time, and they really work to help with any questions or difficulties. I haven't had any trouble with them in over five years of service.
