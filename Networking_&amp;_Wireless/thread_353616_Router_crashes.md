### Post by jml on 2007-02-04
I agree with Pilot JLR.  I have had a DSL connection for several years.  When I first set up my account, I had a similar problem and had to Power Cycle, (ie unplug and plug in) the device.  It took several calls to my ISP to resolve the issue.  For me it was a combination of a defective DSL modem, and an error in configuration at my ISP.  I would suggest that you contact your service provider and ask for help.  Oh, by the way, depending on your service provider's policy on Linux, don't volunteer the information that you are running Linux.  Many ISP's do not support Linux and use it as a convenient excuse not to solve your problem.
