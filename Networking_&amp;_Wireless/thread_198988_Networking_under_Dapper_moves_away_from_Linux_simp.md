### Post by auberginepop on 2006-06-18
After a recent upgrade to Dapper I suffered what appear to be all to common networking problems. These are now fixed. In the process I had a look at Network Manager and some of the other new developments in Dapper. Although those the applicatins seem to work well, one of the things that I find makes linux such a pleasure to work with is the ability to configure almost everything by editing simple text files in the etc directory. This becomes especially important when setting up servers without a gui environment.

By handing over configuration to intermediate applications is Dapper moving away from its Linux roots and towards a Windoze style?
