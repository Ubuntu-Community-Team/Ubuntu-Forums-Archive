### Post by weatherman2 on 2015-08-05
You can use OpenVPN in Ubuntu - I've been using it for years, both as a client (to connect to OpenVPN servers I have set up myself) and as a server.  I have an OpenVPN server on my home network, and I connect to it remotely with my Ubuntu laptop.

I have no idea what "Hotspot Shield" is - a paid service?  If they use OpenVPN, then yes, you should be able to connect to them with your Ubuntu computer.  They may not necessarily support Linux, but the certificates etc. for OpenVPN should work.
