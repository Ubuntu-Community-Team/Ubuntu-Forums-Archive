### Post by chili555 on 2007-02-07
I'm not quite sure I fully understand your question. Are you trying to set up a static IP address so outsiders can reach your website or server or for gaming? Or, are you just trying to surf the web and download and email?

If it's the latter, as I suspect, there is probably nothing you need to do! Plug the router in after connecting it to the cable-modem or dsl in the appropriate slot, then hook your computer to the router with a dynamic IP address and go to town!

How does the traffic get to your PC if the address is dynamic, changing from day to day, you ask? Well, routers are smarter than they look. They do, out of the box, Network Address Translation, or NAT. Your computer, let's call her 192.168.1.3, asks the router for a web page; the router goes out on the web and gets it and passes it back to 192.168.1.3. At the same time, your roommate/GF/wife/life-partner/husband/child asks for a web page on another computer, let's say 192.168.1.4. The router gets it and passes the page to the correct computer. 

If you need more help, please post back and we'd be happy to help.
