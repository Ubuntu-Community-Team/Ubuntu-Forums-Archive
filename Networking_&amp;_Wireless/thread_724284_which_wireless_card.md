### Post by unutbu on 2008-03-14
I am just a novice, but I was recently in the market for a wireless card, and these are some problems I encountered:

1) Not all cards sold in around the world will be easily available in your country.
People might recommend cards that work great, but you may not be able to buy them (easily) in the UK.

2) You may find lots of reports on the web or in this thread about cards that work, but if the cards are old then a new model may now be in stores and again you might have trouble locating an older card. Beware that as the version number of a card changes, so can the underlying chipset, and this info is often not reported on the box. It can even be hard to find on the internet. (The method to get a wireless card working depends on the chipset).

Although finding out what works for other people was very helpful, 
I also suggest you visit your local and/or internet stores and find out what cards are available to you.
