### Post by Giradman on 2007-11-16
**Scott** - not sure that I can be specific about 'how' to get your Netgear router talking to the IBM laptop w/ a wireless connection; but you'll need a 'wireless' access point attached to your Netgear router along w/ a PCMCIA or USB 'wireless' adapter for the laptop - you likely already know this need, but I would probably check the Netgear website (and their forums) for a compatible combination that will work w/ your current router.

In addition, you might want to considered adding more RAM to that laptop - I have an old IBM TPX40 (almost 4 yrs of age) - replaced an 18 GB HD that had died, then put on Ubuntu 7.1 - install went fine (used the alternate CD); the laptop had 256 MB RAM - seemed somewhat slow to me (i.e. opening programs, etc.), so decided to add another 256 MB RAM (just $24 from Kingston going through Google w/ a 25% discount) - really noticeable change in the speed of the laptop.  Good luck! :)
