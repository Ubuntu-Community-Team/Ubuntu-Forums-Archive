### Post by spd106 on 2007-07-10
Aside from the obvious one [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/WifiDocs/WirelessCardsSupported).

Also try the FSF's website. They only recommend cards with open source drivers.
[http://www.fsf.org/resources/hw/net/wireless/cards.html](http://www.fsf.org/resources/hw/net/wireless/cards.html).

The Linux Wireless Wiki has a list of chipsets with good support on the mac80211 stack included in the recently released kernel 2.6.22, which will be in Ubuntu 7.10 (Gutsy).
[http://linuxwireless.org/](http://linuxwireless.org/).

Buying from a vendor who supports GNU/Linux is probably the best route. Though you might have to pay more or wait a little longer. I saw a bargain Atheros based 802.11b/g pci card for £8 +p&p a few weeks ago, some random site was offloading stock. So if you look around you can find them.
