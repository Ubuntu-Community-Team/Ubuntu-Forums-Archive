### Post by jml on 2006-05-09
A couple suggestions.  Even though you have two identicle laptops, check the specifications of each carefully.  You might want to go into your bios setup screen to see what type of wireless card is installed in each laptop and see if they are the same.  Manufacturers are notorious for switching out different wireless cards in the same laptop.  (On an HP laptop I own, the same model comes with two different cards.)

Also, I have found that Ubuntu does a much better job of managing network connections, both wired and wireless, than Fedora Core does.  I've never been satisfied with Fedora's capabilities with wireless.  As to Fedora Core 5 specifically, it has been engineered specifically to "enhance" the desktop experience.  As I read more about it, it turns out that many applications that used to be installed by default are now left out.  This may also be why Fedora Core 5 does not work.

I have a question for you.  Since Ubuntu works perfectly on one laptop, why not just install Ubuntu on the other laptop as well?
