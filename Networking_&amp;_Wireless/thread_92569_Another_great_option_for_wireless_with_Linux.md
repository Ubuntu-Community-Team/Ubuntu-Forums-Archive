### Post by gexla on 2005-11-19
** Note:  Scroll down to skip story **

This is not a request for help but I have been so pleased about a solution I have come across that I thought I would post about it here.

Internet connections have been a real problem with my adoption of Linux.  In the early days of spotty drivers and a lack of user friendly distro's I could never get my dial-up connection to work.  When I finally went to a broadband connection I adopted a wireless network at the same time.  This of course was back in the days when wireless support for Linux was horrible. 

So with Linux it has always been something.  Without an internet connection my computer is largely useless wether I am using Linux or Windows.  Now with the coming days of "the internet as a platform" it is looking more possible than ever that my Linux box could someday replace WIndows.  Unfortunately until now I have always had to keep a Windows box handy for Internet Connection Sharing with Linux running on my secondary and much slower machine.  (unfortunately if you are a heavy gamer and graphical designer as I am then it is still not possible for me to dump Windows entirely)

*** Now to the point ***

I picked up a Belkin F5D7330 802.11g Wireless Ethernet Adapter the other day and now for the first time I am able to surf without the Windows lifeline support!  This device receives a signal from my wireless router and plugs into my computer via an ethernet cable.  The best part is that no drivers are required for the device (except for those needed to get your ethernet card working which is much easier than wireless.)  The device is much more spendy than a wireless card but considering all the time I had to spend on getting a wireless card working and then having to buy more than one card because the first one did not work, it is well worth it.  It is also handy for connecting a game console connected to wireless.  One of the best features might be the ability to connect this adapter to a network device such as a router or switch to have a always on (does not rely on a computer with a shared connection being booted) shared internet connection between multiple computers that do not have a wireless card.

There are other brands besides Belkin that have this device but Amazon had the best reviews for the Belkin brand.  Anyone who is fed up with trying to get wireless cards workin with Linux might give this device a try!

### Post by gexla on 2005-11-20
**Linksys WET54G Wireless-G Ethernet Bridge**

_Description:_
[I]The versatile Wireless-G Ethernet Bridge can make any wired Ethernet-equipped device a part of your wireless network. At home, use the Bridge to connect game consoles, set-top boxes, or computers to your Wireless-G network and its shared high-speed Internet connection. In the office, convert your Ethernet-wired printer, scanner, camera, notebook or desktop into a wireless networked device.

It's completely driver-free, so it works on any platform and under any operating system! Since there's no drivers to load, setup is a snap -- configure the network settings through your PC's web browser, then plug it into your device and go. And physical installation is simplified by support for Power Over Ethernet. With an optional POE Adapter, you can mount the Bridge wherever you want -- power and data are both supplied through the Category 5 Ethernet cable.[/I] 

**D-link DWL-G810 Wireless Bridge**

**Netgear WGE101  54 Mbps Wireless Ethernet Bridge** 

**Belkin F5D7330 802.11g Wireless Ethernet Adapter **

Comments:
The above are all different brands of the device that I am using.  I suggest you type the model numbers into a shopping site such as Amazon or NewEgg and read all of the reviews.  The reviews will tell you how they work and will also give you valuable advice.  I found at the time the Belkin brand had the best reviews and mine works great.  Not only is my wireless working on Linux but the bridge has increased my wireless speed enormously.  The other thing I am really happy about as mentioned above is that I will be able to plug this device into a switch and share my connection with all of my computers in the area without relying on any kind of internet connection sharing provided by one computer that has to stay booted at all times.  

Also note that most of the above devices only support WEP out of the box but will also support WPA after a firmware upgrade.  This is very important as WEP is not secure.

Note that some of the devices are able to get power through ethernet while others require a power adapter.  The Belkin requires a power adapter which means it is not quite as mobile as the others, but this is not a problem for me.
