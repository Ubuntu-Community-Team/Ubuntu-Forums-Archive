### Post by espressobeanie on 2009-11-13
It's a kernel problem with linux kernel 2.6.31.6, I imagine. I can connect, but after approx 2-3 hrs, something crashes, not driver related, and terminates that connection. The only way you can get back online is to reboot, or unplug and replug the device, open a root terminal and play with iwconfig and dhclient. It's easier to do for a wifi USB device than PCI.

Useful commands (if interface is named wlan0):

ifconfig stop wlan0
dhclient -r wlan0
ifconfig start wlan0
*'dhclient wlan0'

That star means, that the re-enable command for dhclient is inaccurate. Wiki search that cmd. 

You could always downgrade the kernel to the next lowest one. I've heard that wireless is much more stable there. Do this from the Synaptic Package Mgr. It's under 'linux-header'.
