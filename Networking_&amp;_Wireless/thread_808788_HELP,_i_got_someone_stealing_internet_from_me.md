### Post by cab_bae on 2008-05-26
Well basically need to know what router you have so that you can put a password to protect your network as well as change your SSID so that the person stealing wont be able to change the password. This is to prevent the person stealing to know what kind of router  you have. For instance I have a D-link 524 and the way to configure it, you have to connect to it and for my router it is http:\\192.168.0.1 and from there I can do everything I need to prevent other people from knowing what kind of router I have. What I did was change my SSID to say Abraham instead of D-link. After that when you go to http:\\192.168.0.1 it asks for a users name and password. Default factory settings are Admin and password field is empty. So those settings I changed to prevent anyone from trying to change my configurations and passwords.
