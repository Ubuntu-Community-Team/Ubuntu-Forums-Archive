### Post by tturrisi on 2006-10-05
Generally, a thin client is a comp that boots from a cdrom or floppy disk and the os & apps are on the cdrom or floppy, everything runs from a ram drive.  Most linux based thin clients are used as firewalls on a lan or gateways for the lan.  In the past, a thin client was something on the order of a dummy-terminal, i.e. the apps run from the server & all the user has is a keyboard & monitor.

Thin Client:  [http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/t/thin_client.html](http://www.webopedia.com/TERM/t/thin_client.html)

(thin kl&#299;´&nt) In client/server applications, a client designed to be especially small so that the bulk of the data processing occurs on the server.

Although the term thin client usually refers to software, it is increasingly used for computers, such as network computers and Net PCs, that are designed to serve as the clients for client/server architectures. A thin client is a network computer without a hard disk drive, whereas a fat client includes a disk drive.
