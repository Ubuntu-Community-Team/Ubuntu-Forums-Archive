### Post by sofasurfer on 2012-03-08
I have been trying to get my network running. I had a linksys router n300. It was overkill for my needs so I took it back and bought a Netgear n150. I was very happy to see that even though they have all the standard Windblows crap on their dvd they also had Linux documentation. This is a very good sign that we are being recognized. So, I ask that you try to purchase Netgear products that supply Linux documentation and packages.

Mostly I want to add that my adapter was giving me a lot of trouble getting it set up. So I contacted [www.thinkpenguin.com](www.thinkpenguin.com) and grilled them on their product compatability. I then ordered a n150 adapter from them.

My router was configured with my network name and security type (WPA & WPA2). I put my adapter into the pci slot and started the computer. A window came up that said "networks available. click the icon and choose the network you desire". I clicked on my network and I was on line, just that fast. So I am highly recommending thinkpenguin.com for your Linux hardware. This was the easiest hardware install I have ever had. They will get my business from now on.
