### Post by SeijiSensei on 2022-01-25
The better solution is to edit /etc/systemd/resolved.conf and add your preferred server to the "DNS=" line and any alternatives to the "FallBackDNS" field. Use spaces to separate multiple servers.  You can also specify search domains there as well. For instance,
```

[Resolve]
DNS=192.168.100.100 
FallbackDNS=97.107.141.13 8.8.8.8
Domains=example.com example.org
#LLMNR=no
#MulticastDNS=no
#DNSSEC=no
#DNSOverTLS=no
#Cache=yes
#DNSStubListener=yes
#ReadEtcHosts=yes

```

### Post by TheFu on 2022-03-07
This thread clearly shows why **resolved** and **network-manager** are purged from all my systems.  If anything bad happens, they are just in there to gunk up the works. They are completely unnecessary.  I can't say that every other method is "easier", since that is dependent on the person's skills, but for a desktop that never moves, setting up the networking once certainly is easier and doesn't tend to be broke due to resolved or network-manager failures.

If the routing isn't correct due to all the plugging and unplugging, connecting and disconnecting of the different network devices, fix that.

As for DNS, all the complexity added to make things seem "easier", just isn't necessary.  That complexity is just trying to put 2 lines into the /etc/resolv.conf file. Nothing more. If you can use sudoedit, then you can manually make the DNS point to 1.0.0.1 and 1.1.1.1 and never worry about it again.  2 lines. That's it. But be certain to disable any of the 'helper' tools that can get in the way and constantly try to change that file.
