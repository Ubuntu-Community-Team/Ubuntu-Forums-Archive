### Post by Handssolow on 2011-03-02
A Toshiba 35MX-S114 laptop with Atheros AR5001 works but unless the laptop is in the same room as the router I am not able to connect.

After a relative gave me their old laptop, I installed 10.04 Lucid but I am having problems with the Atheros AR5001 based wifi. I've been able to update the OS using wifi with the laptop in the same room as the router. If I move about 9 metres away from the router into another room, with applet showing 3/4 blue bars and effectively still within line of sight of the router, I lose the connection. Wifi receive is working so well that the Network Manager Applet detects several of my neighbours' routers but not well enough for me to connect to our router.

Originally on the Toshiba laptop under XP there's supposed to be a hotkey Fn+F8 which enables/disables the wifi antenna, in contrast Fn+F9 does disable the touchpad as stated in the manual.

Any suggestions? My router is recognised but I cannot connect. Is it a driver issue? I think others have had this problem but what's the answer?

### Post by Handssolow on 2011-03-02
I've just got it working and have a slightly red face, I should have known. It's a bit like my netbook.
There's a slide switch on the right hand side of the laptop's case that I'd not previously noticed which enables/disables some aspect of the wifi system. It was in the off position. There's also an orange LED indicator light. When the switch is on, I can connect to my router much further away that before. There's a wifi symbol on the black switch.

I'd read the manual that I'd downloaded from Toshiba which only seems to mention the Fn+F8 hotkey though there's an illustration showing the switch.

So, if the wifi slide switch is in the off position, I can connect to my router but only if I am in the same room. In the on position the range is much greater and I can connect to the router from another room.
