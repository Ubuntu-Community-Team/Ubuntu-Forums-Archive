### Post by Junkieman on 2009-06-12
Hello. IRC allows you to chat without registering a nick, however because so many people have registered, chances are a nick you want has already been taken.

Registering a nick allows you to own that nick, ie when you connect to an IRC server, you issue a special command with your chosen password, otherwise the server will automatically rename you to a default nick after a few minutes.

These commands are done sending a /msg to the nickserv channel, I assume you can issue these commands through the normal Pidgin chat window, but I have never tried Pidgin with IRC. Full-featured IRC clients are probably your best bet, since many of them have build-in functionality to automatically execute those commands for you!

[See here]("http://linuxbasics.org/irc/how_to_register_an_irc-nickname") for details on the registration commands, and why not give XChat try, it's in Add/Remove :)
