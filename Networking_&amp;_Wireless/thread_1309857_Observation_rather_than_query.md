### Post by The Digger on 2009-11-01
I've been reading loads on the forum about the problems people have been having getting their wireless to work after upgrading to 9.10, and I'd just like to share my experience, and solution, as a *totally* non-technical user. After the upgrade, I went to look for a device driver for my wireless card, but there was not a *single* hardware driver listed, for *anything*. I followed loads of postings on the various fora but try as I might I just couldn't get my wireless to work. I therefore re-loaded 9.04 from the CD I still have, loaded the driver provided (MadWiFi, I think?), and now I've got my wireless back.................

My point is this - it is just beyond belief to me that the developers haven't yet sorted this out, it seems to be a recurring problem from release to release. I'm a *massive* fan of Linux, and support the principle totally, BUT until the developers can make the whole wireless experience as slick as it is in Windows now (which connects instantly, with no effort at all on the part of the user having to delve into obscure programming code etc) in all versions, *even* the appalling Vista,  then Linux will always remain the minority OS, which is such a damn shame.
