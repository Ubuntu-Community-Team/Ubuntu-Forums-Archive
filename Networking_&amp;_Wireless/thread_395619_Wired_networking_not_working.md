### Post by dannyboy79 on 2007-03-28
well we need to make sure that the forcedeth module is loaded. so type in 
modprobe -l | grep for
at the command line. look into downloading the latest forcedeth.ko driver also. here is a forum about the trouble with your ethernet controller, check it out. [http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=74738](http://www.nvnews.net/vbulletin/showthread.php?t=74738)
that other link I had posted I can't seem to find but look into this: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=231330&page=2](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=231330&page=2)
it states that the issue is fixed in edgy and it's just a dns issue but looking at your resolv.conf it appears as though your dns server is defined. are you using edgy? can you ping your other boxes as well as gogle? I also notice that you have wlan0 listed with an ip, you don't want to have all these differnet interfaces active at the same time, please "de-activate" your wirless and any others that are not your wired connection. Also, is your dsl router really a dns server? I am pretty sure it's not, it's just a dns forwarder, this is a problem. If you can log into your router and find out what the real dns servers are that are provided by your isp, than we need to add 1 or both of them within your /etc/dhcp3/dhclient.conf file. So edit that file and find this line:
#prepend domain-name-servers 127.0.0.1;
and then make it look like this:
prepend domain-name-servers 208.67.222.222 208.67.220.220;
(NOTE: put in whatever dns servers you got from your dsl router or from your isp, make sure you leave a space between them and leave the ; at the end)

OR

you could change this line found within that same dhclient.conf file
domain-name, domain-name-servers, host-name,
to:
domain-name, host-name,
I removed the domain-name-servers so that whatever you put within the dns section within your system, admin, networking dialog box, they will stay there and won't get changed everytime you get an ip from the dhcp server. If you do this option, just put the dns servers you got from the dsl router or from your isp into that dialog box that I mentioned before and since your dhclient.conf isn't asking for dns servers from the dhcp server, those will stay there.

NOTE: Don't do both solutions, only do 1!!

let me know how it goes
