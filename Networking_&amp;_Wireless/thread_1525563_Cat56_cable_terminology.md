What is horizontal vs stranded?

This can probably be restated as: horizontal vs. vertical and solid vs multi-strand.   

In the first example we would be talking about the wiring for a single level (i.e. one floor) vs the wiring the connects multiple floors (i.e vertical or riser) together.

In the second  example we are talking about the physical construction of the wire.  Solid (single strand)vs multi-strand wire making up the twisted pairs.

In permanent wiring you can use single strand wire for the twisted pairs as it does not get moved (and therefore broken).  Multi-strand is more flexible and is typically used for patch cords where it can withstand the stresses of being moved when switching hardware and ports.   
> 
If I remember right, UTP is unshielded twisted pair and STP is shielded twisted pair?
Yes.> 
What is solid and plenum solid cabling?

Here I think you mean standard vs plenum.  Standard cat5/6 cabling has volatile components in the insulation that are poisonous when exposed to fire.  Plenum wiring is designed to be used where air (oxygen) breathed.  Plenum means "airway".

Why the differences?  Cost; single strand is cheaper than multi-strand and standard cable is cheaper than plenum type.

All of this falls under the TIA/EIA-568-B specifications. You can read about it [**_here_**]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TIA/EIA-568-B")
