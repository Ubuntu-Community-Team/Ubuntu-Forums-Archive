### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-07-05
I think that Pidgin is the best application available, as most development efforts have been focused there.  I don't know of any Linux distribution that comes bundled with anything other than Pidgin.

I know also of Kopete, a KDE application, that also supports chat over multiple protocols.  But I think that Pidgin can do everything Kopete can, and much more.

Also AIM has a [generic Linux release]("http://www.aim.com/get_aim/linux/latest_linux.adp") that looks like it hasn't been touched since 1998, but you can try it if you want.  Other messaging services might offer Linux releases as well for their protocols.

As for skins and why Pidgin doesn't support them, read [this]("http://developer.pidgin.im/wiki/Using%20Pidgin#CanIskinPidgin").  As that site explains, you can still always change font, colors and GTK theme even though there are no "skins."

There's [Miranda-IM]("http://www.miranda-im.org/") which is multiprotocol like Pidgin but supports skins.

Hope you find something you like!
