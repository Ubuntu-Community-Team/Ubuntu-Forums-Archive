### Post by TheFu on 2022-03-14
Do any other devices work using the connection to the ISP? If no, check your router. If that doesn't have any connection, I'd call my ISP to see what they know. If they think everything is fine, I'd swap in an old router I have laying  around to see if that worked or not. 

The normal way to troubleshoot is to figure out where in the path the issue lies, then swap the smallest part in that failure path out until the problem is determined.

Don't forget to check the ethernet cable and try a different port on the router too, for example. 

"Internet doesn't work" may seem clear, but it doesn't say if **ping google.com** works or doesn't or if **ping 1.1.1.1** works or not. Those are very different failures, assuming everything else is fine.
