### Post by Kevbert on 2008-04-20
If signal strength is low, do you have anything working at 2.4GHz such as a digital cordless phone, microwave oven, or is the PC or router near speakers ?
Next, can you see other networks on your PC ?  You may want to change the wireless channel.
An easy way to boost the signal is to use an external aerial for either the router or the wireless card.  Assuming the wireless card has a built in antenna, this won't be an option.  If the router has a detachable antenna this is the best option.  Antennas such as this can be used: [http://www.amazon.co.uk/WiFi-Wireless-Network-WLAN-Antenna/dp/B000PHCZBA/ref=sr_1_4?ie=UTF8&s=electronics&qid=1208681740&sr=8-4]("http://www.amazon.co.uk/WiFi-Wireless-Network-WLAN-Antenna/dp/B000PHCZBA/ref=sr_1_4?ie=UTF8&s=electronics&qid=1208681740&sr=8-4")  
It seems strange that you can move the PC to another room and the signal improves (proving this is your signal and not someone else's).  It's more likely an interference problem.
