### Post by Cappy on 2007-08-20
After looking through vast amounts of information, I will share a few things:
1) a connection called eth0:avahi is SUPPOSED to appear on your computer when you unplug the cable.
2) The fopen() error signifies that the file inside of the directory /var/lib/avahi-autoipd was not readable/writable by avahi. I chmod 777ed just to be sure.
3) I tried reinstalling avahi-autoipd as was marked as a fix on launchpad but no luck.
4) disabling avahi-daemon made no difference

Best I can tell, either my hardware stopped working completely correctly or (possibly?) there was a problem with my MAC address on this network. So basically something non-softwareish. Biggest clue, in hindsight, was the "eth0 up" "eth0 down" over and over, as it appeared on windows as well as ubuntu. 

My router was hit by lightning a few days ago but my computer seemed fine. Maybe not =P

Solution: I bought a 32-bit PCI Netgear 10/100 FA311 from Office Depot, slid it in, and it worked with 0 setup and I was instantly connected. Marking this long thread (nearly a blog by now) as solved.
