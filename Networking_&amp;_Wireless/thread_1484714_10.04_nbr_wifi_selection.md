### Post by pcal on 2010-05-16
I see heaps of folks having trouble getting wifi up and running with 10.04nbr. This isn't the problem I have...

My Fujitsu M2010 works just fine, and wifi picks up my network, as well as networks run by both my neighbours (all encrypted).

Most of the time, 10.04 doesn't even try to connect to any network. I can open networking, click on my network, and everything works just fine. But when 10.04 does try to connect by itself, it always chooses one of my neighbours networks to connect to.

What I can't find, is the setting to tell it my network is the default, and the only network to auto-connect to if available.

Can someone point me to the right setting, please...

Thanks in anticipation,

Pcal

### Post by cariboo on 2010-05-16
Right-click the network manager icon in the top panel and select Edit Connection, Click the Wireless tab, select the AP you want to connect to, then click edit, once the editing window open make sure connect automatically is checked, it is in the top left corner.

### Post by pcal on 2010-05-16
:) Thankyou!

Somehow, my neighbours network was set to autoconnect! (Perhaps it was detected first, on the first 10.04 boot?)

Changed it, and all is well now.
