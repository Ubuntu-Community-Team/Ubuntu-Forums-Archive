### Post by blastus on 2008-10-12
I think what you are talking about is called "bonding." You bond two interfaces together to increase performance. It will increase capacity but not speed because bonding two interfaces is like a road that has two lanes on it but the speed limit is still the same. In other words, if the maximum speed you get with one wireless N interface is 10 mb/s, then if you bonded another interface to it, your maximum speed would still be 10 mb/s but you would be able to have two different file transfers (for example) both at 10 mb/s.
