### Post by David_UK on 2010-11-18
10.04 was connecting to wireless on boot up without a problem.
After updating, it can no longer do it.  I have to click on the wireless icon (which has an exclamation mark through it) and connect to hidden.  I can then choose my wireless profile and am asked for my elevated privileges password.  Once supplied, the connection is made.
I presume the encryption password is 'locked' now at boot.
Any suggestions?  Thanks

### Post by David_UK on 2010-11-21
Update:
A fresh installation of Ubuntu 10.04 also works fine.  After installation, I click on the 'no network connection' icon and 'connect to hidden network.  Enter SSID and WPA2 pass - then, when asked to enter a password I leave it blank and accept the warning about unencrypted information.  Perhaps that was my original error - using a password for network manager, which in turn needed my user password to connect...?  
Anyway - solved.  Hope this is of use to others.
