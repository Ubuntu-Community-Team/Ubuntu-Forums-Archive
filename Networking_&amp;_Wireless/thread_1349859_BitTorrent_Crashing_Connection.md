### Post by diablo75 on 2009-12-08
Several years ago I had a Linksys router that would completely lock up (and I mean LOCK UP) after just a couple minutes of bittorrent traffic went over it.  It didn't matter what OS I was using.  There was a firmware upgrade available and applying it fixed the problem but only for a short period of time.  Eventually I got tired of the problem and went out to buy a new router.  These things do seem to wear out after a lot of usage.  I would still recommend Linksys routers, though.  I went to Netgear after that old Linksys and while it worked great for a while, Netgear has really been pulling some shady things with their products lately.  One router I bought from them a few months ago had obviously been used as the little tamper resistant sticker that sealed the back had another sticker already ripped in half under it, and the sticker on the back of the router covering the downlink ports was wrinkled and reapplied.  I could type forever about this but [I wrote a blog documenting all that happened]("http://davestechsupport.com/blog/2009/05/30/ill-never-buy-a-netgear-router-again/").  Whatever you do, DON'T buy a new router from Wal-Mart.  I have a feeling they've forced their vendors to drop their bulk prices to such a low that they have no choice but to sell them faulty merchandise packaged as new.

Edit:  I typed "Linksys" instead of "Netgear" in the sentence talking about their "shady things with their products."
