### Post by MaindotC on 2010-09-14
I need some clarification.  Is the Ubuntu machine the htpc?  When you say you hooked up the laptop to "the same ethernet cable that serves the htpc" - does that mean you streamed a 1080p movie from your laptop to the TV?  Actually that doesn't make sense because you wouldn't have a Cat 5 cable plugged into your TV but try and provide a little more details on how the laptop suddenly got involved.

I will say this - 100mbps or 1gbps is the "bandwidth".  In plumbing terms, that's how wide a pipe is.  The actual data transfer rate, which is dependent on several factors that you likely cannot control, is the "throughput".  In plumbing terms that's how much water you fit through those pipes, per second.  I know that theoretically those values should be equivalent but they aren't.

I didn't find a link that readily answered the question of how fast a network you need but I would imagine a GBPS network would be more than enough.  I'm confused about this "streaming" business.  When you say streaming, does that mean you are playing a video on your laptop that resides on the htpc?

This is uncharted territory for me but I really want this to work for you.  I would imagine as long as your network is set up properly it's really an application issue - but if you have a RAID setup then the data transfer off the hard disks should be more than enough surface area to constantly keep the gbps NIC's spewing data.  Clarify some of those phrases and we'll get this figured out.
