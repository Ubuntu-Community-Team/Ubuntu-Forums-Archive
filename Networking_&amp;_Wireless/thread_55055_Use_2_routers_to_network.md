---

### Post by gruepig on 2005-08-07
That probably won't work. Even if you can do that, it's almost certainly not something you want to do.  Both of your routers are probably NAT devices, and having a NAT behind another NAT can lead to problems.  What you want is a router with two wired ethernet ports or a router with one wired ethernet port and a switch or hub.
