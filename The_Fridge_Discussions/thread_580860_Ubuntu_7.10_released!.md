### Post by TheFridge on 2007-10-19
In case you missed the flurry of release announcements, Ubuntu 7.10 (sometimes known to its friends as Gutsy Gibbon) has been released (the little &#8220;7.10 is here&#8221; image on the left might have also tipped you off).
 Anway, check out the following announcement for the news:
 [LIST] 
[*][Ubuntu 7.10]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/ubuntu710") 
[*][Desktop edition]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/ubuntu-desktop710") 
[*][Server edition]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/ubuntu-server710") 
[*][Ubuntu family]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/ubuntu-family710") [/LIST] As per usual, 7.10 will be supported for 18 months on both desktops and servers. Users requiring a longer support lifetime on servers may choose to continue using Ubuntu 6.06 LTS, with security support until 2011, rather than upgrade to or install 7.10.
 And, as usual, Ubuntu 7.10 is also the basis for new 7.10 releases of [Kubuntu]("http://kubuntu.org/announcements/7.10-release.php"), [Xubuntu]("http://xubuntu.org/news/gutsy/release") and [Edubuntu]("http://www.edubuntu.org/news/7.10-release").
 **Helping Shape Ubuntu**
 If you would like to help shape Ubuntu, take a look at the [URL="http://www.ubuntu.com/community/participate/"]list of ways
you can participate[/URL].
 Developers should consider attending the upcoming Ubuntu Developer Summit in Cambridge, Massachusetts (US) starting 29 October.  More information is available at the [UDS-Boston page]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UDS-Boston").
