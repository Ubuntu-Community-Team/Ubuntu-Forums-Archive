### Post by TheFridge on 2007-07-04
[Akademy 2007]("http://akademy2007.kde.org"), the KDE World Summit, is happening now in Glasgow.  The week long event is being sponsored by Kubuntu thanks to Canonical.  Mark Shuttleworth gave one of the [keynote speeches]("http://dot.kde.org/1183385741/") talking about challenges to the free software desktop and provoked a debate among KDE developers by suggesting they adopt a fixed release schedule.  Kubuntu developer Jonathan Riddell also gave a talk on the upcoming features in the Gutsy release.  Videos (and slides for other talks) are available on the [programme web page]("http://akademy2007.kde.org/conference/programme.php").
The week continues with BoFs, hacking and a day trip to Loch Lomond.  The [Photos page]("http://ubuntuforums.org//wiki.kde.org/aKademy+2007+Photos") gives you a feel of the excitement.  Needless to say the machines in the hacklab and the 50 node [Icecream]("http://en.opensuse.org/Icecream") distributed compiling cluster are all running Kubuntu.
