### Post by TheFridge on 2009-10-13
We are pleased to announce that this cycle&#8217;s Ubuntu Open Week will be held the week after Ubuntu 9.10&#8217;s release, from 2 November to 6 November in #ubuntu-classroom on Freenode. The sessions take place from 1500UTC to 2200UTC.

 Ubuntu Open Week is a week full of IRC tutorial sessions on a range of subjects, designed to help people get involved in the Ubuntu community. It is given by many of the brightest, most capable members of the Ubuntu community, and covers a range of subjects including packaging, bug triage, translations, accessibility, automated testing, loco teams, mentoring, Launchpad, desktop team, training team and much more.

 There will also be the always popular &#8220;Ask Mark&#8221; session (Wednesday 4 Nov @ 15.00UTC) in which you have an hour to ask Mark Shuttleworth, the founder of Ubuntu, your burning questions. Jono will also be providing an Introduction and Community Q+A session (Mon 2 Nov @ 15.00UTC) in which you can ask your questions about the community, Ubuntu, Canonical and anything else.

 For the very first time we&#8217;re going to have a week of IRC sessions in Spanish in order to motivate community members from Spanish speaking countries to get involved in the Ubuntu community. More info here. Visita el vínculo y súmate para aprender más acerca de Ubuntu, la comunidad, como usarlo y aportar. Hay charlas para principiantes, usuarios avanzados y expertos [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuOpenWeek_ES](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuOpenWeek_ES)

 The schedule is up, so let&#8217;s get started! (A few slots left open, if you want them, holler at Jorge [jcastro])

 Per:  Jorge Castro, 10-13-2009
