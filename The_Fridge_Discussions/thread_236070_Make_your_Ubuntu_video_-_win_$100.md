### Post by TheFridge on 2006-08-14
We&#8217;ve all seen how cool technologies - such as [Ruby on Rails]("http://www.rubyonrails.com/") - have wowed people through video.
[Ubuntu Video]("http://www.ubuntuvideo.com/") is a new blog that can do the same for our favourite operating system. John Little, the site&#8217;s creator, hunts down Ubuntu-related movies and links to them using YouTube.
He told The Fridge what motivated him to get the site online:
[INDENT]Video allows us to dispel the old Linux myths: that it&#8217;s difficult, command line driven, there are no games for it, that sort of thing. I want Ubuntu users to flood the internet with videos that demonstrate the truth!
[/INDENT]John&#8217;s even running a [competition to find the best in Ubuntu Video]("http://www.ubuntuvideo.com/could_you_use_a_hundred_bucks"). He&#8217;s offering $100 to the winner and makes some suggestions as to what you might make:
[LIST=1]
[*]Record your own Ubuntu commercial.
[*]Show us how you use Ubuntu in a unique way or place.
[*]Record an instructive screencast.
[*]Make a mini documentary about free software.
[*]Go to a major retailer and ask to by a PC with Ubuntu pre-installed. Take your camera.
[*]Show us your linux gaming rig.[/LIST]Enter the competition at [UbuntuVideo.com]("http://www.ubuntuvideo.com/") or get involved in this and similar projects by joining the [Ubuntu Marketing mailing list]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo/ubuntu-marketing").
