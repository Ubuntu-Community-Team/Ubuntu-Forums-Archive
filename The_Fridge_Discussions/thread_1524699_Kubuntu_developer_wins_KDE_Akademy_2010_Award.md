[IMG]http://people.canonical.com/~jriddell/akademy/aurelien.jpg[/IMG]
Top Kubuntu developer **Aurélien Gâteau** ([agateau]("https://launchpad.net/~agateau")) has been honoured with an [Akademy Award]("http://community.kde.org/KDE_Project_History/Contributor_Awards") for 2010.  The Akademy Awards are given out each year at the annual KDE Akademy conference; the jury being formed of previous prize-winners.

 Aurélien won the award for his work on [**Gwenview**]("http://gwenview.sourceforge.net/"), the image viewing application which ships with Kubuntu.  He was also commended at Akademy for work on the [**KDE Status Notifier**]("http://www.notmart.org/misc/statusnotifieritem/index.html") specifications which are currently being heavily adopted by the Ubuntu project, under the name [**Application Indicators**]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DesktopExperienceTeam/ApplicationIndicators").

 The annual [Akademy]("http://akademy.kde.org/") conference is being held this week in Tampere in Finland, a city just fifteen kilometres from the small world-famous town called [&#8220;Nokia&#8221;]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nokia,_Finland") after which half of the worlds&#8217; mobile phones are named.  Developers and contributors at Akademy are discussing their plans to make the KDE Plasform, Plasma workspaces and Applications rock even harder.  Much of the discussion is  about how to integrate with Intel and Nokia&#8217;s [Meego]("http://meego.com/") mobile system.

 On receiving the award Aurélien said he was stunned and thanked his wife *Gwen*, who gave her name to the software he had written, before remembering to say a big &#8220;*thank you to Canonical for sponsoring Akademy*&#8221; and addressing more practical issues &#8220;*I am only annoyed that the award frame is too large to take home in my luggage!*&#8221;. Anyone with a private jet who can offer assistance with transporting the giant award is invited to contact Aurélien directly.
