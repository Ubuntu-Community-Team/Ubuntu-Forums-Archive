### Post by TheFridge on 2009-03-31
Short version: if you&#8217;re interested in learning what it takes to learn packaging, mark **Thursday** in your calendar. From now on **Thursday** is **Packaging Training Day**.

 Based on our experience with initiatives like the MOTU Q&A Sessions, the [Ubuntu Developer Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek") and [Ubuntu Open Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuOpenWeek"), the Ubuntu Developer Community is offering the following training effort starting in April:

 
[LIST] 
[*]one 1-hour session every week 
[*]short demo of packaging techniques 
[*]rest of the hour: ask all the questions you like 
[*]in **#ubuntu-classroom on irc.freenode.net** 
[*]with translators in #ubuntu-classroom-{de,es,it,fr,&#8230;} if you&#8217;re not sure enough asking your questions in English yet 
[/LIST]
 We rotate session times to make sure we cover all timezones:

 
[LIST] 
[*]1st Thursday of the month,  6:00 UTC 
[*]2nd Thursday of the month, 12:00 UTC 
[*]3rd Thursday of the month, 18:00 UTC 
[*]4th Thursday of the month,  0:00 UTC 
[*](5th Thursday of the month,  6:00 UTC) 
[/LIST]
 So for April we&#8217;re very proud to announce the following sessions:

 
[LIST] 
[*]** 2nd April, 06:00 UTC: Daniel Holbach, Fixing an Ubuntu bug** 
[*]** 9th April, 12:00 UTC: James Westby, bzr builddeb &#8211;in-15-minutes** 
[*]**16th April, 18:00 UTC: Didier Roche, How-to update a package** 
[*]**23rd April, 00:00 UTC: , TBA** 
[*]**30th April, 06:00 UTC: Daniel Holbach, Getting Started with Ubuntu Development** 
[/LIST]
 Do you want to give a session? Do you have questions? Would you like to request a session? Would you just like to hang out? Do you want to help out with translations? Head over to **[Packaging/Training]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Packaging/Training")** and add yourself.

 This is an effort by the Ubuntu Developer Community, if you can do something to improve it and help others to get the most out of it, help out. This is going to be what we make of it. Get involved now!
