### Post by TheFridge on 2007-05-08
In a recent story published by [Heise Online]("http://www.heise.de/english/"), D-Link is publishing a LiveDVD featuring the [Kubuntu]("http://www.kubuntu.org") GNU/Linux operating system. This DVD features a router development environment for the [HorstBox Professional VoIP router]("http://www.d-link.de/?go=gNTyP9CgrdFOIC4AStFCF834mptYKO9ZTdvhLPG3yV3oVox7haltbNlwaaFp6DQoHDruzidK/4gAAw==") (page available in German only). The HorstBox Professional contains a WLAN router with ADSL modem as well as a system that runs the Asterisk telephone system.
 [INDENT]According to D-Link open source is an important component of its product strategy. &#8220;That a complete development environment would be made available was already apparent when HorstBox Professional was still in its conception phase. The reason for this is obvious: The requirements that future-proof systems capable of flexibly responding to the desires of customers need to meet are continuing to grow. In this way we can meet these requirements in an optimal fashion,&#8221; Horst Toddenroth, head of the Advanced Products and Solutions Department at D-Link Central Europe, said.
[/INDENT] Head on over to Heise Online and read the [story in its entirety]("http://www.heise.de/english/newsticker/news/88428").
