### Post by TheFridge on 2007-12-02
The first alpha release of the next version of Ubuntu, Hardy Heron, has been released. This is the first in a series of alpha and beta releases that will eventually lead to the final Ubuntu 8.04 in April of next year. This release brings a lot of new and updated packages from Debian as well as the latest X.org 7.3. You can read the [full release announcement]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel-announce/2007-December/000357.html"), see the [release notes]("http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/hardy/alpha1") or [download it]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/releases/hardy/alpha-1/"). Versions of [Kubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/kubuntu/releases/hardy/alpha-1/"), [Edubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/edubuntu/releases/hardy/alpha-1/"), [Xubuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/xubuntu/releases/hardy/alpha-1/"), [Ubuntu JeOS]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/jeos/releases/hardy/alpha-1/"), [Gobuntu]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/gobuntu/releases/hardy/alpha-1/") and [Ubuntu Studio]("http://cdimage.ubuntu.com/ubuntustudio/releases/hardy/alpha-1/") have also been released.
