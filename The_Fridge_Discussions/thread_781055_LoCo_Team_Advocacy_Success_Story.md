### Post by TheFridge on 2008-05-03
You may have already read about it, heck you may have already heard it yourself! The [Minnesota (USA) LoCo]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MinnesotaTeam") team went about contacting local media outlets and agencies to inform them about the recent Ubuntu 8.04 release, the Loco Team&#8217;s release party, and the exciting possibilities of using an open source operating system. Jon Gordon of  [National Public Radio (NPR)]("http://www.npr.org/") hosts a daily segment called [ Future Tense]("http://news.minnesota.publicradio.org/programs/futuretense/") which brings its listeners the latest technology topics in daily five-minute capsules. Future Tense is heard in the United States during broadcasts of the CBC&#8217;s [As It Happens]("http://www.cbc.ca/aih/"). Gordon contacted our very own [Tony Yarusso]("http://tonyyarusso.com") for an interview which was sent over the airwaves this mid last week.
 [ Tony wrote on his blog:]("http://tonyyarusso.com/floss/interviewed-npr")
 [INDENT] Apparently it went well, as he decided to run it, so without further ado I&#8217;m happy to announce that I was on the radio, talking about Ubuntu! So, I got the double-whammy of getting myself personally on the news, plus getting an Ubuntu story in mainstream media (and a quite large, respected outlet of such at that). Nifty. 
[/INDENT] The premise of the five minute sound bite asks: *Is Ubuntu ready for the mainstream?*
 You can hear Tony&#8217;s answer by visiting the show&#8217;s archive at [ Future Tense]("http://www.publicradio.org/columns/futuretense/2008/05/01.shtml"). MP3, RealAudio, and AAC formats provided.
 Kudos and congratulations to Tony and the rest of the [Minnesota (USA) LoCo]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MinnesotaTeam") team for their awesome job.
 Want to get involved in advocating Ubuntu? You can see this kind of success too! Visit the [advocacy page]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoActivism") and [LoCo Team knowledge base]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LoCoTeamKnowledgeBase") on the [Ubuntu wik]("http://wiki.ubuntu.com")i for more information, tips, and hints on advocating as a team and as an individual. 
 Help spread the great news and be sure to pat Tony on the back the next time you see him!
