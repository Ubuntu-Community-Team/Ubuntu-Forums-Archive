### Post by TheFridge on 2007-01-09
With less than a week away linux.conf.au 2007 is shaping up to be better than ever. The conference will run from the 15th through the 20th of January. linux.conf.au, a purely volunteer effort hosted in a different Australian city each year, is one of the world&#8217;s best Open Source and Free Software developer community conferences. This year the conference will be held at the Kensington campus of the University of New South Wales in Sydney.
 Included in the events are talks from some of our very own:
 [LIST] 
[*][Jono Bacon]("http://lca2007.linux.org.au/profile/1037") 
[*][Scott James Remnant]("http://lca2007.linux.org.au/profile/93") 
[*][Robert Collins]("http://lca2007.linux.org.au/profile/231") 
[*][Matthew Garrett]("http://lca2007.linux.org.au/profile/53") [/LIST] There is a free Open Day on Thursday, January 18th, from 3pm until 8pm. During this time the Australia Local Community Team will be doing their best to spread Ubuntu by handing out swag bags and do flashing cool demos for the attendees. The team will also hold an unofficial, casual, late dinner at a nearby pub after the Open Day. Details of the dinner can be found at [https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-au/2007-January/001106.html](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-au/2007-January/001106.html).
 More information about linux.conf.au can be found at [http://lca2007.linux.org.au/](http://lca2007.linux.org.au/). A breakdown of the entire event can be viewed at [http://lca2007.linux.org.au/Programme](http://lca2007.linux.org.au/Programme).
