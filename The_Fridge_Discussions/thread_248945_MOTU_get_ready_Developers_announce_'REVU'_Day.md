  Hot on the footsteps of our recent story of [Reinhard Tartler being interviewed]("http://behindubuntu.org/interviews/ReinhardTartler/") (on Behind Ubuntu) about his work on &#8216;REVU&#8217; and the Masters of the Universe team, we&#8217;re delighted to discover *REVU Day*.
 Daniel Holbach [announced on the ubuntu-devel]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel/2006-September/020349.html") mailing list that **September 8th** will be *REVU Day* for the edgy development process.  REVU is the tool used to vet and check new packages before they are uploaded in the Ubuntu system.  We&#8217;re told that the idea for the day will be to green-light as many new packages as possible, with Daniel adding &#8220;we&#8217;ll make an effort to get all the goodness on REVU into Ubuntu&#8221;.
 During an Ubuntu development cycle, the rate of new features is gradually slowed down as more and more time is spent on bug-fixing and quality checking.  One of the last areas to close for new features is the *Universe* and *Multiverse* components which between them contain the majority of *off-the-beaten-track* packages&mdash;for example, many games, much multimedia and scientific software.
 *Universe* contains many thousands of programs, all neatly wrapped up and packaged for easy installation using Add/Remove programs or Debian&#8217;s famous apt-get tool.  Eventually the [MOTU]("http://www.ubuntuforums.org/glossary#term16") will switch to full stabilisation and bug-fixing mode for *Universe* as well, that date is approaching fast.  On September 28th automatic &#8220;syncing&#8221; (copying and importing) of packages from Debian will stop and the procedures documented in [UniverseFreeze]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UniverseFreeze") will take over.
 If you one of the many people with software that you are hoping to see in Ubuntu 6.10, or perhaps have software already in the [REVU system]("http://revu.tauware.de/"), then be sure to pop along to the #ubuntu-motu IRC channel in one weeks time.  Hope to see you there!
 If you&#8217;re like to know more information, about starting out, or getting involved with the *Masters of the Universe* team, we recommend the [MOTU Guide]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU") followed by a visit to #ubuntu-motu for questions, drinks and chit-chat.  You&#8217;ll getting to grips in no-time at all!
