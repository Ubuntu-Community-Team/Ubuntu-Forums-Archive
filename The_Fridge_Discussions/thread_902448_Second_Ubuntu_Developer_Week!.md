### Post by TheFridge on 2008-08-27
The second **[Ubuntu Developer Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek")** will be held from **Sep 1st** to **Sep 5th** in **#ubuntu-classroom** on **irc.freenode.net**.

 [**Digg It!**]("http://digg.com/linux_unix/Second_Ubuntu_Developer_Week_announced")

 &#8216;There are [Wiki docs]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU/GettingStarted"), [MOTU Videos]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU/Videos"), [MOTU Interviews]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU/Interviews") and we&#8217;re seeing more and more contributors each day. This is all good and well, but there&#8217;s nothing like talking to real people, asking real questions in a real-time environment.

 &#8216;The [Ubuntu Developer Week]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek?action=show&redirect=UbuntuDeveloperWeek") is designed to give you an overview of what’s going on in the Ubuntu Developer world. Speak to the developers, learn, ask questions and finally realise “It’s true, I *can* make a difference by helping out here.&#8221;

 &#8216;Let&#8217;s take a closer look at the sessions we have:

 
[LIST] 
[*]**Packaging 101** - [MOTU]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU") [Daniel Holbach]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DanielHolbach") will talk you through the bare-bone structure of an Ubuntu package and how to get there the easiest way. 
[*]**Upstream Bug Linkages** - In his session [Jorge Castro]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JorgeCastro") will demonstrate the importance of linking Upstream bugs, acting as a liaison with upstream projects and be an important tie between upstream projects and Ubuntu. 
[*]**Introduction to MOTU** - [Iulian Udrea]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/IulianUdrea") has been very active in the [MOTU]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU") team and will share his experiences with you and of course answer all your questions. 
[*]**Soyuz and all that Jazz** - [Celso Providelo]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/CelsoProvidelo") is not only going to run one session, he&#8217;s going to run two. This is the second one and will get you up to scratch on Launchpad&#8217;s Distribution Management. 
[*]**Working with UbuntuGNOME QA (tips&tricks)** - [Pedro Villavicencio]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PedroVillavicencio") is amazing. He&#8217;s from Chile, knows GNOME Bug numbers by heart and just generally a great guy. Obviously he&#8217;s in touch with the GNOME Bug Squad a lot, he will show you how to contribute to both Ubuntu, and GNOME at the same time. 
[*]**How do I fix an Ubuntu bug?** - [Daniel Holbach]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DanielHolbach") will take you on the journey from finding a bug to work on, the process of fixing it and getting it integrated into Ubuntu. 
[*]**Introduction to BZR** - [David Futcher]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DavidFutcher") has used [bzr]("http://bazaar-vcs.org/") in his work on Ubuntu and will guide you on your way of learning one of the most important tools in today&#8217;s development ecosystem: distributed revision control. 
[*]**Kernel module packaging with DKMS** - [Mario Limonciello]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MarioLimonciello") will talk you through state of the art kernel module packaging with [DKMS]("http://linux.dell.com/dkms/") (*Dynamic Kernel Module Support*). 
[*]**Using the Launchpad Web Service API** - Leonard Richardson and Barry Warsaw, two of the [Launchpad]("http://launchpad.net/") hackers behind the [Launchpad Web Service API]("https://help.launchpad.net/API") will demonstrate a few examples and answer questions about the project that is hopefully going to put an end to all Launchpad screen-scraping. 
[*]**Launchpad Hacks** - [Brian Murray]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BrianMurray"), Ubuntu&#8217;s bugmaster does bug triage, bug statistics and chearleading on #ubuntu-bugs at day and writes Greasemonkey scripts at night. He&#8217;s done a lot of useful little hacks that make Bug Triage quicker and more fun. He will show you how. 
[*]**bzr for packaging** - After David Futcher&#8217;s session and if you&#8217;re into packaging, you should be ready to go for [James Westby]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JamesWestby") and the magic he will teach you. James is not only author of bzr-builddeb, but also heavily involved in [DistributedDevelopment]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DistributedDevelopment"). 
[*]**How do I update a package properly** - If that&#8217;s the question you&#8217;ve pondering yourself, [MOTU]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MOTU") and Mentoring Mastermind [Cesare Tirabassi]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/CesareTirabassi") is your man. Updating, Building, Testing, Tips and Tricks all included in the session. 
[*]**Introduction to PPAs** - [Launchpad]("http://launchpad.net/") hacker [Celso Providelo]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/CelsoProvidelo") has put a lot of work into [Soyuz]("http://launchpad.net/soyuz") and [PPAs]("http://help.launchpad.net/PPA"). He will explain how to make the best use of Personal Package Archives. 
[*]**Introduction to the [Server Team]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ServerTeam")** - [Mathias Gug]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MathiasGug") of the [Server Team]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ServerTeam") is a brilliant guy - always around to help you get started in the Ubuntu&#8217;s server land, generally helpful and easy to work with. His session will show you where to get involved if you&#8217;re into Ubuntu and servers. 
[*]**Various ways to patch a package** - Two great french contributors in the MOTU team, [Christophe Sauthier]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/ChristopheSauthier") and [Didier Roche]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DidierRoche"),  are delivering this session: get ready to patch packages and make your changes fit neatly into existing packages. 
[*]**Automated Testing for the Desktop** - [Ara Pulido]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AraPulido") has been busy working on automated Desktop tests for Ubuntu and will show how to make best use of them, how to get started and what to bear in mind. 
[*]**A [WebKit]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/WebKit") browser in PyKDE** - You&#8217;re into KDE? Always wanted to start hacking? [Jonathan Riddell]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JonathanRiddell") is definitely your man: he&#8217;ll show you how much fun PyKDE is. 
[*]**Having fun with the [Mozilla Team]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MozillaTeam")** - Firefox, Extensions, Xulrunner, Thunderbird, etc is right up your alley? [Alexander Sack]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/AlexanderSack") and the [Mozilla Team]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MozillaTeam") are the people you&#8217;re looking for. 
[*]**How to avoid making Archive Admins unhappy** - Lots of changes Ubuntu Developers upload land directly in the archive admins for extra scrutiny checks. [Steve Langasek]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/SteveLangasek") gives the ultimate session to avoid common pitfalls and make archive admins enjoy their work with you. 
[*]**Ask Matt** - [Matt Zimmerman]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/MattZimmerman") is not only amazing, he&#8217;s also technical leader of the Ubuntu project, CTO of Canonical, chair of the Technical Board and great at playing the guitar. We are happy to have him here to run a Q&A session with him. 
[*]**Unit testing Python code, with code coverage measurement** - Debian and Ubuntu developer [Lars Wirzenius]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/LarsWirzenius") is going to talk about code testing and code coverage measurement. If you want to learn how to write *good* code, make sure you don&#8217;t miss the session. 
[*]**Introduction to the Installer Team** - Ever wondered how Ubuntu&#8217;s installer works? Did you ever think: &#8220;I&#8217;d really like to get involved there.&#8221;? This is your chance, meet [Evan Dandrea]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/EvanDandrea"). 
[*]**Introduciton to the Security Team** - [Kees Cook]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/KeesCook") and [Jamie Strandboge]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/JamieStrandboge") are amazing, they work day and night to keep your and your mom&#8217;s Ubuntu machine safe and secure. Want to get started in the Security world? Kees and Jamie will show you how. 
[*]**Kernel Discussion** - We have [Ben Collins]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BenCollins") here who will lead the Kernel discussion. Ever wanted to help out Ubuntu&#8217;s Kernel hackers? Ever wanted to know how you can get a foot into the door in the Kernel team? This is your chance. 
[/LIST]
 Check out the [timetable]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek"), [how to join in]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek/JoiningIn") and the &#8220;[rules.]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/UbuntuDeveloperWeek/Rules")&#8221;
