### Post by TheFridge on 2009-03-18
The Ubuntu Indian team was founded in February 2006 and celebrated its 3rd anniversary last month. Since the very beginning, it has been growing gradually and now has over [1000 members]("https://launchpad.net/~indian-team") (and counting). The community is managed by a [team]("http://ubuntu-in.info/wiki/index.php/Teammembers") of enthusiastic individuals, Ubuntu Members and MOTUs. They are responsible for maintaining the [mailing list]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/mailman/listinfo/ubuntu-in"), [IRC channel]("irc://chat.freenode.net/ubuntu-in") and the [community documentation]("http://ubuntu-in.info/wiki/index.php/Documents").

 Among the countless achievements, the prominent team activities include:

 
[LIST] 
[*]Participating in the Global Bug Jam - I 
[*]Being a part of [FOSS.in]("http://foss.in/about") 2007; Debian/Ubuntu Project day 
[*]Promoting Ubuntu and distribution of Ubuntu CDs/DVDs at gatherings 
[*]Representing Ubuntu-India at FOSS conferences, schools and colleges 
[/LIST]
 Back in 2006, the LoCo team started their forums running on [SMF 1.1.7]("http://www.simplemachines.org/"). Due to some serious hosting issues, the forum had to be discontinued and it stayed down for about 4 months. Yesterday, a brand new forum was set-up and it is now open for registrations. You can find it at [forum.ubuntu-in.info]("http://forum.ubuntu-in.info/"). The board runs on [bbPress]("http://bbpress.org/"), a surprisingly light, fast and highly customizable forum software from the creators of [WordPress]("http://wordpress.org/").

 So don&#8217;t wait any longer! Come be a part of the shining new [forum]("http://forum.ubuntu-in.info/") and the Ubuntu-India community.
