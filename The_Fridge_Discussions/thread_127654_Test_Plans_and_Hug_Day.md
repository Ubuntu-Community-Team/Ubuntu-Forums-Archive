### Post by TheFridge on 2006-02-09

	 <p>Got a spare computer and some free time? Daniel Holbach is <a href="https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel-announce/2006-February/000075.html">looking</a> for volunteers for the new <a href="https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Testing">testing program</a>. It&#8217;s a short list of things you can check off while installing a test release. There is also a longer, more thorough test is available for those that want to really put the installation process through its paces. Feeback is direct through the wiki pages, and will go a long way to getting good data back to the developers to see what needs to be fixed. Instructions and quick links to CD images are available on the <a href="https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Testing/Introduction">introduction</a> page.</p>
<p>Daniel also wants everyone to know that this upcoming Bug Day will be very special:</p>
<blockquote><p>But we want this to be a special Bug Day. We&#8217;re going to have lists of things to do of different Ubuntu teams, so we can better track the impact we had. Planning is one thing, but we&#8217;ll surely stick to our concept of success: the Hug Day. This is a very special Bug Day: on Hug Day, when someone closes a bug, then someone else should hug him/her. Why? This is a very special way for us to tell everyone that we love contributions! And triaging bugs is a really big contribution.</p></blockquote>
<p>See his <a href="https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-devel-announce/2006-February/000074.html">announcement</a> for more details on getting involved.</p>
