 The Ubuntu 10.04 LTS release is almost there and we&#8217;re getting ready to celebrate the awesomeness of this new shiny version and the great work of everyone involved in making it possible.

 All over the world, from [Maracaibo]("http://loco.ubuntu.com/events/team/91/detail/") to [Beijing]("http://loco.ubuntu.com/events/team/110/detail/"), people are ultimating the details of their Lucid release parties to mark this new milestone in the Ubuntu family and to have a great time.

 Check out the [Ubuntu LoCo Directory]("http://loco.ubuntu.com"), where we&#8217;ve got already [48 release parties]("http://loco.ubuntu.com/events/global/16/detail/") registered. We can surely make this figure grow!

 If your party is still not there, either because you haven&#8217;t started the organization or because you have not registered it yet, here&#8217;s what you can do:

 
[LIST] 
[*] If you haven&#8217;t organized a release party, check out the [guide]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/BuildingCommunity/RunningReleaseParty") to help you with the planning 
[/LIST]
 
[LIST] 
[*] If you have a release party scheduled, be sure to:</p>
[LIST] 
[*] **Add it to the LoCo Directory**. [Add your party to the LoCo directory]("http://loco.ubuntu.com/events/team/add/"). 
[*] **Spread the word**. Blog about it, advertise it, tell your friends, tell local media, tell podcasts and otherwise promote it. 
[*] **Stay in touch**. Join the IRC channel #ubuntu-release-party on irc.freenode.net 
[*] **Take pictures!**. If you post photos to Flickr, please tag them with ***ubuntu***, ***release***, ***party***, ***2010***, ***10.04*** and ***lucid***. Jono Bacon the Ubuntu Community Manager is wanting to put together a collage of release party group photos, so take a group photo of your party people and email *jono AT ubuntu DOT com* with a link to the photo online and the subject *&#8220;Lucid Release Party Photo&#8221;* &#8212; ensure that you specify which LoCo Team party it is at.  
[/LIST]
 
[/LIST]
 Let&#8217;s make the Lucid Lynx rock!
