### Post by TheFridge on 2007-05-07
As a testament to Ubuntu&#8217;s growing mainstream success, Canonical and Dell this week [announced]("http://www.ubuntu.com/news/dell-to-offer-ubuntu") that they were [working together to offer Ubuntu 7.04]("http://www.dell.com/content/topics/segtopic.aspx/ubuntu?c=us&cs=19&l=en&") (Feisty Fawn) on select desktops and notebooks. The deal was struck in response to an overwhelming number of requests made on the [Dell IdeaStorm website]("http://www.ideastorm.com/") for Ubuntu to be bundled with Dell systems.
 Mark Shuttleworth said of the deal in a video interview posted on [the Dell blog]("http://direct2dell.com/one2one/archive/2007/05/01/13147.aspx"), &#8220;It&#8217;s very exciting to see this free software community actually expressing itself; and it&#8217;s equally exciting to see a very large company listening to that freedom of expression&#8221;.
 According to Dell, notebooks and desktops preloaded with Ubuntu will be available in the USA in the next few weeks.
