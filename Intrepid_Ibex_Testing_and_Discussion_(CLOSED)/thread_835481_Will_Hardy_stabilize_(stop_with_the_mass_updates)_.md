### Post by Raptor45 on 2008-06-20
Large numbers of updates is not necessarily any indication that Hardy was rushed. It simply means that a lot of extra work has been going into fixing bugs in Hardy, more than in previous release cycles. There will always be a group claiming a release was rushed for some reason.

If the frequency of updates is an annoyance, just set update manager to only check for updates weekly or bi-weekly and install automatically. There is no reason to be complaining about that.

Sticking to deadlines is very important, especially for business customers, and there are many other reasons why Ubuntu is very strict about meeting its schedule. One missed release will cascade into other delays and problems. Missing targets is also seen as unprofessional, and unpredictable releases make it harder for companies to plan and schedule for changes.

Inflammatory references comparing Ubuntu and Microsoft are uncalled for and unnecessary.
