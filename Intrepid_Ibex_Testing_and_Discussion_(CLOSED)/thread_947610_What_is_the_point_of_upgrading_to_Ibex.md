Each new release improves over the previous one in various, usually small, sometimes large, ways. I prefer to update about once a year (every other release) because I custom build many applications and don't like to rebuild them every six months. After a year, the improvements are usually pretty obvious.

If your wireless works fine and you're happy with Hardy, as I am, there's no reason to upgrade. Unless of course you want to. It's entirely a personal preference. 

As for keeping personal preferences between installs, you can create a separate partition as your home folder. That way, when you reinstall, you can use a 'clean' install and all your preferences will be saved. That said, I have had minor issues when using this method. Old icons haunt my new install, etc.
