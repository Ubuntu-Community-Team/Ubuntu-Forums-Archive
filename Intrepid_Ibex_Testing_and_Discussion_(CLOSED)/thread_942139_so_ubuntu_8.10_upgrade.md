### Post by haydnc on 2008-10-08
The instructions for upgrading to Ubuntu Intrepid can be found here:
[http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/intrepid/beta](http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/intrepid/beta)

That said, you should probably strongly consider waiting until it is out of Beta and has actually been released. 

Beta software, like alpha is intended only for those who really know what they're doing and don't mind the risk that sometimes their operating system might break when updates are run.

Edit: As an example you should take a look through the Intrepid forums - there are plenty of posts in there with people asking for help because an update has messed up something on their beta Operating System.

### Post by Jim! on 2008-10-08
I have been using the Beta since release, If your going to begin using it then download the .ISO from [http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/intrepid/beta](http://www.ubuntu.com/testing/intrepid/beta). You do not have to do a clean install you can just do an upgrade of your current install and keep all the files and things where they are - but take note that this is a beta and the possibilty of your data being 'lost' is there so make sure to backup anything important first;)

So to answer your question: It does not have to be a fresh install over 8.04 and you don't need to reformat anything - unless something goes wrong:)
