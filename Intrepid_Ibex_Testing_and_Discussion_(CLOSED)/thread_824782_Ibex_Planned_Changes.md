### Post by quickshade on 2008-06-13
Funny, alpha and beta were made up to track the softwares status and provide testers with a more usable "flow" of how things should work. For example, people see that amarok 2 is in the test repos for KDE 4.1. So it must work, But reading the label suggest that it's still alpha quality software, it's not feature complete and still has many changes that will be made. Same with KDE 4.1. It's a beta program, which means most if not all the features are in, now it's time to polish them and bug test them. and RC means this is the final product, besides some final tweaking and bug fixes. It's funny to me, because you really should have you plans laid out before you release an alpha, you should know what features your aim for and what you can't do in one release. Not having that is a big problem thats going to lead to a sloppy release. 

I think KDE 4.1 development did it decently. Declare a list of all major changes you want to make and show that to anyone, then make another one broken down by section and showing what people want to get done. Normal users won't understand it, but people can track the status of it very easily. Just my 2 cents.
