### Post by mc4100 on 2008-10-08
[Edit: Damn, the title was supposed to be "A better *looking* Totem"]

I figured II testing/discussion would be the best place for this, since it might break things:

Although some people are die-hard mplayer fans, and others still, swear by VLC; for me, Totem is the best option for movies: a quick and simple way to play a video, with good format support (through GStreamer) and some useful features like a YouTube plug-in. 

Until now, I had one big criticism I lived with ... the UI.

User Interface has also been a huge deal with me, and no one ever seems to get it just right. It's nobody's fault -- it's all about personal preference -- and as I said, I can live with it. Anyway, I've been looking at the UI's of different apps, making some mockups of what I like (e.g., an evolution one in the Cafe) ... but it's a nice bonus when you find something perfect for you that *actually exists* ...

I'll attach a screenshot of [this]("http://bugzilla.gnome.org/show_bug.cgi?id=552378"), made by some genius (not sarcasm :)) [Edit: His name is Alexander V. Butenko] -- it's **not a mockup.**

It makes a few changes:
[list][*]The unspeakably awful (and strangely omnipresent) "sidebar"-button, is gone. Vanished. Hit F9 if you want to view the Sidebar. 
[*]"Time:" has been removed. Why was this even needed?
[*]And everything is on a single line, so it looks neater.[/list]

OK, I said it was perfect for me, and I lied: it still doesn't let me hide the status bar (seriously, every other app can do this! Even the Log Viewer!!), but it's a huge improvement.
What do you think?
