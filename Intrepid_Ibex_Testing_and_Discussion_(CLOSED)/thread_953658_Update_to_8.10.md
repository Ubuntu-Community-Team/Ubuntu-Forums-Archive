### Post by aeiah on 2008-10-20
upgrades should in theory be smoother and smoother as time goes on. if there arent any new features you like and everything is working fine, then there's no reason to upgrade. im still running 7.10.

there is a chance that modules and drivers are not kept when everything is upgraded. this is because they're tailored to suit whatever kernel is running, and with a new version of ubuntu comes a new kernel. this distro should be clever enough to upgrade everything for you but if you've compiled drivers and modules manually you may have to do the same again. if you just installed your graphics card and wifi drivers through the restricted hardware manager there shouldnt be a problem. at worse it just means clicking in there again once you've upgraded.
