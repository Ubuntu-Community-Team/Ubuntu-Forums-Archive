### Post by RAOF on 2008-10-01
> **MacUntu said:**
> Maybe good for Emountu but I like themes that are easy on eyes and soul. There's a reason "dark on light" was the first choice for thousands of years.

Yes.  Because (a) we were using subtractive display technologies, where dark-on-light makes more sense, and (b) because it was just plain *easier* to make dark marks on light stuff than light marks on dark stuff.

The historical preference for dark-on-light may not be optimal for our new additive, backlit display technologies.  For example, there seems to be some research suggesting that light-on-dark text is easier to read and causes less eye-strain on computer screens.

> **RAOF said:**
> Yes. (b) because it was just plain *easier* to make dark marks on light stuff than light marks on dark stuff.

Ok, you got me there. That argumentation wasn't invulnerable. :D

> **MaX said:**
> Could you point out that research to me? I couldn't find anything from Google...

The cool thing about research: For ever pro-paper you can find a con-paper. ;) What I've found many times: the reading speed of dark-on-light is faster than that of light-on-dark. One reason may be halation effects that occur even on new displays (thanks to Overdrive technology aso.). 

Let light-on-dark be easier on the eyes but that doesn't automatically mean it's more readable. Glossy displays are another problem: the darker the colors, the more your display turns into a mirror.

Last but not least: colors affect humans. Choosing a dominant color that does so in a negative way isn't very helpful I guess.
