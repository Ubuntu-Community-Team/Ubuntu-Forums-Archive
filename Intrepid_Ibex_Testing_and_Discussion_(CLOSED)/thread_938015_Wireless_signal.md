### Post by the real omni on 2008-10-04
Well I must say I'm quite impressed with the progress of Ibex.

Still almost a month before the LTS rollout and my system is pretty much rock solid for stability - only a weird keybinding issue ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=934444](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=934444)), a hardware driver issue ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=935460](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=935460)), a REALLY weird boot-up bug ([https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/276611](https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/276611)), and power management ([http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=935473](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=935473)) are giving me any kind of grief.

However, here's what I notice on the other side of the coin:

- HUGELY increased performance of 3D and 2D accelerated graphics: Compiz effects, 3D desktop, and xwinwrap animated desktops are all lightning fast!

- Significantly increased wireless reception: I haven't moved my laptop (HP Pavilion dv6500) or router, but having upped to 8.10 my average signal strength has gone from ~60% to ~75% which makes a HUGE difference in transfer speeds due to less dropped packets.

- Faster execution times for just about everything.

- Faster install times.

- Was having weird issues in 8.04 with thunderbird and enigmail that even the moz devs couldn't fix - those melted away with the 8.10 update.

- RealVNC used to take AGES to draw the first screen before it would accept input - now it takes about 1.5 seconds.

Can't wait for the LTS (hopefully it'll resolve the issues listed above) but already I'm lovin' Ibex even in its fledgeling state. 

Kudos to the dev team for the work done thus far! To contrast their results, here were my observations when moving from XP to Vista:

- Fancier skin.

- Slightly improved security for average home users who didn't know how to properly secure XP, at the cost of constantly pestering the user for "Allow/Deny" requests.

- Slower operation (updating the OS requires upgrading the hardware in a lot of cases!)

- Less program compatibility.

That's about it. :P So based on my observations, the Ubuntu dev team is lightyears ahead of "the big boys" (and by "big" I mean "bloated")
