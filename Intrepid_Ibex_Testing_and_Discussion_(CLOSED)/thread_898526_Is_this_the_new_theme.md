### Post by Kernel Sanders on 2008-08-23
Forgive the ignorance, but i'm not even remotely competent enough to test Intrepid myself at this stage :(

I have been made aware that [*THIS*{Click me}]("http://www.ubuntu-pics.de/bild/2371/screenshot015GKBZ.png") is the new theme for intrepid.

If so, it looks awesome! Really really professional. Actually makes me go "wow" right from the get go. It will surely help Linux adoption for sure.

My general opinion is that the new theme should be targetted to make NEW USERS go "wow". The people that are more than proficient can change and customise the theme themselves with little hassle, so I think it would be a bad move to tone down the theme in response to criticism from more established users. Then you have a theme the established users like from the get go, but not quite "wow" enough to tempt new users.

Therefore, push the boat out, and let the eye candy go wild. It will draw new users like a moth to a flame, and if the established users don't like it or think it's too much, then they are more than able to change it themselves!

My $0.02 :)

### Post by Mazza558 on 2008-08-23
I'm not a big fan. I think there's a few glaring problems with it at the moment, and I hope this isn't the absolute final theme.

Ignoring AWN/Emerald (which won't be enabled by default), here's some problems I spotted:

- Menu bar doesn't match the rest of the window colors
- The lines on the scrollbars extend over the arrows
- In Firefox, website logos in the address bar will not match the cream background

If the metacity theme shown in an earlier alpha was used, this would look pretty good. I'm not convinced with the color scheme overall either - it'd be nicer if the titlebar and window blended.
