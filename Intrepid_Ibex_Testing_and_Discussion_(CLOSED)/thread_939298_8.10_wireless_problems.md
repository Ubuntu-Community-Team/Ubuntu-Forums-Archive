### Post by pytheas22 on 2008-10-06
wicd will probably not help in your case.  There's an underlying problem with your wireless driver that's preventing the interface from being brought up properly, which is what we need to figure out before anything else.

I did find this [bug report]("https://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+source/linux/+bug/278350") which seems to address your problem.  The error messages coming out in the dmesg log there match yours, and the behavior is the same--wireless worked fine in Hardy, but not in Intrepid.

Unfortunately, no one has found a solution yet, but the bug report was only filed a few days ago.  I think your best bet at this point is to keep following that report; hopefully within a few days this will be resolved.  It seems to be an issue that will affect many PCMCIA cards on Intrepid, so they will probably put a priority on resolving it.

Also feel free to join in the bug report discussion directly if you have time--they will appreciate any information you can provide.

If the bug report stops getting responses or your issue isn't solved by the time Intrepid is released in stable form, please post back here.  But for the time being, you're going to be able to get much better help from the people on Launchpad than from me--they actually know something about how Intrepid and wireless drivers work.
