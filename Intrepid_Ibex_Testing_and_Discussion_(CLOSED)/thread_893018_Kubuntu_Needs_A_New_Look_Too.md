### Post by jlacroix on 2008-08-17
Ubuntu is supposed to get a whole new look, but what about Kubuntu?

As you all should remember, the LTS status of Kubuntu was cancelled so they could focus on KDE 4.0. 

With that focus on KDE 4 came nothing exciting. The KDE 4.0.x version of Kubuntu Hardy was nothing more than a vanilla install with KDE3 components thrown in, and (in my opinion, no offense) it ended up being the worst implementation of KDE 4.0.x compared to all other distros. The "focus" on KDE 4 that caused Kubuntu Hardy to lose its LTS status didn't pay off at all.

The implementation of KDE 4.1 in Intrepid and Hardy seems to be very good, but it's also very vanilla. Are they going to make a theme at all for Kubuntu Intrepid, or just use a vanilla KDE 4 install all over again? I would hope that they would customize it with Kubuntu specific plasmoids and themes.

I'll accept Kubuntu Intrepid regardless, because I can see that good work has been done with it since I've not encountered any bugs yet. However, I am hoping that it's default themes/wallpapers/icons and everything else gets some love.

Has anything been announced yet?
