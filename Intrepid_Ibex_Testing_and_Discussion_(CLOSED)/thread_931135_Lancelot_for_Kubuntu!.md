### Post by 1cewolf on 2008-09-26
Link to Brainstorm idea: [http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/13768/](http://brainstorm.ubuntu.com/idea/13768/)

I like Kubuntu, but it still doesn't have much of an identity. I don't think anyone is going to argue with me on that one. Replacing the Kickoff menu with the Lancelot menu as the default could be an important step towards carving out its niche.

If you don't know what I'm talking about, visit Lancelot's web site and read up on it before you continue.

[http://lancelot.fomentgroup.org/main](http://lancelot.fomentgroup.org/main)

The Kickoff menu is pretty nifty. I think that it's an improvement over the classic menu, it offers more functionality, and it looks pretty darned cool. The problem with Kickoff is that it's simply not the best. It offers improvements over the limitations of the classic menu without going the extra mile and providing the the kind of functionality which I believe should define the default KDE 4 menu. Lancelot is superior in many ways to Kickoff.

*Kickoff splits the Favorites and Applications into two separate tabs. Lancelot consolidates them neatly into one tab.
*Lancelot's "Applications" tab is closer to the menu icon than Kickoff's. Trivial, but noteworthy.
*Lancelot's dual columns make its Computer tab neater and easier on the eyes.
*Lancelot's "computer" tab has the System Monitor. Trivial, but helpful and appropriately placed. 
*Lancelot's "computer" tab makes better distinctions than Kickoff's - "places", "removable drives", and "fixed drives".
*Lancelot's "documents" tab lets you create a new document and view recent ones. The integrated functionality is helpful and it helps to keep the "Favorites" cleaner.
*Lancelot's "contacts" tab integrates e-mail and IM functionality. Kickoff offers nothing comparable.
*Kickoff has a tab for its shutdown options. Lancelot's shutdown options do not and are viewable regardless of what tab is open.
*Lancelot arranges the items in the menu depending on the position of the menu button which activates it so that the most used categories appear nearest to the mouse.
*In Kickoff's application view, it only displays one column at a time. Lancelot displays multiple columns at once and has a breadcrumb (I think that's the proper term) display at the top.
*Lancelot's "no-click activation" lets you accomplish anything with just one click or even no clicks at all! No-click is downright addictive once you start to use it and it's a nice feature for accessibility. Lancelot also supports two other "clicking" configurations if you like to click.
*With Kickoff, you're forced to use a single menu launcher icon. Lancelot lets you do that OR split its 4 tabs as separate icons, kind of like what GNOME does. That alone may draw in GNOME users and the added flexibility is only a positive thing. 
*If you do choose to use a single menu launcher icon, Lancelot makes it easy for you to use a custom icon.
*You can drag and drop parts of Lancelot onto the desktop as separate Plasmoids. Kickoff offers nothing of the sort.

No distribution, to my knowledge, has included Lancelot as the standard menu. For the life of me, I can't understand why, but I think Kubuntu should be the first! Lancelot is superior to Kickoff on a number of fronts and Kubuntu could distinguish itself by adopting it.

If you haven't tried out Lancelot yet, it's in the repos. Download it, try it out, and see if you don't agree with me.
