### Post by Jeremy Jackins on 2008-10-16
I accidentally solved my problem. I don't know when it started working, so here's everything I did until I noticed that it was working: 
First I opened synaptic and purged anything to do with fglrx or the open source ati driver. Then I opened jockey and installed fglrx again. I rebooted, and jockey said that fglrx was NOT installed, but synaptic said it was. I purged all the fglrx packages again, then pressed ctrl-alt-f1 and stopped gdm, reinstalled fglrx, ran dpkg-reconfigure -phigh xserver-xorg, and then ran sudo aticonfig --initial, and then rebooted. At this point I noticed my window moving/resizing and scrolling problems were gone. I then went into the appearance menu, and enabled desktop effects, but this brought up a dialogue asking me to install the proprietary ati driver, which I cancelled, but desktop effects remained enabled. I'm afraid to touch anything, but right now everything seems to be working normally.
