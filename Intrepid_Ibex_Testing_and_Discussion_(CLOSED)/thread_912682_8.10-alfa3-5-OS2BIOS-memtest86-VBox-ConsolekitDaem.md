Gawd, your seriously hard work, but I'll try to help. :)

Im sure there is countless websites around where you could lookup what English words mean in your native language. Tip one, try not to be lazy and help yourself. Waffle is a phrase used to describe unnecessarily long and convoluted obfuscation of the subject matter at hand. The saying goes, keep it simple silly (KISS).

Refer to the link in my sig about how to contribute to Intrepid. Amongst other things it has wiki details on reporting bugs and info from the bug squad team about the status, lifecycle, best practices and so forth. There is a mountain of information there for you.

During an alpha, you should be expecting crashes and other problems. Again if you were to google the software development lifecycle and alpha releases specifically you'd find a ton of information on the Internet about what this means.

If you have intermittant crashing and other general instability it is often best to stress test the machine. This will establish that various subsytems are working properly and that your machine is/is not a solid base to commence alpha testing on. Stress testing should involve tests of your CPU, cache, memory, GPU involving both function and thermal stability. Google for tools to do this.

Consolekit was not developed primarily by Ubuntu. This is common where projects are seen to be "upstream" and distributions make decisions about what upstream projects to use, what patches to apply to the native code if any and how to configure it to their distro.

Its best not to change file permissions for the system file system and restrict your permissions changes to the user's home directory.
