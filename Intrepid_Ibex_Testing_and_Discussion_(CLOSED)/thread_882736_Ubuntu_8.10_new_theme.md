### Post by BwackNinja on 2008-08-08
The way I see it, [https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DesktopTeam/Specs/SpitAndPolish](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/DesktopTeam/Specs/SpitAndPolish) needs to be done, and then we work from there. We have nice-looking decorationless windows now with beautiful, smooth, rounded corners. This can and should be applied to the menus, just like in willwill's mockup. The only things I don't like about that mockup are the yellow and the use of AWN by default. I personally don't like docks, and the only one I've been able to put up with for more than a couple hours is kiba-dock and that didn't last long either. The most important part of the SpitAndPolish spec IMO is the *ability* to use rgba not necessarily the use of rgba everywhere by default. If gtkStatusIcon can get updated and gnome-panel with it, then we would be able to set a partially transparent png for the panel background, text wouldn't less visible (using a compositor like compiz and setting it there), and no simply using the wallpaper as the background of the panel which disrupts the illusion when rotating a transparent cube. You would also be able to set a transparent background as the nautilus background (and experiment with different semitransparent effects) and use the wallpaper plugin to draw the background, which could also be linked to the work here: [http://gsocblog.jsharpe.net/archives/category/gsoc](http://gsocblog.jsharpe.net/archives/category/gsoc) and have the background still set in gnome appearance preferences. A final simple eyecandy thing that can be done is to have a wallpaper slideshow, which is a feature built-in to gnome, and the most notable slideshow I know of is this: [http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=784881](http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=784881), which fades slowly (literally taking hours to change) between different variations of Dawn of Ubuntu, showing the time of day. Having something like that would be amazing I think.

### Post by schmidtbag on 2008-08-08
The default theme for Ubuntu really isn't that bad and I don't think people understand WHY its brown.  Is brown ugly? Sure, but I see the coloring as a kind of trademark.  Ubuntu is an African word. ### Post by analystscouch on 2008-09-02
Decided to stop lurking and add my personal view about the difficulty with that touchy subject "the default theme".

I suppose one of the basic problems with designing a default theme is walking a path between unobtrusive & "busy". Themes with lots of "effects" may be distracting and busy, but I want to suggest that unobtrusive (which in fact I usually support) can also have its problems...

My feeling is that the problem with the current theme (Human) is that it is almost *too* unobtrusive. Such that it has sort of tipped over from being unobtrusive (which is good for the default theme I think) to being quite hard to actually tell the difference between different parts of the desktop, because everything is the same shade of grey (panels, menubar, window border, window background). On my monitor (which isn't great) when I hover over a running application on the panel the change of shade is so minor that it is virtually impossible to tell that anything has changed. The same thing when I hover over a scrollbar (now, I'm not saying that there HAS to be a hover colour, only that if there is going to be one it might as well be visible!).

Next, basically what NewHuman did was to do the exactly the same thing as Human, but with dark brown, make almost everything look exactly the same. But it went one step further with the window title bar, because even this became dark brown, with only a very thin line at the top which was in any way different (to show the windows as active). The new HumanMurrine in Ken W's PPA does the same to that theme, almost removing the one remaining bit of differentiation (the thick brown title bar), by making it thin like in NewHuman.

This is why, personally, I like the hybrid dark/light themes (newWave etc.). Because the panels/menubar (which tend to be dark) are still unobtrusive, they don't have load of flashy patterns/transparency/multiple-colours to distract me from what really matters - the content inside the window. But they also show a differentiation between elements on the desktop (well, some don't currently, but there is the promise of differentiation, whilst remaining unobtrusive, in the spirit of some of these designs).

Given that these themes may not be ready in time for intrepid I think a temporary solution might be tweaking the current themes: The human-murrine (in intrepid or Ken W's PPA) could be improved by simply changing the shade of either a) the gnome panel or b) the background of the windows. The point being to make the windows stand out slightly from the panels. My preference would be black, but even just a slightly different shade of grey might do it.

Anyone agree; totally disagree? :)

Best,
Nick

### Post by Greyed on 2008-09-27
> **SunnyRabbiera said:**
> why are thse dark themes so popular?
I cant see half of them.

Because monitors, unlike paper, are projective.  Paper is reflective.  Put a piece of paper next to your monitor, wait until after dark then turn off all the that would project light into the room in which your computer is housed.  You'll noticed two things.
