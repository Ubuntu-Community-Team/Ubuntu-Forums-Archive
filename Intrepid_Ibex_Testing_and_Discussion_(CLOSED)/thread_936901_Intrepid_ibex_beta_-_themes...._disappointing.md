### Post by *g!t5^_)*(H on 2008-10-05
Some personal considerations ...

Is not the first time Mark promises something not possible to deliver due various reasons, sadly is not his fault if 'the community' can't provide professional themes (maybe gnome design and compiz situation are responsible too)

I don't know why Ubuntu keeps the brown color, Ubuntu.com has been red and orange for some time and these are good looking colors for everyone. (I've never seen posts for new theme colors for Ubuntu.com, but there are thousands of posts asking for better Ubuntu theme.

I don't like the themes purposed by the community, in fact I don't like dark (near black) themes because I like to see the shadows near the menus and windows, and these shadows disappears using black themes. But I recognize some people has been working a lot, their job received congratulations from thousands of users, and they deserve to have their themes (at least) installed by default (not activated by default, but accessible without special downloads)

Hopefully I'm wrong, but I don't see Ubuntu going as far as OSX with theme and usability in a near future. We still have to wait until Gnome 3.0 is out, to have better integration/usability desktop experience. Then we will depend on Ubuntu's taste on tunning themes (and we know there are no good aesthetics decisions coming from Ubuntu). My desire is Mark paying good money from a professional studio (not expensive, but people who knows what they do), to have at last a good looking machine.

Until then, if Ubuntu remains looking like now, the most part of people will think: "It's free as in beer because looks like poo, and probably works like poo". 

What people see when they turn on the live CD installer, their first impression is the most important part of captivation. If it fails with a non good looking theme (and by people's criticism in the forums), like the one we have now. Ubuntu is committed to be used only as a third player.
