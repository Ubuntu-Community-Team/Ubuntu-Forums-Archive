### Post by alligoodw on 2008-09-22
I downloaded the ISO image of Intrepid Ibex Alpha 6 and burned it to a CD.  Rebooted the system and selected the option that allowed me to run the OS without altering the hard drive - I never made it to the desktop.

It will be a glorious day when Ubuntu, any version, is capable of installing itself and identifying Nvidia graphics cards correctly. I could never get past the fact that Intrepid couldn't identify my graphics card; I have a HP laptop.  I rebooted the system into Windows Vista and discarded the CD.  

The OBJECT is clear:  install CD and identify ALL hardware correctly.  Then ENJOY said OS and live the good life.  When it gets as simple as putting in the CD and booting into the OS with no problems, then you've reached a milestone.  

Mind you, I realize that this is an alpha release.  It was evident in the boot-up process.
