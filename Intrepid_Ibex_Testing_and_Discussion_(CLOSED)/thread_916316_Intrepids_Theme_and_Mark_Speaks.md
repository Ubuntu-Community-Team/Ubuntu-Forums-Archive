### Post by psyke83 on 2008-09-10
Judging by many of the posts on the [ubuntu-art]("https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-art/") mailing list (by "seasoned" members, though I won't name names), the general view is that there is a severe lack of communication and understanding between Mark and the art team.

Mark may have a grand vision of innovation in various aspects of Ubuntu, but the reality is that we're dealing all with upstream's work. We can't significantly modify UI paradigms unless we have folks actively working upstream on GTK and QT, for example (and as far as I know, we don't - or at least they're not working with the art team). Meanwhile, real progress has been stifled due to these grand visions and a lack of realism towards what's feasible *now*, with the code and resources we have.

If you look at Ubuntu art mockups, for example, in 99% of cases you'll  see a major interface change presented (usually it's a major re-tooling of Nautilus). That's simply not possible with our resources! In the real world, we need someone to create a concept against something realistic, such as [The Widget Factory]("http://www.stellingwerff.com/?page_id=10"), and work on making that concept work as a real GTK theme, using the resources we have. Significant interface changes can only realistically happen with the GTK/GNOME and QT/KDE folks.

That's just my two cents based on my limited experience, and despite what I've said, there is still plenty of great work getting done by the art team and community alike.
