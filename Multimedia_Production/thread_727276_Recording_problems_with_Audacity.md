### Post by Irihapeti on 2008-03-19
What may have happened - and I admit I'm only guessing - is that the config files have got messed up. If so, then reinstalling the program wouldn't help any. You could try removing the config files and let the program create new ones. Audacity keeps its config files in .audacity-data in your user directory. You'll need to check "show hidden files" to be able to see it.

### Post by jsilas on 2008-05-08
I'm in that same boat. Sometimes I can get Audacity to work by logging out and logging back in. Sometimes I need to restart Audacity. As far as your input device goes. Open Audacity, pull the "edit" menu, choose "preferences". A window (sorry to use that word) will pop up. Choose "audio I/O" on the left side of the window (sorry again) You will see a couple of options. Two being "playback" and "recording. Pull the "playback device" menu and select your audio output device. That could be your sound card or an audio interface. You'll have to choose your Recording Device also, that would be pretty much the same, thats just your mic or whatever your using as an input. ANYWAY.
