### Post by liquid circuit on 2008-04-01
In the world of audio production, the title of your thread doesn't really make it clear what you are looking for.

Now, I haven't done much work with audio production on linux, so I don't have any recommendations for specific software.  However, what you are looking for is referred to as a "sampler."

Now, what you DON'T want is a "drum sampler", as these will usually only play the sample (the sound file) at a specific pitch, and different samples will usually be mapped to different keys (so you can emulate a drum kit).

And it sounds like you also want either an on-screen virtual keyboard (that sends MIDI data to the sampler), or an app that will map your physical computer keyboard and make it act like a MIDI keyboard.

You may be able to find an app with all these features built-in.  Hopefully you can find something fairly basic, because samplers can get pretty complicated.  If you can't find one with a keyboard built in, you are going to need to learn a little bit about MIDI routing to get the keyboard sending data to the sampler.
