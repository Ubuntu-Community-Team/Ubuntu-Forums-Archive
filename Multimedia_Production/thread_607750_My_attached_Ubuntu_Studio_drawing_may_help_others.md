### Post by theorganloft on 2007-11-09
Attached please find a drawing of my studio configuration.  It may be a help to one of you trying to build your home studio.  

The most difficult configuration was to be able to monitor while recording.  I used a mixer's Aux send and returned the playback to the Tape-In on the mixer so that I can isolate the 2 signals. The playback signal cannot re-enter the recording. I can monitor through headphones and/or monitors.  When podcasting, I never turn on the live speakers because of the live microphone.  

To make my synthesizer piano have a more natural sound, I open the mic while playing it and you can hear my breathing and fingers as I play the keyboard.  You get a little background reverberation from the room too. It almost fools the ear into thinking that the piano is real. 

One other important instrument is my compressor limiter, expander and gate.  This instrument protects and processes the raw signal from the mixer to the Delta Revolution audio input.  It keeps the input signal clean and steady.  This works well for live recording where a guitar or Drum can send some pretty strong peaks through the board and possibly damage your computer audio card. :guitar:

Answer: Monitoring was my biggest problem.  You do not want to record from a source that you playback on unless you can completely isolate the signal hence the AUX Out for record and I come in the Tape In on the mixer.  On my Mackie mixer, the Tape in can be physically separated from the board.  I can then hear the board and the tape  through the headphones.  This way I can use one sound card: the m-audio delta for both playback and record and my configuration is less complicated.
