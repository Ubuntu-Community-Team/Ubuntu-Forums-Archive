### Post by NickZA on 2007-08-31
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_management](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Color_management)

In essence -- how accurate is the conversion of color between what you see/create onscreen and what you see on paper (or other medium) once it's printed. That depends on a host of factors including the physical characteristics of printers, video adapters and monitors, drivers, the way art programs render their colours, and all sorts.

EDIT: But essentially, the operating system can "align" these factors to some degree, as I understand it. In windows you can go to Color Management and modify settings / calibrate your setup for these purposes.
