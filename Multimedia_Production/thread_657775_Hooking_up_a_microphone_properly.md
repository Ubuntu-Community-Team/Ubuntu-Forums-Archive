### Post by steevc on 2008-01-04
I'm not sure how the microphone could get damaged by plugging it into a PC. I think that site is a bit alarmist about using standard sound cards. I'm sure there are plenty of people out there using them for recording music, but they will not produce the best results. The microphone pre-amp on the card is likely to be very basic and may not do the SM57 justice. I would expect you could get much better results just using a cheap mixer plugged into the line-in as that is likely to have better pre-amps.

I'm surprised you got any signal via the line-in.

There should be some sort of microphone boost option in the software mixer that may help.

There's a review of the SM57 at

[http://www.tweakheadz.com/review_of_the_sm57.htm](http://www.tweakheadz.com/review_of_the_sm57.htm)

The forums on that site are pretty newbie friendly if you have questions about this sort of thing, but there is not that much mention of Linux.
