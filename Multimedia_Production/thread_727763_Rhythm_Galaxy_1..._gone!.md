### Post by eric71 on 2008-03-18
I have had good results with the Colombo kit for Hydrogen from Musix, a debian based music distro. Their stuff usually has installed fine on Ubuntu for me:

[https://www.musix.org.ar/wiki/index.php/Colombodrumkit-1-en](https://www.musix.org.ar/wiki/index.php/Colombodrumkit-1-en)

If you are interested in loops, I can't recommend Drums On Demand highly enough, though not exactly cheap. I'm not too good at making realistic drum patterns, so using loop libraries has been a huge help for me.

### Post by kensonic on 2008-03-20
Yes, being stuck inside the Reason universe can be annoying at times, though I can't deny what a great tool it's been for me over the years. Rewire to the rescue!

BTW, if you all you want to do is extract the samples from Reason Refills, there's a free utility floating around that does a fine job, though it's somewhat crude and fiddly. Do a search for: Refill Viewer. I use it when I want to port my Reason projects that use Refills without having to drag around a 500 mb Refill when I'm only using 5 of the samples ;-)...
