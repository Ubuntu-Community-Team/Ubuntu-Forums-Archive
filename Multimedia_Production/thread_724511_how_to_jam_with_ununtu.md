### Post by nalmeth on 2008-03-14
I'm sure there are places to download samples, by why not just create your own samples to jam to?

Use hydrogen to create the drum sequences first. Doing this ensures your entire track is on 'time'
Import the track into ardour
Then use zynaddsubfx to create some smooth baselines over top of the drums (you have to play it live however). 
Then jam to the sample, and record each take for review afterwards. Hearing yourself play is a great way to improve your playing. 
You notice the subtle habits that you usually wouldn't hear otherwise.

As for guitars, perhaps you could use a midi tab editor like tuxguitar to write guitar parts, but the guitar sound is kinda cheesy. Obviously it would be best to play the guitar yourself if possible.

Anyway, I don't know if this answered your question, but its what I try to do when I have no one to jam with
