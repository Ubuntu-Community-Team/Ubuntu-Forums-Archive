### Post by IanW on 2007-07-18
The cassette tape was launched in C-45 & C-90 lengths as the maximum length of _both sides_ of a 12-inch vinyl album was 45-minutes.

A standard CD will take 74-minutes of audio (Why? - it's the length of a "slow" rendition of Beethoven's 9th Symphony).

"Overburning" can add a couple of minutes at the risk of making the disc unreadable in a standard audio player.

An easy alternative is to go to the store and ask for 80- or 90-minute CDs?

### Post by Jeremy23 on 2007-07-31
CD audio is uncompressed. Therefore, it is not possible to make it occupy more or less than the 650 or so MB when you have 74 minutes of audio.

As Motoxrdude and luisbg suggested, I recommend you burn a CD with .ogg or .mp3 files instead. I recommend .ogg because 1) it is a free format and 2) they are actually slightly smaller than .mp3.
