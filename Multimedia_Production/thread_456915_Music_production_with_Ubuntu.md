It really depends on what he is doing.
There are a number of pretty cool apps for making music, and recently even VST instruments can be run natively on Linux.
There are mainly two stumbling blocks right now.
Sustain pedal may be a problem in some cases.
Drum kits seem to lack proper close and open of high-hat.

None of the sequencers are trying to emulate Cubase, So there are differences in both strengths and weaknesses. Cubase is a pretty nice piece of software, but the dongle is annoying, and portage of creations are not always possible between versions, The dongle check eats CPU power, and Steinberg is attempting lockins on saved formats, extensions, and external tools.

On the other hand, there is very powerful possibilities in Linux with Jack, because almost all music software can communicate through Jack. So every sound module can be used with any sequencer.
This kind of flexibility does not exist in Windows, and is a killer feature IMO.

My advise is to try out Ubuntu Studio, (Because of the realtime kernel).
I believe it will be another year or two, before FOSS totally kick Steinberg & co. But only the future can say for sure. Today it does in some areas, and not in others.

Let your friend play with some of the apps in Ubuntu Studio, then he can ask here if he has specific problems and wishes.
