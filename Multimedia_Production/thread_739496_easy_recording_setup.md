### Post by em3raldxiii on 2008-03-29
There is an application called ARDOUR which might appeal to you.  It's very similar to Nuendo, Cubase, etc.  It's not necessarily easier to use, but it is definitely more feature rich than audacity.

Also, I have a Korg D12 recording unit as well.  Something you might try (depending on how many tracks you have), is plugging your unit's output into your soundcard's input; then simply record straight across in order to back up your existing recordings.  Then you can feel free to erase recorded material from your recorder.  We found this was particularly handy when doing recordings of live events with the recording unit (when we didn't have a computer at our disposal).  Later, we'd slap the recording onto our computer.  Great for sampling and stuff too.

Anyhoo ... give Ardour a try and see how it goes.

Also, there is an entire Ubuntu distribution project called Ubuntu-Studio that might interest you.  Plenty of recording-related help :D
