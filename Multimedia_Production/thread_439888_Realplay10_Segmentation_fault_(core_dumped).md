### Post by Pocadotty on 2007-05-11
Don't know... 

I tried real player for Linux 2 years ago.  I got it semi working on Fedora Core 5, but it was fairly useless on their, and i wasn't able to get it working on Ubuntu 5.something from the binaries anyway.

My advice is to use something else.  I use Amarok for my all my listening needs and I love it!!!  Out of all the media players on all the OSs that I have come across, amarok is my favorite.  Try a few different players, a fair amount of the ones in the repo's are pretty good.

If you absolutley MUST HAVE real player I would try compiling from source... you can get the source from the Helix community here:
[https://player.helixcommunity.org/2005/downloads/](https://player.helixcommunity.org/2005/downloads/)

your likely better off with something else though... If its file formats your worried about, between Ubuntu restricted extras and Gstreamer you can play almost everything.

Best of luck...
