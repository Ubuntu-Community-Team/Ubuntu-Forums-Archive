### Post by deadgobby on 2007-06-26
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MidiSoftwareSynthesisHowTo](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MidiSoftwareSynthesisHowTo)
 All so look at [http://www.vintagesynth.com/](http://www.vintagesynth.com/)   forums section for more information. The synth forums has oodles of synth guru's that may help you. As getting USB to communicate strait  with the Akia, you may need to do some mojo. Before going to mojo route. Try rebooting ubie with the Akia on and with the USB plugged in. I wounder if Ubie will see it. I do not have such a keyboard and I do not think any one has tried to hack into it. 
 A good solution  is that you could take a  removable usb storage stick and load up the wav files from ubie and plug it right into the usb port of the Akia. USB sticks are cheap and most are recognized by Ubie. Heck you can just look for the penguin on the back of the USB package if in doubt. I can take a USB stick and load up MP3 files and works fine with windows and vis versa. So like you edit your wav sample with Audacity and save it on the USB stick, plug it into the Akia and it should work. Unless the Akia wants to format it the USB stick to Apple lingo.
