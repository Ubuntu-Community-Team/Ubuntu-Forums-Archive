### Post by SmallAxe on 2007-07-17
Aloha,

      First things first, you need to chose an audio program that will recognize your mixing board.  Audacity is probably the simplest.

The issue that you could possible run into is that there may not be driver support for your hardware through the usb. As a work around you can run a set of RCA to eighth inch into your computer's input.

Ideally if your hardware is recognized  by one program it should be recognized by MOST.

Once you are sure that you can monitor the audio in from your board check out:

VLC
Darwin
Shoutcast 
Icecast

Shoutcast and Icecast would be best for audio especially for broadcast.  Darwin and VLC are a little harder to configure and work better for video.  Darwin is also useful for streaming to mobile devices.

Though your main issue will be getting your machine to recognize your board, as the drivers are usually proprietary and most companies have not caught on to the growing REVOLUTION of Linux [UBUNTU] users.

I personally own a Presonus Firepod and went as far as requesting that the company develop a driver for Linux users. Though they haven't done so yet they were receptive to the idea.
