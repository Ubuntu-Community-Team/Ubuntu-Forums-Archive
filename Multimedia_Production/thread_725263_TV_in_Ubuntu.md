### Post by Paul Weaver on 2008-03-15
Ignore Myth for starters, you need to get your card working first. Myth has it's own unrelated issues to tackle that and it's not worthwhile looking at them until your tv card is working. Unlike Windows, Linux comes with drivers built in. You should be able to just plug and go. Unfortunately many companies don't give the Linux people the required information to write the drivers. These are usually on substandard hardware, where the work is done by "secret" software instead of the hardware.

Unfortunately The HVR-1600 isn't supported in Linux.

[http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV-HVR-1600](http://www.linuxtv.org/wiki/index.php/Hauppauge_WinTV-HVR-1600)

However there are experimental attempts to get this particular card working (as is the Linux way). Unfortunately that means a lot of trial and error, you'd basically become a developer.
