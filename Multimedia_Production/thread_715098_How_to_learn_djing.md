### Post by Stochastic on 2008-03-04
Well without turntables, your best software to use would be Mixxx [http://www.mixxx.org](http://www.mixxx.org)

Now as for learning, that all depends on what you want to do?  DJing is a very broad topic and many people have many different interpretations of that. 
- If you want to learn how to DJ as many radio DJs do, then I'd say fiddle around with Amarok (not really DJing in my opinion). 
- If you want to beatmatch songs to make remixes or long techno/hip hop/whatever style sets (like club DJs) then Mixxx should get you started on that process.  Though IMHO a turntable or other such physical controller would be much better to learn on.  My one piece of advice for a beginner doing this is to learn with house music, then move to the style you like.
- If you want to learn how to scratch and be a turntablist I'd HIGHLY recommend you go buy a pair of turntables.  But if you don't then try terminatorX [http://terminatorx.org/](http://terminatorx.org/)

As for the actual process of learning how to work these tools, there are lots of 'teach yourself to DJ' tutorials around the net.  I taught myself back in the day, and it's helped me build my own technique.

Now audio editing etc... is a whole other kettle of (related) fish.  Programs such as Ardour, Audacity, Rezound, Jamin, JACK, and the LADSPA plugin set are all good tools in that trade/industry.  Though for a newbie a simpler program such as Jokosher (though it's not terribly stable) might be a smoother learning curve.

Hope this helps, and feel free to ask more questions for clarification.
