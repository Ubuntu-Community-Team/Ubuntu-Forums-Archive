### Post by radi0j0hn on 2008-01-02
I can't help with MIDI, but I have been doing audio recording and editing for more than 15 years for radio broadcasting and audio books. 

I am using Ubuntu 64 bit on a new HP unit that has Vista. When in Windows mode, I use an "In-port" USB device that is nothing but audio in via L and R RCA plugs.  This unit got good ratings for transformer isolation and build quality. In short, it only has to do one thing, audio IN and it does it well.  I use an Onkyo device if I need to transfer old DATs digitally.

So, reading your post, I plugged in the In-port and fired up Audacity.  It worked fine, with my mic going into a control board and to the In-port.

My GUESS is that  most USB devices, especially those for input only should work.  B&H photo has a whole catalog of tasty interfaces starting at under $100 for one with 2 mic ports and MIDI.

Pretty amazing.  My original rig from the early '90s used a 500 MB (not GB) Maxtor drive that cost $1,000 and some editing gear that was "destructive."  That meant once you cut, you couldn't undo!

Still beat tape and a razor blade!    John
