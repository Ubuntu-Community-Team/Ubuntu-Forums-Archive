### Post by disturbed1 on 2007-10-27
It's honestly a matter of personal opinion. GoGo **is** a lower quality encoder than Lame, when given the same settings. Meaning, if you use both Lame and GoGo at the highest quality settings, Lame will sound better. GoGo on the highest setting is *better* than Lame on the lowest setting ;).

I'd listen to the tracks, if they sound good enough to **you**, then keep doing what your doing. My girlfriend rips all of her music using 128kb CBR mp3, and swears by it. I use oggenc -q 8 and think it's good enough, but I cringe when I listen to her mp3 tracks on my portable :)

I used to use GoGo-no-coda years and years ago. It would take my 486dx about a day with Lame and Blade, but GoGo was much quicker.
