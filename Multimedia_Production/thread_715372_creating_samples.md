### Post by Stochastic on 2008-03-05
Okay, before we go any further let's get some terms defined:

Sample - a bit of recorded soundwave

Sampler - a tool that plays back samples (often pitch-shifted or altered)

Synth - a series of tools that together create soundwaves

Synth Preset - a set of levels, rates, and routings that make a synth create a certain waveform

sorry if that's a bit pedantic, but it seems like you were confusing what ZynAddSubFX does.  It's a Synth and not a sampler.  You will have a very hard time taking bits from a movie and making ZynAddSubFX play them.

Now I'm not that fluent with ZynAddSubFX, so I'm not sure of it's exact capabilities and maybe it does have a wavetable oscillator feature that you can load samples into, but that's not it's primary source of sound creation (from what I know).  Actually, I just checked wikipedia and it seems to think that Zyn doesn't use external samples anywhere.

To make Synth Presets for ZynAddSubFX I'd suggest picking up a couple books on audio synthesis from your local library (it's a complicated world you've just stepped into, but a fun one!).  Start with additive synthesis and then go to subtractive synthesis.  By then you'll know your way around Zyn and can start experimenting to find your perfect sounds.
