### Post by longhaired1 on 2007-09-23
Hi,

I am committed to moving all of my business and personal computing to Linux. The majority of my income is generated from CGI programming and development, and for the most part that has already been moved to the Ubuntu OS.

The next phase involves moving my music and video production to Ubuntu. My focus is on live recording and I am building a monster of a machine to that end. My current audio interface is a Presonus Firestudio and my preferred DAW is Cubase SX3, though I am also looking into Reaper and Ardour. 

My Firestudio has 8 simultaneous inputs and I'll need to add a DigiMax FS to get the full 16 that I need. The problem of course is that Presonus doesn't offer Linux support. It appears that others have had success hooking up their Firepods, but I have yet to hear from someone who has used the Firestudio successfully with Linux. 

The question is, do I purchase the Digimax from Presonus and hope that Linux support eventually comes through via the open source community, which would marry me to Windows for an indefinite period of time, or cut my losses and look for a hardware solution that I know will operate well on Linux?

If I go for the latter option, does anyone have recommendations for the recording interface I should consider?

### Post by Stochastic on 2007-09-23
Well the firestudio is basically the "new model" of the firepod and to my knowledge the chipsets are the same.  According to the ffado (free firewire audio drivers) website, > Presonus has promised to provide us with everything we need. (but hasn't delivered yet) so the ffado guys will soon have interfaces on which to test their drivers etc..  That being said, I'd stick with the interface you have and wait for the ffado drivers to be written.  Possibly talk to the developers to see if you could beta test.
