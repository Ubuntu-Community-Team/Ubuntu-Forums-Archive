### Post by mcking on 2008-03-10
Hey all, I've been doing some research into setting up a small home recording studio.  My requirements are (to me) pretty simple -- 6-8 Mic-level inputs that work pain-free in Linux so I can route directly to jack for recording/mixing with Ardour.  4 drum mics, bass, guitar, vocals plus one extra for good measure should allow me to record a standard 3-4 person band and still have freedom to mix with a little flexibility.

My first thought was to use an inexpensive preamp box, like the Nady PRA-8, plugged into a Lexicon Omega  USB interface.  My research shows that the Omega "just works" in Ubuntu,  The only drawback is that while this gives 8 channels, they are routed to 4 busses available for recording.

Then I saw that for $100 more, Tascam makes the US-1641, which allows for 8 separate mic inputs, instead of the 8 inputs -> 4 busses, like the Omega. Looks like the perfect device for me, as long as it works in Linux.

I couldn't find any info on the Tascam US-1641, only older/smaller models that need some futzing to get working.  Does anyone have any experience yet with the Tascam US-1641 in Linux?

### Post by sodasound on 2008-03-15
I just bought a Lexicon Alpha because my Tascam US122 required a buggy wrapper for Windows drivers, and some magic words in the command line I  wasn't ready for. I suspect the 1641 is too recent for even that solution to work, and Tascam doesn't really co-operate with Windows much either. I'd go for the Lexicon.
