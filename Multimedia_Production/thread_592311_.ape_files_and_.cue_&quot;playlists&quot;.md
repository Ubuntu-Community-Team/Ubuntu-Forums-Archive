### Post by julian67 on 2007-10-26
> **Iuliux said:**
> Lossless with "". Flac, Ape and others are not 100% lossless, the analog to digital transformation involeves loss ;) The conversion from ape to flac of flac to ape is lossy.

If you decompress a flac or an ape file to wav and then compare it with the original wav there is no difference. That is why it is called lossless. Lossless. No loss. It loseth not. Nothing is taken away. Lossless. 

When you convert from ape to flac (or the other way round) the intermediate stage is wav. 

ape>wav>flac or flac>wav>ape

It may be that a gui conversion app does not *show *you the wavs, they would usually be briefly in /tmp before being deleted, but this *is* what happens. 

There is zero loss in converting ape to flac or flac to ape. You can decompress the resulting files and see the wavs are identical to those extracted from the CDDA (Compact Disc Digital Audio).

There is no analogue to digital stage in any part of this. The data on your audio CD is digital. The ripped wav is digital. ape files are digital. flac files are digital. Conversion to analogue only occurs when the files are *listened to* as audio, at which point your soundcard or portable or other audio device converts the file (be it mp3, ogg, ape, flac, wav etc), and that Digital Analogue Converter in your audio device sends the analogue to an amplification device and that sends the signal to your earphones/speakers. 

The only way that there is any analogue stage in ripping/encoding would be if you are ripping vinyl/cassettes/8 track *or* ripping CDs by recording from the line out instead of extracting the CDDA.
