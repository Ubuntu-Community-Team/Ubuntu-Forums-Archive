### Post by philc on 2008-02-05
> **sw40c said:**
> I have found lots of great video editors but they do not mention still image utilization in their FAQs or documentation. 

Try Open Movie Editor. [http://openmovieeditor.sourceforge.net/HomePage](http://openmovieeditor.sourceforge.net/HomePage)

You can drop images directly onto the timeline and manipulate their "length" in the same way as a video. 

I've written a How-To for fade-in and fade-out transition in OME. This involves dropping a black image onto the timeline for the fade to black.

[http://stream0.org/2008/01/open-movie-editor-howto-fade-t.html](http://stream0.org/2008/01/open-movie-editor-howto-fade-t.html)

There's also a pretty good set of general effects available for OME via the Frei0r plugins.
[http://freshmeat.net/projects/frei0r/](http://freshmeat.net/projects/frei0r/)

OME relies on Libquicktime for rendering, so a QuickTime file output that plays fine in Windows is easy. AVIs, with perhaps a generic DV codec, are also options.
