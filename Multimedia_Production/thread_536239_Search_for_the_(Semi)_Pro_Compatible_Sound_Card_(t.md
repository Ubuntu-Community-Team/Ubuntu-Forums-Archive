### Post by nalmeth on 2007-08-27
I honestly don't know of any companies that make their own sound card drivers for linux (except maybe Intel). It is mostly done by the community. I hope someone can say otherwise though

Firewire devices are supported by the FreeBob project (now called FFADO)

But generally, any sound card compatible with ALSA will work with JACK. 

Here is a decent starting point for you, the ALSA Sound Card Matrix:
[http://bugtrack.alsa-project.org/main/index.php/Matrix:Main](http://bugtrack.alsa-project.org/main/index.php/Matrix:Main)

Good Luck Chuck

You might check M-Audio product.
They release drivers standard for Windows and MAC. They usually have a linux driver for most products.
Quality wise. their target audience are musicians/audiophiles, so the sound is 24-bit @, 96KHz. This Audio/MIDI controller board has Windows/MAC/Linux drivers. I think the sound processor used is the VIA Envy-24, but the high end version that supports 8 channel DSP mixing/routing.
[http://www.m-audio.com/products/en_us/Audiophile2496-main.html](http://www.m-audio.com/products/en_us/Audiophile2496-main.html)
They have lower end, less expensive/functional cards also.
