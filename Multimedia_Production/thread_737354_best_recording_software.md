### Post by Stochastic on 2008-03-28
> **markekeller said:**
> Are you talking about audio recording?  I find [Audacity]("http://audacity.sourceforge.net/") to be a first-rate program for that sort of thing.

I personally have had quite poor results with audacity, but it is very beginner-friendly.  I'd recommend Ardour (kinda like pro-tools), Jamin (mastering software),  possibly Rosegarden if you like cubase-style apps, JACK (as a sound driver and a connection app between software), and the LADSPA plugin set for all your effects.  This is a very brief rundown, there's been lots of blogs written on all this - just do a quick google search.  I'd highly recommend the Ubuntu Studio distro as its realtime kernel is quite powerful for audio.
