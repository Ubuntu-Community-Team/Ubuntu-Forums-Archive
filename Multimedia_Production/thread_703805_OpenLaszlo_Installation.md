### Post by Permafrost91 on 2008-03-28
sorry about my lack of response ... my original quest was to find a way to create a flash video player with variable playback speed. I found OpenLaszlo on another forum as a suggestion to replace Adobe Flash. Insofar as my original quest was concerned, OL disappointed.

The application let's you (via your web browser) create websites in either Flash or DHTML. But it is limited to the widgets included in OL and the user interface (IMO) is not all that intuitive at first. It's easy to use once you get used to it though. I think OL is great for beginners but once you have a few years of webdesign under your belt and are fluent in a scripting language (any of the P's), then OL is likely to not meet your needs.

I have long since abandoned OL and have just upgraded to Hardy and in the process have lost the OL installation so unfortunately I cannot provide any more feedback about how I actually got it to work. I do remember having to run the program from the command line and then visiting localhost via a specific port, but it's been too long for me to be more specific.

More luck to all of you!
