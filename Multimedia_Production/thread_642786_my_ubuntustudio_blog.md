### Post by mr.thraz on 2008-01-03
thanks Lagos,
at this point I'll be running through the different apps i use for audio production.
one per week.
[LIST]
[*]Hydrogen2
[*]Rosegarden
[*]Lmms
[*]Ardour 
[*]Audacity
[*]Zynaddsubfx
[/LIST]
not necessarily in that order.

I will then begin to make a song from start to finish using these apps.
it will be at that time that I'll show the mixing and master ability of the jack suite.

there is no one app that can replace a daw like flstudio, cubase or reason.
but working together all these Linux apps can do much more than  any one of the win/mac apps could do alone.

rosegarden and muse are perhaps closest to reason (which is an odd duck even in windows or macintosh) but if you look at them by themselves they will come up short in terms of ease of use and functionality. however once one gets use to linking apps together through jack as needed, you'll find you have very few limitations that would mirror a traditional daw.

Linux daws use LADSPA as plugins  and they have extended functionality through DSSI which can allow the use of vsti's with the help of wine.

all of this will get covered in my blog as time goes on. the hydrogen vid should be up as of the time of this posting.
