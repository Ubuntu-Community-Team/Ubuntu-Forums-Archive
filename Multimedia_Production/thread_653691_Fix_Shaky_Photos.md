### Post by kefurd06 on 2007-12-31
i'll start by saying i have no good news for ya :(

i think what you're seeing on the camera is a low res image that the camera loads quickly, and then after it shows the low-res picture, it loads the whole image and the image looks crispier (this would be the post-processing that you're talking about; i see it on my camera all the time.). this is done so that u can flip thru ur pics quickly when u need to. 

it could be that the screen on the camera is small enough that you don't notice the shakiness nearly as much as u would on a big ol monitor, or printout for that matter. i run into that from time to time.

i know of no image processing software or technology that can clean up a shaky image. that isn't saying much though, i've only dabbled in photo editing as an off and on hobby over the years. 

i know that the slower the shutter speed of the camera, the more likely you are to get a blurred, shaky picture. the shutter speed is slowest in dimly lit areas. you shouldn't have as much of a problem outdoors on a sunny day, but if this is a budget and/or vga camera (640x480 resolution images), they often have slow shutter speeds, even outdoors.

if this is an expensive camera, you should have some options. check the manual. otherwise, you may want to invest in a tripod/camera stand. yes i know that it is an underwater camera. i was attempting to cheer you up with a bit of humor.
