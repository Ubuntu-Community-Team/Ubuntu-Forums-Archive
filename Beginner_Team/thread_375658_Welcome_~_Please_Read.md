### Post by bodhi.zazen on 2007-03-04
_Purpose_ : To welcome new Ubuntu users and enhance their initial experience.

_Scenario_ : A stranger in a strange land ...

Booting to a new OS is often exciting, but navigating system administration is daunting ...

My perspective has been that, more often then not:

1. People often confuse "familiarity" with "ease of use". For example, many new users have always run the OS that was installed for them, they have used it for years, and because of their long standing familiarity accept this OS as the de facto "gold-standard".

Enter a new OS, a new way of doing things and different = difficult. While it is true change is hard, it may be the transition is difficult not the new OS itself (of course there are times when in fact the new OS is the one being difficult, lord knows I've seen that too).


2. "Normal" people use their computers as tools and expect (IMO unrealistically) that a new OS will "just work". The unrealistic expectation here being that there will be little or no learning curve with a new OS and/or a new OS is just like the old OS, including running program X.


3. Barring any "special needs" for specific hardware/software, most people are able to transition into Ubuntu as a primary OS. Guidance through this transition period is invaluable to new users and more often then not they feel like "giving something back", and thus the Ubuntu forums. 


_The goals of the Beginning Team is to assist new users to _:

[LIST=1]
[*]Evaluate Ubuntu to see if it will fit their needs.
[*]Obtain and boot Ubuntu.
[*]Install Ubuntu.
[*]Transition into Ubuntu.
[*]Become members of the Ubuntu community.
[/LIST]


It should go without saying, just as every Operating System (OS) has advantages and disadvantages, every user has a unique perspective and set of computing needs. It is thus unreasonable to promote Ubuntu, or any other OS, as meeting all the needs of all users all the time.

The "round hole, square peg" scenario needs to be avoided. If there is a poor match between Ubuntu (as an OS) and a new users needs/expectations the best response is to direct them to a better fitting OS.

[SIZE=2]**[COLOR=darkred]This is NOT the proper forum to BASH Operating Systems.[/COLOR]**[/SIZE]


_Joining the team_ :

Well, to some extent, if you are active on the forums you are a part of the team.

Characteristics I think will be helpful to Members of this Beginner Team :

[LIST]
[*]A sympathetic approach to new users.
[*]The maturity to help new users without setting unrealistic expectations of Ubuntu or bashing other OS.
[*]I would think both a background in education/teaching or experience with multiple OS would be invaluable.
[/LIST]

I am in receipt of a long list of requests to join the team. Thank all of you for your interest I will start to work through the list ...

Traffic on the IRC has started to pick up. Feel free to come on over and meet the team :) Ideas/suggestions welcome.
