### Post by ferg on 2007-03-14
When I migrated to Ubuntu September/October last year it would have been great to have some kind of basic beginner's guide - I've often thought that a small PDF/other file that contained some basic guides to get you going in Ubuntu; such as setting up printers, networking, basic customisation etc; would be very helpful. This file, or a link to it if it was a 'live' online guide, could be placed on the desktop. The first thing new users will see is this 'Basic Guide' - it's all about making the process of switching more intuitive. It was only until about a month after I installed Ubuntu I really got into reading articles on these forums and the Ubuntu Wiki. Simply, I enivisage that many users just don't know these resources exist for them. This 'Basic Guide' could also incorporate links to more detailed articles in the documentation/wiki/forums so that new users feel they have an adequate pool of information to guide them. The major stumbling block for many users migrating from Windows is that they can't find the support they need in order to progress with the switch - they hit a problem and think "That didn't happen in Windows - who do I ask for help?". That's not to say that it isn't there, but as a community we need to make sure that new users are aware of the systems of help and support in place. Personally I didn't really have much trouble, I've been using Linux on and off for years, so there wasn't really a transition period as such for me. There were questions I still wanted to ask, particularly about starting out in Ubuntu.

I'm very interested in what the Beginner Team has to offer to Ubuntu and I think it's a great idea that we try to help new users as much as possible. :) That's my ten cents... I think once you get into the forums and the wiki and discover the masses of really helpful people you can solve any problems pretty swiftly and easily. 

Fergus
