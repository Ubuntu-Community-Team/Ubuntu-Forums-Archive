### Post by Rhubarb on 2008-01-24
Ok, there are two ways to solve your problem here.

The first and easiest way is to go out and buy a broadband router modem that's suitable for your connection.  It must have a network port on the back of it (and similarly you must have a network port on the back of your PC - most do).
Configure up the router modem for your line so it works in windows (if it works in windows, it'll work in ubuntu).  If you're unsure about this, ask a friend who is a bit tech savvy to help you out.

The second way allows you to keep your existing speedtouch modem, but does involve a lot of time and probably a lot of frustration if you're not familiar with the linux command line:
[http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-391404.html](http://ubuntuforums.org/archive/index.php/t-391404.html)
[http://www.linux-usb.org/SpeedTouch/ubuntu/index.html](http://www.linux-usb.org/SpeedTouch/ubuntu/index.html)
