### Post by arvevans on 2008-08-09
New words are part of the learning process.  Learning a new OS usually involves learning the terminology that goes with that system, even if that OS is MS-Windows.  You had to learn some new words when you first started using computers, why stop at that point and refuse any further enhancement of your knowledge and skill?

The alternative to learning and improving one's knowledge would seem to be the ostrich approach (stick your head in the sand and pretend that the danger has gone away because now you cannot see or hear it).  This approach makes one look a little ludicrous with your posterior sticking out and your head protected.  :lolflag:

Rather than "*not using certain technology terms*" I would suggest building and using an on-line reference for learning the technical terminology involved with computing and the use of computers as everyday tools in our lives.

I do agree though with the avoidance of certain obscene or culturally objectionable words and abbreviations.  When I see these in a post I usually click on "Next Thread" because this use of profanity usually indicates a person with limited intelligence and he/she probably would not understand any help we might try to give them.
