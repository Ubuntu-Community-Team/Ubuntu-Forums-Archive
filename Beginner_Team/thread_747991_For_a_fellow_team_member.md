______________________________________________________________________
Ubuntu crew,

Sorry its been such a long time- I havent forgot about all of you guys
back there :). Its been stupid busy here; we have at least 5-10

get to the questions:

LeAstrale:

really have a chance to eat. If I do, someones pissed that this didn't
get done, or that that was left unfinished.
2) No. I hate it out here.. My computer is literally the only 'thing' I
enjoy out here. I like troubleshooting electronics, but not all the bs
paperwork and stuff that goes with it.
3) Ill get into this later.. Don't worry- its good news.
4) Im an aircraft electronics tech- I fix most of the flight control
electronics in the fighters and   helicopters out here.
5) Yeah, weve had some good talks. I miss dealing with intelligent and
generally open-minded people. It takes being away to realize what you
take for granted, including people.

Ajmorris:
1) Eh.. Im dealing with it. I guess its not all bad. Im making over 4k a
month right now and now I only owe $900. So if nothing else, im getting
out of debt! Seen Singapore.. That was awesome.
2) Heh.. I had some wine..er.. Nvidia/xorg problems. Heh.. Make sure if
you have an Nvidia card that you do some research. Youll see why later
in the email.
Oh and hey- I cant give specifics, but its entirely possible that I
might be visiting the country you live in.

Joeb454:
Yeah, we havent talked a whole lot, but they were good times
nonetheless. Some positive things, which I generally have a hard time
acknowledging come from being out here..

Maddeth:
Dude- you have no idea how bad I miss my bike. As soon as I get back
home, im riding my bike to Las Vegas.. That's one of those objects that
seriously becomes a drug- once you ride, youll always be a rider.

Bodhi_Zazen:
Im being safe out here, although anything can happen. I wanted to tell
you: the guide you made on booting multiple distros has helped me,
literally, at least 5 times between my own computer and others..

Rocket2DMn:
Hows it going man? Just another day here.. Miss all of you as well. Ill
be back, trust me! Don't really have anything specific to say either..
Pretty boring here atm.. Converting people to FOSS? Heh.. Later in the
email.

Alan_M:
Heh.. YOU the annoying one? Im the *******, remember? Youre just another
one of the gang ;) At least weve got a good crew on a good cause, eh?

Yappy:
I will be safe while doing THEIR duty- my duties and agendas are
different than theirs. That will make sense once you get to know me
better. Some of the other guys might tell you what I mean.
Im in AIMD, which basically fixes the avionics coming out of aircraft.

Tronyx/ Andy: I appreciate the effort put forth in coordinating this
(LeAstrale for the idea). I know it's a small thing.. But its good to
keep in touch with a community that shares interests...

A common question im sure you all have on your mind: How many people has
GSF (Jason, btw) gotten to use Linux/FOSS? Well, let me just say there
are currently 7 laptops in my shop, and only 1 is running windows! I
really didn't push anything.. It just happened this way. Many of the
guys liked the fact that I had DVDs with tons of software (repo DVD's)
and gave Linux a try because of it. Now, they all like the OS a lot,
mostly siting how insanely customizable it is. Sorry, but theyre all
using KDE ;) 2 have already completely gotten rid of windows- I stopped
dual booting as well (all nix now). Its funny- im getting contacted by a
lot of random people asking me Linux oriented questions, most of them
basic, but still. Johnson (a friend who I converted a while ago) and I
have become like the linux guru's of the ship, at least in terms of
having software, install cds, and knowledge of video/audio/wine
configuration, etc. We collectively have nearly every piece of software
needed to make a complete nix install (dvds, etc). If im not working or
sleeping, im helping others (and myself, below) setup nix.
Unfortunately, I don't have pSX anymore since I had to reinstall.. As
the story goes below..

So, when a convert decided to buy the 8.04 install cd and repo set, I
couldn't resist. I had to apt-get dist-upgrade. The upgrade itself went
fine, and I had a working install, but an Nvidia/xorg issue made me
think something had gotten messed up. I reinstalled 8.04 after backing
up everything (including my .wine and .cedega folders), and set up the
video drivers only to find I had the same issue with all my games. For
some reason, with the latest xorg, kernel, and video drivers, all games
not running in my native 1680x1050 resolution got the bottom qaurter of
the screen cutoff. Damn.

I decided to be crafty. I copied the DVD's to my external terabyte
drive, and edited the sources.list to recongnize it- no more disc
swapping. I installed kubuntu 8.04 with a separate / and /home, then
edited my sources.list to use the 7.10 repos, also stored on my
external. Updated apt, installed the 7.10 kernel, headers, restricted,
etc. I then booted to that kernel, removed kubuntu-desktop (just the
metapackage) so that when I attempted to remove xorg, it wouldn't delete
my entire KDE packages (it did that before). I then installed 7.10's
xorg and nvidia driver. Basically, I have 7.10's kernel, xorg,
restricted (wireless), and nvidia drivers, with everything else 8.04.
Now my games work without the bottom being cutoff. Strange thing is.. I
havent played my games really at all since I got here- I think it was
just the challenge. Ive since updated the repos to 8.04, so anything new
I install is from its repos instead of 7.10. Works awesome, except that
it always says I have 45 upgradeable packages ;)

Well, this is a novel, so I better close it and get off the computer..
Take it easy guys- it may take a while, but ill write back!

GSF1200S/Jason
