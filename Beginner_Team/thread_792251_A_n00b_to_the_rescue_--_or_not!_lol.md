I also think a glossary of abbreviations and terms wouldn't hurt. Couldn't seem to find one in the forum itself, and I know from experience most people won't try to look elsewhere because, to a newbie, Ubuntu is a complete OS in itself. The relationship with linux is not necessarily quite clear and can be confusing

So I'm available to compile a beginner's glossary (not a complete 20,000-word version) if you think it might be helpful.

As a non-techie semi-geek, the only piece of advice I can give to help newcomers feel at home is this:

- Create a document consultation tree in the Absolute Beginners section, starting with the basics. Too often, searches lead to too many posts, most of which are not titled clearly enough to appear relevant to the inexperienced user.
- Create awareness with experienced users that they need to explain WHY this or that command line should be used (I'm personally wary of entering anything I don't UNDERSTAND in my terminal)
- Remind experienced forum posters that there is a steep learning curve, and that most newbies only know Windows, a GRAPHICAL user interface. They might benefit from starting there instead of jumping straight into a literal sea of command lines. Understanding what you're doing comes before aesthetics, speed and customization.

And remember -- concepts that may appear quite simple to experienced users are brand new to newbies. Try to remember how lost you all probably were the first time you logged in to a linux system...

That's all I can think of for now!
