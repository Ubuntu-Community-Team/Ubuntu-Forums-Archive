### Post by wog on 2007-04-24
Adding into the issue of beta software, some users upgrade looking for a resolution for bugs. 

For instance, I was having trouble with BitTornado, and there was a new version which fixed the issue, but it simply wasn't available for install through apt-get until I upgraded to Edgy. 

Still another problem with upgrading is in the  difference between Linux and Windows cultures. Under Windows, if an update comes out, no matter what it is, you download it because (on the positive side) you are assured by M$ that your system will work better if you load it, or (at the negative) you are assured that if you *don't* update, your system will no longer work.

In Linux, all upgrades are optional, so you get to choose, yet someone moving over from the Windows culture upgrades as soon as something new presents itself because of that previous understanding of when and why to upgrade!

Someone earlier mentioned people venturing into Linux territory alone are adventurous souls, but I'd add they sometimes have no other alternative. I am surrounded by Windows experts, but no-one near me is expert at Linux. Either I experiment with Linux alone or I do not experiment with Linux, period. Almost everything I've learned about Linux and everything I've learned about Ubuntu I've learned from these forums.
