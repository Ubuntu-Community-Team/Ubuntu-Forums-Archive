### Post by stream303 on 2008-02-11
Just something I have had cooking in my head since the dapper days about ppc hardware support:

Why doesn't Ubuntu/Canonical release Alphas that have automated file collection and testing routines that report the results back from users/testers to them automatically?

It would go something like this - you have a PPC machine you are willing to load an automated test release on solely for that purpose during the Alpha stages.

Shell scripts could be created and loaded by Canonical to test the effectiveness of their proposed changes, hardware detection etc, and send the results back to a Canonical server.

We're not talking about them ssh'ing into the boxes themselves - perhaps just a cron job that the machine performs and sends results back so that way you are in charge.

Certainly they don't have access to the wide variety of hardware we have out in the field.  Why not take advantage of it remotely somehow?
