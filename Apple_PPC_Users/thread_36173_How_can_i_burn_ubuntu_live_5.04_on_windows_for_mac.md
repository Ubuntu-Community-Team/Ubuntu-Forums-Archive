### Post by dekoi on 2005-05-22
Since an .iso is an .iso is an .iso, it shouldn't matter on what platform you make the ppc-cd. My best guess is a problem with the CD, which may be related to the following:

1. You burned a copy of the .iso file, not burned the .iso to a bootable CD.

2. You burned the .iso correctly, but the md5 sum is different than what Ubuntu lists, and you need to re-download the .iso.

3. You used a CD-RW, not a CD-R to make the .iso, and the G3 can't read the CD-RW.

4. All the above are fine, but the CD-R was just a bad disk.

Keep us posted if that was the problem, and best of luck.
