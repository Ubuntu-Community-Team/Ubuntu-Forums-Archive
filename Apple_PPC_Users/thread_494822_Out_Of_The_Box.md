### Post by stmiller on 2007-07-07
Any of the iBooks/Powerbooks. Sleep only works on notebooks with ATI graphics chip.

Airport extreme works, but you have to install firmware which is easy as 

apt-get install bcm.....  (See the PowerPCFAQs)

Airport (original) works.

My (Early 2005) Powermac G5 works fine, with no issues also.

PowerPC is really good now. Wireless, sleep, sound, any and all multimedia codecs, etc. all work. There is no Adobe Flash, but Gnash works for Youtube and is Flash 7 compatible.

3D drivers are buggy, as ATI nor Nvidia have developed PowerPC drivers.

### Post by joninkrakow on 2007-07-10
Don't know if I could say it's "out of the box" but my Pismo (with a Daystar G4 chip) has required _very_ little tweaking. I had to run the dconfig to get my monitor to 1024x756, but that was beans. Also, I needed to double-check my audio-in settings, but I realized my mistake there. (the Ubuntu recording app defaults to "capture") and once I realized to change that _before_ I do anything, I was ok). I haven't yet tried my wifi PC card because I haven't been anywhere to use it, but it's the card from the original Airport Base Station, so it should work.

personally, I'm very impressed with Ubuntu out of the box compared to other distrobutions like Yellow Dog (supposedly specializes on PowerMacs) and OpenSUSE, both of which came highly regarded for Macs. Ubuntu, in comparison to them is just about genuine turn-key--at least on my Pismo.
