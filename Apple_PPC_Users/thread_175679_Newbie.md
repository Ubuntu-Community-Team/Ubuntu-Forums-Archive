### Post by louis_nichols on 2006-05-13
First of all: Wellcome tpo the quite wonderful world of Ubuntu :)

Second: your first lesson about Linux is that it has no viruses, spyware, malware and all that crap. Ubuntu doesn't even need you to configure a firewall, since it has no listening daemons started by default. :D

About the printer... that could be a little tricky, but not necessarily. Plug it in, go under System>Administration>Printing, double click on new printer and follow the instructions. It will most likely be recognized, but might not have  a driver. If your exact model isn't in the list with Epson drivers, try some drivers that are there of similar Epson models. Quite frequently, this is a good solution.

If you can't get it working, I suggest you visit [http://www.linuxprinting.org](http://www.linuxprinting.org) They have good advice and support forums. Your solution might already be there, or you can ask on the forums. As always, tho, google is also a good friend.

### Post by louis_nichols on 2006-05-13
Open the kde control center, go to "KDE Components>Component Chooser". You will have a list to the right. Choose "web browser" there, then check the option "in the following browser" and in the text field enter *firefox*
