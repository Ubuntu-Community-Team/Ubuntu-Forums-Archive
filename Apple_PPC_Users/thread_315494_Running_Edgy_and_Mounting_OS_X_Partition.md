### Post by stream303 on 2006-12-09
Don't discount the community support.  Debian, from which Ubuntu is based upon, is solely community supported.  There are other community-based distros.

There has been a lot of talk, meetings, etc about this but I'm going to wait and see how it really pans out.  Until then, Edgy rocks on my G5 - for my purposes.

If/when the time comes to switch, you'll know.  Let's put it this way - the time you spend with Ubuntu won't be wasted whether you run Dapper or Edgy.  Instead of switching, another alternative is to be active in *multiple* communities.  Perhaps set up a dual-boot situation later on.

As for OSX mounting, yes you can do it although I don't have experience with it.  There are some good threads about it here, although you have to take into consideration the stability of the current read-write situation of HFS+, user-id's, permissions etc.
