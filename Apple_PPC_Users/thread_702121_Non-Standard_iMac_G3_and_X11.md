### Post by stream303 on 2008-02-21
I took a look at that monitor's specs at:

[http://www.monitorworld.com/Monitors/compaq/additional/qsmv940.pdf](http://www.monitorworld.com/Monitors/compaq/additional/qsmv940.pdf)

and would be tempted to change your xorg.conf manually to this:  (caveat! It's not my monitor, so be careful!)

```
Section "Monitor"
	Identifier	"iMac"
	Option		"DPMS"
	#HorizSync	60-60
	** HorizSync      31-85**
         #VertRefresh	75-117
        ** VertRefresh     50-75**
EndSection

Section "Screen"
	Identifier	"Default Screen"
	Device		"ATI Technologies Inc Rage 128 Pro Ultra TR"
	Monitor		"iMac"
	DefaultDepth	24
	SubSection "Display"
		Modes		**"1280x1024"** "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
	EndSubSection
EndSection
```

1280x1024 is listed as the max flicker-free resolution, whereas 1600x1200 is just listed as the max, but might be flicker-prone.  Maybe look at the docs and try some of these out...

Whatever you do, make sure you backup your xorg.conf file, say to xorg.conf.original in case you ever need to go into rescue mode and copy it back as xorg.conf
