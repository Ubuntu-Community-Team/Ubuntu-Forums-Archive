### Post by stmiller on 2007-05-04
Put in the CD, then hold down 'C' to boot from a CD. If that doesn't seem to work, you can hold down the 'Alt' key on Macs to get to a boot menu, then choose the CD.

### Post by SectionThree on 2007-05-05
I know from experience that you'll need to buy third-party software to burn an ISO image from OS 9 (such as Toast), but finding it might be a problem. A quick google search for OS X 10.2 (which will definitely run on your new machine) yielded many results. Dual-booting that with Ubuntu will give you the best of both worlds, or at least, Disc Copy, which comes with OS X, will burn your ISO for you :)

Also, you might want to consider upgrading the memory. I have my old blue slot-loading iMac maxed out at 256 MB and yours, with its RW drive (mine's read-only), sounds like a later model than mine. eBay's a good place to look.

Installing memory is pretty easy, you'll find the specs with a quick search on the [URL="http://support.apple.com/specs/"]
apple site[/URL].
