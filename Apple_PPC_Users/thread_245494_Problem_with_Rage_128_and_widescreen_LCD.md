### Post by crimson on 2006-08-27
I've been trying to get a Sony MFM-HT75W 17" widescreen LCD ( 1280x768 ) panel to work with a Mac G4 Cube, which has an ATI Rage 128 video card. I installed from a live CD and added the 1280x768 video mode, when it wasn't auto-detected, and the thing was working yesterday. I changed nothing in the configuration, just swapped in another (regular 17" LCD) screen to compare and then switched back and now I can't seem to get X to come up in any useful mode -- when using the ADC port (with an ADC-to-DVI adapter). Connecting via analog VGA, everything is fine, but the picture quality is not as good. This thing was beautiful via DVI (during my brief encounter yesterday when it was working), and I must have that back! :)

Anyway, yeah, I'm stumped. I'm also confused as to why the initial mode that Ubuntu boots up into (and the mode the non-X terminal screens run in) is 1280x768 @ _50 Hz_ (according to the screen's hardware menu display). But at least that works. But when X starts, the monitor says there's no signal and shuts off.

If I use video=r128:1024x768 to tell X to stick to 1024x768, the X display comes up, but there are two vertical bars that are shuffled around on the display, and a section repeated. In other words, not very useful. (This is how the live CD booted up as well, BTW, when I was running via DVI.)

I ran through reconfiguring with "dpkg-reconfigure xserver-xorg" after reading through many posts here, but no change. I'm quite baffled as to what could have changed, and why things aren't working now.
