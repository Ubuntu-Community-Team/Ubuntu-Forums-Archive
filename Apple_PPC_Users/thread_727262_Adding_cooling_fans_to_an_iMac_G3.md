### Post by ristosu on 2008-03-18
Interesting idea. My experience with this iMac DV is that the air, coming from the top, is as warm, as it is on any CRT. Then there is the bottom part where the processor is located. The problem is that there seems to be no route for the air to come up. On the other hand, the G3 is quite cool compared with its computing power. ### Post by markosjal on 2008-06-27
You can use a 5 or 12 volt fan. Most probably the 12 Volt fans are easier to find. 

Take the power from the hard disk power cable, where there is 5 and 12 volts available. . In fact you can buy a PC fan with this type of connector, and the power connector to the Mac hard disk is the same as a PC. I would however recommend a very long cable as the best place in my opinion to put the fan is at the TOP of the cabinet exhausting air out of the cabinet, but the Disk drive is at the bottom. 
I think the BEST way, with the least amount of case modification is to take two 5 Volt mini fans and place them under the vent holes just in front of the handle (where there is the most heat, as hot air rises)
Have a look here see if they have 1 inch fans 
[http://www.frozencpu.com/cat/l2/g36/c231/list/p1/Fans-5_Volt_Fans.html](http://www.frozencpu.com/cat/l2/g36/c231/list/p1/Fans-5_Volt_Fans.html)
Just buy a PC Disk Drive Y cable and use the spare connection to power the two fans.


On an additional note while you are there , you can install some white LEDs to illuminate the case from the inside. It is a 21st century look to your old iMac.
