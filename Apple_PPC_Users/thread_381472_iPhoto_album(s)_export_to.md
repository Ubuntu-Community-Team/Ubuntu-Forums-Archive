### Post by TDave on 2007-03-11
I haven't used iPhoto for a good while, but if memory serves me correctly, the export feature basically exports a directory of all the photos, placed in directories according to date (ie. 2006 > 05 > 27) similar to how they get organised in the Pictures area of the OS X User directory.
If that's correct, then my personal preference on the best way to handle them is to import them into F-Spot, which will manage them in a similar manner directory-wise, but relies on your tags to organise them, rather than the individual albums you're used to.

My experience with Digikam, whilst positive, seemed to lend weight to the 'each directory is an album' philosophy, meaning looking through the whole library was difficult.

To the best of my knowledge there isn't (yet) a photo app that will seamlessly handle a full iPhoto import with custom albums organised and immediately ready for use, but I could be wrong.

Personally, I ran with a whole mix-match of ideas for a while, starting by not using any photo manager in linux and manually organising everything into it's own folders, to using Digikam as a front-end to this manual management, until finally running F-Spot (I avoided it for a long time because I didn't like the way it stored the files on the hard drive, and a lot of my older photos had the wrong dates in the EXIF information, so they appeared in the wrong places on F-Spots timescale). I have simply abandoned the old, incorrectly dated photos to the manual filesystem out of the way and use F-Spot for all my latest photos, and love it.
If your photos are date and time-tagged correctly, I would suggest giving F-Spot a try, but if not then maybe Digikam would serve your needs better. But, as for a completely seamless approach as mentioned above, I think you may struggle.

LPhoto ([http://www.lphoto.com](http://www.lphoto.com)) may also be worth a look, although I have no experience with this.

Hope this helps and isn't too rambling!


EDIT: Provided the Exif tags are in order date-wise, adding new tags (and reorganising things) should be fairly quick and painless... although it does sound like you have rather a lot to do!
