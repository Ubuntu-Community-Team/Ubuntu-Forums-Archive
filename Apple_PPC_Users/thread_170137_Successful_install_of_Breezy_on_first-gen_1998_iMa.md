The client's awaiting wifi in her apartment, so she hasn't had a chance to test it at home yet, but we're crossing our fingers. KDE easily connected to my Canon G3 camera, K3B instantly detected an Iomega USB CD burner and successfully burned an audio CD, and GIMP, Inkscape, Firefox and mplayer are all happily running on a box which a week before was running OS X 10.1. That's right, the OS before Safari.

This wouldn't have been feasible if the previous owner hadn't tricked out the box with a faster 500MHz CPU, larger HD and 512Mb of memory, but since they did, it was a viable candidate for installing Linux. I'm debating dusting off the 330Mhz G4/1G RAM/37Gb HD PowerMac 7500 and seeing if it's worthwhile to put Breezy on an OldWorld 1995 Mac with first-gen Open Firmware, no video card and USB as a PCI aftermarket option.
