### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-08-11
256MB of ram, is really not to small an amount to cause problems.  The main difference you will notice between having 256MB and 512+MB is the speed of the system when you have more than one application open.  Firefox alone uses over 40MB of ram when running with only one tab.  I would suggest purchasing as much memory as you are willing to spend on the iBook.  The iBook you are considering, I believe supports up to 1.25gigs of ram.  

To help make the most out of web media, I use two Firefox plug-ins.  [MediaPlayerConnectivity]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446") and [VideoDownloader.]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/2390")  MediaPlayerconnectivity allows you to open web videos in your preferred video player.  I like to use VLC to play videos on my iBook.  VLC will play just about any video codec out there and is a godsend on the PowerPC.  You can also use Democracy Player (now known as Miro) to search, download and watch videos from YouTube. 
```

sudo apt-get install democracyplayer
```

Wireless on the iBooks also tends to work very well.  My iBook has a Broadcom BCM4318 wireless card and it works surprising well.  Last weekend I listened to Virgin Radio for over 4 hours straight on my iBook and it never disconnected from my network.  I am using my iBook on a 128bit WEP wireless network.

As Auria stated, please read the [PowerPC FAQ ]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCFAQ").  You can find answers to many of the questions that you may have concerning Linux on PowerPC there. 

And if you were wondering what desktop environment I use on my iBook G4,  I use Gnome exclusively.  I actually feel that Gnome is faster on my iBook then OS X 10.4.
