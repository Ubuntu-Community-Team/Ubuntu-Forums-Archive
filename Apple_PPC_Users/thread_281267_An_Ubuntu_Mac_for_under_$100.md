### Post by jaradronald on 2006-10-20
I paid $125 for a PowerBook G3 last week, and it's running Ubuntu 5.10 beautifully.  The specs are below:

PPC G3 - 266 Mhz
128 MB RAM
10 GB Hard Drive
Ethernet

When looking for an old Mac you need to keep in mind whether it's an "Old World" or "New World" Powerbook.  Mine is an "Old World Wallstreet" whatever that means...  :-k  It was $125 on craigslist.com and it's my new toy...  I'll probably give it to my sister as an early Christmas gift.

Personally I'd say if you're looking for an old laptop to put Linux on, go with a PC.  They are EVERYWHERE and the x86 repositories are SO MUCH richer than the PPC ones (no diss to those working HARD to compile from source for everybody).  Using a PC is nice too because WINE :KS  (a Windows Emulator [I SAID IT!]) comes pre-compiled, and you can run your favorite Windows 95/98 software piece-o-cake.

Just try to make sure you have AT LEAST 128 MBs of RAM (RAM is so cheap, u should max out whatever old system you buy when you get the chance).  And a BASIC Ubuntu install is going to take at least 2 GBs (not to mention you'll need space for your swap partition).  Unless you're going to carve out a basic "server" install without the Gnome GUI and then use apt-get to load some X GUI and the rest of ur software, you're going to need at least 4 GBs to play with on the hard drive (AT LEAST!).  Hard drives again are freakin' cheap, so just find a bigger one if you have to.  ;-)
