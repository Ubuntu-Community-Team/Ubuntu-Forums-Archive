### Post by handylinux on 2007-01-01
The 3400 was an excellent laptop in its time -- ahead of its time, as I recall. I had one as my primary computer for about a year and a half (replaced with the almost-identical original G3, aka '3500').

You can learn more about the 3400 at [Low End Mac]("http://www.lowendmac.com/pb2/3400.shtml"), which is also the prime [general resource]("http://www.lowendmac.com") for users of older Macs.

You don't say what speed your 3400 is, or how much RAM or HD you have. It'll run anything from Mac OS 7.6.1 to 9.1. 7.6.1 is a very nice "classic Mac" experience; there's even a whole [website]("http://main.system7today.com") devoted to it.

LEM's [PowerBooks]("http://www.lowendmac.com/lists/powerbooks.shtml") and [Swap]("http://www.lowendmac.com/lists/swap.html") email lists would be good places to get help and software for your 3400.
