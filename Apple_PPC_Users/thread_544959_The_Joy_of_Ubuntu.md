### Post by jso2897 on 2007-09-07
A neighbor of mine gave me an old Tangerine Imac. It was mechanically OK, but it had MacOS (or, as I like to call it "Babys First Operating System") on it, and was, of course, useless. So I got Xubuntu loaded on it, and it was OK - for a thin OS, Xubuntu is good - but it's pretty feature weak next to Fiesty Ubuntu. At that point, however, I was able to get a good look at the hardware, and discovered that the previous owner had upgraded to RAM to 256mb. So I went to Ubuntu Fiesty. I just finished to install, it went flawlessly, and the old doorstop is now an actual computer that will be useful to somebody.
Ha,HA, Steve! You can take your planned obsolescence and shove it up your Cupertino!
:lolflag:

I did, however make an interesting discovery. Evidently, at least in it's PPC iteration, Xubuntu apparently creates a lot of superfluous descriptions in the network interface file, and when I upgraded to Ubuntu, I had to Gedit etc/network/interface to remove all but the basic loopback description. Odd.
