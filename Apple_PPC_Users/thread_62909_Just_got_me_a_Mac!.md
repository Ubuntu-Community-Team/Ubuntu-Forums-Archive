### Post by lubod on 2005-09-18
Ubuntu, or any Linux on any CPU, could very possibly NOT want to play with a lot of ATI graphics cards, and this is ATI's fault for only releasing closed source binary drivers tied to particular kernel versions, infrequently updated, and often buggy/lacking the features of their windows or OS X counterparts.

In general most linux advocates prefer Nvidia - their drivers are largely (entirely?) closed source too, but seem better supported, more up to date, less buggy.

Worth a try if you can return/exchange the ATI card or reuse it in another configuration, but don't be too surprised if it won't work optimally in Linux. It should work ok in OS X as ATI makes/updates those drivers more often and most OS X releases have them built in.

CPU upgrade should be relatively trouble free in any OS.

While I enjoy Ubuntu (I'm not a troll so I wouldn't be here if I didn't) if you get the CPU upgrade, and the video card is at least a Radeon or equivalent, see if you can get OS X 10.4 it is seriously impressive. You may find you want a dual boot 10.4 and Ubuntu config. or even OS X only. :-)
