### Post by VidiotGeek on 2008-01-13
I think you should have a little boot loader prompt that comes up shortly after turning the computer on that lists 3 options. Mine gives me these: For Mac OS type "X" for Ubuntu Linux type "L" & for CDROM type "C". Make your selection & press enter. If that doesn't work, & you happen to have ANOTHER Mac at your disposal, then you could try booting in Target Disk mode on your machine & then booting the other Mac from the CD-ROM to install Mac OS X on your G4's internal HD. My guess is though that if it isn't responding to your boot key combos, you probably won't be able to get it in target disk mode anyway. Look for that prompt when you first boot & see if that helps.
