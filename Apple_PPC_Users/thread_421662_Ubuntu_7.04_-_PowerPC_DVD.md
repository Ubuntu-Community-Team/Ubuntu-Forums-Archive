### Post by magicfab on 2007-04-25
Mr. Tener got in touch with me today at the Canonical Global Support Services center. 

At this point we don't have any free resources to dedicate to producing a free DVD ISO release for PPC. Is anyone in the community able to do so, or provide a procedure to do it, for free or for a fee ? I proposed finding out if we could do that, but it would make more sense to see this coming from the community. If we did provide such an ISO it would create expectations about supporting it which we are not doing at the time being for Ubuntu 7.04.

Just as a reminder this has nothing to do with existing or future support of PowerPC from Canonical as a company. Existing Ubuntu PowerPC releases will continue to be maintained for the duration of their supported life cycles, including Ubuntu 6.06 LTS which will be supported on PowerPC servers until 2011.

The official position of Canonical as a company has been explained in several public places, and although I am not the most knowledgeable person about PowerPPC at Canonical I wanted to provide a few resources at hand to help get context and understand / explain this decision.

Official announcement:
[https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-announce/2007-February/000098.html](https://lists.ubuntu.com/archives/ubuntu-announce/2007-February/000098.html)

Detailed review that lead to that decision:
[https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCReview](https://wiki.ubuntu.com/PowerPCReview)

PowerPC users and developers:
[https://launchpad.net/~ubuntu-powerpc](https://launchpad.net/~ubuntu-powerpc)

If anyone has further questions I'll answer them here publically, I'll subscribe to this thread.

Cheers,
