### Post by cobelloy on 2005-10-01
A few months ago on a whim I bid on one of those cute blue iMacs on eBay for my daughter, the same kind we had at university in the computer labs back in '98 (or there abouts). I had remembered they were expensive computers compared to some PCs and lacking floppy drives too, but I had thought they were real performers. Sadly although my brand-new eBay iMac came with fresh installs of both os9 and osX neither were much use, it being a 350mHz g3 (well looked after, with upgraded memory).

osX was slow at everything

os9 had no applications left to be found free on the internet that I could use

So my daughter had to watch jumpy avi movies that crashed alot (too bad, not much I can do without spending up big on a 1gb upgrade to the ram)

Anyway, the shipit people sent me out a ppc version and I played around with that all weekend and got vlc on it plus some codecs and WOW!! all her CD movies play brilliantly, and on the smaller screen the small movie files (350-700MB) seem quite good quality.

So Ubuntu/Gnome definately outperforms osX and os9, smooth scrolling, fast boot up (at least as fast as osX anyway), copies files much quicker, opens app's quickly etc.

But I also need to point out that the first computer I used Ubuntu on was my trusty 400mhz celeron, which even with every tweak and the most minimal desktop (not much of a console user) it still had performance issues (compared to Win98 for example). So I am impressed to say that UbuntuPPC/Gnome on Mac also outperforms PC's of the same era!

No wonder Mac lovers are so hardcore about their computers!

And a pox on Apple for discontinuing all compatibility for os9 so fast!!

I just wanted to add this in here since I wasn't sure if I wanted to go with Ubuntu PPC because if you look at the forums it looks like ***everyone*** has a problem of some sort that is driving them crazy, but in fact this install on the little iMac was easier and faster than the installs I have done on my old celeron PC and my newer P4. At the end of the installation the screen was perfect (resolution etc), sound was on, and even the internal modem was up and running perfectly!!

I guess once it all works there's just no need to get back on the forum!
