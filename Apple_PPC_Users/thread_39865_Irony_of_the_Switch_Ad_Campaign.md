### Post by dkitty on 2005-06-06
My first reaction to [this news](http://www.apple.com/pr/library/2005/jun/06intel.html) was... "WHAT!?!?" Then I thought about it. I had hoped that all the Rhapsody rumors would lead to a version of the Mac OS that would have a built-in windows emulation on a Mac and built-in Mac emulation on a windows box... an operating system that could span processing architectures. The Mac OS could be ported to machines with Intel architecture. Applications could slip back and forth from Mac to Windows with minimal developmental effort. I never dreamed that it might lead to the end of the PowerPC processor, a technology that has been leaps and bounds ahead of Intel's drek for years.

I'm sad. It all makes sense in a business sort of way. But this is end of an era sort of stuff. You know? I suppose this happens to everyone eventually (TRS-80s, commadore64, etc.) but the computer of my childhood, the PPC Macintosh as I know it, has just gone the way of the dinosaur.

Terra Soft has pledged to continue development of Linux for the PowerPC architecture. If Apple drops support of the PowerPC architecture, which I imagine will happen, then Yellow Dog Linux will be a popular product for older iMacs, Xserves, and the like.

Will other Linux distro teams continue PPC development? Will Gentoo? Will Debian? Will Ubuntu?

i686, here I reluctantly come...
