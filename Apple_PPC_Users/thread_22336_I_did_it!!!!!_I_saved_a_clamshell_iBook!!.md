### Post by poofyhairguy on 2005-03-27
YES! I did it! I got Hoary installed on an original 300mhz "clamshell" ibook. This has been a big project for me. The laptop is SOO pretty, but OS9 sucks (I dislike OSX but not as much, not nearly). But now its happily running Ubuntu. I hope to do a BIG writeup on my steps (I'm working to make a website for it) but here are some quick notes:

I bought an airport card and it worked out on the box with Ubuntu. The extra 256 mbs of RAM I added made this thing run better than my x86 laptop with 100 more mhz but only 128mb ram. I originally installed Kubuntu because I thought KDE was less demanding (its faster on my other laptop) but it ran like a DOG on the clamshell. Very slow!!! I guess Gnome needs more RAM and KDE needs more horsepower. Oh well, Gnome is prettier anyway....

both kubuntu and Ubuntu installed as easily as on my x86 boxes. Synaptic tells me that the PPC edition has just as many packages in the Universe as the x86 version has! Sure some things can't be found (window media player codecs and a real flash player), but I will use my desktop for playing media files and I HATE flash in general. Its mostly used for ads and sites that aren't important...No offense to those that like/use flash but it destroys the essence of web browsing. The only other limit is 800x600 max resolution. This could be fixed easily if I can find a way to hack the "applications, places, system" menu to be items (and therefore take up less space on the panel which allows me to shrink the panels into one (KDE style).

As I said, I'll write more latter ( a lot more) but I had to post a new topic from my saved ibook!

 You don't need to "hack" the "applications, places, system" menu (the gnome menu bar applet), you just need to take your pointer to an empty area of the bottom panel, click while holding the fn and f12 keys and from the menu that comes out click on "add applet" and add the "main menu" applet.
Then you can delete the top panel.
