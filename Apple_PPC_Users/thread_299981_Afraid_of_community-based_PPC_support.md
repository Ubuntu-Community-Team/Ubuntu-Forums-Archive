### Post by stream303 on 2006-11-15
Ok, so official PPC support is being discussed at Ubuntu.

Maybe I'm naive, but many posts seem to be afraid of community-based support based on the history of other projects.

While waiting for the other shoe to drop (or not), I'm wondering what it takes to make a positive out of this.  Surely as Ubuntu-ans we might have a different outlook/philosophy.  

Obviously a lot depends on developers, but I'd like to know how we could do better if it does happen.  Would the sky really fall?  Would we *want* to use our PPC's after 5 years anyway - would they still be relevant?

My answer is obviously yes - I hate to see good working hardware go to waste and I'd like to see it married to Ubuntu forever.

I don't know what it takes to start a community, although the one here is fantastic!  Is it really that hard?

### Post by stmiller on 2006-11-15
Well it's a big job. For Mandrake PPC when it went to community-only/unsupported, there was only ONE person making a kernel, and disc iso. And the distro relied on OLD packages. There was no one to make updated packages. I mean, who is going to maintain 9GB of software packages for ubuntu PPC? Handle all the package depends? And keep repositories updated? And what about updates?

So it is problematic. I think ubuntu is a great distro, but the fact that they are even talking about dropping it is bad news for us. Giving it to the 'community' is just a nice way of letting it die off, instead of just dropping it altogether- I'm afraid.  :-|
