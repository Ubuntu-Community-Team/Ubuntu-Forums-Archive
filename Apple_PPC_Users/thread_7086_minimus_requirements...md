### Post by franx on 2004-12-05
An iMac would probably work fine.  I installed an Airport card in mine and it works flawlessly under Ubuntu, which recognized it immediately.  Note that the old iMacs have early ATI Rage 128 RL cards that cannot be removed and upgraded.  That means you're stuck with 8 megs of VRAM.  The good news is that, consulting these forums, I was able to activate DRI/3D acceleration on that card -- which lets me do simple 3D games like Tuxracer.

I've found my iMac G3/350 a great "learning-Linux" machine.

### Post by dkitty on 2004-12-10
Ditto what franx said. I'm running ubuntu on an iMac that's only a little faster than the one you saw in the paper infatuated_ipod. I did a few tweaks for it to all fit my personal tastes (firewall, make it stop checking a time server... minor stuff) and I have no complaints whatsoever. The OpenOffice.org software takes a bit to start but handles the MS Office files from my workplace so well that I barely notice the change in environment.

I miss flash, as there's no version of flash or shockwave for PPC linux (yet). That's a small thing though.

I've two suggestions. The first is that if you've the spare cash and are curious, call the person selling that iMac quickly and check it out thoroughly. Ask about the power switch... if it needed maintanence. If the iMac is in good overall condition and the cd/dvd drive runs well then I'd get it.

Second, get a bit more ram for it in the near future. You don't have to have more ram than 256mb for things to run smoothly, but it's nice and a cheap upgrade.

For the record, I had the same problem with YDL 4 franx... slooooooooooooooowwwww. And that was when I was lucky enough to get X to boot.
