### Post by avtolle on 2006-08-17
I suspect the answer to your question lies in what you are planning to use the MacBook for, that is, how disk intensive will the apps, etc. you will be routinely running be. If what you do requires frequent disk access, obviously pay the extra money. If, however, you access the HDD infrequently, then I don't think the speed differential will be all that noticeable. Note and disclaimer: I am far from a hardware expert; just my thoughts.

### Post by Greycloak on 2006-08-17
The 7200 RPM drive should actually make a HUGE difference in performance. I had an old desktop PC that had a 5400rpm drive in it and I upgraded to a 7200rpm IDE drive.  Since then everything has run much faster, and it isn't even a SATA drive.

I would go for the 7200rpm.
