To run Mac OS X on any hardware other than Apple is a blatant violation of their EULA. Most likely, the version of OS X you downloaded was the one meant only for developers. There are rumors that Apple may open up their OS to use on non-Apple hardware (which I hope they will), but until they do what you are attempting is very illegal, and I think it violates what the Open Source movement is trying to accomplish. At the very least, it gives us a bad reputation. You shouldn't find much help on these forums.

Perhaps you could pick up a used Apple PPC computer? Their prices have plummeted since Apple's switch to Intel. I have an older G4 laptop which dual boots Ubuntu and OS X quite nicely, and I can move files easily between partitions. Their is lots of information on the forums on how to accomplish a dual-boot on PPC.
