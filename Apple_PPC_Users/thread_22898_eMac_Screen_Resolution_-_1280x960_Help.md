### Post by virgule on 2005-06-23
I think its a missing Modeline line.. Thats what solved a similiar issue on my box. Use gtf to generate a Modeline line that you will insert in **Section "Monitor"** just below Option "DPMS", then, manually add the resolution values in **Section "Screen"**:
```

# gtf 1280 960 75

  # 1280x960 @ 75.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: **75.15 kHz**; pclk: 129.86 MHz
  Modeline "1280x960_75.00"  129.86  1280 1368 1504 1728  960 961 964 1002  -HSync +Vsync

```
**Note the bold 75.15 kHz?** You have to make sure this does not exceed your monitor's HorizSync capacities.. Here, they do. Check the hardware specs for your particular monitor model at the vendor's website or on the back of it. If it really is too much, try with 74, 73, 72.. and so on until it fit safely within the range. Its quite frequent xorg 'autodetect' erroneous values.. yes, this happened for me too ;) 
At the end, xorg.conf should like that:
```

Section "Monitor"
Identifier "iMac"
Option "DPMS"
HorizSync 71-73
VertRefresh 70-140
# 1280x960 @ 75.00 Hz (GTF) hsync: 75.15 kHz; pclk: 129.86 MHz
  Modeline "1280x960_75.00"  129.86  1280 1368 1504 1728  960 961 964 1002  -HSync +Vsync
EndSection

Section "Screen"
Identifier "Default Screen"
Device "ATI Technologies, Inc. Radeon 9200 (RV280)"
Monitor "iMac"
DefaultDepth 24
SubSection "Display"
Depth 1
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 4
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 8
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 15
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 16
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
SubSection "Display"
Depth 24
Modes "1280x960" "1024x768"
EndSubSection
EndSection

```
