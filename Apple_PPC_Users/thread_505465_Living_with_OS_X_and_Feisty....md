### Post by tcrroadie on 2007-07-20
I feel the same as you do about the freedom that Linux and Open Source software gives me on my pc's.  I have a late model iBook G4 that is dual booting OS X 10.4 and Ubuntu Feisty.  My iBook has an ATI Radeon 9550 video card, so with the use of the open source ATI driver set, I am able to have full 3D support and Beryl.  Beryl is very fancy and fun on my iBook G4.  

Since I installed Ubuntu Feisty on my iBook G4, I hardly ever touch OS X.  I pretty much only use OS X anymore for downloading and transferring Podcast to my Rio mp3 player.  

My preferences for my iBook G4 are as follows.  

Linux distrobution - Ubuntu
  Great community, the largest software repository for PowerPC, and the Apt package manager.  

Desktop - Gnome
  Gnome just has that nice clean consistent look that I desire.  And these days looks much better than KDE.  I ran KDE as my primary desktop for 2 1/2 years until last fall.  

Web Browser - Mozilla Firefox 
  Used for many obvious resons.  Has some great media plugin tools that really make Firefox very usable on PowerPC.  Namely a Firefox plugin called [MediaPlayerConnectivity]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/446")
Another great plugin for Firefox is [VideoDownloader]("https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/2390").  VideoDownloader gives you the ability to easily download Flash videos from sites such as YouTube.  

Another online video search-download-play application is an application called Democracy Player.  Democracy player will allow you to search videos on YouTube and download and play these video files within Democracy player.  

Music Player - Rhythombox 
  A full featured music player for Gnome that is easy to use and looks great. 

Another music player option that I love is the use of Streamtuner with XMMS.  Streamtuner makes it easy to search and select online music streams and XMMS is an extremely light music player that has some great looking skins and sounds great. 

Video Player - VLC 
  In my opinion, VLC is the best video player that is available for Linux.  VLC is incredibly stable, easy to use, and will play any video format that you can throw at it.  It will even play ISO video images.

Can you tell I like to use my pc's for media?  :)
