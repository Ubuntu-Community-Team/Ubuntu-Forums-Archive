### Post by littleoracle on 2008-04-10
I just finished getting my clamshell up and running on Feisty the other day. Xubuntu was definitely the way to go and i had no problems with the installation. I had tried the regular Ubuntu alternate install and my machine just couldn't hack it.

The only issues I had were with sound and video. Gnash worked for me for regular flash files, but youtube was a no go. I also had to adjust the volume through the terminal. I'll see if I can figure out how I did that (found it somewhere on the forums) but then you might not have that problem.

Personally, I couldn't get the Totem player to play movies, but that was fine as I prefer VLC anyway, so I would suggest that.

And something else that I've not seen mentioned is how to maximize your small screen real estate. I moved the top bar down to the bottom of the screen and moved all the icons over to the bottom bar. I also turned on autohide so that i have as much screen space as possible. Installed tinymenu into firefox as well and was able to get down to one navigation bar there as well. 

I'm kind of new to this stuff, but I'd be glad to offer any help I can. So far, Xubuntu has been treating my old machine very well.
