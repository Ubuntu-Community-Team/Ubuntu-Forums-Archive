### Post by SteveGeorge on 2006-06-24
If you haven't used Linux on a Mac before I would do a lot of research on the specific model first.  Compared to i386, it's a world apart ... it's like using Linux in 2001.  Bear in mind that things you take for granted may not work (suspend/wireless) and some definitely won't (no flash, games, commercial apps such as vmware for example).  You may not mind (I don't that much) but it's good to be aware.

I'd suggest that you get your mate to use the mac system profiler to send you all the information about the machine first.  You definitely want to know the video, sound, wireless etc.

Then use [http://tuxmobil.org/apple.html](http://tuxmobil.org/apple.html) and [http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/apple.html](http://www.linux-on-laptops.com/apple.html) to check for compatability.  If you've been using Linux for a while this will remind you of what you had to do when Redhat 7.0 was out ;-) ... but it really will be worth it :-)

Best of luck.
