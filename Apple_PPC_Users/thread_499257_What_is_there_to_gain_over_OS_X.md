### Post by Auria on 2007-07-12
My view, based on PPC computers (might be different on others)

What Linux has over OS X (i don't mention the other way around since you didn't ask for it so i'll assume you already know that)
- Freedom/Open source : less restrictive licenses, access to all source code
- Linux apps feel native : yes you can run gimp on mac but let's face it X11.app is crap
- Upgrade for free : You don't need to pay a few hundreds of bucks when a major update comes up.
- More ability to fine-tune and customize
- Choice of desktop environment. (for instance a light one can make your computer faster)
- Downloading latest g++ on OS X is 900mb, on Linux it's 40 mb ;) (off course there's less in the package, but OS X likes to eat your bandwidth for the sake of not confusing the user...)
- Ability to run on any computer (OS X can only be installed on apple hardware...)
- multiple desktops (yeah 10.5 will have that but that's a few hundreds of bucks away while linux has it for free)

And there is no need to wipe out your HD, you can dual-boot and see by yourself (you also mention windows - that's on a different computer right?)

Most of all, i think if you are totally happy with your computer as it is now i see no urge to change, but of course experimentating can be fun (and can be very frustrating in the beginning too)

i personally never did switch to linux as my main system for some reasons like lack of flash that supports everything, and also my printer doesn't work.

### Post by johng4 on 2007-07-12
Personally.. I keep OS X on my iBook (same model as yours, except I put in an additional 1GB of ram, so I'm up to 1.5GB of RAM) for a few specific reasons.  Namely helping customers who use OS X, and for the few design jobs that I just feel like using it for.

But.. linux has the following advantages...

1.  I find linux to run faster than Tiger.  Despite Tiger's graphically pleasant, and smooth appearance, I get better program response from linux.  Numerous benchmarks have shown this as well, but there will always be debate.  I personally feel that linux is faster than MacOS... just not as pretty sometimes.

2. Desktop Choices.  With Ubuntu 7.04 I can run a number of desktops with ease.. such as Fluxbox, Gnome, KDE, and XFCE.  Mac offers 1, and after the novelty of Expose and the Dock wear off, you're left with a restrictive unintuitive DE.  Gnome and KDE are highly configurable, and very intuitive.  Beyond that, they are way more functional.

3.  Software, Software, Software... MacOS despite iLife has nothing to offer you as far as choice when it comes to programs.  They have some damn fine programs, but honestly I hate iTunes, and Mail is garbage.  Linux out of the box gives you SO many choices on every application you use, and best yet, they are free.

4. Support.  Eventually the PPC platform will be all but dead to Apple.  Support is already dropping to a noticable level.  In the Linux world, its gaining more and more steam.  

I forsee a change in the Ubuntu policy in the future when more and more "forsaken" PPC Macs start losing the ability to stay up to date with their Mactel overlords.  Even if Ubuntu doesn't, there's several projects that will.  

PowerPCs are very solid platforms, and it still amazes me that Apple would abandon the platform.  IBM marches on, and soon enough we'll have a new level of Power7 computers out there... if not servers.

5. Wireless ease of use.  I have had numerous problems putting passphrases into Mac OSX, which makes me put in the hex keys... in linux I put the passphrase in for the same connection and I'm connected.

Easy.

Mac's Advantage...

1. Media codec support.  Flash works much better on OS X, as well as a lot of other codecs, however Linux is catching up fast.  Give it time on this one.  Gnash in Gutsy will be YouTube capable, which is a big deal.  Democracy makes some big steps which each update, and will soon be a great all around media streamer.

2. Pretty-factor.  The desktop is smoother, but that doesn't pay the bills.

### Post by stmiller on 2007-07-12
Well, use what you like, is what I always say. OS X is nice, and so is Ubuntu. The iBooks in particular are 100% Linux friendly and compatible. Install and everything works great.

Some bad things about PowerPC Linux at the moment:

No adobe flash (good thing?) But there is gnash for youtube
No skype if this concerns you
airport extreme cards restricted to 802.11b at the moment
3D drivers are problematic, but ATI seem usable

But lots of good things about running Linux:

- No restrictions on content or media; no software calling home; total freedom.

- Advanced controls over cpu speed, and other hardware controls if you wish to alter them. Slow your  hard drive down, under clock your CPU, etc. whatever you want.

- PowerPC Linux will continue to be supported and advanced for many years. IBM has several people working full time on developing Linux on the POWER arch and its derivatives. Just search any recent kernel changelog for 'powerpc'

- Fully 64bit top to bottom available for G5 computers with Gentoo, and Debian ppc64, if you actually need to use that machine for the full potential it was created for. (Ubuntu runs a 64bit kernel and 32bit userland on G5 machines, which works great too of course). Significant gain in performance on these machines in 64bit Linux vs. OS X. That's why the Terrasoft company did so much business selling G5 Xserve clusters and such to NASA and other outfits running Linux.

- It's free, and software is free.

Anyways, those are my opinions. :)
