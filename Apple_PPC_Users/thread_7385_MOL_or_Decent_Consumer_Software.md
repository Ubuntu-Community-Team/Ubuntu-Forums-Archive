### Post by slux on 2004-12-07
No-one except Macromedia can do much about Flash. Well, there are the GPL'd flash libs but they're not much of a substitute and I'd imagine implementing Flash fully is a lot of work that few are willing to do.

Let's just hope Flash fades away soon like it deserves to IMO, the web is supposed to be based on open standards and 90% of the flash I see today is ads anyway.

Happily, the rest of the missing stuff isn't a problem at all for my personal use - I think I've never willingly surfed to a shockwave-using site, I very rarely am interested in any quicktime clips... Something I've missed?

As for mp3, mpeg and divx it's mostly an ideologic decision on part of the Ubuntu (and Debian) developers. Both teams seem to be interested in keeping non-free software off their distro as opposed to [some](http://www.linux-mandrake.com/)  others who prefer a more pragmatic approach (except for p2p which they refuse to include unlike Debian). These codecs (well, I'm told most of the multimedia world) have software patent issues and it's safer not to include them. Not only that, it's making the free software non-patent encumbered alternatives such as Ogg Vorbis, Ogg theora etc. more desirable. 

It is true that not having these things by default is for some users an inconvenience, but for me it's still preferable to taking the easy road in everything. You can still get all the support for a little tweaking (someone should make prebuilt packages so it isn't necessary to compile yourself) but people who don't need it don't have to keep proprietary components on their system.

And finally, to be frank, the people that suffer the most from the slow proprietarization of GNU/Linux are ppc users. If we'd be running x86 we'd have Flash, Nvidia/ATI 3D acceleration for the latest cards, drivers for a bunch of other hardware, win32 codecs for mplayer etc. To an extent that goes even if you are on OS X. If there's anyone who should be interested in promoting digital freedom, it's us.

PS.
The fact that Ubuntu isn't presently asking for anything in return for their distro doesn't mean you can't pay for the software if you like it. Donate to your favourite free software project or organization today. :)
