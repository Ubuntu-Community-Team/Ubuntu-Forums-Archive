### Post by Midwest-Linux on 2007-09-16
Good news, I had a old PC laptop that was pretty much shot. It had 128 MB ram the same as these IMACS uses (144 pin SO Dimms), and I also had a 64 MB (144 pin SO Dimm). I opened it up using instructions from a Mac site. These Macs use exactly the same 144 Pin SO drams as laptop Rams do. Make sure these are PC 100 So Drams 144 pins..they are interchangeable with IMACs and PC laptops. The PC 100 144 Pin So Drams are not that much more expensive than regular 168 pin PC 100's.

 
 Disclaimer, this is what I did and how it was done to my computer. If you are not comfortable with disassembling your computer, then take it to Apple, or get a tech friend to do this for you. You are at your own risk doing this, I will not be liable for any mishaps or mistakes for anyone or anything. This is a little more complicated than just taking off a cover, and adding some ram. Having said this.

It was fairly straight forward ....unplug computer, turn it upside down on a cloth picture tube face down (I used a pillow) unscrew the one screw by the bottom handle. Take off the plastic bottom cover, lift up... slide off. There are two screws at the top (you will them after the main bottom cover is off) , unscrew these (make sure that the screws don't fall down into the chassis), after that unscrew the cable that looks like a VGA cable, pull out. 

Carefully, unhook the cable assembly to the right pull out, another cable assembly slightly to the left, pull that out gently. The whole unit should slide out. After pulling unit out, face unit up, note the shield cover with holes in it, thats where the slot memory goes. 

(now it gets a little complicated, If you just want to install/or add just one ram stick them just pull off the sheild cover and replace ram. If you want to also add another ram stick to the bottom...then you must go a little further)

Now once you pop off the shield cover, you will only see ONE slot, thats the top slot, for the bottom slot, you have to lift the circuit board off, you do this by unhooking the heatsink retaining pin, remove heatsink retaining pin, lift heatsink, gently popoff board, install or replace lower ram, turn over board face down like the way it was, gently press the board back on. Carefully replace heatsink, carefully put retaining pin over heatsink, now replace the top RAM. Once done you just replaced both ram sticks, and just follow reverse order to assemble everything back. 

[http://manuals.info.apple.com/en/iMacG3_originalInstallGuide.PDF](http://manuals.info.apple.com/en/iMacG3_originalInstallGuide.PDF)

Now I have 161 MB Ram on the IMAC, surely enough to run at least Xbuntu....maybe even Ubuntu 7.04.
