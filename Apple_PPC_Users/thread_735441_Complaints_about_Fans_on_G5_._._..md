### Post by stream303 on 2008-03-26
As a followup, many G5 iMac owners have been through multiple motherboard and power supply replacements,

While I have been fortunate, and have not had that problem, you can bet that during an Ubuntu install, I've got my hand constantly checking the warmth of the power supply.  Luckily, it seems that the fans run cool, but I have no idea how much they are taxing the power supply when they go full-bore.

I think that Debian does the right thing by having it under control during install.  I have never found out why Ubuntu does not do this - do they not have the code, have the manpower to install it, or are they doing something with the kernel that makes it necessary to leave it out of the install, but implement it upon reboot?

When I think about it, If I had a friend with a G5 that was relying solely on me to use Ubuntu under the condition that it won't kill his G5 iMac box, I'd truthfully have to say NO.  Use Debian, or some other installer that doesn't make the fans scream for the 12 minutes or so that it takes to install.  I'm taking chances with my machine, but I'm not going to with somebody else's.
