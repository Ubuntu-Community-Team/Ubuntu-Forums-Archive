### Post by 3rdalbum on 2006-07-16
The lack of PPC Flash player is due to Macromedia's/Adobe's laziness; not Ubuntu's.

PPC Ubuntu still has a future, as the above poster said. Flash on PPC has some kind of future too; for instance, if you want Flash 9 to be ported to PPC Linux as well as x86 Linux, you should e-mail Adobe and tell them! I'm sure all it would require would be a couple of minutes of recompilation and finding some community people to test it.

Also, Gnash is an open-source implementation of Flash Player. It will run on PowerPC, so hopefully that will eventually solve your problem!

### Post by stream303 on 2006-12-19
Canonical actually did me a favor by indicating their inability to support PPC in the future - I got involved with Debian which has no current plans to drop ppc support.  I like both distros for different reasons and the knowledge you gain from running either is for the most part applicable to both.

I created a nearly identical Debian setup (Etch - testing) and I am currently running a very lean Etch with just Fluxbox as my window manager on my G5 iMac.  I found that the KDE/Gnome/Xfce desktops just got in my way on either distro.  Thing is, I have a much better understanding of what's going on underneath Ubuntu so the knowledge gleaned from either environment is useful. (Some of you G3 owners might want to look into this if you want to get a little more low-level with perhaps an Edgy server install or Debian netinstall option)

The big issue for me was the community - and I found a similar one for Debian here (aside from their mailing lists):

[http://forums.debian.net](http://forums.debian.net)

To make a long story short, I'm having a blast applying the things I learn from both distros to each other.  And if Ubuntu PPC were to go away tomorrow, I'm all set.  Both have great communities and support.  So don't switch - just run both! :)
