### Post by Vyse of Arcadia on 2005-11-23
Although I have a second generation iMac, I had the same problem. I did find a fix for it. At the prompt(black screen, white letters) that says "boot:" type "live-powerpc64" and then hit enter.

Unfortunately, althought that advances the boot process, I've never managed to boot the Ubuntu live CD completely. It does all of the ncurses stuff where it loads all sorts of...loady stuff, but as soon as the desktop environment starts to load, it freezes, leaving nothing but a brown background and an immovable cursor to stare at while the cooling fans loudly blast air out of the back of the iMac.
