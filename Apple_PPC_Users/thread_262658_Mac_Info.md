### Post by zxee on 2006-09-22
I was going to pass on this because I'm a ppc user-also have a homebuilt peecee. I saw your HG Wells quote and as a lifelong fan of his writings how could I not respond.
Ok-if you want to save $$ you might consider the ppc macs they are definately usable. I've read a bit about the intel macs but am not impressed at this time-I don't even use intel on the computers I've assembled. 
Apple choose not to include a modem on their new macs-which I realize is not an issue for many users. I think-though not sure that firewire 800 is gone too.
Re: wireless the broadcom internal chip was pretty difficult to get working in ppc macs-no support from broadcom but someone got it working after a long time-so I think you can expect that kind of support generally.
On ppc there was no grub since there isn't a mbr per say and I've read that ubuntu replaces the mac bootloader on intel macs.
Probably the best info I can provide here is that amazon frequently offers substantial discounts on many of it's computers including those from apple. Good luck.

### Post by Ross Hend on 2006-09-24
Dapper supports my Macbook Pro's wireless card perfectly, I can connect to my WPA network without any hastle.

Generally it's well supported, I use eEFIt as my boot manager, as the GRUB install fails at the end of the OS install.

I made the mistake of buying strait from Apple. I suggest buying somewhere that you can get an easy replacement or even refund if you need it.

As for OSX, the directory structure is simular to most unix variants, they have of course, been changed ALOT.

All programs are contained in /Applications and home directories in /Users.

### Post by blaine00 on 2006-09-29
I recently bought a MacBook [not the a MacBook Pro] so I should be able to help you with some of that.

1.) [http://bin-false.org/?p=17](http://bin-false.org/?p=17) gives you a step by step guide to installing Ubuntu on a MacBook as well as getting your wireless card to work.
2.) Linux seems to perform very well on the MacBook. I have only used Dapper as a live CD so far, but it seems to be fast and all the 3D screen savers run fast and clean.
4.) I've really enjoyed Mac OS X. It is both beautiful and fast. I've become spoiled to a lot of the features such as Expose and Dashboard... I don't think I could go back to Windows if I wanted to. Now, the only problem I have with OS X thus far, is it doesn't seem to be very customizable. For example, you are stuck with one theme and have very little control over the aspects of that theme. It would be nice if they could at least allow you to use the themes of older version of Mac OS X, I actually liked the transparent look of the windows more than this new metal look.
5.)It is a Unix file system, but all the system folders are hidden by default. When you browse the harddrive of a fresh install of OS X, you only see folders like "Applications", "Users" and "Libraries". Once you set Finder to see hidden folders, all the "bin", "lib", "opt" stuff appears. I guess it is the file system of older Macs kinda merged with Unix.
6.) I bought my MacBook from the Apple Store in Houston, but I guess it would have been just as nice to order from their website. I think they do free shipping. The problem with buying it from a store is, you are limited on the configurations you can choose from. If you want a larger harddrive, more RAM, or something like that, you need to order it online.

Hope I was somewhat helpful!
