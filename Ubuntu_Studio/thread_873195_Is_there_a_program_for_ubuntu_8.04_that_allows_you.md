### Post by Stochastic on 2008-07-28
Mixcraft and Virtual DJ are very different applications.

To replace Mixcraft, check out Audacity (as has already been suggested), Ardour, Jokosher, or Rosegarden - there are many other multi-track audio editors in the Linux world, these are just a handful.  Audacity is the more stable beginner-friendly one.

To replace Virtual DJ, take a look at MIXXX.  There are a couple other apps similar to mixxx, but it's the most complete one out there.
