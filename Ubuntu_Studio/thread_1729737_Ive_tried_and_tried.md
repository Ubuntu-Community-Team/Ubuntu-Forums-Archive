### Post by FuzzShifter on 2011-04-21
You may want to consider doing things one at a time and rebooting in between.

I have gotten to the point where Ubuntu can be installed (with multimedia support and 3D graphics) in less than two hours, and quite stable.

There a few things that I've learned about Ubuntu over the last 4 years...

1. Don't install every program in the repositories at once.

2. Be aware that your computer is finite, while your desires are infinite.

3. If you have made 50 changes to your installation before asking for help with a problem, you now have 51 potential problems.

4. Reinstalling is easier than fixing my own mistakes.

5. Getting angry at a computer has absolutely no impact on its performance.

To the OP:

Perhaps you could be specific about your issues instead of ranting.

You see, this is a cost free OS, and support is offered by real human beings that aren't being paid to hear you blow a blood vessel in your eye just because your computer doesn't know what you want it to do.
