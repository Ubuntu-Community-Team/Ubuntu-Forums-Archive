Hi, the extra ntfs drive will remove a performance bottleneck, and prolong the life of your system drive. 

ntfs-3g exists to let you safely read and write to standard windows ntfs partitions. Look at google with this search

fstab entry ntfs-3g

to see how people choose to configure it. I have one vst,
that has a 14 gig soundset, so due to its size, I linked it to

/home/user/.wine/drive_c/Program Files/Steinberg/VstPlugins the standard location for vsts in windows/linux. This is the kind of functionality you can have by installing ntfs-3g (which is often installed by default in some linuxi.

I would recommend this scheme for a musicians 160 gig drive
windows 60 gig
/       10 gig
/home   88 gig
/swap    2 gig

extra drive for recording, and giant samplesets/movie collections etc

For luck, I don't let a windows partition go beyond 2/3 full.

The 10 gig /root is partly to hold samples and saved data for Hydrogen, in its usr/share/hydrogen/data folder. My root partition has less than 4 gig of actual linux apps/logs/configs/fonts etc. Allow more for the root partition if you play larger 3D games, or install their data sets into /home.

Good luck!
