### Post by mango42 on 2010-03-17
One of my biggest (musical creativity!) hang-ups since MIDI came on the scene has been how to lay down tracks based on 'human syncronisation' rather than being forced to figure out the tempo by hand before recording.

ISTR early versions of Cubase on the Atari attempted this but it got dropped for some reason. How difficult can it be for music software coders to achieve a basic functionality of this much desired feature (by me, at least!) ?

Forget rehearsals and all the stiffness that brings into previously spontaneous genius (:>), let's have a function that sets the tempo on the fly based on four notes incoming from a MIDI keyboard. Lexicon (and others) do it in some of their FX processors, so why not in a MIDI sequencer like my favorite-du-jour, Qtractor?

Please?

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-03-17
Possibly because someone not playing to a click track is going to have a varied tempo throughout their performance? One bar might be 107bpm and the next would be 109bpm. Or did you mean that the first bar would set the tempo for the whole track? In which case you could just set tempo manually by a few mouse clicks, I think it is called tap tempo on most programs.

I know what you mean though, there are a lot of classic performances done with very loose timing.
