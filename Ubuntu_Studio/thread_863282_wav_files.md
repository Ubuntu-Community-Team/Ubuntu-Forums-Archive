### Post by jtniehof on 2008-07-18
For WAV editing, I always found [GNoise]("http://gnoise.sourceforge.net/") worked quite well for me, but it's no longer packaged for Ubuntu (and is quite old). [Audacity]("http://audacity.sourceforge.net/") is available (universe repository). I did a quick check and it displays sample rate and size. You can change the sample rate after opening. When you export a file, you can specify the sample type (16 bit PCM, 32 bit float, etc., it's under "Options").

You can also, of course, edit the waveform itself...it wasn't clear to me what you meant by "make modifications."
