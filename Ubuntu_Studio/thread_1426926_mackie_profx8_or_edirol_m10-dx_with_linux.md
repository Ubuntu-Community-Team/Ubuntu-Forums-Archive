### Post by bcschmerker on 2010-03-27
The [Mackie® ProFX8 and ProFX12](http://www.mackie.com) are both USB 1.1, functional with USB 2.0 systems.  The same vendor's Onyx series (different line from ProFX) is set up for IEEE 1394.

Extrapolating from experience with a [SIIG® USB SoundWave 7.1 Pro](http://www.siig.com/), the ProFX8 should hook up with little trouble.  Haven't tested the Onyx under FFADO to date, however.

### Post by DuggyFreddy on 2010-05-29
Well, after waiting two months, I finally received my Mackie ProFx8.  It does everything I wanted.  I have been able to record onto Ardour and Traverso using Jack to Route thru calf effects, jammin, and jkmeter.  I have been able to mix out onto a tape player thru the mackie, into my eq and my dad's old fisher tube amp.  And then I have been able to master that back thru jammin' and the calf compressor.  No problems yet!  It sounds great, and best of all it has no driver, no software controls, just a real machine w/ real knobs.  It will work just great live also, as I will be able to use it as board, mic pre-amp, and play tracks off the laptop along w/ the live mix in real time.  Pretty sweet.  It was about $240 US.  Took two months after I ordered it, but worth the wait.
