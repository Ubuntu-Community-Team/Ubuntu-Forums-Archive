### Post by linux4life88 on 2009-10-25
Go to edit then chose preferences (I think you can use ctrl + p as a shortcut). Then you can edit things such as number of channels, sample rate, sample format, mp3 bit rate to export to and so on and so forth. Just start playing with the settings until you get something that works well for you and has the quality you want. Hope this helps out.
