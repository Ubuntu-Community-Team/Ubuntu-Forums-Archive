Well, the answer is yes - Ubuntu does have a large selection of these applications.  The next question is what do you mean by 'compose music'?

If you really want to write dots on a page (i.e. if you're composing music for a real musician to play later), then take a look at Denemo, MuseScore, or even Rosegarden.  All of these programs have notation abilities and can play the piece back to you (although the performance of it will clearly be a computer rendering rather than a humanistic musical piece).

If you want to create music on your computer without the real-world performer, then your possibilities grow enormously.  First, I can't recommend enough the Ubuntu Studio documentation [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio) for beginners to read.  Second, you may find any of these applications very useful: lmms, hydrogen, zynaddsubfx, Muse, and qsynth.

Let me know if you need any further help/recommendations, the world of multimedia creation in Ubuntu can be a bit tricky at times.

Hope this helps.
