### Post by sgx on 2011-05-27
You can add new apps one at a time, no problem, using synaptic.

When you decide which are your linux keepers, you can experiment with
using synaptic to remove the rest. If synaptic wants to also
uninstall things you are unfamiliar with, do a google search
before proceeding. Keep the unknown, when in doubt.

I change synaptic preferences to save installed apps in the cache,
then collect them for future use on usbsticks/CDs

/var/cache/apt/archives  is often where they are stored.

Linux can change and break things, so the newest may not be what you need or want, and some things disappear off the net, so its lucky to keep them safely.
Cheers
