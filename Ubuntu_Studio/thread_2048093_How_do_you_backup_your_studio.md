### Post by sgx on 2012-09-04
Extra copies of important files should also go to a separate
trusted physical location, fire, flood, catastrophic storms, quake, theft, and lightning, can't be predicted or stopped,
but can be avoided. Burn songs, art and data to media,
so magnetism is not an invisible enemy.

A separate /home partition, gives you the reinstall option
of preserving /home by not formatting it during a fresh install
keeping the many configurations and data intact,
and saving you time.

Clonezilla type system imaging is cool, but helps windows users
the most, who have nightmarish reinstall prospects in too many instances.

Once your recording system is working, don't tinker.
Set up an external drive, to test new linux things on, and to use as
your recording destination drive, sample storage, drive-image storage, etc 
Using a second drive for recording also removes a common performance bottleneck.
Cheers
