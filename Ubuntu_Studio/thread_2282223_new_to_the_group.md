### Post by marseille2 on 2015-06-27
I tried Cinelerra's new version since it now works with a variety of codecs. It wasn't as stable as I needed it to be, but it will have a familar interface for those who cut on Final Cut Pro or Adobe Premier. It's pretty typical as far as timeline and compositor (similar interface to Final Cut Pro and Premiere Pro). BUt when it comes to motion graphics, After Effects is the industry standard. Blender does an impressive job offering an open source alternative, but it's so different that an editor who learns on industry software is in for a shock. It'll help if they know some python.
