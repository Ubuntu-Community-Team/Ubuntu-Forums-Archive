### Post by skullmunky on 2009-02-26
> **ZeldaFan said:**
> I heard that a lot of movie studios use linux for their primary animation software (e.g. Dreamworks does). What kind of software does this include specifically? I understand that this software is not necessarily open source (and probably costs a lot of money), but I am just curious.

They're often talking about Maya, but also Renderman, Houdini, the Autodesk Flame/Flint/Smoke/ etc. systems, and also a lot of the time they're talking about custom, proprietary in-house tools.  With Maya, for example, a studio will often craft their own customized UI, tools, and workflow around the Maya core, specifically for the work they're doing and with extraneous stuff removed.  

Render farming and networking is one reason for using Linux.  Another is historical - many studios began using SGI and Irix, so the migration to Linux has been a natural progression.  A third reason is the ability to really customize and fine-tune systems and software.

I just saw a neat talk by the team from Disney who did the stereoscopic 3D for Bolt, showing their customized controls for adjusting the 3D effect in Maya on Linux.
