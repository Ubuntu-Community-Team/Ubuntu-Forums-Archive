### Post by sgx on 2010-11-26
Are you a skilled musician?
What instrument(s) do you play?
What objectives you wish to achieve?
Do you want to meet accepted professional standards?
What is your budget?

The audio tools as a whole, are not wonderful. There are some
excellent individual softwares, but they lack financial support,
and can have lulls in developement, or abandonment, when the author has a change in needs and priorities. Some are tools made for a personal solution, yet shared freely, but without the promise of meeting public expectations.

It will take a lot of effort to learn how the pieces fit, count on 100
hours in the search engines over the next few months. The results will
depend on purchasing hardware that you can verify works in linux, and your
tenacity on configuring the parts to work as a whole.  

In the meantime, using the forum search here, with brand names like
edirol, maudio, and application names like qjackctl, ardour, zynaddsubfx,
etc will bring up valuable discussions of common issues, and how people
have overcome them. I personally feel it is worth the effort, because the
incidental knowledge you will gain in the process, can help you secure
employment, or advancement within a company system.
Cheers

### Post by SleepWalkerX on 2010-12-02
Don't stress out bro, take it one step at a time.  If you're choosing PC parts just google search the model and manufacturer name and see if it plays well with the Linux kernel.  Some helpful commands are dmesg (what's going on in your kernel), lsusb (attached usb devices), lspci (attached pci devices).  

Wikipedia those audio systems and software and see how they fit together.
