### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-12-02
> Is it possible to emulate a violin in zynaddsubfx, for example?  Should I be looking at VSTs?
VSTs are designed specifically for Windows, so I wouldn't start buying any of those right away (you can use them with Linux but it is not simple). For more realistic sounds you should look at qsynth which can play soundfonts. [Soundfonts]("http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SoundFont") don't just emulate other sounds, they will be actual recordings of those sounds (i.e. samples). Many soundfonts are available for free here on the internets, and some are good quality. 

For synths I like:
Phasex - lots of fun to use and easy to shape new sounds with
AMS - somewhat clunky design but it sounds great (careful with the volume)
Bristol - Again the GUI is not pretty, but the vintage synth sounds are impressive

If you don't know what to do with your synths, just pick a simple one (like explorer from the Bristol set) and play around with it. Use presets as a starting point and create a simple midi pattern, then tweak the synth until you see the way the parameters effect the music.

If you are still stuck then pick out one of your favourite producers, and just try to copy their sound as best you can, it is a great way to learn the ropes.

### Post by RaumTrug on 2010-12-03
if you want a violin, sample one (with a good mic, or buy sample cd's, or even find some moderately good samples for free on the internet, or rip single note sounds with different expression from some violin solo from record/cd), and use a "sampler", for example linuxsampler to control it via midi.

samplers (or "trackers" which are basically nerdy samplers) are besides synthesizers the most important tools in electric/digital music. expecially hip-hop uses samples/samplers like mad - for drums, loops, etc. and they sound like, errrr...the samples you feed them ;)
