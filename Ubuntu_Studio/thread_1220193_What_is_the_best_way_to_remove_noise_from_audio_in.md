### Post by kayosiii on 2009-07-23
The best way is not to get the noise in there in the first place...
Seriously getting it right when recording will save you many times more work trying to get rid of the excess noise and the quality will be much better.

Assuming that you can't rerecord it Audacity and Gnome Wave Cleaner are the two best Linux programs for cleaning Audio. With video you are going to have to be more specific about what kind of repairs you want to do.

### Post by kayosiii on 2009-07-23
> **Rytron said:**
> Okay. I can clean up just audio in Audacity with ease. I guess for recording video with audio, the best thing is to have a proper microphone & quite surroundings. Thanks for the advice.

If possible you should record using an external mic from the camera. This will avoid the internal motor noise from the camera. Better yet you can record from an external recording device but if you this you should also get/make a clapboard so you can sync sound to video. Highly directional mics are useful for cutting out unwanted sounds.

With Video be aware that Consumer level cameras digital video cameras should produce good results providing your scene is adequately lit and not overly busy. Poor lighting or very busy scenes will weaken the quality conciderably.
