### Post by John Fraser on 2009-07-22
I would welcome any guidance regarding appropriate software and techniques for making video from still digital images.

I want to start with an image (a portrait, for example) and make a short video clip of, say, a zoom from a medium close-up (head and shoulders shot) to a big close up (just the eyes). I would also like to be able to create short clips consisting of zooms and pans across still images of landscapes.

I have tried various search engines to answer this problem, but without success. I can't find anything in Ubuntu Studio, either.

Does a 'software rostrum camera' exist in Ubuntu, or any Linux distro? Can one use Blender to do this? If so, how?

Under Windows, one may use Adobe AfterEffects. There is also a programme called DigiRostrum.

Has anyone tackled this challenge using Ubuntu?

All advice gratefully received.

### Post by paulmerchant on 2009-07-24
Blender can do this. It might be a bit challenging for someone new to Blender, however.

There's a new Linux application that was made specifically for turning still images into video called SMILE. It's not in the repository, but it should be easy to install on Ubuntu. Most of the stuff I've seen made with SMILE is full of cheesy transitions, but it appears to give users the ability to do camera type moves, too.
