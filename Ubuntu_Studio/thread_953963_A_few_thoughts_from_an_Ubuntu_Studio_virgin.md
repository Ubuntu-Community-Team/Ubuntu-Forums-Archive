### Post by luciuslowe on 2008-10-20
Hi, I'm Gary "Lucius" Lowe - house music producer/DJ, audio recording engineer (call it what you will!) and would-be house megastar! 
A few months ago during a discussion on audio production softare, My younger brother in law jumped up, claiming Reason was crap, he'd not managed to make anything with it. Another bod jumped up saying he should get Cubase as anything else is crap and then he'd be able to make some wicked tunes. I quietly claimed that it ain't what you use but how you use it that counts. Why do you use Cubase then? was the reply. I use Cubase because in my opinion it is the best tool to do the jobs I need to do in the way I want. To settle the debate I decided to hunt around for a software package I had never used before and make a track to illustrate my point. My search led me finally to Ubuntu and from there Ubuntu Studio. My mission? To produce a release-quality track using only Ubuntu Studio and any other Linux plugins/instruments I come across - No VSTi's, DXi's et al, just UBS and my uncanny ability to knock out a half decent house track. My secondary mission is to record, mix and master a demo for a local rock band using just Ardour.
Installed Ubuntu Studio on 19/10/2008. First impressions were wow, what a lot of kit for nothing! Pretty much straight away I managed to send a midi signal into Rosegarden, playing out audio through one of the bundled synths. A bit naff but it was an audio signal all the same and my first faltering step into the Garden of Roses! Couldn't figure out where or if there was an option to load further plugins so I decided to have a wander through the other apps that came with UBS. After 10 mins or so of experimenting I managed to get a midi signal into both Rosegarden and Hydrogen. Another 20 mins delving into Patchage and I got Rosegarden to send a midi signal into Hydrogen, playing from Rosegarden's sequencer. The one thing I didn't manage is to send an audio signal from Hydrogen back into Rosegarden's mixer. After a quick read of a few posts on the Ubuntu forum I'm not actually sure this bit was possible. It appears that audio could be streamed from both Hydrogen and Rosegaden into Ardour (right or wrong?). Next stage is to try this RG/H/A method. The JACK standard (including patchage) seems like a good idea, if a little drawn out (pun definitely intended!) Its almost comparable to wiring up a real-world studio and plugging across the patchbay sockets! Definitely a world away from Cubase, where everything happens under the hood so-to-speak. It has an almost educational or tutorial approach, showing how, why and where things are connected across the audio/midi network. Not sure it'd be the perfect start for a complete novice to computer-based audio production, but a different way of working shouldn't hinder anyone with a little background knowledge. 

I've also downloaded LMMS, JSampler and LinuxSampler but not installed just yet. Any tips n tricks are welcome. I'm not quite sure I understand the synth/sampler front end/back end business yet but I'm definitely gonna have fun finding out! Gotta figure out how to install them first.....

Peace,
LL

### Post by Stochastic on 2008-10-20
> **luciuslowe said:**
> I've also downloaded LMMS, JSampler and LinuxSampler but not installed just yet. Any tips n tricks are welcome. I'm not quite sure I understand the synth/sampler front end/back end business yet but I'm definitely gonna have fun finding out! Gotta figure out how to install them first.....

You're still installing the old windows/mac way (download via wepages/torrents then install) while in Ubuntu you only need to open a package manager and click install on your favorite applications.  Try opening a terminal and doing ```
sudo apt-get install lmms
```
you'll need to run lmms by opening a terminal and doing ```
lmms
``` or adding a launcher somewhere that will do that for you, but the installation should be easy as pie!

Oh and welcome to the community, and thanks for your thoughts.
