### Post by marseille2 on 2016-03-09
Yah ...  IMO if you like stability, it's a good rule of thumb to do LTS to LTS upgrades. And it's not so bad to avoid the alpha stages of the LTS version, and let the bug reports roll in before you update. If you go this route, you can still use the [LTS enablement stack]("https://wiki.ubuntu.com/Kernel/LTSEnablementStack") ... which is nice for upgrading to the latest kernel, and avoiding the hassle of the 9 month versions and the bugs in the early LTS releases.
