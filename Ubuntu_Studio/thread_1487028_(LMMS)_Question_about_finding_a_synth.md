### Post by sgx on 2010-05-18
Hi, find a synth with selectable waveforms, choose a saw,
then find the tuning controls, the naming conventions vary from
synth to synth, fine, detune, pitch etc, and adjust them away from unison values. You can also carefully at low volume, alter the type of filter, lowpass, highpass, bandpass, none, and the filter controls, cutoff, resonance, frequency, depth, env, keyfollow, gain etc. FX like distortion, compression, short delay amounts, all can be used to advantage and according to taste. Synths with layering, multitimbral, or unison voicing feature, or several instances of low cpu synths,
enable the design of amazing sounds. Google the term supersaw, and see how the opinions rage on which synth is king of such sounds. :)
