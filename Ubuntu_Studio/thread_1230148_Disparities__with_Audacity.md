### Post by trulan on 2009-08-03
An unfortunate truth in the world of open source software is that the documentation is often the last thing to get updated.  Also, the repositories are seldom up to date with the latest versions available from the developers.  Audacity however is a well established program and the version in the repository, while not the latest, should meet your needs.

That being said, have you made any progress in recording your tapes?  Audacity is a fine program for a project like that.  Do the recording level indicators show anything?  (there's a little mic icon, click on it to enable monitoring).  Does a waveform show up when you try to record?  Audacity is a pretty intuitive program, keep working at it.  And feel free to as questions about specific problems.

igorzwx has had problems with Pulse Audio and is thinking that you will as well.  Many users have had no problems however, and there's no need creating one if it isn't there for you.
