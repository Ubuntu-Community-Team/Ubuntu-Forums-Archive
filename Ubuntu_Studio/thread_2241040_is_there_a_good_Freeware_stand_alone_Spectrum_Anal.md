### Post by tgalati4 on 2014-08-24
Audacity has spectral analysis--load in a track, select a portion of the track and select--Plug-ins-Frequency Analysis.

Just about all of the music players have spectral visualizations.  If you want a real time analyzer (RTA), there are lots of [Windows]("http://www.studiotoolz.net/audio-software/spectrum-analyzer/") programs, but sadly none in the linux world that I know of.  Perhaps you can get one to work in WINE.  I use a couple of programs on my old Nokia n800 and n810 for field work (like tuning organs or quick venue EQ).

Many smart phones have RTA's on Android.  Just be aware that the microphones used in phones are generally crappy and won't give good results without calibration.  Compare the response to some know signal levels and you will see that smart phones are generally useless below 60 Hz for capturing bad bass waves.
