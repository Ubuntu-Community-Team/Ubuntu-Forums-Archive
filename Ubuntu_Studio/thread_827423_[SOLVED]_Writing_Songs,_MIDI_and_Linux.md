### Post by nalmeth on 2008-06-13
A tablature is a more simplified version of a musical score for guitarists who don't know how to read/write music. :p
Instead of using notes, keys and octaves, the six lines on the score represent the six strings of a guitar, and numbers represent the fret number on the guitar. This greatly simplifies music writing for guitarists :D

The tablature editor is meant simply for editing the score. Tuxguitar is definitely tailored for guitarists (you can even bring up a fretboard to enter notes rather than entering the fret numbers), [B]but you can edit the old-fashioned way just as easily. 
[/B] 
.gp* files are guitar pro files. Guitar Pro is a proprietary tab editor, but you don't need to worry about this. You can make your score, export it to .mid and import it into any other editor that supports midi (like LMMS or Rosegarden).
