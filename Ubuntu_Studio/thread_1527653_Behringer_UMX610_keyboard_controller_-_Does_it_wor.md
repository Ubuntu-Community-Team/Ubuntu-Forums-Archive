### Post by trulan on 2010-07-09
From Behringer's product page:  [http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/UMX610.aspx](http://www.behringer.com/EN/Products/UMX610.aspx)
> USB/Audio interface included for recording  and playback of any digital music file. Works with your PC or Mac  computer—no setup or drivers
If it works on any PC or Mac with no setup or drivers, it will work on Linux as well.  Also, the site claims that the included EnergyXT will work on Linux.

The included software instruments and VST's are a lot less likely to work on Linux.  Those things tend to be hit or miss.

But as far as the hardware goes, it looks like it should work.  (No, I don't have one, hopefully somebody else will chime in who does.)

### Post by gonk on 2010-07-20
I have the older UMX61 model keyboard, and all the MIDI stuff works fine in Linux.  I'd expect the UMX610 to be the same because it looks like the same thing with a new paint job.

I'm not sure about the UMX610, but the UMX61 key action can be a bit stiff for some people compared to other synth keyboards.  Also changing the UMX MIDI parameters on the keyboard can be a bit clumsy because you don't have any screen/LEDs to give you feedback on what you are doing.  Although this doesn't end up being a big problem because I leave the knobs/buttons at their standard CC values, and manually map them to what I want in the PC software.
