### Post by sgx on 2011-07-25
Studio 4 has an advantage in single-mindedness. Linux kernels, and the apps around them, do compete, splits and camps form, and progress suffers. The Puppy Studio designer is free to roll his own, without
outside interference, and is clever enough to put together excellent
versions, and has been at it for a long time. The puppy package management is quite different than synaptic, with pros and cons, but it's easy enough to use, and most items of studio interest are already installed. :)

As to better, faster etc, for a musician sick of windows, too poor for
a Mac, a ready-to-go linux setup is a boon. The popularity of roll-your-own
distros is on the rise, and fast minimalist audio setups can compete,
once the learning curve is climbed.
