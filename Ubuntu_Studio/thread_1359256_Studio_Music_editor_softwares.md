### Post by trulan on 2009-12-19
There's a whole lot of stuff that might meet your requirements.  Here's a page with links to a whole bunch of it:
[http://www.linux-sound.org/](http://www.linux-sound.org/)

Linux is built around the concept of free and open source software.  All the really good stuff is free.  ;)

Ardour is the standard for a Digital Audio Workstation.  It needs the jack audio server.  So Jack and Ardour would be a good place to start.   Also, consider installing Ubuntu Studio for a ready-to-go package.

If you give more specific information on exactly what you want to do, I'm sure somebody can give you some more specific directions as to what software to look into.  The learning curve for linux pro audio is a bit steep but it is worth it.  Good luck!
