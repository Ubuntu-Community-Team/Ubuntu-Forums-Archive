### Post by Bucky Ball on 2015-12-01
Welcome. You could use Audacity to record it all, but not clear how you have things configured. 

I guess you are playing with drum loops 'live', changing them and what not, and the student is playing live over them. What device are you recording the student on exactly (where is their recorded take going)?

Couple of questions for clarity:

1/ Do you want to create the drum loops in Ubuntu?
2/ What do you want to be recording all at once?
3/ Are you wanting to take the stereo output of the 'live' session, drum loops and student, and record that on the computer?

As mentioned, Audacity (in the Software Centre or, if you are running UStudio, probably already installed) can record whatever you input and easily edit. It has a pretty quick learning curve, especially if you've played around with digital audio editing before, and it's brilliant for 'on the fly' situations. You can edit and paste loops end to end, sure. 

Might fit your needs, might not. :)
