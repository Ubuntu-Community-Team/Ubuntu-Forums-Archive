### Post by RaumTrug on 2011-01-14
very nice, and thanks for sharing! a psychedelic trip , but yet in positive vibrations, which is kind of rare nowadays. harmonics & feel remind me a bit of "mouse on mars", if you know what I mean.

but on the mixdown:

I liked the (slightly to almost completely) out-of-sync passages, but sometimes it's just too much out of sync for too long. I'd (personally) rather prefer playarounds with chaotic rhythms that originate from some "straight" rhythm, and go back to an "easily understandable" one after that. just repeating is often not enough. or go real haywire and then swap to a breakdown of sometheing "completely unrelated different" thing... ;)

also, the low-end is a bit "queer"...it's just that "ploppy" bassdrum, no other low's (and I've been listening with studio headphones...). maybe for your next tracks (or editions of this one): 1.) add a bass-melodyline supporting the harmonics "from time to time". 2.) do something to the ploppy bassdrum (and also to the bassline I just suggested to add)...some reverb effects, for example - filtered, so only the low freq "grumble" of the reverb comes through, maybe with some slight "chorus" and dynamics effects relative to the bassline & the bassdrum.

but all in all it shows a lot of talent, harmonically, and expecially the play with different cymbals & also backwards-drum-passages! maybe you could do more with "melodically interpreted cymbals (and such...)"?

keep up your work, it rocks! :D
