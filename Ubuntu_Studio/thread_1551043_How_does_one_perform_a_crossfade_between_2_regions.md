### Post by Jormeliini on 2010-08-12
Go to Options -> Crossfades. Tick Active, Show, Created Automatically if not already. Select Span Entire Overlap. Now just drag audio region on top of another and crossfade is done. Overlapping part should turn yellow and show an X. You can edit the crossfade by right clicking it and selecting Crossfade -> Edit.

### Post by the downtown canon on 2010-08-12
I was having the same problem till i set the crossfades to be active and created automatically under the options tab - this automatically crossfades any overlapping regions of audio. If you dont want them crossfading then you can just trim the regions to meet at a particular point. I find this the easiest solution.
