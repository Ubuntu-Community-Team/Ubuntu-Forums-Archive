### Post by CrocoDuck on 2016-03-28
Hi there!

I have no reports of Linux used on touch devices for this purpose. However, there is some interest in this particular application involving board computers, for example Raspberry Pi. There is a whole section on [Linux Musicians]("https://linuxmusicians.com/viewforum.php?f=53") (have a look also on the [DIY]("https://linuxmusicians.com/viewforum.php?f=52") section) and [Autostatic]("https://autostatic.com/") done some work on it.

That makes me think that performance wise the hardware of current tablets should be ok. However, my feeling is that to use a tablet up to 100% of its audio potentialities it would be better to build a system that is not necessarily minimal but it is well configured. My fear on a platform like Ubuntu Touch would be that there are too many programs, services and processes getting in the way. Still, I guess it would be the best way to make a quick and dirty test of how things work.

By the way, maybe it is a little off topic, but have you heard of [MOD Duo]("https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/modduo/mod-duo-the-limitless-multi-effects-pedal")?
