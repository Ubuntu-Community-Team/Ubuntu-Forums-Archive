### Post by bluesscream on 2011-06-19
a java and flash-based browser-game like DAW :)
Coca Cola industries with their energy-drink "burn" host the servers.
[http://www.burn.com/en_GB/home/home.html](http://www.burn.com/en_GB/home/home.html)
[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burn_%28energy_drink%29](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burn_%28energy_drink%29)
Professionally made, you can easily produce and share techno-music,
but shurely no environment for performing live in realtime, so latencies might be less relevant. 
My midi equipment was recognized, but I had no success learning the integrated synth's controllers and playing them on the fly. This might be caused by my actual jack2 setting with a2j, which is buggy  for other java and wine stuff too.
Noteworthy: lots of hosted files, loops and samples under cc-license.
For a first impression of their possibilities and limitations:
[http://burn-studios.audiotool.com/radio/](http://burn-studios.audiotool.com/radio/)
Seems to be a nice toy, but for a little more ambitious productivity lmms would be a far better choice and barely more complicated to setup.
Audiotool's power will be cloud orientated diy music production and so it might be seriously pointing ways ahead for future linux audio developement too.
