### Post by CrocoDuck on 2014-09-03
Hi! I use [this piece of hardware]("http://m-audio.com/products/en_us/Uno.html") to interface midi stuff. Back to the time of Ubuntu 10.04 I had to install just [a simple package]("http://packages.ubuntu.com/lucid/midisport-firmware"), you see also a list of all the similar devices that are supported. Nowadays, if I don't remember wrong, that hardware is supported out of the box with an updated Arch Linux installation. I am not sure about the support for the latest Ubuntu version, just have a search online. You can of course buy a sound card with midi, [make sure it is supported by Linux]("http://ubuntuforums.org/showthread.php?t=2236015"). I have a Focusrite Scarlett 2i4 which has midi and it works with Linux well and out of the box... but I don't recommend it to you: it is overpriced with respect the quality of the (so much praised???) analogue circuitry inside.
