Hi, a line-out on an amp, to a line-in on a computer is fine,
also going the opposite direction, and in your case, the 'power amp in' on the hotrod might work, check if it receives stereo or mono. You may have to experiment getting the laptop volume loud and clear enough. The other inputs are probably normal
guitar inputs, instead of a line input, different levels, so you
would need a boost from some preamp device. Going the opposite direction, as in recording your amps output on your laptop,
some amps have a headphone-output
that doubles as a line-out, but is attached to the amps 
volume control. See what the manual says about the preamp-out,
you may need to watch the volume levels carefully.
(That can work to your advantage when recording rakarrack.)

An output designated for a speaker cabinet, might melt something expensive, so stay sober when connecting things ;)

If you have a favorite stompbox, you could try feeding the laptop
signal into it, to det more gain if needed/desired.

Guitarix2 and Calf Plugins/lv2-rack should also get used,
if you get around to recording, and are complimentary to rakarrack.
Cheers

### Post by sgx on 2011-11-02
more examples of connections ratings and schemes

[http://www.crownaudio.com/kb/entry/304/](http://www.crownaudio.com/kb/entry/304/)

At the moment, I have my guitar hooked up to
a computer usb guitar interface, a clean signal going
from that, to rakarrack, and the rakarrack fx signal going
from the soundcard stereo line-out, to a stereo2mono jack,
and then into a line6 amps guitar input.

This amp has master, drive, and channel control knobs, and I set
the guitar volume knob at 7 or 8, the amps master volume
at 8 or 9, and use the channel volume to bring up actual listening volume.

I then adjust the bass, mid, treble, and drive controls,
and one last tweak with the channel volume. :popcorn:

A simpler setup I often use is just the guitar interface to
to rakarrack, rakarrack to timemachine for recording,
and headphones, monitors, or home stereo
for listening.

[http://support.fender.com/manuals/guitar_amplifiers/Hot_Rod_Deluxe_manual.pdf](http://support.fender.com/manuals/guitar_amplifiers/Hot_Rod_Deluxe_manual.pdf)

Sounds like the Fender rep was someones kid, hired on the cheap.
The pre-amp Out and power-amp In form an fx-loop, or path
for chaining a second similar amp. So there is lots to
experiment with, starting with low volumes.
