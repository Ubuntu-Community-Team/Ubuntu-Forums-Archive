### Post by mango42 on 2010-09-11
Hi,

Can anyone recommend a software oscilloscope or preferably an *accurate* (<0.1Hz) audio spectrum Frequency Counter for Linux?

I've had a look at [these progs]("http://ubuntulandforever.blogspot.com/2010/03/meter-bridge-mctools-lite-mhwaveedit.html") but doesn't look like they fit the bill.


The reason I ask is that I am in the process of retuning all my synths to the [Solfeggio scale]("http://www.2012yayas.com/FaceTheMusic.html"). Many years back I looked into the standardisation to A=440Hz and discovered the 1939 outcry over the British Standards Institute agreeing with one Herr Goebels. Now it turns out that the so-called Illuminati predated this desire by at least 20 years! See [this]("http://www.henrymakow.com/musicalscale.html") recent article for more detail, if you're interested in why some music seems to provoke hysteria, aggression etc, rather than harmony and the good vibes mankind forever seeks.

I suppose it would be too much to ask whether some brave programmer has attempted a software rendition of [Cymatics]("http://cymatica.com/2009/11/17/top-10-cymatics-youtube-videos/")? :popcorn:

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-09-11
I know that zyn simply allows you to change A to whatever frequency you like (File>Scale Settings). Have you tried gtkguitune? It is a guitar tuner by design, but seems to be able to do what you need, if you can figure out how to hook your synths up to it (does not seem to work with jack).
