### Post by dannyboy79 on 2010-09-17
i know this is an old thread but i may have the answer in case anyone stumbles on it in future.
you're saying it jagged when you play it in kino after capturing? i turn my preview window way down as that just eats more cpu while capturing dv sources. the preview or player options in kino are not a good way to see if you're getting good footage IMO. also, the video source is probably interlaced. i use kino to capture my firewire source (dazzle hollywood dv bridge, convert analog component video to digital) which is interlaced video. then i bring it into kdenlive, do my edits, transitions, add music or whatver, then i render and i chose "force progressive". good luck
