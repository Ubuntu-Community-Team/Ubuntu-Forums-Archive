### Post by SantoPeligro on 2011-01-03
I've done several clean installs in the past few months, each time trying a new multimedia ubuntu distro. I started with APODIO, and found switching to ustudio to be ultimately advantageous, but the jack configuring (especially for rt) was more complicated than in APODIO (buggy/inconsistent). After several different tweaks to ustudio (installing karmic and upgrading to lucid, clean installing lucid, trying both with different ppa's) to satisfy what I was missing from APODIO, there were a few too many workarounds I wasn't happy with. One thing I couldn't solve, in the most recent configuration, and that one only, was my internal soundcard was not taking stereo input, but two channels of LEFT signal! Strange eh?

So I resolved I was going to have to just partition out a few more spaces and run multiple ubuntu distros to get all of what I want with the least amount of prep work.

I stumbled upon dreamstudio. I see the guy behind that posts around here some times. All I can say is this was the easiest set up, and smoothest working jack in RT function I've seen from my laptop. She's no spring chicken: Dell Inspiron 9300. Six or seven years old! Everything has been super smooth right out of the box. There are adjustments I have to make to suit my workflow, but that's nothing compared to what I've had to go through in my last several installs.

dreamstudio gets my vote so far. Now I'm gonna go rock out....:guitar:
