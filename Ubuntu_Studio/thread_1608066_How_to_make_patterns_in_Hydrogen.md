### Post by lykeion on 2010-10-28
Well, I'm a drummer myself and when I started with digital music creation I tried to emulate how a real drummer would play when I created the drum midi patterns. I can't say I ever succeeded to get the right feel so I mostly used sample CDs for the drum parts.

Regarding creating a snare-roll. I haven't got much experience with using Hydrogen (mostly worked in Cubase), but if it's possible to use your own sample sounds you should try to get hold of good wav samples for snare sounds. A ghostnote played on a snare is not the same as a normal snare hit at low volume. Try to use different samples for left- and righthand and alternate with those. Adjust the length of the notes and increase the volume to emulate the roll. Avoid adding "Swing".

You can use the same technique when creating double kick rolls.

Here's an article with patterns (not sure what program it is, maybe FruityLoops, but it shouldn't matter):
[http://www.sound-unsound.com/forums/tutorials/4269-guide-ghost-snares-midi.html](http://www.sound-unsound.com/forums/tutorials/4269-guide-ghost-snares-midi.html)

EDIT
Played around a little bit with Hydrogen. Have you tried to install the extra hydrogen-drumkits. The Millo_Multilayered is pretty decent and also have a snare-roll sample.

Hi, there is a velocity control for each beat in the patterns, a vertical line to drag with the mouse, so if you had a roll pattern that had 32 beats, you could make it crescendo, then place the same pattern in the song grid next to it, with the sound receding. You can draw in undulations, exclamations etc. to give your beats more feel.

The patterns are just filling in the grid by clicking the grid location to add or remove the percussion instrument you desire from the loaded kit.
The beat can be playing as you do this, so there is mystery. Place your patterns in the song grid in the order you desire. Non percussion samples
can also be turned into kits, so collections of riffs and hooks played by
any instrument can be useful patterns.

swells:

Choose Millo3_demo1.h2song

Click play in the transport to hear it. The Mode button to the right of the transport changes between song and pattern mode, click it to pattern, and also click the loop icon.

Choose the Crash pattern from the list of patterns on the left. It displays a kick and a crash on the beat grid below. (you can edit by clicking in the grid while it plays. Click an existing beat to remove it, click the short vertical lines on the grid to place a beat.)

Right-click on Crash, in the instrument list to the left of the beat grid in the bottom half of the screen, and choose 'Fill notes', and do the same for kick. Now every beat has the crash and kick.

At the bottom screen are the vertical velocity bars, drag and or click them to form a V shape for the crash, and a left-to-right swell for the kick.
Now play this in pattern mode with loop turned on.
If you like it, or your own twist of it, use the Project menu
'export pattern as' option to save it.

Now go to the song grid, and put this pattern in every square on the Crash row, with the venerable left mouseclicks, so you can enjoy the new swells throughout the song. Hydrogen places few limits on creativity, and offers many options to energize our songs.
