### Post by Stochastic on 2009-05-26
Yes. Lots.

(I really wanted to just leave the message at that, but my conscience prevents me)

Since you've included audacity in your list that you're happy with, it blows the list of comparable softwares almost wide open.  You're also quite non-specific as to what category of software you're looking for; audio editor, multi-track editor, audio recording, midi sequencing, etc...

Here are a couple off the top of my head (some still in development, others are nearing the end of their development rope, others still are in perpetual evolution but widely usable).

Jokosher
Rezound
Ecasound (command line, but full of features)
Snd (tricky to get used to but crazy customizable
MusE
QTractor
Traverso
Rosegarden
Wired
Aldrin

Oh and here are some non-FOSS ones available
Rezound
EnergyXT
Reaper (needs a working wine install)

In general you may find this webpage helpful: [http://apps.linuxaudio.org/](http://apps.linuxaudio.org/)
