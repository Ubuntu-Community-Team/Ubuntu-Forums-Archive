### Post by Stochastic on 2009-11-07
> **henryfranz2005 said:**
> ok sir thanks, but I just tried jokosher, can I have "musical typing"? I mean just like on garageband you put an instrument then you use the keyboard for input? thanks!

You can do this either with a sigular more complicated program like [LMMS]("apt:lmms"), or by connecting a few programs together as is described here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToSeq24Introduction](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToSeq24Introduction)

Programs to link together would need to be a virtual keboard (like [vkeybd]("apt:vkeybd")) connected to a synth (like [zynaddsubfx]("apt:zynaddsubfx") or optionally a sounfont player) and a way to record the synth (like [jokosher]("apt:jokosher")).

It's up to you which route you find easier/better/creatively enjoyable.

### Post by sgx on 2009-11-07
Hi, Henry, You may consider linuxsampler, and the purchase of some excellent soundsets in tascam .gig format, afforable, and some are world class. The sampler
has a few mouseclicks needed to get things going, but when used
with the great zynaddsubfx synth, and hexter for all those DX7 sysex files on the net, and fluidsynth with some quality soundfonts, (fluidR3 GM soundfont, and Unison are good general midi soundfonts) you have a lot of potential.
This presupposes that you have run a jackd system on your mac, and are familiar with its routing schemes. Lots of threads on the net to google qjackctl gui if needs be.
