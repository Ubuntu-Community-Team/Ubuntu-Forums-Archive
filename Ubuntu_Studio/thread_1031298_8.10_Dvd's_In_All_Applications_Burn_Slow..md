### Post by Diskdoc on 2009-02-05
Ok, I finally saw it:

[I][SIZE="1"][ 21.132356] ata4.00: WARNING: ATAPI DMA disabled for reliablity issues. It can be enabled
[ 21.132359] ata4.00: WARNING: via pata_ali.atapi_dma modparam or corresponding sysfs node.[/SIZE][/I]

After some digging around it seems there is a chipset flaw or kernel bug causing this. It's possible to force DMA through one of the following means:

1) "options pata_ali atapi_dma=1" in /etc/modprobe.d/options
2) "echo 1 > /sys/module/pata_ali/parameters/atapi_dma" in /etc/rc.local
3) "pata_ali.atapi_dma=1" after defoptions in /boot/grub/menu.lst

I put in all three just in case :) It seems to fix burning CDs/DVDs but I guess it's too early to say what it does to system stability.

Hopefully this one will get fixed in future Ubuntu releases.
