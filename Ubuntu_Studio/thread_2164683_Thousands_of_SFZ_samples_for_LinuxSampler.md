### Post by foxanthony on 2013-08-01
In hindsight this is rather obvious, but after scouring the internet for a year and discussing it on linuxmusicians.com for months; I finally stumbled across a large selection of SFZ samples that work in LinuxSampler at [https://www.digitalsoundfactory.com/dimension_products](https://www.digitalsoundfactory.com/dimension_products)

I can't have been the first person to discover that Digital Sound Factory has a large library of samples that will play in LinuxSampler, but after a year of searching I never did find any reference to it. So I posted this information to [http://www.linuxmusicians.com/viewtopic.php?f=4&t=11331](http://www.linuxmusicians.com/viewtopic.php?f=4&t=11331) and a fellow linuxmusician has kindly entered it in the Linux Audio Wiki [http://wiki.linuxaudio.org/wiki/instruments](http://wiki.linuxaudio.org/wiki/instruments) 

Hopefully others will help make it known that Linux pro-audio users have a very good sample library available to them.:D

They do have some free samples. Also, when purchasing the SFZ packs they include a copy of Dimension LE which does run in Wine and will make some of the scripting that LinuxSampler isn't able to utilize available.
