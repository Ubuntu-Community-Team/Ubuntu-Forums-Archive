### Post by jejeman on 2012-09-21
I usually find my self using just one. Maybe the reason for that is restircted setup I have - Separate X servers, so I can't share windows of one aplication across them.
Usually in two screen setup I will put on secondary screen Qjacktl and patchage. Also I'm useing it for guitar tuner, timemachine or other simple task applications e.g sound analyses - Something that needs to be look at all the time. And on the Main screen I will put DAW and other apps like synths, effects etc, across multiple workspaces. 
I got used to use multiple workspaces so I really don't need two screens. Once the session is settled, only thing I look at is DAW, and that fits on one workspace.

### Post by sgx on 2012-09-21
I use one monitor, with four virtual desktops, as needed.
One deluxe giant monitor with multiple virtual desktops, might save some energy cost, and be easier on your eyes, and their fatigue,
very important for musicians. Monitor displays are rapidly moving objects, and the eyes often recalibrating between different monitors
may not be as beneficial

nVidia has good dual monitor support, if it is of great benefit.
Editing synth parameters, and viewing the playback on a second
screen. might be very handy for preset designers. Having Ardour
and Reaper on different monitors would also be good, and Hydrogen can
easily use a full screen, nice to have it open to perfect beats that
are part of a daw mix.

Mastering is another area where vast screen real estate can save
the stress of flipping desktops, which although not difficult,
requires nore brain-memory activity, than having everything
before your very eyes.  I would aim for having multiple but
Identicle monitors, budget allowing. I like nice big CRTs,
with actual brightness and contrast knobs
