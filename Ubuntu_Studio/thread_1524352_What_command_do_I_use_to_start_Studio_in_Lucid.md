### Post by mango42 on 2010-07-05
Have you installed UbuntuStudio or are you wanting to use audio/video programs from within the vanilla version of Lucid lynx?

If you look in Synaptic for **ubuntustudio-audio** you should find what you're looking for, if it's sound manipulation you're after.

Then there's the issue of setting it up to work correctly on your system. These are good places to start:-

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/UbuntuStudio)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToJACKConfiguration](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToJACKConfiguration)
[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToQjackCtlConnections](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/HowToQjackCtlConnections)

I suspect however that you are asking the wrong question ;-) as Studio is an *environment* rather than a specific program, and for audio/video work is tuned specifically for real-time use, hence the dedicated real-time kernel.

HTH - there's loads of HowTo get started articles and help right here on this sub-forum. I know for a fact I would never have got started without it! ;-)
