### Post by skullmunky on 2010-04-09
Read the Macbook Pro guides closely, they're very helpful.  Macbooks have weird hardware and often require an unusual amount of extra patching and tweaking after installing Ubuntu.  The good news is that the hardware's weird but consistent, so once the fixes are figured out for a particular model the results are repeatable.

[https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBookPro](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/MacBookPro)

Pay close attention to the sections on Sound, and check for any issues with wifi and the graphics card before installing.  Sound has been the main irritant for me on my last few MBP's, there's something consistently bizarre with whatever apple does with their sound cards so you may have to install some extra patches and edit some config files to make it work smoothly.
