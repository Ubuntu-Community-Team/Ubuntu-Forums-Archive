### Post by snowpine on 2011-01-08
The LTS releases have historically come in the Spring every 2 years (June 2006, April 2008, April 2010, and most likely April 2012) as you can read here: [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Ubuntu_releases](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Ubuntu_releases)

Ubuntu Studio is not actually a separate operating system, but rather a version of Ubuntu with a different selection of default applications. So it follows the same release and support cycle.

### Post by snowpine on 2011-01-08
Support for 8.04 is ending in 3 months, so I would not recommend it at this point in time. I do not know why the download page still recommends it.

All packages in Ubuntu Studio are also part of Ubuntu and therefore are fully supported by Canonical.

As for a supported hardware list, here is a good place to start: [http://webapps.ubuntu.com/certification/](http://webapps.ubuntu.com/certification/)

### Post by sgx on 2011-01-11
One strength of linux is the easy re-installations, utilizing
separate /home partitions, installs on usb sticks, wubi installs,
live CD/DVDs, external hard-disks, so one can be adventuresome
compared to windows setups. The top ten linux apps are quite
excellent, and there are always new and interesting tools being
created. Compatible hardware is the key, and staying flexible
outside the box means boredom won't be much of an issue. Throw in
a lot of fine commercial apps that work in wineasio setups, and sonic
limitations won't be a problem. More devs are turning to cross-platform
developement, which bodes well for the future.
