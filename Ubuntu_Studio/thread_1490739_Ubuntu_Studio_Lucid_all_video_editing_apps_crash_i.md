### Post by sgx on 2010-05-23
I would google look into PPAs with updated video apps to add to your
repositories list, and press 'reload' in synaptic, and reinstall everything that crashed, and their support libs and codecs, perhaps
an update will have been dragged in. The google

name-of-video-chip linux crash ubuntu 2010

If no clues emerge from that and similar searches,

1. Install older ubuntu
2. If video is part of your future, get a very fast desktop computer
with a nice nvidia graphics card. Support for other brands will probably never catch up to nvidia. 

If you are new to linux, 'hardware purchases known to work' are of the highest importance,
due to limitations of relying on unpaid software authors, working in separate corners of the
world, with varying goals and skills. Add to that the newness of 10.04, many bugs get fixed
in the first 3 months, so I mentioned the older version, Karmic 9.10, may be  worth installing
if  no solutions are found soon.

Cheers
