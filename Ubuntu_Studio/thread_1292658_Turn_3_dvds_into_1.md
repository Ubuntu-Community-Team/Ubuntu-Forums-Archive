### Post by ryanisablond on 2009-10-20
> **ram4nd said:**
> I need quality to stay original and i need subtitles to dvd movie selection

You're in luck! Handbrake can rip subtitles and keep the original video quality. 

I'm using version 0.9.3 on a Mac (don't think the Linux version will be too different) and there's a tab-like-thing right in the middle of the screen which lets you select "Audio & Subtitles" options. You can force it to rip the subtitles there.

In the "Video" tab-like-thing, you'll have all sorts of options as to what quality you want to rip at. Personally, I find it easiest to use one of the included presets by clicking the "Toggle Presets" button. You will probably want the "film" preset. If not, you can tweak the settings to your heart's desire. Uncompressed video is big, so make sure you have several gigs free HD space. 

Now that you've imported your video files, follow stinger30au's devede instructions to burn your own disk. Hope that helps!
