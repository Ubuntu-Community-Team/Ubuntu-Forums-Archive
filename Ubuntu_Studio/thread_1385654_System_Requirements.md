### Post by lev-unr on 2010-01-20
Also before you go spending $1,000 on a new computer with all the latest requirements install it on your current one and see how it works for you. 
 
Often times, you will be surprised just how well ubuntu works against all odds. 
 
Unless you are planning on starting a productions studio it is rarely necessary to invest that much money on hardware. 
 
For example, when I first started to use studio - I installed it and worked with it for months on an old Gateway laptop that I picked up for $250 at best buy in 2006. 
 
That thing was a piece of crap under windows but it was pushed to its full potential using studio -- and I was able to get a lot of work done without investing a bunch of money on equipment. 
 
Just a thought.
