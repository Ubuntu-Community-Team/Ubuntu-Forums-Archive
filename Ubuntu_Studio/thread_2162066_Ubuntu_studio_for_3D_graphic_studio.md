### Post by BreezyBrooke on 2013-07-13
Buying a card designed for 3d modeling is a good start. I'm currently using a Nvidia Quadro 600 and it doubled the rendering performance of gimp. Blender is no exception. Also, a more powerful CPU also works. Like a quad core for 3D modeling. Or like me, a good dual core for complex 2D rendering.

Also, are the Proprietary drivers installed? They can be a big help. The ones installed by default for Nvidia and ATI are at about 30% effeciency more or less depending on the card.
