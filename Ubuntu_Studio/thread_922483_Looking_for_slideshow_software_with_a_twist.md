### Post by jsully1 on 2008-09-17
My friend has an idea where he's going to have a camera going around and transmitting pictures back to a laptop. He's looking for some sort of slideshow software that will be able to realize that there are new images in a folder, and then move on to them after it completes the initial batch.

For example, while it's showing pictures 1-10, pictures 11 through 15 are added. Ideally when it gets to 10 it will just continue on through 15.

After that I suppose that it could loop, though it would be cool if it just sat on 15 and waited for you to add 16. Does anything like this exist in Ubuntu, on OS for that matter?

---

### Post by vanadium on 2008-09-17
Eye of gnome, the default picture viewer, will do just that. If you launch it in a directory and press F5, The slide show will start and loop when it reached the end. If ou add pictures, the next time the new pictures will also be included.
