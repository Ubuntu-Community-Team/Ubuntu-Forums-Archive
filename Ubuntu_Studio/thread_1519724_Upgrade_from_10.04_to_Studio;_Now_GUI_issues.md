### Post by #r00t on 2010-06-28
I have a Gateway NV series laptop. Installed Lucid 10.04 Desktop a month ago, been running just fine...

Got a wild hair to upgrade the install to 'Studio' by installing the Studio packages, per instructions found here: [https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Ubuntu%20Studio%20Upgrade%20from%20Ubuntu](https://help.ubuntu.com/community/Ubuntu%20Studio%20Upgrade%20from%20Ubuntu)

Did a reboot, noticed my Login window was different.

Logged in, and quickly realized that now when an important window takes focus (volume control, compiz config, anything like that really) all other open windows fade away to reveal a half-opaque desktop. Once I close the window, everything open fades back into focus. 

I can't for the life of me figure out why it is doing this, and how to fix it, but it is driving me nuts!

I've searched and can't seem to find the same problem, so I figured I would ask you guys!

Also, did this install KDE or something? I noticed a program called 'KPackageKit' was installed with this, and I think it's a KDE app (still a n00b)... 

I just want my desktop back to how it was! Ubuntu is already somewhat sluggish on this laptop, and this fade effect sure isn't helping!

Thanks in advance!

EDIT: Of course, after 2 hours of tinkering, I post this... Then 10 minutes later, I fix it... For some reason, when I went to System>Preferences>Appearance none of the options were 'checked'... Although I did still have advanced appearance capabilities, nothing in the menu showed that it had a selection, so, I clicked 'Normal', and viola! No more stupid fade!
