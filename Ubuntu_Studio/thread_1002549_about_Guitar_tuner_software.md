### Post by Stochastic on 2008-12-05
> **frankleeee said:**
> I don't want to argue with you but your completely wrong have you ever seen a orchestra or a piano tuner use anything but their ear. In a orchestra 90 people tune to the lead or concert masters A.


Piano tuners refer to a tuning fork to correct the pitch of the piano, then they tune every other string to that.  Orchestras use the lead Oboe as it is an instrument that rarely varies in tuning.  There is always a reference point - which is good, it helps your ears learn proper A440 tuning so that one day you can claim to have perfect pitch.

But you raise an ok argument about using your ears to tune whenever possible.

As for the issue of not getting fmit to work, have you read through the configuration page on their website: [http://home.gna.org/fmit/configure.html](http://home.gna.org/fmit/configure.html)
In the main screen, click on the pair of tools to open the settings dialogue, then choose the capture tab, then select your current audio driver.  If you're running Jack, use that, if you're not, use ALSA.
