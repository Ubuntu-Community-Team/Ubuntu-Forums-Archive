### Post by kayosiii on 2010-03-09
if you have lots of $$$ then there is Nuke or Houdini and Apple may still perhaps sell shake though that one will be a bit dated. Nuke in particular is concidered one of the best Compositors out there.

If you don't have $$$ ramen is an interesting new entry to the field. Cinelerra contains some Compositing features. I am not sure what is happening to Jahshaka.

Finally blender contains both a video sequence editor and a node based compositor. I have a book called Blender Compositing which could be very handy if you go this route.

There isn't really anything that works exactly the way that After Effects works. Node based compositing seems to be more popular these days.
