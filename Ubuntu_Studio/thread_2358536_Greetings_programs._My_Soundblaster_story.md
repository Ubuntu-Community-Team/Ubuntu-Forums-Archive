### Post by mte2 on 2017-04-14
Found ubuntu studio and knew it would be great for playing around with audio creation. Had some issues trying to use an old AMD board and decided to use a spare Dell workstation instead. Install went well except I had no sound.
I had looked through my pile of soundcards and had two identical looking Audigy 2 cards, so I chose the one with a joystick/midi.
Every diagnostic I ran showed volume and channels and bouncing meters and unmuted audio coming from the correctly identified soundblaster. I was able to switch to 2.1 and 5.1 and 7.1 and see it in alsamixer.
Verified the card worked in a windows machine.
Looking closer at the second audigy card I saw it said SB0350 instead of SB0240. But it didn't have the Zs on it that the creative site said. If it was a 350 it would be a better card so I installed that one.
Installing that card, I got the sound I was expecting after following the external jacks layout from the 240 card for a 5.1 setup. If I want 7.1 I will need a digital cable and system. But the 5.1 is working perfect.
I guess my advice would be to verify the SB number on the card instead of the name, as OEM models used by Dell and HP were different manufactures.
Other than a slower boot, Ubuntu Studio does everything like regular LTS so I think I will try it on my next few builds as well.
