### Post by modmadmike on 2013-09-25
I have a EeePC 901 with a physically broken headphone connector. I remember hearing that HD-Audio (such as the ALC269 in the EeePC) have remap-able ports. [http://www.reaper-x.com/2012/02/13/how-to-remap-retasking-realtek-onboard-jacks-ports/]("http://www.reaper-x.com/2012/02/13/how-to-remap-retasking-realtek-onboard-jacks-ports/") shows how to do it in windows, but I only use Linux on all my computers (well I also have a MacBook Pro late-2011 that dual boots OSX/Linux). I am fairly sure it can be done by editing alsa conf files or something similar (Yes I know that the microphone jack also carries a voltage, but from what I hear HDAudio can completely reconfigure the onboard traces).
