### Post by mjitkop on 2010-06-12
What this means is that Ubuntu does not know how to open MP3 files by default. It needs to be told to use a specific application.

Take a look at the Ubuntu online help about this:

[http://library.gnome.org/users/user-guide/stable/gosnautilus-add-actions.html.en](http://library.gnome.org/users/user-guide/stable/gosnautilus-add-actions.html.en)

I hope this helps.

Thinking about it more, are you able to play other MP3 files that you know? It could be that you don't have MP3 codecs installed. If my previous instructions don't work and you can't play MP3 files, you may want to look at this other online help page:

[https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/musicvideophotos/C/music-listen.html](https://help.ubuntu.com/10.04/musicvideophotos/C/music-listen.html)
