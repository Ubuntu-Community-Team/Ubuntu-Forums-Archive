### Post by sgx on 2011-10-26
Use an old desktop computer with maudio pci soundcard, and nvidia video card, an older model so it has kernel support. Laptops are the worst for audio production. You pay through the nose for modern design implementations that are useless for audio production.

Get a mainboard with as close to 3 ghz cpu cores
as you can find, because actual multi-core support is not uniform among
systems and apps. So the fastest first core wins. 2 gig of ram and up
will do well, there are sweet spots in ram pricing, gamers sell off
second generation systems that may be excellent for audio production, when a supported soundcard is added.

The best bargain is buying what works, not what the wife likes. :popcorn:
