### Post by paulmerchant on 2008-09-11
Except for high-end, proprietary NLE's used by film studios, you might not find what you need on the NLE side of things. As far as I know, there aren't even any Linux NLE's for sale at the moment (MainActor died a year or two ago).

There's a lot of open source activity in this area, though. You can try Cinelerra. I'm waiting for a Cinelerra fork to make more progress before I try it again. Other projects, like Kino and PiTiVi, hold promise.

For professional editing today, you can do just about anything with some of the many transcoding tools on Linux and the Blender sequence editor. (You could even learn to composite amazing 3D effects over your video.) Don't be fooled by Blender's interface or small size. It's a professional powerhouse.

There are a number of great audio applications for your audio mixes (i.e. Ardour). Unfortunately, using Blender, Ardour, etc., will give you a steep learning curve to a convoluted work flow.
