### Post by Ergo on 2010-08-09
I've personally used the WD "book" style of external USB hard drive.  The 1 and 2 TB sized drives are getting pretty cheap to buy.  I have been video capturing and creating movies with these drives for several years.  The only set back is the drives will go into a sleep mode and have to "wake up" when you need to access them, but aside from that, they can keep up with your demand.

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-08-09
If you are recording multi track audio, then I would avoid using external USB if you can, they will not perform like a SATA drive would, which could mean latency in your projects.

My netbook only has USB inputs. I'll be using a USB card with two channels in and out, so no more than two tracks will be recorded at once. I suppose I might end up with ten 48kHz stereo tracks' worth in Ardour to play back. Is there a real risk of running out of moxie in such a situation?

### Post by cchhrriiss121212 on 2010-08-09
You should be Ok with that, but it would depend on how many tracks you are using as USB transfer rates will only go to a finite amount. If you are reading and writing to the disk at the same time it could prove too much.
What I would do is to test it out with a small USB stick if you have one handy, then you will know whether or not a larger disk will be a good investment.
