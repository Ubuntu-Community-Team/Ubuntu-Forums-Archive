### Post by Stochastic on 2008-10-08
Yeah, it does depend on the design as ddarsow said.

In general it's bad news to slice up images to use as banners/layout for your websites (the GIMP method), though for some tasks that is the best way.  Whenever possible it'd be best/most compatible across systems/most stable/fastest/etc... to take all the background colors/sections/regions you have, and implement those directly in the CSS code (the layout section of the html code).  I may be speaking over your head here, but these are the regions you need to be looking into converting to, if you want the thing to look right.

It may be possible to take and have some WISIWYG html editor open your current file (after a myriad of conversions I'm sure), but figuring all that out may be more work than it's worth (probably after all those conversions, half the items will be out of place etc...).

In the end, the correct answer to the question depends on your design.  Can we see it, even if you have to blur out the names of businesses or censor any other content?
