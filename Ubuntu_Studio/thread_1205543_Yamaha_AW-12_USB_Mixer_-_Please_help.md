### Post by trulan on 2009-07-06
1. Is the hissing sound there even when the mixer is not connected to the computer?  Does it show up on your recordings?  It may be an issue with your mixer itself.  Possibly one of your faders, or your trim volumes, is all the way up?
2. For two sets of headphones, buy a headphone Y (just a few dollars) and plug them both in that way.  For three or more, you'll want a headphone amp (a bit more money).
3. Does your mixer have an EQ or effects on its main channels, or hooked up?  You may be modifying the signal that is fed to the headphones or speakers, while USB is getting more of a raw sgnal (ie, you might be boosting the bass, which you hear in the headphones, but the USB hookup is pulling its signal off before the bass boost.)  If so, that's OK.  It's almost always better to put your EQ and effects on with your software after you record, then mix it down into its final format.

Are you using Ubuntu?  If so, which version?
