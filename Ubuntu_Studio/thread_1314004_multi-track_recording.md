### Post by lexen on 2009-11-07
If it's only on playback then that means that you are recording hiss.  Are you going through a mixboard, or is your mic attached directly to your computer?  Either way, I would try turning the volume down on your mic either with the mixboard, or with your sound settings.

   All that said, you don't want to amplify it after the fact, so if the mic is already too quiet, then you may be out of luck.  If it is too loud, then adjust the volume settings in as many places as you can, making sure not to over do it in any one spot (ie, don't have your mixboard turned all the way down and have the volume control at max).

### Post by thorgal on 2009-11-08
laptops onboard cards have crappy AD converters. Most of the focus on those chips is on the playback side so you get a "nice" audio experience when playing back MP3s or internet streams (what most laptop users do).

As soon as you get into recording, you are not the average laptop user (unless all you care is network-conferencing, skyping, etc, in which case, it's not so dramatic as simple software noise filters were added to these conferencing apps, without you knowing that it does a bit of noise filtering behind your back).

For proper recording of external sources, you will need a dedicated device and good AD converters. There are reasonably good USB sound cards that will definitely provide much better recording quality. If you're into more pro stuff, then it would be wiser to invest into a proper sound device (M-Audio and RME come to mind).
