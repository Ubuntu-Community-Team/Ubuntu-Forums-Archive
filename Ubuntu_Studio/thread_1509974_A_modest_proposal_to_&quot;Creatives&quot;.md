### Post by cfhowlett on 2010-06-14
Greetings Ubuntu Studio Users:

Forgive me if this issue has been addressed or implemented before.  I'm still learning my way around the various communities and projects and might well have missed relevant efforts.  But if I have the privilege of actually proposing a viable innovation...

As I look around the coffee shop to see who else is availing themselves of the free and fast wifi connection, one thing becomes obvious; most of these folks are Mac users.

I live in a town which is effectively a suburb of a major city.  Most of my fellow wifi explorers are high school or college students or the occasional business person.  

I trust it's not too heretical to state my opinion that Apple designs excellent hardware and software.  However, the thought of locking myself into Apple's vertical matrix  is even more objectionable than the cost of acquiring a "premium" computer.  So, I have become an avid student and user of Ubuntu Studio.

One has to appreciate the rabid adoration of the Mac crowd as well as admire the integration of all things Mac into a fairly cohesive and persistent marketing effort.  For example, scroll to the bottom of this web site;

[http://www.faculty.fairfield.edu/faculty/hodgson/Courses/so191/Projects2007/Stott/Site/Welcome.html](http://www.faculty.fairfield.edu/faculty/hodgson/Courses/so191/Projects2007/Stott/Site/Welcome.html)

"Made on a Mac"
[img]http://innovationfeeder.files.wordpress.com/2007/11/made-on-a-mac.jpg[/img]

How pithy.  How simple, elegant, effective and memorable.  Apple officially licensed this art for some time; [http://www.theapplecollection.com/various/MacMade/AppleWebBadgesMadeOn.html](http://www.theapplecollection.com/various/MacMade/AppleWebBadgesMadeOn.html)
 and dictated a standard usage policy;
[http://www.apple.com/about/webbadges/index.html](http://www.apple.com/about/webbadges/index.html).  Naturally, Apple users also crafted their own vision of this art;
[http://allaboutiweb.com/index.php/2008/08/free-custom-badges/](http://allaboutiweb.com/index.php/2008/08/free-custom-badges/).  Apple no doubt condones if not outright supports such innovation resulting in some enviable endorsements; [http://the-tech.mit.edu/Projects/Macmade/](http://the-tech.mit.edu/Projects/Macmade/).  

So, my point; shouldn't we have a similar, and no doubt superior, item for our favorite distro?  Personally, I've elected to include something along these lines in whenever I can;
[http://spreadubuntu.neomenlo.org/files/Tabbed%20Tearsheet.sla-page1_0.jpeg](http://spreadubuntu.neomenlo.org/files/Tabbed%20Tearsheet.sla-page1_0.jpeg) and [http://dl.dropbox.com/u/7001421/Washington%20Ubuntu/Ubuntu%20Card.pdf](http://dl.dropbox.com/u/7001421/Washington%20Ubuntu/Ubuntu%20Card.pdf),
but I would really like to see an OFFICIAL "Made in Ubuntu" type piece for marking our creations.  It could be effectively deployed in print and electronic mediums.  I believe that this item would help promote the Ubuntu Studio distribution and, as an added benefit, knock a bit of SMUG out of the Mac crowd.  Just sayin...

Ubuntu Studio developers and users; What say ye?

### Post by zettberlin on 2010-06-18
The point is: the free as in speech feature is the only real substantial feature of most of the software that is written for Linux. Apps like Ardour or ZynaddSubFX or AMS are quite similar to comparable, established propriatary apps for Win/Mac. In detail there are differences that come with the fact, that marketing does not have that influence in the process of making of most of the free apps but in the end it is still software that follows design-patterns established in the 1990ies.

There is also the influence of the fact, that most Linux-audio programmers cannot/do not make any money with their work and thus cannot work fulltime on the software we use. But this is a thing that can be changed and should be changed.

If I would have to "market" free audio-software on Linux I would concentrate on this:

Freedom

Collaboration

Openness

Community
