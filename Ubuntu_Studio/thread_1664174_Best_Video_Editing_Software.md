### Post by wkulecz on 2011-01-11
For a dose of reality, none of these will come close to Sony Vegas Movie Studio in actual functionality.

Openshot and KDEnlive are maybe half-way there, if you can overlook some crashes.  Cinelerra or Blender Video sequence editor are supposed to be the "best" but good luck getting started with them.

If yuo want ot play with video editing for free Linux is fine, but if you need to actually produce output videos my advice on the low end is to dual boot into Windows 7 and run Sony Vegas Video.  If your time is worth anything at all you'll quickly recoup the cost of the the vegas software and Windows.

### Post by wkulecz on 2011-01-12
> I was able to do a couple of screencasts (still + videos + background music) very fast in Blender despite of very little knowledge of Blender, if any. I do agree that it's not the best solution though.

That is encouraging, but its minimally editing video and ignores the major bug-a-boo of video editing -- keeping audio and video in sync during and after editing multiple video files with transitions.


Here are some nice high quality videos you can download to test your editor in a variety of resolutions.

[http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/hd/HDGalleryCollection_archive_1.html](http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/hd/HDGalleryCollection_archive_1.html)

Openshot loses audio video sync when scrubbing thru one of these.  These are a good analog of the non-hobby video editing I have to do -- take a professionally produced video file and cut out a few clips to be used in Power Point presentations.  Losing A/V sync is a showstopper.

So far none of the Linux video editors have been up to the task, or were so hard to get started with that I gave up trying.  So far OpenShot has come the closest on one of the large 720p mp4 files.

My hobby video editing is much more complicated (multiple camera angles of scenes) than what I do to help people out on the job.  Of course good support of the video formats actually captured but camcorders is paramount but I do have one mid-range camcorder than produces HD mp4 files that Linux can playback and OpenShot or KDEnlive sort of edit.

But progress is being made, so I keep trying when I have some free time.
