### Post by sgx on 2011-03-05
You can try booting a live cd, mount your hard-drive(s) and copy needed files and desired settings to a usb stick. Then reinstall. If you can, I would set up a second partition, 10 or 12 gigs for   /   (the root partition is  /  ) use double your ram amount for /swap, and the rest for /home. This way, new installs can keep
the settings in /home/username, when you choose not to repartition /home during a future new install.

Most system upgrades that skip a version, are ill advised, since linux
has so much active developement, and there is no way unpaid app developers can act in perfect concert.

If you have 2 cd/dvd burners, or 1 burner, and also a non-burner, you could boot a live cd/dvd on the non-burner, and burn files you want to keep, in case a usbstick is not handy.
Cheers
